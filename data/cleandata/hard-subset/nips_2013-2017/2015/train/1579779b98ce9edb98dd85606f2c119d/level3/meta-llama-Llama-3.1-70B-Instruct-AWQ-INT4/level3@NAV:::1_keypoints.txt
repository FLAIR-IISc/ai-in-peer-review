- Proposes a novel decision-tree learning method that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters via a global objective.
- Establishes a connection between decision-tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.
- Develops a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to optimize the surrogate objective.
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and provides a thorough related-work review.
- Experimental results show non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines on several classification benchmarks.
- Strength: novel, elegant approach with potential to advance decision-tree learning.
- Strength: well-organized presentation and detailed performance analysis.
- Weakness: experimental performance is modest—appropriate but not outstanding.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and practical runtime considerations.
- Weakness: limited discussion of potential applications and broader implications.
- Suggestion: consider dropping and re-optimizing the last split and its leaf with standard methods to improve results.
- Suggestion: evaluate whether global optimization increases overfitting and analyze trade-offs against existing methods.
- Suggestion: provide more implementation details, including hyperparameters and computational resources used.
- Overall: promising and well-presented work that could make a significant contribution with revisions addressing weaknesses.
