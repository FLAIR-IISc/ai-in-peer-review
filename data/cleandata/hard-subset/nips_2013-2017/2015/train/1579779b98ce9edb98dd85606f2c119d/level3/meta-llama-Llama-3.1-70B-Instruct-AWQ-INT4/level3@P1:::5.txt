This paper proposes a novel approach to learning decision trees by optimizing an upper bound on empirical loss, regularized with L2 and optimized via stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The authors establish a connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables, which enables the use of techniques from structured prediction to improve decision tree learning.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear and concise introduction that sets the stage for the rest of the paper. The problem formulation is elegant and well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of related work in the field.

The key contribution of the paper is the development of a global algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions. The authors show that this approach can lead to better generalization performance than traditional greedy methods, which optimize the split functions one node at a time.

The empirical results presented in the paper are somewhat unconvincing, as the global optimization approach only significantly outperforms greedy trees on a few datasets. Additionally, the computational cost of the global optimization approach is higher than that of greedy methods, which may be a concern for large datasets.

One potential issue with the paper is the lack of clarity in the description of the Stable SGD algorithm. The authors provide a high-level overview of the algorithm, but do not provide enough details to allow the reader to fully understand how it works. Specifically, it is unclear how data points are assigned to leaves and how the assignment is updated during the optimization process.

Another minor issue is the lack of clarity in the explanation of how the parameters of the tree are projected onto the simplex in practice. The authors mention that this is done to enforce the constraint that each row of the parameter matrix sums to one, but do not provide enough details to allow the reader to fully understand how this is implemented.

Finally, there are a few minor issues with the writing and formatting of the paper. Specifically, there are a few instances of confusing phrasing, and some of the sentences are corrupted or misspelled. Additionally, the acronym "FSGD" is used without being defined.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and well-motivated approach to decision tree learning, but the empirical results are not convincing enough to fully support the claims made in the paper. With some revisions to address the issues mentioned above, the paper could be even stronger.

Strengths:

* Elegant formulation connecting tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables
* Global algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions
* Empirical results show improved generalization performance over greedy methods on some datasets

Weaknesses:

* Empirical results are not convincing enough to fully support the claims made in the paper
* Computational cost of the global optimization approach is higher than that of greedy methods
* Lack of clarity in the description of the Stable SGD algorithm
* Minor issues with writing and formatting

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to decision tree learning
* The global algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions is a significant contribution to the field
* The empirical results show improved generalization performance over greedy methods on some datasets

Arguments for rejection:

* The empirical results are not convincing enough to fully support the claims made in the paper
* The computational cost of the global optimization approach is higher than that of greedy methods
* The lack of clarity in the description of the Stable SGD algorithm is a significant issue

Recommendation:

* Accept with minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above
* Request additional empirical results to further support the claims made in the paper
* Request clarification on the description of the Stable SGD algorithm and the implementation of the simplex projection.
