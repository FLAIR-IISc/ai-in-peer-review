- Proposes a novel non-greedy, global decision-tree optimization by reformulating tree learning as structured prediction with latent "sign vector" branching decisions.
- Relaxes the non-differentiable, non-convex empirical loss to a differentiable (still non-convex) upper bound inspired by max-margin structured prediction.
- Derives an SGD-based optimization that requires loss-augmented inference at each step and provides efficient algorithms for that subproblem.
- The approach is original and theoretically elegant, offering a significant conceptual contribution and new research directions.
- Key observation: SGD updates are sparse, affecting only one node's parameters per step, suggesting adaptive tree growth from zero initialization.
- Major practical concern: L2 regularization likely yields dense, less interpretable oblique splits; L1 sparsity trade-offs are not explored.
- Experimental validation is weak: pruned greedy-tree baselines are insufficiently described and comparisons to simple global strategies (e.g., ensembles on data subsets) are missing.
- Ambiguity in handling zero-weight nodes is a critical edge case that needs precise definition.
- Paper title mentions "forests" but method is developed and evaluated only for single trees, which is misleading.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution but unconvincing practical utility and experiments; major revisions needed on experimental design, interpretability, and clarity.
