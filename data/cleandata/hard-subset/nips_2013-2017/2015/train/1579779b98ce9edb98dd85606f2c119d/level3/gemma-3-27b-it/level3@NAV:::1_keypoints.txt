- Novel approach linking decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that enables efficient stochastic gradient descent.  
- Allows joint optimization of split functions and leaf parameters, moving beyond greedy methods.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and grounded in relevant prior work.  
- Algorithmic complexity O(d^2 p) is a notable improvement, making training of deep trees feasible.  
- Regularization strategy is proposed to mitigate overfitting concerns.  
- Experimental gains over greedy baselines are modest and inconsistent across datasets.  
- Increased method complexity is not yet justified by dramatically better empirical performance.  
- Reviewer recommends trying a post-hoc re-optimization of the last split and leaf node to potentially improve results.  
- Calls for a more thorough investigation of overfitting and generalization effects despite regularization.  
- Suggests analyzing learned tree structures and comparing their complexity to greedy-produced trees.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, clear, and potentially impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental results not sufficiently convincing and insufficient analysis of overfitting.
