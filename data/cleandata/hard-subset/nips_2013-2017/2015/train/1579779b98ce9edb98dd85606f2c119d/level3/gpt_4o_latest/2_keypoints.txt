- Proposes a novel non-greedy joint optimization for decision tree parameters by framing induction as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex-concave surrogate upper bound on empirical loss optimized via SGD.  
- Presents fast loss-augmented inference reducing complexity to O(d^2 p), improving scalability for deep trees.  
- Empirically outperforms greedy baselines on multiple datasets with better generalization and less overfitting.  
- Mathematical derivation and surrogate objective are clearly presented and theoretically motivated.  
- The empirical loss bound (Eq. 7) derivation is unclear and lacks a complete proof in the supplement.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; missing comparison to greedy training with non-axis-aligned splits (e.g., OC1).  
- The problem of unassigned leaves during training is insufficiently addressed and SSGD’s effectiveness is not thoroughly evaluated.  
- Requiring predefined tree depth is a limitation that is not explored experimentally.  
- No direct runtime comparison with greedy methods is provided despite claims of efficiency.  
- Suggest clarifying and completing the proof of the empirical loss bound.  
- Suggest adding baselines with non-axis-aligned greedy methods and exploring initialization/regularization for unassigned leaves.  
- Suggest experiments analyzing tree depth effects and direct runtime comparisons to validate scalability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the theoretical and experimental concerns.
