- Proposes a novel global decision-tree optimization algorithm by reformulating learning as structured prediction with latent variables.
- Handles a non-differentiable, non-convex loss by deriving a differentiable upper bound and optimizing it with stochastic gradient descent.
- Main contributions: casting tree learning as structured prediction and developing efficient algorithms to optimize the objective.
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and includes a thorough related-work review.
- Experiments on several benchmark datasets show non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines and are less prone to overfitting.
- Lacks detailed analysis of the algorithm's computational complexity.
- Experimental evaluation is limited—needs comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- Provides insufficient insight into hyperparameter choices (e.g., regularization ν and learning rate η).
- Significance: promising potential to improve decision-tree accuracy and efficiency, with applications in CV and NLP.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address complexity analysis, experimental breadth, and hyperparameter discussion.
