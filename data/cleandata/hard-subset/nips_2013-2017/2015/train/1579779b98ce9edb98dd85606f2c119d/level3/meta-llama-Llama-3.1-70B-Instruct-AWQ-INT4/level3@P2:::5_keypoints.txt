- Proposes a novel algorithm to learn decision trees with linear (oblique) split functions by optimizing a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss, regularized with L2 and optimized via SGD.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables, enabling the upper-bound formulation.  
- Frames tree learning as a global optimization problem over joint configurations of oblique splits and leaf parameters using binary latent decision variables.  
- Introduces Stable SGD (SSGD), a conservative SGD variant that changes data-to-leaf assignments more cautiously than standard SGD.  
- Empirical results show non-greedy (globally optimized) trees often give better test performance across depths and reduce overfitting.  
- Improvements over greedy trees are significant only on a few datasets, and the global method is more computationally intensive.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient background for non-expert readers.  
- The structured-prediction-with-latents connection is an original and important theoretical contribution.  
- Significance is promising given widespread use of decision trees, but more evidence is needed to claim consistent superiority over greedy methods.  
- Major empirical weakness: limited convincing evidence that global optimization consistently outperforms greedy baselines.  
- Methodological clarity issue: description of SSGD is unclear — authors should detail data-to-leaf assignment, provide pseudo-code, and explain the procedure.  
- Figure 2 needs clarification: state whether results use SGD or SSGD and define what constitutes an “active” leaf.  
- Minor issues and typos throughout the manuscript should be fixed.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address empirical evidence gaps, clarify SSGD and Figure 2, and correct minor errors; reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10, Clarity 9/10.
