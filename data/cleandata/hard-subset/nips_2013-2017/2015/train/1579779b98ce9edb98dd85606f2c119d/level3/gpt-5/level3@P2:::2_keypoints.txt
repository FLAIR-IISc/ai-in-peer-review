- Paper proposes non-greedy, joint optimization of oblique split parameters and leaf distributions under a global objective to improve decision trees.  
- Formulates oblique splits as structured prediction with latent variables and derives a surrogate upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Introduces Hamming-ball radius-1 restriction for loss-augmented inference, reducing per-example complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p).  
- Proposes Stable SGD (SSGD) that freezes leaf assignments for several iterations to mitigate inactive leaves.  
- Experiments on multiclass datasets (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST) show consistent gains over greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique, CO2).  
- Novelty: latent-structured view of oblique splits and Hamming-ball loss-augmented inference are original contributions relative to greedy and soft-routing methods.  
- Technical soundness: overall formulation is compelling but the empirical loss upper bound exposition is confusing and may be incorrect without extra conditions.  
- The proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is incomplete/unclear and needs full, self-contained proof.  
- SSGD heuristic seems ad hoc; a principled connection to EM, CCCP, or block-coordinate minimization is missing.  
- Algorithmic components (fast inference, per-row norm constraints) are reasonable but require deeper justification for some choices.  
- Empirical evaluation shows improvements but is limited: missing relevant baselines, incomplete ablation studies, and pre-specified depth without full analysis.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but the bound presentation needs more precise statements and corrected use of max vs argmax.  
- Terminology needs clarification (e.g., precise definition of “leaves” and what “d+1 distinct values” refers to under Hamming radius 1).  
- Supplement must include full proofs (not sketches) for key propositions and the loss bound.  
- Significance: direction is important for single-tree accuracy and interpretability but current shortcomings limit immediate impact.  
- Concern that Hamming-ball restriction’s practical impact and effect on learned models is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Suggestion: clarify/correct loss upper bound, state conditions under which it holds, and provide complete proof addressing counterexamples.  
- Suggestion: provide full proof of Proposition 1 and discuss implications of per-row norm constraints and scale behavior.  
- Suggestion: formalize SSGD as alternating minimization or CCCP and justify its use theoretically.  
- Suggestion: empirically compare full loss-augmented inference vs Hamming-ball restriction across depths and datasets.  
- Suggestion: add greedy oblique-split baseline using discriminative node training (SVM/logistic) with/without initialization.  
- Suggestion: provide ablations on initialization, ν, SSGD vs vanilla SGD, and Hamming-ball radius.  
- Suggestion: analyze depth selection, pruning/validation-based stopping, and sensitivity to depth and stopping criteria.  
- Suggestion: expand runtime analysis—end-to-end training time vs baselines and scaling with n, p, d.  
- Pros: tackles important problem, introduces novel latent-structured surrogate and efficient inference trick, shows consistent empirical improvements.  
- Cons: potential flaw/ambiguity in loss bound, incomplete proofs, heuristic SSGD, missing baselines/ablations, and unconvincing demonstration of fast inference benefits.  
- Reviewer questions: precise conditions for loss bound; sensitivity to initialization and ν; frequency and persistence of inactive leaves and SSGD effect; differences between full vs Hamming-ball inference.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending clarification/correction of the bound, full proofs, stronger justification for SSGD, and additional empirical validation.
