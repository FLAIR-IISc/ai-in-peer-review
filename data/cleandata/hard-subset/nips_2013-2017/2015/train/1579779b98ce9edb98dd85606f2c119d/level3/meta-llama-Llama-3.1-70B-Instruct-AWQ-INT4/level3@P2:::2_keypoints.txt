- Proposes joint training of all decision-tree parameters via a single optimization using a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Establishes a novel link between decision-tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Main ideas are well-motivated, clearly explained, and the derivation of the upper bound is clean.  
- Empirical evaluation and presentation are insufficient and need substantial improvement.  
- Argument around line 216 in the empirical loss bound is confusing and likely incorrect.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points, suggesting problems with the objective or initialization.  
- Fixed-assignment heuristic seems suboptimal; alternatives like EM or CCCP should be considered.  
- The necessity and impact of the efficient loss-augmented inference are unclear given limited tree depth.  
- Brute-force inference is feasible at depth ≤16, so restricting search to a Hamming ball needs broader validation.  
- Terminology in the inference discussion is unclear—explicitly clarify what is meant by "leaves."  
- Missing baseline: greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization is not compared.  
- Limitation that tree depth must be pre-specified is not discussed or empirically evaluated; more depth-varying experiments needed.  
- Minor presentation fix: replace argmax with max in the loss bound expression.  
- Strengths: original research direction, clear exposition, and valuable proposed solution for joint tree training.  
- Originality is high due to the new algorithm and conceptual connection to structured prediction.  
- Significance is moderate because the algorithm’s impact is not thoroughly evaluated empirically.  
- Technical quality is mostly sound but marred by potential theoretical flaws and incomplete proofs.  
- Clarity is generally good but some parts of the presentation and experimental discussion require improvement.
