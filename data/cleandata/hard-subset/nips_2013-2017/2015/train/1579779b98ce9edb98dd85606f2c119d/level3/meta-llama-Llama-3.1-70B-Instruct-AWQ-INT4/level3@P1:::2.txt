This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning by jointly optimizing the split functions and leaf parameters via a single optimization with an upper-bound approximation for tractability. The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, allowing them to formulate a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss. This bound is then optimized using stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to find a locally optimal configuration of the split functions.

The paper is well-written, and the derivation is clean. However, there are some potential flaws in the theoretical reasoning. For example, the argument around line 216 is confusing, and an example shows that the claimed upper bound may not hold. Additionally, the proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is incomplete.

The experiments and their presentation also need improvement. Some leaves receive no data points, which may indicate issues with the objective or initialization. The fixed-assignment heuristic seems suboptimal and could be better motivated. Furthermore, the necessity and impact of the efficient loss-augmented inference are questioned, as brute-force is feasible for depth ≤ 16.

The paper is missing some important baselines, such as a comparison to a greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization. Additionally, the limitation that tree depth must be pre-specified should be discussed and evaluated more carefully with experiments varying depth.

Despite these shortcomings, the proposed solution is valuable, and investigating joint training for decision trees is an important and original research direction. The authors demonstrate that their non-greedy decision trees outperform greedy decision tree baselines in several classification benchmarks.
