- Proposes a non-greedy global optimization for oblique decision trees that jointly optimizes all split functions and leaf parameters.  
- Models the sequence of routing decisions as latent variables within a structured prediction framework.  
- Derives a continuous convex–concave upper bound surrogate loss and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Develops fast loss-augmented inference that reduces per-example gradient computation from exponential to polynomial in tree depth.  
- Connects to prior work (structured SVMs with latent variables, soft-split and look-ahead methods) and advances non-greedy tree learning.  
- Paper is very clear and the formulation is elegant and well-motivated.  
- The approach is original and significant, enabling principled extensions (e.g., different regularizers, kernelization).  
- Technical contributions are sound and make optimization of reasonably deep trees practical.  
- Empirical gains over greedy baselines are consistent but modest rather than dramatic.  
- Claims about reduced overfitting via norm regularization lack direct empirical validation.  
- Method requires a fixed, greedily initialized tree structure and does not address learning tree structure or sensitivity to initialization.  
- Suggests a hybrid post-processing: re-optimize last-layer splits greedily to potentially boost performance.  
- Recommends experiments (e.g., train–test gap vs. depth) to directly evaluate overfitting risk.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution that merits publication despite somewhat limited empirical improvements.
