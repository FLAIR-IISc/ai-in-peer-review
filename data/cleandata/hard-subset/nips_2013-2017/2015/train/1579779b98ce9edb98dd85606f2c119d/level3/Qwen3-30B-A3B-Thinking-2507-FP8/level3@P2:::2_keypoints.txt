- Proposes a novel structured-prediction-with-latent-variables formulation for joint optimization of decision tree splits and leaf parameters.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling SGD-based optimization.  
- Key technical contribution is an efficient loss-augmented inference procedure with O(d²p) complexity, making deep-tree training feasible.  
- Novel connection to structured prediction distinguishes this work from soft-split and margin-based prior methods.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over greedy baselines across multiple datasets.  
- Well-written and clearly presented, though some terminology (e.g., "leaves" in inference) is confusing.  
- Major theoretical flaw: the empirical loss bound (Equation (7)) is incomplete/incorrect in cases where Wx has elements near zero.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is incomplete and fails to handle Wx elements near zero.  
- Implementation issues: some leaves receive no data points, indicating problems with objective or initialization.  
- The fixed-assignment heuristic (SSGD) is inadequately justified and may be suboptimal compared to EM or CCCP.  
- Necessity of the efficient inference procedure is questionable given claimed depth limit (d ≤ 16) where brute-force is feasible.  
- Insufficient validation of the Hamming-ball approach beyond showing computational speedup.  
- Significant limitations reduce impact: tree depth must be pre-specified and no experiments varying depth are shown.  
- Missing baseline comparisons to greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits (e.g., OC1).  
- Specific requested fixes: address argmax vs. max in the bound, clarify "leaves" terminology, and provide runtime comparison for deeper trees (e.g., d=20).  
- Overall assessment: promising direction with clear empirical gains but undermined by a fundamental theoretical flaw and experimental/validation gaps; recommend rejection pending major revisions.
