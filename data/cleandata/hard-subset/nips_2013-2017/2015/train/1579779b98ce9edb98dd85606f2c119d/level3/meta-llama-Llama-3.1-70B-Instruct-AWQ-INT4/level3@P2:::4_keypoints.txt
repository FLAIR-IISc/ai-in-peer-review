- Proposes reframing decision tree optimization as a latent structured prediction problem with a differentiable upper-bound surrogate for a non-differentiable loss.  
- Main contribution: novel casting of tree optimization as structured prediction and development of efficient SGD-based optimization algorithms.  
- Introduces sparse gradient updates where each SGD step typically updates a single node, suggesting adaptive growth of tree complexity.  
- Raises conceptual issue: treating zero-weight nodes as always taking the right branch is problematic; nodes should be allowed to output and splitting/pruning behavior must be defined.  
- Regularization concern: L2 may produce dense, non-interpretable splits; unclear whether L1 would enforce useful sparsity without harming performance.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks detail on baselines and pruning strategies used for the greedy method.  
- Suggests adding comparisons to simple global baselines (e.g., multiple greedy trees on random subsets) for a fairer evaluation.  
- Inference descriptions (Sections 6.2 and 6.4) are unclear and should be summarized or made more explicit.  
- Title mentions forests but the paper neither develops nor evaluates forest extensions; extension to forests and expected performance are unclear.  
- Technical quality: paper is sound with well-defined optimization and supporting theoretical and experimental results.  
- Clarity: generally clear and concise, but some notation and terminology need better explanation.  
- Originality: high, leveraging structured prediction and latent variable ideas for a novel tree optimization framework.  
- Significance: moderate — promising approach but limited experimental validation leaves benefits and limitations not fully established.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address ambiguities and expand experimental comparisons.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, technically solid, well-written, and potentially practically useful.  
- Rejection arguments: limited experiments, unresolved issues about zero-weight handling and regularization, and missing forest treatment.
