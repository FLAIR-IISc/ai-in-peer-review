- Proposes a novel global optimization algorithm to jointly train all decision-tree parameters (splits and leaf posteriors).  
- Reformulates decision-tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a tractable convex-concave upper bound on the tree’s empirical loss.  
- Claims greater scalability and departure from greedy methods like CART and ID3.  
- Strength: original and significant direction, with a clean derivation of the surrogate objective.  
- Main theoretical concern: the empirical loss upper-bound argument (around l.216f) is confusing and may be incorrect.  
- Supplement proof issue: the proof for Proposition 1 appears incomplete.  
- Experimental gap: necessity of efficient loss-augmented inference is not demonstrated for tested depths (≤16).  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to standard greedy methods for non-axis-aligned splits.  
- Limitation: pre-specification of tree depth is a major shortcoming that needs discussion and evaluation.  
- Training anomaly: some leaves receive no data, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization.  
- Heuristic concern: the fixed-assignment (SSGD) heuristic seems ad-hoc and needs better motivation or theoretical connection (e.g., to EM or CCCP).  
- Reviewer willingness: would reconsider score if authors fix the upper-bound soundness issues and add requested baselines and runtime analysis.
