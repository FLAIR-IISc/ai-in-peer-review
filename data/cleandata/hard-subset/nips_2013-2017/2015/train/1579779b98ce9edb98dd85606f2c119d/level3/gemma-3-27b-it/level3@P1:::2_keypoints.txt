- Introduces joint global optimization of decision tree splits and leaves via a structured-prediction, latent-variable formulation.
- Main technical contribution: convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling optimization with SGD.
- Well-written, clear motivation, and insightful connection between decision trees and latent-variable structured prediction.
- Potential theoretical flaws: unclear reasoning around line 216f and a counterexample suggesting the claimed upper bound may not always hold.
- Proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement appears incomplete, raising validity concerns.
- Experiments use benchmarks and show some improvement over greedy baselines but are not fully convincing.
- Empirical issue: some leaves receive no training points, indicating problems with objective, initialization, or regularization.
- The Stable SGD (SSGD) heuristic to address empty leaves feels ad-hoc and lacks sufficient justification or comparison to alternatives like EM/CCCP.
- Claimed efficiency from “fast loss-augmented inference” is questionable given small tree depths explored; brute-force may suffice at these depths.
- Restricting inference search to a Hamming ball needs stronger validation; discussion of “leaves” during inference is unclear.
- Originality is strong: moving beyond greedy induction and relating to prior soft-split/global methods is valuable.
- Missing baseline: greedy training with non-axis-aligned splits without the paper’s special initialization should be compared.
- Pre-specifying tree depth is a limitation; depth effects should be varied and analyzed more systematically.
- Overall assessment: interesting and promising but theoretical issues and incomplete empirical validation lead reviewer to lean toward rejection pending rebuttal and stronger experiments.
