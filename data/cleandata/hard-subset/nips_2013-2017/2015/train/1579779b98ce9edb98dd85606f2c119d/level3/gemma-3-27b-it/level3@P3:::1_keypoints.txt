- Proposes an elegant, promising method for globally optimizing fixed-structure decision trees beyond greedy approaches.  
- Reduces decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables using a convex-concave upper bound; technically sound and well-presented.  
- Paper is clearly written, logically organized, reproducible, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Core contribution: a tractable surrogate objective enabling global optimization and a complexity reduction from O(2^d p) to O(d 2^p), enabling deeper trees.  
- Introduces a regularizer to mitigate overfitting and clearly articulates the limitations of greedy methods.  
- Experimental results are positive but modest; improvements over greedy baselines are not dramatic.  
- Comparison to CO2 (a greedy method using the same bound) shows limited gains across datasets.  
- The effect of the regularization parameter ν is analyzed, but interactions with tree depth and dataset size need deeper study.  
- Major concern: potential overfitting from global optimization; lacks a focused analysis of the generalization gap versus greedy baselines.  
- Suggests a post-processing/local-search refinement: drop the last split and re-optimize it greedily to correct suboptimal global decisions.  
- Discussion of scalability to very large datasets and very high-dimensional feature spaces is limited and should be expanded.  
- Overall: technically novel and impactful work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address generalization analysis and the proposed local refinement.
