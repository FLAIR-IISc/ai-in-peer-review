- Proposes decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables to enable global optimization of split functions.
- Develops a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss optimized via SGD with complexity O(d²p) for depth-d trees.
- Key innovation: principled connection between decision trees and structured prediction, a notable conceptual advance over prior non-greedy methods.
- Theoretical formulation, loss-augmented inference, and complexity analysis are sound and well-motivated.
- Empirical results are unconvincing: non-greedy method only clearly outperforms greedy baselines on a few datasets.
- Proposed method is substantially more computationally intensive (Figure 4) without consistent performance gains.
- Unclear reporting: Figure 2 does not state whether standard SGD or SSGD was used; “active” leaves are undefined.
- SSGD procedure is ambiguously described, lacking detail on assigning points to leaves and missing clear pseudo-code.
- Algorithm 1 (line 10) omits explanation of how Θ is projected onto the simplex, hindering reproducibility.
- Several minor technical issues: confusing feature phrasing, incorrect use of “argmax,” undefined acronym FSGD, and multiple typos.
- Supplementary material contains errors in sign notation and loss function formulation that need correction.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but experimental weaknesses, unclear methods, and typos undermine the paper’s practical significance; needs more experiments, clearer SSGD description, and fixes.
