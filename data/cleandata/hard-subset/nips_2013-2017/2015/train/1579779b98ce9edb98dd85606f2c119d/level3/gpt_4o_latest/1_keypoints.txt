- Introduces a novel global optimization approach for decision trees using a nonconvex relaxation that jointly optimizes all split functions and leaf parameters.  
- Establishes a theoretical connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Leverages a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss for optimization.  
- Uses stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to enable scalable training for deep trees.  
- Provides a detailed computational complexity analysis and a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces runtime.  
- Experimental results on standard benchmarks show the non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines in generalization.  
- Strength: addresses a long-standing limitation of greedy induction with a significant and novel theoretical contribution.  
- Strength: method is scalable, clearly written, and reproducible.  
- Weakness: empirical improvements are modest and depend on dataset choice.  
- Weakness: paper does not sufficiently investigate whether global optimization increases overfitting risk.  
- Weakness: did not apply the suggested post-hoc refinement of optimizing the last split/leaf in the standard way.  
- Weakness: benchmark selection is limited and may not demonstrate the method’s full potential on challenging real-world tasks.  
- Suggestion: run experiments varying regularization and tree depth to assess overfitting.  
- Suggestion: implement and evaluate greedy refinement of the last split and leaf after global optimization.  
- Suggestion: expand experimental evaluation to harder or more diverse real-world datasets.  
- Pro: novel, theoretically sound approach with potential to inspire follow-up work.  
- Con: practical benefits are unclear due to modest empirical gains.  
- Conclusion: meaningful contribution deserving publication; theoretical and methodological advances outweigh limited experimental impact.
