- Proposes a novel non-greedy method for training decision trees by jointly optimizing all split parameters and leaf predictions.
- Frames tree learning as structured prediction with the input-to-path mapping (left/right decisions) as latent variables.
- Derives a continuous, piecewise-smooth upper bound on the discontinuous empirical loss, inspired by latent structural SVMs.
- Surrogate objective is convex-concave and optimized using Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD).
- Provides an efficient "loss-augmented inference" algorithm that reduces naive exponential complexity in tree depth to polynomial, making the approach practical.
- Situates the work within prior NP-complete optimal-tree research and contrasts with expensive prior non-greedy methods and soft-split models.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing to axis-aligned, random oblique, OC1, and a greedy variant across multiple benchmarks.
- Strength: technically sound formulation and solid theoretical contribution in deriving the surrogate objective.
- Strength: Fast loss-augmented inference is a critical, practically enabling technical contribution.
- Strength: Original and insightful use of structured prediction techniques for hard-split decision trees.
- Strength: Paper is clear and well-organized with good motivation, figures, and algorithmic exposition.
- Strength: Framework is general and opens avenues (kernels, alternative regularization) for future work.
- Weakness: Empirical gains over greedy baselines are modest (typically ~1–2%), raising questions about practical payoff.
- Weakness: Paper only studies single trees and does not evaluate impact when used inside ensembles (e.g., Random Forests, boosting).
- Weakness: Stable SGD (SSGD) to prevent inactive leaves appears heuristic; sensitivity to greedy initialization is not fully analyzed.
- For acceptance: novel, principled formulation with an efficient optimization algorithm and consistent empirical improvements.
- Against acceptance: small practical accuracy gains and missing evaluation within ensemble settings.
- Overall: high-quality, novel, and practical contribution with modest empirical improvements but strong potential to inspire follow-up work.
