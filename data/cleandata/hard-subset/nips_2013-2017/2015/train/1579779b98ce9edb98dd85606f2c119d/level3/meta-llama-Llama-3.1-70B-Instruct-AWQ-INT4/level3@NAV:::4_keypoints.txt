- Reformulates decision-tree optimization as a latent structured prediction task.
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and a differentiable upper-bound approximation.
- Minimizes the upper bound with SGD, requiring repeated loss-augmented inference.
- Provides efficient algorithms for loss-augmented inference, enabling training of deep trees.
- Main contributions: casting trees as structured prediction and developing efficient optimization methods.
- Can handle arbitrary loss functions with computational complexity O(d p) (d=depth, p=features).
- Proposes joint-parameter regularization to discourage overfitting.
- Experimental section is limited and lacks sufficient detail on baselines and pruning for the greedy method.
- Title mentions forests but the paper neither addresses nor evaluates forests.
- Ambiguity around zero-weight nodes; authors propose allowing outputs at nodes and defining pruning/splitting clearly.
- Use of L2 regularization may produce dense, non-interpretable splits; testing L1 is suggested.
- Clarity issues in describing the inference algorithms (notably Sections 6.2 and 6.4).
- Notes potential extension to learn sparse split functions via sparsity-inducing regularization on W.
- Overall: a clever and efficient reformulation with promising contributions, but practical usefulness and experimental validation are currently unconvincing.
