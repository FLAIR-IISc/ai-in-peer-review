- Proposes joint global optimization of split parameters and leaf posteriors for decision trees instead of greedy training.  
- Connects decision tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Presents an efficient SGD optimization with O(d²p) per update and a loss-augmented inference algorithm.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over greedy baselines (including CO2) on multiple classification benchmarks.  
- Strength: novel and important research direction addressing limitations of greedy tree learning.  
- Strength: elegant theoretical formulation and clear derivation of the upper bound and inference algorithm.  
- Weakness: potential flaw in Proposition 1—claim that the bound tightens with larger W may be incorrect and the supplement proof appears incomplete.  
- Weakness: some leaves receive no data during training, suggesting objective/initialization issues and raising concerns about the fixed-assignment Stable SGD heuristic.  
- Weakness: fixed-assignment heuristic lacks motivation; more principled alternatives like EM or CCCP should be considered.  
- Weakness: necessity of the efficient loss-augmented inference and Hamming ball restriction is unclear given brute-force feasibility for moderate depths; broader validation needed.  
- Weakness: missing critical baseline—no comparison to greedy training with non-axis-aligned (oblique) splits without special initialization.  
- Weakness: tree depth must be pre-specified and paper lacks experiments varying depth to assess this practical limitation.  
- Minor issues: mathematical notation (argmax→max) and unclear leaf/inference terminology need clarification.  
- Recommendation: marginal rejection pending fixes; authors should correct the theoretical proof, add the missing baseline, run depth-varying experiments, and clarify terminology.  
- Final assessment scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 5/10; overall promising direction but currently lacking technical soundness and completeness.
