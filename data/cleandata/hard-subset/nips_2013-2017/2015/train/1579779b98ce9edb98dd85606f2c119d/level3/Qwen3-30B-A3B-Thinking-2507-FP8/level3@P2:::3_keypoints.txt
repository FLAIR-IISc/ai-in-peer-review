- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning method by framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that can be optimized with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Key technical advance: reduces gradient update complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling training of deeper trees.  
- Empirically outperforms greedy decision tree baselines across multiple classification benchmarks with consistent test accuracy improvements.  
- Demonstrates reduced overfitting and better generalization controlled by a regularization parameter ν.  
- Relates to prior non-greedy work, distinguishing itself from Bennett’s multi-linear programming and soft-split methods like Hierarchical Mixture of Experts.  
- Properly cites margin-based tree learning literature and acknowledges NP-completeness of optimal tree construction.  
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous derivations and thorough experimental validation.  
- Well-organized and clearly written; algorithms are described in sufficient implementation detail.  
- Lacks deeper intuition on why the non-greedy approach improves generalization.  
- Improvements over greedy methods are generally small, though consistent.  
- Limited exploration of regression tasks and some claims of novelty are narrowly tied to the specific upper-bound formulation.  
- Significance: addresses a long-standing limitation of greedy tree construction and offers extensible directions (sparse splits, kernels).  
- Recommendation: generally positive—technically strong and valuable contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance despite modest empirical gains.
