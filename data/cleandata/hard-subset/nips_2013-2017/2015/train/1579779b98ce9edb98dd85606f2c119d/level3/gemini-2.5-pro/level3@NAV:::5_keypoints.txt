- Proposes a global algorithm for learning oblique decision-tree splits by framing split selection as a latent-structure structured-prediction problem.
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective and regularizes split parameters with an L2 constraint.
- Optimizes the surrogate objective using stochastic gradient descent (SGD).
- Strength: elegant, novel, and principled formulation that enables global, non-greedy optimization and integrates regularization.
- Connects to prior work on overcoming greedy tree induction and to latent-variable structural SVMs.
- Weakness: empirical results are unconvincing—significant improvements over greedy baselines appear on only a few datasets despite higher computational cost.
- Reproducibility and clarity issues: “Stable SGD” is described vaguely with no pseudo-code, and the mechanism for assigning/updating points to leaves is unclear.
- Figure 2 is ambiguous (unclear which SGD variant is shown and what “active” leaf means).
- Practical details missing: projection of Θ onto the simplex in Algorithm 1 requires further explanation.
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution but insufficient empirical evidence and clarity to justify adoption; needs stronger experiments or clearer, more reproducible algorithmic descriptions.
