- Proposes a novel framework for non-greedy decision tree learning by jointly optimizing split functions under a global objective.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables to enable deep-tree learning.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes the surrogate objective using SGD for scalability.  
- Presents an efficient loss-augmented inference algorithm with time complexity O(d^2 p) intended for deep trees.  
- Introduces Stable SGD (SSGD), a conservative SGD variant that changes leaf assignments less frequently using a larger bound.  
- Shows empirical improvements over greedy decision tree baselines on several classification benchmarks.  
- Lacks comparison to greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization.  
- Contains potential flaws in theoretical reasoning and some claims are not fully supported by analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in dataset variety and depth and lacks comprehensive comparisons and deeper analysis.  
- Questions raised about the necessity and impact of the efficient loss-augmented inference given tree depth limited to 16.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized but some terminology and section connections need clarification (e.g., “leaves” in inference).  
- The work is original and proposes an important research direction with adequate related-work positioning.  
- Significance is moderate: advances the state of the art but impact is not fully explored due to limited evaluation.  
- Overall technically sound but incomplete; requires improvements in theory, experiments, and presentation to strengthen contribution.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, structured-prediction link, and demonstrated empirical gains; rejection arguments: theoretical issues, incomplete experiments, missing key comparisons, and questioned algorithmic necessity.
