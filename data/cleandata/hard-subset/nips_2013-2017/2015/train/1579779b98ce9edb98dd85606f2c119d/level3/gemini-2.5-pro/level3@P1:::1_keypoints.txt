- Presents a novel non-greedy framework for jointly optimizing all split functions and leaf parameters of decision trees under a global objective.  
- Reformulates tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, treating routing decisions as latent.  
- Derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss as a tractable surrogate objective.  
- Optimizes the surrogate with SGD and introduces a fast loss-augmented inference reducing per-step complexity from exponential to quadratic in depth.  
- Constrains split vector norms to control bound tightness and act as regularization.  
- Proposes Stable SGD (SSGD) and other practical algorithmic details to address optimization challenges.  
- Empirically shows consistent but modest improvements (≈1–2% on some datasets) over greedy baselines.  
- Paper is technically sound, complete, and builds on structured SVM/latent variable ideas applied elegantly to trees.  
- Exceptionally clear, well-written, and well-motivated with consistent notation and helpful figures.  
- Highly original connection between decision trees and structured prediction likely to inspire future work.  
- Potentially significant: addresses a fundamental limitation of decision trees and could influence non-greedy methods for ensembles.  
- Weakness: empirical gains are modest relative to increased training complexity and compared to ensemble methods.  
- Weakness: claims of reduced overfitting need more direct analysis (e.g., plots of generalization gap across depths and regularization).  
- Suggested improvement: hybrid approach—globally optimize then prune/re-optimize final-layer splits greedily to capture local structure.  
- Overall recommendation positive: novelty, elegance, and practicality outweigh modest empirical limitations, making the paper a strong candidate for acceptance.
