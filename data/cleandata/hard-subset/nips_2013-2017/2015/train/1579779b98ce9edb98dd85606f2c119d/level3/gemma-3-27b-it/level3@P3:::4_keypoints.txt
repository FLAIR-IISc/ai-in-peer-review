- Novel reformulation: casts global decision-tree optimization as structured prediction with sign vectors as latent variables.
- Optimization approach: introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss optimized via a differentiable upper bound and SGD.
- Computational efficiency: develops algorithms achieving O(d^2 p) complexity per gradient update.
- Conceptual contribution: departs from greedy induction and enables joint optimization of splits.
- Empirical property: observes sparse SGD updates that often affect only one node, suggesting adaptive/infinite-depth potential.
- Zero-weight ambiguity: paper implicitly defaults to the right branch for zero-weight nodes, which is arbitrary and unclear.
- Pruning recommendation: should allow outputs at zero-weight nodes and define principled pruning/splitting behavior.
- Regularization concern: L2 regularization likely yields dense, non-interpretable splits, undermining tree interpretability.
- Missing analysis: lacks experimental investigation of L1 vs L2 tradeoffs and impact on sparsity and performance.
- Baseline clarity: descriptions of baselines, especially the “naive/greedy” method and pruning, are insufficient for fair comparison.
- Additional baselines needed: should compare to simple global strategies (e.g., multiple greedy trees on random subsets).
- Inference clarity: Sections 6.2 and 6.4 need more concise and explicit descriptions of inference procedures.
- Title mismatch: title mentions “forests” but the paper evaluates only single trees, which is misleading.
- Initialization issue: zero-weight initialization concerns are important; exploring zero-to-nonzero growth is a promising future direction.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel insightful reformulation, efficient optimization, sparse-update property, potential to overcome greedy limits.
- Arguments against acceptance: zero-weight/pruning ambiguity, interpretability loss from L2, weak experimental validation and baselines, title misleading.
- Overall recommendation: promising ideas but insufficient practical validation and clarity; recommend rejection in current form with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
