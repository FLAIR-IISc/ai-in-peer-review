- Proposes a novel global optimization formulation for decision tree learning that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters.  
- Establishes a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Develops a convex–concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss as a surrogate objective.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and includes a thorough review of related work.  
- Uses stochastic gradient descent to optimize the surrogate, enabling scalable training on large datasets.  
- Experiments on benchmark datasets show the proposed non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines, but improvements are modest.  
- Experimental gains are somewhat disappointing in magnitude, limiting empirical impact.  
- Global optimization may increase risk of overfitting, though regularization (e.g., norm constraints) may help.  
- Suggested improvement: combine global optimization with standard local optimization for the final splits and leaf nodes.  
- Reviewer judges strengths to outweigh weaknesses and recommends publication due to the approach’s elegance and potential to spur further research.  
- Pros summarized: elegant approach, effective use of SGD, significant conceptual contribution; Cons summarized: limited empirical improvement and potential overfitting.
