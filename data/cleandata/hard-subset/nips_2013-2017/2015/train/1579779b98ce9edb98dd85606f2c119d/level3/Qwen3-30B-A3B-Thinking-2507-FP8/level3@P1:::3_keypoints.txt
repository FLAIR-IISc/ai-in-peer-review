- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree optimization by framing learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that can be optimized with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Main technical contribution: fast loss-augmented inference reducing complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p).  
- Hamming-ball formulation (eq.13) enables the O(d^2 p) inference algorithm, making deep tree learning feasible.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with a valid proof of the upper bound (eq.7) and thorough complexity analysis.  
- Experimental results show consistent improvements over greedy baselines, though improvements are generally modest.  
- Experiments are well-conducted with careful hyperparameter tuning and comparisons to axis-aligned trees, OC1, and random oblique trees.  
- Paper is clear and well-written; algorithm (Algorithm 1) is precise and results are reproducible with helpful supplementary material.  
- Originality is high: generalizes non-greedy tree optimization to arbitrary loss functions and links to structured prediction literature.  
- Computational efficiency is a significant advance over prior non-greedy approaches limited to shallow trees.  
- Significance is moderate: useful computationally, but modest accuracy gains may limit immediate practical adoption.  
- Lacks sufficient analysis of why improvements are modest and how to combine the method with other techniques for larger gains.  
- Practical deployment considerations (integration into existing ML pipelines) are under-discussed.  
- Regularization parameter ν helps control overfitting but requires better justification across different problem domains.
