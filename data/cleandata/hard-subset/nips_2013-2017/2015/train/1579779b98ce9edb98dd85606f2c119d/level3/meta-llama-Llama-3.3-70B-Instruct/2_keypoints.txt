- Proposes jointly optimizing all split functions and leaf parameters of decision trees using a global objective.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss for optimization.  
- Paper is well-written, significant, and addresses limitations of greedy tree induction methods.  
- Original and challenging contribution: joint training of trees via a latent-variable structured prediction formulation.  
- Technical concern: validity of the upper bound in Equation 7 is questioned and a counterexample is provided.  
- Empirical concern: some leaves receive no data points, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization.  
- Doubt about the necessity of efficient loss-augmented inference since greedy checking might be feasible.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard greedy method training non-axis-aligned split functions.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and should include additional experiments such as runtime comparisons.  
- Practical limitation: tree depth must be specified in advance and this constraint needs more discussion.  
- Overall assessment: method is significant but technical soundness and experimental validation require further scrutiny.
