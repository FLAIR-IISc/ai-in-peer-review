- Proposes non-greedy joint training of oblique decision trees optimizing split parameters and leaf posteriors under a single global objective.  
- Frames tree learning as latent structured prediction and introduces a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss optimized with (stochastic) gradient methods.  
- Addresses exponential loss-augmented inference by restricting search to a Hamming ball of radius 1, yielding O(d^2 p) per update.  
- Empirical results show gains over greedy axis-aligned and oblique baselines on several benchmarks.  
- Builds on OC1, CO2 depth-1 optimization, and latent/structural SVMs but differs by jointly training all nodes with a global surrogate.  
- Strength: Joint training of trees is an original, timely, and under-explored direction.  
- Strength: Clear derivation, strong connection to latent structured prediction, and good overall writing.  
- Strength: Practical algorithmic contributions (SGD/SSGD, per-example O(d) gradients, fast loss-augmented inference).  
- Strength: Potential for broader impact (e.g., kernelized splits, sparsity).  
- Weakness: Potential flaw in the empirical loss bound—argument is confusing, a counterexample may invalidate the claimed upper bound; needs a precise statement and complete proof.  
- Weakness: Proposition 1’s supplemental proof is incomplete; monotonicity under scaling requires rigorous conditions and tie-handling.  
- Minor fix: in the loss bound expression argmax should be max.  
- Weakness: Some leaves receive no assignments during training; the “stable SGD” fixed-assignment heuristic appears ad hoc and needs principled alternatives (EM/CCCP, annealing, entropy regularization) and analysis of convergence/stability.  
- Weakness: Necessity and impact of the fast Hamming-ball inference unclear—brute-force may be feasible for depths ≤16; request ablations comparing full vs. Hamming-ball inference and broader depth/size/runtime/accuracy validation.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous terminology—clarify what “leaves” means in the inference discussion (which leaf indices are reachable by flipping one bit).  
- Weakness: Experimental coverage incomplete—missing a baseline that greedily trains non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization, and lacking systematic variation of tree depth, runtime comparisons, and wall-clock training curves.  
- Pro for acceptance: Novel joint optimization with promising empirical results.  
- Pro for acceptance: Clean theoretical connection and practical training recipe.  
- Con for acceptance: Theoretical correctness of the bound and Proposition 1 remains unclear.  
- Con for acceptance: Experimental evaluation and baselines are incomplete and heuristic components are under-motivated.  
- Preliminary recommendation: Borderline—may change after a rebuttal that addresses the bound, Proposition 1, and additional experiments/ablations.
