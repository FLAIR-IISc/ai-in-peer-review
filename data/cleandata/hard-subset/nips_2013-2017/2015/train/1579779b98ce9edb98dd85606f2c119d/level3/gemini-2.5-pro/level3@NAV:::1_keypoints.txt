- Proposes a novel framework for non-greedy, global optimization of decision trees with fixed structure.  
- Reframes finding optimal oblique splits as a structured prediction problem with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective.  
- Optimizes the surrogate efficiently using stochastic gradient descent.  
- Offers a scalable, principled alternative to greedy algorithms (CART/ID3) and previous intractable global methods.  
- Paper is elegant, technically sound, clearly written, and likely to stimulate further research.  
- Linking decision tree training to structured prediction is an original and insightful contribution.  
- Introduces a "fast loss-augmented inference" procedure making optimization practical for deeper trees.  
- Experimental gains over greedy baselines are consistent but modest, raising practical-value questions.  
- Insufficient analysis of overfitting and whether the regularization effectively prevents overly complex fits.  
- Suggests a hybrid improvement: follow global optimization with local refinement (e.g., re-optimize last split and leaves).  
- Conclusion: despite modest empirical gains, the novelty and rigor justify publication and future work.
