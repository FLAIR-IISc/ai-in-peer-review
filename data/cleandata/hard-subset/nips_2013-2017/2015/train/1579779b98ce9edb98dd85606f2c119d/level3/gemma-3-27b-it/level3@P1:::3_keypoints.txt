- Novel formulation: frames decision tree induction as structured prediction with latent variables, enabling global (non-greedy) optimization.  
- Efficient gradient computation: develops O(d) gradient computation and fast loss-augmented inference enabling training of deeper trees.  
- Theoretical foundation: optimizes a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and analyzes regularization effects.  
- Empirical gains: consistently outperforms greedy baselines on benchmarks, though improvements are modest.  
- Clear presentation: paper is well-written with helpful algorithmic details (Alg 1).  
- Builds on prior work: correctly positions contribution relative to existing non-greedy, soft-split, and Mondrian approaches.  
- Modest improvements vs. complexity: gains may be too small to justify added computational cost in some cases.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance sensitive to regularization parameter ν, with risk of validation overfitting.  
- Limited baselines: lacks comparisons to more recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., gradient boosting, deep approaches).  
- Initialization dependence: requires greedy (CO2) initialization; impact of other initializations not explored.  
- Scalability concerns: practical application to very high-dimensional data is unclear due to O(d p) complexity.  
- Acceptance case: novel, theoretically sound, computationally improved, and empirically effective — justifies acceptance.  
- Rejection case: modest gains, hyperparameter and initialization issues, and incomplete evaluation could limit impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — add accuracy/efficiency trade-off discussion, robust tuning strategies, and expanded comparisons.
