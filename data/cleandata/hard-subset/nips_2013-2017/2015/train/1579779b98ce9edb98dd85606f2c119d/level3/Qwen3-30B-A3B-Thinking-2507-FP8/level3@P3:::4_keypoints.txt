- Reformulates decision tree optimization as latent structured prediction using sign vectors to enable global (non-greedy) optimization of splits.  
- Proposes a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a differentiable surrogate optimized with SGD.  
- Develops efficient loss-augmented inference algorithms with O(d²p) complexity, making training deep trees feasible.  
- Two main contributions: (1) connecting decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables, and (2) providing efficient optimization algorithms.  
- Empirical results claim consistent improvements over greedy baselines, with better generalization and reduced overfitting.  
- Strength: conceptually clean and clever reformulation bridging tree methods and structured prediction with solid theoretical justification.  
- Strength: technically sound, well-motivated inference algorithms with clear complexity analysis.  
- Strength: experimental results show consistent gains across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness: Title mentions "forests" but paper neither addresses nor evaluates forests, creating confusion.  
- Weakness: Ambiguity about handling zero-weight nodes (tie-breaking/pruning/splitting behavior not specified).  
- Weakness: L2 regularization likely produces dense, non-interpretable splits, contradicting interpretability motivation; L1 alternatives not tested.  
- Weakness: Experimental baselines lack detail (e.g., pruning strategies) and omit comparisons to simple global baselines like multiple greedy trees on random subsets.  
- Weakness: No analysis of whether the method prefers adding new nodes versus revising existing ones, leaving algorithmic behavior unclear.  
- Weakness: Claim of "sparse gradient updates" is insufficiently analyzed; potential to grow trees from zero weights is mentioned but not explored and zero-weight initialization remains a critical, untested case.  
- Overall: Technically interesting and novel but practically unconvincing due to interpretability concerns, zero-weight ambiguities, insufficient experimental validation, and title-content misalignment; recommendation: weak reject pending substantial revisions.
