- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree learning framework by casting optimization as latent structured prediction using sign vectors as latent variables.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and optimizes a differentiable upper bound via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Main contributions: reframing decision-tree optimization as structured prediction and developing efficient optimization algorithms.  
- Novel and interesting approach that links decision-tree learning to structured prediction, enabling use of efficient optimization methods.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of the computational complexity, aiding assessment of scalability.  
- Ambiguity about zero-weight nodes and the implied branch choice (right branch) is problematic and not explicitly addressed.  
- Experimental methodology lacks sufficient detail on baselines and pruning strategies, hindering fair comparison.  
- Use of L2 regularization may produce dense, non-interpretable splits; L1 regularization should be considered and its tradeoffs evaluated.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; should compare to additional simple global baselines (e.g., multiple greedy trees on random subsets).  
- Title mentions forests but the paper neither addresses nor evaluates forests, which is misleading.  
- Sections on inference (6.2 and 6.4) are dense and should be summarized or made more explicit for clarity.  
- Paper is well-written and original, technically sound, but practical usefulness and experimental validation are currently unconvincing.
