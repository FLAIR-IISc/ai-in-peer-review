- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree formulation that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters via a global objective.
- Establishes a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, yielding a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss.
- Derives a surrogate objective that can be efficiently optimized with stochastic gradient descent, enabling training of deeper trees on large datasets.
- Introduces a modification that substantially reduces computational complexity, making the method scalable to deeper/complex trees.
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and thorough grounding in related work (ID3, CART, CO2, and structured prediction literature).
- Experimental results show non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST) but only by modest margins.
- Authors acknowledge limited empirical gains and emphasize the method’s conceptual contribution over dramatic accuracy improvements.
- Suggests post-processing (dropping and re-optimizing the last split/leaf greedily) as a potential way to refine performance.
- Raises concern that global optimization may increase overfitting; current use of regularization (ν) is noted but insufficiently analyzed.
- Recommends deeper analysis of the trade-off between model complexity and generalization, and comparisons of tree complexity between methods.
- Originality is strong: novel combination of structured prediction principles with scalable non-greedy tree learning distinct from prior work.
- Significance is moderate but promising, with potential extensions (e.g., kernelized decision trees) and to spur further research.
- For acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, clarity, scalability, and research potential argue in favor.
- Against acceptance: modest empirical improvement, limited overfitting analysis, and unclear practical impact.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add overfitting analysis and explore the proposed post-processing refinement.
