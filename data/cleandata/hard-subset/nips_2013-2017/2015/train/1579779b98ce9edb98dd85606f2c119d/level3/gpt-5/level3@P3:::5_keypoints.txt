- Summary: Proposes a global non-greedy method for learning oblique decision trees by optimizing a convex–concave upper bound with latent binary splits, uses a Hamming-ball-1 restriction to reduce loss-augmented inference to O(d^2 p), and introduces SSGD to avoid inactive leaves; evaluated against axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique trees, and CO2.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends global/lookahead and margin-based tree methods and connects decision-tree training to latent/structured SVMs while preserving hard routing unlike HMEs.  
- Strength: Elegant latent reformulation casting splits as h = sgn(Wx), tying trees to structured prediction frameworks.  
- Strength: Scalability via the Hamming-ball-1 bound enabling tractable inference for deeper trees.  
- Strength: Principled regularization using per-row L2 constraints with analysis of scale effects.  
- Strength: SGD-friendly gradients and sparse row updates that are straightforward to implement.  
- Weakness: Empirical results are unconvincing—only modest, inconsistent gains over strong greedy baselines and substantially higher computational cost.  
- Weakness: SSGD is underspecified—missing precise definition/schedule for keeping assignments, making reproduction and attribution of gains unclear.  
- Weakness: Notation and clarity issues (missing figure refs, undefined acronyms, supplement errors) hinder reproducibility.  
- Quality: Theoretical bounds and complexity arguments are sound, but experiments do not convincingly justify practical advantage and runtimes lack fair cost–benefit reporting.  
- Clarity: Generally clear motivation, but key procedural details missing—SSGD pseudocode, definition of “active” leaves, and ambiguity in Algorithm 1’s projection step.  
- Originality: Novel latent-structured formulation for oblique trees and the specific Hamming-ball-1 upper bound for fast loss-augmented inference.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful if empirical accuracy–depth–runtime trade-offs improve; currently gains are modest and inconsistent.  
- Requests for reproducibility: Provide full SSGD pseudocode (initialization of a(x), freeze schedule, which parameters update, complexity), clarify Figure 2 (SGD vs SSGD, define active leaf), specify Θ projection/parameterization, and give Hamming-ball-1 inference pseudocode.  
- Experimental suggestions: Add accuracy vs. training-time plots and wall-clock times, stronger greedy oblique baselines with tuning, comparisons to small ensembles with comparable inference cost, and statistical significance plus active-leaf stats.  
- Minor issues/typos: Fix argmax/max usage in Eqs. (7)–(9), reference Figure 1 where f(·) is introduced, correct corrupted sentences/undefined FSGD, and address supplement sign/notation errors.  
- Pros: Principled and elegant formulation, efficient Hamming-ball-1 inference, and practical SGD training.  
- Cons: Limited empirical improvements per cost, underspecified SSGD and implementation details, and reproducibility hampered by clarity issues.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject—method is promising conceptually but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer exposition to be compelling.
