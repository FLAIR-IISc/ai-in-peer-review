Summary: The paper proposes a non-greedy training procedure for decision trees that jointly optimizes oblique split parameters and leaf posteriors under a single global objective. The approach links tree learning to latent structured prediction, introducing a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizing it with (stochastic) gradient methods. To address the exponential complexity of loss-augmented inference, the paper proposes restricting search to a Hamming ball of radius 1 around the current split decisions, yielding O(d^2 p) per update. Empirically, the method is compared against greedy axis-aligned and oblique baselines and shows gains on several benchmarks. The work builds on OC1-style oblique trees, CO2’s depth-1 optimization, and latent/structural SVMs, while differing by jointly training all nodes with a global surrogate.

Strengths:
- Original and timely direction: joint training of trees is important and under-explored.
- Clear derivation and connection to latent structured prediction; well-written overall.
- Practical algorithmic contributions (SGD/SSGD, per-example O(d) gradients, fast loss-augmented inference).
- Potential for impact beyond trees (e.g., kernelized splits, sparsity).

Weaknesses and questions (Quality/Clarity/Significance):
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss bound: the argument around l.216f is confusing, and a simple counterexample suggests the claimed upper bound might not always hold; please provide a precise statement and complete proof. Proposition 1’s supplement proof is incomplete; the monotonicity with scaling needs rigorous conditions (and handling ties).
- Minor but important: in the loss bound expression, argmax should be max.
- Some leaves receive no assignments during training; this suggests issues with the objective/initialization. The fixed-assignment “stable SGD” heuristic seems ad hoc; please justify or replace with a principled method (e.g., EM/CCCP, annealing, entropy regularization) and analyze convergence/stability.
- Necessity and impact of the “fast” loss-augmented inference are unclear: for depths ≤16, brute-force over all leaves is feasible. Please provide broader validation (depth/size/runtime/accuracy trade-offs) and ablations comparing full vs. Hamming-ball inference.
- Terminology: clarify what “leaves” means in the inference discussion (which leaf indices are reachable when flipping one bit off the current path).
- Experimental coverage: missing a baseline that greedily trains non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization. Please also vary tree depth systematically (since depth must be pre-specified) and report runtime comparisons and wall-clock training curves.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel joint optimization for trees with promising empirical results.
- Clean connection to latent structured prediction and practical training recipe.

Cons for acceptance:
- Theoretical correctness of the bound and Prop. 1 unclear.
- Experimental evaluation and baselines incomplete; heuristic components under-motivated.

Preliminary recommendation: borderline; I may adjust after a clear rebuttal addressing the bound, Prop. 1, and additional experiments/ablations.
