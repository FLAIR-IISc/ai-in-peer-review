- Proposes non-greedy global training for fixed-structure oblique decision trees by modeling routing as latent binary variables and optimizing a convex–concave surrogate.  
- Surrogate resembles margin-rescaled latent structural SVMs, yielding a nonconvex relaxation optimized via SGD/SSGD.  
- Derives exact and fast loss-augmented inference; fast variant restricts to a Hamming-1 neighborhood with O(d^2 p) per-update complexity.  
- Applies per-row norm constraints on W to regularize the bound and empirically mitigate overfitting; bound tightness grows with W scale (Prop.1).  
- Evaluates on multiclass LibSVM benchmarks against CART, OC1, random oblique trees, and a greedy depth-1 bound-optimized baseline (CO2).  
- Conceptually elegant and clearly written; latent-variable formulation and bound are well explained.  
- Strong technical contribution: careful derivation, analysis of scaling behavior, and a scalable loss-augmented inference routine.  
- Practical optimization design: simple SGD/SSGD with projections and clear complexity analysis.  
- Framework is general (supports arbitrary losses) and invites extensions like sparse/structured regularization, kernels, and regression.  
- Empirical gains are modest in places and do not include comparisons to strong modern baselines such as boosted trees.  
- Limited analysis of generalization: training/test gaps, robustness to noise, and capacity-vs-overfitting are not systematically quantified.  
- Nonconvexity concerns remain: limited study of sensitivity to initialization, restarts, and stability across runs.  
- Trade-offs of the Hamming-1 fast inference (bound looseness vs accuracy) are not deeply examined.  
- Technically sound with correct connections to latent structural SVMs; proof details appropriately deferred to the supplement.  
- Clarity is excellent: notation, algorithms, complexity discussion, and figures (e.g., ν vs pruning) are crisp.  
- Originality is high: first end-to-end non-greedy training of fixed-depth hard-split trees using a tractable surrogate and scalable stochastic optimization.  
- Significance is moderate-to-high: likely to spur follow-up work despite mixed empirical margins.  
- Suggested improvement: post-optimization path-wise local refinement of bottom splits to recover performance lost by surrogate looseness.  
- Suggested experiments: systematic overfitting assessment (train/val curves, label-noise tests), ablations (SGD vs SSGD, ν, η, Hamming-1 vs exact), and multiple initializations.  
- Suggested baselines/scope: include boosted trees and regression results to better contextualize accuracy and generality.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant, novel, technically solid framework with potential for follow-up work.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited empirical advantage over strong baselines and insufficient analysis of generalization and optimization stability.  
- Recommendation: Accept (poster) — substantive contribution and likely to stimulate further research despite empirical limitations.  
- Score justification: balanced by clarity, technical quality, and potential impact versus modest empirical gains and limited overfitting analysis.
