- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree method that jointly optimizes all split functions and leaf parameters, linking tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Provides a clear, concise related-work overview that highlights limitations of prior greedy methods and the contribution of the new approach.  
- Shows the method can handle any general loss while retaining O(dp) inference complexity inherent to tree structure.  
- Introduces a surrogate objective that enables regularization of joint tree parameters to discourage overfitting.  
- Presents a theoretically justified formulation including an upper bound on empirical loss and an SGD-based optimization algorithm with a norm regularizer on W.  
- Experimental results show non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines on several benchmarks, though improvements are not consistently large.  
- Suggests a pragmatic post-processing step: drop and re-optimize the last split and leaf with standard methods to potentially boost performance.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and meets criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; claims are supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Major concern: potential for increased overfitting under global optimization and insufficient evaluation/mitigation of this issue.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — address overfitting concerns and add further experiments; overall rating 8/10.
