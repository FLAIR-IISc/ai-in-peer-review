- Presents a novel non-greedy algorithm that formulates joint optimization of oblique tree splits and leaf parameters as a single global objective.  
- Casts decision-tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, treating example paths as latent.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Key technical contribution: a fast loss-augmented inference reducing per-step complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), enabling deeper trees.  
- Strong originality: a principled global optimization framework that departs from greedy node-by-node induction.  
- Clean derivation of the surrogate objective but several technical and empirical weaknesses weaken the submission.  
- Major theoretical concern: the validity of the main upper bound (Eq. 7) is not convincingly proven and may admit counterexamples.  
- Incomplete proofs: justification for regularization on W (Proposition 1) is insufficient in the supplement.  
- Optimization issues: training can produce inactive leaves, and the proposed Stable SGD (SSGD) is presented as an ad-hoc fix without strong theoretical grounding.  
- Suggest linking SSGD to established alternating schemes (e.g., EM or CCCP) to better motivate the approach.  
- Fast inference trade-off is under-explored: restricting search to a Hamming ball of radius 1 is strong and needs ablation on accuracy vs speed.  
- Experimental gaps: missing a strong greedy oblique-split baseline (e.g., OC1) and no sensitivity analysis for the required pre-specified tree depth.  
- Minor clarity issues: notation in loss-bound expressions (use max instead of argmax for values) and ambiguous "leaves" terminology in loss-augmented inference.  
- Pros for acceptance: tackles an important problem, offers original formulation, and shows promising empirical gains over some baselines.  
- Cons for rejection: potential theoretical flaws, heuristic optimization fixes, insufficient validation of the fast approximation, and incomplete experimental comparisons.  
- Recommendation: revise and resubmit after addressing theoretical proofs, better motivation/analysis of SSGD, ablation of fast inference, and inclusion of strong greedy oblique baselines and depth sensitivity analysis.
