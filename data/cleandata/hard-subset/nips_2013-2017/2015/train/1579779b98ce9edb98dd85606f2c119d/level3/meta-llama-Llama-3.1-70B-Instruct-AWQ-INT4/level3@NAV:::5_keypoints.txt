- Proposes a novel algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions via optimizing a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.
- Establishes a connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.
- Uses stochastic gradient descent (SGD) and L2 regularization on W to enable scalable training and reduce overfitting.
- Paper is well-written with a thorough related-work review and clear explanation of the approach.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on several benchmark datasets, but results are mixed across datasets.
- Reported performance improvements over greedy baselines are not consistently significant.
- Proposed method has higher computational cost compared to greedy decision tree methods.
- Description of the stable SGD algorithm is unclear and lacks implementation details.
- Missing details on how data points are assigned to leaves and absence of pseudo-code for key procedures.
- Figure captions are unclear; unclear whether results use SGD or stable SGD and what "active" leaf means.
- Overall assessment: interesting approach but experimental evidence is not yet convincing—acceptance: borderline.
- Recommendations: provide detailed stable SGD description and pseudo-code, clarify figure captions and define "active" leaf, strengthen experimental validation, and reduce computational cost.
