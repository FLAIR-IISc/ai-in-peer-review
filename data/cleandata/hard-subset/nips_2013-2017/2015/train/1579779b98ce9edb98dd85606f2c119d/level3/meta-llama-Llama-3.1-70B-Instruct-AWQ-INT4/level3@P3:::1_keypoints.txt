- Proposes an elegant, efficient non-greedy method to optimize decision trees with fixed structure.  
- Establishes a novel connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it via stochastic gradient descent to find local optima.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review and clearly explains contributions and advantages.  
- Technical details are sound and supported by a detailed computational-complexity analysis.  
- Experimental results are disappointing: non-greedy trees do not significantly outperform greedy baselines.  
- Authors provide thoughtful analysis of experimental results and suggest ways to improve performance.  
- Paper lacks a full evaluation of the impact of global optimization on overfitting.  
- Insufficient analysis of how norm regularization affects overfitting and model fit.  
- Suggested practical improvement: drop and re-optimize the last split and its leaf using standard methods after global optimization.  
- Overall contribution is significant and likely to spur further research in decision-tree optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, new theoretical link, clear presentation, and solid complexity analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical gains and incomplete analysis of overfitting/regularization effects.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address overfitting and regularization analysis (Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10).
