- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm for optimizing decision trees with linear split functions via structured prediction with latent variables.
- Uses a convex-concave upper bound of empirical loss and SGD to jointly optimize split functions and leaf parameters.
- The paper is well-motivated and connects decision tree induction with structured prediction in an elegant way.
- Significant computational improvement claimed: complexity reduced from O(2^d p) to O(d 2^p) (review notes reduction from O(2dp) to O(d2p) — check exact notation).
- Introduces a regularized surrogate objective to mitigate overfitting for deeper trees.
- Identifies inactive-leaf issues and proposes a Stable SGD (SSGD) algorithm, though its description is opaque.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and results are inconclusive; gains over greedy baselines are inconsistent and often small.
- The non-greedy method has higher computational cost, questioning practical benefits.
- Figure 2 is unclear about whether results use SGD or SSGD and what constitutes an “active” leaf.
- Experiments should compare against stronger baselines (e.g., Random Forests, advanced ensemble techniques).
- Algorithm 1 needs detail on projecting leaf parameters Θ onto the simplex to enforce row-sum constraints.
- Several presentation issues: confusing phrasing (lines 407–408), repeated use of “argmax” should be “max” in Eqs. (7)–(9), and Figure 1 is missing.
- Multiple typos and undefined terms (lines 141, 238, 301, 360 FSGD undefined, 404) that require correction.
- Supplementary material errors: sign notation on line 29 should be “=” not “∈”, and loss should use Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)) as per main text.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound formulation; enables training deeper trees via complexity reduction; principled regularization.
- Arguments against acceptance: unconvincing empirical results; unclear SSGD description; multiple minor errors and inconsistencies.
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but experimental weaknesses and presentation issues warrant a major revision before strong acceptance.
