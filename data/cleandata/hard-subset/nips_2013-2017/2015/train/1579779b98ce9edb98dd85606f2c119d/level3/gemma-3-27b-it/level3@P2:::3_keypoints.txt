- Proposes a novel formulation of decision-tree training as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and uses SGD for optimization.  
- Claims improved scalability (reduced complexity to O(d^2 p)) enabling training of deeper trees.  
- Empirical results show consistent but modest improvements over greedy baselines on benchmarks.  
- Builds on prior decision-tree and structured-prediction literature and motivates non-greedy approaches by NP-completeness of optimal trees.  
- Theoretical justification via the convex-concave bound gives a solid foundation for the method.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Main weakness: accuracy gains are relatively small, questioning practical benefit versus added complexity.  
- Method depends on greedy initialization (CO2), raising concerns about sensitivity to initialization and local optima.  
- Performance is sensitive to the regularization hyperparameter ν, requiring careful tuning without extensive guidance.  
- Limited experimental comparison to stronger modern baselines (e.g., extremely randomized trees, gradient-boosted trees).  
- Lacks deeper discussion of practical trade-offs (accuracy vs. computation vs. interpretability).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, scalable, and consistently outperforms greedy baselines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest improvements, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited baseline comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution worth accepting if authors address stronger baseline comparisons and provide more practical/hyperparameter guidance; recommendation: Accept.
