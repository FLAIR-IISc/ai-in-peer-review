- Paper proposes a non-greedy decision-tree learning method by casting tree optimization as structured prediction with latent variables and deriving a convex-concave upper bound for SGD optimization.  
- Key insight: treat tree navigation as latent-variable inference where leaf assignment is the argmax of a score, enabling joint optimization of oblique splits.  
- Theoretical contributions: novel connection to structured prediction, a convex-concave surrogate loss, and a rigorous complexity analysis (O(d²p) per update) avoiding exponential enumeration.  
- Introduces Stable SGD (SSGD) that conservatively updates leaf assignments to improve training stability.  
- Method is general (works with classification/regression and arbitrary losses) and extends prior non-greedy work to deeper trees.  
- Strength: formulation is elegant, technically sound, and principled, with solid theoretical motivation.  
- Strength: complexity analysis and derivation of the surrogate objective are rigorous.  
- Experimental weakness: empirical evidence is insufficient — gains are marginal or dataset-dependent (e.g., <1% on Protein), not consistently outperforming greedy methods.  
- Ambiguity in experiments: unclear whether plots use standard SGD or SSGD, and "active leaves" is undefined, undermining interpretability.  
- Computational concern: O(d²p) overhead is not justified by consistent accuracy gains; greedy methods can match accuracy with much lower training time on some datasets (e.g., SensIT).  
- Reproducibility/clarity issues: SSGD lacks algorithmic detail (initialization, update procedure, how long assignments are fixed); pseudo-code is needed.  
- Implementation ambiguities: Algorithm 1’s projection of Θ onto the simplex is unspecified, "FSGD" is undefined, Figure 1 is never referenced, and several typos/notation errors (argmax vs max, wrong symbols) appear.  
- Significance: strong theoretical contribution but limited practical impact as presented; lacks analysis of when global optimization helps or fails (e.g., high-dimensional regimes).  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on major revisions — clarify and pseudo-code SSGD and "active leaves," report SGD vs SSGD consistently, provide runtime vs accuracy comparisons across datasets, and fix notation/typos.
