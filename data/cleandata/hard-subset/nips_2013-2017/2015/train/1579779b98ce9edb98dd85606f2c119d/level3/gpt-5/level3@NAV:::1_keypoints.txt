- Proposal: non-greedy, globally optimized training for fixed-topology decision trees by casting oblique split learning as a latent structured prediction problem.  
- Technical approach: derive a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss to enable joint stochastic optimization of all splits and leaf parameters.  
- Computational contribution: fast loss-augmented inference restricted to a Hamming ball around current decisions, reducing update cost to O(d^2 p) and enabling deep trees.  
- Positioning: situates work relative to global tree optimization (Bennett), latent/structural SVMs, oblique trees (OC1), CO2, soft trees/HME, and online forests.  
- Quality: formulation and surrogate are technically sound and well-motivated, with clear pseudocode and complexity analysis; optimization is nonconvex but well-specified.  
- Empirical limitation: gains over strong greedy baselines are modest on some datasets and not uniformly compelling.  
- Generalization concern: paper lacks a thorough overfitting analysis and ablation of surrogate tightness/regularization.  
- Clarity: writing is elegant and clear, with explicit notation, assumptions, and algorithm explanations.  
- Originality: novel link between tree optimization and latent structured prediction and a distinctive fast inference method different from CO2.  
- Significance: addresses an important limitation of greedy trees and is likely to spur follow-up work on better surrogates, hybrid training, and scaling.  
- Suggested improvement 1: after global optimization, drop the last level and re-optimize final split and leaf parameters with greedy/CART-style refitting.  
- Suggested improvement 2: provide a thorough overfitting analysis (train–validation gaps vs depth/ν, cost-complexity pruning, label-noise stability, effect of global flexibility).  
- Suggested improvement 3: broaden empirical comparisons to include soft trees/HME and modern oblique/boosted baselines and test per-tree gains in small ensembles.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear, principled formulation; novel connection to latent structured prediction; solid algorithmic (fast inference) contribution; addresses a long-standing limitation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: mixed empirical improvements, limited overfitting analysis, nonconvex surrogate’s practical advantage unclear, and fixed-topology assumption limits topology learning/pruning.
