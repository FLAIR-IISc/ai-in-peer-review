- Proposes a novel formulation of decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling optimization by stochastic gradient descent.
- Interprets tree navigation (path selection) as a latent-variable inference problem.
- Presents an efficient algorithm with O(d²p) complexity per gradient update, making deep non-greedy trees feasible.
- Improves computational efficiency from previous exponential-cost non-greedy methods.
- Builds on prior work (Bennett, Yu et al.) while distinguishing itself from soft-split models and CO2.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized.
- Theoretical foundation is sound with well-justified approximations and complexity analysis.
- Algorithmic implementation is elegant; fast loss-augmented inference is a notable technical contribution.
- Authors explicitly address limitations, including non-convexity and regularization trade-offs.
- Experimental improvements over greedy baselines are modest (typically 1–3% accuracy gains).
- Empirical gains are inconsistent across datasets.
- Paper lacks a thorough analysis of overfitting behavior (e.g., training vs. test error gaps).
- No rigorous comparison of overfitting metrics across methods is provided.
- Experimental design is appropriate, but results do not show dramatic improvement over existing methods.
- Clarity is excellent; algorithm descriptions are reproducible for experts.
- Originality is strong: novel connection to structured prediction and an efficient global optimization technique.
- Practical significance is limited by modest empirical gains, though the work may inspire future research on global tree optimization.
- Pros for acceptance: elegant theoretical framework, novel efficient algorithm, clear presentation, addresses a core limitation, potential to spur follow-up work.
- Cons for acceptance: modest empirical gains, insufficient overfitting analysis, sensitivity to regularization tuning, limited to fixed tree structures.
- Suggested improvement: after global optimization, drop and re-optimize the last split/leaf to potentially exploit tree capacity and reduce inactive leaves.
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and publishable; recommend acceptance despite underwhelming empirical gains.
