- Proposes a novel decision tree optimization formulation as a latent structured prediction problem.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and approximates it with a differentiable upper bound optimized via SGD.  
- Optimization requires loss-augmented inference; authors provide efficient algorithms making this computationally feasible.  
- Main contributions: (1) casting decision tree learning as structured prediction, (2) developing efficient optimization algorithms.  
- Enables non-greedy, joint optimization of split functions, potentially improving generalization over traditional greedy methods.  
- Supports sparse gradient updates where each SGD step may only change one node, enabling adaptive growth from zero weights.  
- Ambiguity about zero-weight node behavior: unclear if method mimics greedy strategies or prefers adding nodes over revising existing ones.  
- Concern that L2 regularization may yield dense, non-interpretable splits, harming practical interpretability.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: baseline and pruning details are unclear and comparisons to simple global strategies are missing.  
- Clarity issues: inference descriptions (Sections 6.2 and 6.4) need summarization and the title mentions forests without addressing/evaluating them.  
- Overall verdict: clever and efficient reformulation but practical usefulness and experimental validation are currently unconvincing.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
