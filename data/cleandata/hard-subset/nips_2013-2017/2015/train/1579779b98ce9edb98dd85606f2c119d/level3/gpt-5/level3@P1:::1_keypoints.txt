- Proposes a non-greedy training method for oblique decision trees that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters under a global objective.
- Recasts tree routing as latent binary variables and upper-bounds empirical loss with a convex–concave surrogate inspired by structured prediction with latent variables.
- Derives two bounds: a full loss-augmented inference objective and a fast Hamming-ball variant restricting to a neighborhood around current routing.
- Fast variant enables per-example optimization in O(d^2 p), making deeper trees computationally feasible.
- Training uses (projected) stochastic gradient descent with row-wise norm constraints on W and a “Stable SGD” (SSGD) to avoid inactive leaves.
- Initialization via standard greedy trees and sparse SGD updates make the method practical.
- Empirically shows consistent improvements over greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique) and the greedy CO2 variant on several LibSVM multiclass datasets.
- Connects decision-tree learning to structured prediction (latent-variable view and loss-augmented inference) and scales beyond prior global/margin-based tree methods.
- Contrasts with probabilistic soft-split methods that trade off tree-time inference cost.
- Strength: conceptual clarity—a clean latent-variable formulation with principled regularization via per-node norm constraints.
- Strength: algorithmic contribution—efficient Hamming-1 loss-augmented inference with clear computational complexity.
- Strength: practicality—SGD/SSGD, sensible initialization, and empirical runtime analysis for the fast bound.
- Weakness: empirical gains are modest and strongest modern baselines (e.g., boosted trees, extremely randomized trees) are not included.
- Weakness: assumes fixed full binary structure and depends on greedy initialization; sensitivity to initialization is not analyzed.
- Weakness: limited overfitting/generalization analysis—evidence for reduced overfitting is primarily qualitative.
- Weakness: surrogate tightness trade-off—fast Hamming-1 bound may limit global coordination; ablation is brief.
- Quality: technical development is sound and well-motivated, but lacks deeper analysis of surrogate tightness vs. optimization dynamics and the role of ν.
- Clarity: paper is elegant and clear; minor fixes suggested (small running example, summarize SSGD in pseudocode).
- Originality: novel latent-structured view and efficient Hamming-ball loss-augmented inference in the decision-tree context.
- Significance: scalable non-greedy training for trees is impactful and likely to spur follow-on work even if single-tree gains are moderate.
- Suggested improvements: add stronger baselines (boosting, extremely randomized trees), overfitting analyses (train/val/test curves, active leaves, calibration), and ablations (full vs Hamming-1, SSGD vs SGD, initialization sensitivity).
- Suggested practical tweaks: post-process by collapsing/re-optimizing deepest splits, report wall-clock/memory for larger trees, clarify Θ regularization, and discuss growing/pruning structure as future work.
- Pros for acceptance: principled, efficient framework with solid theoretical connections and demonstrated improvements over greedy baselines.
- Cons against acceptance: modest empirical gains, insufficient overfitting/generalization analysis, and reliance on fixed structure/initialization.
- Overall assessment: well-executed and clearly presented contribution likely to be influential; leans toward acceptance contingent on strengthening empirical and overfitting analyses.
