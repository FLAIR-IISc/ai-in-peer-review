- Novel formulation: casts decision tree learning as a structured prediction problem with latent variables, enabling principled global optimization.  
- Theoretical contribution: derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss providing a solid mathematical foundation.  
- Computational contribution: introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm with O(d^2 p) complexity, enabling training of deeper trees.  
- Regularization: uses L2 regularization motivated by the properties of the upper bound to control overfitting.  
- Stable SGD unclear: description of the Stable SGD variant lacks detail on how data-to-leaf assignments are managed and updated; pseudocode is needed.  
- Limited empirical gains: experiments show inconsistent and often marginal improvements over greedy baselines, sometimes performing worse, raising questions about practical benefit.  
- Figure 2 ambiguity: does not specify whether reported results use SGD or Stable SGD and fails to define an “active” leaf.  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguity: Line 10 (Θ update) omits how Θ is projected onto the simplex to ensure valid row-wise probability distributions.  
- Minor errors and inconsistencies: confusing phrasing, inconsistent use of argmax/max, missing reference to Figure 1, undefined acronym FSGD, typographical and supplementary-material errors.  
- Quality assessment: mathematically and algorithmically sound, but practical impact is weakened by empirical and clarity issues.  
- Clarity assessment: generally well-written but requires clearer exposition of Stable SGD, Algorithm 1 details, and tighter notation.  
- Originality: novel application of structured prediction to decision trees, though built on existing methods—overall moderate originality.  
- Significance: theoretical advances are valuable, but limited empirical improvement reduces overall significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: elegant, theoretically sound formulation with potential for future extensions and ties to structured prediction.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest empirical gains, unclear Stable SGD, missing implementation details, and multiple minor errors.  
- Overall recommendation: reject with encouragement to improve empirical validation, clarify Stable SGD and implementation details, and fix technical issues for resubmission.
