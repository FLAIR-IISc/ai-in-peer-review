- Novel reformulation: treats decision-tree learning as a latent structured prediction problem using sign vectors and a differentiable upper-bound on loss.  
- Computational claim: theoretical reduction to O(d) complexity enables feasible SGD training of deeper trees.  
- Builds on prior structured-prediction work and introduces a surrogate objective plus efficient loss-augmented inference algorithms.  
- Presentation: paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Experimental shortcomings: insufficient detail on baselines, notably pruning strategies for the greedy method.  
- Missing comparisons to simple global baselines (e.g., multiple greedy trees trained on random subsets).  
- Regularization concerns: use of L2 may harm interpretability; need systematic L1 vs L2 investigation.  
- Zero-weight node assumption is unclear/problematic—paper should explicitly define pruning/splitting behavior at such nodes.  
- Initialization issue: potential dynamics from zero-weight initialization require further analysis (raised in post-rebuttal).  
- Title mismatch: mentions “forests” but contains no experiments or treatment of forests.  
- Clarity issues in Sections 6.2 and 6.4 regarding inference details.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel and efficient approach addressing limitations of greedy methods, with potential for adaptive, deep trees.  
- Cons for acceptance: weak experimental validation, practical interpretability and zero-weight handling concerns, and unsupported forest claim.  
- Overall verdict: theoretically promising but not empirically convincing; recommend rejection with encouragement to address concerns.
