- Proposes a novel method to learn decision trees by optimizing an upper bound on empirical loss with L2 regularization using SGD.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables, enabling transfer of techniques.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with an elegant and well-motivated problem formulation.  
- Main contribution: a global algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions.  
- Claims that global optimization can yield better generalization than traditional greedy, node-wise methods.  
- Empirical results are only convincing on a few datasets and do not strongly support broad claims.  
- Global optimization approach has higher computational cost than greedy methods, raising scalability concerns.  
- Description of the Stable SGD algorithm lacks sufficient detail, especially how data points are assigned and reassigned to leaves.  
- Explanation of parameter projection onto the simplex is insufficiently detailed in practice.  
- Minor writing and formatting issues, including corrupted/misspelled sentences and an undefined acronym (FSGD).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach and a significant global algorithm contribution with some empirical gains.  
- Arguments for rejection: weak empirical support, higher computational cost, and lack of clarity in key algorithmic details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, request additional empirical results and clarifications on Stable SGD and simplex projection.
