- Novel theoretical link between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Use of a convex-concave upper bound plus L2 regularization to mitigate overfitting.  
- Reduced gradient-update complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), improving feasibility for deeper trees.  
- Empirical evaluation on multiple benchmarks shows some generalization gains over greedy baselines.  
- Empirical gains are marginal and may not justify the substantially higher computational cost.  
- Description of stable SGD (SSGD) and how data points are assigned to leaves is insufficient for reproducibility.  
- Figure 2 is ambiguous about SGD vs. SSGD and the definition of “active” leaves.  
- Projection of Θ onto a simplex in Algorithm 1 is poorly explained and needs implementation details.  
- Writing issues (typos, unclear phrasing, inconsistent equations/terminology) reduce readability.  
- Supplementary material contains typographical errors and unclear notation.  
- Requested fixes: provide pseudocode for SSGD, elaborate the projection step, clarify figures/“active” leaves, and improve writing and supplementary material.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical perspective and a principled optimization framework that could inspire extensions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical benefits, high computational cost, and clarity/reproducibility concerns.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution but borderline for acceptance pending improvements to practical impact and clarity.
