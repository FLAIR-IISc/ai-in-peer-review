- Proposes a novel, non-greedy global optimization for decision trees by framing split learning as structured prediction with latent m-bit decision vectors.
- Replaces discontinuous empirical loss with a continuous (non-convex) convex-concave surrogate inspired by latent structural SVMs and optimizes it with SGD.
- Main computational challenge: exponential number of latent decision vectors makes loss-augmented inference intractable naively.
- Technical contributions: an exact loss-augmented inference algorithm and a fast variant restricting search to a Hamming-1 ball, reducing per-update complexity from exponential to quadratic in tree depth.
- Empirical results on multiclass benchmarks show the method outperforms greedy baselines.
- Situates work among prior tree induction and structured prediction research; differs from Hierarchical Mixtures of Experts and earlier non-greedy methods by maintaining hard splits and offering scalable optimization.
- Strength — originality/significance: elegant, principled reformulation that connects decision trees with latent structured prediction.
- Strength — technical quality: sound derivation of the surrogate, clever fast inference algorithm, and scalable SGD with projections.
- Strength — future potential: framework readily extends to sparse (L1) or kernelized splits and suggests new classes of tree algorithms.
- Weakness — interpretability: oblique, L2-regularized splits will be dense and hard to interpret; L1 regularization is only suggested as future work.
- Weakness — ambiguity: paper does not specify behavior when a split weight vector is zero (sgn(0) undefined), leaving model initialization/growth unclear.
- Weakness — experiments: baseline descriptions lack crucial details (e.g., whether greedy trees used post-pruning), potentially biasing comparisons.
- Weakness — baselines incomplete: missing stronger/simple global baselines (e.g., many greedy trees on random subsets or random restarts, Random Forest–style selection).
- Weakness — clarity: title reference to "randomized forests" is misleading since only single-tree training is studied; inference algorithm descriptions are dense and would benefit from pseudocode.
- Interesting direction/question: fast SGD updates are sparse (affecting only path nodes), raising whether the optimizer will refine existing splits or activate new splits from zero — an intriguing dynamics to analyze.
- Summary: pro — novel, technically sound, opens research directions; con — experimental validation, interpretability, and some technical/clarity issues need improvement.
