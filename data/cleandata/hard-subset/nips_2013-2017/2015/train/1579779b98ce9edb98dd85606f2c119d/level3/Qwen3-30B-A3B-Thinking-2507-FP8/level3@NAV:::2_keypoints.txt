- Proposes a novel framework for non-greedy decision tree learning by jointly optimizing split parameters and leaf posteriors as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss and an efficient gradient computation method with O(d²p) complexity, enabling SGD training of deep trees.  
- Research direction is significant and original, addressing longstanding limitations of greedy tree induction.  
- Paper is well-written and the theoretical derivation is generally clean, with an insightful connection to structured prediction.  
- Theoretical claims contain flaws: argument around line 216 is confusing and a simple counterexample suggests the claimed upper bound may not hold.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is incomplete and requires fixing.  
- Paper does not address how the objective handles leaves that receive no data points, indicating potential optimization issues.  
- The fixed-assignment heuristic appears suboptimal and is insufficiently motivated; alternatives like EM or CCCP would be more appropriate.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: the necessity of the efficient loss-augmented inference is questionable because depth ≤16 allows brute-force.  
- Fails to compare against a greedy baseline for non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization, weakening empirical claims.  
- Tree depth must be pre-specified, yet no experiments vary depth to assess this limitation.  
- Presentation of results is unclear in places (e.g., ambiguous terminology for "leaves" in inference).  
- Overall, core idea is valuable but theoretical and experimental shortcomings reduce impact; a rebuttal and additional experiments are necessary for acceptance.
