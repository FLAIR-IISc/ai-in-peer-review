- Proposes framing decision tree split optimization as a structured prediction problem enabling joint (non-greedy) optimization across the tree.  
- Uses a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss to enable efficient global training of splits.  
- Demonstrates a reduction in computational complexity to O(d) (d = tree depth), allowing scalable training with SGD.  
- Establishes connections to structured prediction with latent variables and related models (HME, CO2).  
- Positions work well within prior literature and contrasts with greedy methods like ID3/CART.  
- Empirical results on SensIT, Connect4, Protein, and MNIST show non-greedy trees generally outperform greedy baselines, though gains are often small.  
- Provides a useful ablation study on the regularization parameter ν.  
- Improvements in accuracy are modest, which may limit practical impact for some applications.  
- Paper relies on initialization from a greedy tree, which could be a limiting dependency.  
- Would benefit from clearer discussion of when non-greedy optimization is most advantageous and the computational trade-offs in practice.  
- Well-written, theoretically sound, and novel — reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
