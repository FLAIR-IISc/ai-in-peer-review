This paper proposes an algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions at the nodes, optimizing the tree parameters globally, as opposed to the standard greedy approach. The authors introduce an upper bound on the tree's empirical loss, which is regularized via L2 regularization of the split function weights to achieve a smoother optimization problem. The algorithm uses stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to identify the tree parameters that minimize the upper bound.

The tree optimization problem is elegantly formulated by establishing a connection with structured prediction with latent variables. However, the empirical results presented in the paper do not convincingly demonstrate that global optimization is a better alternative to greedy learning of the tree. The globally optimized tree significantly outperforms the greedy tree on only a couple of datasets, while being more computationally intensive.

The paper is well-structured, but some points require clarification:

* The stable version of SGD is not clearly described. It would be helpful to provide a pseudo-code and explain how data points are assigned to leaves.
* Figure 2: It is unclear whether the results were obtained using SGD or stable SGD. Additionally, the definition of an "active" leaf is not provided.
* In line 10 of Algorithm 1, the update and projection of \Theta on a simplex require further explanation. In practice, how is this projection problem solved? It is also worth mentioning that this projection ensures that each line of \Theta sums up to one.

Minor comments and typos:

* Lines 407-408: The statement about tuning the "number of features evaluated when building an ordinary axis-aligned decision tree" is unclear, as all features are evaluated at each node in a standard decision tree.
* Equations (7), (8), and (9): "argmax" should be replaced with "max".
* Figure 1 is not mentioned in the main text.
* Line 141: The sentence "...to the index of the leaf on by this path" contains an error.
* Line 238: The phrase "...the solution to loss-augmented inference..." should be pluralized to "...the solutions to...".
* Line 301: The phrase "An key observation" should be corrected to "A key...".
* Line 360: The acronym FSGD is not defined.
* Line 404: The phrase "...a tree with minimum training errors" likely means "...tuning errors".

In the supplementary material:

* Line 29: The notation "max{g \in sgn...}" should be corrected to "max{g = sgn...}".
* Line 30: The equation "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(\Theta^Tf(g), y)..." should be corrected to "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(\Theta^Tf(sgn(Wx)), y)".

While the tree optimization problem is elegantly formulated, the results presented do not convincingly demonstrate the benefits of global optimization over greedy learning.
