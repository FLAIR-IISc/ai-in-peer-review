- Novel framework: non-greedy optimization of decision trees via reformulation as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Technical contribution: derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling efficient global optimization by SGD (Eq.7, Prop.1).  
- Complexity improvement: reduces training cost from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p) with a modified surrogate objective, making deep-tree training feasible.  
- Empirical results: consistent 1–2% test accuracy gains on LibSVM benchmarks versus greedy baselines, with no significant overfitting.  
- Comparison: outperforms CO2 (a greedy variant of the upper bound), supporting the value of non-greedy optimization.  
- Strength — Quality: rigorous theoretical derivation and provable bound tightening with W norm; addresses NP-complete bottleneck.  
- Strength — Originality: novel connection between decision trees and latent structured prediction; handles arbitrary loss while preserving O(dp) inference.  
- Strength — Significance: enables large-scale deep tree applications and introduces ν regularization to control overfitting.  
- Strength — Clarity: well-organized presentation, clear problem formulation, algorithm details, and intuitive loss-augmented inference.  
- Weakness — Modest gains: absolute performance improvements are relatively small compared to state-of-the-art forest methods.  
- Weakness — Limited ablation: insufficient analysis of how ν interacts with tree depth and dataset size.  
- Weakness — Scope: focuses on binary trees; N-ary extensions are mentioned but not explored.  
- Overall verdict: Significant, well-founded advancement with practical validation; recommended for acceptance despite marginal absolute performance gains.
