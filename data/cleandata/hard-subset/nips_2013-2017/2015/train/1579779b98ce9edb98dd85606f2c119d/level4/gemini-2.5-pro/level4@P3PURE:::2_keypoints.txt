- Proposes joint training of all decision-tree parameters and derives a tractable upper bound on empirical loss for optimization.  
- Paper is well-written and original/significant, but has concerns about theoretical validity and limited experiments.  
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss upper-bound (Eq. 7); counterexample suggests the claimed inequality may not hold and the proof appears incomplete.  
- Some leaf nodes become empty during training; the fixed data-to-leaf heuristic is ad-hoc and needs intuition or formal motivation (e.g., EM or CCCP).  
- Question the practical need for the proposed efficient loss-augmented inference since brute-force is feasible at depth 16; unclear how Hamming-ball restriction affects general performance.  
- Missing baseline: a greedy, layer-by-layer tree trained with non-axis-aligned splits should be included for direct comparison.  
- Requiring a pre-specified fixed tree depth is a notable limitation; more discussion and sensitivity analysis are needed.  
- Overall: interesting and important problem but requires stronger theoretical clarification, broader experiments (including runtimes and baselines), after which the reviewer may revise their score.
