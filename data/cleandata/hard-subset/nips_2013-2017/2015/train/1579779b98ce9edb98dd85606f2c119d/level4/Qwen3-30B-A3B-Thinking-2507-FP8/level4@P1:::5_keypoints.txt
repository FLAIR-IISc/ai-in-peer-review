- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree learning method by formulating tree optimization as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Develops a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that can be optimized with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Establishes a theoretical connection between decision tree navigation and latent-variable structured prediction.  
- Achieves efficient optimization with time complexity O(d² p), improving over prior exponential methods (e.g., Bennett).  
- Method differs from soft-split approaches (e.g., Hierarchical Mixture of Experts) by retaining decision tree computational advantages.  
- Strongest contribution is the elegant theoretical formulation enabling global optimization of tree parameters.  
- Experimental results show non-greedy trees often generalize better than greedy baselines, especially across tree depths.  
- Empirical evidence is limited: clear advantages appear on only a few datasets and improvements are marginal on others.  
- Computational cost is higher than standard greedy methods; a detailed comparison of training times is needed.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written overall, but has several clarity issues that must be addressed.  
- Stable SGD variant is poorly explained: unclear leaf assignment/updating; a pseudocode would improve clarity.  
- Figure 2 should state whether results use standard SGD or Stable SGD, and the paper must define “active” leaf.  
- Minor fixes required: clarify Θ projection (rows sum to one for softmax), correct misleading phrase about “number of features evaluated,” and replace incorrect "argmax" with "max" in equations 7–9.  
- Address missing discussion of Figure 1 in the main text and fix formatting/errors in the supplementary material.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions—strong theoretical contribution and potential practical impact, but needs clearer exposition and more comprehensive experiments.
