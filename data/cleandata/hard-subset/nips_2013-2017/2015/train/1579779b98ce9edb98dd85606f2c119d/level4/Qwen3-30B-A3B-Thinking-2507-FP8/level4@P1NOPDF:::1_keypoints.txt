- Paper merits publication for an elegant nonconvex relaxation approach to global optimization of decision trees with fixed structure.  
- Core method is well-motivated, clearly presented, and a promising direction for future work.  
- Empirical results are underwhelming: reported performance gains over baselines are marginal.  
- The paper fails to convincingly show that global optimization outperforms standard greedy methods on common benchmarks.  
- Suggestion: use a hybrid strategy—keep global optimization except re-optimize the final split and its leaf with conventional techniques to improve results.  
- The manuscript should explicitly address whether the global optimization increases overfitting risk; current evaluation does not explore this trade-off.  
- Conceptual contribution is strong enough to justify publication despite experimental weaknesses.  
- Incorporating the suggested refinement in future work could substantially improve empirical impact.  
- Current results do not invalidate the method but do not fully realize its theoretical promise; stronger empirical validation would warrant a higher score.  
- Writing is clear and technical arguments are sound, making the paper a valuable contribution despite experimental limitations.
