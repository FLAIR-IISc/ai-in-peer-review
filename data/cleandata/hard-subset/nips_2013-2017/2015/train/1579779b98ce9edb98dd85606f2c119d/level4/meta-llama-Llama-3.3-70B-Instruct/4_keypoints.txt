- Presents a novel global optimization of decision trees by framing as latent structured prediction with sign-vector latent variables.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and a differentiable non-convex upper bound for optimization.  
- Minimizes the bound with SGD, requiring repeated loss-augmented inference solved by efficient algorithms.  
- Key contribution: new structured-prediction view and accompanying efficient optimization methods.  
- Raises idea of gradually introducing non-zero weights to effectively optimize (potentially infinite) tree depth and adapt complexity to data.  
- Ambiguity in handling zero-weight nodes; current treatment seems arbitrary and needs a precise definition tied to pruning.  
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization yields dense weights that may destroy decision-tree interpretability; L1 suggested but may hurt performance.  
- Experimental comparisons limited: only a standard greedy baseline used without details on pruning strategies or parameters.  
- Recommends adding experiments with multiple pruning strategies, naive global methods (e.g., training on random subsets), and other baselines.  
- Paper clarity generally good but inference sections (6.2, 6.4) are verbose and could be more concise and explicit.  
- Practical utility is uncertain due to interpretability issues and incomplete experimental validation.  
- Initialization and weight-evolution analysis could reveal stronger advantages but is currently lacking.  
- Title mentions forests but the paper does not address this — potential future work.  
- Post-rebuttal, zero-weight initialization and gradual weight growth remains a key open direction deserving further exploration.
