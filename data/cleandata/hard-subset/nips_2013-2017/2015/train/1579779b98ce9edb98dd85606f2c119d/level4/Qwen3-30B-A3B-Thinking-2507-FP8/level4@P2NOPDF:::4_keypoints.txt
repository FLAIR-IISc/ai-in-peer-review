- Proposes reformulating global decision tree optimization as latent structured prediction using sign vectors and a differentiable upper-bound loss optimized by SGD.
- Key contributions: casting global DT optimization as structured prediction and providing efficient loss-augmented inference algorithms.
- Sparse gradient updates (only one node’s weights modified per step) are intriguing and suggest potential for incremental tree growth from zero.
- Paper fails to explore whether sparse updates lead to equivalence with greedy methods or convergence to minimal trees under regularization.
- Main practical concern: interpretability is undermined because L2 regularization yields dense, non-interpretable node weights.
- The authors do not evaluate L1 or other sparsity-inducing regularizers to test the accuracy/interpretability tradeoff.
- Experiments lack sufficient baseline comparisons and details about the greedy baseline’s pruning strategy.
- The evaluation omits simple global optimization baselines (e.g., ensembles of greedy trees on random subsets), weakening superiority claims.
- Handling of zero-weight nodes is ambiguous and problematic—defaulting to the right branch is questionable and may affect theory and implementation.
- A principled treatment would view zero weights as pending splits or model weight activation as explicit tree growth, which the paper does not do.
- Authors’ rebuttal that random initialization avoids zero-weight issues does not address the promising zero-initialized growth direction.
- Minor clarity issues: Section 6.2’s inference description and Section 6.4 need clearer, more precise wording.
- Conclusion: technically clever and interesting, but practical relevance is limited by interpretability conflict, insufficient experiments, and unaddressed edge cases.
- Requested fixes: test L1 regularization, compare baselines with standardized pruning, and explicitly model/re-evaluate zero-weight behavior and weight activation.
- Title claim about “forests” is unsupported, as the paper focuses on single trees only.
