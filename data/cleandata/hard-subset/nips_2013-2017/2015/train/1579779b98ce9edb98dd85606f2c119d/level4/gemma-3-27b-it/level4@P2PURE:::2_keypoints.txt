- Proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters (splits and posteriors) via a single optimization with upper bounding and approximations.  
- Well-written and novel direction with potentially significant contribution despite shortcomings.  
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss bound: the argument that the RHS increases for all g ≠ sgn(Wx) is incorrect (counterexample shows RHS can be smaller), and Prop.1 proof is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points, suggesting problems with the objective or initialization; the fixed leaf-assignment heuristic seems sub-optimal.  
- The fixed-assignment heuristic might be justifiable via EM or concave-convex procedures (analogy to clustering).  
- Unclear importance and impact of the efficient loss-augmented inference design; exhaustive search may be feasible up to depth 16, so the Hamming-ball restriction’s generality/performance gain is unclear.  
- The term “leaves” and its precise meaning in the inference/search context should be clarified.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard greedy training approach for non-axis-aligned splits.  
- Tree depth must be chosen a priori; this limitation requires deeper discussion and experiments varying depth.  
- Suggest replacing argmax with max in the loss-bound expression.  
- Recommend adding runtime experiments and a more comprehensive empirical evaluation; reviewer is open to reconsidering score after rebuttal addressing these points.
