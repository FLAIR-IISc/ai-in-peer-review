- Proposes an elegant, well-motivated non-greedy method for learning decision trees by optimizing an upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Paper is very clearly written with strong explanation of technical details.  
- Insightful connection to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Clever use of a convex-concave upper bound to make optimization tractable.  
- Experimental results are mixed: sometimes outperform greedy baselines but improvements are inconsistent and not always significant.  
- Lacks analysis explaining why the method underperforms on some datasets and which problem types it suits best.  
- Potential overfitting risk from optimizing a global objective with limited regularization; norm constraint may be insufficient.  
- Needs analysis of generalization performance and exploration of additional regularization or early stopping.  
- Suggests a promising extension: fine-tune last splits and leaf nodes after global optimization to possibly boost performance.  
- Overall contribution is novel and promising, but weaker experimental validation and limited analysis of limitations argue for caution.
