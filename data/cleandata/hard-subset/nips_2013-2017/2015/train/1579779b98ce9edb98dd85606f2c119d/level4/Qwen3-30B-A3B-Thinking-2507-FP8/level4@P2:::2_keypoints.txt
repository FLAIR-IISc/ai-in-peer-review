- Proposes a non-greedy joint optimization for decision trees by framing learning as structured prediction with latent variables and deriving a convex-concave upper bound for SGD training.  
- Establishes a novel theoretical connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derivation of the upper bound (Eq. 7) is well-motivated but contains a critical logical flaw: the claim that RHS increases for all g ≠ sgn(Wx) is contradicted by a counterexample, leaving the bound’s validity in doubt.  
- Supplementary proof of Proposition 1 does not address the counterexample, so the theoretical foundation needs correction.  
- Experimental results show consistent improvements over greedy baselines, but require more thorough ablation studies.  
- Lacks a proper baseline for non-axis-aligned greedy trees (standard greedy oblique-split training is missing).  
- Clarity issues: the explanation of “inactive leaves” is insufficient and it’s unclear why leaves become unassigned and how this affects performance.  
- Ambiguity in the efficiency discussion: the role and significance of “leaves” in runtime/efficiency analysis is not clearly explained.  
- Practical limitation: method requires tree depth to be specified in advance, and the paper lacks detailed discussion of this constraint’s implications.  
- Claims an efficient optimization method with O(d²p) complexity for deep trees, which is a useful contribution if validated.  
- Provides a principled regularization approach via row-wise norm constraints.  
- Missing runtime comparisons to demonstrate claimed efficiency gains.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on fixing the upper bound proof, explaining inactive leaves, adding the missing greedy baseline, and discussing tree depth and runtime more thoroughly.
