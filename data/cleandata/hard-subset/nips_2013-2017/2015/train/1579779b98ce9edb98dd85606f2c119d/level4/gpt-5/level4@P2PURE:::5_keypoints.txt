- Proposes global optimization of decision trees with linear split functions (jointly learning split weights and leaf outputs) using an upper bound on empirical loss, L2 regularization, and SGD.  
- Optimization is elegantly connected to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing: globally optimized trees outperform greedy trees on only a few datasets and are more computationally expensive.  
- “Stable” SGD is not clearly described—unclear how data points are assigned to leaves; pseudocode is needed.  
- Figure 2: unclear whether results use plain SGD or stable SGD and unclear definition of an “active” leaf.  
- Algorithm 1 (line 10): unclear how Θ is projected onto the simplex in practice; note that this projection enforces each row of Θ sums to one.  
- Minor clarity/typo issues: ambiguous wording about tuning number of features for axis-aligned trees; replace “argmax” with “max” in (7)–(9); Figure 1 is never referenced; multiple sentence/wording errors (lines 141, 238, 301, 360, 404).  
- Supplementary-material fixes: line 29 should be max{g = sgn...}; line 30 should use f(sgn(Wx)) instead of f(g) in that expression.  
- Overall: formulation is strong but paper needs clearer methodological details and stronger, more convincing empirical evidence.
