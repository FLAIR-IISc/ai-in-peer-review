- Recommends acceptance: strong, elegant paper with potential to inspire follow-up work; current rating justified despite empirical shortcomings.
- Clearly written; proposes a nonconvex relaxation for globally optimizing fixed-structure decision trees.
- Experimental results are somewhat underwhelming; stronger empirical evidence would have earned a higher score.
- Suggests a practical modification: after global optimization, prune the final split in each tree and re-optimize that last level and leaf nodes with a greedy algorithm.
- Requests evaluation of overfitting risk: global optimization methods may be more susceptible to overfitting than greedy alternatives.
