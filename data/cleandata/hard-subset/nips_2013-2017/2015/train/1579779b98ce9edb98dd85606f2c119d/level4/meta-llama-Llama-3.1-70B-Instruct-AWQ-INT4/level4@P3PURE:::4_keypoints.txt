- Proposes novel reformulation of global decision tree optimization as latent structured prediction with efficient algorithms and SGD via loss-augmented inference.  
- Casting as structured prediction is interesting and enables sparse gradient updates that change only one node per step, offering computational advantages.  
- Sparse updates suggest intriguing possibilities like growing an infinitely deep tree from zero weights, but the link to known greedy strategies and implications of regularization need analysis.  
- Handling of nodes with zero weights is ambiguous; defaulting to the right branch is dubious and a clearer rule (e.g., outputting a decision and later splitting) is needed.  
- Major practical concern: method likely produces non-interpretable, dense splits under L2 regularization; L1 might enforce sparsity but could hurt performance and must be tested.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficiently detailed: no description of pruning strategies for the greedy baseline and no comparable tuning of naive methods.  
- Should compare against simple global-optimization baselines (e.g., greedy training on random subsets with model selection) to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Paper clarity is acceptable overall but inference sections (notably Sections 6.2 and 6.4) need clearer, more explicit explanations.  
- Overall verdict: clever and novel approach that raises important questions, but practical usefulness is unclear due to interpretability concerns and weak experimental support.  
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight issue remains critical; authors should explore zero initialization and gradual weight growth as an important direction.
