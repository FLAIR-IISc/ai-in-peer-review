- Proposes searching for the best fixed-depth decision tree by casting learning as a structured prediction problem with latent variables.  
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that enables global optimization of tree parameters.  
- Uses stochastic gradient descent to optimize the bound, allowing joint/global parameter learning rather than greedy splits.  
- Demonstrates improved generalization and test accuracy over traditional greedy methods in experiments.  
- Improvements in accuracy are generally small, leaving unclear whether gains are practically significant.  
- Establishes a novel and useful connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction.  
- The convex-concave bound and SGD optimization are key technical contributions.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with comparisons to several baselines, but lacks deeper analysis of why the method improves performance.  
- Relevant and a good fit for NIPS due to its link to structured prediction and machine learning significance.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
