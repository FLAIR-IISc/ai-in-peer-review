- Introduces a non-greedy algorithm for learning oblique decision trees by jointly optimizing all split functions and leaf parameters.  
- Frames tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective.  
- Optimizes the surrogate objective with stochastic gradient descent (SGD).  
- Key technical contribution: an efficient loss-augmented inference algorithm reducing complexity from exponential to O(d^2 p), enabling deeper tree training.  

- Strength — originality: novel and elegant formulation linking decision trees to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Strength — technical quality: sound derivation of surrogate objective and non-trivial, practical inference speedup.  
- Strength — principled optimization: global, regularized objective offers natural complexity control and may mitigate overfitting.  

- Weakness — empirical validation: results are not consistently convincing; improvements over greedy baselines are not uniformly significant given higher computational cost.  
- Weakness — unclear when and why method succeeds or fails; stronger empirical analysis or targeted experiments are needed.  

- Reproducibility issue: "Stable SGD" (SSGD) is poorly described; mechanism for fixing/updating data-to-leaf assignments needs formal description or pseudocode.  
- Experimental ambiguity: paper does not state whether Figure 2 uses standard SGD or SSGD, a critical detail for interpreting results.  
- Terminology issue: "active leaf" is used but never formally defined.  
- Implementation detail missing: update of leaf parameters Θ (e.g., softmax) needs specification of projection to maintain valid probability distributions.  

- Typos/notation errors: argmax should be max in equations (7)-(9); Fig.1 is not referenced; multiple line-specific typos and grammar errors noted.  
- Undefined acronym: "FSGD" is used without prior definition (likely refers to fast SGD variant).  
- Ambiguous experimental description: reference to "number of features evaluated" during axis-aligned tree building is confusing and likely conflates feature subsampling (random forests) with standard tree algorithms.  
- Hyperparameter tuning concern: tuning reported as minimizing training error should likely use validation error to avoid overfitting.  

- Supplementary corrections: notation fixes suggested (e.g., proper max subscript and correct loss argument involving sgn(Wx)).  
- Conclusion: promising, well-motivated technical contribution but requires stronger empirical evidence and clearer, more reproducible algorithmic/experimental descriptions before being a strong contribution.
