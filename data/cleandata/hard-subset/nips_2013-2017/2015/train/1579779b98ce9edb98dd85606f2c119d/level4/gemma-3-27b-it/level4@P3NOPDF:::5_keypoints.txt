- Paper proposes global optimization for decision trees with linear split functions, using an L2-regularized upper bound on empirical loss and SGD for learning.
- Elegant formulation and useful connection to structured prediction with latent variables.
- Empirical evaluation does not convincingly show global optimization consistently outperforms greedy methods.
- Global method shows improvements on a few datasets but incurs significant computational cost; trade-off not justified by results.
- Implementation of the “stable” SGD variant is insufficiently described, especially how data points are assigned to leaves; pseudocode needed.
- Figure 2 lacks context: unclear whether results use standard SGD or the stable variant; definition of “active” leaf is missing.
- Algorithm 1’s projection of Θ onto a simplex (line 10) needs a practical solution method and note that rows sum to one (probability distribution over leaves).
- Clarify and fix wording in Lines 407–408 about “number of features evaluated” which may misrepresent standard decision tree practice.
- Equations (7), (8), and (9) should use “max” instead of “argmax.”
- Figure 1 is referenced but not discussed in the main text; discuss or remove reference.
- Fix incomplete sentence at Line 141.
- Correct Line 238 to “…the solutions to loss-augmented inference…”
- Correct grammatical error at Line 301: “An key observation…” → “A key observation…”
- Define FSGD (Line 360).
- Clarify Line 404, likely meant “tuning” errors instead of “a tree with minimum training errors.”
- Supplementary: fix Line 29 to “max{g = sgn…” and Line 30 to include l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y). 
- Overall: theoretically sound and elegant, but needs clearer exposition, bug fixes/typos, and stronger empirical evidence to justify computational cost and claimed advantages.
