1. Proposes reframing global decision-tree optimization as (latent) structured prediction with sign-vector latent variables.  
2. Introduces a non-differentiable loss and a differentiable upper bound optimized by SGD.  
3. Provides efficient algorithms for the required loss-augmented inference problems.  
4. Key novelty: casting tree optimization as structured prediction and deriving sparse gradient updates.  
5. Empirical comparison is limited to a standard greedy baseline; overall performance gains over simple methods seem modest.  
6. Practical motivation is unclear: trees are mainly used for interpretability, which this method may sacrifice.  
7. Regularization choice concern: L2 likely harms interpretability; L1 could help but may reduce performance.  
8. Ambiguity about node behavior when weight equals zero—current right-branch rule seems suboptimal and needs precise definition.  
9. Unexplored opportunity: incremental growth from zero weights could adapt model complexity and relate to greedy strategies.  
10. Questions remain about whether the method prefers adding nodes vs. revising existing ones and its convergence under regularization.  
11. Experimental baselines lack detail (e.g., pruning strategies) and need comparable hyperparameter tuning and additional simple global methods.  
12. Paper writing is generally good but inference descriptions (Sections 6.2 and 6.4) need clearer, more concise explanation.  
13. Title mentions forests but the paper does not address forest models.  
14. Post-rebuttal: zero-weight issue persists as a critical concern; random initialization does not resolve the conceptual point.  
15. Overall: clever and novel approach, but practical utility and experimental validation are not fully convincing.
