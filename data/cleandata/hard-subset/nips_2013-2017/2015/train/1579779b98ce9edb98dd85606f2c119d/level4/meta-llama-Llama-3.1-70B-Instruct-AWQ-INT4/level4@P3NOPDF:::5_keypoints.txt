- Proposes globally optimizing decision trees with linear split functions via an upper bound on empirical loss, L2 regularization, and SGD.  
- Formulation is elegant and draws a clear connection to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing: global optimization significantly outperforms greedy learning on only a couple datasets and is more computationally intensive.  
- Description of the “stable” SGD variant is unclear—assignment of data points to leaves needs explanation and a pseudo-code would help.  
- Figure 2 is ambiguous: it’s not stated whether results use SGD or stable SGD, and “active” leaf is undefined.  
- Algorithm 1 (line 10): the update and projection of Θ onto a simplex is not explained—how the projection is solved in practice and that each row of Θ sums to one should be stated.  
- Incorrect/misleading claim (lines 407–408) about evaluating a subset of features when building axis-aligned trees; standard trees evaluate all features at each node.  
- Notational/terminology fixes: replace “argmax” with “max” in Eqs. (7)-(9); Figure 1 is not referenced in the text.  
- Grammar/typos to fix: line 141, line 238, line 301 (“An key” → “A key”), line 360 (define FSGD), line 404 (“minimum training errors” → “tuning errors”).  
- Supplementary material typos: line 29 should read max{g = sgn...; line 30 should read sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y).  
- Overall conclusion: elegant theoretical formulation but experimental validation and several clarity/typo issues need to be addressed.
