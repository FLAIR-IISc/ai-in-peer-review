Edit after feedback: After discussion with the other reviewers and reading the rebuttal, I still believe this is a good paper that deserves publication because of the elegance of the approach and its potential to stimulate further research on related methods. I agree that the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. With stronger empirical results I would likely have rated the paper even higher, so I believe my current score is appropriate.

The paper is very clearly written and proposes a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The approach is elegant, but the empirical performance is underwhelming.

One straightforward modification would be to, after performing the global optimization, discard the last split in each tree and then reoptimize that split and its associated leaf node using standard procedures; this might improve results. It is also important to evaluate whether the global optimization generally leads to increased overfitting.
