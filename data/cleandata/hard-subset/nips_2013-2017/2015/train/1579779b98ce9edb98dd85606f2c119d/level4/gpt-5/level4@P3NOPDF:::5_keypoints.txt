- Summary: Proposes globally optimized decision trees with linear split functions via a smooth surrogate objective derived from a loss upper bound and L2 regularization, optimized with (stable) SGD under a latent structured-prediction view.

- Strength: Conceptually elegant bridge between decision-tree learning and latent structured prediction.

- Strength: Principled objective design using a loss upper bound plus L2 regularization to stabilize optimization.

- Strength: Potentially broadly applicable where greedy split selection is suboptimal.

- Weakness: Experiments do not convincingly show consistent superiority over strong greedy induction; wins are few and often small.

- Weakness: Method appears substantially more computationally intensive than greedy training, but computational trade-offs (time, epochs, convergence) are not reported.

- Weakness: Key methodological details are unclear, hindering reproducibility and interpretation.

- Request: Provide precise description/pseudocode of “stable” SGD and explain how and when examples are assigned/updated to leaves (per step/mini-batch/epoch) and how it differs from standard SGD.

- Request: For Algorithm 1 (line 10), specify the projection routine onto the simplex (e.g., Euclidean projection), cite it, and state that each row of Θ is nonnegative and sums to one.

- Request: Report computational costs (wall-clock time or number of gradient updates) versus greedy baseline, specify hardware, and include convergence plots for objective/validation performance.

- Request: Provide sensitivity analyses varying tree depth, number of leaves, and regularization strength; clarify stopping criterion and learning-rate schedule.
