- Proposes framing optimal fixed-depth decision tree search as a structured prediction problem.
- Method consistently outperforms traditional greedy tree construction on test accuracy.
- Magnitude of accuracy improvements is generally modest.
- Central idea is innovative and well-motivated, enabling exploration of a large tree space.
- Reframing helps find superior trees that greedy methods can miss.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares against several baselines.
- Main limitation is computational complexity, which may hinder scalability and real-time use.
- Authors should discuss runtime/compute requirements and possible mitigation strategies.
- Overall, a compelling and promising contribution despite modest gains.
