This paper proposes a non-greedy method for learning decision trees by optimizing an upper bound on the empirical loss of the tree's predictions on the training set. The approach is elegant and well-motivated, and it is likely to lead to more research on related approaches.

The paper is very clearly written, and the authors do a great job of explaining the technical details of their approach. The connection to structured prediction with latent variables is particularly insightful, and the use of a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss is a clever way to make the optimization problem more tractable.

The experimental results are somewhat disappointing, however. While the authors do demonstrate that their approach can outperform some greedy baselines, the improvements are not always significant, and the results are not consistent across all datasets. It would be helpful to see more analysis of why the approach is not performing better, and what kinds of datasets or problems it is best suited for.

One potential limitation of the approach is that it may be prone to overfitting, since it is optimizing a global objective function without any explicit regularization. The authors do use a norm constraint on the weights of the decision tree, but it is not clear whether this is sufficient to prevent overfitting. It would be helpful to see some analysis of the generalization performance of the approach, and whether it is possible to improve the results by adding additional regularization or early stopping.

Another potential extension of the approach would be to use it as a starting point for further optimization. For example, after using the global optimization to get an initial decision tree, it might be possible to throw away the last split in each tree and optimize it and the leaf node in the standard way. This could potentially lead to better results, and it would be interesting to see whether this approach is effective.

Overall, I think this is a good paper that deserves publication on the basis of the elegance of the approach and its potential to lead to more research on related approaches. However, I do think that the experimental results could be stronger, and that the authors could do more to analyze the limitations and potential extensions of their approach.

Strengths:

* Elegant and well-motivated approach to non-greedy decision tree learning
* Clear and well-written paper
* Connection to structured prediction with latent variables is insightful
* Use of convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss is clever

Weaknesses:

* Experimental results are somewhat disappointing
* Approach may be prone to overfitting
* Limited analysis of generalization performance and potential extensions

Arguments for acceptance:

* The approach is novel and well-motivated
* The paper is clearly written and easy to follow
* The connection to structured prediction with latent variables is insightful

Arguments for rejection:

* The experimental results are not strong enough to demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach
* The approach may be prone to overfitting, and the authors do not provide sufficient analysis of this issue
* The paper could benefit from more analysis of the limitations and potential extensions of the approach.
