Paper presents a global optimization algorithm for decision trees with linear split functions using an upper bound on empirical loss, L2 regularization, and SGD to learn tree parameters.  
Nice theoretical contribution linking decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables.  
Paper is well-structured, clear, and relates to prior work concisely.  
Empirical results are unconvincing: global optimization significantly outperforms greedy trees on only a couple of datasets.  
Proposed method is more computationally intensive than greedy learning, raising practical concerns.  
Need clearer description and pseudo-code for the “stable SGD” variant and specification of whether experiments used SGD or stable SGD.  
Clarify how data points are assigned to leaves, what an “active” leaf means in Figure 2, and how leaf assignments are handled during training.  
Numerous minor typos and errors to fix (argmax→max in eqs. 7–9; Figure 1 not cited; grammatical/typo fixes on lines 141, 238, 301, 360, 404; “number of features evaluated” wording).  
Supplementary material contains errors to correct (lines 29–30 sgn/max notation and a typo in the sgn(Wx) expression).  
Overall contribution is interesting and non-trivial but would benefit from stronger empirical evidence and clearer algorithmic exposition.  
Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, theoretically interesting link to structured prediction, clear presentation.  
Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical benefits over greedy methods, lack of clarity on key algorithmic details, and higher computational cost.
