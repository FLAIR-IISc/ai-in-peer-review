- Proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters (splits and posteriors) via a single non-greedy optimization.  
- Formulates an optimization, derives an upper bound and approximation to enable tractable SGD-based training.  
- Paper is generally technically sound and well-written, but some theoretical justifications need closer scrutiny.  
- Authors address overfitting with regularization, but further analysis of stability and sensitivity is needed.  
- Mathematical derivations are clear, but experiment presentation, hyperparameter choices, and runtime impact need improvement.  
- Novel application of structured-prediction techniques to jointly optimize decision trees.  
- Method could improve accuracy and generalization, supports general loss functions, and appears scalable, but relies on pre-defined tree depth.  
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss upper bound (Eq. 7); the provided justification is insufficient and needs a rigorous proof.  
- Some leaves become inactive (receive no data) during training; the offered heuristic is inadequate and a principled fix is needed.  
- Claims about efficiency of fast loss-augmented inference need quantitative comparison to exhaustive search, especially for deep trees.  
- The Hamming-ball restriction’s impact on performance and the definition of "leaves" in the supplement require clarification.  
- Missing baseline: greedy training with non-axis-aligned splits (without axis-aligned initialization) should be included.  
- Pre-defining tree depth is a significant limitation; authors should discuss methods for selecting or pruning depth dynamically.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, sound foundations (with caveats), scalable optimization, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable bound derivation, inactive leaves, limited experiments and missing baseline, and reliance on fixed depth.  
- Overall: interesting and potentially valuable work; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing the detailed concerns and clarifications.
