- Proposes non-greedy joint optimization of oblique split functions and leaf parameters under a single global objective.  
- Frames tree training as structured prediction with latent split-decision vector and derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss optimized by SGD.  
- Introduces fast loss-augmented inference by restricting search to a Hamming ball, reducing per-example complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p).  
- Empirical results show accuracy gains and reduced overfitting versus several greedy baselines and the CO2 method on multiclass benchmarks.  
- Positions work relative to greedy induction (ID3/CART), soft-split models, prior global tree optimization, and structural SVM/latent-variable methods.  
- Quality: optimization framework and bound-driven SGD are well motivated, and the Hamming-ball inference is technically useful; empirical evaluation is promising but limited.  
- Clarity: formulation and structured-prediction connection are clear, but some experimental terminology, baselines, and inactive-leaf behavior need clearer explanation; supplement proofs need tightening.  
- Originality: joint non-greedy oblique-split training via a latent-variable bound and the Hamming-ball inference are novel contributions.  
- Significance: could influence tree/forest training and offer better accuracy–depth trade-offs, but practical impact needs stronger baselines and runtime analysis.  
- Eq. (7) / loss bound: current presentation is confusing; replace argmax with max, expand the proof to cover edge cases, and give a clearer, self-contained justification.  
- Inactive leaves: training produces leaves with no data; authors should discuss causes, whether this is regularization or an artifact, motivate Stable SGD formally (e.g., EM/CCCP analogy), and report re-activation rates.  
- Hamming-ball restriction: need empirical quantification of any accuracy loss from radius-1 restriction, clarification whether the supplement’s single example generalizes, and precise use of the term “leaves” in runtime discussion.  
- Missing baseline: include a standard greedy oblique-split tree trained from scratch (no specialized initialization) to isolate gains from joint optimization versus initialization/split family.  
- Depth selection: method requires max depth a priori; provide validation curves, sensitivity analyses to ν and depth, and discuss implicit pruning and stopping/pruning criteria.  
- Runtime and scalability: add direct wall-clock runtime comparisons between full loss-augmented inference and Hamming-ball variant, and report throughput across depths/datasets.  
- Pros summary: principled latent-structured formulation, efficient SGD with O(d^2 p) per-example inference, empirical improvements over greedy baselines, and intuitive norm-based regularization.  
- Cons summary: unclear loss-bound presentation and proofs, opaque inactive-leaf dynamics with heuristic SSGD, missing strong greedy oblique baseline, and limited analysis of depth/accuracy-runtime trade-offs.  
- Post-rebuttal request: incorporate clarified loss-bound reasoning (replace argmax with max), expand proofs to edge cases, and add runtime comparisons; overall assessment is positive pending these clarifications.
