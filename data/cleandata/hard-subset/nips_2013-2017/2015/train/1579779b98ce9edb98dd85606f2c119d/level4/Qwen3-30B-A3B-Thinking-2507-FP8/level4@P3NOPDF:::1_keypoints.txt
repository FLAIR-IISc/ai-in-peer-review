- Proposes a compelling nonconvex relaxation that transforms combinatorial decision-tree optimization into a continuous problem solvable by gradient methods.  
- Method avoids local optima of greedy induction; exposition is clear and technically accessible.  
- Theoretical novelty and elegance justify publication and promise to catalyze future work in structured/interpretable models.  
- Empirical results are weak: only marginal (1–2%) average accuracy gains on standard datasets and inconsistent superiority/scalability.  
- The paper fails to address whether global optimization increases overfitting risk and lacks cross-validation or regularization analysis.  
- Recommended hybrid: drop the final split post-optimization and re-optimize it (e.g., with CART) to improve empirical performance.  
- Authors should more thoroughly discuss trade-offs and test overfitting metrics and regularization (e.g., L1 on split weights).  
- Overall recommendation: accept for strong theory and clarity, but experimental limitations and suggested refinements should be addressed in revisions.
