- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree optimization by framing induction as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss to enable tractable optimization.  
- Achieves O(d) gradient computation complexity, making deep tree training practical.  
- Optimizes the bound efficiently using stochastic gradient descent (SGD).  
- Empirical results show the non-greedy method outperforms greedy baselines on several classification benchmarks, though gains are modest.  
- Builds on and positions itself within extensive prior work on decision trees and structured prediction.  
- Paper is generally well-written and explains technical details clearly.  
- Provides a thorough and appropriate literature review.  
- Key strength: the novel formulation connecting trees to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Key strength: substantial computational efficiency improvement (O(d)).  
- Key strength: empirical validation across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness: improvements in accuracy are relatively small and need deeper analysis of when gains occur.  
- Weakness: results appear sensitive to the regularization hyperparameter ν, requiring careful tuning.  
- Weakness: dependence on greedy-tree initialization may affect outcomes; alternative initializations not explored.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to classification; regression and other data types not evaluated.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of SGD convergence and other formal guarantees.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, practical complexity gains, and validated empirically.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest practical gains, hyperparameter sensitivity, and limited theoretical justification.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to analyze improvement sources and robustness to hyperparameters.
