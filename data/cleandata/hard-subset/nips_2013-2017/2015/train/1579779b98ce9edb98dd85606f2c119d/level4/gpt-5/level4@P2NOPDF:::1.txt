After reading the rebuttal and discussing with the other reviewers, I maintain that this is a good paper worthy of publication, primarily for the elegance of its formulation and its potential to stimulate further research on related methods. The experimental results are somewhat disappointing; with stronger empirical performance I would likely raise my rating, so the current score reflects a balance between novelty and evidence.

The paper is very clearly written and proposes a non-convex relaxation for global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The approach is elegant and tackles a challenging problem in a principled way.

My main concern is the limited strength of the empirical results. The gains reported are modest, and the evaluation does not fully demonstrate the practical advantage of the method over strong baselines.

A simple refinement that may improve performance is a hybrid training step: after performing the global optimization, remove the final split in each tree and re-optimize that last split and the associated leaf parameters using standard (local/greedy) procedures. This could help recover performance lost to relaxation artifacts while retaining the benefits of the global step.

It is also important to assess whether global optimization increases overfitting relative to conventional training. Please provide evidence such as training vs. validation/test error, sensitivity to regularization, and performance as a function of dataset size or tree complexity. This analysis would clarify the method’s generalization behavior.

Overall, despite the underwhelming empirical results, the clarity, elegance, and potential impact of the approach justify acceptance. Stronger experiments would further elevate the paper.
