- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning method that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters under a global objective.  
- Establishes a novel connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex-concave surrogate upper bound on empirical loss that can be optimized with SGD.  
- Key technical contribution: reduces computational complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), making deeper tree training feasible.  
- Theoretical foundation and surrogate objective are sound and provide principled regularization for joint optimization.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized, but some methodological details need clarification.  
- The SSGD variant is insufficiently explained—unclear how data points are assigned to leaves; pseudo-code is needed.  
- Figure 2 lacks specification of whether results use standard SGD or SSGD and needs definition of “active” leaf.  
- Empirical results show improvements on some datasets (SensIT, Protein) but are inconsistent across benchmarks (Connect4, MNIST).  
- Paper does not explain why gains are dataset-dependent nor analyzes training-time vs. accuracy trade-offs.  
- Comparison to prior and recent non-greedy tree work is limited; connections to Mondrian forests and other approaches should be strengthened.  
- Comparison with CO2 is helpful but restricted to depth-1 trees, limiting empirical context.  
- Minor issues: unclear projection of Θ onto simplex in Algorithm 1, confusing phrasing about “tune the number of features evaluated,” incorrect use of “argmax” vs. “max” in several equations, Figure 1 never discussed, and some grammatical errors.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical originality and clear technical contribution, but significance is not fully demonstrated due to limited and inconsistent empirical validation.
