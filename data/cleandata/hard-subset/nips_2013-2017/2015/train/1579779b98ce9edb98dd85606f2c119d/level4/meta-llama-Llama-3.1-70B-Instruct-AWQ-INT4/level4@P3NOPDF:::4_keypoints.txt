- Proposes global decision-tree optimization by casting it as (latent) structured prediction with sign-vector latent variables.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and a differentiable upper-bound optimized by SGD.  
- Efficient algorithms are provided to solve the required loss-augmented inference problems.  
- Main contributions: novel formulation of DT optimization as structured prediction and efficient optimization algorithms.  
- Sparse gradient updates (only one node weight changes per step) enable interesting possibilities like growing an effectively infinite tree from zero weights.  
- Unclear whether the method is equivalent to known greedy strategies or will, under regularization, converge to small trees—needs analysis.  
- Ambiguity about handling zero node weights: current implicit “take right branch” rule is dubious and should instead allow making a terminal decision to enable later splitting (ties to pruning).  
- Practical motivation concern: method likely yields non-interpretable, dense splits under L2 regularization; L1 might enforce sparsity but could hurt performance—requires testing.  
- Experiments lack detail on baseline pruning/regularization; naive methods should be tuned similarly and simple global baselines (e.g., greedy on random subsets) should be compared.  
- Paper is reasonably clear but inference sections (Sec.6.2, 6.4) could be summarized/made more explicit.  
- Overall: clever and interesting approach but practical usefulness is unclear due to interpretability issues and insufficient experimental comparisons.  
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight case remains a critical gap; reviewer encourages exploring zero initialization with gradual weight growth.
