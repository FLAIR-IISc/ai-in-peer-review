The paper "Efficient Non-greedy Optimization of Decision Trees" presents a novel approach to optimizing decision trees by formulating the problem as a global optimization task. The authors propose a non-greedy method that uses stochastic gradient descent to optimize an upper bound on the empirical loss of the tree's predictions on the training set.

The main idea is to associate a binary latent decision variable with each split node in the tree and use these latent variables to formulate the tree's empirical loss. This formulation enables the authors to establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables. The proposed approach is elegant and well-motivated, and it has the potential to lead to more research on related approaches.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The technical contributions are sound, and the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, and the authors could have provided more insights into the performance of their approach.

One potential improvement to the approach is to combine the global optimization with the standard greedy optimization method. For example, after using the global optimization, the authors could throw away the last split in each tree and optimize it and the leaf node in the standard way. This might lead to better results and provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

Another important aspect to evaluate is whether the global optimization approach generally leads to more overfitting. The authors could have provided more analysis on this topic and discussed the potential trade-offs between the global optimization approach and the standard greedy method.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is elegant and well-motivated. While the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, the paper has the potential to lead to more research on related approaches, and the authors provide a good starting point for further investigation.
