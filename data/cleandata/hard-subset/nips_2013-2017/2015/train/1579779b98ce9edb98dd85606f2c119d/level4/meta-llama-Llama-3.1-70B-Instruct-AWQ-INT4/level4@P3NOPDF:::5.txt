This paper proposes an algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions at the nodes, where the tree parameters are optimized globally, as opposed to the standard greedy approach. The algorithm formulates an upper bound on the tree's empirical loss and regularizes it using L2 regularization of the split function weights to achieve a smoother optimization problem. Stochastic gradient descent is then employed to identify the tree parameters that minimize the upper bound.

The tree optimization problem is elegantly formulated by establishing a link with structured prediction with latent variables. However, the empirical results presented in the paper do not convincingly demonstrate that global optimization is a superior alternative to greedy learning of the tree. The globally optimized tree significantly outperforms the greedy tree on only a couple of datasets, while being more computationally intensive.

The paper is well-structured, but some points require clarification. The stable version of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) is not clearly described. Specifically, the process of assigning data points to leaves is not well-explained, and a pseudo-code would be beneficial. Additionally, Figure 2 lacks clarity: it is unclear whether the results were obtained using SGD or stable SGD, and the definition of an "active" leaf is not provided.

In Algorithm 1, line 10, the update and projection of \Theta onto a simplex are not clearly explained. In practice, how is this projection problem solved? It would also be worth mentioning that this projection ensures that each line of \Theta sums up to one.
