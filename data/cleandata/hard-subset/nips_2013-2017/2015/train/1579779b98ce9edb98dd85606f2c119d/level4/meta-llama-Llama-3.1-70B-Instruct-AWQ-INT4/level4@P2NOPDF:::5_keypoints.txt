- Proposes globally optimizing decision trees with linear split functions using an upper bound on empirical loss and L2 regularization for smoother optimization.  
- Uses stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to minimize the proposed upper bound and learn tree parameters.  
- Formulates tree learning via a connection to structured prediction with latent variables—an elegant theoretical contribution.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly show global optimization is superior to greedy learning; only a few datasets show significant improvement.  
- Globally optimized trees are more computationally intensive compared to greedy methods.
