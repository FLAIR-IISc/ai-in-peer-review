- Proposes a novel decision-tree learning approach by formulating tree induction as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction, enabling a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Uses the formulation to enable efficient learning of deep trees via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Well-motivated paper that clearly explains limitations of traditional greedy decision-tree induction.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on multiple benchmark datasets showing improved test performance and better generalization than greedy baselines.  
- Strengths: original idea, technically sound, and experimentally convincing.  
- Provides detailed analysis of the method, including its structured-prediction connection and convex-concave bound.  
- Weakness: absolute improvements in test accuracy over greedy baselines are relatively small.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity, especially compared to greedy methods.  
- Clarity issues: notation is sometimes confusing and some explanations could be more intuitive.  
- Presentation could be improved with more visualizations or illustrations to explain key concepts.  
- Overall: solid contribution with potential to improve decision-tree accuracy and generalization; recommended but could be strengthened by addressing noted weaknesses.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
