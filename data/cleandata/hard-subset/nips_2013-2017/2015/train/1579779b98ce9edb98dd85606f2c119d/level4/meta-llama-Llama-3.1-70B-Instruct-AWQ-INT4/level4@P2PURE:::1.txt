After reevaluating the paper in light of the feedback and the authors' rebuttal, I reaffirm my initial assessment that this work merits publication, primarily due to the elegance of its approach and its potential to stimulate further research in related areas. Although I concur with the other reviewers that the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming, I believe that the paper's technical contributions and clarity outweigh its limitations. In fact, I would likely have assigned an even higher score if the results had been more compelling. The paper proposes a nonconvex relaxation for globally optimizing decision trees with a fixed structure, presenting a clear and well-written exposition of an elegant approach. However, the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. One potential strategy to enhance the results would be to discard the last split in each tree after global optimization and re-optimize it, along with the leaf node, using standard methods. Furthermore, it is crucial to investigate whether global optimization generally leads to increased overfitting, as this could have significant implications for the approach's applicability.
