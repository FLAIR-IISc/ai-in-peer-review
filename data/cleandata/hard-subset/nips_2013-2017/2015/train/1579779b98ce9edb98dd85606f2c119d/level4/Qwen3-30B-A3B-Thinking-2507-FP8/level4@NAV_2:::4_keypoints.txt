- Proposes a novel reformulation of global decision-tree optimization as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss that enables efficient training with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Key technical result reduces computational complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), making deep tree training feasible.  
- Reformulation yields sparse gradient updates where only one node’s weights are updated per step, a useful technical insight.  
- Empirically shows better generalization and reduced overfitting versus standard decision trees on multiple benchmarks.  
- Weak motivation: global optimization may sacrifice interpretability because L2-regularized weights are dense; need discussion of tradeoffs.  
- Suggest exploring L1 regularization or other methods to maintain sparsity and interpretability.  
- Experimental section lacks detail on baseline methods (pruning strategies and tuning); baselines should be tuned comparably.  
- Implementation/edge-case issue: handling of zero weight vectors (defaulting to right branch) is arbitrary and needs clarification and analysis.  
- Title mismatch: paper mentions “forests” but only addresses single trees—either change title or extend method.  
- Writing is generally clear but inference description (Section 6.2) could be simplified by explaining per-leaf maximization first.  
- Overall: technically interesting contribution, but practical significance is unclear until interpretability and experimental concerns are addressed.
