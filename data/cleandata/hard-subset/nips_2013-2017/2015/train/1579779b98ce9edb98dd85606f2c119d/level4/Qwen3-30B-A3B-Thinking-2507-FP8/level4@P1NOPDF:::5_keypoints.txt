- Proposes global optimization for decision trees with linear split functions, jointly optimizing split weights and leaf outputs via minimizing a regularized upper bound on empirical loss using SGD.  
- Frames the problem as structured prediction with latent variables; formulation is mathematically sound and theoretically interesting.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing: global optimization only significantly outperforms greedy learning on a few datasets.  
- Computational cost of the global method is substantially higher, and marginal gains do not justify complexity for most cases.  
- Request clearer explanation of the stable SGD variant, specifically how data points are assigned to leaves during optimization; pseudocode is recommended.  
- Clarify whether Figure 2 shows standard SGD or stable SGD results; explicitly define what an “active” leaf means.  
- In Algorithm 1, state how Θ is projected onto the simplex (e.g., specify the simplex projection algorithm) and note that each row of Θ sums to one.  
- Recommend more experiments comparing scalability and runtime against greedy methods to demonstrate practical utility.  
- Suggest deeper analysis of when global optimization provides tangible benefits over greedy approaches (dataset characteristics, tree depth, etc.).  
- Presentation lacks sufficient detail to assess method’s potential; clearer algorithmic descriptions and more thorough empirical analysis are needed.
