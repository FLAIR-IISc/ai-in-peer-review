

This paper presents a clear and elegant nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The approach is well-motivated and technically sound, offering a principled method for addressing a challenging problem in tree-based learning. While the experimental results are modest and fall short of the potential suggested by the theoretical contribution, the paper remains compelling for publication due to its conceptual novelty and the likelihood that it will stimulate further research on global optimization techniques for tree models. 

I note the authors' rebuttal and discussions with other reviewers. Although the results are disappointing, the paper's elegance and potential to catalyze future work justify a positive recommendation. A minor improvement could be made by, after applying the global optimization, removing the final split in each tree and optimizing that split and its associated leaf node using standard methods—this might yield better empirical performance. Additionally, the authors should explicitly acknowledge that the global optimization approach may inherently increase overfitting risk, as the optimization targets training loss without regularization, which warrants discussion in the evaluation.
