- Proposes a novel non-greedy method that jointly trains all split functions and leaf parameters under a global objective.  
- Connects decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Introduces a surrogate objective enabling SGD training with O(d) gradient complexity per example, making deep tree learning feasible.  
- Empirically outperforms greedy decision tree baselines across multiple classification benchmarks.  
- Addresses a longstanding limitation of greedy tree induction by optimizing splits with awareness of lower-level structure.  
- Theoretical derivation (Equation 7) is compelling but the proof appears incomplete and may not hold in all cases.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized; problem formulation and structured-prediction connection are clear.  
- Experimental section needs more detailed comparisons (runtime, ablations) and clearer presentation of results.  
- The “inactive leaves” phenomenon is insufficiently explained and the SSGD heuristic for fixing leaf assignments seems ad hoc.  
- The necessity and impact of the Hamming ball approximation for loss-augmented inference are not convincingly justified.  
- Missing baseline comparison to standard greedy non-axis-aligned tree methods (e.g., OC1) weakens empirical claims.  
- Novel contribution: tractable joint optimization formulation represents a meaningful advancement over prior non-greedy approaches.  
- Significant practical value for applications needing high-accuracy deep trees; authors suggest sensible future directions (sparsity, kernels).  
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing the upper-bound proof, clarifying inactive leaves, validating the Hamming approximation, and adding stronger baselines.
