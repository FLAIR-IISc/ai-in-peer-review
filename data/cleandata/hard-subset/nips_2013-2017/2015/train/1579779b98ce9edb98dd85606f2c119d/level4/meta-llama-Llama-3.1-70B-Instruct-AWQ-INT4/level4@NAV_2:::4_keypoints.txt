- Novel reformulation: casts global decision tree optimization as (latent) structured prediction with a clever, novel formulation and efficient optimization algorithms.  
- Loss formulation: proposes a non-differentiable, non-convex loss approximated by a differentiable upper bound optimized via SGD.  
- Inference algorithms: provides efficient loss-augmented inference procedures needed by the SGD sequence.  
- Sparse updates: gradient steps appear sparse (potentially changing only one node's weight), raising questions about links to greedy strategies.  
- Tree growth idea: suggests starting from zero weights to grow arbitrarily deep trees, but unclear whether this mimics greedy methods or yields compact trees under regularization.  
- Zero-weight ambiguity: behavior for nodes with zero weights is ill-defined (paper defaults to taking the right branch), which needs precise definition and pruning analogues.  
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization likely produces dense, non-interpretable splits; L1 could enforce sparsity but may hurt performance — requires empirical evaluation.  
- Experimental shortcomings: experiments lack details on baselines, pruning/regularization choices for the naive method, and comparable tuning.  
- Missing baseline comparisons: should include trivial global strategies (e.g., greedy on random subsets) and tune them similarly for fair comparison.  
- Clarity issues: paper is mostly clear but inference sections (notably 6.2 and 6.4) could be stated more explicitly and summarized.  
- Overall assessment: clever and interesting approach that raises valuable questions but practical usefulness is unclear due to interpretability and experimental gaps.  
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, clever structured-prediction reformulation, and efficient algorithms.  
- Cons for acceptance: unconvincing motivation regarding interpretability, insufficient experimental detail, and unaddressed zero-weight case.
