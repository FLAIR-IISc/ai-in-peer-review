- Novel reformulation: casts global decision-tree optimization as a structured prediction problem.
- Algorithmic contribution: devises an efficient algorithm to solve the resulting optimization.
- Method: non-greedy decision-tree learning using SGD on an upper bound of empirical loss.
- Optimization technique: performs SGD via loss-augmented subproblems that exploit problem structure.
- Sparse updates: gradient updates are sparse, suggesting possibilities like gradually growing infinitely deep trees.
- Novelty: the formulation and solution approach are original and interesting.
- Motivation concern: unclear why this method is needed given that decision trees are valued for interpretability.
- Interpretability risk: the learned trees may be non-interpretable, undermining a key decision-tree benefit.
- Regularization question: unclear whether L1 or other regularizers would address interpretability/non-sparsity issues.
- Experimental gaps: experiments lack detail, especially about baselines and pruning strategies for naive approaches.
- Baseline comparison: insufficient exploration of trivial global-optimization baselines that might be competitive.
- Clarity issues: paper is generally readable but some sections (notably inference) need clearer presentation.
- Practical significance: unclear if performance gains over naive methods are practically meaningful.
- Acceptance arguments: novel idea, efficient algorithm, and intriguing potential for infinite-depth optimization.
- Rejection arguments: unclear motivation regarding interpretability, insufficient experimental detail, and uncertain practical benefit.
