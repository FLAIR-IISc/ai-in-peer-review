- Presents a non-convex relaxation for globally optimizing fixed-structure decision trees by formulating learning as structured prediction with latent variables.
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling efficient training via SGD with O(d) gradient computation per example (d = tree depth).
- Builds on and addresses limitations of greedy methods (ID3, CART) and connects to related work (multilinear programming, Mondrian Processes, HME).
- Key conceptual contribution: linking decision tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables to obtain a tractable optimization framework.
- Strength: mathematically rigorous formulation and significant complexity analysis making deep tree training feasible.
- Strength: novel perspective and joint optimization framework that departs from traditional greedy approaches.
- Strength: exceptionally clear writing, consistent notation, and logical algorithm presentation.
- Strength: potential for future impact—opens avenues for regularization choices and kernel extensions.
- Weakness: experimental gains over greedy baselines are modest or comparable in some cases, limiting empirical convincement.
- Weakness: potential for overfitting with non-greedy optimization not thoroughly explored; regularization (ν) effects need deeper analysis.
- Weakness: sensitivity to initialization (e.g., starting from greedy CO2) is mentioned but not evaluated in depth.
- Argument for acceptance: theoretically sound, novel, significant technical contributions, and clear exposition likely to inspire follow-up work.
- Argument for rejection: insufficient empirical evidence of clear advantage and incomplete treatment of overfitting risks.
- Overall: strong, elegant, and technically sophisticated paper that merits publication pending deeper experimental/regularization analysis.
- Score: 7/10 (Accept).
