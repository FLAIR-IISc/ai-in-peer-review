- Introduces a non-greedy framework for jointly learning all split functions and leaf parameters by framing decision-tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a tractable surrogate objective for the discrete tree optimization.  
- Optimizes the surrogate objective using stochastic gradient descent for practical training.  
- Presents an efficient loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces inference complexity from exponential to polynomial in tree depth, enabling scalability to deep trees.  
- Provides a principled, optimization-based alternative to traditional greedy induction methods (e.g., CART, ID3).  
- Technical contribution is novel and connects global tree optimization to established structured prediction methods.  
- Surrogate objective is continuous and piecewise-smooth, giving a sound relaxation of the intractable discrete problem.  
- Method is general and applicable to various loss functions.  
- Development of a scalable training algorithm makes the approach practically relevant.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and likely to inspire further research in global tree optimization.  
- Experimental gains over strong greedy baselines are modest on benchmark datasets, tempering practical significance.  
- Global optimization may be more prone to overfitting; the paper lacks thorough empirical analysis comparing regularization to alternatives like post-pruning or leaf-sample constraints.  
- Suggestion: consider a hybrid approach re-optimizing or replacing the final layer of splits with a greedy procedure to better capture local structure.  
- Acceptance arguments: original formulation, novel connection to structured prediction, technically sound framework, and practical algorithmic contribution.  
- Rejection arguments: modest empirical improvements and insufficient analysis of overfitting risk in global optimization.
