- Proposes a global optimization framework for decision trees with linear split functions, jointly optimizing split weights and leaf outputs by minimizing an upper bound on empirical loss with L2 regularization and SGD.
- The formulation elegantly connects to structured prediction with latent variables and is theoretically sound.
- Empirical results are unconvincing: global optimization outperforms greedy trees on only a few datasets and incurs substantially higher computational cost.
- Stable SGD mechanism is unclear—need pseudocode and precise description of how data points are assigned to leaves for reproducibility.
- Figure 2 is ambiguous about whether results use standard SGD or stable SGD; the term “active” leaf must be defined.
- Algorithm 1 projects Θ onto a simplex after updates—specify the practical projection algorithm and note that this enforces each row of Θ to sum to one.
- Request broader empirical evaluation: test on more and larger datasets to substantiate claims of superiority.
- Ask for clear analysis of computational trade-offs (e.g., wall-clock time vs. accuracy) to justify added complexity.
- Require discussion of why global optimization succeeds on some datasets but not others.
- Paper is well-structured but needs clarification on several technical points before acceptance.
- Many minor corrections/typos throughout (e.g., Line 407–408 wording on “number of features evaluated”; replace “argmax” with “max” in Eqs. 7–9; Figure 1 not referenced; multiple line-specific typos).
- Supplementary material contains formula typos that must be fixed (e.g., Line 29 “max{g ∈ sgn...}” → “max{g = sgn...}”; Line 30 fix sgn(Wx) notation).
