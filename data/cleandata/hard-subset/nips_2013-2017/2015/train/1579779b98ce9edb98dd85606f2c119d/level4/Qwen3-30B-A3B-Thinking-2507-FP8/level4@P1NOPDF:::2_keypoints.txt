- Joint training of decision-tree parameters is an important and original direction.
- Proposed approach is valuable but has notable limitations that need addressing.
- Derivation flaw: counterexample shows the RHS of the empirical-loss bound can decrease for g ≠ sgn(Wx), so the proof of Proposition 1 is incomplete.
- Some leaves receive no data points, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization; the fixed-assignment heuristic appears suboptimal and needs justification (e.g., EM or CCCP).
- Loss-augmented inference: depth capped at 16 makes brute-force feasible; unclear how Hamming-ball restricted search affects performance and what "leaves" means in that context; only one result reported.
- Missing critical baseline: standard greedy training for non-axis-aligned split functions (without initialization) is not compared.
- Requiring a predetermined tree depth is a limitation that needs deeper discussion and experimental sensitivity analysis.
- Postscript: authors clarified Eq.(7) (argmax→max) and score was adjusted; recommend adding runtime comparisons, more rigorous evaluation, and fixing the derivation flaw.
