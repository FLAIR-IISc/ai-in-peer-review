This paper addresses the well-known limitations of greedy algorithms in decision tree induction, proposing a novel non-greedy approach based on formulating the problem as structured prediction with latent variables. The core idea is to jointly optimize split functions at all levels of the tree, leveraging a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss and stochastic gradient descent for efficient training. This approach is related to, but distinct from, previous work on global tree optimization (e.g., Bennett [1]), and offers a compelling alternative to methods relying on soft splits (e.g., [13]) which can lose the computational efficiency of decision trees. The paper establishes a clear connection to structured prediction [25], demonstrating awareness of relevant literature.

The strengths of this work lie in its theoretically sound formulation, addressing a long-standing problem in machine learning. The derivation of the convex-concave upper bound and the reduction of the gradient computation complexity from O(2dp) to O(d2p) are significant technical contributions. The experimental results on benchmark datasets consistently demonstrate improved performance compared to greedy baselines, including OC1 [17] and CO2 [18], suggesting the proposed method effectively mitigates the suboptimality of greedy tree induction. The inclusion of a stable SGD variant (SSGD) shows a thoughtful consideration of practical optimization challenges. The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and experimental setup.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. While the experimental results are positive, the magnitude of improvement over the baselines appears modest. A more thorough ablation study investigating the impact of different components (e.g., the regularization parameter ν, the choice of loss function) would strengthen the analysis.
