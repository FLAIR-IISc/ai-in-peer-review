- Proposes an elegant nonconvex relaxation for globally optimizing decision trees with fixed topology; exposition is clear and accessible.  
- Conceptually strong and likely to spur follow-up work on global optimization for tree models.  
- Empirical performance is underwhelming relative to strong baselines, limiting practical impact.  
- Paper does not convincingly address increased overfitting risk from global optimization of a highly nonconvex objective.  
- Evaluation fails to explore simple hybrid refinements that could improve generalization.  
- Suggestion: apply a hybrid refinement—refit final splits and leaves locally (e.g., greedy split selection or short coordinate descent) after global optimization.  
- Suggestion: assess overfitting by reporting train vs. test gaps, learning curves, using nested CV/validation for tuning, comparing pruned versions, and ablating regularization strength.  
- Suggestion: strengthen empirical evaluation with competitive baselines (CART with pruning, oblique trees, boosted trees), careful hyperparameter tuning, more datasets, and statistical significance measures.  
- Suggestion: provide runtime and scalability measurements with respect to depth, features, and dataset size.  
- Suggestion: add sensitivity analyses for initialization, relaxation parameters, and tree topology, plus visualizations of learned decision boundaries/split orientations.  
- Minor: add brief positioning relative to prior global tree learning methods (e.g., oblique trees, MIP formulations).  
- Minor: clarify assumptions required for the relaxation and discuss failure modes (e.g., degenerate or overly confident splits).  
- Overall: despite weak empirical results, the idea is elegant and worth publishing for its conceptual contribution and potential to catalyze hybrid and follow-up work.
