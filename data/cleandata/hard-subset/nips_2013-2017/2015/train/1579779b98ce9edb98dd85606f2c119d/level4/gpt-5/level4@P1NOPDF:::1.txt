Edit after feedback: After discussion with the other reviewers and consideration of the rebuttal, I still believe this is a good paper that merits publication for the elegance of its approach and its potential to stimulate further research on related methods. I agree that the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. With stronger empirical performance I would have rated the paper even higher, so I think my current score is appropriate.

The paper is very clearly written and proposes a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The approach is elegant and well motivated, but the empirical results do not fully match the promise of the method.

One straightforward improvement to try would be a simple post-processing step: after performing the global optimization, discard the last split in each tree and then optimize that split and the corresponding leaf node in the standard way. This might yield better predictive performance.

It is also important to evaluate whether the global optimization increases overfitting in practice. Please assess this explicitly.
