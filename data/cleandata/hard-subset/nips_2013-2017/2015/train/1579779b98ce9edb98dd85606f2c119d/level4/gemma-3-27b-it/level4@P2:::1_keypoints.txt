- Proposes a novel nonconvex relaxation for globally optimizing decision-tree parameters with fixed structure.  
- Frames decision-tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Enables efficient optimization using stochastic gradient descent.  
- Significant technical contribution: substantially reduces gradient computation cost, allowing training of deeper trees.  
- The approach is theoretically sound and mathematically well-motivated.  
- Writing is exceptionally clear and the paper is well organized.  
- Experimental gains over greedy baselines are modest and not dramatic.  
- Suggestion: refining final splits and leaf nodes with the standard greedy method could improve results.  
- Global optimization may increase risk of overfitting; authors use regularization but this warrants further study.  
- Limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly the regularization parameter ν.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong theoretical basis, technical efficiency, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: underwhelming empirical improvements and potential overfitting concerns.  
- Overall assessment: a good paper deserving publication; elegance and potential for future work outweigh modest experimental results.
