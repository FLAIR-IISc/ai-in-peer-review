1. Proposes non-greedy global optimization of decision trees by treating split decision sequences as latent variables in a structured prediction framework.  
2. Derives a convex-concave surrogate (upper) bound, inspired by latent variable structural SVMs, to make the piecewise-constant loss tractable.  
3. Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm reducing SGD update cost from exponential in depth to O(d²p), enabling deeper trees.  
4. Strength: novel, significant formulation moving beyond greedy induction that could inspire future work.  
5. Strength: solid technical contributions — tractable surrogate objective and scalable inference addressing key computational bottleneck.  
6. Strength: general framework supporting multiple loss functions for classification and regression.  
7. Main weakness: potential flaw in the upper bound (Eq. 7) — a counterexample (Wx=[5;−5]) suggests RHS can be smaller than LHS; Proposition 1’s proof seems incomplete.  
8. Concern about "inactive leaves" and the SSGD heuristic — fixing assignments feels ad hoc; asks for intuition and a formal connection (e.g., EM or CCCP).  
9. Experimental gaps: need directe runtime and accuracy comparison between full loss-augmented inference and the Hamming-ball approximation to quantify trade-offs.  
10. Missing baseline: no comparison to a greedy oblique decision tree trained with the same split parametrization to isolate benefits of non-greedy training.  
11. Limitation: model requires pre-specified tree depth; requests discussion and sensitivity analysis of this hyperparameter.  
12. Recommendation: borderline reviewer leaning to rejection pending clarification of the theoretical bound and stronger experimental evidence.  
13. Minor: replace 'argmax' with 'max' where the maximized value (not the arg) is used (e.g., Eq. 7).
