- Proposes non-greedy training of oblique decision trees by treating split decisions as latent binary variables and optimizing a global surrogate objective.  
- Derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Key technical contribution: loss-augmented inference restricted to a Hamming ball of radius 1, reducing per-example gradient cost from exponential to O(d^2 p).  
- Introduces “Stable SGD” (SSGD) to stabilize leaf assignments during training.  
- Practical algorithm: simple per-example gradients, row-norm constraints on W as regularization, and initialization from greedy trees.  
- Empirical results: consistent, modest gains over strong greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, CO2) and reduced overfitting on LibSVM benchmarks.  
- Connects to prior work: links classic greedy trees, oblique trees, structured prediction (max-margin/latent structural SVMs), margin-based trees, HME, and CO2.  
- Novelty: joint non-greedy optimization of all splits and fast loss-augmented inference are original relative to prior oblique/CO2 methods.  
- Strength: well-motivated formulation establishing a principled latent structured prediction view of tree training.  
- Strength: efficiency improvement via Hamming-ball restriction making deep trees feasible for SGD.  
- Strength: framework supports general loss functions and keeps O(dp) test-time inference.  
- Weakness: test accuracy improvements are often small, limiting practical significance vs. well-tuned greedy trees.  
- Weakness: narrow experimental scope—single trees on few datasets, no ensemble comparisons (RF/boosting).  
- Weakness: sensitivity to greedy initialization and limited ablation on initialization and SSGD vs. SGD.  
- Weakness: limited analysis and guidance on hyperparameter ν (row-norm constraint) despite its empirical importance.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to modern boosted oblique trees/gradient boosting and incomplete time–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Weakness: claims about regression and general-loss handling are not empirically supported.  
- Quality: surrogate bound and optimization are sound, complexity reductions plausible; nonconvexity acknowledged and addressed reasonably.  
- Clarity: generally well-written with good notation and algorithm outline but needing clearer intuition and more experimental detail for reproducibility.  
- Significance: meaningful advance for single-tree oblique methods, but broader impact limited by modest gains and dominance of ensemble methods.  
- Suggestions: compare to strong ensemble baselines (GBM, RF), report accuracy and runtime trade-offs.  
- Suggestions: provide ablations on initialization, ν, and SSGD; report variance over multiple seeds.  
- Suggestions: evaluate regression tasks and higher-dimensional datasets to validate generality.  
- Suggestions: expand runtime comparisons across datasets and depths, and analyze bias introduced by the Hamming-radius-1 bound.  
- Overall assessment: solid, principled, and scalable contribution with consistent but modest empirical gains; recommended weak accept pending stronger experimental comparisons and analyses.
