- Addresses suboptimality of greedy decision tree construction by proposing joint non-greedy optimization of all split and leaf parameters.
- Frames decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss for SGD optimization.
- Key technical contribution: reduces computational complexity from exponential to O(d²p) via a modified surrogate objective, enabling deeper tree training.
- Empirical results show non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines across multiple datasets.
- Major theoretical concern: potential flaw in derivation of Equation (7); counterexample (Wx = [5; -5], g = [1;1]) suggests the bound may not hold.
- Supplementary proof of Proposition 1 does not address the identified counterexample; authors must clarify or correct the derivation.
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematics, but experimental section needs improvement.
- Critical baseline missing: greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits (without the non-greedy initialization), making attribution of gains unclear.
- Presentation of results (Fig. 2) is unclear about exact performance gains over greedy methods.
- Unexplained phenomenon: some leaves become inactive during training, which could reduce model capacity and should be analyzed.
- Originality is high: joint optimization of whole tree and connection to structured prediction are novel contributions.
- The Hamming-ball approximation and scalable optimization are significant technical advances over prior non-greedy methods.
- Significance: method can improve decision tree performance and train deep trees with general loss functions, but unexplained issues weaken impact.
- Strengths: novel non-greedy approach, solid theoretical framing, substantial complexity reduction, and consistent empirical improvements.
- Weaknesses: possible invalid theoretical bound, missing critical baseline, inactive leaves problem, and insufficient analysis of tree depth and runtime.
- Recommendation: borderline acceptance conditional on resolving the theoretical issue and adding experiments addressing the missing baseline and inactive leaves.
