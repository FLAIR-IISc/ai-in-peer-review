- Proposes a novel global optimization of decision trees by casting them as latent structured prediction with sign-vector latents.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss approximated by a differentiable upper bound and minimized via SGD.  
- Each SGD step requires loss-augmented inference; the paper provides efficient algorithms for this.  
- Main contributions: the structured-prediction formulation and an efficient optimization procedure.  
- Notable property: gradient updates are sparse, modifying only one node per step, suggesting potential for “growing” trees.  
- Lacks analysis of whether the sparse-update growth is equivalent to greedy methods or can produce finite trees under regularization.  
- Ambiguity about nodes with zero weight vectors (paper implies default right-branching); this should be precisely defined and linked to pruning.  
- Practical concern: L2 regularization likely yields dense, non-axis-aligned splits that undermine interpretability of single trees.  
- No evaluation of L1 or other sparsity-inducing regularizers to recover interpretable (1-sparse) splits.  
- Experimental comparisons are weak: greedy baseline details, pruning strategy, and tuning are insufficiently specified.  
- Recommends including other global baselines (e.g., many greedy trees on random subsets) and equal tuning effort for baselines.  
- Clarity issues in inference sections (6.2, 6.4) — could be summarized and explained more explicitly.  
- Title mentions “forests” but the paper does not address or evaluate forests.  
- Overall: clever and thought-provoking, but practical usefulness is unclear due to interpretability and experimental gaps.  
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight behavior remains a critical unresolved issue; authors encouraged to explore zero initialization and sparse growth.
