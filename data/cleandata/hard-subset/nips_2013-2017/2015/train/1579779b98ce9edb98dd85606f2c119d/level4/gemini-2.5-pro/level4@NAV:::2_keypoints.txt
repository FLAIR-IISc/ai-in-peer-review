- Proposes jointly optimizing all decision tree parameters via a unified optimization and an approximated upper bound.  
- Manuscript is well-written but the theoretical justification of the bound has potential issues.  
- Mathematical derivations are clear; experimental section and result presentation need significant improvement.  
- The joint training paradigm for decision trees is novel and important.  
- Method is a valuable step forward but its impact is limited by several shortcomings needing attention.  
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss bound derivation; a counterexample suggests the bound can fail and the supplementary proof is incomplete (suggested `argmax`→`max` clarification).  
- Some leaf nodes receive no data, which is unusual and may indicate problems with the objective or initialization; the fixed data-to-leaf heuristic needs stronger justification (e.g., EM or concave-convex analogies).  
- Motivation for efficient loss-augmented inference is weak: with depth 16 exhaustive search might be feasible; need experiments on the Hamming ball restriction, clarification of “leaves” in supplement, and runtime comparisons.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks a crucial baseline: greedy training of oblique-split trees from scratch.  
- Requiring tree depth a priori is a notable limitation and needs discussion plus a sensitivity analysis.  
- Reviewer is open to revising their score pending careful explanations and clarifications in the rebuttal.
