- Proposes a nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of fixed-structure decision trees via a structured prediction with latent variables formulation.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling tractable optimization.  
- Shows the surrogate objective’s gradient can be computed efficiently, allowing SGD training of deep trees.  
- Reduces gradient computation complexity (reported improvement from O(2dp) to O(d2p)).  
- Positions work against greedy decision tree induction and connects to prior structured prediction literature.  
- The approach is theoretically sound, technically innovative, and clearly presented.  
- Proposed stochastic gradient descent algorithm is well-designed and computationally efficient.  
- Experimental results show non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines but improvements are modest.  
- Authors note need for careful hyperparameter tuning, especially the regularization constant ν.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of how regularization affects tree complexity and generalization.  
- Suggests investigating simple post-processing (re-optimizing splits/leaves) to reduce overfitting and boost performance.  
- Limited discussion of practical considerations and potential limitations.  
- Overall assessment: elegant, valuable contribution that merits acceptance despite modest experimental gains.
