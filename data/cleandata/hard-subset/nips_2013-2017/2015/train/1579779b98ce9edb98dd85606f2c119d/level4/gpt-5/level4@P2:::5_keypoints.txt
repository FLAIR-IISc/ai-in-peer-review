- Proposes a non-greedy global training method for decision trees with oblique splits using a latent-variable surrogate objective optimized by SGD.
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference variant restricting to a Hamming ball (radius 1) to reduce per-example complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p).
- Experiments compare the method to greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique) and CO2 across several multiclass datasets.
- Connects tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables and builds on upper-bound techniques from latent/structural SVM literature.
- Preserves inference efficiency of trees while enabling global coordination of splits.
- Strength: principled latent-variable formulation and convex–concave upper bound for tree learning.
- Strength: practical algorithmic advance via fast loss-augmented inference making deeper trees feasible.
- Strength: sensible SGD training with per-row norm constraints and a Stable SGD (SSGD) variant to address leaf inactivity.
- Strength: empirical evaluation includes multiple datasets and comparisons to strong greedy baselines.
- Weakness: empirical gains are modest and dataset-dependent, sometimes small relative to extra training cost.
- Weakness: insufficient specification of SSGD — need pseudocode and precise definition of “active” vs. “inactive” leaves (e.g., assignment counts over a window).
- Weakness: Figure 2 unclear—must state whether curves show SGD or SSGD and separate/annotate plots if mixed.
- Weakness: fast Hamming-ball restriction may affect surrogate tightness; need discussion and ablation comparing full vs. fast loss-augmented inference.
- Weakness: sensitivity to initialization not analyzed; report results for random vs. CO2 initialization.
- Weakness: unclear handling of leaf parameter constraints — clarify whether simplex projection is used or softmax suffices.
- Weakness: comparisons limited to single trees; report results with pruning, bagging, or boosting to assess additive benefits in realistic settings.
- Quality assessment: sound theoretical development but mixed empirical evidence for substantial accuracy gains over greedy oblique trees.
- Clarity assessment: generally well written but needs clearer exposition of SSGD, “active” leaves, Θ updates, and linking Figure 1 and algorithmic variants to main text.
- Originality assessment: novel combination of latent-variable formulation and fast loss-augmented inference within global tree optimization.
- Significance assessment: potentially important if global optimization consistently improves performance or tree size, but current experiments are inconclusive.
- Pros summary: elegant formulation, efficient training via fast inference and SGD, clear regularization on split weights, and empirical improvements on some benchmarks.
- Cons summary: modest/inconsistent gains, inadequate SSGD and active-leaf details, limited sensitivity analyses (ν, initialization, surrogate choice), and lack of ensemble/pruning context.
- Minor comments/typos: clarify line 407–408 about features evaluated per node; use “max” not “argmax” in Eqs (7–9); cite Figure 1 in text; fix grammatical errors at lines 141, 238, 301, 360, 404.
- Supplementary corrections: fix notation at lines 29–30 (sgn and function arguments).
- Overall recommendation: paper is technically solid and well-motivated but needs clearer SSGD/Θ implementation details, ablations (full vs. fast surrogate, initialization), and runtime/accuracy trade-off analyses to strengthen empirical claims.
