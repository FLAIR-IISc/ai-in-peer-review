- Paper introduces joint optimization of decision-tree splits and posteriors as a single tractable optimization problem, a novel and important direction.
- Strong writing and clear derivation overall, but theoretical reasoning has potential issues.
- Experimental section and its presentation require improvement.
- Novelty is high: joint training of all decision tree parameters versus greedy layer-wise methods.
- Significance is substantial, though some shortcomings weaken the contribution.
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss bound (Eq. 7); the provided argument (lines 216–217) appears incomplete and can be contradicted by counterexamples.
- The proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement does not address the identified counterexample, indicating an incomplete theoretical justification.
- Some leaves receive no data points, which is puzzling and may indicate problems with the objective or initialization; current fixed-assignment heuristic seems suboptimal.
- The efficient loss-augmented inference design choices need more discussion, including the impact of restricting to a Hamming ball and clarity on what 'leaves' refers to.
- For depth up to 16, exhaustive greedy search may be feasible; authors should quantify the performance trade-offs of their inference restriction.
- Missing baseline: no comparison to standard greedy training with non-axis-aligned splits (without initialization).
- Requirement to pre-specify tree depth is a limitation that needs deeper discussion and empirical study of its influence.
- Suggest replacing argmax with max in the bound reasoning; include runtime comparisons and broader experiments to strengthen the paper.
- Reviewer is open to reassessing the score pending a compelling rebuttal addressing derivation issues and expanded empirical evaluation.
