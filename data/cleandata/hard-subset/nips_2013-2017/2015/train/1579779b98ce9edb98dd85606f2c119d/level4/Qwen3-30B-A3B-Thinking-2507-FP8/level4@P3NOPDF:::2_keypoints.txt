- Proposes joint training of split decisions and leaf probabilities via a single optimization with a tractable upper bound.  
- Critical theoretical flaw: derivation of the empirical loss bound (Eq. 7) is incorrect — the claim that the RHS always increases for all g ≠ sgn(Wx) is false.  
- Counterexample (Wx = [5; -5], g = [1;1]) shows the RHS can be lower than the target loss, violating the upper-bound requirement.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is incomplete and does not address the counterexample; a corrected, rigorous proof is required.  
- Aside from the bound issue, the mathematical derivation is otherwise logical.  
- Experimental section lacks sufficient detail and comparisons to support claims.  
- Ambiguity: the term “leaves” in the Hamming ball context is undefined (tree leaves vs. feature indices).  
- Some tree leaves appear unassigned during training (Figure 2), which is unusual and needs justification or analysis.  
- The heuristic for assigning data points to leaves is labeled “sub-optimal” but is unmotivated; it should be related to established methods (e.g., EM or CCCP).  
- Missing critical baseline: greedy training of non-axis-aligned trees (without initialization) to isolate the benefit of joint training.  
- Tree depth must be specified a priori; authors should analyze depth sensitivity (ablation across depths/datasets).  
- Efficiency concern: Hamming ball loss-augmented inference (up to 2^16 candidates) needs justification and quantification of its performance impact.  
- Minor fixes: replace argmax with max in the loss bound, clarify runtime comparisons with methods like CART, and explicitly define “leaves.”  
- Recommendation: idea is promising but acceptance requires a corrected loss-bound proof, inclusion of the missing baseline, and depth/clarity analyses before the reviewer will revise their score.
