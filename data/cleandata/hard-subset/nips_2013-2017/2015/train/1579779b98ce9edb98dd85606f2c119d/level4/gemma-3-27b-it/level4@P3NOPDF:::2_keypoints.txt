- Proposes a novel joint training method for all decision tree parameters (splits and posterior probabilities) via a single optimization formulation.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented but contains a potential flaw in the theoretical justification.
- Core theoretical derivation is clean, but the experimental presentation and analysis are insufficient.
- Joint training for decision trees is an original and important departure from greedy methods.
- The approach is significant but tempered by theoretical concerns and limited empirical validation.
- Empirical loss bound derivation appears incorrect (counterexample shows the claimed inequality can fail); replacing argmax with max likely needed.
- Several leaves receive no data in experiments, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization; fixed assignment heuristic needs better motivation (e.g., EM/CCCP parallels).
- Claimed efficiency of loss-augmented inference is unclear given shallow trees (depth ≤16) where brute-force is feasible; impact of Hamming-ball restriction and definition of “leaves” need clarification and generalization tests.
- Experiments lack a crucial baseline: greedy training for non-axis-aligned splits without initialization.
- Method requires specifying tree depth a priori; sensitivity to depth should be discussed and evaluated more thoroughly.
- Overall: promising contribution but requires correction/clarification of the loss bound, stronger baselines, deeper experimental analysis (including runtime), and a satisfactory rebuttal to raise the score.
