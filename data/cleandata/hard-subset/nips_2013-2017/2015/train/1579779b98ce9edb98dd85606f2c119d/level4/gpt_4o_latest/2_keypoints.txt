- Paper proposes jointly optimizing all decision-tree parameters (splits and posteriors) via a single optimization reformulated and approximated for tractability.  
- Manuscript is well-written but contains potential reasoning/derivation issues.  
- Derivation is clearly presented, but experiments and their presentation need enhancement.  
- Joint training of decision trees is original and addresses an underexplored problem.  
- Proposed approach is valuable but has limitations that need addressing.  
- Major concern: the empirical loss bound derivation (Eq. 7, lines 216f) is unclear/potentially flawed; provided example shows the bound may not hold and the supplementary proof is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points; this unexpected behavior suggests problems with the objective or initialization and challenges the fixed-leaf assignment strategy.  
- Suggestion: justify assignments or optimization via frameworks like EM or concave-convex procedures used in clustering.  
- Question the necessity of efficient loss-augmented inference given manageable tree depths (e.g., 2^16 evaluations); unclear impact of restricting search to a Hamming ball and ambiguous use of the term “leaves.”  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard greedy training baseline with non-axis-aligned splits (without initialization).  
- Limitation: tree depth must be pre-specified; need more experiments on the effect of depth.  
- Minor suggestions: replace argmax with max for clarity, add runtime comparisons, and provide a more detailed evaluation.  
- Overall: promising joint-training idea but experimental evaluation is limited and a derivation flaw must be addressed; reviewer may revise score after a thorough rebuttal.
