- Novel framing: Treats decision tree induction as structured prediction to search for optimal trees of fixed depth, a novel and well-motivated idea.  
- Empirical gains: Method consistently outperforms greedy tree building in test accuracy, but improvements are typically modest.  
- Advantage: Allows modeling dependencies between splits, offering better exploration of tree space than greedy methods.  
- Experimental scope: Evaluation covers multiple datasets and baselines, showing consistent but often small accuracy improvements.  
- Analysis gaps: Needs detailed breakdown of where gains occur and discussion of failure cases or datasets where performance is comparable or worse.  
- Computational concerns: Lacks quantified runtime/complexity analysis (scaling with depth and dataset size) and clarity on feasibility for larger/deeper trees.  
- Related work: Connections to structured prediction and tree-structured outputs should be drawn more explicitly, with clearer differentiation from existing methods.  
- Presentation fixes: Some figures need larger fonts/clearer labels and a brief glossary of notation would help readers.  
- Recommendation: Solid contribution worthy of acceptance, contingent on addressing complexity, dataset-specific performance, and related-work comparisons.
