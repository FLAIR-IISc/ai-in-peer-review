- Proposes global decision-tree optimization as latent structured prediction using sign vectors and a differentiable non-convex upper bound optimized by SGD with loss-augmented inference.  
- Key contributions: casting tree optimization as structured prediction and providing an efficient algorithm for the resulting objective.  
- Novel and promising approach that opens interesting research directions.  
- Gradient updates are sparse—each step appears to modify only a single node’s weights—suggesting computational and methodological implications.  
- This sparsity invites a “tree growing” idea (initialize weights at zero and activate nodes gradually), but the paper lacks analysis comparing this to greedy strategies.  
- Ambiguity in handling zero-weight nodes: defaulting to the right branch seems arbitrary; treating zero nodes as leaves that can later split is a natural alternative that needs precise definition.  
- Zero-initialization and its dynamics should be explored experimentally and theoretically as a core aspect of the method.  
- Interpretability concerns: L2 regularization likely yields dense, uninterpretable splits; L1 might induce sparsity but could hurt accuracy—this trade-off must be evaluated.  
- Experimental weaknesses: baseline greedy method details (including pruning/tuning) are not provided and should be tuned comparably.  
- Additional simple baselines for global optimization (e.g., many greedy trees on random data subsets with model selection) should be included.  
- Clarity issues: inference procedure (Sec. 6.2 and 6.4) could be described more succinctly/explicitly; title mentions “forests” but work targets single trees.  
- Overall verdict: innovative and thought-provoking but practical utility remains unclear due to interpretability trade-offs and insufficient experimental comparison.
