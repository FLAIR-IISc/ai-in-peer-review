- Proposes a non-greedy joint-training algorithm for decision trees optimizing splits and leaf distributions under one global objective via a convex–concave surrogate and SGD.  
- Relates oblique linear splits to latent structured prediction and derives efficient loss-augmented inference with O(d^2 p) time, enabling deeper trees.  
- Experiments show non-greedy trees often generalize better than greedy baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique, CO2 depth-1).  
- Connects to prior work on latent structured prediction, max-margin trees, oblique splits, and soft-split models like HME, and contrasts with greedy induction methods.  
- Formulation and fast inference are elegant and novel; the derivation is mostly clear but the empirical loss bound presentation has issues.  
- Theory is generally well written, but Eq. 7 and some terminology (e.g., “leaves”) and explanations need clarification.  
- The approach is original and significant if the bound and optimization are sound, potentially influential for joint tree optimization.  
- Empirical evidence is promising but limited; missing important baselines, ablations, runtime comparisons, and depth-sensitivity analysis.  
- Pros: addresses greedy induction limitations with a principled joint optimization framework.  
- Pros: novel connection to latent structured prediction and a tractable surrogate objective.  
- Pros: efficient per-example gradients (O(d)) and loss-augmented inference (O(d^2 p)).  
- Pros: shows empirical gains and appears less prone to overfitting as depth increases.  
- Cons: derivation and interpretation of the loss upper bound (Eq. 7) are confusing and possibly incorrect as stated.  
- Cons: some leaves receive no data, indicating potential instability or optimization/objective issues.  
- Cons: restricting loss-augmented inference to a Hamming ball may bias solutions; benefit and bias not fully quantified.  
- Cons: missing baseline—greedy training of oblique splits from scratch—to isolate benefits of joint training.  
- Cons: tree depth must be fixed beforehand; practical impact and depth-selection strategy are under-discussed.  
- Cons: experimental evaluation lacks runtime comparisons and ablation studies (ν, initialization, SSGD vs SGD).  
- Detailed comment: Eq. 7’s claimed upper bound appears invalid in some Wx examples; proof in supplement seems incomplete.  
- Detailed comment: inactive leaves and the proposed stabilization heuristic look ad hoc; motivate via EM/CCCP analogies.  
- Detailed comment: quantify the runtime and accuracy tradeoff of full vs Hamming-ball loss-augmented inference and clarify “leaves” terminology in that context.  
- Detailed comment: include greedy oblique-split baseline and provide validation curves/sensitivity for depth and ν to show implicit pruning.  
- Post-rebuttal: author clarification helped; suggest replacing argmax with max to avoid ambiguity in the bound statement.  
- Overall: important and potentially impactful approach, but needs clearer theoretical presentation of the bound and a more thorough experimental evaluation to be convincing.
