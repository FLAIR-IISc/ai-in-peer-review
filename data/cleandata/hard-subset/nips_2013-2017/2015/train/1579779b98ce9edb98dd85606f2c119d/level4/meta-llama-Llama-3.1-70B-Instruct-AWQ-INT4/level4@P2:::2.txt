The paper "Efficient Non-greedy Optimization of Decision Trees" proposes a novel method for jointly training all parameters of decision trees, which is an important topic since current methods train trees in a greedy manner, layer by layer. The task is phrased as a single optimization problem which is then upper-bounded and approximated to obtain a tractable formulation.

The paper is well-written, and the derivation is clean. However, there might be flaws in the reasoning. Specifically, the argument in lines 216-217 is confusing, and it seems that there might be a flaw in the derivation of the empirical loss bound. The example provided in the original review illustrates the potential issue: when Wx = [5;-5], choosing g = [1;1] results in a smaller value of the right-hand side of Equation (7), which could possibly be smaller than the left-hand side and hence not a valid upper bound.

The paper's clarity could be improved by addressing this potential issue and providing a more detailed explanation of the derivation. Additionally, the experiments and their presentation could be improved. For instance, the authors could provide more details on the experimental setup, such as the specific datasets used, the hyperparameters tuned, and the evaluation metrics employed.

The paper's originality is high, as it investigates joint training for decision trees, which is an important topic. The proposed solution is valuable, although there seem to be some shortcomings. For example, the method requires the depth of the tree to be specified ahead of time, which is a limitation that should be discussed more carefully.

Some specific comments on the paper include:

* The authors should address the potential flaw in the derivation of the empirical loss bound and provide a more detailed explanation of the reasoning.
* The authors should provide more details on the experimental setup and evaluation metrics employed.
* The authors should discuss the limitation of requiring the depth of the tree to be specified ahead of time and provide a more careful experimental evaluation regarding the depth parameter.
* The authors could provide a baseline that trains non-axis aligned split functions of trees in a standard greedy manner, i.e., without any initialization.
* The authors could provide more results on the importance of the efficient loss-augmented inference and the impact of the restricted search space introduced by the Hamming ball on performance.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed method is valuable. However, there are some potential issues that need to be addressed, and the experimental evaluation could be improved.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel method for jointly training all parameters of decision trees, which is an important topic.
* The paper is well-written, and the derivation is clean.
* The proposed solution is valuable, and the experimental results show promising performance.

Arguments against acceptance:

* There might be a flaw in the derivation of the empirical loss bound.
