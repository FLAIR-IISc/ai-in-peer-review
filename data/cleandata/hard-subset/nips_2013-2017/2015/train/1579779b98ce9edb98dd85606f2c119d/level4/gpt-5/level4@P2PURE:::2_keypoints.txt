- Paper proposes jointly training all decision tree parameters via a single optimization with an upper-bounded, tractable approximation.
- Writing and derivations are generally clear, but there may be flaws in the loss-bound reasoning.
- Experiments and their presentation are limited and need improvement.
- Joint training of decision trees is an important and novel direction.
- Proposed solution is valuable but has shortcomings that affect significance.

- Potential flaw in empirical loss bound: the argument around l.216f is confusing and a counterexample shows the bound may not hold; proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement seems incomplete.
- Some leaves receive no data points, which is unusual and may indicate problems with the objective or initialization; the fixed-assignment heuristic seems ad hoc and possibly suboptimal—could be motivated via EM/CCCP-style methods.
- Efficient loss-augmented inference: restricting search to a Hamming ball may affect performance; exhaustive search up to depth 16 is feasible, so more analysis and generalization of the Hamming-ball results are needed; clarify what “leaves” means in this context.
- Missing baseline: a greedy method training non–axis-aligned (oblique) splits without initialization should be included.
- Tree depth must be specified in advance; discuss this limitation and analyze sensitivity to depth more thoroughly.
- Minor suggestion: replace “argmax” with “max” in the loss-bound expression.
- Request for runtime comparison and more careful empirical evaluation; reviewer may adjust score pending a rebuttal addressing derivation and experiments.
