- Proposes global optimization of decision trees by reformulating as (latent) structured prediction with sign-vector latent variables.  
- Defines a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and approximates it with a differentiable upper bound optimized by SGD.  
- Provides efficient algorithms for the required loss-augmented inference and shows experiments versus a standard greedy method.  
- Main contributions: novel casting as structured prediction and devising efficient optimization algorithms.  
- Notes sparse gradient updates (often changing only one node’s weight per step), which is an interesting computational property that is underexplored.  
- Raises the idea of growing model complexity (e.g., infinitely deep tree starting from zero weights) and questions how this relates to greedy strategies and convergence behavior.  
- Points out unclear handling of zero-weighted nodes (paper suggests taking the right branch); recommends explicitly defining behavior and considering outputting a decision to allow later nonzero weights.  
- Questions practical motivation: method may produce non-interpretable dense weights due to L2 regularization; suggests exploring L1 for sparsity and interpretability.  
- Experimental comparisons lack detail: baseline pruning strategies and tuning are not described, and comparisons to simple global approaches (e.g., many greedy runs) are missing.  
- Inference explanation needs clarification (Sec. 6.2 could be summarized as optimizing over g per leaf then over leaves; Sec. 6.4 also needs clearer exposition).  
- Paper omits discussion/extension to decision forests.  
- Post-rebuttal: author response indicates random weight initialization; reviewer recommends exploring zero initialization and gradual weight growth as a promising direction.
