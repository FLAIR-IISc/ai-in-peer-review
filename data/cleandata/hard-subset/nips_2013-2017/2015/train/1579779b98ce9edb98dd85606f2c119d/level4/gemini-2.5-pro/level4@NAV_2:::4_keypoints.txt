- Novel formulation: reframes global training of oblique decision trees as a latent structured prediction problem, which is an elegant, novel connection.  
- Objective: derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a tractable surrogate for global tree optimization.  
- Optimization: minimizes the surrogate via SGD and provides an efficient loss-augmented inference algorithm.  
- Empirical result: non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines on several benchmarks.  
- Sparse updates: SGD updates only a small subset of node weights per step, suggesting computational benefits and potential for adaptive tree growth—an idea not fully explored.  
- Missing analysis: the paper does not analyze whether zero-initialized growth would mimic greedy methods or converge to finite trees with regularization.  
- Ambiguity: unclear handling of split nodes with zero weight vectors (implied arbitrary default branch); needs a principled definition analogous to pruning.  
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization likely yields dense, non-interpretable oblique splits, undermining a key motivation for single trees.  
- Regularization trade-off: switching to L1 could induce sparsity and interpretability, but empirical effects on performance are untested.  
- Experimental weaknesses: baseline descriptions lack key details (e.g., pruning strategies) and may not be as thoroughly tuned as the proposed method.  
- Missing comparisons: should compare to other simple global strategies (e.g., best tree from multiple greedy runs on random subsets).  
- Clarity: inference procedures could be described more explicitly—e.g., state loss-augmented inference as maximizing over decision paths per leaf.  
- Scope/title mismatch: title’s mention of “forests” is misleading because the work and experiments focus only on single trees.
