- Proposes a novel reformulation: global decision-tree optimization as latent structured prediction with sign-vector latents and a differentiable upper-bound on a non-differentiable loss.  
- Provides efficient algorithms for loss-augmented inference and SGD-based minimization of the non-convex surrogate.  
- Key contribution: casting global tree optimization as structured prediction and devising efficient optimization techniques—novel and theoretically interesting.  
- Sparse gradient updates: each SGD step modifies only one node’s weight, enabling potential tree growth from zero initialization.  
- Paper insufficiently explores implications of sparse updates—whether growth improves over greedy strategies or yields bounded tree sizes remains unclear.  
- Critical ambiguity on zero-weight nodes: authors assume the right branch is taken, which is problematic; zero weights should be treated as pending splits and linked to pruning.  
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization yields dense splits, undermining the main value of trees; L1 could enforce sparsity but may hurt performance—this tradeoff is untested.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks detail: pruning strategy for the greedy baseline is unspecified while multiple regularization values are tested for the proposed method.  
- Missing baselines: should compare against greedy trees with varied pruning and simple global baselines (e.g., training on random subsets and selecting by validation).  
- Clarity issues: Sections 6.2 and 6.4 need clearer exposition—specifically clarify that the objective is maximized over leaves after maximizing over splits per leaf.  
- Title mismatch: paper mentions “forests” but only addresses single trees.  
- Post-rebuttal note: random initialization avoids zero-weight issues in practice, but the theoretically appealing zero-initialization growth strategy remains unexplored and should be pursued.
