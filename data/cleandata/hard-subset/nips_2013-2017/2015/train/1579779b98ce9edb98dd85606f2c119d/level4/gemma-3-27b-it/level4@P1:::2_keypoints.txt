- Proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters via a single optimization with tractable upper-bound approximations.
- Technically sound and well-written, but potential issues exist with the reasoning behind the loss bound.
- Derivation is generally clean; presentation of experiments and results needs improvement.
- Joint training for decision trees is a novel and valuable direction.
- Proposed method is significant and promising compared to greedy approaches, though shortcomings remain.
- Potential flaw in empirical loss bound: counterexample shows RHS could be smaller than the loss, and the proof appears incomplete.
- Some leaves receive no data during training, suggesting problems with the objective or initialization; fixing assignments is a suboptimal heuristic.
- Fast loss-augmented inference is efficient, but impact of restricting search to the Hamming ball (esp. for depth 16) is unclear and may not generalize; clarify what “leaves” means in that experiment.
- Missing baseline: greedy training of non-axis-aligned split functions without axis-aligned initialization.
- Tree depth is fixed a priori, which is a significant limitation requiring more discussion and experiments on depth effects.
- Recommend replacing argmax with max for clarity, adding runtime comparisons, and providing a more comprehensive evaluation.
