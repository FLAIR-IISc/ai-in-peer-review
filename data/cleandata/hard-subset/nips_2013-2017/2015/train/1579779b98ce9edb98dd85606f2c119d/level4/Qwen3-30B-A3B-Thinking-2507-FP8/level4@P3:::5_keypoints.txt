- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning method that jointly optimizes all split functions and leaf parameters instead of greedy node-by-node learning.  
- Connects decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Shows the surrogate objective’s gradient can be computed in O(d) per example, enabling efficient SGD training of deep trees.  
- Main technical contribution: reformulates the loss upper bound to reduce complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p).  
- Builds on prior work (Bennett, Hierarchical Mixture of Experts, CO2) and extends CO2 by optimizing the bound globally rather than greedily.  
- Theoretical analysis (Proposition 1) justifies the regularization approach and supports the formulation.  
- Experiments validate feasibility but report only modest performance gains over greedy baselines on most datasets.  
- Paper would benefit from deeper analysis of when the non-greedy approach yields substantial advantages.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-structured, but some sections need greater precision.  
- Stable SGD (SSGD) description is insufficient—lack of detail on leaf assignments and updates; pseudocode is recommended.  
- Term "active leaves" is used without definition and should be clarified.  
- The projection step for Θ in Algorithm 1 needs practical implementation details (simplex projection).  
- Originality: novel connection to structured prediction and a scalable, general framework applicable to any loss.  
- Significance: provides a principled alternative to greedy trees, though practical impact is limited by modest gains and O(d^2 p) per-step cost.  
- Minor issues: confusing phrase about "number of features evaluated," incorrect use of "argmax" vs "max" in equations, Figure 1 referenced but not discussed, and several grammatical/notation errors in main and supplementary text.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound contribution with efficient implementation and feasible results; recommended for acceptance with minor clarifications and fixes.
