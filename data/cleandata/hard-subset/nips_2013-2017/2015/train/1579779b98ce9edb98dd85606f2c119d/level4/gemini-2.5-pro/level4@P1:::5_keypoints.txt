- Paper proposes a non-greedy algorithm for learning oblique decision trees that jointly optimizes split weights and leaf class distributions.
- Core formulation casts tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.
- Authors derive a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective.
- Objective is L2-regularized and optimized with stochastic gradient descent (SGD).
- Main strength: elegant, novel connection between decision-tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.
- Main weakness: empirical results are unconvincing—clear wins on only a few datasets and marginal gains on others.
- Practical concern: method is considerably more computationally intensive than greedy baselines, weakening the case for adoption.
- Clarification needed: the Stable SGD (SSGD) variant and the process for assigning data points to leaves should be explained in detail, with pseudocode.
- Clarification needed: Figure 2 should state whether results use standard SGD or the SSGD variant.
- Clarification needed: the term "active leaf" is used but not precisely defined.
- Clarification needed: Algorithm 1 (line 10) Θ update—explain practical implementation and the projection onto the probability simplex; state that each row of Θ becomes a valid probability distribution.
- Minor fix: in Equations (7),(8),(9) replace argmax with max.
- Minor fix: Figure 1 is not referenced in the main text.

- Clarification needed: Lines 407–408—specify if "number of features evaluated" refers to Random Forest–style feature subsampling or something else.

- Overall summary: elegant and principled formulation, but inconsistent empirical benefits and higher computational cost reduce practical significance.
