- Proposes a non-greedy, global optimization for decision trees with linear splits, optimizing split weights and leaf distributions jointly rather than sequentially.  
- Frames decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective.  
- Regularizes split weights with L2 constraints and uses stochastic gradient descent to minimize the surrogate, enabling efficient training of deeper trees.  
- Strength: elegant, well-motivated formulation that links decision trees to structured prediction and is clearly written.  
- Weakness: empirical results do not convincingly show clear advantages over greedy training; improvements occur on few datasets and come with higher computational cost.  
- Recommendation: more comprehensive comparisons and analyses are needed to identify when global optimization yields meaningful gains.  
- Clarification needed: describe the "stable SGD" variant in detail (pseudo-code) and explain how data points are assigned to and updated for leaves.  
- Clarification needed: state whether Figure 2 uses standard SGD or stable SGD and define the term "active" leaves precisely.  
- Clarification needed: specify how the projection of Θ onto the simplex in Algorithm 1 is implemented and that it ensures each row sums to one.  
- Minor correction: line 407–408 misleadingly mentions tuning "number of features evaluated" for axis-aligned trees—standard trees evaluate all features per node.  
- Minor correction: Equations (7)–(9) should use "max" rather than "argmax."  
- Minor issues: Figure 1 is referenced but not discussed; grammatical/typo fixes at lines 141, 238, 301, 360, and 404.  
- Supplementary fixes: line 29 should read "max{g = sgn...}" and line 30 should read "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^Tf(sgn(Wx)), y)".  
- Overall: theoretical contribution is sound and interesting, but the paper needs stronger empirical validation and clearer exposition of technical details.
