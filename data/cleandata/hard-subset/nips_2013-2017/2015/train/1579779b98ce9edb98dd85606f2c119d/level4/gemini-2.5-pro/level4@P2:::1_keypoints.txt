- Proposes a novel framework casting non-greedy decision tree learning as structured prediction with the sequence of binary decisions as latent variables.  
- Derives a continuous convex-concave upper bound surrogate for the discontinuous empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm reducing complexity from exponential to quadratic in tree depth, enabling deep-tree optimization.  
- Empirical evaluation on multi-class benchmarks shows consistent but modest improvements over greedy baselines.  
- Original and elegant formulation that reframes global tree optimization within a principled machine learning paradigm.  
- Strong technical contribution: sound derivation of the surrogate objective and identification/solution of the computational bottleneck.  
- Paper is very clearly written and well organized, making the formulation and optimization easy to follow.  
- Potentially significant: framework supports arbitrary losses/regularization and could inspire follow-up work (e.g., kernels, L1 regularization).  
- Experimental gains are modest relative to the increased training complexity, raising questions about practical adoption.  
- Claim that the method is less prone to overfitting is insufficiently supported; global optimization can increase overfitting risk.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of generalization (e.g., train/test gap across depths) to substantiate regularization effects.  
- No ablation on initialization sensitivity—the method is initialized with a greedy CO2 procedure but robustness to other initializations is not evaluated.  
- Suggests a hybrid refinement: re-optimize final-layer splits and leaf parameters greedily after global optimization to improve performance.  
- Recommends expanding discussion of the regularization parameter ν and its relation to model generalization and active leaves.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality, original work with clear exposition and important technical advances; acceptance recommended despite modest empirical gains.
