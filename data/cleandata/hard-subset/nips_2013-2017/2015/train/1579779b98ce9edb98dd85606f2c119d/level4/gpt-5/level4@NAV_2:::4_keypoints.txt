- Proposes globally optimizing decision trees via a latent structured-prediction formulation where latent variables are sign vectors for internal binary decisions.  
- Replaces the original non-differentiable empirical loss with a differentiable upper-bound surrogate, enabling SGD-based optimization.  
- Introduces efficient loss-augmented inference procedures required for SGD updates.  
- Main contributions: structured-prediction formulation for global tree learning and tractable optimization algorithms.  
- Method is novel and thought-provoking but raises several practical questions.  
- Practical reservations about motivation and the magnitude of improvement over simple baselines.  
- Sparse gradient updates (often modifying only a single node) are computationally interesting and suggest unexplored options like growing a tree from zero-initialized weights.  
- Unclear whether a growth procedure would degenerate to greedy induction or, with regularization, converge to a finite tree—requires deeper analysis.  
- Ambiguity about handling zero-weight nodes: defaulting to the right branch seems questionable; better to allow leaf prediction until a split is introduced.  
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization yields dense oblique splits; switching to L1 could improve sparsity but may harm performance—this tradeoff needs empirical study.  
- Experimental reporting lacks important baseline details, especially pruning strategies and tuning for the greedy baseline.  
- Should compare to simple “globalization” baselines (e.g., many greedy trees on subsets then select by target loss).  
- Clarity could be improved: inference sections (e.g., Sec. 6.2 and 6.4) can be streamlined and made more explicit.  
- Title mentions forests but the paper does not address ensembles.  
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight initialization remains a pivotal issue; authors are encouraged to study gradual growth from zero weights more thoroughly.
