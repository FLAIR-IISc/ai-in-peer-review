

The authors propose an interesting approach to finding the optimal decision tree of fixed depth by framing it as a structured prediction problem. Their method outperforms conventional greedy algorithms for decision tree construction in terms of test accuracy, though the improvements are modest. While the structured prediction formulation provides a principled alternative to heuristic search, the marginal gains in predictive performance raise questions about the practical significance of the proposed technique. The experimental results demonstrate consistent but small advantages over standard baselines across multiple datasets, suggesting the approach may offer theoretical value without substantial real-world impact. The authors should more clearly articulate the scenarios where this method would be preferable to established techniques, particularly given the computational overhead of the structured prediction framework. The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of the trade-offs between model complexity, training time, and the limited accuracy gains observed.
