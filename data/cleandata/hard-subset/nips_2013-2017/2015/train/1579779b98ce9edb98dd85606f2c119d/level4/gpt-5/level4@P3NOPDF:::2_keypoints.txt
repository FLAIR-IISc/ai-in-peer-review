- Paper proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters via a single upper-bounded objective to improve over greedy induction.  
- Writing and derivations are clear, but there is a concerning gap in the empirical loss upper-bound proof that needs stronger justification.  
- Originality is good: joint training of splits and leaf posteriors is an important direction beyond greedy methods.  
- Significance is promising but tempered by theoretical and experimental limitations.  
- Major issue: the claimed empirical loss upper bound (Eq. 7) appears incorrect as stated; counterexample shows the RHS can be smaller than claimed—proof and assumptions must be clarified or corrected.  
- Request: explicitly state any constraints or margin assumptions on g (or restrict the search set) required for the bound, and update proofs and implications if the bound changes.  
- Some leaves receive no data (empty leaves), which may indicate problems with objective, regularization, or initialization; report frequency and dataset/depth dependence.  
- Ask whether the objective implicitly prunes leaves, and recommend considering an EM/CCCP-style latent-assignment update rather than the current heuristic.  
- Efficiency claim: quantify runtime benefit of Hamming-ball restricted loss-augmented inference vs exhaustive search, report accuracy trade-offs as radius varies, and provide a runtime breakdown (inference vs updates).  
- Clarify and standardize terminology for “leaves” vs “leaf assignments/leaf codes.”  
- Missing baseline: include a greedy oblique tree baseline (non-axis-aligned splits optimized locally) to isolate gains from global joint training.  
- Fixed-depth requirement is a practical limitation—evaluate performance vs depth, discuss over/underfitting and scaling, and consider mitigation (sparsity/pruning/early stopping).  
- Experimental gaps: add runtime comparisons to greedy axis-aligned and oblique trees, report incidence and impact of empty leaves, sensitivity to Hamming radius and depth, and clarify dataset sizes, feature dims, and hyperparameter selection.  
- Presentation fixes: ensure bound statements precisely match proofs, replace “argmax” with “max” in Eq. (7), and define/standardize notation at first use.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer’s primary concern about Eq. (7) was addressed; camera-ready should reflect conditions and corrections, but more runtime and broader evaluation are still needed.  
- Overall: method is principled and potentially impactful if the bound is corrected and empirical evaluation (especially greedy oblique baseline and runtime/empty-leaf analysis) is strengthened.
