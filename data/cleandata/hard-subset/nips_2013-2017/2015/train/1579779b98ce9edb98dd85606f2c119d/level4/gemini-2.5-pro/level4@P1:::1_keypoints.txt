- Reframes decision-tree learning as structured prediction with latent binary split vectors (path as latent variable).  
- Derives a convex–concave upper bound surrogate objective enabling joint optimization of all split functions and leaf parameters.  
- Optimizes the global objective with SGD and introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces complexity for deep trees.  
- Offers a principled, non-greedy alternative to traditional node-wise greedy algorithms like CART and ID3.  
- Strength — highly original and elegant formulation that moves beyond local greedy heuristics.  
- Strength — paper is very clear and well-written with consistent notation and thorough exposition.  
- Strength — solid technical contributions: the surrogate objective derivation and the efficient inference algorithm.  
- Weakness — empirical improvements over strong greedy baselines are modest, raising practical-utility concerns given added complexity.  
- Weakness — limited analysis of overfitting and generalization behavior relative to greedy methods despite L2 regularization.  
- Suggestion — try a hybrid: use global optimization for coarse structure then re-optimize final splits/leaves greedily.  
- Suggestion — add experiments (e.g., training vs. validation error over epochs) to directly assess overfitting and generalization.  
- Final verdict — positive recommendation: strong, publishable paper whose theoretical contributions outweigh the currently modest empirical gains.
