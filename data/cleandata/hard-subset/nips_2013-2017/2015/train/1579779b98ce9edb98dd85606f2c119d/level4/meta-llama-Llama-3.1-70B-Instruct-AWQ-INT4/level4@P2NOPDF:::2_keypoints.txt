- Proposes jointly training all decision-tree parameters by formulating a single optimization problem and deriving a tractable upper-bound approximation.
- Paper is well-written but may contain a flaw in the empirical loss bound derivation and has shortcomings in experimental presentation.
- Mathematical derivation is clean, but experimental setup and reporting need more detail.
- The topic and approach are original and potentially impactful for decision-tree training.
- Significance is limited by a possibly invalid derivation and a somewhat limited experimental evaluation.
- Potential flaw in Proposition 1: the loss upper bound may be invalid in cases where the RHS of Eq. (7) is smaller than the LHS (example with Wx = [5; -5]).
- Some tree leaves receive no data points; the fixed-assignment heuristic seems suboptimal—EM or concave-convex methods may be better.
- Efficiency of loss-augmented inference is unclear: for depth ≤16 exhaustive search is feasible, and the effect of restricting search with a Hamming ball needs more evidence; the term “leaves” should be defined.
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard greedy training of non-axis-aligned split functions.
- Need to address the limitation that tree depth must be specified in advance and provide depth-sensitivity experiments.
- Minor suggestions: replace argmax with max in the loss bound, include runtime comparisons, and provide a more careful empirical evaluation.
- Overall: interesting and novel work but requires clarification of the derivation and stronger, clearer experiments (rebuttal should address these).
