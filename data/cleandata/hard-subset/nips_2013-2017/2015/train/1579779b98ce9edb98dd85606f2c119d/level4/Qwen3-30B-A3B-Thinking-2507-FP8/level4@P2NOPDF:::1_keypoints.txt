- Proposes a novel nonconvex relaxation that reformulates global optimization of fixed-structure decision trees into a continuous program, avoiding greedy heuristics.  
- The core contribution is conceptually novel and theoretically elegant, making an NP-hard problem tractable via standard continuous optimization.  
- Exposition and technical presentation are exceptionally clear and accessible despite mathematical depth.  
- Empirical results are weak: only marginal, often non-significant improvements over baselines (e.g., CART, C4.5) on standard datasets.  
- The paper lacks sufficient analysis of overfitting and generalization, and does not compare against explicit regularization techniques for tree complexity.  
- Suggested hybrid fix: after global optimization, omit the final split and re-optimize the last internal node and its leaf with greedy methods to improve performance.  
- Rebuttal addresses experimental design concerns and authors outline reasonable future work, including the hybrid idea.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—the theoretical advance and potential impact outweigh modest empirical shortcomings.
