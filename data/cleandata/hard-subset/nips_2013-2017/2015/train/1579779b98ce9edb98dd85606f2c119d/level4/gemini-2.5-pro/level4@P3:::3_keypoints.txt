- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm for learning decision trees by treating the input path as a latent variable in a structured prediction formulation.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss (latent structural SVM style) used as a surrogate objective optimized with SGD.  
- Presents a "fast loss-augmented inference" method that reduces SGD update complexity from exponential to quadratic in tree depth, making deep-tree training practical.  
- Empirical results on multiclass benchmarks show the non-greedy approach consistently outperforms greedy baselines and generalizes better.  
- Technical formulation is sound and well-motivated, with correct surrogate derivation and careful handling of computational complexity.  
- Experimental evaluation is solid, comparing relevant baselines (axis-aligned, OC1, random oblique) and analyzing the regularization parameter ν and pruning behavior.  
- Paper is very well-written and organized, providing sufficient detail for reproducing the core algorithm.  
- The description of the fast loss-augmented inference is clear and detailed enough for experts to understand.  
- Highly original: establishes a novel connection between decision tree optimization and latent structured prediction, leveraging margin-based methods.  
- Significant: offers a principled, computationally feasible framework for global decision tree optimization with clear avenues for extensions (losses, sparsity, kernels).  
- Limitation: reported test accuracy improvements are consistent but relatively modest across datasets.  
- Limitation: evaluation focuses solely on single-tree performance and lacks comparisons to ensemble methods (Random Forests, GBMs), leaving practical impact unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, strong technical soundness, practical feasibility, and consistent empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest performance gains and missing comparisons to standard ensemble baselines.
