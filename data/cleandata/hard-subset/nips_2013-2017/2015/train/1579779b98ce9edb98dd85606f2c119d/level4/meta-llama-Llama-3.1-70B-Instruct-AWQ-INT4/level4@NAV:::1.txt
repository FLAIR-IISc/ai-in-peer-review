After considering the feedback and deliberating with fellow reviewers, I reaffirm that this paper merits publication, primarily due to its innovative approach and potential to stimulate further research in related areas. Although the experimental results fell short of expectations, I believe the current score is justified, as even more compelling results would likely have earned an even higher rating. The paper presents a lucid exposition of a nonconvex relaxation technique for globally optimizing decision trees with a predefined structure. While the approach is elegant, its experimental outcomes are somewhat underwhelming. A potential refinement could involve discarding the final split in each tree, followed by standard optimization of the resulting leaf node, which may yield improved results. Furthermore, it is crucial to assess whether the global optimization method is more prone to overfitting.
