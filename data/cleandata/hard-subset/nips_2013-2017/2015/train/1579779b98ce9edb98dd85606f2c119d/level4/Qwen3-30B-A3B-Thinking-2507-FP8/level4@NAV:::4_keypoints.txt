- Reformulates global decision-tree optimization as (latent) structured prediction using sign vectors to encode node decisions.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and a differentiable upper bound optimized by SGD via loss-augmented inference.  
- Devises efficient algorithms to solve the loss-augmented inference problems.  
- Novel approach that raises interesting questions about optimization dynamics, especially the sparsity of gradient updates (each SGD step changes only one node).  
- Sparse updates imply potential for growing trees dynamically from zero-initialized weights, letting model complexity adapt to data — this possibility is not explored.  
- Critical ambiguity in handling zero-weight nodes (e.g., defaulting to the right branch) needs precise definition; this behavior is akin to pruning and affects correctness.  
- Interpretability concerns: L2 regularization likely yields dense weight vectors, undermining DT interpretability; L1/sparsity and associated performance trade-offs are untested.  
- Experimental deficiencies: baselines omit pruning details for greedy methods and omit simple global-optimization baselines (e.g., greedy on random subsets).  
- Sections 6.2 and 6.4 require clearer exposition of the inference procedures.  
- Title mentions forests but the paper does not actually address forest extensions.  
- Post-rebuttal issue: zero-initialization and its promising dynamic-growth implications remain insufficiently investigated despite claims that random initialization avoids the problem.  
- Summary judgment: clever reformulation and algorithms, but practical utility and experimental validation are unclear and insufficiently convincing.
