This paper presents an elegant nonconvex relaxation approach to globally optimize decision trees with a fixed structure. Following discussion with other reviewers and consideration of the rebuttal, I maintain a positive assessment and recommend acceptance. The core contribution is a theoretically sound and well-articulated method, and I anticipate it will stimulate further research in this area.

However, the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. While the method demonstrates promise, the observed performance gains are not substantial. A potential avenue for improvement would be to refine the final split in each tree *after* applying the global optimization technique. Specifically, one could discard the last split and re-optimize it, along with its associated leaf node, using standard methods. This may help to mitigate some performance limitations. 

It’s also important to acknowledge a potential trade-off: global optimization techniques, while powerful, can be prone to overfitting. Evaluating and discussing the model’s generalization ability, perhaps through more extensive validation or regularization strategies, would strengthen the analysis. 

Despite the less-than-compelling empirical results, the novelty and clarity of the proposed approach, combined with its potential to influence future work, justify publication. A stronger set of experimental outcomes would likely warrant a higher score, and I believe the current score appropriately reflects the paper’s merits and limitations. The paper is very clearly written and easy to follow.
