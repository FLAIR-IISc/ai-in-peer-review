- Proposes global optimization of decision trees with linear split functions using a convex-concave upper bound and L2 regularization, optimized by SGD.  
- Elegant formulation linking tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly show consistent benefits over greedy tree learning; gains appear only on a few datasets and incur higher computational cost.  
- Request clearer description of the "stable SGD" variant and explicit procedure for assigning data points to leaves; pseudo-code recommended.  
- Clarify whether Figure 2 uses SGD or stable SGD and precisely define what constitutes an “active” leaf.  
- In Algorithm 1 (line 10), explain how Θ is projected onto the simplex in practice and state that the projection ensures each row of Θ sums to one.  
- Minor issues/typos: unclear statement about number of features evaluated for axis-aligned trees (lines 407–408).  
- Minor corrections: replace "argmax" with "max" in eqs. (7)–(9).  
- Figure 1 is referenced but not discussed in the main text.  
- Grammatical/wording fixes: line 141, line 238 (“solutions”), line 301 (“A key observation”), line 404 (likely “validation” errors).  
- Clarify acronym FSGD (line 360).  
- Supplementary material fixes: line 29 should read `max{g = sgn...}`; line 30 should use `sgn(Wx)` inside f(...).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and elegant formulation, tractable surrogate objective for global optimization, demonstrates feasibility of training deep trees with SGD.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited and inconsistent empirical improvements, unclear stable SGD harming reproducibility, computational complexity concerns for large datasets.
