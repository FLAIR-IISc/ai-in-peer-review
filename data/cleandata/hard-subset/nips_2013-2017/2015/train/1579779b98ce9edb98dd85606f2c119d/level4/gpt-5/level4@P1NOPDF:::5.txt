This paper proposes an algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions. Unlike standard greedy training, the method jointly optimizes all tree parameters—both the split-function weights at internal nodes and the output distributions at leaves—by minimizing a global upper bound on the empirical loss. To obtain a smoother optimization problem, the bound is regularized with an L2 penalty on the split weights, and the parameters are learned via stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The optimization is elegantly framed through a connection to structured prediction with latent variables.

While the formulation is appealing, the empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate that global optimization is a superior alternative to greedy training. The globally optimized trees clearly outperform the greedy baseline on only a few datasets, and the computational cost is higher.

The paper is well structured, but several points require clarification:
- The “stable” version of SGD is not clearly described. How are data points assigned to leaves during training? Pseudo-code would be helpful to make the procedure precise.
- Figure 2: Are these results obtained with standard SGD or with the stable SGD variant? Please define precisely what constitutes an “active” leaf.
- Algorithm 1, line 10: Θ is updated and then projected onto a simplex. How is this projection implemented in practice? It would also be helpful to note explicitly that this projection enforces each row of Θ to sum to one.

Minor comments and typos:
- Lines 407–408: You mention tuning “the number of features evaluated when building an ordinary axis-aligned decision tree.” This is unclear, as standard decision trees typically consider all features at each split. Please clarify what is tuned (e.g., a random subset as in random forests?).
- Equations (7), (8), and (9): “argmax” should be replaced with “max.”
- Figure 1 is never referenced in the main text; please cite and discuss it or remove it.
- Line 141: “…to the index of the leaf on by this path.” The end of this sentence is ungrammatical; please correct.
- Line 238: “…the solution to loss-augmented inference…” should be “…the solutions to…”
- Line 301: “An key observation…” should be “A key observation…”
- Line 360: What is FSGD? Please define the acronym on first use.
- Line 404: “…a tree with minimum training errors.” Do you mean “tuning” (validation) errors?

Supplementary material:
- Line 29: max{g ∈ sgn…} should be max{g = sgn…}
- Line 30: sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^T f(g), y) should be sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y)

In summary, the optimization framework is elegant and well motivated, but the experimental evidence is not yet persuasive.
