This is a very clearly written paper that proposes a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The approach is elegant and likely to stimulate further research on related methods.

After discussion with the other reviewers and reviewing the author rebuttal, I maintain that this is a strong paper that deserves publication. Its primary weakness, however, lies in the somewhat disappointing experimental results. While stronger results would have warranted a higher rating, the current score is appropriate, reflecting the balance between an innovative methodology and its empirical validation.

To potentially improve performance, the authors could consider a hybrid strategy. For example, after applying the proposed global optimization, the final split in each tree could be discarded and then re-optimized along with its leaf nodes using a standard greedy algorithm.

Furthermore, it is important to evaluate the method's propensity for overfitting. Global optimization techniques can be more susceptible to this than greedy alternatives, and an empirical analysis of this aspect would significantly strengthen the paper.
