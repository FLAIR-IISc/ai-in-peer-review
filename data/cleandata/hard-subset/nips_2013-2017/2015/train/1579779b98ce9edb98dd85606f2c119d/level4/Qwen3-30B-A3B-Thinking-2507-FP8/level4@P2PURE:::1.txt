

I continue to believe this is a strong paper deserving publication, primarily due to the elegance of its nonconvex relaxation approach for global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure, and its potential to stimulate further research on related methods. While I acknowledge the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming, the paper's core contribution remains compelling. With stronger empirical results, the score would likely be higher; thus, the current assessment is appropriate. A straightforward improvement would be to discard the final split in each tree after global optimization, then optimize this split and its corresponding leaf node using standard methods, which could yield better performance. It is also important to evaluate whether global optimization generally leads to increased overfitting.
