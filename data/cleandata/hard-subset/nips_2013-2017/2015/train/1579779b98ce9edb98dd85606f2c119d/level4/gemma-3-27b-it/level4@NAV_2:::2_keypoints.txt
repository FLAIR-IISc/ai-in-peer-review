- Proposes joint training of decision tree splits and posterior probabilities via a single optimization and upper-bounded empirical loss, linking tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Technically sound approach addressing a challenging problem, representing significant effort toward non-greedy tree learning.  
- Potential flaw in the derivation of the empirical loss bound (Eq. 7); justification when g ≠ sgn(Wx) is unclear and the proof of Prop. 1 seems incomplete.  
- Example countercase (Wx = [5; -5]) shows the right-hand side of Eq. (7) could be smaller than ℓ(·), undermining the claimed upper bound.  
- Some leaves receive no data points, suggesting problems with the objective or initialization; the proposed assignment-fixing heuristic appears suboptimal.  
- Recommend motivating assignment fixing via expectation-maximization or concave-convex procedures, akin to clustering techniques.  
- Clarity is generally good for derivations, but experimental presentation and analysis need more detail.  
- Importance of efficient loss-augmented inference is unclear for modest depths (up to 16); naive enumeration might be feasible and the effect of Hamming-ball restriction needs stronger evaluation.  
- Clarify what is meant by “leaves” in the experiments and analysis.  
- Key baseline missing: non-axis-aligned split functions trained with a standard greedy approach (without axis-aligned initialization).  
- Pre-specifying tree depth is a limitation that requires deeper discussion and experimental investigation of its impact.  
- Suggest replacing argmax with max in the bound notation; request runtime comparison and more extensive evaluation; reviewer may revise score after rebuttal.
