- Proposes a non-greedy algorithm for oblique decision trees by optimizing a global surrogate objective via latent structured prediction and a convex–concave upper bound.  
- Models tree inference with an m-bit latent code h and derives loss bounds combining linear score h^T W x with leaf loss.  
- Derives a full loss-augmented inference bound and a fast Hamming-ball (radius 1) variant to enable efficient updates (O(d^2 p)).  
- Uses SGD (and a proposed Stable SGD variant) to jointly optimize split weights W under per-row norm constraints and leaf parameters Θ.  
- Experiments on SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST show single non-greedy trees generally outperform greedy baselines across depths.  
- Builds on Bennett, OC1, max-margin trees, HME, structured SVMs, and authors’ CO2; main novelty is the global objective and Hamming-ball inference.  
- Strength: clear, principled formulation linking decision-tree training to latent structured prediction.  
- Strength: well-motivated convex–concave upper bound and insightful Hamming-ball restriction for fast inference.  
- Strength: joint W/Θ optimization with per-node norm constraints provides natural regularization; analysis of W scaling is helpful.  
- Strength: empirical comparisons show consistent improvements and reduced overfitting for single trees.  
- Strength: complexity analysis and fast inference variant make training deeper trees computationally feasible.  
- Weakness: empirical gains are modest and dataset-dependent; lacks statistical significance testing and error bars.  
- Weakness: higher computational overhead vs greedy training; needs fair wall-clock comparisons and consistent training budgets.  
- Weakness: unclear contribution of initialization (axis-aligned → CO2 → non-greedy); ablation on initialization sensitivity is needed.  
- Weakness: SSGD is under-specified and its individual impact on accuracy/stability is not isolated in experiments.  
- Weakness: focus on single trees limits assessment of practical impact; scalability to ensembles is not explored.  
- Quality: technical development and loss bounds appear sound; Hamming-ball inference is a neat, plausible complexity improvement.  
- Clarity: overall well written but SSGD description is too brief—needs pseudo-code, precise leaf assignment/update rules, and stopping criteria.  
- Clarify whether Θ are projected/constrained or left as unconstrained logits (and whether a softmax suffices); specify if Figure 2 used SGD or SSGD.  
- Originality: novel framing as latent structured prediction with convex–concave bound and efficient Hamming-ball inference; SSGD is a practical addition.  
- Significance: framework could improve single oblique trees and inform ensembles; main value is the optimization approach rather than large accuracy gains.  
- Requests: provide SSGD pseudo-code, define active/inactive leaves and reporting metrics, quantify SSGD vs SGD, show initialization ablations, and report fair wall-clock times.  
- Minor comments/typos: clarify feature-evaluation wording, use “max” not “argmax” where appropriate, fix several line-specific phrasing/figure-reference errors.  
- Pro-accept arguments: principled, novel optimization framework; efficient inference; consistent improvements over greedy baselines.  
- Con-accept arguments: modest, dataset-specific gains; extra computational cost; insufficient ablation/detail on SSGD and initialization effects.
