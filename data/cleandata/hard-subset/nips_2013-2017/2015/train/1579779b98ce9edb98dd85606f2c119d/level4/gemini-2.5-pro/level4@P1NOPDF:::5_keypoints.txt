- Proposes a novel algorithm to globally optimize all decision-tree parameters (linear split weights and leaf class distributions) instead of greedy node-wise training.  
- Core idea: minimize a regularized upper bound on empirical loss via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: elegant formulation linking the problem to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing: method outperforms greedy baselines on only a few datasets and is more computationally intensive.  
- Reproducibility concern: description of the "stable SGD" variant is unclear—needs explicit procedure/pseudocode for assigning data points to leaves.  
- Ambiguity in experiments: unclear whether Figure 2 uses standard SGD or stable SGD; “active” leaf is undefined.  
- Algorithmic detail missing: practical method for projecting Θ onto the simplex in Algorithm 1 should be described and clarified that each Θ row sums to one.  
- Clarify feature-selection statement (Lines 407–408): does it mean evaluating a subset of features per split (as in Random Forests) or something else?  
- Notational fixes: Equations (7),(8),(9) should use "max" instead of "argmax".  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 is never referenced in the text.  
- Multiple minor typos/grammar: incomplete sentence at Line 141; pluralization at Line 238; "An key" → "A key" at Line 301; undefined acronym "FSGD" at Line 360.  
- Clarify Line 404: hyperparameter tuning should reference validation/tuning error rather than training error.  
- Supplementary corrections: Line 29 likely `max{g = sgn...}`; Line 30 should consistently use `l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y)`.  
- Overall summary: principled and elegant global-training formulation, but experimental evidence and several methodological/clarity details need strengthening before claiming superiority over greedy methods.
