- Proposes joint learning of all decision-tree parameters (splits and leaf posteriors) via a single optimization, upper-bounded and approximated into a practical surrogate.  
- Paper is well written and derivations are neat, but there may be reasoning issues in places.  
- Experiments and their presentation are limited and could be strengthened.  
- Possible flaw in the empirical loss bound derivation (Eq. 7): counterexample where g ≠ sgn(Wx) can produce a smaller RHS, and the proof of Proposition 1 appears incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no assigned data, which is unusual and may indicate problems with the objective or initialization; the heuristic of fixing leaf assignments seems suboptimal (though could be likened to an EM/concave-convex procedure).  
- Doubts about the practical importance of the efficient loss-augmented inference: exhaustive search is feasible up to depth 16, so restricting search to a Hamming ball needs stronger justification and broader evidence; clarify what “leaves” refers to in that context.  
- Missing baseline: a greedy method for training oblique (non-axis-aligned) splits without initialization.  
- The method requires tree depth to be fixed in advance; this constraint needs more discussion and a thorough experimental study of depth.  
- Reviewer appreciated authors’ clarification on the loss bound and suggests replacing argmax with max; score was updated.  
- Recommends adding a runtime comparison and more thorough empirical evaluation to strengthen the paper.  
- Reviewer is willing to adjust the score pending a careful rebuttal addressing the derivation and empirical concerns.
