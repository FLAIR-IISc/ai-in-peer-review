- Reformulates global decision-tree optimization as latent structured prediction using sign vectors for internal-node decisions.
- Proposes a non-differentiable, non-convex loss approximated by a differentiable (still non-convex) upper bound and optimized via SGD.
- Provides efficient algorithms for the required loss-augmented inference during SGD.
- Key contributions are the structured-prediction casting and the efficient optimization algorithms; the approach is novel and interesting.
- Gradient updates are sparse—apparently each step alters only one node’s weight—which may have implications beyond computational efficiency.
- Raises the possibility of growing an effectively infinite tree from zero weights, letting complexity increase naturally, which needs analysis (e.g., relation to greedy strategies).
- Ambiguity about the zero-weight case: paper implies taking the right branch for zero weights, which is dubious; better to allow decisions/pruning and define this case precisely.
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization yields dense, non-interpretable splits; L1 might enforce sparsity but could harm performance and needs empirical testing.
- Experimental weaknesses: insufficient baselines and missing details on pruning strategies for the naive greedy comparator.
- Recommends also comparing trivial global strategies (e.g., greedy on random subsets) and tuning naive methods similarly to the proposed method.
- Clarity issues in inference sections (e.g., 6.2 and 6.4) — could be summarized or made more explicit.
- Practical usefulness is unclear due to interpretability issues and limited experimental validation.
- Title mentions forests but the paper does not address the forest case, which is misleading.
- Post-rebuttal: the zero-initialization/growth scenario remains important and unexamined; random initialization likely avoids the issue in practice, but the growth behavior should be explored.
