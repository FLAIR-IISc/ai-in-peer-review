The authors propose an interesting way to search for the best decision tree of a specific depth by posing it as a structured prediction problem. This approach allows for the global optimization of the tree's parameters, which can lead to better generalization performance compared to traditional greedy methods. The proposed method is based on formulating the decision tree learning problem as a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss, which can be optimized using stochastic gradient descent.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it can outperform traditional greedy methods in terms of test set accuracy. However, the improvements tend to be small, and it is not entirely clear whether the proposed method is significantly better than existing approaches.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to establish a link between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables. This connection allows the authors to leverage advances in structured prediction to develop a more effective decision tree learning algorithm. The use of a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss is also a key contribution, as it enables the optimization of the tree's parameters using stochastic gradient descent.
