- Proposes a novel global objective that jointly optimizes split functions across all tree levels instead of greedy layerwise training.  
- Frames decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, offering a principled new perspective.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that allows efficient optimization via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Reduces computational complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p), making training of deeper trees feasible.  
- Extends prior non-greedy work by overcoming earlier computational and loss-function limitations.  
- Clear, precise, and well-structured exposition that makes the technical development accessible.  
- The connection to structured prediction is elegantly and convincingly explained.  
- Significant theoretical contribution and originality compared to recent incremental/greedy-focused papers.  
- Rigorous theoretical development with a clear proof of the proposed upper bound.  
- Shows how regularization parameter ν controls the trade-off between complexity and generalization.  
- Experimental results show improved generalization over greedy baselines but gains are sometimes marginal.  
- Claims about global optimization increasing overfitting are not fully supported by experiments and need further investigation.  
- Limited analysis of the effect of ν on overfitting and model complexity; more exploration needed.  
- Could benefit from combining the method with standard post-processing (e.g., pruning) and broader comparisons to state-of-the-art tree methods.  
- Overall technically sound and potentially influential; recommendation to accept but strengthen experimental analysis.
