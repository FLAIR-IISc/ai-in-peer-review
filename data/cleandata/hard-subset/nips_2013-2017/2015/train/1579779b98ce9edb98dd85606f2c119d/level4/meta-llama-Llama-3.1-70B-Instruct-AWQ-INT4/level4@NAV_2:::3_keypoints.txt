- Proposes searching for the best fixed-depth decision tree by framing it as a structured prediction problem.  
- Enables global optimization of decision trees, addressing discrete and sequential splitting challenges.  
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss that can be optimized with stochastic gradient descent.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of formulation, derivation, and optimization procedure.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms typical greedy tree-building approaches on test accuracy.  
- Accuracy improvements are small and it is unclear if they are statistically or practically significant.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of computational complexity and scalability considerations.  
- Limited experimental evaluation on only a few datasets, raising questions about generalization.  
- Lacks detailed comparisons with widely used baselines such as random forests and gradient boosting.  
- Does not address the interpretability of the learned decision trees.  
- Original idea of structured prediction for trees is valuable, though use of convex-concave bounds is not novel.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and promising, but needs more extensive experiments, stronger baselines, and interpretability discussion.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
