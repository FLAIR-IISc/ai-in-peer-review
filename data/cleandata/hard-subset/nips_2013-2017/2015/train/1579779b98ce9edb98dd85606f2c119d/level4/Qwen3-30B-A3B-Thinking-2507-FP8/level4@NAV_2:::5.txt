

This paper introduces a non-greedy approach for training decision trees with linear split functions by optimizing tree parameters (split weights and leaf distributions) globally rather than sequentially. The authors establish a connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables, formulating a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss. This surrogate objective is regularized via L2 constraints on the split function weights, and the authors employ stochastic gradient descent to minimize the bound, enabling efficient training of deep trees.

The problem formulation is elegant and well-motivated, effectively linking decision tree optimization to structured prediction. The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the technical contributions accessible.

However, the empirical results do not strongly support the claim that global optimization offers significant advantages over greedy tree learning. The proposed method only demonstrates clear performance improvements over greedy baselines on a limited number of datasets, while incurring higher computational costs. The paper would benefit from more comprehensive comparisons and analysis of when global optimization provides meaningful gains.

Several points require clarification:
- The stable SGD variant is not sufficiently explained. A pseudo-code would help clarify how data points are assigned to leaves and how leaf assignments are updated.
- It's unclear whether the results in Figure 2 were obtained using standard SGD or stable SGD. Additionally, the precise meaning of "active" leaves should be defined.
- In Algorithm 1, line 10 updates Θ and projects it onto a simplex. The paper should specify how this projection is implemented and note that it ensures each row of Θ sums to one.

Minor issues and typos:
- Lines 407-408 mention tuning "the number of features evaluated" when building axis-aligned trees, which is misleading as standard decision trees evaluate all features at each node.
- Equations (7), (8), and (9) use "argmax" where "max" is appropriate.
- Figure 1 is referenced but never discussed in the main text.
- Line 141 contains a grammatical error: "...to the index of the leaf on by this path."
- Line 238 should read "the solution to loss-augmented inference" rather than "solutions."
- Line 301 contains a grammatical error: "An key observation" should be "A key observation."
- Line 360 references "FSGD" without definition.
- Line 404 likely contains a typo: "minimum training errors" should be "minimum validation errors."

Supplementary material corrections:
- Line 29: "max{g ∈ sgn...}" should be "max{g = sgn...}"
- Line 30: "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^Tf(g), y)" should be "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^Tf(sgn(Wx)), y)"

While the theoretical formulation is sound and the approach represents an interesting alternative to greedy tree learning, the empirical evidence does not sufficiently demonstrate the advantages of the proposed method over standard greedy approaches. The paper would be strengthened by more thorough experimental validation and clearer exposition of key technical details.
