- Recommendation: Support acceptance — paper is worthy of publication for its elegant formulation and potential to stimulate further research.  
- Concern: Experimental results are modest; current score balances novelty against limited empirical evidence.  
- Clarity: Paper is very clearly written and presents a principled, elegant non-convex relaxation for global optimization of fixed-structure decision trees.  
- Main empirical worry: Evaluation does not convincingly demonstrate practical advantage over strong baselines.  
- Suggested improvement: Use a hybrid training step — after global optimization, remove and re-optimize the final split and leaf parameters with local/greedy methods.  
- Suggested analysis: Check for increased overfitting by reporting training vs validation/test error, sensitivity to regularization, and performance vs dataset size or tree complexity.  
- Overall: Despite weak experiments, clarity, elegance, and potential impact justify acceptance; stronger experiments would strengthen the paper.
