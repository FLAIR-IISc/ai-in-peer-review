- Contribution: joint end-to-end training of decision trees (splits and leaf posteriors) via a single optimization made tractable with an approximate upper bound.  
- Overall: well-written with clean math and high originality/significance, but value reduced by unclear theory and weak experiments.  
- Theoretical bound: justification of the empirical loss bound (Eq. 7) is confusing and potentially incorrect in some cases; the supplementary proof is incomplete and the main derivation needs clearer presentation (e.g., replace argmax with max).  
- Empty leaves: some tree leaves receive no data, suggesting optimization or initialization issues; the fixed assignment heuristic needs better motivation and links to methods like EM or CCCP.  
- Loss-augmented inference: necessity of the proposed efficient search is unclear since depth-16 trees have only 65,536 leaves; the paper should quantify the impact of the restricted Hamming-ball search and clarify what "leaves" means.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard greedy, layer-by-layer training for non-axis-aligned splits trained from scratch, which is an important baseline to include.  
- Pre-specified depth: requiring the tree depth to be set in advance is a limitation that needs more discussion and sensitivity analysis.  
- Experiments: overall experimental evaluation should be strengthened, including runtime comparisons against relevant baselines.
