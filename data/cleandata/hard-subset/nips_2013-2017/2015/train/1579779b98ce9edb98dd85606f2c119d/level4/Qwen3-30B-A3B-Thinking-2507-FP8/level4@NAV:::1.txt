

After discussion with other reviewers and consideration of the rebuttal, I maintain that this paper merits publication due to its elegant approach and potential to stimulate further research on related methodologies. While I acknowledge the experimental results are somewhat marginal, stronger outcomes would likely warrant a higher rating; thus, the current assessment remains appropriate. The paper presents a clearly written, nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. Although the approach is elegant, the experimental results are marginally disappointing. A straightforward improvement would involve pruning the final split in each tree after global optimization and re-optimizing that split and its associated leaf node via standard methods, potentially yielding enhanced performance. Additionally, it is critical to evaluate whether the global optimization framework inherently increases the risk of overfitting.
