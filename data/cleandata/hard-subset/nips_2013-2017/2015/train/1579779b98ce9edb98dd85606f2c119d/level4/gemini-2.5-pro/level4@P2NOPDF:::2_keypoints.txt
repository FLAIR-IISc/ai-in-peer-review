- Paper proposes end-to-end joint optimization of decision trees (split functions and leaf posteriors) via a single optimization and a convex upper-bound surrogate.

- The problem is important and the joint-training formulation is elegant and clearly derived.

- Reviewer’s enthusiasm is tempered by a potential flaw in a core theoretical derivation and by weaknesses in the experimental evaluation.

- Major concern: the upper-bound derivation in Eq.(7) appears incorrect — a counterexample shows the RHS can be smaller than the original loss, and the proof of Proposition 1 seems incomplete.

- Major concern: experiments report empty leaf nodes, suggesting issues with the objective or optimization; the proposed fixed-assignment heuristic seems ad-hoc and needs better intuition or formal motivation (e.g., EM or CCCP).

- Missing baseline: include a greedy, layer-by-layer tree trained with the same non-axis-aligned split functions to separate benefits of joint training from split expressiveness.

- Question necessity of restricted loss-augmented inference: for D=16 brute-force over ~65k paths may be feasible; quantify impact of Hamming-ball restriction and compare runtimes.

- Limitation: requirement to pre-specify tree depth is significant; discuss more prominently and evaluate sensitivity to this hyperparameter.

- Clarity issue: experimental presentation needs improvement (e.g., clarify the meaning of "leaves" in the supplement) and more careful evaluation and description.

- Reviewer is open to revising their assessment pending authors’ response, especially regarding the correctness of the loss bound.
