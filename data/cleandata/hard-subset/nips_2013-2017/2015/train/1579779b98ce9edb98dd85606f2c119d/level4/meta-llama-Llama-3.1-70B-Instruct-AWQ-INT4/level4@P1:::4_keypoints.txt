- Proposes global decision-tree optimization via latent structured prediction using sign-vector latent variables.
- Introduces a non-differentiable non-convex loss approximated by a differentiable upper bound, optimized by SGD with loss-augmented inference.
- Provides efficient algorithms for the required loss-augmented inference steps.
- Main contributions: novel reformulation as structured prediction and efficient optimization algorithms.
- Noted novelty and interesting theoretical questions raised by the approach.
- Gradient updates appear sparse — each SGD step changes only one node's weight — a property noted but under-explored.
- Suggests the possibility of growing an effectively infinite-depth tree from zero weights, allowing complexity to increase naturally.
- Questions whether such growth would correspond to known greedy strategies or converge to limited-node trees with proper regularization.
- Ambiguity about what happens when a node's weight is zero (paper implies taking the right branch); reviewer suggests outputting a decision/pruning instead.
- Practical concern: decision trees are valued for interpretability, but L2 regularization likely yields dense, non-interpretable splits; L1 might help but could hurt performance.
- Experiments lack sufficient baseline details, particularly which pruning strategies were used for the naive method.
- Recommends testing other simple global strategies (e.g., greedy on random subsets) and applying comparable hyperparameter searches for baselines.
- Clarity is generally good, but inference descriptions (Sections 6.2 and 6.4) could be clearer and more explicit.
- Overall verdict: clever and interesting method, but practical usefulness is unclear due to interpretability issues and insufficient experimental comparisons.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel reformulation, clever method, shown performance gains over naive methods.
- Arguments against acceptance: unconvincing motivation, inadequate baseline/experimental detail, interpretability not addressed, zero-weight case unanalyzed.
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight case remains a critical unresolved issue; reviewer encourages exploring zero-initialization and gradual weight growth.
