After discussion with the other reviewers and reading the rebuttal, I continue to view this as a good paper that merits publication, primarily because of the elegance of the approach and its potential to spark further research on related methods. I agree that the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. With stronger empirical results I would likely have rated the paper higher; given the current evidence, my score feels appropriate.

The paper is very clearly written and proposes a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The approach is elegant, though the empirical performance does not fully match the promise of the method.

A simple modification worth exploring is the following: after performing the global optimization, discard the last split in each tree and then re-optimize that split and the associated leaf node using the standard local procedure. This may yield better results in practice. It is also important to evaluate the extent to which the global optimization tends to overfit; an explicit analysis or empirical assessment of overfitting would strengthen the paper.
