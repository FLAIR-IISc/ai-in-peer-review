- Clearly written paper proposing an elegant nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of fixed-structure decision trees, likely to stimulate further research.
- Reviewer, after discussion and rebuttal, considers it a strong paper deserving publication.
- Primary weakness: somewhat disappointing experimental results, which limit the empirical validation and justify the current score.
- Suggestion: try a hybrid strategy—after global optimization, discard the final split in each tree and re-optimize splits and leaf nodes with a standard greedy algorithm.
- Recommendation: empirically evaluate susceptibility to overfitting, since global optimization methods can be more prone to it than greedy alternatives.
