- Proposes jointly optimizing oblique split weights and leaf parameters under a single global objective instead of greedy node-wise induction.  
- Derives an upper bound on the tree’s empirical loss and adds L2 regularization on split weights for better-behaved optimization.  
- Minimizes the surrogate objective via stochastic gradient descent to learn internal node weights and leaf distributions.  
- Nicely casts the problem as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: clear improvements only on a subset of datasets and the method is computationally more expensive than greedy induction.  
- Paper is well organized but several methodological details need clarification, especially the Stable SGD variant.  
- Needs explanation of how examples are assigned to leaves during “frozen” phases in Stable SGD and inclusion of pseudocode.  
- Figure 2 results should state whether they use standard SGD or Stable SGD, and the paper must precisely define an “active” leaf.  
- Algorithm 1: clarify how Θ is projected onto a simplex in practice and note this enforces each row of Θ to sum to one.  
- Minor/tightening comments: clarify what “number of features evaluated” refers to when tuning axis-aligned trees.  
- Notational corrections: replace “argmax” with “max” in Eqs. (7)–(9).  
- Editorial fixes: cite Figure 1 in the main text and correct multiple typos/phrasing errors (lines 141, 238, 301, 360, 404).  
- Clarify the abbreviation FSGD (line 360) and whether “tuning” vs “training” errors is intended (line 404).  
- Supplementary corrections: fix sign/max expressions (lines 29–30) to use correct sgn and f(sgn(Wx)) forms.  
- Overall assessment: the optimization framework is elegant but empirical evidence currently does not convincingly demonstrate advantage over greedy methods.
