- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree learning method by formulating tree optimization as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling global (non-greedy) optimization.  
- Enables efficient training via stochastic gradient descent with complexity O(d²p), making deep-tree training feasible.  
- Evaluated on multiple classification benchmarks, showing consistent but modest improvements over greedy baselines.  
- Novel theoretical framework connecting decision trees to structured prediction is a major contribution.  
- Develops a computationally efficient algorithm with rigorous, convincing complexity analysis.  
- Experimental results show better generalization and reduced overfitting compared to greedy methods across depths.  
- Theoretical derivations and bound optimization are technically sound and well-justified.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, though the structured-prediction connection could be made more intuitive.  
- Algorithm descriptions are precise but need more detail on the fast loss-augmented inference step.  
- Main weakness: performance gains are statistically significant but modest in magnitude.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of practical scenarios where the method outperforms alternatives like random forests or boosting.  
- Would benefit from stronger contrast with prior non-greedy work (e.g., Bennett, Mondrian forests) regarding scalability.  
- Potential extensions (sparsity regularization, kernels) are noted but not explored, limiting practical impact.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical advance with consistent empirical gains — recommend acceptance.
