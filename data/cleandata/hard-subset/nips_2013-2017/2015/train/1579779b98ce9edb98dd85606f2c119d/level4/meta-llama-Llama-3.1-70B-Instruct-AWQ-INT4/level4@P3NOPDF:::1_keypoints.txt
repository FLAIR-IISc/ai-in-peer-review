- Reviewer endorses publication after discussion, reaffirming initial positive assessment.  
- The proposed nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of fixed-structure decision trees is elegant and well-written.  
- The approach has significant potential to stimulate further research in related areas.  
- Experimental results are somewhat disappointing and not entirely convincing.  
- Despite weak experiments, the theoretical contributions outweigh the limitations.  
- Stronger empirical results would have led to a higher score.  
- Suggests a hybrid method: combine global optimization with standard optimization for the last split and corresponding leaf.  
- Recommends investigating whether the global optimization approach is more prone to overfitting.  
- With refinements addressing experimental limitations, the method could lead to significant advancements in decision tree optimization.
