- Paper proposes joint optimization of all decision-tree parameters (splits and leaf posteriors) via a global surrogate objective instead of greedy node-wise training.  
- Manuscript is clearly written and derivations are tidy, but experiments and presentation need strengthening.  
- Novelty: joint training of decision-tree parameters is valuable; significance is moderate with noted shortcomings.  
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss bound (Eq. 7)—counterexample where the RHS can be smaller than the loss, and Prop. 1 proof appears incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data assignments; this is surprising and may indicate issues with the objective or initialization; the proposed heuristic of fixing assignments is suboptimal.  
- Suggest framing the fixed-assignment heuristic in an EM/CCCP-style argument similar to clustering methods.  
- Question the necessity of the efficient loss-augmented inference: brute-force over 2^16 may be feasible; need more evidence on how Hamming-ball constraints affect performance and clarity on “leaves” terminology.  
- Missing baseline: greedy training of oblique (non-axis-aligned) splits without special initialization.  
- Tree depth must be specified a priori; the paper should discuss this limitation and analyze sensitivity to depth more thoroughly.  
- Replace argmax with max in the loss-bound clarification as suggested by reviewer.  
- Recommend adding runtime comparisons and a more thorough empirical evaluation; reviewer may update score after a careful rebuttal.
