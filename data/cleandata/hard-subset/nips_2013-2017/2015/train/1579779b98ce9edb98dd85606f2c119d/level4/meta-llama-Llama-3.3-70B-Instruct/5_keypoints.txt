- Proposes globally optimizing decision trees with linear split functions by minimizing a regularized upper bound on empirical loss instead of greedy splitting.  
- Uses L2 regularization on split weights and stochastic gradient descent to optimize tree parameters (split weights and leaf distributions).  
- Frames the optimization elegantly via a connection to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly show global optimization outperforms greedy learning — superior on only a few datasets and much more computationally intensive.  
- Paper structure is clear but several methodological descriptions need clarification, especially the "stable" SGD variant and how data points are assigned to leaves; pseudocode suggested.  
- Figure 2 is ambiguous about whether results use SGD or stable SGD; the term "active" leaf needs precise definition.  
- Algorithm 1, line 10: the update and projection of Θ onto the simplex should be explained, including how the projection is solved and noting it enforces each row of Θ sums to one.  
- Minor text errors and confusing wording: line 407–408 about number of features evaluated in axis-aligned trees; lines 141, 301 grammar; line 238 pluralization; line 360 introduces "FSGD" without explanation; line 404 likely means "tuning" errors.  
- Equation/formula corrections: replace "argmax" with "max" in Eqs. (7)–(9).  
- Figure 1 is not referenced in the main text and should be cited.  
- Supplementary material typos: Line 29 should be "max{g = sgn...}" and Line 30 fix to "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^Tf(sgn(Wx)), y)".  
- Overall: elegant formulation and theoretical connection, but experimental evidence and clarity issues limit the paper's persuasive power.
