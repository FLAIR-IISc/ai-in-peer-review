After discussion with the other reviewers and considering the rebuttal, I remain positive about this paper and believe it warrants publication. This is due to the elegance of the proposed approach and its potential to stimulate further research in related areas. While the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, I believe the current score is appropriate; stronger results would likely merit a higher rating. 

The paper very clearly presents a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. This is an elegant approach. However, the experimental performance is not as compelling as the theoretical contribution. One potential improvement could be to refine the trees after global optimization by re-optimizing the final split and its associated leaf nodes using standard methods – this might lead to better empirical results. It’s also important to acknowledge that global optimization techniques generally have a tendency to increase overfitting, and this should be considered when evaluating the results.
