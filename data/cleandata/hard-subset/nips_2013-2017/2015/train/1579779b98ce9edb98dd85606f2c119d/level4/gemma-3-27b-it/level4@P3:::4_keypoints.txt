- Proposes global optimization of decision trees via a latent structured prediction reformulation with sign-vector latents and a differentiable upper-bound loss optimized by SGD.  
- Key contributions: casting DT optimization as structured prediction and developing efficient loss-augmented inference algorithms.  
- Novel and interesting approach, but practical motivation and significance over naive methods are unclear.  
- Important observation: gradient updates are sparse (each step modifies only one node), which could enable novel growth/pruning strategies.  
- Suggests exploring growing infinitely deep trees from zero-initialized weights and whether this is equivalent to or distinct from greedy strategies.  
- Ambiguity about behavior when a node’s weight is zero (paper implies taking right branch); should define explicit output/pruning behavior.  
- Major interpretability concern: L2 regularization yields dense, non-interpretable splits; L1 might help but could hurt performance.  
- Experiments lack detail on baselines, pruning strategies, and hyperparameter tuning for the naive method.  
- Recommends comparing to trivial global strategies (e.g., greedy on random subsets) and matching hyperparameter sweeps for baselines.  
- Clarity issues in inference description; sections 6.2 and 6.4 could be simplified and made more explicit.  
- Paper does not discuss extension to decision forests, which limits practical relevance.  
- Overall scores: Quality 3, Clarity 3, Originality 4, Significance 3; Recommendation: Weak Reject.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel reformulation, efficient optimization algorithms, empirical improvements over greedy baselines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak motivation/interpretability, incomplete experiments and baseline comparisons, unresolved zero-weight/node-growth behavior.
