- Proposes algorithm for decision trees with linear split functions that globally optimizes split weights and leaf output distributions.  
- Introduces an upper bound on empirical loss with L2 regularization on split weights and uses SGD to minimize it.  
- Formulation is elegant and connects tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly show global optimization beats greedy trees; improvements are seen on only a few datasets and at higher computational cost.  
- Description of the "stable" SGD variant is unclear—needs explanation of how data points are assigned to leaves and pseudo-code.  
- Figure 2: unclear whether results use SGD or stable SGD and what an "active" leaf means.  
- Algorithm 1 (line 10): unclear how projection of Θ onto the simplex is solved in practice; note that projection enforces each row sums to one.  
- Comment about tuning "number of features evaluated when building an ordinary axis-aligned decision tree" is misleading—standard trees evaluate all features at each node.  
- Equations (7), (8), (9): use "max" instead of "argmax."  
- Figure 1 is not referenced in the main text.  
- Typo/clarity at line 141: sentence fragment "...to the index of the leaf on by this path."  
- Line 238: grammar should be "the solutions to loss-augmented inference..."  
- Line 301: typo "An key observation..." → "A key observation..."  
- Line 360: undefined abbreviation "FSGD" — needs definition.  
- Line 404: phrase "a tree with minimum training errors." likely meant "tuning errors."  
- Supplementary line 29: typo "max{g ∈ sgn...}" → "max{g = sgn...}".  
- Supplementary line 30: expression should read "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y)".  
- Overall assessment: strong formulation but insufficient empirical evidence to support the proposed global optimization approach.
