- Proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters via a single optimization problem with an upper-bound approximation for tractability.
- Paper is well written and derivations are clean, but may contain reasoning flaws.
- Experimental presentation and evaluation are limited and could be improved.
- Original and important topic: joint training for decision trees.
- Proposed solution is valuable but has shortcomings.
- Major concern: possible flaw/incompleteness in the empirical loss bound derivation (counterexample showing RHS can be smaller).
- Some leaves end up with no data points, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization; the fixed-assignment heuristic may be suboptimal and could be motivated by EM/CCCP.
- Questioning the need for the efficient loss-augmented inference: for depth ≤16 brute-force may be feasible; impact of the Hamming-ball restricted search on performance is unclear and insufficiently generalized.
- Ambiguity about what "leaves" refers to in experimental descriptions.
- Missing baseline: greedy training of trees with non-axis-aligned split functions (without special initialization).
- Method requires pre-specifying tree depth; this limitation needs more discussion and depth-sensitivity experiments.
- Minor fixes/suggestions: replace argmax with max; include runtime comparisons.
- Reviewer is willing to reconsider score pending a careful rebuttal/explanation of the loss bound.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, better than greedy baselines in reported experiments, well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential derivation flaw, limited experimental evaluation and baselines, and requirement to fix tree depth ahead of time.
