- Presents a novel, elegant nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of decision trees with predefined structure.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the motivation and method.  
- The formulation departs from greedy methods and could inspire significant future research.  
- Empirical results are modest; improvements over greedy trees are not substantial.  
- Risk of overfitting from global optimization is not adequately addressed or evaluated.  
- Suggests a hybrid/post-processing approach: prune and locally re-optimize leaves to improve generalization.  
- After rebuttal, reviewer still recommends publication based on conceptual novelty despite limited experiments.
