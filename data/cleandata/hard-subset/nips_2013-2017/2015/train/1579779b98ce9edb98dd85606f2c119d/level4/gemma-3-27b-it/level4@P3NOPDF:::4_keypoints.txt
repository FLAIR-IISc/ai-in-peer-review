- Proposes global optimization of decision trees by reformulating as (latent) structured prediction using sign-vector latent variables.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable/non-convex loss and a differentiable upper-bound optimized by SGD via loss-augmented inference.  
- Provides efficient algorithms for the loss-augmented inference subproblems.  
- Main contribution: novel casting of tree optimization as structured prediction and efficient optimization algorithms.  
- Observes sparse gradient updates—each SGD step appears to affect only one node’s weight vector, a potential computational advantage.  
- Suggests unexplored possibility of growing an infinitely deep tree from zero-initialized weights and questions relation to greedy methods.  
- Ambiguity/critical issue: behavior when a node’s weight equals zero is not precisely defined and affects pruning/decision semantics.  
- Practical motivation concern: method likely yields dense, non-interpretable trees under L2 regularization; sparsity via L1 may hurt performance.  
- Experimental weaknesses: insufficient baseline details (pruning strategies), lack of comparable global/alternate baselines, and unclear parameter tuning for baselines.  
- Explanatory clarity: parts of inference (Section 6.2, 6.4) could be clearer and more explicitly described.  
- Title inconsistency: paper mentions forests in title but does not address random forests or ensembles.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer reiterates zero-weight issue and encourages exploration of zero-initialization/growth behavior; overall method is clever but needs stronger motivation and experiments.
