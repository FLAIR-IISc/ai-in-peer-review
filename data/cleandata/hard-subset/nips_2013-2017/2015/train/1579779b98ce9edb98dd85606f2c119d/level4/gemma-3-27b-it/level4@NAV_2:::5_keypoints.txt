- Proposes a global optimization algorithm for decision trees with linear split functions, jointly learning split weights and leaf outputs.  
- Uses an upper bound on empirical loss with L2 regularization on split weights, optimized via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Frames decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, enabling use of established techniques.  
- Optimization procedure is tractable but computationally more expensive than greedy methods.  
- Empirical results show improvements on some datasets but are inconsistent across benchmarks.  
- Paper is generally well-structured and clearly written.  
- Stable SGD algorithm description is opaque and needs clearer explanation and pseudo-code for leaf assignment updates.  
- Figure 2 caption must clarify whether results use standard SGD or Stable SGD and define “active” leaf.  
- Algorithm 1 (line 10) lacks details on projecting Θ onto the simplex and ensuring rows sum to one.  
- Several wording, grammar, and notation issues across the main text need fixing (multiple specific line corrections cited).  
- Equations (7)–(9): replace "argmax" with "max."  
- Figure 1 is referenced but not discussed in the main text.  
- Undefined abbreviation: "FSGD" (line 360) should be defined.  
- Line 404: phrase likely should be "tuning errors" instead of "training errors."  
- Supplementary material contains notation errors that need correction (lines 29–30).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; insightful connection to structured prediction; sensible regularization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inconsistent empirical gains, higher computational cost, and insufficient algorithmic detail for reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but encourage revision with stronger empirical evidence and clearer Stable SGD exposition.
