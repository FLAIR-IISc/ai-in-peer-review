After discussing with the other reviewers and considering the rebuttal, I still believe this is a good paper that warrants publication, primarily due to the elegance of the approach and its potential to inspire further research on related methods. I concur with the other reviewers that the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. Nevertheless, I think that with more compelling results, I would likely rate the paper even higher, which is why I believe this score is justified.

The paper proposes a nonconvex relaxation for globally optimizing decision trees with a fixed structure, and it is written in a very clear and concise manner. The approach is undeniably elegant, but unfortunately, the experimental results are not as impressive. One potential modification to consider would be to discard the last split in each tree after applying the global optimization, and then optimize the last split and the leaf node using standard methods, which may lead to improved results. Furthermore, it is crucial to assess whether global optimization tends to result in increased overfitting.
