- Proposes an elegant nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of fixed-structure decision trees.  
- Approach is well-motivated, clearly presented, and likely to stimulate further research.  
- Core idea—relaxing discrete tree structure to continuous space—is compelling and mathematically sound.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and makes a complex topic accessible.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but has reservations due to experimental results.  
- Experimental evaluation is mixed: gains over baselines are small or sometimes worse, raising practical-impact concerns.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations in rebuttal, but their explanations don’t fully bridge theory-to-empirical performance gap.  
- Suggests a simple modification: after global optimization, remove final split and refine it (and its leaves) with a greedy local optimization.  
- Warns global optimization may cause overfitting; recommends explicit discussion and investigation of regularization strategies.  
- Overall contribution is novel and promising, providing a solid foundation for future work despite current empirical weaknesses.
