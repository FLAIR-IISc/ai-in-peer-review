

This paper proposes an algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions at nodes, optimizing tree parameters (i.e., split function weights and leaf output distributions) globally rather than greedily. The approach derives an upper bound on the tree's empirical loss, regularizes it via L2 regularization of split function weights to ensure smooth optimization, and employs stochastic gradient descent to minimize this upper bound.  

The tree optimization problem is elegantly formulated through a connection to structured prediction with latent variables. However, the empirical results fail to convincingly demonstrate that global optimization outperforms greedy tree learning. The globally optimized tree significantly outperforms the greedy tree on only a few datasets, while exhibiting higher computational cost.  

The paper is well-structured, but several points require clarification:  
- The stable SGD variant is not clearly described. Specifically, how are data points assigned to leaves? A pseudocode would be beneficial.  
- Figure 2: Are these results obtained using SGD or stable SGD? What precisely constitutes an "active" leaf?  
- In Line 10 of Algorithm 1, $\Theta$ is updated and projected onto a simplex. How is this projection solved in practice? It should also be noted that this projection ensures each row of $\Theta$ sums to one.
