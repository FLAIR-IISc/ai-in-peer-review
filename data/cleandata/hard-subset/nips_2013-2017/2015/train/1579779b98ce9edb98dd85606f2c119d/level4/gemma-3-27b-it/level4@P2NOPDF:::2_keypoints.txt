- Introduces joint training of all decision tree parameters (splits and leaf posteriors) as a single optimization problem, claiming novelty over greedy layer-by-layer methods.
- Formulates an upper-bounded approximation to make the joint optimization tractable.
- Writing is generally clear, but the derivation of the empirical loss bound contains a potential flaw.
- Empirical loss bound concern: a counterexample (W x = [5; -5], ĥ = sgn(Wx), g = [1;1]) shows the RHS of Eq.(7) can be smaller than the loss, invalidating the claimed upper bound.
- The supplementary proof of Proposition 1 does not address this counterexample; replacing argmax with max may fix the issue.
- Some tree leaves receive no data points, suggesting problems with the objective, initialization, or assignment heuristic (current fixed assignment seems suboptimal).
- Suggestion to motivate assignment as an EM or concave-convex procedure similar to clustering approaches.
- Questions about loss-augmented inference efficiency: limiting tree depth to 16 makes exhaustive search feasible, so impact of the restricted Hamming-ball search space on performance is unclear and needs broader evaluation.
- The term “leaves” and its specific meaning in the loss-augmented inference experiments require clarification.
- Missing baseline: greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization should be included for fair comparison.
- Pre-specifying tree depth is a limitation; more experiments should explore sensitivity to depth and runtime performance.
