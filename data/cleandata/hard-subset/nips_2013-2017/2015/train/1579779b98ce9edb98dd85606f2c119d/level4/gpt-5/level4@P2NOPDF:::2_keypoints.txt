- Proposal: jointly train all decision-tree parameters by framing training as a single optimization with an upper bound and tractable approximation; addresses an important gap vs. greedy training.  
- Quality: generally well written but theoretical reasoning has potential issues.  
- Clarity: clean derivation, but experiments and their presentation need strengthening.  
- Originality: timely and valuable study of joint training for decision trees.  
- Significance: promising approach but requires clarification and further evaluation.  
- Theoretical concern: possible flaw/ambiguity in the empirical loss bound—counterexample shows the proposed RHS can be smaller than the loss, and the supplement proof appears incomplete.  
- Unused leaves: some leaves receive no data, suggesting issues with objective or initialization; the heuristic fix seems ad hoc and should be justified (e.g., as EM or CCCP).  
- Loss-augmented inference: question the necessity of the specialized inference; brute-force may be feasible for depth ≤16—need ablation of Hamming-ball radius and clarification of “leaves” vs. assignments.  
- Missing baseline: add a greedy non-axis-aligned split baseline without initialization to isolate benefits of joint optimization.  
- Fixed depth limitation: requiring pre-specified tree depth is a notable drawback; discuss and evaluate sensitivity to depth.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer accepted the authors’ loss-bound explanation, suggests replacing “argmax” with “max,” and updated the score.  
- Additional suggestions: include runtime (training/inference) comparison and a more comprehensive empirical evaluation (Hamming radius ablation, depth/init sensitivity, comparison to greedy oblique baseline).  
- Overall: addresses an important problem; experimental evaluation is limited and initial derivation concerns need resolution—willing to adjust judgment after clarifications and additional experiments.
