- Proposes a non-greedy training procedure for oblique decision trees by casting tree training as latent structured prediction optimizing splits and leaves jointly under a global surrogate objective.  
- Introduces a convex–concave upper bound (Eq. 7) and a computationally efficient Hamming-ball radius-1 variant (Eq. 13) for tractable training.  
- Derives O(d) per-example gradient computation and O(d^2 p) loss-augmented inference, enabling SGD training of deep oblique trees with per-row norm constraints on W.  
- Introduces “stable SGD” (SSGD) which fixes leaf assignments for several steps to prevent collapsing leaves.  
- Builds on and contrasts with greedy axis-aligned trees (ID3/CART), oblique methods (OC1), max-margin/tree-SVM, soft-split HMEs, MILP approaches, online forests, and generalizes CO2 beyond depth-1.  
- The surrogate bound is well motivated and valid; per-row norm constraints are reasonable; complexity reductions are convincing, but the optimization is non-convex with no convergence or generalization guarantees.  
- Empirical results show consistent but modest accuracy gains and reduced overfitting over single-tree greedy baselines across several datasets and depths.  
- Runtime benefits of the fast Hamming-ball bound are demonstrated, but no statistical significance tests are reported.  
- Evaluations are limited to single trees; comparisons to strong ensembles (random forests, boosting) and soft/probabilistic tree models are not performed.  
- Reproducibility: initialization and hyperparameter tuning described, pseudocode provided, but important implementation details (preprocessing, batch size, momentum, epochs, tuning budgets for baselines) are missing.  
- Clarity: paper is generally readable; mapping to latent variables and navigation function is clear and Algorithm 1 is helpful.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: give a compact O(d^2 p) outline for Hamming-ball loss-augmented inference, summarize SSGD schedule, and include a small running example showing g vs. h and f(g) changes.  
- Originality: novel connection between tree training and latent structured prediction; fast Hamming-ball bound and per-row norm regularization are distinguishing contributions.  
- Significance: a solid, principled single-tree method with feasible complexity and consistent gains, but broader impact depends on scaling to ensembles and stronger empirical advances.  
- Pros: principled global formulation, efficient training for deep oblique trees, consistent accuracy improvements with reduced overfitting, and clear role of norm constraints as regularization/pruning.  
- Cons: gains are small without statistical tests, limited to single-tree evaluation, insufficient ablations (SSGD vs SGD, ν and radius sensitivity, initialization effects), and no empirical comparison to soft-split models.  
- Suggestions: add ablations (SGD vs SSGD, full vs Hamming-bound), report statistical significance/confidence intervals, evaluate ensembles using proposed trees, compare to HME, and provide more implementation details or code.  
- Recommendation: borderline to weak accept — technically sound and elegant with modest empirical benefits and limited evaluation scope, likely useful but not a breakthrough.
