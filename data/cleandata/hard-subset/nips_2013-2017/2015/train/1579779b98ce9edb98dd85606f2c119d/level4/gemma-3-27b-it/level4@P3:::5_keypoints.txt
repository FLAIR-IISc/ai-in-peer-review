- Proposes a novel algorithm to learn decision trees with linear split functions by jointly optimizing all split parameters and leaf distributions via a structured prediction with latent variables formulation.
- Derives a regularized upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with stochastic gradient descent.
- Demonstrates feasibility of training deep trees efficiently and reports performance improvements over greedy baselines on several benchmarks.
- Connects decision tree optimization to structured prediction, providing a theoretical foundation for the method.
- Develops a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm with O(d^2 p) complexity, enabling practical training of deep trees.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and situates the work within relevant prior literature.
- Empirical gains over strong greedy baselines are inconsistent and dataset-dependent, with modest improvements in some cases.
- The non-greedy approach is more computationally expensive than greedy methods, raising scalability concerns.
- Description of the Stable SGD algorithm is insufficient; the mechanism for assigning data points to leaves is unclear and needs pseudo-code.
- Figure 2 needs clarification whether results use SGD or Stable SGD and a definition of an “active” leaf.
- Algorithm 1, Line 10: explain how projection of Θ onto the simplex is implemented and that it ensures each row sums to one.
- Clarify Line 407–408 regarding tuning number of features in axis-aligned trees, since standard trees evaluate all features at each split.
- Equations (7)–(9): replace "argmax" with "max" for correctness.
- Figure 1 is referenced but not discussed; add a brief explanation of its relevance in the main text.
- Multiple minor typos and phrasing errors identified (Lines 141, 238, 301, 360, 404) that should be corrected and FSGD defined.
- Supplementary material contains notational errors that must be fixed (specified corrections to sgn and l(·) expressions).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretically grounded approach, addresses greedy induction limitation, and achieves computational efficiency for deep trees.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited and inconsistent empirical benefits, higher computational cost, and unclear Stable SGD description.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution but practical benefits not fully convincing; recommendation is weak reject pending stronger empirical evidence, Stable SGD clarification, and fixes of minor issues.
