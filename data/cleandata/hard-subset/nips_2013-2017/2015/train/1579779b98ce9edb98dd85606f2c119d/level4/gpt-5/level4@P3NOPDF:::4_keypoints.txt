- Reformulates global optimization of decision trees as latent structured prediction using sign-vector latent variables and optimizes a differentiable surrogate via SGD and loss-augmented inference.
- Provides efficient algorithms for the loss-augmented inference problems.
- Key novelty: structured-prediction formulation enabling principled global training beyond greedy splits.
- Strength: conceptually elegant reformulation enabling non-greedy global training.
- Strength: clear algorithmic contribution with reductions to loss-augmented inference and efficient solvers.
- Strength: optimization exhibits sparse updates that typically modify a single node per step, suggesting computational benefits and interesting model-growth dynamics.
- Concern: need analysis of sparse-update dynamics—when is it better to add a node vs. revise an existing split, and does the method effectively become a regularized greedy procedure in some regimes?
- Concern: need conditions under which regularization yields convergence to a finite number of active nodes (as suggested by Fig. 2).
- Concern: ambiguity at zero weights—current rule (take right branch) is arbitrary; alternative of treating node as a leaf and allowing later splits should be defined and analyzed.
- Concern: zero-initialization dynamics and formalization of leaf→internal-node transitions require theoretical and empirical study and relation to pruning/growing strategies.
- Practical motivation: if interpretability is a goal, L2 oblique splits are dense and hard to interpret; paper should evaluate L1 or group sparsity and report sparsity vs. accuracy trade-offs.
- Experiments: greedy baseline is under-specified—pruning strategies, selection criteria, and hyperparameter ranges must be detailed and tuned comparably.
- Experiments: add additional baselines including global-selection heuristics, stronger oblique/non-greedy methods, and standard non-tree models (random forests, boosting) for context.
- Reporting: include training time, convergence behavior, learned tree depth/size, variation with regularization, and an ablation on surrogate bound tightness.
- Clarity: streamline inference sections (e.g., summarize Section 6.2 and make Section 6.4’s decomposition explicit) and state initialization, zero-weight behavior, and tie-breaking rules.
- Scope: title mentions forests but no forest experiments—either narrow the title or extend the method to ensembles and evaluate scalability.
- Post-rebuttal: zero-initialization remains a key unresolved issue; authors should analyze and experiment with it and relate resulting procedure to known greedy methods.
- Overall: a clever, promising reformulation with efficient algorithms but needs stronger practical motivation, interpretability-focused regularization and metrics, clearer zero-weight handling, and broader/tuned baselines to be convincing.
