This paper presents an algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions at the internal nodes. Unlike standard greedy, top-down approaches, the proposed algorithm optimizes all tree parameters—the weights of the split functions and the output distributions at the leaves—globally. The authors achieve this by framing the problem as structured prediction with latent variables, which elegantly leads to an L2-regularized upper bound on the tree's empirical loss. This objective function is then minimized using stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to learn the tree parameters.

While the formulation of the tree optimization problem is elegant, particularly its connection to structured prediction with latent variables, the empirical results presented in the paper are not fully convincing. The proposed global optimization approach significantly outperforms the greedy baseline on only a couple of datasets, while being considerably more computationally intensive. This raises questions about the practical trade-offs and benefits of the proposed method over simpler, standard techniques.

The paper is well-structured, but several points require clarification:

*   The description of the "stable SGD" variant is unclear. Specifically, the mechanism for assigning data points to leaves needs further explanation. Including pseudo-code for this procedure would greatly improve clarity.
*   Regarding Figure 2, it is not specified whether these results were obtained using the standard SGD or the stable SGD variant. Additionally, the term "active leaf" is not defined; please clarify its meaning.
*   In Algorithm 1 (line 10), the parameter Θ is updated and then projected onto a simplex. The paper should describe the practical method used to solve this projection problem. It would also be helpful to explicitly state that this projection ensures that each row of Θ sums to one, enforcing a valid probability distribution.
