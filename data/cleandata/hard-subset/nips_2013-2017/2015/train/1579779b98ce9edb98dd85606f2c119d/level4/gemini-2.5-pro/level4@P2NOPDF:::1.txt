This paper proposes a novel and elegant non-convex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a pre-defined structure. The work is very clearly written and presents a theoretically interesting alternative to the standard greedy, heuristic-based methods for training decision trees.

**Post-Rebuttal Assessment**

After discussion with the other reviewers and considering the author rebuttal, my recommendation remains to accept the paper. The primary merit of this work lies in the elegance of its approach, which is likely to inspire further research into related global optimization techniques for tree-based models. While I concur with the other reviewers that the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming, the conceptual contribution is strong enough to warrant publication. Stronger empirical results would have elevated this paper to a higher score, but the current rating is appropriate given its balance of strengths and weaknesses.

**Strengths**

1.  **Novelty and Elegance:** The core contribution is a principled and well-formulated non-convex relaxation for a notoriously difficult optimization problem. This moves the field beyond purely greedy approaches and offers a new perspective on decision tree training.

2.  **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written. The authors do an excellent job of motivating the problem and clearly articulating their proposed method, making the complex subject matter accessible.

3.  **Potential for Impact:** The ideas presented in this paper are likely to stimulate further research. The framework could potentially be extended or adapted to other structured prediction problems, opening up new avenues of investigation.

**Weaknesses**

1.  **Disappointing Experimental Results:** The main weakness is that the empirical validation does not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over traditional techniques. The performance gains are marginal, which raises questions about the practical utility of adopting this more complex optimization procedure over simpler, faster, and well-established greedy methods.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

The authors could consider a few avenues to potentially strengthen the empirical performance and analysis:

1.  **Hybrid Optimization Strategy:** The global optimization method could be used to find a strong initial tree structure, followed by a local refinement step. For example, one could apply the proposed algorithm and then discard the final split in each branch, re-optimizing that split and the subsequent leaf node values using a standard greedy criterion. This hybrid approach might leverage the benefits of a globally-aware structure while still allowing for fine-tuning at the leaves.

2.  **Analysis of Overfitting:** A critical aspect to evaluate is the model's propensity to overfit. Global optimization provides the model with a greater capacity to fit the training data perfectly, which can often lead to poorer generalization on unseen data compared to heuristic methods, whose sub-optimality can act as a form of implicit regularization. An explicit analysis of this trade-off would be a valuable addition to the paper.
