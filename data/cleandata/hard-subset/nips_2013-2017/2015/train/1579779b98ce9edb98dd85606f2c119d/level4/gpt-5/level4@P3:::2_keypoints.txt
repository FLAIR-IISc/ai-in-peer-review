- Proposes a non-greedy algorithm that jointly optimizes all split parameters and leaf predictors via a latent-variable structured-prediction view of tree training.
- Derives a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss enabling efficient SGD optimization.
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference restricted to a Hamming ball of radius 1, reducing per-example complexity from exponential in depth to O(d^2 p).
- Empirically shows consistent accuracy improvements over greedy baselines on several LibSVM multiclass benchmarks.
- Positions work relative to prior greedy (ID3/CART), non-greedy (MIP, max-margin trees), soft-split (HME) methods, and relates to latent structured SVMs and CO2.
- After clarification, the loss upper bound (Eq. 7) is technically sound; suggestion to use max instead of argmax and specify tie-breaking for sgn(Wx).
- The per-row norm constraint on W is a sensible regularizer; SGD/SSGD and projection steps are standard and appropriate.
- Empirics demonstrate scaling advantages of fast inference, but the evaluation is limited and could be more comprehensive.
- Paper is generally well written with clear connections to latent structured prediction and helpful complexity analysis.
- Presentation issues: clarify usage of max/argmax and tie-breaking, define “leaves” and the d+1 Hamming-ball candidates, and situate SSGD with EM/CCCP intuition.
- Main novelty is the latent-variable reformulation with a tractable convex–concave surrogate and an efficient Hamming-ball loss-augmented inference scheme.
- Significance: practical importance for standalone trees and ensembles, with scalability and consistent gains suggesting adoption potential.
- Strengths include a principled global objective, per-example complexity independent of number of leaves, interpretable regularization, consistent accuracy gains, and usable algorithmic details.
- Key weaknesses: limited experimental scope (datasets, seeds, significance), missing baselines (from-scratch oblique trees), and lack of depth/model-selection guidance.
- Requests: report wall-clock runtime vs depth and dataset size, compare exact vs Hamming-ball inference on accuracy and bound tightness, and quantify inactive leaves and their impact.
- Suggests discussing model selection/automatic pruning, providing validation curves across depths, and exploring entropy/balancing regularizers to prevent degeneracy.
- Pros for acceptance: principled method, efficient scalable training, consistent empirical gains, and addressing a longstanding limitation of greedy induction.
- Cons for acceptance: narrow experiments, presentation clarifications needed, and unexplored sensitivity to initialization and depth; recommends acceptance conditional on stronger experiments and clarity improvements.
