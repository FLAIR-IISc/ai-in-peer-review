After reviewing the authors' rebuttal and discussing the paper with the other reviewers, I maintain my positive assessment. This is a strong paper that merits publication due to the elegance of its core idea and its potential to stimulate further research on related approaches.

This very clearly written paper proposes a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The primary strength of the work lies in this elegant formulation. However, I agree with my fellow reviewers that the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. While the novelty of the method is significant, stronger empirical results would have warranted an even higher rating. As it stands, I believe the current score appropriately reflects the balance between the paper's theoretical contribution and its current practical performance.

For future work, the authors might consider a simple modification that could improve performance: after completing the global optimization, prune the final split in each tree and then re-optimize that split and its corresponding leaf nodes using a standard greedy procedure.

Additionally, an important aspect to evaluate is the method's propensity for overfitting. One might hypothesize that a global optimization technique is more susceptible to overfitting than traditional greedy approaches, and an empirical investigation into this would be a valuable addition.
