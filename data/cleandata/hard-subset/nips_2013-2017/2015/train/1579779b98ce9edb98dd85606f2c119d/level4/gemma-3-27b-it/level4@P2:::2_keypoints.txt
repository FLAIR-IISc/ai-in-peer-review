- Proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters via a single optimization with an upper bound and tractable approximation.  
- Topic is important because existing methods are typically greedy and layer-wise.  
- Method appears technically sound but the empirical loss bound derivation may be flawed and needs clearer justification.  
- Paper is generally well-written; derivations are clean but experimental presentation and implementation/hyperparameter details need improvement.  
- The formulation (structured prediction with latent variables) and optimization techniques are a novel and valuable contribution despite prior non-greedy work.  
- The approach could yield significant improvements in performance and generalization if limitations are addressed.  
- Potential flaw: the argument for the upper bound (lines 216–218) requires clarification and the supplementary proof needs careful verification.  
- Some leaves remain unassigned to data points, suggesting issues with the objective, initialization, or optimization; more justification and experiments required.  
- The Hamming-ball restriction for loss-augmented inference needs stronger empirical justification and comparison to exhaustive search; clarify "leaves" in the supplement.  
- Missing baseline: no greedy baseline training non-axis-aligned splits is included to isolate benefits of non-greedy optimization.  
- Fixed tree depth is a limitation; sensitivity analysis or adaptive depth selection should be explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, novel formulation, promising empirical gains, and an efficient optimization approach.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear loss-bound derivation, unassigned leaves concern, limited experiments, missing baselines, and fixed depth limitation.  
- Overall: promising and valuable contribution but requires a strong rebuttal and additional theoretical and experimental clarification to be ready for acceptance.
