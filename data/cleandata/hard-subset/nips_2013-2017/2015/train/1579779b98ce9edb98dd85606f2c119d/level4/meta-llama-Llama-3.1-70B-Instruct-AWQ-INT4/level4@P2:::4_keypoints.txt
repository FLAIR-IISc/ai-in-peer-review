- Proposes global optimization of decision trees by reformulating as (latent) structured prediction using sign-vector latent variables.  
- Reformulation enables using SGD to optimize an upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Main contributions: casting global DT optimization as structured prediction and developing efficient optimization algorithms.  
- Method permits SGD via solving loss-augmented problems that exploit the problem's structure.  
- Gradient updates are sparse, typically modifying only one node's weight vector per update.  
- Suggests potential to grow arbitrarily deep trees by initializing weights at zero and making them nonzero over time.  
- Concern: motivation and significance of gains over naive methods are unclear.  
- Concern: L2 regularization may produce dense, non-interpretable weight vectors, undermining decision-tree interpretability.  
- Concern: Experiments lack sufficient detail on baselines (e.g., pruning strategies) and other comparisons.  
- Presentation is generally well-written but inference description and some sections need clearer/succinct exposition.  
- Overall summary: novel and clever approach, but practical usefulness is uncertain due to interpretability and experimental detail issues.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 6/10.
