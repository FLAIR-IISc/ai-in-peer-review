Summary: The paper proposes a method to jointly train all parameters of decision trees (both decision splits and leaf posterior probabilities). This is an important direction because most existing approaches train trees greedily, layer by layer. The task is cast as a single optimization problem, which is then upper-bounded and approximated to yield a tractable formulation.

Comments:
- Possible flaw in the derivation of the empirical loss bound. At minimum, the argument around l.216f is confusing: ‚Äú... for all values of $\bg \neq \sgn(W\bx)$, the right-hand side can only become larger than when $\bg = \sgn(W\bx)$ ...‚Äù. Consider the example $W\bx = [5; -5]$, so $\hat \bh = \sgn(W\bx) = [1; -1]$ and $\hat \bh^\top W\bx = 10$. Choosing $\bg = [1; 1]$ gives $\bg^\top W\bx = 0$. Then the right-hand side of Eq. (7) is
$\bg^\top W\bx - \hat \bh^\top W\bx + \ell(\ldots) = -10 + \ell(\ldots)$,
which could be smaller than $\ell(\ldots)$ and thus not a valid upper bound. The proof of Prop. 1 in the supplementary material does not cover this case and therefore seems incomplete.
- Why are some leaves not assigned any data points? This is unusual and may indicate an issue with the objective or the initialization. The proposed heuristic with a fixed assignment of data to leaves seems suboptimal; it might be possible to motivate it via an EM/CCCP-style procedure, similar to other clustering methods.
- Regarding efficient loss-augmented inference: with depth at most 16, exhaustively checking $2^{16} = 65{,}536$ configurations still seems feasible. How much did restricting the search to a Hamming ball affect performance? You present one result in the supplement, but does this generalize? Also, what exactly is meant by ‚Äúleaves‚Äù in that context?
- A baseline that greedily trains non‚Äìaxis-aligned (oblique) split functions without any initialization is missing.
- The tree depth must be specified in advance. This limitation should be discussed more carefully, and the experiments should more thoroughly analyze sensitivity to the depth parameter.
