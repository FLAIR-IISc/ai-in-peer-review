- Proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters via a single optimization, approximated to a tractable form.  
- Well-written and original direction, but potential flaws in reasoning and limited experimental evaluation.  
- Confusing/possibly incorrect derivation of the empirical loss bound (Eq. 7); provided counterexample suggests the proof (Prop.1) is incomplete.  
- Some tree leaves receive no data points; this is unusual and may indicate problems with the objective or initialization.  
- The authors’ fixed assignment heuristic for empty leaves seems sub-optimal; an EM/concave–convex justification might help.  
- Questioned importance of the efficient loss-augmented inference design: restricted search (Hamming ball) impact on performance is unclear and may not be necessary for depths ≤16.  
- The meaning and role of “leaves” in the inference/search context need clarification.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard greedy training of non-axis-aligned splits without initialization.  
- Tree depth must be specified a priori; this limitation needs deeper discussion and more experiments analyzing depth sensitivity.  
- Suggestion to replace argmax with max in the exposition; request for a runtime performance comparison to strengthen the paper.  
- Overall: interesting and valuable problem, but experiments are limited and the potential derivation flaw should be addressed in rebuttal.
