- Paper proposes jointly training all decision tree parameters via a single optimization problem approximated for tractability.  
- Well-written and clean derivation, but there may be flaws in the theoretical reasoning.  
- Potential error in the empirical loss bound: a constructed counterexample shows the claimed upper bound can fail and Prop. 1’s proof in the supplement is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points; this is unexplained and may indicate problems with the objective or initialization; the proposed fixed-assignment heuristic seems ad hoc.  
- The claim about the necessity of efficient loss-augmented inference is questioned since trees of depth ≤16 allow exhaustive search; impact of the Hamming-ball restricted search is unclear and its generality is not demonstrated.  
- The term “leaves” is not clearly defined in places, hurting clarity.  
- Missing baseline: greedy training of trees with non-axis-aligned split functions without any special initialization.  
- Need to address the significant limitation that tree depth must be specified in advance and to evaluate sensitivity to depth more thoroughly.  
- Suggest replacing argmax with max in the bound and provide a clearer explanation in rebuttal; score may change after clarification.  
- Recommend adding runtime comparisons and a more thorough experimental evaluation to strengthen the paper.
