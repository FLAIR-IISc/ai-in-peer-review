- Proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters via a single optimization, approximated for tractability.  
- Paper is well-written but contains potential reasoning flaws that need resolution.  
- Derivation is clear, but experiments and their presentation require improvement.  
- Joint training of decision trees is an original and significant direction.  
- Flaw in empirical loss bound derivation (Eq. (7)): counterexample shows the claimed upper bound may not hold; proof of Proposition 1 is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization; the fixed-assignment heuristic is likely suboptimal.  
- Suggestion to justify leaf-assignment heuristics via EM or concave-convex procedures (akin to clustering).  
- Loss-augmented inference restricts search to a Hamming ball; unclear how much this hurts performance for deep trees (e.g., depth 16) and what “leaves” precisely refers to.  
- Missing baseline: greedy training of non-axis-aligned split functions (without the proposed initialization) is not included.  
- Requiring tree depth in advance is a limitation; needs deeper discussion and systematic evaluation of depth effects.  
- After author response: recommend replacing argmax with max in derivation; derivation concern still needs careful resolution.  
- Recommend adding runtime comparisons and broader experimental evaluation; reviewer is open to adjusting score after a comprehensive rebuttal.
