- Introduces a novel structured-prediction formulation for building optimal fixed-depth decision trees that avoids greedy splitting.  
- Method is theoretically sound and yields consistent but modest test accuracy improvements over standard baselines.  
- Reported accuracy gains are small (rarely exceed 1–2%), raising doubts about practical impact.  
- Lacks thorough computational trade-off analysis: no runtime or memory comparisons versus greedy or other optimization methods.  
- No detailed scalability evaluation for larger datasets or deeper trees, limiting assessment of real-world applicability.  
- Experimental evaluation centers on small-scale UCI-style datasets, leaving generalization to high-dimensional/high-volume data unclear.  
- Missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of the structured loss versus optimization strategy, reducing interpretability.  
- Writing is clear but needs a more precise explanation of why the structured approach outperforms greedy methods (e.g., feature interactions or overfitting mitigation).  
- Overall contribution is incremental; paper’s main value is methodological framing rather than demonstrated practical advantage.  
- To strengthen the paper, authors should show larger gains on relevant settings or provide a rigorous cost–benefit/scalability analysis.
