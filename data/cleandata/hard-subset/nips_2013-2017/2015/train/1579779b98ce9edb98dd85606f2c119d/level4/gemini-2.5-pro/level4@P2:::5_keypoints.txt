- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm for learning oblique decision trees by framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent binary path variables.  
- Derives a global convex-concave upper bound as a smooth surrogate empirical-loss objective optimized jointly over splits and leaves via SGD.  
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces per-step complexity from exponential to polynomial in tree depth.  
- Applies the method to benchmark classification datasets and compares against several greedy baselines.  
- Strength: original, conceptually elegant formulation connecting decision-tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Strength: technically solid derivation of the surrogate objective and development of an efficient inference procedure.  
- Strength: use of SGD and a smooth surrogate makes the approach applicable to large-scale datasets.  
- Weakness: empirical gains over greedy baselines are marginal in many cases, questioning practical significance given added complexity.  
- Weakness: higher computational cost than greedy methods, with unclear payoff across general use cases.  
- Weakness: paper lacks clarity in algorithmic details, hindering reproducibility.  
- For acceptance: originality and conceptual contribution could inspire future work in the area.  
- For acceptance: technical contributions (surrogate objective and fast inference) are strong.  
- Against acceptance: limited empirical significance and modest performance improvements do not justify complexity.  
- Against acceptance: unclear description of important algorithmic components (e.g., Stable SGD) reduces reproducibility.  
- Key clarity question: provide detailed pseudocode and exact mechanism for determining/updating data point-to-leaf assignments a(x) in Stable SGD.  
- Key clarity question: clarify whether experimental "non-greedy" results use standard SGD or Stable SGD.  
- Key clarity question: define precisely what constitutes an "active" versus "inactive" leaf (implicit pruning).  
- Key clarity question: explicitly define "FSGD" (presumably SGD with fast loss-augmented inference).  
- Minor corrections: replace argmax with max in Eqs. (7)-(9); fix pluralization and typos (e.g., "An key" → "A key").  
- Minor clarification: line 404 likely should refer to "validation error" rather than "training error" for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Minor clarification: explain what "number of features evaluated" means when tuning axis-aligned trees (since CART typically evaluates all features).  
- Supplement fixes: correct notation in supplement (e.g., max{g = sgn...} and l(Θ^Tf(sgn(Wx)), y) for consistency).
