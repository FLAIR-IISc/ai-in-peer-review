- Paper proposes jointly training all decision-tree parameters by reframing as a single optimization and deriving an upper bound and tractable approximations.  
- Quality: paper is well written but may contain reasoning flaws.  
- Clarity: derivations are clean, but experiments and their presentation need improvement.  
- Originality: tackling joint training for decision trees is important and timely.  
- Significance: solution is valuable but has notable shortcomings.  
- Possible flaw in empirical loss bound (Eq. 7): reviewer provides a counterexample showing the bound argument may be incorrect and notes the supplementary proof appears incomplete.  
- Unassigned leaves: some leaves receive no data, suggesting problems with the objective or initialization; the proposed fixed-assignment heuristic seems ad hoc but might be justifiable via EM/concave–convex arguments.  
- Efficient loss-augmented inference: question about impact of restricting search to a Hamming ball, whether the reported single result generalizes, and clarification needed on what “leaves” refers to.  
- Missing baseline: request to include a baseline that trains non–axis-aligned splits with a standard greedy procedure (no special initialization).  
- Depth selection: tree depth must be chosen a priori; this limitation needs fuller discussion and systematic evaluation.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors’ explanation of the loss bound was helpful (suggest replacing argmax with max); reviewer requests runtime comparisons and more extensive empirical evaluation.  
- Pre-rebuttal overall: interesting and relevant submission with limited experiments and a potential derivation issue; reviewer is willing to revise score after rebuttal.
