- Reformulates search for an optimal fixed-depth decision tree as a structured prediction problem.  
- Represents an interesting departure from standard greedy top-down algorithms (e.g., CART, C4.5).  
- Main strength is the conceptual reframing, offering a fresh perspective on tree induction.  
- Promises to overcome greedy methods’ failure to capture non-myopic feature interactions.  
- Experiments show consistent improvements in test accuracy over greedy baselines.  
- Reported accuracy gains are marginal, questioning practical impact.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational cost (e.g., training time) to assess trade-offs.  
- Would be improved by clearer complexity discussion and compelling scenarios where the global approach clearly outperforms greedy methods.
