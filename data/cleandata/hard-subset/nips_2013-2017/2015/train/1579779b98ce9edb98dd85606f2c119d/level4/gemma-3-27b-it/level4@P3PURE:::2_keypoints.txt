- Paper proposes jointly training all decision tree parameters (splits and posteriors) via a single optimization with an upper-bound approximation.
- Well-written and novel direction, but potential flaws exist in reasoning and some experimental presentation issues.
- Derivation of the empirical loss bound (Eq. 7) is questionable; reviewer provides a counterexample and finds the proof of Proposition 1 incomplete.
- Suggestion to replace argmax with max in the clarified reasoning.
- Some leaves receive no data points, which is unusual and may indicate problems with the objective or initialization.
- The fixed assignment heuristic for leaf-data allocation appears sub-optimal; an EM or concave-convex motivation might be appropriate.
- Questioning the necessity of the efficient loss-augmented inference and the impact of restricting search to a Hamming ball versus full greedy search up to depth 16.
- Definition of “leaves” in the experimental/inference context is unclear.
- Missing baseline: no standard greedy training of non-axis-aligned split functions (without initialization) is included.
- Tree depth must be specified a priori; paper should discuss this limitation and evaluate sensitivity to depth more thoroughly.
- Experimental evaluation is limited; a runtime comparison and broader experiments would strengthen the paper.
- Reviewer is willing to adjust their score pending a comprehensive rebuttal addressing these theoretical and empirical concerns.
