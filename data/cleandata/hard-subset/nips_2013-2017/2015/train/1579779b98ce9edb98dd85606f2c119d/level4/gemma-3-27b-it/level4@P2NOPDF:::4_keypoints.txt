- Proposes a novel global decision-tree optimization by reformulating as latent structured prediction using sign vectors, deriving a differentiable upper bound and SGD algorithms, evaluated against greedy training.

- Main contributions: recasting tree optimization as structured prediction and developing efficient optimization methods; theoretically interesting.

- Practical motivation and magnitude of empirical gains over simpler alternatives are unclear.

- Notable sparsity in gradient updates (each update changes one node’s weight) could enable new strategies (e.g., gradually growing an infinitely deep tree), but this potential is unexplored.

- Unclear relationship to greedy methods: could converge to equivalent solutions, novel variants, or limited-depth trees with regularization—needs investigation.

- Ambiguity in handling zero-weighted nodes: current description implies always traversing right branch, which is likely suboptimal; should define behavior (e.g., output decision and allow later splitting).

- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization likely yields dense, non-interpretable weights; L1 sparsity may hurt performance—trade-offs must be studied.

- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: baseline pruning strategies and hyperparameter tuning for greedy methods not detailed; simple global baselines (e.g., greedy on random subsets) omitted.

- Paper clarity is generally good but inference exposition (Section 6.2 and 6.4) could be more succinct and explicit.

- Overall: clever and thought-provoking approach, but practical utility remains unconvincing due to interpretability issues and weaker experimental validation.

- Title mismatch: mentions forests but does not address them.

- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight initialization/behavior still a key open question; reviewer encourages exploring zero-init gradual-growth regime.
