- Novel idea: frames optimal fixed-depth decision tree learning as a structured prediction problem; well-motivated.  
- Clear formulation and strong connection to existing structured prediction techniques.  
- Empirical results: outperforms standard greedy tree construction across datasets, but accuracy gains are generally modest.  
- Experimental evaluation is reasonably thorough with comparisons to several baselines.  
- Unclear why improvements are small—need analysis whether due to decision tree limits or the structured prediction formulation.  
- Presentation issues: notation is inconsistent (e.g., definition of 'z' buried); recommend a notation table and more concise explanations (loss function description verbose).  
- Computational complexity is a major limitation: scales poorly with tree depth and dataset size; requires more detailed discussion, runtime evidence on larger datasets, and exploration of approximations/heuristics.  
- Related work: adequate overall but lacks deeper comparison to optimal tree construction methods (e.g., dynamic programming approaches).  
- Overall: solid, novel, and technically sound work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address notation, complexity discussion, and related work expansion.
