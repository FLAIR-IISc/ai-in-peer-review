- Summary: Proposes a non-greedy global optimization for decision trees by treating paths as latent variables and optimizing a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss via SGD with loss-augmented inference.  
- Quality and Clarity: Generally well-written with a clear, elegant derivation, but there are concerns about technical correctness and limited experiments.  
- Originality and Significance: Novel and potentially significant framing (latent structured prediction for trees); could be a major contribution if technically sound.  
- Main theoretical concern: The derived upper bound in Eq. (7) may be invalid in some cases (counterexample given); the proof (Prop.1) appears incomplete and needs clarification.  
- Inactive leaves & SSGD: Some leaves become empty during training; the proposed Stable SGD heuristic seems ad-hoc and needs formal justification (e.g., EM or CCCP interpretation).  
- Fast loss-augmented inference: The Hamming-ball radius-1 approximation speeds inference but its practical necessity and impact on accuracy are not convincingly demonstrated; more ablation needed.  
- Experimental baselines: Missing a key baseline — a standard greedy oblique tree learner trained from scratch — to assess the benefits of global optimization.  
- Fixed tree depth: Method requires pre-specified depth; sensitivity to this parameter and practical implications are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Conclusion: Interesting and important idea but limited experiments and a potentially critical theoretical flaw; reviewer would reconsider after a careful rebuttal addressing these issues.
