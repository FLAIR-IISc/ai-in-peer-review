Following the author rebuttal and discussion with my fellow reviewers, I maintain my position that this is a strong paper worthy of acceptance. Its publication is merited by the elegance of the proposed method and its potential to inspire further investigation into related approaches. I do concede that the empirical validation is somewhat underwhelming. However, more compelling results would have warranted an even higher score, so I believe my current rating is appropriate.

This is a very clearly written paper that proposes a nonconvex relaxation for globally optimizing decision trees of a fixed structure. The approach is elegant, though the experimental results are somewhat disappointing.

One suggestion for a straightforward modification that might improve performance is to apply the global optimization, then prune the final split in each tree, and subsequently re-optimize that last level and the leaf nodes using a conventional greedy algorithm.

Additionally, it is important for the authors to evaluate the possibility that a global optimization technique, such as the one proposed, may be more susceptible to overfitting compared to standard greedy alternatives.
