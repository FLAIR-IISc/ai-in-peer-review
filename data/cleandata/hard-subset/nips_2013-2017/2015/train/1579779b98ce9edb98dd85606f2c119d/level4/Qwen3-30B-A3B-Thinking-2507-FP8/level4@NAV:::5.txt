

This paper introduces an algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions at nodes, optimizing tree parameters (split function weights and leaf output distributions) globally rather than via standard greedy methods. An upper bound on empirical loss is proposed and regularized using L2 regularization on split weights to enable smoother optimization, with parameters identified through stochastic gradient descent. While the tree optimization problem is elegantly formulated via a link to structured prediction with latent variables, the empirical results fail to convincingly demonstrate that global optimization outperforms greedy tree learning. The globally optimized tree significantly outperforms greedy trees on only a few datasets, at the cost of increased computational complexity.  

The paper is well-structured but requires clarification on several points:  
- The stable SGD variant lacks sufficient detail; specifically, the assignment of data points to leaves needs explanation, and a pseudocode would be beneficial.  
- Figure 2: Are results from SGD or stable SGD? What constitutes an "active" leaf?  
- Line 10 of Algorithm 1 updates and projects Θ onto a simplex; please clarify the projection method and note that this ensures each row of Θ sums to one.
