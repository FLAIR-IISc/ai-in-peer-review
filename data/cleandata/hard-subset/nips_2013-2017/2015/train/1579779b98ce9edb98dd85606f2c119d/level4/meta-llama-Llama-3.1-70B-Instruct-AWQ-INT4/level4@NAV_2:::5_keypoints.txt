- Proposes a novel algorithm for globally optimizing decision trees with linear split functions using an upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Employs L2 regularization on split weights and frames tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Paper is well-structured and the algorithm is clearly and concisely described.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing: global optimization only outperforms greedy learning on a few datasets and is more computationally intensive.  
- Main contributions are the problem formulation and an efficient algorithm to optimize the proposed upper bound.  
- Provides a thorough computational complexity analysis and demonstrates scalability.  
- The “stable” SGD variant is not clearly described; a pseudo-code and clarification are needed.  
- Contains minor comments and typographical errors throughout the paper.  
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental support, but the significance and impact are not well highlighted.  
- High originality, but comparisons to other state-of-the-art approaches are insufficient.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, complexity analysis, clear organization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical evidence favoring global optimization, need for clearer exposition and stronger empirical comparisons.
