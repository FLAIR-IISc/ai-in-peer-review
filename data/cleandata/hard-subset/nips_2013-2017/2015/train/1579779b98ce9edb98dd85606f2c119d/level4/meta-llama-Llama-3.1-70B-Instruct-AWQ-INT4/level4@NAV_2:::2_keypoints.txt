- Proposes joint training of all decision-tree parameters via a single optimization problem with an upper-bound approximation for tractability.  
- Well written with a clean derivation, but experiments and presentation need improvement and reasoning may have flaws.  
- Novel connection between decision-tree learning and structured prediction with binary latent variables per split, plus a convex-concave upper bound and SGD optimization.  
- Derivation of the empirical loss bound is unclear (line 216 confusing) and the proof of Proposition 1 is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points, suggesting possible problems with the objective or initialization; the fixed-assignment heuristic seems suboptimal.  
- The fixed-assignment heuristic could be better motivated via EM or concave-convex procedures, akin to clustering approaches.  
- The claimed need for efficient loss-augmented inference is not convincing for shallow trees (depth ≤16) where brute-force search is feasible.  
- Unclear how the Hamming-ball restricted search space affected performance.  
- Missing baseline that trains non-axis-aligned split functions greedily.  
- Requirement to specify tree depth a priori is a limitation and needs more careful discussion and evaluation.  
- Experimental evaluation is somewhat limited and should be more thorough.  
- Overall: novel and well-motivated work but weakened by unclear theoretical steps, missing baselines, and limited experiments.
