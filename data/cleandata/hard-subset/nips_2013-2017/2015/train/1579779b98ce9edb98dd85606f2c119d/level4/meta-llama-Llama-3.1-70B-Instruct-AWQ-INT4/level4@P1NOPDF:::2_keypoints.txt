- Proposes joint training of decision-tree splits and leaf posteriors via a single optimization approximated for tractability.  
- Well written with a clean derivation, but there may be flaws in the theoretical reasoning.  
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss upper-bound (Eq. 7); reviewer gives a counterexample showing the bound may not hold and says the supplemental proof is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points, which is unusual and may indicate issues with the objective or initialization.  
- The heuristic of fixing data-to-leaf assignments seems sub-optimal; could be motivated/justified via EM or CCCP-like approaches.  
- Questions the practical need for the efficient loss-augmented inference: brute-force over 2^depth may be feasible for depth ≤16; asks how restricting to a Hamming-ball affects performance and requests clarification of “leaves.”  
- Missing a baseline that trains non-axis-aligned split functions in the standard greedy manner (without initialization) for fair comparison.  
- The requirement to set tree depth ahead of time is a limitation that should be discussed and evaluated more thoroughly.  
- Experimental presentation and evaluation are limited; runtime comparisons and more extensive depth experiments are requested.  
- Minor suggestion: replace argmax with max in the loss-bound exposition.  
- Reviewer is open to adjusting their score pending a careful rebuttal addressing the derivation flaw and expanded experiments.
