- Proposes a non-greedy decision tree optimization by reformulating learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Introduces a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective enabling SGD-based optimization.  
- Provides computational complexity O(d²p) for optimization (d = tree depth, p = input dimension).  
- Jointly optimizes split functions across all levels under a global objective, unlike conventional greedy node-wise methods.  
- Novel theoretical connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction is a key contribution.  
- Develops an efficient algorithm for loss-augmented inference that scales to deep trees.  
- Empirical results show non-greedy approach outperforms several greedy baselines across classification benchmarks.  
- Extends prior global optimization work by overcoming computational limitations and handling general loss functions.  
- The convex-concave bound is rigorously derived and algorithmic details are thoroughly developed.  
- Paper lacks details on hyperparameter tuning and the impact of different regularization strategies.  
- Comparison omits important baselines (e.g., pruning strategies for greedy trees, ensembles of greedy trees).  
- Exposition could be clearer—Section 6.2 should emphasize independent leaf inference; Section 6.3 should clarify the Hamming ball constraint.  
- Originality is strong due to the structured-prediction reformulation and efficient algorithms.  
- Practical significance is questionable because L2-regularized dense weights harm interpretability; L1 or sparse alternatives are not explored.  
- Handling of zero-weight nodes is arbitrary and potentially problematic, especially if weights are initialized to zero.  
- Title mentions "forests" but the paper evaluates only single trees, missing discussion of forest construction.  
- Overall: technically sound and interesting, but needs stronger experimental comparisons and solutions for interpretability and zero-weight issues before acceptance.
