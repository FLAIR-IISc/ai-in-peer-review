This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree learning by formulating it as a structured prediction problem with latent variables. The authors establish a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction, enabling the use of a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss. This formulation allows for efficient learning of deep trees using stochastic gradient descent.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of traditional greedy decision tree induction methods. The proposed approach is thoroughly evaluated on several benchmark datasets, demonstrating improved test performance and better generalization compared to greedy baselines.

The strengths of the paper include its originality, technical soundness, and thorough evaluation. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the proposed method, including its connection to structured prediction and the use of a convex-concave upper bound. The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

However, there are some weaknesses to the paper. The improvements in test set accuracy over greedy baselines are relatively small, which may limit the practical impact of the proposed method. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed method, particularly in comparison to greedy baselines.

In terms of clarity, the paper is generally well-written, but there are some areas that could be improved. The notation is sometimes confusing, and the authors could provide more intuitive explanations of the proposed method. The paper could also benefit from more visualizations or illustrations to help explain the key concepts.

Overall, the paper is a solid contribution to the field of decision tree learning, and the proposed approach has the potential to improve the accuracy and generalization of decision trees. However, the authors could further improve the paper by addressing the weaknesses mentioned above.
