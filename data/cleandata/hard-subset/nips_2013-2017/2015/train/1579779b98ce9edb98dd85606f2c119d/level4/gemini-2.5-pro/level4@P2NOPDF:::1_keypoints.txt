- Recommendation: Accept after rebuttal; conceptual contribution strong despite underwhelming experiments.  
- Primary merit: Proposes an elegant non-convex relaxation for global optimization of decision trees with predefined structure.  
- Novelty: Principled alternative to standard greedy, heuristic-based tree training.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and clearly motivated.  
- Potential impact: Likely to inspire further research and extensions to other structured prediction problems.  
- Weakness: Empirical results are disappointing—only marginal gains over simpler, faster greedy methods.  
- Suggestion: Try a hybrid strategy—use the global method to find tree structure then locally refine splits/leaves.  
- Suggestion: Analyze overfitting/generalization trade-offs introduced by the global optimization approach.
