After discussing with the other reviewers and considering the rebuttal, I remain convinced that this paper merits publication due to the elegance of its approach and its potential to inspire further research on related methods. Although I agree that the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming, I believe that this score is still appropriate, as stronger results would likely have earned an even higher rating.

The paper proposes a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure, presenting a clear and well-written approach. However, the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. One potential modification to consider is discarding the last split in each tree after applying global optimization, and then optimizing the last split and the leaf node using standard methods, which may lead to improved results. Furthermore, it is essential to evaluate whether global optimization generally leads to increased overfitting, as this could be an important consideration for future research.
