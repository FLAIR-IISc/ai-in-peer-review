Summary: A method for jointly training all parameters of decision trees (decision splitting and posterior probability) has been proposed. This is an important topic because current methods train trees greedily, layer by layer. This task is expressed as a single optimization problem, which is then upper-bounded and predictable to obtain a smooth formula.

Quality - The paper is well-written but may have flaws in the reasoning. See below for details. Clarity - The derivation is clear, but the experiments and their presentation could be improved. Originality - Investigating joint training for decision trees is an important topic. Importance - The proposed solution is valuable, although it seems to have some shortcomings. See below for details.
Notes: - Error in the derivation of the empirical loss bound? At least the following argument in l.216f is confusing: `... for all values of $\bg \neq \sgn(W\bx)$, the right-hand side can only be greater than $\bg = \sgn(W\bx)$ ...' Let's see why I am confused: W\bx = [5;-5]
--> \hat \bh = [1;-1] = \sgn(W\bx)
--> \hat \bh^T W\bx = 10
Choosing g = [1;1] gives g^T W\bx = 0. Therefore, the right-hand side of equation (7) is
g^T W\bx - \hat \bh^T W\bx +
\ell(...) = -10 + \ell(...)
which is possibly smaller than \ell(...) and therefore has no valid upper bound. Note that the proof of proposition 1 in the supplementary material does not cover this case and is therefore incomplete.
- Why are some leaves not assigned any data points? This seems odd and may indicate a problem with the objective or initialization. The suggested estimate of a fixed assignment of data-points to the tree's leaves seems sub-optimal, but like other clustering techniques, an expectation maximization/minimization process could be induced?
- I am thinking about importance for designing efficient loss-enhancing estimates. The depth of the tree seems to be a maximum of 16, and it still seems possible to greedily check 2^16 = 65536 values. How much did the restricted search space introduced by the Hamming ball affect performance? A result is given in the supplementary material, but is it generalized? And what does 'leaves' mean?
- I lack a baseline that trains the non-axis aligned splitting functions of the trees in a standard greedy manner, i.e., without any initialization.
- The depth of the tree needs to be specified beforehand. I think this limit should be discussed more carefully. The authors could also provide a more careful experimental evaluation of the depth parameter.
