- Proposes learning decision trees with linear split functions via a global optimization of split weights and leaf output distributions using a regularized upper bound and SGD.  
- Elegant formulation connecting tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly beat greedy tree learning: gains only on few datasets and with substantially higher computational cost.  
- Need clearer description and pseudocode for the "stable SGD" variant, especially how data points are assigned to leaves.  
- Clarify Figure 2: state whether results use standard or stable SGD and precisely define what constitutes an “active” leaf.  
- Explain and detail the simplex projection in Algorithm 1 and state that it enforces rows of Θ to sum to one (valid probability distributions).  
- Fix unclear phrasing about “number of features evaluated when building an ordinary axis-aligned decision tree” (lines 407–408).  
- Replace “argmax” with “max” in Equations (7), (8), and (9).  
- Discuss Figure 1 in the main text (it is referenced but not explained).  
- Correct sentence/typo issues: line 141 fragment, line 238 pluralization, line 301 “An key”→“A key”, define “FSGD” at line 360, and clarify “tuning” vs “training” errors at line 404.  
- Supplementary fixes: line 29 change `max{g \in sgn...}` to `max{g = sgn...}`; line 30 use `l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y)` instead of `l(Θ^T f(g), y)`.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution but needs stronger empirical evidence and improved implementation clarity to justify practical advantages.
