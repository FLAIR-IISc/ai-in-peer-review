- Presents a novel non-greedy algorithm that jointly learns all split functions and leaf parameters by minimizing a global objective instead of greedy node-wise induction.  
- Formulates decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent binary vectors for node decisions and derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a tractable surrogate.  
- Optimizes the surrogate with SGD and introduces a fast loss-augmented inference method reducing per-step complexity from exponential in depth to O(d²p), making deeper trees practical.  
- Empirical results show consistent but modest test-accuracy improvements over standard greedy baselines across benchmarks.  

Strengths:
- Novel and elegant formulation connecting decision trees to latent structured prediction, offering a principled global optimization view.  
- Technically sound derivation of the surrogate objective and appropriate use of SGD.  
- Critical development of a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that makes the method scalable.  
- Clear, well-written presentation that lays out motivation and technical details logically.  
- Framework is general and suggests promising extensions (e.g., sparsity regularization, kernelized splits).

Weaknesses:
- Experimental gains are modest relative to the increased algorithmic complexity and training cost.  
- Potential for overfitting: larger training–test accuracy gaps observed despite norm regularization.

Questions / Suggestions:
- Clarify trade-off between bound tightness (ν) and generalization; consider additional regularizers (e.g., L1) to encourage simpler splits.  
- Consider hybrid refinements such as greedy re-optimization of leaf parameters or “unfreezing” final split layers after global training to improve final performance.

Overall assessment:
- High-quality, conceptually strong paper with meaningful contributions and potential to stimulate future research; merits acceptance despite modest empirical gains.
