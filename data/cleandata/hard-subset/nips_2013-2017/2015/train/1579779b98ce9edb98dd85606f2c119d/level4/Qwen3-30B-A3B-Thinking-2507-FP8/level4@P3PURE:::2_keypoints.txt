- Proposes joint training of all decision-tree parameters (splits and posteriors) via a single optimization with an upper-bound approximation for tractability.  
- Well-written manuscript but contains a potential flaw in the empirical loss bound derivation that needs careful verification.  
- Mathematical derivations are clean; experimental design and presentation require significant improvement.  
- Investigating joint training for trees is an original and meaningful contribution.  
- Proposed solution is potentially significant but has notable shortcomings that limit impact.  
- Major technical concern: the claimed upper bound on empirical loss (Eq. 7) is questionable—counterexamples show the RHS can be smaller than the loss, and the proof is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points, suggesting issues with the objective, initialization, or the fixed-assignment heuristic; the EM/CCCP justification is unclear.  
- Efficiency claim for loss-augmented Hamming-ball search is under-supported—restricted search vs. brute-force (e.g., 2^16 checks) impact is not shown and "leaves" terminology is ambiguous.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard greedy training method using non-axis-aligned splits without special initialization.  
- Method requires tree depth to be specified a priori; the paper lacks rigorous analysis of depth sensitivity.  
- Post-rebuttal: authors clarified the loss bound (replace argmax with max), reviewer adjusted score, but recommends runtime comparisons and a more thorough evaluation.
