After discussion with the other reviewers and considering the rebuttal, I remain positive about this paper and believe it deserves publication. This is largely due to the elegance of the proposed approach and its potential to stimulate further research in related areas. While the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, I believe the current score is appropriate. With stronger results, I would likely rate the paper even higher.

The paper very clearly presents a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. It’s an elegant approach, though the experimental results are not as compelling. One relatively simple improvement could be to, following the global optimization step, remove the final split in each tree and then optimize it – along with the associated leaf node – using a standard method. This might lead to better performance. It’s also important to consider that global optimization generally has a tendency to increase overfitting, and this should be evaluated.
