Presents a novel non-greedy framework for joint optimization of decision tree split functions and leaf parameters via structured prediction with latent variables.

Models each input’s decision path as a latent variable and derives a convex-concave upper-bound surrogate (inspired by latent structural SVMs) optimized with SGD.

Develops a fast loss-augmented inference method that reduces per-update complexity from exponential to O(d²p) by restricting search to a Hamming ball of radius one.

Empirically, non-greedy trees consistently outperform greedy baselines across multiple datasets and depths, showing better generalization and less overfitting.

Strength: Novel, principled formulation linking global tree optimization to latent structured prediction, enabling regularization and general loss functions.

Strength: Technically sound derivation with a practical algorithm to handle the exponentially large latent space.

Strength: The Hamming-ball-based fast inference is a clever, crucial innovation that makes deep-tree training feasible.

Strength: Strong empirical results and extensibility (e.g., sparsity regularizers, kernels) suggest broader impact.

Weakness: Performance gains are modest (~1–2%); paper lacks a detailed discussion of accuracy vs. increased training cost.

Weakness: No comparison to ensemble methods (Random Forests/GBMs), so applicability to ensemble settings is unclear.

Weakness: Limited computational analysis—missing wall-clock training-time comparisons to greedy baselines for practical assessment.

Weakness: Insufficient clarity/experiments on sensitivity to initialization and the empirical benefit of Stable SGD versus standard SGD.

Overall: High-quality, influential contribution with a strong conceptual advance and practical algorithmic innovations; recommended for acceptance despite concerns about gain magnitude and some missing experiments.
