This very clearly written paper proposes a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a pre-specified, fixed structure.

**Strengths:**
The primary strength of this work is its elegant formulation. The proposed optimization approach is novel and theoretically interesting.

**Weaknesses:**
The main weakness lies in the experimental results, which are somewhat disappointing. The empirical performance of the proposed method does not fully demonstrate a clear advantage over existing techniques, which diminishes the practical impact of the proposed theoretical contribution.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**
1.  A potential way to improve performance could be to introduce a hybrid approach. After performing the global optimization, the final split at each leaf could be discarded and re-optimized using a standard greedy splitting criterion along with the leaf node values. This might combine the benefits of global structure optimization with a strong local decision at the leaves.

2.  The authors should also investigate the issue of overfitting. Global optimization methods are often more prone to overfitting than their greedy counterparts. An experimental evaluation of this aspect would be an important addition to the paper.
