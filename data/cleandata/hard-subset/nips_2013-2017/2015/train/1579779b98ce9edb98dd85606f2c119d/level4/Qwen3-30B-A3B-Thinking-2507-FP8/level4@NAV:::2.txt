

Review Summary: The paper proposes a method for jointly training all parameters (decision splits and posterior probabilities) of decision trees, addressing a key limitation of current greedy, layer-by-layer approaches. The task is formulated as a single optimization problem, which is then upper-bounded and approximated for tractability.  
Quality: The paper is well-written but contains potential flaws in reasoning; see detailed comments below.  
Clarity: The derivation is clear, though experiments and their presentation require improvement.  
Originality: Investigating joint training for decision trees represents an important direction.  
Significance: The proposed solution holds value, though notable shortcomings exist; see detailed comments below.  

Comments:  
- **Flaw in empirical loss bound derivation (l.216f):** The argument `... for all values of $\bg \neq \sgn(W\bx)$, the right hand side can only become larger than when $\bg = \sgn(W\bx)$ ...` is questionable. Consider $W\bx = [5;-5]$:  
  - $\hat \bh = [1;-1] = \sgn(W\bx)$, so $\hat \bh^T W\bx = 10$.  
  - For $\bg = [1;1]$, $\bg^T W\bx = 0$, yielding RHS of Eq. (7) as $0 - 10 + \ell(\cdots) = -10 + \ell(\cdots)$, which may be smaller than $\ell(\cdots)$ and thus invalid. The proof of Prop. 1 (supplementary) omits this case, rendering it incomplete.  
- **Unassigned leaves:** Some leaves receive no data points, which appears anomalous and may indicate issues with the objective or initialization. The heuristic for fixed data-point assignment to leaves seems suboptimal; a justification via expectation maximization or concave-convex procedure (as in clustering) would be valuable.  
- **Efficiency of loss-augmented inference:** With tree depth ≤16, greedily evaluating $2^{16} = 65,536$ values remains feasible. How significantly does the Hamming ball approximation impact performance? One supplementary result is provided, but generalizability is unclear. Clarify the meaning of "leaves" in this context.  
- **Missing baseline:** A baseline training non-axis-aligned split functions via standard greedy methods (without initialization) is required for fair comparison.  
- **Depth parameter limitation:** Tree depth must be specified upfront. This constraint warrants deeper discussion, and the experimental evaluation of depth should be more rigorous.  

*Thanks to the authors for clarifying the loss bound reasoning (Eq. 7); replacing $\arg\max$ with $\max$ is recommended. As noted in my summary, I have adjusted my score. An experiment comparing runtime would strengthen the paper, and a more thorough evaluation would benefit readers. The submission addresses an interesting problem, but the experimental evaluation is limited and a derivation flaw exists. I am willing to adjust my score following a careful rebuttal.*
