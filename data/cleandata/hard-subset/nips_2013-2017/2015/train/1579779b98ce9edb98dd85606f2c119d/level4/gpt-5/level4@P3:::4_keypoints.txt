- Proposes casting global decision-tree learning as structured prediction with binary latent routing codes and optimizes a convex-concave upper bound via SGD.
- Key technical contribution: efficient loss-augmented inference with a radius-1 Hamming-ball relaxation reducing complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d^2 p).
- Introduces per-node norm constraints, supports arbitrary loss functions, and a “stable SGD” (SSGD) variant to reduce leaf inactivity.
- Initialization via standard greedy methods is straightforward; sparse updates under the fast bound affect at most one node per example.
- Connects to prior work on global tree optimization, oblique trees, hierarchical mixtures of experts, and latent structural SVMs.
- Empirically, single trees trained with the method consistently outperform several greedy single-tree baselines on LibSVM multiclass datasets.
- Strength: conceptually novel reduction to latent structured prediction with a principled surrogate objective.
- Strength: solid technical contributions (upper bound, Hamming-ball fast inference, projected SGD, SSGD).
- Strength: practical scalability—per-example computation scales with depth and is compatible with large datasets.
- Strength: generality—supports various losses and regularization with a tunable ν parameter.
- Weakness: oblique ℓ2-constrained splits are dense and less interpretable; lacks experiments with sparsity-inducing regularizers (ℓ1/group).
- Weakness: baselines limited to greedy single-tree methods; missing tuned pruning baselines, simple global baselines, and ensemble comparisons (bagging/RFs/boosting).
- Weakness: tie-breaking behavior for w_i^T x = 0 or near-zero weights is unspecified and could cause brittleness.
- Weakness: Sections 6.2 and 6.4 need clearer summaries; emphasize that the Hamming-ball solution differs from sgn(Wx) in at most one bit.
- Weakness: reproducibility details missing for SSGD (freeze duration), initialization choices, optimizers/momentum/minibatch settings, projection frequency, and ν schedule.
- Quality: reduction, bound, and fast inference appear technically sound; some implementation details require clarification.
- Empirical support: results are consistent but the experimental suite is narrow and lacks ablations (fast vs full bound, with/without SSGD, ν sensitivity).
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and organized; adding a brief algorithmic summary of fast loss-augmented inference would help.
- Originality: high—novel latent-structured perspective and efficient Hamming-ball relaxation.
- Significance: moderate-to-high if empirical evaluation is strengthened and sparsity/interpretability are addressed.
- Suggested experiments: add pruning baselines with tuned parameters, ensemble comparisons, and sparse regularizer evaluations to report sparsity-accuracy trade-offs.
- Suggested analyses: clarify tie-breaking, test a “grow-on-demand” zero-initialization under sparsity, and provide ablations for bound variants and SSGD.
- Acceptance pro: principled, efficient, and novel method with consistent gains over greedy baselines.
- Acceptance con: limited baselines, no sparsity/interpretability results, and under-specified practical details.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept conditional on stronger empirical evaluation (pruning/ensembles) and clarification of practical implementation details.
- Post-rebuttal note: initialization clarification helped but zero-weight behavior remains important; suggest investigating a sparse “grow-on-demand” variant.
