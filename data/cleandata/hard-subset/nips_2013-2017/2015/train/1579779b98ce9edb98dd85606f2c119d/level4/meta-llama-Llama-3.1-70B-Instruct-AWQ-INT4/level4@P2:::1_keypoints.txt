- Proposes a novel non-greedy global optimization for decision trees using SGD on an upper bound of empirical loss.  
- Introduces binary latent decision variables per split and links tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- The approach is elegant, well-motivated, and technically sound.  
- Writing and exposition are clear, concise, and well-organized.  
- Experiments indicate effectiveness but the empirical results are somewhat disappointing.  
- Lacks deeper analysis and insights into experimental performance.  
- Does not sufficiently evaluate whether the global method increases overfitting versus greedy methods.  
- Suggests combining global optimization with greedy refinement (e.g., re-optimize last split and leaf) to potentially improve results.  
- Originality: provides a new and interesting perspective on decision tree optimization.  
- Quality: solid technical contributions but weaker experimental evaluation.  
- Significance: has potential to spur further research on related approaches.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address experimental analysis, trade-offs, and limitations.
