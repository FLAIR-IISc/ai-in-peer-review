- Proposes a global optimization algorithm for decision trees with linear split functions using a structured-prediction-with-latents formulation and a convex upper bound optimized by SGD.  
- Novelty: jointly learns all split hyperplane weights and leaf label distributions, moving beyond greedy node-by-node training.  
- Strength: elegant theoretical formulation linking decision-tree learning to structured prediction, enabling global optimization with standard techniques.  
- Weakness: empirical results are unconvincing—significant advantage over greedy training appears on only a few datasets.  
- Weakness: performance gains are often marginal, raising questions about practical value given added complexity.  
- Weakness: method is more computationally intensive; paper lacks detailed training-time analysis to quantify the trade-off.  
- Clarification needed: the "stable" SGD variant is insufficiently described—provide pseudo-code and precise update rules for assigning points to leaves.  
- Clarification needed: Figure 2 results should state whether standard or stable SGD was used and define what constitutes an "active" leaf.  
- Clarification needed: explain how projection of Θ onto the simplex (Algorithm 1, line 10) is performed and note it enforces valid leaf probability distributions.  
- Minor correction: clarify “number of features evaluated” (lines 407–408)—specify if feature subsampling (e.g., Random Forest-style) was used.  
- Minor correction: replace "argmax" with "max" in Equations (7)–(9).  
- Minor correction: reference Figure 1 in the main text.  
- Minor copyedits: fix grammatical/wording errors at lines 141, 238, 301, and clarify/define acronym FSGD at line 360.
