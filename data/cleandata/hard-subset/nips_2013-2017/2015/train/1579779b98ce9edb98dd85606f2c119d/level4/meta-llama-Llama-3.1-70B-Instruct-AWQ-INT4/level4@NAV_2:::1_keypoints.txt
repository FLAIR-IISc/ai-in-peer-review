- Proposes a novel convex-concave upper bound formulation linking decision tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables, enabling efficient learning of deep trees.  
- Jointly optimizes split functions at all levels and leaf parameters via a global objective, unlike standard greedy node-wise methods.  
- Shows relation to finding optimal oblique (linear-combination) splits and formulates a tractable optimization objective.  
- Technically sound with thorough analysis and use of stochastic gradient descent for scalable training.  
- Experiments indicate non-greedy trees outperform greedy baselines, but overall empirical improvements are modest.  
- Unclear why the non-greedy approach yields only limited gains; experimental results are somewhat disappointing.  
- Potential overfitting concern for deep trees; limited analysis of generalization performance.  
- Related-work review is comprehensive for decision trees but lacks context/comparison to ensemble methods (random forests, gradient boosting).  
- Strengths: elegant approach, clear writing, solid technical treatment, and scalable optimization.  
- Weaknesses: weak empirical gains, insufficient generalization analysis, and need for broader comparisons to other ML models.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, elegant method with potential to spur further research and well-presented analysis.  
- Rejection arguments: underwhelming experimental results and insufficient analysis of generalization and relation to other models.
