- Paper proposes globally optimized decision trees with linear split functions via an upper bound on empirical loss and L2-regularized split weights.  
- Optimization performed with stochastic gradient descent to minimize the proposed upper bound.  
- Formulation is elegant and leverages a connection to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly show global optimization beats greedy learning; wins are limited to a few datasets.  
- Globally optimized trees are more computationally intensive than greedy trees.  
- Clarification needed on the "stable" SGD variant, including how data points are assigned to leaves and a pseudo-code.  
- Figure 2 is unclear about whether results use SGD or stable SGD and does not define what an "active" leaf is.  
- Algorithm 1 (line 10): the update and projection of Θ onto a simplex need a practical explanation and must state that each row of Θ sums to one.  
- Minor clarity/typo: line 407–408 about tuning "number of features evaluated" is misleading for standard decision trees.  
- Notational/typographical fixes: replace "argmax" with "max" in Eqns (7)–(9).  
- Figure 1 is not referenced in the main text.  
- Line 141 contains a malformed sentence ("...to the index of the leaf on by this path").  
- Line 238: pluralize "the solution to loss-augmented inference" → "the solutions to...".  
- Line 301: fix "An key observation" → "A key observation."  
- Line 360: FSGD is not defined.  
- Line 404: "a tree with minimum training errors" likely meant "tuning errors."  
- Supplementary material corrections: line 29 change "max{g \in sgn...}" → "max{g = sgn...}"; line 30 fix expression to use sgn(Wx) inside f: "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^Tf(sgn(Wx)), y)".  
- Overall: elegant formulation but results and presentation need strengthening and clarifications to be convincing.
