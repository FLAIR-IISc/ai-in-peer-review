This paper proposes a non-greedy approach for optimizing decision trees with a fixed structure. The authors formulate the problem of finding optimal linear-combination (oblique) splits for decision trees as a structured prediction problem with latent variables. They develop a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss and optimize it using stochastic gradient descent (SGD). The proposed approach is efficient and scalable, making it feasible to train deep trees.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. The link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables is novel and interesting. The formulation of the decision tree learning problem as a convex-concave upper bound is also a key contribution of the paper.

The experimental results are somewhat disappointing, as the proposed approach does not significantly outperform the baseline methods. However, the authors do demonstrate that their approach can lead to better generalization performance and less overfitting.

One potential improvement to the approach would be to combine the global optimization with a standard greedy optimization step. For example, after using the global optimization to determine the splits, the last split in each tree could be thrown away and re-optimized using a standard greedy approach. This could potentially lead to better results.

It is also important to evaluate the potential for overfitting when using global optimization. The authors provide some discussion of this issue, but more experimentation would be helpful in understanding the trade-offs between global optimization and overfitting.

Overall, the paper is well-written and proposes an elegant approach to decision tree optimization. While the experimental results are not as strong as they could be, the paper is still a valuable contribution to the field and is likely to lead to more research on related approaches.
