This paper proposes a novel and elegant nonconvex relaxation for globally optimizing the parameters of decision trees with a fixed structure. The core idea is to formulate the decision tree learning problem within a structured prediction framework, leveraging latent variables and a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss. This allows for efficient optimization via stochastic gradient descent. While the experimental results are somewhat modest, the approach is theoretically sound and has the potential to inspire further research in this area.

The paper clearly identifies the limitations of traditional greedy decision tree induction algorithms and positions its contribution as a step towards overcoming these limitations. The connection made between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables is insightful. The technical contributions, particularly the reduction of gradient computation complexity from O(2dp) to O(d2p), are significant and enable training deeper trees. The writing is exceptionally clear and well-organized, making the paper easy to follow and understand. The authors adequately inform the reader about the key technical details and motivations behind their approach.

However, the empirical validation, while demonstrating improvement over greedy baselines, feels somewhat underwhelming. The gains observed on the benchmark datasets are not substantial, and the authors acknowledge this in their rebuttal. The suggestion to refine the final split and leaf node using the standard greedy approach is a sensible one and may lead to more pronounced improvements. A potential concern is that the global optimization, while promoting better tree structure, might also increase the risk of overfitting, which the authors address through regularization but may warrant further investigation. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed approach offers a fundamentally different perspective on decision tree learning, moving beyond the limitations of greedy algorithms.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The formulation based on structured prediction and the development of a convex-concave upper bound are well-motivated and mathematically justified.
*   **Efficiency:** The reduction in gradient computation complexity makes the approach scalable to deeper trees and larger datasets.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand, with clear explanations of the key concepts and technical details.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Modest Experimental Results:** The empirical gains over greedy baselines are not substantial.
*   **Potential for Overfitting:** Global optimization could lead to overfitting, requiring careful regularization.
*   **Limited Exploration of Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** A more thorough exploration of the regularization parameter ν could provide further insights into the algorithm's performance.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to a long-standing problem in machine learning.
*   The technical contributions are significant, particularly the efficient gradient computation.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, making it accessible to a broad audience.
*   The approach has the potential to inspire further research in global optimization of decision trees.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The experimental results are somewhat disappointing and do not demonstrate a dramatic improvement over existing methods.
*   The potential for overfitting needs to be carefully considered.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a good paper that deserves publication. The elegance of the approach and its potential to drive further research outweigh the somewhat modest experimental results. While stronger results would certainly warrant a higher rating, the current score reflects the paper’s significant contributions and its overall quality as a scientific contribution. I concur with the other reviewers' assessment that the paper warrants acceptance.
