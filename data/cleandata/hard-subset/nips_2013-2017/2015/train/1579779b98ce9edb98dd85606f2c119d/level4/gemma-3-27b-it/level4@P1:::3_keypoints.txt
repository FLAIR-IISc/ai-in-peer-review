- Reformulates decision tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, enabling joint non-greedy optimization of splits and leaf parameters.
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss to guide global optimization.
- Reduces gradient computation complexity from O(2dp) to O(d2p), allowing training of deeper trees.
- Demonstrates consistent test accuracy improvements over greedy baselines on benchmarks (SensIT, Connect4, Protein, MNIST), though gains are modest.
- Well-defined mathematical formulation with clear problem statement and technical exposition.
- Appropriately situates the work within related literature on decision trees, structured prediction, and optimization.
- Novel and technically sound contribution that offers a promising alternative to greedy induction.
- Empirical validation is thorough across multiple datasets, lending credibility to claims.
- Performance improvements are generally small, raising questions about practical impact versus added complexity.
- Method shows sensitivity to the regularization hyperparameter ν, necessitating careful tuning.
- Initialization using a greedy tree (CO2) may bias results; robustness to different initializations is unclear.
- Paper lacks deep analysis of failure cases and scenarios where the method underperforms baselines.
- Description of the Stable SGD (SSGD) algorithm is insufficiently detailed, especially regarding leaf-update criteria and benefits.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, significant complexity reduction, consistent empirical gains, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited magnitude of accuracy improvements, hyperparameter tuning burden, and insufficient failure-mode analysis.
- Overall assessment: valuable and publishable contribution that would benefit from expanded discussion of limitations, hyperparameter guidance, failure analysis, and alternative initializations.
