- Proposal: Non-greedy training of oblique decision trees by optimizing a single global objective over all internal splits and leaf parameters.
- Key idea: Treat internal split decisions as latent variables and derive a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss inspired by structured prediction with latent variables.
- Inference challenge: Naive loss-augmented inference is exponential in tree depth; authors introduce a fast surrogate restricting inference to a Hamming ball of radius one.
- Complexity: Surrogate yields O(d^2 p) complexity per SGD step (d = depth, p = feature dimension).
- Optimization: Training via SGD with per-row norm constraints on W to control bound tightness and avoid degenerate solutions.
- Stabilization: “Stable SGD” variant fixes data-to-leaf assignments for multiple updates to prevent premature leaf deactivation.
- Experiments: Evaluated on LibSVM multiclass datasets comparing to axis-aligned greedy trees, OC1, random oblique trees, and CO2; typically higher test accuracy and less overfitting at larger depths.
- Relation to prior work: Connects classical non-greedy and margin-based tree methods to latent structured prediction; contrasts with soft/probabilistic trees and oblique-split heuristics.
- CO2 relation: CO2 is covered as a special-case greedy variant of the proposed bound.
- Theoretical quality: Latent-variable reformulation, bound derivation, and complexity analysis are clear and technically sound.
- Regularization analysis: Effect of W-norm on bound tightness is well-motivated; per-row norm constraint is reasonable.
- Algorithmic quality: Fast radius-1 loss-augmented inference is practical and projection/gradient steps are straightforward.
- Empirics critique: Methodology reasonable, but improvements over strong baselines are sometimes modest; single-tree focus limits practical relevance.
- Missing experiments: No regression results reported despite framework supporting them.
- Clarity: Paper is well written and organized; explanations and pseudocode are clear.
- Minor clarity issues: Reiterate intuition behind Hamming-ball restriction and specify SSGD schedule more concretely.
- Originality: Novel link between hard decision trees and latent structured prediction; original fast inference and per-row norm use.
- Significance: Conceptually promising direction for non-greedy global training; practical impact tempered by modest single-tree gains.
- Strengths: Principled formulation, efficient training, structured-prediction connection, careful norm analysis, and clear reproducibility.
- Weaknesses: Modest empirical gains, lack of regression/ensemble comparisons, sensitivity to initialization, under-characterized SSGD, limited statistical analysis.
- Suggested improvements: Systematic overfitting analysis, hybrid refinements (global + greedy), report regression results, ablations on initialization/SSGD, include ensembles, and explore sparse/kernelized splits.
- Acceptance arguments: Strong, original conceptual contribution with clean, efficient algorithm and potential to catalyze follow-up work.
- Rejection arguments: Experimental evidence not uniformly compelling; practical aspects (regression, ensembles, SSGD) insufficiently evaluated.
- Overall assessment: Well-written, elegant paper advancing decision tree methodology with an efficient global training method; merits acceptance but would benefit from stronger experimental validation.
