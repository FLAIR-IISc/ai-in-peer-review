- Introduces a novel non-greedy, global optimization method for decision trees by jointly training all split functions and leaf parameters.  
- Frames tree training as structured prediction with the tree path treated as a latent variable.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss, enabling a tractable surrogate objective.  
- Optimizes the surrogate with stochastic gradient descent (SGD).  
- Provides a "fast loss-augmented inference" algorithm to reduce per-update complexity, making deep-tree training feasible.  
- Experiments show non-greedy trees outperform several greedy baselines and appear less prone to overfitting.  
- The derivation/justification of the upper bound (Equation 7) in the main text is unclear and needs clearer explanation.  
- Some leaves become inactive (no assigned data) during training; the cause and implications need more analysis and formal motivation for the proposed Stable SGD fix.  
- The fast inference approximation (restricting search to Hamming ball radius 1) needs empirical validation of the accuracy vs. speed trade-off.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a standard greedy oblique-split learner to isolate benefits of global optimization vs. oblique splits.  
- Practical limitation: tree depth must be pre-specified, unlike greedy methods that grow until stopping criteria; sensitivity analysis is needed.  
- Overall, the approach is novel, principled, and empirically promising, though clarity and experimental coverage should be strengthened.
