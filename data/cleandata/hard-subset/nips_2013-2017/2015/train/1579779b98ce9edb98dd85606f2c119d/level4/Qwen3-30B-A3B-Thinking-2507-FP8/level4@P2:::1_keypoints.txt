- Proposes a novel non-greedy method that jointly optimizes split functions at all tree levels with a global objective instead of greedy node-wise training.
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound surrogate on empirical loss enabling principled global optimization.
- Enables efficient training using stochastic gradient descent with computational complexity O(d²p), making deep trees feasible.
- Differs from prior non-greedy work by supporting general loss functions while preserving computational advantages of trees.
- Strong technical quality: elegant theoretical formulation, rigorous complexity analysis, and well-justified optimization.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.
- Original and novel contribution that addresses a long-standing limitation of greedy decision tree methods.
- Potentially significant: could advance tree learning and inspire further non-greedy research and applications.
- Experimental results are methodologically sound but show only marginal improvements over greedy baselines across datasets.
- Notable strengths: theoretical elegance, clear exposition, thorough complexity analysis, and principled framework.
- Suggested improvements: (1) try a post-processing step pruning/optimizing final splits and leaves, and (2) investigate and report overfitting behavior of global optimization.
- Overall recommendation: technically strong and influential work that should be accepted despite limited experimental gains.
