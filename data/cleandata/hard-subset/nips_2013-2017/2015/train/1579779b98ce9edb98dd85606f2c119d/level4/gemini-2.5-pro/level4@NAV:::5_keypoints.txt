- Introduces a method for training decision trees with linear classifiers at internal nodes using global optimization of all tree parameters.  
- Derives a regularized upper bound on empirical loss with L2 regularization to smooth optimization and uses SGD to minimize it.  
- Formulation elegantly connects tree optimization to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical evidence is weak: improvements are significant on only a few datasets and not convincingly better than greedy induction.  
- Method incurs higher computational cost compared to standard greedy baselines.  
- Description of the stable SGD variant is unclear, especially how data points are assigned to leaves; pseudo-code is needed.  
- Figure 2 lacks specification of whether results use standard or stable SGD and fails to define "active" leaf.  
- Algorithm 1 (line 10) projects Θ onto a simplex but the practical projection method and the fact it enforces row-wise probability distributions are not explained.  
- Confusing statement about tuning the "number of features evaluated" for axis-aligned trees—standard algorithms typically evaluate all features.  
- Equations (7)–(9) use "argmax" where "max" is appropriate.  
- Figure 1 is not referenced in the main text.  
- Multiple typos and grammatical errors throughout (lines 141, 238, 301, 360, 404) and an undefined acronym FSGD.  
- Supplementary material contains notation errors (lines 29–30) and likely incorrect expressions involving sgn(Wx) in the loss term.  
- Overall: clever problem formulation but empirical validation and several clarifications/corrections are needed.
