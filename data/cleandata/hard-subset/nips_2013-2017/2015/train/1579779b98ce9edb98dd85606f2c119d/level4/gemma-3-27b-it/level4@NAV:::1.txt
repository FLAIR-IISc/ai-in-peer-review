Following discussion with other reviewers and consideration of the rebuttal, I maintain my assessment that this is a strong paper warranting publication. The work is particularly notable for the elegance of its proposed approach, and its potential to stimulate further research in this area. While the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming, I believe this is reflected in the assigned score, and stronger results would certainly justify a higher rating. 

The paper clearly presents a nonconvex relaxation technique for the global optimization of decision trees with a pre-defined structure. Though the approach is elegant, the experimental validation is limited. A potential improvement could involve refining the final split in each tree – specifically, discarding it after global optimization and subsequently optimizing it alongside the corresponding leaf node using standard methods – which might yield improved performance. It’s also crucial to acknowledge that global optimization techniques are generally susceptible to increased overfitting, a point that should be carefully considered in the evaluation.
