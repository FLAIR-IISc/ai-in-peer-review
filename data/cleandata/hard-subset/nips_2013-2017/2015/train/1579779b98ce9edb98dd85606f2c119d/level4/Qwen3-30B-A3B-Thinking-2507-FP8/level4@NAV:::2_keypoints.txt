- Proposal: joint training of all decision tree parameters (decision splits and posterior probabilities) via a single optimization problem with an upper-bound and tractable approximation.  
- Quality: paper is well-written but contains potential reasoning flaws.  
- Clarity: mathematical derivation is clear, but experiments and their presentation need improvement.  
- Originality: joint training of trees is an important and novel direction.  
- Significance: solution is valuable but has notable shortcomings in theory and evaluation.  
- Theoretical flaw: the empirical loss upper-bound derivation (Eq. 7) is incorrect as argued — counterexample (W x = [5; −5]) shows the RHS can be smaller; Prop. 1 proof omits this case and is incomplete.  
- Unassigned leaves: some leaves receive no data points, indicating possible issues with the objective or initialization; the heuristic of fixing data-point assignments is suboptimal and needs justification (e.g., EM or CCCP).  
- Loss-augmented inference efficiency: with depth ≤16 brute-force over 2^16 leaves is feasible; need to quantify how the Hamming-ball approximation affects performance and clarify what “leaves” refers to.  
- Missing baseline: paper lacks a baseline that trains non-axis-aligned split functions with standard greedy methods (without special initialization).  
- Depth parameter issue: requiring tree depth a priori is a limitation that needs deeper discussion and more rigorous experimental evaluation.  
- Recommended experiments/changes: replace argmax with max in the bound, add runtime comparisons, and provide a more thorough empirical evaluation; reviewer is open to revising their score after a careful rebuttal.
