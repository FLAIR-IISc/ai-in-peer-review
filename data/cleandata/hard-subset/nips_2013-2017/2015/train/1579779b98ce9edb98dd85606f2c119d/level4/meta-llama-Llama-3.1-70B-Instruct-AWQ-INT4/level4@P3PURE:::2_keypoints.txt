- Paper proposes joint training of all decision tree parameters via a single optimization with an upper-bound approximation, addressing greedy layer-wise limitations.
- Significant contribution and timely topic, but experimental evaluation is limited and could be improved.
- Potential flaw in the empirical loss bound derivation: counterexample shows the claimed upper bound in Eq. (7) may be invalid.
- Proof of Proposition 1 in the supplement is incomplete and does not cover the problematic case.
- Some leaves receive no data points, which is unusual and may indicate problems with the objective or initialization.
- Fixed assignment heuristic for leaf assignments seems suboptimal; an EM or concave-convex motivation might be more appropriate.
- Importance of efficient loss-augmented inference is overstated; brute-force over 2^16 is still feasible and the benefit of Hamming-ball restriction is unclear and under-evaluated.
- The term "leaves" is not clearly defined in the paper.
- Missing baseline: greedy training of non-axis-aligned split functions without special initialization is not compared.
- Requiring tree depth to be specified in advance is a significant limitation that needs deeper discussion and experimental analysis.
- Runtime comparison and more thorough experimental evaluation (including generalization of Hamming-ball results) would strengthen the paper.
- Reviewer is open to adjusting score if authors provide a careful rebuttal addressing the derivation and experimental concerns.
