Here is a proposal for a method of jointly training all parameters of a decision tree (decision splitting and backward probability). This is an important issue because current methods train the tree layer by layer in a greedy manner. This task is stated as a single optimization problem, which is then applied to obtain a bounded and approximate formula.

**Quality:** The paper is well-written, but there may be some flaws in the reasoning. See below for details.

**Clarity:** The derivation is clear, but the tests and their presentation could be improved.

**Originality:** Research on joint training for decision trees is an important topic.

**Importance:** The proposed solution is valuable, although it seems to have some flaws. See below for details.

**Comments:**

*   **Error in the empirical loss boundary proposal?** The following argument in l.216f is misleading: `... $\bg \neq \sgn(W\bx)$ for all values of $\bg$, the right-hand side can only be large when $\bg = \sgn(W\bx)$ ...' To illustrate why I am confused, see the following example: W\bx = [5;-5]
--> \hat \bh = [1;-1] = \sgn(W\bx)
--> \hat \bh^T W\bx = 10
If g = [1;1] is chosen, g^T W\bx = 0. Therefore, the right-hand side of equation (7) is
g^T W\bx - \hat \bh^T W\bx +
\ell(...) = -10 + \ell(...)
which is probably smaller than \ell(...) and therefore not a valid upper bound. Note that this event was not included in the proof of proposition 1 in the supplementary material and is therefore incomplete.
*   **Why are some leaves not given any data points?** This seems odd and may indicate a problem with the purpose or starting point. The proposed heuristic of assigning data points specifically to the leaves of the tree is probably not optimal, but could it be possible through an expectation maximization/convex-concave approach like other clustering techniques?
*   **I am wondering about its importance for efficient loss-enhancing estimation designs. The depth of the tree is probably a maximum of 16, and it seems still possible to greedily test the value 2^16 = 65536. How much has the bounded search space induced by the Hamming ball affected performance? A result is given in the supplementary material, but is it generally applicable? And what does 'leaf' mean?**
*   **I am missing a baseline that trains the non-axis aligned splitting functions of the tree in a standard greedy manner, i.e., without any starting point.**
*   **The depth of the tree must be specified beforehand. I think this limitation should be discussed more carefully. The authors could also conduct more careful experimental evaluation of the depth parameter.**
--- Thanks to the authors for carefully explaining the reasoning behind the loss boundary (equation 7). You could have used \max instead of \arg\max. As stated in my summary, I adjusted my score.
