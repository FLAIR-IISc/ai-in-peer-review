This paper proposes a nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The core idea is to formulate a surrogate objective that allows for joint optimization of split functions and leaf parameters, addressing the limitations of greedy tree induction. The approach establishes a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, enabling efficient training via stochastic gradient descent. While the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, the elegance of the proposed approach and its potential to stimulate further research in this area make it a worthwhile contribution.

The authors clearly articulate the limitations of traditional greedy decision tree algorithms and motivate their non-greedy approach effectively. They demonstrate a strong understanding of related work, situating their contribution within the broader context of decision tree learning and structured prediction. The technical details are well-presented, and the derivation of the convex-concave upper bound is convincing. The reduction of gradient computation complexity from O(2dp) to O(d2p) is a significant practical contribution, enabling the training of deeper trees.

However, the experimental evaluation does not fully showcase the benefits of the proposed method. While the authors demonstrate improvements over greedy baselines on several datasets, the gains are often modest. The comparison to CO2 [18] is insightful, highlighting the value of the non-greedy optimization, but stronger and more consistent results would significantly strengthen the paper. The discussion of regularization (parameter ν) and its impact on pruning is valuable, but further analysis of the trade-off between regularization strength and generalization performance would be beneficial. It would also be worthwhile to investigate the potential for overfitting introduced by the global optimization and perhaps explore strategies to mitigate it. One potential direction, as suggested by the authors themselves, could be to refine the final split and leaf node using a standard greedy approach after the global optimization stage.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The formulation of decision tree learning as a structured prediction problem with latent variables is a novel and insightful contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed surrogate objective and optimization algorithm are technically sound and well-motivated.
*   **Efficiency:** The reduction in gradient computation complexity makes the approach practical for training deep trees.
*   **Potential Impact:** The work opens up new avenues for research in non-greedy decision tree learning and may lead to improved performance on challenging classification tasks.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Modest Experimental Results:** The performance gains over greedy baselines are not consistently substantial across all datasets.
*   **Limited Empirical Analysis:** The analysis of the regularization parameter and its effect on overfitting could be more comprehensive.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a good paper with a solid technical foundation. While the experimental results are not overwhelming, the elegance of the proposed approach and its potential impact on the field justify publication. I believe that addressing the points raised above in a revised version would further strengthen the paper.
