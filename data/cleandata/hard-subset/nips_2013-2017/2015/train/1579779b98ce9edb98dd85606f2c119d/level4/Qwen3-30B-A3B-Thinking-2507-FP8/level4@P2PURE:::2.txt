

Summary: This paper proposes a method for jointly training all parameters (decision splits and posterior probabilities) of decision trees, addressing an important gap as current approaches train trees greedily layer by layer. The problem is formulated as a single optimization task, which is then upper-bounded and approximated to yield a tractable solution.  
Quality: The paper is well-written but contains potential reasoning flaws; see detailed comments below.  
Clarity: The derivation is clear, though experiments and their presentation require enhancement.  
Originality: Investigating joint training for decision trees represents a significant topic.  
Significance: The proposed solution holds value, though notable shortcomings exist; see detailed comments below.  

Comments:  
- **Flaw in empirical loss bound derivation (l.216f):** The claim that "for all $\bg \neq \sgn(W\bx)$, the right-hand side can only become larger than when $\bg = \sgn(W\bx)$" is questionable. Consider $W\bx = [5; -5]$:  
  - $\hat \bh = \sgn(W\bx) = [1; -1]$, so $\hat \bh^T W\bx = 10$.  
  - Choosing $\bg = [1; 1]$ yields $\bg^T W\bx = 0$.  
  - The right-hand side of Eq. (7) becomes $0 - 10 + \ell(\cdots) = -10 + \ell(\cdots)$, which may be smaller than $\ell(\cdots)$, violating the upper bound. The proof of Proposition 1 in the supplementary material does not address this case and is therefore incomplete.  
- **Unassigned leaves:** Some leaves receive no data points, which appears problematic and may indicate an issue with the objective or initialization. The proposed heuristic of fixed data-point assignment to leaves is suboptimal; could it be justified via expectation maximization or the concave-convex procedure, as in clustering techniques?  
- **Loss-augmented inference design:** Given a maximum tree depth of 16 (where $2^{16} = 65,536$ candidate splits remain feasible via greedy search), how significant is the performance impact of the restricted search space imposed by the Hamming ball? One result in the supplementary material is provided, but does this generalize? Clarify the meaning of "leaves" in this context.  
- **Missing baseline:** A baseline that trains non-axis-aligned split functions via standard greedy training (without initialization) is absent.  
- **Tree depth specification:** The need to specify tree depth in advance is a limitation that warrants deeper discussion. The authors should also provide a more rigorous experimental evaluation of the depth parameter.
