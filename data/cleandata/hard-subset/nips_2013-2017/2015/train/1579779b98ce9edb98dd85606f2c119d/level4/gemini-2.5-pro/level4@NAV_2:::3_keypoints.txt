- Proposes a novel global optimization framework for decision trees, jointly optimizing all split functions and leaf parameters to overcome greedy induction suboptimality.  
- Frames tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables, treating each data point's path as a latent variable.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective and optimizes it via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Introduces a fast loss-augmented inference algorithm that reduces per-update complexity from exponential to polynomial in tree depth.  
- Empirically shows improved performance over greedy baselines on benchmark classification tasks.  
- Original and elegant formulation that advances beyond decades of greedy heuristics.  
- Significant because it generalizes to various losses and incorporates regularization to control complexity and improve generalization.  
- Technically sound derivations and complexity analysis; the fast inference algorithm makes training deep trees practical.  
- Paper is well-written and the connection to latent structured prediction is clearly explained.  
- Reported test accuracy gains are often modest relative to increased training complexity; practical significance needs more discussion.  
- Description of the Stable SGD (SSGD) variant is brief and lacks precise criteria/pseudocode for updating data-to-leaf assignments.  
- Initialization reliance on the CO2 greedy algorithm raises questions; an ablation comparing simpler initializations is needed to quantify the benefit of global fine-tuning.  
- Lacks discussion/comparison with tree ensembles (Random Forests/Boosted Trees); exploring how non-greedy trees could benefit or be used in ensembles would strengthen the paper.
