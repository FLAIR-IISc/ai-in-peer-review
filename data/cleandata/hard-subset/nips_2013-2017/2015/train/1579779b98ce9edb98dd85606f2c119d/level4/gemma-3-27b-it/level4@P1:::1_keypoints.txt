- Proposes a nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of fixed-structure decision trees allowing joint optimization of splits and leaves.  
- Formulates a surrogate objective linking decision tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Enables efficient training via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound with convincing technical presentation.  
- Reduces gradient computation complexity from O(2dp) to O(d2p), making deeper-tree training practical.  
- Clearly motivates limitations of greedy tree induction and the need for a non-greedy approach.  
- Situates the work well within related literature on decision trees and structured prediction.  
- Experimental results show improvements over greedy baselines but gains are often modest.  
- Comparison to CO2 is informative and highlights benefits of non-greedy optimization.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and could be strengthened with more comprehensive experiments.  
- Regularization parameter (ν) discussion is useful but requires deeper analysis of pruning vs. generalization trade-offs.  
- Potential overfitting from global optimization is a concern and mitigation strategies should be explored.  
- Authors suggest a promising hybrid: refine splits/leaves greedily after global optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel formulation, technical soundness, computational efficiency, and potential research impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest empirical gains and limited analysis of regularization/overfitting.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid and elegant work whose impact would be increased by stronger and more thorough experiments.
