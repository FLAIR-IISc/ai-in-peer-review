- Contribution: Proposes a globally optimized decision tree with linear split functions, optimizing split weights and leaf output distributions jointly via SGD on a regularized upper bound of empirical loss.  
- Technical strength: Elegantly formulates tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical weakness: Experiments do not convincingly show clear advantages over greedy trees—improvements on few datasets and at higher computational cost.  
- Clarification needed: Details of the “stable” SGD implementation and how data points are assigned to leaves (pseudocode requested).  
- Figure 2 ambiguity: Specify whether results use SGD or stable SGD and define what counts as an “active” leaf.  
- Algorithm 1 detail: In line 10, explain how Θ is projected onto the simplex in practice and note that projection enforces each Θ row sums to one.  
- Minor errors/typos in main text: Multiple wording and notation issues (e.g., lines 141, 238, 301, 360, 404, equations (7)-(9), Figure 1 not referenced).  
- Supplementary material corrections: Two incorrect expressions on lines 29–30 need fixing (sgn notation and function argument).  
- Overall assessment: Solid theoretical formulation but experimental evidence and implementation details need strengthening to justify the proposed global optimization.
