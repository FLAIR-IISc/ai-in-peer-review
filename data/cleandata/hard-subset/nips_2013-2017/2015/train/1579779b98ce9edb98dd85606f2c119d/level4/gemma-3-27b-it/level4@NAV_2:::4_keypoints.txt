- Proposes reformulating global decision-tree optimization as (latent) structured prediction with sign-vector latent variables.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and a differentiable upper-bound approximation.  
- Minimization via SGD requires solving loss-augmented inference problems, for which efficient algorithms are provided.  
- Experiments compare the proposed method against a standard greedy training baseline.  
- Main contributions: casting DT optimization as structured prediction and devising efficient optimization algorithms.  
- The approach is novel and raises interesting theoretical and practical questions.  
- Concern about practical motivation and unclear magnitude of performance gains over simple methods.  
- Notable property: gradient updates are sparse—each step appears to change only one node’s weight—raising unexplored possibilities.  
- Suggests potential for growing an infinitely deep tree from zero weights, but asks whether this reduces to known greedy strategies or yields limited-size trees with regularization.  
- Critical ambiguity: behavior when a node’s weight equals zero (paper implies taking the right branch), which has pruning analogues and needs precise definition.  
- Major practical concern: if interpretability is the goal, L2-regularized dense weight vectors harm interpretability; L1 might help but could reduce performance and needs testing.  
- Experiments lack sufficient baseline detail (e.g., pruning strategies for the naive method) and should compare against more trivial global strategies.  
- Suggests trying additional baselines like greedy training on random training subsets and validating on full data.  
- Clarity issues: inference sections (e.g., Sec. 6.2 and 6.4) could be clearer and more explicit in presentation.  
- Paper title mentions forests but does not address forests in the content.  
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight case remains a critical concern; reviewer prefers exploring zero initialization and gradual weight growth as an interesting direction.  
- Overall: very clever and interesting approach, but practical utility is unclear due to interpretability concerns and insufficient experimental detail.
