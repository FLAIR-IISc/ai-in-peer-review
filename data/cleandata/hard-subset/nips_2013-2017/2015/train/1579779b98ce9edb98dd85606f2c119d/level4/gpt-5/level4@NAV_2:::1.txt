Post-rebuttal update: After discussing with the other reviewers and considering the authors’ response, I continue to view this as a strong paper that merits acceptance, primarily because of the elegance of the method and its potential to catalyze further research on similar approaches. I acknowledge that the empirical results are somewhat underwhelming. With more compelling experiments I would likely assign a higher rating; as is, my current score feels appropriate. The paper is very clearly written and introduces a nonconvex relaxation for global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The approach is elegant, though the experimental gains are modest. One straightforward modification would be to, after applying the global optimization, remove the final split in each tree and then optimize that split and the leaf node in the standard way, which might improve performance. It is also important to assess whether global optimization tends to increase overfitting.
