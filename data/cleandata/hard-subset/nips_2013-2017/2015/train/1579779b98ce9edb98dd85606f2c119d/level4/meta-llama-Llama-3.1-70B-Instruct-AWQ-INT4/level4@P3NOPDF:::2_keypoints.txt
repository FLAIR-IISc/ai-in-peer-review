- Proposes joint training of all decision-tree parameters (splits and posteriors) via a single optimization problem with an upper-bound approximation.  
- Well-written and clear derivation, but experiments and presentation need improvement.  
- Original and important topic: joint training of trees vs. greedy layer-by-layer methods.  
- Significant contribution but has notable shortcomings and possible derivation errors.  
- Major concern: claimed empirical loss upper bound may be invalid — counterexample shows RHS can be smaller than intended; proof of Proposition 1 is incomplete.  
- Some leaves receive no data points, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization; the fixed-assignment heuristic seems ad hoc and might need EM/CCCP-style justification.  
- Efficiency of loss-augmented inference is unclear: brute-force over 2^16 may be feasible, so impact of restricting search to a Hamming ball is uncertain and not shown to generalize; “leaves” not well defined.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to standard greedy training of non-axis-aligned split functions without special initialization.  
- Limitation: tree depth must be fixed a priori; authors should discuss this restriction and evaluate sensitivity to depth.  
- Minor suggestions: replace argmax with max in Eq.7, include runtime comparisons, and provide a more thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Overall: interesting problem and promising method, but limited experiments and a possible theoretical flaw mean rebuttal/explanation could change the review outcome.
