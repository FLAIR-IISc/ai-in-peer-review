- Proposes a globally optimized decision-tree learning algorithm with linear split functions using an upper bound on empirical loss, L2 regularization, and SGD.

- Elegantly formulates tree optimization by connecting it to structured prediction with latent variables.

- Empirical results are unconvincing: global optimization substantially outperforms greedy learning on only a couple of datasets while being more computationally expensive.

- Stable SGD is not clearly described; need explanation of how data points are assigned to leaves and a pseudo-code for the procedure.

- Figure 2 is unclear about whether results use SGD or stable SGD, and the paper does not define what constitutes an “active” leaf.

- Algorithm 1 (line 10) projects Θ onto a simplex after update; the paper should state how the projection is solved and note that each row of Θ sums to one.

- Unclear phrase about “number of features evaluated when building an ordinary axis-aligned decision tree” — standard trees typically evaluate all features at each node.

- Equations (7), (8), (9): replace "argmax" with "max."

- Figure 1 is not referenced in the main text.

- Typo at line 141: incorrect sentence fragment ("...to the index of the leaf on by this path").

- Line 238: grammatical fix — "the solutions to loss-augmented inference" (plural).

- Line 301: grammar fix — "A key observation" (not "An key observation").

- Line 360: acronym "FSGD" is used but not defined.

- Line 404: likely meant "tuning errors" should be "training errors" (or clarify intended meaning).

- Supplementary material fixes: line 29 should read "max{g = sgn...}" and line 30 should read "sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y)".

- Overall assessment: well-structured and elegant formulation, but experimental evidence does not convincingly support global optimization over greedy methods.
