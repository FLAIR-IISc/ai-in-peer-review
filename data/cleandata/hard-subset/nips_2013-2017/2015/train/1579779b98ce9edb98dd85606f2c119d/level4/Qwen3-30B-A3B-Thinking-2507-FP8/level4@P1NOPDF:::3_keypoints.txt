- Proposes finding optimal fixed-depth decision trees by framing the problem as structured prediction.  
- Offers a principled alternative to heuristic/greedy tree construction methods.  
- Empirically outperforms conventional greedy algorithms in test accuracy, but improvements are modest.  
- Experimental gains are consistent across multiple datasets but remain small, questioning real-world impact.  
- Marginal accuracy improvements raise concerns about the practical significance of the technique.  
- Computational overhead of the structured prediction framework may outweigh benefits in many settings.  
- Paper should better articulate preferable scenarios and discuss trade-offs between model complexity, training time, and limited accuracy gains.
