This paper proposes a novel approach to decision tree optimization by formulating the problem as a convex-concave upper bound on the empirical loss. The authors establish a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables, enabling efficient learning of deep trees. The paper is well-written, and the approach is elegant.

The main idea of the paper is to optimize the split functions at all levels of the tree jointly with the leaf parameters, based on a global objective. This is in contrast to standard greedy algorithms that optimize the split functions one node at a time. The authors show that the problem of finding optimal linear-combination (oblique) splits for decision trees is related to structured prediction with latent variables and formulate a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed solution. The use of stochastic gradient descent for optimization enables effective training with large datasets. The experiments on several classification benchmarks demonstrate that the resulting non-greedy decision trees outperform greedy decision tree baselines.

However, the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, and it is not entirely clear why the non-greedy approach does not lead to more significant improvements. One potential issue is that the non-greedy optimization may lead to overfitting, especially when the trees are deep. The authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the generalization performance of the proposed approach.

In terms of related work, the authors provide a comprehensive overview of existing decision tree algorithms and highlight the key differences between their approach and previous work. However, it would be helpful to provide more context on how the proposed approach relates to other machine learning models, such as random forests or gradient boosting machines.

Overall, I think this is a good paper that deserves publication on the basis of the elegance of the approach and its potential to lead to more research on related approaches. However, the experimental results could be stronger, and the authors should provide more analysis of the generalization performance of the proposed approach.

Strengths:

* Elegant approach to decision tree optimization
* Well-written paper with a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed solution
* Effective use of stochastic gradient descent for optimization

Weaknesses:

* Experimental results are somewhat disappointing
* Limited analysis of the generalization performance of the proposed approach
* Could benefit from more context on how the proposed approach relates to other machine learning models

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and elegant approach to decision tree optimization

Arguments for rejection:

* The paper could benefit from more analysis of the generalization performance of the proposed approach
* The authors should provide more context on how the proposed approach relates to other machine learning models.
