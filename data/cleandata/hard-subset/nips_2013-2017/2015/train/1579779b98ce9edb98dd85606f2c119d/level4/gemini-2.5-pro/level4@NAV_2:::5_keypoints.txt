- Introduces a novel non-greedy algorithm for decision trees with oblique splits, learning all split and leaf parameters jointly via a global objective.  
- Frames tree optimization as structured prediction with latent variables, providing a fresh and principled perspective.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective and optimizes it with SGD.  
- Proposes a modification (Stable SGD) to make training deep trees computationally feasible, but the description is unclear and needs pseudocode.  
- Experimental validation is weak: improvements over greedy baselines are marginal and not consistently convincing.  
- Increased computational complexity is a concern given limited empirical gains; broader or stronger results are needed.
