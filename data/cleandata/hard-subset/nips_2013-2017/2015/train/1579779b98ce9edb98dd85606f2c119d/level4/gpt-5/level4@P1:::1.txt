Post-rebuttal update: After discussion with the other reviewers and reading the rebuttal, I continue to believe this is a good paper that merits publication, primarily for the elegance of the approach and its potential to stimulate further work on related methods. I agree that the experimental results are somewhat disappointing; with stronger empirical performance I would have rated the paper higher, so I think my current score is appropriate.

Summary: The paper is very clearly written and proposes a nonconvex relaxation for the global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure, offering a principled alternative to standard greedy training. The approach is elegant and well presented.

Strengths:
- Elegant formulation of a non-greedy, global optimization procedure for decision trees with fixed structure.
- Clear, well-organized exposition.

Weaknesses:
- The experimental results are underwhelming relative to the promise of the method.

Suggestions for improvement:
- A simple post-processing variant could be informative: after applying the global optimization, discard the last split on each path and then reoptimize that split and the associated leaf parameters using the standard (greedy) procedure. This might yield stronger results with minimal added complexity.
- It is important to evaluate whether global optimization tends to increase overfitting. A more thorough analysis of overfitting behavior would strengthen the empirical section.
