- Proposes globally optimizing decision trees by reformulating as (latent) structured prediction with sign-vector latent variables for internal-node decisions.
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and a differentiable (still non-convex) upper-bound surrogate for optimization.
- Minimization via SGD requires solving a sequence of loss-augmented inference problems, for which efficient algorithms are provided.
- Key contributions: casting tree optimization as structured prediction and devising efficient optimization algorithms.
- Approach is novel, interesting, and raises intriguing research questions.
- Reviewer questions practical motivation and the significance of performance gains over simpler methods.
- SGD gradient updates appear sparse (each step may modify only one node’s weight), which the paper frames as a computational advantage.
- Sparse updates suggest unexplored possibilities (e.g., initializing zero weights and organically growing an infinitely deep tree), which the paper does not analyze.
- Raises the question whether organic growth would be equivalent to known greedy strategies or could yield sparse trees with proper regularization.
- Critical ambiguity about behavior when a node’s weight is zero—paper’s implied tie-breaking (taking right branch) is questionable and needs precise definition.
- Zero-weight behavior ties directly to pruning strategies and should be carefully analyzed.
- Practical concern: method likely yields non-interpretable trees due to L2-regularized dense weights; L1 might help but could hurt performance if enforcing 1-sparse splits.
- Experimental details are insufficient: naive baseline pruning strategies are not described, and the naive method should be tested with varied pruning/parameters.
- Suggests comparing against other straightforward global strategies (e.g., greedy training on random subsets with selection by validation loss).
- Paper is reasonably well-written but some inference descriptions could be more concise and clearer (e.g., Sections 6.2 and 6.4).
- Title mentions forests but the paper does not address random forests or ensemble cases.
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight initialization remains a critical issue; authors initialized weights randomly, but exploring zero-initialization and gradual growth is encouraged.
- Overall: clever reformulation and efficient algorithm, but practical utility, interpretability, and experimental validation are not fully convincing.
