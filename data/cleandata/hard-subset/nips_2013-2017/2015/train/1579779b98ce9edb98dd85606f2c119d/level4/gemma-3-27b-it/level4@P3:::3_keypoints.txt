- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision-tree induction method by formulating tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables and jointly optimizing all split functions.
- Uses a convex–concave upper bound on empirical loss and stochastic gradient descent for efficient training.
- Connects to structured prediction literature and is related to prior global tree optimization and soft-split methods, while remaining distinct.
- Theoretically solid formulation with derivation of the convex–concave upper bound.
- Significant technical contribution in reducing gradient computation complexity via algorithmic improvements.
- Introduces a stable SGD variant (SSGD) to address practical optimization challenges.
- Experimental results show consistent improvement over strong greedy baselines (e.g., OC1, CO2) on benchmark datasets.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly explains motivation, methodology, and experiments.
- Improvement magnitude over baselines is modest.
- Ablation study and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., regularization ν, loss choice) are limited and should be expanded.
- Related work discussion is incomplete, missing recent advances such as differentiable tree methods.
- Mentions the kernel trick but fails to elaborate on practical implications or challenges.
- Initialization procedure (axis-aligned tree and CO2) is briefly described and lacks detail on its effect on performance.
- Recommendation: accept, with requested revisions to add ablation studies, expand related work discussion, and clarify initialization details.
