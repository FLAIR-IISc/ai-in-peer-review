- Paper proposes jointly learning decision-tree split weights and leaf distributions by minimizing a global upper bound on empirical loss, regularized with L2 and optimized by SGD.
- Strength: Elegant formulation connecting the problem to structured prediction with latent variables.
- Strength: Principled idea of globally optimizing splits and leaf distributions under a regularized upper bound.
- Main concern: Empirical evidence does not convincingly show global optimization outperforms greedy induction—benefits appear limited to a few datasets.
- Main concern: Method incurs substantially higher computational cost, leaving practical advantage unclear given the accuracy vs. cost trade-off.
- Clarification needed: “Stable SGD” is not described—how are data points assigned to leaves during training; provide pseudocode.
- Clarification needed: Figure 2—are results from standard SGD or stable SGD, and what defines an “active” leaf?
- Clarification needed: Algorithm 1 (line 10) projects Θ onto a simplex—explain the projection implementation and note it enforces each row to sum to one.
- Minor: Lines 407–408 confusingly state tuning “number of features evaluated” for ordinary trees—standard trees typically consider all features; clarify.
- Minor: Equations (7)-(9) use “argmax” but should use “max.”
- Minor: Figure 1 is never referenced in the main text.
- Minor: Line 141 contains a malformed sentence fragment.
- Minor: Line 238 grammar—“the solution to loss-augmented inference” should be plural (“the solutions to…”).
- Minor: Line 301 typo—“An key observation” should be “A key observation.”
- Minor: Line 360 undefined acronym—define FSGD.
- Minor: Line 404 wording—“a tree with minimum training errors” likely intended “minimum tuning/validation errors.”
- Supplementary: Line 29 typo—use “max{g = sgn...}” instead of “max{g ∈ sgn...}.”
- Supplementary: Line 30 expression should read sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y).
- Conclusion: Framework is well motivated but experiments don’t provide compelling, consistent evidence for global optimization’s advantage; clarifying training variants, implementation details, and evaluation would strengthen the paper.
