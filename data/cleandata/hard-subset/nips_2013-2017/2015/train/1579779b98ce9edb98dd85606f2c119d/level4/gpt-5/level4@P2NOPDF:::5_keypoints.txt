- Proposes globally optimized decision trees with linear split functions, jointly learning split weights and leaf outputs by minimizing an upper bound on empirical loss with L2 regularization and SGD.  
- Optimization is elegantly connected to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Empirical evidence is weak: globally optimized trees outperform greedy baselines on only a few datasets and require substantially more computation.  
- "Stable" SGD is not described clearly—need concise pseudocode and a clear rule for how examples are assigned to leaves during training.  
- Figure 2: unclear whether results use standard SGD or the stable variant; must precisely define what counts as an “active” leaf.  
- Algorithm 1, line 10: projection of Θ onto a simplex needs implementation details and an explicit note that each row of Θ is being constrained to sum to one.  
- Clarify what is tuned in line 407–408 about “number of features evaluated” for standard trees (e.g., feature subsampling as in random forests?).  
- Equations (7), (8), (9): replace “argmax” with “max.”  
- Figure 1 is never cited in the main text and should be referenced or removed.  
- Line 141: fix the garbled phrase ending “...to the index of the leaf on by this path.”  
- Line 238: change “the solution to loss-augmented inference...” to “the solutions to...”.  
- Line 301: change “An key observation...” to “A key observation...”.  
- Line 360: define the acronym FSGD.  
- Line 404: “a tree with minimum training errors” likely should read “validation/tuning errors.”  
- Supplementary material fixes: line 29 change max{g ∈ sgn...} to max{g = sgn...}; line 30 change sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^T f(g), y) to sgn(Wx)^T Wx + l(Θ^T f(sgn(Wx)), y).  
- Recommendation: strengthen experiments—add more datasets, report rigorous runtime/accuracy trade-offs, and clarify training variants—to justify the extra computational cost of global optimization.
