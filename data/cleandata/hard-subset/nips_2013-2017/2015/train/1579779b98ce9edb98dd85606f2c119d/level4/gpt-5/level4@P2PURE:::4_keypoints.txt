- Contribution: Reformulates global decision-tree optimization as latent structured prediction with sign-vector latents and a differentiable surrogate loss optimized via SGD and efficient loss-augmented inference algorithms.
- Novelty: The structured-prediction formulation and efficient optimization algorithms are novel and interesting.
- Sparse updates: SGD updates are sparse, often modifying only a single internal node’s weight vector, which is presented as a computational advantage.
- Growing trees idea: Sparse updates suggest the possibility of initializing all weights at zero and gradually growing an effectively infinite tree as weights become nonzero—an idea the paper does not explore.
- Relation to greedy/pruning: It is unclear whether the proposed procedure would reduce to known greedy strategies, prefer creating new nodes over revising existing ones, or converge to compact trees under regularization; more analysis is needed.
- Zero-weight ambiguity: The behavior when a node’s weight vector is exactly zero is ill-specified (paper suggests taking the right branch), which has important implications for pruning and model selection.
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization produces dense, likely non-interpretable splits; switching to L1 could enforce sparsity but may hurt performance—this trade-off should be empirically evaluated.
- Experimental gaps: Baselines are under-specified (pruning strategies not detailed); naive baselines should be tuned similarly and include trivial global strategies (e.g., multiple greedy trees on random subsets).
- Inference clarity: Sections on inference (esp. Sec. 6.2 and 6.4) could be clearer and more explicit in their exposition.
- Forests claim: The title references forests but the paper does not address the forest setting.
- Post-rebuttal stance: Zero-weight behavior remains a critical issue; random initialization may mask it, and the reviewer encourages exploring zero initialization and gradual growth.
- Overall assessment: A clever, thought-provoking reformulation with efficient algorithms, but practical utility is unclear due to interpretability concerns and gaps in experimental validation.
