- Introduces a novel global optimization algorithm for learning decision trees with linear split functions, avoiding greedy induction.  
- Jointly optimizes split weights and leaf distributions via a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss with L2 regularization.  
- Uses stochastic gradient descent (SGD) for tractable minimization and connects decision tree learning to structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Claims an efficient gradient computation (O(d)) and reduces complexity enabling training of deeper trees on large datasets.  
- Builds on prior decision tree literature and targets the suboptimality of greedy tree induction.  
- Offers a fundamentally different, potentially more compact and generalizable approach compared to improved greedy methods.  
- Strength: genuinely novel and theoretically well-motivated via the structured prediction perspective.  
- Strength: computational efficiency improvements are significant for scaling to deeper trees.  
- Strength: clear and well-organized presentation overall.  
- Weakness: empirical gains over greedy baselines are inconsistent and improvements are substantial only on a few datasets; computational cost is higher.  
- Weakness: SSGD (Stable SGD) is insufficiently explained—assignment to leaves, pseudocode, and definition of "active" leaves are unclear.  
- Weakness: missing implementation detail on projection onto a simplex in Algorithm 1 (line 10).  
- Minor issues: several grammatical errors, typos, and incorrect/missing references (detailed below).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with meaningful computational gains and valuable structured-prediction connection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inconsistent empirical superiority, inadequate SSGD/implementation details for reproducibility, and minor writing errors.  
- Specific comments/typos: inaccurate statement about feature evaluation (L407-408); replace "argmax" with "max" in Eqns (7)-(9); Figure 1 unreferenced; multiple line-specific wording/notation fixes and unclear abbreviation FSGD.  
- Overall recommendation: promising paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen empirical evaluation, clarify SSGD and implementation, and fix identified errors.
