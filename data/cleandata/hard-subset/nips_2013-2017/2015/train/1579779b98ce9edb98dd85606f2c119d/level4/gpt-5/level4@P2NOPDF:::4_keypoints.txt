- Reformulates global decision tree optimization as a latent structured prediction problem with sign-vector latent variables.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and optimizes a differentiable (still non-convex) upper bound via SGD.  
- Each SGD step requires loss-augmented inference; the paper provides efficient procedures for this inference.  
- Main contributions: the structured-prediction formulation of global DT optimization and the design of efficient optimization algorithms; novel and thought‑provoking.  
- Key strength: SGD updates are sparse (typically modify a single node’s weight), suggesting computational benefits and richer modeling (e.g., grow-as-needed trees).  
- Raises open questions about when the method mimics or improves on greedy strategies, and whether adding nodes or revising existing nodes is preferable under regularization.  
- Ambiguity around zero-weight nodes: current tie-breaking (go right) is unsatisfying; better to treat zero-weight nodes as leaves with option to split later.  
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization yields dense, less-interpretable splits; L1 could restore sparsity but may harm accuracy — this trade-off needs empirical study.  
- Experimental evaluation is under-specified: greedy baseline pruning strategy and hyperparameters are not detailed and should be tuned comparably.  
- Recommend adding simple “global” baselines (e.g., multiple greedy trees on bootstraps/select best) and clarifying shared feature preprocessing, depth limits, and stopping criteria.  
- Clarity suggestions: streamline inference sections (sec.6.2 summarize as maximize g per leaf then over leaves) and give a step‑by‑step of loss-augmented inference showing which node is updated.  
- Summary verdict: clever and original with efficient algorithms and interesting sparse updates, but practical impact unclear due to interpretability issues and limited/unclear experiments.  
- Note: title mentions forests but the paper does not address forest models.  
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight initialization/grow-as-needed remains a fundamental and promising regime that should be analyzed and experimented with.
