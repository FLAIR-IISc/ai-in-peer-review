Following discussion with other reviewers and consideration of the rebuttal, I maintain my assessment that this is a strong paper meriting publication. The work is notable for the elegance of its proposed approach and its potential to stimulate further research in this area. While the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming, I believe the current score appropriately reflects this. The paper presents a clearly articulated nonconvex relaxation technique for the global optimization of decision trees with a predetermined structure. This is a promising method, though the empirical validation could be improved. A potential refinement would be to re-optimize the final split and associated leaf nodes using standard techniques after the global optimization phase, which may yield performance gains. It is also crucial to acknowledge and evaluate the inherent risk of increased overfitting associated with global optimization methods.
