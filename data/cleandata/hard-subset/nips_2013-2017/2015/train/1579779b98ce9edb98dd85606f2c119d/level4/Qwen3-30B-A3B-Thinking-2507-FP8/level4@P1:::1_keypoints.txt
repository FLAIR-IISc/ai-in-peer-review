- Proposes a novel non-greedy method that jointly optimizes split functions across all tree levels under a global objective.  
- Establishes a new connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Formulates a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss and proves the bound tightens as the weight matrix norm increases.  
- Key technical advance: reduces computational complexity from O(2^d p) to O(d² p) with a modified surrogate objective, enabling deep-tree training with SGD.  
- Provides an efficient loss-augmented inference algorithm and maintains O(d p) test-time inference complexity.  
- Handles general loss functions (not limited to binary 0-1 loss) unlike some prior non-greedy methods.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous and well-explained, with accessible mathematical exposition.  
- Algorithmic details are clear and implementation appears straightforward to reproduce.  
- Novelty is strong, properly contextualized relative to prior work (Bennett, CO2, HME, etc.).  
- Writing and organization are excellent, with helpful figures and clear technical narrative.  
- Experimental evaluation shows only modest performance gains over greedy baselines across multiple datasets.  
- Limited empirical evidence that the method yields substantial generalization improvements; potential for increased overfitting addressed only via norm regularization.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of when the non-greedy approach provides significant practical advantages.  
- Comparison to CO2 and other baselines shows marginal gains, questioning practical impact despite theoretical elegance.  
- Paper is a valuable theoretical contribution likely to stimulate further research; reviewer recommends acceptance despite empirical limitations.
