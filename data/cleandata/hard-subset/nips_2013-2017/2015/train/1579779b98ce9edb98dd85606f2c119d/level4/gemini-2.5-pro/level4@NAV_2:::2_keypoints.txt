- Proposes a novel non-greedy method that jointly trains all split functions and leaf parameters as a single global optimization.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree learning and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss as a surrogate objective to make optimization tractable.  
- Optimizes the surrogate objective with SGD and introduces a technical contribution to reduce gradient computation complexity for deep trees.  

- Strength: original and significant formulation that moves beyond greedy node-by-node tree induction.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized derivation of the surrogate objective.  
- Strength: potential for substantial impact if validated, enabling more compact and accurate trees.  

- Weakness: potential flaw in the proof of the upper bound (Equation 7); counterexample suggests the RHS can be smaller than the LHS, so the bound may not hold.  
- Weakness: minor suggestion to use max instead of argmax in the sgn(Wx) expression (Equation 5).  
- Weakness: some leaf nodes become "inactive" during training, indicating problematic optimization dynamics; proposed Stable SGD (SSGD) appears heuristic and under-motivated.  
- Weakness: SSGD should be better motivated or connected to established frameworks (e.g., EM or CCCP).  
- Weakness: fast loss-augmented inference (Hamming ball radius 1) needs stronger justification; ablation vs exact inference for moderate depths (d=10–14) is suggested.  
- Weakness: experimental baselines are incomplete—missing comparison to a standard greedy oblique-tree method (e.g., OC1) not used only for initialization.  
- Weakness: method requires tree depth specified in advance; sensitivity analysis to depth and discussion of this practical limitation are needed.  

- Overall: conceptually strong contribution, but a possible theoretical bound error and limited experimental evaluation reduce the paper’s current impact.
