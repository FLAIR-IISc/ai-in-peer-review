- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree optimization by framing tree learning as structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters, addressing limitations of greedy methods like ID3 and CART.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and connects the method to structured prediction literature.  
- Shows efficient gradient computation with O(d) complexity for trees of depth d, enabling deeper tree training.  
- Introduces Stable SGD (SSGD) and discusses regularization (ν) to improve algorithmic stability and leaf activation.  
- Empirical evaluation on SensIT, Connect4, Protein, and MNIST shows consistent improvements over greedy baselines, though gains are modest.  
- Theoretical grounding and clear presentation are key strengths of the paper.  
- Reduction of gradient complexity to O(d) is a significant technical contribution.  
- Regularization treatment and algorithmic stability considerations are well addressed.  
- Weakness: magnitude of empirical gains is relatively small and not transformative.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of dataset or depth conditions under which the method outperforms greedy approaches.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to more recent/advanced tree methods (e.g., gradient boosting, Mondrian processes).  
- Weakness: related work discussion could be more critical and some design choices (e.g., specific regularization form) need stronger justification.  
- Recommendation: solid contribution; accept with minor revisions to expand analysis, comparisons, and justification of design choices.
