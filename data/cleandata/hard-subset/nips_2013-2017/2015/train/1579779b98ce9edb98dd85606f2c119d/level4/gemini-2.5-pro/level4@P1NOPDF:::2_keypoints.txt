1. Summary: Proposes joint training of all decision-tree parameters via a single optimization with an approximate upper bound to make it tractable.  
2. Quality: Well-written but contains a potential flaw in the derivation of a key theoretical bound.  
3. Clarity: Objective derivation is clear, but the experimental section and presentation need significant improvement.  
4. Originality: Addresses the novel and important problem of moving beyond greedy, layer-wise tree training.  
5. Significance: Valuable contribution, but impact is limited by the potential theoretical flaw and narrow experiments.  
6. Comment 1 (theoretical bound): The upper-bound derivation (Eq.7) is unconvincing and a counterexample shows the proof (and use of argmax) may be incorrect/incomplete.  
7. Comment 2 (empty leaves): Some leaves receive no data after training, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization; the proposed heuristic fix is ad hoc and could be better framed (e.g., EM or CCCP).  
8. Comment 3 (loss-augmented inference): The need for an efficient inference algorithm is not strongly motivated given feasible brute-force depth; impact of the Hamming-ball heuristic is unclear and terminology is ambiguous.  
9. Comment 4 (missing baseline): Experiments lack an important baseline: greedy training of non-axis-aligned split functions for direct comparison.  
10. Comment 5 (fixed depth): Tree depth is a required hyperparameter; this limitation should be discussed and studied via ablations.  
11. Comment 6 (runtime): Paper lacks experimental runtime comparisons to baselines to assess practical trade-offs.  
12. Conclusion: Interesting problem and approach, but two main weaknesses—possible theoretical flaw and limited experimental evaluation—must be addressed for reconsideration.
