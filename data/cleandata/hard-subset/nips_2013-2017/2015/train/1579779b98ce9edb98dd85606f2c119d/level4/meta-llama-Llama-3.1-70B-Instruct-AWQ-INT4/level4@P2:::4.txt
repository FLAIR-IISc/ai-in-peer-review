The submission proposes an algorithm for global optimization of decision trees based on a reformulation of the problem as (latent) structured prediction. The latent structured variables are sign vectors encoding the decisions made at each internal node in the tree.

The paper presents a novel approach to decision tree optimization, formulating the problem as structured prediction with latent variables. This reformulation allows for the use of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) to optimize an upper bound on the empirical loss of the tree's predictions on the training set. The key contributions of the work are casting the global DT optimization as structured prediction and devising efficient algorithms to optimize the objective.

The method has several advantages, including the ability to perform SGD by solving a sequence of loss-augmented optimization problems that leverage the special structure of the problem. Additionally, the gradient updates are sparse, involving changes to only one node's weight vector at a time. This property raises interesting possibilities, such as optimizing over an infinitely deep tree by initially setting all weights to zero and gradually growing the tree by making more weights nonzero.

However, there are some concerns regarding the motivation for the method and the significance of the performance gains over naive methods. Decision trees are often used due to their interpretability, but the proposed method may produce non-interpretable results due to the L2 regularization producing dense weight vectors. Furthermore, the experiments do not provide enough detail regarding other baselines, such as pruning strategies for the naive approach.

The paper is well-written, but some sections could be clearer. The parts describing inference could be improved, and some sections could be summarized more concisely.

In summary, the submission proposes a clever approach to decision tree optimization, but its practical usefulness is unclear due to concerns about interpretability and a lack of details in the experiments.
