- Reformulates global decision tree optimization as a latent structured prediction using sign vectors, enabling gradient-based training via loss-augmented inference.  
- Introduces an SGD approach with sparse gradient updates that modify only a single node per step, suggesting possible automatic/adaptive tree growth by activating weights from zero.  
- Strength: mathematically sound and conceptually novel; connects DT optimization to structured prediction and presents technically solid inference algorithms (Sections 6.2–6.4), though some parts are opaque.  
- Major interpretability concern: L2 regularization yields dense weights that destroy DT interpretability; no evidence L1 or other sparsity methods preserve performance—method doesn’t justify its use when interpretability is required.  
- Theoretical ambiguity about zero weights: treating zero weights as defaulting to the right branch is unjustified; zero-initialization should imply unsplit/leaf semantics and changes growth dynamics—needs clarification and analysis.  
- Experimental weaknesses: insufficient benchmarking and unfair baselines (greedy method without pruning/hyperparameter parity); missing trivial global baselines and broader comparisons to validate claimed gains.  
- Clarity/scope issues: Sections 6.2 and 6.4 need concise summaries; title mentions "forests" but only single trees are addressed.  
- Required revisions: explicitly address interpretability (e.g., L1 with performance validation), analyze tree growth under zero-initialization, and expand experiments with fair baselines and pruning strategies.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on major revisions to resolve interpretability, zero-weight theory, and experimental rigor.
