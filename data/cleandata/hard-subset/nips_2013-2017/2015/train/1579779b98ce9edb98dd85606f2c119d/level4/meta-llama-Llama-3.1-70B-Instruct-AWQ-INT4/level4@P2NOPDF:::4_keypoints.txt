- Reformulates global decision-tree optimization as structured prediction with latent sign vectors encoding internal-node decisions.  
- Introduces a non-differentiable, non-convex loss and a differentiable non-convex upper bound to approximate it.  
- Minimizes the surrogate via SGD, requiring repeated loss-augmented inference; provides efficient algorithms for these subproblems.  
- Main contribution: novel casting of global DT optimization as structured prediction plus efficient optimization methods.  
- Gradient updates are sparse—each step alters only one node's weight—offering computational advantages.  
- Method suggests interesting possibilities like growing tree depth or adapting model complexity (even infinite-depth trees).  
- Practical usefulness and motivation are unclear; performance gains over naive methods are not convincingly significant.  
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization can produce dense, non-interpretable splits; unclear impact of L1 or enforcing 1-sparse splits.  
- Experimental details are insufficient: baselines unclear, pruning strategy for greedy baseline unspecified, and simple baselines (e.g., greedy on random subsets) not considered.  
- Paper is mostly well written but parts—especially inference—need clearer exposition.  
- Overall: clever approach that raises questions, but practical impact is uncertain due to interpretability and experimental gaps.  
- Post-rebuttal: zero-weight initialization remains a critical issue; authors should analyze initializing weights at zero and gradually increasing them.
