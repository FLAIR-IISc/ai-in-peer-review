Edit after response: After discussing it with other reviewers and seeing the rebuttal, I still think this is a good paper worthy of publication based on the elegance of the approach and the fact that it is likely to lead to more research on related approaches. I agree that the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. Nevertheless, with the strong results, I would probably give the paper an even higher rating, so I think this score is appropriate. The very clearly written paper proposes a non-convex discount for global optimization of decision trees with a fixed structure. The approach is elegant, but the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. A simple change would be to, after using this global optimization, remove the final partition in each tree and optimize it and the leaf nodes in the standard way, which could yield better results. It is also important to evaluate whether global optimization generally leads to over-fitting.
