- Paper proposes jointly training all decision-tree parameters (splits and posteriors) via a single optimization with an upper-bound approximation.

- Critical flaw: the empirical loss bound (Equation 7) is incorrectly derived; counterexample shows RHS can be smaller for g ≠ sgn(Wx), and the supplementary proof doesn't fix this.

- Technical reasoning around the bound requires careful scrutiny and completion before claims are valid.

- Unassigned leaves observed during training suggest issues with the objective or initialization; need to clarify if this is methodological or numerical and consider EM/CCCP-style justification.

- Efficiency concern: restricting loss-augmented inference to a Hamming ball (for deep trees) needs stronger evaluation since exhaustive greedy search may be feasible; supplementary Table S4 is insufficient.

- Ambiguity: clarify what "leaves" refers to in experiments (tree leaves or other) and the impact of the Hamming-ball constraint across datasets.

- Missing baseline: no comparison to standard greedy training of non-axis-aligned trees, which is essential to isolate the benefit of joint optimization.

- Tree depth must be specified a priori — discuss practical implications, sensitivity to depth choice, and report performance vs. depth.

- Experimental issues: lack of runtime comparisons and insufficient presentation/detail in experimental design.

- Overall: idea is meaningful and potentially significant, but the proof gap and limited experimental validation undermine confidence; reviewer would reconsider acceptance if these issues are thoroughly addressed.
