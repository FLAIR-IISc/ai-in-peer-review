- Novel formulation: frames global decision-tree optimization as a latent structured prediction problem with sign vectors as latent variables.  
- Optimization approach: introduces a non-convex, non-differentiable loss, relaxed to a differentiable upper bound and minimized via SGD.  
- Efficient inference: paper provides algorithms for the required sequence of loss-augmented inference problems.  
- Main contributions: original formulation and efficient optimization algorithms for global tree training.  
- Sparse gradient updates: each SGD step appears to change only one node's weight, an understudied and potentially important property.  
- Tree-growth idea: sparsity suggests the possibility of growing an infinitely deep tree from zero-initialized weights, raising questions about equivalence to greedy methods or the role of regularization.  
- Zero-weight handling ambiguity: unclear how nodes with zero weights are treated (default right branch vs explicit leaf), which affects pruning/splitting behavior.  
- Interpretability concern: L2 regularization likely yields dense, non-interpretable splits; L1 could enforce sparsity but may harm performance—needs experimental validation.  
- Experimental shortcomings: insufficient detail on baselines, unspecified pruning strategies, and lack of comparable tuning for standard greedy methods.  
- Missing comparisons: should include simple global heuristics (e.g., multiple greedy trees on random subsets) for fair evaluation.  
- Clarity issues: inference algorithm descriptions (notably Sections 6.2 and 6.4) could be made more explicit and succinct.  
- Title mismatch: paper mentions forests in the title but does not address them in the content.  
- Overall judgment: clever and thought-provoking formulation but practical utility unclear due to interpretability issues and incomplete experimental comparisons.  
- Post-rebuttal point: reviewer reiterates importance of analyzing zero-weight initialization and favors exploring zero-based tree growth despite authors' random initialization response.
