- Paper proposes jointly training all decision-tree parameters via a global optimization and a convex upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Writing and derivations are generally clear, but a potential flaw exists in a key loss-bound derivation.  
- Joint optimization of tree parameters is original and significant, potentially overcoming greedy local optima.  
- Main theoretical concern: the loss upper-bound argument appears incorrect (counterexamples show the bound can be violated); supplementary proof seems incomplete.  
- Experiments show some leaves become empty after training, suggesting issues with the objective or initialization; the proposed fixed-assignment heuristic is ad hoc and needs better justification.  
- The practical necessity of the proposed efficient loss-augmented inference is unclear for reported depths; runtime comparisons with full search and more analysis of Hamming-ball restrictions are requested.  
- Missing experimental baseline: a non-axis-aligned split tree trained greedily; this comparison is needed to isolate gains from joint optimization.  
- Method requires pre-specified tree depth (no dynamic growth); sensitivity analysis to depth and discussion of this limitation are missing.  
- Overall: approach is compelling but needs a corrected/clarified loss bound and stronger, more thorough experiments before stronger acceptance; reviewer may raise score if issues are resolved.
