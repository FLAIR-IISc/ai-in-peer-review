- Proposes a novel method for jointly training all decision-tree parameters by framing the task as a single optimization problem and deriving a tractable upper-bounded approximation.  
- Paper is well-written with a clean mathematical derivation.  
- Potential flaw/confusing argument around lines 216–217: the derivation of the empirical loss bound appears suspect.  
- Concrete counterexample: for Wx = [5; −5], choosing g = [1;1] can make the RHS of Eq. (7) smaller than the LHS, questioning the validity of the bound.  
- Authors should provide a clearer, more detailed explanation and fix or justify the empirical loss bound derivation.  
- Experimental section lacks crucial details (datasets used, hyperparameters tuned, evaluation metrics) and needs more transparency.  
- Method requires the tree depth to be specified a priori; this limitation should be discussed and empirically examined.  
- Recommend adding a baseline that trains non-axis-aligned split functions greedily without initialization to compare fairly.  
- Recommend more experiments/ablation on the importance of efficient loss-augmented inference and the effect of the Hamming-ball restricted search space.  
- Overall originality is high and results look promising, but key theoretical and experimental issues must be addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel joint-training approach, clear writing, valuable contribution with promising results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: possible derivation flaw, limited experimental evaluation, and the restrictive requirement to predefine tree depth.
