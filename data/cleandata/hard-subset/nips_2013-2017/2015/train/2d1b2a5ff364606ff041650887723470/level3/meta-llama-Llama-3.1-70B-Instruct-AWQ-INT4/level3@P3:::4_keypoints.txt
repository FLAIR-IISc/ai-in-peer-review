- Presents a significant extension of Plan & Vershynin (2015) by precisely analyzing Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Shows the asymptotic estimation error for generalized LASSO under arbitrary non-linear links and finds it identical for linear and non-linear link functions.  
- Derives the first asymptotically exact expression for the error, capturing the roles of link function, signal structure, regularizer, and regularization parameter.  
- Uses the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework to obtain tight, precise error bounds.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically well-organized with helpful examples and illustrations.  
- Strength: provides a precise analysis of generalized LASSO in the non-linear measurement setting.  
- Limitation: relies on iid standard normal (Gaussian) measurement matrix assumption, limiting practical applicability.  
- Limitation: lacks guidance on selecting the regularizer based on the set K containing x0, an important practical issue.  
- Limitation: insufficient citation and discussion of literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction.  
- Limitation: limited discussion of practical implications and how results could be applied in practice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel precise analysis, use of CGMT, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy Gaussian assumption, weak guidance on regularizer selection, incomplete literature coverage, and limited discussion of implications.  
- Overall scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
