- Novel approach to learning the Multinomial Logit (MNL) model from ordinal data.
- Extension of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and Gaussian design.
- Precise, explicit expressions for error estimation are derived.
- Accuracy validated via many special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm).
- Builds on Plan & Vershynin with novel explicit performance-error expressions.
- Insightful application to designing optimal quantizers in q-Bit compressed sensing and connection to Lloyd–Max quantizer.
- Technically sound: theory and experiments support the claims.
- Well-written and well-organized with standard notation and adequate background.
- Originality lies in combining familiar techniques (Generalized Lasso, MNL) to solve a new problem.
- Significance: potential impact on personalized recommendation systems and revenue management.
- Asymptotic finding: performance with non-linear measurements matches that of linear noisy measurements.
- Strength: careful and honest evaluation of strengths and weaknesses.
- Weakness: treatment is somewhat unbalanced due to lack of space.
- Suggested improvements: include more experimental results and more background on the MNL model.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
