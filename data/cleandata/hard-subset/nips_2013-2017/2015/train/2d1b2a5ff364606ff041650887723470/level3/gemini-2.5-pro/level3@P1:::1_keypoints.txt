- Problem: Estimates structured high-dimensional signal x0 from non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) with Gaussian design.  
- Method: Analyzes square-root Generalized LASSO applied directly to these non-linear measurements.  
- Main result: Asymptotic, exact characterization of MSE showing equivalence to a linear model yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi with μ,σ determined by g.  
- Technique: Leverages precise LASSO analysis tools (e.g., CGMT) to derive the formulas.  
- Relation to prior work: Strengthens and formalizes intuition from Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin by moving from bounds to exact asymptotics.  
- Scope: Results apply for general regularizers and non-linearities, with specializations to sparse and group-sparse cases.  
- Validation: Theoretical predictions are convincingly validated by simulations (Figures 1 and 2).  
- Quality: Technically deep and sound work, self-contained and complete.  
- Clarity: Well-written and clearly organized introduction and messaging; core message communicated effectively.  
- Weaknesses: Numerous typographical errors (especially in appendices) that reduce polish and need proofreading.  
- Originality: Highly original — first asymptotically exact performance characterization for regularized LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Potential missed literature: Reviewer not expert in entire single-index or sufficient-dimension-reduction fields, so some related work might be missing.  
- Significance (theory): Provides a powerful analytical reduction from non-linear to linear problems, enabling further theoretical work.  
- Significance (practice): Justifies LASSO use in non-linear/quantized settings and yields practical applications (e.g., optimal q-bit quantizer design via minimizing σ²/μ²).  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, clean theoretical result; deepens understanding of LASSO; extends important prior work; demonstrates practical utility.  
- Rejection concerns: Asymptotic nature leaves finite-sample performance uncharacterized; manuscript needs careful proofreading.
