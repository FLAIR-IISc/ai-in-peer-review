- Main contribution: shows asymptotic estimation error for generalized LASSO is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions under specific conditions, extending prior work to µ ≠ 0.  
- Provides precise theoretical analysis and explicit expressions for the estimation error.  
- Demonstrates practical implications for quantized measurements and analyzes the Lloyd–Max algorithm.  
- Positions work within single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, building on Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin.  
- Novelty and rigor: the equivalence result is surprising and backed by rigorous proofs.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and notationally consistent.  
- Connection to recent precise LASSO analyses (Stojnic; Thrampoulidis et al.) is appropriate.  
- Major omission: fails to cite Yi et al. (2015), which addresses sparse recovery with µ = 0 and establishes a closely related equivalence—this citation should be added.  
- Limitation: strong Gaussian measurement-vector assumption; need discussion/justification and potential extensions to non-Gaussian matrices.  
- Practical gap: lacks guidance on selecting convex regularizers and tuning parameters based on the constraint set K.  
- Dependence on µ and σ: reviewer asks whether these dependencies are necessary and if results can be generalized to reduce sensitivity.  
- Empirical validation is limited; more experiments across diverse link functions and signal structures are needed.  
- Recommendation: overall strong and worthy of acceptance, contingent on addressing Gaussian assumption, regularizer selection, µ/σ dependence, and adding Yi et al. (2015).
