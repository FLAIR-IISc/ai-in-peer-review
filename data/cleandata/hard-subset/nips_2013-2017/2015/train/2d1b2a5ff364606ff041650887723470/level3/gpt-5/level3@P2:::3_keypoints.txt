- Problem setup: analyzes generalized LASSO under non-linear single-index measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) with Gaussian A and convex regularizer f.  
- Main equivalence: Theorem 2.1 shows asymptotic error equals that of a linear model with effective parameters μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Exact error: Theorem 2.2 gives an exact asymptotic error via a three-scalar convex program involving a functional F defined through the Moreau envelope of the Fenchel conjugate.  
- Sparse refinement: Theorem 2.3 provides a refined, simulation-matching characterization for sparse recovery.  
- Specializations: results specialized to ℓ1 (sparse), group-ℓ1,2 (group-sparse), and nuclear-norm (low-rank) regularizers.  
- Quantization application: q-bit compressive sensing application shows minimizing σ^2/μ^2 optimizes error and links the objective to Lloyd–Max stationary points.  
- Novelty: extends Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin to high-dimensional proportional regimes, penalized LASSO with general convex f, and precise asymptotics via CGMT/AMP techniques.  
- Strength — conceptual clarity: clean equivalence principle reducing non-linear to linear measurement analysis.  
- Strength — technical: asymptotically exact, low-dimensional error formula applicable across regularizers.  
- Strength — breadth: unified treatment of multiple structures via the F functional.  
- Strength — practical impact: elegant, novel quantizer-design insight with practical linkage to Lloyd–Max.  
- Strength — experiments: simulations closely match theoretical predictions.  
- Weakness — design assumption: analysis restricted to Gaussian i.i.d. designs; robustness to sub-Gaussian/rotationally invariant designs should be discussed.  
- Weakness — Assumption 1: needs more rigorous formalization and concrete sufficient conditions (suggest epi-/Mosco-convergence tools).  
- Weakness — terminology/notation: misuse of “proximal function” for Moreau envelope and inconsistent use of “algorithm” vs “estimator”; reserve “prox” for proximal operator.  
- Weakness — constrained vs penalized: should explicitly state equivalence (λ ↔ constraint radius) and clarify transfer of results.  
- Weakness — edge cases and estimation: address μ = 0 scenarios (e.g., even g) and discuss estimating μ,σ when g is unknown and plug-in effects on the error formula.  
- Weakness — exposition: include a brief proof sketch for Theorem 2.1 (CGMT route) instead of relegating all proofs to the appendix.  
- Questions for authors: provide sufficient conditions for Assumption 1, robustness to non-Gaussian designs, methods for estimating μ,σ from data, and practical guidance for selecting λ using the formulas.  
- Minor fixes: correct Moreau/prox naming, use “estimator/convex program” not “algorithm,” fix figure caption and typos, ensure consistent square-root LASSO definition, and consider adding a proof sketch/flowchart for computing F.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant; recommendation — Accept.
