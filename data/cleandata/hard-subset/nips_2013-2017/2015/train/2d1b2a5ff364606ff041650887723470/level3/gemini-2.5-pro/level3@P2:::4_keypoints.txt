- Problem: Estimates structured signal x₀ from non-linear measurements yᵢ = gᵢ(aᵢᵀx₀) and studies using Generalized LASSO on these measurements.  
- Main result: Asymptotic MSE of LASSO with non-linear measurements equals that for a linear model yᵢ = µ aᵢᵀx₀ + σ zᵢ, where µ and σ depend only on g (Theorem 2.1).  
- Contribution: Replaces prior non-asymptotic bounds with exact asymptotic error formulas and provides a general framework (Theorem 2.2).  
- Applications: Applies framework to sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery and to q-bit quantizer design (optimizing σ²/µ², connected to Lloyd–Max).  
- Strength: Strong, elegant and significant theoretical advance giving precise, intuitive characterization of LASSO performance under non-linear measurements.  
- Strength: Clear writing, precise theorem statements, and empirical results that validate theoretical predictions.  
- Strength: Original derivation of exact asymptotic errors and novel link to quantizer design.  
- Weakness/question: Analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix—need discussion on whether this is a proof-technical limitation (e.g., CGMT) or a fundamental restriction and potential extension to sub-Gaussian ensembles.  
- Weakness/question: Practical guidance lacking on choosing convex regularizer f(x) given a constraint set K; authors should discuss how to map K to f.  
- Weakness/question: Request more intuition for why only µ and σ matter asymptotically and why higher-order moments of g vanish in the high-dimensional limit.  
- Weakness/question: Omission of relevant literature—should place work within single-index/sufficient-dimension-reduction context and directly compare to Yi et al. (2015), which handles the µ=0 case.  
- Overall evaluation: Technically sound and significant paper likely worthy of acceptance if authors address missing citations (especially Yi et al.) and clarify limitations (Gaussian matrices and µ≠0).
