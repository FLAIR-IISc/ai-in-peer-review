- Studies asymptotic estimation error for Generalized LASSO with arbitrary nonlinear link functions.  
- Main contribution: extends Plan & Vershynin to nonzero µ and shows asymptotic error equals that for linear measurements, with precise error expressions.  
- Paper is well-written and grounded in high-dimensional statistics and sparse recovery.  
- Application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates practical relevance and informs optimal quantizer design.  
- Major weakness: strong Gaussian assumption on measurement vectors is not justified and limits generality.  
- Does not explore robustness of results to other measurement matrix ensembles.  
- Practical issue: selection of regularization parameter λ (based on K containing x0) is not addressed; lacks practical guidance.  
- Theorem’s dependence on µ and σ may be overly strong; authors should discuss necessity or potential tightening.  
- Literature review incomplete; missing key single-index/sufficient dimension reduction works (e.g., Ichimura 2006; Li & Duan 1989).  
- Missing discussion of Yi et al. (2015) on equivalence of linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation when µ = 0.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound result, clear presentation, and impactful applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unjustified Gaussian assumption, lack of λ-selection guidance, and incomplete citations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing Gaussian assumption and literature review gaps.
