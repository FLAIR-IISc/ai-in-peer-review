

**Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"**

This paper investigates the estimation performance of the Generalized LASSO when applied to non-linear measurement models of the form $y_i = g_i(\mathbf{a}_i^T \mathbf{x}_0)$, where $g_i$ are independent copies of a link function $g$. The main contribution is proving that under certain asymptotic conditions (with the measurement matrix having i.i.d. Gaussian entries), the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO for non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled and noisy observations. Specifically, the paper shows $\|\hat{\mathbf{x}} - \mu \mathbf{x}_0\|_2^2$ is asymptotically identical to the error of the Generalized LASSO applied to linear measurements $y_i = \mu \mathbf{a}_i^T \mathbf{x}_0 + \sigma z_i$, where $\mu = \mathbb{E}[\gamma g(\gamma)]$ and $\sigma^2 = \mathbb{E}[(g(\gamma) - \mu \gamma)^2]$ for $\gamma \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$. This extends the recent work of Plan and Vershynin (2015) who established that the constrained LASSO solution estimates $\mu \mathbf{x}_0$ but only provided loose upper bounds. The authors also derive precise asymptotic characterizations of the error, which are the first-known precise results in this context.

**Strengths:**

*Quality:* The paper makes a technically sound contribution with rigorous proofs under reasonable assumptions. The main theorems are well-supported by theoretical analysis and extensive numerical validation. The connection between non-linear and linear measurements is established with mathematical rigor, and the results generalize previous work on the LASSO with linear measurements.

*Clarity:* The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized. The authors provide excellent motivation for the problem, carefully distinguish their contributions from prior work, and present the technical content with clarity. The paper is sufficiently detailed to allow reproduction of the results.

*Originality:* The precise asymptotic characterization of the Generalized LASSO error for non-linear measurements represents a significant advancement over previous work. The paper extends Plan and Vershynin's results to provide precise error expressions rather than loose bounds, which is a notable contribution to the field. The connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer as a consequence of the main result provides a practical insight for optimal quantizer design.

*Significance:* The results have important implications for understanding LASSO-type estimators in non-linear measurement settings. The precise error characterization provides theoretical foundations for practitioners using these methods in applications with non-linear measurements. The connection to optimal quantizer design in compressed sensing is particularly noteworthy.

**Weaknesses:**

*Quality:* The paper assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices. The authors do not sufficiently justify why this assumption is necessary for the main result. While Gaussian assumptions are common in high-dimensional analysis, it would be valuable to discuss whether the results hold for more general measurement ensembles or to provide intuition about the necessity of this assumption.

*Originality:* The paper misses important citations to the single-index model literature (e.g., [4,5,14,15] mentioned in the paper) which has studied similar problems in classical statistics. Additionally, the recent work by Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions should be cited, as it addresses sparse recovery even when $\mu = 0$ and shows equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation.

*Significance:* While the paper discusses convex regularizers, it does not provide clear guidance on how to select the appropriate regularizer based on the structure of $\mathbf{x}_0$ (i.e., the set $K$ containing $\mathbf{x}_0$). This is a practical limitation for potential users of the method.

**Arguments for acceptance:** The main result is technically sound and provides a precise characterization of the Generalized LASSO error in non-linear measurement settings. The paper extends previous work by Plan and Vershynin to provide precise asymptotic results rather than loose bounds. The connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer provides a valuable practical insight.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The paper misses important citations to the single-index model literature and recent work by Yi et al. (2015). The necessity of the Gaussian measurement assumption is not sufficiently justified. The paper does not provide clear guidance on selecting regularizers based on the structure of $\mathbf{x}_0$.

**Overall recommendation:** The paper makes a valuable contribution to understanding Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. While the work is technically sound and well-written, the missing citations and lack of justification for the Gaussian assumption are significant weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address these issues.
