- Problem: Estimating structured signal x0 from non-linear measurements yi = g(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian A, using the Generalized LASSO that treats measurements as linear.

- Main theoretical contribution: Precise asymptotic characterization of MSE showing Generalized LASSO on non-linear measurements is equivalent to an effective linear model yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ zi, where μ, σ depend only on g.

- Method: Derives an explicit scalar convex optimization formula for asymptotic MSE (‖x̂ − μx0‖²) using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT).

- Specializations: Applies the general result to sparse recovery (ℓ1 LASSO), group-sparse recovery (ℓ1,2 Group LASSO), and low-rank recovery (nuclear norm).

- Application: Uses theory to design optimal q-bit quantizers in compressive sensing, reducing design to minimizing σ²/μ² and connecting to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.

- Strength — Significance/Originality: Provides a substantially tighter, asymptotic characterization than prior non-asymptotic bounds (e.g., Plan & Vershynin), giving explicit computable error formulas.

- Strength — Technical Quality: Rigorous theoretical framework supported by numerical simulations across settings, confirming predicted equivalence to the noisy linear model.

- Strength — Insightful Application: Quantizer design result is practically actionable and ties elegantly to classical quantization theory.

- Weakness — Clarity/Organization: Presentation is condensed and unbalanced due to space constraints; central assumptions and derivations are terse with technical details pushed to the appendix.
