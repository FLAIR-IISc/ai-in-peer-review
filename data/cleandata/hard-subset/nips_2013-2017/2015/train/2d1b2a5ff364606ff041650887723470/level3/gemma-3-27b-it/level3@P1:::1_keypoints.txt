- Main contribution: Shows asymptotically the Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is equivalent to a linear model with appropriately scaled measurements and noise.  
- Precise error characterization: Provides explicit, exact expressions for the estimation error (strengthening prior upper-bound results).  
- Optimal quantizer: Identifies the Lloyd–Max quantizer as optimal for minimizing estimation error in q-bit compressive sensing.  
- Builds on prior work: Extends results of Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin by giving precise formulas rather than bounds.  
- Practical relevance: Addresses real-world measurement nonlinearities and has direct implications for compressive sensing and signal processing.  
- Theoretical rigor: Analysis is mathematically sound, asymptotic, and carefully defines necessary assumptions.  
- Presentation quality: Generally clear exposition but marred by numerous typos, especially in proofs.  
- Methodology: Uses asymptotic analysis and links nonlinear and linear measurement models under stated assumptions.  
- Novelty: Precise error formulas and the quantizer identification are novel and useful contributions.  
- Reproducibility concern: Typographical errors in proofs could hinder reproducibility and clarity.  
- Literature gap: Reviewer unfamiliarity with single-index and sufficient dimension reduction literature raises potential overlap concerns; deeper engagement is needed.  
- Limitation of applicability: Results rely on strong assumptions (Gaussian measurement matrix and specific signal structure), which may limit generality.  
- Overall verdict: Significant and practically relevant work; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: Fix typos in proofs and add discussion clarifying novelty relative to the single-index model literature.
