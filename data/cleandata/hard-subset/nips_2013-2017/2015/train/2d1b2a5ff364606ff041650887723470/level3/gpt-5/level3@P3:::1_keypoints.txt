- Main result: Generalized square-root LASSO with nonlinear measurements asymptotically behaves like a linear model yi ≈ μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with exact three-scalar error characterization (Theorem 2.2).  
- Methodology: Uses CGMT-based precise asymptotic analysis to derive a convex three-scalar program for the estimator error.  
- Specializations: Provides instantiations for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank regularizers.  
- Novel application: Uses characterization to design optimal q-bit quantizers; Lloyd–Max rules arise as optimal for minimizing LASSO error.  
- Relation to prior work: Strengthens Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin by moving from consistency/bounds to exact asymptotics for regularized LASSO and connects to AMP/CGMT literature.  
- Strength — clarity of message: Clear, compelling statement that nonlinear measurements reduce to an effective linear model with μ and σ.  
- Strength — technical rigor: Strong technical development with convincing CGMT derivations and precise formulas.  
- Strength — practical consequence: Quantizer-design implication is interesting and practically relevant.  
- Writing: Generally well written and well positioned relative to prior work.  
- Limitation — design assumptions: Restricted to i.i.d. Gaussian designs and independent links; robustness to sub-Gaussian/anisotropic/correlated designs not addressed.  
- Limitation — μ ≠ 0 required: Results exclude even-symmetric links (μ = 0); practical diagnostics/remedies are not fully discussed.  
- Limitation — Assumption 1 opaque: The Moreau-envelope limit condition is technical and needs more intuition and worked examples beyond ℓ1 and ℓ1,2.  
- Limitation — low-rank case underdeveloped: Setup present but F not concretely evaluated and experiments are missing.  
- Empirics: Empirical validation should include comparisons to single-index tailored estimators and other nonlinear/quantized baselines.  
- Presentation issues: Numerous typos, minor inconsistencies, and confusing nomenclature around "generalized LASSO"/"l2-LASSO"/"square-root LASSO" that need fixing.  
- Quality assessment: Technical development appears sound and convincing, though proofs are dense and require careful proofreading.  
- Clarity suggestions: Add intuition and recipes for computing F, a worked nuclear-norm example, and consistent terminology.  
- Extension suggestions: Discuss (or conjecture) extensions to sub-Gaussian/anisotropic designs and provide guidance when μ is small or unknown (estimation, dithering, alternative estimators).  
- Recommendation: Accept — precise, insightful theory with broad applicability and novel practical implications.
