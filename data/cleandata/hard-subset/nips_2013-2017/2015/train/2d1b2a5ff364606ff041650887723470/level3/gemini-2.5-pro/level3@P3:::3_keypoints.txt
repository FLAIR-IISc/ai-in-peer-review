- Problem: Estimating structured signal x0 from nonlinear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) with Gaussian A using generalized Lasso.
- Main contribution: Shows exact high-dimensional asymptotic equivalence to a linear model y_lin = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with μ,σ set by moments of g.
- Theoretical advance: Derives novel, explicit asymptotic MSE formulas for generalized Lasso under nonlinear measurements.
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger to high-dimensional regime and sharpens Plan & Vershynin’s non-asymptotic bounds to exact asymptotics.
- Validation: Numerical experiments confirm theory for Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization.
- Application: Uses results to design optimal q-bit quantizers via minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and links to Lloyd–Max quantizer.
- Strength: Technically deep, elegant, and provides fundamental insight into why linear decoders work for nonlinear problems.
- Originality: First precise asymptotic characterization for generalized Lasso with nonlinear measurements, capturing signal, regularizer, link, and dimensions.
- Presentation issue: Paper is well-written but unbalanced; space constraints omit some important details.
- Terminology suggestion: Refer to generalized Lasso as a "convex program" or "decoder" instead of an "algorithm."
- Notation suggestion: Use established notation for Moreau envelope (e.g., e_{f,τ}) instead of labeling it "proximal function" to avoid confusion.
- Assumption 1: Should be given a more formal interpretation (e.g., via variational analysis or random process convergence) to clarify scope.
- Formulation equivalence: Briefly comment how parameters of the equivalent linear model change for quadratically penalized Lasso.
- Minor corrections: Fix math rendering in Figure 1 caption (e.g., n=768 -> $n=768$) and a typo on page 5, line 247.
- Recommendation: Results are strong and the minor issues are easily fixable; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
