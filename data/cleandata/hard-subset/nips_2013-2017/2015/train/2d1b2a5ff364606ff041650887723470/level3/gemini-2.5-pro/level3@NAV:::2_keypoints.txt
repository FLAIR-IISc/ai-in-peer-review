- Main contribution: Precise asymptotic characterization of l2-lasso MSE under single-index (non-linear) measurements, showing equivalence to a linear model with scaled signal and specific Gaussian noise set by the link function.

- Significance/originality: First result extending precise asymptotic l2-lasso analysis from linear to general single-index models, accommodating sparse/group-sparse/low-rank structures and relaxing Gaussian prior assumptions.

- Theoretical grounding: Rigorous technical development building on Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin, and using CGMT; claims are well-supported.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and structured, making complex analysis accessible.

- Application: Theory is usefully demonstrated via an application to optimal quantizer design.

- Weakness — exposition: Core intuition from Brillinger (1982) motivating the equivalence is relegated to Appendix A and should be moved into the main text for better motivation.

- Weakness — practical relevance: The paper should discuss practical choices between l2-lasso and standard l2^2-lasso given that non-linearities manifest as effective noise variance, and whether results theoretically justify preferring l2-lasso.

- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution with minor clarifications and additional practical discussion recommended to enhance impact.
