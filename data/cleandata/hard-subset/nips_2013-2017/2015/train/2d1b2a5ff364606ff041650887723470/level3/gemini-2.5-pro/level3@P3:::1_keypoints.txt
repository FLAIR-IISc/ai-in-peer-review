- Problem: Studies estimation of structured signal from measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0) (single-index model) and analyzes naive use of generalized square-root LASSO on these non-linear measurements.  
- Main contribution: Proves an exact high-dimensional asymptotic equivalence to an effective linear model y_i = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with μ,σ from moments of g, and gives a deterministic convex-program formula for asymptotic MSE.  
- Significance/originality: Provides a precise explanation for why a misspecified linear estimator works, sharpening and generalizing prior results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin).  
- Technical quality: Strong theoretical development (Theorem 2.2), broadly applicable to many convex regularizers (sparsity, group, low-rank) and link functions, with accurate empirical validation.  
- Clarity: Well-written and motivated; effectively connects to prior work and includes a compelling application (optimal quantizer design).  
- Presentation weakness: Manuscript contains numerous typographical errors, especially in appendices/proofs, which undermines confidence and needs careful proofreading.  
- Related-work weakness: May not fully situate results within the wider single-index and sufficient-dimension-reduction literature; authors should ensure comprehensive citation/comparison.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, contingent on correcting typos; paper is novel, important, and of clear interest to the community.
