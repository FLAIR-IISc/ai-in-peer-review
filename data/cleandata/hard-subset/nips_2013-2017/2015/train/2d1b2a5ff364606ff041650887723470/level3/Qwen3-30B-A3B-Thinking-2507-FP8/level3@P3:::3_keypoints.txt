- Main contribution: precise asymptotic error formulas for Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements, extending Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin.  
- Key insight: for Gaussian measurement matrices, yi = g(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to linear measurements yi = μ(ai^T x0) + σ zi with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].  
- Main technical result: Theorem 2.2 characterizes ‖x̂ - μx0‖² as the solution to a convex program involving the Moreau envelope of the regularizer.  
- Technical novelty: extends precise LASSO analysis (Donoho–Maleki–Montanari; Thrampoulidis et al.) to non-linear measurement models.  
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments across Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm cases that support theoretical predictions.  
- Application: q-bit compressive sensing — proves the optimal quantizer is the Lloyd–Max quantizer and links theory to quantizer design.  
- Rigor and clarity: theoretical analysis is rigorous and derivations well-justified; overall clarity is good.  
- Originality and significance: novel explicit error expressions and fundamental understanding of non-linear measurement effects; strong relevance to high-dimensional statistics and signal processing.  
- Positioning: well-situated within NIPS community and recent precise LASSO literature.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novel theory, clear equivalence insight, practical quantizer application, thorough experiments.  
- Weaknesses/minor issues: refers to Generalized LASSO as an “algorithm” instead of a convex program/decoder.  
- Terminology fixes: clarify “proximal function” as the Moreau envelope and formalize Assumption 1 via variational analysis.  
- Presentation/format issues: unbalanced detail due to space, figure caption should render n=768 properly, typo on p.5 line 247, and clarify “regularized” vs “penalized” formulations.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to address formatting and terminology issues.
