- Problem studied: high-dimensional estimation under single-index (nonlinear) measurements y_i = g(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian A and structured x0.  
- Main equivalence: asymptotically, LASSO error with nonlinear measurements equals that under linear model y = μAx0 + σz with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].  
- Precise asymptotics: derive an explicit scalar convex program whose minimizer α* gives the limiting error lim ||x̂ − μx0||2 = α*.  
- Specializations: apply framework to sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and low-rank (nuclear norm) regularizers with matching/extending prior results.  
- Quantization application: for q-bit quantized measurements, threshold/level design reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, yielding Lloyd–Max quantization as optimal for LASSO error.  
- Novelty: first asymptotically precise analysis of LASSO-type estimators under broad nonlinear measurements and regularizers, removing Gaussian-x0 assumptions in prior precise analyses.  
- Technical quality: CGMT-based analysis appears sound; main theorems and scalar program are clearly stated and extend prior precise results.  
- Assumption 1: reasonable for common regularizers (ℓ1, ℓ1,2, nuclear), with concrete F(c1,c2,c3) provided in many cases.  
- Limitations: analysis assumes Gaussian A, μ ≠ 0, and bounded moments; universality to sub-Gaussian A is not addressed.  
- Clarity suggestions: bring the Brillinger-style equivalence from Appendix A into the main text and add a short intuitive sketch.  
- Exposition suggestion: briefly exemplify Assumption 1 (ℓ1, ℓ1,2, nuclear) in the main text so readers can compute F quickly.  
- Practical guidance: discuss l2 (square-root) vs l2^2 LASSO — provide side-by-side error predictions, study sensitivity to σ-misspecification, and propose tuning heuristics using κ*, λcrit.  
- Additional questions: comment on μ = 0 cases and behavior as μ → 0, provide evidence for universality to sub-Gaussian designs, and consider releasing code for reproducibility.  
- Minor fixes: correct abstract typo defining σ^2 and other small typographical errors, and consistently cite square-root LASSO literature (e.g., Belloni et al.).  
- Pros: strong technical contribution, clear equivalence with practical implications (quantization design), relaxed x0 assumptions, good writing and insightful specializations.  
- Cons: reliance on Gaussian A and μ ≠ 0, key equivalence hidden in appendix, and limited practical guidance/comparisons between l2 and l2^2 LASSO.  
- Recommendation: Accept — high-quality theoretical contribution with practical implications; requested clarifications are straightforward.
