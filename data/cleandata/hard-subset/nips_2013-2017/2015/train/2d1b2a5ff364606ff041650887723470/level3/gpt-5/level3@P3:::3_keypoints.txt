- Paper analyzes generalized Lasso under non-linear single-index measurements and shows asymptotic equivalence to a scaled noisy linear model with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2], yielding precise error predictions via a scalar convex program (Theorems 2.1–2.2).  
- Specializations cover ℓ1 (Lasso), group Lasso, and nuclear-norm regularization, recovering and extending known precise analyses for sparse/low-rank recovery.  
- Novel, practical application: q-bit quantizer design reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and stationary points coincide with Lloyd–Max outputs, linking estimation-optimal and MSE-optimal quantization.  
- Strength: technically solid extension of precise error analysis to non-linear single-index models with clear identification of effective linear parameters.  
- Strength: generality across many convex regularizers with interpretable scalar characterizations and strong empirical validation.  
- Strength: convincing experiments that match theoretical predictions across λ and δ and demonstrate the quantizer-design insight.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on Gaussian design; unclear applicability to sub-Gaussian or structured measurement ensembles.  
- Weakness: Key Assumption 1 is stated informally and should be formalized via variational-analysis/epi-convergence or LLN arguments for separable/unitarily invariant regularizers.  
- Weakness: exposition imbalance—proof ideas (e.g., CGMT route) are brief while some technical definitions (like F) are abstract for non-experts.  
- Weakness: minor notational overload and inconsistencies (notably σ vs σ^2) that can confuse readers.  
- Quality: technically sound and careful; theorems are plausible and well-supported by experiments; specializations align with existing literature.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but some key definitions and assumptions are left at a high level and need clearer presentation; figures are informative.  
- Originality: high—provides novel explicit asymptotic error expressions beyond prior bounds and a fresh Lloyd–Max connection for quantizer design.  
- Significance: significant for high-dimensional statistics and compressed sensing—useful for tuning regularization and principled quantizer design across regularizers.  
- Detailed suggestions: avoid calling generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; discuss penalized vs constrained mapping; rename “proximal function” to Moreau envelope; fix σ notation, μ=0 remarks, minor typos/figure caption math and clarify “regularized LASSO” meaning.  
- Acceptance arguments: strong novel theory, broad applicability, solid empirical validation, and a practical, impactful quantizer-design result.  
- Rejection arguments: limited to Gaussian matrices and unclear presentation of Assumption 1/notation for a general audience.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
