- Problem & contribution: Precise asymptotic characterization of the estimation error for Generalized LASSO applied to non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0), treating them as linear.

- Main theorem: In high-dimensional i.i.d. Gaussian designs, ||x̂ − µx0||^2 equals the error from an equivalent linear model yi = µ ai^T x0 + σ zi, with µ,σ determined by g.

- Explicit formula & validation: Provides an explicit (complex) asymptotic error formula (Theorem 2.2) and validates accuracy via simulations for sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery and q-bit quantization.

- Strength — precision: Advances prior work by moving from upper bounds to exact asymptotic error expressions that match simulations.

- Strength — elegance: Shows a clean equivalence between non-linear and specific linear models, quantifying non-linearity by µ and σ.

- Strength — breadth: Analysis covers general convex regularizers and important special cases (ℓ1, group ℓ1,2, nuclear norm) with concrete examples.

- Strength — clarity: Well-written, organized, and clearly states assumptions and results.

- Weakness — missing related work: Fails to engage with the extensive SIM/SDR literature on direction estimation under unknown nonlinear links.

- Weakness — omission of Yi et al. (2015): Does not discuss closely related results that establish equivalence even when µ = 0 and show alternative estimators can succeed where LASSO fails.

- Weakness — Gaussian assumption: Relies on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement vectors without discussing necessity or possible extensions to other ensembles (e.g., sub-Gaussian).

- Weakness — µ/σ dependence: Does not clarify whether dependence on µ and σ reflects fundamental limits or LASSO-specific limitations, especially for µ = 0 cases.

- Weakness — practical guidance: Offers no guidance on choosing the convex regularizer f(x) from knowledge of the signal set K.

- Evaluation — quality: Technically sound with complete, detailed theoretical analysis.

- Evaluation — clarity: Very clear and easy to follow.

- Evaluation — originality: Novel precise characterization but originality tempered by omission of closely related prior work.

- Evaluation — significance: Theoretically interesting and potentially influential for theorists, but practical significance limited by Gaussian assumption and lack of application guidance.
