This paper studies the asymptotic estimation error for linear estimation with arbitrary nonlinear link using generalized LASSO, extending the work of Plan and Vershynin to the case of non-zero mean. The main claim of the paper is that the asymptotic error is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions. The paper provides a precise evaluation of the limit of the squared error of the LASSO estimator under non-linear measurements, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The paper is well-written, and the results are interesting and novel. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem, including a discussion of the relevant literature and a clear explanation of their methodology. The paper also includes several numerical experiments that validate the theoretical results.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. First, the authors assume that the measurement vectors are Gaussian, which may not be realistic in all scenarios. It would be interesting to see how the results generalize to other distributions. Second, the paper does not provide a clear explanation of how to choose the regularizer based on the set K containing x0. This is an important practical question, and the authors should provide more guidance on this issue. Finally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the dependence of the theorem on the parameters µ and σ characterizing link difficulty.

In terms of related work, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction. The authors should also cite the recent work of Yi et al. on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, which addresses sparse recovery even when µ = 0 and shows equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication, pending revisions that address the issues mentioned above.
