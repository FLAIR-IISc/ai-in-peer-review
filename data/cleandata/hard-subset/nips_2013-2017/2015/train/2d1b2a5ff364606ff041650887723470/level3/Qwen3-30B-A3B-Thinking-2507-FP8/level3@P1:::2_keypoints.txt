- Addresses estimation of structured signals from non-linear measurements using the regularized Generalized LASSO.  
- Main theoretical insight: Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a linear model yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with μ, σ determined by the link g.  
- Provides precise asymptotic error expressions for the regularized Generalized LASSO that hold for all regularization values λ.  
- Extends precise LASSO error analysis from linear to non-linear measurements, generalizing prior work (e.g., Brillinger).  
- Shows practical application to q-bit compressive sensing and proves the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal.  
- Uses rigorous proofs via the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework.  
- Numerical experiments (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) strongly support theoretical predictions.  
- Situates contributions relative to prior work (Plan & Vershynin 2015; Thrampoulidis, Oymak & Hassibi 2015) and clarifies novelty.  
- Clarity is good overall, but the crucial equivalence to single-index models is relegated to Appendix A and should be moved earlier to justify a key claim.  
- Originality is strong: first precise error analysis for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements across λ and broad regularizers/link functions.  
- Significance is high due to implications for quantized measurement systems and guidance on regularization/quantization design.  
- Minor concern: paper does not discuss practical implications or guidance on choosing between l2-LASSO and l2^2-LASSO variants.  
- Recommendation: minor presentation tweaks and a brief practical discussion would strengthen an otherwise excellent, NIPS-worthy paper.
