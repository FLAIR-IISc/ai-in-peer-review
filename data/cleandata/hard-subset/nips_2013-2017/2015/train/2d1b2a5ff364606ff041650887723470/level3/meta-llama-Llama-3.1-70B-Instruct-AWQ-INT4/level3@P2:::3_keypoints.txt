- Studies estimation of structured signals from non-linear measurements using the Generalized LASSO.  
- Main theoretical claim: Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to one with appropriately scaled linear noisy measurements under certain conditions.  
- Extends Plan and Vershynin by analyzing the regularized (not just constrained) Generalized LASSO.  
- Derives precise asymptotic error formulas for estimation performance.  
- Provides empirical validation across special cases (LASSO, group LASSO, nuclear norm).  
- Application to q-bit compressive sensing is insightful and well-presented.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and a pleasure to read.  
- Quality rated 9/10; clarity 8.5/10; originality 9/10; significance 9/10.  
- Suggestion: expand some sections to provide more detail.  
- Suggestion: give a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using variational analysis.  
- Suggestion: change Moreau envelope notation to avoid confusion with the proximal operator.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel explicit error expressions, clear proofs, strong empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: some sections lack detail, notation confusion, and Assumption 1 needs stronger formal grounding.
