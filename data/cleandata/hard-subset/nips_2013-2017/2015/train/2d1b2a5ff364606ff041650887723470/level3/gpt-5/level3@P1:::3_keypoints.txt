- Main theoretical contribution: proves an exact asymptotic equivalence between nonlinear single-index measurements and an effective linear model for penalized generalized LASSO, plus a scalar convex program giving the exact asymptotic squared error.
- Key formula: error depends on measurements only through μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2], reducing nonlinear analysis to a linearized model.
- Specializations: derives concrete results for ℓ1 (sparse), ℓ1,2 (group-sparse), and nuclear-norm (low-rank) regularizers, recovering and extending prior linear-case LASSO analyses.
- Quantizer application: shows minimizing estimator error is equivalent to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 over quantizer parameters and connects this to Lloyd–Max stationary points.
- Relation to prior work: advances Brillinger’s low-dim consistency and Plan–Vershynin’s non-asymptotic bounds by giving exact asymptotic equivalence and precise error characterizations using CGMT/AMP-style techniques.
- Technical approach: solid use of existing precise LASSO analysis tools (CGMT/AMP families) extended to broader regularizers and nonlinear links.
- Empirical support: simulations closely match theory, including two-regime behavior in compressed settings and quantizer-design experiments.
- Assumptions and caveats: relies on i.i.d. Gaussian design, requires μ ≠ 0 (excludes even/degenerate links), and uses an abstract Assumption 1 on the variational limit F that is somewhat hard to verify.
- Clarity and exposition: generally well-written and organized, but the general-theorem section is dense and some mappings (penalized vs constrained) and technical assumptions need more explanation.
- Practical guidance needed: paper should give more concrete recipes for computing F beyond canonical cases and clearer guidance on using Theorem 2.2 in practice.
- Terminology/notation issues: avoid calling generalized LASSO an “algorithm”; reserve “prox” for proximal operator and rename Moreau envelope; fix minor typos and notation inconsistencies.
- Quantizer discussion: highlight Lloyd–Max yields stationary points for σ^2/μ^2 and discuss conditions for local/global optimality and sensitivity to λ and δ.
- Edge-case discussion: recommend adding brief analysis or guidance for μ near zero or zero and possible alternative decoders in those cases.
- Suggested experiments: include mildly non-Gaussian/sub-Gaussian designs to test robustness and provide code/parameters for reproducibility.
- Pros for acceptance: strong, novel theoretical contribution; broad applicability across convex regularizers; clear practical implications; convincing empirical validation.
- Cons for acceptance: Gaussian-design limitation, abstract Assumption 1, and exposition could better guide practitioners.
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
