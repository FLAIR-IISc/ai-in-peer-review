- Main result: Shows the Generalized LASSO's asymptotic estimation error under yi = g(ai^T x0) equals that under linear measurements yi = μai^T x0 + σzi with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].

- Novelty/extension: Extends Plan & Vershynin by providing precise asymptotic error expressions (not just upper bounds) for structured, regularized estimation.

- Theoretical generalization: Generalizes Brillinger’s classical result to regularized estimation with structured signals.

- Practical application: Demonstrates for q-bit compressive sensing that the optimal quantizer is the Lloyd–Max quantizer.

- Empirical validation: Clear figures validate theoretical predictions across sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signals.

- Presentation quality: Well-written and well-organized, making complex technical content accessible.

- Technical soundness: Precise asymptotic analysis is solid and thorough.

- Originality caveat: Strong originality in precise error characterization, but core equivalence builds on existing Plan & Vershynin work.

- Significance: Results have both theoretical import and practical impact (e.g., optimal quantization design).

- Major limitation: Assumes Gaussian measurement matrix A without sufficient justification for necessity of this assumption.

- Missing guidance: Does not provide actionable guidance on choosing the regularizer or constraint set K based on x0’s structure.

- Incomplete analysis: Heavy reliance on μ and σ parameters without fully analyzing necessity or possible simplifications under special cases.

- Missing citations: Fails to cite single-index model literature (e.g., Li & Duan 1985; Ichimura 1993).

- Missing related work: Omits discussion of Yi et al. (2015), which addresses sparse recovery with non-linear measurements when μ = 0 and is a direct precursor.

- Recommendation summary: Strong candidate for acceptance given precise theory and practical application, contingent on addressing missing citations and justifying the Gaussian assumption.
