- Main result: Proves exact asymptotic equivalence—generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements behaves like a linear model with effective parameters μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2]; Theorem 2.2 gives a scalar optimization for asymptotic MSE and corollaries cover sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank cases, plus a Lloyd–Max quantizer-stationarity observation.  
- Relation to prior work: Generalizes Brillinger’s fixed-dimension result, sharpens Plan–Vershynin’s bounds by treating regularized LASSO with exact asymptotics, and leverages CGMT similar to precise LASSO analyses.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous CGMT-based derivations, careful specialization to common regularizers, verified Assumption 1 for key norms, and simulations closely match theory.  
- Gaussian-design dependence: Reviewer requests discussion of whether Gaussianity is essential and whether results extend to rotationally invariant ensembles, sub-Gaussian designs (universality), or general isotropic matrices; empirical tests beyond Gaussian encouraged.  
- Role of μ and σ: Asks for an argument or reference why only μ and σ can influence asymptotic error under Gaussian A, and explicit treatment of the μ = 0 case (does LASSO fail to recover direction and can alternatives help).  
- Regularizer selection guidance: Notes lack of practical guidance for choosing f given a known set K—recommend discussing gauge/atomic norms, descent cone/statistical dimension tradeoffs, and constrained vs surrogate regularizers’ effects on F.  
- Tuning λ: Requests a practical tuning rule (e.g., estimate μ,σ from data and pick λ minimizing limiting error) and comments on sensitivity to μ,σ misspecification when g is unknown.  
- Clarity improvements: Suggest providing intuition for Assumption 1 and F, collecting explicit F for main regularizers, explaining the two regimes around λcrit and their phase-transition relation, and clarifying scope/optimality of the Lloyd–Max claim.  
- Originality: Novel exact equivalence and CGMT-based error characterization; quantizer-design angle adds practical impact; recommends discussing related works like Yi et al. (2015).  
- Significance: Provides sharp conceptual and practical insight into when LASSO “ignores” nonlinearity (via μ,σ); impact would increase if universality beyond Gaussian is explored.  
- Requested citations/comparisons: Add Sliced Inverse Regression (Li, 1991), Cook & Weisberg SDR literature, and compare in detail to Yi, Wang, Caramanis, Liu (2015).  
- Pros summary: Technically strong, clean μ/σ equivalence, broad structural specializations, and a useful quantizer-design implication.  
- Cons summary: Strong reliance on Gaussian designs, limited advice on choosing f and tuning λ, insufficient treatment of μ = 0 regime, and missing relevant citations/comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept; ask authors to address Gaussian-design scope, add guidance on regularizer/tuning selection, discuss μ = 0 alternatives, and incorporate the missing literature to strengthen the submission.
