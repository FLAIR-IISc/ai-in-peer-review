- Problem setup: analyzes square-root LASSO for structured signal recovery from non-linear measurements yi = g(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian a_i in high-dimensional asymptotics (m,n→∞, m/n fixed).
- Central result: asymptotic MSE of LASSO with non-linear measurements equals the MSE from an equivalent linear model yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i.
- μ and σ are determined solely by the first and second moments of g evaluated on a standard Gaussian.
- Extends classical Brillinger linearization and Plan–Vershynin bounds to provide asymptotically exact error formulas for regularized LASSO.
- Provides deterministic, low-dimensional convex optimization characterizations of the asymptotic MSE.
- Numerical simulations show theoretical predictions are remarkably accurate.
- Broad applicability to many convex regularizers and examples: sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery, and optimal quantizer design.
- High technical quality: rigorous derivations connecting to AMP/CGMT-level precision.
- Writing and structure are clear; main theorems and implications are well presented.
- Main weakness: numerous typographical errors, especially in appendices/proofs, which impede verification.
- Reviewer note: reviewer not expert in single-index/sufficient-dimension-reduction literature, so some prior work may be missing.
- Recommendation: strong acceptance conditional on thorough proofreading to correct typos.
