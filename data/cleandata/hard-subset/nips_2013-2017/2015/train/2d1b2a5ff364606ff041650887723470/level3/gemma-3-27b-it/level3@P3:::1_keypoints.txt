- Problem: Analyzes Generalized LASSO performance for estimating structured signals from non-linear measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian a_i and unknown link functions g_i.  
- Main contribution: Provides asymptotically exact expressions for estimation error and shows equivalence to scaled, noisy linear measurements characterized by µ and σ^2.  
- Theoretical foundation: Builds on Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, improving prior upper bounds to precise error characterizations.  
- Methods: Leverages recent precise LASSO analyses and tools like the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT).  
- Application: Applies results to q-bit compressive sensing and derives optimal quantization levels via a connection to the Lloyd–Max algorithm.  
- Strength — technical soundness: Analysis appears rigorous under Gaussian and large-sample assumptions, yielding explicit asymptotic errors.  
- Strength — originality: Novel precise error formulas for non-linear measurements and the equivalence insight are original contributions.  
- Strength — significance: Results are potentially impactful for practical non-linear measurement scenarios (e.g., sensor nonlinearity, 1-bit CS).  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is generally well-written, organized, and effectively motivated.  
- Weakness — typos/proofs: Numerous typos, especially in proofs, undermine readability and confidence in technical details.  
- Weakness — related work: Insufficient engagement with single-index and sufficient dimension reduction literature (e.g., Hastie & Tibshirani 1990, Li 1991).  
- Weakness — assumptions: The unit-norm assumption for x0 is presented as simplification but needs clearer discussion of practical implications and generalization.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, technically solid, significant results with clear presentation warrant acceptance.  
- Rejection/concern arguments: Proof typos, incomplete related-work discussion, and unclear x0-norm assumptions require addressing.  
- Required revisions: Thorough proofreading of proofs, expanded discussion of single-index literature and connections, and clarification of the x0 unit-norm assumption.
