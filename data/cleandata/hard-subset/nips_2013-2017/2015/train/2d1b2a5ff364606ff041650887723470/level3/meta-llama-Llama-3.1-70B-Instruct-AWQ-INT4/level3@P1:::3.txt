**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements" presents a study on the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. The authors show that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear ones and derive precise asymptotic error formulas building on the work of Plan and Vershynin. The paper provides empirical validation across special cases, including Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm, and presents an insightful application to q-bit Compressive Sensing (CS) quantizer design and its relation to the Lloyd-Max algorithm.

**Strengths**

1. **Quality**: The paper is a solid, well-done piece of work that provides a significant contribution to the field of compressed sensing and signal processing.
2. **Originality**: The authors derive novel explicit expressions for estimation error, extending prior work in the field.
3. **Insightful application**: The paper presents a practical application of the theoretical results to q-bit CS quantizer design, which is a significant contribution to the field.
