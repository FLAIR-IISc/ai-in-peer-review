- Paper derives explicit asymptotic estimation error for Generalized LASSO with nonlinear link functions, showing equivalence to a scaled linear model.
- Extends prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) to a more general setting and broader class of regularizers and link functions.
- Mathematical derivations appear rigorous with clear statement of assumptions.
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and includes illustrative figures that validate theory.
- Practical implications discussed, including q-bit compressive sensing and Lloyd–Max quantizer design.
- Main novelty: precise equivalence between nonlinear and scaled linear measurement models for Generalized LASSO.
- Strength: theoretical rigor grounded in high-dimensional statistics techniques.
- Strength: results apply to a wide class of regularizers and link functions, increasing applicability.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement vectors without sufficient justification or discussion of non-Gaussian ensembles.
- Weakness: does not address how to select the appropriate regularizer for a given signal class (practical model selection).
- Weakness: lacks rigorous proof of the necessity of parameters µ and σ; more justification or evidence is needed.
- Weakness: missing citations to relevant literature on single-index models, sufficient dimension reduction (e.g., Li 1991), and Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, clear presentation, and practical relevance; advances understanding of LASSO robustness.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited generality due to Gaussian assumption, incomplete discussion on regularizer selection and µ/σ necessity, and omitted related work.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address Gaussian assumption limitations, add discussion on regularizer selection and µ/σ necessity, and incorporate the missing citations.
