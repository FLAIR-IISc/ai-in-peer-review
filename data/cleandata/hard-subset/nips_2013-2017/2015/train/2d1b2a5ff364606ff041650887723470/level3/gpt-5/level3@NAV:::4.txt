Summary. The paper analyzes generalized LASSO under nonlinear single-index measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian measurement vectors. Its main result (Thm 2.1) shows an asymptotic equivalence: the squared error of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements equals that of a corresponding linear model with effective gain μ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2], extending Brillinger’s classical LS analysis and the upper bounds of Plan–Vershynin to precise, high-dimensional asymptotics for broad convex regularizers. A scalar convex program (Thm 2.2) provides exact limiting risk; corollaries cover sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery, and an application to quantized sensing links optimal quantizer design to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 (recovering Lloyd–Max).

Relation to prior work. This connects to precise LASSO risk via AMP and CGMT (Bayati–Montanari; Stojnic; Oymak–Thrampoulidis–Hassibi) and to the single-index/Sufficient Dimension Reduction literature (e.g., Ichimura; Li & Duan). A closely related line is Yi et al. (2015), who study optimal linear estimation under unknown link, including sparse recovery in the μ = 0 regime and an equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation.

Strengths.
- Technically strong and clean reduction; sharp, interpretable scalar characterization.
- Broad coverage of convex regularizers; concrete corollaries beyond ℓ1.
- Insightful design implication for quantization.
- Clear writing and useful figures.

Weaknesses and questions.
- Gaussian A. Please clarify whether rotational invariance is essential (CGMT/goodness-of-fit) or merely technical. Can the results extend to i.i.d. sub-Gaussian, isotropic log-concave, or designs with mild correlations (universality)? Any counterexamples?
- Choice of regularizer. The analysis assumes a convex f; how should practitioners pick f given a feasible set K (as in Plan–Vershynin)? Can F be related to statistical dimension/Gaussian width to guide f and λ selection?
- Dependence on μ and σ. Are these dependences information-theoretically necessary (e.g., lower bounds) or artifacts of analysis? Can one estimate μ,σ from data and retain the same asymptotics (plug-in robustness)?
- μ = 0 regime. The current assumptions exclude it; discuss limitations and connections to 1-bit CS and to Yi et al. (who show recovery even when μ = 0).
- Assumption 1 and F. Provide more guidance/examples (especially for nuclear norm) and computational recipes.
- Empirics. Add non-Gaussian designs and unknown-link cases; report sensitivity to λ mis-specification.

Missing citations. Broaden related work on single-index and SDR (e.g., Li’s SIR, Hristache et al.) and cite Yi et al. (2015) and connect/contrast results.

Evaluation.
- Quality: High; proofs appear careful; results are precise.
- Clarity: Generally clear; add guidance on Assumption 1/F and universality.
- Originality: Strong; first precise asymptotics for generalized LASSO with nonlinear links.
- Significance: High; unifies nonlinear sensing with linear LASSO risk; practical design implications.

Pros for acceptance: novel and sharp theory; broad applicability; clear exposition; impactful insights. Cons: reliance on Gaussianity; limited discussion of μ = 0 and universality; missing related work/context.

Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on addressing the above clarifications and citations.
