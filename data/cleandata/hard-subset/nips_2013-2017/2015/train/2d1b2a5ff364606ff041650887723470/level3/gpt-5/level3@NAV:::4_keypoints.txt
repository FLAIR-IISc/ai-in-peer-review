- Paper analyzes generalized LASSO with nonlinear single-index measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) (Gaussian ai) and shows asymptotic equivalence to a linear model with effective gain μ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Theorem 2.1 gives the squared-error equivalence; Theorem 2.2 reduces limiting risk to a scalar convex program; corollaries treat sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery and optimal quantizer design.  
- Places work within AMP/CGMT precise LASSO risk literature and single-index/SDR literature; closely related to Yi et al. (2015).  
- Strength: technically strong, clean reduction with a sharp, interpretable scalar characterization.  
- Strength: broad coverage of convex regularizers and concrete corollaries beyond ℓ1.  
- Strength: useful design insight for quantization (minimizing σ^2/μ^2, recovers Lloyd–Max).  
- Strength: clear writing and informative figures.  
- Weakness: reliance on Gaussian measurement matrix A — need clarification on whether rotational invariance is essential or results admit universality to sub-Gaussian/isotropic/log-concave or correlated designs.  
- Weakness: lack of practical guidance for choosing regularizer f given a feasible set K; request relation to statistical dimension/Gaussian width and λ selection.  
- Weakness: unclear if dependence on μ and σ is information-theoretically necessary or an artifact; ask whether μ,σ can be estimated from data with plug-in asymptotics.  
- Weakness: current analysis excludes the μ = 0 regime; needs discussion and connection to 1-bit compressed sensing and Yi et al.’s results showing recovery when μ = 0.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 and the function F need more examples and guidance (especially for nuclear norm) plus computational recipes.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation should add non-Gaussian designs, unknown-link scenarios, and sensitivity analysis to λ mis-specification.  
- Missing citations: broaden related work on single-index/SDR (e.g., Li’s SIR, Hristache et al.) and explicitly cite/contrast Yi et al. (2015).  
- Evaluation/recommendation: high-quality, original, and significant work with precise asymptotics; recommend weak accept contingent on addressing Gaussianity, μ=0, universality, Assumption 1/F guidance, and related-work gaps.
