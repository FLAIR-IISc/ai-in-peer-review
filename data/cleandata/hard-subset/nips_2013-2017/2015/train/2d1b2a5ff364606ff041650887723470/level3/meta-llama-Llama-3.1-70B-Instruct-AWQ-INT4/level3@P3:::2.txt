This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of signal processing and machine learning by extending the precise asymptotic characterization of the l2-lasso (square-root lasso) to single-index models and relaxing the usual Gaussian prior assumption on x0. The authors demonstrate that the l2-lasso treats non-linear measurements exactly as if they were scaled and noisy linear measurements with a scaling factor µ and noise variance σ2.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, including a discussion of relevant literature and the main contributions of the paper. The technical sections are also well-organized and easy to follow.

The main result of the paper is Theorem 2.1, which relates the error of the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements to the error of the same algorithm when used under appropriately scaled noisy linear measurements. The authors also derive an asymptotically exact expression for the error in Theorem 2.2, which is a significant improvement over previous results.

The paper also provides several examples and applications of the main result, including sparse recovery, group-sparse recovery, and low-rank matrix recovery. These examples demonstrate the practical relevance of the paper's results and provide insight into the behavior of the l2-lasso in different scenarios.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it assumes a unit Euclidean norm for x0, which may not be realistic in all scenarios. However, the authors provide a clear justification for this assumption and note that it is mostly for simplicity of presentation.

Another potential weakness is that the paper does not provide a clear comparison with other existing methods, such as the l2^2-lasso. However, the authors do note that the l2-lasso's oracle regularization parameter does not depend on noise variance, which may make it a more attractive option in certain scenarios.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of signal processing and machine learning, and the results are well-supported by theoretical analysis. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the authors provide several examples and applications of the main result.
