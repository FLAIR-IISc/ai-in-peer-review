

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO when applied to non-linear measurement models of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), extending the work of Plan & Vershynin (2013) to cases where the scaling parameter μ = E[γg(γ)] ≠ 0. The main contribution is Theorem 2.1, which establishes that the asymptotic estimation error for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is identical to that of a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled and noisy measurements (yi = μai^T x0 + σzi, where σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2]). The paper further provides precise error formulas in Theorem 2.2, which generalizes previous results on linear measurements to the non-linear setting.

The paper builds upon the classical work of Brillinger (1980) on linear regression with non-linear link functions and the recent work of Plan & Vershynin (2013) on structured signal recovery with non-linear measurements. It extends these results by providing precise asymptotic error expressions rather than loose upper bounds, and by considering the general case where μ ≠ 0. The authors demonstrate the applicability of their results to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signal recovery, as well as to q-bit compressive sensing.

**Strengths:**
- The paper makes a technically sound contribution by extending Plan & Vershynin's results to the general case with μ ≠ 0.
- The main result (Theorem 2.1) is well-motivated and presented with clear intuition.
- The error formula in Theorem 2.2 is precise and general, applicable to various structured signal recovery problems.
- The paper is well-written, with clear organization and effective illustrations of the theoretical results.
- The application to q-bit compressive sensing (Section 2.3) demonstrates the practical relevance of the theoretical results.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper assumes Gaussian measurement vectors without justification for why this assumption is necessary. While Gaussian measurements are common in high-dimensional statistics, the authors should address whether the results extend to other measurement ensembles or if the Gaussian assumption is critical to their analysis.
- The paper discusses convex regularizers but fails to address the important practical question of how to choose the regularizer based on the set K containing x0, which is a fundamental aspect of structured signal recovery.
- The dependence on μ and σ characterizing the link function is established in the paper, but the authors do not address whether these dependencies are necessary or if they arise from the analysis framework.
- The paper misses important citations to literature on single-index models (e.g., Li & Duan, 1985; Semiparametric Least Squares) and sufficient dimension reduction, which study linear/subspace estimation under unknown link functions.
- The paper omits recent work by Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, which addresses sparse recovery even when μ = 0 and shows equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with well-supported theoretical claims. The proofs are deferred to the appendix, and the main results are presented with clear mathematical rigor. The authors are careful to note the limitations of their assumptions (e.g., the unit-norm assumption for x0).

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written with clear organization. The main results are presented with appropriate mathematical precision, and the connections to previous work are well-explained. The figures effectively illustrate the accuracy of the theoretical predictions.

**Originality:** This work extends Plan & Vershynin's results to the general case with μ ≠ 0, providing the first precise asymptotic error analysis for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. While the general equivalence principle is not entirely novel, the precise error expressions and the extension to μ ≠ 0 are significant contributions.

**Significance:** The paper addresses an important practical problem (estimation with nonlinear measurements) with theoretical rigor. The finding that non-linear measurements can be treated as scaled and noisy linear measurements has important implications for the design of measurement systems and recovery algorithms. The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrating the optimality of the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly noteworthy.

**Pro and Con Arguments for Acceptance:**
*Pro:* The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, extending previous work with precise error expressions. The results are well-motivated, technically sound, and have clear practical implications.

*Con:* The paper misses important citations to relevant literature on single-index models and recent work by Yi et al. The lack of discussion on regularizer selection based on the structure of x0 is a notable gap. The Gaussian measurement assumption requires stronger justification.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. With minor revisions to address the missing citations and the regularizer selection question, it would be an excellent contribution to NIPS. I recommend acceptance with the understanding that the authors should address these concerns in the camera-ready version.
