- Problem: Analyzes estimation of structured high-dimensional signal x0 from single-index non-linear measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) using the standard l2 (square-root) LASSO.  
- Main result: Shows asymptotically the l2-LASSO MSE on the non-linear model equals its MSE on an equivalent linear model y_i = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with μ and σ determined by g.  
- Novelty: Provides a simple, fundamental equivalence that generalizes Brillinger (1982) and sharpens recent bounds by Plan & Vershynin (2015) to exact asymptotic formulas.  
- Technical approach: Uses the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework to obtain rigorous, precise asymptotic MSE characterizations.  
- Extension: Relaxes Gaussianity assumptions on non-zero signal entries, allowing more general signal distributions.  
- Empirical support: Simulations across sparse and group-sparse settings and different signal distributions match theoretical predictions closely.  
- Application: Demonstrates utility by reducing optimal quantizer design to minimizing the σ^2/μ^2 ratio.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, with explicit assumptions and illustrative figures.  
- Motivation clarity: Suggests moving Brillinger’s motivating discussion into the introduction to better justify the central claim earlier.  
- Practical implications missing: Should discuss how the equivalence favors l2 (square-root) LASSO over l2^2-LASSO given unknown effective noise σ^2 from the link g.  
- Assumptions caveat: Results assume a random signal drawn from a distribution (RMT setting) rather than deterministic arbitrary signals—this distinction should be acknowledged.  
- Overall assessment: Technically strong, significant contribution with strong empirical support; minor clarifications would improve practical impact; strong recommendation for acceptance.
