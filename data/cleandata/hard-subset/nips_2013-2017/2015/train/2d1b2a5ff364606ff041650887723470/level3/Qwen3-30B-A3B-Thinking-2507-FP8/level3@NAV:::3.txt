

This paper makes a significant contribution to understanding the behavior of the generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements. The authors extend the classical work of Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin by establishing precise asymptotic error formulas when measurements take the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), where g is a non-linear link function. Their key insight shows that non-linear measurements can be treated as noisy linear measurements with scaling factor μ = E[γg(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2], where γ ~ N(0,1). The paper derives explicit expressions for the estimation error ‖x̂ - μx0‖2 that hold for all values of the regularization parameter λ and for a wide class of regularizers, including sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery scenarios.

The work is technically sound with solid theoretical results that build upon and significantly extend prior work. The empirical validation across multiple special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm) and the insightful application to q-bit compressive sensing quantizer design (showing the optimal quantizer is the Lloyd-Max quantizer) demonstrate strong significance. The paper's originality is clear through its novel explicit expressions for estimation error, which represent the first precise results in this context.

While the paper is well-written overall, there are some clarity issues that should be addressed: the caption for Figure 1 should render 'n=768' as $n=768$; there's a typo on page 5, line 247; the "proximal function" should be correctly identified as the Moreau envelope; Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal variational analysis interpretation; and the generalized Lasso should be referred to as a convex program rather than an algorithm. The authors should also clarify the equivalence between regularized and penalized formulations.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to high-dimensional statistics and compressed sensing, with results that advance both theory and practice. The precise error analysis provides a foundation for understanding and optimizing LASSO-based recovery under non-linear measurements, with the quantizer design application being particularly compelling. The work meets all quality criteria and represents a significant advancement over previous non-asymptotic results.
