- Extends precise asymptotic characterization of the l2-LASSO (square-root LASSO) to single-index models with non-linear measurements, relaxing Gaussian prior on x0.  
- Shows non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) can be asymptotically treated as scaled noisy linear measurements yi ≈ μ ai^T x0 + σ zi with μ, σ determined by g.  
- Upgrades prior loose upper bounds (Plan & Vershynin 2015) to precise error predictions and explicit estimation-error formulas.  
- Provides error expressions that hold for all regularization parameters λ and for multiple structured signals (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).  
- Claims to be the first precise asymptotic analysis for this non-linear measurement setting.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and thoroughly treats theoretical foundations.  
- Effectively builds on recent precise LASSO analyses using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework.  
- Main-text clarity issue: the key equivalence to scaled linear models (Brillinger 1982) is only in Appendix A but referenced in the main text and should be moved up.  
- Practical relevance needs more discussion—specifically whether results favor l2-LASSO over l2^2-LASSO in practice given oracle λ independence from noise variance.  
- Strong technical quality with rigorous proofs and extensive experimental validation.  
- High significance due to unified theoretical framework and direct implications for designing optimal quantizers (q-bit compressive sensing application).  
- Original contribution: precise characterization of error under non-linear measurements significantly extends prior work; overall a high-quality advancement in high-dimensional statistics.
