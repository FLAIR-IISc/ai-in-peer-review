- Significant theoretical contribution: asymptotic equivalence between non-linear measurements and scaled, noisy linear measurements.  
- Core idea well-motivated by prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) and rigorously extended to give precise error characterizations.  
- Builds on recent advances in LASSO analysis and successfully generalizes them to the non-linear setting.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and mathematically sound.  
- Illustrative figures effectively aid understanding.  
- Problem setting (structured signal estimation from non-linear measurements) is relevant to applications like quantized compressive sensing.  
- Crucial equivalence to Brillinger’s single-index model is only shown in Appendix A and should be briefly stated in the main text.  
- Recommend adding a brief sentence in the main text to reference Appendix A’s recasting of the single-index model as a linear model with scaled signals and non-standard noise.  
- Lacks discussion of practical algorithm selection implications, particularly whether l2-lasso should be preferred over l2^2-lasso given oracle regularization independence from noise variance.  
- Suggest adding discussion or simulations comparing l2-lasso and l2^2-lasso in finite-sample, non-linear measurement settings.  
- Theoretical focus on asymptotics may not fully address finite-sample noise-variance dependence in practice.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical foundation, novel extension to non-linear measurements, clarity, relevance, and clear connections to existing literature.  
- Weaknesses: minor clarity omission regarding Brillinger equivalence and limited practical relevance discussion/comparison to alternatives.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant advance, rigorous results, clear writing, meaningful extension of prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of practical implications and the minor clarity issue could hinder understanding.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify Brillinger connection and address practical implications.
