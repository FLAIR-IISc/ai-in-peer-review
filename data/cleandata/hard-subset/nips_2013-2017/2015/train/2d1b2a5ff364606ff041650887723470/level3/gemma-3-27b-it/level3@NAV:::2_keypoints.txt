- Investigates Generalized LASSO for non-linear measurements, building on Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin.  
- Main contribution: provides precise asymptotic characterization of estimation error for non-linear measurements.  
- Demonstrates equivalence of non-linear measurement problem to a scaled and noisy linear problem.  
- Practical consequence: justifies using the Lloyd-Max quantizer for optimal q-bit compressive sensing performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and logically presented.  
- Mathematical derivations appear rigorous and properly grounded in prior work.  
- Appropriately connects to literature in statistics, econometrics, and signal processing.  
- Numerical results (Figures 1 & 2) effectively support theoretical claims.  
- Nice application-level discussion linking theory to Lloyd-Max quantization.  
- Crucial equivalence to a linear model (Brillinger 1982) is relegated to Appendix A rather than highlighted in the main text.  
- Recommend mentioning the single-index-to-linear equivalence in the main text (around line 140) to improve clarity and accessibility.  
- Paper does not address whether l2-lasso (square-root) is preferable to l2^2-lasso in practice; a discussion or simulation would strengthen practical relevance.  
- Strong theoretical contribution and sound methodology; overall recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
