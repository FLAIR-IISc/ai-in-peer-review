- Studies asymptotic estimation error for structured signal recovery using generalized LASSO with arbitrary nonlinear link functions.  
- Builds on Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, extending prior analyses to provide precise asymptotic characterizations rather than only upper bounds.  
- Central claim: with i.i.d. Gaussian measurements, LASSO performance under a nonlinear link is asymptotically identical to an equivalent linear model yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with μ, σ determined by the link.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a precise, deterministic formula for the asymptotic error—strong advancement over loose bounds.  
- Requires clarification on the Gaussian measurement assumption and whether results extend to sub-Gaussian ensembles.  
- Lacks practical guidance on choosing the optimal convex regularizer given the structure set K containing x0.  
- Needs discussion on whether dependence on μ and σ is fundamental or an artifact of the analysis.  
- Missing key citations and connection to literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction.  
- Overlooks relevant prior work by Yi et al. (2015) that also establishes linear–nonlinear equivalence and addresses the µ = 0 case; must clearly differentiate contributions.  
- Overall novelty is questionable in light of existing literature; major revisions required to address concerns and contextualize contributions.
