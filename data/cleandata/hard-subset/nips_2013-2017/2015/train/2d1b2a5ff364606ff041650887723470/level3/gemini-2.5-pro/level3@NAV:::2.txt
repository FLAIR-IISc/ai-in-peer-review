This paper studies the problem of estimating a structured signal from non-linear measurements, a setup known as the single-index model. The authors analyze the performance of the Generalized LASSO (or l2-lasso), which naively treats the non-linear measurements as if they were linear. Building on the classical work of Brillinger and more recent results by Plan and Vershynin, the paper's main contribution is a precise asymptotic characterization of the mean-squared error. The key finding is that, in the high-dimensional limit, the l2-lasso's performance on a non-linear problem is equivalent to its performance on a linear problem with a scaled signal and specific additive Gaussian noise, where the scaling and noise variance are determined by the non-linear link function.

**Strengths:**
*   **Originality and Significance:** The paper provides a significant theoretical contribution by extending the precise asymptotic characterization of the l2-lasso from linear models to the more general single-index models. This is, to my knowledge, the first result of its kind and deepens our understanding of why simple linear estimators work surprisingly well for certain non-linear problems. The framework is general, accommodating various signal structures (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and relaxing the common Gaussian prior assumption on the signal.
*   **Quality:** The technical work appears sound, connecting to and rigorously extending established lines of research based on techniques like the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT). The claims are well-supported by the provided theoretical analysis, and the application to optimal quantizer design is a compelling demonstration of the theory's utility.
*   **Clarity:** Overall, the paper is well-written and structured, making a complex topic accessible.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**
*   **Clarity:** The core intuition, stemming from Brillinger's (1982) work on the equivalence between the single-index model and a linear model with non-standard noise, is crucial for motivating the analysis. This point is currently confined to Appendix A. Mentioning it in the main text would provide valuable context for the problem setup and better justify the approach.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The paper could strengthen its practical impact by discussing the choice between the l2-lasso and the standard l2^2-lasso. A known advantage of the l2-lasso is that its oracle regularization parameter is independent of noise variance. Since this work shows that non-linearities manifest as an effective noise variance σ², the authors should address whether their results provide a theoretical basis for preferring the l2-lasso in these practical settings. This would be a valuable insight for practitioners.

In summary, this is a strong theoretical paper with novel and significant results. Addressing the minor points on clarity and practical implications would further enhance its impact.
