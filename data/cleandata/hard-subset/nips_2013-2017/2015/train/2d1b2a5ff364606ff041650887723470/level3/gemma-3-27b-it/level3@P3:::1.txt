This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO for estimating structured signals from measurements corrupted by non-linear transformations. Specifically, it provides asymptotically precise expressions for the estimation error when the measurements are of the form *y<sub>i</sub>* = *g<sub>i</sub>*( *a<sub>i</sub><sup>T</sup>x<sub>0</sub>*), where *a<sub>i</sub>* are rows of a Gaussian measurement matrix and *g<sub>i</sub>* are independent, potentially unknown, link functions. The key contribution lies in demonstrating that, asymptotically, the performance of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that with scaled and noisy linear measurements, characterized by a proportionality constant *µ* and noise variance *σ<sup>2</sup>*.

The work builds upon Brillinger’s earlier findings regarding consistent estimation with non-linear measurements and extends the more recent results of Plan and Vershynin, who established upper bounds on the performance of a constrained LASSO.  This paper significantly improves upon those bounds by providing *precise* error characterizations. It connects to a broader literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, although the authors acknowledge potential gaps in their awareness of this field (which I will address below).  Furthermore, the work leverages and generalizes recent advances in the precise analysis of the LASSO with linear measurements, utilizing tools like the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT). The application to quantized measurements (q-bit compressive sensing) and the derivation of optimal quantization levels via a connection to the Lloyd-Max algorithm are interesting and demonstrate the practical implications of the theoretical results.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper appears technically rigorous, deriving explicit asymptotic expressions for the estimation error. The use of asymptotic analysis and appropriate modeling assumptions (Gaussian measurements, large-sample limits) provides a solid theoretical foundation.
*   **Originality:** The derivation of precise error expressions for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is a novel contribution. The demonstration of equivalence to a linear model with scaled and noisy measurements is a valuable insight. The application to q-bit compressive sensing and the optimality result concerning the Lloyd-Max quantizer are also original.
*   **Significance:**  The results are potentially significant for applications where measurements are inherently non-linear (e.g., sensors with non-linear response curves, 1-bit compressive sensing). Providing a precise understanding of the performance limits in these scenarios is crucial for algorithm design and parameter tuning. The work advances the state-of-the-art by moving beyond upper bounds to precise characterizations.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The notation is mostly clear, and the derivations are presented in a logical manner. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and summarizes the contributions.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Typos and Proofs:** The authors themselves acknowledge numerous typos, particularly in the proofs. This detracts from the readability and confidence in the technical details. A careful proofreading is essential.
*   **Related Work – Single-Index Models:** The authors briefly mention the single-index model literature but seem somewhat dismissive of its relevance. This is a potentially significant omission. A more thorough review of this literature would be beneficial. While the paper focuses on a regularized estimation framework, much of the single-index model work deals with *identification* of the index function, which is related to the constant of proportionality *µ* in this work. Exploring these connections more deeply could strengthen the paper. Relevant works include Hastie & Tibshirani (1990, *Journal of the Royal Statistical Society Series B*) and Li (1991, *The Annals of Statistics*).
* **Assumptions:** The assumption of unit Euclidean norm for *x<sub>0</sub>* is stated as a simplification, but it's impact could be better discussed. While the authors state that the results can be generalized, the practical implications of this assumption need to be clarified.



**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   Novel and technically sound results providing precise error characterization for a challenging problem.
*   Demonstration of a fundamental equivalence between non-linear and scaled linear measurement models.
*   Potential for significant impact in applications involving non-linear measurements.
*   Generally clear and well-organized presentation.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   Numerous typos in the proofs raise concerns about the overall rigor.
*   Insufficient engagement with the single-index model literature.
*   The unit norm assumption on x0 should be discussed more thoroughly.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the presence of typos and a potentially incomplete consideration of related work in single-index models, this is a strong paper with significant theoretical contributions. The derivation of precise error expressions for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is a valuable result that advances the understanding of this important problem.  I recommend acceptance, contingent upon the authors addressing the following:

1.  **Thorough Proofreading:**  A careful review and correction of all typos, especially in the proofs, is crucial.
2.  **Expanded Related Work:**  A more comprehensive discussion of the single-index model literature and its connections to this work.
3. **Clarification of x0 Norm Assumption:** Discussion of the implications of assuming unit Euclidean norm for x0.
