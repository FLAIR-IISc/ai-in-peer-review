- Extends square-root LASSO asymptotic characterization to single-index (non-linear) measurement models, relaxing Gaussian prior on x0.  
- Proves asymptotic equivalence between generalized LASSO with yi = g(ai^T x0) and LASSO with linearized measurements yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi, with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].  
- Equivalence holds for a wide class of link functions g and regularizers f, providing the first precise asymptotic error formulas for this setting.  
- Builds on Brillinger (1982) and significantly extends Plan & Vershynin (2015) by delivering precise errors rather than upper bounds.  
- Results cover multiple structured signal classes (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and empirical results (Figures 1–2) match theoretical predictions.  
- Technically rigorous and well-supported; proofs in appendix, main theorems clearly stated; assumptions (Gaussian measurement matrix, unit-norm signal) are acknowledged.  
- Clarity issue: the crucial equivalence insight is only in Appendix A and should be presented in the main text to improve motivation.  
- Originality: novel contributions include relaxing Gaussian prior assumption and providing theoretical justification for the Lloyd–Max quantizer in compressed sensing.  
- Significance: practically relevant for 1-bit quantization and censored measurements; supports using LASSO without specialized algorithms for many non-linear measurement settings.  
- Practical question: paper should discuss whether l2-lasso’s oracle regularization (independent of noise variance) makes it preferable to l2^2-lasso in practice.  
- Minor improvements suggested: add more intuition for technical components (e.g., role of the Moreau envelope) and expand practical implications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add the key equivalence to the main text and better address practical algorithm-selection implications.
