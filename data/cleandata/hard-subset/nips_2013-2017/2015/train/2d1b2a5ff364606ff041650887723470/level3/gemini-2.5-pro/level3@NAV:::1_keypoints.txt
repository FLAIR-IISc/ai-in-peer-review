- Paper provides an asymptotic characterization of generalized square-root LASSO for structured signals measured via a non-linear/unknown transform in high-dimensional Gaussian settings.  
- Key result: performance with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a linear model with a specific signal scaling factor and additive noise variance determined by the non-linearity.  
- Extends earlier results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) by moving from bounds to exact asymptotic formulas, matching state-of-the-art LASSO analysis.  
- Major strength: elegant theoretical reduction of a complex non-linear problem to a well-understood linear one, enabling direct use of linear LASSO theory.  
- Practical demonstration: framework recovers the classical Lloyd–Max quantizer in optimal quantizer design, showing applied relevance.  
- Originality/significance: precise characterization is a substantial advance in understanding regularized estimation with non-linear measurements.  
- Main weakness: many typographical errors, notably in appendix proofs, which harm clarity and make verification difficult.  
- Reviewer caveat: reviewer is not an expert in single-index models or sufficient dimension reduction and may have missed related prior work.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on thorough proofreading and correction of the manuscript.
