- Studies Generalized LASSO for yi = gi(ai^T x0) with random link g and shows asymptotic equivalence to a scaled noisy linear model.  
- Main result: estimation error ‖x̂ - μx0‖2 matches that of linear measurements yi = μai^T x0 + σzi with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].  
- Provides precise asymptotic characterizations improving on prior non-tight upper bounds (extends Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin).  
- First precise high-dimensional asymptotic analysis for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, extending recent linear LASSO analyses.  
- Technical quality: rigorous, well-structured proofs that give deep understanding of estimator behavior.  
- Originality: novel extension to non-linear measurements with exact error expressions.  
- Significance: practical implications for quantized sensing; shows Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for minimizing estimation error.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized with a good balance of intuition and technical detail.  
- Weakness: numerous typos in proofs that need correction and may suggest carelessness.  
- Weakness: literature coverage could better compare to extensive single-index / sufficient dimension reduction work.  
- Weakness: practical implications of asymptotic assumptions are not fully explained.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix typos; overall significant and technically strong.
