- Studies Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements under Gaussian design.  
- Shows non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear ones and derives precise asymptotic error formulas.  
- Builds on and extends Plan and Vershynin’s work using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT).  
- Main technical contribution: tight, exact asymptotic error characterization (beyond prior upper bounds).  
- Practical application: designs optimal quantizers for compressive sensing and shows Lloyd–Max yields lower error.  
- Good balance of theory and applications with illustrative simulations and examples.  
- Well-written and organized technical sections, but some parts feel rushed due to space constraints.  
- Would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the Generalized LASSO’s limitations and practical guidance.  
- Minor errors/typos: Figure 1 caption formatting (n=768), typo on page 5 line 247, and mislabeling the Moreau envelope as “proximal function.”  
- High evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8.5/10, Originality 9.5/10, Significance 9.5/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, notable use of CGMT, and insightful practical applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of limitations and presence of minor editorial/notation issues.
