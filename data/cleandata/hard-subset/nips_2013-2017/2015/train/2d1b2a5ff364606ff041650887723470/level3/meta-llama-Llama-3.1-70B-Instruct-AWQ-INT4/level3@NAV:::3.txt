This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of signal processing and machine learning, specifically in the area of compressed sensing and sparse recovery. The authors consider the problem of estimating a structured signal from non-linear measurements, which is a common scenario in many applications. They propose a novel approach based on the Generalized LASSO algorithm, which is a popular method for sparse recovery.

The main contribution of this paper is the derivation of precise asymptotic error formulas for the Generalized LASSO algorithm with non-linear measurements. The authors show that, under certain conditions, the estimation performance of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that of the same algorithm with linear measurements. This result is surprising, as it suggests that the non-linear measurements can be treated as noisy linear measurements with a scaling factor and noise variance that depend on the link function.

The authors also provide a precise error formula for the Generalized LASSO algorithm, which involves a convex program that can be efficiently solved numerically. They demonstrate the accuracy of this formula through simulations and show that it can be used to predict the performance of the algorithm in various scenarios.

One of the key strengths of this paper is its ability to generalize previous results on the Generalized LASSO algorithm to the case of non-linear measurements. The authors build on the work of Plan and Vershynin, who considered a constrained version of the Generalized LASSO, and extend their results to the regularized version of the algorithm. They also provide a more precise error formula than previous work, which makes it possible to accurately predict the performance of the algorithm.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a logical flow of ideas and a good balance between theory and simulations. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related literature and clearly explain the contributions of their work.

Some minor suggestions for improvement include:

* Figure 1 caption formatting: 'n=768' should be rendered as $n=768$.
* Typo on page 5, line 247 (at "When ...").
* The labeled "proximal function" is the Moreau envelope of index τ; change notation since prox usually denotes the proximal operator.
* Assumption 1 should be given a more formal interpretation using variational analysis.
* Do not call the generalized Lasso an "algorithm"; refer to it as a convex program or decoder.

Overall, this is a solid piece of work that makes a significant contribution to the field of compressed sensing and sparse recovery. The results are novel and well-supported by theoretical analysis and simulations.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to sparse recovery from non-linear measurements.
* The authors derive precise asymptotic error formulas for the Generalized LASSO algorithm.
* The results are well-supported by theoretical analysis and simulations.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper assumes a Gaussian design matrix, which may not be realistic in all scenarios.
* The authors do not provide a clear interpretation of their results in terms of the underlying signal structure.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results for practical applications.
