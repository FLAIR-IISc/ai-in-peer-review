- Problem and scope: Studies high-dimensional estimation from nonlinear single-index measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) with structured x0 via convex regularizer f, analyzing generalized LASSO.  
- Main result (Thm 2.1): Asymptotic equivalence—generalized LASSO error with nonlinear measurements equals that with linearized measurements y_lin = A(μ x0)+σ z, where μ = E[γ g(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Main result (Thm 2.2): Provides an exact scalar convex program characterizing asymptotic squared error; yields specialized fixed-point formulas for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank settings.  
- Quantization application: Shows optimal quantizer for minimizing LASSO error corresponds to stationary points of Lloyd–Max under objective σ^2/μ^2.  
- Relation to prior work: Strengthens Brillinger’s classical LS link-misspecification result and Plan–Vershynin’s high-dimensional bounds by providing precise asymptotics and covering regularized LASSO for arbitrary λ.  
- Relation to precise linear-model analyses: Connects to AMP/CGMT results by showing nonlinearity affects only μ and σ^2, enabling reuse of linear-model analyses.  
- Novelty vs concurrent work: Quantized CS and Lloyd–Max consequence are novel; concurrent work (Yi et al.) on μ=0 regimes exists and merits deeper comparison.  
- Methodology quality: Appears to rely on CGMT and careful convex-regularizer handling via function F; specializations match simulations.  
- Rigor/readability issues: Clarify “probability one” in Theorem 2.1 (limit equality vs. almost-sure convergence of differences).  
- Assumption 1 concern: Central assumption defining F needs discussion of sufficient conditions (LLN/empirical process) beyond provided examples.  
- μ ≠ 0 requirement: The exclusion of μ = 0 and σ^2 > 0 omits important regimes (even links, symmetric quantizers); paper should explain what fails and whether direction-recovery can be handled.  
- Intuition request: Add an intuitive explanation why only μ and σ^2 matter (e.g., Hermite expansion and rotational invariance) and whether higher-order terms matter for non-Gaussian A.  
- Practical guidance: Include a recipe/table for computing F for common regularizers (ℓ1, group, nuclear) and fixed-point equations; give a low-rank worked example.  
- Quantizer experiments: Recommend a small experiment showing Lloyd–Max choices minimize predicted σ^2/μ^2 and lower empirical LASSO error.  
- Design assumption question: Results depend on i.i.d. Gaussian A—clarify necessity, extension to sub-Gaussian or rotationally invariant ensembles, and behavior under anisotropic designs.  
- Dependence on μ and σ^2: Request explicit argument (Hermite expansion) and regularity conditions (e.g., L2 integrability) for sufficiency of μ and σ^2 under Gaussian A.  
- Regularizer and λ selection: Ask for guidance on choosing f and λ (e.g., characterizing λ_crit beyond sparsity, heuristics from F for method selection).  
- μ = 0 literature gap: Suggest deeper comparison to methods that handle μ = 0 (Yi et al., certain 1-bit models) to clarify complementarities and limitations.  
- Related-work placement: Recommend connecting to sliced inverse regression and other SDR literature for broader context.  
- Pros summary: Strong, precise theoretical contributions; simple μ/σ^2 equivalence principle; covers multiple structures; practical quantizer implication; clear writing and convincing experiments.  
- Cons summary: Reliance on Gaussian designs; limited treatment of μ = 0 regimes; Assumption 1 and F need operationalization; low-rank section lacks worked example and additional empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: Well-executed, important, and broadly relevant paper whose impact would increase by addressing design assumptions, μ = 0 regimes, and strengthening comparisons and practical guidance.
