The paper "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements" presents a novel and interesting result that characterizes the performance of the Generalized LASSO algorithm when applied to non-linear measurements. The authors derive asymptotic guarantees for the algorithm's performance, which provide a precise and insightful understanding of its behavior.

The paper's main contribution is the derivation of an explicit expression for the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO algorithm when applied to non-linear measurements. This result is significant because it shows that the algorithm's performance is equivalent to that of a linear measurement model, up to a constant of proportionality. This equivalence is established through a precise analysis of the algorithm's behavior in the asymptotic regime, where the number of measurements and the dimension of the signal grow large.

The authors' approach is based on a combination of theoretical tools, including the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) and the Approximate Message Passing (AMP) framework. These tools allow them to derive a precise expression for the estimation error, which takes into account the non-linear measurement function, the regularizer, and the signal structure.

The paper's results have important implications for the design of quantization schemes in compressive sensing. The authors show that the optimal quantization scheme can be determined by minimizing the ratio of the variance of the measurement noise to the square of the mean of the measurement function. This result provides a practical guideline for designing optimal quantization schemes in compressive sensing applications.
