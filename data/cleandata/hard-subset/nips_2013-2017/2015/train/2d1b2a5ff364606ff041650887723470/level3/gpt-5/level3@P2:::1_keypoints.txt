- Paper studies generalized square-root LASSO under single-index model yi = g(a_i^T x0) and proves asymptotic equivalence to a linear model y_lin = μ A x0 + σ z with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Derives an exact scalar optimization predicting asymptotic MSE and specializes results to sparse, group-sparse, and partially low-rank settings.  
- Practical corollary: optimal quantizer design for LASSO reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, with Lloyd–Max as a stationary-point method.  
- Main conceptual message is clear: in high dimensions the generalized LASSO “sees” the nonlinearity only through (μ, σ^2).  
- Technical approach is solid, using CGMT to obtain asymptotically exact expressions for general convex f and all λ.  
- Concrete specializations and informative figures validate theoretical predictions.  
- Central limitation: analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix A, limiting robustness to other ensembles (sub-Gaussian, rotationally invariant, structured).  
- Assumption 1 (limit of scaled Moreau envelope of f*) is abstract; verified for ℓ1/group-ℓ1 but low-rank case is left high-level and needs extra spectral conditions and examples.  
- Equivalence requires μ ≠ 0 and finite σ^2, excluding even/degenerate links; paper should clarify excluded nonlinearities and alternatives when μ = 0.  
- Results are asymptotic; finite-sample rates or concentration bounds are not provided and only illustrated empirically.  
- Presentation suffers from numerous typos, notational inconsistencies, and some terse technical statements; proofreading and an appendix on Assumption 1 are recommended.  
- Original contribution is significant: novel exact asymptotic error characterization extending prior work and an original quantizer-design corollary.  
- Overall assessment: technically credible and impactful for high-dimensional M-estimation and nonlinear compressed sensing, but scope limited by Gaussian assumption and some incomplete treatments.  
- Suggested additions/questions: assess robustness to non-Gaussian A, methods to estimate μ and σ^2 from data, finite-sample confidence bands, concrete low-rank spectral examples, and fuller citation/connection to single-index/SDR literature.  
- Recommendation: lean accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: medium.
