## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the asymptotic estimation error for linear estimation with arbitrary nonlinear link functions using the generalized LASSO. Building upon the work of Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, the authors extend these results to the case where µ ≠ 0, demonstrating that the estimation performance of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear model with scaled and noisy measurements. The core contribution lies in providing precise expressions for the asymptotic error, a significant advancement over existing bounds.

The paper is well-written and clearly presents a fairly interesting and technically sound result. The motivation is well-established, connecting to problems in compressive sensing with quantized measurements and single-index models. The mathematical development appears rigorous, and the authors carefully state their assumptions. The inclusion of illustrative figures (referenced in the text) is a positive aspect, demonstrating the practical relevance of the theoretical findings. The connection drawn to the optimal quantizer (Lloyd-Max) is a noteworthy consequence.

However, several points require attention. The assumption of Gaussian measurement vectors is a significant limitation. The paper doesn’t adequately justify *why* this assumption is necessary, nor does it discuss the potential implications or avenues for extending the results to other measurement matrix distributions. This is a crucial question for the broader applicability of the work. 

Furthermore, while the paper discusses convex regularizers, it lacks a practical discussion on *how* to choose the regularization parameter λ based on the set K containing x0. This selection problem is central to applying the generalized LASSO and deserves further consideration. The dependence of the theorem on µ and σ, characterizing the link function's difficulty, also feels somewhat opaque. A clearer explanation of whether these dependencies are truly necessary, or if simplifications are possible under certain conditions, would be beneficial.

The related work section is incomplete. It omits key citations to the literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which have extensively studied linear/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. Crucially, the recent work by Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, which addresses sparse recovery even when µ = 0 and demonstrates equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation, is missing. This omission is particularly significant given the overlap in themes.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Technically sound and presents novel, precise results.
*   Clear writing and well-motivated problem.
*   Potential impact on applications involving nonlinear measurements.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Strong Gaussian assumption without justification.
*   Lack of discussion on regularizer selection.
*   Incomplete related work section.



**Overall:** Despite the strengths, the missing citations and the lack of justification for key assumptions (Gaussianity) are concerning. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent upon addressing these points. Specifically, the authors should provide a rationale for the Gaussian assumption, expand the related work section, and discuss the practical considerations for choosing the regularization parameter.
