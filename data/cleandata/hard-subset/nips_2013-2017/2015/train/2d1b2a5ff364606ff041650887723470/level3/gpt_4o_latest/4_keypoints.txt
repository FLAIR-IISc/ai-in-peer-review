- Problem: Estimating structured signal x0 from nonlinear measurements using Generalized LASSO in high dimensions.
- Main theoretical contribution: Asymptotically precise error characterization for arbitrary nonlinear link functions under i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices.
- Key result (Theorem 2.1): Error for nonlinear measurements is equivalent to that for appropriately scaled and noisy linear measurements.
- Explicit mapping: Provides formulas for scaling factor μ and noise variance σ^2 determined by the link function g(·).
- Special cases: Analyses for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery are provided.
- Empirical validation: Numerical experiments confirm the theoretical error predictions.
- Strength — Novelty: Extends prior low-dimensional results to high-dimensional regularized settings, offering a novel theoretical advance.
- Strength — Generality: Results apply to a wide range of link functions and regularizers, covering many structured recovery problems.
- Strength — Practical impact: Equivalence to linear measurements yields actionable insights (e.g., optimal quantizer design like Lloyd–Max).
- Strength — Numerical support: Experiments validate the theoretical formulas and predictions.
- Weakness — Gaussian A assumption: Restricting A to i.i.d. Gaussian entries may limit practical applicability and needs justification.
- Weakness — Regularizer guidance: Paper lacks guidance on choosing the regularizer f(·) for given signal structure.
- Weakness — Role of μ and σ: Dependence on μ and σ is not fully explored or justified for characterizing estimation difficulty.
- Weakness — Literature coverage: Missing discussion/citation of related work (e.g., single-index models, sufficient dimension reduction, Yi et al., zero-μ sparse recovery).
- Weakness — Clarity: Presentation is dense (notably Theorem 2.2 derivation via CGMT) and would benefit from more intuitive explanations.
- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions to address assumptions, regularizer selection, literature gaps, parameter interpretation, and exposition.
- Required revisions: (1) Justify Gaussian measurement assumption and its implications, (2) provide guidance for choosing regularizer, (3) expand literature review to include missing references, (4) clarify necessity/role of μ and σ, (5) improve exposition of key derivations.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound results with practical implications and numerical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: Restrictive assumptions, missing references/related-work discussion, and limited clarity in presentation.
