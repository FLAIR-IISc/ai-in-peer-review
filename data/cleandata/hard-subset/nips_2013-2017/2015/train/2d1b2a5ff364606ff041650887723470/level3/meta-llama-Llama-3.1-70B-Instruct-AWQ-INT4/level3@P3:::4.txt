This paper presents a significant extension of the work of Plan and Vershynin (2015) by providing a precise analysis of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. The authors study the asymptotic estimation error for linear estimation with arbitrary non-linear link using generalized LASSO and show that the asymptotic error is identical for linear and non-linear link functions.

The main contribution of the paper is a precise evaluation of the asymptotic behavior of the error of the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements. The authors derive an asymptotically exact expression for the error, which is the first-known precise result in this context. The expression accurately captures the role of the link function, the structure of the signal, the regularizer, and the regularizer parameter.

The paper is well-written and presents fairly interesting results. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, and the technical sections are well-organized and easy to follow. The paper also includes several examples and illustrations that help to clarify the results.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a precise analysis of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. The authors' use of the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework is particularly noteworthy, as it allows them to derive tight bounds on the error of the Generalized LASSO.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One of the main limitations of the paper is its reliance on the Gaussian assumption for the measurement vectors. The authors assume that the entries of the measurement matrix A are iid standard normal, which may not always be the case in practice. It would be interesting to see if the results could be extended to other distributions.

Another area where the paper could be improved is in the discussion of the selection problem for the regularizer. The authors discuss convex regularizers but do not provide much guidance on how to choose the regularizer based on the set K containing x0. This is an important problem in practice, and it would be helpful to have more discussion on this topic.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from more citations to the literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction. While the authors do provide some citations, they do not provide a comprehensive overview of the existing literature on these topics.

Finally, the paper could be improved by providing more discussion on the implications of the results. While the authors do provide some examples and illustrations, they do not provide much discussion on how the results could be used in practice.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents significant contributions to the field. However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved, including the discussion of the Gaussian assumption, the selection problem for the regularizer, and the implications of the results.
