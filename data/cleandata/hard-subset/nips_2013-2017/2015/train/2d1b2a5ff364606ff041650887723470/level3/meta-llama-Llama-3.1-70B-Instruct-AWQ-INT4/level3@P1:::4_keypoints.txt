- Main claim: Theorem 2.1 states the asymptotic estimation error for generalized LASSO is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions.  
- Contribution: Extends Plan and Vershynin’s results to settings with unknown nonlinearity (µ ≠ 0).  
- Model assumption: Analysis assumes Gaussian measurement vectors, which may limit generality.  
- Regularization: The paper does not discuss how to choose the convex regularizer based on the set K containing x0.  
- Dependence on link parameters: The role of µ and σ in characterizing link difficulty is presented but may not be the only relevant dependences.  
- Missing related work: Lacks citations to single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction literature on estimation under unknown links.  
- Missing recent work: Does not cite Yi et al.’s recent results on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions for sparse recovery.  
- Quality: Technically sound with well-supported theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Clarity: Well-written, clear introduction, and organized, easy-to-follow proofs.  
- Originality: Provides a new extension and interpretation of generalized LASSO for unknown link functions.  
- Significance: Results could inspire further research and impact applications in signal/image processing and machine learning.  
- Acceptance concerns: Limitations include Gaussian assumption, lack of discussion on regularizer selection, and missing relevant citations.
