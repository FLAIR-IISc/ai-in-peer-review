- Problem: Estimating structured signals from non-linear measurements using the Generalized LASSO.  
- Main contribution: Shows asymptotic equivalence between non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) and an appropriately scaled linear model yi = μ(ai^T x0)+σzi for Gaussian A.  
- Precise result: Derives exact asymptotic expressions for the Generalized LASSO estimation error (Theorem 2.2) and specializes them to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank cases.  
- Novelty: Extends prior consistency results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) to provide precise error formulas for all λ and many regularizers/link functions.  
- Practical implication: Enables understanding of LASSO behavior with non-linear measurements and informs optimal q-bit quantizer design via the Lloyd–Max connection.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous proofs and significant theoretical advance over prior upper-bound-only results.  
- Clarity (positive): Well-written exposition, logical organization, clear definitions, and helpful figures validating error predictions.  
- Clarity (negative): Numerous typos in the manuscript and proofs detract from professionalism.  
- Literature gap: Reviewer unfamiliar with single-index model/sufficient dimension reduction literature and suggests the paper may undercompare to that body of work.  
- Scope limitation: Focuses on the square-root LASSO; extension/clarity about applicability to the standard LASSO (least-squares + λf) is desirable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: First precise asymptotic characterization for non-linear measurements, fundamental equivalence result, practical quantizer design implications, and strong exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Typos harming polish, possible insufficient engagement with related single-index literature, and narrow focus on square-root LASSO rather than standard LASSO.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance; technical contribution is substantial and issues are fixable before camera-ready.
