- Core contribution: shows asymptotic equivalence between non-linear measurements and noisy linear measurements for Generalized Lasso, with precise error formulas.
- Builds on and extends prior work (Plan & Vershynin; Brillinger) by providing explicit asymptotic error characterizations beyond prior bounds.
- Theoretical derivations are rigorous and technically sound.
- Empirical validation across Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization demonstrates broad applicability.
- Application to q-bit compressive sensing links results to Lloyd–Max quantizer design and offers novel quantization insights.
- Assumptions and limitations are clearly stated, but intuition for some technical choices (e.g., Assumption 1) is underdeveloped.
- Presentation imbalance: theory is detailed while motivation and intuitive explanations need strengthening.
- Minor issues: figure caption formatting, a typo on page 5 line 247, and nomenclature concerns.
- Terminology critique: “proximal function” should be called a Moreau envelope; Generalized Lasso should be called a convex program/decoder, not an “algorithm.”
- Arguments for acceptance: novel explicit error formulas, strong theoretical and empirical support, insightful application, meaningful extension of prior work.
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation imbalance and minor technical/nomenclature issues.
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to clarify intuition and fix minor issues.
