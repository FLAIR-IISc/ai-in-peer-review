- Problem setup: high-dimensional estimation under single-index/nonlinear model y_i = g(a_i^T x0) with iid Gaussian rows A and structured x0.
- Method analyzed: generalized square-root LASSO (minimize ||y − Ax||2 + λ f(x)) with convex regularizer f.
- Main result: asymptotic equivalence to an effective linear model y_i = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i with μ = E[γ g(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μ γ)^2].
- Precise characterization: a scalar convex program whose unique optimizer gives the asymptotic squared-error.
- Specializations: explicit formulas and predictions for ℓ1 (sparse), group-ℓ1,2 (group-sparse), and nuclear-norm (low-rank) regularizers.
- Quantization application: minimizing LASSO error reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, implying Lloyd–Max quantizer optimal for q-bit design.
- Relation to prior work: extends Brillinger’s link-misspecification result and Plan–Vershynin bounds; connects to AMP/CGMT precise LASSO analyses.
- Technical approach: CGMT-like arguments; assumptions and proof framework appear sound.
- Empirics: simulations corroborate theoretical predictions across undersampled (δ<1) and oversampled (δ>1) regimes.
- Assumptions: iid Gaussian design, independent link instances, finite moments, μ ≠ 0, linear m,n asymptotics, and a technical regularity (Assumption 1).
- Limitation — design: Gaussian design is essential to proofs; robustness to sub-Gaussian or structured matrices is not addressed.
- Limitation — μ = 0: analysis excludes cases where μ = 0 (e.g., certain symmetric links or extreme quantizers).
- Limitation — finite-sample and tuning: limited guidance on finite-sample deviations and practical λ selection beyond asymptotics.
- Limitation — comparisons: quantization application lacks comparisons to estimators tailored to 1-bit/q-bit compressed sensing.
- Clarity: generally well-organized with accessible scalar optimization presentation, but numerous typos and notation inconsistencies require careful proofreading.
- Suggestions: discuss extensions to non-Gaussian/structured designs, clarify computation for nuclear-norm case, show how to estimate μ and σ from data, provide λ-tuning guidance and sensitivity plots, compare to specialized quantized-CS methods, and address μ = 0 regime.
- Overall assessment: original and significant within Gaussian-design scope; technically strong and practically relevant, leaning toward acceptance pending editorial fixes and expanded robustness discussion.
