- Central result: For i.i.d. Gaussian measurements, generalized LASSO with nonlinear single-index measurements has the same asymptotic MSE as if measurements were linear with effective gain μ=E[γg(γ)] and noise σ^2=E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Precise characterization: Theorem 2.2 gives an exact scalar convex program predicting asymptotic error for general convex regularizers under m/n→δ.  
- Specializations: The paper specializes the formula to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery (e.g., Theorem 2.3 for ℓ1 with general sparse priors).  
- Quantized sensing application: Designing quantizer thresholds to minimize σ^2/μ^2 aligns with Lloyd–Max, explaining optimality for LASSO error.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger’s fixed-p link-violation results and strengthens Plan–Vershynin nonasymptotic analyses by giving asymptotically exact regularized-LASSO risks.  
- Methodology: Leverages CGMT to obtain sharp high-dimensional risk formulas, connecting to AMP/CGMT LASSO literature.  
- Strength — conceptual clarity: The equivalence “nonlinear ≡ linear with (μ,σ)” is simple, powerful, and precisely stated.  
- Strength — technical contribution: Provides a broad, exact asymptotic risk characterization for many convex regularizers.  
- Strength — practical insight & experiments: Quantizer design insight and empirical figures match theoretical predictions.  
- Weakness — Gaussian design assumption: Theory requires i.i.d. N(0,1) rows; applicability to sub-Gaussian, Rademacher, partial orthogonal, or correlated designs is unclear and needs discussion/experiments.  
- Weakness — μ and σ sufficiency: The claim that only μ and σ matter needs justification whether it is information-theoretically necessary or an artifact of proofs (e.g., Hermite expansions).  
- Weakness — μ=0 excluded: The paper assumes μ≠0, excluding symmetric links like noiseless 1-bit; should cite and compare to work handling μ=0 (e.g., Yi et al. 2015) and discuss possible extensions (higher-order Hermite terms, dithering).  
- Weakness — choice of regularizer vs set K: Lacks practitioner guidance on selecting f for a given structure K and no explicit tie to geometric complexity measures (Gaussian width/statistical dimension).  
- Weakness — low-rank case incomplete: Assumption 1 and the function F(c1,c2,c3) need concrete spectral-measure conditions and numerical evaluation for canonical low-rank models.  
- Weakness — unknown g in practice: While LASSO can be run without knowing g, calibrating/interpreting x̂ depends on μ,σ; paper should discuss consistent estimation of μ and σ from data.  
- Related literature gap: Should add citations/positioning with classical single-index/SDR methods (SIR, SAVE, MAVE, Ichimura, Härdle–Hall–Ichimura) and recent link-agnostic high-dimensional methods.  
- Quality assessment: Technical development is solid and validated for ℓ1 sparse case and λ_crit phenomenon; low-rank section needs more work.  
- Clarity notes: Paper is well organized but should clarify that objective uses ||y−Ax||_2 (not squared), state Assumption 1 earlier, and give intuition (Hermite expansion) for why only μ and σ matter.  
- Originality and significance: Likely the first asymptotically exact risk characterization for generalized LASSO under nonlinear single-index measurements; results are significant for theory and practical design (e.g., quantizers).  
- Acceptance pros: Strong novel theory, clear unification under (μ,σ), practical consequences with convincing experiments.  
- Acceptance cons: Restricted to Gaussian designs, excludes μ=0 regime with missing comparisons, and low-rank analysis/Assumption 1 need better motivation.  
- Suggested improvements: Discuss extensions beyond Gaussian designs and necessity of μ,σ dependence; cite Yi et al. and SDR literature; provide methods to estimate μ,σ; and flesh out low-rank example with concrete F and experiments.
