- Paper analyzes asymptotic estimation error of Generalized LASSO with nonlinear link functions, extending Plan and Vershynin to nonzero µ.  
- Main claim (Theorem 2.1): asymptotic error matches that of appropriately scaled linear measurements.  
- Subsequent theorems give precise error expressions depending on regularization and link-function properties.  
- Application to q-bit compressive sensing shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal.  
- Situates work within compressive sensing and statistical estimation literature; connects to convex Gaussian min-max theorem and AMP.  
- Technical derivations appear rigorous and exposition is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Numerical validation (figures) supports theoretical findings.  
- Strong assumption of Gaussian measurement vectors is not justified or explored for robustness to other distributions.  
- Paper lacks guidance on choosing the regularizer f based on the signal structure or solution set K.  
- The role and necessity of dependence on µ and σ are not thoroughly investigated or potentially minimized.  
- Omits relevant citations in sufficient dimension reduction (e.g., Li & Duan 2008; Garnham & Prendergast 2015) and Yi et al. (2015) on µ = 0 sparse recovery.  
- Overall assessment: solid and technically sound contribution but weakened by missing citations and limited discussion of key assumptions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
