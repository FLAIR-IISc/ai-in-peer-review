- Central contribution: exact high-dimensional asymptotic characterization of LASSO MSE for non-linear measurements, showing equivalence to a scaled linear model yi = µ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i.

- Main theorem (Theorem 2.1): estimation error with non-linear measurements matches that of LASSO on an effective linear model with constants µ and σ determined by the link g.

- Technical advance: derives precise computable error formulas (Theorem 2.2) using an extension of frameworks like the CGMT, going beyond prior upper bounds.

- Applicability: provides specific characterizations for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery and a practical example (optimal q-bit quantizer via minimizing σ²/µ²).

- Writing and clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, assumptions and motivations are clear, and connections to prior work are well-presented.

- Missing citation: fails to adequately acknowledge related work by Yi et al. (2015), which studies similar high-dimensional equivalence and handles the µ=0 case.

- Literature context: should broaden discussion of single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction from statistics/econometrics.

- Gaussian measurement assumption: heavy reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian A is a limitation; authors should discuss necessity and extendability to sub-Gaussian or other ensembles.

- µ and σ dependence: unclear whether the effective SNR characterization is fundamental or an artifact of using LASSO; discussion needed on estimators that exploit known non-linearity.

- Regularizer guidance: paper analyzes performance for a given f(x) but offers little practical guidance on choosing regularizers or translating constraint sets to penalized forms.

- Recommendation summary: strong, elegant technical contribution worthy of acceptance pending addressing missing citations and limitations related to Gaussian assumptions and discussion of µ=0 case.
