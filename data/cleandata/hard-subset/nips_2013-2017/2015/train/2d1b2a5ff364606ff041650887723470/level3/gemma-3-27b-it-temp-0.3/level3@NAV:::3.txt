## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso for signal estimation when measurements are subject to non-linear transformations. The core contribution lies in demonstrating that, under Gaussian design, these non-linear measurements can be effectively treated as noisy linear measurements, and crucially, deriving precise asymptotic error formulas building upon the work of Plan & Vershynin. This is a significant step forward, providing a rigorous theoretical foundation for using regularized estimation techniques in scenarios with non-linearities, such as quantized compressive sensing.

The paper is technically sound and well-motivated. The authors clearly articulate the problem, connect it to existing literature in single-index models and compressive sensing, and build upon prior work in a logical manner. The derivation of the asymptotic error formulas is a substantial achievement, and the validation through simulations, particularly the application to q-bit CS quantizer design and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, is insightful and strengthens the claims. The demonstration that the optimal quantizer aligns with the well-known Lloyd-Max criterion is a particularly compelling result. 

However, the paper suffers from some imbalances in clarity. While generally well-written, the technical details are dense, and the rapid pace through the derivations might hinder understanding for readers less familiar with the specific mathematical tools employed. The space constraints are acknowledged, but expanding on key steps, especially in the proofs, would improve accessibility. 

The originality of the work is strong. The explicit error expressions represent a novel extension of existing results, offering a more precise understanding of estimation performance in non-linear measurement settings. The connection between the generalized Lasso and the equivalent linear model is a valuable theoretical contribution.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical foundation with precise asymptotic error formulas.
*   Novel and significant extension of prior work.
*   Insightful application to q-bit CS and connection to Lloyd-Max.
*   Solid empirical validation supporting the theoretical results.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Clarity could be improved through more detailed explanations and derivations.
*   Some minor technical issues (detailed below) need addressing.

**Specific Comments:**

*   Figure 1 caption: “n=768” should be rendered as $n=768$.
*   Page 5, line 247: Typo at “When…”.
*   The labeled “proximal function” is the Moreau envelope of index τ; change notation since “prox” usually denotes the proximal operator.
*   Assumption 1 should be given a more formal interpretation using variational analysis.
*   Avoid calling the generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; refer to it as a convex program or decoder.



Overall, this is a high-quality paper with significant contributions to the field. Addressing the minor clarity issues and technical comments would further strengthen the manuscript and make it more accessible to a wider audience. I recommend acceptance.
