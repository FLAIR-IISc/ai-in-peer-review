- Main result: Shows Generalized LASSO on non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on linear measurements with parameters μ and σ derived from g.  
- Precise characterization: Provides the first-known precise asymptotic error expressions for Generalized LASSO under the stated model.  
- Model specifics: Assumes measurements y_i = g(a_i^T x0) with i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix and independent copies of link g.  
- Theoretical rigor: Technically sound proofs under reasonable assumptions with extensive theoretical analysis.  
- Empirical support: Extensive numerical validation corroborates the theoretical results.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Plan and Vershynin (2015) by moving from loose bounds to precise error characterizations.  
- Practical insight: Connects results to Lloyd–Max quantizer design, offering guidance for optimal quantization in compressed sensing.  
- Clarity and presentation: Exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and reproducible.  
- Limitation — Gaussian assumption: The necessity of the i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix assumption is not sufficiently justified or discussed for broader ensembles.  
- Limitation — missing citations: Important related work in single-index models and Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown links is omitted.  
- Limitation — regularizer guidance: Lacks clear, practical guidance on selecting convex regularizers or the set K based on structure of x0.  
- Acceptance case: Valuable, novel, and technically rigorous contribution that advances understanding of LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Rejection concerns: Citation omissions and insufficient justification of Gaussian assumptions are significant and should be addressed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add missing citations and justify or discuss the Gaussian measurement assumption.
