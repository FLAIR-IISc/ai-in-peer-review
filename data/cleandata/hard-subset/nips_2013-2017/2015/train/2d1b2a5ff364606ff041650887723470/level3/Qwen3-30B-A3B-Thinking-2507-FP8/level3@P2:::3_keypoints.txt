- Addresses estimation of structured signal x₀ from non-linear measurements yᵢ = gᵢ(aᵢᵀx₀) using the Generalized LASSO.
- Main contribution: precise asymptotic characterization of error ‖x̂ - μx₀‖₂² with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].
- Key insight: Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on linear measurements y = μAx₀ + σz.
- Extends Brillinger (1982) and Plan & Vershynin (2015) by providing exact asymptotic expressions rather than bounds.
- Leverages the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework to derive rigorous results.
- Includes comprehensive experiments (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) validating theoretical predictions.
- Novel, practical application: connects q-bit compressive sensing quantizer design to the Lloyd-Max quantizer.
- Paper is technically sound and well-executed with rigorous analysis and strong empirical support.
- Generally well-written and organized, with clear statement of main contributions for the NIPS audience.
- Minor clarity/notation issues: Figure 1 caption formatting, typo on page 5, confusing Moreau envelope notation, and clarify that Generalized LASSO is a convex program.
- Assumption 1 needs a more formal interpretation via variational analysis.
- Some technical details omitted due to space constraints and the relationship between constrained vs regularized LASSO could be expanded.
- Strength: first precise asymptotic error formulas for non-linear measurement LASSO and clean equivalence to scaled linear model.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis built on recent LASSO advances and validated empirically.
- Strength: practical significance for quantizer design and guidance on regularization under non-linearities.
- Weakness: minor notational inconsistencies and presentation issues that should be fixed in revision.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, novel practical insights, sound methods, and strong validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation/formalization of Assumption 1 and some omitted technical details need improvement.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
