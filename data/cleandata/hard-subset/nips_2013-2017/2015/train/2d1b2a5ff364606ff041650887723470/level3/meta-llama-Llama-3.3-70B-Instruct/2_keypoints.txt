- Presents a convex relaxation method to learn the MNL model from ordinal data, applied to collaborative ranking and bundled choice modeling.
- Provides theoretical guarantees including upper bounds on error and a minimax lower bound.
- Extends analysis of l2-lasso to single-index models, relaxing the Gaussian prior assumption.
- Shows single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise, offering heuristic justification.
- Highlights advantage of l2-lasso over l2^2-lasso because its regularization parameter is independent of unknown noise variance.
- Includes numerical experiments illustrating the method’s performance.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains methodology and results clearly.
- Technically sound with thorough analysis of the convex relaxation approach.
- Pro-accept: novel approach, theoretical guarantees, clear presentation, and significant contribution to the field.
- Con-accept: relies on a low-rank assumption that may not hold in practice.
- Con-accept: lacks empirical comparison with other MNL learning approaches.
- Con-accept: does not thoroughly analyze computational complexity; authors should address these limitations.
