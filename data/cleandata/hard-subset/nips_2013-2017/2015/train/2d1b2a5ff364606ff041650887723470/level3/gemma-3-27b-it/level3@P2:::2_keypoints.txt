- Paper establishes precise asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and a scaled linear model with effective scale μ and noise variance σ².  
- Defines μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ) − μγ)²], showing the non-linear problem reduces to linear analysis asymptotically.  
- Builds on Brillinger (1982) and Plan & Vershynin (2015), extending results to high-dimensional settings with tighter, more general characterizations.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical proofs and clearly stated assumptions, with technically sound arguments.  
- Derives precise expressions for estimation error across structured signals (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and various regularizers.  
- Analysis is general, applicable to a wide class of link functions g and regularizers f.  
- Demonstrates practical application to q-bit compressive sensing and shows Lloyd–Max algorithm is suitable for optimizing quantization.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses consistent notation that aids readability.  
- Strength: significant theoretical novelty and originality in asymptotic error characterization for non-linear measurements.  
- Strength: practical relevance through quantization application and guidance for optimal design.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of how μ and σ² should influence practical choices (e.g., l2-lasso vs l2²-lasso).  
- Weakness: key equivalence to single-index models/Brillinger (1982) is buried in Appendix A and should be highlighted in the main text.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (limit of Fenchel conjugate) may be hard to verify in practice; more guidance on admissible f would help.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a fundamental estimation problem, is rigorous and novel, well-presented, and has promising applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical implications need stronger emphasis and the main-text placement of the key equivalence should be improved.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance (confidence 8/10) with minor revisions to address raised points.
