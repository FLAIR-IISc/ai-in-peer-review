

# Review: "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper addresses an important extension of the Generalized LASSO framework to non-linear measurement models. The authors extend precise asymptotic characterization of the square-root LASSO (l2-lasso) to single-index models, relaxing the common Gaussian prior assumption on the signal x0. They establish a precise asymptotic equivalence between the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = gi(ai^T x0) and the LASSO with appropriately scaled linear measurements yi = μai^T x0 + σzi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1). This equivalence holds for a wide class of link functions g and regularizers f, providing the first precise asymptotic characterization in this context.

The work builds upon and significantly extends the classical results of Brillinger (1982) and recent contributions by Plan and Vershynin (2015). While Plan and Vershynin provided upper bounds for the constrained Generalized LASSO, this paper delivers precise error formulas for the regularized LASSO across various structured signal recovery problems (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank). The authors demonstrate that their theoretical results accurately predict empirical performance across different scenarios, as illustrated in Figures 1-2.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and well-supported by rigorous asymptotic analysis. The proofs are deferred to the appendix, but the main results are clearly presented and supported by numerical evidence. The authors carefully address the limitations of their assumptions (e.g., Gaussian measurement matrix, unit-norm signal), and the theoretical results are properly contextualized within existing literature. The paper provides a complete characterization of the estimation error, which is a significant advancement over previous upper-bound results.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The main technical contribution (Theorem 2.1) is clearly stated and motivated. However, the equivalence between the single-index model and the linear model with scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise (Brillinger 1982), which is crucial for justifying the blockquote on line 140, is only presented in Appendix A. This key insight should be mentioned in the main text to improve clarity and help readers understand the motivation for the main result.

**Originality:** The paper makes a significant contribution to the literature on structured signal recovery. While Plan and Vershynin (2015) established a relationship between non-linear and linear measurements, this work provides the first precise asymptotic characterization of the Generalized LASSO performance in the non-linear measurement setting. The extension to non-Gaussian signal priors (relaxing the Gaussian assumption on x0) and the derivation of precise error formulas for multiple structured signal classes (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) represent substantial novel contributions. The paper also provides a theoretical justification for the Lloyd-Max quantizer as the optimal quantizer for compressed sensing, which appears to be new.

**Significance:** The results have clear practical relevance for applications involving non-linear measurements (e.g., 1-bit quantization, censored measurements). The precise error characterization provides valuable insights for practitioners, including the observation that the optimal quantizer is the Lloyd-Max quantizer. The paper should address whether the l2-lasso's oracle regularization parameter (which does not depend on noise variance) makes it preferable to the l2^2-lasso in practice, as this is a practical question the results could inform. The authors' claim that the estimation performance of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically the same as with linear measurements provides a powerful theoretical foundation for using LASSO in non-linear measurement settings without requiring specialized algorithms.

**Pro for Acceptance:**
- First precise asymptotic characterization for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements
- Extends previous work of Plan and Vershynin with precise error formulas
- Provides theoretical justification for optimal quantization (Lloyd-Max)
- Well-written with clear organization and thorough numerical validation
- Relaxes the Gaussian prior assumption on x0, broadening applicability

**Con for Acceptance:**
- Key insight about the equivalence to linear measurements should be mentioned in main text (currently only in Appendix A)
- Could better address practical implications for preferring l2-lasso over l2^2-lasso in practice
- Some technical details (e.g., the role of the Moreau envelope) might benefit from additional intuition

Overall, this is a strong contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics and signal processing. The paper addresses an important theoretical gap with precise results that have clear practical implications. The technical quality is excellent, the results are novel and significant, and the writing is clear. The minor issue with the main text clarity (equivalence not mentioned in main text) is easily fixable, and the practical relevance question could be addressed more explicitly.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity (adding the key equivalence to the main text) and to better address the practical implications of their results for algorithm selection. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the theoretical understanding of LASSO under non-linear measurements and provides valuable insights for practitioners dealing with non-linear measurement systems.
