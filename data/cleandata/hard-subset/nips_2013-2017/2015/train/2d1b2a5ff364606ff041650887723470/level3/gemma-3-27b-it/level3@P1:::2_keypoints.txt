- Shows that Generalized LASSO error with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a scaled linear model with additive Gaussian noise.  
- Provides explicit, precise asymptotic error expressions valid in high-dimensional regimes and for a broad class of regularizers.  
- Identifies the Lloyd–Max quantizer as the optimal quantizer for minimizing estimation error.  
- Extends and connects prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) and situates results within literature on single-index models, compressed sensing, and regularized regression.  
- Uses Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) and recent high-dimensional analysis techniques, lending strong theoretical rigor.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and uses consistent notation; motivations are clear.  
- Main equivalence result is under-emphasized in the main text and overly deferred to Appendix A, reducing accessibility.  
- Does not fully explore practical implications of the equivalence, such as guidance on choosing between l2-LASSO and alternatives or implications for tuning regularization.  
- The optimization in Theorem 2.2 may be computationally challenging in practice and requires more discussion of computational cost.  
- The Lloyd–Max algorithm recommendation for quantization is useful but limited without broader computational context.  
- Pro-acceptance: significant theoretical advance, rigorous analysis, broad applicability, and clear presentation.  
- Con-acceptance: limited practical guidance on algorithm/parameter choices and potential computational barriers to applying the theory.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing practical implications and adding brief discussion of computational complexity and algorithmic guidance.
