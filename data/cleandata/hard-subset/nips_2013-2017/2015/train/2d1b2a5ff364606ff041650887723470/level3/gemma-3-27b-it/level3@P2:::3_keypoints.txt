- Core contribution: shows non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear ones and derives precise asymptotic error formulas extending prior work.  
- Theoretical basis: analysis builds on high-dimensional statistics and random matrix theory to provide rigorous foundations for LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Empirical validation: experiments cover Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear-norm regularization and validate theoretical predictions.  
- Practical application: insightful application to q-bit compressive sensing linking results to Lloyd–Max quantizer design.  
- Mathematical rigor: derivations and proofs appear rigorous and clearly presented.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear but occasionally unbalanced; some technical steps and assumptions need more detail.  
- Notation/terminology: use of “proximal function” is misleading (it’s the Moreau envelope) and “algorithm” for Generalized LASSO is imprecise.  
- Assumption 1: would benefit from a more formal interpretation, e.g., via variational analysis.  
- Originality: provides novel, explicit, asymptotically precise expressions for estimation error rather than just bounds.  
- Impact: precise formulas capture effects of link function, signal structure, regularizer, and penalty, enabling performance optimization.  
- Generality: results apply to a wide range of regularizers and non-linear measurement models.  
- Connections: paper situates itself well within existing literature and refines previous results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin).  
- Strengths summary: theoretical rigor, novelty, generality, practical relevance, and strong literature connections.  
- Weaknesses summary: minor clarity issues, terminology imprecision, need for a formal take on Assumption 1, and small formatting/typo errors.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant theoretical contribution, rigorous proofs, validated empirically, and useful application to quantized sensing.  
- Counterarguments: some sections may be hard for non-experts and minor presentation/terminology issues reduce polish.  
- Overall assessment: strong recommendation to accept; technically novel, practically relevant, rated 8/10 (Strong Accept).
