- Main contribution: proves ℓ2-LASSO on non-linear single-index measurements asymptotically equals solving a linear model y = μ A x0 + σ z with μ = E[γ g(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].  
- Technical method: leverages Brillinger’s single-index insight, the Plan–Vershynin constrained formulation, and CGMT-style analysis for precise asymptotic error predictions.  
- Generality: results hold for broad convex penalties (sparsity, group sparsity, low-rank) and for general priors p_x0 with bounded moments (beyond Gaussian nonzeros).  
- Sparse recovery: provides closed-form fixed-point characterizations for sparse signals.  
- Quantization application: applies theory to q-bit quantization and recovers Lloyd–Max quantizers as stationary points minimizing induced LASSO error.  
- Soundness and empirical fit: analysis appears technically careful; risk formula is convex/computable and empirical figures match predictions.  
- Limitations — design: relies on i.i.d. Gaussian design A, limiting generality to other measurement ensembles.  
- Limitations — edge cases and samples: excludes the μ = 0 case and explores only modest finite-sample behavior.  
- Clarity issue: the Brillinger “single-index ≡ linear” equivalence is relegated to Appendix A but should be stated and used in the main text (e.g., to justify line 140).  
- Assumption 1: needs more intuition, expanded discussion, and additional worked examples to demystify its scope.  
- Identifiability: the treatment of μ = 0 and the “direction-only” identifiability should be clarified.  
- Practical relevance: should discuss whether results favor ℓ2-LASSO over ℓ2^2-LASSO when noise variance is unknown and provide a small empirical comparison.  
- Parameter estimation: the paper should comment on or provide methods for estimating μ and σ when g is unknown.  
- Originality: novel and timely extension of precise ℓ2-LASSO asymptotics from linear to single-index models, unifying AMP, CGMT, and single-index lines of work.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong, general, precise theory; broad applicability; useful design insights for quantization.  
- Cons for acceptance: Gaussian-design restriction, key equivalence buried in appendix, Assumption 1 needs more intuition, limited practical guidance on choosing estimator variants and estimating μ/σ.
