This paper studies the asymptotic estimation error of linear estimation with arbitrary nonlinear link functions using the generalized LASSO. The authors extend the work of Plan and Vershynin to the case where the mean of the link function, denoted by µ, is not equal to zero. The main claim of the paper is that the asymptotic error is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions, as stated in Theorem 2.1.

The paper is well-written and presents fairly interesting results. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, and the technical sections are well-organized and easy to follow. The paper includes several examples and simulations that illustrate the accuracy of the theoretical results.

However, there are a few issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, the Gaussian assumption on the measurement vectors is not well-motivated. The authors should provide more explanation on why this assumption is necessary and how it affects the results. Secondly, the paper discusses convex regularizers but does not address the problem of selecting the regularizer based on the set K containing x0. This is an important issue that needs to be considered in practice.

Furthermore, the theorem's dependence on µ and σ characterizing link difficulty is not well-explained. The authors should provide more insight into why these dependences are necessary and how they affect the results. Additionally, the paper is missing citations to relevant literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction that study linear/subspace estimation under unknown link functions.

Finally, the paper is also missing a citation to the recent work by Yi et al. on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, which addresses sparse recovery even when µ = 0 and shows equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation.
