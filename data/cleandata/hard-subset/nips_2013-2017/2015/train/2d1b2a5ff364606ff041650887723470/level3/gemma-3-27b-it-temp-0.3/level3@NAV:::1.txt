## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO for estimating signals from measurements corrupted by non-linear functions. The core contribution lies in deriving asymptotically precise expressions for the estimation error, demonstrating that the non-linear measurement problem can be effectively approximated by a scaled and noisy linear measurement problem. This is a significant result, providing a theoretical justification for the continued use of LASSO-type methods in scenarios with non-linearities.

The work builds upon prior results by Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, significantly strengthening them by providing explicit error characterizations rather than just consistency guarantees. The authors carefully consider the asymptotic regime where both the signal dimension and the number of measurements grow large, offering a more relevant analysis for modern high-dimensional applications. The paper is well-written and clearly presents the mathematical derivations, though it suffers from a noticeable number of typos, particularly within the proofs, which detract from readability. 

A key strength is the connection established between the non-linear measurement problem and a corresponding linear one, allowing the authors to leverage existing results from the well-developed field of linear estimation. The application to q-bit compressive sensing is a compelling example, demonstrating how the theoretical results can be used to optimize quantization parameters. However, I admit unfamiliarity with the single-index model/sufficient dimension reduction literature, and it's possible relevant prior work in those areas has been overlooked. 

Despite the strong theoretical contributions, the paper’s practical implications are somewhat limited. While the analysis provides valuable insights, translating these insights into concrete improvements in algorithm design or parameter tuning might require further investigation. The assumption of Gaussian measurement matrices, while common, also restricts the applicability of the results.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Provides a novel and theoretically sound analysis of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.
*   Derives precise error characterizations, offering a significant improvement over existing results.
*   Establishes a clear connection between non-linear and linear measurement problems.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Numerous typos throughout the paper, particularly in the proofs, hinder readability.
*   The practical implications of the results are not fully explored.
*   My unfamiliarity with the single-index model literature raises the possibility of overlooked related work.



Overall, this is a solid theoretical contribution with a clear and well-defined scope. Addressing the typos and potentially acknowledging the related work in sufficient dimension reduction would strengthen the paper considerably. I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the identified issues.
