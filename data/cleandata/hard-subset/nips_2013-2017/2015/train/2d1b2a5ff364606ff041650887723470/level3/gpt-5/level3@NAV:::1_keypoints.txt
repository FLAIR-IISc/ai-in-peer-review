- Problem/result: Proves asymptotic equivalence between generalized square-root LASSO on nonlinear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) and an effective linear model with signal μx0 and Gaussian noise of variance σ2, where μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)2].
- Main technical deliverable: Provides a precise scalar optimization (Theorem 2.2) characterizing asymptotic MSE and specializes it to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank settings.
- Practical implication: Ties estimation error to σ2/μ2 and yields an optimal quantizer-design perspective linked to Lloyd–Max.
- Technical quality: Strong and (to reviewer’s knowledge) novel — gives a convex three-scalar characterization for nonlinear observations beyond Plan–Vershynin’s bounds.
- Empirical validation: Special cases (e.g., soft-thresholding fixed points) are convincing and align with simulations.
- Assumptions: Clearly stated (i.i.d. Gaussian design, μ ≠ 0, bounded σ2, Assumption 1 on envelopes) but need more discussion on necessity and robustness beyond Gaussian designs.
- Clarity issues: Paper is well organized but contains numerous typos, minor inconsistencies, stray subscripts/superscripts, and occasional missing constants; symbols should be defined on first use.
- Presentation suggestion: Consider moving key parts of Assumption 1’s verification into the main text for clarity.
- Originality: High — extends prior nonasymptotic work to precise asymptotics and unifies several LASSO variants; quantizer implication is novel.
- Significance: High — offers actionable guidance for practitioners using LASSO under unknown nonlinearities and for measurement-system design.
- Literature gap: Reviewer not deeply familiar with single-index/SDR literature; suggests adding brief comparisons to Ichimura (1993), Li & Duan (1989), and related semiparametric efficiency results.
- Pros for acceptance: Precise general asymptotic characterization, conceptual nonlinear-to-linear equivalence, solid special cases and numerical validation.
- Cons against acceptance: Heavy reliance on Gaussian designs, limited robustness discussion, many typos, Assumption 1 needs clarification, limited connection to broader SDR literature.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on thorough proofreading and a brief expansion of related work.
