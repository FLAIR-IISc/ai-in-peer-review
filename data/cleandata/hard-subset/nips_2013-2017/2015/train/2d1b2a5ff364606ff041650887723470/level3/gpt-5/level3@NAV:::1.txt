Summary and relation to prior work: The paper studies recovery of structured signals from nonlinear measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0) using the generalized square-root LASSO (minimize ||y − Ax||2 + λ f(x)). Building on Brillinger’s classical linearization result and the constrained LASSO bounds of Plan & Vershynin, the authors prove an asymptotically exact equivalence: under i.i.d. Gaussian designs and broad classes of convex regularizers and link functions, the error of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements matches that of the same method under an effective linear model with scaled signal μx0 and Gaussian noise of variance σ2 determined by g via μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)2]. They then give a precise scalar optimization formula (Theorem 2.2) for the asymptotic MSE, and specialize it to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank settings, recovering known linear-measurement results and providing new ones. An interesting consequence is an optimal quantizer design perspective, tying the error to σ2/μ2 and connecting to Lloyd–Max.

Quality: The technical contribution is strong. The equivalence theorem and the convex three-scalar characterization are clear and, to my knowledge, new for nonlinear observations. Specializations (e.g., sparse recovery via soft-thresholding fixed points) are convincing and align with simulations. Assumptions (Gaussian A, μ ≠ 0, σ2 bounded, Assumption 1 on envelopes) are clearly stated but could use more discussion on necessity and robustness beyond Gaussian designs.

Clarity: The paper is well organized and largely clear, but there are numerous typos and minor inconsistencies, especially in proofs (e.g., “mesaurements,” “redifining,” “eventhough,” stray subscripts/superscripts, occasional missing constants). Please proofread thoroughly; define all symbols on first use, and consider moving key parts of Assumption 1’s verification into the main text.

Originality: High. This goes beyond Plan–Vershynin’s nonasymptotic bounds to precise asymptotics for regularized programs and unifies several LASSO variants. The quantizer design implication is neat.

Significance: High. The results provide actionable guidance for modelers using LASSO-type methods under unknown nonlinearities and for measurement system design.

Caveats and related work: I am not deeply familiar with the single-index/sufficient-dimension-reduction literature and may have missed connections (e.g., Ichimura 1993; Li & Duan 1989; related semiparametric efficiency results). A brief comparison would strengthen the positioning.

Pros for acceptance:
- Precise, general asymptotic characterization with clear practical implications
- Conceptual equivalence result (nonlinear-to-linear) underpins multiple corollaries
- Solid special cases and numerical validation

Cons:
- Heavy reliance on Gaussian designs; limited robustness discussion
- Numerous typos; some assumptions (Assumption 1) could be clarified
- Limited connection to broader single-index/SDR literature

Author responses and other reviews did not change my assessment. Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on careful proofreading and a brief related-work expansion.
