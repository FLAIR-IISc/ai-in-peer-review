- Studies Generalized LASSO for non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) with iid link functions g and Gaussian measurement matrices.  
- Main asymptotic result: error with non-linear measurements equals error for linear model yi = μ(ai^T x0) + σ zi with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].  
- Provides a precise asymptotic error formula expressed via a convex optimization involving the Fenchel conjugate and Moreau envelope of the regularizer.  
- Derives explicit formulas for important signal models: sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signals.  
- Demonstrates empirical validation showing theoretical predictions match simulations across multiple settings.  
- Applies results to q-bit compressive sensing and shows the optimal quantizer is the Lloyd–Max quantizer.  
- Extends prior work: refines Brillinger’s non-regularized analysis and strengthens Plan & Vershynin’s loose upper bounds for the regularized case.  
- Analysis is technically rigorous, building on CGMT and recent advances for Generalized LASSO with linear measurements.  
- Theoretical results are novel and represent the first precise characterization for this problem.  
- Broad applicability across various regularizers and link functions is a key strength.  
- Practical significance is high, with direct design implications for quantizers in compressive sensing.  
- Authors carefully acknowledge and discuss limitations of their assumptions.  
- Presentation clarity is a minor weakness: main results (Theorem 2.2) are dense and could be made more accessible.  
- Assumption 1 needs clearer, more formal interpretation using variational analysis.  
- Terminology errors: Generalized LASSO called an “algorithm” instead of a convex program.  
- Notation error: “proximal function” is actually the Moreau envelope, not the proximal operator.  
- Minor formatting and typographical issues (e.g., Figure 1 caption LaTeX formatting, typo on page 5).  
- Overall quality is excellent, originality strong, significance high, and reviewer recommends acceptance.
