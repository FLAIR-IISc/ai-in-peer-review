- Paper extends precise asymptotic characterization of Generalized LASSO to non-linear measurements yi = gi(ai^T x0), showing equivalence to linear measurements with scaled signal μx0 and additive noise σz.  
- Provides explicit formulas μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1).  
- Main contribution: precise asymptotic error expressions for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements for all λ and many structured signals (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).  
- Numerical experiments (Figures 1–2) support the theoretical predictions.  
- Builds on and extends CGMT-based analysis to handle non-linear measurement models.  
- Shows optimal 1-bit quantizer is the Lloyd–Max quantizer, giving theoretical justification for a common heuristic.  
- Quality: technically rigorous with detailed proofs (Theorems 2.1–2.3) and strong theoretical support.  
- Clarity strength: well-written and organized, with clear presentation of main results.  
- Clarity weakness: the central equivalence between single-index and linear measurements is deferred to Appendix A and not justified in the main text.  
- Originality: first to give asymptotically precise results for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements; extends prior work (Brillinger 1982; Plan & Vershynin 2013).  
- Significance: results have important theoretical and practical implications (e.g., quantization, censored measurements).  
- Practical relevance weakness: paper does not discuss whether findings favor l2-LASSO over l2^2-LASSO in practice despite implications for oracle regularization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically strong, well-supported by proofs and experiments, and impactful for applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient emphasis/justification of the key equivalence in main text and lacking discussion on practical choice between LASSO variants.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to move/justify the key equivalence into the main text and address practical implications.
