- Main result: asymptotically exact characterization of ℓ2-LASSO error under single-index measurements via a Nonlinear = Linear reduction.
- Effective linear surrogate: nonlinear measurements behave like scaled linear measurements with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise variance σ2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)2].
- Exact risk given by a scalar convex program; specializes to simple formulas for sparse and group-sparse cases (e.g., ℓ1 results in eqs. 14–15).
- Extends Brillinger (1982) to high-dimensional regime and strengthens Plan & Vershynin by providing exact asymptotic limits.
- Allows general priors on x0 with bounded moments and multiple convex regularizers (ℓ1, ℓ1,2, nuclear norm), relaxing Gaussian-nonzero assumptions.
- Attractive application: quantizer design reduces to minimizing σ2/μ2, with Lloyd–Max stationary points solving this surrogate.
- Technical quality: strong, CGMT/AMP-consistent analysis with simulations supporting predictions across link functions and regularizers.
- Assumption 1 (Moreau envelope limit F) is abstract but reasonable; verified for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2, sketched for nuclear norm—needs more detail.
- Key limitation: requires μ ≠ 0 (discusses implications for direction vs. scale recovery).
- Analysis restricted to i.i.d. Gaussian A; extensions to subgaussian or correlated designs are not covered.
- Clarity: well written and organized; contributions and empirical figures are informative.
- Suggestion: promote the Nonlinear = Linear theorem from Appendix A to the main text as a named theorem tied to Brillinger.
- Suggestion: collate all assumptions (on g, μ, σ, x0 normalization) in one place and clarify randomness in g and expectations.
- Originality: first asymptotically exact error analysis of LASSO-type estimators under general single-index measurements.
- Significance: unifying principle allowing use of linear-model formulas for many nonlinearities; likely to impact follow-on work.
- Practical impact: provides actionable insight for quantized compressive sensing via Lloyd–Max connection.
- Pros: conceptually clean equivalence, covers multiple structures, relaxes prior assumptions, and aligns with precise-analysis literature.
- Cons/questions: equivalence theorem relegated to appendix; request to elevate and connect to Brillinger explicitly.
- Cons/questions: limited practical guidance on choosing λ and limited experiments comparing ℓ2-LASSO vs ℓ22-LASSO under unknown g/σ.
- Cons/questions: need more concrete treatment or numerical recipe for Assumption 1/F in the nuclear norm/low-rank case.
- Cons/questions: scope limited to Gaussian A—comment on potential extensions requested.
- Suggested improvements: move core theorem to Section 2 with proof sketch; add section on tuning λ and data-driven strategies; discuss ℓ2 vs ℓ22 preferences.
- Suggested improvements: include a concrete low-rank example (e.g., spiked model) with computed F and validation plot.
- Suggested improvements: in quantization section, run experiments optimizing (t, ℓ) via Lloyd–Max for several q and compare to baselines.
- Minor comments: clarify expectations in eq. (3) and note that even g (μ = 0) yields only direction recovery.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant contribution; with suggested clarifications and added experiments it would be a valuable NIPS paper.
