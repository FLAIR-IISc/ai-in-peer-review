1. Main contribution: Provides a precise asymptotic analysis showing square-root LASSO on single-index (non-linear) measurements is equivalent to a linear model with scaled signal and Gaussian noise.  
2. Equivalence detail: The non-linear problem maps to yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with μ and σ determined solely by the link function g.  
3. Exact error formulas: Derives deterministic, asymptotically exact MSE expressions (Theorem 2.2) depending on dimensions, regularizer, signal structure, μ, and σ.  
4. Generality: Framework handles a wide class of convex regularizers and relaxes Gaussian priors on non-zero signal entries.  
5. Extensions/applications: Applies results to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery, and to optimal quantizer design.  
6. Theoretical significance: Moves beyond non-asymptotic bounds to the first asymptotically exact characterization for this setting.  
7. Practical insight (implicit): Suggests that simple linear estimators remain effective under non-linear distortions via the μ/σ mapping.  
8. Empirical validation: Simulation results (Figures 1 and 2) strongly corroborate theoretical predictions.  
9. Writing quality: Paper is well-written, clearly positioned relative to prior work, and theorems are precisely stated.  
10. Weakness — proof placement: The central "non-linear = linear" justification is deferred to the appendix; a high-level proof sketch should be in the main text.  
11. Weakness — missed practical discussion: Fails to emphasize that square-root LASSO’s λ independence from noise variance makes it especially practical here, since σ from g need not be estimated.  
12. Originality: Novel and significant — first asymptotically precise error formulas for regularized estimators in the single-index model, and novel extension beyond Gaussian priors.  
13. Overall evaluation: High quality, clarity, originality, and significance; recommend adding the suggested high-level proof intuition and practical discussion on choosing l2 vs l2^2 LASSO.
