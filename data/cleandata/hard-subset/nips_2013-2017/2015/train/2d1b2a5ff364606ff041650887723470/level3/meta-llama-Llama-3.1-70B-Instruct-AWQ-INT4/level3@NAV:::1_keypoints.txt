1. Derives asymptotic guarantees for the Generalized LASSO on Gaussian measurements possibly passed through an unknown nonlinear function, giving a precise performance characterization.  
2. Leverages the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) to obtain tight bounds and rigorous asymptotic analysis.  
3. Shows that performance with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to linear measurements up to a scaling factor and effective noise variance determined by the link function.  
4. Provides an exact characterization of the limit of the squared error for the Generalized LASSO.  
5. Validates theoretical predictions with simulations, demonstrating good empirical agreement.  
6. Extends prior work (e.g., Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) to the nonlinear measurement setting.  
7. Applies results to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank matrix recovery, supplying precise error predictions for these problems.  
8. Paper is well-written and situates itself clearly within the existing literature.  
9. Lacks sufficient discussion of practical implications and guidance for applying the results in real-world settings.  
10. Would benefit from more comparisons with other commonly used methods in practice.  
11. Needs additional details on computational complexity for the algorithms beyond the Generalized LASSO.  
12. Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; Rating 8/10; Confidence 8/10.
