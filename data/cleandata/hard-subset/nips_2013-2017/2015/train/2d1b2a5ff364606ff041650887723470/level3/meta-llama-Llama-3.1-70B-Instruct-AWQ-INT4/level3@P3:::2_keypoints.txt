- Extends precise asymptotic characterization of the l2-lasso (square-root lasso) to single-index models and relaxes Gaussian prior assumption on x0.  
- Shows l2-lasso treats non-linear measurements as scaled noisy linear measurements with scale µ and noise variance σ^2.  
- Main result (Theorem 2.1) relates Generalized LASSO error under non-linear measurements to error under appropriately scaled noisy linear measurements.  
- Theorem 2.2 gives an asymptotically exact expression for the error, improving on prior results.  
- Provides practical examples: sparse recovery, group-sparse recovery, and low-rank matrix recovery.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear introduction, literature discussion, and organized technical sections.  
- Technical claims are sound and well-supported by theoretical analysis.  
- Clarity is high; technical sections are easy to follow and examples aid understanding.  
- Originality is strong due to the single-index extension and relaxed prior assumptions.  
- Results are significant and potentially impactful for signal processing and ML.  
- Potential weakness: assumes unit Euclidean norm for x0, which may be unrealistic despite justification.  
- Potential weakness: lacks clear comparison with other methods (e.g., l2^2-lasso).  
- Note: l2-lasso’s oracle regularization parameter does not depend on noise variance, a practical advantage.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons and the unit-norm assumption.  
- Overall scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
