- Extends statistical performance analysis of the 2-LASSO to single-index models, removing the Gaussian prior assumption.  
- Derives precise asymptotic estimation-error formulas for the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements.  
- Shows equivalence of performance to scaled and noisy linear measurements, connecting single-index and linear models.  
- Suggests this single-index ↔ linear-model connection should be stated explicitly in the main text for clarity.  
- Provides rigorous results that quantify error dependence on the regularizer, measurement matrix, and link function.  
- Validates theoretical predictions with numerical experiments that align well with theory.  
- Applies the theory to quantized compressive sensing and derives optimal quantization thresholds/levels via the Lloyd–Max algorithm.  
- Requests clearer practical justification for choosing the 2-LASSO over the 2²-LASSO, especially regarding noise-variance independence.  
- Notes the paper is well-written but dense; recommends improving accessibility and moving key insights earlier.  
- Strengths: novel rigorous theory, clear non-linear/linear connection, practical quantization application, and experimental validation.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient practical motivation for estimator choice, insufficient prominence of the single-index/linear connection, and dense theoretical exposition.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to address clarity and practical-motivation issues.
