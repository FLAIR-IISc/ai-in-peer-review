- Paper gives an asymptotically exact MSE characterization for Generalized LASSO with unknown non-linear measurements.
- Shows equivalence: non-linear measurements y_i = g(a_i^T x0) behave like linear noisy measurements μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i for Gaussian A, with μ,σ from g.
- Derives explicit MSE formulas depending on (m,n), regularizer, and signal structure.
- Builds on and significantly strengthens prior bounds by Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin.
- Framework is general and applies to convex regularizers (ℓ1, group ℓ1, nuclear norm).
- Validated empirically for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery with strong agreement to theory.
- Presents a compelling application to q-bit compressive sensing and links optimal quantizer design to minimizing σ²/μ² via Lloyd–Max.
- Strength: novel and significant theoretical contribution mapping a hard non-linear problem to a well-understood linear one.
- Strength: technically rigorous analysis producing precise, computable error formulas.
- Strength: practical impact through the quantizer design application and clear empirical support.
- Weakness: presentation is dense and some technical sections (e.g., Assumption 1) lack intuitive explanation.
- Weakness: minor terminological imprecision (calling Generalized LASSO an “algorithm” rather than an estimator/convex program).
- Detailed suggestions: clarify equivalence of sqrt-LASSO and ℓ2-penalized LASSO via λ mapping; give intuition/variational context for Assumption 1.
- Detailed suggestions: use consistent terminology for Moreau envelope vs proximal operator; fix Figure 1 caption formatting and minor typos.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, substantial contribution with strong empirical validation — reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
