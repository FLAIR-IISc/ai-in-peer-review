- Novel result: characterizes Generalized LASSO performance with non-linear measurements.
- Main contribution: explicit asymptotic expression for estimation error, showing equivalence to a linear measurement model up to a constant.
- Methods: analysis combines Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) and Approximate Message Passing (AMP).
- Error expression accounts for non-linear measurement function, regularizer, and signal structure.
- Practical implication: guides optimal quantization by minimizing measurement-noise variance divided by squared measurement mean.
- Writing: paper is well-written and organized but contains numerous typos, especially in proofs.
- Related work: reviewer unfamiliar with single-index model and sufficient-dimension-reduction literature and may have missed prior art.
- Strengths: novel/interesting result, explicit error characterization, important implications for compressive sensing quantization.
- Weaknesses: many typos in proofs; potential omission of relevant prior work.
- Originality: novel use of CGMT and AMP to obtain precise asymptotic guarantees.
- Significance: valuable contribution with meaningful impact on compressive sensing design.
