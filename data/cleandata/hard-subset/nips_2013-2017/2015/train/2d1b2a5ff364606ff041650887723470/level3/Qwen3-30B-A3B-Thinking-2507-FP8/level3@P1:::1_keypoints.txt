- Problem studied: estimating structured signals from non-linear measurements using the Generalized LASSO.
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 2.1): for Gaussian measurement matrices, non-linear measurements yi = gi(ai^T x0) are asymptotically equivalent to linear measurements yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].
- Consequence: existing precise error analyses for LASSO with linear measurements can be applied to the non-linear case.
- Precise asymptotic error characterization (Theorem 2.2): limiting error given by a deterministic convex optimization problem.
- Novelty: first known precise asymptotic characterization of Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements.
- Applicability: framework demonstrated for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery with numerical validation.
- Practical implication: optimal quantizer for minimizing estimation error is the Lloyd–Max quantizer; relevant to q-bit compressive sensing.
- Technical strength: rigorous proofs using CGMT and careful treatment of the asymptotic regime m/n = δ ∈ (0,∞).
- Presentation: well-written, clearly organized, with effective figures illustrating theory.
- Weakness: numerous typos and inconsistent indices/subscripts in proofs that may distract or reduce confidence.
- Possible omission: reviewer less familiar with single-index model literature, so some prior work might be missing from references.
- Recommendation: strong, original, and practically relevant contribution warranting acceptance pending careful proofreading to fix typos.
