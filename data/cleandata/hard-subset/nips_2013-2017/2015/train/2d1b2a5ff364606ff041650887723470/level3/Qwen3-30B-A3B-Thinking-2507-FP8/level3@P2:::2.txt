

**Review for "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"**

**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper extends the precise asymptotic characterization of the Generalized LASSO (LASSO with structured signals) to the case where measurements are non-linear, i.e., yi = gi(ai^T x0) where gi are non-linear link functions. The authors demonstrate that the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO with such non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to the error when measurements are linear with a scaled signal (μx0) and additive noise (σz), where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1). This generalizes previous work by Brillinger (1982), who showed that the least squares solution is consistent up to a constant μ under fixed dimension settings, and Plan & Vershynin (2013), who provided non-asymptotic upper bounds for the constrained LASSO.

The paper's main contribution is establishing precise asymptotic error expressions for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, which hold for all values of the regularization parameter λ and for a wide class of structured signals (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank). A key finding is that the optimal quantizer for 1-bit measurements is the Lloyd-Max quantizer, providing theoretical justification for a widely used heuristic.

**Strengths**

*Quality*: The paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs of the main results (Theorems 2.1-2.3). The authors provide precise asymptotic characterizations that hold for all values of λ, and for a wide class of structured signals. The theoretical results are well-supported by numerical experiments in Figures 1-2. The analysis builds on recent advances in the CGMT framework and extends them to non-linear measurements.

*Clarity*: The paper is well-written and well-organized. The main results are clearly presented with proper motivation. However, the key equivalence between single-index and linear measurements (central to the paper's contribution) is only mentioned in Appendix A, while the main text refers to it as "well-known" without proper justification. This weakens the paper's presentation, as the key insight should be highlighted earlier.

*Originality*: This is a highly original contribution. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first paper to provide asymptotically precise results for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. While Plan & Vershynin (2013) considered the constrained LASSO with non-linear measurements, their results were non-asymptotic upper bounds with unknown constants, whereas this paper provides precise expressions. The extension to non-Gaussian priors for x0 (in contrast to previous work) is also a notable contribution.

*Significance*: The results have significant theoretical and practical implications. The precise error characterization allows for better understanding of the LASSO's behavior with non-linear measurements, which is relevant in many real-world applications (e.g., 1-bit quantization, censored measurements). The finding that the optimal quantizer for 1-bit measurements is the Lloyd-Max quantizer provides a theoretical justification for an empirically successful heuristic.

**Weaknesses**

*Clarity*: The key insight (equivalence between non-linear and linear measurements) is not properly justified in the main text. The authors should move this key equivalence to the main text to strengthen their argument and justify the reference to Brillinger's result.

*Practical Relevance*: The paper does not address whether these results support preferring the l2-LASSO over the l2^2-LASSO in practice. Since the l2-LASSO's oracle regularization parameter does not depend on noise variance, this is a significant practical consideration that the authors should address.

**Arguments for Acceptance**
- The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by providing the first precise asymptotic characterization of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.
- The results are well-supported by rigorous proofs and numerical experiments.
- The paper extends previous work by Brillinger (1982) and Plan & Vershynin (2013) in a non-trivial way.
- The findings have practical implications for 1-bit quantization and other non-linear measurement models.

**Arguments Against Acceptance**
- The key insight (equivalence between non-linear and linear measurements) is not properly justified in the main text.
- The paper does not address whether these results support preferring the l2-LASSO over the l2^2-LASSO in practice.

**Overall Recommendation**

This paper makes a strong theoretical contribution to the understanding of the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements. The main results are technically sound and extend previous work in an important way. The paper is well-written and well-organized, with the exception of the lack of proper justification for the key equivalence in the main text. The authors should address these weaknesses in a revised version. Given the novelty and significance of the results, I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper is highly relevant to NIPS as it advances our understanding of a fundamental problem in high-dimensional statistics with applications to compressed sensing, quantized measurements, and structured signal recovery.
