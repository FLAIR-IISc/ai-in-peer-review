- Paper introduces a convex relaxation method to learn the Multinomial Logit (MNL) model from ordinal data with applications in collaborative ranking and bundled choice modeling.
- Authors provide theoretical guarantees: upper bounds on estimation error and a lower bound on the minimax error rate.
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 2.1) assumes the linear measurement vector is normally distributed, which may be a strong/strict assumption needing justification.
- The paper discusses convex regularizers but does not explain how the chosen regularizer relates to the set containing the true signal.
- Equivalence result between linear and nonlinear measurements depends on parameters that characterize estimation hardness; the necessity of these dependencies is unclear.
- The manuscript omits references to related literatures such as single index models and sufficient dimension reduction that study linear estimation under unknown link functions.
- A recent relevant work by Yi et al. on sparse recovery with unknown link functions and equivalence of linear/nonlinear high-dimensional estimation is not discussed.
- Contribution significance: provides a polynomial-time inference algorithm with provable guarantees for learning MNL from ordinal data.
- Strength: clear writing, well-organized structure, and sufficient detail for expert reproduction of results.
- Strength: thorough performance analysis including both upper and lower error bounds.
- Weakness: missing citations to pertinent related work (single index, SDR, Yi et al.).
- Weakness: restrictive normality assumption for measurement vector requires further explanation or relaxation.
- Weakness: unclear whether the parameter dependencies in the equivalence result are necessary or can be improved.
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing missing references and clarifying the normality assumption and dependency necessity.
