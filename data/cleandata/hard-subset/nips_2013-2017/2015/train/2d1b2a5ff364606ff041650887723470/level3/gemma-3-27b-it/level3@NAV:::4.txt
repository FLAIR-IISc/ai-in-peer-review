## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the asymptotic estimation error for linear estimation with arbitrary nonlinear link functions, utilizing the Generalized LASSO. The core contribution lies in extending the results of Plan & Vershynin to scenarios where the expectation of the link function (µ) is non-zero, demonstrating that the asymptotic error is remarkably identical for both linear and nonlinear link functions. The authors provide precise expressions for this error, a significant advance over existing bounds.

The work is well-written and presents a fairly interesting result, building upon a strong foundation in high-dimensional statistics and sparse recovery. The paper clearly articulates the motivation and situates its contribution within the broader context of single-index models and compressed sensing. The inclusion of the application to q-bit compressive sensing further illustrates the practical relevance of the findings, especially regarding optimal quantizer design. 

However, the paper does have some weaknesses. The Gaussian assumption on the measurement vectors is a significant limitation, and the authors do not sufficiently justify *why* this assumption is necessary. Exploring the robustness of the results to other matrix ensembles would strengthen the work. The choice of the regularizer parameter (λ) based on the set K containing x0 is a practical challenge not fully addressed. While the paper discusses convex regularizers, it lacks guidance on how to select an appropriate λ in practice. 

Furthermore, the dependence of the theorem on µ and σ, characterizing the difficulty of the link function, seems potentially strong. It would be beneficial to discuss whether these dependences are truly necessary or if tighter bounds could be achieved under more general conditions. The literature review is also incomplete. Important citations are missing, specifically work on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, such as those by Ichimura (2006) and Li & Duan (1989), which have long studied linear/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. Moreover, the recent work by Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, demonstrating equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation even when µ = 0, should be discussed and contrasted with the current findings.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound result extending existing work.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Potentially significant implications for applications like q-bit compressive sensing.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Strong Gaussian assumption needs further justification.
*   Lack of guidance on regularizer selection.
*   Incomplete literature review.



**Overall:** While the paper presents valuable theoretical contributions, addressing the limitations regarding the Gaussian assumption and literature review would significantly strengthen the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on addressing the points raised above.
