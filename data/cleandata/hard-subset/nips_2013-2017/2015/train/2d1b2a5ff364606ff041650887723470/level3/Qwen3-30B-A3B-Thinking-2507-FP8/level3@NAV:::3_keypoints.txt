- Significant contribution: analyzes behavior of generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements, extending Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin.  
- Provides precise asymptotic error formulas for measurements yi = g(ai^T x0).  
- Key insight: non-linear measurements can be treated as noisy linear ones with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1).  
- Derives explicit expressions for estimation error ‖x̂ - μx0‖2 valid for all regularization parameters λ and many regularizers (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).  
- Technically sound and builds upon, significantly extending, prior theoretical work.  
- Strong empirical validation across Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm cases.  
- Compelling application to q-bit compressive sensing showing the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal.  
- Originality: first precise explicit error results in this non-linear measurement context.  
- Writing is generally good but has clarity issues: fix Figure 1 caption, a typo on p.5 line 247, label "proximal function" as Moreau envelope, treat generalized Lasso as a convex program, clarify regularized vs. penalized equivalence, and give a formal variational interpretation of Assumption 1.  
- Overall: valuable advancement for high-dimensional statistics and compressed sensing, meeting quality criteria.
