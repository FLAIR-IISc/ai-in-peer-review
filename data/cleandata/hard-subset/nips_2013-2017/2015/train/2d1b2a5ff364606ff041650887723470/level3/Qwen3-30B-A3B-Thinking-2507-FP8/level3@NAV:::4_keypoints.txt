- Main contribution: Theorem 2.1 shows the asymptotic error of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements equals that of a linear model y = μ a^T x0 + σ z, with μ = E[γ g(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].
- Derived precise error expressions for structured recovery problems (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank), extending previous linear-measurement results.
- Extends Plan & Vershynin’s work by providing the first precise characterization of Generalized LASSO performance under non-linear measurements.
- Technical quality is strong, with rigorous asymptotic analysis using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework.
- Practical relevance: results have important implications for applications such as 1-bit quantization.
- Concern: The Gaussian measurement assumption in Theorem 2.1 is not justified and its necessity is unclear.
- Concern: The paper does not address how to choose the regularizer f based on the structure set K containing x0, limiting practical implementation guidance.
- Concern: The role and necessity of the parameters μ and σ (which characterize the link function difficulty) are not adequately discussed or justified.
- Missing related work: the single-index model literature (e.g., Li & Duan 1985; semiparametric least squares) is not cited.
- Missing related work: recent work by Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, especially relevance when μ=0, is omitted.
- Overall assessment: theoretically significant and well-executed, but omissions and unresolved assumptions reduce the paper’s impact; addressing these would strengthen both theory and practical relevance.
