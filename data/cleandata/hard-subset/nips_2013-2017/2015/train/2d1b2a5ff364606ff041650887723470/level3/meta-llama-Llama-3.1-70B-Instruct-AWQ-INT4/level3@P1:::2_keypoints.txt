- Extends precise asymptotic characterization of the l2-lasso (square-root lasso) to single-index models.  
- Relaxes the usual Gaussian prior assumption on x0, increasing applicability to real-world data.  
- Shows l2-lasso is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise, a crucial theoretical insight.  
- Notes equivalence to linear models was previously established by Brillinger (1982), a reference overlooked in the main text.  
- Equivalence result is relegated to Appendix A and should be moved or more fully explained in the main text.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses precise mathematical notation.  
- Oracle regularization parameter does not depend on noise variance, raising practical questions about choosing l2-lasso vs l2^2-lasso.  
- Authors should discuss the practical relevance and comparative preference of l2-lasso over l2^2-lasso.  
- Results have significant potential impact on compressed sensing and signal processing algorithms.  
- With minor revisions (clarify equivalence and practical implications), the paper could become a seminal contribution.  
- Strengths: novel theoretical extension, relaxed prior assumptions, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: need for clearer exposition of equivalence and discussion of practical implications for regularization choice.
