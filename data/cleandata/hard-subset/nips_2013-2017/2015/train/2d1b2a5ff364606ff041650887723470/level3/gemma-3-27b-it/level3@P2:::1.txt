This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO for estimating a structured signal from measurements corrupted by a nonlinear link function. The authors demonstrate that, under certain conditions (specifically, i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix), the performance of the Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with scaled and noisy observations. They derive precise expressions for the estimation error, providing a significant improvement over existing bounds and offering insights into the role of the link function, regularizer, and regularization parameter. A particularly interesting consequence is the identification of the optimal quantizer for minimizing estimation error.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a practical problem that arises in scenarios with nonlinear measurement devices or intentionally designed nonlinear measurements (e.g., 1-bit quantization). The main contribution – the precise characterization of the estimation error – is significant and provides a strong theoretical foundation for understanding the behavior of the Generalized LASSO in this context. The connection to the linear measurement case is insightful and allows leveraging existing results for analysis. The application to q-bit compressive sensing and the derivation of the optimal quantizer are also valuable.

However, the paper is not without its weaknesses. While the authors acknowledge their unfamiliarity with the single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature, it is possible that relevant prior work exists within these fields that is not adequately addressed. A more thorough survey of these areas would strengthen the originality claim. While the authors cite Brillinger [5] and Plan & Vershynin [17], a more detailed comparison with related results in these papers and their limitations would be beneficial. 

The writing quality is generally good, but numerous typos and minor errors, particularly within the proofs, detract from the overall presentation. A careful proofreading pass is strongly recommended. Furthermore, the presentation could be improved by explicitly stating the assumptions more clearly and concisely at the beginning of each section. The notation is reasonably consistent, but the frequent use of asymptotic notation (e.g., “P→”) requires careful attention from the reader.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Contribution:** Derivation of precise asymptotic error bounds for the Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements.
*   **Insightful Connection to Linear Case:** Demonstrating equivalence to a scaled and noisy linear measurement model simplifies analysis and allows leveraging existing results.
*   **Practical Application:**  Application to q-bit compressive sensing and identification of the optimal quantizer.
*   **Well-Motivated Problem:** Addresses a relevant problem arising in various signal processing and machine learning applications.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Potential Overlook of Related Work:** Limited discussion of the single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature.
*   **Typographical Errors:** Numerous typos and minor errors, particularly in the proofs.
*   **Presentation:** Could be improved with clearer statement of assumptions and more concise notation.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and significant theoretical result with a clear impact on the field.
*   The derived error bounds are precise and provide valuable insights into the behavior of the Generalized LASSO.
*   The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates the practical relevance of the work.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The potential oversight of related work in the single-index model/sufficient dimension reduction literature weakens the originality claim.
*   The numerous typographical errors detract from the overall quality and readability of the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the weaknesses, I believe the paper’s strengths outweigh its flaws. The theoretical contribution is substantial, and the results are likely to be of interest to a wide audience within the NIPS community. However, addressing the typographical errors and thoroughly investigating the related literature are crucial for enhancing the paper’s quality and impact.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Conduct a more comprehensive literature review, particularly focusing on the single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature.
2.  Thoroughly proofread the paper to eliminate all typographical errors.
3.  Consider clarifying the assumptions and notation for improved readability.
