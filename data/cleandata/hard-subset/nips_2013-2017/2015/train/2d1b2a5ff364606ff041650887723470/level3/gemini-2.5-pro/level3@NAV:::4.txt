This paper studies the asymptotic estimation error for structured signal recovery using the generalized LASSO with arbitrary nonlinear link functions. The work builds upon classical results by Brillinger and more recent work by Plan & Vershynin, extending their analysis to provide precise asymptotic characterizations where previous results offered only upper bounds. The central claim is that, under i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, the estimation performance of the LASSO with a nonlinear link function is asymptotically identical to its performance with an equivalent linear model, yi = μaix0 + σzi. The constants μ and σ depend only on the link function, providing an elegant and powerful equivalence principle.

The paper is well-written, and its main theoretical contribution—a precise, deterministic formula for the asymptotic error—is a significant strength. The analysis successfully moves beyond loose bounds to offer an exact characterization, which is a valuable addition to the theoretical understanding of these models.

However, several aspects of the paper require clarification and further discussion. First, the reliance on Gaussian measurement vectors is a key assumption, and the authors should elaborate on why this is necessary for the analysis. A discussion on whether these results could extend to broader sub-Gaussian ensembles would be beneficial. Second, from a practical standpoint, the paper discusses the use of general convex regularizers but offers no guidance on how to select the optimal regularizer based on the structure set K containing the ground truth signal x0. Addressing this selection problem would enhance the paper's applicability. Additionally, the theorem’s dependence on the parameters µ and σ, which characterize the difficulty of the link function, is central; the authors should comment on whether these dependencies are fundamental or an artifact of the chosen analysis.

Most critically, the paper is missing key citations that impact its originality and significance. The authors should connect their work to the broader literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction. More specifically, they seem unaware of recent and highly relevant work by Yi et al. ("Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear transform," 2015), which also establishes an equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation and addresses sparse recovery even in the challenging µ = 0 case. The current manuscript must clearly differentiate its contributions from this prior work.

In summary, while the paper presents an interesting theoretical result, its novelty is questionable in light of existing literature. Major revisions are needed to address the questions raised and properly contextualize its contributions.
