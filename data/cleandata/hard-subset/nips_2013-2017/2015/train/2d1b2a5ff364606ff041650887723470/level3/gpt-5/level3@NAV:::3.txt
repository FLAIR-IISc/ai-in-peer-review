Summary and relation to prior work: This paper analyzes generalized Lasso estimators under non-linear measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian design. Building on Brillinger’s classical LS consistency (fixed n, m→∞) and the non-asymptotic bounds of Plan and Vershynin for constrained programs, the authors prove an asymptotically exact equivalence: in high dimensions the generalized Lasso under non-linear observations behaves as if the data were linear with effective parameters μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2]. They then derive precise asymptotic squared-error formulas for penalized generalized Lasso with broad convex regularizers, covering Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm. Empirically, they validate the predictions and provide an insightful application to quantizer design, showing that optimizing σ^2/μ^2 recovers a Lloyd–Max-type solution for q-bit CS.

Quality: The work is technically solid and carefully executed. The asymptotically exact characterization, together with extensive simulations across sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank settings, convincingly supports the claims. The results sharpen prior bounds and unify several special cases.

Clarity: The paper is well written but somewhat unbalanced: main ideas are clear, yet space constraints push essential technical detail (e.g., the role of F in Theorem 2.2) into appendices. Please avoid referring to the generalized Lasso as an “algorithm”; call it a convex program or decoder. The object labeled “proximal function” is the Moreau envelope of index τ; please change notation since prox commonly denotes the proximal operator. Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal variational-analysis interpretation (e.g., epi-convergence/Moreau envelope limits). Minor edits: Figure 1 caption formatting: $n=768$. Typo on p.5, line 247 (“When ...”). Regularized (penalized) and constrained formulations are equivalent under an appropriate parameter-radius mapping; please comment explicitly. Some notation could be streamlined to connect more directly to AMP/CGMT literature.

Originality: High. The explicit asymptotic error expressions for penalized estimators under non-linear measurements appear new and extend the Plan–Vershynin program from bounds to precise formulas, including group Lasso and nuclear norm.

Significance: High. The equivalence principle and closed-form performance characterization should impact theory and practice in high-dimensional inference and quantizer design.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Precise, novel theory extending influential prior work
- Broad applicability (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm)
- Strong empirical validation and impactful quantizer-design application

Arguments against acceptance:
- Restricted to Gaussian designs and μ≠0
- Assumption 1 needs clearer formalization
- Some presentation imbalances due to space constraints
