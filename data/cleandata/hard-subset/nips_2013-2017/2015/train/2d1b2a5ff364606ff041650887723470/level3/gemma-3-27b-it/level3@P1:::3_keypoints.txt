- Core contribution: shows asymptotically that Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to LASSO on scaled/noisy linear measurements and provides precise error formulas.  
- Theoretical advancement: derives explicit asymptotic estimation-error expressions that extend and refine prior results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin).  
- Setting: analysis focuses on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices and single-index model link functions.  
- Empirical validation: experiments across sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery convincingly match theory.  
- Practical application: demonstrates relevance to q-bit compressive sensing and connects to Lloyd–Max quantizer design.  
- Literature positioning: clearly situates work within single-index models, generalized LASSO, and recent LASSO analyses (Donoho, Stojnic, Thrampoulidis, etc.).  
- Technical soundness: mathematical derivations appear rigorous and make substantive technical contributions (Moreau envelope, Fenchel conjugate use).  
- Accessibility concern: presentation is dense and assumes familiarity with high-dimensional statistics, likely limiting accessibility.  
- Assumption issue: Assumption 1 (for Theorem 2.2) needs clearer formal interpretation and justification, possibly via variational-analysis references.  
- Terminology/notation issues: calling Generalized LASSO an “algorithm” is imprecise (should be convex program/decoder); “proximal function” vs. “prox” notation could be clarified.  
- Minor errors: formatting problem in Figure 1 caption ($n=768$) and a typo on page 5 (line 247) need correction.  
- Recommendation: strong paper with significant theoretical and practical merits; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity and notation issues.
