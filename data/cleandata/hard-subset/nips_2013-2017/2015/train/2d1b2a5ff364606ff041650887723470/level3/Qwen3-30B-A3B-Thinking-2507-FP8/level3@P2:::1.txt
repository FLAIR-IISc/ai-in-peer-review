

# Review for NIPS 2015

This paper addresses the problem of estimating structured signals from non-linear measurements using the Generalized LASSO. The authors show that for Gaussian measurement matrices and under certain asymptotic conditions, the performance of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = gi(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear measurement model yi = μ(ai^T x0) + σzi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1). The main contribution is the derivation of precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error, which had not been previously established in this context.

The paper builds upon the work of Brillinger (1982) and Plan and Vershynin (2013) who established that the LASSO solution is consistent up to a constant of proportionality μ under certain conditions. The authors significantly extend these results by providing precise asymptotic error expressions that hold for all values of the regularization parameter λ and for a wide class of regularizers f and link functions g.

**Strengths:**

*Quality:* The paper provides technically sound theoretical results with rigorous proofs (despite some typos in the proofs). The authors establish a precise asymptotic characterization of the estimation error, which is a significant advance over previous work that provided only upper bounds. The derivation of the precise error formula (Theorem 2.2) and its specialization to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery cases (Theorems 2.3, 2.4, 2.5) demonstrates strong theoretical contribution.

*Originality:* The work represents a significant extension of previous results on the LASSO with linear measurements to the non-linear measurement setting. The equivalence between non-linear and appropriately scaled linear measurements is a novel insight, and the derivation of precise error expressions in this context appears to be the first of its kind. The connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer for optimal q-bit compressive sensing is particularly insightful.

*Significance:* The results have important implications for practical applications where measurements are non-linear (e.g., 1-bit quantization, censored observations). The precise error characterization enables better understanding of the LASSO's behavior in non-linear settings, which is valuable for practitioners and researchers. The connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer provides a concrete, optimal solution for quantization design in compressive sensing.

*Clarity:* The paper is well-written, with clear exposition of the main results and their implications. The presentation is logical and well-organized, with careful definitions of assumptions and theorems. The figures illustrating the accuracy of the error predictions are helpful for understanding the results.

**Weaknesses:**

*Clarity:* Despite being generally well-written, the paper contains numerous typos, especially in the proofs (e.g., "messaurements" in the abstract, "asymptotically" misspelled as "asymptotically" in Section 2.2.1, and several other minor errors). These detract from the paper's professionalism.

*Originality:* The reviewer is unfamiliar with the single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature, and may have missed related prior work. While the paper cites relevant work by Brillinger, Plan and Vershynin, and others, a more comprehensive comparison with the broader literature in these areas would strengthen the paper's positioning.

*Quality:* The paper focuses on the generalized square-root LASSO (min_x ||y - Ax||_2 + λf(x)), but it would be valuable to clarify how the results extend to the standard LASSO (min_x 1/2||y - Ax||_2^2 + λf(x)), which is more commonly used in practice.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- Provides the first precise asymptotic characterization of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements
- Establishes a fundamental equivalence between non-linear and appropriately scaled linear measurements
- Derives practical implications for optimal quantizer design in q-bit compressive sensing
- Well-written with clear exposition of main results and their significance

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- Contains numerous typos that undermine the paper's professionalism
- May not sufficiently distinguish from related work in single-index model literature
- Focuses on the square-root LASSO rather than the more common standard LASSO formulation

Overall, this is a strong theoretical contribution that addresses an important problem in signal recovery with non-linear measurements. The precise asymptotic characterization of the Generalized LASSO's performance is novel and valuable, with clear practical implications. The paper would benefit from a thorough proofreading to eliminate typos, and a more comprehensive comparison with relevant literature in single-index models. Despite these minor weaknesses, the technical contribution is significant and advances the state of the art in structured signal recovery.

I recommend acceptance, as the paper makes a substantial theoretical contribution with clear practical relevance. The minor issues noted are easily addressable in the camera-ready version.
