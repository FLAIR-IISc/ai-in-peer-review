- Addresses estimation of structured signals from nonlinear measurements using the Generalized LASSO.  
- Extends Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin to give asymptotically precise error characterizations for Gaussian measurements and nonlinear link functions.  
- Main contribution: explicit error formulas showing equivalence between nonlinear measurements and appropriately scaled noisy linear measurements.  
- Results are novel and offer significant insights into Generalized LASSO behavior in high-dimensional regimes.  
- Theoretical derivations are rigorous and asymptotic guarantees are carefully derived.  
- Numerical experiments validate the theoretical predictions.  
- Provides practical applications, e.g., optimizing quantization thresholds for compressive sensing.  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized, accessible to mathematically strong readers.  
- Several typos, particularly in proof sections, that could hinder clarity.  
- Reviewer has limited familiarity with single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature, possibly affecting novelty assessment.  
- Originality is high: first-known precise error predictions for Generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements and extension to regularized settings.  
- Recommendation: accept, subject to fixing typos and expanding discussion of related single-index literature.
