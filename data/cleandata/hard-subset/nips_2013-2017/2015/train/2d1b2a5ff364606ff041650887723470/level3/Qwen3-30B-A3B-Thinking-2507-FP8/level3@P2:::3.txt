

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper addresses the problem of estimating a structured signal x₀ ∈ ℝⁿ from non-linear measurements of the form yᵢ = gᵢ(aᵢᵀx₀), where gᵢ are independent copies of a random link function g. The authors study the performance of the Generalized LASSO estimator (minₓ ‖y - Ax‖₂ + λf(x)) when used with such non-linear measurements. The main contribution is a precise asymptotic characterization of the estimation error ‖x̂ - μx₀‖₂², where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ) - μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1). The key insight is that the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements behaves asymptotically like the same algorithm applied to linear measurements y = μAx₀ + σz, where z has i.i.d. standard normal entries.

This work extends the classical results of Brillinger (1982) on least squares estimation with non-linear measurements and the more recent work of Plan and Vershynin (2015) on the constrained Generalized LASSO. While Plan and Vershynin established upper bounds showing that the error is bounded by quantities involving μ and σ, this paper provides precise asymptotic expressions for the error, which are novel in this context. The authors connect their results to recent advances in the precise analysis of the LASSO with linear measurements (e.g., Thrampoulidis et al., 2015; Bayati and Montanari, 2011), demonstrating how their framework generalizes these results to non-linear settings.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and well-executed. The authors establish precise asymptotic error formulas for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements under a Gaussian design, which is a significant extension of prior work. The proofs leverage the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework, which has been successfully applied to linear measurement settings. The paper includes thorough experimental validation across multiple structured signal recovery scenarios (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and demonstrates the accuracy of their theoretical predictions. The application to q-bit compressive sensing quantizer design, where they establish a connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, is particularly insightful.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and well-organized. The main contributions are clearly stated, and the technical exposition is mostly accessible to a NIPS audience. However, there are some minor issues: the caption for Figure 1 incorrectly formats "n=768" instead of using LaTeX notation ($n=768$), and there's a typo on page 5, line 247. The paper would benefit from a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using variational analysis, and the authors should clarify that the Generalized LASSO is a convex program rather than an algorithm. The notation for the Moreau envelope is also confusing since it's typically denoted as the proximal function.

**Originality:** The paper makes a significant original contribution by deriving the first-known precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. While Plan and Vershynin (2015) showed that the error is bounded by quantities involving μ and σ, this paper provides exact expressions that reveal the precise role of these parameters. The connection to Lloyd-Max quantizer design in q-bit compressive sensing is also novel and insightful. The paper clearly differentiates itself from previous work on single-index models and structured signal recovery.

**Significance:** The results have both theoretical and practical significance. Theoretically, they extend the understanding of the LASSO to non-linear measurement models, which are common in real-world applications (e.g., 1-bit quantization, censored measurements). Practically, the precise error characterization allows for better quantizer design in compressive sensing applications, as demonstrated by the connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer. The results also provide guidance for practitioners on how to choose regularization parameters and interpret estimation performance in the presence of non-linearities.

**Strengths:**
- Provides the first precise asymptotic error formulas for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements
- Establishes a clean equivalence between non-linear and appropriately scaled linear measurements
- Demonstrates the connection to Lloyd-Max quantizer in q-bit CS, which is insightful and practical
- Rigorous theoretical analysis supported by comprehensive empirical validation
- Builds naturally on recent advances in LASSO analysis for linear measurements

**Weaknesses:**
- Assumption 1 requires more formal interpretation using variational analysis
- Minor notational inconsistencies (e.g., "proximal function" for Moreau envelope)
- Some space constraints limit the inclusion of all technical details
- The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the relationship between the constrained and regularized LASSO formulations

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by providing precise error formulas for a non-trivial extension of the LASSO framework
- The connection to Lloyd-Max quantizer design is both novel and practically valuable
- The technical approach is sound and builds on well-established frameworks
- The results are well-validated through both theory and experiments

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- The paper's presentation could be improved with better formalization of Assumption 1
- Minor notational issues and formatting inconsistencies
- Some technical details are omitted due to space constraints

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a clear and significant contribution to the field. The theoretical results are novel and well-supported, and the practical implications for quantizer design in compressive sensing are particularly compelling. While there are minor issues with notation and presentation that could be addressed in revision, these do not undermine the paper's core contributions.

I recommend acceptance.
