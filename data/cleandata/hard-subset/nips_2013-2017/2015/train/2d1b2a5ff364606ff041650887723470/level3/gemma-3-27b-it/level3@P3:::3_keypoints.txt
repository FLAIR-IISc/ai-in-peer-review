- Key contribution: shows that under Gaussian design, non-linear measurements behave like noisy linear measurements for the Generalized Lasso, with explicit asymptotic error formulas.
- Builds on prior work (Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin) and significantly extends it by deriving precise error expressions rather than bounds.
- Theoretical result depends on link-function characteristics, regularization parameter, and signal structure.
- Practical application: q-bit compressive sensing example linking optimal design to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.
- Strong mathematical foundation using random matrix theory and convex optimization; proofs and asymptotic analysis are rigorous.
- Novelty: first to provide explicit error formulas for Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements.
- Practical relevance: theoretical findings have clear implications for quantizer design and compressive sensing.
- Writing and organization are clear; motivation, methodology, and results are well explained.
- Literature review is comprehensive and situates the work within relevant prior art.
- Weakness: some derivations are terse and would benefit from additional detail (space constraints suspected).
- Weakness: notation issue—using "prox" for the Moreau envelope is confusing and should be changed.
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (regularizer behavior) lacks a formal variational-analysis interpretation and needs a more precise statement.
- Weakness: calling the Generalized Lasso an "algorithm" is misleading; should be called a convex program/decoder.
- Specific corrections: fix Figure 1 caption formatting, a typo on page 5 line 247, and clarify mapping between regularized and penalized formulations with an equation.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, novel error formulas, relevant q-bit application, clear exposition, and experiments validating theory.
- Arguments against acceptance / concerns: need for more detailed derivations, clearer notation/terminology, and a stronger formal statement of Assumption 1.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worth acceptance with minor revisions addressing notation, terminology, Assumption 1, and some added derivational detail.
