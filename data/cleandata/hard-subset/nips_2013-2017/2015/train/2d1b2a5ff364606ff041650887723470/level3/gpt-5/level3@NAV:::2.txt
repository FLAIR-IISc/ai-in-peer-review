This paper studies generalized LASSO under non-linear single-index measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0), and shows that, with Gaussian design A, the square-root LASSO (ℓ2-LASSO) behaves asymptotically as if the data came from a linear model y = μ A x0 + σ z, with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2]. Building on Brillinger’s classical single-index insights and the Plan–Vershynin constrained formulation, the authors leverage CGMT-style analysis to deliver asymptotically precise error predictions for the regularized estimator with broad convex penalties (sparsity, group sparsity, low rank). They further specialize to sparse recovery (yielding closed-form fixed-point characterizations) and apply the theory to q-bit quantization, recovering Lloyd–Max quantizers as stationary points for minimizing the induced LASSO error. A noteworthy aspect is relaxing the common Gaussian prior on x0: the results hold for general px0 with bounded moments, beyond prior precise analyses that assumed Gaussian nonzeros.

Quality: The analysis appears technically sound and careful. The “non-linear = linear” equivalence is rigorously formalized, and the precise risk formula is convex and computable. Assumption 1 is reasonable and verified for key regularizers; empirical figures align with predictions. Limitations include reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian A, exclusion of μ = 0, and modest finite-sample exploration.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written. However, the Brillinger equivalence (single-index ≡ linear with scaled signal and non-standard noise) is only in Appendix A; it should be stated in the main text and explicitly used to justify the blockquote on line 140. Assumption 1 could be further demystified with an expanded discussion and more worked examples. Please also clarify the treatment of μ = 0 and direction-only identifiability.

Originality: Extending precise asymptotics of ℓ2-LASSO from linear to single-index models with general priors on x0 and general convex penalties is novel and timely, consolidating disparate lines of work (AMP, CGMT, single-index).

Significance: The work gives sharp guidance for performance prediction and design (e.g., quantizer optimization) and connects non-linear sensing to a well-understood linear surrogate, which others can build upon.

Practical relevance: Since the ℓ2-LASSO’s oracle λ is variance-free, the paper should discuss whether the results argue for ℓ2-LASSO over ℓ2^2-LASSO in practice under unknown noise variance, ideally with a small empirical comparison and comments on estimating μ, σ when g is unknown.

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong, general, and precise theory; unifies prior results
- Broad applicability to common structures and penalties
- Useful design insight for quantization

Cons for acceptance:
- Gaussian-design restriction; limited finite-sample/robustness evidence
- Key equivalence buried in appendix; Assumption 1 needs more intuition
- Limited practical guidance on choosing ℓ2 vs ℓ2^2 and on estimating μ, σ
