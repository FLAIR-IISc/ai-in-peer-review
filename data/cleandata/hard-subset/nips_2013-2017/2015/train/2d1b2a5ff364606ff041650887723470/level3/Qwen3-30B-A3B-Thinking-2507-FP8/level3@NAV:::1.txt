

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO for estimating structured signals from non-linear measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), where the link function g is unknown and potentially nonlinear. The main contribution is deriving precise asymptotic guarantees for the estimation error, showing that the performance of the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements is equivalent to that under appropriately scaled linear measurements with parameters μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ) - μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1). The authors establish a precise error formula (Theorem 2.2) that characterizes the estimation performance in the asymptotic regime where m/n = δ, and demonstrate its applicability to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery problems.

The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by extending previous work on the single-index model (Brillinger, Plan and Vershynin) to provide the first asymptotically precise characterization of the Generalized LASSO's performance under non-linear measurements. The results are technically sound, with rigorous proofs that build on recent advances in convex optimization and high-dimensional statistics. The authors effectively connect their findings to practical applications, such as demonstrating that the Lloyd-Max quantizer minimizes estimation error in q-bit compressive sensing.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with thorough discussion of related work. However, it contains numerous typos, particularly in the proofs (e.g., missing parentheses, inconsistent notation), which detract from readability. While the reviewer is unfamiliar with the single-index model literature and may have missed some related prior work, the paper's technical contributions appear novel within the context of the NIPS community.

Strengths: High theoretical quality, significant extension of prior work, precise error characterization, broad applicability across structured signal recovery problems.

Weaknesses: Typos in proofs (though this is a minor issue), limited discussion of practical implementation challenges.

The paper advances the state of the art in understanding the behavior of LASSO-type estimators under non-linear measurements, with clear implications for signal processing and machine learning applications. The results provide both theoretical insights and practical guidance for measurement design. The contribution is original, significant, and well-executed, making it a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
