## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO for estimating signals from measurements corrupted by unknown non-linear functions. The core contribution is deriving asymptotically precise expressions for the estimation error, demonstrating that, surprisingly, the non-linear measurement problem can be effectively treated as a linear one with appropriately scaled measurements and added noise. This is a significant extension of prior work by Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin. 

The paper is well-written and clearly presents its theoretical results. The authors convincingly build their argument, starting from established results and progressively refining them to address the non-linear measurement scenario. The connection to the single-index model literature is also appropriately noted. The inclusion of applications to q-bit compressive sensing, and the demonstration of how the results can be used to optimize quantization schemes, further strengthens the paper's practical relevance.

However, my understanding of the single-index model/sufficient dimension reduction literature is limited, and it is possible that the authors haven’t fully captured the breadth of related work in those fields. While references are provided, the field is extensive. Furthermore, while the proofs seem logically sound, the manuscript contains a considerable number of typos, particularly within the proofs themselves, which detract from the overall clarity and polish. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Result:** The precise characterization of estimation performance is a valuable contribution to the field.
*   **Practical Implications:** The equivalence to a linear model simplifies analysis and allows for the application of existing tools. The q-bit compressive sensing application is a compelling example.
*   **Well-Written:** The paper is generally clear and logically structured.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Potential Oversight of Related Work:** Limited familiarity with the single-index model literature might mean relevant prior work is underrepresented.
*   **Numerous Typos:** The abundance of typos, especially in the mathematical derivations, impacts readability and raises concerns about attention to detail.
*   **Reliance on Gaussianity:** The assumption of Gaussian entries in the measurement matrix is restrictive, though common in the field.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the presence of typos and potential gaps in related work coverage, the paper presents a novel and significant theoretical result with potential for practical impact. While not a perfect manuscript, the strengths outweigh the weaknesses. I lean towards **acceptance**, but strongly recommend the authors thoroughly proofread and address the identified typos, and expand their literature review to ensure thoroughness.
