- Main contribution: Theorem 2.1 shows Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements asymptotically equals LASSO with scaled noisy linear measurements (μ and σ defined).  
- Precise error characterization: Theorem 2.2 provides exact asymptotic error formulas generalizing linear-measurement results to the non-linear setting.  
- Extends prior work: Builds on Plan & Vershynin (2013) and Brillinger (1980), extending to the general μ ≠ 0 case with precise asymptotics.  
- Applicability: Results apply to sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery and q-bit compressive sensing; shows optimality of Lloyd–Max quantizer in that context.  
- Technical quality: Paper is technically sound, proofs are rigorous (deferred to appendix), and assumptions (e.g., unit-norm x0) are stated.  
- Presentation: Well-written and well-organized with clear intuition and effective figures illustrating theoretical predictions.  
- Originality: Novel extension providing the first precise asymptotic error analysis for generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements when μ ≠ 0.  
- Significance: Important practical implication that non-linear measurements can be treated as scaled noisy linear ones, impacting measurement design and recovery methods.  
- Missing justification: Assumes Gaussian measurement vectors without arguing whether results extend to other ensembles or if Gaussianity is critical.  
- Regularizer selection: Fails to address practical question of how to choose the convex regularizer based on the structure/set K containing x0.  
- Dependence on link parameters: Does not discuss whether dependence on μ and σ is intrinsic or an artifact of the analysis framework.  
- Missing citations: Omits relevant literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction (e.g., Li & Duan 1985) and semiparametric least squares.  
- Missing related work: Omits recent work by Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions and sparse recovery when μ = 0.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions to add missing citations and address regularizer-selection and Gaussian-assumption concerns.
