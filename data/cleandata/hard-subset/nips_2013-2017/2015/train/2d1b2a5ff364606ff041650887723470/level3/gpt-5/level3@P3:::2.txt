Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper studies high-dimensional estimation under single-index (nonlinear) measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0), where A has i.i.d. standard normal entries and x0 is structured and high-dimensional. The authors analyze the generalized LASSO with an ℓ2 data-fit (square-root LASSO) and a convex regularizer f and show:

- Nonlinear = linear equivalence: asymptotically, the estimation error of the LASSO with nonlinear measurements equals that under a linear model y = μAx0 + σz, with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2], γ ∼ N(0,1).
- Precise asymptotics: they derive an explicit scalar convex optimization whose unique minimizer α* gives lim ‖x̂ − μx0‖2 = α*, and specialize it to sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and low-rank (nuclear norm) settings.
- Applications: for q-bit quantized measurements, their characterization reduces the design of thresholds/levels to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, leading to Lloyd–Max quantization as optimal for minimizing the LASSO error.

This generalizes Brillinger’s 1982 consistency of least squares under link violation (for fixed n, m → ∞) to high-dimensional LASSO with precise error expressions; strengthens the Plan–Vershynin constrained-LASSO bounds to exact asymptotics; and extends precise LASSO analyses (AMP and CGMT-based) from linear to nonlinear measurements. Notably, it relaxes the Gaussian prior assumption on x0 used in earlier precise characterizations (e.g., Gaussian nonzeros for ℓ22-LASSO or ℓ2-LASSO), allowing broad classes of structured signals via Assumption 1 on the Moreau envelope functional.

Quality:
- The technical core (CGMT-based analysis) appears sound and leverages mature tools. The equivalence theorem and scalar program are clearly stated, and the specializations (e.g., Theorem 2.3) match and extend prior precise results.
- Assumption 1 is reasonable for common regularizers (ℓ1, ℓ1,2, nuclear) and priors, and the paper provides concrete F(c1,c2,c3) where possible.
- Limitations are stated: Gaussian A, μ ≠ 0, bounded moments. It would be helpful to discuss universality (sub-Gaussian A) and outline which steps truly require exact Gaussianity.
- Minor issue: in the abstract σ should be defined by σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2] (a small typo there).

Clarity:
- Overall well written and organized. Figures support the theory.
- Important clarity suggestion: the Brillinger-style equivalence between the single-index model and a scaled linear model with nonstandard noise is central. It currently appears only in Appendix A; it should be explicitly stated in the main text (e.g., right after defining μ and σ^2) and referenced to justify the blockquote on line 140. A short intuitive sketch would greatly help readers.
- Consider briefly exemplifying Assumption 1 in the main text (ℓ1, ℓ1,2, nuclear norm) so readers can quickly see how to compute F.

Originality:
- First asymptotically precise analysis of LASSO-type estimators under nonlinear measurements across a wide class of link functions and regularizers.
- Extends prior precise results by removing Gaussianity assumptions on x0’s distribution (beyond sparsity level and bounded moments).
- The quantizer-design implication (Lloyd–Max) for minimizing LASSO error is a neat and useful corollary.

Significance:
- Advances theoretical understanding of robust use of LASSO under model misspecification/nonlinear sensing, a practically relevant regime (quantization, saturation, Tobit, 1-bit CS).
- The scalar program provides actionable predictions across λ, δ, structure, and g. The quantization design result may influence practice.

Practical relevance and guidance on l2- vs l22-LASSO:
- Because the ℓ2-LASSO (square-root LASSO) has an oracle regularization parameter that is pivotal in σ (does not require noise-variance knowledge), your results are particularly pertinent. Please discuss whether the asymptotic formulas recommend preferring ℓ2-LASSO over ℓ22-LASSO in practice under unknown link/noise. Suggestions:
  - Provide side-by-side error predictions (or finite-sample plots) for ℓ2- and ℓ22-LASSO under the same single-index setting, showing sensitivity to σ-misspecification for ℓ22-LASSO and robustness of ℓ2-LASSO.
  - Use the linear-equivalence to examine if consistent estimators of (μ,σ) can recover near-optimal λ for ℓ22-LASSO; if not, emphasize the practical advantage of ℓ2-LASSO.
  - In the sparse case, leverage your κ*, λcrit characterization to propose simple tuning heuristics.

Additional comments and questions:
- Scope: please comment on μ = 0 cases (even g or symmetric quantization) beyond citing alternatives; can small asymmetries restore μ ≠ 0, and how does the error scale as μ → 0?
- Design matrices: any evidence (empirical or theoretical) for universality to sub-Gaussian A?
- Reproducibility: consider releasing code for the scalar program and experiments.
- Minor edits: fix small typos (e.g., “mesaurements,” “redifining”), and consistently refer to the square-root LASSO, citing Belloni et al. (2011).

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong, technically solid extension of precise LASSO analysis to nonlinear measurements.
- Clear equivalence result with practical implications (quantization design).
- Relaxed assumptions on x0 and broad coverage of regularizers.
- Good writing quality and insightful specializations.

Cons / weaknesses:
- Gaussian A and μ ≠ 0 assumptions limit scope; universality not addressed.
- The key equivalence is hidden in the appendix; Assumption 1 could be demystified earlier.
- Practical guidance on choosing between ℓ2- and ℓ22-LASSO is not fully discussed; experiments could be expanded.

Recommendation: Accept. This is a high-quality theoretical contribution with meaningful practical implications, and the requested clarifications are straightforward to address.
