- Studies generalized LASSO with single-index nonlinear measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0), i.i.d. Gaussian A, structured x0; proves asymptotic equivalence to a linear model with effective gain μ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].
- Uses CGMT to derive a precise high-dimensional error formula reducible to a scalar convex program and specializes results to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank settings.
- Applies theory to q-bit compressive sensing: minimizing LASSO error reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, and Lloyd–Max quantization is argued to produce stationary points.
- Technical approach is sound and timely; extending from bounds to precise asymptotics for nonlinear measurements with general convex regularizers is a significant contribution.
- Assumption 1 (limit of Moreau-envelope expressions) appears reasonable in standard models but needs better motivation and explanation.
- Proofs are deferred but follow prior CGMT analyses; experiments reported align with theory though broader empirical validation is needed.
- Paper is generally clear but contains typos and notational inconsistencies (e.g., normalization of x0, role of μ and σ when g is unknown).
- The “non-linear = linear” statement should be emphasized as an error-level equivalence valid in the high-dimensional limit, not an exact equality.
- Originality is high—likely the first precise characterization of generalized LASSO under nonlinear single-index measurements across a broad class of regularizers.
- Significance is high—results have practical implications for estimator choice, λ tuning, and quantizer design in nonlinear sensing systems.
- Main limitation: strong reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian design and asymptotic analysis.
- Suggested extension: discuss applicability to sub-Gaussian or rotationally invariant ensembles.
- Suggested treatment: address the μ = 0 case more fully (e.g., dithering or alternative estimators).
- Suggested practical steps: provide methods to estimate μ and σ from data (method-of-moments/calibration) and study robustness to misspecification.
- Suggested experiments: include finite-sample studies with non-Gaussian designs and real datasets to broaden empirical support.
- Technical clarifications requested: make F(c1,c2,c3) explicit for nuclear norm under standard spectral models and add a short proof sketch for the Lloyd–Max stationary-point claim.
- Pros for acceptance: strong novel theory, broad applicability, and clear improvements/connection to prior AMP/CGMT and Plan–Vershynin work.
- Cons against acceptance: heavy Gaussian/asymptotic reliance, some opaque assumptions, limited empirical breadth, and clarity/notation issues.
