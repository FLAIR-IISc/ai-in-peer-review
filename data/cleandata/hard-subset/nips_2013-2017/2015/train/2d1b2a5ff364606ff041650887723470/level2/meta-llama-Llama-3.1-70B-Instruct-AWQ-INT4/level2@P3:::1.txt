**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper considers the problem of estimating an unknown signal vector x0 from a vector of measurements y, where each measurement yi is a non-linear function of a linear combination of the entries of x0. The authors propose using the Generalized LASSO algorithm to solve this problem, which is a natural extension of the classical LASSO algorithm to non-linear measurements. The main contribution of the paper is a precise evaluation of the asymptotic behavior of the error of the Generalized LASSO algorithm, which is shown to be equivalent to the error of a corresponding linear measurement model.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds on previous work by Brillinger, Plan, and Vershynin, who studied the asymptotic behavior of the least squares solution in the presence of non-linear measurements. The authors extend these results to the Generalized LASSO algorithm and provide a more precise characterization of the error. The paper also relates to recent work on the precise analysis of the LASSO algorithm with linear measurements, which has been studied using various frameworks such as the Approximate Message Passing (AMP) framework and the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT).

**Strengths**

1. **Precise characterization of the error**: The paper provides a precise evaluation of the asymptotic behavior of the error of the Generalized LASSO algorithm, which is a significant improvement over previous results.
2. **Extension to non-linear measurements**: The paper extends the results of previous work to non-linear measurements, which is a more realistic scenario in many applications.
