- Paper gives an asymptotically exact error characterization for generalized LASSO under non-linear single-index measurements y_i = g(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian i.i.d. design.  
- Main result: in high-dimensional Gaussian regime, non-linear measurements behave like linear ones with effective gain μ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Provides a scalar convex optimization in (α,β,τ) whose optimum α* yields the asymptotic error lim ||x̂ − μx0||2.  
- Specializes results to sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and outlines low-rank (nuclear norm) cases, with explicit sparse-case equations.  
- Applies theory to q-bit quantized compressed sensing, showing LASSO error minimization reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and connects to Lloyd–Max quantizers.  
- Analysis builds on CGMT and extends prior precise-error analyses from linear to non-linear observation models.  
- Technical assumptions: Gaussian i.i.d. design, linear asymptotics m/n → δ, regularizer normalization/subdifferential bounds, and a technical Assumption 1 about a limiting functional via Moreau envelope.  
- Theoretical development appears careful and well-posed; scalar program has unique solution and simulations match theory including phase transitions.  
- Clarity is good overall; suggest moving verification of Assumption 1 for common regularizers into main text and adding an intuitive CGMT sketch and μ=0 discussion.  
- Originality: first precise asymptotic error formulas for generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements and a novel quantizer-design implication.  
- Significance: unifying principle treating many non-linearities via (μ,σ^2), practical guidance for λ tuning and sample complexity, and measurement/quantizer design insights.  
- Strengths: clear equivalence result, precise scalar characterization, multi-structure coverage, practical quantizer insight, and convincing simulations.  
- Weaknesses/suggestions: need robustness analysis beyond Gaussian A, move Assumption 1 proofs to main text, provide estimators for μ and σ^2, add concrete low-rank example, and address finite-sample concentration and μ=0 regime.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical advance, broad applicability, and practical consequences aligned with conference interests.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on Gaussian asymptotics, underdeveloped low-rank treatment, and limited practical/finite-sample guidance.  
- Overall recommendation: solid and timely contribution; accept with minor additions on robustness, μ/σ^2 estimation, and a concrete low-rank example.
