- Paper studies Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) and shows asymptotic error equivalence to a scaled noisy linear model.
- Proves ‖x̂ - μx0‖2 asymptotically equals LASSO error with measurements yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi, where μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].
- Generalizes and strengthens Plan and Vershynin (2015) by providing precise asymptotic error expressions rather than non-asymptotic upper bounds.
- Main technical contribution is Theorem 2.2, giving the estimation error as the solution to a convex optimization program.
- Validated by numerical experiments (Figures 1–2) showing excellent agreement between theory and practice across measurement models and regularizers.
- Applied results to q-bit compressive sensing and shows optimal quantizer corresponds to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.
- Builds on the CGMT framework; proofs are rigorous and placed in the appendix.
- Clearly situates contribution relative to prior work (Brillinger 1982; Plan & Vershynin 2015) and is well-written and organized.
- Strength: precise characterization of estimation error for non-linear measurements, filling an important gap.
- Strength: elegant equivalence connecting non-linear measurements to scaled linear measurements.
- Strength: practical impact via optimal quantizer design.
- Strength: thorough treatment of multiple structured signal models (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).
- Weakness: assumes μ ≠ 0, excluding important cases like binary (sign) measurements with μ = 0 and offers no adaptation strategy.
- Weakness: provides asymptotic results but no concrete rates of convergence for finite-sample behavior.
- Weakness: connection between Lloyd–Max algorithm and optimal quantizer is deferred to Appendix C rather than developed in main text.
- Recommendations: add discussion of μ = 0 cases, provide convergence rate insights, and expand main-text treatment of Lloyd–Max connection.
- Significance: high — provides theoretical foundation for using Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and practical guidance for quantizer design.
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution suitable for NIPS; recommended for acceptance.
