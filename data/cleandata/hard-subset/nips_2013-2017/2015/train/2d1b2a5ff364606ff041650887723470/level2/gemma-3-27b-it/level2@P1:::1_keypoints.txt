- Core contribution: proves asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO error with nonlinear measurements and a scaled linear model, with explicit error expressions.  
- Novelty: derives precise, previously unavailable error formulas that reveal the role of the nonlinear link function.  
- Practical implication: shows Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for minimizing estimation error in certain quantized measurement scenarios.  
- Theoretical rigor: builds on Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin with clear assumptions and well-structured proofs.  
- Connections to prior work: clearly situates and improves upon references [5] and [17].  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and organized, with a motivating introduction.  
- Strength: significant advancement in understanding LASSO under nonlinear measurements.  
- Strength: results have clear relevance to compressive sensing and quantized data analysis.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 is strong and may not hold broadly; needs clearer intuition and discussion of implications.  
- Weakness: computational cost of finding optimal Lloyd–Max quantizer parameters is not thoroughly addressed.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited—figures 1–2 are helpful but more extensive simulations are needed.  
- Weakness: notation is dense and occasionally hard to follow; clearer subscripts/superscripts would help.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: expand discussion of Assumption 1, increase empirical validation, and address computational cost/mitigation for the quantizer.  
- Review summary ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
