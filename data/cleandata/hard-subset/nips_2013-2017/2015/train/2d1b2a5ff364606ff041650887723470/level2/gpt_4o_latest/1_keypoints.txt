- Problem: Estimating structured signal x0 from nonlinear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) with unknown link g(·).
- Contribution: Extends Generalized LASSO to nonlinear measurements and gives precise asymptotic error predictions for i.i.d. Gaussian A.
- Main theoretical result: Equivalence between performance under nonlinear measurements and scaled noisy linear measurements characterized by μ and σ^2 from g(·).
- Validation: Rigorous theoretical analysis supported by simulations for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.
- Practical application: Uses results to optimize quantization schemes in compressive sensing (Lloyd–Max algorithm).
- Strength — Novelty: First precise asymptotic error expressions for Generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements.
- Strength — Generality: Framework applies broadly across link functions, regularizers, and structured signal models.
- Strength — Practical implications: Demonstrates useful application to quantizer design and shows empirical support.
- Strength — Clarity: Theorems clearly stated with explicit dependence on μ, σ^2, δ, λ and illustrative figures.
- Weakness — Assumptions: Analysis depends on i.i.d. Gaussian A and asymptotic limits, limiting immediate real-world applicability.
- Weakness — Accessibility: Dense, technical derivations (CGMT, convex optimization) may be hard for non-expert readers.
- Weakness — Experiments: Limited experimental scope; lacks tests on real datasets or non-Gaussian measurement matrices.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve accessibility and expand experimental validation.
- Pro acceptance: Novel precise theory, broad applicability, and demonstrated practical relevance.
- Con acceptance: Limited treatment of non-Gaussian/finite settings and dense exposition may hinder broader impact.
