## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO when applied to estimation problems with non-linear measurements. The core contribution is demonstrating that, asymptotically, the estimation error with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled measurements and added noise. The authors derive precise expressions for this error, providing a valuable theoretical understanding of the LASSO’s behavior in this context and showing that the optimal quantizer for minimizing estimation error is the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

The paper is well-motivated, building upon previous work by Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin. The authors clearly articulate the problem and its relevance to applications involving non-linear measurement devices. The mathematical development is rigorous, and the derivation of the asymptotic error expression appears sound. The connection to the linear measurement model is a significant result, simplifying the analysis and leveraging existing theoretical tools. The application to q-bit compressive sensing and the link to the Lloyd-Max quantizer are interesting and demonstrate the practical implications of the theoretical findings. 

A strength of the paper is its completeness in addressing the necessary assumptions and providing a detailed analysis. The authors acknowledge limitations, such as the assumption of Gaussian measurement matrices and the need for further work on low-rank recovery. The inclusion of multiple theorems and a clear presentation of the asymptotic regime enhances clarity. However, the paper could benefit from more intuitive explanations of some of the mathematical derivations. While the authors provide references, some readers might find it challenging to follow the connections to related work without a deeper background in the field. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and significant theoretical result demonstrating equivalence between non-linear and scaled linear measurement models.
*   Precise asymptotic error analysis, providing a strong theoretical foundation.
*   Clear presentation and rigorous mathematical development.
*   Practical application to q-bit compressive sensing with a link to optimal quantization.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The mathematical complexity might limit accessibility for some readers.
*   The reliance on Gaussian measurement matrices could be a limitation in certain applications.
*   Further exploration of the low-rank recovery case would strengthen the work.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper with a significant theoretical contribution. While the mathematical complexity is a potential barrier, the clarity of the presentation and the importance of the results outweigh this concern. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve accessibility and potentially expand on the limitations discussed. The authors should be encouraged to resubmit a refined version.
