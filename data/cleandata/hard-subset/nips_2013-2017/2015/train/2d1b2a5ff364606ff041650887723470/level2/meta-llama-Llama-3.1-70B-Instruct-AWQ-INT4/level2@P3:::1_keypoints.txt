- Problem: Estimating a signal x0 from non-linear measurements yi that are functions of linear projections, using the Generalized LASSO.  
- Main contribution: Precise asymptotic evaluation of Generalized LASSO error, shown equivalent to error under a corresponding linear model.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger/Plan/Vershynin results and ties to recent precise LASSO analyses (AMP, CGMT).  
- Strength: Provides a more precise characterization of asymptotic error than previous results.  
- Strength: Extends analysis to non-linear measurements relevant in practice.  
- Strength: Unifies understanding of error for linear and non-linear measurement models.  
- Weakness: Relies on technical assumptions (e.g., existence of pdf for x0, bounded moments) that may be restrictive.  
- Weakness: Limited numerical experiments; needs more extensive empirical validation.  
- Weakness: No discussion of computational complexity of the Generalized LASSO.  
- Originality: Novel and significant precise error characterization and unification of linear/non-linear cases.  
- Significance: Results are relevant for signal processing, image processing, and machine learning with non-linear measurements.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, with consistent notation and proofs provided in the appendix.  
- Recommendation: Accept to NIPS subject to minor revisions addressing technical assumptions and expanding numerical results.
