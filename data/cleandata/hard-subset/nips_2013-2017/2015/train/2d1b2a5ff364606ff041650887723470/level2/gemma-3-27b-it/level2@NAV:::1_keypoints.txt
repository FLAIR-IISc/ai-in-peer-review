- Central claim: Asymptotic equivalence of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements to LASSO on scaled, noisy linear measurements with explicit scaling/noise parameters.
- Main contribution: Precise expressions for asymptotic estimation error under non-linear measurements.
- Novel finding: Optimal quantizer for minimizing estimation error is the Lloyd–Max quantizer.
- Motivation: Addresses practical measurement device non-linearities and justifies LASSO use in those settings.
- Rigor: Mathematical treatment and derivations are rigorous and build on prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin).
- Scope: Extends existing results to a more general non-linear measurement setting and connects to multiple literatures.
- Presentation weakness: Paper is dense and heavily notational, making core ideas hard to grasp without strong background.
- Assumptions concern: Key assumptions (e.g., Assumption 1) are not sufficiently explored or validated for practical scenarios.
- Numerical validation weakness: Limited numerical illustrations and insufficient robustness checks across link functions and signals.
- Practical implications: Discussion of practical use of derived error formulas is limited beyond the quantizer result.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound equivalence; significant asymptotic error analysis; impactful quantizer connection.
- Arguments against acceptance: Dense presentation; limited discussion/verification of assumptions; insufficient numerical validation.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add more illustrative examples, intuitive explanation of assumptions, and expanded numerical validation.
