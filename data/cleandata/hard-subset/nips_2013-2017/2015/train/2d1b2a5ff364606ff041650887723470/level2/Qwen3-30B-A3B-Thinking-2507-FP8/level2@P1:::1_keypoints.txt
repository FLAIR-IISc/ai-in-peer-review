- Problem studied: Behavior of Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x_0) with Gaussian measurement matrix entries.  
- Main equivalence: Estimation error with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear model y_lin = A(μx_0)+σz with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].  
- Main contribution: Theorem 2.2 gives a precise asymptotic expression for the estimation error as the solution to a convex optimization program.  
- Extensions: Results apply to structured signals (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) with explicit error formulas for each case.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger’s unregularized case and refines Plan & Vershynin’s non-asymptotic upper bounds to precise asymptotic errors.  
- Practical application: Establishes a connection to Lloyd–Max quantizer design for optimal q-bit compressive sensing.  
- Empirical validation: Numerical experiments (Figures 1–2) show excellent agreement between theory and simulations.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes thorough related-work discussion and assumption specification.  
- Assumption concern 1: Assumes ‖x_0‖_2 = 1 throughout; generalization claimed but practical implications should be discussed.  
- Assumption concern 2: “Assumption 1” is referenced for general results but its restrictiveness beyond analyzed cases is unclear and needs more intuition.  
- Novelty clarification: Some error formulae are claimed novel even for linear measurements, but specific differences from existing linear-results should be explicitly stated.  
- Technical assessment and recommendation: Paper is technically sound, significant, and practically relevant; reviewer recommends acceptance.
