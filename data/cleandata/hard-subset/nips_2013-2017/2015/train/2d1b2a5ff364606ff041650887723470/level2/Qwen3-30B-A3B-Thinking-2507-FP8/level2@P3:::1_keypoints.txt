- Main contribution: shows Generalized LASSO on non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a linear model with scaled and noisy measurements, and derives precise error expressions.  
- Error characterization: expressions depend on the link function via μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1).  
- Theoretical advance: provides exact asymptotic errors rather than the previous loose upper bounds.  
- Builds on prior work: extends results of Brillinger (unconstrained) and Plan & Vershynin (constrained), improving precision.  
- Methodology: proofs use the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework adapted to the non-linear setting.  
- Theorems: main results (Thms 2.1, 2.2) rigorously establish the non-linear→linear equivalence asymptotically.  
- Practical application: applies theory to q-bit compressive sensing and shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal in this setting.  
- Novelty: novel precise asymptotic error expressions for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Impact: results give theoretical guidance for LASSO parameter selection in non-linear/quantized measurement problems.  
- Clarity and presentation: paper is well-organized, clearly written, with effective figures validating theory.  
- Literature context: related-work section thoroughly situates contributions in single-index models and Generalized LASSO literature.  
- Limitations: results are asymptotic and may not directly translate to small finite-sample problems.  
- Modeling assumption: assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, which is standard but potentially restrictive for some applications.  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution with practical implications; suitable for acceptance and presentation.
