- Paper studies generalized LASSO under single-index/nonlinear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0), proving asymptotic equivalence to a linear model y = μA x0 + σz with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].

- Main theoretical contributions: Theorem 2.1 (exact asymptotic equivalence via CGMT) and Theorem 2.2 (precise scalar optimization giving limit of ‖x̂−μx0‖^2 for general convex regularizers).

- Specialized results: Theorem 2.3 gives closed-form characterizations for sparse/group-sparse settings via soft-thresholding; low-rank/nuclear-norm case is discussed but not fully developed.

- Uses CGMT framework appropriately to reduce to a scalar convex program and build on prior AMP/CGMT literature for linear models.

- Assumptions include iid Gaussian design, independence, and μ ≠ 0 for identifiability; Assumption 1 (Moreau envelope convergence) holds for common regularizers.

- Empirical validation: Simulations show strong finite-sample agreement across links and structures, though broader coverage would strengthen robustness claims.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with good contextualization relative to Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin.

- Originality: Novel and impactful extension of Brillinger to high-dimensional regimes and sharpening of Plan–Vershynin by providing asymptotically exact formulas for regularized programs.

- Significance: High — results enable porting linear LASSO analyses to nonlinear settings and guide practical choices like λ and quantizer design.

- Practical corollary: Reduces optimal quantizer design to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, with Lloyd–Max quantizers shown to be stationary points of this objective.

- Strength: Broad framework covering many convex penalties and interpretable dependence of error on δ, λ, μ, σ^2, and structure via F.

- Strength: Simulations and specializations to ℓ1 and group ℓ1,2 validate theoretical predictions.

- Weakness: Results rely on iid Gaussian designs; extension to rotationally invariant or sub-Gaussian designs is only discussed qualitatively.

- Weakness: Low-rank/nuclear-norm case is sketched but lacks a concrete example and numerical demonstration.

- Weakness: Notational inconsistency in σ definition (variance vs. mean) needs standardization to σ^2 throughout.

- Weakness: Paper lacks guidance on data-driven estimation of μ, σ^2, and λ (e.g., CV or SURE under equivalent linear model).

- Weakness: Excludes μ = 0 cases (e.g., even g); discussion on consequences and alternative estimators is brief and should be expanded.

- Weakness: Lloyd–Max result guarantees only stationary points; conditions for global optimality and empirical comparisons to other quantizers are missing.

- Weakness: Finite-sample convergence rates and sensitivity to deviations from Gaussian A are not quantified; plots vs n and misspecification experiments are suggested.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, rigorous, broadly applicable theoretical result with clear practical implications and convincing experiments for sparse/group-sparse cases.

- Arguments against acceptance: Dependence on Gaussian design limits applicability, incomplete low-rank demonstration, and omitted practical aspects like estimating μ, σ^2, and λ.

- Overall evaluation: Strong, timely theoretical contribution with clear practical consequences; with minor clarifications and an added low-rank example it is NeurIPS-quality.
