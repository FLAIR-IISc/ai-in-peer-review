- Problem: Estimating high-dimensional structured signals from non-linear measurements using the Generalized LASSO.  
- Main contribution: Proves an exact high-dimensional equivalence between LASSO with non-linear measurements and an associated linear Gaussian-noise model.  
- Result form: Provides an explicit characterization of the mean-squared error as the solution to a simple, low-dimensional convex program.  
- Novelty: First asymptotically exact error characterization for regularized LASSO in the high-dimensional regime, extending prior classical and non-asymptotic results.  
- Practical implication: Enables leveraging existing LASSO linear-measurement theory to analyze non-linear settings common in practice (e.g., quantization, saturation).  
- Application: Demonstrates utility by linking LASSO estimation error under quantization to the classical Lloyd–Max quantizer design.  
- Rigor and validation: Theoretical claims are technically sound and validated by numerical simulations that closely match predictions.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and well-situated within related literature.  
- Limitation 1: Analysis relies on strong assumptions (asymptotic m,n→∞ with fixed ratio and i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix), limiting established practical applicability to finite dimensions or other ensembles.  
- Limitation 2: Computing the error formula for general structures can require evaluating a nontrivial limit (F), which may be difficult in practice.  
- Overall judgment: Outstanding theoretical paper whose strengths substantially outweigh its inherent methodological limitations.
