Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies high-dimensional estimation under single-index/nonlinear measurement models yi = gi(a_i^T x0), where A has iid N(0,1) rows and x0 is structured (e.g., sparse, block-sparse, low-rank). The estimator is the generalized LASSO with convex regularizers. The main claim is striking: asymptotically, the estimation error of the generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements equals that of the same LASSO under an equivalent linear model y = μA x0 + σ z, where μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2], γ ∼ N(0,1). This generalizes Brillinger’s classic result for least squares (m → ∞ at fixed n) and considerably sharpens recent work by Plan and Vershynin, which provided non-tight upper bounds for a constrained program.

Using the CGMT framework, the authors provide (i) an asymptotically exact equivalence theorem (Theorem 2.1), and (ii) a precise scalar optimization (Theorem 2.2) that gives the limit of ‖x̂ − μx0‖^2 for any convex regularizer satisfying a mild normalization and an envelope-convergence assumption (Assumption 1). They further specialize to sparse and group-sparse settings, deriving closed-form characterizations via soft-thresholding operators (Theorem 2.3), and discuss low-rank recovery with nuclear norm. An application to quantized compressive sensing uses the equivalence to reduce optimal quantizer design to minimizing σ^2/μ^2; they argue Lloyd–Max yields stationary points of this objective.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The use of CGMT to derive exact asymptotics for generalized LASSO is appropriate and builds on a mature line of recent work. The reduction to a scalar convex program is compelling, and the sparse/group-sparse specializations are consistent with AMP/CGMT literature in the linear case. Simulations align well with predictions.
- Assumptions: Gaussian design and independence are standard for precision results of this type. The requirement μ ≠ 0 is clearly articulated and unavoidable for identifiability of direction under single-index models. Assumption 1 (existence of the limiting normalized Moreau envelope of the conjugate) is satisfied for common regularizers (ℓ1, group ℓ1,2), and the paper outlines how to verify it; the low-rank case is discussed but not fully developed.
- Empirical validation: Figures show strong finite-sample agreement across links and structures, though broader coverage (e.g., very small m/n, more link functions) would further support robustness.

Clarity:
The paper is generally clear, well-organized, and situates contributions with respect to Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin, as well as AMP/CGMT analyses for linear measurements. A few notation/definition issues are noted below. The role of μ and σ^2 is nicely highlighted and used to produce actionable insights (quantizer design).

Originality:
The core equivalence result for generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements appears novel and impactful. Prior work (Plan–Vershynin) provided non-asymptotic bounds with unknown constants for constrained programs; this paper gives asymptotically exact expressions for regularized programs and general convex penalties. The quantizer-design consequence (Lloyd–Max as stationary for minimizing σ^2/μ^2) is a neat and useful corollary.

Significance:
High. Precise characterizations guide practice (e.g., tuning λ, comparing regularizers, designing quantizers) and advance understanding of when ignoring nonlinearity still yields optimal asymptotic behavior. The equivalence allows one to port the rich body of linear LASSO error analyses directly to broad nonlinear settings.

Strengths:
- Asymptotically exact equivalence between nonlinear and linear models for generalized LASSO.
- General framework covering many convex regularizers; clear specializations to ℓ1 and group ℓ1,2.
- Precise scalar optimization for the error; interpretable dependence on δ, λ, μ, σ^2, and structure via F.
- Extension of Brillinger to high-dimensional regimes and strengthening of Plan–Vershynin’s bounds.
- Practical implication for quantizer design; elegant reduction to σ^2/μ^2 and Lloyd–Max connection.
- Simulations that validate predictions across regimes.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Scope of A: Results require iid Gaussian designs. It would help to discuss (even briefly) whether and how the arguments might extend to rotationally invariant/sub-Gaussian designs, or to known covariance Σ (e.g., via whitening), and what breaks without Gaussianity (where CGMT’s tightness is crucial).
- Assumption 1: While verified for ℓ1 and group ℓ1,2, the low-rank/nuclear norm case is left as a sketch. Providing at least one concrete low-rank example with computed F and matching experiments would strengthen the generality claim.
- Notational consistency: There is a minor inconsistency where σ is defined once as E(g(γ) − μγ) (should be zero for centered residual) and elsewhere correctly as σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2]. Please standardize to σ^2 for the variance throughout.
- λ selection: The results give precise error for any fixed λ and identify a λcrit in the sparse case. A discussion on data-driven estimation of μ, σ^2, and λ (e.g., via cross-validation, SURE-like criteria under the equivalent linear model) would increase practical impact, especially when g is unknown.
- μ = 0 cases: The paper notes exclusion of even g and other cases with μ = 0. It would be useful to briefly discuss what the theory predicts in that regime (e.g., impossibility for direction with generalized LASSO) and whether alternative estimators are preferable.
- Quantizer design: The Lloyd–Max result is stated as providing stationary points. Please clarify conditions for global optimality (if any), and include empirical comparisons between LM, uniform quantization, and possibly constrained LM (e.g., saturation constraints) across q and δ, reporting the resulting MSE predicted by Theorem 2.2.
- Finite-sample behavior: Provide plots of convergence vs n for representative settings to quantify the rate at which asymptotics become accurate, and sensitivity to model misspecification (e.g., mild deviations from Gaussian A).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, clean, and broadly applicable theoretical result (nonlinear-to-linear equivalence) with rigorous, precise asymptotics.
- Connects and unifies multiple strands of literature (Brillinger, Plan–Vershynin, CGMT/AMP) and pushes the frontier from bounds to exact formulas.
- Practical implications for quantizer design and for understanding LASSO under model mismatch.
- Clear exposition and convincing experiments for sparse and group-sparse settings.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Dependence on Gaussian design limits immediate applicability in some domains; extensions are only discussed qualitatively.
- Nuclear-norm/low-rank case is not fully instantiated; the general framework is asserted but not demonstrated numerically.
- Some practical aspects (estimating μ, σ^2, choosing λ) are left to the reader.

Overall evaluation:
This is a strong and timely theoretical contribution with clear practical consequences. With minor clarifications and an additional low-rank example, it would make an excellent NeurIPS paper.
