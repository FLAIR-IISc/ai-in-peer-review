- Main contribution: Proves that Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a scaled noisy linear measurement model and derives a precise error formula.  
- Builds on prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) and provides substantially sharper asymptotic expressions for estimation error.  
- Practical relevance: Uses the error formula to analyze and optimize q-bit quantization schemes for compressive sensing.  
- Theoretical significance: Theorems 2.1 and 2.2 advance understanding of LASSO under non-linear measurements and link to single-index models.  
- Paper is well-motivated and the problem setup is clearly defined, with a comprehensive related work section.  
- Strength: Derivation of the error expression and connection to scaled linear measurements are notable technical achievements.  
- Clarity issue: High equation density and few intuitive explanations make complex derivations hard to follow.  
- Specific clarity gap: Assumption 1’s meaning and justification are not clearly explained.  
- Section 2.3.2 (Lloyd-Max connection) feels abrupt and under-explained, lacking sufficient detail.  
- Lack of concrete examples: More illustrations for sparse recovery and low-rank matrix recovery would aid understanding.  
- Modeling limitation: Restrictive assumption of i.i.d. Gaussian entries in the measurement matrix A; sensitivity to deviations is not discussed.  
- Empirical gap: Heavy reliance on asymptotic analysis with no numerical experiments to validate finite-sample performance.  
- Algorithmic gap: Limited exploration of practical algorithmic improvements beyond the Lloyd-Max discussion.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer favors acceptance due to novelty and impact, contingent on improving clarity, justifying Assumption 1, expanding practical implications, and adding numerical validation.
