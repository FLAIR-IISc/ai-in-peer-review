- Problem: analyzes high-dimensional estimation under single-index measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) using regularized generalized LASSO.  
- Main claim: asymptotic equivalence — LASSO error with non-linear measurements equals error for an effective linear model y = μAx0 + σz with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Result: Theorem 2.2 gives a precise scalar optimization (via a Moreau-envelope functional F) for the asymptotic squared error lim ||x̂−μx0||^2.  
- Specializations: closed-form characterizations for sparse (ℓ1) and group-sparse (ℓ1,2) cases; discussion/sketch for low-rank (nuclear norm).  
- Relation to prior work: generalizes Brillinger’s LS consistency, sharpens Plan & Vershynin’s bounds, and extends precise linear-measurement LASSO analyses to non-linear settings.  
- Application: for q-bit quantization, LASSO performance reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 over quantizer parameters; Lloyd–Max quantization yields stationary points.  
- Strength: clear, simple non-linear-to-linear equivalence (explicit μ,σ) with broad design implications.  
- Strength: strong technical depth — general scalar variational formula covering many convex regularizers and x0 distributions.  
- Strength: concrete, closed-form results for sparse and group-sparse settings and improved expressions in compressed regimes (δ<1).  
- Strength: quantized sensing application is elegant and practically useful.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (existence of F from Moreau envelope of Fenchel conjugate) is crucial but opaque; scope beyond i.i.d. coordinates is unclear.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on Gaussian design/A for CGMT — robustness to subGaussian/isotropic designs not addressed.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2.1’s convergence mode (a.s./in probability) is not precisely stated and should be clarified.  
- Weakness: excludes μ=0 (e.g., even link functions); unclear what parts of results survive or how to adapt estimators.  
- Weakness: low-rank/nuclear-norm case is only sketched, lacks conditions for F and numerical validation.  
- Quality: claims are well-supported and match experiments for sparse/group-sparse; broader experiments (e.g., ReLU, probit) would strengthen generality.  
- Clarity: overall clear but dense; needs more intuition for F/Assumption 1 and fixes for notational inconsistencies.  
- Originality: high — novel precise asymptotics for generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Significance: high — practical guidance for measurement/quantizer design and theoretical unification.  
- Suggestions: clarify Theorem 2.1 and assumptions on g; expand Assumption 1 with sufficient conditions and examples beyond i.i.d. x0.  
- Suggestions: add experiments for nontrivial link functions and low-rank matrices; discuss estimating μ,σ when g unknown.  
- Suggestions: comment on extensions to subGaussian A and to square-loss LASSO 1/2||y−Ax||_2^2+λf(x); provide quantizer optimization details and comparisons.  
- Cons summary: dependence on Gaussian designs, opaque technical assumption, incomplete low-rank treatment, notation/clarity issues, limited empirical breadth.  
- Minor fixes: correct Ax0→Ax in abstract, norm notation consistency, distinct symbols for x0 variants, explicit limit/convergence wording in Theorem 2.1, fix footnotes/references.  
- Recommendation: accept.
