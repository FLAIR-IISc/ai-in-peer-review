## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the Generalized LASSO for signal estimation from non-linear measurements, building upon prior work by Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin. The central claim is that, asymptotically, the performance of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that of a LASSO applied to scaled and noisy linear measurements, where the scaling and noise parameters are explicitly characterized in terms of the non-linear link function. The authors provide precise expressions for the estimation error, a novel contribution, and demonstrate a surprising consequence: the optimal quantizer for minimizing estimation error is the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a practical problem where measurement devices often exhibit non-linearities. The mathematical treatment is rigorous, and the derivation of the asymptotic error expressions appears sound. A significant strength lies in the extension of existing results to a more general setting, providing a theoretical justification for using LASSO-type algorithms even with non-linear measurements. The connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is a particularly interesting and potentially impactful finding. The authors thoughtfully connect their work to relevant literature in statistics, econometrics, and signal processing.

However, the paper suffers from some weaknesses. The presentation is dense and relies heavily on mathematical notation, making it challenging to grasp the core ideas without a strong background in the area. While the theorems are precise, the assumptions required for their application (e.g., Assumption 1) are not fully explored in terms of how easily they hold in practical scenarios. The numerical illustrations, while present, are limited and could be expanded to demonstrate the robustness of the results to variations in the link function and signal structure. Furthermore, the discussion of the practical implications of the derived error formulae, beyond the quantizer result, could be more detailed. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound result establishing equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models.
*   Precise asymptotic error analysis, a significant advancement in the field.
*   Interesting connection to the optimal quantization problem and the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Dense and mathematically challenging presentation.
*   Limited discussion of the practicality and verification of key assumptions.
*   Insufficient numerical validation to demonstrate the robustness of the results.

**Overall:**

Despite the density and limited practical discussion, this paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of LASSO-type algorithms with non-linear measurements. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should consider adding more illustrative examples, providing a more intuitive explanation of the key assumptions, and expanding the numerical validation to cover a broader range of scenarios. Encouraging the authors to revise and resubmit would likely result in a very valuable contribution to the NIPS community.
