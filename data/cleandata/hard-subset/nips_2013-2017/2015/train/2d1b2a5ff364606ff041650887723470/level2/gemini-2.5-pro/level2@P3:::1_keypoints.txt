- Problem: analyzes high-dimensional recovery of structured signal x0 from non-linear measurements y_i = g(a_i^T x0).  
- Main theoretical finding: asymptotic equivalence of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements to an effective linear model y_i = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i.  
- μ and σ depend only on the link function g.  
- Delivers an exact asymptotic characterization of MSE via a simple scalar optimization, moving beyond prior performance bounds.  
- Extends and builds on prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) by providing precise asymptotic results for regularized LASSO.  
- Framework is general: applies to a wide class of convex regularizers and non-linear link functions.  
- Special-case analyses provided for ℓ1, group-ℓ1,2, and nuclear-norm regularization.  
- Empirical validation: simulations (Figs. 1–2) show strong agreement with theoretical predictions.  
- Practical application: uses characterization to design optimal q-bit quantizers by minimizing σ^2/μ^2, linking to Lloyd–Max.  
- Strength: technically deep with precise, verifiable error predictions and broad applicability.  
- Suggestion: provide more intuitive discussion of Assumption 1, its role, and extensions/limitations for other regularizers.  
- Suggestion: expand the low-rank matrix recovery section and cite random matrix results that justify the singular-value distribution convergence.  
- Suggestion: discuss the μ = 0 limitation (e.g., even link functions like g(x)=|x|), and whether LASSO fails or requires a different analysis.  
- Evaluation summary: high technical quality, excellent clarity, strong originality, and significant practical and theoretical impact.
