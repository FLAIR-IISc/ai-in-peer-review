This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO in the context of non-linear measurements, demonstrating its equivalence to a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled and noisy observations. The work builds upon existing literature from Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, providing significantly more precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error. The key contribution lies in establishing this equivalence and deriving an accurate formula for the error, which allows for an analysis of optimal quantization schemes for compressive sensing.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a practical problem where measurement devices may introduce non-linearities. The problem setup is clearly defined, and the connection to existing single-index models is appropriately acknowledged. The theoretical results, specifically Theorems 2.1 and 2.2, appear to be significant advancements in understanding the behavior of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. The derivation of the error expression and the connection to scaled linear measurements are particularly noteworthy. The application to q-bit compressive sensing, demonstrating the use of the results for optimizing quantization levels, further strengthens the practical relevance of the work. The extensive related work section is commendable, thoroughly covering relevant literature and placing the contributions in context.

However, the paper suffers from some issues that detract from its clarity and impact. While the mathematical notation is generally standard, the sheer number of equations and the lack of intuitive explanations for some of the more complex derivations make the paper difficult to follow. For instance, the meaning of Assumption 1 and its justification are not immediately clear.  More intuitive explanations and intermediate steps would significantly improve readability. The discussion of the optimization problem in Section 2.3.2, while presenting a useful result regarding the Lloyd-Max algorithm, feels somewhat abrupt and lacks sufficient detail to fully understand the connection. Providing more concrete examples, particularly in the context of the sparse recovery and low-rank matrix recovery scenarios, would further illustrate the applicability of the theorems.

Regarding potential weaknesses, the assumption of i.i.d. Gaussian entries in the measurement matrix A is restrictive. While commonly used, it would be beneficial to briefly discuss the sensitivity of the results to deviations from this assumption. The paper focuses heavily on asymptotic analysis. While this provides valuable insights into the behavior of the estimator in large dimensions, it would be helpful to include some numerical experiments to demonstrate the practical performance of the proposed approach in more realistic settings.  Finally, the connection between the theoretical results and practical algorithmic improvements, beyond the Lloyd-Max discussion, is not fully explored.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and significant theoretical contribution, providing precise asymptotic error analysis for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.
*   Clear demonstration of equivalence to a linear measurement model, offering valuable insights into the problem.
*   Practical application to q-bit compressive sensing with potential for optimizing quantization schemes.
*   Thorough and well-structured related work section.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Mathematical complexity and lack of intuitive explanations hinder readability.
*   Restrictive assumption of i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix.
*   Limited discussion of practical performance and algorithmic implications beyond the Lloyd-Max algorithm.
*   Reliance on asymptotic analysis without supporting numerical experiments.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution to the field of compressive sensing and statistical estimation. While the mathematical complexity and lack of detailed explanations detract from its accessibility, the novelty and potential impact of the results are substantial.  The reviewer recommends acceptance, contingent upon the authors addressing the clarity issues by providing more intuitive explanations, expanding the discussion of practical implications, and potentially including some numerical validation of the theoretical findings. Addressing Assumption 1’s justification would be particularly valuable.
