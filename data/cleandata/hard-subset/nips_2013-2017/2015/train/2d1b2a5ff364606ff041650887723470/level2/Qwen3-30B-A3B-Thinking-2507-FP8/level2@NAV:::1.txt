

This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by establishing an asymptotic equivalence between the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and a corresponding linear measurement model. The authors demonstrate that for measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with scaled and noisy observations yi = μai^T x0 + σzi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1). 

The paper's main strength lies in its precise theoretical analysis using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem framework, which provides asymptotically exact error expressions rather than loose upper bounds as in previous work (e.g., Plan and Vershynin). The authors derive explicit expressions for the estimation error in several important special cases (sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery), with numerical validation showing excellent agreement between theoretical predictions and empirical results. This work significantly extends Brillinger's classical results to the regularized LASSO setting and establishes that the optimal quantizer for 1-bit measurements is the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

The paper is technically sound with clear theoretical contributions, well-motivated by relevant prior work, and makes a novel contribution to the understanding of LASSO under non-linear measurements. The theoretical results are supported by rigorous proofs and compelling numerical evidence. The writing is clear and well-organized, making complex theoretical concepts accessible.

A minor limitation is the assumption that the signal has unit norm, which may require adjustment for certain applications. Additionally, while the paper provides theoretical insights, it could benefit from more discussion of practical implementation considerations for real-world applications.

Overall, this paper makes a strong contribution to the theoretical understanding of structured signal recovery under non-linear measurements. The results are significant, well-supported, and provide valuable insights that will inform future work on LASSO-type algorithms in non-linear measurement settings. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and makes a novel contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance.
