- Main contribution: proves that LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent (in estimation performance) to LASSO with linear measurements.  
- Provides an asymptotically exact expression for the LASSO estimation error under non-linear measurements.  
- Results apply broadly to sparse recovery, group-sparse recovery, and low-rank matrix recovery.  
- Analysis uses rigorous techniques (Approximate Message Passing and Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem).  
- Modeling assumptions include i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices and a non-linear, random link function.  
- Results are asymptotic; finite-dimensional behavior may deviate from the theory.  
- Paper is practically useful for signal processing, image processing, and machine learning applications.  
- Demonstrates strong field knowledge and thorough literature review.  
- Claims and derivations are novel relative to prior work on linear measurements.  
- Paper appears reproducible with sufficient methodological and mathematical detail.  
- Limitation: assumptions may not hold in many practical scenarios (non-Gaussian matrices, deterministic links).  
- Constructive criticism: reliance on modeling assumptions and asymptotic limits could limit practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication given significance, novelty, and practical relevance.
