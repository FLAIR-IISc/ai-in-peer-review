

This paper investigates the behavior of the Generalized LASSO when applied to non-linear measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), where gi are independent copies of a random link function g. The main contribution is establishing that under certain conditions, the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO for non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with scaled and noisy measurements. The authors derive precise expressions for the error that depend on the link function g through μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1).

The work extends previous results by Brillinger (for the unconstrained case) and Plan and Vershynin (for a constrained version of the Generalized LASSO). While Plan and Vershynin provided upper bounds with unknown constants, this paper establishes precise asymptotic expressions for the error, representing a significant theoretical advance. The results are derived using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem framework, which has been successfully applied to linear measurement cases in recent literature.

The paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs and well-motivated assumptions. The main theorems (2.1 and 2.2) establish the equivalence between non-linear and linear measurements in a precise asymptotic sense. The application to q-bit compressive sensing, demonstrating that the optimal quantizer is the Lloyd-Max quantizer, is a particularly valuable contribution that connects theoretical results to practical applications.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with effective figures illustrating the accuracy of the theoretical predictions. The related work section thoroughly contextualizes the contributions within the literature on single-index models and Generalized LASSO. The authors carefully distinguish their results from previous work, noting where their approach provides improvements over existing bounds.

The originality of the work is strong, as it provides precise asymptotic error expressions for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, whereas previous work only offered upper bounds. The specific application to q-bit compressive sensing with the Lloyd-Max quantizer connection represents a novel practical contribution. The paper demonstrates how the structure of the signal, the choice of regularizer, and the link function collectively influence estimation performance.

The significance of the results is high, as they provide a theoretical foundation for using LASSO with non-linear measurements, which is common in applications like quantized measurements. The precise error expressions can inform practical parameter selection for LASSO in non-linear settings. The connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly important for practitioners designing quantization schemes.

One minor limitation is that the results are asymptotic, which might not directly apply to small-scale problems. However, the authors appropriately note that the asymptotic regime is relevant to high-dimensional problems of interest in modern signal processing. A more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the asymptotic results for finite problem sizes would strengthen the paper.

The paper assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, which is common in theoretical analysis but might be restrictive for some applications. While this assumption is standard in the field, a brief discussion of how the results might extend to other measurement ensembles would be beneficial.

Overall, this is a strong theoretical contribution that advances understanding of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. The precise error expressions provide a deeper understanding of how the link function affects estimation performance, which can guide practical implementation. The work is well-suited for NIPS as it addresses a fundamental question in high-dimensional statistics with clear theoretical contributions and practical applications. The paper makes a significant contribution to the literature on structured signal recovery and should be accepted for presentation.
