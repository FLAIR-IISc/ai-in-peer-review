- Presents a significant contribution: precise analysis of Generalized LASSO for non-linear measurements.  
- Main claim: asymptotic estimation performance with non-linear measurements matches that with linear measurements up to a scaling factor and link-function-dependent noise variance.  
- Theoretical support: Theorems 2.1 and 2.2 establish asymptotic equivalence between non-linear and linear-measurement Generalized LASSO.  
- Provides specific analyses for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery, validated by numerical simulations.  
- Uses the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) to derive tight bounds on estimation error (key novelty).  
- Well-written, clear introduction, consistent notation, and organized presentation of technical results.  
- Thorough discussion of related literature with comprehensive, relevant references.  
- Results offer a useful framework with implications across signal processing, image processing, and machine learning.  
- Paper is complete: modeling assumptions, technical derivations, simulations, and concise conclusion included.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: results are asymptotic and may not hold for small finite samples.  
- Assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix, which may limit applicability in some real-world settings.  
- Does not provide a comprehensive analysis of computational complexity of the Generalized LASSO.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel, and valuable addition to the literature; recommended for acceptance with awareness of limitations.
