- Problem: Estimating structured signal x0 from measurements yi = g_i(a_i^T x0) with unknown/random nonlinearity g.  
- Method analyzed: Generalized LASSO estimator applied to these non-linear measurements.  
- Main result: Asymptotic characterization showing LASSO on non-linear measurements is equivalent to LASSO on an effective linear model yi = μ(a_i^T x0) + σ z_i.  
- Effective parameters: μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1).  
- Scope: Precise asymptotic mean-squared error formula (Theorem 2.2) with specializations to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.  
- Novelty: Extends Brillinger’s fixed-dimension result and tightens Plan & Vershynin’s bounds to exact asymptotics for penalized LASSO.  
- Technical quality: High; theoretical claims appear sound and assumptions are clearly stated (i.i.d. Gaussian A, asymptotic regime).  
- Experimental validation: Strong — simulations closely match asymptotic predictions (Figures 1 and 2).  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized; introduction and main results are clearly presented.  
- Suggested improvement: Include a short, high-level proof sketch in the main text explaining how CGMT yields μ and σ.  
- Originality: Highly original application of CGMT to single-index models, first to provide exact asymptotic errors for this setting.  
- Significance: Important and likely influential; simplifies analysis of non-linear measurements by reducing to a linear equivalent and has practical implications.  
- Application: Demonstrates use in optimizing q-bit quantizers, connecting to Lloyd–Max algorithm.  
- Pros for acceptance: Fundamental equivalence principle, first exact asymptotics for generalized LASSO here, strong numerical validation, practical impact via quantizer design.  
- Cons against acceptance: Relies on restrictive i.i.d. Gaussian measurement assumption; low-rank recovery section less fully developed.
