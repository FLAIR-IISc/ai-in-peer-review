The article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models, relaxing the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of x_0. This contribution is worthwhile to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The article is well-written. However, there are a couple of points that could be improved. Firstly, as pointed out by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. Although this connection is rigorously described in Appendix A, it would be beneficial to mention it in the main article as well, in order to provide a heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140.

Secondly, the article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. The main benefit of the l2-lasso over the popular l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (usually unknown) noise variance (sigma). A practical question that the results could shed light on is whether this justification for the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso is valid. Specifically, it would be interesting to explore whether the theoretical benefits of the l2-lasso translate to practical advantages in scenarios where the noise variance is unknown.

Overall, the article provides a valuable extension of the existing work on l2-lasso to single-index models, and its contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators is worthwhile.
