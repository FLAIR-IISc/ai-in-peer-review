

This paper addresses the important question of whether the Generalized Lasso can effectively estimate structured signals from non-linear measurements. The authors build upon the foundational work of Brillinger (1983) and Plan & Vershynin (2013) to establish that, under Gaussian measurement matrices, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled and noisy observations. Specifically, they prove that for measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), the Generalized Lasso solution x̂ satisfies ‖x̂ - μx0‖_2^2 = ‖x̂_lin - μx0‖_2^2 with high probability as n,m → ∞, where x̂_lin is the solution to the linear measurement model yi = μai^T x0 + σzi with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1). The authors derive precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error that hold for all λ > 0 and a wide class of regularizers and link functions, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. They further apply these results to design optimal quantizers in q-bit compressive sensing, showing that the optimal quantizer is the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs of the main results. The authors establish a precise asymptotic characterization of the estimation error, which is a significant improvement over the upper bounds provided by Plan and Vershynin. The theoretical results are well-supported by numerical experiments that validate the accuracy of the predictions across various signal structures and measurement models. The paper properly addresses the limitations of the approach, noting that the signal can only be recovered up to a constant of proportionality when the link function is unknown.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The main contributions are clearly articulated, and the mathematical presentation is mostly precise. However, the treatment of the Moreau envelope (referred to as "proximal function" on page 5) is confusing; the standard term in convex analysis is "Moreau envelope," and the notation used is inconsistent with established literature. Additionally, the paper occasionally refers to the Generalized Lasso as an "algorithm" rather than a convex optimization program, which is technically inaccurate and potentially confusing for readers.

Originality: The paper makes several significant contributions to the literature. The most important is the derivation of precise asymptotic error expressions for the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements, which are the first of their kind. While Plan and Vershynin established that the Lasso solution is consistent up to a constant of proportionality, this paper provides the exact asymptotic error expression, showing that the non-linear measurement model behaves identically to a scaled linear model. The application to optimal quantizer design in q-bit compressive sensing, connecting to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, is particularly insightful and demonstrates the practical significance of the theoretical results.

Significance: This work significantly advances our understanding of how to handle non-linear measurements in structured signal recovery. The precise error expressions enable better theoretical analysis of the Lasso in non-linear settings and provide practical guidance for designing optimal quantizers in compressed sensing applications. The results are applicable across multiple domains, including compressed sensing with 1-bit measurements, quantized regression, and single-index models.

Pro and Con for Acceptance:
Pro:
- Establishes precise asymptotic error expressions for Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements, a significant theoretical advance
- Provides the first exact characterization of performance in this context
- Demonstrates practical application to optimal quantizer design, connecting to Lloyd-Max quantizer
- Well-supported by rigorous proofs and numerical validation
- Builds upon and significantly extends Plan and Vershynin's work

Con:
- Minor notational inconsistencies (e.g., "proximal function" instead of "Moreau envelope")
- Slightly unbalanced treatment of the technical content (some aspects are covered in detail while others are more brief)
- The paper occasionally refers to the Generalized Lasso as an "algorithm" rather than a convex program

Specific comments:
1. Page 4, line 193: The relationship between the regularized and penalized versions should be clarified with the appropriate mathematical correspondence.
2. Figure 1 caption: "n=768" should be formatted as "$n$=768" for consistency with mathematical notation.
3. Page 5, line 247: There is a typo in the phrase "When ..." that needs correction.
4. Page 5, line 294: The term "proximal function" should be replaced with "Moreau envelope," which is the standard terminology in convex analysis.
5. Page 5, Assumption 1: A more formal interpretation using variational analysis would strengthen the paper's theoretical foundation.
6. The paper should consistently refer to the Generalized Lasso as a convex program or optimization problem rather than an algorithm.

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of structured signal recovery with non-linear measurements. The theoretical results are novel, technically sound, and have clear practical implications. The minor issues noted above can be easily addressed in revision. The paper significantly advances our understanding of the Generalized Lasso in non-linear settings and should be accepted for publication at NIPS.
