The reviews of other reviewers and the authors' response have not significantly altered my opinion of the paper. Although I must acknowledge that I am not familiar with the single index model/sufficient dimension reduction literature, and therefore, I may be unaware of relevant previous work. The authors derive asymptotic guarantees for the generalized square-root lasso applied to Gaussian measurements that are optionally transformed by a possibly unknown nonlinear function. These results are very interesting, as they provide a precise characterization of the performance of the method. Overall, the paper is well-written, although it contains several typos, particularly in the proof section.

I would recommend accepting this paper, as it presents novel and significant contributions to the field. However, I suggest that the authors proofread their manuscript carefully to eliminate the existing typos, which may impede the clarity and readability of their work.

One potential concern is that the paper assumes a relatively high level of mathematical sophistication, which may limit its accessibility to a broader audience. Nevertheless, this is a minor issue, as the target audience of this paper appears to be researchers with a strong background in statistics and machine learning.

In conclusion, I believe that this paper is a valuable addition to the existing literature and deserves to be published in its current form, pending minor revisions to correct the existing typos.
