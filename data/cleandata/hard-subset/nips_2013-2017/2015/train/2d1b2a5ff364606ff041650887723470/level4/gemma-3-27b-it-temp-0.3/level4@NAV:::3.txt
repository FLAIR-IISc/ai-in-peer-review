This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso in the context of non-linear measurements with a Gaussian design. Leveraging and extending the foundational work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors derive novel, precise explicit expressions for error estimation within an asymptotic regime. The accuracy of these theoretical predictions is effectively demonstrated through validation with several established special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm. Furthermore, the application of these results to the design of optimal quantizers for q-Bit Compressed Sensing, and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, offers valuable insight.

This is a strong contribution to the field. 

The paper is generally well-written, although the presentation feels somewhat uneven in its coverage. The inherent limitations of space likely prevent a more exhaustive treatment of all necessary details. 

The work is original, building upon existing results from Plan and Vershynin [17] and significantly expanding upon them. Specifically, the explicit expressions for performance estimation error represent a novel advancement.

Comments:

1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent given an appropriate mapping between parameters.
2) Caption of Figure 1:  Please replace 'n=768' with '$n$=768'.
3) Page 5, line 247: There appears to be a typographical error in the phrase beginning “When…”.
4) Page 5, line 294: The function the authors refer to as the “proximal function” is well-established in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope associated with index \tau. The current notation is somewhat unconventional, as “prox” is typically reserved for the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope itself. A change in notation is recommended.
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption using concepts from variational analysis would be beneficial.
6) The authors should refrain from characterizing the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm.” It is more accurately described as a (convex) program or, using terminology common in Compressed Sensing, a decoder.
7) The experimental results demonstrate the performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and validate the theoretical error estimations. The paper establishes that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with such measurements is equivalent to that of a linear, Gaussian noisy model. The authors provide novel precise explicit expressions of the estimation error, and confirm their findings with many special cases (including the Lasso, the group Lasso and the nuclear norm). The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is very insightful.
