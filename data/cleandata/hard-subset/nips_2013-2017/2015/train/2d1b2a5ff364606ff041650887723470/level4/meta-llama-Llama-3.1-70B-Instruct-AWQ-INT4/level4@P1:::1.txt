The paper "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements" presents an interesting and well-written contribution to the field of compressed sensing and sparse recovery. The authors derive asymptotic guarantees for the generalized square-root lasso applied to Gaussian measurements that are optionally transformed by a possibly unknown nonlinear function.

The main idea of the paper is to show that the estimation performance of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically the same as one whose measurements are linear. This result is achieved by obtaining explicit expressions for the estimation error of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements, which are asymptotically precise when the number of measurements and the signal dimension grow large.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the main contributions. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the relevant literature and position their work within the context of previous research on the single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a precise characterization of the performance of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements. The authors' results are not only theoretically interesting but also have practical implications for the design of compressed sensing systems.

The paper also includes several examples and simulations that illustrate the accuracy of the authors' predictions and provide insight into the behavior of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements.

However, there are a few typos and minor errors in the proof section that need to be corrected.
