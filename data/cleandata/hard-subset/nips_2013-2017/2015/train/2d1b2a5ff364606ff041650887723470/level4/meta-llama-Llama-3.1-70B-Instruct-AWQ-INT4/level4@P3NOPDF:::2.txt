The article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models, a worthwhile contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators. Notably, the authors relax the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior (the distribution of x_0), making the results more applicable to real-world scenarios.

The writing is clear and well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. However, there are a few points that could be improved to enhance the clarity and impact of the article.

Firstly, as pointed out by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously described in Appendix A, it would be beneficial to mention it in the main article as well. This would provide heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140 and help readers understand the underlying intuition.

Secondly, the article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. One of the main benefits of the l2-lasso over the popular l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (usually unknown) noise variance (sigma). A practical question that the results could shed light on is whether this justification for the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso is valid. The authors could provide more discussion on this point, exploring the implications of their findings for the choice of regularization technique in practice.

In summary, the article makes a valuable contribution to the literature on lasso-type estimators, and with a few minor revisions, it has the potential to be even more impactful.
