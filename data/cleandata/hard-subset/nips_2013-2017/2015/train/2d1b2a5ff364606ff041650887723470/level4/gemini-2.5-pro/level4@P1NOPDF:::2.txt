This paper extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models. It also relaxes the common Gaussian assumption on the signal prior (the distribution of $x_0$), making it a worthwhile contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The paper is well-written. However, I have two suggestions for improvement:

1.  The analysis relies on the well-known equivalence between the single-index model and a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise, as established by Brillinger (1982). While this connection is described rigorously in Appendix A, it should also be mentioned in the main body of the paper. Doing so would provide the reader with a valuable heuristic justification for the statement on line 140, which currently lacks immediate context.

2.  The paper focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square-root lasso. A key motivation for preferring this estimator over the standard l2^2-lasso is that its "oracle" regularization parameter is independent of the (usually unknown) noise variance, σ. The authors' results are well-positioned to shed light on this practical consideration. A discussion of whether their analysis confirms the validity of this justification for using the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso would be a valuable addition and would significantly strengthen the paper's practical implications.
