- Paper analyzes asymptotic behavior of Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0).  
- Main result (Theorem 2.1): estimation error with non-linear measurements equals that of an effective linear model with scale μ and noise σ^2 in the n→∞ limit.  
- μ and σ^2 defined by μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2] for γ~N(0,1).  
- Extends Brillinger (1981) and Plan & Vershynin (2013) by providing precise asymptotic error predictions rather than loose bounds.  
- Proofs are technically sound, rigorous, and leverage the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework.  
- Multiple concrete applications (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) demonstrate broad applicability.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and figures validate theoretical predictions.  
- Exposition needs slightly more detail on assumptions of Theorem 2.1, especially the role of the link function g.  
- Original contribution: first asymptotically precise analysis of Generalized LASSO for non-linear measurements.  
- Novel connection to Lloyd–Max quantizer for optimal quantization (Section 2.3).  
- Results have significant practical implications (e.g., 1-bit quantization, censored data) and inform measurement and regularization design.  
- Weakness: Gaussian measurement matrix assumption should be better justified for practical non-Gaussian scenarios.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of relation to single-index model literature and how this work differs from classical results.  
- Weakness: Inadequate comparison to Yi et al. (2015), especially regarding the μ = 0 case and equivalence claims.  
- Weakness: Lacks practical guidance on choosing regularizers tailored to the structure of x0.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel theoretical contribution; rigorous proofs; practical relevance; clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need stronger justification of Gaussian assumption; better contextualization within related literature; more guidance on regularizer selection.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address literature context, Gaussian assumption justification, and regularizer discussion.
