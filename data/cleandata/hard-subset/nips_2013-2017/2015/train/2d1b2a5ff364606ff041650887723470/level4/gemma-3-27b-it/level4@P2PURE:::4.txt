This paper investigates linear estimation problems with nonlinear link functions. Building upon the recent work of Plan and Vershynin, it analyzes the asymptotic estimation error when employing the generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0. The key finding is that, surprisingly, the asymptotic error is identical for both linear and nonlinear link functions, as formalized in Theorem 2.1. 

However, Theorem 2.1 relies on the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be restrictive in certain applications. The authors should clarify the necessity of this assumption for the theorem's validity.

I have a few questions for the authors: 1. Given that the true parameter vector x₀ lies within a set 𝜅, how should the regularization parameter be selected based on this set? While the paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, it does not explicitly address this practical selection problem. 2. The result demonstrates equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements, yet the conclusion involves parameters μ and σ, which quantify the difficulty of estimation from the nonlinear function g. I am interested in the authors’ perspective on whether these dependencies are essential and, if so, why.

The bibliography appears incomplete. 1. The paper does not cite relevant work on single index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which study linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. 2. The recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," presents a novel algorithm for sparse recovery, even when the link function has μ = 0, and establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under specific conditions – a conclusion supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper with interesting and potentially significant results.
