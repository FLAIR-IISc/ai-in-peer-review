- Problem: Estimating structured signals from nonlinear measurements using the Generalized LASSO framework.  
- Main theorem (2.1): For Gaussian measurement matrices, asymptotic error under nonlinear measurements equals that under linear measurements yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with μ, σ determined by the link g.  
- Contribution: Provides precise asymptotic characterizations of estimation error for a wide class of link functions and convex regularizers, extending prior non-asymptotic bounds.  
- Result detail: Derives explicit error expressions capturing roles of the link function, signal structure, regularizer, and regularization parameter.  
- Validation: Rigorous theoretical analysis supplemented by empirical examples.  
- Structured cases: Comprehensive treatment with precise predictions for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signals.  
- Application: q-bit compressive sensing example identifies optimal quantization thresholds via the Lloyd–Max algorithm, showing practical relevance.  
- Limitation: Analysis assumes Gaussian measurement matrices, potentially limiting applicability; extension to other ensembles is not addressed.  
- Question 1: The paper assumes convex regularizers but does not guide selection of the regularizer for a given signal structure (set κ).  
- Question 2: The dependence on μ and σ (from g) should be better explained/interpreted as measures of the difficulty of the nonlinear measurement process.  
- Literature: Paper should more thoroughly review single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature and cite related work such as Yi et al. on sparse recovery with μ = 0.  
- Overall assessment: Well-written, rigorous, and significant theoretical contribution with clear presentation and practical implications.
