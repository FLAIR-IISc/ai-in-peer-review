- Paper studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link (building on Plan & Vershynin) and analyzes asymptotic error of generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0.  
- Theorem 2.1 claims the asymptotic estimation error is the same regardless of whether the link function is linear.  
- Theorem 2.1 relies on Gaussian measurement vectors; this assumption may be too restrictive and needs justification.  
- Authors should explain why the Gaussian assumption is necessary (or whether it can be relaxed).  
- Unresolved: how to choose a regularizer based on the set κ containing x0—convex regularizers are discussed but selection strategy is unclear.  
- Clarification needed on the necessity of dependencies on μ and σ in the claimed equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements.  
- Missing references to single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction literature that address estimation under unknown link functions.  
- Missing citation of Yi et al.’s recent work on optimal linear estimation under unknown links and sparse recovery when μ = 0, which is directly relevant.  
- Overall: well-written paper with intriguing and valuable results.
