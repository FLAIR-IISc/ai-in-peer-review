- Extends precise statistical performance analysis of the l2-lasso to single-index (nonlinear measurement) models — a valuable contribution.
- Derives explicit expressions for estimation error, offering a more complete asymptotic picture than prior work.
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly presented, and well-structured.
- Recommend briefly including the Brillinger (1982) observation (linear model with scaled signal and non-standard noise) in the main text to aid intuition for the core result.
- Ask for discussion on whether the l2-lasso’s advantage (regularization not depending on noise variance) is preserved under the established equivalence to a scaled linear model.
- Technical soundness: rigorous mathematical analysis with correct derivations and clear assumptions.
- Originality: novel extension of precise error analysis techniques to nonlinear measurement settings.
- Significance: deepens theoretical understanding of Generalized LASSO performance, with notable practical consequence for optimal quantization (Sec. 2.3).
- Clarity: notation is standard and mathematical arguments are generally easy to follow.
- Criticism: limited practical discussion — more elaboration on real-world implications would strengthen the paper.
- Criticism: reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix is a strong assumption; authors should acknowledge limitations and possible relaxations.
- Overall: strong, original, and significant paper; minor clarifications would improve impact — recommend acceptance.
