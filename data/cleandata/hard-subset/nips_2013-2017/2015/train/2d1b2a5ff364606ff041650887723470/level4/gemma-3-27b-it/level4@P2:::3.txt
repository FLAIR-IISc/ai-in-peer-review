This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso in the context of non-linear measurements with a Gaussian design. Building on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors provide novel and precise explicit expressions for the estimation error, extending previous results to an asymptotic regime. A key finding is that, asymptotically, the performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that with scaled and noisy linear measurements. The authors validate their theoretical results through several special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. An insightful application to the design of optimal quantizers in q-bit compressive sensing, and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, is also presented.

**Quality:** This is a strong technical contribution. The authors tackle a relevant problem and provide rigorous theoretical analysis leading to novel and useful results. The connection to existing work is clearly established and the theoretical findings are supported by examples.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, though dense with technical details. The notation is consistent, but the mathematical treatment can be challenging for readers not already familiar with the field. While the paper is comprehensive, some sections could benefit from more intuitive explanations, particularly regarding the implications of the derived formulas. The discussion regarding the connection to variational analysis (mentioned in the comments below) might be valuable for clarity.

**Originality:** The work extends the existing literature in a significant way. While building upon Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors achieve a higher level of precision by moving to an asymptotic analysis and providing explicit error expressions. These expressions represent a novel contribution and offer valuable insights into the behavior of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements. The application to q-bit compressive sensing and the link to the Lloyd-Max quantizer also demonstrates originality.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Theoretical Results:** The precise error expressions are a significant advancement in understanding the performance of the Generalized Lasso in this setting.
*   **Rigorous Analysis:** The paper employs a solid mathematical framework and provides detailed proofs for its claims.
*   **Practical Implications:** The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates the practical relevance of the theoretical findings.
*   **Clear Connection to Prior Work:** The paper effectively situates itself within the existing literature and builds upon previous research.
*   **Comprehensive Validation:** The results are validated through examples and special cases, enhancing confidence in their correctness.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Technical Density:** The paper's technical complexity may make it challenging for some readers to fully grasp the details.
*   **Limited Intuition:** While mathematically rigorous, some sections could benefit from more intuitive explanations of the results.
*   **Minor Notational Issues:** A few minor typographical errors and potentially awkward notation choices exist (see specific comments below).

**Specific Comments:**

1.  Page 4, line 193: The phrasing regarding "regularized and penalized versions" could be clarified. Emphasizing the equivalence with appropriate scaling of the regularization parameter would be helpful.
2.  Caption of Figure 1: Correct the notation to '$n$=768' for consistency.
3.  Page 5, line 247: Correct the typographical error at “When…”.
4.  Page 5, line 294: The term "proximal function" is potentially misleading. Consider using "Moreau envelope," which is the standard terminology in convex analysis. While the authors are aware of the concept, the current usage may confuse readers.
5.  Page 5, Assumption 1: Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption, perhaps referencing results from variational analysis, could enhance clarity and rigor.
6.  Throughout the paper: Avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm.” It is a convex optimization *problem* or *program*.
7.  Regarding experiments: While the paper presents illustrative examples, a more comprehensive empirical evaluation might further strengthen the conclusions.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution with novel and precise results.
*   The analysis is rigorous and well-supported.
*   The findings have potential practical implications in areas such as q-bit compressive sensing.
*   The paper is well-connected to the existing literature.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The technical density may limit accessibility for some readers.
*   The lack of a more comprehensive empirical evaluation.
*   Minor notational and phrasing issues.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The strengths of the work significantly outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the specific comments outlined above. Addressing these comments will further improve the clarity and impact of this already strong contribution.
