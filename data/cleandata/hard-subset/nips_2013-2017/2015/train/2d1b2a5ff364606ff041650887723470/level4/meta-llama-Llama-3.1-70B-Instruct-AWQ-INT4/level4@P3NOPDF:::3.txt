This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. A commonly used heuristic is to treat non-linear observations as noisy linear observations. Building on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend the results of [17] and, by moving to an asymptotic regime, provide novel precise explicit expressions of the error estimation. The accuracy of their predictions is confirmed with various special cases, including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and the nuclear norm. The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

The quality of this work is high, and the authors demonstrate a clear understanding of the subject matter. The paper is well-written, although the treatment is somewhat unbalanced, likely due to space constraints. The authors have made a concerted effort to provide necessary details, but some sections feel a bit rushed.

The originality of this work lies in its expansion of the results in [17] and the provision of novel explicit expressions for performance estimation error. The authors have successfully built upon existing research and have made meaningful contributions to the field.

There are a few minor issues that require attention. On page 4, line 193, it should be noted that the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent with appropriate correspondence. Additionally, the caption of Figure 1 should be corrected to read '$n$=768'. A typo on page 5, line 247, should be corrected to read 'When...'. Furthermore, the notation used on page 5, line 294, to describe the proximal function is somewhat awkward, as 'prox' is generally used to refer to the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope. It would be more accurate to use the term 'Moreau envelope of index τ'.

It would be beneficial to provide a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using results from variational analysis. This would add clarity and depth to the discussion. Additionally, the authors should avoid referring to the generalized Lasso as an algorithm, as it is more accurately described as a convex program or decoder.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the generalized Lasso in estimating errors with non-linear measurements. The authors successfully show that the theorems predict the estimation error and provide novel precise explicit expressions of the estimation error. The application of these results to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS is a significant contribution to the field.

Overall, this is a well-written and high-quality paper that makes meaningful contributions to the field. With a few minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above, this paper has the potential to be even stronger.
