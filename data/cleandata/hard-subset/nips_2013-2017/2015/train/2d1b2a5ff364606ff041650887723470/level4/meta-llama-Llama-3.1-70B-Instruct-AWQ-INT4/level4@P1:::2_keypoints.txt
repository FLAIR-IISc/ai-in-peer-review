- Extends precise asymptotic characterization of the l2-lasso to single-index models, a worthwhile contribution.  
- Well-written and clearly communicates main ideas and contributions.  
- Thorough literature summary and good contextualization with prior NIPS and related work.  
- Organized logically and accessible to readers familiar with the topic.  
- Technically sound with rigorous derivations and proofs provided in appendices.  
- Relaxes the usual Gaussian prior assumption, increasing applicability.  
- Does not explicitly mention the link between single-index models and linear models with scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise (Brillinger 1982); connection is only in Appendix A.  
- Should mention the Brillinger-style connection in the main text to provide heuristic justification for a key statement.  
- Lacks a sufficient discussion of practical implications, particularly guidance on choosing l2 (square-root) vs l2^2-lasso.  
- Could better explain whether the square-root-lasso’s oracle tuning independence from noise variance justifies its practical use.  
- Original and significant: provides new insights into l2-lasso performance in single-index models.  
- Results have implications for design of regularization techniques and interest for researchers/practitioners.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, broader applicability, clear writing, and practical implications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of the explicit Brillinger connection and insufficient practical discussion on regularization choice.
