

The paper extends the Generalized LASSO framework to handle non-linear measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), where the link function g is potentially unknown. The authors demonstrate that the asymptotic performance of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with scaled and noisy observations yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1). This work builds upon the classical results of Brillinger (1980s) who established consistency for the unregularized case, and the more recent work of Plan and Vershynin (2015) who provided non-asymptotic upper bounds for the constrained Generalized LASSO. The authors significantly advance these results by providing precise asymptotic error characterizations for the regularized Generalized LASSO across a wide range of structured signal models (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).

Quality: The paper presents technically sound results with rigorous mathematical proofs. The asymptotic error characterizations are well-established through the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem framework, and the assumptions are clearly stated and justified. The authors correctly address the limitations of previous work by providing precise expressions rather than loose upper bounds. The experimental validation in Figures 1-2 demonstrates excellent agreement between theoretical predictions and empirical results.

Clarity: The paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear logical flow from problem statement to theoretical results to applications. The technical presentation is precise and accessible to the target audience. The minor typos noted in the draft review (particularly in the proof section) do not significantly hinder understanding but should be corrected in the final version.

Originality: This work represents a significant advance in the field, providing the first-known precise asymptotic characterizations for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. The results extend previous work in the single-index model literature (e.g., Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin) by establishing a precise equivalence between non-linear and appropriately scaled linear measurement models, which is both theoretically novel and practically useful.

Significance: The findings have important implications for practical applications involving non-linear measurements, particularly in quantized sensing. The paper demonstrates that the Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal for minimizing estimation error in the Generalized LASSO framework, a result with direct applications in signal processing and compressed sensing. The precise error characterizations also provide valuable insights for practitioners regarding the optimal choice of regularization parameters and the fundamental limits of estimation under non-linear measurements.

The paper is a strong contribution to the field, advancing our theoretical understanding of structured signal estimation with non-linear measurements while providing practical insights for applications. The minor typographical errors should be addressed in the final version, but these do not detract from the paper's substantial technical contribution.
