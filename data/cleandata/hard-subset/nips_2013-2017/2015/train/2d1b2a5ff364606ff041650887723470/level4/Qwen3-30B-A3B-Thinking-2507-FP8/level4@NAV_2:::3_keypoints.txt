- Main result: Shows Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to a linear model yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi, with matching estimation error ‖x̂ - μx0‖₂² and μ,σ defined via Gaussian expectations.

- Provides precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error of the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements.

- Validates results across structured signal models: sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.

- Application: proves the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for minimizing estimation error in q-bit compressive sensing.

- Theoretical quality: technically sound with rigorous proofs, building on recent precise LASSO characterizations.

- Clarity: paper is well-structured and readable, though some technical elements and notations need clearer presentation.

- Originality: novel extension of Plan & Vershynin to precise asymptotics for non-linear measurements, distinct from prior linear-measurement LASSO work.

- Significance: important for theoretical understanding and practical measurement/quantizer design in hardware-constrained systems.

- Strength: first precise asymptotic characterizations for Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements.

- Strength: demonstrates equivalence between non-linear measurements and appropriately scaled noisy linear measurements.

- Strength: derives explicit error formulas for multiple structured signal cases.

- Strength: establishes Lloyd–Max quantizer optimality for q-bit compressive sensing.

- Strength: rigorous proofs with careful technical attention.

- Weakness: Assumption 1 presentation could be strengthened via a more formal variational-analysis interpretation.

- Weakness: Notational confusion around proximal concepts (Moreau envelope vs prox operator).

- Weakness: Inconsistent terminology—sometimes calls the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm” rather than a convex program.

- Weakness: Minor typographical and editorial issues throughout the manuscript.
