This paper considers linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, focusing on the asymptotic estimation error using the generalized LASSO for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0. The main contribution is the equivalence of the asymptotic error for linear and nonlinear measurements, as shown in Theorem 2.1. This result assumes that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be a strict assumption in some cases. The authors should explain why this assumption is necessary for the conclusion to hold.

A few questions arise from the paper: 1. How does one choose a regularizer based on the set K in which x0 lies? While the assumption of a convex regularizer is discussed, this problem seems not to be addressed yet. 2. Although the result shows that nonlinear measurements are equivalent to linear measurements, the conclusion involves μ and σ, which characterize the hardness of estimation from the nonlinear function g. It would be interesting to know if the authors have any thoughts on the necessity of these dependencies.

Some references are missing, including those on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which study linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. A recent paper, "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation" by Yi et al., introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions that have zero μ. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under proper conditions, supported by careful theoretical proof and empirical results.

The paper is well-written with fairly interesting results. However, the authors should address the mentioned questions and include relevant references to strengthen the paper's contributions and connections to existing literature.
