- Paper studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link function, extending Plan & Vershynin to generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0.  
- Main claim (Theorem 2.1): asymptotic estimation error is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions.  
- Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors; reviewer requests clarification on why this normality assumption is necessary or restrictive.  
- Open question 1: the paper does not address how to choose a convex regularizer based on the constraint set Κ containing the true parameter x₀.  
- Open question 2: although equivalence is shown, the result depends on μ and σ that quantify difficulty under the nonlinear link; authors should justify the necessity of these dependencies.  
- Missing related work: literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction for linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions is not reviewed.  
- Missing citation: Yi et al.’s "Optimal Linear Estimation under Unknown Link Functions" should be discussed, as it addresses sparse recovery for μ = 0 and establishes related equivalence results with theory and experiments.
