- Core result: Asymptotic equivalence of Generalized LASSO error for non-linear measurements to that of a linear model with scaled measurements and additive Gaussian noise.  
- Precision: Authors derive exact asymptotic expressions for the estimation error, improving over prior bounds.  
- Practical implication: Results applied to q-bit compressive sensing and yield insights into optimal quantizer design.  
- Connection to algorithms: Paper relates optimal quantization to the Lloyd–Max algorithm.  
- Novelty: Provides a significant, precise characterization of LASSO under non-linear measurements beyond previous work.  
- Theoretical rigor: Mathematical derivations appear rigorous with clearly stated assumptions and extension to the high-dimensional m/n → δ regime.  
- Clarity: Well-written and well-organized; complex arguments made accessible.  
- Limited LASSO comparison: Insufficient exploration of implications for choosing l2 (square-root) LASSO versus l2^2-LASSO in the non-linear setting.  
- Heuristic exposition: Core equivalence relies on Brillinger’s ideas and, while proven in the appendix, lacks an intuitive summary in the main text.  
- Assumption opacity: Assumption 1 (limit involving the Fenchel conjugate) is technically heavy and could use a more intuitive explanation or simpler sufficient conditions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound, precise results with practical relevance and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Needs deeper discussion on l2 vs l2^2-LASSO, stronger heuristic justification in the main body, and clarification of Assumption 1 for broader accessibility.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with minor fixable weaknesses; recommended for acceptance (score 7/10).
