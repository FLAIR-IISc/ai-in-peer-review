- Paper studies linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, extending Plan and Vershynin to analyze asymptotic error of generalized LASSO for arbitrary links with mu ≠ 0.
- Main result (Theorem 2.1): asymptotic estimation error equals that of an equivalent linear model regardless of whether the link is linear or nonlinear.
- Theorem 2.1 assumes i.i.d. standard normal measurement matrix; this Gaussianity requirement may be restrictive and needs clarification or extension to sub-Gaussian/general designs.
- Missing practical guidance on choosing the convex regularizer f given the structural set K that characterizes x0.
- Although nonlinear and linear measurements are equivalent asymptotically, the constants mu and sigma encode difficulty from g; question whether this dependence is fundamental or can be mitigated by algorithms or alternative formulations.
- Related work under-cited: literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction for estimating index vectors/subspaces under unknown links.
- Related work under-cited: Yi et al.’s “Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear transform,” which proposes algorithms for sparse recovery even when mu = 0 and is closely related.
- Overall assessment: paper provides careful theoretical proofs, supporting empirical results, and makes a clear, meaningful contribution.
