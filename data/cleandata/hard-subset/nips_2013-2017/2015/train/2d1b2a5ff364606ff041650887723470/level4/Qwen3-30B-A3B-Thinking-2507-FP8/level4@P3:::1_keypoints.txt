- Problem: Estimating a signal x0 from nonlinear measurements yi = gi(ai^T x0) with Gaussian ai and unknown link g.  
- Main result: Generalized LASSO on these nonlinear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on linear measurements with scaling μ and noise variance σ².  
- Scaling/noise formulas: μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ) − μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1).  
- Scope: First precise asymptotic characterization of the regularized Generalized LASSO’s estimation error that holds for all regularization parameters λ.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on and significantly extends Brillinger (1980s) and Plan & Vershynin (2013) by providing quantitative error expressions.  
- Applicability: Results apply to a wide range of structured signals (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and regularizers (ℓ1, nuclear norm, etc.).  
- Theoretical quality: Rigorous proofs and precise asymptotic guarantees supporting the main claims.  
- Clarity/experiments: Well-organized, generally clear exposition with illustrative examples and figures validating theoretical predictions.  
- Originality: Novel quantitative linkage between the link function, signal structure, λ, and error; shows Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for q-bit compressive sensing.  
- Significance: Important theoretical and practical implications—simplifies analysis of nonlinear measurements and informs measurement/quantizer design.  
- Weakness: Several typos and some proof details reduce readability; Assumption 1 needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Weakness: Inadequate positioning relative to the single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literatures.  
- Weakness: Practical guidance could be stronger with more concrete application examples and advice on parameter selection.  
- Overall assessment: Substantial, technically sound contribution that merits acceptance.
