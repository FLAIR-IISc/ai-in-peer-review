

Summary: This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurement models with Gaussian design matrices, extending the foundational work of Plan and Vershynin [17]. The authors demonstrate that for non-linear measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled and noisy observations. Specifically, they establish that the estimation error ‖x̂ - μx0‖₂² for the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements matches the error of the same algorithm applied to linear measurements yi = μai^T x0 + σzi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ) - μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1). This work significantly advances the understanding of the Generalized Lasso by providing precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error that are novel in this context, building upon recent precise characterizations of the LASSO with linear measurements [2, 10, 16, 18, 19, 21]. The results are validated across multiple structured signal cases (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and yield the insightful application to optimal quantizer design in q-bit compressive sensing, where they prove the Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal.

Quality: The paper presents technically sound results with rigorous proofs establishing precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error. The authors carefully address both strengths and limitations of their approach, demonstrating thorough analysis of the error behavior across different measurement regimes and signal structures. Their theoretical contributions significantly advance the understanding of LASSO-type estimators under non-linear measurements, providing concrete characterizations that were previously unavailable.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured and clearly written, though the presentation of certain technical elements could be improved. The main results are well-motivated and the special cases (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery) are handled with appropriate technical detail. The application to q-bit compressive sensing is particularly well-explained and insightful.

Originality: The paper makes significant original contributions by extending Plan and Vershynin's [17] work to provide precise asymptotic characterizations of the Generalized Lasso's performance under non-linear measurements. The explicit error expressions derived for various structured signal recovery problems are novel, as is the demonstration that the Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal for minimizing estimation error in q-bit compressive sensing. The paper clearly differentiates itself from previous NIPS work on the LASSO with linear measurements [2, 10, 16, 18, 19, 21] by addressing the non-linear measurement setting.

Significance: The results have substantial significance for both theoretical understanding and practical applications. The precise error characterization provides deeper insights into the behavior of the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements, which is valuable for practitioners designing measurement systems. The application to optimal quantizer design in q-bit compressive sensing is particularly impactful, as it establishes a theoretically grounded approach to quantizer design that has practical implications for hardware-constrained signal acquisition systems.

Strengths:
- Provides the first precise asymptotic characterizations of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements
- Demonstrates equivalence between non-linear and appropriately scaled linear measurements
- Derives novel explicit expressions for estimation error across multiple structured signal cases
- Establishes the optimality of the Lloyd-Max quantizer for q-bit compressive sensing
- Rigorous proofs with careful attention to technical details

Weaknesses:
- The presentation of Assumption 1 could benefit from a more formal interpretation using variational analysis
- Some technical notations (e.g., the Moreau envelope) are not clearly distinguished from related concepts
- The paper occasionally refers to the Generalized Lasso as "an algorithm" rather than a convex program
- Minor typographical issues in the manuscript (detailed in comments below)

Comments:
1) Page 4, line 193: The distinction between regularized and penalized versions should be clarified with appropriate correspondence.
2) Caption of Figure 1: 'n=768' should be '$n$=768'.
3) Page 5, line 247: Correction needed at 'When ...'.
4) Page 5, line 294: The proximal function is more accurately described as the Moreau envelope in convex analysis; the current notation is confusing as 'prox' generally refers to the proximal operator, not the envelope.
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: A more formal interpretation using variational analysis would strengthen the presentation.
6) The paper should consistently refer to the Generalized Lasso as a convex program rather than an algorithm.
7) The experimental results effectively demonstrate the accuracy of the theoretical predictions across multiple cases, but a brief discussion of practical implementation considerations would be valuable.
