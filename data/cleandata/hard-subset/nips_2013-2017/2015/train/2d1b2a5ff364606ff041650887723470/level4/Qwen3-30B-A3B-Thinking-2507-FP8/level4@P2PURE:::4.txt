

This paper examines linear estimation with a nonlinear link function. Building on recent work by Plan and Vershynin, it analyzes the asymptotic estimation error using generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0. The key finding, stated in Theorem 2.1, is that the asymptotic error remains identical whether the link function is linear or nonlinear.  

Theorem 2.1 assumes the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be restrictive in certain applications. The authors should clarify why this assumption is necessary for the conclusion to hold.  

Two specific questions remain:  
1. How to select the regularizer based on the set Κ containing the true parameter x₀? While the paper discusses convex regularizers, this selection problem remains unaddressed.  
2. Although the result shows equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements, the conclusion depends on μ and σ, which quantify the estimation difficulty under the nonlinear link function g. The authors should comment on the necessity of these dependencies.  

The paper omits key references:  
1. Single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction studies linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, yet the paper provides no review of this literature.  
2. Yi et al.'s recent paper "Optimal Linear Estimation under Unknown Link Functions" introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery with link functions having μ = 0. It establishes the equivalence between high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under appropriate conditions, supported by rigorous theoretical proofs and empirical results. This well-written paper presents interesting findings.
