This paper investigates linear estimation with nonlinear link functions. Building on Plan and Vershynin, it analyzes the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized LASSO for arbitrary link functions with mu ≠ 0. The key finding is that, irrespective of whether the link is linear or nonlinear, the asymptotic error matches that of an equivalent linear model, as formalized in Theorem 2.1.

Theorem 2.1 assumes the measurement matrix has i.i.d. standard normal entries, which may be restrictive in some settings. The authors should clarify why Gaussianity is required and whether the conclusions extend to sub-Gaussian or more general designs.

A few questions:
1) How should one choose the regularizer given the structural set K that characterizes x0? While the paper discusses convex regularizers, concrete guidance on selecting f based on K appears to be missing.
2) Although the result equates nonlinear and linear measurements, the constants mu and sigma capture the intrinsic difficulty induced by g. Do the authors have thoughts on whether this dependence is fundamental or if it can be reduced through algorithmic choices or alternative formulations?

Some related work seems under-cited. 1) The single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literatures study estimation of the index vector/subspace under unknown link functions; these areas are not adequately reviewed. 2) The recent paper “Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear transform” by Yi et al. proposes new algorithms for sparse recovery even when mu = 0; it is closely related and warrants discussion.

Overall, the paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under appropriate conditions. The conclusions are supported by careful theoretical proofs and empirical results. This is a clearly written paper with interesting and meaningful contributions.
