- Studies the Generalized Lasso for non-linear measurements under Gaussian design and formalizes the heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as linear observations plus noise.
- Extends Plan and Vershynin’s foundational results into an asymptotic regime and derives precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error.
- Validates theoretical predictions across key special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear-norm regularization).
- Applies results to design optimal quantizers in q-bit compressed sensing and connects to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with a solid contribution.
- Writing is generally clear but presentation balance could be improved; some technical details omitted due to space.
- Originality: builds on prior work but contributes novel explicit formulas for performance/error estimation.
- Comment: regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate parameter correspondence.
- Comment: notation in Figure 1 caption should render n=768 in math mode.
- Comment: typographical error at page 5, line 247 (sentence beginning “When ...”).
- Comment: the “proximal function” used is actually the Moreau envelope of index τ; the label ‘prox’ is unconventional and may confuse readers.
- Comment: Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal interpretation using variational analysis concepts.
- Comment: avoid calling generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; prefer “convex program” or “decoder.”
- Experimental results effectively demonstrate applicability to non-linear measurements and validate the theoretical error predictions.
