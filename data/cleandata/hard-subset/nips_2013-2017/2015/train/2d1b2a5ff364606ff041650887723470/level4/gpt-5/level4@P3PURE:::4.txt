This paper studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link function. Building on the recent work of Plan and Vershynin, it analyzes the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized Lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0. The main result (Theorem 2.1) shows that, irrespective of whether the link function is linear or nonlinear, the asymptotic error is exactly the same.

Assumptions and scope. Theorem 2.1 is proved under the assumption that the linear measurement vectors are drawn from a normal distribution. This may be restrictive in some applications. Please clarify why Gaussianity is necessary for the conclusion to hold and whether the result can be extended to broader classes of measurement distributions.

Questions and clarifications:
- Choice of regularizer from the set κ containing x0. While the paper assumes a convex regularizer, it does not address how to select the regularizer based on prior knowledge that x0 lies in a set κ. Guidance or examples on designing the regularizer from this set would make the results more actionable.
- Dependence on μ and σ. Although the result indicates that nonlinear measurements are equivalent to linear measurements, the bounds still depend on μ and σ, which characterize the difficulty of estimation induced by the nonlinear link g. Are these dependencies necessary or could they be removed or weakened? Any intuition or discussion would be helpful.

Related work and missing references:
- The literatures on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction investigate linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. The paper does not review this body of work; situating the contribution relative to these areas would improve the context.
- A recent paper, “Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation” by Yi et al., proposes new algorithms for sparse recovery even when μ = 0 and establishes an equivalence between high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under suitable conditions, supported by careful theory and empirical results. This work appears closely related and should be discussed.

Overall evaluation. This is a well-written paper with interesting results. Addressing the necessity of the Gaussian measurement assumption, providing guidance on regularizer selection from the set containing x0, clarifying the role and necessity of the μ and σ dependencies, and expanding the discussion of related work would strengthen the paper.
