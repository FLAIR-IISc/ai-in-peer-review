

The authors' response and other reviewers' comments have not altered my assessment of this paper. While I acknowledge my limited familiarity with the single index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature, I may have overlooked relevant prior work. The paper presents a compelling contribution: asymptotic guarantees for the generalized square-root lasso applied to Gaussian measurements transformed by a possibly unknown nonlinear function. The results offer a precise characterization of the method's performance under these conditions, which is both theoretically significant and well-motivated. The paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear exposition and logical flow, though several typographical errors—particularly in the proofs—require correction. These minor issues do not detract from the paper's core technical merits or its potential impact. The theoretical analysis is rigorous and addresses an important gap in understanding the behavior of regularized estimators under nonlinear transformations. Given the clarity of the presentation and the novelty of the results, I believe this work makes a valuable contribution to the field. The authors should address the noted typos and ensure all proof details are meticulously verified in the final version.
