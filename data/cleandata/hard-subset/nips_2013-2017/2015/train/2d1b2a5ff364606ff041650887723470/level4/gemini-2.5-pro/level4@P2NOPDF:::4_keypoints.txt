- Paper studies asymptotic error of generalized Lasso under arbitrary nonlinear link functions and shows (Theorem 2.1) the error matches the linear case when μ ≠ 0.  
- Well-written paper with an interesting and potentially significant result.  

Major issues:
- Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors; authors should justify whether Gaussianity is fundamental or an artifact and discuss relaxing to sub-Gaussian/other distributions.  
- Missing connections to Single Index Models (SIM) and Sufficient Dimension Reduction (SDR); these literatures address the same problem and should be discussed.  
- Missing citation and comparison to "Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear link function" (Yi et al.), which treats μ=0 cases and shows related equivalence results; direct comparison of assumptions and contributions is needed.  

Questions for authors:
- Practical guidance on choosing the convex regularizer R(x) given prior knowledge of set K is lacking—please elaborate on practical selection.  
- The error depends on μ and σ from the nonlinearity g; please clarify why this dependence is necessary and how it reconciles with the claimed equivalence to the linear case.  

- Overall recommendation: address the Gaussian assumption, add relevant literature positioning (SIM/SDR and Yi et al.), and clarify practical/interpretational points to improve clarity and impact.
