- Studies Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0), showing asymptotic equivalence to a linear model with scaled noise under Gaussian measurements.
- Provides precise asymptotic error expressions (not just upper bounds) for a wide range of regularizers (ℓ1, group ℓ1, nuclear norm).
- Derives that non-linear measurement performance matches linear model yi = μai^T x0 + σzi with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].
- Extends Plan and Vershynin’s work by replacing bounds with exact error characterizations in the high-dimensional regime m/n = δ ∈ (0,∞).
- Uses the convex Gaussian min-max theorem to generalize analysis to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.
- Shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for q-bit compressive sensing — a practical design implication.
- Theoretical results are rigorous and supported by comprehensive numerical validation.
- Proofs are technically sound and assumptions are generally well-justified.
- Clarity is good overall, but Assumption 1 needs stronger motivation and interpretation (e.g., via variational analysis).
- Notation for the Moreau envelope (“proximal function”) should be clarified to match standard convex analysis terminology.
- The paper should more clearly distinguish the Generalized LASSO formulation from algorithms used to solve it.
- Would benefit from more intuition on practical implications of the precise error expressions.
- Originality is strong: exact error expressions and the quantizer connection are novel contributions.
- Significance is substantial for sensor networks, medical imaging, and other applications with measurement non-linearities.
- Recommendation: accept — substantial theoretical advance, well-validated, with clear practical relevance.
