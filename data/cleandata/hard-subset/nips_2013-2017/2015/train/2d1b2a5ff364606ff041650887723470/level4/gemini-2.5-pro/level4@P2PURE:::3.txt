**Summary:** This paper analyzes the Generalized Lasso for non-linear measurements with a Gaussian design. The authors build upon the common heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear observations. Extending the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], this paper moves to an asymptotic regime to provide novel, precise, and explicit expressions for the estimation error. The accuracy of these theoretical predictions is validated across several important special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. A particularly insightful contribution is the application of these results to the design of optimal quantizers in q-Bit Compressive Sensing and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

**Quality:** This is a high-quality paper that presents a significant contribution.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written, although the treatment of some topics is unbalanced. However, space constraints appear to prevent the inclusion of all necessary details.

**Originality:** The paper builds upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], expanding on it in several novel ways. For instance, the explicit expressions for the estimation error are a new contribution.

**Comments:**

1.  Page 4, line 193: It should be noted that the regularized and penalized versions of the problem are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence of their parameters.

2.  Caption of Figure 1: In the caption, 'n=768' should be typeset as '$n$=768'.

3.  Page 5, line 247: There appears to be a typo in the line beginning "When...".

4.  Page 5, line 294: The function referred to as the "proximal function" is widely known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index τ. The `prox` notation is potentially confusing, as it is standardly used for the proximal operator, not the Moreau envelope. I suggest changing the notation to avoid this ambiguity.

5.  Page 5, Assumption 1: It would be instructive to provide a more formal interpretation of this assumption, perhaps by drawing on results from variational analysis.

6.  The term "generalized Lasso" should not be used to refer to an algorithm. It is more accurately described as a convex program or, using terminology from compressed sensing, a decoder. The authors should revise the text to reflect this distinction.

7.  The experiments effectively demonstrate the application of the generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and validate the theorems' predictions for the estimation error.
