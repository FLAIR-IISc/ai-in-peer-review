

This paper makes a significant contribution by extending precise asymptotic characterizations of the l2-lasso (square root lasso) to single-index models, while relaxing the standard Gaussian prior assumption on the signal vector. The work provides valuable theoretical insights into the statistical behavior of lasso-type estimators under more general conditions, representing a meaningful advancement in the literature. The manuscript is well-written and clearly structured, though two points warrant attention for improved clarity and impact.

First, the connection between single-index models and linear models with scaled signals and non-standard noise—established rigorously in Appendix A (referencing Brillinger, 1982)—should be explicitly stated in the main text. The equivalence is critical for motivating the model setup and justifying the approach taken in Section 3. As noted in the block quote on line 140, the authors claim this equivalence "allows us to reduce the problem to a linear model," but this heuristic is currently confined to the appendix. Integrating this key insight earlier (e.g., in Section 2) would strengthen the narrative flow and help readers immediately grasp the modeling rationale, rather than requiring them to consult supplementary material.

Second, while the paper focuses on the l2-lasso (square root lasso), it does not address a practical implication of its primary theoretical advantage over the standard l2²-lasso: the regularization parameter’s independence from the unknown noise variance σ. The square root lasso’s “oracle” property—where the optimal λ scales as √(log p / n) without requiring knowledge of σ—motivates its use in practice. However, the paper’s extension to single-index models leaves open whether this key benefit persists in the non-linear setting. Specifically, the authors should discuss whether the noise structure in single-index models (e.g., heteroscedasticity induced by the link function) alters the dependence of optimal λ on σ. Addressing this would directly answer a critical question for practitioners: *Is the square root lasso still preferable to the l2²-lasso in single-index contexts, or does the non-linear structure reintroduce σ-dependence in the regularization parameter?* A brief analysis or discussion of this point would significantly enhance the paper’s practical relevance.

The manuscript’s core contribution remains strong: precise characterization of the l2-lasso’s performance in single-index models under non-Gaussian priors. The theoretical framework is sound, and the asymptotic analysis is well-executed. To maximize its impact, the authors should refine the exposition to clarify the linear model equivalence upfront and explicitly engage with the practical implications of their results for regularization parameter selection. These minor revisions would elevate an already solid contribution into a more compelling resource for both theoretical and applied researchers working with high-dimensional non-linear models. The paper is suitable for publication pending these clarifications.
