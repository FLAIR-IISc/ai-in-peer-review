- Reviewer’s opinion unchanged by others and authors’ response; reviewer unfamiliar with single-index/sufficient-dimension-reduction literature.
- Paper derives asymptotic guarantees for the generalized square-root LASSO with Gaussian measurements possibly transformed by unknown nonlinear functions.
- Provides a precise asymptotic characterization linking nonlinear measurements to equivalent scaled/noisy linear measurements (parameters μ and σ).
- Derives explicit, asymptotically precise estimation-error expressions for regularized Generalized LASSO in high dimensions.
- Identifies the Lloyd–Max quantizer as optimal for minimizing estimation error in the considered setting.
- Paper is technically rigorous with a clear progression of arguments and a well-defined asymptotic framework.
- Writing is generally clear and well-structured, with consistent notation and clearly stated main results.
- Contribution is original and significant for problems with nonlinear/quantized measurements, informing algorithm design and tuning.
- Multiple typos and minor errors, especially in proofs, detract from polish and readability.
- Empirical validation is limited (only a single figure); more numerical experiments are needed to strengthen practical claims.
- Strong assumption of i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix entries limits generality; robustness to other ensembles not studied.
- Literature review could better address connections to single-index model and sufficient-dimension-reduction work.
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on fixing typos and ideally expanding empirical evaluation.
