The reviews of other reviewers and the authors' response have not significantly affected my opinion of the paper. That said, I am not familiar with the single index model/sufficient dimension reduction literature, so I could very well be unaware of previous work that may be relevant. The authors derive asymptotic guarantees for the generalized square-root lasso applied to Gaussian measurements that are (optionally) transformed by a possibly unknown nonlinear function. The results are very interesting, as they provide a precise characterization of the performance of the method, and the paper is very well written (although there are quite a few typos, especially in the proof section).

This paper considers the problem of estimating an unknown signal from nonlinear measurements, specifically addressing the Generalized LASSO framework. The authors establish a significant connection between the performance of the Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements and its performance with corresponding scaled and noisy linear measurements. They derive precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error, offering a strong theoretical foundation for utilizing the Generalized LASSO in scenarios with nonlinearities, such as those arising from quantized measurements.

The paper builds upon classical work by Brillinger and more recent contributions from Plan and Vershynin. The authors significantly strengthen these results by providing explicit, asymptotically precise expressions for the estimation error in the regularized Generalized LASSO setting as the dimensions of the problem grow. A key finding is that, asymptotically, the performance is equivalent to that of linear measurements with appropriate scaling and noise characteristics, quantified by parameters μ and σ. An interesting consequence highlighted is the identification of the Lloyd-Max quantizer as optimal for minimizing estimation error.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper appears technically rigorous, with a clear progression of arguments and a well-defined asymptotic framework. The derivation of precise error expressions is a substantial contribution.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly structured, providing a good explanation of the problem setup, related work, and the main contributions. The notation is consistent, and the key results are clearly stated.
*   **Originality:** The derivation of asymptotically precise error expressions for the Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements represents a novel and significant advance in the field. The connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is also a noteworthy result.
*   **Significance:** The results have the potential to be impactful in applications where nonlinear measurements are prevalent, such as compressed sensing with quantized data. The precise error characterization provides valuable insights for algorithm design and parameter tuning.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Typos/Minor Errors:** As noted, the paper contains several typos, particularly within the proof sections. While these do not fundamentally undermine the technical arguments, they detract from the overall polish and readability.
*   **Limited Empirical Validation:** While the paper presents theoretical results, empirical validation is limited to a single figure. More extensive numerical experiments could strengthen the findings and provide practical insights.
*   **Assumptions:** The assumption of i.i.d. Gaussian entries in the measurement matrix is relatively strong. Investigating the robustness of the results to other matrix ensembles could be beneficial.
*   **Literature Review:** While the paper references relevant work, the connection to the single-index model/sufficient dimension reduction literature could be further explored. The authors acknowledge a lack of familiarity with this area, and a more thorough review could uncover potentially relevant prior work.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound contribution to the theory of sparse recovery with nonlinear measurements.
*   The derivation of precise asymptotic error expressions is a significant achievement.
*   The results have the potential to impact practical applications of compressed sensing and related fields.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly structured.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The presence of typos detracts from the overall quality of the presentation.
*   Limited empirical validation.
*   The strong assumption of Gaussian i.i.d. matrix entries.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field. The theoretical results are significant, and the paper is generally well-written and clear. The weaknesses identified are relatively minor and do not fundamentally undermine the quality of the work. I recommend acceptance, contingent upon the authors addressing the typos and potentially expanding the empirical evaluation.
