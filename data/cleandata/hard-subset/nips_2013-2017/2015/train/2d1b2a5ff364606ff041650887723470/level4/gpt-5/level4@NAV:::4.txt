This paper studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link function. Building on recent work by Plan and Vershynin, it analyzes the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0. The main finding, stated in Theorem 2.1, is that the asymptotic error is exactly the same regardless of whether the link is linear or nonlinear.

Theorem 2.1 assumes that the linear measurement vectors are sampled from a normal distribution, which may be restrictive in some scenarios. The authors should explain why this assumption is necessary for the conclusion.

A few questions: 1. How should the regularizer be chosen based on the set \kai containing x0? While the paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, it does not appear to address this selection issue. 2. Although the result equates nonlinear and linear measurements, the conclusion depends on μ and σ, which characterize the difficulty of estimation under the nonlinear link g. Do the authors have thoughts on whether these dependencies are essential?

Some references seem to be missing. 1. The single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literatures study linear vector/subspace estimation under an unknown link function, but the paper does not review this area. 2. A recent paper, “Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation” by Yi et al., introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery even when μ = 0, and establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under suitable conditions, supported by rigorous theory and empirical results.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with fairly interesting results.
