This paper studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link function. Building on recent work by Plan and Vershynin, it analyzes the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized Lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0. The main finding is that, regardless of whether the link is linear or nonlinear, the asymptotic error matches that of the linear case, as stated in Theorem 2.1.

Theorem 2.1 assumes that the linear measurement vectors are drawn from a normal distribution, which may be rather restrictive in practice. The authors should explain why this assumption is necessary for the result and whether (or how) it might extend to more general designs.

A few questions:
1. How should one choose the regularizer based on the set K containing x0? While the paper discusses convex regularizers, it does not appear to address how to select the regularizer given prior knowledge that x0 lies in K.
2. Although the results show that the nonlinear case is equivalent to the linear measurement case, the conclusions still depend on μ and σ, which characterize the difficulty of estimation under the nonlinear link g. Do the authors have thoughts on the necessity of these dependencies?

Some references are missing:
1. The literatures on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction study linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, yet the paper does not review work in this area.
2. The recent paper “Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation” by Yi et al. proposes a new algorithm for sparse recovery even when the link function has μ = 0, and establishes the equivalence between high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear links under appropriate conditions, supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with interesting results.
