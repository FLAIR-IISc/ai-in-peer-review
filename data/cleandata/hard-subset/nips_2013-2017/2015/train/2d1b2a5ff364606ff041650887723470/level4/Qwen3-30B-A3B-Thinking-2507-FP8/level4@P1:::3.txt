

Summary: This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso for non-linear measurement models with Gaussian design matrices. The authors extend the work of Plan and Vershynin by establishing precise asymptotic expressions for estimation error when measurements follow yi = gi(ai^T x0), where gi are independent copies of a link function g. Their key insight is that, under appropriate conditions, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements is asymptotically identical to that of a linear measurement model with scaled and noisy observations yi = μai^T x0 + σzi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ) - μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1). The authors derive explicit error expressions for a wide range of regularizers (including L1, group-L1, and nuclear norms) and validate their results through extensive theoretical analysis and numerical experiments.

Quality: The paper makes a technically sound contribution with rigorous asymptotic analysis. The authors establish precise error predictions that generalize and extend previous results by Plan and Vershynin, who provided only upper bounds. The theoretical framework is well-developed, with careful attention to the interplay between the link function, signal structure, and regularizer. The results hold for general convex regularizers and link functions, making them broadly applicable. The experimental validation across multiple signal structures (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) strengthens the technical contribution.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written and well-organized, though some sections could benefit from more detailed explanations. The connection between non-linear and linear measurement models is clearly articulated, and the main results are presented with appropriate mathematical precision. The exposition of the Moreau envelope and proximal operators could be improved for better accessibility to readers unfamiliar with convex analysis.

Originality: The precise error expressions for the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements represent a significant advancement over previous work. While Plan and Vershynin established qualitative equivalence between non-linear and linear measurements, this paper provides explicit asymptotic error expressions that are novel in this context. The connection to optimal quantizer design (specifically, the Lloyd-Max quantizer) provides a compelling application that demonstrates the practical significance of the theoretical results.

Significance: The results have important implications for compressed sensing with quantized measurements, particularly in the design of optimal quantization schemes. The authors demonstrate that the optimal quantizer for minimizing estimation error is precisely the Lloyd-Max quantizer, which is widely used in signal processing. This bridges theoretical analysis with practical implementation considerations, making the work highly relevant to both researchers and practitioners.

Strengths:
- Precise error characterization for Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements
- Comprehensive analysis covering multiple signal structures (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank)
- Clear connection between theoretical results and practical quantizer design
- Rigorous mathematical treatment with careful attention to technical assumptions
- Comprehensive experimental validation across diverse settings

Weaknesses:
- The treatment of Assumption 1 would benefit from more formal interpretation using variational analysis
- The notation for the Moreau envelope (referred to as "proximal function" in the paper) is non-standard; it would be clearer to use the standard term "Moreau envelope"
- The paper occasionally refers to the Generalized Lasso as an "algorithm" rather than a convex optimization program
- Minor typographical errors (e.g., "shouldbe" in Figure 1 caption) that should be corrected

Specific comments:
1. Page 4, line 193: The relationship between regularized and penalized versions of the Generalized Lasso is well-known in optimization theory. The authors should clarify that these formulations are equivalent under appropriate parameter correspondence.

2. Figure 1 caption: The notation "n=768" should be formatted as "$n$=768" for consistency with mathematical typesetting standards.

3. Page 5, line 247: There is a minor grammatical error ("When ...") that should be corrected.

4. Page 5, line 294: The term "proximal function" is non-standard. The authors should use the standard convex analysis term "Moreau envelope" throughout the paper to avoid confusion.

5. Page 5, Assumption 1: The authors should provide a more formal interpretation of this assumption using results from variational analysis to help readers understand its significance.

6. The paper occasionally refers to the Generalized Lasso as an "algorithm" rather than a convex optimization program. This terminology should be consistently corrected throughout.

7. The paper's application to q-bit compressive sensing is particularly compelling. The authors correctly identify that the optimal quantizer is the Lloyd-Max quantizer, which is a significant contribution to the field of quantized compressed sensing.

Overall, this is a strong contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics and compressed sensing. The paper addresses an important problem with significant theoretical and practical implications, and the results advance our understanding of how to handle non-linear measurements in structured signal recovery. The technical content is sound, the analysis is rigorous, and the practical implications are well-articulated. I recommend acceptance.
