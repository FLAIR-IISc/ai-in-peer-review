

This paper extends prior work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the square-root Lasso (l2-lasso) to single-index models, relaxing the standard Gaussian prior assumption on the signal vector $x_0$. This represents a significant contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of Lasso-type estimators. The manuscript is well-written and technically sound.

However, two points warrant clarification to strengthen the presentation:

1.  **Equivalence to Linear Model:** As noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is statistically equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While Appendix A rigorously establishes this connection, it should also be explicitly stated in the main text. This would provide immediate heuristic justification for the claim made in the blockquote on line 140, improving accessibility for readers encountering the model for the first time.

2.  **Practical Relevance of Square-Root Lasso Advantage:** The paper focuses on the square-root Lasso (l2-lasso), whose primary practical advantage over the standard Lasso (l2^2-lasso) is that its optimal regularization parameter (the "oracle" value) does not depend on the unknown noise variance $\sigma$. The current analysis, while theoretically rigorous for the single-index setting, does not explicitly address whether this key practical benefit of the square-root Lasso persists in the single-index model. It would be valuable for the authors to discuss whether their results support the use of the square-root Lasso over the standard Lasso in this context, particularly given the relaxation of the Gaussian assumption. A brief analysis or discussion on how the noise structure in the single-index model impacts the regularization parameter selection could significantly enhance the paper's practical relevance.

Overall, the paper makes a valuable theoretical contribution by extending precise asymptotic analysis to a broader model class. Addressing the points above—particularly incorporating the linear model equivalence into the main text and clarifying the practical implications of the square-root Lasso's noise-independence property—would substantially improve the paper's clarity and impact. The work is suitable for publication in NIPS upon addressing these minor but important points.
