- Central result: In the high-dimensional asymptotic regime, Generalized LASSO on non-linear measurements yi = g(a_i^T x0) has identical performance to LASSO on linearized measurements yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, with μ,σ determined by g.

- Main contribution: Provides the first asymptotically precise, explicit error characterizations for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, strengthening prior upper bounds.

- Theoretical scope: Results capture the interplay between signal structure, regularizer, link function (via μ and σ), and problem dimensions.

- Applicability: Framework applies broadly to convex regularizers and structured signals; demonstrated for l1 (LASSO), l1,2 (Group LASSO), and nuclear-norm regularization.

- Practical application: Shows optimal q-bit quantizer design reduces to minimizing σ²/μ² and connects to the classic Lloyd–Max quantizer.

- Empirical support: Simulations show a close match between theoretical predictions and empirical LASSO performance.

- Strength — significance: Provides a rigorous justification for treating many non-linear measurement systems as scaled, noisy linear ones, simplifying analysis and design.

- Strength — technical novelty: Moves beyond non-asymptotic bounds to exact asymptotic error formulas, a substantial advance.

- Strength — generality: Demonstrates versatility by covering sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery cases.

- Weakness — Assumption 1: Key assumption is presented abstractly and needs a more intuitive or formally connected explanation to variational/convex geometry concepts.

- Weakness — terminology: Should call Generalized LASSO an estimator/convex program (not an "algorithm"); replace nonstandard prox notation with "Moreau envelope" and standard proximal operator terminology.

- Weakness — presentation: Paper is dense; include a brief high-level proof sketch in the main text to provide intuition for the convergence to the linear model.

- Minor issues/typos: Minor editorial fixes needed (e.g., typeset n in Figure 1, typo at p.5 line 247, revise Moreau envelope notation at p.5 line 294).
