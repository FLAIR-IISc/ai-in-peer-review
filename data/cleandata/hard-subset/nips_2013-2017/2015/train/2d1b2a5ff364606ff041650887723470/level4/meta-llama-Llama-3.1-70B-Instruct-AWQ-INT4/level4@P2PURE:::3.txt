Summary: This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, building upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17]. By leveraging this foundation and moving to an asymptotic regime, the authors derive novel, precise, and explicit expressions for error estimation. These predictions are validated through various special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm. Furthermore, the application to designing optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit Compressed Sensing (CS) and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer offers valuable insights.

Quality: This is a well-crafted piece of work.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written, although the treatment is somewhat unbalanced, likely due to space constraints, which prevents the inclusion of all necessary details.

Originality: The paper expands upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] in several novel aspects, including the derivation of explicit expressions for performance estimation error.

Comments:

1. On page 4, line 193, it is noted that the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent, given an appropriate correspondence.

2. The caption of Figure 1 should be corrected to read '$n$=768' instead of 'n=768'.

3. A typo is present on page 5, line 247, in the phrase "When ...".

4. On page 5, line 294, the term "proximal function" is more commonly known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index $\tau$. The notation "prox" is typically used for the proximal point/operator, rather than the Moreau envelope. It is suggested that the notation be revised to avoid confusion.

5. It would be instructive to provide a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 on page 5, utilizing results from variational analysis.

6. The authors should refrain from referring to the Generalized Lasso as an algorithm in the text, as it is more accurately described as a convex program or decoder, in line with common terminology in Compressed Sensing.

7. The experiments demonstrate the application of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements, as well as the theorems' predictions of estimation error. This paper proves that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that with linear and noisy (Gaussian) measurements. The authors provide novel, precise, and explicit expressions for estimation error and validate their findings through various special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm. The application to designing optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.
