- Main result: Proves that in the high-dimensional limit, Generalized LASSO on single-index (non-linear) measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on a corresponding linear model with a rescaled signal and effective noise.  
- Scaling/noise characterization: The rescaling factor μ and effective noise σ depend only on the non-linear link function g.  
- Broad regularizer coverage: Analysis applies to a wide class of convex regularizers (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank, etc.).  
- Relaxed prior assumptions: The work removes the common requirement of a Gaussian signal prior.  
- Explicit MSE formulas: Derives explicit, interpretable mean-squared-error formulas linking measurements, signal structure, regularizer, and link function.  
- Technical rigor: Uses sophisticated high-dimensional probability tools and appears technically sound.  
- Empirical validation: Numerical simulations show excellent agreement with theoretical predictions.  
- Practical application: Demonstrates utility via an application to optimal quantizer design.  
- Suggestion 1 (exposition): The intuitive/heuristic justification (rooted in Brillinger 1982) for the core equivalence should be brought into the main text rather than only the appendix.  
- Suggestion 2 (practicality of square-root LASSO): Discuss whether the noise-blind advantage of l2 (square-root) LASSO remains practically useful given that the effective noise σ depends on the unknown link g.  
- Strength summary: Novel, significant, and general contribution extending precise high-dimensional analysis to non-linear measurement settings.  
- Acceptance recommendation: Minor exposition suggestions but no technical flaws; paper is recommended for acceptance.
