- Main result: Shows generalized LASSO with non-linear single-index measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on linear measurements y = μAx0 + σz with μ = E[γ g(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].
- Precise error formula: Derives a scalar convex program in three variables whose solution α* gives the asymptotic squared error ‖x̂ − μ x0‖^2 → α*^2.
- Scope: Framework handles general convex regularizers (ℓ1, group ℓ1,2, nuclear norm) and specializes to sparse, block-sparse, and low-rank recovery.
- Quantizer application: Reduces q-bit quantizer design to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and shows Lloyd–Max quantizers are stationary points of this objective.
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger’s result to high-dimensional, regularized settings and sharpens Plan–Vershynin non-asymptotic bounds with asymptotically exact formulas.
- Connection to LASSO literature: Complements AMP/CGMT precise analyses by showing transferability of those characterizations to non-linear links via μ and σ.
- Technical soundness: Theorems 2.1–2.2 are rigorous under Gaussian designs and linear asymptotics; assumptions are standard for CGMT analyses and verified for key regularizers.
- Empirical validation: Simulations support theoretical predictions across regimes and regularizers.
- Rigor/completeness: Proofs are deferred to the appendix; main text states assumptions clearly and gives interpretable formulations.
- Clarity improvements: Recommend moving the heuristic “non-linear equals linear” explanation (Brillinger-style) into the main text to motivate results earlier.
- μ and σ interpretation: Request clearer guidance on the meaning and estimation of μ and σ^2 when g is unknown and how to use the formulas in practice.
- λ tuning guidance: Ask for more intuition and actionable rules for selecting λ, especially the λcrit phenomenon for δ < 1.
- Minor fixes: Correct notation inconsistency for σ/σ^2 and typos (e.g., “mesaurements,” “uknown,” “nuclearnorm”).
- Square-root LASSO: Suggest discussing whether square-root LASSO remains oracle-free of σ under non-linear links and comparing it empirically to ℓ2^2-LASSO.
- Low-rank case: Recommend including a concrete low-rank example (e.g., random orthogonal singular vectors) and a recipe to compute the Moreau-envelope limit F(c1,c2,c3).
- Robustness: Ask for remarks or experiments on sensitivity to non-Gaussian or mildly correlated designs (e.g., sub-Gaussian A).
- μ = 0 regime: Request explicit discussion of limitations when μ = 0 (e.g., sign-only links) and remedies like dithering to restore identifiability.
- Estimating μ, σ^2: Suggest a brief note on method-of-moments or other consistent estimators from (A,y) and whether plug-in use preserves accuracy.
- Code/data: Encourage releasing code for solving the scalar convex program and reproducing figures.
- Pros: First asymptotically exact error characterization for generalized LASSO under broad non-linear links; clear linear-equivalence insight; covers multiple regularizers; useful quantizer design implication.
- Cons: Relies on Gaussian design and leaves robustness empirically unaddressed; Assumption 1 is technical and only partially instantiated (low-rank); insufficient practical guidance on λ and μ/σ estimation; minor presentation/placement issues.
- Recommendation: Strong accept after minor revisions addressing clarity, early heuristic exposition, and brief discussions on λ selection and μ/σ estimation.
