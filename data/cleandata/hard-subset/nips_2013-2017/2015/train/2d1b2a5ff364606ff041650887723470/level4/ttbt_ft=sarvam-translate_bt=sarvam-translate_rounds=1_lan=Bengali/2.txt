This article expands on the work of accurately identifying the statistical performance of l2-Lasso in a single-index model. The article also relaxes the assumption of a normal Gaussian distribution over the prior (x_0). This is a valuable contribution to the literature on the specific asymptotic behavior of the Lasso-type estimator.

The article is well-written. However,
1. As Brillinger (1982) notes, the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with scaled unknown signals and non-standard noise. This connection is strictly described in Appendix A, but it should also be mentioned in the main article to intuitively support the statement in blockquote at line 140.
2. The article focuses on l2-Lasso, also known as square-root-Lasso. The main advantage of l2-Lasso over the popular l2^2-Lasso is that there is no dependence on the noise variance (sigma) of the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter (usually unknown). A practical question that may clarify the results is whether this justification of l2-Lasso over l2^2-Lasso is valid.
