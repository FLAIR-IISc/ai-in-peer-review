- Analyzes Generalized LASSO for structured signal recovery with non-linear measurements y_i = g(a_i^T x0) and Gaussian measurement matrix A.
- Main contribution: asymptotically exact characterization showing LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to a linear model y_i = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i in the high-dimensional limit.
- μ and σ are determined by the link function via μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2].
- Provides an explicit asymptotic MSE formula (Theorem 2.2) and specializes results to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.
- Demonstrates practical application to q-bit quantizer design, reducing error minimization to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and using the Lloyd-Max algorithm.
- Significant theoretical advance: first asymptotically precise characterization for LASSO with non-linear observations, improving on prior non-asymptotic bounds.
- Technically rigorous: uses CGMT framework to derive explicit, computable error expressions for a broad class of convex regularizers and link functions.
- Well-written and organized with clear statement of results and useful practical example (quantizer design).
- Empirical simulations closely match theoretical predictions, validating the asymptotic analysis.
- Major limitation: analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix entries, restricting practical applicability and raising questions about extensions to sub-Gaussian ensembles.
- Missing related work: insufficient discussion of single-index models/sufficient dimension reduction literature and omission of Yi et al. (NIPS 2015) addressing the μ=0 case.
- Needs clarification on the role of μ and σ, especially why LASSO fails when μ = 0 and how alternative methods address that scenario.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, insightful, technically sound, empirically validated, and relevant to ML and signal processing communities.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive Gaussian matrix assumption and failure to cite/discuss key contemporaneous work (Yi et al., 2015).
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing missing related work and clarifying limitations (Gaussian assumption and μ ≠ 0 condition).
