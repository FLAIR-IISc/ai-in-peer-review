

This paper studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link function, extending the recent work of Plan and Vershynin by analyzing the asymptotic estimation error of generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with $\mu \neq 0$. The authors demonstrate that, regardless of whether the link function is linear or nonlinear, the asymptotic error matches exactly that of the linear case, as established in Theorem 2.1.  

Theorem 2.1 assumes the linear measurement vectors are sampled from a normal distribution, which may be restrictive for certain applications. The authors should clarify why this assumption is necessary for the validity of their conclusion.  

Two key questions remain:  
1. How should the regularizer be chosen based on the set $\Omega$ containing the true signal $x_0$? While the paper discusses convex regularizers, it does not address the problem of selecting the regularizer relative to $\Omega$.  
2. Although the result shows equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements, the conclusion depends on $\mu$ and $\sigma$, which characterize the estimation difficulty under the nonlinear link $g$. The authors should elaborate on the necessity of these dependencies.  

Additionally, the paper omits relevant references:  
1. Single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction literature study linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, yet this work fails to cite key contributions in this area.  
2. Yi et al.'s recent paper "Optimal Linear Estimation under Unknown Link Function" introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery with link functions having $\mu = 0$, establishing equivalence between linear and nonlinear estimation under suitable conditions. This paper provides rigorous theoretical proofs and empirical validation, making it a closely related and well-written work that should be discussed.  

The paper presents interesting results, but addressing the above points would significantly strengthen its contribution.
