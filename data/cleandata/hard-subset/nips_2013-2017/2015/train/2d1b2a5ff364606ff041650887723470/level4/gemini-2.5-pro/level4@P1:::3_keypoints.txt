- Analyzes Generalized LASSO for structured-signal recovery from non-linear measurements with Gaussian design and derives asymptotic exact error expressions.  
- Main result: proves asymptotic equivalence between the non-linear problem and a linear LASSO with additive Gaussian noise, characterizing the scaling factor and noise variance.  
- Empirically validates theoretical predictions on standard LASSO (sparse), Group LASSO (group-sparse), and nuclear-norm (low-rank) cases.  
- Applies framework to design optimal q-bit quantizers for compressive sensing, linking to Lloyd–Max quantizer.  
- Novelty: first asymptotically exact characterization of estimation error for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Rigorous equivalence: elevates prior heuristics into a precise, quantitative tool.  
- Generality: applicable to a wide class of convex regularizers and signal structures.  
- Quality: technically strong and significant extension beyond prior non-asymptotic bounds.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized but dense; some sections may be challenging for non-experts.  
- Suggestion: expand intuition behind key theoretical steps (e.g., in the appendix) to improve readability.  
- Terminology: use the standard term “Moreau envelope” instead of `prox` for the Fenchel-conjugate-derived function.  
- Suggestion: provide a formal variational-analysis interpretation of Assumption 1 to clarify its implications.  
- Terminology: refer to Generalized LASSO as a “convex program,” “estimator,” or “decoder” rather than an “algorithm.”  
- Note: briefly state equivalence between constrained and regularized (penalized) LASSO forms when introducing the constrained version.
