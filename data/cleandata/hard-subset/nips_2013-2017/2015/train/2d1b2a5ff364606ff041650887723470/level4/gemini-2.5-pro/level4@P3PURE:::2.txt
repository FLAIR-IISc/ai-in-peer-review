This paper extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the ℓ2-lasso (or square-root lasso) to the setting of single-index models. A key feature of the analysis is the relaxation of the standard Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of the signal. The paper is well-written and represents a worthwhile contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

However, there are a few points that could improve the clarity and impact of the work:

1.  The paper could be improved by discussing the connection between single-index models and linear models earlier in the main text. As established by Brillinger (1982), a single-index model can be viewed as equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is described rigorously in Appendix A, introducing it earlier would provide valuable intuition and help heuristically justify the statement made on line 140.

2.  The analysis focuses on the ℓ2-lasso. A primary motivation for using the ℓ2-lasso over the more conventional ℓ2²-lasso is that the optimal (or "oracle") value of its regularization parameter does not depend on the noise variance σ, which is often unknown in practice. The paper would be strengthened by a discussion of how its theoretical results bear on this practical justification. For instance, do the findings support the claim that the ℓ2-lasso's independence from σ offers a tangible advantage in the settings considered here?
