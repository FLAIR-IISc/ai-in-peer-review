- Paper studies Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian designs, showing a noisy-linear heuristic is accurate asymptotically.  
- Builds on Plan and Vershynin [17], providing novel explicit asymptotic error expressions.  
- Experimental validation strong: predictions match special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm) and application to q-bit compressive sensing / Lloyd–Max quantizer is insightful.  
- Quality: solid contribution.  
- Clarity: well written overall but exposition feels somewhat unbalanced given topic complexity.  
- Originality: extends prior work meaningfully; explicit performance formulas are new.  
- Comment: regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate parameter mapping (p.4, l.193).  
- Caption fix: replace ‘n=768’ with '$n$=768' in Figure 1 caption.  
- Typo: page 5, line 247 contains a typo in “When…”.  
- Notation: the function called “prox” is actually the Moreau envelope of index τ; recommend renaming and using standard proximal operator notation.  
- Suggestion: give a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 via variational analysis results.  
- Terminology: avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; refer to it as a convex program or decoder.
