This article presents a valuable extension of existing work on the statistical performance of the l2-lasso, now applied to the context of single-index models, and importantly, relaxes the typical Gaussian prior assumption on the input variable distribution. This represents a significant and worthwhile addition to the literature concerning the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The manuscript is clearly written and well-organized. However, I suggest two points for consideration:

1. The equivalence between single-index models and linear models with scaled signals and non-standard noise, as highlighted by Brillinger (1982) and rigorously demonstrated in Appendix A, would benefit from mention within the main text. This would provide a heuristic justification for the claim made in the blockquote on line 140.

2. The article specifically investigates the l2-lasso (or square root-lasso). A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more common l2^2-lasso is its regularization parameter’s independence from the noise variance (sigma). The theoretical results presented could be further strengthened by exploring whether they support this practical justification for preferring the l2-lasso in situations where sigma is unknown.
