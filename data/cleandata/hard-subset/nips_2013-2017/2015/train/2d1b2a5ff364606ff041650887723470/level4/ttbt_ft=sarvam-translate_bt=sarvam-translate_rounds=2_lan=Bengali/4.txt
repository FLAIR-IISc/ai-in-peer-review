This paper discusses linear estimation with a non-linear link function. Inspired by the recent work of Plan and Varshinin, it studies the error of infinite estimates using generalized Lasso for arbitrary link functions with \mu \ne 0. It is shown that the infinite error is exactly equal to the error shown in Theorem 2.1, regardless of whether the link function is linear or non-linear.

Theorem 2.1 will apply when the vector of linear estimates is sampled from a normal distribution, which can be strict in some cases. The author may need to explain why this assumption is necessary for this conclusion.

A few questions: 1. How to choose a regularizer based on the set \kai where x_0 is located? Although this paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, it seems that the previously mentioned problem has not yet been solved. 2. Although the result shows that the linear estimate is equal to the non-linear estimate, the conclusion involves \mu and \sigma, which describes the difficulty of estimating from the non-linear function g. I would like to know if the authors have any idea about the necessity of these dependencies.

Some references are missing. 1. Research on linear vector/subspace estimates under unknown link functions with single-index models and insufficient dimensionality reduction. However, this paper does not review any research papers in this field. 2. A recent paper by E and others, "Optimal linear estimates under unknown linear estimates," also introduces new algorithms for rare recovery with link functions involving zero \mu. The paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimates with linear and non-linear link functions under appropriate conditions. The conclusion is supported by careful theoretical evidence and empirical results. It is a well-written paper with quite interesting results.
