- Studies Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, treating non-linear observations as noisy linear ones.  
- Main contribution: asymptotic, precise explicit formulas for estimation error extending Plan and Vershynin [17].  
- Empirical/theoretical validation via special cases: Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization.  
- Insightful application to optimal q-bit quantizer design and its relation to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.  
- Technical analysis is sound, well-motivated, and demonstrates deep literature knowledge.  
- Paper is generally well-written but dense; space limits leave some details concise.  
- Notation is mostly consistent and accessible.  
- Novelty: explicit error expressions and quantizer-design connection are significant original contributions.  
- Suggestion: change “proximal function” terminology to “Moreau envelope” and adjust notation (prox usually denotes proximal operator).  
- Suggestion: avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; refer to it as a convex program or decoder.  
- Suggestion: provide a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using variational analysis results.  
- Minor editorial fixes: fix caption formatting for Figure 1 and a typo on page 5.  
- Pro-acceptance: addresses an important problem, offers novel theory, precise error analysis, practical implications, and strong technical execution.  
- Con-acceptance: mathematically dense and asymptotic setting may limit accessibility and some practical applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with minor presentation and terminology improvements.
