- Central finding: Applying standard LASSO to non-linear measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) is effective and its performance can be precisely characterized.  
- Asymptotic equivalence: In high-dimensional i.i.d. Gaussian settings, error of LASSO with non-linear measurements equals that of a linear model y_i = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i.  
- Explicit formulas: The paper gives closed-form expressions for the scaling μ and effective noise σ in terms of the link function g.  
- Technical advance: Provides asymptotically exact deterministic formulas for limiting MSE for general convex regularizers (Theorems 2.2, 2.3).  
- Methodology: Analysis builds on the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) and appears technically solid.  
- Empirical validation: Numerical simulations show excellent agreement with theoretical predictions across various settings.  
- Generality: Framework covers many signal structures (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and non-linearities including 1-bit quantization.  
- Practical implication: Reduces design (e.g., q-bit quantizer) to minimizing σ²/μ², connecting to Lloyd-Max quantizer design.  
- Literature context: Should better situate results within the broader single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written but needs proofreading to fix typos in supplementary material and appendices.  
- Assumptions/limitations: Results assume i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices and a specific high-dimensional asymptotic limit; extensions to other ensembles or non-asymptotic guarantees should be discussed.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; reviewer recommends acceptance.
