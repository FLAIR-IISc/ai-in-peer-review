- Paper analyzes linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, extending Plan and Vershynin’s work to generalized LASSO for arbitrary links with µ ≠ 0.
- Main theoretical claim (Theorem 2.1): asymptotic estimation error for generalized LASSO is equivalent for nonlinear and linear measurements.
- Paper gives precise error expressions that incorporate the link function, signal structure, regularizer, and regularization parameter.
- Assumes i.i.d. Gaussian entries for measurement matrix A, which may be a restrictive modeling choice.
- The manuscript should discuss sensitivity to deviations from Gaussian A or extensions to other matrix ensembles.
- Lacks practical guidance on how to choose the appropriate convex regularizer based on the structure of x0.
- The dependence of results on µ and σ raises unanswered questions about whether knowledge of g provides significant performance gains.
- Related work is incomplete: misses literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction studying estimation under unknown links.
- Overlooks Yi et al. (2015), which addresses sparse recovery when µ = 0 and shows related linear/nonlinear equivalence under different conditions.
- Strength: provides rigorous, novel theoretical analysis of generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements.
- Strength: the equivalence result between linear and nonlinear measurements is a significant simplification for analysis.
- Strength: the derived error formulas clearly identify factors affecting estimation performance.
- Strength: connection to optimal quantization (Section 2.3) offers a practical application of the theory.
- Weakness: Gaussian measurement matrix assumption may limit applicability.
- Weakness: discussion on selecting regularizers is insufficient.
- Weakness: related work section needs expansion to include relevant complementary studies.
- Overall: well-written and technically sound, but would be strengthened by addressing assumptions, regularizer selection, and literature coverage.
