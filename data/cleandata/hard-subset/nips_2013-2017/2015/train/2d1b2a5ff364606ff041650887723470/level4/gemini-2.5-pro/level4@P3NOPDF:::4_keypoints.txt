- Main contribution: Shows generalized LASSO's asymptotic estimation error for nonlinear links equals the linear case when the link derivative has non-zero mean (μ ≠ 0) (Theorem 2.1).  
- Strengths: Rigorous proofs, empirical validation, and clear writing.  
- Major concern: Theoretical results assume Gaussian measurement vectors—need discussion on whether this is fundamental or can be relaxed (e.g., to sub-Gaussian ensembles).  
- Major concern: Missing connections to Single Index Models (SIM) and Sufficient Dimension Reduction (SDR) literature on estimating linear vectors under unknown link functions.  
- Major concern: Missing detailed comparison to concurrent work (Yi et al.) that handles the μ = 0 case and claims similar equivalence and sparse recovery results.  
- Question: Practical guidance needed on selecting the convex regularizer when the true signal structure (set K) is unknown.  
- Question: Request for intuition why the asymptotic error still depends on nonlinearity parameters μ and σ rather than being absorbed into a generic noise term if the problem is truly equivalent to the linear case.
