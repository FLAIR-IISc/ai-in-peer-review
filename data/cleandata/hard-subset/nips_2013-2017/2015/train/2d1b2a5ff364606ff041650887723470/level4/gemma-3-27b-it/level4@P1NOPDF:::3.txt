Summary: This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian designs. A common heuristic suggests treating non-linear observations as noisy linear observations. Building on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend their results and, by considering an asymptotic regime, provide novel and precise explicit expressions for error estimation. The accuracy of these predictions is confirmed through several special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm. The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit compressive sensing and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality: This is a solid contribution.

Clarity: The paper is well-written overall, though the treatment feels somewhat unbalanced. The inherent complexity of the topic prevents a fully detailed exposition within the space constraints.

Originality: The paper builds upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] and expands it in several novel aspects. For instance, the explicit expressions for performance estimation error are new.

Comments:

1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate parameter correspondence.

2) Caption of Figure 1: ‘n=768’ should be '$n$=768’.

3) Page 5, line 247: There is a typo in “When…”.

4) Page 5, line 294: The function the authors refer to as the proximal function is well-known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index τ. The notation used here is slightly awkward, as “prox” generally denotes the proximal point or operator, not the Moreau envelope. I suggest a change to address this.

5) Page 5, Assumption 1: Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption using results from variational analysis would be instructive.

6) The authors should avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm” in the text. It is more accurately described as a (convex) program or, using terminology common in compressive sensing, a decoder.

7) Experiments: The authors demonstrate the results of applying the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and show how the theorems predict the estimation error. This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso in the context of non-linear measurements and Gaussian designs, proving that, asymptotically, its estimation performance is equivalent to that of a linear and noisy (Gaussian) measurement model. The authors provide novel and precise explicit expressions for the estimation error and validate their findings with several special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm. The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit compressive sensing and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer remains a particularly insightful aspect of the work.
