

This paper investigates linear estimation under a nonlinear link function, extending recent work by Plan and Vershynin to analyze asymptotic estimation error via generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with $\mu \neq 0$. The key finding (Theorem 2.1) demonstrates that the asymptotic error remains identical regardless of whether the link function is linear or nonlinear.  

Theorem 2.1 assumes the linear measurement vector is drawn from a normal distribution, an assumption that may be overly restrictive in certain applications. The authors should clarify why this normality condition is essential for their conclusion.  

Two specific points require elaboration:  
1. How should the regularizer be selected based on the set $\mathcal{K}$ containing $x_0$? While the paper discusses convex regularizers, it does not address this selection problem.  
2. Although the result equates nonlinear and linear measurements, the conclusion depends on $\mu$ and $\sigma$, which characterize the estimation hardness under the nonlinear link $g$. The authors should discuss the necessity of these dependencies.  

Additionally, the work omits key references:  
- Single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction address linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, yet the paper lacks relevant citations in this area.  
- Yi et al.'s recent paper "Optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions" establishes equivalence between linear and nonlinear link estimation for sparse recovery under proper conditions, including cases with $\mu = 0$. This well-written work provides rigorous theoretical and empirical support for the equivalence claim.
