This paper investigates the problem of linear estimation from measurements observed through a nonlinear link function. Building upon the recent work of Plan and Vershynin, the authors study the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized Lasso for an arbitrary link function, under the condition that a key parameter, $\mu$, is non-zero. The central finding, presented in Theorem 2.1, is that the asymptotic estimation error is identical for both linear and nonlinear link functions. This is a well-written paper with an interesting and potentially significant result.

However, there are several points that could be clarified and strengthened.

**Major Points:**

1.  **Restrictive Measurement Assumption:** The main result in Theorem 2.1 relies on the assumption that the linear measurement vectors are sampled from a Gaussian distribution. This can be a restrictive assumption in many practical applications. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation of why this assumption is necessary for their analysis. Is it a fundamental requirement for the conclusion to hold, or is it an artifact of the proof technique? A discussion on the potential for relaxing this assumption to sub-Gaussian or other distributions would significantly broaden the impact of the work.

2.  **Missing Connections to Relevant Literature:** The paper is missing citations to at least two highly relevant areas of research.
    *   **Single Index Models (SIM) and Sufficient Dimension Reduction (SDR):** These fields are fundamentally concerned with estimating a linear vector or subspace when the observations are passed through an unknown, nonlinear link function. This is precisely the problem setting of the current paper. A discussion of the rich literature in SIM and SDR would provide valuable context and help to position the paper's contributions more clearly against existing work.
    *   **"Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear link function" by Yi et al.:** This recent work is directly related to the topic of this paper. Yi et al. introduce an algorithm for sparse recovery that works even for link functions where $\mu=0$, a case not covered by the present analysis. They also establish a similar equivalence between high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions, supported by both theoretical proofs and empirical results. Given the strong overlap in both the problem and the conclusions, the authors must discuss this paper and carefully compare their respective results, assumptions, and contributions.

**Questions for the Authors:**

1.  **Practical Choice of the Regularizer:** The analysis assumes the use of a convex regularizer, R(x), which is related to a convex set K that contains the true signal $x_0$. While this is a standard theoretical setup, the paper does not offer guidance on how to select an appropriate regularizer in practice based on prior knowledge of the set K. Could the authors elaborate on the practical implications of this choice?

2.  **Dependence on Link Function Parameters:** The main conclusion is that the asymptotic error is the same for linear and nonlinear measurements. However, the error formula itself depends on $\mu$ and $\sigma$, which are parameters derived from the nonlinear function $g$. This seems to imply that the "hardness" of the estimation problem is still characterized by the properties of the nonlinearity, which appears to be in tension with the headline claim of equivalence. Could the authors provide more intuition on the necessity of these dependencies and reconcile this with the claim that the error is the same as in the linear case?

In summary, the paper presents a valuable theoretical result. The clarity and impact of the paper would be substantially improved by addressing the restrictive Gaussian assumption, positioning the work within the context of existing literature (particularly SIM/SDR and the work of Yi et al.), and clarifying the practical and interpretational questions raised above.
