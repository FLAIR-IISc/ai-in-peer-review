

This paper extends the precise characterization of the LASSO's statistical performance to single-index models with non-linear measurements. The authors demonstrate that the Generalized LASSO with measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0) has asymptotically equivalent estimation performance to a linear measurement model with scaled observations yi = μai^T x0 + σzi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] with γ ~ N(0,1). This work significantly advances the literature by providing precise asymptotic error expressions for the regularized Generalized LASSO, generalizing and improving upon earlier results by Brillinger (1982) and Plan and Vershynin (2015), who established the result only for the unconstrained LASSO and provided only upper bounds. The paper also demonstrates an important application to q-bit compressive sensing, showing how the derived expressions can be used to design optimal quantizers.

The paper is technically sound (Quality), with rigorous proofs and precise asymptotic error expressions that hold for a wide class of regularizers and non-linear functions. The authors provide clear theoretical justification for their results and validate them with empirical illustrations. The work represents a significant advance in the precise analysis of LASSO-type estimators (Originality), extending the state-of-the-art beyond previous bounds to exact expressions. The results have practical significance (Significance) for applications involving non-linear measurements, such as 1-bit quantization and other measurement systems with nonlinearities.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized (Clarity), with appropriate mathematical notation and logical flow. The main contribution is well-motivated and the connection to previous work is adequately discussed.

However, two minor points could be improved: First, the connection to Brillinger's work (1982), which established the equivalence between single-index models and linear models with scaled signals and non-standard noise, is mentioned only in Appendix A. This important insight should be highlighted in the main text to provide heuristic justification for the key theoretical result presented in the paper. Second, the paper focuses on the l2-LASSO (square root LASSO) but does not address whether the key practical advantage of this approach (the regularization parameter being independent of noise variance) carries over to the non-linear measurement setting. This would be a valuable addition for practitioners considering the application of these methods.

Overall, this is a strong contribution to the literature on LASSO-type estimators with precise asymptotic analysis. The results are technically sound, the writing is clear, and the work advances the state-of-the-art in this important area.
