- Paper studies high-dimensional estimation from nonlinear observations yi = g(ai^T x0) using Generalized LASSO with Gaussian design and structured x0, deriving asymptotically exact error formulas in the proportional regime m/n → δ.
- Main result: nonlinear observations are asymptotically equivalent to a linear model y_lin = A(μ x0) + σ z with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].
- Theorem 2.2 provides a single-letter convex optimization whose unique minimizer gives the exact asymptotic squared error, capturing δ, λ, the regularizer via a functional F, and g via (μ, σ^2).
- Specializations include sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and low-rank (nuclear norm) cases, plus a roadmap to compute F for nuclear norm.
- Application to quantized compressed sensing shows LASSO quantizer-design reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and links Lloyd–Max iterations to stationary points of that objective.
- Extends Brillinger’s and Plan–Vershynin’s results by giving precise asymptotics for regularized estimators in the proportional regime and connects to AMP/CGMT literature.
- Relates to single-index and sufficient-dimension-reduction literature and to results handling μ = 0, e.g., Yi et al. (2015).
- Technical soundness: results are clearly stated and appear rigorous, leveraging Gaussian rotational invariance and CGMT-type arguments.
- Central assumption: i.i.d. Gaussian design is crucial; Assumption 1 on existence of F is reasonable and verified for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2, with nuclear-norm details deferred.
- Experiments match theory (including quantization); more non-Gaussian design experiments would strengthen robustness claims.
- Clarity: paper is well organized and readable, but should clarify necessity of Gaussianity and scope of extensions, streamline notation, and state identifiability/unit-norm convention up front.
- Originality: the equivalence principle and precise error characterization for Generalized LASSO under nonlinear observations are novel and important.
- Significance: practical guidance for tuning LASSO under nonlinear observations and quantizer design; likely impactful in compressed sensing and high-dimensional statistics.
- Strengths: clean equivalence principle, broad coverage of regularizers, concrete specializations, and insightful quantizer-design application; strong ties to classical and modern literature.
- Weaknesses: heavy reliance on Gaussian design without detailed robustness discussion; requirement μ ≠ 0 excludes key nonlinearities; some technical details (nuclear norm/F) deferred.
- Suggestions: discuss robustness to sub-Gaussian/orthogonally invariant designs, address μ = 0 case or relate to existing work, advise on choosing f for structure, expand intuition/examples for Assumption 1, and add finite-sample/λ-tuning guidance.
- Minor comments: fix typos and notation inconsistencies (μ, σ, σ^2), ensure complete references (SDR/single-index), clarify ℓ2-LASSO vs square-root LASSO terminology, and number figures/equations consistently.
- Questions for authors: do results extend beyond Gaussian designs; can λ be chosen via Theorem 2.2 (e.g., minimize α*(λ)); sensitivity to estimating μ and σ^2 when g is unknown; worked example of F for a spiked low-rank model?
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, precise theoretical contribution with practical implications and solid integration of prior influential work.
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on Gaussian design, μ ≠ 0 limitation, and some deferred technical details and incomplete related-work coverage.
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on addressing scope limitations and related-work gaps.
