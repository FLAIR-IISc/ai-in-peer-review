Summary:
This paper studies the Generalized Lasso under nonlinear measurements with a Gaussian design. A common heuristic is to treat nonlinear observations as noisy linear ones; building on Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend those results and, by working in an asymptotic regime, derive novel and precise explicit expressions for the estimation error. Their predictions are validated across several important special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. The application to designing optimal quantizers in q-bit compressed sensing and its connection to the Lloyd–Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality:
This is a strong piece of work.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well written, though somewhat unbalanced; space limitations prevent inclusion of all necessary details.

Originality:
While the work builds on Plan and Vershynin [17], it introduces several genuinely new aspects, notably the explicit expressions for the estimation error in the asymptotic regime.

Comments:
1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized versions are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence.

2) Caption of Figure 1: “n=768” should be “$n$=768”.

3) Page 5, line 247: There is a typo in “When …”.

4) Page 5, line 294: What the authors call the proximal function is well known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index $\tau$. The notation is awkward as “prox” is typically reserved for the proximal operator (proximal point), not the Moreau envelope. I suggest changing the notation.

5) Page 5, Assumption 1: It would be instructive to provide a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis.

6) Please avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm.” It is a convex program (or “decoder,” to use terminology common in compressed sensing), not an algorithm.

7) Experiments: The authors present results applying the Generalized Lasso to nonlinear measurements and show how their theorems accurately predict the estimation error. The paper proves that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with such nonlinear, Gaussian-design measurements matches that of a linear model with Gaussian noise. The authors provide novel, precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error and confirm these findings across several special cases (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The application to designing optimal quantizers in q-bit CS, and its relation to the Lloyd–Max quantizer, is very insightful.
