- Paper studies Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, providing precise asymptotic error expressions building on Plan & Vershynin.
- Authors justify the heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear ones and derive explicit estimation-error formulas.
- Results validated empirically across special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm) and match theoretical predictions.
- Application to optimal q-bit quantizer design and connection to Lloyd–Max quantizer is insightful and practically relevant.
- Technical analysis is strong, rigorous, and builds effectively on existing literature.
- Paper is well-written but mathematically dense; notation could be more consistent and some details are omitted due to space.
- Novel contributions include explicit asymptotic error expressions and the quantizer design connection.
- Comment: clarify equivalence between regularized and penalized LASSO (Page 4, line 193).
- Comment: fix Figure 1 caption formatting (‘n=768’ → ‘$n$=768’).
- Comment: fix typographical error on Page 5, line 247.
- Comment: the “proximal function” should be identified as the Moreau envelope and the notation for prox should be changed.
- Comment: give Assumption 1 a more formal interpretation using variational analysis.
- Comment: avoid calling the generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; call it a convex program or decoder.
- Comment: experiments confirm theory but would be stronger with comparisons to other methods.
- Overall assessment: significant, technically sound contribution with strong presentation; recommendation: Strongly Accept.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel precise theory, practical quantizer link, rigorous proofs, clear organization.
- Arguments against acceptance: mathematical density limiting accessibility and limited numerical comparisons to other methods.
