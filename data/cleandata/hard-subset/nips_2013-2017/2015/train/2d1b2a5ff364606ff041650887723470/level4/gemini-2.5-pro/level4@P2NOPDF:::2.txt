This paper extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to the broader class of single-index models. A key strength of the analysis is its relaxation of the common Gaussian assumption on the signal's prior distribution. This work is a valuable contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The paper is well-written and the technical arguments appear sound. However, I have two suggestions that could further improve its clarity and impact:

1.  As noted by Brillinger (1982), a single-index model can be viewed as an equivalent linear model with a scaled signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously detailed in Appendix A, mentioning it earlier in the main text would be beneficial. Specifically, it would provide valuable intuition and help to heuristically justify the statement made on line 140, making the core argument easier for the reader to follow.

2.  The analysis focuses on the l2-lasso (or square-root lasso). A primary motivation for using the l2-lasso over the more conventional l2^2-lasso is that its optimal ("oracle") regularization parameter does not depend on the noise variance, which is typically unknown in practice. The theoretical results presented here are well-positioned to shed light on this practical consideration. I would encourage the authors to add a discussion on whether their precise asymptotic characterization supports or refines this justification for preferring the l2-lasso. Such a discussion would significantly enhance the practical relevance of the work.
