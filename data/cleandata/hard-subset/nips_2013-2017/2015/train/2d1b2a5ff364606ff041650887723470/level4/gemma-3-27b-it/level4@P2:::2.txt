This paper extends the existing literature on the precise statistical performance of the l2-lasso to the setting of single-index models, also known as models with nonlinear measurements. This is a valuable contribution to understanding the asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators. The authors derive explicit expressions for the estimation error, providing a more complete picture than previous work.

The paper is generally well-written and clearly presented. However, a few points could be strengthened. First, the connection between the single-index model and a linear model with scaled signal and non-standard noise, initially observed by Brillinger (1982), is rigorously demonstrated in Appendix A but should also be briefly mentioned within the main text. This would provide a more intuitive justification for the core result presented around line 140, and help readers understand why the Generalized LASSO can perform well despite the nonlinearity.

Second, the paper focuses on the l2-lasso (also termed the square-root-lasso). A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more commonly used l2^2-lasso is that its optimal regularization parameter does not depend on the noise variance (σ^2), which is often unknown in practice. It would be interesting to discuss whether the results presented offer any practical insight into validating this justification for preferring the l2-lasso in this context. Does the equivalence established with the scaled linear model preserve this advantage, or are there scenarios where the noise variance still plays a critical role in parameter selection?

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a rigorous mathematical analysis, building upon established results in high-dimensional statistics and optimization. The derivations appear correct, and the assumptions are clearly stated.
*   **Originality:** Extending precise error analysis techniques to non-linear measurement models is a novel and significant contribution.
*   **Significance:** The results provide a deeper theoretical understanding of the performance of the Generalized LASSO in a wider range of applications, including those involving quantized or otherwise non-linear measurements. The practical consequence regarding optimal quantization (Section 2.3) is particularly noteworthy.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and largely clearly written. The notation is standard, and the mathematical arguments are generally easy to follow.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Practical Discussion:** While the theoretical results are strong, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of their practical implications. The connection to the choice between l2-lasso and l2^2-lasso is one example, but further elaboration on real-world applications would be valuable.
*   **Assumptions:** The assumption of i.i.d. Gaussian entries in the measurement matrix A is relatively strong. While common in the literature, it would be helpful to briefly acknowledge the limitations of this assumption and potential directions for future work that relax it.



**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The technical analysis is sound, the results are original and significant, and the paper is generally well-written. Addressing the minor points raised above would further strengthen the paper and increase its impact. I recommend acceptance.
