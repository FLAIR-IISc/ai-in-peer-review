- Contribution: Extends precise asymptotic characterization of the Generalized LASSO to non-linear measurements and relaxes Gaussian prior assumptions on x0.  
- Writing: Article is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with consistent notation.  
- Theorem note: The equivalence of the single-index model to a linear model with scaled signal and non-standard noise (Brillinger 1982) is proven in Theorem 2.1 but should be mentioned in the main text as heuristic justification.  
- Practical comparison: The paper should discuss whether the Generalized LASSO’s advantage over standard `2-LASSO for non-linear measurements is practically significant.  
- Applicability: Results apply to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank matrix recovery problems.  
- Strength: Provides a precise, novel theoretical characterization of Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements.  
- Strength: Results are supported by theoretical analysis and simulations and cover a wide range of problems.  
- Weakness: Lacks a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the results.  
- Weakness: Assumes measurement matrix A has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, which may limit realism and generalizability.  
- Originality: Novel contribution to the literature on LASSO-type estimators with previously unknown results.  
- Significance: Findings have important implications for regularization parameter choice and measurement design in practice.  
- Quality: Technically sound, thorough literature discussion, and well-supported conclusions.  
- For acceptance: Novel, significant, and technically robust results with clear implications.  
- Against acceptance: Reliance on Gaussian A assumption and insufficient discussion of broader implications and practical comparisons.
