Paper studies Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements under Gaussian design, extending Plan & Vershynin.

Core idea: treat non-linear observations as noisy linear measurements to derive asymptotic error behavior.

Provides precise, explicit asymptotic formulas for estimation error.

Validates predictions on special cases: Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm.

Application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit compressed sensing and relation to Lloyd–Max quantizer.

Quality: solid work demonstrating clear understanding of the topic.

Clarity: well-written but presentation is somewhat unbalanced due to space constraints and omitted details.

Originality: novel extension of [17] with explicit performance-estimation expressions.

Technical note: authors correctly state equivalence between regularized and penalized formulations (pg4, line193).

Minor corrections: Figure 1 caption formatting should read '$n$=768'; typo on pg5, line247.

Notation issue: using 'prox' for the Moreau envelope is awkward—suggest revising to standard proximal/operator notation.

Recommendation: provide a formal variational-analysis interpretation of Assumption 1.

Terminology: avoid calling the generalized Lasso an "algorithm"; use "convex program" or "decoder" for consistency.

Experiments: empirical results confirm asymptotic equivalence to linear noisy measurements and match theoretical predictions.
