- Summary: Studies Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, extending Plan & Vershynin to derive asymptotic, explicit estimation-error expressions validated on several special cases and applied to q‑bit quantizer design (link to Lloyd–Max).

- Quality: Strong piece of work.

- Clarity: Generally well written but exposition feels unbalanced and omits some necessary details.

- Originality: Novel extensions of Plan & Vershynin framework; explicit error expressions are new.

- Comment 1: Regularized (constrained) and penalized formulations are equivalent with an appropriate parameter correspondence.

- Comment 2: Figure 1 caption formatting: “n=768” should be typeset as “$n$=768”.

- Comment 3: Typo on page 5, line 247 in “When …”.

- Comment 4: The “proximal function” used is actually the Moreau envelope; notation is confusing since “prox” usually denotes the proximal operator—suggest changing notation.

- Comment 5: Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal interpretation using variational analysis results.

- Comment 6: Avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; it is a convex program or “decoder” in CS terminology.

- Comment 7 (Experiments): Empirical results confirm the theorems across Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm cases; q‑bit CS application and connection to Lloyd–Max quantizer are insightful.
