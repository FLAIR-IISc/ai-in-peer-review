- Main contribution: Theorem 2.1 shows asymptotic estimation error for generalized Lasso is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions when measurement vectors are Gaussian, extending Plan & Vershynin.

- The normality (Gaussian) assumption on measurement vectors is potentially restrictive and needs justification or clarification.

- The paper fails to explain how to choose the convex regularizer relative to the feasible set Ω containing the true parameter x0.

- The dependence of the asymptotic error on μ and σ (link-function parameters) must be clarified—are they intrinsic to estimation difficulty or artifacts of the analysis?

- Important related work is missing: single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literatures that study estimation under unknown link functions.

- The recent ICML 2015 paper by Yi et al. ("Optimal Linear Estimation Under Unknown Link Function") presents similar equivalence results (including μ=0 cases) with rigorous proofs and experiments and should be discussed.

- Minor language/notation issues need fixing (e.g., "asymptotical"→"asymptotic", "considers about"→"considers"); Theorem 2.1 should explicitly state equivalence requires μ ≠ 0.

- Overall assessment: novel and promising result but weakened by the normality assumption, unaddressed regularizer selection, and missing engagement with key prior work; addressing these would strengthen the contribution.
