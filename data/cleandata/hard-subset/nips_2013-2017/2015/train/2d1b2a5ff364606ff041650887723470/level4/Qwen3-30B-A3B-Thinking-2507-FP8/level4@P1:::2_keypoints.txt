- Extends precise asymptotic analysis of Generalized LASSO to non-linear measurements, linking performance to a scaled linear model.  
- Shows that for i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, error with yi = g(ai^T x0) equals error with linearized yi = μai^T x0 + σzi, with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].  
- Theorem 2.1 proves the asymptotic equivalence between non-linear and scaled linear measurement models.  
- Theorem 2.2 gives an exact expression for estimation error as the solution to a convex optimization.  
- Specializes results to sparse recovery (Thm 2.3), group-sparse, and low-rank matrix recovery settings.  
- Demonstrates practical application to q-bit compressive sensing and shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer minimizes estimation error.  
- Technically rigorous: proofs are careful and extend prior qualitative results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) to exact error characterization.  
- Acknowledges limitations such as the unit-norm signal assumption which restricts applicability to some link functions.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, though the key motivation linking non-linear and linear models should appear in the main text rather than Appendix A.  
- Novel contribution: first precise asymptotic characterization of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements beyond prior upper bounds.  
- Significant practical implications for 1-bit and quantized compressed sensing and for choosing regularization/quantizers.  
- Strengths: first precise characterization, rigorous equivalence proof, comprehensive coverage of signal structures, practical q-bit application, clear theory-to-practice connections.  
- Weaknesses: equivalence motivation placement, focus on l2-LASSO vs. l2^2-LASSO needs clearer justification (regularization "oracle" issue), and unit-norm assumption should be discussed more.  
- Overall: excellent theoretical contribution with minor presentation improvements recommended; strong candidate for acceptance.
