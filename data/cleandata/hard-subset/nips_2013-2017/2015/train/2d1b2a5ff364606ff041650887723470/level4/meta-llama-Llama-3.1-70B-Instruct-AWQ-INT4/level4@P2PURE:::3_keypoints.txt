1. Studies Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, extending Plan and Vershynin to an asymptotic regime.  
2. Derives novel, precise, explicit asymptotic expressions for estimation error.  
3. Validates theoretical predictions on special cases: Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm.  
4. Applies results to design optimal q-bit quantizers in compressed sensing and relates to the Lloyd-Max quantizer.  
5. Paper quality: well-crafted.  
6. Clarity: generally well-written but treatment is somewhat unbalanced and missing some details (likely due to space).  
7. Originality: provides new explicit performance-estimation expressions beyond [17].  
8. Technical note: regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with an appropriate correspondence (p.4, l.193).  
9. Editorial fixes: Figure 1 caption should read n=768; typo on p.5, l.247.  
10. Notation suggestion: call the "proximal function" the Moreau envelope of index tau and reserve "prox" for the proximal operator (p.5, l.294).  
11. Suggestion: provide a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using variational analysis.  
12. Terminology: do not call the Generalized Lasso an "algorithm"; describe it as a convex program or decoder.  
13. Experiments support the claim that asymptotic performance with non-linear measurements equals that with linear noisy Gaussian measurements.
