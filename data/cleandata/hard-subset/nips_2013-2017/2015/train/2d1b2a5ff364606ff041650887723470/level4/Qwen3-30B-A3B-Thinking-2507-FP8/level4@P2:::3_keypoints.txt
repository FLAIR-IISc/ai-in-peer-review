- Addresses whether the Generalized Lasso can recover structured signals from non-linear measurements and establishes asymptotic equivalence to a scaled linear model under Gaussian measurements.  
- Main theoretical result: with yi = g(ai^T x0), the Generalized Lasso error ‖x̂ - μx0‖_2^2 matches that from linear measurements yi = μai^T x0 + σzi with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2] as n,m → ∞.  
- Provides precise asymptotic error expressions valid for all λ > 0 and a broad class of regularizers (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm) and link functions.  
- Demonstrates the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for q-bit compressive sensing using the derived theory.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous proofs and a precise asymptotic characterization that improve on Plan & Vershynin’s upper bounds.  
- Numerical experiments validate the theoretical predictions across different signal structures and measurement models.  
- Notes limitation: signal recovery is only possible up to a constant of proportionality when the link function is unknown.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with clearly articulated main contributions.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation and confusing treatment of the Moreau envelope (called “proximal function”); nonstandard terminology.  
- Terminology problem: occasionally refers to the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm” rather than as a convex optimization program.  
- Originality: first exact asymptotic error expressions for Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and novel connection to quantizer design.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of non-linear measurement recovery and has practical implications for 1-bit measurements, quantized regression, and single-index models.  
- Pros: precise asymptotic results, first exact characterization, practical application to quantizer design, rigorous proofs, and strong extension of prior work.  
- Cons: minor notational inconsistencies, uneven depth in technical exposition, and occasional misuse of terminology.  
- Specific suggested fixes: clarify regularized vs. penalized correspondence (p.4, l.193); format Figure 1 caption consistently; correct typo on p.5, l.247; replace “proximal function” with “Moreau envelope” (p.5, l.294); strengthen Assumption 1 with variational analysis; consistently call Generalized Lasso a convex program.
