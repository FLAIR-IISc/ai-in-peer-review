Summary: This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements and a Gaussian design. Leveraging the heuristic that non-linear observations can often be approximated as noisy linear observations, the authors extend the results of Plan and Vershynin [17]. By considering an asymptotic regime, they derive novel, precise, explicit expressions for error estimation. The accuracy of these predictions is validated through numerous special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. The application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit Compressed Sensing, and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, provides a particularly insightful contribution.

Quality: This is a strong piece of work.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written, although the presentation is somewhat unbalanced. The necessary level of detail is limited by space constraints.

Originality: Building upon the foundational work of Plan and Vershynin [17], this paper expands upon their results in several novel aspects. Specifically, the explicit expressions for performance estimation error represent a significant advance.

Comments:
1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate parameter correspondence.
2) Caption of Figure 1: ‘n=768’ should be ‘$n$=768’.
3) Page 5, line 247: There is a typographical error in ‘When …’.
4) Page 5, line 294: The function the authors refer to as the proximal function is, in convex analysis, known as the Moreau envelope of index \tau. The notation used is somewhat awkward, as “prox” is typically reserved for the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope. A change in notation is recommended.
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption using results from variational analysis would be instructive.
6) The authors should refrain from referring to the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm.” It is more accurately described as a (convex) program or, adopting terminology common in Compressed Sensing, a decoder.
7) Experiments: The authors demonstrate the application of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and show how their theorems predict the estimation error. In essence, this paper proves that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with such measurements is equivalent to that of a linear, noisy (Gaussian) measurement model. The authors provide novel, precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error and confirm their findings with several special cases (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit Compressed Sensing, and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, is particularly insightful.
