Summary:
This paper explores the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, building upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17]. The authors capitalize on this foundation to provide novel, precise, and explicit expressions for error estimation in an asymptotic regime. They validate the accuracy of their predictions through various special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm. Notably, the application of their work to designing optimal quantizers in q-Bit Compressed Sensing (CS) and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer offers valuable insights.

Quality:
This is a well-crafted piece of research that expands existing knowledge in the field.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well-written, although the treatment is somewhat uneven, likely due to space constraints that prevent the inclusion of all necessary details.

Originality:
The paper builds upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] and introduces several novel aspects, including explicit expressions for performance estimation error.

Comments:

1. On page 4, line 193, it is noted that the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent, provided there is an appropriate correspondence between them.

2. The caption of Figure 1 should be corrected to read '$n$=768' instead of 'n=768'.

3. A typo is present on page 5, line 247, in the phrase 'When ...'.

4. On page 5, line 294, the authors refer to the proximal function, which is more commonly known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index τ. The notation used is somewhat awkward, as 'prox' typically denotes the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope. It is suggested that the notation be revised.

5. In Assumption 1 on page 5, providing a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis could be instructive.

6. Throughout the text, the authors should avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an algorithm. Instead, it is more accurately described as a convex program or decoder, in line with commonly used terminology in Compressed Sensing (CS).

7. The experiments presented demonstrate the application of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and how the theorems predict estimation error. The paper proves that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that of linear and noisy (Gaussian) measurements. The authors provide novel, precise, and explicit expressions for estimation error and validate their findings through various special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm. The application of this work to designing optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer offers valuable insights.
