The paper advances precise characterizations of the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to the setting of single-index models. It also relaxes the standard Gaussian assumption on the prior (the distribution of x_0). Overall, it is a valuable contribution to the literature on sharp asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.
The article is well-written. However,
1. as noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. The connection is rigorously developed in Appendix A, but it should also be flagged in the main text to heuristically justify the statement in the blockquote on line 140.
2. the article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square-root lasso. Its main advantage over the popular l2^2-lasso is that the “oracle” choice of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (usually unknown) noise variance (sigma). A practical question your results could inform is whether this rationale for preferring the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso is indeed valid.
