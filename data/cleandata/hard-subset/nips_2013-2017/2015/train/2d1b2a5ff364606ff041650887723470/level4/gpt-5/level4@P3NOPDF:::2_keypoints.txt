- Summary: Provides precise asymptotic characterization of the ℓ2 (square-root) Lasso for single-index models (SIMs) while relaxing Gaussian prior assumptions on the signal; clearly written and technically careful.

- Strength: Extends sharp risk characterizations to SIMs, broadening applicability beyond linear models.

- Strength: Relaxes Gaussian prior on x0, increasing generality.

- Strength: Analysis appears rigorous with well-organized proofs and clear notation.

- Strength: Topic is timely and relevant for tuning and method selection in high-dimensional regression with unknown noise.

- Main comment 1: Emphasize the SIM-to-linear equivalence in the main text (not just Appendix A) and include a short remark/figure showing slope, effective noise variance, and induced correlation to aid intuition.

- Main comment 2: Clarify practical advantage of ℓ2-Lasso vs ℓ2^2-Lasso under SIMs—whether oracle λ remains independent of σ, what replaces σ if not, provide analytical/empirical comparisons, and give concrete tuning guidance (and comment on cross-validation performance).

- Main comment 3: State scope and assumptions upfront—precise moment/tail conditions on x0, conditions on the link function and which scalar parameters determine risk, and design assumptions (e.g., Gaussian i.i.d.) with comments on robustness to sub-Gaussian designs.

- Main comment 4: Improve presentation and intuition—add a short “takeaways” paragraph after main theorems, include a standalone remark/proposition from Appendix A in the main text, and, if feasible, add a small simulation comparing asymptotic formulas and tuning between ℓ2- and ℓ2^2-Lasso.

- Minor: Cite Brillinger (1982) when discussing SIM-to-linear equivalence, state proportional asymptotics (n,p→∞ with fixed ratio) in the introduction, and include a notation glossary to reduce overload.

- Overall assessment: Strong and timely contribution; recommend acceptance after addressing the above clarifications and additions.
