- Studies Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design and approximates non-linear observations as noisy linear ones.  
- Derives asymptotic, precise, explicit error expressions and validates them analytically and empirically.  
- Confirms results on special cases: Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization.  
- Applies theory to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit Compressed Sensing and links to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.  
- Overall quality: strong work.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but presentation is somewhat unbalanced and space limits detail.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of Plan and Vershynin with novel explicit performance-error formulas.  
- Correction: regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate parameter correspondence.  
- Editorial: Figure 1 caption formatting error (‘n=768’ should be ‘$n$=768’) and a typographical error on p.5, line 247.  
- Notation: the so-called “proximal function” is the Moreau envelope of index τ; reserve “prox” for the proximal operator and revise notation.  
- Suggestion: give a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using variational analysis results.  
- Terminology: do not call the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; call it a convex program or decoder.  
- Experiments: empirical results substantiate that asymptotic performance with non-linear measurements matches a noisy linear Gaussian model and support the theoretical predictions.
