This paper extends precise characterizations of the statistical performance of the ℓ2-Lasso (square-root Lasso) to single-index models and relaxes the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior (the distribution of x0). It is a worthwhile contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of Lasso-type estimators. The article is well written. However:

1. As pointed out by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. Although this connection is described rigorously in Appendix A, it should also be mentioned in the main text to heuristically justify the statement in the blockquote on line 140.

2. The paper focuses on the ℓ2-Lasso (square-root Lasso). The main benefit of the ℓ2-Lasso over the ℓ2^2-Lasso is that the oracle choice of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (typically unknown) noise variance σ. A practical question that the results could shed light on is whether this justification for preferring the ℓ2-Lasso over the ℓ2^2-Lasso is valid.
