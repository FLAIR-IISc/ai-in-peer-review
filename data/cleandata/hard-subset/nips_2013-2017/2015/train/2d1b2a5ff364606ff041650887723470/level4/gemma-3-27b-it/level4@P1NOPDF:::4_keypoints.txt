- Topic: Studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link using generalized lasso, motivated by Plan & Vershynin, focusing on arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0.  
- Main result: Theorem 2.1 shows the asymptotic estimation error is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions.  
- Assumption concern: Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors; this may be restrictive and needs clarification/justification.  
- Practical question: How should one choose the convex regularizer given the constraint set Kai where x₀ lies?  
- Theoretical question: If nonlinear and linear measurements are equivalent, why do the conclusions still depend on μ and σ that characterize g’s difficulty?  
- Missing related work: The single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literatures on estimation under unknown link functions are not discussed.  
- Missing related work: The recent Yi et al. paper (“Optimal linear estimation under unknown link function”) — handles μ = 0 and establishes related equivalence — is not cited.  
- Overall judgment: Well-written paper with fairly interesting results, supported by theory and empirical evidence.
