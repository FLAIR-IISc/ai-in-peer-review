### **Summary**

This paper analyzes the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and a Gaussian design matrix. It builds upon the heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear observations, a concept explored by Plan and Vershynin [17]. The authors extend the results of [17] by operating in an asymptotic regime, which allows them to derive novel, precise, and explicit expressions for the estimation error. The accuracy of these theoretical predictions is validated across several important special cases, including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. A particularly insightful contribution is the application of these results to the design of optimal quantizers in q-Bit Compressive Sensing and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

### **Quality**

This is a strong and well-executed piece of theoretical work.

### **Clarity**

The paper is generally well-written and clear. However, the presentation feels somewhat unbalanced, likely due to space constraints which prevent the inclusion of all necessary details.

### **Originality**

The work builds directly on the foundational results of Plan and Vershynin [17]. The primary novelty lies in extending this previous work in several key aspects, most notably the derivation of explicit expressions for the estimation error performance, which are new.

### **Comments**

1.  On page 4, line 193, it would be helpful to explicitly state that the regularized and penalized versions of the problem are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence of their parameters.

2.  In the caption of Figure 1, 'n=768' should be typeset in math mode as '$n=768$' for consistency.

3.  There appears to be a typo on page 5, line 247, beginning with the word "When...".

4.  On page 5, line 294, the function referred to as the "proximal function" is known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index τ. The "prox" notation is potentially confusing, as it is standardly reserved for the proximal operator, not the envelope itself. I recommend adopting the standard terminology and notation to avoid ambiguity.

5.  Regarding Assumption 1 on page 5, it would be instructive for the reader if the authors provided a more formal interpretation, perhaps by connecting it to established results from variational analysis.

6.  Throughout the text, the generalized Lasso is sometimes referred to as an "algorithm". To be precise, it is a convex optimization problem or program (or a "decoder" in Compressive Sensing terminology), not an algorithm for solving it. I suggest correcting this terminology for improved precision.

7.  The experimental section effectively validates the paper's theoretical claims by demonstrating how well the derived theorems predict the estimation error when the generalized Lasso is applied to non-linear measurements.
