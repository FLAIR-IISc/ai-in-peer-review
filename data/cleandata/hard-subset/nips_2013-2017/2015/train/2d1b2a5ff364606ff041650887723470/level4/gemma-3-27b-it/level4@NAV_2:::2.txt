The article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models. It is a worthwhile contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The article is well-written and clearly presented. The authors thoroughly motivate their work, connecting it to classical results by Brillinger and more recent advancements by Plan and Vershynin. The theoretical results are rigorously presented, and the inclusion of illustrative figures strengthens the paper. The extensive related work section demonstrates a strong understanding of the field.

However, a few points could be addressed to further enhance the manuscript. First, while the connection between single-index models and scaled linear models with non-standard noise is rigorously demonstrated in Appendix A, explicitly mentioning this equivalence in the main body of the paper—perhaps near line 140—would provide a more intuitive justification for the core argument. This would highlight the continuity with existing literature and aid reader understanding. Second, the paper focuses on the l2-lasso (also known as the square root-lasso). A key benefit of the l2-lasso over the more common l2^2-lasso is that the optimal regularization parameter is independent of the noise variance, a potentially significant advantage in practice. Exploring whether the presented results support or challenge this practical justification for preferring the l2-lasso would be a valuable addition.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The paper provides precise asymptotic performance guarantees for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, a previously unaddressed problem.
*   **Significance:** The results have the potential to impact the design and analysis of estimation algorithms in applications with non-linear measurement devices, such as compressive sensing with quantized measurements.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis is rigorous and builds upon a strong foundation of existing work.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and organized, making the complex technical details accessible to the reader.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The connection to existing literature regarding the benefits of the l2-lasso could be explored further.
*   While the paper demonstrates theoretical precision, the practical implications and computational complexity of applying the derived error formulas are not fully discussed.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. The minor suggestions mentioned above could further strengthen the manuscript. I recommend acceptance.
