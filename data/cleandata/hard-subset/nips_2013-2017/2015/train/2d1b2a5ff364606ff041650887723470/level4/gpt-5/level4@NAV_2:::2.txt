The paper broadens precise characterizations of the statistical behavior of the l2-LASSO to the single-index model setting. It also relaxes the usual Gaussian prior assumption on x0. Overall, it makes a valuable contribution to the literature on sharp asymptotics for LASSO-type estimators.

The paper is clearly written. However,
1. As noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model can be recast as a linear model with a scaled signal and nonstandard noise. While Appendix A provides a rigorous treatment of this equivalence, it would be helpful to mention it in the main text to heuristically support the claim in the block quote around line 140.
2. The work centers on the l2-LASSO (square-root LASSO). Its typical advantage over the more common l2^2-LASSO is that the “oracle” choice of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (often unknown) noise variance σ. A practical question that the results here could help clarify is whether this rationale for preferring l2-LASSO over l2^2-LASSO actually holds in this setting.

The paper advances precise characterizations of the statistical performance of the l2-LASSO to single-index models and constitutes a worthwhile addition to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of LASSO-type estimators.
