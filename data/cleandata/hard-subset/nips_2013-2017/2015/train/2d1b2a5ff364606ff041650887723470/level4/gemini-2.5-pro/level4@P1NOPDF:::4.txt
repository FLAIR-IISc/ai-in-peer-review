This paper addresses the problem of linear estimation under a nonlinear link function. Inspired by the recent work of Plan and Vershynin, the authors study the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized LASSO for arbitrary link functions where the parameter µ is non-zero. The central result, presented in Theorem 2.1, is that the asymptotic error is identical for both linear and nonlinear link functions.

A key assumption for Theorem 2.1 is that the measurement vectors are sampled from a Gaussian distribution. This is a strong assumption that may not hold in all practical scenarios. It would strengthen the paper if the authors could provide some justification for why this assumption is necessary for their analysis, or discuss the potential for extending the results to sub-Gaussian or other broader classes of distributions.

I have a few questions for the authors:
1.  The choice of the regularizer appears to depend on the set K containing the true signal x_0. While the paper discusses the requirement for a convex regularizer, it does not address the practical issue of selecting an appropriate regularizer when the set K is unknown. Could the authors elaborate on this point?
2.  The main result establishes an equivalence between the linear and nonlinear measurement models in terms of their asymptotic error characterization. However, the final error bound still depends on µ and σ, which are parameters of the nonlinear link function g and characterize the difficulty of the estimation problem. Could the authors share their thoughts on the necessity of this dependence? Does this imply that while the form of the asymptotic error is the same, the actual magnitude of the error is still fundamentally tied to the properties of the specific nonlinearity?

The paper could be further strengthened by discussing its relationship to related literature, as there appear to be some missing citations to relevant work:
1.  The literature on single-index models (SIM) and sufficient dimension reduction (SDR) also deals with estimating a linear vector or subspace under an unknown link function. A discussion of how the present work relates to, or differs from, this line of research would be beneficial.
2.  A recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear measurement," introduces an algorithm for sparse recovery that applies even in cases where µ = 0. That work also establishes an equivalence between linear and nonlinear models under certain conditions. The authors should discuss this highly relevant work and clarify the novelty of their own contributions in light of these existing results.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with interesting results.
