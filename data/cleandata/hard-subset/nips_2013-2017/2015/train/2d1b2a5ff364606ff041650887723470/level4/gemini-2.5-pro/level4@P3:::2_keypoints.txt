- Central contribution: proves an asymptotic equivalence that LASSO on non-linear single-index measurements y = g(a^T x0) matches LASSO on an effective linear model y = μ a^T x0 + σ z in the high-dimensional Gaussian-design limit.  
- μ and σ are scalar parameters determined solely by the link function g, summarizing the non-linearity's effect.  
- Builds on and extends Brillinger's single-index ideas and Plan & Vershynin's non-asymptotic bounds to give precise asymptotic MSE formulas.  
- Analysis uses a CGMT-based framework and yields first asymptotically exact error predictions for LASSO in this setting.  
- The theory is general, covering sparse, group-sparse, low-rank structures and non-Gaussian signal priors.  
- Numerical simulations validate the theoretical predictions convincingly.  
- Practical application: reduces optimal q-bit quantizer design to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, linking to the Lloyd–Max algorithm.  
- Strength: fundamental and insightful result simplifying model-mismatch understanding to two scalars (μ, σ).  
- Strength: significant technical novelty by moving from upper bounds to exact asymptotic characterizations.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clearly structured, and theorems are precisely stated.  
- Strength: results have clear practical relevance and actionable design principles.  
- Weakness: paper lacks upfront intuition connecting the result to known heuristics (e.g., Brillinger 1982), which should be mentioned earlier.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on the choice and tuning of square-root (l2) LASSO versus standard (l2^2) LASSO and whether λ remains noise-blind given effective μ and σ.  
- Overall: strong, publishable work with minor exposition and practical-tuning discussion gaps.
