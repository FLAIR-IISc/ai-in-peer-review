The article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models. It is a worthwhile contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The article is well-written and clearly presented. However, a couple of points could further strengthen the discussion. First, as noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously detailed in Appendix A, briefly mentioning it in the main text would provide a helpful heuristic justification for the statement around line 140.

Second, the article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square-root-lasso. A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more common l2^2-lasso is that the optimal regularization parameter does not depend on the (typically unknown) noise variance, σ^2. It would be interesting to consider whether the results of this work support or challenge this justification for preferring the l2-lasso in practice. Exploring this aspect could further highlight the practical implications of the theoretical findings.

The paper’s strengths are its technical soundness, the novelty of extending precise analysis to non-linear measurements, and the clear presentation of results. The derivation of explicit expressions for the estimation error is a significant achievement. The theoretical results are supported by figures demonstrating good agreement between predicted and observed performance.

Potential weaknesses include the reliance on a Gaussian assumption for the entries of the measurement matrix A. While common in the literature, it would be valuable to briefly discuss the potential sensitivity of the results to deviations from this assumption. Additionally, the discussion of applications, particularly in Section 2.3, could be expanded to illustrate the potential impact of the findings in more detail.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically significant extension of existing results on the l2-lasso to the more general setting of non-linear measurements.
*   The derived expressions for the estimation error are precise and asymptotically exact, providing a deeper understanding of the performance of the Generalized LASSO.
*   The work is well-written, clearly organized, and supported by illustrative figures.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The reliance on Gaussian assumptions may limit the direct applicability of the results in certain scenarios.
*   The discussion of practical implications, particularly in Section 2.3, could be more detailed.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend acceptance of this paper. Despite minor potential weaknesses, the theoretical contributions are substantial and the work is of high quality. The paper addresses a challenging problem with rigorous analysis and provides valuable insights into the behavior of the Generalized LASSO in the presence of non-linear measurements. It represents a significant advance in the field and is likely to be of interest to researchers working on sparse recovery, compressed sensing, and related areas.
