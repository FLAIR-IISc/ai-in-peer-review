This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. A known heuristic often used is that non-linear observations may be treated as noisy linear observations. Capitalizing on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors here extend the results of [17], and by moving to an asymptotic regime, they provide novel precise explicit expressions of the error estimation. The accuracy of their predictions is confirmed with many special cases (including the Lasso, the group Lasso and the nuclear norm). The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is very insightful.

Quality: This is a nice piece of work. The paper provides a significant contribution to the field by establishing a precise and explicit expression for the estimation error of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements. The authors' use of an asymptotic regime allows for a more detailed analysis of the problem, and their results have important implications for the design of optimal quantizers in compressive sensing.

Clarity: The paper is quite well-written, though the treatment is somewhat unbalanced. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the relevant background material. However, the lack of space prevents including all the necessary details, and some sections feel a bit rushed. Additionally, the notation used in the paper is not always consistent, and some definitions could be clarified.

Originality: The paper builds upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] and expands in several aspects that are novel. For instance, the explicit expressions of the performance estimation error are new. The authors' use of an asymptotic regime and their application to the design of optimal quantizers in compressive sensing are also original contributions.

Comments:

1. Page 4, line 193: the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent with appropriate correspondence. This point could be clarified further, as it is not entirely clear how the two versions are related.

2. Caption of Figure 1: 'n=768' should be '$n$=768'. This is a minor typo, but it should be corrected for consistency.

3. Page 5, line 247: a typo at 'When ...'. This typo should be corrected to ensure that the text is clear and easy to read.

4. Page 5, line 294: what the authors call the proximal function is (very well) known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index $\tau$. The notation used there is awkward as prox is generally used for the proximal point/operator but not for the Moreau envelope. I suggest changing it to avoid confusion.

5. Page 5, Assumption 1: I think it would instructive to give this assumption a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis. This would help to clarify the underlying mathematical structure of the problem.

6. The authors should avoid calling the generalized Lasso an algorithm in the text. It is certainly a (convex) program (or decoder to borrow the terminology widely used in CS), but not an algorithm. This is a minor point, but it is important to use precise language when describing mathematical objects.

7. Experiments: the authors show the results when applying the generalized Lasso to the non-linear measurements, and how the theorems predict the estimation error. However, it would be useful to include more experimental results to demonstrate the robustness of the method. Additionally, the authors could provide more details about the experimental setup and the parameters used in the simulations.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. With some minor revisions to address the comments above, the paper has the potential to be a valuable resource for researchers working on compressive sensing and related areas.
