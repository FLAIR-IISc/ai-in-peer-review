- Extends Generalized LASSO to non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) with possibly unknown link g.
- Shows asymptotic performance equals that of a linear model yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].
- Builds on Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, moving from consistency/upper bounds to precise asymptotic error characterizations.
- Provides precise asymptotic error formulas for regularized Generalized LASSO across structured signal models (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).
- Uses the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem framework with rigorous, well-justified assumptions and proofs.
- Correctly addresses limitations of prior work by replacing loose bounds with exact asymptotic expressions.
- Experimental results (Figures 1–2) show excellent agreement between theory and empirical performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear logical flow and accessible technical presentation.
- Minor typographical errors appear in proofs and should be corrected in the final version.
- Represents a novel and significant advance by establishing precise equivalence between non-linear and scaled linear measurement models.
- Demonstrates practical significance for quantized sensing, showing Lloyd–Max quantizer optimality for minimizing estimation error.
- Offers practical insights for choosing regularization parameters and understanding fundamental estimation limits under non-linear measurements.
