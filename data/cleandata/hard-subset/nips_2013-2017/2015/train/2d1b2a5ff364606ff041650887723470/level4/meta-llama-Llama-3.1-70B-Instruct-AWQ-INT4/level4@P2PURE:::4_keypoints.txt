- Paper studies linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, extending Plan & Vershynin, and shows via Theorem 2.1 that generalized Lasso’s asymptotic error matches the linear case even for arbitrary link functions (μ ≠ 0).
- Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors, which may be overly restrictive; the authors should justify this assumption and its necessity for the result.
- The paper does not address how to choose the regularizer based on the constraint set K containing x0, beyond assuming a convex regularizer.
- The dependence of the error on μ and σ (which quantify the difficulty of estimation from g) is noted, but the paper gives no insight into whether these dependencies are necessary.
- Important related literatures are omitted, notably work on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction for linear/subspace estimation under unknown link functions.
- A recent paper by Yi et al. (on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions) is not cited; it gives an algorithm for sparse recovery even when μ=0 and proves equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation under conditions, with theoretical and empirical support.
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with interesting results, but needs justification of assumptions and more thorough connection to related work.
