- Studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link, extending Plan & Vershynin to analyze asymptotic error of generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0.  
- Main result (Theorem 2.1): asymptotic estimation error is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions.  
- Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors, which may be restrictive; authors should justify this assumption and its necessity.  
- Unclear how to choose the regularization parameter based on the set Kai containing the true parameter x₀; parameter selection conditional on Kai is not addressed.  
- Although linear/nonlinear equivalence is shown, the result still depends on μ and σ; authors should discuss whether these dependencies are necessary and what they reveal about the link function.  
- Paper omits discussion of related work in single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction that study estimation under unknown link functions.  
- Recent related work (Yi et al.) on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions—covering sparse recovery even when μ = 0 and showing equivalence—should be cited and discussed.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with interesting and potentially significant results.
