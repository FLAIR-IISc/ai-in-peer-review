- Main contribution: Provides an exact high-dimensional asymptotic characterization of Generalized LASSO under single-index (non-linear) measurements, showing equivalence to a linear model with effective parameters μ and σ^2 determined by g.  
- Technical novelty: Strengthens prior work by moving from non-asymptotic upper bounds to asymptotically exact error formulae for the regularized LASSO.  
- Theoretical foundation: Central result (Theorem 2.2) gives a general error formula via a scalar optimization problem and specializes to common regularizers.  
- Relation to prior work: Generalizes Brillinger’s fixed-dimension equivalence and refines Plan & Vershynin’s high-dimensional bounds.  
- Applicability: Framework covers sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery, with extensions to non-Gaussian priors.  
- Empirical validation: Numerical experiments closely match theoretical predictions, supporting the analysis.  
- Practical demonstration: Application to optimal quantizer design shows practical utility of the theory.  
- Writing and clarity: Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and situates contributions in existing literature.  
- Suggestion — intuition: Recommend adding a brief heuristic explanation (e.g., Gaussian projection of a_i^T x_0 and using first two moments of g) to motivate the core equivalence.  
- Suggestion — l2 vs l2^2 LASSO: Ask for discussion on whether square-root LASSO’s robustness to unknown noise variance extends to robustness against unknown non-linearity g.  
- Suggestion — low-rank section: Low-rank recovery treatment is underdeveloped; request more details on singular value distribution convergence or clarification of scope.  
- Overall assessment: Excellent theoretical contribution, minor clarifications would strengthen practical insights; recommend acceptance.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: First asymptotically precise error characterization, insightful non-linear=linear equivalence, general framework, rigorous analysis with convincing experiments.  
- Con-acceptance concern: Mainly that discussion and intuition could be expanded to improve accessibility and practical interpretation.
