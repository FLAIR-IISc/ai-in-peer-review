

Summary: This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design matrices. Building on Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend their framework to an asymptotic regime, deriving novel explicit expressions for estimation error. These predictions are rigorously validated across multiple special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm minimization), and the work offers significant insights into designing optimal quantizers for q-bit compressed sensing, linking them to the Lloyd-Max quantizer.  

Quality: This is a well-executed contribution with strong theoretical and practical implications.  

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written but suffers from an unbalanced treatment of technical content. Key details on special cases and the quantizer application are underdeveloped due to space constraints, though the core arguments remain accessible.  

Originality: The work extends [17] by providing explicit asymptotic error expressions—a novel contribution. While rooted in prior theory, the extension to non-linear measurements and the explicit error characterization represent meaningful advances.  

Comments:  
1. Page 4, line 193: Clarify that the regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under a specific parameter correspondence.  
2. Figure 1 caption: Correct "n=768" to "$n$=768".  
3. Page 5, line 247: Fix typo at "When ...".  
4. Page 5, line 294: The "proximal function" referenced is standardly known as the Moreau envelope of index $\tau$ in convex analysis. The notation is misleading, as "prox" typically denotes the proximal operator, not the envelope. Replace with "Moreau envelope" for precision.  
5. Page 5, Assumption 1: Provide a formal interpretation using variational analysis to clarify its geometric or analytical significance.  
6. Avoid referring to the "Generalized Lasso" as an "algorithm." It is a convex optimization program (or decoder, per CS terminology), not an algorithmic method.  
7. The experimental section redundantly restates the summary. Streamline by removing the repeated description of the main result (as covered in the summary) and focus instead on:  
   - How the theorems predict estimation error in non-linear settings.  
   - The validation of predictions across special cases (e.g., Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm).  
   - The connection between the quantizer design and Lloyd-Max theory.  

The paper’s core insight—that non-linear measurements asymptotically behave like noisy linear observations—is compelling and well-supported. The explicit error formulas and quantizer application are particularly valuable. Minor revisions to notation (Comment 4) and phrasing (Comment 6) will enhance precision. The space constraints limiting depth on special cases are understandable but should be acknowledged. Overall, this work makes a significant contribution to the theory of structured estimation under non-linear models.
