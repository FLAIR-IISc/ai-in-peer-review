- Addresses how well the Generalized LASSO estimates structured signals when measurements are nonlinear functions of the signal.  
- Main insight: for Gaussian measurement matrices, nonlinear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) are asymptotically equivalent to linearized measurements yi = μ(ai^T x0)+σzi with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].  
- Equivalence holds for all regularization parameters λ and for a wide class of regularizers and link functions.  
- Theorem 2.1 establishes the nonlinear–linear equivalence; Theorem 2.2 gives an explicit asymptotic error formula.  
- Builds on and extends Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin by providing precise asymptotic characterizations rather than loose bounds.  
- Results leverage recent precise error analyses for the Generalized LASSO with linear measurements.  
- Special-case analyses cover sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank matrix recovery.  
- Practical application: q-bit compressive sensing example shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal under this model.  
- Strengths: highly original, elegant, broadly applicable theory with clear exposition and convincing figures.  
- Technical quality: rigorous proofs, careful assumptions, and precise asymptotic results valid for all λ.  
- Weaknesses: assumptions on the link function (e.g., μ ≠ 0, why g cannot be even) need more discussion.  
- Limitations: analysis restricted to Gaussian measurement ensembles and experiments are limited (only two figures); some minor typos/formatting issues in proofs.  
- Overall judgment: strong theoretical contribution with practical implications; recommendation for acceptance.
