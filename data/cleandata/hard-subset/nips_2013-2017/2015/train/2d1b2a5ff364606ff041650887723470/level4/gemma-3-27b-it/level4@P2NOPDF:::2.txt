This paper extends the theoretical understanding of the l2-lasso to the more general setting of single-index models, and importantly, relaxes the common assumption of Gaussian priors on the input features. This represents a valuable contribution to the growing body of work focused on characterizing the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators. The manuscript is generally well-written and clearly presented.

However, I have a couple of suggestions for improvement.

First, the connection between single-index models and scaled linear models with non-standard noise – a relationship rigorously detailed in Appendix A and originally highlighted by Brillinger (1982) – deserves explicit mention within the main text. Specifically, acknowledging this equivalence would provide a more intuitive justification for the claim made in the blockquote on line 140. Briefly outlining this connection would aid readers in understanding the underlying rationale for the analysis.

Second, the paper concentrates on the l2-lasso (also known as the square root-lasso). A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more commonly used l2<sup>2</sup>-lasso is its regularization parameter’s independence from the often-unknown noise variance (σ). A compelling practical question that the theoretical results presented could potentially address is whether this theoretical justification for preferring the l2-lasso remains valid in practice. Investigating or discussing the implications of these findings for practical model selection would strengthen the paper's impact. 

In summary, this work offers a significant theoretical advancement in understanding l2-lasso performance within the single-index model framework. Addressing the above points would further enhance the clarity and practical relevance of the contribution.
