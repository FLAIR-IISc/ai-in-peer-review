- Studies structured signal recovery from non-linear (single-index) measurements and analyzes the Generalized (`l2`)-LASSO that treats measurements as linear.  
- Provides an asymptotically precise characterization of the LASSO's mean-squared error in high-dimensional proportional-growth regimes.  
- Shows LASSO on non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on a scaled linear model with additive Gaussian noise; scaling and noise depend only on the non-linear link.  
- Analysis covers a wide class of convex regularizers (e.g., `l1`, group, nuclear norms) and relaxes Gaussian signal prior assumptions.  
- Extends Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem based analysis to this problem domain with solid technical derivations.  
- Numerical simulations confirm the derived asymptotic formulae are remarkably accurate.  
- Applies results to optimal quantizer design, showing Lloyd–Max quantizer minimizes the LASSO estimation error.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly states main results, and explores important special cases (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).  
- Weakness: the single-index-to-linear-model insight is classical (Brillinger 1982); the introduction should frame the work as a precise modern characterization rather than a novel discovery.  
- Weakness: should discuss why `l2`-LASSO (square-root LASSO) is particularly suitable here, given effective noise variance depends on the unknown link function, and relate this to `l2^2`-LASSO choices.
