This paper extends precise performance characterizations of the l2-lasso (square-root lasso) to single-index models and relaxes the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of x0. It is a valuable contribution to the literature on the exact asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators. The manuscript is clearly written. I have two main suggestions:

1) As noted by Brillinger (1982), a single-index model can be reformulated as a linear model with a scaled, unknown signal and nonstandard noise. Although Appendix A rigorously establishes this equivalence, a brief discussion in the main text would be helpful. In particular, highlighting this connection earlier would heuristically justify the statement in the block quote around line 140.

2) The paper focuses on the l2-lasso (square-root lasso). Its commonly cited advantage over the l2^2-lasso (the standard least-squares loss) is that the oracle value of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (typically unknown) noise variance sigma. It would be useful if the authors could clarify whether their results support this rationale in the single-index setting they analyze and under the relaxed prior assumptions. In other words, do the asymptotics indicate that optimal tuning for the l2-lasso remains variance-free here, or is there residual dependence on sigma? Any theoretical or empirical guidance on this point would strengthen the practical implications.

Overall, the paper makes a worthwhile contribution by extending precise performance characterizations to a broader model class and weaker prior assumptions. Addressing the points above would further improve the exposition and clarify the practical message.
