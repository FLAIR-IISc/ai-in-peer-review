- Studies estimation of structured signals from nonlinear measurements y_i = g(a_i^T x_0) using the Generalized LASSO.  
- Main insight: for i.i.d. Gaussian A, estimation error with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that with scaled noisy linear measurements y_lin = μA x_0 + σz.  
- Provides explicit formulas μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] (γ ~ N(0,1)) linking nonlinear to effective linear model.  
- Theorem 2.1 establishes the asymptotic equivalence; Theorem 2.2 gives precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error.  
- Results hold for all regularization parameters λ and a wide class of regularizers (ℓ1, group ℓ1, nuclear norm), improving on prior qualitative bounds.  
- Extends and significantly sharpens earlier work by Plan & Vershynin and classical results by Brillinger.  
- Practical implication: shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for minimizing estimation error in quantized compressed sensing.  
- Paper is technically rigorous, well-structured, and supported by numerical experiments (Figures 1–2).  
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with the single-index model literature (e.g., Ichimura 1993) and discussion of how it relates/extends those results.  
- Weakness: does not discuss closely related recent work by Yi et al. (2015), particularly cases with μ = 0 that this paper excludes.  
- Weakness: limited discussion/justification of the critical i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix assumption and possible extensions beyond it.  
- Overall assessment: substantial and novel theoretical contribution with clear practical implications; recommended for acceptance.
