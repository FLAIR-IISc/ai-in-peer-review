- Paper studies asymptotic estimation error of generalized LASSO for arbitrary nonlinear link functions with non-zero mean; Theorem 2.1 claims error matches that of linear measurements.  
- Assumes Gaussian linear measurement vectors; reviewer questions necessity and asks for justification since this may be a strict assumption.  
- Reviewer asks how to choose a regularizer given the constraint set K; selection/design of an appropriate regularizer is not addressed.  
- Result reduces nonlinear measurements to equivalent linear ones but depends on parameters μ and σ; reviewer questions whether these dependencies are necessary.  
- Missing literature: no review of single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which study related problems with unknown link functions.  
- Missing recent reference: Yi et al. (paper on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions) that proposes an algorithm for sparse recovery even when μ = 0.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with interesting, significant results and potential impact in signal processing and machine learning.  
- Strengths: substantial contribution, clear presentation, accessible to a broad audience, and supported by theoretical and empirical results.  
- Weaknesses: Gaussian measurement assumption may be too strict, regularizer selection not discussed, μ/σ dependence may be unnecessary, and some relevant references are omitted.
