

The paper makes a valuable contribution by extending precise asymptotic analysis of the square root Lasso (l2-lasso) to single-index models, relaxing the standard Gaussian prior assumption on the signal vector $x_0$. This work significantly advances the literature on the statistical behavior of lasso-type estimators in non-Gaussian settings.

The manuscript is well-written, but two points merit clarification. First, as noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While Appendix A rigorously establishes this equivalence, it should also be briefly mentioned in the main text to heuristically support the claim in the quoted statement on line 140. This would strengthen the motivation for the model extension.

Second, the paper focuses on the square root Lasso, whose primary advantage over the standard lasso (l2²-lasso) is that its oracle tuning parameter avoids dependence on the unknown noise variance $\sigma$. The results should explicitly address whether this key justification for preferring the square root Lasso remains valid in the single-index context. This practical question is central to the paper’s contribution and deserves direct discussion.
