- Paper analyzes generalized LASSO under nonlinear link functions and proves (Theorem 2.1) that asymptotic estimation error with µ ≠ 0 matches that of linear measurements.  
- Core contribution: precise asymptotic error characterization that encapsulates the effect of the link function via parameters µ and σ.  
- Builds on Plan & Vershynin and appropriately notes connection to Brillinger; theoretical results appear rigorous and well-grounded in literature.  
- Generalizes prior results to a broad class of link functions and regularizers and provides explicit error formulas.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated through application to q-bit compressive sensing and discussion of the Lloyd–Max algorithm.  
- Strength: Novel and surprising equivalence result showing generalized LASSO robustness to nonlinear measurements.  
- Strength: High precision in asymptotic error expressions, improving on existing bounds.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized exposition with consistent notation.  
- Weakness: Strong Gaussian assumption for measurement matrix A may be restrictive; needs discussion on relaxing this assumption and real-world implications.  
- Weakness: Paper mentions regularizer role but lacks practical guidance on selecting the optimal regularizer for x0’s structure.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of how dependence on µ and σ should inform measurement system design.  
- Weakness: Missing related work coverage, notably single-index models, sufficient dimension reduction, and Yi et al. (“Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear transform”).  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, significant theoretical result with sound analysis, clear presentation, and practical relevance to the NIPS community.  
- Argument against acceptance: Limited generality due to Gaussian assumption, insufficient regularizer guidance, and omitted related works weaken positioning.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper recommended for acceptance conditional on addressing missing citations (Yi et al., single-index literature), discussing Gaussian-assumption limitations, and adding a brief discussion on regularizer selection.
