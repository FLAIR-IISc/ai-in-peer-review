- Paper studies high-dimensional estimation from nonlinear measurements yi = g(a_i^T x0) and analyzes generalized LASSO with convex regularizer f, showing asymptotic equivalence to a linear model y_lin = μ A x0 + σ z with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].

- Theorem 2.1: asymptotic equality of estimation error between the nonlinear measurement model and the equivalent linear surrogate.

- Theorem 2.2: exact asymptotic error formula given by a three-scalar convex optimization depending on δ=m/n, λ, μ, σ, and a Moreau-envelope limit F that encodes structure and regularizer.

- Specializations: results worked out for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery; applied to quantized compressive sensing where Lloyd–Max quantizer is shown optimal for LASSO error.

- Strength — conceptual clarity: clean nonlinear-to-linear reduction with explicit μ and σ parameters.

- Strength — generality: covers a wide class of convex regularizers (ℓ1, group ℓ1,2, nuclear norm) and many link functions g with precise dependence on problem parameters.

- Strength — precision: provides an exact scalar convex program for limiting error and insightful special-case formulas that match simulations.

- Strength — practical impact: quantizer-design implication (Lloyd–Max optimality) is interesting and relevant.

- Weakness — Gaussian A assumption: analysis requires A with i.i.d. N(0,1) entries; need discussion on universality to sub-Gaussian, isotropic, or rotational ensembles.

- Weakness — assumptions on μ and σ: requires μ ≠ 0 and bounded σ^2, excluding some links (e.g., even g) and μ=0 cases; needs discussion on necessity and estimation of μ,σ when g is unknown.

- Weakness — choice of regularizer and λ: paper lacks practical guidance on selecting f for a model class K, mapping constrained to penalized forms, and tuning λ despite Theorem 2.2 giving exact risk vs λ.

- Weakness — Assumption 1 and F: Moreau-envelope limit F is central but needs more intuition and recipes for computing F in settings like nuclear norm with general spectral distributions.

- Weakness — clarity and related work: presentation is dense; needs a high-level proof roadmap and broader literature review on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction (SDR) including Yi et al. (2015) for μ=0 cases.

- Quality: technically strong and precise results with simulations supporting theory; clarifying Gaussianity necessity and Assumption 1 would further strengthen the work.

- Clarity: generally clear but dense; would benefit from proofreading, consistent notation, a brief proof roadmap, and more examples for computing F in low-rank cases.

- Originality: appears to be the first asymptotically exact error analysis for generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements for general convex regularizers; Lloyd–Max quantizer consequence is novel.

- Significance: high — unifies single-index observations with modern high-dimensional analysis and has practical consequences for quantized sensing, one-bit CS, and censored models.

- Specific suggestions: discuss Gaussianity vs universality, provide guidance on choosing f and tuning λ (use derived equations/λcrit), elaborate on estimating μ and σ and post-scaling, expand SDR-related citations, and present experiments with non-Gaussian A and more structured cases.

- Pros summary: precise nonlinear→linear equivalence, exact asymptotic error characterization across multiple structures, practical quantizer implication, strong alignment between theory and experiments.

- Cons summary: strong Gaussian measurement assumption, limited practical guidance on f/λ, sparse coverage of SDR/μ=0 related work, and a dense treatment of Assumption 1/F requiring more exposition.
