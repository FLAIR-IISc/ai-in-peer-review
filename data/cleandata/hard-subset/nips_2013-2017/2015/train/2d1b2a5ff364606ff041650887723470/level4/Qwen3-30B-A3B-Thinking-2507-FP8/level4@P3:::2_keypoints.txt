- Studies Generalized LASSO performance with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0), extending Brillinger (1982) and Plan & Vershynin (2015).  
- Proves asymptotic equivalence to a linear model yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1).  
- Equivalence and error formulas hold for a wide class of link functions g and regularizers f.  
- Derives precise asymptotic estimation error expressions that capture signal structure, regularizer, and measurement parameters.  
- Uses the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework to obtain exact error characterizations.  
- Applies the framework to common structured signals: sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.  
- Novel application to q-bit compressive sensing showing the Lloyd–Max quantizer minimizes estimation error.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, technically sound, and validated with experiments.  
- Main limitations: assumes unit-norm signal and requires technical conditions on g.  
- Minor weakness: the key connection between non-linear and equivalent linear measurements is relegated to an appendix and should be more clearly explained in the main text.  
- Significance: provides deeper, precise insights beyond prior upper bounds and has practical relevance for non-linear measurement systems.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with practical and theoretical impact — recommend acceptance.
