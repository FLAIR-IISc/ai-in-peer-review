This article builds upon existing research by providing a precise characterization of the statistical performance of the l2-lasso in the context of single-index models, while also relaxing the traditional Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of x_0. This work constitutes a valuable addition to the literature on the asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The article is well-written, but there are a couple of suggestions for improvement:
1. The equivalence between the single-index model and a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise, as noted by Brillinger (1982), should be mentioned in the main article, in addition to its rigorous treatment in Appendix A, to provide heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140.
2. The article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more popular l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter is independent of the (typically unknown) noise variance (sigma). The results presented in the article could be used to investigate whether this justification for preferring the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso holds in practice.
