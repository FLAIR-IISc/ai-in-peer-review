Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies LASSO-type estimation under non-linear single-index measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0), where the link function g may be unknown and random and the measurement matrix A has i.i.d. N(0,1) entries. The main result shows an exact asymptotic equivalence: the generalized LASSO with such non-linear measurements has the same asymptotic estimation error as the same LASSO applied to linear measurements y = μ A x0 + σ z, with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2] for γ ∼ N(0,1). Building on this equivalence, the authors derive a precise, scalar convex optimization (in three variables) whose unique solution α* yields the asymptotic squared error ‖x̂ − μ x0‖^2 → α*^2. The framework accommodates general convex regularizers (e.g., ℓ1, group ℓ1,2, nuclear norm) and provides specializations for sparse, block-sparse, and low-rank recovery. An application to q-bit quantized compressed sensing uses the equivalence to reduce the design of quantizers to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, showing that Lloyd–Max quantizers are stationary points of the relevant objective.

This work connects several lines of research. It extends the classical Brillinger result (for unregularized LS with fixed n and m → ∞) by providing high-dimensional, precise characterizations and by covering regularization. It substantially sharpens the recent non-asymptotic bounds of Plan and Vershynin for constrained LASSO under non-linear measurements by giving asymptotically exact error formulas and by handling the regularized LASSO. It also complements precise LASSO analyses under linear measurements (AMP-based and CGMT-based results by Bayati–Montanari, Stojnic, and Thrampoulidis et al.) by showing that the same precise characterizations transfer to the non-linear regime via μ and σ.

Quality:
- Technical soundness: Theorems 2.1–2.2 provide a clear equivalence and a precise error characterization under Gaussian designs and linear asymptotics. The assumptions (bounded subdifferentials, Assumption 1 on the Moreau envelope limit) are standard for CGMT-style analyses and are verified for ℓ1 and group ℓ1,2; the low-rank case is outlined with a reasonable path to verification. The specializations (e.g., Theorem 2.3) are clean and align with prior linear-measurement results where available.
- Empirical validation: Simulations in the figures corroborate the theory across several regimes and regularizers.
- Rigor and completeness: Proofs are deferred to the appendix; the main text states assumptions clearly and provides interpretable formulations.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well-written and well-organized. A few improvements could further aid readability:
- Move the heuristic explanation of the “non-linear equals linear” equivalence to the main text early on (not only in Appendix A), explicitly connecting to Brillinger’s argument. This would justify the central claim in the introduction more transparently.
- Clarify the role and interpretation of μ and σ^2 when g is unknown, including guidance on estimating them from data, and how that affects the practical use of the precise error formulas.
- Provide more intuition and actionable guidance on tuning λ, especially the λcrit phenomenon for δ < 1 in Theorem 2.3 (e.g., defaults or data-driven rules).
- Minor notational issue: in the abstract, σ is defined without squaring; later σ^2 is correctly defined. Please make these consistent. Also correct minor typos (e.g., “mesaurements,” “desribed,” “uknown,” “eventhough,” “redifining,” “nuclearnorm”).

Originality:
The asymptotically exact equivalence between non-linear and appropriately scaled noisy linear measurements for the generalized LASSO, combined with precise error formulas that hold for a wide class of convex regularizers, appears to be new. The quantizer design consequence (Lloyd–Max emerges as stationary for minimizing σ^2/μ^2) is also insightful.

Significance:
The results unify and extend precise LASSO error analyses to a broad non-linear measurement class, which is common in practice (quantization, censoring, sign measurements). The scalar optimization formula offers a practical tool to predict performance and to guide the design of link functions/quantizers. This should be useful to both theorists and practitioners.

Specific comments and suggestions:
- Heuristic equivalence in main text: Explicitly sketch the Brillinger-style decomposition yi = μ a_i^T x0 + w_i with w_i uncorrelated with a_i^T x0 and Var(w_i) ≈ σ^2. This will motivate Theorem 2.1 and the choice of error metric ‖x̂ − μ x0‖^2.
- Square-root LASSO vs ℓ2^2-LASSO: A key practical selling point of the square-root LASSO is its reduced dependence on the noise variance. Given the equivalence to a linear model with effective variance σ^2 depending on g, please discuss whether the oracle λ for the square-root LASSO remains free of σ, and how this compares, in practice and asymptotically, to the ℓ2^2-LASSO under non-linear links. A short empirical comparison would be valuable.
- Assumption 1: For the low-rank case, consider including a concrete example (e.g., random orthogonal singular vectors with a specified singular value distribution) and a brief derivation or numerical recipe to compute F(c1,c2,c3).
- Robustness: Comment on how sensitive the equivalence and error predictions are to deviations from Gaussian A (e.g., sub-Gaussian or mildly correlated designs). If some elements extend, a short remark or experiment would increase impact.
- μ = 0 regime: Since even link functions (e.g., pure sign without dithering) yield μ = 0, explicitly discuss limitations in this regime and whether simple modifications (e.g., dithering or known scaling) recover identifiability and bring the results back into scope.
- Practical estimation of μ, σ^2: When g is unknown but i.i.d. across measurements, can μ and σ^2 be estimated consistently from (A, y) (e.g., via method-of-moments), and would plugging them into the formulas retain accuracy? A brief note would help practitioners.
- Code/data: Consider releasing code for evaluating the scalar convex program in (10) and for reproducing the figures.

Pros:
- First asymptotically exact error characterization for generalized LASSO under broad non-linear single-index measurements.
- Clear, interpretable equivalence to a linear model with effective scaling and noise; unifies prior results.
- Covers multiple important regularizers and structures; includes a useful application to quantizer design.
- Strong technical execution and convincing empirical validation.

Cons:
- Dependence on Gaussian design; robustness to non-Gaussian designs is not addressed empirically.
- Some assumptions (Assumption 1) are technical and only partially instantiated (low-rank case left for future work).
- Practical guidance on λ selection and on estimating μ, σ^2 when g is unknown could be expanded.
- Minor notation/typos and a missed opportunity to state the core equivalence earlier in the main text.

Overall recommendation:
A strong, well-executed contribution that advances precise understanding of LASSO under realistic non-linear measurement models. I recommend acceptance after minor revisions addressing clarity, the early heuristic exposition of the equivalence, and a brief discussion of λ selection and μ/σ estimation.
