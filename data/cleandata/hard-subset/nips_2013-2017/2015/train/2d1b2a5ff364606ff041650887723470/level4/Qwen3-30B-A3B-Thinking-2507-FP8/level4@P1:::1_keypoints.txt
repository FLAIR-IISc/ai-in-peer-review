- Studies Generalized LASSO performance with non-linear measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0).  
- Proves asymptotic equivalence to a linear model y_i = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i with μ = E[γ g(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].  
- Provides the first precise asymptotic error expressions for Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements.  
- Shows estimation error depends on the link function only through the scalars μ and σ.  
- Derives application to optimal quantizer design, proving the Lloyd–Max quantizer minimizes LASSO estimation error.  
- Technical approach is strong, leveraging the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem with careful mathematical analysis.  
- Main theorems (2.1, 2.2) are proved under comprehensive, well-justified high-dimensional assumptions.  
- Extends precise error formulas to structured recovery problems: sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank.  
- Paper is generally clear with a well-motivated introduction and rigorously presented main results.  
- Proof sections contain typos and minor notational inconsistencies that require proofreading.  
- Original contribution is significant, improving on prior consistency bounds (e.g., Plan & Vershynin) by giving exact errors.  
- Results are practically significant for applications like 1-bit quantization, censored data, and nonlinear sensors.  
- Main limitation: analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, which may limit practical applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept; minor typos do not undermine the paper’s quality or contributions.
