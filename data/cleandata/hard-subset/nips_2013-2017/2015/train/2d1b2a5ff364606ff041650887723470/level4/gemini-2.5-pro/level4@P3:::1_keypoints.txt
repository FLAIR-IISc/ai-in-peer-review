- Main result: Proves asymptotic equivalence between LASSO on non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) and LASSO on linear measurements yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi in the m,n→∞ limit.
- μ and σ defined explicitly as μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1).
- Provides an exact asymptotic characterization of the estimation error ‖x̂ - μx0‖^2 via a small deterministic convex optimization.
- Specializes the general result to sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and low-rank (nuclear norm) recovery settings.
- Practical application: analyzes q-bit quantized compressive sensing and shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer minimizes the LASSO estimation error (minimizes σ^2/μ^2).
- Significance/novelty: Offers a clean, powerful explanation for why applying LASSO to non-linear observations works and is likely influential.
- Technical quality: Delivers precise, asymptotically exact error formulas capturing δ, signal structure (F), link function (μ,σ), and λ; validated numerically.
- Clarity and organization: Well-written paper with clear motivation, context, and logical progression from theorem to applications.
- Practical relevance: Theory yields concrete design guidance (e.g., optimal quantizer) with real-world implications.
- Weakness: Paper could better situate and discuss connections to the extensive literature on single-index models in statistics/econometrics.
- Weakness: Supplementary proofs contain typos and minor grammatical errors that need proofreading.
- Acceptance argument: Strong pro points (fundamental theory, exact formulas, clear writing, practical application) outweigh the minor cons (positioning and appendix proofreading).
