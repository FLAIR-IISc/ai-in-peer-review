- Problem: Analyze asymptotic performance of Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements y_i = g(a_i^T x_0).  
- Main theoretical result: Under Gaussian measurement matrices, estimation error for non-linear measurements equals that for linear measurements y_i = μa_i^T x_0 + σz_i asymptotically.  
- Definitions: μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1).  
- Novelty: First precise asymptotic characterization (not just bounds) of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, extending Plan & Vershynin (2013) and Brillinger’s classical results.  
- Technical method: Rigorous analysis using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework.  
- Results: Explicit error formulas showing roles of g, signal structure, regularizer f, and λ.  
- Empirical validation: Extensive simulations (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) showing excellent agreement with theory.  
- Application: Compelling q-bit compressive sensing example; optimal quantization thresholds match Lloyd–Max quantizer.  
- Presentation: Clear, well-organized, precise mathematical exposition; assumptions and special cases well explained.  
- Significance: Provides strong theoretical foundation for non-linear measurement systems with practical design implications.  
- Weakness — Gaussian assumption: Needs clearer discussion of necessity and limitations of Gaussian measurement matrices.  
- Weakness — μ = 0 case: Paper does not adequately handle μ = 0 (e.g., g(x)=sign(x)); should acknowledge and compare to Yi et al. (NIPS 2015).  
- Weakness — literature coverage: More discussion needed of related single-index model literature.  
- Recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution with practical implications; reviewer recommends acceptance.
