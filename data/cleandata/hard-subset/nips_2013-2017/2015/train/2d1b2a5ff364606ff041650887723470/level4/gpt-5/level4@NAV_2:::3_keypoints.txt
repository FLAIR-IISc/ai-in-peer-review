- Summary: Analyzes generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design and derives asymptotic, explicit expressions for estimation error, validating predictions across multiple settings.  
- Quality: Solid and technically sound contribution.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and well-organized, though some sections feel uneven in depth due to space limits.  
- Originality: Advances prior work (Plan & Vershynin) by providing novel, explicit error formulas in the asymptotic regime.  
- Comment 1: Constrained and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate parameter mapping.  
- Comment 2: Figure 1 caption formatting should read “n = 768.”  
- Comment 3: Typo on page 5, line 247 needs correction.  
- Comment 4: The term “proximal function” used is the Moreau envelope with index τ; “prox” usually denotes the proximal operator—revise terminology/notation.  
- Comment 5: Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal interpretation using variational analysis tools.  
- Comment 6: Do not call the generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; refer to it as a convex optimization program or “decoder.”  
- Comment 7 / Experiments: Empirical results support the main claim that non-linear measurements behave asymptotically like linear measurements with Gaussian noise; the q-bit CS quantizer design and its Lloyd–Max connection are valuable applications.
