This paper explores the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. A common heuristic approach treats non-linear observations as noisy linear observations. Building upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend these results and derive novel precise explicit expressions for error estimation in an asymptotic regime. The accuracy of their predictions is validated through various special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm. The application of their findings to designing optimal quantizers in q-Bit Compressed Sensing (CS) and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

The paper is well-written, although the treatment is somewhat uneven, likely due to space constraints that prevent the inclusion of all necessary details.

This work is an extension of the research by Plan and Vershynin [17] and introduces novel aspects, such as explicit expressions for performance estimation error.

Some minor corrections and suggestions are noted:
1) On page 4, line 193, it is worth noting that the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent under appropriate correspondence.
2) The caption of Figure 1 should be corrected to read '$n$=768' instead of 'n=768'.
3) A typo is present on page 5, line 247, at 'When ...'.
4) The term 'proximal function' on page 5, line 294, is more commonly referred to as the Moreau envelope of index \tau in convex analysis. The notation 'prox' typically denotes the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope. Consider revising the notation for clarity.
5) It would be instructive to provide a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 on page 5, utilizing results from variational analysis.
6) The authors should refrain from referring to the Generalized Lasso as an algorithm in the text, as it is more accurately described as a convex program or decoder.
7) The experimental results demonstrating the application of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and the corresponding estimation error predictions are noteworthy.
