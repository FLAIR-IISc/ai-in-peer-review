

Summary: This paper examines the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design. It builds upon the heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear observations, extending the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] to an asymptotic regime. The authors derive novel, precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error, which are validated across multiple special cases (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The application to designing optimal quantizers in q-Bit CS and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality: This is a well-executed piece of work.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written, though the treatment is somewhat unbalanced. The limited space, however, prevents inclusion of all necessary details.

Originality: The paper extends Plan and Vershynin [17] in several novel aspects, notably providing explicit expressions for the estimation error performance.

Comments:  
1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized versions are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence.  
2) Caption of Figure 1: 'n=768' should be '$n$=768'.  
3) Page 5, line 247: Typo at 'When...'.  
4) Page 5, line 294: The "proximal function" referred to is well-known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index $\tau$. The notation is awkward, as 'prox' typically denotes the proximal point operator, not the Moreau envelope. We suggest revising this.  
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: A more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis would be instructive.  
6) Avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an "algorithm." It is a convex program (or decoder, per common CS terminology), not an algorithm.  
7) The experiments demonstrate the Generalized Lasso applied to non-linear measurements, with the theorems accurately predicting estimation error. The paper establishes that asymptotically, the estimation performance under non-linear measurements matches that under linear noisy measurements (Gaussian), providing novel, precise error expressions validated across key special cases. The application to q-Bit CS quantizer design and its relation to Lloyd-Max is insightful.
