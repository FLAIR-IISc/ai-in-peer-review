- Paper studies generalized LASSO under arbitrary nonlinear link functions and claims Theorem 2.1: asymptotic error is identical for linear and nonlinear measurements.
- Theorem 2.1 relies on Gaussian measurement vectors, which is a strong assumption; authors should justify necessity or discuss extension to sub-Gaussian/other distributions.
- Practical issue: choice of convex regularizer depends on set K containing x0, but the paper doesn’t address how to select a regularizer when K is unknown.
- Clarification needed on dependence of final error bound on µ and σ: does this mean error magnitude is fundamentally tied to the specific nonlinearity despite the form being the same?
- Missing discussion of related work on single‑index models (SIM) and sufficient dimension reduction (SDR), which also estimate linear structure under unknown link functions.
- Missing discussion of Yi et al.’s recent paper on optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear measurement (handles µ=0) and how the present contributions differ or extend that result.
- Overall assessment: well written with interesting results, but would be strengthened by addressing assumptions, practical regularizer selection, and connections to related literature.
