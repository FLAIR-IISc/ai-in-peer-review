This paper extends the analysis of the l2-lasso's precise statistical performance to the single-index model framework. The work also relaxes the standard Gaussian assumption on the prior (x_0's distribution), representing a valuable contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.
The manuscript is well-written. However, two points should be addressed:

1.  As noted by Brillinger (1982), a single-index model can be viewed as equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously established in Appendix A, it would be beneficial to mention it in the main text to offer a heuristic justification for the statement made in the blockquote on line 140.
2.  The paper focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square-root lasso. The principal advantage of the l2-lasso over the more common l2^2-lasso is that its optimal regularization parameter is independent of the noise variance (sigma), a quantity that is typically unknown. The findings presented here could help answer a practical question: is this justification for preferring the l2-lasso to the l2^2-lasso valid in practice?

This work expands the precise characterization of the l2-lasso's statistical properties to single-index models and is a worthwhile addition to the literature on the asymptotic behavior of such estimators.
