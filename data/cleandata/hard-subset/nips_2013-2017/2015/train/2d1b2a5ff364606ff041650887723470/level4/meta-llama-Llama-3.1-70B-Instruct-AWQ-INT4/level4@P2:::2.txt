This article presents a significant extension of the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models, thereby relaxing the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of x0. The authors' contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators is worthwhile.

The article is well-written, and the authors have done an excellent job in presenting their results. However, there are a few points that could be improved upon. Firstly, as pointed out by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. Although this connection is described rigorously in Appendix A, it would be beneficial to mention it in the main article to provide a heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140. This would help readers understand the underlying relationship between the single-index model and the linear model.

Secondly, the article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. One of the main benefits of the l2-lasso over the popular l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter has no dependence on the (usually unknown) noise variance (sigma). A practical question that the results could shed light on is whether this justification for the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso is valid. The authors could provide more insights into this aspect and discuss the implications of their results in this context.

Overall, the article is well-structured, and the authors have presented their results in a clear and concise manner. With a few minor adjustments, the article has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of statistical learning.
