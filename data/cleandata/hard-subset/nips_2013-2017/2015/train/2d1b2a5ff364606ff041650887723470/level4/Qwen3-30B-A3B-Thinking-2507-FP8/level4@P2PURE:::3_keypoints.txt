Paper studies the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design.  
Extends Plan & Vershynin to an asymptotic regime, deriving precise explicit expressions for estimation error.  
Theoretical predictions validated across special cases including Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm.  
Application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit compressed sensing and connection to the Lloyd–Max quantizer is insightful.  
Quality assessment: strong contribution.  
Clarity: paper is well-written but presentation is somewhat unbalanced and omits necessary details due to space constraints.  
Originality: novel extension of prior work, notably providing explicit error formulas.  
Comment: regularized and penalized versions are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence.  
Editorial: Figure 1 caption should use “n=768” formatting; typo on page 5, line 247.  
Notation issue: the “proximal function” referred to is the Moreau envelope of index τ; “prox” usually denotes the proximal operator—revise notation.  
Suggestion: provide a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using variational analysis.  
Terminology: avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; it is a convex program/decoder.  
Experiments: empirically show asymptotic performance matches that under linear noisy measurements and validate explicit error expressions and quantizer design application.
