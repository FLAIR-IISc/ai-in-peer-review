Summary
The paper studies estimation in single-index models y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) with structured x0 and Gaussian measurement matrix A. It proposes using the generalized LASSO (the “ℓ2-LASSO” or square-root LASSO) with a convex regularizer f that matches the structure (e.g., ℓ1, group ℓ1,2, nuclear norm), even when the link g is nonlinear and possibly unknown. The central result shows an asymptotic equivalence: when m,n → ∞ with m/n → δ, the estimation error of the generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements is exactly the same as for a linear model with scaled signal and Gaussian noise, y = μ A x0 + σ z, where μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)2], γ ∼ N(0,1). Building on this, the authors derive precise asymptotic formulas for the mean squared error via a scalar convex optimization (Theorem 2.2) under general convex regularizers, and they specialize to sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and low-rank (nuclear norm) settings, obtaining closed-form characterizations in some cases (e.g., Theorem 2.3). An application to q-bit quantized compressive sensing shows the generalized LASSO’s error is minimized by the Lloyd–Max quantizer, aligning quantizer design with estimation error rather than reconstruction fit.

Relation to prior work
The results significantly extend Brillinger (1982), who showed least squares is consistent up to a scaling under nonlinear links with Gaussian designs (in the low-dimensional regime), and Plan & Vershynin (2015), who provided high-probability upper bounds for constrained LASSO under nonlinear observations. This paper offers asymptotically exact error formulas (not just bounds), applies to regularized rather than constrained forms, and covers a broad class of convex regularizers. The analysis connects to precise LASSO error characterizations under linear models (AMP- and CGMT-based works), generalizing them to the nonlinear single-index setting via the μ,σ reduction.

Strengths
- Clear main message: nonlinear single-index measurements behave asymptotically like linear measurements with a specific scale μ and noise σ, for the purposes of generalized LASSO estimation.
- First asymptotically precise error characterizations for generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements, with broad applicability to common regularizers.
- Results apply for all λ and explicitly capture the roles of δ, λ, the regularizer f, and the link function g (through μ,σ).
- Specializations (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) make the theory concrete; simulations match predictions well.
- Practical consequence: principled quantizer design via Lloyd–Max emerges from the theory.

Weaknesses and suggestions
- The equivalence to a linear model with scale μ and noise σ—long recognized (e.g., Brillinger)—is carefully developed in Appendix A; a brief, intuitive explanation in the main text would help readers and would directly support the statement around line 140.
- The paper focuses on the ℓ2-LASSO (square-root LASSO). Its appeal lies in not requiring knowledge of σ to tune λ. Given the precise error formulas, it would be valuable to:
  - Compare predicted errors for ℓ2-LASSO versus ℓ2^2-LASSO under the same nonlinear-to-linear reduction.
  - Discuss whether the variance-independence advantage of ℓ2-LASSO persists in this nonlinear setting and how sensitive performance is to λ.
  - Provide guidance (or a data-driven rule) for choosing λ in practice.
- Assumptions and scope:
  - The Gaussian design is crucial; robustness to sub-Gaussian or correlated designs is not addressed and should be discussed.
  - The theory assumes μ ≠ 0 and specific regularity (Assumption 1); clarifying when these are met and what happens when μ = 0 would improve scope awareness.
  - Although g can be unknown, practical use may require estimating μ and σ; a short discussion of estimators for μ,σ (and their effect on performance) would be helpful.
- Clarity and reproducibility:
  - Provide a compact recipe for computing F(c1,c2,c3) for the common regularizers considered and code to solve the scalar optimization (10).
  - Lighten notation and highlight the main optimization (10) earlier with a brief interpretation of α,β,τ.

Evaluation
- Quality: Technically strong and careful, with precise asymptotics and consistency checks against known linear-model results. Proofs leverage modern tools for sharp characterizations.
- Clarity: Generally well written, but the key nonlinear-to-linear equivalence deserves a main-text heuristic, and guidance on λ and μ,σ would aid practitioners.
- Originality: High. This appears to be the first asymptotically exact treatment of generalized LASSO error under nonlinear single-index measurements with general convex regularizers.
- Significance: High. It unifies nonlinear and linear settings for generalized LASSO, informs regularizer and quantizer design, and extends precise-error analysis beyond ℓ1 under linear models.

Pros
- Asymptotically exact, interpretable error characterization for a broad class of nonlinear models and regularizers.
- Bridges and strengthens classic (Brillinger) and modern (Plan–Vershynin, CGMT/AMP) results.
- Practical insights for quantization and potentially for λ tuning.

Cons
- Restricted to Gaussian designs and asymptotic regime; no finite-sample guarantees.
- Limited discussion of λ selection and of estimating μ,σ from data.
- The core equivalence is relegated to the appendix; main-text intuition is missing.

Recommendation
A strong contribution to the theory of high-dimensional estimation under nonlinear measurements. I recommend acceptance after minor revisions to (i) elevate the nonlinear-to-linear equivalence in the main text, (ii) discuss ℓ2-LASSO versus ℓ2^2-LASSO and practical λ selection, and (iii) comment on robustness beyond Gaussian designs and on estimating μ,σ.
