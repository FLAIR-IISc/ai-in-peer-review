- Paper studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link, extending Plan & Vershynin to analyze asymptotic error of the generalized Lasso for arbitrary links with μ ≠ 0.  
- Main claim (Theorem 2.1): asymptotic estimation error is identical for linear and nonlinear links.  
- Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors; reviewer asks authors to justify this potentially restrictive assumption.  
- Question: how should the regularizer be chosen given the set K containing x0 — selection guidance is not provided.  
- Question: the equivalence still depends on μ and σ (link difficulty); are these dependencies essential?  
- Missing literature: the paper omits review of single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction work on estimation under unknown links.  
- Missing citation: recent Yi et al. paper on optimal linear estimation / sparse recovery when μ = 0 and equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with fairly interesting results.
