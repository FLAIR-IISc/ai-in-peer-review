This article presents a valuable extension of existing work on the statistical performance of the l2-lasso, now applied to the context of single-index models, and importantly, relaxes the common Gaussian prior assumption on the input variable. This represents a significant and worthwhile contribution to the field of asymptotic analysis for lasso-type estimators.

The manuscript is clearly written and well-organized. However, I suggest two points for consideration:

1. The equivalence between the single-index model and a linear model with scaled signal and non-standard noise – a connection highlighted by Brillinger (1982) and rigorously detailed in Appendix A – would benefit from brief mention within the main text. This would provide a helpful heuristic justification for the statement made on line 140.

2. The study specifically examines the l2-lasso (or square root-lasso). A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more common l2^2-lasso is its regularization parameter’s independence from the noise variance (sigma). It would be interesting to consider whether the results presented offer insight into the practical validity of this justification for preferring the l2-lasso. 

Overall, this article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models. It is a worthwhile contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.
