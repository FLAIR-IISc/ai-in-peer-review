- Main claim: For linear parameter estimation with an unknown nonlinear link (μ ≠ 0), the asymptotic error of the generalized Lasso matches that of the linear model (Theorem 2.1).  
- Builds on Plan and Vershynin’s work and provides a surprising equivalence between linear and nonlinear asymptotic errors under the stated conditions.  
- Key assumption: measurement vectors are Gaussian; the necessity and implications of Gaussianity are not justified and may be restrictive in practice.  
- Request to discuss whether the result extends to sub-Gaussian or more general designs, or to clarify why Gaussianity is essential.  
- Regularizer choice: paper assumes a convex regularizer but gives no guidance on selecting it based on prior knowledge that x0 ∈ K (e.g., structured/low-complexity sets).  
- Suggest providing practical/theoretical guidance tying the regularizer to K (atomic norms, descent cones, compatibility with K).  
- Dependence on μ and σ: final error depends on these link-dependent parameters; question whether these dependences are information-theoretically necessary.  
- Ask for lower bounds or analysis identifying regimes where the influence of μ and σ is unavoidable or negligible.  
- Missing related work: literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction should be reviewed and positioned relative to this paper.  
- Missing citation/comparison: recent paper by Yi et al. on optimal linear estimation under unknown link (handling μ = 0) should be cited and contrasted.  
- Overall assessment: paper is clearly written with interesting, nontrivial results; addressing Gaussianity, regularizer selection, μ/σ necessity, and missing references would improve clarity and relevance.
