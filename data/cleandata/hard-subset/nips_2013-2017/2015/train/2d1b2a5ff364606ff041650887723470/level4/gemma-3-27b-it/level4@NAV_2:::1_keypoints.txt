- Derives precise asymptotic error formulas for Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements, showing asymptotic equivalence to a scaled linear model.  
- Provides much tighter error bounds than prior work (builds on Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin).  
- Theorem 2.2 gives a precise general error characterization; Theorem 2.3 specializes results to sparse recovery.  
- Connects theory to q-bit compressive sensing and the Lloyd–Max algorithm, showing practical relevance.  
- Well-motivated, clearly written, and technically rigorous; supporting figures validate the derived formulas.  
- Strong originality and significant impact on understanding estimation limits under nonlinear measurements.  
- Main limitation: relies on Gaussian measurement matrix assumption, reducing applicability to non-Gaussian designs.  
- Secondary limitation: assumes unit-norm x0 for simplicity; generalization to arbitrary norms is acknowledged but not fully developed.  
- The connection to the single-index model literature is noted but could be explored more deeply.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant technical contributions and likely influential despite stated assumptions.
