- Addresses estimation of structured signals from non-linear measurements yi = g(a_i^T x0) using Generalized LASSO.  
- Main result (Theorem 2.1): LASSO performance with non-linear links is asymptotically identical to an equivalent linear model yi = µ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i.  
- µ and σ are determined by the first and second moments of g under a standard Gaussian input.  
- Provides an exact asymptotic formula for mean-squared error (Theorem 2.2).  
- Demonstrates accuracy for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.  
- Presents an application to optimal multi-bit quantizer design.  
- Strength: Elegant equivalence that reduces a complex non-linear problem to a linear analysis.  
- Strength: Precise asymptotic formulas capturing roles of (m, n, λ), signal structure, and link function.  
- Strength: Generality across convex regularizers and link functions, with strong simulation support.  
- Weakness: Analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix, limiting practical applicability and extension to sub-Gaussian ensembles is unclear.  
- Question: More intuition is needed for why only µ and σ (first and second moments) suffice and higher-order moments are irrelevant asymptotically.  
- Weakness: Little guidance on choosing regularizers for specific signal structures beyond standard pairings.  
- Weakness: Literature review misses broader single-index/sufficient-dimension-reduction work and omits Yi et al. (2015), which addresses the µ=0 case and proposes an algorithm for it.  
- Overall: Technically sound, original, and significant work that merits acceptance, but should address Gaussian assumptions and discuss/compare to Yi et al. (2015).
