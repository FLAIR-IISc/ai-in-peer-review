Summary: This paper studies the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements under a Gaussian design. A common heuristic is to treat non-linear observations as noisy linear ones. Building on Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend those results and, by moving to an asymptotic regime, derive novel, explicit, and precise expressions for the estimation error. The accuracy of these predictions is validated across several special cases (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The application to designing optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality: This is a solid piece of work.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written, though somewhat unbalanced; limited space prevents inclusion of all necessary details.

Originality: While building on Plan and Vershynin [17], the paper introduces several novel aspects. In particular, the explicit expressions for the performance (estimation) error are new.

Comments:
1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence.
2) Figure 1 caption: 'n=768' should be typeset as '$n$=768'.
3) Page 5, line 247: There is a typo in "When ...".
4) Page 5, line 294: What the authors call the “proximal function” is well known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index tau. The notation is awkward, since prox is commonly used for the proximal operator (mapping), not the Moreau envelope. I recommend revising the terminology.
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: It would be instructive to provide a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis.
6) Please avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm” in the text; it is a convex program (or “decoder,” in CS parlance), not an algorithm.
7) Experiments: The authors present results applying the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and show that the theorems accurately predict the estimation error. The paper proves that, with non-linear measurements and a Gaussian design, the asymptotic estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso matches that of a linear model with Gaussian noise. It provides new, precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error; confirms these findings across multiple special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm); and offers an insightful application to the design of optimal quantizers in q-Bit CS, including its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer.
