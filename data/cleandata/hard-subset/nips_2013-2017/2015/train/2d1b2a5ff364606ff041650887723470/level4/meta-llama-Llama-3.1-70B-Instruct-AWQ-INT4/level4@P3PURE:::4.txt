This paper explores linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, building upon the recent work of Plan and Vershynin. The authors investigate the asymptotic estimation error using generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with \mu \ne 0, demonstrating that the asymptotic error remains the same regardless of whether the link function is linear or nonlinear, as shown in Theorem 2.1.

However, Theorem 2.1 relies on the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be restrictive in certain cases. The authors should provide a clear explanation for the necessity of this assumption in order for the conclusion to hold.

Several questions arise from the paper. Firstly, how should the regularizer be chosen based on the set \kai in which x_0 lies? Although the assumption of a convex regularizer is discussed, the problem of selecting an appropriate regularizer appears to be unresolved. Secondly, while the results show that nonlinear measurements are equivalent to linear measurements, the conclusion involves dependencies on \mu and \sigma, which characterize the hardness of estimation from the nonlinear function g. It would be interesting to know if the authors have any insights into the necessity of these dependencies.

Furthermore, there are some notable references missing from the paper. Research on single index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which study linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, is not reviewed. Additionally, a recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions that have zero \mu. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under proper conditions, supported by both theoretical proof and empirical results.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with fairly interesting results, presenting a valuable contribution to the field.
