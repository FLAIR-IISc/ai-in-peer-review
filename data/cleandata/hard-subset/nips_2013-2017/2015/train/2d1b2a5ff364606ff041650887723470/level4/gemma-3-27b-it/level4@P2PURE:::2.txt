This article extends the precise statistical performance analysis of the l2-lasso to the setting of single-index models, and relaxes the common Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of x_0. This represents a valuable contribution to the literature concerning the asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators. 

The article is well-written. However, I have two points for consideration:

1. As noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is mathematically equivalent to a linear model with a scaled, unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously established in Appendix A, briefly mentioning it within the main text would provide a helpful heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140.

2. The article concentrates on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more common l2^2-lasso is that its optimal regularization parameter does not depend on the often-unknown noise variance (sigma). The results presented could be further leveraged to investigate whether this theoretical advantage provides a practical justification for preferring the l2-lasso in applications.

This article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models. It is a worthwhile contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.
