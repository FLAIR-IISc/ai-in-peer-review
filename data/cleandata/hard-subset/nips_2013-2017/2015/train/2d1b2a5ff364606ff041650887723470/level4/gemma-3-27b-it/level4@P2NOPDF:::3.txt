This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso under a Gaussian design with non-linear measurements. A common heuristic suggests treating non-linear observations as noisy linear ones; the authors leverage the foundational work of Plan and Vershynin [17] to rigorously extend their results. By considering an asymptotic regime, they derive new, precise, explicit expressions for error estimation. The accuracy of these predictions is demonstrated through validation with several special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. A particularly insightful application is presented in the design of optimal quantizers for q-Bit compressed sensing, and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is compelling.

Overall, this is a strong contribution. The paper is generally well-written, although the depth of coverage feels somewhat uneven, likely constrained by space limitations. 

The work demonstrates clear originality. It builds directly on Plan and Vershynin [17], providing significant extensions and novel results, notably the explicit expressions for performance estimation error.

Here are some specific comments and suggestions:

1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate scaling of the regularization parameter.

2) Caption of Figure 1: Please replace 'n=768' with '$n$=768' for consistent mathematical notation.

3) Page 5, line 247: There appears to be a typographical error in the phrase beginning 'When…'.

4) Page 5, line 294: The function the authors refer to as the “proximal function” is, in convex analysis, more accurately described as the Moreau envelope of index $\tau$. The current notation is also slightly awkward; ‘prox’ is conventionally used to denote the proximal point or operator, not the Moreau envelope. I recommend a change in terminology to avoid confusion.

5) Page 5, Assumption 1: Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption using results from variational analysis would be beneficial and instructive.

6) Throughout the paper, the authors should avoid characterizing the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm.” It is more accurately described as a (convex) optimization program or, in the context of compressed sensing, a decoder.

7) The experimental results effectively demonstrate the performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and validate the theoretical predictions regarding estimation error. In essence, the paper proves that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that of a linear, Gaussian noise model. The novel, precise expressions for estimation error are supported by these findings and numerous special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm). The application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit compressed sensing and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.
