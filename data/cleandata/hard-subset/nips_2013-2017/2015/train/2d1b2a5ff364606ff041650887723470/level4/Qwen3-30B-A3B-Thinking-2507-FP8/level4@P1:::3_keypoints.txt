- Studies Generalized Lasso for nonlinear measurements with Gaussian designs, extending Plan & Vershynin.  
- Key insight: asymptotic estimation with nonlinear measurements is equivalent to a linear model yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].  
- Derives precise asymptotic error formulas for many convex regularizers (L1, group-L1, nuclear, etc.).  
- Provides rigorous asymptotic analysis that sharpens prior upper bounds into exact error predictions.  
- Framework applies to general convex regularizers and link functions, giving broad applicability.  
- Extensive numerical experiments validate theoretical predictions across sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signals.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, though some sections need clearer explanations.  
- Exposition of the Moreau envelope and proximal operators should be improved for non-expert readers.  
- Novelty: provides explicit asymptotic error expressions beyond the qualitative equivalence previously known.  
- Practical link: shows optimal quantizer for estimation error is the Lloyd-Max quantizer, relevant to quantized compressed sensing.  
- Significance: important implications for compressed sensing with quantized measurements and quantizer design.  
- Strength: mathematically rigorous with careful attention to technical assumptions.  
- Strength: comprehensive coverage of multiple signal structures and regularizers.  
- Strength: clear connection between theoretical results and practical quantizer design.  
- Strength: strong experimental validation across diverse settings.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 needs a more formal interpretation using variational analysis.  
- Weakness: Non-standard notation calling the Moreau envelope a "proximal function"; should use standard terminology.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent terminology referring to the Generalized Lasso as an "algorithm" rather than a convex program.  
- Weakness: Minor typographical and grammatical errors (e.g., "shouldbe" in Figure 1 caption).  
- Specific: clarify equivalence between regularized and penalized Generalized Lasso formulations (page 4, line 193).  
- Specific: format "n=768" consistently with mathematical typesetting in Figure 1 caption.  
- Specific: correct minor grammatical error on page 5, line 247.  
- Specific: use "Moreau envelope" instead of "proximal function" (page 5, line 294).  
- Specific: provide variational-analysis interpretation of Assumption 1 (page 5, Assumption 1).  
- Specific: consistently correct terminology for Generalized Lasso throughout the paper.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution to high-dimensional statistics and compressed sensing; recommend acceptance.
