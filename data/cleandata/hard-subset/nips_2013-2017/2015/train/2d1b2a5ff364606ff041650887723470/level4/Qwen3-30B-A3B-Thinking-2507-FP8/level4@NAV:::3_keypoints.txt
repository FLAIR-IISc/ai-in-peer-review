- Paper studies Generalized Lasso under non-linear Gaussian measurements and treats non-linear observations as noisy linear measurements.  
- Authors extend Plan & Vershynin’s work using an asymptotic framework to derive precise explicit formulas for estimation error.  
- Predictions validated empirically across special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm).  
- Application to designing optimal quantizers in q-bit compressed sensing and connection to Lloyd–Max quantizer is insightful.  
- Quality: well-executed contribution.  
- Clarity: well-written but somewhat unbalanced; space constraints omit some details.  
- Originality: builds on prior work but introduces novel explicit error expressions.  
- Comment 1: Regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate parameter correspondence.  
- Comment 2: Figure 1 caption typo: use "$n$=768" instead of 'n=768'.  
- Comment 3: Minor typo on Page 5, line 247 at 'When ...'.  
- Comment 4: “Proximal function” is non-standard—Moreau envelope of index τ is the correct term; prox usually denotes the proximal operator, so revise notation/terminology.  
- Comment 5: Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal variational-analysis interpretation.  
- Comment 6: Avoid calling the generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; it is a convex program/decoder in standard CS terminology.  
- Comment 7: Experiments confirm asymptotic performance matches noisy linear model and validate the theoretical error expressions.
