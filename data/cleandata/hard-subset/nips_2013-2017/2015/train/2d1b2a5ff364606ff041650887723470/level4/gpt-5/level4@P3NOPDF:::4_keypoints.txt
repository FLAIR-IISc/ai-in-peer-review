- Summary: Paper shows generalized Lasso under unknown nonlinear link g (with μ ≠ 0) has the same asymptotic error as the corresponding linear model under Gaussian design (Theorem 2.1).  
- Strength: Conceptually clean: asymptotic equivalence between nonlinear and linear measurements once effective parameters are accounted for.  
- Strength: Broad generality: applies to arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0 and to general convex regularizers.  
- Strength: Potential practical impact: justifies use of convex estimators for many nonlinear measurement models.  
- Concern (1) Gaussian design: need clarification on whether Gaussianity is essential, which proof steps use exact Gaussian properties, and possible extensions to sub-Gaussian/isotropic designs or partial results.  
- Concern (2) Regularizer choice: lacks principled guidance for selecting a convex regularizer from prior set K (e.g., gauge, atomic norms) and relating error to geometric quantities like Gaussian width; also need discussion of data-driven/adaptive selection.  
- Concern (3) Role of μ and σ: question whether μ- and σ-dependence is information-theoretically necessary, whether matching lower bounds exist, and whether μ, σ can be consistently estimated and plugged in.  
- Concern (4) Boundary/special cases: missing treatment of μ = 0 cases (e.g., symmetric sign), potential modified estimators or additional structure to restore consistency, and finite-sample error/rates.  
- Relation to prior work: paper should connect to single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction literatures that estimate linear indices under unknown links.  
- Relation to prior work: discuss recent high-dimensional results (e.g., Yi et al.) addressing μ = 0, clarifying differences in assumptions, targets, and guarantees.  
- Presentation: define μ and σ at first use, maintain consistent notation, and explicitly state assumptions on design, g, and the regularizer.  
- Presentation: give practical interpretations of μ and σ for common nonlinearities (logistic, probit, sign with dithering) and how they map to effective SNR.  
- Presentation: include empirical illustrations showing equivalence across different g and robustness beyond Gaussian designs.  
- Overall assessment: Well-written, timely, and conceptually appealing result; acceptance recommended contingent on addressing Gaussian-robustness, regularizer guidance, μ/σ necessity and estimation, and expanded related work.
