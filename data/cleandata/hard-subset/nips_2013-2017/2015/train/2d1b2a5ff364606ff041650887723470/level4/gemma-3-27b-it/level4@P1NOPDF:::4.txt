This paper investigates linear estimation with a nonlinear link function. Motivated by the recent work of Plan and Vershynin, it studies the asymptotic estimation error when using a generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0. The key finding is that the asymptotic error is identical for both linear and nonlinear link functions, as demonstrated in Theorem 2.1.

Theorem 2.1 relies on the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be restrictive in certain applications. The authors should clarify why this assumption is necessary for the conclusion to hold.

I have a few questions for the authors: 1. How should the regularizer be chosen based on the set Kai, within which x₀ lies? While the paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, it doesn’t yet address this practical selection problem. 2. The result demonstrates equivalence between nonlinear and linear measurement, yet the conclusion involves μ and σ, which characterize the difficulty of estimation from the nonlinear function g. I am interested in the authors’ thoughts on the necessity of these dependencies.

Several relevant references are missing. 1. The single index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature studies linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, yet this work is not discussed or reviewed. 2. A recent paper, “Optimal linear estimation under unknown link function” by Yi et al., introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery, even when the link function has μ = 0. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under appropriate conditions. The conclusion is well-supported by both theoretical proofs and empirical results. 

Overall, this is a well-written paper presenting fairly interesting results.
