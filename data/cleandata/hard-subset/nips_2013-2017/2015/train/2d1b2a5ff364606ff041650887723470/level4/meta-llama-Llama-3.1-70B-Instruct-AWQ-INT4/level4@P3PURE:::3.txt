This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, a problem that is often addressed using the heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear observations. Building on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend these results and, by moving to an asymptotic regime, provide novel precise explicit expressions of the error estimation. The accuracy of their predictions is confirmed with several special cases, including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and the nuclear norm. Furthermore, the application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer offers valuable insights.

The quality of this work is high. The paper is well-written, although the treatment is somewhat unbalanced, likely due to space constraints that prevent the inclusion of all necessary details.

The paper builds upon existing work by Plan and Vershynin [17] and expands on it in several novel aspects. For instance, the explicit expressions of the performance estimation error are new.

Several minor issues and suggestions for improvement are noted below:

1. On page 4, line 193, it should be clarified that the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent with appropriate correspondence.
2. The caption of Figure 1 should be corrected to read '$n$=768' instead of 'n=768'.
3. A typo is present on page 5, line 247, at the beginning of the sentence 'When ...'.
4. On page 5, line 294, the authors refer to the Moreau envelope of index \tau as the proximal function. While this is correct, the notation used is unconventional, as 'prox' is generally used for the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope. It is suggested that the notation be changed to avoid confusion.
5. Assumption 1 on page 5 could be given a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis, which would provide additional context.
6. Throughout the text, the authors should avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an algorithm. Instead, it is a convex program (or decoder, to use the terminology widely used in CS).
7. The experiments demonstrate the application of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and show how the theorems predict the estimation error. This paper proves that asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with such measurements is the same as if the measurements were linear and noisy (Gaussian). The authors provide novel precise explicit expressions of the estimation error and confirm their findings with several special cases, including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and the nuclear norm.
