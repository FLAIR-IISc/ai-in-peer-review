- Studies generalized LASSO for recovering structured signal from non-linear measurements with Gaussian design and derives precise high-dimensional asymptotic error expressions.
- Main contribution: asymptotic equivalence to scaled noisy linear measurements yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].
- Provides a scalar convex program that yields the asymptotic MSE for a wide class of convex regularizers and all λ > 0.
- Specializes theory to sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and low-rank (nuclear norm) settings and validates results via simulations.
- Application to q-bit compressive sensing: optimizing quantizer to minimize LASSO error recovers the Lloyd–Max quantizer.
- Quality: analysis is technically sound and claims are supported; empirical results align well with theory.

- Originality: builds on prior work but makes substantial novel contributions, notably the exact equivalence and precise error characterizations.
- Significance: first asymptotically precise performance characterizations for LASSO-type estimators with non-linear measurements under Gaussian design; μ and σ capture impact of non-linearities.
