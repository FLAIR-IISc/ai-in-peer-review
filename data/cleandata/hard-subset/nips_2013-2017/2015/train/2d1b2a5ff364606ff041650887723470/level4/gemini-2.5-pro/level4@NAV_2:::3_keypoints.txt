- Core contribution: Proves that in high-dimensional asymptotics, non-linear measurements are equivalent to a scaled linear model with additive Gaussian noise for Generalized LASSO.
- Main theoretical advance: Provides asymptotically exact, explicit MSE formulas rather than prior non-asymptotic upper bounds.
- Validation: Numerical experiments confirm predictions across LASSO (sparse), Group LASSO (group-sparse), and nuclear-norm (low-rank) cases.
- Practical application: Shows q-bit compressive sensing quantizer design reduces to minimizing σ²/μ², linking optimal design to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.
- Significance/originality: Represents a notable advance in understanding interplay of link function, signal structure, regularizer, and dimensions.
- Technical quality: Analysis is sound and the equivalence to an effective linear noisy model is well established and supported.
- Clarity concern: Presentation is dense in places; expanding intuition behind technical assumptions and results would help readability.
- Notation/terminology: Recommend using “Moreau envelope” instead of prox notation for ef,τ(v), and refer to Generalized LASSO as a “convex program/estimator” rather than an “algorithm.”
- Theoretical presentation: Assumption 1 is central but would benefit from a more formal interpretation or connection to variational/convex analysis.
- Minor suggestion: Note equivalence between constrained and penalized LASSO formulations via parameter mapping for completeness.
- Typos: Caption of Figure 1 should typeset n=768 correctly, and there is a typo on page 5, line 247.
