This paper extends the precise statistical characterization of the l2-lasso (or square-root lasso) to single-index models. A key contribution is the relaxation of the common Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of the signal. The work is a valuable addition to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The paper is well-written; however, I have two suggestions for improvement:

1.  As established by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is described rigorously in Appendix A, it should also be mentioned in the main text to provide a heuristic justification for the statement on line 140.

2.  The analysis focuses on the l2-lasso. A primary motivation for using the l2-lasso over the standard l2^2-lasso is that the optimal regularization parameter is independent of the (typically unknown) noise variance, σ. The results presented here offer an opportunity to investigate whether this justification remains valid in the single-index setting. A discussion of this practical implication would strengthen the paper.
