Summary: The paper studies generalized Lasso estimators under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design. Building on the framework of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors move to an asymptotic regime and derive exact, explicit formulas for the estimation error, showing that the generalized Lasso under non-linear observations behaves as if the observations were linear with additive Gaussian noise whose parameters depend on the nonlinearity. The theory covers a broad class of convex regularizers (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm), and the predictions are validated empirically. An application to designing optimal quantizers for q-bit compressed sensing and its connection to the Lloyd–Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality: This is a strong and technically solid piece of work. The asymptotically exact characterization is valuable and goes beyond prior non-asymptotic bounds, yielding concrete performance predictions and design insights (e.g., for quantization).

Clarity: The paper is generally well written, though the exposition is somewhat unbalanced and space constraints obscure some details. A clearer statement of the asymptotic regime and more consistent notation would improve readability.

Originality and significance: The work extends [17] in meaningful ways. The exact error characterization for generalized Lasso under non-linear sensing, its specialization to popular regularizers, and the quantizer design consequences appear new and practically relevant.

Detailed comments and suggestions:
- Penalized vs constrained forms (Page 4, line 193): Please note explicitly that the penalized and constrained formulations are equivalent under mild regularity, with a one-to-one correspondence between the penalty parameter and the constraint radius (e.g., via KKT conditions). A short pointer or lemma would help readers unfamiliar with this standard equivalence.
- Figure 1 caption: “n=768” should be typeset consistently; if using math mode, write n=768.
- Page 5, line 247: There is a typo beginning at “When …”; please correct.
- Proximal notation (Page 5, line 294): What you call the “proximal function” is the Moreau envelope (of index τ) in convex analysis. The symbol prox is widely used for the proximal operator prox_{τg}; using it for the envelope is nonstandard and potentially confusing. I suggest denoting the envelope as e_τ(g) or M_τ g, and reserving prox for the operator.
- Assumption 1 (Page 5): It would be helpful to provide a more formal interpretation using variational analysis. For instance, connect the assumption to the geometry of the descent/tangent cone at the true parameter and its Gaussian width, or to standard regularity conditions (e.g., sharpness or error bounds). A brief statement clarifying how this assumption ensures identifiability and a well-posed asymptotic limit would aid comprehension.
- Terminology: Avoid calling the generalized Lasso an “algorithm.” It is a convex program/estimator (or “decoder” in the compressed sensing terminology). Algorithms (e.g., proximal gradient, ADMM) solve the program.
- Asymptotic regime: Please state precisely the scaling of n, p, sparsity/structure, and regularization parameters. A short discussion of how the asymptotics translate to finite-sample performance (e.g., how large n must be for accurate prediction) would be valuable for practitioners.
- Constants induced by the nonlinearity: Since the equivalence to a linear Gaussian-noise model hinges on specific constants (e.g., mean-slope μ and effective noise variance σ^2), consider collecting their definitions, assumptions for their existence, and practical estimation strategies (when f is unknown) in one place. A small subsection or table would improve usability.
- Robustness: Briefly discuss robustness to deviations from Gaussian design (e.g., sub-Gaussian or light-tailed designs) and to model mismatch in the nonlinearity. If the theory relies crucially on Gaussianity (e.g., via CGMT or Gordon’s theorem), state this clearly.
- Experiments: The empirical section is convincing. A few improvements could further strengthen it:
  - Report standard deviations/confidence intervals across trials.
  - Describe how the regularization parameter is chosen (oracle, cross-validation, theory-driven), and include a short sensitivity analysis.
  - When illustrating the “linearization” effect, include a direct comparison to the linear-noise surrogate predicted by the theory, using the same μ, σ^2.
  - For the quantizer design, comment on when the optimal design departs from Lloyd–Max and why (e.g., interaction with the estimator’s geometry), and include results across multiple bit depths q.
- Presentation:
  - Include a concise proof sketch outlining the main tools (e.g., CGMT/Gordon inequalities, convex geometry of descent cones) and how they yield exact asymptotics.
  - Where space is tight, add missing technical details and proofs to the supplement and refer to them clearly in the main text.
  - Ensure consistent notation for vectors, norms, and operators throughout.

Minor corrections:
- Caption of Figure 1: write “n=768” (math font optional).
- Page 4, line 193: clarify equivalence of penalized and constrained forms (as above).
- Page 5, line 247: fix the typo beginning with “When …”.
- Page 5, line 294: rename the “proximal function” to the “Moreau envelope” and adjust notation accordingly.
- Page 5, Assumption 1: add a more formal interpretation via variational/convex analysis.
- Throughout: avoid referring to the generalized Lasso as an algorithm.

Overall assessment: This paper offers a clear and useful asymptotic characterization of generalized Lasso estimators under non-linear sensing with Gaussian design, with thoughtful applications and solid empirical support. Despite some presentation issues and minor notation/terminology concerns, the contribution is substantive and timely. I recommend acceptance after minor revisions.
