Summary: This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. A common heuristic suggests that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear observations. Building upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend their results and, by moving to an asymptotic regime, provide novel and precise explicit expressions for error estimation. The accuracy of these predictions is demonstrated through numerous special cases, including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and the nuclear norm regularization. The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit Compressive Sensing (CS) and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality: This is a strong piece of work.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written, though the treatment is somewhat unbalanced, likely due to space constraints that prevent inclusion of all necessary details.

Originality: The paper builds upon the foundational work of Plan and Vershynin [17], expanding it in several novel aspects. Notably, the explicit expressions for performance estimation error represent a significant contribution.

Comments:

1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate parameter correspondence.

2) Caption of Figure 1: ‘n=768’ should be ‘$n$=768’.

3) Page 5, line 247: There appears to be a typographical error in the phrase "When…".

4) Page 5, line 294: The function the authors refer to as the “proximal function” is well-known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope associated with parameter $\tau$. The current notation is somewhat awkward, as “prox” is typically reserved for the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope itself. I recommend changing the notation.

5) Page 5, Assumption 1: Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption using results from variational analysis would be instructive.

6) The authors should avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm.” It is more accurately described as a (convex) program, or a decoder, following terminology commonly used in Compressive Sensing.

7) Experiments: The authors demonstrate the application of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and validate the theoretical predictions for estimation error. This paper proves that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with such measurements is equivalent to that of the Lasso with linear, noisy (Gaussian) measurements. The authors provide novel and precise explicit expressions for the estimation error, and confirm their findings through various special cases (including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and the nuclear norm). The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.
