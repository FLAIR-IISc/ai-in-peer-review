This paper considers linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, specifically studying the asymptotic estimation error using the generalized LASSO for arbitrary link functions with a non-zero mean. The authors draw inspiration from the recent work of Plan and Vershynin, and their results show that the asymptotic error is the same for both linear and nonlinear link functions, as stated in Theorem 2.1.

The paper assumes that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which might be a strict assumption in some cases. The authors should provide further explanation on why this assumption is necessary for the conclusion to hold.

Several questions arise from the paper. Firstly, how does one choose a regularizer based on the set K in which x0 lies? While the assumption of a convex regularizer is discussed, the problem of selecting an appropriate regularizer for a given set K is not addressed. Secondly, the result shows that nonlinear measurements are equivalent to linear measurements, but the conclusion involves the parameters μ and σ, which characterize the hardness of estimation from the nonlinear function g. It would be interesting to know if the authors have any thoughts on the necessity of these dependencies.

There are also some missing references. The paper does not review any literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which study linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. Additionally, a recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions that have zero μ. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under proper conditions, supported by careful theoretical proof and empirical results.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with fairly interesting results. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field, and their work has the potential to impact various applications. However, addressing the above questions and incorporating the missing references would further strengthen the paper.
