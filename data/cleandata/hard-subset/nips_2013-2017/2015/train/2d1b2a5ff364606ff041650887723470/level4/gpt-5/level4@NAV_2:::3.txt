Summary: This paper studies the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with a Gaussian design. Building on the common heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear ones, the authors extend Plan and Vershynin [17] by moving to an asymptotic regime and deriving new, explicit, and precise expressions for the estimation error. Their predictions are validated across several settings (standard Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The application to q-bit compressed sensing for quantizer design and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly illuminating.

Quality: This is a solid and technically sound contribution.

Clarity: The manuscript is generally clear and well-organized, though some parts feel uneven in depth. Space constraints understandably limit detailed exposition in places.

Originality: While grounded in Plan and Vershynin [17], the paper advances the state of the art in several novel ways, notably via explicit formulas for prediction error.

Comments:
1) Page 4, line 193: the constrained and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate parameter mapping.
2) Figure 1 caption: “n=768” should be typeset as “n = 768.”
3) Page 5, line 247: there is a typo starting with “When ...”.
4) Page 5, line 294: what is called the “proximal function” is widely known as the Moreau envelope with index τ. The current notation is confusing, since “prox” typically denotes the proximal operator, not the envelope. Please revise the terminology/notation.
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: it would be helpful to provide a more formal interpretation using tools from variational analysis.
6) Please avoid referring to the generalized Lasso as an “algorithm.” It is a convex optimization program (or “decoder,” in CS terminology), not an algorithm.
7) Experiments: the authors present results applying the generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and show that the theoretical predictions closely match the observed estimation error. The main claim—that asymptotically the generalized Lasso under these non-linear measurements performs as if the measurements were linear with Gaussian noise—is supported empirically. The work offers precise error expressions and confirms them across Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm cases. The study of optimal quantizer design in q-bit CS and its link to Lloyd-Max is a valuable and insightful application.
