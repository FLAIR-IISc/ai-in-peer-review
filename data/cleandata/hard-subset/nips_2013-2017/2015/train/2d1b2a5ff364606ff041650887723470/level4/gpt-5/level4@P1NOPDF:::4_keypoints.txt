- Paper shows generalized Lasso asymptotic error with unknown nonlinear link equals the linear-case error (Theorem 2.1).
- Theorem 2.1 relies on Gaussian measurement vectors; authors should explain which parts need Gaussianity and whether result extends to sub-Gaussian/isotropic/heavy-tailed designs.
- Need clarification which proof steps use rotational invariance or exact Gaussian properties and possible modifications for non-Gaussian ensembles.
- Paper assumes a convex regularizer from a known set K containing x0 but gives no practical guidance on constructing/selecting K for sparsity, group structure, low-rank, etc.
- Authors should discuss sensitivity of guarantees to misspecification of K and offer practical regularizer-selection advice.
- Bounds depend explicitly on μ and σ; reviewer asks whether these dependencies are information-theoretically necessary or artifacts of the analysis/estimator.
- Request insight on whether alternatives or conditions could reduce or remove dependence on μ and σ, and comments on tightness of those factors.
- Paper omits comparison to single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction literature; add brief positioning relative to that work.
- Must cite and contrast the recent Yi et al. paper on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, which is closely related and handles μ = 0 for sparse recovery.
- Overall positive assessment but asks for clarifications on Gaussian design necessity, regularizer selection, μ/σ dependence, and related-work discussion to strengthen the paper.
