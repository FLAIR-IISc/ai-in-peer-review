The article makes a valuable contribution to the literature by extending the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models, while also relaxing the common Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of x_0. The paper is well-written, presenting a worthwhile advancement in the understanding of the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

However, there are a couple of points that could be improved. Firstly, as noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. Although this connection is rigorously described in Appendix A, it would be beneficial to mention it in the main article as well, providing a heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140. This addition would enhance the clarity of the paper.

Secondly, the article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more popular l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (usually unknown) noise variance (sigma). A practical question that the results could shed light on is whether this justification for the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso holds in practice.
