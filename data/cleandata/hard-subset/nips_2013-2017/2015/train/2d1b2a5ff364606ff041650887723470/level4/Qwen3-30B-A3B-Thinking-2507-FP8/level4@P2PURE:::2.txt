

The article extends prior work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso (also known as the square root-lasso) to single-index models, while relaxing the standard Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of $x_0$. This constitutes a valuable contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.  

The article is well-written. However:  
1. As noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While Appendix A rigorously establishes this connection, it should also be explicitly stated in the main text to provide heuristic justification for the claim in the block quote on line 140.  
2. The paper focuses on the l2-lasso, whose primary practical advantage over the standard l2²-lasso is that the "oracle" regularization parameter value does not depend on the (typically unknown) noise variance $\sigma$. The results should address whether this theoretical justification for preferring the l2-lasso over the l2²-lasso holds in the single-index model context.
