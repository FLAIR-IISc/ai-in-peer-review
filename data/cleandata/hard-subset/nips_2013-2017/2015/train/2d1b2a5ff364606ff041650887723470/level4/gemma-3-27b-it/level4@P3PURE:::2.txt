The article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models, and relaxes the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of x_0. This is a worthwhile contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The article is well-written. However, I have two suggestions for improvement. 

1. As pointed out by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously described in Appendix A, it should also be mentioned in the main article to heuristically justify the statement in the blockquote on line 140.

2. The article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. A key benefit of the l2-lasso over the more common l2^2-lasso is that the “oracle” value of the regularization parameter is independent of the (often unknown) noise variance (σ). A practical question that the results could potentially address is whether this theoretical justification for preferring the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso holds in practice.
