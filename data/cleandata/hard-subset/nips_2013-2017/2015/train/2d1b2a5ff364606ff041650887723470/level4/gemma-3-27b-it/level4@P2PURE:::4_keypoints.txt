- Main contribution: Shows asymptotic estimation error of generalized lasso is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions (Theorem 2.1).  
- Surprise: Equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements is surprising and central to the paper’s claim.  
- Gaussian assumption concern: Theorem 2.1 relies on Gaussian measurement vectors—authors should clarify whether this assumption is necessary or can be relaxed.  
- Regularization tuning question: Paper assumes a convex regularizer but does not give practical guidance on choosing the regularization parameter given x0 ∈ K.  
- Role of μ and σ: Although equivalence is claimed, the final error depends on μ and σ from the link g; the reviewer asks if these dependencies are essential and why.  
- Incomplete bibliography: Missing citations to single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction literature relevant to unknown link functions.  
- Missing related work: Does not cite Yi et al.’s recent paper on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, which reports similar equivalence results and a sparse-recovery algorithm for μ = 0.  
- Overall judgment: Well-written paper with interesting and potentially significant results.
