This paper explores linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, drawing inspiration from the recent work of Plan and Vershynin. It investigates the asymptotic estimation error using generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with \mu \ne 0. Notably, the results show that the asymptotic error remains the same regardless of whether the link function is linear or nonlinear, as demonstrated in Theorem 2.1.

Theorem 2.1 relies on the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be too restrictive in certain cases. The authors should provide justification for this assumption and explain why it is necessary for the conclusion to hold.

Several questions arise from the paper. Firstly, how does one choose a regularizer based on the set \kai in which x_0 lies? Although the paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, this issue appears to remain unaddressed. Secondly, while the results suggest that nonlinear measurements are equivalent to linear measurements, the conclusion involves dependencies on \mu and \sigma, which characterize the hardness of estimation from the nonlinear function g. I would appreciate the authors' thoughts on the necessity of these dependencies.

The paper lacks references to relevant work in the area. For instance, research on single index models and sufficient dimension reduction has explored linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, but these studies are not reviewed in the paper. Additionally, a recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions that have zero \mu. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under proper conditions, supporting its conclusion with thorough theoretical proof and empirical results. Overall, this is a well-written paper with fairly interesting results.
