This paper explores the concept of linear estimation with a nonlinear link function, drawing inspiration from the work of Plan and Vershynin. The authors investigate the asymptotic estimation error using generalized lasso for an arbitrary link function with \mu \ne 0, revealing that the asymptotic error remains the same regardless of the link function's linearity, as demonstrated in Theorem 2.1.

Theorem 2.1 is predicated on the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be too restrictive in certain scenarios. The authors should provide justification for this assumption and its necessity in supporting the conclusion.

Several questions arise from this study: 1. What is the method for selecting a regularizer based on the set \kappa containing x_0? Although the paper discusses convex regularizers, this issue appears to be unresolved. 2. The findings suggest equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements, but the conclusion involves dependencies on \mu and \sigma, which characterize the estimation difficulty from the nonlinear function g. It would be beneficial to know the authors' thoughts on the necessity of these dependencies.

Notable references are missing from the paper. Firstly, research on single index models and sufficient dimension reduction has addressed linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, yet these studies are not reviewed. Secondly, a recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," presents a novel algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions having zero \mu. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under specific conditions, supported by rigorous theoretical proof and empirical results.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with intriguing results, providing valuable insights into the field of linear estimation with nonlinear link functions.
