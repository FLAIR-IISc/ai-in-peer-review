Summary: This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. A known heuristic often used is that non-linear observations may be treated as noisy linear observations. Capitalizing on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend the results of [17] and, by moving to an asymptotic regime, provide novel precise explicit expressions for the estimation error. The accuracy of their predictions is confirmed with many special cases (including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and the nuclear norm). The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit compressive sensing and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is very insightful.

Quality: This is a strong piece of work. The technical analysis appears sound, and the results are presented with a good degree of rigor. The authors effectively build upon existing literature to provide a precise characterization of the performance of the Generalized Lasso in a challenging setting. 

Clarity: The paper is well-written, though the technical details are dense and require careful attention. The presentation is largely clear, but the mathematical notation could be slightly more consistent. The lack of space, however, prevents including all necessary details.

Originality: The paper builds upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] and expands upon it in several novel aspects. The explicit expressions for the estimation error are particularly noteworthy.  The connection to optimal quantizer design is also a valuable contribution.

Comments:
1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized versions of the LASSO are equivalent with appropriate correspondence.
2) Caption of Figure 1: ‘n=768’ should be ‘$n$=768’.
3) Page 5, line 247: There is a typographical error at “When…”.
4) Page 5, line 294: The function the authors call the proximal function is, in fact, well-known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope with index τ. The notation used there is awkward as “prox” is generally used for the proximal point/operator but not for the Moreau envelope. I suggest changing the notation.
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: It would be instructive to give this assumption a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis.
6) The authors should avoid referring to the generalized Lasso as an “algorithm” throughout the text. It is a (convex) program or decoder (to borrow terminology widely used in compressed sensing), but not an algorithm.
7) The experimental results effectively demonstrate the accuracy of the theoretical predictions. Showing the performance relative to other relevant methods could further strengthen the conclusions.

Overall Assessment: This paper presents a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements. The results are technically sound, clearly presented, and demonstrate a strong understanding of the relevant literature.

Arguments for acceptance:
*   Novel and precise theoretical results.
*   Clear connection to practical problems like q-bit compressive sensing.
*   Strong technical analysis and rigorous proofs.
*   Well-written and organized presentation.

Arguments against acceptance:
*   The mathematical density may make the paper challenging for a broad audience.
*   The lack of extensive numerical comparisons to other methods. 

Recommendation: Strongly Accept. This paper is of high quality and makes a valuable contribution to the field.
