- Contribution: Precisely characterizes Generalized LASSO error with nonlinear measurements and shows asymptotic equivalence to a linear model with scaled, noisy observations.  
- Contribution: Provides explicit error expressions and links the nonlinear case to existing linear estimation results.  
- Contribution: Identifies the Lloyd–Max quantizer as optimal for minimizing estimation error, connecting to quantization theory.  
- Novelty: Addresses a challenging and relevant problem with significant theoretical advancement.  
- Technical soundness: Mathematical analysis is rigorous with impressive explicit derivations.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and notation/ideas are clearly presented.  
- Significance: Results have practical implications (e.g., compressive sensing with low-precision ADCs) and cover multiple special cases (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).  
- Thoroughness: Explores concrete examples and illustrations across several recovery settings.  
- Weakness: Limited engagement with single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature; more related-work comparison needed.  
- Weakness: Typographical errors in proofs that should be corrected before publication.  
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (convergence related to the regularizer) appears restrictive and lacks intuitive justification or conditions for validity.  
- Weakness: Practical computational cost of implementing the Lloyd–Max quantizer is not discussed.  
- For acceptance: Novel, technically sound, clearly presented work with important theoretical and practical implications.  
- Against acceptance: Incomplete literature coverage, typographical issues, and potentially restrictive assumptions.  
- Overall: Strong recommendation to accept, contingent on fixing typos and better justifying Assumption 1.
