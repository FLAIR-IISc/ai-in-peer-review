This paper considers the problem of linear estimation in the presence of a nonlinear link function. Building upon the recent work of Plan and Vershynin, the authors study the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized Lasso for an arbitrary link function, provided that the parameter μ is non-zero. The central result, presented in Theorem 2.1, is that the asymptotic error is identical for both linear and nonlinear link functions. This is a well-written paper with interesting results.

However, there are some aspects that could be improved:

A potential limitation is the assumption in Theorem 2.1 that the measurement vectors are sampled from a Gaussian distribution. This may be overly restrictive in some applications, and the authors should clarify the necessity of this assumption for their conclusions to hold.

I also have a few questions for the authors:
1.  How should the regularizer be chosen in practice, based on the set Κ that contains the true signal x₀? While the paper assumes a convex regularizer, it does not provide guidance on this selection process.
2.  The paper's conclusion establishes an equivalence between the linear and nonlinear settings. However, the resulting error bounds depend on μ and σ, which are parameters characterizing the nonlinearity of the link function g. Could the authors comment on the necessity of this dependence, given the claimed equivalence?

The paper would also be strengthened by a discussion of related work in the following areas:
1.  **Single Index Models (SIM) and Sufficient Dimension Reduction (SDR):** This body of literature is highly relevant as it addresses the estimation of linear vectors or subspaces under unknown link functions. The current draft does not appear to engage with this research area.
2.  **A recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear observation,"** introduces an algorithm for sparse recovery that applies even when μ = 0. That work also establishes the equivalence between high-dimensional estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under certain conditions, supporting its conclusions with careful theoretical proofs and empirical results.
