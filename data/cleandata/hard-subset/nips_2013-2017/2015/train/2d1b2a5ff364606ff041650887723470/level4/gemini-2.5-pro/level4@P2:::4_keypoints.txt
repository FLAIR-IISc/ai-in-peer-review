- Main contribution: Precise asymptotic characterization showing LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to an effective linear model yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i in the high-dimensional limit.  
- Key parameters: The non-linearity g is fully summarized by μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2], capturing its entire impact on LASSO error.  
- Theorems: Theorem 2.1 establishes the equivalence; Theorem 2.2 gives a computable closed-form for the asymptotic mean-squared error.  
- Specializations: Results are specialized to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery, demonstrating broad applicability.  
- Application: Shows Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for minimizing LASSO error in quantized compressive sensing.  
- Significance & originality: Provides an elegant, exact asymptotic refinement of prior non-asymptotic bounds (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin).  
- Technical quality: Analysis appears sound and builds on recent precise high-dimensional estimator techniques; results are practically computable.  
- Clarity: Well-written, organized, and supported by simulations that match theory closely.  
- Impact: Likely influential by reducing complex non-linear problems to a linear principle useful for analysis and design.  
- Limitation: Analysis assumes A has i.i.d. Gaussian entries; extension to other ensembles (e.g., sub-Gaussian) is not addressed.  
- Missing related work: Does not discuss Yi et al. (2015), which handles cases with μ = 0 and is relevant for comparison.  
- Presentation suggestions: Should emphasize the single-index model connection more and provide intuitive explanation for why μ and σ suffice to capture g in the Gaussian high-dimensional regime.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong, novel, and technically solid paper; recommended for acceptance.
