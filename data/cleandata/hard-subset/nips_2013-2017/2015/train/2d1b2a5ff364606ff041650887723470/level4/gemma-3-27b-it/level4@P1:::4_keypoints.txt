- Shows that for generalized LASSO with nonlinear link functions, the asymptotic estimation error matches the linear-measurement case (Theorem 2.1).
- Extends Brillinger’s ideas to high-dimensional, regularized estimation and provides explicit asymptotic error expressions.
- Identifies the Lloyd–Max quantizer as optimal for minimizing estimation error in quantized measurement settings.
- Novel contribution: precise error characterization for generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements.
- Results cover a broad class of link functions and regularizers.
- Theoretical derivations and proofs are rigorous and clear.
- Paper is well-organized and readable despite mathematical complexity.
- Reliance on Gaussian measurement vectors may be restrictive; sensitivity to non-Gaussianity is not analyzed.
- Lacks practical guidance on selecting regularizers tailored to the underlying signal x0.
- The error characterization depends on μ and σ of the link function; unclear if or when these dependencies can be reduced or avoided.
- Related work coverage is incomplete, notably missing connections to single-index models, sufficient dimension reduction, and Yi et al. (2015).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, explicit formulas, practical quantizer insight, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: Gaussian assumption limits, missing regularizer guidance, μ/σ dependence, incomplete literature review.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address Gaussian assumption, regularizer selection discussion, and expanded related work (e.g., Yi et al.).
