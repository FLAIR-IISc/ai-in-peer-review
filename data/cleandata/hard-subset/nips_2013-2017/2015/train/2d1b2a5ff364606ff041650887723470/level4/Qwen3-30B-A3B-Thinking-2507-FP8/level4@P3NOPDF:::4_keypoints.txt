- Core contribution: Theorem 2.1 shows asymptotic estimation error for generalized Lasso is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions when measurement vectors are Gaussian.  
- Theoretical significance: Demonstrates that nonlinearity need not increase asymptotic estimation difficulty under the specified conditions.  
- Strength: Clear theoretical derivation, sound technical arguments, and well-structured presentation.  
- Major concern 1 — Gaussianity: The Gaussian measurement-vector assumption is restrictive and needs explicit justification or extension to weaker distributions.  
- Major concern 2 — Regularizer choice: The paper does not explain how to choose the convex regularizer given the unknown set K or how that choice affects asymptotic error.  
- Major concern 3 — Role of μ and σ: The dependence of the error bound on link parameters μ and σ is not explained; it’s unclear if these parameters are unavoidable or avoidable under other assumptions.  
- Related work omission: Fails to cite foundational literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction.  
- Critical missing citation: Neglects Yi et al. (NIPS 2014) “Optimal Linear Estimation under Unknown Link Functions,” which addresses similar equivalence results and empirical validation.  
- Required revisions: Justify Gaussian assumption, detail regularizer selection and its impact, analyze the role of μ/σ, and engage with the related literature (especially Yi et al.).  
- Overall: Core insight is compelling but scope, practical relevance, and literature context must be clarified to meet conference standards.
