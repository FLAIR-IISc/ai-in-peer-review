- Paper studies generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design, models non-linear observations as linear + noise, extends Plan & Vershynin, and derives asymptotic closed-form estimation error expressions validated on Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm cases; includes insightful application to optimal q-bit quantizer design and connection to Lloyd–Max.

- Quality: strong piece of work.

- Clarity: generally well written but uneven in emphasis; space limits omit some necessary details.

- Originality: advances prior work by providing new explicit formulas for estimation error.

- Comment: regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence (p.4, l.193).

- Comment: Figure 1 caption typo: 'n=768' should be '$n$=768'.

- Comment: Typo on page 5, line 247 in 'When ...'.

- Comment: What authors call the "proximal function" is the Moreau envelope with index τ; notation using prox is misleading and should be changed.

- Comment: Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal interpretation using variational analysis results.

- Comment: Avoid calling the generalized Lasso an algorithm; it is a convex program (or decoder).

- Experiments: Empirical results corroborate theoretical predictions that generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements asymptotically matches performance of a linear model with Gaussian noise, including implications for optimal quantizer design.
