- Summary: Analyzes generalized Lasso for nonlinear measurements with Gaussian design, extending Plan & Vershynin to give precise asymptotic estimation-error formulas, validated on Lasso/group Lasso/nuclear norm and applied to q-bit compressive sensing with a Lloyd–Max connection.

- Quality: High-quality paper making a significant contribution.

- Clarity: Well-written but some topics are unevenly treated and space constraints omit details.

- Originality: Builds on prior work but contributes novel, explicit expressions for estimation error.

- Comment 1: Note that the regularized and constrained (penalized) formulations are equivalent under an appropriate parameter correspondence.

- Comment 2: Figure 1 caption should typeset n=768 properly (use math mode).

- Comment 3: Typo at page 5, line 247 in the line beginning "When...".

- Comment 4: The "proximal function" is actually the Moreau envelope of index τ; using prox notation is misleading—change notation to avoid confusion with the proximal operator.

- Comment 5: Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal interpretation, e.g., via variational analysis results.

- Comment 6: Do not call "generalized Lasso" an algorithm; describe it as a convex program or decoder instead.

- Comment 7: Experiments effectively demonstrate the method on nonlinear measurements and validate the theoretical error predictions.
