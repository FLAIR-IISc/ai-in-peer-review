

This paper extends the precise asymptotic analysis of the Generalized LASSO to the important case of non-linear measurements, establishing a rigorous connection between estimation performance with non-linear measurements and an appropriately scaled linear measurement model. The authors prove that when the measurement matrix has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = gi(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to the error of the same algorithm applied to linear measurements yi = μai^T x0 + σzi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1). This generalizes previous work by Brillinger (1982) and Plan and Vershynin (2015), who established qualitative equivalence but without precise error characterization.

The main contribution is Theorem 2.1, which establishes the asymptotic equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models, and Theorem 2.2, which provides a precise expression for the estimation error as the solution to a convex optimization problem. The authors further specialize these results to important cases: sparse recovery (Theorem 2.3), group-sparse recovery, and low-rank matrix recovery. The paper also demonstrates a practical application to q-bit compressive sensing, showing that the optimal quantizer minimizing estimation error is the celebrated Lloyd-Max quantizer.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs and careful analysis. The authors establish precise asymptotic characterizations that hold for a wide class of regularizers and link functions. The theoretical development is thorough, and the authors appropriately acknowledge limitations, such as the assumption that the signal has unit norm (which excludes certain link functions). The asymptotic analysis is comprehensive, extending the previous work of Plan and Vershynin (2015) from upper bounds to exact error characterization.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a logical progression from general results to specific applications. The technical content is presented accessibly, though the connection between non-linear and linear measurement models could be more clearly motivated in the main text rather than being confined to Appendix A. The authors provide detailed explanations of the key results and their implications, making the paper accessible to experts in the field.

Originality: This work makes a significant contribution by providing the first precise asymptotic characterization of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. While Plan and Vershynin (2015) established the qualitative equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models, they only provided upper bounds with unknown constants. This paper delivers precise error expressions, which is novel and valuable for both theoretical understanding and practical applications. The application to q-bit compressive sensing, showing that the Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal, represents an important practical contribution.

Significance: The results have substantial implications for applications involving non-linear measurements, such as 1-bit compressed sensing, quantized measurements, and other measurement scenarios with nonlinearities. The precise error characterization enables better understanding of how the link function, regularization parameter, and signal structure affect estimation performance. The finding that the Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal provides a clear design guideline for practical systems, which is of direct interest to practitioners.

Strengths:
- First precise characterization of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements
- Rigorous proof of equivalence between non-linear and scaled linear measurement models
- Comprehensive analysis across multiple signal structures (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank)
- Practical application to q-bit compressive sensing with optimal quantizer result
- Clear connection between theoretical analysis and practical implications

Weaknesses:
- The connection between non-linear and linear measurements could be better motivated in the main text (currently primarily in Appendix A)
- The focus on the l2-LASSO (square-root LASSO) rather than the more common l2^2-LASSO should be more clearly justified, particularly regarding the practical implications of the "oracle" property of the regularization parameter
- The unit norm signal assumption restricts applicability to certain link functions and should be more thoroughly discussed

Overall, this is an excellent theoretical contribution that significantly advances our understanding of LASSO-type estimators in the presence of non-linear measurements. The precise error characterization is novel and valuable, and the practical application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates tangible significance. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a substantial contribution to the literature. With minor improvements in the presentation of the key equivalence result, this would be an outstanding contribution to NIPS.
