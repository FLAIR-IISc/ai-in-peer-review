- Paper studies generalized lasso for linear estimation with nonlinear link functions and claims asymptotic estimation error is identical for linear and nonlinear links (Theorem 2.1).
- Theorem 2.1 relies on Gaussian measurement vectors; this assumption may be too restrictive and needs justification for necessity.
- The paper does not explain how to choose the regularizer based on the constraint set κ where x0 lies, despite assuming convex regularizers.
- Although nonlinear measurements reduce to linear-equivalent results, the dependence on μ and σ (hardness parameters) is not discussed for necessity or interpretation.
- Important related literature is missing, notably work on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction addressing estimation under unknown link functions.
- The recent Yi et al. paper on optimal linear estimation/sparse recovery with zero μ and nonlinear links is not cited, despite bearing directly on the paper’s claims.
- Overall the manuscript is well written with interesting results, but addressing assumptions, regularizer selection, necessity of μ/σ dependencies, and adding relevant references would strengthen it.
