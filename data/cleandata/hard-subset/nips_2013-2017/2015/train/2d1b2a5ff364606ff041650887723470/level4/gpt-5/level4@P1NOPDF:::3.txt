Summary: This paper studies the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design. A common heuristic is to treat non-linear observations as noisy linear ones. Building on Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend those results and, in an asymptotic regime, derive precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error. Their predictions are validated across several important special cases (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The application to optimal quantizer design for q-bit compressed sensing (CS) and its relation to the Lloyd–Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality: This is a strong piece of work.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written, though the exposition feels somewhat unbalanced; space limitations likely prevented the inclusion of some necessary details.

Originality: While grounded in Plan and Vershynin [17], the paper extends their framework in novel ways. In particular, the explicit expressions for the estimation error are new.

Comments:
1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized (constrained) and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence between parameters.

2) Caption of Figure 1: “n=768” should be typeset as “$n$=768”.

3) Page 5, line 247: There is a typo in “When …”.

4) Page 5, line 294: What the authors call the “proximal function” is known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope with parameter τ. The notation is awkward, since “prox” is standard for the proximal operator, not the envelope. I suggest changing the notation accordingly.

5) Page 5, Assumption 1: It would be instructive to provide a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis.

6) Throughout: Please avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm.” It is a (convex) program (or “decoder,” in compressed sensing terminology), not an algorithm.

7) Experiments: The authors report results for applying the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and show that their theorems accurately predict the estimation error. The paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, and proves that asymptotically its performance matches that of the (corresponding) linear Gaussian-noise model. The authors provide novel, precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error, which are confirmed in multiple special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm). The application to designing optimal quantizers in q-bit CS, and its connection to the Lloyd–Max quantizer, is very insightful.
