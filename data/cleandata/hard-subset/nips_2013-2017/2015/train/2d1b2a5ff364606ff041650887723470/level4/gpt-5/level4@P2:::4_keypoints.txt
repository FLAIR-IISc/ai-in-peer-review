- Paper studies recovery from nonlinear single-index observations yi = gi(a_i^T x0) using generalized LASSO and shows asymptotic equivalence to a linear model with effective parameters μ and σ.

- Theorem 2.1 formalizes the equivalence; Theorem 2.2 gives a scalar optimization for the asymptotic squared error; specializations and simulations for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank cases validate the theory.

- Application to q-bit compressed sensing: minimizing LASSO error reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, implying Lloyd–Max quantization is optimal.

- Strength: Conceptually clean and practically useful reduction—g affects performance only through (μ,σ).

- Strength: Provides precise, asymptotically exact error characterizations (not just bounds), extending CGMT analyses to nonlinear links.

- Strength: Broad applicability across convex regularizers and structures, with helpful specializations.

- Strength: Quantizer-design consequence is elegant and actionable.

- Strength: Generally clear writing, good comparisons to prior work, and informative figures.

- Weakness: Main results assume i.i.d. Gaussian design; unclear if rotational invariance is essential or extendable to subgaussian or structured ensembles.

- Weakness: Dependence on μ and σ — paper lacks guidance on estimating these from data when g is unknown and on sensitivity to estimation error.

- Weakness: Limited practical guidance mapping structural assumptions to choice of regularizer f and on selecting λ using Theorem 2.2.

- Weakness: Assumption 1 (existence of the envelope-conjugate limit F) is verified for some norms but needs more intuition and general conditions for other regularizers.

- Weakness: Excludes the important μ = 0 regime; should discuss connections to work that handles μ = 0 (e.g., Yi et al. 2015).

- Weakness: Theorem 2.1 phrasing “with probability one” should clarify it means almost-sure convergence of asymptotic limits, not finite-n pathwise equality.

- Quality: Theoretical development appears careful, specializations convincing, and empirical results align with predictions.

- Reproducibility note: Add details on numerical solution of the scalar programs (initialization, convexity checks, runtime).

- Clarity: Fix typos and notation inconsistencies; collect definitions of μ, σ, δ, and F in one place.

- Originality: First asymptotically exact error characterization for generalized LASSO under nonlinear single-index measurements; novel quantizer corollary.

- Significance: Results give interpretable prescriptions for performance prediction, regularization tuning, and measurement design; broadly relevant.

- Literature: Recommend more explicit situating within single-index and sufficient-dimension-reduction literature and discussion of Yi et al. (2015).

- Pros for acceptance: Strong theory, clear conceptual reduction, practical impact on quantizer design and tuning, empirical validation.

- Cons for acceptance: Heavy reliance on Gaussian designs, limited treatment of μ estimation and robustness, limited practical guidance on regularizer and λ selection.
