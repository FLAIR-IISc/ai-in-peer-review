The article relaxes the assumption of a normal Gaussian distribution on the prior (x_0). The article is well-written. However,
1. As Brillinger (1982) noted, the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with scaled unknown signals and non-standard noise. This connection is strictly described in Appendix A, but it should also be mentioned in the main article to intuitively support the statement in blockquote on line 140.
2. The article focuses on l2-Lasso, also known as square-root-Lasso. The main advantage of l2-Lasso over the popular l2^2-Lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter (usually unknown) does not depend on the noise variance (sigma). A practical question that may clarify the results is whether this justification of l2-Lasso over l2^2-Lasso is valid.
