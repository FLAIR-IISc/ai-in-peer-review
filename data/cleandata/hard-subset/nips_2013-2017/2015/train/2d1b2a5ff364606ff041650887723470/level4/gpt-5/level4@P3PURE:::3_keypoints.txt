- Paper studies Generalized Lasso under nonlinear measurements with Gaussian design and models nonlinear observations as noisy linear ones.  
- Builds on Plan and Vershynin [17] and derives novel explicit asymptotic expressions for estimation error.  
- Predictions validated empirically for special cases: Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization.  
- Application to designing optimal q-bit quantizers in compressed sensing and connection to the Lloyd–Max quantizer is insightful.  
- Overall quality: strong piece of work.  
- Clarity: generally well written but unbalanced; space limits omit some necessary details.  
- Originality: extends prior work with genuinely new asymptotic error expressions.  
- Comment: regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence (p.4, l.193).  
- Comment: Figure 1 caption formatting should read “n=768” as “$n$=768”.  
- Comment: Typo on p.5, l.247.  
- Comment: The paper’s “proximal function” is the Moreau envelope; “prox” usually denotes the proximal operator—suggest changing notation.  
- Comment: Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal interpretation using variational analysis.  
- Comment: Avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; it is a convex program or “decoder.”  
- Experiments: theorems accurately predict estimation error and show asymptotic equivalence to a linear model with Gaussian noise; quantizer application confirmed.
