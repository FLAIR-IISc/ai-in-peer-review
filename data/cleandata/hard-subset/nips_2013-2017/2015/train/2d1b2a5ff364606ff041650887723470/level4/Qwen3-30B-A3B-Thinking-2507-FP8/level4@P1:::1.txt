

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO when applied to non-linear measurements of the form $y_i = g_i(a_i^T x_0)$, where $g_i$ is a possibly unknown nonlinear link function. The authors establish a remarkable equivalence between this non-linear setting and a linear measurement model with scaled and noisy measurements, showing that the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically identical to that of a linear model with measurements $y_i = \mu a_i^T x_0 + \sigma z_i$, where $\mu = \mathbb{E}[\gamma g(\gamma)]$ and $\sigma^2 = \mathbb{E}[(g(\gamma) - \mu \gamma)^2]$ for $\gamma \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$. This extends prior work by Brillinger (1980s) and Plan & Vershynin (2013), who established consistency results but without precise error characterization.

The paper makes several significant contributions. First, it provides precise asymptotic error expressions for the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements, which to the best of our knowledge constitute the first known results of this kind. Second, it demonstrates that the error behavior depends on the link function $g$ only through the parameters $\mu$ and $\sigma$, which is a powerful simplification. Third, it derives a novel application to optimal quantizer design, showing that the Lloyd-Max quantizer minimizes the estimation error for the Generalized LASSO.

The technical quality of the paper is excellent. The authors establish their main results (Theorems 2.1 and 2.2) under a comprehensive set of assumptions that are well-justified and consistent with the high-dimensional statistics literature. The proofs leverage recent advances in the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem framework and demonstrate careful mathematical analysis. The paper also provides precise error formulas for specific structured recovery problems (sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank), extending previous results in these domains.

The clarity of the paper is generally good, though some sections could be improved. The introduction effectively sets up the problem and relates it to prior work. The main results are well-motivated and presented with sufficient mathematical rigor. However, the proof sections contain several typos and minor notational inconsistencies that detract from the otherwise strong presentation. The authors could benefit from a careful proofreading pass to eliminate these errors.

The originality of the work is strong. While Plan & Vershynin (2015) established bounds for the constrained Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, this paper provides the first precise characterization of the error, which is a significant advance. The connection between non-linear measurements and linear ones with scaled noise is both elegant and practically useful, as it allows leveraging existing analysis for linear measurements. The application to optimal quantizer design (Section 2.3) further demonstrates the paper's significance.

The significance of the results is substantial. The precise error characterization provides deeper theoretical understanding of the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements, which is relevant to numerous applications including 1-bit quantization, censored observations, and measurement devices with nonlinearities. The paper also provides practical insights into optimal quantizer design, showing that the Lloyd-Max quantizer minimizes estimation error for the Generalized LASSO—a result with immediate implications for hardware design in compressed sensing systems.

The paper's main limitation is that it assumes the measurement matrix has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, which is a standard assumption in high-dimensional statistics but may not hold in all practical settings. However, the authors clearly acknowledge this limitation and note that their results could potentially be extended to other measurement ensembles.

In terms of the four NIPS criteria:
- Quality: The paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs and well-supported claims.
- Clarity: The paper is well-written overall, though proof sections contain typos that need correction.
- Originality: The precise error characterization under non-linear measurements is novel.
- Significance: The results have both theoretical importance and practical implications for measurement design.

The paper makes a strong contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics and structured signal recovery. The results extend a line of important work by Plan & Vershynin, while providing the first precise error characterization for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. The connection to optimal quantizer design is particularly compelling and demonstrates the practical relevance of the theoretical results.

I recommend acceptance. The paper addresses a meaningful problem with a technically sound approach that provides both theoretical depth and practical insights. The only minor issue is the typos in the proof sections, which are easily fixable and do not detract from the paper's overall quality and contribution.
