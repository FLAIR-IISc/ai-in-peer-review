The paper "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements" presents an interesting and well-written analysis of the generalized square-root LASSO applied to Gaussian measurements that are optionally transformed by a possibly unknown nonlinear function. The authors derive asymptotic guarantees for the performance of the method, providing a precise characterization of its behavior.

The main contribution of the paper is the proof that, under certain conditions, the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is equivalent to one with linear measurements. This result is established through a series of theorems and lemmas, which provide a rigorous and detailed analysis of the problem. The authors also present several examples and simulations to illustrate the accuracy of their predictions.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the main concepts and techniques used. The writing style is concise and precise, making it easy to understand the authors' arguments and results.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for analyzing the performance of the generalized LASSO under different types of measurements. The authors' use of the single-index model and the sufficient dimension reduction literature provides a rich and nuanced understanding of the problem, and their results have important implications for a wide range of applications.
