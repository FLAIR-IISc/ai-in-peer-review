This paper discusses linear estimation with a nonlinear link function. Inspired by the recent work of Plan and Varshinin, it studies the asymptotic estimation error using generalized Lasso for arbitrary link functions with \mu \ne 0. It is shown that the asymptotic error is exactly equal to the error shown in Theorem 2.1, regardless of whether the link function is linear or nonlinear.

Theorem 2.1 will apply when the linear estimation vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which can be strict in some cases. The author may need to explain why this assumption is necessary for this conclusion.

A few questions: 1. How to choose the regularizer based on the set \kai where x_0 is located? Although this paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, it seems that the previously mentioned problem has not yet been solved. 2. Although the result shows that the nonlinear estimation is equal to the linear estimation, the conclusion involves \mu and \sigma, which describe the difficulty of estimating from the nonlinear function g. I would like to know if the authors have any idea about the necessity of these dependencies.

Some references are missing. 1. Research on linear vector/ subspace estimation under unknown link functions with single index models and inadequate dimension reduction. However, this paper does not review any research papers in this field. 2. A recent paper by E and others, "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," introduces new algorithms for sparse recovery even with link functions involving zero \mu. The paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under appropriate conditions. The conclusion is supported by careful theoretical evidence and empirical results. It is a well-written paper with quite interesting results.
