- Studies Generalized Lasso under Gaussian design with non-linear measurements, extending Plan & Vershynin’s framework.  
- Derives precise asymptotic, explicit expressions for estimation error.  
- Validates predictions empirically on special cases: Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization.  
- Applies theory to design optimal q-bit compressed sensing quantizers and connects to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original contribution; generally well-written but uneven depth (space-limited).  
- Correction: regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate scaling of the regularization parameter.  
- Minor fixes: consistent mathematical notation in Figure 1 caption (n=768) and a typographical error on page 5, line 247.  
- Terminology: the “proximal function” should be called the Moreau envelope (index τ); reserve ‘prox’ for the proximal operator.  
- Suggestion: provide a formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using variational analysis results.  
- Terminology: avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; describe it as a convex optimization program or decoder.  
- Experiments substantiate that asymptotically the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements behaves like a linear Gaussian noise model and support the new error formulas.
