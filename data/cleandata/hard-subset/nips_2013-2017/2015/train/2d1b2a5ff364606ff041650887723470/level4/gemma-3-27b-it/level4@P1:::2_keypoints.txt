- Extends precise asymptotic characterization of the l2-lasso to single-index (non-linear) measurement models.  
- Worthwhile and novel contribution to literature on precise behavior of LASSO-type estimators.  
- Well-written and clearly presented with rigorous derivations and supportive figures.  
- Derivation of explicit, asymptotically exact expressions for estimation error is a significant technical achievement.  
- Appendix A rigorously connects single-index models to linear models with scaled signal and non-standard noise; this heuristic should be briefly mentioned in the main text.  
- The paper studies the l2-lasso (square-root-lasso); it should discuss whether results support the practical advantage that its optimal regularization is independent of unknown noise variance.  
- Reliance on Gaussian measurement matrix entries is a potential limitation and the sensitivity to non-Gaussian designs should be briefly discussed.  
- Discussion of applications (particularly Section 2.3) is thin and could be expanded to illustrate practical impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension to non-linear measurements, precise error expressions, clear organization, and empirical agreement.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Gaussian assumption may limit applicability and practical implications are under-discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—minor weaknesses do not undermine substantial theoretical contributions.
