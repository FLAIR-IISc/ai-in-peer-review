

The article extends the precise characterization of the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models while relaxing the usual Gaussian prior assumption on the signal distribution. This constitutes a valuable contribution to the literature on the asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The paper is well-written. However, the following points warrant attention:

1.  As established by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously developed in Appendix A, it should also be explicitly stated in the main text to heuristically justify the claim in the blockquote on line 140.

2.  The article focuses on the l2-lasso (also known as the square root lasso). A key theoretical advantage of the l2-lasso over the standard l2²-lasso is that its oracle regularization parameter is independent of the (typically unknown) noise variance σ. The paper should explicitly address whether this theoretical justification for preferring the l2-lasso over the l2²-lasso holds in practice, as this is a critical consideration for applied users.
