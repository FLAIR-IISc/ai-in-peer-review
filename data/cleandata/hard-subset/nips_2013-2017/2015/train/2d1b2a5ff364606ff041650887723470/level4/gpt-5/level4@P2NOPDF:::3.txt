Summary: This paper studies the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design. Building on Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors move to an asymptotic regime and derive precise, explicit formulas for the estimation error. Their predictions are validated across several canonical instances (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm regularization). The application to designing optimal quantizers for q-bit compressed sensing and its connection to the Lloyd–Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality: This is a strong and well-executed contribution.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, though somewhat unbalanced; space constraints appear to preclude inclusion of some important details.

Originality: While the work builds on Plan and Vershynin [17], it advances the state of the art in several meaningful ways. In particular, the explicit asymptotic error formulas are new and valuable.

Comments:
1) Page 4, line 193: Please clarify the equivalence between the penalized and constrained (regularized) formulations, and specify the correspondence between the penalty parameter and the constraint radius.

2) Figure 1 caption: “n=768” should be typeset as “n = 768” (or in math mode as “n = 768”).

3) Page 5, line 247: There is a typo beginning with “When …”; please correct.

4) Page 5, line 294: What is called the “proximal function” here is, in convex analysis, the Moreau envelope with parameter τ. The notation “prox” is typically reserved for the proximal operator, not the envelope. I recommend renaming the function accordingly to avoid confusion.

5) Page 5, Assumption 1: It would be helpful to give a more formal interpretation using tools from variational analysis (e.g., subdifferentials, tangent/normal cones, or regularity conditions). This would clarify when the assumption holds and how it can be verified.

6) Please avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm.” It is a convex optimization program (or “decoder,” in the terminology common in compressed sensing), not an algorithm per se.

7) Experiments: The results show that applying the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements yields asymptotic performance matching that of a linear Gaussian-noise model, in line with the theory. The explicit error predictions closely track empirical performance across the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm settings. The quantizer design for q-bit compressed sensing and its relationship to the Lloyd–Max quantizer are well-motivated and illuminating.
