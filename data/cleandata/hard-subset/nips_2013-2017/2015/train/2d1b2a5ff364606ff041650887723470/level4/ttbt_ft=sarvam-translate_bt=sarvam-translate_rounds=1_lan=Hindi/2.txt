This article extends the work of accurately representing the statistical performance of l2-lasso to a single-index model. It also relaxes the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior (x_0's distribution). This is a significant contribution to the literature on the exact asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The article is well-written. However,
1. As noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with scaled unknown signals and non-standard noise. The appendix A describes the connection rigorously, but it should also be mentioned in the main article to empirically justify the statement given in the blockquote at line 140.
2. The article focuses on l2-lasso, also known as square-lasso. The main advantage of l2-lasso over the popular l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter (usually unknown) does not depend on the noise variance (sigma). A practical question that the results may shed light on is whether this justification of l2-lasso over l2^2-lasso is valid.
