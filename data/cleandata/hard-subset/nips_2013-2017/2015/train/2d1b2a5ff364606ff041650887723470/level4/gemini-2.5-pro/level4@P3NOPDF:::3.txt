**Summary**

This paper analyzes the performance of the Generalized Lasso for non-linear measurement models with a Gaussian design matrix. It builds upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], which established that non-linear observations can often be treated as linear observations with additive noise. The primary contribution of this work is to formalize and extend this heuristic by moving to an asymptotic regime. In this setting, the authors derive novel, precise, and explicit expressions for the estimation error. The theoretical predictions are shown to be highly accurate across several important special cases, including the standard Lasso, the group Lasso, and nuclear norm minimization. A particularly insightful application is presented for designing optimal quantizers in q-bit compressed sensing, drawing a connection to the classic Lloyd-Max quantizer.

**Overall Assessment and Comments**

This is a strong and well-executed piece of theoretical work. While it builds directly on the foundational results of [17], the extension to an asymptotic analysis and the derivation of explicit error expressions represent a significant and novel contribution. The results provide valuable, precise insights into a widely used heuristic, and the application to quantizer design is both elegant and practical.

The paper is generally well-written and clear. However, the presentation feels somewhat unbalanced, likely due to space constraints, with some theoretical aspects deserving more detailed exposition. The experimental section effectively validates the theoretical claims by demonstrating the close match between the predicted and observed estimation errors.

Below are detailed comments for improving the manuscript.

**Major Comments**

1.  **Terminology for Moreau Envelope:** On page 5, line 294, the function referred to as the "proximal function" is well-known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope (of index τ). The `prox` notation is almost universally reserved for the proximal operator itself, not its envelope. This choice of notation is unconventional and could be confusing to readers familiar with convex optimization literature. I strongly recommend adopting the standard terminology and notation for the Moreau envelope to improve clarity and align with established conventions.

2.  **Interpretation of Assumption 1:** On page 5, Assumption 1 is central to the analysis. It would be instructive for the reader if the authors could provide a more formal interpretation of this assumption, perhaps by connecting it to established concepts from variational analysis. This would add theoretical depth and help readers better understand its implications.

3.  **Program vs. Algorithm:** Throughout the text, the Generalized Lasso is occasionally referred to as an "algorithm." It is more accurately described as a convex program, a decoder, or an estimator. An algorithm would be a specific method used to *solve* this program (e.g., ADMM, FISTA). Please revise the text for consistency and precision on this point.

**Minor Comments and Typos**

1.  **Page 4, line 193:** When discussing the regularized and penalized versions of the problem, it would be helpful to briefly state that these formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence of their parameters.

2.  **Caption of Figure 1:** The variable 'n=768' should be formatted as a mathematical expression, i.e., '$n=768$'.

3.  **Page 5, line 247:** There appears to be a typo at the beginning of the sentence starting with "When...". Please proofread this line.
