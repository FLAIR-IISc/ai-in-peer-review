Summary: This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, building on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17]. By treating non-linear observations as noisy linear observations, the authors extend the results of [17] and provide novel, precise, and explicit expressions for error estimation in an asymptotic regime. The accuracy of their predictions is confirmed through various special cases, including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and the nuclear norm. Furthermore, the application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit Compressed Sensing (CS) and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.

Quality: This is a nice piece of work that demonstrates a clear understanding of the subject matter.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, although the treatment is somewhat unbalanced due to space constraints, which prevents the inclusion of all necessary details.

Originality: The paper expands on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] and introduces novel aspects, including explicit expressions for performance estimation error.

Comments:

1) Page 4, line 193: The authors correctly note that the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent with an appropriate correspondence.

2) Caption of Figure 1: The caption should be corrected to read '$n$=768' instead of 'n=768'.

3) Page 5, line 247: A typo is present in the sentence starting with 'When...'.

4) Page 5, line 294: The authors refer to the Moreau envelope of index \tau as the proximal function. While this is a well-known concept in convex analysis, the notation used is somewhat awkward, as 'prox' generally denotes the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope. It is suggested that the notation be revised for clarity.

5) Page 5, Assumption 1: Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption using results from variational analysis would be instructive.

6) To maintain consistency with standard terminology, the authors should avoid referring to the generalized Lasso as an algorithm in the text. Instead, it is more accurate to describe it as a convex program or decoder, as commonly used in Compressed Sensing.

7) Experiments: The authors demonstrate the application of the generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and show how the theorems predict the estimation error. This paper provides a rigorous analysis of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, proving that asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with such measurements is equivalent to that of linear and noisy (Gaussian) measurements. The authors provide novel, precise, and explicit expressions for the estimation error and confirm their findings through various special cases, including the Lasso, the group Lasso, and the nuclear norm. The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.
