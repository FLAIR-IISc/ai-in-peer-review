

Review Summary: This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian design. Building upon the heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as noisy linear measurements, the authors extend the results of Plan and Vershynin [17] by adopting an asymptotic framework, deriving novel precise explicit expressions for the estimation error. The accuracy of these predictions is validated across multiple special cases (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The application to designing optimal quantizers in q-Bit CS and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.  
Quality: A well-executed contribution.  
Clarity: The paper is well-written, though the treatment is somewhat unbalanced; the space constraints limit inclusion of all necessary details.  
Originality: The work builds upon Plan and Vershynin [17] and introduces novel extensions, notably the explicit expressions for the estimation error performance.  
Comments:  
1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under appropriate parameter correspondence.  
2) Caption of Figure 1: 'n=768' should be '$n$=768'.  
3) Page 5, line 247: Typo at 'When ...'.  
4) Page 5, line 294: The term 'proximal function' is non-standard; it is well-known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index $\tau$. The notation is awkward, as 'prox' typically denotes the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope. Revise accordingly.  
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: A more formal interpretation using variational analysis would be instructive.  
6) Avoid referring to the generalized Lasso as an 'algorithm'; it is a convex program (or decoder, per standard CS terminology).  
7) The experiments demonstrate the generalized Lasso applied to non-linear measurements, confirming the theorems' predictions for estimation error. The paper establishes that asymptotically, the estimation performance matches that of a linear noisy measurement model. The novel explicit error expressions are validated across key special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm), and the application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit CS (relating to Lloyd-Max quantizer) is insightful.
