- Paper studies the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design, approximating non-linear observations as noisy linear ones to derive precise asymptotic error formulas.  
- Authors provide novel, explicit performance-estimation expressions extending Plan & Vershynin into an asymptotic regime.  
- Predictions are validated empirically and analytically through special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm) and applied to q-Bit compressed sensing and Lloyd–Max quantizer design.  
- Overall work quality is high and makes a meaningful contribution.  
- Manuscript is generally clear but exhibits uneven treatment of topics, likely due to space limits.  
- Regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent when an appropriate correspondence is used.  
- Minor typesetting error in Figure 1 caption: 'n=768' should be typeset consistently (e.g., '$n$=768').  
- Typographical error on Page 5, line 247 at the start of the sentence beginning "When ...".  
- The term "proximal function" is imprecise: the authors likely mean the Moreau envelope of index τ; "prox" commonly denotes the proximal operator—recommend notation/terminology change.  
- Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal interpretation using variational analysis results.  
- Avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an "algorithm"; it is more precisely a convex program or, in CS terminology, a decoder.  
- Experiments demonstrate that asymptotically the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements matches performance under linear noisy Gaussian measurements, supported by the novel explicit error formulas.
