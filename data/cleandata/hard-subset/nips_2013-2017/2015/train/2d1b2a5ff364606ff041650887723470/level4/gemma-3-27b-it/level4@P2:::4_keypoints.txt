- Studies generalized LASSO for linear estimation with nonlinear link functions and non-zero proportionality µ.  
- Theorem 2.1: asymptotic estimation error is equivalent for nonlinear and scaled linear measurements.  
- Performance determined by link-function parameters µ and σ.  
- Theorem 2.2: explicit asymptotic error expressions linking link function, signal structure, regularizer, and regularization parameter.  
- Application to quantized compressive sensing; identifies Lloyd–Max quantizer as optimal.  
- Strength: novel theoretical equivalence simplifying analysis of nonlinear measurements.  
- Strength: technically rigorous derivations extending prior work to nonlinear settings.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing and mostly consistent notation.  
- Strength: practical relevance via quantization application and optimal quantizer identification.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on Gaussian measurement vectors; robustness to other distributions not addressed.  
- Weakness: limited guidance on choosing the regularizer f for specific signal structures.  
- Weakness: missing related work, notably the single-index/sufficient-dimension-reduction literature and Yi et al. (2015).  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion on implications of dependence on µ and σ for algorithm design/performance.  
- For acceptance: significant, sound contribution with clear presentation and practical applications.  
- Against acceptance: restricted generality due to Gaussian assumption, missing discussion on regularizer selection and related work, and incomplete treatment of µ/σ implications.  
- Overall: technically strong and recommend accept with minor revisions to add missing references, discuss Gaussian assumption limits, and provide brief regularizer guidance.
