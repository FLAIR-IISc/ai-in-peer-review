This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. A known heuristic often used is that non-linear observations may be treated as noisy linear observations. Capitalizing on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors here extend the results of [17], and by moving to an asymptotic regime, they provide novel precise explicit expressions of the error estimation. The accuracy of their predictions is confirmed with many special cases (including the Lasso, the group Lasso and the nuclear norm). The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is very insightful.

The paper is well-written and the results are clearly presented. The authors have made a significant effort to provide a thorough and detailed analysis of the problem, and their contributions are well-motivated and well-explained.

The quality of the paper is high, and the results are well-supported by theoretical analysis and numerical simulations. The authors have also done a good job of providing a clear and concise summary of the main contributions of the paper.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity. The authors have done an excellent job of explaining the problem, the approach, and the results in a clear and concise manner. The paper is well-organized, and the notation is consistent throughout.

The originality of the paper is also high. While the authors build upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], they have made significant contributions to the field by providing novel precise explicit expressions of the error estimation. The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is also new and insightful.

However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. On page 4, line 193, the authors mention that the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent with appropriate correspondence. This statement could be clarified further. Additionally, there are a few typos in the paper, such as on page 5, line 247, where the word "When" is misspelled.

Furthermore, the authors could benefit from providing more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using results from variational analysis. This would provide a deeper understanding of the assumption and its implications.

Finally, the authors should avoid calling the generalized Lasso an algorithm in the text. It is a convex program or decoder, but not an algorithm.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with significant contributions to the field. With a few minor revisions, it has the potential to be a high-impact paper.

Minor issues:

* Page 4, line 193: clarify the statement about the regularized and penalized versions being equivalent.
* Page 5, line 247: correct the typo.
* Provide a more formal interpretation of Assumption 1 using results from variational analysis.
* Avoid calling the generalized Lasso an algorithm in the text.
