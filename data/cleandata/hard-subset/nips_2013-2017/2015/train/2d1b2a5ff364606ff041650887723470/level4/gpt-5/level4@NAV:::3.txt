Review- Summary: This paper studies the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with a Gaussian design. A common heuristic treats such non-linear observations as linear ones corrupted by noise. Building on Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend those results and, by passing to an asymptotic regime, derive new, precise, closed-form expressions for the estimation error. Their predictions are validated across several notable instances (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear-norm regularization). The application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit CS and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is particularly insightful.
 Quality: This is a strong piece of work.
 Clarity: The paper is generally well written, though somewhat uneven in emphasis. Space limitations prevent inclusion of all necessary details.
 Originality: While grounded in Plan and Vershynin [17], the paper advances the topic in several novel ways; in particular, the explicit formulas for the estimation error are new.
 Comments:
 1) Page 4, line 193: the regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence.
 2) Caption of Figure 1: 'n=768' should be '$n$=768'.
 3) Page 5, line 247: there is a typo in 'When ...'.
 4) Page 5, line 294: what the authors call the proximal function is well known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope with index \tau. The notation is awkward, since prox typically denotes the proximal operator, not the Moreau envelope. I recommend changing it.
 5) Page 5, Assumption 1: It would be instructive to provide a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis.
 6) The authors should avoid referring to the generalized Lasso as an algorithm. It is a (convex) program (or a decoder, in CS terminology), not an algorithm.
 7) Experiments: The authors present results for applying the generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and compare them to the theoretical predictions for the estimation error. This work analyzes the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design and proves that, asymptotically, its estimation performance matches that of a linear model with Gaussian noise. The paper offers new, precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error and corroborates them across many special cases (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The discussion on optimal quantizer design for q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is very illuminating.
