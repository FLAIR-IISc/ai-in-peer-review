After reading the authors’ response and the other reviews, my overall assessment remains unchanged. I am not deeply familiar with the single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature, so I may be unaware of closely related prior work.

The paper derives asymptotic guarantees for the generalized square-root Lasso applied to Gaussian measurements that may be passed through an (optionally unknown) nonlinear transformation. The results are interesting and provide a sharp, precise characterization of the method’s performance.

Strengths:
- Tackles an important and timely problem setting.
- Offers clear, rigorous asymptotic guarantees.
- The exposition is generally strong and accessible.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- There are numerous typographical and minor errors, especially in the proof section. A thorough proofreading is needed.
- Given my limited familiarity with SIM/SDR, a more explicit discussion of how the results relate to key prior work in those areas (assumptions, rates, and regimes of applicability) would help readers situate the contribution.

Overall, I find the results compelling and the paper well written aside from the typos. With careful editing and a clearer positioning relative to the SIM/SDR literature, this work would make a valuable contribution.
