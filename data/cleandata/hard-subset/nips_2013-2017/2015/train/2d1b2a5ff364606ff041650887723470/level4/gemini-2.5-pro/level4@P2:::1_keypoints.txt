- Paper analyzes Generalized (square-root) LASSO for estimating structured x0 from non-linear measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) with i.i.d. Gaussian A and possibly unknown g.  
- Main result: asymptotically, LASSO on non-linear measurements is equivalent to LASSO on linear measurements y_lin = A(μx0) + σz, with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].  
- Provides exact deterministic formulas for asymptotic MSE ||x̂ - μx0||^2, improving prior work that gave only consistency or bounds.  
- Framework is general: applies to many convex regularizers (ℓ1, group LASSO, nuclear norm) and various structured signals.  
- Theoretical predictions are validated by numerical simulations showing strong agreement.  
- Practical application: simplifies q-bit quantizer design to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and links to Lloyd–Max quantization.  
- Significance: gives deep insight into why mismatched LASSO performs well under non-linear observations.  
- Originality and technical quality: first precise asymptotic characterization; theorems explicitly connect δ, signal structure, regularizer, λ, and nonlinearity.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized with a clear introduction and presentation of results.  
- Generality: results cover sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery and include novel special-case results.  
- Assumptions/limitations: analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian A and large-system asymptotics (m,n→∞ with fixed ratio), so finite-sample and non-Gaussian behavior is not addressed.  
- Minor issues: appendix/proofs contain typographical errors and would benefit from proofreading.  
- Related-work connection: could better situate results within the broader single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature.  
- Recommendation: strong accept; strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses.
