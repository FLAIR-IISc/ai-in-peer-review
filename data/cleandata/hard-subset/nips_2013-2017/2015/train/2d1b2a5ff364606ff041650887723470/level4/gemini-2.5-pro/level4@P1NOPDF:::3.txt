### **Review**

**Summary:**
This paper analyzes the performance of the Generalized Lasso for non-linear measurements with a Gaussian design. It builds on the common heuristic that non-linear observations can be treated as linear observations corrupted by noise. Extending the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors analyze this problem in an asymptotic regime, providing novel and precise expressions for the estimation error. The accuracy of these theoretical predictions is demonstrated for several important special cases, including the standard Lasso, the group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. A particularly insightful contribution is the application of this framework to the design of optimal quantizers for q-bit compressed sensing, drawing a clear connection to the classic Lloyd-Max quantizer.

**Quality:**
This is a high-quality paper that makes a solid theoretical contribution to the field.

**Clarity:**
The paper is generally well-written and clear. However, the presentation feels somewhat unbalanced, likely due to space constraints that prevent the inclusion of all necessary details.

**Originality:**
While the paper builds directly on the foundational work of Plan and Vershynin [17], it makes several novel contributions. Most notably, the derivation of precise, explicit expressions for the estimation error in the asymptotic regime is new and significant.

**Comments:**
1.  **Page 4, line 193:** It is worth noting that the regularized and penalized problem formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence of their parameters.

2.  **Caption of Figure 1:** The variable 'n=768' should be typeset in math mode as '$n$=768'.

3.  **Page 5, line 247:** There appears to be a typo in the sentence beginning "When ...".

4.  **Page 5, line 294:** The function referred to as the "proximal function" is well-known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope (of index τ). The `prox` notation is potentially confusing, as it is standardly used for the proximal operator, not the envelope. I would strongly suggest the authors adopt the standard terminology and notation to avoid ambiguity.

5.  **Page 5, Assumption 1:** It would be instructive for the reader if the authors provided a more formal interpretation of this assumption using concepts from variational analysis.

6.  **Terminology:** Throughout the text, the authors refer to the generalized Lasso as an "algorithm." This is imprecise. The generalized Lasso is a convex optimization problem (or a "decoder" in compressed sensing terminology), not an algorithm for solving it. This distinction should be clarified in the manuscript.
