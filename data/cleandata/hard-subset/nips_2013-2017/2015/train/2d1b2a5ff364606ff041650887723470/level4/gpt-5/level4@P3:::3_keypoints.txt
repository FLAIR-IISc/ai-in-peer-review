- Main result: In high-dimensional linear-asymptotic regime (m/n → δ), generalized LASSO with non-linear single-index measurements is asymptotically equivalent to generalized LASSO with linear Gaussian measurements y = μAx0 + σz, where μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].  
- Provides asymptotically exact squared-error expressions via a low-dimensional convex program depending on δ, λ, the regularizer f, and structure of x0 (functional F).  
- Theory covers common regularizers (ℓ1, group ℓ1,2, nuclear norm) and is validated numerically.  
- Application: q-bit quantizer design reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 over quantizer thresholds/levels, linking to Lloyd–Max quantizer as a stationary solution.  

- Extends Brillinger’s classical LS non-linear measurement results to high-dimensional, regularized estimators.  
- Strengthens Plan and Vershynin’s non-asymptotic bounds to precise asymptotics with exact μ,σ dependence.  
- Leverages precise-error tools (AMP/CGMT-based) from linear measurement analysis and generalizes them to non-linear observations and broader regularizers.  

- Quality: Results are technically sound, crisply stated, and scalar convex program matches simulations.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally readable but has nonstandard terminology (e.g., “proximal function”), some typos/notation issues, and needs more intuition for scalar variables (α, β, τ) and Assumption 1.  
- Originality: First asymptotically exact characterization of generalized LASSO error under non-linear single-index measurements across many regularizers; quantizer-design implication is novel.  
- Significance: High — unifies treatment of non-linearities for generalized LASSO and provides actionable design/tuning insights.  

Pros for acceptance: sharp equivalence result, precise usable error predictions for multiple structures, identification of μ and σ as sufficient statistics, practical q-bit quantizer prescription, and strong experimental validation.  

Cons and limitations: essential reliance on Gaussian design (no sub-Gaussian/structured designs), opaque Assumption 1, exclusion of μ = 0 cases, limited finite-sample guidance (λ selection, estimating μ/σ when g unknown), and experiments lacking some details (trial counts, error bars, non-Gaussian matrices).  

Specific suggestions: clarify equivalence of constrained vs penalized formulations and λ mapping; fix notation in figures/captions; correct typos; rename “proximal function” to Moreau envelope and avoid overloading “prox”; provide formal interpretation of Assumption 1 via variational analysis/epi-convergence; avoid calling generalized LASSO an “algorithm”; add λ-selection guidance, sensitivity plots, Monte Carlo details, error bars, and a sub-Gaussian/structured A experiment; in quantizer section, show σ^2/μ^2 correlates with empirical error and detail Lloyd–Max optimization; discuss practical estimation of μ and σ from data and plug-in strategies; add intuition for roles of α, β, τ in Theorem 2.2.  

Overall assessment: Strong, timely contribution with broad applicability and solid validation; recommend acceptance after addressing clarity and presentation issues.
