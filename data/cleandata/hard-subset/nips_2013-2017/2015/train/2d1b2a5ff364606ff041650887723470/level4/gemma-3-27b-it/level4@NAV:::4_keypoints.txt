- Paper studies linear estimation with a nonlinear link, extending Plan & Vershynin to generalized LASSO when μ ≠ 0.  
- Main claim: asymptotic estimation error matches Theorem 2.1 and is unchanged between linear and nonlinear links.  
- Strength: clear, well-written presentation with potentially significant results.  
- Limitation: Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors, which may be a strong/unnatural assumption in some applications.  
- Request clarification on whether the Gaussian assumption is necessary for the theorem's validity.  
- Question: How should the regularization parameter be chosen relative to the constraint set κ containing the true parameter x₀?  
- Open issue: paper requires a convex regularizer but does not address optimal regularization selection with respect to κ.  
- Question: Why does the error characterization still depend on μ and σ (measuring nonlinear difficulty) despite equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements?  
- Suggest adding references to single index models and sufficient dimension reduction literature on linear/subspace estimation under unknown link functions.  
- Recommend citing Yi et al. ("Optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions") for related theory and algorithms (including sparse recovery when μ = 0) and empirical support for equivalence claims.
