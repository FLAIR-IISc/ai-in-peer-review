This paper considers linear estimation in the presence of a nonlinear link function. Motivated by recent work from Plan and Vershynin, it investigates the asymptotic estimation error achieved using the generalized LASSO for arbitrary link functions with μ ≠ 0. The key result, Theorem 2.1, demonstrates that the asymptotic error is equivalent whether the measurements are linear or nonlinear. 

The paper builds upon Brillinger’s earlier work and extends it to high-dimensional settings and regularized estimation. The authors obtain explicit expressions for the estimation error, providing a precise characterization of the performance of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements, a contribution they claim is novel. An interesting consequence of their result is the identification of the Lloyd-Max quantizer as optimal for minimizing estimation error in the case of quantized measurements.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The precise characterization of the estimation error for the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements appears to be a significant contribution.
*   **Generality:** The results are presented for a broad class of link functions and regularizers.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper is theoretically sound, with clear mathematical derivations and proofs.
*   **Practical Implications:** The identification of the optimal quantizer (Lloyd-Max) has practical implications for compressive sensing with quantized measurements.
*   **Clear Presentation:** Despite the mathematical complexity, the paper is relatively well-organized and clearly written.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Gaussian Assumption:** The reliance on Gaussian measurement vectors might be restrictive. While justified, a discussion of the sensitivity of the results to deviations from Gaussianity would be beneficial.
*   **Regularizer Choice:** The paper acknowledges the role of the regularizer but does not provide specific guidance on choosing an appropriate regularizer based on the properties of the underlying signal x₀. This is a known challenge in LASSO-based methods, but a discussion of how to address it in this context would be valuable.
*   **Dependence on μ and σ:** While the paper establishes the equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements, the resulting error depends on μ and σ, which characterize the properties of the nonlinear link function. It would be helpful to explore whether these dependencies are fundamental or if there are scenarios where they can be minimized or circumvented.
* **Missing Related Work:** The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive review of related work, particularly in the areas of single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, where similar problems are studied. The recent work by Yi et al. ("Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear transform") which provides alternative algorithms and establishes equivalence under different conditions should be cited and discussed.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound contribution to the field of high-dimensional estimation.
*   The precise error characterization provides valuable insights into the performance of the generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements.
*   The identification of the optimal quantizer has practical implications.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presents its findings.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The Gaussian assumption may limit the applicability of the results.
*   The lack of guidance on regularizer selection and the unavoidable dependence on μ and σ could be seen as limitations.
*   The review of related work could be more comprehensive.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field. The theoretical results are well-established, and the insights gained are valuable. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I recommend acceptance, with minor revisions to address the concerns raised above. Specifically, I suggest the authors address the Gaussian assumption, discuss regularizer selection, and expand the related work section to include recent contributions like Yi et al. (2015).
