Summary and relation to prior work:
This paper analyzes the performance of the l2-LASSO (square-root LASSO) for single-index models with nonlinear measurements yi = gi(aᵢᵀx0), where A has i.i.d. Gaussian entries and the gi are i.i.d. copies of a possibly unknown link function g. The main result shows that, asymptotically, the l2-LASSO under nonlinear measurements behaves exactly as it would under a linear model with effective scaling and noise: yi = μ aᵢᵀx0 + σ zi, where μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)2], γ ∼ N(0,1). Building on this equivalence, the paper provides precise asymptotic expressions for the estimation error via a low-dimensional convex optimization (Theorem 2.2), covering a wide range of convex regularizers and broad priors on x0 that go beyond the common Gaussian assumption. The framework unifies and sharpens earlier asymptotic and non-asymptotic analyses.

This work substantially extends Brillinger’s classical result for least squares, which established consistency up to a scale factor μ in the fixed-n, m → ∞ regime, to the high-dimensional setting with general convex regularization. It also strengthens the results of Plan and Vershynin, who established non-asymptotic upper bounds for a constrained LASSO under nonlinear measurements, by providing exact asymptotic characterizations for the regularized LASSO. The results connect to and generalize precise-error analyses for LASSO-type estimators with linear measurements (e.g., Bayati–Montanari; Oymak–Thrampoulidis–Hassibi) and yield new insights, including an application to optimal q-bit quantization where the Lloyd–Max quantizer emerges naturally.

Strengths:
- Clear and rigorous derivation of an asymptotic equivalence between nonlinear single-index measurements and an effective linear model with parameters (μ, σ2) determined solely by g.
- Precise, asymptotically exact error characterizations for the l2-LASSO that hold for broad classes of convex regularizers and priors on x0, relaxing the usual Gaussian prior assumption.
- Conceptually unifying: the same scalar optimization that characterizes error under linear measurements also governs the nonlinear case after the (μ, σ2) reduction.
- Practical implications, including a principled route to selecting quantization thresholds and levels via the induced σ2/μ2 trade-off, with the Lloyd–Max solution identified as stationary for the relevant objective.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
1) As Brillinger (1982) emphasized, the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously developed in Appendix A, it should also be stated explicitly in the main text to provide a heuristic justification of the block-quoted claim around line 140. Making this reduction prominent early would help readers interpret the subsequent precise results and their dependence on (μ, σ2).

2) The paper focuses on the l2-LASSO (square-root LASSO). A key practical motivation for l2-LASSO over the more common l2^2-LASSO is that the “oracle” regularization parameter for l2-LASSO does not depend on the (typically unknown) noise variance σ. The present results could clarify whether this justification holds in the single-index, nonlinear setting after reduction to the effective linear model. It would be helpful for the paper to discuss, either theoretically via the derived error formulas or empirically via simulations, whether the optimal tuning for l2-LASSO indeed enjoys robustness to σ (and to μ) in this setting, and how this compares to the l2^2-LASSO.

Qualitative evaluation:
- Quality: The paper appears technically sound, with careful assumptions and clear statements of asymptotic regimes. The proofs (as indicated) leverage modern tools and are consistent with the literature on precise LASSO analysis.
- Clarity: The manuscript is generally well-written and organized. Elevating the nonlinear-to-linear reduction (currently in Appendix A) into the main narrative, and adding a brief heuristic for why μ and σ2 suffice to capture the effect of g, would improve accessibility.
- Originality: The precise equivalence and error characterizations for l2-LASSO under nonlinear single-index measurements, with general regularizers and relaxed priors on x0, represent a substantive and original advance over prior bounds-based results.
- Significance: The results are likely to be influential for both theory and practice, offering sharp guidance for performance prediction, parameter tuning, and system design (e.g., quantization), particularly in high-dimensional regimes.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Strong theoretical contribution with precise asymptotics that generalize and sharpen prior work.
- Broad applicability across regularizers and signal models; relaxed assumptions on x0.
- Clear implications for practical design (e.g., quantization).

Arguments against acceptance:
- The central equivalence to an effective linear model is not emphasized in the main text, which may obscure the intuition behind the results.
- The paper does not yet address the practical tuning advantage traditionally claimed for l2-LASSO over l2^2-LASSO in the nonlinear setting, despite having the tools to do so.
