- Main contribution: proves that in high-dimensional limit with i.i.d. Gaussian measurements, generalized LASSO on non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on a linear model yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, where μ and σ depend only on moments of g.
- Provides exact asymptotic mean-squared error formulas for a wide class of convex regularizers (including ℓ1, ℓ1,2, nuclear norm).
- Empirical validation: theoretical predictions match simulations closely.
- Broad applicability: framework covers sparsity, group-sparsity, low-rank recovery and yields novel concrete results for these cases.
- Practical implication: shows in q-bit compressive sensing the optimal Lloyd–Max quantizer minimizes LASSO estimation error.
- Limitation: analysis assumes A has i.i.d. Gaussian entries; extension to other ensembles (e.g., sub-Gaussian) is not addressed.
- Literature gap: single-index model and sufficient dimension reduction literature is not reviewed comprehensively.
- Comparative gap: paper restricts to μ ≠ 0 but does not compare to specialized methods (e.g., Yi et al.) that work when μ = 0.
- Ask for intuition: request for clearer physical/geometric explanation why E[γ g(γ)] and E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2] are the fundamental quantities.
- Quality assessment: technically strong and rigorous.
- Clarity assessment: well-written, organized, and effectively illustrated.
- Originality assessment: highly original extension giving exact asymptotic error characterizations.
- Significance assessment: results are important and likely to influence theory and practical system design (e.g., quantization).
