- Paper extends precise asymptotic analysis of Generalized LASSO to non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0), showing equivalence to linear measurements with parameters μ and σ².  
- Authors derive explicit asymptotic squared-error formulas valid for all regularization λ and broad convex regularizers (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).  
- Builds on CGMT to obtain precise error characterizations rather than upper bounds, improving over prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, proofs thorough, and assumptions (i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices) are well-justified.  
- Provides first-known precise results for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements — a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Application to q-bit compressive sensing shows Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal, linking theory to practical quantizer design.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written for the NIPS audience, with helpful illustrative figures.  
- Key insight (equivalence between non-linear and linear models) is only rigorously shown in Appendix A and is insufficiently discussed heuristically in the main text.  
- Focus is exclusively on the l2-lasso (square-root LASSO) and lacks thorough comparison to the more commonly used l2^2-lasso, omitting discussion of practical implications like oracle property under non-linear measurements.  
- Strengths: generality across structured signals, elegant non-linear/linear connection, and practical implications for measurement design.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of l2^2-lasso comparison and placement of the main theoretical connection in the appendix rather than the main text.  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to move the non-linear/linear discussion into the main text and to better compare l2-lasso with l2^2-lasso.
