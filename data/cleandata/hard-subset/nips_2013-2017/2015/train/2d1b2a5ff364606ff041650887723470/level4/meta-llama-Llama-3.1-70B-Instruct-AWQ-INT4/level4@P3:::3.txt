This paper considers the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. A known heuristic often used is that non-linear observations may be treated as noisy linear observations. Capitalizing on the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors here extend the results of [17], and by moving to an asymptotic regime, they provide novel precise explicit expressions of the error estimation. The accuracy of their predictions is confirmed with many special cases (including the Lasso, the group Lasso and the nuclear norm). The application to the design of optimal quantizers in the context of q-Bit CS and its relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is very insightful.

The paper is well-written, and the treatment is mostly clear. However, the lack of space prevents including all the necessary details, which may make the paper somewhat unbalanced. The authors should consider providing more explanations or references to help readers understand certain concepts, especially for those without a strong background in convex analysis or variational analysis.

The paper builds upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] and expands in several aspects that are novel. For instance, the explicit expressions of the performance estimation error are new. However, the authors should be more precise in their terminology. Specifically, they should avoid calling the generalized Lasso an algorithm in the text, as it is a convex program or decoder, but not an algorithm.

The experiments in the paper show the results when applying the generalized Lasso to the non-linear measurements, and how the theorems predict the estimation error. However, the authors could provide more details about the experiments, such as the specific settings and parameters used.

Some minor corrections and suggestions are:

* Page 4, line 193: the regularized and penalized versions are equivalent with appropriate correspondence.
* Caption of Figure 1: 'n=768' should be '$n$=768'.
* Page 5, line 247: a typo at 'When ...'.
* Page 5, line 294: what the authors call the proximal function is (very well) known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index \tau. The notation used there is awkward as prox is generally used for the proximal point/operator but not for the Moreau envelope. I suggest changing it.
* Page 5, Assumption 1: I think it would instructive to give this assumption a more formal interpretation using results from variational analysis.

Overall, this is a nice piece of work that provides novel insights into the performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements. With some minor corrections and clarifications, the paper can be even more effective in conveying its contributions to the readers.
