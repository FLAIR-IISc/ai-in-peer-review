This article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models, a worthwhile contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators. The authors relax the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior (the distribution of x0), making the results more applicable to a broader range of scenarios.

The article is well-written, with clear explanations and a logical flow of ideas. However, there are a few points that could be improved upon. Firstly, as pointed out by Brillinger (1982), the single index model is equivalent to a linear model with scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously described in Appendix A, it would be beneficial to mention it in the main article as well, to provide a heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140.

Secondly, the article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. One of the main benefits of the l2-lasso over the popular l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter has no dependence on the (usually unknown) noise variance (sigma). A practical question that the results could shed light on is whether this justification for the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso is valid. The authors could explore this point further, providing more insight into the implications of their results for practitioners.

Overall, the article is a valuable contribution to the field, providing new insights into the performance of the l2-lasso in single-index models. With some minor revisions to address the points mentioned above, it has the potential to be an even more impactful and influential work.

Strengths:

* Extends previous work on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators to single-index models
* Relaxes Gaussian assumption on prior distribution, making results more applicable
* Well-written and clear explanations

Weaknesses:

* Could benefit from a more explicit discussion of the connection between single-index models and linear models with scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise
* Could explore the implications of the results for practitioners, particularly in relation to the choice between l2-lasso and l2^2-lasso

Originality:

* The article presents new results on the precise asymptotic behavior of the l2-lasso in single-index models, which is a novel contribution to the field.

Significance:

* The results have implications for practitioners working with lasso-type estimators in single-index models, particularly in terms of the choice of regularization parameter and the potential benefits of using the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso.

Clarity:

Quality:
