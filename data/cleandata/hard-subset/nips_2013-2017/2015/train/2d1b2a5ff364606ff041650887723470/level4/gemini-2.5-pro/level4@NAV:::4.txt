This work investigates the problem of linear estimation when measurements are acquired through a nonlinear link function. Building upon the recent research of Plan and Vershynin, the paper analyzes the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized LASSO for an arbitrary link function, provided that μ ≠ 0. The central result, presented in Theorem 2.1, demonstrates that the asymptotic error is identical for both linear and nonlinear link functions.

The validity of Theorem 2.1 is predicated on the assumption that the measurement vectors are drawn from a Gaussian distribution. This assumption may be restrictive in some practical applications. The authors should elaborate on why this normality assumption is essential for their conclusion to hold.

A few points for clarification:
1.  While the paper discusses the use of a convex regularizer, it does not offer guidance on how to select an appropriate regularizer based on the set χ that contains the ground truth signal x₀. Could the authors elaborate on this selection process?
2.  The main result establishes an equivalence in estimation error between the linear and nonlinear models. However, the conclusion retains a dependence on μ and σ, which are parameters that quantify the characteristics of the nonlinear function g. I would appreciate the authors' perspective on the necessity of this dependence, given the claimed equivalence to the linear case.

The manuscript could be strengthened by including a discussion of the following related work:
1.  The literature on Single Index Models (SIM) and Sufficient Dimension Reduction (SDR) is highly relevant, as these fields focus on estimating linear vectors or subspaces under unknown link functions. The current draft does not position itself with respect to this body of work.
2.  A recent paper by Yi et al. on optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear measurements introduces a novel algorithm for sparse recovery that is effective even for link functions where μ = 0. That work also establishes the equivalence between high-dimensional linear and nonlinear estimation problems under proper conditions, supporting its claims with rigorous theoretical proofs and empirical validation.

Overall, this is a well-written manuscript presenting results of significant interest.
