- Paper shows that Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a scaled, noisy linear model under certain conditions, enabling precise error characterization.  
- Derives explicit expressions for mean-squared error and identifies the Lloyd-Max quantizer as optimal for minimizing estimation error.  
- Builds on and connects to prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) and recent LASSO analysis advances.  
- Novel contribution: rigorous theoretical analysis of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements fills a gap in the literature.  
- Technical soundness: clear derivations and careful asymptotic analysis yield strong theoretical results.  
- Clarity: well-written and logically organized presentation that explains motivations, assumptions, and results.  
- Significance: results are potentially impactful for applications with inherently non-linear or quantized measurements (e.g., compressive sensing).  
- Strengthens literature connections but could further engage single-index model/sufficient dimension reduction research.  
- Typographical errors in the proofs detract from polish and require proofreading.  
- Limited empirical validation: more extensive experiments are needed to demonstrate practical accuracy of theoretical predictions.  
- Strong assumptions (Gaussian measurement matrices, specific link function properties) limit understanding of sensitivity to deviations.  
- Potential overlap with single-index model literature should be investigated and discussed.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, technically rigorous, clearly presented results with practical relevance.  
- Rejection arguments: numerous typos, insufficient empirical validation, sensitivity to assumptions and possible related prior work.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions to fix typos, add empirical validation, and address single-index literature.
