This paper studies the problem of estimating a structured signal from nonlinear measurements using the Generalized LASSO algorithm. The authors provide a precise analysis of the estimation error and show that, under certain conditions, the performance of the Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is equivalent to that with linear measurements.

The paper is well-written and the results are interesting. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the related work. The technical proofs are also well-organized and easy to follow.

The main contribution of the paper is Theorem 2.1, which shows that the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically the same as that with linear measurements. This result is significant because it provides a theoretical foundation for using the Generalized LASSO algorithm in scenarios where the measurements are nonlinear.

One potential limitation of the paper is the assumption that the measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution. This assumption might be strict in some cases, and the authors should explain why it is necessary for the conclusion to hold.

Another question that arises from the paper is how to choose the regularizer based on the set in which the signal lies. While the assumption of a convex regularizer is discussed, the problem of choosing the regularizer is not addressed.
