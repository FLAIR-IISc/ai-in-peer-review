## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission

**Summary:** This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized Lasso under a Gaussian design with non-linear measurements. Leveraging and extending the foundational work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors provide novel, precise, and explicit expressions for error estimation in an asymptotic regime. A key finding is that, asymptotically, non-linear measurements can be effectively treated as noisy linear observations when employing the Generalized Lasso. The theoretical results are validated through numerous special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. Furthermore, the application of these results to the design of optimal quantizers within the context of q-Bit Compressed Sensing, and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, is particularly insightful.

**Quality:** This is a strong and well-motivated contribution. The theoretical results appear sound and the connection to practical applications is compelling.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured. However, the treatment feels somewhat unbalanced, potentially due to space constraints preventing a more comprehensive presentation of supporting details. While the core ideas are accessible, some aspects benefit from further elaboration.

**Originality:** The work builds upon the important contributions of Plan and Vershynin [17], but significantly expands upon them in several novel ways. Most notably, the derivation of explicit expressions for performance estimation error represents a substantial advance. The application to quantizer design also provides a novel and valuable perspective.

**Comments:**

1. **Page 4, line 193:** The statement regarding the equivalence of regularized and penalized versions should be clarified. While true, specifying the correspondence (e.g., the relationship between the regularization parameter and the penalty parameter) would enhance understanding.

2. **Caption of Figure 1:** The notation “n=768” should be formatted as “$n$=768” for consistency with mathematical notation.

3. **Page 5, line 247:** A minor typographical error exists in the phrase “When…”. Please correct this.

4. **Page 5, line 294:** The authors’ use of “proximal function” is slightly unconventional. The described function is more accurately recognized in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of the index $\tau$. The notation “prox” is typically reserved for the proximal operator/point and not the Moreau envelope itself. I recommend revising the notation to avoid confusion.

5. **Page 5, Assumption 1:** Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption using concepts from variational analysis would be instructive and could strengthen the theoretical foundation.

6. **Terminology:** The authors should avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an “algorithm” throughout the text. It is more accurately described as a (convex) program or a decoder, consistent with common terminology in Compressed Sensing.

7. **Experiments & Summary:** The experimental section effectively demonstrates the applicability of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and validates the theoretical error predictions. To reiterate the key contribution: this paper demonstrates that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that obtained with linear, noisy (Gaussian) measurements. The authors provide novel, precise expressions for the estimation error and support these findings with demonstrations across several common regularization techniques (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm). The insightful application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit Compressed Sensing, and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, further strengthens the paper’s impact.
