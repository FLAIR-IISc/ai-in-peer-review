This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with a Gaussian design. Leveraging the foundational work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend their results to an asymptotic regime, yielding new, precise, explicit expressions for error estimation. The accuracy of these predictions is validated across numerous special cases, including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. The application to optimal quantizer design within q-Bit Compressed Sensing, and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer, provides a valuable and insightful perspective.

This is a strong contribution. 

The paper is generally well-written, though the presentation is somewhat uneven in its focus. The inherent limitations of space likely preclude a more comprehensive inclusion of supporting details.

The work demonstrates originality by building upon Plan and Vershynin [17] and expanding upon it in several novel ways. Specifically, the explicit formulas for performance estimation error represent a significant advancement.

Specific comments:

1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent with appropriate parameter correspondence.
2) Caption of Figure 1:  ‘n=768’ should be formatted as '$n$=768’.
3) Page 5, line 247: There is a typographical error in the phrase “When…”.
4) Page 5, line 294: The function the authors term “proximal” is, in fact, well-established in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope associated with index \tau. The current notation is somewhat unconventional, as “prox” is typically reserved for the proximal point or operator, not the Moreau envelope itself. A change in notation is recommended.
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: Providing a more formal interpretation of this assumption, drawing upon results from variational analysis, would be beneficial.
6) The term “algorithm” should be avoided when referring to the Generalized Lasso. It is more accurately described as a (convex) program or, following terminology common in Compressed Sensing, a decoder.
7) The experiments effectively demonstrate the application of the Generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements, and confirm the theoretical predictions regarding estimation error. This work demonstrates that, asymptotically, the estimation performance of the Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements is equivalent to its performance with linear, Gaussian noise. The authors furnish novel, explicit expressions for the estimation error and support their findings through various special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The insightful application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit Compressed Sensing, and its relationship with the Lloyd-Max quantizer, further enhances the paper’s value.
