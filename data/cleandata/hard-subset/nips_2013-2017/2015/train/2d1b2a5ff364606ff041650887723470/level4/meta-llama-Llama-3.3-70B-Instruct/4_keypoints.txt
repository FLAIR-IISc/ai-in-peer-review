- Studies linear estimation under nonlinear link functions using generalized lasso and shows asymptotic estimation error is the same for linear and nonlinear links (Theorem 2.1).  
- Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors; reviewer requests justification for this potentially restrictive assumption.  
- Paper does not address how to choose a regularizer based on the constraint set K containing x0, despite assuming a convex regularizer.  
- Questions why dependencies on mu and sigma remain in conclusions if nonlinear and linear measurements are equivalent, and asks for authors' perspective on the necessity of these dependencies.  
- Omits literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction that study linear vector/subspace estimation with unknown link functions.  
- Fails to cite recent work by Yi et al. on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, which gives algorithms for sparse recovery (including mu=0) and shows equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation.  
- Overall assessment: well-crafted paper with intriguing findings.
