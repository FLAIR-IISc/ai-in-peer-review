

Summary: This paper examines the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements and Gaussian design. It builds upon Plan and Vershynin [17] by extending their results to an asymptotic regime, yielding novel precise explicit expressions for estimation error. The authors validate their predictions across multiple special cases (including the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm). The application to designing optimal quantizers in q-Bit CS and its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer is insightful.  
Quality: This is a strong contribution.  
Clarity: The paper is well-written, though the treatment is somewhat unbalanced due to space constraints limiting necessary details.  
Originality: The work extends Plan and Vershynin [17] in novel aspects, particularly providing explicit expressions for the estimation error.  
Comments:  
1) Page 4, line 193: The regularized and penalized versions are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence.  
2) Caption of Figure 1: 'n=768' should be '$n$=768'.  
3) Page 5, line 247: Typo at 'When ...'.  
4) Page 5, line 294: The "proximal function" referred to is standardly known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index $\tau$. The notation is awkward, as "prox" typically denotes the proximal point/operator, not the Moreau envelope. We suggest revising the notation.  
5) Page 5, Assumption 1: A more formal interpretation using variational analysis would be instructive.  
6) Avoid referring to the Generalized Lasso as an algorithm. It is a (convex) program or decoder (as commonly used in CS), not an algorithm.  
7) Experiments: The paper demonstrates the Generalized Lasso applied to non-linear measurements, showing that asymptotically, its estimation performance matches that under linear noisy measurements (Gaussian). The authors provide novel explicit error expressions, validated across special cases (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm), and offer an insightful application to optimal quantizer design in q-Bit CS with relation to the Lloyd-Max quantizer.
