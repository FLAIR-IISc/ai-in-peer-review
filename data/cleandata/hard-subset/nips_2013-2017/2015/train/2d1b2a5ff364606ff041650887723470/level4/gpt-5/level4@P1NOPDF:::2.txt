This paper extends precise characterizations of the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models and relaxes the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior (i.e., the distribution of x0). It is a worthwhile contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators, and the manuscript is generally well written. However:

1) As noted by Brillinger (1982), a single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a rescaled unknown signal and nonstandard noise. Although Appendix A rigorously develops this connection, it should also be highlighted in the main text to provide a heuristic justification for the claim in the blockquote around line 140. Making this equivalence explicit earlier would help readers understand why results for linear models with nonstandard noise are relevant here and would clarify the intuition behind your asymptotic characterizations.

2) The paper focuses on the l2-lasso (square-root lasso). A commonly cited advantage of the l2-lasso over the standard l2^2-lasso is that the oracle choice of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (typically unknown) noise variance σ. A practical question that your results could illuminate is whether this justification for preferring the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso truly holds in the single-index setting and under your relaxed assumptions on x0. In particular, do your precise asymptotics confirm that the optimally tuned l2-lasso remains effectively variance-free, while the optimally tuned l2^2-lasso depends on σ? Or does the single-index structure (and/or non-Gaussian x0) reintroduce sensitivity to σ through other problem parameters? A brief theoretical or empirical comparison, or explicit guidance on tuning under your model, would be valuable for practitioners.
