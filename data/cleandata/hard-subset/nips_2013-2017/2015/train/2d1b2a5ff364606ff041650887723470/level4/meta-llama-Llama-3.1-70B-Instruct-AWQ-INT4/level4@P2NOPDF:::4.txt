This paper explores linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, building upon the recent work of Plan and Vershynin. It investigates the asymptotic estimation error using generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with \mu \ne 0. Notably, the paper demonstrates that the asymptotic error is identical regardless of whether the link function is linear or nonlinear, as shown in Theorem 2.1.

Theorem 2.1 relies on the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution. The authors should clarify the necessity of this assumption for the conclusion to hold, as it may be overly restrictive in certain cases.

Several questions arise from the paper. Firstly, the choice of regularizer based on the set \kappa in which x_0 lies is not adequately addressed. Although the paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, it would be beneficial to explore this issue further. Secondly, while the results show equivalence between linear and nonlinear measurements, the conclusion depends on \mu and \sigma, which characterize the hardness of estimation from the nonlinear function g. The authors' thoughts on the necessity of these dependencies would be valuable.

Some relevant references are missing from the paper. For instance, research on single index models and sufficient dimension reduction has explored linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. Additionally, a recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," presents a new algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions that have zero \mu. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under proper conditions, supported by thorough theoretical proof and empirical results.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with interesting results.
