- Main result: l2-LASSO under nonlinear single-index measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a linear model yi = μ aᵢᵀx0 + σ zi, with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)2].
- Provides precise asymptotic error expressions via a low-dimensional convex scalar optimization (Theorem 2.2) for broad convex regularizers and priors on x0.
- Extends Brillinger’s classical fixed-n result to high-dimensional settings with general convex regularization.
- Sharpens and generalizes prior non-asymptotic bounds (Plan & Vershynin) to exact asymptotics for regularized LASSO.
- Connects to precise-error analyses for linear-measurement LASSO (e.g., Bayati–Montanari; Oymak–Thrampoulidis–Hassibi).
- Practical application: guides optimal q-bit quantization and identifies Lloyd–Max quantizer as a stationary solution for the derived objective.
- Strength: clear and rigorous derivation of the nonlinear→effective-linear equivalence with parameters determined solely by g.
- Strength: asymptotically exact error characterizations that relax the usual Gaussian prior assumption on x0.
- Strength: conceptual unification—same scalar optimization governs error after (μ,σ2) reduction.
- Strength: meaningful practical implications for parameter/design choices (e.g., quantization).
- Weakness: the nonlinear-to-linear reduction is relegated to Appendix A and should be made prominent in the main text for intuition.
- Weakness: paper does not analyze whether l2-LASSO’s oracle tuning robustness to noise variance σ (the usual practical motivation) holds in the single-index nonlinear setting.
- Suggestion: explicitly state the reduction and provide a heuristic early; discuss or simulate robustness/tuning of l2-LASSO vs l2^2-LASSO w.r.t. σ and μ.
- Quality: technically sound with careful assumptions and modern proof techniques.
- Clarity: generally well-written but would benefit from elevating the reduction and adding a brief heuristic for why μ and σ2 suffice.
- Originality & significance: substantive, original advance likely to influence theory and practice in high-dimensional settings.
- For acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, broad applicability, and clear practical implications.
- Against acceptance: lack of emphasis on the central equivalence in the main text and omission of analysis on l2-LASSO’s claimed tuning advantage in the nonlinear setting.
