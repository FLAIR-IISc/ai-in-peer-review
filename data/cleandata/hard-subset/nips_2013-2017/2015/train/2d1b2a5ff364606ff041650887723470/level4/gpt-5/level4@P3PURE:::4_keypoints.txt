- Main contribution: analyzes generalized Lasso for arbitrary nonlinear links and shows (Theorem 2.1) asymptotic estimation error equals that for a linear link (μ ≠ 0).
- Gaussianity assumption: theorem is proved for Gaussian measurement vectors; clarify why Gaussianity is necessary and whether results extend to broader measurement distributions.
- Regularizer choice: paper assumes a convex regularizer with κ containing x0 but gives no guidance on how to construct/select the regularizer from κ; include design examples or selection guidance.
- μ and σ dependence: bounds depend on μ and σ characterizing the link g; clarify whether these dependencies are necessary, can be weakened/removed, and provide intuition.
- Related work missing: cite and discuss literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which study estimation under unknown links.
- Missing reference: discuss the closely related recent paper by Yi et al. on optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear links and its algorithms/theory/empirics.
- Overall: well-written and interesting result, but addressing Gaussian assumption, regularizer selection, μ/σ role, and related-work coverage would strengthen the paper.
