- Paper studies Generalized Lasso with non-linear measurements under Gaussian design and derives novel, explicit asymptotic formulas for estimation error, validated empirically.
- Quality: solid piece of work.
- Clarity: generally well written but somewhat unbalanced; space constraints omit some details.
- Originality: builds on Plan & Vershynin [17] but introduces new explicit performance/error expressions.
- Comment: Regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence (fix notation/statement on p.4).
- Comment: Figure 1 caption should typeset n as $n$=768.
- Comment: Typo on page 5, line 247 ("When ...").
- Comment: The term “proximal function” is misleading — it is the Moreau envelope of index tau; prox usually denotes the proximal operator; revise terminology and notation.
- Comment: Assumption 1 would benefit from a more formal interpretation using variational analysis results.
- Comment: Avoid calling the Generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; it is a convex program or “decoder.”
- Experiments: Theorems accurately predict estimation error; asymptotic performance with non-linear measurements equals that of a linear model with Gaussian noise; validated for Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm; application to q-bit quantizer design and connection to Lloyd–Max is insightful.
