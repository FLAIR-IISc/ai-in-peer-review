**Summary:** This paper investigates the Generalized Lasso for non-linear measurements under a Gaussian design framework. It formalizes the common heuristic that non-linear observations can be approximated as linear observations with added noise. Building upon the work of Plan and Vershynin [17], the authors extend these foundational results into an asymptotic regime, thereby deriving novel, precise, and explicit expressions for the estimation error. The theoretical predictions are convincingly validated across several important special cases, such as the Lasso, group Lasso, and nuclear norm regularization. A particularly insightful contribution is the application of these findings to the design of optimal quantizers in q-Bit Compressed Sensing, including its connection to the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

**Quality:** This is a high-quality paper that presents a solid contribution to the field.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, although the presentation could be more balanced. It is understandable that due to space constraints, not all technical details could be included.

**Originality:** While the work is founded on the results of Plan and Vershynin [17], it introduces several novel and significant extensions. Specifically, the derivation of explicit expressions for the performance estimation error is a new contribution.

**Comments:**
1.  On page 4, line 193, it is worth noting that the regularized and penalized formulations are equivalent under an appropriate correspondence of their parameters.
2.  In the caption for Figure 1, the notation 'n=768' should be rendered in math mode as '$n$=768'.
3.  A typographical error appears to be present on page 5, line 247, at the beginning of the sentence "When ...".
4.  Regarding page 5, line 294: The function the authors term the "proximal function" is widely known in convex analysis as the Moreau envelope of index \(\tau\). The use of `prox` is unconventional here, as this notation is standardly reserved for the proximal operator, not the envelope. I suggest adopting the standard terminology to avoid confusion.
5.  For Assumption 1 on page 5, it would be instructive to provide a more formal interpretation using concepts from variational analysis. This could offer readers deeper insight.
6.  The manuscript occasionally refers to the generalized Lasso as an "algorithm." It would be more precise to call it a convex program or a decoder (using terminology from the compressed sensing literature), as an algorithm is a procedure for solving such a program.
7.  The experimental results effectively demonstrate the application of the generalized Lasso to non-linear measurements and successfully validate the accuracy of the theoretical theorems in predicting the estimation error.
