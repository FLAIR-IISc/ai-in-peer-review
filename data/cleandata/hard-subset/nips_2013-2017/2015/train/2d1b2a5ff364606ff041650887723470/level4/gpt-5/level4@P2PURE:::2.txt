The paper extends precise characterizations of the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models and relaxes the usual Gaussian assumption on the distribution of x0. It is a valuable contribution to the literature on the precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators. The paper is well written. However:

1. As noted by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and nonstandard noise. While this connection is rigorously developed in Appendix A, a brief explanation should also appear in the main text to heuristically justify the statement in the blockquote on line 140.

2. The paper focuses on the l2-lasso (square-root lasso). Its main advantage over the more common l2^2-lasso is that the “oracle” choice of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (typically unknown) noise variance σ. A practical question that the results could illuminate is whether this justification for preferring the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso remains valid in the single-index setting considered here.
