This paper studies the asymptotic estimation error of the generalized LASSO algorithm for linear estimation with nonlinear link functions. The authors provide a precise analysis of the estimation error and show that, under certain conditions, the asymptotic error is the same for both linear and nonlinear link functions.

The paper builds upon recent work by Plan and Vershynin and extends their results to arbitrary link functions with a non-zero mean. The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of an asymptotically exact expression for the estimation error, which is shown to be equivalent to the error of the generalized LASSO with linear measurements.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear and concise presentation of the results. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and provide a detailed proof of the main theorem.

However, there are a few issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution might be too restrictive in some cases. The authors should provide a clear explanation of why this assumption is necessary for the conclusion to hold.

Additionally, the paper does not address the problem of choosing a regularizer based on the set K in which x0 lies. While the assumption of a convex regularizer is discussed, the authors should provide more guidance on how to choose a regularizer in practice.

Furthermore, the paper does not review any literature on single index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which are relevant to the problem of linear estimation under unknown link functions. The authors should provide a more comprehensive review of the literature to situate their work in the context of existing research.

Finally, the paper does not cite a recent paper by Yi et al. that introduces a new algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions that have zero mean. The authors should consider citing this paper and discussing its relevance to their work.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with interesting results. However, the authors need to address the issues mentioned above to strengthen their contribution.
