- Main result: In the high-dimensional asymptotic limit, Generalized LASSO with measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) has the same estimation error as a linear LASSO with measurements yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi, where μ and σ depend only on g.
- Contribution vs prior work: Generalizes Brillinger’s fixed-dimension unregularized results to high-dimensional, regularized settings and strengthens Plan & Vershynin by providing asymptotically exact error formulas instead of upper bounds.
- Significance/quality: Elegant and powerful theoretical insight explaining why linear-method LASSO works well for certain nonlinear measurements, with practical application (optimal Lloyd-Max quantizers).
- Originality/technical depth: First explicit asymptotically exact MSE formulas for LASSO with nonlinear observations (Theorems 2.2, 2.3); technically deep and novel even for specialized linear scenarios (group-sparse, low-rank).
- Clarity and experiments: Well-written, clearly organized, with numerical simulations that closely corroborate theoretical predictions.
- Weakness — presentation: Manuscript has several typographical errors (especially in appendices) and needs careful proofreading.
- Weakness — assumptions: Analysis relies on strong assumptions (i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix and m,n → ∞ asymptotics); finite-sample or other ensemble discussions are lacking.
- Weakness — related work: Relationship to the extensive single-index model literature could be clarified further to determine if similar equivalence results are known under different assumptions.
- For acceptance: Provides a highly significant, novel, and rigorously derived equivalence result with first exact performance characterization and strong empirical validation.
- Against acceptance: Results depend on restrictive technical assumptions common in the field, and the paper contains typographical errors that should be fixed before publication.
