- Paper derives asymptotically exact error formulas for generalized Lasso under non-linear Gaussian measurements, showing equivalence to a linear Gaussian-noise surrogate.  
- Broad class of convex regularizers covered (Lasso, group Lasso, nuclear norm) with empirical validation and a novel application to q-bit quantizer design.  
- Strong, technically solid contribution that advances beyond prior non-asymptotic bounds and yields concrete performance/design insights.  
- Writing is generally good but exposition is unbalanced and some details are obscured by space constraints.  
- Improve clarity by stating the asymptotic regime and maintaining more consistent notation throughout.  
- Original and significant extension of Plan and Vershynin [17], with new exact characterizations and practical implications.  
- Explicitly note equivalence of penalized and constrained formulations (one-to-one mapping via KKT) and add a brief pointer or lemma.  
- Fix Figure 1 caption formatting to consistently typeset “n=768.”  
- Correct the typo on Page 5 beginning at “When …”.  
- Rename the “proximal function” to the Moreau envelope (e_τ(g) or M_τ g) and reserve prox for the proximal operator to avoid confusion.  
- Provide a variational/convex-geometry interpretation of Assumption 1 (descent/tangent cones, Gaussian width, identifiability).  
- Avoid calling the generalized Lasso an “algorithm”; term it a convex program/estimator or decoder.  
- Precisely state scaling assumptions for n, p, sparsity/structure, and regularization parameters, and discuss finite-sample translation/practical sample sizes.  
- Collect definitions and assumptions for nonlinearity-induced constants (μ, σ^2), and give practical estimation strategies when f is unknown.  
- Discuss robustness to deviations from Gaussian design and model mismatch; state reliance on Gaussianity if proof tools (CGMT/Gordon) require it.  
- Improve experiments: report standard deviations/confidence intervals, state how regularization is chosen and include sensitivity analysis.  
- In experiments, directly compare to the predicted linear-noise surrogate using the same μ, σ^2, and show quantizer designs across multiple bit depths q.  
- Include a concise proof sketch of main tools (CGMT/Gordon, convex geometry of descent cones) and move missing technical details to the supplement with clear references.  
- Ensure consistent notation for vectors, norms, operators, and avoid terminology inconsistencies throughout.  
- Overall recommendation: substantive, timely work recommended for acceptance after minor revisions addressing presentation, notation, and experimental clarity.
