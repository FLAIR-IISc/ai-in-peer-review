The article makes a worthwhile contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators by extending the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models. Notably, it relaxes the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior distribution of x_0, thereby broadening its applicability.

The writing style is clear and well-organized. However, there are a couple of suggestions to further improve the article's clarity and impact.

Firstly, as pointed out by Brillinger (1982), the single index model can be equivalently represented as a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. Although this connection is rigorously described in Appendix A, it would be beneficial to mention it in the main article as well, in order to provide a heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140. This would help readers better understand the underlying reasoning.

Secondly, the article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. A key advantage of the l2-lasso over the more commonly used l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (usually unknown) noise variance (sigma). A practical question that the results could shed light on is whether this justification for the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso holds true in practice. Discussing this aspect would further enhance the article's impact and relevance.

Overall, the article is a valuable addition to the existing literature on lasso-type estimators, and with a few minor revisions, it has the potential to make an even more significant contribution to the field.
