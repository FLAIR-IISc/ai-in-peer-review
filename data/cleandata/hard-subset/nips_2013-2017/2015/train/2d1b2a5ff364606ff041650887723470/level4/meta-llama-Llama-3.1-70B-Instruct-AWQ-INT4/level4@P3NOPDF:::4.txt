This paper explores the problem of linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, building upon the recent work of Plan and Vershynin. The authors study the asymptotic estimation error using the generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with \mu \ne 0, and surprisingly, they find that the asymptotic error is identical regardless of whether the link function is linear or nonlinear, as stated in Theorem 2.1.

Theorem 2.1 relies on the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be too restrictive in certain scenarios. The authors should provide a clear justification for this assumption and explain why it is necessary for the conclusion to hold.

Several questions arise from the paper. Firstly, the authors do not address how to choose the regularizer based on the set \kappa in which x_0 lies. Although the paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, this issue seems to be overlooked. Secondly, while the results show that nonlinear measurements are equivalent to linear measurements, the conclusion involves dependencies on \mu and \sigma, which characterize the hardness of estimation from the nonlinear function g. It would be interesting to know if the authors have any insights on the necessity of these dependencies.

The paper lacks references to relevant works in the area. For instance, research on single index models and sufficient dimension reduction has explored linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions, but these studies are not reviewed or acknowledged in the paper. Furthermore, a recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," presents a new algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions that have zero \mu. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under proper conditions, supported by both theoretical proof and empirical results.

Overall, this is a well-written paper with interesting results. However, addressing the aforementioned questions and incorporating relevant references would strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the topic.
