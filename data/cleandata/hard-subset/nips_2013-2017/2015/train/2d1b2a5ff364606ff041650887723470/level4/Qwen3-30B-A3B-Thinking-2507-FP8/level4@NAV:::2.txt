

The article extends the characterization of the statistical performance of the l2-lasso (square root lasso) to single-index models while relaxing the standard Gaussian prior assumption on the signal distribution. This represents a valuable contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.  

The manuscript is well-written. However, two points merit attention:  
1. As established by Brillinger (1982), the single-index model is equivalent to a linear model with a scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. While this connection is rigorously detailed in Appendix A, it should be explicitly stated in the main text to heuristically justify the claim in the blockquote on line 140.  
2. The focus on the l2-lasso (square root lasso) highlights its key theoretical advantage over the standard l2²-lasso: the oracle regularization parameter is independent of the unknown noise variance (σ). A practical question for the results to address is whether this theoretical advantage translates to meaningful benefits in real-world applications.
