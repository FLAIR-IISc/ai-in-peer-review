- Paper provides asymptotic analysis of the Generalized Lasso under non-linear measurements with Gaussian designs, deriving explicit estimation error formulas and applications (e.g., q-bit quantizer design linked to Lloyd–Max).

- Overall assessment: strong, novel extension of Plan & Vershynin with precise predictions and experiments that closely match theory, though presentation is somewhat unbalanced.

- Major: The paper calls the Moreau envelope the "proximal function" and uses prox notation improperly; recommend standard Moreau envelope terminology and notation.

- Major: Assumption 1 is central but needs a clearer, formal interpretation—preferably connected to variational analysis concepts.

- Major: The Generalized Lasso is described as an "algorithm" in places; it should be called a convex program, decoder, or estimator, reserving "algorithm" for specific solvers.

- Minor: When discussing regularized vs. penalized formulations, briefly state their equivalence under appropriate parameter correspondence.

- Minor: Caption of Figure 1 should format n=768 as a mathematical expression (e.g., $n=768$).

- Minor: Typo on page 5, line 247 at the beginning of a sentence; proofread and correct.
