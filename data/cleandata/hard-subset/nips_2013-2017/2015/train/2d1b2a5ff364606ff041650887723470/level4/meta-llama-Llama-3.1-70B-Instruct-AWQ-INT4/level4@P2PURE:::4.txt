This paper explores linear estimation with nonlinear link functions, building upon recent work by Plan and Vershynin. It investigates the asymptotic estimation error using generalized lasso for arbitrary link functions with \mu \ne 0, revealing that the asymptotic error remains the same regardless of the linearity of the link function, as demonstrated in Theorem 2.1.

However, Theorem 2.1 relies on the assumption that the linear measurement vector is sampled from a normal distribution, which may be overly restrictive in certain scenarios. The authors should provide a clear justification for this assumption and explain why it is necessary for the conclusion to hold.

Several questions arise from this work. Firstly, how can the regularizer be chosen based on the set \kai in which x_0 lies? Although the paper discusses the assumption of a convex regularizer, this specific issue appears to be unaddressed. Secondly, while the results show that nonlinear measurements yield the same asymptotic error as linear measurements, the conclusion depends on \mu and \sigma, which characterize the hardness of estimation from the nonlinear function g. It would be interesting to know if the authors have any insights into the necessity of these dependencies.

Furthermore, there are some notable omissions in the references. The paper does not review any work on single index models and sufficient dimension reduction, which study linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. Additionally, a recent paper by Yi et al., "Optimal linear estimation under unknown linear estimation," presents a new algorithm for sparse recovery even with link functions that have zero \mu. This paper establishes the equivalence of high-dimensional linear estimation with linear and nonlinear link functions under proper conditions, supporting its conclusion with rigorous theoretical proof and empirical results. Overall, this is a well-written paper with fairly interesting results.
