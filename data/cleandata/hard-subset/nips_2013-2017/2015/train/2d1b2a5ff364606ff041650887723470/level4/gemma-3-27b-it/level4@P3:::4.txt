This paper investigates linear estimation in the presence of nonlinear link functions, building upon the work of Plan and Vershynin. The authors study the asymptotic estimation error achieved using the generalized LASSO for arbitrary link functions with µ ≠ 0, demonstrating that, surprisingly, the asymptotic error is equivalent to that obtained with linear measurements (Theorem 2.1). The core contribution lies in providing a precise characterization of this error, capturing the influence of the link function through parameters µ and σ. 

The paper is well-motivated, clearly presenting the problem and its relevance to applications with nonlinear measurement devices. The connection to Brillinger’s earlier work is appropriately highlighted. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of the relevant literature, and the theoretical results appear rigorous. The generalization of existing results to the non-linear case, and the explicit error formulas, are significant contributions. The application to q-bit compressive sensing and the discussion of the Lloyd-Max algorithm further enhance the practical relevance of the work.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The equivalence result (Theorem 2.1) is a surprising and valuable finding, suggesting that the generalized LASSO can effectively handle nonlinear measurements despite being designed for linear models.
*   **Precision:** The derivation of precise asymptotic error expressions is a significant improvement over existing bounds.
*   **Generality:** The results apply to a broad class of link functions and regularizers.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The notation is consistent and the main ideas are clearly explained.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates the practical implications of the theoretical results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Gaussian Assumption:** The assumption of Gaussian entries for the measurement matrix A may be restrictive. While stated, a more detailed discussion of the impact of relaxing this assumption, and potential avenues for generalization, would be beneficial.  It's important to acknowledge the limitations of this assumption in the context of real-world measurements.
*   **Regularizer Choice:** The paper mentions the role of the regularizer but doesn't delve into specific guidance for *choosing* the optimal regularizer based on the underlying structure of x<sub>0</sub>. While acknowledging the convex requirement, more discussion of how to navigate this choice in practice would be helpful.
*   **Dependence on µ and σ:** The authors acknowledge that the error depends on µ and σ, which characterize the difficulty of estimation due to the nonlinear function g. However, further exploration of the implications of this dependence, and how it might guide the design of measurement systems, would be valuable.
*   **Missing Related Work:** The paper could benefit from a more thorough review of related work in single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction, particularly concerning linear vector/subspace estimation under unknown link functions. Also, the recent work by Yi et al. ("Optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear transform") presents an alternative algorithm for sparse recovery even when µ = 0, and would be a relevant citation. This work provides an interesting comparison point and demonstrates related advancements in the field.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and significant theoretical result with strong potential impact.
*   The technical analysis appears sound and the proofs are likely correct.
*   The clarity of the presentation and the relevance of the application make the paper accessible and valuable to the NIPS community.
*   The work addresses a fundamental question about the robustness of the generalized LASSO to nonlinearities.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The strong Gaussian assumption limits the generality of the results.
*   The lack of specific guidance on regularizer selection could hinder practical application.
*   Omission of certain related works (e.g., Yi et al., single-index model literature) weakens the positioning of the paper within the existing body of knowledge.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with valuable theoretical contributions. While the Gaussian assumption and the lack of specific guidance on regularizer selection are weaknesses, they do not outweigh the novelty and significance of the results. I recommend **acceptance** contingent upon the authors addressing the missing related work (Yi et al. and single-index model references) and briefly discussing the potential implications and limitations of the Gaussian assumption. A short discussion on how the choice of regularizer might depend on knowledge (or lack thereof) of the structure of x<sub>0</sub> would also be beneficial.
