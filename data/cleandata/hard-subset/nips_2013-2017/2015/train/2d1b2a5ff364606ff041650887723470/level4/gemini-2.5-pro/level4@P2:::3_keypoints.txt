- Main result: Asymptotically, Generalized LASSO with non-linear Gaussian measurements is equivalent to LASSO with linear measurements of a scaled signal corrupted by Gaussian noise.  
- Key parameters: The equivalent linear model’s scaling and noise depend only on the first and second moments of the non-linear link function.  
- Contribution: Provides precise, explicit asymptotic formulas for estimation error, strengthening prior non-asymptotic bounds (e.g., Plan & Vershynin).  
- Validation: Theoretical predictions are corroborated by numerical simulations and special-case analyses (ℓ1, ℓ1,2, nuclear-norm).  
- Practical application: Reduces optimal q-bit quantizer design to maximizing µ²/σ² and connects to the Lloyd–Max quantizer.  
- Significance: High-quality, theoretically significant advance at the intersection of high-dimensional statistics and signal processing.  
- Technical soundness: Results are built on established high-dimensional inference techniques and appear well-supported by derivations and experiments.  
- Generality: Framework covers a wide class of convex regularizers and non-linear link functions.  
- Clarity issue: Presentation is dense; some technical aspects are brief and could be expanded for readability.  
- Assumption 1: Central but potentially opaque; needs a more intuitive explanation and connections to standard assumptions.  
- Terminology: Calling the Generalized LASSO an “algorithm” is imprecise; it should be termed an estimator, optimization problem, or decoder.  
- Moreau/Prox terminology: The paper mislabels the Moreau envelope as a “proximal function”; use standard “Moreau envelope” and reserve “prox” for the proximal operator.  
- Constrained vs penalized LASSO: Authors should note the close/usually equivalent relationship between constrained and penalized forms and the tuning-parameter correspondence.  
- Minor fixes: Several small typos and typesetting issues (e.g., Figure 1 caption, page 5 line 247) need correction.
