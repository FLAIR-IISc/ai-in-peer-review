The article extends the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models, and also relaxes the usual Gaussian assumption on the prior (the distribution of x_0). This contribution is worthwhile to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.

The article is well-written. However, there are a couple of points that could be improved:

1. As pointed out by Brillinger (1982), the single index model is equivalent to a linear model with scaled unknown signal and non-standard noise. Although this connection is described rigorously in Appendix A, it would be beneficial to mention it in the main article as well, to provide heuristic justification for the statement in the blockquote on line 140. This would help readers understand the underlying relationship between the models.

2. The article focuses on the l2-lasso, also known as the square root-lasso. One of the primary benefits of the l2-lasso over the more popular l2^2-lasso is that the "oracle" value of the regularization parameter does not depend on the (usually unknown) noise variance (sigma). A practical question that the results could shed light on is whether this justification for the l2-lasso over the l2^2-lasso holds true. It would be interesting to see the authors discuss or analyze this aspect in more detail.

The article's extension of the work on precisely characterizing the statistical performance of the l2-lasso to single-index models is a valuable contribution to the literature on precise asymptotic behavior of lasso-type estimators.
