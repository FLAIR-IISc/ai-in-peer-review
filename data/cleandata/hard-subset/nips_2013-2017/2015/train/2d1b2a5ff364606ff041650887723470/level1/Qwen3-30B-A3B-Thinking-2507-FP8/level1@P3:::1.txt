

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper makes a significant contribution to understanding the behavior of the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements. The authors establish that, under Gaussian measurement matrices, the estimation performance of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = gi(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to that of the same algorithm applied to linear measurements yi = μ(ai^T x0) + σzi, where μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ) - μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1). This extends the work of Brillinger (1981) and Plan and Vershynin (2015), who established consistency results but only provided loose upper bounds. The authors provide precise asymptotic error expressions for various structured signal recovery problems (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and apply their results to q-bit quantization, showing that the Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal.

The paper's main strengths are:

1. **Technical Quality**: The authors provide rigorous asymptotic analysis using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem framework, which is well-suited for this problem. The proofs (deferred to appendix) appear sound, and the results represent a substantial improvement over previous work. The precise error formulae (Theorem 2.2) are novel and significant.

2. **Originality**: The key insight that non-linear measurements can be treated as scaled linear measurements with noise is original and powerful. The paper is clearly distinct from previous NIPS work on LASSO with linear measurements (e.g., Donoho & Johnstone, 2011; Thrampoulidis et al., 2013-2015) and extends the Plan-Vershynin framework to provide precise results rather than loose bounds.

3. **Significance**: The theoretical results have immediate practical implications, particularly for q-bit quantization where the authors show the Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal. The precise error expressions provide valuable insights for practitioners regarding how to choose regularization parameters and understand the trade-offs in non-linear measurement settings.

4. **Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear organization and effective use of figures to illustrate the theoretical results. The connection between non-linear and linear measurements is elegantly explained.

The paper has some notable weaknesses:

1. **Assumptions**: The results rely on strong assumptions (Gaussian measurement matrix, unit-norm x0, specific conditions on f), which limit the applicability of the results to non-Gaussian settings or signals with arbitrary norms. The paper should discuss the robustness of these results to deviations from these assumptions.

2. **Finite Sample Performance**: While the asymptotic results are precise, the paper doesn't sufficiently address how well these results translate to finite sample settings, which is crucial for practical applications. The experimental validation (Figures 1-2) is limited to specific cases and doesn't cover a broad range of finite sample sizes.

3. **Connection to Practical Applications**: The q-bit quantization application is interesting but could be strengthened by providing more concrete examples of how these results might guide real-world quantizer design.

4. **Comparison to Recent Work**: The paper cites Plan and Vershynin (2015) as a reference, but it's unclear whether this was a preprint available at the time of submission. The paper should clarify this.

The paper represents a significant advancement in the theory of LASSO with non-linear measurements. It provides the first precise asymptotic analysis of the Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements, which is a substantial improvement over the existing upper bounds. The results have clear theoretical and practical implications, particularly for quantized measurement systems.

**Arguments for Acceptance**:
- Provides the first precise asymptotic error analysis for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements
- Extends and sharpens previous results by Plan and Vershynin
- Offers a unified framework for understanding non-linear measurements as scaled linear measurements
- Contains a concrete application to q-bit quantization with the surprising result that Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal

**Arguments Against Acceptance**:
- Relies on strong assumptions that limit the scope of applicability
- Limited discussion of finite sample performance
- Some technical details are deferred to the appendix, which might make the paper less accessible

Overall, this paper makes a substantial theoretical contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics and compressed sensing. The results are well-motivated, technically sound, and have clear implications for practical applications. While the assumptions limit the scope, the paper provides a valuable framework that will likely guide future research in this area. The precision of the results represents a significant advance over previous work, making this paper an excellent fit for NIPS.

I recommend acceptance. The paper's theoretical contributions are substantial, and the precise error expressions provide valuable insights that will benefit both theoretical and applied researchers.
