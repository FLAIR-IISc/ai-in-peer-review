- Core result: Asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and a scaled noisy linear measurement model, with explicit estimation error formulas.  
- Quantization consequence: Shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for minimizing estimation error in this setting.  
- Motivation and relevance: Addresses practical sensor non-linearities and connects clearly to Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin; relevant to compressive sensing and ML.  
- Mathematical rigor: The analysis is generally rigorous, assumptions are stated, and extensions to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery are provided.  
- Practical application: Includes application to q-bit compressive sensing and links to Lloyd–Max, adding practical relevance.  
- Notation and readability: Dense notation and many references reduce accessibility and disrupt flow.  
- Numerical validation: Limited empirical results (only Figures 1–2); needs more comprehensive experiments and exploration of regime validity.  
- Precision claim ambiguity: The claim of being the “first precise analysis” is not clearly distinguished from existing “tight” or scaling results.  
- Assumption clarity: Assumption 1 and other conditions are presented abruptly and need more intuitive explanation and discussion of implications.  
- q-bit section disconnect: The q-bit quantization application feels somewhat disconnected and requires deeper integration with the main theory.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel theoretical equivalence, rigorous analysis, generality across regularizers, and potential practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Poor accessibility, sparse numerical validation, unclear precision claims, and insufficiently explained assumptions.  
- Overall verdict: Solid and potentially impactful work but currently a weak accept; recommend revisions to improve clarity and expand experiments.
