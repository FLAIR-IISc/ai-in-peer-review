- Main contribution: Establishes precise asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) and an appropriately scaled/noisy linear model.  
- Theoretical advance: Provides exact asymptotic expressions for squared error incorporating the link g, signal structure, and regularization λ.  
- Novelty: First asymptotically precise results for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, improving on prior upper bounds.  
- Methodology: Rigorous analysis using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework.  
- Empirical validation: Comprehensive numerical experiments for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery supporting the theory.  
- Practical impact: Results have immediate applications to non-linear measurement scenarios (e.g., 1-bit quantization) and quantizer design.  
- Surprising finding: Shows Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal for q-bit compressive sensing under the model.  
- Clarity: Well-organized presentation with precise mathematical formulation.  
- Strengths summary: Rigorous theoretical contribution, precise error characterization, practical implications, strong numerical support.  
- Weaknesses summary: Analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, limiting applicability to some structured matrices.  
- Additional weakness: Proofs contain technical complexity that may challenge some readers.  
- Recommendation: Strong contribution, well-suited for NIPS.
