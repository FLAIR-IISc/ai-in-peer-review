- Main contribution: proves that Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a scaled, noisy linear LASSO under Gaussian measurement matrices, with explicit error expressions.
- Extends prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) by providing precise asymptotic error characterizations rather than just bounds.
- Covers a broad class of regularizers and analyzes sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery scenarios.
- Practical application: applies results to q-bit compressive sensing to optimize quantization and thresholds, linking to the Lloyd-Max algorithm.
- Strength: strong, novel theoretical results with precise error formulas.
- Strength: well-situated in existing literature with clear citations across fields.
- Strength: demonstrates practical relevance through a concrete application.
- Strength: thorough analysis across multiple recovery settings showing generality.
- Weakness: relies strongly on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix assumption, limiting real-world applicability.
- Weakness: Assumption 1 is complex and difficult to verify in practice; needs more discussion and guidance.
- Weakness: dense presentation with many theorems/equations; lacks intuitive explanations for less technical readers.
- For acceptance: offers mathematically rigorous, potentially impactful advances in understanding LASSO with non-linear measurements.
- Against acceptance: strong assumptions and technical complexity may limit generalizability and accessibility.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but request revisions to clarify/justify assumptions (especially Assumption 1) and add more intuitive exposition.
