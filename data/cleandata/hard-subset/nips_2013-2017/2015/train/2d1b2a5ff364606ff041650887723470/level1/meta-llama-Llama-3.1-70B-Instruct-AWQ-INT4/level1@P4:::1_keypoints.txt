- Studies estimation from non-linear measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0) and shows Generalized LASSO is effectively equivalent to a linear-measurement problem with a precise asymptotic error characterization.  
- Extends prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) to the regularized Generalized LASSO and provides an error formula that depends on the link g, signal structure, λ, and m.  
- Derivations are rigorous and based on the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT).  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate the accuracy of the theoretical error formula across several settings.  
- Key limitation: assumes measurement matrix A has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, which may be unrealistic in some applications.  
- Missing a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of solving Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Does not investigate robustness of the error formula to model misspecification or data outliers.  
- Original and significant: provides a precise asymptotic error result for non-linear measurements beyond previous linear or loose-bound results.  
- Potential practical impact: informs design of optimal quantization schemes for compressive sensing.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains the CGMT framework and derivations clearly.  
- Reviewer’s overall recommendation: accept for publication despite noted limitations.
