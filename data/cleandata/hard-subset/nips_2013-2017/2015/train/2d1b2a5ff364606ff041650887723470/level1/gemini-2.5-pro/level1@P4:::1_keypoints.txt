- Main contribution: shows that generalized LASSO on non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on an effective linear model yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi with μ,σ determined by g.
- Asymptotic setting: results hold in high-dimensional limit m,n → ∞ with fixed ratio δ = m/n.
- Extends prior work: generalizes Brillinger’s fixed-dimension result and sharpens Plan & Vershynin by providing asymptotically exact MSE for regularized LASSO.
- Technical approach: non-trivial extension of the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) to handle non-linear measurements.
- Theoretical rigor: main claims supported by Theorems 2.1–2.3 with clearly stated assumptions.
- Empirical validation: numerical simulations across different priors and regularizers show excellent agreement with theoretical predictions.
- Clarity and exposition: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and makes complex results accessible with explicit modeling assumptions.
- Originality: first precise error characterizations for LASSO under a general non-linear model; novel insights even for some linear-measurement special cases.
- Practical application: enables principled system design (e.g., optimal q-bit quantizer design using Lloyd–Max) by reducing analysis to a simple optimization.
- Theoretical significance: provides a unifying conceptual tool that simplifies analysis of model mismatch in high dimensions.
- Limitation — asymptotics: results are asymptotic with no finite-sample guarantees, though simulations suggest accuracy at moderate sizes.
- Limitation — Gaussian design: analysis depends critically on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix A, limiting extension to other ensembles.
- Limitation — μ ≠ 0: requires μ = E[γ g(γ)] ≠ 0, excluding even link functions (e.g., g(x)=|x|) that lose sign information.
- Recommendation summary: technically strong, novel, and significant work meriting acceptance despite standard assumptions restricting generality.
