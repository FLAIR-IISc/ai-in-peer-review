## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO when applied to estimation problems with non-linear measurements. The core contribution is demonstrating, under specific assumptions (Gaussian measurement matrix), that the estimation error of the non-linear LASSO is asymptotically equivalent to that of a linear LASSO with scaled and noisy measurements. This equivalence is quantified through explicit expressions for the error, providing precise insights into the role of the non-linear link function and regularization parameters. 

The paper builds upon classical work by Brillinger and more recent results by Plan & Vershynin. It significantly strengthens these results by moving beyond upper bounds and deriving asymptotically precise error characterizations for a broader class of regularizers. The authors also explore an application to q-bit compressive sensing, showing how the derived results can be used to optimize quantization levels and thresholds, even identifying a connection to the Lloyd-Max algorithm.

**Strengths:**

*   **Strong Theoretical Results:** The core theorem providing the equivalence between non-linear and scaled linear measurements is a significant contribution. The resulting precise error formula is a valuable addition to the field.
*   **Clear Connections to Existing Work:** The paper carefully positions its work within the broader literature, appropriately citing relevant contributions from statistics, econometrics, and signal processing.
*   **Practical Application:** The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates the practical relevance of the theoretical findings.
*   **Thorough Analysis:** The paper explores various scenarios, including sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery, showcasing the generality of the results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Strong Assumptions:** The reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices is a significant limitation. While common in theoretical analyses, it may not hold in many real-world scenarios.
*   **Complexity of Assumption 1:** Assumption 1, crucial for the main theorem, is stated but requires significant effort to verify in specific applications. A more detailed discussion of its applicability and potential challenges would be helpful.
*   **Presentation:** While technically sound, the paper is dense and could benefit from more intuitive explanations of the key concepts, especially for readers less familiar with the technical details. The numerous theorems and equations can be overwhelming.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and mathematically rigorous analysis of a fundamental problem in statistical estimation.
*   The results have the potential to significantly advance our understanding of the performance of LASSO-type algorithms in non-linear measurement settings.
*   The application to q-bit compressive sensing highlights the practical relevance of the work.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The strong assumptions limit the generalizability of the results.
*   The technical complexity of the paper may make it inaccessible to a broad audience.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the limitations, I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field. The theoretical results are strong, and the insights gained are valuable. I recommend **acceptance** with a request for the authors to consider addressing the concerns regarding the assumptions and presentation in a revised version. Specifically, providing more guidance on verifying Assumption 1 and adding more intuitive explanations of the core concepts would significantly enhance the paper’s impact.
