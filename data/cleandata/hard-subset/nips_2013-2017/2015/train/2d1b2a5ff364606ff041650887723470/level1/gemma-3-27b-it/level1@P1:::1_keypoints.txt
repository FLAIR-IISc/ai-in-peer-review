- Core contribution: Proves asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements and a linear model with explicit scaling and noise, and gives precise error formulas.  
- Application highlight: Analyzes quantized measurements and shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer is optimal in this setting.  
- Theoretical strength: Builds on solid statistical and signal-processing foundations with rigorous, well-motivated derivations.  
- Novelty/significance: The equivalence result and exact error characterization are surprising, valuable, and extend prior work substantially.  
- Practical relevance: Results directly inform design for quantized systems and other practical measurement scenarios.  
- Presentation: Generally well-organized with standard notation and clear statement of key results.  
- Literature coverage: Thorough review connecting to relevant statistics, signal processing, and ML work.  
- Technical density: Paper is very technical and may be hard to follow for readers not expert in high-dimensional statistics.  
- Strong assumptions: Relies on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix and unit-norm signal normalization; robustness to deviations is not fully explored.  
- Computational considerations: Does not discuss the practical computational cost of finding optimal quantization parameters.  
- Empirical validation: Limited simulations; more experiments across link functions and signal structures would strengthen the claims.  
- Clarity in proofs: Transitions between theorems and proofs and some dense proof notation could be smoothed and better explained.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, significant theoretical results; rigorous analysis; practical quantization result; well-written and well-researched.  
- Rejection concerns: Accessibility due to technical density, limited empirical validation, and unexamined robustness of assumptions — overall recommend accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and add experiments.
