- Summary: Establishes an exact asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) and a linear model yi = μ ai^T x0 + σ zi for i.i.d. Gaussian A, with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].

- Main technical contribution: Theorem 2.1 proves precise asymptotic equivalence; Theorem 2.2 gives an explicit error formula depending on x0 structure, regularizer f, and link g.

- Builds on and strengthens prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) by moving from bounds to exact characterizations in the non-linear setting.

- Originality: First precise asymptotic characterization of LASSO under non-linear measurements and an elegant connection to Lloyd–Max quantization.

- Significance: Provides practical guidance for using LASSO with quantized/censored data and for selecting regularization via the derived error formula.

- Clarity: Well-written, logically organized, with clear statements and accessible exposition for readers familiar with LASSO and CGMT.

- Weakness — measurement ensemble: Assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix, limiting direct applicability to structured ensembles like Fourier or other CS matrices.

- Weakness — finite-sample relevance: Results are asymptotic (m,n→∞, m/n=δ); more finite-dimensional experiments across signal types and link functions would improve practical validation.

- Weakness — degenerate case μ=0: Does not handle μ=0 (e.g., even g or sign function), which requires different analysis and has important practical implications.

- Recommendation: Accept — significant, rigorous theoretical advance with clear practical relevance, though discussion of extensions would strengthen the paper.
