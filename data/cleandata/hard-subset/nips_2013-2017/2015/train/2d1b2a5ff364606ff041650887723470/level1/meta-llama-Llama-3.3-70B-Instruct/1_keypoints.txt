- Main contribution: Extends Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin results to give a precise asymptotic analysis of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, showing equivalence to linear measurements up to a link-function-dependent scale and noise variance.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and necessary background.  
- Technical contributions: Provides detailed error prediction and characterizes the optimal quantizer for q-bit compressive sensing.  
- Strength: Significant extension to non-linear measurement settings common in applications.  
- Strength: Precise, rigorous analysis of Generalized LASSO.  
- Strength: Clear presentation of technical results.  
- Weakness: Assumes Gaussian measurement matrix, which may be unrealistic in some scenarios.  
- Weakness: Analysis is asymptotic and may not accurately reflect finite-sample behavior.  
- Weakness: Lacks sufficient numerical experiments to validate theoretical claims and demonstrate practicality.  
- Pro-acceptance: Significant extension, precise characterization, and important implications for compressive sensing and signal processing.  
- Con-acceptance: Gaussian assumption, asymptotic limits, and limited numerical validation weaken practical confidence.  
- Numerical ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10, Overall 8.8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add numerical experiments, discuss Gaussian-assumption limitations, and expand on practical implications.
