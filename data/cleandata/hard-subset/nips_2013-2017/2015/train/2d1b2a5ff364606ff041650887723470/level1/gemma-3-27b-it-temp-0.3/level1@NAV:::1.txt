## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO when applied to measurements corrupted by non-linear link functions. The core contribution is demonstrating that, asymptotically, the estimation error with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled and noisy observations. The authors derive precise expressions for this error, building upon prior work by Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin, and recent advances in high-dimensional statistics. They also highlight a practical consequence: the optimal quantizer for minimizing LASSO estimation error is the Lloyd-Max quantizer.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a relevant problem in signal processing and statistics where non-linearities are common. The mathematical development is rigorous, and the authors carefully state their assumptions. The connection to existing literature is clearly established, referencing key works in both statistics and machine learning. The generalization of Brillinger’s result to the high-dimensional setting and the precise error characterization are significant contributions. The exploration of applications to q-bit compressive sensing further demonstrates the practical relevance of the findings.

However, the paper suffers from a degree of density and a steep learning curve. While the mathematical rigor is commendable, the presentation could be more accessible. The numerous theorems and equations, while technically sound, can be overwhelming. The discussion of Assumption 1 feels somewhat abrupt and could benefit from more intuition. While the authors acknowledge the complexity of evaluating F in the low-rank case, further guidance on practical implementation or approximation strategies would be valuable. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong theoretical contribution:** Provides precise asymptotic error analysis for a practically relevant problem.
*   **Novelty:** Extends existing results to the high-dimensional regime and provides a precise equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models.
*   **Significance:** Offers insights into the design of optimal quantization schemes and provides a theoretical foundation for using LASSO with non-linear measurements.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Accessibility:** The paper is mathematically dense and may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with the specific technical background to fully grasp.
*   **Practical Guidance:** Limited discussion on practical implementation or approximation strategies for the derived results.



**Overall:** I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution with potential practical implications. Addressing the accessibility concerns and providing more guidance on practical implementation would further strengthen the work.
