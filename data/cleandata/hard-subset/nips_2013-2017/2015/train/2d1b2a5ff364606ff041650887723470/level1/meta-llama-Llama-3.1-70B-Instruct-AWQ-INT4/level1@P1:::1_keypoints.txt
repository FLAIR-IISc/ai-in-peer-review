- Main idea: Analyze Generalized LASSO for nonlinear measurements y = g(Ax0) and show it is equivalent to a linear model y = μAx0 + σz with a precise asymptotic error characterization via a convex program.
- Model/setup: Assumes A has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, g is a random link function, and signal structure is encoded by regularizer f with tuning λ.
- Strength — Novelty: Extends LASSO analysis to nonlinear measurements, a novel contribution.
- Strength — Precise error: Provides an exact asymptotic error formula (not just upper bounds) equal to a convex optimization solution.
- Strength — Generality: Framework is broad and applicable to many nonlinear measurement problems.
- Weakness — Technical assumptions: Relies on strong assumptions (Gaussian iid A, bounded link functions) that may not hold in practice.
- Weakness — Computational complexity: Does not analyze computational costs or scalability of the proposed approach.
- Weakness — Limited experiments: Experimental evaluation is sparse and may not sufficiently demonstrate practical performance.
- Originality: Novel theoretical extension of existing linear LASSO results to nonlinear measurement regimes.
- Significance: Potentially important implications for algorithm and system design involving nonlinear measurements.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on prior LASSO analyses for linear measurements and significantly extends them to nonlinear cases.
- Clarity: Well-written and clear with detailed explanations of assumptions and derivations.
- Quality: Technically rigorous and well-presented.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel analysis, precise error characterization, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: Strong/possibly unrealistic assumptions, no complexity analysis, and limited empirical validation.
- Recommendation: Accept, despite noted limitations.
