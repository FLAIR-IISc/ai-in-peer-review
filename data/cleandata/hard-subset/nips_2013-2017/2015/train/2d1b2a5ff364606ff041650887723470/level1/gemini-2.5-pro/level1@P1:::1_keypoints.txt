- Problem: Estimating a high-dimensional structured signal x0 from nonlinear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) with Gaussian A using the standard Generalized LASSO despite model mismatch.  
- Central result: Asymptotic equivalence — LASSO error ||x_hat - mu x0||^2 equals the error from an equivalent linear model y_lin = mu A x0 + sigma z, with mu and sigma determined by g.  
- MSE characterization: Provides an exact deterministic asymptotic MSE via a simple low-dimensional convex program and specializes to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank cases.  
- Application: Uses the theory for optimal quantizer design, showing minimizing LASSO error is equivalent to minimizing sigma^2/mu^2, solvable by Lloyd–Max.  
- Connections: Generalizes Brillinger, sharpens Plan & Vershynin, and extends the CGMT framework to nonlinear measurement models.  
- Strength — significance: Elegant, powerful reduction that transfers precise linear LASSO results to many nonlinear settings (quantized, censored, saturated).  
- Strength — rigor: Technically sound CGMT-based analysis with strong numerical validation showing near-perfect finite-sample agreement.  
- Strength — originality: Novel asymptotically exact characterization for regularized LASSO and a creative application to quantizer design.  
- Strength — clarity: Well-written, well-motivated, clearly stated results, and helpful specializations.  
- Weakness — intuition: Paper lacks a concise high-level explanation of why the equivalence holds; a sketch of the mechanism would aid accessibility.  
- Weakness — Assumption 1: Stated technically (Moreau envelope of Fenchel conjugate); needs a more intuitive description for generic regularizers.  
- Weakness — limitations: Analysis requires mu ≠ 0, excluding even nonlinearities (e.g., g(x)=|x|); this restriction should be discussed more prominently.  
- Weakness — low-rank case: Treatment is less complete, relies on extra singular-value assumptions, and feels preliminary.  
- Pro acceptance: Fundamental, impactful, novel, technically strong, and likely influential.  
- Con acceptance: Technical machinery may be challenging for some readers and the low-rank contribution is underdeveloped.  
- Conclusion: Strong recommend accept; identified weaknesses are mainly about exposition and completeness in one case.
