- Paper shows generalized LASSO with nonlinear single-index measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with linearized measurements ylin = μAx0 + σz, where μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Result holds for i.i.d. Gaussian A in the high-dimensional limit m,n → ∞ with m/n → δ, and gives exact asymptotic error via a scalar convex program (Eq. (10)).  
- Uses CGMT to reduce the problem to a deterministic scalar optimization whose optimizer α* gives the limit of ||x̂ − μx0||.  
- Instantiates theory for ℓ1 (sparse), ℓ1,2 (group-sparse), and sketches nuclear-norm (low-rank), with explicit fixed-point conditions for ℓ1.  
- Practical application: q-bit quantizer design reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, and Lloyd–Max (LM) algorithm yields stationary points for this objective.  
- Strength — conceptual clarity: clean reduction capturing nonlinearity through (μ,σ^2), generalizing Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin.  
- Strength — technical depth: nontrivial CGMT derivation giving a usable scalar program showing how δ, λ, g, and f interact.  
- Strength — breadth: covers multiple structural regularizers and provides new precise ℓ1 results beyond linear analyses.  
- Strength — practical insight: actionable quantizer-design rule and connection of LM to stationary points.  
- Strength — empirical validation: experiments match theory and the linear-equivalent predictions.  
- Weakness — statement precision: Theorem 2.1 wording should clarify convergence to the same deterministic limit (a.s.), not sample-wise equality.  
- Weakness — Assumption 1 is abstract; need formal sufficient conditions for nuclear-norm case and a worked example for F(c1,c2,c3).  
- Weakness — Gaussian A reliance: no discussion of universality to sub-Gaussian or structured matrices (e.g., partial Fourier).  
- Weakness — μ ≠ 0 requirement omits important nonlinearities; should delineate which g violate this and behavior as μ → 0.  
- Weakness — LM claim overstated: paper shows LM gives stationary points but not global optimality; wording should be toned down or conditions added.  
- Clarity issues: auxiliary variables (α,β,τ) in Eq. (10) lack intuition, several typos, and footnote flow interruptions.  
- Practicality: need guidance for tuning λ (use of λcrit, sensitivity analysis, or cross-validation guidance).  
- Related work: should cite and contrast with GAMP/SE literature for nonlinear channels and matched estimators.  
- Suggestions: clarify Theorem 2.1, formalize Assumption 1 for low-rank, discuss universality, refine LM claims, add quantizer experiments and λ-selection guidance, and expand experiments to other nonlinearities.  
- Acceptance arguments: elegant, novel, technically rigorous result with broad applicability and useful quantizer insight plus strong empirical support.  
- Rejection concerns: heavy Gaussian assumption, abstract key assumption for low-rank, some overstated claims, and lack of finite-sample guarantees.
