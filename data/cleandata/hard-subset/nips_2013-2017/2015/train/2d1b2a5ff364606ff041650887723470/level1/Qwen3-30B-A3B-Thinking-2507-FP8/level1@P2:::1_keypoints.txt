- Main result: Shows Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with linear measurements y = μ(a^T x0) + σz, with exact formulas for μ and σ.  
- Precise error predictions: Derives asymptotically exact estimation error expressions, improving on prior upper bounds.  
- Methodology: Extends the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework to obtain rigorous asymptotic results.  
- Empirical validation: Numerical experiments corroborate theoretical predictions (Figures 1–2).  
- Model coverage: Analysis handles multiple structured signal models (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and provides reproducible details.  
- Quantization insight: Proves the Lloyd–Max quantizer minimizes estimation error for the studied setting.  
- Clarity and presentation: Exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and precise, with consistent notation and helpful examples.  
- Limitations: Relies on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices and technical assumptions (e.g., Assumption 1) that may restrict generality to common regularizers.  
- Practical scope: Strong theoretical contribution but limited discussion of implementation details for real-world non-Gaussian measurement systems.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant theoretical advance with clear practical implications for quantized sensing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Precise error predictions, elegant non-linear–to–linear equivalence, rigorous proofs, and practical quantizer design insight.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Dependence on Gaussian matrix assumption, restrictive technical assumptions, and limited implementation discussion.
