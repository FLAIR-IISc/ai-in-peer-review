- Problem: Estimate structured signal x0 from measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) using Generalized-LASSO that fits a linear model with convex regularizer.  
- Main result (Thm 2.1): In high-dimensional asymptotics, LASSO error on the non-linear model equals error on an equivalent linear model y_lin = A(μx0) + σz with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2].  
- Precise MSE (Thm 2.2): Asymptotic mean-squared error is given by a small deterministic scalar optimization and specialized for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank cases.  
- Application: Optimal q-bit quantizer design for compressive sensing reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, for which Lloyd–Max is effective.  
- Literature connection: Extends Brillinger, sharpens Plan & Vershynin, and generalizes AMP/CGMT analyses to non-linear measurement models.  
- Technical quality: Exceptional rigor and completeness with proofs and simulations that match theory closely.  
- Clarity: Clear motivation, precise theorems, consistent notation, and logical presentation making dense material accessible.  
- Originality: Novel exact asymptotic equivalence for regularized LASSO under non-linear measurements and new specializations for structured models.  
- Theoretical significance: Reduces many non-linear distortions under Gaussian measurements to signal attenuation plus effective Gaussian noise—powerful analytical simplification.  
- Practical significance: Enables practitioners to predict LASSO performance without knowing g and gives actionable quantizer design insights.  
- Weakness: Relies on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix and asymptotic limits; limited discussion of robustness to structured/non-Gaussian A or finite-sample behavior.  
- Weakness: Low-rank matrix recovery section is less developed and depends on extra assumptions about singular value convergence.  
- Weakness: Main text omits an intuitive, high-level sketch of why the equivalence theorem holds.  
- Acceptance pros: Fundamental, novel, technically strong, well-supported empirically, broadly applicable, and high potential impact.  
- Acceptance cons: Minor caveats—strong standard assumptions and a slightly underdeveloped low-rank treatment.
