- Main result: Shows that in high-dimensional asymptotics, LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to LASSO on an effective linear model y = μAx + σz, with μ,σ given by moments of g.
- Novelty: First asymptotically exact MSE characterization for regularized LASSO under non-linear measurements, strengthening Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin results.
- Methodology: Extends the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework to analyze the non-linear measurement setting.
- Theoretical output: Provides precise, explicit error formulas (Theorems 2.2 and 2.3) linking signal structure, regularizer, sample complexity, and non-linearity.
- Practical impact: Application to optimal quantizer design reduces a complex LASSO-dependent optimization to a simple σ²/μ² optimization, showing real engineering utility.
- Empirical validation: Experiments show strong agreement with theory even at moderate dimensions.
- Writing and presentation: Clear, well-organized paper with good literature context.
- Limitation: Results are asymptotic (n → ∞, m/n → δ); no finite-sample guarantees.
- Minor suggestion: Add more intuition and examples about the scope and failure modes of Assumption 1.
- Verdict: Technically deep, elegant, and practically relevant—strong candidate for acceptance.
