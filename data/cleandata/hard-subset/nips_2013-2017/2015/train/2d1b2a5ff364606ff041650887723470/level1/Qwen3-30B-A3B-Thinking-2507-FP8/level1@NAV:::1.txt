

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO for estimating structured signals from non-linear measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0). The authors establish a precise theoretical equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models, showing that the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically identical to that of a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled and noisy observations. This significantly extends the classical work of Brillinger (1980s) and recent results by Plan and Vershynin (2015), who provided only upper bounds rather than precise error characterizations. The authors derive explicit asymptotic expressions for the squared error that incorporate the non-linear link function g, the signal structure, and the regularization parameter λ.

The paper demonstrates strong quality through rigorous theoretical analysis using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem framework, supported by comprehensive numerical validation across sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signal recovery scenarios. The clarity is excellent, with well-organized presentation and precise mathematical formulation. The originality is substantial, as the authors provide the first asymptotically precise results for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, extending previous work that only provided loose bounds. The significance is high, as the findings have immediate practical implications for applications involving non-linear measurements (e.g., 1-bit quantization), and the authors derive the surprising result that the Lloyd-Max quantizer is optimal for q-bit compressive sensing.

A minor weakness is that the paper assumes the measurement matrix has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, which may limit direct applicability to some structured measurement matrices. However, the authors carefully justify this assumption as common in the literature.

Strengths: Rigorous theoretical contribution, precise error characterization, practical implications for quantization design, comprehensive numerical validation.
Weaknesses: Restricted to i.i.d. Gaussian measurements, some technical complexity in the proofs.

Pro: The paper provides the first precise asymptotic analysis of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, significantly extending prior work. The results have clear practical implications for signal processing applications.

Con: The restriction to i.i.d. Gaussian measurements may limit immediate applicability to some real-world scenarios.

The paper makes a strong contribution to the field, advancing our understanding of LASSO performance under non-linear measurements with precise theoretical guarantees. It is well-suited for NIPS.
