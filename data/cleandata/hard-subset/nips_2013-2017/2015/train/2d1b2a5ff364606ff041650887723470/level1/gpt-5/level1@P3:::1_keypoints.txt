- Main result: Generalized LASSO under nonlinear measurements behaves asymptotically like linear measurements y = μAx0 + σz with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].
- Precise asymptotics: Provides exact error formulas via a three-parameter convex program (Theorem 2.2) using CGMT.
- Specializations: Applies results to sparse (including non-Gaussian coefficients), group-sparse, and low-rank settings.
- Quantization application: Shows LASSO error reduction boils down to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 over quantizer thresholds/levels, linking to Lloyd–Max quantizers.
- Conceptual strength: Clean, surprising equivalence that nonlinear measurements are “seen” as linear with effective gain/noise.
- Technical strength: Deep, rigorous analysis producing low-dimensional convex characterizations of error.
- Practical strength: Useful design implication for quantizers and accommodates broad convex regularizers (ℓ1, group norms, nuclear norm under Assumption 1).
- Literature positioning: Extends Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin and provides first asymptotically exact characterization for generalized LASSO with nonlinear observations.
- Limitation — Gaussian A: Analysis relies on i.i.d. Gaussian sensing matrix due to CGMT, unlike some prior subgaussian results.
- Limitation — Theorem 2.1 phrasing: Statement implies almost-sure pathwise equality; should instead state equality of deterministic limits.
- Limitation — Assumption 1 opaque: Central assumption (Moreau envelope limit of f*) is not transparent; needs clearer sufficient conditions, especially for nuclear norm.
- Limitation — low-rank specialization incomplete: Evaluation of the function F(c1,c2,c3) is deferred, weakening that example.
- Limitation — μ = 0 excluded: Analysis requires μ ≠ 0, leaving out important nonlinearities and direction-only recovery cases.
- Limitation — finite-sample behavior: Results are asymptotic with limited diagnostics; guidance on how large n,m must be for good approximation is lacking.
- Limitation — λ selection: Paper lacks explicit, practical recipes for tuning λ using the theoretical formulas and critical λ thresholds.
- Limitation — randomness in g: Assumes i.i.d. g_i; needs clarification that deterministic g (plus noise) is covered and what role g randomness plays.
- Presentation issues: Notational overload (x0/x̄0), normalization, and square-root LASSO variant could be clarified earlier; footnotes disrupt flow.
- Suggested fixes: Restate Theorem 2.1 as deterministic-limit equality, give sufficient conditions/worked example for Assumption 1 (nuclear norm), discuss robustness to non-Gaussian/rotational ensembles.
- Suggested experiments: Expand empirical study across multiple nonlinearities, vary δ and λ, and report finite-sample gaps to theory.
- Suggested practical guidance: Add a subsection showing how to select λ from fixed-point equations and clarify Lloyd–Max optimality (global vs local) with numerical comparisons.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, elegant equivalence with strong theoretical guarantees, first precise asymptotics for nonlinear observations, broad applicability and impactful design consequences.
- Rejection concerns: Dependence on Gaussian designs, unclear Assumption 1 and incomplete low-rank example, and limited finite-sample/practical guidance.
