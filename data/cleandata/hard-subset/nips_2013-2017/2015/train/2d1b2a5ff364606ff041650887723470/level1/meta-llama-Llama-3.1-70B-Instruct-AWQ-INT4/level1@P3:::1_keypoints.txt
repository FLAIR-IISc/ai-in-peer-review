- Main contribution: Shows Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to one with linear measurements (up to a scalar).
- Core idea: Introduces a new framework that precisely predicts asymptotic error behavior for non-linear measurements.
- Technical context: Extends Brillinger and Plan–Vershynin’s work to non-linear measurement settings.
- Strength: Provides a precise, rigorous analysis of the Generalized LASSO in the non-linear case.
- Strength: Results give a more comprehensive theoretical understanding of sparse recovery under non-linear observations.
- Strength: Paper is well-written, with a clear introduction and thorough literature review.
- Weakness: Analysis assumes measurement matrix A has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, limiting practical generality.
- Weakness: No detailed analysis of the algorithm’s computational complexity is provided.
- Weakness: Lacks thorough empirical or theoretical comparison with other existing algorithms.
- Originality: Presents a novel analytical framework and precise asymptotic results for Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements.
- Significance: Findings could influence development and performance evaluation of algorithms for non-linear compressed sensing.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing the paper’s contribution and clarity.
- Counterarguments: Concerns about Gaussian assumption, missing complexity analysis, and limited comparisons are cited as reasons for potential rejection.
