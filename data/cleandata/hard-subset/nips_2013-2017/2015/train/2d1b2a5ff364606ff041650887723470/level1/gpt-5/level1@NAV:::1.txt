This paper studies recovery in single-index models y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0) with structured x0 via the generalized LASSO. Building on Brillinger’s classical result (consistency up to a scalar in least squares with Gaussian regressors) and the constrained-LASSO bounds of Plan and Vershynin, the authors prove an asymptotically exact equivalence: for i.i.d. Gaussian A, generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements behaves as if the measurements were linear with an effective gain μ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2] (γ ∼ N(0,1)). Using the CGMT-type precise analysis previously developed for linear models (e.g., Bayati–Montanari; Thrampoulidis et al.), they provide a scalar convex program that yields the exact asymptotic MSE for broad convex regularizers and all λ. They instantiate the formula for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank settings, and show a design consequence for q-bit quantized sensing: minimizing the LASSO error reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 over quantizers, which leads to the Lloyd–Max solution.

Quality: The technical development appears solid and carefully positioned relative to prior precise analyses in the linear case (AMP and CGMT). Theorems 2.1–2.2 are clear, with specializations (Theorem 2.3) that recover and generalize known results. Simulations align with predictions. A minor clarity issue: in the abstract σ is defined without a square; later σ^2 is used—please fix the inconsistency.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written and organized. Assumption 1 (existence of the limit defining F) is somewhat abstract; it would help to front-load more concrete sufficient conditions and provide worked examples beyond sparsity (e.g., explicit F for nuclear norm under common spectral models). The low-rank section could benefit from a concrete numerical recipe for evaluating F.

Originality: The nonlinear-to-linear equivalence at the level of exact asymptotic error for generalized LASSO appears new, going well beyond prior upper bounds for constrained formulations and beyond L1-only or high-SNR regimes in linear models.

Significance: High. The result unifies the analysis of LASSO under broad nonlinearities and structures and yields actionable design insights (quantizer optimization). It is likely to influence both theory and practice.

Pros for acceptance:
- Precise, general asymptotic characterization under nonlinear measurements.
- Clean reduction to an effective linear model (μ, σ^2) with actionable consequences.
- Coverage of multiple regularizers and structures; connection to quantizer design.

Cons:
- Reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian A; robustness to sub-Gaussian or structured designs is not addressed.
- Asymptotic focus; limited finite-sample guidance and empirical breadth.
- Assumption 1 may be nontrivial to verify in some applications.
