- Studies recovery in single-index models y_i = g(a_i^T x0) with structured x0 using generalized LASSO.  
- Main theoretical claim: for i.i.d. Gaussian A, generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a linear model with effective gain μ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].  
- Uses CGMT/AMP-style precise analysis to derive a scalar convex program that gives the exact asymptotic MSE for broad convex regularizers and all λ.  
- Specializes the general formula to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank settings, recovering and generalizing known results.  
- Derives a design consequence for q-bit quantized sensing: minimizing LASSO error reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 over quantizers, yielding the Lloyd–Max solution.  
- Technical development is solid and well-positioned relative to prior precise analyses (AMP, CGMT, Plan & Vershynin).  
- Theorems 2.1–2.3 are clear and simulations match theoretical predictions.  
- Minor clarity issue: σ is inconsistently defined (σ vs σ^2) in the abstract and main text.  
- Assumption 1 (existence of the limit defining F) is abstract and may be hard to verify in practice.  
- Recommend front-loading concrete sufficient conditions and providing worked examples for F beyond sparsity (e.g., explicit F for nuclear norm under common spectral models).  
- Low-rank section would benefit from a concrete numerical recipe for evaluating F.  
- Originality: high — provides a new exact nonlinear-to-linear asymptotic equivalence beyond prior upper bounds and restricted settings.  
- Significance: high — unifies LASSO analysis under broad nonlinearities and structures with actionable design implications.  
- Cons: reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian A (no sub-Gaussian/structured designs), asymptotic focus with limited finite-sample guidance, and potential difficulty verifying Assumption 1.
