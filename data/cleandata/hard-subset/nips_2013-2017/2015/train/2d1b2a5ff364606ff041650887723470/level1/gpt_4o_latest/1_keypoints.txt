- Main contribution: Provides precise asymptotic characterization of Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements, showing equivalence to appropriately scaled/noisy linear measurements and giving explicit error formulas.
- Method/novelty: Extends classical and recent work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) to derive rigorous high-dimensional results.
- Applicability: Error expressions cover sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery scenarios.
- Practical application: Applies results to quantization in compressive sensing and shows the Lloyd–Max quantizer minimizes estimation error.
- Strength — originality/depth: First asymptotically precise results for this problem class, filling a significant gap.
- Strength — theoretical contribution: Explicit error formulas and the non-linear-to-linear measurement connection are significant advances.
- Strength — rigor: Use of CGMT and formal derivations are technically sound.
- Strength — clarity/numerics: Well-organized theorems with numerical validation (Figures 1–2) supporting claims.
- Weakness — presentation: Dense derivations may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with CGMT; needs simplification/summaries.
- Weakness — assumptions: Results rely on Gaussian measurement matrices and high-dimensional limits; robustness to deviations is not discussed.
- Weakness — experiments: Numerical validation is limited in scope; lacks real-world datasets and alternative measurement models.
- Weakness — comparison to prior work: More detailed comparison of bounds (tightness/general applicability) to existing results is needed.
- Accept argument: Significant, novel, technically sound work with clear practical implications—merits acceptance.
- Reject/concern argument: Presentation accessibility, limited experiments, and untested robustness to non-Gaussian settings warrant caution.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental validation.
