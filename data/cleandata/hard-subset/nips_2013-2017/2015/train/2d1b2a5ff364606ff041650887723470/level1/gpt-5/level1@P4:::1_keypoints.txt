- Problem: high-dimensional estimation from non-linear single-index measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) with i.i.d. Gaussian rows and structured x0; analyze Generalized LASSO with ℓ2 data-fit and convex regularizer without knowing g.

- Theorem 2.1: asymptotic equivalence — LASSO error with non-linear measurements equals that with linear model ylin = μ A x0 + σ z, where μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].

- Theorem 2.2: precise asymptotic squared error characterized by a three-scalar convex min–max program (10) depending on δ = m/n, λ, μ, σ, and a functional F capturing the regularizer and signal model.

- Theorem 2.3 (sparse case): explicit fixed-point equations involving soft-thresholding η, covering non-Gaussian nonzero distributions and matching prior precise linear-model results.

- Low-rank case: sketched path but concrete evaluation of the limiting functional F is deferred.

- Application: quantizer design for minimizing LASSO error reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2; Lloyd–Max algorithm gives stationary points for this objective.

- Relation to prior work: extends Brillinger’s scaling-consistency to high-dimensional LASSO with convex regularizers and refines Plan–Vershynin’s non-asymptotic bounds to asymptotically exact formulas.

- Methodology: leverages CGMT to reduce the analysis to scalar optimization, consistent with prior precise analyses (AMP/CGMT literature).

- Assumptions: Gaussian A, i.i.d. copies of g, bounded nonzero μ and σ, linear asymptotics m/n→δ, and an abstract Assumption 1 enabling the limiting functional F via Moreau envelopes.

- Technical soundness: theorems are clearly stated and plausible given Gaussian rotational invariance; derivations follow established CGMT paradigms.

- Sparse-specialization technical correctness: the sparse result aligns with known ℓ1/ℓ2-LASSO analyses and appears technically correct for general nonzero distributions.

- Quantization claim: correctly reduces design to σ^2/μ^2 minimization with a well-motivated Lloyd–Max connection.

- Major limitations: requirement σ^2>0 (excludes noiseless linear g), critical reliance on Gaussian A, abstractness of Assumption 1, and incomplete low-rank evaluation.

- Clarity: overall well organized and standard notation; the Moreau-envelope definition of F may be opaque to some readers.

- Minor issues: typos (e.g., Ax0 in abstract, “mesaurements”), inconsistent capitalization, and need to clarify that Theorem 2.1 is equality of asymptotic limits.

- Originality: the precise non-linear→scaled-linear equivalence for Generalized LASSO and extension to general convex regularizers/group-sparse settings are novel.

- Significance: provides actionable exact predictions for LASSO error under broad non-linear models, informing λ selection, sample complexity, and system design (e.g., quantization).

- Pros for acceptance: strong, general theoretical contribution; unifying equivalence principle; coverage of multiple structures; explicit sparse-case formulas; empirical validation.

- Cons for acceptance: dependence on i.i.d. Gaussian design, abstract Assumption 1 and deferred low-rank details, exclusion of σ^2=0/μ=0 cases, and lack of discussion on estimating μ and σ from data.

- Suggested improvements: state asymptotic-equality more clearly and treat σ^2=0 by continuity; provide sufficient conditions/examples for Assumption 1 in low-rank models with a worked numerical F; discuss robustness to sub-Gaussian designs; add guidance on estimating μ and σ when g is unknown; fix typos and notation inconsistencies.
