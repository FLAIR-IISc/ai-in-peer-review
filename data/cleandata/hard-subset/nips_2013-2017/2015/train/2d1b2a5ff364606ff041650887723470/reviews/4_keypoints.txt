- Paper studies asymptotic estimation error for linear estimation with arbitrary nonlinear link using generalized LASSO, extending Plan & Vershynin to µ ≠ 0.  
- Main claim (Thm 2.1): asymptotic error is identical for linear and nonlinear link functions.  
- Theorem 2.1 assumes Gaussian measurement vectors; reviewer asks why this Gaussian assumption is necessary.  
- Question: how should one choose the regularizer based on the set K containing x0 — paper discusses convex regularizers but not this selection problem.  
- Question: theorem’s dependence on µ and σ characterizing link difficulty — reviewer asks whether these dependences are necessary.  
- Missing citations: literature on single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction that study linear/subspace estimation under unknown link functions.  
- Missing citation: recent work by Yi et al. on optimal linear estimation under unknown link functions, which addresses sparse recovery even when µ = 0 and shows equivalence between linear and nonlinear high-dimensional estimation.  
- Overall impression: paper is well written and presents fairly interesting results.
