- Main result: Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with scaled noisy linear measurements, with μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].
- Precise error identity proved: ||x̂ - μx₀||₂² = ||x̂_lin - μx₀||₂² in the high-dimensional asymptotic regime m/n→δ.
- Extends prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) from upper bounds to exact asymptotic error characterization.
- Technique: rigorous proofs using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) and carefully stated assumptions.
- Applies to structured recovery: ℓ₁ (sparse), ℓ₁,₂ (group-sparse), and nuclear-norm (low-rank) regularizers.
- Practical application: q-bit compressive sensing with optimized quantization thresholds derived from the theory.
- Strength — Quality: technically sound, well-motivated proofs, and clear assumption statements.
- Strength — Originality: novel insight that non-linear measurements reduce to scaled noisy linear ones; new precise error formula for general regularizers.
- Strength — Clarity: well-written, organized, consistent notation, and good connections to prior work.
- Strength — Significance: useful guidance for practitioners (λ tuning) and broad implications via equivalence to linear LASSO analysis.
- Weakness — Lack of experiments: no numerical validation to confirm theoretical error predictions.
- Weakness — Novelty overlap: insufficiently distinguishes contributions from recent related work by Thrampoulidis et al.
- Weakness — Density: some parts (Theorem 2.2, optimization program, function F) are dense and could use more intuition.
- Weakness — Applicability: analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, limiting extension to other ensembles.
- Pro-accept: significant theoretical advance, elegant equivalence, broad generalization to structured recovery, and useful q-bit CS application.
- Con-accept: missing empirical validation, unclear separation from related LASSO literature, and restrictive unit-norm assumption on x₀.
- Overall assessment: substantial and technically strong contribution; recommended for acceptance.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Reviewer confidence: 4/5.
