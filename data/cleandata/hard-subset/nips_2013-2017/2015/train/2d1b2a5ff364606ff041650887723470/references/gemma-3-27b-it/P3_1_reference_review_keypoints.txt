- Main result: Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a Generalized LASSO with scaled noisy linear measurements, characterized by parameters µ and σ², with a precise error formula (Theorem 2.2).  
- Validation: Theoretical predictions are supported by simulations for sparse recovery and q-bit compressive sensing.  
- Significance: The equivalence is a profound simplification allowing reuse of linear LASSO literature to analyze non-linear settings.  
- Precision: The paper derives a detailed asymptotic error characterization that improves on prior upper bounds.  
- Generality: Results apply to a wide class of link functions, regularizers, and measurement matrices.  
- Literature linkage: Builds on and carefully positions itself relative to Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin, and existing LASSO analyses.  
- Practical relevance: Application to q-bit compressive sensing and use of Lloyd–Max quantization shows real-world applicability.  
- Presentation: Well-written and organized despite technical complexity.  
- Weakness — Assumption 1: The restrictive Assumption 1 has limited justification beyond the ℓ1 case and raises generality concerns.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: Evaluating the error formula (solving the convex program) may be computationally challenging in high dimensions or for complex regularizers.  
- Weakness — Empirics limited: Empirical validation focuses on sparse recovery; more experiments for other regularizers (e.g., low-rank) and non-linearities are needed.  
- Weakness — Finite-sample discussion: Analysis is purely asymptotic with little discussion of finite-sample behavior or deviations from asymptotic predictions.  
- For acceptance: Major theoretical advance, precise analysis, broad applicability, practical examples, and strong writing justify publication.  
- Against acceptance: Concerns about Assumption 1, practical computational cost, and limited empirical breadth temper enthusiasm.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, original contribution; recommend addressing Assumption 1 and expanding empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
