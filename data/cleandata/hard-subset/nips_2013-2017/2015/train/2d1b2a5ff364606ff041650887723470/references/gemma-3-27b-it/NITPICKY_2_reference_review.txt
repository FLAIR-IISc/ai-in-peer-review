This paper investigates the equivalence of LASSO performance with non-linear measurements to that of scaled and noisy linear measurements. The core result, Theorem 2.1, establishes this equivalence, and Theorem 2.2 provides a precise error formula. The authors build upon prior work by Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, extending their results to high-dimensional settings and incorporating the complexities of structured signals and regularizers. They also apply their findings to the specific case of q-bit compressive sensing, proposing an algorithm for optimizing quantization levels.

The paper is undeniably ambitious and tackles a fundamental question regarding the applicability of LASSO to non-linear measurement models. The mathematical derivations appear rigorous, and the effort to generalize existing results is commendable. The connection to the Lloyd-Max algorithm for q-bit compressive sensing is a practical and insightful addition.

However, despite the technical sophistication, the paper suffers from a multitude of minor, yet distracting, flaws that collectively weaken its impact. My overall impression is that of a paper that would benefit significantly from a meticulous round of editing and refinement. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The precise characterization of the error in the non-linear case is a valuable contribution, bridging the gap between theoretical understanding of linear LASSO and its application to more complex scenarios.
* **Generality:** The authors attempt to cover a broad range of regularizers and link functions, demonstrating the versatility of their approach.
* **Practical Application:** The application to q-bit compressive sensing provides a concrete example of the theory's utility.
* **Rigorous Analysis:** The mathematical framework and derivations appear sound.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Formatting inconsistencies:** Numerous typographical errors and inconsistencies are present throughout the paper. For example, the notation used for vectors (bold vs. non-bold) is inconsistent. Subscripts and superscripts are occasionally misused. The citation style fluctuates. (e.g. "[4,5,14,15]" vs "[1, 6, 13]")
* **Redundancy and Verbosity:** The introduction and literature review are excessively lengthy. The authors repeatedly state the same ideas in slightly different ways. The discussion often meanders, and could be significantly condensed without losing information.
* **Lack of Concrete Numerical Examples (beyond Fig 1 & 2):** While Figures 1 and 2 provide *some* validation, the results are presented without sufficient detail regarding the parameter settings, and a broader set of experiments are needed to truly demonstrate the robustness and practical relevance of the theoretical findings.  Specifically, the experiment with q-bit compressive sensing is barely touched upon.
* **Assumptions & Normalization:** The persistent assumption of unit Euclidean norm for x0 (and the associated footnote) feels cumbersome and detracts from the generality of the results. While the authors acknowledge its simplification, its frequent reiteration is distracting. Assumption 1 seems unusually strong; justification for its necessity is lacking.
* **Notational Overload:** The introduction of numerous variables and symbols, coupled with inconsistent notation, makes the paper difficult to follow. A more careful selection of notation could greatly improve readability.
* **Minor Technical Issues:**  The definition of µ and σ² in (3) could be more clearly presented. The discussion surrounding the Fenchel conjugate in Section 2.2.3 feels abrupt and lacks sufficient context for readers unfamiliar with convex analysis.
* **Repetitive phrasing**: Terms like "Interestingly" are used excessively at the beginning of sentences, weakening their impact.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* The theoretical results are novel and potentially impactful.
* The paper addresses an important problem in signal processing and statistical learning.
* The connection to q-bit compressive sensing is a promising application.

**Con:**

* The numerous minor flaws (formatting, verbosity, notation, assumptions) significantly detract from the paper's quality.
* The lack of extensive numerical validation raises concerns about the practical relevance of the results.
* The paper feels somewhat incomplete; certain aspects (e.g., Assumption 1) require further elaboration.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the theoretical merit of the work, I am inclined to **reject** this paper. While the core ideas are valuable, the presentation is marred by a multitude of minor flaws that make it difficult to assess the full significance of the contribution. A thorough revision addressing these issues – including meticulous editing, simplification of the notation, expansion of the numerical experiments, and justification of the assumptions – would be necessary for reconsideration. The level of polish currently present is not commensurate with the standards expected for NIPS publication.
