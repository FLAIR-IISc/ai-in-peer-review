- Main contribution: Proves that generalized LASSO under non-linear single-index Gaussian measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on an effective linear model yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i with μ = E[γ g(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Precise error formula: Derives an asymptotically exact scalar convex optimization (Theorem 2.2) for the squared error ‖x̂ − μx0‖^2 for broad convex regularizers via a functional F from the Moreau envelope of f*.  
- Specializations: Provides concrete characterizations for ℓ1 (sparse), ℓ1,2 (group-sparse), and nuclear-norm (low-rank) regularizers; gives an explicit fixed-point formula for sparse recovery (Theorem 2.3).  
- Application: Uses results to design q-bit quantizers by minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and links Lloyd–Max stationary points to this criterion.  
- Methodology: Builds on CGMT and orthogonal decomposition of Gaussian designs to justify the effective linear model and to derive the scalar optimization.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger’s classical link-misspecification consistency to high-dimensional LASSO and strengthens Plan & Vershynin by providing asymptotically exact expressions and covering regularized formulations.  
- Novelty: First precise asymptotic characterization of LASSO under non-linear output channels across many convex regularizers; unifies previous precise analyses by showing non-linearities enter only via (μ, σ^2).  
- Technical soundness: Derivation and use of CGMT are appropriate; sparse-case results match known linear-model/AMP formulas in special cases.  
- Assumptions: Analysis assumes i.i.d. Gaussian design, linear asymptotics m/n→δ, and μ ≠ 0; Assumption 1 on existence of a Moreau-envelope limit is abstract but verified for ℓ1/ℓ1,2 and partially discussed for nuclear norm.  
- Validation: Empirical simulations show close agreement but experiments could be broader (more g types, more λ values); no finite-sample guarantees are provided.  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized but needs more intuition for F, a brief proof sketch for Theorem 2.1, clarification that limits are almost-sure asymptotic, and fixes for typos and notation (e.g., scaling of A).  
- Originality gap: Should discuss and contrast with generalized AMP/GAMP literature for non-linear output channels and estimators tailored to such channels.  
- Significance: Provides a unifying, actionable recipe to extend linear-model LASSO analyses to non-linear measurements and offers practical guidance (e.g., quantizer design).  
- Pros summary: Clear equivalence via (μ, σ^2); asymptotically exact scalar characterization; concrete specializations; practical application to quantizer design; strong CGMT foundation and consistency with known cases.  
- Cons summary: Limited to Gaussian i.i.d. designs; Assumption 1 is abstract and low-rank case is only partially resolved; no finite-sample error bars; practical estimation of μ and σ from data not addressed; missing discussion of GAMP literature.  
- Suggested revisions: Discuss extensions beyond Gaussian A, provide guidance for estimating μ and σ from data and analyze plug-in effects, broaden experiments (ReLU, logistic, saturations, λ sensitivity), add proof sketch for Theorem 2.1, give more intuition and a worked example for evaluating F in the low-rank case, and cite/contrast GAMP work.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically solid, novel, and broadly useful; recommended to address exposition, scope, and additional experiments in the final version.
