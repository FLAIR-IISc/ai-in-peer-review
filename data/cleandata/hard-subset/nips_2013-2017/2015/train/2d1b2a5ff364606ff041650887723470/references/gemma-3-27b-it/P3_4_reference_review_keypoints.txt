- Paper shows Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with scaled, noisy linear measurements under Gaussian designs.
- Provides precise asymptotic error formulas that capture the effect of the link function, regularizer, and regularization parameter.
- Results are general across a broad class of link functions g and regularizers f, and do not require knowledge of g.
- Analysis is rigorous, using tools like the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem with clearly stated assumptions.
- Illustrative examples (1-bit quantization, Tobit model, q-bit sensing with Lloyd–Max) validate the theory and demonstrate practical implications.
- Paper situates contributions well within single-index models, compressive sensing, and high-dimensional statistics literature.
- Main novelty is a surprising equivalence that simplifies non-linear measurement analysis and extends precise linear-measurement results.
- Manuscript is technically dense; some sections may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with the methods.
- Assumption 1 (limiting behavior of the Fenchel conjugate) seems ad-hoc and needs more justification or conditions for verifiability.
- Computing optimal regularization parameters or error bounds in complex settings may remain computationally challenging; this limitation isn’t fully discussed.
- Practical applications beyond the q-bit sensing example are underexplored and could strengthen impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, mathematical rigor, generality, and practical algorithmic connections.
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to technical complexity and insufficient discussion of Assumption 1.
- Overall assessment: strong, high-quality contribution; recommendation is Strong Accept with requests for more intuitive explanations and a detailed discussion of Assumption 1.
