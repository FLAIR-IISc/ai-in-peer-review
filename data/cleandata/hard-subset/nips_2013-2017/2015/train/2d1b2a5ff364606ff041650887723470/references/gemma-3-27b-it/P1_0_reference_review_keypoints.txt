- Main result: LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with scaled noisy linear measurements under Gaussian measurements, characterized by constants µ and σ^2.  
- Provides a precise asymptotic error formula clarifying roles of link function, signal structure, regularizer, and λ.  
- Applies result to q-bit compressive sensing and proposes an algorithm for optimal quantization levels.  
- Situates work within single-index models, compressive sensing, and prior results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin); connects to recent precise LASSO analyses and sufficient dimension reduction.  
- Extensive and appropriate citations show good familiarity with relevant literature.  
- Novel and surprising theoretical contribution that simplifies analysis by reducing non-linear to scaled linear case.  
- Precise error analysis enables deeper understanding and potential algorithmic improvements.  
- Broad applicability across many link functions and regularizers; q-bit CS example is compelling.  
- Rigorous asymptotic mathematical analysis with clear assumptions.  
- Well-written, logically organized, and consistent notation despite density.  
- Empirical figures convincingly support theoretical claims.  
- Paper is technically dense and may be inaccessible to readers not versed in high-dimensional statistics.  
- Strong reliance on Gaussian measurement matrices and unit-norm x0 limits immediate applicability; sensitivity to assumption violations is not discussed.  
- q-bit compressive sensing section feels rushed; connection to Lloyd–Max and practical implications are underdeveloped.  
- Limited numerical experiments; lacks demonstrations in more realistic scenarios.  
- Proofs are complex and placed in appendices, making quick validation difficult.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, practical-impact potential, rigorous analysis, and relevance to the community.  
- Arguments against acceptance: high technical barrier and limited empirical validation reduce accessibility and practical evidence.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution whose strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (improve intuition, discuss assumption sensitivity, expand q-bit/Lloyd–Max discussion, add more numerical experiments).
