

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO when applied to non-linear measurement models of the form yi = gi(a^T_i x0), where g is a non-linear link function. The authors establish a precise asymptotic equivalence between the LASSO with non-linear measurements and a properly scaled LASSO with linear measurements. Specifically, they show that the squared error of the LASSO with non-linear measurements converges to the same value as the squared error of the LASSO with linear measurements of the form yi = µa^T_i x0 + σzi, where µ and σ^2 are defined as E[γg(γ)] and E[(g(γ)-µγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1). This extends previous work by Brillinger (1983) who established a similar result for least squares without regularization, and Plan and Vershynin (2015) who provided non-asymptotic upper bounds for a constrained LASSO.

The main theoretical contribution is Theorem 2.1, which establishes the equivalence, and Theorem 2.2, which provides a precise asymptotic error formula for the Generalized LASSO. The authors then specialize their results to sparse recovery (Theorem 2.3), group-sparse recovery, and low-rank matrix recovery. They also apply their results to q-bit compressive sensing, demonstrating how to optimally choose quantization thresholds and levels.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework, which is well-established in the literature. The proofs are carefully constructed and the results are supported by numerical experiments (Figures 1-2). The authors address the limitations of their assumptions (e.g., unit norm signal, i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix) and provide clear conditions under which their results hold.

2. **Novelty:** This paper appears to be the first to derive asymptotically precise results for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. The equivalence result (Theorem 2.1) significantly generalizes previous work by Plan and Vershynin [17], who only established non-asymptotic upper bounds. The precise error formula (Theorem 2.2) improves upon previous work by providing tight characterizations rather than loose upper bounds.

3. **Significance:** The results have important implications for practitioners using LASSO in non-linear measurement settings. The equivalence result simplifies the analysis of non-linear measurement models, and the precise error formula can guide the selection of regularization parameters. The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates a practical use case with clear implications for quantized sensing systems.

4. **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the main results are presented in a logical sequence. The authors provide sufficient detail to understand the contributions while deferring technical proofs to the appendix, which is appropriate for a conference paper.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Asymptotic Assumptions:** The analysis is strictly asymptotic (n → ∞), which limits direct applicability to finite-sample settings. While asymptotic analysis is common in high-dimensional statistics, the paper could better address the gap between theoretical guarantees and practical finite-sample performance.

2. **Measurement Matrix Restriction:** The authors assume the measurement matrix has i.i.d. Gaussian entries, which is a common assumption but limits the applicability to other measurement structures (e.g., structured random matrices or deterministic designs).

3. **Unit Norm Signal Assumption:** The paper assumes the signal has unit Euclidean norm, which is a simplification that might not hold in all applications. While the authors acknowledge this and note how to extend the results to signals with arbitrary norm, this assumption could limit the scope of the theoretical results.

4. **Limited Discussion of Practical Implementation:** While the paper provides a theoretical framework, it could better discuss how practitioners might implement these results in real-world applications, particularly regarding the estimation of µ and σ^2 from data when the link function g is unknown.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by establishing a precise equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models for the LASSO.
- The results extend previous work by Plan and Vershynin [17] to provide tight asymptotic characterizations rather than loose upper bounds.
- The precise error formula (Theorem 2.2) is novel and has practical implications for parameter selection in LASSO applications.
- The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates a meaningful practical use case.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- The asymptotic nature of the results may limit their direct applicability to finite-sample settings.
- The restriction to i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices limits the scope of the theoretical results.
- The unit norm signal assumption is a simplification that might not hold in all practical scenarios.

Overall, this paper makes a strong theoretical contribution that extends the understanding of the LASSO under non-linear measurements. The results are technically sound, novel, and significant for both theoretical and applied aspects of high-dimensional statistics. While the asymptotic assumptions are a limitation, they are common in this line of work and the paper carefully addresses the scope of their results. The paper is well-written and provides a clear path for future research in this area. I recommend acceptance for NeurIPS.
