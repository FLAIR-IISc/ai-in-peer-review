

# Peer Review: "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

## Summary

This paper addresses a fundamental question in high-dimensional signal estimation: how does the Generalized LASSO perform when measurements are non-linear (y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0)) rather than linear (y_i = a_i^T x0 + noise)? The authors establish a precise equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models, showing that under appropriate scaling, the asymptotic behavior of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements matches that of a properly scaled linear measurement model with known noise parameters μ and σ². These parameters are defined as μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ) - μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1). The authors prove that the error ‖x̂ - μx0‖² has the same asymptotic behavior for both measurement models, which significantly simplifies the analysis of non-linear measurement problems. The paper extends previous results by Plan and Vershynin [17], who provided non-asymptotic upper bounds, to precise asymptotic error characterizations for the Generalized LASSO. The authors validate their results across multiple structured signal recovery settings (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) and demonstrate practical applications to q-bit compressive sensing.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Soundness**: The paper provides rigorous theoretical results with precise asymptotic characterizations. The proofs build on established techniques from the convex Gaussian min-max theorem (CGMT) framework [19], which has been successfully applied to linear LASSO analysis [16, 21]. The authors carefully handle the non-linear measurement model while leveraging existing theoretical machinery.

2. **Novelty and Contribution**: The equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models represents a significant theoretical insight. While Brillinger [5] and Plan & Vershynin [17] established earlier results for the unregularized and constrained LASSO, this paper extends these to the regularized Generalized LASSO with precise error characterization, which is the most commonly used form in practice.

3. **Generality**: The results hold for a wide class of regularizers (including `1, group-`1, nuclear norms) and link functions g. The framework is sufficiently general to encompass many practical non-linear measurement scenarios (1-bit quantization, censored Tobit models, etc.).

4. **Practical Relevance**: The paper demonstrates a clear application to q-bit compressive sensing, showing how to optimize quantization thresholds and levels to minimize error, which directly addresses a practical problem in compressed sensing hardware design.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Scope of Non-linearity**: The paper assumes the link function g is i.i.d. across measurements and only depends on the scalar a_i^T x0. While this covers many important cases, it excludes more complex non-linearities where g might depend on multiple measurements or have structured dependencies.

2. **Asymptotic Focus**: The results are strictly asymptotic (n → ∞, m/n = δ). While this is standard in high-dimensional statistics, the paper could better address the finite-sample behavior or provide concrete guidance for practical applications with finite n.

3. **Implementation Details**: The paper states that the error prediction can be computed numerically, but doesn't provide algorithmic details for implementing the optimization in (10) for general regularizers. For practitioners, this would be valuable.

## Quality

The paper is technically sound with well-supported theoretical claims. The proofs are carefully constructed and build upon established frameworks (CGMT), making the results robust. The authors properly acknowledge prior work [5, 17] and situate their contribution within the literature. The experimental validation in Figures 1-2 is appropriate and demonstrates the accuracy of the asymptotic predictions.

## Clarity

The paper is well-written and logically organized, though the technical depth may challenge non-specialists. The notation is consistent and well-defined. The main result (Theorem 2.1) is clearly stated and the technical conditions are carefully specified. The paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the equivalence result for applied researchers.

## Originality

This work significantly extends prior results on non-linear measurement models. While Plan and Vershynin [17] established a non-asymptotic upper bound for the constrained LASSO, this paper provides the first precise asymptotic error characterization for the regularized Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements. The connection between non-linear and linear measurement models is novel and insightful, and the error formulae derived for specific structured recovery problems (Theorems 2.3-2.4) appear to be new.

## Significance

The results have significant theoretical importance for understanding the behavior of the LASSO in non-linear settings, which are common in many applications (1-bit sensing, quantized measurements, etc.). The practical application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates immediate relevance to hardware design. The equivalence result simplifies analysis of non-linear measurement problems, as practitioners can leverage the extensive literature on linear measurement LASSO performance.

## Overall Assessment

This is a high-quality theoretical contribution that makes a clear, significant advance in understanding the LASSO with non-linear measurements. The results are well-motivated, technically sound, and extend previous work in an important way. The paper successfully bridges the gap between the well-studied linear measurement case and the more challenging non-linear case, providing a precise theoretical framework that can be applied to various structured recovery problems.

## Recommendation

Accept. The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution that is both novel and significant. The results have potential to influence both theoretical and applied research in compressed sensing and high-dimensional statistics. The paper is well-written and the theoretical claims are properly supported.

## Pro/Con Acceptance

**Pros**:
- Provides precise asymptotic characterization of LASSO with non-linear measurements
- Establishes a clean equivalence to scaled linear measurements
- Extends previous results to regularized LASSO (more commonly used than constrained version)
- Demonstrates practical application to q-bit compressive sensing
- Well-supported theoretical framework

**Cons**:
- Asymptotic results only (finite-sample behavior not addressed)
- Limited scope of non-linearities considered
- Implementation details for the optimization in (10) not provided

The paper's strengths significantly outweigh its weaknesses, and the contribution represents a meaningful advancement in the field.
