This paper presents a surprising and strong result: the Generalized LASSO, when applied to measurements corrupted by a non-linear link function, behaves *as if* the measurements were linear with appropriately scaled noise. This is a powerful claim, and the authors back it up with rigorous analysis, extending prior work on linear measurements and building on recent results regarding non-linear measurements by Plan & Vershynin. The core insight – characterizing the non-linearity via µ and σ² – is elegant and offers a compelling explanation for the observed equivalence.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** This is the most significant strength. The result is genuinely surprising and challenges the intuition that non-linear measurements should necessitate fundamentally different recovery techniques. It reframes the problem, allowing established tools for linear models to be readily applied.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper employs a strong theoretical framework, building on existing literature (CGMT, AMP) and extending it to the non-linear setting. The asymptotic analysis appears sound, and the derivations are clearly presented (though dense).
* **Generality:** The results are quite general, applicable to a wide class of link functions *g* and regularizers *f*.
* **Practical Implications:** The demonstration of equivalence opens the door to leveraging existing analysis and algorithms for linear models in scenarios with non-linear measurements. The application to q-bit compressive sensing and the proposed algorithm using Lloyd-Max, though brief, highlights potential practical impact.
* **Connections to Existing Work:** The paper does a good job of situating its contribution within the existing literature on single-index models, compressive sensing, and sparse recovery.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Density of Presentation:** The theoretical sections are very dense and may be difficult for a broad audience to follow. While mathematically rigorous, increasing readability would be beneficial.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** While Figure 1 is promising, the empirical validation feels somewhat limited. A more comprehensive set of experiments exploring different link functions, regularizers, and signal structures would strengthen the claims. Specifically, demonstrating the performance of the LASSO in the non-linear case and comparing it to the performance using the equivalent scaled linear model would be very convincing.
* **Assumptions:** Assumption 1, while stated, feels a bit opaque and requires careful consideration. A clearer explanation and justification for its necessity would be helpful.
* **q-bit Compressive Sensing Application:** This section feels slightly underdeveloped. While the link to Lloyd-Max is interesting, more discussion of how this result translates into practical improvements for q-bit CS would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The core result is highly novel and represents a significant theoretical advance.
* The analysis is rigorous and builds on a strong foundation of existing work.
* The potential implications for applying linear model analysis to non-linear problems are substantial.
* The paper addresses a fundamental question in signal recovery and provides a surprising and insightful answer.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The dense mathematical presentation may limit accessibility.
* The empirical validation could be more comprehensive.
* The application to q-bit compressive sensing feels somewhat rushed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a highly original and impactful result. While the presentation is dense and the empirical validation could be expanded, the novelty and theoretical rigor are compelling. The potential implications of this work are significant, and I believe it has the potential to influence future research in signal recovery and related fields. I lean heavily towards acceptance, prioritizing the groundbreaking nature of the contribution over minor presentational or empirical limitations. The result is truly a surprising one that fundamentally shifts our understanding of LASSO in non-linear measurement settings.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
