- Summary: Shows that LASSO on y = g(a^T x) is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on scaled noisy linear measurements y' = μ a^T x + σ z, with μ,σ determined by g, and gives a precise error characterization via a convex program.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical result that simplifies analysis of LASSO under non-linear measurements.  
- Strength: Broad generality covering many link functions g, regularizers f, and high-dimensional regimes including m < n.  
- Strength: Provides precise (not just upper-bound) error characterizations and a concrete optimization formulation.  
- Strength: Situates contribution relative to Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, extending prior results to high dimensions and general regularizers.  
- Strength: Practical implications demonstrated for q-bit compressive sensing, including optimizing quantization thresholds.  
- Strength: Clear and well-organized presentation with consistent notation.  
- Weakness: Technical Assumption 1 is hard to verify in practice; more explanation and examples beyond the l1 case are needed.  
- Weakness: Solving the derived convex program (eq. 10) may be computationally challenging for general f and g; discussion of approximations/solvers is lacking.  
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited; more simulations across diverse g and f and visualization near optimal λ would strengthen the paper.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of scenarios where the equivalence breaks down or non-linearities produce qualitatively different behavior.  
- Originality: Highly original extension establishing precise equivalence between non-linear and scaled linear measurements for LASSO with general regularizers.  
- Significance: Potentially high impact for high-dimensional statistics, signal processing, and applications like compressive sensing and medical imaging.  
- Argument for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, precise error characterization, clear presentation, and broad applicability.  
- Argument against acceptance: Applicability may be limited by Assumption 1 and the empirical validation is not extensive enough.  
- Overall assessment: Very strong paper with minor weaknesses that do not undermine its contribution.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, with suggestions to clarify Assumption 1 and expand empirical validation.
