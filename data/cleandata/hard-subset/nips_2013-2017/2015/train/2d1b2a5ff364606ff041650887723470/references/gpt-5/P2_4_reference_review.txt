Summary
The paper studies the performance of the generalized LASSO when the measurements are non-linear single-index observations yi = gi(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian sensing matrix A and unknown (possibly random) link function g. The central result is an asymptotically precise equivalence: in high dimensions with m/n → δ, the estimation error of the LASSO under non-linear measurements is identical to that of the same LASSO run on a linear model with an effective gain and noise, yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ z_i, where μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0,1). Building on the CGMT framework, the authors then give a scalar convex optimization formula (Theorem 2.2) for the asymptotic squared error ‖x̂ − μx0‖^2, valid for a broad class of convex regularizers via a functional F defined from the Moreau envelope of the conjugate f*. They specialize this to sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and low-rank (nuclear norm) settings; for sparse recovery they derive an explicit fixed-point characterization (Theorem 2.3). As an application, they show how the result yields a principled design criterion for q-bit quantizers (minimize σ^2/μ^2), and relate stationary points of the Lloyd–Max algorithm to stationary points of this criterion.

Relation to prior work: The result extends Brillinger’s classical consistency of least squares under link misspecification to high-dimensional LASSO and shows precise finite-SNR behavior when m/n is fixed. It strengthens Plan & Vershynin’s non-asymptotic upper bounds for constrained LASSO under non-linear measurements by giving asymptotically exact expressions (and by covering regularized, not just constrained formulations). It also unifies and generalizes precise-error analyses of LASSO for linear models (via AMP and CGMT) by showing that non-linearities enter only through (μ, σ^2). This appears to be the first precise asymptotic characterization for LASSO under non-linear output channels across a wide range of convex regularizers.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The equivalence to an effective linear model with parameters (μ, σ^2) under Gaussian A is well motivated by orthogonal decompositions of Gaussian designs and is compatible with single-index theory. The CGMT-based derivation of the scalar optimization (Theorem 2.2) is a strong and appropriate tool. The sparse specialization (Theorem 2.3) matches prior linear-model results in special cases and recovers known AMP-based formulas when qX0 is Gaussian, lending credibility.
- Assumptions: Gaussian i.i.d. design, linear asymptotics m/n→δ, and μ ≠ 0 are clearly stated. Assumption 1 (existence of a limit of the Moreau-envelope-based functional) is abstract but verified for ℓ1/ℓ1,2 and plausibly holds for many regularizers; for nuclear norm the paper outlines conditions and defers full evaluation of F.
- Validation: The paper reports simulations (figures referenced) showing close agreement; more breadth (different g such as ReLU, logistic; various λ) would further bolster confidence. No finite-sample guarantees are provided; the results are asymptotic almost-sure limits.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well organized, with a clear narrative from equivalence to precise formula to specializations and application. The definition of F and the role of Assumption 1 could use more intuition (e.g., relate F to denoising risk of the proximal operator). A brief proof sketch of Theorem 2.1 (how the non-linear channel reduces to μAx0 plus orthogonal noise under Gaussian A, and why CGMT transfers) would help readers. There are several typos and minor grammatical issues (e.g., “mesaurements”, “eventhough”, “redifining”); these should be cleaned. Clarify that Theorem 2.1 asserts equality of almost-sure limits of errors (not equality at finite n). Explicitly state scaling of A (N(0,1) entries) and its impact relative to the common N(0,1/m) scaling.

Originality
- The core equivalence and precise characterization for LASSO under non-linear measurements appear novel and important. The work goes beyond Plan–Vershynin by giving exact asymptotics and beyond prior precise LASSO analyses by handling non-linear channels. One related line the paper should discuss is generalized AMP/GAMP for non-linear output channels (e.g., Rangan 2011 and follow-ups), which provides precise asymptotics for certain estimators tailored to non-linear measurements; contrasting with LASSO’s mismatched use would situate the contribution more fully.

Significance
- The results are likely to be influential: they give a unifying recipe to reuse precise linear-model LASSO analyses for a broad class of non-linearities via (μ, σ^2), offer actionable guidance (e.g., quantizer design via minimizing σ^2/μ^2), and extend to several widely used structures (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank). The work advances our understanding of robustness of convex M-estimators to link misspecification and provides concrete, computable predictions.

Pros
- Conceptually crisp equivalence between non-linear and linear settings via (μ, σ^2).
- Asymptotically exact scalar optimization characterization valid for broad convex regularizers.
- Clear specializations (ℓ1, ℓ1,2) and new formulas beyond prior linear-only precise results.
- Practical application to quantizer design; connection to Lloyd–Max is insightful.
- Strong methodological foundation (CGMT); results align with known special cases.

Cons
- Limited to i.i.d. Gaussian designs; no discussion of sub-Gaussian or rotationally invariant extensions.
- Abstract Assumption 1; low-rank case left partially unresolved (evaluation of F deferred).
- No finite-sample error bars; empirical validation could be broader.
- Practical issues when μ is unknown (e.g., sign link) and how to estimate μ, σ from data are not addressed.
- Missing discussion of related GAMP literature for non-linear channels.

Requests and suggestions
- Discuss robustness beyond Gaussian A (e.g., rotationally invariant/sub-Gaussian) and whether proofs extend.
- Add guidance for estimating μ and σ from data when g is unknown, and the impact of plug-in estimation on error.
- Expand experiments to additional link functions (ReLU, logistic, saturated non-linearities) and report sensitivity to λ.
- Provide a short proof sketch for Theorem 2.1 and more intuition for F in Theorem 2.2.
- Clarify the low-rank case by either giving concrete conditions under which F can be computed numerically or including a worked example.
- Cite and contrast with generalized AMP/GAMP for non-linear measurements.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper makes a clear, technically solid, and broadly useful theoretical contribution that will interest the NeurIPS community working on high-dimensional inference, convex optimization, and robust sensing. The limitations are mostly about scope (Gaussian designs) and exposition; addressing the suggested clarifications would further strengthen the final version.
