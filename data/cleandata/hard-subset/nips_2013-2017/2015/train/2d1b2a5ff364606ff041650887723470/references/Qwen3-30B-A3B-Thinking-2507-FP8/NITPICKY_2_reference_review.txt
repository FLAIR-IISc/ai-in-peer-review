

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper claims to establish an asymptotic equivalence between LASSO with non-linear measurements and LASSO with appropriately scaled linear measurements. The authors derive precise error predictions for the LASSO under non-linear measurements, showing that the performance can be characterized by the same parameters (μ, σ²) that appear in the linear case.

While the paper presents an interesting connection between linear and non-linear measurement models, several technical inconsistencies and unclear points significantly undermine the quality of the work.

First, the paper repeatedly uses the word "exactly" when describing the equivalence (e.g., "the LASSO treats non-linear measurements exactly as if they were scaled and noisy linear measurements"), despite the results being strictly asymptotic. This misleading language appears throughout the paper and creates a false impression of exact equivalence.

Second, Equation (3) defines μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1), but fails to specify the distribution of the link function g. Is g a random function with a specific distribution? This critical detail is missing, making the definitions ambiguous.

Third, the paper states "we assume that μ and σ² are nonzero and bounded" in Section 2.1, but doesn't explicitly exclude cases where μ=0 (e.g., when g is even). The paper mentions that "this excludes for example link functions g that are even" but fails to explain why μ=0 cases are problematic for the main results.

Fourth, the paper claims "with probability one" for the limit in Theorem 2.1 without specifying the mode of convergence (almost sure vs. in probability), which is a significant technical oversight.

Fifth, the paper uses inconsistent notation throughout. It defines x0 as the unknown signal but then uses x0 for the normalized signal (x0 = x0/‖x0‖2) without clear distinction. The scaling factor 1/√n in Equation (9) is also not justified or explained.

Sixth, the paper cites references inconsistently (e.g., [4,5,14,15] in the text but not clearly identified in the reference list). The citation [11, Sec. 2.2] mentioned in the text is not provided in the reference list.

Seventh, the paper claims to be the first to derive "asymptotically precise results on the performance of any LASSO-type program" for non-linear measurements, but the reference to [20] for the Gaussian case appears to contradict this claim, as [20] is for linear measurements.

Eighth, in the sparse recovery section, the authors claim "The special case of Theorem 2.3 for which qX0 is Gaussian has been previously studied in [20]." However, [20] deals with linear measurements, not non-linear, so this isn't a direct comparison.

Finally, the paper makes an important assumption that x0 has unit Euclidean norm (‖x0‖2 = 1) to simplify the presentation, but fails to clearly explain how this normalization affects the interpretation of the results, particularly regarding the magnitude of the signal.

While the paper presents an interesting connection between linear and non-linear measurement models, these technical inconsistencies and lack of precision in the claims significantly undermine the contribution. The paper's strong claims about equivalence are not precisely supported by the technical results, and the lack of clarity on fundamental aspects of the model (distribution of g, meaning of "exactly," treatment of μ=0 cases) make it difficult to evaluate the work's significance.

As a perfectionist reviewer, I find these technical inconsistencies too numerous and fundamental to overlook. The paper requires substantial revision to clarify these points before it could be considered for publication at NIPS. The minor flaws accumulate to the point where they overshadow the paper's potential contributions. I therefore recommend rejection.
