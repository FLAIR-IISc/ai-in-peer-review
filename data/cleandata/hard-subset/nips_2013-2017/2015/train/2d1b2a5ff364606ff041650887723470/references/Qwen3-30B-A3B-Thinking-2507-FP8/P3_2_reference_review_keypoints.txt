- Main contribution: Proves asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and LASSO with scaled linear measurements; Theorems 2.1–2.2 give exact squared-error predictions via μ, σ², δ, and signal function F.

- Strength — theoretical novelty: First precise error characterization for LASSO with non-linear measurements, advancing beyond prior upper bounds (e.g., Plan & Vershynin).

- Strength — technical soundness: Proofs use established CGMT techniques and numerical results (Figures 1–2) strongly validate the theory.

- Strength — practical relevance: Provides a principled q-bit compressive sensing application for optimal quantization threshold design with hardware implications.

- Strength — broad applicability: Framework handles many regularizers (including nuclear norm) and link functions, covering sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.

- Weakness — restrictive signal norm: Assumes ||x0||2 = 1, excluding cases where signal magnitude matters; claimed generalization lacks technical detail and effect on error formula is unclear.

- Weakness — incomplete related work: Overlooks Yi et al. (2015) on optimal linear estimation under unknown nonlinear transforms; relationship between the works needs clarification.

- Weakness — missing computational discussion: No treatment of practical implementation or scalability for solving the convex optimization that yields the error formula.

- Weakness — performance metric concerns: Using squared error under unit-norm assumption is questionable when g is unknown and only direction matters; stronger justification needed.

- Overall assessment: Significant, rigorous, and broadly applicable theoretical advance with strong empirical support; limitations are important but not fatal.

- Recommendation: Accept, with requested revisions to address the related-work comparison and clarify/generalize the signal norm assumption.
