- Summary: Shows that Generalized LASSO with nonlinear measurements yi = g(aTi x0) is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with scaled noisy linear measurements yi = µaTi x0 + σzi, with precise error formula.  
- Novelty & significance: Highly original result that extends linear LASSO theory and algorithms to broad classes of nonlinear measurement models.  
- Technical soundness: Rigorous analysis built on existing LASSO theory, with clear assumptions and derivation of a precise error formula.  
- Generality: Holds for a wide class of link functions g and regularizers f, and does not require knowledge of g.  
- Practical implications: Enables applications like q-bit compressive sensing and optimization of quantization via derived parameters µ and σ.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized with clear notation and connections to prior work.  
- Assumptions: Relies on Gaussian measurement matrices and unit-norm x0; robustness to non-Gaussian A and relaxed norms is not fully addressed.  
- Computational complexity: Computing the error formula can be challenging for complex regularizers; Lloyd–Max algorithm discussion lacks detailed complexity/convergence analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation: Limited simulations—more extensive experiments across link functions, signal structures, and noise levels would strengthen claims.  
- Assumption 1: The technical requirement around the limit condition seems ad-hoc and would benefit from a more intuitive justification.  
- Quality score: 9/10 — technically strong and significant but assumption-heavy.  
- Clarity score: 8/10 — generally clear, with some sections needing clarification (e.g., Assumption 1).  
- Originality score: 10/10 — highly original contribution.  
- Significance score: 9/10 — likely to be influential with practical impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel rigorous result, broad applicability, practical relevance (q-bit CS), and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Restrictive assumptions (Gaussian A, unit norm x0), computational evaluation gaps, and limited empirical validation.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept — significant, rigorous work deserving publication despite minor/addressable weaknesses.
