- Main contribution: Proves that generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with appropriately scaled noisy linear measurements (y_lin = A(μx0) + σz).  
- Setting: Results hold in the high-dimensional asymptotic regime (m,n → ∞ with m/n = δ fixed).  
- Methodology: Uses the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) to derive precise error formulas.  
- Scope: Provides exact error characterizations for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery.  
- Application: Applies theory to q-bit compressive sensing and derives optimal quantization design.  
- Validation: Experiments (Figures 1–2) match theoretical predictions, supporting the analysis.  
- Quality: Technically sound and builds on recent precise LASSO analyses; proofs are rigorous.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, but some technical proofs are dense and may challenge readers unfamiliar with CGMT.  
- Originality: Novel extension of precise LASSO analysis to non-linear measurements, beyond prior non-asymptotic bounds.  
- Significance: Important practical implications for quantized and 1-bit sensing; guides regularization and measurement design.  
- Strengths: Precise theoretical advance, rigorous equivalence proof, broad applicability, practical q-bit algorithm, solid experiments.  
- Weaknesses: Reliance on asymptotic regime limits finite-sample applicability; presentation density for some theorems; assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices.  
- Comparative gap: Limited empirical/theoretical comparison with alternative methods for non-linear measurements (e.g., binary measurement techniques).  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — contribution is significant, well-justified, and of broad interest.  
- Acceptance arguments: First precise characterization for non-linear LASSO, clear equivalence result, concrete error formulas, q-bit application, validated experiments.  
- Rejection concerns: Applicability limited by asymptotics, technical accessibility, and limited method comparisons.
