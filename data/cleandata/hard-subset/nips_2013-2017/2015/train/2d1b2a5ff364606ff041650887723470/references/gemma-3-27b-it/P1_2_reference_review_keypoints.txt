- Core result: Shows that generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(aTi x0) is asymptotically equivalent (under Gaussian measurements) to LASSO on scaled, noisy linear measurements characterized by μ and σ².  
- Precise asymptotic analysis: Delivers exact error formulas (not just bounds) using modern tools (e.g., CGMT/AMP).  
- Generality: Applies to a broad class of link functions g and regularizers f, covering sparse, low-rank, and group-sparse signals.  
- Literature connection: Builds on and extends Plan & Vershynin and Brillinger, situating results within single-index models and compressive sensing.  
- Practical application: Applies theory to q-bit compressive sensing to guide quantizer design and thresholds.  
- Presentation: Generally well-written and organized, though some sections (notably q-bit) could be streamlined.  
- Limitation — Gaussian assumption: Results rely on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement vectors, limiting applicability and robustness to deviations.  
- Limitation — Assumption 1: The Fenchel-conjugate limit assumption may be strong for some regularizers and needs more discussion or relaxation.  
- Limitation — computational aspects: Insufficient discussion of practical/efficient methods to solve the general optimization in (10).  
- Limitation — q-bit section: Feels rushed; the Lloyd–Max connection is abrupt and lacks validating experiments.  
- Limitation — numerical validation: Figures are limited; broader simulations across parameters and link functions are needed.  
- Quality judgment: Technically sound and rigorous; strongly positive overall quality.  
- Clarity judgment: Generally clear but mathematically dense; positive with room for streamlining.  
- Originality judgment: Novel core equivalence result and extension of error analysis; strongly positive originality.  
- Significance judgment: High impact potential across compressive sensing, signal processing, and inference; strongly positive significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Groundbreaking, mathematically solid result with broad applicability and clear connection to prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Dependence on Gaussian measurements, potentially restrictive Assumption 1, and underdeveloped q-bit validation.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address robustness to Gaussian assumptions and to expand the q-bit sensing details/validation.
