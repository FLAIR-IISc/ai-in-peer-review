- Summary: Extends precise analysis of Generalized LASSO to non-linear measurements, showing asymptotic equivalence to linear measurements scaled by μ with noise variance σ^2 and providing precise error formulas.  
- Main technical contributions: Theorem 2.1 (equivalence of non-linear and scaled linear measurements) and Theorem 2.2 (precise asymptotic error formula); specializations to sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery and q-bit compressive sensing.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical depth and rigor: Uses the CGMT framework to obtain precise asymptotic characterizations beyond prior loose upper bounds.  
- Novel insight: Shows non-linear measurements act like scaled noisy linear measurements parameterized by μ and σ^2.  
- Comprehensive specialization: Derives concrete error formulas for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank cases, demonstrating practical applicability.  
- Practical application: Applies theory to q-bit compressive sensing and links optimal quantization thresholds to the Lloyd–Max algorithm.  
- Clear relation to prior work: Effectively situates and extends results from Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin, and recent precise LASSO analyses.

Weaknesses:
- Measurement matrix restriction: Assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurements without discussion of extension to sub-Gaussian or structured ensembles.  
- Link function knowledge assumption: Requires knowing μ and σ^2 (hence g), with no guidance on estimating them when g is unknown.  
- Unit-norm assumption: Assumes ∥x0∥2 = 1 (handled by redefining g), but lacks practical discussion for non-normalized signals.  
- Computational complexity: Evaluating the scalar function F(c1,c2,c3) for general regularizers may be challenging and is not discussed in depth.  
- Finite-sample behavior: Provides asymptotic results but lacks experiments or discussion on convergence rates for moderate n.

Overall assessment:
- High-quality theoretical contribution that rigorously extends LASSO analysis to non-linear measurements with significant potential impact.

Recommendation:
- Accept; the paper is novel, well-motivated, and technically sound despite common theoretical assumptions.

Specific suggestions:
- Add discussion on potential extensions to non-Gaussian or structured measurement matrices and cite related work.  
- Clarify the assumption about the link function g and what is meant by "generically random."  
- Include or discuss finite-sample behavior empirically or as future work to show asymptotic convergence rates.  
- Explicitly state how to handle the case ∥x0∥2 ≠ 1 in practice.
