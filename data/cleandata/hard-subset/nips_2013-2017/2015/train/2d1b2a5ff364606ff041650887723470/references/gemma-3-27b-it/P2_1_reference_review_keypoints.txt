- Paper shows Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with scaled noisy linear measurements.
- Provides a precise asymptotic error formula and validates results with simulations.
- Specialized results for sparse recovery and q-bit compressive sensing are presented.
- Novelty/significance: the equivalence result is a substantial theoretical contribution for non-linear sensing analysis.
- Technical soundness: derivations are rigorous and build on strong prior work in high-dimensional statistics.
- Precise results: Theorem 2.2 gives actionable error characterization; Theorem 2.3 addresses sparse recovery.
- Clarity: well-structured paper with generally clear notation and effective motivation.
- Completeness: main results and implications are presented in the body; proofs are deferred to the appendix.
- Weakness: Gaussian measurement vector assumption (A) is restrictive and limits generality.
- Weakness: Assumption 1 needs more discussion on how to verify it in practice.
- Weakness: q-bit compressive sensing section feels underdeveloped and lacks clear practical benefits.
- Weakness: heavy reliance on the appendix for technical details makes quick assessment harder.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, precise theoretical result with strong technical grounding and promising applications.
- Argument against acceptance: limited generality due to Gaussian assumption, superficial q-bit application, and appendix dependence.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; limitations do not outweigh strengths.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: discuss extensions to non-Gaussian measurements, clarify verification of Assumption 1, and strengthen/practice-justify the q-bit compressive sensing section.
