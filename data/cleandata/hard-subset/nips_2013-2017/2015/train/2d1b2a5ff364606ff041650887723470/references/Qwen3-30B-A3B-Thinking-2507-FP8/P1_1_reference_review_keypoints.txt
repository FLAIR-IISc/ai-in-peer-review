- Establishes asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and with scaled linear measurements y_lin = μAx₀ + σz.  
- Provides precise asymptotic error formula (Theorem 2.2) and proves the main equivalence (Theorem 2.1).  
- Specializes results to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery (including Theorem 2.3).  
- Applies theory to q-bit compressive sensing and shows how to optimize quantization thresholds.  
- Builds on and extends prior precise analyses of Generalized LASSO and generalizes Plan & Vershynin’s constrained results to the regularized setting.  
- Technically rigorous with well-motivated proofs and numerical experiments validating theoretical predictions.  
- Delivers precise error predictions rather than loose upper bounds, allowing better tuning of regularization λ.  
- Novel insight: equivalence between non-linear and appropriately scaled linear measurements.  
- Significant practical implications for 1-bit quantization, censored data, and other non-linear measurement settings.  
- Well-organized and clearly written; main theorems and special cases are accessible.  
- Some notational inconsistencies (e.g., definition of μ) that could confuse readers.  
- Relies on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrix and assumes known μ and σ², limiting generality.  
- Lacks discussion on estimating μ and σ² when g is unknown and on extensions to non-Gaussian measurement models.  
- Limited guidance on practical implementation, computational complexity, and solving the optimization in equation (19) at scale.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix notation and expand practical implementation/estimation discussion.
