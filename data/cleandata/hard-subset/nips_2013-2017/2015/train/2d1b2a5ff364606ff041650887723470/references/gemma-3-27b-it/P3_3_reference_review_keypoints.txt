- Overall: Significant theoretical result showing LASSO on non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a scaled, noisy linear model; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Core contribution: Precise asymptotic characterization of generalized LASSO error for broad non-linear link functions in high-dimensional (m/n constant) regimes.  
- Equivalence finding: LASSO behaves as if measurements are scaled by µ with noise variance σ² determined by the link function g.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends and sharpens earlier consistency results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) and leverages recent precise LASSO error analyses.  
- Generality: Results apply to many link functions (including 1-bit and Tobit) under standard Gaussian measurement vectors.  
- Precision: Provides exact asymptotic error expressions rather than just upper bounds, enabling better performance understanding.  
- Clarity & presentation: Well-written, clear notation, organized exposition, and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Illustrative examples: Demonstrates applicability to sparse and low-rank recovery and discusses q-bit compressive sensing with Lloyd–Max.  
- Main weakness — asymptotic focus: Results are asymptotic, limiting direct relevance for small n or m; practical implications should be discussed.  
- Main weakness — unit-norm assumption: Assuming x0 has unit norm simplifies analysis; authors should explicitly show how to generalize.  
- Main weakness — computing F: Practical calculation of F in Assumption 1 for arbitrary regularizers is challenging and needs discussion of approximations.  
- Secondary weakness — Lloyd–Max connection: The q-bit/Lloyd–Max discussion feels underdeveloped and could be framed as future work.  
- Detailed comments/questions: Clarify Assumption 1 with concrete regularizer examples; relate Theorem 2.3 conditions to empirical distribution convergence; state computational cost scaling; give practitioner guidelines for link/regularizer choice.  
- For acceptance: Novel, rigorous, well-positioned contribution with broad theoretical impact.  
- Against acceptance: Primarily theoretical and asymptotic, which may limit immediate practical utility.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address asymptotic limitations and generalize the unit-norm assumption.
