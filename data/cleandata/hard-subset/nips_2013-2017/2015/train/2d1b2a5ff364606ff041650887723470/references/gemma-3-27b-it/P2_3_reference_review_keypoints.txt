- Main result: Proves that LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with scaled, noisy linear measurements in high-dimensional Gaussian settings, with explicit µ and σ² depending on g.  
- Theorem: Provides a precise error characterization (Theorem 2.2) and links performance to a specific optimization problem.  
- Empirical support: Simulations show the theoretical predictions match observed performance; application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrated.  
- Novelty/significance: The equivalence result is original and simplifies analyzing LASSO under non-linearities, enabling reuse of linear LASSO theory.  
- Theoretical rigor: Assumptions and proofs are carefully stated and technically solid (proofs in appendix).  
- Generality: Analysis covers sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery, indicating broad applicability.  
- Literature positioning: Thorough, clear comparison to prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) and newer LASSO analyses.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized overall, though some sections are dense and require careful reading.  
- Weakness — Assumption 1: The assumption is opaque and needs clearer explanation and examples of practical settings where it holds.  
- Weakness — Computation: The optimization in Theorem 2.2 may be difficult to solve in practice; more discussion of approximation/solution strategies is needed.  
- Weakness — Empirics limited: Simulation scope is narrow; testing more nonlinear functions g and varied settings would strengthen validation.  
- Weakness — Gaussian focus: Heavy reliance on Gaussian measurement vectors; robustness to non-Gaussian designs is not addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, rigorous theoretical contribution with practical relevance (q-bit sensing) and clear statement of limitations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Potential restrictiveness of Assumption 1, computational intractability of the error formula, and limited empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant theoretical impact; minor weaknesses do not outweigh contributions.  
- Recommendation and required revisions: Accept with minor revisions — clarify Assumption 1, discuss computational strategies for Theorem 2.2, and expand empirical evaluation to more g functions.
