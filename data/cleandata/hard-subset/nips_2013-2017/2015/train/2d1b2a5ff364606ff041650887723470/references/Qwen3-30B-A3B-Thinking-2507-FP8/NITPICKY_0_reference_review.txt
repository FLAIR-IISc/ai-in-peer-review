

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper claims to establish an equivalence between LASSO estimation under non-linear measurements and a linear measurement model with appropriately scaled and noisy measurements. The main result (Theorem 2.1) states that the asymptotic error of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is identical to that of the same algorithm applied to scaled linear measurements with parameters μ and σ defined in (3).

**Strengths:** The paper makes a theoretically interesting contribution by connecting non-linear measurement models to linear ones. The authors build on recent work by Plan and Vershynin [17] and extend their results to the regularized LASSO case with precise error characterization. The extension to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery (Theorems 2.2-2.3) demonstrates the applicability of their framework.

**Major Technical Concerns:**

1. **Ambiguous Notation and Definitions:** The paper defines μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²] for γ ~ N(0,1), but fails to clarify whether g is a random function (as stated in the text) or a fixed function. The expectation should explicitly account for the randomness in g, but the notation is ambiguous throughout.

2. **Critical Condition Not Justified:** The paper assumes μ ≠ 0 (in equation 3 and Theorem 2.1) but provides no justification for why this condition is necessary or how the results would change if μ = 0. This is particularly problematic since the paper states that "x0 can, in principle, be estimated only up to a constant of proportionality" when g is unspecified, which implies μ could be zero.

3. **Asymptotic Statement Misrepresentation:** Theorem 2.1 claims "‖x̂-μx0‖²² = ‖x̂lin-μx0‖²²" in the limit, but this is misleading. The equality only holds asymptotically in the limit as n→∞, not for finite n. The phrasing suggests an exact equality, which is not the case.

4. **Inconsistent Regularizer Definition:** In the group-sparse section, they define f(x) = ∑t i=1 ‖[x0]i‖2, but this should be f(x) = ∑t i=1 ‖[x]i‖2 since the regularizer is applied to the estimate x, not the true signal x0.

5. **Error in q-bit Compressive Sensing Analysis:** In equation (20), they claim σ² = τ² - μ² where τ² = 2∑`i²·(Q(ti-1)-Q(ti)). However, for a quantizer, the variance should be E[g(γ)²] - μ², not τ² - μ². The quantity τ² as defined is not the second moment of g(γ), creating a fundamental error in their analysis.

6. **Inconsistent Terminology:** The paper alternates between "Generalized LASSO" and simply "LASSO" without clear justification. In section 1, they state they "often drop the term 'Generalized' and refer to (2) simply as the LASSO," yet they continue to use "Generalized LASSO" throughout the rest of the paper.

**Minor Concerns:**

- The paper claims to be "the first to derive asymptotically precise results on the performance of any LASSO-type program" for non-linear measurements, but doesn't properly acknowledge related work that may have addressed similar problems (e.g., [24] for binary measurements).

- The reference list contains incomplete citations (missing page numbers, conference details).

- In Theorem 2.3, they define λcrit = 0 for δ > 1, which seems counterintuitive and requires clarification.

**Conclusion:**

While the paper addresses an interesting theoretical question and builds on previous work, it contains multiple critical technical errors that undermine the main claims. The ambiguity in the definition of μ and σ, the unjustified assumption μ ≠ 0, the asymptotic statement misrepresentation, and the fundamental error in the q-bit analysis are particularly concerning. The paper would benefit from a complete revision to clarify these points and correct the mathematical errors.

As a perfectionist reviewer, I find these technical inconsistencies too significant to overlook. While the core idea has potential, the current presentation contains too many fundamental errors to be accepted at NIPS.

**Recommendation:** Reject.
