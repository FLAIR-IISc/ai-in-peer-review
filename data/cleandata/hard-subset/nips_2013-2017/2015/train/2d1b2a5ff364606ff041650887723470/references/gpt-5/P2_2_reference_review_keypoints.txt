- Central claim: For Gaussian designs, the asymptotic error of generalized LASSO under non-linear single-index measurements equals that under an effective linear model with µ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−µγ)^2]; Theorems 2.1–2.2 give a single-letter scalar convex characterization of the squared error.  
- Specializations and applications: Explicit formulas for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2 regularizers, sketch for nuclear norm, and application to q-bit quantized compressed sensing showing σ^2/µ^2 as a design objective and LM fixed points as stationary.  
- Novelty: First asymptotically exact characterization of generalized LASSO error with non-linear measurements and unknown link for general convex regularizers.  
- Technical soundness: Rigorous CGMT-based analysis consistent with prior precise LASSO work; verifies Assumption 1 for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2; sparse specialization matches simulations.  
- Key assumptions and scope: Relies on Gaussian measurement matrices, finite µ and σ^2 with µ≠0, and asymptotic regime m,n→∞ with m/n→δ; excludes direction-only links like sign without modification.  
- Empirical evaluation: Simulations for sparse and group-sparse settings closely match theory; quantized CS example compelling; low-rank empirical validation is missing.  
- Clarity and exposition: Generally clear with good literature context, but contains typos/notation inconsistencies and Theorem 2.1 should explicitly state limits coincide a.s., not pathwise equality.  
- Assumption 1 presentation: Technical and abstract; recommend a short table of common regularizers/signal priors with explicit F and a worked nuclear-norm example.  
- Tuning λ: The role of λ and λcrit is insightful, but paper should give practical guidance for choosing λ (e.g., via fixed-point equations or estimation of µ,σ from data).  
- Originality assessment: High — extends prior heuristics/upper-bounds to exact asymptotics and general convex f; novel link between σ^2/µ^2 and quantizer design.  
- Significance assessment: High — provides practitioners a principled effective-SNR view under link misspecification and tools for tuning/design (quantization, thresholds, λ).  
- Pros summary: Conceptually clean equivalence, precise scalar error characterization, covers general convex regularizers, practical quantizer design insight, well-contextualized with matching simulations.  
- Cons summary: Restricted to Gaussian designs, Assumption 1 is abstract (needs more concrete verification for nuclear norm), excludes µ=0 links, asymptotic focus with limited finite-sample and low-rank experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: Clarify Theorem 2.1 and µ≠0 dependence; add explicit F and simulation for nuclear norm; discuss/experiment with non-Gaussian designs or justify Gaussianity; provide λ-selection guidance; fix typos/terminology.  
- Overall verdict: Technically solid, original, and significant contribution that with minor revisions meets NeurIPS standards; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
