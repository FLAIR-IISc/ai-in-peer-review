- Main result: Proves that in high dimensions LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with scaled noisy linear measurements, with µ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-µγ)²].

- Technical quality: Rigorous, technically sound analysis using CGMT with complete proofs and precise error formulas for structured signals.

- Assumptions: Clear statement of assumptions, but limited discussion on robustness to violating i.i.d. Gaussian measurements and unit-norm signal assumptions.

- Numerical validation: Figures convincingly demonstrate close match between theory and simulations across settings.

- Practical application: Shows how results inform optimal q-bit compressive sensing quantization design.

- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized, consistent notation, and helpful figures making results accessible to experts.

- Originality: Novel and elegant insight treating non-linear measurements as scaled/noisy linear ones; extends precise error analysis beyond sparse to group-sparse and low-rank cases.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on and distinct from Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin by providing precise asymptotic characterizations for regularized LASSO.

- Significance: Theoretically meaningful and practically useful; advances analysis of high-dimensional statistical problems and impacts signal processing and ML.

- Strength: Provides precise theoretical characterization for a wide class of regularizers and link functions.

- Strength: Applies results to multiple structured-signal settings (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank).

- Strength: Demonstrates practical utility via q-bit compressive sensing example.

- Strength: Uses well-established CGMT framework and supplies complete proofs.

- Strength: Well-presented with clear assumptions and theorems.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of implications when i.i.d. Gaussian measurement assumption is relaxed.

- Weakness: Limited comparison or discussion of alternative methods for non-linear measurements (e.g., binary-measurement methods).

- Weakness: Little discussion of computational aspects and practical implementation complexity.

- Weakness: Does not thoroughly relate to specialized methods proposed for certain measurement types (e.g., binary).

- Recommendation: Strong accept — recommend acceptance at NIPS.

- Additional suggestion: Add brief discussion of computational complexity and explicitly relate results to other recent non-linear measurement methods (e.g., binary measurement literature).
