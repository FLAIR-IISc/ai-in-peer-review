- Problem and method: Studies high-dimensional single-index model y_i = g(a_i^T x0) with Gaussian A and proposes generalized LASSO (ℓ2 loss + convex regularizer).  
- Main result (Thm 2.1): Asymptotic equivalence—LASSO error with nonlinear observations equals error for an effective linear model y = μAx0 + σz with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Precise risk (Thm 2.2): CGMT yields a scalar convex optimization predicting asymptotic error for general convex regularizers via a function F capturing the regularizer and signal law.  
- Special cases: Provides concrete formulas for sparse and group-sparse recovery and discusses (but does not fully work out) the low-rank/nuclear-norm case.  
- Application (quantization): Reduces optimal q-bit quantizer design for LASSO to minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and suggests Lloyd–Max as a practical method.  
- Relation to Brillinger: Extends Brillinger’s fixed-d, m→∞ LS scaling result to proportional high-dimensional regimes and to regularized LASSO.  
- Relation to Plan & Vershynin: Goes beyond their non-asymptotic bounds by providing asymptotically exact risks and precise dependence on (μ,σ).  
- Relation to AMP/CGMT literature: Leverages CGMT to extend precise LASSO analyses for linear measurements to the nonlinear link setting; connects to Bussgang intuition.  
- Conceptual strength: Provides a clean, general “nonlinear = linear with effective (μ,σ)” equivalence that clarifies folklore.  
- Technical strength: Offers a low-dimensional, convex characterization of asymptotic risk applicable to a family of convex regularizers.  
- Practical significance: Gives guidance for λ tuning and quantizer design, enabling transfer of linear LASSO insights to nonlinear problems.  
- Assumption 1 concern: Generality depends on verifying the limit defining F; nuclear-norm/low-rank case is sketched but needs a fully worked example.  
- Theorem 2.1 wording: Must clarify convergence claim—state equality of almost-sure limits of errors rather than equality of finite-n errors.  
- μ = 0 edge cases: Paper assumes μ ≠ 0 but does not fully delineate boundary cases or provide concrete alternatives when μ = 0.  
- Monotonicity claim: The quantizer-design objective (minimize σ^2/μ^2) needs an explicit monotonicity result or lemma linking risk to effective noise variance.  
- Experimental limitations: Experiments are limited; need broader validation across link functions (sign, ReLU, logistic), regularizers, λ sensitivity, and comparisons of LM vs uniform quantizers.  
- Missing discussion: Should contrast with generalized AMP/GAMP for nonlinear channels and clarify differences from model-based GAMP predictions.  
- Notational/typo issues: Minor typos/confusing footnotes, ambiguous statements (e.g., “do not require μ = 0”), and need clearer normalization/scaling and intuition for parameters like β in (10).  
- Quality assessment: Theory appears sound and consistent with known linear cases; sparse case is solid, but low-rank treatment and experiments need strengthening.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally readable for experts but would benefit from more intuition for (10), a CGMT sketch, and guidance for verifying Assumption 1.  
- Originality: High—novel asymptotically exact equivalence between nonlinear and effective linear models for generalized LASSO.  
- Significance: High—useful for researchers and practitioners dealing with mismatched links, quantization, and high-dimensional estimation.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel, impactful equivalence; precise risk formulas; unifies prior work; practical quantizer design insight.  
- Cons for acceptance: Heavy reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian designs, incomplete Assumption 1/low-rank treatment, and limited experiments.  
- Questions for authors: Can results extend to sub-Gaussian/rotational designs; are there simple sufficient conditions for uniqueness of α* in (10); provide a concrete low-rank F and numerics; can you formalize risk monotonicity in σ^2/μ^2?  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (leaning).
