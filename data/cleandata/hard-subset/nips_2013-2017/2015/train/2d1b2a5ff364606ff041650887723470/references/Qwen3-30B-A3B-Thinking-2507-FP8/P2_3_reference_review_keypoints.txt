- Summary: Establishes asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO on non-linear measurements and LASSO on appropriately scaled/noisy linear measurements, with precise error formulas and q-bit compressive sensing application.

- Quality: Rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems 2.1/2.2) that builds on prior work; assumptions include i.i.d. Gaussian measurements and bounded nonzero μ,σ.

- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized, with consistent notation, precise theorem statements, and figures validating theory.

- Originality: First asymptotically precise error analysis for LASSO with non-linear measurements; novel insight that non-linear links reduce to scaling μ and noise σ².

- Significance: Substantial impact—shows LASSO needs no algorithmic change for non-linear measurements if scaling/noise are accounted for, informing parameter selection and hardware design.

- Strengths: Rigorous error characterization, clear model connection, practical q-bit optimization, comprehensive experiments, and meaningful extension of prior results.

- Weaknesses: Relies on Assumption 1 (Moreau envelope limit), excludes even link functions (μ = 0), and focuses on asymptotic regime (n → ∞).

- Recommendation: Strong accept (NIPS) — technically sound, original, clearly presented, and practically valuable.
