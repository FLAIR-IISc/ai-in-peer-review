- Main claim: Asymptotic equivalence — LASSO with nonlinear single-index measurements behaves like LASSO with linear measurements y_lin = μ A x0 + σ z, with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].
- Theorem 2.1: Proves nonlinear = linear equivalence of asymptotic MSE for Gaussian A.
- Theorem 2.2: Reduces limiting MSE to a deterministic scalar convex program involving δ, λ, μ, σ and a functional F from the Moreau envelope of f*.
- Specializations: Explicit solutions for ℓ1, group-sparse, and partial low-rank settings, including δ>1 and δ<1 regimes and a λ_crit phase transition (Theorem 2.3 for ℓ1).
- Application: Shows LASSO error minimization is equivalent to minimizing σ^2/μ^2, and argues Lloyd–Max quantizer design yields stationary points for that objective.
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger to high-dimensional scaling, sharpens Plan–Vershynin by giving exact asymptotics, and complements AMP-related linear-results via CGMT for nonlinear measurements.
- Strength: Clear conceptual message and broad applicability across convex regularizers with a rigorous CGMT-based derivation and tractable scalar optimization.
- Strength: New precise ℓ1 results for non-Gaussian coefficients and characterization of λ_crit behavior.
- Weakness: Assumption 1 (limit of scaled Moreau-envelope functional) is abstract and only partially validated beyond ℓ1; low-rank case underdeveloped and lacks experiments.
- Weakness: Theorem 2.1 wording should clarify equality of limiting values rather than random errors themselves.
- Weakness: Analysis restricted to i.i.d. Gaussian A; robustness to sub-Gaussian or structured sensing is not addressed.
- Weakness: Framework excludes μ=0 cases (e.g., even nonlinearities); no extension via higher-order statistics or alternative estimators is provided.
- Weakness: Practical calibration left open — when g is unknown, μ and σ^2 are not estimated or discussed empirically.
- Weakness: Quantizer-design section is interesting but light on experiments and comparisons; finite-sample accuracy and convergence rates are not quantified.
- Suggestions: Clarify Theorem 2.1, expand Assumption 1 with examples/tables for F, add low-rank and more nonlinearity experiments, discuss extensions beyond Gaussian A and μ=0, and include methods to estimate μ and σ^2 from data.
- Overall assessment: High originality, strong theoretical quality and significance; recommendation: Accept (strong).
