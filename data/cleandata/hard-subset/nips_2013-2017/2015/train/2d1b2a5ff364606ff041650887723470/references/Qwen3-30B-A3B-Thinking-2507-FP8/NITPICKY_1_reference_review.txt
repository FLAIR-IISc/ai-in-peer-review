

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

## Summary
This paper investigates the equivalence between LASSO estimation with non-linear measurements (yi = gi(a_i^T x0)) and linear measurements (yi = μa_i^T x0 + σzi), where μ and σ are defined in terms of the link function g. The authors prove that the asymptotic estimation error of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements matches that of the same algorithm with appropriately scaled linear measurements. The paper establishes precise error formulas for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery, generalizing previous work by Plan and Vershynin [17] and others.

## Strengths
The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by extending precise error analysis from linear to non-linear measurement models. The key insight that non-linear measurements can be asymptotically treated as scaled linear measurements with appropriate noise parameters is valuable. The paper provides a general framework applicable to various regularizers (including sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank structures) and derives precise error formulas that generalize previous results for linear measurements. The application to q-bit compressive sensing is particularly insightful and demonstrates the practical utility of the theoretical framework.

## Weaknesses

### Formatting and Notational Issues
1. The title contains a hyphen inconsistency: "Non-linear" vs. standard NIPS usage of "nonlinear" without hyphen.
2. Inconsistent capitalization of "LASSO" throughout the paper (e.g., "LASSO" vs. "lasso").
3. "mesaurements" appears as a typo in section 1.1.
4. The reference format is inconsistent (e.g., "Asymptotically exact error analysis for the generalized `22-lasso" should be "generalized `2-lasso" or "generalized squared-error LASSO").

### Technical Ambiguities
1. The paper claims "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements" but fails to precisely define "equivalent" in the title. The equivalence is asymptotic and in terms of error behavior, not measurement equivalence. This should be clarified immediately in the abstract.

2. In Theorem 2.1, the phrase "with probability one" lacks specification of the random variables (A, x0, g). The paper should explicitly state "with probability one over the randomness of A, x0, and g."

3. The paper states "for a wide class of functions f and g" but never defines this class. This is critical for understanding the scope of the result.

4. The notation in Theorem 2.2 is confusing: "x̂(n) := min x 1√ n { ‖y(n) −A(n)x‖2 + λf (n)(x) }" - it's unclear whether the 1/√n factor is inside or outside the minimization. The paper should clarify this notation.

5. In the sparse recovery section, the paper uses "x0 = x0/‖x0‖2" which creates confusing notation as it reuses x0 for two different quantities. This should be rewritten with a different symbol (e.g., x̃0 = x0/‖x0‖2).

6. The paper states "µ := E[γg(γ)]" without specifying that γ ~ N(0,1). While this is clear from context, it should be explicitly stated.

### Methodological Concerns
1. The claim "the same is true when g(x) = sign(x), eventhough g might be known here" contains a typo ("eventhough" should be "even though").

2. In the introduction, the paper states "the first term of the objective function in (2) tries to fit Ax to the observed vector y presuming that this is of the form yi = aTi x0+noise" - this is misleading. The LASSO minimizes the squared error, not the linear model directly.

3. The paper claims "for all values of λ" but doesn't address the behavior when λ approaches 0 or ∞, which could be important for practical implementation.

4. The relationship to Plan and Vershynin [17] is not clearly articulated. The paper states "Our result can be viewed as a generalization of the above in several directions" but doesn't explicitly state how it differs from [17] in a way that's immediately clear to the reader.

5. In section 2.3.2, the paper states "σ2 := σ2(`, t) := τ2 − µ2" without defining τ2. The paper should explicitly state that τ2 = 2Σ`i^2·(Q(ti-1)-Q(ti)) as defined earlier.

## Overall Assessment
While the paper presents a theoretically interesting extension of previous work on LASSO with linear measurements to non-linear measurements, the numerous technical ambiguities, notational inconsistencies, and formatting errors significantly undermine the paper's clarity and precision. The core result is valuable, but the presentation lacks the rigor expected in a NIPS paper. The paper's contribution is significant, but the current presentation fails to clearly articulate the novelty and scope of the result.

The paper would benefit from:
1. Correcting all notational inconsistencies
2. Explicitly defining all technical terms and assumptions
3. Clarifying the precise meaning of "equivalence" throughout
4. Improving the presentation of the relationship to previous work
5. Addressing the technical ambiguities identified above

Given the numerous technical and presentation issues that affect the clarity and rigor of the paper, I recommend rejection. While the core idea is potentially interesting, the current version does not meet the high standards expected for publication at NIPS. The paper would require substantial revision to address these issues before it could be considered for acceptance.
