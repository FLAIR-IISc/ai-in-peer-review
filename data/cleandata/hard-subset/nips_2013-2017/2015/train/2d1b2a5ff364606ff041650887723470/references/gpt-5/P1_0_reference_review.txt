Summary
The paper studies recovery of structured signals from non-linear single-index measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0) using the Generalized LASSO (ℓ2 data-fit plus convex regularizer). Its main result shows a precise and striking equivalence: for Gaussian sensing matrices, the asymptotic mean-squared error (MSE) of the LASSO with non-linear measurements is identical to that of the same LASSO run on an equivalent linear model with effective gain and noise given by µ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − µγ)^2], γ ~ N(0,1). Thus, asymptotically, the LASSO “sees” y as ylin = µ A x0 + σ z regardless of the specific nonlinearity, provided µ ≠ 0.

Building on the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT), the authors provide:
- Theorem 2.1: equality of the asymptotic MSEs of the non-linear and matched linear problems.
- Theorem 2.2: an exact scalar convex program in three variables whose optimizer α* gives the asymptotic MSE ‖x̂ − µx0‖^2 → α*^2, encapsulating δ = m/n, λ, the nonlinearity via (µ, σ^2), and the signal structure and regularizer via a functional F defined through the Moreau envelope of f*.
- Specializations: precise formulas for sparse recovery with ℓ1-regularization (Theorem 2.3), and extensions to group-sparse (ℓ1,2) and a framework for low-rank (nuclear norm).
- Application: q-bit quantized compressed sensing; they show that minimizing σ^2/µ^2 over thresholds/levels optimizes asymptotic LASSO MSE and justify Lloyd–Max as a stationary-point method for this objective.

Relation to prior work
This generalizes classical results of Brillinger (LS under single-index models for fixed n, m→∞) and sharpens Plan–Vershynin’s non-asymptotic bounds for constrained LASSO under non-linear measurements by providing asymptotically exact error expressions for the regularized LASSO. It leverages recent precise LASSO analyses for linear models via AMP/CGMT (e.g., Bayati–Montanari, Stojnic, Oymak–Thrampoulidis–Hassibi, Thrampoulidis et al.), but extends them to a broad class of non-linearities.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The approach and conclusions are well supported. The decomposition of g(a^T x0) into an effective linear component plus uncorrelated noise is valid for Gaussian A, and the CGMT framework is appropriate for deriving exact asymptotics. The sparse case derivation recovers and extends known ℓ2-LASSO formulas, lending credibility.
- Assumptions: The Gaussian design and µ ≠ 0 are clearly stated. Assumption 1 (existence of the limit defining F) is nontrivial but justified and verified for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2; for nuclear norm, conditions and computation of F are sketched but not fully developed. The requirement of unit-norm x0 is discussed and removable by reparametrization.
- Completeness: The core theory is complete for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2. The low-rank section outlines how to apply the framework but defers evaluation of F; this is a minor gap. The quantization application is thoughtful; connecting LM to minimizing σ^2/µ^2 is a nice byproduct.
- Experiments: Figures (not included here) reportedly validate predictions across nonlinearities and structures. It would help to include more quantitative comparisons, especially for group-sparse/low-rank and across λ and δ regimes.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well organized, with a helpful literature discussion. Some suggestions:
- Theorem 2.1 currently reads as almost-sure equality of errors; it should be stated as convergence of the errors (limits coincide) with probability one to avoid implying finite-n equality.
- Assumption 1 and F: provide more intuition, examples, and a short recipe for computing F for common f (e.g., fused-LASSO).
- Notation/typos: fix minor typos (“mesaurements”, “redifining”), unify scaling conventions in (2) vs (9), and clarify the role of the 1/√n factor.
- Explicitly state how µ, σ^2 are “bounded” (uniformly in n) and clarify what happens if µ is small.

Originality
High. To my knowledge this is the first asymptotically exact characterization of generalized LASSO error under broad non-linear single-index measurements, unifying many nonlinearities by two scalar parameters (µ, σ^2). It significantly strengthens Plan–Vershynin’s bounds and extends precise linear-model LASSO theory to a new regime.

Significance
High. The equivalence result is conceptually impactful and practically useful: any precise error prediction for the linear LASSO can be ported to the nonlinear single-index setting once (µ, σ^2) are known or estimated. The quantization design application illustrates practical utility. This work will likely influence both theory (extensions to other convex programs, other structures) and practice (robust use of LASSO under link misspecification).

Pros
- Elegant and powerful equivalence: reduces a broad nonlinear setting to a linear surrogate.
- Precise, computable asymptotic error formulas; general regularizers via F.
- Strong connections to and extensions of influential prior work (Brillinger; Plan–Vershynin; CGMT).
- Practical insight for quantizer design; LM justification for minimizing σ^2/µ^2.
- Clear assumptions and thorough discussion.

Cons
- Gaussian measurement assumption; no treatment of sub-Gaussian/structured designs.
- Requires µ ≠ 0; the µ = 0 case (e.g., symmetric one-bit) excluded, though discussed.
- Assumption 1 is abstract; low-rank case left partially open.
- Experimental section could be broader; code or numerical recipe for solving (10) would aid reproducibility.
- Minor clarity/notation issues.

Questions for authors
1) Can the equivalence extend to sub-Gaussian i.i.d. A or rotationally invariant ensembles? Which steps of the proof fail without exact Gaussianity?
2) For µ = 0, can one recover a precise error characterization for direction-only estimators or via modified LASSO objectives?
3) For nuclear norm, can you provide a concrete example where F can be computed (e.g., spiked model with specified singular value distribution)?
4) In practice, if g is unknown, can µ and σ be estimated from data to rescale outputs, and how sensitive is performance to estimation error?
5) Provide guidance or code for numerically solving (10) and computing F for common f.

Minor issues/typos
- Several typos (“mesaurements”, “eventhough”, “redifining”, missing spaces/punctuation).
- Theorem 2.1 wording: clarify convergence vs equality.
- Define ζ earlier or remove if unused later.
- Clarify the role and interpretation of λcrit and the two regimes in Theorem 2.3.

Recommendation
Accept. This is a strong theoretical contribution with clear novelty and significance, well grounded in modern techniques, and with practical implications. Addressing clarity and expanding experiments would further strengthen the paper.
