- Main: Proves asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(ai^T x0) and LASSO with scaled linear measurements yi = μ(ai^T x0)+σ zi under i.i.d. Gaussian A and large n.
- Main: Provides precise error characterization (Theorem 2.2) via a convex optimization involving the Moreau envelope F, extending Plan & Vershynin’s loose bounds to regularized LASSO.
- Main: Applies results to sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery and analyzes q-bit compressive sensing for optimal quantizer design.

- Strength: Substantial theoretical contribution giving exact asymptotic equivalence and error formulas.
- Strength: Rigorous technical analysis using the CGMT framework with well-motivated assumptions.
- Strength: Novelty in providing the first precise error characterization for regularized LASSO and general convex regularizers.
- Strength: Strong practical implications for tuning and predicting performance, notably in q-bit CS.
- Strength: Clear, well-organized exposition and consistent notation.

- Weakness: Limited experimental validation and lack of detailed numerical comparisons or statistical tests.
- Weakness: Restrictive unit-norm assumption for x0 and dependence on μ ≠ 0 in some important cases.
- Weakness: Computing F (Moreau envelope) can be computationally challenging for general regularizers; no general guidance provided.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to alternative methods for non-linear measurements.

- Overall assessment: Strong, novel, and technically sound paper that merits acceptance despite empirical and assumption limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept.

- Pro: First precise characterization of Generalized LASSO error for non-linear measurements.
- Pro: Extends prior work from loose bounds to exact predictions for regularized LASSO.
- Pro: Derives error formulas for multiple structured recovery problems.
- Pro: Practical relevance for q-bit compressive sensing and parameter tuning.
- Pro: Rigorous proofs and clear presentation.

- Con: Empirical validation is limited and needs expansion.
- Con: Results depend on unit-norm assumption and μ ≠ 0, limiting applicability.
- Con: Lack of practical methods for evaluating F for arbitrary regularizers.
- Con: Does not sufficiently benchmark against competing methods for non-linear measurement problems.

- Suggestion: Add extensive experiments comparing predicted vs. empirical errors across various nonlinearities and signal structures.
- Suggestion: Discuss/relax the unit-norm assumption and address cases where μ = 0.
- Suggestion: Provide guidance, algorithms, or heuristics for computing F for general regularizers.
- Suggestion: Expand comparisons to alternative approaches for non-linear measurements (e.g., q-bit CS and recent optimal linear estimation work).
