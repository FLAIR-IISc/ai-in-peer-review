

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

## Summary
This paper investigates the behavior of the Generalized LASSO when applied to non-linear measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0), where gi is a non-linear link function. The authors establish that, under certain conditions (particularly when the measurement matrix has i.i.d. Gaussian entries), the estimation error of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to that of the LASSO with appropriately scaled and noisy linear measurements. Specifically, they show that non-linear measurements can be treated as linear measurements with scaling factor μ and noise variance σ², where μ and σ² are defined based on the link function g (equation 3). The paper provides precise error formulas for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signal recovery problems, and applies these results to q-bit compressive sensing.

## Strengths
1. **Theoretical Contribution**: The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by extending previous work by Plan and Vershynin [17] from loose upper bounds to precise asymptotic error predictions. This is an important advancement in the understanding of structured signal recovery under non-linear measurements.

2. **Technical Soundness**: The proofs of the main theorems are rigorous, and the authors provide thorough theoretical analysis with well-defined assumptions. The paper correctly references the relevant literature, including the seminal work by Brillinger [5] and recent advances in LASSO analysis.

3. **Practical Application**: The application to q-bit compressive sensing (Section 2.3) is particularly compelling. The authors demonstrate how their theoretical results can guide the optimal design of quantization thresholds and levels, providing a concrete practical use case for their theory.

4. **Comprehensive Coverage**: The paper successfully extends the analysis to multiple structured signal recovery problems (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank), providing new precise error formulas for each case. This breadth significantly enhances the paper's contribution.

## Weaknesses
1. **Strong Assumption on Signal Norm**: The paper assumes throughout that x0 has unit Euclidean norm (Footnote 4), which is a significant restriction. While the authors note that this can be relaxed by redefining g, they don't thoroughly address how this assumption affects the practical applicability of their results when the signal norm is unknown.

2. **Gaussian Measurement Matrix Assumption**: The analysis relies heavily on the measurement matrix having i.i.d. Gaussian entries. While this is common in high-dimensional statistics, the paper doesn't sufficiently discuss how the results would change for non-Gaussian measurement matrices, which are common in practice.

3. **Unknown Link Function**: The constants μ and σ² depend on the unknown link function g. While the paper acknowledges this, it doesn't provide a practical method to estimate these constants from data, limiting the immediate applicability of the results.

4. **Limited Discussion of Breakdown Conditions**: The paper doesn't sufficiently discuss the conditions under which the equivalence between non-linear and linear measurements breaks down. For instance, it would be valuable to know how the results change when the measurement vectors are not Gaussian or when the link function has certain properties.

## Originality and Significance
The paper makes a genuinely novel contribution by establishing the precise asymptotic equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement models for LASSO-type algorithms. This is a significant extension of previous work by Plan and Vershynin [17] and provides a theoretical foundation for using LASSO in non-linear settings. The significance is enhanced by the practical application to q-bit compressive sensing, which demonstrates how the theory can guide the design of optimal quantization schemes.

## Constructive Suggestions for Improvement
1. **Address the Signal Norm Assumption**: Provide a more detailed discussion of how the results change when x0 doesn't have unit norm, including a practical method to estimate the norm from data.

2. **Expand the Discussion of Measurement Matrix Assumptions**: Add a brief discussion of how the results might generalize to non-Gaussian measurement matrices, perhaps citing related work on robustness of LASSO to non-Gaussian measurements.

3. **Improve Practical Implementation Discussion**: Include a more detailed discussion of how to estimate μ and σ² from data, perhaps by suggesting methods like moment matching or using a small calibration set.

4. **Clarify Limitations**: Add a section discussing the limitations of the current analysis, particularly when the link function is unknown or when the measurement matrix is not Gaussian.

## Overall Assessment
This is a technically strong paper that makes a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of LASSO-type algorithms under non-linear measurements. The results are well-supported by rigorous analysis and have clear practical implications for applications like q-bit compressive sensing. While the paper has some limitations related to the assumptions made, these are common in high-dimensional statistical analysis and can be addressed in future work.

The paper is well-positioned to make an important contribution to the field of structured signal recovery. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limitations mentioned above. The work represents a clear advancement over previous results and provides a valuable theoretical foundation for using LASSO in non-linear measurement settings.
