

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper claims to establish a precise equivalence between the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and the same algorithm applied to scaled and noisy linear measurements. The authors extend the work of Plan and Vershynin [17] by providing asymptotically precise error analysis for the regularized LASSO with non-linear measurements, showing that the error behavior is equivalent to that of a linear measurement model with appropriate scaling and noise.

## Strengths
The paper makes a theoretically interesting contribution by extending the single-index model analysis to the regularized LASSO setting. The authors provide precise error formulas for several structured signal recovery problems (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank), which appear to be novel in the specific generality claimed. The connection between non-linear measurements and linear measurements through the parameters µ and σ² is conceptually valuable.

## Weaknesses

1. **Misleading Title and Overstated Claims**: The title claims "equivalence" which is misleading. The paper actually shows asymptotic equivalence in error behavior, not exact equivalence. The main claim in the abstract ("the LASSO treats non-linear measurements exactly as if they were scaled and noisy linear measurements") is incorrect; it's only asymptotically equivalent in the large n regime.

2. **Critical Notational Inconsistencies**: 
   - Equation (1) uses inconsistent notation "a T i x0" (should be "a_i^T x0")
   - In equation (3), the expectation is poorly defined (over what? γ and g?).
   - The paper switches between "µ" and "µ" in equations (3) and (4).
   - In equation (14), it's written as "κλ" in some places and "κλ" in others.

3. **Logical Flaws in Theorem Statements**:
   - Theorem 2.1 claims "‖x̂− µx0‖22 = ‖x̂lin − µx0‖22" but this is not equality (which would imply identical values) but asymptotic equivalence. The paper should use "asymptotically equal" or "converges to the same limit" instead of "=".
   - In 2.2.2, "If δ > 1, then define λcrit = 0" appears to be a typo. λcrit should be a non-trivial value.

4. **Inadequate Distinction from Prior Work**:
   - The paper claims to be "the first to derive asymptotically precise results on the performance of any LASSO-type program" for non-linear measurements, but [17] already established a constrained version of the result. The paper should acknowledge this and clarify how its precise error analysis differs from [17]'s upper bounds.
   - The reference to [20] as the "first precise analysis" for the `2-LASSO with Gaussian signals is misleading, as the paper doesn't properly distinguish its contribution from [20].

5. **Technical Errors**:
   - In 2.3.2, the paper states "σ2 := σ2(`, t) := τ2 − µ2" but τ2 is defined as "τ2 := τ2(`, t) = 2 L∑ i=1 `2i · (Q(ti−1)−Q(ti))" which doesn't match the standard definition of variance.
   - The claim that "the link-function might as well be unknown or unspecified" is problematic. If g is unknown, how do we determine µ and σ²? The paper doesn't address this critical issue.

6. **Formatting and Presentation Issues**:
   - Inconsistent use of "non-linear" vs "nonlinear".
   - Figure 1 is mentioned but not included in the text.
   - The references have inconsistent formatting (some with journal names, some without).
   - "m/n = δ ∈ (1,∞)" in equation (5) is inconsistent with δ ∈ (0,∞) elsewhere.

7. **Overstated Significance**: The paper claims "the first precise analysis result for the `2-LASSO stated in that generality" for sparse recovery, but the special case of Gaussian signals was already covered in [20]. The paper doesn't sufficiently distinguish its contribution from prior work.

## Recommendation

While the paper makes an interesting theoretical contribution, the numerous technical flaws, misleading claims, and notational inconsistencies significantly undermine its quality. The title's promise of "equivalence" is not justified, and the main result is misstated as an equality rather than asymptotic equivalence. The paper fails to properly acknowledge and distinguish from prior work by Plan and Vershynin [17], which already established the key conceptual insight about the constant of proportionality µ.

The paper's claims of novelty are overstated, and the technical execution contains multiple errors that would prevent reproducibility. Given NIPS's standards for rigorous theoretical work, these flaws are substantial enough to warrant rejection. The paper would benefit from a complete rewrite to correct these issues, clarify the asymptotic nature of the equivalence, and properly contextualize the contribution relative to prior work.

The paper is not suitable for publication at NIPS in its current form.
