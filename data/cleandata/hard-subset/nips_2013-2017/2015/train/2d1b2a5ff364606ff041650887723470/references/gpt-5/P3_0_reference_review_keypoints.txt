- Main result: Shows asymptotic equivalence between generalized LASSO with non-linear single-index measurements and LASSO on an effective linear model y_lin = µAx0 + σz, with µ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−µγ)^2].  
- Technical contribution: Derives an exact asymptotic MSE via a three-scalar convex program (Theorem 2.2) using CGMT-type techniques and provides explicit specializations for ℓ1, group ℓ1,2, and a roadmap for nuclear norm.  
- Application: Reduces q-bit quantizer design to minimizing σ^2/µ^2 and suggests using Lloyd–Max to find stationary points for that objective.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger to proportional asymptotics, sharpens Plan–Vershynin non-asymptotic bounds, and builds on precise LASSO analyses via AMP/CGMT while connecting to one-bit/quantized CS.  
- Strength — conceptual clarity: Provides a clean, rigorous reduction of non-linear single-index links to an effective linear model determined only by µ and σ^2.  
- Strength — technical depth: Offers a precise, low-dimensional convex characterization separating roles of δ, λ, g (through µ, σ^2) and the regularizer (through functional F).  
- Strength — generality: Applies to many convex regularizers and gives explicit derivations for important cases like ℓ1 and group sparsity.  
- Strength — significance: Unifies and strengthens several lines of work and gives actionable insights for front-end/quantizer design.  
- Weakness — Assumption 1 and F opaque: The Moreau-envelope-based functional F is not clearly instantiated for common penalties (ℓ1, nuclear norm); scaling/normalization and c3 dependence need reconciliation.  
- Weakness — Theorem 2.2 notation: Equation (10) has ambiguous parentheses/factors making reproduction difficult; needs precise typesetting.  
- Weakness — µ and σ^2 assumptions: Main results assume µ≠0 but manuscript is inconsistent about µ possibly being zero (even links); need explicit statements and discussion of µ=0 cases.  
- Weakness — Gaussian A scope: Results assume i.i.d. Gaussian design; authors should discuss robustness to sub-Gaussian or rotationally invariant matrices and which proof steps require exact Gaussianity.  
- Weakness — finite-sample behavior: No rates of convergence or concentration bounds are given; asymptotic claims would benefit from finite-sample guarantees.  
- Weakness — unknown g in practice: When g is unknown, computing µ and σ^2 is nontrivial; discuss estimation from data and whether plugging consistent estimates preserves asymptotics.  
- Weakness — experiments missing/incomplete: Manuscript refers to figures but contains none; needs experiments validating general claims and quantizer design (e.g., LM vs uniform).  
- Weakness — minor presentation issues: Typos, grammatical errors, inconsistent references, and incomplete citations hinder readability.  
- Questions for authors: Provide precise/Sketch proof of the linearization step (role of Gaussianity/Stein), explicit F and error predictions for group/low-rank examples, convexity/uniqueness conditions for the scalar program, and evidence on LM convergence to global minimizers for symmetric quantizers.  
- Pros: Elegant, unifying theoretical contribution with precise characterizations and potential high impact for practitioners.  
- Cons: Some technical assumptions uninstantiated (nuclear norm), presentation/notation issues, and limited empirical validation and robustness discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept (7/10) — technically solid and well-motivated; improve clarity on assumptions/equations and add examples/experiments.
