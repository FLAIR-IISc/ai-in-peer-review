- Main result: In high-dimensional proportional regime, generalized LASSO on non-linear single-index measurements has the same asymptotic squared error as LASSO on an equivalent linear model y_lin = μ A x0 + σ z, with μ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − μγ)^2].  
- Theorem 2.2: Provides an exact three-variable convex optimization whose unique solution α* equals the limiting error ‖x̂ − μ x0‖, capturing δ, λ, μ, σ^2, x0 structure, and the regularizer via a function F from the Moreau envelope of f*.  
- Specializations: Closed-form fixed-point equations for sparse (ℓ1), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and a framework for low-rank (nuclear norm) recovery; explicit λcrit and error expressions for ℓ1.  
- Relation to prior work: Generalizes Brillinger’s fixed-n LS misspecification results to proportional asymptotics and extends Plan–Vershynin’s non-asymptotic constrained LASSO bounds to precise asymptotics.  
- Application: Quantizer design for q-bit compressive sensing reduces to minimizing σ^2/μ^2; Lloyd–Max algorithm converges to stationary points of this objective.  
- Technical quality: Results are strong, positioned within AMP/CGMT analyses, consistent with known linear-measurement results, and assumptions are stated with concrete verifications for sparse cases.  
- Proof/assumption gaps: Many proof details deferred; Assumption 1 (existence of limiting F via Moreau envelope of f*) is abstract and not fully developed for low-rank case.  
- Empirical evaluation: Experiments match theory but scope/coverage unclear—finite-sample behavior and sensitivity to λ selection are not fully explored.  
- Clarity/exposition: Overall clear and well organized; contribution of μ and σ^2 is communicated crisply.  
- Notational issues: Unconventional scalings (e.g., 1/√n) are harmless but could use brief motivation.  
- Theorem statement: Theorem 2.1 should be rephrased to state convergence of errors/limits rather than equality of random quantities with probability one.  
- Typos and grammar: Several typos and minor grammatical errors need correction and consistent notation.  
- Originality: Equivalence principle for generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements in proportional regime and the unification via F are novel and useful.  
- Significance: Likely influential for theory and practice; reduction to μ and σ^2 provides immediate insight into effect of non-linearities and enables transfer of linear results.  
- Strengths: Conceptually clean equivalence, precise optimization-based error characterization for general convex regularizers, meaningful specializations, and practical quantizer-design link.  
- Weaknesses: Gaussian A assumption limits generality; requirement μ ≠ 0 excludes important even links (e.g., sign) without guidance; low-rank case lacks worked-out F; limited discussion on estimating μ, σ^2 from data.  
- Experimental weaknesses: Limited discussion on finite-n accuracy, λ selection guidance, robustness to g mismatch, and non-Gaussian A.  
- Suggested extensions: Consider rotationally invariant/sub-Gaussian designs, provide data-driven estimators for μ and σ^2, clarify convergence/uniqueness conditions, analyze μ = 0 regimes, and expand low-rank computations.  
- Practical suggestions: Add a section guiding λ selection (compute λcrit), include more experimental details (finite n, varied g, non-Gaussian A), and expand low-rank example (e.g., spiked model with Marchenko–Pastur).  
- Recommendation: Strong theoretical paper recommended for acceptance, contingent on clarifying Theorem 2.1, improving exposition, and expanding empirical and low-rank parts where feasible.
