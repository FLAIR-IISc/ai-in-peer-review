- Proves an exact asymptotic equivalence between Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and standard LASSO with scaled linear measurements y = µAx₀ + σz.  
- Provides a precise asymptotic error characterization (Theorem 2.2) reducible to a tractable three-scalar optimization.  
- Generalizes Brillinger’s single-index result and extends Plan & Vershynin from constrained to regularized LASSO with exact error formulas.  
- Demonstrates practical implications for q-bit compressive sensing, showing optimal quantization thresholds minimize σ²/µ².  
- Connects the quantizer optimization to the Lloyd–Max algorithm, offering a concrete design procedure.  
- Technically rigorous with careful proofs and clearly stated assumptions.  
- Honest about limitations, especially the asymptotic (n→∞) nature of the results.  
- Assumes unit-norm signal x₀, which is restrictive and only partially addressed via a footnote.  
- Assumes i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, limiting immediate applicability to other ensembles.  
- Limited empirical validation beyond the q-bit quantization example; broader practical evaluation is lacking.  
- Well-written, builds on relevant literature, and situates contributions clearly.  
- Significant theoretical contribution with clear implications for theory and measurement-system design.  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS).
