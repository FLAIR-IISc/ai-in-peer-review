- Main contribution: Proves an “equivalence principle” that LASSO on nonlinear single-index measurements has the same asymptotic MSE as LASSO on an effective linear model y_lin = µ A x0 + σ z with µ = E[γ g(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−µγ)^2].  
- Theorem 2.1: Nonasymptotic sequences with Gaussian A and m/n→δ yield limiting LASSO error under nonlinear g equal to that under the effective linear model.  
- Theorem 2.2: Provides an exact scalar convex optimization giving the limiting MSE ||x̂ − µ x0||^2 for general convex regularizers via a functional F involving Moreau envelopes.  
- Special cases: Derives explicit formulas/fixed-point equations for ℓ1 (sparse), group LASSO (block-sparse), and outlines a roadmap for low-rank (nuclear norm).  
- Application: Reduces q-bit quantizer design to minimizing σ^2/µ^2; shows Lloyd–Max finds stationary points of this objective.  
- Relation to Brillinger: Extends classical linearization (consistency up to scale) from fixed-n to high-dimensional asymptotics and from LS to regularized LASSO with precise MSE.  
- Relation to Plan & Vershynin: Tightens and complements prior nonasymptotic upper bounds by providing exact asymptotics and clarifying µ,σ roles.  
- Relation to AMP/CGMT literature: Builds on precise LASSO error analyses (AMP/CGMT) and generalizes them to nonlinear observations.  
- Conceptual quality: Results are technically sound, intuitive, and consistent with score-function identities; simulations match theory.  
- Assumptions: Requires i.i.d. Gaussian A, µ ≠ 0, m/n→δ, and a technical Assumption 1 on Moreau envelope limits (verified for common structures).  
- Completeness: Main proofs are deferred to appendices; sparse and group-sparse derivations given; low-rank case remains sketchy and computationally unresolved.  
- Clarity: Well-organized presentation, but Theorem 2.1 phrasing and Assumption 1 need clearer statements; more algorithmic guidance would aid reproducibility.  
- Originality: The precise equivalence principle and asymptotic characterization for regularized LASSO under nonlinear measurements are novel and nontrivial.  
- Significance: Conceptually unifying and practically useful for tuning λ and quantizer design; applicable across multiple structures.  
- Scope limitation: Restricted to i.i.d. Gaussian A; extensions to rotationally invariant or sub-Gaussian designs are not addressed.  
- Identifiability concern: µ may be zero for some g, making direction recovery impossible; paper should discuss µ=0 regimes and alternatives.  
- Finite-sample issue: Paper lacks nonasymptotic deviation bounds or finite-n calibration guidance for practitioners.  
- Estimation of µ,σ: Practical methods for estimating µ and σ from data are not provided; suggestion to propose moment-based or pilot-data estimators.  
- Low-rank gap: Low-rank specialization is left partly abstract with no concrete F evaluation example; a spiked model instance is recommended.  
- Minor clarity fixes: Clarify variables/domains in Theorem 2.2, provide code/pseudocode for computing F and solving (10), and improve figure captions.  
- Pros: Strong novel theory, general across convex regularizers, elegant equivalence, practical implications, and solid validation.  
- Cons: Gaussian A and asymptotic focus, Assumption 1 needs broader justification, low-rank case underdeveloped, and missing practical guidance on µ/σ estimation.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept to accept — significant, well-founded advance that would benefit from addressing clarity and applicability concerns.
