This paper investigates the equivalence of LASSO performance with nonlinear measurements to that of scaled and noisy linear measurements. The core contribution lies in establishing a precise asymptotic error expression that captures the influence of the link function, signal structure, regularizer, and regularization parameter. While the idea of extending linear LASSO analysis to nonlinear settings is natural and valuable, the presentation suffers from a high level of technical detail and, crucially, a lack of substantial novelty beyond extending known results to a slightly broader setting. The numerous references to prior work indicate a well-aware author base, but the lack of concise summarization and clear differentiation from established results obscures the paper's actual contribution.

**Strengths:**

*   **Rigorous Analysis:** The paper employs a sophisticated mathematical framework to derive precise error bounds, building on existing results in high-dimensional statistics and compressive sensing.
*   **General Framework:** The authors aim for a general treatment, encompassing various link functions and regularizers, demonstrating a broad scope.
*   **Connection to Existing Work:** The paper explicitly links its findings to established results by Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin, and others, providing context and acknowledging related contributions.
*   **Practical Implications:** The discussion of q-bit compressive sensing and the application of the Lloyd-Max algorithm provide a potential avenue for practical application.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Incremental Contribution:** While technically sound, the core result – that nonlinear measurements behave like scaled linear measurements – feels like a natural extension of existing work and lacks a truly groundbreaking insight. The mathematical complexity overshadows the fundamental idea.
*   **Overly Detailed Presentation:** The paper is exceedingly detailed, including many technical derivations and intermediate results that could be relegated to the appendix. This makes it difficult to grasp the key contributions without significant effort. The sheer density of equations and notation is overwhelming.
*   **Lack of Concise Summary:** The introduction, while thorough, lacks a concise summary of the key result that a reader could grasp quickly. The connection between the theoretical analysis and practical implications is also not sufficiently emphasized.
*   **Formatting Inconsistencies:** The formatting could be improved. For example, the usage of "e.g." and the placement of citations within sentences is inconsistent. Footnotes are used extensively, which interrupts the flow of reading.
*   **Assumptions & Conditions:** The number of assumptions required to arrive at the final results is substantial (e.g., Gaussian measurements, unit norm signals, specific conditions on the regularizer and its conjugate). While necessary for rigorous analysis, they limit the applicability of the findings and are not always clearly justified.
*   **Limited Empirical Validation:** While Figure 1 is mentioned, it is not included in the provided excerpt. The reliance on theoretical analysis without more substantial empirical validation weakens the claims. The discussion of the q-bit example feels somewhat tacked on.
*   **The "Novelty" Claim is Weak:** The statement that "some of the error formulae...have not been previously known even under the prism of linear measurements" requires significantly more justification. The authors need to explicitly articulate *which* error formulas are novel and why previous work did not derive them.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The mathematical analysis is rigorous and correct.
*   The paper presents a generalization of existing results to a broader class of measurement models.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The contribution is incremental and lacks a transformative impact.
*   The paper is overly detailed, making it difficult to understand the key contributions.
*   The numerous assumptions and conditions limit the practical relevance of the findings.
*   The lack of more extensive empirical validation is concerning.
*   The formatting and writing style could be improved.



**Overall Assessment:**

I lean towards **rejection**. While the paper is technically sound and demonstrates a deep understanding of the relevant literature, the incremental nature of the contribution, the overly detailed presentation, and the lack of substantial novelty outweigh the strengths. The paper feels more like a technical exercise in extending existing results than a breakthrough that advances the field in a significant way. A major revision addressing the clarity, conciseness, and novelty issues would be necessary for reconsideration. Specifically, the authors should focus on highlighting the truly novel aspects of their work, streamlining the presentation, and providing more compelling evidence of the practical impact of their findings. The extensive reliance on theory without sufficient empirical validation also needs to be addressed.
