

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

## Summary
This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO when applied to non-linear measurement models of the form y_i = g_i(a_i^T x_0). The main contribution is Theorem 2.1, which shows that the asymptotic estimation error of the LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that of the LASSO with appropriately scaled linear measurements (with scaling factor μ and noise variance σ^2 as defined in equation (3)). The paper extends recent precise error analyses of the LASSO with linear measurements to the non-linear case, providing precise error formulas for sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signal recovery. The work builds on prior results by Plan and Vershynin [17] but provides tighter asymptotic characterizations.

## Strengths
The paper addresses an important problem with clear practical relevance to compressed sensing with quantized measurements. The main theoretical result (Theorem 2.1) is interesting and potentially valuable for practitioners working with non-linear measurements. The paper provides precise error formulas for several important special cases (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery), which are novel extensions of prior work. The connection between non-linear and linear measurement models is well-motivated and provides a useful conceptual framework for understanding LASSO performance under non-linearities.

## Weaknesses

1. **Major Notational and Technical Inconsistencies**: The paper contains numerous technical inconsistencies that undermine its credibility. In Theorem 2.1, the authors claim "‖x̂− µx0‖22 = ‖x̂lin − µx0‖22" but the linear measurement model they consider is y_lin = A(µx0) + σz, which is inconsistent with the standard LASSO setup where measurements should be y = Ax + noise. This is a critical error that needs to be corrected.

2. **Formatting and Typographical Errors**: The paper contains multiple typos and inconsistent formatting. In Section 1.1, "µ 6= 03" contains an apparent typo ("3"). The paper uses inconsistent notation for the estimator (x̂ vs. x̂(n)) and inconsistent citation formatting (e.g., "In Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems,, , ,"). The reference list has multiple entries with incomplete journal names and inconsistent punctuation.

3. **Overstated Claims**: The title and abstract claim "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements," but the paper actually shows asymptotic equivalence in estimation error, not equivalence of the measurements themselves. This overstatement is misleading and should be corrected.

4. **Insufficient Clarification of Key Concepts**: The paper fails to clearly explain the distinction between the cases where μ = 0 and μ ≠ 0, which is critical for understanding the results. The definition of λ_crit in Theorem 2.3 is not properly motivated, and the paper doesn't explain why the error behavior changes at this critical threshold.

5. **Inconsistent Presentation of Prior Work**: The paper cites [17] as having "non-asymptotic" results while claiming their own work is the "first to derive asymptotically precise results." However, [17] does provide asymptotic results in their Remark 1.8, and the paper doesn't clearly distinguish how their precise results improve upon [17]'s bounds.

6. **Technical Errors in Key Equations**: In Section 2.3.2, the authors state σ^2 = τ^2 - μ^2, but τ^2 is defined as τ^2 = 2Σ`i^2(Q(ti-1)-Q(ti)), while σ^2 = E[(g(γ)-μγ)^2] = τ^2 - μ^2. This definition is inconsistent with the earlier definition of σ^2 in equation (3), creating confusion.

7. **Missing Figure Reference**: The paper references "Figures 1-2" multiple times but no figures are included in the submission, making it impossible to verify the claims about numerical validation.

8. **Inconsistent Terminology**: The paper uses "non-linear mesaurements" in the title (typo) and inconsistently refers to "LASSO" versus "Generalized LASSO" without clear justification for the distinction.

## Overall Assessment

While the paper addresses an interesting problem and contains some novel theoretical results, the numerous technical inconsistencies, overstatements, and formatting errors significantly undermine its quality. The main result (Theorem 2.1) is potentially valuable but is presented with critical notational errors that make it difficult to evaluate. The paper's claims about equivalence between non-linear and linear measurements are overstated and misleading.

The paper would benefit from a thorough rewrite to correct the technical inconsistencies, clarify the key concepts (especially regarding μ = 0 vs. μ ≠ 0), and properly distinguish their contributions from prior work. The missing figures also make it impossible to evaluate the empirical validation claims.

## Recommendation

Reject

The paper contains too many technical inconsistencies and overstatements to be considered for publication at NIPS. While the core idea is interesting, the execution is poor due to multiple fundamental errors and lack of clarity. The paper would need substantial revision to address these issues before it could be considered for publication.
