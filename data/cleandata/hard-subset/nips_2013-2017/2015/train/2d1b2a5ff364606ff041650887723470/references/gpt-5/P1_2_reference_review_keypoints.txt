- Main claim: generalized LASSO with non-linear single-index measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on an effective linear model y_lin = µ A x0 + σ z, with µ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − µγ)^2].  
- Theorem 2.1 formalizes the non-linear = linear equivalence in the high-dimensional limit m/n → δ under mild normalization and Assumption 1.  
- Theorem 2.2 gives an exact asymptotic MSE as the solution of a three-scalar convex program involving µ, σ^2, δ, λ and a geometry-encoding function F.  
- Theorem 2.3 specializes to ℓ1 regularization, yielding simpler equations and identifying a critical λcrit regime when δ < 1.  
- Application: q-bit quantized compressed sensing—minimizing σ^2/µ^2 over quantizer thresholds/levels is asymptotically optimal; Lloyd–Max is connected as a practical design.  
- Relation to prior work: extends Brillinger (1982) from fixed n to high-dimensional regularized estimators.  
- Relation to Plan & Vershynin (2015): provides asymptotically exact characterization beyond their non-asymptotic upper bounds.  
- Leverages CGMT-style techniques to generalize precise LASSO risk results for broad convex regularizers via the F functional.  
- Technical quality: results are technically strong and plausible under Gaussian design; scalar convex program is well-posed and reduces to known cases.  
- Assumption 1 is abstract; authors verify it for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2 and sketch nuclear norm—more intuition/checklist would help.  
- Experiments reportedly validate theory across links and structures, but more finite-sample accuracy and variability details are needed.  
- Subtle issue: original model's noise depends on A, but surrogate uses independent Gaussian noise—explanation of why dependence vanishes under CGMT would improve clarity.  
- Clarity issues: terse notation around Theorem 2.2, minor typos/formatting, and µ = 0 cases need clearer discussion.  
- Low-rank section: F is deferred; at least one concrete worked example (e.g., spiked model) is needed.  
- Originality: the equivalence principle for generalized LASSO under non-linear observations appears new and significantly advances prior work.  
- Significance: conceptually important for reducing non-linear single-index problems to an effective linear SNR, with practical implications for measurement/quantizer design.  
- Limitations: heavy reliance on Gaussian design, requirement µ ≠ 0, and asymptotic setting restrict immediate broad applicability.  
- Suggestions: clarify µ = 0 scenarios and alternative estimators, discuss robustness to sub-Gaussian/structured designs, provide guidance on estimating µ and σ^2 when g is unknown, and add more empirical detail and a low-rank example.  
- Pros: strong asymptotic theory, broad convex-regularizer coverage, practical quantizer design implication, unifies LASSO under link misspecification.  
- Cons: Gaussian-design dependence, abstract Assumption 1, limited worked low-rank example, sparse discussion on estimating µ/σ and related 1-bit CS literature.  
- Recommendation: Accept (weak accept to accept); substantial novel theoretical contribution with high confidence.
