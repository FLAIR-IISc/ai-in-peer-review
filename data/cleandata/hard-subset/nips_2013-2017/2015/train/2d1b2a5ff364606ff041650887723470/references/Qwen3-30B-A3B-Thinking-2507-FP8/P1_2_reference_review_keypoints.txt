- Proves that Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to a suitably scaled linear measurement model with parameters μ = E[γg(γ)] and σ² = E[(g(γ)-μγ)²].
- Shows the asymptotic behavior of the error ‖x̂ - μx0‖² is identical for non-linear and appropriately scaled linear measurements (m/n → δ).
- Extends prior non-asymptotic and unregularized results (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) to precise asymptotic error characterizations for the regularized Generalized LASSO.
- Builds rigorous proofs using the convex Gaussian min-max theorem (CGMT) framework, leveraging established linear LASSO analysis techniques.
- Applies to a wide class of regularizers (ℓ1, group-ℓ1, nuclear norm) and link functions, covering sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank recovery settings.
- Validates theoretical predictions empirically (Figures 1–2) and across multiple structured signal recovery problems.
- Demonstrates practical application to q-bit compressive sensing, including optimizing quantization thresholds and levels.
- Technical strength: results are carefully constructed, theoretically sound, and well-supported by proofs.
- Novelty: first precise asymptotic error characterization for regularized Generalized LASSO under non-linear measurements.
- Generality: framework encompasses many practical non-linear measurement scenarios (1-bit, censored/Tobit models).
- Practical significance: simplifies analysis of non-linear measurement problems by reducing them to well-studied linear-case results.
- Limitation: assumes g is i.i.d. across measurements and depends only on the scalar a_i^T x0, excluding multivariate/structured non-linearities.
- Limitation: results are strictly asymptotic; finite-sample behavior and guidance for finite n are not addressed.
- Limitation: lacks algorithmic/implementation details for numerically solving the optimization in (10) for general regularizers.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized but may be technically challenging for non-specialists and could discuss practical implications more.
- Overall judgement: a high-quality, significant theoretical contribution; recommendation — Accept.
