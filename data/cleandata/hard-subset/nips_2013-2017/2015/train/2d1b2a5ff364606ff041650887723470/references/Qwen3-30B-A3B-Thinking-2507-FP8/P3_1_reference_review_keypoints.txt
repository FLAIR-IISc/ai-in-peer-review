- Paper establishes a precise equivalence between LASSO with non-linear measurements and LASSO with appropriately scaled linear measurements (defined by μ and σ^2).  
- Theorem 2.1: asymptotic equivalence showing LASSO treats non-linear measurements as scaled linear measurements plus Gaussian noise.  
- Uses Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT); proofs are technically rigorous and build on recent high-dimensional analysis.  
- Framework generalizes to multiple structured recovery problems (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank), demonstrating broad applicability.  
- Practical implication: for q-bit compressive sensing the paper gives an actionable criterion—minimize σ^2/μ^2—to choose optimal quantization thresholds.  
- Limitation: analysis assumes μ ≠ 0 and does not handle important μ = 0 cases (e.g., sign measurements).  
- Limitation: computing the precise error formula (involving F(c1,c2,c3)) can be computationally challenging for complex structures like low-rank matrices.  
- Limitation: empirical validation is limited (sparse cases shown; low-rank only theoretical), so experimental support is incomplete.  
- Limitation: no comparisons with specialized methods designed for non-linear or binary measurements to contextualize practical advantages.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution that extends prior loose bounds to exact asymptotic characterization and is broadly relevant.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Pro summary: precise error characterization, general framework across structures, practical q-bit guidance, rigorous CGMT-based analysis.  
- Con summary: restriction to μ ≠ 0, limited experiments (especially low-rank), lack of method comparisons, and computational complexity of error prediction.
