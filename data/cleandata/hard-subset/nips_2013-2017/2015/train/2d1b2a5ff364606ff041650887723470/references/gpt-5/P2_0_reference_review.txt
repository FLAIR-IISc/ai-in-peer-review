Summary
The paper studies generalized LASSO under non-linear single-index measurements y_i = g_i(a_i^T x0), with Gaussian design A and unknown/unspecified link g. Its main result is an asymptotically exact “equivalence principle”: for a wide class of convex regularizers f and signal models, the squared error of LASSO with non-linear observations is the same (in the large n, m with m/n→δ regime) as that of LASSO applied to an equivalent linear model with an effective scalar gain µ = E[γ g(γ)] and effective noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−µγ)^2], γ∼N(0,1). Theorem 2.1 formalizes the equivalence, and Theorem 2.2 gives a precise scalar convex program whose unique optimizer α* equals the asymptotic error ‖x̂−µx0‖. The framework covers standard sparsity (`1), group sparsity (`1,2), and sketches a path for low-rank (nuclear norm). For sparse signals, Theorem 2.3 yields closed-form characterizations (via two scalar equations) including a phase transition λcrit. As an application, the paper shows that optimal q-bit quantizer design for LASSO reduces to minimizing σ^2/µ^2; the Lloyd–Max algorithm is shown to produce stationary points for this objective.

Relation to prior work
- Brillinger (1982) analyzed least squares under single-index models with fixed n and m→∞, showing consistency up to a scale. This paper generalizes to high-dimensional regimes and from LS to generalized LASSO, providing exact asymptotics.
- Plan & Vershynin (2015) gave non-asymptotic upper bounds for constrained LASSO under non-linear observations, identifying the same µ, σ parameters. This work sharpens those to precise asymptotics, treats the regularized (more practical) LASSO, and provides exact formulas valid across SNR and λ.
- For linear measurements, exact risk for LASSO has been analyzed via AMP or CGMT (Bayati & Montanari; Donoho et al.; Stojnic; Oymak/Thrampoulidis/Hassibi). This paper extends the CGMT-based precise analysis paradigm to non-linear links, yielding new formulas even for some linear cases (e.g., beyond Gaussian nonzeros or beyond `1).

Quality
- Technical soundness: The reduction to an equivalent linear model via µ, σ is compelling and consistent with prior indicative results. The CGMT-based machinery and the Moreau-envelope-based functional F deliver a clean scalar optimization characterization (Theorem 2.2). The sparse specialization (Theorem 2.3) produces interpretable fixed-point equations and sharp λcrit behavior, matching known linear results when g is linear. The application to quantizer design is well motivated.
- Claims vs. assumptions: Results rely on i.i.d. Gaussian A, independence of g_i, and µ, σ bounded and µ≠0. The additional Assumption 1 (existence of a limit for a normalized Moreau envelope of f*) is reasonable and verified for several standard f, but it is somewhat opaque; the paper provides helpful instantiations for `1 and `1,2, and sketches the low-rank case.
- Empirical support: The figures (not shown here) reportedly validate the predictions across link functions and structures. More finite-n robustness plots (e.g., n from hundreds to thousands, misspecified λ) would strengthen the case, but the theoretical contribution stands on its own.

Clarity
- The overall narrative is clear: introduce single-index, define µ, σ, state equivalence, give precise program, then specializations. Proofs are in the appendix, as expected for NeurIPS. Some statements would benefit from sharpening:
  - Theorem 2.1 should be phrased as convergence of the difference of errors to zero (rather than equality with probability one, which can be read as stronger than intended).
  - Assumption 1 could be accompanied by a short lemma listing sufficient conditions for common regularizers and signal priors (the paper does it for sparsity and groups; adding a precise low-rank example would help).
  - Notation and normalization (1/√n in (9), f scaling in (b)) are nonstandard but consistent; a brief remark explaining why they simplify the CGMT analysis would help.
- Minor typos and grammar (e.g., “mesaurements”, “redifining”, capitalization of Gaussian) should be fixed. Some references are incomplete.

Originality
- The equivalence principle with exact asymptotics for generalized LASSO under non-linear links is novel. Prior work gave bounds (Plan–Vershynin) or handled linear models precisely. The way µ and σ fully summarize the impact of g for LASSO’s asymptotic risk is elegant and practically useful.
- The general formulation via F(c1,c2,c3) covers many convex regularizers beyond `1, which is a meaningful extension over previous precise analyses.

Significance
- The result unifies analysis across a broad class of non-linear measurement channels: one can reuse linear LASSO risk formulas by plugging in effective µ, σ. This offers actionable guidance for system design (e.g., quantizer thresholds) and for understanding when LASSO “works” under link misspecification.
- The sparse specialization provides explicit equations for risk and tuning, informing λ selection and phase transitions.
- Limitations (Gaussian designs, asymptotics) temper immediate practical impact, but the conceptual advance is likely to influence subsequent theory and method development.

Strengths
- Clear, general equivalence with precise asymptotics; simple characterization by three scalars (α,β,τ).
- Broad coverage of convex regularizers and structured signals; new exact results even for linear cases.
- Concrete, useful application to q-bit quantizer design; connection to Lloyd–Max is insightful.
- Builds on and strengthens a vibrant line of NeurIPS-relevant work (CGMT/AMP, compressive sensing, single-index models).

Weaknesses and suggestions
- Design assumptions: i.i.d. Gaussian A is restrictive; discuss whether sub-Gaussian/correlated designs are possible (even conjecturally), or include a brief robustness experiment.
- Assumption 1 is abstract; add a theorem with sufficient conditions covering `1, `1,2, and nuclear norm under standard priors.
- Clarify Theorem 2.1 phrasing (limit equality vs. pathwise equality). In Sec. 2.3, justify why minimizing σ^2/µ^2 suffices for minimizing the LASSO error, given that α* in (10) depends on µ and σ separately; specify conditions (e.g., fixed λ, scale invariances) under which the ratio is the right objective.
- Practical aspects: Discuss how µ and σ could be estimated from data when g is unknown (e.g., method-of-moments with pilot estimates), and how λ should be tuned in practice.
- Related work: Consider citing generalized AMP for non-linear channels (e.g., GAMP) to position differences (here, the estimator is LASSO rather than AMP; analysis is precise for convex programs).

Recommendation
- Overall: Strong accept. The paper provides a clean and broadly applicable theoretical contribution with precise guarantees, advances the state of the art beyond prior bounds, and offers useful insights for practice. With minor clarifications and additional discussion on assumptions and practical tuning, it would make a valuable NeurIPS contribution.

Arguments for acceptance
- Novel, technically solid equivalence and exact risk characterization for a widely used estimator.
- General framework covering many structures; new results even for linear settings.
- Clear implications for quantized sensing and link misspecification.

Arguments against acceptance
- Restrictive Gaussian design and asymptotic regime.
- Some assumptions/notations are opaque; minor clarity issues.
- Limited empirical exploration of robustness and finite-sample behavior.
