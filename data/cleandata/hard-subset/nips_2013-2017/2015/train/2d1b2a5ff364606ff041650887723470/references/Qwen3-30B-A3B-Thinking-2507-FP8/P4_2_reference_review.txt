

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper addresses a fundamental question in high-dimensional structured signal recovery: how does the Generalized LASSO perform when measurements are non-linear, i.e., yi = gi(ai^T x0) rather than linear yi = ai^T x0 + noise? The authors establish a precise equivalence between the LASSO with non-linear measurements and a corresponding LASSO with linear measurements scaled by parameters μ and σ^2 defined through the link function g. This extends previous work by Brillinger [5] (which considered least squares without regularization in the fixed-dimension, large-sample limit) and Plan and Vershynin [17] (which provided non-asymptotic bounds for constrained LASSO in high dimensions).

The main contribution is Theorem 2.1, which establishes that for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, the asymptotic squared error ‖x̂ - μx0‖^2 is identical to the squared error of the LASSO with linear measurements of the form yi = μai^T x0 + σzi. Theorem 2.2 then provides a precise characterization of this error as the solution to a convex optimization problem, generalizing previous precise error analyses for linear measurements [2, 10, 16, 18, 19, 21]. The authors specialize their results to sparse recovery (Theorem 2.3), group-sparse recovery, and low-rank matrix recovery, demonstrating the generality of their approach. Finally, they apply their theory to optimize quantization thresholds in q-bit compressive sensing.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness (Quality):** The paper provides rigorous asymptotic analysis under well-justified assumptions (Gaussian measurement matrix, appropriate signal structure, etc.). The proofs of the main theorems are deferred to the appendix, which is appropriate for a conference paper. The authors carefully establish the connection to previous work and clarify the limitations of their results.

2. **Novelty (Originality):** The paper is the first to establish asymptotically precise results for the LASSO with non-linear measurements. While Plan and Vershynin [17] considered constrained LASSO with non-linear measurements, they provided only loose upper bounds, whereas this work derives exact asymptotic error expressions. The key insight that non-linear measurements can be treated as scaled and noisy linear measurements is novel and generalizes previous results.

3. **Significance:** The result has immediate implications for understanding and applying LASSO-type methods in settings with non-linear measurements (e.g., 1-bit quantization, censored data). The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates how the theory can guide practical design choices, such as optimal quantization thresholds.

4. **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The main results are presented in a logical sequence, with appropriate special cases and examples (Figures 1-2) illustrating the accuracy of the theoretical predictions. The notation is consistent throughout.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Asymptotic Focus:** The results are strictly asymptotic (n→∞), which may limit immediate applicability to finite-dimensional problems. While asymptotic analysis is common in this field, the authors could have provided more discussion about how their results might translate to finite samples.

2. **Limited Scope of Applications:** The paper focuses on the Generalized LASSO, but doesn't consider other approaches to non-linear measurement problems (e.g., [24] for binary measurements). The low-rank recovery case (Section 2.2.4) is less developed than the sparse recovery case.

3. **Assumption of Unit Norm Signal:** The paper assumes ‖x0‖2 = 1 for simplicity (Footnote 4), which excludes some cases where the signal norm might be unknown or important. While the authors note this is a simplification, they don't thoroughly discuss how this might affect practical applications.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by extending precise LASSO analysis to non-linear measurements.
- The main result (Theorem 2.1) is novel and generalizes previous work in a non-trivial way.
- The precise error characterization (Theorem 2.2) provides valuable theoretical insights for practitioners.
- The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates the practical relevance of the theory.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- The asymptotic nature of the results may limit their immediate practical impact.
- The paper doesn't address how the theory applies to non-Gaussian measurement matrices, which are common in practice.
- The low-rank matrix recovery case is less developed than the sparse recovery case.

This paper makes a strong theoretical contribution that extends the well-established literature on the Generalized LASSO to a non-linear measurement setting. The results are technically sound, novel, and significant for both theoretical understanding and practical applications in compressed sensing and signal processing. The paper is well-written and clearly presents its contributions relative to previous work. The limitations (asymptotic results, unit norm assumption) are acknowledged and reasonable given the scope of the work. The paper meets all the criteria for a high-quality NIPS submission.
