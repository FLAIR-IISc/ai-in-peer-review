- Main result: Under Gaussian measurements and an asymptotic regime, Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with scaled, noisy linear measurements (parameters µ and σ² depend on g).  
- Provides precise error formulas (Theorems 2.1 and 2.2) quantifying this equivalence rather than loose bounds.  
- Results are general: apply to a wide class of link functions g and regularizers f in a reasonably broad asymptotic setting.  
- Demonstrates concrete applications: sparse recovery, group-sparse recovery, and q-bit compressive sensing.  
- Mathematical presentation is clear and well-defined, aiding comprehension of derivations.  
- Positions the work well within literature, extending Plan & Vershynin and connecting to single-index models and sufficient dimension reduction.  
- Novelty/significance: the equivalence result is a substantial theoretical contribution that simplifies analysis of non-linear measurement settings.  
- Precision: detailed formulas give deep insight into effects of link function, regularization, and measurement count.  
- Accessibility concern: the paper is highly technical and may be hard to follow for non-specialists; more intuition/examples would help.  
- Assumption 1 is ad-hoc and insufficiently justified; its validity beyond sparse recovery needs more thorough exploration.  
- Computational challenge: evaluating the error (computing optimal α*) can be difficult in practice; authors should discuss practical approximation methods.  
- Limited empirical validation: Figure 1 is promising but more extensive simulations (non-Gaussian signals, complex regularizers) are needed.  
- Pro arguments: original, rigorous, general, and extends significant prior work—compelling reasons to accept.  
- Con arguments: technical complexity, Assumption 1, computational/practical issues, and limited experiments temper acceptance.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; weaknesses are minor and addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, specifically clarifying Assumption 1, improving accessibility, discussing computational methods for α*, and expanding empirical validation.
