- Main claim: Generalized LASSO on nonlinear single-index measurements behaves asymptotically like linear measurements with effective scale µ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − µγ)^2].  
- Theorem 2.2: Exact asymptotic MSE is given by a 3-scalar convex program depending on δ, λ, the regularizer via F (Moreau envelope of Fenchel conjugate), and only µ, σ^2 from g.  
- Sparse specialization: For ℓ1 regularization (Theorem 2.3) the scalar formula reduces to fixed-point equations with a critical λ and phase transition matching known linear results.  
- Quantization application: Optimal q-bit quantizer design for LASSO reduces to minimizing σ^2/µ^2; Lloyd–Max iterations provide stationary points for this objective.  
- Relation to Brillinger: Extends classical fixed-n results to high-dimensional proportional asymptotics and to regularized LASSO.  
- Relation to Plan–Vershynin: Complements non-asymptotic upper bounds by providing asymptotically exact penalized-LASSO risk and the precise role of µ, σ^2.  
- Relation to existing precise LASSO analyses: Builds on CGMT/AMP blueprint to generalize precise risk characterizations to ignore the link and to other convex regularizers via F.  
- Technical soundness: The nonlinear=linear equivalence is clear and compelling, formalizing prior hints in the literature.  
- Theorem 2.2 concreteness: The scalar variational formula is explicit and matches linear-measurement characterizations in special cases.  
- Assumption 1: Encapsulates regularizer/signal structure and is verified for ℓ1 and group ℓ1,2; low-rank case plausible but needs more concrete sufficient conditions.  
- Uniqueness/convexity: Statement that α* is unique should be supported with a brief justification (convexity/strict convexity when σ^2>0 and properties of F).  
- Rigorous wording: Theorem 2.1’s strong equality should be clarified to mean equality of asymptotic limits (a.s.) rather than finite-n equality.  
- Originality: First asymptotically exact risk characterization of penalized generalized LASSO under unknown single-index links and the first precise demonstration of risk depending on g only via µ, σ^2.  
- Significance: Provides a practical prediction/tuning tool for LASSO under unknown links and yields actionable design rules (e.g., quantizer optimization).  
- Clarity issues: Notation around the Moreau envelope and the expression n e√n(f∗),c3(·) is confusing and needs cleaner definition; several minor typos.  
- Low-rank section: Brief and lacking conditions ensuring existence of F; more detail (e.g., random orthogonal singular vectors) would help.  
- Empirical validation gaps: Recommend finite-n convergence plots, sensitivity to λ mis-tuning, tests with non-Gaussian/structured A, and comparisons to link-aware estimators (logistic/probit LASSO, GAMP).  
- Pros: Conceptually clean equivalence, precise error formula generalizing prior linear results, explicit sparse-case characterization, and practical quantizer-design implication.  
- Cons/questions: CGMT relies on Gaussian A—universality to other ensembles is unaddressed; Assumption 1 is abstract; estimating µ and σ^2 from data is not treated; µ = 0 case excluded and should be discussed.  
- Suggested improvements: Add methods to estimate µ, σ^2 from data, guidance on choosing λ via the scalar program, expand empirical tests (non-Gaussian A and link-aware baselines), clarify Theorem 2.1 and Moreau-envelope notation, and fix typos.  
- Overall assessment: Strong and impactful theoretical contribution that needs minor clarifications and expanded empirical/robustness analysis to maximize practical value.
