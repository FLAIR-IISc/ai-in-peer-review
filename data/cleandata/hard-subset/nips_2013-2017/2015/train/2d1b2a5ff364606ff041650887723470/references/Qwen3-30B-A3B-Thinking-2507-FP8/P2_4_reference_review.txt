

# Review: "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to the problem of structured signal recovery from non-linear measurements. The authors establish a precise equivalence between the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and a corresponding LASSO with linear measurements, showing that non-linear measurements can be treated as scaled and noisy linear measurements with scaling factor µ and noise variance σ² defined in terms of the link function g.

## Summary and Relation to Prior Work

The paper builds on foundational work by Brillinger (1980s) and Plan & Vershynin (2015) who studied the LASSO with non-linear measurements. The key advance is extending previous results to provide precise asymptotic error characterizations for a wide class of regularizers (beyond just ℓ₁-norm) and for all values of the regularization parameter λ. The main result (Theorem 2.1) shows that the error of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically identical to the error of the same algorithm with linear measurements scaled by µ and with noise variance σ². Theorem 2.2 then provides a precise error formula capturing the roles of the link function g, signal structure, regularizer f, and regularization parameter λ.

## Strengths

**Technical Quality:** The paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs for all theorems. The assumptions are clearly stated, and the results are derived under those assumptions. The authors carefully establish the connection between non-linear and linear measurements, providing a unifying framework for understanding LASSO performance. The paper correctly cites and builds upon prior work (Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin, and others), demonstrating a thorough understanding of the field.

**Originality:** The key insight that non-linear measurements can be treated as scaled and noisy linear measurements is novel and generalizes previous work. The precise error formula (Theorem 2.2) extends previous results for the LASSO with linear measurements to the non-linear case, and the application to various structured recovery problems (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) demonstrates the breadth of the approach. The paper is the first to derive asymptotically precise results on the performance of any LASSO-type program with non-linear measurements.

**Significance:** The result has immediate implications for many applications involving non-linear measurements (e.g., 1-bit quantization, censored measurements). The application to q-bit compressive sensing (Section 2.3) provides a principled approach to designing quantizers, which is a practical contribution. The precise error prediction allows for better parameter tuning in practice, which is valuable for both theory and applications.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and well-organized. The notation is consistent, and the key results are clearly stated. The figures effectively illustrate the accuracy of the theoretical predictions across different settings (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank recovery).

## Weaknesses

**Assumptions:** The assumption that the measurement matrix has i.i.d. Gaussian entries is common in high-dimensional statistics but limits the applicability to settings where measurements aren't Gaussian. The paper could acknowledge this limitation more explicitly.

**Practical Applicability:** While the paper provides a theoretical framework, it doesn't adequately address how to estimate µ and σ² in practice when the link function g is unknown. This is a significant gap for practical applications, as the paper's main result relies on knowing these parameters.

**Asymptotic Focus:** The paper focuses on the asymptotic regime (n → ∞), which might not directly apply to small-scale problems where practitioners often work. The authors could briefly discuss the practical relevance of their asymptotic results.

## Overall Assessment

This is a high-quality theoretical contribution that significantly advances our understanding of the LASSO with non-linear measurements. The paper is technically sound, original, and has significant implications for both theory and practice. While there are some limitations in the assumptions and practical applicability, these are common in theoretical works of this nature and don't detract from the overall contribution.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The theoretical results are novel, well-supported, and extend previous work in a meaningful way. The precise error characterization will be valuable to researchers working on structured signal recovery with non-linear measurements. The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates the practical relevance of the theory.

## Key Points for Authors

1. Consider adding a brief discussion of how to estimate µ and σ² when g is unknown, as this is crucial for practical applications.
2. Clarify the limitations of the Gaussian measurement matrix assumption and suggest potential directions for future work to extend the theory to non-Gaussian measurements.

## Pro and Con for Acceptance

- **Pro:** The paper makes a significant theoretical advance with precise error characterizations for a wide class of non-linear measurements.
- **Pro:** The results unify previous work and provide a framework for understanding LASSO performance under non-linear measurements.
- **Con:** The Gaussian measurement matrix assumption limits practical applicability.
- **Con:** The paper doesn't adequately address how to estimate µ and σ² in practice when g is unknown.

This paper meets all the key NIPS reviewing criteria with strong quality, clarity, originality, and significance. It represents a meaningful contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics and signal processing.
