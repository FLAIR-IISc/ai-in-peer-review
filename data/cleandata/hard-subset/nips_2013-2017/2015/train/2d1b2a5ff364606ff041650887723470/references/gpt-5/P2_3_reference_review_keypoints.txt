- Studies LASSO with non-linear single-index observations and claims asymptotic equivalence to a scaled noisy linear model with µ = E[γ g(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−µγ)^2].

- Theorem 2.1 formalizes the non-linear = linear correspondence; Theorem 2.2 gives the asymptotic squared error as the unique solution of a three-variable convex optimization involving a scalar function F (Assumption 1).

- Specializations include sparse (`1), group-sparse (`1,2), and low-rank (nuclear norm) cases; Theorem 2.3 provides explicit fixed-point equations for the sparse case.

- Application to q-bit compressive sensing: design reduces to minimizing σ^2/µ^2, linked to Lloyd–Max quantizer design.

- Generalizes Brillinger’s single-index LS result to high-dimensional regularized LASSO and refines Plan–Vershynin bounds by providing exact asymptotics including µ and σ.

- Builds on CGMT/AMP techniques and extends precise analyses to non-linear measurements and broader convex regularizers.

- Technical soundness: claims are well-motivated, consistent with prior heuristics, and sensible under stated assumptions (Gaussian A, i.i.d. links, nonzero bounded µ,σ, m/n→δ).

- Proof completeness: main statements are precise and proofs are provided in the appendix; sparse results match known cases and are numerically verifiable.

- Limitation: analysis relies on Gaussian design; extensions to sub-Gaussian or structured designs are not addressed.

- Limitation: Assumption 1 is technical and its verification is demonstrated only for some regularizers; scope beyond shown cases is unclear.

- Limitation: low-rank case is sketched but lacks a fully worked-out F and concrete numerical validation.

- Clarity: paper is well organized and makes the roles of µ and σ explicit, but more intuition and recipe for computing F would improve readability.

- Presentation issues: several typos, incomplete references, and Theorem 2.1 phrasing should emphasize equality of asymptotic limits (a.s.).

- Originality: the precise non-linear = linear equivalence for regularized LASSO and the unified scalar optimization capturing arbitrary convex regularizers are novel contributions.

- Significance (conceptual): provides a broadly useful reduction from non-linear to linear LASSO that leverages existing theory.

- Significance (practical): offers actionable guidance for tuning, quantizer design, and predicting error across λ, δ, and structures.

- Strengths: precise asymptotic characterization, clear identification of µ and σ as sufficient link-dependent quantities, and useful specializations.

- Weaknesses: strong Gaussian assumption, central technical assumption not fully explored, underdeveloped low-rank case, and limited experimental detail in the main text.

- Suggestions: clarify Theorem 2.1 wording; expand conditions guaranteeing Assumption 1 and provide a table/recipes for F for common regularizers; flesh out low-rank case with numerical results; discuss sub-Gaussian robustness; fix typos and references.

- Recommendation: reviewer leans toward acceptance, citing technical novelty and impact, conditional on addressing clarity and completeness issues (especially low-rank).
