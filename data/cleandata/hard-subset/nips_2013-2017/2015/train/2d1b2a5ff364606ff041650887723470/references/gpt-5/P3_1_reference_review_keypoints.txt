- Studies high-dimensional estimation from nonlinear single-index measurements and shows asymptotic equivalence to an effective linear model yi = μ a_i^T x0 + σ zi for Gaussian designs.  
- Uses CGMT to derive a scalar convex optimization whose optimizer α* gives lim ||x̂ − μx0||^2 = α*^2, with specializations to sparse, group-sparse and nuclear-norm regularizers.  
- Provides closed-form fixed-point characterizations for the sparse case and connects to state-evolution intuition.  
- Proposes optimizing q-bit quantizers by minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and shows Lloyd–Max iterations converge to stationary points of this objective.  
- Extends prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin; AMP/CGMT literature) by giving exact asymptotics for regularized LASSO under nonlinear measurements.  
- Key conceptual message: a wide class of nonlinearities collapse to two scalars (μ, σ^2) that fully determine asymptotic LASSO performance.  
- Technical strength: elegant CGMT-based derivation and a three-variable convex program applicable to many convex regularizers under Assumption 1.  
- Broad applicability: covers many link functions (quantization, sign, ReLU-like) and regularizers (ℓ1, group ℓ1,2, nuclear norm) in principle.  
- Practical impact: provides actionable guidance for quantizer design and links to established algorithms (Lloyd–Max).  
- Theorem 2.1 needs a precise restatement clarifying mode of convergence (a.s. vs in probability) and that equality refers to limits rather than random variables.  
- Gaussian design is critical to the proof; the paper lacks discussion or evidence for universality beyond Gaussian/isotropic designs.  
- Assumption 1 (existence/evaluation of F via Moreau envelope) is nontrivial; nuclear-norm case is underdeveloped and lacks concrete verification or examples.  
- The analysis assumes known μ,σ; the paper does not provide practical estimators for μ,σ or guidance for tuning λ when g is unknown.  
- The μ = 0 case (even g) is excluded and needs discussion on what fails and possible alternative approaches.  
- Finite-sample behavior is insufficiently explored; more experiments are needed to show how large n,m must be for asymptotic predictions to hold.  
- Presentation issues: inconsistent normalization (1/√n), unclear interpretation/recipe for computing F(c1,c2,c3), incomplete bibliography, and minor typos.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong, elegant theoretical contribution with broad relevance and useful design insights.  
- Cons for acceptance: restricted to Gaussian designs, underdeveloped low-rank treatment, unclear statements (Theorem 2.1), and lack of practical guidance for unknown g.  
- Questions for authors: clarify convergence mode in Theorem 2.1; comment on universality to sub-Gaussian designs and provide empirical evidence; evaluate F in a spiked nuclear-norm model with simulations; propose estimators for μ,σ and assess sensitivity to estimation error.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality and impactful work that merits acceptance after clarifying theorem statements and strengthening low-rank and empirical sections.
