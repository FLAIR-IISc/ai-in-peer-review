

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper makes a striking theoretical contribution by establishing a precise equivalence between the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and the Generalized LASSO with linear measurements. The key insight is that for large dimensions, non-linear measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0) can be asymptotically treated as scaled linear measurements with scaling factor μ = E[γg(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2] for γ ~ N(0, 1). This equivalence holds for a wide class of link functions g and regularizers f.

The main contribution is Theorem 2.1, which establishes this precise equivalence. The authors then derive a precise error formula (Theorem 2.2) that characterizes the asymptotic behavior of the estimation error under this equivalence. They specialize their results to several important cases: sparse recovery (Theorem 2.3), group-sparse recovery, and low-rank matrix recovery. The paper also provides a compelling application to q-bit compressive sensing, demonstrating how to optimize quantization thresholds based on the derived error formula.

The paper builds on recent advances in the precise analysis of the LASSO with linear measurements (e.g., using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem) and extends these results to non-linear settings. The authors relate their work to previous results by Brillinger (1980s) and Plan and Vershynin (2015), showing how their results generalize and improve upon these earlier works.

The paper's strengths are numerous. First, the main insight is highly novel and bold - the equivalence between LASSO with non-linear measurements and LASSO with linear measurements is not obvious and represents a significant conceptual advance. Second, the paper provides a precise error formula that captures the role of the link function, signal structure, regularizer, and regularizer parameter. Third, the application to q-bit compressive sensing is particularly compelling, as it demonstrates how the theoretical results can inform practical algorithm design. Fourth, the paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides excellent context by relating to previous work. Finally, the numerical results in Figures 1-2 validate the theoretical predictions.

While the paper has some limitations (the assumption of i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices, the asymptotic nature of the results, and the unit norm assumption for x0), these do not undermine the core contribution. As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty over methodological perfection, I find the main insight to be sufficiently significant to outweigh these minor limitations.

The significance of this work is substantial. The equivalence result provides a unifying perspective on LASSO with non-linear measurements, showing that they can be analyzed using the well-established framework for linear measurements. This insight has both theoretical importance and practical implications, as demonstrated by the q-bit compressive sensing application. The paper also provides novel error expressions for several structured signal recovery problems that appear to be previously unknown.

The paper is technically sound, with rigorous proofs and careful theoretical development. The authors provide clear statements of their assumptions and results, and the paper is well-structured with a logical flow from problem statement to main results to special cases and applications.

This paper makes a bold and significant contribution that advances our understanding of LASSO with non-linear measurements. It is not an incremental improvement on existing work but rather introduces a new conceptual framework that could guide future research in non-linear measurement systems. The equivalence result is a major theoretical insight that could change how we approach these problems in the future.

I strongly recommend acceptance of this paper. The novelty of the main insight, the precision of the theoretical results, and the compelling application to q-bit compressive sensing make this a significant contribution to the field. The paper meets all the criteria for a high-quality NIPS submission, with exceptional originality and significance.
