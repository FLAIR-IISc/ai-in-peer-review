This paper investigates the relationship between LASSO with non-linear measurements and its linear counterpart, aiming to provide a precise asymptotic error analysis for the non-linear case. The core result establishes that, under Gaussian measurements, the LASSO effectively treats non-linear measurements as scaled and noisy linear measurements, characterized by constants µ and σ². While the problem is well-motivated and builds upon prior work in single-index models and high-dimensional statistics (Brillinger, Plan & Vershynin, etc.), the paper suffers from a remarkably high density of minor flaws in presentation, notation, and justification, detracting significantly from its overall impact.

The introduction, while covering relevant background, is verbose and occasionally repetitive. The justification for assuming unit norm for x0 feels tacked on and relies heavily on footnote justification – a better integration into the main text would be preferable. The transition between motivating the problem and presenting the contributions is somewhat disjointed. The statement regarding the Generalized LASSO being tailored to linear models feels unnecessarily critical; it's a natural extension, not a flaw.

The technical presentation in Section 2, while generally rigorous, is riddled with inconsistencies. The notation is often unclear. For example, the use of x(n)0 vs x0 is confusing and not consistently applied. The explanation for Assumption 1 is insufficient. Why is it “no more than a normalization condition”? What specific challenges does it address? The jump from stating the assumption to asserting its satisfaction for specific regularizers (like the L1 norm) lacks crucial details. The derivation of F(c1, c2, c3) for the L1 case, while correct, feels rushed and lacks clear explanation. 

Theorem 2.2 and the subsequent theorems (2.3) for specific cases (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) are presented without sufficient discussion of their practical implications. The connection between the derived theoretical results and concrete algorithmic improvements or parameter tuning strategies is weak. For instance, the mention of the Lloyd-Max algorithm in Section 2.3.2 feels abrupt and requires more context. The claim that the LM algorithm is an effective solution method needs justification beyond simply stating that it converges to a stationary point. 

Furthermore, several minor errors and stylistic issues detract from the overall polish. “µ 6= 03” is a peculiar notation. The use of “Henceforth” is overused. The phrase “with high probability” is used imprecisely – what does "high probability" actually mean in these asymptotic settings? The phrasing throughout the paper often feels unnecessarily convoluted. The figures are referenced but not described in the text – their content should be briefly summarized to enhance understanding. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The problem addressed is relevant to a growing body of work on non-linear models and compressive sensing.
*   The theoretical results provide a precise characterization of the estimation error under non-linear measurements.
*   The connection established between non-linear and linear measurements offers potential for leveraging existing analysis tools.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The paper is overly dense and suffers from a high number of minor flaws in notation, presentation, and justification.
*   The theoretical results, while correct, lack clear practical implications or algorithmic guidance.
*   The writing is often convoluted and unclear, hindering readability and understanding.
*   The lack of a thorough discussion of the limitations and potential extensions of the work.
*   The constant reference to the "optimality" of methods without defining what optimality *means* in the context of the problem.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potential significance of the results, the numerous flaws in presentation and justification outweigh the contributions. The paper reads more like a collection of technical calculations than a cohesive scientific narrative. While the core ideas are sound, the execution is lacking in rigor and clarity. I recommend **rejection**. The authors should significantly revise the paper to address the issues raised above, focusing on clarity, conciseness, and a more thorough discussion of the practical implications of their results. A careful proofread to eliminate minor errors is also strongly recommended.
