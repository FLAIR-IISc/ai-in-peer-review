## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

**Summary:**

This paper establishes a surprising and powerful connection between the Generalized LASSO applied to non-linear measurements and its performance relative to a corresponding linear measurement model. Specifically, the authors demonstrate that, asymptotically, the performance of the LASSO with non-linear measurements `yi = g(aTi x0)` is equivalent to that of a LASSO applied to scaled and noisy linear measurements `yi = µaTi x0 + σz`, where `µ` and `σ` characterize the link function `g`. This allows leveraging existing, precise analyses of the LASSO in the linear case to understand its behavior in non-linear scenarios. They provide a precise error formula, validate it with simulations, and discuss implications for applications like q-bit compressive sensing.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Significance:** The core result – the equivalence between non-linear and scaled linear measurements for LASSO estimation – is a significant theoretical contribution. It provides a powerful tool for analyzing and potentially designing algorithms for non-linear measurement settings. This is particularly impactful given the growing interest in non-linear sensing modalities.
* **Technical Soundness:** The paper builds on a strong foundation of existing work in high-dimensional statistics and compressive sensing, and the mathematical derivations appear rigorous. The authors carefully state their assumptions and provide a clear roadmap for the proofs (deferred to the appendix).
* **Precise Results:**  The derivation of a precise error formula (Theorem 2.2) is a key strength, moving beyond loose bounds and offering actionable insights into the interplay between the link function, regularizer, and estimation performance.  The specialized results for sparse recovery (Theorem 2.3) and q-bit compressive sensing add further practical value.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is well-structured, and the notation is generally clear.  The introduction effectively motivates the problem and connects it to relevant literature.
* **Completeness:** While proofs are in the appendix, the main body of the paper provides a complete presentation of the theoretical results and their implications. The discussion of related work is thorough.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Assumptions:** The assumption of Gaussian measurement vectors (A) is somewhat restrictive. While standard in many compressive sensing analyses, it would be valuable to discuss the potential for extending the results to other measurement matrix distributions.  Similarly, Assumption 1, while reasonable, could benefit from a more detailed discussion of its verification in different settings.
* **Practicality of q-bit Compressive Sensing Application:**  The section on q-bit compressive sensing, while illustrative, feels somewhat underdeveloped. While the authors correctly point out the link to Lloyd-Max optimization, the practical benefits of their theoretical result in this specific context could be more clearly articulated.  A more detailed discussion of how the theoretical results translate into concrete improvements in q-bit compressive sensing would strengthen this section.
* **Appendix Dependence:** The bulk of the technical details are relegated to the appendix. While this is common, it makes it harder for a quick assessment of the complete technical argument during the review process.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically significant result that bridges a gap between linear and non-linear measurement models.
* The derived error formula provides a precise characterization of LASSO performance in non-linear settings, offering valuable insights for algorithm design and analysis.
* The paper is technically sound, well-written, and thoroughly motivated.
* The potential applications, particularly in non-linear sensing, are promising.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The reliance on Gaussian measurement matrices limits the generality of the results.
* The q-bit compressive sensing application section is somewhat superficial.
* The heavy reliance on the appendix for technical details.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant theoretical contribution. The results are novel, technically sound, and have the potential to impact the field of compressive sensing and related areas. While the limitations regarding the Gaussian assumption and the underdeveloped application section are valid concerns, they do not outweigh the paper's strengths. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.** I recommend acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the following:

1. **Discussion of Generalization:** Briefly discuss the possibility of extending the results to non-Gaussian measurement matrices.
2. **Clarification of Assumption 1:** Provide a more detailed discussion of how to verify Assumption 1 in common scenarios.
3. **Strengthen q-bit Application:** Elaborate on the practical implications of the results for q-bit compressive sensing, potentially including numerical examples or a comparison with existing algorithms.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** Excellent. The paper is technically rigorous and offers a significant contribution to the field.
* **Clarity:** Very Good. The paper is well-written and organized.
* **Originality:** Excellent. The core result is highly original and provides a novel perspective on LASSO estimation.
* **Significance:** Excellent. The results have the potential to significantly impact the field of compressive sensing and related areas.
