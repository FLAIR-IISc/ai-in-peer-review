## Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the performance of the Generalized LASSO algorithm when applied to estimation problems with non-linear measurements of the form `yi = gi(a^T i x0)`. The core contribution is demonstrating that, asymptotically, the error behavior of the LASSO with non-linear measurements is equivalent to that of a LASSO applied to *scaled and noisy linear measurements*. Specifically, the authors identify a scaling factor `µ` and noise variance `σ^2` characterizing this equivalence, defined in terms of the link function `g`. They derive a precise error formula and demonstrate its accuracy through simulations, relating it to existing work on LASSO with linear measurements. The paper builds upon recent results in high-dimensional statistics and compressive sensing and provides a theoretical foundation for understanding the use of LASSO in scenarios where the measurement model deviates from linearity. They also apply their results to the specific case of q-bit compressive sensing, providing a framework for optimizing quantization levels.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous mathematical analysis, building upon established frameworks like the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem and recent advances in LASSO error analysis. The proof appears technically sound and provides valuable insights.
* **Novel and Significant Result:** The equivalence result is a significant contribution. It offers a powerful simplification for analyzing non-linear measurement problems, allowing researchers to leverage the extensive literature on linear LASSO.
* **Generality:** The results hold under quite general assumptions on the link function `g` and the regularizer `f`, enhancing the paper’s broad applicability.
* **Practical Implications:**  The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates the practical utility of the findings and provides a concrete example of how the theoretical results can be used to optimize system performance.  The connection to the Lloyd-Max algorithm is a nice touch.
* **Clear Presentation (mostly):** The introduction clearly motivates the problem and summarizes the key contributions. Theorems are clearly stated.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Notationally Dense:** The paper is highly theoretical and employs a lot of mathematical notation. While necessary for precision, it can make the paper difficult to follow for readers less familiar with the field. Some definitions could be more intuitive.
* **Limited Empirical Validation:** While the authors provide figures illustrating the accuracy of their error prediction, the empirical validation is relatively limited. It would be beneficial to include more diverse examples, potentially with varying signal structures and link functions. The figures are difficult to interpret without carefully reading the detailed explanations in the text.
* **Assumptions:** While the authors acknowledge the assumptions, the impact of violating these assumptions (specifically Assumption 1) isn't fully discussed.  A more detailed discussion of the limitations of the analysis would be helpful.
* **Connection to Related Work:** While the literature review is extensive, it feels somewhat like a list of references rather than a cohesive narrative. A more focused discussion on *how* this work significantly advances beyond existing results would strengthen the paper.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  The paper is technically sound and provides a complete piece of work, given the scope. Claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. The authors are careful in stating their assumptions and acknowledging limitations. **Strong.**
* **Clarity:** The paper could be more accessible. While mathematically rigorous, the density of notation and the lack of intuitive explanations hinder readability. **Moderate.**
* **Originality:** The equivalence result and the precise error analysis for non-linear measurements are novel contributions. The application to q-bit sensing, while not entirely new, is well-integrated. **Strong.**
* **Significance:** The results are likely to be influential in the field of compressive sensing and statistical estimation. The simplification provided by the equivalence result will facilitate further research on non-linear measurement models. **Strong.**



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant theoretical result with broad implications.
* The analysis is rigorous and well-supported.
* The application to q-bit sensing demonstrates practical relevance.
* The paper addresses a challenging problem in a novel and insightful way.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper’s density and technicality might limit its accessibility to a broader audience.
* The empirical validation could be more comprehensive.
* A more focused discussion of the relationship to existing work would be beneficial.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field. The theoretical result is novel and important, and the analysis is rigorous. While the paper could benefit from improved clarity and more comprehensive empirical validation, the strengths outweigh the weaknesses.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the empirical results. Specifically, I suggest the authors:

*   Add more intuitive explanations of the key concepts and results.
*   Include a more diverse set of examples in the empirical validation.
*   Provide a more focused discussion of the relationship to existing work, highlighting the unique contributions of this paper.
*  Briefly discuss the consequences of violating Assumption 1.
