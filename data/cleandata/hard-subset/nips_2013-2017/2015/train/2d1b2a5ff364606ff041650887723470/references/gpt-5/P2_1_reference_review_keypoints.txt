- Main claim: Proves asymptotic equivalence between generalized LASSO with non-linear single-index measurements and an effective linear model y = μAx0 + σz with μ = E[γg(γ)], σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−μγ)^2].  
- Precise MSE: Derives an exact high-dimensional MSE characterization via a three-variable convex program depending on δ, λ, (μ,σ), and a Moreau-envelope-based functional F.  
- Scope: Covers broad convex regularizers (not just ℓ1), includes sparse and group-sparse specializations, and discusses nuclear-norm low-rank cases.  
- Quantization application: Uses equivalence to design optimal q-bit quantizers by minimizing σ^2/μ^2 and connects to Lloyd–Max.  
- Relation to prior work: Unifies and extends Brillinger’s consistency, Plan–Vershynin bounds, and CGMT/AMP precise LASSO analyses by providing an asymptotically exact mapping.  
- Technical soundness: Results are carefully stated with standard CGMT-style assumptions and plausible given Gaussian rotational invariance; scalar program matches linear-measurement limits.  
- Numerical support: Experiments (as described) corroborate theoretical predictions.  
- Assumption concerns: Assumption 1 (Moreau-envelope convergence) is technical and opaque; verified for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2 but nuclear-norm treatment is incomplete.  
- Design limitation: Analysis relies critically on Gaussian A; no robustness claims to sub-Gaussian or correlated designs.  
- Asymptotics only: Results are asymptotic with no finite-sample deviation bounds or convergence rates provided.  
- μ requirement: Equivalence requires μ ≠ 0; handling or estimating μ when g is unknown is minimally discussed.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and explains μ,σ roles, but needs more intuition for Assumption 1 and a list of regularizers where it holds.  
- Low-rank clarity: Low-rank section lacks a concrete worked example (e.g., spiked model) and explicit expression/evaluation of F.  
- Probability notation: Should clarify the meaning of “with probability one” and whether the same A is used for linear and non-linear problems.  
- Suggested extensions: Provide intuition/proofs for Assumption 1 across regularizers, explore sub-Gaussian or non-isotropic designs, and add robustness experiments.  
- Practical guidance: Recommend adding methods to estimate μ and σ from data and assessing the effect of plug-in estimation on performance.  
- Empirical improvements: More diverse simulations (non-Gaussian x0, different g, finite-n behavior) would strengthen claims.  
- Overall judgment: Highly original, significant, and technically solid contribution; reviewer recommends strong accept.
