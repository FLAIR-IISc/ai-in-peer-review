- Core result: Asymptotic equivalence of Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements to LASSO on scaled, noisy linear measurements, with explicit scaling/noise parameters from g.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides precise asymptotic error formulas (not just bounds) for a broad class of link functions g and regularizers f.  
- Methodology: Builds on recent precise analyses of LASSO with linear measurements and extends them to the non-linear single-index model.  
- Relation to prior work: Connects to Brillinger (least squares consistency), Plan & Vershynin (constrained LASSO), and q-bit compressive sensing literature.  
- Rigor: Mathematical derivations appear thorough and well-justified using high-dimensional statistics and random matrix tools.  
- Practical implication: Application to q-bit compressive sensing and a Lloyd–Max–based algorithm illustrate real-world relevance.  
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-written, clearly motivated, and logically organized.  
- Assumption concern: Assumption 1 (limit involving prox of f*) is opaque and needs more intuitive explanation and broader applicability beyond ℓ1.  
- Computational issue: Computing the error formulas in complex settings may be challenging; more discussion of computational/approximation strategies is needed.  
- Empirical validation: Numerical experiments are limited (Figure 1 promising), should be expanded across more g, f, and signal structures.  
- Finite-sample/robustness: Lacks discussion on translation to finite dimensions and non-Gaussian measurement vectors.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, significant theoretical result with precise analysis and practical connections; strong writing and impact potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Opaque key assumption and limited empirical validation raise questions about practical robustness and applicability.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, impactful paper whose strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses.  
- Recommendation & rating: Strong Accept; recommended for NIPS, rating 8.5/10.
