Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies high-dimensional estimation from single-index (non-linear) measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0), with Gaussian design a_i, using the generalized LASSO with convex regularizer f and square-root data-fidelity. The main result is an asymptotic equivalence: for a wide class of link functions g and regularizers f, the estimation error of LASSO under non-linear measurements is the same as the error under a corresponding linear model with effective scaling µ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − µγ)^2], γ ~ N(0,1). Formally, as n,m→∞ with m/n→δ, the error ‖x̂ − µx0‖ converges to that under linear observations ylin = A(µx0) + σ z. Beyond equivalence (Theorem 2.1), the paper gives a precise asymptotic error characterization (Theorem 2.2) via a three-scalar convex optimization whose objective depends on a function F encoding f and the distribution of x0 through a Moreau-envelope limit. Specialization yields explicit characterizations for sparse (Theorem 2.3 via soft-thresholding), group-sparse (vector soft-thresholding), and a sketch for low-rank (nuclear norm), together with an application to optimal q-bit quantization design via minimizing σ^2/µ^2, connecting to Lloyd–Max.

This work extends classical results: Brillinger (1982) showed LS under link violations is consistent up to scale in fixed-n; Plan & Vershynin (2015) provided non-asymptotic upper bounds for constrained LASSO under single-index models. The present paper advances precise asymptotics for regularized LASSO under non-linear links, building on recent precise analyses for linear measurements via AMP and the CGMT (e.g., Bayati–Montanari; Stojnic; Oymak–Thrampoulidis–Hassibi; Thrampoulidis et al.). The claimed novelty is the exact non-linear→linear reduction and precise scalar error formulae for general convex regularizers and all λ.

Quality (technical soundness):
- The modeling assumptions are clear: i.i.d. Gaussian A, single-index g with finite nonzero µ, σ^2, linear asymptotics m/n→δ, convex f satisfying a normalization, and a probabilistic model for x0 capturing structure.
- Theorems are stated with explicit conditions. Theorem 2.1’s reduction is plausible under CGMT. Theorem 2.2 yields a convex scalar program for the limiting error, with F defined via a Moreau-envelope limit (Assumption 1). The sparse and group-sparse cases verify Assumption 1 and produce computable expressions; the sparse result recovers and generalizes prior precise LASSO risk formulas.
- The low-rank case’s verification and computation of F are only sketched; details are deferred.
- The application to quantization is consistent with the reduction: optimizing σ^2/µ^2 is a principled surrogate for minimizing asymptotic error; the Lloyd–Max connection is justified.
- A few statements would benefit from precision: Theorem 2.1 says “with probability one, ‖x̂ − µx0‖2 = ‖x̂lin − µx0‖2 in the limit,” which should more formally read “their limits coincide almost surely” (equality of random limits, not of finite-n quantities).

Clarity:
- The paper is generally well organized, with a clear motivation, assumptions, and progressive specialization. Notation is mostly standard.
- Some notation is overloaded and could confuse readers: x0 denotes both the original unknown and the normalized vector, and x0 := x0/‖x0‖2 is introduced—renaming one of these would improve clarity.
- There are typos and minor grammatical issues (“mesaurements,” “eventhough,” “redifining,” “prism”), and some displays have formatting glitches (e.g., “n e√n(f∗)(n),c3”).
- Assumption 1 is central; providing more intuition and a checklist for common f (e.g., nuclear norm) would help practitioners assess applicability.
- The mapping between the chosen square-root LASSO objective and the more common squared-loss LASSO is discussed; adding a brief note on how results adapt to the latter would improve completeness.

Originality:
- The equivalence of non-linear single-index measurements to effective linear measurements in precise high-dimensional asymptotics for regularized LASSO appears novel. Prior work provided non-asymptotic bounds for constrained LASSO; this work yields exact asymptotics for penalized LASSO and general convex f.
- The precise error characterizations for group-sparse and, partially, low-rank regularization under non-linear links seem new.
- The quantizer design via σ^2/µ^2 with a Lloyd–Max connection is a useful, original application.

Significance:
- The reduction provides a unifying lens to analyze and tune LASSO under a broad class of non-linearities (1-bit, Tobit, quantization), leveraging existing linear-measurement theory. This is practically impactful and conceptually clarifying.
- The scalar programs give concrete performance predictions and can guide λ selection and quantizer design.
- Limitations (Gaussian designs, µ≠0, asymptotic regime) narrow scope but still cover many important cases.

Pros:
- Clear, principled reduction from non-linear to linear models with effective parameters (µ, σ^2).
- Precise asymptotic error formulas via a low-dimensional convex program; explicit results for sparsity.
- Extends beyond ℓ1 to general convex regularizers; covers group-sparsity and sketches low-rank.
- Useful application to quantization design; connects to Lloyd–Max.
- Situates contributions with respect to Brillinger, Plan–Vershynin, AMP/CGMT literature.

Cons:
- Strong reliance on Gaussian design; robustness to sub-Gaussian/structured designs is not addressed.
- Excludes µ = 0 links (e.g., even g); no treatment of that important corner case.
- Assumption 1 is technical and not fully verified for nuclear norm; low-rank section lacks detailed derivations or experiments.
- Theorem 2.1 phrasing could be more precise about convergence versus equality.
- Practical estimation of µ and σ^2 when g is unknown is not discussed.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Clarify Theorem 2.1 to state equality of almost sure limits, not of finite-n quantities.
- Resolve notation conflicts for x0 versus its normalized version; streamline footnotes.
- Expand the low-rank section: verify Assumption 1 under concrete generative models, provide numerical evaluation of F, and add experiments.
- Discuss robustness beyond Gaussian designs and any extensions via universality.
- Briefly indicate how to adapt results to the standard squared-loss LASSO and how λ should scale.
- For unknown g, comment on estimating µ and σ^2 (e.g., method-of-moments) or on direction-only recovery.
- Proof sketches or an outline of the CGMT argument would aid readability.

Overall assessment:
Within its assumptions, the paper provides technically sound and novel precise asymptotics that unify the analysis of LASSO under non-linear measurements by reduction to an effective linear model. The results are significant for both theory and practice. Addressing clarity points and expanding the low-rank case would further strengthen the submission.
