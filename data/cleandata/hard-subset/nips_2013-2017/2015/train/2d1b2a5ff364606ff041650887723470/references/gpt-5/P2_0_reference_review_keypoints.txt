- Main result: Proves an asymptotically exact equivalence between LASSO under non-linear single-index measurements and LASSO under an effective linear model with µ = E[γg(γ)] and σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−µγ)^2].
- Theorem 2.1: Formalizes the equivalence (should be phrased as convergence of error differences rather than pathwise equality).
- Theorem 2.2: Gives a scalar convex program whose unique optimizer α* equals the asymptotic error ‖x̂−µx0‖.
- Theorem 2.3 (sparsity): Provides closed-form fixed-point equations for sparse signals, including a phase transition λcrit.
- Coverage: Framework handles `1, group `1,2, and sketches nuclear-norm/low-rank extensions.
- Application: Shows optimal q-bit quantizer design for LASSO reduces to minimizing σ^2/µ^2 and connects to Lloyd–Max stationary points.
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger (fixed n) and sharpens Plan & Vershynin’s non-asymptotic bounds to precise asymptotics for regularized LASSO.
- Methodology: Extends CGMT/Moreau-envelope analysis to non-linear links, yielding new exact formulas even for some linear cases.
- Technical soundness: Reduction to an effective linear model and the CGMT-based scalar characterization are compelling and consistent with known results.
- Assumptions: Results depend on i.i.d. Gaussian A, independent g_i, bounded µ, µ≠0, and an abstract Assumption 1 (existence of a Moreau envelope limit).
- Assumption 1 critique: Reasonable but opaque; authors verify for `1 and `1,2 and only sketch low-rank—suggest adding precise low-rank conditions.
- Empirical support: Figures reportedly validate theory across links and structures, but more finite-n robustness experiments (varying n, misspecified λ) would strengthen the paper.
- Clarity: Overall narrative is clear but Theorem 2.1 wording, Assumption 1, and nonstandard normalizations need sharpening and brief explanatory remarks.
- Minor issues: Typos, occasional grammar errors, and some incomplete references should be fixed.
- Originality: Novel exact asymptotics and the succinct µ,σ summary of g’s effect are original and useful.
- Significance: The result unifies many non-linear channels and provides practical guidance (e.g., quantizer design), though limited by Gaussian design and asymptotic regime.
- Strengths: Clean general equivalence, broad coverage of convex regularizers, new exact results for linear cases, and a concrete application to quantized sensing.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: Discuss extensions to sub-Gaussian/correlated designs, add a theorem giving sufficient conditions for Assumption 1 (including nuclear norm), clarify why minimizing σ^2/µ^2 is sufficient, and discuss estimation of µ,σ and λ tuning in practice.
- Related work gap: Recommend citing generalized AMP/GAMP literature to contrast estimator and analysis differences.
- Recommendation: Strong accept with minor clarifications; solid theoretical contribution with practical implications.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, technically solid, broadly applicable, and connects to practical design problems.
- Rejection arguments: Restrictive Gaussian design, some opaque assumptions/notation, and limited finite-sample empirical exploration.
