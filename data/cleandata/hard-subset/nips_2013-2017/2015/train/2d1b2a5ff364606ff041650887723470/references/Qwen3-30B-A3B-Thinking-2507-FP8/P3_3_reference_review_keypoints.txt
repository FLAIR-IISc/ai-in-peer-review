- Main contribution: proves an asymptotic equivalence between LASSO with non-linear measurements and LASSO with appropriately scaled linear measurements.  
- Key insight: the non-linear link g is fully characterized by two parameters μ and σ^2, reducing the non-linear problem to a linear one.  
- Theoretical precision: provides exact asymptotic error formulas (Theorems 2.1–2.2) rather than loose upper bounds.  
- Methodology: rigorous analysis using the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) framework.  
- Generality: framework applies to a wide class of regularizers (e.g., sparse, group-sparse, nuclear norm) and link functions.  
- Practical application: shows utility for q-bit compressive sensing and how to optimize quantization by minimizing σ^2/μ^2.  
- Builds on prior work: advances beyond Plan and Vershynin by giving precise asymptotics where prior results were loose non-asymptotic bounds.  
- Limitation — asymptotic: results hold in the n,m → ∞ regime with fixed δ, raising questions about finite-sample behavior and convergence rates.  
- Limitation — normalization: assumes ‖x_0‖_2 = 1 (restrictive), with generalization noted but not incorporated into main results.  
- Limitation — experiments: limited numerical validation; more diverse simulations (varying δ, different g) would strengthen claims.  
- Limitation — computation: error formula requires solving a three-variable convex program, but computational cost and practicality are not discussed.  
- Overall assessment: significant, rigorous theoretical advance with clear practical implications; recommended for acceptance.
