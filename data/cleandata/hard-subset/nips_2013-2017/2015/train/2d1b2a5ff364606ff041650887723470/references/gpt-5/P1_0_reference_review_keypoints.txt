- Main result: Asymptotic MSE of generalized LASSO with non-linear single-index measurements equals that of a matched linear model with effective gain µ = E[γg(γ)] and noise σ^2 = E[(g(γ)−µγ)^2] for Gaussian A (µ ≠ 0).
- Theorem 2.1: Shows equality/convergence of asymptotic MSEs between the non-linear problem and the matched linear problem (should state as almost-sure limits).
- Theorem 2.2: Reduces asymptotic MSE to an explicit scalar convex program in three variables; its optimizer α* gives the limit ‖x̂−µx0‖^2 → α*^2 and incorporates δ, λ, (µ,σ^2), and signal structure via a functional F.
- Specializations: Provides precise formulas for ℓ1-regularized sparse recovery (Theorem 2.3) and extensions to group-sparse (ℓ1,2) and a framework outline for low-rank/nuclear-norm cases.
- Quantization application: For q-bit quantized compressed sensing, minimizing σ^2/µ^2 over thresholds/levels optimizes asymptotic LASSO MSE; Lloyd–Max is justified as a stationary-point method for this objective.
- Methodology: Analysis leverages the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) and a decomposition of g(a^T x0) into an effective linear part plus uncorrelated noise, relying critically on Gaussian A.
- Relation to prior work: Generalizes Brillinger’s single-index LS results and sharpens Plan–Vershynin’s bounds by providing asymptotically exact regularized-LASSO errors; builds on AMP/CGMT LASSO analyses.
- Technical soundness: Approach and conclusions are well supported; sparse-case derivations recover and extend known exact LASSO formulas.
- Assumptions: Gaussian design and µ ≠ 0 clearly stated; Assumption 1 (existence of F) is nontrivial but justified for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2; nuclear-norm conditions are sketched but not fully developed.
- Completeness: Theory is complete for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2; low-rank/nuclear-norm section outlines application but leaves computation of F partially open.
- Experiments: Reportedly validate predictions across nonlinearities and structures, but would benefit from more quantitative comparisons (especially group-sparse/low-rank and across λ and δ).
- Clarity/suggestions: Revise Theorem 2.1 wording to convergence, provide intuition/recipes/examples for Assumption 1 and computing F (e.g., fused-LASSO), fix notation/typos and scaling convention inconsistencies, clarify boundedness/role of µ.
- Originality: High — first asymptotically exact characterization of generalized LASSO under broad single-index nonlinearities, unifying them via (µ,σ^2).
- Significance: High — conceptually impactful and practically useful; allows porting precise linear-LASSO error predictions to nonlinear settings and informs quantizer design.
- Pros (summary): Elegant equivalence reducing nonlinearity to two scalars; computable asymptotic formulas for general regularizers; strong links to influential prior work; practical quantization insight; clear assumptions and discussion.
- Cons (summary): Restricted to Gaussian measurements; excludes µ = 0 cases; Assumption 1 abstract and low-rank case incomplete; experiments and reproducibility materials (code/recipes) lacking; minor clarity/notation issues.
- Key questions for authors: (1) Can results extend to sub-Gaussian or rotationally invariant A and which proof steps require exact Gaussianity? (2) Can µ = 0 cases be characterized (direction-only estimators/modified objectives)? (3) Provide a concrete spiked-model example where F for nuclear norm is computed. (4) If g unknown, how well can µ and σ be estimated from data and how sensitive is performance to estimation error? (5) Provide guidance/code for numerically solving (10) and computing F for common f.
- Minor issues/typos: Fix spelling errors (“mesaurements”, “redifining”, “eventhough”), clarify Theorem 2.1 convergence wording, define or remove ζ, and clarify λcrit/regime interpretation in Theorem 2.3.
- Recommendation: Accept.
