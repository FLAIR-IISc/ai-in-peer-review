- Main contribution: Establishes that Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO with linear measurements scaled by µ and noise variance σ² determined by the link g.  
- Theorem 2.1: Proves the asymptotic error of non-linear measurement LASSO matches that of the corresponding scaled linear measurement LASSO.  
- Theorem 2.2: Provides a precise asymptotic error formula capturing the roles of g, signal structure, regularizer f, and λ.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Brillinger and Plan & Vershynin, extending results to a wide class of regularizers and all λ values.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous proofs, clear assumptions, and sound derivations under stated conditions.  
- Originality: Novel insight that non-linear measurements reduce to scaled noisy linear ones, generalizing prior special-case results.  
- Significance: Immediate implications for applications like 1-bit and q-bit compressive sensing and better parameter tuning.  
- Practical application: Offers a principled approach to quantizer design (q-bit compressive sensing) and practical error predictions.  
- Clarity: Well-written, organized, consistent notation, and illustrative figures validating theory.  
- Limitation — Gaussian assumption: Reliance on i.i.d. Gaussian measurement matrices restricts applicability to non-Gaussian settings.  
- Limitation — µ and σ² estimation: Does not provide methods for estimating µ and σ² when the link function g is unknown, hindering practical use.  
- Limitation — Asymptotic focus: Results are asymptotic (n → ∞) and may not directly translate to small-sample practical scenarios.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance due to novelty, rigor, and significance.  
- Actionable suggestions: Add discussion on estimating µ and σ² in practice and clarify/acknowledge Gaussian measurement limitations and directions for non-Gaussian extensions.  
- Pros for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance and unifying framework for LASSO with non-linear measurements.  
- Cons for acceptance: Gaussian measurement assumption and lack of guidance on estimating µ and σ² in practice.
