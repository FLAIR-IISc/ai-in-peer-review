Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies generalized LASSO with non-linear single-index measurements yi = gi(a_i^T x0), where A has i.i.d. Gaussian entries and gi are i.i.d. copies of a (possibly random) link function g. The main result (Theorem 2.1) establishes that, in high-dimensional Gaussian designs, the asymptotic mean-squared error (MSE) of the generalized LASSO with such non-linear measurements is exactly equal to the asymptotic MSE under a linear Gaussian model with an effective scaling µ = E[γ g(γ)] and effective noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − µγ)^2], γ∼N(0,1). Thus non-linear measurements are “equivalent,” for LASSO error, to a scaled-and-noisy linear model. Theorem 2.2 then gives an explicit scalar convex optimization whose unique solution α* yields the MSE limit: lim ‖x̂ − µx0‖^2 = α*^2. The error expression is general, capturing δ = m/n, λ, the link function (through µ, σ^2), and the structure/regularizer (via an abstract functional F derived from the Moreau envelope of the conjugate f*). Specializations include sparse recovery with ℓ1 (Theorem 2.3), group-sparse (ℓ1,2), and a discussion for low-rank (nuclear norm). An application to q-bit quantized compressive sensing shows that minimizing the LASSO error reduces to minimizing σ^2/µ^2 over quantizer thresholds and levels, and argues that Lloyd–Max yields stationary points for this objective.

The work extends classic results of Brillinger (asymptotics for LS with non-linear link in fixed-n, m→∞) by (i) moving to the proportional high-dimensional regime m/n→δ and (ii) replacing LS by generalized LASSO. It advances beyond Plan and Vershynin’s constrained LASSO bounds for non-linear measurements by providing asymptotically exact formulas (rather than upper bounds with unknown constants) and by treating the regularized (penalized) LASSO. It builds on precise LASSO analyses with linear measurements via AMP and the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT), generalizing to broad convex regularizers through the functional F.

Quality (technical soundness)
- Assumptions: A is i.i.d. Gaussian; gi i.i.d.; µ, σ^2 finite with µ≠0; proportional asymptotics m/n→δ; x0 drawn from a distribution with coordinate-wise bounded second moments; convex regularizer f with controlled growth; Assumption 1 requiring the pointwise limit of the normalized Moreau envelope of f* exists.
- Main claims: (i) asymptotic “nonlinear = linear” equivalence for LASSO error; (ii) a scalar convex program for exact MSE; (iii) concrete characterizations for ℓ1 and ℓ1,2; (iv) an application to optimal quantization design via minimizing σ^2/µ^2.
- Soundness: The results are consistent with prior CGMT/AMP analyses in the linear case and with Brillinger/Plan–Vershynin in terms of the role of µ, σ^2. The reduction to a scalar convex program and the specializations (Theorem 2.3) align with known exact formulas for ℓ2-LASSO with ℓ1 in Gaussian designs. The “equivalence” statement is asymptotic; clarifying that it concerns equality of limits (or almost sure convergence of the difference to zero) would improve precision. Assumption 1 is abstract but verified for important cases (ℓ1, ℓ1,2), and reasonable conditions for nuclear norm are sketched.
- Completeness: Proofs are deferred to the appendix; the main text provides intuition and concrete instantiations. The low-rank case is not fully worked out (F left for future work). Non-Gaussian/sub-Gaussian designs are not covered.

Clarity
The exposition is generally clear and well-organized, with a logical progression from model to general theorem to special cases and application. Notation is heavy but standard. Suggestions:
- State Theorem 2.1 explicitly as equality of limits or almost sure convergence of the difference to zero for complete clarity.
- Provide a brief proof sketch of the CGMT-based reduction in the main text.
- Give more guidance on computing F for common regularizers, and explicitly discuss numerical solution of the scalar program (10).
- Fix minor typos and unify footnotes.

Originality
The asymptotically exact reduction of generalized LASSO with non-linear single-index measurements to an effective linear Gaussian model, with a precise MSE formula valid for broad convex regularizers, appears novel. Prior work provided bounds (Plan–Vershynin) or exact formulas for linear measurements under specific regularizers/distributions; extending exactness to the non-linear setting and to general convex f via the functional F is a substantive contribution.

Significance
The results provide a unifying and simple lens—via (µ, σ^2)—to understand and predict LASSO performance under a wide class of non-linearities, including quantization and censoring. This enables principled tuning and design (e.g., quantizer optimization), and transfers a large body of linear-AMP/CGMT results to non-linear measurement regimes. Given the prevalence of non-linear sensing (quantized CS, 1-bit CS, generalized linear models), this is impactful.

Pros
- Precise asymptotic characterization, not just bounds.
- Broad applicability to convex regularizers and multiple structures.
- Clear identification of effective parameters (µ, σ^2) governing performance.
- Concrete specialization to sparse and group-sparse; quantization design insight.
- Extends and unifies several lines of literature.

Cons
- Restricted to i.i.d. Gaussian designs; no results for sub-Gaussian/structured A.
- Assumption 1 is abstract; low-rank case left at a high level.
- Asymptotic-only; finite-sample guidance not addressed.
- Theorem 2.1 phrasing could be tightened to avoid ambiguity about limits.
- Empirical validation is mentioned but limited details are provided in the excerpt.

Questions and suggestions for the authors
- Can the CGMT-based proof be adapted to sub-Gaussian designs or to Gaussian rows with covariance (i.e., ellipsoidal designs)? If not, what breaks?
- For the nuclear norm case, can you provide a worked example (e.g., spiked model) where F is computed via the limiting singular value distribution?
- Is µ estimable from data when g is unknown, and how sensitive is the equivalence to misspecification of µ, σ^2?
- Can you comment on finite-sample corrections or concentration rates of the error around the limit?
- In the quantization section, include experiments across q and δ showing that designs minimizing σ^2/µ^2 indeed minimize LASSO error in practice.

Recommendation
Accept. The paper is technically solid, novel, and significant, with clear implications for a broad class of non-linear sensing problems. Minor clarifications and additional examples would further strengthen the presentation.
