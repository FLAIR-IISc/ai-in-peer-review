- Main result: Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements yi = g(a_i^T x0) is asymptotically equivalent to a scaled noisy linear model characterized by scale µ and noise variance σ² under Gaussian measurements.
- Provides precise asymptotic error characterization that depends on λ, the regularizer f, the link function g, and the ratio m/n.
- Result applies to a broad class of link functions g and regularizers f.
- Connects to and generalizes prior work (Brillinger; Plan & Vershynin) and compressed sensing literature.
- Practical application: proposes a q-bit compressive sensing application and an algorithm for optimal quantization levels (Lloyd–Max based).
- Significant theoretical contribution: novel and powerful simplification for non-linear measurement analysis.
- Mathematical analysis is rigorous and delivers exact asymptotic bounds.
- Presentation is generally clear and well-structured, though mathematically dense.
- Strong assumptions (Gaussian measurement matrices, unit-norm signals, specific regularizer/signal conditions) may limit applicability; sensitivity to assumption violations is under-discussed.
- Technical density may restrict accessibility to non-expert readers.
- Lloyd–Max algorithm discussion is somewhat hand-wavy; needs more justification for convergence to a stationary point.
- Empirical validation is limited; more experiments across link functions and regularizers would strengthen the paper.
- Frequent footnotes disrupt the flow; some content should be integrated into the main text.
- Review scores: Quality—Excellent (technically sound); Clarity—Good; Originality—Excellent; Significance—Excellent.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous result with practical relevance and generalization of existing work.
- Arguments against acceptance: high technical density, strong assumptions, and limited empirical evidence.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, strongly recommended for acceptance with suggestions to add illustrative examples, discuss assumption limitations, and better justify the Lloyd–Max connection.
