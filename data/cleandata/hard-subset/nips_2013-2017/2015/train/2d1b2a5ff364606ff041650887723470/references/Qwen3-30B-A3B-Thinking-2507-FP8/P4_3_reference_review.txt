

# Review of "LASSO with Non-linear Measurements is Equivalent to One With Linear Measurements"

This paper investigates the Generalized LASSO estimator when applied to non-linear measurements of the form yi = gi(ai^T x0). The authors establish a fundamental equivalence between the performance of the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements and that of a Generalized LASSO with appropriately scaled and noisy linear measurements. The key insight is that non-linear measurements can be treated as linear measurements scaled by μ = E[γg(γ)] and with noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) - μγ)^2], where γ ~ N(0, 1). This work extends previous results by Brillinger (1980s) and Plan & Vershynin (2015), who considered the constrained LASSO and provided only upper bounds, to the more commonly used regularized Generalized LASSO with precise error characterizations.

The paper makes several significant contributions. First, Theorem 2.1 establishes the asymptotic equivalence between non-linear and linear measurement regimes. Second, Theorem 2.2 provides a precise error formula for the Generalized LASSO with non-linear measurements, which is a deterministic convex optimization problem with three scalar variables. The paper then specializes this general result to sparse, group-sparse, and low-rank signal recovery, providing explicit error formulas for each case. The application to q-bit compressive sensing (Section 2.3) demonstrates the practical implications of the theoretical results, showing how to optimize quantization thresholds and levels to minimize estimation error.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper makes rigorous mathematical arguments with careful attention to assumptions (i.i.d. Gaussian measurements, convex regularizers, etc.). The proofs are referenced to the appendix, which is acceptable for a conference paper. The authors properly contextualize their work within the relevant literature, particularly building on Plan & Vershynin (2015) and recent advances in precise error analysis for the LASSO with linear measurements.

2. **Novelty:** The paper provides a precise characterization of the LASSO with non-linear measurements, going beyond previous work that only provided upper bounds. The equivalence result (Theorem 2.1) is elegant and fundamentally important. The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates practical significance.

3. **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized, with clear notation and well-structured presentation. The main results are clearly stated, and the paper provides concrete examples (Figures 1-2) that illustrate the theoretical findings. The special cases (sparse, group-sparse, low-rank) are well-motivated and demonstrate the generality of the approach.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Assumptions:** The results rely on strong assumptions (i.i.d. Gaussian measurements, convex regularizers, asymptotic regime with n→∞, m/n=δ). While these are standard in high-dimensional statistics, the paper could better discuss the limitations of these assumptions for practical applications.

2. **Empirical Validation:** While Figures 1-2 show promising results, the paper lacks detailed experimental validation beyond these figures. More comprehensive empirical results would strengthen the claims about the accuracy of the theoretical predictions.

3. **Comparison to Related Work:** The paper references recent work on precise error analysis for the LASSO with linear measurements (e.g., Thrampoulidis et al., 2015), but doesn't clearly articulate how their technical approach differs from these works. The connection between their framework and the Convex Gaussian Min-max Theorem (CGMT) used in previous work could be clarified.

**Pro and Con Arguments for Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- The equivalence result (Theorem 2.1) is elegant and fundamentally important for understanding the LASSO's behavior with non-linear measurements.
- The precise error characterization (Theorem 2.2) significantly advances the field beyond previous upper bounds.
- The application to q-bit compressive sensing demonstrates clear practical significance.
- The paper is technically sound and builds upon a solid foundation of previous work.

*Con:*
- The strong assumptions limit the immediate applicability of the results.
- Limited empirical validation beyond Figures 1-2.
- The paper could better clarify the technical distinctions from recent work on precise error analysis.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to the understanding of the LASSO with non-linear measurements. The key equivalence result provides a fundamental insight that will inform future research and applications in compressed sensing, signal processing, and high-dimensional statistics. The precise error characterization is a substantial improvement over previous work that only provided upper bounds. While the paper makes strong assumptions that limit immediate applicability to some practical settings, the theoretical results are sound and the implications for q-bit compressive sensing demonstrate clear practical relevance.

The paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a novel contribution that advances the state of the art in high-dimensional statistics. It is clearly in scope for NIPS, as it addresses a fundamental problem in signal recovery with important connections to machine learning and statistics.

**Recommendation:** Accept
