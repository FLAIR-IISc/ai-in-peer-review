- Main result: Generalized LASSO with nonlinear Gaussian measurements is asymptotically equivalent to LASSO on linear measurements with effective gain µ = E[γ g(γ)] and noise variance σ^2 = E[(g(γ) − µγ)^2].  
- Theorem 2.1: Asymptotic squared error ‖x̂ − µx0‖^2 matches that of linear model y_lin = A(µx0) + σz.  
- Theorem 2.2: Exact error characterized via a scalar convex optimization depending on δ, λ, f, px0 and g only through µ and σ.  
- Specializations: Explicit formulas provided for ℓ1 (sparse) and ℓ1,2 (group-sparse); low-rank (nuclear norm) setup given but not fully evaluated.  
- Application: Quantizer design reduced to minimizing σ^2/µ^2; Lloyd–Max shown to converge to stationary points for this objective.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Brillinger’s single-index consistency to high-dimensional regularized LASSO and sharpens Plan–Vershynin’s non-asymptotic bounds with exact asymptotics.  
- Connections: Consistent with Bussgang-type decompositions and AMP/GAMP insights; citing signal-processing literature would strengthen context.  
- Technical soundness: Main theorems plausible and align with Gaussian design phenomena; CGMT is an appropriate rigorous tool.  
- Key assumption: Gaussian design is critical; Assumption 1 (Moreau envelope limit) is opaque but verified for common penalties like ℓ1 and ℓ1,2.  
- Identifiability: Requirement µ ≠ 0 is important and addressed; direction-only recovery noted when µ = 0.  
- Completeness: Sparse/group-sparse cases are complete with empirical validation; low-rank case lacks full evaluation (F not computed).  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured and readable but notation is heavy; needs clearer guidance on Assumption 1 and estimating µ, σ when g is unknown.  
- Presentation issues: Minor typos and missing references (e.g., AMP/GAMP, Bussgang) should be fixed.  
- Originality: Novel and important equivalence and scalar error characterization; extension to general convex regularizers via CGMT is notable.  
- Significance: High — provides practical bridge to reuse linear LASSO theory for nonlinear/unknown links, useful for tuning and algorithm design.  
- Strengths: Conceptually clean equivalence, precise asymptotics, multiple structure coverage, empirical validation, and practical quantizer design application.  
- Weaknesses: Restricted to Gaussian designs, Assumption 1 is abstract, low-rank case incomplete, and limited guidance for estimating µ, σ in practice.  
- Suggested improvements: Discuss extensions to sub-Gaussian/rotational ensembles, broaden and exemplify Assumption 1, provide estimator and sensitivity analysis for µ and σ, add finite-sample experiments, and expand quantization details.  
- Acceptance arguments: Strong, novel theoretical contribution with practical impact and good empirical support.  
- Rejection arguments: Gaussian-only setting, abstract assumptions, and limited practical guidance for unknown g.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (strong).
