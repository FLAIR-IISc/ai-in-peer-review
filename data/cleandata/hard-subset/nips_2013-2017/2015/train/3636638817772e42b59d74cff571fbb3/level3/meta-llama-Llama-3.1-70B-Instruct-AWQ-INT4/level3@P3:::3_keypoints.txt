- Proposes novel synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized U-statistic estimation.
- Clearly motivates the problem by highlighting limitations of existing gossip algorithms for pair-dependent quantities.
- GoSta-sync converges to the true U-statistic at rate O(1/t), with data-dependent and network spectral-gap terms in the bound.
- GoSta-async achieves a slightly worse convergence rate of O(log t / t).
- Analysis uses a "phantom nodes" reformulation to separate data propagation and averaging, and derives bounds via a transition matrix.
- Numerical experiments on two real-world datasets show GoSta methods outperform the U2-gossip baseline and reduce estimate variance across nodes.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated algorithms addressing a gap in gossip methods for U-statistics.
- Strength: comprehensive and rigorous theoretical analysis clarifying dependencies on data and network properties.
- Strength: thorough, well-executed numerical experiments demonstrating practical interest.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with existing algorithms and discussion of trade-offs (convergence vs. memory/communication).
- Weakness: extension to nodes with multiple observations is mentioned but not fully explored.
- Weakness/request: per-iteration run-times for GoSta and U2-gossip are missing and needed to assess practical utility.
- Overall assessment: well-written, technically sound, and significant (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithms, rigorous analysis, and strong experimental validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparative analysis and missing runtime/per-iteration cost information.
