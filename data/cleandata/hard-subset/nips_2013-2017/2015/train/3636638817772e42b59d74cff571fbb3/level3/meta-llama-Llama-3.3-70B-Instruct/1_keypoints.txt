- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta) for decentralized computation of U-statistics (pair-dependent quantities like AUC and within-cluster scatter).  
- Provides theoretical guarantees with convergence rates O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous settings.  
- Paper is well-written with clear problem setup, related work, and approach.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis supporting the algorithms.  
- Extensive experiments demonstrate superiority over existing methods (e.g., U2-gossip).  
- Detailed comparison with U2-gossip highlighting advantages in convergence rate, memory, and communication costs.  
- Strength: tackles a less-investigated problem (decentralized U-statistics) with novel algorithms.  
- Strength: rigorous convergence analysis and bounds.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation showing practical interest.  
- Weakness: assumes graph is connected and non-bipartite, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: synchronous algorithm requires a global clock, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: lacks broader discussion of potential applications beyond the two examples.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — novel, efficient algorithms with solid theory and experiments despite some practical limitations.
