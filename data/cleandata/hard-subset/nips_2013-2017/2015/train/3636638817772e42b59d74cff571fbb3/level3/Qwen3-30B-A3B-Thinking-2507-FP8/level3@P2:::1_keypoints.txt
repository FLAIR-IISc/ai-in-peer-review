- Addresses distributed estimation of U-statistics, a gap in decentralized computation relevant to AUC, variance, Gini, etc.
- Notes existing gossip work focuses on separable statistics; U-statistics require pairwise averaging and are untreated.
- Builds on U2-gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2010) and identifies its memory, communication, and asynchronicity limitations.
- Proposes two novel algorithms: GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous) that propagate data and maintain local U-statistic estimates.
- Introduces "phantom nodes" reformulation to enable clean convergence analysis.
- Proves O(1/t) convergence for GoSta-sync with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.
- Proves O(log t / t) convergence for GoSta-async, with analysis revealing dependence on graph spectral gap.
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, technically sound, and shows improvements over U2-gossip.
- Demonstrates memory and communication efficiency improvements compared to prior work.
- Empirical evaluation across multiple network topologies (complete, 2D grid, Watts-Strogatz) supports theoretical claims.
- Applications tested: AUC estimation (SVMguide3) and within-cluster scatter (Wine Quality), showing lower error and variance.
- Experimental results show performance gap widens as network size increases, favoring GoSta algorithms.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible with effective figures.
- Original contribution: first effective extension of gossip algorithms to degree-two U-statistics and novel analytic technique (phantom nodes).
- Main limitations: restricted to degree-two U-statistics and weaker convergence rate in the asynchronous case.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant theoretical and practical contribution with comprehensive validation.
