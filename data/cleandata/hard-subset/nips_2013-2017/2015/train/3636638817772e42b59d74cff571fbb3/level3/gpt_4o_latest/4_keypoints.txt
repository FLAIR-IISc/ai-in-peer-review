- Addresses decentralized computation of U-statistics via novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms.
- Claims improved convergence rates: O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).
- Reduces memory and communication costs compared to prior work.
- Theoretical contributions include comprehensive convergence proofs and bounds.
- Introduces "phantom nodes" as a novel analytical tool to separate data propagation and averaging.
- Empirical validation on real datasets shows improved performance over U2-gossip.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis and sound proofs supporting the claims.
- Strength: Clear exposition and inclusion of both synchronous and asynchronous settings.
- Strength: Addresses a relevant problem with practical constraints (memory/communication).
- Weakness: Algorithmic novelty is limited; builds on existing randomized gossip methods.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is weak, lacking comparisons to multiple randomized or simpler baselines.
- Weakness: Practical aspects (scalability to very large networks, robustness to node failures) are not thoroughly explored.
- Argument for acceptance: Important problem, rigorous theory, and empirical gains over U2-gossip.
- Argument against acceptance: Limited novelty, insufficient experimental breadth, and missing practical insights.
- Recommendation: Borderline; strengthen experiments with broader baselines and address scalability/robustness to improve impact.
