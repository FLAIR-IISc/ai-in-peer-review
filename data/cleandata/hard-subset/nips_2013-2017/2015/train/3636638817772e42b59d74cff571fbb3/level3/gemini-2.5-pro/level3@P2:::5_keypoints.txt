- Introduces GoSta synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of degree-two U-statistics.  
- U-statistics (e.g., AUC, sample variance) cannot be handled by standard separable-function gossip; GoSta combines data-propagation (swapping auxiliary observations) and averaging steps.  
- Provides rigorous analysis: synchronous convergence O(1/t), asynchronous O(log t / t).  
- Derives explicit constants in bounds depending on data properties and network spectral gap, showing faster convergence on better-connected graphs.  
- Uses an original "phantom networks" reformulation central to the analysis.  
- Situates work relative to prior art (notably U2-gossip [17]) and claims improvements in rate, memory, communication, and provides a fully asynchronous algorithm.  
- Strength: significant, original contribution addressing an important decentralized learning problem.  
- Strength: technically strong with rigorous proofs and insightful analysis linking topology and data distribution to performance.  
- Strength: thorough experiments on real datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) showing superior accuracy and lower variance versus U2-gossip.  
- Strength: paper is very clear, well-organized, and accessible; algorithms and theory are well-presented.  
- Weakness: theoretical asynchronous rate is slower than synchronous, but experiments show comparable performance; needs discussion whether the log factor is an artifact of proof or negligible in practice.  
- Weakness: asynchronous algorithm assumes nodes know pk = 2dk/|E|, requiring global |E| information; should state and discuss this assumption or how to obtain |E|.  
- Weakness: brief treatment of extension to nodes holding multiple observations; would benefit from a short elaboration on modifications and challenges.  
- Overall: reviewer recommends acceptance—high-quality, impactful work with minor clarifications requested.
