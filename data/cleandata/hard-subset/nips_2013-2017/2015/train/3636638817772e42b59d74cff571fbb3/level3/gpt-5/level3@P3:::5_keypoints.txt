- Tackles decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics with two gossip algorithms: synchronous GoSta-sync and asynchronous GoSta-async.  
- Uses a “phantom node” construction to decouple data propagation from averaging for clean linear-operator analysis.  
- Proves convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) for GoSta-sync and O(log t / t) for GoSta-async.  
- Convergence rates depend on the network spectral gap and data-dispersion terms in H.  
- Empirically outperforms prior U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs with lower cross-node variance.  
- Builds on gossip/distributed averaging literature but targets non-separable pairwise functionals and improves memory/communication per iteration versus U2-gossip.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound with block-triangular transition matrix and phantom graph arguments.  
- Provides a refined comparison bound showing U2-gossip scales roughly as √n/t times spectral-gap factors.  
- Assumes undirected, connected, non-bipartite graphs and symmetric H with H(X,X)=0; convergence proven in expectation.  
- Asynchronous method requires nodes to know their degree and |E| to compute activation probabilities—should be stated explicitly.  
- Experiments use real datasets, three network families, and 50 runs; results consistently favor proposed methods.  
- Lacks reporting of communication complexity (messages/bytes) to ε-accuracy and wall-clock/runtime metrics, which would support lower-cost claims.  
- Limitations: only expectation guarantees (no high-probability or a.s. rates), coarse asynchronous constants depending on ||H||.  
- Limitations: no treatment of directed/time-varying graphs, delays, packet loss, or failures; comparisons limited to U2-gossip and supplement baselines.  
- Paper is well organized and clear; algorithms and phantom-network device are well explained.  
- Minor clarity issues: need to clarify relation between 1 − λ2(W2) and Laplacian eigenvalues, U-statistic normalization choice, and per-iteration communication/memory costs.  
- Originality: novel propagate-and-average design, phantom-node analysis, and unbiased-counter asynchronous variant constitute meaningful advances.  
- Significance: relevant to AUC, variance, Gini, Kendall tau, and decentralized systems; rates and empirical gains are likely useful to follow-on work.  
- Pros: important problem, clear algorithms for sync/async, solid theory with explicit rates, strong empirical validation, well written.  
- Cons: expectation-only guarantees, looser async bounds with H-dependent constants, limited experimental scope, requirement of degree/|E| knowledge, no directed/time-varying analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: add high-probability/variance bounds, report communication and memory complexity to reach ε, discuss directed/time-varying extensions and robustness, explore spectral-gap acceleration, clarify spectral-gap notation and normalization, and test additional tasks and larger networks.  
- Overall: strong paper that advances decentralized estimation of pairwise statistics; reviewer leans toward acceptance after minor clarifications.
