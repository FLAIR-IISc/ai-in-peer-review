- Proposes synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-two U-statistics.
- Addresses a gap: existing gossip methods handle separable statistics but not pairwise U-statistics.
- Formalizes the estimation problem as Ûn(H) = 1/n² ∑i,j H(Xi,Xj) for symmetric H.
- GoSta-sync attains O(1/t) convergence; GoSta-async attains O(log t / t) convergence.
- Introduces "phantom nodes" to separate data propagation from estimation for analysis and algorithm design.
- Provides theoretical guarantees with explicit data-dependent (dispersion) and network-dependent (spectral gap) terms.
- Empirical results show GoSta outperforms prior U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks across multiple graph topologies.
- Experiments demonstrate lower variance and consistent performance gains across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs.
- Paper is clearly written with well-structured presentation, precise math, and clear pseudocode.
- Original contribution: improves on U2-gossip in convergence rate, memory usage, and communication cost.
- Significance is high because U-statistics are fundamental in many ML/data-analysis tasks and distributed systems.
- Major weakness: missing per-iteration timing analysis to validate practical efficiency claims versus U2-gossip, especially for high-dimensional data.
- Minor weakness: experimental evaluation limited to two applications; adding at least one more U-statistic example would strengthen the case.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, with suggestion to include timing results in the camera-ready version.
