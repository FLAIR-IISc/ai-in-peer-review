- Introduces GoSta, synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.  
- Extends distributed averaging to pair-dependent statistics (e.g., AUC, within-cluster scatter).  
- Builds on Pelckmans et al. (2010) and claims improved convergence, memory, and communication efficiency.  
- Technically sound with rigorous analysis and proven convergence rates: O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Uses an elegant "phantom network" construction as a central proof technique.  
- Thorough experimental validation on real-world datasets and varied network topologies showing superior performance to prior work.  
- Exceptionally clear, well-organized writing linking theory and experiments effectively.  
- Novel algorithmic idea: combine data propagation (swapping auxiliary observations) with estimate averaging.  
- High significance: practical, theoretically grounded approach for important U-statistics problems in distributed ML.  
- No major weaknesses; paper is complete and polished.  
- Minor suggestion: explore adaptive communication schemes to speed convergence on poorly connected networks.  
- Strong recommendation: advances the state of the art and of high interest to the community.
