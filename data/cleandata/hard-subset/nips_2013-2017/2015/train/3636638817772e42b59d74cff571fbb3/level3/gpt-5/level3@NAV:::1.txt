This paper proposes GoSta, a pair of synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics. The key idea is to disentangle data propagation (via swapping a single auxiliary observation per node along randomly selected edges) from estimate averaging, so that each node maintains a running local estimate of the global U-statistic. The authors prove convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous), with explicit dependence on problem difficulty and network connectivity (via the spectral gap). Relative to U2-Gossip, GoSta uses less memory and communication per iteration (one auxiliary observation and one edge activation vs. two), and achieves faster rates. Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs demonstrate consistent improvements. The paper is clearly written, well motivated, and positioned with respect to gossip averaging (e.g., Boyd et al.) and prior work on U-statistics (U2-Gossip).

Quality: The analysis is technically sound. The “phantom nodes” reformulation is insightful and yields transparent bounds with data- and graph-dependent terms. The comparison to U2-Gossip is careful and informative. Some limitations remain: results are in expectation (no high-probability bounds), the constants in the asynchronous log t/t bound are opaque, and the model assumes connected, non-bipartite graphs with uniform edge sampling and knowledge of degrees/|E| for unbiasedness. Practical costs of repeatedly swapping high-dimensional observations are discussed qualitatively but not profiled.

Clarity: The manuscript is well organized and readable. Algorithms are specified precisely. A succinct complexity table contrasting memory, per-iteration bandwidth, and total communication to reach a target error would further improve clarity.

Originality: The combination of averaging with single-observation propagation for U-statistics and the phantom-network analysis are novel relative to U2-Gossip and classic gossip averaging.

Significance: Decentralized U-statistic estimation is important (AUC, variance, Gini, scatter). The demonstrated improvements and asynchronous operation make the contribution practically relevant, though the empirical scope (two datasets, moderate n) could be expanded.

Pros:
- Novel, principled algorithms with clear theoretical guarantees.
- Faster convergence and lower resource usage than U2-Gossip.
- Works in asynchronous settings; analysis links to spectral gap.
- Strong empirical evidence; clear writing and positioning.

Cons:
- Only expectation guarantees; no tail bounds or almost-sure rates.
- Limited experimental breadth and scalability studies.
- Assumes uniform edge activation and knowledge of |E|, degrees.
- No robustness to message loss/latency or quantization.

Suggestions: Add high-probability bounds, robustness/quantization experiments, communication-to-accuracy tradeoffs, larger-scale tests and additional U-statistics (e.g., Kendall tau), and comparisons to approximate decentralized baselines (sketching/sampling).
