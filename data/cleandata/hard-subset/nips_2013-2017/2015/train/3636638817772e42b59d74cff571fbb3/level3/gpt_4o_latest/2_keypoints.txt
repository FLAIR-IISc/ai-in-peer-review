- Proposes a novel gossip-style decentralized algorithm for computing higher-order U-statistics over distributed data.  
- Introduces synchronous and asynchronous variants that swap auxiliary state variables to induce a random walk of observations.  
- Provides rigorous convergence guarantees: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous schemes.  
- Analyzes network-dependent factors (e.g., spectral gap) and compares performance to the U2-gossip algorithm.  
- Empirical results on real datasets show faster convergence and better memory efficiency than baselines.  
- Addresses an important and underexplored problem in decentralized computation of statistics like AUC and variance.  
- Missing connections to key prior work (e.g., Dimakis et al.) and insufficient literature review on lifting constructions for faster gossip.  
- The induced edge distribution from the asynchronous “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” process is not clearly specified.  
- Problem statement and theorem assumptions about data distribution and error guarantees need clearer articulation.  
- Lacks comparison with flooding-based methods that trade memory for communication, leaving trade-offs underexplored.  
- Minor grammatical and phrasing issues reduce readability and require revision.  
- Authors' response was constructive but did not address baseline/lifting comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept after major revisions to strengthen prior-work connections, clarify assumptions, and add comparisons.
