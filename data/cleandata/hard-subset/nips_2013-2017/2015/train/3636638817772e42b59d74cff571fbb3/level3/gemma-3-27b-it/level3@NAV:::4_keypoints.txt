- Addresses distributed estimation of U-statistics, an important problem with ML applications (variance, AUC, clustering).  
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for U-statistics.  
- Algorithms combine data propagation and local estimate averaging in an intuitive design.  
- Provides theoretical convergence bounds: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t), dependent on data and topology.  
- Uses "phantom nodes" as a clever analytical technique.  
- Empirical validation includes AUC and within-cluster point scatter experiments.  
- Major weakness: no comparison to any baseline methods, despite claiming superiority over prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation too narrow: needs broader network topologies, dataset sizes, and demonstration of connectivity effects.  
- Argument for acceptance: tackles a relevant problem with an intuitive algorithm and comprehensive theoretical analysis.  
- Argument against acceptance: lacks empirical baselines and has insufficient experimental breadth.  
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after adding baseline comparisons and stronger experiments.
