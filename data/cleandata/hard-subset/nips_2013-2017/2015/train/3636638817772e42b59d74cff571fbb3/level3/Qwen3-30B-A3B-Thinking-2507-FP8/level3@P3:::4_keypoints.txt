- Paper introduces GoSta algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics using a "phantom node" reformulation to enable analysis.  
- Targets U-statistics (pairwise averages like AUC, variance) that standard gossip cannot compute.  
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms with convergence rates O(1/t) and O(log t/t), explicitly depending on network and data properties.  
- Builds on and relates to prior gossip literature, improving on U2-gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2013).  
- Strength: Comprehensive and rigorous theoretical analysis; phantom node technique yields explicit spectral-gap and data-dependent bounds.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant and underexplored problem in distributed statistics with relevant application examples.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation and precise algorithm descriptions linking construction to execution.  
- Weakness: Experiments lack meaningful baseline comparisons beyond U2-gossip.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental scope—only two datasets and three network types; lacks study of harder distributions, topologies, and scale.  
- Weakness: Insufficient practical analysis of communication and memory scaling with network size and data dimensionality.  
- Pro: Theoretical contribution is strong and well-justified, filling an important gap.  
- Pro: Algorithmic approach is elegant and nontrivially extends gossip to non-separable statistics.  
- Con: Weak empirical validation undermines claims of practical superiority.  
- Con: Paper does not address performance under realistic conditions (noisy links, dynamic topologies).  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with major revisions—add stronger baselines, broader experiments, and detailed analysis of communication/memory costs.
