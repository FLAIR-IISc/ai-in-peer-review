This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which are essential in various applications such as decentralized estimation, sensor networks, and peer-to-peer networks. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem, discussing the limitations of existing gossip algorithms and the importance of U-statistics in machine learning and data mining.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have done an excellent job in motivating the problem and explaining the proposed algorithms. The technical content is sound, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence rates of the proposed algorithms. The experimental results on two real-world datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, outperforming the U2-gossip baseline across various graph scenarios.

The strengths of the paper include:

1. Novel algorithms: The authors propose new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. Convergence analysis: The authors provide a thorough convergence analysis of the proposed algorithms, establishing bounds on the convergence rates.
3. Experimental results: The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods on two real-world datasets, showing improved performance compared to the U2-gossip baseline.

However, there are some weaknesses and concerns:

1. Computational complexity: The authors do not provide per-iteration run-times for both the proposed method and U2-gossip. This information is crucial to assess the practical utility of the proposed method, as slower iterations may limit its applicability.
2. Comparison to other methods: While the authors compare their method to U2-gossip, it would be beneficial to compare it to other existing methods for computing U-statistics, such as centralized algorithms or other decentralized methods.
3. Robustness to network failures: The authors assume a connected and non-bipartite graph, but it would be interesting to investigate the robustness of the proposed algorithms to network failures or changes in the graph structure.

In conclusion, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of decentralized estimation and gossip algorithms. The proposed methods are novel, and the convergence analysis is thorough. However, the authors should provide more information on the computational complexity and compare their method to other existing approaches.
