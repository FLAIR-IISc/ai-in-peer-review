- Proposes two gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, to compute U-statistics in decentralized networks.  
- Extends gossip averaging techniques to handle pairwise-dependent U-statistics by expressing them as averages of local values.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including convergence proofs and rates for GoSta-sync.  
- Introduces GoSta-async (no global clock) with a convergence proof and similar empirical performance to the synchronous version.  
- Compares against prior U2-gossip, showing GoSta-sync has faster convergence and lower memory requirements.  
- Empirical evaluation on decentralized AUC estimation and within-cluster point scatter.  
- Experimental results indicate GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip with lower average relative error and node variance.  
- Paper is well-written and presents a clear, comprehensive analysis.  
- Strength: novel approach to decentralized U-statistics with solid theoretical and experimental support.  
- Strength: convincing comparison to U2-gossip demonstrating practical advantages.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to other baselines (e.g., centralized or other decentralized methods).  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to only two applications, reducing evidence of broad applicability.  
- Suggestions: add comparisons with more baseline methods, evaluate additional applications, and discuss limitations and future work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10.
