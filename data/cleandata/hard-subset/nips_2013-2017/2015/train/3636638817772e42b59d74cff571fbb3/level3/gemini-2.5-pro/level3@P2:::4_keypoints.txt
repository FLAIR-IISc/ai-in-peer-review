- Problem: Computing degree-2 U-statistics in decentralized networks where standard gossip for separable functions fails.
- Contribution: Proposes GoSta algorithms for synchronous and asynchronous gossip-based U-statistic estimation.
- Core mechanism: Each node keeps a local estimate and an auxiliary data item; paired nodes average estimates and swap auxiliary data to propagate pairwise information.
- Theoretical results: Proves convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).
- Analysis detail: Convergence bounds depend explicitly on network connectivity (spectral gap) and data-dependent terms via a "phantom network" technique.
- Comparison to prior work: Shows significant improvements over Pelckmans & Suykens (2011) in rate, memory, and communication.
- Experiments: Empirical validation on real datasets for AUC and within-cluster scatter, showing practical advantages over [17].
- Strength — significance: Tackles an important, under-explored problem with relevant ML applications (AUC, clustering).
- Strength — quality: Rigorous, comprehensive theoretical analysis with elegant techniques and both sync/async algorithms.
- Strength — clarity: Well-written, well-structured, intuitive algorithms and precise statements of results.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is narrow, comparing only to U2-gossip [17] and lacking simple baselines (e.g., random-walk aggregation, flooding).
- Originality: Combines standard gossip primitives (averaging + swapping) effectively; novelty mainly in adaptation and rigorous analysis rather than a new primitive.
- Arguments for acceptance: Important problem, simple/practical algorithms, strong theoretical contributions, clear improvement over the only direct competitor.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experimental scope makes it hard to assess performance relative to more naive distributed strategies.
- Recommendation: Accept with the revision request to strengthen experiments by adding one or two intuitive baselines (e.g., random-walk data collection or broadcast/flooding) and discuss trade-offs.
