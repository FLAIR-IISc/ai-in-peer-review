This paper proposes novel gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors introduce two algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, which achieve faster convergence rates than the prior state-of-the-art algorithm, U2-Gossip. The algorithms operate in realistic asynchronous settings and have superior memory and communication costs compared to U2-Gossip. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis, as well as extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets.

The key contribution of this paper is the development of gossip algorithms that can efficiently compute U-statistics, which are essential in many machine learning and data mining applications. The authors' approach is based on a novel reformulation of the problem using "phantom nodes," which allows them to model the data propagation and averaging steps separately. This leads to the development of two algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, which achieve faster convergence rates than U2-Gossip.
