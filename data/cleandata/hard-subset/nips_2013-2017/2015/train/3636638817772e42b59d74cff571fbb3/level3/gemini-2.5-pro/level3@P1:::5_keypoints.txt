- Paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms ("GoSta") for distributed estimation of degree-two U-statistics (e.g., variance, AUC).
- Core idea: combine propagation of auxiliary observations via swapping with local estimate averaging to achieve consensus.
- Introduces a "phantom network" construction to decouple and analyze data propagation and averaging steps.
- Theoretical guarantees: synchronous algorithm converges at O(1/t); asynchronous version converges at O(log t / t).
- Demonstrates improved convergence, lower memory and communication overhead compared to prior U2-gossip work.
- Rigorous and complete theoretical analysis that is well-supported by proofs.
- Empirical validation on real datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) across multiple network topologies (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz).
- Experiments effectively illustrate the influence of network connectivity predicted by theory.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and includes clear pseudocode (Algorithms 1–2) and helpful figures.
- Original contributions: novel algorithmic approach (swapping + averaging) and analytic technique (phantom network) distinct from prior work.
- Significance: practical and theoretically grounded methods for a broadly relevant class of statistics in decentralized ML and networks.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, simple/effective algorithms, strong theory and experiments, excellent clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: reviewer finds no significant weaknesses.
- Suggested improvements: briefly explain source of the log t factor in the asynchronous analysis and provide more detail on weighting when nodes hold multiple observations.
