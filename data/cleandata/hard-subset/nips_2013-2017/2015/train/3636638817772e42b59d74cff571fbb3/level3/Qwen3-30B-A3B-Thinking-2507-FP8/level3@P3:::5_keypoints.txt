- Introduces GoSta-sync and GoSta-async algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics.  
- Proposes a novel "phantom nodes" reformulation that enables clean convergence analysis.  
- Proves convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with explicit dependence on spectral gap and data.  
- Shows theoretical and practical improvements over prior U2-gossip, including lower communication and memory costs.  
- Provides a refined analysis of U2-gossip explaining its inferior performance.  
- Empirically validates on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks, with GoSta outperforming U2-gossip in speed, accuracy, and node variance.  
- Experiments span complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz topologies; performance gap increases with network size.  
- Technical quality: rigorous convergence proofs and sound theoretical development.  
- Clarity: well-organized, clearly written, and precise algorithm descriptions; phantom node construction is well explained.  
- Originality: first extension of gossip methods to U-statistics; phantom node technique is novel.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem for distributed ML with immediate practical applicability.  
- Strengths: novel algorithms, strong theory, comprehensive experiments, clear presentation, practical benefits.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of higher-order U-statistics, sparse async implementation details, and comparisons to additional baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—meets NIPS quality standards.
