- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous settings.  
- Uses a "phantom nodes" reformulation to enable rigorous convergence analysis with explicit data and network-dependent terms.  
- Addresses an important and underexplored problem—extending gossip methods from means to U-statistics (e.g., variance, AUC, Gini).  
- Strong theoretical rigor and clear presentation of results and assumptions.  
- Well-structured paper with clear writing and systematic exposition of problem, methods, theory, and experiments.  
- Empirical validation on real datasets (AUC and within-cluster scatter) shows improvements over prior work [17].  
- Comparison to existing method U2-gossip is thorough, but evaluation lacks a broader set of baseline algorithms.  
- Big-O results omit discussion of constant factors, limiting insight into practical performance.  
- Adaptive communication schemes are mentioned but not explored experimentally.  
- Asynchronous algorithm is only briefly evaluated; more empirical study is needed to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Generalization to nodes holding multiple observations is stated but underdeveloped and not demonstrated experimentally.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address broader baselines, constant factors, deeper async validation, and expanded discussion/experiments on generalization.
