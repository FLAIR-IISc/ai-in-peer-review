- Proposes novel gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync, GoSta-async) for computing U-statistics in decentralized settings.  
- Extends classical gossip methods to efficiently handle pair-dependent quantities, a less investigated area.  
- Achieves faster convergence rates than prior state-of-the-art U2-Gossip.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and properly positioned relative to prior work.  
- Clear problem statement and well-described algorithms with pseudocode.  
- Notation is clear and consistent throughout.  
- Provides thorough convergence analysis with theoretical guarantees for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets demonstrating superiority over baselines.  
- GoSta-sync outperforms U2-Gossip in average relative error and variance across nodes.  
- Performance gap grows in favor of GoSta as graph size increases.  
- Advantages over U2-Gossip include lower memory and communication costs.  
- Methods can be extended to nodes with multiple observations (by splitting nodes) with similar convergence.  
- Comprehensive related work discussion and clear articulation of how limitations are addressed.  
- Technically sound; claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; balanced evaluation.  
- High originality: introduces new algorithms and approaches for decentralized U-statistics.  
- High significance: addresses an important problem and is likely to impact researchers and practitioners.  
- Weakness: would benefit from additional comparisons to other baseline methods.  
- Weakness: could provide more insight into practical implications and real-world applications.
