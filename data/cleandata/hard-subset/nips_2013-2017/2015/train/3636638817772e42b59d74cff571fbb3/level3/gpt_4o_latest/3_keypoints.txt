- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics.  
- Targets an underexplored problem where standard gossip is inefficient because U-statistics depend on observation pairs.  
- Provides rigorous convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms.  
- Introduces a novel "phantom nodes" reformulation as an analytical tool for proving convergence.  
- Empirical evaluation on two real-world datasets shows significant improvements over U2-gossip in convergence speed and communication efficiency across network topologies.  
- Demonstrates advantages in convergence rate, memory usage, and communication costs compared to U2-gossip.  
- Offers insights into how convergence depends on network connectivity and data properties.  
- Includes both synchronous and asynchronous variants, increasing practical applicability.  
- Lacks runtime-per-iteration comparisons with U2-gossip, limiting assessment of practical efficiency in constrained environments.  
- Related-work discussion could be expanded to compare with a broader set of decentralized optimization techniques.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, solid theory, strong empirical results, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing per-iteration runtime comparison and broader related-work comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address runtime comparison concerns.
