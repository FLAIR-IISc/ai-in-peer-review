- Introduces two novel gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for decentralized U-statistic estimation with proven O(1/t) and O(log t/t) convergence rates.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presents techniques, making it easy to follow.  
- Provides a comprehensive review of related gossip-work, highlighting limitations of prior approaches and advantages of the proposed methods.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with mathematical proofs supporting convergence claims.  
- Introduces "phantom nodes" to separately model data propagation and averaging, enabling tight convergence bounds.  
- Experimental results show the proposed algorithms outperform existing approaches in accuracy and convergence speed.  
- Empirically analyzes network topology effects, offering insights into connectivity vs. convergence trade-offs.  
- Strength: Novel algorithms with improved convergence guarantees over existing methods.  
- Strength: Thorough, rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: Comprehensive related-work discussion.  
- Strength: Strong experimental validation.  
- Weakness: Focused on U-statistics, possibly limiting applicability to other decentralized estimation problems.  
- Weakness: Theoretical proofs may be hard to follow for readers lacking strong probability/linear algebra background.  
- Originality: Novel algorithms and the innovative "phantom nodes" modeling approach.  
- Significance: Makes a meaningful contribution to decentralized estimation with potential applicability across sensor networks, P2P systems, and distributed ML.  
- Recommendation: Accept for publication at NIPS.
