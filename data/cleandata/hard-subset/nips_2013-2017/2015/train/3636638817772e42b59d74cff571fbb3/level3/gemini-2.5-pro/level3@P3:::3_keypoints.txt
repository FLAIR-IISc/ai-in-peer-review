- Addresses decentralized computation of degree-2 U-statistics, a problem standard gossip protocols cannot handle.  
- Proposes two randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, combining data propagation (swapping auxiliary observations) with estimate averaging.  
- Builds on and aims to improve U2-gossip (Pelckmans et al., 2011), which uses only data propagation.  
- Main contribution: novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for U-statistic estimation.  
- Theoretical contribution: convergence rates O(1/t) for GoSta-sync and O(log t/t) for GoSta-async, with bounds depending on data and network spectral gap.  
- Empirical contribution: experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across multiple network topologies showing superior per-iteration convergence versus U2-gossip.  
- Significance/motivation: tackles an important, under-explored problem with clear practical examples (sensor networks, AUC, clustering).  
- Quality/originality: technically strong, novel “phantom network” analysis, and a fully asynchronous variant removing global clock requirements.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, definitions, algorithms, and comparisons are clear.  
- Empirical evaluation: well-designed experiments on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs; GoSta-sync shows faster convergence and lower variance per iteration.  
- Main weakness: evaluation measures iterations not wall-clock time, leaving practical efficiency unclear.  
- Missing analysis: unclear per-iteration computational and communication cost comparison between GoSta and U2-gossip.  
- Recommendation: add wall-clock time plots (average relative error vs time) to confirm practical speedups.  
- Question: GoSta-async performs comparably in practice despite slower theoretical rate—discuss whether the O(log t/t) bound is loose or constants make the difference negligible.  
- Overall: high-quality, significant contribution, but strengthened by addressing per-iteration complexity and time-based evaluation.
