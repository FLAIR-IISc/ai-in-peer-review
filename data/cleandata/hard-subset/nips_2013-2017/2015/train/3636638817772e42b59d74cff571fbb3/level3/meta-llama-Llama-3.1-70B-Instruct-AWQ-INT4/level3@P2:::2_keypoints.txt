- Proposes GoSta, a gossip-style decentralized algorithm for pairwise U-statistics using "phantom nodes."  
- Novel reformulation separates data propagation and estimate updates via phantom nodes.  
- Provides convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous variants.  
- Technically sound with rigorous analysis and supportive experimental results.  
- Innovative and well-motivated contribution to decentralized computation.  
- Recommend stronger connections to existing gossip literature and related ideas.  
- Suggest highlighting the key algorithmic trick: swapping a secondary state so observations perform coupled random walks.  
- Clarify the induced edge-distribution under the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" model—“drawing a random edge” is ambiguous.  
- Compare to a flooding/memory-heavy baseline (nodes store received tagged observations) to illustrate tradeoffs and judge the 1/t rate.  
- Expand related work to include Dimakis et al. and other omitted gossip approaches.  
- Investigate lifting constructions (e.g., LADA, lifted Markov chains) as potentially accelerating mixing.  
- Theorems should state assumptions on initial data distribution (worst-case vs probabilistic) and make error guarantees explicit as functions of initial data.  
- Reviewer could not verify supplemental proofs due to time constraints.  
- Minor language, notation, and typo fixes needed, including clarification on bipartite directed-graphs.  
- Experiments should include baseline comparisons to demonstrate empirical benefits.  
- Overall positive assessment: well-written, valuable contribution; reviewer raised score to 8 and recommends acceptance.
