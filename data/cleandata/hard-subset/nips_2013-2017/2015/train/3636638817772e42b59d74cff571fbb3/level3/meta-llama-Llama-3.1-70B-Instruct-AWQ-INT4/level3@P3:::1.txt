This paper proposes novel gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population. The authors achieve faster convergence rates than prior state-of-the-art methods, specifically U2-Gossip, and demonstrate superior performance in terms of memory and communication costs. The paper provides theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis, as well as extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets, showcasing the superiority of the proposed approach over existing baselines.

The main idea of the paper is to extend gossip algorithms to distributed estimation of U-statistics. The authors propose two algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, which operate in synchronous and asynchronous settings, respectively. Both algorithms rely on two types of iterative information exchange in the network: propagation of local observations across the network and averaging of local estimates. The authors show that the local estimates generated by their approach converge in expectation to the value of the U-statistic at rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) for the synchronous and asynchronous versions, respectively.

The paper is well-written, well-motivated, and properly positioned relative to prior work. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of decentralized estimation of U-statistics and the limitations of existing gossip algorithms. They also provide a thorough review of related work in gossip algorithms and U-statistics.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed algorithms achieve faster convergence rates than prior state-of-the-art methods.
* The algorithms operate in realistic asynchronous settings, making them more practical for real-world applications.
* The paper provides theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis, which is essential for understanding the behavior of the algorithms.
* The empirical evaluation on two datasets demonstrates the superiority of the proposed approach over existing baselines.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes that the network graph is connected and non-bipartite, which may not always be the case in real-world applications.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the communication and memory costs of the algorithms, which is essential for understanding their practicality.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of decentralized estimation of U-statistics and provides a novel approach to computing these statistics in a distributed setting. The proposed algorithms are well-suited for real-world applications, and the theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis provide a solid foundation for understanding their behavior.
