- Paper proposes GoSta, a novel gossip-style decentralized algorithm for computing pairwise U-statistics across networked nodes.
- GoSta’s convergence is analyzed: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t) rates.
- Convergence bounds include data-dependent terms (estimation hardness) and network-dependent terms (spectral gap).
- Numerical experiments show GoSta outperforms existing methods, including U2-gossip, on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs.
- Strength: novel decentralized algorithm tailored to U-statistics.
- Strength: thorough theoretical convergence analysis with problem- and network-dependent terms.
- Strength: empirical validation demonstrating superiority over baselines.
- Improve connection to existing gossip literature and related ideas.
- Emphasize the key algorithmic trick (swapping a secondary state/initial observation) in the main text.
- Clarify the induced edge-distribution under the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” model and what “drawing a random edge” means.
- Add comparison to a flooding/memory-heavy alternative (nodes store tagged observations) to highlight tradeoffs and contextualize the 1/t rate.
- Expand related work to include other gossip approaches (e.g., Dimakis et al.) and similar prior work.
- Theorems/analysis should state assumptions on initial data distribution (worst-case vs probabilistic) and make error guarantees explicit as functions of initial data.
- Overall: valuable contribution; rating 8 conditional on addressing comments and recommendation to accept if revisions are made.
