- Proposes novel gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) for decentralized computation of U-statistics.
- Introduces "phantom nodes" reformulation to separate data propagation and averaging steps.
- Achieves faster convergence rates than prior state-of-the-art (U2-Gossip).
- Supports realistic asynchronous settings with GoSta-async.
- Exhibits superior memory and communication costs compared to U2-Gossip.
- Provides detailed convergence analysis and theoretical guarantees, including convergence to the true U-statistic and rate bounds.
- Empirical evaluation on two datasets demonstrates superiority over existing baselines.
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and properly positioned relative to prior work.
- Strong technical contribution with significant implications for decentralized ML and data mining.
- Assumes graph is connected and non-bipartite, which may not hold in all practical scenarios.
- Phantom node construction may limit suitability for very large-scale networks due to complexity.
- Empirical evaluation could be more comprehensive by including additional datasets and baselines.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing significant contribution and solid theoretical/empirical support, despite noted limitations.
