- Proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics.  
- Provides a comprehensive review of related gossip algorithms and highlights limitations of existing approaches for U-statistics.  
- Paper is well-organized and techniques are presented clearly and concisely.  
- Derives theoretical convergence rates: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t).  
- Convergence analysis is thorough with detailed proofs for both algorithms.  
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform U2-gossip in average relative error and variance across nodes.  
- Performance gap versus U2-gossip is especially significant in early iterations.  
- Evaluates algorithms on multiple network topologies (complete, 2D grid, Watts–Strogatz), demonstrating robustness.  
- Provides a thorough comparison with existing approaches and empirical validation of advantages.  
- Strength: clear exposition, logical structure, and useful techniques for researchers.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of the algorithms’ limitations and potential future research directions.  
- Weakness: computational complexity analysis is brief and needs more detailed practical implications.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, useful, comprehensive analysis (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel useful techniques, solid theoretical and experimental analysis, robustness across networks, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient exploration of limitations and future work, insufficiently detailed computational complexity analysis.
