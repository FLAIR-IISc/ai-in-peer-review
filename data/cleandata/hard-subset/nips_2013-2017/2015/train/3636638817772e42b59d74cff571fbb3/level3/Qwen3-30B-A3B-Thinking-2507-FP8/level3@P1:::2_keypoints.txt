- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of pairwise U-statistics.  
- Extends gossip methods to non-separable statistics (AUC, Gini mean difference, within-cluster point scatter).  
- Key innovation: nodes maintain auxiliary observations that perform coupled random walks while averaging local estimates.  
- Proven convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with data and network dependent terms.  
- Builds on Boyd et al. (2006) and makes a valuable contribution to distributed computation in networks.  
- Needs stronger connections to related work — cite Dimakis et al. (Proc. IEEE, 2010) and Geographic Gossip (Dimakis et al., 2008).  
- Analysis of “phantom nodes” resembles lifting in Markov chains; cite Diaconis et al. on lifting Markov chains.  
- Algorithmic insight is clever and should be emphasized earlier rather than buried in the algorithm description.  
- Communication model description is ambiguous — clarify what “drawing a random edge” means in asynchronous settings (uniform edge vs node-initiated neighbor).  
- Theoretical results are sound but should explicitly state data-distribution assumptions (worst-case vs probabilistic).  
- Convergence terms claimed as “explicit data and network dependent” are not sufficiently unpacked for practical interpretation.  
- Should include a flooding-based baseline (store tagged received observations) to better demonstrate memory/communication advantages.  
- Experiments show superiority over U2-gossip for AUC and within-cluster scatter; scaling figure (Fig. 3a) is compelling.  
- Experimental baselines are limited; additional comparisons would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Minor issues: typo in “bipartite,” inconsistent graph Laplacian notation, and inconsistent theorem referencing.  
- Overall: solid theoretical and empirical contribution; with revisions on related work, model clarity, and baselines, it is a strong NIPS candidate.
