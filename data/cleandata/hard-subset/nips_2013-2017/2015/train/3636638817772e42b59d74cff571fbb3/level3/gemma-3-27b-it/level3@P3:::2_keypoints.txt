- Problem relevance: Decentralized computation of pairwise U-statistics is important and under-explored with broad ML/data-mining applications.  
- Algorithmic innovation: Proposes gossip-style algorithms that swap a secondary state (initial observation), inducing coupled random walks to handle U-statistics.  
- Theoretical analysis: Provides convergence rate bounds for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter show improvements over prior approach [17].  
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-written and organized.  

- Limited connection to gossip literature: Needs stronger discussion of how the approach builds on or differs from existing gossip and consensus methods (e.g., Dimakis et al. 2006).  
- Ambiguous edge distribution: Unclear whether nodes uniformly pick existing neighbors or attempt random connections; induced edge distribution must be specified.  
- Comparative analysis missing: Lacks a thorough comparison to flooding-based (memory-heavy) alternatives to quantify trade-offs and convergence practicality.  
- Related work incomplete: Omits recent gossip advances for heterogeneous or resource-constrained networks.  
- Lifting constructions: Should explore connections to lifting techniques (e.g., LADA, lifted Markov chains) to potentially speed mixing/convergence.  
- Assumptions & error guarantees: Theorems should state assumptions on initial data (worst-case vs. probabilistic) and express error bounds as functions of initial data.  
- Supplemental proofs unclear: Supplemental material is hard to verify; proofs need clearer, more self-contained presentation.  
- Minor issues: Small language/notation clarifications required (e.g., bipartite directed-graph terminology).  

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, offers practical algorithms with theoretical and empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Weak literature positioning, edge-selection ambiguity, and missing comparisons limit assessment of trade-offs.  

- Overall assessment: Solid contribution recommending conditional acceptance pending expanded related work, clarified edge model, flooding comparison, and discussion of lifting constructions.
