- Proposes novel gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) for distributed computation of U-statistics.  
- Achieves faster convergence than prior state-of-the-art (U2-Gossip) with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Algorithms rely on propagation of local observations and averaging of local estimates across the network.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis for the proposed methods.  
- Empirical evaluation on two datasets shows superior performance over existing baselines.  
- Operates in realistic asynchronous settings, increasing practical applicability.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and well-positioned relative to prior work.  
- Strengths: faster convergence, realistic async operation, solid theoretical analysis, and strong empirical results.  
- Weakness: assumes the network graph is connected and non-bipartite, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of communication and memory costs.  
- Quality, clarity, originality, and significance each rated 9/10 by the reviewer.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, practical algorithms, and strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive graph assumptions and insufficient resource-cost analysis.
