This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors provide a thorough motivation for their work, highlighting the importance of U-statistics in various applications and the limitations of existing gossip algorithms. The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining their approach and results.

The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, are designed to compute U-statistics efficiently in a decentralized setting. The authors provide convergence bounds for both algorithms, showing that they converge to the true U-statistic at rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t), respectively. The authors also compare their algorithms to the U2-gossip baseline, demonstrating that their approach outperforms U2-gossip across various graph scenarios.

The experimental results reported in the paper are based on two datasets: the SMVguide3 binary classification dataset and the Wine Quality dataset. The authors evaluate the performance of their algorithms using the average relative AUC error and provide a detailed analysis of their results. The results show that the proposed algorithms outperform U2-gossip in terms of average error and variance.
