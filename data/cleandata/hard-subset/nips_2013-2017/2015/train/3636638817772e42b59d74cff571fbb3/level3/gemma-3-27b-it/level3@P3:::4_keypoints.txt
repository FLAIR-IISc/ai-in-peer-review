- Proposes novel randomized gossip algorithms for distributed U-statistic computation, a relevant but underexplored problem.  
- Core idea: propagate data and average local estimates; intuitive and practical for distributed analysis.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical convergence analysis with bounds for synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Uses a "phantom node" reformulation to motivate proofs, though this increases notation complexity.  
- Technically sound and rigorous; algorithms and analysis constitute a complete contribution rather than a position piece.  
- Extension to asynchronous setting adds novelty.  
- Experimental results show improvements over prior work [17], but evaluation is limited.  
- Major weakness: lacks comparison to basic or intuitive baselines (e.g., simple propagation, naive averaging ignoring U-statistic structure).  
- Absence of broader baselines makes it hard to judge practical significance and degree of novelty.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized; algorithms and notation are consistent and readable.  
- Suggestion: better explain the link between phantom nodes and the actual algorithm and add more intuitive explanations alongside proofs.  
- Reproducibility: enough detail to implement experiments, but doing so requires substantial effort.  
- Significance: problem is important and bounds relating performance to network/data are valuable, but impact is limited by missing comparisons.  
- Recommendation: weak reject—paper is promising but needs broader experimental comparisons to establish practical value.
