- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync/GoSta-async) for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a connected network.  
- Key algorithmic trick: swap a per-iteration auxiliary “secondary state” so observations perform coupled random walks, enabling local approximation of hi = (1/n)∑j H(Xi,Xj).  
- Analysis uses a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple averaging from data propagation and yields rates O(1/t) (sync) and O((log t)/t) (async) with constants depending on graph spectral gap and data dispersion.  
- Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter show improvements over U2-gossip with lower memory and communication costs.  
- Relation to prior work: extends classical randomized gossip for separable sums and is conceptually related to multi-token/random-walk rumor spreading.  
- Compared to U2-gossip, the methods use less memory/communication and empirically converge faster.  
- Strength: decentralizes non-separable U-statistics with constant memory per node and simple per-iteration updates.  
- Strength: elegant phantom-network analysis with explicit dependence on spectral gap and data dispersion.  
- Strength: provides both synchronous and asynchronous variants with rigorous expected convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: practical applications demonstrated with consistent empirical gains.
