- Paper proposes GoSta, a decentralized algorithm that computes degree-two U-statistics by having each node maintain a local estimate and an auxiliary observation that is swapped with neighbors.  
- GoSta runs gossip averaging on estimates and swaps auxiliary observations to induce coupled random walks so nodes see representative samples for pairwise comparisons.  
- The authors present synchronous and asynchronous versions with proven convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous), with rates depending on graph spectral gap and data-dependent variance terms.  
- Novel proof technique: use of a "phantom network" to decouple averaging and data propagation dynamics.  
- Empirical results on real datasets (AUC and within-cluster scatter) show clear gains over the main competitor U2-gossip.  
- Strength: Significant and original contribution addressing a challenging decentralized non-separable statistic problem with a simple, memory-efficient mechanism.  
- Strength: Solid theoretical analysis linking convergence to network topology and data properties.  
- Strength: Generally clear writing and an easy-to-understand main algorithm.  
- Weakness: Related work section is brief and misses key references and broader positioning within gossip/decentralized literature.  
- Weakness: Experiments compare only to U2-gossip; more baselines (including naive/memory-heavy ones) are needed to contextualize tradeoffs and rates.  
- Weakness: Some communication-model and theoretical assumptions are ambiguous and should be clarified for reproducibility.  
- Request: Emphasize the core swapping/random-walk mechanism more prominently as a generalizable idea.  
- Request: Cite Dimakis et al. (2014) and discuss connections to lifting techniques (e.g., Lifted MCMC, LADA) and whether they could accelerate propagation.  
- Request: Clarify whether edge sampling is edge-centric or node-centric and ensure analysis matches the stated model.  
- Request: Add a flooding/memory-intensive baseline to benchmark speed-memory tradeoffs and compare termination time to GoSta.  
- Request: Make explicit assumptions on the data (X1..Xn) and detail how bounds depend on data properties (norms of H and h).  
- Minor: Reviewer didn’t verify supplementary proofs; fix small notational/definition issues (e.g., bipartite graph wording) and perform proofreading.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept; initial score 7, revised score 8 after discussion.
