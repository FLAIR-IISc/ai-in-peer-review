This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. U-statistics are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population, and their efficient computation is crucial in various applications. The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work on gossip algorithms and clearly explain the limitations of existing approaches in computing U-statistics.

The paper is well-organized, and the techniques are presented in a clear and concise manner. The authors derive theoretical convergence rates for both synchronous and asynchronous algorithms, with the synchronous algorithm achieving a rate of O(1/t) and the asynchronous algorithm achieving a rate of O(log t / t). The convergence analysis is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed proof of the convergence rates.

The experimental results demonstrate the performance and convergence behavior of the proposed algorithms. The authors compare their approach with the U2-gossip algorithm, which is the only other gossip algorithm designed for computing U-statistics. The results show that the proposed algorithms outperform U2-gossip in terms of average relative error and variance across nodes. The performance gap between the two algorithms is significant, especially in the early iterations.

The study addresses an area of interest, and the techniques proposed are useful to researchers in the field. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the algorithms' performance on different network topologies, including complete graphs, two-dimensional grids, and Watts-Strogatz networks. The results show that the proposed algorithms are robust and can handle various network structures.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and organization. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, followed by a comprehensive review of related work. The techniques are presented in a logical and easy-to-follow manner, making it easy for readers to understand the proposed algorithms.

The paper also provides a detailed analysis of the algorithms' performance, including theoretical convergence rates and experimental results. The authors provide a thorough comparison with existing approaches, demonstrating the advantages of their proposed algorithms.
