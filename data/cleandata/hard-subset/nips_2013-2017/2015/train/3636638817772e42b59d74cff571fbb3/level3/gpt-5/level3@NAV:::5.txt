This paper introduces synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) randomized gossip algorithms to estimate degree-2 U-statistics in decentralized networks. The key idea is to interleave two operations each iteration: (i) propagating auxiliary observations via random edge swaps so each node can approximate its local U-term hi, and (ii) averaging node estimates to drive consensus toward Ûn(H). The authors prove convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous), with explicit dependencies on data dispersion and the spectral gap of the underlying graph for the synchronous case. Compared to prior work on gossip averaging and aggregation (e.g., Boyd et al., 2006; Dimakis et al., 2010; Kempe et al., 2003) and the only prior decentralized method for U-statistics (U2-gossip by Pelckmans & Suykens), the proposed algorithms reduce communication/memory (one auxiliary observation per node) while improving rates. Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across several graph families corroborate the theory and show clear advantages over U2-gossip.

Quality: The technical development is sound; the “phantom nodes” reformulation cleanly separates data propagation from averaging and yields interpretable constants. The synchronous rate exhibits a sharp 1/t decay with spectral-gap dependence as expected. The asynchronous analysis is correct but yields a looser O(log t/t) with a graph-dependent constant that is not made explicit. Experiments are well executed but focus on two tasks and do not report message, memory, or wall-clock complexity to reach a target error.

Clarity: The paper is well organized and clearly written. Algorithms are precise, and the analysis is easy to follow. Minor suggestion: make c′(G) explicit or bounded in terms of λ2 and degrees; clarify the cost of H evaluations when d is large.

Originality: Extending gossip to pairwise U-statistics with simultaneous propagation/averaging is novel. The reformulation and rate improvements over U2-gossip are substantive.

Significance: U-statistics underpin many ML metrics (AUC, variance, rank corr.). The methods are likely to be useful to researchers working on decentralized learning and monitoring.

Pros:
- Clear, elegant algorithms with improved rates and lower resource usage than U2-gossip.
- Explicit spectral-gap dependence; strong empirical validation across graphs.
- Useful, general framework for many U-statistics.

Cons:
- Only expectation bounds; no high-probability or variance/concentration guarantees.
- Asynchronous bound hides spectral terms; practical impact unclear.
- Limited experimental breadth; no communication/wall-clock vs. accuracy plots.
- Requires knowing degrees/|E|; robustness to clock skew or packet loss not studied.

Overall: Strong paper; I recommend acceptance after addressing the empirical reporting and clarifying the asynchronous constants.
