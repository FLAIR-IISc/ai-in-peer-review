- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) for decentralized computation of U-statistics.  
- Addresses a key gap: prior gossip methods cannot efficiently handle pairwise interactions required by U-statistics (e.g., AUC, variance).  
- GoSta maintains local estimates while propagating data across the network.  
- Proven convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous settings.  
- Improves on U2-Gossip’s O(1/t) rate while using lower memory and communication costs.  
- Strong theoretical analysis employing a “phantom node” reformulation to separate data propagation and averaging.  
- Thorough empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster point scatter across three network topologies.  
- Excellent clarity and organization, with proper positioning and clear comparisons to prior work.  
- Original contribution addressing an underexplored problem distinct from previous gossip approaches.  
- High significance for ML applications and practical use in resource-constrained distributed networks.  
- Minor limitation: phantom network analysis may need careful explanation for readers unfamiliar with graph-theoretic reformulations.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance to NIPS, citing strong theory, implementation, and empirical evaluation.
