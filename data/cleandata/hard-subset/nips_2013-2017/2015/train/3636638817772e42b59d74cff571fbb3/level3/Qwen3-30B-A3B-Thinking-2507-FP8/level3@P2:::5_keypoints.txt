- Addresses an important gap by extending gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics that depend on pairs of observations.  
- Proposes two novel randomized algorithms, GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous), that propagate data and maintain local U-statistic estimates.  
- Introduces a novel "phantom nodes" analysis technique to separate data propagation and averaging for convergence proofs.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.  
- Convergence bounds explicitly incorporate network connectivity via the spectral gap of the graph Laplacian.  
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance to U2-gossip across multiple topologies and real-world datasets (AUC and within-cluster scatter).  
- Experimental results show faster convergence and lower variance across nodes, validating practical competitiveness.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with precise problem formulation, clear pseudocode, and reproducible supplementary material.  
- Original contribution: meaningful extension of gossip algorithms to a wider class of statistics with a novel analytical approach.  
- Significant practical implications for sensor networks, peer-to-peer systems, and decentralized machine learning.  
- Authors transparently acknowledge limitations, notably increased memory requirements for auxiliary observations.  
- Minor concern: asynchronous rate O(log t / t) is slightly slower than some algorithms for simpler statistics, though experiments are competitive.  
- Overall assessment: high quality, original, significant work that meets criteria for acceptance; strong recommendation to accept.
