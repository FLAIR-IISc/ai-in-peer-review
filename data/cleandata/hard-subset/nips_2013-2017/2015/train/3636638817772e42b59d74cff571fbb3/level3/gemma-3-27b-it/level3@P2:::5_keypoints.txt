- Presents new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics, filling a gap beyond sample mean estimation.  
- Algorithms maintain local estimates while propagating data, achieving O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) convergence rates.  
- Extends prior gossip work to handle pairwise-averaging U-statistics, improving on limitations of earlier methods (e.g., Pelckmans & Suykens [17]).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with explicit bounds that incorporate data properties and network topology.  
- Introduces “phantom nodes” as a useful analytical tool, explained clearly.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and uses clear mathematical notation.  
- Numerical experiments (including SMVguide3 and Wine Quality) convincingly show faster convergence and lower variance versus U2-gossip.  
- Practical relevance highlighted for sensor networks, P2P systems, and distributed ML on large datasets.  
- Assumes undirected graph and uniform data distribution, which may limit applicability to more complex or heterogeneous settings.  
- Asynchronous algorithm has a weaker theoretical rate (O(log t / t)) and practical trade-offs between synchronization overhead and speed are not fully explored.  
- Requires known network degree for asynchronous pk calculations, which may not hold in dynamic networks.  
- Comparison baselines are limited; broader comparisons to other distributed estimation methods would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, novel, theoretically sound, and empirically validated work with strong recommendation for acceptance.
