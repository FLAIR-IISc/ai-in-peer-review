- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.  
- Addresses limitations of existing gossip methods for pair-dependent U-statistics via propagation of local observations and averaging.  
- Provides clear problem statement and detailed algorithm descriptions with pseudocode.  
- Thorough convergence analysis with synchronous rate O(1/t).  
- Asynchronous convergence bound established as O(log t / t).  
- Offers a refined convergence comparison to the existing U2-gossip algorithm.  
- Experiments show performance advantages and accurate estimation; GoSta-sync and GoSta-async perform comparably.  
- Paper is well-organized and techniques are clearly presented.  
- Strength: novel algorithms in a less investigated area (U-statistics gossiping).  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: assumes connected, non-bipartite graphs, limiting some real-world applicability.  
- Weakness: network model used for analysis may not capture full real-world network complexities.  
- Pro acceptance: novelty, thorough analysis, clarity, and demonstrated empirical performance.  
- Con acceptance: restrictive graph assumptions and simplified network model.  
- Quality: technically sound with validated convergence results.  
- Clarity: clear presentation and detailed pseudocode.  
- Originality: novel contribution to decentralized U-statistics estimation.  
- Significance: relevant and useful to researchers in decentralized estimation of U-statistics.
