- Introduces GoSta algorithms for decentralized U-statistics with synchronous (O(1/t)) and asynchronous (O(log t / t)) convergence rates.  
- Key innovation: nodes swap a secondary state (initial observation) so observations perform coupled random walks—this mechanism should be more prominently highlighted.  
- Fills an important gap by extending gossip algorithms to pairwise (U-statistic) estimation in networks.  
- Needs stronger connections to existing gossip literature—explicitly cite Dimakis et al. and related seminal work.  
- Relate the phantom network construction to lifting techniques (e.g., LADA, lifted Markov chains) which might speed mixing.  
- Clarify the induced edge-distribution: does “drawing a random edge” mean uniform sampling over edges or the wake-up/contact model’s induced distribution?  
- Compare to a flooding-based alternative (nodes store tagged observations) to illustrate communication–memory tradeoffs and to contextualize the 1/t rate.  
- Theorems omit explicit assumptions on initial data distribution (worst-case vs. probabilistic) and do not express error bounds as functions of initial data.  
- Minor language/notation errors (e.g., bipartite directed-graph point) need correction.  
- Strengths: novel phantom-network reformulation, rigorous convergence analysis, and compelling experiments showing GoSta outperforms U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter.  
- Experiments would be stronger with additional baseline comparisons to better demonstrate empirical advantages.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions (score 8).
