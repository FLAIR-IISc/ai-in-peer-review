This paper proposes novel gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors extend the classical gossip algorithms to efficiently compute quantities that depend on pairs of observations, which is a less investigated area. The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, achieve faster convergence rates than the prior state-of-the-art, U2-Gossip.

The paper is well-written and well-motivated, and the authors properly position their work relative to prior research. The problem statement is clear, and the proposed algorithms are well-described. The authors provide a thorough convergence analysis, including theoretical guarantees for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.

One of the strengths of the paper is its extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets, which demonstrates the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing baselines. The experimental results show that GoSta-sync outperforms U2-Gossip in terms of average relative error and variance across nodes, and the performance gap widens in favor of GoSta as the size of the graph increases.

The paper also highlights the advantages of the proposed algorithms over U2-Gossip, including lower memory and communication costs. The authors note that their methods can be extended to situations where nodes contain multiple observations, and similar convergence results are achieved by splitting each node into a set of nodes.

In terms of related work, the authors provide a comprehensive review of gossip algorithms and their applications in decentralized computation. They also discuss the limitations of existing algorithms and how their proposed methods address these limitations.

The paper is well-organized, and the notation is clear and consistent throughout. The authors provide a detailed description of the algorithms, including pseudocode, which makes it easy to follow and implement the methods.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.
