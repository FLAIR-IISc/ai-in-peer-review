- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms using a "phantom network" to estimate U-statistics in decentralized settings, addressing pairwise interaction requirements.
- Novelty: introduces coupled random walks and secondary state swapping, differing from prior U2-gossip approaches and reducing memory/communication costs.
- Significance: tackles an important practical problem (e.g., distributed AUC) with improved theoretical convergence rates (O(1/t) synchronous, O(log t/t) asynchronous).
- Clarity: algorithms and phantom-network concept are well-structured and experiments show empirical gains over U2-gossip.
- Related work gap: omits key gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. 2008/2010), weakening contextualization.
- Analysis depth: convergence theorems lack explicit assumptions about initial data distribution and error bounds tied to initial conditions.
- Algorithmic ambiguity: unclear edge-selection model—node-wakeup vs. uniform edge selection—which affects convergence properties.
- Experimental gap: missing a flooding-based baseline to fairly assess communication and memory efficiency.
- Suggestion: expand related work to include foundational gossip papers and recent decentralized U-statistics work.
- Suggestion: clearly specify the edge-selection probability model (e.g., each edge chosen with probability 1/|E| per iteration).
- Suggestion: add flooding baseline comparisons to demonstrate communication/memory advantages concretely.
- Suggestion: discuss potential improvements using lifting techniques (e.g., LADA/lifted Markov chains) relevant to phantom networks.
- Suggestion: explicitly state assumptions on initial data distribution and provide error bounds as functions of initial conditions.
- Overall: strong technical contribution with theoretical and empirical advantages; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address literature, clarity, and analysis gaps.
