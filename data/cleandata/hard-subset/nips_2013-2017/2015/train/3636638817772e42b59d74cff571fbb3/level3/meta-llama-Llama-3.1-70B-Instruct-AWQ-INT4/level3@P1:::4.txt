This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. U-statistics are a class of statistics that depend on pairs of observations, such as the sample variance, Area Under the Curve (AUC), and within-cluster point scatter. The authors' algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, are designed to estimate these statistics in a network of nodes, where each node has access to a single data observation.

The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed algorithms, including their convergence rates and theoretical guarantees. The authors show that their algorithms converge to the true U-statistic at a rate of O(1/t) for the synchronous case and O(log t/t) for the asynchronous case, where t is the number of iterations. They also provide a detailed comparison with a previous algorithm, U2-gossip, and demonstrate that their algorithms have faster convergence rates and lower memory and communication costs.

The paper also presents numerical experiments on two real-world datasets, the SMVguide3 binary classification dataset and the Wine Quality dataset, to evaluate the performance of the proposed algorithms. The results show that GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip in terms of average relative error and variance across nodes, and that the performance gap between the two algorithms increases as the size of the graph increases.

Strengths of the paper include:

* Comprehensive analysis of the proposed algorithms, including their convergence rates and theoretical guarantees.
* Detailed comparison with a previous algorithm, U2-gossip, and demonstration of the advantages of the proposed algorithms.
* Numerical experiments on real-world datasets to evaluate the performance of the proposed algorithms.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* Lack of comparison to baseline methods, which makes it difficult to evaluate the novelty and significance of the proposed algorithms.
* Limited experimental evaluation, which only includes two real-world datasets and does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms in different scenarios.

To strengthen the paper, the authors could consider including intuitive/randomized baselines in their experimental evaluation and providing a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms in different scenarios.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper addresses an interesting problem in the field of decentralized computation and provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed algorithms.
* The numerical experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms in practice.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks comparison to baseline methods, which makes it difficult to evaluate the novelty and significance of the proposed algorithms.
* The experimental evaluation is limited and does not provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms in different scenarios.
