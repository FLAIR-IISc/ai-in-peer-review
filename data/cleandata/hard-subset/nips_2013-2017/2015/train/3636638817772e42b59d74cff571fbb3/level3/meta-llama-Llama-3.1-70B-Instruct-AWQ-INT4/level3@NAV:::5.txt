This paper introduces two novel gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for estimating U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of these algorithms, demonstrating that they achieve O(1/t) and O(log t/t) convergence rates, respectively.

The paper is well-organized and clearly presents the techniques, making it easy to follow. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of related work in gossip algorithms, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and the advantages of their proposed methods.

The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-supported by mathematical proofs. The authors use an innovative approach, introducing "phantom nodes" to model the data propagation and averaging steps separately. This allows them to derive tight bounds on the convergence rates of the algorithms.

The experimental results demonstrate the practical interest of the proposed algorithms, showing that they outperform existing approaches in terms of accuracy and convergence speed. The authors also investigate the impact of network topology on the performance of the algorithms, providing valuable insights into the trade-offs between connectivity and convergence rate.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of decentralized estimation and gossip algorithms. The proposed methods have the potential to be widely applicable in various domains, including sensor networks, peer-to-peer systems, and distributed machine learning.
