- Studies decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics using gossip-style communication and proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async.  
- Each node keeps an auxiliary Yk and running average Zk; swapping Y’s makes observations perform coupled random walks to approximate hk = (1/n)∑j H(Xk,Xj).  
- Convergence in expectation: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O((log t)/t), with rates depending on spectral gap λ2(Wα(G)).  
- Analysis introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple estimation and propagation.  
- Empirical results (AUC, within-cluster scatter) on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs show improvements over U2-gossip in error and across-node variance, with lower memory/communication per iteration.  
- Connects to classical gossip/consensus and separable-function aggregation; directly improves on U2-gossip.  
- Relation to lifted chains and location-aided/accelerated consensus (geographic gossip, LADA) should be positioned more explicitly.  
- The phantom-network linear-time-varying analysis is elegant and yields explicit spectral-gap-dependent constants; high-level steps appear plausible.  
- Asynchronous scheme uses local counters mk to debias; pk = 2dk/|E| is consistent with uniform-over-edges activation.  
- Convergence guarantees are expectation-only over algorithm randomness with fixed data; this should be stated up front.  
- Asynchronous bound is looser; adding variance or high-probability bounds would substantially strengthen claims.  
- Missing baselines: include a flooding/accumulate (tagged observations or Bloom-filter sketches) and a centralized oracle; provide communication- or wall-clock-normalized cost-to-accuracy plots.  
- Edge-selection model is inconsistently described; make the induced edge distribution explicit and clarify relation between node-activation and uniform-over-edges views.  
- In async, justify pk = 2dk/|E| and state what nodes must know (dk, |E|); discuss estimating |E| online and robustness to mis-specification.  
- Emphasize the central trick (coupled random-walk data propagation via Y-swaps) in the introduction and discussion.  
- Expand related work to include accelerated consensus (Chebyshev/polynomial), push-sum for directed graphs, geographic gossip/location-aided averaging, and lifted Markov chains.  
- Discuss potential for acceleration (polynomial/Chebyshev, non-backtracking/lifted walks) and whether averaging (Polyak–Ruppert) could reduce constants.  
- Clarify whether bounds are worst-case or depend on data norms; provide c′(G) scaling details for async and consider concentration bounds.  
- Practicality concern: mk counters require |E| knowledge—discuss distributed estimation of |E| or alternative step-size schemes.  
- Pros: addresses an important underexplored problem, simple algorithms with provable convergence, lower memory/communication, and insightful phantom-node analysis.  
- Cons: limited baselines, expectation-only guarantees, ambiguity in edge-selection modeling and required global knowledge, and incomplete integration of lifted/accelerated consensus literature.  
- Numerous minor editorial/notation fixes needed (consistent hyphenation, notation for Wα(G)/λ2, expectations over edge randomness, H(X,X)=0 assumption, typos).  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (8).
