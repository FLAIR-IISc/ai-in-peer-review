- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics with provable convergence guarantees.  
- Core idea: propagate local observations and iteratively average local estimates using "phantom nodes" to simplify analysis.  
- Established convergence rates: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t), with bounds depending on data and network spectral gap.  
- Demonstrated superior empirical performance over U2-Gossip on SMVguide3 and Wine Quality across multiple network topologies.  
- Novelty: fills an under-explored gap by extending gossip algorithms to important U-statistics (AUC, variance, Gini mean difference).  
- Technical soundness: well-defined algorithms and rigorous convergence analysis for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments on real datasets and varied topologies (complete, 2D grid, Watts-Strogatz).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and includes clear algorithms and experimental setup for reproducibility.  
- Related work: thorough comparison to prior gossip literature and clear distinction from U2-Gossip in convergence, memory, and communication.  
- Weakness: asynchronous convergence rate O(log t / t) is weaker than synchronous O(1/t) and could be improved or further analyzed.  
- Weakness: assumes static network topology, limiting applicability to dynamic real-world networks.  
- Weakness: limited baseline comparisons beyond U2-Gossip, reducing broader context of performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, technically solid improvements, insightful theoretical analysis, and validated empirically.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weaker async rate, simplified network model, and limited comparisons to other distributed methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to discuss dynamic topologies and expand baseline comparisons.
