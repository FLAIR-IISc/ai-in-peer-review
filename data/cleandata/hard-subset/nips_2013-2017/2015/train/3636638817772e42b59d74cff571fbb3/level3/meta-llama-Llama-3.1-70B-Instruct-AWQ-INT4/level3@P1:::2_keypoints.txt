- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics in decentralized networks.  
- Motivates need: existing gossip methods fail for U-statistics because they depend on observation pairs.  
- Identifies limitations of U2-gossip: O(1/t) rate but high memory and communication costs.  
- Algorithms combine data propagation and averaging; nodes swap a secondary state so observations perform coupled random walks.  
- Introduces "phantom nodes" reformulation to separate and analyze propagation and averaging steps.  
- Proves convergence: GoSta-sync achieves O(1/t), GoSta-async achieves O(log t / t).  
- Numerical experiments (AUC and within-cluster scatter) show GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip in error and variance.  
- Early performance gap attributed to significant exponential term in GoSta-sync's bound during initial steps.  
- Strength: novel algorithms addressing an important decentralized U-statistics problem.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating practical benefits.  
- Weakness: needs stronger connections to broader gossip literature and related ideas.  
- Weakness: unclear induced edge-distribution from the node-wakes-and-contacts-neighbor model.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to memory-heavy flooding/tagging alternatives to clarify tradeoffs.  
- Weakness: related work section omits other relevant gossip approaches.  
- Weakness: theorems should state assumptions on initial data distribution and make error guarantees explicit.  
- Originality: new algorithms and the "phantom nodes" modeling approach are original contributions.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential applications in sensor, peer-to-peer, and social networks.  
- Quality: paper is well-written with clear notation and organization.  
- Clarity: explanations and notation are clear, with a notation list provided.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel methods, solid analysis, and convincing experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: methodological and presentation gaps (literature links, model clarification, additional comparisons, assumption clarity).  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the raised comments and suggestions.
