- Addresses gap in decentralized computation by proposing gossip algorithms for estimating U-statistics (pairwise-dependent quantities).  
- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms tailored to U-statistics.  
- Introduces a "phantom node" reformulation that separates data propagation from estimation updates for analysis.  
- Derives convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms.  
- Convergence bounds explicitly depend on data properties and network topology (spectral gap).  
- Improves on prior work (U2-gossip) in memory, communication costs, and support for asynchrony.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with meaningful, network-aware convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluation on AUC and within-cluster point scatter shows superior accuracy and lower variance than U2-gossip.  
- Experiments conducted on real datasets across three network topologies (complete, 2D grid, Watts–Strogatz) with results widening for larger networks.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes sufficient detail and supplementary material for reproducibility.  
- Authors acknowledge trade-offs between convergence rate and per-iteration computational cost compared to U2-gossip.  
- Major concern: no per-iteration run-time comparisons provided, leaving practical runtime advantage unclear.  
- Recommendation: strong, significant contribution contingent on adding per-iteration runtime experiments to assess practical utility.
