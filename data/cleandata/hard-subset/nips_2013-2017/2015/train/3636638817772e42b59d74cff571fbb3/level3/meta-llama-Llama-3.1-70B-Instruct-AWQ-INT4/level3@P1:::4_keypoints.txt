- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async randomized gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics in decentralized networks where each node holds one observation.  
- Targets U-statistics (pairwise-dependent stats) like sample variance, AUC, and within-cluster scatter.  
- Provides theoretical convergence: O(1/t) for synchronous, O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with formal guarantees.  
- Shows GoSta variants have faster convergence and lower memory/communication costs than prior U2-gossip.  
- Empirical tests on SMVguide3 and Wine Quality datasets: GoSta-sync yields lower average relative error and variance across nodes.  
- Observes performance gap between GoSta and U2-gossip increases with graph size.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical analysis and convergence proofs.  
- Strength: detailed direct comparison to prior work (U2-gossip) highlighting practical advantages.  
- Strength: real-world dataset experiments supporting claims.  
- Weakness: no comparison to simple/intuitive/randomized baseline methods, hindering assessment of novelty.  
- Weakness: limited experimental evaluation (only two datasets, few scenarios).  
- Recommendation: include randomized/intuitive baselines and broader experiments; overall rating 7/10 and accept with minor revisions.
