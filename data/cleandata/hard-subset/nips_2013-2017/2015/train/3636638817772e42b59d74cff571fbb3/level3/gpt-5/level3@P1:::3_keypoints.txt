The paper addresses decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics and proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async combining random-walk data propagation with local averaging.

Introduces a "phantom nodes" analysis that decouples data propagation from averaging for theoretical tractability.

Proves expectation convergence with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous), with explicit dependence on data dispersion and network spectral gap.

Empirically outperforms U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs.

Algorithmic advantage: uses one auxiliary observation per node (vs two in U2-gossip), fewer exchanges, supports asynchrony without a global clock, and shows improved rates/constants.

Refined comparison to U2-gossip shows a √n penalty for U2 due to lack of averaging.

Strength: clear, well-motivated problem with broad practical U-statistics applications (AUC, variance, Gini, Kendall tau, within-cluster scatter).

Strength: simple synchronous and asynchronous protocols with elegant phantom-network proof technique.

Strength: theoretical guarantees explicitly depend on network spectral gap, matching consensus intuition.

Strength: asynchronous variant uses local Poisson clocks and per-node counters, improving practicality.

Strength: empirical validation shows consistent gains and lower across-node variance; scaling suggests advantage increases with n.

Weakness: per-iteration runtime and communication cost not reported, leaving wall-clock advantages unclear.

Request: report CPU time per iteration/edge-activation, messages/bytes per iteration, and wall-clock to reach target error across graphs and dimensions d.

Weakness: non-bipartite graph assumption — need discussion of adaptation (e.g., lazy updates) and whether guarantees transfer.

Weakness: Algorithm 2 requires pk = 2dk/|E| — unclear if |E| is assumed known or can be estimated without biasing mk.

Weakness: assumptions on H (boundedness or moment conditions) are not explicitly stated; clarify to ensure finite constants.

Weakness: asynchronous bound uses unspecified c′(G); request expression in spectral terms (e.g., Laplacian gap).

Weakness: experiments limited to two datasets and degree-2 U-statistics; request broader tasks (e.g., Kendall tau) and larger n,d.

Request: include wall-clock plots and communication-normalized comparisons, and quantify memory in bytes/node vs U2-gossip.

Weakness: results are in expectation only; request high-probability or mean-square bounds for practitioner confidence.

Quality: technically sound with insightful phantom-network technique, but some constants/assumptions need clarification.

Clarity: well written and organized; suggest summarizing assumptions on H/graph and detailing computational/communication costs per iteration.

Originality: extends gossip to non-separable U-statistics with new analysis and asynchronous design, improving over prior work.

Significance: moderate–strong given broad relevance of U-statistics and practical asynchronous algorithm, but empirical section needs strengthening.

Requested additions before acceptance: runtime/communication metrics, explicit c′(G) bound, bipartite adaptation discussion, H assumptions and high-probability bounds, and memory cost quantification.

Pros for acceptance: important problem, simple algorithms with solid spectral-theory backing, asynchronous practical design, consistent empirical improvements, and reusable phantom-nodes analysis.

Cons against acceptance: missing wall-clock/communication-normalized results, unspecified asynchronous constant and H assumptions, limited experimental breadth, and potential reliance on global |E|.

Overall recommendation: leaning toward accept contingent on adding requested practical comparisons and clarifying assumptions/constants.
