## Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

This paper introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms designed for estimating U-statistics in a distributed network setting. U-statistics, requiring pairwise averaging, present a challenge for traditional gossip algorithms focused on simpler averaging tasks. The authors successfully address this by proposing algorithms that combine data propagation and local estimate averaging. A key strength is the derivation of theoretical convergence rates: O(1/t) for the synchronous case and O(log t / t) for the asynchronous version, with explicit dependence on data and network characteristics.

The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly presents the techniques. The introduction effectively motivates the problem, highlighting the relevance of U-statistics and the limitations of existing approaches. The use of “phantom nodes” to facilitate the convergence analysis is a clever and insightful technique. The presentation of the algorithms (Algorithms 1 & 2) is concise and easy to follow. The experimental results, demonstrating performance on AUC and within-cluster point scatter estimation, convincingly support the theoretical findings and show improvements over the existing U2-gossip algorithm [17]. 

The originality of this work lies in extending the well-established gossip framework to handle the more complex computation of U-statistics. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from prior work, particularly [17], and demonstrate improvements in both convergence rate and computational cost. The significance of this contribution is substantial, as it provides researchers with valuable tools for decentralized estimation in various applications, including sensor networks, peer-to-peer systems, and large-scale data analysis. The analysis linking convergence rates to network properties (spectral gap) is particularly insightful.

However, a minor weakness is the somewhat limited scope of experimental comparison. While the comparison to U2-gossip is valuable, including comparisons to more standard distributed estimation techniques (even if not directly applicable to U-statistics) could further strengthen the evaluation. Additionally, the discussion of the asynchronous algorithm’s performance (GoSta-async) could be expanded, as the practical results appear comparable to the synchronous version despite the slightly weaker theoretical guarantee.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important and under-explored problem in distributed estimation.
*   Provides novel and theoretically sound algorithms with proven convergence rates.
*   Demonstrates practical advantages through compelling experimental results.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited comparison to alternative distributed estimation methods.
*   Asynchronous algorithm’s practical performance warrants further discussion.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed estimation. The technical soundness, clarity, originality, and significance all meet the standards for acceptance at NIPS. I recommend acceptance.
