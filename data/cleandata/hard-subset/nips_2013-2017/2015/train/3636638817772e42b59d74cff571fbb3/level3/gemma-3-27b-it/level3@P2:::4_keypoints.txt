- Introduces randomized synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) gossip algorithms for distributed U-statistic estimation with empirical improvement over prior work [17].
- Provides convergence bounds: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t), with dependence on data and network topology.
- Extends standard gossip averaging to handle pairwise interactions using a “phantom nodes” reformulation for cleaner analysis.
- Asynchronous algorithm adapts synchronous updates for lack of global clock.
- Properly situates work within extensive gossip and spectral graph theory literature and cites most relevant prior art.
- Technically sound: rigorous convergence proofs and creative use of phantom nodes.
- Addresses an important and practical problem in distributed statistics and ML.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with concise algorithm descriptions and defined notation.
- Explicit rate analysis that reveals dependence on data and network parameters.
- Empirical results show lower error and variance than the U2-gossip baseline [17].
- Limited baselines: lacks comparison to simple or intuitive alternatives (e.g., naive distributed U-statistic, randomized averaging).
- Incremental novelty: extension to pairwise interactions feels natural and not highly original; clearer statement of key innovations needed.
- Experimental depth is limited: should explore more datasets, network topologies, and parameter impacts (connectivity, degree distribution).
- Asynchronous rate is notably weaker than synchronous and its practical impact is underexplored.
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theory, relevant problem, and positive empirical evidence.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak baseline comparisons, incremental contribution, and unresolved weaker asynchronous convergence.
- Overall assessment: solid paper with reservations; recommended weak accept conditional on stronger baselines, expanded experiments, and deeper analysis of asynchronous performance.
