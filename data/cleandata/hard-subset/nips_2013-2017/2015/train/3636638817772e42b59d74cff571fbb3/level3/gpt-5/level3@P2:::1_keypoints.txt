- Paper studies decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip-style protocols, proposing GoSta-sync and GoSta-async with random-walk auxiliary observations and consensus averaging.  
- Uses a "phantom nodes" construction to decouple data propagation and averaging, proving convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t/t) (async) that depend on data dispersion and network spectral gap.  
- Positioned against classic randomized gossip and U2-gossip; claims one auxiliary observation per node, single edge activation per iteration, and stronger bounds than U2-gossip.  
- Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across three topologies shows improved convergence and lower across-node variance, matching spectral-gap trends.  
- Clear problem formulation and strong motivation: pairwise U-statistics are important and underexplored in decentralized settings.  
- Novel algorithmic design coupling random-walk data propagation with consensus averaging, with an asynchronous variant for realistic deployments.  
- Solid theoretical analysis using block-triangular state evolution on a phantom network, yielding interpretable data- and graph-dependent constants.  
- Improved communication and memory costs over U2-gossip with refined baseline comparisons.  
- Empirical results consistently show better accuracy and stability across tasks and topologies.  
- Guarantees are in expectation only; lacks high-probability or almost-sure convergence results common in gossip literature.  
- Asynchronous bound is weaker (O(log t/t)) with unspecified constants c′(G); looseness and gap to synchronous rate not thoroughly explored experimentally.  
- Experimental scope is limited: only two datasets, moderate n, no stress tests on high dimensionality, message loss, delays, or directed/asymmetric links.  
- Assumes nodes know their degree and total number of edges to compute pk and counters, which may be unrealistic without network discovery.  
- Assumes uniform random edge activation; no analysis for Metropolis weights, non-uniform/time-varying activation, or push-sum for directed graphs.  
- Restriction to degree-2 U-statistics; extensions to higher-order U-statistics are only briefly discussed.  
- Technical soundness: state evolution and spectral analysis appear correct; asynchronous analysis less tight and would benefit from high-probability results.  
- Writing is clear and well organized; phantom-node proof technique improves accessibility; minor typos and reference/footnote issues.  
- Originality: novel propagate-and-average approach for U-statistics and phantom-node analysis, plus an asynchronous algorithm and refined U2-gossip comparison.  
- Significance: meaningful advance for decentralized pairwise functional estimation with potential to become standard after stronger probabilistic guarantees and broader experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: provide high-probability bounds, analyze non-uniform edge activation and degree estimate errors, sharpen async constants, report communication bits and scaling with dimension, assess robustness to loss/delays, and discuss higher-order U-statistics extensions.  
- Pros for acceptance: important problem, conceptually clean algorithms with improved theory/practice, realistic asynchronous model, and clear analysis tied to spectral gap.  
- Cons for acceptance: only expectation guarantees, weaker async analysis, limited experiments, and strong modeling assumptions (global |E|, uniform sampling).  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid and novel contribution but would benefit from tightened async analysis and broader experiments.  
- Reviewer confidence: High on novelty and relation to prior gossip work; moderate on tightness of asynchronous bound.
