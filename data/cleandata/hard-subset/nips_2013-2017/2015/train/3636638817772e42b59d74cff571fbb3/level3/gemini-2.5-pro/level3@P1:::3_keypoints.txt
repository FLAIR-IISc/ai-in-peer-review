- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta) for distributed estimation of degree-two U-statistics.  
- Algorithm combines data propagation (node data swaps) and estimate averaging to accelerate consensus.  
- Uses a novel "phantom network" construction for rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Proven convergence rates: O(1/t) synchronous and O(log t/t) asynchronous, with bounds depending on data and network spectral gap.  
- Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter show GoSta outperforms prior U2-gossip baseline across multiple network topologies.  
- High technical quality with non-trivial, well-supported theoretical contributions and explicit constants relating to topology and data.  
- Original contribution, notably a fully asynchronous algorithm for U-statistics in decentralized settings.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and well-positioned within related work.  
- Significant practical relevance for large-scale ML, sensor networks, and decentralized optimization tasks.  
- Main experimental weakness: comparisons are iteration-based; wall-clock time plots are needed to confirm real-world runtime benefits.  
- Needs more detailed accounting of per-iteration computational and communication complexity (messages, data sizes, local computation).  
- Overall: strengths substantially outweigh minor experimental concerns; recommendation to accept.
