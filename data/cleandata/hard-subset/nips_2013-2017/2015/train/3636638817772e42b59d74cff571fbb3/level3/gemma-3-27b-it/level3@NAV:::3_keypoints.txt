- Addresses the important and underexplored problem of distributed U-statistic estimation in networks.
- Proposes two novel randomized gossip algorithms: GoSta-sync and GoSta-async.
- Provides theoretical convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous.
- Bounds include explicit dependencies on data and network characteristics.
- Builds on established gossip algorithm literature and extends it to U-statistics.
- Introduces the clever “phantom nodes” formulation to facilitate analysis.
- Demonstrates theoretical and empirical improvement over the U2-gossip baseline.
- Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks show GoSta outperforming U2-gossip, especially as network size grows.
- Paper is well motivated, clearly written, and has a strong theoretical foundation.
- Major practical concern: no per-iteration runtime comparison with U2-gossip, which could offset convergence gains.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on adding per-iteration runtime comparisons in the revision.
