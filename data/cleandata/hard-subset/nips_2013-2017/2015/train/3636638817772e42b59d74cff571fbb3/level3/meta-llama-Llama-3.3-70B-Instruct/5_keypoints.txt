- Unified framework for non-asymptotic error and structured recovery in norm-regularized regression with sub-exponential design and noise.  
- Establishes a relationship between Gaussian and exponential widths of sets to transfer Gaussian-width results to sub-exponential settings.  
- Derives precise sample complexity bounds for sub-exponential designs using that relationship.  
- Theoretical and empirical finding: estimation error decays slower for sub-exponential designs than for Gaussian designs.  
- Paper is well-organized and provides comprehensive theoretical and experimental analysis.  
- Key contribution: the Gaussian–exponential width connection that extends existing results to sub-exponential designs.  
- Experiments on synthetic data demonstrate method effectiveness and illuminate sub-exponential behavior.  
- Limitation: analysis is restricted to sub-exponential distributions, not broader heavy-tailed families.  
- Limitation: experimental validation is limited to synthetic datasets and lacks real-world tests.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with comprehensive analysis.  
- Suggested future work: extend to other heavy-tailed distributions and validate on real-world datasets.
