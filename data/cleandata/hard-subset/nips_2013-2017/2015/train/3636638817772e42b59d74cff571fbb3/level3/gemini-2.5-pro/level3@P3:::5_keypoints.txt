- Introduces GoSta: novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics that standard gossip can't compute.  
- Combines data propagation (swapping observations) with estimate averaging to extend gossip to pairwise statistics.  
- Improves over prior U2-gossip by offering better theoretical rates and lower resource requirements.  
- Derives non-asymptotic convergence rates: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t).  
- Uses a creative "phantom network" proof technique to decouple data propagation and averaging.  
- Convergence bounds link performance to data-dependent dispersion and network spectral gap.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with concrete motivation and precise theorems (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Pseudocode and comparison to U2-gossip effectively highlight gains in memory, communication, and convergence.  
- Empirically validated on AUC and within-cluster scatter using real datasets, showing GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip in error and variance.  
- Experiments confirm degradation on less-connected graphs and that the performance gap grows with network size, matching theory.  
- Asynchronous GoSta shows competitive empirical performance despite weaker theoretical rates.  
- Weakness: asynchronous algorithm assumes nodes know their degree and total number of edges, which may be unrealistic in dynamic or unknown topologies.  
- Weakness: constant c'(G) in the asynchronous bound is not explicitly characterized in terms of graph properties.  
- Weakness: comparison to U2-gossip would benefit from a more formal or targeted simulation demonstrating the claimed √n scaling.  
- Overall: technically strong, practically relevant, minor weaknesses only; strong recommendation for acceptance.
