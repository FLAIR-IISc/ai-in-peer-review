The paper "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics" presents a novel approach to computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors propose two gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for estimating U-statistics in a network of nodes. The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the algorithms, including convergence rates and experimental results.

The main idea of the paper is to extend gossip algorithms, which are commonly used for decentralized averaging, to the computation of U-statistics. U-statistics are a class of statistics that depend on pairs of observations, making them more challenging to compute in a decentralized setting. The authors' approach is based on the observation that U-statistics can be written as an average of local values, which can be computed using gossip algorithms.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the GoSta-sync algorithm, including a proof of convergence to the true U-statistic. The authors also provide a comparison with the U2-gossip algorithm, which is a previous approach to computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The results show that GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip in terms of convergence rate and memory requirements.

The paper also presents an asynchronous version of the algorithm, GoSta-async, which does not require a global clock. The authors provide a proof of convergence for this algorithm as well, and experimental results show that it performs similarly to GoSta-sync.

The experimental results are presented for two applications: decentralized estimation of the Area Under the ROC Curve (AUC) and within-cluster point scatter. The results show that GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip in both applications, with a lower average relative error and standard deviation across nodes.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and comprehensive analysis of their approach. The experimental results are convincing, and the comparison with U2-gossip is helpful in understanding the advantages of the proposed approach.
