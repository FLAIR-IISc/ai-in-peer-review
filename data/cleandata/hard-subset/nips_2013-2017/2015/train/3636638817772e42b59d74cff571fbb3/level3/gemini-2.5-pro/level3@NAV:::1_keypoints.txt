- Introduces GoSta, randomized gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of degree-two U-statistics, addressing a significant problem beyond simple averages.  
- Posits a substantial improvement over prior U2-gossip by reducing communication cost, memory requirements, and removing the need for synchrony.  
- Provides synchronous and asynchronous algorithms with theoretical convergence rates O(1/t) and O(log t / t), respectively.  
- Theoretical claims are validated by extensive empirical evaluation on two real-world datasets.  
- Demonstrates clear empirical superiority to U2-gossip in convergence speed, memory usage, and communication overhead.  
- The asynchronous variant is practically important for decentralized systems without global clocks.  
- Method is novel: combines auxiliary observation swapping (data propagation) with estimate averaging.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-motivated, and organized; technical details are easy to follow.  
- Uses an elegant "phantom network" construction for convergence analysis.  
- Minor weaknesses: no discussion on extending GoSta to U-statistics of degree >2.  
- Empirical evaluation could be strengthened by testing more network topologies and larger-scale datasets.  
- Note the theoretical gap for asynchronous case (O(log t / t)) versus its strong empirical performance.  
- Overall recommendation: excellent, original, significant contribution; strongly recommend acceptance.
