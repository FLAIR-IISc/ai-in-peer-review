- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync/async) for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics (e.g., AUC, within-cluster scatter).
- Core idea: combine random neighbor data propagation with averaging of local estimates so each node tracks the global U-statistic.
- Introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple propagation and averaging for analysis.
- Derives explicit convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with constants depending on data dispersion and graph spectral gap.
- Empirical evaluation on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs shows consistent outperformance of the U2-gossip baseline.
- Experiments on two datasets report AUC via average relative AUC error and show performance improves with connectivity, matching theory.
- Theory is sound and tied carefully to graph spectra; comparison to U2-gossip clarifies the benefit of averaging.
- Paper is well written and organized; the phantom-node analysis is explained clearly, with some technical details in the supplement.
- Original contribution: extending gossip methods to U-statistics with both sync and async protocols and providing explicit rates beyond prior U2-gossip work.
- Significance: applicable to many important statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, Kendall tau) and useful for decentralized ML.
- Main concern: practical utility unclear due to missing per-iteration runtime, communication, and memory cost comparisons.
- Recommended additions: report per-iteration runtimes for GoSta-sync/async and U2-gossip, bytes communicated and H-evaluations per iteration, and wall-clock time to reach target relative error.
- Pros: clear motivation, strong convergence bounds, consistent empirical gains, and an asynchronous algorithm suited to realistic settings.
- Cons: no per-iteration runtime/communication analysis and limited experimental breadth (only two datasets and limited metrics).
