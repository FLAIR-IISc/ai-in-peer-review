- Proposes synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) randomized gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics in distributed settings.  
- Strong motivation: U-statistics (pairwise averages) are important for tasks like AUC and variance estimation but less studied in distributed gossip literature.  
- Provides convergence rate bounds with analysis depending on both data and network properties; uses a phantom node reformulation as a proof technique.  
- Empirical evaluation on real datasets (AUC and within-cluster scatter) shows consistent faster convergence than U2-gossip across network topologies.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and connects to related decentralized computation and gossip work.  
- Novel contribution: addresses a relatively unexplored problem of computing U-statistics with gossip algorithms.  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous convergence analysis and bounds provided.  
- Empirical validation: experiments convincingly support the claimed performance improvements.  
- Clarity: technical presentation and notation are consistent and readable despite density.  
- Practical relevance: applications demonstrated are widely useful in ML and data mining.  
- Missing detailed per-iteration runtime comparison; overall runtime advantage may not hold if per-iteration cost is higher.  
- Network model assumes uniformly random edge selection, which may not reflect many real-world networks.  
- Convergence analysis for the asynchronous algorithm is less detailed and needs stronger justification or expansion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, sound theory, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of per-iteration runtime breakdown and simplified network model limiting generalizability.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; recommend acceptance with a request to add more detailed runtime/per-iteration analysis for the final version.  
- Quality: Excellent; Clarity: Very Good; Originality: Excellent; Significance: Good.
