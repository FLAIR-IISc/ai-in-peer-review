This paper proposes novel gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which is a significant contribution to the field of decentralized estimation. The authors achieve faster convergence rates than prior state-of-the-art (U2-Gossip) and demonstrate the superiority of their approach through extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets. The paper is well-written, well-motivated, and properly positioned relative to prior work.

1. Novel algorithms: The authors propose new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. Faster convergence rates: The authors demonstrate that their algorithms achieve faster convergence rates than prior state-of-the-art (U2-Gossip).
3. Realistic asynchronous settings: The authors consider realistic asynchronous settings, where nodes may not have access to a global clock, and propose a fully asynchronous algorithm.
4. Superior memory and communication cost: The authors demonstrate that their algorithms have superior memory and communication cost compared to U2-Gossip.
5. Theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis: The authors provide theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis for their algorithms, which is a significant contribution to the field.
6. Extensive empirical evaluation: The authors conduct extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets, which demonstrates the practical interest of their approach.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

1. Complexity of the algorithms: The algorithms proposed by the authors are complex and may be difficult to implement in practice.
