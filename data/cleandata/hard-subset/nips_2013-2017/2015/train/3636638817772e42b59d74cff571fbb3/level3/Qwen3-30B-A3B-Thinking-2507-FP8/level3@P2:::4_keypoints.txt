- Addresses a gap: extends gossip algorithms to distributed estimation of U-statistics, which are harder than separable statistics.  
- Main proposal: synchronous and asynchronous GoSta algorithms with convergence rates O(1/t) and O(log t/t).  
- Novel reformulation: introduces "phantom nodes" to enable clean convergence analysis for pairwise statistics.  
- Theoretical contribution: comprehensive analysis with explicit bounds showing dependence on data and network spectral gap.  
- Empirical claim: GoSta outperforms prior U2-gossip and requires lower memory and communication, especially for high-dimensional data.  
- Experiments: evaluated on AUC estimation and within-cluster point scatter using SMVguide3 and Wine Quality datasets across multiple network topologies.  
- Results: consistent superiority of GoSta over U2-gossip, with larger gains as network size increases.  
- Writing and organization: paper is clear, well-organized, and situates work within existing gossip literature.  
- Critical weakness: lacks comparisons to meaningful baselines beyond U2-gossip (e.g., naive local computation or adapted standard gossip).  
- Novelty concern: unclear how conceptually different GoSta is from U2-gossip despite the phantom nodes framing.  
- Limited experiments: only two applications tested, not covering other important U-statistics (e.g., Gini mean difference, Kendall tau).  
- Scientific quality: sound theoretical analysis and meaningful experiments, but empirical claims are weakened by insufficient baselines.  
- Recommendation: weak reject due to inadequate empirical validation.  
- Suggested improvements: add naive distributed and adapted gossip baselines, expand U-statistic applications, clarify novelty versus U2-gossip, and discuss limitations like topology sensitivity.
