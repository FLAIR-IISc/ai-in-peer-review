Summary and relation to prior work
This paper tackles the decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a network using gossip. U-statistics include many widely used functionals such as AUC, sample variance, Gini mean difference, Kendall’s tau, and within-cluster scatter, all of which depend on pairwise interactions and are not separable across nodes. The authors propose two algorithms: GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous), which combine (i) propagation of auxiliary data via random pairwise swaps and (ii) averaging of local estimates. A key analysis device is a “phantom nodes” construction that decouples data propagation and averaging and enables convergence analysis.

For GoSta-sync, the paper proves convergence in expectation at O(1/t), with constants that separate data-dependent dispersion terms from a network-dependent spectral gap term (1 − λ2(2)). For GoSta-async, the paper proves O(log t / t) rates. The analysis also refines the rate for the prior U2-gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, IFAC) and argues that GoSta enjoys both faster convergence (no √n penalty) and lower memory/communication per iteration. Experiments on AUC (svmguide3) and within-cluster scatter (Wine Quality) across three graph families (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz) show that GoSta-sync achieves lower average relative error and lower cross-node variance than U2-gossip, with the performance gap widening on larger graphs.

This work sits squarely within the NeurIPS literature on gossip/consensus and distributed optimization [Boyd et al. 2006; Dimakis et al. 2010; Nedić & Ozdaglar 2009; Duchi et al. 2012] and connects to U-processes in ML [Clémençon, NIPS]. To my knowledge, apart from U2-gossip, there has been little prior work on decentralized estimation of U-statistics; this paper substantially advances that frontier.

Strengths
- Clear problem motivation: many relevant statistics in ML are pairwise and not separable.
- Novel algorithms for both synchronous and asynchronous settings with provable rates, explicit dependence on spectral gap and data dispersion.
- Elegant analysis via phantom nodes; the decomposition into propagation and averaging is insightful.
- Stronger rates and strictly lower per-iteration state/communication versus U2-gossip; refined comparison bound for U2-gossip is helpful.
- Experiments on multiple graph topologies; consistent improvement in mean error and variance; scaling with n in line with theory.
- Writing is clear and well organized; assumptions and algorithmic steps are easy to follow.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Practical efficiency not fully characterized: per-iteration wall-clock time and communication volume are not reported. If GoSta’s iterations are slower than U2-gossip (e.g., due to additional averaging or bookkeeping), the overall time-to-accuracy advantage may shrink.
- Experimental scope is limited: only two datasets and moderate d; claims about benefits in high-dimensional regimes (memory/communication) would be stronger with larger-d experiments and explicit byte counts.
- Bounds are in expectation; no high-probability or finite-time concentration results are provided, despite the stochastic nature of activation and data movement.
- Asynchronous algorithm requires knowledge of degrees and |E| to compute pk; in many networks these are unknown or dynamic. Robustness to degree/edge-count estimation error is not discussed.
- Uniform random edge activation is assumed; generalization to non-uniform or directed/asymmetric communication (common in practice) is not analyzed.
- Bipartite graphs are excluded; a simple “lazy” variant is likely to fix this and could be discussed.

Quality
- The technical development is sound and careful. The spectral-gap dependence is standard and appropriate, and the phantom-node construction is a clever device to capture coupled random walks plus averaging. Proofs are not included in the main text, but the stated results are coherent.
- Experiment design is reasonable for an initial study, but missing system-level metrics (time, messages, bytes, energy) limit practical conclusions.

Clarity
- The paper is well written and structured. Algorithms are easy to implement from the descriptions. Minor suggestions: explicitly summarize per-iteration compute, memory and communication for GoSta vs U2-gossip in a small table; clarify the requirement of |E| and how nodes obtain/approximate it; briefly explain how to handle bipartite graphs (e.g., laziness) and directed graphs.

Originality
- High. Extends gossip to a non-separable class with both synchronous and asynchronous variants and provides sharper rates than the only prior method. The analysis technique is also original.

Significance
- High. U-statistics are ubiquitous in ML and statistics; enabling their decentralized estimation with guarantees is valuable. The dependence on the spectral gap gives actionable insights for network design. The algorithms could become building blocks for distributed optimization of pairwise losses.

Specific questions and requests to authors
- Please report per-iteration wall-clock time and communication per iteration (messages and bytes), for both GoSta and U2-gossip, and the total time/messages to reach a target relative error. This is especially important because practical utility may be limited if GoSta’s iterations are much slower than U2-gossip.
- Provide a comparison of memory footprint per node as a function of data dimension d, and measure for large-d settings.
- Can you provide high-probability bounds (e.g., via martingale techniques) or at least variance bounds to complement expectation results?
- How sensitive is GoSta-async to inaccuracies in pk arising from unknown |E| or degree estimates? Can you propose a variant that avoids global |E| (e.g., using local activations and self-normalized step sizes)?
- Can the analysis and algorithms extend to non-uniform neighbor selection (general stochastic matrices) and to directed graphs?
- Please comment on handling bipartite graphs (e.g., lazy random walks) and whether the rates change.

Pros
- Strong theoretical guarantees with clear network/data dependence.
- Asynchronous variant with convergence guarantees.
- Consistent empirical gains over U2-gossip across graphs; lower cross-node variance.
- Lower memory and communication per iteration.

Cons
- Missing system-level benchmarks (time, bytes, messages).
- Limited empirical scope (datasets, dimensionality); no high-probability bounds.
- Assumptions on known |E| and non-bipartite graphs; limited discussion of robustness/generalization.

Recommendation
- Leaning accept. The contribution is technically solid, original, and relevant to NeurIPS. Addressing the runtime/communication measurements and clarifying robustness would significantly strengthen the paper.

Confidence
- High: familiar with gossip/consensus literature and distributed estimation; moderate familiarity with U-statistics.
