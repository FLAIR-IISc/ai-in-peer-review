- Problem & contribution: Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics using per-node running estimate Zk and a random-walking auxiliary observation Yk.  
- Algorithmic steps: Each iteration updates local running average H(Xk,Yk) and averages Z across a selected edge while swapping Y-values.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Shows expectation convergence to the global U-statistic with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async), constants depend on data dispersion and graph spectral gap.  
- Proof technique: Introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to separate averaging from data propagation in the analysis.  
- Comparison to prior work: Targets pairwise-dependent functionals and contrasts with U2-gossip, claiming better rates and lower memory/communication.  
- Relation to literature: Builds on randomized pairwise averaging and consensus/gossip literature (spectral-gap dependence) and classical U-statistics theory.  
- Theoretical limitations: Results are in expectation only — no high-probability, almost-sure convergence, or variance/concentration bounds provided.  
- Async-specific issues: Asynchronous bound hides a graph-dependent constant, scales with ||H||, and lacks the synchronous data-dependent structure; tightness unclear.  
- Practical assumption: GoSta-async requires nodes to know pk = 2dk/|E| (i.e., |E|), which may be unrealistic and is not addressed.  
- Experimental strengths: Empirical tests on AUC and within-cluster scatter across graph classes show consistent improvement over U2-gossip.  
- Experimental weaknesses: Narrow evaluation—only compares to U2-gossip, lacks communication/memory/computation measurements, and minimal asynchronous experiments.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written and algorithms clear; phantom-network exposition is dense and would benefit from more intuition and earlier definition of pk.  
- Suggested clarity fix: Add a brief complexity table (state size, messages, H-evals per iteration) to improve readability.  
- Originality: Problem is underexplored and phantom-node proof is novel, but algorithmic approach is a natural extension of known gossip ideas and incremental over U2-gossip.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for many metrics (AUC, variance), with spectral-gap insights, but practical significance limited by weak empirical evaluation.  
- Pros: Relevant problem, simple implementable algorithms with low state, solid spectral-gap-dependent guarantees, elegant phantom-graph analysis.  
- Cons: Limited baselines and measurements, only expectation bounds, async needs |E| knowledge, and novelty is incremental.  
- Recommended additional baselines: Naive Monte Carlo with gossip, token-based all-pairs sampling with broadcasts, spanning-tree aggregation, and random pairing without running-average weighting.  
- Suggested empirical metrics: Report messages, bytes, H-evaluations, memory per node, time-to-ε, and sensitivity to costly H and degree heterogeneity.  
- Robustness and extensions requested: Analyze/experiment with high-probability bounds, variance decay, directed/time-varying graphs, message loss, heterogeneous activations, multiple observations per node, and privacy constraints.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — solid technical work but modest novelty and insufficient empirical evidence to justify acceptance.  
- Reviewer confidence: High for literature and empirical assessment; moderate on fine proof details.
