- Addresses gap: extends gossip algorithms to distributed estimation of U-statistics (degree two) rather than separable statistics like means.
- Proposes two algorithms: synchronous GoSta-sync and asynchronous GoSta-async for distributed U-statistic estimation.
- Key innovation: combines propagation of local observations via auxiliary variables with averaging of local estimates.
- Analysis technique: introduces a "phantom nodes" reformulation to enable tractable convergence analysis.
- Convergence rates: proves O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, improving on prior U2-Gossip.
- Efficiency: lower memory and communication costs than U2-Gossip (fewer auxiliary observations/exchanges).
- Theory accounts for both data-dependent difficulty and network-dependent spectral gap terms.
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments on AUC (SMVguide3) and within-cluster scatter (Wine Quality).
- Empirical results: GoSta consistently outperforms U2-Gossip across topologies (complete, 2D grid, Watts-Strogatz), with gap widening as network size grows.
- Clarity and presentation: well-written paper with precise pseudocode, clear figures, and accessible exposition.
- Originality: novel problem focus in gossip literature and creative phantom-nodes analysis technique.
- Significance: addresses important statistics (AUC, variance, Gini) with practical algorithmic advantages.
- Limitations: scope limited to degree-two U-statistics; async theoretical rate slightly worse than synchronous.
- Recommendation: strong contribution with rigorous theory and experiments; reviewer recommends acceptance.
