## Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

This paper addresses the important problem of distributed estimation of U-statistics, a class of functionals crucial in many applications but less explored in the context of gossip algorithms compared to simple averages. The authors propose novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for this task, deriving theoretical convergence rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) respectively. The work is well-motivated, clearly presented, and offers a valuable contribution to the field of distributed estimation.

The paper builds upon the established body of work in gossip algorithms, notably referencing [19, 5, 2], which focus on simpler averaging tasks. It correctly identifies the limitations of existing approaches when applied to U-statistics and positions its contribution as filling a gap in the literature. The introduction of “phantom nodes” to facilitate the convergence analysis is a clever technique, and the derived convergence rates, dependent on both data characteristics and network topology (through the spectral gap, as highlighted in [3]), are a significant theoretical achievement.  The comparison to the prior work of [17] is also appropriately framed, emphasizing the improved convergence and lower communication/memory costs of the proposed algorithms.

The strengths of this paper lie in its solid theoretical foundation, clear exposition, and empirical validation. The organization is excellent, and the mathematical derivations appear sound. The experiments using AUC and within-cluster point scatter on real datasets convincingly demonstrate the algorithms’ performance advantages and accurate estimation capabilities compared to U2-gossip [17].  The inclusion of different network topologies in the experimental evaluation is a valuable addition, illustrating the impact of network connectivity on convergence rates.

However, a minor weakness is that the paper could benefit from a more extensive comparison to alternative distributed estimation techniques beyond just U2-gossip. While the focus on gossip algorithms is clear, briefly discussing how the proposed methods compare to, for example, distributed stochastic gradient descent or other distributed optimization approaches would provide further context. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Introduces a new approach to a challenging problem in distributed estimation.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:**  Provides strong theoretical guarantees on convergence rates.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  Demonstrates the algorithms' effectiveness through comprehensive experiments.
*   **Significance:**  Addresses a relevant problem with potential impact in various applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited comparison to methods outside of the gossip algorithm family. (Minor)



Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the point regarding comparison to related techniques.
