- Paper introduces a novel gossip-style decentralized algorithm for computing pairwise U-statistics (e.g., variance, AUC).  
- Extends decentralized averaging of separable functions to the harder setting of pairwise interactions across a network.  
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous algorithms enabling nodes to compute a global U-statistic using only local communication.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees (O(1/t) rate).  
- Main strength: elegant and original solution with a clever mechanism of swapping a secondary state (initial observation) while averaging local estimates.  
- Key algorithmic insight: swapped observations perform coupled random walks, enabling indirect interaction of every data-pair over time.  
- Recommendation to emphasize the observation-swapping mechanism more clearly as the central contribution.  
- Communication model is ambiguous—clarify the edge distribution induced by the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” model.  
- Theoretical analysis should state assumptions on initial data distribution (worst-case vs probabilistic) and express error guarantees explicitly as a function of the data.  
- Suggest comparing the O(1/t) rate to a memory-intensive flooding baseline where nodes store all received observations.  
- Related work is sparse—expand citations (e.g., Dimakis et al., Proc. IEEE) and explore connections to lifted Markov chains and acceleration techniques.  
- Experiments are promising but lack comparisons to relevant baselines; supplementary proofs were not verified due to time constraints.  
- Several minor notational and language issues need fixing.  
- Overall verdict: strong paper, recommend acceptance conditional on addressing clarity, theoretical precision, related work, and experimental baselines.
