This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics, which are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population. The authors address a less investigated area of computation of U-statistics, relying on averaging over pairs of observations, and provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis of their approach.

The paper relates to previous work on gossip algorithms for decentralized averaging in networks, but the focus on U-statistics is a novel contribution. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, including existing gossip algorithms and their limitations, and position their approach as an improvement over the state-of-the-art.

The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, are designed to efficiently compute U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The synchronous algorithm, GoSta-sync, assumes a global clock and achieves a convergence rate of O(1/t), while the asynchronous algorithm, GoSta-async, does not require a global clock and achieves a convergence rate of O(log t/t). The authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence rates, including explicit data and network-dependent terms.

The paper also presents numerical experiments on real datasets, comparing the performance of GoSta-sync and GoSta-async to the state-of-the-art algorithm, U2-gossip. The results show that GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip on various network topologies, including complete graphs, two-dimensional grids, and Watts-Strogatz networks. The asynchronous algorithm, GoSta-async, has comparable performance to GoSta-sync, despite its slightly worse theoretical convergence rate.

The strengths of the paper include its comprehensive theoretical analysis, novel application of gossip algorithms to U-statistics, and thorough experimental evaluation. However, the paper has some weaknesses, including the lack of comparison to baseline methods and the limited discussion of the practical implications of the results.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their approach. The paper is a complete piece of work, and the results are significant and relevant to the field.

The clarity of the paper is good, with a clear and well-organized structure. The writing is concise, and the authors provide sufficient information for the expert reader to reproduce the results. However, some sections, such as the description of the algorithms and the theoretical analysis, may be challenging for non-experts to follow.

The originality of the paper is high, as it addresses a novel application of gossip algorithms to U-statistics. The approach is new and distinct from previous work, and the authors provide a comprehensive analysis of their method. The paper is well-referenced, and the authors adequately acknowledge previous work in the field.
