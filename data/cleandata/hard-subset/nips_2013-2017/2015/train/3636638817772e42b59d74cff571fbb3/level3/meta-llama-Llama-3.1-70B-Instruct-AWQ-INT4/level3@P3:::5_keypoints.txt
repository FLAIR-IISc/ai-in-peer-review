- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) for decentralized estimation of U-statistics in networks.  
- Provides a thorough introduction and clear explanation of U-statistics, problem statement, and proposed algorithms.  
- Paper is well-organized and techniques are clearly presented.  
- Theoretical convergence rates established: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms.  
- Novel proof technique using a "phantom nodes" reformulation, a notable methodological contribution.  
- Experiments demonstrate GoSta outperforms existing approaches, including U2-gossip.  
- Empirical comparison highlights advantages in convergence rate, memory, and communication costs.  
- Paper is accessible to a broad audience with sufficient background material.  
- Strength: novel algorithms addressing decentralized U-statistics estimation.  
- Strength: thorough convergence analysis with theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: strong experimental validation of performance and convergence behavior.  
- Weakness: assumes uniform distribution over network edges, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and future research directions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant novelty, solid theoretical analysis, and convincing experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unrealistic edge-distribution assumption and missing computational complexity evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; considered a significant contribution.
