- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics (pair-dependent statistics).
- Proves convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with explicit dependence on data and network properties.
- Extends gossip-based decentralized averaging methods to the more complex U-statistics problem, filling a literature gap.
- Empirically outperforms the U2-gossip algorithm in convergence speed, memory usage, and communication efficiency.
- Strength: Technically sound with rigorous theoretical analysis and innovative "phantom nodes" reformulation separating data propagation and averaging.
- Strength: Well-organized paper with clear exposition, detailed pseudocode, and supplementary material aiding reproducibility.
- Strength: Original contribution that advances decentralized U-statistics computation with improved guarantees and practical performance.
- Strength: High significance for applications in sensor networks, social media analysis, and distributed machine learning due to efficiency and reduced resource needs.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited to two applications (AUC and within-cluster scatter) and three network types; broader tests are needed.
- Weakness: Theoretical analysis of the asynchronous algorithm is less detailed; constants and network-dependence warrant further study.
- Weakness: Comparisons are mainly against U2-gossip; missing benchmarks with other decentralized estimation or adaptive communication methods.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends accepting the paper due to strong theoretical and practical contributions.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically rigorous algorithms with clear guarantees; significant empirical improvements; clear writing and reproducibility; high relevance and impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experimental scope and baselines; less comprehensive theoretical treatment for the asynchronous method.
- Overall: Strong scientific contribution, well-suited for conference presentation.
