- Addresses distributed estimation of U-statistics, a less-explored class of functionals for gossip algorithms.  
- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms tailored for U-statistics.  
- Derives theoretical convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous.  
- Employs “phantom nodes” as a clever analytical tool for convergence analysis.  
- Shows convergence dependence on data properties and network topology via the spectral gap.  
- Positions work relative to prior gossip literature, identifying limitations of existing averaging methods.  
- Compares favorably to U2-gossip [17], claiming improved convergence and lower communication/memory costs.  
- Strength: solid theoretical foundations and clear, well-organized exposition.  
- Strength: empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks using real datasets.  
- Strength: experiments consider multiple network topologies to illustrate connectivity effects.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to distributed estimation techniques outside the gossip family (e.g., DSGD or other distributed optimization methods).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add broader comparisons to related distributed techniques.
