- Proposes novel synchronous and fully asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, advancing decentralized estimation methods.  
- Achieves faster convergence rates than prior state-of-the-art (U2-Gossip).  
- Explicitly handles realistic asynchronous settings where nodes lack a global clock.  
- Demonstrates superior memory and communication costs compared to U2-Gossip.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis for the proposed algorithms.  
- Validates practical effectiveness via extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets.  
- Algorithms are complex and may be difficult to implement in practice.  
- Scalability to large-scale networks is not demonstrated.  
- Robustness to node failures or other network faults is not evaluated.  
- Overall, the paper is a significant, well-written contribution but needs future work on implementation complexity, scalability, and robustness.
