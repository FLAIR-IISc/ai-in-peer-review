This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors provide a well-motivated and well-written paper that addresses a less investigated area in the context of gossip algorithms. The proposed methods, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, are designed to efficiently compute U-statistics, which are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population.

The paper presents a clear and concise introduction to the problem, followed by a thorough review of related work in gossip algorithms. The authors then describe their approach, including the convergence analysis for both synchronous and asynchronous settings. The numerical results presented in the paper demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over the previously introduced U2-gossip approach.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a novel solution to the problem of computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates for both synchronous and asynchronous settings, which is a significant improvement over previous work.
* The numerical results presented in the paper demonstrate the practical interest of the proposed algorithms.

However, there are some weaknesses and concerns that need to be addressed:

* The reviewer requests per-iteration run-times for both the proposed method and U2-gossip to assess the practical utility of the proposed method.
* There is a concern that if the proposed method's iterations are much slower than U2-gossip, its practical utility may be limited.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of gossip algorithms and decentralized computation. The proposed methods have the potential to be widely used in various applications, and the authors have demonstrated their superiority over previous approaches. However, the concerns regarding the per-iteration run-times and the practical utility of the proposed method need to be addressed in future work.
