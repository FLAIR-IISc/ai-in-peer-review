- Tackles decentralized U-statistic computation with synchronous/asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms and provides convergence rate bounds, addressing an important problem with practical applications.
- Core algorithmic idea: nodes swap a secondary “observation” during averaging, yielding coupled random walks that are clever and effective.
- Paper needs stronger, more explicit connections to existing gossip literature to clarify how it builds on or diverges from prior work.
- The “phantom node” reformulation is an intriguing central trick but is not sufficiently highlighted in the main text; its analytical role should be emphasized.
- Lacks comparative analysis against simple alternatives (e.g., flooding-based tagged observation re-transmission) to clarify tradeoffs and the achieved 1/t convergence.
- Related work should be expanded to include Dimakis et al. (Proc. IEEE) and other gossip algorithms, and the authors should consider acceleration via lifting constructions (e.g., LADA, lifted Markov chains).
- Theoretical analysis must state assumptions on the initial data distribution (worst-case vs. probabilistic) and give concrete error guarantees as functions of initial data.
- Experiments are reported but lack baseline comparisons beyond [17], limiting empirical validation of practical benefits.
- Reviewer could not fully verify supplemental proofs due to time constraints; several minor language and notation issues need correction.
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, novel algorithms, and initial convergence guarantees.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak linkage to existing literature, insufficient emphasis on the algorithmic trick, and inadequate comparative/experimental validation.
- After authors’ response and planned revisions, reviewer raised score to 8 contingent on adequately addressing the suggested improvements.
