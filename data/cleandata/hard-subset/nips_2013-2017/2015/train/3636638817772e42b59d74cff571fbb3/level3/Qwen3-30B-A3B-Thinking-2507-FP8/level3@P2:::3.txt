

# Review for "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

This paper proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) for decentralized estimation of U-statistics in networked systems. The authors address an important gap in the literature, as existing gossip algorithms can efficiently compute separable statistics (like sample means) but not U-statistics, which require pairwise interactions. The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed computation.

The authors formulate the problem of estimating degree-two U-statistics as Ûn(H) = 1/n²∑i,jH(Xi,Xj), where H is a symmetric function. They propose two algorithms: GoSta-sync for the synchronous setting with O(1/t) convergence rate, and GoSta-async for asynchronous networks with O(log t/t) convergence. The key innovation is the use of "phantom nodes" to reformulate the problem and analyze convergence, which allows them to separate data propagation from estimation steps. The paper establishes theoretical guarantees with explicit data and network-dependent terms, and demonstrates empirical superiority over the previous U2-gossip baseline [17] on two real-world applications (AUC estimation and within-cluster point scatter) across multiple network topologies (complete, grid, and Watts-Strogatz graphs).

The paper excels in quality, providing rigorous convergence analysis supported by theoretical bounds that depend on both data characteristics (through dispersion measures) and network topology (through spectral gap). The experimental results are comprehensive, showing consistent performance gains across different network structures and problem instances, with lower variance in estimates due to the averaging step. The clarity of the paper is excellent, with well-structured presentation, clear pseudocode, and precise mathematical formulation. The originality is strong, as the authors properly contextualize their contribution against related work, noting that U2-gossip [17] was the only prior approach to this problem, and that their method improves upon it in convergence rate, memory usage, and communication cost.

The significance of this work is high, as U-statistics are fundamental to many applications in machine learning and data analysis (AUC, variance, Gini mean difference, etc.). The proposed algorithms provide a practical solution to a previously unaddressed problem in decentralized systems, with clear potential for adoption in sensor networks, peer-to-peer systems, and distributed machine learning. The authors also identify promising directions for future work, including adaptive communication schemes and extension to decentralized optimization.

The main weakness concerns practical implementation details. The paper does not provide per-iteration run-times for the proposed GoSta algorithms compared to U2-gossip. Given that the authors claim lower memory and communication costs, it's crucial to demonstrate that the proposed algorithms aren't significantly slower per iteration than U2-gossip. If the iteration time is substantially higher for GoSta, the practical advantage might be limited despite better convergence rates. The authors should include a timing analysis of both algorithms, especially for high-dimensional data (d large), as the paper notes this is particularly relevant for memory and communication efficiency.

Another minor concern is that the experimental section focuses only on two applications. While the AUC and within-cluster point scatter examples are well-chosen, the authors could strengthen their argument by showing results on at least one additional U-statistic application.

To summarize the key points:

**Strengths:**
- Well-motivated problem addressing an important gap in decentralized computation
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with meaningful convergence bounds
- Clear and well-written presentation of algorithms and results
- Strong empirical validation across multiple network topologies and applications
- Clear comparison to the previous U2-gossip method
- Practical significance for real-world decentralized systems

**Weaknesses:**
- Missing per-iteration timing analysis to validate practical efficiency claims
- Limited to two application examples (though well-chosen)

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed algorithms, with a clear theoretical foundation and strong empirical validation. The proposed GoSta algorithms represent a meaningful advancement over the previous work, with better convergence rates and lower memory/communication costs. The only major concern is the lack of timing information to confirm that the theoretical advantages translate to practical efficiency gains.

I recommend acceptance. The paper addresses an important problem with a novel solution, provides rigorous theoretical guarantees, and demonstrates clear empirical advantages. The minor concern about timing analysis is not sufficient to outweigh the paper's strong contributions. I would encourage the authors to include timing results in the camera-ready version to further strengthen the practical implications of their work.
