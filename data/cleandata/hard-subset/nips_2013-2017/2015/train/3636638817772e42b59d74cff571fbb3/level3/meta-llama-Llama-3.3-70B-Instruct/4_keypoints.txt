- Novel framework for non-asymptotic error and structured recovery in norm-regularized regression with sub-exponential design matrices and noise.  
- Establishes a relationship between Gaussian and exponential widths, enabling use of existing Gaussian-width results to bound exponential widths.  
- Generalizes sub-Gaussian results to the sub-exponential setting, replacing Gaussian width dependence with exponential width.  
- Proves a stronger Restricted Eigenvalue (RE) condition for Lasso and group-lasso problems.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear problem setup, background, and stated contributions.  
- Technical sections are detailed and clearly explained, making arguments easy to follow.  
- Provides experimental results that validate the theoretical findings.  
- Lacks sufficient practical context on why sub-exponential design matrices and noise matter in real-world applications.  
- Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, especially more detailed comparisons between Gaussian and sub-exponential cases.  
- Meets most conference criteria and relates work to prior NIPS and other literature.  
- Would benefit from more detailed arguments explicitly weighing acceptance pros and cons and emphasizing impact.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
