- Problem: estimating U-statistics in decentralized networks; U-statistics are non-separable and incompatible with standard gossip averaging.  
- Proposed methods: two randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Core idea: combine data propagation (nodes swap auxiliary observations) with explicit estimate averaging.  
- Theoretical results: convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Analysis technique: novel "phantom network" reformulation showing dependence on data terms and the graph spectral gap.  
- Empirical results: GoSta converges faster per iteration than U2-gossip across multiple topologies (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz) and for AUC and within-cluster scatter.  
- Improvement over prior work: outperforms U2-gossip and claims lower communication/memory overhead (one swap vs. two swaps).  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, precise notation, and informative figures; phantom network explained well.  
- Originality: novel focus on U-statistics, new data-propagation + averaging approach, and first fully asynchronous algorithm for this problem.  
- Significance: broadly applicable to large-scale ML, sensor networks, and P2P systems; could enable decentralized optimization with U-statistics.  
- Main weakness: experiments report iterations only; per-iteration computational cost and wall-clock time comparisons versus U2-gossip are not analyzed.  
- Recommendation: strong accept; suggest adding per-iteration complexity discussion and, if possible, wall-clock time experiments in the final version.
