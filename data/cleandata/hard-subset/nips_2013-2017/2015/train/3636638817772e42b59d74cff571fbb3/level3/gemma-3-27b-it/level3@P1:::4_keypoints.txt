- Addresses distributed estimation of U-statistics, a less-explored but important problem for global population properties (e.g., AUC, variance).
- Proposes two gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) combining data propagation with averaging of local estimates.
- Introduces "phantom nodes" to simplify analysis and enable convergence proofs.
- Derives explicit convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous cases.
- Convergence bounds depend on data and network characteristics, notably the spectral gap.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and connects performance to spectral graph theory.
- Extensions to asynchronous settings are valuable for realistic, unsynchronized networks.
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient and limited to two specific applications.
- Lacks comparison to simple or randomized baselines (e.g., distributed average) to contextualize gains.
- Experimental presentation needs more comprehensive visualizations across network sizes and topologies.
- Originality is somewhat unclear; more discussion of related work beyond [17] and recent NIPS papers is needed.
- Notation in theoretical sections is dense and could use more intuitive explanations of key concepts.
- Practical impact is moderate but currently limited by weak experimental validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel approach, relevance to distributed estimation, clear network-performance links.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate experiments, limited related-work discussion, dense presentation.
- Overall assessment: weak accept, contingent on improved experiments and stronger related-work/contextualization.
