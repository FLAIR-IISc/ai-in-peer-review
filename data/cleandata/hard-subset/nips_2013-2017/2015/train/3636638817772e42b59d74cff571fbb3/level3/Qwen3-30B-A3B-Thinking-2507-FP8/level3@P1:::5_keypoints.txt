- Addresses a clear gap: extends gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics (AUC, variance, Gini mean diff) previously unhandled in gossip frameworks.  
- Proposes two algorithms: GoSta-sync and GoSta-async to propagate data and maintain local U-statistic estimates.  
- Core innovation: introduces "phantom nodes" to decouple data propagation from averaging, enabling rigorous analysis.  
- Convergence results: synchronous O(1/t) with constants tied to graph Laplacian spectral gap; asynchronous O(log t / t).  
- Improves on U2-gossip: better convergence rates and removes the √n factor, lowering memory and communication costs.  
- Technical quality: elegant phantom-node construction and tight bounds quantifying data complexity and network topology interplay.  
- Proofs are thorough and supported by supplementary lemmas relating spectral quantities.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter show GoSta outperforms U2-gossip across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz networks.  
- Empirical scaling: performance gap widens with network size; GoSta-async matches sync performance in practice despite looser theory.  
- Originality: novel treatment of U-statistics in gossip settings and new combination of auxiliary observations with local averaging.  
- Precise comparison to prior work: explains U2-gossip’s limitations (lack of averaging) and situates contributions within NIPS gossip literature.  
- Practical significance: enables decentralized computation of key ML metrics useful for sensor networks and privacy-sensitive applications.  
- Efficiency: lower per-iteration memory (O(1) auxiliary observations) and communication (single edge swap) suited for high-dimensional data.  
- Minor weakness: asynchronous theoretical bound is less sharp than synchronous and classic gossip mean rates.  
- Minor weakness: experiments only compare to U2-gossip, lacking baselines for decentralized mean estimation for context.  
- Minor weakness: phantom-node idea is not immediately intuitive—would benefit from a small explanatory diagram.  
- Presentation: well-structured, precise writing, adequate supplementary material, and avoids overclaiming.  
- Overall judgment: rigorous, impactful contribution with minor addressable weaknesses; strong candidate for acceptance.
