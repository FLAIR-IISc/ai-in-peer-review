- Introduces GoSta-sync and GoSta-async gossip algorithms to estimate degree-2 U-statistics in decentralized networks.  
- Core idea: interleave propagation of auxiliary observations (random edge swaps) with averaging to approximate local U-terms and reach consensus on Ûn(H).  
- Proves convergence in expectation: synchronous O(1/t) with explicit spectral-gap dependence; asynchronous O(log t / t) with a looser, graph-dependent constant.  
- Uses a “phantom nodes” reformulation that cleanly separates data propagation from averaging and yields interpretable constants.  
- Improves over prior decentralized U-statistics method (U2-gossip) by reducing communication/memory (one auxiliary observation per node) and improving rates.  
- Theoretical development is sound; synchronous rate shows sharp 1/t decay as expected.  
- Asynchronous analysis is correct but yields a looser bound and hides graph-dependent constants (c′(G)).  
- Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across several graph families supports the theory and outperforms U2-gossip.  
- Experiments lack reporting of message counts, memory usage, and wall-clock complexity to reach target error.  
- Paper is well organized and clearly written; algorithms and analysis are easy to follow.  
- Suggest making c′(G) explicit or bounded in terms of λ2 and degrees and clarifying cost of H evaluations for large d.  
- Important and original extension of gossip to pairwise U-statistics with practical significance for decentralized ML metrics.  
- Cons: only expectation bounds (no high-probability/concentration results); asynchronous constant impact unclear; limited experimental breadth; requires knowing degrees/|E| and robustness to clock skew/packet loss not studied.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance after addressing empirical reporting and clarifying asynchronous constants.
