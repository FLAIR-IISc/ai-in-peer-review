- Problem & contribution: Decentralized computation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip; proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async using local estimate averaging plus auxiliary-observation swapping (coupled random walks).
- Theory: Proves convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) using a novel "phantom network" analysis.
- Experiments: Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter show GoSta outperforms prior U2-gossip [17].
- Strength — significance/originality: Tackles an important problem and improves on prior work in convergence rate, memory, and communication.
- Strength — technical quality: Provides rigorous convergence proofs with rates depending on data properties and network spectral gap; phantom-network is clever.
- Strength — algorithm design: Simple, effective mechanism that propagates pairwise information without storing the full dataset.
- Weakness — clarity/framing: The key insight that auxiliary observations perform coupled random walks is understated and should be emphasized.
- Weakness — communication model: Assumes uniform edge sampling; needs clarification and discussion of node-centric (wake-up) models where edge distribution differs unless graph is regular.
- Weakness — contextualization: O(1/t) rate should be contextualized (e.g., compared to flooding) to clarify trade-offs between speed and memory/communication.
- Weakness — related work: Related work is narrowly focused on [17]; should reference broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al.) and connections to lifting techniques like LADA.
- Weakness — experiments: Lacks additional baselines (e.g., flooding) to assess absolute performance despite clear improvement over [17].
- Weakness — theoretical clarity: Theorems give expected-error guarantees but should state assumptions on initial data and make dependence on H and h explicit in the main text.
- Minor: Note bipartite graphs can produce periodic behavior; fix minor typos and grammatical issues.
- Summary — pro: Novel, technically solid, and practically effective approach that advances decentralized U-statistics computation.
- Summary — con: Needs better positioning in literature and more comprehensive empirical baselines.
- Recommendation: Strong accept contingent on revisions addressing framing, baseline comparisons, and discussion of lifting/related work.
