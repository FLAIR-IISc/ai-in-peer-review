- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync, GoSta-async) for computing U-statistics in decentralized settings.
- Addresses a significant gap since prior gossip work focused mainly on simple statistics like the sample mean.
- Achieves faster convergence rates than prior state-of-the-art (U2-Gossip).
- Offers improvements in memory and communication costs compared to existing methods.
- Theoretical guarantees and convergence analysis are rigorous and provide concrete bounds.
- Introduces a clever "phantom network" technique to analyze the synchronous algorithm.
- Empirical evaluation on two datasets convincingly demonstrates superiority of the proposed methods.
- Paper is technically sound and complete, clearly defining the problem, algorithms, analysis, and experiments.
- Well-written and well-organized with clear notation and implementable algorithm descriptions (Algorithm 1 and 2).
- Original contribution by extending gossip algorithms to the more complex class of U-statistics.
- Properly situates work among prior research and explains shortcomings of U2-Gossip.
- Significant practical impact for distributed machine learning, sensor networks, and large-scale statistics.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, strong theory, empirical validation, clarity, and practical significance.
- Arguments against acceptance: spectral-graph-theory-heavy analysis may be challenging for some readers; async algorithm's practical gains over sync are limited despite slightly worse theoretical rate.
- Overall assessment: excellent, significant contribution with strong recommendation for acceptance.
