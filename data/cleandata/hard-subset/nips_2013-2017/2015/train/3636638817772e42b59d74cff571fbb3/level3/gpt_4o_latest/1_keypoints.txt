- Introduces GoSta, novel synchronous and asynchronous Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in decentralized networks.  
- Targets U-statistics (pairwise averages) important for metrics like AUC and within-cluster scatter.  
- Addresses limitations of U2-Gossip by reducing memory and communication costs and adding an asynchronous variant.  
- Proves faster convergence: O(1/t) synchronous and O(log t / t) asynchronous with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.  
- Uses an innovative "phantom nodes" analytical framework to model data propagation and averaging.  
- Positions work well within existing Gossip literature and fills a gap in decentralized U-statistics computation.  
- Strong technical quality with rigorous theoretical guarantees and clear analysis.  
- Empirical validation on real datasets shows GoSta outperforms U2-Gossip across multiple network topologies and scales.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and supported by comprehensive supplementary material.  
- Weakness: scalability to extremely large networks (e.g., millions of nodes) is not fully discussed.  
- Weakness: assumes nodes know their degree and total edge count, which may be unrealistic in dynamic or partial networks.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other decentralized baselines beyond U2-Gossip.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel algorithms, strong theory and experiments, high practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited scalability discussion and restrictive node knowledge assumptions.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
