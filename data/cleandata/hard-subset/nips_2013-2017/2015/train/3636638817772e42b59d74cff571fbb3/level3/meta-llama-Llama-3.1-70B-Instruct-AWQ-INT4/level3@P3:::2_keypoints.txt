- Proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing pairwise U-statistics using nodes that swap a secondary state so observations perform coupled random walks.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and numerical experiments demonstrating efficiency and robustness.  
- Addresses an important problem in decentralized estimation of U-statistics and is a valuable contribution.  
- Weak linkage to existing gossip literature; needs explicit discussion of relations to prior work (e.g., Dimakis et al., Kempe et al., Mosk-Aoyama & Shah, U2-gossip).  
- Notation and language issues: the term “phantom nodes” is not clearly defined.  
- Ambiguity in the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” model; clarify the induced edge-distribution and what “drawing a random edge” means.  
- Related work section is limited and should be expanded to include more relevant papers on gossip and decentralized estimation.  
- Theorems and analysis should state assumptions on the initial data distribution (worst-case vs probabilistic) and make error guarantees explicit as functions of initial data.  
- Suggest investigating lifting constructions (e.g., LADA or lifted Markov chains) to potentially accelerate mixing.  
- Experiments are promising but need more baseline comparisons, including centralized and other decentralized algorithms (e.g., U2-gossip).  
- Comparison to a flooding/memory-heavy alternative is useful to illustrate trade-offs.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the above issues; reviewer score = 8.
