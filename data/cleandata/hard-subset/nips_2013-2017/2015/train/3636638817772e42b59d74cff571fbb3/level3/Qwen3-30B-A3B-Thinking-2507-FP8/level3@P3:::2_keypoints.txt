- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for decentralized U-statistics using auxiliary-observation swapping that yields coupled random walks; convergence O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async).

- Fills an important gap for non-separable statistics (e.g., AUC, within-cluster scatter) with an elegant algorithmic idea and solid theory tied to network spectral gap and data properties.

- Experiments on real data show clear empirical advantages over prior U2-gossip in convergence speed and variance reduction.

- Needs stronger connections and citations to foundational gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. 2010; Geographic Gossip 2008) to better position the work.

- Asynchronous model is ambiguous about edge selection; must state whether edges are drawn uniformly from E or weighted by node degrees.

- Should compare to a flooding baseline (O(n) memory per node) to clarify memory vs. convergence tradeoffs relative to the proposed O(1) memory method.

- Recommend investigating lifting constructions (e.g., LADA/lifted Markov chains) to potentially accelerate mixing and improve network-dependent terms.

- Theorems should explicitly state assumptions on initial data distribution (worst-case vs. probabilistic) and provide error bounds as functions of initial data.

- Ensure supplemental proofs are complete and clearly documented, as the reviewer could not verify them within time constraints.

- Fix minor language/notation issues: clarify “coupled random walks” phrasing, bipartite directed-graph notation, and use consistent design-matrix notation (X vs. X).

- Experiments should include additional baselines beyond U2-gossip (e.g., flooding with memory tradeoffs) to strengthen empirical claims.

- Recommendation: Strong accept pending minor revisions; reviewer raised score from 7 to 8.
