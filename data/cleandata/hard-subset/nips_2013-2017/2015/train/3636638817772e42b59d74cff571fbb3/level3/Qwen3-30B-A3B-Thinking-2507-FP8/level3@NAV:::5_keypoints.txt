- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms to estimate U-statistics in decentralized networks.  
- Extends gossip methods beyond mean estimation to handle pairwise-dependent statistics like AUC, variance, and within-cluster scatter.  
- Addresses limitations of prior work (e.g., U2-gossip) that had high memory/communication costs and no asynchronicity.  
- Achieves provable convergence rates: O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.  
- Strong technical quality with rigorous convergence proofs that significantly improve theoretical guarantees over U2-gossip.  
- Key innovation is the “phantom nodes” reformulation, separating data propagation from averaging in the analysis.  
- Experimental validation on real datasets (SMVguide3, Wine Quality) shows faster convergence and better estimation accuracy across multiple network topologies (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz).  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear definitions, intuitive algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1–2), and effective visualizations (Figures 2–3).  
- Prior work is appropriately contextualized, and limitations of U2-gossip are thoroughly analyzed (Section 3).  
- Originality is strong: applying gossip to U-statistics and the phantom node technique provide a novel analytical framework.  
- Significance is high since U-statistics are fundamental in ML and decentralization is practically important.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, comprehensive experiments, and practical gains in memory/communication efficiency.  
- Weaknesses: practical implementation details (e.g., handling node failures) are underdeveloped, and the effect of network topology on convergence constants could be discussed more.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, well-presented, substantial contribution — strongly recommend acceptance.
