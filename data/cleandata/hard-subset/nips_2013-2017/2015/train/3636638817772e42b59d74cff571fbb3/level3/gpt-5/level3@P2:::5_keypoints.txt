- Paper tackles decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over networks using gossip.  
- Proposes two algorithms: GoSta-sync (synchronous averaging + one auxiliary swap) and GoSta-async (asynchronous with per-node activation counters).  
- Maintains only one auxiliary observation per node, improving memory and communication over U2-gossip.  
- Converges in expectation to the global U-statistic with explicit graph- and data-dependent rates.  
- Synchronous rate: O(1/t) with an exp(−ct) transient term; asynchronous rate: O(log t / t).  
- Analysis uses a novel “phantom nodes” construction to decouple averaging and data propagation.  
- Bounds depend on the spectral gap (eigenvalues of Wα(G)), showing benefit of well-connected graphs.  
- Refines analysis of U2-gossip and identifies a harmful √n factor due to lack of estimate averaging.  
- Experiments (AUC on svmguide3, within-cluster scatter on Wine Quality) show faster convergence and lower across-node variance than U2-gossip.  
- Synchronous and asynchronous variants perform similarly in practice.  
- Technical soundness: algorithms are well-defined and proofs are careful; phantom-network is a neat analytical device.  
- Asynchronous unbiased averaging achieved via mi ← mi + 1/pi trick is appropriate.  
- Theorems give expectation bounds but lack high-probability or mean-square guarantees.  
- Suggestion: provide high-probability/MSE bounds and make constants (e.g., c′(G)) explicit.  
- Suggestion: discuss robustness to message loss, delays, bipartite graphs (lazy updates), and directed/time-varying graphs (push-sum).  
- Suggestion: add per-iteration cost table and report wall-clock and communication complexity in experiments.  
- Experimental suggestions: test degree-heterogeneous (scale-free) graphs, non-uniform edge sampling, larger graphs, and additional U-statistics (e.g., Kendall tau).  
- Privacy concern: swapping raw data may be problematic; discuss sketching or privacy-preserving variants.  
- Open question: can the phantom-node technique extend to higher-order U-statistics (m>2) and what are the rates/overheads?  
- Open question: would mini-batch swapping improve constants without much memory growth?  
- Open question: how sensitive is asynchronous performance to inaccurate pk or partial knowledge of |E|?  
- Open question: can non-asymptotic graph-dependent constants for c′(G) be derived?  
- Pros for acceptance: original problem, simple practical algorithms, strong spectral-gap-dependent theory, clear empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: no high-probability guarantees, conservative asynchronous bound, limited exploration of heterogeneous/dynamic networks, no communication/wall-clock reporting, bipartite/directed graphs not fully handled.  
- Overall assessment: solid and timely contribution with theoretical and practical impact; leans toward acceptance with suggested clarifications and additional empirical characterization.
