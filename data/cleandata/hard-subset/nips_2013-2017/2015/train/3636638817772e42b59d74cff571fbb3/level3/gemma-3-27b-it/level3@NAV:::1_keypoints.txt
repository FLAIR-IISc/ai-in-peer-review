- Addresses decentralized estimation of U-statistics, an underexplored problem compared to average computation.  
- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for U-statistic estimation.  
- Algorithms improve on U2-Gossip in convergence rate, memory footprint, and communication cost.  
- Core design propagates raw data while maintaining local U-statistic estimates.  
- Introduces “phantom nodes” technique for rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Proves convergence rates: O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous).  
- Empirical evaluation on AUC and within-cluster scatter datasets shows consistent superiority to U2-Gossip across topologies.  
- Demonstrates benefits of an averaging step and reduced communication overhead.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with detailed experimental setup and results.  
- Correctly connects spectral graph theory to convergence behavior.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest directions for future work.  
- Main weakness: analysis and experiments focus only on degree-two U-statistics.  
- Asynchronous algorithm’s experimental validation is somewhat limited.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—significant theoretical and practical contribution likely to impact the community.
