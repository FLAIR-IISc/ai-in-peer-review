- Tackles computing U-statistics in decentralized networks using gossip; harder than simple averaging due to pairwise dependencies.  
- Proposes two randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) combining data propagation (swapping auxiliary observations) with estimate averaging.  
- Directly extends and aims to improve upon the prior U2-gossip method.  
- Significance: addresses practically relevant U-statistics (e.g., AUC, sample variance) and offers a novel contribution to decentralized computation.  
- Theoretical quality: provides rigorous convergence analysis with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Uses an elegant "phantom network" technique for the theoretical analysis.  
- Clarity: paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and algorithms/intuitions are effectively explained.  
- Experiments: empirical results show GoSta outperforms U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter across datasets and network topologies.  
- Main weakness: evaluation omits per-iteration wall-clock time, reporting only iteration-based convergence.  
- Concern: abstract claims lower communication/memory, but Algorithm 1’s local update loops over all n nodes, which may increase per-iteration cost.  
- Request: authors should provide per-iteration run-time comparisons and plots of error versus wall-clock time to confirm practical performance gains.
