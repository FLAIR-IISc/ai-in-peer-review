- Presents new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.  
- Achieves convergence rates of O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Important and timely extension of gossip algorithms beyond simple averages.  
- Core algorithmic idea: maintain and swap auxiliary observations to enable coupled random walks of data.  
- Convergence analysis appears rigorous, with theoretical guarantees for both settings.  
- Algorithms and problem motivation are clearly described.  
- Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster point scatter shows practical effectiveness.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete; needs broader coverage of gossip and distributed computation literature (e.g., Dimakis et al.).  
- Ambiguity in edge selection: unclear whether contact is uniformly random among a node’s neighbors when it awakens.  
- Lacks trade-off analysis versus a naive baseline that stores/forwards all observations (memory/communication comparison and relative convergence).  
- Missed potential connections to lifting schemes and lifted Markov chains (e.g., LADA) for accelerated mixing.  
- Theorems should state assumptions on initial data distribution and give error guarantees explicitly as functions of initial data.  
- Minor error: phrase “a bipartite directed-graph” is incorrect—bipartite graphs are undirected.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a relevant problem, offers novel practical algorithms, rigorous theory, and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete related work, unclear edge-selection description, and lack of baseline comparison limit evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution; strengths outweigh concerns; reviewer raised score from 7 to 8.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing related work, edge-selection clarity, baseline comparisons, and exploration of lifting-scheme connections.
