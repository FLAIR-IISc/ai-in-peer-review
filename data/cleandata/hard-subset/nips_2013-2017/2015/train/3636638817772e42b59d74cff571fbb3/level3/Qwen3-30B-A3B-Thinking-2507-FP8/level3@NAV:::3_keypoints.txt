- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta) for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.  
- GoSta simultaneously propagates data across the network and maintains local U-statistic estimates.  
- Provides convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous settings.  
- Extends prior gossip literature to handle pairwise data dependencies, improving on U2-gossip’s higher communication and memory costs.  
- Strong theoretical contributions with explicit convergence bounds that incorporate data- and network-dependent terms.  
- Empirical results (AUC and within-cluster scatter) on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz topologies show lower error and reduced variance versus U2-gossip.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with clear algorithms and proofs.  
- Major weakness: lacks per-iteration runtime comparisons between GoSta and U2-gossip, which could affect practical utility due to GoSta’s two-step update.  
- Overall quality: high; originality: strong; significance: high given U-statistics’ role in AUC and clustering evaluation.  
- Overall verdict: technically sound and well-executed; acceptance likely if runtime concerns are addressed.
