Paper proposes GoSta, a family of synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in decentralized networks.  
Key mechanism: each node keeps a local U-statistic estimate plus an auxiliary observation, averaging estimates and swapping auxiliaries to propagate pairwise information.  
Extends canonical gossip averaging to handle pairwise statistics (e.g., AUC, sample variance).  
Significance: tackles a well-motivated, important problem for large-scale ML, sensor networks, and P2P systems.  
Quality: technically rigorous with proofs linking convergence to network topology; synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t) rates.  
Clarity: paper is well-written and the algorithm is intuitive.  
Weakness: experimental evaluation is weak—comparison only to U2-gossip and missing simple baselines (e.g., random sampling or data flooding).  
Weakness: algorithmic components are incremental extensions of standard gossip primitives; main novelty lies in formal analysis.  
Recommendation: strengthen experiments by adding simple baselines and move/summarize supplementary results into the main paper to better demonstrate practical performance.
