- Problem: Extends gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics in decentralized networks where statistics depend on pairs of observations.  
- Proposal: Introduces GoSta algorithms (synchronous and asynchronous) for decentralized U-statistic estimation.  
- Theory: Provides convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit bounds.  
- Method intuition: Uses "phantom nodes" to enable simultaneous data propagation and local pairwise estimation.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on gossip mean-estimation algorithms [2,10,15] and prior distributed U-statistics work [17].  
- Strengths — formulation: Clear problem statement and comprehensive theoretical analysis.  
- Strengths — relevance: Addresses practically important statistics (e.g., AUC, within-cluster scatter) in distributed settings.  
- Empirics: Shows faster convergence and lower variance than U2-gossip [17] on real datasets.  
- Major weakness: Lacks comparisons to simple or randomized baselines (naive distributed, other gossip variants, or centralized implementations).  
- Novelty concern: Unclear distinction and advantages relative to prior distributed U-statistics methods [17].  
- Recommendation: Reject due to insufficient experimental baselines; suggests adding intuitive baselines and more detailed experimental analysis.
