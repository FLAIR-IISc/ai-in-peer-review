- Proposes two novel gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) for distributed estimation of U-statistics, extending gossip methods beyond separable statistics.
- Addresses an important problem since U-statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, cluster scatter) require pairwise averaging and are not handled efficiently by standard gossip.
- Provides rigorous convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.
- Uses a clever "phantom nodes" reformulation to separate data propagation from averaging in the theoretical analysis.
- Convergence proofs are technically sound and link performance to network spectral gap and data dispersion.
- Empirical evaluation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, 2D grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs shows GoSta consistently outperforms U2-gossip in speed and variance reduction.
- Performance gap increases with network size, demonstrating scalability advantages of GoSta.
- Claims significant improvement over U2-gossip in memory and communication costs per iteration.
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition, good related-work positioning, and distinct contribution relative to prior gossip/U-statistics work.
- Main practical concern: paper lacks per-iteration run-time measurements and detailed computational complexity analysis for GoSta versus U2-gossip.
- Unclear whether additional computations (e.g., maintaining auxiliary observations) could make GoSta iterations slower in practice despite better asymptotic rates.
- Needs more discussion of implementation considerations and wider baseline comparisons beyond U2-gossip.
- Overall assessment: technically strong, significant contribution with strong experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address computational-efficiency and implementation details.
