- Paper addresses decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over networks using gossip, targeting non-separable pairwise functionals (AUC, variance, Gini, etc.).  
- Proposes two algorithms: GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous) combining random pairwise swaps for data propagation and averaging of local estimates.  
- Introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple data propagation and averaging for convergence analysis.  
- GoSta-sync: proven convergence in expectation at O(1/t) with constants separating data dispersion and network spectral gap (1 − λ2(2)).  
- GoSta-async: proven convergence at O((log t)/t).  
- Refines prior U2-gossip rate and claims GoSta has faster convergence (avoids √n penalty) and lower per-iteration memory/communication.  
- Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs show GoSta-sync yields lower mean relative error and cross-node variance than U2-gossip, with larger gaps on bigger graphs.  
- Situates work within gossip/consensus and distributed optimization literature and fills a gap in decentralized U-statistics estimation beyond U2-gossip.  
- Strength: clear motivation that many ML statistics are pairwise and non-separable.  
- Strength: novel synchronous and asynchronous algorithms with provable rates and explicit dependence on spectral gap and data dispersion.  
- Strength: elegant phantom-node analysis separating propagation and averaging.  
- Strength: stronger theoretical rates and lower per-iteration state/communication versus U2-gossip; refined comparator bounds.  
- Strength: experiments across multiple topologies matching theoretical scaling; clear writing and organization.  
- Weakness: practical efficiency not fully characterized—per-iteration wall-clock time and communication volume are not reported.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope (only two datasets, moderate dimensionality); lacks large-d experiments and explicit byte/message counts.  
- Weakness: theoretical bounds are only in expectation; no high-probability or finite-time concentration/variance bounds.  
- Weakness: GoSta-async requires knowledge of node degrees and |E| to compute pk, which may be unknown or dynamic; robustness not analyzed.  
- Weakness: assumes uniform random edge activation; lacks analysis for non-uniform or directed/asymmetric communication.  
- Weakness: bipartite graphs excluded; a lazy-walk variant could be a simple fix but is not discussed.  
- Quality: technical development is sound; phantom-node device is clever; proofs omitted from main text but results coherent.  
- Clarity: paper is well written and algorithms easy to implement; suggests adding a small table summarizing per-iteration compute/memory/communication and clarifying |E| requirement and bipartite/directed handling.  
- Originality: high—extends gossip to non-separable U-statistics with original analysis and sharper rates.  
- Significance: high—enables decentralized estimation of ubiquitous U-statistics with actionable spectral-gap dependence; useful building block.  
- Requests: report per-iteration wall-clock time and communication and total time/messages to reach target error; compare memory footprint as function of dimension d and test large-d.  
- Requests: provide high-probability or variance bounds, analyze sensitivity of GoSta-async to errors in pk, propose variants avoiding global |E|, and extend analysis to non-uniform/directed graphs and bipartite cases.  
- Pros: strong theoretical guarantees, asynchronous variant, consistent empirical gains, and lower per-iteration memory/communication.  
- Cons: missing system-level benchmarks, limited empirical scope, no high-probability bounds, assumptions on known |E| and non-bipartite graphs.  
- Recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on addressing runtime/communication measurements and robustness clarifications.  
- Reviewer confidence: high.
