- Proposes synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.  
- Targets the less-explored problem of computing U-statistics (pairwise averaging) in networks, a novel application of gossip methods.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis with explicit data- and network-dependent convergence terms.  
- GoSta-sync achieves O(1/t) convergence assuming a global clock; GoSta-async achieves O(log t / t) without a global clock.  
- Empirical evaluation on real datasets compares algorithms across complete graphs, 2D grids, and Watts–Strogatz networks.  
- GoSta-sync outperforms the prior U2-gossip method; GoSta-async shows comparable practical performance despite a slightly worse theoretical rate.  
- Strengths: solid theoretical grounding, novel contribution, thorough experiments, well-referenced and reproducible.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparisons to baseline methods and sparse discussion of practical deployment implications.  
- Some algorithm descriptions and theoretical sections may be difficult for non-expert readers.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are well-supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Originality is high; approach is distinct from previous gossip literature.  
- Significance is high for decentralized estimation, with demonstrated practical relevance.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution whose strengths outweigh its weaknesses.
