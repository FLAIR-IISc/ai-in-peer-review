- Proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in decentralized settings.
- Provides a thorough introduction, motivating the problem and explaining limitations of existing gossip algorithms.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed algorithms.
- Technical content is sound with a detailed convergence-rate analysis.
- Experimental results on two real-world datasets show the proposed methods outperform the U2-gossip baseline across various graph scenarios.
- Strength: novel algorithms that extend gossip methods to U-statistics.
- Strength: rigorous convergence analysis with established bounds.
- Strength: empirical validation demonstrating improved performance versus U2-gossip.
- Weakness: no reported per-iteration run-times for the proposed method or U2-gossip, hindering practical assessment.
- Weakness: limited comparisons—only compared to U2-gossip rather than centralized or other decentralized methods.
- Weakness: assumes connected, non-bipartite graphs and does not evaluate robustness to network failures or topology changes.
- Conclusion: significant and original contribution to decentralized estimation, but needs more on computational complexity and broader comparisons to strengthen practical relevance.
