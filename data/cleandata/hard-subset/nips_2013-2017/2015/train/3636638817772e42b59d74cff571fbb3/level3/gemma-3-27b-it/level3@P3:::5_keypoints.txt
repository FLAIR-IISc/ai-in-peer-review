- Introduces novel synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) gossip algorithms for estimating U-statistics in networks.  
- Derives theoretical convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms.  
- Empirically outperforms prior U2-gossip method and demonstrates practical gains on tasks like AUC and within-cluster scatter.  
- Well-organized and clearly presented with concise algorithm descriptions (Algorithm 1 and 2) and clear notation.  
- Builds on prior gossip and spectral graph theory work, addressing the pairwise averaging challenge specific to U-statistics.  
- Uses a clever "phantom nodes" construction to simplify analysis and proofs.  
- Theoretical derivations appear rigorous, but full verification depends on supplementary material.  
- Discussion of limitations is brief; more analysis needed for scenarios like highly heterogeneous networks.  
- Original contribution: extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics, plus the novel phantom-node analytic technique and both sync/async variants.  
- Significant impact potential because U-statistics are fundamental in ML (e.g., AUC) and distributed computation is increasingly important.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, clear presentation, strong theoretical guarantees, and empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited limitations discussion, reliance on supplementary proofs, and need for broader comparisons to other distributed estimation methods.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand limitations discussion and ensure supplementary proofs are reviewed.
