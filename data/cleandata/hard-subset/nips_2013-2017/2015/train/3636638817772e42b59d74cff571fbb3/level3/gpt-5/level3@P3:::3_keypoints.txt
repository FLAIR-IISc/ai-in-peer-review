- Paper addresses decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip, proposing synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) algorithms.
- Key technique: “phantom nodes” reformulation decoupling data propagation from estimate averaging to enable convergence analysis.
- Each iteration mixes auxiliary-data propagation (random edge swaps) and estimate averaging so nodes approximate hi = (1/n)∑j H(Xi,Xj) and consensus on Ûn(H).
- Theoretical convergence: GoSta-sync achieves O(1/t) with constants depending on data dispersion and graph spectral gap; GoSta-async achieves O((log t)/t).
- Compared to U2-gossip, the proposed methods use fewer auxiliary variables, less communication per iteration, remove a √n factor in bounds, and leverage averaging for better rates.
- Empirical evaluation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, 2D grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs shows consistent gains in average relative error and lower cross-node variance vs. U2-gossip.
- Strength: well-motivated, clearly written, with accessible phantom-node analysis and solid unbiasedness plus explicit-rate theory.
- Strength: both synchronous and fully asynchronous variants are analyzed and memory/communication advantages over U2-gossip are articulated.
- Weakness: missing per-iteration wall-clock/runtime comparisons; practical utility depends on per-iteration costs relative to U2-gossip.
- Reviewer requests detailed runtime/communication breakdowns: wall-clock per iteration, H evaluations per global iteration, bytes communicated per iteration/node, nodes activated, and message sizes.
- Requested comparisons: convergence vs. wall-clock, vs. total H evaluations, and vs. total bytes sent (cost-normalized curves).
- Experimental scope is modest — only two tasks/datasets and three graph types; larger d, non-Euclidean H, and more realistic sparse/scale-free graphs are suggested.
- Asynchronous bound O((log t)/t) appears loose compared to empirical behavior; high-probability bounds and finite-time constants are requested.
- Practical assumption concern: nodes knowing |E| to set pk may be unrealistic; clarify how |E| is obtained or provide a local-estimation variant.
- Robustness to link failures, delays, and time-varying graphs is not explored and should be addressed.
- Quality: technical development appears sound with informative comparative bounds against U2-gossip, but lacks timing/complexity to connect theory to runtime.
- Clarity: well-written; suggested additions include a concise table contrasting per-iteration compute/memory/communication for GoSta-sync, GoSta-async, and U2-gossip.
- Clarity: state explicit per-iteration global cost (H evaluations and edges communicated) and clarify label propagation for AUC in asynchronous swaps.
- Originality: novel formulation and analysis for U-statistics in gossip settings; phantom-node and asynchronous constructions are original.
- Significance: enabling decentralized U-statistics estimation with provable rates and good empirical performance is valuable and broadly applicable.
- Specific author suggestions: add per-iteration and per-activation runtime scaling with d and n; report cost-normalized convergence; include heavier H (Kendall tau, RBF MMD); explore non-uniform sampling/accelerated gossip; discuss unknown |E| handling.
- Pros for acceptance: clear problem relevance, strong theory with interpretable constants, both sync/async algorithms, empirical superiority, and reduced memory/communication.
- Cons against acceptance: no per-iteration runtime/communication comparisons, limited experimental breadth, and loose asynchronous bound with missing robustness results.
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept, conditional on adding per-iteration runtime and cost-normalized analyses to strengthen practical claims.
