- Paper tackles decentralized estimation of U-statistics (e.g., AUC, variance), a less-studied problem than distributed means.
- Proposes two randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for decentralized U-statistic computation.
- Algorithms propagate data and maintain local estimates, achieving convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).
- Introduces "phantom nodes" in analysis, a novel tool for proving convergence.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with explicit bounds depending on data and network properties.
- Demonstrates empirical superiority over U2-gossip in convergence speed and resource efficiency on real datasets.
- Addresses practical constraints like limited memory, communication, and lack of global synchronization.
- Paper is well-organized and clear, covering both synchronous and asynchronous settings.
- Original contribution: extends decentralized computation literature to computationally harder U-statistics.
- Experimental scope is limited to two applications (AUC and within-cluster scatter) and a few network types.
- Theoretical analysis of GoSta-async is less detailed; constants like c'(G) are not explicitly characterized.
- Comparisons are primarily to U2-gossip; broader baselines (e.g., other decentralized optimization methods) are missing.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, original contribution with solid theory and empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: need for broader experiments and more comprehensive asynchronous analysis.
- Recommendation: overall strong submission with minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
