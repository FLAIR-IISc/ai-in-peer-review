- Problem: decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a network using gossip, which standard averaging gossip cannot handle directly.
- Algorithms: two methods proposed — GoSta-sync and GoSta-async — that interleave pairwise auxiliary-observation swaps (random walks) with local averaging.
- Analysis tool: introduces a “phantom nodes” lifted-product-graph construction to decouple data propagation from averaging in the analysis.
- Convergence rates: proves expectation-only convergence at O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous, with constants depending on data dispersion and network spectral gap.
- Comparison to prior work: derives a refined bound for U2-gossip showing slower convergence (√n factor), arguing GoSta is faster in relevant regimes.
- Synchronous analysis: neat and technically sound, appropriately uses spectral-gap-based decomposition and data-dependent error terms.
- Asynchronous analysis: plausible but weaker; relies on correction mk to ensure unbiased steps, hides constants c′(G), and includes an extra log factor that may be proof-artifact.
- Limitations in guarantees: only expectation guarantees provided; no high-probability or mean-square error bounds and no formal variance analysis across nodes.
- Graph assumptions: non-bipartite graph assumption not fully addressed; suggestions to handle bipartite cases via lazy walks or Metropolis weights are missing.
- Practical requirement: asynchronous algorithm needs knowledge of |E| to set edge selection probabilities pk, which is often unrealistic.
- Clarity suggestions: clarify decentralized computation of AUC denominator, explicitly state boundedness assumptions on H for Theorem 2, and make edge-selection model/local knowledge precise.
- Robustness/extension requests: discuss directed/time-varying graphs and potential extensions to degree >2 U-statistics with multiple auxiliary tokens.
- Empirical results: experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across three topologies show improved performance over U2-gossip and sensible scaling trends.
- Empirical weaknesses: limited dataset/network sizes, no communication or wall-clock comparisons (sync vs async), no robustness tests (noise, quantization, packet loss).
- Baselines: recommended to compare with separable-estimate-then-average baselines or sampling-based approximations to strengthen empirical claims.
- Originality: method is simple and novel relative to prior U-statistics gossip, with an elegant phantom-node analysis related to lifting ideas.
- Significance: practical, lightweight gossip for common pairwise metrics (variance, AUC, Gini, etc.) with topology-dependent guarantees; useful building block for decentralized pairwise losses.
- Pros for acceptance: simple implementable algorithms, solid theoretical guarantees, insightful analysis, and supportive empirical results.
- Cons for acceptance: expectation-only bounds, async requires global |E| with opaque constants and log factor, non-bipartite assumption not handled, and limited experimental scope.
- Questions for authors: provide mean-square/high-probability bounds, remove |E| requirement or propose alternatives (push-sum/local counters), give explicit c′(G) expression, and clarify async log factor necessity.
