- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics.  
- Claims faster convergence and lower memory/communication costs compared to prior U2-gossip.  
- Builds on existing gossip averaging literature and extends it to pairwise U-statistics.  
- Each iteration combines data propagation and averaging so nodes keep local U-statistic estimates.  
- Provides convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous.  
- Detailed theoretical comparison showing advantages over U2-gossip in rate and resource use.  
- Empirical validation on real datasets demonstrating practical performance gains.  
- Assumes a global clock or synchronization, which may be unrealistic in many networks.  
- GoSta-async addresses asynchrony but has slightly worse convergence than GoSta-sync.  
- Uses a "phantom network" construction for analysis without clear justification or intuition.  
- Lacks deeper discussion of trade-offs between convergence rate, memory, and communication.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novel decentralized U-statistics algorithms, solid analysis, and experiments.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: synchronization assumption, slower async rate, and insufficient discussion of implications.
