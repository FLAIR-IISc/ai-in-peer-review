- Problem: computing degree-two U-statistics in decentralized networks where standard gossip (for separable sums) does not apply.  
- Method: two GoSta algorithms (synchronous and asynchronous) combining data-swapping random walks with standard gossip averaging.  
- Novel analysis: introduction of a "phantom network" to model coupled data-propagation and averaging as a single linear system.  
- Theoretical rates: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t), with dependence on data variance and network spectral gap.  
- Improvement over prior work: more efficient memory/communication and the first fully asynchronous gossip method for U-statistics.  
- Re-analysis of U2-gossip: reveals a harmful sqrt(n) factor in its convergence due to lack of averaging.  
- Experiments: thorough empirical validation on AUC and cluster scatter across multiple topologies showing GoSta outperforms U2-gossip; gap grows with network size.  
- Presentation: paper is very well-written, clear pseudocode, and helpful figures (phantom network explained).  
- Strength (significance): extends gossip algorithms to a broad class of practical U-statistics with an elegant approach.  
- Strength (technical): rigorous, insightful theoretical analysis that may be of independent interest.  
- Strength (practical): synchronous and asynchronous versions show comparable empirical performance.  
- Weakness: asynchronous algorithm requires knowledge of pk = 2dk/|E| (needs global |E|), weakening decentralization—should be stated and discussed.  
- Weakness: proof gives extra log t factor for async rate despite similar experiments; authors should clarify whether this is an artifact of analysis.  
- Weakness: handling multiple observations per node is only cursorily described (node-splitting); needs detailed treatment and impact on spectral gap.  
- Weakness: analysis implicitly assumes bounded kernel H (||H|| appears); this assumption should be made explicit and implications discussed.  
- Overall: excellent, significant contribution with minor clarifications suggested; strong candidate for acceptance.
