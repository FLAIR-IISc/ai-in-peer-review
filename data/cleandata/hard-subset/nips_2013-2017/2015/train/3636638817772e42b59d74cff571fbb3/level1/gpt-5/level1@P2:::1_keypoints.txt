- Paper studies decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip, proposing GoSta-sync and GoSta-async protocols.
- GoSta-sync: global synchronous iterations with running averages, one random edge averaging and swapping auxiliary observations (coupled random walks).
- GoSta-async: asynchronous local-clock version with per-node counters scaled by pk = 2d_k/|E| to ensure unbiasedness without global iteration counters.
- Theoretical guarantees: E[Z_k(t)] → Û_n(H) with explicit rates O(1/t) (sync) and O((log t)/t) (async), depending on data dispersion and network spectral gap.
- Analysis uses an original “phantom nodes” construction to decouple data propagation from estimate averaging.
- Related work: builds on randomized gossip literature and improves over prior U2-gossip (single auxiliary observation, one edge swap, lower communication/memory and better n-dependence).
- Empirics on AUC and within-cluster scatter show GoSta outperforms U2-gossip across multiple graph topologies, aligning with theory.
- Technical analysis appears sound; spectral-gap dependence and derived rates are plausible.
- Paper assumes undirected, connected, non-bipartite graphs and uniform random edge selection; these assumptions should be highlighted.
- Boundedness or moment assumptions on H and the exact matrix norm used in bounds are not explicitly stated and should be clarified.
- Asynchronous correctness requires knowledge of |E| (pk); feasibility of disseminating or estimating |E| is not discussed.
- Experiments are clear but limited: no stress tests on very sparse/large-diameter graphs, limited high-dimensional/communication-cost exploration, and few tasks.
- Clarity is generally good; algorithm descriptions and phantom-node idea are well explained.
- Suggested clarity improvements: define matrix norm and H assumptions, make |E|/pk requirement explicit and discuss estimation, clarify decentralized realization of uniform edge draws, and add a complexity comparison table vs U2-gossip.
- Originality: novel extension to U-statistics with single auxiliary observation and the phantom-node analysis technique.
- Significance: moderate-to-high — useful for sensor/peer-to-peer/federated settings and provides interpretable graph/data dependence for practitioners.
- Pros: addresses underexplored problem, simple practical algorithms, clear theoretical guarantees, reusable analysis technique, empirical validation, both sync/async variants.
- Cons: requires |E| and uniform edge activation, missing explicit H boundedness conditions, narrow experimental scope, privacy concerns when swapping raw observations, no robustness to directed/time-varying or bipartite graphs.
- Questions for authors: can pk (or |E|) be estimated online; do results extend to lazy updates for bipartite graphs; what precise assumptions on H are needed; can you report communication vs error in high-dimensional/multi-observation settings; can raw-observation exchanges be replaced by sketches for some H?
- Overall assessment: strong paper with solid theory, simple algorithms, and empirical support; recommend acceptance contingent on clarifying H assumptions, practical edge selection/|E| issues, and limitations/applicability.
