- Tackles decentralized estimation of U-statistics, a less-explored problem than distributed mean estimation.  
- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for U-statistic estimation.  
- Provides convergence rate bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms.  
- Analysis explicitly incorporates data and network characteristics via spectral gap considerations.  
- Introduces a “phantom nodes” technique to reformulate the problem and facilitate analysis.  
- Builds on prior gossip work focused on separable functions, addressing the challenge of averaging over pairs of observations.  
- Demonstrates empirical superiority over U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks with real datasets.  
- Shows reduced memory and communication costs compared to U2-gossip.  
- Evaluates performance across multiple network topologies (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz).  
- Theoretical contributions and practical validation are major strengths supporting novelty and significance.  
- Connection between the phantom node model and actual implementation needs clearer, more explicit explanation.  
- Asynchronous algorithm analysis (O(log t / t)) is brief and should better address local clock assumptions and implications.  
- Comparisons are limited to U2-gossip; broader comparisons to other distributed estimation techniques would strengthen the paper.  
- Notation (especially Section 2) is dense and could be made more accessible; overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
