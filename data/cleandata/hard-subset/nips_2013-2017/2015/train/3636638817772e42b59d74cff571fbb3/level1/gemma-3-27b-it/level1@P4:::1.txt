This paper introduces new gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics, extending existing work primarily focused on sample mean estimation. The core idea is to combine propagation of local observations with averaging of local estimates, and to leverage a “phantom node” reformulation for convergence analysis. The authors present both synchronous and asynchronous algorithms with theoretical convergence guarantees (O(1/t) and O(log t/t) respectively) and demonstrate empirical superiority over a prior approach (U2-gossip) on AUC and within-cluster point scatter estimation.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The convergence analysis appears rigorous, though detailed examination requires delving into the supplementary material. The theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments. The authors acknowledge the limitations of the phantom node reformulation, noting it’s an analytical tool and doesn’t impact the algorithmic implementation. The comparison with U2-gossip is valuable, highlighting the benefits of the averaging step in the proposed algorithms.  However, the paper doesn't fully explore the trade-offs between the synchronous and asynchronous algorithms beyond stating the theoretical rates. The connection between the spectral gap and convergence speed is clearly stated and intuitively plausible. The choice of datasets for experiments is reasonable, but the limited number of datasets and specific parameter settings (e.g., for the Watts-Strogatz network) could be considered weaknesses.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, though dense in places. The notation is well-defined, though a glossary or quick reference would be helpful given the numerous mathematical symbols. The motivation for U-statistics is clearly laid out. Algorithm 1 and 2 are presented clearly, but a high-level intuitive explanation of *why* these steps work would improve understanding. The discussion of the phantom nodes is somewhat abstract and could benefit from a more concrete example illustrating its role in the analysis.  The flow between sections is logical, but some sections, particularly the beginning of Section 4, jump quickly into technical details without sufficient context.

**Originality:** The problem of distributed U-statistic estimation is less explored than distributed mean estimation, providing a good starting point for novelty. The proposed algorithms and the phantom node reformulation represent a novel approach to addressing this problem.  The authors appropriately cite related work, including gossip algorithms for mean estimation and the prior work on U-statistics (U2-gossip). While the algorithms build upon existing gossip frameworks, the adaptation to U-statistics and the associated analysis represent a significant contribution. The comparison to U2-gossip is a positive aspect, clearly differentiating the proposed work.

**Significance:**  U-statistics are important data functionals with applications in diverse areas (AUC, variance, Gini mean difference, etc.). Efficient distributed estimation of these statistics is relevant to a wide range of applications in networked systems and machine learning. The proposed algorithms offer improved convergence rates compared to the existing U2-gossip approach. The practical evaluation on real datasets demonstrates the potential benefits of the algorithms.  The theoretical results, linking convergence rates to network properties (spectral gap), provide valuable insights. While the specific performance gains might be modest in some cases, the work opens up possibilities for further research in decentralized machine learning and data analysis.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a less-explored, yet important, problem in distributed estimation.
*   Novel algorithms with theoretical convergence guarantees.
*   Empirically demonstrates superior performance compared to existing approaches.
*   Clearly written and well-organized (despite some density).
*   Strong technical foundation and rigorous analysis.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The phantom node reformulation, while effective for analysis, is abstract and potentially confusing.
*   Limited exploration of the trade-offs between synchronous and asynchronous algorithms.
*   Experiments could be expanded with more datasets and parameter settings.
*   The practical gains over U2-gossip aren’t overwhelmingly large in the presented experiments.




Overall, this is a solid and well-executed paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of distributed estimation. I recommend acceptance.
