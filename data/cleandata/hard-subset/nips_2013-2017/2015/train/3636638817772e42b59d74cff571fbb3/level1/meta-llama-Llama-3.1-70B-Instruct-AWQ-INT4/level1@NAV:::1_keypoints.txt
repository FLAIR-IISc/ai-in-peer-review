- Summary: Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized U-statistics with O(1/t) and O(log t/t) convergence and reduced memory/communication costs.

- Strength: Novel algorithms: Introduces new decentralized algorithms for U-statistics.

- Strength: Improved convergence rates: Achieves faster theoretical convergence than prior work.

- Strength: Lower resource costs: Requires less memory and communication than previous methods.

- Strength: Theoretical analysis: Provides thorough convergence proofs and error bounds.

- Weakness: Limited experiments: Evaluation is limited to only two datasets.

- Weakness: Narrow comparisons: Lacks comparisons to other applicable decentralized algorithms.

- Weakness: Robustness not addressed: No discussion of performance under failures or errors.

- Originality/Significance: Makes a significant, novel contribution with improved rates and solid theory.

- Clarity/Organization: Well-written, clear presentation and organized theoretical and experimental sections.

- Argument for acceptance: Significant contribution to decentralized U-statistics, novel methods, and strong theory.

- Argument against acceptance: Insufficient experimental breadth, missing baseline comparisons, and no robustness analysis.

- Overall recommendation: Positive—should be accepted, with a request for expanded experiments and comparisons in future work.
