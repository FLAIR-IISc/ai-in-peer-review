This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors demonstrate that their algorithms achieve faster convergence rates and lower memory and communication costs compared to the existing approach, U2-gossip.

The main ideas of the paper are related to gossip algorithms, which have been extensively studied in the context of decentralized averaging in networks. The authors build upon the work of [2] and extend it to the computation of U-statistics, which rely on averaging over pairs of observations. The proposed algorithms combine two steps at each iteration: data propagation and averaging, allowing each node to maintain a local estimate of the U-statistic.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms, establishing bounds of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) for the synchronous and asynchronous cases, respectively.
* The paper includes a detailed comparison with the existing approach, U2-gossip, highlighting the advantages of the proposed algorithms in terms of convergence rate, memory, and communication costs.
* The authors provide numerical experiments on real datasets, demonstrating the practical interest of their algorithms.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The paper assumes that nodes have access to a global clock or can be synchronized, which might not be realistic in all scenarios. The asynchronous algorithm, GoSta-async, is a step in the right direction, but its convergence rate is slightly worse than that of GoSta-sync.
* The authors could provide more insights into the choice of the "phantom network" construction, which is used solely for the convergence analysis. While it is mentioned that this construction is not used in the actual algorithm, it is not entirely clear why this approach is necessary.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results, particularly in terms of the trade-offs between convergence rate, memory, and communication costs.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes novel algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting, which is an important problem in many distributed systems.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms, establishing bounds that are better than those of the existing approach.
* The paper includes numerical experiments on real datasets, demonstrating the practical interest of the proposed algorithms.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The asynchronous algorithm, GoSta-async, has a slightly worse convergence rate than GoSta-sync.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the implications of the results.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their algorithms. While there are some areas for improvement, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of decentralized computation and U-statistics.
