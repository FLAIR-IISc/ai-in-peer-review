- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics, extending gossip methods to pairwise-dependent quantities.
- Fills a gap since existing gossip algorithms cannot efficiently compute U-statistics needed for metrics like AUC and Gini mean difference.
- Introduces "GoSta" algorithms that combine data propagation with local averaging.
- Uses a novel "phantom nodes" reformulation to enable convergence analysis.
- Provides rigorous convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms.
- Convergence rates include data- and network-dependent terms reflecting problem hardness and connectivity.
- The phantom-node-based proof technique is elegant and well-executed.
- Empirical results on SMVguide3 and Wine Quality show clear superiority over U2-gossip in speed and variance reduction.
- The performance advantage increases as network size grows.
- Paper is clear and well-organized with precise algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1–2) and appropriate notation.
- Significant practical impact: reduces memory and communication costs and improves convergence for widely used U-statistics.
- Strengths: novel extension, strong theory, compelling experiments, clear writing.
- Weaknesses: phantom node concept needs more intuitive explanation; limited exploration of behavior in extremely large or sparse networks.
- Highly original relative to recent NIPS gossip papers and meets criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
- Recommendation: valuable contribution that deserves acceptance.
