- Introduces GoSta-sync and GoSta-async: randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-two U-statistics.
- Core mechanism: pairwise averaging plus swapping an auxiliary observation, creating random walks of data so nodes eventually form all pairs.
- Analysis uses a "phantom network" construct to decouple data propagation and averaging, yielding explicit convergence rates.
- Proven convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous, O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with constants depending on network spectral gap and data variance.
- Demonstrates theoretical and practical improvements over prior U2-gossip (avoids detrimental √n factor; lower memory/communication; asynchronous variant).
- Empirical validation on real datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) across multiple network topologies supports theoretical claims.
- Technical quality is high: simple algorithms, rigorous analysis, and a strong re-analysis of competing work (refined U2-gossip rate).
- Clarity is excellent: well-written, organized, intuitive exposition (phantom network figure helpful), and reproducible experimental details.
- Originality is strong: novel combination of data-swapping random walks with averaging and an original analytical technique.
- Significance is high: addresses a practical primitive for decentralized ML, with an important asynchronous variant for real-world systems.
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, elegant solution, strong theoretical contribution, practical relevance, and convincing experiments.
- Suggestions/weaknesses: explain whether async log t factor is an analysis artifact or negligible in practice; expand details for nodes with multiple observations; add a high-level proof sketch for Theorem 1.
