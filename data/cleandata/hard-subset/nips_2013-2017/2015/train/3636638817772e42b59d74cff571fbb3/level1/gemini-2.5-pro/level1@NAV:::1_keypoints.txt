- Addresses distributed estimation of degree-two U-statistics (e.g., AUC, sample variance) over networks using gossip algorithms.  
- Proposes GoSta, a novel randomized gossip algorithm with synchronous and asynchronous variants.  
- Core idea: combine random-walk data propagation (nodes swap auxiliary observations) with averaging of local U-statistic estimates.  
- Builds upon and directly improves the prior U2-gossip algorithm (Pelckmans et al., 2010).  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis using a "phantom network" formulation.  
- Establishes convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous versions.  
- Analysis identifies dependence on the network spectral gap, linking performance to connectivity.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, uses real datasets and relevant U-statistics (AUC, within-cluster scatter), and shows GoSta empirically outperforms U2-gossip.  
- Claims improvements in memory, communication, and convergence performance, corroborated by theory and experiments.  
- Paper is very clearly written, well-organized, and reproducible.  
- Minor weakness: asynchronous variant requires nodes to know their degree and the total number of edges |E|, reducing applicability in fully decentralized settings and needing more explicit discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—original, technically sound, significant contribution with minor practical caveat.
