- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.
- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.
- Provides a thorough analysis and comparison with existing work (U2-gossip).
- Demonstrates superiority via numerical experiments on real datasets.
- Strength: introduces new algorithms for a relatively underexplored decentralized U-statistics problem.
- Strength: convergence bounds with explicit data/network terms give insight into performance.
- Strength: direct comparison with U2-gossip highlights advantages of the proposed approach.
- Strength: numerical experiments show practical interest and empirical validation.
- Weakness: assumes the network graph is connected and non-bipartite, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: synchronous algorithm requires a global clock, potentially infeasible in many settings.
- Weakness: lacks discussion of communication and memory costs, an important omission for decentralized methods.
- Pro acceptance: significant contribution, informative convergence analysis, and strong empirical results.
- Con acceptance: restrictive assumptions and missing resource-cost analysis limit practical applicability.
- Overall: well-written, clear, and a meaningful but somewhat incremental contribution that should address assumptions and cost analyses in future work.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
