- Provides thorough convergence analysis for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.
- Shows faster convergence than the existing U2-gossip algorithm in theory and experiments.
- Derives convergence rates with explicit dependence on network topology and data distribution.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains technical details and notation clearly.
- Includes experiments on real-world datasets across multiple network types (complete, 2D grid, Watts–Strogatz).
- Strength: detailed theoretical derivations that illuminate algorithm performance under different scenarios.
- Strength: empirical evaluation provides practical insights into algorithm behavior.
- Limitation: analysis assumes connected, non-bipartite networks, which may not cover all practical topologies.
