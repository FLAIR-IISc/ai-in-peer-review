- Problem: decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics (pairwise functionals) via gossip-style communication, which standard gossip cannot handle directly.
- Algorithms: GoSta-sync — each node keeps one auxiliary Y performing random-walk swaps and a local running average Z with pairwise averaging on selected edges.
- Algorithms: GoSta-async — similar but only selected nodes update Z using node-specific counter mk and debiasing by pk = 2dk/|E|.
- Analysis device: “phantom nodes” construction augments the state to decouple averaging and data propagation, enabling explicit expected convergence rates.
- Convergence (sync): E[Zk(t)] → Ûn(H) at O(1/t) with data-dependent dispersion terms and network dependence via spectral gap (λ2 of W2(G)).
- Convergence (async): E[Zk(t)] → Ûn(H) at O(log t / t); bound is weaker and hides graph-dependent constants.
- Comparison to prior work: Improves on U2-gossip by using a single auxiliary and averaging, reducing memory/communication and converging faster empirically and analytically.
- Experiments: AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs support theoretical claims and show variance reduction from averaging.
- Strengths: simple, low per-iteration cost; clear and elegant convergence analysis; refined rate for U2-gossip; experiments align with theory.
- Originality: novel extension of gossip to non-separable pairwise statistics and an insightful phantom-node analysis; async debiasing trick is practical.
- Significance: useful primitive for sensor/peer-to-peer and federated settings; spectral-gap dependence informs topology-aware design.
- Main weaknesses: results only in expectation (no mean-square/high-probability/AS bounds); async bound omits data-dependent terms and hides constants.
- Practical concerns: assumes uniform random edge selection and knowledge of |E| (for pk) which may be unrealistic in decentralized settings.
- Suggested fixes: discuss/implement local edge selection (node-initiated, Metropolis, Poisson clocks), alternative step-size rules avoiding |E|, and online estimation of pk.
- Scope and robustness: assume static, undirected, connected, non-bipartite graphs; discuss extensions to time-varying/directed graphs (push-sum) and bipartite handling.
- Experimental suggestions: include more baselines (e.g., random-walk token methods, mini-batch exchanges) and communication-to-accuracy/robustness comparisons.
- Modeling note: clarify handling of general H (non-zero diagonal, standard U-statistic normalization n/(n−1)).
- Minor comments: clarify decentralized implementation of uniform edge sampling, explicit handling of bipartite graphs (lazy updates), and report per-iteration message/memory costs.
- Acceptance case: important primitive, clean algorithms, solid interpretable theory, novel analysis, and supportive experiments — reviewer leans toward acceptance.
- Rejection case: practical deployment assumptions not fully addressed, async bound loose and lacking high-probability guarantees, and experiments could be broader.
