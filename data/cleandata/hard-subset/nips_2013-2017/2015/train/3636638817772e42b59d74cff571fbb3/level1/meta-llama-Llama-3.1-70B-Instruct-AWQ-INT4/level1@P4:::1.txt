**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, to efficiently compute U-statistics in a decentralized setting. U-statistics are a type of data functional that depend on pairs of observations and are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population. The algorithms are designed to operate on a network of nodes, where each node holds a single data observation, and aim to estimate the U-statistic of interest by propagating data across the network and maintaining local estimates.

The paper relates the proposed algorithms to previous work on gossip algorithms, which have been extensively studied in the context of decentralized averaging in networks. However, existing gossip algorithms cannot be used to efficiently compute U-statistics, and the paper highlights the limitations of previous approaches, such as U2-gossip.
