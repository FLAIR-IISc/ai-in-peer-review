- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed U‑statistic estimation, a less-explored but important problem.  
- Provides convergence rate bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms.  
- Proposes a "phantom node" reformulation enabling rigorous convergence analysis.  
- Theoretical analysis appears rigorous, with detailed proofs referenced in the supplementary material.  
- Empirical results on real datasets show clear improvements over prior work (U2-gossip) in speed and communication cost.  
- Authors acknowledge and analyze how convergence rates depend on data and network characteristics.  
- Phantom-node technique is clever but adds conceptual complexity to the presentation.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and algorithm descriptions are concise and clear.  
- High mathematical detail may challenge readers unfamiliar with spectral graph theory.  
- Could use a more intuitive explanation of the spectral gap’s role and practical implications of rate dependencies.  
- Original contribution: extending gossip algorithms to pairwise U‑statistics and introducing the phantom-node approach.  
- Significant practical impact for distributed statistical tasks (AUC, variance, Gini, etc.) with solid theoretical and empirical support.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, strong theory, solid experiments, clear improvement over U2-gossip, well-presented, high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: added complexity from phantom nodes, heavy spectral/mathematical detail, abstract statement of rate dependencies lacking practical parameter discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly accept with a minor revision to expand discussion of network/data dependencies in the convergence rates.
