- Introduces new gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics, filling a gap where most gossip work targets simpler statistics like the mean.  
- Core algorithmic idea: combine data propagation with local averaging to efficiently estimate complex U-statistics.  
- Provides convergence rate bounds for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Numerical experiments show superior performance versus prior U2-gossip.  
- Technical analysis appears rigorous, though full verification is nontrivial.  
- Convergence bounds explicitly depend on both data properties and network spectral characteristics.  
- Asynchronous extension is valuable but converges slower (logarithmic rate) than synchronous.  
- Analysis relies on a “phantom node”/phantom graph reformulation that enables spectral tools but feels contrived and less intuitive.  
- The necessity of the phantom construction is not fully justified beyond analytical convenience.  
- Extension to multiple observations per node is mentioned but treated briefly and lacks detailed analysis.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, but dense; notation is standard and algorithms are clearly presented.  
- Background section is adequate but could use more intuitive explanations for readers unfamiliar with gossip/spectral theory.  
- Phantom node construction is the least clear aspect and would benefit from stronger visual/intuition aids beyond the provided figure.  
- Originality: novel application of gossip methods to U-statistics and an insightful use of phantom nodes in this context.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for distributed ML tasks (AUC, variance, clustering), with practical gains in convergence and communication/memory.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel problem, sound analysis, strong empirical validation, clear presentation, and practical relevance.  
- Rejection/concern arguments: phantom-node complexity reduces intuition, scalability with network size/data dimensionality not fully addressed, and limited treatment of multi-observation nodes.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability concerns and expand the multiple-observation extension.
