**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for decentralized estimation of U-statistics in networks. U-statistics are statistical quantities that depend on pairs of observations, such as the sample variance, Area Under the Curve (AUC), and Gini mean difference. The proposed algorithms are designed to efficiently compute these quantities in a decentralized setting, where data is distributed across a network of nodes.

The algorithms are based on iterative information exchange in the network, combining data propagation and averaging steps. The synchronous algorithm, GoSta-sync, assumes a global clock, while the asynchronous algorithm, GoSta-async, does not require a global clock. The authors provide convergence rate bounds for both algorithms, with GoSta-sync achieving a rate of O(1/t) and GoSta-async achieving a rate of O(log t/t).

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel algorithms**: The paper proposes new algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics, which is a less investigated area compared to decentralized estimation of sample mean statistics.
2. **Convergence rate analysis**: The authors provide a thorough convergence rate analysis for both algorithms, which is essential for understanding their performance.
3. **Experimental evaluation**: The paper includes an experimental evaluation of the proposed algorithms on real datasets, which demonstrates their practical interest.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Complexity of the analysis**: The convergence rate analysis is based on an original reformulation of the problem using "phantom nodes," which may be challenging to follow for some readers.
2. **Limited comparison to existing work**: While the paper compares the proposed algorithms to U2-gossip, it would be beneficial to include a more comprehensive comparison to other existing algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper proposes novel algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics, which is a significant contribution to the field of distributed computing and machine learning. The convergence rate analysis and experimental evaluation demonstrate the practical interest of the proposed algorithms.
