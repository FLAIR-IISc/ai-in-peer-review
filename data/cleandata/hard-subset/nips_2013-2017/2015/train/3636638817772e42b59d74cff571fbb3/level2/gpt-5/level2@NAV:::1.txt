Summary and relation to prior work: The paper tackles decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over networks via randomized gossip. The authors introduce synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) algorithms that maintain a single auxiliary observation per node and combine data propagation with local averaging. They prove convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t/t) (async), with explicit data- and graph-dependent constants (spectral gap). A key analytical device is a “phantom nodes” construction that decouples propagation from averaging. Compared with U2-gossip [17], the proposed methods reduce memory/communication, remove the global clock requirement, and achieve tighter rates absent a √n factor; experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter over complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs corroborate the theory. The work connects to and extends classical gossip averaging [2], geographic/accelerated consensus [6,13], and distributed optimization [7,16], while addressing an underexplored non-separable objective class.

Quality: The theoretical claims are clearly stated and (per supplement) carefully proved, with explicit dependence on the spectral gap and problem dispersion. The asynchronous estimator’s unbiased time-tracking via mk(t) is neat. Empirical support is positive but somewhat limited: comparisons are mostly to U2-gossip; evaluation is in iterations rather than wall-clock or bits sent; robustness (packet loss, delays) is not explored.

Clarity: The paper is generally well organized. Algorithms are precise, and the phantom network exposition is helpful, though dense. Minor suggestions: tighten notation around Wα(G) and emphasize assumptions (connected, non-bipartite, H symmetric, H(X,X)=0) upfront; discuss practical computation of |E| for pk in fully decentralized settings.

Originality: Extending gossip to U-statistics with averaging plus the phantom-node analysis is novel relative to [17] and standard gossip literature. The asynchronous variant and refined rate comparison to U2-gossip add value.

Significance: U-statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, scatter) are widely used; a scalable, low-memory decentralized estimator is practically useful. The explicit graph dependence is informative for network design.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel formulation and analysis; reduced memory/communication.
- Solid convergence guarantees with explicit constants.
- Meaningful applications and empirical gains over prior art.

Cons for acceptance:
- Narrow experimental scope and baselines; no wall-clock/communication-vs-accuracy tradeoffs.
- Assumptions on edge sampling and knowledge of |E|; limited robustness analysis.
- Focus on degree-2 U; higher-order extensions only sketched.

Constructive suggestions:
- Report communication/computation per ε-accuracy and wall-clock on varied hardware; include push-sum/accelerated consensus baselines and geographic/accelerated variants where applicable.
- Evaluate robustness to message loss/asynchrony and degree heterogeneity; relax the need for global |E|.
- Provide ablations showing spectral-gap effects and extend experiments to higher-order U-statistics if feasible.
