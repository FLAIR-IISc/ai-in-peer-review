- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync, GoSta-async) for decentralized computation of U-statistics, using phantom nodes for analysis.  
- Addresses an important gap: U-statistics in decentralized settings (e.g., AUC, within-cluster scatter) which are more complex than mean/sum statistics.  
- Provides detailed convergence proofs and explicit network- and data-dependent rate bounds (O(1/t) synchronous, O(log t / t) asynchronous).  
- Theoretical results clearly characterize dependence on network properties like the spectral gap.  
- Algorithms are designed for practical constraints (limited memory, asynchronous communication), making them suitable for large-scale distributed systems.  
- Empirical experiments on real datasets (SMVguide3, Wine Quality) and multiple topologies show faster convergence and better memory/communication efficiency than U2-gossip.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains algorithms, theory, and experiments; supplementary material adds further detail.  
- Limited application scope: experiments focus on AUC and within-cluster scatter and would benefit from additional U-statistics to show generality.  
- Narrow baseline comparison: primarily compared to U2-gossip; should include other decentralized methods (e.g., for mean/sum) for a fuller evaluation.  
- Asynchronous algorithm’s theoretical analysis is less detailed; constants and deeper exploration of the O(log t / t) rate are lacking.  
- Insufficient discussion of scalability: missing detailed analysis of computational and communication overhead as network size increases.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand application scope, broaden baselines, and deepen asynchronous analysis.
