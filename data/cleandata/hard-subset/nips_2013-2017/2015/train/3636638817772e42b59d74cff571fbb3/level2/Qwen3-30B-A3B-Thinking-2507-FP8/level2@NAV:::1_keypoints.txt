- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) for distributed estimation of U-statistics, filling a gap since existing gossip methods handle only separable statistics.  
- Claims faster convergence rates (O(1/t) and O(log t/t)) with lower memory and communication costs compared to U2-gossip.  
- Provides strong theoretical support with rigorous convergence proofs using a novel "phantom nodes" reformulation to analyze data propagation and averaging.  
- Convergence bounds explicitly include data-dependent terms and network spectral-gap terms, showing better-connected networks accelerate convergence.  
- Theoretical comparison correctly identifies a √n disadvantage in U2-gossip (eq. 10), and this disadvantage is empirically validated.  
- Demonstrates solid literature knowledge and correctly positions the work within gossip algorithm research.  
- Applies methods to relevant ML tasks (AUC and within-cluster point scatter) demonstrating practical relevance.  
- Experiments on real datasets and multiple network topologies (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz) show GoSta consistently outperforms U2-gossip with lower error and variance.  
- Makes a meaningful advancement by addressing U2-gossip’s communication and asynchronicity limitations.  
- Notes limited discussion of some limitations (e.g., robustness to node failures or highly dynamic networks) that should be acknowledged.  
- Overall assesses the paper as high quality, clear, significant, and recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
