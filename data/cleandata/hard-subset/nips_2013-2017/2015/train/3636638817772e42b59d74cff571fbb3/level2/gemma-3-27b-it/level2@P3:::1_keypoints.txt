- Introduces novel randomized gossip algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics, filling a gap beyond averaging-focused literature.  
- Provides convergence rate bounds for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Uses “phantom nodes” as a clever analytical technique for the synchronous case.  
- Theoretical analysis ties convergence rates to both data and network characteristics.  
- Numerical experiments on real datasets and different network topologies show superior performance vs. prior work [17].  
- Paper is well-structured with clear problem statement, related work, algorithms, and experiments.  
- Mathematical notation and algorithmic descriptions are clear and readable.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and cites key papers.  
- Asynchronous algorithm inclusion addresses practical synchronization concerns in distributed systems.  
- Constants hidden in O-notation are not explicitly characterized; more concrete bounds are needed.  
- Asynchronous convergence analysis (O(log t / t)) is less detailed and needs deeper justification or extension.  
- Experimental section lacks detailed parameter settings and sensitivity analysis (e.g., Watts-Strogatz k and p effects).  
- Comparison is primarily against a single baseline ([17]); broader comparisons to other distributed estimators are missing.  
- Reliance on supplementary material is frequent; key proofs and details should be ensured there and highlighted.  
- Phantom node technique increases analytical complexity and needs a more intuitive explanation and clearer emphasis that it’s purely analytical.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical soundness, empirical validation, and clarity support publication.  
- Rejection concerns: missing concrete constants, limited experimental baselines, and weaker asynchronous analysis argue for revision.  
- Overall assessment: significant, high-quality contribution deserving acceptance pending major revisions to address constants and expanded experiments.
