- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in distributed networks.  
- U-statistics (pairwise averages) are important but less studied than simple averages, relevant to AUC, variance, Gini mean difference.  
- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous).  
- Theoretical and empirical improvements over prior work [17], including lower computational cost.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written; problem setup and notation are mostly clear.  
- Some notation choices (e.g., using H for both function and graph) could cause confusion.  
- Phantom node technique is a clever proof tool but somewhat complex and dense.  
- Algorithms (Algorithms 1 & 2) are presented straightforwardly.  
- Major strength: solid theoretical contributions with explicit data and network-dependent bounds.  
- Empirical validation (AUC, within-cluster scatter) supports theory and shows superiority, especially for larger networks.  
- Experiments test multiple topologies (complete, grid, Watts-Strogatz), strengthening evaluation.  
- Weakness: phantom node approach and proofs are relegated to supplement, reducing accessibility.  
- Weakness: asynchronous algorithm analysis feels rushed and lacks deeper justification for comparable empirical performance.  
- Weakness: constant factors in convergence bounds and deeper spectral-gap/network impact analysis are insufficiently explored.  
- Experimental scope is limited to two applications and comparisons mainly against U2-gossip; broader baselines/datasets would help.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with recommendation for acceptance, pending clarifications and expanded analysis.
