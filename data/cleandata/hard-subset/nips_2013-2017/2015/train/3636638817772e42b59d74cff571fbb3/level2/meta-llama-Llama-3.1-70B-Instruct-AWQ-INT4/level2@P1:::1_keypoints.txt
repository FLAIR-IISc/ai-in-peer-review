- Proposes two novel randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for decentralized computation of U‑statistics.
- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms with rigorous analysis.
- Introduces a novel "phantom nodes" reformulation to separate data propagation and averaging steps.
- Provides thorough convergence proofs and technically rigorous analysis.
- Numerical experiments show proposed methods outperform existing approaches like U2‑gossip.
- Clear, concise presentation of problem, algorithms, and related work.
- Thorough review of related literature, highlighting limitations of prior gossip algorithms.
- Technical contributions are significant and well-motivated, with strong potential impact for decentralized estimation.
- Paper is well‑written and technically sound overall.
- Assumes a relatively high level of mathematical sophistication, which may limit accessibility.
- Notation and mathematical derivations may be challenging for some readers to follow.
- Lacks a detailed discussion of practical implications such as scalability and robustness to network failures.
