This paper proposes randomized synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in a distributed setting. U-statistics, involving averaging over pairs of observations, are important for various applications like variance estimation, AUC, and Gini mean difference, where standard gossip algorithms fall short. The authors establish convergence rate bounds of O(1/t) for the synchronous case and O(log t/t) for the asynchronous case, with network and data-dependent terms. They also present numerical experiments demonstrating the superiority of their approach over a previously introduced method [17].

The paper is well-structured and addresses a relevant problem in distributed estimation. The motivation is clear, and the authors effectively highlight the limitations of existing gossip algorithms for U-statistics. The introduction provides a good overview of the problem, related work, and contributions. The mathematical notation is generally clear, though some equations could benefit from more spacing and explanation. The introduction of “phantom nodes” is a clever technique for analyzing the convergence of the algorithms, and the proof sketches provided in the text are convincing. The comparison to the existing work [17] is valuable, and the empirical results support the theoretical findings.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed algorithms extend the capabilities of gossip algorithms to a broader class of statistics beyond simple averages, specifically U-statistics.
*   **Theoretical Analysis:** The convergence rate bounds are rigorously derived and provide insights into the factors affecting performance (network structure, data properties). The inclusion of data-dependent terms is a valuable addition.
*   **Practical Evaluation:** The experimental results on real datasets (AUC and within-cluster point scatter) demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithms compared to a baseline approach.
*   **Clarity of Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The problem statement is clear, and the algorithmic descriptions are understandable.
*   **Asynchronous Algorithm:** Providing an asynchronous version alongside the synchronous one increases the practical applicability of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity of Phantom Node Analysis:** While innovative, the “phantom node” approach adds complexity to the analysis and might make it difficult for some readers to fully grasp the details. More intuitive explanations of the connection between the phantom network and the original network would be helpful.
*   **Limited Comparison:** The comparison is mainly to [17]. It would be valuable to compare the performance against other relevant methods, even if they are not specifically designed for U-statistics (e.g., distributed variance estimation techniques).
*   **Network Model:** The assumption of undirected graphs may limit the applicability of the algorithms in certain scenarios. Exploring extensions to directed graphs could be beneficial.
*   **Parameter Tuning:** The paper does not discuss the sensitivity of the algorithms to parameter settings (e.g., the choice of network topology). A discussion of parameter tuning guidelines would be helpful.
*   **Minor Notation Issues:**  The use of  `H(X, X)` = 0 in the problem statement feels restrictive and isn’t fully justified. It might be worthwhile to briefly address what happens if this condition isn’t met.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a challenging problem in distributed estimation with a novel and theoretically sound approach.
*   The algorithms are well-motivated, and the convergence analysis provides valuable insights into their performance.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the algorithms.
*   The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of gossip algorithms and has the potential to be used in various applications.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The complexity of the “phantom node” analysis might make it difficult for some readers to fully understand the technical details.
*   The comparison to other related methods is limited.
*   The discussion of parameter tuning and the limitations of the network model is lacking.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of distributed estimation. The authors have presented a novel and theoretically well-supported approach for computing U-statistics using gossip algorithms. While the complexity of the analysis and the limited comparison are minor concerns, the strengths of the paper outweigh these weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the clarity of the phantom node explanation and expanding the comparison to other relevant methods. It would also be beneficial to discuss parameter sensitivity and the assumptions regarding the network topology.
