- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async randomized gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics in decentralized networks.
- Claims convergence rates O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous versions.
- Claims improved convergence, memory, and communication costs over existing U2-gossip.
- Applicable to various U-statistics including AUC and within-cluster point scatter.
- Introduces a "phantom nodes" reformulation to separately analyze data propagation and averaging.
- Provides theoretical analysis establishing the stated convergence rates.
- Numerical experiments show lower average relative error and variance versus U2-gossip.
- Demonstrates practical usefulness for distributed machine learning and data mining tasks.
- Authors show good command of prior work, correct notation, and comprehensive references.
- Novelty lies in the algorithms and analysis offering a more efficient decentralized solution.
- Paper is generally complete with sufficient details for reproducibility, though some proofs are deferred.
- Limitations: may not scale well to very large networks or high-dimensional data.
- Some technical proofs relegated to supplementary material, potentially hindering readability.
- Lacks comprehensive comparisons with all other existing U-statistic estimation algorithms.
