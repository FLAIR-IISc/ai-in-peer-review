- Tackles decentralized estimation of degree-two U-statistics using gossip, a challenging extension beyond simple averaging.  
- Proposes two algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, combining local estimate averaging with auxiliary-data swapping.  
- Core mechanism: nodes maintain a local U-statistic estimate and an auxiliary observation; neighboring pairs average estimates and swap observations.  
- Introduces a "phantom network" analytical tool to decouple data propagation and estimate averaging.  
- Provides rigorous convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous cases.  
- Improves over prior U2-gossip in memory, communication cost, and convergence speed; refines theoretical comparison to U2-gossip.  
- Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across multiple topologies supports theoretical claims and shows practical superiority.  
- Strength: significant, original contribution with elegant algorithmic idea and strong, insightful theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: clear writing, good organization, and helpful illustrations (e.g., Figure 1).  
- Weakness: asynchronous bound (O(log t / t)) is looser than synchronous despite similar empirical performance; needs discussion whether log factor is an artifact.  
- Weakness: experiments lack a simple non-gossip baseline (e.g., k-hop aggregation or spanning-tree routing) for additional context.  
- Weakness: description of U2-gossip is relegated to supplement; include a brief high-level summary in main text for self-containment.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — high-quality, novel work with minor issues that can be addressed in revision.
