- Problem: decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics in peer-to-peer networks using randomized gossip; proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async that swap tokens and average local estimates.  
- Analytical tool: introduces a “phantom network” construction to decouple data propagation from estimate averaging for convergence analysis.  
- Theoretical guarantees: convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O((log t)/t) (asynchronous), with bounds depending on data dispersion and network spectral gap Wα(G).  
- Comparison to prior work: refines analysis of U2-gossip, revealing a √n penalty and higher per-iteration memory/communication for U2-gossip.  
- Novelty: extends gossip methods to non-separable U-statistics with both synchronous and fully asynchronous algorithms.  
- Theory strength: explicit dependence on problem hardness and network connectivity; phantom-node analysis is elegant and potentially of independent interest.  
- Practicality: lower memory and communication per iteration than U2-gossip (single auxiliary observation and single activated edge); async variant uses only local activations and simple counters.  
- Empirical results: shows improved accuracy and lower across-node variance versus U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter across multiple graph topologies; empirical trends match theory.  
- Main weakness: results are in expectation only; lacks high-probability or almost-sure concentration/finite-sample deviation bounds.  
- Implementation issue: asynchronous algorithm requires knowledge of |E| via pk = 2dk/|E|, which may be unavailable in truly decentralized settings.  
- Communication-cost concern: analysis counts iterations but not message size; swapping raw observations (especially high-dimensional Xi) can be expensive and needs accounting.  
- Privacy concern: raw-data swapping may be unsuitable for privacy-constrained networks; no sketching, hashing, or secure-aggregation alternatives proposed.  
- Optimality question: unclear whether the provided rates are minimax optimal under the communication model; no lower-bound discussion.  
- Experimental limitations: datasets and network scales are moderate; need larger networks, scale-free topologies, spectral-gap variation, and experiments with multiple observations per node.  
- Robustness question: assumptions (connected non-bipartite graphs, uniform random edge selection) limit applicability; sensitivity to non-uniform selection, time-varying graphs, or packet loss is unclear.  
- Quality: algorithms are well specified; proofs are sketched with clear intuition and deferred details; rate expressions are interpretable.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear; suggests summarizing Theorem 2 proof ideas, explicitly stating edge-selection assumptions, and adding communication/memory complexity per iteration and to reach ε-accuracy.  
- Originality: combining single-token data propagation with averaging and phantom-node analysis is novel relative to prior gossip methods.  
- Significance: enabling decentralized estimation of widely used U-statistics with rigorous rates is valuable and likely to be adopted or extended.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear problem formulation, practical sync/async algorithms, theoretical rates with explicit data/network dependence, lower memory/communication than prior work, and supportive empirical validation.  
- Cons for acceptance: expectation-only guarantees, requirement of |E| in async variant, limited large-scale and multi-observation experiments.  
- Suggested improvements: provide high-probability bounds or empirical CIs; devise async variant not requiring |E| (e.g., Poisson clocks, push-sum, adaptive pk); report communication complexity and effect of data dimension; explore sketching/privacy-preserving extensions; add experiments varying spectral gap and multiple observations per node.  
- Overall recommendation: solid and timely contribution with theoretical and empirical advances over prior art; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
