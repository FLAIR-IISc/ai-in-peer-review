- Proposes randomized synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in distributed settings.  
- U-statistics (e.g., variance, AUC, Gini mean difference) require pairwise averaging where standard gossip algorithms fail.  
- Establishes convergence bounds: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with network- and data-dependent terms.  
- Introduces “phantom nodes” as an analytical technique; proof sketches are provided and convincing.  
- Empirical results on real datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) show superiority over prior method [17].  
- Paper is well-structured with clear motivation, related work overview, and understandable algorithm descriptions.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and includes useful data-dependent insights.  
- Providing an asynchronous variant improves practical applicability.  
- Some equations could use better spacing and more explanation for clarity.  
- Phantom node analysis adds complexity and needs more intuitive explanation of its connection to the original network.  
- Comparisons are limited mainly to [17]; broader benchmarking against other relevant methods is missing.  
- Assumes undirected graphs, limiting applicability; extensions to directed graphs are not explored.  
- No discussion of parameter sensitivity or practical tuning guidelines (e.g., network topology effects).  
- Minor notation issue: assumption H(X,X)=0 is restrictive and not fully justified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theory, empirical validation, and significant contribution to gossip algorithms.  
- Arguments against acceptance: complex analysis may hinder accessibility, limited comparisons, and insufficient discussion of tuning and network assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify phantom nodes, expand comparisons, and discuss parameter sensitivity and topology assumptions.
