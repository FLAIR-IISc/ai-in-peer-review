- Problem and methods: Decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via randomized gossip, introducing GoSta-sync and GoSta-async that keep one auxiliary observation per node and combine data propagation with local averaging.  
- Theoretical results: Convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async) with explicit data- and graph-dependent (spectral gap) constants.  
- Key analysis tool: “Phantom nodes” construction that decouples propagation from averaging.  
- Comparison to prior work: Improves on U2-gossip [17] by reducing memory/communication, removing global clock requirement, and achieving tighter rates (no √n factor).  
- Experiments: Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs supports theory.  
- Quality of proofs: Theoretical claims are clearly stated and carefully proved; asynchronous estimator’s unbiased time-tracking via mk(t) is a neat device.  
- Empirical limitations: Experiments are somewhat limited—mainly compared to U2-gossip, measured in iterations (not wall-clock or bits sent), and robustness (packet loss, delays) not explored.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well organized; phantom-network exposition is helpful but dense; some notation and assumptions should be emphasized upfront.  
- Originality: Novel extension of gossip to U-statistics with phantom-node analysis; asynchronous variant and refined rate comparison add value.  
- Significance: Practical importance for widely used U-statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, scatter) and informative explicit graph dependence for network design.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel formulation/analysis, reduced memory/communication, solid convergence guarantees with explicit constants, meaningful applications and empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: Narrow experimental scope and baselines, lack of wall-clock/communication-vs-accuracy tradeoffs, assumptions on edge sampling and knowledge of |E|, limited robustness analysis, and focus only on degree-2 U-statistics.  
- Constructive suggestions: Report communication/computation per ε-accuracy and wall-clock, include push-sum/accelerated consensus baselines, evaluate robustness to message loss/asynchrony and degree heterogeneity, relax global |E| requirement, provide spectral-gap ablations, and extend experiments to higher-order U-statistics.
