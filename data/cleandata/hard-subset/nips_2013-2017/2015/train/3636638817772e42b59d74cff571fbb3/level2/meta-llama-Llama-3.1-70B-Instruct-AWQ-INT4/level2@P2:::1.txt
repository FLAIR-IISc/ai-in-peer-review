The paper "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics" proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms, showing that they outperform existing methods.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The introduction provides a good background on the problem of decentralized estimation and the importance of U-statistics. The related work section is comprehensive, covering various gossip algorithms and their limitations.

The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, are well-motivated and cleverly designed. The use of "phantom nodes" to model the data propagation and averaging steps is a nice touch. The convergence analysis is rigorous, and the authors provide explicit data and network-dependent terms in their bounds.

The numerical experiments are convincing, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing methods. The comparison with U2-gossip is particularly useful, highlighting the advantages of the new algorithms.

The paper's strengths include:

1. Originality: The paper proposes new algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting, which is a novel contribution.
2. Rigorous analysis: The authors provide a thorough convergence analysis, including explicit bounds and a detailed proof.
3. Comprehensive experiments: The numerical experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.

The paper's weaknesses include:

1. Limited scope: The paper focuses on U-statistics, which might limit its applicability to other decentralized estimation problems.
2. Complexity: The algorithms and analysis might be challenging to understand for readers without a strong background in graph theory and decentralized estimation.

To improve the paper, the authors could consider:

1. Providing more insights into the practical implications of their results, such as the potential applications and benefits of their algorithms.
2. Exploring the extension of their algorithms to other decentralized estimation problems, such as computing other types of statistics or functions.
3. Comparing their algorithms with other decentralized estimation methods, such as those based on consensus or diffusion.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant contribution to the field of decentralized estimation. The paper is suitable for publication in a top-tier conference like NIPS.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes novel algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting.
* The convergence analysis is rigorous and provides explicit bounds.
* The numerical experiments are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper's scope is limited to U-statistics, which might limit its applicability.
* The algorithms and analysis might be challenging to understand for readers without a strong background in graph theory and decentralized estimation.
