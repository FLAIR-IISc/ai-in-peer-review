- Addresses decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over networks using randomized gossip, which standard averaging cannot handle due to pairwise dependence.
- Proposes GoSta-sync: synchronous pairwise gossip that updates running averages, averages estimates between activated nodes, and swaps auxiliary observations, analyzed via a “phantom nodes” construction.
- Proves GoSta-sync converges in expectation to the true U-statistic at rate O(1/t) with constants separating data-dispersion terms from a network spectral term (W2(G) spectral gap).
- Proposes GoSta-async: fully asynchronous variant where only the activated pair updates and per-node counters m_k are scaled by p_k = 2 d_k / |E| to preserve unbiasedness.
- Proves GoSta-async converges in expectation at rate O(log t / t).
- Both algorithms require only one auxiliary observation per node and one edge activation per iteration, keeping memory and communication minimal.
- Compares to U2-gossip [17], showing GoSta has lower per-iteration cost and a refined bound explains U2-gossip’s empirical √n disadvantage.
- Experiments on AUC estimation and within-cluster scatter across several graph topologies corroborate theoretical predictions and show GoSta outperforming U2-gossip.
- Situates work within gossip and U-statistics literature, connecting to pairwise averaging, geographic gossip, and spectral-gap analyses.
- The phantom-graph analytical device cleanly decouples data propagation and averaging dynamics and aids the convergence proofs.
- Theory quality is strong: clear assumptions (connected, non-bipartite), plausible rates consistent with stochastic approximation, and explicit spectral-gap dependence.
- A notable modeling requirement is that GoSta-async needs nodes to know p_k = 2 d_k / |E|, which may be impractical in fully decentralized settings.
- Empirics are supportive but limited: two datasets, no communication- or wall-clock time-to-accuracy curves, and scalability only up to ~1.6k nodes.
- Clarity is generally good: algorithms explicit, phantom-graph explained, notation mostly consistent, but some minor clarifications suggested.
- Originality: combining gossip with a single auxiliary token, phantom-graph analysis, and refined U2-gossip comparison appear novel.
- Significance: practical relevance for decentralized estimation of pairwise statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, etc.) with low memory/communication and explicit spectral dependence.
- Pros: simple implementable algorithms, principled analysis with interpretable constants, stronger theory and lower cost than U2-gossip, experiments matching theory, and reusable phantom-graph technique.
- Cons: async requires |E| knowledge, only expectation guarantees (no high-probability/L2 or across-node disagreement bounds), synchronous uniform edge selection may be unrealistic, limited datasets and scaling, and bipartite graphs excluded without discussed fixes.
- Essential suggestion: clarify practicality and robustness of p_k in GoSta-async and consider alternatives or bias-corrections that avoid needing |E|.
- Essential suggestion: add communication-complexity results (error vs messages/words and simple latency/bandwidth wall-clock model) to substantiate efficiency claims.
- Essential suggestion: discuss or provide a lemma on handling bipartite graphs (e.g., lazy updates or Wα with α>2) to remove periodicity.
- Optional suggestion: include high-probability or L2 bounds (martingale concentration), quantify across-node disagreement, and extend experiments to larger n, higher-dim data, and more topologies.
- Optional suggestion: give more intuition on data-dependent terms, discuss extensions to k>2 U-statistics, and outline privacy-preserving variants.
- Overall assessment: solid, timely contribution that advances decentralized estimation beyond separable aggregates; with clarifications on async assumptions and additional communication-focused evaluation, it is a strong NIPS submission.
