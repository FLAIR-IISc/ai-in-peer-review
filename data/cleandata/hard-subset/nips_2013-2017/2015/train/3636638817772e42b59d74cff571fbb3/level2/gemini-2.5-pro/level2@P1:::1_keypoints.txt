- Proposes GoSta synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-two U-statistics by combining data propagation (random-walking auxiliary observations) with estimate averaging.

- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with convergence rates O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous GoSta, and introduces a novel "phantom network" proof technique to decouple processes.

- Offers a refined analysis of prior U2-gossip showing its convergence is worse by a factor of sqrt(n), enabling a fair theoretical comparison.

- Empirical validation on real-world datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) and multiple network topologies shows GoSta outperforms U2-gossip in accuracy, convergence speed, and resource use, with gaps widening with network size.

- Strength: Addresses an important practical problem (decentralized U-statistics) with an original, simple, and effective algorithmic idea.

- Weakness: Async algorithm requires nodes to know pk = 2dk/|E|, implying global knowledge of |E|, which contradicts fully decentralized assumptions and needs discussion or remedies (estimation via gossip).

- Weakness: Clarify derivation of node activation probability pk, since uniform edge sampling implies node activation probability dk/|E|, not 2dk/|E| as stated.

- Weakness: Synchronous model assumes all nodes update simultaneously before a single pair communicates, a strong global-clock assumption that may be impractical and should be discussed relative to standard gossip models.

- Weakness: Experiments show GoSta-async matches GoSta-sync despite worse proven asymptotic rate; authors should provide intuition on constants or regimes where the theoretical gap is negligible.
