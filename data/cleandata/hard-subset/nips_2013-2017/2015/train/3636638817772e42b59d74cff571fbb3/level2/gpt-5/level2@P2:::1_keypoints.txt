- Proposes two randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync, GoSta-async) for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics using a single auxiliary observation per node and spatial/temporal averaging.
- Introduces a “phantom graph” construction to decouple data propagation and averaging, enabling tractable analysis.
- Proves convergence in expectation to the exact U-statistic with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).
- Provides explicit rate dependence on data dispersion and the network spectral gap.
- Claims improved rates and lower memory/communication per iteration compared to prior U2-gossip.
- Positions work relative to classic randomized gossip/consensus literature and the lone prior U-statistics gossip method.
- Analysis separates mixing of estimates (W2 operator) and data propagation (block-diagonal W1 operators), yielding plausible bounds.
- Shows intuition for a √n penalty in U2-gossip from not leveraging spatial averaging.
- Convergence guarantees are only in expectation; no high-probability, MSE, or almost-sure results provided.
- Asynchronous algorithm requires nodes to know pk = 2dk/|E| (thus |E|), which may be unrealistic in decentralized settings.
- Replacing the required step-size with local visit counts or online estimates is not analyzed but suggested as important to address.
- Algorithms assume undirected, connected, non-bipartite graphs with uniform random edge activations; bipartite, directed, and time-varying networks are largely out of scope.
- Synchronous scheduler model (global uniform random edge each iteration) needs clarification for practical implementation.
- Notational ambiguity: eigenvalue indexing (β2 vs βn−1) and relation between Wα(G) and Laplacian should be standardized.
- Experiments show faster convergence of GoSta-sync vs U2-gossip on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs and comparable async vs sync performance.
- Experimental comparison may be unfair: U2-gossip uses two edges and two auxiliary observations per iteration; communication-normalized comparisons are needed.
- Variance-across-nodes behavior is shown empirically but lacks theoretical variance/consensus decay analysis.
- Asynchronous empirical evaluation is limited (single figure); more networks and sizes would strengthen the O(log t / t) claim.
- Sensitivity to graph size/spectral gap is shown for complete graphs; extending to grids/WS would reinforce spectral-gap dependence.
- Paper is generally well-written; phantom graph and transition-matrix exposition are clear.
- Computational cost per iteration and memory requirements should be stated explicitly (one auxiliary vector and scalar per node; counter for async).
- Novelty: single-auxiliary-data gossip for U-statistics and phantom-graph analysis are meaningful refinements over prior work.
- Significance: relevant to ML tasks (AUC, scatter, Gini, Kendall tau) with practical memory/communication improvements, but limited by expectation-only guarantees and async global knowledge requirement.
- Pros: principled algorithms, solid analysis with spectral-gap dependence, lower per-iteration resources, empirical support across graph families.
- Cons: expectation-only convergence, async needs |E|, synchronous scheduling practicality unclear, experimental comparisons not normalized for communication.
- Suggestions: provide MSE/high-probability bounds, remove or mitigate need for |E| (online estimation or visit-count stepsizes), discuss bipartite/directed/time-varying cases and message loss robustness.
- Suggestions (experiments): normalize comparisons by messages/words or wall-clock time, expand async experiments and spectral-gap sensitivity across more graphs.
- Suggest standardizing eigenvalue notation and clarifying any deviation from classical U-statistic normalization.
