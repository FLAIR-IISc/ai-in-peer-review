1. Proposes GoSta gossip algorithms (synchronous and asynchronous) for distributed estimation of U-statistics.  
2. Maintains local estimates while propagating data across the network via novel algorithm design.  
3. Provides theoretical convergence: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous settings.  
4. Introduces a "phantom node" construction that cleanly separates data propagation and averaging in analysis.  
5. Convergence bounds explicitly include data-dependent and network-dependent terms.  
6. Empirical results on real datasets (AUC and within-cluster scatter) show faster convergence and lower variance than U2-gossip.  
7. Clearly positions contributions relative to prior work, particularly U2-gossip [17], highlighting theoretical and practical advantages.  
8. Well-motivated problem: fills a gap in gossip literature on U-statistics.  
9. Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with proofs and supplementary details supporting claims.  
10. Paper is well-written, with clear pseudocode, consistent notation, and reproducible supplementary material.  
11. Novelty: addresses an unstudied problem and introduces an elegant phantom node technique.  
12. Significance: offers lower communication/memory costs and practical gains for real-world distributed systems.  
13. Weakness: Limited contextualization with broader distributed-statistics literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. 2010).  
14. Weakness: Experimental comparisons are limited to U2-gossip; missing centralized and simple baseline methods.  
15. Weakness: Asynchronous theoretical analysis is less detailed—constant c'(G) is not characterized.  
16. Weakness: Lacks discussion of practical implementation issues (node failures, partitions, time-varying topologies).  
17. Recommendation: Accept — technically strong, important contribution, with minor easily-fixable weaknesses.
