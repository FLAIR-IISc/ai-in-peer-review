- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) for distributed estimation of U-statistics (pairwise-dependent metrics like AUC, variance).
- Key innovation is reformulating propagation with "phantom nodes" to separate data movement from averaging, enabling convergence analysis.
- Fills an important gap since prior gossip work handled only single-observation statistics.
- Provides rigorous theoretical convergence rates: O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous), depending on data and network spectral gap.
- Shows clear advantages over prior U2-gossip in communication and memory complexity and in empirical convergence speed.
- Empirical validation is comprehensive: real datasets (SMVguide3, Wine Quality), multiple network topologies (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz), and several performance metrics.
- Paper is very well written with clear notation, precise pseudocode, and effective figures illustrating results.
- Phantom node technique is novel and enables derivation of convergence rates, distinguishing this work from related gossip literature.
- Significant practical impact for distributed ML/statistics because many important metrics are U-statistics and GoSta reduces communication/memory costs.
- Minor weaknesses: could provide more explicit memory/communication footprint comparisons and discuss performance on disconnected graphs or nonuniform data distributions.
- Overall assessment: technically strong, well-supported by experiments, and recommended for acceptance.
