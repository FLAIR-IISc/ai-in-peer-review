- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.  
- Algorithms combine iterative data propagation and averaging across network nodes.  
- GoSta-sync assumes a global clock; GoSta-async is fully asynchronous.  
- Proven convergence rates: GoSta-sync O(1/t), GoSta-async O(log t / t).  
- Novel contribution: addresses decentralized U-statistics, a less-studied problem vs. sample means.  
- Strength: thorough convergence rate analysis for both synchronous and asynchronous variants.  
- Strength: experimental evaluation on real datasets demonstrating practical interest.  
- Weakness: convergence analysis uses "phantom nodes" reformulation, which is complex and may be hard to follow.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison (primarily against U2-gossip) — broader baselines missing.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to distributed computing and machine learning with practical utility.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with concise writing and consistent mathematical notation.  
- Pro acceptance: novel algorithms, solid theoretical analysis, and empirical validation.  
- Con acceptance: analytical complexity and insufficient comparisons to existing methods.  
- Recommended improvements: add broader baseline comparisons, clearer explanations/examples of the convergence analysis, and additional experiments to show robustness.
