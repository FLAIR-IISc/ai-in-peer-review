This paper presents new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which are essential in many machine learning and data mining applications. The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, efficiently estimate U-statistics in a decentralized setting, where data is distributed across a network of nodes.

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The proposed algorithms converge to the true value of the U-statistic at rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) for the synchronous and asynchronous versions, respectively.
2. The algorithms outperform the existing U2-gossip algorithm in terms of convergence rate and memory and communication costs.
3. The algorithms can be applied to various U-statistics, including the Area Under the Curve (AUC) and the within-cluster point scatter.

The support for these claims is provided through theoretical analysis and numerical experiments. The convergence rates are established using a novel reformulation of the problem using "phantom nodes," which allows for a separate analysis of the data propagation and averaging steps. The numerical experiments demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over U2-gossip in terms of average relative error and variance.

The usefulness of the proposed algorithms is evident in their ability to efficiently estimate U-statistics in a decentralized setting, which is essential in many modern applications, such as distributed machine learning and data mining.
