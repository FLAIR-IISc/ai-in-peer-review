- Problem: Estimating degree-two U-statistics (e.g., AUC, sample variance) in decentralized networks.
- Contribution: Proposes GoSta, a family of randomized gossip algorithms for synchronous and asynchronous settings.
- Core idea: Combine a data propagation step (neighbor swap of auxiliary observation) with a consensus averaging step.
- Improvement over prior work: Directly improves on U2-gossip in communication and memory efficiency.
- Originality: Introduces a fully asynchronous algorithm, increasing practicality for real-world distributed systems.
- Theoretical novelty: Uses a "phantom network" construction for elegant convergence analysis.
- Convergence rates: Derives O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, depending on spectral gap and data properties.
- Significance: Addresses an important problem—efficient decentralized computation of fundamental U-statistics.
- Clarity and presentation: Exceptionally well-written and well-organized, making algorithms and analysis easy to follow.
- Empirical validation: Thorough experiments on real datasets for two U-statistics show faster convergence and lower variance than state-of-the-art.
- Minor weakness: Theoretical bound for the asynchronous algorithm is slightly weaker than for the synchronous one, despite comparable empirical performance.
- Suggested fix: A brief discussion of the gap between asynchronous theory and practice would strengthen the paper.
- Overall verdict: Excellent, novel, and significant contribution deserving acceptance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel efficient algorithms, rigorous/original analysis, strong empirical results, high clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: None of significance.
