- Introduces GoSta synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics.
- GoSta combines local averaging with auxiliary-data swapping so information about all data points spreads through the network.
- Claims improved memory and communication efficiency compared to the prior U2-gossip method.
- Provides convergence rates: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t).
- Analysis uses a novel "phantom network" reformulation to decouple data propagation and averaging dynamics.
- Theoretical rates explicitly depend on data properties and network topology (via the spectral gap).
- Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter demonstrate superior performance over the state-of-the-art.
- Strength: addresses an important, underexplored problem with a novel, elegant algorithmic idea.
- Strength: technically sound with thorough convergence proofs and refined analysis of competing methods.
- Strength: well-written, clearly organized, and experimentally well-presented.
- Weakness: Algorithm 2 (asynchronous) is ambiguous about whether later Zi/Zj updates use pre- or post-averaged values and needs clarification.
- Weakness: the phantom network concept would benefit from more high-level intuition before the formal block-matrix treatment.
- Weakness: assumption H(X,X)=0 is not universal; the paper should discuss implications or how analysis changes if relaxed.
- Weakness: asynchronous convergence constant c'(G) is unspecified; more insight into its dependence on graph properties would strengthen the result.
