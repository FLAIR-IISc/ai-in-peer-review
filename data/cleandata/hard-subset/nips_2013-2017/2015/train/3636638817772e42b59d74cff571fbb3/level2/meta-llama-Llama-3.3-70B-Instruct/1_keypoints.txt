- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized U-statistics, claiming faster convergence and lower memory/communication costs.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem introduction and thorough related work.  
- Algorithms are well-motivated and clearly described.  
- Theoretical analysis gives convergence rate bounds for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Use of "phantom nodes" is a clever modeling technique that simplifies analysis.  
- Provides a comparison with U2-gossip that highlights the proposed methods' advantages.  
- Numerical experiments on two real datasets show GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip in average relative error and variance, with gaps widening on larger graphs.  
- Strengths: novel algorithms, sound theoretical analysis, and convincing experiments.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical implications and potential applications.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparisons with other existing (non-gossip) algorithms.  
- Weakness: assumption of connected, non-bipartite network graphs may limit practical applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant contribution with practical potential.  
- For acceptance: novelty, solid theory, and strong empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, need for broader comparisons, and limited practical discussion.
