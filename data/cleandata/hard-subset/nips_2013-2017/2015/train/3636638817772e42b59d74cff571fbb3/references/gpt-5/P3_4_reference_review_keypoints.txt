- Proposes decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via two randomized gossip algorithms: GoSta-sync and GoSta-async.
- GoSta-sync: single auxiliary observation per node performs random-walk swaps; synchronous running average with pairwise averaging.
- GoSta-async: only activated edge updates; uses per-node adaptive step sizes based on expected activations.
- Introduces a “phantom network” construction to decouple data propagation from averaging for analysis.
- Proves convergence in expectation to the U-statistic with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async), separating data-dispersion and spectral-gap terms.
- Refines analysis of prior U2-gossip, showing an unfavorable √n factor for that method.
- Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter show improved error and variance across several graph topologies.
- Positions work relative to classic gossip, geographic gossip, decentralized averaging, and U2-gossip; phantom-node idea relates to lifting/Kronecker analyses.
- Synchronous analysis is elegant and convincing: block-triangular state evolution and Wα(G) operators explain the O(1/t) rate.
- Asynchronous algorithm is natural and plausible, but the O(log t/t) bound leaves constant c′(G) unspecified and the norm in bounds ambiguous.
- Comparison to U2-gossip is informative but per-iteration fairness is only partly addressed (U2 touches two edges and swaps two auxiliaries).
- Convergence is shown only in expectation; no mean-square or high-probability concentration bounds are provided.
- Assumptions are standard (connected, non-bipartite graph; symmetric H; H(x,x)=0) and rely on uniform edge sampling.
- Paper is generally well written and organized; phantom network exposition is clear and helpful.
- Notation issues: matrix norms (e.g., for H and H − h1^T) are not precisely specified; some typos/formatting errors.
- Practicality concern: Algorithm 2 requires nodes to know |E| via pk = 2dk/|E|; need discussion of Metropolis/local weights and analysis impact.
- Originality: novel extension of gossip to pairwise U-statistics with provable rates, phantom-node analysis, async variant, and refined U2 comparison.
- Significance: relevant to federated sensing and peer-to-peer analytics; likely of interest to the NeurIPS community.
- Experiments are supportive but limited: only two real datasets and three synthetic graph models; need larger/diverse graphs to highlight spectral-gap effects.
- Recommend adding communication-normalized comparisons (messages/bytes per node) and more results for the asynchronous algorithm (wall-clock/event-driven metrics).
- Pros: clear/principled algorithms, interpretable spectral-gap rates, reduced memory/communication, instructive analysis, empirical improvements.
- Cons: expectation-only convergence, async requires global |E| knowledge, unspecified norms/constants, experimental evaluation could be broader and communication-normalized.
- Suggestions/questions: provide mean-square/high-probability bounds (at least for sync), clarify matrix norms and c′(G), explain async implementation without global |E| and analysis effects, extend experiments and discuss extension to U-statistics of order m>2.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — clear advance but needs clarity/practicality fixes and stronger empirical comparisons.
