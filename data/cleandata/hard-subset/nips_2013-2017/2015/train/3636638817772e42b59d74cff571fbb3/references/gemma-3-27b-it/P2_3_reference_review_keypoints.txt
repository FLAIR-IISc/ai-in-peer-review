- Problem: Extends gossip algorithms to estimate non-separable U-statistics (e.g., variance, AUC, correlations) in decentralized networks.
- Method: Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms called GoSta.
- Analysis trick: Introduces "phantom nodes" to reformulate data propagation and enable rigorous analysis.
- Convergence: Proves O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms with data- and network-dependent terms (spectral gap).
- Empirics: Shows GoSta outperforms prior U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks.
- Strength — significance: Broadens applicability of gossip methods to important non-separable statistics.
- Strength — novelty: Phantom node construction is an elegant, original analytical tool.
- Strength — theory: Strong, nuanced convergence analysis linking performance to data and network topology.
- Strength — empirical: Real-world experiments support theoretical claims.
- Strength — presentation: Generally clear writing, definitions, and organization.
- Weakness — baselines: Limited comparison; lacks evaluation against ADMM or other consensus/distributed estimation methods.
- Weakness — clarity: Phantom node argument can be hard to grasp and needs more intuitive explanation.
- Weakness — async rate: Asynchronous convergence (O(log t / t)) is weaker than synchronous; trade-offs need deeper discussion.
- Weakness — experiments: Only two U-statistics on two datasets; needs broader experimental validation.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- Revision requests: Expand related work comparisons, clarify phantom node construction, discuss improving asynchronous rate, and broaden experimental evaluation.
