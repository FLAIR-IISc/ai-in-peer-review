- Overall: Paper presents a valuable extension of gossip algorithms to distributed U-statistics and is recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Contribution: Proposes GoSta (synchronous and asynchronous) combining data propagation and averaging for decentralized U-statistic estimation.  
- Theoretical: Provides rigorous convergence guarantees (O(1/t) synchronous, O(log t/t) asynchronous) that include data- and network-dependent (spectral gap) terms.  
- Practical advantage: GoSta improves memory and communication efficiency over prior work [17].  
- Asynchrony: The GoSta-async algorithm addresses realistic asynchronous network settings.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on real datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) convincingly support the theoretical claims and outperform [17].  
- Methodological novelty: Introduces a “phantom node” technique to reformulate the problem and aid analysis.  
- Analysis complexity: The theoretical presentation is dense and may be inaccessible to readers unfamiliar with spectral graph theory.  
- Spectral gap guidance: Paper links convergence to the spectral gap but lacks practical guidance on leveraging or optimizing it in network design.  
- Baselines: Comparison is primarily to [17]; broader comparisons to standard distributed estimation methods are missing.  
- Assumptions: The symmetric kernel assumption H(X,X)=0 may be restrictive and adaptation to non-symmetric kernels is not discussed.  
- Visualization: The phantom network figure (Figure 1) is cluttered and could be made clearer.  
- Interpretation request: Theorem 1’s data-dependent terms need more intuitive explanation about which U-statistics converge faster or slower.  
- Asynchronous modeling: The connection between Poisson clock modeling and practical asynchronous implementation should be clarified.  
- Topology experiments: Experiments should more directly explore how network topology and spectral gap affect convergence speed.  
- Notation: Recommend standardizing notation throughout (consistent symbols for matrices/vectors).  
- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem, offers novel algorithms with proofs, improved efficiency, and strong experiments.  
- Against acceptance: Complexity of analysis, limited baseline comparisons, and insufficient practical discussion of spectral gap are concerns.  
- Required revisions: Simplify theoretical explanations, expand experimental baselines and topology analysis, and discuss practical implications of the spectral gap.
