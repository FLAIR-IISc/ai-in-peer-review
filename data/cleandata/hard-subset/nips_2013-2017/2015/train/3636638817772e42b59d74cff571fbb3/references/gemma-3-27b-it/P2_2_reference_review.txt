## Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting using gossip algorithms. Existing gossip algorithms are limited to separable functions, and U-statistics, which depend on pairs of observations, fall outside this scope. The authors propose both synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta) for this task. They present convergence analysis demonstrating rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) for the synchronous and asynchronous versions, respectively, and highlight the dependence of these rates on both network topology and problem characteristics.  A clever "phantom node" reformulation is used to facilitate the analysis. Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster point scatter estimation demonstrates the superiority of their approach compared to a recent related work (U2-gossip).

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Decentralized estimation of U-statistics is relevant to a wide range of applications (machine learning, data mining, statistics) and fills a gap in existing gossip algorithm capabilities.
* **Novel Approach:** The proposed algorithms (GoSta) and the "phantom node" reformulation offer a creative solution to a non-trivial problem. Combining data propagation with averaging is a key insight.
* **Strong Theoretical Analysis:** The convergence analysis is rigorous, providing clear rates and characterizing the influence of network topology (spectral gap) and data characteristics.
* **Empirical Validation:**  Experiments on real datasets demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the algorithms and their improvement over the U2-gossip approach. The experiments cover different network topologies and applications.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the key ideas accessible. The algorithms are clearly presented and the notation is consistent.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Analysis:** While rigorous, the analysis utilizing the "phantom node" network adds complexity and might be difficult for some readers to fully grasp without significant effort.  The connection between the phantom network and the practical implementation could be made more explicit.
* **Asynchronous Convergence Rate:** The asynchronous convergence rate of O(log t/t) is relatively slow compared to other asynchronous gossip algorithms achieving O(1/t). While mathematically sound, it might limit the practical applicability in certain scenarios.
* **Limited Comparison:** While comparison to U2-gossip is valuable, including a comparison to simpler baseline decentralized estimation methods (e.g., decentralized gradient descent) would provide a broader context and highlight the specific benefits of the gossip-based approach.
* **Discussion of Practical Considerations:** The paper could benefit from a discussion of practical considerations like the impact of network dynamics (node failures, varying communication costs) on algorithm performance.



**Evaluation based on NIPS Criteria:**

* **Quality:**  The paper is technically sound, with a rigorous analysis and compelling empirical results.  Claims are well-supported. It represents a complete piece of work that tackles a well-defined problem. The authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., rate of asynchronous version). **Strong.**
* **Clarity:**  The paper is generally well-written, though the phantom node construction requires careful reading. The organization is logical.  Adding more intuitive explanations for the phantom node approach would improve clarity. **Good.**
* **Originality:**  The approach of extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics is novel. The phantom node technique is a clever contribution. Related work is acknowledged, and the paper clearly differentiates itself. **Strong.**
* **Significance:**  The results are potentially impactful, enabling decentralized computation of important statistical quantities.  The work advances the state of the art in distributed estimation.  The practical demonstrations suggest potential real-world applications. **Good.**

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant problem in decentralized computation.
* Presents a novel and theoretically sound solution.
* Provides strong empirical evidence of effectiveness.
* Clear writing and organization.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Asynchronous convergence rate could be improved.
* Phantom node approach adds complexity.
* Limited comparison to alternative baseline methods.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong contribution** to the field of distributed computation and is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.  The novelty of the approach, the rigor of the analysis, and the compelling empirical results outweigh the minor weaknesses.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised regarding clarity of the phantom node approach and inclusion of baseline comparisons.
