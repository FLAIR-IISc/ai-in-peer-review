This paper tackles a challenging and important problem: extending gossip algorithms to efficiently compute U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The core idea of leveraging “phantom nodes” to reformulate the problem and enable the application of existing gossip-based averaging techniques is clever and novel. While the theoretical analysis appears rigorous (though a detailed check is beyond the scope of this review), and the experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed approach compared to a recent baseline, the overall boldness of the approach and the significance of extending gossip algorithms to this new class of problems heavily outweigh minor shortcomings.

**Summary:** The authors present randomized synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. They introduce a reformulation using “phantom nodes” that allows leveraging existing gossip algorithms. They provide convergence rate analysis and validate their approach on AUC and within-cluster point scatter estimation using real data.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics is a significant advancement. Gossip algorithms are well-established for averaging, but adapting them to functions requiring pair-wise interactions is non-trivial. The “phantom node” approach is a creative solution.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The convergence analysis appears rigorous, providing bounds that depend on both network topology and data characteristics. The connection to the spectral gap of the network is insightful.
*   **Practical Relevance:** U-statistics are fundamental in various machine learning applications. Decentralized computation of these statistics is crucial for large-scale datasets distributed across networks.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on AUC and within-cluster point scatter confirm the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms. Comparison to the existing U2-gossip algorithm provides a strong benchmark.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear definitions and notation.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Phantom Node Complexity:** The “phantom node” construction, while elegant, adds a layer of complexity to understanding the algorithm's operation. The explanation could be more intuitive.
*   **Asynchronous Analysis:** The convergence rate for the asynchronous setting (O(log t/t)) is slower than the synchronous rate (O(1/t)). While this is a common trade-off, a deeper analysis of the factors contributing to the slower convergence would be valuable.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are limited to two U-statistic examples. Expanding the experimental evaluation to other U-statistics and datasets would strengthen the results.
*   **Network Model:** The analysis relies on well-defined graph structures. Real-world networks are often more complex. Investigating the robustness of the algorithms to variations in network topology would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant gap in the literature by extending gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics.
*   The proposed approach is novel and leverages a clever reformulation of the problem.
*   The theoretical analysis provides valuable insights into the convergence behavior of the algorithms.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach and outperform the existing baseline.
*   The limitations are acknowledged, and the authors suggest directions for future work.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The phantom node construction adds complexity to the algorithm’s understanding.
*   The asynchronous convergence rate is slower than the synchronous rate.
*   The experimental evaluation is limited to two U-statistic examples.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong paper that presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of decentralized computation. The authors have successfully extended gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics, providing both theoretical guarantees and empirical validation. While the paper has some minor weaknesses, they are outweighed by its strengths. The originality and potential impact of this work are substantial. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, primarily focused on providing more intuition for the phantom node construction and expanding the experimental evaluation to include a broader range of U-statistics.
