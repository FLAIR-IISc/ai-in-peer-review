Summary
The paper tackles decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a communication network using gossip-style protocols. The key idea is to decouple two intertwined processes: (i) data propagation, by letting each node maintain and swap a single auxiliary observation Y that performs a random walk; and (ii) estimation averaging, by pairwise averaging of local estimates Z. Two algorithms are proposed: GoSta-sync (synchronous global clock) and GoSta-async (fully asynchronous with local Poisson clocks). A novel “phantom nodes” analysis lifts the process to an O(n^2)-size augmented graph used only for analysis, enabling clean characterization of the expected dynamics via block matrices built from Laplacian-based operators Wα(G). The authors prove convergence in expectation to the target U-statistic, with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async). Bounds exhibit data-dependent terms (dispersion of H) and network-dependent terms via the spectral gap (βn−1 or 1−λ2). Compared to U2-gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens), GoSta uses half the memory and fewer communications per iteration and achieves tighter rates; a refined analysis of U2-gossip is also provided. Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter over complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs demonstrate improved accuracy and variance reduction, with larger gains for better-connected graphs and larger n.

Relation to prior work
The paper builds on randomized gossip for averaging (Boyd et al.) and rumor spreading/consensus literature, and addresses a gap: efficient decentralized computation of pairwise (non-separable) statistics. The closest prior work is U2-gossip, which propagates two auxiliary samples without averaging; the present work adds an averaging layer and a new analysis framework. The paper situates rates in terms of spectral gap as is standard in gossip, connects to geographic/accelerated variants, and outlines extensions to multiple observations per node.

Quality
- Theory: The main convergence results are technically sound and plausible. The Wα(G) formulation connects cleanly to the Laplacian; the dependence 1−λ2(2)=βn−1/|E| is consistent. The asynchronous estimator uses local counters mk to recover a 1/r running average when a node updates r times; unbiasedness-in-expectation is clear. The refined U2-gossip bound clarifies when GoSta dominates.
- Scope and assumptions: Results are in expectation; no almost-sure or high-probability guarantees are provided. The analysis assumes connected, non-bipartite graphs and uniform random edge selection. The need for nodes to know |E| and their degrees to compute pk may be nontrivial in some deployments. The async rate has an unspecified constant c′(G) and depends on ||H||; the choice of norm should be stated explicitly.
- Experiments: The comparisons to U2-gossip are appropriate, and the trends match the theory (benefit grows with spectral gap and n). However, experiments include 2D grids (which are bipartite), while the theorems assume non-bipartite graphs; please reconcile this (e.g., by adding laziness/self-loops or discussing practical robustness). More baselines (e.g., sampling-based approximations to U-statistics; centralized MapReduce-like aggregation if available) would help contextualize gains. Reporting communication and memory costs per method per iteration, and scalability in wall-clock or messages to reach a fixed error, would strengthen the empirical case.

Clarity
The paper is generally well-organized, with a helpful construction of the augmented graph and clear pseudocode. Some clarity issues:
- Precisely define the matrix norm used in bounds (Frobenius/operator).
- Explicitly state the knowledge required at nodes (|E|, degrees) and how it is obtained in a decentralized manner.
- For AUC, clarify how the class-count denominator is computed/distributed (it is separable and could be handled by standard averaging).
- Minor notation density in Section 4 (B, C, S2(0)) could be streamlined; a small worked example would aid intuition.

Originality
The combination of single-sample propagation with explicit averaging for U-statistics, and the phantom-node analysis yielding spectral-gap–aware rates, appears novel and nontrivial. The async scheme with local counters is a neat adaptation. Providing a refined analysis of U2-gossip is also valuable.

Significance
Estimating pairwise statistics (AUC, within-cluster scatter, rank correlation, tests) is important in decentralized sensing and large-scale graph analytics. The proposed algorithms reduce memory/communication vs prior work and provide faster convergence, with clear dependence on network connectivity. This is likely to be useful to researchers building decentralized learning and monitoring systems, and it opens doors to optimization over U-statistics.

Pros
- Clear, principled algorithms for synchronous and asynchronous settings.
- Convergence rates with explicit network and data dependence; improved over U2-gossip.
- Simple implementation: one auxiliary observation and one edge activation per iteration.
- Solid empirical evidence across metrics and network topologies.
- Useful refined bound for U2-gossip.

Cons
- Convergence only in expectation; no high-probability or almost-sure guarantees.
- Assumes non-bipartite graphs; experiments include bipartite grids without discussing fixups.
- Async bound uses an unspecified c′(G) and an ambiguous norm; limited insight into constants.
- Requires knowledge of |E| and degrees; practicality not discussed.
- Limited baselines and lack of explicit communication/memory/runtime comparisons.

Questions for authors
- Can you add laziness or self-loops to handle bipartite graphs formally, and comment on the grid experiments?
- Which norm is used in Theorems 1–2, and can c′(G) be made explicit?
- Can you provide high-probability bounds via martingale techniques or concentration for time-averaged estimates?
- How sensitive are results to non-uniform or time-varying edge activation and to dynamic graphs?
- For AUC, how is the denominator computed in a decentralized way?

Suggestions
- Report message sizes and iterations-to-accuracy across methods.
- Discuss removing the need for global |E| via local degree gossip or online estimation of pk.
- Consider robustness to quantization, packet drops, and delays.
- Outline extensions to higher-order U-statistics and provide complexity trade-offs.

Recommendation: Weak accept. The paper makes a meaningful and technically solid contribution to decentralized estimation of pairwise statistics with improved algorithms and analysis. Addressing the noted clarity and scope issues (especially bipartite graphs and constants) would further strengthen it.

Reviewer confidence: Medium-high.
