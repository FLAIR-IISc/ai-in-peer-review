

# Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

## Summary

This paper addresses a significant gap in distributed computation: extending gossip algorithms to efficiently estimate U-statistics, which are quantities depending on pairs of observations (e.g., sample variance, AUC, Gini mean difference). The authors propose two novel gossip-based algorithms (synchronous GoSta-sync and asynchronous GoSta-async) that combine data propagation across the network with local averaging. The key innovation is a "phantom node" reformulation that enables convergence analysis. The paper demonstrates theoretically and empirically that their approach converges faster (O(1/t) vs. O(1/t) for U2-gossip but with better constants) with lower memory and communication costs compared to prior work. This work extends the applicability of gossip algorithms beyond separable functions to more complex statistics.

## Strengths

1. **Significant Technical Contribution**: The paper identifies a meaningful gap in gossip algorithms literature (existing methods cannot efficiently compute U-statistics) and provides a principled solution. The phantom node construction is elegant and enables rigorous convergence analysis.

2. **Strong Theoretical Analysis**: The convergence rates (O(1/t) for synchronous, O(log t/t) for asynchronous) are well-supported with detailed proofs in the supplementary material. The analysis correctly identifies the dependence on network connectivity (spectral gap) and data properties, which is crucial for understanding practical performance.

3. **Clear Experimental Validation**: The experiments on real datasets (SMVguide3 for AUC and Wine Quality for within-cluster point scatter) convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over U2-gossip across different network topologies (complete graph, 2D grid, Watts-Strogatz). The results align with theoretical predictions.

4. **Well-Structured Presentation**: The paper is well-organized with clear problem definition, related work, algorithm descriptions, and thorough analysis. The pseudocode for both algorithms is particularly helpful.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Comparison with Other Methods**: While the comparison with U2-gossip is strong, the paper lacks comparison with other relevant distributed methods for similar problems (e.g., distributed optimization approaches for U-statistics). A broader comparison would better establish the novelty and significance.

2. **Practical Implementation Details Missing**: The paper doesn't discuss practical considerations like handling network partitions, node failures, or how the algorithms scale to extremely large networks (e.g., >100,000 nodes). The phantom node construction, while clever for analysis, might raise implementation concerns.

3. **Theoretical Bound Interpretation**: The theoretical bounds contain complex data-dependent terms (e.g., ||h - Ûn(H)1n||). While the paper correctly notes these reflect problem difficulty, a more intuitive explanation of how these terms affect practical performance would be helpful.

4. **Limited Discussion of Limitations**: The paper doesn't thoroughly discuss scenarios where the proposed algorithms might underperform (e.g., highly asymmetric networks or when the U-statistic function H has specific properties).

## Pro and Con Arguments for Acceptance

**Pro**: The paper makes a clear, significant contribution to distributed algorithms by extending gossip methods to handle U-statistics. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, the experimental validation is strong, and the practical implications for decentralized machine learning are substantial.

**Con**: The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with other distributed methods, more detailed discussion of practical limitations, and additional implementation considerations for large-scale deployment.

## Suggestions for Improvement

1. **Expand the experimental comparison**: Include a comparison with at least one additional method from the distributed optimization literature (e.g., DGD or EXTRA for U-statistic minimization) to better establish the relative advantages.

2. **Clarify theoretical bounds**: Add a brief discussion explaining how the data-dependent terms in the bounds affect practical performance, possibly with a simple example.

3. **Address practical implementation**: Add a short subsection discussing how the algorithms would handle network partitions or node failures, and provide a more concrete discussion of scalability to very large networks.

4. **Strengthen the limitations section**: Explicitly discuss scenarios where the proposed algorithms might not be optimal (e.g., for specific types of U-statistics or network topologies) and suggest potential extensions.

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of distributed algorithms by solving a meaningful problem (U-statistic estimation) that has been overlooked in gossip literature. The theoretical analysis is sound, the experimental validation is thorough, and the practical implications are clear. While there are minor areas for improvement, the work is technically sound, well-written, and represents a significant advancement in the application of gossip algorithms to distributed statistics. The paper meets NeurIPS standards for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the suggestions above. The authors have made an important contribution that will be valuable to researchers and practitioners working on decentralized machine learning and distributed statistics.
