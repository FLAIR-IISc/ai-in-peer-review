

# Peer Review: Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics

This paper addresses an important gap in distributed computing literature: the extension of gossip algorithms to efficiently estimate U-statistics (statistics depending on pairwise observations), which are prevalent in machine learning (e.g., AUC, sample variance, Gini mean difference). The authors correctly identify that existing gossip algorithms designed for separable functions (like averages) cannot efficiently compute U-statistics, with the only prior work being U2-gossip [17] which has significant limitations.

## Summary and Relation to Prior Work
The paper builds on foundational gossip algorithms for distributed averaging [19, 2, 18], which compute statistics of the form ∑f(Xi) but cannot handle U-statistics ∑∑H(Xi,Xj). The authors' key insight is to reformulate the problem using "phantom nodes" to separate data propagation (allowing each node to estimate hi = (1/n)∑H(Xi,Xj)) from averaging (converging to the global U-statistic). This leads to two novel algorithms: GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous), with convergence rates O(1/t) and O(log t/t) respectively. The theoretical analysis carefully decomposes the convergence rate into data-dependent terms (reflecting problem hardness) and network-dependent terms (related to spectral gap), showing faster convergence on well-connected networks.

## Strengths
1. **Novel Problem Formulation:** The paper identifies a meaningful gap in distributed statistics computation and provides a well-motivated solution. U-statistics are crucial in machine learning, and distributed estimation is practically important for large-scale systems.

2. **Elegant Theoretical Approach:** The phantom node reformulation is elegant and enables clean convergence analysis. The separation of data propagation and averaging steps is particularly insightful.

3. **Strong Theoretical Results:** The convergence proofs are rigorous, with careful analysis of both data-dependent and network-dependent terms. The comparison to U2-gossip shows clear theoretical advantages (better convergence rate and lower memory/communication costs).

4. **Practical Validation:** Experiments on real datasets (SMVguide3 for AUC, Wine Quality for within-cluster point scatter) demonstrate consistent performance improvements over U2-gossip across different network topologies (complete, grid, Watts-Strogatz). The variance reduction due to averaging is particularly well-documented.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited Scope of Experimental Validation:** Only two U-statistics (AUC and within-cluster point scatter) are tested. Additional experiments with other U-statistics (e.g., Gini mean difference, Kendall tau) would strengthen the claim of broad applicability.

2. **Theoretical Comparison with U2-gossip Needs Clarification:** While the paper shows GoSta has better convergence rates, the comparison in Equation (10) is somewhat abstract. A concrete example demonstrating how the √n factor in U2-gossip's rate leads to significant practical differences would be more convincing.

3. **Implementation Details for Asynchronous Algorithm:** The paper assumes nodes know their degrees and total edges (for maintaining mk(t)), which may not be realistic in all networks. The practical implementation challenges of this counter maintenance deserve more discussion.

4. **Limited Comparison with Alternative Approaches:** The paper doesn't compare with alternative distributed approaches for U-statistics, such as using a small number of centralized iterations or other distributed methods for non-separable functions.

## Overall Assessment
This is a strong theoretical contribution that addresses a meaningful gap in distributed computation. The paper is technically sound, with rigorous analysis supported by well-designed experiments. The problem is significant for the NIPS community, as distributed statistical estimation is increasingly important in machine learning.

The main limitation is the narrow scope of experimental validation, but this doesn't undermine the core theoretical contribution. The paper makes a clear and significant advance over prior work (U2-gossip), with both better theoretical guarantees and practical performance.

## Recommendation
**Accept with minor revisions.** The paper makes a valuable contribution to distributed algorithms for machine learning. The authors should:
1. Add experiments with at least one additional U-statistic
2. Provide a concrete example demonstrating the practical impact of the theoretical rate difference between GoSta and U2-gossip
3. Discuss implementation challenges for the asynchronous algorithm's counter maintenance

The paper's technical quality, novelty, and significance meet NIPS standards. The results are important and advance the state of the art in distributed statistical computation.
