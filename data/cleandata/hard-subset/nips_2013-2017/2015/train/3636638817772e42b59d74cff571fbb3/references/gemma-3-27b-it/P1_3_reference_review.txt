## NIPS Review: Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics

**Paper Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting using gossip algorithms. Existing gossip algorithms are limited to computing averages of separable functions, and cannot handle the pairwise dependencies inherent in U-statistics (e.g., variance, AUC). The authors propose synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) that propagate local observations and average local estimates to converge to the U-statistic. They provide convergence rate analyses – O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous – dependent on both network topology (spectral gap) and data characteristics. A key technical contribution is the use of “phantom nodes” to reformulate the problem for analysis. Empirical evaluation on AUC and within-cluster scatter demonstrates the superiority of their approach over a previous U-statistic gossip algorithm (U2-gossip).

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the well-established literature on gossip algorithms for decentralized averaging (Boyd et al., Kempe et al.). It identifies a key limitation of existing algorithms – their inability to handle non-separable functions like U-statistics. The work directly addresses this gap and improves upon the initial attempt by Pelckmans and Suykens (U2-gossip) by achieving faster convergence, lower memory usage, and introducing an asynchronous variant. The paper correctly situates itself within the broader context of distributed computation and estimation.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized manner is a relevant and challenging problem with applications in various fields, as highlighted in the introduction.
* **Novel Approach:** The combination of data propagation and estimate averaging, coupled with the "phantom node" reformulation, is a clever and effective strategy.
* **Strong Theoretical Analysis:** The paper provides rigorous convergence rate analyses for both synchronous and asynchronous settings. The dependence on the spectral gap and data characteristics provides valuable insights.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the practical benefits of GoSta, showing improved performance compared to U2-gossip on real datasets and different network topologies.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The algorithm descriptions and explanations are clear and concise.
* **Asynchronous Algorithm:** The development of an asynchronous version expands the applicability of the method to more realistic, less-controlled environments.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Phantom Node Complexity:** While the phantom node reformulation is crucial for analysis, it introduces conceptual complexity. The connection between the phantom nodes and the actual algorithm operation could be made more explicit in the explanation.
* **Assumptions:** The asynchronous analysis relies on several assumptions (e.g. uniform activation probabilities), which may not hold in all real-world scenarios. Discussion of the robustness of the algorithm under violated assumptions would be helpful.
* **Limited Comparison:** While the comparison to U2-gossip is important, it would be beneficial to include comparisons with other relevant distributed estimation methods, even if they don't directly address U-statistics. This could further solidify the contribution of the paper.
* **Practical Considerations:** The paper could benefit from a discussion of practical limitations, such as the impact of network churn or node failures, and potential mitigation strategies.
* **Convergence Constant:** The convergence rates are given as O(1/t) and O(log t/t), but the constants involved (especially those related to the spectral gap and data characteristics) significantly impact the practical performance. More discussion about how these constants are affected by network size and data properties would be valuable.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles an important and previously unaddressed problem in decentralized computation.
* The proposed algorithms are novel, theoretically sound, and empirically validated.
* The convergence analysis provides valuable insights into the performance of the algorithms.
* The inclusion of an asynchronous variant enhances the practical applicability of the work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The phantom node reformulation adds complexity to the explanation.
* The analysis relies on assumptions that might not always hold in practice.
* The comparison to other methods is limited.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of decentralized computation. The authors have successfully extended gossip algorithms to efficiently compute U-statistics, providing both theoretical guarantees and empirical validation. The weaknesses are relatively minor and do not detract from the overall quality of the work.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. I recommend the authors address the points raised regarding the clarity of the phantom node explanation, the robustness of the assumptions, and the inclusion of further comparative analysis. A discussion of practical considerations and the impact of the convergence constants would also be beneficial.



**Confidence:** 8/10
