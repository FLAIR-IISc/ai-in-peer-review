- Proposes GoSta gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics (e.g., AUC, variance, Gini) filling a gap in gossip literature.
- Introduces a dual-step approach (data propagation + averaging) achieving O(1/t) convergence synchronous and O(log t / t) asynchronous.
- Presents rigorous, novel convergence proofs using the "phantom nodes" reformulation to separate propagation and averaging.
- Analyses convergence rates in terms of data-dependent hardness and network-dependent spectral gap.
- Empirical results on real datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) show faster convergence, lower variance, and reduced communication/memory versus U2-gossip.
- Experiments are thorough across network types (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz) with detailed error analysis supporting theory.
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written; algorithms and pseudocode are easy to follow.
- Mathematical context for convergence is sufficient while remaining readable; figures effectively illustrate gains.
- Key innovation and originality: fundamentally different from prior work via dual-step method and phantom node technique.
- High significance: enables distributed computation of non-separable U-statistics, important for many ML applications.
- Strengths: solves an open problem, rigorous theory with meaningful rates, practical advantages, strong experiments, elegant analysis, clear presentation.
- Weaknesses: some convergence proof details may be hard without graph-theory background.
- Weaknesses: could provide more detailed computational complexity analysis beyond memory/communication.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on extending approach to higher-order U-statistics.
- Recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, novel, well-supported empirically, and a clear advancement over prior work.
