- Proposes two decentralized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) for estimating degree-2 U-statistics using one auxiliary observation per node and gossip-based averaging.
- Introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple averaging and data propagation in the analysis.
- Proves convergence in expectation: O(1/t) for synchronous (with explicit data- and graph-dependent constants involving the spectral gap) and O((log t)/t) for asynchronous.
- Derives a refined bound for prior U2-gossip showing an unfavorable √n factor; claims lower memory/communication and faster convergence than U2-gossip.
- Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across three graph families supports theory and shows variance reduction from averaging.
- Builds on randomized gossip and spectral-gap based rates; extends to pairwise U-statistics and connects to decentralized optimization literature.
- Phantom-network analysis is novel in this context and resembles product/lifted Markov chain techniques.
- Synchronous analysis is technically sound and insightful with a useful data/network decomposition (c(G)=1−λ2(W2)).
- Asynchronous algorithm is practical but its theoretical bound is looser: c′(G) is unspecified and lacks the data-dependent decomposition.
- Convergence guarantees are only in expectation; no high-probability, almost-sure, or finite-time MSE bounds are provided.
- Technical inconsistency: claimed value 1−λ2(W2)=1/n for complete graph appears incorrect (should be 2/(n−1)); needs clarification.
- Clarity is generally good; phantom-node construction aids intuition, but some proofs are deferred and key proof steps could be sketched.
- Some typos, formatting issues, and incomplete references; assumptions (symmetric H, H(X,X)=0) need explicit discussion of necessity.
- Theorems assume non-bipartite graphs but experiments use a bipartite 2D grid—this mismatch must be reconciled (e.g., add laziness/self-loops or extend theorems).
- Practicality concern: GoSta-async requires pk=2dk/|E| (global |E| knowledge); suggest online estimation or alternative weighting (Metropolis/push-sum).
- Communication comparison should be normalized by transmitted words (U2 swaps two d-vectors vs GoSta swaps one) rather than iterations.
- Experimental scope is limited (only two datasets); broader tests over varying d, sparser/path graphs, scale-free networks, and different Watts–Strogatz p would strengthen claims.
- Strengths: simple practical algorithms, lower memory/communication, novel analysis, and empirical-theory agreement.
- Weaknesses: expectation-only bounds, unspecified async constants, bipartiteness assumption vs experiments, limited experimental breadth, and minor technical inconsistencies.
- Recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on addressing bipartite-graph assumption, clarifying async constants and spectral constant, and adding concentration results or communication-normalized comparisons.
