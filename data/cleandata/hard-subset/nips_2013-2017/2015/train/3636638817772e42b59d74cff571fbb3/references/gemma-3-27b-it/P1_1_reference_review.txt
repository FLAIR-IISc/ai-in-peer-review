## Peer Review for "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting using gossip algorithms.  U-statistics, prevalent in various machine learning tasks (AUC, variance, etc.), require pairwise interactions, which traditional gossip algorithms designed for separable functions struggle with. The authors propose both synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms, dubbed GoSta, leveraging “phantom nodes” for a novel reformulation and analysis.  They demonstrate convergence rates of O(1/t) for the synchronous version and O(log t/t) for the asynchronous version, with bounds dependent on both network topology and data characteristics. Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter estimation confirms the superiority of their approach over existing work (specifically, U2-gossip [17]).

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon the established foundation of gossip algorithms for distributed averaging (Boyd et al. 2006, Kempe et al. 2003), which have been extensively studied and refined (e.g., Duchi et al. 2011).  The core contribution lies in extending these algorithms to a non-separable function class (U-statistics). The work directly addresses the limitations of existing approaches, notably U2-gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens 2017), by achieving faster convergence and reduced communication/memory costs.  The utilization of phantom nodes for analysis is a clever technique that allows for a tractable theoretical analysis, and is a significant advancement over previous attempts.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The extension of gossip algorithms to U-statistics is a significant contribution. The phantom node reformulation is a creative and effective analytical tool.
* **Theoretical Rigor:**  The convergence analysis provides clear bounds, showcasing the algorithm’s performance dependency on network connectivity (spectral gap) and data characteristics. The rigorous proof helps understand the theoretical limitations and guides further improvements.
* **Practical Relevance:** U-statistics are used in a wide range of applications, making the development of efficient decentralized algorithms highly valuable.  The experimental results demonstrate the algorithm’s practical performance on real-world datasets.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1 & 2) are clear and concise.
* **Comparison to Baseline:** A fair comparison against the existing U2-gossip algorithm highlights the improvements achieved by GoSta.
* **Asynchronous Version:** Providing both synchronous and asynchronous algorithms increases the practical applicability of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Analysis:** While rigorous, the analysis involving phantom nodes can be difficult to follow without a strong background in spectral graph theory. While the phantom node construction is clearly explained, the reasoning behind it could be further motivated for readers less familiar with the technique.
* **Network Model:** The assumption of undirected graphs, while common, might not always hold in real-world scenarios. Exploring the performance under directed or weighted graphs could be beneficial.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The influence of the network parameters (degree distribution, spectral gap) on convergence speed isn’t thoroughly explored in the experiments. A more systematic investigation of the algorithm's behavior across different network topologies would strengthen the results.
* **Limited Baseline Comparison:** While the comparison to U2-gossip is valuable, contrasting the performance against standard distributed averaging techniques adapted for U-statistic estimation (even if suboptimal) could offer a broader perspective.
* **Practical Considerations:** The authors mention adaptive communication schemes as future work.  Discussing the practical challenges of implementing these algorithms in real-world distributed systems (e.g., dealing with node failures, message delays, security concerns) would add further value.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant problem with a novel and theoretically sound approach.
* The convergence analysis is rigorous and provides valuable insights into the algorithm’s behavior.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of GoSta compared to existing methods.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presents its contributions.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The analysis is complex and may be inaccessible to some readers.
* The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, particularly regarding the influence of network topology.
* The discussion of practical implementation challenges is limited.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a strong contribution to the field of distributed estimation. The problem addressed is important, the proposed solution is novel and well-motivated, and the theoretical analysis is rigorous.  While the complexity of the analysis and the limited scope of the experimental evaluation are minor weaknesses, they do not outweigh the significant strengths of the paper.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.**  I recommend the authors:

1.  Provide a more intuitive explanation of the phantom node construction.
2.  Conduct a more systematic investigation of the algorithm's performance across different network topologies.
3.  Briefly discuss practical considerations for deploying these algorithms in real-world systems.
