Summary
The paper proposes two randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics: a synchronous version (GoSta-sync) and an asynchronous version (GoSta-async). Each node i maintains an auxiliary sample Y_i that performs a random walk and a local estimate Z_i that is averaged both temporally (running average of H(X_i, Y_i)) and spatially (pairwise averaging across a randomly sampled edge). The analysis introduces a “phantom-node” construction to decouple data propagation from estimate averaging. Theorems establish convergence in expectation to the true U-statistic: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with constants depending on a “spectral gap” term and data-dependent norms. The paper compares to U2-gossip [17], claiming better convergence, lower memory, and lower communication, and provides experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across several graph families.

Strengths
- Problem is relevant: computing non-separable pairwise quantities in decentralized settings is important and underexplored.
- Clear algorithmic idea: combine random-walk-based data propagation with averaging; the phantom-node analysis is a neat device.
- Theoretical guarantees: expectation convergence with explicit rates and graph/data dependence; asynchronous case handled.
- Empirical results broadly support improved performance over U2-gossip; variance reduction due to averaging is well illustrated.
- Practicality: algorithms use simple, local operations and require modest state per node (one auxiliary sample).

Weaknesses and concerns (many minor but numerous; some technical)
- Spectral gap constant appears incorrect in multiple places. With W_α(G) = I − 2/(α|E|) L(G), one has 1 − λ2(W2) = β2/|E|, where β2 is the second smallest Laplacian eigenvalue. The paper states 1 − λ2(2) = β_{n−1}/|E| and claims for the complete graph 1 − λ2(2) = 1/n. For K_n, β2 = n and |E| = n(n−1)/2, so 1 − λ2(W2) = 2/(n−1), not 1/n. Please fix the indexing, the statement, and the complete-graph example.
- Norms are unspecified for matrices. Theorems use ||H − h 1_n^T|| and ||H|| without defining whether this is Frobenius, operator, or max norm. This affects constants and interpretability.
- Asynchronous probability pk: Under “Draw (i, j) uniformly at random from E” for undirected E, P(node k selected) = d_k/|E|. The paper sets pk = 2 d_k/|E| (Algorithm 2, line 1), implying either ordered edges or a mismatch. This inconsistency should be resolved and used consistently in both algorithm and proof.
- Justification of unbiasedness in async: The argument “E[m_k(t)] = t, therefore unbiased” is imprecise. The key property is that at a node’s k-th activation, p_i m_i equals k, so the step-size is 1/k. Please state this directly and avoid expectations of reciprocals ambiguity.
- Conditions on graph: Non-bipartite graphs are required but not motivated. In practice, pairwise gossip often adds laziness or Metropolis weights to handle bipartite graphs; remarking on this would improve robustness and scope.
- Comparison baseline scope: Only U2-gossip is compared. There exist accelerated/averaging schemes (e.g., Chebyshev acceleration, geographic gossip) and push-sum for directed/asymmetric settings that could be extended to pairwise kernels; at minimum, discuss why they do or do not apply and whether such accelerations could speed your W_α mixing.
- Communication/memory cost claims are qualitative. Please quantify per-iteration communication (number of reals transmitted; size of auxiliary observation) and memory (one d-dimensional vector vs two for U2-gossip) and report total communication to reach a target error.
- AUC formulation and decentralization: The kernel requires scaling by counts of positive and negative samples (denominator in Eq. (3)). How are these counts obtained in a decentralized way? If this normalization is embedded in H, nodes need global counts (or an auxiliary gossip to compute them). This practical detail is not addressed.
- U-statistic definition: You use 1/n^2 sum including i = j but enforce H(X,X) = 0. This is fine but nonstandard; it would be cleaner to either strictly exclude diagonal terms or clearly justify the choice throughout proofs and experiments.
- Clarity issues and formatting errors:
  - Numerous hyphenation/line-break artifacts (e.g., “lo- cal,” “Our results generalize…” broken sentence after (4)).
  - Algorithm numbering and punctuation inconsistent (Algorithm 1 has misnumbered steps and stray “:”).
  - “need not to be aware” → “need not be aware.”
  - “a rate 1 Poisson process” → “a rate-1 Poisson process.”
  - Some equations lack punctuation or have missing brackets; consistency in transpose symbols and vector orientation could be improved.
  - Figures/Tables referenced (Table 1, Figures 2–3) not present; ensure captions and axes clearly state units and y-scale (error metric).
- The refined U2-gossip bound (Eq. (10)) has a √n factor; a brief derivation or pointer would help. Also, explain the regimes where 1/(1 − λ2(1)^2) could compensate.
- Reproducibility: No code, seeds, or detailed hyperparameters (e.g., number of iterations, stopping criteria) are provided. Exact graph generation parameters for Watts–Strogatz are given, but not the random seeds or multiple realizations statistics beyond “50 runs.”
- References are incomplete and inconsistently formatted: many lack years, venues, page numbers; some entries have empty fields. Please fix to meet NeurIPS style.

Quality
The core algorithms are simple and likely correct; the analysis framework is interesting. However, there are technical inaccuracies (spectral gap constants), underspecified norms, and a few modeling inconsistencies (pk). Proofs are deferred; given the issues above, I am not fully confident without the supplement.

Clarity
Readable overall, but marred by many formatting and grammar issues. Several key definitions (matrix norms) are missing; some arguments are hand-wavy (async unbiasedness). The paper needs careful copy-editing.

Originality
Extends gossip to pairwise kernels with a novel phantom-node analysis and provides async variants. Prior U2-gossip exists; the combination of averaging with propagation and the analysis appear incrementally novel but meaningful.

Significance
Useful step for decentralized pairwise statistics; potential impact in sensor networks and distributed ML. That said, the empirical section is modest in scope, and the mismatch in spectral constants undermines confidence in the theoretical claims as stated.

Pros
- Addresses a relevant, underexplored problem with simple, implementable algorithms.
- Provides synchronous and asynchronous analyses with explicit rates.
- Empirical results show clear gains over a prior method.

Cons
- Technical inaccuracies in spectral gap constants; norms unspecified.
- Inconsistencies in async selection probability pk and justification of unbiasedness.
- Many presentation/formatting issues; references incomplete.
- Limited baselines and ablations; communication/memory advantages not quantified.
- Practical details for AUC normalization in decentralized setting not addressed.

Recommendation
Lean reject. The paper has promising ideas and likely correct core results, but the accumulation of technical inaccuracies, inconsistencies, and presentation deficiencies should be addressed before publication. With corrections to the spectral gap statements, clarified norms, consistent async modeling, improved experimental rigor, and thorough copy-editing, this work could become a strong submission.
