Summary
The paper tackles decentralized estimation of second-order U-statistics over a network via gossip. It proposes two algorithms:
- GoSta-sync: synchronous pairwise-averaging gossip augmented with single-observation propagation (each node keeps an auxiliary sample Yk that performs coupled random walks via swaps). Each node updates a running average of H(Xk, Yk), then the selected pair averages their estimates and swaps Y.
- GoSta-async: an asynchronous version where only the activated pair updates, with per-node counters mk to debias the running average.

The analysis introduces a “phantom network” of size O(n^2) to decouple averaging from data propagation and derives convergence in expectation to the U-statistic, with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t/t) (async). The rates depend on data dispersion terms and the spectral gap of the network (via Wα(G) matrices). The approach is compared theoretically and empirically to U2-gossip [Pelckmans & Suykens], claiming better rates and reduced communication/memory.

Relation to prior work is appropriate regarding classical gossip averaging and separable functions, and the niche of pairwise U-statistics.

Strengths
- Addresses a relevant and nontrivial gap: distributed computation of U-statistics using gossip, with both synchronous and asynchronous variants.
- The “phantom nodes” analytical device is neat and potentially reusable.
- Clear high-level algorithms; simple local state and communication (one auxiliary variable) compared to U2-gossip.
- Convergence-in-expectation guarantees with problem- and graph-dependent constants; the synchronous O(1/t) bound is reasonable.
- Empirical comparisons on two tasks (AUC and within-cluster scatter) across multiple graph topologies support the practical value.

Weaknesses and detailed issues
Technical correctness/clarity:
- Asynchronous selection probability pk: Algorithm 2 sets pk = 2 dk / |E| for uniform edge sampling. The probability that a uniformly drawn edge is incident to node k is dk / |E|, not 2 dk / |E|. As written, pk can exceed 1 (e.g., star graph center), which is impossible for a probability and directly affects the debiasing scheme (lines 4–7). This is a substantial flaw that needs correction and re-analysis.
- Use of Wα(G): Wα(G) is defined only for α > 1, yet the analysis repeatedly uses W1(G) (for swaps). Either relax the definition or justify α = 1; as is, this is inconsistent.
- Spectral gap identification: The paper states 1 − λ2(2) = βn−1/|E| and calls βn−1 the “second smallest” Laplacian eigenvalue. Standard notation is β1 = 0 ≤ β2 ≤ … ≤ βn; the second smallest is β2. For W2 = I − (1/|E|) L, 1 − λ2(W2) = β2/|E|, not βn−1/|E|. This indexing/sign error appears central to the constants and should be fixed.
- Norms in Theorem 1: The bound uses ‖H − h 1n^T‖ where ‖·‖ was defined as the ℓ2 vector norm. For matrices, which norm is used (spectral, Frobenius)? This ambiguity undermines the interpretability of the rate constants.
- Necessity of non-bipartite assumption: For W2(G) = I − (1/|E|)L, connectedness implies λ2(W2) < 1. The non-bipartite condition appears unnecessary here (unlike for non-lazy random walks). Please clarify or adjust assumptions.
- Asynchronous model consistency: The text mixes a node-Poisson-clock model with “uniform over edges” activation. These are not equivalent unless one enforces specific edge weights. The paper assumes knowledge of |E| to set pk, which is often unrealistic in decentralized settings. No discussion is provided on how nodes learn |E|.
- Running-average updates in GoSta-sync vs averaging step: The block-matrix derivation assumes a particular order of operations and linearity; while plausible, the mapping to M1(t), M2(t), M3(t) is compressed. Given the centrality of this analysis, more detail is needed (beyond supplement) to ensure there is no hidden bias from averaging after injecting the 1/t H term.
- AUC kernel: θ is declared θ ∈ Rd−1 while X ∈ Rd; this mismatch is unexplained. Also, while the provided kernel seems symmetric, the symmetry should be explicitly verified from Eq. (3) to satisfy the stated assumptions H symmetric and H(x,x)=0.

Scope and completeness:
- Guarantees are only in expectation; no almost-sure or high-probability bounds are provided. Variance behavior is empirically discussed but not analyzed.
- Communication/time complexity: No rigorous accounting of message and memory costs per iteration versus convergence speed is provided. Empirical plots show iterations, not wall-clock or message complexity—important for gossip.
- Experimental coverage: The main comparisons use only GoSta-sync vs U2-gossip. The asynchronous algorithm is only briefly compared to sync on one setting; no stress tests on highly heterogeneous graphs (e.g., stars) or larger n. Claims about spectral gap influence are not quantitatively validated (Table 1 and figures are not included here; no systematic sweep of spectral gaps).
- Practical assumptions: GoSta-async assumes nodes know |E| and their degree; also, uniform edge sampling typically requires centralized coordination or extra mechanisms—this should be discussed.
- Proofs are deferred to the supplement; given the novelty, at least proof sketches for Theorems 1–2 would help readers assess technical soundness.

Presentation/formatting:
- Numerous minor grammatical issues (“need not to be aware”, “perfomance”), hyphenation artifacts (“lo-cal”), and spacing (“U -statistics”) throughout.
- References are incomplete and inconsistent (missing years, venues, pages; sometimes only a title or volume). This is below NeurIPS standards.
- Notation slips: mixing βn−1 vs β2; using W1 despite α > 1; ambiguous matrix norms.
- Figures/tables referenced in text (Table 1, Figures 2–3) are not present here; ensure they are legible, with axes labeled, and include confidence intervals.

Questions for authors
1. Please correct pk in Algorithm 2 and re-derive Theorem 2 accordingly. If you intended a different activation model, specify it precisely.
2. Clarify the precise matrix norm used in Theorem 1, and fix the spectral-gap indexing.
3. Is non-bipartiteness necessary for your results with W2? If so, why?
4. Can you provide high-probability or almost-sure convergence statements, or at least variance bounds?
5. How do nodes obtain |E| in a decentralized setting? Can pk be estimated online without bias?
6. Please report message complexity to achieve a given ε-accuracy versus U2-gossip, and include asynchronous experiments on heterogeneous graphs.

Pros
- Novel and useful extension of gossip to U-statistics.
- Elegant analysis idea (phantom nodes).
- Simple algorithms with promising empirical behavior.

Cons
- Technical inconsistencies (pk, W1 usage, spectral-gap indexing, norm ambiguity).
- Unrealistic/underspecified assumptions for async activation.
- Limited experimental and complexity evaluation.
- Presentation and reference quality need significant polishing.

Recommendation
Reject. While the core idea is interesting and potentially impactful, the paper currently contains several technical and presentation issues that undermine confidence in the results and their applicability. With corrections to the asynchronous scheme, tighter/clearer analysis, and stronger empirical/complexity validation, this work could be a strong submission in a future iteration.

Confidence
High.
