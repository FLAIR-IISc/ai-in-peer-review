

# Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

This paper proposes gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics, extending existing gossip algorithms that can only handle separable functions of observations. The authors introduce synchronous and asynchronous versions (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) that maintain local estimates of the U-statistic while propagating data across the network. They claim to achieve faster convergence rates (O(1/t) and O(log t/t)) with lower memory and communication costs compared to prior work [17].

## Major Issues

The most severe issue is a fundamental mathematical error in the paper's definition of U-statistics. The authors define the U-statistic as:

Ûn(H) = (1/n²) ∑_{i,j=1}^n H(Xi, Xj)

However, the standard definition of a degree-two U-statistic (as cited in [12] and commonly used in statistics) is:

U = (1/(n(n-1))) ∑_{i≠j} H(Xi, Xj)

The paper claims "the usual definition of U-statistic differs slightly from (1) by a factor of n/(n-1)," which is incorrect. With H(X,X)=0 as assumed, the paper's definition is equivalent to:

Ûn(H) = (1/n²) ∑_{i≠j} H(Xi, Xj) = ((n-1)/n) * (1/(n(n-1))) ∑_{i≠j} H(Xi, Xj) = ((n-1)/n) * U

Thus, the paper's definition differs from the standard U-statistic by a factor of (n-1)/n, not n/(n-1). This is a critical error that affects the entire paper's theoretical foundation and experimental validation. The authors should have used the standard definition to maintain consistency with the statistical literature.

## Additional Concerns

1. **Notation inconsistencies**: The paper uses "d" for both data dimensionality (X ⊆ R^d) and node degree (di = |{j : (i, j) ∈ E}|), which is highly confusing and unprofessional. This should be fixed immediately.

2. **Mathematical errors in convergence rates**: The paper claims GoSta-sync achieves O(1/t) convergence, but the bound in Theorem 1 contains an exponential term (e^{-ct}) that doesn't align with the stated rate. The convergence rate should be clearly specified as O(1/t) plus exponentially decaying terms.

3. **Inconsistent U-statistic definition**: The paper uses both "U-statistic" and "U -statistics" (with a space) inconsistently throughout the paper.

4. **Algorithm 1 notation**: In Algorithm 1, step 4 should be "Zp ← ((t-1)/t)Zp + (1/t)H(Xp, Yp)" for clarity, rather than "t-1t" which is ambiguous.

5. **Experimental claims**: The paper claims "GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip on every network" without providing statistical significance tests to support this claim, which is essential for scientific rigor.

6. **Incomplete references**: Several references are incomplete (e.g., "Modern Graph Theory, volume 184, Béla Bollobás, , , " with missing publication details).

7. **Theoretical comparison**: The paper claims "we achieve faster convergence together with lower memory and communication costs" compared to [17], but doesn't clearly quantify the improvement or provide a theoretical comparison of the rates.

8. **Inconsistent notation in proofs**: The paper uses "1n" for the vector of ones, which is confusing and should be standardized to "1" or "1_n" for clarity.

## Minor Issues

- The paper states "the spectral gap of the network graph [3]" without defining it explicitly, though it's defined in the supplementary material.
- In the AUC definition (equation 3), the indicator function should be I{`i(θ>Xi) > `j(θ>Xj)} rather than I{`i(θ>Xi) > -`j(θ>Xj)}.
- The paper uses "e>1" for the first standard basis vector, which is non-standard notation (should be "e_1" or "e_1^T").
- The paper claims the convergence bound features "data-dependent terms" but doesn't provide intuition for their significance.

## Strengths

- The phantom node approach for reformulating the problem is interesting and potentially useful for other distributed estimation problems.
- The algorithms are well-motivated and address a real gap in distributed estimation of non-separable functions.
- The experimental results on AUC and within-cluster point scatter provide some empirical validation.

## Conclusion

While the paper addresses an interesting problem and has some promising ideas, the fundamental mathematical error in the definition of U-statistics undermines the entire contribution. The paper's core premise is based on an incorrect formulation of the problem, which affects both the theoretical analysis and the experimental validation. The notation inconsistencies and other minor issues further detract from the paper's quality.

I recommend rejection. The authors should revise the paper to correct the U-statistic definition, resolve the notational inconsistencies, and provide a more rigorous theoretical comparison to prior work before resubmission. The problem is interesting, but the current paper is not scientifically sound due to the critical mathematical error.
