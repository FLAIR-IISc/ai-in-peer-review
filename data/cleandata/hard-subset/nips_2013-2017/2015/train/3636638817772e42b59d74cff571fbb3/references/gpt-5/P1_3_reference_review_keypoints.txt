- Problem: Decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics where each node holds one observation and can only communicate with neighbors.

- Algorithms: Proposes two randomized gossip algorithms — GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (fully asynchronous) — combining neighbor swaps of auxiliary observations with local averaging.

- Analysis technique: Introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple averaging and propagation dynamics.

- Theoretical guarantees: In connected, non-bipartite graphs, both algorithms’ local estimates converge in expectation to the global U-statistic.

- Rates: GoSta-sync achieves O(1/t) convergence in expectation with explicit constants; GoSta-async achieves O(log t / t) convergence in expectation.

- Network dependence: Convergence rates depend on data-dispersion terms and the graph spectral gap, aligning with classic consensus analyses.

- Comparison to prior work: Requires only one auxiliary observation per node and one edge activation per iteration (vs two for U2-gossip); authors derive a refined bound for U2-gossip revealing an extra √n factor.

- Empirics: Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs show faster convergence and lower across-node variance for GoSta.

- Strength — relevance: The problem is central to many ML tasks (AUC, Kendall τ, within-cluster scatter) and timely for decentralized settings.

- Strength — algorithms: Simple, implementable protocols with low per-iteration communication and memory requirements.

- Strength — theory: Provides interpretable, explicit convergence rates and a better synchronous rate than U2-gossip.

- Strength — analysis novelty: Phantom-graph construction is clean and potentially useful elsewhere.

- Strength — empirical consistency: Results consistently favor GoSta over U2-gossip across tasks and topologies.

- Weakness — probabilistic guarantees: Guarantees are in expectation only; no high-probability or almost-sure bounds provided.

- Weakness — asynchronous bound: GoSta-async’s O(log t / t) bound is looser and hides constants c′(G); tightness and possible O(1/t) achievability are not discussed.

- Weakness — graph/selection assumptions: Requires non-bipartite graphs and uniform random edge selection; fixes for bipartite or time-varying edge models are not fully addressed.

- Weakness — knowledge requirement: GoSta-async needs pk = 2dk/|E| (hence |E|), which may be unknown locally; no practical online estimation strategy is provided.

- Weakness — comparison fairness: Comparisons could be normalized by communication/computation (H evaluations, bytes) or wall-clock time to better isolate efficiency.

- Weakness — experimental scope: Only two U-statistics/tasks and moderate-scale datasets; lacks larger n/d experiments and broader U-statistic types (e.g., Kendall τ).

- Clarity issues: Minor notational clarifications needed (explicit H matrix, norms, block-matrix shapes) and several typos to fix.

- Originality: Problem setting and averaging+swap design with phantom node analysis are novel relative to limited prior work.

- Significance: Decentralized computation of U-statistics is impactful and could be a building block for pairwise decentralized optimization.

- Pros for acceptance: Important problem, simple improved algorithms, solid interpretable theory, and consistent empirical gains.

- Cons against acceptance: Expectation-only guarantees, weaker asynchronous bounds, limited experiments, and practical gaps (|E| knowledge, bipartite/time-varying graphs, robustness).

- Questions for authors: Can you provide high-probability or almost-sure guarantees? Can pk be estimated online without bias? How do results extend to bipartite or time-varying graphs? Can you report error vs total H evaluations and bytes communicated? Any results on additional U-statistics or larger-scale settings?

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
