## Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of distributed U-statistic estimation in decentralized networks using gossip algorithms. Existing gossip algorithms are limited to separable functions, hindering the computation of statistics like variance or AUC which require pairwise interactions. The authors propose both synchronous and asynchronous gossip-based algorithms ("GoSta") and prove convergence rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) respectively. They achieve this through a clever reformulation involving "phantom nodes" to model data propagation and averaging separately.  The paper demonstrates improved convergence and lower communication/memory costs compared to prior work (U2-gossip [17]) and validates the approach through experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter estimation.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Extending gossip algorithms to handle non-separable U-statistics is a significant contribution, opening up a broader range of decentralized estimation problems.
* **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis, including the convergence proofs, appears rigorous and well-motivated. The phantom node reformulation is a clever technique to analyze the algorithm. The connection to the spectral gap of the network graph provides valuable insight into performance dependencies.
* **Practical Relevance:** The problem tackled has real-world applications in decentralized data analysis (sensor networks, peer-to-peer systems, etc.).  The experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter demonstrate practical applicability.
* **Comparison to Prior Work:** A clear and compelling comparison to U2-gossip [17] is presented, highlighting the benefits of GoSta in terms of convergence and resource requirements.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and contributions.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of the Phantom Node Approach:** While insightful, the phantom node reformulation adds complexity to understanding the algorithm.  The justification for this approach, while present, could be made more intuitive. The connection between the phantom nodes and the actual implementation in Algorithm 1 might not be immediately clear to all readers.
* **Limited Experimental Scope:** The experiments are limited to two applications and a relatively small set of network topologies. While these experiments are valuable, expanding them to more diverse datasets and network structures would strengthen the conclusions.  The experiments focus heavily on comparison to U2-gossip; adding comparisons to other relevant distributed estimation methods (even if not gossip-based) would be beneficial.
* **Asynchronous Convergence Rate:** The O(log t/t) convergence rate for the asynchronous setting, while reasonable, is less compelling than the O(1/t) rate for the synchronous case. Further investigation into improving the asynchronous rate could be valuable.
* **Practical Considerations for Large Networks:** While the analysis discusses spectral gap dependence, the practical implications for very large, complex networks (e.g., social networks) aren't fully explored.  Scaling the algorithm to extremely large datasets might pose challenges not fully addressed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of distributed estimation. The problem is well-motivated, the proposed solution is technically sound, and the experimental results are promising. The novelty of extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics is significant, and the theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for understanding the algorithm's behavior. While the phantom node approach adds complexity and the experiments could be more extensive, these are relatively minor weaknesses. 

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and significant extension of gossip algorithms.
* Rigorous theoretical analysis with clear convergence bounds.
* Demonstrated improvement over existing methods (U2-gossip).
* Practical relevance with compelling experimental validation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Complexity of the phantom node formulation.
* Limited experimental scope.
* Asynchronous convergence rate is less compelling than the synchronous rate.

**Rating:**

**Strong Accept (8/10)** - This paper is a solid contribution that meets the NIPS standards for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  Minor revisions to improve the clarity of the phantom node explanation and expand the experimental evaluation would further strengthen the paper.



**Review Criteria Breakdown:**

* **Quality:** Excellent. The paper is technically sound and claims are well-supported.
* **Clarity:** Good.  The writing is generally clear, but the phantom node approach could benefit from further explanation.
* **Originality:** Excellent. The extension of gossip algorithms to U-statistics is a novel contribution.
* **Significance:** Excellent. The results are important and have the potential to impact the field of decentralized estimation.
