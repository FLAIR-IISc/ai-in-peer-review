- Paper extends gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics via GoSta (synchronous and asynchronous) using a novel "phantom nodes" approach.  
- Proves convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with dependence on data norms and network spectral gap.  
- Builds on prior gossip literature and shows theoretical advantages over U2-gossip in rate, communication, and memory.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and the phantom node construction enables clean convergence proofs.  
- Experiments (AUC and within-cluster scatter on SMVguide3 and Wine Quality) validate theoretical claims and show lower error and variance than U2-gossip.  
- Network topology experiments (complete, 2D grid, Watts–Strogatz) illustrate the role of network connectivity in convergence.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and presents algorithms clearly with pseudocode.  
- Lacks explicit quantification of communication complexity in bits per iteration, especially for high-dimensional data.  
- The practical advantage of the asynchronous O(log t / t) rate over synchronous O(1/t) is not sufficiently justified.  
- Experimental scope is limited to two U-statistics; more diverse statistics or topologies would strengthen generality claims.  
- Some technical comparisons (e.g., detailed U2-gossip analysis) are deferred to the supplementary material rather than the main text.  
- Suggested improvements: add a bits-per-iteration communication complexity table, discuss when asynchronous is preferable, include another U-statistic experiment (e.g., Gini mean difference), and expand phantom node explanation in the main text.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — novel, theoretically sound, practically validated, and a significant advance over state-of-the-art.
