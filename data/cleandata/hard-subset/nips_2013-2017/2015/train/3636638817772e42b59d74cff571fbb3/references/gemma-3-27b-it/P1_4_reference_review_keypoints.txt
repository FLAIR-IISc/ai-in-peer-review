- Problem: Distributed estimation of U-statistics is important and hard for traditional gossip algorithms.  
- Proposal: Introduces randomized synchronous and asynchronous GoSta algorithms that combine data propagation and averaging.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Proves convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous) with dependence on network topology and data.  
- Phantom nodes: Uses a “phantom node” reformulation to facilitate analysis, but the explanation is somewhat convoluted.  
- Empirical results: GoSta outperforms U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter across different network topologies.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on gossip averaging literature and improves on U2-gossip in memory, communication, and synchronicity; references spectral graph theory for connectivity effects.  
- Strengths: Addresses an important problem, presents a novel approach, offers strong theory, includes an asynchronous variant, and is clearly written.  
- Weaknesses: Phantom node explanation lacks intuition, asynchronous rate is weaker, baseline comparisons are limited, practical deployment issues are not discussed, and datasets are small.  
- Pro arguments: Significant, well-motivated contribution with solid theory and supporting experiments.  
- Con arguments: Needs clearer phantom-node exposition, improved async analysis, broader baselines, and more practical discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: Clarify the phantom node explanation, provide deeper discussion/possible improvements of asynchronous convergence, and add a centralized baseline with communication constraints.
