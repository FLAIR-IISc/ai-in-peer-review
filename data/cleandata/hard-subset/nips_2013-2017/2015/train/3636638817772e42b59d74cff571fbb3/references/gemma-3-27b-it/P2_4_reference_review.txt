## Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting using gossip algorithms. Existing gossip algorithms are limited to separable functions, making them unsuitable for U-statistics which involve averaging over pairs of observations. The authors propose both synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms based on propagating local observations and averaging local estimates. They provide convergence rate analyses, showing O(1/t) and O(log t/t) convergence for the synchronous and asynchronous versions respectively, dependent on both data characteristics and network topology.  They introduce a clever "phantom node" reformulation to aid their analysis and demonstrate the superiority of their approach through experiments on AUC and within-cluster point scatter estimation.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Computing U-statistics in a distributed manner is a relevant and challenging problem with applications in diverse areas like machine learning and data mining.
* **Novel Approach:** The proposed algorithms extend the capabilities of gossip algorithms to a non-separable function class, significantly broadening their applicability.
* **Strong Theoretical Analysis:** The paper provides rigorous convergence analysis for both synchronous and asynchronous settings, establishing clear bounds on the convergence rate. The inclusion of data-dependent and network-dependent terms in the bounds is a strong point, highlighting the algorithm's adaptability.
* **Clever Reformulation:** The use of "phantom nodes" is a creative and effective technique for simplifying the analysis and gaining insights into the algorithm’s behavior.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed algorithms compared to a baseline (U2-gossip) on real-world datasets, confirming the advantages claimed in the theoretical analysis.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the core ideas accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Analysis:** While strong, the theoretical analysis is quite involved and relies on concepts from spectral graph theory that may not be familiar to all readers. A more intuitive explanation of how the network properties affect convergence would be helpful.
* **Limited Comparison:**  While U2-gossip is a natural comparison, the paper would benefit from comparisons to other relevant distributed estimation algorithms (even if they aren’t specifically designed for U-statistics).  This would further establish the unique contribution of the proposed algorithms.
* **Asynchronous Results:** The convergence rate for the asynchronous setting (O(log t/t)) is slower than the synchronous setting (O(1/t)). While this is often the case, a more in-depth discussion of the trade-offs between synchrony and asynchrony, and potential techniques to mitigate the slower convergence, would be valuable.
* **Practical Considerations:** The paper lacks a detailed discussion of the practical considerations for implementing the algorithms in real-world scenarios, such as handling node failures or varying network conditions.



**Review of Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and a complete analysis. The authors demonstrate careful consideration of both strengths and weaknesses of their approach.  The use of the phantom node technique is a particularly strong indicator of the quality of the work.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is generally clearly written, although the theoretical sections require some background knowledge.  The organization is logical, and the notation is consistent.
* **Originality:** The approach of extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics is novel, and the authors clearly differentiate their work from existing literature. The phantom node formulation is also an original contribution.
* **Significance:** The results are significant because they address a practical problem in distributed estimation and expand the capabilities of a widely used algorithmic framework (gossip algorithms).  The potential applications in areas like machine learning and data mining are substantial.

**Arguments for and against acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Solves an important and previously unaddressed problem in distributed estimation.
* Provides strong theoretical guarantees and empirical validation.
* Introduces a novel and effective algorithmic approach.
* Advances the state-of-the-art in gossip algorithms.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The theoretical analysis is complex and may be difficult for some readers.
* The comparison to existing algorithms is limited.
* The slower convergence rate of the asynchronous algorithm could be a concern.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept** for NIPS. The authors present a novel and theoretically sound approach to a significant problem in distributed estimation. While the analysis is complex and the comparison to other algorithms could be more extensive, the originality and potential impact of the work are substantial. The paper meets all four evaluation criteria at a high level.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points mentioned above, particularly expanding the related work discussion and providing more intuitive explanations of the theoretical results.
