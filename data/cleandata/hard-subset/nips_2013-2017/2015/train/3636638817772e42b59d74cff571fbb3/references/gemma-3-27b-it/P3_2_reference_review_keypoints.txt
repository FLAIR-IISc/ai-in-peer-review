- Tackles distributed estimation of U-statistics using gossip; proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async.
- Improves over prior work ([17]) in convergence rate, memory usage, and communication cost.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, using spectral graph theory; bounds include data- and network-dependent terms.
- GoSta-sync achieves O(1/t) convergence; GoSta-async converges at O(log t / t), i.e., slower.
- Introduces a "phantom node" reformulation to facilitate analysis, which feels contrived.
- Algorithms require lower memory and communication, making them practical for resource-constrained networks.
- Empirical results (AUC and within-cluster scatter) show GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip across several topologies.
- Inclusion of an asynchronous algorithm broadens applicability to realistic, unsynchronized networks.
- Paper is generally well written and clearly organized.
- Phantom nodes add an extra abstraction that may confuse readers; a direct analysis might be preferable.
- Need more discussion on trade-offs between synchronization and the slower async convergence rate.
- Experimental scope is limited (only two U-statistics and three network topologies).
- Lack of analysis/discussion on sensitivity to network hyperparameters (edge probabilities, network size).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses significant problem, presents novel/theoretically sound algorithms with strong empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: phantom-node complexity, slower async rate, and limited experimental evaluation.
- Requests clearer, intuitive explanation mapping phantom nodes back to original algorithm behavior.
- Asks for clarification on practical impact of slower GoSta-async and when asynchronicity is preferable.
- Queries whether there are theoretical limits on which U-statistics can be efficiently estimated.
- Asks for analysis of performance sensitivity to network parameters.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify phantom nodes, elaborate sync vs async trade-offs, expand experiments, and discuss sensitivity.
