

# NIPS Peer Review: "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

## Summary
This paper addresses a significant gap in decentralized computation: the inability of standard gossip algorithms to efficiently compute U-statistics (quantities depending on pairs of observations, such as sample variance, AUC, and Gini mean difference). The authors propose "GoSta" algorithms (both synchronous and asynchronous) that extend gossip frameworks to handle U-statistics. Their key innovation is a novel "phantom nodes" approach for analysis, enabling them to prove convergence rates of O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous settings. The paper builds upon existing gossip literature (e.g., [2, 10, 15]) while addressing limitations of the only prior work on U-statistics [17] (U2-gossip). The authors demonstrate superior performance through experiments on AUC and within-cluster point scatter estimation using real datasets.

## Strengths

**Originality and Significance:** This work is highly original in addressing a clear gap in distributed computing literature. U-statistics are fundamental in machine learning (AUC, variance, etc.), yet no prior gossip algorithms could efficiently compute them. The paper makes a significant contribution by extending gossip algorithms to this important class of statistics. The problem is well-motivated, and the solution is both theoretically sound and practically relevant for decentralized applications.

**Theoretical Quality:** The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-executed. The phantom node construction is clever and enables clean convergence proofs. The convergence rates are well-established with clear dependencies on both data properties (through the norm terms) and network connectivity (through the spectral gap). The comparison to U2-gossip [17] is particularly compelling, with the authors showing a theoretical advantage in both convergence rate and communication/memory complexity.

**Experimental Validation:** The experiments are well-designed and directly address the theoretical claims. The comparison to U2-gossip on real datasets (SMVguide3 and Wine Quality) demonstrates clear practical advantages in both error rate and variance reduction. The analysis of network topology effects (complete graph, 2D grid, Watts-Strogatz) effectively illustrates the network-dependent term in the convergence rate. The supplementary material's additional comparisons provide further validation.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The problem statement is precise, the algorithms are described with pseudocode, and the key ideas are presented in a logical flow. The phantom node concept is somewhat abstract but is well-explained in the context of the convergence analysis.

## Weaknesses

**Communication Complexity Details:** While the paper claims lower memory and communication costs compared to U2-gossip, it doesn't explicitly quantify these differences in terms of bits per iteration. For high-dimensional data (which is a key concern in the paper), a more detailed comparison of communication complexity would strengthen the claim.

**Asynchronous Rate Justification:** The paper establishes an O(log t/t) rate for asynchronous convergence, but doesn't sufficiently explain why this is practically advantageous over the O(1/t) synchronous rate. Since log t grows very slowly, the practical difference might be marginal in many applications. A more detailed discussion of when the asynchronous variant would be preferred would improve the paper.

**Limited Experimental Scope:** While the two applications (AUC and within-cluster point scatter) are well-chosen, the paper could benefit from additional experiments on different U-statistics or more diverse network topologies to further validate the generality of the approach.

**Supplementary Material Dependency:** Some key technical details (e.g., the precise analysis of U2-gossip's convergence rate) are deferred to supplementary material. While this is acceptable, the main paper would benefit from a more self-contained presentation of these comparisons.

## Recommendation

This paper makes a substantial contribution to the field of decentralized computation. It addresses a well-motivated problem with a novel solution, provides rigorous theoretical analysis, and demonstrates clear practical advantages through experiments. The work is of high quality, well-written, and makes a significant advance beyond the state-of-the-art (U2-gossip).

**Pro Acceptance Arguments:**
- Solves a clear gap in gossip algorithms for U-statistics
- Novel phantom node analysis technique
- Strong theoretical guarantees with practical validation
- Better convergence rates and lower communication complexity than prior work
- Clear comparison to relevant prior work

**Con Acceptance Arguments:**
- Could provide more explicit communication complexity comparison
- Needs better justification for asynchronous rate's practical advantage
- Limited to two applications (though well-chosen)

## Specific Suggestions

1. Include a table comparing communication complexity (in terms of bits) between GoSta and U2-gossip for high-dimensional data.

2. Add a brief discussion explaining when the asynchronous variant would be preferable to the synchronous one, given the log t factor.

3. Consider adding a third experiment on a different U-statistic (e.g., Gini mean difference) to further demonstrate the generality of the approach.

4. Provide a more detailed explanation of the phantom node construction in the main text to improve accessibility.

## Final Assessment

This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed optimization and gossip algorithms. The problem is well-motivated, the solution is novel and theoretically sound, and the experimental validation is thorough. The authors have clearly improved upon the state-of-the-art with both better theoretical guarantees and practical advantages. The minor weaknesses do not detract from the overall quality and significance of the work.

I recommend acceptance at NIPS. This paper would be a valuable addition to the literature, providing a crucial extension to gossip algorithms that enables distributed computation of fundamental statistics used throughout machine learning.
