- Studies decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip and proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async.  
- Each node keeps a local estimate Z_i and one auxiliary sample Y_i that random-walks; updates combine running average H(X_i,Y_i) and pairwise gossip averaging.  
- Introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple analysis of estimate averaging and data propagation.  
- Proves expected convergence to the U-statistic with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async), depending on data-dispersion terms and graph spectral gap (1−λ2).  
- Uses half the memory and half the communication per iteration compared to U2-gossip and derives a refined bound for U2-gossip.  
- Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across multiple graph topologies show lower error and variance for GoSta.  
- Builds on randomized gossip literature and targets non-separable pairwise quantities (U-statistics), filling a gap in prior work.  
- Connects convergence rates explicitly to the spectral gap, emphasizing network topology effects.  
- Theory is clearly stated and plausible; phantom-node analysis is technically neat; proofs are in the supplement.  
- Assumes connected, non-bipartite graphs and symmetric H with H(x,x)=0; boundedness assumptions for H are implicit and should be stated.  
- Guarantees are in expectation only; no almost-sure or high-probability bounds are provided.  
- Asynchronous algorithm requires knowledge of pk = 2d_k/|E|; robustness to estimation error or unknown |E| is not analyzed.  
- Experiments are presented versus iterations; communication- or time-normalized comparisons are lacking to substantiate efficiency claims.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-structured, though some notational and formatting issues (e.g., block matrix B) need polishing.  
- Core algorithmic idea and the phantom-node analysis technique are novel in this context; refined U2-gossip bound is a useful contribution.  
- Work is significant because U-statistics underpin many measures (AUC, Kendall’s tau, scatter), and decentralized, low-overhead estimation is practically valuable.  
- Pros: simple low-memory algorithms, interpretable data- and graph-dependent expected-rate guarantees, novel analysis, empirical validation, asynchronous variant.  
- Cons: only expectation guarantees (no high-probability/variance decay bounds), pk requirement for async, non-bipartite assumption not fully addressed, iteration-based experiments, limited analysis of multiple observations per node.  
- Suggestions: provide high-probability/variance bounds, discuss lazy-gossip for bipartite graphs and weighted/Metropolis edge selection, clarify boundedness/moment conditions for H, explore online estimation/sensitivity of pk, add communication-normalized plots, fix block matrix description.  
- Recommendation: Accept (7/10).  
- Reviewer confidence: High.
