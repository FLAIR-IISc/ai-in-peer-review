This paper addresses the challenging problem of extending gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics, a class of quantities frequently encountered in machine learning and statistics that are intractable with standard gossip approaches. The core idea – combining data propagation with local estimate averaging, and cleverly reformulating the problem using “phantom nodes” – is genuinely novel and quite elegant. This is a significant step beyond simply applying existing gossip frameworks, and targets a fundamental limitation. The theoretical convergence analysis, while detailed, appears sound, and the network-dependency showcased through the spectral gap is a nice touch. The empirical validation, while limited to two applications, is encouraging.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The extension of gossip algorithms to U-statistics is a highly original contribution. This genuinely opens up new avenues for decentralized computation.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The convergence analysis appears rigorous, and the inclusion of data-dependent and network-dependent terms provides valuable insight. The phantom node reformulation is clever.
*   **Potential Impact:** Successful decentralized computation of U-statistics has broad implications for various applications, particularly in distributed machine learning and data mining.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, making the core ideas accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments are restricted to two specific U-statistics and relatively simple network topologies. A more comprehensive evaluation, including more complex networks and a wider range of U-statistics, would strengthen the claims.
*   **Asynchronous Convergence Rate:** The O(log t/t) rate for the asynchronous algorithm is slower than the synchronous rate. While not a fatal flaw, it raises questions about the practical benefits of the asynchronous approach, especially given the added complexity. Further analysis/explanation could be beneficial.
*   **Practical Considerations:** The phantom node formulation, while theoretically useful, adds to the conceptual complexity. It would be helpful to discuss how this translates into practical implementation challenges.
*   **Comparison to Baselines:** While U2-gossip is compared, the inclusion of more standard decentralized estimation methods (even if suboptimal for U-statistics) would offer a better baseline for evaluating the gains achieved by the proposed approach.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a truly novel idea with the potential to significantly impact the field of decentralized computation.
*   The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides valuable insights into the convergence behavior of the proposed algorithms.
*   The empirical results, while limited, are promising and support the theoretical findings.
*   The problem addressed is important, and the proposed solution offers a compelling approach to overcoming a fundamental limitation of existing gossip algorithms.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The empirical evaluation is somewhat limited and could be strengthened with more extensive experiments.
*   The asynchronous convergence rate is less compelling than the synchronous rate.
*   The phantom node formulation adds complexity that may hinder practical implementation.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and significant contribution to the field. While the empirical evaluation could be more comprehensive, the originality and theoretical rigor of the work are compelling. The paper introduces a new direction for research in decentralized computation, and the potential impact is substantial. I believe the strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses. I am willing to overlook the limited empirical validation and the slightly slower asynchronous convergence rate given the boldness of the approach and its potential to open new research avenues.

**Recommendation:** Accept. This paper warrants inclusion in NIPS, as it demonstrates a clear advance in the state of the art.
