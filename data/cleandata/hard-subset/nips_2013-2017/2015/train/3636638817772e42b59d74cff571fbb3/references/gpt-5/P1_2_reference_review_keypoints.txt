- Paper studies decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip and proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async.  
- Each node stores Xi, a local estimate Zk, and one auxiliary Yk that random-walks; iterations swap Y on a random edge and average estimates.  
- GoSta-sync converges in expectation at O(1/t) with data-dependent constants and a spectral-gap network factor; GoSta-async achieves O(log t/t).  
- Analysis uses a “phantom network” construction to decouple propagation and averaging, yielding a block-matrix recursion.  
- Refines U2-gossip analysis, showing a √n/t dependence that explains U2’s poorer empirical performance.  
- Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across topologies show GoSta’s superior accuracy and lower variance; sync and async perform similarly in practice.  
- Prior gossip work handles separable aggregates; U2-gossip is closest but uses two auxiliaries, more memory/communication, and is synchronous.  
- Main novelty: marrying averaging with single-sample propagation and providing explicit data- and graph-dependent convergence rates.  
- Phantom-node analytic device resembles lifted/product graph approaches but is a new application to U-statistics gossip.  
- Asynchronous variant adapts the Poisson-clock model and uses local counters/stepsizes to retain unbiasedness.  
- Strength: very small per-iteration footprint (one edge activation, one auxiliary sample per node, swap + averaging).  
- Strength: rigorous expectation convergence analysis with explicit spectral-gap dependence.  
- Strength: clear theoretical explanation of U2-gossip’s empirical inferiority (√n factor).  
- Strength: practical relevance to many U-statistics (AUC, Kendall tau, scatter) with real-data validations.  
- Weakness: only expectation convergence proven; no high-probability or mean-square error bounds provided.  
- Weakness: async algorithm requires nodes to know pk = 2dk/|E| and |E|, which is often unrealistic and not sufficiently addressed.  
- Weakness: O(log t/t) async bound is looser and constant c′(G) is unspecified; potential for tighter O(1/t) under mild assumptions not explored.  
- Weakness: limited robustness assumptions (undirected, static, connected, non-bipartite); lacks discussion of directed/time-varying/delayed/lossy networks.  
- Weakness: experiments report iterations but not communication/memory to reach ε or wall-clock time under realistic asynchrony.  
- Weakness: data-dependent constants in bounds (norms involving h and H) are not empirically probed for sensitivity.  
- Weakness: scope restricted to degree-2 U-statistics; no discussion of extending to higher-order U-statistics.  
- Quality: technical development is sound, consistent with established gossip analyses; proofs deferred but plausible.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear; phantom network explained well; should explicitly state per-iteration costs and clarify bipartite/non-bipartite and |E| assumptions.  
- Originality: timely and underexplored problem; combining averaging with single-sample propagation and the analysis appears novel.  
- Significance: important building block for decentralized evaluation/learning; reduces memory/communication and benefits from spectral-gap dependence.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel/elegant algorithms, solid theory with interpretable constants, practical async variant, empirical validation.  
- Cons for acceptance: lacks concentration guarantees, async needs global |E| knowledge, limited robustness and time-to-accuracy analysis.  
- Suggestions: provide high-probability or mean-square bounds; discuss/implement local pk estimation or adaptive stepsizes; report communication/time-to-ε; add notes on bipartite/directed/time-varying graphs; study sensitivity to data constants and higher-order U-statistics.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
