- Addresses gap in gossip algorithms by proposing distributed methods (GoSta-sync, GoSta-async) for estimating degree-2 U-statistics.  
- Introduces a "phantom nodes" reformulation to analyze the coupled data propagation and averaging.  
- Proves convergence rates O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms.  
- Theoretical analysis ties convergence to network spectral gap and data dispersion and avoids the √n factor in U2-gossip.  
- Practical relevance: applicable to AUC, sample variance, Gini mean difference; focuses on reducing communication and memory.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: compares to U2-gossip across multiple topologies and real tasks, showing consistent superiority and widening gap with larger networks.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear notation and implementable algorithm descriptions.  
- Limitation: restricted to degree-2 U-statistics; extensions to higher-order U-statistics are not developed.  
- Limitation: asynchronous algorithm requires nodes to know their degree and total edges (pk = 2dk/|E|), which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Limitation: theoretical comparison to U2-gossip (Equation 10) is somewhat superficial and needs deeper analysis of the √n effect.  
- Limitation: scalability analysis is limited (experiments only cover n ≈ 50–1599); broader scaling behavior is not thoroughly studied.  
- Overall assessment: substantial, novel, technically sound contribution bridging theory and practice in distributed estimation.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Pros: novel theoretical framework, strong experimental validation, practical improvements, high relevance to distributed ML.  
- Cons: limited to degree-2 U-statistics and requires network knowledge for the asynchronous version.
