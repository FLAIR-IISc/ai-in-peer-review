

# Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

This paper proposes "GoSta" algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics using gossip protocols. The authors introduce synchronous and asynchronous algorithms that combine data propagation with local averaging to compute U-statistics efficiently. While the problem is well-motivated and the approach appears novel, the paper contains numerous technical and formatting inconsistencies that undermine its credibility and reproducibility.

## Major Technical Issues

1. **Definition of U-statistic**: The paper defines the U-statistic as Ûn(H) = (1/n²)∑_{i,j=1}^n H(Xi,Xj), but states that "the usual definition of U-statistic differs slightly from (1) by a factor of n/(n-1)." This is incorrect. The standard definition of a degree-2 U-statistic is (1/((n choose 2)))∑_{i<j} H(Xi,Xj) = (2/(n(n-1)))∑_{i<j} H(Xi,Xj), which is fundamentally different from the paper's formulation. The paper's definition includes all pairs (i,j), including i=j, which contradicts the standard definition where H(X,X)=0 per their own definition. This fundamental misunderstanding of the U-statistic concept significantly undermines the paper's technical foundation.

2. **Missing Reference**: The paper repeatedly cites "U2-gossip" as "only investigated in [17]" but fails to include this reference in the bibliography. The reference appears in the text but is omitted from the reference list, creating a serious credibility issue.

3. **Algorithm 1 Typo**: In Algorithm 1, step 4 contains the expression "Zp ← t⁻¹t Zp + 1 t H(Xp, Yp)" which is mathematically nonsensical. It should be "Zp ← ((t-1)/t)Zp + (1/t)H(Xp, Yp)".

4. **Wine Quality Dataset Error**: The paper states "the Wine Quality dataset which contains n = 1599 points in d = 12 dimensions, with a total of K = 11 classes." This is factually incorrect. The standard Wine Quality dataset (UCI) has 1599 samples but only 3 classes (white wine, red wine, and quality ratings), not 11 classes as claimed.

5. **Supplementary Material Absence**: The paper repeatedly refers to "supplementary material" for proofs and additional experiments, but no supplementary material is provided. The proofs of Theorems 1 and 2 are claimed to be in the supplementary material, making the paper incomplete for evaluation.

## Formatting and Presentation Issues

1. **Inconsistent Notation**: The paper inconsistently uses "U-statistic" with and without a space ("U -statistic"), and the notation for the graph Laplacian L(G) is used without defining it properly in the main text.

2. **Figure References**: The paper references "Figure 2" and "Figure 3" but these figures are not included in the provided text, making it impossible to evaluate the experimental results.

3. **Citation Problems**: The bibliography contains incomplete references such as "Modern Graph Theory, volume 184, Béla Bollobás, , , " with empty fields for authors and publication details.

4. **Typographical Errors**: Multiple typos throughout the paper, including "perfomance" instead of "performance" in the description of Figure 2.

5. **Proofs in Supplementary Material**: The paper states "Proof. See supplementary material" for both Theorems 1 and 2, but the supplementary material is not provided, making the technical claims unverifiable.

## Minor Technical Concerns

1. The phantom network construction (Section 4.1) is described as "G̃ = (Ṽ, Ẽ) where each node k ∈ V is connected to its counterpart vkk ∈ Vk" but the formal definition "Ṽ = V ∪ (∪nk=1Vk)" is written with inconsistent notation (n vs. n).

2. In the convergence rate analysis (Theorem 1), the bound states "∥E[Z(t)] - Ûn(H)1n∥ ≤ 1/(ct)∥h - Ûn(H)1n∥ + (2/(ct) + e^{-ct})∥H - h1n^T∥" but the term "e^{-ct}" is problematic as it contradicts the stated O(1/t) convergence rate.

3. The comparison to U2-gossip in equation (10) claims "the √n factor dominates," but the bound actually has "√n/t" as a factor, which is inconsistent with the stated convergence rate.

4. The asynchronous algorithm (Algorithm 2) uses "mi ← mi + 1/pi" but the definition of pi = 2dk/|E| is not consistent with standard gossip protocols where communication probabilities are typically proportional to degree.

## Overall Assessment

While the problem of distributed U-statistic estimation is interesting and the GoSta algorithms represent a novel approach, the paper suffers from multiple fundamental technical errors, formatting inconsistencies, and missing critical components (supplementary material, figures). The incorrect definition of U-statistics is particularly problematic as it undermines the entire theoretical foundation of the work. The paper's claims about convergence rates and comparisons to prior work cannot be verified due to the missing proofs and incomplete references.

The paper would benefit from a thorough revision to correct the U-statistic definition, include all references, fix the algorithm pseudocode, provide complete experimental results with figures, and clarify the theoretical analysis. As it stands, the technical inconsistencies and missing components prevent me from recommending acceptance.

**Recommendation: Reject**
