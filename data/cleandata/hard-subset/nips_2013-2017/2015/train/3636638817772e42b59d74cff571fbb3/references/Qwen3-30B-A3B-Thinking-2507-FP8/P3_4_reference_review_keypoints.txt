- Problem: Extends gossip algorithms to distributed estimation of U-statistics (pairwise-dependent quantities), which standard gossip cannot handle efficiently.  
- Contribution: Proposes GoSta algorithms (synchronous and asynchronous) and a novel "phantom nodes" construction for convergence analysis.  
- Theoretical strength: Proves O(1/t) convergence for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous GoSta with bounds showing dependence on spectral gap and data dispersion.  
- Comparison to prior work: Shows GoSta outperforms U2-gossip, arguing U2-gossip has a √n disadvantage due to lack of averaging.  
- Methodological insight: Phantom node construction cleanly separates data propagation and averaging, enabling rigorous convergence proofs.  
- Experiments: Empirical results on real datasets and three network topologies demonstrate faster convergence and lower variance than U2-gossip; scalability trends match theory.  
- Strength — clarity: Clearly motivates the problem and relevance to applications like AUC and within-cluster scatter.  
- Weakness — quantitative costs: Lacks concrete quantitative comparisons of memory and communication (e.g., bytes per iteration, memory per node).  
- Weakness — implementation details: Does not specify data partitioning across nodes for experiments (contiguous vs randomized).  
- Weakness — async justification: Does not compare asynchronous GoSta’s log t / t rate to standard asynchronous gossip averaging to assess extra overhead from U-statistics.  
- Weakness — large-scale scalability: Experiments only go up to n=1599; paper does not address scaling to millions of nodes (memory/communication per node).  
- Weakness — related work: Insufficient discussion connecting to recent gossip algorithms for distributed optimization that may have comparable convergence properties.  
- Overall assessment: Technically strong, novel, and well-validated contribution that meaningfully advances distributed U-statistics estimation.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
