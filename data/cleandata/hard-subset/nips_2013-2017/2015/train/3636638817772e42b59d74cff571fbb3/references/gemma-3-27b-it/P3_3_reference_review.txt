## NIPS Review: Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting using gossip algorithms. Existing gossip-based approaches are limited to separable functions, making them unsuitable for U-statistics which require pairwise interactions. The authors propose both synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) that propagate data and average local estimates to converge towards the U-statistic. They provide theoretical convergence guarantees, demonstrating rates of O(1/t) for the synchronous version and O(log t/t) for the asynchronous one, with data- and network-dependent terms.  A clever reformulation using "phantom nodes" aids the analysis. Experimental validation on AUC and within-cluster point scatter showcases the superiority of GoSta over a recent baseline (U2-gossip).

**Relationship to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the well-established literature of gossip algorithms for distributed averaging (Boyd et al., Kempe et al.). It correctly identifies the limitations of existing gossip methods for non-separable functions. The crucial comparison point is the work of Pelckmans & Suykens (U2-gossip), which the authors significantly improve upon in terms of convergence rate, memory usage, and communication costs.  The paper also appropriately references relevant work on spectral graph theory (Chung, Tsitsiklis) for analyzing network connectivity and its impact on convergence.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:**  Extending gossip algorithms to efficiently compute U-statistics is a significant contribution. This opens doors to decentralized computation of important statistical measures like AUC and variance without centralized control.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides rigorous convergence analysis for both synchronous and asynchronous algorithms. The convergence rates are clearly stated and depend on both data characteristics and network topology, offering valuable insights.
* **Phantom Node Reformulation:** The use of "phantom nodes" is a clever technique to enable a tractable analysis of the data propagation process. This makes the derivation of the convergence bounds possible.
* **Practical Validation:** The experimental results on real datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of GoSta, showing superior performance compared to U2-gossip in terms of both accuracy and convergence speed.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear definitions and notations.  Algorithm 1 and 2 are presented concisely and understandably.
* **Asynchronous Algorithm:** The inclusion of an asynchronous algorithm addresses the practical limitation of requiring a global clock in synchronous settings.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Analysis:** While rigorous, the analysis involving phantom nodes and matrix manipulations is quite complex and may be challenging for readers unfamiliar with spectral graph theory and stochastic matrix analysis. More intuitive explanations of the key steps would be beneficial.
* **Network Model:** The assumption of undirected graphs and uniform edge selection might be limiting in some real-world scenarios. Investigating the performance under different network topologies (e.g., directed graphs, weighted edges) could strengthen the results.
* **Data Dependence:** The data-dependent terms in the convergence bounds are not fully characterized. Understanding the influence of the data distribution on the convergence rate could lead to adaptive algorithms.  Specifically,  the norm ||H - h1>n|| appears in the bound; a discussion of how this term behaves in practice would be helpful.
* **Practical Scalability:** While GoSta improves on U2-gossip, the memory requirement for auxiliary observations (Yk) can still be a concern for extremely high-dimensional data.  Exploring techniques to reduce the memory footprint (e.g., sketching, dimensionality reduction) would be valuable.
* **Comparison to Simpler Baselines:** While the comparison to U2-gossip is important, it would be beneficial to also compare GoSta to simpler distributed estimation methods, even if they are less efficient, to demonstrate the advantage of the proposed approach more clearly.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant problem with a novel and well-motivated approach.
* The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides valuable insights into the convergence behavior.
* The experimental results validate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The complexity of the analysis might limit the accessibility of the paper to a wider audience.
* The limitations of the network model and data dependence warrant further investigation.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed estimation. The proposed algorithms are novel, theoretically sound, and empirically validated. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I believe this paper is suitable for acceptance at NIPS.

**Recommendation:** **Accept**

**Confidence:** 8/10.
