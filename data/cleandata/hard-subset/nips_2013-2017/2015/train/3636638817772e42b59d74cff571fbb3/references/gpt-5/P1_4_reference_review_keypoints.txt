- Tackles decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip; decouples data propagation (single auxiliary observation Y random walk) and estimation averaging (pairwise averaging Z); proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async.  
- Introduces a “phantom nodes” analysis lifting the process to an O(n^2)-size augmented graph and characterizes expected dynamics via Laplacian-based block matrices Wα(G).  
- Proves expectation convergence to the U-statistic with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async); bounds depend on data dispersion and network spectral gap.  
- Compared to U2-gossip: uses half the memory, fewer communications per iteration, and achieves tighter rates; includes a refined analysis of U2-gossip.  
- Experiments (AUC, within-cluster scatter) on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs show improved accuracy and reduced variance, with larger gains for better-connected graphs and larger n.  
- Builds on randomized gossip and rumor-spreading literature and fills a gap for efficient decentralized computation of pairwise (non-separable) statistics.  
- Closest prior work U2-gossip propagated two samples without averaging; this work adds averaging and a novel analysis framework.  
- Theory judged technically sound: Wα(G) links to the Laplacian, async estimator unbiased via local counters, and refined U2 bounds clarify when GoSta dominates.  
- Scope/assumptions: results are in expectation only; require connected, non-bipartite graphs and uniform random edge selection.  
- Practical requirement: nodes must know |E| and node degrees to compute pk, which may be nontrivial in deployments.  
- Async bound issues: contains unspecified constant c′(G) and an ambiguous norm ||H|| — the norm should be explicitly stated.  
- Experimental mismatch: includes bipartite 2D grids despite non-bipartite theoretical assumption; needs reconciliation (e.g., laziness/self-loops) or discussion of robustness.  
- Experiments could be strengthened with more baselines (sampling approximations, centralized aggregation) and explicit reporting of communication, memory, and wall-clock/messages-to-target-error.  
- Clarity issues: specify matrix norm in bounds, explicitly state node knowledge requirements, explain decentralized computation of AUC denominator, and simplify dense notation with a worked example.  
- Originality: single-sample propagation + explicit averaging and phantom-node analysis are novel; async local-counter scheme and refined U2 analysis are valuable.  
- Significance: important for decentralized sensing and graph analytics; reduces memory/communication and gives faster, network-aware convergence.  
- Pros: principled sync/async algorithms, explicit network/data-dependent rates, simple implementation (one auxiliary observation, one edge activation), solid empirical support, useful U2 refinement.  
- Cons: only expectation guarantees, non-bipartite assumption vs. bipartite experiments, async constants/norm ambiguous, requires global |E|/degrees, limited baselines and missing explicit cost comparisons.  
- Questions for authors: add laziness/self-loops for bipartite graphs, specify norm and c′(G), provide high-probability bounds, discuss sensitivity to non-uniform/time-varying edges and dynamic graphs, and explain decentralized AUC denominator computation.  
- Suggestions: report message sizes and iterations-to-accuracy, remove need for global |E| via local estimation, consider robustness to quantization/packet drops/delays, and outline extensions to higher-order U-statistics and complexity trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on addressing clarity and scope issues (especially bipartite graphs and unspecified constants).  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium-high.
