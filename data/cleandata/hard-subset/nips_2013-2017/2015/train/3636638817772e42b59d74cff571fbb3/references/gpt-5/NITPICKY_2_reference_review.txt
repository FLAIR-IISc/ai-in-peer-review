Summary
The paper tackles decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a connected network via randomized gossip. The core idea is to decompose Ûn(H) into an average of node-local quantities hi and to combine (i) propagation of data samples through the network via random swaps and (ii) averaging of local estimates across randomly selected edges. Two algorithms are proposed: GoSta-sync (global clock) and GoSta-async (fully asynchronous with local clocks). A “phantom nodes” construction is used for analysis. The authors prove convergence in expectation to Ûn(H); the synchronous algorithm achieves an O(1/t) rate with data- and graph-dependent constants, while the asynchronous one achieves O(log t/t). They compare to U2-gossip, arguing better rates, lower memory and communication, and validate on AUC and within-cluster scatter over several graph families.

Strengths
- Problem relevance: decentralized estimation of pairwise statistics (AUC, scatter, Kendall tau, etc.) is important and under-explored relative to separable sums.
- Algorithmic simplicity: one auxiliary observation per node plus pairwise averaging; asynchronous variant accommodating heterogeneous degrees.
- Analytical contributions: “phantom nodes” reformulation decoupling data propagation and averaging; rates that incorporate spectral gap and data dispersion.
- Practicality: reduced memory/communication vs U2-gossip; experiments on real datasets and different graphs.

Weaknesses and technical concerns
- Convergence only in expectation: No almost-sure or mean-square convergence or concentration bounds are provided. This is weaker than typical gossip analyses (which often provide a.s. or L2 convergence).
- Spectral-gap constant error: In Sec. 5, for the complete graph, the paper claims 1 − λ2(2) = 1/n. With W2(G) = I − (1/|E|)L(G), the correct value is 1 − λ2(2) = μ2/|E|, and for K_n, μ2 = n and |E| = n(n−1)/2, yielding 2/(n−1), not 1/n. This discrepancy propagates to the interpretation of rates.
- Notational inconsistencies/ambiguities:
  - “βn−1 is the second smallest eigenvalue of L(G)” is inconsistent; standard notation would be β2 (the algebraic connectivity). Using βn−1 suggests second largest.
  - Eq. (8): “W2(G) t−s” presumably means W2(G)^(t−s); the exponent is missing/ambiguous.
  - Norms: ‖·‖ is defined as the ℓ2 norm, but applied to matrices H (Theorem 1, 2) without specifying whether it is spectral or Frobenius. The bound in Theorem 1 mixes vector and matrix norms without clear submultiplicativity arguments.
  - Boldface H as the n×n matrix with entries H(Xi,Xj) is used in S2(0) but not explicitly defined earlier.
- Algorithmic details lacking/assumptions:
  - GoSta-sync requires all nodes to compute H(Xk,Yk) every iteration, even though only one edge communicates. The per-iteration computational cost is O(n) evaluations of H (potentially expensive in high d). Experimental comparisons do not normalize error vs total compute or total messages.
  - GoSta-async assumes each node knows pk = 2dk/|E| and therefore knows |E|. This is a nontrivial global parameter that many decentralized settings do not assume; no method is provided to estimate |E| in-network.
  - AUC normalization requires global counts of positives and negatives (denominator in Eq. (3)). The paper does not explain how these counts are obtained in a decentralized way or whether they are assumed known; this can bias/complicate the mapping to H.
- Comparison to U2-gossip:
  - Theoretical comparison is not apples-to-apples: constants of O(1/t) can scale poorly with n via 1/c(G) ≈ |E|/μ2, potentially O(n) on dense graphs, whereas the refined U2 bound includes √n but different spectral terms. The claim of “faster convergence” needs a more nuanced discussion of constants across graph families.
  - Experiments count iterations but do not match communication/computation budgets (GoSta-sync has n H-evaluations per iteration; U2 has 2 swaps and one H-evaluation per node per iteration). Plots should be vs total H evaluations and total messages to be fair.
- Theory clarity:
  - The time-varying transition product leading to Eq. (8) is sketched; key steps (bounding Cesàro averages of W2 products with the data-propagation term) are omitted. The presence of the e−ct term in Theorem 1 comes from averaging dynamics but is not derived in the main text.
  - Theorem 2 provides only a graph-dependent constant c′(G) and a bound proportional to ‖H‖, losing the informative data-dependent dispersion terms of Theorem 1. This weakens the interpretability of the asynchronous rate.
- Scope and experiments:
  - Only two tasks; no Kendall’s tau, Gini MD, or variance—although they are mentioned as motivating. No sensitivity to spectral gap (e.g., vary Watts–Strogatz p, grid size) shown quantitatively against theory.
  - No study of robustness to degree heterogeneity or bipartite graphs (where assumptions fail).
- Presentation/formatting issues (numerous minor but distracting):
  - Hyphenation and broken words (“lo- cal”), grammar (“need not to be aware”), typos (“perfomance”).
  - Inconsistent reference numbering (e.g., U2-gossip appears as [17] in text but references list maps [17] to Tsitsiklis; Pelckmans–Suykens appears as [16]).
  - Several references incomplete (years/pages missing).
  - Footnotes appear as “1/2/3” in the body but are not consistently tied to text or appear misplaced.

Relation to prior work
The problem extends beyond standard gossip for separable sums; prior U-statistics gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens) is correctly cited, but the novelty is in combining averaging with single-token propagation and the phantom-graph analysis. That said, related distributed estimation of pairwise interactions and token-based random walks (e.g., rumor spreading, pair sampling) could be more comprehensively discussed, as could recent consensus/gradient tracking literature relevant to bias/variance trade-offs.

Suggestions for improvement
- Correct the spectral gap constant and indexing; specify matrix norms throughout and fix Eq. (8) notation. Provide a derivation for the e−ct term.
- Strengthen convergence results (at least L2 convergence); if not possible, clearly state the scope (expectation only).
- Clarify assumptions: how to obtain |E| and class counts in a decentralized way (or run auxiliary gossip to estimate them).
- Rework experimental evaluation to equalize communication and computation budgets; report wall-clock proxy (total H evaluations and messages), and include more tasks and spectral-gap sweeps.
- Provide more details on the multiple-observations extension.
- Clean up references, typos, and formatting.

Pros
- Addresses a relevant and nontrivial decentralized estimation problem.
- Simple algorithms; asynchronous variant is practical.
- Insightful analysis approach; data- and graph-dependent rates (sync).

Cons
- Only expectation convergence; theoretical exposition has errors/ambiguities.
- Incorrect spectral constant; notational and referencing inconsistencies.
- Experimental methodology does not control for compute/communication fairness.
- Reliance on global parameters (|E|, class counts) not fully addressed.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The paper has promising ideas and likely useful algorithms, but the accumulation of technical inaccuracies, unclear assumptions, and experimental fairness issues detracts from the overall scientific rigor. With corrections and strengthened analysis/experiments, this could become a solid contribution.
