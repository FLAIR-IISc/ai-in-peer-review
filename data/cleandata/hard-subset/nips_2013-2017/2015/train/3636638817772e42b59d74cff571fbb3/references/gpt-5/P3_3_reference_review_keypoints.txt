- Problem & algorithms: Decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics with two gossip algorithms — GoSta-sync (synchronous random-walk + averaging) and GoSta-async (fully asynchronous with corrected local clocks).

- Analysis approach: Introduces a “phantom node” construction to decouple averaging and data propagation.

- Convergence guarantees: Proves E[estimate] → true U-statistic with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async), dependent on data dispersion and graph spectral gap (1 − λ2).

- Improvement over prior work: Claims better rate and lower communication/memory than U2-gossip by combining random-walk observations with consensus and propagating only one auxiliary observation.

- Technical soundness: Modeling and block upper-triangular transition-matrix analysis are solid; synchronous rate and unbiased async correction mk are well handled.

- Expectation-only bounds: Theorems provide only expectation guarantees; no MSE or high-probability/concentration results.

- Async bound ambiguity: O(log t / t) uses an unspecified graph-dependent constant c′(G); unclear if the log factor is inherent or an artifact.

- Practical assumptions: Assumes uniform random edge activations and knowledge of degrees/|E| for pk, which may not hold in decentralized settings.

- Communication cost concerns: Swapping full d-dimensional data per communication; lacks quantitative per-iteration and total-bit comparisons and discussion of compression/quantization.

- Experimental limitations: Small-scale experiments (n ≤ 1599), limited async evaluation, no wall-clock timing, few graph types; needs larger, sparser, heterogeneous graphs and more baselines.

- Suggested clarifications: Some matrix-dimension notations (M2) terse; non-bipartite assumption could be removed with laziness; H(X,X)=0 assumption restrictive.

- Significance & originality: Addresses important, underexplored problem (AUC, Gini, etc.), offers simple implementable methods and a potentially reusable phantom-node analysis tied to spectral gap.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well written; would benefit from brief proof sketches and a summary figure/table of per-iteration costs and bounds.

- Recommended improvements: Provide MSE/high-probability bounds, make c′(G) explicit, handle bipartite graphs via lazy updates, address robustness to unknown |E|/pk and directed/time-varying graphs, quantify communication, and expand experiments.

- Pros: Relevant problem, novel phantom-node analysis, spectral-gap dependent rates, practical algorithms (sync + async), and empirical support.

- Cons: Only expectation guarantees, unspecified async constants, strong practical assumptions, unquantified communication costs, and modest experimental scale.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid, original contribution that would be strengthened by addressing theoretical and experimental gaps.
