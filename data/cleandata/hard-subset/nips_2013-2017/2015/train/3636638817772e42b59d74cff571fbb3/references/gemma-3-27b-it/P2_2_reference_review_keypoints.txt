- Problem addressed: decentralized estimation of U-statistics via gossip algorithms, which go beyond separable-function limits of existing methods.
- Proposed solution: synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta) with a "phantom node" reformulation to enable analysis.
- Convergence results: synchronous rate O(1/t) and asynchronous rate O(log t / t), with dependence on network topology and data characteristics.
- Novelty: phantom node technique and combining data propagation with averaging to handle pairwise-dependent statistics.
- Empirical validation: experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter show GoSta outperforms U2-gossip across network topologies.
- Strength — significance: tackles an important, widely applicable problem in distributed estimation.
- Strength — theory: rigorous convergence analysis characterizing spectral-gap and problem-dependent effects.
- Strength — presentation: generally clear writing, organized algorithms, consistent notation.
- Weakness — analysis complexity: phantom node construction complicates understanding and could be better connected to implementation.
- Weakness — async rate: O(log t / t) is slower than some asynchronous gossip methods (O(1/t)), potentially limiting practicality.
- Weakness — comparisons: limited baselines (only U2-gossip); missing comparisons to simpler decentralized methods like decentralized gradient descent.
- Weakness — practical considerations: lacks discussion of network dynamics (node failures, varying communication costs) and other real-world issues.
- Evaluation summary: reviewers rate quality and originality strong, clarity and significance good; technically sound and impactful.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically solid, empirically effective, well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: slower async rate, added complexity from phantom nodes, limited baseline comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify phantom node approach and add baseline comparisons.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
