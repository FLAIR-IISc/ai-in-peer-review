- Extends gossip algorithms to distributed estimation of U-statistics, addressing limitations of standard gossip for pairwise averages.
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous GoSta algorithms that combine data propagation and averaging.
- Provides convergence analyses: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous, with dependence on spectral gap and data-dependent terms.
- Introduces a "phantom nodes" reformulation to enable rigorous analysis of the distributed process.
- Demonstrates improvements over prior U2-gossip in convergence, memory, and communication costs.
- Empirically validates the approach on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks, confirming theoretical advantages.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and uses well-defined notation.
- Phantom node formulation increases analytical complexity and needs clearer intuitive explanation and connection to the original problem.
- The asynchronous O(log t/t) rate is relatively slow compared to other asynchronous averaging algorithms; unclear if this is inherent or improvable.
- Limited comparison to simpler baselines (e.g., direct asynchronous averaging of H(Xi,Xj)) weakens contextualization of gains.
- Experimental scope is narrow (two applications, few topologies); broader datasets and network structures would strengthen claims.
- Discussion of network selection (e.g., Watts–Strogatz) is cursory and should address trade-offs in communication cost vs. convergence speed.
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
- For acceptance: important problem, novel and theoretically sound solution, empirical improvements, and clear presentation.
- Against acceptance: phantom node complexity, slow async rate, limited baselines and experiments.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting clearer intuition for phantom nodes, discussion of async improvements, baseline comparisons, expanded experiments, and detailed network trade-offs.
