- Paper studies decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip, proposing GoSta-sync and GoSta-async.  
- Each node maintains one auxiliary sample that random-walks via swaps while nodes average local estimates.  
- Introduces a "phantom nodes" construction to decouple data propagation from averaging and derive linear recursion bounds.  
- Proves convergence in expectation: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with constants depending on data dispersion and graph spectral gap.  
- Compared to U2-gossip, the methods use less memory/communication and yield stronger theoretical rates; experiments show faster convergence and lower variance.  
- Technical modeling is careful; block-matrix recursion separates data- and graph-dependent terms, and asynchronous counters yield unbiased updates.  
- Empirical results support theoretical superiority and the provided comparison bound for U2-gossip (including a √n penalty).  
- Main caveat: only expectation convergence is proven—no high-probability or mean-squared error bounds or theoretical variance/disagreement control.  
- Asynchronous result has an extra log factor and an implicit constant c′(G); dependence on the spectral gap could be more explicit.  
- Asynchronous updates require nodes to know pk = 2dk/|E| (degree and total edges), which may be unrealistic; robustness or online estimation of pk is not addressed.  
- Minor methodological caveat: normalization handling when H(X,X)≠0 (diagonal terms) needs brief discussion regarding induced bias.  
- Exposition is generally clear and intuitive; phantom-node analysis and block structure aid understanding.  
- Presentation issues: typos, missing Table 1, dense notation, deferred derivations, and unclear phrasing like “1 Poisson process.”  
- Experimental scope is limited (two datasets, moderate n); lacks wall-clock, message-complexity, time-varying/directed graph tests, and multi-sample node experiments.  
- Originality: combination of single auxiliary random-walk sample, phantom-node analysis, and fully asynchronous scheme is novel and nontrivial.  
- Significance: enables low-memory, low-communication decentralized estimation of common metrics (AUC, Kendall tau, within-cluster scatter) with explicit spectral-gap dependence.  
- Pros: meaningful problem, practical algorithms, solid interpretable theory, novel analysis technique, unbiased asynchronous variant, and favorable empirical performance.  
- Cons: expectation-only guarantees, asynchronous log-factor and pk knowledge requirement, modest experiments, and presentation gaps.  
- Key suggestions: provide MSE/high-probability or disagreement bounds, explore online estimation/robustness of pk, investigate whether O(1/t) is possible asynchronously, add communication/memory/runtime comparison table, and extend discussion/formalism for time-varying/directed graphs and multiple samples per node.  
- Overall evaluation: technically competent and original work with practical significance; reviewer leans toward weak accept pending clarifications and minor additions.
