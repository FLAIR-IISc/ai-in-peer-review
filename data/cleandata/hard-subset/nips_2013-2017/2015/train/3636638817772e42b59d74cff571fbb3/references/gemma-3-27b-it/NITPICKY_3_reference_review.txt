The paper proposes randomized synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, a class of quantities not efficiently computable with standard gossip approaches. The core idea involves propagating local observations and averaging local estimates, cleverly formulated using "phantom nodes" to facilitate analysis. The authors provide convergence rate analysis and experimental validation on AUC and within-cluster point scatter estimation.

While the problem addressed is relevant and the proposed algorithms represent a valuable extension to the gossip framework, the paper suffers from a concerning number of minor flaws and a lack of thoroughness in several areas. My overall assessment leans towards rejection, despite acknowledging the novelty of the approach.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics is a genuine contribution.
*   **Theoretical Analysis:** The convergence rate analysis, while complex, is a significant achievement. The inclusion of data-dependent and network-dependent terms is commendable.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The experiments provide some evidence of the algorithm’s efficacy, albeit limited.
*   **Clear Problem Statement:** The introduction clearly motivates the problem and outlines the limitations of existing approaches.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Notational Inconsistencies & Sloppiness:** The paper contains numerous minor notational inconsistencies. For example, the definition of `[p]` is given, but then used without consistent formatting (sometimes with brackets, sometimes without). The symbol for the identity matrix changes between `In` and `I`. The definition of `H` states `H(X,X)=0`, but this isn't utilized in any derivation.  These inconsistencies, while individually small, accumulate and detract from readability.
*   **Phantom Node Explanation:** The explanation of the phantom nodes is conceptually sound, but the figure (Figure 1) is crucial and lacks sufficient detail. The connections between the original nodes and their phantom counterparts are not clearly illustrated, making it difficult to follow the mathematical derivations.
*   **Mathematical Rigor:** The transition to the matrix representation (Equation 5 onwards) feels abrupt. More explanation is needed to justify the mapping between the algorithm and the matrix operations. The connection between the phantom nodes and the matrix `M3(t)` is particularly unclear.
*   **Asynchronous Algorithm Presentation:** Algorithm 2 introduces `mk(t)` without adequately explaining its purpose or how it ensures unbiased estimation in the asynchronous setting. The convergence rate (Theorem 2) is significantly weaker (log t/t) and lacks a clear explanation of why the asynchronous version performs worse, beyond the general challenges of asynchronous algorithms.
*   **Experimental Scope:** The experimental evaluation is limited. Only two applications (AUC and within-cluster point scatter) are considered. More diverse U-statistics and datasets should be used to demonstrate the algorithm's generalizability.  The networks used are also quite simple.
*   **Comparison to U2-gossip:** The comparison to U2-gossip is superficial. The claim that GoSta outperforms U2-gossip is supported, but the analysis of the convergence rates (Equation 10) lacks detail and clarity. A more detailed discussion of the trade-offs between memory, communication, and convergence rate would be beneficial.
*   **Missing Details:** The “supplementary material” is frequently referenced (e.g., analysis of U2-gossip, detailed proofs), but it’s unacceptable to rely on unprovided material for assessing the core claims of the paper. Key details *must* be present in the main body of the paper.
*   **Formatting:** Several minor formatting issues are present. For instance, equation numbering seems inconsistent and some lines are excessively long.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important problem in decentralized computation.
*   Presents novel algorithms with theoretical guarantees.
*   Demonstrates the feasibility of computing U-statistics in a distributed manner.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   Numerous minor flaws and inconsistencies detract from the paper's quality.
*   Lack of thoroughness in explanation and presentation.
*   Limited experimental evaluation.
*   Reliance on unprovided supplementary material.
*   The complexity of the analysis may overshadow the practical impact of the work.



**Overall Recommendation:** Reject. 

The paper presents a valuable idea, but the numerous shortcomings in presentation, rigor, and completeness prevent me from recommending acceptance. The authors need to address the notational inconsistencies, provide clearer explanations of the key concepts (particularly the phantom nodes and the asynchronous algorithm), expand the experimental evaluation, and include all essential details within the main body of the paper. A thorough revision addressing these issues could potentially warrant reconsideration.
