- Summary: Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async to estimate degree-2 U‑statistics by decomposing the statistic into per-node averages and interleaving auxiliary-data random walks with consensus averaging.

- Analysis tool: Introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to embed propagation and averaging into a block-triangular linear system for clean expectation dynamics.

- Convergence results: Proves expectation convergence with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O((log t)/t) (async), with constants separating data difficulty (H norms) and network connectivity (spectral gap).

- Comparison to prior work: Improves on U2-gossip by using less memory/communication (one auxiliary observation, one edge per iteration), faster convergence (removes √n factor), and providing an asynchronous variant.

- Technical soundness: Synchronous analysis is carefully derived and credible; async counter scheme (increment by 1/pk) is clever and plausible; proofs in supplement appear consistent.

- Scope and assumptions: Results are expectation-only (no a.s. or high-probability/MSE bounds); requires connected, non-bipartite undirected graphs; async requires knowledge of pk = 2dk/|E|.

- Communication/model clarity: Communication model toggles between random edge draws and Poisson clocks; equivalence is asserted but could be explained more explicitly.

- Empirical evidence: Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across three graph families support claims and show GoSta outperforms U2-gossip, though scale is modest (n ≲ 1600).

- Experimental limitations: No ablation on data difficulty or spectral gap, no wall-clock/communication-to-ε analysis, and limited task/diversity and scale.

- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and well-organized; phantom-node idea is well-motivated; some technical details deferred and async rate intuition could be expanded.

- Originality: Novel algorithmic design and phantom-node analysis; asynchronous unbiased local-counter construction is a meaningful contribution beyond prior U2-gossip work.

- Significance: Relevant for distributed estimation of common statistics (AUC, variance, rank correlations); improved rates and lower resource use are practically meaningful and extensible.

- Pros: Simple implementable algorithms, improved theoretical guarantees, asynchronous design without global clock, experiments that corroborate theory.

- Cons: Expectation-only guarantees, assumptions on graph non-bipartiteness and known pk, limited experimental breadth/scale, focus only on degree-2 U-statistics.

- Suggestions: Provide MSE/high-probability bounds and ε-communication complexity; discuss lazy/push-sum fixes for bipartite/directed graphs and online pk estimation; run experiments varying spectral gap and report communication to ε; comment on extensions to higher-order U-statistics and privacy.

- Overall evaluation: Weak accept — solid, relevant contribution with addressable weaknesses; reviewer confidence high.
