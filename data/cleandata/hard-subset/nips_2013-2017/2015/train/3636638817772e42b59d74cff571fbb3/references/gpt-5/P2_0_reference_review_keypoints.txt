- Paper tackles decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip, propagating data so nodes compute local hi and averaging to get Ûn(H).
- Proposes two algorithms: GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (fully asynchronous).
- Introduces a “phantom nodes/network” construction to decouple data propagation from averaging and enable linear dynamical analysis.
- Proves convergence in expectation to Ûn(H) with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t/t) (async).
- Bounds include data-dependent dispersion terms and network-dependent spectral-gap dependence (Laplacian), showing faster convergence on well-connected graphs.
- Compared to U2-gossip, GoSta uses one auxiliary observation and one edge activation per iteration, supports asynchrony, and has better rate constants (no √n factor).
- Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs support theoretical claims and show variance reduction from averaging.
- Technical approach is sound; phantom-network reformulation yields an upper-triangular block system aligning with consensus rates tied to spectral gaps.
- Proof sketches are convincing, though key proofs are deferred to the supplement.
- Main theoretical limitation: guarantees are only in expectation—no high-probability, almost-sure, or mean-square error bounds.
- Assumes connected, non-bipartite, undirected graphs; non-bipartite assumption should be motivated and laziness noted as a fix for bipartite graphs.
- GoSta-async requires nodes to know pk = 2dk/|E| (degree and total edges), which may be unrealistic; impact of estimating pk online is not addressed.
- GoSta-sync assumes global uniform edge sampling (a global edge oracle); distributed implementations (e.g., Metropolis/local sampling) are not discussed.
- Notational clarity issues: ambiguous norm notation (vector vs matrix) and H should be explicitly defined as the n×n matrix [H(Xi,Xj)].
- Experiments lack communication/wall-clock metrics and larger-scale tests to illustrate spectral-gap scaling; async bound constants are opaque.
- Originality: novel combination of local running averages, single-observation propagation, phantom-node analysis, and an unbiased local time-counter async variant.
- Significance: practical decentralized estimator for common U-statistics with improved communication/memory and spectral-gap insights; useful though not a consensus-theory breakthrough.
- Pros: clear principled algorithms, async version, solid data- and network-dependent theory, lower memory/communication per iteration, empirical validation.
- Cons: expectation-only guarantees, pk knowledge requirement, global edge-sampling assumption, limited experimental metrics and scalability.
- Suggested improvements/questions: provide high-probability/MSE/a.s. convergence, analyze sensitivity to pk errors and online estimation, handle bipartite graphs via laziness, clarify matrix norm and H definition, report communication/wall-clock costs and larger graphs, discuss extension to m>2 and directed/weighted graphs.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clear, incremental but meaningful contribution with supportive experiments; recommend acceptance subject to minor clarifications on pk and implementation details.
