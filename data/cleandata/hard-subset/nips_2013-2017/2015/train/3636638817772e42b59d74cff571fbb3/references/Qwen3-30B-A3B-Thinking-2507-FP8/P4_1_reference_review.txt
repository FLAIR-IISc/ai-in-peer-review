

# Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

This paper addresses a significant gap in distributed computation: existing gossip algorithms can efficiently compute separable functions of data (like averages) but cannot efficiently compute U-statistics (which depend on pairs of observations). The authors propose two novel gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for distributed estimation of U-statistics, which are important quantities in machine learning (e.g., AUC, sample variance, within-cluster point scatter). The work builds on established gossip algorithm literature (e.g., [10, 2, 15]) but extends it to handle the non-separable nature of U-statistics, which previous work (U2-gossip [17]) could not address efficiently.

The paper's main technical contribution is a novel "phantom nodes" technique for convergence analysis, which allows the authors to separate data propagation and averaging steps in the network. They prove that GoSta-sync converges at rate O(1/t) and GoSta-async at rate O(log t/t), with both rates featuring data-dependent terms and network-dependent terms related to the graph's spectral gap. This analysis significantly improves upon U2-gossip [17], which achieves O(1/t) convergence but with higher memory and communication costs. The authors demonstrate through experiments on AUC and within-cluster point scatter estimation that their algorithms outperform U2-gossip in both convergence speed and variance across nodes.

**Strengths:**
1. **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis is rigorous, with clear convergence proofs for both algorithms. The phantom nodes technique provides a novel and valid approach to the problem, and the convergence bounds meaningfully incorporate data-dependent and network-dependent factors. The experimental results consistently support the theoretical claims across multiple datasets and network topologies (complete, grid, Watts-Strogatz).

2. **Novelty:** The paper clearly identifies and addresses a gap in existing gossip algorithm literature. The GoSta algorithms represent a genuine extension beyond U2-gossip, with concrete improvements in convergence rate (for the synchronous case), communication complexity, and memory usage. The comparison with U2-gossip is thorough and well-justified.

3. **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is well-structured with clear notation, logical flow, and sufficient detail for reproducibility. The algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1 and 2) are precise, and the experimental setup is well-motivated.

4. **Significance:** U-statistics are fundamental quantities in machine learning, and their distributed estimation has practical importance for sensor networks, peer-to-peer systems, and large-scale data processing. The proposed algorithms provide a theoretically sound and practically superior solution to this problem.

**Weaknesses:**
1. While the phantom nodes technique is clever, it results in an analysis that is complex and somewhat artificial (the phantom network is O(n²) in size, though only used for analysis). The paper could better explain why this approach is necessary rather than other potential techniques.

2. The paper could better position itself against related work. For example, while U2-gossip is discussed, other potential approaches to distributed U-statistic estimation (e.g., using other distributed optimization methods) are not explicitly compared.

3. The asynchronous convergence rate (O(log t/t)) is weaker than the synchronous rate (O(1/t)), though the authors acknowledge this. The paper could more thoroughly explain why this rate gap exists and whether it's inherent to the problem or could be improved.

**Pro/Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- The paper solves a well-motivated problem with clear theoretical and practical significance.
- The proposed algorithms demonstrably improve upon the only prior work (U2-gossip) in key metrics (convergence rate, memory, communication).
- The theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides meaningful insights into the problem's difficulty.
- Experimental results robustly support the theoretical claims across multiple datasets and network structures.

*Con:*
- The phantom nodes technique, while effective for analysis, creates a somewhat artificial model that may not generalize easily to other problems.
- The asynchronous algorithm's theoretical rate is weaker than the synchronous version, though experiments show comparable practical performance.
- The paper could better address why previous work (including the authors' own prior work [17]) didn't solve this problem more efficiently.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed algorithms by extending gossip techniques to handle U-statistics, which have wide applications in machine learning. The theoretical analysis is sound, the experimental validation is thorough, and the practical advantages over prior work are clear. While the phantom nodes technique adds complexity to the analysis, it is a valid approach that enables the key theoretical results. The paper meets all four NIPS criteria: it is technically sound, clearly written, original in its approach to a meaningful problem, and significant for both theoretical and practical distributed computation.

I recommend acceptance. The paper presents a well-executed contribution that advances the state of the art in distributed algorithms for U-statistic estimation, with clear theoretical and empirical justification for the proposed approach.
