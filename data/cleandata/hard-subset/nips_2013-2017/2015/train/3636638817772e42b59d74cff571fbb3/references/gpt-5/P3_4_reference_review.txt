Summary and contributions
The paper addresses decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a network via gossip. It proposes two randomized algorithms:
- GoSta-sync (synchronous): each node keeps a single auxiliary observation that performs a random walk via pairwise swaps; at each iteration nodes do a running average with H(Xk, Yk) and the two endpoints of a randomly selected edge average their local estimates.
- GoSta-async (asynchronous): similar dynamics, but only the activated pair updates, with per-node adaptive step sizes based on the expected number of activations.

A key analytical idea is a “phantom network” construction that decouples data propagation and averaging: for each source k, a copy of the graph propagates H(Xk, ·) as if by coupled random walks. This yields convergence of the node-wise estimates in expectation to the target U-statistic with rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t/t) (async). The bounds separate data-dependent dispersion terms from a network-dependent term (spectral gap), and the authors refine the rate for the closest prior algorithm (U2-gossip) to show an unfavorable √n factor. Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter over several graph topologies support the theoretical claims and show improved error and variance.

Relation to prior work
Gossip for separable aggregates is classic; the paper correctly situates itself with [Boyd et al.], geographic gossip, and decentralized averaging/optimization lines. Estimating pairwise quantities via gossip has mainly been explored by Pelckmans & Suykens (U2-gossip). The present work clarifies why naive extensions underperform (no averaging; two walkers), proposes lower memory/communication variants, and provides sharper analysis tying convergence to the graph Laplacian spectrum. The phantom-node analysis is reminiscent of lifting techniques and Kronecker-product analyses in consensus, but applied in a tailored way to handle U-statistics.

Quality (technical soundness)
- The synchronous analysis is elegant and convincing: the block-triangular state evolution, Wα(G) operators, and the decomposition of the error into a consensus/mixing term and a data-propagation term clearly explain the O(1/t) rate plus an exponentially decaying transient. The dependency on the spectral gap is standard and expected.
- The asynchronous algorithm is natural and seems correct; the per-node weighted running averages compensate for nonuniform activations. The O(log t/t) rate is plausible but the constant c′(G) is left unspecified and the norm in the bound is ambiguous (see clarity).
- The comparison to U2-gossip via a refined bound is useful and aligns with intuition; however, the fairness of per-iteration comparisons is only partly addressed since U2-gossip touches two edges and swaps two auxiliary variables per iteration.
- Convergence is established only in expectation. In gossip, mean-square and high-probability/finite-time bounds are often available. The absence of variance/concentration analysis leaves open how quickly the random estimates concentrate around their mean in realistic regimes.
- Assumptions are standard (connected, non-bipartite; H symmetric, H(X, X) = 0). The reliance on uniform edge sampling is explicit.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well written and organized. The phantom network exposition is clear and helpful.
- Notation issues: the norm used on matrices such as H and H − h1^T is not precisely specified (Frobenius vs operator vs vectorized ℓ2). Earlier the norm is defined for vectors; please clarify.
- In Algorithm 2, the requirement that nodes know pk = 2dk/|E| assumes global knowledge of |E|; this is mentioned but downplays practicality. Clarify whether Metropolis-Hastings choices or local-normalized weights would work and how the analysis changes.
- Minor typos/formatting: several footnotes inline are broken; “1 Poisson process” should be “rate-1 Poisson process”; some equations have missing punctuation.

Originality
- Extending gossip to pairwise U-statistics with provable rates and lower resource usage than U2-gossip is a novel and timely contribution.
- The phantom-node analysis is an original and insightful device for this setting.
- The asynchronous variant and refined comparison bound add value.

Significance and empirical evaluation
- Estimating U-statistics (AUC, Gini difference, Kendall’s tau, scatter) in distributed settings is important in federated sensing and peer-to-peer analytics. The proposed methods should be of interest to the NeurIPS community working on decentralized statistics and optimization.
- Experiments demonstrate consistent gains across graphs and tasks, with variance reduction due to averaging. However:
  - Only two real datasets and three synthetic graph models are used; adding larger and more challenging graphs (e.g., expanders vs grids vs sparse scale-free with skewed degrees) would better illustrate spectral-gap effects.
  - Include communication-normalized comparisons (e.g., messages or bytes per node vs error) to fairly reflect the lower communication/memory footprint claim versus U2-gossip.
  - Provide results for the asynchronous algorithm beyond a single figure and discuss wall-clock or event-driven performance.

Pros
- Clear and principled algorithms for a relevant decentralized problem class (U-statistics).
- Sharp, interpretable convergence rates with spectral-gap dependence.
- Reduced memory/communication versus prior work; practical appeal.
- Clean analysis via phantom networks; instructive comparison to U2-gossip.
- Solid empirical evidence of improvement.

Cons
- Convergence only in expectation; no concentration or mean-square rates.
- Asynchronous method assumes knowledge of |E|; practicality and robustness to nonuniform activation not fully discussed.
- Norms/constants in bounds are not fully specified; c′(G) opaque.
- Experimental evaluation could better normalize for communication and broaden scenarios.

Questions and suggestions for authors
- Can you provide mean-square bounds or high-probability guarantees, at least for GoSta-sync, using standard mixing/concentration tools?
- Clarify the matrix norm used in all bounds and specify c′(G).
- Discuss how to implement GoSta-async without global |E|: e.g., use Metropolis weights or edge-based Poisson clocks, and how this affects pk and the analysis.
- Extend experiments to report error vs total words transmitted (or bytes) and to additional graph families varying spectral gaps.
- Outline how the approach extends to U-statistics of order m > 2 (e.g., m − 1 random walkers), even if the complexity scales.

Overall recommendation
Weak accept. The paper presents a clear advance over prior work on decentralized estimation of pairwise statistics with principled analysis and practical algorithms. Addressing the clarity/practicality issues and strengthening the empirical comparison would further improve the contribution.
