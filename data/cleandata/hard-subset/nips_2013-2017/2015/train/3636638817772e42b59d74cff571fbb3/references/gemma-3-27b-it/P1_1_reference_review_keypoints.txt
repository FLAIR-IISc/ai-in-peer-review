- Paper proposes GoSta, synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms using “phantom nodes” to estimate U-statistics in decentralized networks.  
- Demonstrates convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous) with bounds depending on network topology and data.  
- Empirically validates GoSta on AUC and within-cluster scatter, showing improvements over U2-gossip.  
- Extends classical gossip algorithms to non-separable U-statistics, addressing a known limitation in prior work.  
- Phantom node reformulation is a novel analytical tool enabling tractable convergence proofs.  
- Theoretical analysis links performance to spectral gap and data characteristics, providing rigorous convergence bounds.  
- Providing both synchronous and asynchronous variants increases practical applicability.  
- Paper is well-written with clear algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1 & 2).  
- Comparison to U2-gossip is fair and highlights GoSta’s advantages in speed and resource use.  
- Analysis involving phantom nodes is complex and may be hard to follow for readers without spectral graph theory background.  
- Assumes undirected graphs, leaving behavior on directed or weighted graphs unexplored.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks systematic exploration of network topology effects (degree distribution, spectral gap).  
- Limited baseline comparisons—should include adapted distributed averaging methods for broader context.  
- Practical deployment issues (node failures, delays, security, adaptive communication) are only briefly discussed and left as future work.  
- Paper is a strong, novel contribution with rigorous analysis and promising empirical results.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, specifically to clarify phantom node intuition, study performance across network topologies, and discuss practical deployment considerations.
