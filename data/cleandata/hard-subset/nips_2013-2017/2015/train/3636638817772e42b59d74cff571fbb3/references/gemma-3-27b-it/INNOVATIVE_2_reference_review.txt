This paper addresses the challenging problem of extending gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics, a class of functions crucial in many machine learning applications yet previously intractable for efficient decentralized computation via gossip. The core idea of using “phantom nodes” to reformulate the problem and enable propagation of data alongside averaging is genuinely novel and quite clever. This is a significant step beyond prior work, particularly [17], and opens up a pathway for applying decentralized computation to a wider range of statistical estimation problems. The convergence analysis, while technical, appears sound and provides valuable insights into the factors influencing performance (spectral gap, data dependence). The experimental validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter is a welcome addition, demonstrating practical benefit.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The extension of gossip algorithms to U-statistics is a genuinely new contribution. The phantom node approach is a particularly insightful idea.
*   **Significance:** U-statistics are fundamental in numerous ML applications, making this work potentially impactful. Enabling their distributed estimation opens doors to previously inaccessible scenarios.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The convergence analysis is well-developed and provides useful theoretical guarantees, including data and network-dependent rates.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate practical benefits on real datasets and show improvement over existing methods.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the core ideas accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Complexity:** The phantom node construction adds significant complexity to the analysis, potentially hindering broader understanding and implementation. While justified by the results, it's a substantial conceptual leap.
*   **Asynchronous Convergence:** The O(log t/t) convergence rate for the asynchronous setting is somewhat slower than the synchronous rate and could benefit from further investigation. Though this is a common tradeoff.
*   **Limited Baselines:** While U2-gossip is a relevant comparison, exploring alternatives (even centralized methods for context) could strengthen the evaluation. The supplementary material addresses this somewhat, but a more integrated presentation would be ideal.
*   **Network Assumptions:** The analysis heavily relies on the spectral gap of the network. The sensitivity of performance to deviations from idealized network conditions isn’t fully explored.
*   **Practical Considerations:** While the theoretical memory cost improvement over U2-gossip is noted, a more detailed discussion of the practical memory footprint for high-dimensional datasets would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The core idea is highly original and addresses a significant limitation in decentralized computation.
*   The theoretical results are strong and provide valuable insights.
*   The experimental results support the claims and demonstrate practical benefits.
*   The paper is well-written and presents a clear explanation of the proposed algorithms.
*   The authors have identified a clear gap in the literature and provided a compelling solution.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The phantom node construction adds significant complexity.
*   The asynchronous convergence rate is slightly weaker.
*   The network assumptions may limit the applicability in real-world scenarios.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and significant contribution. While the phantom node approach introduces complexity, the benefits of extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics outweigh this drawback. The theoretical analysis is solid, and the experimental results are encouraging. I believe this work has the potential to open up new avenues of research in decentralized computation and statistical estimation.  I'm willing to overlook the minor flaws in the convergence rate and limited baselines, given the novelty and potential impact of the approach.

**Recommendation:** **Accept** (with minor revisions - clarify discussion of practical memory requirements, and briefly address sensitivity to network deviations)
