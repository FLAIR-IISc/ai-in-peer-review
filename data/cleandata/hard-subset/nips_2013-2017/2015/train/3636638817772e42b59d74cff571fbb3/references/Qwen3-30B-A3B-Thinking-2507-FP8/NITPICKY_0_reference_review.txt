

# Review of "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

## Summary
This paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics, addressing a gap in existing gossip literature which typically handles only separable functions (e.g., averages). The authors introduce "GoSta" algorithms that maintain local estimates and propagate data through auxiliary observations. They prove convergence rates of O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous settings, with network-dependent terms related to spectral gaps. The paper compares favorably to U2-gossip [17] in theory and experiments on AUC and within-cluster point scatter.

## Strengths
The paper identifies a meaningful gap in distributed estimation literature and provides a theoretically sound approach. The convergence proofs are generally well-structured, and the experiments on real datasets demonstrate practical relevance. The phantom node construction is an interesting technical innovation for the analysis. The comparison to U2-gossip is thorough, highlighting advantages in communication complexity and memory requirements.

## Weaknesses

1. **Critical Mathematical Errors**: The paper contains fundamental mathematical errors that undermine the theoretical claims. In equation (1), the authors claim the standard U-statistic differs by a factor of n/(n-1), but the correct factor is (n-1)/n. The standard U-statistic is defined as (n choose 2)^{-1}∑_{i<j}H(X_i,X_j), which equals (n-1)/n times the definition in equation (1). This error affects the entire theoretical framework.

2. **Inconsistent Notation and Formulas**: 
   - Equation (1) has formatting issues: "1 n2 n∑ i,j=1" should be "1/n² ∑_{i,j=1}^n"
   - Algorithm 1, line 4 has "t−1t" which should be "(t-1)/t"
   - Theorem 1's bound contains "1/ct" but c is defined as "c = c(G) := 1− λ2(2)", making the constant dimensionally inconsistent
   - The comparison to U2-gossip (equation 10) has an incorrect factor of √n, which is not justified by the analysis

3. **Technical Inaccuracies**: 
   - The claim "For a complete graph G of size n > 0, 1− λ2(2) = 1/n" is incorrect. For a complete graph, the second smallest eigenvalue of the Laplacian is n, so 1 - λ₂(2) for the matrix W₂(G) would be 1 - (2/n), not 1/n.
   - The expression "∥H - h1_n^T∥" in Theorem 1 is dimensionally inconsistent. H is an n×n matrix, h is an n-vector, and 1_n^T is a 1×n vector, so their difference is not defined.

4. **Missing Key Citations**: The paper cites [17] as the only prior work, but fails to mention [16] which also addresses distributed U-statistics. This omission weakens the novelty claim.

5. **Implementation Details Missing**: The experiments section lacks critical implementation details (e.g., specific values for communication rates, initializations, or stopping criteria) that would be necessary for reproducibility.

6. **Formatting Issues**: 
   - "U -statistics" appears with a space in multiple places (should be "U-statistics")
   - Inconsistent use of "n" vs "n" (sometimes italicized, sometimes not)
   - Algorithm 2 has "Zi ← (1− 1pimi )Zi" which should be "(1 - 1/(p_i m_i))"

## Recommendation

While the paper addresses a meaningful problem and contains some interesting technical insights, the fundamental mathematical errors and inconsistent notation undermine the theoretical contributions. The convergence rate claims are based on incorrect definitions and algebraic manipulations. The experimental results are promising but cannot be properly evaluated without knowing the precise implementation details. The paper would need substantial revision to correct these errors before it could be considered for publication.

The mathematical errors are not minor; they are fundamental to the paper's theoretical claims. A paper that makes such basic errors in its mathematical formulation cannot be considered sound, regardless of the novelty of the problem or the practicality of the results. The reviewers must be confident in the technical correctness of the paper's claims, and these errors prevent that confidence.

The paper has potential value but currently fails to meet NIPS's quality standards due to these critical mathematical errors. I recommend rejection.
