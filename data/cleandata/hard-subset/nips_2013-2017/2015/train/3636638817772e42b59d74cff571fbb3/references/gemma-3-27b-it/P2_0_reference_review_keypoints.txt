- Problem: Extend gossip algorithms to decentralized estimation of U-statistics, which require non-separable pairwise interactions.  
- Contribution: Propose synchronous and asynchronous GoSta algorithms with proven convergence rates O(1/t) and O(log t / t).  
- Technique: Introduce a "phantom node" reformulation to model data propagation and averaging separately for analysis.  
- Experiments: Show improved convergence and lower communication/memory vs U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks.  
- Novelty: First significant extension of gossip algorithms to non-separable U-statistics.  
- Theoretical soundness: Rigorous convergence proofs and analysis linking performance to the network spectral gap.  
- Practical relevance: Applicable to sensor networks and peer-to-peer systems; experiments demonstrate real-world applicability.  
- Comparison to prior work: Clear and compelling comparison to U2-gossip highlighting GoSta benefits.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and organized; introduction motivates contributions clearly.  
- Complexity concern: Phantom node approach adds conceptual complexity and could be made more intuitive.  
- Implementation clarity: Connection between phantom nodes and Algorithm 1 may not be immediately clear to readers.  
- Experimental limitations: Evaluation limited to two applications and a small set of network topologies; broader datasets and methods would strengthen claims.  
- Async limitation: Asynchronous convergence O(log t / t) is weaker than synchronous O(1/t) and could be improved.  
- Scalability concern: Practical implications for very large, complex networks and extreme-scale datasets are not fully explored.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable contributions; minor revisions suggested to clarify phantom nodes and expand experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel extension, rigorous analysis, empirical improvements over prior work, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Phantom node complexity, limited experimental scope, and less compelling asynchronous rate.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).  
- Review criteria: Quality—Excellent; Clarity—Good; Originality—Excellent; Significance—Excellent.
