

# Peer Review: "Extending Gossip Algorithms to Distributed Estimation of U-statistics"

## Summary

This paper addresses a significant gap in distributed computation: extending gossip algorithms to estimate U-statistics, which depend on pairs of observations (e.g., sample variance, AUC, Gini mean difference). While existing gossip algorithms efficiently compute separable functions like averages, they cannot handle U-statistics. The authors propose two new algorithms—GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous)—that combine data propagation and averaging steps. Their key innovation is a "phantom node" reformulation that allows them to separate data propagation from averaging, leading to convergence rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) for the synchronous and asynchronous versions, respectively. The paper demonstrates through theory and experiments that these algorithms significantly outperform the previous state-of-the-art (U2-gossip) in terms of convergence rate, memory usage, and communication cost.

This work builds on the extensive literature of gossip algorithms for distributed averaging (e.g., [19, 10, 2]) and extends it to a more complex class of functions. It also relates to the U2-gossip algorithm [17], which the authors correctly identify as the only prior work on distributed U-statistics computation.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Soundness and Theoretical Contribution**: The paper presents a rigorous theoretical analysis with well-constructed proofs. The phantom node approach is elegant and provides valuable insight into the problem structure. The convergence rates are clearly established and show how network properties (spectral gap) and data characteristics affect performance.

2. **Novelty and Significance**: The problem of distributed U-statistic estimation has been largely unaddressed in the literature. The authors' solution is both novel and practically significant, as U-statistics are widely used in machine learning applications (AUC, clustering quality measures, etc.). The paper clearly demonstrates how their approach improves upon U2-gossip, which is the only prior work in this area.

3. **Experiments and Validation**: The experimental section is strong, with two well-chosen applications (AUC estimation and within-cluster point scatter) on real datasets. The results consistently show GoSta's superiority over U2-gossip across multiple network topologies (complete, grid, Watts-Strogatz). The performance gap increases with network size, which aligns with the theoretical predictions.

4. **Clarity and Organization**: The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The problem statement, algorithm descriptions, and theoretical results are presented logically. The phantom node concept is well-explained with a helpful figure.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Experimental Scope**: While the two applications (AUC and within-cluster point scatter) are well-chosen, the paper would be stronger with additional applications to better demonstrate the breadth of the approach. For example, including results on Kendall tau correlation or Gini mean difference would further validate the framework.

2. **Technical Detail in Proofs**: The supplementary material contains the full proofs, but some of the technical details in the convergence analysis (particularly the transition matrix construction) could be more intuitively explained in the main paper. The phantom node concept, while elegant, might be challenging for some readers to grasp without more intuition.

3. **Practical Considerations**: The paper focuses on theoretical performance but doesn't address how the algorithms would behave in the presence of communication noise, node failures, or varying node speeds in a real-world setting. These are important practical considerations for distributed systems.

4. **Comparison with Baselines**: While the comparison with U2-gossip is strong, the paper doesn't compare with other natural baselines (e.g., centralized computation followed by distributed averaging, or other distributed optimization methods for U-statistics).

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed computation. The problem is important, the solution is novel and well-justified, and the results are compelling. The authors have clearly identified a gap in the literature and provided a rigorous solution that outperforms existing approaches.

The paper meets all the NIPS criteria:
- **Quality**: The theoretical analysis is sound and well-supported by experiments.
- **Clarity**: The paper is well-written and well-organized.
- **Originality**: The problem of extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics is novel, and the phantom node approach is an elegant solution.
- **Significance**: U-statistics are widely used in machine learning, and distributed estimation is a crucial capability for large-scale applications.

## Recommendation

I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS. The work is technically sound, makes a clear contribution to the field, and is well-supported by both theory and experiments. The improvements over U2-gossip are substantial, and the results have clear practical implications for distributed machine learning applications.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. Consider adding a brief explanation of the phantom node concept in the main text to help readers grasp this key insight more quickly.

2. Include one more application example in the experiments (e.g., Kendall tau correlation) to further demonstrate the generality of the approach.

3. Briefly discuss how the algorithms would behave in more challenging distributed settings (e.g., with communication noise or node failures).

4. In the related work section, consider adding a more detailed comparison of the communication complexity between GoSta and U2-gossip to highlight the practical advantages more explicitly.

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed algorithms, and I believe it would be valuable to the NIPS community. The results are both theoretically interesting and practically relevant, making this a strong candidate for acceptance.
