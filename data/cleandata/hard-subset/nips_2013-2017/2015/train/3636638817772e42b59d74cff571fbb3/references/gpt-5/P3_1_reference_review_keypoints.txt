- Problem: Decentralized computation of degree-2 U-statistics via randomized gossip; standard gossip can't handle pairwise functions.
- Methods: Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async combining random-walk propagation of auxiliary tokens and averaging of local estimates.
- Analysis tool: Introduces a “phantom network” (O(n^2) nodes) to decouple averaging and data propagation.
- Convergence rates: Shows expected O(1/t) for synchronous and O((log t)/t) for asynchronous; bounds depend on spectral gap and data-dependent norms.
- Comparison to prior work: Requires one auxiliary observation and one edge update per iteration versus two for U2-gossip; derives a refined U2-gossip bound with an extra √n factor.
- Experiments: Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across three graph families supports theoretical claims.
- Strength: Simple, low per-iteration communication and memory (one edge activation and one auxiliary token per node).
- Strength: Solid theory with explicit dependence on connectivity (1 − λ2) and informative data terms.
- Strength: Phantom-network reformulation is an elegant, potentially reusable conceptual contribution.
- Strength: Fair, constructive comparison to U2-gossip including tighter rates and explanation of averaging benefits.
- Weakness: Ambiguity in norm notation—matrix/vector norms (spectral vs Frobenius) not specified, affecting constants.
- Weakness: All results assume connected, non-bipartite graphs; treatment of bipartite graphs (laziness/self-loops) and effects on constants not discussed.
- Weakness: Asynchronous update intuition is hand-wavy—needs clearer explanation of unbiasedness and local vs global averaging.
- Weakness: Synchronous GoSta recomputes H everywhere each iteration, which can be wasteful for expensive H; incremental variants deserve discussion.
- Weakness: GoSta-async requires knowledge of |E| (pk = 2dk/|E|), which may be unrealistic; robustness to not knowing |E| is not addressed.
- Weakness: Limited empirical baselines—only compared to U2-gossip; lacks alternatives (two-phase sampling, push-sum variants, sketches) and tests on very poorly connected graphs or message/compute cost per accuracy.
- Weakness: Privacy/security of swapping raw observations not discussed; sketches or secured exchanges might be needed in applications.
- Quality: Technically sound; operator derivation convincing, but asynchronous proof intuition should be expanded in the main text.
- Clarity: Well written overall; improve precision on norms, bipartite assumption, and Algorithm 2’s weighting; fix minor typos.
- Originality: Novel extension of gossip to U-statistics with a new analysis technique and improved rates vs prior work.
- Significance: Important problem (AUC, clustering); simple approach with actionable guidance (spectral gap matters) and potential to inspire extensions.
- Questions/suggestions: Can bipartite graphs be handled via laziness and how do bounds change?
- Questions/suggestions: Can GoSta be adapted to directed/time-varying graphs via push-sum?
- Questions/suggestions: Can redundant H evaluations be reduced (endpoint-only updates with corrected weights)?
- Questions/suggestions: Clarify which matrix norm is used and how constants depend on it.
- Questions/suggestions: How sensitive is GoSta-async to misspecification or lack of knowledge of |E|?
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses meaningful problem with simple practical algorithm, strong theory, and clear improvements over U2-gossip.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited empirical scope and baselines; practical assumptions (|E| knowledge, raw-observation swaps) and minor clarity issues remain.
