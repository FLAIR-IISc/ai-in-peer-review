Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes two randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous), to estimate degree-2 U-statistics in decentralized networks. Each node holds a single observation and maintains a single “auxiliary observation” that is swapped along randomly selected edges, while also maintaining a running average of H(Xi, Y) and performing pairwise averaging of local estimates. The key insight is to decouple “data propagation” (to approximate each hi = (1/n)∑j H(Xi, Xj)) from “averaging” (to aggregate hi across nodes). The analysis introduces a phantom-node construction to model data propagation and derive convergence rates. The authors prove convergence in expectation to the true U-statistic at O(1/t) for synchronous and O((log t)/t) for asynchronous updates, with constants depending on a data-dispersion term and on the spectral gap of the network. They contrast their approach with U2-gossip [Pelckmans & Suykens ’10], showing better memory/communication per iteration and faster convergence both theoretically and empirically. Experiments on AUC estimation and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs support the claims.

This work extends the well-studied line on gossip for separable averages (Boyd et al. ’06; Kempe et al. ’03; Dimakis et al. ’10) to the non-separable pairwise setting of U-statistics, which has received limited attention beyond U2-gossip. The spectral-gap dependence mirrors classic results in distributed averaging/consensus and rumor spreading, and the asynchronous modeling follows standard Poisson clock arguments.

Quality
– Technical soundness: The algorithms are simple and sound. The phantom-node analysis is novel and insightful for separating mixing dynamics. The expectation-convergence proofs and rates are plausible and consistent with intuition (faster on better-connected graphs). However:
  • The guarantees are only in expectation for the estimates; no mean-squared error or high-probability bounds are provided, and variance across nodes/time is not analyzed theoretically. This limits practical guidance.
  • There are inconsistencies in the spectral-gap discussion: with Wα(G) = I − 2/(α|E|) L, the second-largest eigenvalue of W2 should be 1 − β2/|E| (β2 is the second-smallest eigenvalue of L), implying 1 − λ2(2) = β2/|E|. The paper states βn−1 is the second smallest and later claims 1 − λ2(2) = 1/n on complete graphs; for K_n, β2 = n and |E| = n(n−1)/2, so 1 − λ2(2) = 2/(n−1), not 1/n. Please correct indices and constants.
  • In the asynchronous model, edges are said to be drawn uniformly from E via an equivalent global clock. With independent node Poisson clocks plus uniform neighbor selection, edge selection is degree-biased; the use of pk=2dk/|E| partly addresses the update step, but the averaging step still assumes uniform edge sampling. This equivalence needs clarification or a formal argument (or adopt a lazy/Metropolis chain).
  • Both algorithms assume the graph is connected and non-bipartite. Standard remedies (self-loops/lazy updates) are not discussed; adding them would broaden applicability.

– Empirical evaluation: Experiments consistently favor GoSta over U2-gossip and show the expected dependence on topology. Yet the experimental section is relatively narrow: modest n and d, only two tasks, limited exploration of spectral gap vs. convergence, and minimal evaluation of the asynchronous algorithm. Communication and memory costs are not quantified beyond qualitative statements, and no comparison to simple baselines (e.g., decentralized sampling of a subset of pairs, flooding, or two-stage: average local hi estimates after random walks) is provided.

Clarity
The paper is generally well-written and organized. The phantom-node exposition is helpful. Some notation and minor typos should be fixed:
– Correct the β-indexing and the complete-graph spectral gap constant.
– Make explicit the distribution of edge selection in both sync/async settings; if uniform over edges is required, outline a decentralized implementation or adjust the analysis.
– Clarify the AUC example: the denominator depends on counts of positives/negatives; indicate how these counts are estimated/assumed known in the decentralized setting.
– A few formatting glitches (line breaks in algorithms, missing references formatting) can be cleaned up.

Originality
Extending gossip to U-statistics with a single auxiliary observation per node and combining with averaging is a neat and, to my knowledge, novel contribution beyond U2-gossip. The phantom-node analysis technique appears original and may be reusable for other non-separable decentralized statistics.

Significance
Estimating U-statistics in decentralized settings is relevant (AUC, Kendall tau, Gini, Wilcoxon, within-cluster scatter). The results provide practical algorithms with provable rates and favorable per-iteration costs. The expectation-only guarantees and limited empirical scope slightly temper impact, but the contribution is solid and likely to be built upon.

Pros for acceptance
– Addresses an important and underexplored problem in decentralized computation.
– Simple algorithms with low memory/communication; both synchronous and fully asynchronous variants.
– Clear theoretical guarantees with spectral-gap dependence; insightful analysis via phantom nodes.
– Empirical results demonstrate consistent improvements over prior art.

Cons for acceptance
– Inconsistencies/typos in the spectral-gap constants and indexing; unclear edge-selection model in async.
– Only expectation bounds; lack of variance/high-probability or mean-square error analysis.
– Experimental scope is limited (small datasets, few topologies, minimal async evaluation), and no communication/accuracy trade-off quantification.
– Requires knowledge of |E| for pk; practicality in large or dynamic networks is not addressed.

Suggestions for improvement
– Provide MSE or high-probability bounds (e.g., via martingale concentration) and/or bound variance across nodes.
– Fix spectral-gap statements and clarify the edge selection process; consider lazy/Metropolis updates to handle bipartite graphs and remove the non-bipartite restriction.
– Remove the need for global |E| by estimating pk online (e.g., via local degree and neighbor sampling) or by using unbiased push-sum-like weighting.
– Add experiments varying spectral gap (e.g., parametrized WS graphs) to empirically validate the dependence; include larger n/d and more U-statistics (e.g., Kendall tau).
– Quantify communication/memory per ε-accuracy and compare to U2-gossip; include asynchronous wall-clock simulations under heterogeneous activation.
– Discuss extensions to directed/asymmetric communication (push-sum) and to nodes with multiple observations beyond the brief remark.

Recommendation: Weak accept
The paper makes a clear, nontrivial extension of gossip to U-statistics with both algorithmic and theoretical contributions and shows empirical gains over the main prior approach. Addressing the noted inconsistencies, expanding the theoretical guarantees beyond expectation, and strengthening the experiments would solidify the contribution.
