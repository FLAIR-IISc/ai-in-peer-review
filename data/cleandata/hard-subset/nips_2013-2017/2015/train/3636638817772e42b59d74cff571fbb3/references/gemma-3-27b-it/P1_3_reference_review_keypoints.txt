- Problem: Extends gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics in decentralized networks, handling pairwise dependencies standard algorithms cannot.
- Method: Proposes synchronous and asynchronous GoSta algorithms that propagate local observations and average local estimates.
- Theoretical results: Provides convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous) depending on network spectral gap and data properties.
- Technical contribution: Introduces “phantom nodes” reformulation to enable analysis of the algorithm.
- Empirical validation: Shows GoSta outperforms prior U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter across network topologies.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on gossip/averaging literature and improves on Pelckmans & Suykens (U2-gossip) with faster convergence, lower memory, and an async variant.
- Strength — relevance: Tackles an important, practical decentralized estimation problem.
- Strength — novelty: Combines data propagation, estimate averaging, and phantom nodes in a clever way.
- Strength — theory: Delivers rigorous convergence analyses with insights on topology and data dependence.
- Strength — experiments & clarity: Empirical support across datasets and clear writing/algorithms; includes asynchronous algorithm for realism.
- Weakness — clarity: Phantom node construction adds conceptual complexity and needs clearer explanation.
- Weakness — assumptions: Asynchronous analysis depends on assumptions (e.g., uniform activation) that may not hold in practice; robustness discussion lacking.
- Weakness — comparisons: Limited empirical comparison beyond U2-gossip; broader baselines would strengthen claims.
- Weakness — practical issues: Lacks discussion of network churn, node failures, and mitigation strategies.
- Weakness — constants: Convergence rates omit discussion of constants (spectral gap/data effects) that impact practical performance.
- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated algorithms addressing an unaddressed problem; async variant increases applicability.
- Against acceptance: Conceptual complexity, potentially unrealistic assumptions, and limited comparative evaluation.
- Overall: Strong contribution worth acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, robustness, and comparative/practical discussions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
