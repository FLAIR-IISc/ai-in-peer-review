- Problem addressed: distributed estimation of U-statistics (pairwise averages) using gossip algorithms.  
- Proposed methods: synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms named GoSta.  
- Key idea: propagate local observations and average local estimates to handle pairwise U-statistics.  
- Theoretical guarantees: convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous), tied to network spectral properties and data terms.  
- Analysis technique: novel “phantom node” construction used for convergence proofs.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter show GoSta outperforms prior work on real datasets.  
- Comparison to prior work: improves over [17] in convergence rate, memory usage, and communication cost.  
- Related literature: correctly situates within gossip/distributed averaging literature and spectral graph theory.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension of gossip algorithms to efficiently estimate U-statistics.  
- Practical improvements: lower memory and communication overheads make the approach more scalable.  
- Asynchronous contribution: provides an asynchronous variant suitable for real-world unsynchronized networks.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written, organized, and algorithms are clearly presented.  
- Complexity of analysis: proofs are involved and reliance on phantom nodes may be hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Network assumptions: analysis assumes connected, undirected graphs; robustness to disconnection or directed graphs is not addressed.  
- Limited experimental scope: experiments use only specific topologies (complete, grid, Watts-Strogatz) and could explore more network parameter variations.  
- Limited baselines: comparison focuses on U2-gossip; simpler distributed averaging baselines are lacking.  
- Practical considerations missing: does not discuss node failures or heterogeneous node capabilities.  
- Acceptance arguments: important problem, novel and theoretically sound algorithms, and convincing empirical results.  
- Rejection concerns: complex analysis, insufficiently comprehensive experiments, and lack of practical robustness discussion.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; suggest clearer intuition for phantom nodes, discussion of directed/disconnected networks, broader experiments and baselines, and practical considerations like failures and heterogeneity.
