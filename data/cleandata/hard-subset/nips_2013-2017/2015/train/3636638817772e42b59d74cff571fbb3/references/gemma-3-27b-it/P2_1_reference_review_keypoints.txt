- Problem: Extends gossip algorithms to distributed estimation of U-statistics, addressing pairwise dependency challenges.  
- Proposal: Introduces synchronous and asynchronous GoSta algorithms that propagate data and average local estimates.  
- Theory: Provides convergence guarantees—O(1/t) synchronous and O(log t / t) asynchronous—dependent on data and network spectral gap.  
- Technique: Uses a "phantom nodes" reformulation as a key analytical tool.  
- Empirics: Demonstrates improved performance over U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks.  

- Strength: Tackles an important, under-addressed problem in decentralized U-statistics estimation.  
- Strength: Novel combination of data propagation, local averaging, and phantom nodes.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis linking convergence to network topology.  
- Strength: Empirical results validate practical effectiveness.  
- Strength: Clear writing and organization.

- Weakness: Comparisons limited mainly to U2-gossip; lacks broader baselines (e.g., distributed SGD, simple averaging).  
- Weakness: Phantom node analysis adds substantial complexity, reducing accessibility.  
- Weakness: Asynchronous analysis is less detailed and yields a slower bound; justification could be expanded.  
- Weakness: Experiments use relatively small networks; larger and varied topologies not explored.

- Quality: Technically sound and rigorously analyzed.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written but phantom node analysis needs more intuition.  
- Originality: Novel application of gossip methods and phantom nodes to U-statistics.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for distributed machine learning and statistics.

- For acceptance: Addresses a challenging problem, offers a novel, theoretically grounded solution with strong empirical support, and links performance to network properties.  
- Against acceptance: Limited baseline comparisons, complex analysis, and weaker asynchronous results.

- Overall: Strong accept recommendation; minor issues (clarity, broader experiments) do not undermine contribution.  
- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.
