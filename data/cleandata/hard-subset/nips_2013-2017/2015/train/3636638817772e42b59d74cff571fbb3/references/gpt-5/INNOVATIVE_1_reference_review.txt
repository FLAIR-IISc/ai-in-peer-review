Summary
The paper studies decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a network using gossip protocols. The authors propose two algorithms: GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous). The core idea is to couple two interleaved processes: (i) propagation of data across the network via random swaps so each node can form a local average hi = (1/n) Σj H(Xi,Xj); and (ii) standard pairwise gossip averaging of the local estimates to approach Ûn(H) = (1/n) Σi hi. A novel “phantom nodes” analytical construction decouples data propagation from averaging to derive clean convergence bounds. For GoSta-sync, E[Zk(t)] → Ûn(H) with rate O(1/t) and data/network-dependent constants involving the spectral gap. For GoSta-async, the bound is O(log t/t). Compared to the prior U2-gossip [Pelckmans & Suykens ’09], the proposed methods reduce memory/communication, work asynchronously, and achieve tighter rates (avoiding a √n factor). Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter over several graph families support the claims.

Relation to prior work
- Builds on classical pairwise randomized gossip for averaging (Boyd et al. ’06) and its asynchronous variants.
- Addresses a problem largely untouched by prior gossip literature: distributed estimation of pairwise (non-separable) functionals; closest is U2-gossip (requiring two parallel random walks without averaging).
- The analysis ties rates to spectral properties similar to consensus, but introduces a novel phantom-graph device to account for data propagation.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: clean decomposition of U-statistics estimation into propagation + averaging, with a new analytical framework (phantom nodes).
- Asynchronous algorithm with unbiased iteration-count correction; practical and aligns with realistic, decentralized settings.
- Theoretical rates: O(1/t) sync and O(log t/t) async, with interpretable data- and graph-dependent terms; clear improvement over U2-gossip, including lower storage/comm costs.
- Empirical validation across tasks (AUC, within-cluster scatter) and topologies; consistent superiority to U2-gossip in accuracy and variance.

Weaknesses / questions
- Only convergence in expectation is proved; standard gossip analyses often provide almost-sure or high-probability guarantees. Can the results be strengthened beyond expectation?
- The constant c′(G) in the asynchronous bound is unspecified; even a qualitative dependence on degrees/spectral gap would help.
- Edge selection model is uniform over E; many gossip settings use a general stochastic matrix P. Can the analysis extend to non-uniform edge sampling and directed/time-varying graphs?
- Assumption that each node knows dk and |E| to compute pk may be restrictive. Can pk be estimated online, or can the update be made robust to unknown |E|?
- Empirical evaluation is narrow: only U2-gossip as a baseline. It would be informative to include additional decentralized baselines (e.g., push-sum variants, tree-based reduce, or map-reduce emulations with limited leaders), and to report communication and memory costs explicitly (messages per edge, bytes moved).
- Minor correctness issue: the manuscript states 1 − λ2(W2) = 1/n for the complete graph; with W2 = I − L/|E|, we have 1 − λ2 = β2/|E| = n/(n(n−1)/2) = 2/(n−1), not 1/n.
- Clarity: the phantom-graph argument is elegant but dense. A small running example or figure tracing a few steps would help readers.

Quality
The paper appears technically sound, with a clear algorithmic design and matching analysis. The bounds are plausible and connect to classic Laplacian/spectral-gap reasoning. The async unbiasedness via mk(t) is neat. Some proofs are deferred; strengthening to almost-sure convergence and clarifying constants would improve rigor.

Clarity
Generally well written and organized. Algorithm boxes are clear. The phantom graph section is terse; more intuition/examples would help. Minor notational issues (e.g., indexing of Laplacian eigenvalues β2 vs βn−1) and the complete-graph spectral-gap value should be corrected.

Originality
High. The problem (U-statistics via gossip) is under-explored, and the combination of averaging with single-observation propagation plus the phantom-node analysis is novel relative to U2-gossip and standard gossip.

Significance
Moderate to high. U-statistics cover many useful metrics (AUC, rank correlation, dispersion), and enabling their decentralized estimation with lightweight, asynchronous gossip is valuable. The results should be of interest to the distributed learning/signal processing community and could serve as a building block for decentralized optimization with pairwise losses.

Pros for acceptance
- Clear, nontrivial conceptual advance with novel analysis.
- Practical asynchronous algorithm with better memory/communication profile and improved rates over prior art.
- Solid empirical evidence consistent with theory.

Cons against acceptance
- Only expectation convergence; constants in async bound unspecified.
- Limited baselines and communication/memory reporting.
- Minor technical inaccuracies and clarity gaps.

Questions/suggestions for authors
- Can you provide almost-sure or high-probability convergence results?
- Clarify dependence of c′(G) on spectral gap/degree.
- Generalize to non-uniform edge sampling (arbitrary P) and discuss directed/time-varying graphs.
- Add communication/memory cost plots and broader baselines.
- Correct the complete-graph spectral-gap statement and eigenvalue indexing.

Recommendation: Weak Accept
Confidence: High on novelty/significance; medium on fine-grained technical constants due to some details in the supplement.
