- Tackles distributed estimation of U-statistics, a challenging problem due to pairwise observation dependencies.
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) for U-statistics.
- Uses “phantom nodes” reformulation as a key technical insight enabling analysis.
- Shows convergence guarantees: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t).
- Theoretical bounds depend on network topology (spectral gap) and data characteristics.
- Empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter demonstrates improvements over U2-gossip.
- Experiments cover multiple network topologies (complete, grid, Watts–Strogatz).
- Paper is generally clear, well-written, and logically organized.
- Phantom node explanation and figure could be more intuitive; a step-by-step example would help.
- Asynchronous rate O(log t / t) is less attractive than synchronous O(1/t); practical trade-offs need more discussion.
- Comparison limited mainly to U2-gossip; broader baseline comparisons would better contextualize gains.
- Scalability discussion (communication and memory) for very large networks is insufficient and needs quantitative analysis.
- Approach and phantom-node analysis are original contributions.
- Work is significant for enabling practical distributed computation of important statistics.
- Technical quality is high with rigorous proofs and convincing experiments.
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an important problem, novel technique, strong theory and experiments, clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity of phantom nodes, weaker async rate, limited baseline/comparative analysis.
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept.
