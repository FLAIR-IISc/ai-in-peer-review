- Problem: distributed estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over networks with pairwise coupling that standard gossip cannot handle.  
- Algorithms proposed: GoSta-sync (synchronous) — nodes keep auxiliary Yk, average Zk with H(Xk,Yk), swap Y’s over random edge and average Z’s.  
- Algorithms proposed: GoSta-async (asynchronous) — random edge selected each iteration, endpoints average Z’s, update running averages with adjusted counters, and swap Y’s.  
- Main theoretical result: E[Zk(t)] → Ûn(H) on connected, non-bipartite graphs.  
- Rates: synchronous O(1/t) with explicit problem- and graph-dependent constants (spectral gap 1−λ2(W2)); asynchronous O((log t)/t) with coarser constants.  
- Analysis technique: phantom-network of size O(n^2) decouples data propagation (Y random walks) from averaging (Z gossip), yielding block-triangular expected transition operator.  
- Comparison to prior work: refined bound for U2-gossip showing √n/t degradation due to lack of averaging.  
- Empirics: improvements demonstrated on AUC and within-cluster scatter across several graph families; asynchrony performs comparably in practice.  
- Strength: problem novelty and practical relevance for metrics like variance, AUC, Kendall’s tau, within-cluster scatter.  
- Strength: algorithmic simplicity — one auxiliary per node, one edge interaction per iteration; lower memory/communication than U2-gossip.  
- Strength: clean spectral-gap-based analysis aligning with prior gossip theory.  
- Strength: synchronous rate gives interpretable data-dependent dispersion terms and dependence on 1−λ2(W2).  
- Weakness: results only in expectation — no high-probability, almost-sure, or MSE bounds; concentration guarantees are missing.  
- Weakness: asynchronous bound is coarse (O(log t/t)) and omits informative data-dependent and spectral-gap terms; likely tighten-able to O(1/t).  
- Weakness: async algorithm requires global |E| knowledge for p_k and m_k — strong and potentially impractical decentralization assumption.  
- Weakness: activation model unclear — uniform random edge sampling assumed but not shown implementable via local Poisson clocks or decentralized mechanisms.  
- Weakness: non-bipartite graph assumption not addressed for bipartite cases (e.g., laziness/Metropolis adjustments and their effect on constants).  
- Weakness: limited comparisons — should test U2 variants with averaging, push-sum/broadcast adaptations, and larger/sparser graphs to reveal spectral-gap effects.  
- Weakness: generality concern — handling of H(X,X)≠0 and standard n(n−1) normalization/diagonal exclusion needs clarification.  
- Weakness: clarity/organization — some derivations terse; suggest more intuition for phantom network, illustrative example, notational fixes, and explicit per-iteration cost comparisons.  
- Questions for authors: provide MSE/high-probability bounds (at least synchronous), tighten async to O(1/t) with spectral-gap constants, and avoid |E| knowledge (e.g., via local counters, Metropolis, or degree-proportional clocks).  
- Questions for authors: clarify realistic activation model and how to implement uniform edge sampling decentralizedly; comment on handling bipartite graphs.  
- Questions for authors: consider empirical spectral-gap sweep (e.g., Watts–Strogatz rewiring) and extensions to U-statistics of order m>2.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept — useful and original contribution with solid theory and experiments, but needs stronger async analysis and clearer decentralized implementability.
