- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics, using propagated local observations and averaged local estimates.
- Provides convergence analyses: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t), with bounds depending on data and network topology.
- Introduces a "phantom node" reformulation to simplify analysis and gain algorithmic insight.
- Empirically validates methods on AUC estimation and within-cluster point scatter, outperforming U2-gossip baseline.
- Addresses an important and practically relevant problem in distributed estimation.
- Extends gossip algorithms to non-separable functions, broadening their applicability.
- Delivers strong, rigorous theoretical guarantees including data- and network-dependent terms.
- Uses a creative and effective phantom node technique for analysis.
- Experiments confirm practical effectiveness on real-world datasets.
- Paper is generally well-written and well-organized.
- The theoretical analysis is complex and relies on spectral graph theory, which may be inaccessible to some readers.
- Comparisons are limited to U2-gossip; broader comparisons to other distributed estimation methods are lacking.
- Asynchronous algorithm converges slower (O(log t / t)) than synchronous; trade-offs and mitigation strategies are under-discussed.
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical implementation issues (node failures, variable network conditions).
- Technically sound with well-supported claims and a notable analytical technique (phantom nodes).
- Clear overall organization, though theoretical sections require background knowledge.
- Novel contribution: extending gossip to U-statistics and phantom node formulation.
- Significant impact potential for distributed estimation in machine learning and data mining.
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an important open problem, strong theory and experiments, novel approach, advances gossip algorithms.
- Arguments against acceptance: complex analysis, limited empirical comparisons, slower asynchronous convergence.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions to expand related work and provide more intuitive explanations of theoretical results.
