- Summary: Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async for decentralized degree-2 U-statistics using auxiliary-sample random walks plus averaging, analyzed via a “phantom nodes” lift; proves expected rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async) and shows lower memory/communication and faster convergence vs U2-gossip with supporting experiments.

- Relation to prior work: Extends randomized gossip literature to U-statistics, refines analysis of U2-gossip, and leverages standard spectral graph and Poisson clock tools.

- Theory quality: Analysis is technically sound (block-triangular operator, spectral dependence); data-dependent terms are informative but results are only in expectation and lack high-probability/MSE bounds.

- Assumptions: Uses standard uniform edge selection and non-bipartite connectivity; async requires nodes to know pk = 2dk/|E| and counters, but robustness to misspecification is not discussed.

- Completeness: Main proofs are deferred to the supplement but key ideas and comparisons to U2-gossip are adequately presented.

- Experiments: Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter across multiple graph types support claims; would benefit from message/byte complexity per ε-accuracy and wider spectral-gap analyses.

- Clarity: Paper is readable and explains the phantom construction; suggested clarifications include handling H(X,X)≠0, explicit per-node communication/memory comparison, how |E| is obtained, and minor proofreading/references fixes.

- Originality: Novel combination of random-walk data propagation with averaging, the phantom-node analytical framework, an asynchronous unbiased-counter variant, plus a refined U2-gossip analysis.

- Significance: Addresses a relevant decentralized problem with simple, resource-efficient algorithms and explicit graph/data dependence likely useful to practitioners and researchers.

- Pros: Clear formulation and motivation, simple low-cost algorithms, solid theoretical guarantees with interpretable dependencies, and consistent empirical validation.

- Cons: Guarantees only in expectation, async requires knowledge of |E|, experiments lack some baselines and communication-accuracy tradeoffs, and focus is limited to degree-2 U-statistics.

- Questions/suggestions: Request high-probability bounds (e.g., Azuma/Freedman), sensitivity analysis to pk errors or adaptive alternatives, extensions to non-uniform/time-varying sampling, privacy considerations or sketching, and discussion of higher-order U-statistics.

- Recommendation: Accept (moderate reviewer confidence).
