- Paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm for computing pairwise U-statistics across networked nodes.  
- Reviewer finds the contribution valuable but asks for stronger connections to existing gossip literature and related ideas.  
- Key algorithmic trick: nodes swap a secondary state (initial observation) during averaging so observations perform coupled random walks — this should be highlighted in the main text.  
- Clarify the induced edge-distribution from the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” model; “drawing a random edge” is ambiguous.  
- Compare to a flooding/memory-heavy alternative (nodes store received tagged observations) to show tradeoffs and whether the 1/t convergence rate is good or bad.  
- Expand related work: cite Dimakis et al. (Proc. IEEE) and other gossip approaches omitted from current references.  
- Investigate lifting constructions (e.g., LADA, lifted Markov chains) as they appear closely related and may accelerate mixing.  
- Theorems/analysis should state assumptions on the initial data distribution (worst-case vs. probabilistic) and make error guarantees explicit as a function of initial data.  
- Reviewer could not verify the supplemental proofs due to time constraints.  
- Small language/notation/typo fixes suggested (several specific phrasing and bipartite directed-graph point).  
- Experiments should include baseline comparisons to demonstrate empirical benefits.  
- After author revisions, reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 and recommends acceptance if comments are addressed.
