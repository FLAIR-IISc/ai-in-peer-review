This paper presents novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for the decentralized estimation of degree-two U-statistics. The authors correctly identify that while distributed averaging of separable functions is well-understood, U-statistics, which depend on pairs of observations, represent a less-explored and important area with applications like AUC, sample variance, and within-cluster scatter. The proposed method, GoSta, cleverly combines a data propagation step, where nodes swap auxiliary data points, with a local averaging step to diffuse estimates across the network. This work directly improves upon the only known prior approach for this problem, U2-gossip [17], by offering better theoretical guarantees, an asynchronous variant, and lower resource requirements.

**Strengths:**
*   **Significance and Originality:** The paper addresses an important and practical problem in distributed machine learning and signal processing. The proposed algorithms for decentralized U-statistic estimation are novel and provide a valuable contribution beyond standard distributed averaging.
*   **Technical Quality:** The theoretical analysis is a major strength. The paper provides explicit convergence rate bounds of O(1/t) for the synchronous case and O(log t/t) for the asynchronous one. The proofs, based on an original reformulation using "phantom nodes," are technically sound and provide clear insights into how convergence depends on both the network's spectral properties and the data's statistical properties.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and well-organized. The problem statement is precise, the algorithms are described clearly, and the theoretical results are stated formally.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The theoretical claims are well-supported by experiments on two distinct real-world tasks (AUC and within-cluster scatter estimation). The results demonstrate convincingly that the proposed GoSta algorithm outperforms the U2-gossip baseline across different network topologies (complete, grid, and Watts-Strogatz), both in terms of convergence speed and estimate variance.

**Weaknesses:**
*   **Comparison of Per-Iteration Cost:** The experiments demonstrate faster convergence with respect to the number of iterations. The paper also argues that GoSta has lower memory and communication costs per iteration compared to U2-gossip. This practical advantage would be more impactful if it were quantified directly in the experiments. Including a comparison of wall-clock time or a table detailing the per-iteration computational complexity, memory usage, and communication volume would make the claimed efficiency gains over the baseline more explicit and compelling.

In summary, this is a high-quality paper presenting a significant and well-executed contribution to the literature on gossip algorithms. The combination of novel algorithms, rigorous theoretical analysis, and solid empirical results makes it a strong submission.
