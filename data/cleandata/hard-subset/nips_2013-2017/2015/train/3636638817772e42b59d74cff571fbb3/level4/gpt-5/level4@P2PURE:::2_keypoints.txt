- Paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute U-statistics (e.g., variance, correlation) over networked observations.
- Overall: solid contribution to gossip literature but needs stronger connection to existing approaches.
- Reviewer could not verify correctness of the supplemental material due to time constraints.
- Algorithmic mechanism: nodes swap and average estimates and also swap secondary state carrying initial observations, which makes initial observations perform coupled random walks—this should be made explicit.
- Related work: expand and position against broader prior work (e.g., Dimakis et al. survey) rather than the current cursory treatment.
- Protocol clarification needed: specify the exact edge-selection distribution induced by the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” process.
- Baseline comparison missing: compare to flooding-with-memory (store-tagged observations) to evaluate the benefit of averaging and the competitiveness of the 1/t rate, especially on expanders.
- Connections to lifting: consider literature on lifted Markov chains and LADA-style constructions to accelerate gossip and relate them to the proposed method.
- Assumptions and guarantees: clarify whether analyses provide worst-case guarantees over all initial data vectors, state data-distribution assumptions, and how error depends on initial data.
- Small editorial fixes suggested (several wording and definition issues across pages).
- After author feedback: reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 but still requests updates to related work (including lifting/LADA) and experiments to include baselines.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the above clarifications, related-work expansion, and baseline experiments.
