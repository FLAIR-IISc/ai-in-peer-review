

This paper addresses an important gap in distributed estimation algorithms by extending gossip methods to efficiently compute U-statistics, which require pairwise averaging over observations. Previous work on gossip algorithms primarily focuses on estimating separable statistics like sample means [2, 10, 15], while U-statistics (which require pairwise interactions) have received limited attention. The authors identify that existing approaches like U2-gossip [17] suffer from high memory and communication costs, as well as asynchronous operation limitations. Their contribution introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms that simultaneously propagate data across the network and maintain local estimates of U-statistics, with convergence rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) respectively.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality. The authors provide rigorous convergence proofs using an elegant "phantom node" reformulation that separates data propagation and averaging steps. The theoretical analysis properly accounts for both data-dependent terms (reflecting problem difficulty) and network-dependent terms (related to spectral gap), which is a significant improvement over prior work. The convergence bounds for their GoSta algorithms are tighter than those in [17], and their analysis of U2-gossip reveals a √n factor that explains its slower practical performance. The numerical experiments on AUC and within-cluster point scatter using real datasets (SMVguide3 and Wine Quality) convincingly demonstrate superior performance across different network topologies (complete graphs, 2D grids, and Watts-Strogatz networks), with lower average error and reduced variance.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear. The problem statement and notation are carefully defined, and the algorithms are presented with precise pseudocode (Algorithms 1 and 2). The phantom node concept is introduced in a way that makes the complex convergence analysis accessible, and the supplementary material provides sufficient detail for reproducibility. The authors appropriately reference related work throughout, including the key gossip algorithms literature [5, 18, 19] and U-statistic theory [12, 14].

The originality is strong. While gossip algorithms for separable statistics are well-studied, this paper successfully extends the framework to handle non-separable U-statistics, which are crucial for important applications like AUC, Gini mean difference, and within-cluster point scatter. The authors clearly articulate how their approach differs from [17], particularly in its asynchronous operation, reduced memory requirements, and tighter convergence rates. The problem formulation and algorithmic approach represent a novel combination of gossip techniques with U-statistic estimation.

The significance of this work is substantial. U-statistics are fundamental for many statistical applications in distributed settings, yet they have been largely overlooked in decentralized computation. The proposed algorithms offer practical advantages over existing methods, with demonstrated performance gains on real-world datasets. The convergence rate analysis provides theoretical justification for the observed improvements, and the algorithms' applicability to high-dimensional data makes them relevant to current machine learning challenges. This work could serve as a foundation for decentralized optimization of U-statistics in future research.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel extension of gossip algorithms to U-statistics with significant practical and theoretical advantages
- Rigorous convergence analysis with clear dependency on problem and network parameters
- Comprehensive experimental validation on real datasets across multiple network topologies
- Well-written with clear presentation and sufficient technical detail
- Demonstrated superiority over the only existing approach (U2-gossip)

Cons for acceptance:
- The paper could better clarify the practical computational complexity per iteration (e.g., time per iteration for GoSta vs. U2-gossip), though the experimental results suggest the performance gains outweigh any potential overhead
- The phantom node construction, while elegant for analysis, may be conceptually challenging for some readers
- The paper doesn't explore applications to other important U-statistics (e.g., Kendall tau, Wilcoxon test statistics) beyond the two shown

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of distributed optimization. The technical quality is excellent, the approach is clearly original, and the significance is well-established through both theoretical analysis and empirical results. The authors have addressed the limitations of prior work while maintaining the simplicity and robustness that make gossip algorithms appealing. I believe this paper would be of great interest to the NIPS community, as it advances our understanding of decentralized estimation for non-separable statistics in a way that is both theoretically sound and practically useful. The work is clearly positioned within the relevant literature and makes a strong case for its significance to the field.
