This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics, addressing limitations of existing approaches. The proposed algorithms demonstrably achieve faster convergence rates compared to the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009) and, crucially, operate effectively in asynchronous communication environments – a more realistic and challenging setting. The empirical evaluation provides strong evidence supporting the superiority of the proposed algorithms in terms of convergence speed and resource utilization.

The paper is well-written and clearly motivated. The authors effectively position their work within the existing literature, highlighting the shortcomings of current methods and articulating the specific contributions of their approach. To the best of my understanding, the algorithms presented are novel, offering improvements over U2-Gossip in both convergence properties and computational cost (considering memory and communication requirements). The ability to function correctly in asynchronous settings is a particularly valuable contribution.

The authors bolster their theoretical findings with a comprehensive empirical evaluation across two datasets. This experimental work includes comparisons to relevant baseline algorithms, convincingly demonstrating the practical advantages of the proposed algorithms. The results presented further validate the theoretical claims and strengthen the overall contribution of the paper. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The proposed Gossip algorithms represent a clear advancement in the field of distributed U-statistic computation.
* **Convergence Rate:** The reported faster convergence rate compared to U2-Gossip is significant, potentially enabling more efficient computations in large-scale distributed systems.
* **Asynchronous Support:** The algorithms’ ability to operate in asynchronous environments is a major advantage, making them more applicable to real-world distributed systems where communication delays are inevitable.
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The extensive experimental results provide compelling evidence of the algorithms’ effectiveness and efficiency.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.

**Weaknesses:**

* While the authors compare to U2-Gossip, a broader comparison with other relevant distributed computation frameworks (e.g., parameter servers, other Gossip-based approaches for similar tasks) would further contextualize the benefits of their approach.  Specifically, it would be helpful to understand how the communication complexity scales compared to alternatives.
* The choice of datasets, while providing initial validation, could be expanded. Exploring datasets with varying characteristics (e.g., different dimensionality, data distributions, and scale) would provide further insight into the robustness and generalizability of the algorithms.
* The analysis of the communication cost could be made more precise.  While stated as an advantage, quantifying the reduction in communication rounds or bytes transferred compared to U2-Gossip (and potentially other baselines) would be impactful.
* A discussion of the limitations of the proposed algorithms would be beneficial. Are there specific scenarios or data characteristics where the algorithms might perform poorly?



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field of distributed computation. The proposed Gossip algorithms offer a compelling combination of faster convergence, support for asynchronous environments, and strong empirical validation. The weaknesses identified are minor and do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend accepting this paper. The work is novel, well-executed, and has the potential to impact a range of applications requiring distributed U-statistic computation. Addressing the points raised in the weaknesses section could further strengthen the paper and its impact.
