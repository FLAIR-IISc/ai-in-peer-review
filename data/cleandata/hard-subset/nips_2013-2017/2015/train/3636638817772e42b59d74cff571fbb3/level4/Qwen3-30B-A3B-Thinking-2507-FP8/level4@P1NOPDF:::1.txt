

This paper introduces novel gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, achieving faster convergence rates than the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) while operating effectively in more realistic asynchronous settings. The proposed approach demonstrates clear theoretical and empirical advantages over existing methods. The paper is well-motivated, clearly positions its contributions relative to prior work, and presents a technically sound argument for the novelty and superiority of the proposed algorithms. Specifically, the authors establish faster convergence rates and lower memory and communication costs compared to U2-Gossip, with the added benefit of robustness in asynchronous environments—addressing a significant limitation in current gossip-based U-statistic computation.

The theoretical analysis is sound, and the empirical evaluation is comprehensive, featuring rigorous comparisons across two datasets against relevant baselines. The experimental results consistently confirm the claimed advantages in convergence speed and computational efficiency, strengthening the paper's core contributions. The presentation is clear and well-organized, enhancing the accessibility of the technical content. While the focus on asynchronous settings is particularly valuable for practical deployment, the authors could further clarify the specific conditions under which the asynchronous guarantees hold (e.g., bounded delays, failure models), though this does not detract from the overall significance of the work.

The paper makes a solid contribution to distributed optimization for U-statistics, offering both theoretical rigor and practical relevance. The proposed algorithms represent a meaningful advancement over existing gossip methods, particularly in their ability to handle asynchronous communication without sacrificing convergence speed. The combination of strong theoretical results and thorough empirical validation makes this a compelling submission. The writing is generally clear, though minor grammatical improvements would enhance readability (e.g., "as far I can see" → "as far as I can see"). Overall, this is a high-quality paper that advances the state of the art in distributed U-statistic computation and deserves publication.
