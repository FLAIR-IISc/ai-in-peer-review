- Proposes new gossip algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics.  
- Demonstrates faster convergence than U2-Gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009).  
- Designed to operate in realistic asynchronous distributed settings.  
- Algorithms are novel and improve convergence as well as resource costs (memory and communication).  
- Paper is well written, clearly motivated, and well positioned relative to prior work.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees and analysis for the methods.  
- Conducts extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets against relevant baselines.  
- Experiments substantiate theoretical claims and show superiority over existing algorithms.  
- Overall, presents an effective, well-justified approach validated by both theory and experiments.
