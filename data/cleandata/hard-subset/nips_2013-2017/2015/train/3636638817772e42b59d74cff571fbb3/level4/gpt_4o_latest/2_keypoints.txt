- Paper introduces a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute pairwise U‑statistics (variance, correlation, scatter coefficients) over networked observations.  
- Primary innovation: when two nodes average estimates they also swap a secondary state (initial observation), causing observations to perform a coupled random walk — this should be emphasized.  
- Analysis approach uses multiple copies of the graph to track these random-walking observations; this motivation should be made clearer in the main text.  
- Reviewer requests stronger connection to prior work on gossip algorithms; current related work coverage is insufficient.  
- Suggest citing and discussing Dimakis et al. (Proc. IEEE) for a broader state-of-the-art perspective.  
- Clarify the induced edge distribution from the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process — the phrase "drawing a random edge" is ambiguous and needs precision.  
- Propose comparing to a memory-heavy flooding approach (nodes store tagged originating values) to evaluate trade-offs and whether the observed 1/t convergence rate is optimal or suboptimal.  
- Recommend examining lifting constructions (e.g., Jung/Shah/Shin, Li/Dai LADA, Chen/Lovasz/Pak) as they are closely related and may accelerate mixing.  
- Ask authors to state assumptions on initial data explicitly and clarify whether guarantees are worst-case over all initial vectors X or depend on data distribution.  
- Minor language/notation fixes and typos listed for pages 1, 4, 6, and 7.  
- Reviewer could not fully verify supplementary material due to time constraints.  
- After author feedback the reviewer raised the score from 7 to 8 but still requests incorporation of lifting literature and experimental baseline comparisons; recommendation to accept if comments are addressed.
