- Problem: Decentralized computation of U-statistics where each node holds one observation (examples: sample variance, AUC, Kendall’s tau).  
- Contribution: Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async gossip algorithms that propagate observations and average local estimates using auxiliary-observation swaps.  
- Mechanism: Each node keeps an auxiliary observation that is swapped on communication, making observations perform coupled random walks.  
- Analysis tool: Introduces a “phantom graph” construction to model data propagation and analyze convergence.  
- Convergence: Establishes expected rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O((log t)/t) (asynchronous), with constants separating data dispersion from network spectral-gap terms.  
- Comparison: Claims lower memory/communication and faster convergence than U2-gossip.  
- Experiments: Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter across several network topologies support the claims.  
- Relation to prior work: Needs stronger positioning within broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al.) and explicit discussion of lifting-based accelerations (Jung–Shah–Shin, LADA, Chen–Lovász–Pak).  
- Strength: Clear, practical algorithms for non-separable statistics with elegant phantom-graph analysis.  
- Strength: Provides an asynchronous variant removing global clock assumptions.  
- Strength: Explicit data/network-dependent rates and reduced memory/communication vs U2-gossip.  
- Weakness: Related work treated narrowly; connections to lifted Markov chains and acceleration techniques are underdeveloped.  
- Weakness: Edge-selection process in the asynchronous model is ambiguous and must be specified precisely (uniform-edge vs node-wake model).  
- Weakness: Assumptions on data and the scope of guarantees (worst-case conditional on X vs distributional) are unclear and should be stated.  
- Weakness: Missing baseline comparisons, notably a flooding-style baseline with memory limits, to contextualize performance.  
- Suggestion: Emphasize the swap/random-walk mechanism earlier in the paper to motivate the phantom-node analysis.  
- Suggestion: State exact edge-selection distribution used in analysis (e.g., pij = 1/|E| vs degree-proportional) and ensure consistency.  
- Suggestion: Compare empirically/analytically to flooding baselines and discuss when averaging is preferable (e.g., expanders vs memory constraints).  
- Suggestion: Discuss whether lifting analyses could yield sharper mixing bounds or guide accelerated swap designs.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly state whether expectations are over algorithmic randomness only (conditional on data) and whether bounds hold uniformly over all data matrices.  
- Minor edits: Several small language and notation fixes suggested (bipartite/undirected wording, phrasing corrections on pp.1,4,6,7).  
- Acceptance assessment: Addresses an important problem with solid algorithms and analysis; recommends acceptance conditional on stronger related-work connections and additional baselines.  
- Final score change: Reviewer raised initial score from 7 to 8 after author clarifications; recommends acceptance pending revisions.
