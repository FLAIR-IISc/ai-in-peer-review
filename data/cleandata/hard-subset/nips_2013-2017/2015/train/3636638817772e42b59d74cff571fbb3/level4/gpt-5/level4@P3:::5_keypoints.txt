- Introduces two randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics using one auxiliary observation per node.  
- Algorithmic update: chosen edge endpoints average estimates and swap auxiliary observations; running averages updated synchronously (all nodes) or asynchronously (active pair).  
- Analysis uses a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple data propagation from averaging, yielding convergence-in-expectation guarantees.  
- Convergence rates: GoSta-sync O(1/t); GoSta-async O(log t / t), with constants depending on data dispersion and the graph spectral gap.  
- Situates work relative to randomized gossip literature and contrasts with prior U2-gossip that propagates two observations without averaging.  
- Provides refined analysis showing U2-gossip has √n/t factor and argues averaging in GoSta often outweighs faster propagation of U2-gossip.  
- Empirical validation on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs for AUC and within-cluster scatter supports theoretical improvements.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 are technically sound under standard assumptions (connected, non-bipartite graphs); phantom-node analysis is insightful.  
- Asynchronous unbiasedness justified via counters mk with E[mk(t)] = t; async rate bound plausible but somewhat loose.  
- Missing high-probability or finite-time concentration (tail) bounds to strengthen theoretical contributions.  
- Assumes uniform edge selection and that nodes know degrees and |E| for pk in async; alternatives (e.g., Metropolis–Hastings) are not discussed.  
- Experimental reporting is per iteration; lacks comparisons versus message complexity, bytes communicated, or wall-clock time.  
- No evaluation under realistic asynchrony (variable delays, message loss) or large-scale/high-dimensional stress tests.  
- Paper is well written and organized; phantom network explanation is clear.  
- Readability improvements suggested: state uniform edge-sampling earlier, clarify sync vs async updates, and define pk/|E| before Algorithm 2.  
- Recommend streamlining notation (Wα(G), L(G), spectral quantities) and fixing minor typos/capitalization.  
- Original contribution: extending gossip to U-statistics with single-observation propagation plus averaging and novel phantom-node analysis.  
- Asynchronous variant with unbiased counters is a practical and useful addition.  
- Significance: enables decentralized estimation of important statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, Kendall tau), with informative spectral-gap dependence.  
- Pros: low per-iteration memory/communication, solid convergence analysis, variance reduction via averaging, reusable phantom-node technique.  
- Cons: only expectation guarantees (no tail bounds), loose async bound with graph-dependent constant, reliance on |E| knowledge, limited experimental realism and scale.  
- Suggested improvements: add high-probability/variance decay results, explore alternative weightings (Metropolis/push-sum), report messages/bytes and wall-clock in async simulations.  
- Further suggestions: test larger/dynamic graphs, provide per-iteration complexity summary (compute/storage/comm), and clarify handling of H(X,X) ≠ 0 and nodes with multiple data points.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, timely contribution with some theoretical and experimental gaps; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
