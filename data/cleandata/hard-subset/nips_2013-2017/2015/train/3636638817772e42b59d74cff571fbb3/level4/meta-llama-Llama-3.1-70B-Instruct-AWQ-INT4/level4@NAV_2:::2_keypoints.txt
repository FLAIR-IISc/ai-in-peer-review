- Paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute U-statistics across a network; contribution is nice but needs stronger connection to existing work.

- Core algorithmic trick: nodes swap a secondary state (their initial observation) so initial observations perform coupled random walks — this should be stated clearly and used to motivate analysis via multiple graph copies.

- Literature gap: cite and situate the work within broader gossip/statistics literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. Proc. IEEE) rather than the current cursory treatment.

- Clarify the edge-sampling model: the induced distribution on edges from the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process must be specified (what "drawing a random edge" means).

- Compare to a flooding-with-memory baseline (nodes store received tagged observations) to show tradeoffs and whether the 1/t convergence rate is good or bad (flooding may perform well on expanders).

- Relate the method to lifting constructions for accelerated gossip (e.g., LADA, lifted Markov chains) and incorporate those references in related work.

- Analysis clarity: state assumptions on initial data (worst-case vs distributional) and make error guarantees explicit as a function of the initial data vector X.

- Minor fixes: bipartite definition implies directed edges; several wording/grammar fixes on pp.1,4,6,7.

- After author feedback: reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 but still urges adding lifting-related citations and including baseline comparisons in experiments.

- Recommendation: accept pending revisions that clarify math/related work and add experimental baselines.
