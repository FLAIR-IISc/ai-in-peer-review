This work introduces novel Gossip algorithms designed for the computation of U-statistics. The proposed methods demonstrate an improved convergence rate over the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm from Pelckmans and Suykens (2009) and, crucially, are designed to operate in more practical asynchronous settings. Empirical results further confirm the advantages of the proposed algorithms when compared to existing techniques.

The paper is well-articulated, the problem is strongly motivated, and the authors have effectively positioned their work within the existing literature. The proposed algorithms appear to be novel and superior in terms of both convergence speed and resource efficiency (memory and communication) when compared to U2-Gossip. The extension to asynchronous environments is a significant strength.

In addition to the theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors provide extensive empirical validation across two datasets, including comparisons to established baselines. This experimental work substantially strengthens the paper's contributions. In essence, this paper delivers new Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics that offer faster convergence and function in realistic asynchronous settings, with comprehensive experiments to validate their performance against the state-of-the-art.
