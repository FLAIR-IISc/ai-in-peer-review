- Proposes a decentralized gossip-style algorithm for computing U-statistics over pairs of observations; valuable contribution but needs better placement in existing literature.

- Core mechanism: nodes average estimates and swap a secondary state (initial observation), causing initial observations to perform a coupled random walk; this should be emphasized in the main text.

- Literature gap: missing broader survey/context (e.g., Dimakis et al.) and other related work on gossip-based statistic computation.

- Ambiguity in edge-selection model: clarify the induced distribution when a node "wakes up and contacts a neighbor" versus drawing a random edge.

- Request baseline comparison to flooding/broadcast approaches (nodes store received values and compute U-statistic) to assess performance and memory trade-offs.

- Recommend discussing connections to lifting/accelerated gossip methods (e.g., Jung-Shah-Shin Lifting, LADA, Chen-Lovasz-Pak).

- Clarify assumptions on initial data (worst-case vs distributional) and state how error guarantees depend on the data.

- Minor fixes: bipartite graph definition appears directed; several wording corrections on p4, p6, p7.
