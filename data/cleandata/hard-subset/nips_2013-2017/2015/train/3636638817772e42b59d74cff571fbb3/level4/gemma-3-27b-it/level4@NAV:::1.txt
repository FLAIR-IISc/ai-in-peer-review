This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. These algorithms exhibit a faster convergence rate than current state-of-the-art methods, such as the U2-Gossip algorithm [Pelckmans, Suykens'09], and operate effectively in more practical asynchronous environments. Experimental results confirm the improved performance of the proposed algorithms compared to existing approaches.

The paper is clearly written, effectively motivates the research problem, and appropriately contextualizes the work within the relevant literature. The proposed algorithms appear to be original and offer advantages over U2-Gossip in terms of convergence and resource utilization (memory and communication overhead). Importantly, the algorithms are designed to function in asynchronous settings.

Supporting the theoretical contributions, the authors provide comprehensive empirical validation using two datasets and comparisons to established baselines. This strong empirical support further reinforces the significance of the work. In summary, this paper presents new Gossip Algorithms for computing U-statistics, achieving faster convergence and extending applicability to realistic asynchronous scenarios, all substantiated by thorough experimental evaluation.
