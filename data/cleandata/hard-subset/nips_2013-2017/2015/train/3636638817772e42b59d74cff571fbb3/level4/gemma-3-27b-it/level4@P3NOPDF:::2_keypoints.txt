- Presents a novel gossip-style decentralized algorithm for U-statistics by exchanging both estimate values and initial observation data, creating coupled random walks for each observation.  
- Valuable contribution to gossip algorithm literature but needs stronger connections to prior work and clearer theoretical foundations.  
- Reviewer lacked time to fully verify correctness of details in the supplementary material.  
- Explicitly highlight the mechanism of exchanging estimates and initial data in the main text to motivate the multi-copy graph analysis.  
- Expand the related work review to cover broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al.) and better position this contribution.  
- Clarify the induced edge-selection distribution from the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” process (e.g., uniform or degree-weighted).  
- Compare the proposed method to a flooding/memory-increase baseline where nodes store tagged observations and compute U-statistics locally, especially on expander graphs.  
- Investigate lifting constructions (e.g., Jung–Shah–Shin, Li–Dai, Chen–Lovasz–Pak) and LADA for potential acceleration and insights.  
- Explicitly state assumptions about the initial data distribution (worst-case vs. stochastic) and define error guarantees as functions of the initial data X.  
- Minor corrections: clarify bipartite definition (directedness), and fix wording/typos on p4, p6, and p7 as noted.  
- After author feedback, reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 but still requests addition of lifting discussion and experimental baselines (e.g., flooding); recommends acceptance contingent on strong revision.
