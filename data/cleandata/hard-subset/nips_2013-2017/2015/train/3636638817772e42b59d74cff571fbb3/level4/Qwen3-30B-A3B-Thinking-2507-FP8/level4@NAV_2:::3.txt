

This paper presents novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics, a significant extension beyond the well-studied case of mean estimation in distributed networks. The authors address an important gap in the literature, as U-statistics (such as AUC, empirical variance, and within-cluster point scatter) are crucial for describing global statistical properties but have received limited attention in decentralized settings. The work builds upon prior gossip algorithms for separable functions [10, 2, 15] while recognizing that these cannot efficiently compute U-statistics, which depend on pairwise observations. The paper's key contribution is the introduction of "GoSta" algorithms that combine data propagation with local estimate averaging, establishing convergence rates of O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous settings with explicit data and network-dependent terms. The authors demonstrate theoretical advantages over the recent U2-gossip approach [17], particularly in memory and communication complexity, and provide empirical validation on two real-world applications (AUC and within-cluster point scatter) across multiple network topologies.

Strengths:
- The paper makes a technically sound contribution with clear theoretical analysis establishing convergence rates that depend on both data properties and network connectivity (via spectral gap), which is a significant improvement over prior work.
- The writing is clear and well-organized, with a logical progression from problem formulation to algorithm design, theoretical analysis, and empirical validation.
- The work demonstrates strong significance by addressing an important class of statistics (U-statistics) that have practical applications in machine learning (AUC for classification, clustering quality measures) while being previously neglected in decentralized settings.
- The comparison to U2-gossip is thorough and well-motivated, with the authors providing refined convergence rate analysis that explains why their approach outperforms existing methods.

Weaknesses:
- While the paper provides convergence rate bounds and empirical results, the draft review's concern about runtime per iteration is valid and needs addressing. The authors should explicitly report computation time per iteration for both their algorithms and U2-gossip to demonstrate practical viability. If their method requires significantly more computation per iteration, this could undermine the claimed advantages, especially for resource-constrained networks.
- The experimental section could be strengthened by including additional baselines and more comprehensive network topologies to better establish the generality of their approach.

The paper meets all four criteria for quality assessment:
- Quality: The theoretical analysis is sound with well-supported convergence bounds, and the experimental validation on real datasets provides strong empirical support.
- Clarity: The paper is well-written with clear notation and logical flow, though the runtime per iteration concern requires additional clarification.
- Originality: The approach using phantom nodes for analysis represents a novel technical contribution that distinguishes this work from prior gossip algorithms and U2-gossip.
- Significance: U-statistics are fundamental to many statistical applications, and the proposed algorithms enable efficient decentralized computation of these quantities, which has clear potential for practical impact.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of distributed algorithms, advancing the state of the art in decentralized computation of non-separable statistics. The main concern regarding computational complexity per iteration needs to be addressed in the final version to fully establish the practical utility of the proposed methods. I recommend acceptance with the understanding that the authors should provide runtime comparisons in the final version.
