- Addresses the important but under-explored problem of distributed estimation of U-statistics in networks.  
- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms that propagate data while maintaining local U-statistic estimates.  
- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with bounds depending on data characteristics and network topology.  
- Introduces a “phantom node” reformulation that enables rigorous analysis and appears clever.  
- Convergence analysis is comprehensive and highlights improved performance on well-connected networks and easier estimation problems.  
- Numerical experiments show performance superior to a previously introduced approach.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents the mathematical framework.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to simpler or adapted baseline methods (e.g., straightforward averaging or existing gossip variants).  
- Phantom node construction would benefit from a more intuitive explanation for broader accessibility.  
- Extension to nodes holding multiple observations is mentioned but needs elaboration on implications and trade-offs.  
- Asynchronous setting discussion should clarify practical implementation issues and the impact of clock modeling assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen experiments and clarify the phantom/asynchronous aspects.
