- Problem & contribution: Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics via gossip, using one auxiliary observation per node and averaging updates; proves O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async) rates with constants depending on data dispersion and network spectral gap, and introduces a “phantom nodes” analysis; refines U2-gossip rates.

- Novelty: First to give both synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms with explicit rates and network/data dependencies for U-statistics beyond prior U2-gossip.

- Strength — scope: Extends gossip methods to important pairwise U-statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, Kendall tau, within-cluster scatter).

- Strength — theory: Clear, interpretable convergence guarantees tied to spectral graph theory; original phantom-network construction.

- Strength — algorithm design: Low resource use (one auxiliary observation per node, single edge activation per iteration), and a practical asynchronous variant.

- Strength — experiments: GoSta-sync converges faster and with lower across-node variance than U2-gossip; GoSta-async performs comparably in practice.

- Weakness — practical cost metrics missing: Need wall-clock time, per-iteration runtimes (computing H, messaging, sync overhead), communication complexity, memory per node, and time/messages to reach target ε to assess real-world utility.

- Weakness — |E| requirement in async: GoSta-async assumes nodes know pk = 2dk/|E| (hence |E|), which may be unrealistic; paper should discuss estimating |E| online, removing the need (push-sum, lazy walks, local sampling), and robustness to pk errors.

- Weakness — bipartite graphs: Analysis assumes non-bipartite graphs; authors should discuss lazy updates (self-loops) to avoid periodicity and whether analysis extends.

- Weakness — edge selection model: Synchronous experiments sample edges uniformly, which may require a central scheduler; clarify mapping to local Poisson-clock neighbor selection and whether guarantees carry over.

- Weakness — privacy: Method propagates raw observations (Y swaps); discuss privacy-preserving variants (sketches, secure aggregation, random features).

- Weakness — limited empirical breadth: Experiments limited to AUC and within-cluster scatter with n ≈ 10^3–10^3.5; request larger graphs, sensitivity to connectivity (vary Watts–Strogatz p), multi-observation-per-node experiments, and additional U-statistics (e.g., Kendall tau).

- Quality: Algorithms and analyses appear technically sound and well connected to spectral graph theory; proofs deferred but plausible; some assumptions (non-bipartite, |E| knowledge) need clearer discussion.

- Clarity: Generally clear and well organized; phantom-network is explained well; suggest more intuition for Wα(G), a summary of per-iteration cost vs U2-gossip, and minor typo fixes.

- Originality: High — novel combination of data propagation and averaging for U-statistics, phantom-node analysis, and asynchronous rates.

- Significance: Moderate to high — U-statistics are pervasive and the improved rates and lower per-iteration resources are meaningful, especially on well-connected graphs.

- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an underexplored, important problem with a simple general method, solid theory with explicit bounds, clear improvements over prior work, and a practical async algorithm.

- Cons for acceptance: Lacks wall-clock and communication-cost evaluation, relies on |E| knowledge in async, has limited empirical breadth, and omits privacy discussion.

- Overall recommendation: Positive—well-motivated and carefully analyzed with promising empirical results; reviewer leans toward acceptance conditional on clarifying practical cost metrics and the |E| assumption and expanding experiments if space permits.
