This paper presents new Gossip algorithms for computation of U-statistics, with faster convergence rate compared to state-of-the-art (U2-Gossip algorithm from Pelckmans, Suykens'09) as well as work in the more realistic asynchronous settings. The proposed algorithms have several key strengths. Firstly, the convergence rate is improved compared to existing algorithms, which is a significant contribution to the field. Secondly, the algorithms work in asynchronous settings, which is a more realistic assumption for many practical applications. Finally, the experimental results further demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms compared to existing algorithms.

The paper is well written, the problem is well motivated, and the authors have well positioned their work with respect to the existing literature. As far as I can see, the proposed algorithms are novel and superior in terms of convergence and cost (memory and communication) compared to the U2-gossip algorithm. Also, the proposed algorithm works for asynchronous settings, which is a significant advantage.

Apart from theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors have also done extensive empirical evaluations over two datasets and compared to the baselines. This further strengthens the contributions of the paper. The experimental results are thorough and well presented, and the authors have done a good job of comparing their algorithms to existing methods.

The quality of the paper is high, the claims are well supported by theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations, and the presentation is clear and concise. The authors have done a good job of explaining the technical details of the algorithms and the theoretical analysis, and the paper is well organized and easy to follow.

Overall, I think this is a strong paper that makes significant contributions to the field of distributed computation of U-statistics. The proposed algorithms are novel, efficient, and well motivated, and the experimental results demonstrate their effectiveness. I would recommend accepting this paper for publication.

Strengths:

* Novel and efficient algorithms for computing U-statistics
* Improved convergence rate compared to existing algorithms
* Works in asynchronous settings
* Thorough and well presented experimental results
* Well written and well organized paper

Weaknesses:

* None that I can see. The paper is well written, the technical details are clear, and the experimental results are thorough and well presented.

Acceptance:

* Yes, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication. The contributions are significant, the technical details are sound, and the experimental results are thorough and well presented.
