- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical convergence guarantees: synchronous O(1/t), asynchronous O(log t / t).  
- The derived rates improve upon prior gossip-based methods for U-statistics in distributed settings.  
- Technical contributions are clearly articulated with well-structured derivations justifying the analysis.  
- Addresses a relevant problem domain (large-scale statistical learning and networked systems) with broad applicability.  
- Novelty: handling U-statistics via gossip protocols with formal convergence proofs not seen before.  
- Experimental results substantiate theoretical claims, showing clear performance advantages across datasets and network topologies.  
- Empirical validation shows reduced communication rounds and faster convergence, particularly for the asynchronous variant.  
- Experiments are well-designed with appropriate metrics (estimation error, iterations) and statistical significance.  
- Paper is well-organized with accessible definitions, assumptions, and pseudocode that facilitate reproducibility.  
- Minor suggested improvement: add discussion of per-iteration computational complexity or scalability limits beyond experiments.  
- Overall assessment: meaningful, theoretically sound, empirically validated contribution deserving strong acceptance recommendation.
