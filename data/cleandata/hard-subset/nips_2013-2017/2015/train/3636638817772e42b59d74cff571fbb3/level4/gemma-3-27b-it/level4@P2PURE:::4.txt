This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in a distributed setting. The proposed algorithm is intuitively appealing, although its novelty appears limited – despite the thoroughness of the provided proofs.

A significant weakness is the lack of comparison to baseline methods. Several intuitive baselines could be readily constructed using randomized algorithms, and exploring these would strengthen the empirical evaluation. While the paper tackles an interesting problem, the evaluation is weak due to the absence of baseline comparisons.
