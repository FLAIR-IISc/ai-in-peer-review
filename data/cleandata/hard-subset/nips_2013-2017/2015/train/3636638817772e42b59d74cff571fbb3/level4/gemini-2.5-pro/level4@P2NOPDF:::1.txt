This paper presents novel gossip algorithms for the decentralized computation of U-statistics. The proposed methods offer significant improvements over the state-of-the-art, specifically the U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009). Key advantages include a provably faster convergence rate and, critically, the ability to operate in more realistic asynchronous network settings.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The problem of decentralized U-statistic estimation is well-motivated, and the authors effectively position their contributions with respect to the existing literature. The proposed algorithms appear to be novel. From a theoretical standpoint, they are demonstrably superior to prior work, not only in convergence speed but also in terms of reduced memory and communication costs. The extension to asynchronous environments is a particularly valuable and practical contribution.

The theoretical analysis is complemented by a thorough empirical evaluation. The authors conduct extensive experiments on two datasets, comparing their proposed algorithms against relevant baselines. The results presented convincingly demonstrate the practical advantages of the new methods, aligning with the theoretical guarantees. This combination of rigorous theory and empirical validation significantly strengthens the paper's contributions.
