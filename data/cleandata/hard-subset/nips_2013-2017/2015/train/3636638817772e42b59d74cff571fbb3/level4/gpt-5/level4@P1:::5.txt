This paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics. Each node maintains a local estimate while data propagate across the network. The authors provide convergence guarantees: O(1/t) for the synchronous version and O(log t / t) for the asynchronous version. Experiments demonstrate strong performance and accuracy, with clear advantages over alternative approaches.

Quality: The technical development appears sound. The algorithms are derived clearly, and the convergence rates are established. The empirical results support the theoretical claims and illustrate the benefits of the proposed methods.

Clarity: The paper is well organized and easy to follow. The presentation thoughtfully connects the algorithmic design to the convergence analysis and provides experiments that demonstrate the practical impact.

Originality: The work introduces new gossip algorithms tailored to U-statistics, extending beyond the well-studied case of averaging separable functions. The specific treatment of synchronous and asynchronous settings with the stated convergence rates is a valuable contribution.

Significance: The problem is of interest for decentralized learning and sensing applications where pairwise statistics such as AUC, empirical variance, Gini mean difference, and within-cluster scatter are relevant. The proposed techniques are likely to be useful to various researchers working on distributed computation, and the empirical performance is compelling.

Pros:
- Clear algorithms for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.
- Proven convergence rates of 1/t and log t / t, respectively.
- Strong empirical results demonstrating performance and accuracy advantages.
- Well-written and well-structured presentation.

Cons:
- None identified in this review.
