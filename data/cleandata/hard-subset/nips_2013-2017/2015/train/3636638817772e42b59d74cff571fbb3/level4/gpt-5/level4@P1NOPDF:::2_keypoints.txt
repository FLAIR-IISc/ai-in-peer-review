- Paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute U-statistics (e.g., variance, correlation) by averaging and swapping secondary state variables.
- Main contribution is solid, but the connection to prior work and known ideas needs strengthening.
- Algorithmic insight: clarify that initial observations execute coupled random walks when nodes swap secondary state variables, motivating analysis via graph lifts.
- Related work: expand discussion and position the paper within broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al., Proc. IEEE 2010) to clarify novelty.
- Edge selection: explicitly state the edge-selection distribution induced by the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” process (degree-proportional vs. uniform-over-edges, etc.).
- Baseline comparison: add a flooding-based baseline where nodes store received observations tagged by origin, and compare its memory/accuracy trade-offs to the proposed method.
- Lifting constructions: discuss prior work on lifted Markov chains to accelerate gossip/consensus (e.g., Jung et al. 2007; Li & Dai 2010; Chen, Lovász & Pak 1999) and relate to your construction.
- Data assumptions and guarantees: state whether error guarantees are worst-case over initial data X, in expectation, or under distributional assumptions, and make dependencies explicit.
- Small fixes: correct bipartite definition that implies directed graphs and the typos/phrasing on p4, p6, p7 (specific edits supplied).
- After author feedback, reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 but still requests stronger engagement with lifting methods and inclusion of the recommended baselines.
- Overall recommendation: accept if revisions address lifting/related work, edge-selection clarity, data-assumption guarantees, and stronger experimental baselines.
