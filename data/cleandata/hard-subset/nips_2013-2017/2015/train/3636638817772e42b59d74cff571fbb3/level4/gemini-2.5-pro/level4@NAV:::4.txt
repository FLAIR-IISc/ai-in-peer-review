This work addresses the challenge of computing average statistics in a distributed setting. While the proposed algorithm is conceptually straightforward, which may limit its perceived novelty, the theoretical analysis is rigorous and the accompanying proofs are thorough.

A significant limitation of the paper is the absence of any comparison against baseline methods. It is highly likely that several intuitive baselines, such as those leveraging randomized algorithms, could be formulated. Incorporating such comparisons into the experimentation is essential. In its current form, the paper addresses a relevant problem, but the empirical evaluation is weak due to the lack of these necessary benchmarks.
