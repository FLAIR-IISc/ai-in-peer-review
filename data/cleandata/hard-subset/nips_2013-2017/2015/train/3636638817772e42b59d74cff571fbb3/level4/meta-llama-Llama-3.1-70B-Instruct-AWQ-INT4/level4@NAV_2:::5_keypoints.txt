- Proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics in a decentralized setting.  
- Extends gossip methods to quantities that depend on pairs of observations, addressing a less-studied problem.  
- Establishes convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear background and related-work discussion.  
- Algorithms and convergence analysis are introduced in a logical, easy-to-follow manner.  
- Numerical experiments show the proposed methods outperform existing approaches such as U2-gossip on real datasets.  
- Strength: novel contribution to gossip algorithms by handling U-statistics.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with explicit convergence bounds.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation on real-world datasets and detailed comparison with prior work.  
- Weakness: assumes a simple network model where each node has a single observation.  
- Weakness: extension to nodes with multiple observations is mentioned but not fully explored.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion of practical implications, applications, and real-world limitations.  
- Originality: significant and novel extension of gossip algorithms to U-statistics with rigorous analysis.  
- Significance: addresses an important decentralized-estimation problem with potential impact in ML, data mining, sensor networks, and related applications.  
- Recommendation balance: compelling theoretical and empirical contributions argue for acceptance, but limited modeling scope and practical discussion are arguments against.
