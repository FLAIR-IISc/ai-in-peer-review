This paper introduces new gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The proposed methods achieve faster convergence than the state of the art (the U2-Gossip algorithm of Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) and operate in more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results further highlight the superiority of the proposed approach over existing methods.

The paper is clearly written, the problem is well motivated, and the authors position their contribution appropriately with respect to prior work. To the best of my knowledge, the algorithms are novel and superior in terms of convergence and resource costs (memory and communication relative to U2-Gossip). In addition, the proposed approach supports asynchronous operation.

Beyond the theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors conduct extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets and compare against relevant baselines. This further strengthens the paper’s contributions. Overall, the work proposes new gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, with faster convergence and applicability to realistic asynchronous settings. Furthermore, the methods are evaluated and contrasted with the state of the art through extensive experiments.
