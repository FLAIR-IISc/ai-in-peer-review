The paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms to efficiently compute U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The main idea of the paper is to extend gossip algorithms, which have been extensively studied in the context of decentralized averaging in networks, to compute quantities that take the form of an average over pairs of observations.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, including the motivation, background, and related work. They also provide a thorough description of their approach, including the proposed algorithms, convergence analysis, and experimental results. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and easy to follow.

The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, are novel and interesting. The authors provide a detailed convergence analysis of the algorithms, including bounds on the convergence rate. The experimental results show that the proposed algorithms outperform the baseline method, U2-gossip, in various scenarios.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and concision. The authors do an excellent job of explaining complex concepts and ideas in a clear and easy-to-understand manner. The paper is also well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' line of reasoning.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. First, the authors could provide more details on the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms. Specifically, it would be helpful to know the run-time per iteration for the proposed and U2-gossip methods. If each iteration of the proposed method is much slower than U2-gossip, then the paper would be of limited utility.

Additionally, the authors could provide more experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms. While the results on the two datasets are promising, it would be helpful to see results on additional datasets to further demonstrate the robustness of the approach.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel and interesting approach to computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. With a few minor revisions to address the areas mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
