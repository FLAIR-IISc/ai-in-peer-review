This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. These algorithms exhibit a faster convergence rate than current state-of-the-art methods, such as the U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009), and operate effectively in more practical asynchronous environments. Experimental results validate the improved performance of the proposed algorithms compared to existing approaches.

The paper is clearly written, effectively motivates the problem, and appropriately contextualizes the work within the existing literature. The proposed algorithms appear to be original and offer advantages over U2-Gossip in terms of convergence speed and resource utilization (memory and communication). Importantly, the algorithms are designed to function in asynchronous settings. 

Complementing the theoretical analysis, the authors provide comprehensive empirical validation using two datasets and comparisons to established baselines, further supporting the paper’s contributions. In summary, this work presents new Gossip Algorithms for U-statistic computation, demonstrating both faster convergence and applicability to realistic asynchronous scenarios, substantiated by thorough experimental evaluation.
