- Addresses decentralized computation of U-statistics via gossip — a significant extension covering measures like variance, AUC, and Gini mean difference.  
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms with convergence rate bounds and improvements over prior work.  
- Core idea: nodes exchange estimates plus auxiliary state variables representing initial observations, letting observations perform random walks (reformulated using “phantom nodes”).  
- Manuscript should highlight the auxiliary-state/phantom-node mechanism more prominently to better motivate the analysis.  
- Related work section needs expansion (e.g., cite Dimakis et al. 2010) to better situate the contribution within broader gossip-based statistical computation.  
- Clarify how a “random edge” is drawn (uniformly or weighted by edge properties).  
- Include a baseline comparison where nodes flood and maintain all received observations to evaluate memory/communication trade-offs and whether O(1/t) is competitive.  
- Discuss lifting/acceleration constructions (Jung et al. 2007; Li & Dai 2010; Chen, Lovász & Pak 1999) as they relate to the phantom-node approach.  
- Clarify theoretical assumptions about worst-case initial data and explicitly state error guarantees as functions of initial data.  
- Minor language fixes and clarify that the bipartite graph definition currently implies directionality and should be fixed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, solid theoretical analysis, empirical validation, and potential impact on distributed statistical estimation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited connection to existing work, missing baseline comparisons, and some clarity issues in presentation.  
- Recommendation: accept (score 8/10), contingent on addressing related-work expansion and inclusion of baseline comparisons.
