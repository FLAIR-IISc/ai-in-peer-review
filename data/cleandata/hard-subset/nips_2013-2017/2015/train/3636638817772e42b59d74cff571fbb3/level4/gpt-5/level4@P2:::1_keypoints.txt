- Proposed two randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics.  
- Each node stores a local estimate plus one auxiliary observation that diffuses via random edge selections.  
- Analysis uses a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple data propagation from averaging.  
- Proven convergence in expectation to the target U-statistic with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Bounds explicitly depend on data dispersion around local means and on network spectral gap.  
- Compared to U2-gossip, methods use less memory and communication (one auxiliary per node, one edge per iteration) and achieve better theoretical and empirical performance.  
- Experiments (AUC, within-cluster scatter) across several network topologies support claimed improvements and show lower variance across nodes.  
- Strength: clear motivation—many relevant statistics are non-separable U-statistics unsuited to standard gossip.  
- Strength: novel methodology combining data propagation with local averaging and phantom-network analysis.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees with finite-time bounds tied to network spectral properties and improved rates over prior work.  
- Strength: practical asynchronous algorithm suitable for unsynchronized networks.  
- Strength: empirical validation shows consistent gains over U2-gossip.  
- Weakness: results are expectation-only; no high-probability or almost-sure convergence or node-wise variance control.  
- Weakness: asynchronous bound is looser and depends on a global norm; data-dependent refinements are not provided.  
- Weakness: model assumes connected, non-bipartite undirected graphs and uniform edge activation; robustness to message loss, delays, time-varying or directed topologies is not addressed.  
- Weakness: algorithm requires pk = 2dk/|E|, so nodes need |E|—how to obtain/estimate it and effect of misspecification is unclear.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited (two datasets, few network sizes); missing communication/iteration complexity to reach ε, wall-clock times, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: narrow baseline comparison (mainly U2-gossip); missing alternatives like push-sum, randomized pairing with reservoir sampling, or mini-batch pairwise estimators.  
- Weakness: some proofs deferred—key proof sketches beyond the phantom idea would aid readability.  
- Clarity suggestions: explain how nodes learn |E|, handle bipartite graphs (lazy randomization), and present memory/communication costs vs. U2-gossip with intuition for constants in rates.  
- Originality: addresses underexplored problem; synchronous/asynchronous schemes and phantom analysis are a genuine advance.  
- Significance: simple, scalable algorithms applicable to ranking, clustering, and correlation tasks in distributed networks, though broader validation and stronger probabilistic guarantees would increase impact.  
- Open questions for authors: provide high-probability bounds, |E|-free async variants, sensitivity to non-uniform activation/degree heterogeneity, use of mini-batches for sparse graphs, and extensions to time-varying or directed graphs.  
- Pros for acceptance: fills gap on decentralized U-statistics, sound theory with explicit data/network dependence, practical asynchronous algorithm with lower resource use, consistent empirical improvements.  
- Cons for acceptance: expectation-only guarantees, conservative async rate, limited baselines and experimental scope, and restrictive graph/activation assumptions.
