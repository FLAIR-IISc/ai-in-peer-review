- Proposes new Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics with faster convergence than prior work.  
- Demonstrates the algorithms operate in realistic asynchronous settings.  
- Empirical results on two datasets show superiority over existing baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and places the work clearly in context.  
- Algorithms are novel and improve on convergence, memory, and communication compared to U2-Gossip.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including convergence rate bounds and comparisons.  
- Extensive experimental evaluation strengthens the theoretical claims.  
- Related work review (Section 3) is clear and helps situate the contribution.  
- Strengths: novelty, faster convergence, asynchronous operation, and rigorous theory + experiments.  
- Weaknesses: minor presentation issues and some sections (notably Section 4) could be better organized.  
- Weaknesses: some notation and terminology need clearer explanation for non-expert readers.  
- Technical quality is high and claims are well-supported.  
- Clarity/organization is good overall but can be improved in parts.  
- Originality and significance are high, with potential broad impact in decentralized computation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; should be accepted for publication.
