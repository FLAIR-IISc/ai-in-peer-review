- Supplementary material correctness was not verified due to time constraints.  
- Main algorithm uses dual-state updates where nodes average estimates and swap a secondary state (initial observation) causing observations to perform coupled random walks; this key mechanism should be emphasized.  
- Related work is insufficiently discussed; situate the contribution within broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. survey) to clarify novelty.  
- Communication model is ambiguous: specify the edge-selection probability (uniform edge sampling vs. node-wakes-and-contacts-neighbor) and its implications.  
- Missing baseline comparison: evaluate against a flooding/memory-rich approach where nodes store received observations and compute U-statistics locally to contextualize the 1/t convergence and memory–performance trade-offs.  
- Possible connection to "lifting" Markov chains and accelerated gossip should be investigated and discussed (cite Jung et al., Li & Dai, Chen et al.).  
- Theoretical guarantees need clarity: state assumptions on initial data and how error bounds depend on the initial data vector (worst-case vs. distributional statements).  
- Minor edits: clarify bipartite graph notation for directed/undirected implication and fix multiple phrasing/grammar errors (several lines on p4, p6, p7).  
- Recommendation: strengthen exposition of core mechanism, expand related work (including lifting connections), and include flooding baseline experiments; if addressed, the paper merits acceptance.
