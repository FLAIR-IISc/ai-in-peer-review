- Addresses distributed computation of U-statistics, filling a gap beyond sample-mean gossip literature.  
- Proposes novel gossip algorithms that improve convergence and resource usage (memory, communication) over U2-Gossip.  
- Extends algorithms to the asynchronous setting, increasing practical applicability.  
- Introduces a “phantom node” reformulation to facilitate convergence analysis.  
- Proves convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.  
- Theoretical bounds incorporate data-hardness terms and network spectral-gap dependencies.  
- Empirical evaluation on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks shows clear superiority to U2-Gossip.  
- Experiments across complete, 2D grid, and Watts–Strogatz topologies validate theory about network connectivity effects.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates itself within relevant related work.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical and empirical contribution addressing an under-explored problem.  
- Weakness: phantom-node technique needs more intuitive explanation in the main text.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to other baseline gossip/averaging algorithms for broader context.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of algorithm limitations such as performance under highly skewed data.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-supported contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
