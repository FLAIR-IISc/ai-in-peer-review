This paper addresses the challenge of computing average statistics in a distributed setting, presenting an algorithm that, while intuitive, may not exhibit significant novelty, despite being supported by thorough proofs.

A notable limitation of the paper is the absence of comparisons with established baseline measures. It is likely that multiple intuitive baselines can be devised using randomized algorithms, and some of these should be considered for experimental evaluation. Although the paper tackles an intriguing problem, its evaluation is compromised by the lack of baseline comparisons, which undermines the overall assessment of the proposed approach.
