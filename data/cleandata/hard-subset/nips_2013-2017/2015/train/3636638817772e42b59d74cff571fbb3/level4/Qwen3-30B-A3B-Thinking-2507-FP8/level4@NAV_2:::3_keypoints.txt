- Presents novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms ("GoSta") for decentralized estimation of U-statistics, filling a gap beyond mean estimation.

- Addresses an important problem since U-statistics (AUC, empirical variance, within-cluster scatter) are crucial yet underexplored in decentralized settings.

- GoSta combines data propagation with local estimate averaging to handle pairwise-dependent U-statistics unlike separable-function gossip.

- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit dependence on data properties and network spectral gap.

- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and links convergence to both data and network connectivity.

- Writing is clear and well-organized, with a logical progression from formulation to algorithms, theory, and experiments.

- Demonstrates practical significance by targeting widely used statistics (e.g., AUC) with potential real-world impact.

- Provides a thorough, well-motivated comparison to U2-gossip, showing advantages in memory and communication complexity and refined convergence rates.

- Empirical validation on two real-world applications across multiple topologies supports the theoretical claims.

- Main weakness: runtime per iteration is not reported; authors should provide computation-time comparisons with U2-gossip to assess practical viability.

- Additional weakness: experiments would be strengthened by more baselines and a broader range of network topologies.

- Overall assessment: meets criteria—quality (sound theory/experiments), clarity (good, but runtime unclear), originality (novel phantom-node analysis), significance (enables decentralized U-statistics); recommendation: accept pending runtime comparisons.
