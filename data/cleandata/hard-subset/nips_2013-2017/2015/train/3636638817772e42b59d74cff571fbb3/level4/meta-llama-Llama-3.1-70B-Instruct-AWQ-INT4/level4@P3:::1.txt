This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms, demonstrating a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art U2-gossip algorithm.

The paper is well-written, and the problem is well-motivated, with a clear positioning of the work within the existing literature. The proposed algorithms are novel and offer superior convergence rates and lower memory and communication costs compared to U2-gossip. Notably, the algorithms also work in asynchronous settings, making them more practical for real-world applications.

The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of their algorithms, including a reformulation of the problem using "phantom nodes" to model data propagation and estimate updates. They establish convergence rate bounds of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) for the synchronous and asynchronous cases, respectively, with explicit data and network-dependent terms.

The experimental results presented in the paper further demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms. The authors evaluate their algorithms on two real-world datasets, the SMVguide3 binary classification dataset and the Wine Quality dataset, and compare their performance to U2-gossip. The results show that the proposed algorithms achieve better performance in terms of average relative error and variance, with a significant improvement in the asynchronous setting.

The paper also provides a thorough review of related work on gossip algorithms, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and the advantages of the proposed algorithms. The authors discuss the potential applications of their work, including decentralized optimization of U-statistics, and outline future research directions.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed computing and machine learning, providing new algorithms and analysis for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The paper is well-written, and the results are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental evaluations.

Strengths:

* Novel and efficient gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics
* Improved convergence rates and lower memory and communication costs compared to state-of-the-art
* Works in asynchronous settings, making it more practical for real-world applications
* Comprehensive analysis and experimental evaluations

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a uniform distribution over the edges for node selection, which may not always be the case in practice
* The analysis and experiments focus on specific types of networks (complete graph, two-dimensional grid, and Watts-Strogatz), and it is unclear how the algorithms will perform on other types of networks

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of distributed computing and machine learning
* The proposed algorithms are novel and offer improved convergence rates and lower memory and communication costs
* The paper provides a comprehensive analysis and experimental evaluations to support the results

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper assumes a uniform distribution over the edges for node selection, which may not always be the case in practice
* The analysis and experiments focus on specific types of networks, and it is unclear how the algorithms will perform on other types of networks

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but suggest that the authors provide additional analysis and experiments to address the limitations mentioned above.
