This paper presents new Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. The proposed algorithms achieve faster convergence rates compared to the current state-of-the-art (the U2-Gossip algorithm by Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) and extend to more realistic asynchronous settings. Experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms compared to existing approaches.

The paper is well-written, and the problem is clearly motivated with strong connections to relevant applications. The authors effectively position their work within the existing literature, providing appropriate citations and context. As far as I can ascertain, the proposed algorithms are novel and demonstrate improvements over existing methods in terms of convergence and computational cost (memory and communication, specifically in comparison to U2-gossip). The extension to asynchronous settings is a significant strength.

Beyond the theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors provide comprehensive empirical evaluations on two datasets, comparing their performance against established baselines. This robust experimental validation further strengthens the contributions of the paper. The paper introduces novel Gossip Algorithms for computing U-statistics, exhibiting faster convergence rates and applicability to realistic asynchronous scenarios. The algorithms are rigorously evaluated and compared to state-of-the-art methods through extensive experiments.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The algorithms presented are a novel approach to computing U-statistics in a decentralized manner.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides convergence rate bounds for both synchronous and asynchronous settings, supported by a detailed analysis.
*   **Practical Performance:** Experimental results demonstrate superior performance compared to existing algorithms (U2-gossip) on real-world datasets.
*   **Asynchronous Setting:** Addressing the asynchronous case is a valuable contribution, as it reflects more realistic deployment scenarios.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Complexity of Analysis:** The “phantom node” reformulation, while enabling the analysis, adds to the complexity of understanding the theoretical underpinnings. (Although well explained, it may pose a barrier for some readers.)
*   **Limited Baseline Comparison:** While U2-gossip is a key comparison point, comparing against other relevant distributed estimation algorithms (beyond those mentioned briefly) might further strengthen the results.
