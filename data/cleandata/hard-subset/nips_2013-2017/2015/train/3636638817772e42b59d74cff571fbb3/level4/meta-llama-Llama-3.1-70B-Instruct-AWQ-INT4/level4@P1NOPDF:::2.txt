This paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized aggregation/averaging algorithm to compute higher-order statistics measured over pairs of observations distributed across a network of nodes. These U-statistics encompass various quantities of interest, including sample variance, correlation, and scatter coefficients, as well as other aggregate measures. The main contribution of this work lies in its extension of gossip algorithms to compute these statistics.

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the literature on gossip algorithms, but it would benefit from a clearer connection to existing approaches and ideas.

Comments:

1. The algorithm's key insight is that when two nodes exchange their estimate values and average them, they also swap a secondary state variable representing their initial observation. This results in the initial observations performing a random walk, albeit coupled. Highlighting this aspect more prominently in the main manuscript would enhance clarity and motivate the analysis method via multiple copies of the graph.

2. The authors should situate their work within the broader context of gossip approaches for computing statistics. A comprehensive review of the state of the art, such as Dimakis et al.'s Proc. IEEE paper, would provide a more nuanced understanding of the current contribution. The existing references and explanations in the manuscript are somewhat cursory.

3. On page 4, it would be helpful to clarify the induced distribution on edges resulting from the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process. The phrase "drawing a random edge of the network" lacks specificity regarding the distribution.

4. An alternative approach to this problem would be to allocate more memory at each node and utilize the same contact process to flood each node's value across the network. Nodes would store received observations, tagged with the node that initially possessed them. At each time step, nodes could calculate the U-statistic on the subset of data they possess. Comparing this method to the current approach would provide insight into the benefits of averaging and the performance loss due to limited memory at each node. Without this comparison, it is unclear whether the rate 1/t is satisfactory. For expanders, the flooding method may be quite effective.

5. The authors should examine the work on lifting constructions for accelerating gossip algorithms, as it appears closely related to their construction. Notable examples include K. Jung, D. Shah, and J. Shin's "Fast gossip through lifted Markov chains," W. Li and H. Dai's "Location-aided fast distributed consensus," and F. Chen, L. Lovasz, and I. Pak's "Lifting markov chains to speed up mixing."

6. Typically, gossip algorithm analyses assume the worst-case scenario for the initial data distribution, providing guarantees over all initial data vectors $X$. However, the problem statement and theorem do not explicitly state the assumption on the data distribution and the guarantees on the error as a function of the initial data.

Minor corrections:

- p1: The bipartite definition using (S \times T) \cup (T \times S) implies directed graphs.
- p4: "let us stress out that" should be "we stress that"
- p4: "awakes and contact" should be "awakes and contacts"
- p6: "requires to initiate" should be "requires initiating"
- p7: "allows to create" should be "allows us to create"

AFTER AUTHOR FEEDBACK:

I appreciate the authors' clarifications and modifications, which have prompted me to revise my initial score from 7 to 8. However, I still recommend that the authors thoroughly examine lifting constructions, particularly LADA, and update the related work section accordingly. Furthermore, the experiments should include baseline comparisons to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.

This paper presents a well-structured approach to extending gossip methods for computing complex statistics in distributed settings, such as sensor networks. While the mathematical formulation and connection to prior work are somewhat unclear, assuming the authors address the comments raised in this review, I recommend accepting this paper.
