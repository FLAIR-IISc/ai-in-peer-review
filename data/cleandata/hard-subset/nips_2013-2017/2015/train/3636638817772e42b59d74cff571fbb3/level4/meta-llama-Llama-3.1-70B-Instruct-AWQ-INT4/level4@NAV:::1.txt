This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for U-statistics computation, achieving a faster convergence rate than the current state-of-the-art, specifically the U2-Gossip algorithm proposed by Pelckmans and Suykens in 2009. Notably, these algorithms also perform effectively in more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results provided further substantiate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing methods.

The manuscript is well-crafted, with a clear motivation for the problem and a thorough positioning of the authors' work within the existing literature. Upon review, the proposed algorithms appear to be innovative, offering superior convergence rates and lower costs in terms of memory and communication compared to the U2-Gossip algorithm. Moreover, their applicability in asynchronous settings is a significant advantage.

The authors provide both theoretical guarantees and analyses, complemented by extensive empirical evaluations across two datasets, where they are compared against baseline methods. This multifaceted approach reinforces the contributions of the paper, demonstrating the effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed Gossip algorithms for U-statistics computation in both synchronous and asynchronous environments.
