- Proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics.  
- Clear problem statement motivated by sensor and peer-to-peer network needs.  
- Well-organized paper with good explanations of algorithms and analysis.  
- Introduces "phantom nodes" to model data propagation and estimate updates—clever modeling device.  
- Convergence analysis is thorough and convincing.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate effectiveness and include comparisons to existing methods.  
- Needs a more comprehensive literature review situating work among existing gossip and decentralized computation methods.  
- Should clarify the induced edge distribution from the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process.  
- Could consider alternative approaches, e.g., allowing more memory per node and flooding values with the same contact process.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid analysis, and strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient literature positioning, unclear edge-distribution modeling, and overlooked alternative designs.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address cited weaknesses.
