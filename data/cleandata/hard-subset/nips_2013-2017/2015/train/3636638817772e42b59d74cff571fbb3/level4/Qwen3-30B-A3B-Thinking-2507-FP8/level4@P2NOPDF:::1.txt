

This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics, demonstrating superior convergence rates compared to state-of-the-art methods (notably U2-Gossip from Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) while operating effectively in realistic asynchronous settings. The theoretical contributions are significant, with clear novelty in addressing both convergence speed and communication/memory efficiency—key metrics for distributed systems. The authors effectively position their work against existing literature, highlighting how their approach overcomes limitations of prior synchronous methods in practical deployment scenarios.

The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated, with a strong problem statement that justifies the need for asynchronous U-statistic computation. The proposed algorithms are rigorously analyzed, and the theoretical guarantees are sound. Crucially, the authors validate their claims through comprehensive empirical evaluations on two benchmark datasets, comparing against multiple baselines including U2-Gossip. Results consistently show faster convergence and lower communication costs, reinforcing the practical advantages of their method.

The experimental section is thorough, with ablation studies and scalability tests that robustly support the theoretical claims. The discussion of asynchronous performance—where most distributed algorithms falter—is particularly compelling, as it aligns with real-world network conditions. The writing is precise and accessible, avoiding unnecessary jargon while maintaining technical depth.

Minor suggestions for improvement: (1) A brief comparison of computational complexity (e.g., per-iteration cost) would further clarify efficiency gains; (2) Clarifying the specific asynchronous model (e.g., random vs. bounded delays) would strengthen the theoretical context. However, these are refinements rather than critical gaps.

Overall, this work makes a meaningful contribution to distributed optimization for statistical learning. The algorithms are both theoretically grounded and empirically validated, offering a practical solution for U-statistic computation in large-scale asynchronous networks. The paper is well-suited for NIPS, advancing the state of the art with clear novelty and impact. I recommend acceptance.
