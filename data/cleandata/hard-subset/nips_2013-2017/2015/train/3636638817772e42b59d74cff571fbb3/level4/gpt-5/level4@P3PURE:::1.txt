This paper proposes new gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The methods achieve faster convergence than the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) and are designed to operate in more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results further demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing approaches.

The paper is well written, the problem is clearly motivated, and the authors position their work appropriately within the existing literature. To the best of my knowledge, the algorithms are novel and superior in terms of both convergence and cost (memory and communication) compared to U2-Gossip. Importantly, the proposed approach also works in asynchronous environments.

Beyond theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors provide extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets and compare against relevant baselines. These results convincingly support the paper’s contributions. Overall, the work presents new gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics with faster convergence and applicability to realistic asynchronous settings, validated through comprehensive experiments against the state of the art.
