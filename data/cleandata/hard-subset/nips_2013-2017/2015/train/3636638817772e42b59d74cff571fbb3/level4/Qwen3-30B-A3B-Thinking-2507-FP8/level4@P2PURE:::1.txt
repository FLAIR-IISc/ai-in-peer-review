

This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, demonstrating faster convergence rates compared to the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009), as well as superior performance in realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results further validate the proposed algorithms' superiority over existing approaches through extensive evaluations on two datasets. The paper is well-written, with a clearly motivated problem and strong positioning relative to the existing literature. The proposed algorithms are novel, offer improved convergence and reduced computational cost (in terms of memory and communication) relative to U2-Gossip, and effectively operate in asynchronous settings. Beyond theoretical guarantees and analysis, the thorough empirical evaluation strengthens the paper's contributions significantly.
