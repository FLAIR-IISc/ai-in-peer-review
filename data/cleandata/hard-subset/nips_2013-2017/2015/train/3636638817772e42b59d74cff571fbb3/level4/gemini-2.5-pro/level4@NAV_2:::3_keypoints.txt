- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-two U-statistics.  
- Identifies the gap that U-statistics (pairwise-dependent) are less-explored than separable-function distributed averaging.  
- Introduces GoSta, which combines data propagation (nodes swap auxiliary data points) with local averaging to diffuse estimates.  
- Directly improves on prior U2-gossip by offering better theoretical guarantees, an asynchronous variant, and lower resource requirements.  
- Significance/originality: addresses an important, practical problem in distributed ML and signal processing with a novel approach.  
- Technical quality: provides explicit convergence rates (O(1/t) synchronous, O(log t/t) asynchronous) and solid proofs using a “phantom nodes” reformulation.  
- Analysis shows convergence depends on both network spectral properties and data statistical properties.  
- Clarity: paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and well-organized.  
- Empirical validation: experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter show GoSta outperforms U2-gossip across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz topologies in speed and variance.  
- Weakness: per-iteration cost and resource-advantage claims are not quantified in experiments; lacks wall-clock time or a table detailing per-iteration computational complexity, memory, and communication.
