This paper introduces synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The motivation is strong and the manuscript is well-written. The proposed method is evaluated on two datasets, demonstrating superior performance over the U2-gossip baseline across varied graph topologies based on the average relative AUC error. A theoretical contribution of the paper is the provision of convergence bounds for the proposed algorithms.

However, the experimental evaluation would be significantly strengthened by including a comparison of the per-iteration run-times for the proposed method against the U2-gossip baseline. This comparison is critical, as a substantially higher computational cost per iteration for the proposed method would limit its practical utility, regardless of its convergence properties.
