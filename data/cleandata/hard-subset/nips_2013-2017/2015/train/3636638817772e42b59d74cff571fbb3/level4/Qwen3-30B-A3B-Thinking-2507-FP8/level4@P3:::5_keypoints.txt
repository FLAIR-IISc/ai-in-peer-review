- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics, filling a gap in decentralized computation literature.
- Main contribution: a method that simultaneously propagates data and maintains local U-statistic estimates, with convergence O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous).
- Builds on existing gossip averaging work but addresses limitations of U2-gossip (high memory/communication and no asynchronous operation).
- Introduces "phantom nodes" to separate data propagation and averaging, reducing memory to one auxiliary observation per node and enabling asynchrony.
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis for both settings with explicit data- and network-dependent terms, highlighting the role of the spectral gap.
- Experiments on real datasets (SMVguide3 for AUC, Wine Quality for scatter) and multiple topologies validate theory and show consistent gains over U2-gossip.
- Presents a refined rate analysis for U2-gossip explaining its poorer performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with consistent notation and a helpful figure illustrating the phantom network.
- Supplementary material appears sufficient for reproducibility; main text could add a brief discussion of the phantom network’s role.
- The phantom node technique is an original, meaningful advance for decentralized U-statistics.
- Related work is appropriately cited, though inclusion of more recent decentralized U-statistics work could strengthen context.
- Significant practical impact: lower communication/memory and faster convergence make the algorithms suitable for resource-constrained networks.
- Strengths: novelty, rigorous theoretical analysis, practical efficiency, comprehensive experiments, and clear presentation.
- Weakness: phantom node construction is somewhat abstract and needs more intuitive explanation.
- Weakness: comparison to U2-gossip could be strengthened by more detailed communication complexity analysis.
- Weakness: limited exploration of applications beyond AUC and within-cluster scatter.
- Weakness: asynchronous theoretical rate is slower than synchronous, though empirical performance is comparable.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, substantive contribution with strong theoretical and empirical support; reviewer recommends acceptance.
