- Problem & contribution: Decentralized estimation of degree-2 U‑statistics via pairwise gossip with two algorithms (GoSta‑sync and GoSta‑async) using estimate averaging and swapping a single auxiliary observation per node.  
- Key mechanism: Swapping auxiliary observations causes raw data to perform coupled random walks while node estimates are averaged.  
- Analysis technique: Introduces a “phantom nodes”/graph‑lifting construction to decouple averaging from data propagation in the convergence analysis.  
- Convergence results: Proves expectation convergence rates O(1/t) for synchronous and O((log t)/t) for asynchronous algorithms, with explicit dependence on network spectral gap and data dispersion.  
- Empirical claims: Experiments on AUC and within‑cluster scatter across several graph topologies show consistent gains over U2‑gossip and reduced memory/communication per iteration.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on randomized gossip and non‑separable statistic gossip literature; the phantom construction connects to lifted Markov chains and consensus acceleration (LADA), needing more explicit discussion.  
- Theoretical quality: Elegant decoupling and meaningful rates/ constants, but results are only in expectation — high‑probability/variance bounds and stronger async analysis are missing.  
- Correctness: Results appear plausible and standard tools are used, though supplementary proofs were not fully checked by the reviewer.  
- Empirical quality: Good range of graphs and real data, but stronger baselines (e.g., flooding) and detailed communication/memory cost comparisons are lacking.  
- Clarity issues: Must explicitly state that auxiliary observations execute random walks, precisely define the edge selection distribution, and clarify data assumptions (adversarial vs. probabilistic).  
- Originality: Novel and simple idea to use one auxiliary sample per node plus phantom‑node analysis for U‑statistics; asynchronous local‑clock variant is also a useful contribution.  
- Significance: Practical relevance for sensors/peer‑to‑peer systems with explicit network‑dependent rates; potential for broader impact if linked to acceleration methods and concentration guarantees.  
- Suggested additions: Emphasize random‑walk intuition earlier, broaden related work (e.g., Dimakis survey), clarify pk/edge model, evaluate a flooding baseline, and discuss possible lifting/acceleration extensions.  
- Guarantees request: Specify whether results hold for all fixed datasets or under a data model and add concentration/high‑probability bounds if possible.  
- Minor typos/wording: Fix bipartite wording for undirected edges and several phrasing issues (examples given for p.4, p.6, p.7).  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, implementable algorithms; explicit network‑ and data‑dependent theoretical rates; handles sync and async; lower memory/communication and empirical improvement over U2‑gossip.  
- Cons for acceptance: Only in‑expectation guarantees, weaker async rate, incomplete coverage of lifting/acceleration literature and broader baselines, and some clarity issues on edge selection and data assumptions.  
- Final recommendation after rebuttal: Authors’ revisions address many concerns; encourage integrating lifting/acceleration literature and baseline comparisons — recommend acceptance pending those refinements.
