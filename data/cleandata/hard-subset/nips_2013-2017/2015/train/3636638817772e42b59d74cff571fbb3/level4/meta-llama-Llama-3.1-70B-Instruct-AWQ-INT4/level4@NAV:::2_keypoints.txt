- Paper proposes a decentralized gossip-style algorithm to compute U-statistics (e.g., variance, correlation) over pairs of observations in a network.  
- Algorithm novelty: exchanges estimates and swaps a secondary state so initial observations perform coupled random walks; this should be highlighted in the main text.  
- Reviewer requests clearer connection to existing gossip methods and literature (e.g., Dimakis et al.).  
- Clarify the edge-selection distribution induced by the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process—"drawing a random edge" needs specification.  
- Suggest comparing to a higher-memory flooding approach where nodes store received observations and compute U-statistics on possessed subsets, especially on expander graphs.  
- Recommend examining and citing lifting constructions for accelerating gossip (e.g., Jung et al., Li & Dai, Chen et al.) as closely related work.  
- Analysis should state assumptions on the initial data distribution and whether guarantees are worst-case over all initial data vectors X.  
- Reviewer could not verify correctness of supplementary material and flags this as a concern.  
- Minor corrections: fix bipartite notation to avoid implying directed graphs and correct several grammatical phrases (pages 1,4,6,7).  
- After author feedback, reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 and found clarifications satisfactory.  
- Further actions requested: expand related work to include LADA/lifting, add baseline comparisons in experiments, and clarify mathematical connections to prior work.  
- Overall: well-structured contribution with potential for acceptance if the above clarifications and additional experiments/references are addressed.
