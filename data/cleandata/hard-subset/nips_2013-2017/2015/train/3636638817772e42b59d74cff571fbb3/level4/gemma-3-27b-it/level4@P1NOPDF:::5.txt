The paper introduces both synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. It rigorously derives these techniques and demonstrates convergence rates of 1/t and log t / t, respectively.

The paper is well-organized and presents results that effectively demonstrate the algorithms’ performance. The problem area is of considerable interest, and the proposed techniques are therefore likely to be valuable to a range of researchers. This work introduces novel gossip algorithms for U-statistic estimation, achieving favorable convergence rates. Experiments showcase performance advantages over existing approaches and illustrate the accuracy of the proposed methods.

The main strength of this paper lies in its theoretical analysis. The convergence rate analysis is particularly strong and provides a solid foundation for understanding the behavior of the algorithms. However, the experimental section, while demonstrating improvement, could be strengthened. Specifically, the comparison to baseline methods feels somewhat limited. It would be beneficial to see comparisons to a wider range of relevant algorithms, particularly those commonly used in distributed U-statistic estimation.  A discussion of the computational cost (e.g., communication rounds) associated with achieving a given level of accuracy would also be valuable.

I also found myself wondering about the sensitivity of the algorithms to network topology. While gossip algorithms are generally robust, a brief investigation into how performance varies with different network structures (e.g., varying degree distributions, presence of bottlenecks) could add further insight.  

Furthermore, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the assumptions underlying the theoretical results. For example, what assumptions are made about the data distribution? Are there limitations related to the size or dimensionality of the data? Clarifying these aspects would enhance the practical applicability of the work.

Despite these minor points, the paper makes a clear contribution to the field. The proposed algorithms offer a promising approach to distributed U-statistic estimation, and the theoretical analysis is convincing. The results presented are encouraging, and with some additional experimentation and discussion, this work could become a significant resource for researchers working on large-scale data analysis. 

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the points raised regarding baseline comparisons, computational cost, network topology sensitivity, and a more detailed discussion of the underlying assumptions.
