- Problem & contribution: Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics using simultaneous data propagation and local averaging.  
- Convergence: Proves O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) convergence in expectation with constants depending on data dispersion and network spectral gap.  
- Analysis technique: Introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple data propagation from averaging and obtain explicit bounds.  
- Empirical results: Shows faster, more stable convergence than U2-gossip on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs.  
- Resource advantages: Requires less memory and communication per iteration than U2-gossip (one pairwise swap vs. two) and supports asynchronous operation.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends gossip methods beyond separable sums; directly improves on U2-gossip by reducing auxiliary state, adding averaging, enabling asynchrony, and tightening rates.  
- Strength — motivation: Clear problem formulation linking U-statistics to practical metrics (AUC, variance, Gini, scatter).  
- Strength — technical: Novel synchronous/asynchronous algorithms with network- and data-dependent convergence guarantees.  
- Strength — analysis: Phantom-node reformulation yields clean separation and explicit bounds.  
- Strength — practicality: Lower memory/communication and asynchronous implementation increase deployability.  
- Strength — experiments: Consistent empirical improvements and reduced node variance support claims.  
- Weakness — runtime reporting: Lacks wall-clock and per-iteration runtime comparisons; needs time-to-accuracy, messages/bytes per iteration, and memory per node.  
- Weakness — fair comparison: Should compare under matched communication/computation budgets (e.g., equal number of pair communications or bytes).  
- Weakness — scalability: Needs theoretical and empirical discussion of scaling with data dimension d and large n, especially when H is expensive.  
- Weakness — decentralization assumption: GoSta-async requires nodes to know degrees and total edges to form pk; clarify how this is obtained and robustness to estimation errors.  
- Weakness — probabilistic guarantees: Could be strengthened by high-probability or finite-time variance bounds, especially for async execution.  
- Presentation suggestion: Add a succinct per-iteration complexity table (local compute, messages, memory) to aid readability.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, original, and practically relevant; acceptance favored if authors add detailed runtime/communication/memory comparisons and clarify decentralization assumptions.
