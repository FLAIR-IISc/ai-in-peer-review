- Valuable contribution: proposes gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute U-statistics but needs stronger connections to existing approaches and clearer exposition; supplement not verified.  
- Point 1: Emphasize that swapping a secondary state makes initial observations perform a coupled random walk and mention this explicitly in the main text to motivate the analysis.  
- Point 2: Broaden related work: situate the paper within wider gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al.) rather than current cursory citations.  
- Point 3: Clarify the edge-selection distribution induced by the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process—"drawing a random edge" is underspecified.  
- Point 4: Compare against a memory-intensive flooding alternative (nodes store/tag received observations and compute local U-statistics) to show benefits of averaging and evaluate whether 1/t is good or bad.  
- Point 5: Investigate lifting constructions and related accelerated-gossip work (e.g., Jung/Shah/Shin; Li/Dai LADA; Chen/Lovasz/Pak) as these appear closely related.  
- Point 6: State assumptions about initial data explicitly—clarify whether guarantees are worst-case over all initial vectors X and express error bounds as functions of initial data.  
- Minor corrections: several wording/notation fixes (bipartite definition implies directedness; wording changes on pp.1,4,6,7).
