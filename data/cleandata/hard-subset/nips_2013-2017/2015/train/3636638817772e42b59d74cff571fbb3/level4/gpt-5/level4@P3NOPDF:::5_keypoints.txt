- Paper introduces two gossip-based decentralized algorithms (synchronous and asynchronous) for U-statistic estimation with convergence rates 1/t and (log t)/t, supported by experiments.
- Clear motivation: decentralized U-statistics (e.g., pairwise similarity, kernels) is practically relevant and understudied compared to mean estimation.
- Solid theoretical guarantees: stated rates are plausible for synchronous vs asynchronous regimes.
- Practical algorithms: simple, implementable gossip formulations that avoid centralized coordination.
- Empirical support: experiments show favorable accuracy–efficiency trade-offs against baselines.
- Good presentation and logical organization from algorithms to analysis and experiments.
- Unclear assumptions: paper should state precise conditions on the kernel, data distribution, and network model and their effect on constants and finite-sample behavior.
- Degenerate U-statistics not addressed: need clarification whether rates hold or change under Hoeffding degeneracy.
- Graph-topology dependence is missing: convergence constants should be explicit in terms of spectral gap or mixing time.
- Communication and memory costs are not quantified: per-iteration communication, total message complexity, and storage requirements (especially for higher-order U-statistics) are needed.
- Asynchronous model details are vague: specify activation/delay/loss models and robustness to message loss or heterogenous clocks.
- Experimental depth is limited: recommend more topologies, larger network sizes, robustness tests (delays/packet drops), wall-clock time, and message complexity reporting.
- Baseline fairness unclear: need details on baseline implementations and comparable communication budgets, plus a centralized oracle for context.
- Improve readability with concise pseudocode and an “assumptions at a glance” summary.
- Highlight key lemmas and sketch proof ideas, and point readers to where constants are derived.
- Contribution is novel and significant: applying gossip to U-statistics fills a useful gap and aligns with distributed learning needs.
- Suggest explicitly stating/relaxing assumptions (e.g., to sub-Gaussian or finite moments) where possible.
- Make convergence constants explicit, showing dependence on kernel properties, data variance, and graph spectral parameters.
- Address how the analysis changes for degenerate U-statistics via Hoeffding decomposition.
- Provide detailed communication and memory complexity bounds as a function of U-statistic order and network size.
- Expand experiments with scalability plots, robustness scenarios, and accuracy vs communication breakdowns.
- Add positioning relative to decentralized optimization methods (dual averaging, ADMM) and randomized gossip for non-linear aggregates.
- Discuss potential variance reduction or mini-batching strategies to improve constants, especially for the asynchronous method.
- Ask authors to clarify: exact network assumptions for asynchronous guarantees, dependence of bounds on spectral gap and network size, coverage of degenerate U-statistics, per-node memory footprint and possibility of unbiased sketches, and extensions to time-varying graphs or adversarial delays.
- Overall: well-written, timely, and promising; with clearer assumptions, explicit constants, deeper experiments, and topology/degeneracy discussion the paper would be a strong contribution—reviewer leans positive.
