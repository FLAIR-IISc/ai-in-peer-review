This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in a distributed environment. The proposed algorithm is intuitively appealing, though its novelty appears limited – however, the accompanying proofs are comprehensive and rigorous.

A key weakness of this work is the lack of comparison to baseline methods. Several intuitive baselines could be readily constructed using randomized algorithms, and their inclusion would significantly strengthen the empirical evaluation. While the paper tackles an interesting problem, the evaluation is weak due to the absence of any baseline comparisons.
