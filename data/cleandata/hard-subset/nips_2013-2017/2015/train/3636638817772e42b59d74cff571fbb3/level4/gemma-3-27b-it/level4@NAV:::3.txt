This paper presents a well-motivated and clearly written contribution to the area of synchronized and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on two datasets, reporting average relative AUC error as a key metric. Results indicate a significant performance improvement over the U2-gossip baseline across a diverse set of graph topologies. Notably, the paper also includes theoretical convergence bounds for the proposed method. 

To further strengthen the evaluation, I recommend the inclusion of per-iteration run-times for both the proposed method and U2-gossip. Understanding the computational cost per iteration is crucial; if the proposed method exhibits substantially slower iteration times compared to U2-gossip, it could limit the practical applicability of this work.
