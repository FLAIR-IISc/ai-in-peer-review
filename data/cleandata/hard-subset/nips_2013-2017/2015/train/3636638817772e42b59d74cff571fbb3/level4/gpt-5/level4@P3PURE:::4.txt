This paper addresses the computation of average statistics in a distributed environment. The proposed algorithm is intuitive and may lack clear novelty; however, the theoretical analysis is thorough and the proofs are comprehensive. A major weakness is the absence of comparisons with any baseline measures. It should be straightforward to construct several intuitive baselines—such as those based on randomized algorithms—and include them in the experiments. Overall, the paper tackles an interesting problem, but the evaluation is weak due to the lack of baseline measures.
