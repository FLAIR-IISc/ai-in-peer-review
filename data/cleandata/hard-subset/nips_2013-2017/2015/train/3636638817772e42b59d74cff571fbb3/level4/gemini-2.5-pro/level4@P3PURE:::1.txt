This paper presents novel gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The proposed algorithms offer a faster convergence rate than the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm and, notably, are designed to operate in more realistic asynchronous settings.

The paper is well-written, the problem is clearly motivated, and the authors have effectively positioned their work with respect to the existing literature. To my knowledge, the proposed algorithms are novel and superior to prior methods not only in convergence speed but also in terms of computational cost (i.e., memory and communication overhead) when compared to U2-Gossip.

The theoretical guarantees and analysis are substantiated by extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets. These experiments, which include comparisons to relevant baselines, further strengthen the paper's contributions by demonstrating the practical superiority of the proposed algorithms.
