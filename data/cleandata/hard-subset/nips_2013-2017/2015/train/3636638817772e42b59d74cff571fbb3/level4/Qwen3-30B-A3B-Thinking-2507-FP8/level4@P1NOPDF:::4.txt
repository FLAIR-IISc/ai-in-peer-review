

This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in distributed systems. While the proposed algorithm is intuitive and the accompanying proofs are comprehensive, its novelty is not clearly established. The core contribution appears to build upon existing approaches without sufficient differentiation from prior work.

A significant methodological weakness is the absence of baseline comparisons. The authors fail to evaluate their method against plausible alternatives, particularly simple randomized baselines that could be easily constructed for this problem. For instance, strategies such as random sampling or uniform aggregation across nodes would provide meaningful reference points. Including such baselines is essential for establishing the algorithm's practical value and demonstrating its advantages over straightforward alternatives. Without these comparisons, the claimed effectiveness remains unsubstantiated, and the evaluation lacks rigor. The paper presents an interesting problem, but the experimental validation is fundamentally incomplete due to this omission.
