- Proposes synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics.  
- Provides comprehensive analysis including convergence rates and use of "phantom nodes" for tractable analysis.  
- Problem is well-motivated with a clear overview of challenges and prior work.  
- Algorithms are intuitive and well-explained.  
- Numerical experiments show effectiveness, particularly versus U2-gossip.  
- Evaluation is limited to two datasets and three network types; needs more extensive experiments and additional baselines.  
- Phantom-node modeling is a clever technique aiding analysis.  
- Algorithmic novelty is somewhat limited; paper needs clearer differentiation from existing gossip methods.  
- Paper lacks sufficient context on practical applications and potential impact.  
- Scores well on quality and clarity per NIPS criteria; originality is limited; significance is clear but evaluation is weak.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, thorough analysis, empirical evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, narrow evaluation, and insufficient discussion of impact; recommendation: accept with revisions to expand discussion and experiments.
