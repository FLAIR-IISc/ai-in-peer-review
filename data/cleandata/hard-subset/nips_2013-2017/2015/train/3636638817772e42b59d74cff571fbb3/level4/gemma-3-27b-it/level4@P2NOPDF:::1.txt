This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. The proposed algorithms achieve a faster convergence rate than existing state-of-the-art methods, specifically the U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009), and importantly, operate effectively in asynchronous network environments – a more realistic setting for many distributed applications. Experimental results convincingly demonstrate the superior performance of the new algorithms compared to established baselines.

The paper is clearly written and well-motivated. The authors effectively contextualize their work within the existing literature, highlighting the gap their contribution addresses. To the best of my understanding, the proposed algorithms represent a genuine advancement, offering improvements in convergence speed and computational cost (in terms of both memory and communication) when compared to U2-Gossip. The ability to handle asynchronous updates is a significant practical advantage.

Beyond strong theoretical foundations and analysis, the authors bolster their claims with comprehensive empirical evaluations. These experiments, conducted on two datasets, provide compelling evidence supporting the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms. This thorough experimental validation significantly strengthens the paper’s contributions. 

In summary, this work presents a valuable contribution to the field of distributed computation. The development of faster, more practical Gossip algorithms for U-statistics addresses a relevant and challenging problem, and the authors demonstrate their effectiveness through both theoretical analysis and extensive experimentation.
