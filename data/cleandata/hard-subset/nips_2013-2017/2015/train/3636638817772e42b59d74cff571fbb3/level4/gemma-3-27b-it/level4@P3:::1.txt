This paper presents new Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, addressing a gap in the literature where distributed estimation has largely focused on simpler statistics like the sample mean. The proposed algorithms offer faster convergence rates compared to the current state-of-the-art (the U2-Gossip algorithm by Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) and extend to the more practical asynchronous setting. Empirical results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms compared to existing approaches.

The paper is well-written and clearly motivated, effectively positioning the work within the broader context of gossip algorithms and distributed estimation. The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of related work, including references to foundational papers in gossip algorithms (Boyd et al., 2005; Dimakis et al., 2007) and U-statistics (Lee, 1990). The proposed algorithms appear novel, offering improvements in convergence and resource usage (memory and communication) over U2-Gossip. The extension to asynchronous settings is a significant contribution. 

The technical contributions are solid. The authors introduce a clever reformulation of the problem using “phantom nodes” to facilitate the convergence analysis. The convergence rate bounds of O(1/t) for the synchronous case and O(log t/t) for the asynchronous case are clearly stated and appear well-justified, with explicit dependencies on data and network properties. The inclusion of data-dependent terms reflecting the problem’s hardness and network-dependent terms related to spectral gap is a strength.

Beyond the theoretical guarantees, the extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets (AUC and within-cluster point scatter) adds significant weight to the claims. Comparing against U2-Gossip provides a direct and compelling demonstration of the algorithm’s effectiveness. The experiments on different network topologies (complete graph, 2D grid, Watts-Strogatz) further illustrate the impact of network connectivity, as predicted by the theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Addresses an under-explored problem – distributed computation of U-statistics.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** Provides clear convergence rate bounds with informative dependencies.
*   **Practical Relevance:**  Extends to asynchronous settings and demonstrates superior performance in experiments.
*   **Clarity:** Well-written and organized presentation, making the paper easy to follow.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** Thorough experimental validation on real datasets and varied network topologies.

**Weaknesses:**

*   While the "phantom node" technique is clever, it might benefit from further intuitive explanation. The connection between the phantom nodes and the actual data propagation could be more clearly articulated in the main text.
*   The comparison to other gossip algorithms beyond U2-Gossip is limited. Including a baseline comparison to simpler averaging-based gossip algorithms, even if expected to perform poorly, would provide more context.
*   The discussion of the limitations of the algorithms could be expanded. For example, the impact of highly skewed data distributions on performance isn’t addressed.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant gap in the literature, extending the applicability of gossip algorithms to a broader class of statistical problems.
*   The technical contributions are substantial, providing both theoretical guarantees and practical improvements over existing methods.
*   The empirical results are compelling and support the claims made by the authors.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The “phantom node” technique, while effective, requires careful reading to fully grasp its purpose.
*   The limited comparison to other baseline algorithms.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of distributed estimation. The authors have developed novel and effective algorithms for computing U-statistics using gossip protocols, supported by strong theoretical and empirical evidence. The paper is of high quality and clearly meets the standards for acceptance at NIPS.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
