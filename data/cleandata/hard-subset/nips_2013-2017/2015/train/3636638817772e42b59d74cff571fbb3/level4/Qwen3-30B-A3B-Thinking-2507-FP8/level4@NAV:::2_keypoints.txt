- Paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute higher-order U-statistics (e.g., variance, correlation, scatter) across networks.  
- Main algorithmic innovation: nodes exchange estimates while swapping a secondary state tracking initial observations, enabling coupled random walks of initial data — this mechanism should be explicitly highlighted.  
- Reviewer views the contribution as valuable but urges stronger connections to prior gossip literature.  
- Manuscript should better situate itself with respect to Dimakis et al. (state-of-the-art overview) and other core gossip works.  
- Clarify the edge-selection distribution in the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" model on page 4; “drawing a random edge” is underspecified.  
- Compare with an alternative flooding-with-tags approach (nodes flood initial values and compute U-statistics locally) to assess if the O(1/t) averaging rate is advantageous, especially on expanders.  
- Incorporate related work on lifting constructions for accelerated gossip (e.g., Jung et al. LADA, Li & Dai, Chen et al.), which directly relate to the proposed method.  
- Explicitly state the data-distribution assumptions in theorems (current analysis assumes worst-case initial data); clarify error guarantees as a function of initial data.  
- Minor corrections: bipartite definition implies directed graphs; several wording fixes (e.g., “stress out”→“stress”, “awakes and contact(s)”, “requires initiating”, “allows us to create”).  
- After author feedback, reviewer raised score from 7 to 8; suggested further examination of lifting constructions (LADA) and addition of baseline experiments.
