This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. These algorithms offer a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009), demonstrating faster convergence rates. A key advantage is their ability to operate in more realistic asynchronous settings.

The paper is well-written, the problem is clearly motivated, and the authors effectively position their work within the existing literature. To the best of my knowledge, the algorithms presented are novel. They appear superior to the U2-Gossip baseline not only in convergence speed but also in terms of memory and communication costs. The ability to function in asynchronous environments is another substantial contribution.

The theoretical claims are well-supported by extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets. By comparing against established baselines, the experimental results convincingly demonstrate the practical superiority of the proposed approach and significantly strengthen the paper's contributions.

In summary, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution. The authors present novel, theoretically-sound Gossip algorithms for U-statistics that outperform the state of the art in several key areas: convergence speed, resource efficiency, and applicability to asynchronous settings. The work is further strengthened by thorough empirical validation.
