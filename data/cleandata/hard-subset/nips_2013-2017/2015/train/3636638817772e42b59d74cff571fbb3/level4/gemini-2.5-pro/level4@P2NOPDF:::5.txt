This paper proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for the distributed estimation of U-statistics. The authors provide a rigorous theoretical analysis, establishing convergence rates of O(1/t) for the synchronous algorithm and O(log t / t) for the asynchronous version. These contributions address the important problem of decentralized computation for a broad and fundamental class of statistical estimators.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The problem is well-motivated, and the proposed algorithms are described with sufficient detail. A key strength of this work is its theoretical contribution; the derivation of the convergence rates is technically sound and provides strong guarantees on the performance of the proposed methods.

The topic of distributed statistical estimation is of significant interest to the machine learning community, and the proposed gossip-based approach offers an effective and practical solution. The methods presented are likely to be valuable to researchers and practitioners working on decentralized learning problems.

Furthermore, the empirical evaluation is thorough and persuasive. The experiments effectively demonstrate the practical advantages of the proposed algorithms over existing approaches, corroborating the theoretical findings regarding convergence speed and accuracy.

In summary, this paper makes a solid contribution by introducing two novel and efficient gossip algorithms for estimating U-statistics. The work is supported by both strong theoretical analysis and compelling experimental results. The proposed methods exhibit favorable convergence properties and demonstrate superior performance in practice, making this a valuable addition to the literature on distributed and decentralized machine learning.
