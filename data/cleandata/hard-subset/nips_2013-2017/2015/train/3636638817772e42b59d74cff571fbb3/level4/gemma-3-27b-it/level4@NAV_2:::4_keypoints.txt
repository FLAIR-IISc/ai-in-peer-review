- Addresses the important, underexplored problem of decentralized estimation of U-statistics vs. sample mean estimation.  
- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for this problem.  
- Establishes convergence rate bounds with explicit data and network dependency; proofs appear comprehensive and rigorous.  
- Reformulation using “phantom nodes” is a clever analytical technique enabling the theoretical results.  
- Shows favorable comparisons to prior work [17], achieving faster convergence with lower memory and communication costs.  
- Core idea of propagating data and maintaining local estimates is intuitive and well-motivated for U-statistics (AUC, variance, Gini).  
- Providing both synchronous and asynchronous algorithms broadens practical applicability.  
- Experimental evaluation exists but is relatively weak and could be strengthened.  
- Missing comparisons to simpler baseline methods (e.g., naive averaging or adapted gossip mean algorithms) to contextualize gains.  
- Experiments focus on only two applications (AUC and within-cluster scatter); broader U-statistics and datasets would improve generalizability.  
- More detailed analysis of the impact of different network topologies and spectral gap on performance is needed.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution and novelty merit acceptance, but empirical evaluation should be expanded.
