This paper proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for the distributed computation of U-statistics. The work is well-motivated, and the manuscript is clearly written, making the contributions easy to follow. A key strength of the paper is its theoretical contribution, which includes the derivation of convergence bounds for the proposed methods.

The empirical evaluation is conducted on two datasets, with performance measured by the average relative AUC error. The results convincingly demonstrate that the proposed algorithms significantly outperform the U2-gossip baseline across various graph topologies.

My primary concern relates to the practical efficiency of the proposed algorithms. While the paper establishes superior performance in terms of the number of iterations required for convergence, it omits a comparison of the computational cost per iteration. A crucial point for assessing the practical utility of the method is the wall-clock time. If each iteration of the proposed algorithm is substantially more computationally expensive than an iteration of U2-gossip, the observed reduction in the number of required iterations might not translate to an overall speedup.

To strengthen the paper, I would strongly recommend that the authors include a comparison of the run-time per iteration (or total wall-clock time to convergence) for both their proposed methods and the U2-gossip baseline. This information is vital for a complete assessment of the algorithms' efficiency and would make the practical benefits of the work much clearer. Without this analysis, the utility of the proposed method remains in question.
