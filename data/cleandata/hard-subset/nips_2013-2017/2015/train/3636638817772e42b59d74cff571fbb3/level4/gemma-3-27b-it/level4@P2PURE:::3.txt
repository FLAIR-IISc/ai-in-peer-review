The paper is well-motivated and clearly written. The authors present results on two datasets, evaluating performance using the average relative AUC error. Their method significantly outperforms the U2-gossip baseline across a variety of graph structures. Notably, the paper provides convergence bounds for the proposed approach, which computes U-statistics using synchronized and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms. However, I would like to see runtime measurements per iteration for both the proposed method and U2-gossip. If each iteration of the proposed method is substantially slower than U2-gossip, the practical utility of this work would be limited.
