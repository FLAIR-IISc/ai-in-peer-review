- Proposes a gossip-style decentralized averaging algorithm that swaps a secondary state (initial observation) during pairwise averaging to compute higher-order statistics via a random-walk mechanism.
- Nice contribution to gossip literature but needs clearer connection and positioning within broader gossip/statistics computation work.
- Recommend citing broader prior work (e.g., Dimakis et al. Proc. IEEE) to situate the paper in the state of the art.
- Clarify the induced edge selection distribution from the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process and rephrase the ambiguous "drawing a random edge of the network."
- Compare against a natural alternative: allow more memory and flood node values using the same contact process to provide a baseline and assess benefits of averaging and memory-limited performance loss.
- Include baseline experiments to evaluate the claimed 1/t convergence rate and overall performance.
- Examine and cite lifting constructions for accelerating gossip (e.g., Jung et al. "Fast gossip through lifted Markov chains", Li & Dai's LADA) and discuss relevance to the proposed method.
- Clarify assumptions on the initial data distribution and state guarantees on error as a function of initial data (worst-case vs distributional assumptions).
- Minor textual corrections: rephrase bipartite definition to avoid implying directed graphs; p4: "let us stress out that" → "we stress that", "awakes and contact" → "awakes and contacts"; p6: "requires to initiate" → "requires initiating"; p7: "allows to create" → "allows us to create."
- Reviewer increased score from 7 to 8 after authors' feedback but still requests addressing lifting constructions and adding experimental baselines; recommends acceptance if comments are addressed.
