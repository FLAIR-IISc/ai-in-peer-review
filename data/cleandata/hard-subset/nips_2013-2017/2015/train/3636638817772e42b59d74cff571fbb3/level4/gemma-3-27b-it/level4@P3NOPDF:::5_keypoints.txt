- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Synchronous algorithm achieves a convergence rate of 1/t.
- Asynchronous algorithm converges at a rate of log(t)/t.
- Tackles an important distributed ML/statistics problem needing efficient, scalable U-statistic computation.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presents theoretical derivations and connections to existing gossip methods.
- Experimental results show advantages over baselines in convergence speed and accuracy with fair comparisons.
- Core contribution: rigorously analyzed gossip-based alternative to parameter-server or synchronous averaging approaches.
- Theoretical analysis and derivation of convergence rates appear sound and clearly explained.
- Inclusion of both synchronous and asynchronous variants broadens practical applicability across different network settings.
- Practical impact of the logarithmic factor in the asynchronous rate and the hidden constant factors is not fully explored.
- Paper assumes knowledge of the U-statistic’s underlying function; sensitivity to inaccuracies or approximations is not discussed.
- Lacks discussion of limitations and potential classes of U-statistics where the algorithms may perform poorly.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, suitable for acceptance with minor revisions to address practical impact, assumption sensitivity, and limitations.
