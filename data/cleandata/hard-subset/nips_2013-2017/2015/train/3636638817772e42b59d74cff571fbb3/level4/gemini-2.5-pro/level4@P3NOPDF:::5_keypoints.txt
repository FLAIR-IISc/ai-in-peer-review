- Novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for decentralized U-statistic estimation that avoid a central coordinator.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees: convergence rates of O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized with clear motivation, algorithm descriptions, and analysis.  
- Thorough empirical validation showing faster convergence and better final estimation accuracy than baselines, consistent with theory.  
- Needs a clearer, explicit statement and discussion of underlying assumptions (network topology, communication model, kernel properties).  
- Lacks a detailed scalability analysis—impact of node count, network diameter, and sparsity should be addressed via experiments or theory.  
- Should better situate the work within broader distributed estimation paradigms (e.g., ADMM, other consensus methods).  
- Overall a strong contribution with minor clarifications needed to be conference-ready.
