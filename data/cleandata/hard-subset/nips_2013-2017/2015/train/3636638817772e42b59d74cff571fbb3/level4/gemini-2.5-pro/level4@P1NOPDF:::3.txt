This paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. The work is well-motivated and the paper is clearly written. A key strength is the theoretical analysis, which provides convergence bounds for the proposed method.

The empirical evaluation, conducted on two datasets using the average relative AUC error, demonstrates that the proposed method significantly outperforms the U2-gossip baseline across a variety of network topologies.

However, the experimental analysis could be considerably strengthened by including a comparison of the run-time per iteration for both the proposed method and the U2-gossip baseline. This information is crucial for assessing the practical utility of the algorithm. If the per-iteration computational cost of the proposed method is substantially higher than that of U2-gossip, its practical applicability could be limited, despite its superior convergence properties.
