- Paper is well motivated and clearly written.
- Introduces synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics and provides convergence bounds for both.
- Empirical evaluation on two datasets (average relative AUC error) shows substantial gains over U2-gossip across diverse graph topologies.
- Key missing piece: runtime measurements — report per-iteration wall-clock times and, if possible, per-epoch or time-to-fixed-accuracy for both proposed methods and U2-gossip.
- Practical concern: if each iteration of the proposed methods is significantly slower than U2-gossip, the practical impact may be limited despite favorable iteration complexity/error metrics.
- Recommendation: include detailed runtime and computational/communication cost comparisons to substantially strengthen the paper’s practical relevance; overall contribution (motivation, theory, empirical gains) is compelling.
