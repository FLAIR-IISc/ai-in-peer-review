This paper presents new gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The proposed methods achieve faster convergence than the state of the art—specifically the U2-Gossip algorithm of Pelckmans and Suykens (2009)—and are designed to operate in more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results further demonstrate their superiority over existing algorithms.

The paper is well written, the problem is clearly motivated, and the authors position their work appropriately with respect to the existing literature. As far as I can tell, the algorithms are novel and improve both convergence and resource costs (memory and communication) compared to U2-Gossip. A notable strength is their applicability to asynchronous environments, which better reflects practical distributed systems.

In addition to providing theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors conduct extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets and compare against relevant baselines. These experiments substantiate the theoretical claims and strengthen the overall contribution.

Overall, this work introduces effective and well-justified algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics, with faster convergence and better practicality than prior approaches, validated by both theory and experiments.
