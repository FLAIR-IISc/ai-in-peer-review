- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics with derived convergence rates (1/t and log t / t).  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis; convergence rate proofs are a main strength.  
- Paper is well-organized and addresses a relevant problem likely useful to researchers.  
- Experiments demonstrate performance advantages and accuracy of proposed methods.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited; broader baselines (other distributed U-statistic methods) should be included.  
- Lacks discussion of computational cost (e.g., communication rounds) needed to reach a given accuracy.  
- Does not investigate sensitivity to network topology (degree distributions, bottlenecks) — needs empirical analysis.  
- Theoretical assumptions are under-specified (data distribution, limits on size/dimensionality); clarify practical limitations.  
- Overall contribution is clear and promising; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the above points.
