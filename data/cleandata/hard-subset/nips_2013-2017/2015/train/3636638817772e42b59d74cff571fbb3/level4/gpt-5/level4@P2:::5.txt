Summary
The paper proposes two randomized gossip algorithms—GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous)—for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a network where each node holds a single observation. Each node maintains a local estimate and an auxiliary observation that performs a random walk through the network via pairwise swaps. The algorithms combine two operations at each iteration: data propagation (to estimate node-wise averages hi) and averaging of local estimates (to reach the global U-statistic). The authors prove unbiasedness and convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous), with constants that make explicit the dependence on data dispersion and the network’s spectral gap. A “phantom nodes” construction underlies the analysis. Compared to the prior U2-gossip algorithm [17], the proposed methods require less memory and communication, and the authors provide refined bounds suggesting faster convergence in practice. Experiments on AUC estimation and within-cluster point scatter across several network topologies (complete, 2D grid, Watts-Strogatz) show lower error and variance than U2-gossip.

Relation to prior work
Classical gossip algorithms achieve fast averaging for separable statistics such as sums and means [2, 5, 10, 15], sometimes exploiting geography or topology for acceleration [6, 13]. Estimating U-statistics, which depend on pairs of observations, is more challenging and has received limited attention; U2-gossip [17] is the closest prior work. This paper extends gossip methodology to pairwise statistics with both synchronous and asynchronous variants, providing explicit rates and a more communication- and memory-efficient design.

Strengths
- Clear problem formulation and motivation: many important statistics (AUC, variance, Gini difference, within-cluster scatter) are U-statistics.
- Novel analysis tool (phantom nodes) that cleanly separates averaging from data propagation.
- Theoretical guarantees: unbiasedness and explicit convergence rates with network- and data-dependent constants; refined comparison to U2-gossip.
- Practical asynchronous algorithm that does not require a global clock and uses local counters to maintain unbiasedness.
- Empirical validation on real datasets across multiple network topologies, showing consistent gains over U2-gossip and illustrating the role of connectivity.

Weaknesses
- Several key proofs are deferred to the supplement; a brief proof sketch in the main text would aid readability.
- The asynchronous algorithm assumes nodes know their degree and the total number of edges (for pk and counters mk), which may be unrealistic in some deployments; alternatives or discussion of this assumption would strengthen the paper.
- Experimental scope is limited: two tasks and a modest variety of graphs; comparisons do not report communication (bytes) or memory costs per unit accuracy, which matter for high-dimensional data.
- The analysis and algorithms focus on degree-2 U-statistics; extensions to higher-order U-statistics are only hinted at.
- Robustness aspects (message loss, delays, non-uniform or time-varying edge activation) are not explored.

Quality
The paper is technically solid. The convergence analysis is carefully structured, with data-dependent dispersion terms and spectral-gap dependence that align with intuition from spectral graph theory. The refined bound for U2-gossip is a useful contribution that clarifies why the proposed averaging step helps. Experiments are well executed and support the claims, though broader metrics (e.g., communication cost) would add depth.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written and well organized. Algorithm descriptions are clear, and notation is mostly standard. Minor suggestions: briefly motivate the phantom-node construction in the main text; summarize the constants in the bounds and their practical magnitude; and clarify communication/memory complexity per iteration for both GoSta and U2-gossip.

Originality
The combination of randomized gossip with auxiliary-observation propagation for U-statistics, the asynchronous design with unbiased counters, and the phantom-node analytical framework appear novel relative to prior work, especially beyond U2-gossip [17].

Significance
U-statistics are widely used, and enabling their decentralized estimation with provable guarantees is valuable for distributed learning and sensing. The explicit spectral-gap dependence is informative for system designers. While the rates are sublinear (as expected for this harder problem), the empirical speed and reduced resource footprint over U2-gossip suggest practical impact.

Pros
- Extends gossip beyond separable functions to important pairwise statistics.
- Provable convergence with explicit, interpretable constants.
- Lower memory/communication than U2-gossip; practical asynchronous variant.
- Consistent empirical improvements across datasets and topologies.

Cons
- Reliance on knowledge of |E| and degrees in the asynchronous setting.
- Limited experimental metrics (no bandwidth/memory vs. accuracy trade-offs).
- Proofs largely in the supplement; limited discussion of robustness and higher-order U-statistics.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide a short proof sketch for the main theorems in the paper body.
- Report communication (messages and bytes) and memory per node, and plot error versus total communicated bits.
- Discuss how to relax or estimate pk without global knowledge of |E|.
- Explore non-uniform edge sampling or accelerated mixing (e.g., leveraging [6, 13]).
- Add experiments on robustness to message loss and on larger, more heterogeneous graphs.
- Outline how the approach could extend to higher-order U-statistics.

Overall assessment
A solid, well-motivated contribution with clear theoretical and empirical advances over prior work on decentralized U-statistics. I view it as suitable for acceptance after addressing the above clarifications.
