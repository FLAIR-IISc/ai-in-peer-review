This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. The proposed algorithms achieve a faster convergence rate than the current state-of-the-art (the U2-Gossip algorithm from Pelckmans & Suykens’09) and, importantly, operate effectively in more realistic asynchronous environments. Experimental results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms compared to existing approaches. 

The paper is well-written and clearly motivated, with the authors effectively situating their work within the existing literature. To the best of my knowledge, the algorithms presented are novel and offer improvements in both convergence and computational cost—specifically, reduced memory and communication overhead compared to U2-Gossip—while also accommodating asynchronous updates. 

Beyond the theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors provide extensive empirical validation using two datasets and comparisons to relevant baselines. This robust experimental evaluation further strengthens the paper’s contributions. In summary, this work presents new Gossip Algorithms for computing U-statistics, achieving faster convergence and extending applicability to realistic asynchronous settings, all substantiated by thorough experimental evaluation and comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
