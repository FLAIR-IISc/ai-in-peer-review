This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for the decentralized estimation of U-statistics, which are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their techniques, including a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of the proposed algorithms.

The paper begins by introducing the problem of estimating U-statistics in a decentralized setting, where data is partitioned across a network of nodes. The authors highlight the limitations of existing gossip algorithms, which are not designed to efficiently compute quantities that depend on pairs of observations. They then present their new algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, which achieve convergence rates of O(1/t) and O(log t/t), respectively.

The technical presentation is sound, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms. They also compare their approach to existing methods, including the U2-gossip algorithm, and demonstrate the advantages of their techniques through numerical experiments.

The experiments are conducted on two real-world datasets, and the results show that the proposed algorithms outperform existing approaches in terms of accuracy and convergence rate. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of the implications of their results and suggest potential extensions of their work.
