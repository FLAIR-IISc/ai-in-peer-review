- Introduces GoSta synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics (pairwise averages) in decentralized networks.
- Combines data propagation (nodes swap auxiliary observations) with estimate averaging so nodes gradually "see" the full dataset while converging.
- Provides rigorous convergence rates: synchronous O(1/t) and asynchronous O(log t / t), with dependence on network connectivity (spectral gap) and data terms.
- Uses a "phantom network" analytical tool to decouple propagation and averaging, making proofs tractable and insightful.
- Re-analyzes U2-gossip, revealing an unstated sqrt(n) dependence and theoretically explaining GoSta’s improved scalability.
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance to U2-gossip on real datasets for AUC and within-cluster scatter across multiple network topologies.
- Develops a fully asynchronous algorithm (GoSta-async), increasing practical applicability in realistic distributed systems.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and well-positioned relative to existing gossip literature.
- Experimental results validate theoretical claims and show the performance gap widens with network size.
- Weakness: the asynchronous constant c'(G) in Theorem 2 is not explicitly characterized in graph-theoretic terms.
- Weakness: extension to nodes holding multiple observations is only briefly mentioned; more detail on implementation and performance impact would help.
