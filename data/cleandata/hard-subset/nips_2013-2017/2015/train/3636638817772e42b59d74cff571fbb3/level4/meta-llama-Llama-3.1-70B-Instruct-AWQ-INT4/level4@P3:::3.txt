This paper proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors provide a thorough analysis of their approach, including convergence bounds, and demonstrate its effectiveness on two real-world datasets, the SMVguide3 binary classification dataset and the Wine Quality dataset.

The paper is well-motivated, and the results presented in the experiments section are impressive, showing that GoSta-sync outperforms the baseline method, U2-gossip, in various scenarios. The authors also provide a detailed comparison of their approach with existing gossip algorithms, highlighting the advantages of their method.

The main contributions of the paper are the introduction of the GoSta-sync and GoSta-async algorithms, which are designed to efficiently compute U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms, showing that they achieve a rate of O(1/t) and O(log t/t) for the synchronous and asynchronous cases, respectively.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The experiments section is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and easy-to-understand manner.
