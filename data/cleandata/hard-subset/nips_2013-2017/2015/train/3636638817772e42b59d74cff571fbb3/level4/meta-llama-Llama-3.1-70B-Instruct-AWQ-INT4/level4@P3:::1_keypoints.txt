- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics with clear motivation and literature positioning.  
- Demonstrates significant convergence improvements over state-of-the-art U2-gossip.  
- Algorithms are practical for real-world use because they handle asynchronous settings.  
- Introduces "phantom nodes" reformulation to model data propagation and estimate updates.  
- Establishes convergence rate bounds: O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit data- and network-dependent terms.  
- Shows lower memory and communication costs compared to U2-gossip.  
- Experimental results on SMVguide3 and Wine Quality datasets show better average relative error and variance, notably in asynchronous runs.  
- Paper is well-written and provides comprehensive theoretical analysis and experimental evaluation.  
- Strength: novel, efficient algorithms with improved rates and resource costs.  
- Strength: comprehensive analysis and empirical validation.  
- Weakness: assumes uniform edge selection for node sampling, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: experiments and analysis limited to complete graph, 2D grid, and Watts–Strogatz networks—generalization to other topologies unclear.  
- Argument for acceptance: significant contribution with novel algorithms, improved performance, and solid analysis/experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: practical assumptions and limited network evaluations leave questions about broader applicability.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but request additional analysis and experiments to address the uniform-edge assumption and broader network topologies.
