- Summary: Proposes a gossip algorithm that averages estimates and swaps a secondary state (initial observation) to create random walks enabling decentralized computation of pairwise U-statistics, with convergence-rate analysis.

- Strength: Timely and relevant extension of gossip algorithms to higher-order statistics.

- Strength: Algorithm is simple, communication-efficient, and easily implementable.

- Strength: Analysis connects naturally to random-walk and lifted-graph viewpoints.

- Strength: Empirical results are promising and presentation is generally clear.

- Major concern 1: Clarify the core algorithmic mechanism—make the swapping of secondary states and induced random walks explicit in the main text to motivate the analysis.

- Major concern 2: Strengthen related work positioning, especially relative to Dimakis et al. (2010), push-sum, randomized incremental, and token/random-walk schemes.

- Major concern 3: Explicitly specify the edge-selection distribution (uniform, degree-proportional, activation clock, etc.) because convergence rates depend on it.

- Major concern 4: Compare against flooding/memory and hybrid baselines (and provide experiments) to show when averaging-with-swap is advantageous and how the 1/t rate compares in practice.

- Major concern 5: Relate the method to lifting and accelerated consensus (cite Jung et al. 2007, LADA 2010, Chen/Lovász/Pak 1999), explain if the process is a lift and whether acceleration guarantees apply.

- Major concern 6: Clarify assumptions and guarantees—state whether results are worst-case or in expectation, the exact error metric, and how constants depend on spectral gap, degree distribution, and data properties.

- Experimental suggestions: add flooding/memory/hybrid baselines, show dependence on topology (paths, grids, expanders, small-world) and degree heterogeneity, ablate memory and communication budget, and state simulation edge-selection clearly.

- Minor presentation issues: fix several wording/grammar points, clarify bipartite/directed vs undirected edges, and define notation consistently between main text and supplement.

- Limitation of review: Reviewer did not verify correctness of supplemental proofs and technical lemmas.

- Overall assessment: Elegant and promising approach; recommend acceptance pending stronger related-work discussion and added baseline experiments.

- After author feedback: Authors addressed several concerns; reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 and recommends acceptance contingent on integrating revisions.
