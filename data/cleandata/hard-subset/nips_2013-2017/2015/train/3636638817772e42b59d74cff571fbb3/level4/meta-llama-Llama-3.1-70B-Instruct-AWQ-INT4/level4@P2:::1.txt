This paper presents new Gossip algorithms for computation of U-statistics, achieving faster convergence rates compared to the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) in both synchronous and asynchronous settings. The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, demonstrate superiority over existing methods through rigorous theoretical analysis and extensive empirical evaluations.

The authors have effectively motivated the problem, positioning their work within the existing literature on gossip algorithms and U-statistics. The paper is well-written, and the proposed algorithms appear novel, offering improved convergence rates and reduced memory and communication costs compared to U2-Gossip.

The theoretical guarantees and analysis provided for both algorithms are thorough and convincing. The authors have also conducted extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets, comparing their algorithms to state-of-the-art baselines. These experiments further strengthen the contributions of the paper, demonstrating the practical interest and superiority of the proposed algorithms.

The paper's organization and presentation are clear, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed algorithms. The use of relevant citations and references is appropriate, demonstrating the authors' familiarity with the existing literature.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and a clear presentation of the results. The authors have carefully evaluated the strengths and weaknesses of their algorithms, providing a balanced view of their contributions.

Regarding originality, the proposed algorithms and their analysis appear novel, offering significant improvements over existing methods. The authors have also explored the application of their algorithms in asynchronous settings, which is a valuable contribution to the field.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential impact on the field of distributed computation and U-statistics. The proposed algorithms offer faster convergence rates and reduced costs, making them more suitable for large-scale applications.

In conclusion, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of gossip algorithms and U-statistics, offering novel algorithms with improved convergence rates and reduced costs. The thorough theoretical analysis and extensive empirical evaluations further strengthen the paper's contributions, making it a valuable addition to the existing literature.

Arguments For Acceptance:

1. Novel algorithms with improved convergence rates and reduced costs.

3. Extensive empirical evaluations demonstrating superiority over state-of-the-art baselines.
4. Clear and well-organized presentation.
5. Significant potential impact on the field of distributed computation and U-statistics.
