- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized U-statistic estimation.
- Evaluated on two datasets, showing improved average relative AUC error over U2-gossip across various network topologies.
- Provides convergence bounds and theoretical analysis linking performance to data characteristics and network topology.
- Uses a “phantom node” technique in the analysis.
- Empirical validation also includes within-cluster point scatter estimation, supporting superiority over U2-gossip.
- Strength: addresses a relevant and challenging decentralized estimation problem.
- Strength: algorithms are novel and grounded in gossip protocol design.
- Strength: theoretical convergence guarantees are a strong contribution.
- Strength: experiments on real datasets convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.
- Weakness: lacks per-iteration runtime comparisons to U2-gossip, limiting assessment of practical utility.
- Weakness: asynchronous convergence rate (O(log t / t)) is weaker than synchronous (O(1/t)) and needs deeper discussion of trade-offs.
- Weakness: limited range of network sizes tested — scalability to large networks is unclear.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to simpler baselines (e.g., centralized computation then distribution).
- Recommendation: overall strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to include runtime measurements.
