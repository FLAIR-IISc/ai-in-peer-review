- Introduces synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) for distributed estimation of U-statistics using coupled random walks and "phantom nodes."
- Fills a significant gap: extends gossip methods beyond separable mean statistics to pairwise-dependent U-statistics.
- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit dependence on network and data properties.
- Technical analysis is rigorous, with clear theoretical bounds and meaningful network/data dependency characterization.
- Empirical results on AUC and within-cluster scatter show strong performance and outperform prior U2-gossip method.
- Originality: novel extension of gossip to U-statistics and an elegant "phantom nodes" analytical technique.
- Significance: addresses an important problem for distributed systems and has practical value for sensor networks and large-scale data processing.
- Related work is underdeveloped; must better situate the work within the broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. survey).
- Algorithm description needs clearer emphasis of the core mechanism: nodes exchange estimates and auxiliary observations to enable coupled random walks.
- Missing baseline comparison: include flooding/multiple-observation storage baseline to justify advantages and the claimed rates under memory constraints.
- Data-assumption clarity: explicitly state distributional or worst-case assumptions underlying the theoretical guarantees.
- Minor edits: correct phrasing/typos on Pages 4, 6, 7 and clarify that the bipartite definition applies to undirected graphs.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing related work, algorithm clarity, baseline comparison, and explicit data assumptions.
