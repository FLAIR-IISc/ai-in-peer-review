- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for distributed U-statistics computation.
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written, making contributions easy to follow.
- Strong theoretical contribution: derivation of convergence bounds for the proposed methods.
- Empirical evaluation on two datasets using average relative AUC error as the metric.
- Results show the proposed algorithms significantly outperform the U2-gossip baseline across various graph topologies.
- Primary concern: practical efficiency not fully assessed — only number of iterations to convergence is compared.
- Missing comparison of computational cost per iteration and wall-clock time versus U2-gossip.
- Recommendation: include run-time per iteration or total wall-clock time to convergence to validate practical utility; without it, overall efficiency is uncertain.
