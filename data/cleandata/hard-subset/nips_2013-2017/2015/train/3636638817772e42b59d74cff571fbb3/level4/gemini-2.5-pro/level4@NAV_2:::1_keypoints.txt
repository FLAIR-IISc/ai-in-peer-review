- Problem: Decentralized estimation of U-statistics (pairwise averages) that standard gossip cannot compute directly.  
- Prior work: Builds on Pelckmans & Suykens (2009) and the U2-gossip algorithm.  
- Main contribution: Introduces GoSta, a family of randomized gossip algorithms for synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Core idea: Combine random swaps to propagate observations with local averaging of estimates.  
- Theoretical results: Convergence rate O(1/t) for synchronous GoSta and O(log t / t) for asynchronous GoSta.  
- Network dependence: Analysis explicitly characterizes dependence on network connectivity via the spectral gap.  
- Technical novelty: Uses a reformulation with “phantom nodes” to enable rigorous analysis.  
- Improvement over prior work: Removes a limiting sqrt(n) factor present in U2-gossip and gives stronger guarantees.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and the phantom-network proof technique is clearly explained.  
- Empirical validation: Thorough experiments (AUC and within-cluster scatter) across three topologies show faster convergence and lower variance than U2-gossip.  
- Weakness: GoSta-async requires nodes to know selection probabilities pk = 2dk/|E|, i.e., global edge count |E|, which may be unrealistic in fully decentralized settings.  
- Weakness: Theoretical O(log t / t) slowdown for async is not evident in experiments; discussion on whether the log factor is loose or negligible would help.  
- Pro-accept: Addresses an important problem, proposes novel efficient algorithms, gives strong theory, and supports claims with experiments.  
- Con-accept: The global |E| assumption for async should be better justified or discussed (e.g., robustness to estimates or distributed estimation).
