2. Strengthen literature connection: paper should more comprehensively position itself within the large body of gossip/statistical computation work (e.g., Dimakis et al.).

3. Random-walk interpretation: the core mechanism (exchanging estimates and initial observations) effectively induces coupled random walks for initial observations and should be explicitly highlighted.

4. Clarify edge-selection distribution: the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” process must specify the exact distribution induced on network edges rather than saying “drawing a random edge.”

5. Compare with flooding baseline: evaluate a flooding approach (nodes store tagged observations) as a performance benchmark to judge competitiveness of the 1/t rate, especially on expanders.

6. Investigate lifting literature: discuss connections to lifting constructions and Location-Aided Distributed Averaging (LADA) with references (Jung–Shah–Shin, Li–Dai, Chen–Lovasz–Pak).

7. State data-assumption clearly: explicitly declare whether analysis assumes worst-case initial data distributions and what error guarantees hold for all X.

8. Experimental additions: include baseline comparisons (flooding and LADA-related methods) in experiments to strengthen empirical evaluation.
