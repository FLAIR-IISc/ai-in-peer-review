This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in distributed settings. The proposed algorithm is intuitive, but its novelty is not clearly established relative to existing approaches. On the positive side, the theoretical analysis is thorough and the proofs are comprehensive.

The main weakness is the empirical evaluation. The paper does not compare against any baseline methods, which makes it difficult to assess the practical value of the contribution. It should be straightforward to construct and include several intuitive baselines, especially those based on randomized or consensus-style techniques, and evaluate them under comparable conditions. Without such comparisons, the experimental section is insufficient to support the claimed advantages of the method.

In summary, while the problem is interesting and the theory is well developed, the lack of baseline comparisons and the overall weakness of the evaluation significantly limit the paper’s impact.
