- Addresses the important problem of estimating degree-two U-statistics in decentralized networks where standard gossip methods (for separable functions) fail.  
- U-statistics (e.g., variance, AUC, within-cluster scatter) are crucial for ML but require pairwise averaging, posing a gossip challenge.  
- Proposes two randomized gossip algorithms: GoSta-sync (synchronous) and GoSta-async (asynchronous) for decentralized U-statistic estimation.  
- Core idea: combine data-propagation via random-walking auxiliary observations with local averaging so node estimates converge to the global U-statistic.  
- Introduces the “phantom network” abstraction to decouple propagation and averaging, enabling rigorous analysis.  
- Proves convergence rates O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous algorithms, with dependence on data difficulty and network spectral gap.  
- Demonstrates practical improvements over prior U2-gossip [17]: faster convergence, lower memory (one auxiliary per node) and reduced communication (one communicating pair per step).  
- Presents a fully asynchronous version, removing the need for a global clock, which is important in practice.  
- Empirical results on real datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) across multiple topologies support the theoretical claims and show superior performance to the baseline.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and organized, with precise algorithm descriptions and experiments.  
- Weakness: GoSta-async requires nodes to know pk = 2dk/|E|, implying knowledge of the total edge count |E| (global information) which should be discussed practically.  
- Weakness: Empirical parity between async and sync contradicts the O(log t) theoretical gap; a brief discussion on whether the log t factor is loose or constants dominate would be helpful.  
- Weakness: Remark on extending to multiple observations per node is brief; more intuition or guidance (e.g., on setting edge weights) would aid practitioners.  
- Overall: High-quality, novel, and rigorous contribution that advances decentralized U-statistic estimation and is of clear interest to the distributed ML and gossip communities.
