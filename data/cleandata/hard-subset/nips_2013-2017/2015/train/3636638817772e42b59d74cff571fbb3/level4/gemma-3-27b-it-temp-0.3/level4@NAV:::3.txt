This paper presents a well-motivated and clearly written approach to computing U-statistics using synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on two datasets, reporting results based on average relative AUC error. Performance gains are substantial compared to the U2-gossip baseline across a variety of graph structures. Furthermore, the paper includes valuable theoretical contributions in the form of convergence bounds for the proposed algorithms. 

To further strengthen the evaluation, I recommend the inclusion of run-time measurements per iteration for both the proposed method and U2-gossip. Understanding the computational cost of each iteration is crucial; significantly slower iteration times for the proposed method could limit its practical applicability despite the observed performance improvements.
