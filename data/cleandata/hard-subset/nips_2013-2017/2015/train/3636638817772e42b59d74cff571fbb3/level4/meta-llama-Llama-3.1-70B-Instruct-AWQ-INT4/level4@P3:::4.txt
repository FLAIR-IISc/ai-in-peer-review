This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in a distributed environment. The main idea of the algorithms is to combine two steps at each iteration: a data propagation step to allow each node to estimate the U-statistic, and an averaging step to ensure convergence to the desired value.

The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the convergence rates of the proposed algorithms, showing that they achieve faster convergence rates than existing algorithms, such as U2-gossip. The analysis is based on a novel reformulation of the problem using "phantom nodes," which allows the authors to model the data propagation and averaging steps separately.

The paper also presents numerical experiments on real datasets, comparing the performance of the proposed algorithms to that of U2-gossip. The results show that the proposed algorithms achieve better performance on all types of networks, both in terms of average error and variance.

Strengths of the paper:

* The proposed algorithms are intuitive and easy to understand, making them a good contribution to the field of distributed computing.
* The analysis of the convergence rates is comprehensive and provides a clear understanding of the behavior of the algorithms.
* The numerical experiments are well-designed and provide a clear comparison of the performance of the proposed algorithms to that of existing algorithms.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The paper does not compare the proposed algorithms to any baseline measures, which makes it difficult to evaluate their performance in a broader context.
* The evaluation of the algorithms is limited to two specific applications, and it is not clear how well they would perform on other types of problems.
* The paper does not provide any discussion of the potential limitations or challenges of the proposed algorithms, such as scalability or robustness to node failures.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Consider adding baseline measures to the numerical experiments to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms.
* Discuss the potential limitations or challenges of the proposed algorithms and how they could be addressed in future work.
* Consider providing more details on the implementation of the algorithms and how they could be applied in practice.
