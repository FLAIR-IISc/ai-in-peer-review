This paper presents novel gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, achieving a faster convergence rate compared to the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009). Moreover, the proposed algorithms operate effectively in more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results further demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing methods.

The paper is well-written, with a clear motivation for the problem and a thorough positioning of the work within the existing literature. Upon examination, the proposed algorithms appear to be novel and superior in terms of convergence rate and cost (memory and communication) compared to the U2-Gossip algorithm. Notably, the proposed algorithm also functions effectively in asynchronous settings.

In addition to providing theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors have conducted extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets, comparing their approach to baseline methods. This further strengthens the contributions of the paper. Overall, this paper presents new gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, characterized by a faster convergence rate and effectiveness in realistic asynchronous settings. The algorithms are evaluated and compared to the state-of-the-art through extensive experiments, demonstrating their superiority.

Strengths:

- The paper proposes novel gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, achieving faster convergence rates than existing methods.
- The algorithms operate effectively in asynchronous settings, making them more practical for real-world applications.
- The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, including guarantees and convergence rates.
- The paper includes extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets, comparing the proposed algorithms to baseline methods.

Weaknesses:

- None noted.

Suggestions for improvement:

- Consider providing more detailed information about the experimental setup, including the specific parameters used for each algorithm.
- It may be helpful to include a more in-depth discussion of the implications of the results, particularly in terms of how the proposed algorithms can be applied in practice.

Rating: 9/10

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant contribution to the field of gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The inclusion of theoretical guarantees, empirical evaluations, and a thorough analysis of the results makes this a strong paper. With some minor revisions to address the suggested improvements, this paper has the potential to be even more impactful.
