This paper presents novel Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. The proposed algorithms offer significant improvements over the state-of-the-art, demonstrating a faster convergence rate than the U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, '09) while also operating in more realistic asynchronous settings.

The paper is well-written, the problem is clearly motivated, and the authors effectively position their work with respect to the existing literature. As far as I can see, the proposed algorithms are novel and superior, not only in terms of convergence but also in memory and communication costs when compared to U2-Gossip. The extension to asynchronous environments is a key practical advantage.

The contributions are strengthened by the inclusion of both theoretical guarantees and extensive empirical evaluations. The experiments, conducted on two datasets and compared against baselines, provide strong evidence for the practical superiority of the proposed algorithms.
