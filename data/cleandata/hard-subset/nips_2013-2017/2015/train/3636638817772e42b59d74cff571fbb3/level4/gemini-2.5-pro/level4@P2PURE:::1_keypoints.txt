- Proposes novel Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics.  
- Demonstrates faster convergence than U2-Gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens, ’09).  
- Operates in realistic asynchronous settings, extending prior synchronous methods.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear motivation and literature positioning.  
- Algorithms are novel and superior in convergence, memory, and communication costs vs U2-Gossip.  
- Asynchronous extension is a significant practical advantage.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluations on two datasets against baselines strongly support practical superiority.
