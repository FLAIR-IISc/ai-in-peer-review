The paper is well-motivated and well-written, presenting results on two datasets evaluated based on the average relative AUC error. Compared to the baseline U2-gossip, the proposed method demonstrates significantly improved performance across varied scenarios, including different graph structures. Furthermore, the paper provides convergence bounds for the proposed approach. 

However, I would like to see a comparison of the run-times per iteration for both the proposed method and U2-gossip. If each iteration of the proposed method is substantially slower than U2-gossip, the utility of the paper may be limited. The authors propose both synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which is a notable contribution.
