- Paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute pairwise U-statistics; contribution is nice but needs better connection to prior work.  
- Key algorithmic trick: nodes swap a secondary state (their initial observation), causing observations to perform coupled random walks; this should be highlighted in the main text and motivates the analysis via multiple graph copies.  
- Broader related-work survey needed — cite and situate against prior gossip/statistics literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. Proc. IEEE) rather than the current cursory treatment.  
- Clarify the induced distribution on edges from the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process; “drawing a random edge” is ambiguous.  
- Consider and compare an alternative flooding approach where nodes store received observations (tagged by origin) to compute U-statistics locally — this could clarify when the averaging method is preferable and whether rate 1/t is good or bad (esp. on expanders).  
- Investigate and compare to lifting constructions for accelerating gossip (e.g., Jung et al., Li & Dai, Chen et al.), as the method appears related.  
- Analysis needs to state assumptions on the initial data distribution explicitly and clarify whether guarantees are worst-case over all initial vectors and how error depends on the initial data.  
- Small edits/typos suggested throughout (examples: bipartite definition implies directed graphs; several phrasing fixes on p4–p7).  
- After author feedback the reviewer raised score (7→8) but still requests related-work updates and inclusion of baseline experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending revisions that address the above clarifications, related work, and experiments.
