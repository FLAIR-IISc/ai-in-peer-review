- Paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute higher-order U-statistics (variance, correlation, scatter) over networked pairs of observations.  
- Valuable contribution to gossip literature, but connection to prior work is weak and should be strengthened.  
- Reviewer could not fully verify the supplement due to time constraints.

- Comment 1: Algorithm exchanges both estimates and the originating observations, inducing coupled random walks; this mechanism should be explicitly highlighted to motivate the analysis.  
- Comment 2: Broaden related work (e.g., Dimakis et al.) to better situate the contribution within existing gossip/statistical computation literature.  
- Comment 3: Clarify the induced edge distribution from the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process — “drawing a random edge” is insufficiently specific.  
- Comment 4: Compare to a flooding-with-memory alternative (nodes store tagged received observations) to assess trade-offs and whether the 1/t convergence is favorable, especially on expander graphs.  
- Comment 5: Investigate and discuss connections to lifting constructions that accelerate gossip (e.g., Jung–Shah–Shin, Li–Dai, Chen–Lovász–Pak) and LADA.  
- Comment 6: State assumptions about the initial data distribution explicitly and provide error guarantees as a function of the worst-case initial data vector X.

- Minor points: bipartite definition as written implies directed graphs; several phrasing/typo fixes (p4–p7) suggested.  
- After author feedback: reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 but still requests inclusion of lifting/LADA discussion and baseline comparisons in experiments; recommends acceptance conditional on addressing the comments.
