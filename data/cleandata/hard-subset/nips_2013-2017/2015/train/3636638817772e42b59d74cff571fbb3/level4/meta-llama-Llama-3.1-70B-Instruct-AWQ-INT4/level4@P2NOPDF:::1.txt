This paper introduces novel gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics, achieving a faster convergence rate compared to the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) and operating effectively in more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing methods.

The paper is well-written, with a clear motivation for the problem and a thorough positioning of the work within the existing literature. Upon examination, the proposed algorithms appear to be novel and superior in terms of convergence rate and cost (including memory and communication) compared to the U2-Gossip algorithm. Notably, the proposed algorithm is also applicable to asynchronous settings.

In addition to providing theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors have conducted extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets, comparing their approach to baseline methods. This comprehensive evaluation further reinforces the contributions of the paper. Overall, this work presents new gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, characterized by improved convergence rates and suitability for realistic asynchronous settings, and evaluates these algorithms through extensive experiments.
