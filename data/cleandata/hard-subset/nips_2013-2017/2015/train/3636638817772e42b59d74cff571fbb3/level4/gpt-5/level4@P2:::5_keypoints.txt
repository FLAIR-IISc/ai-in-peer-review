- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics using local estimates plus an auxiliary observation that random-walks via pairwise swaps.
- Each iteration mixes data propagation (estimating node-wise hi) and averaging of local estimates to reach the global U-statistic.
- Proves unbiasedness and convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with constants depending on data dispersion and the network spectral gap.
- Analysis relies on a novel “phantom nodes” construction that separates averaging from data propagation.
- Compared to prior U2-gossip, the methods use less memory and communication and have refined bounds suggesting faster practical convergence.
- Experiments (AUC and within-cluster scatter) across complete, 2D grid, and Watts-Strogatz graphs show lower error and variance than U2-gossip.
- Situates work as extending gossip algorithms from separable statistics to pairwise U-statistics; U2-gossip is the closest related method.
- Strength: clear problem motivation since many important statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, scatter) are U-statistics.
- Strength: phantom-node analysis is a novel analytical tool that cleanly decouples algorithmic components.
- Strength: provides explicit, data- and network-dependent theoretical guarantees and refined comparison to U2-gossip.
- Strength: practical asynchronous variant that preserves unbiasedness via local counters without a global clock.
- Strength: empirical validation on real datasets and multiple topologies supports claimed benefits.
- Weakness: many key proofs are deferred to the supplement; main text lacks proof sketches to aid readability.
- Weakness: asynchronous algorithm assumes knowledge of node degrees and total edges (pk, mk), which may be unrealistic in deployments.
- Weakness: experimental scope is limited (two tasks, modest graph variety) and omits communication (bytes) and memory-vs-accuracy comparisons.
- Weakness: focus restricted to degree-2 U-statistics; extensions to higher-order U-statistics are only briefly mentioned.
- Weakness: robustness to message loss, delays, or non-uniform/time-varying edge activations is not explored.
- Quality: technically solid convergence analysis with intuitive spectral-gap dependence; refined U2-gossip bound is a helpful clarification.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written and organized; could better motivate phantom nodes, summarize constants, and clarify per-iteration communication/memory.
- Originality: combination of gossip with auxiliary-observation propagation, asynchronous unbiased counters, and phantom-node analysis appears novel.
- Significance: important practical contribution enabling decentralized estimation of U-statistics with provable guarantees and informative spectral-gap dependence.
- Pros: extends gossip to pairwise statistics, gives provable convergence with interpretable constants, reduces resource needs, and shows consistent empirical improvements.
- Cons: reliance on |E| and degrees in async setting, limited experimental metrics, proofs mainly in supplement, limited robustness discussion.
- Suggestions: include short proof sketches in main text; report messages/bytes and memory per node and plot error vs communicated bits; discuss estimating/relaxing pk without global |E|; explore non-uniform edge sampling or accelerated mixing; test robustness and larger heterogeneous graphs; outline extensions to higher-order U-statistics.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated contribution with clear theoretical and empirical advances over prior work; suitable for acceptance after addressing the listed clarifications.
