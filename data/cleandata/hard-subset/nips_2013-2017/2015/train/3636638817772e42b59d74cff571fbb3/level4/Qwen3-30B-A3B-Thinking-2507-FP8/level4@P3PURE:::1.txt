

This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, demonstrating faster convergence rates compared to the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) while operating effectively in more realistic asynchronous settings. The proposed algorithms also exhibit superior efficiency in terms of memory and communication costs relative to U2-Gossip. The paper is well-motivated, clearly positioned within the existing literature, and well-written. The authors provide rigorous theoretical guarantees alongside extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets, comparing their approach against relevant baselines. These experiments robustly confirm the theoretical advantages, establishing the proposed algorithms as both novel and superior in practice.
