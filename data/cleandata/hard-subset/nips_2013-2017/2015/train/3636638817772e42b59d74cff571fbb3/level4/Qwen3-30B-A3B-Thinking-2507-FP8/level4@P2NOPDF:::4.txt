

This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in distributed systems. While the proposed algorithm is intuitive, its novelty is not clearly established; the comprehensive proofs represent a positive aspect. A significant weakness is the absence of baseline comparisons. Several straightforward baselines—such as randomized sampling or gossip-based protocols—should be included to contextualize the results. Without these comparisons, the evaluation lacks rigor, and claims regarding efficiency or accuracy remain unsubstantiated. The problem is of interest, but the insufficient evaluation weakens the paper's contribution.
