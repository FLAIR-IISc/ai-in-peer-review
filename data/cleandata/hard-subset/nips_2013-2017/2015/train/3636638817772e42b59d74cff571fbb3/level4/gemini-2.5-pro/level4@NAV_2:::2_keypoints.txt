- Problem & contribution: Proposes GoSta synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms that compute U-statistics by averaging estimates while swapping observations, with convergence rates O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t / t) (async).
- Significance: Tackles an important, practical problem—decentralized computation of widely used U-statistics for sensor networks and large-scale systems.
- Originality: Novel combination of estimate averaging and observation swapping; elegant "phantom network" analysis technique.
- Theoretical quality: Analysis appears sound; convergence bounds explicitly depend on data and network spectral gap.
- Empirical quality: Experiments on real datasets convincingly show GoSta outperforms prior U2-gossip in accuracy and variance.
- Clarity issue: Need clearer intuition and exposition that initial observations perform coupled random walks via the swapping step to motivate the phantom-network analysis.
- Related work: Literature review is narrow—should cite broader surveys (e.g., Dimakis et al. 2010) and discuss connections to lifted Markov chains (e.g., Jung et al. 2007; Li & Dai 2010; Chen et al. 1999).
- Baseline comparison: Missing a natural flooding-based baseline comparison to contextualize convergence speed and memory/trade-offs.
- Technical ambiguities: Clarify the edge-selection model (uniform over E vs node-wakeup) and state assumptions on initial data for the convergence guarantees (worst-case or distributional).
- Minor edits: Fix bipartite graph notation and several wording choices on pp.1,4,6,7.
- Recommendation: Strong, well-supported contribution; recommend acceptance if authors address related-work connections, baseline comparison, and technical clarifications.
