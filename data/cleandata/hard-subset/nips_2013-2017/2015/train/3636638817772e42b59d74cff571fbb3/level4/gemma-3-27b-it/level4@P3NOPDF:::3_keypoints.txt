- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics with clear motivation and readable presentation.
- Evaluated on two datasets using average relative AUC error as the performance metric.
- Proposed methods significantly outperform the U2-gossip baseline across various graph structures.
- Provides theoretical convergence bounds, strengthening the paper’s rigor.
- Inclusion of both synchronous and asynchronous variants increases applicability to different network environments.
- Major missing evaluation: no computational cost analysis (per-iteration runtime) to assess practical feasibility.
- Recommends reporting wall-clock time to convergence in addition to per-iteration timings.
- Requests more detailed descriptions/statistics of graph characteristics used (diameter, degree distribution, clustering coefficient).
- Suggests adding a parameter-sensitivity analysis to show performance dependence on hyperparameters.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with important practical improvements; score 7/10 (Strong Accept) contingent on addressing runtime concerns.
- Direct questions for authors: per-iteration runtimes vs U2-gossip, detailed graph structure statistics, and sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.
