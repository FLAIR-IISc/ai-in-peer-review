This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in a distributed environment. The proposed algorithm is intuitive and supported by comprehensive proofs.

While the theoretical analysis appears sound, the paper has two primary weaknesses. First, the novelty of the algorithm is not clearly established. Given its intuitive nature, the paper would be strengthened by a more explicit discussion of how the proposed method differs from and improves upon existing techniques.

Second, and more significantly, the empirical evaluation is weak due to a complete lack of baseline comparisons. The experiments are presented in a vacuum, making it impossible to gauge the practical significance or performance benefits of the proposed algorithm. To properly contextualize the results, the authors should have compared their method against several relevant baselines. For example, simple randomized or gossip-based algorithms could serve as effective points of comparison.

In conclusion, while the paper tackles an interesting problem and provides a theoretically grounded solution, its contribution is undermined by a weak evaluation. The absence of baseline comparisons makes the claimed performance of the algorithm difficult to verify and interpret.
