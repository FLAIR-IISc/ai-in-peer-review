- Proposes a novel gossip-based method for aggregating U-statistics by exchanging estimates and secondary state variables representing initial observations.  
- Conceptually sound approach that fills a gap in decentralized computation of higher-order statistics for sensor networks.  
- Algorithm design uses secondary states to enable coupled random walks of initial observations, offering a natural convergence mechanism.  
- Mathematical formulation is generally clear and experimental results demonstrate practical feasibility.  
- Related work is insufficiently contextualized; must engage foundational literature (e.g., Dimakis et al., 2010) and contrast with methods for non-linear statistics.  
- Needs explicit comparison to lifting/acceleration constructions (e.g., Jung et al. 2007; Chen et al. 1999; Li & Dai, 2010) and discussion of how the coupling mechanism relates to these techniques.  
- Missing analysis and experiments comparing against a flooding baseline and memory-constrained variants; should quantify trade-offs between memory usage and convergence speed (1/t vs. possible faster flooding rates on expanders).  
- Technical clarity issues: specify edge-sampling distribution, state worst-case vs. distributional assumptions for error bounds (e.g., O(1/t) for all initial data vectors), and correct graph/bipartite definitions.  
- Several minor presentation edits are needed (e.g., wording fixes on Pages 1, 4, 6, 7).  
- Authors’ revisions improved the paper, raising score, but two critical gaps remain: explicitly discuss lifting constructions and include flooding-baseline experiments under varying memory constraints.  
- Recommendation: Accept after revisions contingent on addressing lifting-related related work, baseline experiments, and clarifying technical assumptions.
