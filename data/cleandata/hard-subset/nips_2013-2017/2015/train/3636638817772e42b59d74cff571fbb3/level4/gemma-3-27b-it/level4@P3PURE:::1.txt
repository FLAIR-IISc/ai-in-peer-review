This paper presents novel Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. The proposed algorithms achieve a faster convergence rate compared to the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009) and, crucially, operate effectively in more realistic asynchronous settings. Experimental results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing approaches.

The paper is well-written, clearly motivating the problem and effectively positioning the work within the relevant literature. To the best of my understanding, the algorithms are novel and offer improvements over U2-Gossip in terms of convergence speed and resource cost – specifically, memory and communication complexity. The ability to handle asynchronous settings is also a significant advantage. 

Beyond the theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors provide extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets, comparing their performance against established baselines. This strong empirical validation further strengthens the contributions of the paper. In summary, this work introduces new Gossip Algorithms for computing U-statistics, offering both faster convergence and applicability to realistic asynchronous environments, substantiated by thorough experimental evaluation and comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
