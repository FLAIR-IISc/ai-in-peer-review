- Proposes two novel randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, for computing U-statistics in decentralized networks.  
- Targets limitations of existing gossip methods for U-statistics that require averaging over pairs of observations.  
- Builds on U2-gossip [17] and highlights U2-gossip’s higher memory/communication costs and need for a global clock.  
- GoSta-sync achieves convergence rate O(1/t).  
- GoSta-async achieves convergence rate O(log t / t).  
- Provides detailed convergence analysis via transition matrices and expected state evolution.  
- Demonstrates superiority over U2-gossip in convergence rate, memory usage, and communication cost.  
- Empirical evaluation on AUC estimation and within-cluster scatter tasks.  
- Experiments run on complete graph, 2D grid, and Watts–Strogatz networks.  
- Results show GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip in average relative error and variance.  
- Strength: novel algorithms tailored for decentralized U-statistics computation.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical analysis of convergence rates.  
- Strength: experimental results supporting claimed improvements.  
- Weakness: comparison to baseline measures is insufficient and could be deeper.  
- Weakness: evaluation is somewhat weak; additional experiments are recommended.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, efficient algorithms with solid analysis and supportive experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited apparent novelty (algorithms are intuitive) and weak empirical evaluation.  
- Overall: meaningful contribution to decentralized U-statistics, but would benefit from more extensive experiments and baselines.
