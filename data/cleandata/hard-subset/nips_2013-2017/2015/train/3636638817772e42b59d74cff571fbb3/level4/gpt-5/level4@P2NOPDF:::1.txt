This paper introduces new gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. Relative to the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009), the proposed methods converge faster, incur lower memory and communication costs, and function effectively in realistic asynchronous settings. 

The paper is clearly written, the problem is well motivated, and the positioning with respect to prior work is careful and convincing. To the best of my knowledge, the algorithms are novel and address both theoretical and practical aspects of distributed U-statistics computation.

Theoretical analysis provides solid convergence guarantees, and the empirical evaluation—conducted on two datasets and benchmarked against strong baselines—consistently supports the claimed advantages. The experimental results clearly demonstrate improvements over existing methods, including in asynchronous environments.

Overall, this is a strong contribution. The combination of improved convergence, reduced resource requirements, and robustness to asynchrony, together with thorough analysis and experiments, makes the work both original and practically relevant.
