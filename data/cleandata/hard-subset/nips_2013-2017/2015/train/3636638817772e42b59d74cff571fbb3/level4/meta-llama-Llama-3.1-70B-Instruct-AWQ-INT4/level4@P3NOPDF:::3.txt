The paper is well-motivated and well-written, presenting a clear and compelling case for the proposed approach. The results of the authors' method are evaluated on two datasets, with performance measured in terms of average relative AUC error. A comparison with the baseline U2-gossip algorithm demonstrates that the proposed method performs significantly better across various scenarios, including different graph structures.

One of the strengths of the paper is the provision of convergence bounds for the proposed method, which provides theoretical guarantees for its performance. However, to further assess the practical utility of the approach, it would be beneficial to include run-times per iteration for both the proposed method and the U2-gossip algorithm. If each iteration of the proposed method is substantially slower than U2-gossip, the advantages of the new approach may be limited by its computational efficiency.

The paper proposes synchronized and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which is a valuable contribution to the field. Overall, the authors have made a significant effort to present a thorough and well-evaluated approach, and with some additional information on computational efficiency, the paper has the potential to make a substantial impact.
