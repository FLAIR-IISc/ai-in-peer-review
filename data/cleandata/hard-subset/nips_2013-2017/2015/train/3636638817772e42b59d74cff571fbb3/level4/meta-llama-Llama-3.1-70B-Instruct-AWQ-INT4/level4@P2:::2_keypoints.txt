- Proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute pairwise U‑statistics by nodes exchanging estimates and swapping a secondary state so observations perform coupled random walks, analyzed via a novel multiple-copy graph construction.  
- Nice contribution to gossip literature but needs stronger positioning against prior work (e.g., Dimakis et al. Proc. IEEE) and a more thorough state-of-the-art discussion.  
- Clarify the exact induced distribution on edges from the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process — “drawing a random edge” is underspecified.  
- Compare with a natural alternative where nodes flood and store received observations (tagged by origin) and compute U‑statistics on their local subset to evaluate memory–performance tradeoffs versus the proposed averaging.  
- Examine related work on lifting constructions for accelerating gossip algorithms, since the paper’s construction appears connected to lifting techniques.  
- State explicit assumptions on the initial data distribution and whether theorem guarantees are worst-case over all initial data vectors X; provide error bounds as a function of the initial data.  
- Scores and recommendation: Quality 8, Clarity 8, Originality 7, Significance 8, Recommendation — Accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor editorial fixes: bipartite definition on p1 implies directed graphs; wording fixes on p4 (“we stress that”, “awakes and contacts”), p6 (“requires initiating”), and p7 (“allows us to create”).
