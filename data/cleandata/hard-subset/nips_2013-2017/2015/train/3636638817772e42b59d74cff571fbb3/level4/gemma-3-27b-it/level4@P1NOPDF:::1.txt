This paper presents novel Gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. The proposed algorithms achieve a faster convergence rate compared to the state-of-the-art (specifically, the U2-Gossip algorithm from Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009) and operate effectively in more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing approaches.

The paper is well-written, the problem is well-motivated, and the authors have effectively positioned their work within the existing literature. To the best of my knowledge, the proposed algorithms are novel and offer improvements in terms of convergence and cost – specifically, reduced memory and communication overhead compared to U2-Gossip. A significant advantage is the algorithm’s ability to function correctly in asynchronous settings.

Beyond strong theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors provide extensive empirical evaluations using two datasets and comparisons to relevant baselines. This further strengthens the contributions of the paper. In summary, this paper introduces new Gossip Algorithms for computing U-statistics, achieving faster convergence rates and enabling operation in realistic asynchronous environments. The algorithms are thoroughly evaluated and compared to state-of-the-art methods through comprehensive experimentation.
