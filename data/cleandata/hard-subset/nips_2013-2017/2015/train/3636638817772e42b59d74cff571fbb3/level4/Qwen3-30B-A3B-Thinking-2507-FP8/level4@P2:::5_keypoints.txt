- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms (GoSta) for distributed estimation of U-statistics, addressing a clear gap versus distributed mean estimation.
- U-statistics require pairwise interactions (e.g., AUC, variance), and existing gossip work mostly targets separable functions; this paper fills that gap.
- Proposes algorithms that propagate data and maintain local U-statistic estimates with convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).
- Substantially improves on prior U2-gossip [17], reducing memory and communication costs and supporting asynchrony.
- Provides rigorous convergence proofs using a novel "phantom nodes" reformulation that decouples data propagation from averaging.
- Convergence bounds explicitly include data-dependent and network-dependent (spectral gap) terms, offering insight into performance drivers.
- Theoretical analysis is comprehensive with detailed proofs in the supplementary material.
- Empirical evaluation on real datasets (AUC on SMVguide3, within-cluster scatter on Wine Quality) shows GoSta outperforming U2-gossip across topologies (complete, 2D grid, Watts–Strogatz) in accuracy and variance reduction.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized; algorithms are described clearly and supplementary material supports reproducibility.
- Originality is strong: adaptation of gossip to U-statistics is novel and well-differentiated from prior work and related NIPS papers.
- Significance is high due to applicability to important ML metrics (e.g., AUC) and potential impact on distributed learning scenarios.
- Minor weakness: limitations and practical implementation issues (node failures, dynamic networks) are not fully addressed, and comparisons are limited mainly to U2-gossip.
- Overall recommendation: strong technical contribution, well-validated and clearly presented; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
