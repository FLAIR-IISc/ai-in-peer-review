- Problem & contribution: Computes U-statistics in decentralized networks via GoSta (synchronous/asynchronous) using averaging plus observation-swapping, yielding random-walk mixing of observations and convergence O(1/t) (sync) and O(log t/t) (async); empirically beats U2-gossip.

- Novelty: Extends gossip algorithms to second-order U-statistics, a less-explored and practically important problem.

- Algorithmic simplicity: GoSta is intuitive and elegant, combining standard averaging with an observation-swapping mechanism.

- Theoretical guarantees: Provides formal convergence rates that capture dependence on network topology (spectral gap) and data properties.

- Empirical improvement: Shows clear empirical and theoretical advantages over prior U2-gossip in convergence, memory, and communication.

- Related-work gap: Related work section is cursory; should cite and discuss standard surveys (e.g., Dimakis et al., 2010) for context.

- Connection to lifting: The phantom-network analysis resembles lifting/Markov-chain acceleration techniques and should be compared to lifting literature (e.g., Jung et al. 2007; Li & Dai 2010; Chen et al. 1999) to assess originality.

- Missing baseline: Lacks comparison to a flooding/broadcast baseline (memory-intensive) which would clarify trade-offs between memory/communication and convergence speed.

- Data-assumption clarity: Needs explicit statements about assumptions on initial data distribution and how error bound constants depend on the initial data vector.

- Algorithmic intuition placement: The random-walk intuition for observations should be stated earlier (intro or start of Sec. 4) to motivate the phantom-graph analysis.

- Sampling ambiguity: Clarify the edge selection process ("Draw (i,j) uniformly from E")—whether edges are chosen uniformly or via node-driven wake-up (degree-biased).

- Significance: Results are significant and practically useful for decentralized monitoring and analysis; addressing the noted gaps would further strengthen impact.

- Minor edits: Fix bipartite graph notation for undirected graphs and several phrasing/typo issues (examples given on p2, p4, p6, p7).

- Overall recommendation: Accept, conditional on addressing literature positioning and flooding-baseline comparison.
