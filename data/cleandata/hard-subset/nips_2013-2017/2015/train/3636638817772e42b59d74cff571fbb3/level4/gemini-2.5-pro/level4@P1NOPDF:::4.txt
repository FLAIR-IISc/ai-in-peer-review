This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in a distributed environment. The proposed algorithm is intuitive, and the accompanying theoretical proofs are comprehensive.

However, the novelty of the algorithm itself seems limited. The primary weakness of the paper is the lack of any comparison to baseline methods in the experimental evaluation. It is likely that several intuitive baselines could be formulated, for instance, based on randomized algorithms. Such comparisons are essential for contextualizing the performance of the proposed method and justifying its contribution.

In summary, while the paper tackles an interesting problem, its contribution is significantly weakened by an evaluation that does not include any baseline measures.
