- Proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm for computing U-statistics (pairwise stats) via a novel decentralized averaging protocol.  
- Targets important statistics such as sample variance, correlation, and scatter coefficients.  
- Core mechanism: nodes average estimates and swap a secondary state (initial observation), making initial observations perform coupled random walks.  
- Paper addresses a relevant problem and offers an interesting extension of gossip protocols; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending revisions; proofs not verified.  
- Major: Algorithmic mechanism is not clearly explained—authors should explicitly present the random-walk perspective and the multi-copy graph construction.  
- Major: Related-work discussion is insufficient—must compare to Dimakis et al. survey and distinguish from lifting/acceleration methods (Chen et al. 1999; Jung et al. 2007; Li & Dai 2010).  
- Major: Lacks comparison to a natural baseline—need theoretical/empirical comparison to a flooding-based gossip approach to assess trade-offs (memory, communication, convergence).  
- Major: Theoretical assumptions are ambiguous—clarify whether guarantees hold for any initial data and how error bounds depend on initial configuration.  
- Minor: Clarify bipartite graph notation that currently suggests directed edges.  
- Minor: Specify the exact edge-selection probability from the "node wakes up and contacts a neighbor" communication model (uniform edges vs degree-weighted).  
- Minor: Suggested grammatical fixes (examples: p4 "let us stress out that"→"we stress that"; "awakes and contact"→"awakes and contacts"; p6 "requires to initiate"→"requires initiating"; p7 "allows to create"→"allows us to create").
