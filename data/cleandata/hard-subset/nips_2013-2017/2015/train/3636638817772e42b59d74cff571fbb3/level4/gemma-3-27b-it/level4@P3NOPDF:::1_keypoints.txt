- Introduces novel Gossip algorithms for distributed U-statistic computation improving on prior methods.  
- Reports faster convergence rates compared to U2-Gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009).  
- Supports asynchronous communication, making it applicable to realistic distributed settings.  
- Claims improvements in computational cost and resource usage (memory and communication).  
- Theoretical results are provided and bolstered by experiments.  
- Empirical evaluation on two datasets shows superior convergence speed and efficiency versus baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates contributions within related work.  
- Weakness: Lacks broader comparisons to other distributed frameworks (e.g., parameter servers, other gossip variants).  
- Weakness: Dataset choices are limited; needs evaluation across more varied data characteristics and scales.  
- Weakness: Communication-cost analysis is not sufficiently quantified (rounds/bytes transferred).  
- Weakness: No discussion of limitations or scenarios where the algorithms may perform poorly.  
- Overall: Strong paper with tangible contributions; recommendation to accept, with minor revisions to address weaknesses.
