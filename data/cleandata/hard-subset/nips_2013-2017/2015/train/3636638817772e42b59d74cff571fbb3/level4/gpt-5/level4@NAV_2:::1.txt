This work introduces new gossip algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics. Compared to the U2-gossip method of Pelckmans and Suykens (2009), the proposed approaches achieve faster convergence and explicitly handle the more practical fully asynchronous setting. The experimental results further substantiate the claimed advantages over existing methods.

The paper is clearly written, the problem is well-motivated, and the related literature is appropriately positioned. To the best of my knowledge, the algorithms are novel and improve upon prior work in both convergence behavior and resource requirements (memory and communication relative to U2-gossip). The inclusion of an asynchronous variant is an additional practical benefit.

In addition to theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors provide extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets with comparisons to baselines, which strengthens the overall contribution. In sum, the paper presents new gossip algorithms for U-statistic estimation with improved convergence and support for realistic asynchronous operation, backed by comprehensive experimental validation against the state of the art.
