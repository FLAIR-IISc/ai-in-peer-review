

This paper introduces novel Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, demonstrating faster convergence rates than the state-of-the-art U2-Gossip algorithm (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009) while operating effectively in realistic asynchronous settings. The paper is well-written, with a clearly motivated problem statement and strong positioning relative to existing literature. The proposed algorithms are novel, offer superior convergence properties, and reduce memory and communication costs compared to U2-Gossip, with valid operation in asynchronous environments. Beyond rigorous theoretical analysis, the authors provide extensive empirical validation across two datasets against relevant baselines, significantly strengthening the paper's contributions. The experimental results robustly confirm the proposed methods' superiority over existing approaches.
