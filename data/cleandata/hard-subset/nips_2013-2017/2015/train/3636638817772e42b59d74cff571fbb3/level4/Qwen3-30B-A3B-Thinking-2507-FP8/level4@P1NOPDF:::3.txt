

The paper presents a well-motivated and clearly written study on randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, offering both synchronous and asynchronous variants. The approach demonstrates significant superiority over the U2-gossip baseline across diverse graph topologies, as evidenced by results on two datasets evaluated using average relative AUC error. The theoretical contribution includes convergence bounds for the proposed method, which strengthens the technical foundation.  

A critical question remains regarding computational efficiency: the authors should report per-iteration runtime comparisons between their method and U2-gossip. If each iteration of the proposed algorithm is substantially slower, this could significantly limit the method's practical utility despite its strong empirical performance. Providing these runtime metrics would be essential for a balanced assessment of the approach's real-world applicability.
