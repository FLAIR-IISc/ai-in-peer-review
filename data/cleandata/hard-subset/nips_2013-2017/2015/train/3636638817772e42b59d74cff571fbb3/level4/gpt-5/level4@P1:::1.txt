Summary
This paper proposes new randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a network. The authors present both a synchronous variant (GoSta-sync) and a fully asynchronous variant (GoSta-async). Each node maintains a local estimate and a single auxiliary observation; at each iteration, nodes update their running average of H(Xk, Yk), then a randomly selected edge’s endpoints average their estimates and swap auxiliary observations. Theoretical analysis shows that the expected local estimates converge to the target U-statistic at rates O(1/t) for the synchronous case and O((log t)/t) for the asynchronous case, with constants that depend explicitly on the data and the network’s spectral gap. A key proof device is a “phantom node” construction that decouples averaging and data propagation. Compared to U2-gossip (Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009), the proposed methods use less memory and communication (one auxiliary observation and a single swap per iteration) and achieve faster convergence. Experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter over several network topologies corroborate the theoretical advantages.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on the large literature on gossip-based averaging (e.g., Boyd et al., Kempe et al.) and extends it from separable statistics to pairwise U-statistics, a setting for which prior decentralized methods are scarce. The most closely related approach, U2-gossip, requires two auxiliary observations per node and two swaps per iteration, and achieves a slower rate; the present paper improves on both rate and resource usage and provides an asynchronous version, which is more realistic in practice.

Quality
- Theory: The convergence guarantees are clearly stated and technically sound, with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O((log t)/t) (asynchronous). The dependence on network connectivity via the spectral gap is standard and informative, and the data-dependent terms quantify problem difficulty. The phantom network analysis is original and well motivated.
- Algorithms: The design is simple and practical: single-auxiliary propagation coupled with averaging. The asynchronous variant removes the global clock assumption while preserving convergence in expectation.
- Experiments: The empirical evaluation on two real datasets and multiple network types consistently shows lower error and variance than U2-gossip, aligning with theory.

Clarity
The paper is well written and well organized. The problem is clearly motivated (AUC, variance, Gini, within-cluster scatter), and related work is appropriately covered. Algorithm descriptions are precise, and the analysis is presented with helpful intuition (e.g., spectral gap effects and phantom nodes). The experimental setup and results are easy to follow.

Originality
Extending gossip from separable averages to U-statistics with a one-auxiliary, swap-and-average mechanism is novel, as is the phantom network proof technique. The asynchronous extension with explicit rate guarantees adds further novelty.

Significance
U-statistics underpin many important metrics and test statistics, so enabling their decentralized computation with rigorous guarantees is valuable. The improved rates and lower communication/memory costs over U2-gossip, together with an asynchronous algorithm, make the contribution practical and likely to influence subsequent work on decentralized learning and statistics.

Pros
- Clear, principled algorithms for synchronous and asynchronous settings.
- Explicit, interpretable convergence rates with data and network dependencies.
- Lower memory and communication cost than U2-gossip (one auxiliary, one swap).
- Strong empirical validation across tasks and network topologies.
- Good exposition and positioning within the literature.

Cons
- None major identified.

Overall assessment
This paper presents novel, well-analyzed, and practically relevant algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics. The theoretical advances over prior work and the strong empirical results make it a solid contribution.
