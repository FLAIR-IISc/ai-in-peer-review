- Proposes a novel family of Gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics with provably faster convergence and support for asynchronous settings.
- The algorithms overcome limitations of prior work (notably U2-Gossip) by improving convergence speed and practicality in real-world asynchronous networks.
- Provides strong theoretical guarantees including faster convergence rates and favorable memory/communication cost analyses.
- Empirical validation on two datasets shows consistent superiority of the proposed methods over relevant baselines.
- Paper is very well-written, with clear motivation, literature context, and technical presentation.
- Minor question: clarify generalizability of the analysis and algorithms to U-statistics of arbitrary degree k and required modifications or challenges.
- Minor question: discuss sensitivity of convergence rates to network topology properties (e.g., sparsity, diameter, degree distribution).
- Conclusion: an excellent, timely, and rigorous contribution likely of high interest to the community.
