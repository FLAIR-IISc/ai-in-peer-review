- Paper addresses decentralized computation of U-statistics (pairwise-based measures) via gossip algorithms.
- Proposes GoSta: synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms that swap observations to create a data random walk.
- The swapping mechanism allows each node's estimate to incorporate all observation pairs over time.
- Theoretical convergence rates given: O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).
- Empirical results show GoSta outperforms prior U2-gossip baseline.
- Technical contribution is sound and experiments support theoretical claims.
- Main weakness: insufficient comparison to other natural baselines, especially memory-intensive ones.
- Clarity issue: intuition for the swapping/random-walk mechanism and the "phantom network" should be explained more clearly in the main text.
- Related-work gap: literature review on gossip and consensus (e.g., Dimakis et al. 2010) is too brief and should be strengthened.
- Ambiguity in model: the paper must clarify the assumed distribution for "drawing a random edge" (important for asynchronous model and pk).
- Missing baseline: compare against a flooding/broadcasting approach to clarify trade-offs between memory and convergence, especially on well-connected graphs.
- Theoretical analysis should discuss connection to lifting/ lifted Markov chains and cite related work on lifting to accelerate mixing.
- Theorem assumptions unclear: make explicit assumptions on initial data X and dependence of error bounds on initial values.
- Minor typos and phrasing issues need fixes (several examples provided).
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions to related work, clarifying algorithmic details, adding flooding baseline comparison, and discussing lifting connections.
