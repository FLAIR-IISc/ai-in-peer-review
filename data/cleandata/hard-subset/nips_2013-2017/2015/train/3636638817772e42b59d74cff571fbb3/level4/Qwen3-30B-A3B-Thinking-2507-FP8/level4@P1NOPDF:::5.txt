

This paper introduces synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for estimating U-statistics, achieving convergence rates of 1/t and log t / t, respectively. The technical presentation is clear, with well-structured derivations supporting the theoretical claims. The problem of distributed U-statistic estimation is important for large-scale learning systems, and the proposed methods provide a practical solution with strong theoretical foundations. Experiments confirm the algorithms' performance advantages over existing approaches and demonstrate their accuracy across multiple settings. The work makes a valuable contribution to distributed optimization, offering a novel framework that addresses a significant gap in the literature. While the experimental section could further explore scalability under heterogeneous network conditions, the core theoretical and empirical results are compelling and well-validated. Overall, this paper presents a solid advance in the area, with clear relevance to researchers working on distributed statistical learning.
