- Proposal: Introduces GoSta synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms to estimate U-statistics in distributed networks using data propagation plus averaging steps.  
- Motivation: Addresses the gap that standard gossip algorithms cannot efficiently compute pairwise U-statistics; builds on prior U2-gossip work.  
- Analytical tool: Uses a novel "phantom nodes" formulation to decouple and analyze data propagation and averaging dynamics.  
- Theoretical results: Proves convergence rates O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t/t) for asynchronous GoSta, with explicit dependence on network spectral gap and data properties.  
- Improvement over prior work: The analysis shows GoSta avoids a detrimental √n factor present in U2-gossip’s convergence bound.  
- Novelty and significance: Presents a novel, practically important solution—especially the asynchronous variant—for decentralized U-statistic estimation.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous theoretical analysis and principled comparison to prior methods.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and well-organized presentation.  
- Empirical validation: Convincing experiments on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs showing faster convergence and lower variance than U2-gossip.  
- Weakness — timing: Experiments measure iterations but not wall-clock time or per-iteration cost; run-time comparisons are needed to confirm practical advantages.  
- Weakness — async theory vs practice: Asynchronous GoSta performs comparably to synchronous empirically despite a worse theoretical O(log t/t) rate; authors should discuss whether the log factor is an artifact of analysis.  
- Overall assessment: Strong contribution to distributed ML with rigorous theory and convincing experiments; recommended for acceptance.
