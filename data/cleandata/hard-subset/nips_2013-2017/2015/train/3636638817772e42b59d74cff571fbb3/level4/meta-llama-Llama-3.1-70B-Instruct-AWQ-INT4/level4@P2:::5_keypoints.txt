- Proposes new synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of U-statistics.  
- Targets decentralized U-statistic estimation where existing gossip methods poorly handle pairwise-dependent quantities.  
- Claims convergence rates O(1/t) for GoSta-sync and O(log t / t) for GoSta-async.  
- Provides a thorough and technically sound convergence analysis.  
- Compares to prior methods (e.g., U2-gossip) and demonstrates theoretical/empirical advantages.  
- Numerical experiments on two real-world datasets show better accuracy and convergence than baselines.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents ideas.  
- Assumes relatively simple network structure; unclear performance on more complex networks.  
- Does not give a detailed computational complexity analysis of the algorithms.  
- Some notation/terminology may be hard for readers without graph-theory/decentralized-estimation background.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested additions—provide complexity analysis and evaluate on more complex network structures.
