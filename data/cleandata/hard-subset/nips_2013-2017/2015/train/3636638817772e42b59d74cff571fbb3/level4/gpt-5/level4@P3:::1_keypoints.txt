- Proposes two randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync, GoSta-async) for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics using one auxiliary observation per node that performs a random-walk swap.  
- Nodes update local estimates via running averages of H(Xi, Yi) and perform standard gossip averaging to reduce disagreement.  
- Proves convergence in expectation with rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O((log t)/t) (asynchronous).  
- Convergence bounds include explicit data-dependent terms (local hi difficulty) and network-dependent terms via the spectral gap (1 − λ2).  
- Introduces a “phantom node” construction to decouple data propagation from averaging, yielding clean matrix analyses.  
- Positions work against classical gossip literature and most closely compares to U2-gossip, arguing GoSta needs fewer auxiliaries and edge activations.  
- Provides refined theoretical comparisons showing GoSta’s advantage over U2-gossip and empirical validation on AUC and within-cluster scatter tasks.  
- Theory: convergence results are clearly stated and plausible; spectral-gap dependence is standard and highlighted.  
- Analysis: phantom network argument is elegant and appears sound (detailed in supplement).  
- Algorithms: GoSta-sync requires one auxiliary and one edge activation per iteration; GoSta-async uses local counters mk(t) to keep running averages unbiased.  
- Empirics: experiments on multiple graph topologies show improved accuracy and lower across-node variance compared to U2-gossip.  
- Missing high-probability or mean-square error (MSE) bounds—only expectation convergence is proved.  
- GoSta-async assumes nodes know pk = 2dk/|E| (thus |E|), which may be impractical in decentralized settings.  
- Communication and memory advantages are argued but not quantitatively measured; messages/bytes per node and per-iteration memory are not reported.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized and readable; phantom node construction is a helpful explanatory device.  
- Suggest explicitly stating graph assumptions (connected, non-bipartite) and addressing bipartite cases (e.g., lazy walks).  
- Suggest clarifying degree and |E| requirements for GoSta-async and whether approximations are acceptable.  
- Recommend adding a concise table summarizing per-iteration memory and communication for GoSta-sync, GoSta-async, and U2-gossip.  
- Originality: novel combination of single-auxiliary design, coupled propagation+averaging, and phantom-node analysis; asynchronous variant and refined U2 bounds are meaningful contributions.  
- Significance: advances decentralized estimation of U-statistics with actionable spectral-gap insights; likely to be used/extended in decentralized learning.  
- Pros: principled algorithms, explicit data/network-dependent guarantees, practical asynchronous variant, solid empirical gains, elegant analysis.  
- Cons/questions: lack of MSE/high-probability guarantees; reliance on global |E| in async updates; limited experimental scale; unclear extension to degree >2 U-statistics; robustness to dynamic/directed/lossy networks not addressed.  
- Recommendation: strong accept—important problem, novel efficient algorithms, and clear empirical/theoretical gains, with suggested improvements around stochastic guarantees and communication/memory quantification.
