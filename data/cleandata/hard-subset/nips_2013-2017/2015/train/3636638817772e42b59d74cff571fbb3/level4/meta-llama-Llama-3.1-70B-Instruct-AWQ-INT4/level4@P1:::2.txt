This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms to efficiently compute U-statistics, which are essential in describing global properties of a statistical population. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms and demonstrate their superiority over existing approaches through numerical experiments.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The authors do a great job of introducing the problem, explaining the limitations of existing gossip algorithms, and presenting their new approach. The technical details are clearly explained, and the analysis is thorough.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to balance theoretical rigor with practical considerations. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms, which is essential for understanding their performance. At the same time, they also provide numerical experiments that demonstrate the practical effectiveness of their approach.

The paper also makes a significant contribution to the field of gossip algorithms. The authors' new approach to computing U-statistics is innovative and has the potential to be widely applicable. The fact that their algorithms can be used in both synchronous and asynchronous settings makes them even more versatile.

In terms of related work, the authors do a good job of situating their approach within the broader context of gossip algorithms. However, they could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other approaches, such as the lifting constructions mentioned in the review comments.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of gossip algorithms. With some revisions to address the review comments, it has the potential to be even stronger.
