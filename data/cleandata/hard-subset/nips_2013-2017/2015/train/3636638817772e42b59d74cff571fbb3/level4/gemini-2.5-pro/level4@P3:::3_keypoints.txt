- Problem: Decentralized estimation of U-statistics (averages over pairs) where standard gossip algorithms for separable sums do not apply; prior baseline is U2-gossip.  
- Main contribution: Two randomized gossip algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, combining data propagation (auxiliary-data swaps) and estimate averaging.  
- Analytical novelty: Introduces a "phantom network" proof technique to separate data propagation and averaging in analysis.  
- Convergence guarantees: Proves O(1/t) rate for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with explicit dependence on spectral gap and data-dependent variance.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous theoretical analysis and refined convergence bounds for prior U2-gossip enabling principled comparison.  
- Experimental evaluation: Strong empirical comparison on AUC and within-cluster scatter using real datasets across multiple topologies (complete, grid, Watts-Strogatz).  
- Empirical results: GoSta-sync consistently outperforms U2-gossip in convergence speed and stability (lower node-wise variance).  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear motivation, formal problem statement, and easy-to-follow pseudocode.  
- Originality: Novel algorithmic mechanism (data swapping + explicit averaging) and novel analytical tool (phantom network).  
- Significance: Practical and theoretical relevance to distributed ML and sensor networks; lower per-iteration communication/memory and an important asynchronous variant.  
- Suggested improvement 1: Include an explicit per-iteration cost comparison table (memory per node, communication per iteration, node-pair interactions).  
- Suggested improvement 2: Discuss the gap between asynchronous O(log t / t) and synchronous O(1/t) rates—artifact of proof or fundamental limitation.  
- Acceptance arguments: Addresses an important problem, proposes novel effective algorithms, provides rigorous theory and strong empirical validation, and is clearly written.  
- Minor weakness: Practical per-iteration cost advantages are claimed but not explicitly summarized in a direct comparison.  
- Recommendation: Strongly recommend acceptance.
