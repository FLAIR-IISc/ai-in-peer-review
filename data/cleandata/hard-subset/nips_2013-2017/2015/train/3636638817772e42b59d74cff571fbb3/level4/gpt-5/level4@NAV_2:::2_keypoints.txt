1. Contribution: Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip methods for decentralized pairwise U‑statistics, proving convergence rates O(1/t) and O(log t / t) with data- and network-dependent constants and showing empirical gains over U2-gossip.

2. Overall assessment: Solid contribution to gossip literature but needs tighter connections to existing methods; reviewer did not check supplemental material.

3. Algorithmic mechanism: Recommend making the swap-of-auxiliary-state and resulting coupled random walks (phantom nodes) explicit in the main text to improve readability and motivate the analysis.

4. Positioning: Paper should better situate itself within broader gossip/aggregate literature (e.g., Dimakis survey) to clarify novelty relative to established techniques.

5. Edge-selection distribution: Clarify the exact edge-selection distribution under the "node wakes up and contacts a neighbor" model—“draw a random edge” is ambiguous.

6. Alternative baseline: Compare to a flooding baseline that stores received samples (with more memory per node) to evaluate when averaging is beneficial and whether the 1/t rate is optimal in practice, especially on well-connected graphs.

7. Relation to lifted Markov chains: Discuss connections and differences with lifting literature (e.g., Jung et al. 2007; Li & Dai 2010; Chen, Lovász & Pak 1999) and consider incorporating lifting ideas.

8. Data assumptions and guarantees: Explicitly state data assumptions, whether bounds are uniform worst-case over X or distributional, what randomness is averaged over (edges vs data), and how bounds scale with data-dependent terms.

9. Small edits: Clarify bipartite definition (directed vs undirected) and fix minor wording/grammar issues on p1, p4, p6, p7.

10. Post-response verdict: Authors’ revisions address several concerns; reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 but still asks for lifting literature inclusion and flooding-style experimental comparisons; recommends acceptance with suggested revisions.
