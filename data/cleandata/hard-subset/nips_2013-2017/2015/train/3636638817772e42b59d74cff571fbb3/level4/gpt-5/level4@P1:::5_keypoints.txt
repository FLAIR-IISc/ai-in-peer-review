- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics where nodes maintain and propagate local estimates.
- Derives clear algorithms with proven convergence rates: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous.
- Empirical results demonstrate strong performance and accuracy, outperforming alternative approaches.
- Technical development is sound, with clear derivations and theoretical guarantees that match experiments.
- Paper is well organized and easy to follow, linking algorithm design to convergence analysis and experiments.
- Introduces novel gossip methods specifically for U-statistics, extending beyond averaging separable functions.
- Treats both synchronous and asynchronous settings with valuable, stated convergence analyses.
- Problem is significant for decentralized learning/sensing tasks (AUC, variance, Gini, within-cluster scatter) and useful to researchers.
- Pros: clear algorithms, proven rates, strong empirical results, and well-written presentation.
- Cons: None identified in this review.
