- Presents a decentralized gossip-style algorithm for computing higher-order statistics (variance, correlations) across networked nodes; valuable contribution.  
- Needs stronger connection to existing gossip literature and prior work (e.g., Dimakis et al. Proc. IEEE).  
- Paper structure is good but the algorithm’s mechanics—especially the random-walk behavior of initial observations—require clearer explanation.  
- Clarify the induced distribution on edges from the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” process.  
- Consider and compare an alternative flooding approach where nodes store received observations to evaluate performance under memory constraints.  
- Examine and cite lifting constructions that accelerate gossip (e.g., Jung, Shah, and Shin; Li and Dai) as they appear related.  
- Explicitly state assumptions about the initial data distribution and provide error guarantees as a function of initial data (worst-case vs. average-case).  
- Include baseline comparisons in experiments to demonstrate empirical effectiveness.  
- Make minor corrections: revise bipartite definition, fix grammatical issues, and rephrase unclear sentences.  
- Authors’ revisions were promising and score was raised, but further work on lifting (e.g., LADA) and related work/baselines is still required.  
- Overall recommendation: accept if the raised concerns are addressed and suggested revisions are implemented.
