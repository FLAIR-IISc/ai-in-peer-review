- Introduces novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics, filling a gap in decentralized computation for pairwise-dependent statistics.  
- Focuses on U-statistics (e.g., AUC, Gini mean difference, within-cluster scatter) that existing separable-statistic gossip cannot efficiently compute.  
- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit dependence on data properties and network spectral gap.  
- Advances gossip literature by addressing a previously unexplored problem class and building on foundational work.  
- Improves over U2-gossip in convergence, communication complexity, and asynchrony support while discussing memory trade-offs.  
- Technical quality is strong with rigorous proofs; the "phantom nodes" reformulation is an elegant analytical tool.  
- Notes increased implementation complexity due to maintaining auxiliary observations despite theoretical/empirical gains.  
- Presentation is clear and reproducible: consistent notation, organized sections, and helpful figures.  
- Experiments (SMVguide3, Wine Quality) across complete, 2D grid, and Watts-Strogatz graphs show faster convergence and lower variance than U2-gossip, with gaps growing with network size.  
- Substantial originality with proper citation of related NIPS work and clear distinction from prior approaches.  
- Work is significant for practical distributed ML/statistics applications involving U-statistics on decentralized data.  
- Minor weakness: does not explore integrating adaptive communication schemes (natural future direction).  
- Recommendation: strong accept for NIPS based on technical soundness, originality, significance, and clarity.
