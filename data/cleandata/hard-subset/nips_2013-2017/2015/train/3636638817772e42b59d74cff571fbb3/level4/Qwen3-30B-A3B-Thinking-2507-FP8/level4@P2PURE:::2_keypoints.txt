1. Paper proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm for aggregating U-statistics (variance, correlation, scatter) across networked nodes.  
2. Reviewer finds the extension to gossip literature valuable but requests stronger connections to existing approaches and ideas.  
3. Clarify in the main text the key mechanism: nodes swap a secondary state (initial observation) causing coupled random walks and motivating the multiple-graph-copy analysis.  
4. Strengthen related work discussion, especially citing Dimakis et al. (Proc. IEEE) as a more relevant context.  
5. Clarify the induced edge-selection distribution in the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process—“drawing a random edge” is ambiguous.  
6. Compare the proposed method to a local-memory flooding/tagging baseline (flood initial values, compute U-statistics locally) and assess whether the 1/t convergence rate is advantageous (e.g., on expanders).  
7. Examine lifting constructions and accelerated gossip methods (e.g., LADA); cite Jung–Shah–Shin 2007, Li–Dai 2010, Chen–Lovász–Pak 1999.  
8. Explicitly state data-distribution assumptions: clarify whether analysis assumes worst-case initial data vectors X and make error guarantees a function of initial data.  
9. Experiments must include baseline comparisons to demonstrate empirical benefits.  
10. Minor wording/typo corrections for several lines on pp.1,4,6,7 as listed by the reviewer.  
11. Reviewer increased score after author response (7 → 8) and recommends acceptance conditional on addressing the above clarifications and related-work/experiment refinements.
