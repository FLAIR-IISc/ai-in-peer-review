This paper proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for the decentralized estimation of U-statistics. The authors provide a rigorous theoretical analysis, deriving convergence rates of 1/t for the synchronous algorithm and log(t)/t for its asynchronous counterpart.

The paper is well-organized, and its contributions are presented with clarity. The problem of estimating U-statistics in a distributed setting is highly relevant, making the proposed techniques a valuable contribution to the field. These algorithms will likely be of considerable interest to researchers working on large-scale, decentralized machine learning.

The theoretical findings are substantiated by a solid set of experiments. These results clearly demonstrate the performance advantages and accuracy of the proposed methods when compared to existing approaches. In summary, this work introduces effective gossip algorithms for a significant problem, supported by both strong theoretical guarantees and compelling empirical validation. The favorable convergence rates are a notable achievement.
