1. Proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute U-statistics (higher-order stats) across networked nodes — a valuable contribution.

2. Needs a clearer connection to broader related work; cite and situate against state-of-the-art surveys (e.g., Dimakis et al. Proc. IEEE).

3. Key algorithmic innovation is exchanging both estimates and initial observations, inducing a (coupled) random walk on observations — this should be highlighted to motivate the analysis.

4. Clarify the exact edge-selection distribution in the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” model; “drawing a random edge” is ambiguous.

5. Compare to an alternative flooding approach where nodes store tagged observations — analyze memory vs averaging trade-offs and whether averaging improves performance (e.g., convergence rate 1/t, behavior on expanders).

6. Investigate and cite lifting/acceleration techniques for gossip (e.g., LADA and lifted Markov chain papers) as they are directly relevant.

7. Explicitly state assumptions on the initial data distribution (worst-case vs stochastic) and provide error guarantees as a function of the initial data vector X.

8. Experimental evaluation should include baseline comparisons to demonstrate empirical advantages.

9. Minor editorial fixes recommended (bipartite definition, wording and typos on p4, p6, p7).

10. After author revisions, reviewer raised score from 7 to 8 and conditionally recommends acceptance if the above concerns (especially related work and experiments) are addressed.
