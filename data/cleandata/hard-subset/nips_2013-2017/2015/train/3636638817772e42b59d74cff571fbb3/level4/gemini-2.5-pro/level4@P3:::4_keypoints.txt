- Summary: Proposes GoSta, synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-two U‑statistics using data propagation (auxiliary swap) and local averaging, with convergence rates O(1/t) and O(log t/t) via a "phantom network" analysis.

- Strength 1: Tackles a significant, well-motivated problem (decentralized U‑statistics) and is clearly written and positioned.

- Strength 2: Provides rigorous, novel theoretical analysis using the phantom-network tool, yielding bounds that expose dependence on network spectral gap and data terms.

- Strength 3: Algorithmically more efficient than prior U2‑gossip—uses less memory (one auxiliary vs two) and less communication (one edge activation), and includes a practical asynchronous variant.

- Weakness 1: Experimental evaluation is limited—compares only to U2‑gossip and should include simple baselines (e.g., two‑phase gossip then local computation, or random-walk data collection) to better assess practical gains.

- Weakness 2: Algorithmic primitives (pairwise averaging, data swapping/random walks) are standard; novelty lies mainly in their combination and analysis rather than a fundamentally new mechanism.

- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem, offers strong theoretical guarantees improving prior work, and presents resource-efficient synchronous and asynchronous algorithms.

- Against acceptance: Empirical validation is weak due to lack of broader baseline comparisons, limiting assessment of practical impact.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance, with a request to discuss and (if possible) add alternative baseline experiments in the final version.
