The paper is well-motivated and clearly written, effectively conveying the authors' approach and results. The performance of their method is evaluated on two datasets, with the average relative AUC error serving as the primary metric. A comparison with the baseline method, U2-gossip, demonstrates the proposed approach's superior performance across diverse scenarios, including various graph structures.

The authors provide convergence bounds for their method, adding theoretical depth to the paper. However, to fully assess the practicality of the proposed approach, it would be beneficial to include runtime analyses for both the proposed method and U2-gossip. Specifically, reporting the runtime per iteration for each method would help determine whether the proposed method's potential performance gains are offset by increased computational costs. If each iteration of the proposed method is significantly slower than U2-gossip, the utility of the paper may be limited.

The paper makes a valuable contribution by proposing both synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics.
