The paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics, which are essential in many distributed systems. The authors provide a clear motivation for their work, highlighting the limitations of existing gossip algorithms in efficiently computing U-statistics. The paper is well-written, and the results are presented on two datasets, evaluating the average relative AUC error.

The authors compare their method to the baseline U2-gossip algorithm and demonstrate its superiority in various scenarios, including different graph structures. The paper also provides convergence bounds for the proposed method, which is a significant contribution to the field.

However, there are some aspects that need to be addressed to further improve the paper. Firstly, it would be beneficial to include run-times per iteration for both the proposed method and U2-gossip. This information is crucial in assessing the practical utility of the proposed method, as it may be the case that each iteration of the proposed method is significantly slower than U2-gossip, which would limit its applicability.

Additionally, the authors could provide more details on the computational complexity of their method, including the memory and communication costs. This information would help readers better understand the trade-offs between the proposed method and existing algorithms.

Furthermore, it would be interesting to see a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method on various datasets and graph structures. This would provide a more robust understanding of the method's performance and its limitations.

In terms of related work, the authors could provide a more detailed discussion of existing gossip algorithms and their limitations in computing U-statistics. This would help readers better understand the significance of the proposed method and its contributions to the field.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the results are promising. However, addressing the above-mentioned aspects would strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to computing U-statistics in distributed systems.
* The authors provide convergence bounds for their method, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The results are presented on two datasets, evaluating the average relative AUC error.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear motivation for their work.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks information on run-times per iteration for both the proposed method and U2-gossip.
* The computational complexity of the proposed method, including memory and communication costs, is not thoroughly discussed.
