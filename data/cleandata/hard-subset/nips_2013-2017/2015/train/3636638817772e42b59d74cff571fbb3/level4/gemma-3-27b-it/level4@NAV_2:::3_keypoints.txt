- Addresses decentralized estimation of U-statistics, an important and under-explored problem with many applications.  
- Proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for U-statistics.  
- Establishes convergence rate bounds and shows improvements over prior work (U2-gossip).  
- Theoretical analysis connects performance to network structure via the spectral gap.  
- Uses a “phantom node” reformulation in convergence analysis, offering novel insights.  
- Provides explicit data and network dependency in the convergence bounds.  
- Experimental results on two datasets show superior performance to U2-gossip across multiple network topologies.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and links to real-world tasks (e.g., AUC, within-cluster scatter).  
- Related work section is comprehensive and situates the contribution within gossip literature.  
- Inclusion of both synchronous and asynchronous variants increases applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed per-iteration computational/runtime complexity analysis comparing to U2-gossip.  
- Weakness: extensions to multiple observations per node are mentioned but need more practical elaboration and overhead analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, faster convergence, rigorous theory, strong empirical validation, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing runtime-per-iteration analysis and insufficient detail on multi-observation extensions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance pending minor additions on computational complexity.
