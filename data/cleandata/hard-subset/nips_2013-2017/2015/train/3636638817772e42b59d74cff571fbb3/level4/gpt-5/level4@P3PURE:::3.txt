The paper is well motivated and clearly written. It proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. Empirical results on two datasets, evaluated using average relative AUC error, show that the proposed method performs much better than the U2-gossip baseline across varied graph scenarios. The paper also provides convergence bounds for the proposed method.

I would like to see per-iteration run-times for both the proposed methods and U2-gossip. If each iteration of the proposed method is much slower than U2-gossip, the practical utility of the approach would be limited.
