- Contribution: proposes a gossip-based decentralized method to compute pairwise higher-order U-statistics (variance, correlation, scatter coefficients); solid contribution but needs clearer positioning in prior work.  
- Core algorithmic mechanism: nodes not only average estimates but swap a secondary state with initial observations, causing coupled random walks — this should be stated explicitly.  
- Literature coverage: existing gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. Proc. IEEE survey) and other relevant works are under-cited; related work should be expanded.  
- Edge/contact model: clarify the edge distribution induced by the “node wakes up, contacts neighbor” process — “drawing a random edge” is ambiguous.  
- Alternative baseline: compare to a flooding approach where nodes store tagged observations (more memory) to show when averaging helps and whether the 1/t rate is competitive, especially on expander graphs.  
- Lifting connections: the method appears related to lifting constructions for faster gossip (cite Jung et al. 2007; Li & Dai 2010; Chen et al. 1999) and should be discussed.  
- Theoretical assumptions: unclear whether error guarantees are worst-case over initial data; explicitly state assumptions on data distribution and error bounds as a function of initial data.  
- Experiments: include baseline comparisons in experiments to demonstrate empirical benefits.  
- Small edits: several minor wording and definition fixes (bipartite definition implies directed edges; multiple phrasing/typo fixes).  
- Supplemental material: reviewer did not verify supplements due to time; correctness not independently checked.  
- After author feedback: authors’ clarifications improved the paper and reviewer raised score from 7 to 8, but lifting-related discussion and experimental baselines still required.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on strong revision addressing prior-work connections, theoretical assumptions, lifting constructions, and including baseline experiments.
