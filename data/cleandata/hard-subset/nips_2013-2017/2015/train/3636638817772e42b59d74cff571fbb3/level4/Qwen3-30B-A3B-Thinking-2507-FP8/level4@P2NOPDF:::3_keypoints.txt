- Well-motivated, clearly written study on randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics.  
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous variants with significant performance gains over U2-Gossip.  
- Empirical evaluation on two datasets using average relative AUC error; proposed method consistently outperforms baseline across network scenarios.  
- Inclusion of convergence bounds and sound theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Experimental design validates robustness across diverse graph topologies.  
- Major omission: no runtime analysis or per-iteration computational time reported.  
- Concern that higher per-iteration cost could negate claimed scalability and practical utility.  
- For deployment, computational efficiency (wall-clock per-iteration times under identical hardware) is as important as accuracy.  
- Authors should add per-iteration timing comparisons between their method and U2-Gossip.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and empirical contribution, but practical viability is unclear without runtime measurements.
