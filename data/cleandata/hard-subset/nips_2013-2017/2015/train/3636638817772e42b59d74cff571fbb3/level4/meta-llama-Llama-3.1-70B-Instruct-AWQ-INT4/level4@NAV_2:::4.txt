This paper proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms, coined GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, to efficiently compute U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the convergence rates of both algorithms, demonstrating their superiority over existing approaches.

The paper relates to previous work in gossip algorithms, particularly in the context of decentralized averaging in networks. The authors acknowledge the limitations of existing algorithms in computing U-statistics, which rely on averaging over pairs of observations. They build upon the work of [17], which introduced the U2-gossip algorithm, but highlight its drawbacks, including higher memory and communication costs, and the requirement of a global clock.

The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, are designed to address these limitations. GoSta-sync achieves a convergence rate of O(1/t), while GoSta-async achieves a rate of O(log t/t). The authors provide a detailed analysis of the convergence rates, including the derivation of the transition matrix and the expected state evolution. They also compare their algorithms to U2-gossip, demonstrating their superiority in terms of convergence rate and memory and communication costs.

The paper includes experimental results on two applications: decentralized estimation of the Area Under the ROC Curve (AUC) and within-cluster point scatter. The authors compare the performance of GoSta-sync and U2-gossip on three types of networks: complete graph, two-dimensional grid, and Watts-Strogatz. The results show that GoSta-sync outperforms U2-gossip in terms of average relative error and variance.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes novel algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting.
* The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the convergence rates of both algorithms.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing approaches.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with baseline measures, as suggested in the original review.
* The authors may consider including additional experiments to further demonstrate the effectiveness of their algorithms.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes novel and efficient algorithms for computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting.
* The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the convergence rates of both algorithms.
* The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms over existing approaches.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper may lack clear novelty, as the algorithms themselves are quite intuitive.
* The evaluation is weak, and additional experiments may be necessary to further demonstrate the effectiveness of the algorithms.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of decentralized computation and U-statistics. While it may benefit from additional experiments and comparisons with baseline measures, the proposed algorithms and their analysis are well-presented and demonstrate a clear advantage over existing approaches.
