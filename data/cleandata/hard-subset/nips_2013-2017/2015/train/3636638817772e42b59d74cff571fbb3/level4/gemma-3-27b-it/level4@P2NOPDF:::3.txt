This paper presents synchronized and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for the computation of U-statistics. The motivation is clear and the paper is well-written. The authors evaluate their approach on two datasets, reporting results in terms of average relative AUC error. Their method demonstrates a significant performance improvement over the U2-gossip baseline across a variety of graph structures. A key strength of the work is the inclusion of convergence bounds, providing theoretical guarantees for the proposed algorithms.

However, a crucial aspect missing from the evaluation is a detailed analysis of computational cost. While the paper demonstrates superior accuracy, it fails to report per-iteration runtimes for both the proposed method and the U2-gossip baseline. This information is critical for assessing the practical utility of the contribution. If each iteration of the proposed algorithm is substantially slower than U2-gossip, the gains in accuracy may be offset by increased computational expense, potentially limiting the real-world applicability of the method. Therefore, including these runtime comparisons is essential. 

Specifically, understanding the scalability of the proposed algorithms is paramount. Knowing the runtime per iteration, especially as the graph size increases, will allow the community to properly assess the trade-offs between accuracy and efficiency. Without this information, it is difficult to judge whether the improved performance justifies the potential increase in computational burden.
