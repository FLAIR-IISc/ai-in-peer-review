- Extends gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics in distributed networks, addressing a gap beyond averaging.  
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms that maintain local U-statistic estimates while propagating data.  
- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) with explicit dependence on data and network properties.  
- Highlights significance because U-statistics cover important measures (AUC, empirical variance, Gini mean difference, within-cluster scatter).  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, with convergence bounds reflecting data- and network-dependent factors.  
- Experimental results on real datasets (AUC with SVMguide3, within-cluster scatter on Wine Quality) show superiority over U2-gossip in convergence speed and communication efficiency.  
- Needs stronger connection to broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. overview) to better situate the contribution.  
- Should address lifting constructions and related work (LADA, lifted Markov chains) relevant to the phantom network approach.  
- Clarify the distribution over edges when stating “drawing a random edge of the network” (specify probabilities).  
- Better explain the core insight that initial observations perform coupled random walks, motivating the phantom network analysis.  
- Provide a more detailed comparison with flooding (store/propagate all observations) to justify advantages under memory limits.  
- Explicitly state assumptions on the data distribution in the convergence analysis (current presentation gives worst-case guarantees but lacks clarity).  
- Fix minor grammatical issues and wording glitches.  
- Overall well-written, convincing experiments, technically strong; recommend acceptance after addressing the minor concerns and related-work connections.
