- Problem: Computes degree-two U-statistics in decentralized networks, which standard gossip algorithms cannot handle efficiently.
- Methods: Proposes synchronous (GoSta-sync) and asynchronous (GoSta-async) gossip algorithms combining auxiliary-data swapping and local estimate averaging.
- Algorithm detail: Each node keeps a local U-statistic estimate and an auxiliary observation; steps alternate between swapping observations and averaging estimates.
- Theory: Proves convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous), with bounds depending on network spectral gap and data variance.
- Proof technique: Introduces a "phantom network" construction that decouples data propagation from estimate averaging for elegant analysis.
- Experiments: Empirical results on real datasets (AUC and within-cluster scatter) show GoSta methods outperform prior U2-gossip.
- Strength — significance: Addresses an important, practical extension from simple averages to U-statistics with wide ML and sensor-network applicability.
- Strength — theory: Provides rigorous, insightful convergence analysis and improved rates over prior work.
- Strength — empirical/state-of-art: Demonstrates clear improvements in memory, communication, and convergence relative to U2-gossip.
- Strength — practicality: Offers both synchronous and asynchronous variants suitable for real deployments without global synchronization.
- Weakness — novelty: Core algorithmic mechanism (swap + average) is an intuitive combination of known gossip primitives and may lack deep novelty.
- Weakness — experiments: Evaluation is limited to comparison with a single prior algorithm and lacks simple baseline methods (e.g., random walks or flooding) for broader assessment.
- Overall: Solid, technically rigorous contribution with clear gains over prior work, tempered by modest algorithmic novelty and limited experimental baselines.
