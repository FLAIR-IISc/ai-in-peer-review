This paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The work is well-motivated and clearly written, and includes theoretical convergence bounds for the proposed methods.

The experimental evaluation, conducted on two datasets using the average relative AUC error, shows that the proposed algorithm significantly outperforms the U2-gossip baseline across various graph structures. However, the evaluation would be strengthened by a comparison of the run-time per iteration for both the proposed method and the U2-gossip baseline. This information is critical, as the practical utility of the proposed algorithm would be limited if its per-iteration computational cost is significantly higher than that of the baseline, regardless of its convergence advantages.
