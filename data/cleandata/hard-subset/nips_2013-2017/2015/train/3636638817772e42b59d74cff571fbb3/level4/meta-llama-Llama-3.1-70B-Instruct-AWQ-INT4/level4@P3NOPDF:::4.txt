This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in a distributed environment. The proposed algorithm is intuitive, but its novelty may be limited. However, the proofs provided are comprehensive and well-established.

One major weakness of the paper is the lack of comparison with baseline measures. The authors could have generated several intuitive baselines based on randomized algorithms and included them in the experimental evaluation. This omission makes the evaluation of the proposed algorithm weaker than it could have been. While the paper tackles an interesting problem, the absence of baseline comparisons and the overall evaluation framework detracts from its impact.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should consider including comparisons with baseline measures, such as simple randomized algorithms or existing methods for computing average statistics in distributed environments. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed algorithm's performance and help establish its novelty and significance.
