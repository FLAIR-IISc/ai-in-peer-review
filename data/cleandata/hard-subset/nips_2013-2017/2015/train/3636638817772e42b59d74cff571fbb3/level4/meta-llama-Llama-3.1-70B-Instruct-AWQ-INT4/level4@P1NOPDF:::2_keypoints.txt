- Proposes a gossip-style decentralized algorithm to compute pairwise U-statistics (variance, correlation, scatter coefficients) across a network.  
- Main algorithmic insight: nodes swap a secondary state (initial observation) during averaging, causing initial observations to perform coupled random walks — highlight this more in the manuscript.  
- Strengthen connection to existing gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al.) and provide a more comprehensive related-work review.  
- Clarify the exact edge-selection distribution induced by the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process instead of saying "drawing a random edge."  
- Compare to an alternative flooding/memory approach (nodes store received tagged observations) to assess trade-offs and whether the observed 1/t rate is satisfactory.  
- Investigate and cite lifting constructions (e.g., LADA, lifted Markov chains) as they appear closely related and may accelerate mixing.  
- Explicitly state assumptions and guarantees regarding the initial data distribution and error bounds over all possible initial data vectors X.  
- Minor language and notation corrections (several typos and a directed/undirected bipartite-graph definition issue).
