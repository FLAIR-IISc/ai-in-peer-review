- Tackles decentralized estimation of degree-two U-statistics over networks using gossip-style communication, addressing non-separability of pairwise averages.
- Proposes GoSta-sync: synchronous randomized gossip with a running-average update using a local auxiliary sample that diffuses via random edge swaps and pairwise consensus.
- Proposes GoSta-async: fully asynchronous variant where only the selected pair updates and nodes correct for non-uniform activations via normalization by pk.
- Introduces a “phantom network” construction to decouple data propagation from estimate averaging, enabling block-matrix recurrence analysis.
- Proves convergence in expectation: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with constants depending on data dispersion and the graph spectral gap.
- Improves on U2-gossip in rate constants and communication/memory costs, removing a √n disadvantage from the prior method.
- Experiments (AUC and within-cluster scatter) across multiple topologies show faster convergence than U2-gossip, measured by relative error vs iterations.
- Algorithms are simple and implementable: single auxiliary sample per node, swapping along edges, and local running averages.
- Analysis is technically sound, clearly identifying roles of data-dependent dispersion and network connectivity; phantom-node proof technique is original and useful.
- Convergence results are in expectation only; high-probability or variance bounds are not provided.
- Empirical evaluation lacks explicit message complexity, wall-clock runtime under asynchrony, sensitivity to spectral gap, larger graphs, and higher dimensions.
- Notation can be heavy (block matrices, Wα(G)); intuition behind constants and Laplacian dependence should be summarized in the main text.
- Phantom-network role should be emphasized earlier to avoid impression of quadratic storage costs.
- Asynchronous algorithm assumes nodes know pk (degrees and |E|); realism of this assumption and alternatives should be discussed.
- Originality: novel combination of data propagation and estimate averaging with one auxiliary sample per node and phantom-node proofs extends beyond prior U2-gossip.
- Significance: enables decentralized estimation of many important pairwise statistics (AUC, variance, Gini, scatter) with improved communication/memory and convergence.
- Pros condensed: clear problem formulation, practical low-overhead algorithms, solid theoretical analysis with interpretable constants, meaningful comparison to U2-gossip, synchronous and asynchronous variants.
- Cons condensed: only expectation guarantees, limited empirical metrics and scale, strong asynchronous assumptions (knowledge of pk), limited discussion on higher-order U-statistics.
- Suggested improvements: provide concentration/high-probability bounds, report communication/memory and time-to-accuracy, test robustness to approximate pk and time-varying/directed graphs, add more baselines and discuss extensions to k>2 and non-symmetric kernels.
- Recommendation: solid, well-motivated contribution meeting NIPS criteria; lean toward acceptance conditional on strengthened experimental reporting and clarification of asynchronous assumptions.
