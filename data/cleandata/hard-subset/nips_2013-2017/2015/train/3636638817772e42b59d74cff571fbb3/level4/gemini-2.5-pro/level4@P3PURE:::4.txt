This paper addresses the problem of computing average statistics in a distributed environment. The proposed algorithm is intuitive, which may limit its novelty. However, the theoretical proofs that support it are comprehensive.

A significant weakness is the paper's lack of comparison to any baseline methods. It seems that several intuitive baselines could be derived from randomized algorithms, and their inclusion would be necessary for a thorough experimental evaluation.

In summary, while the paper addresses an interesting problem, the evaluation is weak due to the complete absence of baseline comparisons. This makes it difficult to assess the practical advantages of the proposed method.
