- Proposes new gossip algorithms for distributed U-statistics with faster convergence and lower memory/communication than U2-Gossip.  
- Algorithms are designed for realistic asynchronous environments and supported by theoretical guarantees.  
- Empirical evaluations on two datasets show consistent outperformance of existing baselines.  

- Clear problem motivation and well-positioned, readable presentation.  
- Novel algorithmic contributions addressing both synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Strong theoretical analysis with convergence guarantees and comparison to prior work.  
- Practical relevance due to support for asynchronous operation.  
- Empirical validation demonstrating faster convergence and lower resource use.  

- Asynchrony assumptions are not precisely stated (bounded vs. unbounded delays, drops, out-of-order messages, atomicity, clock drift, fairness).  
- Network model needs clarification (undirected vs. directed graphs, time-varying topologies, dependence on spectral gap/mixing time).  
- Non-asymptotic rates and constant factors (dependence on node count, U-statistic order, kernel properties) are not presented side-by-side with prior work.  
- Resource accounting is incomplete: per-node memory and per-iteration communication costs should be explicitly reported and tabulated.  
- Scope of supported U-statistics (order, kernel assumptions, degeneracy) and extension to higher-order statistics are unclear.  
- Robustness to heterogeneous delays, packet loss, and node failures is not evaluated or fully discussed.  
- Experimental coverage is limited: need more topologies, sizes, and variance reporting (confidence intervals).  
- Baseline tuning and fairness of comparisons (hyperparameter protocols, matched budgets, wall-clock/time/message parity) need clarification.  
- Deployment assumptions unclear: whether global knowledge (node count, degrees, spectral gap) is required or purely local operation suffices.  

- What exact asynchrony model is assumed in analysis and experiments (bounds, drops, ordering)?  
- Do convergence rates depend explicitly on graph spectral properties, and what is that dependence?  
- What are per-node memory and per-iteration communication costs (big-O and practical units)?  
- Do algorithms require knowledge of total node count or other global parameters?  
- Can the methods handle directed or time-varying graphs, and what analysis changes are needed?  
- Are estimators unbiased at every iteration or only asymptotically; how does bias-variance evolve?  
- How do methods scale with U-statistic order; are there bottlenecks for order > 2?  

- Use consistent terminology and provide the full citation for U2-Gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009).  
- Add high-level pseudocode to improve readability and reproducibility.  
- Include a primer or appendix on U-statistics and target applications for broader accessibility.  

- Overall: strong, timely contribution with solid theory and empirical gains; leaning toward acceptance pending clearer asynchrony/network assumptions, detailed resource accounting, and broader experiments.
