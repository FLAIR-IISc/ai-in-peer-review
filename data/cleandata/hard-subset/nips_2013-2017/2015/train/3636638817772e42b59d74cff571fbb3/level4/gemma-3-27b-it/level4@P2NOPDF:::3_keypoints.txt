- Presents synchronized and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics; motivation is clear and paper is well-written.
- Experiments on two datasets report average relative AUC error and show substantial improvement over the U2-gossip baseline across various graph structures.
- Strength: provides convergence bounds and theoretical guarantees for the proposed algorithms.
- Major omission: no analysis of computational cost—per-iteration runtimes for the proposed method and U2-gossip are not reported.
- Concern: if the proposed algorithm’s iterations are substantially slower, accuracy gains may be negated by higher computational expense.
- Recommendation: include runtime comparisons to properly assess practical utility and trade-offs between accuracy and efficiency.
- Scalability issue: need per-iteration runtimes as graph size increases to judge whether improved performance justifies any added computational burden.
