- Introduces new gossip algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics.  
- Achieves faster convergence than U2-gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009).  
- Explicitly handles a fully asynchronous, more practical setting.  
- Improves resource requirements (memory and communication) relative to U2-gossip.  
- Algorithms appear novel and improve on prior work in convergence and resources.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and literature is appropriately positioned.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees and analysis for the proposed methods.  
- Presents extensive empirical evaluation on two datasets with comparisons to baselines, supporting claimed advantages.  
- Overall, a strong contribution combining improved algorithms, practical asynchronous support, theory, and experiments.
