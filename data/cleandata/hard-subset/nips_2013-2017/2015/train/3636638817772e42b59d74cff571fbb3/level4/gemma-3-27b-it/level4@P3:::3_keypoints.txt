- Presents new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics, addressing a gap in existing gossip methods.
- Motivation is clear: U-statistics are important for global properties (e.g., variance, AUC) and existing algorithms struggle to compute them efficiently.
- Core technical idea: use “phantom nodes” to reformulate the problem and enable convergence analysis.
- Theoretical contribution: convergence rates of O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).
- Empirical validation: experiments on SMVguide3 and Wine Quality datasets show improvements over U2-gossip.
- Evaluation covers multiple network topologies (complete graph, 2D grid, Watts-Strogatz), demonstrating robustness.
- Paper includes both synchronous and asynchronous variants, increasing practical applicability.
- Presentation is clear and well-written; technical details are accessible.
- Major weakness: no detailed runtime (per-iteration computational cost) comparison against U2-gossip.
- Practical benefit unclear if proposed iterations are significantly more expensive than U2-gossip’s.
- Theoretical bounds include data-dependent terms, but the connection to specific U-statistic characteristics (e.g., range of H(X,X')) is underexplored.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, strong theory, clear empirical gains, and novel methodology.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing runtime analysis and insufficient insight into data-dependent convergence terms.
- Overall recommendation: accept, with a request that the authors add per-iteration runtime comparisons and better relate data-dependent terms to problem hardness.
