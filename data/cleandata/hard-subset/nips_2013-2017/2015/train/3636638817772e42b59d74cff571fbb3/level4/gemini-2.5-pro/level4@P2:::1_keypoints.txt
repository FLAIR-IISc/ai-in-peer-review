- Introduces GoSta, a new family of randomized gossip algorithms for distributed estimation of U-statistics (averages over pairs), addressing an important under-explored problem with applications like variance and AUC.

- Method combines a data-propagation step (auxiliary observations swap/random-walk) and an averaging step; provides synchronous and asynchronous variants.

- Theoretical contribution: convergence rates of O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous) proven via a novel "phantom network" reformulation, improving on prior O(1/√t) results.

- Analysis links performance to data-dependent factors (kernel variance) and network topology (spectral gap), and refines theoretical comparison to U2-gossip.

- Empirical validation on real-world datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) supports theoretical claims.

- Strength — Significance & originality: extends gossip framework to U-statistics in a novel, non-trivial way with clear improvements over prior methods.

- Strength — Technical quality: rigorous, insightful analysis with explicit rates and an elegant phantom-network proof technique.

- Strength — Practical relevance: presents a fully asynchronous algorithm and demonstrates lower per-iteration memory and communication costs.

- Strength — Clarity & organization: well-written, clearly motivated, and carefully positioned within related work.

- Weakness — Async requires global info: Algorithm 2 assumes nodes know |E| (total edges), which may need an extra consensus step and should be explicitly discussed.

- Weakness — Log factor gap: the O(log t / t) bound is worse than synchronous O(1/t); authors should discuss whether the log factor is proof-artifact or fundamental.

- Weakness — Experimental scope: could test more diverse network topologies (e.g., bottlenecks, communities) to further probe spectral-gap effects.

- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant work with minor issues; strongly recommended for acceptance.
