- Problem: decentralized computation of U-statistics (pairwise-averaged statistics) over networked nodes.
- Proposal: novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms that combine data propagation with local estimate averaging.
- Key mechanism: nodes swap a secondary state (initial observation) so observations perform coupled random walks across the network.
- Analysis tool: reformulation using “phantom nodes” to study convergence behavior.
- Theoretical results: convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous), with dependence on data and network topology.
- Empirics: experiments show improved performance over a previously introduced method.
- Literature gap: strengthen connection to broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al.) to better position the work.
- Clarification needed: specify the distribution for edge selection in the asynchronous setting (how a “random edge” is drawn).
- Baseline comparison: evaluate alternative approaches (e.g., nodes storing/flooding all received observations) to justify the advantage of the averaging strategy and the 1/t rate, especially on well-connected graphs.
- Related methods: investigate links to lifting constructions for accelerated gossip (Jung et al., Li & Dai, Chen et al.) given the use of phantom nodes.
- Assumptions: explicitly state data-distribution assumptions and provide corresponding error guarantees instead of relying on a worst-case implicit assumption.
- Experimental request: include more comprehensive baseline comparisons in evaluations.
- Minor edits: fix bipartite graph definition (currently implies directed edges) and wording corrections (“we stress that”, “awakes and contacts”, “requires initiating”, “allows us to create”).
- Recommendation: reviewer supports acceptance after revisions but urges addressing lifting literature and adding stronger baseline comparisons.
