- Well-motivated, clearly written study of randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics.  
- Proposes both synchronous and asynchronous algorithm variants.  
- Empirically outperforms the U2-gossip baseline across diverse graph topologies on two datasets (measured by average relative AUC error).  
- Theoretical contribution: provides convergence bounds, strengthening the technical foundation.  
- Major missing piece: no per-iteration runtime comparisons to U2-gossip; slower iterations could harm practical utility, so runtime metrics are essential.
