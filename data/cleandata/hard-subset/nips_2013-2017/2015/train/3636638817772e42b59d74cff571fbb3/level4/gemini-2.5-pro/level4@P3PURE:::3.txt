This paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The paper is well-motivated and clearly written, and provides theoretical convergence bounds for the proposed methods. The empirical results, presented on two datasets and evaluated using average relative AUC error, demonstrate that the proposed approach significantly outperforms the U2-gossip baseline across varied graph structures.

The primary weakness of the evaluation is the absence of a comparison of the run-times per iteration. While the proposed method shows superior convergence in terms of iterations, its practical utility is unclear if each iteration is significantly more computationally expensive than that of the U2-gossip baseline. The inclusion of per-iteration timing comparisons would be necessary to substantiate the method's overall efficiency and would substantially strengthen the paper. Without this information, the paper's contribution is of limited utility.
