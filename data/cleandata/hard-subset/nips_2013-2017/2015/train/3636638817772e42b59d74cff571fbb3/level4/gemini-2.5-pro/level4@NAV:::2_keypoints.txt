- Paper proposes a gossip-based decentralized averaging algorithm to compute higher-order U-statistics (variance, correlation, scatter, etc.) over networks.
- Supplementary material correctness not verified due to time constraints.
- Clarify that nodes swap a secondary state (original observation), causing observations to perform coupled random walks; explain this dynamic in main text.
- Position the work more clearly within broader gossip literature (e.g., Dimakis et al. survey) and expand related work.
- Specify the exact induced edge probability distribution for the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" process instead of saying “drawing a random edge.”
- Compare to a flooding-based baseline (nodes store/broadcast initial values) to quantify trade-offs and put the reported 1/t convergence rate in context.
- Discuss connections to lifting constructions (e.g., LADA, lifted Markov chains) and cite relevant accelerating-gossip literature.
- State clearly whether performance guarantees are worst-case over all initial data vectors X, and detail assumptions on data distributions and how error depends on initial data.
- Minor editorial/notation fixes: bipartite definition implying directed graph, and several phrasing/typo corrections on pp.1,4,6,7.
- After author feedback the reviewer raised score from 7 to 8, but still asks for a thorough review of lifting constructions and related-work revision.
- Experimental evaluation should be updated to include the suggested baseline comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: paper is a valuable, well-written contribution but requires revisions addressing prior-work linkage, clarity of formulation, and baseline experiments for acceptance.
