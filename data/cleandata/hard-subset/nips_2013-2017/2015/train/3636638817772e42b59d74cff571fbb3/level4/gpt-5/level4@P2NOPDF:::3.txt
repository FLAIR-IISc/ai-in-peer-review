The paper is well motivated and clearly written. It introduces synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics and provides convergence bounds for both.

Empirically, the method is evaluated on two datasets using average relative AUC error and shows substantial gains over the U2-gossip baseline across a variety of graph topologies. These results suggest strong performance in diverse scenarios.

A key missing piece is runtime. Please report per-iteration wall-clock times (and, if possible, per-epoch or to a fixed accuracy) for both the proposed algorithms and U2-gossip. If each iteration of the proposed methods is significantly slower than U2-gossip, the practical impact could be limited, even if the iteration complexity or error metrics look favorable.

Overall, the combination of clear motivation, theoretical guarantees, and improved empirical accuracy is compelling. Including detailed runtime and computational/communication cost comparisons would substantially strengthen the paper’s practical relevance.
