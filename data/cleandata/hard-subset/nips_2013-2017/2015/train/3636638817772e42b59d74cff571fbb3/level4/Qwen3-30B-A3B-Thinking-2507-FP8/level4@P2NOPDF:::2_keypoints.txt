- Paper proposes a gossip-based decentralized algorithm for higher-order statistics over pairs of observations, extending gossip literature.  
- Core mechanism: nodes swap a secondary state tracking initial observations, creating coupled random walks—this should be explicitly framed in the main text.  
- Multiple graph copies motivation and analysis should be clarified by highlighting the state-swapping interpretation.  
- Related work omission: must cite and situate against Dimakis et al. (Proc. IEEE, 2010) survey on gossip-based statistics.  
- Related work omission: engage with lifting/acceleration constructions (e.g., Jung et al. Allerton 2007, Li & Dai IEEE Trans. IT 2010, Chen et al. STOC 1999) and LADA.  
- Clarity: specify the edge/contact selection distribution (uniform over edges, degree-weighted, etc.) for the "node wakes up, contacts neighbor" model.  
- Clarity: explicitly state data assumptions—analysis currently implies worst-case initial vectors X but this isn’t in theorem statements.  
- Baseline comparison: include and analyze a flood-based aggregation with node memory alternative (tagged observations computing U-statistics) to contextualize O(1/t) rate.  
- Experimental gap: add empirical comparisons with the flood-based baseline to demonstrate practical significance of the proposed averaging method.  
- Minor fixes: clarify bipartite definition (directed vs. undirected), and correct wording/grammar typos on pp.1,4,6,7 as noted.  
- After author feedback: revisions improved clarity and reviewer raised score from 7 to 8.  
- Remaining issues to resolve before final acceptance: deeper engagement with lifting constructions (especially LADA) and inclusion of flood-based baseline comparisons in experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the above literature, clarity, and baseline comparison concerns.
