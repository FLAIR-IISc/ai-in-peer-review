- Presents new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized computation of U-statistics.
- Derives convergence rates O(1/t) for the synchronous algorithm and O(log t / t) for the asynchronous algorithm.
- Paper is well-organized and includes experiments showing performance improvements over existing approaches.
- Addresses an important problem (decentralized U-statistics) with wide ML and data-mining applications.
- Builds on gossip literature and highlights challenges of U-statistics requiring pairwise averaging.
- Introduces a "phantom node" construction to facilitate analysis.
- Strength: rigorous convergence analysis with spectral graph theory linking speed to network connectivity.
- Strength: clear writing, well-defined notation, and straightforward algorithm descriptions.
- Strength: original extension of gossip algorithms to efficiently compute U-statistics.
- Strength: significant practical impact supported by empirical results.
- Weakness: phantom-node-based analysis is complex and may be hard to follow without more intuition.
- Weakness: comparisons are limited (strong against U2-gossip [17] but missing broader baselines).
- Weakness: asynchronous algorithm and its convergence analysis lack sufficient detail.
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound, rigorous analysis, solid experiments, and clear presentation.
- Against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to analytical complexity, limited baseline comparisons, and underdeveloped async analysis.
- Overall: significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify phantom-node intuition and expand experimental baselines.
