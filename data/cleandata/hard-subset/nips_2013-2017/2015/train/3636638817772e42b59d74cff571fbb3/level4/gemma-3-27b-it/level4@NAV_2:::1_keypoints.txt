- New decentralized Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics with faster convergence than prior U2-Gossip.
- Algorithms extend to realistic asynchronous settings.
- Demonstrated improvements in convergence and computational cost (memory and communication) versus U2-Gossip.
- Rigorous theoretical guarantees: convergence rate bounds for both synchronous and asynchronous cases.
- Well-written, clearly motivated, and well-positioned within related work with appropriate citations.
- Strong empirical validation on two real-world datasets showing superior performance to established baselines.
- Novel algorithmic contribution to decentralized U-statistics estimation.
- Asynchronous treatment is a valuable, practical contribution.
- Analysis uses a “phantom node” reformulation that increases theoretical complexity and may hinder accessibility.
- Baseline comparisons are somewhat limited; broader comparisons to other distributed estimation algorithms would strengthen the evaluation.
