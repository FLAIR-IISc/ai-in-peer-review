- Paper addresses decentralized estimation of U-statistics (e.g., AUC, variance) using gossip algorithms, a problem harder than separable-sum averaging.
- Proposes two randomized algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, that propagate auxiliary data via random swaps while averaging local U-statistic estimates.
- Introduces a novel "phantom network" analytical tool to decouple data propagation and estimate averaging.
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous settings.
- Theoretical rates explicitly depend on data properties and the network spectral gap, aligning with gossip literature.
- Builds upon and improves U2-gossip, reducing memory and communication costs.
- Algorithmic idea (gossip averaging + random swaps) is a novel combination for this problem.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and results are presented concisely.
- Main weakness: experimental evaluation is narrow, comparing only to U2-gossip.
- Missing comparisons to simple baselines (e.g., random sampling of nodes, cumulative data propagation) that would contextualize practical gains.
- Authors should add such simple heuristic baselines to strengthen empirical claims.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on strengthening the experimental section to include simpler baselines.
