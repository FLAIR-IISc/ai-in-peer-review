- Addresses gap by extending gossip algorithms to compute U-statistics (pairs-based averages) in decentralized settings.  
- First comprehensive approach for efficient decentralized computation of U-statistics (e.g., AUC, variance, within-cluster scatter).  
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms that propagate data while maintaining local U-statistic estimates.  
- Establishes convergence rates of O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Introduces a novel "phantom nodes" reformulation to simplify analysis of coupled propagation and averaging.  
- Convergence bounds include explicit data-dependent terms and network-dependent terms tied to the spectral gap.  
- Empirical evaluation on SMVguide3 (AUC) and Wine Quality (cluster scatter) shows clear superiority over U2-gossip [17].  
- Demonstrates better average relative error and reduced variance across nodes across multiple network topologies (complete, 2D grid, Watts–Strogatz).  
- Well-written with clear notation, precise problem statement, and appropriate experimental design.  
- Properly situates work relative to prior gossip literature and clarifies improvements over existing decentralized U-statistics work.  
- Major weakness: lacks detailed per-iteration computational complexity analysis and comparison to U2-gossip.  
- Overall originality and significance are strong, with clear applications to resource-limited decentralized systems.  
- Recommendation: accept, pending addition of computational cost analysis to fully assess practical utility.
