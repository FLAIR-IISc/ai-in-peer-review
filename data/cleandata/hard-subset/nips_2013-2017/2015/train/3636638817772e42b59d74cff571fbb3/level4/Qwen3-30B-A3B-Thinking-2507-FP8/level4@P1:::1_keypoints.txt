- Introduces GoSta-sync and GoSta-async gossip algorithms for distributed computation of U-statistics.  
- Addresses a literature gap: gossip for U-statistics (pairwise data interactions) rather than separable statistics.  
- Builds on and improves U2-gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens 2009) with significant enhancements.  
- Proposes a framework combining data propagation with local averaging and a "phantom node" reformulation.  
- Establishes convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t / t) (asynchronous).  
- Achieves faster convergence and lower memory/communication per node (one auxiliary observation vs two in U2-gossip).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with dependencies on data norms and network spectral gap clearly identified.  
- Empirical evaluation on AUC and within-cluster scatter across complete, 2D grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs.  
- Experiments show GoSta algorithms outperform U2-gossip in convergence speed and stability.  
- Paper quality and clarity are excellent; complex techniques (phantom node) are well explained.  
- Originality is strong; problem and solution are novel within the gossip literature.  
- Significance is high for distributed ML applications (sensor networks, P2P, large-scale analysis).  
- Strengths: novel approach, superior theoretical rates, comprehensive experiments, clear exposition, practical efficiency gains.  
- Weaknesses: phantom node technique requires careful reading, asynchronous analysis is more complex, practical implications of spectral-gap dependence could be expanded.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and recommended for acceptance.
