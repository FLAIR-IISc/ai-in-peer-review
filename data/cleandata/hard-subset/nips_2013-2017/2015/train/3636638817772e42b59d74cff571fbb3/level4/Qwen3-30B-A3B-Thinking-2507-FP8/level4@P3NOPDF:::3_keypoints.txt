- Well-motivated, clearly written contribution on randomized gossip algorithms for distributed U-statistics.  
- Proposes synchronous and asynchronous algorithm variants with rigorous convergence bounds.  
- Significant theoretical advancement with solid convergence guarantees supporting the algorithm design.  
- Empirical evaluation on two datasets shows substantial improvement over U2-gossip across diverse graph topologies using average relative AUC error.  
- Empirical results are well-presented and convincingly demonstrate superior accuracy versus the baseline.  
- Choice of evaluation metric (average relative AUC error) is appropriate and standard for the domain.  
- Major omission: no runtime or per-iteration timing data reported for either the proposed methods or U2-gossip.  
- Practical viability depends on per-iteration computation and scalability; slower iterations could negate accuracy gains in wall-clock time.  
- If each iteration is 2–3× slower, overall runtime advantage may vanish—this trade-off must be addressed.  
- Recommendation: include median/mean iteration times (e.g., ms) for both algorithms across datasets alongside AUC results.  
- If iteration time is comparable or marginally higher, the contribution remains compelling; if substantially slower, discuss limitations and optimizations.  
- Also clarify whether the asynchronous variant incurs additional communication overhead compared to U2-gossip.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and empirical work, but missing runtime comparison warrants a minor revision before acceptance.
