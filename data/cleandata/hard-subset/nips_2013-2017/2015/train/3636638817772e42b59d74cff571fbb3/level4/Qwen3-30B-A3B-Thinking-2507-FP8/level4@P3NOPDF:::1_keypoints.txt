- Introduces novel Gossip algorithms for distributed U-statistic computation, filling a scalability gap in statistical learning.
- The methods achieve faster convergence rates than U2-Gossip and are effective in asynchronous settings.
- Theoretical guarantees are provided for both synchronous and asynchronous communication models.
- Convergence rate strictly dominates U2-Gossip with explicit iteration bounds for target accuracy.
- Maintains convergence guarantees under non-deterministic node execution and communication delays.
- Communication and memory costs are quantified and shown to be comparable or lower than U2-Gossip.
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world datasets validate theoretical claims against multiple baselines.
- Empirical results show much faster convergence, fewer communication rounds, and lower memory usage.
- Asynchronous experiments simulate realistic network issues (varying speeds, packet loss) and show stable performance where synchronous baselines degrade.
- Ablation studies on network size and asynchrony levels plus statistical significance testing strengthen empirical findings.
- Writing is clear, well-motivated, and the paper connects theory to implementation with effective visualizations.
- Minor suggestions: report per-iteration computational complexity and clarify asynchronous model assumptions (e.g., bounded delays).
- Overall strong contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
