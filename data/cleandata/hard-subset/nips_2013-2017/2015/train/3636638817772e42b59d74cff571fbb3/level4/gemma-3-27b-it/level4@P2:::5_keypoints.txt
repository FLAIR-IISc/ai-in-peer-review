This paper introduces randomized synchronous and asynchronous gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics in distributed networks.

It proves convergence rates O(1/t) (synchronous) and O(log t/t) (asynchronous) with explicit data- and network-dependent constants.

Experiments on real datasets (AUC, within-cluster scatter) show the proposed methods outperform prior work [17].

Situates contribution within gossip algorithm literature and highlights relevance of U-statistics to ML/data-mining tasks.

Strength: Novel algorithmic approach extending gossip methods to pairwise U-statistics.

Strength: Solid theoretical guarantees, including dependence on the network spectral gap.

Strength: Empirical validation on real-world tasks demonstrates practical effectiveness.

Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation that aids understanding.

Strength: High practical relevance for decentralized large-scale data analysis.

Weakness: "Phantom node" construction is clever but adds explanatory complexity and could use more intuition.

Weakness: Asynchronous convergence (O(log t/t)) is weaker than synchronous; trade-offs and improvement strategies are underexplored.

Weakness: Analysis assumes connected, non-bipartite graphs; behavior on other topologies is not addressed.

Weakness: Limited baseline comparisons beyond [17]; broader benchmarks would strengthen empirical claims.

Argument for acceptance: Addresses a challenging, practically important problem with novel, theoretically sound, and empirically validated solutions.

Argument against acceptance: Accessibility issues around phantom nodes and the relatively weak asynchronous rate.

Overall assessment: Strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify phantom-node intuition and expand experimental baselines.
