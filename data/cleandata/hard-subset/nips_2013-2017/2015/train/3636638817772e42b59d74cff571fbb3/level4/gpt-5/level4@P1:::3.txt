Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms (GoSta-sync and GoSta-async) for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics over a network. Each node holds one observation, maintains an auxiliary observation that performs a (coupled) random walk via neighbor swaps, and updates a local running average of H(Xk, Yk). In parallel, local estimates are averaged when an edge is activated. The analysis introduces a “phantom nodes” construction to decouple data propagation from averaging. The authors prove convergence in expectation to the desired U-statistic, with rates O(1/t) for the synchronous algorithm and O(log t / t) for the asynchronous one. The bounds make explicit data-dependent terms and network-dependent terms tied to the spectral gap, showing faster convergence on well-connected graphs. The work is positioned against standard gossip for separable sums/averages (e.g., Boyd et al.) and, most directly, against U2-gossip (Pelckmans & Suykens), the prior method for U-statistics. Compared to U2-gossip, the proposed approach uses less memory/communication per iteration, handles asynchrony, and achieves stronger rates. Experiments on AUC and within-cluster point scatter across complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs show consistently lower average relative error and lower variance across nodes, with GoSta-async performing comparably to GoSta-sync in practice.

Strengths:
- Well-motivated problem: many important global metrics (AUC, variance, Gini, Kendall tau, within-cluster scatter) are U-statistics and not directly amenable to standard gossip.
- Clear algorithms for both synchronous and asynchronous settings, with practical node-local operations.
- Theoretical guarantees with explicit dependence on data dispersion and spectral gap; the analysis via phantom nodes is elegant and informative.
- Improved communication/memory profile versus U2-gossip, which requires propagating two auxiliary observations and two edge activations per iteration.
- Empirical results on two real datasets across multiple graph topologies show faster error decay and lower across-node variance compared to U2-gossip.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- The paper evaluates error versus iteration count but does not report per-iteration runtime or communication cost (e.g., wall-clock time, bytes sent, number/size of messages), nor memory footprint per node as a function of data dimension d. If each iteration of the proposed methods is materially slower than U2-gossip, the practical benefit could diminish. Please report per-iteration runtimes and communication/memory costs for GoSta-sync/async and U2-gossip across the tested graphs.
- Experiments focus on two datasets and two U-statistics. Broader coverage would strengthen the empirical case, though the current choices are representative.

Quality:
- The algorithms are technically sound and carefully analyzed. Convergence in expectation is established with clear dependence on graph connectivity. Proof details are deferred to the supplement, which is standard.
- It would help to quantify constants in the asynchronous bound (c′(G)) and briefly discuss whether high-probability or almost-sure convergence can be obtained, but this is not essential for acceptance.

Clarity:
- The paper is clearly written and well organized. The phantom network is explained as an analysis device, which avoids confusion about algorithmic complexity.
- Consider adding a concise comparison table summarizing per-iteration computation, communication, and memory for GoSta-sync, GoSta-async, and U2-gossip.

Originality:
- Extending gossip to U-statistics with simultaneous data propagation and averaging, together with the phantom-node analytical framework and an asynchronous variant, appears novel relative to prior work.

Significance:
- U-statistics are widely used, and the ability to compute them efficiently in decentralized settings is valuable. The improved rates and practical advantages over U2-gossip make this contribution likely to be used and built upon.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses an important and underexplored problem in decentralized estimation.
- Provides both synchronous and asynchronous algorithms with provable convergence and favorable rates.
- Lower memory/communication requirements and empirically superior performance across diverse graphs.
- Clear presentation and solid theoretical grounding.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Missing per-iteration runtime and communication/memory measurements; practical efficiency versus U2-gossip remains unquantified in wall-clock terms.
- Limited variety of datasets/statistics in experiments.

Requested clarifications:
- Report per-iteration wall-clock time, number/size of messages, and memory footprint per node for GoSta-sync, GoSta-async, and U2-gossip, ideally across graph types and data dimensions.
- Briefly discuss how sensitive performance is to the spectral gap in practice (e.g., by plotting error versus iterations rescaled by 1/(1 − λ2(2))).
- Confirm what node-local knowledge is required in the asynchronous setting (e.g., degrees, |E|) and how robust the method is if these are estimated.

Overall assessment:
The paper is well motivated and written, provides theoretical convergence bounds, and demonstrates strong empirical performance relative to U2-gossip on two datasets. If per-iteration runtime and communication/memory costs are comparable or better than U2-gossip, the contribution is practically compelling. I encourage acceptance contingent on adding the requested per-iteration efficiency measurements.
