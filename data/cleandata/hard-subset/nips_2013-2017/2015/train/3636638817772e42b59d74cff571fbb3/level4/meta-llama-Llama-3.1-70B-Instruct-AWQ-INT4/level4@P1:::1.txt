This paper presents new Gossip algorithms for computation of U-statistics. The proposed algorithms have a faster convergence rate compared to the state-of-the-art (U2-Gossip algorithm from Pelckmans and Suykens, 2009) and also work in the more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results further demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms compared to existing algorithms.

The paper is well-written, and the problem is well-motivated. The authors have well-positioned their work with respect to the existing literature. As far as I can see, the proposed algorithms are novel and superior in terms of convergence and cost (memory and communication) in comparison to the U2-gossip algorithm. Moreover, the proposed algorithm works for asynchronous settings.

Apart from theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors have also done extensive empirical evaluations over two datasets and compared to the baselines. This further strengthens the contributions of the paper.

The main ideas of the paper are related to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere, particularly in the context of gossip algorithms for decentralized computation. The authors have provided a clear and concise review of the related work in Section 3, which helps to understand the position of their work in the broader context.

The strengths of the paper include the novelty of the proposed algorithms, their faster convergence rate, and their ability to work in asynchronous settings. The authors have also provided a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithms, including convergence rate bounds and comparisons to existing algorithms.

The weaknesses of the paper are minor and primarily related to the presentation. Some of the sections, particularly Section 4, could be better organized and easier to follow. Additionally, some of the notation and terminology could be better explained for readers who are not familiar with the field.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors have been careful in evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

The clarity of the paper is good, but could be improved in some sections. The organization of the paper is logical, and the authors have provided sufficient background information for readers to understand the context and significance of the work.

The originality of the paper is high, as the proposed algorithms are novel and provide significant improvements over existing algorithms. The significance of the paper is also high, as the proposed algorithms have the potential to be widely used in decentralized computation and machine learning applications.

In conclusion, I believe that the paper is a strong contribution to the field and should be accepted for publication. The authors have presented new and significant results, and the paper is well-written and well-organized.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* Novelty of the proposed algorithms
* Faster convergence rate compared to state-of-the-art
* Ability to work in asynchronous settings
* Thorough theoretical analysis and experimental evaluation
* High originality and significance of the paper

Arguments con acceptance:

* Minor issues with presentation and organization
* Some notation and terminology could be better explained
