- Addresses distributed aggregate computation (averaging) with a simple iterative algorithm; problem is practically relevant.  
- Core algorithm is straightforward and well-motivated but its primary contribution/novelty is unclear.  
- Algorithm resembles known consensus-based and gossip techniques; paper fails to differentiate from existing methods.  
- No clear statement of novelty or comparison to foundational approaches weakens the contribution.  
- Empirical evaluation is the most significant weakness: only synthetic data and a single dataset are used.  
- No baseline comparisons (e.g., randomized gossip, periodic synchronization, uniform random node selection) are provided.  
- Claim of significantly reduced communication overhead is unsupported without comparative experiments.  
- Experimental details are insufficient for reproduction (missing network size, topology, noise levels, etc.).  
- Reported metrics (e.g., communication rounds) lack context—per-round costs and net benefits are not analyzed.  
- Theoretical analysis and proofs are technically sound but rely on standard assumptions and ignore practical issues (node failures, heterogeneous delays, asynchrony).  
- Suggestions: clearly articulate novelty versus prior work, include rigorous comparisons to strong baselines across diverse settings, and provide detailed experimental setups and metrics.  
- Recommendation: reject due to lack of comparative evidence and unclear differentiation from existing methods.
