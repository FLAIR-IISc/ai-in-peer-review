- Proposes GoSta-sync and GoSta-async randomized gossip algorithms for decentralized estimation of degree-2 U-statistics using a single auxiliary observation that performs neighbor swaps and local running averages.  
- Introduces a “phantom nodes” analytical construction to decouple data propagation from averaging.  
- Proves convergence in expectation: O(1/t) for synchronous and O(log t / t) for asynchronous, with bounds depending on data dispersion and graph spectral gap.  
- Positions work against standard gossip and prior U2-gossip, claiming less memory/communication per iteration, asynchrony handling, and stronger rates.  
- Empirical results (AUC, within-cluster scatter) on complete, grid, and Watts–Strogatz graphs show lower average relative error and lower across-node variance; GoSta-async matches GoSta-sync in practice.  
- Strength: tackles an important class of global metrics (many are U-statistics) not handled by standard gossip.  
- Strength: provides clear, practical algorithms for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees explicitly tie convergence to data and network properties; phantom-node analysis is elegant.  
- Strength: improved communication/memory profile relative to U2-gossip (one auxiliary observation vs two).  
- Strength: empirical superiority across multiple graph topologies and datasets.  
- Weakness: experiments report error vs iterations but omit per-iteration wall-clock time, bytes/messages sent, and memory footprint as function of data dimension.  
- Weakness: limited empirical coverage—only two datasets and two U-statistics tested.  
- Suggestion: report per-iteration runtimes, communication costs, and memory per node for GoSta-sync/async and U2-gossip across graphs.  
- Suggestion: add a concise comparison table of per-iteration computation, communication, and memory for the three methods.  
- Quality: algorithms are technically sound with standard deferment of proof details to the supplement.  
- Minor request: quantify constants in the asynchronous bound (c′(G)) and discuss possibility of high-probability or almost-sure convergence.  
- Clarity: paper is well written and organized; phantom network explanation prevents confusion about algorithmic complexity.  
- Originality: simultaneous data propagation and averaging for U-statistics and the phantom-node analysis (plus asynchronous variant) appear novel.  
- Significance: important and broadly applicable contribution if practical efficiency matches claimed advantages.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses underexplored problem, provides provable synchronous and asynchronous algorithms with favorable rates and lower resource needs, and shows empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks per-iteration runtime/communication/memory measurements and has limited experimental breadth.  
- Requested clarifications: report wall-clock time, message counts/sizes, memory per node; analyze sensitivity to spectral gap (e.g., rescaled plots); confirm required node-local knowledge in asynchronous setting and robustness to estimates.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical and empirical work; encourage acceptance contingent on adding per-iteration efficiency measurements.
