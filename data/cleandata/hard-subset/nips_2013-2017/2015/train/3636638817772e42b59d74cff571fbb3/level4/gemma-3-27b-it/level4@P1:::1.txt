This paper presents new Gossip algorithms for computing U-statistics. The proposed algorithms achieve faster convergence rates compared to the state-of-the-art (the U2-Gossip algorithm from Pelckmans & Suykens, 2009) and also extend to more realistic asynchronous settings. The experimental results further demonstrate the superiority of the proposed algorithms compared to existing approaches.

The paper is well-written and clearly motivates the problem, positioning the work effectively within the existing literature. To the best of my understanding, the proposed algorithms are novel and offer improvements in terms of convergence and computational cost (memory and communication) compared to U2-Gossip. Importantly, the proposed algorithm also functions in asynchronous settings.

Beyond the theoretical guarantees and analysis, the authors provide extensive empirical evaluations on two datasets, comparing their methods to relevant baselines. This significantly strengthens the paper’s contributions. The work addresses a challenging problem in distributed computation – extending gossip algorithms to U-statistics – and offers a compelling solution with both theoretical and practical validation.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed algorithms are a novel extension of gossip algorithms to the challenging problem of U-statistic computation.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides convergence rate bounds for both synchronous and asynchronous settings.
*   **Improved Performance:** The algorithms demonstrate faster convergence and lower communication costs compared to existing methods (specifically U2-Gossip).
*   **Asynchronous Setting:** The work extends to the practically relevant asynchronous model.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Thorough experimental evaluations on real datasets support the theoretical findings.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Complexity of Proofs:** The convergence analysis relies on a “phantom node” reformulation, which may be difficult for some readers to fully grasp without careful study. (Though, this is a reasonable complexity given the novelty of the contribution.)
*   **Limited Baseline Comparison:** While U2-Gossip is a key comparison, it might be valuable to compare against other, perhaps less directly related, distributed estimation algorithms as a broader context.
