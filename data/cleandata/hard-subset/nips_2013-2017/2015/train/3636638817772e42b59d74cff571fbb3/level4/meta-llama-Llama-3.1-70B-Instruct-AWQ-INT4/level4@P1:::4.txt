This paper proposes new synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for efficiently computing U-statistics in a decentralized setting. The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of their approach, including convergence rates and numerical experiments. 

The problem of decentralized estimation of U-statistics is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear overview of the challenges and existing work in this area. The proposed algorithms, GoSta-sync and GoSta-async, are intuitive and well-explained, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of their convergence properties. The use of "phantom nodes" to model the data propagation and averaging steps is a clever technique that allows for a more tractable analysis.

The numerical experiments provide some evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms, particularly in comparison to the U2-gossip algorithm. However, the evaluation is limited to only two datasets and three types of networks, and it would be beneficial to see more extensive experimentation, including comparisons to additional baseline methods.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the algorithm itself may not be particularly novel, and the authors could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how their approach differs from existing work in gossip algorithms. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more context on the potential applications and impact of the proposed algorithms.

In terms of the NIPS criteria, the paper scores well on quality, as the analysis is thorough and well-executed. The paper is also well-written and clear, scoring well on clarity. However, the originality of the algorithm is somewhat limited, and the paper could benefit from more discussion of how the approach differs from existing work. The significance of the paper is clear, as it addresses an important problem in decentralized estimation, but the evaluation could be more extensive.
