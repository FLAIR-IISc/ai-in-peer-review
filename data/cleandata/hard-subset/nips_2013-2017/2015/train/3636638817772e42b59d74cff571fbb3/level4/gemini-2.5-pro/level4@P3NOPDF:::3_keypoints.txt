- Proposes novel synchronous and asynchronous randomized gossip algorithms for distributed U-statistics computation, a well-motivated problem.

- Paper is clearly written with coherent explanations of methods.

- Strong empirical performance: outperforms U2-gossip on two datasets by substantially lower average relative AUC error across varied network topologies.

- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees, adding rigor and insight into algorithm behavior.

- Addresses both synchronous and asynchronous settings, increasing real-world applicability.

- Primary concern: practical computational cost and runtime are not fully evaluated.

- Requests comparison of wall-clock time per iteration or detailed per-iteration computational complexity (e.g., FLOPs) versus U2-gossip.

- Notes risk that faster convergence in iteration count may not translate to faster total runtime if per-iteration cost is higher.
