- Proposed training method is valuable and timely for mapping deep networks to spiking-neuron hardware with discrete synapses.  
- Paper is generally well-written but Section 1’s concluding claims risk implying it is the first such approach and need rewording; demonstrating on TN validates the method but is not itself novel.  
- Clarify what "0.15 bits per synapse" means in terms of precision constraints.  
- Provide a unified description or schematic of the network topology.  
- Report the added training time cost due to probabilistic synaptic updates.  
- Explain why the 30-core ensemble yields lower accuracy than the smaller 5-core network.  
- Explain why the 5-core ensemble results show minimal variance across trials.  
- Specify input spike rates during testing and whether the input is presented as a spike train.  
- Fix inconsistency in Ref. 12 (line 399) which lists constraints like bias=0—this contradicts "train-then-constrain"—and correct the typo "approach approach."  
- Explicitly compare the method to existing spiking backpropagation rules (e.g., SpikeProp) to contextualize originality.  
- Note that the addressed constraints are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms.  
- Question practical efficiency: reported accuracy depends on a 64-ensemble approach, implying large hardware overhead for marginal gains.  
- Core contribution is sound but needs sharper technical precision and clearer justification of design choices to strengthen the paper.
