- Proposes a novel method to reconcile high-precision continuous variables in backpropagation with spike-based devices that have binary synapses.  
- Treats analog variables as probabilities and samples them to map an offline-trained network onto a spiking-chip for deployment.  
- Adapts this probabilistic sampling strategy to backpropagation and validates it on the MNIST benchmark.  
- Successfully maps the trained network onto two different architectures, optimizing trade-offs between size and accuracy, with performance measured directly on-chip.  
- Manuscript is clear, well-structured, highly original, and has potential applicability to other algorithms and hardware platforms.  
- Reviewer recommends adding citations to Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 when discussing existing hardware.  
- Paper under-emphasizes the broader significance and practical impact of the work, particularly regarding TrueNorth deployment.  
- Unclear how much advantage this method provides over alternative approaches cited in references [9]–[12].  
- Missing methodological details on input-to-spike conversion and the spike frequencies used.  
- Lacks details on shared weight values and the specific neuron models implemented on-chip.  
- Overall results are convincing but providing the omitted details would improve clarity and increase the paper’s impact.
