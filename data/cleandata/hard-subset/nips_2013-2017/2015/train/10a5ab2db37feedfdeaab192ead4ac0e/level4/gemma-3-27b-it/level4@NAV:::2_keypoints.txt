- Addresses mapping trained neural networks to spike-based hardware, bridging continuous backpropagation and binary synapses (e.g., TrueNorth).  
- Proposes a probabilistic mapping that treats analog backpropagation variables as probabilities for spike sampling.  
- Adapts backpropagation to this strategy and validates on MNIST, mapping to two TrueNorth architectures with on-chip results.  
- Manuscript is excellent, clearly presented, original, and potentially broadly applicable across hardware and approaches.  
- Reviewer asks to include related hardware work (Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 in the discussion.  
- Requests stronger articulation of the method’s significance and its practical impact for deploying chips like TrueNorth and specific application domains.  
- Calls for clearer comparison to existing alternatives (references [9–12]) to delineate advantages.  
- Requests more implementation details: input encoding (spike conversion, frequencies), weight sharing, and neuron models used.
