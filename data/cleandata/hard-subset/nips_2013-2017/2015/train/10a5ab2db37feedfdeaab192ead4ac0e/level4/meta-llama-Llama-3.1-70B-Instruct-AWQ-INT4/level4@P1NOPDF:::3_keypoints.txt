- Presents a backprop training method using spike probabilities tailored to hardware with spiking neurons and discrete synapses.  
- Method is useful and adaptable to multi-neuron hardware constraints, making a valuable contribution.  
- Paper is generally clear but Section 1 claims should be rephrased to avoid implying novelty that isn’t accurate.  
- Demonstrating performance on TrueNorth should be framed as validation of the training method, not a novel feature.  
- Clarify the meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse" in Section 2.  
- Provide a concise description of the network topology.  
- Report the increase in training time caused by probabilistic synaptic connection updates.  
- Explain why a single ensemble of the 30-core network performs surprisingly worse than the 5-core network.  
- Discuss why different ensembles of the 5-core network yield similar results.  
- State the input spike rates during testing and clarify whether the input is a spike train.  
- Note that Reference 12 includes training constraints (e.g., bias=0), so it is not purely a train-then-constrain approach (and fix the typo).  
- Add discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp) for context.  
- Highlight that the training constraints are specific to TrueNorth and may not generalize, and that achieving target accuracy may require more hardware resources (e.g., 64-ensemble).
