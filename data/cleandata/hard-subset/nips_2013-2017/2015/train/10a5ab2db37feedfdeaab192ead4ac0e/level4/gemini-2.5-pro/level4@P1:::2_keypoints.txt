- Presents a "constrain-then-train" method to train spiking networks with low-precision discrete synapses by treating continuous variables as probabilities for backpropagation, then sampling deployed spiking instances.
- Validated on MNIST, demonstrating a trade-off between 99.42% accuracy and 0.268 µJ/image energy by deploying configurations on IBM TrueNorth.
- Strength: Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, and logically presented with convincing results.
- Strength: Highly original approach—probabilistic training plus sampling for binary synapse deployment is novel and elegant.
- Strength: Potentially high impact and generalizable to neuromorphic platforms beyond TrueNorth.
- Weakness: Broader significance and specific practical applications of enabling TrueNorth deployment are not sufficiently articulated.
- Weakness: Lacks direct comparison to alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]) to clarify advantages over prior work.
- Missing detail: How input pixel values are converted to input spike probabilities (mapping function).
- Missing detail: Whether single time-step (1 ms) encoding implies a single-sample/rate-based code per image.
- Missing detail: Exact shared weight values (sij) used in deployment and whether +1/−1 initialization are the final deployed values.
- Missing detail: Specific TrueNorth neuron model and parameters used to implement history-free thresholding dynamics.
- Related-work suggestion: Include other neuromorphic platforms (e.g., Qiao et al. 2015) for a more complete overview.
- Related-work suggestion: Cite Esser et al., "Convolutional networks for fast, energy-efficient neuromorphic computing" (arXiv:1506.05427).
- For acceptance: Novel, sound technique that reconciles backpropagation with highly efficient spiking hardware; strong results.
- Against acceptance: Paper should better position its significance, compare to prior art, and provide missing implementation details to enable reproducibility.
