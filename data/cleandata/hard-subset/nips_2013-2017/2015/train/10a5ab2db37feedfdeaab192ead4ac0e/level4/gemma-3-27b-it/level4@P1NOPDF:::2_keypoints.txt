- Method: interpret analog variables as probabilities and sample them to map offline-trained networks onto spiking chips with binary synapses.  
- Algorithmic contribution: adapt backpropagation to train networks under this probabilistic/binary-synapse mapping.  
- Validation: demonstrated on MNIST and mapped trained networks onto the TrueNorth device.  
- Hardware results: mapped to two architectures optimized for size and accuracy and reported performance measured directly from the chip.  
- Presentation: manuscript is high-quality, clear, concise, and original with potential applicability to other hardware.  
- Missing citations: should consider adding Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015, and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Significance concern: paper does not clearly highlight how the method facilitates practical deployment of TrueNorth or identify specific application areas.  
- Comparative advantage unclear: not evident how this method outperforms or differs in practice from alternatives cited in [9]–[12].  
- Missing implementation details: needs specifics on input-to-spike conversion (e.g., spike frequencies), handling of shared weight values, and the neuron models used.
