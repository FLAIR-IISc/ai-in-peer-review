- Empirical results are strong and novel on the experimental side, especially in accuracy and reported power consumption.  
- Experimental section is thorough and would be valuable if fully reproducible.  

Major concerns about novelty and prior work:
- Section 3.2’s algorithm largely overlaps with Zhao et al. (1996) and Soudry et al. (Expectation Backpropagation, 2014); similarities in approximations and update rules need explicit acknowledgement.  
- The “constrain-then-train” methodology is not novel and was already proposed/tested in [15]; the text should credit [15] and clarify differences.  
- The paper mischaracterizes Expectation Backpropagation (EP); EP is a backprop-style update derived via expectation propagation for binary neurons/weights and can handle constrained connectivity—novelty claims should be corrected.  
- Incorrect state-of-the-art claims: prior best results for binary weights/neurons on MNIST were achieved by [15]; include direct, like-for-like comparisons (no augmentation) with error bars.

Suggestions to strengthen the paper:
- Explicitly state what is new (engineering vs algorithmic contributions) and highlight any new derivational steps or theorems with ablation studies.  
- Add a detailed relationship-to-prior-work section mapping each approximation/update to [14] and [15], and explain any substantive differences in “constrain-then-train.”  
- Include head-to-head baselines by reproducing or citing faithful implementations of [15] across all claimed datasets, matched for preprocessing and model capacity.  
- Clarify modeling details: activation functions (sign?), handling of zero-inputs, surrogate gradients, and any training/testing stochasticity and ensemble formation.  
- Improve reproducibility: provide full hyperparameters, training schedules, initialization, and detailed power-measurement methodology (metrics, setup, avg vs peak).  

Minor comments:
- Fix typographical errors (e.g., “backpropgation”) and ensure references are complete and consistently formatted.  

Overall evaluation:
- Strong empirical and power-efficiency contributions, but current framing overstates novelty and under-cites antecedents; revise positioning, SOTA claims, and add matched comparisons to be supportable for acceptance.
