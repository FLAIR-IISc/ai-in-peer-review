- Proposal: Represent spikes and binary synapses as continuous probabilities to enable backprop in a differentiable surrogate space, then map to TrueNorth by sampling and ensemble averaging.
- Architecture & training: Introduce a TrueNorth-compatible sliding-window topology and a weight-initialization scheme that balances excitatory/inhibitory strengths to reduce redundancy.
- Results: Report 99.42% MNIST with a 64-member ensemble (108 μJ/image) and 92.7% with a single member (0.268 μJ/image) running in real time on TrueNorth.
- Prior-art overlap: The probabilistic backprop approach is closely related to stochastic bit-stream networks [14] and Expectation Backprop (EBP) [15], sharing Gaussian approximations and train-on-probabilities/sample-at-test strategies.
- Strength — experiments: Strong experimental validation with excellent accuracy–energy trade-offs on real hardware.
- Strength — deployment: Demonstrates real-time deployment on TrueNorth with a topology honoring constrained connectivity and low-precision synapses.
- Strength — engineering: Core-level initialization (balanced s-values and minimized column inner products) is well-motivated and useful for low-precision neuromorphic systems.
- Strength — clarity: Clear description of hardware mapping, ensemble formation, and training heuristics (dropout, augmentation, learning-rate schedule).
- Weakness — novelty claims: Claims of novelty are overstated given EBP and related prior work; introduction and discussion should be revised to properly credit prior art.
- Recommendation on positioning: Recast contributions as adapting probabilistic backprop to TrueNorth’s specific constraints, proposing topology/initialization, and providing the hardware demo and energy measurements.
- Claim clarification: Clarify how the method differs from EBP (e.g., TrueNorth strength sharing, specific gradient approximations, end-to-end hardware mapping).
- SOTA comparison: Correct prior-SOTA attributions and ensure comparisons use consistent settings (e.g., match data augmentation and other conditions).
- Algorithmic clarity: Explicitly acknowledge similarity of Gaussian/independence approximations and gradient-through-expectation steps to [14,15], and highlight where derivations differ (e.g., dropped σ^−3 terms, block-quantized strengths, bias constraints).
- Ablations requested: Provide ablations for single-member vs ensemble, with vs without augmentation, and energy–accuracy scaling with ensemble size; compare with EBP under matched conditions.
- Energy reporting: Clarify energy accounting (active vs leakage, per-core normalization) and provide accuracy at equal time budgets (e.g., single 1 ms tick) for reproducibility.
- Approximation validation: Include brief empirical validation of the Gaussian approximation’s accuracy (e.g., empirical vs predicted firing probabilities).
- Evaluation — quality: Experimental section is strong and plausible, but theoretical approximations are standard and need small validation.
- Evaluation — clarity: Paper is generally well organized but should better delineate prior methodological overlap and novelty.
- Evaluation — originality: Methodological originality is incremental over [14,15]; main novelty is adaptation to TrueNorth and hardware-scale demo.
- Evaluation — significance: High significance due to state-of-the-art energy efficiency and strong accuracy on real neuromorphic hardware.
- Pros (summary): Excellent energy/accuracy trade-offs on hardware; practical topology and initialization; clear path to scalable low-power deployment.
- Cons (summary): Overstated novelty; incomplete or inaccurate SOTA comparisons; limited ablations on ensemble/augmentation contributions.
- Final recommendation: After authors revise claims/citations and SOTA comparisons (especially to [15]) and add requested clarifications/ablations, the paper would make a strong contribution.
