- Central contribution: Maps offline-trained continuous weights to binary synapses by treating weights as probabilities and stochastically sampling them, validated on MNIST and deployed on TrueNorth in size- and accuracy-optimized configurations with hardware-measured performance.

- Strength — Clarity: Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, and logically structured.

- Strength — Originality: Novel idea to interpret continuous-valued weights as probabilities for setting binary synapses.

- Strength — Potential impact: Approach is broadly applicable to other neuromorphic platforms with synaptic constraints.

- Strength — Experimental validation: Demonstrated viability with concrete performance figures measured on TrueNorth hardware.

- Weakness — Context/significance: Paper fails to clearly articulate the broader significance and specific practical applications enabled by the method.

- Weakness — Comparison to alternatives: Lacks thorough comparison with existing discrete/binary-weight training methods (refs [9]–[12]) and discussion of relative advantages/trade-offs.

- Weakness — Missing experimental details: Key reproducibility details are omitted and should be provided.

- Missing detail — Input encoding: How static MNIST images were converted into temporal spike trains is not specified.

- Missing detail — Network/neuron parameters: Neuron model and characteristic spike frequencies on the chip are not reported.

- Missing detail — Weight parameters: Specific values of shared weights used in the hardware implementation are not provided.

- Minor suggestion: Hardware platform discussion should cite related work (e.g., Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015 and arXiv:1506.05427).

- Overall: High-quality, novel, and promising work that would be strengthened by elaborating significance, adding comparative analysis, and including omitted experimental details.
