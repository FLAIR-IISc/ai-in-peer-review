- Proposes treating analog variables as probabilities and sampling them to map offline-trained networks onto binary-synapse spiking hardware.  
- Adapts backpropagation to support the probabilistic-to-spike mapping approach.  
- Validated method on MNIST and mapped networks to two architectures optimized for size and accuracy.  
- Reports performance results obtained from the TrueNorth chip.  
- Manuscript is high quality, clear, and presents a novel contribution with potential cross-platform applicability.  
- Reviewer requests citing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 when discussing hardware platforms.  
- Significance not well emphasized: unclear how the method enables practical deployment on TrueNorth or identifies concrete application domains.  
- Advantages over alternative methods (e.g., refs [9]–[12]) are not sufficiently demonstrated.  
- Paper is well-structured and results are compelling, but lacks essential implementation details.  
- Missing implementation details include input spike conversion methods, spike frequencies, shared weight values, and neuron model specifications.
