- Paper proposes a backprop-based training method on spike probabilities that explicitly incorporates TN neuromorphic hardware constraints.
- Topic is timely and practically useful given interest in mapping deep networks to constrained neuromorphic platforms.
- Demonstrating the trained network on the target hardware (TN) is a valuable validation, but running on TN alone is not a novelty claim.
- Training approach is well motivated by hardware considerations and practically relevant.
- Claims at the end of Section 1 should be reworded to avoid implying first-of-its-kind novelty.
- The network topology (layers, neuron counts, connectivity, synapse precision, enforced architectural constraints) is not clearly consolidated in one place.
- Clarify what “0.15 bits per synapse” means, how it is defined/measured, and its relation to TN’s discrete synapse implementation.
- Quantify additional training time introduced by the probabilistic synaptic update versus a non-probabilistic baseline and discuss convergence implications.
- Explain why the single-ensemble 30-core network performance is much lower than the 5-core network, and analyze variance across ensembles.
- Explain why different ensembles of the 5-core network give approximately the same results and provide a hypothesis or variance analysis.
- Specify input testing details: whether inputs are spike trains, spike rates and durations, encoding scheme, spike statistics, and impact on latency/accuracy trade-offs.
- Correct characterization of Reference 12: it includes constraints during training (e.g., bias=0) and is not purely “train-then-constrain”; also fix the “approach approach” typo.
- Discuss and compare the method with prior spiking backpropagation rules (e.g., SpikeProp) regarding objectives, learning signals, differentiability assumptions, discrete-synapse handling, and hardware-awareness.
- Discuss which constraint-handling aspects are TN-specific versus generalizable to other neuromorphic systems (different quantization, neuron models, connectivity limits).
- Address accuracy–resource trade-offs: dependence on ensembles (e.g., 64-ensemble), required hardware scaling, and implications for latency, power, and area in deployment.
- Minor edits: reword the novelty claim in Section 1 to avoid overstatement and fix the typo on line 399.
