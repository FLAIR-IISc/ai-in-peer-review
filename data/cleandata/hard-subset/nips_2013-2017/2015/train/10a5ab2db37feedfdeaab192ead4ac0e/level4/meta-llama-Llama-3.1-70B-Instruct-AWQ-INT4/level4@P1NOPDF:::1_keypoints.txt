1) Algorithm in Sec. 3.2 strongly resembles methods in [14] and [15]; the final algorithm is effectively a hybrid derived with identical approximations and should be acknowledged.  
2) The "constrain-then-train" methodology (learn weight distribution then sample fixed ensemble) is not novel — it was proposed and tested in [15]; the relationship should be clarified.  
3) Novelty claim (i) is misleading: expectation backpropagation from [15] already gives an EP-based backprop update for binary neurons/weights and can handle constrained connectivity; the claim should be revised.  
4) Citations of prior state-of-the-art for binary weights/neurons appear incorrect; [15] achieved top MNIST results without data augmentation.  
5) The paper delivers strong experimental results (accuracy and power consumption), but its general methodology and algorithmic contributions are less novel than claimed.
