- Paper proposes training neural networks with binary weights/neurons and a novel algorithm, but claims of novelty and accuracy are questioned.
- Algorithm in Section 3.2 closely resembles methods in refs [14] and [15], effectively a hybrid using identical approximations; this connection should be acknowledged.
- The "constrain-then-train" methodology (learn weight distribution, sample fixed network at test) is presented as novel but was already suggested and tested in [15]; relationship must be clarified.
- Novelty claim (i) is misleading because [15] presents an "expectation backpropagation" update for binary neurons and weights that can handle constrained connectivity.
- Citation of prior state-of-the-art is incorrect: ref [15] appears to have achieved previous best results on MNIST without data augmentation, matching this paper’s setup.
- Experimental results (accuracy and power consumption) are strong and novel despite methodological/algorithmic novelty concerns.
- Overall, methodology and algorithm are less novel than claimed, though the authors’ willingness to address these issues is appreciated.
