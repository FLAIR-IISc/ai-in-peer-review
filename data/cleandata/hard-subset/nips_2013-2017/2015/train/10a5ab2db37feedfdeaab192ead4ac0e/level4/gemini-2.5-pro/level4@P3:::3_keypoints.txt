- Novel probabilistic "constrain-then-train" method that treats spiking outputs and binary synapses as continuous probabilities for backprop-compatible training.  
- Uses Gaussian CDF to approximate neuron firing probability and maps trained probabilities to deterministic spiking networks by sampling; ensembles improve performance.  
- Validated on MNIST and deployed on IBM TrueNorth, achieving up to 99.42% accuracy with a 64-network ensemble and strong energy-efficiency (0.268 μJ/image for a single network).  
- Addresses the important practical problem of running high-performance models on power-constrained neuromorphic hardware.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results with actual hardware validation and clear accuracy-vs-energy trade-offs.  
- Novel contribution: unified framework handling spiking neurons, binary synapses, and restrictive connectivity together; includes a data-independent synapse-initialization heuristic.  
- Hardware detail ambiguity: explanation of “0.15 bits per synapse” for the lookup table scheme is unclear and should clarify whether it’s amortized and how derived.  
- Network topology description is fragmented; consolidate layer parameters (R, F, S) for the 5-core and 30-core networks for reproducibility.  
- Input encoding for MNIST into spikes is insufficiently detailed—clarify whether single Poisson spikes per pixel or alternate sampling is used.  
- Training computational cost is unclear; quantify relative training time or overhead compared to standard backprop.  
- Analysis gap: explain why the 5-core network saturates with ensembling while the 30-core network continues to improve—capacity vs. ensemble diversity question.  
- Positioning/novelty wording should be softened to emphasize novelty of the combined constraints rather than implying first-in-kind SNN/backprop methods.  
- Compare and discuss relation to other SNN training methods (e.g., SpikeProp and temporal-domain backprop variants) to clarify trade-offs.  
- Generalizability concern: method shown on TrueNorth—add discussion on adapting core principles to other neuromorphic platforms (e.g., SpiNNaker).  
- Resource limitation: the 64-member ensemble requires 1920 cores (~half a TrueNorth chip); explicitly note this hardware cost as a deployment limitation.
