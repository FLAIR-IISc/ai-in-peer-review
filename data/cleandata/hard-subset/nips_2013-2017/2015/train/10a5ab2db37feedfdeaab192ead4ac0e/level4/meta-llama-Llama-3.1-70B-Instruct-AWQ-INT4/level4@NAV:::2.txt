The authors present a novel approach to reconciling the precision requirements of continuous variables in backpropagation algorithms with the binary synapse constraints of spike-based neural network devices. By treating analog variables as probabilities and sampling them to map the offline-trained neural network to the spiking network chip for deployment, they demonstrate a viable strategy for adapting backpropagation to this paradigm. The proposed method is validated on the MNIST benchmark and mapped onto two distinct architectures, yielding optimized performance figures for both size and accuracy. The manuscript is of exceptional quality, clarity, and originality, with potential applications to other approaches and hardware platforms.

Notably, the authors' review of existing hardware platforms could be further enhanced by including the work of Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and the study available at http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427. However, the paper could benefit from a clearer emphasis on the significance and practical implications of the proposed method, particularly in relation to its potential applications and advantages over alternative approaches, such as those referenced in [9] to [12].

The authors successfully demonstrate the applicability of backpropagation to spiking neural networks with binary synapses, validating their method through the deployment of the trained network on the TrueNorth device. The paper is well-organized and effectively communicates its key findings. While the results are compelling, additional details would be beneficial, including the input conversion process, frequency parameters, shared weight values, and neuron models used in the experiments.
