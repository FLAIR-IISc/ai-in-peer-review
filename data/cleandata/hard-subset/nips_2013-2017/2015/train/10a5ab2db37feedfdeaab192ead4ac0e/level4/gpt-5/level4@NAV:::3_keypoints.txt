- Paper proposes a backpropagation-based training method using spike probabilities and explicitly modeling hardware constraints for spiking neurons and discrete synapses — a timely topic for multi-neuron hardware deployment.  
- The proposed training approach is useful given constraints of emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms.  
- Rephrase end of Section 1 to avoid implying the paper is the first to propose spiking-neuron/reduced-precision synapse training; don’t present the TN demonstration as a novel contribution but as validation.  
- In Section 2, clarify what “0.15 bits per synapse” means.  
- Consolidate the network topology description in a single place.  
- Report how much additional training time is introduced by probabilistic synaptic connection updates.  
- Explain why the single-ensemble result for the 30-core network is so low compared to the 5-core network.  
- Explain why different ensemble results for the 5-core network are so similar.  
- Specify input spike rates during testing and whether inputs are provided as spike trains.  
- Note that Ref. 12 includes training-time constraints (e.g., bias=0), so it is not purely a train-then-constrain approach; fix the typo “approach approach.”  
- Include a discussion or comparison with other spiking backpropagation rules, e.g., SpikeProp.  
- Significance: methods that incorporate hardware constraints are important, but the considered constraints are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize.  
- If reported results rely on a 64-ensemble, clarify that achieving target accuracy may require additional hardware resources.
