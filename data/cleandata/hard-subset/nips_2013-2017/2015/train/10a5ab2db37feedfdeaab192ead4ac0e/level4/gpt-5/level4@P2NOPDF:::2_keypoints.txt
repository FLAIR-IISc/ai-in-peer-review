- Paper goal: map high-precision backpropagation parameters to binary synapses by interpreting analog weights as sampling probabilities for deployment on spiking neuromorphic hardware.
- Method: adapt backpropagation to support probabilistic mapping from analog parameters to sampled binary synapses.
- Validation: trained on MNIST and implemented on TrueNorth in two size/accuracy trade-off configurations with measured on-chip performance.
- Strength: manuscript is well written, clearly structured, and easy to follow.
- Strength: approach is original, methodologically sound, and provides a practical bridge between conventional training and spike-based deployment.
- Strength: experimental validation on TrueNorth and measured results significantly strengthen the contribution.
- Strength: technique appears broadly applicable and may help other neuromorphic platforms.
- Weakness: practical significance and real-world impact are underemphasized; unclear which application classes benefit most.
- Weakness: unclear advantages over alternative strategies (references [9]–[12]) and lacking clear differentiation.
- Weakness: important implementation details are missing, limiting reproducibility and interpretability.
- Missing details (specific): input-to-spike encoding scheme and preprocessing, firing-rate regimes during inference, handling and values of shared weights, and neuron models/parameters on hardware.
- Suggestion: expand discussion of practical significance with concrete scenarios (latency, energy, memory, robustness) where method provides clear benefits.
- Suggestion: provide explicit comparisons with alternatives ([9]–[12]), including trade-offs in accuracy, resource usage, and deployment complexity; include empirical or analytical comparisons if possible.
- Suggestion: add methodological details needed to reproduce/extend the work (precise input-to-spike conversion, typical spike rates/timing assumptions, shared weight implementation details, full TrueNorth neuron specifications).
- Bibliography suggestion: when surveying hardware platforms, cite Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience (2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 to better place the work in context.
- Overall assessment: compelling method with convincing TrueNorth deployment; with clearer significance, differentiation from prior work, and added implementation details, the paper would be a strong contribution.
