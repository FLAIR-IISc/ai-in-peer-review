- Introduces a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities for hardware with spiking neurons and discrete synapses, relevant for mapping deep nets to multi-neuron hardware.
- Proposed training method is valuable given emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints.
- Rephrase Section 1 claims to avoid implying the paper is the first to use spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses; present TN hardware results as validation, not novelty.
- Clarify what "0.15 bits per synapse" means.
- Provide a concise description of the network topology.
- Quantify the impact of probabilistic synaptic connection updates on training time.
- Explain the performance disparity between 30-core and 5-core networks and the consistency across different 5-core ensembles.
- Report input spike rates and explicitly state the nature of the input (e.g., spike trains).
- Correct characterization of Reference 12: training included constraints (e.g., bias=0), so it is not merely a train-then-constrain approach; fix the typo "approach approach".
- Add discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp).
- Note that the considered constraints are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms.
- If results depend on a 64-ensemble approach, disclose that this increases required hardware resources to achieve the reported accuracy.
- Overall assessment: an interesting, well-organized paper presenting a useful training method that incorporates backpropagation while accounting for specific hardware constraints.
