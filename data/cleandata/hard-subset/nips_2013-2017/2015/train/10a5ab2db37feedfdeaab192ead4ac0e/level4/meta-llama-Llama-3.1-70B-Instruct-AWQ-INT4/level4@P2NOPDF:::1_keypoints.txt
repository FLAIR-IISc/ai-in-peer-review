- Strong experimental results (accuracy and power) but concerns about methodological and algorithmic novelty and presentation.  
- Algorithm in Section 3.2 closely resembles/appears as a hybrid of methods in refs [14] and [15]; identical approximations used—must be acknowledged.  
- The "constrain-then-train" approach is not novel—ref [15] already suggested and tested this; relationship should be clarified.  
- Novelty claim (i) in the introduction is misleading because ref [15]’s "expectation backpropagation" handles binary neurons/weights and constrained connectivity.  
- Previous state-of-the-art citations are incorrect: ref [15] achieved the best MNIST results without augmentation under a comparable setup.  
- Conclusion: experimental contributions are strong, but algorithm/methodology novelty is overstated and needs accurate positioning relative to prior work.
