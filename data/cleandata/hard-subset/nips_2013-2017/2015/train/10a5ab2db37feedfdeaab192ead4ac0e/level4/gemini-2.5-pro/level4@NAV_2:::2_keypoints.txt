- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" probabilistic framework to train deep networks for spiking, low-precision neuromorphic hardware.  
- Treats synapses and neuron activations as probabilities during offline training to enable modified backpropagation.  
- Maps the trained probabilistic network to hardware by sampling to create one or more network instances for deployment.  
- Validated on MNIST using IBM TrueNorth, achieving 99.42% accuracy and strong energy-efficiency trade-offs.  
- Strength: original, elegant, and well-written solution that reconciles continuous training with discrete spiking hardware.  
- Strength: significant results with hardware demonstration showing state-of-the-art spiking-network accuracy and efficiency.  
- Strength: method appears generalizable to other neuromorphic platforms with similar constraints.  
- Weakness: needs clearer discussion of broader real-world application impact beyond digit recognition.  
- Weakness: lacks direct empirical comparison to alternative spiking-network training methods (refs [9-12]).  
- Weakness: some experimental details (input encoding, exact neuron parameters, shared synaptic strengths) are insufficient for full reproducibility.  
- Weakness: literature review of neuromorphic platforms is incomplete; should cite additional relevant work (e.g., Qiao et al. 2015; Min et al. 2015).
