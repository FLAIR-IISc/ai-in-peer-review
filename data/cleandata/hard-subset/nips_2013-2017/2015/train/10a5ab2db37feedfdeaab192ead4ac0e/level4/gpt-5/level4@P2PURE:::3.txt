The authors present an interesting paper proposing a backpropagation-based training method that uses spike probabilities and explicitly accounts for the constraints of a specific hardware platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses. This is a timely topic given the current interest in mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware platforms.

Quality: The proposed training method is useful, especially in light of the constraints imposed by emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms.

Clarity: The paper is easy to read. However, the claims at the end of Section 1 should be reworded; as currently phrased, they can be read as implying that this is the first training methodology to employ spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses. I would also avoid presenting the demonstration on the specific hardware platform (TN) as a novel contribution; running the network on TN is best viewed as validation of the training method rather than a standalone novelty.

Specific questions and requests:
- In Section 2, what does “0.15 bits per synapse” mean?
- The network topology is not clearly described in one place; please provide a concise, centralized description.
- How much longer is the training time due to the probabilistic synaptic connection update?
- Why is the result from a single ensemble of the 30-core network so low, especially compared to the 5-core network?
- Why are the results across different ensembles of the 5-core network so similar?
- What are the input spike rates during testing? Is the input a spike train?
- Reference 12 (line 399) includes constraints (e.g., bias = 0) during training, so it is not purely a train-then-constrain approach. Also, there is a typo on that line (“approach approach”).

Originality: Please include a discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop-type rules, such as SpikeProp.

Significance: Developing training methods that consider hardware constraints is of clear interest. The constraints considered during training here are based on the TN architecture, and may not all apply to other platforms. If the reported results rely on a 64-ensemble, achieving the stated accuracy will require commensurately greater hardware resources. Overall, this is an interesting paper on a backpropagation-based training method that takes into account the constraints of a specific platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
