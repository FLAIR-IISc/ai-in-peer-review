- Proposes treating analog variables as probabilities and sampling them to map trained networks onto spike-based chips with binary synapses.
- Adapts backpropagation to accommodate probabilistic/ sampled mapping and validates approach on MNIST.
- Successfully maps trained networks onto two TrueNorth-based architectures optimized for size and accuracy, with on-chip performance figures.
- Manuscript is high-quality, clear, and shows originality with potential applicability across other hardware platforms.
- Reviewer suggests citing additional hardware-relevant works (Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015 and arXiv:1506.05427).
- Significance is under-emphasized: unclear how this enables practical deployment of TrueNorth or which real-world applications it suits.
- Lacks clear comparison/advantages versus alternative methods cited in references [9]–[12].
- Some implementation details are missing: input-to-spike conversion method and frequencies used.
- Missing details on shared weight values and exact neuron models employed.
- Overall results are convincing, but adding the above details would increase the paper’s clarity and impact.
