- Proposes treating analog variables as probabilities sampled to map trained continuous networks to spike-based devices with binary synapses, adapting backpropagation for spiking networks.  
- Validated on MNIST, mapping networks onto two architectures optimized for size and accuracy, with chip-measured performance reported.  
- Manuscript is well-written, clear, original, and potentially useful across other approaches and hardware platforms.  
- Recommend citing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 for a more comprehensive hardware-platform review.  
- Needs stronger emphasis on significance: clarify practical applications and concrete use cases for deploying the TrueNorth chip.  
- Lacks clear comparison to alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]); should discuss relative advantages and limitations.  
- Missing methodological details that hinder reproducibility: how inputs are converted to spikes, spike frequencies, shared weight values, and neuron models used.  
- Overall a significant contribution that, with the suggested revisions, could have a larger impact.
