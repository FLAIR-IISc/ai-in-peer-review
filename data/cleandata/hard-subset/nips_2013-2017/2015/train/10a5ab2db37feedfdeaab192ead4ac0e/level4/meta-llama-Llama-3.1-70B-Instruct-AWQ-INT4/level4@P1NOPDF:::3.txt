The authors present an interesting paper on a backpropagation training method that utilizes spike probabilities and accounts for the hardware constraints of a specific platform, featuring spiking neurons and discrete synapses. This topic is currently of great interest in the context of mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware platforms.

Quality: The proposed training method is particularly useful, especially when considering new multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints. Its ability to adapt to these constraints makes it a valuable contribution to the field.

Clarity: The paper is generally easy to follow. However, the claims made at the end of Section 1 could be rephrased, as they may be misinterpreted to suggest that the paper proposes a novel training methodology employing spiking neurons and synapses with reduced precision for the first time. This is not entirely accurate. Furthermore, demonstrating the network's performance on a specific hardware platform, in this case, the TrueNorth (TN) chip, should be viewed as a validation of the training method rather than a novel feature.

In Section 2, the authors should clarify the meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse." A concise description of the network topology is also lacking. Additionally, the authors should provide information on the increase in training time due to the probabilistic synaptic connection update. The results from a single ensemble of the 30-core network are surprisingly low, especially when compared to the 5-core network. It would be beneficial to discuss the reasons behind this discrepancy. Moreover, the authors should explain why the results of different ensembles of the 5-core network are similar. The spike rates for the inputs during testing should also be provided, and it should be clarified whether the input is a spike train. Finally, Reference 12 (line 399) includes constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, indicating that it is not solely a train-then-constrain approach (correcting the typo "approach approach").

Originality: The authors should provide a discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop-type rules, such as SpikeProp, to better contextualize their work.

Significance: The development of new training methods that consider the constraints of hardware platforms is of great interest. However, the constraints considered during training in this paper are specific to the TN architecture and may not be universally applicable to other platforms. If the results are based on a 64-ensemble, it implies that more hardware resources are required to achieve the desired network accuracy. Overall, the paper presents an interesting training method using backpropagation that takes into account the hardware constraints of a particular platform featuring spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
