- Paper presents a timely backpropagation training method using spike probabilities tailored for spiking-neuron, discrete-synapse hardware.  
- Method is valuable given emerging multi-neuron hardware with inherent constraints.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Claims at end of Section 1 should be rephrased to avoid implying this is the first reduced-precision spiking training method; TN demonstration is validation, not a novel feature.  
- “0.15 bits per synapse” needs clarification.  
- Network topology is not described in one place, making structure hard to grasp quickly.  
- The impact of probabilistic synaptic connection updates on training time is not reported.  
- Unexplained performance drop for a single ensemble of the 30-core network versus 5-core network; conversely, why 5-core ensembles are consistent.  
- Clarify input spike rates during testing and whether inputs are presented as spike trains.  
- Reference 12 indicates constraints are applied during training (not just post-training); fix typographical error (“approach approach”).  
- Add discussion comparing the method to other spiking backpropagation rules (e.g., SpikeProp) to contextualize novelty.  
- Significance is high for hardware-aware training, but constraints are TN-specific and may not generalize.  
- Reliance on a 64-ensemble implies substantial hardware resources are needed to reach target accuracy.
