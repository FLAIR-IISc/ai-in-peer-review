- Proposes training neural networks for neuromorphic hardware by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities so standard backprop can be used.  
- Trained networks can be mapped to neuromorphic hardware by sampling those probabilities to produce one or more deployable networks.  
- Demonstrated strong results on MNIST: 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ/image and 92.7% accuracy at 0.268 μJ/image.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the method and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Concern: novelty of methodology/algorithm is unclear because the approach is similar to prior work (refs. [14], [15]).  
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge similarity to prior work and clearly explain differences and improvements.  
- Claim of novelty for offline backprop-to-spiking/low-bit/constrained-connectivity networks is misleading given ref. [15] proposed a similar approach.  
- Authors must correct their citation of previous SOTA; prior best for binary weights/neurons on MNIST (no augmentation) appears to be ref. [15].  
- Overall: technically sound with strong experimental results but needs to address novelty and citation issues.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong, low-power SOTA experimental results and clear presentation of method/advantages.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear novelty, insufficient differentiation from prior work, and incorrect referencing of prior SOTA.
