- Proposes a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities that accounts for hardware constraints of a spiking-neuron, discrete-synapse platform.  
- Topic is relevant for mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware.  
- Method is useful and valuable for adapting training to hardware limitations.  
- Paper is generally readable but some sections need rewording for clarity.  
- Rephrase claims at end of Section 1 to avoid implying first-time novelty of spiking/reduced-precision training; emphasize adaptability to hardware.  
- The TN hardware run should be presented as validation of the method, not a novel contribution.  
- Section 2 needs clarification: explain the meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse."  
- Provide a clear, concise description of the network topology.  
- Report how much training time increases due to probabilistic synaptic updates.  
- Explain why the single-ensemble 30-core network performance is unexpectedly low compared to the 5-core network.  
- Explain why different ensembles of the 5-core network yield similar results.  
- Provide input spike rates during testing and clarify whether inputs are spike trains.  
- Minor correction: Ref 12 (line 399) includes training constraints (e.g., bias=0), so it is not purely a train-then-constrain approach.  
- Strengthen originality by discussing/comparing to other spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- Significance: work is important for hardware-aware training, but constraints are TN-specific and achieving target accuracy may require more hardware resources (e.g., 64 ensembles).
