This paper presents an interesting approach to training neural networks with binary weights and neurons. While the authors demonstrate strong experimental results in terms of accuracy and power consumption, there are several concerns regarding the novelty and presentation of their methodology and algorithm.

Firstly, the authors should acknowledge that the algorithm presented in Section 3.2 bears a strong resemblance to the methods described in references [14] and [15]. Specifically, the final form of their algorithm appears to be a hybrid of these two approaches, derived using identical approximations. A clear mention of this connection would strengthen the paper by providing a more accurate representation of the state-of-the-art.

Furthermore, the discussion section gives the impression that the "constrain-then-train" methodology, which involves learning a weight distribution and then sampling from it to produce a fixed network ensemble at test time, is a novel contribution. However, this approach was already suggested and tested in reference [15]. The authors should clarify the relationship between their work and this existing research to avoid any potential misrepresentation.

Additionally, the novelty claim (i) presented in the last paragraph of the introduction is misleading. Reference [15] describes an "expectation backpropagation" algorithm, which is a backpropagation update rule using expectation propagation, specifically designed for binary neurons with binary weights. This algorithm can easily accommodate constrained connectivity, making the authors' claim less novel than presented.

Moreover, the cited previous state-of-the-art results for binary weights and neurons are incorrect. To the best of my knowledge, the algorithm presented in reference [15] achieved the best results on the MNIST dataset (http://arxiv.org/pdf/1503.03562v3.pdf) without data augmentation, which is consistent with the experimental setup in this paper.

In light of the authors' feedback, I would like to emphasize that this paper presents strong and novel experimental results. However, both the general methodology and the algorithm itself are not as novel as claimed in the paper. By addressing these concerns and providing a more accurate representation of the state-of-the-art, the authors can significantly strengthen their contribution.
