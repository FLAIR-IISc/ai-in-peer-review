- Section 3.2 must acknowledge the strong similarity to [14] and [15]; the presented algorithm is essentially a hybrid of those methods using the same approximations.  
- The paper incorrectly implies the “constrain-then-train” approach is novel, whereas this methodology was proposed and evaluated in [15].  
- The novelty claim (i) is inaccurate: expectation backpropagation [15] already provides a backprop/EP update for binary neurons and weights and can be adapted to constrained connectivity.  
- The reported comparison to prior state-of-the-art is flawed: the best prior results for binary weights/neurons were achieved by [15] on MNIST without the data augmentation used here.  
- After revisions: the experimental results (accuracy and power consumption) are strong, but the general methodology and algorithmic contributions are overstated in novelty.
