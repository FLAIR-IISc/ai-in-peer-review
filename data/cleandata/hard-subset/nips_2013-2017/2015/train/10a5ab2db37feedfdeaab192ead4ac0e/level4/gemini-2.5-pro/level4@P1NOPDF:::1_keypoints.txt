1. Overall: strong experimental accuracy and power savings, but methodological novelty is overstated and must be corrected for proper context.

2. Section 3.2: The proposed update rule closely resembles and appears to be a hybrid of methods from refs [14] and [15]; this overlap must be explicitly acknowledged and discussed.

3. Discussion paragraph: Presenting the "constrain-then-train" approach as novel is incorrect—this methodology (learn a weight distribution, sample to produce a fixed ensemble) was previously proposed and evaluated in [15].

4. Introduction novelty claim (i): Misleading—Expectation Backpropagation from [15] already provides backprop updates for binary weights and neurons and can be applied to constrained connectivity.

5. Baselines/comparison: The paper omits key prior results—particularly the strongest MNIST results for binary weights/neurons from [15]—and should include them for an accurate performance comparison.
