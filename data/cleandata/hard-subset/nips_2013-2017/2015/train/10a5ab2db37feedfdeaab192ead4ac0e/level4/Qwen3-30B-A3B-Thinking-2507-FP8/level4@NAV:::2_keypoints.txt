- Novel approach: treat analog variables as probabilities and sample them to transition offline-trained networks to spike-based hardware with binary synapses.  
- Propose an adapted backpropagation algorithm compatible with this probabilistic sampling; validated on MNIST.  
- Map the trained network onto two hardware architectures optimized for size and accuracy and report measured performance from a physical chip.  
- Manuscript is high quality, exceptionally clear, original, and potentially applicable to other hardware platforms.  
- Significance underemphasized: unclear how the method specifically enables practical TrueNorth deployment or identifies concrete application domains.  
- Advantages over alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]) are not sufficiently justified.  
- Critical implementation details are missing: input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, shared weight values, and neuron model specifications.  
- Recommend citing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 when discussing hardware platforms.
