- Algorithm in Section 3.2 strongly resembles and appears to be a hybrid of methods from refs [14] and [15], sharing identical approximations — authors should acknowledge this.
- The discussion's opening may falsely imply that the "constrain-then-train" approach is novel, but this method was previously proposed and evaluated in [15].
- The claim of novelty in the introduction (novelty (i)) is misleading: expectation backprop in [15] is a backprop update using expectation propagation for binary neurons/weights and can be adapted to constrained connectivity.
- The paper’s cited state-of-the-art claims seem incorrect: the best prior results for binary weights/neurons on MNIST (no augmentation) appear to be those from [15].
- After author feedback: experimental results are strong (accuracy and power improvements), but the overall methodology and algorithm are less novel than asserted.
