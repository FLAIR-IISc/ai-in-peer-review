1) Section 3.2 algorithm closely resembles and is essentially a hybrid of methods [14] and [15], derived using identical approximations—this should be explicitly acknowledged.

2) The Discussion’s first paragraph wrongly implies the “constrain-then-train” approach is novel, but it was already proposed and tested in [15].

3) Novelty claim (i) in the Introduction is misleading: the “expectation backpropagation” algorithm in [15] is a backpropagation update based on expectation propagation for binary neurons/weights and can support constrained connectivity.

4) The cited prior state of the art is incorrect; the previous best results for binary weights and neurons were achieved by [15] on MNIST without data augmentation.

5) Edited conclusion: the paper presents strong experimental results (accuracy and power), but the methodology and algorithm are not as novel as claimed.
