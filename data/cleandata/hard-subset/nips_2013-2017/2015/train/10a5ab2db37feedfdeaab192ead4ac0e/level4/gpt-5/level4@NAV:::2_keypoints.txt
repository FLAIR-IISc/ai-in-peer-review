- Proposes reconciling high-precision continuous variables in backprop with binary-synapse spiking hardware by treating analog variables as probabilities sampled for chip deployment.
- Reinterprets analog network parameters as probabilities and samples them to transfer offline-trained networks to spiking chips.
- Adapts backpropagation to work with the probabilistic/binary-synapse sampling scheme.
- Validates the approach on the MNIST benchmark and maps the trained model onto two chip architectures optimized respectively for size and for accuracy.
- Reports performance figures measured directly on the TrueNorth chip.
- Manuscript is high quality, clear, original, and potentially beneficial to other methods and hardware platforms.
- Reviewer requests citing Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015, and arXiv:1506.05427 when discussing available hardware.
- Significance is underemphasized: unclear how the method enables practical TrueNorth deployments and which specific applications would benefit.
- Unclear whether and to what extent this method outperforms alternative approaches cited in [9]–[12].
- Important experimental details are omitted (input-to-spike conversion, firing rates, shared weight values, neuron models), which weakens reproducibility.
