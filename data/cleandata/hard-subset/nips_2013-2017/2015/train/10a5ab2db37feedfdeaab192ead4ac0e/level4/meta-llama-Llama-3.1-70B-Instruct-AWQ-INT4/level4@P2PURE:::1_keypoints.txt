- Algorithm in Sec. 3.2 strongly resembles methods in refs [14] and [15]; the final algorithm appears to be a hybrid of those using identical approximations and should be acknowledged.

- The discussion's opening may mislead readers into thinking a novel "constrain-then-train" method is introduced, but this approach was previously proposed and tested in [15]; clarify the relationship.

- Novelty claim (i) is misleading because [15]’s "expectation backpropagation" already provides a backprop update using expectation propagation for binary neurons/weights and can accommodate constrained connectivity.

- The paper’s citation of prior state-of-the-art is likely incorrect: [15] reportedly achieved the best results for binary weights/neurons on MNIST without augmentation, similar to this paper’s setup.

- The authors have agreed to address these citation and novelty concerns.

- While experimental results on accuracy and power consumption are strong, the general methodology and algorithm are not as novel as claimed.
