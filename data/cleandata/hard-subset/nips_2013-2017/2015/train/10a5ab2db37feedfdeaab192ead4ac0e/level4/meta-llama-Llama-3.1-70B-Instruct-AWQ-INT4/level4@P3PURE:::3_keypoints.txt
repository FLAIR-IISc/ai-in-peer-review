- Paper proposes a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities that accounts for hardware constraints of a multi-neuron spiking platform.  
- Topic is timely and relevant for mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware.  
- Proposed training method is useful for emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints.  
- Paper is generally easy to read but some claims need rewording to avoid implying first-ever contribution.  
- Demonstration on the TN hardware should be framed as validation of the method, not as a novel feature.  
- Section 2 requires clarification: the meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse" is unclear.  
- Network topology is not described clearly in a single place, making the architecture hard to follow.  
- Authors should report how much additional training time is caused by the probabilistic synaptic updates.  
- Results: a single-ensemble 30-core network performs surprisingly poorly compared to the 5-core network—this discrepancy needs explanation.  
- It is unclear why different ensembles of the 5-core network yield similar results; authors should clarify.  
- Provide spike rates for inputs during testing and explicitly state whether inputs are spike trains.  
- Reference 12 involves training with constraints (e.g., bias=0), so it is not a simple train-then-constrain approach; also fix the "approach approach" typo.  
- Compare or discuss related spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp) to better position the novelty of the method.  
- The constraints considered are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms.  
- If results rely on a 64-ensemble, this implies increased hardware resources are required to reach target accuracy.
