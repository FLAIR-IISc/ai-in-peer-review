- Proposes treating analog variables as probabilities and sampling them to map offline-trained networks onto binary-synapse spiking hardware.  
- Adapts backpropagation to work with the probabilistic/spiking mapping approach.  
- Validates the method on MNIST and demonstrates successful mapping to the TrueNorth chip.  
- Maps the trained network onto two architectures optimized separately for size and accuracy.  
- Reports performance metrics obtained directly from the chip.  
- Manuscript is high quality, very clear, well-organized, and concise.  
- Work is original and could benefit other approaches and hardware platforms.  
- Reviewer requests citing Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015, and arXiv:1506.05427 when discussing alternative hardware.  
- Paper does not sufficiently emphasize the overall significance and practical impact of the work.  
- Unclear how the method facilitates real-world deployment of the TrueNorth chip and which specific applications would benefit.  
- Advantages of this method over alternative approaches (references [9]–[12]) are not explicitly compared.  
- Important technical details are missing: input-to-spike conversion process, spike frequencies used, shared weight values, and neuron models.
