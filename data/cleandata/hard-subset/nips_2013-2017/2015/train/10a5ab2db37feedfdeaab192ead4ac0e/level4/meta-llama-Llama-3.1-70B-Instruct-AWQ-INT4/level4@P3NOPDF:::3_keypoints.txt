- Proposes a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities tailored to hardware with spiking neurons and discrete synapses (TrueNorth).

- Training method is useful for adapting deep networks to multi-neuron hardware constraints.

- Paper is well-written but Section 1 contains claims that should be rephrased to avoid overstating novelty.

- Running the network on TrueNorth should be presented as validation of the method, not as a novel contribution.

- Clarify what "0.15 bits per synapse" means.

- Provide a single, clear description of the network topology.

- Discuss how the probabilistic synaptic connection update impacts training time.

- Explain why results from the 30-core ensemble are surprisingly low compared to the 5-core network.

- Explain why different ensembles of the 5-core network give consistent results.

- Report input spike rates during testing and clarify whether inputs are presented as spike trains.

- Fix typo on line 399 ("approach approach") and note that Reference 12 imposes training constraints (e.g., bias=0), so it is not a pure train-then-constrain approach.

- Compare and discuss related spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp) to establish originality.

- Discuss generalizability of the approach beyond the TrueNorth architecture and whether the considered constraints apply to other platforms.

- Acknowledge and discuss the hardware-resource vs. accuracy trade-off (e.g., using a 64-ensemble to reach target accuracy).

- Overall assessment: interesting and valuable contribution contingent on the requested clarifications and additional comparisons.
