- Proposes treating analog variables as probabilities and sampling them to map offline-trained networks to spiking neuromorphic hardware.  
- Adapts backpropagation to be compatible with binary synapses and spiking neurons for deployment.  
- Validated method on MNIST and mapped networks onto two TrueNorth architectures optimized for size and accuracy.  
- Performance figures were measured directly on-chip, demonstrating practical deployment.  
- Manuscript is high quality, very clearly written, and well-structured.  
- Work is original and potentially useful for other methods and hardware platforms.  
- Suggests citing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 when reviewing hardware platforms.  
- Significance could be emphasized more—unclear how the method facilitates real-world TrueNorth deployment or outperforms alternative approaches.  
- Achieves strong results: state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy for spiking networks and excellent TrueNorth power efficiency.  
- Demonstrates a full pipeline from training to hardware deployment, strengthening credibility.  
- Scope is limited: evaluation only on MNIST and a single hardware platform (TrueNorth).  
- Missing implementation details that hinder reproducibility (input-to-spike conversion and frequencies, shared weight values, specific neuron models).
