- Proposes backprop-based training for spiking neuromorphic hardware by representing spikes and synaptic on/off states as continuous probabilities and mapping sampled networks to TrueNorth with ensemble averaging.

- Enforces TrueNorth-like constrained connectivity via structured sliding-window topology and respects low-precision synapses during training.

- Reports MNIST results: 99.42% with 64-network ensemble (108 μJ/image) and 92.7% with a single network (0.268 μJ/image) in real time.

- Strength: The constrain-then-train strategy is principled, avoiding post hoc discretization and enabling direct hardware mapping.

- Strength: Probabilistic forward model (Gaussian approximation of weighted Bernoulli inputs) and derived gradients are reasonable, with a thoughtful initialization to decorrelate core outputs.

- Strength: Empirical results show strong energy–accuracy trade-offs and a clean TrueNorth hardware mapping.

- Concern: Gaussian and independence assumptions, and dropping the σ^−3 term in gradients, need empirical justification (sensitivity analyses/ablations).

- Concern: Quantify training-time overhead from learning synaptic probabilities (wall-clock time, convergence vs. dense/continuous baseline).

- Concern: Consolidate all architecture parameters (R, F, S per layer, neurons per core, class-neuron allocation) in one place for reproducibility.

- Concern: Explain ensemble behavior — why the 30-core single-member is weaker than the 5-core, and why varying ensemble sizes for the 5-core give similar accuracy; discuss variance vs. bias effects.

- Concern: Clarify input encoding at test time — single 1 ms Bernoulli sample vs. spike trains, and sensitivity to encoding duration.

- Concern: Explain hardware accounting statement “0.15 bits per synapse” (derivation and amortization given axon types and per-neuron tables).

- Concern: Reword novelty claims to clearly state what is new (combination of spiking neurons, sub-trinary synapses, constrained connectivity, probabilistic mapping) and treat TrueNorth demonstration as validation.

- Related-work note: Ref. 12 includes training-time constraints; adjust discussion and fix the “approach approach” typo.

- Clarity suggestion: Add a self-contained figure/table with complete topology and mapping parameters.

- Clarity suggestion: Give an intuitive explanation of the initialization objective (minimizing column inner products) and how −1/1 assignments and axon-type balancing achieve it.

- Clarity suggestion: Include a short paragraph justifying probabilistic approximations and their failure modes (low fan-in, strong input correlations).

- Originality: Combining probabilistic spikes/synapses with backprop under strict connectivity/precision is compelling; compare explicitly to SpikeProp, surrogate-gradient SNNs, and binary probabilistic backprop methods.

- Significance: Bridging backprop training with energy-efficient deployment is important and the μJ-level, real-time results are impactful for embedded applications.

- Significance concern: Discuss generality beyond TrueNorth (other synapse precisions, neuron models, routing limits) and which parts are platform-agnostic.

- Significance concern: Quantify hardware/latency implications of ensembles (e.g., 64 members → 1920 cores) and present accuracy vs. ensemble size trade-offs to guide designers.

- Pros: Hardware-aware training framework, strong energy–accuracy trade-offs, real-time silicon results, thoughtful initialization and topology design.

- Cons: Limited analysis of probabilistic-gradient approximations, incomplete topology/training/encoding details, novelty claims need scoping, heavy reliance on ensembles without detailed resource analysis.

- Recommendation: Solid contribution; accept with revisions addressing claims, related work, probabilistic assumption analyses, consolidated architecture details, and training/ensemble trade-offs.
