- Proposes mapping offline-trained continuous weights as probabilities to sample binary synapses for spiking hardware, adapts backprop for this stochastic mapping, validates on MNIST, and deploys on two TrueNorth configurations with hardware-measured performance.

- Manuscript is high-quality, well-structured, clearly written; method is original and potentially applicable beyond TrueNorth; results are convincing.

- Major weakness: broader significance not sufficiently highlighted—paper does not explain practical deployment scenarios or which applications benefit most.

- Major weakness: lacks clear comparison or quantification of advantages over alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]).

- Reproducibility concern: missing details on how inputs are converted into spikes.

- Reproducibility concern: spike frequencies used during operation are not specified.

- Reproducibility concern: values of the shared weights are not provided.

- Reproducibility concern: specific neuron models implemented on the chip are not described.

- Recommendation: include citations to Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 when reviewing hardware platforms.
