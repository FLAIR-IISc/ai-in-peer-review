- Proposed backpropagation training for spiking networks tailored to neuromorphic hardware is valuable and practical.  
- Rephrase novelty claims: emphasize the specific method for hardware-constrained backprop rather than being "first" to use spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses.  
- Present the TrueNorth demonstration as validation of the training method, not the primary novel contribution.  
- Clarify what “0.15 bits per synapse” means for weight resolution and its effect on performance.  
- Consolidate the network topology description into a single clear location.  
- Quantify computational overhead of probabilistic synaptic updates versus deterministic training (training time impact).  
- Explain the low accuracy of a single-ensemble 30-core network compared to the 5-core network.  
- Investigate why results are consistent across different ensembles of the 5-core network.  
- Provide input details: spike rates during testing and whether inputs are presented as spike trains or converted from a rate code.  
- Fix editorial issues (typo at line 399) and correct the discussion of Ref. 12 to note its training-time constraints (e.g., bias=0).  
- Broaden related-work discussion and compare the method to other spiking backprop algorithms (e.g., SpikeProp), highlighting strengths/weaknesses.  
- Discuss generalizability of the TN-driven constraints to other neuromorphic platforms and acknowledge hardware cost of relying on a 64-ensemble configuration.  
- Overall: paper is well-written and significant; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the above clarifications and comparisons.
