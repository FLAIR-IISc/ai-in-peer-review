- Proposes training spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable backpropagation for spiking networks and low-precision hardware compatibility.
- Timely and relevant topic for deploying deep learning on resource-constrained embedded neuromorphic systems.
- Technical approach is sound and well-motivated, enabling direct mapping to neuromorphic hardware.
- Experiments on MNIST show compelling accuracy and energy-efficiency results on TrueNorth.
- Lacks detailed analysis of how probabilistic synaptic updates affect training convergence speed and stability.
- Justification for specific hyperparameter choices (learning rate schedule, dropout) is insufficient.
- Needs stronger comparison with alternative spiking training methods (e.g., SpikeProp) and a more comprehensive literature review.
- Claims of being “first” are overstated and should be tempered throughout the paper.
- Section 2’s explanation of “0.15 bits per synapse” is unclear and needs clarification.
- Network topology description is fragmented; consolidate into one section with a clear diagram.
- Discussion of results is unclear, notably the lower performance of single-member 30-core network versus 5-core and why 5-core ensembles behave similarly.
- Missing details on input spike representation used during testing (spike rates, spike trains).
- Paper is generally well-written but contains a minor typo ("approach approach" on line 399).
- Demonstration on TrueNorth is valuable validation but not inherently novel; core innovation is the training method.
- Concern about generalizability to other neuromorphic platforms with different constraints.
- Scalability concerns due to need for a 64-member ensemble to reach high accuracy; single-network performance is weak.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical problem, viable hardware-mappable training methodology, strong energy-efficiency results, clear technical motivation.
- Arguments against acceptance: novelty claims overstated, insufficient comparisons to prior spiking methods, limited generalizability discussion, ensemble scalability issues, and missing methodological details.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions focusing on clarifying technical details, strengthening originality/comparisons, and discussing limitations and future directions.
