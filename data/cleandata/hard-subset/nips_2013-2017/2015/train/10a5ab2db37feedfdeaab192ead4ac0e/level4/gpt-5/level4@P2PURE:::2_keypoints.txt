- Proposes treating analog backpropagation variables as probabilities and sampling them to map offline-trained networks to binary-synapse spiking hardware.  
- Adapts backpropagation to support the probabilistic mapping from analog weights to binary synapses.  
- Validates the approach on the MNIST benchmark.  
- Maps the trained network onto two TrueNorth-optimized architectures: one size-optimized and one accuracy-optimized.  
- Reports on-chip performance measurements from the TrueNorth device.  
- Manuscript is very high quality, clear, and the contribution is original and broadly useful for other hardware platforms.  
- Reviewer recommends adding citations to Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 when surveying existing hardware.  
- The paper does not sufficiently emphasize the significance or practical impact for deploying TrueNorth in real applications.  
- Unclear how the method compares in advantage to alternative approaches (e.g., those cited in [9]–[12]).  
- Important experimental details are missing: how inputs are converted into spikes, the firing rates/frequencies used, shared weight values, and the neuron models.
