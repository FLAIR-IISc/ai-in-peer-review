- Section 3.2’s algorithm closely mirrors prior work ([14], [15]); the final method is effectively a hybrid of those approaches using the same approximations.  
- The “constrain-then-train” paradigm claimed as novel was already proposed and empirically evaluated in [15].  
- Novelty claim (i) is misleading because [15] already provides a backpropagation-style (expectation backpropagation) update for binary neurons and weights and can handle constrained connectivity.  
- The paper’s prior state-of-the-art claims are incorrect: [15] reportedly achieved the strongest MNIST results for binary weights and neurons under the same (no augmentation) evaluation protocol.  
- Revised assessment: experimental results are strong and show novel accuracy/power-efficiency benefits, but the overall methodology and training algorithm are less novel than claimed.
