- Paper proposes training networks with continuous weights/activations constrained as spike/synapse probabilities for natural mapping to neuromorphic hardware, demonstrating strong MNIST results (99.42% ensemble64; 0.268 μJ/image ensemble1 on TrueNorth).  
- Well-motivated problem that correctly identifies the incompatibility between backpropagation’s continuous values and spiking neuromorphic hardware.  
- Claims to be first to combine backprop, spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity, but reviewer disputes this novelty.  

Strengths:
- Strong experimental results showing high accuracy and excellent energy efficiency.  
- Clear motivation and well-defined problem statement.  
- Practical implementation on TrueNorth hardware, highlighting real-world applicability.  
- Well-written and logically organized manuscript.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited novelty: core algorithm in Sec. 3.2 resembles prior work (notably [14,15]) and needs clearer attribution and differentiation.  
- Misleading claims of novelty in the introduction and discussion (claims contradicted by ref [15]).  
- Inaccurate or unfair comparisons to prior state-of-the-art: reported baselines (e.g., refs [12], [15]) use different methodologies (data augmentation, etc.) and are not directly comparable.  
- Insufficient discussion of the approximation in Equation 4 and its potential impact on accuracy.  

Overall assessment:
- Valuable and practically relevant contribution with encouraging experiments, but overstated novelty and inadequate acknowledgment of related work weaken the paper.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Impressive experimental accuracy and energy-efficiency results.  
- Practical relevance for energy-efficient embedded systems.  
- Clear presentation and implementation details.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Overclaimed novelty and insufficient citation/acknowledgment of closely related prior work.  
- Potentially misleading comparisons to prior results.  
- Limited analysis of algorithmic approximations.

Recommendation (accept with minor revisions):
- Explicitly acknowledge and differentiate from [14,15].  
- Revise novelty claims in introduction and discussion to accurately reflect prior work.  
- Provide a fair, rigorous comparison to ref [15], clarifying methodological differences.  
- Expand discussion of Equation 4’s approximation and its effects on accuracy.
