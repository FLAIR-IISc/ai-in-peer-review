- Proposes a backpropagation-based training method that uses spike probabilities and handles discrete synapses for a specific spiking-neuron hardware platform.  
- Method is valuable given constraints of emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms.  
- Paper is well-written, but claims at the end of Section 1 should be rephrased to avoid implying this is the first-ever spiking/discrete-synapse training method; running on TN hardware should be framed as validation, not novelty.  
- Clarify the meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse" in Section 2.  
- Provide a single, comprehensive description of the network topology.  
- Report how much additional training time is incurred by probabilistic synaptic connection updates.  
- Explain why the single-ensemble 30-core network performs much worse than the 5-core network, and why results across different 5-core ensembles are so consistent.  
- Provide input spike rates during testing and state whether inputs are represented as spike trains.  
- Note that Ref. 12 applies constraints during training (e.g., bias=0), so it is not purely a "train-then-constrain" approach; fix the "approach approach" typo.  
- Include comparisons or discussion of other spiking backpropagation methods (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- Constraints targeted are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize across platforms.  
- Achieving reported accuracy may require substantial hardware (e.g., a 64-ensemble configuration), raising resource concerns.
