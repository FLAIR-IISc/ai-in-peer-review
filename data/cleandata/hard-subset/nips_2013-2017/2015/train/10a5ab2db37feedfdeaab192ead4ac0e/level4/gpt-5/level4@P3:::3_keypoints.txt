- Proposes backprop-based training for spiking neuromorphic hardware by treating spikes and binary synapses as probabilities in [0,1] and optimizing a differentiable surrogate.
- Trained probabilistic networks are sampled into binary spiking networks and combined via ensemble averaging; hardware-aware initialization balances synapses.
- Demonstrated on IBM TrueNorth with MNIST: 99.42% (64-member ensemble) and 92.7% (single network) at very low energy per inference.
- Positioned between ANN-to-SNN conversion methods and direct constrained/binary training methods, using a Gaussian CLT approximation for gradient flow.
- Uses a static probabilistic neuron model and Gaussian approximation rather than temporal SpikeProp-style gradients.
- Emphasizes TrueNorth-specific constraints (constrained connectivity, per-core weight sharing, axon types) in training and initialization.
- Probabilistic surrogate and Gaussian approximation are technically reasonable given large fan-in, but need quantitative checks (e.g., ablations).
- Empirics on MNIST are solid and include energy-accuracy trade-offs, but several methodological details and analyses are missing for robustness/generalization.
- TrueNorth deployment meaningfully validates hardware applicability; energy measurements appear fairly reported.
- Novelty claims should be restrained and rephrased to emphasize the specific combination of spiking neurons, sub-trinary precision, constrained connectivity, and hardware-consistent training.
- Running on TrueNorth should be framed as validation rather than a primary novelty claim.
- Consolidate network topology description into one self-contained specification (R, F, S, cores/neurons per layer, fan-in, axon usage, pooling).
- Fix minor clarity issues (e.g., duplicated phrase "approach approach").
- Clarify the "0.15 bits per synapse" amortization calculation for axon-type lookup tables and sharing per core.
- Report additional training cost details: wall-clock time, epochs-to-convergence, and compute resources compared to dense ANN training.
- Provide a CLT sanity check (e.g., histogram of summed inputs with fitted Gaussian and failure modes at low fan-in).
- Ablate the gradient approximation by showing results with and without the σ^3 term to justify dropping it.
- Quantify the effect of the hardware-aware weight/sign-balancing initialization versus naive random initialization on convergence and final accuracy.
- Explain ensemble behavior: why single-network accuracy for the 30-core model is much lower than 5-core ensembles, and include mean±std over multiple samplings per ensemble size.
- Analyze 5-core ensemble accuracies across sizes (bias/variance decomposition, capacity limits) to explain similar performance.
- Clarify input encoding: whether testing uses a single 1 ms Bernoulli spike per pixel or longer spike trains, and report effective input spike rates and per-layer firing rates.
- Discuss portability: how axon types, blockwise weight sharing, and 256 fan-in constraints affect transfer to other neuromorphic platforms and what minimal assumptions are needed.
- Compare explicitly to SpikeProp-like methods and expectation backprop for binary networks, highlighting differences in neuron models, gradient estimators, and connectivity constraints.
- Nuance the comparison to "train-then-constrain" approaches (e.g., Ref. 12) that also enforce constraints during training.
- Originality: the combined approach (probabilistic backprop surrogate + spiking + low-precision + hardware constraints) is notable but novelty should be carefully scoped against prior binary/spiking work.
- Significance: compelling accuracy/energy trade-offs for low-power inference, but reliance on large ensembles and MNIST-only evaluation limit broader impact.
- Pros: clear hardware-aware method, strong energy-accuracy trade-offs on real hardware, practical initialization and mapping, scalable training loop.
- Cons: novelty overclaimed, limited empirical breadth (MNIST only), heavy ensemble reliance, missing analyses (training cost, approximations, initialization effects, ensemble variance, full topology, input encoding).
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending clarified claims and additional analyses addressing the questions above.
