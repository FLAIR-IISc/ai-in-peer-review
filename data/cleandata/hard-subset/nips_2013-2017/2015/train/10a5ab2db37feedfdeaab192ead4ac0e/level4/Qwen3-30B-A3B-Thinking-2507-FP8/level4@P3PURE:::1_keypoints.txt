1) Section 3.2 algorithm is conceptually identical to methods in refs [14] and [15], using the same approximations and forming a hybrid — this must be acknowledged.

2) The Discussion incorrectly presents the "constrain-then-train" approach as novel, whereas it was previously introduced and validated in ref [15].

4) The cited prior state-of-the-art is incorrect: ref [15] (arXiv:1503.03562v3) reportedly achieved the best MNIST accuracy for binary weights/neurons without augmentation and was not acknowledged as such.

5) Overall: despite strong accuracy and power results, the core methodology and algorithm do not have the claimed novelty.
