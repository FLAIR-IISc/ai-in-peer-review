- Presents a novel probabilistic sampling approach to map offline-trained continuous networks onto spiking hardware with binary synapses.  
- Adapts backpropagation to be compatible with probabilistic sampling for deployment on spike-based chips.  
- Validated method on MNIST and successfully mapped the network onto the TrueNorth chip with empirical hardware results.  
- Optimized for both model size and accuracy in hardware deployment.  
- Manuscript is well-written, clearly structured, and demonstrates significant technical merit and originality.  
- Claims broader applicability for neuromorphic computing beyond the specific hardware used.  
- Fails to contextualize the practical significance or specify real-world target use cases (e.g., edge computing, low-power sensors).  
- Lacks comparison against alternative methods (particularly references [9]–[12]) to demonstrate tangible advantages (energy, overhead, accuracy).  
- Omits critical implementation details limiting reproducibility: input spike encoding and parameters.  
- Omits spike frequency ranges used during inference, hindering performance assessment.  
- Does not explain how shared weight values are managed across spiking neurons.  
- Omits exact neuron model and parameters (e.g., leaky integrate-and-fire specifics).  
- Missing citation of relevant prior work (Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015 and arXiv:1506.05427), weakening literature positioning.  
- Requests the authors explicitly address application relevance, comparative advantages over existing binary-synapse techniques, and provide full implementation details to enable replication.  
- Overall: conceptually elegant and promising, but needs clearer significance, comparative analysis, and technical transparency to strengthen impact.
