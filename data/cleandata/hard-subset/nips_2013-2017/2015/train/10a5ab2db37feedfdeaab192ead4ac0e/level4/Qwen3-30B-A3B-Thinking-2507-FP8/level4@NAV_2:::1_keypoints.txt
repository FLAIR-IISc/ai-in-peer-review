- Paper proposes a probabilistic training methodology to enable backpropagation on neuromorphic hardware with spiking neurons and low-precision synapses.  
- Continuous-valued synapses and neuron outputs represent probabilities, allowing standard backpropagation and direct hardware mapping via ensemble sampling.  
- Demonstrated on TrueNorth: 99.42% accuracy with 64-ensemble at 108 μJ/image, and 92.7% with 1-ensemble at 0.268 μJ/image.  
- Strength: Empirical results are compelling — highest reported spiking-network accuracy and best energy efficiency for real-time MNIST on neuromorphic hardware.  
- Strength: Probabilistic training framework is technically sound, well-motivated, and maps clearly to hardware constraints.  
- Strength: Network architecture (progressive mixing topology) effectively respects TrueNorth connectivity limits.  
- Weakness: Training algorithm is structurally similar to expectation backpropagation (Soudry et al., 2014) and stochastic bit-stream networks (Zhao et al., 1996) using identical Gaussian gradient approximations, but this similarity is not acknowledged.  
- Weakness: The claimed novelty of the “constrain-then-train” methodology is incorrect — Soudry et al. explicitly use ensemble sampling from a probabilistic weight distribution to produce fixed test networks.  
- Weakness: Intro claim of novelty is overstated; Soudry et al. already demonstrated backprop for binary neurons/weights with constrained connectivity.  
- Weakness: Incorrect state-of-the-art citation — Soudry et al. achieved 97.88% on MNIST with binary weights/neurons without augmentation, matching the paper’s setup.  
- Recommendation: Paper is valuable and experimentally rigorous and should be accepted if the authors revise claims of novelty and properly cite prior probabilistic backpropagation and ensemble-sampling work.
