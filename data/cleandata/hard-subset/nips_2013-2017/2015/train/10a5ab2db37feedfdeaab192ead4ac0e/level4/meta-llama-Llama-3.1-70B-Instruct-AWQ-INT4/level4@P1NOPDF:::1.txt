1) The authors should acknowledge that the algorithm presented in section 3.2 bears a strong resemblance to the methods described in references [14] and [15]. Specifically, the final form of the algorithm appears to be a hybrid of these two approaches, derived using identical approximations. A clear mention of this connection would strengthen the paper by providing a more accurate representation of the work's origins.

2) The first paragraph of the discussion may give readers the impression that the "constrain-then-train" methodology, which involves learning a weight distribution and then sampling from it to produce a fixed network ensemble at test time, is a novel contribution. However, this approach was previously suggested and tested in reference [15]. To avoid confusion, the authors should clarify the relationship between their work and this existing research.

3) The novelty claim (i) presented in the last paragraph of the introduction is also somewhat misleading. The "expectation backpropagation" algorithm described in reference [15] is a backpropagation update rule that utilizes expectation propagation, specifically designed for binary neurons with binary weights. Notably, this algorithm can easily accommodate constrained connectivity. The authors should revise their claim to more accurately reflect the state of existing research in this area.

4) The paper's citations of previous state-of-the-art results for binary weights and neurons appear to be incorrect. To the best of my knowledge, the algorithm presented in reference [15] achieved the best results for binary weights and neurons, as demonstrated on the MNIST dataset (http://arxiv.org/pdf/1503.03562v3.pdf) without data augmentation, similar to the approach taken in this paper.

After considering the authors' feedback, I would like to reiterate that this paper presents strong and novel experimental results, particularly in terms of accuracy and power consumption. However, both the general methodology and the algorithm itself are not as novel as claimed in the paper.
