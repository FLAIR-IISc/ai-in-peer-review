The authors propose a novel approach to reconcile the need for high precision of continuous variables in backpropagation algorithms with the desire to use spike-based neural network devices with binary synapses. They introduce the idea of considering the analog variables as probabilities that are sampled to map the neural network used off-line for training to the spiking network chip used for deployment of the application. This approach is innovative and has the potential to be useful for other approaches and other hardware platforms.

The manuscript is well-structured, clear, and concise. The authors provide a thorough explanation of their method, including the adaptation of the backpropagation algorithm to be compatible with the probabilistic representation of the neural network. The validation of the method on the MNIST benchmark is convincing, and the performance figures measured from the chip demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity. The authors do an excellent job of explaining complex concepts in a way that is easy to understand. The text is well-organized, and the figures and tables are informative and helpful.

The work is also original, and the authors provide a clear contribution to the field. The idea of using probabilities to represent analog variables is novel, and the adaptation of the backpropagation algorithm to work with these probabilities is a significant achievement.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One of the main limitations is that the significance of the work is not clearly highlighted. While the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the MNIST benchmark, it is not clear how this method will be useful in practical applications or how it compares to other alternative options.

Additionally, some details are missing from the paper. For example, the authors do not provide information on how the inputs are converted into spikes, what frequencies are used, or what the shared weight values are. These details would be helpful in understanding the implementation of the method and in replicating the results.

In terms of related work, the authors should mention Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and the paper at http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427. These references are relevant to the field and would provide additional context for the reader.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a significant contribution to the field. With some additional details and a clearer explanation of the significance of the work, the paper would be even stronger.
