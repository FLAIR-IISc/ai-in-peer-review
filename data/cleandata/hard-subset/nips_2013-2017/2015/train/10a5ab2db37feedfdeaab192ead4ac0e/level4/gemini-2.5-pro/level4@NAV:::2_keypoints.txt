- Proposes treating continuous weights as probabilities and stochastically sampling them to produce binary synapses for spiking neuromorphic hardware.  
- Adapts backpropagation to a probabilistic framework and demonstrates effectiveness on MNIST.  
- Maps the trained network onto two hardware configurations (size-optimized and accuracy-optimized) and reports on-chip performance metrics.  
- Manuscript quality is exceptional: clear, well-written, highly original, and extensible to other methods and hardware.  
- Suggests strengthening hardware review by citing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Main weakness: insufficient articulation of broader significance and unclear how the method enables real-world TrueNorth deployment or which application domains benefit.  
- Lacks comparative analysis against alternative approaches (references [9–12]) to establish advantages.  
- Missing important implementation details: input-to-spike conversion method, spike frequencies, shared weight values, and specific neuron models used on the hardware.  
- Overall: presents a viable training-to-deployment technique with compelling TrueNorth validation, but needs broader context, comparisons, and implementation specifics.
