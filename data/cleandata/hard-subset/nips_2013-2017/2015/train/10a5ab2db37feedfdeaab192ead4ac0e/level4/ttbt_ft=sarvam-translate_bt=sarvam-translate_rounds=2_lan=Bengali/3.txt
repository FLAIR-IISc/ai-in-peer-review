The authors have presented an interesting paper on a backpropagation training method using spike probability, considering the hardware limitations of a specific platform with spiking neurons and reduced accuracy synapses. Currently, this is a matter of interest in mapping deep networks onto multi-neuron hardware platforms.

**Quality:** The proposed training method is particularly useful for new multi-neuron hardware platforms with limitations.

**Clarity:** The article is easy to read. The claims made at the end of the first chapter could be rewritten, as it is not clear from a careful reading of the article that it is proposing a training method using spiking neurons and reduced accuracy synapses for the first time. I would not see it as a new feature that includes a demonstration of running this network on a specific hardware platform, such as TN. Running the network on TN is a validation of the training method.

In the second chapter, what does 0.15 bits per synapse mean? The network topology is not clearly described in one place. How much more training time is required due to potential synaptic connection updates? Why are the results so low from a single ensemble of a 30-core network, especially when compared to a 5-core network? Why are the results from different ensembles of a 5-core network almost the same? What is the spike rate for the inputs during testing? Is the input a spike train? Line 399 of ref 12 includes a training time constraint (e.g., bias=0), so it is not just a train-then-constraint method (the typo in this line, "approach approach").

**Originality:**
Could the authors discuss or compare it with other spiking backprop-type rules like spikeprop?

**Importance:** The development of new training methods that consider hardware platform limitations is of great interest. The limitations considered in this article are based on the TN architecture, and these limitations may not apply to other platforms. If the results are based on a 64-ensemble, it means that more hardware resources must be dedicated to achieving the required accuracy of the network. An interesting paper about a training method using backpropagation, considering the hardware limitations of a specific platform with spiking neurons and isolated synapses.
