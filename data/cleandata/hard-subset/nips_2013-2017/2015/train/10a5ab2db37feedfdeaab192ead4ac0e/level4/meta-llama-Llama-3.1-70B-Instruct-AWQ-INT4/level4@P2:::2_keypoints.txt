- Proposes sampling analog backpropagation variables as probabilities to map trained networks to spike-based devices with binary synapses.
- Approach is well-suited for neuromorphic hardware (e.g., IBM TrueNorth) that uses spiking neurons and low-precision synapses.
- Demonstrates mapping from backprop-trained network to TrueNorth and reports high MNIST accuracy with low power consumption.
- Manuscript is clear, concise, and easy to follow.
- Method is original and offers a new perspective on adapting backpropagation to spiking networks and binary synapses.
- Work is relevant to the neuromorphic computing community and leverages strengths of both training and deployment platforms.
- Lacks clear explanation of practical applications and real-world usefulness.
- Does not sufficiently compare advantages over alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]).
- Missing methodological details: input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies used, shared weight values, and neuron model specifics.
- Related work omission: should cite Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 for context.
- Quality score: 8/10 (technically sound but could use more context and detail).
- Clarity score: 9/10 (well-written overall but some details missing).
- Originality score: 9/10 (novel idea).
- Significance score: 7/10 (needs clearer practical impact and comparison to alternatives).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel adaptation of backprop, effective MNIST results, well-written presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear practical usefulness, missing comparisons to alternatives, and insufficient methodological details.
