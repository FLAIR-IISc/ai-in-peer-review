- Addresses gap between backprop-trained DNNs and energy-efficient neuromorphic computing by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities for backpropagation.  
- Demonstrated on MNIST with sparse network deployed to TrueNorth: 99.42% accuracy with 64-model ensemble and 0.268 μJ/image with a single model.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear motivation and a concise, sufficient description of TrueNorth hardware.  
- Provides a detailed, technically sound explanation of the probabilistic training method and gradient calculations.  
- Network topology and constrained connectivity approach are clearly articulated.  
- Results are presented effectively, including accuracy, energy figures, and comparisons to prior work.  
- A major strength is experimental validation on real neuromorphic hardware, showing significant power-efficiency gains for digit recognition.  
- The “constrain-then-train” strategy is sensible and practically useful for hardware-constrained networks.  
- Authors demonstrate good familiarity with related work in backpropagation and neuromorphic computing.  
- Claims of novelty are overstated: the algorithm closely resembles Zhao et al. (1996) and Soudry et al. (2014) and these should be explicitly acknowledged.  
- The paper’s claim that “constrain-then-train” is novel is misleading because Soudry et al. (2014) proposed and evaluated a similar approach.  
- The introduction’s novelty claim about backpropagation for spiking neurons with constrained connectivity and low-precision synapses is exaggerated given Soudry et al. (2014)’s related capabilities.  
- Comparison to state-of-the-art is inaccurate; prior results (notably Soudry et al., 2014) on MNIST with binary weights/neurons should be included.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong experimental results on TrueNorth, clear motivation, detailed method, and significant contribution to low-power neural implementations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated novelty, insufficient citation/discussion of closely related prior work, and inaccurate comparisons to prior state-of-the-art.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable paper worth accepting if revised to acknowledge similarities to Zhao (1996) and Soudry (2014) and to provide accurate comparisons to prior work.
