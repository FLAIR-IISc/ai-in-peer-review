- Proposes a backpropagation-based training method using spike probabilities that accounts for hardware constraints of spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
- Timely topic given interest in mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware platforms.
- The training method is useful for emerging multi-neuron hardware constraints.
- Paper is easy to read but Section 1 claims should be reworded to avoid implying novelty where none exists.
- Running the network on the TN hardware should be presented as validation of the method, not a standalone novel contribution.
- Clarify what “0.15 bits per synapse” in Section 2 means.
- Provide a concise, centralized description of the network topology.
- Report how much additional training time is incurred by the probabilistic synaptic connection update.
- Explain why the single-ensemble 30-core network result is much lower than the 5-core network.
- Explain why results across different ensembles of the 5-core network are so similar.
- Specify input spike rates during testing and confirm whether the input is a spike train.
- Note that Reference 12 includes constraints during training (e.g., bias = 0), so it is not a pure train-then-constrain approach; also fix the “approach approach” typo.
- Add discussion/comparison with other spiking backprop rules such as SpikeProp.
- Constraints used are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize; achieving reported accuracy with a 64-ensemble requires proportionally greater hardware resources.
- Overall assessment: an interesting paper on a backpropagation-based training method that accounts for platform-specific constraints.
