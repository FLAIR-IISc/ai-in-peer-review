- Proposes a method to map conventionally trained networks to spiking hardware by interpreting analog variables as probabilities to bridge continuous training precision and binary synapses.
- Introduces a modified backpropagation algorithm compatible with the proposed probabilistic mapping.
- Validates the approach experimentally on the MNIST dataset.
- Implements the method on IBM TrueNorth and reports performance for two architectures optimized for size and accuracy.
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and technically proficient.
- Core idea is original and potentially applicable across multiple neuromorphic platforms.
- Recommends citing related hardware work by Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.
- Paper needs a stronger articulation of practical significance, deployment benefits on TrueNorth, and target application areas.
- Lacks clear comparison to alternative methods (e.g., refs [9]–[12]) making it unclear if the approach offers substantial advantages.
- Experimental results are convincing but the presentation lacks sufficient implementation detail for reproducibility.
- Requests more information on input encoding (conversion to spikes), spiking frequencies, handling of shared weight values, and specific neuron models used.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with promising approach; addressing significance and granular experimental details would substantially improve the paper.
