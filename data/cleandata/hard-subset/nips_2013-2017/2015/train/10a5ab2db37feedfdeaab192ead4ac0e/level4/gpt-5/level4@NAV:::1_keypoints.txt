- The algorithm in Section 3.2 is highly similar to Zhao et al. (1996) and Soudry et al. (2014), effectively a hybrid of those methods using the same approximations.  
- The paper presents the "constrain-then-train" approach as novel, but this was already proposed and evaluated in Soudry et al. (2014).  
- Novelty claim (i) is misleading: Expectation Backpropagation ([15]) is a backprop-style update via expectation propagation for binary neurons/weights and can support constrained connectivity.  
- The cited prior state-of-the-art is incorrect; Soudry et al. (2014) reported the strongest binary-weight/-neuron results on MNIST (no augmentation).  
- While the paper yields strong experimental accuracy and power-consumption results, the overarching methodology and algorithmic novelty are overstated.
