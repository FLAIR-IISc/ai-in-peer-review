The authors propose a novel approach to reconcile the need for high precision of continuous variables in backpropagation algorithms with the desire to use spike-based neural network devices with binary synapses. They introduce the idea of considering the analog variables as probabilities that are sampled to map the neural network used off-line for training to the spiking network chip used for deployment of the application. This approach is well-suited for neuromorphic hardware, such as the TrueNorth chip, which is designed to operate with spiking neurons and low-precision synapses.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by training a neural network using backpropagation and then mapping it to the TrueNorth chip. They present results on the MNIST benchmark, achieving high accuracy and low power consumption. The manuscript is well-written, clear, and concise, making it easy to follow the authors' argument.

One of the strengths of the paper is its originality. The authors provide a new perspective on how to adapt backpropagation to work with spiking neural networks and binary synapses. The work is also relevant to the field of neuromorphic computing, as it provides a way to leverage the strengths of both backpropagation and neuromorphic hardware.

However, there are some areas that could be improved. The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how their method will be useful in practical applications. They also do not discuss the advantages of their approach over alternative methods, such as those cited in [9] to [12]. Additionally, some details are missing, such as how the inputs are converted into spikes, the frequencies used, the shared weight values, and the neuron models.

In terms of related work, the authors should mention Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and the paper at http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427. These references could provide additional context and insights into the field of neuromorphic computing.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of the topic. However, they could improve the paper by providing more context, discussing the advantages of their approach, and including more details about their method.
