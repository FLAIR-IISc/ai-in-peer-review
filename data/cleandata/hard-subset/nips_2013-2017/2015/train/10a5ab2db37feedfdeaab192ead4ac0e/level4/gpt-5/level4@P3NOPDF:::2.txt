Summary
The paper proposes a principled way to bridge the precision gap between continuous-valued backpropagation and spike-based neuromorphic hardware with binary synapses. The key idea is to interpret trained analog parameters as probabilities and sample them to obtain binary synaptic configurations suitable for deployment. The authors adapt backpropagation to accommodate this probabilistic mapping and validate the approach on MNIST. They demonstrate deployment on the TrueNorth chip with two alternative network mappings optimized for either size or accuracy, and report performance measured directly from hardware.

Strengths
- Clear, well-structured manuscript with a coherent narrative from training to hardware deployment.
- Original and timely contribution addressing a central challenge in neuromorphic computing: mapping high-precision training results to low-precision, event-driven hardware.
- Convincing empirical validation on a physical device (TrueNorth), including measured performance rather than solely simulation results.
- Demonstration of two deployment strategies (size-optimized and accuracy-optimized) is useful for practitioners facing different resource constraints.
- The proposed framework could be applicable to other neuromorphic platforms, not just TrueNorth.

Weaknesses and requests for clarification
- Significance and practical impact: The paper does not clearly articulate what new capabilities this method enables for TrueNorth in real applications. Please discuss concrete deployment scenarios (e.g., low-power always-on vision, keyword spotting, sensor fusion), the achieved accuracy/latency/energy trade-offs, and how these compare to established baselines on digital hardware or alternative neuromorphic mappings.
- Comparison to prior work: While related work is cited, the advantages of this approach over alternatives (e.g., weight quantization, stochastic rounding, ensemble or multi-synapse encoding, surrogate-gradient methods cited in [9]–[12]) are not quantified. Provide side-by-side comparisons on accuracy, resource utilization (cores, fan-in/out usage), latency (inference time window), and energy.
- Experimental scope: MNIST is a helpful starting point but is limited. To demonstrate scalability, consider adding results on a more challenging dataset (e.g., N-MNIST, CIFAR-10 with appropriate encoding, or a simple event-driven task), or at least discuss expected behavior and constraints when scaling to deeper/conv architectures on TrueNorth.
- Methodological details missing:
  - Input encoding: How are pixels converted to spikes (rate vs. temporal coding, Poisson vs. deterministic)? What time window and firing rates are used?
  - Neuron and synapse models: Specify neuron parameters (leak, threshold, reset, refractoriness), synaptic dynamics, and any on-chip variability management.
  - Probabilistic mapping: Precisely define how analog parameters are converted to probabilities and sampled (Bernoulli over synapses? over time?). Is sampling repeated across time steps, across synapse replicas, or across an ensemble of subnetworks? How many samples are used at inference?
  - Training mechanics: Is stochasticity injected during training (e.g., straight-through estimator, gradient through sampling)? How is gradient variance controlled? What loss, regularization, and schedules are used to encourage deployability (e.g., weight clipping, sparsity, noise injection)?
  - Resource constraints: Detail mapping constraints (fan-in/out, core utilization, routing limits), shared weight values, and how conflicts are resolved. Quantify the overhead of duplicating synapses/neurons to approximate probabilities.
  - Performance metrics: Report latency (integration window), throughput, and energy per inference on-chip. Provide confidence intervals across seeds and discuss device-to-device variability.
- Two architecture variants: Clarify the exact architectural differences, resource usage, and trade-offs. A plot or table contrasting accuracy vs. number of cores/synapses/latency/energy would help readers choose a configuration.
- Robustness: Analyze sensitivity to (i) number of samples or synapse replicas, (ii) input spike rate, (iii) neuron threshold/leak settings, and (iv) weight noise or mismatch. Such ablations would strengthen the empirical case.

Missing or suggested references
- Please include: Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience (2015), which reviews/illustrates relevant neuromorphic platforms and may contextualize your hardware mapping.
- Also cite the work at arXiv:1506.05427, which is directly relevant to hardware-constrained learning and deployment.
- When positioning against [9]–[12], briefly summarize how your probabilistic mapping differs conceptually and practically (e.g., training-objective alignment, deployment complexity, accuracy/energy benefits).

Minor presentation suggestions
- Provide a concise algorithmic description of the training and mapping pipeline, including where randomness is applied and how parameters are constrained during training to match hardware limits.
- Ensure all hardware measurements include the experimental setup (chip configuration, measurement apparatus, number of trials).
- If space permits, add a short limitations section outlining when the approach may not be suitable (e.g., tight-latency temporal coding tasks where long rate windows are impractical).

Overall assessment
This is a high-quality, clearly written paper with a novel and practical contribution to deploying trained networks on spike-based hardware with binary synapses. The empirical validation on TrueNorth is compelling. However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by (i) a clearer articulation of practical significance and application scenarios, (ii) a more thorough comparison to competing methods, and (iii) additional methodological details about encoding, neuron/synapse models, resource constraints, and on-chip performance metrics. Addressing these points, along with the suggested citations, would make the contribution both more reproducible and more broadly impactful. I am positive about the paper’s potential and lean toward acceptance pending clarification of the above issues.
