1) Please acknowledge that the algorithm in Section 3.2 closely mirrors prior work in [14] J. Zhao, J. Shawe-Taylor, and M. van Daalen, “Learning in stochastic bit stream neural networks,” Neural Networks, 1996, and [15] Soudry, Hubara, and Meir, “Expectation backpropagation: Parameter-free training of multilayer neural networks with continuous or discrete weights,” NIPS 2014. In its final form, the proposed method is effectively a hybrid of these two approaches and is derived using the same approximations.

2) The first paragraph of the Discussion suggests that the “constrain-then-train” paradigm (learning a weight distribution and sampling it to construct a fixed ensemble at test time) is novel, but this strategy was already proposed and empirically evaluated in [15].

3) Novelty claim (i) at the end of the Introduction is also misleading. The “expectation backpropagation” method in [15] is a backpropagation-style update rule (via expectation propagation) implemented for binary neurons (sign activation, with zero input essentially never attained) and binary weights, and it can readily incorporate constrained connectivity.

4) The prior state-of-the-art claims are incorrect. To the best of my knowledge, the strongest previous MNIST results for binary weights and binary neurons were achieved by [15], evaluated without data augmentation—matching the evaluation protocol used here (see http://arxiv.org/pdf/1503.03562v3.pdf).
