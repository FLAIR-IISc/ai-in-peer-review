- Tackles deploying deep learning on energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware by reconciling backpropagation with spiking neurons and discrete synapses.  
- Treats spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable standard backpropagation for training.  
- Maps trained probabilistic networks to neuromorphic hardware by sampling, and uses an ensemble of sampled networks for inference.  
- Demonstrated on TrueNorth with MNIST: 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ/image (64-member ensemble) and 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image (single model).  
- Well-motivated work that clearly outlines the need to bridge deep learning and energy-constrained hardware.  
- Experimental results are compelling, showing large improvements in power efficiency and practical relevance via TrueNorth.  
- Claim of novelty is overstated; approach closely relates to prior work (e.g., Zhao et al. and Soudry et al.) and shares similar approximations.  
- The Section 3.2 algorithm resembles existing expectation/probabilistic backpropagation methods and appears hybridized from prior work.  
- The "constrain-then-train" ensemble-sampling methodology is not novel and was previously proposed/evaluated in Soudry et al.  
- State-of-the-art accuracy comparisons are potentially inaccurate and require verification and correct attribution to prior best results.  
- Paper strengths: addresses an important problem, uses a clever probabilistic approach, shows strong empirical results, and is clearly written.  
- Paper weaknesses: overstated novelty, inaccurate or incomplete comparisons to prior work, and insufficient detailed comparisons with related algorithms.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound solution, compelling experimental evidence, and likely significant impact on neuromorphic computing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: novelty claims need tempering and state-of-the-art comparisons must be carefully verified; revise to acknowledge related work.  
- Overall: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to properly cite related work and correct comparative claims.
