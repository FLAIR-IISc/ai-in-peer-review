The authors propose a method to reconcile the high precision requirements of backpropagation with the binary synapses and spiking neurons of neuromorphic hardware. Their key idea is to treat analog variables as probabilities, sampled to map a network trained offline to a spiking network chip for deployment. They adapt backpropagation to be compatible with this strategy and validate their method on the MNIST benchmark, mapping the trained network onto two different architectures optimized for size and accuracy, and presenting performance figures measured directly on the chip. The manuscript is of high quality and very clearly written. The work is original and potentially useful for other approaches and hardware platforms. While reviewing available hardware platforms, the authors might consider citing Qiao et al., *Frontiers in Neuroscience* 2015, and the work presented at http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427. The significance of the work could be further emphasized; it is not immediately clear how this method will facilitate the deployment of the TrueNorth chip in practical applications, or its advantages over alternative approaches (e.g., those cited in [9]–[12]). 

The authors present a method for applying backpropagation to spiking neural networks with binary synapses, successfully demonstrating its effectiveness by mapping the trained network onto the TrueNorth device. The paper is well-structured, clear, and concise. The results are convincing, although some details could be expanded upon – for example, how inputs are converted into spikes (including frequencies used), the shared weight values, and the specific neuron models employed.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of treating synapses and neuron outputs as probabilities bridges a significant gap between backpropagation-based training and neuromorphic hardware constraints.
*   **Strong Results:** The authors achieve state-of-the-art accuracy on MNIST when compared to other spiking neural network implementations, and demonstrate excellent power efficiency on the TrueNorth chip.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow, making the complex concepts accessible.
*   **Practical Demonstration:** The authors demonstrate a full pipeline, from training to deployment on actual hardware, which strengthens the credibility of their work.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Significance:** While the results are impressive, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the broader impact and potential applications of this approach.
*   **Limited Scope:** The work is currently limited to the MNIST dataset and the TrueNorth chip. It would be helpful to discuss the potential for generalization to other datasets and hardware platforms.
*   **Missing Details:** Some implementation details are lacking (e.g. input encoding, neuron model specifics) which would allow for easier reproduction of the results.
