- Novel method: treat analog variables as probabilities and sample them to map offline-trained networks to spiking devices with binary synapses.  
- Empirical validation: tested on MNIST and mapped to two architectures, yielding optimized size and accuracy trade-offs.  
- Manuscript strengths: exceptional quality, clarity, and originality with broader applicability to other approaches and hardware.  
- Hardware review gap: should include Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Need clearer emphasis on significance and practical implications, and explicit advantages over alternatives (refs [9]–[12]).  
- Demonstrated deployment: successfully applied backprop-derived networks to TrueNorth, showing applicability to spiking binary-synapse hardware.  
- Presentation: paper is well-organized and effectively communicates its key findings.  
- Missing experimental details: provide input conversion process, frequency parameters, shared weight values, and neuron models used.
