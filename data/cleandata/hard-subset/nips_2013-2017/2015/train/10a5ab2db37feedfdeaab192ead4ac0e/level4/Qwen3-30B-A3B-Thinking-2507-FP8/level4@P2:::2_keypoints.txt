- Problem: bridges deep learning (backprop) and neuromorphic computing (spiking neurons, binary synapses, constrained connectivity) for energy-constrained embedded systems.  
- Method: "constrain-then-train" — impose hardware constraints first, then train a probabilistic representation with backprop, sampling probabilities to produce discrete hardware implementations.  
- Key insight: treat continuous variables as probabilities that can be sampled to create discrete synapses/neurons for neuromorphic hardware.  
- Results: TrueNorth MNIST — 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ/image (ensemble 64) and 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image (ensemble 1).  
- Contribution: first end-to-end solution combining backprop, spiking neurons, sub-trinary/binary synapses, and constrained connectivity with real-time TrueNorth deployment.  
- Comparison to prior work: integrates elements previously handled separately and outperforms prior spiking/high-precision (99.12%) and binary (97.88%) networks; much lower energy per image than prior low-power approaches.  
- Technical quality: method is technically sound, adapts backprop appropriately, and experimental validation (including energy measurements) is thorough.  
- Theoretical gap: minor concern — no detailed theoretical analysis of the Gaussian approximation (Equation 3), though empirical results support it.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written with strong figures and technical exposition, but some implementation details are under-specified (spike conversion, neuron dynamics, parameter choices).  
- Originality: novel "constrain-then-train" paradigm and progressive mixing topology; appropriate citations but missing a couple relevant references (Qiao et al. 2015, arXiv:1506.05427).  
- Significance: results on MNIST are impressive, but broader real-world impact and concrete application scenarios (e.g., mobile/edge use cases) are not clearly articulated.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, first real-time TrueNorth deployment with strong accuracy/energy tradeoffs, comprehensive comparisons, and strong experimental validation.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient discussion of broader significance, limited comparison to alternative deployment methods, and some implementation specifics lacking.  
- Pro-acceptance points: solves a fundamental deployment problem, achieves superior accuracy/energy tradeoffs, introduces a novel training paradigm, and is well-validated and written.  
- Con-acceptance points: unclear real-world significance, incomplete comparisons to alternatives, and missing implementation details; recommend clarifying applications and adding comparisons/details before acceptance.
