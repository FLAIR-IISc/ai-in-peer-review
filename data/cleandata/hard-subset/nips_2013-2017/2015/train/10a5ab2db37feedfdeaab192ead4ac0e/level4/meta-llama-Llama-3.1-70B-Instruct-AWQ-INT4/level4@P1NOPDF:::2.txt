The authors propose a method to reconcile the need for high precision in continuous variables within backpropagation algorithms with the desire to utilize spike-based neural network devices with binary synapses. They introduce the concept of treating analog variables as probabilities, which are sampled to map the neural network used offline for training onto the spiking network chip used for application deployment. This approach enables the adaptation of the backpropagation algorithm to accommodate this strategy, and the authors validate their method using the MNIST benchmark. Furthermore, they successfully map the trained network onto two distinct architectures, optimizing for size and accuracy, and present performance figures measured directly from the chip.

The manuscript is of high quality and clarity, and the work demonstrates a good degree of originality, with potential applications in other approaches and hardware platforms. Notably, in reviewing the various hardware platforms available, the authors may also consider referencing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and the paper available at http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427.

However, the significance of the work is not adequately highlighted in the paper. Specifically, it is unclear how the proposed method will facilitate the deployment of the TrueNorth chip in practical applications and what specific applications it is suited for. Additionally, the extent to which this method offers advantages over alternative options, such as those cited in references [9] to [12], is not clearly established.

The authors present a method for applying backpropagation to spiking neural networks with binary synapses and validate it by successfully mapping the trained network onto the TrueNorth device. The paper is well-structured and clear, effectively conveying the main points. The results are convincing, although some details are lacking, such as the conversion of inputs into spikes, the frequencies used, shared weight values, and neuron models. Providing these details would further enhance the paper's clarity and impact.
