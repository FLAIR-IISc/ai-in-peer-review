- Proposes training spiking neural networks by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable standard backpropagation.  
- Trains networks that map directly to neuromorphic hardware (TrueNorth) with constrained connectivity and binary synapses.  
- Demonstrates high MNIST accuracy (99.42% with 64-ensemble) and very low energy use (0.268 μJ/image with single ensemble).  
- Provides a clear probabilistic interpretation and a carefully derived training methodology with justified approximations (e.g., Gaussian approx. in Eq. 3).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, well-documented, and appropriately compared to prior work.  
- Acknowledges limitations such as reliance on ensemble averaging to reach top accuracy and analyzes accuracy vs. energy trade-offs.  
- Paper is technically sound and complete, not merely a position or conceptual paper.  
- Generally well-organized and clearly written, with the main idea effectively presented.  
- Some technical details lack clarity—e.g., the derivation/explanation of "0.15 bits per synapse."  
- Network topology description is fragmented and needs consolidation.  
- Figure 1 should better explain how the probabilistic training network maps to the discrete deployment network.  
- Missing explicit discussion of spike rates during testing and whether inputs are represented as spike trains.  
- Novelty lies in unifying spiking neurons, discrete synapses, and constrained connectivity in training (constrain-then-train).  
- Running on TrueNorth provides strong hardware validation, though the distinction between methodological novelty and hardware validation could be clearer.  
- Work is significant for neuromorphic computing, offering a practical path to energy-efficient deep models and direct correspondence between training and deployment error.  
- Strengths: novel probabilistic approach, comprehensive TrueNorth validation, clear differentiation from prior work, competitive MNIST results.  
- Weaknesses: unclear specific technical points ("0.15 bits"), fragmented topology description, limited discussion of ensemble/resource trade-offs, missing comparison to SpikeProp.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution with convincing experiments; recommendation for acceptance.
