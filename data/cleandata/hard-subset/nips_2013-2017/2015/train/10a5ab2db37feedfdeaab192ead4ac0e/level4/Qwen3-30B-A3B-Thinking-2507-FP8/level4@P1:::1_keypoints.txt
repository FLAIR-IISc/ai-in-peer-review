- Addresses bridging backpropagation-based training with energy-efficient spiking neuromorphic hardware.  
- Proposes training with continuous-valued spike and synapse probabilities, then sampling to create discrete spiking networks for deployment.  
- Demonstrates strong results on MNIST: 99.42% at 108 μJ/image (ensemble of 64) and 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image (single network).  
- Training methodology reconciles backpropagation with neuromorphic constraints by treating synapses and spikes as continuous during training.  
- Deployment on TrueNorth achieves impressive energy efficiency while maintaining high accuracy.  
- Network topology (progressive mixing, constrained connectivity) is well-suited to neuromorphic hardware limits.  
- Core training algorithm closely resembles expectation backpropagation methods (Soudry et al., 2014) using probabilistic binary weights and neurons.  
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge and clarify differences from Soudry et al. (2014) and similar prior works.  
- The claimed novelty of "constrain-then-train" and ensemble sampling is misleading because analogous approaches appear in Soudry et al. (2014).  
- Novelty claim (i) asserting first demonstration of backprop-trained spiking networks with low-bit synapses and constrained connectivity is incorrect given prior binary-weight/binary-neuron results.  
- Comparisons to prior state-of-the-art are inaccurate or misleading (misrepresentation of Soudry et al. results and other reported accuracies).  
- Experimental results are strong, well-documented, and show substantial energy advantages over other low-power approaches.  
- Strengths: strong energy-efficient results, clear deployment strategy, clear probabilistic training explanation, useful literature comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: overstated novelty, insufficient acknowledgment of similar probabilistic backprop work, inaccurate prior comparisons, lack of detailed differentiation from Soudry et al.  
- Recommendation: revise novelty claims, more precisely discuss differences from prior work, and correct comparisons to existing literature before publication.
