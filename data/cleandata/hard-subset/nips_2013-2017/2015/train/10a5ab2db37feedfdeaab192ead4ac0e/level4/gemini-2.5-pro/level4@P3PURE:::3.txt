This paper presents a backpropagation-based training method that uses spike probabilities to train networks of spiking neurons with discrete synapses. The method is specifically designed to accommodate the hardware constraints of a target multi-neuron platform, a topic of significant interest for mapping deep neural networks onto neuromorphic hardware.

**Quality & Clarity**

The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The proposed training method is useful, particularly in the context of emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms with inherent constraints. However, several points require clarification to improve the paper's completeness and impact.

The claims made at the end of Section 1 could be rephrased for greater precision. As currently worded, they might inadvertently suggest that this is the first training methodology for spiking neurons with reduced-precision synapses. Clarifying the specific contributions relative to prior work would be beneficial. Furthermore, while the demonstration of the network on the TrueNorth (TN) hardware is a valuable validation of the proposed training method, it might be more accurately framed as a proof-of-concept rather than a core novel feature of the algorithm itself.

The authors should also address the following questions:
*   In Section 2, the meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse" is unclear and requires explanation.
*   The network topology is not described in a single, consolidated location, making it difficult to grasp the full architecture.
*   What is the training time overhead introduced by the probabilistic synaptic connection updates compared to a standard approach?
*   An explanation is needed for the performance discrepancy between the 30-core and 5-core networks. Why does a single ensemble of the 30-core network perform so poorly, especially in comparison to the 5-core network?
*   Conversely, why are the results across different ensembles of the 5-core network so similar?
*   What were the spike rates of the inputs during testing? Were the inputs presented as spike trains?
*   The characterization of Reference [12] (line 399) as solely a "train-then-constrain" approach appears inaccurate, as that work also incorporates constraints (e.g., bias=0) during the training phase. Additionally, there is a typo on this line ("approach approach").

**Originality**

To better situate this work within the existing literature, the authors should provide a more detailed discussion and comparison with other backpropagation-style learning rules for spiking networks, such as SpikeProp and its variants.

**Significance**

The development of training methods that directly incorporate hardware constraints is a significant and timely contribution to the field. However, the impact could be better contextualized. The constraints considered are specific to the TrueNorth architecture, which may limit the method's direct applicability to other neuromorphic platforms; a discussion of the method's generality would be welcome. Additionally, achieving high accuracy requires a 64-network ensemble, which implies a substantial commitment of hardware resources. This trade-off between accuracy and resource cost is an important practical consideration that warrants further discussion.
