- Proposes a method to reconcile high-precision backpropagation with spike-based hardware using binary synapses.  
- Treats analog variables as probabilities and samples them to map trained networks to spiking chips.  
- Integrates this probabilistic sampling approach into the backpropagation algorithm.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on MNIST with the trained network mapped to hardware.  
- Successfully ports the network onto two TrueNorth architectures optimized for size and accuracy, with chip-measured performance.  
- Manuscript quality is exceptionally high and unusually clear.  
- The work is original and likely useful for other methods and hardware platforms.  
- Reviewer requests inclusion of related hardware work by Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Paper omits discussion of the method’s practical significance and its advantages over alternative approaches (refs [9]–[12]).  
- Some important implementation details are missing: input-to-spike conversion, operating frequencies, shared weight values, and neuron models.
