- Proposes training spiking, discrete-synapse networks by treating spikes and synapses as continuous probabilities to enable standard backpropagation.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of topology, training methodology, and deployment mapping.  
- Strong experimental results: 99.42% accuracy on MNIST at 108 μJ/image and 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image.  
- Technically sound and the approach to energy-efficient neuromorphic computing is well-motivated and timely.  
- Section 3.2’s algorithm closely resembles prior work (refs [14] and [15]) and similarity should be acknowledged.  
- The “constrain-then-train” methodology is not novel and was previously suggested/tested in ref [15].  
- Authors overstate novelty (claiming first demonstration of several features) and should temper those claims.  
- Claim of best-to-date MNIST accuracy is inaccurate—ref [15] reports comparable results without augmentation.  
- Discussion section needs more context and a more accurate representation of previous work.  
- With revisions addressing prior-work attribution and novelty claims, the paper could make a significant contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: compelling results, clear writing, and relevant/important problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated originality, algorithm similarity to prior work, and lack of proper acknowledgment of earlier methods.
