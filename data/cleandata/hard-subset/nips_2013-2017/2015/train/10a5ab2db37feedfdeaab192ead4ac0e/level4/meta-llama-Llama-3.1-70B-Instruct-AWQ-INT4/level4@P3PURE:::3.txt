The authors present an interesting paper about a backpropagation training method that utilizes spike probabilities and takes into account the hardware constraints of a particular platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses. This topic is of current interest in mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware platforms.

Quality: The proposed training method is useful, especially when considering new multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints.

Clarity: The paper is easy to read, but some claims require clarification. For instance, the statements at the end of Section 1 could be reworded, as they may suggest that the paper proposes a novel training methodology employing spiking neurons and synapses with reduced precision for the first time. However, this is not the case. Additionally, demonstrating the network's performance on a specific hardware platform, in this case, TN, should be viewed as a validation of the training method rather than a novel feature.

Section 2 requires further clarification on several points. Specifically, the meaning of 0.15 bits per synapse is unclear. The network topology is not clearly described in one place, making it difficult to understand the architecture. Furthermore, the authors should provide information on how much longer the training time is due to the probabilistic synaptic connection update. The results from a single ensemble of the 30-core network are surprisingly low, especially when compared to the 5-core network. It is also unclear why the results of the different ensembles of the 5-core network are similar. Moreover, the spike rates for the inputs during testing should be provided, and it should be clarified whether the input is a spike train. Lastly, Reference 12 on line 399 includes constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, which is not a simple train-then-constrain approach (note: there is a typo on this line, "approach approach").

Originality: A discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop-type rules, such as SpikeProp, would be beneficial to assess the novelty of the proposed method.

Significance: The development of new training methods that consider the constraints of hardware platforms is of great interest. However, the constraints considered in this paper are specific to the TN architecture and may not apply to other platforms. If the results are based on a 64-ensemble, it implies that more hardware resources are required to achieve the desired network accuracy. Overall, this is an interesting paper about a training method using backpropagation that takes into account the hardware constraints of a particular platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
