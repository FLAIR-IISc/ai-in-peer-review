- Section 3.2’s algorithm is directly adapted from refs [14] and [15], forming a hybrid via identical approximation techniques; the paper fails to acknowledge this foundational relationship.  
- The opening paragraph wrongly implies the "constrain-then-train" methodology is novel, despite it being proposed and validated in [15].  
- The novelty claim (i) is misleading because [15] already introduced Expectation Backpropagation for binary-weight/sign-activated networks, supporting constrained connectivity and expectation-based updates—matching this paper’s technical approach.  
- The paper’s claim of prior state-of-the-art is incorrect: ref [15] reports the strongest MNIST results for binary-weight/binary-neuron networks (no augmentation), so this paper does not surpass SOTA.  
- While experiments (accuracy and power efficiency) are strong, the methodological/algorithmic claims require major revision; contributions are not sufficiently distinct from prior work (especially [15]) and the introduction/related work must be corrected.
