- Empirical results are strong in accuracy and potential power savings.
- The paper’s claims of novelty are significantly overstated.
- Core training algorithm closely resembles methods from Zhao et al. [14] and Soudry et al. [15] and lacks explicit acknowledgment or differentiation.
- Introduction claim of a “backpropagation-compatible” rule is misleading because Expectation Backpropagation (EBP) in [15] already provides such updates for binary-weight/binary-neuron networks and constrained connectivity.
- The “constrain-then-train” methodology is not new and was previously proposed and validated in [15].
- Experimental comparisons are flawed: the paper omits direct comparison to the relevant state-of-the-art (notably [15]) on MNIST with binary weights/neurons.
- Recommendation: revise novelty claims, explicitly situate contributions relative to [14]/[15], and compare results against the true state-of-the-art baseline.
