- Presents a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities tailored to hardware-limited spiking neurons and discrete synapses — a timely, compelling study.  
- Methodology is valuable given emerging constrained multi-neuron hardware platforms.  
- Paper is well-written, but Section 1’s wording should avoid implying novelty of spiking/reduced-precision training itself; frame TN platform results as validation, not the primary novelty.  
- Clarify “0.15 bits per synapse” and provide a consolidated description of the network topology for easier understanding.  
- Discuss the increased training time incurred by probabilistic synaptic updates.  
- Explain the unexpectedly low performance from a single ensemble of the 30-core network compared to the 5-core network.  
- Report and discuss consistency of results across different ensembles of the 5-core network.  
- Specify input spike rates during testing and whether input data are presented as spike trains.  
- Note that Reference 12 involves constrained training (e.g., bias=0) and thus isn’t strictly a “train-then-constrain” approach; fix the “approach approach” typo.  
- Strengthen originality discussion by comparing to existing spiking backprop algorithms (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- Significance is clear but constrained to TN-specific limits, which may reduce generalizability to other platforms.  
- Achieving reported accuracy requires a 64-ensemble configuration, indicating substantial hardware resource demands.
