- Proposes treating trained continuous weights as probabilities and stochastically sampling them to configure binary synapses for spiking neuromorphic hardware.
- Presents an adapted backpropagation algorithm for this probabilistic-to-binary mapping and validates it on MNIST.
- Demonstrates mapping the trained network to two hardware-optimized architectures (size vs. accuracy) and reports performance measured directly from the chip.
- Manuscript is high quality, clearly presented, and the technique is original with potential broader applicability.
- Broader significance is underexplained: unclear how this facilitates TrueNorth deployment in practical domains or its concrete advantages over alternatives (refs [9–12]).
- Experimental section lacks reproducibility details: how inputs are converted to spikes, spike frequencies used, shared weight values, and exact neuron models on the chip.
- Review of hardware platforms misses relevant citations, e.g., Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.
