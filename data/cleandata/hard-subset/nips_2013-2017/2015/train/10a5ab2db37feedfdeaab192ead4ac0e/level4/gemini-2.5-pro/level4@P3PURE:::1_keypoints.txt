- Strong, novel experimental results (accuracy and power) but the paper overstates the novelty of its methodology and algorithm.

1. The algorithm in Sec. 3.2 closely resembles prior work and is effectively a hybrid of the methods in [14] and [15], using the same approximations.  
2. The "constrain-then-train" approach is not novel; it was previously proposed and evaluated in [15].  
3. Novelty claim (i) is misleading because Expectation Backpropagation [15] is also a backprop-based update for binary networks and can be adapted to constrained connectivity.  
4. The paper's assertions about prior state-of-the-art are incorrect; [15] achieved the best prior MNIST results under the same experimental setup.
