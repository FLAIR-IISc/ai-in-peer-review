- Proposed training method is valuable and timely for multi-neuron hardware with constraints.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Claims in end of Section 1 should be rephrased to present TN hardware results as validation, not the core novel contribution.  
- Clarify what “0.15 bits per synapse” means.  
- Provide a comprehensive, centralized description of the network topology.  
- Address how the probabilistic synaptic update rule affects training time.  
- Explain why a single ensemble of the 30-core network performs poorly compared to the 5-core network.  
- Explain why results across different ensembles of the 5-core network are consistent.  
- State input spike rates during testing and whether inputs are presented as spike trains.  
- Note that Reference 12 implies training constraints (e.g., bias=0), so the method is not simply “train-then-constrain”; fix the “approach approach” typo.  
- Discuss and compare the method to existing spiking backprop rules such as SpikeProp.  
- Constraints targeted are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms.  
- The requirement of a 64-ensemble to reach reported accuracy indicates high hardware resource demands.  
- Overall, an interesting paper describing a backprop method tailored to spiking neurons with discrete synapses.
