The authors present an interesting paper on a backpropagation training method utilizing spike probabilities, and importantly, considers the hardware constraints of a spiking neuron and discrete synapse platform. This is a timely topic given the increasing interest in mapping deep networks onto specialized multi-neuron hardware.

**Quality:** The proposed training method is particularly valuable given the emergence of new multi-neuron hardware platforms with inherent constraints.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow. However, the claims made at the end of Section 1 could be rephrased. As currently stated, they suggest a novel training methodology employing spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses, which may be misleading. Demonstrating the network’s functionality on a specific hardware platform – in this case, TN – should be presented as a validation of the training method, rather than a core novel contribution. 

In Section 2, the meaning of “0.15 bits per synapse” requires further clarification. The network topology is not described comprehensively in a single location. Additionally, the paper should address how the probabilistic synaptic connection update affects training time. The low performance observed from a single ensemble of the 30-core network, particularly in comparison to the 5-core network, warrants explanation. Similarly, an explanation is needed for why the results across different ensembles of the 5-core network are consistent. Finally, what are the spike rates of the inputs during testing? Is the input presented as a spike train? Reference 12 (line 399) mentions training constraints (e.g., bias=0) and is therefore not simply a “train-then-constrain” approach (note a typographical error: “approach approach” on this line).

**Originality:** The authors should provide a discussion comparing their method to other existing spiking backpropagation rules, such as SpikeProp.

**Significance:** Developing new training methods that account for hardware constraints is an important research direction. However, the constraints considered in this paper are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms. The requirement for a 64-ensemble to achieve the reported accuracy indicates a substantial demand for hardware resources. Overall, this is an interesting paper describing a backpropagation training method tailored to the hardware constraints of a platform featuring spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
