This paper addresses the critical challenge of bridging the gap between the algorithmic strengths of backpropagation and the energy efficiency of neuromorphic hardware, specifically spiking neural networks with low-precision synapses. The authors propose a novel approach of treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities, enabling standard backpropagation for training a network that can then be mapped onto a neuromorphic platform via sampling and ensemble averaging. They demonstrate this on the MNIST dataset, achieving competitive accuracy with significant energy efficiency on the TrueNorth chip. 

The work builds upon a significant body of research in both deep learning and neuromorphic computing [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8]. Prior efforts have explored backpropagation with spiking neurons but relied on high-precision weights [9, 10, 11, 12] or conversely, used low-precision synapses with continuous outputs [13]. Other probabilistic approaches exist [14, 15], but typically assume full interlayer connectivity. This work distinguishes itself by presenting the *first* offline training methodology using backpropagation for a network employing spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, *and* constrained connectivity—a combination crucial for practical neuromorphic deployment. The authors also achieve state-of-the-art accuracy on MNIST for spiking networks and demonstrate compelling energy efficiency on TrueNorth, surpassing previously published results [16].

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of probabilistically mapping a continuous-valued, backpropagation-trained network to a discrete, spiking implementation is highly innovative.
*   **Strong Performance:** The achieved accuracy on MNIST (99.42% with an ensemble of 64) is excellent, exceeding previous results with spiking networks.
*   **Energy Efficiency:** The reported energy consumption (108 μJ per image with the 64-member ensemble, and 0.268 μJ with a single member) is a significant achievement.
*   **Hardware Demonstration:** Validating the approach on actual hardware (TrueNorth) is a crucial and valuable contribution.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the method and results. The included diagrams (e.g. Figure 3A) are particularly helpful.
*   **Thoroughness:** The authors address key implementation details, such as network topology and initialization strategies, contributing to reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Significance Discussion:** While the technical results are strong, the paper lacks a detailed discussion of the broader implications and potential impact of this work. How does this approach unlock new applications for TrueNorth or other neuromorphic platforms?
*   **Comparative Analysis:** The paper briefly mentions advantages over previous work, but a more in-depth comparison to alternative methods for deploying networks on neuromorphic hardware (e.g., [9]–[12]) would strengthen the argument for the proposed approach.
*   **Implementation Details:** Although generally thorough, some details regarding the spiking representation of inputs (e.g., frequencies, encoding schemes), specific neuron models used, and the distribution of shared weight values could be expanded for greater clarity and reproducibility. 
*   **Related Work:** While the authors cite relevant work, they could consider including recent advancements in neuromorphic hardware and algorithms, such as [Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015] and [http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427].

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a genuinely novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem in neuromorphic computing.
*   The results are compelling, demonstrating significant improvements in both accuracy and energy efficiency.
*   The hardware demonstration on TrueNorth provides strong evidence of practical viability.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the method and results.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the broader significance and potential impact of the work.
*   A more comprehensive comparison to alternative deployment methods would strengthen the paper’s contribution.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a high-quality paper with the potential to significantly impact the field of neuromorphic computing. The authors have successfully addressed a critical challenge and demonstrated a promising solution with strong experimental results. While some aspects of the significance discussion and comparative analysis could be strengthened, the overall contribution is substantial. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, expanding the discussion of potential applications and providing a more thorough comparison to existing deployment techniques would elevate the paper further.
