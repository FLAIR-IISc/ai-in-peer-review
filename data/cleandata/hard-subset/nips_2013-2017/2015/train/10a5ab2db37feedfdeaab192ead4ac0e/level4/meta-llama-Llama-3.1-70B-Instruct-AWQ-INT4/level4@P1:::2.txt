The authors propose a novel approach to reconcile the need for high precision of continuous variables in backpropagation algorithms with the desire to use spike-based neural network devices with binary synapses. They introduce the idea of considering the analog variables as probabilities that are sampled to map the neural network used offline for training to the spiking network chip used for deployment of the application. This approach allows for the adaptation of the backpropagation algorithm to be compatible with the constraints of spiking neural networks, enabling the training of networks that can be efficiently deployed on neuromorphic hardware.

The manuscript is of high quality, with clear and well-organized writing that effectively communicates the authors' ideas and methodology. The work is original and has the potential to be useful for other approaches and hardware platforms. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, although they could further enhance this section by mentioning other relevant hardware platforms, such as those described in Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and the paper at http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427.

The significance of the work is not fully highlighted in the paper. While the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the MNIST benchmark, they could better explain how their method will be useful in deploying the TrueNorth chip in practical applications and what specific advantages it offers over alternative options, such as those cited in [9] to [12]. Additionally, the authors could provide more details on the implementation, such as how the inputs are converted into spikes, the frequencies used, the shared weight values, and the neuron models employed.

The results presented in the paper are convincing, and the authors demonstrate the potential of their approach for optimizing both accuracy and energy efficiency. However, they could further strengthen their claims by providing more comprehensive evaluations, such as comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods and more detailed analyses of the trade-offs between accuracy and energy efficiency.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of neuromorphic computing, and with some revisions to address the mentioned points, it has the potential to be even more impactful.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and original approach to adapting backpropagation for spiking neural networks with binary synapses.
* The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their methodology and results.
* The work has the potential to be useful for other approaches and hardware platforms.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the significance of the work and its potential impact on practical applications.
* The authors could provide more comprehensive evaluations and comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
* Some details on the implementation, such as input conversion, frequencies, and neuron models, are not fully explained.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
