- Contribution: Proposes a "constrain-then-train" method that trains probabilistic networks under TrueNorth hardware constraints and maps them to deterministic spiking networks for deployment.  
- Validation: Demonstrates strong MNIST results, trading high accuracy (99.42% with 64-network ensemble) for extreme energy efficiency (92.7% accuracy at 0.268 μJ/image with a single network).

Strengths
- Addresses an important problem: bridging high-performance DL with energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware for embedded deployment.  
- Well-motivated methodology: incorporating hardware constraints during training ensures deployment improvements follow training gains.  
- Compelling empirical results on TrueNorth showing tunable accuracy/efficiency trade-offs.

Weaknesses / Requests for clarification
- Clarity of novelty: claims should emphasize the simultaneous handling of spiking neurons, low-precision binary synapses, and hardware connectivity constraints, not implying first-ever training of such networks.  
- Lookup table detail: the phrase "0.15 bits per synapse" needs a clearer explanation.  
- Topology description: network topology details are scattered and should be consolidated (diagram/table) for readability and reproducibility.  
- Input encoding/testing: unclear whether inputs were spike trains or single patterns and what spike rates were used during testing.  
- Training cost: authors should report computational/training time and compare it to standard backpropagation.  

Analysis of results needing explanation
- Why does a single-member 30-core network perform worse than a single-member 5-core network despite larger capacity?  
- Why does ensembling provide almost no benefit for the 5-core network as ensemble size grows from 1 to 64?

Related work / comparisons
- Comparison to "train-then-constrain" methods should be nuanced; some prior work (e.g., Ref [12]) already includes hardware-aware constraints.  
- Broaden related work discussion to include backprop-inspired spiking algorithms (e.g., SpikeProp variants) for better positioning.  
- Minor: fix typo "approach approach" (line 399).

Generality and practicality
- Method is tightly coupled to TrueNorth-specific constraints; discuss how it would generalize or be adapted to other neuromorphic platforms.  
- Resource trade-offs: 99.42% accuracy requires a 64-member ensemble (1920 cores); authors should discuss practical accuracy vs. hardware resource trade-offs.
