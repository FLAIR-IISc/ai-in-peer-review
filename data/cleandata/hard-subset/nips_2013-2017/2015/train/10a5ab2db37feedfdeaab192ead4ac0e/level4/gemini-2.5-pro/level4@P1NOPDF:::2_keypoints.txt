- Novel method: trains spiking neural networks with binary synapses by treating continuous weights as probabilities and sampling them for hardware deployment using a modified backpropagation.
- Validation: demonstrated on MNIST and successfully mapped to the TrueNorth chip, with hardware-measured results for two architectures (size-optimized and accuracy-optimized).
- Manuscript quality: well-structured, clearly written, highly original, and potentially applicable to other constrained networks and hardware.
- Weakness — significance: lacks discussion of broader impact and which real-world applications would benefit most from this method.
- Weakness — comparisons: needs a more direct comparison to alternative approaches (unclear advantages over methods in refs [9–12]).
- Weakness — experimental details: missing crucial implementation details (input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, shared weight values, specific neuron models) that hinder reproducibility.
- Weakness — literature review: omits relevant prior work such as Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.
- Overall: innovative and promising work, but requires clearer significance, stronger comparisons, and additional experimental details.
