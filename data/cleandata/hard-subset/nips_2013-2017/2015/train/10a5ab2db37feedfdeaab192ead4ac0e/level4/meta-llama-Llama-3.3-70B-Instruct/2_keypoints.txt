- Proposes a novel method treating analog variables as probabilities sampled to reconcile high-precision backprop with binary-synapse spiking hardware.  
- Adapts backpropagation to this probabilistic sampling strategy and validates it on the MNIST benchmark.  
- Demonstrates mapping of the trained network onto two distinct chip architectures with optimizations for size and accuracy.  
- Reports measured performance metrics from the TrueNorth chip deployment.  
- Manuscript is high quality, clear, original, and potentially beneficial to other approaches and hardware platforms.  
- Review of existing hardware platforms is incomplete — missing references to Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Lacks a clear emphasis on the practical significance and unique advantages of the method for deploying TrueNorth compared to alternative methods ([9]–[12]).  
- Important implementation details are omitted: input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies used, shared weight values, neuron models, and other deployment specifics.
