- Algorithm in Section 3.2 closely resembles methods in cited refs [14] and [15]; final algorithm is a hybrid of those and uses identical theoretical approximations.
- First paragraph of Discussion wrongly implies novelty of the "constrain-then-train" approach, which was previously proposed and tested in [15] (learn weight distribution, sample fixed ensemble at test time).
- Novelty claim (i) is misleading: Expectation Backprop from [15] is a backprop update derived via expectation propagation, implemented for binary neurons/weights and applicable to constrained connectivity.
- Reported state-of-the-art comparisons are incorrect; prior best results for binary weights and neurons were from [15] on MNIST without augmentation and should be the primary comparison.
- After authors' edits: experiments show strong novel results in accuracy and power consumption, but claims about novelty of the methodology and algorithm remain overstated.
