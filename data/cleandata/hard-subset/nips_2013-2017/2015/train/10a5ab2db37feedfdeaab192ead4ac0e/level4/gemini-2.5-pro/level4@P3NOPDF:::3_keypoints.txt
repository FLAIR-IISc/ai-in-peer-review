- Proposes a novel backpropagation-based training method for SNNs that incorporates TrueNorth hardware constraints and uses spike probabilities.  
- Validates the method by deploying the trained network on the TrueNorth neuromorphic system.  
- Significant contribution for mapping deep networks to energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware.  
- Impact is somewhat limited by focus on TrueNorth-specific constraints; adaptability to other platforms is not discussed.  
- High accuracy depends on ensembling 64 networks, raising important hardware resource and trade-off questions.  
- Novelty claims should be tempered; prior work on reduced-precision synapses exists.  
- Needs direct comparison to other spiking backpropagation methods (e.g., SpikeProp and successors).  
- Paper is generally clear but omits several technical and experimental details needed for reproducibility.  
- Clarify the meaning and derivation of "0.15 bits per synapse."  
- Provide a consolidated, explicit description of the network topology.  
- Report the computational overhead and training-time impact of probabilistic synaptic updates versus standard backprop.  
- Specify the input representation during testing (spike-train encoding and characteristic spike rates).  
- Explain why a single 30-core network ensemble performs poorly compared to 5-core networks and why 5-core ensemble variance is low.  
- Correct the characterization of reference [12] (may include some constraints during training).  
- Minor typographical error: "approach approach" on line 399.
