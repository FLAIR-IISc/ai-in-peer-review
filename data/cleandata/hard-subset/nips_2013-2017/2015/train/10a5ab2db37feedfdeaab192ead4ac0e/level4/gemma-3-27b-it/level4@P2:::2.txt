This paper addresses the critical challenge of bridging the gap between the algorithmic success of backpropagation and the energy efficiency of neuromorphic hardware. The authors propose a novel method to train networks using backpropagation, treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities, enabling a natural mapping to spiking neural network chips like TrueNorth. This is achieved by constraining network values to the range [0, 1] and interpreting them as probabilities of spiking or synaptic activation. The method is validated on MNIST, demonstrating strong performance with both small and larger networks deployed on the TrueNorth chip, achieving state-of-the-art power efficiency for digit recognition.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, presenting a complex idea in a digestible manner. The approach is original, offering a promising solution to a significant problem in neuromorphic computing. While related work is cited, the authors might benefit from referencing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and the work presented in http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427 for a more comprehensive overview of existing hardware platforms.

A key strength of the work is its practical demonstration on TrueNorth, providing concrete evidence of its viability. However, the paper would be strengthened by a more thorough discussion of the significance and broader impact of this work. It currently lacks a clear articulation of *how* this method facilitates the deployment of TrueNorth in real-world applications, and a comparative analysis of its advantages over existing methods (e.g., [9-12]) would be valuable.  Furthermore, while the results are compelling, expanding on implementation details—such as input encoding schemes (spike frequencies), shared weight strategies, and specific neuron models used—would improve reproducibility and provide deeper insight. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The proposed method offers a novel solution to a long-standing problem: reconciling backpropagation with neuromorphic hardware constraints.
*   **Strong Performance:** The results on MNIST demonstrate competitive accuracy and, critically, superior energy efficiency compared to existing approaches on TrueNorth.
*   **Clarity & Quality:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and technically sound.
*   **Practical Validation:** The demonstration on TrueNorth provides concrete evidence of the method’s feasibility.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Discussion of Significance:** The paper lacks a detailed discussion of the broader implications and potential real-world applications of the method.
*   **Missing Implementation Details:** Certain implementation details, such as input encoding and neuron models, could be more thoroughly explained.
*   **Comparative Analysis:** A more comprehensive comparison with alternative approaches would strengthen the argument for the method’s superiority.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a high-quality paper with a novel and promising approach. While a more in-depth discussion of the significance and a more detailed description of the implementation would be beneficial, the strong technical merit and practical validation make it a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance.
