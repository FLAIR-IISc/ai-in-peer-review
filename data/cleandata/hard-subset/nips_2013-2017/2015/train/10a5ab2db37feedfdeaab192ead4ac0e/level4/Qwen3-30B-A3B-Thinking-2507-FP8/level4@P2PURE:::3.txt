

The authors present an interesting paper on a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities that accounts for hardware constraints of a specific platform featuring spiking neurons and discrete synapses. This topic is of current interest for mapping deep networks onto multi-neuron hardware platforms.  

**Quality:** The proposed training method is useful, particularly for emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints.  

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written. However, the claims at the end of Section 1 should be reworded, as they currently suggest—without careful reading—that the paper proposes the first training methodology employing spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses. The demonstration of running the network on the specific hardware platform (TN) should not be presented as a novel feature; it serves as validation of the training method, not a core contribution.  

In Section 2:  
- What does "0.15 bits per synapse" mean?  
- The network topology is not clearly described in one location.  
- How much longer is training due to probabilistic synaptic updates?  
- Why is the result from a single 30-core network ensemble so low compared to the 5-core network?  
- Why are the results for different 5-core network ensembles nearly identical?  
- What are the input spike rates during testing? Is the input a spike train?  
- Ref 12 (line 399) includes constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, so it is not merely a "train-then-constrain" approach (typo: "approach approach" should be "approach").  

**Originality:** The authors should discuss or compare their method with other spiking backpropagation rules, such as SpikeProp.  

**Significance:** Developing new training methods that consider hardware constraints is highly relevant. However, the constraints addressed here are specific to the TN architecture and may not apply to other platforms. If results rely on a 64-ensemble, this requires additional hardware resources to achieve the target network accuracy.  

This is an interesting paper on a training method using backpropagation that accounts for hardware constraints of a platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
