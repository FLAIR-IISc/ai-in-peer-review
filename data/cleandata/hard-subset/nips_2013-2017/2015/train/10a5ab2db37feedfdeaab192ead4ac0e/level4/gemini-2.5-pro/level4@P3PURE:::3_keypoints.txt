- Paper is well-written and the proposed backpropagation-based method for spiking networks with discrete synapses is useful for hardware-constrained platforms.
- Rephrase claims in Section 1 to avoid implying novelty over prior reduced-precision spiking-synapse training methods.
- Frame the TrueNorth demonstration as a proof-of-concept validation rather than a core algorithmic novelty.
- Explain the meaning and calculation of "0.15 bits per synapse" in Section 2.
- Provide a single, consolidated description of the network topology so the full architecture is clear.
- Report the training-time overhead introduced by the probabilistic synaptic updates versus standard training.
- Explain why a single ensemble of the 30-core network performs poorly compared to the 5-core network.
- Explain why results across different ensembles of the 5-core network are so similar.
- Report input spike rates during testing and clarify whether inputs were presented as spike trains.
- Correct the characterization of Reference [12] (it also applies constraints during training) and fix the typo ("approach approach").
- Add a more detailed comparison to prior backpropagation-style spiking learning rules (e.g., SpikeProp and variants).
- Discuss the method’s generality beyond TrueNorth and which constraints are architecture-specific versus transferable.
- Address the practical trade-off between accuracy and hardware cost, notably the need for a 64-network ensemble to reach high accuracy.
