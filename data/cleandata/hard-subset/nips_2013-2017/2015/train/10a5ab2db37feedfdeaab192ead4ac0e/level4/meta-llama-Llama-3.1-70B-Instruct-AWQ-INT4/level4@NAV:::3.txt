The authors present a thought-provoking paper that introduces a backpropagation training method incorporating spike probabilities, while accounting for the hardware constraints of a specific platform featuring spiking neurons and discrete synapses. This topic is particularly relevant in the context of mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware platforms.

Quality: The proposed training method holds significant value, particularly in light of emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms with inherent constraints.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. However, the claims made at the end of Section 1 could be rephrased to avoid suggesting that the paper pioneers a training methodology utilizing spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses. Furthermore, demonstrating the network's functionality on a specific hardware platform, in this case TN, should be viewed as a validation of the training method rather than a novel feature. 

In Section 2, the meaning of 0.15 bits per synapse requires clarification. A concise description of the network topology is also needed. Additionally, the impact of probabilistic synaptic connection updates on training time should be quantified. The disparity in performance between the 30-core and 5-core networks warrants explanation, as does the consistency in results across different ensembles of the 5-core network. The spike rates for input testing should be provided, and the nature of the input (e.g., spike train) should be specified. Reference 12 on line 399 includes constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, which is not merely a train-then-constrain approach (correct the typo "approach approach").

Originality:
A discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop-type rules, such as SpikeProp, would be beneficial.

Significance: The development of novel training methods that consider hardware platform constraints is highly relevant. However, the constraints considered in this paper are specific to the TN architecture and may not be universally applicable. If the results are based on a 64-ensemble approach, this implies a need for increased hardware resources to achieve the desired network accuracy. Overall, the paper presents an interesting training method that incorporates backpropagation and accounts for the hardware constraints of a specific platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
