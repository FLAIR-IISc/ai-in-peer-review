- Paper proposes a backpropagation-based training scheme that models spikes and discrete synapses probabilistically and honors constraints of a specific neuromorphic platform.  
- Direction is timely and aligns with efforts to port deep networks to multi-neuron neuromorphic hardware.  
- Approach is practically useful for emerging platforms that constrain connectivity, precision, and neuron dynamics.  
- Paper is generally clear and readable but some claims (end of Section 1) are overstated and should be rephrased to avoid implying novelty of TN deployment.  
- Clarify what “0.15 bits per synapse” means concretely in practice.  
- Consolidate the network topology description into one section for readability.  
- Report additional training time incurred by probabilistic synaptic connection updates.  
- Explain why the 30-core single-ensemble accuracy is relatively low compared to the 5-core network, and why different ensemble sizes for the 5-core network yield similar performance.  
- Specify input spike encoding used during testing (spike rates and time windows).  
- Correct phrasing about Reference 12 (it incorporates constraints during training, e.g., bias=0) and remove the duplicated word “approach.”  
- Discuss and compare with other spiking backprop methods (e.g., SpikeProp), clarifying similarities, differences, and applicability to constrained hardware.  
- Note that many constraints are TN-specific and may not transfer directly to other platforms, and that achieving top-line accuracy with a 64-member ensemble increases hardware footprint.
