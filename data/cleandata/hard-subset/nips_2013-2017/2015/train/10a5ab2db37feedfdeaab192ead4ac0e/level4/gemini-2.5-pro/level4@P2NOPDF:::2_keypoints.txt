- Introduces a probabilistic method to map continuous off-line learned weights to binary synapses for spiking hardware deployment.  
- Adapts backpropagation to this probabilistic framework.  
- Validated on MNIST with successful deployment and chip-measured results on IBM TrueNorth.  
- Reports results for two network architectures optimized for size or accuracy.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, and logically structured.  
- The probabilistic mapping is an original and potentially broadly applicable contribution to neuromorphic hardware.  
- Empirical TrueNorth implementation provides a strong practical proof-of-concept.  
- Lacks discussion of the broader practical impact and which application types benefit most from the pipeline.  
- Insufficient comparative analysis with alternative SNN training/mapping methods (e.g., refs [9–12]) on accuracy, training cost, and hardware resource use.  
- Missing reproducibility details: how MNIST pixel values were converted to input spike trains.  
- Missing reproducibility details: spiking frequencies and temporal dynamics used during chip inference.  
- Missing reproducibility details: the neuron model implemented on the hardware.  
- Missing reproducibility details: whether weight sharing was used and the specific shared weight values.  
- Suggests adding related references (e.g., Qiao et al. 2015; arXiv:1506.05427).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality and original work that would be strengthened by broader impact discussion, comparative evaluation, and additional experimental details.
