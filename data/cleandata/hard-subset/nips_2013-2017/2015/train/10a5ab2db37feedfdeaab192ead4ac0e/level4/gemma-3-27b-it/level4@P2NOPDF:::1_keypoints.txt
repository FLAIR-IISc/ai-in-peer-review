- Strong experimental results: paper demonstrates high accuracy and low power consumption.
- Claims of novelty require revision for both the general methodology and the specific algorithm.
- Section 3.2’s algorithm closely resembles prior work and should acknowledge its relationship to [14] and [15]; the implementation appears to be a hybrid of those approaches.
- The “constrain-then-train” methodology is presented as novel but was previously proposed and evaluated in [15].
- The introduction’s novelty claim is misleading because expectation backpropagation [15] is a related backprop rule for binary networks and can be adapted to constrained connectivity.
- The paper’s assessment of prior state-of-the-art is inaccurate; the highest reported performance for binary weights/neurons was achieved by [15].
- A fairer comparison would replicate the [15] MNIST results without data augmentation to provide an accurate benchmark.
