- Introduces a probabilistic mapping and modified backpropagation to translate trained continuous-valued networks to binary TrueNorth hardware; validated on MNIST with two architectures — novel, well-written contribution.  
- Core strength: provides an elegant, practical route for deploying trained networks onto low-precision neuromorphic hardware.  
- Experimental validation on real TrueNorth hardware demonstrates feasibility and real-world applicability.  
- Paper needs a clearer discussion of significance and impact—explain why this method is crucial for unlocking practical applications and which applications benefit most.  
- Related work: include recent references (e.g., Qiao et al., Frontiers 2015; arXiv:1506.05427) and provide a clearer comparison against methods in [9]–[12].  
- Must explicitly quantify how and to what extent this method outperforms existing solutions.  
- Reproducibility gap: specify input encoding — how continuous inputs are converted to spike trains and which coding scheme is used (rate vs. temporal, etc.).  
- Reproducibility gap: report spiking parameters, including spike frequencies and how they were chosen.  
- Reproducibility gap: detail how shared weight values are handled in the spiking implementation.  
- Reproducibility gap: provide a clear description of the neuron model used on TrueNorth (e.g., IF or LIF) and its exact parameters.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with a novel contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the above concerns.
