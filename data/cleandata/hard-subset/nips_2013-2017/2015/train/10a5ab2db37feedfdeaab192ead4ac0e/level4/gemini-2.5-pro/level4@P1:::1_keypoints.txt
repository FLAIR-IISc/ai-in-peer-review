- Strong experimental results on neuromorphic hardware, but methodological and algorithmic novelty are likely overstated and should be better situated in prior work.  
- The algorithm in Sec. 3.2 closely mirrors Zhao et al. (1996) and Soudry et al. (2014), appearing as a hybrid using the same approximations; this similarity must be acknowledged.  
- The "constrain-then-train" methodology is not novel and was previously proposed/demonstrated in Soudry et al. (2014); the paper should frame its contribution relative to that work.  
- The introduction’s novelty claim is misleading because Expectation Backpropagation (Soudry et al., 2014) already provides backprop-like updates for binary neurons/weights and can handle constrained connectivity.  
- The state-of-the-art comparison is inaccurate: prior best MNIST results for binary-weight networks (Soudry et al., arXiv:1503.03562v3) were achieved without data augmentation, so the paper should correct claims and account for augmentation effects.
