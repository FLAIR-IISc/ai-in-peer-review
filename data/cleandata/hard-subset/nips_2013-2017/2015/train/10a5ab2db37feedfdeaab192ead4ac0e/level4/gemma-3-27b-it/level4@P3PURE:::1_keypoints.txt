- Authors must acknowledge strong similarity between their algorithm and prior works [14] (Zhao et al. 1996) and [15] (Soudry et al. 2014); the presented method is a hybrid using identical approximations.  
- The paper’s discussion implies the “constrain-then-train” methodology is novel, but this approach was previously proposed and tested in [15].  
- Novelty claim (i) is misleading: the “expectation backpropagation” in [15] already provides a backprop update for binary neurons/weights and can be adapted to constrained connectivity.  
- The cited prior state-of-the-art is inaccurate; [15] reported the highest known performance for binary weights and neurons on MNIST without data augmentation.  
- After authors’ feedback: experimental accuracy and power results are strong and novel, but the overall methodology and algorithmic contributions are overstated in novelty.
