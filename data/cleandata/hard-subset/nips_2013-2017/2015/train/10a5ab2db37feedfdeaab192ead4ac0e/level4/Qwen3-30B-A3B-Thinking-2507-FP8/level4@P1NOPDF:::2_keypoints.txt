- Proposes a novel method treating continuous backprop variables as probabilities and sampling them to map offline-trained networks onto spiking hardware, with an adapted backprop algorithm and MNIST validation; demonstrates deployment on TrueNorth for two architecture trade-offs and reports measured chip performance.

- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, technically sound, clearly structured, and presents an original idea with potential relevance beyond the specific platform.

- Significance is not well articulated: unclear how the method enables practical TrueNorth deployment or what concrete real-world use cases it supports.

- Lacks quantitative comparison to existing alternatives (e.g., refs [9]–[12]); a comparative analysis is needed to demonstrate advantages.

- Critical implementation details are missing (input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, shared weight values, exact neuron model), hindering reproducibility and practical assessment.

- Important prior work on neuromorphic platforms (Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015; arXiv:1506.05427) is not cited and should be included.

- Generalizability beyond MNIST is not discussed; absence of experiments or analysis on more complex tasks weakens the broader impact claim.

- Overall: technically strong and promising contribution, but its full impact requires clearer practical utility, comparative advantages, and detailed implementation information.
