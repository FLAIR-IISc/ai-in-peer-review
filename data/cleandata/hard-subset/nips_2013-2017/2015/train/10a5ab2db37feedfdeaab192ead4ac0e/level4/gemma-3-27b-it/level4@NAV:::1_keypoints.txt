1) Section 3.2 should acknowledge strong resemblance to prior work (Zhao et al. 1996 and Soudry et al. 2014), framing the presented algorithm as a hybrid derived from similar approximations.  
2) The paper’s opening implies novelty for the "constrain-then-train" approach, but this methodology was previously proposed and evaluated in [15].  
3) The novelty claim (i) is overstated: expectation backpropagation [15] already provides a backprop-style update for binary neurons and weights and can handle constrained connectivity.  
4) The reported state-of-the-art comparison is likely inaccurate: [15] achieved better MNIST results with binary weights/neurons without data augmentation, whereas the current work uses augmentation.  
5) Experimental results on accuracy and power are compelling, but the core methodology and algorithmic novelty are overstated.
