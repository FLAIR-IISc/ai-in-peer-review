- Proposes probabilistic sampling of continuous weights to map high-precision networks onto binary-synapse SNN hardware.  
- Adapts backpropagation to the probabilistic sampling framework and validates on MNIST with competitive accuracy.  
- Demonstrates deployment on the TrueNorth chip in two hardware-optimized variants (size-focused and accuracy-focused).  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and technically sound with high-quality results presentation.  
- Fails to justify why this approach is transformative for real-world TrueNorth deployment.  
- Does not specify concrete applications (e.g., edge AI, low-power sensors) where the method has decisive advantages.  
- Lacks direct quantitative comparisons to alternatives (quantization, weight binarization, surrogate-gradient SNN training) cited in [9]–[12].  
- Omits important prior work citations (Qiao et al., Frontiers 2015; arXiv:1506.05427) that contextualize TrueNorth-like architectures.  
- Missing input spike-conversion details (encoding scheme, spike frequency, time window), harming reproducibility.  
- Unclear neuron and weight model specifics (LIF vs. other models, weight-sharing patterns and precision).  
- Hardware configuration details are absent (number of cores, memory constraints for both variants).  
- Method of computing on-chip accuracy (post-spike decoding) is not described.  
- Key hardware metrics—energy per inference, latency, power consumption—are not reported, leaving hardware utility unquantified.  
- Mentions potential generalization to other neuromorphic platforms (Loihi, SpiNNaker) but does not develop this broader applicability.  
- Required revisions: explicitly state practical impact and use cases, provide missing technical/hardware specifications, and integrate the omitted prior work to demonstrate significance.  
- Overall: technically strong and original, but significance and completeness require major revision to demonstrate real-world value.
