The authors present an engaging paper describing a backpropagation-based training approach that leverages spike probabilities and explicitly accounts for the hardware constraints of a specific platform featuring spiking neurons and discrete synapses. This is a timely topic for deploying deep networks on multi-neuron hardware platforms.

Quality: The proposed training approach is useful, especially given the constraints of emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms.

Clarity: The paper is generally clear and readable. The statements at the end of Section 1 should be rephrased; as written, a cursory reading could suggest the paper is the first to propose a training methodology using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses. I would also not frame the demonstration on TN as a novel contribution; running on TN serves as a validation of the training method.
In Section 2, please clarify what “0.15 bits per synapse” means. The network topology is not consolidated in one place. How much additional training time is introduced by the probabilistic synaptic connection updates? Why is the single-ensemble result for the 30-core network so low, especially relative to the 5-core network? Why are the different ensemble results for the 5-core network so similar? What are the input spike rates during testing? Is the input provided as a spike train? Ref. 12 on line 399 includes constraints during training (e.g., bias=0), so it is not purely a train-then-constrain approach (also a typo on this line: “approach approach”).

Originality:
Could the authors include a discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop-type rules such as SpikeProp?

Significance: Developing training methods that incorporate hardware constraints is of substantial interest. The constraints considered here are specific to the TN architecture and may not directly transfer to other platforms. If the reported results rely on a 64-ensemble, this implies additional hardware resources are required to achieve the target accuracy. Overall, this is an interesting paper on a backpropagation-based training method that accounts for the constraints of a particular platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
