- Proposes a backpropagation-based training method operating on spike probabilities that explicitly incorporates hardware constraints for spiking-neuron, discrete-synapse platforms.  
- Timely and useful approach for mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware with tight resource and precision limits.  
- Strength: explicitly accounting for hardware constraints during training improves applicability to constrained platforms.  
- Clarity issue: rephrase end of Section 1 to avoid implying a first-ever demonstration of training with spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses.  
- Clarify that running the network on TN is a validation of the training method, not a standalone novel contribution.  
- Clarification needed: define what “0.15 bits per synapse” precisely means.  
- Present the full network topology in one location (layers, neuron counts, connectivity, fan-in/fan-out, quantization levels).  
- Report additional training time or overhead introduced by the probabilistic synaptic update mechanism.  
- Explain why the single-ensemble result for the 30-core network is so low compared to the 5-core network.  
- Explain why different ensembles of the 5-core network produce similar performance.  
- Specify input spike rates during testing and whether inputs are presented as spike trains.  
- Note that Reference 12 imposes constraints during training (e.g., bias = 0), so it isn’t purely train-then-constrain; also fix the “approach approach” typo.  
- Originality: include discussion and empirical/conceptual comparison with other spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp), highlighting similarities, differences, and advantages of the spike-probability formulation under hardware constraints.  
- Significance concern: discuss portability of the method beyond the TN architecture and whether the constraints generalize to other platforms.  
- Resource/accuracy trade-off: discuss reliance on a 64-ensemble for reported accuracy, trade-offs between accuracy and ensemble/core count, and whether similar gains are achievable with fewer resources.  
- Overall: interesting and relevant contribution that needs the above clarifications and comparisons to make its impact and novelty clearer.
