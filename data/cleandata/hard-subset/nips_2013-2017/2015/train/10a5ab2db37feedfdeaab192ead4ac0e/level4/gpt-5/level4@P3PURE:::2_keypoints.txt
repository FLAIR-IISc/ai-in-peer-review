- Proposes a strategy to reconcile backpropagation precision with spike-based devices that use binary synapses.
- Reinterprets analog variables as probabilities and samples them to map off-line trained networks to spiking hardware.
- Adapts backpropagation to be compatible with the proposed probabilistic sampling strategy.
- Validates the method on MNIST.
- Maps the trained network onto two architectures (size-optimized and accuracy-optimized) and reports on-chip performance measurements.
- Manuscript is high quality, clear, and well structured.
- Approach is original and potentially broadly useful for other methods and hardware platforms.
- Reviewer requests additional citations: Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience (2015) and http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427.
- Significance not sufficiently highlighted: unclear how this enables practical deployment of TrueNorth and which specific applications would benefit.
- Unclear comparative advantages over alternative options, including methods cited in [9]–[12].
- Important implementation details missing: how inputs are converted into spikes.
- Missing details on firing rates/frequencies used.
- Missing information on shared weight values.
- Missing specification of the neuron models employed.
