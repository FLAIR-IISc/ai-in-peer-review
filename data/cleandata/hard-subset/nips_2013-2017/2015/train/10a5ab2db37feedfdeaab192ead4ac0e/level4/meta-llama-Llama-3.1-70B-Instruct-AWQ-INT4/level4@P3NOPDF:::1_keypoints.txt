- Paper proposes training neural networks with binary weights and neurons and reports strong experimental results.
- Experimental results are novel and strong in terms of accuracy and power consumption.
- Concern about lack of novelty and presentation clarity for the methodology and algorithm.
- Algorithm in Section 3.2 is deeply similar to references [14] and [15] and appears to be a hybrid derived using identical approximations.
- Authors should explicitly acknowledge the connection to and origins in [14] and [15].
- The "constrain-then-train" methodology is not novel and was previously suggested and tested in [15]; this should be clarified.
- Novelty claim (i) is misleading because reference [15]’s expectation backpropagation already provides a backprop update for binary neurons/weights and can handle constrained connectivity.
- Citation of prior state-of-the-art is incorrect: prior best results for binary weights/neurons were from [15] on MNIST without augmentation.
- Recommend rephrasing novelty and methodological claims to accurately reflect connections to existing work while retaining the paper’s strong experimental contributions.
