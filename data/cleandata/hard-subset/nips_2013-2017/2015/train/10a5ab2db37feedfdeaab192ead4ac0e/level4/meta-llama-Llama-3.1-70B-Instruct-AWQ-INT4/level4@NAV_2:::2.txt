The authors propose a novel approach to reconcile the need for high precision of continuous variables in backpropagation algorithms with the desire to use spike-based neural network devices with binary synapses. By considering the analog variables as probabilities that are sampled to map the neural network used off-line for training to the spiking network chip used for deployment of the application, they introduce a new paradigm for training and deploying neural networks. This idea is well-motivated and clearly explained in the manuscript.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by adapting the backpropagation algorithm to be compatible with this strategy and validating the method proposed on the MNIST benchmark. The results are impressive, with the authors achieving high accuracy and low power consumption on the TrueNorth chip. The manuscript is well-structured and clear, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and methodology.

The originality of the work is one of its strongest aspects. The authors provide a thorough review of previous work in the area, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and the advantages of their proposed method. The significance of the work is also notable, as it has the potential to enable the deployment of neural networks on low-power, spike-based hardware.

However, there are a few areas where the manuscript could be improved. Firstly, the authors could provide more details on the specific applications where their method would be useful. While they mention the potential for deploying the TrueNorth chip in practical applications, they do not provide concrete examples or use cases. Additionally, the authors could compare their method to other alternative options, such as those cited in [9] to [12], to highlight its advantages and disadvantages.

In terms of clarity, the manuscript is well-written and easy to follow. However, there are a few areas where the authors could provide more details to help readers understand their methodology. For example, they could provide more information on how the inputs are converted into spikes, what frequencies are used, and what neuron models are employed.

Overall, the manuscript is well-written, clear, and original. The authors provide a compelling case for their proposed method, and the results are impressive. With a few minor revisions to address the areas mentioned above, the manuscript has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
