- Proposes a novel method to train networks with backpropagation by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities in [0,1].  
- Constrains network values to [0,1] and interprets them as spiking or synaptic activation probabilities for natural mapping to neuromorphic chips.  
- Demonstrates practical deployment on the TrueNorth chip, validating feasibility on both small and larger networks.  
- Validated on MNIST with competitive accuracy and state-of-the-art energy efficiency for digit recognition on TrueNorth.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and presents complex ideas digestibly.  
- Approach is original and addresses a significant problem in neuromorphic computing.  
- Reviewer suggests citing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 for a more complete related-work review.  
- Lacks a thorough discussion of broader significance and how the method enables real-world TrueNorth deployments.  
- Needs a comparative analysis against existing methods (e.g., refs [9–12]) to better demonstrate advantages.  
- Missing implementation details that would aid reproducibility: input encoding schemes (spike frequencies), shared weight strategies, and specific neuron models used.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong MNIST performance, clarity, and practical hardware validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of significance, missing implementation details, and insufficient comparative analysis.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, promising contribution with recommendation for acceptance despite areas needing more depth.
