- Novel approach reconciling continuous-variable precision with spike-based binary-synapse devices via probabilistic sampling mapping.
- Adapts backpropagation to be compatible with spiking neural networks for deployment on neuromorphic hardware.
- Manuscript is high-quality, clear, well-organized, original, and potentially useful across hardware platforms.
- Related work review is thorough but should also mention hardware like Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.
- Significance is underemphasized; paper should better explain practical benefits for TrueNorth deployment and advantages over alternatives ([9]–[12]).
- Lacks implementation details such as input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, shared weight values, and neuron models.
- Results on MNIST are convincing and indicate potential for optimizing accuracy and energy efficiency.
- Needs more comprehensive evaluations, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and detailed accuracy vs. energy trade-off analyses.
- Overall a valuable contribution to neuromorphic computing that could be more impactful with revisions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, clear presentation, and cross-platform applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of significance, incomplete evaluations, and missing implementation details.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
