- Proposes training spiking networks with backpropagation by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities, enabling mapping to neuromorphic hardware via sampling and ensemble averaging.
- Demonstrates the method on MNIST using the TrueNorth chip, achieving competitive accuracy with much better energy efficiency than prior approaches.
- Technically sound and complete work; probabilistic representation of spikes/synapses is well-motivated and clearly explained.
- Experimental validation on TrueNorth is a strong contribution, showing practical feasibility and energy-performance trade-offs.
- Paper evaluates both high-performance and high-efficiency network configurations and reports detailed trade-off results.
- Provides detailed network parameters and a clear weight initialization strategy, aiding reproducibility.
- Exceptionally clear, well-organized writing with helpful figures, equations, and accessible methodology description.
- Highly original combination of backpropagation with spiking/binary-synapse techniques and constrained, hardware-aligned connectivity.
- Novel network topology designed to match neuromorphic core-to-core connectivity.
- Related work is adequately cited but should consider adding Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 for completeness.
- Significant potential impact: enables low-power intelligent systems by combining algorithmic strengths with neuromorphic hardware.
- Needs broader discussion of applicability to more complex datasets and comparison to alternative deployment strategies ([9]-[12]).
- Reproducibility could be improved by providing more details on spike encoding (frequencies), neuron models, and shared weight values.
