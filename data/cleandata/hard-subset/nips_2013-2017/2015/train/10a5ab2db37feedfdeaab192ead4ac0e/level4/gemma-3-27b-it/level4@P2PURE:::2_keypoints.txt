- Proposes mapping backprop-trained continuous-value networks to binary-synapse spiking hardware by interpreting analog variables as sampling probabilities.  
- Adapts backpropagation to accommodate the probabilistic mapping required for spiking implementation.  
- Validates the method on MNIST and demonstrates feasibility.  
- Successfully maps the trained network onto two TrueNorth architectures optimized for size and accuracy, with performance reported from hardware.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, original, and potentially impactful across hardware platforms.  
- Suggests the authors should cite related hardware work (Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Requests clearer articulation of the practical significance and which applications benefit most from deployment on TrueNorth.  
- Asks for comparison and advantages relative to alternative spiking-network training strategies (refs [9]–[12]).  
- Calls for more implementation details: input encoding (spike conversion and frequencies).  
- Requests clarification on shared weight values used in hardware mapping.  
- Requests details on the neuron models employed in the implementation.
