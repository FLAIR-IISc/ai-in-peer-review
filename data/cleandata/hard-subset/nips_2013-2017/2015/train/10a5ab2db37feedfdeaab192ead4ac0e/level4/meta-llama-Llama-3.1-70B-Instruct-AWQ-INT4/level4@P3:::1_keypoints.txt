- Proposes training spiking, discrete-synapse neuromorphic networks by treating spikes and synapses as continuous probabilities so standard backpropagation can be used.  
- Trained probabilistic networks are mapped to hardware by sampling to create one or more networks and combining them via ensemble averaging.  
- Demonstrated on TrueNorth with MNIST: ensemble of 64 achieves 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ/image; single network achieves 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly explains methodology and experimental setup.  
- Experimental results are strong and significant, showing large improvements in accuracy and energy efficiency over prior work.  
- Approach has high potential for adoption in neuromorphic computing due to demonstrated trade-offs between accuracy and power.  
- Major novelty concerns: algorithm in Sec. 3.2 is very similar to prior works [14] and [15] and uses identical approximations.  
- The "constrain-then-train" methodology (learn distribution then sample ensemble) is not novel and was previously suggested/tested in ref [15].  
- Claim (i) of novelty in the introduction is misleading because expectation backpropagation (ref [15]) can be adapted to constrained connectivity.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to acknowledge prior related work and correct overstated novelty claims.
