1) Section 3.2 would benefit from acknowledging the strong resemblance of the presented algorithm to the work in [14] J. Zhao, J. Shawe-Taylor, and M. van Daalen, "Learning in stochastic bit stream neural networks," Neural Networks, vol. 9, no. 6, pp. 991 - 998, 1996 and [15] Soudry, Daniel, Itay Hubara, and Ron Meir. "Expectation backpropagation: Parameter-free training of multilayer neural networks with continuous or discrete weights." Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems. 2014, noting it represents a hybrid approach derived through similar approximations.
2) The discussion's opening paragraph currently implies novelty for the "constrain-then-train" methodology (learning a weight distribution followed by ensemble creation via sampling), yet this approach was previously proposed and evaluated in ref [15].
3) The novelty claim (i) presented in the introduction’s final paragraph appears overstated. The "expectation backpropagation" algorithm [15] constitutes a backpropagation update rule (utilizing expectation propagation) specifically designed for binary neurons (employing a sign activation function, avoiding exact zero inputs) and binary weights, and is readily adaptable to constrained connectivity scenarios.
4) The reported comparison to prior state-of-the-art results appears inaccurate. Based on my understanding, ref. [15] previously achieved superior performance with binary weights and neurons, demonstrated on the MNIST dataset (http://arxiv.org/pdf/1503.03562v3.pdf) without relying on data augmentation, a technique utilized in the current work.
