- Proposes a backpropagation training method for spiking neural networks designed for constrained multi-neuron, discrete-synapse hardware.  
- Method is valuable and practical given growing constrained hardware platforms.  
- Paper is generally well-written and accessible.  
- Claims in Section 1 should be rephrased to avoid implying a novel “first” demonstration of reduced-precision synapse training.  
- Novelty lies in combining probabilistic training with specific hardware constraints, not in individual components.  
- Demonstration on the TN platform should be presented as validation of the training method, not as the primary novel contribution.  
- Clarify the meaning of “0.15 bits per synapse.”  
- Provide a consolidated description of the network topology for clarity.  
- Quantify the increased training time from the probabilistic synaptic update scheme.  
- Explain why the 30-core ensemble performs worse than the 5-core network and why 5-core ensembles show consistent performance.  
- Report input spike rates during testing and clarify whether inputs are presented as spike trains for reproducibility.  
- Fix minor typographical errors (e.g., “approach approach” on line 399) and note that Reference 12 uses training constraints (e.g., bias=0), so it is not purely “train-then-constrain.”  
- Compare and discuss the approach relative to established spiking backprop methods (e.g., SpikeProp) and highlight specific hardware-related advantages.  
- While significant and potentially impactful, the hardware constraints are TN-specific and may not generalize; the need for a 64-ensemble to reach accuracy indicates substantial hardware resource demands that should be discussed.
