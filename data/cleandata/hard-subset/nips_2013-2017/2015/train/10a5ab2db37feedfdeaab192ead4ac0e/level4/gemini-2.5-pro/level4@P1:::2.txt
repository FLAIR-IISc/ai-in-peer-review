This paper presents a method for training spiking neural networks with low-precision, discrete synapses using backpropagation. The core contribution is a "constrain-then-train" approach that bridges the gap between the continuous-valued parameters required for backpropagation and the constraints of energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware, such as the IBM TrueNorth chip. The authors treat continuous variables like neuron outputs and synaptic connections as probabilities, which allows for standard gradient-based optimization during an offline training phase. The resulting probabilistic network is then sampled to create one or more instances of a spiking network for deployment. The authors validate their approach on the MNIST dataset, demonstrating a trade-off between high accuracy (99.42%) and high energy efficiency (0.268 µJ per image) by deploying different network configurations on the TrueNorth hardware.

**Strengths**

*   **Quality and Clarity:** The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, clear, and well-structured. The proposed methodology is presented logically, and the results are convincing.
*   **Originality:** The work is highly original. The concept of training a probabilistic network with backpropagation and then sampling from it for deployment on spiking hardware with binary synapses is a novel and elegant solution to a significant problem in the field.
*   **Potential Impact:** This method could be highly influential, as it provides a practical pathway for applying the power of deep learning to highly efficient neuromorphic computing platforms. The approach also appears generalizable to other hardware beyond TrueNorth.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

*   **Significance and Context:** While the technical contribution is strong, the paper does not fully articulate its broader significance. It would be beneficial to elaborate on the specific practical applications where this method could enable the deployment of the TrueNorth chip. Furthermore, the paper could be strengthened by a more direct comparison to the alternative approaches cited in references [9]-[12], clarifying the specific advantages of the proposed method over these existing techniques.

*   **Missing Implementation Details:** The paper lacks some key details that would be necessary for reproducibility. Specifically:
    *   How are the input pixel values converted into input spike probabilities (P(xi=1) ≡ x̃i)? Is this a simple linear mapping?
    *   The paper mentions that each image was encoded using a single time step (1 ms). Does this imply a rate-based code where the input probabilities are sampled once per image presentation?
    *   What are the specific shared weight values (sij) used? The initialization section describes a strategy of using +1 and -1, but are these the final values used during deployment?
    *   Could the authors provide more details on the specific neuron model and parameters used on the TrueNorth chip to implement the history-free thresholding dynamics?

*   **Related Work:** In the discussion of neuromorphic hardware platforms, the authors could consider including other relevant systems to provide a more complete overview. For instance:
    *   Qiao et al., "A reconfigurable 64k-neuron array with 16-M-synapse for spike-based neural and synaptic processing," Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015.
    *   Esser et al., "Convolutional networks for fast, energy-efficient neuromorphic computing," arXiv:1506.05427.

**Summary of Arguments**

*   **Arguments for Acceptance:** This paper introduces a novel and important technique for training networks for neuromorphic hardware. The method is sound, the results are strong, and the paper is very clearly written. It successfully reconciles the powerful backpropagation algorithm with the constraints of highly efficient spiking hardware.

*   **Arguments against Acceptance:** The paper could better position itself by elaborating on its significance and providing a clearer comparison with prior art. Several key implementation details are missing, which currently hinders reproducibility. These points should be addressed to strengthen the paper.
