- Paper tackles deploying deep learning on neuromorphic hardware by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable standard backpropagation.  
- Trained probabilistic networks are mapped to hardware via sampling and ensemble averaging; demonstrated on TrueNorth with MNIST achieving 99.42% (ensemble 64) and 0.268 μJ/image (ensemble 1).  
- Builds on prior work in stochastic binary neurons/synapses but claims novelty in combining spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, constrained connectivity, and backprop on real hardware.  
- Strength: Strong empirical results showing high accuracy and improved energy efficiency on TrueNorth.  
- Strength: Practical “constrain-then-train” strategy offering a clear pathway for neuromorphic deployment.  
- Strength: Clear presentation and thorough experimental setup.  
- Strength: Novel integration of multiple constraints into a functioning system.  
- Weakness: Novelty is overstated; learning weight distributions and sampling ensembles has been explored previously (notably [15]).  
- Weakness: Training algorithm closely resembles prior methods ([14], [15]) and should explicitly acknowledge these connections.  
- Weakness: Potentially inaccurate comparison to prior state-of-the-art on MNIST; prior best for binary weights/neurons may be in [15].  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of trade-offs between accuracy, energy consumption, and network size.  
- For acceptance: Significant empirical demonstration and practical applicability on real neuromorphic hardware.  
- Against acceptance: Overstated novelty, insufficient acknowledgement of related work, and questionable prior-art comparisons weaken the contribution.  
- Overall: Strong paper with significant potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify novelty and prior-work comparisons.
