The authors propose a novel approach to reconcile the need for high precision of continuous variables in backpropagation algorithms with the desire to utilize spike-based neural network devices with binary synapses. They introduce the concept of treating analog variables as probabilities that are sampled to map the neural network used offline for training to the spiking network chip used for deployment of the application. This adaptation enables the use of backpropagation algorithms on spiking neural networks with binary synapses. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method by validating it on the MNIST benchmark and mapping the trained network onto two different architectures, optimizing for size and accuracy. They also present performance figures measured from the chip.

The manuscript is of high quality, with excellent clarity. The work is original and has the potential to be useful for other approaches and hardware platforms. However, in reviewing the different hardware platforms available, the authors should also mention Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and the paper at http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.05427.

While the authors present a method for applying backpropagation to spiking neural networks with binary synapses and validate it by successfully mapping the trained network onto the TrueNorth device, the significance of the work is not clearly highlighted in the paper. It is not explicitly stated how the proposed method will be useful in deploying the TrueNorth chip in practical applications, and in what specific applications. Furthermore, it is not clear if and to what extent this specific method has advantages over other alternative options, such as those cited in [9] to [12].

The paper is well-structured and clear, and the results are convincing. However, some details are lacking, such as how the inputs are converted into spikes, the frequencies used, the shared weight values, and the neuron models. Providing this information would further strengthen the paper and enhance the understanding of the proposed method.
