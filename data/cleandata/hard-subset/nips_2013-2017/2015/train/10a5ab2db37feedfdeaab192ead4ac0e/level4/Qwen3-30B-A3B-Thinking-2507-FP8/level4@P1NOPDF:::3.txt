

The authors present an interesting method for backpropagation training that accounts for hardware constraints of spiking-neuron platforms with discrete synapses—a timely topic for mapping deep networks to emerging neuromorphic hardware.  

**Quality**: The proposed training method is valuable, particularly for hardware-aware deep learning development.  

**Clarity**: The paper is generally well-written, but two points need clarification. First, the concluding claims of Section 1 risk misinterpretation: as phrased, they imply the paper introduces the first training approach using spiking neurons and low-precision synapses. This requires careful rewording. Second, demonstrating the network on the TN platform validates the training method but does not constitute novelty.  

Section 2 raises several technical questions:  
- What does "0.15 bits per synapse" signify in terms of precision constraints?  
- The network topology lacks a unified description; a schematic or concise summary would improve clarity.  
- How much longer does training take due to probabilistic synaptic updates?  
- Why does the 30-core ensemble achieve lower accuracy than the 5-core network despite its larger scale?  
- Why do the 5-core ensemble results show minimal variance across trials?  
- What are the input spike rates during testing? Is the input a spike train?  
- Ref. 12 (line 399) states constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, contradicting the "train-then-constrain" description; note the typo "approach approach."  

**Originality**: The authors should explicitly compare their method with existing spiking backpropagation rules (e.g., SpikeProp) to contextualize their contribution.  

**Significance**: Hardware-aware training methods are highly relevant, but the constraints addressed here derive specifically from the TN architecture. These may not generalize to other platforms. Furthermore, the reported accuracy relies on a 64-ensemble approach, implying significant hardware resource overhead for marginal gains—raising questions about practical efficiency.  

The core contribution is sound: a training method that integrates hardware constraints for spiking-neuron platforms. However, the current presentation requires sharper technical precision and clearer justification of design choices. Addressing the points above would substantially strengthen the paper.
