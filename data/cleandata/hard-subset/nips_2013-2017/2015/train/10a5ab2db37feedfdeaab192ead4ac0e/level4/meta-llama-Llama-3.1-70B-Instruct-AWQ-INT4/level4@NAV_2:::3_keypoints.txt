- Presents a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities tailored to spiking-neuron, discrete-synapse hardware constraints.  
- Main contribution: a learning rule and network topology that reconcile backprop with neuromorphic hardware by training on continuous [0,1] spike/synapse probabilities for easy mapping to deployment.  
- Method is useful and thoroughly evaluated for accuracy and energy efficiency, but some Section 1 claims should be reworded to avoid implying novelty that it may not have.  
- Clarity issues: the 0.15 bits per synapse explanation and the network topology are insufficiently clear, and training-time overhead from probabilistic synapse updates is not reported.  
- Results questions: unexplained low performance for a single ensemble of the 30-core network versus the 5-core, similar outcomes across 5-core ensembles, and missing input spike-rate / spike-train details.  
- Originality: paper should compare and contrast with existing spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp) to better situate the work.  
- Significance: addressing hardware-aware training is important, but the TN-specific constraints may limit applicability—discussion of portability and limitations is needed.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel hardware-aware training approach, thorough performance/energy analysis, and well-written presentation.  
- Cons for acceptance: unclear topology and 0.15-bit explanation, unanswered questions in results, and insufficient discussion of applicability to other platforms.
