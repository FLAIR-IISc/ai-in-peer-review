- Introduces a method to bridge continuous-valued backpropagation with binary-synapse spiking neuromorphic hardware.  
- Reinterprets continuous activations and weights as probabilities and samples them for spiking deployment.  
- Adapts backpropagation to the probabilistic framework and validates on MNIST.  
- Deploys trained models on two TrueNorth hardware configurations (compactness-optimized and accuracy-optimized) and measures on-chip performance.  
- Manuscript is high quality, clear, and well organized.  
- Contribution is original and likely transferable to other methods and hardware targets.  
- Reviewer requests citing Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience (2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 when surveying hardware.  
- Paper does not sufficiently articulate practical significance for TrueNorth deployments or specific application domains.  
- Comparative benefits over alternative methods (e.g., refs [9]–[12]) are not clearly established.  
- Demonstrates a viable procedure for training spiking networks with binary synapses via backprop and deploying on TrueNorth.  
- Some experimental and implementation details are missing (input-to-spike encoding scheme, firing rates, shared/lookup weight values, neuron model configurations).
