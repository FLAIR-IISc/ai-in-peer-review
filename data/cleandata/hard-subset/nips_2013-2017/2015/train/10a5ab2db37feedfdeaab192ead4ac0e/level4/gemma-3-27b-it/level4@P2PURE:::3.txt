The authors present an interesting paper on a backpropagation training method for spiking neural networks, explicitly considering the hardware constraints of a platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses. This is a timely topic given increasing interest in mapping deep networks onto specialized multi-neuron hardware.

Quality: The proposed training method is valuable, particularly given the emergence of new, constrained multi-neuron hardware platforms.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and accessible. However, the claims made at the end of Section 1 could be rephrased. As currently stated, they may be misinterpreted to suggest the paper introduces the first training methodology employing spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses. Furthermore, demonstrating the network’s functionality on a specific hardware platform (TN in this case) should be presented as a validation of the training method, rather than a novel contribution in itself. 

In Section 2, the meaning of “0.15 bits per synapse” requires clarification. The network topology is not concisely described in a single location. What is the increase in training time attributable to the probabilistic synaptic connection update? The performance of a single ensemble within the 30-core network appears unexpectedly low, especially in comparison to the 5-core network. Why do the results across different ensembles of the 5-core network remain consistent? What are the spike rates of the inputs during testing, and is the input data presented as a spike train? Reference 12 (line 399) describes training with constraints (e.g., bias=0), and therefore does not represent a purely “train-then-constrain” approach (a typographical error appears on this line: “approach approach”).

Originality: 
Could the authors provide a discussion comparing their work to other established spiking backpropagation algorithms, such as SpikeProp?

Significance: The development of novel training methods that account for hardware constraints is an important contribution. However, the constraints considered here are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms. The reported results rely on a 64-ensemble configuration, which implies substantial hardware resources are required to achieve the desired network accuracy. This is an interesting paper describing a backpropagation training method that accounts for the hardware constraints of a spiking neural network platform with discrete synapses.
