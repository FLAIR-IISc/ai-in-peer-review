- Paper proposes a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities tailored to hardware with spiking neurons and discrete synapses (TrueNorth-like).
- Contribution is useful for mapping deep networks to multi-neuron hardware platforms and addresses incompatibility between backprop and neuromorphic hardware.
- Paper quality: method is practical and authors show clear understanding of hardware-constrained training challenges.
- Clarity: paper is well-written but Section 1 claims need rewording to avoid overstating novelty.
- Clarify meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse" in Section 2.
- Combine and more clearly describe the network topology in a single section.
- Discuss how probabilistic synaptic connection updates affect overall training time.
- Results lack reported training time for the 30-core network and comparison to the 5-core network’s training time.
- Explain why a single ensemble of the 30-core network performs surprisingly poorly compared to the 5-core network.
- Investigate whether similar results across 5-core ensembles indicate ensemble size has little impact on performance.
- Originality: include discussion/comparison with other spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp) to contextualize novelty.
- Significance: approach is relevant, but authors should discuss applicability of their TrueNorth-based constraints to other hardware platforms.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel reconciliation of backprop with neuromorphic constraints, strong understanding of challenges, well-written presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear network description and training-time implications, unanswered questions in results, and lack of comparison to prior spiking backprop methods.
