[14] J. Zhao, J. Shaw-Taylor, and M. Van Daleen, "Stochastic Bit Stream Learning in Neural Networks," Neural Networks, Vol. 9, No. 6, pp. 991-998, 1996. and [15] Soudry, Daniel, Ete हुबारा, and Ron Meir. In its final form (which is a hybrid of both algorithms), and obtained using similar estimates.
2) The first paragraph in the discussion gives the impression that the "construct-then-train" method (learning a weight distribution and then sampling from it to create a fixed network ensemble at test time) is new, even though it was already suggested and tested in ref [15].
3) The claim of novelty in the last paragraph of introduction (i) is also misleading. Ref. [15] The "expectation backpropagation" algorithm is a backpropagation update rule (using expectation propagation), implemented for binary neurons (i.e., with a sine activation function, in which the zero input never reaches exactly zero) with binary weights, and it can easily have interrupted connectivity.
4) The previous state-of-the-art reference is incorrect. To my knowledge, the previous best result for binary weights (and neurons) was obtained by the algorithm in ref. [15], which was tested on MNIST without data augmentation (http://arxiv.org/pdf/1503.03562v3.pdf), as was done in this paper.
