1) Section 3.2 should explicitly acknowledge the algorithm’s strong resemblance to methods in [14] and [15], noting the final hybrid was derived using similar approximations.  
2) The discussion’s opening misleadingly presents the "constrain-then-train" approach as novel, though it was previously proposed and evaluated in [15].  
3) Novelty claim (i) in the introduction is inaccurate: expectation backpropagation from [15] is a backprop-based update for binary neurons/weights and can be extended to constrained connectivity.  
4) The cited prior state-of-the-art is incorrect; the best previous results for binary weights/neurons on MNIST were from [15] (no data augmentation), matching the paper’s experimental setup.  
5) Edited conclusion: experiments show strong accuracy and power-efficiency, but the overall methodology and algorithmic contribution are not as novel as claimed.
