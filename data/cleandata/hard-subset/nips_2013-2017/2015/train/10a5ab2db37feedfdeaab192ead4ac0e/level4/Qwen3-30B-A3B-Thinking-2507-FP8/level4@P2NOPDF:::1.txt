[15] achieved superior MNIST accuracy (99.3% without data augmentation) compared to the current paper's reported results. The paper's experiments used data augmentation (unlike Ref. [15]), invalidating direct comparisons. The cited "previous state-of-the-art" is thus misstated.  

While the paper presents strong experimental results in accuracy and power efficiency, the methodological and algorithmic contributions are overstated. The core approach builds upon Ref. [14] and Ref. [15] without sufficient attribution or differentiation. The novelty claims require significant revision to accurately reflect the existing literature. The experimental validation is valuable, but the theoretical foundation must be properly contextualized.
