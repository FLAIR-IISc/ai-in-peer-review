- Proposes a probabilistic framework to map high-precision training variables to binary-synapse spiking hardware by treating analog values as sampling probabilities.  
- Adapts backpropagation to support the probabilistic training-to-spiking conversion.  
- Validates the approach on MNIST and demonstrates deployment on two TrueNorth hardware configurations with measured chip performance.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally well-written, technically high quality, and original.  
- Claims potential applicability to broader hardware platforms beyond TrueNorth.  
- Fails to articulate concrete practical applications or real-world use cases enabled by this deployment method.  
- Does not substantiate advantages over alternative methods (e.g., refs [9]–[12]) through comparative analysis or trade-off discussion.  
- Omits citation of relevant hardware work (Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015 and arXiv:1506.05427).  
- Lacks essential implementation details: input spike encoding strategy is not described.  
- Missing information on spike frequencies and shared weight values used in experiments.  
- Does not specify the neuron models employed, hindering reproducibility and deeper evaluation.
