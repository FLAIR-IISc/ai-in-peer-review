

Quality: The proposed training method is useful for addressing hardware constraints on emerging multi-neuron platforms with spiking neurons and discrete synapses, making it timely for hardware-aware deep learning.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. However, the claims in the final paragraph of Section 1 require rewording; as currently phrased, they risk implying the paper proposes the first training methodology using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses. This is inaccurate. Additionally, demonstrating network execution on the specific TN platform should not be presented as a novel feature; it serves only as validation of the training method, not as a core contribution.

Section 2 requires clarification on the meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse." The network topology is not clearly described in a single location. The paper also does not specify how much longer training takes due to the probabilistic synaptic update mechanism. The results for a single ensemble of the 30-core network are notably low compared to the 5-core network; this discrepancy needs explanation. Furthermore, the results across different ensembles of the 5-core network are nearly identical—this consistency requires discussion. The spike rates for inputs during testing and whether inputs are spike trains are also unaddressed. Ref 12 (line 399) includes constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, so it is not merely a "train-then-constrain" approach (typo: "approach approach").

Originality: The authors should discuss and compare their approach with other spiking backpropagation rules, such as SpikeProp, to better establish novelty.

Significance: While developing hardware-constrained training methods is highly significant, the constraints considered here are specific to the TN architecture. These constraints may not apply broadly to other platforms. The results reported for the 64-ensemble case imply that achieving the stated accuracy requires dedicating substantial additional hardware resources, which is an important consideration for practical deployment.
