Summary
This paper proposes a backpropagation-based training method for spiking neuromorphic hardware with low-precision synapses and constrained connectivity. The key idea is to represent spikes and synaptic on/off states as continuous probabilities in [0,1], enabling standard gradient-based training while matching the constraints of the deployment hardware. After training, networks are mapped to hardware (TrueNorth) by sampling these probabilities to instantiate one or more spiking networks, whose outputs are combined via ensemble averaging. The network topology respects TrueNorth’s limited fan-in per core using a structured sliding-window connectivity. On MNIST, the authors report 99.42% accuracy with an ensemble of 64 networks (108 μJ per image) and 92.7% with a single network (0.268 μJ per image), both running in real time.

Relative to prior work, the paper connects strands that have typically been addressed separately: training spiking networks with high-precision weights, training networks with discrete synapses but continuous neurons, and binary/binary probabilistic backprop under full connectivity. The authors claim novelty in combining spiking neurons, sub-trinary synaptic precision, and constrained connectivity within an offline backpropagation framework, and in demonstrating state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy for spiking systems alongside real-time deployment on TrueNorth.

Quality
- Strengths: The constrain-then-train strategy is principled and matches deployment constraints, avoiding post hoc discretization. The probabilistic forward model (Gaussian approximation of weighted Bernoulli inputs) and the derived gradients are reasonable, and the initialization scheme to decorrelate core outputs is thoughtful. The empirical results show strong energy–accuracy trade-offs and a clean hardware mapping.
- Concerns/questions:
  - Assumptions: The Gaussian and independence assumptions, and the approximation dropping the σ^−3 term in the gradient, merit empirical justification (e.g., sensitivity analyses, ablations).
  - Training cost: Please quantify the training-time overhead introduced by learning synaptic connection probabilities (e.g., wall-clock time, convergence rate compared to a matched dense/continuous baseline).
  - Architecture details: The network topology is described across multiple sections; consolidating all architectural parameters (R, F, S per layer, number of neurons per core, class-neuron allocation) in one place would improve reproducibility.
  - Ensemble behavior: Why is the single-member 30-core network relatively weak compared to the 5-core network, and why do different ensemble sizes of the 5-core network yield similar accuracy? A discussion of variance reduction vs. bias across ensemble size would help.
  - Input encoding: Please clarify the input spike rates at test time. Is each image presented as a single 1 ms Bernoulli sample per input line, or as a spike train over multiple timesteps in any experiments? How sensitive are results to the encoding duration?
  - Hardware accounting: In Section 2, “0.15 bits per synapse” for the lookup-table scheme is unclear. Please explain the derivation (e.g., amortized bits per synapse given 4 axon types and per-neuron tables).
  - Claims in Section 1: The phrasing could be read as claiming the first training method with spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses. Please reword to clearly delineate what is and is not novel (e.g., the combination plus constrained connectivity and the specific probabilistic mapping), and treat the TrueNorth demonstration as validation rather than a novelty in itself.
  - Related work: Ref. 12 includes training-time constraints (e.g., threshold/bias balancing) and is not purely train-then-constrain; please adjust the discussion accordingly. Also fix the typo “approach approach.”

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-written. Suggestions:
- Provide a self-contained figure/table with the complete topology and mapping parameters.
- Add a brief intuitive explanation of the initialization objective (minimizing column inner products to reduce redundancy) and how the −1/1 assignments and axon-type balancing achieve it.
- Include a short paragraph justifying the probabilistic approximations and when they may fail (e.g., low fan-in, strong input correlations).

Originality
The probabilistic interpretation of spikes/synapses coupled with backprop under strict connectivity and precision constraints is a compelling combination. For completeness, please compare and contrast with spiking backprop variants such as SpikeProp and its successors, emphasizing differences in neuron models, discretization, gradient surrogates, and hardware-awareness. A discussion of how your method relates to recent surrogate-gradient methods for SNNs would also strengthen the positioning.

Significance
Bridging high-performing backprop training with energy-efficient neuromorphic deployment is important. Demonstrating real-time classification with μJ-level energy per inference is impactful for embedded applications. That said:
- Generality: Many constraints arise from TrueNorth (axon types, binary crossbar, fixed fan-in). Please discuss how the method would adapt to other platforms (e.g., different synapse precisions, neuron models, routing limits), and which parts are platform-agnostic.
- Resource trade-offs: High accuracy relies on ensembles (e.g., 64 members using 1920 cores). Please quantify the hardware/latency implications and characterize the accuracy vs. ensemble size trade-off to guide system designers.

Pros
- Clear, hardware-aware training framework with a direct mapping to spiking deployment.
- Strong energy–accuracy trade-offs and real-time performance on silicon.
- Thoughtful initialization and topology design to fit connectivity constraints.

Cons
- Limited analysis of the approximations underlying the probabilistic gradients.
- Incomplete clarity on topology, training cost, and input encoding details.
- Claims of novelty could be more carefully scoped; related work discussion needs refinement.
- Heavy reliance on ensembles for peak accuracy without a detailed resource analysis.

Recommendation
A solid and timely contribution. With clarifications to claims and related work, additional analysis of the probabilistic assumptions, and consolidated architectural details and training/ensemble trade-offs, this paper would make a valuable addition to the conference.
