- Presents a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities for hardware with spiking neurons and discrete synapses, relevant for mapping deep networks to multi-neuron platforms.  
- Proposed method is useful for emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints.  
- Well-written paper, but end-of-Section-1 claims should be reworded to avoid implying it is the first method using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses.  
- Demonstration on the TN hardware is validation of the method, not a novel contribution in itself.  
- Clarify what "0.15 bits per synapse" means.  
- Provide a single clear description of the network topology.  
- Report how much longer training takes due to probabilistic synaptic updates.  
- Explain why a single 30-core network ensemble performs worse than the 5-core network.  
- Explain why different 5-core network ensembles produce nearly identical results.  
- State input spike rates during testing and whether inputs are presented as spike trains.  
- Note that Ref. 12 includes training constraints (e.g., bias=0) so it is not purely a "train-then-constrain" approach; fix the "approach approach" typo.  
- Discuss or compare the method with other spiking backpropagation rules (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- The addressed constraints are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms.  
- If achieving results relies on a 64-ensemble, this requires additional hardware resources to reach target accuracy.  
- Overall: an interesting, relevant paper on hardware-aware backpropagation for spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
