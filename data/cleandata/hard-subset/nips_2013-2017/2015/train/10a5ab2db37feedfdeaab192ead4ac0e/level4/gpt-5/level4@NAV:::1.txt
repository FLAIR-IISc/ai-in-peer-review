1) The authors should explicitly note that the algorithm in Section 3.2 is highly similar to those in the cited works [14] J. Zhao, J. Shawe-Taylor, and M. van Daalen, "Learning in stochastic bit stream neural networks," Neural Networks, vol. 9, no. 6, pp. 991–998, 1996, and [15] Soudry, Daniel, Itay Hubara, and Ron Meir. "Expectation backpropagation: Parameter-free training of multilayer neural networks with continuous or discrete weights." Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems, 2014, in its final form (a hybrid of both algorithms), and was derived using the same approximations.

2) The first paragraph of the discussion implies that the "constrain-then-train" approach (learn a weight distribution, then sample from it to form a fixed ensemble at test time) is novel, although this was already proposed and evaluated in ref [15].

3) Novelty claim (i) in the last paragraph of the introduction is also misleading. Ref. [15]’s "expectation backpropagation" is a backpropagation update rule (via expectation propagation), implemented for binary neurons (sign activation, where zero input is effectively never reached) with binary weights, and it can readily support constrained connectivity.

4) The cited prior state-of-the-art is incorrect. To the best of my knowledge, the previous strongest results for binary weights (and neurons) were obtained by the algorithm in ref. [15], evaluated on MNIST (http://arxiv.org/pdf/1503.03562v3.pdf) without data augmentation, as in this paper.
