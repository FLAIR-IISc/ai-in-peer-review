

The authors propose a novel method to reconcile high-precision continuous-variable requirements in backpropagation with the constraints of spike-based neural network hardware featuring binary synapses. They introduce a probabilistic framework where analog variables during training are treated as probabilities, sampled to map the trained network onto a spiking neural network (SNN) hardware platform for deployment. The authors adapt backpropagation to this strategy, validate it on MNIST, and demonstrate successful mapping onto two TrueNorth hardware configurations optimized for size and accuracy, reporting measured performance from the chip. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, with high technical quality and clarity. The work is original and holds potential utility for broader hardware platforms beyond the specific context explored.  

However, several critical points require attention. The significance of the approach is underdeveloped: the paper fails to articulate concrete practical applications where this method enables TrueNorth deployment, nor does it clarify how it specifically addresses real-world use cases. Additionally, the advantages of this method over alternatives (e.g., those referenced in [9]–[12]) are not substantiated through comparative analysis or discussion of trade-offs. The authors should also cite relevant hardware platform work, including Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and [arXiv:1506.05427].  

While the results are convincing, the methodology lacks essential implementation details: the input spike encoding strategy, employed spike frequencies, shared weight values, and specific neuron models used are not sufficiently described. These omissions hinder reproducibility and deeper evaluation of the proposed technique.
