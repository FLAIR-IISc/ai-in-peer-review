This paper presents an interesting backpropagation training method for spiking neural networks, specifically designed with the hardware constraints of a neuromorphic platform – one featuring spiking neurons and discrete synapses – in mind. This is a timely and relevant topic, given the increasing interest in mapping deep learning models onto specialized, multi-neuron hardware.

**Quality:** The proposed training method is valuable, particularly in the context of emerging neuromorphic hardware platforms with inherent limitations. The work demonstrates a practical approach to training spiking networks under realistic hardware constraints, addressing a key challenge in the field.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. However, the claims made at the end of Section 1 could be rephrased to avoid potential misinterpretation. As currently stated, they suggest the novelty lies in *being the first* to employ spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses in a training methodology. A more accurate framing would emphasize the specific method developed to address hardware constraints within a backpropagation framework. Furthermore, while demonstrating the network’s functionality on the TrueNorth (TN) platform is a significant achievement, it should be positioned as a *validation* of the training method, rather than a primary novel contribution.

Several points require further clarification. In Section 2, the quantification of “0.15 bits per synapse” needs elaboration. What does this value represent in terms of synaptic weight resolution, and how does it impact network performance? The network topology is described piecemeal throughout the paper; a consolidated description in a single location would greatly improve readability. The paper should also discuss the computational overhead introduced by the probabilistic synaptic connection updates – specifically, how much longer does training take compared to a deterministic approach? 

The results presented are also open to questions. The low accuracy obtained from a single ensemble of the 30-core network, especially in comparison to the 5-core network, requires explanation. Similarly, the consistency of results across different ensembles of the 5-core network warrants investigation; what accounts for this stability? Finally, information regarding input characteristics is missing. What are the spike rates for inputs during testing? Is the input data presented as a spike train, or converted from a rate code? A minor editorial note: Line 399 contains a typographical error (“approach approach”) and the discussion of Ref 12 is slightly inaccurate. The reference explicitly includes constraints (e.g., bias=0) *during* training, rather than solely relying on a “train-then-constrain” paradigm.

**Originality:** While the paper tackles an important problem, it would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work. Specifically, the authors should position their method relative to other spiking backpropagation algorithms, such as SpikeProp. A comparative analysis, outlining the strengths and weaknesses of each approach, would strengthen the paper’s originality claim.

**Significance:** The development of new training methods that explicitly consider hardware constraints is a crucial step towards realizing the potential of neuromorphic computing. However, the current work’s constraints are primarily dictated by the TN architecture. It’s important to discuss the generalizability of these constraints – will they apply to other neuromorphic platforms with different characteristics? Furthermore, the reliance on a 64-ensemble configuration to achieve satisfactory accuracy suggests a potentially significant hardware resource requirement, which should be acknowledged. 

In summary, this is a well-written and relevant paper addressing a key challenge in the field of neuromorphic computing. Addressing the points raised above regarding clarity, comparison to related work, and generalizability would significantly strengthen the manuscript. The contribution is valuable, and the work is worthy of acceptance, contingent upon revisions addressing these concerns.
