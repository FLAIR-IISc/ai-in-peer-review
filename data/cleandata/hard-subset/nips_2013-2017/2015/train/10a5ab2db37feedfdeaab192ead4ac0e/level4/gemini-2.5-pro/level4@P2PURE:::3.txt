This paper presents a backpropagation-based training method for spiking neural networks that accounts for the hardware constraints of a specific neuromorphic platform with discrete synapses. This is a timely and relevant topic, given the growing interest in mapping deep networks onto such hardware.

**Quality and Significance:**
The proposed training method is valuable, particularly for its consideration of hardware constraints, which is a critical step for the practical application of neural networks on platforms like TrueNorth. The development of new training methods that are co-designed with hardware is of great interest to the community.

**Clarity and Suggestions for Improvement:**
While the paper is generally easy to read, several areas could be improved for clarity and precision.

*   **Clarity of Contribution:** The claims at the end of Section 1 could be reworded. As they stand, they could be misread to suggest that this is the first training methodology for spiking neurons with reduced-precision synapses. Furthermore, the demonstration on the TrueNorth platform should be framed as a validation of the training method's effectiveness rather than a novel contribution in itself.

*   **Request for Clarification:** The following points require further explanation:
    *   What is the precise meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse" in Section 2?
    *   The network topology is not clearly described in a single, consolidated section.
    *   What is the overhead in training time resulting from the probabilistic synaptic connection updates?
    *   What are the input spike rates during testing, and is the input a spike train?

*   **Experimental Results:** The rationale behind some of the results is unclear:
    *   Why is the performance of a single 30-core network ensemble so low, especially when compared to the 5-core network?
    *   Conversely, why are the performance figures for the different ensembles of the 5-core network so similar to one another?

*   **Related Work:**
    *   The paper would be strengthened by a discussion and comparison with other backpropagation-inspired rules for spiking networks, such as SpikeProp.
    *   The characterization of reference [12] on line 399 as solely a "train-then-constrain" approach is inaccurate, as that work incorporates constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training. Please also correct the typo on this line ("approach approach").

**Potential Limitations:**
*   **Generalizability:** The constraints considered during training are specific to the TrueNorth architecture. A discussion on how these constraints and the proposed method might (or might not) apply to other neuromorphic platforms would be beneficial.
*   **Resource Cost:** Achieving high accuracy requires a 64-network ensemble, which implies a significant dedication of hardware resources. This trade-off between accuracy and resource cost warrants further discussion.
