- Core idea: Train spiking networks by treating spikes and binary synapses as continuous probabilities, learn with modified backpropagation, then sample deterministic networks and ensemble outputs for deployment.

- Validation: Trained sparse networks on MNIST and deployed on IBM TrueNorth, showing trade-offs between accuracy and energy (e.g., 99.42% with 64-network ensemble at 108 µJ/image; 92.7% with a single network at 0.268 µJ/image).

- Strength — Significance: Addresses the important problem of running high-performance networks on power-constrained neuromorphic hardware.

- Strength — Novelty: Elegant “constrain-then-train” approach reframes non-differentiable hardware elements as probabilities to enable gradient-based optimization.

- Strength — Empirical results: Strong real-hardware results and detailed accuracy vs. energy analysis validate practical utility.

- Strength — Practical demonstration: Provides a full pipeline from training to deployment on a large-scale neuromorphic platform (TrueNorth).

- Weakness — Generalizability: Method seems tightly coupled to TrueNorth-specific constraints; unclear how it adapts to other neuromorphic architectures.

- Weakness — Ensemble resource cost: Top accuracy requires a 64x ensemble, implying large increases in hardware usage and energy, which may limit applicability.

- Weakness — Unclear or surprising experimental behaviors: Single 30-core vs. 5-core network performances and the saturation of 5-core ensemble improvements lack explanation.

- Weakness — Limited comparisons: Needs broader comparison to other SNN training paradigms (e.g., SpikeProp) and ANN-to-SNN conversion techniques to contextualize contributions.

- Suggestion — Clarify novelty claims: Rephrase claims to emphasize addressing the combination of spiking neurons, extreme synapse quantization, and connectivity constraints within a backpropagation framework.

- Suggestion — Technical clarifications: Explain "additional 0.15 bits per synapse" for the lookup table, consolidate topology parameters (R, F, S) for 5- and 30-core networks, and report training-time overhead versus standard backprop.

- Suggestion — Input encoding details: Provide specifics on MNIST input encoding during testing (e.g., rate-coding, timesteps, spike rates).

- Suggestion — Analyze results: Explain single 30-core network performance and why 5-core ensemble accuracy saturates with more members.

- Suggestion — Related work: Add discussion comparing the approach to ANN-to-SNN conversions and direct SNN training rules, and nuance claims about prior works like [12].

- Minor correction: Fix typo "approach approach" and revise characterization of [12] to reflect its hardware-aware constraints during training.
