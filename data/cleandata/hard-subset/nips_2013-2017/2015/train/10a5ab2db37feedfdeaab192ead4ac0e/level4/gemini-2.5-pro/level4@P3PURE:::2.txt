This paper proposes a method to reconcile the high-precision continuous variables required by backpropagation with the binary synapses of spike-based neural hardware. The core idea is to treat the continuous-valued weights of an offline-trained network as probabilities, which are then sampled to configure the binary synaptic states of a spiking neural network chip for deployment. The authors adapt the backpropagation algorithm to be compatible with this stochastic mapping strategy and validate their approach on the MNIST benchmark. The resulting network is mapped onto two different TrueNorth chip configurations, optimized for either size or accuracy, and the paper presents performance metrics measured directly from the hardware.

The manuscript is of high quality, well-structured, and clearly written. The proposed method is original and its principles could be valuable for other research efforts and hardware platforms beyond TrueNorth. The results are convincing and demonstrate a successful application of backpropagation to a spiking neural network with binary synapses deployed on the TrueNorth device.

However, the paper has several weaknesses that should be addressed.

A major weakness is that the broader significance of the work is not sufficiently highlighted. The paper does not clearly articulate how this method facilitates the deployment of the TrueNorth chip in practical scenarios or specify which applications would benefit most. Furthermore, the advantages of this specific method over alternative approaches, such as those cited in references [9] to [12], are not clearly discussed or quantified.

The experimental results, while convincing, lack several key implementation details that would be necessary for reproducibility. Specifically, the paper should clarify: how the inputs are converted into spikes, the spike frequencies used during operation, the values of the shared weights, and the specific neuron models employed on the chip.

Finally, in their review of available hardware platforms, the authors should also consider citing Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and the work presented in arXiv:1506.05427.
