1) The authors should clarify that the algorithm detailed in Section 3.2 is conceptually analogous to the approaches in the cited references [14] and [15]. Its final implementation is effectively a synthesis of these prior algorithms and is derived using the same foundational approximations.

2) The first paragraph of the Discussion section incorrectly suggests the novelty of the "constrain-then-train" methodology. This approach, which involves learning a weight distribution and then sampling from it to create a fixed network for inference, was previously proposed and validated in reference [15].

3) The novelty claim (i) in the introduction requires revision. The "Expectation Backpropagation" algorithm from reference [15] already serves as a backpropagation update rule based on expectation propagation, is implemented for binary neurons and weights, and can accommodate constrained connectivity.

4) The cited state-of-the-art benchmarks appear to be inaccurate. To my knowledge, the leading results for networks with both binary weights and neurons were achieved by the algorithm in reference [15]. That work was also evaluated on the MNIST dataset without data augmentation, making it a more appropriate comparison for the results presented here.
