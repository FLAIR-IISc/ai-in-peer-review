- Proposed backpropagation training method for spiking networks is valuable for constrained multi-neuron hardware.  
- Paper is well-written, but claims in Section 1 should be rephrased to avoid implying it is the first such method; hardware demonstration (TN) should be framed as validation, not a novel contribution.  
- Clarify the meaning of “0.15 bits per synapse.”  
- Provide a concise, single-location description of the network topology.  
- Quantify the increase in training time due to the probabilistic synaptic connection update.  
- Explain why a single ensemble in the 30-core network performs unexpectedly poorly, and why 5-core network ensembles show consistent results.  
- Report input spike rates during testing and confirm whether inputs are presented as spike trains.  
- Note that Reference 12 uses constrained training (e.g., bias=0) and is not purely “train-then-constrain”; fix the “approach approach” typo.  
- Include a discussion comparing this method to established spiking backpropagation algorithms (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- The considered constraints are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms.  
- Achieving reported accuracy relies on a 64-ensemble configuration, implying substantial hardware resources.
