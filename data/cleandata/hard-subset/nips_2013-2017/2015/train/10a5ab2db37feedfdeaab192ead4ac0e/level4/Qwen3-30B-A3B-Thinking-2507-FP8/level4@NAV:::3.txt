

Quality: The proposed training method is valuable for emerging multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints.  
Clarity: The paper is well-written. However, the claims in Section 1’s conclusion require rewording, as they currently imply this work is the first to propose a training methodology using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses. The demonstration of network execution on the TN platform should not be presented as a novel feature; it serves as validation of the training method. In Section 2, clarify the meaning of "0.15 bits per synapse." The network topology is not explicitly described in a single location. Address the additional training time due to probabilistic synaptic updates. Explain why the 30-core ensemble results are significantly lower than the 5-core results and why the 5-core ensemble results show minimal variation. Specify the input spike rates during testing and whether inputs are spike trains. Ref 12 (line 399) includes constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, so it is not merely a "train-then-constrain" approach (typo: "approach approach").  
Originality: Please discuss or compare the method with other spiking backpropagation rules, such as SpikeProp.  
Significance: While developing hardware-constrained training methods is highly relevant, the constraints considered here are specific to the TN architecture and may not generalize to other platforms. Additionally, achieving results via a 64-ensemble approach likely requires substantial hardware resources, which impacts practicality.
