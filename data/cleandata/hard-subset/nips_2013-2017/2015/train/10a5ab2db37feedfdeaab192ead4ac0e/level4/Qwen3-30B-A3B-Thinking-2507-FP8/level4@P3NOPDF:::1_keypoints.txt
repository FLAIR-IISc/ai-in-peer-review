- Experimental results are promising (strong accuracy and 4.1× power reduction) but novelty is overstated.  
- Section 3.2’s algorithm is fundamentally derived from the same approximations as [14] and [15]; the lineage must be explicitly acknowledged.  
- The presented method is effectively a hybrid of [14] (stochastic bit-stream approximations) and [15] (expectation backpropagation), not a novel algorithm.  
- The paper’s description obscures the connection to prior work and misrepresents the contribution’s originality.  
- The “constrain-then-train” methodology is not novel; [15] explicitly proposed and validated this approach for binary networks.  
- Claim (i) that expectation backpropagation ([15]) “cannot handle constrained connectivity” is factually incorrect; [15] does support constrained/sparse connectivity.  
- The MNIST SOTA comparison is incorrect: [15] reports 99.3% (no augmentation), not 99.0%, under the same protocol.  
- Manuscript must be revised to credit [14] and [15], correct the constrain-then-train claim, retract the false technical claim about constrained connectivity, and update the SOTA comparison.  
- While empirical contributions are valuable, misrepresentation of prior work undermines credibility and cannot be justified by experiments alone.  
- Recommendation: Major Revision — reject unless the authors fully revise the manuscript to address all points above.
