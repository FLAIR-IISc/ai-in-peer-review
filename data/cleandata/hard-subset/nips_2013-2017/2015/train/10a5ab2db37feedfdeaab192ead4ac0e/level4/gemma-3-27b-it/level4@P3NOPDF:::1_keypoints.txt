- Positive experimental results: strong accuracy with binary weights/activations and significant power savings.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions.  
- Main concern: paper overstates methodological and algorithmic novelty.  
- "Constrain-then-train" approach is not new—previously proposed by Soudry et al. [15].  
- Expectation Propagation in Soudry et al. already provides a backprop-like update for binary neurons and weights, undermining novelty claim (i).  
- Algorithm in Section 3.2 closely resembles Zhao et al. [14] and Soudry et al. [15]; authors must acknowledge and explain similarities/differences.  
- Comparison to prior state-of-the-art is inaccurate: best prior binary results were obtained by Soudry et al. on MNIST without data augmentation.  
- Experiments should include MNIST results without data augmentation for fair comparison.  
- Specific revision requests: revise intro novelty claim, rephrase discussion to cite prior art, add explicit relation to Zhao and Soudry in Section 3.2, and add MNIST (no augmentation) experiments.  
- Overall: valuable contribution for efficient deep learning but must clarify relation to prior work and temper novelty claims.
