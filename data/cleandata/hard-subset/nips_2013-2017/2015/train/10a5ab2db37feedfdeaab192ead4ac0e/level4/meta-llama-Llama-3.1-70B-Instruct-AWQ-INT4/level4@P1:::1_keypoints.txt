- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" methodology to train networks that map directly to neuromorphic hardware (TrueNorth), demonstrated on MNIST.  
- Experimental results claim state-of-the-art accuracy and improved power efficiency on TrueNorth, with detailed accuracy vs. power trade-off analysis.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains methodology, experiments, benefits, and limitations.  
- Major concern: algorithm in Sec. 3.2 is very similar to refs [14] and [15], using identical approximations; authors should acknowledge and clarify differences.  
- The "constrain-then-train" methodology is not novel—ref [15] already suggested and tested it; the paper must state how it differs or builds upon that work.  
- Novelty claim (i) in the introduction is misleading because ref [15]'s expectation backprop is a backprop update for binary neurons/weights and can support constrained connectivity.  
- Reviewer asserts the cited previous SOTA is incorrect; ref [15] likely had the previous best MNIST results for binary weights/neurons without augmentation.  
- Reviewer asks the authors to explicitly compare/contrast their algorithm with [14]/[15], acknowledge prior work, correct the novelty claim, and update SOTA citations.  
- Despite novelty concerns, the experimental contributions are strong and could make a significant contribution after revisions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical gains, useful trade-off analysis, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable originality and insufficient discussion of limitations/drawbacks.  
- Overall recommendation: revise the paper to address the cited concerns and resubmit.
