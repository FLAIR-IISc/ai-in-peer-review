The author presents an interesting research paper on a backpropagation training method using spike probability, considering the hardware constraints of a specific platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses. This is a topic of current interest in mapping deep networks onto multi-neuron hardware platforms.

**Quality:** The proposed training method is particularly useful in the consideration of new multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints.
**Clarity:** The paper is easy to read. The claims made at the end of Section 1 can be rewritten because, if not read carefully, it suggests that the paper is proposing a training method for the first time that uses spiking neurons and low-precision synapses. I will not include the performance of running this network on a specific hardware platform, in this case TN, as a novel feature. Running the network on TN is a verification of the training method.

In Section 2, what does 0.15 bits per synapse mean? The network topology is not clearly described in one place. How much longer is the training time due to potential synaptic connection updates? Why is the result so low from a single ensemble of a 30-core network, especially when compared to a 5-core network? Why are the results of different ensembles of 5-core networks almost the same? What are the spike rates for input during testing? Is the input a spike train? Ref 12 line 399 includes constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, so it is not just a train-then-constraint approach (typo on this line, "approach approach").

**Originality:**
Could the author provide a discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop-type rules like SpikeProp?
**Importance:** The development of new training methods that consider hardware platform constraints is a topic of great interest. The constraints considered during training in this paper are based on the TN architecture; these constraints may not apply to other platforms. If the results are based on a 64-ensemble, it means that more hardware resources will have to be dedicated to achieving the required accuracy of the network. An interesting paper about a training method using backpropagation that takes into account the hardware constraints of a specific platform with spiking neurons and discrete synapses.
