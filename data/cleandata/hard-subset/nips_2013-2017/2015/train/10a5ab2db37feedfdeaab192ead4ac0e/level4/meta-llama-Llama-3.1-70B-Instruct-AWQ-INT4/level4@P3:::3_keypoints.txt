- Proposes a novel backpropagation-based training method tailored to hardware constraints of spiking neurons and discrete synapses.  
- Introduces a "constrain-then-train" approach that constrains the network to match the deployment system before training.  
- Enables direct mapping from the training network to the deployment network, allowing efficient backprop-based training for neuromorphic hardware.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness by training on MNIST and deploying on the TrueNorth chip with high accuracy and low power consumption.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides thorough descriptions of topology, training, and mapping procedures.  
- Results are impressive, achieving state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy while consuming significantly less power than alternatives.  
- Main novelty bridges deep learning and neuromorphic computing, offering practical deployment benefits.  
- Lacks a clear, detailed comparison with other spiking backpropagation methods (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- Scalability to larger datasets and more complex networks is not addressed and remains unclear.  
- Constraints used during training are specific to TrueNorth, possibly limiting applicability to other neuromorphic platforms.  
- Quality rated 9/10: technically sound and well-motivated.  
- Clarity rated 9/10: easy to follow and well-explained.  
- Originality rated 8/10: novel contribution but needs clearer comparison to prior spiking backprop rules.  
- Significance rated 9/10: strong potential for enabling low-power deployment, but applicability and scalability need discussion.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, effective demonstration on a challenging dataset, and clear presentation.  
- Rejection concerns: insufficient comparison to prior spiking backprop methods, unclear scalability, and TrueNorth-specific constraints.
