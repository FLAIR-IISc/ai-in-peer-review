- Proposes training spiking neural networks with low-precision synapses via probabilistic backprop: train continuous network offline, then sample ensembles to map to neuromorphic hardware.
- Demonstrates method on TrueNorth with MNIST: 99.42% at 108 μJ/image (64-ensemble) and 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image (1-ensemble).
- Claims significant energy-efficiency gains (e.g., 4 μJ at 95% accuracy vs prior 6 mJ) and provides real-hardware validation.
- Empirical results are strong and show a clear energy–accuracy tradeoff with ensemble size.
- Practical contribution in mapping continuous training to discrete neuromorphic deployment and demonstrating TrueNorth implementation.
- Novelty claims are overstated: methodology closely resembles expectation backprop (Soudry et al. 2014) and earlier work (Zhao et al. 1996).
- Paper fails to properly acknowledge foundational prior work ([14],[15]) and should explicitly reference these methods.
- The claim of being “first” to demonstrate offline backprop for spiking, lower-than-trinary precision, constrained connectivity is misleading given Soudry et al.’s results.
- Accuracy-comparison statements are inaccurate—Soudry et al. reported 99.12% with binary weights/neurons, conflicting with the paper’s comparisons.
- The “constrain-then-train” sampling-to-ensemble approach is not novel and was suggested/tested in prior work.
- Strengths: strong experiments on real hardware, practical deployment approach, clear presentation, and compelling energy-accuracy results.
- Weaknesses: overstated novelty, inaccurate benchmarking, insufficient citation of prior probabilistic backprop methods, and missing explicit references in Section 3.2.
- Recommended fixes: properly cite and discuss [14] and [15], correct novelty and benchmark claims, and clarify how this work extends or differs from existing methods.
- Overall: strong empirical contribution and practical validation but requires substantial revision to accurately position and credit prior work.
