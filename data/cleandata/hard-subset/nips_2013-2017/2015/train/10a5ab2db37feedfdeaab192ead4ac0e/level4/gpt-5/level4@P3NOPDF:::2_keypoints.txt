- Proposal: Interpret trained analog parameters as probabilities and sample them to obtain binary synapses for spike-based hardware, adapting backpropagation for this mapping and validating on MNIST and TrueNorth.  
- Strength: Clear, well-structured manuscript with a coherent training-to-deployment narrative.  
- Strength: Original and timely solution to mapping high-precision training to low-precision neuromorphic hardware.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical validation on physical TrueNorth hardware with measured performance.  
- Strength: Demonstrates two deployment strategies (size-optimized and accuracy-optimized) useful for different resource constraints.  
- Strength: Framework appears applicable to other neuromorphic platforms beyond TrueNorth.  
- Weakness: Practical impact unclear—needs concrete deployment scenarios and comparisons of accuracy/latency/energy trade-offs versus baselines.  
- Weakness: Insufficient quantitative comparison to prior methods (weight quantization, stochastic rounding, multi-synapse encoding, surrogate gradients); provide side-by-side metrics (accuracy, resource use, latency, energy).  
- Weakness: Experimental scope limited to MNIST; needs results or discussion on scalability to harder/event-driven datasets and deeper/conv architectures.  
- Method detail missing: Specify input encoding (rate vs temporal, Poisson vs deterministic), time windows, and firing rates.  
- Method detail missing: Provide neuron and synapse model parameters (leak, threshold, reset, refractoriness) and variability handling.  
- Method detail missing: Precisely define probabilistic mapping and sampling scheme (per-synapse vs per-time vs ensemble, number of samples at inference).  
- Method detail missing: Clarify training mechanics (stochasticity during training, gradient estimators, regularization, schedules to encourage deployability).  
- Method detail missing: Detail resource constraints and mapping (fan-in/out, core utilization, routing, synapse/neuronal duplication overhead).  
- Method detail missing: Report on-chip performance metrics (latency/integration window, throughput, energy per inference) with confidence intervals and device variability.  
- Clarify: Exact differences, resource usage, and trade-offs between the two architecture variants; include a comparative accuracy vs cores/synapses/latency/energy plot or table.  
- Robustness: Provide sensitivity analyses for number of samples/replicas, input spike rate, neuron parameter settings, and weight noise/mismatch.  
- Missing citations: Add Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience (2015) and arXiv:1506.05427; summarize how this method differs from refs [9]–[12].  
- Minor presentation: Include concise algorithmic description of training/mapping pipeline, full hardware measurement setup, and a limitations section discussing unsuitable scenarios.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, novel, and practically relevant work with compelling TrueNorth validation; leans toward acceptance pending clarifications and additional comparisons/details.
