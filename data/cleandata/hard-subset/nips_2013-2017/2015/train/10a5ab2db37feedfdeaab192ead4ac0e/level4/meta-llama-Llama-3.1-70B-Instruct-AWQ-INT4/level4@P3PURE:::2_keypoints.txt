- Proposes novel method: treat analog variables as probabilities and sample them to map offline-trained networks to spiking chips with binary synapses, enabling backpropagation for such spiking networks.

- Validated on MNIST and mapped the trained network onto two architectures optimized for size and accuracy, with performance figures measured on-chip.

- Manuscript is high-quality, clear, original, and potentially useful for other approaches and hardware platforms.

- Reviewer requests citation of additional related work: Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.

- Significance and practical impact are not clearly highlighted—unclear how the method aids real-world deployment of TrueNorth and which specific applications benefit.

- Unclear comparative advantages over alternative methods (references [9]–[12])—needs explicit comparison.

- Missing technical details that would strengthen the paper: how inputs are converted into spikes, spike frequencies used, shared weight values, and the neuron models.
