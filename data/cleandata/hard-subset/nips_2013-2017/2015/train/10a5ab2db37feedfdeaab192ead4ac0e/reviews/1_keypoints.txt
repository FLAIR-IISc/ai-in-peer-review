- Section 3.2 algorithm is deeply similar to and effectively a hybrid of refs [14] and [15], derived using identical approximations; the authors should acknowledge this.  
- The discussion’s first paragraph gives the impression that the "constrain-then-train" methodology is novel, despite it being suggested and tested in ref [15].  
- Novelty claim (i) in the introduction is misleading because ref [15]’s "expectation backpropagation" is a backprop update using expectation propagation for binary neurons and weights and can accommodate constrained connectivity.  
- The paper cites incorrect prior state-of-the-art: ref [15] achieved the best-known results for binary weights/neurons on MNIST without data augmentation.  
- After authors’ feedback: experimental results (accuracy and power) are strong and novel, but the overall methodology and algorithmic contributions are overstated in novelty.
