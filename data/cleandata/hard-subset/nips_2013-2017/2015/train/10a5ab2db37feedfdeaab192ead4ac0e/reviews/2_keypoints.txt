- Proposes reconciling high-precision backpropagation with spike-based devices by treating analog variables as probabilities sampled to produce binary synapses.  
- Adapts backpropagation to be compatible with the probabilistic sampling strategy for spiking networks.  
- Validated method on MNIST and mapped trained networks onto TrueNorth, optimizing for size and accuracy.  
- Presents chip-measured performance figures for two different hardware architectures.  
- Manuscript quality and clarity are very high; work is original and broadly useful across platforms.  
- Reviewer recommends adding citations to Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015 and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Paper does not sufficiently highlight the significance or practical deployment scenarios for TrueNorth.  
- Unclear which specific applications would benefit and how this method enables practical deployment.  
- Lacks clear comparison of advantages over alternative methods (e.g., refs [9]–[12]).  
- Results are convincing but missing implementation details (input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, shared weight values, neuron models).  
- Suggests clarifying how inputs are encoded as spikes and providing parameter/frequency details for reproducibility.
