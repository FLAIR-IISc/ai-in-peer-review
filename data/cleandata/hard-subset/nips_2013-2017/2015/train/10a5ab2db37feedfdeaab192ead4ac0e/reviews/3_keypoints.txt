- Paper presents a backpropagation training method using spike probabilities that accounts for hardware constraints of a spiking-neuron, discrete-synapse platform—timely and interesting.  
- Proposed training method is useful for new multi-neuron hardware platforms with constraints.  
- Paper is easy to read.  
- Claims in Section 1 are worded in a way that may wrongly imply novelty of using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses; reword to avoid that impression.  
- Demonstration on the TN hardware should be presented as validation of the method, not as a separate novelty claim.  
- Clarify what "0.15 bits per synapse" means in Section 2.  
- Network topology is not clearly described in a single, consolidated place.  
- Report how much longer training takes due to probabilistic synaptic connection updates.  
- Explain why the single ensemble 30-core network performs much worse than the 5-core network.  
- Explain why different ensembles of the 5-core network yield similar results.  
- Provide input spike rates during testing and clarify whether inputs are spike trains.  
- Note that Ref. 12 enforces constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training, so it is not purely a train-then-constrain approach; fix the "approach approach" typo.  
- Add discussion/comparison with other spiking backprop rules such as SpikeProp.  
- Significance: hardware-aware training is important, but the TN-specific constraints may not generalize and using a 64-ensemble increases hardware resource needs for target accuracy.
