- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" probabilistic approach that trains synapse-on probabilities and biases for deployment on TrueNorth’s spiking, binary-synapse, limited-connectivity hardware using a Gaussian approximation for weighted Bernoulli sums.

- Fixes synaptic strengths S a priori with a balanced ±1 design to satisfy axon-type constraints and minimize pairwise redundancy.

- Uses sampling of the trained probabilistic network to generate one or more binary deployed networks and ensembles sampled networks to boost accuracy.

- Demonstrated on MNIST: up to 99.42% with a 64-member ensemble (1920 cores) at 108 μJ/classification, and 0.268 μJ/classification at 92.7% with a single 5-core network; a claimed 4 μJ at 95% must be reconciled with these numbers.

- Strength: principled probabilistic surrogate enabling standard gradient descent with a sensible and efficient Gaussian approximation and derivative derivation.

- Strength: hardware-aware initialization and topology (balanced S, progressive mixing/local connectivity) aligned with TrueNorth constraints.

- Strength: strong real-hardware demonstration with compelling energy/throughput metrics and competitive spiking-MNIST accuracy.

- Strength: generally well-organized paper with explicit equations, hyperparameters, and clear mapping from training to deployment.

- Weakness: Gaussian and independence approximations (and dropping the σ^-3 term) are not rigorously justified and lack ablation to show their impact on accuracy/gradients.

- Weakness: only learning c̃ and biases while keeping S fixed is a strong constraint; no comparison to learning discrete s-values or per-axon-type choices.

- Weakness: headline accuracy relies heavily on large ensembling; single-member accuracy and variance across samplings are not reported, making comparisons to prior work unclear.

- Weakness: inconsistent energy reporting across abstract and results; need exact configuration(s) achieving reported energy/accuracy tradeoffs and clear energy accounting (active vs leakage).

- Weakness: evaluation limited to MNIST only; generality to other datasets or locally connected/convolutional variants is unproven.

- Clarity issue: deployment specifics underspecified (construction algorithm for S, output neurons per class, sliding-window connectivity schedules), hindering reproducibility.

- Clarity issue: definition of pk and the Gaussian aggregation for class-average spike counts should be spelled out with explicit µ and σ.

- Clarity issue: topology resembles locally connected (unshared convolution) layers and should be explicitly compared/contrasted with standard conv layers.

- Originality: the joint handling of spiking neurons, sub-trinary synapses, and constrained connectivity with probabilistic backprop and tailored initialization is novel, but should be situated against locally-connected and binary-weight/activation baselines.

- Significance: provides a practical training-to-hardware pipeline with measured energy/throughput benefits for neuromorphic ML, though reliance on ensembles and limited single-network characterization may limit deployment efficiency.

- Suggestion: report single-member accuracies, ensemble scaling curves with error bars, and variance across samplings.

- Suggestion: provide ablations for σ^-3 term, balanced S vs random S, output replication per class, and data augmentation benefits.

- Suggestion: consider regularizing c̃ toward {0,1} to reduce sampling variance and report the learned c̃ distribution.

- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to fan-in and input dependence (CLT assumptions) and compare to a train-then-constrain baseline on the same topology.

- Pros for acceptance: clear, hardware-grounded method; strong real-hardware energy results; novel integration of probabilistic backprop with binary synapse learning and tailored initialization/topology.

- Cons for acceptance: heavy reliance on ensembling for SOTA accuracy; limited empirical scope (MNIST only) and missing ablations; theoretical approximations lack validation.

- Recommendation: weak accept, contingent on addressing clarity gaps, reconciling energy claims, and adding ablations/comparisons.
