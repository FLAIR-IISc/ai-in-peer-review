- Presents a method to train with continuous values for backprop that maps directly to spiking networks with low-precision synapses and constrained connectivity, achieving SOTA MNIST accuracy and improved TrueNorth energy efficiency.  
- Positions the work relative to prior efforts, noting novelty in combining spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity unlike most prior work.  
- Novelty: simultaneous use of backprop, spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity addresses energy-constrained deployment.  
- Strong empirical results: 99.42% on MNIST for spiking network and 4 µJ/classification on TrueNorth.  
- Practical focus: direct mapping to TrueNorth with detailed topology and training procedure for reproducibility.  
- Clear methodology: well-motivated “constrain-then-train” approach and justified gradient approximations.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: systematic study of ensemble size vs. accuracy and energy.  
- Well-written and organized with useful figures and tables.  
- Weakness: impact and error introduced by the backprop approximation (Eq. 4) are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to MNIST, leaving generalization to larger datasets (e.g., ImageNet) unclear.  
- Weakness: choice of topology parameters (R, F, S) lacks clear systematic rationale.  
- Weakness: synapse-strength initialization via covariance minimization is complex and not intuitively explained.  
- Weakness: ensemble scalability concerns—64 members requiring 1920 cores may be impractical for many applications.  
- For acceptance: addresses a crucial problem, is novel, empirically strong, and clearly presented.  
- Against acceptance: limited dataset evaluation, potential harms from approximations, and questionable ensemble scalability.  
- Overall: strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revision: provide detailed analysis of error from Eq. 4 approximations.  
- Suggested revision: discuss challenges and strategies for scaling to more complex datasets.  
- Suggested revision: clarify rationale or systematic method for choosing R, F, and S topology parameters.  
- Suggested revision: explain computational cost and intuitive role of the synapse initialization procedure.  
- Suggested revision: explore alternatives or strategies to reduce large ensemble sizes while maintaining performance.
