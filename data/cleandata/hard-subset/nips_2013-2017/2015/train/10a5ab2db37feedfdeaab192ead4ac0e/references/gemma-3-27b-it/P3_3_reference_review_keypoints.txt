- Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed solution bridging backpropagation and energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware; recommend acceptance.  
- Core idea: "Constrain-then-train" — train offline using continuous probabilistic representations that map directly to binary spiking neurons and low-precision synapses.  
- Topology: Uses a progressive mixing strategy to handle constrained connectivity.  
- Novelty/positioning: Simultaneously addresses spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity—an advance over prior work.  
- Performance: Achieves state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy (99.42%) for spiking networks.  
- Hardware demo: Real-time deployment on TrueNorth with major power savings (4 µJ vs. 6 mJ) validating practicality.  
- Method clarity: Clear, well-defined equations and rationale for approximations and mapping to hardware.  
- Experiments: Thorough, including ablation studies showing accuracy vs. energy trade-offs with ensemble size.  
- Writing/organization: Well-written and easy to follow despite topic complexity.  
- Weakness — gradient approximation: Equation (4) approximation is used for efficiency but lacks analysis of its impact on learned weights and convergence.  
- Weakness — topology generalizability: Progressive mixing is effective for MNIST but unclear how it scales/adapts to more complex datasets or CNNs.  
- Weakness — initialization assumptions: Synapse-strength initialization relies on independence/Gaussian assumptions that need more rigorous justification.  
- Weakness — neuron dynamics: Limited exploration of alternative neuron models (e.g., LIF) and their potential effects on performance.  
- Significance/originality: Highly significant—enables practical integration of backprop-trained models with neuromorphic hardware and opens path for resource-constrained deployments.  
- Recommendation and revisions: Accept with minor revisions; address analysis of the backprop approximation and discuss topology generalizability.
