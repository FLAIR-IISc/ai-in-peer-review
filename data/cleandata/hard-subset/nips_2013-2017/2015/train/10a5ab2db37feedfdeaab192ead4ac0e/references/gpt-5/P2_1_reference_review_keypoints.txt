- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" probabilistic surrogate to map backprop-trained networks to TrueNorth spiking binary-synapse hardware.  
- Surrogate interprets inputs, neuron outputs, and synapse states as spike/synapse-on probabilities and uses a Gaussian approximation for weighted sums to enable differentiable activations.  
- Trains connection probabilities c̃ and biases while fixing synaptic strengths s with a decorrelating hardware-aware initialization (half +1/half −1 per neuron).  
- Deployment samples binary synapses and spikes from learned probabilities, optionally using an ensemble of sampled networks; topology uses a sliding-window to respect fan-in/core constraints.  
- Novelty: integrates spiking neurons, binary synapses, constrained connectivity, and a probabilistic surrogate that maps directly to TrueNorth—combining previously separate strands of prior work.  
- Soundness concerns: relies on independence and Gaussian approximations plus a dropped σ^−3 term in gradients without quantifying approximation error or impact on learning.  
- Suggests ablation/MC or full-derivative comparisons to validate the gradient and Gaussian simplifications.  
- Parameterization trade-off: learning only c̃ and biases reduces complexity and fits hardware, but fixing s is data-agnostic and may sacrifice optimality; learning s within blocks could be informative.  
- Experimental strengths: strong MNIST accuracy for a spiking, binary-synapse system and real on-chip energy measurements at 1 kHz.  
- Experimental limitations: evaluation limited to MNIST, heavy reliance on ensembles for peak accuracy, unclear single-sample performance for the 30-core model.  
- Reporting inconsistency: conflicting energy/accuracy numbers (abstract vs. results) — require reconciliation and exact configs for cited figures (e.g., the 4 µJ claim).  
- Energy measurement methodology: active+leakage accounting is reasonable but needs clarity on communication/host I/O overheads for fair comparisons.  
- Comparative evaluation: comparisons to related papers would benefit from uniform metrics (accuracy, latency, throughput, energy) and matched datasets.  
- Clarity is generally good with explicit equations and training details, but requests: conditions for neglecting σ^−3, empirical evidence, pseudocode/algorithmic summary, and ensemble/deployment clarification.  
- Ask authors to report per-ensemble-member accuracy/energy and whether multiple sampled networks run concurrently on different cores or a single sampled network is viable.  
- Originality: the end-to-end mapping from differentiable surrogate to constrained spiking hardware plus on-chip demonstration is novel and timely.  
- Significance: meaningful step toward low-power neuromorphic backprop training, but impact limited by single-dataset evaluation and ensemble dependence; single-network deployment results would increase practical relevance.  
- Pros: principled constrain-then-train formulation, hardware-aware initialization, on-chip measurements showing strong energy/throughput, topology respects neuromorphic constraints.  
- Cons: heavy reliance on unquantified approximations, limited to MNIST, ensemble dependence for peak accuracy, reporting inconsistencies and missing measurement/mapping details.  
- Key questions for authors: single-sample 30-core accuracy/energy; ensemble size for >99%; sensitivity to Gaussian/gradient approximations and MC comparisons; ability to learn s-values within block constraints; stability when freezing a single sampled network.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—compelling, hardware-relevant contribution but needs clarified inconsistencies and additional ablations; confidence medium.
