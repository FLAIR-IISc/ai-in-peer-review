- Proposes a “constrain-then-train” method for training probability-based continuous variables to map onto TrueNorth’s binary synapses and spiking neurons via a Gaussian surrogate for firing probability.  
- Training learns connection probabilities c̃ij and biases while synaptic strengths sij are fixed ±1 patterns chosen to decorrelate neurons on a core.  
- Deployment samples synapses and input spikes by Bernoulli draws, optionally forming ensembles by multiple samples for improved accuracy.  
- Topology uses sliding windows over limited fan-in cores (“progressive mixing”) resembling local receptive fields without weight sharing.  
- Novelty: joint handling of spiking neurons, one-bit synapses with shared axon-type scaling, and strict TrueNorth connectivity constraints with real-time, low-energy deployment.  
- Strength: Clear focus on bridging algorithm-hardware mismatch and tailoring method to practical neuromorphic constraints.  
- Strength: Principled differentiable surrogate using a Gaussian CDF with explicit gradients and an approximation to reduce computation.  
- Strength: Hardware-aware initialization aiming to decorrelate pre-activations via balanced ±1 strengths and axon-type distribution.  
- Strength: Demonstrated real-time TrueNorth deployment with measured energy/throughput and strong energy-efficiency numbers.  
- Strength: Competitive MNIST results for spiking/low-precision settings using standard training heuristics and augmentation.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to MNIST; no experiments on harder vision tasks or event-based data.  
- Weakness: Gradient approximation drops σj dependence on c̃ij without ablation or empirical validation of that choice.  
- Weakness: Fixed sij (not learned) may limit expressivity; no comparison to partially learning s-values.  
- Weakness: Ensemble sampling is central to performance, but trade-offs between ensemble size, latency, and energy are not deeply analyzed.  
- Weakness: Theoretical claims rely on Gaussianity and independence approximations without empirical covariance validation.  
- Weakness: Energy accounting methodology is insufficiently detailed and contains inconsistent energy claims (e.g., 4 µJ vs. 0.268 µJ vs. 108 µJ figures).  
- Quality: Overall technically sound but omits σ-term in derivative approximation and relies on bivariate Gaussian independence arguments that need validation.  
- Quality: Learning only c̃ij and biases may produce brittle, gating-based solutions that necessitate ensembles; exploring discrete/block-wise s-learning suggested.  
- Clarity: Several equations and implementation details need clearer presentation (Eq.3, explicit µ/σ dependence, derivative expression with φ, neuron parameterization).  
- Clarity: Energy measurement setup must be specified (what is included/excluded, leakage apportioning) and energy-number discrepancies reconciled.  
- Originality: Novel combination of probabilistic backprop with binary synapses and strict hardware constraints; ensemble-as-sampling is straightforward but effective.  
- Significance: Important practical contribution toward energy-efficient neuromorphic learning, but scalability beyond MNIST and ensemble dependence limit broader impact.  
- Requests: Provide ablation comparing full gradient vs. σ-omitted approximation and report convergence/accuracy differences.  
- Requests: Quantify accuracy loss from fixing sij vs. learning block-wise s-values, even on small-scale experiments.  
- Requests: Measure and report pre-activation correlations within cores before/after initialization and after training to validate decorrelation.  
- Requests: Analyze accuracy/energy vs. ensemble size and compare to single-model spiking baselines under matched latency/throughput.  
- Requests: Clarify and reconcile energy numbers (0.268 µJ @ 92.7%, 4 µJ @ 95%, 108 µJ @ 99.42%) and ensure apples-to-apples conditions.  
- Minor: Rewrite malformed Eq.3 using Φ/erf, specify θ consistently, define pk precisely, and provide full architecture/core allocation details for reproduction.  
- Pros summary: Practical hardware-constrained training with real deployment, competitive MNIST accuracy, thoughtful hardware-aware initialization and topology.  
- Cons summary: Limited task scope, reliance on ensembles, unvalidated gradient approximation, fixed synaptic strengths, and unclear energy reporting.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept; valuable hardware-aware method but needs stronger empirical validation of approximations, initialization benefits, and expanded evaluations.
