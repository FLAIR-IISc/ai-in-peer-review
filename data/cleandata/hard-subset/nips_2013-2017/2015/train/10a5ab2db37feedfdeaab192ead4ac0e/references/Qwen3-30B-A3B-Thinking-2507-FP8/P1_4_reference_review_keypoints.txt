- Addresses reconciling backpropagation with energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware via a "constrain-then-train" probabilistic training approach using spike/synapse probabilities in [0,1].  
- Demonstrated on TrueNorth/MNIST achieving 99.42% accuracy and 4 µJ per classification (claimed 4x better than prior neuromorphic work).  
- Uniquely combines three hardware constraints—spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, constrained connectivity—into a unified backprop-compatible framework.  
- Provides a clear mathematical derivation of gradients for the probabilistic backpropagation method.  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive, including ablation studies on network size, ensemble size, and accuracy–energy tradeoffs.  
- Energy measurements are carefully conducted with distinction between active and leakage power.  
- Progressive mixing network topology is novel and well-motivated by TrueNorth architecture constraints.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis lacks formal guarantees for the Gaussian approximation used in neuron activation.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent spiking-network training methods (e.g., surrogate gradients).  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and organized; mapping from training to deployment is clear.  
- Clarity issue: derivation around Equation 4 and the validity of its approximation may be hard for some readers and needs brief justification.  
- Originality: first demonstration of backpropagation satisfying all three key neuromorphic hardware constraints.  
- Significance: substantial — provides a practical route to deploy deep models on energy-constrained edge/embedded devices; claims >1,000x efficiency vs conventional approaches.  
- Limitation: experiments limited to MNIST with no evaluation on more complex datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, well-executed, significant results; minor revisions to theory discussion and comparisons suggested.  
- Suggested improvements: add discussion on extending to more complex datasets/networks and include comparisons with recent SNN training methods.
