- Paper presents a method to bridge backpropagation and neuromorphic hardware by training continuous-valued networks that map to spiking, low-precision, constrained-connectivity systems.  
- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" strategy using probabilistic spiking representations and constrained connectivity for direct hardware mapping.  
- Novelty arises from combining spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity in one approach.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy (99.42%) within spiking-network constraints.  
- Demonstrates exceptional power efficiency: ~4 µJ per classification at 95% accuracy on TrueNorth.  
- Provides detailed methodology, including gradient equations and network initialization.  
- Validates approach with a real-time hardware demonstration on the TrueNorth chip.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized.  
- Evaluation is limited to MNIST; generalization to more complex datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet) is not shown.  
- Network initialization is complex and sensitivity to initialization parameters is not fully explored.  
- Gradient simplification (Equation 4) is approximate; potential impact on convergence and accuracy is not analyzed.  
- Network topology is tailored to TrueNorth; generality to other neuromorphic platforms is unclear.  
- Significance: provides a concrete pathway for deploying deep learning on energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware with broad implications.  
- For acceptance: addresses a key bottleneck, achieves strong constrained performance, shows major power gains, and uses rigorous methodology.  
- Against acceptance: single-dataset evaluation and complexity of initialization remain concerns.  
- Recommendation: Strongly Accept.
