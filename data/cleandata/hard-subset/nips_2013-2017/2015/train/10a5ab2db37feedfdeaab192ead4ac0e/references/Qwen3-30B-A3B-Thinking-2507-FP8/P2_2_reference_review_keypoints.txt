- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" method to train networks compatible with neuromorphic hardware constraints (spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, constrained connectivity).  
- Represents network parameters as probabilities in [0,1] to enable direct mapping from training to deployment.  
- Uses a probabilistic extension of backpropagation with a derived gradient calculation.  
- Demonstrates results on MNIST deployed to TrueNorth, achieving 99.42% accuracy and 4 µJ per classification (95% accuracy at 1000 imgs/sec).  
- Claims novelty in combining spiking neurons, sub-2-bit synapses, and constrained connectivity within one training framework.  
- Theoretical approach and gradient derivation are technically sound.  
- Experimental setup includes appropriate energy measurements on TrueNorth and competitive MNIST performance.  
- Gaussian approximation in the neuron activation (Eq.3) is not thoroughly validated or empirically justified.  
- The assumption that the right term of Eq.4 is negligible is unsubstantiated.  
- Lacks detail on how independent random draws for sampling the training network are implemented in practice.  
- Comparison to other state-of-the-art spiking approaches is limited to a few references; broader benchmarking is missing.  
- "Progressive mixing" topology and the sliding-window/core input selection are not clearly explained.  
- Mapping from training to deployment network via sampling lacks specification of distributions and impact on performance.  
- Network initialization procedure is described insufficiently for reproducibility.  
- Novelty is somewhat overstated given prior work on probabilistic backprop, binary/trinary synapses, and probabilistic binary neurons/synapses.  
- Main novelty is the integration of all three hardware constraints into one framework, but the distinction from prior work needs clearer articulation.  
- Significant practical contribution due to large energy-efficiency improvement over prior low-power approaches.  
- Limited significance because evaluation is restricted to MNIST; scaling to datasets like CIFAR-10 or ImageNet is not demonstrated.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address approximation validation, implementation details, and clearer differentiation from prior work.  
- Specific suggested improvements: validate Gaussian approximation empirically, detail sampling procedure, clarify topology vs. convolutional nets, better contrast with related probabilistic backprop work, and discuss scalability to harder datasets.
