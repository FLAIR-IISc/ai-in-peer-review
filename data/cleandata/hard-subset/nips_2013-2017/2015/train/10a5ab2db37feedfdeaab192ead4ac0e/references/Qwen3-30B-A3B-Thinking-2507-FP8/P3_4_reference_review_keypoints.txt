- Proposes a novel "constrain-then-train" method that trains continuous [0,1] networks to map directly to spiking neuromorphic hardware with sub-trinary synapses and a probabilistic deployment model.  
- Introduces a progressive mixing topology to respect neuromorphic connectivity constraints and support local-to-global feature responses.  
- Reports state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy (99.42%) and exceptional energy efficiency (4 µJ per classification on TrueNorth), outperforming prior spiking and binary approaches.  
- Provides clear theoretical justification for the probabilistic approximation (Equation 3) and a network initialization scheme.  
- Uses a well-documented experimental setup with appropriate baselines and careful energy measurements.  
- The Gaussian approximation for neuron firing probability is plausible but insufficiently validated for the specific network architectures used.  
- Key assumptions (input independence, large numbers for Gaussian approximation, and µ, ñi, c̃ij ≪ σ) are not empirically demonstrated.  
- The proposed network initialization is theoretically appealing but lacks ablation/experimental evidence of its impact versus standard initializations.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a clear mapping from training to deployment networks.  
- The progressive mixing topology is underexplained—details of the sliding window (e.g., R=16, S=12) and how it yields a feature hierarchy are missing.  
- The combination of probabilistic backpropagation, constrained connectivity, spiking neurons, and sub-trinary synapses is an original and meaningful contribution.  
- The distinction from prior probabilistic backpropagation work (e.g., [15] with full connectivity) needs clearer elaboration to emphasize the novelty of constrained connectivity.  
- The work is highly significant for energy-efficient neuromorphic deployment and edge AI, demonstrating large energy improvements over prior work.  
- Results are limited to MNIST; discussion on applicability to more complex datasets and other neuromorphic hardware is weak or overly broad.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with practical impact—recommendation to accept.
