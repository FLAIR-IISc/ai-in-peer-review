- Summary: Proposes a "constrain-then-train" probabilistic backpropagation that maps networks with spiking neurons and binary synapses directly to TrueNorth, reporting 99.42% on MNIST at 4 µJ/classification.

- Strength: Clear problem statement and novel "constrain-then-train" paradigm that avoids train-then-constrain pitfalls.

- Strength: Technical rigor with Gaussian approximation, gradient derivation, and principled network initialization reducing redundancy.

- Strength: Strong experimental results claiming 99.42% accuracy and exceptional energy efficiency, with thorough comparisons to prior neuromorphic work.

- Strength: Demonstrates practical real-time operation on TrueNorth (1000 images/s) with strong implications for embedded/mobile use.

- Strength: Well-organized, clearly written paper with effective figures and tables.

- Weakness: Heavy reliance on an unverified Gaussian approximation for neuron firing probabilities and gradients, with no empirical validation that required conditions hold.

- Weakness: Accuracy-energy claim undermined by use of a 64-member ensemble (1920 cores); unclear whether 4 µJ is per core or for the ensemble and no discussion of the accuracy–energy tradeoff.

- Weakness: No comparison to state-of-the-art conventional deep learning methods on MNIST, making it hard to assess accuracy tradeoffs versus non-neuromorphic approaches.

- Weakness: Evaluation limited to MNIST; unclear scalability to more complex datasets like CIFAR-10 or ImageNet.

- Weakness: Vague description of the "progressive mixing" topology and insufficient detail on how it differs from or advantages over standard convolutional networks.

- Weakness: Missing analysis of training time, which is important for practical deployment.

- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to other low-precision methods (e.g., binary/quantized networks) despite claims about bits per synapse.

- Overall assessment: Strong, impactful idea with compelling results but significant experimental and validation gaps that must be addressed.

- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions.

- Requested revisions: empirically validate the Gaussian approximation; clarify whether 4 µJ is per core or per ensemble and analyze the accuracy–energy tradeoff; compare to SOTA conventional deep nets on MNIST; provide detailed description and justification of the topology; report training time and compare to other low-precision methods.

- Pro-accept arguments: novel practical approach bridging training and neuromorphic hardware, top reported spiking-network MNIST accuracy, claimed exceptional energy efficiency, solid theoretical basis, and clear presentation.

- Con-accept arguments: unverified critical assumption (Gaussian approx), ensemble-based energy claim weakens impact, lack of SOTA comparisons, limited dataset evaluation, and insufficient topology detail.
