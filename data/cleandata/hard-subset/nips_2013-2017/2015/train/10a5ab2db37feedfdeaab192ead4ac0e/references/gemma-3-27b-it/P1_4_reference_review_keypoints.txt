- Summary: Proposes a method to train networks with backprop on continuous values and directly map them to low-precision spiking networks for neuromorphic hardware, demonstrating SOTA MNIST accuracy and strong energy efficiency on TrueNorth (4 µJ/class at 95%).
- Relation to prior work: Novelly combines training for spiking neurons, low synaptic precision, constrained connectivity, and info-max initialization, differing from prior train-then-constrain and probabilistic approaches.
- Strength — Novelty: Reconciling backpropagation with neuromorphic constraints is a genuine and important contribution.
- Strength — Performance: Achieves 99.42% on MNIST, surpassing previous spiking-network results.
- Strength — Real-world demo: Deployment on TrueNorth with substantial power improvements shows practical viability.
- Strength — Clarity and design: Training/mapping procedure, topology, and [0,1] constraint rationale are well explained and justified.
- Strength — Analysis: Detailed discussion and justification of gradient approximations increase credibility.
- Weakness — Dataset scope: Evaluation is limited to MNIST, leaving generalizability unproven.
- Weakness — Topology specificity: Network parameters (R, S, F) are tuned for MNIST with no guidance for other tasks.
- Weakness — Model approximation: Gaussian approximation in neuron/loss models may be inaccurate for very sparse activity and needs further study.
- Weakness — Hyperparameters: Lacks sensitivity analysis for key training hyperparameters (learning rate, momentum, dropout, batch size).
- Weakness — Scalability: Insufficient discussion of scaling the approach to larger, more complex neuromorphic systems.
- Qualitative: Paper is technically sound, original, significant, and generally well-written, though some equations (Sec. 3.2) need clearer variable explanations.
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a timely problem, shows impressive results and energy gains, presents a novel and well-explained methodology, and is technically solid.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited evaluation (single dataset) and unclear scalability to larger systems.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — expand evaluation to more complex datasets, discuss scalability, and clarify equations in Section 3.2.
