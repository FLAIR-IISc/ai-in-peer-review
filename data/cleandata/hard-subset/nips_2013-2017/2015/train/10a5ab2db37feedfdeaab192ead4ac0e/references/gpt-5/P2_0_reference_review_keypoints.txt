- Proposes a hardware-aware “constrain-then-train” method that learns connection probabilities and biases to map directly to TrueNorth-like spiking hardware with binary crossbars and limited connectivity.  
- Uses continuous [0,1] variables as spike/synapse-on probabilities and a Gaussian approximation of weighted Bernoulli sums to obtain differentiable firing probabilities for backpropagation.  
- Fixes synaptic strengths a priori to a balanced ±1 structure designed to minimize input covariance under axon-type constraints instead of learning weights.  
- Topology enforces local receptive fields and progressive mixing to respect core-to-core fan-in limits; deployment samples binary synapses/spikes and optionally ensembles sampled networks.  
- Demonstrates MNIST results showing an accuracy–energy trade-off: 99.42% with 30 cores and 64-member ensemble at 108 µJ/classification, and 92.7% with 5 cores single-member at 0.268 µJ/classification, with real-time 1000 imgs/s on TrueNorth.  
- Situates contribution at intersection of spiking backprop, low-precision networks, and probabilistic/binary networks, claiming novelty in combining spiking, sub-trinary precision, constrained connectivity, and direct hardware mapping.  
- Probabilistic forward model, gradient derivations, Gaussian approximation, and independence assumptions are coherent and plausible given large fan-in and balanced weights.  
- A priori ±1 weight design is principled (decorrelation/infomax-inspired) and well-motivated for hardware efficiency.  
- Training procedure and deployment mapping are concrete and faithful to hardware; on-hardware measurements and throughput are compelling.  
- Key theoretical approximations (CLT, independence, dropping σ^-3 term) lack empirical validation or ablations to measure their impact.  
- Not learning synaptic strengths may reduce model expressivity; no experiments compare learned s-values (even coarse quantized) versus fixed strengths.  
- Heavy reliance on ensembling to reach top accuracy is insufficiently analyzed: single-sample performance for the 30-core model and ensemble variance are missing.  
- Missing comparisons to train-then-constrain baselines and other binary/spiking methods under matched connectivity constraints.  
- Energy comparison methodology could be inconsistent with others; leakage accounting and measurement protocols need clearer specification to ensure fair comparisons.  
- Paper is generally readable with clear mapping from training to deployment, and training/hyperparameter details provided.  
- Important methodological details are missing or unclear (full s-matrix construction, explicit loss formula via Gaussian approximation, exact 30-core architecture specs).  
- Results lack error bars, standard deviations, training compute cost, and time-to-train; reproducibility would benefit from pseudocode or public code.  
- Conceptual novelty lies in integrating known components (probabilistic surrogates, local connectivity, ensembles) into a hardware-realized pipeline rather than introducing new learning theory.  
- Demonstrating low µJ/classification energy and real hardware throughput is significant for on-device low-power inference.  
- Evaluation scope is limited to MNIST; broader benchmarks or tasks stressing topology are needed to assess generality.  
- Specific requested experiments: report single-member 30-core performance and accuracy vs. ensemble size/variance; ablate full vs. approximate gradients and Gaussian assumption; compare to train-then-constrain baseline; clarify energy measurement; provide s-matrix algorithm/code and training resource details; explore learning coarse-grained s-values.  
- Pros: exact hardware-aware mapping, strong measured energy-efficiency, principled weight/topology design, high MNIST accuracy under strict constraints.  
- Cons: limited evaluation (MNIST only), heavy ensembling, missing ablations/approximation validation, incomplete reproducibility details, limited baseline comparisons.  
- Overall: technically sound and practically relevant with compelling on-chip results, but claims tempered by missing ablations, ensemble dependence analysis, and broader empirical validation; reviewer leans weak accept contingent on additional experiments and clearer reporting.
