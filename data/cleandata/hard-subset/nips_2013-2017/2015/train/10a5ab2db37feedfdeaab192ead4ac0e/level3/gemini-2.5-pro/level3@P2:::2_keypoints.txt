- Introduces a novel "constrain-then-train" probabilistic backpropagation method that treats activations and weights as probabilities for deployment on spiking neuromorphic hardware (TrueNorth).  
- Training learns probabilities for synapse activity and neuron firing; deployment samples network instances from these distributions.  
- Validated on MNIST with hardware-measured results: 64-member ensemble achieves 99.42% accuracy; single small network achieves 92.7% at 0.268 µJ/image.  
- High technical quality: probabilistic adaptation of backprop is sound and experimentally supported by real-chip measurements.  
- Provides a clear trade-off analysis (accuracy vs. energy) across architectures and ensemble sizes.  
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear and well-structured, with effective figures and intuitive explanations.  
- Highly original: first unified backprop-based framework addressing spiking neurons, binary synapses, and constrained connectivity for a neuromorphic architecture.  
- Novel synaptic initialization strategy to decorrelate neuron activity is a clever contribution.  
- Significant contribution bridging deep learning and energy-efficient neuromorphic computing, with potential for embedded applications and adaptation to other platforms.  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing implementation details on input encoding (pixel→spike probabilities, input spike frequencies) and specific hardware/neuron/synapse parameter values.  
- Context/comparison lacking: needs more direct comparison to prior spiking-training methods and clearer argument why "constrain-then-train" outperforms "train-then-constrain"; additional citations suggested (e.g., Qiao et al., Esser et al.).  
- Impact could be strengthened by concrete application examples (e.g., wearable keyword-spotting vs. low-power visual inspection) to contextualize energy/accuracy trade-offs.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, effective method; state-of-the-art hardware results; strong conceptual contribution; excellent presentation.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing implementation details for reproducibility; incomplete comparisons to related work; application scenarios under-specified.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths far outweigh weaknesses; minor revisions suggested and paper is strongly recommended for acceptance.
