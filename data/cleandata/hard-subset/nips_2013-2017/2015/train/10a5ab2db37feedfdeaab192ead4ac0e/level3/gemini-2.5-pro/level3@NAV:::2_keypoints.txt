- Proposes a novel "constrain-then-train" method that treats continuous training variables as probabilities and samples them to produce binary synapses and spiking activations for neuromorphic hardware.
- Demonstrates mapping trained networks directly onto TrueNorth hardware and validates on MNIST with strong results (99.42% accuracy, 0.268 μJ/image).
- Technically sound and elegant adaptation of backpropagation to hardware-constrained, spike-based systems.
- Original contribution in combining spiking neurons, binary synapses, and constrained connectivity within a single training framework.
- Manuscript is exceptionally clear and well organized.
- Fails to emphasize practical applications that would benefit from the reported energy efficiency.
- Lacks critical implementation details needed for reproducibility (input spike-encoding, spike frequencies, shared weight values, neuron model parameters).
- Insufficient comparison to alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]) and missing citation/discussion of related work by Qiao et al.
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the reproducibility and comparison/context weaknesses.
