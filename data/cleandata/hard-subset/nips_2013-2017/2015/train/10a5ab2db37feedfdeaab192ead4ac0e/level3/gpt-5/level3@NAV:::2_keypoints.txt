- Proposes a principled bridge from high-precision backprop to spike-based hardware by treating activations and synapses as probabilities sampled into binary spikes/synapses at deployment.  
- Adapts backprop to a Gaussian-probabilistic surrogate of spiking dynamics to learn connection probabilities and biases under TrueNorth-like constraints (binary synapses, low-precision shared s-values, limited fan-in).  
- Maps trained networks to hardware via independent sampling and optional ensembling; validated on MNIST with two TrueNorth configurations and reported chip-measured energy/throughput.  
- Novelty: end-to-end “constrain-then-train” probabilistic formulation that simultaneously embraces spiking neurons, binary synapses, constrained connectivity, plus on-chip measurements.  
- Technical quality: sound development and well-motivated Gaussian approximation; strong MNIST results supported by chip measurements.  
- Missing implementation details impede reproducibility and fair comparison: input-to-spike encoding (rate vs. temporal, time steps, spike frequencies).  
- Missing neuron model parameters: leak, threshold, reset, refractory.  
- Missing hardware mapping specifics: s-value tables/axon-type assignments, shared-weight schemes, and whether ensemble members share parameters beyond sampled connectivity.  
- Needs clearer head-to-head comparisons against refs [9]–[12] using the same dataset, comparable model sizes, and energy/accuracy metrics.  
- Clarity: manuscript is exceptionally well written and organized; should add citations to Qiao et al. (Frontiers 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 and position explicitly against them.  
- Originality: high — probabilistic backprop for binary synapses on spiking, constrained hardware is a notable advance with broad applicability.  
- Significance: high potential but underplays practical deployment; should articulate concrete scenarios where TrueNorth’s latency/throughput/energy are decisive (e.g., always-on sensing, mobile/edge).  
- Clarify scope of chip measurements: if only TrueNorth, state that; if additional architectures measured, include and clarify.  
- Pros: clear probabilistic training compatible with constraints, strong MNIST accuracy/energy trade-offs with on-chip measurements, broadly applicable beyond one platform.  
- Cons: missing implementation details, limited comparative evaluation with alternatives, insufficient discussion of real-world deployment and TrueNorth significance.  
- Recommendation: Accept contingent on addressing missing details, strengthening comparisons, and better articulating deployment context.
