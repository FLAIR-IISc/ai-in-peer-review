- Proposes a novel method to reconcile high-precision backpropagation with spike-based/neuromorphic devices by treating analog variables as probabilities for binary synapses.  
- Adapts backpropagation to a probabilistic sampling strategy compatible with spiking networks, enabling direct mapping to neuromorphic hardware.  
- Manuscript is well-written, high-quality, original, and broadly useful across platforms.  
- Validated on MNIST and demonstrated effectiveness on the TrueNorth chip, optimizing for size and accuracy.  
- Presents chip-measured performance figures for two different hardware architectures, showing practical deployment potential.  
- Reviewer recommends adding citations (e.g., Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015 and arXiv:1506.05427).  
- Paper does not sufficiently highlight the practical significance or specific deployment scenarios for TrueNorth.  
- Needs clearer discussion of which applications would benefit and how the method enables practical deployment.  
- Lacks a direct comparison of advantages over alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]); adding one would strengthen the paper.  
- Results are convincing but some implementation details are missing, harming reproducibility.  
- Specifically requests details on input spike encoding, parameter values, and frequency settings for reproducibility.  
- Scores high on quality, clarity, and originality per conference review guidelines.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that, with revisions addressing missing citations, comparisons, deployment context, and implementation details, could be highly impactful.
