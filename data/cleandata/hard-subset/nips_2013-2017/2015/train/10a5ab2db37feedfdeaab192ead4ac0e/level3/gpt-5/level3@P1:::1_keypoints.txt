- Proposal: probabilistic backpropagation training for spiking networks with discrete synapses and constrained connectivity, deployed on IBM TrueNorth.  
- Core idea: represent spikes and binary synapses as probabilities in [0,1] and use a CLT-based Gaussian/probit approximation to compute firing probabilities and gradients.  
- Deployment: sample trained connection probabilities and biases to produce spiking networks; ensemble multiple sampled networks by averaging outputs.  
- Topology/hardware: use locally-connected “sliding window” layers (no weight sharing) to match TrueNorth core connectivity; synaptic strength table s_ij is fixed to balanced ±1 patterns to reduce correlations.  
- Empirical results: 30-core, ensemble-64 achieves 99.42% at 108 μJ/image; 5-core, single model achieves 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image, both at 1 kHz throughput on TrueNorth.  
- Relation to prior work: forward/backward equations and Gaussian approximation closely follow expectation backpropagation (EP) [15] and stochastic bit-stream work [14]; derivations and approximations mirror these prior methods.  
- Novelty assessment: algorithmic core and “constrain-then-train” framing are incremental relative to [14,15]; main original contributions are mapping to TrueNorth, hardware-aware topology/initialization, and deployment/energy characterization.  
- Quality: system is well-engineered with strong empirical and energy-throughput measurements; Gaussian/probit approximation is reasonable given fan-in and balanced s_ij.  
- Required acknowledgements: paper should explicitly credit the derivational lineage to [14,15] and clarify concrete differences (e.g., learning only connectivity probabilities, fixed s_ij).  
- Recommended ablations: report accuracy vs. ensemble size (e.g., 1,4,16), effect of data augmentation, sensitivity to s_ij initialization alternatives, and contribution of sliding-window sparsity vs matched-parameter fully connected layers.  
- Clarity fixes: explicitly state that only connection probabilities c̃_ij and biases are learned (s_ij fixed), clarify mapping from class-wise multi-neuron outputs to loss/hardware votes, and explain feature sub-selection across cores.  
- SOTA claims: revise and normalize comparisons for augmentation, ensembling, and architecture size; provide apples-to-apples MNIST numbers (single vs ensemble, augmentation/no augmentation).  
- Generalization/limitations: empirical scope limited to MNIST; discuss generalization to convolutional weight sharing or justify preference for locally-connected layers on TrueNorth.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong hardware-validated energy–accuracy trade-offs, pragmatic mapping to spiking hardware, and a thorough system perspective impactful for low-power ML.  
- Cons for acceptance: overstated novelty (close to [14,15]), uncontrolled SOTA comparisons, and missing ablation studies and broader benchmarks.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance for strong hardware results and system contribution, contingent on revisions to properly credit prior work and add controlled comparisons/ablations.
