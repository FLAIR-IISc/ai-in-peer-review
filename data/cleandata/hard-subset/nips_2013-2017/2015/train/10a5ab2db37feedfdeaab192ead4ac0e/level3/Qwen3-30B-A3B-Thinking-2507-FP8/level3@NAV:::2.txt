

This paper proposes a novel approach to bridge the gap between backpropagation training and neuromorphic hardware by treating continuous variables as probabilities that can be sampled to produce binary synapses. The authors adapt backpropagation for probabilistic sampling in spiking networks, demonstrating their method on MNIST with TrueNorth deployment achieving 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ per image. The work represents a significant advancement over prior efforts that addressed only subsets of this compatibility challenge.

The paper excels in clarity and quality, with a well-structured presentation of both theoretical framework and experimental validation. The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, providing sufficient detail for expert reproducibility. The approach is original in combining constrained connectivity, probabilistic sampling, and backpropagation training for spiking networks with sub-trinary precision synapses—a novel integration not previously demonstrated.

However, the paper falls short in addressing significance and practical deployment. While the authors demonstrate technical feasibility on TrueNorth, they fail to clearly articulate specific real-world applications that would benefit from this method or explain how it enables practical deployment scenarios beyond MNIST digit recognition. The comparison with alternative methods (refs [9]-[12]) is insufficient, making it difficult to assess the relative advantages of their approach.

The manuscript should be strengthened by adding citations to Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 as recommended. Additionally, the paper requires more implementation details regarding input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, and neuron models to ensure full reproducibility.

Overall, this is a technically sound and well-executed piece of work that advances the field of neuromorphic computing. While the significance section needs substantial improvement to demonstrate broader impact, the technical contribution is valuable. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the missing implementation details and strengthen the significance discussion. The work represents an important step toward practical brain-inspired computing systems.
