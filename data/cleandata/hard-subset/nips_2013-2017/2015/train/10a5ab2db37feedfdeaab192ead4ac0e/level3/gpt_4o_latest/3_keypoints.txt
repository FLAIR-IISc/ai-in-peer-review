- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" probabilistic training method to map backprop-trained networks directly to neuromorphic hardware (TrueNorth) without post-training discretization.  
- Demonstrates strong MNIST results: 99.42% accuracy (high-performance) and 92.7% accuracy at 0.268 µJ/classification (low-energy).  

Strengths:
- Highly relevant and practical for neuromorphic platforms, addressing reduced-precision synapses and connectivity limits.  
- Technically sound probabilistic framework with detailed derivations and supporting experiments.  
- Well-written and organized with clear explanations of training and deployment.  
- Significant contribution toward integrating deep learning with energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware; competitive with state-of-the-art SNNs.  

Weaknesses:
- Claims about novelty of running on TrueNorth are overstated—hardware validation rather than a novel platform contribution.  
- Ambiguous terminology (e.g., "0.15 bits per synapse") and insufficient explanation of network topology.  
- Unclear generalizability beyond TrueNorth; applicability to other hardware is not demonstrated.  
- Experimental inconsistencies: poor 30-core performance vs. consistent 5-core results, unclear input spike rates and whether inputs are spike trains.  
- Lacks comparison to other spiking backpropagation methods (e.g., SpikeProp) for contextualization.  
- High-accuracy 64-ensemble setup requires significant hardware resources, raising scalability concerns.  

Suggestions:
- Tone down claims in Section 1 to avoid overstating novelty and emphasize methodological contributions.  
- Clarify ambiguous terms and provide detailed description of network topology.  
- Discuss training time and hardware-specific limitations to explain experimental inconsistencies.  
- Include comparisons with existing spiking backpropagation techniques to highlight uniqueness.  
- Fix minor issues/typos (e.g., "approach approach" on line 399).  

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions contingent on addressing clarity, experimental, and comparative gaps.
