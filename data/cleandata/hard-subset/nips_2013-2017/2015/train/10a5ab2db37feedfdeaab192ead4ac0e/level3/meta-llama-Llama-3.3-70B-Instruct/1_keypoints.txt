- Proposes Norm-POEM: a self-normalized risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback to avoid propensity overfitting.
- Thorough analysis of limitations of the conventional unbiased risk estimator.
- Theoretical analysis of the proposed estimator is sound and well-presented.
- Extensive experiments show Norm-POEM outperforms conventional POEM, improving generalization and robustness.
- Paper is well-organized with clear background and related work exposition.
- Novelty is questionable: self-normalized risk estimators are not new and their application to BLBF lacks originality.
- Algorithm in Section 3.2 resembles a hybrid of previously cited methods and should be acknowledged as such.
- "Constrain-then-train" methodology is not novel and has been proposed and tested in prior work.
- Claim of novelty for “expectation backpropagation” is misleading—it is essentially a backprop update implementable with constrained connectivity.
- Some cited previous state-of-the-art results are incorrect; prior bests for binary weights/neurons were achieved by a different algorithm.
- Authors should more carefully evaluate strengths and weaknesses and correct state-of-the-art claims.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address novelty concerns, acknowledge prior similarities, and correct benchmarking claims.
