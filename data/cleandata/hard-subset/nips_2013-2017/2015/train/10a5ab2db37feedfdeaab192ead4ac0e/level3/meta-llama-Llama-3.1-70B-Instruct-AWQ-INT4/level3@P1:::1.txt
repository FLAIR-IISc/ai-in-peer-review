This paper proposes a novel approach to bridge the gap between backpropagation and neuromorphic computing by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by training a sparsely connected network that runs on the TrueNorth chip using the MNIST dataset, achieving 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ per image.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a learning rule and a network topology that reconciles the apparent incompatibility between backpropagation and neuromorphic hardware. The authors propose a probabilistic representation of the deployment network forward pass, which allows training the network using standard backpropagation. The trained network naturally maps to neuromorphic hardware by sampling the probabilities to create one or more networks, which are merged using ensemble averaging.
