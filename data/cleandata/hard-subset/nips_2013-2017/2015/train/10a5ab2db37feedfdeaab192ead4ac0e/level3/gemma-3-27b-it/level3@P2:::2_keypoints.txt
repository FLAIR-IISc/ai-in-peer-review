- Addresses gap between backprop-trained deep nets and energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities.  
- Proposes probabilistic mapping enabling standard backpropagation training and deployment on spiking chips (e.g., TrueNorth).  
- Circumvents incompatibility between continuous backprop operations and discrete neuromorphic systems.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and accessible to readers unfamiliar with neuromorphic computing.  
- Mathematical formulation and probabilistic model appear technically sound with discussed approximations.  
- Validated experimentally on MNIST and deployed on TrueNorth, showing strong accuracy and energy-efficiency results.  
- Use of TrueNorth hardware for validation significantly strengthens the empirical claims.  
- Novelty: probabilistic mapping from analog-trained networks to spiking platforms is an original contribution.  
- Significance: work can accelerate deployment of deep learning on energy-constrained devices.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of concrete practical deployment scenarios and target applications beyond MNIST.  
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to existing SNN training methods and missing citations (e.g., Qiao et al. 2015, arXiv:1506.05427).  
- Weakness: Missing implementation details hindering reproducibility—specifically input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, shared weight values, and neuron models on TrueNorth.  
- Encoding details (input spike encoding and parameter settings) need clearer elaboration.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, compelling experimental validation, well-written, and high practical potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited deployment discussion, incomplete comparisons to alternatives, and reproducibility concerns due to missing implementation specifics.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant impact potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add implementation details, expand deployment discussion, and deepen comparisons.
