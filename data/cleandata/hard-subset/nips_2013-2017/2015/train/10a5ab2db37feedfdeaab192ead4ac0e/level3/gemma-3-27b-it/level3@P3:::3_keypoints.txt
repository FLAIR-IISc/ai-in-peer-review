Paper presents a probabilistic backpropagation method for spiking neural networks tailored to TrueNorth, enabling standard backprop through spikes/discrete synapses — timely and interesting.  
Well written and easy to follow.  
Novelty is overstated; must better acknowledge prior work on spiking backprop and reduced-precision synapses and frame the TrueNorth demonstration as validation rather than independent novelty.  
Clarify "0.15 bits per synapse" — is this an average and how is the bit allocation broken down?  
Provide a consolidated high-level description (figure/table) of network topology: layer sizes, connectivity (R, S), and neuron counts.  
Report training time impact of probabilistic synaptic updates to assess practical feasibility.  
Analyze and explain the significant performance drop when scaling ensembles from 5 to 30 cores.  
Explain why different ensembles of the 5-core network yield consistent performance.  
Report input spike rates during testing and clarify whether inputs are presented as spike trains or single spikes per timestep.  
Correct Ref. 12 description (it enforces constraints during training) and fix the "approach approach" typo.  
Discuss related spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp) and how this method differs in computational cost, hardware compatibility, and performance.  
Highlight significance: hardware-aware training is an important step toward neuromorphic computing but TrueNorth-specific constraints may limit generalizability.  
Note concern that requiring large ensembles (e.g., 64) to reach high accuracy increases hardware resource use and may offset energy-efficiency gains; discuss trade-offs.  
Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical neuromorphic challenge, demonstrates real-chip training on TrueNorth, is clearly written, and is relevant for energy-efficient embedded systems.  
Arguments against acceptance: overstates novelty, lacks clarity on technical details, needs analysis of performance discrepancies, limited generalizability, and high ensemble hardware cost.  
Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the clarity, technical, and analysis concerns.
