- Paper proposes a training method that treats spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable backpropagation for neuromorphic-compatible networks.
- Trained probabilistic networks are sampled into ensembles and averaged for deployment on chips like TrueNorth, demonstrating MNIST results with high accuracy and energy efficiency.
- Technical formulation (Sections 3.2–3.3) is sound and well-justified.
- Paper is generally clear, well-organized, and easy to follow.
- Hardware-aware training is significant and enables combining backpropagation strengths with neuromorphic efficiency.
- The combination of probabilistic backpropagation, constrained connectivity, low-precision synapses, and direct hardware mapping is novel.
- Claims of novelty about spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses are overstated given prior work; novelty should be framed as the combination within the probabilistic framework.
- TrueNorth demonstration should be presented as validation of the method rather than a separate novelty claim.
- The “0.15 bits per synapse” description is unclear and needs clarification beyond on/off state.
- Network topology is described fragmentedly; a consolidated description or diagram is needed.
- Paper omits discussion of training time overhead from probabilistic synaptic updates; comparison to standard backprop is required.
- Large performance drop for the single 30-core ensemble vs. the 5-core network is unexplained and needs analysis.
- Similar results across different 5-core ensembles suggest limited exploration of probability space; deeper analysis is necessary.
- Input representation during testing (spike rates, single spikes vs. spike trains) is not specified.
- Related work is incompletely discussed; Ref. [12] is mischaracterized and comparisons to SpikeProp and other spiking backprop rules are missing.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical problem, technically sound, promising TrueNorth results, and well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated novelty, unclear implementation details, unexplained performance issues, limited related-work discussion, and concerns about generalizability beyond TrueNorth.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with strong execution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, novelty claims, and result analysis.
