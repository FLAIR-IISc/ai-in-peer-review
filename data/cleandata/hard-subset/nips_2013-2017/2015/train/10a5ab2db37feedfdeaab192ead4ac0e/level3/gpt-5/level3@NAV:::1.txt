Summary: The paper proposes a probabilistic surrogate for training spiking, low-precision neuromorphic networks with backpropagation. Discrete synapses and spikes are treated as probabilities in [0,1]; a Gaussian approximation of weighted Bernoulli sums enables differentiable forward/backward passes; learned synapse probabilities are sampled to deploy ensembles on TrueNorth. A hardware-aware topology with constrained fan-in and a weight-initialization scheme balancing ±1 s-values is used. On MNIST, the authors report 99.42% accuracy with a 64-member ensemble at 108 μJ/image and 92.7% with a single member at 0.268 μJ/image, running at 1 kHz on TrueNorth. This line of work relates closely to stochastic/probabilistic backprop for binary neurons/weights and expectation-based updates (e.g., [14], [15]) and prior spiking deployments.

Strengths (Quality/Significance): 
- Strong experimental results with careful hardware measurements; the energy–accuracy trade-off is compelling and practically important for neuromorphic computing.
- The end-to-end system demonstration on TrueNorth with real-time throughput is novel and valuable for the community.
- The topology/initialization tuned to hardware constraints is sensible and clearly described; training hyperparameters and mapping procedure are reproducible.

Weaknesses (Originality/Clarity/Positioning): 
- Section 3.2’s algorithm is deeply similar to, and effectively a hybrid of, [14] and [15], using identical Gaussian/independence approximations and expectation-based gradients. This connection should be explicitly acknowledged and contrasted experimentally.
- The discussion’s “constrain-then-train” framing appears as a novelty claim, but this methodology is suggested and empirically tested in [15]; please revise language.
- Novelty claim (i) is misleading: expectation backpropagation in [15] is a backprop-style update via expectation propagation for binary neurons/weights and can accommodate constrained connectivity; clarify the delta beyond chip-specific mapping and initialization.
- Prior SOTA is mis-cited: [15] achieved the best-known MNIST results for binary weights/neurons without augmentation; please correct the comparison and report results both with and without augmentation/ensembling for fairness.
- Theoretical approximations rely on large, independent fan-in and drop a σ^−3 term; provide sensitivity analyses (fan-in, input correlations) or ablations to support robustness.

Quality: Sound overall, but claims of novelty outpace derivational originality. Clarity: Generally well-written; fix minor typos and sharpen related-work positioning. Originality: Primary novelty lies in system integration, hardware mapping, and empirical efficiency. Significance: High, given the strong accuracy–energy results on real hardware.

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong and novel empirical results on neuromorphic hardware
- Clear, reproducible pipeline; practical impact
- Useful hardware-aware initialization/topology

Cons for acceptance:
- Overstated novelty; insufficient credit to [14], [15]
- Missing direct comparisons to expectation backprop baselines under matched settings
- Limited analysis of approximation validity and ensemble dependence

Recommendation: Weak accept after revising novelty claims and related-work comparisons.
