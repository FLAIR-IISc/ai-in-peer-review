- Tackles bridging backprop-trained deep networks and energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware via a “constrain-then-train” approach.
- Proposes training with continuous probabilities for spiking and synaptic activity to enable direct mapping to TrueNorth.
- Demonstrates strong MNIST accuracy and significantly improved power efficiency, especially for single-ensemble configurations.
- Core idea of using probabilistic representations for discrete elements is logical and useful for neuromorphic deployment.
- Experimental strength lies in measured power results on TrueNorth and detailed network topology/initialization description.
- Section 3.2’s algorithm closely resembles prior work ([14], [15]) and uses identical approximations without acknowledgement.
- The paper incorrectly presents “constrain-then-train” as novel despite prior exploration in ref [15].
- Misstates the state-of-the-art: ref [15] achieved better MNIST results (without augmentation) than cited work [12], undermining the “best accuracy to date” claim.
- Contributions are seen as incremental because methodology builds heavily on existing work.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, significant experimental power-efficiency results and practical pathway for deployment.
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated novelty, missing attributions to closely related prior work, and inaccurate benchmarking.
- Overall recommendation: valuable experimental findings but requires major revision to acknowledge prior work and correct benchmarking before acceptance.
