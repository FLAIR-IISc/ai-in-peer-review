- Novel approach: treat analog variables as probabilities sampled to produce binary synapses to reconcile backpropagation with spiking neuromorphic constraints.  
- Strong empirical results: competitive MNIST accuracy with very low energy consumption on TrueNorth in both high-performance and high-efficiency modes.  
- Clear, well-written manuscript demonstrating strong understanding of backpropagation and neuromorphic computing.  
- Original contribution with broad potential applicability across neuromorphic platforms.  
- Insufficient articulation of broader significance beyond a TrueNorth demonstration and unclear motivation for choosing TrueNorth.  
- Lack of concrete practical deployment scenarios (mentions mobile devices but no examples).  
- Insufficient comparison to existing methods (e.g., binarized neural networks) regarding precision, sparsity, energy, and training complexity.  
- Reproducibility concerns due to missing implementation details: input-to-spike conversion, typical spike frequencies, shared weight values on TrueNorth, and fuller neuron model description.  
- Need clearer description of how inputs are encoded as spikes.  
- Related work cited but paper needs better contextualization within wider spiking SNN training literature (e.g., Qiao et al.).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/technically sound method, strong energy-efficient results, excellent writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak practical relevance justification, insufficient comparisons, and missing implementation details.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with significant potential; accept with minor revisions addressing application relevance and reproducibility.
