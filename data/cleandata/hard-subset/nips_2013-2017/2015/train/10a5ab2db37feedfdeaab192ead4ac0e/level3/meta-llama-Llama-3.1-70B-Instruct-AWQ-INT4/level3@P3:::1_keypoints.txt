- Proposes training neural networks for neuromorphic hardware via a probabilistic deployment network that enables standard backpropagation.  
- Maps the trained model to hardware by sampling probabilities to create networks and merging them with ensemble averaging.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explains methodology, background, and results.  
- Experimental results are strong and demonstrate potential for high performance and energy efficiency on neuromorphic hardware.  
- Method is technically sound with supporting theoretical analysis and reproducible experiments.  
- Algorithm in Section 3.2 is very similar to and effectively a hybrid of refs [14] and [15], using identical approximations; authors should acknowledge and clarify differences.  
- Claiming novelty for the "constrain-then-train" methodology is misleading because it was suggested and tested in ref [15].  
- Novelty claim (i) is misleading since ref [15]’s "expectation backpropagation" already performs backprop updates for binary neurons/weights and can handle constrained connectivity.  
- Paper cites incorrect prior state-of-the-art; ref [15] achieved best-known binary results on MNIST without augmentation and should be correctly represented.  
- While not entirely novel, the paper offers a useful and unique combination of familiar techniques (probabilistic representation + hardware mapping).  
- Results are significant and address a difficult problem better than previous work, with potential impact on neuromorphic computing.  
- Overall strong paper but needs revisions to acknowledge prior work and more clearly explain its distinct contributions.
