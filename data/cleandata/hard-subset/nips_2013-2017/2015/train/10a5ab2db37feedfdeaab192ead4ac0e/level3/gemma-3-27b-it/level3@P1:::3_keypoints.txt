- Proposes a novel backpropagation training method that represents spikes and discrete synapses as probabilities to enable standard backpropagation for spiking networks.  
- Timely and important contribution for deploying deep learning on energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware and embedded systems.  
- Successfully reconciles spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity within a single trainable framework.  
- Builds on substantial prior work in deep learning and neuromorphic chips (e.g., TrueNorth, SpiNNaker) and related training approaches.  
- Core idea of treating discrete elements probabilistically is well-motivated and allows straightforward application of backpropagation.  
- Strong experimental results on MNIST, achieving state-of-the-art accuracy on TrueNorth, with clear and accessible writing.  
- Detailed experimental results and comparisons to existing work strengthen the paper’s validity.  
- Introductory novelty claims are somewhat overstated and should acknowledge prior related work and frame the contribution as a novel combination.  
- TrueNorth demonstration should be presented as validation of the training method rather than a separate novelty claim.  
- Section 2 lacks clarity on the meaning and implications of "0.15 bits per synapse" and needs a more detailed explanation.  
- Network topology description (Section 4) is fragmented; a consolidated diagram or clearer description is needed.  
- Missing experimental details: report computational cost/training time overhead due to probabilistic updates.  
- Unexplained performance disparity between 30-core and 5-core networks and why larger network performs worse.  
- Need explanation for consistent results across different ensembles of the 5-core network and for input spike-rate/testing protocol (spike trains vs rate-coded).  
- Expand related work discussion to include other spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp) and nuance limitations of TrueNorth constraints on generalizability.  
- Minor typo: “approach approach” in Ref. 12; overall text needs minor proofreading.  
- Significance caveat: strong reliance on TrueNorth-specific constraints may limit broader applicability.  
- Scalability/resource concerns due to requirement of large ensembles (e.g., 64-ensemble) to reach high accuracy.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel hardware-aware approach, strong results, clarity, addresses deployment challenge for embedded systems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated novelty, unclear experimental details, generalizability and scalability concerns.  
- Overall assessment: promising and potentially impactful work; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing clarity, experimental details, and limitation discussions.
