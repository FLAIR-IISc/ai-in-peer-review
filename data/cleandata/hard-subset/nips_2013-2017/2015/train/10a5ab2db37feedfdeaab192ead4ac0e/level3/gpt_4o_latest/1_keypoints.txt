- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" methodology to train networks under hardware constraints and map them to TrueNorth neuromorphic chip.  
- Uses probabilistic representations of spikes and synapses and details a mapping process from training to deployment.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental results: 99.42% MNIST accuracy with an ensemble of 64 and 0.268 µJ/classification with a single network.  
- Addresses the important problem of aligning deep learning algorithms with energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware.  
- Presents clear methodology and discusses potential extensions to more complex datasets and architectures.  
- Claims novelty for the constrain-then-train approach, but this technique was previously proposed in reference [15].  
- Algorithm in Section 3.2 largely combines approaches from references [14] and [15] using identical approximations, with insufficient acknowledgment.  
- Misleading claims that this is the first work to incorporate spiking neurons, low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity, since reference [15] already did so.  
- Incorrectly cites its results as prior state-of-the-art for binary weights/neurons despite reference [15] achieving better results.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to lack of novelty and poor attribution, but encourage revision with proper citations and clearer unique contributions.
