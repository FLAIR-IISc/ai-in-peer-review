- Presents a method to train spiking neural networks for neuromorphic hardware (IBM TrueNorth) by treating spikes and binary synapses as continuous probabilities during backpropagation.
- Core contribution: a "constrain-then-train" framework that integrates spiking neurons, very low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity into gradient-based training.
- Trained probabilistic networks are sampled to map onto hardware, and ensembles of sampled networks can be used to boost performance.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on MNIST, achieving 99.42% accuracy for spiking networks and high energy efficiency on TrueNorth (e.g., 0.268 µJ/class at 92.7%).
- Method is novel, principled, and elegant by enabling optimization directly for deployment constraints rather than post-hoc quantization.
- Paper is generally clear and well-written, with a detailed description of the training methodology.
- Claim of being the “first” offline method for the combined constraints is overstated; the main novelty is the successful integration of all constraints into one backprop framework.
- Missing/unclear details: the meaning of “0.15 bits per synapse” for lookup tables, and lack of a consolidated network topology description.
- Missing/unclear details: no reported training overhead or comparison of training time vs. standard MLPs given the probabilistic update rules.
- Missing/unclear details: unclear input representation—how images are converted to spike probabilities, whether inputs are single spikes or spike trains, and typical input spike rates used in testing.
- Results analysis gaps: 30-core network underperforms a 5-core network (≈85% vs. 92.7%), which is counter-intuitive and unexplained (optimization/overfitting questions).
- Results analysis gaps: small gains from larger ensembles for the 5-core network suggest low sampling variance; this behavior is not explained.
- Related-work/discussion: characterization of prior work is somewhat coarse; some prior methods already enforce hardware-like constraints during training and should be discussed more nuance-fully (and SpikeProp-like methods could be compared).
- Generalizability concerns: approach is tightly coupled to TrueNorth constraints and should discuss applicability to other neuromorphic platforms and the accuracy-vs-resource trade-off from using large ensembles.
- Recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance pending revisions to clarify implementation details, explain counter-intuitive results, and moderate novelty/generalizability claims.
