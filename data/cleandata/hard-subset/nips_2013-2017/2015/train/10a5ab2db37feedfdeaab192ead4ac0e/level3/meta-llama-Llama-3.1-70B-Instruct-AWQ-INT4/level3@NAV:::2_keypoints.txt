- Proposes treating analog variables as probabilities to sample binary synapses, adapting backpropagation for probabilistic spiking networks.  
- Validated approach on MNIST, reporting state-of-the-art performance.  
- Demonstrates feasibility by mapping trained networks to the TrueNorth chip, optimizing for size and accuracy.  
- Manuscript is high quality and clearly written, easy to follow.  
- Work is original and broadly useful across neuromorphic platforms.  
- Provides chip-measured performance figures for two hardware architectures, showing practicality.  
- Thorough analysis of accuracy versus energy-efficiency trade-offs.  
- Does not sufficiently highlight the practical significance or deployment scenarios for TrueNorth.  
- Lacks clear, detailed comparison with alternative methods to quantify novelty and advantages.  
- Missing implementation details (input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, shared weight values, neuron models).  
- Needs clearer description of input spike encoding and parameter/frequency settings for reproducibility.  
- Overall contribution is significant and, with minor revisions, could become a seminal work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: originality, clear writing, SOTA MNIST results, practical TrueNorth demonstration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons, missing implementation details, limited deployment context, unclear encoding/parameter details.
