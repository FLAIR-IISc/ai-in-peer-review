- Proposes treating continuous variables as probabilities to sample binary synapses, adapting backpropagation for probabilistic sampling in spiking networks.  
- Demonstrates method on MNIST with TrueNorth deployment, achieving 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ per image.  
- Advances prior work by addressing compatibility between backpropagation and neuromorphic hardware more comprehensively than earlier efforts.  
- Presentation is clear, well-structured, and sufficiently detailed for expert reproducibility.  
- Original integration of constrained connectivity, probabilistic sampling, and backpropagation for spiking networks with sub-trinary precision synapses.  
- Fails to articulate concrete real-world applications or how the method enables practical deployment beyond MNIST.  
- Comparison with alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]) is insufficient, hindering assessment of relative advantages.  
- Missing citations: Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427 should be added.  
- Requires more implementation details (input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, neuron models) for full reproducibility.  
- Overall technically sound and valuable; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address significance and implementation details.
