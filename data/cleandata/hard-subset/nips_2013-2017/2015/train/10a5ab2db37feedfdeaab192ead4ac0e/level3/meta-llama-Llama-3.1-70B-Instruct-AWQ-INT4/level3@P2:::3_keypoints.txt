- Presents a novel backprop-based training approach tailored to spiking-neuron, discrete-synapse hardware.  
- Proposes a learning rule and topology that trains offline with hardware-supported connectivity and continuous values in [0,1] representing spike/synapse probabilities.  
- Mapping from continuous-trained network to probabilistic spiking deployment preserves differentiability for backpropagation.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Demonstration on IBM TrueNorth hardware validates practical applicability.  
- Section 1 wording may misleadingly imply novelty of using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses; rewording needed.  
- Explanation of the network topology is scattered and should be consolidated into a single clear description.  
- Reports 99.42% MNIST accuracy with a 64-ensemble network, which is impressive.  
- Fails to report training-time overhead from probabilistic synaptic updates—important trade-off information is missing.  
- Does not explain why the single-ensemble 30-core network underperforms the 5-core network or why different 5-core ensembles yield similar results.  
- Omits input spike rates during testing and does not clarify whether inputs are spike trains.  
- Incorrectly characterizes Ref. 12 as purely train-then-constrain despite it enforcing constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training.  
- Lacks discussion or comparison with other spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- Significance: highlights importance of hardware-aware training but TN-specific constraints may not generalize to other platforms.  
- Using a 64-ensemble increases hardware resource needs for target accuracy; implications and limitations should be discussed.  
- Overall assessment: novel and interesting approach but requires clarifications and additional analyses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear exposition, and hardware validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potentially misleading claims, scattered topology description, missing training-time and ensemble-performance explanations.  
- Quality: 8/10; Clarity: 9/10; Originality: 8/10; Significance: 7/10.
