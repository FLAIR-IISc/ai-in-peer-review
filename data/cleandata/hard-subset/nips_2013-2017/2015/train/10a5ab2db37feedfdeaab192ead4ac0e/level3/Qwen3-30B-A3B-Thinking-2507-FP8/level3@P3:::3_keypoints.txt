- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" method: train with continuous spike probabilities and synapse values, then sample to map to hardware; demonstrated on MNIST with strong energy-efficiency results.  
- Novelty lies in integrating spiking neurons, extremely low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity in a single backprop-compatible training framework (not in first use of any single element).  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of the probabilistic training approach.  
- Method provides direct mapping from training to hardware, avoiding post-training constraint application.  
- Reports impressive energy efficiency (0.268 μJ/image at 92.7% accuracy), showing real-world applicability.  
- Network topology design appropriately accommodates TrueNorth architectural constraints.  
- Novelty claims are overstated; wording should clarify the contribution is the combination/integration, not the first use of spiking or low-precision synapses.  
- TrueNorth demonstration should be presented as validation of the training method rather than a separate hardware-innovation claim; avoid overstating power-efficiency novelty.  
- Clarify the "0.15 bits per synapse" claim (likely referring to extra LUT bits beyond the 1-bit synapse).  
- Consolidate the scattered network topology description into a single, clear section.  
- Report how much longer training takes due to probabilistic synaptic updates compared to standard backpropagation.  
- Explain why the 30-core single ensemble (larger network) performs worse than the 5-core single ensemble with same ensemble size.  
- Explain why different ensembles of the 5-core network produce similar results — training robustness or small network effects?  
- Clarify input representation during testing: are inputs spike trains, and what are the input spike rates?  
- Correctly characterize Reference 12 (it enforces constraints during training, e.g., bias=0) and fix the typo ("approach approach").  
- Include comparisons to other spiking backprop methods (e.g., SpikeProp) to better contextualize the contribution.  
- Acknowledge limitations: TrueNorth-specific constraints may not generalize to other neuromorphic platforms, and using 64 ensembles for best accuracy greatly increases hardware resource needs (e.g., 1920 cores).  
- Recommendation: technically sound and promising but requires major revisions to clarify novelty, add missing details, and properly contextualize prior work — accept with major revisions.
