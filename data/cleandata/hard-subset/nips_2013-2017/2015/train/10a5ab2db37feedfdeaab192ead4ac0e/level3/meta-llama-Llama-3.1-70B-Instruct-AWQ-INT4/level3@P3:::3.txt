This paper presents a novel approach to training neural networks for energy-efficient neuromorphic computing using backpropagation. The authors demonstrate a learning rule and network topology that reconciles the apparent incompatibility between backpropagation and neuromorphic hardware, which employs spiking neurons and discrete synapses. The approach is timely and interesting, as it addresses the need for energy-efficient neural networks that can be deployed on embedded systems.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, which involves treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities. This allows them to train the network using standard backpropagation, while also providing a direct representation of the deployment system.

One of the strengths of the paper is its demonstration of the approach on the TrueNorth chip, which is a neuromorphic hardware platform. The authors show that their approach can achieve high performance and energy efficiency, with a high-performance network achieving 99.42% accuracy on the MNIST dataset at 108 μJ per image.

However, there are some areas where the paper could be improved. The authors' claims in Section 1 may be worded in a way that implies novelty of using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses, which may not be entirely accurate. Additionally, the demonstration on the TrueNorth hardware should be presented as validation of the method, rather than a separate novelty claim.

The paper could also benefit from more clarity on certain aspects of the approach. For example, the network topology is not clearly described in a single, consolidated place, and the authors could provide more detail on the specific parameters used in their experiments. Additionally, the authors should clarify what "0.15 bits per synapse" means in Section 2.

The authors' approach is also limited by the fact that it requires a customized training rule, which may not be compatible with all neural network architectures. However, the authors do provide a clear explanation of their approach and its advantages, and they demonstrate its effectiveness on a specific task.
