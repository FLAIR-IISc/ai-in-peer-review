- Proposes treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to train with standard backprop, then sampling/ensemble averaging to map to neuromorphic hardware.  
- Demonstrated on TrueNorth with MNIST: ensemble of 64 → 99.42% at 108 μJ/image; single network → 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image.  
- Results are strong and show clear accuracy vs. power-efficiency trade-offs.  
- Methodological and algorithmic novelty is overstated; Section 3.2 closely resembles and hybrids refs [14] and [15] using identical approximations.  
- The "constrain-then-train" idea is not novel and was suggested/tested in ref [15]; novelty claim (i) is misleading.  
- Ref [15]’s expectation backpropagation already provides backprop updates for binary neurons/weights and can handle constrained connectivity.  
- Paper cites incorrect prior state-of-the-art; ref [15] achieved best-known binary results on MNIST without augmentation and should be acknowledged.  
- Authors should explicitly compare and acknowledge similarities to prior work (especially refs [14],[15]).  
- Technically sound with clear, detailed explanations of the approach.  
- Experimental methodology is solid and convincingly demonstrates effectiveness on hardware constraints.  
- Paper needs a more nuanced discussion of limitations and potential application domains.  
- Some sections require better organization and clearer guidance to improve reader flow.  
- Originality is limited but the combination/application of existing techniques to neuromorphic hardware is a useful contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to correct citations, clarify novelty, and expand limitations/comparisons.
