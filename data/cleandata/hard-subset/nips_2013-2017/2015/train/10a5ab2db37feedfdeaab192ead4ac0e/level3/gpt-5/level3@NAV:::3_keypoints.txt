- Paper proposes hardware-aware backprop that trains continuous spike/synapse probabilities then samples spiking networks deployable on TrueNorth (TN).  
- Method enforces TN constraints (binary crossbar, 4 axon types, limited fan-in), uses principled initialization to decorrelate cores, and ensemble averaging across sampled networks.  
- Reported results: MNIST 99.42% with 64-member ensemble (30-core topology) and 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image with a single 5-core network.  
- Technical idea is sound: Gaussian/probabilistic surrogate enables differentiability under TN constraints, and real hardware validation is valuable.  
- Several claims need tightening: avoid implying novelty of “using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses” broadly — novelty is the probabilistic backprop with TN constraints.  
- Present the TN demo as validation/experiment, not as a separate methodological novelty.  
- Missing comparisons/discussion vs. earlier spiking backprop (SpikeProp) and recent surrogate-gradient methods, and vs. expectation-backprop for binary networks.  
- Clarity issues: full network topology (layers, R/F/S, neurons per core, fan-in/fan-out) should be consolidated in one place.  
- Clarify “0.15 bits per synapse” — detail how this is amortized over axon-type LUTs and neurons.  
- Report input spike rates during testing and specify input encoding (Poisson spike trains, single-timestep Bernoulli, etc.).  
- Fix minor text errors (e.g., “approach approach”) and correct claim about Ref.12 (it enforces TN constraints during training, not purely post hoc).  
- Significance concerns: TN-specific constraints may limit generality, and SOTA accuracy relies on a large 64-member ensemble, raising hardware cost.  
- Requested additional reporting: (a) training-time overhead from probabilistic connection updates, (b) why a single 30-core ensemble performs worse than a single 5-core network, (c) why different 5-core ensembles yield similar results.  
- Pros: timely hardware-aware training with real hardware validation; strong energy–accuracy trade-offs; principled initialization.  
- Cons: overstated novelty in intro; missing comparisons to spiking backprop and expectation-backprop; incomplete reporting (bits/synapse, input rates, training overhead); reliance on large ensembles and TN-specificity limits generality.
