- Proposes Norm-POEM, a self-normalized risk estimator for batch learning from logged bandit feedback (BLBF) that avoids propensity overfitting.  
- Demonstrates Norm-POEM’s effectiveness through extensive empirical evaluations on multiple multi-label classification problems.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of method and theoretical foundations.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of the propensity overfitting problem and its consequences.  
- Shows Norm-POEM consistently outperforms the conventional unbiased estimator and analyzes reasons for improvement.  
- Significance: introduces a new training method that addresses a critical BLBF issue, making a valuable field contribution.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with related works (e.g., SpikeProp) to highlight novelty.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational efficiency and potential applications to other domains.  
- Strengths: clear writing, rigorous analysis, and convincing empirical results.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparative baselines and computational/application details.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10.
