This paper proposes a novel approach to reconcile the incompatibility between high-precision backpropagation and spike-based devices, such as neuromorphic chips, by treating analog variables as probabilities sampled to produce binary synapses. The authors adapt backpropagation to be compatible with the probabilistic sampling strategy for spiking networks, allowing for the training of networks that can be directly mapped onto neuromorphic hardware.

The paper is well-written, and the quality of the manuscript is high. The work is original and broadly useful across platforms, making it a valuable contribution to the field. The authors validate their method on the MNIST dataset and demonstrate its effectiveness on the TrueNorth chip, optimizing for both size and accuracy.

The paper presents chip-measured performance figures for two different hardware architectures, showcasing the potential of this approach for practical deployment. However, the reviewer suggests that the manuscript could be improved by adding citations to relevant works, such as Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.

One of the main concerns is that the paper does not sufficiently highlight the significance or practical deployment scenarios for TrueNorth. The authors could benefit from providing more context on which specific applications would benefit from this method and how it enables practical deployment. Additionally, a clear comparison of the advantages of this approach over alternative methods, such as those described in refs [9]–[12], would strengthen the paper.

The results presented in the paper are convincing, but some implementation details are missing. For example, the authors could provide more information on how inputs are encoded as spikes and provide parameter and frequency details for reproducibility. Clarifying these aspects would enhance the paper's overall quality and make it more accessible to readers.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, this paper scores high on quality, clarity, and originality. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the technical aspects of their work and provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the significance and practical implications of their work.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and original approach to reconciling high-precision backpropagation with spike-based devices.
* The authors validate their method on a well-known dataset and demonstrate its effectiveness on a specific hardware platform.
* The paper is well-written, and the quality of the manuscript is high.
* The work is broadly useful across platforms, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper does not sufficiently highlight the significance or practical deployment scenarios for TrueNorth.
* The authors could benefit from providing a clear comparison of the advantages of this approach over alternative methods.
* Some implementation details are missing, which could make it difficult for readers to reproduce the results.
* The paper could benefit from more context on which specific applications would benefit from this method and how it enables practical deployment.

Overall, this paper is a strong contribution to the field, and with some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, it has the potential to be a highly impactful publication.
