- Paper proposes training continuous spike/synapse probabilities via a Gaussian/probit approximation and mapping sampled binaries to TrueNorth, optionally ensembling for accuracy/energy trade-offs.
- Approach closely resembles prior probabilistic/backprop treatments (EBP [15], stochastic bit-stream networks [14]); Section 3.2’s surrogate gradient is conceptually similar to EBP.
- Main novelty is not the learning rule but practical integration with TrueNorth constraints: topology/weight-blocking, mapping/ensembling strategy, and initialization that balances s-values.
- Strong experimental demonstration on real neuromorphic hardware with compelling energy/accuracy numbers (e.g., 0.268 μJ/class @ 92.7%, 4 μJ/class @ 95%).
- Provides a complete pipeline from constrain-then-train through deployment, with sensible engineering choices (balanced s-values, progressive mixing).
- Empirical evidence supports the constrain-then-train perspective under TrueNorth hardware limits.
- Claims of algorithmic novelty are overstated; the authors should explicitly acknowledge and contrast their method with [14,15].
- The Discussion/Intro should be revised to clarify what is new relative to EBP/EP and tone down claim (i).
- Prior SOTA comparisons are inaccurate/unclear; single-model (no-ensemble, no-augmentation) vs. ensemble/augmented results must be reported separately and compared like-for-like.
- Several methodological approximations need empirical justification: dropping the σ^3 term in Eq.4 and the Gaussian/independence assumptions (especially at small fan-in or correlated inputs).
- Recommend ablations on: σ^3 approximation, ensemble size vs. accuracy variability, data augmentation effects, initialization scheme impact, and learned vs. fixed s-values.
- Need calibration analysis comparing probit/CCDF surrogate loss to empirical spike-sampled test-time behavior.
- Topology comparisons are lacking: compare the hardware-aware topology to standard convolutional/topologies with similar parameter budgets to isolate architecture vs. ensembling gains.
- Paper is well-engineered and technically sound overall; hardware measurements appear careful, but gradient simplifications require empirical backing.
- Clarity can be improved by explicitly naming the probit surrogate, making the EBP/EP relation explicit, tidying equations, and reporting confidence intervals over multiple sampling runs.
- Algorithmic originality is limited; principal contribution is practical integration with TrueNorth and the hardware demonstration rather than a novel learning algorithm.
- Significance is high for neuromorphic practitioners because the work shows deployable backprop-trained probabilistic binary networks with excellent energy efficiency.
- Suggested improvements: acknowledge/delineate differences to [14,15], correct SOTA baselines, add the recommended ablations, compare to other surrogate-gradient methods (e.g., straight-through), and discuss portability to other neuromorphic platforms.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong hardware results, end-to-end deployment pipeline, and high practical relevance to efficient ML/neuromorphic communities.
- Arguments against acceptance: overstated novelty relative to prior work and insufficient apples-to-apples comparisons and ablation analyses.
