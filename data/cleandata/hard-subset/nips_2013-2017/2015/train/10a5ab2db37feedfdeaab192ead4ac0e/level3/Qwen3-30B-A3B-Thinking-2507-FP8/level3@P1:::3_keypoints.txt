- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" method that treats synapses and neuron outputs as continuous probabilities during training, then samples to produce spiking, low-precision networks for neuromorphic hardware.  
- Validated on IBM TrueNorth with MNIST: 99.42% accuracy (64-ensemble, 108 μJ/image) and 92.7% accuracy (single ensemble, 0.268 μJ/image).

- Clear, well-structured paper with a compelling problem statement and concise technical exposition.  
- Probabilistic training framework is elegant and ensures training error maps to deployment error, avoiding train-then-constrain pitfalls.  
- Hardware validation on TrueNorth is rigorous and reports state-of-the-art energy-efficiency for MNIST.  
- Network topology is well-motivated for TrueNorth constraints; ensemble averaging effectively leverages hardware parallelism.

- Novelty is overstated: prior work covered spiking neurons with high-precision weights or low-precision synapses with continuous neurons; claim should emphasize integration of all three constraints plus hardware validation.  
- Technical details missing: clarification of "0.15 bits per synapse" (lookup-table overhead) and training time/cost for probabilistic updates.  
- Inconsistent/unexplained results: counterintuitive performance of the 30-core vs 5-core single-ensemble networks and near-identical accuracies across 5-core ensembles need explanation.  
- Reproducibility gaps: input spike rates and spike train durations during testing are unspecified.  
- Network topology description is fragmented across figures and text; needs a consolidated presentation.  
- Generalizability claim is overstated: approach is TrueNorth-specific in implementation but probabilistic framework is likely adaptable; 64-ensemble requirement increases hardware resource needs and may limit benefits.  
- Comparative context lacking/incorrect: should compare to SpikeProp and correct characterization of Ref. 12 (which enforces some constraints during training).

- Pro: strong methodology, rigorous hardware validation, and high energy-efficiency; Con: novelty overstatement, missing implementation details, unexplained anomalies, and limited generalizability.

- Overall: high-quality, technically sound contribution with practical significance for neuromorphic hardware; recommend accept with minor revisions to address novelty claims and reproducibility/details.
