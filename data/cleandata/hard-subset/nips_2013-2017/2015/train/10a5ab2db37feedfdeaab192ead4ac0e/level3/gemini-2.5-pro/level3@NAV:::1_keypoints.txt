- Presents a method to train deep nets for spiking neuromorphic hardware by treating synapses and activations as continuous probabilities during offline training and sampling to map to hardware.  
- Addresses the important problem of reconciling continuous backpropagation with discrete spiking systems for energy-constrained deployment.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental results on TrueNorth: 99.42% accuracy with an ensemble on MNIST and 0.268 μJ/image energy for a single network.  
- Successful physical deployment and measured energy/accuracy trade-offs on TrueNorth are a significant engineering contribution.  
- Claims of methodological novelty are overstated; the core algorithm closely resembles/combines methods from Zhao et al. [14] and Cheng et al. [15].  
- The “constrain-then-train” approach is not new and was previously proposed/demonstrated in [15].  
- The paper’s novelty claim (i) is misleading because expectation backpropagation in [15] already provides backprop updates for binary neurons/weights and constrained connectivity.  
- The prior state-of-the-art citation is incorrect: [15] achieved better binary-network MNIST results without augmentation than the work the authors cite.  
- Recommendation: acceptance should require a major revision that properly situates the method within existing literature (especially [15]) and tempers novelty claims.
