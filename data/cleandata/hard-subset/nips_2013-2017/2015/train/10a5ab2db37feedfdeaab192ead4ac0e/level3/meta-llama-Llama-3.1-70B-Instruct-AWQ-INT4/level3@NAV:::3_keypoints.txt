- Proposes a backpropagation training method for neuromorphic hardware by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable standard backpropagation mapping to hardware.  
- Demonstrates the approach on MNIST using the TrueNorth chip, reporting high performance and energy efficiency.  
- Timely and interesting approach that reconciles backpropagation with neuromorphic hardware constraints.  
- Method is useful for multi-neuron hardware platforms with constrained synapses and spikes.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Section 1 may overstate novelty regarding spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses.  
- TrueNorth demonstration should be framed as validation of the method rather than a separate novelty claim.  
- Network topology description is scattered and not consolidated in one place.  
- "0.15 bits per synapse" concept in Section 2 is not clearly explained.  
- Authors fail to report increased training time due to probabilistic synaptic updates.  
- Performance gap between the single-ensemble 30-core network and the 5-core network is insufficiently explained.  
- Lacks clear discussion on the significance and generalizability of results to other hardware platforms.  
- Paper is technically sound but contains potentially overstated claims and needs more detailed explanations and comparisons (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address clarity, explanation of key concepts, and discussion of generalizability.
