- Core idea: probabilistic relaxation of spikes and binary synapses with a Gaussian approximation yields smooth surrogates and enables “constrain-then-train” backprop under hardware constraints.  
- Mapping: trained networks are sampled into binary/spiking instantiations and mapped to TrueNorth respecting 256 shared inputs, binary crossbar, 4 axon types with shared s-values, and a progressive-mixing topology.  
- Ensembling: multiple sampled instantiations can be ensembled to trade hardware resources for accuracy (e.g., 99.42% on MNIST with 64-member ensemble; 92.7% with a 5-core single network at 0.268 μJ/image).  
- Technical soundness: derivations and gradients are clearly presented; dropping the variance-derivative term is reasonable under assumptions but needs ablation.  
- Empirical support: end-to-end chip-measured results show strong accuracy–energy trade-offs and state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy for SNNs.  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing critical implementation details (input-to-spike encoding, spike rates per layer, neuron model parameters, exact axon s-values and mapping).  
- Clarity: manuscript is well written, organized, and figures/topology descriptions are helpful.  
- Originality: novel combination of probabilistic training for both spikes and binary synapses while respecting TrueNorth connectivity/weight-sharing constraints; progressive-mixing topology is a useful design contribution.  
- Missing citations: add Qiao et al., Frontiers in Neuroscience (2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Significance: method generalizes to other low-precision spiking substrates and ensembling provides a pragmatic deployment knob.  
- System-level discussion lacking: paper underplays deployment scenarios, latency, memory/area impact of ensembles, toolchain, and I/O constraints.  
- Request: discuss concrete use cases and trade-offs for the 5-core “high-efficiency” setting vs larger ensemble settings, including area/memory/latency guidance.  
- Request: clarify advantages over train-then-constrain pipelines and provide controlled comparisons on same topology/hardware.  
- Reproducibility request: specify input encoding details (scaling, normalization, per-image sampling, expected spike counts mean/variance).  
- Reproducibility request: report timing/rates—layer-wise average firing rates, sparsity, and effects of longer time windows on accuracy/energy.  
- Reproducibility request: provide exact TrueNorth neuron parameters (leak, threshold, reset, refractory, stochasticity) and bias-to-leak quantization behavior.  
- Reproducibility request: specify the four axon 9-bit s-values chosen, their distribution, block sharing enforcement, and random-seed strategy for sampling.  
- Reproducibility request: give full training protocol (epochs, learning-rate schedule, decay milestones, dropout placement, augmentations, regularizers).  
- Reproducibility request: include pseudocode for initialization/balancing (orthogonalization/balancing and handling partially utilized cores).  
- Ablation request: quantify error from dropping the σ^−3 term in the gradient and its impact on convergence/accuracy.  
- Ablation request: separate ensembling gains vs single-sample performance—show variance-reduction curves and ensemble size for saturation.  
- Ablation request: report sensitivity to violations of independence/Gaussian assumptions (e.g., correlated inputs).  
- Hardware metrics request: detail measurement setup (counters, sampling interval), leakage accounting, and provide 95% confidence intervals across runs.  
- Comparison clarity: when contrasting with other platforms (SpiNNaker), state which numbers are measured vs from literature and note task/latency/voltage/frequency differences.  
- Pros: principled hardware-matched training, SOTA MNIST for SNNs, excellent energy/throughput with chip-measured results, clear writing and practical topology/initialization insights.  
- Cons: insufficient system-level deployment discussion, missing implementation details hindering reproduction, and limited head-to-head comparisons with strong alternatives.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing requested implementation details, comparisons, and ablations to improve impact and reproducibility.
