- Proposes training by relaxing spikes and binary synapses into continuous probabilities, then sampling to deploy binary synapses and spike trains.  
- Uses a Gaussian approximation for the weighted sum of Bernoulli inputs to enable differentiable spiking probabilities and adapted backpropagation with a gradient approximation.  
- Matches network topology to TrueNorth constraints via sliding windows and progressive receptive-field mixing.  
- Introduces a core-level weight initialization that balances positive/negative s-values and minimizes pairwise inner products to reduce redundancy.  
- Deploys trained probabilistic networks by sampling once or multiple times; ensembles trade energy for accuracy.  
- Reports MNIST results: 30-core network 99.42% with 64-sample ensemble (108 μJ/image), 5-core network 92.7% with single sample (0.268 μJ/image) at 1 kHz.  
- Situates work between backprop-trained SNNs, binary/trinary networks, and neuromorphic hardware; contrasts constrain-then-train with train-then-constrain conversions.  
- Claims novelty in jointly addressing spiking outputs, 1-bit synapses, constrained connectivity, and preservation of standard backprop.  
- Ensemble-by-sampling from one probabilistic model is highlighted as an elegant contribution.  
- Methodology is generally sound; Gaussian and gradient simplifications are reasonable but need ablation validation.  
- Hardware measurements are credible (on-chip, active plus pro-rated leakage energy reported).  
- Missing implementation details hinder reproducibility: input encoding specifics (pixel→spike mapping, timing, rate vs single-sample).  
- Missing neuron model/parameter details: leak, threshold, refractory behavior, reset mode, time constants.  
- Missing synaptic details: exact s-value tables, axon-type assignments, block definitions, and layer-specific s-values.  
- Missing ensemble deployment details: time-multiplexing vs parallel, latency/throughput trade-offs, memory footprint.  
- Power methodology lacks absolute numbers breakdown and sensitivity analysis (batch size, duty cycle, clock).  
- Comparison baselines need clearer matching of input encoding and hardware constraints to be fair.  
- Clarity is good overall but needs fixes: Equation (3) formatting, earlier definition of pk, explicit class-averaging variance computation, and consolidated training hyperparameters/data augmentation.  
- Reproducibility would benefit from a hardware-parameter table and a detailed appendix with neuron/synapse/encoding/power measurement details.  
- Suggested ablations: effect of gradient approximation, ensemble size vs accuracy/energy, s-value blocking schemes, topology hyperparameters, and matched train-then-constrain baselines.  
- Suggest adding related citations and clearer discussion of differences with contemporary low-precision/binarized training and SNN conversion works.  
- Recommends clarifying practical deployment scenarios and how accuracy/energy/latency scale beyond MNIST.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, contingent on adding requested implementation details and stronger comparisons.
