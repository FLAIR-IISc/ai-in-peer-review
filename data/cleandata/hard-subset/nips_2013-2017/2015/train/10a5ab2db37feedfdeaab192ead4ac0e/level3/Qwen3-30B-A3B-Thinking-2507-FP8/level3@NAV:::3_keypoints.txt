- Proposes a backprop training method for SNNs that trains continuous spike probabilities and maps to discrete-synapse hardware by sampling ensembles.  
- Strength: clear presentation and practical validation on TrueNorth hardware.  
- Strength: shows constrain-then-train yields better deployment than train-then-constrain.  
- Strength: strong MNIST result (99.42% with 64-ensemble) at low energy (108 μJ/image).  
- Weakness: novelty is overstated—spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses are not new; TrueNorth demo should be framed as validation.  
- Need to clarify what "0.15 bits per synapse" means.  
- Consolidate network topology description into a single, clear location.  
- Report training time comparisons versus standard backprop.  
- Explain why the single-ensemble 30-core network underperforms the 5-core network.  
- Justify why different 5-core ensembles produce similar results.  
- Specify input spike rates during testing and confirm whether inputs are spike trains.  
- Compare and discuss related spiking backprop methods (e.g., SpikeProp).  
- Fix typo ("approach approach") and note Ref. 12 enforces constraints during training (not purely train-then-constrain).  
- Concern: TrueNorth-specific constraints may not generalize; using a 64-ensemble increases hardware resource requirements for the reported accuracy.  
- Overall: solid, practical contribution but requires careful revision on the above points before acceptance.
