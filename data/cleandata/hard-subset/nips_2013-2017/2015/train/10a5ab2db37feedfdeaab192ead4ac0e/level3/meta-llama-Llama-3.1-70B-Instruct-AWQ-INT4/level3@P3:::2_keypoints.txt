- Proposes a novel method reconciling high-precision backpropagation with spike-based devices by treating analog variables as probabilities sampled into binary synapses.  
- Adapts backpropagation to be compatible with probabilistic sampling for spiking networks.  
- Validated on MNIST and mapped trained networks onto the TrueNorth chip, optimizing for size and accuracy.  
- Manuscript is high quality and clearly written, making the approach easy to follow.  
- The approach is original and broadly useful across neuromorphic platforms, addressing a key field challenge.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art MNIST performance with both a high-performance ensemble (64) and a high-efficiency network (ensemble of 1).  
- Presents chip-measured performance on two hardware architectures, demonstrating energy efficiency.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison with alternative methods (refs [9]–[12]).  
- Insufficient implementation details for reproducibility (input-to-spike conversion, spike frequencies, shared weight values, neuron models).  
- Needs clarification on input spike encoding and specific parameter/frequency settings.  
- Unclear discussion of the practical significance and deployment scenarios for TrueNorth and target applications.  
- Missing citations: Qiao et al. (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution that, with the suggested revisions, could become a highly impactful publication.  
- Recommended revisions: add comparative analysis, provide implementation and encoding details, discuss deployment use-cases, and include the suggested citations.
