- Paper proposes a "constrain-then-train" method using probabilistic spiking neurons and discrete synapses for TrueNorth deployment, reporting 99.42% (64-ensemble, 108 μJ/image) and 92.7% (single-ensemble, 0.268 μJ/image) on MNIST.  
- Experimental results are robust and demonstrate strong accuracy/energy tradeoffs on neuromorphic hardware.  
- TrueNorth implementation provides concrete and impactful validation of the deployment methodology.  
- Network topology (progressive mixing) and the ensemble approach are clearly described.  
- Novelty claims are significantly overstated; the algorithm in Section 3.2 is mathematically identical to expectation backpropagation from Cheng et al., 2015 (ref [15]).  
- The paper incorrectly presents "constrain-then-train" as novel despite ref [15] proposing and testing this approach.  
- The introduction’s claim of achieving the best accuracy is misleading: ref [15] obtained 97.88% on MNIST with binary weights/neurons without augmentation.  
- The paper misstates that ref [15] lacks constrained connectivity, when that work explicitly accommodates it.  
- Core methodology is not original; the algorithmic contribution is derivative of prior probabilistic backpropagation work.  
- Energy-efficiency results are practically significant for neuromorphic systems despite the novelty issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to correct attribution and claims.  
- Required revisions: fully acknowledge ref [15] in Section 3.2 and the introduction, correct the MNIST accuracy comparison, and clarify that "constrain-then-train" was first proposed in ref [15].
