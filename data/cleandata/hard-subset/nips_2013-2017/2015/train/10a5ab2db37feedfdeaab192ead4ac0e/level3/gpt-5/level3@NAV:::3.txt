Summary: The paper proposes a hardware-aware backpropagation method that treats spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities during training, then samples these probabilities to produce one or more spiking networks deployable on TrueNorth (TN). The approach respects TN constraints (binary crossbar, 4 axon types, limited fan-in), uses a principled initialization to decorrelate cores, and leverages ensemble averaging across sampled networks. On MNIST, the method attains 99.42% with a 64-member ensemble (30-core topology) and 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image with a single 5-core network. Relative to prior work that either trains spiking neurons with high-precision weights, relies on weight/threshold balancing post hoc, or trains binary synapses with continuous units, the contribution is a backprop rule that directly accommodates spiking neurons, extremely low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity. Please also relate to earlier spiking backprop rules (e.g., SpikeProp and surrogate-gradient methods) and expectation-backprop for binary networks.

Quality: The technical idea is sound and timely; the Gaussian/probabilistic surrogate facilitates differentiability under TN constraints, and the hardware validation is valuable. That said, several claims need tightening and some critical details are missing. Please (i) reword Section 1 to avoid implying novelty of “using spiking neurons and reduced-precision synapses” per se; the novelty is the specific probabilistic backprop with TN-style constraints; (ii) present the TN demo as validation, not a separate novelty.

Clarity: The paper reads well, but key information is scattered. Please consolidate the full network topology (all layers, R/F/S, number of neurons per core, fan-in/fan-out) in one place. Clarify Section 2’s “0.15 bits per synapse” (how amortized over axon-type LUTs and neurons). Report input spike rates during testing and whether inputs are spike trains (Poisson? single-timestep Bernoulli?). Fix “approach approach” and note that Ref. 12 enforces constraints (e.g., bias=0) during training (not purely train-then-constrain).

Originality: Novel combination of probabilistic surrogates with TN-constrained training. Please add a comparison/discussion vs. SpikeProp and recent surrogate-gradient backprop to position the contribution more clearly.

Significance: Hardware-aware training is important. However, TN-specific constraints may limit generality, and achieving SOTA accuracy requires a 64-member ensemble, increasing hardware cost. Please report (a) the training-time overhead due to probabilistic connection updates, (b) why the single-ensemble 30-core network performs worse than the 5-core network, and (c) why different 5-core ensembles yield similar results.

Pros:
- Timely, clear hardware-aware training with real hardware validation
- Strong energy-accuracy trade-offs; principled initialization

Cons:
- Overstated novelty in intro; missing comparisons to spiking backprop
- Incomplete reporting (bits/synapse, input rates, training-time overhead)
- Reliance on large ensembles and TN-specificity limits generality
