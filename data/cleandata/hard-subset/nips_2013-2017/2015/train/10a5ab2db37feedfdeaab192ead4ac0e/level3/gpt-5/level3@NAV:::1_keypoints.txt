- Summary: Proposes a probabilistic surrogate to train spiking, low-precision neuromorphic networks via probabilistic synapses/spikes and Gaussian approximation of weighted Bernoulli sums, deploying learned synapse probabilities as ensembles on TrueNorth.

- Reports strong MNIST results: 99.42% with 64-member ensemble (108 μJ/image) and 92.7% single-member (0.268 μJ/image) at 1 kHz on TrueNorth.

- Strength: Strong, carefully measured energy–accuracy trade-off with practical importance for neuromorphic computing.

- Strength: Novel, end-to-end system demonstration on real TrueNorth hardware with real-time throughput.

- Strength: Hardware-aware topology and initialization are sensible, clearly described, and reproducible.

- Weakness: Algorithm in Section 3.2 is highly similar to prior work ([14], [15]) using the same Gaussian/independence approximations and expectation-based gradients; this should be explicitly acknowledged and experimentally contrasted.

- Weakness: “Constrain-then-train” novelty claim is misleading because similar methodology is suggested and tested in [15]; revise framing.

- Weakness: Novelty claim (i) is misleading—expectation backpropagation in [15] already provides backprop-style updates for binary neurons/weights and can handle constrained connectivity; clarify the incremental contribution beyond chip-specific mapping/initialization.

- Weakness: Prior SOTA is mis-cited—[15] achieved best-known MNIST results for binary weights/neurons without augmentation; compare fairly by reporting results with/without augmentation and ensembling.

- Weakness: Theoretical approximations assume large independent fan-in and drop a σ^-3 term; provide sensitivity analyses (fan-in, input correlations) or ablations to validate robustness.

- Quality: Overall sound but claims of novelty outpace derivational originality.

- Clarity: Generally well-written; fix minor typos and sharpen related-work positioning.

- Originality/Significance: Main novelty is system integration, hardware mapping, and empirical efficiency; significance is high due to strong accuracy–energy results on real hardware.

- Pros for acceptance: Strong hardware results, reproducible pipeline, and useful hardware-aware initialization/topology.

- Cons for acceptance: Overstated novelty and insufficient credit to [14]/[15], missing direct baseline comparisons under matched settings, and limited analysis of approximation validity and ensemble dependence.

- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on revising novelty claims and improving related-work comparisons.
