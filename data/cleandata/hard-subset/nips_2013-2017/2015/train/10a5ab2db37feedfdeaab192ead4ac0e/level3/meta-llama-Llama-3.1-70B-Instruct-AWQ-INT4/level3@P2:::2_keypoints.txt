- Proposes treating analog variables as probabilities sampled to produce binary synapses and adapts backpropagation for spiking networks.  
- Validated on MNIST and mapped onto IBM TrueNorth, optimizing for size and accuracy.  
- Manuscript is well-written, clearly organized, and includes thorough mathematical derivations.  
- Original, broadly useful approach that bridges high-precision backpropagation and spike-based neuromorphic devices.  
- Demonstrates networks that map to neuromorphic hardware with high accuracy and low power consumption.  
- Lacks sufficient context on TrueNorth’s significance and practical deployment scenarios/applications.  
- Needs a clearer comparison of advantages and weaknesses relative to alternative methods.  
- Missing implementation details critical for reproducibility (e.g., spike encoding, parameter and frequency values).  
- Overall technically sound and significant; suitable for acceptance pending minor revisions.  
- Reviewer recommends adding citations to Qiao et al. (Frontiers 2015) and arXiv:1506.05427.  
- Suggested revisions: expand TrueNorth background, provide encoding/parameter details, and clarify comparative strengths.
