- Proposes a principled probabilistic relaxation to train gradient-based networks for spiking, binary-synapse, connectivity-constrained neuromorphic hardware (TrueNorth).  
- Interprets inputs, neuron outputs, and synaptic connectivity as probabilities in [0,1], using continuous differentiable surrogates during training and sampling binary networks for deployment.  
- Derives neuron spike probabilities via a Gaussian approximation to the weighted sum and adapts backprop with a practical gradient approximation that drops higher-order terms under balanced-input assumptions.  
- Fixes synaptic strengths a priori in balanced ±1 block patterns to minimize on-core redundancy; only synapse on/off probabilities and biases are learned.  
- Maps trained models to hardware by sampling learned probabilities; ensembles of sampled networks reduce variance and improve accuracy without retraining.  
- Uses a sliding-window, locally connected topology that respects TrueNorth core input fan-in and shared input sets (no weight sharing).  
- Novelty: jointly handles spiking neurons, sub-2-bit synapses, and strict hardware connectivity with concrete real-time TrueNorth deployment and energy measurements.  
- Empirical MNIST results: 99.42% with 64-member ensemble (30 cores, 108 μJ/image), 92.7% with a single 5-core instance (0.268 μJ/image), and a claim of 4 μJ at 95% accuracy (needs clarification).  
- Strength: technically sound formulation making backprop compatible with spiking, low-precision, constrained-connectivity hardware.  
- Strength: clear derivation and thoughtful, hardware-aware initialization strategy to minimize inter-neuron redundancy.  
- Strength: real hardware measurements with detailed energy accounting and real-time throughput.  
- Weakness: Gaussian/independence and gradient simplification assumptions are argued qualitatively but lack empirical ablation or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: synaptic strengths are fixed; no comparison to learning block-wise strengths to quantify accuracy/energy/complexity trade-offs.  
- Weakness: lacks direct controlled comparisons to alternative SNN training/conversion methods under matched topology and quantization constraints.  
- Weakness: incomplete reproducibility details (input encoding, neuron parameters, synaptic strength distributions, ensemble averaging, training schedule, measurement protocol).  
- Clarity requests: specify pixel-to-spike mapping, sampling scheme, and any rate/temporal coding used.  
- Clarity requests: provide TrueNorth neuron parameters (leak/threshold/reset/refractory) and bias quantization/mapping details.  
- Clarity requests: describe synaptic strength distributions across axon types and exact ±1 assignments per core.  
- Clarity requests: detail ensemble averaging, output decoding, random seeds, and training hyperparameters (epochs, LR decay, regularization).  
- Clarity requests: reconcile and detail energy measurement methodology (what’s included/excluded for claimed μJ numbers).  
- Originality: combination of probabilistic backprop, binary block-constrained synapses, hardware-aware topology, and silicon demonstration is original and timely; add related citations (e.g., Qiao et al. 2015; arXiv:1506.05427).  
- Significance: advances practical training-to-neuromorphic deployment with strong energy/throughput numbers but is limited by MNIST-only evaluation and locally connected topologies.  
- Recommendation: pros — principled method, real hardware deployment, strong energy numbers, broadly applicable idea; cons — limited evaluation, reliance on ensembles for top accuracy, missing controlled comparisons, and reproducibility gaps.  
- Requests to authors: provide missing implementation details; add controlled comparisons to alternative methods; empirically validate gradient and Gaussian approximations; clarify deployment scenarios and advantages; add requested related-work citations; include a small convolutional experiment or second dataset if feasible.  
- Overall evaluation: strong, well-written contribution with compelling hardware evidence; leans toward acceptance conditional on addressing reproducibility and comparison gaps.
