- Addresses bridging the energy-efficiency gap between neuromorphic hardware and backprop-trained DNNs.  
- Core idea: represent spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable backpropagation.  
- Well-motivated and clearly presented approach with promising TrueNorth MNIST results.  
- Highly relevant for deploying deep learning on edge devices.  
- Novel, elegant probabilistic mapping from continuous networks to discrete neuromorphic hardware.  
- Strong experimental results: 99.42% accuracy (ensemble of 64) and 0.268 μJ/image (ensemble of 1) on TrueNorth.  
- Clear comparisons to prior spiking and low-power methods highlighting the contribution.  
- Detailed network topology and training methodology provided, supporting reproducibility.  
- Includes discussion of limitations and future directions.  
- Evaluation limited to MNIST; no experiments on more complex datasets like CIFAR-10 or ImageNet.  
- Gaussian approximation assumes input independence; potential inaccuracies from input correlations not investigated.  
- Hyperparameter settings briefly noted but lack comprehensive ablation to show their impact.  
- Claim of balanced synaptic initialization needs empirical evidence on training speed and final accuracy.  
- Network topology is TrueNorth-specific; adaptation to other neuromorphic platforms not discussed.  
- Comparisons focus on accuracy and energy only; missing metrics such as inference latency and model size.  
- Overall assessment: significant, novel contribution with strong results on MNIST; recommended for acceptance if expanded evaluations and minor weaknesses are addressed.
