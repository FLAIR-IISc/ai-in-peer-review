- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" method treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities, then sampling to map networks to neuromorphic hardware.  
- Demonstrated on TrueNorth with MNIST: 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ/image (ensemble 64) and 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image (single network).  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and well-motivated methodology linking training error to deployment error under hardware constraints.  
- Strength: impressive experimental results showing high accuracy and unprecedented energy efficiency on low-power hardware.  
- Strength: sufficient technical detail provided to reproduce key methodology steps (probabilistic neurons, initialization).  
- Weakness: theoretical justification for the Gaussian approximation (Eq.3) is insufficient and lacks rigorous analysis or validation.  
- Weakness: assumption of input independence and large counts for Gaussian approximation is not validated across architectures or layers.  
- Weakness: no evidence of how approximation error affects final network performance.  
- Weakness: novelty and "best accuracy to date" claims are not fully substantiated; comparisons to prior spiking and binary-synapse work are incomplete.  
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from related probabilistic backpropagation approaches and missing comparisons to recent spiking-network results.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations and scalability to deeper networks, larger/complex datasets, and potential failure modes of the Gaussian approximation.  
- Recommendation: accept but require revisions to address theoretical concerns, provide a more comprehensive prior-work comparison, and expand discussion of limitations and scalability.
