- Summary: Proposes a novel backprop-based learning rule and topology for energy-efficient neuromorphic networks, validated on MNIST with TrueNorth showing high accuracy and low energy.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on neuromorphic and deep learning literature (Neurogrid, SpiNNaker, TrueNorth, spiking/low-precision training) and claims novelty in applying backprop to spiking neurons with discrete synapses.  
- Strength — Novelty: Introduces a new approach reconciling backpropagation with neuromorphic hardware.  
- Strength — Effectiveness: Demonstrates strong MNIST results on TrueNorth with low energy consumption.  
- Strength — Scalability claim: Authors suggest the method can scale to more complex datasets/networks.  
- Weakness — Complexity: Requires a customized training rule that may be harder to implement than standard backprop.  
- Weakness — Limited generalizability: Tailored to spiking-neuron, discrete-synapse neuromorphic hardware, possibly not applicable to other platforms.  
- Weakness — Lack of theory: Paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the method and its limitations.  
- Quality: Well-written with clear methodology and reproducible experiments, but would benefit from deeper theoretical insight and limitation discussion.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and accessible to readers familiar with deep learning and neuromorphic computing.  
- Originality: Novel combination of deep learning and neuromorphic techniques, though built on existing prior work.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for energy-efficient neuromorphic applications; MNIST/TrueNorth results are promising.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel/original approach, empirical validation on hardware, and potential field impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Implementation complexity, limited applicability beyond neuromorphic hardware, and insufficient theoretical underpinning.  
- Recommendations: Provide a detailed theoretical analysis, expand discussion of limitations and applications, and include experiments on more complex datasets/networks.
