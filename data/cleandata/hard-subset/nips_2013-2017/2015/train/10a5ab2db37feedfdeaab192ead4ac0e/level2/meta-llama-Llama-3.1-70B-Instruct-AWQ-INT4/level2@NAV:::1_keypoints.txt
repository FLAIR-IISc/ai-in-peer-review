- Presents a method to train networks for neuromorphic hardware by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities, enabling standard backpropagation.  
- Demonstrates the method on MNIST with TrueNorth, claiming state-of-the-art performance and energy efficiency.  
- Claims the approach naturally maps to spiking neurons and extremely low-precision synapses.  
- Claims achieves high accuracy-energy tradeoffs (99.42% at 108 μJ/image; 92.7% at 0.268 μJ/image).  
- Provides detailed description of learning rule and network topology to support reproducibility.  
- Experimental results on MNIST substantiate effectiveness and energy-efficiency claims.  
- Method is practically useful for energy-constrained applications (e.g., mobile, autonomous systems).  
- Authors show solid field knowledge and understanding of neuromorphic/deep-learning challenges.  
- Novelty: using continuous probabilities for spikes and discrete synapses is an innovative contribution.  
- Completeness: sufficient methodological detail for reproducibility.  
- Limitations include need for customized training rules and risk of overfitting.  
- Suggests improving evaluation by using more complex datasets and exploring other gradient-based or probabilistic methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical, strong MNIST results, and energy-efficiency gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: specialized audience and uncertain generalizability to other datasets/applications.  
- Reviewer recommendation: paper is a strong contribution and recommended for acceptance.
