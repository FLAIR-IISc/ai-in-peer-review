- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" method that constrains networks to match deployment on neuromorphic hardware before training.  
- Enables use of backpropagation to train networks that map directly to spiking, low-precision synapse hardware.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments on MNIST support the main claims.  
- Achieves 99.42% accuracy and 108 μJ per classification on IBM TrueNorth.  
- Provides detailed discussion of network topology, training methodology, and mapping to deployment hardware.  
- Strengths: novel, clear, well-organized, and significant contribution to neuromorphic training methods.  
- Demonstrates potential real-world applicability for low-power, scalable brain-inspired systems.  
- Limitation: requires a customized training rule, potentially limiting generalizability to other domains.  
- Limitation: evaluation focused primarily on MNIST; lacks results on more complex datasets.  
- Limitation: limited discussion of broader limitations and practical deployment challenges.  
- Recommendation: reviewer favors acceptance based on significance, clarity, and supporting results.
