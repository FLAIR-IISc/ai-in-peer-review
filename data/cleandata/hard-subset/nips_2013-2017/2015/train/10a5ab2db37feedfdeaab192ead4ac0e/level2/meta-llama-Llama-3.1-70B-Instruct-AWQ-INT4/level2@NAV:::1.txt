**Paper Review: Backpropagation for Energy-Efficient Neuromorphic Computing**

**Summary**

The paper presents a novel approach to training neural networks for energy-efficient neuromorphic computing. The authors propose a method to reconcile the incompatibility between backpropagation and neuromorphic hardware by treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities. This allows for the training of a network using standard backpropagation, which can then be mapped to neuromorphic hardware. The authors demonstrate their approach on the MNIST dataset using the TrueNorth chip, achieving state-of-the-art performance and energy efficiency.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The proposed approach can train neural networks that naturally map to neuromorphic hardware with spiking neurons and extremely low-precision synapses.
2. The approach achieves state-of-the-art performance and energy efficiency on the MNIST dataset using the TrueNorth chip.

**Support for Claims**

The authors provide a detailed description of their approach, including the learning rule and network topology. They also present experimental results on the MNIST dataset, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach. The results show that their method achieves high performance and energy efficiency, with a high-performance network achieving 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ per image, and a high-efficiency network achieving 92.7% accuracy at 0.268 μJ per image.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful, as they provide a novel approach to training neural networks for energy-efficient neuromorphic computing. The approach has the potential to be used in a wide range of applications, including mobile devices and autonomous vehicles.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper reflects common knowledge in the field of neuromorphic computing and deep learning. The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the challenges and limitations of current approaches and provide a novel solution to these problems.

**Novelty**

The paper presents a novel approach to training neural networks for energy-efficient neuromorphic computing. The authors' use of continuous probabilities to represent spikes and discrete synapses is a new and innovative idea.

**Completeness**

The paper provides sufficient details for reproducibility, including a detailed description of the learning rule and network topology.

**Limitations**

The authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach, including the need for customized training rules and the potential for overfitting.

**Constructive Criticism**

One potential area for improvement is the use of more complex datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach. Additionally, the authors could explore the use of other gradient-based learning methods or probabilistic components to further improve their approach.

**Arguments for Acceptance**

* The paper presents a novel and innovative approach to training neural networks for energy-efficient neuromorphic computing.
* The approach achieves state-of-the-art performance and energy efficiency on the MNIST dataset.
* The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful and have the potential to be used in a wide range of applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance**

* The paper may be too specialized for a general audience, as it requires a good understanding of neuromorphic computing and deep learning.
* The approach may not be easily generalizable to other datasets or applications.

Overall, I believe that the paper is a strong contribution to the field of neuromorphic computing and deep learning, and I recommend its acceptance.
