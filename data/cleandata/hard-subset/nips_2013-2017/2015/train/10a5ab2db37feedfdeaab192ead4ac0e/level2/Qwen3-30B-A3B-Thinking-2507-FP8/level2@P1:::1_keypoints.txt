- Proposes treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable standard backpropagation and map networks to neuromorphic hardware via probabilistic sampling and ensemble averaging.
- Demonstrates results on TrueNorth: 99.42% accuracy at 108 μJ/image (ensemble of 64) and 92.7% accuracy at 0.268 μJ/image (ensemble of 1).
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis (probabilistic spiking interpretation, Eqs. 2–4) and careful experimental validation.
- Advocates a "constrain-then-train" approach, contrasted with prior "train-then-constrain" methods.
- Thoughtful, data-independent network initialization for optimizing low-precision synapses (Section 3.4).
- Well-written and organized with sufficient implementation details for replication, including TrueNorth parameters.
- Thorough comparison to prior work and clear articulation of differences (combines spiking neurons, <2-bit synapses, and constrained connectivity).
- Novel contribution: first backprop-trained demonstration combining spiking, low-precision synapses, and constrained connectivity, outperforming previous spiking/binary-synapse results.
- Significant practical impact: substantial energy-efficiency improvement relevant for mobile/edge deployment (0.268 μJ vs. 6 mJ in prior work).
- Strength: clear theoretical foundation and meaningful energy–accuracy tradeoffs.
- Strength: practical hardware demonstration and strong experimental validation.
- Weakness: evaluation limited to MNIST; generalization to more complex datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10) is untested.
- Weakness: ensemble sampling increases inference complexity and computational overhead.
- Weakness: Gaussian approximation derivation needs stronger justification (input independence assumption) and scalability to larger networks or other neuromorphic architectures is not fully addressed.
- Overall recommendation: well-motivated, significant, and suitable for NIPS; recommended for acceptance.
