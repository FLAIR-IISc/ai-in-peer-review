**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper presents a novel approach to training neural networks for energy-efficient neuromorphic computing using backpropagation. The authors propose a learning rule and network topology that reconciles the incompatibility between backpropagation and neuromorphic hardware. They demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the MNIST dataset using the TrueNorth chip, achieving high accuracy and low energy consumption.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The authors build upon previous work in neuromorphic computing and deep learning, combining the strengths of both fields. They cite various papers on neuromorphic hardware, such as Neurogrid, SpiNNaker, and TrueNorth, as well as papers on deep learning, including those on backpropagation and convolutional neural networks. The authors also acknowledge previous work on training neural networks with spiking neurons and low-precision synapses, but argue that their approach is novel in its ability to train networks with both spiking neurons and discrete synapses using backpropagation.
