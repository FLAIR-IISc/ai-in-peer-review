- Core contribution: Proposes a "constrain-then-train" probabilistic framework that trains continuous [0,1] neuron outputs and synapses, enabling differentiable backpropagation and sampling to create deterministic spiking networks for neuromorphic hardware.

- Significance/impact: Tackles an important problem at the interface of deep learning and low-power neuromorphic hardware, enabling deployment of high-performance models in power-constrained embedded systems.

- Originality: Introduces an elegant, principled approach that incorporates hardware connectivity constraints during training rather than constraining post hoc.

- Technical quality/results: Strong experiments on TrueNorth with state-of-the-art SNN accuracy (99.42% on MNIST) and excellent energy efficiency (as low as 0.268 µJ per classification).

- Weakness — scope of evaluation: Demonstrated only on MNIST; scalability to harder, real-world datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet subsets) is unclear.

- Weakness — gradient approximation: Uses an approximation for gradient computation (Eq. 4) for efficiency; lacks empirical validation that this simplification does not affect performance or convergence.

- Overall recommendation: Excellent, well-executed, high-impact work; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses and strongly recommended for acceptance.
