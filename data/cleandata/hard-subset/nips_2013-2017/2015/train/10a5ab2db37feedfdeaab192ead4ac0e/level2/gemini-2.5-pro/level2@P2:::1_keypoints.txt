Core contribution: a probabilistic "constrain-then-train" method that trains continuous spike/synapse probabilities with backprop and samples hardware-deployable spiking networks.

Experimental result: Demonstrated on IBM TrueNorth with MNIST, achieving 99.42% accuracy and energy as low as 0.268 μJ/image.

Significance: Bridges high-performance backprop-trained DNNs and energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware, enabling practical low-power deployment.

Originality: Novel approach of training probabilities then sampling to bypass non-differentiability and jointly handle spiking neurons, binary synapses, and hardware connectivity.

Quality: Technically sound derivations, thoughtful weight initialization to decorrelate activity, and strong accuracy/efficiency trade-offs using ensembles.

Weakness — Gaussian approximation: Relies on CLT-based Gaussianity of summed inputs without analysis of validity in deeper layers.

Weakness — Gradient simplification: Drops a term in the gradient (assuming μ ≪ σ) without empirical validation of that assumption during training.

Weakness — Limited scope: Experiments only on MNIST; lacks discussion or evidence of scalability to more complex datasets and deeper/convolutional architectures.

Weakness — No direct comparison: Provides no experimental comparison to train-then-constrain or post-hoc quantization approaches on the same architecture.

Clarity: Well-written and structured; add a diagram for the sliding window and feature sub-selection to improve comprehension.
