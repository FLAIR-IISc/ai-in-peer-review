- Proposes training networks with backprop while constraining neuron outputs and synaptic weights to probabilities to enable direct mapping to spiking neuromorphic hardware.  
- Uses a probabilistic representation of spiking neurons and discrete synapses so standard backpropagation can be applied.  
- Demonstrated on MNIST with 99.42% (high performance) and 92.7% (high efficiency) accuracies, showing state-of-the-art energy efficiency on TrueNorth.  
- Tailors network topology to TrueNorth and provides detailed analysis of the learning rule and mapping process.  
- Builds on prior work combining backprop and spiking/binary networks but uniquely integrates probabilistic constraints and constrained connectivity with real hardware deployment.  

Strengths:
- Novel approach enabling direct deployment of backprop-trained models on neuromorphic hardware.  
- Strong empirical results and significant energy-efficiency gains on actual hardware.  
- Thorough methodological description, including topology and training details.  
- Real-world hardware demonstration on TrueNorth strengthens validity.  

Weaknesses:
- Method involves many approximations and constraints, adding complexity and potentially limiting generality.  
- Evaluation is limited to MNIST; scalability to more complex datasets is not shown.  
- Initialization procedure is complex and lacks thorough sensitivity analysis.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses a significant neuromorphic computing challenge, is novel, and includes a hardware demonstration with promising results.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Complexity may hinder adoption and evaluation is limited to a single dataset.  

Overall recommendation:
- Strong paper with significant potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address complexity, dataset scope, and initialization sensitivity.
