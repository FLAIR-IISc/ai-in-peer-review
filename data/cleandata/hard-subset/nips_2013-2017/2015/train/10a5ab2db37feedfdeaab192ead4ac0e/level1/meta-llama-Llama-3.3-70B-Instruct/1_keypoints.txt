- Proposes a novel "constrain-then-train" method to make networks directly represent deployment constraints for neuromorphic hardware like TrueNorth.  
- Enables backpropagation training for spiking neurons with extremely low-precision synapses by training within deployment constraints.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, methodology, and results.  
- Thorough literature review and good understanding of neuromorphic training challenges and limitations.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on MNIST with high accuracy and low energy consumption.  
- Provides clear, detailed explanation of network topology, training procedure, and mapping to deployment network.  
- Relies on a customized training rule, which may limit applicability to other architectures and tasks.  
- Lacks comparison to other state-of-the-art neuromorphic training methods.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset (MNIST), raising concerns about generalization to complex real-world tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong results, and useful methodological detail for the community.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential method limitations, narrow evaluation, and insufficient benchmarking against alternatives.  
- Overall recommendation: paper makes a significant contribution and is recommended for acceptance, subject to addressing comparisons and broader evaluations.
