**Paper Summary**

The paper "Backpropagation for Energy-Efficient Neuromorphic Computing" proposes a novel approach to train neural networks that can be efficiently deployed on neuromorphic hardware. The authors address the incompatibility between traditional backpropagation algorithms, which use continuous-output neurons and high-precision synapses, and neuromorphic designs, which employ spiking neurons and discrete synapses. The proposed approach treats spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities, allowing for standard backpropagation training. The trained network is then mapped to neuromorphic hardware by sampling the probabilities to create one or more networks, which are merged using ensemble averaging. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on the MNIST dataset using the TrueNorth chip, achieving state-of-the-art performance and energy efficiency.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on training neural networks with spiking neurons and low-precision synapses. However, it introduces a novel approach by using backpropagation to train a network with spiking neurons, discrete synapses, and constrained connectivity. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of related work, including previous efforts on probabilistic backpropagation approaches and neural network adaptations to hardware implementations.
