**Summary of the Paper**

This paper presents a novel approach to training neural networks for energy-efficient neuromorphic computing. The authors aim to reconcile the incompatibility between backpropagation, which requires continuous-output neurons and high-precision synapses, and neuromorphic hardware, which employs spiking neurons and discrete synapses. They propose a learning rule that treats spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities, allowing for training with standard backpropagation. The trained network is then mapped to neuromorphic hardware using ensemble averaging. The authors demonstrate their approach on the MNIST dataset using the TrueNorth chip, achieving state-of-the-art performance with high energy efficiency.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novelty**: The paper presents a new approach to training neural networks for neuromorphic hardware, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Technical Soundness**: The authors provide a thorough mathematical formulation of their approach, including the learning rule and the mapping from the training network to the deployment network.
3. **Experimental Results**: The authors demonstrate their approach on a real-world dataset (MNIST) and achieve state-of-the-art performance with high energy efficiency.
4. **Clarity**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, including the motivations and the technical details.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Limited Generalizability**: The paper focuses on a specific neuromorphic hardware (TrueNorth chip) and a specific dataset (MNIST). It is unclear how well the approach will generalize to other hardware and datasets.
2. **Computational Complexity**: The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their approach, which could be a concern for large-scale deployments.
3. **Comparison to Other Methods**: The authors do not provide a comprehensive comparison to other methods for training neural networks on neuromorphic hardware.
