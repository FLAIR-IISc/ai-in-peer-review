- Proposes treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities to enable backpropagation for neuromorphic hardware.  
- Demonstrates effective deployment on the TrueNorth chip with convincing empirical results.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy and energy efficiency for spiking neural networks.  
- Well-structured, clearly written paper with a strong introduction framing the problem and contributions.  
- Provides detailed explanations of the training network, deployment network, and mapping procedure.  
- Introduces a useful network initialization strategy to minimize covariance between neuron inputs.  
- Presents thorough results including both high-performance and high-efficiency configurations with accuracy and energy metrics.  
- Discussion contextualizes the work and suggests promising future research directions.  
- Gradient approximation (dropping the σ^3_j term) is under-justified and needs more rigorous analysis or empirical validation.  
- Lacks sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters like learning rate, momentum, and dropout.  
- Network topology choices (R, F, S) are not comprehensively justified or compared to alternatives.  
- The role and benefit of the “pyramid” shape in network performance are not fully explained.  
- Generalizability beyond MNIST is uncertain; no experiments on more complex datasets are provided.  
- Reliance on TrueNorth may limit applicability; discussion of adapting to other neuromorphic platforms is brief.  
- References are comprehensive, but missing citations to event-driven spiking convolutional work (e.g., SpikingJelly-related literature).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel probabilistic approach, strong TrueNorth results, clarity, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification for gradient approximation, unclear parameter selection rationale, and limited evidence of generalizability.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions addressing approximation justification, topology parameter choices, and broader applicability.
