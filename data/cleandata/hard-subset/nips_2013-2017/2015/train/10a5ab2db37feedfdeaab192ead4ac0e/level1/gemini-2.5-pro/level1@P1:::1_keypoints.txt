- Presents a probabilistic "constrain-then-train" method to train networks with spiking neurons and binary synapses for neuromorphic hardware (IBM TrueNorth).  
- Represents synapses and neuron outputs as continuous probabilities during training to enable differentiable backpropagation.  
- Approximates neuron activation probability using the complementary CDF of a Gaussian modeling summed Bernoulli inputs.  
- Deploys to hardware by sampling learned probabilities for binary connectivity and converting inputs to probabilistic spike trains; ensembles improve performance.  
- Introduces an a priori synaptic initialization to decorrelate neuron activity, reducing learning to finding binary connectivity patterns.  
- Validated on real TrueNorth hardware with MNIST, achieving 99.42% accuracy for spiking networks and very low energy per image (0.268 µJ).  
- Theoretical motivation and derivation of backpropagation updates are well-founded and clearly presented.  
- Major strength: experimental validation on actual neuromorphic hardware with measured energy-accuracy trade-offs.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly distinguishes training vs deployment networks, and provides reproducible implementation details.  
- Highly original: first unified solution addressing spiking neurons, binary shared synapses, and constrained connectivity within a backprop framework.  
- Significant impact: bridges deep learning and ultra-low-power neuromorphic engineering, enabling complex models in power-constrained devices.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel method, strong significance, excellent results on hardware, and clear presentation.  
- Argument against acceptance: experimental validation limited to MNIST; broader datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10) would strengthen claims.  
- Argument against acceptance: gradient approximation (Equation 4) is justified heuristically without formal analysis or empirical comparison to the full gradient.  
- Overall assessment: strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses, making the work impactful and suitable for publication.
