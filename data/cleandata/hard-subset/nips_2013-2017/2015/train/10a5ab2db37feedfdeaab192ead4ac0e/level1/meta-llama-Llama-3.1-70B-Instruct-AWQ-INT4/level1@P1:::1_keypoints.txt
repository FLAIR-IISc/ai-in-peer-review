- Summary: Proposes training neural nets by treating spikes and discrete synapses as probabilities for backpropagation, then maps networks to neuromorphic hardware via ensemble averaging, demonstrated on MNIST with TrueNorth.

- Novelty: Introduces a new training approach reconciling backpropagation with spiking, discrete-synapse neuromorphic hardware.

- Technical soundness: Provides a thorough mathematical formulation of the learning rule and mapping procedure.

- Experimental results: Demonstrates state-of-the-art MNIST performance on TrueNorth with high energy efficiency.

- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear motivations and technical explanations.

- Limited generalizability: Evaluation is confined to TrueNorth and MNIST, raising questions about broader applicability.

- Computational complexity: Lacks a detailed analysis of computational cost for large-scale deployment.

- Comparison to other methods: Does not present a comprehensive comparison against alternative neuromorphic training methods.

- Originality: Effectively combines deep learning and neuromorphic techniques to produce a novel contribution.

- Significance: Relevant for enabling energy-efficient deployment of neural networks on low-power devices across applications.

- Pro accept: Novel approach, strong real-world results, and meaningful implications for energy-efficient neuromorphic systems.

- Con accept: Limited hardware/dataset scope, missing complexity analysis, and insufficient method comparisons.

- Recommendation: Accept pending minor revisions to include computational complexity analysis and broader comparisons to other methods.
