- Proposes a novel method to train deep networks with backprop while accounting for neuromorphic constraints by modeling spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities.
- Trained probabilistic networks are mapped to neuromorphic hardware by sampling to create networks and merging them via ensemble averaging.
- Demonstrates practical deployment on the TrueNorth chip, showing real-world applicability.
- Reports strong results: 99.42% accuracy (high-performance network) and 92.7% accuracy (high-efficiency network) on MNIST.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses helpful figures and tables.
- Some mathematical derivations in the paper could be explained more clearly.
- Main strength is the originality and strong motivation for bridging backprop and neuromorphic hardware.
- Evaluation is limited to MNIST, a relatively simple dataset, so generalization to complex tasks is untested.
- Lacks detailed information on computational resources required for training and deployment.
- Related work coverage is good, but comparisons to existing spiking deep neural network methods could be deeper.
- Potentially significant impact across tasks like object recognition and NLP, but applications and limitations need more detail.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution deserving acceptance, with caveats about evaluation breadth and comparative/detail reporting.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8.5/10.
