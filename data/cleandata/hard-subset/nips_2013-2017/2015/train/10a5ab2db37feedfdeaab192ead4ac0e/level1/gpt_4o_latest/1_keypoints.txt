- Proposes a "constrain-then-train" method treating spikes and discrete synapses as continuous probabilities for backpropagation, then sampling to map to TrueNorth.
- Reports state-of-the-art MNIST accuracy (99.42%) and ultra-low energy use (0.268 µJ/classification) for two network configurations.
- Introduces a progressive mixing topology to enforce constrained connectivity compatible with neuromorphic hardware.
- Technical soundness: robust theoretical analysis, detailed equations, training/deployment procedures, and reproducible experiments.
- Originality: novel probabilistic treatment that reconciles backpropagation with neuromorphic deployment.
- Significance: sets new benchmarks for spiking network accuracy and energy efficiency.
- Clarity: well-organized, clearly written, with comparisons to prior work.
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to MNIST, leaving generalizability to complex tasks untested.
- Weakness: Demonstrated only on TrueNorth; empirical validation on other neuromorphic platforms is missing.
- Weakness: Training method adds complexity beyond standard backprop, possibly impeding adoption.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a timely problem with novel methodology and strong theoretical/experimental support.
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow evaluation scope and hardware dependency raise concerns about scalability and wider applicability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—authors should expand evaluation and discuss extensions to other datasets and hardware.
