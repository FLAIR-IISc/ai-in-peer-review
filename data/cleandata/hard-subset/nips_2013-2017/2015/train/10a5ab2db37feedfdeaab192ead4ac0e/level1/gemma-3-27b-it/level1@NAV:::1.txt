## Review of “Backpropagation for Energy-Efficient Neuromorphic Computing”

This paper addresses the critical challenge of bridging the gap between the performance of deep learning with backpropagation and the energy efficiency of neuromorphic computing. The authors propose a method to train networks using backpropagation while constraining neuron outputs and synaptic weights to be probabilities, facilitating a natural mapping onto spiking neuromorphic hardware like TrueNorth. This is achieved through a probabilistic representation of spiking neurons and discrete synapses, allowing for standard backpropagation to be employed. The approach is demonstrated on MNIST, achieving competitive accuracy with significantly improved energy efficiency compared to existing methods on TrueNorth.

The work builds upon prior research attempting to combine backpropagation with spiking networks (e.g., [9,10,11,12]) and probabilistic approaches to training binary networks [14,15]. However, it uniquely combines these elements with constrained connectivity and demonstrates real-time operation on actual neuromorphic hardware. The authors carefully address network topology to match TrueNorth’s architecture and provide a detailed analysis of the learning rule and mapping process. The results are compelling, achieving state-of-the-art energy efficiency for digit recognition.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The method of training a network with probabilistic constraints for direct deployment on neuromorphic hardware is a valuable contribution.
* **Strong Results:** Achieving 99.42% accuracy with high performance and 92.7% with high efficiency on MNIST, and demonstrating significantly improved energy efficiency on TrueNorth, are impressive.
* **Thoroughness:** The paper provides a detailed explanation of the methodology, network topology, and training procedure.
* **Hardware Demonstration:** Validating the approach on TrueNorth is a significant strength, moving beyond simulations.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity:** The method involves a number of approximations and constraints which, while necessary, add complexity and potentially limit generality. 
* **Limited Dataset:**  The evaluation is currently limited to MNIST. Demonstrating scalability to more complex datasets would strengthen the findings.
* **Initialization Sensitivity:** The initialization procedure (Section 3.4) is quite involved.  A more thorough analysis of the sensitivity of performance to different initialization schemes would be beneficial.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant challenge in the field of neuromorphic computing.
* The approach is novel and demonstrates promising results.
* The real-world hardware demonstration is a key strength.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The complexity of the method may limit its widespread adoption.
* The evaluation is limited to a single dataset.



**Overall:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential. The proposed method offers a promising path towards deploying powerful deep learning models on energy-efficient neuromorphic hardware. While the complexity and limited dataset evaluation are concerns, the novelty and strong results on TrueNorth make this a worthwhile contribution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the points mentioned in the weaknesses section.
