- Trains RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse systems (swimming, flying, 3D biped and quadruped locomotion).  
- Core method: intertwine supervised learning with trajectory optimization (CIO), inject noise during training, and derive optimal feedback gains with actions.  
- Avoids motion capture, task-specific features, and state machines, providing a unified control framework.  
- Extends prior neural-control and RL work to higher-dimensional, realistic 3D locomotion previously limited to 2D.  
- Explicitly addresses data challenges for controllers and discusses i.i.d. vs. closed-loop data and robust control links.  
- Technically sound with a well-defined optimization, justified noise injection, and proximal regularization.  
- Novel integration of trajectory optimization, neural networks, and feedback gains as a significant contribution.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and includes distributed training details enhancing reproducibility.  
- Demonstrates significant, realistic behaviors with applications in robotics, animation, and biomechanics; produces more natural cyclic behaviors compared to MPC.  
- High computational cost and reliance on cloud resources make the approach less accessible.  
- Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and robustness to parameter changes.  
- Comparisons to direct neural-network training methods are brief; broader comparative experiments would strengthen the paper.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance based on novelty, clarity, technical soundness, and impact.  
- Minor concerns (compute cost, comparative breadth) do not outweigh contributions; reviewer confidence 9/10.
