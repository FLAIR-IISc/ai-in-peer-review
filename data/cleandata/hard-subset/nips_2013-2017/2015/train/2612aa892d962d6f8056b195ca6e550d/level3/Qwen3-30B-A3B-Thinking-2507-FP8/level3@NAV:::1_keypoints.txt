- Proposes training neural-network controllers by embedding optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization and softly coupling optimization with policy learning via a deviation-cost penalty, yielding robust, real-time control.  
- Uses perturbation methods to compute optimal feedback gains during trajectory optimization and embeds them into the neural policy.  
- Method is clearly and cleanly presented with excellent technical organization.  
- Demonstrates a single architecture working across diverse systems (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without task-specific features or state machines.  
- Experiments are thorough and technically sound, providing strong empirical validation.  
- Ablation studies (joint optimization vs static training and noise injection) convincingly support the methodological claims.  
- Distributed training and implementation details are well-justified and effectively address scalability.  
- Original contribution: novel integration of trajectory optimization, neural networks, and control theory, improving robustness and enabling 3D locomotion beyond prior 2D-limited work.  
- High significance: offers a unified framework that can reduce the need for specialized controllers across robot morphologies.  
- Minor limitation: the paper does not deeply explore theoretical guarantees of the optimization procedure, though the practical focus is understandable.
