- Proposes a novel SGD-based method that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters of decision trees.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss and optimizes it with SGD to learn deep trees efficiently.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Experiments show improved performance over greedy baselines on multiple benchmark datasets.  
- Notation around lines 226–240 is unclear and needs clarification.  
- The term "softer feedback gains" is not well-defined and requires explanation.  
- More details needed on computational requirements and scalability of the approach.  
- Robustness to environmental variability is not analyzed, especially relative to recent work (e.g., Abbeel & Levine).  
- Significance: potential to advance state-of-the-art in decision tree learning for tasks requiring deep trees (e.g., CV, NLP).  
- Quality: technically sound formulation with claims supported by experiments.  
- Originality: high — distinct approach leveraging SGD and structured prediction.  
- Overall: significant contribution that could be stronger with clarifications and additional comparisons.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel, well-motivated method; empirical gains over baselines; clear theoretical connection to structured prediction; potential field impact.  
- Con-acceptance: unclear notation and terminology; insufficient computational/robustness analysis; lacks comparisons to recent related work.
