- Proposes a novel non-greedy algorithm that jointly optimizes split functions and leaf parameters with a global objective.  
- Establishes a link between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables and formulates a convex-concave upper bound on empirical loss.  
- Paper is well-written and the mathematical derivations are clear.  
- Experiments show the non-greedy trees outperform greedy decision tree baselines on several classification benchmarks.  
- Strengths: novelty of approach, clarity of formulation, and practical effectiveness.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of computational complexity and demonstrates scalability.  
- Includes a comprehensive related-work review and clearly highlights contributions.  
- Weakness: comparative evaluation is limited—only a few greedy baselines compared.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed insights into hyperparameter choices (regularization ν, learning rate η).  
- Recommendation: accept, but suggest more thorough comparison with state-of-the-art methods and more hyperparameter analysis.
