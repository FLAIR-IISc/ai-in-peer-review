- Presents a novel method combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization (CIO) to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers.
- Introduces a perturbation method to compute optimal feedback gains that stabilize optimal trajectories.
- Demonstrates stable, realistic behaviors across multiple characters and tasks (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped locomotion) and generalization to new tasks/environments.
- Uses distributed training and a custom GPU SGD implementation for efficient training.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of method and components.
- Experimental results are impressive and show complex task performance.
- Claim that their approach produces "softer" feedback gains via parameter λ is not clearly justified or defined.
- Lacks clear comparison to closely related prior work (e.g., Abbeel and Levine).
- Notation around lines 226–240 is confusing; visually similar symbols should be disambiguated.
- Discussion of why LQG was not used should be moved to follow presentation of the proposed strategy for clarity.
- Originality is limited by building on existing trajectory optimization and neural network techniques, though the perturbation method is a novel contribution.
- Significance is high due to potential impact on control, robotics, animation, and gaming.
- Recommendation: accept contingent on addressing notation issues and strengthening comparison to prior work and discussion of novelty/significance.
