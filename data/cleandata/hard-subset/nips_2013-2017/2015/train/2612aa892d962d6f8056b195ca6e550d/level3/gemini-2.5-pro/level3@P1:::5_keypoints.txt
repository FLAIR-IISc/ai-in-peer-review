- Proposes training a single recurrent neural network controller from CIO-generated trajectories to control diverse 3D characters in real time.  
- Key technical contributions: joint optimization interleaving trajectory optimization and network training, sensory noise injection for robustness, and extracting optimal feedback gains from the CIO solver.  
- Demonstrates success on challenging 3D locomotion tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal and quadrupedal walking) across diverse morphologies.  
- Algorithm alternates between optimizing trajectories and regressing the network policy; this design is well-motivated and technically sound.  
- Experimental results and supplementary video convincingly show stable, realistic, and interactive controllers.  
- Ablation study strongly supports the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection; naive training fails catastrophically.  
- The method for computing feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer is clever, principled, and computationally efficient (avoids separate LQR pass).  
- Originality: extends prior work on learning from optimizers with a novel feedback-gain extraction and application to more complex 3D characters.  
- Significance: represents an important step toward automatic synthesis of robust controllers for arbitrary character morphologies.  
- Major limitation: relies on a complete and accurate dynamics/contact model (CIO), making direct real-world robotics application challenging.  
- Clarity issues: paper organization is imbalanced—Section 4 (policy regression) is too brief while implementation details (Section 6) are overly long.  
- Critical omission: lack of detail on the recurrent network—how rt and ṙt are computed/updated is unclear and harms reproducibility.  
- Terminology ambiguity: calling the model a "stochastic neural network" is misleading—policy is deterministic at execution, trained with stochastic perturbations.  
- Recommendations: expand Section 4 to fully describe the recurrent architecture and integration of rt/ṙt.  
- Recommendation: condense implementation/cloud details and reallocate space to network/model description.  
- Recommendation: clarify language to state the network is trained with stochasticity for robustness, not inherently stochastic at runtime.  
- Recommendation: explicitly acknowledge the dependency on an accurate dynamics model as a limitation in the introduction or discussion.  
- Overall assessment: promising, significant work with strong empirical results but requires restructuring and clarifications (especially on recurrence and model assumptions) before acceptance.
