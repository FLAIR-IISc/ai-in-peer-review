- Presents an elegant practical method to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for contact-rich systems by alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) and neural regression.
- Core idea: teach local feedback by embedding analytically computed feedback gains into supervised training targets so the policy learns corrective responses.
- Network outputs desired velocities, contact forces, and recurrent-state updates, and runs real-time with a one-step constrained optimization enforcing dynamics/contacts.
- Key technical ingredients: perturbation-based well-conditioned feedback gain computation reusing CIO quantities.
- Key technical ingredients: noise injection on sensor and hidden states with gain-corrected training targets.
- Key technical ingredients: soft coupling between trajectory optimizer and policy via a deviation penalty.
- Key technical ingredients: distributed asynchronous training with proximal regularization for stability.
- Demonstrated stable, realistic control across diverse morphologies/tasks (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with a single architecture.
- Advances prior work by operating in full 3D with stable cyclic gaits and efficiently computing local gains within a direct trajectory-optimization pipeline.
- Joint trajectory-policy optimization regularizes behavior toward predictable, non-chaotic motions compared to pure MPC.
- Complements model-free RL and classic optimal control, reducing need for hand-designed state machines or motion capture.
- Methodologically sound: insightful, efficient gain derivation (soft-constraint perturbation + Woodbury) reusing Gauss-Newton Hessian inverse.
- Training scheme (stochastic proximal alternating) is reasonable for large nonconvex problems; ablations validate necessity of joint optimization and noise.
- One-step constrained execution formulation is a robust alternative to explicit integration.
- Empirical results are diverse and compelling, though some quantitative evaluations (success rates, robustness, sensitivity to λ and α) are limited.
- Paper is exceptionally well written; algorithmic components and engineering choices are clearly explained.
- Minor clarity suggestions: add a schematic of data flow, discuss learned recurrent-state function, clarify noise units/scales, and stability for spectral radius > 1 initialization.
- Original contributions: embedding analytically computed local feedback gains into training targets and efficient gain computation within CIO.
- Soft coupling of trajectory and policy in a distributed stochastic setting with task-branching is a thoughtful advance over prior imitation-from-optimizer methods.
- Significant: enables a single robust controller across morphologies without MoCap or hand-designed controllers; likely influential for learning-in-the-loop trajectory optimization.
- Strengths summarized: principled gain integration, joint optimization regularization, diverse 3D empirical validation, scalable training, clear presentation.
- Weaknesses summarized: limited quantitative baselines, fidelity concerns with soft constraints and root forces, light sensitivity analysis, limited ablation on recurrent state, and non-rigorous compute/sample-efficiency comparisons.
- Suggested improvements: add head-to-head quantitative baselines, report robustness metrics and sensitivity plots, ablate embedded gains, analyze recurrent state, and clarify root-force handling for sim-to-real.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, broad demonstrations, clear evidence for joint/noise/gain benefits, and high relevance to ML/control/graphics.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient quantitative baselines and potential real-world deployment issues from soft constraint/action parameterization.
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — substantial, well-validated contribution unifying trajectory optimization and neural policy learning for robust, real-time control.
