- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers using trajectory optimization (CIO), noise injection, and task-spec adaptation, demonstrating stable 3D behaviors across swimming, flying, and walking with varied morphologies.  
- Uses trajectory optimization to generate training data (mimicking optimal actions), extending prior RL and stochastic optimal control approaches to high-dimensional continuous spaces.  
- Situates work within controller-design literature, contrasting with motion-capture/state-machine approaches and citing relevant prior trajectory-optimization and neural-control methods.  

Strengths:
- Strong empirical results across diverse, complex locomotion tasks with compelling video evidence.  
- Novel and effective combination of trajectory optimization with neural network training, including CIO, noise injection, and task-spec robustness.  
- Demonstrates scalability via a well-described distributed training architecture.  
- Clearly defines the problem and challenges of learning controllers in high-dimensional spaces.  

Weaknesses:
- Method requires a full dynamical model of the character (Eq. 6), a significant limitation that is insufficiently discussed.  
- Imbalanced section coverage: Sections 4 and 6 are overly detailed while the overview is shallow; the role of recurrent units is not adequately explained.  
- Confusing characterization of the policy as “stochastic” — stochasticity is introduced during training, but the learned policy appears deterministic.  
- Ablation study is limited; more exploration of noise levels, architectures, and optimization parameters is needed.  
- Lacks direct comparative analysis with Model Predictive Control and discussion of trade-offs.  

Overall assessment:
- Compelling and technically sound approach with impressive empirical results, but clarity and applicability limitations need addressing.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on complex locomotion; novel/effective methodology; addresses scaling challenges in deep learning for control.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Reliance on an accurate dynamical model limits practical applicability; insufficient detail on recurrent components and unclear presentation aspects.  

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions: clarify the role of recurrent units, the meaning of “stochastic” policy, discuss model-knowledge limitations, rebalance section depth, and improve presentation flow.
