- Proposes jointly learning optimal trajectories and a neural policy, decoupled into alternating trajectory optimization and supervised policy regression subproblems.
- Builds on prior trajectory-optimization-plus-function-approximation work and demonstrates effectiveness on diverse, challenging 3D characters and locomotion tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal walking).
- Technical approach is sophisticated and well-conceived, enabling reuse of existing methods.
- Empirical results are impressive and compelling (supplementary video), showing strong control over complex morphologies.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with thorough ablative experiments that support the benefits of joint optimization and training with sensory noise.
- Main concern: learned policy is not executed directly but used as a target for per-timestep optimization—motivation and necessity for this extra step need clarification.
- Questions whether the per-timestep optimization is compensating for policy regression errors or residual offline optimization costs, and if the dynamics model alone wouldn’t prevent implausible states.
- Positive note that the approach permits using larger, more practical timesteps.
- Minor issues: typo on line 89 ("to unity") and strengthen the claim on line 153 by citing specific recent approaches/references.
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with excellent technical execution and thorough validation that advances the state of the art, though reviewer is not a domain expert on novelty/significance.
