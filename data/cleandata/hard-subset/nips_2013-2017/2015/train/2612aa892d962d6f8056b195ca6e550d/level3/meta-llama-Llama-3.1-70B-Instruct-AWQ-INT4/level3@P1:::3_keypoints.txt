- Proposes a novel real-time control algorithm combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with a neural network (NN) for physically consistent interactive character control.  
- Main technical contribution: alternating joint training of CIO and NN plus noise injection (data augmentation and additive hidden noise) to improve robustness and generalization.  
- Demonstrated on diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with realistic and stable behaviors.  
- Experimental results indicate the network produces effective control policies, supporting the method’s potential.  
- Training set is small, weakening claims about generalization and raising overfitting concerns for baselines.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief MPC comparison and missing systematic comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Mathematical presentation is mostly clear but lacks definitions/detail in parts (CIO step, Eq. 6), forcing readers to consult external references.  
- Section 3.2 is confusing due to notational issues, making the optimal-action-for-noisy-inputs procedure unclear.  
- Paper should provide code to aid reproducibility and broader adoption.  
- Runtime, timing measurements, and scaling behavior with model complexity are missing but important for real-time claims.  
- Terminology should distinguish “data augmentation” from “noise injection” and re-evaluate the denoising-autoencoder analogy.  
- Some algorithmic choices are unclear (e.g., computing initial X using random initial weights in Alg. 1; using same hyperparameter eta in both steps).  
- Asynchronous joint optimization described in Sec. 6 differs substantially from the alternating scheme in Alg. 1 and should be clarified/connected earlier.  
- Experimental ablations are insufficiently granular; separate ablations for “no data augmentation” and “no hidden noise injection” are recommended.  
- Open question: whether CIO regularization alpha can be annealed to zero and how that affects learning and final policy.  
- Minor writing and notation issues (typos, missing articles) need fixing for clarity.  
- Overall assessment: promising and well-executed application of NNs to character control, but needs broader comparisons, clarifications, additional experiments, and reproducibility artifacts.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the above issues.
