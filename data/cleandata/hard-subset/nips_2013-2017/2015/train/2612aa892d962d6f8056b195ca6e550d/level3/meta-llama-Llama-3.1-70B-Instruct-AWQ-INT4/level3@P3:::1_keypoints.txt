- Proposes a novel method training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse complex characters.  
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, noise injection during training, and training for unexpected task changes.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across multiple dynamical systems and tasks (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) and varied morphologies.  
- Main contribution: a universal synthesis method applicable to arbitrary behaviors, morphologies, online objective changes, noise, and modeling errors.  
- Uses a neural network to learn from an optimizer and reproduce similar behaviors online.  
- Produces stable, realistic behaviors without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, high-quality, and technically impressive.  
- Thorough explanation of neural network architecture, trajectory optimization, and distributed training setup.  
- Results are inspiring, well-presented, and accessible to a broad readership.  
- Strong integration of graphics, control, and neural networks across varied tasks and morphologies.  
- Detailed analysis of noise injection, trajectory optimization, and neural network regression.  
- Thorough evaluation including comparisons to MPC and naive neural network training showing superior stability and realism.  
- Original and well-referenced contribution with potential for wide applicability.  
- Main weakness: approach complexity, requiring substantial computation and expertise in NN and trajectory optimization.  
- Significance: high potential impact in neural network control and trajectory optimization.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add details on complexity/expertise required and potential applications.
