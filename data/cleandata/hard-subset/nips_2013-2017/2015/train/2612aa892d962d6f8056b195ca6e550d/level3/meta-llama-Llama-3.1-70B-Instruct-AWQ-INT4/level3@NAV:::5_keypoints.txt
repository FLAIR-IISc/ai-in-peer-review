- Novel approach: trains recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for complex characters.  
- Method: combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, noise injection during training, and uses optimizer for feedback gains.  
- Empirical validation: demonstrated on multiple 3D characters (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped walking).  
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written with clear explanations, helpful figures/tables, and thorough related work.  
- Background gap: assumes reader familiarity with neural networks and trajectory optimization; more background needed for non-experts.  
- Implementation details lacking: missing specifics on network architecture, number of hidden layers, and activation functions.  
- Analysis weakness: needs more evaluation of controller performance across scenarios, effect of noise injection, and comparisons to other methods.  
- Model assumption concern: assumes full model knowledge (Eq.6); should discuss practicality and ways to relax this assumption.  
- Overall contribution: meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance; contributes to neural network control and trajectory optimization.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novel method, effective combination of techniques, strong demonstrations, meaningful contribution.  
- Against-accept arguments: reliance on full model knowledge, insufficient implementation details, and limited analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add background, implementation specifics, and deeper analysis.
