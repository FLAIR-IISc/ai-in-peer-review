- Trains a small recurrent network as a near-optimal feedback controller that outputs desired velocities, recurrent updates, and contact forces for simulated characters.
- Alternates CIO-based trajectory optimization to produce demonstrations and local gains with supervised regression to fit the RNN to noisy, feedback-augmented trajectories.
- Uses input/hidden-unit noise, randomized task goals, and asynchronous distributed training; runs at test time via a T=1 constrained optimization loop.
- Claims novelty in using a single architecture across multiple 3D morphologies, a perturbation-based method to extract feedback gains, and showing noise/joint optimization reduce covariate shift.
- Strength: Clear integration of CIO with neural regression and sensible handling of covariate shift through noisy inputs and linear feedback targets.
- Strength: Perturbation-based gain computation reuses optimizer linear systems, avoiding a separate LQG pass and offering computational benefits.
- Strength: Demonstrates diverse qualitative results (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) with one training pipeline.
- Strength: Practical distributed training and real-time execution via simple T=1 policy optimization.
- Strength: Ablations indicate joint optimization and noise injection substantially improve performance in at least one task.
- Weakness: Lacks quantitative comparisons to closest prior work ([9],[11]) on metrics like training time, sample counts, success rates, energy, and robustness.
- Weakness: Limited baselines—MPC comparison is insufficient; request to add direct comparisons to [9],[11] and possibly TRPO on same tasks and metrics.
- Weakness: Claims of “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” are unquantified—need gain norms, singular spectra, and condition numbers versus LQG.
- Weakness: “Softer gains” is undefined; ask for precise definition (spectral norm, condition number, temporal smoothness) and sensitivity/robustness analysis showing benefits.
- Weakness: Suggests reordering exposition to present the perturbation-based strategy before critiquing LQG for better flow.
- Weakness: Notation in Sec. 3.2 is confusing—recommend clearer symbols for nominal and perturbed trajectories and a derivation appendix.
- Weakness: Ambiguous policy/action interpretation—clarify whether the network is a control policy or modeling dynamics under an implicit policy.
- Weakness: Mostly qualitative results—request standardized quantitative metrics per domain with confidence intervals and robustness tests.
- Weakness: Dynamics enforcement is soft and may use root forces—discuss physical plausibility and behavior under stricter constraints.
- Weakness: Need evaluation of generalization to unseen goals, sudden goal changes, and sensor/model noise at test time with robustness curves.
- Weakness: Missing implementation details—report T=1 optimization timings, end-to-end query time, network size, control rate, and recurrent state dimensionality effects.
- Evaluation: Technical components are sound and gain computation is a neat contribution, but empirical validation lacks strong quantitative baselines.
- Clarity: Generally clear, but feedback-gain section and notation need revision; reordering LQG critique would help.
- Originality: Incremental over prior trajectory-optimization+supervised-learning work; gain computation and breadth of demonstrations are most original.
- Significance: Potentially significant for real-time controllers across morphologies, but needs stronger quantitative comparisons to demonstrate impact.
- For acceptance: Practical scalable method, useful gain-extraction technique, solid engineering and ablations.
- Against acceptance: Insufficient quantitative comparison to closest prior work, limited metrics/robustness analysis, and some unclear/unquantified claims.
- Overall: Promising application with a useful technical twist; currently borderline accept pending clearer positioning and rigorous quantitative experiments.
