- Proposes training RNN controllers by combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization to produce near-optimal feedback policies.  
- Introduces a neural policy mapping sensory state to optimal actions learned from optimizer-generated trajectories.  
- Injects noise into sensory inputs during training to prevent divergence at execution and uses a quadratic penalty (weight α) to relax constraints.  
- Uses block-alternating (distributed stochastic) optimization to solve the unconstrained problem by alternating variable blocks.  
- Computes optimal feedback gains as a byproduct of trajectory optimization and uses a perturbation method that reuses direct-optimization computation.  
- Demonstrates the method on diverse tasks and morphologies: swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking.  
- Shows learned controllers are robust, predictable, and compare favorably to alternatives like MPC in experiments.  
- Technical contributions include optimal feedback gains, the perturbation reuse method, and the combined supervised–trajectory-optimization training pipeline.  
- Strengths: technically impressive, clearly written, well-organized, and effectively integrates graphics, control, and neural nets across varied morphologies.  
- Weakness: potential reproducibility issues due to required computational resources and expertise.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed empirical comparison to other approaches (e.g., MPC).  
- Originality: introduces novel elements in feedback gain computation and supervised + trajectory optimization combination.  
- Significance: meaningful methodological contribution with broad applications in robotics, animation, and gaming.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; add computational-complexity analysis and stronger comparisons to other methods.  
- Rating: 9/10.
