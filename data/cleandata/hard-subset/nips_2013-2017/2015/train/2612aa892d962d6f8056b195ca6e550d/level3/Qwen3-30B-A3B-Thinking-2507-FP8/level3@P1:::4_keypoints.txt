- Presents RNN controllers trained by combining trajectory optimization with neural network policy learning for diverse characters (swimmer, bird, biped, quadruped).  
- Uses a distributed stochastic optimization framework that alternates Contact-Invariant Optimization and neural network regression with noise injection for robustness.  
- Enables interactive real-time control without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Experiments are technically sophisticated and well-executed across multiple 3D morphologies.  
- Same network architecture yields natural, stable locomotion across very different bodies.  
- Ablation studies show joint optimization with noise injection vastly outperforms naive training on static trajectories.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and largely reproducible; supplementary video effectively demonstrates results.  
- References related work appropriately but should more explicitly contrast with recent RL/2D approaches (e.g., [9,11]).  
- Quality is high: technically sound, strong experimental validation, and practical distributed/cloud training architecture.  
- Originality lies in the specific integration and distributed stochastic optimization; components are familiar but their combination is a meaningful advance.  
- Significance is substantial for character animation and robotics, reducing manual effort for complex behaviors.  
- Main weakness: insufficient justification for the trajectory re-optimization step—unclear why the learned policy alone cannot produce physically plausible trajectories.  
- Scalability concerns: unclear how method and distributed training scale to much larger/complex problems; lacks quantitative compute-cost comparisons to support claims.  
- Minor issues: typo at line 89, vague “recent approaches” reference at line 153, and unclear motivation for specific hyperparameters (σε = 10^-2, λ = 10^2).
