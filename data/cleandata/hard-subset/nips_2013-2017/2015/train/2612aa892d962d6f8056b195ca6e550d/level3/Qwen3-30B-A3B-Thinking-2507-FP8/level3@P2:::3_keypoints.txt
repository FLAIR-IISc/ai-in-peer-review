- Proposes an alternating optimization to train recurrent nets as near‑optimal feedback controllers using CIO-generated trajectories and noise injection.  
- Enables real-time interactive control for swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal characters with a single network architecture.  
- Integrates trajectory optimization (CIO) with neural networks and demonstrates 3D locomotion beyond prior 2D results.  
- Technical approach is well-motivated and shows clear empirical success across diverse character types.  
- Alternating optimization and noise injection effectively mitigate overfitting.  
- Training set is small (100–200 trials, 5 branched segments), weakening generalization claims.  
- Comparative evaluation is inadequate: only a naive static baseline tested and MPC comparison lacks detail/citation.  
- Ablation study is insufficiently granular (doesn’t separate sensory noise from hidden-layer noise).  
- Exposition problems: confusing notation in Section 3.2, unclear perturbation method, and poor linkage between asynchronous training and Algorithm 1.  
- Numerous minor writing/math issues requiring external references for full understanding.  
- Combining known components (CIO, nets, noise) is novel in this specific implementation and meaningful.  
- Practical concerns unaddressed: runtime, scalability with model complexity, and incorporation of orientation targets.  
- Lacks thorough comparisons to established real‑time controllers and direct RL baselines.  
- Overall: promising and meaningful contribution but experimental rigor, clarity, and practical evaluation need substantial improvement.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — merits revision and stronger empirical/technical presentation before acceptance.
