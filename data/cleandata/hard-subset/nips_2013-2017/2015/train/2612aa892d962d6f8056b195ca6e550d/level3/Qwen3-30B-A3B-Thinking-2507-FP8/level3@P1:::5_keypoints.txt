- Presents CIO: interleaved supervised learning and trajectory optimization to train neural controllers for diverse 3D characters without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Core innovation: joint optimization of trajectories and a neural policy with stochastic noise injection and optimal feedback gains so the network learns implicit dynamics.  
- Empirical strength: achieves stable 3D locomotion across varied morphologies (birds, swimmers, quadrupeds) using a single architecture.  
- Ablations show joint optimization and noise injection are critical for robustness; static or noise-free training performs much worse or overfits.  
- Unified architecture across character types avoids task-specific state machines and is a compelling practical contribution.  
- CIO trajectory optimization enables complex behaviors without manual initialization, and the distributed training framework scales efficiently.  
- Major weakness: requires exact physical dynamics (H, C, etc.), limiting applicability to real-world robotics where dynamics are unknown or approximate.  
- Paper omits discussion of deployment on physical systems or whether dynamics can be learned, a serious oversight for applicability.  
- Technical inconsistencies: recurrent units referenced in abstract/intro are not described, undermining clarity of the NN policy.  
- Misleading terminology: policy called "stochastic" despite deterministic policy with stochastic training data—needs clarification.  
- Structural imbalance: Section on NN policy is short and lacks key implementation details, while training/infrastructure receive disproportionate space.  
- Claim of universality is overstated given dynamics dependency and need for task-specific cost functions; controller generalization to novel tasks is not shown.  
- Contribution: meaningful extension of trajectory optimization to 3D with novel joint optimization and noise strategies, high significance for simulation/animation but limited real-robot impact.  
- Recommendation: technically strong but borderline; critical model-dependency and missing technical details warrant rejection unless addressed in revision.
