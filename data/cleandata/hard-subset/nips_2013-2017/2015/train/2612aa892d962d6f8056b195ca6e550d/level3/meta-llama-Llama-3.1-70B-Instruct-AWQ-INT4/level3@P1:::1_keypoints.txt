- Proposes a novel method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems and tasks.
- Method combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, noise injection, and training for unexpected task changes.
- Demonstrated applicability across varied body morphologies and behaviors, including online objective changes and perturbations.
- Learns stable, realistic behaviors without motion capture data, task-specific features, or state machines.
- Strong technical quality with clear mathematical formulations and algorithmic details.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized.
- Impressive empirical results on swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped locomotion tasks.
- Uses sophisticated parallelization to enable efficient training at scale.
- Effective integration of graphics, control, and neural networks across morphologies.
- Shows learning of simple, robust approximations of optimal trajectories.
- Major limitation: requires large computational resources.
- Insufficient detailed analysis of robustness to different disturbances and modeling errors.
- Lacks detailed comparisons with other existing methods, limiting impact assessment.
- Overall: significant, original, and potentially widely applicable contribution.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clarity, strong results, and broad applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: high compute needs, insufficient robustness analysis, and missing comparative evaluation.
