This paper presents a compelling method for learning controllers for complex characters by jointly optimizing trajectories and a neural network policy. The core idea – interleaving trajectory optimization (specifically, Contact-Invariant Optimization or CIO) with policy learning – is well-motivated and demonstrably effective. The authors successfully apply this approach to a diverse set of characters and locomotion tasks in 3D, a significant advancement over prior work largely limited to 2D. The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail to understand the methodology and results. The inclusion of a video alongside the submission is particularly helpful for appreciating the quality of the generated motions.

The paper's strengths lie in its technical sophistication and impressive empirical performance. The authors convincingly demonstrate the benefits of injecting sensory noise during training, improving robustness and generalization. The claim that jointly learning trajectories and policy leads to superior results is also well-supported by the comparative evaluation presented in Figure 2, which shows a substantial performance gap compared to training on a static trajectory dataset. Furthermore, the re-optimization step at each timestep, while adding computational cost, allows for the use of larger timesteps – a practical consideration for real-time control. The modularity of the approach, allowing the same network architecture to control diverse morphologies, is a significant achievement. 

However, as a reviewer not deeply familiar with the domain of character control, assessing the true originality and significance of this work is challenging. While the paper clearly builds upon existing techniques (trajectory optimization, neural networks, reinforcement learning), it's difficult to gauge how novel the specific combination and implementation are within the field. The paper references related work, but a more thorough discussion comparing this approach to state-of-the-art methods specifically tailored to character control would be beneficial. While the authors mention previous work [9, 11], quantifying the improvement over those approaches more precisely would strengthen the contribution.

Several minor points deserve attention. The motivation for the extra optimization step in Section 7, while explained as allowing larger timesteps, could be elaborated further. The reviewer questions whether the residual trajectory cost or policy regression error truly necessitate this re-optimization at each timestep, or whether the inherent dynamics of the system would prevent physically implausible motions. A more detailed discussion of these trade-offs would be valuable. 

On a technical level, Line 153 states "similar to recent approaches" and would benefit from one or two specific references. The explanation for keeping updates small in parameter space during the block-alternation scheme is understandable, but initially counterintuitive; a slightly more detailed justification might improve clarity.  Finally, a typo exists at Line 89 ("to unify.").

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Strong empirical results:** The method demonstrably works well on a variety of challenging tasks and character morphologies.
*   **Technical soundness:** The combination of trajectory optimization and policy learning is well-motivated and implemented with a high level of sophistication.
*   **Clarity and organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.
*   **Practical benefits:**  The method enables real-time interactive control and allows for larger timesteps.
*   **Addresses a challenging problem:** Learning controllers for complex characters is a difficult task, and this work represents a significant step forward.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   **Uncertainty regarding novelty:** Assessing the originality of the approach is difficult without deep domain expertise.
*   **Limited discussion of significance:** The paper doesn’t fully articulate the broader impact of this work within the field of character control or robotics.
*   **Minor technical points:** The motivation for the re-optimization step and the “similar to recent approaches” statement require further clarification.



**Overall:**

Despite my reservations regarding novelty due to limited domain expertise, I believe this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution. The technical prowess demonstrated by the authors is impressive, and the results are compelling. I recommend acceptance, contingent upon addressing the minor points raised above regarding clarification of motivation and referencing.
