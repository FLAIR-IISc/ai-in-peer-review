- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers by embedding feedback gains learned from trajectory optimization.  
- Uses a soft coupling between trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a cost penalty to improve robustness and stability.  
- Resulting policies provide simpler, more robust approximations of optimal trajectories than MPC alone.  
- Builds on prior work in reinforcement learning, stochastic optimal control, and Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO).  
- Main novelty is successfully scaling this combined approach to complex 3D systems (robots and animated characters).  
- Sophisticated parallelization strategy is a key implementation enabler for large-scale optimization.  
- Demonstrations show realistic, stable behaviors across a diverse set of morphologies (swimmers to bipeds).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, technically detailed, and motivations/design choices are well explained.  
- Major strength: seamless integration of graphics, control, and neural networks with strong experimental results.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed empirical comparison to direct reinforcement learning baselines.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of hyperparameter choices and sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: dependence on MuJoCo physics engine may limit applicability to other systems.  
- Recommendation: overall an excellent paper with a strong recommendation for acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
