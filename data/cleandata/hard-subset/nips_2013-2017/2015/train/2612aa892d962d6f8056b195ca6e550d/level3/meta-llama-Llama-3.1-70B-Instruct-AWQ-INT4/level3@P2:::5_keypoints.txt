- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for varied dynamical systems and tasks.
- Proposes combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and training for unexpected task changes.
- Demonstrates promising results on swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking across different morphologies.
- Introduces a policy mapping sensory state to action and a distributed stochastic optimization to jointly find trajectories and a reconstructing policy.
- Training with noise improves robustness and reduces overfitting.
- Strength: novel combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization handling complex dynamics.
- Strength: empirical success across multiple character types and tasks.
- Strength: provides a detailed description of the optimization algorithm and method workflow.
- Major weakness: paper structure is imbalanced—section 4 is too short, section 6 is overly long and detailed.
- Major weakness: inadequate and unclear description of the neural network architecture, especially the recurrent units.
- Major weakness: calling the network "stochastic" is unclear or contradictory and needs justification.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to prior work and limited discussion of the significance and potential impact.
- Quality assessment: generally well-written but needs more detail on network architecture and optimization specifics.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on significant restructuring and clarification of the network and comparisons.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10, Clarity 6/10.
