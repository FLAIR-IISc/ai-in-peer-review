- Presents a method for training recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers across diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, multiple walking gaits).  
- Combines supervised learning, trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization), and noise injection to produce stable, realistic behaviors.  
- Does not rely on motion capture or hand-designed state machines, enabling automated controller learning.  
- Demonstrations across varied morphologies and 3D locomotion are ambitious and a major strength.  
- The core idea of using trajectory optimization to teach a neural network control policies is compelling and potentially scalable to high-dimensional systems.  
- The combination of interleaved supervised learning, trajectory optimization, noise injection, and feedback gain computation is well-reasoned.  
- Claims full model knowledge is required (Eq. 6), but the paper fails to discuss limitations or practical implications of this assumption.  
- Sections 4 and 6 are disproportionately detailed; Section 4 omits the recurrent units mentioned in the abstract, creating an architectural disconnect.  
- The paper calls the network "stochastic" despite its determinism—the apparent stochasticity comes from noise in training data and needs clarification.  
- Organizational and clarity issues (uneven detail, missing explanations) reduce the paper’s overall impact.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after significant restructuring, clearer articulation of limitations, and improved technical clarity.
