- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers by alternating Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and NN training for real-time interactive character control.  
- Uses noise injection during training to improve robustness and generalization without motion capture or task-specific features.  

Strengths:
- Novel combination of CIO and NN training with an alternating optimization scheme.  
- Extends CIO success to 3D locomotion and diverse character morphologies.  
- Demonstrates impressive, realistic behaviors in video results using a single network architecture.  
- Method is grounded in established control theory and ML, with sensible use of noise injection.  
- High potential impact across robotics, animation, and biomechanics.

Weaknesses:
- Experimental evaluation is limited (small training set, 100–200 trials with 5 branches), raising overfitting/generalization concerns.  
- Baselines are weak and MPC comparison is brief, uncited, and insufficiently systematic.  
- Mathematical presentation lacks clarity and context (CIO step, Eq. 6 require external references).  
- Notational inconsistencies (dropped time index t in Sec. 3.2) make procedures hard to follow.  
- Missing implementation details and code, hindering reproducibility and adoption.  
- No runtime measurements or scaling analysis to assess practical real-time feasibility.  
- Terminology issues: distinction between data augmentation and noise injection unclear; denoising autoencoder analogy is weak.  
- Unjustified algorithmic choices (random initialization of Xs, same eta hyperparameter for both optimization steps).  
- Architectural disconnect: asynchronous joint optimization differs from the alternating scheme and needs earlier discussion.  
- Ablation studies are too coarse; need separate evaluations for each component (e.g., hidden noise vs. data augmentation).

Meta-assessment:
- Technically sound core methodology but limited experimental validation weakens claims.  
- Writing is generally good but marred by key notational and explanatory gaps.  
- Original and novel, but needs broader related-work discussion.  
- Significant potential impact, but current evaluation doesn’t fully demonstrate advantages over existing methods.

Decision rationale:
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, promising approach with strong visual results and sound foundations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, weak baselines, missing details, and unjustified choices.

Recommendations:
- Reject now but encourage resubmission after addressing concerns.  
- Expand experiments (larger training set, systematic comparisons to SOTA).  
- Provide clearer definitions/explanations for CIO and Eq. 6 and fix notation in Sec. 3.2.  
- Release code and include runtime and scaling measurements.  
- Justify algorithmic choices and hyperparameters, and reconcile optimization schemes.  
- Perform finer-grained ablation studies and consider annealing the CIO regularization parameter (alpha).
