- Paper presents a novel method training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers by combining supervised learning with Contact-Invariant Optimization, embedding feedback gains, soft coupling via penalty, and noise injection.  
- Technical excellence: sound theoretical analysis and extensive experiments integrating trajectory optimization with neural network training for high-dimensional control.  
- Clarity and organization: well-written, reproducible, with algorithmic steps, parameter settings, and ablation studies.  
- Originality: novel combination of trajectory optimization and network regression, embedding feedback gains and noise injection, achieving stable 3D locomotion.  
- Significance: demonstrates robust, realistic control across diverse morphologies and tasks, offering a unified framework with broad applications.  
- Comparative evaluation: lacks direct comparisons to state-of-the-art reinforcement learning or MPC on performance and computational efficiency.  
- Scalability concerns: dependence on cloud/GPU resources raises questions about real-world scalability in resource-constrained environments.  
- Limited limitations discussion: needs more explicit treatment of transfer to physical robots and handling highly dynamic environments.  
- Argument for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, is technically rigorous, and shows strong experimental validation.  
- Argument against acceptance: absence of direct baselines and unclear practicality/scalability leave important questions unanswered.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution suitable for acceptance, with suggested additions of direct comparisons and a clearer discussion of limitations in revisions.
