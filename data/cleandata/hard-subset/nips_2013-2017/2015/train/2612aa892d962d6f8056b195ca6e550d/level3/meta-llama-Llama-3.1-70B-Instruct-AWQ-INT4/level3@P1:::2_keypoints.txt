- Presents a method combining supervised learning with Contact-Invariant Optimization to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for varied dynamical tasks (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped walking).
- Claims ability to learn successful controllers for diverse morphologies without specialized initialization or motion-capture data.
- Experiments are thorough and the paper is generally well-written and technically sound.
- Needs clearer positioning relative to prior work in trajectory optimization and neural network control; novelty is not well delineated.
- Notation around lines 226–240 is confusing and should be revised for clarity.
- Some technical details lack sufficient explanation or justification (e.g., claim that parameter λ yields “softer feedback gains”).
- Authors should provide explicit comparisons to existing techniques and highlight what is novel about their approach.
- The demonstrated effectiveness across diverse morphologies is a significant contribution to physics-based animation and robotics.
- Pros for acceptance: significant contribution, clear explanation, and thorough experiments.
- Cons for acceptance: unclear relation to prior work, confusing notation, and insufficient justification for some claims.
