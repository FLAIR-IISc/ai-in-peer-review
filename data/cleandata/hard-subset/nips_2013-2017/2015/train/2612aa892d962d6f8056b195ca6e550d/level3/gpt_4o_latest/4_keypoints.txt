1. Introduces a joint optimization method alternating trajectory optimization and policy learning for interactive control of high-dimensional continuous characters.  
2. Practical and scalable approach that reuses established techniques; sensory noise and perturbation-based feedback gains boost robustness and generalization.  
3. Produces realistic, stable behaviors across diverse tasks and morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal), with strong supplementary video evidence.  
4. Well-written and organized paper with sufficient technical detail for reproducibility by expert readers.  
5. Strong validation via ablation studies and comparisons (e.g., static datasets, MPC) supporting joint optimization and noise injection benefits.  
6. Limited theoretical novelty—primarily a combination of existing trajectory optimization and neural regression techniques.  
7. Unclear motivation and necessity for per-timestep re-optimization; potential issues with residual trajectory costs, policy regression errors, and softly enforced physical constraints.  
8. Related-work coverage could be improved by citing recent methods advocating small parameter updates to better contextualize contributions.  
9. Minor typographical error: "to unity" should be "to unify" (line 89).  
10. Pros for acceptance: practical, scalable, state-of-the-art results, clear exposition, and solid empirical validation.  
11. Cons against acceptance: limited theoretical insight, unclear extra optimization step, and missing related-work citations.  
12. Recommendation: Accept if authors clarify and justify the extra re-optimization step and add relevant related-work references.
