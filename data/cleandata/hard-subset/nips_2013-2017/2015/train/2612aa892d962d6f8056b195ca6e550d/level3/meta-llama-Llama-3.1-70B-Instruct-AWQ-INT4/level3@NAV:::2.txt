This paper, "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks," presents a novel approach to training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for physically consistent interactive character control. The authors demonstrate the ability to generate stable and realistic behaviors for a range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with different body morphologies.

The main idea of the paper is to combine supervised learning with trajectory optimization to learn a neural network policy that can reconstruct sense and action pairs of all trajectories at all timesteps. The authors use a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm to solve the problem, which involves interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and using the trajectory optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' ideas and methodology. The use of figures and tables to illustrate the results is also helpful. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work and clearly position their contribution relative to existing literature.

However, there are some areas that need improvement. Firstly, the authors could provide more details on the neural network architecture and the training process. For example, how did they choose the number of layers and hidden units? What was the learning rate and batch size used during training? Secondly, the authors could provide more analysis on the results, such as comparing the performance of their method with other state-of-the-art methods. Finally, the authors could discuss the limitations of their approach and potential future work.
