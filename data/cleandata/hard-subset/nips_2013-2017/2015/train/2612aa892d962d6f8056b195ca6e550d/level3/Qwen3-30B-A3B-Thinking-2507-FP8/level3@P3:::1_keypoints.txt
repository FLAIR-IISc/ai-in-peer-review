- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without motion capture or task-specific features.
- Embeds optimal feedback gains into neural policies and softly couples trajectory optimization with policy learning via a deviation cost penalty.
- Trains policies to learn simpler, more robust approximations of optimal trajectories.
- Demonstrates successful 3D locomotion across diverse morphologies, advancing beyond prior 2D-limited work.
- Provides strong theoretical grounding with derivation of optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization.
- Offers rigorous experimental validation across tasks with thorough ablation studies (comparison to naive NN training and no noise injection).
- Implementation and computational approach are well-documented and reproducible.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with precise mathematics, effective figures, and supportive supplementary video.
- Novel contributions: feedback-gain embedding, soft coupling of trajectory optimization and policy learning, and a single policy architecture for diverse morphologies.
- Significant methodological impact with clear potential for robotics and character animation and advances in real-time control.
- Strengths: clarity, robustness across multiple morphologies, novel integration of techniques, strong theory, and thorough experiments.
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to recent MPC methods, nontrivial computational/training requirements (e.g., 2.5 hours with 30 optimization nodes), and unclear scaling to larger systems.
- Pro for acceptance: clear, significant contribution with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Con for acceptance: potentially prohibitive training time and insufficient contextual comparison of computational requirements.
- Reviewer recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
