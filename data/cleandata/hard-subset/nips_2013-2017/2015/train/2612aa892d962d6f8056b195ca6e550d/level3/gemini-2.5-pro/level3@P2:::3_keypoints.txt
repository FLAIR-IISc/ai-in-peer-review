- Proposes joint, alternating optimization of an RNN policy and Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectories to produce a single real-time controller for diverse simulated characters.  
- Core technical idea: iteratively refine trajectories and policy, with noise injected at sensory inputs (using optimal feedback gains for correct targets) and in hidden units during training.  
- Strength: Significant and general — one unified algorithm produces stable, natural locomotion across bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, and flyers.  
- Strength: Novel training method — joint alternating optimization outperforms naive supervised learning on static optimized trajectories (ablation supports this).  
- Strength: Effective noise strategy — data augmentation via feedback gains plus hidden-unit noise yields local robustness and regularization.  
- Weakness: Presentation clarity — Section 3.2 (optimal feedback gains) is confusing; time indices and how perturbations affect actions need clearer step-by-step explanation.  
- Weakness: Trajectory optimization under-described — cost C(X) and constraints in Eq.(6) lack a concise, self-contained summary.  
- Weakness: Algorithm vs. implementation mismatch — asynchronous distributed architecture in Section 6 differs from Algorithm 1’s strict alternation and needs discussion of stability/implications.  
- Weakness: Comparative evaluation lacking — no systematic comparison to modern learning-based control methods (e.g., policy gradient/actor-critic); qualitative comparison insufficient.  
- Weakness: Ablation study could be more granular — sensory noise and hidden-unit noise effects should be separated.  
- Weakness: Training data size and baselines — robustness claims rely on small datasets (100–200 trials); unclear if baselines had comparable data/diversity.  
- Weakness: Missing runtime metrics — execution time and scaling with model/network complexity are not reported but are critical for real-time use.  
- Question: What is the initial state of trajectories X in Algorithm 1 (random or initialized from CIO with initial θ)?  
- Question: Why use the same regularization parameter η for both trajectory and policy updates — was separate tuning explored?  
- Question: Does the locomotion goal include target orientation (facing angle) and if so how is it handled; how hard to extend if not included?  
- Question: Please clarify terminology — sensory-input noise is data augmentation while hidden-unit noise is regularization; denoising autoencoder analogy may be inexact.  
- Question: Have you considered annealing the CIO penalty weight α so the network gradually finds its own stable solutions?  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, effective framework with compelling results demonstrating a significant advance toward unified controllers for diverse characters.  
- Argument against acceptance: Needs clearer exposition of feedback-gain derivation and stronger experimental comparisons and performance metrics to fully support claims.
