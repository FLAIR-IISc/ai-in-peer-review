- Presents a method training neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers via alternating joint training between Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and neural nets with noise injection, demonstrated on swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal 3D locomotion without mocap or task-specific features.

- Key contribution: alternating joint training that combines trajectory optimization with neural regression plus noise injection to improve robustness and produce stable, realistic control policies across diverse morphologies, advancing beyond prior 2D-limited work.

- Experimental evaluation is severely limited (100–200 trials, 5 branched segments), weakening claims about generalization and raising overfitting concerns.

- Comparative evaluation is superficial: only a brief MPC comparison provided, lacking proper citations and systematic benchmarking against relevant baselines.

- Mathematical presentation is unclear, particularly the CIO formulation and Eq. 6; Section 3.2 has confusing notation (dropped time index t) that obscures the optimal-action-for-noisy-inputs procedure.

- Omits practical details important for real-time use: no runtime measurements, no analysis of scaling with model complexity, and unclear whether orientation is included in the target.

- Terminology needs refinement—distinguish “data augmentation” from “noise injection” and question the denoising-autoencoder analogy.

- Numerous minor writing and notation issues throughout that require correction.

- The asynchronous joint optimization described in Section 6 differs substantially from the alternating scheme in Algorithm 1 but is not properly explained.

- Overall assessment: promising and well-executed core idea with potential impact, but needs more comprehensive experiments, clearer mathematical exposition, and better contextualization in the literature to substantiate claimed advantages.
