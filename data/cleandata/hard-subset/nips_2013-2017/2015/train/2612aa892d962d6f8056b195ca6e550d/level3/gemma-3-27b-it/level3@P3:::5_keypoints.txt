- Presents a method combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and supervised learning to train RNN feedback controllers for diverse 3D dynamic characters (swimming, flying, walking).  
- Aims for a universal control approach applicable across morphologies, reducing hand-tuning and motion-capture dependence.  
- Builds on prior trajectory optimization and neural control work, notably extending successful 2D methods to challenging 3D scenarios.

Quality:
- Technically sound integration of trajectory optimization and neural-network training.  
- Experiments indicate stable, realistic behaviors across varied characters but are concisely presented.  
- Addresses overfitting via noise injection during training.  
- Lacks detail about the trajectory optimization process beyond referencing CIO; sensitivity to CIO parameters is unclear.  
- Does not adequately discuss computational cost of generating training data.

Clarity:
- Overall logical structure; Sections 3 and 7 are well explained.  
- Sections 4 and 6 are too brief; Section 4 fails to explain the role of recurrent units adequately.  
- Description of the network’s “stochasticity” is unclear—network appears deterministic while training data/noise is stochastic.  
- Notation is mostly clear but multiple subscripts (e.g., st(Xi)) can be cumbersome.

Originality:
- Combination of methods is not entirely novel but notable for effective application to diverse 3D characters.  
- Novel contribution in deriving optimal feedback gains (Section 3.2) as an efficient alternative to LQG.  
- Using the same network architecture across characters (universality) is noteworthy.  
- Needs more comparison with recent model-based reinforcement learning approaches.

Significance:
- Promising path toward automating controller creation without hand-tuning or mocap, relevant to robotics, animation, and biomechanics.  
- Limited experimental scope and high data-generation cost raise questions about scalability and practicality; a robotics demonstration would strengthen impact.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Demonstrates stable, realistic control for diverse 3D characters.  
- Introduces a novel method for deriving optimal feedback gains.  
- Offers potential for universal controllers, reducing hand-tuning.  
- Effectively integrates trajectory optimization with neural-network learning.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Insufficient detail on recurrent units (Section 4).  
- Section 6 is underdeveloped.  
- Unclear explanation of network “stochasticity.”  
- Inadequate discussion of computational cost for data generation.  
- Lacks ablation study on CIO parameter sensitivity.  
- Method requires full model knowledge (Eq. 6), a significant limitation.

Overall recommendation:
- Reject with request for major revision: improve balance and detail across sections, clarify stochasticity and recurrent-unit role, expand experimental/ablation analysis, and explicitly address full model knowledge and computational cost.
