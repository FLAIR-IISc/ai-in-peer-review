- Proposes training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers by alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised policy regression.  
- Assumes full model knowledge and differentiable contact dynamics (Eq. 6), making it a model-based control method rather than model-free RL.  
- Method interleaves CIO-generated trajectories and optimal local feedback gains with noisy policy learning, executing in real time via a 1-step dynamics-enforcing optimization.  
- Demonstrations cover swimming, flying, and bipedal/quadrupedal locomotion in 3D, showing broad behavioral scope.  
- Connects to prior work on trajectory-optimization-guided policy learning, MPC, and denoising/dropout regularization; contrasts with model-free methods like TRPO.  
- Technical contributions (alternating optimization and efficient gain computation, Sec. 3.2) are sound and well-motivated.  
- Ablation studies (Sec. 8.1) support the importance of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Heavy reliance on exact dynamics and soft enforcement (allowing root forces) limits physical fidelity and real-world transfer.  
- Baseline comparisons (e.g., against modern policy-gradient methods and MPC) are suggestive but not comprehensive.  
- Clarity is uneven: Sec. 4 (policy regression) is too brief given its centrality, while Sec. 6 (distributed training) is overly operational.  
- Ambiguity about recurrence: the paper claims “small number of recurrent units” but the described architecture appears feed-forward with r as I/O—needs clarification on true recurrent connections and training.  
- Ambiguity about stochasticity: policy at test time appears deterministic, so clarify that “stochastic” refers to training-time input/activation noise and data perturbations.  
- Originality: the synthesis of CIO, supervised policy learning, and gain-perturbation in 3D is a novel and meaningful contribution despite known components.  
- Significance: promising for interactive graphics and robotics with accurate simulators, but hardware transfer requires addressing model mismatch; recommend restructuring, clearer exposition, and stronger baselines.  
- Pros: unified controller for diverse morphologies, clever reuse of CIO for gains, principled noise injection and joint optimization improve robustness.  
- Cons: requires full differentiable dynamics, unclear descriptions of recurrence and stochasticity, disproportionate section coverage, limited baseline comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: promising work but needs clarifications and stronger positioning against baselines; reviewer leans toward weak accept contingent on revisions.
