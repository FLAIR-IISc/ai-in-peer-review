Novel approach combining trajectory optimization with policy learning for interactive control of complex characters.  
Key idea: jointly learn trajectories and a policy by alternating subproblems solvable with existing methods.  
Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and technically sophisticated.  
Demonstrated strong results on diverse tasks (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped walking).  
Training with sensory noise and joint trajectory-policy learning shown to improve robustness.  
Reuses existing methods (Contact-Invariant Optimization, SGD), enabling efficient, scalable, real-time control.  
Thorough analysis of optimization: quadratic proximal regularization and noise help prevent overfitting.  
Unclear motivation for the extra optimization step in Section 7; reviewer expected policy-only execution.  
Reviewer questions whether residual trajectory cost or policy regression error actually necessitate re-optimization.  
Reviewer asks whether system dynamics would naturally prevent physical implausibility without re-optimization.  
Requests references for the phrase "similar to recent approaches" (line 153).  
Typo: "to unity" should be "to unify" (line 89).  
Overall positive impression of results and optimization scheme but uncertainty about novelty/significance due to reviewer’s unfamiliarity with the domain.  
Quality: technically sound with good understanding; needs clearer explanation of Section 7 motivation.  
Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, impressive results, broad application potential.  
Arguments against acceptance: uncertainty about novelty/significance, unclear extra optimization motivation, and whether re-optimization is necessary.
