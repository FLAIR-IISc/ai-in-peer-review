- Proposes joint learning that alternates trajectory optimization and neural network regression to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse complex characters.
- Decoupling into alternating subproblems allows reuse of existing trajectory optimization and regression methods.
- Demonstrates stable, realistic interactive control across swimmers, flyers, bipeds, and quadrupeds using a single architecture.
- Technical implementation is sophisticated, including distributed training, noise injection, and feedback gain computation.
- Joint optimization with noise injection yields large performance gains over naive neural network training (≈100× improvement).
- Results are thorough, well-documented, and supported by ablation studies and supplementary video.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient implementation detail for reproducibility.
- Main contribution is practical/engineering integration of existing techniques rather than new theoretical insights.
- The need for the extra re-optimization step in Section 7 is unclear and not well-motivated.
- Reviewer questions whether residual trajectory cost or policy regression error truly necessitate re-optimization and whether dynamics would prevent implausible behavior.
- Missing a specific reference for the claim “similar to recent approaches” (line 153).
- Minor typo at line 89 (“to unify”).
- Significance is strong for character control, enabling one architecture for varied morphologies, but may be domain-limited relative to broader ML impact.
- Overall assessment: strong technical contribution with impressive empirical results; recommended for acceptance pending minor revisions.
