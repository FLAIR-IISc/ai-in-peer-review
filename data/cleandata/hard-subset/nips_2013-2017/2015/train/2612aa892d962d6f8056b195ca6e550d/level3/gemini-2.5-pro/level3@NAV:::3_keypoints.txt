- Trains an RNN to reproduce outputs of offline trajectory optimization (CIO) to create real-time controllers for diverse physical characters.  
- Main contributions: an alternating joint training scheme refining CIO trajectories and the neural policy, plus noise injection in inputs and hidden layers for robustness.  
- Extends prior supervised learning + trajectory optimization work to complex 3D characters and tasks (walking, swimming, flying).  
- Strength: well-motivated methods with visually impressive, stable, and realistic control policies across varied morphologies.  
- Strength: natural gaits emerge without task-specific engineering, showing promise of combining offline optimization with neural approximators.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited—small training set (100–200 trials) weakens generalization claims.  
- Weakness: comparative evaluation is weak—only a brief, uncited MPC comparison and no systematic baselines versus other policy learning methods.  
- Weakness: lacks runtime analysis and discussion of how performance scales with model complexity for real-time use.  
- Clarity issues: key mathematical details (CIO cost components, derivation of Equation 6) are not self-contained and Section 3.2 has inconsistent notation.  
- Reproducibility concern: code and implementation details are not provided.  
- Recommendation: strengthen experiments with more data, rigorous baseline comparisons, performance benchmarks, clearer math presentation, and released code.
