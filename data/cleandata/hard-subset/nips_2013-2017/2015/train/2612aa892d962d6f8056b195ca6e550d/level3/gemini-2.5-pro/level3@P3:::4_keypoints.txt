- Paper presents a framework that jointly learns optimal trajectories and an RNN policy, combining offline trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) with a neural controller for real-time control.  
- Method formulates a large constrained optimization and solves it by alternating between trajectory optimization (match task costs and current policy) and supervised regression to update the policy.  
- Demonstrated on a diverse set of challenging 3D characters and locomotion tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal and quadrupedal walking) from a unified framework.  
- Strength: Impressive quality and diversity of results—stable, realistic, interactive controllers across very different morphologies and dynamics.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation—ablation studies convincingly show benefits of joint optimization and the importance of injecting sensory noise for generalization.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well organized, making a complex methodology easy to follow.  
- Weakness: Limited conceptual novelty—main contribution is an effective combination/engineering of existing techniques rather than new theoretical insights.  
- Weakness: Motivation for the extra T=1 trajectory optimization during policy execution is unclear—need clarification on whether it corrects residual policy error or enables stable simulation with larger timesteps.  
- Minor: Typo at Line 89 ("to unity" should be "to unify").  
- Minor: Line 153 should cite specific prior work when mentioning proximal regularization and related approaches.  
- Overall: Strong, well-executed systems/application paper with high practical significance; reviewer leans toward acceptance despite reservations about conceptual novelty.
