- Proposes alternating supervised learning and trajectory optimization (CIO) to jointly train neural-network controllers and the trajectory dataset.  
- Core innovation: joint optimization of policy and dataset with strategic sensory + hidden-layer noise injection for robustness.  
- Demonstrates stable, realistic 3D locomotion across diverse characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Advances prior work by scaling trajectory-optimization-based controller training to complex 3D dynamics beyond earlier 2D results.  
- Technical execution is strong with rigorous ablations showing joint training and noise are critical (Fig.2, Table 1).  
- Experimental limitations: small training set (100–200 trials) raises concerns about generalization in high-dimensional control spaces.  
- Ablation conflates sensory and hidden-layer noise, leaving unclear which component drives robustness.  
- Missing runtime/performance measurements (FPS, CPU) undermines claims about real-time practicality for animation/gaming.  
- Trajectory-optimization presentation (Sec.3.2) is confusing with dropped time indices and inconsistent notation, requiring external references.  
- Asynchronous distributed training (Sec.6) is described but poorly connected to Alg.1, creating clarity issues.  
- Denoising autoencoder analogy is misleading—noise here induces optimal-action changes, unlike standard denoising; needs clarification.  
- Writing issues: typos, inconsistent variable usage, and misplaced citations reduce readability and mathematical clarity.  
- Originality is high: joint CIO–NN training and noise strategy using optimal feedback gains are novel for character control.  
- Comparison to closely related work (Levine et al. 2014, Mordatch & Todorov 2012) is insufficiently distinguished.  
- High significance for animation/robotics—unifies control across morphologies and supports interactive goal changes—but real-time and robot-parameter claims need empirical support.  
- Recommended revisions: separate sensory vs. hidden-noise ablation, runtime metrics, comparisons to RL baselines (PPO/SAC), clarify Sec.3.2 notation, and explicitly address orientation handling.  
- Overall assessment: promising and well-executed contribution; weak accept recommended conditional on addressed experimental and clarity concerns.
