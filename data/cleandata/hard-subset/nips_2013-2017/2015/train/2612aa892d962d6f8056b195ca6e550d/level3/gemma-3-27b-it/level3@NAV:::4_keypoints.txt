- Presents a compelling method (CIO) that interleaves trajectory optimization with neural network regression to learn controllers for complex articulated characters.  
- Joint optimization approach enables stable, realistic behaviors across diverse morphologies and tasks (swimming, flying, walking).  
- Decoupling into alternating subproblems allows reuse of existing methods and yields significant improvements over naive approaches.  
- Technically sophisticated: addresses high-dimensional control and uses noise injection and optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization.  
- Demonstrates impressive 3D locomotion and complex movements beyond prior 2D work, supported by strong video results.  
- Clear writing, organization, and helpful cross-references make the paper accessible despite technical depth.  
- Authors acknowledge computational cost and provide a distributed training architecture to manage it.  
- Lacks substantial theoretical novelty—primarily a skillful combination of existing techniques rather than a new algorithmic framework.  
- Broader impact is hard to assess for non-expert readers; domain-specific nature limits generalizability claims.  
- Would benefit from more thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art reinforcement learning methods.  
- The motivation and necessity for the re-optimization step (Section 7) are underexplained and need stronger justification.  
- Minor issues: missing concrete references for "similar to recent approaches" (line 153) and a typo at line 89 ("to unify").  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical and practical contribution with limited theoretical novelty, making it a valuable but not groundbreaking paper.
