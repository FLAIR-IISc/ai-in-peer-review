- Proposes training an RNN controller by alternating joint optimization of desired trajectories and neural policy parameters, combining offline trajectory optimization with supervised learning.  
- Uses Contact-Invariant Optimization to generate physically realistic, task-oriented trajectories as training data.  
- Introduces principled sensory-noise injection during training to improve robustness of the learned policy.  
- Presents a novel technique to extract optimal feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer to guide learning.  
- Demonstrates the method on diverse 3D characters (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds) producing stable, interactive locomotion without motion capture or task-specific state machines.  
- Implementation is technically sophisticated with distributed/GPU architecture and non-trivial engineering.  
- Experiments and ablation studies strongly support key claims, showing joint optimization and noise injection are critical.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and supported by effective figures and tables.  
- Novelty lies in the stochastic, distributed block-alternating formulation, feedback-gain extraction, and principled noise use, extending prior trajectory-optimization + learning work to complex 3D cases.  
- Significance is high for character animation, gaming, and robotics due to automated synthesis of robust controllers for varied morphologies.  
- Main concern: runtime uses an additional optimization to convert policy outputs into feasible next states, raising questions about the need for online optimization despite training a feed-forward policy.  
- Minor issues: request for citations supporting proximal regularization statement and a small typo ("unity" → "unify").
