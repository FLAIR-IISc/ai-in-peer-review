- Proposes alternating joint training between Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and recurrent neural networks to produce near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse character tasks.  
- Introduces noise injection (sensory and hidden-layer) to improve robustness without large additional datasets.  
- Demonstrates real-time-capable control for complex 3D characters (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with realistic, stable behaviors shown in supplementary video.  
- Effectively combines trajectory optimization precision with neural network generalization in a motivated technical contribution.  

- Training set is small (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), weakening claims about generalization and increasing overfitting risk.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief, uncited MPC comparison and no systematic comparisons to recent neural-control methods.  
- Mathematical presentation is deficient: CIO step and Equation 6 lack detail and key terms are undefined.  
- Notational issues (e.g., dropped time index in Section 3.2) make the optimal-action-for-noisy-inputs procedure unclear.  
- Asynchronous joint optimization in Section 6 is not clearly connected to the alternating scheme in Algorithm 1.  
- Missing practical analyses: runtime measurements, scaling with model complexity, and whether targets include orientation as well as position.  
- Terminology confusion between “data augmentation” and “noise injection,” and a questionable denoising-autoencoder analogy.  
- Lack of granular ablation studies to isolate effects of different noise-injection techniques.  

- Suggestions: clarify target orientation handling and explain adding target angle; provide runtime and scaling analysis; expand comparisons to recent neural-control methods.  
- Suggestions: improve mathematical definitions and details (Equation 6, CIO), fix notation in Section 3.2, and link Algorithm 1 to Section 6’s asynchronous approach.  
- Suggests releasing code to improve reproducibility.  
- Suggests conducting finer-grained ablations (e.g., remove data augmentation or hidden noise separately).  

- Overall: promising, well-executed technical contribution that generates diverse, realistic behaviors but requires stronger validation (larger datasets, better baselines, clearer math, and practical analyses) to fully establish significance.  
- Meets NIPS quality criteria on technical content, but acceptance would be strengthened by addressing the listed weaknesses.
