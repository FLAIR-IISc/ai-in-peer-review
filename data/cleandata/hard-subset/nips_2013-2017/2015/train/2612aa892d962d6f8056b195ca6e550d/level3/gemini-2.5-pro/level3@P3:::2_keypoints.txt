- Paper proposes training a small recurrent NN to mimic Contact-Invariant Optimization trajectories, yielding real-time interactive controllers for diverse simulated characters.
- Key idea: interleave offline trajectory optimization with supervised learning to distill non-real-time optimizers into fast parametric policies.
- Demonstrates a single, general network can produce swimming, flying, bipedal walking, and quadrupedal trotting without task-specific features or mocap.
- Strength: impressive and general experimental results showing stable, realistic controllers across many morphologies and tasks.
- Strength: sound methodology—joint optimization and noise injection are validated by ablation studies supporting the training procedure.
- Strength: high potential significance for animation, gaming, and robotics as an automated method for generating interactive controllers.
- Weakness: contribution positioning is unclear—paper should clarify novelty versus being a scaled application of known techniques.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to prior work (e.g., Levine & Koltun, Guided Policy Search); need direct contrasts on sample efficiency, body types supported, runtime cost, and robustness.
- Weakness: claim of solving “substantially harder problems” requires more rigorous evidence and justification.
- Weakness: technical clarity issues—explain what “ill-conditioned” LQG gains mean and why large gains are problematic (numerical instability, oscillations, unrealistic motion).
- Weakness: narrative/order issues—present proposed feedback-gain method first, then justify why LQG was rejected for clearer comparison.
- Weakness: confusing notation (e.g., s(X)=s̄ and cost defined via s(X)−s̄); use clearer, distinct symbols and clarify definitions.
- Weakness: claim that their approach produces “softer” feedback gains needs precise definition, baseline comparison, and justification for allowing initial-state changes.
- Overall: technically sound and significant work with strong results; recommend acceptance if authors improve clarity and explicitly position/compare to related prior work.
