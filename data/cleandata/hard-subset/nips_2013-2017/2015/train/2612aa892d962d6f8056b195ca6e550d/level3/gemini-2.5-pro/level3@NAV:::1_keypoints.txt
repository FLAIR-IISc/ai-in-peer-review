- Proposes a method that softly couples offline trajectory optimization with supervised learning to train RNN policies as near-optimal feedback controllers.  
- Key innovation: embedding optimizer-derived optimal feedback gains into training targets to teach robustness to perturbations.  
- Builds on guided policy search and learning-from-experts literature but introduces a distinct formulation for reusing trajectory optimizer computations.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness across diverse morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without task-specific engineering.  
- Results produce stable, plausible locomotion and bridge offline planning with online execution.  
- High-quality, significant technical contribution that advances controller synthesis by combining optimization, control theory, and deep learning.  
- Exceptional clarity and thoroughness, with well-motivated method descriptions and informative ablation studies.  
- Main weakness: substantial computational cost of distributed training may hinder reproducibility for smaller labs.  
- Minor weakness: lacks a direct quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art model-free RL on at least one task for contextualization.  
- Overall conclusion: outstanding, technically impressive work; strong recommendation for acceptance.
