- Proposes training RNNs as feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems by combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization (CIO).
- Learns a state-to-action (velocity) policy that generalizes across morphologies using a single network architecture.
- Demonstrates stable, realistic locomotion behaviors (including 3D) across varied simulated characters; supplementary video supports results.
- Promising unified control approach that leverages CIO for high-quality data and neural networks for real-time execution and generalization.
- Addresses a key challenge in robotics, animation, and biomechanics by reducing reliance on motion capture and hand-tuned controllers.
- Includes robustness measures such as noise injection and a distributed training architecture to improve scalability.
- Technically sound approach with strong experimental validation and well-supported claims.
- Disproportionate emphasis on implementation details in Sections 4 and 6 reduces overall clarity and balance.
- Insufficient explanation of how recurrent units are integrated and used during training, despite being highlighted as important.
- The claim of a “stochastic” network is misleading; stochasticity appears only in training via noise injection, while the policy is deterministic given a state.
- The paper frames actions as velocities and suggests learning dynamics under an implicit policy, but this description feels hand-wavy and needs elaboration.
- Assumes full model knowledge for accurate dynamics simulation (Equation 6); lacks discussion on sensitivity to model inaccuracies.
- Originality: builds on prior RL and optimal control work but presents a novel, effective combination with notable 3D locomotion results.
- Significance: offers a potentially impactful unified control framework for diverse tasks and morphologies.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/effective method, strong 3D results, unified framework, combines trajectory optimization with neural learning.
- Arguments against acceptance: overly detailed/imbalanced sections, unclear role of recurrent units, misleading “stochastic” label, missing robustness analysis to model errors.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify recurrent architecture, stochasticity, and discuss model inaccuracy impacts.
