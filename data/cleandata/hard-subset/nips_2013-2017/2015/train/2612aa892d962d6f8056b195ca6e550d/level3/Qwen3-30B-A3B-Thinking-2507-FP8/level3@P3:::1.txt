

# Review: Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks

This paper presents a novel method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse physical systems. The authors address the challenge of creating interactive, robust controllers for complex locomotion tasks across varied morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without requiring motion capture or task-specific features. The core innovation lies in embedding optimal feedback gains into neural network policies, soft coupling trajectory optimization and policy learning through a cost penalty for deviation, and training policies to learn simpler, more robust approximations of optimal trajectories.

The work builds upon trajectory optimization methods like Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) [12] and recent neural network control approaches [9, 11], but significantly advances the state of the art by demonstrating successful 3D locomotion across diverse morphologies where prior work was limited to 2D. The paper relates to NIPS work on neural network control [3, 6, 14] and trajectory optimization [9, 11], but makes a clear contribution by integrating these techniques to solve substantially harder problems.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with strong theoretical grounding in trajectory optimization and feedback control. The authors provide a rigorous mathematical formulation of their method, including derivation of optimal feedback gains as a byproduct of trajectory optimization. Their experimental validation across diverse tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) with thorough ablation studies (comparing to naive neural network training and no noise injection) demonstrates robustness and effectiveness. The implementation details and computational approach are well-documented, making the work reproducible. The authors carefully acknowledge limitations, such as the need for trajectory optimization to generate training data.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and well-written. The mathematical formulation is precise, the methodology is logically presented, and the figures effectively illustrate key concepts. The supplementary video provides excellent visual evidence of the method's effectiveness. The paper provides sufficient detail for an expert to reproduce the results, including specific parameter values and implementation details.

**Originality:** The paper makes several novel contributions. The embedding of feedback gains into the neural network policy is a significant technical innovation that improves robustness. The soft coupling of trajectory optimization and policy learning via a cost penalty for deviation is a novel approach to the joint optimization problem. The demonstration of a single policy architecture controlling diverse morphologies without task-specific customization represents a substantial advance over previous work that required specialized controllers for each task type.

**Significance:** This work represents a significant methodological contribution to the field. The ability to train a single neural network policy that can control diverse physical systems with different morphologies and locomotion tasks is highly impactful. The method successfully addresses the challenging problem of creating robust, interactive controllers for complex physical systems. The results are inspiring and demonstrate clear potential for both robotics and character animation applications. The paper makes a strong case for how this approach could advance the state of the art in real-time control.

**Strengths:**
- Exceptionally clear and well-written technical contribution
- Demonstrates robust control across diverse morphologies and tasks
- Novel integration of trajectory optimization with neural network policy learning
- Provides strong theoretical foundation with practical implementation
- Thorough experimental validation including ablation studies
- Successful application to complex 3D locomotion problems

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could more thoroughly compare with recent MPC approaches beyond the limited comparison in Figure 3
- The computational requirements (2.5 hours of training with 30 optimization nodes) may limit applicability for some real-time robotics applications
- The paper doesn't extensively discuss how the method scales to even larger or more complex systems

**Pro and Con for Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Clear, significant methodological contribution addressing a challenging problem
- Strong theoretical foundation with practical implementation
- Demonstrates impressive results across diverse complex systems
- Exceptionally clear presentation and thorough experimental validation
- Represents a meaningful advance over previous work in neural network control

*Con:*
- Training time requirements may be prohibitive for some robotics applications
- Limited comparison with state-of-the-art MPC methods beyond Figure 3
- Some aspects of the computational requirements could be better contextualized

This paper is an outstanding contribution to the field that successfully integrates graphics, control theory, and neural networks. It advances the state of the art in real-time controller synthesis for complex physical systems with a method that is both theoretically sound and practically effective. The work represents a significant step toward the goal of creating universal, interactive controllers for diverse physical systems. I strongly recommend acceptance.
