- Proposes alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) and supervised RNN training to produce near-optimal feedback controllers for complex characters.
- Policy outputs desired velocities, contact forces, and recurrent-state updates; execution enforces dynamics via a short-horizon (T=1) optimization step.
- Improves robustness by adding Gaussian noise to sensory inputs with feedback-gain-adjusted targets and by injecting noise into hidden activations during NN training.
- Implemented as a distributed, asynchronous cloud pipeline with GPU-based NN training.
- Demonstrates interactive, visually plausible control across diverse 3D morphologies (swimmers, fliers, bipeds, quadrupeds) without motion capture or hand-engineered state machines.
- Ablation studies indicate alternating joint training and noise injections are critical for generalization and stability versus naive supervised fitting.
- Connects to prior work combining trajectory optimization and function approximation (e.g., Mordatch & Todorov, Levine & Koltun) and to policy search/deep RL and RNN control literature.
- Strength: single unified architecture and pipeline producing stable behaviors across media and morphologies.
- Strength: principled robustness mechanisms using input noise with gain-adjusted targets and hidden-layer noise.
- Strength: practical, simple-to-implement alternating optimization and effective distributed/asynchronous training setup.
- Strength: T=1 implicit execution step improves stability and permits relatively large timesteps.
- Strength: empirical ablations and architecture sweeps provide insight into important design choices.
- Weakness: limited comparative evaluation—no systematic baselines (GPS/TRPO/MPC) or quantitative metrics like success rate, energy, slip, or stability margins.
- Weakness: small training set (100–200 trials) limits claims about generalization to unseen goals, perturbations, and initial conditions.
- Weakness: clarity and notation issues—key derivations deferred to prior work, Eq. (6) under-specified, Sec. 3.2 notation confusing, and async pipeline differs from Algorithm 1.
- Weakness: missing practical execution details—latency, CPU/GPU budgets, and scalability with DoFs/contacts not reported.
- Weakness: terminology, typos, and minor editorial issues; denoising autoencoder analogy is misleading.
- Weakness: action representation uses desired velocities/contact forces and root forces at execution, potentially reducing physical plausibility and transferability to hardware.
- Quality: technically solid with sensible objectives and novel feedback-gain target adjustment, but some derivations and constraints are under-specified and evaluation is insufficient to claim superiority.
- Clarity: generally readable but needs clearer equations, time indices, and reconciliation of asynchronous vs. alternating training; pseudocode for feedback-gain computation would aid reproducibility.
- Originality: meaningful combination of CIO and guided policy search ideas with a practical robust pipeline; notable scope across 3D morphologies though components relate to prior work.
- Significance: promising for animation and robotics, but impact would increase with quantitative metrics, stronger baselines, runtime analysis, and code/data release.
- Suggestions: add quantitative comparisons to MPC/GPS/TRPO, report metrics (task completion, energy, slip, falls), and run finer-grained noise ablations.
- Suggestions: detail hyperparameter schedules, initialization, sensitivity analyses (η, α, λ), and report execution-time latency and resource usage scaling.
- Suggestions: quantify root forces during execution, discuss hardware-transfer implications, define all symbols in equations, fix typos, and release code or detailed pseudocode/configs.
- Arguments for acceptance: unified practical method producing diverse stable 3D behaviors, principled robustness design, and useful distributed engineering contribution.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak quantitative evaluation and baselines, missing runtime/scalability evidence, and clarity/reproducibility gaps.
- Overall: promising and well-executed approach likely of interest, but needs stronger empirical comparisons, clearer derivations/asynchronous presentation, and runtime metrics to increase impact.
