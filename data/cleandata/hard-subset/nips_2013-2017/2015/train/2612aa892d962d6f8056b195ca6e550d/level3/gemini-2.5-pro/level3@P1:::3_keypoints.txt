- Paper proposes jointly optimizing CIO-generated expert trajectories and a recurrent neural policy with principled noise injection for robust real-time character control.  
- Novelty: iterative refinement of expert trajectories and student policy addresses behavioral cloning distribution shift.  
- Strong empirical results: stable, realistic locomotion for diverse morphologies (bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, flyers) from a single framework.  
- Well-motivated noise injection: sensory augmentation and hidden-unit noise improve robustness; optimal feedback gain computation is a useful design.  
- Ablation evidence supports the importance of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Weak comparative evaluation: lacks systematic quantitative comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., policy search, advanced cloning, MPC details missing).  
- Limited experimental analysis: small reported training set (100–200 trials) raises data-efficiency and fairness concerns for baselines.  
- Missing runtime/performance metrics: no FPS, CPU/GPU usage, or scaling analysis for interactive use.  
- Clarity issues in math and notation (Section 3.2/time index inconsistencies) hinder understanding of feedback gain derivation.  
- Insufficient CIO implementation details and undefined terms in key equations force reliance on external references.  
- Confusing training description: Algorithm 1 (synchronous alternating) vs. text describing asynchronous distributed implementation without discussing convergence differences.  
- Reproducibility concern: lack of public code for a complex implementation.  
- Algorithmic questions: how initial trajectories X_i are generated pre-policy and whether trajectories were optimized without the regression cost initially.  
- Hyperparameter question: use of same proximal regularizer eta for both trajectory and parameter updates lacks justification (may need different scales).  
- Ablation suggestion: separate evaluations for sensory noise vs. hidden-unit noise to isolate their contributions.  
- Terminology/clarity: conflate different forms of "noise injection" and make debatable analogy to denoising autoencoders; proofreading and notational consistency needed.  
- Task detail missing: unclear whether target includes orientation and how orientation is incorporated.  
- Future work idea: consider annealing the regression weight alpha during training as a curriculum to balance bootstrapping and optimizer freedom.
