- Proposes a novel interleaved method training RNN controllers to mimic Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs, improving over naive supervised learning.
- Uses noise injection during training to improve generalization to new situations.
- Demonstrates control across diverse morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) with a single network architecture.
- Produces stable and realistic locomotion behaviors suitable for robotics and animation.
- Builds on established trajectory optimization and neural network techniques in a principled way.
- Paper is generally well-written and explains core concepts clearly, though some sections lack detail.
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only ~100–200 trials), raising concerns about robustness and generalization.
- Comparative evaluation is weak: lacks systematic comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., MPC with citation, DDPG, TRPO).
- Reproducibility is hindered by the absence of publicly available code.
- Mathematical exposition (Section 3, Eq. 6, Section 3.2 notation) is unclear and relies on external references.
- Some algorithmic choices (e.g., constant η in Algorithm 1) are not justified or explored.
- Contains minor writing and notation issues (typos, word choices) that reduce polish.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach bridging trajectory optimization and NN learning, broad generality, and practical, stable behaviors.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, weak comparisons, lack of code, and unclear math presentation.
- Overall assessment: promising and well-executed but insufficient evaluation and clarity; recommendation: Weak Reject pending expanded experiments, stronger baselines, and code release.
