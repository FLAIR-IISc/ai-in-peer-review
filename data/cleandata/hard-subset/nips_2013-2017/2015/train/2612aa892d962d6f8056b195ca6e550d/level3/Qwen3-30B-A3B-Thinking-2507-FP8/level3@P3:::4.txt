

# Review: Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks

This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to function as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse complex dynamical systems, including swimming, flying, and various walking behaviors. The authors propose a joint learning approach that combines trajectory optimization with neural network policy learning, enabling interactive control without requiring motion capture or task-specific features. The key insight is decoupling the optimization into alternating subproblems (trajectory optimization and neural network regression), allowing reuse of existing methods while achieving impressive results across multiple character types.

The paper builds upon recent work in trajectory optimization (Mordatch & Todorov 2013, Levine & Koltun 2014) and neural network control (Levine et al. 2014, Huh & Todorov 2013), but introduces a practical approach for combining these techniques. The authors demonstrate that their method successfully generates stable, realistic behaviors for a wide range of characters with the same architecture, which is a significant advancement over previous work limited to 2D environments.

**Strengths:**
- The technical approach is highly sophisticated, with careful implementation details about the distributed training architecture, noise injection, and feedback gain calculation.
- The method clearly outperforms naive neural network training (two orders of magnitude better), demonstrating the value of their joint optimization approach.
- The results are impressive and well-documented across multiple character types (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds) with consistent performance.
- The paper is well-written and well-organized, with appropriate detail for reproducibility and helpful cross-references.
- The authors provide a clear explanation of the advantages of sensory noise injection and joint learning, with convincing empirical evidence.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper lacks novel theoretical insights, with the core innovation being a practical application of existing techniques rather than a fundamental methodological contribution.
- The motivation for the extra optimization step in Section 7 is unclear; the reviewer expected the policy to run without additional optimization, and it's not well-explained why re-optimization is necessary.
- The reviewer questions whether residual trajectory cost or policy regression error necessitates re-optimization, and whether dynamics would prevent physical implausibility without the additional optimization.
- The paper states "similar to recent approaches" (line 153) without providing specific references for this claim.
- A typo appears at line 89 ("to unify").

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with thorough experimental validation across multiple character types. The implementation details are sufficient for reproduction, and the results are well-supported by empirical evidence. The authors appropriately acknowledge the limitations of their approach and provide ablation studies demonstrating the importance of their key components.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized. The methodology is explained with sufficient detail for an expert reader to reproduce the results. The figures effectively illustrate the key concepts and results, and the supplementary video provides compelling visual evidence of the method's capabilities.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel combination of trajectory optimization and neural network policy learning for interactive character control. While the individual components (trajectory optimization, neural networks) are well-established, their specific integration and application to diverse 3D character control represents a significant practical advancement. However, the novelty is more in the application and engineering than in theoretical innovation.

**Significance:** The results demonstrate a substantial improvement over naive neural network training for interactive character control. The ability to use a single architecture for diverse character types (swimming, flying, walking) with different morphologies is a meaningful contribution to the field. The method has clear applications in animation, robotics, and biomechanics, and the authors provide a compelling argument for its potential impact. However, the significance may be somewhat domain-limited to character control rather than broader machine learning applications.

**Overall Assessment:** This is a strong technical contribution with impressive empirical results that demonstrates a practical solution to a challenging problem in character control. While the novelty is primarily in the application of existing techniques rather than theoretical innovation, the results are compelling and the engineering is sophisticated. The paper is well-written and well-organized, with clear evidence of the method's advantages over alternatives.

The paper is recommended for acceptance, though the reviewer notes that the significance may be limited to the specific domain of character control. The technical approach is sound, the results are impressive, and the paper provides sufficient detail for reproduction. The minor weaknesses (motivation for Section 7, line 153 reference, typo) are easily addressable in revision.

The authors have successfully demonstrated that a joint optimization approach combining trajectory optimization with neural network learning can produce stable, realistic interactive control for diverse complex characters, representing a significant practical advancement in the field.
