- Trains RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse high-DOF contact-rich systems without motion capture or task-specific state machines.  
- Alternates Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with policy regression, embedding local feedback via a(s+ε) ≈ a + As ε.  
- Computes As (local feedback gains) efficiently by reusing the CIO Gauss–Newton inverse (Woodbury/perturbation) instead of a separate LQG pass.  
- Injects Gaussian input noise and hidden-activation noise; uses a quadratic penalty R and proximal block-alternating optimization to couple trajectory and policy learning.  
- At test time the policy outputs desired velocities, recurrent-state updates, and contact forces; next state obtained via a one-step (T=1) soft dynamics solve enabling 20 Hz control.  
- Advances prior guided policy search and trajectory-optimization-plus-function-approximation work by using CIO for 3D contact behaviors and embedding optimal feedback gains.  
- Methodological strength: elegant soft coupling and practical proximal penalty formulation that works asynchronously.  
- Robustness strength: embedding local feedback gains into supervised targets reduces covariate shift and improves closed-loop robustness.  
- Engineering strength: clear algorithmic description, GPU acceleration, and effective distributed/asynchronous implementation.  
- Experimental strength: broad set of 3D characters (swimmer, bird, bipeds, quadrupeds) learned with a single architecture and emergent gaits.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well written, with justified derivations and informative ablations.  
- Insight: qualitative comparison to MPC suggests joint optimization regularizes solutions toward simpler, more robust policies.  
- Weakness — limited quantitative evaluation: few metrics and missing head-to-head comparisons to GPS/TRPO and model-free baselines.  
- Weakness — modeling/transfer concerns: soft dynamics enforcement and action parameterization (including contact forces) raise physical realism and hardware transfer questions.  
- Weakness — lack of theoretical guarantees: no convergence/stability proofs and sensitivity to hyperparameters (λ, α, η) not analyzed.  
- Weakness — incomplete ablations: the specific contribution of feedback-gain embedding vs. denoising/noise is not isolated quantitatively.  
- Weakness — runtime/robustness at execution: per-step T=1 solve adds runtime cost and robustness to larger disturbances isn’t fully characterized.  
- Quality judgment: technically sound; feedback-gain reuse is insightful and the stochastic proximal alternating scheme is empirically convergent.  
- Reproducibility/clout: paper provides enough detail for reproduction given similar physics/CIO; figures and video support claims.  
- Originality: novel and effective combination, with distinctive contribution being embedding and efficient computation of optimal feedback gains.  
- Significance: high — advances robust closed-loop policy learning for complex dynamics and is likely influential for graphics and robotics.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid methodology, diverse results without heuristics, strong ablations, excellent presentation.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited benchmarks, action/dynamics choices limiting hardware claims, and missing theoretical guarantees.  
- Author suggestions: add quantitative metrics and comparisons, isolate feedback-gain ablation, report T=1 runtime and robustness to larger perturbations, discuss real-robot transfer and hyperparameter sensitivity, and release code/pseudocode/CIO settings.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, significant work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
