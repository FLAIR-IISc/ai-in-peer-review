- Trains RNN policies by alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) with policy regression and embeds local feedback gains into supervision.  
- Supervises the NN to match nominal actions and first-order responses to sensor perturbations using a perturbation analysis that reuses the Gauss–Newton Hessian.  
- Softly couples trajectory optimization and policy learning with a quadratic penalty on policy–trajectory mismatch to bias trajectories toward simpler, robust policies.  
- Implements the method in an asynchronous, distributed system and demonstrates real-time controllers on swimming, flying, and varied legged morphologies.  
- Method builds on guided policy search and LQR/LQG ideas but advances the state of the art via gain-augmented supervision, soft coupling, and broad 3D demonstrations.  
- Elegant derivation using Woodbury and reuse of CXX is practically important and technically strong.  
- Ablations (no joint optimization, no noise) substantiate robustness claims.  
- Single-step optimization with implicit dynamics is a sensible execution model.  
- Lacks formal stability or robustness guarantees.  
- Limited quantitative evaluation beyond reconstruction error and few direct comparisons with strong RL baselines (TRPO, modern GPS variants, off-policy actor-critic) or MPC with explicit feedback.  
- Exceptionally clear, well-organized paper with reproducible algorithm, architecture, and distributed implementation; compelling figures/videos.  
- Original and useful idea: embedding trajectory-local feedback gains into NN targets and using soft coupling as a regularizer that promotes cyclic, predictable behaviors.  
- High significance: integrates trajectory optimization, control, and neural policies across diverse tasks and morphologies; likely influential for animation and robotics.  
- Concerns about transfer to real robots due to soft dynamics constraints (root forces) and absence of sim-to-real tests.  
- Computational cost and data efficiency are not systematically characterized.  
- Suggested additions: comparative baselines (TRPO/GPS/MPC-with-gains), robustness metrics (perturbation sweeps), sensitivity analysis of α/λ/η, and discussion of deployment without root forces.
