- Combines supervised learning with contact-invariant trajectory optimization to train neural controllers for diverse 3D characters.  
- Interleaves trajectory optimization and supervised learning, injects noise during training, and uses optimization to obtain feedback gains.  
- Single network architecture controls varied morphologies (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with stable, realistic behaviors.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Technically sound integration enabling real-time control; extends prior 2D work to 3D.  
- Method requires full model knowledge (Eq. 6), limiting applicability to real-world robotics; paper under-addresses model-free extensions.  
- Structural imbalance: Section 4 (policy regression) is too short while Section 6 (distributed training) is overly long; recurrent units in abstract are not explained.  
- Misleadingly calls the network "stochastic" despite the network being deterministic and stochasticity arising from injected training noise.  
- Lacks comparisons to modern RL baselines (e.g., TRPO, PPO), reducing benchmark significance.  
- Claim of “universal synthesis methods” is overstated given the full-model-knowledge constraint.  
- Computational cost (≈2.5 hours training) raises practical concerns.  
- Recommendation: major revision to clarify model-knowledge limits, rebalance/explain Sections 4 and 6 (recurrent units), correct "stochastic" wording, and add RL comparisons.  
- Pro: strong empirical results, effective noise injection and joint optimization, novel application of trajectory optimization with neural nets for 3D character control.  
- Con: limited applicability from model-knowledge requirement, structural/presentation issues, misleading terminology, and insufficient RL benchmarking.
