- Presents a method combining trajectory optimization (CIO) with recurrent neural networks to produce near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse simulated characters (swimmers, fliers, bipeds, quadrupeds) without motion capture or task-specific features.

- Builds on prior trajectory-optimization and neural controller work (notably Levine & Koltun [9] and Mordatch & Todorov [11]) but does not clearly justify how it improves over them.

- Experimental results are strong: a single network architecture controls diverse 3D behaviors, handles unexpected task changes and perturbations, and shows realistic, stable motions.

- Distributed training architecture is well-designed and scalable.

- Ablation studies convincingly show the necessity of joint optimization, noise injection, and trajectory re-optimization.

- Practical contribution: enables interactive control without motion capture or engineered features; video results likely reinforce quality.

- Major weakness: unclear positioning and insufficient comparative analysis against key prior works (learning speed, supported morphologies, runtime cost, robustness).

- Claim about producing “softer feedback gains” (parameter λ) is vague and lacks definition, justification, and concrete examples of issues with ill-conditioned gains.

- Notation around states and costs (lines 226–240) is confusing; similar symbols need disambiguation.

- Exposition issue: discussion of why LQG was not used should be relocated to follow the proposed strategy for clarity.

- Insufficient discussion of prior neural network policy approximation work (e.g., [9]) and unclear novelty relative to those methods.

- Overall: technically sound with compelling experiments but reads more like applying deep learning to existing trajectory-optimization techniques than a fundamentally new method; needs clearer novelty and systematic comparisons.

- Recommendation: weak reject, contingent on substantial revisions to clarify novelty, add comparative analyses, fix technical explanations/notation, and reposition the LQG discussion.
