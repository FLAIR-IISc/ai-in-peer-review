- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree learning method that jointly optimizes all split functions and leaf parameters with a global objective.
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.
- Introduces a convex-concave upper bound on the tree's empirical loss as a surrogate objective.
- Demonstrates promising empirical results that outperform greedy decision tree baselines on several classification benchmarks.
- Well-motivated and clearly presented approach addressing limitations of traditional greedy induction.
- Provides a thorough analysis of the optimization problem and an efficient algorithm to minimize the surrogate objective.
- Experiments convincingly show improved test performance and reduced overfitting for non-greedy trees.
- Needs clearer discussion of the requirement for full model knowledge from the outset (reference to Equation 6).
- Sections 4 and 6 are imbalanced in content and discussion; Section 4 omits mention of recurrent units referenced in the abstract.
- Questionable characterization of a neural network as stochastic when the network appears deterministic.
- Lacks sufficient context and motivation for using stochastic gradient descent and introducing a regularization parameter.
- Suggests restructuring the paper to better balance sections and expand discussion of assumptions and methods.
- Recommends discussing potential applications and extensions, e.g., learning sparse split functions or applying kernel methods to linear split parameters.
- Overall, significant contribution with potential for strong impact pending revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
