This paper proposes a novel approach for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for complex characters. The method combines trajectory optimization with policy learning, allowing for interactive control of diverse characters. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various tasks, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and methodology. The use of cross-references and section headings helps to guide the reader through the text. The authors also provide sufficient detail about their approach, including the optimization algorithm and the neural network architecture.

One of the key strengths of this paper is its technical sophistication. The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of the underlying mathematics and algorithms, and their approach is well-motivated by the need to address the challenges of controlling complex characters. The use of trajectory optimization and policy learning is a clever combination that allows for efficient and effective control.

The results presented in the paper are impressive, with the authors demonstrating the ability to control a range of characters in various tasks. The use of a single network architecture and parameters to create all controllers is a significant achievement, and the authors' approach shows promise for applications in robotics, animation, and gaming.

The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including a comparison with other methods and an analysis of the effects of different parameters on the performance of their algorithm. The use of a biped walking task as a test case is a good choice, as it allows for a clear evaluation of the algorithm's performance.

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of novel theoretical insights. While the authors' approach is technically sophisticated, it is not clear whether it represents a significant advance over existing methods. However, the authors do provide a clear explanation of the advantages of their approach, including the ability to reuse existing methods and the benefits of jointly learning trajectories and policies.

The authors also provide a good discussion of the potential applications of their approach, including robotics, animation, and gaming. The use of their algorithm for interactive character control has significant implications for these fields, and the authors do a good job of highlighting the potential benefits.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide sufficient detail about their approach and methodology, and the use of section headings and cross-references helps to guide the reader through the text. The paper is also well-organized, with a clear introduction, methodology, results, and discussion.

Overall, this is a strong paper that demonstrates a novel approach for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers. The authors provide a clear explanation of their approach, a thorough evaluation of their algorithm, and a good discussion of the potential applications of their work.
