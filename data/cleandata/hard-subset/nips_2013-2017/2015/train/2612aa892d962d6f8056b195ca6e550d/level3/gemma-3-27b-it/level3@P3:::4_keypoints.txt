- Proposes interleaving trajectory optimization (CIO) with neural policy learning to train controllers for complex 3D characters.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across diverse characters and locomotion tasks, advancing beyond prior 2D-focused work.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and supplemented by a useful video showcasing motion quality.
- Injecting sensory noise during training improves robustness and generalization of the learned controllers.
- Jointly optimizing trajectories and policy outperforms training on a static trajectory dataset (Figure 2).
- Re-optimizing trajectories at each timestep enables larger timesteps for real-time control but increases computational cost.
- Method is modular: the same network architecture controls varied morphologies, a notable achievement.
- Reviewer unsure about the true novelty and significance due to limited domain expertise and reliance on existing techniques.
- Paper needs a more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art character-control methods and clearer quantification of improvements over prior work [9, 11].
- Motivation for the per-timestep re-optimization (Section 7) should be elaborated, including whether residual costs truly necessitate it.
- Clarify whether system dynamics alone could prevent physically implausible motions without re-optimization.
- Line 153’s phrase “similar to recent approaches” requires specific citations.
- Provide a clearer justification for keeping parameter updates small in the block-alternation scheme.
- Minor typo at Line 89 ("to unify.") should be fixed.
- Overall recommendation: positive—recommend acceptance contingent on addressing minor clarifications about motivation, references, and comparisons.
