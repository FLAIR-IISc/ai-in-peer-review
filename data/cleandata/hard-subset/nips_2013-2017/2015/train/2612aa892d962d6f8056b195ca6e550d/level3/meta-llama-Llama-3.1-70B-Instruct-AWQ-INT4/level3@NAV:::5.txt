This paper presents a novel approach to training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse complex characters. The method combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and using the trajectory optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by learning controllers for a range of three-dimensional characters, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking tasks.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its components. The use of figures and tables helps to illustrate the concepts and results. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and the advantages of their approach over existing methods.

However, there are some areas that require improvement. Firstly, the authors assume that the reader is familiar with the technical details of neural networks and trajectory optimization. While the paper provides a good overview of the method, it would be helpful to include more background information on these topics, especially for readers who are not experts in these areas.

Secondly, the authors could provide more details on the implementation of their method, such as the specific neural network architecture used, the number of hidden layers, and the activation functions employed. This information would be useful for readers who want to replicate the results or apply the method to their own problems.

Thirdly, the authors could provide more analysis on the results, such as the performance of the controllers in different scenarios, the effect of noise injection on the training process, and the comparison with other methods. This would help to strengthen the paper's contributions and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the method's capabilities.

Lastly, it is worth noting that the paper assumes full model knowledge, as stated in equation 6. This assumption may not always be valid in practice, and the authors could discuss the implications of this assumption and potential ways to address it.

In terms of the conference guidelines, the paper meets the criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method, and the results demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach. The paper also contributes to the field of neural network control and trajectory optimization, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of related work.
