- Proposes combining offline trajectory optimization with supervised learning to train neural-network controllers for real-time, interactive control of simulated characters.  
- Extends prior optimizer+function-approximation work to diverse, complex 3D morphologies and locomotion tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal) in a unified framework.  
- Empirical strength: produces stable, realistic, interactive controllers across varied morphologies with a single general-purpose algorithm.  
- Novel contribution: uses the trajectory optimizer to compute optimal feedback gains (Sec. 3.2), yielding richer training data for robustness to perturbations.  
- Major assumption: method requires full knowledge of model dynamics and a differentiable physics simulator (Eq. 6); this should be stated up front.  
- Organization imbalance: Section 4 (neural policy) is too brief relative to Section 6 (distributed training), giving the impression engineering, not learning, is the main contribution.  
- Omission: Section 4 does not clarify the role or details of the recurrent units mentioned in the abstract.  
- Terminology imprecision: the policy is called “stochastic” though the network appears deterministic and only the training data is stochastic; needs clarification.  
- Overall: promising and significant results, but the manuscript needs substantial restructuring for clarity, balanced focus, and precise framing of assumptions and contributions.
