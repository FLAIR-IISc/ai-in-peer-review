- Presents a method combining trajectory optimization with recurrent neural networks and noise injection to learn robust control policies for diverse dynamical systems.  
- Addresses the hard problem of automated, real-time controllers for complex, stable movements in high-dimensional continuous spaces.  
- Core idea: use Contact-Invariant Optimization to generate trajectories and learn feedback gains rather than merely imitating trajectories.  
- Incorporates stochastic policy training and a distributed optimization architecture for robustness and scalability.  
- Demonstrates impressive locomotion results across swimming, flying, and legged characters without motion capture or hand-crafted controllers.  
- Technical formulation is clear, well-motivated, and includes justified components (soft constraints, feedback gains, proximal regularization).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and supported by helpful figures and supplementary video.  
- The combination of trajectory optimization and neural networks is novel and generalizes across morphologies and tasks.  
- Results are significant, achieving stable 3D locomotion with broad implications for robotics, animation, and biomechanics.  
- Evaluation is thorough, with useful ablations (noise injection, static training) and qualitative comparison to MPC.  
- Main weakness: high computational cost and reliance on distributed/cloud resources, limiting accessibility.  
- Needs more detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (noise levels, regularization, network sizes).  
- Limited direct, systematic comparison to state-of-the-art direct reinforcement learning methods on the same tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, is technically strong, clear, achieves state-of-the-art results, and has broad impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: training cost, insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and limited RL baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: outstanding, significant contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; strong acceptance recommendation.
