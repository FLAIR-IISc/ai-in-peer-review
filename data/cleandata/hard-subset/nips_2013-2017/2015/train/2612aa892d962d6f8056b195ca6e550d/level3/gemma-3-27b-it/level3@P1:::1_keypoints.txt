- Main contribution: Jointly optimizes trajectory optimization (CIO) and recurrent neural network policies to produce near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems.  
- Key technique: Incorporates optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization into the network training signal to improve robustness to noise and regression errors.  
- Scope and results: Demonstrates stable, realistic 3D locomotion across varied body morphologies using a single network architecture.  
- Methodological grounding: Builds on trajectory optimization and control literature, positioning itself between offline optimization and online neural control methods.  
- Technical soundness: Rigorous formulation, clear algorithms, and ablation studies show the importance of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Clarity and presentation: Exceptionally well-written, organized, and supplemented by a compelling video demonstration.  
- Originality: Novel combination of trajectory optimization, neural networks, and feedback-gain integration enabling previously intractable 3D problems.  
- Significance: High impact for robotics, animation, and biomechanics due to generality, scalability, and efficient data generation via optimization.  
- Weakness — computational cost: Reliance on distributed/cloud computing and long training times may hinder adoption; more complexity analysis is needed.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Limited exploration of sensitivity to noise levels, penalty weights, and regularization; deeper analysis would clarify robustness.  
- Weakness — limited comparisons: Lacks systematic quantitative comparison to direct neural controller training methods, weakening claims of superiority.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a hard problem with a novel, well-validated approach, clear presentation, and broad potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Concerns about compute requirements, hyperparameter robustness, and insufficient direct-method comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: Outstanding contribution with minor weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
