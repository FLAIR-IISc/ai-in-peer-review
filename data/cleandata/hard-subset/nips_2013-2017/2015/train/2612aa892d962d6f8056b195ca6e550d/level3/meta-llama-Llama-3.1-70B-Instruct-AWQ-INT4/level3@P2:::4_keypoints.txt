- Proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning for interactive control of complex characters.
- Decouples the problem into alternating subproblems, enabling reuse of existing methods and practical effectiveness.
- Demonstrates high technical sophistication in concept and implementation with impressive, stable, realistic behaviors across tasks.
- Joint learning of trajectories and policy, and training with sensory noise, are shown to be advantageous.
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and detailed with useful cross-references.
- Lacks novel theoretical insights according to the reviewer.
- Reviewer uncertain about the work’s novelty due to limited domain familiarity.
- Broader impact and significance are difficult for the reviewer to judge.
- Questions the need for the additional per-timestep optimization step in Section 7 and whether policy residuals force re-optimization.
- Unclear whether dynamics constraints guarantee physical plausibility despite re-optimizing at each timestep.
- Re-optimizing each timestep allows use of larger timesteps, which the reviewer acknowledges.

- Overall positive impression of results and optimization scheme, but uncertainty remains about novelty and significance.
