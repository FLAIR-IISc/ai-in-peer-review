Summary: This paper trains recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers by alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) with policy regression. A key idea is to embed local feedback gains into the supervision: the NN is trained not only to match nominal actions but also their first-order response to sensor perturbations, obtained efficiently via a perturbation analysis that reuses the Gauss–Newton Hessian. The coupling between trajectory optimization and policy learning is made soft via a quadratic penalty on policy–trajectory mismatch, enabling the optimizer to steer trajectories toward policies that are simpler and more robust than raw MPC/CIO solutions. The method is implemented in an asynchronous, distributed setting and demonstrated on swimming, flying, and diverse legged morphologies, yielding real-time controllers. This work is closely related to guided policy search and trajectory-optimization–supervised RL (e.g., Levine & Koltun 2014; Mordatch & Todorov 2014) and to LQR/LQG-based feedback design, but advances the state of the art with its gain-augmented supervision, soft coupling, and breadth of 3D demonstrations.

Quality: Technically strong. The derivation of feedback gains via Woodbury and reuse of CXX is elegant and practically important; ablations (no joint optimization, no noise) substantiate claims about robustness. The execution model (single-step optimization with implicit dynamics) is sensible. Some limitations remain: no formal stability/robustness guarantees; limited quantitative evaluation (beyond reconstruction error) and few head-to-heads with strong RL baselines (TRPO, modern GPS variants, off-policy actor-critic) or MPC with explicit feedback.

Clarity: Exceptionally clear and well-organized. The algorithm, architecture, and distributed implementation are described in enough detail to reproduce. Figures/video reportedly compelling.

Originality: The embedding of trajectory-local feedback gains into NN targets is a novel and useful twist on GPS-style supervision. The soft policy–trajectory coupling acts as a regularizer that biases toward cyclic, predictable behaviors—an insightful observation.

Significance: High. The approach integrates graphics-style trajectory optimization, control, and neural policies across varied morphologies and tasks, and the results are practically inspiring. Likely to influence both animation and robotics communities.

Pros for acceptance:
- Elegant incorporation of feedback gains into policy learning; principled robustness to disturbances and regression error.
- Soft coupling of trajectory and policy optimization that regularizes toward simpler, more deployable controllers.
- Strong, diverse demonstrations in challenging 3D settings with ablations.
- Clear writing and careful engineering; practical parallelization.

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited quantitative benchmarks vs. TRPO/GPS/actor-critic and MPC controllers.
- No formal stability/robustness analysis or sensitivity study (α, λ, η).
- Reliance on soft dynamics constraints (root forces) may limit robotics transfer; no sim-to-real tests.
- Computational cost and data efficiency not systematically characterized.

Suggestions: Add comparative baselines (TRPO/GPS/MPC-with-gains), report robustness metrics (perturbation sweeps), sensitivity to penalty/gain parameters, and discuss deployment without root forces.
