- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers by combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with supervised learning.
- Demonstrates stable, realistic locomotion for diverse behaviors (swimming, flying, walking) and multiple body types in simulation.
- Uses the optimizer to generate training data and to refine feedback gains; includes noise injection for robustness.
- Experimental results are visually and empirically strong, showing versatility of the approach.
- Paper’s positioning within trajectory optimization and learning-from-demonstration literature is underdeveloped and needs stronger contextualization.
- Claim of solving “substantially harder problems” (e.g., 3D locomotion) is unsupported without direct comparison to prior methods/benchmarks.
- Lacks quantitative comparisons to relevant prior work (e.g., Abbeel & Levine) on metrics like learning speed, body variety, cost, and robustness.
- Explanation of “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” is vague; needs clarification of why that is problematic.
- Rationale for not using Linear Quadratic Gaussian (LQG) control should be presented after describing the proposed strategy for better flow.
- Notational ambiguity (lines 226–240) — similar symbols and definitions (s(X), s̄) are confusing and need fixing.
- Statement that the method produces “softer” feedback gains requires a precise definition of “softer” and evidence.
- Need justification for why perturbing the initial state improves performance.
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions — strengthen related-work positioning and address technical clarifications to solidify the contribution.
