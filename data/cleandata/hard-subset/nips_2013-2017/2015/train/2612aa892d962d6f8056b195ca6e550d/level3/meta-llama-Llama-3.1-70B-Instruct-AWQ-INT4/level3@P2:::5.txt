This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The authors propose a novel approach that combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and training for unexpected changes in the task specification. The paper demonstrates promising results, but it requires significant restructuring to improve clarity and flow.

The main idea of the paper is to use a neural network to learn from a trajectory optimizer and generate similar behaviors online. The authors introduce a neural network control policy that maps a sensory state of the character to an optimal action, and they use a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm to jointly find a collection of optimal trajectories and a policy that can reconstruct the sense and action pairs of all trajectories. The paper also discusses the importance of injecting noise during training to produce more robust movement strategies and to reduce overfitting.

The strengths of the paper include its novel approach to combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization, its ability to handle complex dynamical systems and tasks, and its promising results. The paper also provides a detailed description of the neural network architecture and the optimization algorithm used.
