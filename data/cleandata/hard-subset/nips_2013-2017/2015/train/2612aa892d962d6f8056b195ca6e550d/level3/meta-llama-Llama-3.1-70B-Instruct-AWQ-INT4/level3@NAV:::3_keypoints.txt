- Proposes training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers by combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization, CIO).
- Introduces alternating joint training and noise injection (data augmentation + additive hidden noise) to improve robustness and generalization.
- Experiments show realistic, stable control policies for diverse characters (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped).
- Main contributions are the alternating training scheme and noise-injection methods that outperform straightforward supervised fits to CIO.
- Experimental evaluation is limited by a small training set (100–200 trials, 5 branched segments), weakening generalization claims.
- Comparative evaluation is weak—only a brief MPC comparison and no systematic comparisons to other baselines or methods.
- Mathematical presentation is mostly clear but some parts (CIO step, Eq. 6) lack definitions and needed detail.
- Section 3.2 is confusing due to notation issues, making the optimal-action-for-noisy-inputs procedure unclear.
- Strengths: generates realistic/stable controllers across diverse models and improves robustness via noise injection.
- Weaknesses: limited experiments, weak comparative evaluation, and unclear mathematical notation in places.
- Originality: novel combination of supervised learning, trajectory optimization, and noise injection, but builds on existing methods (CIO, MPC).
- Significance: potential to automate interactive real-time controller design for robotics, animation, and gaming.
- Overall: well-executed work with promising results but needs broader experiments, clearer comparisons, and clearer mathematical exposition.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, robust training improvements, demonstrated diverse character control.
- Arguments against acceptance: small training set, weak comparisons, unclear math, and reliance on prior methods rather than entirely new concepts.
