- Introduces a distributed stochastic optimization that jointly trains a neural policy and optimizes trajectories via Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) for real-time 3D controllers.  
- Embeds optimal feedback gains into the framework to improve closed-loop stability while reusing trajectory optimization computations.  
- Network learns to approximate dynamics under an implicit policy (not outputting a policy directly); actions are represented as velocities for closed-loop control.  
- Avoids motion capture, task-specific features, and state machines, overcoming limitations of prior 2D-only methods.  
- Technically sound with rigorous validation across swimmers, flyers, bipeds, and quadrupeds in 3D.  
- Ablations show joint trajectory-policy optimization outperforms static training by ~100x.  
- Noise injection (sensory and hidden-unit) is essential to prevent overfitting and achieve robustness.  
- A 3-layer, 250-unit network gives the best complexity–accuracy tradeoff per experiments.  
- Feedback gain derivation is novel, efficient, and avoids LQG ill-conditioning.  
- Implementation details and Algorithm 1 are clear and reproducible; figures and supplementary video strongly support results.  
- Original fusion of CIO with neural policy training and embedded gains is novel for high-dimensional 3D locomotion.  
- Demonstrated significance for scalable character animation, robotics, and sensorimotor modeling without hand-crafted engineering.  
- Comparison to MPC and prior work highlights advantages: stable, cyclical, purposeful behaviors absent in MPC and previous neural controllers.  
- Minor limitation: lacks analysis of failure modes under extreme perturbations.  
- Practical drawback: nontrivial compute cost (≈2.5 hours on AWS), but considered acceptable given results.  
- Strong recommendation for acceptance based on methodological contribution, empirical rigor, and cross-disciplinary relevance.
