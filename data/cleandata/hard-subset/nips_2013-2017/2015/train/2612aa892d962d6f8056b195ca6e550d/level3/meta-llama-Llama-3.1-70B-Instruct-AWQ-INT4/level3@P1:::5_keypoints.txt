- Novel approach to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped).
- Method combines supervised learning, trajectory optimization, noise injection, and uses the optimizer to obtain feedback gains as well as actions.
- Main contribution: a universal synthesis method applicable across behaviors, morphologies, changing objectives, and perturbations.
- Demonstrated a single neural controller producing stable, realistic behaviors across multiple characters and tasks.
- Paper is well-written; equations and pseudocode clarify technical components.
- Experimental results are impressive and include thorough evaluations and comparisons.
- Structural imbalance: sections 4 and 6 are disproportionate in coverage and length, with section 4 particularly brief.
- Section 4 omits mention of the recurrent units referenced in the abstract, causing potential confusion.
- Claiming the network is "stochastic" is unclear — the network seems deterministic despite stochastic training data.
- Paper needs more technical detail in section 4, especially about recurrent unit architecture and implementation.
- More context is needed on relevance to broader robotics/control and applicability to real-world problems.
- Overall technically sound and significant; with revisions addressing clarity and detail it could be a strong contribution.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, improved performance over prior work, and technical soundness.
- Arguments for rejection: structural/clarity issues, insufficient technical detail on recurrent units, and limited real-world context.
