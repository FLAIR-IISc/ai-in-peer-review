- Proposes a novel non-greedy decision tree learning method that jointly optimizes split functions at all levels and leaf parameters using a global objective.  
- Establishes a connection between decision tree optimization and structured prediction with latent variables.  
- Derives a convex-concave upper bound on the tree’s empirical loss for optimization.  
- Presents a fast variant of loss-augmented inference to reduce computational complexity.  
- Empirical results on several classification benchmarks show the non-greedy trees outperform greedy decision tree baselines.  
- Strength: technically sound, original, and well-motivated approach with thorough problem analysis.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition that is easy to follow.  
- Weakness: potential scalability issues on very large datasets due to loss-augmented inference complexity.  
- Mitigation: authors propose a faster inference variant to improve scalability.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation could be more extensive and include comparisons to more state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: method may require significant computational resources and tuning, possibly limiting practical adoption.  
- Significance: has potential to improve accuracy and robustness of decision trees and extend to random forests and boosting.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, significant, technically sound, and empirically effective.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scalability concerns, limited experimental breadth, and implementation/tuning complexity.  
- Overall recommendation: paper makes a significant contribution and should be accepted.
