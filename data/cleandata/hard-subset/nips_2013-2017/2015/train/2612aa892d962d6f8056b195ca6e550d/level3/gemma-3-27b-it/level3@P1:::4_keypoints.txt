- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers for complex dynamical systems (swimming, flying, 3D locomotion) by combining trajectory optimization (CIO) and neural networks.  
- Extends prior neural-network control work by successfully tackling significantly more complex, three-dimensional locomotion problems.  
- Main technical contribution: an alternating optimization scheme that decomposes the problem into trajectory optimization and neural-network regression for efficient reuse of existing tools.  
- Training includes noise injection guided by optimal feedback gains to improve robustness and generalization.  
- Uses a distributed/cloud GPU training architecture, demonstrating practical scalability for computationally demanding problems.  
- Empirical results (visuals/video) show stable, realistic behaviors across diverse character morphologies and tasks; ablation studies validate joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Technically sophisticated and well-implemented, but reads more as a substantial engineering achievement than a theoretical breakthrough.  
- Lacks deeper discussion on how this work advances or differs from reinforcement learning and stochastic optimal control approaches.  
- The necessity of the runtime re-optimization step (Section 7) is not fully justified—unclear why the learned policy alone is insufficient or whether regression error mandates re-optimization.  
- Minor issues: typo on line 89, request for specific citations around line 153, and a somewhat counterintuitive explanation for keeping parameter updates small.  
- Recommendation: accept—strong paper with compelling results, conditional on clarifying the runtime optimization rationale and adding requested citations.
