- Presents an RNN controller trained from CIO-generated optimal trajectories using alternating joint optimization, noise injection, and a method to extract feedback gains, applied to diverse characters.  
- Technically sound with strong experimental results and a compelling supplementary video demonstrating stability and realism across morphologies.  
- Ablation study convincingly shows the necessity of joint optimization and sensory/activation noise for generalization and runtime stability.  
- Claim that LQG produces "very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" is asserted but not sufficiently substantiated or exemplified.  
- Derivation of optimal feedback gains (Sec. 3.2) is hard to follow due to confusing notation; clearer symbols for reference vs. variable states are needed.  
- The phrase "produces softer feedback gains" is vague and should be defined quantitatively (e.g., gain matrix norm) with clearer intuition about its benefits.  
- Exposition order should be improved: discuss drawbacks of LQG before introducing the new feedback-gain calculation.  
- Core idea (trajectory optimization → expert data → policy learning) is similar to prior work (Levine & Koltun, Mordatch), so novelty is unclear beyond scaling to 3D.  
- The paper should more explicitly differentiate its contributions from prior work with quantitative comparisons (learning speed, supported characters, robustness, runtime cost).  
- Results are significant for automated controller synthesis in animation, games, and robotics, but impact is tempered by originality concerns.  
- Arguments for acceptance: high-quality, diverse 3D results from a unified method; controllers learned from scratch; strong ablation evidence.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient differentiation from closely related prior work, limited methodological novelty, and unclear technical justification for the feedback-gain claims.  
- Reviewer would reconsider after major revisions that clarify novelty, add explicit comparisons to related methods, and better justify/explain feedback-gain properties.
