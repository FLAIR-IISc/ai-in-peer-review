- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers that produce stable, realistic locomotion across diverse body morphologies.  
- Core idea: use trajectory optimization to generate training data so a neural policy can mimic the optimizer for online, interactive control.  
- Demonstrates strong results across swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal tasks using a single network architecture without task-specific features.  
- Builds on prior neural-control and trajectory-optimization work (notably Contact-Invariant Optimization) and extends optimizer-to-policy ideas from 2D to complex 3D systems.  
- Empirical strengths: impressive, stable behaviors shown in video; careful training procedures (noise injection, interleaved learning/optimization); practical distributed/GPU implementation details.  
- Insufficient contextualization in the literature and weak justification for the claim of solving “substantially harder” problems compared to prior work.  
- Lacks quantitative comparisons (e.g., learning speed, supported morphologies, runtime cost, robustness) and missing strong baselines such as Abbeel & Levine.  
- Motivation for deviating from standard LQG is unclear; issues with large/ill-conditioned gains and the perturbation method are not well explained.  
- Notation in Sec. 3.2 is confusing (bars vs squiggles); explanation of “softer” feedback gains and why initial-state perturbation is preferred needs clarification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: robust, scalable approach; impressive 3D results; useful implementation details; addresses data acquisition and overfitting issues.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor literature positioning, lack of quantitative comparisons, confusing technical exposition, and insufficient justification of the perturbation method.  
- Overall: solid experimental contribution demonstrating potential of combining trajectory optimization with neural nets for control; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve positioning, clarity, and justification.
