- Presents a method that interleaves trajectory optimization and supervised learning to train neural controllers for diverse 3D characters.  
- Core innovation: joint optimization of trajectories and a neural policy that learns state-action mappings and implicit dynamics with stochastic data injection for robustness.  
- Extends prior trajectory-optimization work to challenging 3D continuous control tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) where earlier methods were limited to 2D.  
- Empirical results show stable, realistic behaviors across diverse morphologies and outperform simpler direct-RL approaches (Section 8.1).  
- Integrates contact-invariant optimization (CIO) with neural networks to learn robust controllers, supported by ablation studies demonstrating performance gains.  
- Real-time execution framework is efficient and avoids storing trajectories (Section 7).  
- Critical omission: paper assumes full model knowledge (Eq. 6) but does not state this upfront, affecting reproducibility and scope.  
- Structural imbalance: distributed training (Section 6) is overly long relative to neural regression (Section 4), which is central to the method.  
- Missing detail: Section 4 fails to describe the recurrent units referenced in the abstract, undermining clarity on the architecture.  
- Misleading terminology: the network is described as "stochastic" (Line 405) despite being deterministic; only training data is stochastic and this should be clarified.  
- Recommendation: promising, novel, and technically sound but requires revisions—explicitly state full-model requirement, rebalance Sections 4–6, clarify recurrent units, and correct "stochastic" terminology.  
- Final verdict: Rebuttal + Revision; with corrections the paper would make a strong contribution.
