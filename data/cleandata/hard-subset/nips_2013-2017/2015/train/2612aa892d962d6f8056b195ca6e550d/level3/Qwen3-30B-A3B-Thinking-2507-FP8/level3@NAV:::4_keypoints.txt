- Proposes a joint learning framework alternating trajectory optimization and neural policy regression with sensory noise injection to train controllers without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Demonstrates interactive control of diverse complex 3D characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) where prior work was limited to 2D.  
- Technical execution and clarity are excellent, with well-motivated methodology and thorough ablation studies.  
- Implementation details (distributed training architecture, large 50ms timesteps) are strong and practically impressive.  
- Empirical results show large performance gains over naive neural network training (two orders of magnitude) and confirm the necessity of noise injection to prevent overfitting.  
- Lacks novel theoretical insights; contribution is primarily a practical combination and implementation of existing techniques.  
- Motivation and justification for the extra optimization step (Section 7) are unclear; reviewer questions need for re-optimization and potential physical implausibility due to dynamics.  
- Significance is practical and substantial for 3D locomotion tasks, but the claim of unifying disparate approaches may be overstated.  
- Paper is well-written with only minor issues (typo at line 89) and overall meets NIPS quality and clarity standards.
