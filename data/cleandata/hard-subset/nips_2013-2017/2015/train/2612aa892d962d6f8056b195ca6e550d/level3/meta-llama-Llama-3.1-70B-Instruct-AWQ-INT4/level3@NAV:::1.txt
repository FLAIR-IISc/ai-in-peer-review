This paper, "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks," presents a novel approach to training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for a wide range of dynamical systems and tasks. The authors propose embedding feedback gains into neural network policies to improve robustness to disturbances and regression error. They also introduce a softly coupled trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a cost penalty for deviation.

The paper is exceptionally clear, high-quality, and technically impressive. The authors successfully integrate graphics, control, and neural networks across varied morphologies and tasks, demonstrating trained feedback controllers on a diverse set of robots. The results are inspiring and represent a significant methodological contribution.

The strengths of the paper include its ability to unify disparate approaches to control architectures, its use of sophisticated parallelization to optimize tasks, and its demonstration of successful controllers on a range of complex characters. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including comparisons to other approaches and ablation studies.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it may be challenging to reproduce the results, as the authors rely on a custom GPU implementation and a specific distributed training architecture. However, the authors provide sufficient detail about their approach and implementation to make it possible for others to replicate their work.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, including trajectory optimization and neural network policy regression. The authors also introduce a new approach to computing optimal feedback gains, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The significance of the paper is high, as it addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be used to learn successful controllers for a wide range of complex characters, which has significant implications for robotics, animation, and gaming.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication. It presents a significant methodological contribution, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers.
* The authors demonstrate successful controllers on a diverse set of robots, including complex characters.
* The paper is exceptionally clear, high-quality, and technically impressive.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method, including comparisons to other approaches and ablation studies.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper may be challenging to reproduce, as the authors rely on a custom GPU implementation and a specific distributed training architecture.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational cost of their approach, which may be a concern for some readers.
