- Proposes an offline-to-online pipeline alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) and NN regression, with noise injection and local feedback gains for real-time character control.  
- Builds on CIO and guided policy search literature and contrasts with direct deep RL and MPC.  
- Strength: clear alternating joint optimization tying NN training to a trajectory optimizer with proximal regularization and stochastic perturbations.  
- Strength: novel, efficient perturbation-based computation of feedback gains from the CIO Hessian that avoids ill-conditioned LQG passes.  
- Strength: robustness from complementary noise mechanisms—noisy inputs with linearized action corrections and hidden-layer noise for regularization.  
- Strength: single architecture handles diverse 3D systems (swimmer, bird, bipeds, quadrupeds) producing realistic gaits without motion capture.  
- Strength: practical system with asynchronous distributed implementation, fast offline training, and interactive execution.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation size (100–200 trials, 5 branches each) undermines generalization claims.  
- Weakness: weak comparative evaluation—only brief, uncited MPC comparison and missing systematic baselines like guided policy search or TRPO.  
- Weakness: missing runtime and scaling measurements (per-step cost, CPU/GPU budgets, scaling with DOFs/contacts) crucial for real-time use.  
- Weakness: some mathematical exposition lacks details (missing definitions in Eq.6, gain-derivation notation issues), hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: asynchronous implementation in Sec.6 departs from Algorithm 1’s alternation but this divergence is discussed late and not analyzed.  
- Weakness: ablation studies insufficiently granular—sensory vs hidden noise should be separated and hyperparameter sensitivity (λ, α, η) explored.  
- Weakness: no code or data release and several notational typos reduce reproducibility.  
- Quality: technically sound with neat proximal terms and gain extraction, but generalization claims not fully supported by limited data and weak baselines.  
- Clarity need: define all symbols in Eq.6 and explain soft constraints implementation.  
- Clarity need: keep time index in Section 3.2 and clarify whether sX/aX are per-time or aggregated Jacobians and how As is assembled.  
- Clarity need: state θ initialization in Algorithm 1, how initial Xi are obtained, and justify using same η for both subproblems.  
- Clarity need: distinguish data augmentation (sensory noise + linearized target) from hidden-layer noise injection and clarify the denoising analogy.  
- Clarity need: specify task targets (e.g., whether facing angle is included) and whether adding heading is straightforward.  
- Originality: contributes an efficient gain-computation technique for direct trajectory optimizers, a combined input/hidden noise recipe for closed-loop control, and diverse 3D results—novel in combination.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for animation and robotics if broadly validated; significance limited by sparse comparisons and missing scaling data.  
- Key requested experiments: detail MPC setup and metrics; compare to guided policy search, TRPO, and simple PID/state-machine baselines.  
- Key requested measurements: report control step time, CPU threads, solver iterations, and cost scaling with DOFs/contacts.  
- Key requested analyses: enlarge dataset or show cross-character generalization, report train/test errors and rollout success under perturbations, and perform finer-grained ablations and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Implementation question: clarify annealing of penalty weight α, choices for initial Xi, whether different ηθ/ηX were tried, and provide convergence diagnostics for asynchronous updates.  
- Recommendation: release code/data (or core components) and fix notation/typos to aid reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: promising, well-engineered approach with novel practical contributions; borderline accept leaning positive if comparisons, scaling data, and clarity are improved.
