- Method trains recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers by interleaving trajectory optimization with neural network training for real-time 3D character control.  
- Builds on Contact-Invariant Optimization and extends it with neural policy learning, noise injection during training, and extracting feedback gains from trajectory optimization.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across diverse characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) using a single controller architecture.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Trajectory optimization formulation is technically sound, with proper handling of physical constraints and feedback-gain calculation.  
- High technical quality: clear constrained optimization statement and a justified block-alternating optimization solution (Algorithm 1).  
- Experimental validation is thorough and results across multiple character types are convincing.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized; figures (e.g., Fig. 1) effectively support claims.  
- Provides sufficient implementation details (network architecture, hyperparameters) to aid reproducibility.  
- Notes limitations: requires significant compute (~2.5 hours training) and produces purposeful (not chaotic) behaviors.  
- Minor issues: a typo at line 89 and a request for 1–2 references to support a claim at line 153 about parameter-space update sizes.  
- Originality lies in the technical synthesis of existing components rather than novel theoretical contributions.  
- Practical significance is high for character animation and robotics by enabling interactive control without motion capture or task-specific engineering.  
- Broader ML significance is limited by domain specificity, though the optimization framework could apply to other control tasks.  
- Overall assessment: technically impressive, practically significant, well-executed work that warrants acceptance despite limited theoretical novelty.
