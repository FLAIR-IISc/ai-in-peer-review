

# Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse complex characters (swimming, flying, bipedal and quadrupedal locomotion) in 3D environments. The key innovation lies in a joint optimization framework that interleaves trajectory optimization with neural network training, enabling interactive real-time control without motion capture or task-specific features. The authors build upon Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) [12] and extend it with neural network policy learning, introducing techniques like noise injection during training and using trajectory optimization to obtain optimal feedback gains. This work represents a significant technical advancement over previous approaches [9, 11] that struggled with 3D locomotion, and it unifies disparate control approaches for different character types that typically required specialized architectures.

**Quality** - The paper demonstrates high technical quality. The authors clearly articulate the constrained optimization problem (1) and provide a well-justified solution via block-alternating optimization (Algorithm 1). The experimental validation is thorough, with ablation studies showing the necessity of joint optimization (Fig. 2) and noise injection (Table 1). The results across multiple character types (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness. The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations, noting that the approach requires significant computational resources (2.5 hours of training) and that the method produces "purposeful behaviors" rather than chaotic ones. The trajectory optimization approach (Section 3) is technically sound, with proper handling of physical constraints and feedback gains calculation.

**Clarity** - The paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized. The problem statement, methodology, and results are presented logically with appropriate technical detail. The figures (particularly Figure 1 showing diverse character behaviors) effectively complement the text. The paper provides sufficient implementation details for reproducibility (e.g., network architecture, hyperparameters in Section 8.1). The only minor issues are a typo at line 89 ("to unify.") and a request for one or two references at line 153 to support the claim that "keeping updates small in parameter space is similar to recent approaches" - though the authors' motivation for this design choice is understandable.

**Originality** - The paper makes a valuable contribution by combining trajectory optimization with neural network policy learning for interactive control. While the components (trajectory optimization, neural networks) have appeared separately in prior work [9, 11, 12], the authors' specific implementation of interleaving these methods with noise injection and feedback gain calculation represents a novel technical synthesis. The ability to control diverse 3D characters with a single architecture (unifying approaches that previously required specialized controllers) is particularly noteworthy. However, the authors acknowledge the lack of novel theoretical insights, and the method does build substantially on prior work in trajectory optimization and neural network training.

**Significance** - The results demonstrate significant practical impact for character animation and potentially robotics. The method enables interactive control of diverse complex characters without motion capture or task-specific engineering, which could be valuable for game development, animation, and robotics. The demonstration of stable 3D locomotion for multiple character types (including flying and swimming) represents a meaningful advance over prior work limited to 2D. The paper's claim that "the same algorithm and controller architecture can provide interactive control for multiple creature morphologies" is well-supported by the results. However, the significance for broader machine learning research is somewhat limited by the domain-specific nature of the problem, though the optimization framework could be applicable to other control tasks.

**Pro and Con for Acceptance:**
*Pro:* The paper presents a technically sophisticated solution to a challenging problem with clear empirical validation across multiple domains. The method is well-motivated, clearly explained, and demonstrates significant practical utility. The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the necessity of key components (joint optimization, noise injection).

*Con:* The theoretical novelty is limited, as the approach builds substantially on existing trajectory optimization and neural network techniques. The reviewer's unfamiliarity with the animation/control domain prevents a full assessment of broader impact, though the results appear impressive within this context.

**Overall Assessment:**
This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of character animation and control, demonstrating a technically sophisticated solution that successfully enables interactive control of diverse complex characters in 3D. While the theoretical novelty is limited, the practical significance is high, and the paper is well-written and well-executed. The method's ability to unify disparate control approaches for different character types represents a meaningful advance over prior work. The reviewers' questions about the necessity of re-optimization at each timestep are valid, but the authors provide a reasonable justification (larger timesteps, physical stability), and the results clearly demonstrate the method's effectiveness.

The paper meets NIPS's standards for quality and clarity, and while the significance for broader machine learning research may be limited, its contribution to the specific domain of character animation and control is substantial. The technical execution is impressive, and the results are compelling. I recommend acceptance.
