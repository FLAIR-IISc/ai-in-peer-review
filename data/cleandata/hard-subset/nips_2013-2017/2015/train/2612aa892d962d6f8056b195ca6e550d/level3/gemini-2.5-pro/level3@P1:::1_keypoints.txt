- Proposes a unified framework that jointly optimizes reference trajectories and an RNN policy by alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) and supervised policy learning.  
- Embeds the policy learning objective as a soft penalty within trajectory optimization to bias trajectories toward being learnable.  
- Derives and uses optimal feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer to train the policy on noisy sensory inputs, producing a learned feedback controller rather than only state-action regression.  
- Trains policies that correct small deviations, preventing catastrophic error accumulation in closed-loop control.  
- Demonstrates robust, realistic, and interactive locomotion for diverse 3D characters (bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, flyers) with a single architecture and procedure.  
- Presents a rigorous ablation study showing the importance of joint optimization and noise/feedback gain mechanisms.  
- Provides insightful comparison to MPC, showing joint optimization yields more natural, cyclic behaviors than pure optimization.  
- Paper quality is very high: technically deep, elegant, thoroughly evaluated, and well-organized with clear mathematical derivations.  
- Writing and presentation are clear and accessible, with helpful algorithmic overviews and motivating explanations.  
- Novelty lies in computing optimal feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer and embedding local control info into policy training, plus a practical block-alternating optimization formulation.  
- Significance is high: advances automated synthesis of versatile, robust controllers and likely to impact robotics, animation, and ML.  
- Main weakness: heavy reliance on the power and success of Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) without detailed discussion of potential failure modes.  
- Main weakness: substantial computational cost requiring distributed clusters and GPUs, which may limit accessibility.  
- Main weakness: lacks quantitative comparison to direct reinforcement learning methods; discussion/experiments on this are limited.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths far outweigh minor weaknesses — a significant, polished, and influential contribution.
