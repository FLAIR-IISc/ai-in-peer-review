- Proposes training recurrent neural network controllers using Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) data, alternating joint training, and noise injection for real-time control.
- Effectively combines offline trajectory optimization with online neural adaptability, emphasizing robustness via noise injection analogous to denoising techniques.
- Novelty: successful integration of CIO with NN training applied to complex 3D locomotion across diverse morphologies.
- Demonstrates visually compelling, stable, and realistic locomotion for swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal characters.
- Limited training set (100–200 trials, 5 branched segments) raises concerns about overfitting and weak generalization claims.
- Comparative evaluation is weak—only a brief, uncited MPC comparison; lacks systematic comparisons to RL, state machines, and other trajectory-optimization baselines.
- CIO step (Section 3.2) is underspecified and requires consulting prior work; notation (e.g., dropped time index) obscures handling of noisy inputs.
- Key algorithmic choices (constant eta in optimization, asynchronous joint optimization differing from Algorithm 1) are unjustified and need clarification.
- Terminology and analogy issues: distinction between “data augmentation” and “noise injection” unclear, and the denoising autoencoder analogy should be re-examined.
- Reproducibility concerns: code not provided; missing runtime measurements and scaling analysis limit assessment of real-time applicability.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, promising approach that bridges offline optimization and online NN control with strong visual results.
- Argument for rejection: limited experiments, weak comparisons, and clarity/reproducibility issues; recommend rejection with encouragement to resubmit after addressing concerns.
