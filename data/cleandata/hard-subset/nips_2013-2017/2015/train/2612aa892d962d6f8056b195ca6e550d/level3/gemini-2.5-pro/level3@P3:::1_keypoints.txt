- Trains RNN policies as robust, real-time feedback controllers by using offline trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data.  
- Novel technique: computes optimal local feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer and trains the network to react to noisy inputs, embedding linear feedback into the learned policy.  
- Uses iterative alternating optimization where trajectories are refined by the current policy and the policy is updated from the refined trajectories to produce simpler, robust behaviors.  
- Applies the same architecture to diverse 3D characters (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds), demonstrating broad generality and interactive control.  
- Strong empirical validation with extensive experiments and an ablation study that shows joint optimization and noise injection are necessary.  
- High technical quality and engineering: well-integrated system combining optimization, control, and deep learning, with a distributed training implementation.  
- Method regularizes search toward smoother, more natural gaits and represents a significant, original advance likely to influence future work.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with informative figures and a good conceptual overview.  
- Computational cost is high (distributed ~30 nodes + GPU, hours of training), which may limit adoption.  
- Missing quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art model-free RL (e.g., TRPO); comparison to MPC is qualitative.  
- Reproducibility is challenging due to the complex custom architecture, MuJoCo dependency, and bespoke GPU code.  
- Recommendation: clear accept — outstanding, standout contribution to automated controller synthesis.
