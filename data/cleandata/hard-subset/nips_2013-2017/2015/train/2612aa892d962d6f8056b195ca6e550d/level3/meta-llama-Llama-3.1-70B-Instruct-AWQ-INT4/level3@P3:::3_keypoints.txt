- Proposes real-time control combining supervised learning with Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) to train neural networks for diverse complex characters.
- Introduces alternating joint training and noise injection (data augmentation + additive hidden-layer noise) to improve robustness and generalization.
- Demonstrates networks producing realistic, stable control policies across a variety of character models and tasks.
- Shows benefits over naive supervised training on static CIO trajectories and provides a brief comparison to model-predictive control (MPC).
- Experimental evaluation is limited by a small training set (100–200 trials, 5 branched segments), weakening generalization claims.
- Comparative evaluation is weak—only a brief MPC comparison and no systematic comparisons to other relevant methods.
- Mathematical presentation is mostly clear but key components (the CIO step, Equation 6) lack definitions and detail, forcing readers to consult external references.
- Some notation and writing issues (e.g., dropped time index in Section 3.2, misuse of “unity” vs “unify”) reduce readability and clarity.
- The asynchronous joint optimization described in Section 6 departs from Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme and should be reconciled or introduced earlier.
- Technically sound overall, with theoretical and experimental support, but would benefit from more detailed explanations of CIO and the perturbation method for feedback gains.
- Originality: novel combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization, but needs clearer positioning relative to prior work.
- Significance: method has potential impact on character control and robotics but requires more discussion of practical benefits and limitations.
- Recommendations: add fuller technical details (CIO, feedback gain perturbation), fix notation/writing, provide systematic comparisons to other methods, and expand discussion of practical trade-offs.
