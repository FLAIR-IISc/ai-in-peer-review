This paper proposes a novel method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse complex characters. The approach combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and training for unexpected changes in the task specification. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on a range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking with different body morphologies.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a universal synthesis method applicable to arbitrary behaviors, body morphologies, online changes in task objectives, perturbations due to noise, and modeling errors. The authors achieve this by using a neural network to learn from the optimizer and generate similar behaviors online. The method is able to produce stable and realistic behaviors without requiring motion capture or task-specific features or state machines.
