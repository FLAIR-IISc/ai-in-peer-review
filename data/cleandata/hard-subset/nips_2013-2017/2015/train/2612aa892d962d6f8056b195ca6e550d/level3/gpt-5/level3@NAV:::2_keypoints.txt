- Method: Interleaves trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised training of a compact recurrent policy that outputs desired velocities, contact forces, and recurrent updates for diverse morphologies.
- Training: Injects sensor and network noise, uses proximal regularization, and runs asynchronously/distributed with ablations validating design choices.
- Gain extraction: Introduces a perturbation-based procedure to extract local feedback gains from the CIO Hessian without an LQG pass.
- Test-time control: Uses a short-horizon optimization (T=1) that tracks the policy’s desired velocities for real-time interactive control.
- Experimental strengths: Compelling, broad behavioral suite and careful engineering; ablations substantiate key design decisions.
- Missing baselines: Lacks direct comparisons to closest trajectory-optimization–guided policy methods (Abbeel/Levine et al.) and to an MPC baseline.
- Missing metrics: Needs standardized metrics (learning speed wall-clock/samples, runtime cost—Hz & memory, robustness to disturbances/model/terrain variability, failure/recovery rates).
- “Interactive” unclear: Must quantify control frequency, latency, and real-time performance on stated hardware.
- Notation/clarity issues: Confusing symbol usage (e.g., s(X)=s̄ vs cost using s(X)−s̄); bar/tilde conventions and Lines 226–240 need disambiguation.
- LQG discussion: Move rationale for not using LQG after describing the new gain-computation method and compare directly to LQG (stability, conditioning, performance).
- Feedback gains: Claims of “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” need quantitative evidence (norms, condition numbers, examples) and precise definition of “softer feedback gains according to λ.”
- Originality: Reads as strong application of existing trajectory-optimization + deep learning rather than a fundamentally new algorithm, though the perturbation-based gain extraction may be a novel contribution.
- Required analyses: Isolate and analyze the perturbation-gain method versus LQG, and provide conditioning/stability/task-performance comparisons.
- Significance conditional: Would be impactful if accompanied by rigorous head-to-head comparisons and robustness analyses demonstrating a small, fast network unifying interactive control.
- Arguments for acceptance: Solid engineering, broad behaviors with one architecture, sensible training pipeline with ablations, and a potentially novel practical gain-computation procedure.
- Arguments against acceptance: Unclear novelty relative to prior work, missing direct comparisons and quantitative metrics, and exposition/notation issues that hinder reproducibility.
