- Presents a method for training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for interactive character control in physics simulators.
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, using Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and noise injection during training.
- Does not rely on motion capture or task-specific features, enabling applicability to varied body morphologies and cyclic behaviors.
- Demonstrates results on diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped locomotion).
- Produces neural-network policies capable of real-time execution for robotics, animation, and biomechanics.
- Strength — Technical soundness: well-motivated integration of trajectory optimization and neural network training, with noise injection and distributed training to combat overfitting and scale.
- Strength — Generality: handles diverse body types and tasks without hand-crafted controllers.
- Strength — Real-time execution: practical advancement over traditionally offline trajectory optimization.
- Strength — Reproducibility: detailed algorithm, training setup, and architecture descriptions.
- Weakness — Positioning/novelty: insufficient differentiation and quantitative comparison with prior work (e.g., Abbeel, Levine); claimed advances not emphasized.
- Weakness — Ambiguity in explanations: key terms (e.g., “large and ill-conditioned feedback gains”, “softer feedback gains”) and the rationale for not using LQG are poorly defined.
- Weakness — Notation clarity: visually dense notation (lines 226–240) needs reorganization or clearer explanation.
- Weakness — Evaluation gaps: lacks systematic comparison with alternatives (e.g., MPC, direct policy learning) on runtime, robustness, and task variety.
- Pro: Addresses a challenging problem and shows meaningful generalizability across tasks and morphologies.
- Con: Limited perceived novelty, ambiguous terminology/notation, and incomplete evaluation reduce accessibility and impact.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions contingent on addressing novelty, clarity, and more comprehensive evaluation.
