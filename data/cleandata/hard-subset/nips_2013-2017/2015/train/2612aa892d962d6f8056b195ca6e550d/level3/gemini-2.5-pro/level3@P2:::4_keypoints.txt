- Joint method that alternates Contact-Invariant Optimization for trajectories with supervised regression to train a single recurrent NN controller.
- Introduces principled sensory noise injection and uses optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization to train robust policies.
- Implemented in a distributed, asynchronous system that scales and works on diverse 3D characters (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds) without task-specific heuristics or mocap.
- High technical and engineering quality with strong empirical evidence and impressive supplementary video results.
- Ablation studies clearly show the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection for good test-time performance.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and uses consistent mathematical notation.
- Policy execution includes a per-timestep optimization to match desired velocity subject to physical constraints; the motivation and trade-offs for this step need clearer explanation.
- Builds on prior trajectory-optimization + policy learning work, but contributes novel, effective techniques (feedback gains + noisy training) enabling complex 3D control.
- Results are significant for graphics, robotics, and ML, potentially reducing manual effort for character controllers and aiding novel hardware controllers.
- Minor issues: typo at Line 89 and missing citation(s) for the proximal regularization remark at Line 153.
- Arguments for acceptance: general, powerful framework; state-of-the-art 3D locomotion across morphologies; novel robustness techniques; clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental over prior concepts (scaling rather than new theory); per-frame optimization at execution adds complexity beyond a simple feed-forward controller.
