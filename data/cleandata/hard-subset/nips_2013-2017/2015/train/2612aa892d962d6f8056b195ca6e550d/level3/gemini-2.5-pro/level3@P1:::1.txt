### Summary of the Paper

This paper presents a novel and highly effective method for training recurrent neural network policies to serve as real-time feedback controllers for a diverse range of complex, physically simulated characters. The core contribution is a framework that jointly optimizes a collection of reference trajectories and the neural network policy. This is achieved by alternating between two main steps: 1) using a powerful offline trajectory optimizer (Contact-Invariant Optimization, CIO) to generate optimal state-action sequences, and 2) training a neural network to regress onto this data.

Crucially, the coupling between these two steps is not a simple one-way transfer. The policy learning objective is incorporated as a soft penalty within the trajectory optimization, encouraging the optimizer to find solutions that are learnable by the network. Furthermore, the paper introduces a key innovation for improving robustness: instead of just training the network on state-action pairs, it is trained to replicate the behavior of an optimal feedback controller. This is accomplished by deriving optimal feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer and using them to generate target actions for noisy sensory inputs. The result is a policy that learns not only to follow a trajectory but also how to correct for small deviations, preventing the catastrophic error accumulation common in closed-loop control.

The authors demonstrate the power of their unified framework by successfully synthesizing stable, realistic, and interactive locomotion controllers for a wide variety of 3D characters—including bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, and flyers—using the same architecture and training procedure. This work builds upon recent efforts to combine trajectory optimization with policy learning (e.g., Levine & Koltun, 2014; Mordatch et al., 2014) but represents a substantial leap forward in terms of the complexity and diversity of the tasks solved.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Quality:** The paper is of exceptionally high quality. The proposed method is technically deep, elegant, and rigorously evaluated. The claims are strongly supported by the impressive experimental results and a well-designed ablation study (Figure 2), which clearly demonstrates the critical importance of both the joint optimization scheme and the noise injection/feedback gain mechanism. The comparison with Model Predictive Control (MPC) is particularly insightful, showing that the joint optimization regularizes the trajectory search, leading to more natural, cyclic behaviors than a pure optimization approach would. This is a complete and polished piece of work.

**Clarity:** The paper is a model of clarity. Despite the complexity of the underlying optimization, the authors present their method in a clear, logical, and well-organized manner. The overview in Section 2, combined with Algorithm 1, provides an excellent roadmap. The mathematical formulations, particularly the derivation of optimal feedback gains in Section 3.2, are well-explained and motivated. The writing is precise and accessible.

**Originality:** While the high-level idea of distilling a trajectory optimizer into a neural network policy has been explored before, this paper makes several novel and significant contributions. The primary innovation is the principled method for computing optimal feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer and embedding this local control information into the policy training process. This is a powerful idea that directly targets the stability of the learned controller. The specific formulation of the joint optimization as a distributed, stochastic, block-alternating procedure is also novel and practical. The sheer success of applying a single, unified method to such a diverse set of challenging 3D locomotion problems is, in itself, a mark of originality and a substantial advance over prior work limited to 2D characters.

**Significance:** The significance of this work is very high. It represents a major step towards the goal of automatically synthesizing versatile and robust controllers for complex robotic and animated characters. The results are inspiring and convincingly demonstrate a path to replacing laborious, task-specific controller design with a general-purpose, data-driven approach. The methodology successfully integrates concepts from optimal control, deep learning, and computer graphics, and is likely to be highly influential in all three fields. Practitioners and researchers in robotics and animation are very likely to use and build upon these ideas.

**Weaknesses:** The paper has very few weaknesses.
1.  The method's success is heavily reliant on the underlying power of the Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) algorithm. While CIO is state-of-the-art, the paper does not discuss potential failure modes or limitations of this dependency. The performance of the entire pipeline is gated by the ability of CIO to find high-quality solutions for the specified tasks.
2.  The computational cost of the training procedure is substantial, requiring a distributed cluster of machines and a GPU. This is acknowledged by the authors ("we invest more computing resources in generating the data than in learning from it") and, while justified by the results, may limit the method's accessibility.
3.  The comparison to more direct reinforcement learning approaches is mentioned only briefly as an "impression". While a full-scale comparison is likely beyond the scope of one paper, a more quantitative discussion or experiment would have further strengthened the authors' claims about producing more "realistic and purposeful behaviors".

### Arguments for Acceptance
*   **Pro:** Presents a significant methodological contribution by proposing a novel way to embed optimal feedback gains into a learned policy, greatly improving robustness.
*   **Pro:** Achieves state-of-the-art results on a diverse and challenging set of 3D locomotion tasks, demonstrating unprecedented generality.
*   **Pro:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, making a complex topic accessible.
*   **Pro:** The work has high potential for significant impact across multiple fields, including robotics, computer animation, and machine learning. The results are inspiring and set a new standard for learning-based character control.

### Arguments against Acceptance
*   **Con:** The method has very high computational requirements, potentially limiting its adoption.
*   **Con:** The work's success is tightly coupled to a specific, highly sophisticated trajectory optimizer (CIO), and the paper could discuss this dependency in more detail.

Overall, the strengths of this paper far outweigh its minor weaknesses. It is an outstanding contribution that is technically impressive, clearly presented, and highly significant.
