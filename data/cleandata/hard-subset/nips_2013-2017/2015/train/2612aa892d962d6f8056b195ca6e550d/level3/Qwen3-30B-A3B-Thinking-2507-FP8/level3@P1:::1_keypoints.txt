- Method trains neural controllers that produce stable, realistic 3D locomotion for diverse characters without motion capture or handcrafting.  
- Bridges offline trajectory optimization and online policy learning to enable interactive control.  
- Key innovation: embed optimal feedback gains into the neural policy via a principled noise-injection scheme to ensure closed-loop stability.  
- Formulates a constrained problem relaxed to a quadratic penalty, enabling a clean alternating optimization (trajectory optimization ↔ neural regression).  
- Joint optimization plus noise injection is required—ablations show static training or removing noise fails catastrophically.  
- Feedback gains are computed efficiently by reusing trajectory optimization derivatives, avoiding costly LQG passes.  
- Comprehensive ablation studies strongly validate the necessity and effect of each component.  
- Exceptional clarity: precise definitions, clear algorithms, and compelling visual/supplementary results make the method accessible and reproducible.  
- Original: first to achieve stable 3D locomotion across varied morphologies with a single architecture embedding feedback gains.  
- High significance: unifies disparate control approaches, yields simpler and more robust approximations than MPC, and enables interactive animation and robotics applications.  
- Minor weaknesses: lacks systematic comparisons to direct RL methods and involves non-trivial cloud training time.  
- Implementation/parallelization details (EC2/GPU) are sound but largely engineering; core methodological contribution remains independent.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality, technically impressive, and significant—strong candidate for acceptance.
