- Core idea: alternate between trajectory optimization (CIO) to produce state-action trajectories and supervised learning to train a neural feedback controller.  
- Algorithmic steps: (1) generate optimal trajectories for tasks, (2) train a network to reproduce state-action mappings, iterating these two steps.  
- Novel contributions: principled noise injection for robustness, technique to extract optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimizer, and a distributed training architecture.  
- Empirical results: produces stable, visually realistic gaits for diverse 3D characters (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds) without task-specific engineering.  
- Emergent behaviors: cyclical gaits and trotting arise naturally from the framework rather than being hand-designed.  
- Advances prior work: extends trajectory-optimization-plus-function-approximation approaches to more complex, unstable, and diverse 3D locomotion tasks.  
- Strength: high significance and impressive, diverse results demonstrated in supplementary video.  
- Strength: technically novel idea of extracting feedback gains from direct trajectory optimization and joint optimization shown critical by ablation.  
- Strength: general framework not tied to character-specific state machines, applicable across morphologies.  
- Weakness: paper fails to state upfront that the method is model-based and requires full knowledge of dynamics, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Weakness: poor organization and balance—over-detailed distributed training section and an under-detailed neural network policy section.  
- Weakness: inconsistency about recurrent units—abstract/Section 2 mention memory states, but Section 4 describes a feed-forward network with no recurrence explanation.  
- Weakness: misleading use of the term “stochastic neural network” when the trained controller is effectively deterministic at execution time.  
- Recommendation: promising work with novel ideas and strong results but requires major revision to clarify assumptions, restructure content, and resolve the recurrent-network inconsistency.
