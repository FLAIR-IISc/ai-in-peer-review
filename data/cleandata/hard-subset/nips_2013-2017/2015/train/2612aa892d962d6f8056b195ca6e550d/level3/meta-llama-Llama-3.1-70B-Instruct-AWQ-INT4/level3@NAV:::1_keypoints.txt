- Proposes a novel method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems.  
- Embeds feedback gains into neural network policies to improve robustness to disturbances and regression error.  
- Introduces softly coupled trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a cost penalty for deviation.  
- Presents a new approach to computing optimal feedback gains, a significant technical contribution.  
- Integrates graphics, control, and neural networks across varied morphologies and tasks.  
- Demonstrates successful controllers on a wide range of complex characters and robots.  
- Uses sophisticated parallelization and a custom distributed GPU implementation to scale optimization.  
- Provides a thorough evaluation with comparisons to other methods and ablation studies.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and technically impressive.  
- High significance: addresses a difficult problem more effectively than prior work, with broad implications for robotics, animation, and gaming.  
- Potential reproducibility concerns due to reliance on a custom GPU implementation and specific distributed training architecture.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational cost, which may limit practical assessment by some readers.
