This paper proposes a novel approach to interactive control of complex characters using neural networks. The method combines trajectory optimization with policy learning, allowing for the generation of stable and realistic behaviors for a range of dynamical systems and tasks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on various characters, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the technical details of the proposed method. The use of figures and tables helps to illustrate the concepts and results, making the paper more engaging and easier to understand.

The technical sophistication of the paper is high, and the authors demonstrate a deep understanding of the underlying concepts and techniques. The use of distributed stochastic optimization, trajectory optimization, and neural network policy regression is impressive, and the authors provide a clear explanation of how these components work together to achieve the desired results.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on a range of tasks and characters. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their approach, including comparisons with other methods and ablation studies. The results show that the proposed method outperforms other approaches and is able to learn robust and generalizable policies.
