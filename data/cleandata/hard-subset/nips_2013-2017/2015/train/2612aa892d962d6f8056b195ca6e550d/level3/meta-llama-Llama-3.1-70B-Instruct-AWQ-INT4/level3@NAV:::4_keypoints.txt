- Proposes a novel approach combining trajectory optimization with neural policy learning for interactive control of complex characters.  
- Method produces stable, realistic behaviors across diverse dynamical systems and tasks.  
- Demonstrated on multiple characters (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with strong empirical results.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses figures/tables effectively.  
- High technical sophistication: distributed stochastic optimization, trajectory optimization, and policy regression are well integrated.  
- Thorough evaluation including comparisons and ablation studies showing the method outperforms alternatives.  
- Results indicate learned policies are robust and generalizable.  
- Limited theoretical novelty—paper offers little new theoretical insight.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of limitations and concrete potential applications.  
- Insufficient coverage of broader impact and real-world significance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, as strengths outweigh weaknesses.
