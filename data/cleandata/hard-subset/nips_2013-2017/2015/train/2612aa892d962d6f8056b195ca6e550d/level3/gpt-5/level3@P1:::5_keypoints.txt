- Proposes an alternating training scheme coupling Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with supervised learning of a NN policy that maps compact sensory state to desired velocities, contact forces, and a small recurrent memory update.  
- Assumes full, accurate, differentiable dynamics with contacts (soft constraints in MuJoCo) and allows root forces during execution — this core assumption must be stated up front.  
- Key technical ingredients: interleaved trajectory re-optimization with NN regression under proximal regularization.  
- Key technical ingredients: sensory-noise injection with first-order action corrections from optimal feedback gains computed cheaply via a perturbation/Woodbury reuse of the Gauss-Newton Hessian.  
- Key technical ingredients: hidden-unit noise during training to regularize and improve robustness.  
- Key technical ingredients: distributed, asynchronous training implementation enabling scale.  
- Policy executes in real time by solving a short-horizon (T=1) inverse-dynamics-style optimization to match NN outputs while respecting dynamics.  
- Demonstrates diverse 3D behaviors (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with emergent cyclic gaits and no hand-crafted state machines.  
- Novel practical computation of stabilizing feedback gains directly from direct optimization without a separate LQG pass.  
- Strong engineering: simple to implement with existing CIO and NN toolchains and scalable distributed setup.  
- Ablation studies support the value of joint optimization and noise; architecture sweep gives some model-choice grounding.  
- Weakness: core model/differentiability assumptions and soft-constraint/root-force allowances are not emphasized early and limit robotics applicability.  
- Weakness: paper organization imbalance — Section 4 (policy) needs expansion; Section 6 (implementation) could be shortened or moved to supplement.  
- Weakness: ambiguity about recurrence — unclear whether network is truly recurrent or treats r as external state; missing details on number, initialization, training (BPTT?).  
- Weakness: “stochastic policy” wording misleading — stochasticity appears to be a training device, but test-time determinism must be clarified.  
- Weakness: limited quantitative evaluation and metrics (robustness, success rates, tracking errors, control effort, solver timing, sensitivity analyses, generalization).  
- Weakness: physical realism concerns due to soft constraints and allowed root forces; need to quantify residual constraint violations and ways to harden results.  
- Weakness: insufficient baseline comparisons (MPC with same costs/horizon, modern policy-gradient methods like TRPO, trajectory-centric RL).  
- Quality: algorithmic components are sound; feedback-gain derivation is a valuable technical contribution; convergence is shown empirically but would benefit from diagnostics.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but needs explicit clarification of recurrence design, test-time determinism, model assumptions, and a concise algorithm summary including variable dimensions.  
- Originality: combining CIO with NN regression is incremental, but stochastic training with gain-corrected targets and the feedback-gain computation are novel and useful.  
- Significance: high for animation and model-based control; moderate for robotics given model assumptions and soft constraints.  
- Questions for authors: how many recurrent units and what ablations justify them? Is BPTT used or is r supervised via ṙ targets?  
- Questions for authors: real-time runtimes (ms per step) for the T=1 solve across tasks? Sensitivity to λ in gain computation and guidelines?  
- Questions for authors: do contact schedules truly emerge or are they biased by CIO costs? How does method handle moderate model mismatch (perturbed masses/friction)?  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong technical idea (feedback gains from direct optimization), solid engineering, and broad 3D demonstrations with supporting ablations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear recurrence and stochasticity, organizational issues, limited quantitative evaluation, and reliance on full model knowledge/soft constraints.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and practically relevant; recommend acceptance after restructuring to foreground assumptions, clarifying recurrence and test-time determinism, and strengthening quantitative evaluations.
