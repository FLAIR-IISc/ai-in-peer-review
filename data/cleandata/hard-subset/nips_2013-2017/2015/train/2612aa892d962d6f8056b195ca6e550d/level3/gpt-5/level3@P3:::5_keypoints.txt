- Summary: Alternating optimization couples Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with supervised NN regression to produce near–real-time feedback controllers for diverse 3D contact-rich characters, assuming full knowledge of dynamics and contact models.

- Key ingredient: sensory noise injection with matched action targets using locally optimal feedback gains a_s (Eq. 2).

- Key ingredient: perturbation-based method reusing CIO Gauss–Newton structure to compute well-conditioned local feedback gains (Sec. 3.2).

- Key ingredient: proximal-regularized block-alternating optimization between CIO and NN regression (Alg. 1).

- Key ingredient: asynchronous distributed training architecture enabling scalable data collection and training.

- Policy/execution: network predicts desired velocities, contact forces, and recurrent-state rates; execution advances state via a one-step constrained optimization (Sec. 7).

- Main advance: extends trajectory-optimization-based policy learning to harder 3D, contact-rich morphologies with noise-integrated local feedback targets and interactive behaviors without mocap/state machines.

- Strength: Compelling qualitative demonstrations across multiple challenging 3D systems.

- Strength: Clever reuse of CIO derivatives to compute feedback gains with a tunable softness parameter λ.

- Strength: Proximal joint optimization stabilizes asynchronous training in practice.

- Strength: Principled use of sensory and activation noise with informative ablation studies.

- Strength: Real-time execution using a simple 1-step constrained optimization without retaining full trajectories.

- Weakness: Relies critically on accurate full dynamics and contact models (H, C, J, d(q), friction cone); this core assumption should be explicit and its sim-to-real implications discussed.

- Weakness: Imbalanced sectioning—Sec. 4 (NN policy) is underdeveloped while Sec. 6 (distributed training) is overly detailed.

- Weakness: Ambiguity about recurrence—unclear whether NN is feed-forward with r as input/output or contains recurrent connections; size, initialization, regularization, and ablations for r are unspecified.

- Weakness: Misleading “stochastic network” wording—training uses noise but execution is deterministic; clarify that stochasticity is training-time regularization if that is the case.

- Weakness: Quantitative evaluation is thin—claims like “two orders of magnitude better” and “less chaotic than MPC” need precise metrics, mean±std over seeds, and direct MPC comparisons.

- Weakness: Constraint violations at execution (soft enforcement) are not reported; need to quantify their magnitude and realism impact.

- Weakness: Feedback-gain analysis needs sensitivity to λ, quantitative LQR/LQG comparisons (conditioning, robustness, rollout error), and discussion of stability guarantees or empirical margins.

- Weakness: Action parameterization rationale unclear—justify predicting desired velocities/forces via one-step optimization versus directly predicting torques, preferably with a small comparative study.

- Weakness: Generalization and robustness under out-of-distribution goals/perturbations are not characterized; sweeps over noise scales σε, σγ and robustness plots are needed.

- Weakness: Reproducibility could be improved by consolidating hyperparameters, providing pseudocode for Sec. 3.2 gains, and centralizing training details.

- Quality (NIPS): Technically sound and neat reuse of CIO; needs stronger quantitative evaluation and clarity on recurrence/stochasticity.

- Clarity (NIPS): Generally readable but method section imbalanced; clarify recurrence, action semantics, and metrics; move engineering details to appendix.

- Originality (NIPS): Thoughtful synthesis with a novel feedback-gain extraction and effective noise-target coupling.

- Significance (NIPS): High for graphics/animation and model-based robotics; limited for model-free communities due to full-model reliance.

- Restructuring suggestion: Merge Sections 2–4 into a unified Method with clear subsections for setup, trajectory optimization, feedback gains, NN architecture/recurrence, and training algorithm.

- Restructuring suggestion: Expand Sec. 4 to fully describe recurrent state r, architecture, ablations, and training specifics; move Sec. 6 engineering details to supplemental.

- Acceptance pros: Strong diverse results, principled integration of trajectory optimization and noise-robust regression, and a scalable training pipeline with useful ablations.

- Acceptance cons: Heavy dependence on accurate models, ambiguity about recurrence/stochastic terminology, and insufficient quantitative evaluation.

- Rebuttal request: Clarify recurrence and “stochastic” terminology, explicitly state and discuss the full-model assumption (Eq. 6), and strengthen quantitative evaluations (MPC/LQG comparisons, constraint-violation reporting) to be favorably considered for acceptance.
