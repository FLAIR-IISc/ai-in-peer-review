- Proposes training small recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse simulated characters using supervised learning plus trajectory optimization (CIO).  
- Demonstrates same network architecture can handle diverse cyclic behaviors and body types (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped walking).  
- Shows impressive experimental results with stable, realistic behaviors across a range of dynamical systems and tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear method overview.  
- Method builds on prior use of neural nets to approximate step-by-step policies; novelty relative to that literature (e.g., [9]) is unclear.  
- Prior trajectory-optimization work already produces realistic control signals; authors must clarify what is new about their approach.  
- Needs better positioning and explicit comparison to recent related work, particularly Abbeel and Levine.  
- Should provide explicit comparisons on metrics: learning speed, supported body variety, runtime computation cost, and robustness to environmental variability.  
- Notation around lines 226–240 is confusing; visually similar symbols (bars vs squiggles) must be disambiguated.  
- Claim that "approach produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ" requires definition of "softer" and supporting justification.  
- Overall technically sound experiments, but contribution and novelty are not clearly established relative to recent work.  
- Recommendation: revise to clarify novelty, add comparisons to related work, and fix minor notation/clarity issues.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.
