- Proposes a novel method combining supervised learning with Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamic characters.  
- Introduces a neural-network policy that maps sensory state to desired velocity commands and contact forces.  
- Trains the policy via a distributed stochastic optimization that alternates trajectory optimization and network regression.  
- Demonstrates success on multiple tasks (swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking), including robustness to noise and perturbations.  
- Assumes full model knowledge (stated in Eq. 6) but fails to discuss the realism or implications of this assumption.  
- Section organization is imbalanced: Section 4 (policy regression) is short and lacks implementation details, while Section 6 (distributed training) is disproportionately long.  
- The abstract references recurrent units providing memory, but the paper does not describe their implementation or role.  
- Describes the network as “stochastic” (Line 405) despite the network appearing deterministic; this needs clarification relative to stochastic training data.  
- Scores well on originality for combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization.  
- Scores well on significance for demonstrating effective controllers across varied tasks and providing algorithmic detail.  
- Clarity is lacking in places; the paper would benefit from reorganization and more implementation detail.  
- Recommendation: promising work that should be revised to (1) discuss the full-model assumption, (2) rebalance and expand Section 4 vs. Section 6, (3) describe the recurrent units, and (4) clarify the use of “stochastic.”
