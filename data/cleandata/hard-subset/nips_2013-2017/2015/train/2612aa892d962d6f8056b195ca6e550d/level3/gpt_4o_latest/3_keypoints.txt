- Presents a novel neural-network controller combining supervised learning with Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) for real-time, physically realistic control of diverse 3D models.  
- Introduces noise injection in sensory inputs and hidden layers during training to improve generalization and closed-loop stability.  
- Evaluated on swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking, showing versatile behavior across different morphologies without motion capture or task-specific state machines.  

- Strength: Integrates trajectory optimization with neural training to enable real-time control while preserving physical realism.  
- Strength: Principled noise injection enhances robustness and reduces overfitting in closed-loop control.  
- Strength: Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks and morphologies without task-specific architectures.  
- Strength: Distributed/cloud training shows scalability for large-scale problems.  
- Strength: Produces realistic, natural gaits and motions without hand-crafted features.  

- Weakness: Lacks systematic, quantitative comparisons with state-of-the-art alternatives (e.g., RL, advanced MPC).  
- Weakness: Mathematical explanations for CIO and noisy input handling are superficial and need clearer derivations/examples.  
- Weakness: No public code release, limiting reproducibility and adoption.  
- Weakness: Missing detailed real-time performance metrics and analysis of scalability with model complexity.  
- Weakness: Focuses on positional (x,y,z) targets and omits character orientation control, limiting applicability for rotationally-sensitive tasks.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of scalability and robustness for real-world robotics or production animation pipelines.  

- Suggestion: Add systematic comparisons with alternative controllers (RL, other trajectory optimization).  
- Suggestion: Expand mathematical derivations and provide clearer explanations of key steps.  
- Suggestion: Release code to improve reproducibility and community engagement.  
- Suggestion: Report detailed real-time timing metrics and scalability analysis.  
- Suggestion: Address integration of orientation control into the framework.  

- Recommendation: Accept with revisions—promising scientific contribution but needs stronger comparative evaluation, clarity, and practical performance validation.
