- Presents a method that trains RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D characters by interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization (CIO).
- Uses trajectory optimization to generate training data, letting the network learn controls and dynamics simultaneously with actions defined as velocity.
- Technically sound with thorough experiments across swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal locomotion demonstrating stable, realistic behaviors.
- Major limitation: requires full model knowledge (eq. 6), which reduces applicability to real-world robotics where precise dynamics are unavailable.
- Clarity issues: paper is unbalanced (Sections 4 and 6 overly long) and fails to describe the recurrent units referenced in the abstract.
- Misleading terminology: calls the network "stochastic" despite the network being deterministic and only the training data being stochastic.
- Originality: significant contribution by combining trajectory optimization and neural networks to achieve harder 3D locomotion problems than prior work.
- Significance: provides a generalizable framework for character control impacting animation/biomechanics, but practical robotics impact is limited by model-dependence.
- Strength: demonstrates stable 3D locomotion across diverse morphologies without motion capture or task-specific features.
- Strength: unifies disparate control approaches across character types and provides principled noise injection for robustness.
- Strength: includes clear ablation comparisons (no joint optimization, no noise injection).
- Weakness: dependency on exact dynamics model limits real-world use.
- Weakness: structural presentation problems and misleading descriptions (recurrent units omission, "stochastic" label).
- Recommendation: borderline accept — promising and significant but requires major revisions to address clarity, structure, and the model-knowledge limitation.
