- Combines Contact-Invariant Optimization with recurrent neural network training, interleaving supervised learning and trajectory optimization with noise injection to learn controllers and dynamics jointly.
- Extends prior trajectory-optimization + learning approaches to complex 3D locomotion without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.
- Technical soundness: components are well-justified and optimization / feedback-gain treatment is detailed.
- Empirical results are impressive across diverse characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal); video and comparisons convincingly show benefits.
- Clarity and organization: paper is well-written with effective figures and reproducible detail.
- Thoroughness: addresses overfitting and instability (noise, proximal regularization) and describes distributed training architecture.
- Incremental novelty: core idea builds on prior work and lacks fundamentally new theoretical insights.
- Unclear justification for re-optimizing at each timestep during execution; motivation and driving factors need clarification.
- Limited broader-impact assessment and unclear practical significance beyond engineering scale-up.
- Arguments for acceptance: robust, scalable method; strong 3D results; clear implementation; notable engineering achievement.
- Arguments for rejection: incremental novelty; limited broader-impact analysis; insufficient justification for re-optimization necessity.

- Overall assessment: strong paper, leans toward acceptance (7/10) but should clarify re-optimization rationale and broader impact.
