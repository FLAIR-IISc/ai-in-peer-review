This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The method combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO), to generate stable and realistic behaviors. The authors claim that their approach is able to learn successful controllers for diverse character morphologies without requiring specialized initializations or motion capture data.

The paper is well-written and the experiments are thorough, but the reviewer's comments highlight some areas for improvement. Specifically, the authors need to better position their contribution relative to prior work in trajectory optimization and neural network control. While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of their approach, it is unclear what is novel about their method compared to existing techniques.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method. However, the reviewer's comment about the notation around lines 226-240 being confusing is well-taken, and the authors should consider revising this section to improve clarity.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and easy to follow, but some of the technical details could be explained more clearly. For example, the reviewer's comment about the claim that "our approach produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ" suggests that the authors could provide more justification for this claim.

In terms of originality, the paper builds on existing work in trajectory optimization and neural network control, but the reviewer's comments suggest that the authors need to better position their contribution relative to prior work. The authors should consider explicitly comparing their method to existing techniques and highlighting what is novel about their approach.

In terms of significance, the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the authors' approach for learning successful controllers for diverse character morphologies, which is a significant contribution to the field of physics-based animation and robotics.
