- Presents a method combining trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) with supervised learning to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped).  
- Key innovations: interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, principled noise injection, training for unexpected task changes, and using trajectory optimization to obtain optimal feedback gains.  
- Solves substantially harder 3D locomotion problems than prior work that focused on 2D, using a single neural architecture across character types.  
- Technically sound with clear mathematical formulation and a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm (Algorithm 1) addressing the joint optimization challenge.  
- Thorough experimental validation and ablation studies (Section 8.1) showing the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection for stable behavior.  
- Implementation and experimental details (Section 6, Section 8) demonstrate effectiveness and reproducibility, supplemented by a recommended video.  
- Novel contributions include principled noise injection (Sec 2.1), calculation of optimal feedback gains (Sec 3.2), and a scalable distributed training architecture (Sec 6).  
- Paper is generally well-written and well-organized with clear math and effective figures, though some aspects could be clarified further.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity trade-offs between trajectory optimization and neural network training.  
- Some implementation specifics are under-specified (exact network architecture beyond “3 hidden layers with 250 units each” and other hyperparameters).  
- Comparison to recent deep reinforcement learning control methods (e.g., [14,15]) is limited and could be strengthened.  
- Unclear how the method scales to significantly more complex systems with higher degrees of freedom.  
- Significant practical implications for robotics, animation, and gaming, and promising directions for more complex behaviors and biological plausibility.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, compelling results, technically solid — recommendation for acceptance.
