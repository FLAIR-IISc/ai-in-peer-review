- Paper proposes alternating optimization: CIO trajectory optimizer and a recurrent neural network trained jointly, with the optimizer penalizing deviation from the current policy to produce learnable trajectories.  
- Key technical contributions: principled noise injection using feedback gains derived from direct trajectory optimization, interactive data generation by branching trajectories to random goals, and a distributed asynchronous training architecture.  
- Demonstrated successful, general control of diverse 3D characters (bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmer, flyer) with emergent realistic behaviors from a single algorithm.  
- Strength: Significant and impressive results showing generality and visually compelling, stable gaits without task-specific engineering.  
- Strength: Technical novelty and soundness, notably the method for extracting well-conditioned feedback gains from CIO.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental validation and convincing ablation studies showing joint optimization and noise injection are essential.  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized writing and logical presentation of the algorithm and motivations.  
- Weakness: Limited quantitative comparison to contemporary RL methods (e.g., TRPO); claims about RL behaviors are not directly substantiated.  
- Weakness: Reproducibility issues—precise trajectory cost formulations and weights are not provided.  
- Weakness: Lack of analysis or ablation of the recurrent component; no evidence that recurrence is necessary or used.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a difficult problem, strong state-of-the-art results, novel feedback-gain technique, and solid ablation support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing quantitative RL baselines, omitted reproducibility details, and insufficient evaluation of recurrence.  
- Recommendation: Overall strong accept; strengths outweigh weaknesses and paper is likely to have substantial impact.
