- Presents a novel method combining supervised learning with Contact-Invariant Optimization to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical tasks (swimming, flying, walking).  
- Handles complex 3D dynamics without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines, enabling real-time interactive control.  
- Key innovations: noise injection during training, interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, and joint optimization of feedback gains and actions.  
- Technically sound with a solid theoretical foundation and experiments showing benefits over static training and ablations.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, with detailed methodology, math, and ablation studies.  
- Original integration of trajectory optimization and neural network regression for high-dimensional continuous control.  
- Significant impact potential for robotics, animation, and biomechanics due to scalability and robustness.  
- Lacks direct comparisons to state-of-the-art reinforcement learning or other trajectory-optimization baselines.  
- Unclear generalization to unseen tasks, morphologies, or substantially different environments (e.g., novel terrains/obstacles).  
- Computationally expensive trajectory optimization may limit scalability and real-world applicability; details on efficiency and bottlenecks are insufficient.  
- Pro: innovative approach with impressive, reproducible results across diverse tasks.  
- Con: limited baseline comparisons, unclear generalization, and high computational cost.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should add comparisons to RL methods and discuss generalization and computational efficiency.
