- Proposes combining trajectory optimization (CIO) with neural network regression to train real-time controllers from optimized trajectories.  
- Well-motivated core idea: use trajectory optimization to generate training data so networks can handle complex, high-dimensional control in real time.  
- Achieves stable, realistic locomotion across diverse morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal) using a single network architecture.  
- Clear, well-structured methodology explaining interleaved supervised learning, noise injection during training, and extracting feedback gains from the optimizer.  
- Distributed training architecture and comparative evaluations (showing benefits of joint optimization and noise injection) strengthen the contribution.  
- Builds on prior trajectory optimization and neural control work and advances 3D locomotion capabilities where prior methods struggled.  
- Paper candidly acknowledges trade-offs like computational cost and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on task specifics and cost function design per morphology, limiting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: claim of “more realistic and purposeful behaviors” than direct methods is inadequately substantiated and needs quantitative comparison.  
- Suggestion: include direct comparisons with other neural control methods (e.g., DDPG, TRPO) and metrics beyond visual inspection.  
- Weakness: discussion of optimal feedback gains and the choice of λ is opaque; links to control-theoretic notions (LQR, stability margins) need clarification.  
- Weakness: computational cost and scaling discussion lacks concrete metrics (e.g., GPU hours per character).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective technical contribution, impressive cross-morphology results, clear writing, addresses a hard problem, and provides a thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of task/cost detail, unsubstantiated realism claims, limited feedback-gain discussion, and incomplete computational cost quantification.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to add task-specific costs, stronger comparative evaluations, clearer feedback-gain analysis, and detailed computational costs.
