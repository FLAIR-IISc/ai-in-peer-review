This paper presents a compelling method for training recurrent neural networks to act as feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems, achieving stable and realistic behaviors in locomotion tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal walking) without relying on motion capture or task-specific features. The core idea of interleaving supervised learning (from trajectory optimization via CIO) with noise injection and optimization for unexpected task changes is innovative and demonstrably effective, particularly in 3D spaces where previous work has been limited.

**Quality:** The technical soundness of the paper is high. The combination of trajectory optimization (CIO) with neural network training is well-motivated and clearly explained. The stochastic optimization framework, while complex, is presented logically and justified by the challenges of training in high-dimensional spaces and closed-loop control. The ablation studies (Figure 2) provide strong evidence supporting the importance of both the joint optimization approach and noise injection. The use of a well-established physics engine (MuJoCo) lends credibility to the experimental results. However, the paper doesn’t delve into the theoretical guarantees of convergence for their stochastic optimization algorithm – it’s stated to converge, but lacks a rigorous analysis.  The experiments demonstrate success across a variety of morphologies, suggesting a degree of generality. The use of cloud computing and GPU implementation is acknowledged, which is important given the scale of the problem.  The paper does a reasonable job acknowledging limitations (e.g., comparison to direct methods is “impressionistic”).

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clearly stated, and the individual components of the method are explained in detail.  However, the mathematical notation can be dense at times, and a deeper understanding of trajectory optimization (CIO in particular) would be helpful for readers unfamiliar with the field.  While the algorithmic description is sound, it could benefit from pseudocode further clarifying the data flow between the optimization and neural network training steps. The descriptions of the experimental setup (costs, parameters) are sufficient but could be more comprehensive.  The accompanying video is essential for understanding the quality of the resulting behaviors.

**Originality:** The work presents a novel combination of existing techniques – trajectory optimization and neural networks – in a way that yields significant improvements in the control of complex dynamical systems. The application of this combined approach to 3D locomotion, achieving stable and realistic behaviors, is a key contribution. The specific techniques employed (noise injection, optimization for task changes, feedback gain calculation) represent original contributions that build upon and extend existing methods. The comparison to related work (e.g., [9, 11]) clearly highlights the advancements made in this paper. While the individual components aren't entirely novel, the synergistic integration is.

**Significance:** The results are highly significant. Automating the process of controller design for complex characters has broad implications for robotics, animation, and biomechanics. The ability to train a single network architecture to control diverse morphologies is particularly noteworthy. The demonstrated robustness to noise and unexpected task changes suggests potential for real-world applications where uncertainty is prevalent.  The paper advances the state-of-the-art by extending successful 2D control methods to 3D environments and addressing the challenges of high-dimensional control spaces. The unique dataset generated through trajectory optimization and the resulting insights into the relationship between trajectory optimization and neural network training are valuable contributions to the field. The potential to apply this method to more complex behaviors (getting up, climbing, reaching) and to robotics is exciting.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Significant improvement over existing methods for 3D locomotion control.
*   Novel and effective combination of trajectory optimization and neural networks.
*   Demonstrated ability to control diverse character morphologies with a single architecture.
*   Robustness to noise and task changes.
*   Strong experimental validation and ablation studies.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of rigorous theoretical analysis of the stochastic optimization algorithm’s convergence.
*   Mathematical notation can be dense and challenging for some readers.
*   Comparison to direct methods is qualitative rather than quantitative.
*   Reliance on a specific physics engine (MuJoCo) may limit applicability.



**Overall Assessment:**  This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of control and character animation. The technical soundness, originality, and significance of the work are all high. While some aspects could be further refined (e.g., theoretical analysis, quantitative comparisons), the demonstrated results are compelling and the potential impact is substantial. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to improve clarity and address the concerns raised above.
