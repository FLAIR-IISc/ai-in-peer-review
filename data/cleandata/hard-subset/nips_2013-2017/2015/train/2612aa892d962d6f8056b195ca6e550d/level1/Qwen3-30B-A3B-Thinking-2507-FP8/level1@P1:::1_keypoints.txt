- Summary: Introduces a joint supervised learning and trajectory-optimization method (using Contact-Invariant Optimization) to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D locomotion across morphologies, with noise injection and training for task changes.

- Technical soundness: Derives optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization and uses ablation studies to show key components are necessary.

- Novelty & scope: Extends prior trajectory-optimization methods to hard 3D locomotion across multiple morphologies with a single unified controller architecture.

- Experimental rigor: Comprehensive experiments and ablations demonstrate successful locomotion, superiority to naive training, and insights on MPC behavior.

- Clarity & presentation: Well-organized, clearly written, reproducible methodology with effective figures and supplementary video.

- Limited comparison to deep RL: Insufficient benchmarking against recent methods like PPO or SAC and weak evidence for claims about direct methods.

- Computational complexity: Reports training time as “hours” on cloud but lacks clear comparisons of computational cost versus alternatives.

- Theoretical limitations: No convergence guarantees for the alternating optimization or analysis of generalization beyond demonstrated tasks.

- Lack of robustness testing: Does not systematically evaluate robustness to external perturbations, modeling errors, or sensor noise during execution.

- For acceptance: Solves a significant 3D locomotion problem, offers a unified controller, demonstrates clear technical advances, and provides comprehensive experiments with high potential impact.

- Against acceptance: Missing comparisons to modern deep RL, unclear computational requirements, absent theoretical analysis of optimization, and no systematic robustness evaluation.

- Overall assessment: Strong, significant contribution with minor but addressable weaknesses; recommendation: Accept.
