- Presents a method to train a single recurrent neural network as a real-time feedback controller for diverse 3D characters using offline trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) as expert data.  
- Core contribution: a joint, alternating optimization framework that trains trajectories and the network together.  
- Novel technique for extracting optimal feedback gains from the direct optimizer to create richer training targets under noise.  
- Uses stochasticity (noise injection) during training to improve robustness; ablation shows joint optimization and noise are necessary.  
- Demonstrates success on a challenging, varied set of 3D locomotion tasks (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds).  
- Technically sound with claims supported by experiments and a compelling ablation study (Figure 2).  
- Policy execution involves outputting a desired velocity that is realized via a single-timestep optimization ("implicit integrator"); this mechanism needs clearer explanation.  
- While distilling an optimizer into a policy is not entirely new, the specific formulation, feedback-gain extraction, and distributed architecture form a novel and effective combination.  
- Results are significant: a general-purpose algorithm controlling many morphologies and producing natural gaits without task-specific priors.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, achieves state-of-the-art across diverse characters, and combines techniques with strong empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: brief, mainly qualitative comparison to Model-Predictive Control; insufficient clarity on the real-time policy execution step.  
- Overall verdict: excellent paper with significant contributions and minor clarity weaknesses; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
