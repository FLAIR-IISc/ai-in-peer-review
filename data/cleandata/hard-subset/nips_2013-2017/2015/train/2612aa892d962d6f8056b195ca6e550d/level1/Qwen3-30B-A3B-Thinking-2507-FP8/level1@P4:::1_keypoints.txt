- Presents a method training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D dynamical characters using trajectory optimization and supervised learning.  
- Introduces technical innovations: interleaved (joint) optimization, noise injection, and computing optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization.  
- Successfully achieves stable 3D locomotion (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with a single network architecture without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Builds on prior trajectory optimization and NN-control work but makes significant improvements enabling harder 3D problems.  
- Method is technically sound with clear mathematical formulation and theoretically motivated trajectory optimization and gain calculation.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show joint optimization and noise injection are necessary to prevent policy divergence.  
- Implementation details are sufficient for reproducibility, though dynamics constraints are only softly enforced at execution.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear explanations, precise math, and effective figures.  
- Novelty lies in feedback-gain-as-byproduct method, the noise-injection strategy, and a distributed training architecture applied to diverse 3D characters.  
- Significant contribution addressing a key challenge in animation/robotics by generalizing controllers across morphologies and producing realistic, stable behaviors.  
- Strengths: single-architecture 3D control across character types, rigorous ablations, novel techniques, sound approach, comprehensive experiments.  
- Weaknesses: limited detail on computational/training requirements, no direct comparison to state-of-the-art RL methods, noise-parameter choices lack justification, limited discussion of generalization to unseen morphologies.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance — technically sound, novel, significant, and well-validated.
