- Presents a novel method combining supervised learning and trajectory optimization to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems.  
- Trains with injected noise and uses trajectory optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation including network architecture, trajectory optimization, and distributed training details.  
- Thorough evaluation demonstrating successful controllers for swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped tasks across morphologies.  
- Strong empirical comparisons to other approaches showing method effectiveness.  
- Tackles a challenging control problem with a novel ML + optimization solution.  
- High technical quality, well-motivated and technically sound approach.  
- Original contribution with significant implications for robotics, animation, and gaming.  
- Assumes substantial background in control, ML, and optimization, limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability.  
- Does not clearly discuss method limitations or directions for future work.  
- Needs clearer positioning relative to other approaches in the field.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for NIPS, with requested revisions addressing complexity, limitations, and broader discussion.
