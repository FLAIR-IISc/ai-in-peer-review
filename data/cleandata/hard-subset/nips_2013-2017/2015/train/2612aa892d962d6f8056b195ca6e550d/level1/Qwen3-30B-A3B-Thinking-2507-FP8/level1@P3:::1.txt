

# Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

This paper presents a method for training neural network controllers for diverse character locomotion tasks, combining trajectory optimization with neural network learning. The key innovation is using trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data for a neural network that learns state-action mappings, rather than directly optimizing the neural network policy. The method demonstrates stable 3D locomotion for swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal characters without task-specific features or motion capture.

The work builds on recent NIPS contributions like Levine & Koltun (ICML 2014) and Mordatch & Todorov (NIPS 2014) that explored learning neural network policies via trajectory optimization, but makes several critical technical advances. Previous work was limited to 2D locomotion, while this paper demonstrates successful 3D control across diverse character morphologies. The authors also introduce novel techniques including principled noise injection for robustness, training for unexpected task changes, and a novel method for computing optimal feedback gains that reuses trajectory optimization computations.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and well-executed. The authors provide a clear mathematical formulation of the problem and a detailed algorithmic description. The experimental validation is thorough, with ablation studies showing the importance of key components (noise injection, joint optimization). The implementation details, including distributed training architecture, are well-described. However, the theoretical justification for the approach is limited. While the authors note that neural networks trained with direct methods yield "chaotic behaviors," they don't provide a rigorous comparison to modern deep reinforcement learning methods (e.g., SAC, PPO) that have achieved impressive control results. The paper would be strengthened by comparing to these state-of-the-art RL approaches.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and well-organized. The mathematical formulations are clear, and the experimental results are effectively presented with appropriate visualizations. The description of the trajectory optimization and feedback gain computation is particularly clear. One minor weakness is the somewhat abrupt transition from the neural network architecture description to the training details in Section 4. The connection between the sensory inputs and the physical state evolution could be clarified.

**Originality:** This work makes a significant contribution by demonstrating a unified approach to locomotion control across diverse character morphologies in 3D, where previous work was limited to 2D. The method of interleaving trajectory optimization with neural network training, along with the noise injection and feedback gain techniques, represents a novel combination of established techniques. The paper clearly differentiates itself from prior work (Levine & Koltun, Mordatch & Todorov) by solving substantially harder problems. However, the paper could better address how this approach differs from and improves upon recent deep RL methods that have also demonstrated impressive control capabilities.

**Significance:** The work has substantial significance for character animation, robotics, and biomechanics. The ability to train a single controller architecture for diverse character types and locomotion tasks represents a major advance over previous task-specific approaches. The method's robustness to unexpected task changes and noise is particularly valuable for interactive applications. The paper could better articulate how this work advances the state of the art compared to recent deep RL approaches that have achieved similar results on control tasks. The supplementary video would be invaluable for reviewers to fully appreciate the quality of the generated behaviors.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pros:*
- Demonstrates successful 3D locomotion for diverse character types, a significant advance over previous work limited to 2D
- Provides a unified framework that eliminates the need for task-specific controllers
- Introduces novel techniques (noise injection, feedback gain computation) that significantly improve robustness
- Well-executed experiments with thorough ablation studies
- Clear, well-organized presentation with good technical detail

*Cons:*
- Limited theoretical justification for the approach compared to existing deep RL methods
- Insufficient comparison to recent deep reinforcement learning approaches that have achieved similar control results
- Some technical details (sensory input to state evolution) could be clarified
- The significance could be better articulated in relation to the broader control literature

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of character animation and control. While it could benefit from more comprehensive comparisons to recent deep RL work, the technical advances and demonstrated capabilities are substantial. The work represents an important step toward truly interactive, real-time character control that can generalize across diverse morphologies and tasks. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the comparison to modern deep RL methods and clarify some technical details. The paper's contribution is novel, well-executed, and has significant potential impact on animation, robotics, and neuroscience applications.
