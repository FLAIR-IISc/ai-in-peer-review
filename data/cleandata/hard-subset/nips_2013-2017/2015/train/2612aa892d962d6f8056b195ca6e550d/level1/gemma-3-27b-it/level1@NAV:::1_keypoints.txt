- Proposes training RNNs as feedback controllers by combining trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised learning for real-time control of diverse dynamical systems.
- Uses trajectory optimizer to generate high-quality trajectories, then trains a neural network to mimic optimal control actions.
- Introduces optimal feedback gains, noise injection, and a distributed training architecture to improve robustness and scalability.
- Extends prior work by successfully applying the approach to complex 3D locomotion and multiple character morphologies with a single network.
- Technical soundness: well-motivated integration of trajectory optimization and neural networks; feedback gains improve robustness.
- Impressive empirical results: demonstrated control of swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal characters (video evidence).
- Clear and well-organized presentation of method and experiments.
- Scalable approach via distributed training for computationally demanding tasks.
- Weakness: limited comparative analysis versus alternative control methods (e.g., direct NN training, MPC) across all characters.
- Weakness: insufficient investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity (noise levels, regularization weights).
- Weakness: high computational cost and lengthy training reliant on trajectory optimization.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel combination addressing challenging control problems, strong demonstrations, and application potential in robotics/animation/biomechanics.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical comparisons and practical limitations due to computational expense.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparative analysis and hyperparameter sensitivity.
