- Proposes a novel method to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers using supervised learning plus trajectory optimization (CIO).  
- Combines trajectory optimization with learning to produce stable, realistic behaviors without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.  
- Well-written paper with clear methodology supported by helpful figures and equations.  
- Detailed experimental description and organized presentation of results.  
- Demonstrates realistic, stable controllers for swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped tasks.  
- Method remains robust to noise and perturbations; noise injection during training improves controller robustness.  
- Provides detailed comparisons showing superior stability and realism versus naive static-trajectory NN training and MPC.  
- Introduces a new method for generating optimal feedback gains as part of the approach.  
- Reliance on a specific trajectory optimization method (CIO) may limit general applicability.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational cost, which affects practical applicability.  
- Unclear how well the method generalizes to all character types or tasks.  
- High originality and significance with potential impact on robotics, animation, and gaming.  
- Recommendation: accept, with request for more detail on computational cost and broader applicability.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
