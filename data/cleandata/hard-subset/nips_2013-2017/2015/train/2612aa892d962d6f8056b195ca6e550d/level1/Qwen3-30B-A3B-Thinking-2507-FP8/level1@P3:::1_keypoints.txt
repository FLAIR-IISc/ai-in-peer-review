- Introduces a method that uses trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data for neural network controllers instead of directly optimizing the policy.  
- Demonstrates stable 3D locomotion for diverse morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without task-specific features or motion capture.  
- Advances prior work by extending successful control from 2D to challenging 3D tasks.  
- Introduces principled noise injection for robustness and training for unexpected task changes.  
- Proposes a novel method for computing optimal feedback gains by reusing trajectory optimization computations.  
- Technical formulation and algorithmic description are clear and well-executed.  
- Experimental validation is thorough, with ablation studies showing the importance of noise injection and joint optimization.  
- Implementation and distributed training architecture are well-detailed.  
- Theoretical justification is limited; lacks rigorous analysis of why the approach works.  
- Insufficient empirical comparison to modern deep RL methods (e.g., SAC, PPO) that have strong control performance.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear mathematical exposition and visualizations.  
- Minor clarity issues: abrupt transition from network architecture to training details and unclear link between sensory inputs and physical state evolution.  
- Originality: novel combination of trajectory optimization with neural network training and robustness techniques, and significant 3D generalization over prior work.  
- Significance: substantial impact for animation, robotics, and biomechanics, but needs clearer positioning relative to recent deep RL advances.  
- Recommendation: strong paper worth acceptance with minor revisions to compare against modern deep RL and clarify technical details.
