- Proposes a novel method combining supervised learning and Contact-Invariant Optimization to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for complex characters.  
- Claims to solve substantially harder, 3D problems than prior work limited to 2D.  
- Demonstrates stable, realistic behaviors for diverse characters (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped).  
- Does not rely on motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines, increasing generality.  
- Provides detailed neural network architecture and training procedure to aid reproducibility.  
- Includes thorough evaluation with comparisons to other approaches and parameter analyses.  
- Assumes substantial background in neural networks, control theory, and graphics, reducing accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks a clear explanation of how the neural network generalizes to new situations and tasks.  
- Evaluation is largely qualitative and lacks clear quantitative performance metrics.  
- Omits discussion of practical limitations for real-world deployment (training data requirements, overfitting risks).  
- Originality: presents a novel combination of techniques but builds on existing prior work.  
- Significance: high potential impact for computer graphics, robotics, and biomechanics.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and significant contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and provide stronger, quantitative evaluation.
