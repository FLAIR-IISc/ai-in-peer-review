- Presents a unified RNN-based framework that learns real-time feedback controllers for diverse 3D simulated characters without motion capture or task-specific engineering.
- Uses an iterative alternating optimization that jointly trains a trajectory optimizer (CIO) and an RNN policy, regularizing trajectories to be reproducible by the policy.
- Injects noise into sensory inputs during training to ensure closed-loop robustness and prevent divergence.
- Introduces a principled method to compute optimal feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer and uses a first-order approximation to determine actions under noisy inputs.
- Implements the system in a distributed architecture to handle the high computational cost of trajectory optimization.
- Demonstrates success across swimmers, flyers, bipeds, and quadrupeds using the same architecture and algorithm.
- Builds on guided policy search literature but advances state of the art by scaling to complex 3D locomotion tasks.
- Ablation study shows joint optimization and noise injection are necessary; removing them causes policy failure.
- Technical contribution of extracting feedback gains from CIO is novel and crucial for robustness.
- Paper is technically strong, well-written, and presented as a complete system with implementation details.
- Minor clarity issue: the action representation includes velocities (a_t = [q̇_t, ṙ_t, f_t]), which blurs the line between control and state and could be foreshadowed earlier.
- High significance: advances automated controller synthesis and yields high-quality interactive controllers likely to inspire follow-up work.
- Lacks direct empirical comparison to contemporary pure reinforcement learning baselines to substantiate claims about their shortcomings.
- Relies on a computationally expensive trajectory optimizer and distributed cluster (e.g., ~2.5 hours on ~30 nodes), which may limit accessibility and raises concerns about optimizer sensitivity.
