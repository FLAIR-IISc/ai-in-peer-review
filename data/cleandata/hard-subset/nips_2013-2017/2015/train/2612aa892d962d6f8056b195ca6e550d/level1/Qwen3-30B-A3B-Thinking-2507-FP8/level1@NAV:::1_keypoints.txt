- Presents a novel method to train neural nets as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal).

- Core innovation: combine supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to learn controls and dynamics simultaneously.

- Introduces technical improvements: interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, noise injection during training, training for unexpected task changes, and using trajectory optimization to obtain optimal feedback gains.

- Demonstrates success in stable 3D locomotion where prior methods were limited to 2D.

- Strong experimental validation across multiple character types and locomotion tasks.

- Ablation studies show joint optimization outperforms naive network training by ~100x and that noise injection is critical to prevent overfitting.

- Produces diverse, realistic behaviors across morphologies using a single architecture without motion capture or task-specific features.

- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.

- Related work is properly contextualized, though the “unified” approach could use more explicit comparison to recent character-control methods.

- Strengths: novel trajectory-optimization + NN combination, impressive empirical results, thorough ablations, clear contributions to feedback gain computation.

- Weaknesses: theoretical justification for noise injection and feedback-gain methods is limited; computational complexity trade-offs versus alternatives like MPC not fully addressed.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen theoretical discussion and comparative analysis.
