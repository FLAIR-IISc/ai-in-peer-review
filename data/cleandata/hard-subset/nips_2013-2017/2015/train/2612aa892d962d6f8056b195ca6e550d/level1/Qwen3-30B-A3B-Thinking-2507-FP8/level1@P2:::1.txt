

# Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a wide range of complex dynamical systems, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped locomotion. The authors combine trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization) with supervised learning to train neural networks that generate stable, realistic behaviors without requiring motion capture or task-specific features. The key innovation lies in interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, training for unexpected task changes, and using trajectory optimization to obtain optimal feedback gains. The approach differs from prior work by solving substantially harder 3D locomotion problems rather than just 2D cases.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a well-structured methodology with thorough experimental validation. The authors provide a clear mathematical formulation of the problem (equation 1) and develop a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm (Algorithm 1) that effectively addresses the joint optimization challenge. The ablation studies in Section 8.1 provide strong empirical evidence for the importance of key components (joint optimization, noise injection). The implementation details (Section 6) and experimental results (Section 8) demonstrate the method's effectiveness across diverse character types. The authors appropriately acknowledge limitations (e.g., "both ablations above lead to policy rollouts that quickly diverge from expected behavior"), showing careful evaluation of their approach. However, the paper would benefit from more detailed discussion of the computational complexity trade-offs between the trajectory optimization and neural network training phases.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and well-organized, with a logical flow from problem statement to methodology to results. The mathematical formulations are clear and well-explained. The paper effectively uses figures to illustrate the approach and results. Some aspects could be clarified, particularly regarding the exact network architecture (beyond "3 hidden layers with 250 units each") and the specific implementation details of the trajectory optimization. The supplementary video is strongly recommended for full understanding of the results, but the paper itself provides sufficient detail for reproduction.

**Originality:** The paper makes a significant contribution by combining trajectory optimization with neural network training for control in a way that solves substantially harder problems than previous work. While related work exists (cited as [9, 11]), the authors clearly demonstrate that their method achieves 3D locomotion for multiple character types, whereas previous work was limited to 2D. The specific techniques for injecting noise in a principled way (Section 2.1), calculating optimal feedback gains (Section 3.2), and the distributed training architecture (Section 6) represent novel contributions. The paper adequately references related work, though it might benefit from more explicit comparison with recent deep reinforcement learning approaches to control (e.g., [14, 15]).

**Significance:** The results are highly significant, demonstrating a single neural network architecture that can control diverse complex characters (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with realistic locomotion in 3D space. This represents a substantial advance over previous work that required specialized controllers for each character type. The ability to handle interactive changes in task specification (Section 5) and the demonstration of stable behavior despite perturbations (Section 8.1) further enhance the practical significance. The method has clear potential applications in robotics, animation, and gaming, and the paper's conclusion (Section 9) appropriately suggests future directions for extending this work to more complex behaviors and biological plausibility.

**Strengths:**
- Demonstrates successful control of diverse 3D locomotion behaviors with a single architecture
- Provides thorough ablation studies showing the necessity of key components (joint optimization, noise injection)
- Develops novel techniques for calculating optimal feedback gains and incorporating noise
- Presents a well-structured distributed training approach that scales effectively
- Shows significant improvement over naive approaches (2 orders of magnitude better performance)

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited discussion of computational complexity trade-offs between trajectory optimization and neural network training
- Some implementation details (e.g., exact network architecture, hyperparameters) could be more explicit
- Comparison with recent deep reinforcement learning approaches for control (e.g., [14, 15]) could be strengthened
- The paper doesn't fully address how the method scales to even more complex systems with higher degrees of freedom

**Conclusion:** This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of neural network control by presenting a novel approach that combines trajectory optimization with neural network training to achieve interactive control of diverse complex characters. The method demonstrates significant improvements over prior work, particularly in solving 3D locomotion problems that were previously challenging. The experimental results are compelling and the method has clear potential for applications in robotics and animation. While some implementation details could be clarified, the paper is well-written, technically sound, and makes a significant contribution to the field.

I recommend acceptance. This work addresses an important problem in control with a novel and effective solution that advances the state of the art. The results are well-supported by experiments, and the approach has clear potential for broader application.
