- Proposed method: alternating optimization that trains RNN controllers by interleaving Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with supervised regression on noisy state-action pairs; actions = desired velocities + contact forces; small recurrent state for memory.  
- Key technical contribution: principled sensory-noise injection during training with adjusted targets via locally optimal feedback gains computed efficiently from the direct optimizer using a perturbation/Woodbury trick.  
- Training system: distributed, asynchronous architecture.  
- Results: real-time, stable behaviors demonstrated for swimming, flying, and 3D bipedal/quadrupedal locomotion without motion capture, hand-crafted state machines, or contact schedules.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on guided policy search/CIO and relates to model-free policy search (e.g., TRPO), contrasting with MPC and graphics controllers (e.g., SIMBICON).  
- Quality — positives: method is technically sound, well-motivated, feedback-gain derivation is a useful advance, and ablations support design choices.  
- Quality — negatives: quantitative evaluation is limited (missing success rates, energy, disturbance rejection, generalization metrics), comparison to MPC is qualitative, and there are no formal stability guarantees.  
- Physical realism concern: execution-time “T=1” optimization with soft dynamics (allowing root forces) raises questions about physical fidelity and transfer to real systems.  
- Clarity — positives: generally clear and well-organized with a precise problem statement and algorithm.  
- Clarity — negatives: missing specific details (recurrent state dimension, full network hyperparameters, feedback-gain regularization sensitivity, timing/inference rates) and minor typos.  
- Originality: novel integration of CIO with NN policy learning, especially the efficient computation of local gains for noisy supervision and application to diverse 3D morphologies with a single architecture.  
- Significance: compelling demonstrations for animation and model-based control; potential to influence robust control if strengthened with quantitative benchmarks and stricter dynamics enforcement; real-world robotics impact remains speculative.  
- Pros for acceptance: elegant, practical integration and novel feedback-gain computation; strong diverse 3D demonstrations and ablations; clear potential impact.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited quantitative evaluation and weak baselines vs state-of-the-art, concerns about soft dynamics and action-as-velocity reducing realism/transfer, and missing reproducibility details.  
- Overall recommendation: leaning toward accept, contingent on adding quantitative metrics, stronger baselines, and clarifying execution-time dynamics.
