- Presents a method that trains RNNs as feedback controllers by interleaving trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised learning, noise injection, and optimization for unexpected task changes.
- Demonstrates stable, realistic locomotion (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal walking) in 3D without motion capture or task-specific features.
- Technically sound combination of trajectory optimization and neural network training, with a logically presented stochastic optimization framework.
- Ablation studies strongly support the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection for robust closed-loop control.
- Experiments use MuJoCo and cloud/GPU resources, lending credibility to the empirical results across diverse morphologies.
- Lacks rigorous theoretical convergence analysis for the proposed stochastic optimization algorithm.
- Mathematical notation is sometimes dense and assumes familiarity with trajectory optimization (CIO).
- Algorithmic description could be clearer with pseudocode to clarify data flow between optimization and network training.
- Original contribution lies in the synergistic integration of existing techniques and their successful extension to 3D locomotion.
- Novel technical elements include noise injection, optimization for task perturbations, and feedback gain calculation.
- Significant impact: automates controller design, handles diverse morphologies with a single architecture, and shows robustness to noise and unexpected changes.
- Generates a valuable trajectory-optimization dataset and insights into the relationship between trajectory optimization and neural network training.
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial improvement for 3D locomotion, novel combination of methods, morphology-general controller, robustness, and strong validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing rigorous theoretical analysis, dense notation, qualitative rather than quantitative comparison to direct methods, and reliance on MuJoCo.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with high technical soundness, originality, and significance; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and theoretical discussion.
