- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped locomotion).  
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate stable, realistic behaviors.  
- Introduces a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm that alternates between trajectory optimization and neural-network regression.  
- Proposes a perturbation method to compute optimal feedback gains to stabilize optimal trajectories.  
- Learns both optimal controls and a dynamics model simultaneously from sensory states to actions.  
- Builds on reinforcement learning and stochastic optimal control while drawing from graphics and robotics for a general-purpose sensory representation.  
- Demonstrates scalability by solving harder 3D locomotion problems than prior work.  
- Shows generality: applicable across a wide range of systems without specialized initialization or mocap data.  
- Offers efficiency via a parallelizable distributed optimization scheme.  
- High computational and memory requirements may limit applicability to smaller systems.  
- Algorithm is sensitive to noise; authors mitigate this by injecting noise during training.  
- No theoretical guarantees provided for convergence or stability of the method.  
- Could benefit from additional experiments and comparisons to existing methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, novel contribution with strong potential impact in robotics, graphics, and biomechanics.
