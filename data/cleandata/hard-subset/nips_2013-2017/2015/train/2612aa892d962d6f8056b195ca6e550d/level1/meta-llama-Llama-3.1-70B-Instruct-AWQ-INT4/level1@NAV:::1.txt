The paper "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks" presents a novel approach to training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for a wide range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The authors combine supervised learning with trajectory optimization, specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO), to generate stable and realistic behaviors.

**Summary of Main Ideas:**
The paper introduces a neural network control policy that maps a sensory state of the character to an optimal action, and learns both optimal controls and a model of dynamics simultaneously. The authors propose a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm that alternates between trajectory optimization and neural network regression. They also introduce a perturbation method to compute optimal feedback gains, which helps to stabilize the resulting optimal trajectories.

**Relation to Previous Work:**
The authors build upon previous work in reinforcement learning and stochastic optimal control, and provide a more efficient and scalable approach to solving complex control problems. They also draw inspiration from computer graphics and robotics, and provide a more general-purpose representation of the sensory state.

**Strengths:**

1. **Scalability:** The authors demonstrate the ability to solve substantially harder problems than previous work, including stable and realistic locomotion in three-dimensional space.
2. **Generality:** The method is applicable to a wide range of dynamical systems and tasks, without requiring specialized initializations or motion capture data.
3. **Efficiency:** The authors provide a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm that can be parallelized, making it more efficient than previous approaches.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Complexity:** The algorithm requires a significant amount of computation and memory, which may limit its applicability to smaller systems.
2. **Noise Sensitivity:** The authors acknowledge that the algorithm is sensitive to noise, and propose injecting noise during training to improve robustness.
3. **Lack of Theoretical Guarantees:** The authors do not provide theoretical guarantees for the convergence or stability of the algorithm.

**Originality:**
The paper presents a novel approach to training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers, and combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization in a new way. The authors also introduce a perturbation method to compute optimal feedback gains, which is a new contribution to the field.

**Significance:**
The paper has significant implications for the fields of robotics, computer graphics, and control theory. The authors demonstrate the ability to generate stable and realistic behaviors for a wide range of dynamical systems and tasks, which could lead to new applications in areas such as character animation, robotics, and biomechanics.
