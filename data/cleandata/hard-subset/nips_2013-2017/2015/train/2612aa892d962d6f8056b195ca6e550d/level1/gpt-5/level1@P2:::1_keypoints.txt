- Proposes alternating optimization coupling direct trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised learning of an RNN policy for real-time control of diverse 3D characters.
- Policy outputs desired state velocities, recurrent updates, and contact forces; execution uses a one-step constrained optimization to track these outputs.
- Key components: noisy-sensor training with first-order corrected targets using local feedback gains.
- Novel efficient gain computation inside CIO via soft-constrained perturbation and reuse of Gauss-Newton Hessian inverse.
- Distributed, asynchronous alternation between trajectory updates and NN regression with proximal regularization.
- Training on branched trajectory segments to emulate interactive goal changes.
- Positions work within “learning from an optimizer” literature (GPS, iLQG, Mordatch & Todorov) and contrasts with model-free RL and classical controllers.
- Alternating scheme and proximal/noise tricks are well-motivated and technically sound.
- Gain derivation using soft constraint and Woodbury identity is neat and leverages existing CIO computations.
- First-order target correction is consistent with local linearization.
- Demonstrates diverse 3D behaviors and ablations, but quantitative evaluation is limited.
- Lacks task-level metrics (success rates, errors, energy) and quantitative comparisons to GPS/iLQG, TRPO, or strong MPC baselines.
- Robustness claims (noise, goal changes) are not supported by perturbation tests.
- Execution-time T=1 optimization with soft dynamics (possibly root forces) undermines claims of purely learned feedback and physical realism.
- Several implementation specifics are underspecified (dimensionality/role of recurrent state r, cost terms/weights, rollout success criteria, contact model).
- Clarity is generally good, but needs explicit definitions for r, cost lists, and execution-vs-training cost differences.
- Requests quantitative convergence measures and sensitivity analyses for α, λ, η.
- Action parameterization (contact forces vs torques) needs justification and discussion of physical plausibility.
- Novelty is incremental: main contributions are principled use of local gains for corrected targets, efficient gain computation in CIO, and practical asynchronous training with noise.
- Significance: impactful for character animation and practitioners using direct trajectory optimization; moderate for broader ML/RL due to limited comparisons and reliance on online optimization.
- Pros: scalable practical algorithm, efficient stabilizing gain method, thoughtful noise usage, distributed training, useful ablations.
- Cons: limited quantitative evaluation and baselines, reliance on T=1 soft optimization at execution, insufficient recurrent/cost details, no external perturbation tests, action includes contact forces hard to realize on robots.
- Suggested improvements: add quantitative task metrics and perturbation tests, compare to GPS/iLQG/TRPO and matched MPC, clarify and ablate recurrent state, report sensitivity to hyperparameters and noise, tighten execution realism or clarify animation focus, and release implementation details/code.
- Recommendation: leaning toward accept for graphics/control value contingent on stronger quantitative evaluation and clearer execution-time realism; weaker case for NeurIPS without those additions.
