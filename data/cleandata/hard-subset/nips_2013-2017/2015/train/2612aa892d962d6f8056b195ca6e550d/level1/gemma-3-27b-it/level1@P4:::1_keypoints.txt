- Presents a method combining RNN controllers with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to control diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, walking).  
- Network is trained to predict optimal controls and a dynamics model using optimizer-generated trajectories, with noise injection and distributed stochastic optimization.  
- Single network architecture can control characters with varying morphologies.  
- Builds on prior work that combines trajectory optimization and neural nets but extends substantially to realistic, stable 3D locomotion.  
- Technically sound: clear algorithm, solid integration of CIO, RNNs, and optimal feedback gains to improve robustness.  
- Novelty: scaling trajectory-optimization + neural networks to 3D locomotion and the proposed stochastic optimization/feedback-gain methods are new.  
- Significance: broad applications (robotics, animation, biomechanics) and automated generation of stable, adaptable controllers.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; supplementary video and related-work connections are strong.  
- Empirical validation: demonstrates control across multiple characters and includes ablation studies showing component importance.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks systematic exploration of hyperparameter effects.  
- Weakness — computational cost: heavy reliance on cloud compute and long training times limits accessibility.  
- Weakness — limited comparison to direct methods: no systematic quantitative comparison to alternatives that the authors criticize.  
- Weakness — simulator fidelity: sensitivity of results to the quality of the physics model (MuJoCo) is not analyzed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a hard 3D locomotion problem, shows strong empirical results, creatively combines established techniques, and has high impact potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved hyperparameter sensitivity, high computational demands, and insufficient quantitative baselines.  
- Overall assessment: a strong, significant contribution with manageable limitations; recommendation to accept.
