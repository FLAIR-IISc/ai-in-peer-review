This paper presents a novel method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a wide range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The approach combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and using the trajectory optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains.

The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method, including a detailed description of the neural network architecture, the trajectory optimization process, and the distributed training architecture. The paper also includes a thorough evaluation of the method, comparing its performance to other approaches and demonstrating its ability to learn successful controllers for a variety of characters and tasks.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors' ability to tackle a challenging problem in control theory and provide a novel solution that combines machine learning and optimization techniques.
* The clarity and organization of the paper, making it easy to follow and understand the method and its evaluation.
* The thorough evaluation of the method, including comparisons to other approaches and demonstrations of its ability to learn successful controllers for a variety of characters and tasks.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The paper assumes a significant amount of background knowledge in control theory, machine learning, and optimization techniques, which may make it difficult for non-experts to fully understand and appreciate the method.
* The evaluation of the method is primarily focused on its ability to learn successful controllers for a variety of characters and tasks, but does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity or scalability of the approach.
* The paper does not provide a clear discussion of the limitations of the method or potential avenues for future research.
