- Presents a method training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped walking).  
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training and using the optimizer to obtain feedback gains.  
- Network simultaneously learns control policies and a dynamics model; actions are defined as velocities.  
- Builds on reinforcement learning and stochastic optimal control literature and claims to solve harder 3D continuous high-dimensional problems.  
- Novel combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization enabling stable, realistic behaviors.  
- Handles complex dynamics and high-dimensional state/action spaces, demonstrating broad capability.  
- Thorough evaluation including comparisons to naive neural network training and model-predictive control.  
- Clear, detailed algorithm and implementation description that aids reproducibility.  
- Assumes substantial prior knowledge in RL and stochastic optimal control, limiting accessibility for non-experts.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity, raising concerns for large-scale applications.  
- Evaluation focused mainly on locomotion; generalization to other tasks or domains is unclear.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and well-organized with helpful figures and tables.  
- Original in its approach and application to challenging continuous high-dimensional dynamics.  
- Potentially significant impact for robotics, animation, and gaming, and provides avenues for future work.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution warranting acceptance, conditional on addressing complexity analysis and generalizability concerns.
