- Proposes training a single RNN controller via alternating between Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory generation and supervised regression on those trajectories.  
- Joint optimization of trajectories and network parameters is formulated and solved via alternating minimization (Eq. 4).  
- Novel method to extract optimal feedback gains directly from the CIO Hessian, avoiding a separate LQR pass.  
- Principled sensory-noise injection plus analytically derived noisy-action targets produces robust closed-loop policies.  
- Distributed, asynchronous training architecture to handle large computational load.  
- Demonstrates a unified framework that scales to diverse 3D tasks: swimming, flying, bipedal and quadrupedal locomotion.  
- Experimental results (and supplementary video) convincingly show stable, realistic, and controllable behaviors without motion capture or hand-crafted state machines.  
- Ablation studies strongly support that joint optimization and noise injection are essential for performance and stability.  
- Technical components and theoretical framing are sound and well-reasoned.  
- Runtime policy execution involves a single-step optimization per frame to match desired velocities, meaning the network is not the sole runtime component.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear overview, consistent notation, and supportive figures/tables.  
- Reproducibility is limited: reliance on MuJoCo, CIO, and custom distributed infrastructure plus missing specific trajectory-cost details.  
- Original contributions include generality/scale to 3D, principled denoising for closed-loop control, and feedback extraction from direct optimization.  
- Main weaknesses: high computational training cost, extra runtime optimization step complexity, and unverified claims of superiority over reinforcement learning (no direct RL comparisons).  
- Overall significance is high; the work advances automatic controller synthesis for complex physical characters and is recommended for acceptance.
