The paper "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks" presents a novel method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a range of dynamical systems and tasks. The authors propose a combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization, specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO), to learn a neural network policy that can generate stable and realistic behaviors for complex characters.

The paper relates to previous work in the field of neural network control, reinforcement learning, and stochastic optimal control. However, the authors claim that their method solves substantially harder problems, particularly in three-dimensional space, where previous work was limited to two-dimensional characters. The authors also mention related work in the field of computer graphics and robotics, where physics-based controllers are used to generate realistic movements.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposed method is able to generate stable and realistic behaviors for a range of complex characters, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking.
* The method does not require motion capture or task-specific features or state machines, making it a more general and flexible approach.
* The authors provide a detailed description of the neural network architecture and the training procedure, making it easier to reproduce the results.
* The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the method, including comparisons with other approaches and an analysis of the effects of different parameters on the performance of the algorithm.

However, there are also some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The paper assumes a significant amount of background knowledge in neural networks, control theory, and computer graphics, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the neural network learns to generalize to new situations and tasks, which is a critical aspect of any control algorithm.
* The evaluation of the method is mostly qualitative, and the authors do not provide a clear metric for evaluating the performance of the algorithm.
* The paper does not discuss the potential limitations and challenges of applying this method to real-world problems, such as the need for large amounts of training data and the potential for overfitting.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural network control. However, the authors build on previous work in the field, and the paper does not present a completely new idea.

The significance of the paper is high, as it presents a method that can be used to generate realistic movements for complex characters, which has many potential applications in fields such as computer graphics, robotics, and biomechanics. The paper also has the potential to contribute to the development of more advanced control algorithms for complex systems.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of neural network control. However, there are some areas for improvement, particularly in terms of clarity and evaluation.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of neural network control.
* The method is able to generate stable and realistic behaviors for a range of complex characters.
* The paper includes a thorough evaluation of the method, including comparisons with other approaches.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper assumes a significant amount of background knowledge in neural networks, control theory, and computer graphics.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of how the neural network learns to generalize to new situations and tasks.
* The evaluation of the method is mostly qualitative, and the authors do not provide a clear metric for evaluating the performance of the algorithm.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly in terms of clarity and evaluation.
