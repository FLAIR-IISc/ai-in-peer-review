- Presents a novel method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped) by combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art results in continuous high-dimensional dynamics and demonstrates effectiveness across multiple morphology and locomotion tasks.  
- Uses a single network architecture and shared parameters to create all controllers without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.  
- Produces stable, realistic behaviors and highlights the importance of joint optimization and noise injection for performance.  
- Method complexity may hinder implementation and tuning for practitioners without expertise in trajectory optimization and neural networks.  
- Relies on a specific trajectory optimization method (Contact-Invariant Optimization) and a particular network architecture, potentially limiting generality.  
- Lacks systematic comparisons with alternative methods (e.g., model-predictive control), making full evaluation difficult.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant advance with clear motivation, thorough analysis, and strong experimental validation across tasks.  
- Arguments against acceptance: approach may be too specialized/complex, limited generality due to methodological reliance, and insufficient comparative benchmarking.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; strengths outweigh weaknesses, with suggestions to add comparisons and simplify for broader accessibility.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
