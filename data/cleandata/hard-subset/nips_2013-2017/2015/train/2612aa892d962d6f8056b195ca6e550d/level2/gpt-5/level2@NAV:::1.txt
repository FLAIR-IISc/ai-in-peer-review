Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes an alternating optimization framework that couples direct trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised regression of a recurrent neural network to obtain near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D characters (swimmer, bird, bipeds, quadrupeds). Key ideas include: labeling noisy inputs with first-order corrected actions using optimal feedback gains computed efficiently from the trajectory optimizer; injecting sensory and hidden-unit noise for robustness; and distributed, asynchronous training. At test time, the policy outputs desired velocities/forces and a one-step dynamics-consistent optimization advances the state. The work is closely related to guided policy search and trajectory-optimization-based policy learning (Levine & Koltun ICML’14; Mordatch & Todorov RSS’14) and contrasts with MPC and model-free deep RL (e.g., TRPO).

Strengths:
- Addresses interactive control of high-DOF, contact-rich systems in 3D with a single architecture, demonstrating compelling qualitative behaviors and cyclic gaits without motion capture or state machines.
- Sound integration of trajectory optimization with NN regression; the feedback-gain-based denoising target is a thoughtful and efficient idea that likely improves local stabilizability.
- Ablations (no joint optimization, no noise) support the necessity of the proposed components.
- Practical distributed system and GPU training; clear path to real-time control.

Weaknesses and limitations:
- Empirical support is largely qualitative. Claims of “near-optimal” control lack quantitative metrics (tracking error, energy, robustness, success rates) and statistical significance.
- Baseline comparisons are limited. No head-to-head against GPS/TRPO with equal compute, and the MPC comparison is anecdotal.
- Execution relies on a soft-constrained T=1 optimization (with possible root forces), which may compromise physical realism and complicate transfer to hardware.
- Sensitivity analyses (e.g., λ for gains, noise scales, size of recurrent state) and generalization tests (to unseen goals/morphologies) are minimal; reproducibility may be challenging without code and exact costs.
- Notation and some derivations (Sec. 3.2) could be clearer; several references are incomplete.

Quality: Technically plausible and careful in algorithmic design, but evaluation is incomplete. Clarity: Generally well written; could better specify cost weights and provide more figures/metrics. Originality: Combines known components; the gain-conditioned denoising target and broad 3D scope are novel. Significance: High potential for animation/control; less clear for robotics until hard dynamics and torque control are demonstrated.

Constructive suggestions:
- Report quantitative metrics and robustness tests; include compute/runtime and success rates.
- Compare against GPS/TRPO and MPC under matched costs/horizons.
- Remove root forces or show torque-only results; test on standard MuJoCo benchmarks and, if possible, a hardware proxy.
- Provide sensitivity/ablation on recurrence, λ, and noise; release code and trajectories.

Pros for acceptance: elegant integration; compelling demonstrations; practical impact for interactive control. Cons: insufficient quantitative evaluation and baselines; questions about physical fidelity and transfer.
