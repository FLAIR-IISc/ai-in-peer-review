This paper presents a novel approach to training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for a wide range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The authors also introduce a number of technical improvements, including injecting noise during training, training for unexpected changes in task specifications, and using the trajectory optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains.
