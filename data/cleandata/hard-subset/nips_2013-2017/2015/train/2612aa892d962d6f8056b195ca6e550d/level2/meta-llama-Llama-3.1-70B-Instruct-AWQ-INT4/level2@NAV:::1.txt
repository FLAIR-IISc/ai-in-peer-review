**Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"**

**Summary**

This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The approach combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and using the trajectory optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on various tasks and characters, showcasing its potential for interactive real-time control in robotics, animation, and gaming.

**Claims and Support**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The proposed method can generate stable and realistic behaviors for a range of dynamical systems and tasks.
2. The approach can learn both optimal controls and a model of dynamics simultaneously.
3. The method can be applied to various characters and tasks without requiring specialized initializations or motion capture.

The authors provide strong support for these claims through:

* Experimental results demonstrating the effectiveness of their method on various tasks and characters.
* A thorough analysis of the optimization process and the role of noise injection in improving the robustness of the learned policies.
* A comparison with other methods, including naive neural network training and model-predictive control (MPC), highlighting the advantages of their approach.

**Usefulness and Novelty**

The ideas presented in the paper are practically useful for several reasons:

* The method can be applied to a wide range of tasks and characters, making it a versatile tool for interactive real-time control.
* The approach can learn both optimal controls and a model of dynamics simultaneously, reducing the need for manual tuning and expertise.
* The use of noise injection during training improves the robustness of the learned policies, making them more suitable for real-world applications.

The paper presents a significant improvement over existing approaches, particularly in its ability to handle complex tasks and characters in three-dimensional space. The authors' use of trajectory optimization and stochastic neural networks together combines correct behavior with real-time interactive use, making it a novel contribution to the field.

**Field Knowledge and Completeness**

The paper demonstrates a strong understanding of the relevant literature and common knowledge in the field. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the optimization process and the role of noise injection, and they discuss the limitations of their approach. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide sufficient details for reproducibility.

**Limitations and Future Work**

The authors acknowledge the limitations of their approach, including the need for further analysis of the optimization process and the potential for overfitting. They also discuss potential future work, including the incorporation of muscle actuation effects and the exploration of different neural network architectures.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper presents a novel and effective approach for interactive real-time control of complex characters and tasks.
* The authors provide strong experimental results and a thorough analysis of the optimization process.
* The method has potential applications in robotics, animation, and gaming.

Con:

* The paper may benefit from further analysis of the optimization process and the role of noise injection.
* The authors could provide more details on the computational resources required for training and testing their method.
* The paper may not be suitable for readers without a strong background in neural networks and optimization.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting this paper for publication. The authors present a novel and effective approach for interactive real-time control of complex characters and tasks, and they provide strong experimental results and a thorough analysis of the optimization process. While there are some limitations and potential areas for improvement, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field and has potential applications in robotics, animation, and gaming.
