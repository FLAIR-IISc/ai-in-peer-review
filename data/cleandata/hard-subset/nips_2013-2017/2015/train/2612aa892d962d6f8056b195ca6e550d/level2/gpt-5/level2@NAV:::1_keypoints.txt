- Proposal: alternating optimization couples direct trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised RNN regression to learn near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D characters.

- Core techniques: label noisy inputs with first-order corrected actions using optimal feedback gains computed from trajectory optimizer.

- Core techniques: inject sensory and hidden-unit noise during training and use distributed, asynchronous training for robustness and scalability.

- Core techniques: at test time the policy outputs desired velocities/forces and a one-step dynamics-consistent optimization advances the state.

- Relation to prior work: closely related to guided policy search and trajectory-optimization-based policy learning; contrasted with MPC and model-free RL (e.g., TRPO).

Strengths
- Demonstrates interactive control of high-DOF, contact-rich 3D systems with a single architecture, producing convincing cyclic gaits without mocap or state machines.

- Sound integration of trajectory optimization and NN regression; feedback-gain-based denoising target is effective and efficient for local stabilizability.

- Ablation studies (no joint optimization, no noise) support the necessity of proposed components.

- Practical distributed/GPU training approach with clear path toward real-time control.

Weaknesses / Limitations
- Evaluation is largely qualitative; claims of “near-optimal” lack quantitative metrics (tracking error, energy, robustness, success rates) and statistical significance.

- Baseline comparisons are limited; no head-to-head experiments against GPS/TRPO with matched compute and the MPC comparison is anecdotal.

- Use of soft-constrained T=1 optimization and possible root forces may harm physical realism and complicate hardware transfer.

- Sensitivity analyses and generalization tests (λ for gains, noise scales, recurrent size, unseen goals/morphologies) are minimal, hindering reproducibility without code and exact costs.

- Notation and some derivations need clarification; several references are incomplete.

Constructive suggestions
- Add quantitative metrics, robustness tests, compute/runtime and success rates, and report statistical significance.

- Compare directly against GPS/TRPO and MPC under matched costs and horizons.

- Remove root forces or provide torque-only experiments; test on standard MuJoCo benchmarks and, if possible, hardware proxies.

- Provide sensitivity/ablation studies on recurrence, λ, and noise, and release code and trajectories to aid reproducibility.

Overall assessment
- Quality: technically plausible and careful algorithmically, but evaluation incomplete.

- Originality: combines known components; gain-conditioned denoising target and broad 3D scope are novel.

- Significance: high potential for animation/control; unclear for robotics until harder dynamics and torque control are demonstrated.

- Pros for acceptance: elegant integration, compelling demonstrations, practical impact; Cons: insufficient quantitative evaluation, weak baselines, and physical-fidelity/transfer concerns.
