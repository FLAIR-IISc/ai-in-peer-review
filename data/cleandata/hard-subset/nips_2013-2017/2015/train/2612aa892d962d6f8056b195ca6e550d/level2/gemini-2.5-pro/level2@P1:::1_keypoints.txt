- Combines offline trajectory optimization with a neural-network policy via a joint, block-alternating optimization scheme.
- Demonstrates a single unified algorithm controlling diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal).
- Injects sensory noise during training to improve robustness, training the network to predict optimal actions for perturbed states.
- Introduces a novel, efficient method to extract optimal feedback gains from a contact-invariant trajectory optimizer.
- Uses a data-generation scheme that branches new trajectory segments from existing ones to reflect interactive goal changes.
- Employs a distributed architecture to manage the computational cost of trajectory optimization.
- Extends prior trajectory-optimization-guided policy search work to a much wider and more complex set of 3D characters.
- Strength: Highly significant results producing stable, realistic, and interactive controllers with emergent gaits across morphologies.
- Strength: Technically sound joint optimization; ablation study convincingly shows benefits over static training and need for noise injection.
- Strength: Original contribution in feedback-gain computation and integration with sensory-noise training; works “out of the box” on varied tasks.
- Weakness: Lacks reproducibility details—components and weightings of the cost function C(X) are not specified.
- Weakness: State/action design includes contact forces f (non-standard); intuition for predicting forces and simulator interactions is unclear.
- Weakness: Qualitative MPC comparison is insightful but a more detailed or quantitative comparison would strengthen the argument.
- Weakness: Role and impact of recurrent network components are not analyzed; an ablation or discussion is needed.
- Weakness: Policy execution timing for the single-timestep optimization is not reported; real-time claim needs performance figures.
- Pros summary: General, impactful method with novel feedback-gain technique and strong empirical evidence.
- Cons summary: Missing key reproducibility details, unexplored recurrence contribution, and limited comparison to alternatives.
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper with weaknesses addressable in revision—recommend acceptance.
