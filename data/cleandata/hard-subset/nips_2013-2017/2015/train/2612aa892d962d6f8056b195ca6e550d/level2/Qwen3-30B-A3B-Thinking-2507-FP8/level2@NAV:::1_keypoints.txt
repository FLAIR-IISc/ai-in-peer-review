- Presents a novel method to train neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D locomotion (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) using a single unified architecture.
- Core innovation: interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to obtain optimal feedback gains.
- Uses noise injection during training and joint optimization with trajectory optimization to improve robustness and controller quality.
- Claims interactive control without motion capture or task-specific features across different character morphologies.
- Strong empirical support: successful locomotion across multiple character types with a single network.
- Quantitative improvement: outperforms naive static-trajectory neural training by two orders of magnitude.
- Ablation studies convincingly show necessity of noise injection and joint optimization.
- Produces cyclical, stable behaviors, addressing limitations of MPC’s non-cyclical solutions.
- Method is technically sound, clearly described (Algorithm 1), and appears reproducible.
- Evaluation is comprehensive but lacks discussion of scalability to more complex behaviors.
- Limited discussion of real-world robotics applicability and no comparison to recent reinforcement learning methods.
- Computational requirement (~2.5 hours training) may limit practical deployment in some contexts.
- Overall judgment: high-quality contribution and significant advancement; reviewer recommends acceptance.
