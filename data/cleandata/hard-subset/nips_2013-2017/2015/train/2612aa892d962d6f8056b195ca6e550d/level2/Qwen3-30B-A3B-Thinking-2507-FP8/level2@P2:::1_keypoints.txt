- Paper proposes training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D dynamic systems by interleaving supervised learning with Contact-Invariant trajectory optimization, adding a few recurrent units and noise injection.  
- Single architecture successfully applied to swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal locomotion without task-specific features or motion capture.  
- Clear, technically sound algorithm (Algorithm 1) that properly interleaves trajectory optimization and network training.  
- Ablation study convincingly shows necessity of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Empirical results demonstrate stable, realistic 3D locomotion across multiple character types; supplementary video likely strong visual evidence.  
- Provides a clear theoretical justification for computing optimal feedback gains (section 3.2).  
- Claims of solving “substantially harder problems” lack sufficient quantitative comparison to prior work to substantiate the claim.  
- Scalability is not addressed: training takes hours on cloud resources and unclear how it scales to more complex systems or larger morphologies.  
- Insufficient comparison to contemporary reinforcement learning methods (e.g., PPO, DDPG) that also learn control policies without task-specific features.  
- Unclear distinction from other approaches that simultaneously learn dynamics models; novelty relative to such work not fully clarified.  
- Ablation shows the specific noise-injection method matters, but the paper does not explain why it outperforms standard regularization techniques like dropout.  
- Method has clear significance for animation, robotics, and biomechanics by unifying disparate approaches and enabling diverse, stable locomotion with one architecture.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong technical contribution and empirical results, with minor weaknesses addressable in a camera-ready version.
