- Summary: Proposes an alternating, distributed framework combining offline trajectory optimization (CIO) with a recurrent neural network policy, using noise injection and extracted optimal feedback gains to produce real-time controllers for diverse 3D locomotion tasks.

- High significance/impressive results: Single algorithm yields stable, natural-looking locomotion and emergent cyclic gaits across swimmer, flyer, biped, and quadruped morphologies.

- Strong technical contributions: Novel method to extract well-conditioned optimal feedback gains from the CIO solver and principled noise injection are key to robust closed-loop learning.

- Sound methodology and evaluation: Alternating joint optimization is well-motivated; ablation studies and MPC comparisons convincingly support the main claims.

- Clarity and organization: Paper is well-written, structured, and transparent about parameters.

- Weakness — unclear policy execution: Section 7 implies a T=1 optimization at every frame, contradicting the claim of a fast feed-forward controller and needing clarification on cost and necessity.

- Weakness — vague abstract phrasing: The claim that “the network is not learning a control policy, but rather the dynamics under an implicit policy” is confusing and misleading.

- Weakness — lack of recurrent state analysis: No analysis of what the learned memory state r encodes, despite its likely importance for cyclic behaviors.

- Suggestion — clarify runtime: Substantially revise Section 7 with an explicit algorithm/diagram and computational-cost analysis to explain how the policy output becomes the final state update.

- Suggestion — rephrase abstract: Replace the confusing sentence with clearer wording (e.g., “learning to generate reference velocities for a physics-enforcing optimizer”) to accurately describe the network’s role.

- Suggestion — analyze recurrent state: Add visualization or analysis of r over a gait cycle (for at least one character) to show whether it encodes phase or other useful signals.

- Overall verdict: Excellent, significant contribution with impressive results; main weaknesses are clarifications and additional analysis that can be addressed in revision.
