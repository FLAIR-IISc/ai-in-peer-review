- Presents a method combining trajectory optimization (CIO) and supervised learning to train RNN feedback controllers for diverse 3D dynamical systems.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results: successful control of swimming, flying, and walking 3D characters using a single network architecture.  
- Well-motivated problem statement and clear articulation of limitations of traditional controller design.  
- Novel and compelling combination of trajectory optimization and neural network learning.  
- Ablation studies indicate joint optimization and noise injection are crucial to performance.  
- Video demo effectively visualizes the learned behaviors.  
- Comparisons to other direct methods are impressionistic rather than systematic, weakening claims of superiority.  
- Insufficient analysis of why the method produces “more realistic and purposeful” behaviors; deeper policy analysis is needed.  
- Description of the CIO trajectory optimization process is too brief and may confuse readers unfamiliar with CIO.  
- No justification or discussion for selected algorithmic constants (σε, σγ, α, λ, η) or how they were tuned.  
- Distributed training architecture discussion is superficial and lacks implementation and scaling details.  
- Limited discussion of future work and concrete plans to address current limitations (e.g., simple sensory representation).  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a challenging problem, achieves state-of-the-art results, compelling experiments, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of systematic comparisons, limited explanation of key components (CIO, distributed training), and weak treatment of limitations/future work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add comparisons, clarify CIO and parameter choices, and expand discussion of distributed training and future directions.
