- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers by combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization).  
- Leverages trajectory optimization to generate training data, enabling real-time execution of learned policies for complex dynamics.  
- Demonstrates robustness and generality across diverse morphologies and tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal walking).  
- Avoids reliance on motion capture or hand-crafted state machines, automating controller synthesis.  
- Uses noise injection, training for unexpected task changes, and computes optimal feedback gains for stability and performance.  
- Experimental results and accompanying video convincingly show realistic, stable behaviors across a broad set of characters.  
- Technical contributions (e.g., optimal feedback gain calculation) appear crucial to achieved stability.  
- Lacks rigorous comparative analysis with direct learning methods mentioned in the paper.  
- Hyperparameter choices (α, σε, σγ, η) appear arbitrary; a sensitivity analysis or justification is missing.  
- Distributed training architecture description omits details on synchronization and communication overhead.  
- Computational cost is vaguely described as “hours”; reliance on cloud computing and concrete costs are not discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance, with revisions to address comparative analysis and computational/hyperparameter details.
