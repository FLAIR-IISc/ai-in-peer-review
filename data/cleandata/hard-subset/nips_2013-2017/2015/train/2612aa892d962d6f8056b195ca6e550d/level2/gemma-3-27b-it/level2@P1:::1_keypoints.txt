- Proposes training RNN controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, walking) by interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, noise injection, and task optimization.
- Uses trajectory optimization to learn neural network controllers rather than merely generate training data, extending prior work to 3D locomotion and varied morphologies.
- Demonstrates impressive, stable, and realistic behaviors across systems, supported by a convincing video.
- Novel combination of existing techniques overcomes limitations of each used in isolation.
- Introduces technical improvements (noise injection, feedback-gain optimization) that materially aid performance.
- Employs a distributed training architecture to address scalability and computational challenges.
- Provides thorough evaluation including ablation studies and comparison with naive neural network training.
- Paper is generally well-written and well-organized.
- Lacks detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (noise levels, learning rates, architectures).
- Limited systematic comparison to alternative RL/direct methods (e.g., DDPG, TRPO); claims about direct methods need stronger evidence.
- Needs a more detailed breakdown of computational cost, especially per-character/task training time.
- Tasks are primarily locomotion; method’s generality to goal-directed behaviors (manipulation, reaching) is untested.
- Explanation of feedback gain calculation (Section 3.2) is dense and could use a more intuitive description or diagram.
- Technically sound and experimentally supported; addresses limitations and future work.
- Clarity is good overall but some sections require expanded explanation.
- Originality is modest but contributions (feedback gain optimization, 3D application) are significant.
- Results are significant and impactful for robotics, animation, and biomechanics.
- Arguments for acceptance: state-of-the-art results, novel effective technique, demonstrated scalability and robustness, thorough experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited RL comparisons, missing hyperparameter analysis, and substantial computational cost.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address hyperparameter analysis and comparisons (suggest adding a table comparing to other RL methods).
