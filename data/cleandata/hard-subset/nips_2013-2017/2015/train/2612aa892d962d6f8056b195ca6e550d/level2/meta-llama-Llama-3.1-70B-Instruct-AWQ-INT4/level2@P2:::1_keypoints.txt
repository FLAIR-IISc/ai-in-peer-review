- Proposes a novel method to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers using trajectory optimization (CIO).
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization and extracts optimal feedback gains from the optimizer.
- Introduces technical improvements: noise injection during training and training for unexpected task changes.
- Demonstrates method on diverse dynamical tasks: swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking across morphologies.
- Results show learned controllers produce stable, realistic movements and handle noise/perturbations.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and supported by helpful figures and equations.
- Thorough related-work discussion and clear distinction from prior methods.
- Highlights key challenge that data for neural network controllers is harder to obtain than for vision/speech.
- Emphasizes the importance of noise injection to prevent overfitting and improve generalization.
- Main contribution: a broadly applicable method to learn complex, stable controller behaviors.
- Quality: technically sound with experiments that support the approach.
- Originality: novel approach and useful technical additions (noise, feedback-gain extraction).
- Significance: potential to make a notable impact in robotics and control.
- Weakness: lacks systematic quantitative comparison with existing methods.
- Weakness: insufficient implementation details (network architecture, training parameters).
- Suggested improvements: add detailed baseline comparisons and provide full implementation/training details.
