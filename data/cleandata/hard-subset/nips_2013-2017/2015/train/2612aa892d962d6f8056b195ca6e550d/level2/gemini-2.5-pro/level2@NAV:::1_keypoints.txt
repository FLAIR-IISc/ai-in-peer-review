- Core contribution: alternates offline trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) with supervised learning to train RNNs as real-time controllers for simulated characters.  
- Novelty/advance: successfully applies this paradigm to complex 3D characters with diverse morphologies and locomotion styles, beyond prior 2D work.  
- Significance/originality: unified framework yields high-quality, stable controllers and emergent natural gaits without task-specific engineering.  
- Technical quality: sound approach with principled noise injection and a novel method to extract optimal feedback gains; ablation study validates key components.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and clearly explains the alternating optimization methodology.  
- Weakness — comparative evaluation: evaluation is mainly qualitative/internal and lacks quantitative comparisons to contemporary SOTA methods (e.g., TRPO) or common benchmarks/metrics.  
- Weakness — reproducibility: critical details of trajectory-optimization cost functions (forms and weightings) are omitted, hindering reproducibility.  
- Overall: strong, novel contribution with impressive results; recommend acceptance despite evaluation and reproducibility gaps.
