- Proposes a novel method to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers by combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization.  
- Training includes noise injection and conditioning on unexpected changes in task specifications to improve robustness.  
- Demonstrated across diverse tasks: swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with varied body morphologies.  
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical formulation, algorithmic details, helpful figures/tables, and a strong supplementary video.  
- Strength: novel combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization as a significant contribution.  
- Strength: method is effective across a variety of tasks and morphologies.  
- Strength: clear explanation of approach and algorithmic implementation.  
- Strength: noise injection during training enhances policy robustness.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with existing methods such as MPC and other trajectory optimization approaches.  
- Weakness: does not analyze computational complexity, important for real-time control considerations.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of approach limitations and future research directions.  
- Originality: presents a novel approach but builds on existing work and needs more comparative analysis.  
- Significance: important contribution with potential applications in robotics, animation, and gaming.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.  
- Rating/confidence: 8/10 rating, 9/10 reviewer confidence.
