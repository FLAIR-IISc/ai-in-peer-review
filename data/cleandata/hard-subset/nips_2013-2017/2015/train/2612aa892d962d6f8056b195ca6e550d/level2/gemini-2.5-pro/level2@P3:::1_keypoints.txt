- Proposes a unified method that uses offline trajectory optimization to generate training data for an RNN feedback controller.  
- Frames training as a large-scale joint optimization over trajectories and network parameters, solved by distributed block-alternating optimization.  
- Introduces a principled noise-injection scheme that augments training targets using optimal feedback gains to improve robustness.  
- Presents a novel, efficient method to extract optimal feedback gains directly from the direct trajectory optimizer.  
- Demonstrates the approach on diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) using a single general-purpose architecture without task-specific engineering.  
- Builds on prior trajectory-optimization + policy learning work but scales to more complex, unstable 3D locomotion problems with key technical innovations.  
- Strength: significant and general advance toward universal motor-control methods, producing natural emergent gaits.  
- Strength: technically sound with novel contributions (feedback gain extraction and noise-targeting) and supportive ablation studies.  
- Strength: clear writing and convincing empirical results, including supplementary video and comparison to MPC showing policy regularization benefits.  
- Weakness: need clearer specification of state-action representation and distinction between desired outputs and inner-loop tracking controller.  
- Weakness: reproducibility could be improved by detailing cost functions, weighting, and training data generation strategies (e.g., branching goal distributions).  
- Weakness: clarify the RNN state update mechanics, since using ṙt as part of action is non-standard and suggests explicit integration.  
- Weakness: would benefit from a more detailed discussion comparing trade-offs with model-free RL methods (e.g., sample efficiency vs. dynamics-model reliance).  
- Overall verdict: strong, important contribution with minor clarifications needed for readability and reproducibility.
