- Introduces a method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers using supervised learning combined with Contact-Invariant Optimization (trajectory optimization).
- Network jointly learns optimal controls and a dynamics model without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.
- Training interleaves supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injects noise, and trains for unexpected task changes; trajectory optimizer provides feedback gains as well as actions.
- Novel combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization for controller synthesis.
- Scales to larger, harder control problems, including three-dimensional characters and tasks.
- Training with noise injection improves robustness and reduces overfitting.
- Enables real-time interactive control of characters.
- Method is computationally expensive, requiring cloud resources and GPU implementations.
- Evaluation is limited to a small set of tasks and characters, possibly lacking generality.
- Comparisons are limited—only against naive neural network training on static optimal trajectories.
- Presents a new approach to neural network controllers by integrating trajectory optimization.
- Extends prior work to handle three-dimensional characters and behaviors.
- Potential significant impact on robotics and animation due to real-time interactive control capabilities.
- Advances the state-of-the-art in neural network controllers for dynamical systems.
- Suggests adding more comprehensive evaluations and comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.
- Suggests analyzing computational cost and trade-offs between resources and performance.
- Recommends exploring extensions to other applications such as robotics and gaming.
- Pro arguments: novel approach, scalability to complex 3D tasks, and robustness from noise injection.
- Con arguments: limited evaluation scope, high computational requirements, and potentially unfair baseline comparisons.
