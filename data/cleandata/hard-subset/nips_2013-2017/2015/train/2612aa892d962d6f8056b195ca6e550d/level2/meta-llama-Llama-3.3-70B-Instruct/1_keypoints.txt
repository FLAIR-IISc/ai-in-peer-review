- Proposes a novel method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for physically consistent interactive character control.
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization so a neural network learns from an optimizer to reproduce behaviors online.
- Demonstrates stable, realistic behaviors across multiple dynamical systems and tasks (swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking, varied morphologies).
- Theoretical analysis and experiments support the paper’s main claims.
- Clear, detailed exposition of method, including interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization and extracting optimal feedback gains from the optimizer.
- Uses noise injection during training and a distributed training architecture to mitigate overfitting.
- Produces realistic behaviors without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines, and supports unexpected task changes and real-time interactive control.
- Strength: well-written, well-organized, thorough related work and illustrative figures/tables.
- Limitation: requires large computing resources, which may restrict accessibility.
- Limitation: potential for overfitting and the approach may not suit all characters or tasks.
- Could benefit from more comparisons to other methods and deeper analysis of results.
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Overall: significant and practical contribution with potential applications in robotics and computer graphics.
