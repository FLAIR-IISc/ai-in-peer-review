- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped walking) for interactive real-time control.
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training and using the optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains.
- Claims the method produces stable, realistic behaviors across tasks and characters.
- Claims it can learn optimal controls and a dynamics model simultaneously.
- Claims applicability to various characters/tasks without specialized initialization or motion capture.
- Supports claims with experiments across tasks/characters, analysis of optimization and noise injection, and comparisons to naive NN training and MPC.
- Practically useful: versatile across tasks, reduces manual tuning, and noise injection improves policy robustness for real-world use.
- Novel contribution: handles complex 3D tasks better than prior work by combining trajectory optimization with stochastic neural networks for real-time use.
- Demonstrates strong field knowledge, is well-organized, and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.
- Limitations include need for deeper analysis of the optimization process, potential overfitting, and exploration of other architectures or muscle actuation.
- Additional concerns: insufficient details on computational resources and possible inaccessibility to readers without strong NN/optimization background.
- Recommendation: accept — novel, effective approach with strong experimental support and impactful applications in robotics, animation, and gaming.
