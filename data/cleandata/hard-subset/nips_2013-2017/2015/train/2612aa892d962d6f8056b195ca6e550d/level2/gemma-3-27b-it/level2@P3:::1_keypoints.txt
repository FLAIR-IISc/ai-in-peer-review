- Proposes training RNN feedback controllers by combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization (CIO) to control diverse dynamical systems.  
- Targets high-dimensional continuous control problems (swimming, flying, legged locomotion) and scales to complex 3D locomotion.  
- Demonstrates a single network architecture that controls multiple characters, showing robustness and generalization.  
- Uses noise injection and optimization of feedback gains to improve robustness of learned controllers.  
- Empirical results convincingly show stable 3D locomotion and outperform naive neural network training approaches.  
- Claims relative data-efficiency compared to traditional RL, though quantitative comparisons are limited.  
- Lacks systematic, quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art RL algorithms (e.g., TRPO, PPO), making some claims unsupported.  
- Distributed training description omits details on communication overhead, synchronization, and scalability evidence.  
- Assertion of linear scaling with cluster size is optimistic and unsupported by empirical data or plots.  
- Ablation study supports joint optimization and noise injection but misses sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (noise levels, regularization).  
- Choice and impact of cost functions on behavior are noted but not thoroughly explored.  
- Some related-work citations are dated; more recent work (e.g., model-based RL, differentiable physics engines) should be included.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective approach with convincing results, but requires additional evaluations and analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on RL comparisons, scaling details, hyperparameter sensitivity, and updated citations.
