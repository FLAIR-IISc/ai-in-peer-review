- Proposes alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised regression to RNN policies for real-time control of diverse 3D contact-rich characters.  
- Key novelty: include local feedback gains (As) from CIO in regression targets via an efficient perturbation method to improve closed-loop stability.  
- Injects sensory and hidden-unit noise during training and uses a first-order correction a + As ε to combat covariate shift.  
- Training is distributed/asynchronous; at test time the policy runs in real time with a one-step constrained optimization for dynamics consistency.  
- Strength: addresses a challenging, relevant problem with a clear synthesis of trajectory optimization and neural policies.  
- Strength: technical novelty in deriving feedback-gain-informed targets from the CIO linear systems.  
- Strength: careful robustness treatment via noise injection and ablations showing importance of joint optimization and noise.  
- Strength: single training framework works across multiple morphologies producing plausible cyclic gaits without motion capture.  
- Strength: practical system design achieving interactive runtime performance.  
- Strength: empirical qualitative comparison to an MPC baseline showing behavioral regularity differences.  
- Weakness: runtime uses one-step soft dynamics/contacts allowing root forces, which may hide issues in torque-limited, strictly physical settings.  
- Weakness/question: need quantitative measures of residual/root forces during execution and their effect on realism.  
- Weakness/question: policy outputs velocities and contact forces instead of torques; request ablation on action parameterizations (torques, joint targets).  
- Weakness/question: central feedback-gain contribution needs quantitative ablations (with/without As, LQG vs perturbation As, sensitivity to λ and conditioning).  
- Weakness/question: clarify whether a single policy spans morphologies or each morphology is trained separately; discuss sample efficiency and scaling.  
- Weakness: limited quantitative evaluation of robustness/generalization (random starts, pushes, model errors, terrain variation).  
- Weakness: limited baselines — recommend comparisons to GPS/TRPO/PILCO or physics-based controllers (e.g., SIMBICON) on simpler domains or metrics.  
- Clarity gaps: unspecified recurrent unit size/structure, BPTT usage, training horizon, dataset scale, and alternating optimization convergence diagnostics.  
- Quality: method is technically sound and supported by experiments, but needs more quantitative metrics (fall rates, contact realism, actuation effort) and stronger baselines.  
- Originality: extends guided policy search ideas but distinct in efficient gain extraction from CIO and application to diverse 3D contact behaviors.  
- Significance: could be impactful if validated with stricter dynamics and stronger quantitative evidence; gain extraction technique is independently useful.  
- Suggestions: add robustness metrics and strict-dynamics experiments, ablate action parameterization and recurrence, compare to standard RL/GPS baselines on simpler domains.  
- Suggestions: report sensitivity to λ/σ and dataset size, plot performance vs. number of trajectories, and release detailed implementation/training hyperparameters.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, technically novel, demonstrates joint optimization and noise benefits, real-time execution across morphologies.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on soft dynamics at runtime, limited quantitative evaluation and baselines, and missing methodological details and ablations.
