- Proposes a novel method training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers by combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization.  
- Introduces noise injection, interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, and using optimal feedback gains to improve robustness and generalization.  
- Demonstrates the approach on diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal locomotion) and morphologies.  
- Uses Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) for trajectory generation and integrates neural network regression effectively.  
- Unifies disparate control approaches into a single framework via joint optimization of trajectories and neural network policies.  
- Shows practical significance for robotics, gaming, animation, and biomechanics with interactive, real-time control.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with detailed methodology and experiments.  
- Includes ablation studies and comparisons to alternatives like model-predictive control.  
- Provides sufficient implementation details (algorithms, parameters, architectures) to enable reproducibility.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art reinforcement learning methods for continuous control.  
- Raises scalability concerns due to reliance on cloud computing and GPUs; computational efficiency discussion is limited.  
- Does not explore complex multi-task behaviors or transitions (e.g., locomotion to manipulation) to show broader behavioral diversity.  
- Fails to explicitly discuss limitations such as dependence on accurate physical models and real-world transfer challenges.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, with suggestions to add RL comparisons, discuss scalability, and acknowledge limitations.  
- Pro: Innovative combination of trajectory optimization and neural network training that generalizes across morphologies.  
- Con: Limited RL benchmarking, scalability/resource concerns, and lack of explicit limitations discussion.  
- Overall rating: Strong Accept.
