- Paper proposes jointly learning trajectories and a policy to enable interactive control of complex characters, combining trajectory optimization with policy learning.  
- Main empirical insight: decoupling into alternating subproblems allows reuse of existing methods and works well in practice.  
- Reviewer not deeply familiar with the domain, so cannot judge broader impact; sees technical prowess but no novel theoretical insights.  
- High level of technical sophistication in both concept and implementation, with impressive results.  
- Claimed advantages of using sensory noise during training and of jointly learning trajectories and policy are clearly demonstrated.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, with appropriate detail and useful cross-references.  
- Motivation for the extra optimization step in Section 7 could be better explained; reviewer expected the policy to run without additional optimization.  
- Reviewer questions whether residual trajectory cost or policy regression error necessitates re-optimization and whether dynamics would prevent physical implausibility.  
- Reviewer appreciates that re-optimizing each timestep permits use of larger timesteps.  
- Line 153: asks for one or two references for the statement "similar to recent approaches"; notes keeping updates small in parameter space is counterintuitive but understandable for block-alternation.  
- Typo at line 89: "to unify."  
- Overall positive impression of results and optimization scheme, but uncertain about novelty and significance due to domain unfamiliarity.
