Summary
The paper proposes an alternating optimization framework that combines direct trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization, CIO) with supervised learning of a neural policy to achieve interactive, real-time control of complex 3D characters (bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmer, bird). The policy maps a “sensory” state s = [q, r, q̇prev, fprev] to an action a = [q̇, ṙ, f], and the learned recurrent state r is updated by integration of ṙ. To bridge the gap between offline trajectory optimization and online control, the authors: (i) inject Gaussian noise in sensory inputs during training and adjust the target action with locally optimal feedback gains a_s obtained from a novel perturbation-based sensitivity analysis of the CIO objective; (ii) alternate between optimizing trajectories with a penalty that encourages consistency with the current policy and fitting the policy by regression on noisy, locally corrected targets; (iii) use proximal regularization to stabilize an asynchronous, distributed implementation; and (iv) execute the policy in real time by solving a T=1 constrained optimization (implicit step) to match desired velocities/forces under dynamics and contact constraints. Experiments qualitatively demonstrate stable, cyclic locomotion across diverse morphologies; ablations indicate that joint optimization and noise are important; a brief comparison to MPC suggests the approach regularizes toward predictable gaits.

Relation to prior work
The approach is closely related to Guided Policy Search (GPS) and follow-ups (e.g., Levine & Koltun, ICML’14), which also alternate trajectory optimization with supervised policy learning under a consistency penalty (often handled via ADMM). Compared to GPS, this work: uses CIO as the direct collocation method in contact-rich settings; introduces a practical way to obtain stabilizing feedback gains from a direct optimizer via a soft-constraint perturbation that reuses Gauss-Newton Hessians; and scales to 3D characters. The noise-injection and local linearization of target actions are reminiscent of denoising autoencoders and of local linear models in iLQR/DDP. The comparison with MPC is apt; the method can be viewed as regularized trajectory optimization guided toward a parametric policy.

Strengths
- Tackles a challenging, high-dimensional control problem in 3D with contact and underactuation, producing visually plausible locomotion across diverse morphologies with a single training recipe.
- Methodologically solid decomposition: difficult contact-rich planning offloaded to CIO; train a compact, reactive policy via supervised learning for interactive use.
- Novel and useful derivation of stabilizing gains from a direct optimizer using a soft constraint (λ) and Woodbury identity, avoiding ill-conditioning typical of post-hoc LQG on collocated trajectories.
- Principled noise injection: not only perturbing inputs but also adjusting targets with local gains (a + a_s ε), which is a data augmentation well matched to closed-loop deployment.
- Asynchronous distributed system with proximal regularization; pragmatic and likely of interest to practitioners.
- Clear ablation signal: joint optimization and noise matter; qualitative comparison to MPC indicates different inductive biases (predictable cyclic behaviors).

Weaknesses and questions
- Limited quantitative evaluation. Apart from one figure/table (not included here), claims of “two orders of magnitude” improvement and “real-time” execution are not supported by detailed metrics. Missing: success rates, trajectory costs, robustness to perturbations, tracking error, fps/latency during execution.
- Baselines are weak. The most relevant comparisons would include GPS variants (ADMM-based, DDP-based), model-free RL (e.g., TRPO, newer actor-critic for continuous control), and imitation-style policies trained on CIO data without gain-augmented targets. Only a static-dataset regression and no-noise ablation are shown.
- Realism vs. soft dynamics. During execution, constraints are enforced softly and root forces are allowed; this can mask physical violations and undermines the claim of physically realistic control. Please quantify constraint violations (penetration, complementarity residuals, root forces) and report how often the controller “cheats.”
- Contact and mode changes. Gains are computed around a nominal CIO solution with a fixed (soft) contact schedule. How robust are these gains when contact timing/topology changes under noise or user retargeting? Any evidence of gain-induced instabilities near contact switches?
- Policy representation and recurrence. The paper treats r as a learned memory updated by ṙ but does not report its dimensionality, initialization, or whether the network itself is recurrent. The comment about spectral radius “just above 1” suggests recurrent dynamics, but the policy is described as feedforward. Please clarify, and report sensitivity to r’s size.
- Generalization and coverage. Training data come from CIO trajectories with branched segments and small sensory noise. How far can the user retarget goals and headings before failure? Any evaluation of extrapolation beyond the CIO data manifold?
- Clarity/detail gaps. Important hyperparameters (λ’s role in gain conditioning), computational cost per CIO iteration, and the precise dimensions/scaling of s and a are under-specified. The distribution of goal changes and their effect on behavior diversity are not quantified.

Quality
Technically competent with a sound optimization and learning setup; the gain derivation is a nice contribution. However, empirical validation is mostly qualitative; stronger quantitative evidence and baselines are needed to support claims of robustness, scalability, and superiority over prior GPS/RL methods. The reliance on soft constraints during execution is a significant caveat.

Clarity
Generally clear high-level presentation, but several notational inconsistencies and missing details hinder reproducibility (dimensions/scales of variables, r size, λ sensitivity, exact training regimen, convergence criteria). References have formatting issues; some are incomplete. Figures and more precise metrics would help.

Originality
Moderately original. The overall alternating framework is close to GPS; the main novel aspects are: (i) using CIO in this loop for contact-rich 3D behaviors; (ii) the perturbation-based computation of feedback gains from a direct optimizer and using them for target augmentation under sensory noise; and (iii) an asynchronous proximal variant. These are useful, but incremental relative to GPS/DDP literature.

Significance
Potentially significant for character animation and contact-rich control, especially as a practical recipe that yields interactive controllers. For NeurIPS, impact would be stronger with rigorous quantitative comparisons to contemporary policy learning methods and with evidence that the approach improves robustness/efficiency beyond prior GPS.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add quantitative evaluations: success rate over randomized initial states/goals, perturbation robustness (pushes, sensor noise), constraint violation statistics, and runtime (policy query + T=1 solve).
- Compare against GPS (e.g., ADMM-GPS with LQR/iLQR local controllers), TRPO/modern actor-critic on the same tasks, and policies trained without gain-augmented targets.
- Ablate the feedback-gain augmentation (no a_s ε), and vary λ to show conditioning/robustness trade-offs.
- Report the size and role of r, and whether the policy is recurrent; analyze sensitivity to memory size.
- Tighten the execution-time physics (harder constraints, reduced root force magnitudes) or quantify their effects.
- Improve clarity and references; include architectural/hyperparameter details sufficient to reproduce.

Pros
- Practical, scalable pipeline for 3D contact-rich control; diverse demonstrations.
- Sensible fusion of CIO and supervised policy learning with well-motivated noise and gain augmentation.
- Useful, low-overhead method to extract stabilizing gains from a direct optimizer.

Cons
- Weak quantitative evidence and baselines; unclear robustness and physical fidelity at execution.
- Overlap with GPS; novelty resides mainly in engineering choices and gain computation.
- Some clarity/reproducibility gaps.
