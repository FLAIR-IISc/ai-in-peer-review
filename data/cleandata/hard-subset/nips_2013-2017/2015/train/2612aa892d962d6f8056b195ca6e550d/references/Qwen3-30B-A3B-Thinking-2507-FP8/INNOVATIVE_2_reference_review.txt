

# Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

This paper presents an innovative approach to generating neural network controllers for interactive, physics-based character control across diverse morphologies and locomotion types. The authors combine trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization) with neural network policy learning through a novel distributed stochastic optimization framework. The key insight is that trajectory optimization can generate high-quality training data for neural networks, which can then be used for real-time interactive control, overcoming the limitations of purely offline trajectory optimization or direct reinforcement learning approaches.

The paper makes several important contributions. First, it demonstrates a generalizable approach to character control that works across bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, and flying characters in 3D space, a significant advancement over previous work limited to 2D characters. Second, the authors propose a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm (Algorithm 1) that jointly optimizes trajectories and neural network policies, enabling the network to learn optimal feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer. Third, they introduce a principled approach to noise injection that improves robustness and generalization without requiring extensive data augmentation.

The significance of this work is substantial. By demonstrating that a single neural network architecture can control diverse character types with different morphologies and locomotion styles, the authors present a unified framework that could replace the current ad-hoc approaches requiring specialized controllers for each character type. The ability to generate stable, realistic 3D locomotion through a single unified method represents a meaningful advance over previous work.

The paper's novelty is compelling. While the combination of trajectory optimization and neural networks has been explored before (e.g., [9, 11]), this work is the first to successfully extend these methods to 3D locomotion with diverse character types. The authors convincingly demonstrate that their approach yields more realistic and purposeful behaviors compared to direct training methods, and they provide a principled theoretical foundation for their distributed stochastic optimization approach.

Strengths:
- Novel approach that combines trajectory optimization with neural network training to achieve interactive real-time control
- Demonstrates impressive generalization across diverse 3D character types (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, flying)
- Provides a principled method for incorporating noise to improve robustness
- Includes thorough ablation studies showing the necessity of key components (joint optimization, noise injection)
- Presents a unified framework that could replace disparate specialized controllers

Weaknesses:
- The paper doesn't provide a comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art reinforcement learning approaches (e.g., SAC, PPO) that have been successfully applied to similar control problems
- The trajectory optimization step is computationally intensive (2.5 hours for training), which may limit practical adoption
- Some technical details about the noise injection and feedback gain calculation could be clarified (e.g., how exactly σ_ε and λ are selected)
- The paper doesn't address potential limitations in handling complex interactions with the environment beyond simple locomotion

The paper's technical quality is strong, with a clear theoretical foundation and well-designed experiments. The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the importance of the authors' key innovations. The writing is clear and well-organized, making the technical contributions accessible.

This paper represents a bold step toward unified character control that addresses a significant gap in the literature. While the approach is not perfect and some methodological details could be strengthened, the core idea is novel and the results are impressive. The authors have successfully demonstrated a method that can generate diverse, stable, interactive locomotion across multiple character types in 3D space—a significant advancement over previous work.

I believe this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field. The novelty of the approach, combined with the impressive results across diverse character types, makes it stand out from incremental improvements in the literature. The unified framework for character control represents a significant conceptual advance that could influence future work in animation, robotics, and biomechanics.

The paper aligns well with NIPS' interest in novel machine learning approaches to complex problems. While the work is applied to character control, the distributed stochastic optimization framework could have broader implications for other control and learning problems.

I recommend acceptance. The paper's novel approach to combining trajectory optimization with neural network learning for interactive control represents a significant step forward, and the results demonstrate clear advantages over previous methods. The minor weaknesses noted above do not detract from the paper's overall contribution and novelty.
