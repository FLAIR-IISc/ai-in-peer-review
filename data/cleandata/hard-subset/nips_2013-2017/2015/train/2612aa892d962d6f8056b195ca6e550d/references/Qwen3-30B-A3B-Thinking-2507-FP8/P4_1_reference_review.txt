

This paper proposes a method for training neural network controllers that enable interactive, real-time control of diverse complex 3D characters (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, and flying) by combining trajectory optimization with neural network training. The authors introduce a distributed stochastic optimization approach that alternates between optimizing character trajectories using Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) [12] and training neural network policies. Key innovations include noise injection for robustness and a novel method for computing optimal feedback gains during trajectory optimization. The method is applied to 3D locomotion tasks, representing a significant advancement over previous work limited to 2D characters [9, 11].

The paper builds on trajectory optimization techniques [12] and neural network controllers [9, 11], but makes a clear contribution by demonstrating successful 3D locomotion for diverse character types. Previous work could not achieve stable 3D locomotion, while this method produces realistic behaviors across multiple character morphologies without specialized initialization.

Strengths:
1. Technical soundness: The paper presents a well-structured optimization framework with careful mathematical formulation. The distributed stochastic optimization algorithm (Algorithm 1) is clearly described, and the ablation studies (Section 8.1) provide strong empirical evidence for the importance of the authors' key innovations (joint optimization and noise injection), showing a two orders of magnitude performance improvement over baseline approaches.

2. Experimental validation: The method is thoroughly evaluated across multiple character types in 3D, including bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, and flying creatures. The results demonstrate stable locomotion where previous work failed, with the supplementary video providing compelling visual evidence of diverse behaviors.

3. Clarity: The paper is well-organized with clear explanations of the methodology, mathematical formulations, and results. The implementation details (Section 6) provide sufficient information for reproducibility, and the supplementary video significantly enhances understanding.

4. Practical significance: The approach enables interactive control for diverse character types without specialized initialization, addressing a key limitation of previous work. The method has clear potential applications in robotics, animation, and gaming.

Weaknesses:
1. Limited theoretical analysis: While the empirical results are strong, the paper lacks deeper theoretical analysis of why the distributed stochastic optimization approach converges and how it compares to alternative optimization methods.

2. Implementation details: The paper mentions using cloud computing and GPU implementations but lacks specific details about computational requirements and scalability beyond the current experiments.

3. Comparison to MPC: The brief comparison to Model Predictive Control (Section 8.1) is limited; a more thorough comparison would strengthen the significance claim.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims. The ablation studies effectively demonstrate the importance of the authors' key innovations, and the work represents a complete piece of research with clear methodology, implementation, and evaluation.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for experts to understand and reproduce the results. The mathematical formulations are clear, and the experimental results are well-presented.

Originality: The paper presents a novel combination of trajectory optimization and neural network training for interactive character control. The authors clearly differentiate their work from previous contributions [9, 11] by demonstrating success in 3D space, which previous work could not achieve.

Significance: The results are significant as they demonstrate a method that enables interactive control of diverse 3D characters, advancing the state of the art beyond previous 2D approaches. The method has clear potential for practical applications in robotics, animation, and gaming, and addresses a challenging problem in a demonstrable way.

Pro-acceptance arguments: The paper solves a challenging problem (3D interactive control) with a technically sound approach, provides compelling empirical validation across diverse character types, and demonstrates clear practical significance. The method advances the state of the art and offers a practical solution with broad applicability.

Con-acceptance arguments: The paper lacks deeper theoretical analysis of the optimization approach, and some implementation details are insufficient for full reproducibility. The comparison to MPC is limited.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of character control by demonstrating a method that enables interactive control for diverse 3D characters. The technical approach is sound, the results are compelling, and the method has clear potential for practical applications. The paper meets all key criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance. While the theoretical analysis could be strengthened, the empirical results and practical significance make this a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
