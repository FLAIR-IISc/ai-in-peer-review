

# Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

This paper presents a method for automatically generating neural network controllers for complex character movements through a joint optimization of trajectory optimization and neural network policy learning. The authors address the challenge of creating interactive, real-time controllers for diverse 3D characters (bipedal, quadrupedal, flying, swimming) by combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with neural network training. The key contribution is a distributed stochastic optimization framework that alternates between trajectory optimization and neural network regression while injecting noise to improve robustness. The approach differs from previous work [9, 11] by achieving stable 3D locomotion (previously limited to 2D) and producing more realistic, purposeful behaviors compared to direct neural network training methods.

The paper builds upon trajectory optimization techniques [12] and neural network control approaches [3, 6, 14], but makes several significant technical contributions: (1) a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm (Algorithm 1) that jointly optimizes trajectories and neural networks; (2) a method for computing optimal feedback gains without relying on LQG, which produces better-conditioned gains; and (3) a noise injection strategy that improves robustness and prevents overfitting.

**Strengths:**

1. **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a well-structured optimization framework with clear mathematical derivations. The distributed stochastic optimization approach is theoretically grounded and the ablation studies (Section 8.1) provide strong empirical evidence for the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection. The authors demonstrate the method's effectiveness across multiple challenging 3D character types (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal), which represents a significant advancement over prior work limited to 2D.

2. **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical formulations are presented logically, and the experimental setup is sufficiently detailed to understand the methodology. The supplementary video (mentioned but not provided in the abstract) would likely enhance understanding of the results.

3. **Novelty:** The paper makes several technical contributions that distinguish it from prior work. The distributed stochastic optimization framework (Algorithm 1) is novel in its application to this specific problem. The method for computing optimal feedback gains without LQG is a significant technical improvement that addresses limitations of previous approaches. The demonstration of 3D locomotion across diverse character types represents a substantial step beyond the 2D results of previous work [9, 11].

4. **Significance:** The work addresses a meaningful challenge in character animation and robotics - creating interactive controllers for complex movements across diverse character morphologies. The ability to use a single network architecture for multiple character types with different locomotion patterns has significant practical implications for animation, gaming, and robotics. The results demonstrate realistic, purposeful behaviors that would be difficult to achieve with traditional methods.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** While the paper compares to naive neural network training on static trajectories and no noise injection, it lacks comparison to more recent reinforcement learning approaches for control (e.g., PPO [14], SAC [13]). The paper mentions MPC as a potential alternative but doesn't provide a detailed comparison of performance metrics.

2. **Computational Requirements:** The paper notes that training requires "cloud computing and a GPU implementation" and "on the order of hours," but doesn't provide specific computational benchmarks (e.g., time per iteration, number of parameters, memory requirements) that would help assess the practicality of the approach.

3. **Theoretical Analysis:** While the method is empirically validated, the paper lacks theoretical guarantees about convergence or optimality of the distributed stochastic optimization approach. The authors acknowledge this as a limitation but don't provide deeper theoretical analysis.

**Pro and Con Arguments for Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Demonstrates a significant technical advancement over prior work by achieving stable 3D locomotion for diverse character types
- Presents a novel distributed stochastic optimization framework with clear technical contributions
- Provides thorough ablation studies that validate the necessity of key components (joint optimization, noise injection)
- Shows practical applicability across multiple character types with a single network architecture
- Well-structured, technically sound, and clearly written

*Con:*
- Limited comparison to recent reinforcement learning approaches for control
- Insufficient computational complexity analysis
- No theoretical guarantees for the optimization procedure
- The paper's focus on character animation may be perceived as slightly outside NIPS' typical scope (though the methods have clear relevance to robotics and control)

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a strong technical contribution to the field of neural network control, with significant advances in the ability to create interactive controllers for diverse complex characters in 3D. The distributed stochastic optimization framework is novel, well-validated through comprehensive experiments, and represents a meaningful step forward from previous work. While the computational requirements and limited comparison to recent RL methods are minor weaknesses, they do not detract from the paper's overall quality and significance. The work is technically sound, well-written, and demonstrates clear progress in the field of neural network-based control for complex systems.

The paper meets NIPS' standards for quality, clarity, originality, and significance. It represents a substantial advancement over previous work in this specific application domain, with clear potential for broader impact in robotics and control. The results are well-supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation.
