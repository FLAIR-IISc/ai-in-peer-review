- Summary: Combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with supervised learning to train neural-network controllers for complex, physically simulated characters for real-time interactive control.  
- Key idea: Use CIO to generate optimal trajectories and train a network to mimic them, enabling stable, interactive locomotion.  
- Scalability: Demonstrates learning for diverse 3D morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without hand-tuning or specialized architectures.  
- Stability techniques: Uses principled noise injection (via optimal feedback gains), distributed stochastic optimization, and careful network initialization to improve robustness.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends trajectory-optimization-plus-learning approaches to more complex 3D characters and contrasts with task-specific physics-based controllers.  
- Strength — scalability: Genuine advance over prior 2D-focused work, showing realistic locomotion for complex systems.  
- Strength — unified framework: Presents a compelling, general control approach applicable across morphologies.  
- Strength — synergy: CIO provides high-quality data while networks enable real-time execution; implementation details aid reproducibility.  
- Strength — empirical results: Visually convincing results and supplementary video demonstrating the method’s effectiveness.  
- Weakness — computational cost: High expense of generating training data via trajectory optimization; needs detailed compute/time breakdowns.  
- Weakness — limited comparisons: Insufficient rigorous comparison to alternatives (e.g., MPC, direct RL), making superiority claims anecdotal.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Many constants used with no analysis of sensitivity or robustness to changes.  
- Weakness — generalization: Little discussion or evaluation of policy generalization to new environments, tasks, or unseen character variations.  
- Weakness — optimization horizon: Real-time execution uses T=1; impact of longer horizons isn’t explored.  
- For acceptance: Novel, effective approach with strong empirical scaling to 3D characters and good reproducibility.  
- Against acceptance: High training cost, limited comparative analysis, and weak generalization evidence.  
- Overall: Strong paper with significant contribution; recommended for acceptance despite noted limitations.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Confidence: 8/10.
