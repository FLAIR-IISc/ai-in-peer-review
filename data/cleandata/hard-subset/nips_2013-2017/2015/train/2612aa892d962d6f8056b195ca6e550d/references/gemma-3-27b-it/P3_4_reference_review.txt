## NIPS Review: Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling approach to learning control policies for complex, physically simulated characters. The combination of trajectory optimization (CIO) and neural network learning, along with the specific techniques for handling high dimensionality and instability (noise injection, distributed optimization), represents a significant step forward. The ability to learn a *single* policy applicable to diverse morphologies and tasks is particularly impressive. While the paper is well-written and the results are promising, some aspects could benefit from further clarification and more rigorous comparison to existing methods. I lean towards **Accept** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The authors address the challenge of automating the design of real-time controllers for complex characters, a traditionally manual and time-consuming process. They propose a framework that leverages the strengths of both trajectory optimization (CIO) and neural networks. The core idea is to use CIO to generate a dataset of optimal trajectories, then train a neural network to mimic the CIO’s policy. Crucially, they address the difficulties of training in a closed-loop, high-dimensional control setting through noise injection and a carefully designed distributed optimization scheme.

The paper situates itself well within the relevant literature. It acknowledges the limitations of Reinforcement Learning in high-dimensional continuous spaces and highlights the recent work attempting to bridge trajectory optimization and neural networks ([9, 11]). The authors correctly identify their contribution as scaling this approach to truly 3D characters, a feat not previously achieved.  The discussion of historical control methods (state machines, simplified models) and their limitations is also helpful.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Significance:**  Applying this combined approach successfully to 3D character control *is* a significant advancement. The ability to generate a single policy capable of controlling a diverse range of characters (swimming, flying, bipeds, quadrupeds) demonstrates the generality and potential impact of the method.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method appears technically sound. The use of CIO is well-justified, and the additions of noise injection and distributed stochastic optimization are well-motivated and effectively address key challenges. The explanation of the optimal feedback gains calculation is particularly insightful.
* **Empirical Results:** The results presented are compelling, and the video supplement is crucial for appreciating the quality of the learned behaviors. The comparative ablation studies (Section 8.1) effectively demonstrate the benefits of the joint optimization and noise injection techniques.
* **Clarity of Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clear and concise, and the explanations of the algorithm are easy to follow.
* **Practicality:** The emphasis on leveraging existing implementations (CIO, Newton’s method) and cloud computing makes the approach more accessible.

**3. Weaknesses & Areas for Improvement:**

* **Limited Comparative Evaluation:** While the ablation studies are useful, a more systematic comparison with alternative control methods (e.g., direct Reinforcement Learning approaches like [3, 6, 14] as the authors acknowledge) would strengthen the paper.  Demonstrating a clear advantage over existing methods, even qualitatively, is important.  Simply stating that other methods yield “somewhat chaotic behaviors” is not sufficiently convincing.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper lists the key algorithmic constants used, but doesn’t discuss their sensitivity.  Understanding the robustness of the approach to these hyperparameters is important for reproducibility and practical application.  A brief sensitivity analysis would be valuable.
* **Recurrent State (r):** The role of the recurrent state `r` isn’t fully explained. While its inclusion is mentioned, a more detailed justification for its use and a discussion of its learned representation would be helpful.  What information is being captured in `r`, and how does it contribute to the stability and performance of the controller?
* **Trajectory Optimization Details:**  While the authors mention using MuJoCo, further details about the cost function components (beyond "reach goal position and minimize torque usage") would be beneficial.  Specifically, how are these costs weighted?  
* **Scaling and Complexity:** The authors mention that training takes "on the order of hours" and invests more computing resources in data generation than learning. While this is honest, a more detailed breakdown of the computational cost (e.g., hours per character, resource usage) would provide a better understanding of the scalability of the approach.



**4. Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Significant advancement in automated character control.
* Technically sound and well-motivated method.
* Compelling empirical results demonstrating generality and stability.
* Clear writing and organization.

**Con:**

* Limited comparison to existing methods.
* Lack of hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
* Unclear explanation of the recurrent state's role.




**5. Recommendation & Suggestions:**

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions. 

Specifically, the authors should:

1. **Expand the comparative evaluation:** Include a more rigorous comparison with relevant Reinforcement Learning or other control methods, even if it's a limited experiment.
2. **Address the hyperparameter sensitivity:** Conduct a brief sensitivity analysis of the key algorithmic constants.
3. **Clarify the role of the recurrent state (r):** Provide a more detailed explanation of its purpose and learned representation.
4. **Provide more details on the trajectory optimization cost function:** Specify the weighting of different cost components.
5. **Offer a more detailed breakdown of the computational cost:** Provide a clearer picture of the scalability of the approach.




This is a strong paper with the potential to significantly impact the field of character control. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above will further strengthen the contribution and make it even more valuable to the NIPS community.
