- Summary: Combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with neural networks to train real-time controllers from optimized trajectory datasets, using noise injection and distributed optimization for robust 3D character control.  
- Contribution: Enables interactive control of diverse morphologies (swimming, flying, bipeds, quadrupeds) without hand-designed controllers or motion capture.  
- Novelty: Novel integration of CIO, principled noise injection (using optimal feedback gains), and distributed training for high-dimensional 3D locomotion.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on trajectory-optimization + learning literature and appropriately cites noise-injection and physics-based control work, extending prior 2D results to complex 3D tasks.  
- Strength — challenging problem: Tackles the difficult task of learning robust, generalizable controllers for high-dimensional physical characters.  
- Strength — technique synergy: Leverages offline optimization to generate high-quality training data for a fast, learned policy.  
- Strength — 3D scalability: Demonstrates successful scaling from prior 2D work to complex 3D locomotion tasks.  
- Strength — generality: Same network architecture controls multiple diverse character morphologies, showing method generality.  
- Strength — robustness: Principled noise injection with feedback gains improves robustness and performance.  
- Strength — implementation detail: Provides thorough distributed training and optimization implementation details aiding reproducibility.  
- Strength — validation: Ablation studies convincingly show benefits of noise injection and distributed optimization versus static training.  
- Weakness — computational cost: Heavy computational resources required for trajectory optimization; accessibility is a concern and cost breakdown is lacking.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Limited analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters (noise levels, penalty weights) and robustness to their variation.  
- Weakness — limited direct-method comparison: Lacks systematic comparison to direct RL or other direct methods; only qualitative impressions provided.  
- Weakness — sensory input simplification: Uses a simplified sensory representation that may limit performance in more complex scenarios.  
- Weakness — missing user study: Interactive control is shown visually but lacks quantitative user studies evaluating usability or precision.  
- Quality: Technically sound with claims supported by experiments and clear methodology.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, though some derivations (e.g., optimal feedback gains) could be clearer.  
- Originality: Original and significant combination of CIO, noise injection, and distributed optimization for learned controllers.  
- Significance: Potentially large impact on robotics, animation, and biomechanics by enabling generalizable controllers for diverse characters.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Solves an important problem, presents a novel effective approach, shows strong 3D results, and validates methods thoroughly.  
- Arguments against acceptance: High computational cost, limited comparisons to direct methods, and absence of user study.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — request more detail on computational costs, hyperparameter sensitivity, and (if possible) preliminary comparison to direct methods and discussion of future user studies.
