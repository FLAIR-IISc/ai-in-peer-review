

**Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"**

This paper proposes a method for training neural network controllers for character animation by combining trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) with neural network regression. The authors generate training data via trajectory optimization, then train a neural network to mimic the optimizer's behavior, with noise injection for robustness. The approach is applied to diverse 3D characters including swimmers, flyers, bipeds, and quadrupeds.

**Summary and Relation to Previous Work**
The work builds on trajectory optimization methods for character animation [12, 11] and neural network control [3, 6, 14]. The key innovation is a distributed stochastic optimization approach to jointly optimize trajectories and neural network parameters. The authors claim their method yields "more realistic and purposeful behaviors" than direct neural network training, but provide limited comparison to established methods.

**Strengths**
The technical approach is sound, with a well-designed distributed training architecture. The noise injection technique for robustness is well-motivated and the feedback gains calculation method provides valuable theoretical insight. The application to diverse 3D characters demonstrates impressive capabilities, and the supplementary video likely shows compelling results. The paper also provides useful analysis of the optimization process.

**Weaknesses**
The paper suffers from a critical lack of rigorous validation against established baselines. The authors claim their method "solves substantially harder problems" than previous work but provide no meaningful comparison to state-of-the-art neural network control methods. The only comparison is between their full method and a naive static trajectory training (no joint optimization), which is not a fair comparison to established approaches.

The paper fails to compare with standard reinforcement learning methods for continuous control (e.g., PPO [14], SAC [13]), which are the current benchmarks for neural network control. The authors mention MPC as a potential alternative but provide no quantitative comparison, only a qualitative observation about "cyclical" vs. "non-cyclical" behaviors.

The ablation studies are limited. While they show noise injection is important (no noise vs. with noise), they do not explore key hyperparameters or provide sufficient network architecture comparison beyond a single ablation study (Table 1). The choice of 3 hidden layers with 250 units lacks justification beyond claiming it's the "best performance/complexity tradeoff" without showing the full spectrum of results.

The paper lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility. While they mention "2.5 hours of training on AWS," they don't provide the exact hyperparameters needed to reproduce their results. The distributed training description is vague about how trajectory optimization and neural network training interact.

**Quality and Significance**
The technical approach is sound, but the lack of comprehensive comparison undermines the significance claim. The authors overstate the contribution by claiming their method "yields more realistic and purposeful behaviors" without providing quantitative evidence beyond the limited ablation studies. The comparison to 2D vs. 3D is not a valid comparison of methodological advancement.

**Clarity**
The paper is well-written and organized, with clear technical explanations. The algorithms and figures are well-presented. However, the lack of comparison to established methods is a significant omission in the presentation.

**Overall Assessment**
While the technical approach is interesting and the results visually compelling, the paper fails to provide the necessary rigorous validation required for NIPS. The claims about the method's superiority are not supported by comprehensive comparison with established baselines. The work represents a clever application of known techniques but does not demonstrate a meaningful advancement over existing methods in the field.

**Pro/Con for Acceptance:**

*Pro:*
- Interesting technical combination of trajectory optimization and neural networks
- Demonstrates impressive results on diverse 3D character types
- Well-designed distributed training architecture
- Provides useful analysis of feedback gains calculation

*Con:*
- Lacks comparison with established neural network control methods (PPO, SAC, MPC)
- Limited ablation studies beyond noise injection
- Insufficient detail for reproducibility
- Overstates significance without sufficient evidence
- Fails to address how this method advances beyond existing trajectory optimization approaches

Given the lack of rigorous validation against established baselines, I cannot recommend acceptance. The paper would require substantial revision to include comprehensive comparisons with standard neural network control methods and more thorough ablation studies before it could meet NIPS standards. While the technical approach is sound, the claims of significant advancement are not substantiated by the evidence provided.
