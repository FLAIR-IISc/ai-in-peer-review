

# Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

## Summary
This paper presents a novel approach to automatically generating neural network controllers for interactive character control across diverse 3D physical systems. The authors combine trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) with neural network training to create controllers that can generate stable, realistic movements for various character types (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, flying) without task-specific hand-crafted controllers. The key innovation is a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm that jointly optimizes trajectories and neural network parameters, along with a perturbation method for computing optimal feedback gains. The method demonstrates significant progress over prior work by solving 3D locomotion problems (previously limited to 2D in similar approaches) and enabling interactive control across diverse character morphologies.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with a well-defined problem formulation and detailed algorithmic description. The authors provide a thorough experimental evaluation across multiple character types, with ablation studies demonstrating the necessity of their key innovations (joint optimization, noise injection). The distributed optimization algorithm (Algorithm 1) is carefully designed and shown to converge in experiments. However, the theoretical analysis is limited - while the paper provides empirical evidence of convergence, it lacks formal guarantees for the distributed stochastic optimization approach. The computational requirements (2.5 hours of training) are substantial but justified by the complexity of the problem. The paper demonstrates appropriate honesty in acknowledging limitations, particularly regarding the need for trajectory optimization to generate training data.

## Clarity
The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a logical flow from problem statement to solution to evaluation. The mathematical formulation is precise, though some sections (particularly Section 3.2 on feedback gains) may require familiarity with trajectory optimization. The supplementary video is recommended for full appreciation of the results, which suggests the paper might be challenging to fully evaluate without visual demonstrations. The authors effectively use figures (2 and 3) to compare their method with alternatives. The writing is accessible to machine learning and robotics researchers, though some readers may need to consult references for the trajectory optimization background.

## Originality
The paper makes a meaningful contribution by extending trajectory optimization-based character control to 3D with a unified neural network approach. While the components (trajectory optimization, neural networks) are known, the distributed stochastic optimization framework that jointly optimizes both is novel. The perturbation method for computing feedback gains (Section 3.2) represents a significant technical advancement over prior approaches that would have required additional LQG passes. The paper's key contribution is the demonstration that this unified approach can successfully control diverse 3D characters with a single architecture, which previous work could not achieve. The authors appropriately acknowledge related work [9, 11, 12] while clearly differentiating their approach by solving substantially harder problems.

## Significance
This work addresses a fundamental challenge in character animation and robotics: creating controllers for diverse physical behaviors without task-specific hand-crafting. The ability to use a single architecture across multiple character types (biped, quadruped, swimming, flying) represents a significant step toward unified character control systems. The method enables interactive real-time control, which has immediate applications in gaming, animation, and robotics. The results demonstrate success where previous methods failed (3D locomotion), and the approach could potentially be extended to more complex behaviors (e.g., getting up, climbing). The paper's significance is reinforced by the thorough experimental validation across multiple character types.

## Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**
- Successfully solves a challenging 3D locomotion problem that previous work could not
- Demonstrates a unified approach for diverse character types with a single architecture
- Provides a novel distributed stochastic optimization algorithm that effectively combines trajectory optimization and neural network training
- Includes thorough ablation studies showing the necessity of key innovations
- Clear experimental validation across multiple character types with impressive results

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited theoretical analysis of the distributed optimization algorithm
- Could provide more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods beyond the ablation studies
- Computational requirements (2.5 hours of training) might be prohibitive for some applications
- The relationship to prior work (particularly [9, 11, 12]) could be more clearly articulated

## Recommendation
This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of neural network control for physical systems. The technical approach is sound, the results are compelling, and the significance of the work is clear. While the theoretical analysis could be strengthened, the empirical validation is comprehensive and addresses the core problem effectively. The paper clearly advances the state of the art in character control by demonstrating interactive, realistic locomotion for diverse 3D characters using a single unified architecture.

I recommend acceptance for NeurIPS. This work is technically rigorous, addresses an important problem, and provides a method with clear potential for applications in animation, robotics, and biomechanics. The authors have made a significant contribution that advances the field beyond previous work. The paper is well-written and provides sufficient detail for reproduction, making it a valuable addition to the NeurIPS proceedings.
