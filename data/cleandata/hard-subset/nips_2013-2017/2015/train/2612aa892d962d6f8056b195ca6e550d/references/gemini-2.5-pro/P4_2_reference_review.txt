### Summary of the Paper

This paper presents a novel method for training a neural network policy to perform interactive, real-time control of complex, physically-simulated 3D characters. The core idea is to combine the strengths of offline trajectory optimization, which can produce high-quality and physically realistic motions, with the real-time execution capabilities of a neural network. The authors formulate this as a joint optimization problem, seeking to find both a set of optimal trajectories and a single neural network policy that can generate the actions for all of them.

This large, non-convex problem is solved using a distributed, alternating optimization scheme. In one phase, multiple trajectory optimization processes refine individual motion segments to minimize task-specific costs (e.g., reaching a goal) while also staying close to the actions predicted by the current neural network policy. This phase utilizes Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO). In the other phase, the data from these optimized trajectories is used to train the neural network via standard regression.

A key contribution of this work is a principled approach to injecting noise during training to improve robustness and generalization. Gaussian noise is added to the sensory inputs of the network. Critically, the target action is not the original action from the noiseless trajectory, but rather a first-order correction based on optimal feedback gains. The paper introduces a novel and efficient method to compute these feedback gains directly from the CIO solver, a significant improvement over less stable or more computationally expensive alternatives like running a separate LQR pass. The entire system is implemented in a distributed architecture to make the computationally intensive training process feasible. The authors demonstrate the method's effectiveness by generating controllers for a diverse set of 3D characters, including bipeds, quadrupeds, a swimmer, and a flyer, all using the same underlying algorithm.

This work builds upon recent research that also combines trajectory optimization and policy learning (e.g., Levine & Koltun, 2014; Mordatch et al., 2014). However, it makes a substantial leap by successfully tackling complex 3D locomotion, whereas previous work was largely confined to 2D characters. The authors attribute this advance to their technical improvements, particularly the robust training procedure involving noise and optimal feedback gains.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Quality (Technical Soundness):**

*   **Strengths:** The paper is technically sound and presents a complete, well-realized system. The formulation of the joint optimization problem (Eq. 4) and its decomposition into an alternating scheme is logical and well-motivated. The ablation studies in Section 8.1 provide strong empirical evidence supporting the authors' central claims: both the joint optimization framework and the noise injection techniques are crucial for achieving stable, generalizable policies. The comparison with Model Predictive Control (MPC) is also insightful, showing that the proposed method encourages more natural, cyclic behaviors. A major technical strength is the novel method for calculating optimal feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer (Section 3.2). This is a clever and generalizable technique that reuses existing computations and provides better-conditioned gains.
*   **Weaknesses:** The stability of the overall alternating optimization algorithm is demonstrated empirically but lacks a formal convergence analysis. While the authors note they observe convergence, a brief discussion of the theoretical properties of this block-alternating scheme would strengthen the paper. Additionally, the policy execution step (Section 7) requires solving a single-step optimization problem at each time step. The authors state this runs at real-time rates, but more detail on the computational overhead and its scaling with character complexity would be valuable for assessing its practicality.

**Clarity:**

*   **Strengths:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly structured. Section 2 provides an excellent high-level overview of the method before diving into the specifics of each component. The use of Algorithm 1 to summarize the optimization loop is effective. The results are compellingly presented, and the reference to the supplementary video is crucial for appreciating the quality of the generated motions.
*   **Weaknesses:** Some minor points could be clarified. The definitions of state `st` and action `at` in Section 2 are slightly unconventional, with the action including `q̇t`, which represents the change in pose to the next timestep. The role of the recurrent memory state `r` is introduced in the state/action definitions but is not elaborated upon in the rest of the paper, leaving its function unclear. A sentence or two explaining its purpose would be helpful.

**Originality:**

*   **Strengths:** While the high-level concept of guiding policy learning with trajectory optimization exists in prior work, this paper introduces several novel and critical components that enable a significant advance in the state of the art. The primary novelty lies in the sophisticated approach to robust training: the combination of injecting noise into sensory inputs and training the network to predict the optimally corrected action (`a + asε`). The method for deriving these optimal feedback gains (`as`) from the direct trajectory optimizer is, to my knowledge, original and constitutes a valuable contribution in its own right. The demonstration of a single, unified algorithm successfully controlling such a diverse range of 3D morphologies (walking, swimming, flying) is a novel and powerful showcase of the method's generality.
*   **Weaknesses:** The work relies on several established components, such as the CIO framework for trajectory optimization and standard feed-forward neural networks. The novelty is therefore more in the synthesis and the specific techniques that make the combination work at a new scale, rather than in the invention of entirely new base components.

**Significance:**

*   **Strengths:** The significance of this work is very high. It presents a substantial step towards the automated creation of versatile and robust controllers for complex physical systems. The results are impressive and demonstrate a level of performance and generality for 3D character control that has not been previously achieved with a single, automated method. The approach has clear potential for impact in computer animation, video games, and robotics, where developing controllers for novel morphologies is a challenging and time-consuming task. The technical ideas, especially the feedback gain computation and the joint stochastic optimization, are likely to be influential and built upon by other researchers in control and machine learning.

### Arguments for Acceptance:

1.  **Significant Advance:** The paper successfully scales a policy-learning-from-optimization framework to complex 3D locomotion tasks, a notable improvement over prior work limited to 2D.
2.  **Novel Technical Contributions:** It introduces an original and effective method for robust policy training via noise injection, supported by a novel technique for computing well-conditioned feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer.
3.  **Strong Empirical Validation:** The results are compelling and demonstrated across a wide variety of character morphologies. The ablation studies provide clear evidence for the importance of the proposed contributions.
4.  **High Potential Impact:** The method addresses a long-standing challenge in control and animation and is likely to be highly influential for both practitioners and researchers.

### Arguments against Acceptance:

1.  **Lack of Convergence Guarantees:** The paper does not provide a theoretical analysis of the convergence of its alternating optimization algorithm.
2.  **Minor Clarity Issues:** Certain details, such as the role of the recurrent state `r` and the real-time cost of the execution-time optimization, could be more thoroughly explained.

Overall, the strengths of this paper far outweigh its minor weaknesses. It presents a high-quality, original, and significant contribution to the field.
