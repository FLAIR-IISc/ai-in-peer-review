- Method: Combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with supervised learning to train neural-network controllers for interactive, real-time control of complex simulated characters.
- Key technical ingredients: uses CIO to generate training data, noise injection with optimal feedback gains, and a distributed training architecture for scalability.
- Demonstrated on diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal), showing generality and stable locomotion.
- Relation to prior work: builds on neural control and trajectory optimization literature, differing from direct RL or demonstration-based supervised learning by generating high-quality offline trajectories.
- Claims novelty over recent similar works by tackling higher-dimensional 3D locomotion problems.
- Noise injection rationale: principled, theoretically motivated, related to denoising autoencoder ideas and optimal control techniques.
- Strength — generality: same architecture and training procedure works across multiple morphologies and tasks.
- Strength — stable 3D locomotion: achieves realistic and stable high-dimensional locomotion where prior work was often limited to 2D.
- Strength — technique combination: effective synergy between precise offline optimization and fast online neural control.
- Strength — distributed training: scalable computation infrastructure enables handling complex trajectory generation and learning.
- Strength — ablations: detailed studies validate the importance of joint optimization and noise injection.
- Strength — presentation: the paper is well-written and clearly organized.
- Weakness — computational cost: high cloud resource and long training times may limit reproducibility and accessibility.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: insufficient analysis of sensitivity to network architecture, noise levels, and other constants.
- Weakness — limited comparisons: lacks systematic empirical comparison to alternatives like reinforcement learning and MPC.
- Weakness — interpretability: resulting neural policies remain black boxes with little analysis of internal representations.
- Weakness — soft constraints: use of soft physics constraints may reduce physical realism and introduce inaccuracies.
- Recommendation: overall a strong, novel contribution with clear empirical gains; reviewer recommends acceptance despite noted limitations.
- Suggestions: analyze and reduce computational cost, perform more comparisons with RL/MPC, study interpretability, evaluate hyperparameter sensitivity, and address impacts of soft constraints.
