- Proposes a novel method that jointly trains neural network controllers and trajectory optimizations via distributed stochastic optimization for interactive real-time control of diverse 3D characters.  
- Uses noise injection during training and a new method to compute optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization, avoiding costly LQG passes.  
- Demonstrates a single neural network architecture controlling bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, and flying characters without specialized initialization.  
- Distributed training architecture and implementation details are clearly described; supplementary video provides strong qualitative evidence.  
- Technical innovations (joint optimization algorithm and feedback-gain computation) are sound and well-motivated.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection for robustness.  
- Advances trajectory-optimization methods from 2D to challenging 3D problems and produces natural-looking behaviors without motion capture.  
- Major weakness: limited comparison to modern deep RL methods (e.g., SAC, PPO, DDPG) and no quantitative RL baselines.  
- Evaluation is overly qualitative and relies on a single quantitative metric; lacks additional metrics like task success, energy efficiency, and stability.  
- Insufficient discussion of limitations, scalability to more complex characters/environments, failure modes, and sim-to-real mismatch.  
- Recommendation: accept — substantial technical contribution and impressive results, with the caveat that more RL comparisons and quantitative metrics should be added for the camera-ready.  
- Pros: significant advancement, broad applicability, clear presentation, and strong qualitative results; Cons: limited RL benchmarking, sparse quantitative evaluation, and thin limitations discussion.
