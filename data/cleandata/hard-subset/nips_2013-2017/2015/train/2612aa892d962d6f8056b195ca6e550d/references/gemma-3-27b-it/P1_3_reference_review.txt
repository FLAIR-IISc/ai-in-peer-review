## NIPS Review: Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks

**Summary:**

This paper presents a method for learning neural network control policies for complex, physically simulated characters, enabling interactive real-time control. The core idea is to combine trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization - CIO) with supervised learning. CIO is used to generate a dataset of optimal trajectories, and a neural network is then trained to mimic these trajectories, allowing for online control. The authors address key challenges in scaling this approach to high-dimensional, 3D characters, including the need for robust training, noise injection, and efficient optimization. They demonstrate the method’s efficacy on a variety of characters – swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal – showcasing its generality.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon a long history of neural network control, but distinguishes itself from earlier approaches (e.g., [3, 6, 10, 14]) by leveraging the power of trajectory optimization to generate high-quality training data. This differs from direct reinforcement learning or supervised learning from expert demonstrations, particularly in scenarios where such demonstrations are unavailable or difficult to obtain.  The approach acknowledges recent work combining trajectory optimization and neural networks [9, 11], but claims to tackle substantially more complex problems (3D locomotion) than prior art.  The connection to optimal control and reinforcement learning is also clearly drawn, positioning the work as an attempt to bridge the gap between offline optimization and online control. The use of noise injection is inspired by techniques in graphics and optimal control [20, 6] and shares similarities with denoising autoencoders [17].

**Strengths:**

* **Generality:** A significant strength is the demonstrated ability to apply the same control architecture and training procedure across a diverse range of character morphologies and locomotion tasks.  This is a notable step towards a unified control framework.
* **Stable 3D Locomotion:**  Successfully achieving stable and realistic 3D locomotion with learned controllers is a substantial accomplishment, especially considering the challenges of high-dimensional control and the potential for instability.  This is a clear improvement over existing methods that were largely limited to 2D.
* **Novel Combination of Techniques:** The combination of CIO for data generation and neural network regression is effective, allowing the system to benefit from the precision of trajectory optimization and the real-time capabilities of neural networks.
* **Principled Noise Injection:**  The inclusion of noise injection, motivated by theoretical considerations and coupled with the calculation of optimal feedback gains, appears to be a crucial factor in stabilizing the learning process and preventing overfitting.
* **Distributed Training Architecture:** The use of a distributed training architecture allows the authors to scale the computations necessary for generating and learning from complex trajectories.
* **Detailed Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies comparing performance with and without joint optimization, and noise injection, provide compelling evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a clear explanation of the methodology and results.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** While the distributed training helps, the process is still computationally expensive, requiring significant cloud resources and hours of training time.  This could limit accessibility for researchers without access to similar infrastructure. The authors acknowledge this, but a deeper discussion of potential efficiency improvements would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The paper lists the algorithmic constants used, but a more thorough investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., network architecture, noise levels) would strengthen the findings.
* **Limited Comparison to Alternatives:** The paper hints at a qualitative impression that direct methods yield "chaotic" behaviors, but a more systematic comparison with other relevant approaches (e.g., reinforcement learning, MPC) would provide stronger evidence for the benefits of the proposed method.  The MPC discussion in the results section is a good start but could be expanded.
* **Black Box Nature of Neural Network:** While the approach is effective, the resulting neural network policies are still largely “black boxes”. It would be valuable to explore techniques for interpreting the learned control policies and understanding the underlying representations.
* **Soft Constraints:** While mentioned as a simplification, the use of soft constraints in the physics simulation might not guarantee truly physically realistic behavior and may be a source of subtle inaccuracies.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Demonstrates a novel and effective approach to learning controllers for complex, physically simulated characters.
* Achieves stable 3D locomotion, a significant advancement over previous work.
* Shows excellent generality across different character morphologies.
* Includes a well-motivated and principled noise injection strategy.
* Provides strong empirical results and ablation studies.

**Con:**

* Computationally expensive.
* Limited systematic comparison to alternative control methods.
* Lack of interpretability of the learned policies.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses a challenging problem in robotic control and animation. The proposed method is novel, effective, and demonstrates significant potential. While the computational cost and limited comparative analysis are weaknesses, the strengths of the work outweigh these concerns.  I believe this paper is a valuable contribution to the field and **recommend acceptance** to NIPS.



**Further suggestions for the authors:**

*  Consider discussing potential strategies for reducing the computational cost of training.
*  Conduct a more thorough comparison with alternative control methods (e.g., reinforcement learning, MPC).
*  Explore techniques for interpreting the learned neural network policies.
*  Provide a more detailed analysis of the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameters.
*  Consider mentioning how the soft constraints might impact the physical realism and propose potential ways to address this.
