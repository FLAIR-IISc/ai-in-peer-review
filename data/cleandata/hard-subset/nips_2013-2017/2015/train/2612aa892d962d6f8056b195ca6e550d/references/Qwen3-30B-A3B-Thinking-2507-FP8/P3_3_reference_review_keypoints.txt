- Novel approach combining Contact-Invariant Optimization with neural-network policies via a distributed stochastic optimization framework for diverse 3D characters.  
- Key innovation: a perturbation method that reuses trajectory optimization to compute well-conditioned stabilizing feedback gains.  
- Successfully learns controllers for diverse morphologies (biped, quadruped, swimming, flying) without task-specific tuning.  
- Joint optimization plus noise injection is crucial; ablation shows ~100x improvement over naive methods.  
- Distributed training architecture and implementation details (AWS, data flow) are clearly described; policy execution uses one-step optimization.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees on convergence or optimality; no analysis or proofs provided.  
- Computationally intensive: ~2.5 hours training per controller and heavy reliance on data generation.  
- Insufficient comparison with more recent trajectory-optimization + neural control work (e.g., Kulkarni et al. 2020).  
- Limited investigation of scalability/generalization to more complex tasks like manipulation or object interaction.  
- Overall: strong technical contribution with compelling empirical results that advance 3D locomotion control.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant method and validation despite limitations.  
- Pro-acceptance: diverse 3D control success, novel feedback-gain method, rigorous ablations, clear presentation.  
- Con-acceptance: limited theory, high computational cost, needs better contrast with recent related work.
