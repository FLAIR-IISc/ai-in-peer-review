- Presents a method that uses Contact-Invariant Optimization to generate trajectories and trains neural networks to achieve real-time control of complex 3D articulated characters.
- Tackles scalability in high-dimensional control by combining trajectory optimization, noise injection, and distributed stochastic optimization with GPU implementation.
- Positions the work within prior automated controller design efforts, bridging trajectory optimization and neural-network-based control for complex locomotion.
- Cites relevant neural network training techniques and physics simulation tools (e.g., MuJoCo) appropriately.
- Addresses a challenging, long-standing problem of automated, realistic controller creation for complex characters.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across diverse morphologies (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, flying) with convincing video and ablation studies.
- Novelly combines CIO trajectory optimization with neural network learning to leverage high-quality data for real-time policies.
- Uses principled noise injection guided by optimal feedback gains to improve robustness and generalization.
- Provides a scalable, distributed implementation that makes computationally intensive training practical.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, making the methodology accessible.
- Lacks systematic comparative evaluation against alternative methods (e.g., DDPG, TRPO, MPC).
- Does not thoroughly explore sensitivity to hyperparameter choices or report robustness across settings.
- Focuses mainly on locomotion tasks and does not demonstrate broader behaviors like manipulation or object interaction.
- Implementation appears complex, requiring expertise in both trajectory optimization and neural network training, which may hinder adoption.
- Provides limited detail on cost function term weighting and trade-offs between task completion and physical realism.
- For acceptance: novel, effective approach with strong empirical demonstrations and scalable implementation.
- Against acceptance: limited baselines, unclear hyperparameter robustness, narrow task scope, and implementation complexity.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with weaknesses that can be addressed via expanded comparisons and more detail on cost function and hyperparameters.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add baseline comparisons and more detailed cost/hyperparameter discussion.
