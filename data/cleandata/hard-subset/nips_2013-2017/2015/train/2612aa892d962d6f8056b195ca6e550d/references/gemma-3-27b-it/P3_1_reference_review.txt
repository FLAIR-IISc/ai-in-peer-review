## NIPS Review: Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling and well-executed approach to learning interactive controllers for complex 3D characters. It successfully bridges the gap between offline trajectory optimization and online, real-time control, achieving results significantly surpassing previous work in similar domains. The combination of Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) for data generation and a carefully designed neural network architecture with noise injection is a key strength. While the paper leans heavily on CIO, the contribution lies in scaling this approach to complex 3D characters and enabling interactive control, a significant step forward. I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary and Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper addresses the challenging problem of automating the creation of universal controllers for complex characters, applicable to diverse behaviors and morphologies. Traditional methods are manual and task-specific. The authors leverage the power of trajectory optimization (CIO) to generate training data and then employ a neural network to learn a policy that can replicate these behaviors in real-time, interacting with user input. This approach is motivated by the limitations of both traditional control methods and purely reinforcement learning-based approaches in high-dimensional, continuous spaces. 

The work builds on recent efforts to combine trajectory optimization with neural networks [9, 11], but crucially extends these methods to significantly more complex 3D characters and achieves stable locomotion, something previously limited to 2D scenarios. The authors explicitly acknowledge the historical use of neural networks for control but highlight the recent data availability advantage in areas like vision and speech, contrasting it with the data scarcity problem in control. They also position their work in relation to physics-based control approaches (state machines, simplified models), aiming for a more unified solution.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Significant Advancement in Complexity:** The successful application of this method to complex 3D characters, including swimming, flying, and legged locomotion, is a major achievement. This represents a significant leap beyond prior work which was largely confined to 2D.
* **Robustness and Stability:** The incorporation of noise injection during training, informed by optimal feedback gains, is a particularly strong aspect. This leads to controllers that are more robust to perturbations and avoid chaotic behavior, a common issue with direct neural network control methods.
* **Unified Framework:** The approach demonstrates a promising path towards a unified control framework applicable to diverse character morphologies without requiring bespoke architectures or motion capture data.
* **Detailed Methodology & Ablation Studies:** The paper provides a clear and detailed description of the methodology, including the distributed training architecture and the perturbation method for computing optimal feedback gains.  The ablation studies (Figure 2, Table 1) convincingly demonstrate the importance of joint optimization and noise injection.
* **Practical Implementation & Real-time Performance:** The focus on real-time control is commendable. The optimization-based state evolution (rather than direct integration) further contributes to the practicality of the approach.
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The diverse set of characters and tasks, showcased in the supplementary video, provides strong evidence of the method’s generalizability.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Reliance on CIO:** While the neural network learning component is novel, the overall pipeline heavily relies on the performance of the underlying CIO trajectory optimizer. The paper acknowledges this, but a more in-depth discussion of CIO’s limitations and potential bottlenecks would be beneficial.  How sensitive is the entire pipeline to the quality of the trajectories generated by CIO?
* **Limited Comparative Analysis:** While the ablation studies are helpful, a more direct comparison to other relevant approaches (e.g., direct reinforcement learning methods like PPO or TRPO) would strengthen the paper. The authors mention that direct methods yield “somewhat chaotic behaviors,” but this assertion could be substantiated with quantitative results.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper lists the hyperparameters used, but does not discuss their sensitivity or how they were chosen. A brief analysis of hyperparameter tuning could be insightful.
* **Task Complexity:** The task described (locomotion to a goal) is relatively simple.  Evaluating the method on more complex, multi-stage tasks (e.g., navigation in cluttered environments, manipulation) would further demonstrate its capabilities.



**4. Originality:**

The paper's originality lies in the successful combination and scaling of existing techniques – CIO and neural networks – to achieve a significant advance in interactive control of complex 3D characters.  The specific techniques for noise injection, optimal feedback gain computation, and distributed training contribute to the novelty of the approach. While the individual components are not entirely new, the synergistic combination and the demonstrated results represent a novel and valuable contribution.

**5. Significance:**

The work has the potential to significantly impact several fields, including robotics, animation, and biomechanics. The ability to automatically generate realistic and interactive controllers for complex characters could streamline animation workflows, enable the development of more sophisticated robots, and provide valuable insights into biological motor control. The unified control framework is particularly promising, potentially reducing the need for hand-engineered controllers for different character types.

**Pro/Con Acceptance Arguments:**

**Pro:**
*  Addresses a challenging and important problem.
*  Demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods.
*  Provides a robust and stable solution.
*  Well-written and clearly explained.
*  Strong empirical validation.

**Con:**
*  Heavily reliant on CIO.
*  Limited comparative analysis to other control methods.
*  Hyperparameter sensitivity not explored.

**Recommendations for Revision:**

* **Expand discussion of CIO:**  Address the limitations of CIO and its potential impact on the overall pipeline.
* **Include a more thorough comparative analysis:** Compare the performance of the proposed method to relevant reinforcement learning algorithms.
* **Provide a brief analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity.**
* **Consider evaluating the method on more complex tasks.**




In conclusion, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of character control. The proposed method is well-motivated, thoroughly evaluated, and has the potential to have a real-world impact. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
