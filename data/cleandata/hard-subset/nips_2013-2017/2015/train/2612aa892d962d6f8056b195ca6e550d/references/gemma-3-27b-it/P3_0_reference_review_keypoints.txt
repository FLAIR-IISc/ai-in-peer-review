- Overall: Compelling, technically sound approach combining trajectory optimization and neural nets for interactive control; recommend acceptance.  
- Problem & approach: Uses Contact-Invariant Optimization to generate trajectories and trains neural networks for real-time control, tackling high-dimensional 3D locomotion.  
- Positioning: Well-situated relative to trajectory optimization and direct NN methods, with links to biomechanics and neuroscience.  
- Strength — 3D locomotion: Demonstrates stable, realistic locomotion across diverse characters (swim, fly, biped, quadruped).  
- Strength — joint optimization: Effectively integrates trajectory optimization into the NN training pipeline rather than using it only for data.  
- Strength — noise & regularization: Principled input/hidden noise improves robustness and generalization, with theoretical justification.  
- Strength — distributed optimization: Scalable distributed training addresses computational demands for complex problems.  
- Strength — analysis: Strong ablation studies and MPC comparison clarify component contributions.  
- Strength — clarity: Clear writing, organization, and an informative supplementary video.  
- Weakness — computational cost: Training is computationally expensive (hours on GPU clusters), limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Paper lacks detailed analysis of sensitivity to algorithmic hyperparameters.  
- Weakness — limited interaction scope: Interaction is restricted to goal-position changes; external forces or terrain changes are not explored.  
- Weakness — interpretability: The neural controller is a black box; internal representations and learned features are not visualized.  
- Originality: Novel in the synergistic integration of CIO and NN regression within a joint optimization and distributed training framework.  
- Significance — animation & robotics: Promises automated, generalizable controllers for animation and robot control without manual tuning.  
- Significance — biomechanics/neuroscience: Offers a computational framework for studying motor control in biological systems.  
- For acceptance: Advances control policy learning for complex characters, succeeds on 3D locomotion, provides rigorous analysis, and has broad impact.  
- Against acceptance: High computational cost and limited interactive scenarios explored.  
- Final recommendation: Accept.
