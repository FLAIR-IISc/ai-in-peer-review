- Paper presents a method combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and neural networks for interactive, real-time control of diverse 3D characters (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, flying).
- Main technical contribution is a perturbation method that computes optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization without changing the cost function.
- Uses a distributed stochastic optimization that alternates trajectory optimization and network training, with a principled noise injection strategy for robustness.
- Demonstrates broad applicability: a single network architecture produces stable, realistic locomotion and emergent cyclic gaits across multiple morphologies without task-specific hand-crafting.
- Comprehensive ablation studies show joint optimization and noise injection are critical; naive approaches perform two orders of magnitude worse.
- Distributed training architecture is practical, scales well, and implementation details are sufficient for reproduction.
- Lacks systematic empirical comparisons to standard reinforcement learning methods (e.g., TRPO, PPO), weakening claims about RL producing chaotic behaviors.
- Insufficient analysis of failure cases (e.g., complex terrains, dynamic obstacles) and practical limitations.
- No theoretical convergence analysis provided for the distributed stochastic optimization algorithm.
- Computational cost reporting is vague — training time reported as “hours” without comparison to alternatives like MPC or standard RL.
- Interactive scope is limited to goal-position changes and does not demonstrate richer interactions (obstacle avoidance, dynamic environments).
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with notable innovation in feedback gain computation and strong empirical results.
- Suggested improvements: add systematic RL comparisons, discuss failure cases, include theoretical convergence discussion, and provide detailed computational cost analysis.
