- Combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with supervised learning to train neural controllers for interactive, real-time control of physically simulated characters.
- Uses CIO to generate training trajectories and adds noise injection and distributed stochastic optimization to improve generalization and stability.
- Demonstrates a single network architecture controlling diverse 3D characters (swimmers, fliers, bipeds, quadrupeds).
- Situates work among automated control, reinforcement learning, and MPC, building on prior CIO literature and claiming to tackle harder 3D locomotion problems.
- Strength: tackles high-dimensional, realistic dynamics and automates controller synthesis for complex characters.
- Strength: achieves scalability to 3D locomotion where prior work often focused on 2D.
- Strength: shows versatility across multiple morphologies with one architecture, indicating generalizability.
- Strength: technical innovations include noise injection, distributed optimization, and an efficient perturbation-based feedback gain calculation.
- Strength: generally clear presentation with a helpful algorithm description and informative ablation studies.
- Weakness: limited quantitative comparison to alternative methods (e.g., DDPG, TRPO) — comparisons are largely impressionistic.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning.
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational cost breakdown (time per trajectory, total training) for reproducibility.
- Weakness: task scope is narrow (locomotion to a goal); more complex interactive tasks not explored.
- Weakness: limited discussion of failure cases or scenarios where the approach struggles.
- Qualitative: technically sound, original combination of methods, but some dense notation and derivations could be simplified.
- Significance: promising contribution with potential impact in robotics, animation, and biomechanics.
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS with minor revisions, urging more thorough comparative evaluation and additional analyses.
