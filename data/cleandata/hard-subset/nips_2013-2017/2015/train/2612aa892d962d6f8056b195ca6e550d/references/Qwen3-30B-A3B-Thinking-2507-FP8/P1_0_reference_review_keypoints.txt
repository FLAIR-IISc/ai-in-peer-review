- Summary: Combines contact-invariant trajectory optimization with neural network policies to produce robust, real-time 3D controllers for diverse characters (bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, flyers).

- Strength (Quality): Strong technical rigor with principled feedback gain computation, distributed training, and comprehensive experiments including ablations.

- Strength (Clarity): Exceptionally well-written and organized paper with precise math, clear algorithms, and effective visual/supplementary results.

- Strength (Originality): Novel integration (noise injection, distributed optimization, feedback gains) enabling a single network architecture across varied morphologies.

- Strength (Significance): Meaningful advance for animation/robotics by reducing manual tuning and demonstrating stable 3D locomotion for multiple character types.

- Weakness (Theoretical Foundation): Lacks deeper theoretical analysis or formal guarantees explaining why joint optimization outperforms alternatives.

- Weakness (Comparison to SOTA): Limited benchmarking—does not compare to several recent neural network control methods ([3, 6, 14]).

- Weakness (Computational Cost): Training requires substantial compute and hours of distributed optimization; practicality and training/deployment trade-offs are under-discussed.

- Overall Assessment: High-quality, well-executed work whose strengths outweigh weaknesses and is likely reproducible and impactful.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Suggestion 1: Add brief theoretical analysis explaining stability/advantages of joint optimization.

- Suggestion 2: Include comparisons to more recent neural network control methods.

- Suggestion 3: Provide concrete discussion of training-time versus deployment trade-offs and practicality.

- Suggestion 4: Discuss potential extensions to more complex tasks (e.g., manipulation, object interaction).
