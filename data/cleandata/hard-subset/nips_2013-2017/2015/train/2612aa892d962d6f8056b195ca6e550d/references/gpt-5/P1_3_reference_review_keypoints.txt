- Summary: Alternating trajectory optimization (CIO) and neural policy learning to synthesize and generalize trajectories for interactive 3D character control, with noise injection and perturbation-based feedback gains; uses T=1 inverse-dynamics at runtime and distributed alternating optimization.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on guided policy search and trajectory-centric RL, scaling ideas to 3D locomotion with a practical distributed scheme and a CIO-based gain construction.

Strengths
- Tackles challenging, impactful problem of robust interactive locomotion for high-DOF 3D characters.
- Sensible, well-motivated integration of trajectory optimization and policy learning with a practical alternating/cloud training setup.
- Elegant perturbation-based feedback-gain computation that reuses CIO linear algebra and adds a tunable smoothness parameter.
- Robustness measures (input and hidden noise) are justified and supported by ablation showing reduced divergence.
- Demonstrates breadth across morphologies/tasks using the same NN architecture and hyperparameters.
- Clear description of distributed training and compute budget, making reproduction of the pipeline feasible in principle.

Weaknesses — Quality
- Evaluation is mostly qualitative; missing key quantitative metrics (success rate, time-to-target, energy costs, fall rate, robustness to perturbations, long-horizon rollouts).
- Physical realism compromised by soft dynamics/contacts and direct desired contact-force outputs; root forces may appear in simulation.
- Runtime still requires a T=1 optimization each step, blurring distinction from MPC; per-step computational cost and ablation without optimization are not reported.
- Numerical properties of the feedback-gain construction (sensitivity to λ, conditioning, comparison to LQG gains) are not characterized.
- Role and implementation of the recurrent memory r are unclear; need clarification and ablation on its contribution.

Weaknesses — Clarity
- Missing crucial reproduction details: exact cost terms and feature vectors, full sensory state and action specifications, observation normalization/noise.
- Insufficient quantitative convergence diagnostics for the alternating scheme and unclear role/timing of proximal term η.
- Distributed pipeline timing and training schedule details (noise resampling frequency, CIO iterations per trajectory) are not specified.
- Some references are incomplete or lack bibliographic details.

Originality
- Incremental over prior trajectory-centric policy learning; main novelties are the CIO-integrated perturbation gain computation and the practical distributed alternating application to 3D locomotion.

Significance
- Potentially impactful if physically plausible and robust across morphologies, but current evidence is weakened by lack of quantitative baselines and reliance on soft constraints/force outputs.

Questions for the authors
- Can you provide quantitative metrics (success rate, energy, robustness) across tasks and comparisons to MPC and model-free baselines?
- What is the per-step runtime of the T=1 optimization and can the controller run >100 Hz for complex characters?
- How frequently do soft constraint/root forces activate at test time and what are their magnitudes?
- How sensitive is performance to λ in the gain construction and to sensory/hidden noise scales?
- What is the contribution of memory r; have you tried removing it or replacing it with an RNN?

Pros
- Practical, well-motivated CIO + NN integration and distributed training.
- Efficient and interesting feedback-gain computation.
- Broad qualitative demonstrations and ablations supporting design choices.

Cons
- Limited quantitative evaluation and weak baselines.
- Use of soft dynamics/contacts and direct force outputs undermines physical realism.
- Ambiguity over the role of runtime optimization versus learned policy.
- Missing methodological details needed for full reproduction.

Recommendation
- Borderline accept: promising and technically interesting but needs stronger quantitative evaluation, torque-only/hard-constraint results, and clearer methodological details to be a clear accept.
