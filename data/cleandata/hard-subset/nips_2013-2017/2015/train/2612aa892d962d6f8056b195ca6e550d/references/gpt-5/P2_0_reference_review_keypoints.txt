- Proposes combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with supervised deep policy learning and alternating distributed refinement to learn real-time controllers for high-DOF 3D characters.
- Key technical ideas: synthesize diverse optimal trajectories, fit a compact sensory-to-action neural policy (including learned memory r), and jointly refine trajectories and policy with proximal regularization.
- Novel robustness ingredient: inject Gaussian noise into sensory and hidden units during training and adjust targets using locally optimal feedback gains.
- Efficient feedback-gain computation: perturbation-based method that reuses CIO’s Gauss–Newton inverse via a soft-constraint formulation and Woodbury identity.
- Execution-time controller: policy outputs enforced by a single (T=1) optimization step with soft dynamics constraints to enable 50 ms real-time control.
- Strength: tackles a hard, impactful problem producing diverse 3D behaviors (locomotion, swimming, flying) with a single architecture.
- Strength: sound integration of CIO and policy learning through an alternating, proximal, asynchronous distributed pipeline that is practical and well-described.
- Strength: elegant, computationally efficient gain derivation likely useful beyond this work.
- Strength: thoughtful regularization (sensory/hidden noise, spectral init) explicitly aimed at closed-loop stability.
- Strength: ablations show joint optimization outperforms static imitation and that noise improves stability.
- Weakness: limited quantitative evaluation and lack of rigorous comparisons to GPS or strong model-free baselines like TRPO.
- Weakness: use of soft dynamics constraints and allowed root forces at test time may mask instabilities and reduce physical realism for robotics.
- Weakness: ambiguity and underspecification of the recurrent/memory state r (dimensionality, role, why not an RNN).
- Weakness: policy directly outputs contact forces without thorough analysis of feasibility, friction constraints, or sensitivity to mismatches.
- Weakness: no systematic robustness study (perturbations, model error, sensor delays, OOD goals/terrains).
- Weakness: several reproducibility details missing (exact features/normalization, cost weights, r size, execution timing, convergence behavior).
- Quality: technical components are plausible and motivated, but broader claims need quantitative metrics (task success, falls, energy, constraint violations) and matched baselines.
- Clarity: generally well-written but needs precise specification of s, a, r, cost functions, contact handling, and more quantitative plots.
- Originality: moderate-to-high—combination has antecedents but the stochastic alternation, noise-with-gain targets, and gain computation are novel.
- Significance: valuable for graphics/animation and relevant to RL/control, but impact would increase with stronger quantitative evidence and tighter physical constraints.
- Key questions for authors: dimensionality/initialization/sensitivity of r and why not RNN; statistics on constraint violations and effect of root forces; comparison to LQG gains; matched GPS/TRPO comparisons; real-time compute cost per step.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses challenging problem, offers novel practical gain computation, and presents a scalable distributed training pipeline with supportive ablations.
- Cons for acceptance: missing strong baselines/metrics, softened test-time physics reducing realism, and underspecified design choices (r, action parametrization).
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept—promising contributions and breadth, but needs stronger quantitative evaluation and clarification of key design choices.
