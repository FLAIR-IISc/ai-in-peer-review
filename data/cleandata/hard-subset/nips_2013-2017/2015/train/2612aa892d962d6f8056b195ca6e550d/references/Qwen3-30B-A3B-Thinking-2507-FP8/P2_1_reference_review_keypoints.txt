- Paper introduces a joint framework combining trajectory optimization (CIO) and neural-network training for real-time interactive control of diverse 3D characters.  
- Uses distributed stochastic optimization that alternates optimizing trajectories and training a policy, with noise injection and optimal feedback gains for robustness.  
- Demonstrates robust locomotion for multiple character types (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, flying) and outperforms naive training on static trajectories.  
- Methodologically sound with clear theoretical justification for noise injection and feedback-gain calculation.  
- Ablation studies show the joint optimization and noise injection are necessary, yielding orders-of-magnitude improvements over baselines.  
- Implementation details and hyperparameters are provided, aiding reproducibility; authors acknowledge data-generation limitation.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized; algorithms and experiments are presented accessibly to the target audience.  
- Minor clarity issue: ablation claims (e.g., “two orders of magnitude better”) lack specified metrics.  
- Novel contribution: combines trajectory optimization with network training in a new distributed stochastic framework for 3D interactive control.  
- Key technical novelties: computing optimal feedback gains from trajectory optimization and the distributed stochastic optimization approach.  
- Significant practical implications for animation, robotics, and biomechanics by producing a unified controller for diverse characters.  
- Advances state of the art by achieving interactive 3D control where prior work was mostly 2D.  
- Strength: significant technical contribution integrating trajectory optimization and neural networks.  
- Strength: convincing ablation studies with quantitative evidence for key innovations.  
- Strength: generality — works across diverse 3D characters without specialized architectures.  
- Strength: sufficient implementation detail for reproduction.  
- Strength: strong application potential in robotics and animation.  
- Weakness: ablation study metrics are underspecified.  
- Weakness: lacks direct comparison with modern RL baselines (e.g., PPO, SAC).  
- Weakness: scalability to more complex tasks or larger models is not thoroughly addressed.  
- Weakness: execution-time computational cost for real-time use is not discussed in detail.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, significant contribution deserving acceptance, with minor issues that do not detract from the paper’s value.
