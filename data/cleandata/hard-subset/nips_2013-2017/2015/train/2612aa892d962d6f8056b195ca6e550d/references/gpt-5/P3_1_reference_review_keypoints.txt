- Summary: Proposes alternating optimization coupling Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with supervised neural policy training, using noise and gain-shaped targets from a Gauss–Newton perturbation to enable real-time interactive control at 50 ms steps.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on trajectory-optimization-guided policy learning (e.g., GPS), MPC, and physics-based character control, extending to robust 3D morphologies and extracting stabilizing gains from direct optimizers.  

Strengths:
- Clear formulation tying trajectory optimization and supervised regression with a principled quadratic penalty and proximal terms.  
- Novel, practical method to derive local feedback gains from a direct (non-LQG) optimizer by reusing Gauss–Newton Hessian.  
- Robustness mechanisms: sensory and hidden noise, gain-shaped targets, proximal regularization, and branched data for interactive goals.  
- Demonstrates diverse, visually plausible 3D behaviors across bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, and flyers using a single training recipe.  
- Scalable distributed training and practical real-time execution via a T=1 inverse-dynamics-like enforcement step.  

Weaknesses and questions:
- Quantitative evaluation is thin: lacks task-level metrics (success rates, energy, robustness) and comparisons to strong baselines like GPS, TRPO, or actor-critic methods.  
- Policy outputs contact forces and desired velocities; soft T=1 enforcement can admit nonphysical root forces, raising concerns for real-robot transfer.  
- Recurrent memory r is underspecified: initialization, usage, and empirical benefit are unclear and lack ablation.  
- No stability or conditioning analysis of the closed-loop policy or learned gains; effect of λ and gain statistics are unreported.  
- Asynchronous optimization convergence is only empirically claimed without diagnostic traces of objectives or residuals.  
- High data/compute requirements (30 EC2 nodes + GPU) with no sample-efficiency or scaling analysis for fewer resources.  
- Some clarity gaps: exact CIO cost weights, execution-time cost/frame rates, feature extraction φ, and Jacobian computation details are sparse.  

Quality:
- Technically sound with a well-motivated objective and engineering design, but empirical validation is primarily qualitative and ablation-limited.  

Clarity:
- Generally well written and organized; needs more detail on CIO costs/weights, recurrence, runtime, and distributed protocol.  

Originality:
- Moderate–high: leverages known paradigm (GPS) but contributes novel gain extraction from direct optimization, noisy target shaping, and asynchronous proximal training in 3D.  

Significance:
- Promising for interactive character control and potentially robotics, contingent on stronger quantitative validation and actuation/contact considerations.  

Suggestions for improvement:
- Add quantitative task metrics: goal tracking, success rates, energy/torque, robustness to pushes and noise, and report control FPS.  
- Compare to GPS/ADMM, TRPO or modern actor-critic, and an MPC baseline on at least one matched 3D task.  
- Analyze effect of λ on gain magnitudes, conditioning, and policy performance; report gain statistics.  
- Ablate and clarify the role of r (no r, different memory sizes) and provide initialization details.  
- Test stricter dynamics enforcement (hard constraints, torque-only actuation) and discuss sim-to-real implications.  
- Provide exact CIO cost terms/weights, runtime performance numbers, and more implementation details; consider releasing code or supplements.  
- Report compute/sample-efficiency scaling and diagnostics for asynchronous convergence (objective traces, residuals).  

Pros:
- Elegant coupling of CIO and NN with noisy, gain-shaped supervision.  
- Practical method to obtain stabilizing gains from direct optimizers.  
- Robustness techniques that aid generalization.  
- Diverse, convincing 3D results from a unified approach.  

Cons:
- Limited quantitative evaluation and weak baseline comparisons.  
- Reliance on soft dynamics constraints and direct contact-force commands.  
- High compute burden and unclear sample efficiency.  
- Missing clarity on recurrence, exact costs, and runtime.  

Recommendation:
- Borderline accept: interesting and practical contributions with useful gain derivation and strong qualitative results, but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer limitations.
