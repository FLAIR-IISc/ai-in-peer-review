- Overall: Compelling, well-executed approach combining CIO and neural networks for interactive control of complex 3D characters; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Problem & approach: Uses Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) to generate trajectories and trains neural policies for real-time interactive control of diverse characters.
- Novelty: Scales prior trajectory-optimization + NN methods to complex 3D characters, enabling stable locomotion beyond previous 2D work.
- Key technical contribution: Noise injection guided by optimal feedback gains to improve robustness and avoid chaotic network behavior.
- Unified framework: Demonstrates a general controller approach applicable to varied morphologies (swimming, flying, legged) without task-specific architectures or mocap.
- Method detail: Clear methodology including distributed training and perturbation-based feedback gain computation with convincing ablation studies.
- Real-time performance: Practical implementation using optimization-based state evolution achieves interactive, real-time control.
- Empirical validation: Strong results across diverse characters and tasks, supported by supplementary video evidence.
- Main weakness — CIO reliance: Pipeline heavily depends on CIO; more discussion needed on CIO limitations and sensitivity to trajectory quality.
- Main weakness — limited comparisons: Lacks direct quantitative comparison to RL baselines (e.g., PPO/TRPO) to substantiate claims about chaotic behaviors of direct methods.
- Main weakness — hyperparameters: Hyperparameter choices are listed but sensitivity analysis and tuning rationale are missing.
- Main weakness — task simplicity: Evaluations focus on relatively simple locomotion-to-goal tasks; multi-stage or cluttered-environment tasks would strengthen claims.
- Originality nuance: Individual components are known, but their synergistic combination and scaling to complex characters is a novel and valuable contribution.
- Significance: Potential high impact on robotics, animation, and biomechanics by reducing need for hand-engineered controllers.
- Pro acceptance arguments: Addresses an important problem, shows significant improvements, provides robust/stable solutions, well-written, and strongly validated.
- Con acceptance arguments: Overreliance on CIO, limited comparative analysis, and unexplored hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Recommended revisions: Expand discussion of CIO limitations, add comparisons to RL methods, include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and evaluate on more complex tasks.
