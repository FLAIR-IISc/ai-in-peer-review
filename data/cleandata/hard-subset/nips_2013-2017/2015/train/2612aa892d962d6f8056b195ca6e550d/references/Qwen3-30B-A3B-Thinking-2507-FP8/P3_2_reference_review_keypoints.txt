- Method: combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with neural network training using a distributed stochastic optimization loop and principled noise injection.  
- Claim: achieves stable 3D locomotion for diverse morphologies (bipedal, quadrupedal, flying, swimming) with a single network architecture, beyond prior 2D work.  
- Strength: unified framework handles diverse characters without task-specific, hand-crafted controllers.  
- Strength: trajectory optimization yields high-quality training data while the neural network enables real-time control.  
- Strength: distributed stochastic optimization (Algorithm 1) effectively addresses the joint trajectory–network optimization problem.  
- Strength: noise injection strategy is theoretically motivated and connected to optimal feedback gains.  
- Strength: ablation studies show joint optimization and noise are necessary, yielding ~100x improvement over naive methods.  
- Strength: strong empirical results demonstrating stable locomotion across multiple 3D character types with one architecture.  
- Weakness: insufficiently distinguishes its distributed stochastic optimization from related methods (e.g., ADMM, dual gradient descent; refs [9,11]).  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive empirical comparison to other neural-network-based control methods (e.g., refs [3,6,14]).  
- Weakness: distributed optimization lacks formal theoretical analysis—no convergence guarantees or rates provided.  
- Weakness: claim of solving “substantially harder problems” is unsupported by quantitative comparisons to prior 3D work.  
- Weakness: perturbation method for computing optimal feedback gains is described but not thoroughly validated.  
- Weakness: relationship between noise hyperparameters (σε, σγ) and learned policy quality is not systematically explored.  
- Weakness: no analysis of how network size/architecture affects performance across different morphologies despite using a single architecture.  
- Pro: provides a practical, well-motivated solution with clear applicability to animation and robotics communities.  
- Con: would benefit from stronger theoretical grounding, broader comparative experiments, and systematic hyperparameter analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—strengthen distinctions from related work ([9,11]) and provide more systematic analysis of noise parameters and theory.
