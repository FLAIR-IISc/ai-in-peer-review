**Summary**

This paper presents a novel method for automatically synthesizing interactive, real-time neural network controllers for physically-simulated characters with complex and diverse morphologies. The core idea is to combine offline trajectory optimization with supervised learning in a joint optimization framework. Specifically, the method alternates between optimizing a set of motion trajectories using Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and training a neural network policy to mimic the optimizer's behavior. The key innovations are the tight coupling of these two processes and a principled approach to injecting noise for robustness. Instead of training on static data, the trajectory optimization objective includes a term that encourages trajectories to be reproducible by the current network policy. Furthermore, to train a robust policy, the network is fed sensory inputs corrupted by Gaussian noise, and the target actions are adjusted using optimal feedback gains computed from the trajectory optimizer. This teaches the network not just to follow a trajectory, but how to correct for perturbations. The authors demonstrate the power and generality of their approach by applying the exact same algorithm and network architecture to learn stable locomotion for a wide variety of 3D characters, including bipeds, quadrupeds, a swimmer, and a flyer, with natural, emergent gaits.

**Strengths**

The primary strength of this paper is its novelty and ambition. The goal of a universal method for synthesizing controllers for arbitrary morphologies and tasks is a bold and important one. This work takes a significant step in that direction, moving beyond prior work in 2D to tackle the substantially harder problem of stable, interactive locomotion in 3D. The results are highly compelling and demonstrate a level of generality rarely seen in character control.

The core methodological contribution is also very strong. The idea of jointly optimizing the expert trajectories and the student policy, rather than treating it as a one-shot distillation, is powerful. It allows the policy's inductive bias to regularize the space of possible motions, leading to more consistent and learnable behaviors, as qualitatively shown in the comparison with MPC. The most elegant technical contribution is the method for generating training data for a stochastic policy. By computing optimal feedback gains from the direct trajectory optimizer and using them to generate optimal actions for noisy states, the authors have devised a principled way to teach the network how to stabilize itself. This is a far more sophisticated approach than simply training on a fixed dataset and is shown to be critical for success in the ablation study.

Finally, the empirical validation is a major strength. Demonstrating successful control for bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, and flyers with a single, unified algorithm is a powerful testament to the method's generality. The emergence of natural gaits like walking, trotting, and flapping without being explicitly programmed is a hallmark of a successful synthesis method and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

**Weaknesses**

While the overall approach is impressive, there are a few weaknesses, though they do not detract significantly from the core contribution.

1.  **Heuristic Optimization:** The block-alternating optimization scheme (Algorithm 1) is heuristic and lacks formal convergence guarantees. The paper acknowledges that it may be slower than more complex methods like ADMM and relies on proximal regularization terms to prevent divergence. While this pragmatic approach is justified by the excellent results, a more theoretical analysis of the optimization dynamics would have strengthened the paper.

2.  **Qualitative Comparisons:** The comparison to alternative approaches like direct reinforcement learning and Model-Predictive Control (MPC) is largely qualitative. The authors state their "impression" that direct RL methods produce more chaotic behaviors, but a quantitative comparison against a contemporary baseline (e.g., TRPO) on one of the characters would have made the claims about motion quality more concrete. However, given the significant novelty of the proposed method, this is not a fatal flaw.

3.  **Limited Scope of Interaction:** The "interaction" demonstrated is limited to changing the character's target goal location. The method's robustness to more direct physical perturbations (e.g., being pushed) is not evaluated. This is an acceptable limitation for a paper introducing a new foundational method, but it is a clear avenue for future work.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **High Novelty and Significance:** The paper introduces a powerful and general framework for a very difficult problem. The successful application to diverse 3D characters is a significant advance for the field and is likely to inspire a great deal of follow-up work.
*   **Strong Technical Idea:** The joint optimization of trajectories and policy, particularly the use of optimal feedback gains to enable robust learning, is an elegant and impactful contribution.
*   **Compelling Empirical Results:** The demonstrated controllers are state-of-the-art for this type of learning-based approach, showing a level of quality and generality that is rarely achieved.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   **Lack of Quantitative Baselines:** The paper would be stronger if it included direct, quantitative comparisons to other leading control methods.
*   **Heuristic Nature of the Algorithm:** The optimization procedure is not fully principled and lacks convergence guarantees.

**Recommendation:**

I strongly recommend acceptance. This paper presents a bold, novel, and highly effective approach to a challenging and important problem. The core ideas are creative, and the results are a significant step forward for learning-based control. While there are minor weaknesses, such as the heuristic optimization scheme and the lack of quantitative comparisons, they are far outweighed by the novelty and impact of the contribution. This is precisely the kind of work—prioritizing a significant new direction over methodological perfection—that NIPS should showcase. It provides a foundational method that others are very likely to use and build upon.
