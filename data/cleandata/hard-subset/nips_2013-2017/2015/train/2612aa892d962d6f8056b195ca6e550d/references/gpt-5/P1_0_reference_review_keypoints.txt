- Proposes a scalable method that alternates trajectory optimization (CIO) and supervised learning to synthesize real-time neural controllers for diverse simulated 3D characters.  
- Injects Gaussian noise into sensory inputs and adjusts regression targets using first-order optimal feedback gains computed from trajectory optimizers.  
- Implements a distributed, asynchronous training pipeline (CPU for optimization, GPU for NN) and a real-time T=1 inverse-dynamics optimization for execution.  
- Demonstrates stable, realistic behaviors for swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped locomotion with ablations and network-size comparisons.  
- Builds on guided policy search and contact-rich trajectory optimization, extending these ideas to harder 3D character control settings.  
- Technical novelty: efficient feedback-gain computation via perturbation reuse of Gauss–Newton inverse (C X X−1 and Woodbury).  
- Technical novelty: noise-injection training using gain-informed targets (a + A_s ε) and an asynchronous proximal-regularized block-alternating optimization.  
- Technical novelty: real-time short-horizon optimization that enforces soft dynamics/contacts while tracking policy outputs.  
- Strength: unified pipeline works across multiple morphologies without motion capture or hand-designed state machines.  
- Strength: practical, scalable implementation with clear engineering details and runtime (2.5h on AWS).  
- Weakness: evaluation is largely qualitative with few standardized metrics, no statistics across seeds, and limited baseline comparisons.  
- Weakness: physical realism and hardware applicability are questionable due to soft contact enforcement, root forces, and actions including desired contact forces.  
- Weakness: lack of theoretical/convergence analysis for the alternating asynchronous scheme and limited sensitivity exploration for λ, α, η.  
- Weakness: reproducibility gaps—task costs, feature definitions φ, state/action dimensions, and solver tolerances are underspecified.  
- Comparison to MPC is qualitative; trade-offs (task time, energy, robustness) are not quantified.  
- Overall technical quality is sound and well engineered, but numerical conditioning and effects of λ on As need more detail.  
- Clarity is good at a high level, but notation and exact sensory/feature definitions should be made explicit; results rely heavily on supplemental video.  
- Originality is moderate: incremental but meaningful contributions in feedback-gain extraction, noise-target scheme, and a practical asynchronous training architecture.  
- Significance is high for animation/graphics and model-based control, moderate for broader NeurIPS audience pending stronger quantitative baselines and robustness analyses.  
- Suggested improvements: add quantitative metrics, compare to stronger baselines (GPS variants, TRPO, MPC), sensitivity analyses, hard-contact/torque-level execution, and fuller reproducibility details.  
- Pros: stable 3D behaviors, elegant gain-informed noise approach, practical scalable system with potential impact on interactive control and animation.  
- Cons: qualitative evaluation, soft-contact execution limits realism, missing sensitivity/convergence analyses, incomplete reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept—strong system and technical idea but would benefit from added quantitative evaluations and stronger baselines for full NeurIPS impact.
