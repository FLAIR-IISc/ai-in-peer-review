- Proposes a method that combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with neural network training via a distributed stochastic optimization loop and noise injection.  
- Demonstrates interactive, real-time control for diverse 3D characters (biped, quadruped, swimmer, flyer) using a single network architecture.  
- Provides detailed mathematical formulation and derivations (including optimal feedback gains).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with ablation studies showing noise injection and joint optimization are critical.  
- Reports performance gains of about two orders of magnitude over naive static-dataset neural training.  
- Acknowledges dependence on trajectory optimization to generate training data and significant computational cost (hours on cloud).  
- Clarity is generally good and well-organized, though some derivations lack intuitive explanation and some implementation details hinder reproducibility.  
- Original contribution: first successful trajectory-optimization-based neural control for diverse 3D characters and a novel distributed stochastic optimization scheme.  
- Noise injection technique improves robustness and is a useful extension of prior methods.  
- Limited comparison to state-of-the-art deep RL methods for continuous control (e.g., SAC, DDPG).  
- Does not fully discuss limitations like handling large perturbations, extreme environments, or real-world robot applicability.  
- Significant impact for animation, robotics, and biomechanics by removing need for task-specific hand-crafted controllers.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen RL comparisons and expand discussion of limitations.
