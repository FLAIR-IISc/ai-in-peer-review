- Method combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory generation with supervised learning to train neural controllers for complex 3D characters, using noise injection and distributed optimization.  
- CIO generates diverse optimal trajectories which the neural network learns to mimic and generalize to new situations.  
- Demonstrated stable, realistic locomotion across diverse morphologies (swimming, flying, bipeds, quadrupeds) without task-specific architectures.  
- Advances prior work by achieving stable 3D locomotion where similar methods were previously limited to 2D; related to but distinct from MPC with more cyclical predictable behaviors.  
- Strong empirical results with compelling supplementary video showcasing realistic, stable motions.  
- Effectively addresses data scarcity, overfitting, and instability via noise injection and distributed training.  
- Proposes a unified, general-purpose control framework applicable to varied character morphologies without hand-designed controllers.  
- Provides solid theoretical grounding, including analysis of optimal feedback gains.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear explanations of core ideas and technical details.  
- Limited comparative analysis with other direct neural control methods; lacks quantified comparisons of “chaotic behaviors.”  
- Insufficient sensitivity analysis or justification for key hyperparameters (σε, σγ, α, λ, η).  
- High computational cost of trajectory generation could limit adoption; lacks detailed mitigation strategies (e.g., transfer learning, data augmentation).  
- Empirical focus is primarily on locomotion, so generality claims are not fully validated for manipulation or object interaction.  
- Quality: 8/10 — technically sound and well-supported by experiments.  
- Clarity: 7/10 — generally clear but some derivations could be simplified.  
- Originality: 8/10 — novel application of CIO + neural learning to 3D locomotion.  
- Significance: 8/10 — results are important with potential impact in robotics, animation, and biomechanics.  
- Pros: Significant advance for 3D control, tackles high-dimensional challenges, unified approach, strong empirical backing.  
- Cons: Limited comparisons, missing hyperparameter sensitivity study, and high computational cost.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, with requested improvements on comparative analysis and hyperparameter sensitivity in the final version.
