- Scales neural-network controllers to complex 3D locomotion across diverse morphologies (bipedal, quadrupedal, flying, swimming) using a unified framework.  
- Introduces noise injection and a perturbation-based feedback gain computation to improve stability and robustness of learned policies.  
- Alternates trajectory optimization (CIO) and neural network regression in a distributed stochastic optimization framework, enabling real-time interactive control.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show joint optimization and noise injection are necessary; method outperforms naive NN training by ~100x on test performance.  
- Distributed training architecture is practical and well-documented (2.5 hours on 30 optimization nodes); policy execution via optimization is efficient and stable.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparisons to modern RL methods (e.g., SAC, PPO) and recent neural MPC/model-based RL work, weakening claims about "chaotic behaviors" from direct methods.  
- No theoretical justification for chosen noise parameters (σε, σγ) or the feedback gain calculation, limiting generalizability.  
- Does not evaluate generalization to out-of-distribution tasks (new terrains, fluid properties), a key limitation for real-world use.  
- Training computational cost is substantial and the paper does not explore reducing resource requirements for larger-scale adoption.  
- Situates work appropriately with CIO and prior neural control but should better differentiate from recent neural MPC/model-based RL literature.  
- Overall: strong, practically relevant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add comparisons to modern RL, discuss generalization, and clarify relation to neural MPC.
