- Proposes alternating optimization that combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with supervised learning of a neural policy for interactive, real-time control of diverse 3D characters.
- Policy maps sensory state s = [q, r, q̇prev, fprev] to action a = [q̇, ṙ, f], with learned recurrent state r integrated from ṙ.
- Bridges offline trajectory optimization and online control via: sensory noise injection with target adjustment using perturbation-based feedback gains; alternating trajectory/policy optimization; proximal regularization for async distributed training; and a T=1 implicit runtime solve.
- Demonstrations show visually plausible, cyclic locomotion across bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmer, and bird; ablations indicate joint optimization and noise matter; brief MPC comparison suggests more predictable gaits.
- Method closely related to Guided Policy Search (GPS); novelties include using CIO in the loop, a perturbation-based method to extract stabilizing gains from a direct optimizer (using soft constraints and Woodbury), and scaling to 3D.
- Strength: tackles high-dimensional, contact-rich control with a solid decomposition between planning (CIO) and reactive policy learning.
- Strength: novel, practical derivation of stabilizing gains from a direct optimizer that avoids ill-conditioning of post-hoc LQG.
- Strength: principled noise injection combined with local target linearization (a + a_s ε) for better closed-loop deployment.
- Strength: pragmatic asynchronous distributed implementation with proximal regularization.
- Weakness: limited quantitative evaluation—missing success rates, trajectory costs, robustness to perturbations, tracking error, and runtime/latency metrics.
- Weakness: weak baselines—lacks comparisons to GPS variants, model-free RL (e.g., TRPO/modern actor-critic), and policies trained without gain-augmented targets.
- Weakness: soft enforcement of dynamics and allowed root forces during execution may mask physical violations; constraint violation statistics are not reported.
- Weakness: unclear robustness of feedback gains to contact timing/topology changes and potential instabilities near contact switches.
- Weakness: policy recurrence and memory r are under-specified—dimensionality, initialization, whether the network is recurrent, and sensitivity analyses are missing.
- Weakness: generalization/extrapolation beyond CIO data manifold and extent of retargeting not evaluated.
- Weakness: missing important implementation/hyperparameter details (λ conditioning role, CIO computational cost, precise s and a dimensions) hindering reproducibility.
- Quality: technically competent with a useful gain derivation, but empirical validation is mainly qualitative and insufficient to support strong claims.
- Clarity: generally clear at high level but has notational inconsistencies, missing experimental details, and reference formatting issues.
- Originality: moderately original—framework is close to GPS; main contributions are engineering-level (CIO integration, gain extraction, async proximal variant).
- Significance: potentially impactful for character animation and contact-rich control if strengthened by rigorous quantitative comparisons and robustness evidence.
- Suggestions: add quantitative evaluations (success rates, perturbation robustness, constraint violations, runtimes) and stronger baselines (GPS, RL, no-gain ablations).
- Suggestions: ablate feedback-gain augmentation and vary λ; report r’s size/role and sensitivity; tighten or quantify execution-time physics fidelity.
- Pros: practical, scalable pipeline; sensible fusion of CIO and supervised policy learning; low-overhead method to extract stabilizing gains.
- Cons: weak quantitative evidence and baselines, overlap with GPS literature, and reproducibility/clarity gaps.
