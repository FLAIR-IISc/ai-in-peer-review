- Proposes alternating, distributed combination of Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and supervised neural policy learning for real-time 3D character control.
- Each iteration: re-optimize trajectories with a penalty to match the current policy under sensory noise, then regress a policy to noisy sensory-action pairs with local linear feedback corrections a + A_s ε.
- Novel technique to compute well-conditioned local feedback gains A_s directly from CIO via a soft-constraint perturbation, reusing C_XX^-1 and the Woodbury identity.
- Training is distributed across many CPU nodes for CIO with a single GPU for the neural net, stabilized by proximal regularization for asynchronous updates.
- Execution uses a 1-step implicit dynamics-constrained optimization (T=1 MPC) for real-time control.
- Demonstrations include diverse 3D morphologies (biped, quadruped, bird, swimmer), showing impressive practical results.
- Strength: ambitious, practically relevant goal with broad-interest demonstrations across ML, control, and graphics communities.
- Strength: clean integration of trajectory optimization and supervised learning with a scalable alternating scheme that converges empirically.
- Strength: the A_s derivation is clever and avoids a separate LQG pass while providing tunable smoothing via λ.
- Strength: principled robustness via sensor-noise injection, linearized noisy targets, and hidden-unit Gaussian noise to improve generalization and closed-loop stability.
- Strength: substantial engineering contribution—a practical distributed pipeline and real-time execution capability.
- Weakness: sparse quantitative evaluation—lacks concrete metrics (cost, success/fall rates, energy), statistics, and plots to substantiate claims.
- Weakness: limited empirical baselines—no direct comparisons to GPS/ADMM methods or modern policy-gradient baselines like TRPO.
- Weakness: clarity gaps—underspecified recurrent memory r (dimensionality, initialization, dynamics) and incomplete specification of trajectory costs and weights.
- Weakness: missing quantitative details for ablations, network sweeps, and MPC comparisons mentioned but not reported in text.
- Weakness: physical realism concerns—dynamics enforced as soft constraints may allow non-zero root forces; impact not analyzed.
- Weakness: sensitivity to noise magnitude σε, softness λ, and hidden-noise σ_γ not explored; asynchronous alternating stability only empirically observed.
- Quality: algorithmic components are sound and thoughtfully combined, but experimental evidence and baselines need strengthening.
- Clarity: generally readable but important reproducibility details and some typos remain; a reproducibility appendix is recommended.
- Originality: builds on known trajectory-optimization-guided policy learning but contributes novel A_s computation within CIO and a distributed proximal alternating implementation.
- Significance: potentially impactful for robust interactive control if supported by stronger quantitative evidence and comparisons.
- Suggested fixes: provide task metrics, compare to strong baselines, detail r and cost specs, analyze λ/σε/σ_γ sensitivity, report root-force usage, and include full implementation hyperparameters.
- Pros for acceptance: scalable, practical method with novel technical contribution and compelling 3D demos.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient quantitative evaluation, reproducibility gaps, and execution using soft dynamics constraints undermining physical realism.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on strengthening quantitative evaluation and clarifications.
