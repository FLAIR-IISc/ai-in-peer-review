1. Summary: Proposes neural-network controllers for diverse 3D characters by extending Contact-Invariant Optimization to 3D and jointly optimizing trajectories and network parameters via distributed stochastic optimization with noise injection.

2. Strength — Technical contribution: Extends trajectory-optimization-based neural controllers from 2D to complex 3D locomotion tasks.

3. Strength — Comprehensive evaluation: Validated across biped, quadruped, swimmer, and flyer with strong baselines and clear ablation studies showing component importance.

4. Strength — Practical significance: Enables real-time interactive control without hand-crafted controllers or motion-capture data, producing diverse behaviors automatically.

5. Strength — Methodological clarity: Clearly explains distributed stochastic optimization, perturbation-based feedback gain calculation, and implementation details for reproducibility.

6. Strength — Novelty: Meaningful advance through 3D extension, distributed optimization framework, and systematic noise-injection analysis beyond prior work.

7. Weakness — Limited comparison: Omits comparisons to recent state-of-the-art reinforcement learning methods for continuous control.

8. Weakness — Computational cost: Training is computationally expensive (hours on large clusters with many nodes per GPU) with no discussion of cost reduction.

9. Weakness — Limited theoretical analysis: Lacks deeper theoretical guarantees on convergence of the distributed stochastic optimizer and policy generalization.

10. Weakness — Narrow evaluation scope: Experiments focus only on locomotion and omit harder scenarios like dynamic obstacles, varied terrain, or object interactions.

11. Overall assessment: Strong, well-executed contribution that convincingly extends trajectory-optimization-driven neural control to 3D with impactful methodological innovations.

12. Recommendation: Accept — paper is technically sound, clearly written, original, and significant despite noted limitations.
