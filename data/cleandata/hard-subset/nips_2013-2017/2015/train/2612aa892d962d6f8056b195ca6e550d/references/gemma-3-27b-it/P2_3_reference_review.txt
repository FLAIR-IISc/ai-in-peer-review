## Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a method for learning neural network control policies for complex, 3D articulated characters by combining trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization - CIO) with supervised learning. The core idea is to use CIO to generate a dataset of optimal trajectories and then train a neural network to mimic the control actions from those trajectories, enabling real-time interactive control. The authors address key challenges such as stabilizing the learned policy through noise injection and scaling the training process with a distributed architecture. They demonstrate successful application to diverse characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) exhibiting complex locomotion behaviors.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon the growing trend of using deep learning for control, specifically linking it to trajectory optimization techniques. It acknowledges related work in reinforcement learning (RL) and stochastic optimal control, while positioning itself as addressing the difficulties of applying these methods to high-dimensional, continuous spaces. The paper correctly references recent works [9, 11] that also combine trajectory optimization and neural networks, and clearly articulates its advantage in tackling more complex 3D locomotion.  It also distinguishes its approach from more traditional physics-based control methods which often rely on hand-designed architectures for each character type.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a challenging problem:** Interactive control of complex characters in 3D is a long-standing problem with many potential applications. The paper tackles this problem head-on, moving beyond 2D limitations of prior work.
* **Effective combination of methods:** The synergy between CIO (for data generation) and neural networks (for real-time control) is well-motivated and appears to be effective.  CIO provides high-quality data, and the NN enables generalization and real-time interaction.
* **Novelty in 3D locomotion:** Demonstrating stable and realistic 3D locomotion across diverse character morphologies is a significant achievement, particularly given the difficulties of the problem.
* **Robustness through noise injection:** The principled approach to noise injection, leveraging optimal feedback gains, appears to be crucial for stabilizing the learned policy and preventing overfitting.
* **Scalable implementation:** The distributed training architecture and GPU implementation are essential for handling the computational demands of this problem.
* **Clear presentation of technical details:** The paper provides a good level of detail regarding the trajectory optimization, neural network architecture, and training process.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited comparative evaluation:** While ablation studies are included, a more comprehensive comparison to other control methods (e.g., RL, MPC) would strengthen the claims of superiority.  The assertion that networks trained with direct methods yield “somewhat chaotic behaviors” needs more rigorous justification.
* **Hyperparameter sensitivity:** The algorithm has several hyperparameters (noise levels, regularization weights, network architecture). The sensitivity of the performance to these parameters is not thoroughly discussed.
* **Task Specificity of Cost Functions:** While the paper demonstrates generalization across *character morphology*, the cost functions used for trajectory optimization are quite similar across tasks (reaching a goal, minimizing effort). The flexibility in adapting these costs for different behaviors beyond locomotion isn't fully explored.
* **Limited Discussion of Failure Cases:** The paper primarily presents successful results. Discussion of cases where the method fails (e.g., specific character morphologies, complex tasks) would provide a more balanced assessment.
* **Dependence on CIO:** The method's performance is heavily reliant on the quality of the trajectories generated by CIO.  Any limitations of CIO (e.g., computational cost, difficulties with highly constrained tasks) will directly impact the performance of the learned policy.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach to interactive control of complex characters. The combination of trajectory optimization and neural networks is well-motivated, and the results demonstrate the potential of this approach to address a challenging problem. While there are some weaknesses, particularly regarding comparative evaluation and discussion of limitations, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Tackles a challenging and important problem in robotics and animation.
* Presents a novel and effective method with demonstrated success on diverse character types.
* Clear and well-supported technical contributions.
* Scalable implementation enabling practical application.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited comparative evaluation against alternative control methods.
* Lack of detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity.
* Dependence on the performance of the underlying trajectory optimization algorithm.




**Rating:** **Strong Accept (8/10)**

I believe this paper is a valuable contribution to the field and is worthy of acceptance to NIPS. The authors have presented a technically sound and innovative approach to a challenging problem, and the results are compelling. Addressing the suggestions for improvement (particularly a more thorough comparative evaluation) would further strengthen the paper.
