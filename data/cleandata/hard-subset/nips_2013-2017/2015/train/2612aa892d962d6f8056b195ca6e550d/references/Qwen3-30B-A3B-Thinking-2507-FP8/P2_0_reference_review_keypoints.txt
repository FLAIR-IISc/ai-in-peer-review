- Proposes a novel pipeline combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and neural networks to produce interactive, real-time controllers for diverse 3D characters.  
- Trains networks to mimic optimal trajectories with noise injection using principled optimal feedback gains for robustness.  
- Introduces a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm (Algorithm 1) that scales training without complex solvers like ADMM.  
- Demonstrates a single controller architecture working across bipedal, quadrupedal, flying, and swimming characters.  
- Provides clear mathematical formulation and a comprehensive ablation study validating key components.  
- Shows performance far superior to naive neural network training (about two orders of magnitude).  
- Highlights the critical role of noise injection for robust control.  
- Acknowledges limitations: requires hours of training and depends on trajectory optimization to generate data.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with useful figures and a supplementary video.  
- Would benefit from more intuitive explanation of trajectory optimization and more detail on sensory state representation.  
- Represents a meaningful extension of prior 2D trajectory-optimization work to 3D and varied morphologies.  
- Significant practical impact for animation, gaming, and robotics; potential to generalize to other behaviors.  
- Weaknesses include limited comparison to state-of-the-art RL, limited exploration of network architectures, and unclear limits on character types handled.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong technical contribution, thorough validation, and clear significance despite minor weaknesses.
