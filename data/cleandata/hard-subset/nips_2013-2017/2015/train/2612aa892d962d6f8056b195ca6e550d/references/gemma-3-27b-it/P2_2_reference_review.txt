## Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a method for learning neural network control policies for complex, 3D characters through a combination of trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization - CIO) and supervised learning. The core idea is to use CIO to generate a diverse dataset of optimal trajectories, then train a neural network to mimic these trajectories and generalize to new situations.  Crucially, the authors address challenges of training in high-dimensional control spaces by injecting noise during training and employing a distributed optimization scheme. They demonstrate the method's ability to generate stable and realistic locomotion for a variety of characters (swimming, flying, bipeds, quadrupeds) without requiring task-specific architectures.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon existing work in trajectory optimization (CIO [12]) and neural network control [3, 6, 9, 11]. It distinguishes itself by achieving stable 3D locomotion – a feat not previously demonstrated by similar methods which were limited to 2D characters. The work also relates to model-predictive control (MPC) but argues for the benefits of their approach in generating more cyclical and predictable behaviors.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Empirical Results:** The authors convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness across a diverse range of character morphologies and locomotion tasks.  The supplementary video is particularly valuable in showcasing the realistic and stable movements achieved.
* **Addresses Key Challenges:** The paper tackles critical issues in learning high-dimensional control policies: data scarcity, overfitting, and instability.  The noise injection and distributed training are sensible and well-motivated solutions.
* **Unified Approach:** The method aims for a general-purpose control framework, applicable to a wide variety of characters without hand-designed controllers. This is a significant step towards the ambitious goal of universal control synthesis.
* **Theoretical Grounding:** The paper provides a solid theoretical basis for the method, including a description of optimal feedback gains and the rationale behind the chosen optimization approach.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and provides a clear explanation of the core ideas and technical details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Comparative Analysis:** While the ablation studies (Figure 2) demonstrate the importance of joint optimization and noise injection, a more comprehensive comparison with other direct neural network control methods (e.g., those cited in the introduction) would strengthen the paper.  Specifically, quantifying the “chaotic behaviors” mentioned in relation to those methods would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The authors mention specific values for the algorithmic constants (σε, σγ, α, λ, η), but do not provide extensive sensitivity analysis or a justification for these particular choices.  Understanding the impact of these parameters on performance would be valuable.
* **Computational Cost:** The paper acknowledges the significant computational cost of trajectory generation. While practical for research, this could be a barrier to wider adoption. Discussion on potential strategies for reducing this cost (e.g., transfer learning, data augmentation) would be useful.
* **Focus on Locomotion:** While the paper claims generality, the primary focus is on locomotion.  Extending the method to other types of complex behaviors (manipulation, interaction with objects) would further demonstrate its versatility.



**Assessment of Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and well-supported by experimental results. The combination of CIO and neural networks is a logical and effective approach. The analysis of optimal feedback gains is particularly strong. (Score: 8/10)
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clear and well-organized, but some sections (e.g., the derivation of optimal feedback gains) could benefit from further simplification.  (Score: 7/10)
* **Originality:** The application of CIO in conjunction with neural network learning for 3D locomotion represents a novel contribution. Extending previous work to this dimensionality is significant. (Score: 8/10)
* **Significance:**  The results are important and have the potential to impact areas such as robotics, animation, and biomechanics. The development of a general-purpose control framework is a valuable step towards more intelligent and autonomous systems. (Score: 8/10)

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Demonstrates a significant advance in learning control policies for complex, 3D characters.
* Tackles key challenges in high-dimensional control.
* Presents a unified approach applicable to a diverse range of morphologies.
* Backed by strong empirical results.

**Con:**

* Limited comparative analysis with other control methods.
* Lack of sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters.
* High computational cost.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept.**  Despite some minor weaknesses, this is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of neural network control. The results are impressive, the approach is well-motivated, and the paper is generally well-written. The potential impact of this work is considerable, and it represents a valuable addition to the NIPS program.  I recommend the authors address the weaknesses related to comparative analysis and hyperparameter sensitivity in a final version of the paper.
