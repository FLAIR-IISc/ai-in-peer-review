- Presents a novel method combining trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) with neural network training to generate controllers for diverse 3D characters.
- Introduces a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm that jointly optimizes trajectories and network parameters.
- Proposes a perturbation method for computing optimal feedback gains, avoiding extra LQG passes.
- Demonstrates stable, realistic interactive control for bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, and flying characters, extending prior 2D work to 3D.
- Empirically shows convergence and effectiveness through thorough experiments and ablation studies (joint optimization, noise injection).
- Paper is technically sound with detailed algorithmic description, but lacks formal theoretical guarantees for the distributed optimization.
- Computational requirements are substantial (~2.5 hours training) but justified by problem complexity.
- Well-organized and clearly written; some sections (e.g., feedback gains) require trajectory optimization background and the video is important for full evaluation.
- The distributed optimization framework and feedback-gain perturbation represent novel, meaningful technical contributions.
- Significant for unifying character control across morphologies and enabling interactive real-time control with applications in gaming, animation, and robotics.
- Strengths: solves challenging 3D locomotion, unified single-architecture solution, novel algorithm, comprehensive validation and ablations.
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical analysis, could compare more to state-of-the-art methods, training cost may be prohibitive, relation to specific prior work could be clearer.
- Recommendation: Accept for NeurIPS — strong empirical contribution, rigorous experiments, and clear potential for impact.
