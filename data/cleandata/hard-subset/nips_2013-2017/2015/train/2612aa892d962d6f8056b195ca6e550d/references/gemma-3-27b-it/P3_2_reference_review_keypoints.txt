- Overall assessment: Strong, novel pipeline combining CIO and neural policies for robust 3D character control; recommend accept with minor revisions.

- Summary/prior work: Uses Contact-Invariant Optimization to generate diverse optimal trajectories, then trains noise-injected neural policies for real-time control, extending prior trajectory-optimization approaches to challenging 3D tasks.

- Strength — Technical soundness: Rigorous constrained optimization, noise-based policy training, perturbation-derived feedback gains, and distributed optimization are well-justified.

- Strength — Significant results: Demonstrates stable, realistic 3D locomotion across multiple morphologies and applicability to swimming/flying; strong supplementary video.

- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and explains CIO, noise injection, and distributed optimization clearly.

- Strength — Novelty: Novel combination of CIO, noise training, and joint control/dynamics network, scaled successfully to 3D locomotion.

- Strength — Practical implementation: Provides computational details (cloud/GPU) and enough information for reproducibility.

- Weakness — Limited comparison to direct methods: Lacks systematic, quantitative baselines (e.g., PPO) to substantiate claims about direct RL being inferior.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: No discussion or analysis of sensitivity to algorithmic constants and choices.

- Weakness — Generalization beyond locomotion: Experiments focus on locomotion; other behaviors (getting up, climbing) are mentioned but not demonstrated.

- Weakness — Potential bias in trajectory generation: Policy quality depends on CIO data diversity; potential biases in generated trajectories are not discussed.

- Weakness — Computational cost: Acknowledges high compute but lacks detailed breakdown (time per trajectory, total compute hours).

- Pro arguments for acceptance: Solves a hard problem with a novel effective approach, strong 3D results, technical rigor, and broad impact potential.

- Con arguments against acceptance: Insufficient comparative evaluation with direct methods, limited hyperparameter/bias discussion, and narrow experimental scope focused on locomotion.

- Revision suggestion — Direct method comparison: Add quantitative and qualitative comparisons to a standard RL baseline (e.g., PPO) on the same tasks.

- Revision suggestion — Hyperparameter analysis: Provide sensitivity analysis or discussion of key hyperparameter choices (noise levels, regularization).

- Revision suggestion — Expand evaluation: Demonstrate additional behaviors beyond locomotion (e.g., getting up, climbing).

- Revision suggestion — Data diversity discussion: Discuss potential biases in CIO-generated trajectories and their effects on learned policies.

- Revision suggestion — Computational cost details: Provide detailed compute breakdown (time per trajectory, total compute resources).
