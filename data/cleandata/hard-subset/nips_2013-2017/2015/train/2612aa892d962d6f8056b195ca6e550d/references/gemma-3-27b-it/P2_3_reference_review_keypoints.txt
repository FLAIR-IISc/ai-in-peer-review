- Presents a method combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with supervised learning to train neural-network controllers for complex 3D articulated characters for real-time interactive control.
- Uses CIO to generate optimal trajectories, trains NNs to mimic control actions, stabilizes policies via noise injection, and scales training with a distributed GPU architecture.
- Positions work relative to RL/stochastic optimal control and prior trajectory-optimization+NN methods (cites [9,11]) and emphasizes moving beyond 2D limitations.
- Strength: tackles the challenging problem of interactive 3D control across diverse character morphologies.
- Strength: effective synergy between high-quality CIO data and NNs for generalization and real-time performance.
- Strength: demonstrates stable, realistic 3D locomotion for multiple character types (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal).
- Strength: principled noise-injection strategy using optimal feedback gains improves policy robustness.
- Strength: scalable distributed implementation and clear technical presentation of methods.
- Weakness: limited comparative evaluation against alternative control methods (e.g., RL, MPC); some claims need stronger justification.
- Weakness: many hyperparameters (noise, regularization, network architecture) with insufficient sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: cost functions used are similar across tasks; limited exploration of adapting costs for varied non-locomotion behaviors.
- Weakness: limited discussion of failure cases or scenarios where the method struggles.
- Weakness: strong dependence on CIO quality; CIO limitations (compute cost, handling constraints) may limit learned-policy performance.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective contribution with results that outweigh the noted weaknesses.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, novel and effective method, demonstrated across diverse characters, scalable and well-supported technically.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparative evaluation, lack of hyperparameter analysis, dependence on trajectory optimizer.
- Reviewer rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
