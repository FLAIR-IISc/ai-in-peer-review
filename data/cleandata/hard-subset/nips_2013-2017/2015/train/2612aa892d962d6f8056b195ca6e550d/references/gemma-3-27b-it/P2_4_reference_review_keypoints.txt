- Proposes combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with neural network regression to learn real-time control policies for complex 3D articulated characters.  
- Uses CIO to synthesize optimal trajectory datasets and trains neural networks to mimic them for interactive control.  
- Addresses data acquisition, high-dimensional training, and generalization via noise injection and a distributed training architecture.  
- Demonstrates the method on diverse morphologies: swimming, flying, bipedal and quadrupedal characters.  
- Properly situates itself relative to reinforcement learning, optimal control, and prior trajectory-optimization-to-network work.  
- Differentiates from prior work by tackling more challenging 3D locomotion tasks with a unified architecture.  
- Strength: tackles a difficult problem and automates controller design for complex characters.  
- Strength: scalable approach—CIO for data and networks for real-time control—with noise injection improving generalization (supported by ablations).  
- Strength: unified framework controlling diverse characters with the same architecture and parameters.  
- Strength: detailed implementation, distributed training description, and useful ablation studies.  
- Strength: strong empirical results showing stable, realistic 3D locomotion (supplementary video supports this).  
- Weakness: lacks systematic, quantitative comparison to direct methods like reinforcement learning.  
- Weakness: high computational cost for generating training data via CIO, limiting practicality for frequent retraining.  
- Weakness: no analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters despite listing their values.  
- Weakness: missing explicit interactive/user study demonstrating real-time user control.  
- Qualitative: technically sound, clear analysis of noise injection and optimization; more explanation needed for the recurrent memory state.  
- Originality: novel application of CIO+neural regression to complex 3D locomotion and a clever perturbation method for feedback gains.  
- Significance: potentially high impact for character animation and robotics as a unified control framework.  
- Recommendation: overall a strong accept, with suggested improvements including more comparisons, cost-reduction strategies, and user evaluation.
