

# Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

This paper proposes a method for generating neural network controllers for interactive control of diverse 3D character animations. The authors address the challenge of creating stable, realistic locomotion controllers for various character types (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, flying) without task-specific handcrafting. Their approach combines Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) [12] with neural network training through a distributed stochastic optimization framework. The key innovation is a joint optimization procedure that simultaneously generates optimal trajectories and trains neural network policies to mimic them, with noise injection to improve robustness. The method differs from previous work [9, 11] by achieving stable 3D locomotion (previously limited to 2D) and demonstrating applicability across diverse character morphologies with a single controller architecture.

**Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a well-structured technical approach with clear mathematical formulations of the trajectory optimization problem, optimal feedback gains calculation, and neural network training. The authors provide a detailed distributed stochastic optimization algorithm (Algorithm 1) that effectively decomposes the complex joint optimization problem into manageable subproblems. The ablation studies in Section 8.1 provide strong empirical evidence for the necessity of their key innovations: joint optimization (vs. static training) and noise injection (vs. no noise). The implementation details using AWS EC2 and MuJoCo physics engine are thorough, and the experimental results demonstrate successful control across diverse character types. The paper carefully addresses the challenges of limited training data and instability in closed-loop control by incorporating noise injection and feedback gains.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical formulations are precise, and the authors effectively explain the technical contributions through the framework of trajectory optimization and neural network training. The distinction between the trajectory optimization phase and neural network training phase is particularly well-articulated. The supplementary video is recommended to fully appreciate the results, though the paper itself provides sufficient detail about the character behaviors. The presentation of ablation studies and comparative results is particularly clear.

**Novelty:** The paper builds on recent work in trajectory optimization for character animation [9, 11, 12] but makes a significant contribution by extending this approach to 3D complex locomotion without task-specific adaptations. The joint optimization framework that allows neural networks to learn from trajectory optimization data while maintaining stability represents a meaningful advancement over previous methods. The paper convincingly demonstrates this novelty by showing that previous approaches [9, 11] were limited to 2D characters, while their method achieves stable 3D locomotion across diverse character types. The authors also provide a clear comparison with model-predictive control (MPC), showing that their method produces more predictable and less chaotic behaviors.

**Significance:** The approach has clear significance for animation, robotics, and biomechanics. The ability to generate controllers for diverse character morphologies with a single algorithm represents a substantial step toward unified control architectures. The paper demonstrates this by successfully controlling swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal characters without specialized controllers for each type. The authors also thoughtfully discuss potential applications in robotics, where the trained controllers could be adapted to real-world robots with appropriate physics models. The method's ability to handle interactive changes in task goals (e.g., changing direction mid-trajectory) further enhances its practical relevance.

**Strengths:**
- Demonstrated success across diverse 3D character types (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal)
- Thorough ablation studies proving necessity of key innovations (joint optimization, noise injection)
- Clear technical formulation of the joint optimization problem
- Practical implementation using distributed computing infrastructure
- Strong comparison with related approaches (MPC, static neural network training)

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could provide more explicit comparison with recent 3D control methods beyond [9, 11] to better establish novelty
- Limited discussion of the method's limitations (e.g., handling extreme perturbations, scaling to more complex behaviors)
- Ablation studies are primarily focused on bipedal walking; more diverse ablation would strengthen claims
- Theoretical guarantees for the joint optimization approach could be better explained
- Computational cost details (beyond "on the order of hours") would be helpful for practitioners

**Conclusion:** This paper presents a technically sound and novel approach to interactive character control that successfully extends previous trajectory optimization work to 3D locomotion across diverse character types. The joint optimization framework with noise injection addresses critical challenges in neural network controller training for physical systems. The results are significant for the field of character animation and have potential implications for robotics. While the paper could strengthen its novelty claim with more comprehensive comparison to recent methods and provide more detailed discussion of limitations, the technical contributions and empirical results are compelling.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**
- Pro: Demonstrates a significant extension of previous work to 3D complex locomotion with diverse character types, provides strong empirical validation through ablation studies, and presents a clear, well-structured technical approach.
- Con: Limited comparison with the most recent 3D control methods in the literature, and insufficient discussion of the method's limitations and scalability to more complex behaviors.

Overall, the paper makes a substantial contribution to the field of character animation and control, with clear technical merit, novelty, and significance. It meets the quality criteria for NIPS.
