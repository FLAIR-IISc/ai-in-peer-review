- Proposes a method combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and neural network policies to enable interactive, real-time control of diverse 3D character locomotion.
- Uses distributed stochastic joint optimization (Algorithm 1) and trajectory optimization to generate training data, with noise injection for policy robustness.
- Demonstrates stable, realistic locomotion for multiple 3D morphologies (biped, quadruped, swimming, flying) using a single network architecture.
- Technically sound implementation with well-motivated optimization framework and convincing ablation studies showing importance of joint optimization and noise injection.
- Experimental results are robust, well-documented, and compare favorably to baseline direct NN training methods.
- Original contribution: extends trajectory-optimization-based control to 3D and applies CIO + NN with noise injection across diverse morphologies without specialized initialization.
- Significant practical implications for animation and robotics by producing stable, realistic 3D locomotion across character types.
- Paper is clear and well-organized with solid mathematical formulation and useful supplementary video.
- Fails to adequately compare to recent state-of-the-art deep RL methods (e.g., SAC, PPO), which have achieved 3D locomotion.
- Lacks analysis of computational trade-offs and total training time compared to DRL, limiting practical assessment.
- Does not demonstrate generalization to novel, out-of-distribution morphologies not seen during training.
- Ablation studies are limited to biped walking and should be extended across character types to support robustness claims.
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, novel contribution with impressive results, but should address DRL comparisons, compute cost analysis, broader ablations, and OOD generalization.
