- Proposes combining trajectory optimization (CIO) with neural networks, using noise injection and distributed optimization to learn controllers for complex simulated characters.
- Demonstrates a single policy that generalizes across diverse morphologies and tasks (swimming, flying, bipeds, quadrupeds).
- Uses CIO to generate optimal trajectories and trains a neural network to mimic closed-loop control, addressing high-dimensional instability.
- Novelty: scales trajectory-optimization + learning approach to truly 3D character control.
- Technical soundness: CIO, noise injection, and distributed stochastic optimization are well-motivated; optimal feedback gains explanation is insightful.
- Empirical results are compelling, supported by video supplements and ablation studies showing benefits of joint optimization and noise injection.
- Writing is clear, mathematics concise, and algorithm explanations easy to follow.
- Practicality: leverages existing implementations (CIO, Newton’s method) and cloud computing for accessibility.
- Weakness: limited systematic comparison to alternative control methods (e.g., direct RL), current qualitative claims are insufficient.
- Weakness: lack of hyperparameter sensitivity analysis—robustness to algorithmic constants is not discussed.
- Weakness: role and learned representation of the recurrent state r are not fully explained.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on trajectory optimization cost components and their weightings.
- Weakness: vague computational cost reporting—needs detailed breakdown (hours per character, resources).
