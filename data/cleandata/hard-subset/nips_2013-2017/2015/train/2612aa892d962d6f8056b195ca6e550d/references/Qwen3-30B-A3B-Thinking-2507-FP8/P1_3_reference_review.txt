

# Peer Review: "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

## Summary
This paper presents a method for automatically generating neural network controllers for interactive, real-time control of diverse 3D characters with different morphologies (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming, flying). The approach combines trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) with neural network training to learn controllers that can handle various tasks without specialized hand-crafted controllers. The key insight is to use trajectory optimization to generate training data, then train a neural network to reproduce these trajectories while injecting noise to improve robustness. The authors demonstrate their method on a variety of 3D characters, showing that a single neural network architecture can control different character types without specialized initialization. This work extends previous trajectory optimization-based neural network control methods (Levine & Koltun 2014, Mordatch & Todorov 2012) by successfully scaling to 3D complex characters, where prior work was limited to 2D scenarios.

## Strengths
1. **Technical Contribution:** The paper makes a significant contribution by extending trajectory optimization-based neural network control to 3D complex characters, demonstrating successful locomotion for bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, and flyers. Previous work was limited to 2D, making this a meaningful advance.

2. **Methodological Innovation:** The distributed stochastic optimization approach (Algorithm 1) effectively combines trajectory optimization with neural network training. The noise injection strategy (Section 2.1) and perturbation method for computing optimal feedback gains (Section 3.2) are novel and well-justified.

3. **Empirical Results:** The results are impressive, showing the same neural network architecture controlling diverse character morphologies with different locomotion styles (cyclical flapping for birds, treading water for swimmers, trot gaits for quadrupeds). The ablation studies (Section 8.1) convincingly demonstrate the importance of joint optimization and noise injection.

4. **Practical Impact:** The approach enables interactive control of diverse characters without specialized controllers, with significant implications for animation, robotics, and biomechanics.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the method is well-motivated, the paper lacks theoretical analysis of convergence properties or guarantees of the distributed optimization approach. The authors mention convergence in Section 8.1 but provide no formal analysis.

2. **Comparison with State-of-the-Art:** The paper compares with naive neural network training on static trajectories and ablations but doesn't compare with recent deep reinforcement learning approaches (e.g., PPO, SAC) that could also be used for this task. This is a notable omission given the prominence of RL in control.

3. **Computational Cost:** The training process requires significant computational resources (cloud computing, 2.5 hours of training), which may limit practical adoption. The paper doesn't discuss the computational cost of inference compared to other methods.

4. **Lack of Quantitative Metrics:** The paper shows qualitative results (videos) but lacks quantitative metrics for comparing controller performance (e.g., energy efficiency, stability metrics, comparison to human performance).

## Evaluation
**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with well-designed experiments and clear ablation studies. The method is well-justified with appropriate references to related work. However, the lack of theoretical analysis and quantitative metrics slightly weakens the quality assessment.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The method is described in sufficient detail for experts to understand and potentially reproduce the work. Some parts (e.g., the perturbation method for feedback gains) could benefit from more intuitive explanation.

**Originality:** The paper's approach represents a novel combination of trajectory optimization and neural network training for interactive control of diverse 3D characters. The extension to 3D, diverse morphologies, and interactive control with a single neural network architecture is a meaningful contribution that significantly advances beyond previous work.

**Significance:** The results demonstrate a significant step toward unified control architectures for diverse characters with practical implications for animation, robotics, and biomechanics. The ability to learn controllers for different morphologies without specialized hand-crafted controllers is a substantial practical contribution. However, the lack of comparison with state-of-the-art RL methods limits the significance claim.

## Recommendation
I recommend acceptance. The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of neural network control for complex characters, with significant practical implications. The core method is novel, well-executed, and demonstrates impressive results across diverse character types. The main weaknesses (lack of theoretical analysis, limited comparison with RL methods) are addressable in future work and don't undermine the core contribution.

**Pro-Acceptance Arguments:**
- Demonstrates successful control of diverse 3D characters with a single architecture
- Provides a unified framework for different character types
- Includes thorough ablation studies showing importance of key components
- Well-organized and clearly written with sufficient detail for reproducibility

**Con-Acceptance Arguments:**
- Lacks theoretical analysis of the optimization approach
- Limited comparison with state-of-the-art RL methods
- No quantitative metrics for performance evaluation
- High computational cost for training (2.5 hours on cloud infrastructure)

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of neural network control for complex characters, advancing the state of the art in both methodology and practical applicability. The results are compelling and the approach has clear potential for broader adoption in animation and robotics.
