- Proposes a novel method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical tasks and morphologies.  
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO), to generate stable, realistic behaviors.  
- Network jointly learns optimal controls and a dynamics model; network outputs are defined as velocities.  
- Introduces noise during training to prevent overfitting and improve robustness.  
- Uses trajectory optimization to obtain optimal feedback gains and a perturbation method to stabilize optimal trajectories.  
- Presents a distributed stochastic optimization algorithm that alternates independent trajectory optimization and neural network regression.  
- Demonstrates effective controllers for diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking).  
- Shows the method outperforms naive neural-network training on a static optimal trajectory dataset.  
- Emphasizes that noise injection during training is crucial for avoiding overfitting.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and supported by helpful figures and tables.  
- Discusses potential applications in robotics, animation, and gaming.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.  
- Novelty: approach is strong but builds on existing ideas—trajectory optimization plus neural networks is not entirely new.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, robust realistic controllers across tasks/morphologies, clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art and limited novelty relative to existing trajectory-optimization + NN approaches.
