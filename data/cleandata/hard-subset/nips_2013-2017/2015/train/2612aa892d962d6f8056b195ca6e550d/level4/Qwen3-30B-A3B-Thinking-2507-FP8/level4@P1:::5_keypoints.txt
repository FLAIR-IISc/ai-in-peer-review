- Presents a novel method training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal).
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate controllers without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.
- Network learns a direct state-action mapping that commands velocities and contact forces, implicitly modeling dynamics and feedback.
- Key technical improvements: interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, training for unexpected task changes, and using trajectory optimization to compute optimal feedback gains.
- Extends prior work from mostly 2D/chaotic RL controllers to stable, realistic 3D locomotion across multiple morphologies.
- Significant advancement: single general controller architecture reduces need for manual controller design across character types.
- Strong experimental support: theoretically justified, substantial improvement over baselines (orders of magnitude), and clear ablation studies showing importance of components.
- Well-organized paper with clear methodology and effective supplementary video demonstration.
- Minor clarity issue: Section 4 does not clearly explain the role or implementation of recurrent units referenced in the abstract.
- Technical detail gap: the requirement for full model knowledge (Eq. 6) is not explicitly stated in the main text and should be emphasized.
- Imbalanced coverage: Sections 4 and 6 are overly detailed relative to other parts; neural network training details could be condensed.
- Pro-acceptance: significant technical advancement, generalizability across morphologies, strong validation, and clear impact for animation/robotics/biomechanics.
- Con-acceptance: unclear recurrent architecture role, insufficient emphasis on full model knowledge requirement, and disproportionate section detail.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, accept with minor revisions to clarify recurrent units, state model-knowledge requirement, and condense training details.
