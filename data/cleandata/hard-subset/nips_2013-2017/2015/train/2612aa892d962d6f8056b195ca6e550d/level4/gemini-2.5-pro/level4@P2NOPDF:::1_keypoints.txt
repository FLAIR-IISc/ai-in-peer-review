Proposes a novel strategy that embeds feedback gains directly within the network architecture to train robust control policies.

Method improves robustness to disturbances and regression errors.

Core technique is a soft coupling between trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a cost penalty for deviating from a reference trajectory.

Framework lets the policy learn simpler, more robust approximations of optimal trajectories than those from traditional MPC alone.

Optimization leverages sophisticated parallelization, presented as an implementation detail rather than a primary contribution.

Effectiveness demonstrated on a diverse set of robots performing various tasks.

Paper is exceptionally clear and represents a formidable technical achievement.

Successfully integrates concepts from machine learning, optimal control, and robotics.

Results are compelling, high-quality, and inspiring.
