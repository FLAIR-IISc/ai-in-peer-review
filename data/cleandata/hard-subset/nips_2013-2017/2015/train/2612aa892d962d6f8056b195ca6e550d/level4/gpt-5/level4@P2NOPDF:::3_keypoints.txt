- Paper proposes interleaved training where a neural network imitates Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with alternating optimization between CIO and NN.
- Key contributions: joint training scheme and use of input data augmentation plus hidden-layer noise to improve generalization.
- Experiments show realistic, stable control across diverse 3D character models.
- Important and challenging problem; overall idea is compelling despite seeming straightforward.
- Empirical evaluation is weak: training dataset is small (100–200 trials with short segments), likely causing overfitting in ablations.
- Limited baselines: only a brief MPC comparison is provided and lacks a citation; broader, stronger comparisons are needed.
- Clarity and reproducibility are mixed: mathematical exposition omits several details and relies on prior work.
- CIO step is only sketched in places (e.g., undefined notation around Eq. 6), making reconstruction difficult.
- Section 3.2 (optimal actions for noisy inputs) is confusing: notation degrades, time-dependence of variables is unclear.
- A public code release is strongly recommended to aid reproducibility.
- Runtime and scalability are not reported but are critical for real-time control; provide test-time timings and scaling with character complexity.
- Practical constraints (e.g., per-character CPU budgets in games) require discussion of control-loop efficiency.
- Terminology conflation: distinguish “data augmentation” (noisy input → recomputed targets) from “noise injection” (hidden-layer perturbations) to clarify ablation results.
- Algorithm 1 questions: clarify initialization of X in the first iteration (random θ vs CIO without regularizer) and why same hyperparameter η is used in both optimization steps.
- The asynchronous joint optimization used in experiments differs substantially from the alternating Algorithm 1 and should be foreshadowed and reconciled.
- Suggest splitting the “no noise” ablation into separate tests for no data augmentation and no hidden-layer noise to isolate contributions.
- Suggest studying annealing α to zero during CIO to determine if benefits come from curriculum effects or sustained closeness to NN representable trajectories.
- Notational and wording issues throughout (e.g., “unity” → “unite”, missing articles, misplaced symbols like argmin star) need correction.
- Acronym/order issues: define LQG before use and fix phrasing (“an LQG pass”).
- Several line-specific queries: clarify “biped location” meaning, target definition (position vs orientation), and whether f should be f^{des} at line 315.
- Clarify “root forces” included by soft enforcement of dynamics and their impact on physical plausibility.
- Justify claims such as “sX and aX are subsets of φX” and the reference to a “single pass over the data” vs which objective is reduced.
- Verify Table 1a caption (claims 4 layers but appears to be 3).
- Recommended improvements: (1) broader, better-cited empirical comparisons with larger/diverse datasets and stronger baselines, (2) clearer and complete notation—especially for CIO and noisy-input sections, (3) runtime and scaling measurements, and (4) public code release.
