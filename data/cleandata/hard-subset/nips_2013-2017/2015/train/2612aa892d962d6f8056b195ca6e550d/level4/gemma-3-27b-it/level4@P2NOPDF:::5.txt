This paper presents interesting results, but would benefit from significant restructuring. A key aspect of the proposed method – the requirement for full model knowledge (as detailed in Equation 6) – should be stated more prominently, ideally in the introduction. Currently, its importance isn't immediately apparent.

The paper’s sections feel imbalanced in terms of length and depth. Specifically, Sections 4 and 6 appear disproportionate to the rest of the work; a more even distribution of focus would improve readability and impact. 

Furthermore, Section 4 is notably lacking in discussion regarding the recurrent units mentioned in the abstract. The abstract introduces recurrence as a core element, yet this is not adequately explained or integrated into Section 4’s presentation. The connection between the abstract’s claims and the detailed method should be more clearly established.

Regarding a specific technical detail, line 405 refers to a “stochastic neural network.” However, the description does not convincingly demonstrate stochasticity within the network itself. While the training data is indeed stochastic, the network architecture and operation, as presented, appear deterministic. Clarification regarding the source and nature of stochasticity is needed.

The authors’ response to the rebuttal was helpful and appreciated. Overall, the paper demonstrates promising results, but its presentation needs improvement to effectively communicate the contributions and rationale behind the approach.
