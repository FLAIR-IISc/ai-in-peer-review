

This paper presents a compelling integration of trajectory optimization and policy learning for interactive control of complex characters. The core contribution lies in jointly learning trajectories and policies through an alternating optimization approach, which offers two key practical advantages: (i) leveraging well-established methods for each subproblem and (ii) achieving strong empirical performance. While I found the paper technically sound and well-structured, my limited familiarity with this specific domain prevents me from fully assessing its broader impact, though the work demonstrates clear technical merit without novel theoretical contributions.

The authors successfully combine exceptional technical sophistication across all levels—from conceptual framework to implementation—with impressive empirical results. The claimed benefits of (i) incorporating sensory noise during training and (ii) jointly learning trajectories alongside policies are clearly and convincingly demonstrated. The writing is exceptionally clear for a highly complex topic, striking the right balance of detail. Each section addresses a distinct aspect of the problem, and the cross-references between sections significantly enhance readability.

However, the motivation for the additional optimization step introduced in Section 7 requires further clarification. Given the prior presentation of the framework, I initially expected the policy to operate without needing this extra step. Is the necessity due to residual trajectory cost (or policy regression error) that accumulates when the policy’s actions are fed into the physical simulator? I assumed the forward dynamics evolution inherently enforces physical constraints, preventing implausibility, but I may be overlooking a key aspect. That said, I appreciate how re-optimizing at every timestep enables larger timesteps, which is a practical advantage.

Regarding line 153: "similar to recent approaches" would benefit from one or two specific references. The claim that keeping parameter updates small is sensible seems counterintuitive at first glance, though I understand its rationale in your block-alternating context. Clarifying this point would strengthen the argument.

Line 89 contains a minor typo: "to unify" should be "to unify" (likely a missing space).

The results are indeed impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme, coupled with its implementation, appears well-motivated and sensible. That said, as this research area falls outside my primary expertise, I remain cautious about fully evaluating the novelty and significance of the work. The paper makes a strong technical contribution, but its broader impact within the field would require deeper domain knowledge to assess confidently. The alternating optimization framework is well-justified by the results, and the practical advantages it delivers are clearly articulated.
