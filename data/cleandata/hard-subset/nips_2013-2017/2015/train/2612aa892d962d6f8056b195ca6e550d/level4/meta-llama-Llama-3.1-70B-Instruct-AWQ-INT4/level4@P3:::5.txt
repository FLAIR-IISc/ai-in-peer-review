**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks" presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The controller is a neural network that learns elaborate state-action mappings and implements memory states beyond the physical system state. The paper combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and training for unexpected changes in the task specification.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work in reinforcement learning and stochastic optimal control, but achieves its goal in continuous high-dimensional spaces involving complex dynamics. The method is also related to recent work in computer graphics and robotics, where physics-based controllers are used to generate realistic movements. However, the paper aims to unify these disparate approaches and provide a more general and automated method for designing controllers.

**Strengths**

* The paper presents a novel combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization to train neural network controllers.
* The method is able to generate stable and realistic behaviors for a range of complex characters and tasks.
* The paper provides a detailed description of the algorithm and its components, including the neural network architecture and the trajectory optimization method.
* The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the method in various scenarios, including locomotion of different creatures.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper assumes full model knowledge (Eq. 6), which may not be realistic in all scenarios.
* The section on neural network policy regression (Section 4) is disproportionately short and does not provide enough details about the recurrent units mentioned in the abstract.
* The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of how the stochastic neural network is trained and how the noise injection affects the learning process.
* The section on distributed training architecture (Section 6) is also disproportionately short and could be expanded to provide more details about the implementation.

**Originality and Significance**

* The paper presents a novel approach to training neural network controllers for complex characters and tasks.
