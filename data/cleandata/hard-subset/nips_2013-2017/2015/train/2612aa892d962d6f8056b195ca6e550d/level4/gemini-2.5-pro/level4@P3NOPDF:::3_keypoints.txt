- Proposes real-time control for 3D animated characters by training a neural network to approximate a Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) planner.  
- Key contribution: interleaved/alternating joint optimization where CIO planning and NN training inform each other.  
- Introduces strategic noise injection via input data augmentation and additive hidden-layer noise.  
- Demonstrates realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.  
- Joint optimization and systematic noise are significant, non-trivial advances beyond simple behavioral cloning.  
- Experimental weakness: very small training dataset (100–200 trials) may undermine ablation conclusions (e.g., "no noise" likely overfits due to data scarcity).  
- The "no joint" ablation may also perform better with more diverse/larger training trajectories.  
- Lacks systematic quantitative comparison to competing methods; only a brief qualitative MPC comparison without a reference.  
- Mostly well-written with generally well-motivated math, but several sections lack sufficient detail for replication.  
- Notation and clarity issues: Equation 6 (CIO step) not fully defined; Section 3.2 and time index t are confusing.  
- Reproducibility would benefit greatly from releasing source code.  
- No computational performance metrics reported (inference time per frame, CPU/GPU costs, scaling with model complexity).  
- Multiple minor typos and phrasing errors (e.g., "biped location" vs locomotion, "unity" vs unite/unify, misplaced star in argmin, LQG used before definition).  
- Methodological questions: are final orientation constraints supported and can a target angle be added?  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguities: are initial CIO trajectories computed without network regularization or using random initial network weights?  
- Hyperparameter concern: no justification for using the same eta for both optimization steps.  
- Clarify guarantee that sX and aX are subsets of φX.  
- Section 6’s asynchronous joint optimization differs from Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme; this discrepancy should be highlighted earlier.  
- Ablation "no noise" conflates data augmentation and hidden-layer noise—should be split to isolate effects.  
- Suggest renaming "noise injection" in data generation to "data augmentation" to avoid confusion with denoising techniques.  
- Clarify theta optimization description—reference to the objective and "single pass" is ambiguous.  
- Table 1a caption/architecture mismatch: caption says 4 layers but description implies 3 hidden layers; please clarify.  
- Suggest future experiment: anneal alpha during CIO to test whether network regularization acts as curriculum learning or remains beneficial.  
- Summary: novel and promising approach with important contributions, but needs stronger experiments, comparisons, clearer exposition, and runtime analysis to substantiate claims.
