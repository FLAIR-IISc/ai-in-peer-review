- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers using trajectory optimization-generated data to produce realistic, stable behaviors for diverse simulated characters (swimming, flying, walking).
- Advances prior work by scaling trajectory optimization + neural networks to high-dimensional 3D locomotion and a variety of morphologies without task-specific tuning.
- Key technical contributions include noise injection and optimization of feedback gains, improving robustness and stability.
- Demonstrates versatility by controlling multiple character types with a single network architecture and parameter set.
- Reports notable stability in learned controllers for challenging 3D locomotion tasks.
- Successfully integrates trajectory optimization’s behavior quality with neural networks’ real-time execution.
- Provides thorough implementation details and a distributed training architecture with hyperparameters.
- Includes ablation studies that validate the importance of components like noise injection.
- Limited novelty: needs clearer positioning relative to Levine & Koltun and Abbeel’s group, including comparisons on speed, generalization, cost, and robustness.
- Explanation of “soft(er) feedback gains” is unclear; paper should define the term and justify why modifying initial states is desirable.
- Notation in Section 3.2 (bars/squiggles) is visually confusing and should be revised for readability.
- The claim about “large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” needs more context and a clearer explanation of how the method mitigates this issue.
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous experiments using MuJoCo and well-defined costs, but some sections lack intuitive explanations.
- Originality derives from scaling and unifying existing techniques rather than novel components, yet the demonstrated versatility is significant.
- Results are significant for robotics, animation, and biomechanics due to automated generation of stable, realistic controllers.
- Arguments for acceptance: robust versatile approach, scales to 3D, detailed implementation, and supportive ablation studies.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear novelty positioning, sections needing clarification, and insufficient explanation of soft gains.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to clarify novelty, notation, and the soft-gains discussion.
