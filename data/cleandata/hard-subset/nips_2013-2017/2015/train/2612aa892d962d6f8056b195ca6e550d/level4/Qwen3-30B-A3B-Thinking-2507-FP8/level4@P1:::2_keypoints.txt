- Method: Train recurrent neural networks to approximate near-optimal feedback controllers using trajectory optimization (Contact‑Invariant Optimization) for diverse dynamical characters.
- Key innovation: A single neural network architecture controls diverse morphologies without task-specific features, state machines, or motion capture.
- Contribution: Demonstrates neural-network approximation of trajectory-optimized policies to enable interactive control without mocap or manual controller design.
- Contribution: Shows generalization across swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal characters in challenging 3D environments with one architecture.
- Contribution: Introduces practical training techniques—noise injection, trajectory optimization with feedback gains, and distributed cloud training.
- Results: Compelling experimental validation with stable 3D locomotion across multiple character types and supporting supplementary video.
- Strengths: Well-motivated, carefully executed approach with thorough experiments, clear methodological explanations, and reproducible implementation details.
- Weakness: Insufficient positioning relative to Levine & Abbeel [9,11]; needs explicit comparisons on learning speed, body-shape generalization, runtime cost, and robustness.
- Presentation issue: Explanation and notation around feedback gains (e.g., s(X)=s̄, bars/squiggles, “softer feedback gains”) are confusing and need clearer connection to LQG.
- Claim gap: Paper must better justify why this approach succeeds in 3D where prior trajectory-optimization + neural-network work was limited to 2D.
- Experimental suggestion: Expand comparative analysis (e.g., include MPC) and deepen discussion on why cyclical gaits vs MPC’s non-cyclical behaviors matter.
- Overall: Solid, significant work with strong technical execution and results, but requires clearer relation to prior work and presentation refinements to fully establish novelty.
