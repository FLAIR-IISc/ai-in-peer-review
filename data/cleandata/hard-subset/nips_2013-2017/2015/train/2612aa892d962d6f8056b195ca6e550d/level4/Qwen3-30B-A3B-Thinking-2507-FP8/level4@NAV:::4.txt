

This paper introduces a method that integrates trajectory optimization with policy learning for interactive control of complex characters. The core contribution lies in jointly learning trajectories and policies, supported by the empirical finding that decoupling the optimization into alternating subproblems i) leverages established methods and ii) proves effective in practice. While the paper is well-structured and comprehensible, my limited expertise in this domain prevents a definitive assessment of its broader impact, though it demonstrates clear technical execution without novel theoretical contributions.

The authors exhibit exceptional technical depth across conceptual design, implementation, and results. The advantages of i) incorporating sensory noise during training and ii) jointly learning trajectories and policies are convincingly demonstrated. The writing is clear and appropriately detailed for the problem's complexity, with each section addressing distinct aspects and providing useful cross-references.

The rationale for the additional optimization step in Section 7 requires clarification. Given prior announcements, I expected the policy to operate without supplementary optimization. Is this due to residual trajectory cost or policy regression error accumulating when policy actions are fed into the simulator? While physical dynamics should inherently prevent implausibility, I wonder if the forward evolution accounts for this. I acknowledge that per-timestep re-optimization enables larger timesteps, but the necessity of this step remains unclear.

Line 153: "similar to recent approaches" – please provide one or two representative references. The claim that small parameter updates are beneficial is surprising in general contexts, though I understood its relevance to your block-alternating framework.

Line 89: Typo – "to unify" (should be "to unify").

The results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme appears sound. However, as this research area is outside my primary expertise, I cannot confidently evaluate the novelty or significance of the contribution.
