- Paper proposes alternated training of a neural network and Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with noise injection (data augmentation + hidden-layer noise) to produce real-time 3D character control.  
- Joint training between CIO and NN plus noise injection are significant contributions that improve generalization beyond simply learning CIO outputs.  
- Experiments show the network produces realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.  
- Experimental dataset is small (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), likely causing overfitting in the "no noise" variant and limiting conclusions.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief MPC comparison (no citation) and no systematic comparisons to other direct or learning-based methods.  
- CIO step and some mathematical details are treated superficially (e.g., Eq. 6 notations undefined), making replication difficult.  
- Authors should provide code to aid reproducibility.  
- Section 3.2 (generating optimal actions for noisy inputs) is unclear: notational confusion and dropped time index t make the operations hard to follow.  
- Real-time performance metrics and scaling with model complexity are missing; time measurements and CPU cost analysis are important for practical use (e.g., games).  
- Terminology/wording issues: "biped location" likely should be "locomotion"; "unity" should be "unite/unify"; several grammar fixes needed.  
- Clarify target specification: is only x,y,z targeted and is target facing angle unconstrained; consider adding target angle.  
- Distinguish data augmentation from hidden-layer noise injection; calling re-computed-target augmentation “noise injection” is misleading.  
- Clarify initialization in Alg.1: are initial X’s computed with random θ or without regularization?  
- Justify using the same hyperparameter η in both steps of Alg.1.  
- Many minor notation and editorial errors (LQG used before definition, misplaced argmin star, missing \bar{s} in \tilde{C}, f vs f^{des}, Table 1a layer count, etc.) that need correction.  
- The asynchronous joint optimization described in Section 6 differs substantially from the alternating scheme in Alg.1 and should be highlighted earlier.  
- Suggest splitting the experimental "no noise" ablation into separate “no data augmentation” and “no hidden noise injection” variants.  
- Interesting research question: whether α can be annealed to zero during CIO (curriculum learning vs keeping CIO close to NN capability) — worth investigating.
