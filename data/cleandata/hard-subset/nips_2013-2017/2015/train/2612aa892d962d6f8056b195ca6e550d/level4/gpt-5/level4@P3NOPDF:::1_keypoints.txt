- Proposes embedding feedback gains into neural policies via a soft coupling (deviation penalty) with trajectory optimization to yield robust, implementable controllers.
- Soft coupling lets policies track optimized trajectories while retaining flexibility to deviate when that reduces cost or improves robustness.
- Resulting controllers approximate optimal trajectories but are often simpler and more robust than pure MPC-derived controllers.
- Implementation uses substantial parallelization and is demonstrated across diverse robot morphologies and tasks, showing strong empirical breadth and depth.
- Clear, well-motivated problem formulation and high-quality presentation of methodology, objectives, and experiments.
- Elegant technical contribution: structured policy architecture that amortizes control by combining trajectory optimization and learned feedback gains.
- Practical relevance to control, robotics, and ML communities, with attention to computational bottlenecks.
- Request to clarify how feedback gains are embedded—fixed (e.g., LQR) vs. learned jointly, and whether structure is PD/LQR plus learned residual.
- Ask for sensitivity/ablation study on the deviation penalty weight to show trade-offs between fidelity and robustness.
- Recommend comparisons: pure trajectory imitation baseline and a version without soft coupling to isolate the coupling’s contribution.
- Suggest providing formal or conceptual robustness analysis (e.g., bounds or Lyapunov-style arguments) to explain improved stability margins.
- Ask how policies generalize to unseen disturbances and dynamics mismatches—local vs. broader robustness.
- Request concrete computational cost/accounting (wall-clock, CPU/GPU hours, rollouts), scaling behavior with parallel resources, and main bottlenecks.
- Ask for sim-to-real transfer evidence or discussion (domain randomization, dynamics ID) and clarification of any hardware experiments and safety measures.
- Recommend acknowledging failure modes/limitations (discontinuous contacts, sparse rewards, long-horizon credit assignment) and improving reproducibility via code, hyperparameters, datasets, pseudocode, and architecture schematics.
- Overall: technically sound, clearly written, empirically convincing work with strong recommendation for acceptance.
