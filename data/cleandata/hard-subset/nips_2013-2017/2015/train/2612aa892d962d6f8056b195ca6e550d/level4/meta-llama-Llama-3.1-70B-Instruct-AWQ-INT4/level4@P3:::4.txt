This paper proposes a novel approach to interactive control of complex characters by combining trajectory optimization with policy learning. The main contribution is a joint learning framework that alternates between optimizing trajectories and policy parameters, leveraging existing methods to solve each subproblem. Although the paper's technical sophistication and impressive results are evident, its potential impact and novelty are less clear to a non-expert in this domain.

The authors demonstrate a high level of technical expertise, from conceptual development to implementation, and provide clear explanations of their approach. The paper is well-organized, with each section addressing a distinct aspect of the problem, and cross-references between sections facilitate understanding.

The benefits of using sensory noise during training and learning trajectories jointly with the policy are convincingly demonstrated. The paper's clarity and level of detail are appropriate, given the complexity of the problem and its implementation.

One area for improvement is the explanation of the additional optimization step in Section 7. The motivation for this step could be more clearly articulated, particularly given the previous sections' discussion of policy learning. It appears that the policy's action output may not be sufficient to ensure physically plausible behavior, and the extra optimization step is necessary to account for residual trajectory costs or policy regression errors. However, this could be clarified further.

Additionally, the statement on line 153, "similar to recent approaches," could be supported with one or two relevant references. The idea of keeping updates small in parameter space seems counterintuitive, but the authors' explanation of its utility in their specific scenario is convincing.

A minor typo was found on line 89, where "to unify" should be "to unite."

Overall, the paper's results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and implementation appear sensible. However, a more detailed evaluation of the paper's novelty and significance would require expertise in this specific research area.
