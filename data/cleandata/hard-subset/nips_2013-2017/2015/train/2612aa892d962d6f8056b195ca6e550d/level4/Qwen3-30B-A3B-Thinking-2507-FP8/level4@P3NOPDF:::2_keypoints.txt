- Main concern: technically sound but unclear novelty—needs clearer positioning relative to established trajectory-optimization + policy approximation methods.  
- Must explicitly compare to Abbeel/Levine-style trajectory optimization frameworks and recent related work to justify contribution.  
- Unclear primary contribution—authors should state whether gains are faster learning, broader morphological generalization, lower runtime cost, or improved robustness.  
- Claim that "same architecture works across body types and behaviors" is underspecified—quantify number of body types, behavioral diversity, and compare to prior work.  
- "Very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" is vague—define "large" and "ill-conditioned" with concrete metrics (e.g., condition numbers).  
- Notation is confusing (e.g., use of bar vs squiggle on s); standardize symbols and define them before use.  
- "Softer feedback gains according to parameter λ" lacks context—explain what "softer" means relative to a baseline and link to measurable outcomes (e.g., reduced jerk, stability).  
- Logical flow issue: LQG discussion should follow the authors’ method description, not precede it.  
- Runtime efficiency claim is unsupported—include inference speed benchmarks and concrete comparisons (e.g., × faster than prior methods).  
- Environmental robustness claim is unsupported—add experiments/metrics for perturbation tolerance and failure rates under noise.  
- Recommendation summary: explicitly compare to prior work on learning speed, generalization, runtime, robustness; clarify ambiguous terms with metrics; provide quantitative evidence for claims; restructure LQG placement; and frame the paper as addressing a specific gap rather than an incremental application.
