- Proposes a joint optimization that alternates between Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization and RNN policy training, coupled via a quadratic penalty to keep trajectories learnable.  
- Uses CIO-generated high-quality motion data to train real-time RNN feedback controllers for complex dynamical systems.  
- Introduces principled noise injection on sensory inputs and hidden activations to improve robustness and prevent overfitting.  
- Develops an efficient method to extract optimal feedback gains from CIO so the network learns optimal reactions to small perturbations.  
- Demonstrates on diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal), producing stable, realistic, interactive gaits without mocap or task-specific state machines.  
- High technical quality: clear derivations (notably feedback gains from a direct optimizer) and extensive experiments supporting claims.  
- Ablation studies convincingly show joint optimization and noise injection are necessary for stable policies.  
- Exceptionally clear presentation and organization, with precise math and effective visual results/video.  
- Original contribution: block-alternating optimization, noise+feedback gain integration, and applying the framework to challenging 3D tasks beyond prior 2D work.  
- High significance: generalizable framework bridging slow offline optimizers and fast reactive policies with strong implications for animation, gaming, and robotics.  
- Weakness: computationally intensive training requiring hours on distributed GPU clusters.  
- Weakness: physics enforced as soft penalties can produce small physical inaccuracies (e.g., residual root forces).  
- Weakness: comparison to MPC is largely qualitative; more quantitative metrics (goal achievement, energy, stability) would strengthen the argument.  
- Overall recommendation: clear accept—minor weaknesses are far outweighed by the paper’s contributions and impact.
