This work introduces a method for incorporating feedback gains directly into neural network policies to enhance their robustness against disturbances and regression errors. A soft coupling between trajectory and policy optimization is achieved through a cost penalty for deviation, which enables the policy to learn effective approximations of optimal trajectories. These learned trajectories are shown to be simpler and more robust than those generated by MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. The optimization process leverages sophisticated parallelization, which is presented as a practical necessity rather than a core contribution. Finally, the resulting trained feedback controllers are shown to successfully command a diverse range of robots.

This is a work of the highest caliber; the manuscript is exceptionally clear, demonstrates a masterful command of complex techniques, and presents truly impressive application results. This paper is an elegant synthesis of computer graphics, control theory, and neural networks, all aimed at generating controllers for a wide variety of body morphologies and tasks. It is genuinely inspiring research.
