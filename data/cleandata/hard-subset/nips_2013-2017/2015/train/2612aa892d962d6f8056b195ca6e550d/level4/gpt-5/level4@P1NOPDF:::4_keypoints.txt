- Proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning for interactive control of complex characters.  
- Core idea: jointly learn trajectories and policy, implemented by alternating subproblems to reuse established methods and obtain good empirical performance.  
- Reviewer could follow the paper but is not expert enough to judge its full impact.  
- Strong technical achievement and compelling results, but lacks novel theoretical insights.  
- Experimental support for benefits of injecting sensory noise during training and for jointly learning trajectories with the policy.  
- Paper is clearly written with appropriate detail and helpful cross-references.  
- Motivation for the additional optimization step in Section 7 is unclear; reviewer expected the learned policy to run without extra optimization.  
- Reviewer questions whether the extra optimization is needed to correct residual trajectory cost or policy regression error and notes re-optimizing every timestep allows larger timesteps.  
- Requests representative references for “similar to recent approaches” (l153) and justification or citations for the claim that small parameter-space updates are generally desirable.  
- Minor typo noted at l89: “to unify” (missing space).  
- Overall results and implementation are impressive, but reviewer remains uncertain about novelty and significance due to limited domain expertise.
