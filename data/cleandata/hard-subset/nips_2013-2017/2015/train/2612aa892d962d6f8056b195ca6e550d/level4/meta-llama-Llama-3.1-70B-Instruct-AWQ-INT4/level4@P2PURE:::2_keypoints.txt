- Demonstrates a small, fast neural-network control policy trained with trajectory optimization for interactive physics-based character control, applied across diverse cyclic behaviors and body types.
- The approach is largely an application of deep learning to established trajectory-optimization control techniques whose ability to produce realistic signals is already known.
- Prior work has used neural networks to approximate stepwise actions that trace optimization trajectories (e.g., [9]); the paper needs clearer positioning relative to those studies.
- The authors should explicitly compare to Abbeel & Levine’s recent line of work, addressing learning speed, support for diverse morphologies, runtime computational cost, and robustness to environmental variability.
- The claim that the process produces “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” needs clarification, and the rationale for not using LQG should be presented after the proposed strategy.
- Notation around lines 226–240 (s(X) = \bar s and cost defined via s(X) - \bar s) is confusing due to similar bars/squiggles and should be clarified.
- The statement that the method “produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” needs context: softer than what, how “softness” is defined, and why it is desirable.
- It is unclear why changing the initial state as part of the procedure is beneficial; this choice requires justification.
- Overall the work is sound but its novelty and contribution relative to recent related work are not clearly established.
