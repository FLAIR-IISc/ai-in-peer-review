The authors propose a method for embedding feedback gains into neural network policies, yielding robustness to disturbances and regression errors. They couple trajectory optimization and policy learning via a soft deviation penalty, enabling the policy to approximate optimal trajectories that, in practice, are simpler and more stable than those produced by MPC-style trajectory optimization alone. Although they employ substantial parallelization to optimize the tasks, this serves primarily as infrastructure rather than the core contribution. The trained feedback controllers are then shown to drive a wide array of dynamical systems and morphologies.

This paper is of outstanding quality, exceptionally clear, and technically impressive, with striking empirical results. It artfully integrates ideas from graphics, control, and neural networks to produce controllers for diverse bodies and tasks. Kudos—this is truly inspiring work.
