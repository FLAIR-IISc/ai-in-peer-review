“Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks” demonstrates that a small, fast neural network can represent a control policy for interactive control of a character in a physics simulator. The approach uses trajectory optimization during training to learn a neural network policy. The claimed contribution is that the same architecture works across a range of (more or less) cyclic behaviors and multiple body types.

General Comments
- My reading is that this paper applies deep learning to existing, relatively recent techniques in computer graphics. The ability of trajectory optimization to generate realistic control signals has been established by prior work, some of which is cited here. Likewise, using neural networks to approximate the step-by-step policy actions needed to reproduce trajectories found by trajectory optimization has been explored previously (e.g., [9]). The authors should therefore do more to position their contribution relative to these approaches.
- The closest related line of work, in my view, is by Pieter Abbeel and Sergey Levine. How does the proposed approach compare? Is it intended to learn faster? To accommodate a wider variety of body morphologies? To require less computation at runtime? To be more robust to environmental variability?

Specific Comments
- What exactly is meant by “we found the resulting process can produce very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains”? Please define “ill-conditioned” in this context and indicate how large, in practice.
- Consider moving the discussion of why LQG was not used to after you present your strategy.
- The notation around lines 226–240 is confusing. You introduce s(X) = s̄, and then define a cost in terms of s(X) − s̄. The overbars and tildes/squiggles are visually similar; please simplify or clarify the symbols to avoid ambiguity.
- What do you mean by “our approach produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ”? Softer than what baseline? What does “soft” mean operationally for gains? Why is changing the initial state as part of your procedure desirable?
- Overall, this is sound work, but the contribution relative to other recent related work is not sufficiently clear.
