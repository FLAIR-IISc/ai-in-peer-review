This paper presents a novel and highly effective method for training recurrent neural network policies to serve as real-time feedback controllers for complex dynamical systems. The core idea is to leverage the power of offline trajectory optimization to generate high-quality training data for a neural network, enabling it to produce similar behaviors online. The authors propose an iterative, alternating optimization scheme where a set of optimal trajectories and the parameters of the neural network policy are jointly refined. Key technical contributions include a principled method for injecting noise during training to improve robustness, and a novel technique for extracting optimal feedback gains directly from the trajectory optimizer. This allows the network to learn not just a state-action mapping, but also how to react optimally to small perturbations. The framework is demonstrated on an impressive and diverse range of 3D characters—including swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal morphologies—all controlled using the same general architecture.

This is an outstanding paper that represents a significant contribution to the fields of control, robotics, and computer animation. It combines sophisticated techniques from optimization and machine learning into a cohesive and powerful framework, delivering state-of-the-art results. The work is technically deep, clearly presented, and empirically validated with compelling demonstrations.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Quality and Technical Sophistication:** The paper is technically superb. The proposed framework masterfully integrates several advanced components: Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) for generating physically realistic behaviors, a novel perturbation-based method for extracting optimal feedback gains from a direct optimizer, and a stochastic training regime for the neural network policy. The joint optimization algorithm, which alternates between refining trajectories and regressing the policy, is an elegant solution to a very difficult constrained optimization problem. The empirical results strongly support the claims, showing that the full method is orders of magnitude better than naive training on a static dataset.

2.  **Originality and Novelty:** While the idea of using trajectory optimization to train a policy is not entirely new, this work pushes the boundary significantly. The primary novelty lies in scaling these ideas to complex, 3D, contact-rich locomotion tasks and, more importantly, in the method for creating a robust policy. The technique for computing optimal feedback gains (Section 3.2) and using them to generate target actions for noisy sensory inputs is a crucial and clever contribution. This allows the network to learn a locally linear optimal controller around the reference trajectories, which is key to achieving the stability seen in the results.

3.  **Significance and Impact:** The results are truly impressive. The ability to automatically synthesize stable, natural-looking, and interactive controllers for such a wide variety of character morphologies using a single, unified pipeline is a landmark achievement. This work provides a powerful alternative to traditional hand-engineered controllers or purely model-free reinforcement learning approaches, which often struggle to produce behaviors of this quality and realism. The methodology has high potential for impact in computer graphics (for character animation), robotics (for controller design), and even computational neuroscience.

4.  **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. Despite the complexity of the underlying system, the authors present their method with remarkable clarity. The overview in Section 2 effectively lays out the problem formulation, and the subsequent sections systematically detail each component of the algorithm. The ablation study in Section 8.1 clearly demonstrates the importance of the key technical contributions (joint optimization and noise injection).

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

While this is a very strong paper, a few points could be further clarified or strengthened:

1.  **Comparison to Other Methods:** The ablation study is insightful, but the paper would be even stronger with a comparison to contemporary policy search or reinforcement learning algorithms (e.g., policy gradient methods). The introduction mentions that direct RL methods can produce "somewhat chaotic behaviors," and a quantitative or even qualitative comparison to a state-of-the-art baseline would help substantiate this claim and better position the work within the broader literature on motor control.

2.  **Policy Execution as Optimization:** The policy execution step described in Section 7 involves solving a single-timestep optimization problem to find the next state, rather than simply performing a forward pass through the dynamics model. This is an elegant approach that likely contributes to the system's stability by enforcing physical constraints at runtime. However, it would be beneficial to discuss the computational cost of this step and clarify whether it poses any limitations for real-time performance, especially for more complex characters.

3.  **Scalability of Trajectory Optimization:** The heavy lifting in this framework is performed by the CIO trajectory optimizer. While the distributed architecture is a practical solution, a brief discussion on the fundamental scalability of the optimization—for instance, how its difficulty scales with the time horizon, character complexity, or task constraints—would provide valuable context for researchers looking to apply this method.

**Summary of Arguments:**

*   **Arguments for Acceptance:**
    *   Presents a powerful, general, and automated framework for synthesizing robust controllers for a diverse range of complex 3D characters.
    *   The technical contributions, particularly the method for extracting and utilizing optimal feedback gains for robust policy learning, are novel and significant.
    *   The empirical results are state-of-the-art and visually stunning, demonstrating a major advance in automated motor control.
    *   The paper is exceptionally clear, well-executed, and likely to have a substantial impact on multiple fields.

*   **Arguments Against Acceptance:**
    *   The experimental evaluation could be further strengthened with comparisons to alternative state-of-the-art control methods from the reinforcement learning literature.

Overall, the strengths of this paper far outweigh the minor weaknesses. It is a fantastic piece of work that I expect to be highly influential. I recommend acceptance without hesitation.
