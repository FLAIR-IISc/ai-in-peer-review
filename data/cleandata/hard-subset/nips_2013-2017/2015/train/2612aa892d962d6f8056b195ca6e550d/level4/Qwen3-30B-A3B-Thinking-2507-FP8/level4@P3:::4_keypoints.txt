- Core contribution: A distributed stochastic optimization that alternates trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) and neural policy regression, with noise injection and learned feedback gains to train RNN controllers.  
- Novelty: Extends prior 2D trajectory-optimization methods to robust 3D locomotion across diverse morphologies without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.  
- Empirical result: Demonstrates stable, realistic swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal locomotion in MuJoCo with interactive, near-optimal feedback controllers.  
- Ablation findings: Joint trajectory-policy optimization and sensory/noise injection are critical for robustness; full method outperforms baselines by ~100x on test performance.  
- Theoretical/algorithmic soundness: Trajectory optimization and feedback gain computation are well-formulated and empirically efficient.  
- Reproducibility and implementation: Strong implementation details and use of MuJoCo, supplemented by clear mathematical formulations and videos.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized, precise, and accessible while maintaining technical depth.  
- Generality: A unified architecture yields emergent gaits across morphologies, advancing toward generalized character control.  
- Significance: Enables real-time, interactive control without hand-crafted controllers and has clear potential for robotics and biomechanics, though practical robotics claims are speculative.  
- Weakness — theory context: Needs clearer contextualization of theoretical contribution relative to prior NIPS work and LQG-based approaches.  
- Weakness — feedback-gain benefit: Lacks quantitative comparison showing how the feedback gain computation improves conditioning or efficiency over alternatives.  
- Weakness — robotics relevance: Discussion of sensor noise, model mismatch, and real-robot applicability is underdeveloped.  
- Weakness — runtime optimization clarity: The rationale for the runtime physical optimizer (to enforce constraints and prevent rollout error accumulation) is ambiguous and should be emphasized earlier.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept — minor revisions (clarify runtime step, strengthen robotics/theory discussion) would address the remaining concerns.
