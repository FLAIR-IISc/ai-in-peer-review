

The paper presents an interesting approach to neural network optimization but suffers from significant structural and clarity issues that undermine its contribution. The most pressing concern is the lack of explicit acknowledgment that full model knowledge is required (Eq. 6), which should be stated upfront to set clear expectations. Without this, readers may misunderstand the scope of the method’s applicability.  

Section 4 exhibits a critical misalignment with the paper’s abstract and broader claims. The abstract emphasizes recurrent units as central to the proposed framework, yet Section 4 (which details the core algorithm) omits any discussion of these units. This creates a disconnect: the method’s novelty hinges on recurrent dynamics, but the primary technical section fails to engage with them. The disproportionate focus on non-recurrent aspects in Section 4, contrasted with the brief treatment of recurrent elements in Section 6, suggests poor structural planning. Section 6 should instead elaborate on how recurrent units integrate into the proposed architecture, rather than merely listing experimental results.  

A technical clarification is needed at line 405, where the paper describes the network as "stochastic." The training data is indeed stochastic (e.g., via data augmentation or noise injection), but the network architecture itself appears deterministic. Stochasticity in neural networks typically arises from mechanisms like dropout, random sampling during inference, or probabilistic layers—not from data alone. The authors must explicitly state the source of stochasticity (e.g., "stochasticity is introduced via layer-wise sampling during training" or "the model uses a probabilistic decoder") to avoid misleading readers.  

The rebuttal addressed some concerns but did not resolve the structural imbalance between Sections 4 and 6. While the authors clarified the role of recurrent units, they did not revise Section 4 to reflect this integration. The current draft still presents recurrent units as an afterthought rather than a foundational component. Additionally, the claim about stochasticity remains unsubstantiated; the rebuttal did not specify whether the network employs stochastic operations (e.g., variational inference, random weights) or if "stochastic" refers only to data.  

The paper’s core idea—leveraging full model knowledge for optimization—shows promise, but its execution lacks rigor. The experimental section (Section 6) is overly dense and fails to contextualize results relative to the proposed method. For instance, comparisons to baselines should explicitly address how recurrent units contribute to performance gains, not just report metrics. The absence of ablation studies isolating the impact of recurrence further weakens the claims.  

Minor issues compound these problems:  
- Terminology inconsistencies (e.g., "stochastic NN" vs. "deterministic architecture" in line 405).  
- Overuse of passive voice ("it is observed that..."), reducing clarity.  
- Figures lack sufficient detail (e.g., axis labels in Figure 3 are unreadable).  

The authors’ rebuttal acknowledged the structural issues but did not propose concrete revisions to Sections 4 and 6. A revised version should:  
1. State the requirement for full model knowledge (Eq. 6) in the introduction.  
2. Reorganize Section 4 to center on recurrent units, with subsections detailing their role in the optimization framework.  
3. Replace line 405’s ambiguous phrasing with a precise technical description of stochasticity.  
4. Add ablation studies to isolate the contribution of recurrence.  

While the results are compelling, the current presentation obscures the paper’s value. The structural flaws prevent readers from assessing the method’s validity, and the unresolved technical ambiguity (stochasticity) risks misinterpretation. The authors have a strong foundation but must prioritize clarity and logical flow over incremental results.  

In summary, the paper’s technical merits are promising but currently overshadowed by disorganization and unclear exposition. With targeted restructuring—particularly aligning Sections 4 and 6 with the abstract’s recurrent focus and resolving the stochasticity confusion—it could make a meaningful contribution. As written, however, it fails to communicate its innovation effectively. I recommend rejection unless the authors address these structural and technical gaps in a major revision.
