The authors propose a novel strategy for embedding feedback gains in neural network policies, enabling them to be more robust to disturbances and regression errors. By softly coupling trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for deviation, the policy can learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories. Notably, these approximations are often simpler and more robust than those produced by Model Predictive Control (MPC)-based trajectory optimization alone. The authors utilize sophisticated parallelization techniques to optimize tasks, although this aspect is secondary to the primary contribution of the paper. The proposed method is successfully applied to control a diverse set of robots using the trained feedback controllers.

This paper is of the highest quality, showcasing exceptional clarity, technical mastery, and visually stunning results in its applications. The authors should be commended for their outstanding work, which seamlessly integrates concepts from graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers for a wide range of body morphologies and tasks. The presented research is truly inspiring, demonstrating the potential of this approach to advance the field of robotics and control.
