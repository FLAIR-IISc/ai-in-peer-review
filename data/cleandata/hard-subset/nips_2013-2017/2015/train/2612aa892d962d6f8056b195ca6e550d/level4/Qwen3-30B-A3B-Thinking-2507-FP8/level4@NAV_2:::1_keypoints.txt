- Introduces a unified framework for training neural-network controllers achieving stable, interactive 3D locomotion across diverse morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal).  
- Extends Contact-Invariant Optimization by embedding optimal feedback gains into a neural policy via interleaved trajectory optimization and stochastic regression.  
- Uses noise injection during training to prevent overfitting and improve robustness.  
- Derives feedback gains from trajectory optimization rather than relying on LQG, improving effectiveness.  
- Trains a single policy architecture that handles heterogeneous tasks without task-specific features or motion capture.  
- Jointly optimizes trajectories and policy using a relaxed penalty, enabling better solutions than static neural training.  
- Controllers outperform static neural network training by roughly two orders of magnitude and produce stable cyclic gaits without hand-crafted state machines.  
- Rigorous technical execution with comprehensive ablations validating key components (noise injection, joint optimization).  
- Significant theoretical contribution in feedback gain derivation (Sec. 3.2).  
- Clear presentation, precise definitions, well-documented algorithm, and effective visualizations and supplemental video.  
- Originality: enables real-time control for complex 3D dynamics without domain-specific engineering, contrasting with state machines and MPC limitations.  
- Significance: unifies disparate locomotion control approaches and demonstrates scalability via distributed training.  
- Limitation: focused on locomotion tasks; extension to manipulation or other behaviors is unexplored.  
- Practical drawback: high computational cost (2.5 hours on 30 nodes) may limit accessibility despite cloud scaling.
