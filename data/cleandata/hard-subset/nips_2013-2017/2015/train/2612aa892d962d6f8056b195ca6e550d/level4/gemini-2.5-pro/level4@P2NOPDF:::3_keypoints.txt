1. Proposes NN trained offline to mimic a Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) planner for real-time 3D character control.  
2. Main contribution: joint, alternating optimization coupling CIO and NN so each informs the other's objective.  
3. Introduces two noise schemes: input-perturbation data augmentation and additive hidden-layer noise for robustness/generalization.  
4. Experiments show the network yields realistic, stable control for diverse character models.  
5. Approach compelling for real-time control of arbitrary characters; joint training and noise are key enablers.  
6. Experiments convincingly demonstrate necessity of joint training and noise injection for strong generalization.  
7. Training set is small (100–200 trials × 5 branches), limiting data diversity and possibly causing overfitting.  
8. The “no noise” ablation’s overfitting may stem from limited data; “no joint” could improve with more varied trajectories.  
9. Comparative evaluation is limited: only a brief MPC comparison is provided and lacks a proper citation.  
10. Mathematical formulation is generally clear but some components (notably the CIO) are described at a high level.  
11. Notation in Equation 6 is incomplete/undefined, hindering reproducibility.  
12. Releasing source code would greatly aid replication and community use.  
13. Section 3.2 is hard to follow: confusing notation and omission of the time index t obscure time dependence.  
14. Paper lacks computational-performance analysis (inference times, scaling with model complexity) important for real-time use.  
15. Minor typos: “biped location” → “biped locomotion”; “unity these disparate approaches” → “unite/unify.”  
16. Clarify control target: is it only (x,y,z) position with no final orientation constraint, and how to add target orientation?  
17. Term “noise injection” is imprecise for input perturbation that recomputes targets—this is better described as data augmentation.  
18. Ambiguity in Algorithm 1: are initial trajectories computed using random initial theta or without network regularization?  
19. Question justification for using the same hyperparameter eta for both CIO and network-training steps.  
20. Several grammatical errors and missing words throughout the paper that need correction.  
21. Acronym LQG is used before being defined.  
22. Line 231: argmin formatting appears incorrect; \bar{s} may be missing from \tilde{C}.  
23. Phrase “the minimizer of a quadratic around optimal trajectory” seems to omit a word and needs rewording.  
24. Clarify why sX and aX are guaranteed to be subsets of \phiX.  
25. “Single pass over the data to reduce the objective in (4)” should clarify it refers to optimizing only theta.  
26. Section 6’s asynchronous joint optimization differs substantially from Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme—this discrepancy should be highlighted earlier.  
27. Line 315: likely typo—f should be f^{des}.  
28. Sentence about dynamics constraints being “enforced softly” and potentially including root forces is unclear and needs elaboration.  
29. The “no noise” experiment should be split into two ablations: no data augmentation vs no hidden-layer noise.  
30. Suggest exploring annealing the CIO regularization alpha to zero to test whether joint training provides continuous benefit or acts as curriculum learning.  
31. Table 1a caption/description mismatch: stated 4 layers versus 3 hidden layers plus output—clarify network architecture.
