- Paper proposes a small, fast neural-network control policy trained with trajectory optimization and claims the same architecture works across many cyclic behaviors and body types.  
- The approach appears to apply deep learning to established trajectory-optimization and policy-approximation techniques; clearer positioning against prior work is needed.  
- The authors should explicitly compare to recent Abbeel & Levine lines of work and state advantages (e.g., faster learning, support for varied bodies, lower runtime cost, greater robustness).  
- The claim that the process "produces very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" needs a clearer explanation of the phenomenon and its implications.  
- The rationale for not using LQG should be presented after introducing the proposed strategy to give readers better context.  
- Notation in lines 226–240 (e.g., s(X)=\bar s and cost in terms of s(X)-\bar s) is confusing; revise to avoid similar bars/tildes causing ambiguity.  
- Clarify what is meant by "softer feedback gains according to parameter λ," how this compares to existing approaches, and why it is desirable.  
- The procedure’s step of changing the initial state requires further justification and explanation.  
- While the work seems sound, the novel contribution relative to recent related work is not well established.
