- Proposes a novel approach to train neural networks as feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped).
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate stable, realistic behaviors.
- Introduces noise injection during training to improve robustness and reduce overfitting.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations, helpful figures, and supporting equations.
- Provides a detailed experimental setup and results demonstrating the method’s effectiveness.
- Unifies disparate control and robotics approaches and applies across different body morphologies—a significant contribution.
- Shows neural networks as feedback controllers are promising and practically useful.
- Thoroughly compares to MPC and naive NN training, reporting superior robustness and performance.
- Scores highly on quality, clarity, originality, and significance per conference criteria.
- Main strengths: novelty, combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization, comprehensive comparisons, clarity.
- Main weaknesses: assumes substantial background in control theory and neural networks; some equations/figures may be hard for non-experts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong results, thorough comparisons, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility issues for readers without strong background; some difficult figures/equations.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant paper; reviewer rating 9/10 with high confidence.
