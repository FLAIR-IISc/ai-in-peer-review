- Presents a method to train recurrent neural network feedback controllers for complex characters (swimming, flying, bipedal/quadrupedal locomotion).
- Core idea: jointly optimize trajectories and a neural network policy using trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data.
- Introduces an alternating optimization scheme to make joint trajectory-policy learning tractable.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across diverse, challenging 3D control tasks.
- Effective engineering contribution that combines existing techniques rather than introducing new theoretical concepts.
- Results support benefits of sensory noise injection during training and joint trajectory-policy learning.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.
- Clarification requested on motivation and necessity of the online trajectory optimization (Section 7) and how simulation constraints and residual costs drive its use.
- Suggest adding one or two citations for the “similar to recent approaches…” statement on line 153.
- Minor typo on line 89: “to unify” should likely be “to unite”.
- Reviewer notes limited domain expertise, which reduces confidence in assessing novelty and impact relative to the field.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear presentation, sound technical approach, and reproducibility (supplementary video).
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, need for clarifications, and reviewer’s domain expertise gap.
- Overall recommendation: accept, provided the authors address the minor clarifications suggested.
