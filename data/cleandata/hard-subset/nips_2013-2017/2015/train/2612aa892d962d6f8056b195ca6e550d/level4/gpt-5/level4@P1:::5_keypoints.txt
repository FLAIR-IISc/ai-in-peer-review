- Paper summary: proposes alternating trajectory optimization with supervised learning to train RNNs as near‑optimal feedback controllers and shows real‑time control across varied character morphologies; results are promising.
- State modeling assumptions up front: method requires full knowledge of dynamics and contact model (Eq. 6) with constraints enforced via a physics engine and this strong model‑based assumption should be declared early.
- Rebalance Sections 4 vs 6: Section 6 (distributed training) is overly detailed while Section 4 (neural network policy regression), a central contribution, is too brief and should be expanded.
- Clarify recurrence: the paper emphasizes recurrent units but does not describe the recurrent architecture, how the recurrent state r is updated/trained, or how recurrence interfaces with velocity/force outputs.
- Clarify “stochastic” terminology (Line 405): training uses sensor and hidden‑unit noise, but the policy appears deterministic at test time—specify execution‑time stochasticity or rephrase to avoid confusion.
- Rebuttal note: reviewer’s overall positive assessment stands, but they reiterate the need to state model assumptions early, rebalance exposition, and fully describe the recurrent component.
