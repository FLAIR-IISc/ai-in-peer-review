- Proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning for interactive control of complex characters.
- Core idea: jointly learn trajectories and policy, but decouple into alternating subproblems (optimize trajectories, then policy) to reuse existing methods.
- Empirical finding: decoupling into alternating subproblems is effective in practice.
- Demonstrates high technical sophistication in concepts and implementation, producing impressive results.
- Experiments convincingly show benefits of training with sensory noise and learning trajectories jointly with the policy.
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and provides helpful cross-references and implementation detail.
- Question about Section 7: unclear motivation for the additional re-optimization step—why the learned policy alone isn’t sufficient.
- Asks whether residual trajectory cost or policy regression error necessitates re-optimization and whether dynamics should prevent physically implausible results.
- Notes that re-optimizing at each timestep plausibly enables the use of larger timesteps.
- Requests one or two references for the claim “similar to recent approaches” (Line 153).
- Minor typo: Line 89 “to unify.”
- Reviewer is not an expert in the area and therefore hesitant to judge the work’s novelty and overall impact.
