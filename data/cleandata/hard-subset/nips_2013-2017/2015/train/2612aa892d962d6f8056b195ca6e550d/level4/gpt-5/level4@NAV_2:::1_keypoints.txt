- Proposes embedding feedback gains into neural-network policies to improve robustness to disturbances and regression errors.
- Couples trajectory optimization and policy learning via a soft deviation penalty so policies approximate optimal but simpler, more stable trajectories than MPC alone.
- Uses substantial parallelization for task optimization, primarily as infrastructure rather than the core contribution.
- Trained feedback controllers successfully drive a wide array of dynamical systems and morphologies.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, technically impressive, and of outstanding quality.
- Presents striking empirical results demonstrating the approach’s effectiveness.
- Elegantly integrates ideas from graphics, control, and neural networks to produce versatile controllers.
