"Interactive control of diverse complex characters with neural networks" presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as feedback controllers for a range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, and legged locomotion with varying body morphologies. The core idea is to leverage trajectory optimization to generate training data for a neural network, enabling real-time, interactive control without relying on motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines. The authors demonstrate that a single network architecture can be applied successfully to diverse characters and behaviors.

**General Comments**

This paper explores an application of deep learning to control problems within computer graphics and robotics. The use of trajectory optimization to generate training data for neural network controllers builds upon established techniques. While previous work has demonstrated the effectiveness of trajectory optimization for creating realistic control signals and the potential of neural networks to approximate control policies (e.g., [9]), this paper’s contribution lies in demonstrating the scalability and generality of this approach across a broad spectrum of dynamic systems and body types. However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough positioning of its work relative to recent advancements, particularly those from groups like Pieter Abbeel and Sergey Levine. A clearer articulation of the advantages of this approach – such as speed, support for varied morphologies, runtime computation, or robustness to environmental changes – would strengthen the paper’s impact.

**Specific Comments**

The phrase "we found the resulting process can produce very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" (around line 226) lacks sufficient detail. Elaborating on the implications of these large and ill-conditioned gains would be helpful. 

Consider restructuring the presentation to introduce the limitations of LQG *after* outlining your perturbation method, making the rationale for your approach more apparent.

The notation around lines 226-240 is somewhat confusing. The use of both bars and squiggles (e.g.,  s(X) = \bar s, and a cost term involving s(X) - \bar s) can be visually distracting. Consider employing distinct notation to enhance clarity.

The explanation of “softer feedback gains” (around line 241) is unclear. What does it mean for gains to be "soft" compared to other approaches, and why is modifying the initial state within the optimization procedure beneficial?

**Strengths**

*   **Generality:** The ability to control diverse characters and tasks with a single network architecture is a significant achievement.
*   **Scalability:** The method demonstrates the potential to address more challenging control problems in high-dimensional spaces.
*   **Real-time Control:** The learned neural network policy enables interactive control, a crucial requirement for many applications.
*   **Robustness:** The inclusion of noise injection during training appears to improve the robustness of the learned policies.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The overall structure of the paper is logical and the methodology is explained in detail.

**Weaknesses**

*   **Limited Novelty:** The paper builds upon existing techniques and the incremental contribution could be more clearly defined.
*   **Comparative Analysis:** A more comprehensive comparison with related work, especially recent reinforcement learning approaches, is needed.
*   **Clarity of Explanation:** Certain technical details (e.g., “soft feedback gains”) require further clarification.
*   **Lack of Ablation Detail:** While ablations are presented, more discussion surrounding the magnitude of the performance differences and the reasoning behind those differences is warranted.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a well-executed application of deep learning to a challenging control problem.
*   The demonstrated generality of the approach is promising.
*   The results are supported by thorough experimentation and analysis.
*   The work addresses a significant problem in robotics, animation, and biomechanics.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The paper’s novelty is incremental, and the contribution could be more clearly articulated.
*   The lack of a detailed comparative analysis with related work weakens the paper’s impact.
*   Certain technical explanations require further clarification.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that presents a practical and effective method for learning neural network controllers. While the novelty may be incremental, the demonstrated generality and scalability of the approach are noteworthy. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly by providing a more thorough comparative analysis and clarifying key technical details, would significantly strengthen the paper. I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
