- Novel method: interleaves trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) with neural network policy learning to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse complex characters.  
- Key innovations: supervised learning interleaved with trajectory optimization, noise injection during training, and training for unexpected task changes.  
- Advances over prior work: achieves stable 3D locomotion (vs. prior 2D) and uses a unified architecture across multiple morphologies without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Technical quality: thorough experimental validation across swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal tasks.  
- Ablations: show joint optimization and noise injection are necessary; full method outperforms naive training on static trajectories by ~100x.  
- Method design: trajectory optimization and network training explicitly address closed-loop learning challenges (precision of real-valued outputs vs. i.i.d. samples).  
- Clarity: writing and organization are precise; methodological sections (2–4) are particularly clear and supplementary video effectively demonstrates results.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of prior CIO-based approaches to 3D and a novel combination of trajectory optimization with policy learning techniques.  
- Significance: enables interactive, real-time control of diverse characters without mocap or task-specific engineering and unifies control across morphologies.  
- Broader impact: potential relevance to robotics and RL, but the paper could better articulate advances to general RL/control theory beyond animation.  
- Main weakness: runtime optimization step (Section 7) is insufficiently motivated—unclear why policy outputs alone don't suffice and how residual trajectory cost relates to re-optimization.  
- Comparison gap: paper mentions similarity to feature-based control but does not clearly differentiate or explain improvements over those approaches.  
- Minor issues: missing specific references for proximal regularization at line 153 and a typo at line 89; overall accept recommendation.
