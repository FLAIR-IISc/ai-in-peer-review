- Proposes joint, block-alternating optimization that iteratively refines trajectory optimizations (CIO) and a recurrent policy via supervised learning.
- Leverages offline Contact-Invariant Optimization to generate high-quality motion data used to train neural-network policies.
- Injects sensory noise during training and uses trajectory optimizer to compute feedback gains so the policy can correct small perturbations.
- Demonstrated on diverse, challenging 3D locomotion tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal and quadrupedal walking) with emergent plausible gaits.
- Strength: strong empirical results producing stable, visually plausible locomotion across varied morphologies without task-specific heuristics or mocap.
- Strength: technically sound integration of trajectory optimization and deep learning with informative ablation studies supporting key components.
- Strength: paper is well-written and organized, clearly explaining trajectory optimization, feedback gain calculation, and network regression.
- Weakness: unclear motivation for the per-timestep optimization during policy execution — why direct action rollout is insufficient needs clearer explanation.
- Weakness: novelty is mainly empirical and systems-level (effective synthesis/scaling of existing techniques) rather than a fundamentally new theoretical contribution.
- Minor: add citations for “quadratic proximal regularization” remark and fix typo “unity” → “unify.”
- Summary: overall a strong, well-engineered paper with thorough validation; main limitations are clarity on real-time optimization need and primarily empirical novelty.
