- Presents alternating trajectory optimization and supervised policy regression using CIO, with noise injection, distributed asynchronous training, and T=1 real-time execution.  
- Builds on prior learning-from-trajectory-optimization and CIO work, and efficiently reuses TO computations to compute feedback gains.  
- Tackles challenging 3D control across multiple morphologies with a single architecture and pipeline.  
- Perturbation-based gain computation is elegant and computationally efficient.  
- Noise injected at sensory and network levels improves robustness; ablations support this.  
- Practical distributed asynchronous implementation with proximal regularization is clearly described.  
- Real-time execution without storing trajectories via a short-horizon (T=1) consistency solve is neat.  
- Ablations and MPC comparison provide insight into behavior regularization and cyclicity.  
- Central assumption of full model knowledge and accurate dynamics/contacts must be stated prominently as it limits applicability.  
- Section balance issue: policy regression (Section 4) underdeveloped—needs details on recurrent units, dimensionality, initial state, and whether recurrence is essential.  
- The term “stochastic neural network” is misleading; clarify that training data is noisy but policy appears deterministic at test time.  
- Execution raises realism questions because contact forces are actions and constraints are enforced softly; discuss hard constraints or torque-based alternatives.  
- Quantitative evaluation is limited—add comparisons to strong model-free baselines (e.g., TRPO) and longer-horizon MPC.  
- Need robustness/generalization tests under perturbations, model mismatch, and sensitivity to noise scales (σε, σγ).  
- Notational and editorial issues (typos, inconsistent terms); consider moving system/cluster details to an appendix.  
- Technically sound overall; gain computation is a notable contribution, but report on alternating-scheme convergence and gain regularization (choice of λ) is needed.  
- T=1 execution is reasonable but requires discussion of stability guarantees and constraint satisfaction.  
- Clarity: elevate key assumptions, expand policy architecture description, clarify stochastic vs deterministic execution, and add a concise TO→gains→targets→NN schematic.  
- Originality: combines existing ideas but the pipeline and gain computation applied to diverse 3D tasks are novel and valuable.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept; revise structure, clarify assumptions and policy architecture/stochasticity, and strengthen quantitative evaluation.
