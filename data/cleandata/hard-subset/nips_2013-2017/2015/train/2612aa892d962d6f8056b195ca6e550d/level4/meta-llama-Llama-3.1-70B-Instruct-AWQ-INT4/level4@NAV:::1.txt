This paper presents a novel approach to incorporating feedback gains into neural network policies, enhancing their resilience to disturbances and regression errors. By softly coupling trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for deviation, the policy learns to approximate optimal trajectories in a simpler and more robust manner than traditional MPC-based trajectory optimization. Although the authors leverage sophisticated parallelization techniques for task optimization, this aspect is secondary to the primary focus of the paper. The proposed method is successfully applied to control a diverse range of robots using trained feedback controllers.

Overall, this paper is exemplary in its clarity, technical sophistication, and impressive results. The integration of graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers for various body morphologies and tasks is a remarkable achievement. The quality of the work is outstanding, and the applications presented are truly impressive, making this a compelling and inspiring contribution to the field.
