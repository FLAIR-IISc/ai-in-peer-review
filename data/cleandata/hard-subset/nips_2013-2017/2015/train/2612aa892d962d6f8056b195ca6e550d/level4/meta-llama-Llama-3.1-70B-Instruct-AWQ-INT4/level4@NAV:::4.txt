This paper presents a novel approach to interactive control of complex characters by integrating trajectory optimization with policy learning. The core contribution lies in the joint learning of trajectories and policies, with the added insight that decoupling these optimization problems into alternating sub-problems allows for the reuse of established methods and yields satisfactory results in practice. Although I was able to follow the paper, my limited familiarity with this domain prevents me from fully assessing its potential impact, which appears to be a technically impressive achievement lacking novel theoretical breakthroughs.

The authors demonstrate exceptional technical expertise, from conceptualization to implementation, accompanied by impressive results. The benefits of incorporating sensory noise during training and learning trajectories jointly with policies are convincingly demonstrated.

The paper is well-written, with an appropriate level of detail considering the complexity of the problem and its implementation. Each section focuses on a distinct aspect, and the cross-references between sections are helpful.

However, the motivations for the additional optimization step in Section 7 could be better clarified. Given the preceding sections, I expected the policy to function without the need for extra optimization. Is this due to the residual trajectory cost or policy regression error that accumulates errors when feeding the policy's actions into the physical simulator? Although the forward evolution of dynamics accounts for physical constraints, I may be missing a crucial point. Nevertheless, I appreciate that re-optimizing at every time step enables the use of larger time steps.

A minor suggestion is to provide one or two references for the statement "similar to recent approaches" (line 153). While it seems counterintuitive to limit updates in parameter space, I eventually understood its utility in the context of block-alternating between sub-problems.

Additionally, there is a typo on line 89 ("to unify"). The proposed optimization scheme and implementation appear sensible, and the results are impressive. However, as this research area is not my primary focus, I find it challenging to confidently evaluate the novelty and significance of this work.
