- Summary: Trains RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers by alternating Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory re-optimization with supervised learning; policy outputs desired velocities, contact forces, and recurrent updates, with a T=1 constrained optimizer for state advancement.
- Key ingredients: Gaussian sensory noise during training, perturbation-based extraction of local feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer, and distributed asynchronous alternating optimization with proximal regularization.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on trajectory-optimization-plus-policy-learning (e.g., Levine & Koltun, Mordatch & Todorov) and claims practical advances in breadth of 3D morphologies and a robust training recipe.

Strengths:
- Single NN architecture and training scheme yields stable, realistic 3D behaviors across multiple morphologies without hand-crafted state machines or motion capture.
- Well-engineered pipeline: CIO data generation, noise injection, and practical gain extraction by reusing optimizer linearization/Hessian.
- Real-time execution with a compact policy; no trajectory storage at runtime.
- Ablations (no joint optimization; no noise) support design choices.
- Clear description of distributed training architecture and practical timings.

Weaknesses / Questions:
- Positioning vs related work is unclear; needs direct quantitative comparison to Levine & Koltun and related Abbeel/Levine line on training cost, runtime, robustness, morphologies, and behavior quality.
- Results are primarily qualitative; lacks quantitative metrics for stability, robustness, tracking, failure modes, energy/torque cost, and long-rollout performance.
- Action/execution ambiguity: clarify significance of root forces at execution, physical plausibility of T=1 soft-dynamics optimization, and differences from explicit torque policies with integrators.
- Feedback gains ambiguous: clarify whether issues are high magnitudes, poor conditioning, or numerical/closed-loop instability; provide norms/condition numbers and examples.
- Role of λ unclear: define how λ softens gains, its effect on magnitude/conditioning/closed-loop response, and provide sensitivity analysis.
- Notation clarity needed (Sec. 3.2): avoid confusing symbols for s(X)=s̄ vs noisy s+ε and improve typography/consistency.
- Organization suggestion: present perturbation-based gain computation before LQG and explain why LQG produced ill-conditioned gains.
- Claims of 3D advance need clearer comparison to prior 3D control work and explanation of what enables 3D stability here.
- Generalization limits unspecified: quantify allowable magnitude/rate of test-time goal changes before failure.
- References incomplete; provide full bibliographic details.

Evaluation on NIPS criteria:
- Quality: Technically sound and well-implemented; practical gain derivation is a contribution, but experiments lack quantitative rigor.
- Clarity: Generally readable but notation and terminology (e.g., “soft gains,” conditioning) require clarification and reordering for better narrative flow.
- Originality: Moderate — overall recipe is known; novelty lies in perturbation-based gain computation within direct trajectory optimization and broad 3D results.
- Significance: Promising practical significance for animation and model-based control; scientific impact would increase with stronger quantitative evidence and clearer differentiation from prior work.

Pros for acceptance:
- Unified controller across diverse 3D characters without hand-crafted structure.
- Practical, scalable training pipeline with supporting ablations.
- Useful technique for extracting stabilizing feedback gains from direct trajectory optimization.

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited quantitative evaluation and incomplete comparisons to closely related methods.
- Methodological ambiguities around gain conditioning, “soft gains,” and role of root forces at execution.
- Clarity and positioning relative to prior work need improvement.

Specific requests for revision:
- Add quantitative comparisons to Levine & Koltun, Mordatch & Todorov on shared tasks (training time, data, performance metrics).
- Report robustness metrics: disturbance rejection, fall rate, tracking error, energy/torque usage, and long-horizon stability.
- Quantify and clarify feedback gain issues and the effect of λ; include norms/condition numbers and sensitivity plots.
- Improve notation in Sec. 3.2 and reorder presentation to introduce perturbation-based gains before LQG discussion.
- Discuss action representation and runtime T=1 optimization implications for physical plausibility and differences from torque-integrator rollouts.
- Provide statistics on generalization to branched goal changes (magnitude/rate thresholds).
- Complete and standardize reference bibliographic details.
