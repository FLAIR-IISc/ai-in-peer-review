This paper presents a promising approach with encouraging results; however, its current structure and several points of ambiguity significantly detract from its clarity and impact. The work would be much stronger with a substantial restructuring and clarification of key details.

My primary concerns are as follows:

1.  **Upfront Statement of Assumptions:** The proposed method relies on having complete knowledge of the model's dynamics, as detailed in Equation 6. This is a critical assumption that significantly frames the scope and applicability of the work. For clarity, this requirement should be stated explicitly at the beginning of the paper, likely in the introduction, rather than being introduced later. This would allow readers to immediately understand the context and potential limitations of the approach.

2.  **Structural Imbalance and Discrepancies:** The paper's organization could be improved.
    *   There is a noticeable disproportion in the length and level of detail between Section 4 and Section 6. This imbalance disrupts the flow of the paper and suggests that some sections may be underdeveloped while others could be more concise. The authors should consider re-evaluating the content of these sections to create a more balanced and logical narrative.
    *   There is a significant disconnect between the contributions highlighted in the abstract and the technical details provided in the main text. Specifically, the abstract emphasizes the use of recurrent units, but these are not mentioned or explained in Section 4, where their discussion would be most relevant. This omission makes it difficult for the reader to connect the paper's high-level claims with the methodological details.

3.  **Clarification of Terminology:** On line 405, the model is referred to as a "stochastic neural network." This terminology is confusing. Based on the description, the network architecture itself appears to be deterministic. The stochasticity seems to arise from the training data, not from any inherent randomness in the network's forward pass (e.g., stochastic units or dropout applied at test time). The authors should clarify what aspect of the network itself is stochastic. If the stochasticity is indeed confined to the data distribution, the terminology should be revised to be more precise.

In summary, while the core ideas and results are valuable, the paper requires a revision to improve its structure, clarity, and consistency. By addressing the points above—particularly by stating key assumptions upfront and aligning the abstract with the body of the paper—the authors can present their contributions much more effectively.
