

The paper presents promising results but requires significant restructuring for clarity and technical precision. First, the requirement for full model knowledge (Eq. 6) must be explicitly stated upfront, as it is a critical precondition for the method’s applicability. Currently, this is only introduced later, creating confusion for readers assessing the framework’s scope.  

Second, Sections 4 and 6 exhibit a disproportionate length and focus. Section 4, which details the core methodology, is overly verbose relative to its content, while Section 6 (the experimental evaluation) is disproportionately concise given the complexity of the results. This imbalance undermines the paper’s narrative flow and fails to adequately justify the claims made in Section 4.  

Third, Section 4 omits a key reference to the recurrent units explicitly mentioned in the abstract. The abstract positions these units as central to the approach, yet Section 4 describes the architecture without connecting it to this foundational element. This disconnect weakens the paper’s coherence and misaligns the technical narrative with the high-level claims.  

Finally, Line 405 describes the model as "stochastic," but the network architecture itself appears deterministic. The training data may involve stochastic elements, but the model structure does not inherently incorporate randomness (e.g., no dropout, stochastic layers, or probabilistic inference). This mischaracterization requires clarification to avoid misleading readers about the method’s technical nature.  

In summary, the paper’s core contribution is valuable, but structural and technical inconsistencies must be resolved. The authors should: (1) state the full model knowledge requirement immediately, (2) rebalance Sections 4 and 6 to reflect their respective technical weight, (3) explicitly link Section 4’s architecture to the recurrent units referenced in the abstract, and (4) correct the mislabeling of the network as "stochastic." Addressing these points will substantially strengthen the paper’s rigor and readability.
