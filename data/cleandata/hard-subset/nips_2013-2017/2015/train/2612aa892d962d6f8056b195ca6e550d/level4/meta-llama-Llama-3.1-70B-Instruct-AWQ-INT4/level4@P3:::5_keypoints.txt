- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse complex characters (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped) using supervised learning + trajectory optimization, noise injection, and training for unexpected task changes.  
- Builds on reinforcement learning and stochastic optimal control and aims to unify prior graphics and robotics approaches for high-dimensional continuous dynamics.  
- Novel combination of supervised learning and trajectory optimization to train neural-network controllers.  
- Method produces stable, realistic behaviors across a variety of complex characters and tasks.  
- Paper provides a detailed description of the algorithm, network architecture, and trajectory optimization components.  
- Experiments convincingly demonstrate effectiveness in multiple locomotion and control scenarios.  
- Assumes full model knowledge (Eq. 6), which may be unrealistic for many real-world settings.  
- Section on neural network policy regression is too short and lacks detail about the recurrent units referenced in the abstract.  
- Training of the stochastic neural network and the effects of noise injection are not clearly explained.  
- Distributed training architecture section is underdeveloped and needs more implementation details.  
- Approach is original and combines elements not commonly seen together, with potential impact in graphics, robotics, and RL.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear figures/tables, but could clarify the stochastic network training.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective method with strong experimental results and high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unrealistic model assumptions and insufficient detail in key methodological sections.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
