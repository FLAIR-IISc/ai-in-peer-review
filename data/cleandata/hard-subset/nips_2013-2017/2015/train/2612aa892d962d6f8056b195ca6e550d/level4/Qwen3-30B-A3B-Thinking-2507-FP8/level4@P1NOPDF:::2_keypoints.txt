- Paper proposes a compact neural network to represent control policies learned via trajectory optimization for diverse physics-based character animation.  
- Overall novelty is unclear because similar ideas (trajectory optimization + neural policy approximation) are established; authors must better articulate new contributions.  
- The manuscript lacks a clear comparison to closely related Abbeel & Levine work on metrics like learning speed, body-type generalization, runtime cost, and robustness.  
- The claim about "very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" needs quantitative elaboration (e.g., specific numerical indicators such as near-zero eigenvalues).  
- Discussion of why LQG was not used is poorly placed and should follow the proposed method presentation for better logical flow.  
- Notation is confusing (e.g., s(X) = s̄ and cost s(X) − s̄); visual similarity of symbols should be fixed for clarity.  
- The term "soft" feedback gains and the role of parameter λ are ambiguous—define "soft" (reduced magnitude, smoother responses?) and explain benefits.  
- The purpose and advantage of modifying the initial state during training are not explained and require justification.  
- Recommendation: provide a sharper, explicit comparison to prior methods and clarify technical terms, notation, and paper organization.  
- Conclusion: method is plausible and technically sound but currently fails to convincingly establish novelty or significance within prior policy-learning literature.
