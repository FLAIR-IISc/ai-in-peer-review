- Core idea: Softly couple trajectory optimization with neural-network policy learning using a cost penalty and noise to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for varied dynamical systems, leveraging parallelization for scalability.  
- Context: Builds on control, reinforcement learning, and neural-network literature, addressing data/stability challenges by combining Contact-Invariant Optimization with neural generalization.  
- Technical soundness: Interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, noise injection, and using optimizer-derived feedback gains are well-motivated and technically justified.  
- Empirical scope: Demonstrates impressive, stable, and realistic locomotion/control across diverse 3D robotic morphologies and tasks.  
- Originality: Novel method for obtaining feedback gains from the optimizer and successfully extending trajectory-optimization+NN approaches to challenging 3D problems.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and supported by figures and a video.  
- Significance: Meaningful advance for robotics, animation, and biomechanics, enabling adaptive, real-time controllers for complex bodies.  
- Strength: Strong results and broad applicability across multiple dynamical systems.  
- Weakness: High computational cost—training requires substantial GPU time despite parallelization.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and its impact on performance.  
- Weakness: Lacks systematic quantitative comparison to direct methods to substantiate claims about chaotic behaviors.  
- Acceptance case: Technically sound, novel, clearly presented work with impressive 3D results and broad impact—strongly recommended for acceptance.  
- Rejection case: Computational demands and limited comparative analysis could hinder adoption and weaken claims.  
- Overall assessment: Outstanding paper that pushes boundaries in control and ML; recommended for NIPS acceptance.
