- Presents a novel real-time 3D character control method: an NN trained offline to replicate Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs with alternating, interdependent CIO/NN training.  
- Key innovations: joint (alternating) training of CIO and NN, and noise injection via data augmentation and additive hidden-layer noise.  
- Experiments show the NN produces realistic, stable control policies across diverse characters, improving generalization over the baseline idea.  
- Core idea is conceptually simple, but the joint training and noise strategies materially boost performance.  
- Primary weakness: inadequate comparative evaluation — only a brief, unreferenced MPC comparison; broader baselines/datasets needed.  
- Training data size (100–200 trials with 5 branches each) may be insufficient; "no noise" and possibly "no joint" variants risk overfitting and need more diverse trajectories.  
- Mathematical exposition is mostly clear but requires additional references and clarification for CIO (undefined notations, e.g., Eq. 6).  
- Section 3.2 and general notation are confusing, especially time index "t" and which quantities are time-dependent.  
- Runtime/time measurements for real-time control and scaling with model complexity are missing but critical for practical use (e.g., games).  
- Code release would greatly aid reproducibility.  
- Algorithmic ambiguities: confirm Algorithm 1 initialization (random θ or X from unregularized CIO) and justify using same hyperparameter η in both steps.  
- Section 6’s asynchronous scheme differs substantially from Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme and should be reconciled or noted earlier.  
- “Noise injection” label is misleading: the data-augmentation step is standard augmentation (recompute targets), not denoising.  
- Many minor writing/notation issues and typos needing fixes (word replacements, misplaced stars, missing definitions like LQG, incorrect variable names such as f vs. f^{des}).  
- Specific clarifications requested: whether targets include orientation, how dynamics/root forces are enforced, why sX and aX subsets of φX are guaranteed, and whether α can be annealed to zero.  
- Suggest splitting the current “no noise” ablation into separate “no data augmentation” and “no hidden-noise injection” experiments.  
- Table/figure inconsistencies (e.g., Table 1a caption saying 4 layers but describing 3) and line-numbered errors should be corrected.  
- Overall assessment: promising and compelling approach but requires stronger comparisons, clearer exposition, experimental details (timing/scaling), and minor corrections for acceptance.
