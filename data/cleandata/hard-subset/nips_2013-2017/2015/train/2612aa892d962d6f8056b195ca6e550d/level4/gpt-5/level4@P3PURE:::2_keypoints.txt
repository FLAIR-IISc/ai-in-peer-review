- Paper proposes a small, fast neural network policy for interactive physics-based character control trained via trajectory optimization.
- Claimed contribution: same network architecture works across varied cyclic behaviors and multiple body types.
- Reviewer notes this mainly applies deep learning to established trajectory-optimization techniques already shown to produce realistic control.
- Prior work has used neural nets to imitate trajectory-optimized policies (e.g., [9]); the paper must better position itself relative to these.
- The paper should explicitly compare to recent Abbeel & Levine work: clarify whether the method is faster, supports more body shapes, reduces runtime computation, or is more robust.
- Clarify the statement about “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains”: quantify “large” and specify circumstances causing ill-conditioning.
- Explain what is meant by “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” and softer than which baseline.
- Suggest moving the explanation of why LQG was not used to after the overall strategy description for better flow.
- Notation around lines 226–240 is confusing (s(X)=s̄ and cost in terms of s(X)−s̄); disambiguate overbars/diacritics.
- Justify why changing the initial state in the training procedure is beneficial.
- Overall assessment: work appears sound but the contribution relative to recent related work remains insufficiently clear.
