- Proposed method: NN trained offline to mimic Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs for real-time 3D character control, with alternating joint optimization between CIO and NN.
- Key novelty: alternating (joint) training between CIO and NN rather than independent stages.
- Key novelty: noise injection via data augmentation (input perturbations) and additive noise in NN hidden layers to improve generalization.
- Empirical claim: network produces realistic, stable control policies across diverse character morphologies.
- Strength: addresses a difficult real-time control problem and combines trajectory optimization with learning in a novel way.
- Strength: joint optimization and hidden-layer noise injection shown experimentally to improve generalization.
- Weakness: comparative evaluation is limited — only a brief, unreferenced MPC comparison is included.
- Weakness: training dataset is small (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), likely causing overfitting in “no noise” variant.
- Suggestion: enlarge dataset or diversify training trajectories to better evaluate “no joint” and other ablations.
- Reproducibility: some mathematical details are superficial (particularly CIO / Eq. 6); additional references and code release would help replication.
- Clarity issue: Section 3.2 and some notations (time index t dropped/confusing) are unclear about generating optimal actions for noisy inputs.
- Practical missing evaluation: run-time measurements and scaling with model complexity are not reported and could be a bottleneck in real-time applications.
- Terminology nitpick: calling the data-perturbation step “noise injection” is misleading — it is closer to data augmentation than denoising autoencoder training.
- Algorithm questions: unclear whether initial X’s in Alg.1 use random θ or are computed without regularization; justification for using same η in both Alg.1 steps is missing.
- Optimization scheme confusion: asynchronous joint optimization in Section 6 differs from alternating Alg.1 and should be highlighted earlier.
- Experimental suggestion: split the “no noise” ablation into “no data augmentation” and “no hidden noise injection.”
- Open question: would decreasing α to zero during CIO-only optimization work (curriculum vs. continual regularization effect)?
- Numerous minor editorial/math issues and typos (e.g., "biped location" vs locomotion, unite/unify, undefined LQG, misplaced stars, missing symbols, caption mismatch in Table 1a).
- Clarification request: is the target only 3D position (x,y,z) with no target facing angle, and can a target angle be added easily?
- Overall assessment: promising and visually compelling approach, but limited comparative evaluation, unclear mathematical details, and missing timing/code hinder full assessment of practical applicability.
