- Proposes training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D characters using a joint framework alternating trajectory optimization and policy learning.  
- Extends prior trajectory optimization (e.g., CIO) and neural control work to far more challenging three-dimensional locomotion tasks.  
- Key technical innovations: interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting sensory noise, training for unexpected task changes, and extracting optimal feedback gains via trajectory optimization.  
- Strong experimental validation across multiple morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) producing stable, realistic behaviors without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Implementation is technically sophisticated with a distributed training architecture that scales to complex problems.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, balancing technical detail and accessibility.  
- Demonstrates clear benefits of sensory noise injection and joint trajectory-policy learning with compelling comparative results over naive approaches.  
- Minor clarity concern: Section 7 insufficiently explains why additional trajectory optimization during policy execution is necessary and why the learned policy cannot alone prevent error accumulation.  
- Methodological claim about proximal-style regularization would benefit from citing specific related approaches that use similar small-parameter-update strategies.  
- Suggests stronger, explicit comparisons to related character-control work (e.g., [13, 21]) to better position advances over domain-specific controllers.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid, impressive execution, and a meaningful contribution to neural-network-based control for complex 3D locomotion despite being a somewhat specialized topic.
