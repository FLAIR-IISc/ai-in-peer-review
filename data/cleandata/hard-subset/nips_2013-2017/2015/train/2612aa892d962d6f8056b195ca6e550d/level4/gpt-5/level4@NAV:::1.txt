The authors open by introducing a method for embedding feedback gains into neural network policies, improving robustness to disturbances and regression error. Trajectory optimization and policy optimization are softly linked via a deviation penalty in the cost, allowing the policy to learn faithful approximations of optimal trajectories that are, in practice, simpler and more robust than trajectories produced by MPC-based optimization alone. The tasks are optimized using advanced parallelization, though this serves more as a means than a primary contribution. Finally, a broad spectrum of robots is controlled using the trained feedback controllers.

This paper is of exceptional quality, extraordinarily clear, a tour de force of technique, and it presents striking results in its applications. Bravo. It is a beautiful piece of work that fuses graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers for a diverse range of body morphologies and tasks. It is inspiring.
