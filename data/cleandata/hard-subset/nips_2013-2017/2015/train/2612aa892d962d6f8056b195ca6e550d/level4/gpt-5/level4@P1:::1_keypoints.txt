- Summary: Proposes training neural-network policies with explicit local feedback gains softly coupled to trajectory optimization via a penalty, yielding simpler, more robust behaviors than MPC and real-time control across diverse characters; heavy distributed parallelization used for data generation/scale.

- Strength: Elegant soft coupling lets policies inherit trajectory structure and stability while remaining robust to perturbations.

- Strength: Explicit feedback gains in the learned policy address compounding errors and improve stability and realism.

- Strength: Produces high-quality, visually compelling locomotion across varied morphologies without task-specific state machines or motion capture.

- Strength: Clear, polished presentation with an insightful observation that the joint objective regularizes toward predictable, cyclical behaviors.

- Strength: Strong systems execution — a thoughtfully constructed distributed asynchronous training pipeline that is effective in practice.

- Weakness: Extensive parallelization is primarily an engineering/implementation detail, not a conceptual advance.

- Evaluation — Quality: Excellent; technically sound formulation leveraging trajectory optimization to supervise policy learning with embedded feedback.

- Evaluation — Clarity: Excellent; explanations of coupling and feedback roles are easy to follow.

- Evaluation — Originality: High; the soft coupling plus explicit feedback integration is a distinctive synthesis.

- Evaluation — Significance: High; impactful for ML/control and computer graphics by enabling robust, real-time control across diverse systems.

- Arguments for acceptance: Conceptually clean, well-justified method with strong empirical results and thorough evaluation.

- Arguments against acceptance: None substantial beyond the non-fundamental emphasis on engineering scale.

- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — outstanding paper, technically strong, clearly written, impressive results.
