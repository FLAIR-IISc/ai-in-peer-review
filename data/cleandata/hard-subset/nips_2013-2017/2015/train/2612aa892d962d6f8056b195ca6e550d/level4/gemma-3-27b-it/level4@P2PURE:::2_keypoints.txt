- Paper shows a compact neural network can control diverse, cyclic characters in real-time using trajectory optimization during training.
- Reviewer views the work as an application of existing trajectory-optimization + neural-network ideas rather than a clearly novel method.
- Prior work has already used trajectory optimization to produce realistic control signals; authors must more clearly delineate novelty relative to these.
- The authors should explicitly compare to relevant recent work (notably Pieter Abbeel & Sergey Levine) and state whether benefits are faster learning, broader morphology support, lower runtime cost, or greater robustness.
- Clarify the statement about producing “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” — what causes this and what are the implications?
- Recommend postponing the LQG discussion until after the proposed method is fully presented for clearer exposition.
- Notation around lines 226–240 (s(X) = \bar s and the cost in terms of s(X) - \bar s) is confusing due to bar/squiggle similarity and needs cleanup.
- Explain what is meant by “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ”: softer relative to which baseline, what defines “soft” vs “not soft,” and the reasoning for modifying the initial state.
- Overall the work is sound but its novelty and contribution relative to recent related research require clearer clarification.
