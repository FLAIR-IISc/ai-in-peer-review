

The paper introduces a unified framework for training neural network controllers that achieve stable, interactive locomotion across diverse 3D character morphologies (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without task-specific features or motion capture. It extends Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) [Mordatch et al., NIPS 2012] by embedding optimal feedback gains into a neural policy via interleaved trajectory optimization and stochastic regression. This differs from prior work [Levine & Koltun, ICML 2014; Mordatch & Todorov, RSS 2012] by enabling 3D locomotion (previously limited to 2D) and resolving key challenges: (1) using noise injection during training to prevent overfitting and amplify robustness [Wager et al., NIPS 2013], (2) deriving feedback gains from trajectory optimization (avoiding LQG inefficiencies), and (3) training a single policy architecture for heterogeneous tasks. The method’s core innovation lies in jointly optimizing trajectories and policy via a relaxed penalty (Eq. 4), yielding controllers that outperform static neural network training by two orders of magnitude (Fig. 2) and produce stable cyclic gaits without hand-crafted state machines.

**Strengths:**  
- **Quality:** Rigorous technical execution with comprehensive ablations (noise injection, joint optimization) and strong experimental validation across 5 character types in MuJoCo. The feedback gain derivation (Sec. 3.2) is a significant theoretical contribution.  
- **Clarity:** Well-structured, with precise definitions (Sec. 2), clear algorithmic steps (Algorithm 1), and effective visualizations (Fig. 1, supplementary video). The connection between sensory inputs and velocity commands is logically justified.  
- **Originality:** Solves a key gap in prior work by enabling *real-time* control for *3D* complex dynamics without domain-specific engineering, contrasting with state machines [Rebula et al., ICRA 2011] or MPC’s non-cyclical outputs (Fig. 3).  
- **Significance:** Unifies disparate control approaches (bipedal, quadrupedal, swimming) under one architecture, with clear applications in robotics (Section 9) and biomechanics. The scalability via distributed training (Sec. 6) demonstrates practical viability.  

**Weaknesses:**  
- Limited to locomotion tasks; extension to non-locomotive behaviors (e.g., manipulation) is unexplored.  
- Computational cost (2.5 hours on 30 nodes) may hinder accessibility despite cloud-based scaling.  

**Pro/Con Summary:**  
*Pro:* Groundbreaking 3D locomotion control, unified framework for diverse morphologies, robustness via noise injection, superior to MPC in stability, strong theoretical foundation.  
*Con:* Narrow task scope (locomotion), high compute requirements.  

This work significantly advances neural control for physically plausible character animation and robotics, meeting all NIPS quality criteria with exceptional technical depth. The results are both novel and broadly impactful.
