The paper proposes a strategy for embedding feedback gains in neural-network policies, yielding robustness to disturbances and regression errors. Trajectory optimization and policy optimization are softly coupled via a cost penalty for deviations, allowing the policy to learn reasonable approximations to optimal trajectories that are in fact simpler and more robust than trajectories produced by MPC-based optimization alone. The authors employ sophisticated parallelization to optimize the tasks efficiently; while not the central contribution, it serves as an effective means to an end. Finally, a diverse set of robots are controlled using the trained feedback controllers.

This paper is of the highest quality, exceptionally clear, and represents a tour de force of technique, with beautiful application results. Bravo. It is a compelling body of work that marries graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers for a wide range of body morphologies and tasks. It is inspiring.
