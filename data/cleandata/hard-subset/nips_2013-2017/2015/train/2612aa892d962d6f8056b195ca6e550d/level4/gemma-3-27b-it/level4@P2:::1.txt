This paper presents a novel method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a variety of dynamical systems, including swimming, flying, and walking robots with different body morphologies. The core idea is to softly couple trajectory optimization with neural network policy learning, using a cost penalty to encourage the network to approximate optimal trajectories while simultaneously learning robustness to disturbances and regression errors. The authors leverage parallelization to handle the computational demands of this approach, ultimately demonstrating impressive results across a diverse set of robotic tasks.

This work builds upon a rich history of research in control, reinforcement learning, and neural networks. Traditional control methods often require manual design and are limited in their ability to generalize to new scenarios. Reinforcement learning, while promising, often struggles in high-dimensional continuous spaces. Recent advancements in deep learning have shown potential, but data acquisition and stability remain significant challenges. This paper directly addresses these challenges by combining the strengths of trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization [12]) with the generalization capabilities of neural networks. Related work [9, 11] also explores this combination, but this paper distinguishes itself by successfully tackling substantially harder problems – achieving stable and realistic locomotion in three-dimensional space, a feat previously limited to two-dimensional characters. The authors also draw connections to earlier neural network control approaches [3, 6, 10, 14] but suggest their method yields more purposeful and realistic behaviors.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a well-defined methodology with a clear theoretical basis. The interleaving of supervised learning with trajectory optimization, the injection of noise, and the use of the optimizer for feedback gains are all thoughtfully justified and contribute to the overall success of the approach.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors clearly articulate the motivation, technical details, and results. The use of figures and the encouragement to view the accompanying video further enhance understanding.
*   **Originality:** The combination of trajectory optimization and neural network learning in this manner, particularly the method for obtaining feedback gains directly from the optimizer, represents a novel contribution. The successful application to a diverse range of complex 3D locomotion tasks further demonstrates the originality of the work.
*   **Significance:** This work represents a significant advance in the field of control and animation. The ability to generate stable, realistic, and adaptable controllers for a variety of body morphologies has the potential to impact robotics, animation, and biomechanics. The demonstration of control in complex 3D environments is particularly noteworthy. The method’s capacity to achieve interactive, real-time control is a major step forward.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Computational Cost:** While the authors address the computational demands through parallelization, the training process still requires significant resources (hours of GPU time). This may limit accessibility for researchers with limited computational infrastructure.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions several algorithmic constants, but a more detailed analysis of their sensitivity and impact on performance would be beneficial.
*   **Comparison to Direct Methods:** While the authors express an impression that direct methods yield somewhat chaotic behaviors, a more systematic quantitative comparison would strengthen the argument.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel approach to learning control policies.
*   The results are impressive, demonstrating successful control of diverse and complex characters in 3D environments.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the methodology and results.
*   The work has the potential to significantly impact multiple fields, including robotics, animation, and biomechanics.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The computational cost of the method may be a barrier to adoption for some researchers.
*   A more thorough comparative analysis against other control methods would further strengthen the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a truly outstanding paper. The authors have successfully addressed a challenging problem with a creative and effective solution. The combination of theoretical rigor, clear writing, and impressive results makes this a highly significant contribution to the field. I wholeheartedly recommend this paper for acceptance to NIPS. This work embodies the spirit of NIPS – pushing the boundaries of machine learning and applying it to solve complex and impactful problems.
