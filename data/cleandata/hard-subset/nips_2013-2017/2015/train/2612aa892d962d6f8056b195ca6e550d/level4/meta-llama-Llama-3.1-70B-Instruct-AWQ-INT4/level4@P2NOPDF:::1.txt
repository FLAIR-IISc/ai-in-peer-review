The author proposes a strategy for embedding feedback gains in neural network policies, rendering them robust to disturbances and regression errors. By softly coupling trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for deviation, the policy learns reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories that are simpler and more robust than those produced by MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. Although sophisticated parallelization is utilized for task optimization, this aspect is secondary to the paper's primary focus. The trained feedback controllers are successfully applied to a diverse set of robots.

This paper is of exceptional quality, boasting clarity, technical mastery, and aesthetically pleasing results in its applications. The work seamlessly integrates concepts from graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers for various body morphologies and tasks, making it a truly inspiring contribution.
