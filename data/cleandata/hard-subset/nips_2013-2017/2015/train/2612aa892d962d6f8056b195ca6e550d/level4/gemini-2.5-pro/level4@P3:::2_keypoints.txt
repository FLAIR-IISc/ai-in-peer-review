- Summary: Trains an RNN controller by iteratively optimizing trajectories with Contact-Invariant Optimization and regressing a noisy-robust policy, extracting feedback gains, and demonstrating generality across diverse 3D characters.

- Strength: Impressive and general results — a single method produces stable, realistic interactive locomotion for bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, and fliers.

- Strength: Effective joint optimization framework — iterative optimize-and-regress training clearly outperforms training on a static dataset (strong ablation evidence).

- Strength: Principled use of noise — sensory and hidden-unit noise plus corresponding optimal actions improves robustness and ties to denoising autoencoders.

- Strength: Novel feedback-generation technique — computes feedback gains directly from trajectory optimization (soft-constrained) avoiding an LQG pass.

- Weakness: Positioning vs prior work — paper needs clearer articulation of conceptual novelty and direct comparisons to related methods (Levine, Mordatch, Abbeel) on cost, sample efficiency, robustness, and behavior complexity.

- Weakness: Unsubstantiated claim about LQG — the statement that LQG yields “very large and ill-conditioned” gains needs concrete evidence or an example.

- Weakness: Vague term “softer feedback gains” — define quantitatively (e.g., norm, smoothness) and justify why such gains are preferable.

- Weakness: Confusing notation in Sec. 3.2 — reuse of s̄ for different meanings should be fixed (suggest distinct symbols like s* and s_target).

- Suggestion: Reorder presentation — introduce the alternative feedback-gain method first, then compare to LQG; clarify motivations and benefits.

- Detailed comment: Distributed training architecture is a valuable practical contribution and is well-described.

- Detailed comment: Emergence of cyclic gaits without explicit encoding is a notable result that supports the approach’s strengths.
