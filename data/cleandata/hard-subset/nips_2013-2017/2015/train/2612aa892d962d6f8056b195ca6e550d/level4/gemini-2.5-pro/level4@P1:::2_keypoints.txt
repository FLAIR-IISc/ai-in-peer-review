- Paper trains compact RNN policies for real-time control by using offline trajectory optimization to generate training data and interleaving optimization with supervised learning.
- Key technical components: interleaved trajectory optimization and learning, noise injection into inputs/hidden units for robustness, and a method to extract feedback gains from trajectory optimization.
- Main contribution: scaling and combining established trajectory-optimization + imitation ideas to diverse 3D characters (swimmers, fliers, bipeds, quadrupeds) with several technical refinements.
- Strength — impressive and general results: a single algorithm produces natural, stable interactive locomotion across many morphologies and tasks.
- Strength — well-motivated technical contributions: ablation shows joint optimization and noise injection are critical; static/no-noise variants perform much worse.
- Strength — novel feedback-gain calculation: reuses trajectory-optimization computations to produce feedback terms without an expensive/ill-conditioned secondary optimization.
- Weakness — insufficient positioning vs recent related work (e.g., Levine & Koltun): needs clearer comparison on sample efficiency, training/runtime cost, robustness, and supported tasks/body shapes.
- Weakness — clarity issues in technical exposition: needs better explanation of LQG critique, precise meaning of “softer” gains and λ’s effect, motivation for modifying initial state, and clearer notation in gain derivation.
- Overall assessment: solid, technically sound paper with strong experiments, but its distinct advantages over contemporary policy-learning methods and some technical clarity must be improved.
- Arguments for acceptance: unified framework for diverse 3D controllers, strong empirical impact, and well-motivated/validated technical components.
- Arguments for rejection: unclear novelty/advantages over closely related recent work and insufficiently clear technical descriptions for reproduction.
