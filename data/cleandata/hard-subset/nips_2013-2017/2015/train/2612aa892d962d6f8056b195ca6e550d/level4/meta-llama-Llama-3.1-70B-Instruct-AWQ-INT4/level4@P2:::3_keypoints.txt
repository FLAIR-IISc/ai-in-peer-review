- Proposes a novel method for real-time control of 3D characters using a neural network trained offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs.  
- Core method components: alternating CIO and NN training, data-augmentation and additive hidden-layer noise, and a distributed training architecture.  
- Demonstrated on diverse complex characters: 3D swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking tasks.  
- Experimental results show realistic, stable policies that outperform a naive NN and a brief MPC comparison.  
- Paper is well-written and mathematical derivations are clearly explained and motivated.  
- Some technical aspects (especially the CIO step) are treated superficially and need additional references for full understanding.  
- Providing code would be valuable for community adoption and reproducibility.  
- Comparative evaluation is limited; only a brief comparison to MPC is presented.  
- Experimental validation would be stronger with a larger, more diverse dataset and more baselines.  
- Notation issues (e.g., section 3.2) reduce clarity in places.  
- Missing measurements of computation time for real-time control and other practical performance metrics.  
- Several technical details need clarification (e.g., target angle constraint, initialization of network weights).  
- Overall promising approach with practical potential but requires further evaluation and comparisons to establish broader applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, demonstrated effectiveness, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons, small dataset, and superficially addressed technical details.
