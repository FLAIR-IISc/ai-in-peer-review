

Overall, the paper presents promising results but requires significant restructuring to improve clarity and technical precision. The following points must be addressed:

1.  The paper should explicitly state at the outset that full model knowledge is required (citing Eq. 6), as this is a critical assumption for the proposed method.
2.  Sections 4 and 6 exhibit a significant structural imbalance. Section 4 provides excessive detail on a specific aspect, while Section 6, which should elaborate on the core theoretical contribution, is underdeveloped.
3.  Section 4 fails to reference the recurrent units mentioned in the abstract, creating a disconnect between the claimed contributions and the presented methodology.
4.  Line 405 raises a technical concern: the paper describes the network as "stochastic," but the architecture itself appears deterministic. The training data's stochasticity does not imply the network structure is stochastic; this distinction must be clarified.
