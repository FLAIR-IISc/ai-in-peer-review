The authors present a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers, capable of generating stable and realistic behaviors for a range of dynamical systems and tasks – swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The approach avoids motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines. The controller is a neural network with feed-forward units learning state-action mappings and recurrent units implementing memory beyond the system state. Critically, the network learns the dynamics *under* an implicit policy, rather than directly a control policy. Key features include interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization – CIO), injecting noise during training, training for unexpected task changes, and leveraging the trajectory optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains.

This work builds upon a substantial history of research in control, reinforcement learning, and stochastic optimal control, aiming to automate controller design for complex, high-dimensional systems. The authors position their contribution as scaling existing techniques – particularly combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization – to more challenging problems than previously demonstrated. Recent related work [9, 11] is acknowledged, but the authors argue their method achieves more complex and stable locomotion in three dimensions, a feat not previously accomplished. This improvement is attributed to several technical enhancements detailed in the paper. The work also connects to the historical application of neural networks to control, noting a recent resurgence driven by deep learning’s success in areas with large datasets – a contrast to the data scarcity inherent in control problems, particularly for novel or imaginary characters.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The method appears well-founded, with a clear explanation of the interplay between trajectory optimization and neural network training. The inclusion of noise injection and feedback gain optimization demonstrates careful consideration of practical challenges like overfitting and stability. The use of CIO is a strong foundation for generating high-quality training data.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors clearly explain their approach, the motivations behind their design choices, and the connections to prior work. The mathematical notation is used effectively and consistently.
* **Originality:** The combination of techniques – specifically, the integration of CIO with neural network training and the emphasis on learning feedback gains – represents a novel approach. Scaling this approach to achieve stable 3D locomotion is a significant contribution.
* **Significance:** The results are highly compelling. The ability to train a single network architecture to control a diverse range of characters with realistic and stable behaviors is a major achievement. This has potential implications for robotics, animation, biomechanics, and neuroscience. The method addresses a difficult problem in a demonstrable way and provides a unique approach to controller design. The observation that more compute is used for data generation than learning is a valuable insight.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** While the distributed training architecture is described, the computational demands of the approach (hours of training, cloud computing requirements) could limit its accessibility. However, the authors adequately acknowledge this limitation.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions several algorithmic constants, but a more detailed analysis of their impact on performance and robustness would be beneficial.
* **Comparison to Direct Methods:** The authors acknowledge a need for a systematic comparison with direct methods for neural network controller training. While they express a belief that their method yields superior results, a more quantitative comparison would strengthen their argument.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a technically sound, novel, and significant contribution to the field of control and machine learning.
* The results are highly compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains a complex approach.
* The work has the potential to impact a wide range of applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost of the approach may be a barrier to adoption.
* A more thorough comparison to existing methods would be valuable.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is an outstanding paper that represents a significant advance in the field. The authors have successfully combined established techniques in a novel way to achieve impressive results. The clarity of the writing, the technical soundness of the method, and the significance of the findings strongly recommend acceptance. 

**Recommendation:** Strong Accept.
