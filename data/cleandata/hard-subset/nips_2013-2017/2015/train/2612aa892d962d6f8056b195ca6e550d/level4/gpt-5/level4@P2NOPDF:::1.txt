The paper introduces a method that explicitly incorporates feedback gains into neural network policies, improving robustness to disturbances and to errors from supervised regression. Trajectory optimization and policy learning are softly coupled through a penalty on policy–trajectory deviation, enabling the policy to learn faithful approximations of optimal behaviors while yielding controllers that are simpler and more robust than relying on MPC-generated trajectories alone. The training pipeline leverages substantial parallelization to scale optimization—an implementation strength that supports, but does not overshadow, the central algorithmic contribution. The resulting feedback policies are demonstrated across a diverse set of robotic platforms and tasks.

This is an outstanding piece of work: technically sophisticated, exceptionally clear, and empirically compelling. The paper successfully integrates ideas from graphics, control, and deep learning to produce robust controllers for varied morphologies and objectives. The presentation is cohesive and readable, the experimental validation is thorough, and the results are striking. Overall, this is a tour de force that advances the state of the art in learning-based control and serves as an inspiring example of cross-disciplinary synthesis.
