- Method: NN trained offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs with alternating joint training between CIO and NN.  
- Key technique: noise injection via data augmentation (perturbed inputs) and additive noise in NN hidden layers.  
- Empirical result: network produces realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.  
- Contribution: joint training and noise injection are significant and improve generalization beyond a naïve “learn CIO” baseline.  
- Weakness — limited dataset: training trajectories are few (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), likely causing overfitting (especially “no noise” variant).  
- Weakness — limited comparisons: only a brief MPC comparison (no reference) rather than broader baseline evaluation.  
- Replicability concern: some math (especially CIO / Eq. 6) uses undefined notation and is treated superficially; additional references or code needed.  
- Confusing exposition: Section 3.2 (optimal actions for noisy inputs) has unclear notation and dropped time index t.  
- Missing runtime analysis: need time measurements and scaling with 3D model complexity for real-time use (e.g., games).  
- Clarification asked: is the target only xyz coordinates (no facing angle), and how easy is adding a target angle?  
- Terminology: data augmentation where targets are recomputed should not be called “noise injection” (not the same as denoising autoencoders).  
- Algorithm detail: question whether initial Alg.1 Xs are computed with random theta or without regularization.  
- Hyperparameter question: why use the same eta in both steps of Alg.1?  
- Algorithmic mismatch: asynchronous joint optimization in Sec.6 differs substantially from alternating Alg.1 and should be noted earlier.  
- Experimental split suggestion: separate “no noise” into “no data augmentation” and “no hidden-layer noise injection.”  
- Ablation question: investigate decreasing alpha to zero during CIO — is benefit from curriculum learning or persistent regularization?  
- Minor edits/typos to fix: “biped location”→“biped locomotion”; “unity”→“unify”; missing/incorrect LQG definition and article; many line-specific fixes (argmin star placement, \bar{s} in \tilde{C}, f→f^{des}, etc.).  
- Clarification needed: statement about dynamics constraints and root forces is unclear and should be rephrased.  
- Notational/consistency fixes: ensure sX/aX subset claims, clarify “single pass over the data” phrasing, and correct Table 1a caption (4 vs 3 layers).  
- Overall assessment: promising neural-network approach to character control whose practical benefits and applicability still need stronger evaluation and clarification.
