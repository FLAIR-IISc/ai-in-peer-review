- Paper proposes training neural-network feedback controllers by combining Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) trajectory optimization with supervised learning, demonstrated on swimmers, flyers, bipeds, and quadrupeds.  
- A recurrent neural network is trained to mimic the optimizer, enabling a single, real-time controller for diverse characters without task-specific engineering or motion capture.  
- Key methodological contributions: alternating optimization that interleaves trajectory optimization with policy training, sensory/hidden noise injection for robustness, and extraction of optimal feedback gains from CIO for richer training signals.  
- Results are impressive and diverse: stable, natural-looking locomotion and emergent gaits (e.g., quadruped trotting) across very different morphologies.  
- Technical components are well-motivated and effective; ablation studies show the alternating scheme, noise injection, and feedback gains are crucial for generalization.  
- High significance for character animation, gaming, and robotics due to the unified, optimization-based control paradigm.  
- Main clarity issue: the method requires a complete and accurate dynamics model (for inverse dynamics in Eq.6), which should be stated explicitly to contrast with model-free approaches.  
- Presentation imbalance: Section 4 (policy regression) is too brief while engineering details (Section 6) are overemphasized; expand network description and de-emphasize implementation minutiae.  
- Recurrent unit inconsistency: the memory state r is introduced but not detailed in the network section—implementation (Elman/LSTM/GRU) and training of ṙt must be clarified for reproducibility.  
- Terminology ambiguity: calling the model “stochastic neural networks” is misleading—training uses injected noise but the deployed policy appears deterministic; clarify this distinction.  
- Overall: a powerful, well-executed framework with compelling experiments, but the paper should be restructured and clarified on model assumptions, recurrent implementation, and terminology to maximize impact.
