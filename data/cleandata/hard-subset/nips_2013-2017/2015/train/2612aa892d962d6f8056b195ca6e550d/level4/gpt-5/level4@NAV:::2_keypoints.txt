- Paper proposes a compact, high-speed neural network that encodes control policies via trajectory optimization, claiming one architecture works across cyclic behaviors and multiple morphologies.

- Reviewer sees the approach as applying deep learning to established trajectory-optimization techniques and neural policy approximation; prior work has explored similar ideas.

- The authors must better position their contributions relative to prior methods, especially recent work by Abbeel and Levine.

- Clarify the specific advantages over prior work: faster learning, broader morphology support, lower runtime cost, or greater robustness to environment variation?

- Explain what is meant by “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains.”

- Move the discussion of why LQG was not used to after the authors’ own strategy is presented.

- Fix unclear notation around lines 226–240 (definition s(X)=s̄ and the cost using s(X)−s̄); overbars/diacritics are hard to read.

- Define “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ”: specify softer relative to what, what “soft” means, and why altering the initial state is beneficial.

- Overall assessment: solid work but the novelty and contribution relative to recent related efforts are not articulated clearly enough.
