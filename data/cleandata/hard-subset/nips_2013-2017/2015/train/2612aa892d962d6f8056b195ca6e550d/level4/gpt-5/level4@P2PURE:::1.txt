The paper introduces a method for embedding feedback gains into neural network policies to improve robustness to disturbances and regression errors. Trajectory optimization and policy optimization are softly coupled via a deviation penalty in the cost, enabling the policy to learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories that are simpler and more robust than trajectories produced by MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. The tasks are optimized using sophisticated parallelization, though this is more a means to an end than a central contribution. Finally, the trained feedback controllers are used to control a diverse set of robots.

This is an outstanding paper: exceptionally clear, technically comprehensive, and supported by beautiful application results. Bravo. It elegantly unifies graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers across a wide range of body morphologies and tasks. It is genuinely inspiring.
