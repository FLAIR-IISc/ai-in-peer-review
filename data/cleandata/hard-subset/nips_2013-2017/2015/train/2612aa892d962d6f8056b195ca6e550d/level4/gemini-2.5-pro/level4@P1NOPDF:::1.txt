The authors propose a strategy to embed feedback gains within neural network policies, rendering them robust to disturbances and regression errors. The core of the method is a soft coupling between trajectory optimization and policy optimization, implemented via a cost penalty for deviation. This allows the policy to learn effective approximations of optimal trajectories that are often simpler and more robust than those generated by traditional MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. The authors employ a sophisticated parallelization framework to perform the optimization, though this is presented as a practical necessity rather than a core contribution. The power of this approach is demonstrated by successfully controlling a diverse set of robots with the resulting feedback controllers.

This is a wonderful body of work that successfully marries graphics, control, and neural networks in the pursuit of generating controllers for a wide variety of body morphologies and tasks.
