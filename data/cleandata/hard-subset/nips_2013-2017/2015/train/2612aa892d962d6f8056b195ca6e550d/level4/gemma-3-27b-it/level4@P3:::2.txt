## Review of "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks"

This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as feedback controllers for a variety of dynamical systems, enabling realistic and stable behaviors for characters in simulation – including swimming, flying, and walking with different morphologies. The core idea is to leverage trajectory optimization to generate training data for a neural network, which then learns to reproduce these behaviors online. The authors demonstrate the versatility of this approach by applying it to a diverse set of characters without task-specific tuning.

**Summary and Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds on established techniques in both computer graphics and reinforcement learning. Trajectory optimization, particularly Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) [12], has a proven track record in generating complex motor behaviors. Similarly, the use of neural networks for control is a well-explored area. The authors’ contribution lies in successfully scaling these techniques to more challenging, high-dimensional problems – specifically, achieving stable locomotion in 3D space – and demonstrating a unified approach applicable to diverse character types. This work is related to, and attempts to extend, recent approaches combining trajectory optimization and neural networks [9, 11]. However, the authors argue their method tackles more difficult problems, achieving locomotion in 3D where previous work was limited to 2D. A key component is a set of technical improvements, including noise injection and optimization of feedback gains, which contribute to the success of the method.

**Strengths:**

* **Versatility:** The ability to control diverse characters (swimming, flying, bipeds, quadrupeds) with a single network architecture and set of parameters is a significant achievement. This demonstrates the generality and robustness of the proposed approach.
* **Stability:**  The reported stability of the learned controllers, particularly in 3D locomotion, is noteworthy, as maintaining stability is a major challenge in this domain.
* **Integration of Techniques:** The paper successfully combines the strengths of trajectory optimization (finding high-quality behaviors) with the efficiency and real-time capabilities of neural networks.
* **Detailed Implementation:** The authors provide a comprehensive description of their distributed training architecture and implementation details, including algorithmic choices and hyperparameter settings.
* **Ablation Studies:** The inclusion of ablation studies (Section 8.1) strengthens the claims by demonstrating the contribution of key components, such as noise injection.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty:** While the results are impressive, the paper could more explicitly delineate its novelty relative to closely related work, particularly that of Levine & Koltun [9] and the work from Abbeel’s group. A clearer comparison along dimensions such as learning speed, generalizability to body shapes, computational cost (runtime and training), and robustness to environmental variability would be beneficial.
* **Clarity of "Soft Gains":** The explanation of “softer feedback gains” (Section 3.2) is unclear. The paper needs to better define what is meant by "soft" gains and explain the advantages of this approach compared to more conventional feedback control methods. Why is modifying the initial state a desirable aspect of the optimization procedure?
* **Notation:** The notation in Section 3.2, specifically the use of bars and squiggles (e.g.,  s(X) = \bar s and the subsequent cost function involving s(X) - \bar s), is visually confusing and should be revised for better readability.
* **Discussion of Ill-Conditioned Gains:** The statement about “large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” (Section 3.2) requires further elaboration. Providing more context about why this is a problem and how the proposed method addresses it would improve understanding.

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper appears technically sound, with a clear mathematical formulation and a thorough experimental evaluation. The use of a physics simulator (MuJoCo) and well-defined cost functions suggests a rigorous approach.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. However, as noted above, certain sections (e.g., Section 3.2) could benefit from clarification.  Providing more intuitive explanations alongside the mathematical details would improve accessibility.
* **Originality:** The originality lies in the successful combination and scaling of existing techniques to a challenging problem. While the individual components are not entirely novel, the unified approach and its demonstrated versatility are significant.
* **Significance:** The results are potentially significant for fields such as robotics, animation, and biomechanics. The ability to automatically generate stable and realistic controllers for diverse characters could have practical applications in these domains.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Demonstrates a robust and versatile approach to character control.
* Successfully scales trajectory optimization and neural networks to challenging 3D environments.
* Provides a detailed implementation and thorough experimental evaluation.
* Includes useful ablation studies.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The novelty relative to recent related work could be more explicitly highlighted.
* Some sections require clarification for better understanding.
* The concept of “soft gains” needs a more thorough explanation.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential. While the paper would benefit from a clearer positioning of its contribution relative to recent related work and some improvements in clarity, the technical soundness, versatility, and promising results justify serious consideration for acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
