

This paper proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning to enable interactive control of complex characters. The core contribution is jointly learning trajectories and policies, with the empirical insight that decoupling the optimization into alternating subproblems i) permits reuse of well-documented existing methods and ii) achieves sufficient practical performance. While the paper is clearly written and technically sound, I am not sufficiently immersed in this domain to assess its potential impact; the work demonstrates technical proficiency but lacks novel theoretical insights.  

The authors achieve an impressive level of technical sophistication across concepts, implementation, and results. The claimed advantages—i) incorporating sensory noise during training and ii) learning trajectories jointly with the policy—are clearly demonstrated. The paper's writing is effective, maintaining appropriate detail for the problem's complexity, with each section addressing a distinct aspect and including useful cross-references.  

However, the motivation for the additional optimization step in Section 7 requires clarification. Given the prior presentation, I expected the policy to operate without further optimization. Is this due to residual trajectory cost (or policy regression error) causing accumulated errors when policy actions are fed into the physical simulator? I assumed the forward dynamics evolution inherently enforces physical constraints, preventing implausibility, but I may be overlooking a key point. I acknowledge that re-optimizing at every timestep enables larger timesteps.  

Line 153: "similar to recent approaches" – please provide one or two references. It is counterintuitive to me that restricting parameter updates in parameter space is generally sensible, though I understand its utility in your block-alternating framework.  

Line 89: "to unify" (typo).  

The results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and implementation are well-motivated. However, as this research area falls outside my primary expertise, I am unable to confidently evaluate the novelty or significance of the work.
