- Demonstrates a small, fast neural network policy learned via trajectory optimization for interactive control of diverse characters; claims a single architecture generalizes across characters and quasi-cyclic behaviors.
- Reviewer sees the work as applying deep learning to established trajectory-optimization techniques and prior neural policy approximation, so novelty relative to prior work is unclear.
- Authors should more clearly articulate novelty and compare to recent related work (e.g., Levine & Abbeel) on learning speed, applicability to different morphologies, runtime cost, and robustness to perturbations.
- Clarify the claim that the process "can produce very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" (what is meant and evidence).
- Move the discussion of why LQG was not used to after the proposed strategy is fully presented for better clarity.
- Fix confusing notation around lines 226–240 (e.g., s(X) = \bar s versus s(X) - \tilde s; visually similar diacritics causing difficulty).
- Explain the statement that the approach "produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ": specify the baseline and provide a technical definition of "soft".
- Provide justification for modifying the initial state as part of the optimization procedure.
- Overall judgment: technically sound but contribution relative to recent related work is insufficiently clear.
