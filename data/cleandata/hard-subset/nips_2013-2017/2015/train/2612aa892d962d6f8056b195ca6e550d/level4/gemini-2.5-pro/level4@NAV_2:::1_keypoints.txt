- Introduces an alternating scheme that combines offline trajectory optimization with supervised learning to train neural feedback controllers.  
- Uses Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) to produce optimal state-action trajectories that serve as training data for a recurrent policy.  
- Key technical contributions: principled noise injection into sensory inputs/activations, extraction of optimal feedback gains from the optimizer, and a distributed asynchronous training architecture.  
- Advances prior work by demonstrating a single, unified algorithm that produces stable, realistic control for diverse 3D characters (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds).  
- Strength — Technical quality: well-conceived framework, carefully designed components, and a clever, generally applicable method for extracting feedback gains.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: impressive experiments and ablation study that show the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Strength — Clarity: exceptionally well-written presentation with clear problem formulation and helpful figures/tables.  
- Strength — Originality/significance: produces emergent, natural-looking gaits across morphologies, representing a major advance for animation and robotics.  
- Weakness — Lacks quantitative comparison to modern deep RL methods (e.g., TRPO), relying mainly on qualitative evidence.  
- Weakness — Method depends on a powerful offline trajectory optimizer; discussion of limitations in domains where such optimizers are intractable is missing.  
- Weakness — Policy execution requires solving a small optimization per timestep; details on per-frame real-time cost are not provided.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant advance in generating robust interactive controllers, novel integration of feedback gains and noise, and high-quality, convincing results.  
- Argument against acceptance: minor — insufficient empirical comparison to state-of-the-art reinforcement learning makes performance trade-offs unclear.
