This paper presents a novel approach with promising empirical results. However, the current version suffers from significant structural and clarity issues that should be addressed before it can be considered for publication.

**Major Comments**

The primary weakness of the paper is its overall structure and the clarity of its exposition. A substantial revision is needed to present the work in a more logical and accessible manner.

1.  **Clarity on Model Assumptions:** A critical requirement of the proposed method is access to full model knowledge, as detailed in Equation 6. This is a very strong assumption that significantly limits the practical applicability and generality of the approach. This prerequisite should be stated explicitly and prominently at the very beginning of the paper, ideally in the introduction. As it stands, this crucial detail is buried in the methodology section, which may mislead the reader about the scope and novelty of the contribution.

2.  **Paper Organization and Balance:** The manuscript's structure feels imbalanced and hinders comprehension. Specifically, there is a notable disproportion in the level of detail and length between Section 4 and Section 6. This can make the paper difficult to follow. More importantly, Section 4 fails to introduce or discuss the recurrent units, which are mentioned in the abstract as a key component of the model. This disconnect between the abstract's promise and the body's explanation leaves the reader confused about the core architecture. The paper would benefit greatly from a restructuring that ensures a logical flow, with all essential model components introduced systematically and given appropriate emphasis.

**Minor Comments**

On line 405, the model is referred to as a "stochastic neural network." This terminology seems imprecise. While the network is trained on stochastic data, the network architecture itself appears to be deterministic (i.e., for a given input, the output is fixed). If the network does indeed contain stochastic elements (e.g., variational components or dropout at test time), this needs to be clarified. Otherwise, it would be more accurate to describe it as a deterministic network applied to a stochastic problem.

**Conclusion**

In summary, the paper introduces an interesting method and demonstrates its potential with promising results. However, its effectiveness is undermined by a lack of clarity and poor organization. The reliance on full model knowledge must be made transparent upfront, and the entire paper needs to be restructured to provide a coherent and balanced description of the proposed method. While the core ideas are valuable, the manuscript in its current form is not ready for publication. I would encourage the authors to revise the paper thoroughly to address these issues.
