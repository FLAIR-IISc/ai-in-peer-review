- Proposes training RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers by jointly learning trajectories and a control policy via iterative trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization).
- Generates training data from trajectory optimization and alternates between optimizing trajectories and policy parameters.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal locomotion, producing stable, realistic behaviors without motion capture or task-specific features.
- Integrates existing techniques effectively rather than introducing new theoretical contributions.
- Uses well-motivated implementation choices: supervised learning with trajectory optimization, sensory noise during training, and an alternating optimization scheme.
- Writing and organization are clear, with sections focused on distinct aspects and helpful cross-references.
- Needs clearer motivation for the real-time optimization step in Section 7, specifically whether it addresses residual trajectory cost, policy regression error, or physics constraints.
- Suggests adding specific citations where phrasing like “similar to recent approaches…” is used (line 153).
- Recommends more explicit explanation for the rationale behind keeping parameter updates small in the block-alternating optimization scheme.
- Notes a minor typographical error on line 89 (“to unify”).
- Overall contribution is strong and practical, enabling interactive control without extensive manual tuning.
- Reviewer uncertain about the work’s novelty and broader significance due to limited domain expertise.
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, compelling empirical results, clarity, and practical utility.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, need for domain-expert assessment of impact, and unclear runtime optimization motivation.
