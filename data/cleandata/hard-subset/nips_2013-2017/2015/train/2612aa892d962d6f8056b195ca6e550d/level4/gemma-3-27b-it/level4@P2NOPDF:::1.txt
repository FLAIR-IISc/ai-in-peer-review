This paper introduces a compelling strategy for embedding feedback gains directly within neural network policies, resulting in robust control performance even in the presence of disturbances and model inaccuracies. The core innovation lies in a soft coupling between trajectory optimization and policy optimization, achieved through a cost penalty that encourages the learned policy to approximate optimal trajectories. Notably, the resulting policies yield simpler and more robust behaviors than those typically produced by Model Predictive Control (MPC) alone. While the authors leverage sophisticated parallelization techniques to facilitate optimization, this is presented as an enabling component rather than a primary contribution. The efficacy of the approach is convincingly demonstrated through the successful control of a diverse range of robotic morphologies across varied tasks.

This is an exceptionally strong paper. The work is presented with remarkable clarity, demonstrates a sophisticated command of relevant techniques, and delivers aesthetically pleasing and compelling results. It represents a significant achievement in bridging the fields of graphics, control, and neural networks, effectively generating controllers adaptable to a broad spectrum of robot designs and task requirements. The work is genuinely inspiring and deserves high recognition.
