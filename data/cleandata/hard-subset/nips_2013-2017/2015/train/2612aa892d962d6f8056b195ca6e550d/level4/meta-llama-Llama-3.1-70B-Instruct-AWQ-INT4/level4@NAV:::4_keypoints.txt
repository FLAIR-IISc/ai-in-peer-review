- Proposes joint learning of trajectories and policies, integrating trajectory optimization with policy learning for interactive control of complex characters.  
- Key insight: decoupling into alternating sub-problems enables reuse of established methods and yields practical results.  
- Demonstrates strong technical execution from conceptualization to implementation with impressive empirical results.  
- Shows benefits of adding sensory noise during training and of learning trajectories jointly with policies.  
- Paper is well-written, detailed appropriately, and sections are clearly focused with helpful cross-references.  
- Reviewer sees the work as technically impressive but not offering novel theoretical breakthroughs.  
- Motivation for the additional optimization step in Section 7 is unclear; reviewer expected the policy to work without re-optimization.  
- Questions whether the need for re-optimization stems from residual trajectory cost or accumulated policy regression error when simulating actions.  
- Reviewer notes that re-optimizing at every time step enables use of larger time steps and appreciates this practical benefit.  
- Minor request: provide one or two references supporting the claim "similar to recent approaches" (line 153).  
- Minor typo noted on line 89 ("to unify").  
- Overall, implementation and results are convincing, but reviewer cannot confidently assess novelty/significance due to limited domain familiarity.
