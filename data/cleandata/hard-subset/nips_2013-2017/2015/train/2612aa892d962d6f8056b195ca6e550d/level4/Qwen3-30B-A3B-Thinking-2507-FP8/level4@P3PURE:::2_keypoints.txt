- Paper proposes small, fast neural-network policies trained with trajectory optimization for interactive physics-based character control, claiming generalization across diverse cyclic behaviors and body types.
- This is an application of deep learning to established graphics/control techniques; trajectory optimization and NN policy approximation have prior validation and related work.
- Authors must more clearly position their contribution relative to prior work, especially recent Abbeel/Levine papers.
- Clarify the concrete advantages over prior work: faster learning, broader body-shape support, lower runtime cost, or improved robustness to environment variability?
- Specific: clarify which process produces "very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" and why those gains are ill-conditioned.
- Specific: move the discussion of why LQG was not used to after the method presentation for better logical flow.
- Specific: fix confusing notation around s(X)=\bar{s} and the cost s(X)-\bar{s} (bars vs. squiggles create ambiguity).
- Specific: justify the claim that "our approach produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ" — define softer vs. harder gains, the baseline for comparison, and why initial-state modification is part of the procedure.
- Overall: the work is sound, but the novelty and contribution relative to recent literature need stronger justification.
