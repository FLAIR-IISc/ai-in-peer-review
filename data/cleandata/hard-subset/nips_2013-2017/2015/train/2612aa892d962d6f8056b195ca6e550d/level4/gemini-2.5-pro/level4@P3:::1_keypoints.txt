- Presents a novel joint optimization method that combines offline trajectory optimization with an RNN policy to produce complex, stable behaviors for diverse simulated characters.  
- Formulates concurrent optimization of optimal trajectories and neural network policy parameters.  
- Introduces a principled noise-injection method using optimal feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer to improve robustness to perturbations.  
- Uses an alternating, distributed scheme that interleaves Contact-Invariant trajectory optimization and supervised learning for the RNN.  
- Demonstrates the framework on challenging 3D tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal and quadrupedal locomotion) with a single general architecture.  
- Advances prior work by scaling trajectory-optimization-plus-function-approximation to more complex 3D characters and realistic locomotion without task-specific state machines.  
- High technical quality: well-founded method, strong experimental support, and an effective ablation study showing importance of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Significant impact: produces visually and qualitatively superior emergent behaviors and offers a practical framework likely to influence robotics, animation, and biomechanics.  
- Original contributions: extracting well-conditioned feedback gains from direct trajectory optimization and formulating a large-scale distributed alternating optimization.  
- Exceptionally clear presentation with precise problem statement, detailed algorithm, effective figures, and an informative video.  
- Weakness: lacks a quantitative comparison to state-of-the-art reinforcement learning methods (e.g., TRPO) to benchmark sample efficiency and final performance.  
- Weakness: computationally expensive (cluster of ~30 nodes + GPU, multi-hour training); more discussion of scalability and bottlenecks would help practitioners.  
- Weakness: policy execution uses a single-timestep optimization to enforce dynamics—clarification of its real-time computational cost is needed.  
- Overall: technically deep, rigorously evaluated, clearly written work that is a clear accept and a candidate for best paper.
