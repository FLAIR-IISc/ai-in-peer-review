- Paper introduces an algorithm that trains a neural network offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) for real-time 3D character control, with alternating/joint CIO–NN training and deliberate noise injection.
- Alternating/joint training and hidden-layer plus input-noise data augmentation are central contributions that materially improve generalization.
- Experiments show the network produces realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.
- Experimental evidence is limited by a small dataset (100–200 trials with 5 branches each); stronger results would need more training trajectories.
- The “no noise” and “no joint” ablations may overfit or underperform partly due to limited training data.
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief, uncited comparison to MPC is provided and systematic comparisons to alternative methods are missing.
- Mathematical presentation is mostly clear but reproducing results requires consulting additional references because some elements (especially CIO / Eq. 6) are treated superficially and notation is undefined.
- Section 3.2 (optimal actions for noisy inputs) is confusing; time indexing and notation are unclear.
- Authors should share code to aid reproducibility.
- Report real-time control timing and how computation scales with model complexity (important for CPU-limited settings like games).
- Clarify terminology and wording (e.g., “biped location” vs locomotion; “unity” vs unite/unify).
- Clarify whether targets constrain only xyz position or also heading, and whether adding a desired heading is straightforward.
- Don’t call recomputing targets for perturbed inputs “noise injection”; it is standard data augmentation distinct from denoising-style injection.
- Clarify initialization in Alg. 1: are initial X computed using random θ or without the regularization term?
- Explain rationale for using the same hyperparameter η in both steps of Alg. 1.
- Fix grammar/typos and acronym issues (LQG defined after use; missing articles; misplaced symbols and missing variables in equations).
- Resolve several specific notation/logic issues called out (misplaced star in argmin, missing \bar{s} in \tilde{C}, “minimizer of a quadratic around optimal trajectory” wording, why sX and aX are subsets of φX).
- Clarify which objective is referred to when saying “a single pass over the data to reduce the objective in (4)” and mention earlier that asynchronous joint optimization in Sec. 6 differs substantially from Alg. 1’s alternating scheme.
- Fix likely typos (l.315 f vs f^{des}) and clarify the sentence about soft dynamics constraints possibly including root forces.
- Suggest splitting the “no noise” ablation into separate “no data augmentation” and “no hidden-layer noise injection” experiments.
- Recommend testing whether annealing α to zero during CIO helps (curriculum vs permanent regularization effect).
- Correct Table 1a caption (says 4 layers but likely 3).
- Overall: polished and promising approach, but practical benefits, broader applicability, and comparative strength need stronger experimental evidence and clearer exposition.
