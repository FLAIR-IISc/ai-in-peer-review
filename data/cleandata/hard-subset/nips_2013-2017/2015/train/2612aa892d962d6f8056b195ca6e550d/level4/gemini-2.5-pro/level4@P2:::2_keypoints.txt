- Proposes training an RNN controller from offline trajectory-optimized state-action data (Contact-Invariant Optimization) for real-time feedback control of simulated characters.  
- Core contributions: joint block-alternating optimization of trajectories and policy, sensory/hidden noise injection for robustness, and deriving feedback gains directly from trajectory optimization.  
- Demonstrates a single architecture producing stable, realistic locomotion across swimmers, flyers, bipeds, and quadrupeds without motion-capture or task-specific features.  
- Empirical results are strong: diverse morphologies and tasks handled with realistic, stable behaviors shown in supplementary video.  
- Moves toward a unified framework for character control, avoiding hand-crafted state machines and simplified models.  
- Technical novelty: principled alternating optimization addresses closed-loop instability and feedback gains extraction from direct trajectory optimization avoids a separate LQG pass.  
- Main weakness: insufficient comparison and positioning relative to closely related Guided Policy Search (GPS) and other reinforcement-learning/control work.  
- Needs clearer explanation of how Algorithm 1 (distributed stochastic optimization) differs from GPS alternating methods and what concrete advantages it offers (sample efficiency, runtime cost, robustness, applicability).  
- Feedback-gain claims require substantiation: quantify "ill-conditioned" vs "soft" gains and compare empirically against LQG baselines.  
- The benefit and trade-offs of relaxing the initial state constraint are not well motivated; provide intuition and analysis for this choice.  
- Recommend reorganizing Section 3.2 so critique of LQG follows full presentation of the proposed method for readability.  
- Notation in Section 3.2 is confusing (e.g., s̄ vs s(X)); use clearer, more distinct symbols to improve readability.  
- Overall: solid, convincing empirical work with interesting technical ideas, but needs clearer positioning, stronger empirical validation of the feedback-gain method, and clearer technical exposition.
