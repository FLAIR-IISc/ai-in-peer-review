- Paper proposes training an RNN policy to emulate an offline trajectory optimizer (CIO) via alternating joint optimization and noise injection to produce real-time controllers for diverse 3D characters.
- Joint optimization of the neural policy and trajectory optimizer is a significant contribution and improves generalization over training on a static dataset.
- Strategic noise injection (sensory perturbations with recomputed optimal actions and hidden-unit noise) is critical to prevent overfitting and produce stable policies.
- Experiments show visually realistic, stable locomotion across morphologies (bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, flyers) without task-specific engineering.
- Training dataset size (~100–200 trials with 5 branched segments) seems small, so overfitting in ablations may be unsurprising and larger datasets should be tested.
- Comparative evaluation is weak: limited, qualitative MPC comparison and no systematic comparison to direct policy search / RL baselines.
- Some technical details lack clarity for reproducibility; notation in CIO (Eq. 6) and Section 3.2 (optimal feedback gains) is confusing or missing time indices.
- Source code release would greatly help reproducibility.
- Practical performance details are omitted; policy execution runtime (ms/frame) and scaling with character complexity are needed for interactive claims.
- Typos: "biped location" → "biped locomotion"; "unity these disparate approaches" → "unify" or "unite."
- Unclear whether task goals include orientation in addition to target position; orientation targets may be important in practice.
- The term "noise injection" may be misleading since optimal actions are recomputed for perturbed inputs—this is closer to data augmentation than denoising autoencoders.
- In Algorithm 1, clarify whether initial trajectories X are generated without neural regularization or using a randomly initialized network θ from iteration one.
- Justify using the same hyperparameter η for proximal regularization in both trajectory optimization and network regression steps.
- Please elaborate on specific challenges of adapting ADMM to an asynchronous, stochastic setting.
- The acronym LQG is used before formal introduction.
- Notational errors in Section 3.2: misplaced star in argmin*X and missing s̄ in cost C̃’s arguments.
- Mention earlier that the asynchronous optimization implementation (Section 6) departs from Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme to avoid confusion.
- In Section 7, confirm whether the final optimization term should be ||f - f_des||^2.
- Clarify the sentence about enforcing dynamics constraints "softly" and the implication that this may include some root forces in simulation.
- Split the "no noise" ablation into two experiments to isolate effects: (a) no sensory perturbation/data augmentation and (b) no hidden-unit noise.
- Suggest exploring annealing the policy-matching weight α toward zero to test whether joint optimization acts mainly as curriculum learning or requires continual regularization.
- Table 1a caption inconsistency: caption mentions 4 layers but table lists up to 3 hidden layers.
