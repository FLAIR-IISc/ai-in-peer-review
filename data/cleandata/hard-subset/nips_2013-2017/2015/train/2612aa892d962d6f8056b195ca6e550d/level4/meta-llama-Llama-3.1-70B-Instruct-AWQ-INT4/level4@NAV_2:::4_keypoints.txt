- Proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning for interactive control of complex characters.
- Main contribution: jointly learn trajectories and policy, using alternating subproblem optimization to reuse existing methods and achieve practical performance.
- Technical sophistication and implementation quality are impressive, yielding strong empirical results.
- Demonstrates clear benefits of training with sensory noise and of learning trajectories jointly with the policy.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides appropriate detail with useful cross-references.
- Motivation for the extra optimization step in Sec. 7 is insufficiently explained; reviewer expected the policy to run without re-optimization.
- Reviewer asks whether residual trajectory cost or policy regression error forces re-optimization, and notes re-optimizing enables larger timesteps.
- Suggests adding one or two references around l153 for similar recent approaches; questions intuition about small parameter-space updates.
- Minor typo: l89 should read "to unify".
- Quality: technically sound, thorough evaluation, and convincing experimental results.
- Originality: novel combination of methods, but individual components are not entirely new and need more related-work context.
- Significance: promising impact on character animation/robotics but hard to evaluate without deeper domain expertise.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/effective approach, strong results, thorough evaluation, clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear overall significance, limited novelty of parts, and unclear motivation for Sec. 7 optimization.
