This paper proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning to enable interactive control of complex characters. The central idea is to learn trajectories and the policy jointly, while decoupling the problem into alternating subproblems so that existing, well-documented methods can be reused; empirically, this alternating scheme appears to work well in practice. Although I followed the paper, I am not sufficiently immersed in this area to judge its broader impact with confidence. On the surface, the work demonstrates significant technical prowess but does not appear to introduce novel theoretical insights.

Strengths:
- The paper exhibits a high level of technical sophistication from the conceptual design to the implementation, and the results are impressive.
- The claimed benefits of (i) injecting sensory noise during training and (ii) learning trajectories jointly with the policy are clearly demonstrated.
- The writing is clear, with an appropriate level of detail given the complexity of the problem and implementation. The sectioning is logical, and cross-references are helpful.

Questions and concerns:
- The motivation for the additional optimization step in Section 7 could be explained more clearly. Based on the preceding sections, I expected the learned policy to run without requiring extra optimization. Is the need for this step due to a residual trajectory cost or policy regression error, such that directly feeding the policy’s action into the simulator would accumulate errors? My intuition is that forward dynamics should enforce physical constraints and prevent physically implausible behavior; if that is not sufficient here, an explanation would help. I do appreciate that re-optimizing at each timestep can enable the use of larger timesteps.

Minor comments:
- Line 153: “similar to recent approaches…” Please provide one or two references. It is counterintuitive to me that constraining updates to be small in parameter space is generally desirable; however, I understand why it is useful in your block-alternating setup.
- Line 89: Typo—“to unify.”

Overall assessment:
The results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and implementation seem sensible. However, because this research area is somewhat outside my core expertise, I find it difficult to confidently assess the novelty and significance.
