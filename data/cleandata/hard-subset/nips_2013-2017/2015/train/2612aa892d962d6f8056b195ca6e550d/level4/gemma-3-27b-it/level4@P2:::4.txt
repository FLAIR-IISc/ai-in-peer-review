This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as feedback controllers for complex characters, enabling interactive control across diverse dynamics and tasks – swimming, flying, and both bipedal and quadrupedal locomotion. The core idea is to jointly optimize trajectories and a neural network policy, leveraging trajectory optimization (specifically, Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data and a network to learn and generalize from this data. The authors propose an alternating optimization scheme to make this tractable, and demonstrate strong results across a range of challenging scenarios. While the work doesn’t introduce fundamentally new theoretical concepts, it represents a significant engineering achievement and effectively combines existing techniques to address a difficult problem.

The authors demonstrate a remarkable level of technical sophistication throughout the paper, from the algorithmic design to the implementation details. The results convincingly support the claims regarding the benefits of both sensory noise injection during training and the joint learning of trajectories and policy. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized; each section focuses on a specific aspect of the problem, with helpful cross-referencing. The level of detail is appropriate considering the complexity of the work.

A potential area for clarification lies in the motivation for the online trajectory optimization step described in Section 7. Given the preceding sections, the reader might anticipate the policy to function without continuous re-optimization. The authors could benefit from explicitly addressing whether residual trajectory cost or policy regression error necessitates this step, and clarifying how the physics simulation inherently constrains the solution space. While the benefit of larger timesteps is noted, a more detailed explanation of the need for this continual optimization would be valuable.

On line 153, the statement “similar to recent approaches…” would be strengthened by including one or two relevant citations. The rationale for small updates in parameter space initially seems counterintuitive, though the explanation within the context of the block-alternating optimization scheme ultimately makes sense. A minor typo exists on line 89: “to unify” should likely be “to unite”. 

The paper’s results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and its implementation appear sensible. However, this research area falls somewhat outside my primary expertise, making it difficult to confidently assess the novelty and significance of the work relative to the broader field.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The method successfully controls a diverse range of complex characters in three-dimensional space, a significant advancement over previous work often limited to two dimensions.
*   **Clear and Well-Written Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-organized, clearly explained, and easy to follow, despite the technical complexity.
*   **Sound Technical Approach:** The combination of trajectory optimization and neural network learning is well-motivated and effectively implemented. The alternating optimization scheme is a practical solution to a challenging problem.
*   **Reproducibility:** The authors provide sufficient detail to allow for replication of the results, and the availability of a supplementary video is helpful.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Novelty:** The paper doesn’t introduce fundamentally new theoretical concepts, but rather effectively combines existing techniques.
*   **Domain Expertise:** Assessing the true impact and novelty requires deep expertise in the field of character control, which I do not possess.
*   **Potential for Clarification:** While well-written, some aspects of the approach, particularly the rationale behind the online trajectory optimization, could benefit from further elaboration.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the lack of groundbreaking theoretical novelty, this paper presents a significant engineering accomplishment and demonstrates a practical, effective approach to interactive control of complex characters. The strong empirical results, clear writing, and sound technical foundation suggest that this work is a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the minor clarifications suggested above.
