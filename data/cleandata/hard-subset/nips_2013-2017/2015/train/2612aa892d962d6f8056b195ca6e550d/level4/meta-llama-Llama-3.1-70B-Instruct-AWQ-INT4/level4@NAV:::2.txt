"Interactive control of diverse complex characters with neural networks" demonstrates the feasibility of utilizing a compact, efficient neural network to represent a control policy for interactive character control in a physics simulator.

The proposed approach leverages trajectory optimization during the training phase to learn a neural network policy. The primary contribution lies in showcasing the versatility of this architecture across various cyclic behaviors and body types.

General Comments:

Upon reviewing this paper, it appears to be an application of deep learning to existing techniques in computer graphics. The effectiveness of trajectory optimization in generating realistic control signals has been previously established, with some relevant work cited in the paper.

The use of neural networks to approximate step-by-step actions for tracing trajectories similar to those found by trajectory optimization has also been explored in prior research, such as [9]. Therefore, the authors should provide a clearer positioning of their work relative to these established approaches.

A relevant comparison can be drawn with the recent work by Pieter Abbeel and Sergey Levine. It would be beneficial for the authors to discuss how their approach compares to this line of work, specifically in terms of learning speed, support for diverse body shapes, computational requirements at runtime, and robustness to environmental variabilities.

Specific Comments:

The statement "we found the resulting process can produce very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" requires further clarification.

Consider reorganizing the discussion of LQG to follow the presentation of the proposed strategy.

The notation in lines 226-240 is unclear. The introduction of s(X) = \bar s and the subsequent definition of a cost in terms of s(X) - \bar s are visually confusing due to the similarity of the bars and squiggles.

The statement "our approach produces softer feedback gains according to parameter \lambda" is unclear. It would be helpful to specify what is meant by "softer" gains, how they compare to alternative approaches, and why this is a desirable outcome.

Additionally, the rationale behind changing the initial state as part of the procedure is not explicitly stated. While the work is sound, the contribution relative to other recent related work requires further clarification.
