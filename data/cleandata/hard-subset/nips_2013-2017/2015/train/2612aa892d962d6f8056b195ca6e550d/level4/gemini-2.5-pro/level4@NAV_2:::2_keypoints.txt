- Proposes training an RNN controller for real-time feedback using offline Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) to generate near-optimal state-action trajectories.
- Key training contributions: block-alternating optimization interleaving trajectory optimization and network regression.
- Adds noise at sensory inputs and hidden units to improve robustness and prevent overfitting.
- Introduces a perturbation-based method to compute optimal feedback gains directly from the trajectory optimizer.
- Demonstrates a single network architecture producing stable, realistic interactive locomotion for diverse 3D characters (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds).
- Empirically advances prior trajectory-optimization-plus-network work by scaling to more complex 3D locomotion tasks.
- Strength: unified framework learns controllers for varied morphologies and locomotion styles without task-specific state machines, producing high-quality motions.
- Strength: technically sound combination of offline optimizer and compact neural policy, with sensible choices (noise, joint optimization) critical for success.
- Strength: thorough ablation study showing joint optimization and noise injection are crucial for execution-time performance.
- Weakness: originality/positioning unclear relative to closely related work; needs clearer comparison to contemporary policy search methods and articulation of specific advantages (cost, robustness, scalability, behavior quality).
- Weakness: brief comparison to MPC; claim of more predictable/less chaotic behavior needs quantitative support and deeper explanation of why neural regularization yields this.
- Clarification requests: provide intuition or concrete example of LQG producing large/ill-conditioned gains, reorder Section 3.2 to present the perturbation-based gains method before contrasting with LQG, fix confusing notation around s(X)=s̄ and perturbed vs. optimized states, and define/justify what is meant by “softer feedback gains.”
