- Presents a real-time control method using an NN trained to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs, with alternating joint training between CIO and NN.  
- Key contributions are the joint training scheme and noise-based data augmentation (including hidden-layer noise), which improve generalization.  
- Experiments show the network can produce realistic, stable controllers across varied character models.  
- Main experimental weakness: small training set (100–200 trials with 5 branches each) risks overfitting, especially in the “no noise” variant.  
- Comparative evaluation is limited: brief MPC comparison lacks citation and broader baselines are missing.  
- Mathematical presentation is mostly clear but some derivations (notably around the CIO step and Eq. 6) use undefined notation and are superficially treated.  
- Section 3.2 (optimal actions for noisy inputs) is confusing; notation and omitted time indices make intent unclear.  
- Releasing code would greatly aid reproducibility and community adoption.  
- Missing runtime/compute measurements for real-time control and no analysis of how performance scales with model complexity.  
- Terminology/phrasing issues and typos throughout (e.g., “biped location”, “unity” → “unite/unify”, grammar fixes suggested).  
- Clarify whether target includes character facing angle (x,y,z only currently) and whether adding angle would be straightforward/beneficial.  
- The term “noise injection” is misleading for the data-augmentation step; it’s better described as data augmentation since targets are recomputed.  
- Unclear algorithmic details: whether initial X’s in Alg. 1 use random θ or are initialized without regularization.  
- Question rationale for using the same hyperparameter η in both steps of Alg. 1.  
- Several editorial/math errors: LQG is used before definition, incorrect article usage, misplaced star in argmin (l.231), missing words and subscripts, f vs f^{des} (l.315).  
- Claims like “sX and aX are subsets of φX” need justification.  
- The “no noise” experiment should be split into “no data augmentation” and “no hidden noise injection” for clearer attribution.  
- Suggest investigating whether α can be reduced to zero (CIO-only) to test if benefits arise from curriculum learning or persistent CIO guidance.  
- The asynchronous joint optimization in Section 6 differs substantially from Alg. 1’s alternating scheme; this distinction should be clarified earlier.  
- Table/caption mismatch: Table 1a caption states 4 layers but appears to show 3.  
- Overall assessment: promising and compelling approach, but needs stronger comparative experiments, clearer math/algorithmic details, runtime analysis, and minor edits to fully establish practical value.
