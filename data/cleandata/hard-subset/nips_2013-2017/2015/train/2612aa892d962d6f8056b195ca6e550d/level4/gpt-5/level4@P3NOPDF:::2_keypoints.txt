- Paper demonstrates small, fast neural nets can represent interactive control policies trained from trajectory optimization across cyclic behaviors and morphologies.

- Strength: Emphasizes runtime efficiency and interactivity important for character control.

- Strength: Uses optimized trajectories as strong supervision to train policies for complex dynamics.

- Strength: Empirically shows the compact architecture transfers across behaviors and morphologies without extensive per-task redesign.

- Main concern: Poorly positioned relative to prior work in trajectory optimization, imitation learning, and guided policy search.

- Must clarify distinctive contribution versus guided policy search and related frameworks.

- Question: What specific advantages does the method provide (training speed, handling morphologies, robustness, runtime lightness)?

- Question: What trade-offs exist in sample efficiency, stability, or robustness compared to prior methods?

- Question: Is novelty in the architecture, training pipeline, or empirical breadth—frame claims and provide supporting ablations.

- Clarify “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains”: define precisely and provide quantitative evidence and failure examples.

- LQG discussion should be reorganized: present the proposed strategy first, then concrete reasons (empirical/theoretical) why LQG was not used.

- Fix confusing notation (e.g., s(X)=s̄ used as both constraint and penalty); use distinct symbols like s* and write penalties clearly.

- Define what “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” means (gain magnitudes, stiffness/damping, regularization), provide metrics and show trade-offs with tracking/robustness.

- Justify changing the initial state during training: curriculum vs avoiding local minima vs stability, and quantify its effect on generalization and convergence.

- Experimental evaluation: provide direct comparisons to guided policy search on runtime latency, controller size, sample efficiency, training wall-clock time, robustness, and morphology transfer.

- If possible, include head-to-head benchmarks or controlled ablations isolating claimed advantages.

- Include ablations: network size vs performance/latency; effect of λ on stability/tracking; impact of initial-state variation; sensitivity to modeling/simulator errors.

- Clarify problem statement up front: goals, assumptions, and precise scope (cyclic behaviors, contact-rich dynamics, etc.).

- Improve organization by fully introducing the method before comparing alternatives like LQG.

- Tighten notation, avoid similar diacritics, and define all hyperparameters, training schedules, and optimization tolerances for reproducibility.

- Specify which architectural components are fixed versus retuned when claiming generality across bodies and behaviors.

- Overall: work is sound and practically relevant with promising results but underplays relation to prior art and lacks empirical validation of distinct advantages.

- Recommendation: inclined to weak reject unless authors sharpen novelty, provide principled comparisons, clarify technical issues (ill-conditioning, λ, notation, initial states), and include targeted ablations.

- With improved framing, comparisons, and ablations, the work could make a valuable contribution.
