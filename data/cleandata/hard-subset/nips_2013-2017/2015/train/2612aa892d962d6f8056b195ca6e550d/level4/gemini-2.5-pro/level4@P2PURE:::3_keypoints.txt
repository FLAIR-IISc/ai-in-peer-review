- Proposes alternating optimization where a neural network is trained offline to reproduce a Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) controller, with each stage informing the other's objective.  
- Introduces noise injection via data augmentation (input perturbations) and noise added to hidden layers.  
- Experiments show the method yields realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.  
- Joint training scheme and noise injection are significant contributions that improve generalization beyond a straightforward NN-to-CIO mimic.  
- Training dataset is small (100–200 trials with 5 branches each), raising concerns about overfitting and limited generalization.  
- "No noise" ablation overfits on the small dataset; "no joint" variant might also improve with more varied trajectories.  
- Comparative evaluation is limited; only a brief, uncited discussion of Model Predictive Control (MPC) is provided.  
- Mathematical formulations are generally clear but some components (notably the CIO step and Equation 6) lack definition and detail, harming reproducibility.  
- Reproducibility would be improved by releasing source code.  
- Section 3.2 (generating optimal actions for noisy inputs) is confusing: notation is unclear and the time index t is dropped without explanation.  
- Timing/inference measurements for real-time control are missing; scaling with 3D model complexity should be reported.  
- Clarify whether the target includes orientation or only (x,y,z); orientation targets are important in practice.  
- The term "noise injection" for recomputing targets on perturbed inputs is imprecise; this is better described as data augmentation.  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguities: are the first trajectories generated with randomly initialized network weights or without network-based regularization?  
- No justification given for using the same hyperparameter (eta) in both optimization steps of Algorithm 1.  
- Several typographical and wording issues: "biped location" vs "locomotion," "unity" vs "unite/unify," missing articles in multiple sentences.  
- Acronym LQG is used before being defined; an article "a" is missing before "LQG pass."  
- Specific notation errors: misplaced star in argmin (line 231), possible missing \bar{s} in \tilde{C}, and incomplete phrase "the minimizer of a quadratic around optimal trajectory."  
- Question whether sX and aX are guaranteed subsets of \phiX; clarify these set relationships.  
- Comment that reducing the objective in (4) while only optimizing theta is misleading; Algorithm 1's objective would be more accurate to reference.  
- Asynchronous joint optimization described in Section 6 differs substantially from the alternating scheme in Algorithm 1 and this distinction should be highlighted earlier.  
- Suggest splitting the "no noise" ablation into two experiments: "no data augmentation" and "no hidden-layer noise injection."  
- Propose investigating annealing alpha to zero during optimization to test if joint training acts as curriculum or provides ongoing regularization.  
- Clarify ambiguous sentence about dynamics constraints being enforced softly and potential inclusion of root forces.  
- Table 1a discrepancy: caption says 4 layers but network appears to have 3—please clarify.
