- Paper integrates trajectory optimization with policy learning for interactive control of articulated characters, alternating between the two subproblems.  
- Central idea: jointly learn trajectories and policy while practically alternating optimizations to leverage established methods and achieve good performance.  
- The work is a notable technical achievement but does not appear to introduce new theoretical insights.  
- Authors demonstrate strong technical rigor from design through implementation, producing compelling behaviors.  
- Experiments convincingly support benefits of injecting sensory noise during training.  
- Experiments also convincingly support benefits of co-optimizing trajectories with the policy.  
- Paper is clearly presented with appropriate technical detail and helpful cross-references between sections.  
- Clarification requested for the extra optimization step in Section 7 — why the learned policy alone isn’t sufficient and whether residual trajectory cost or regression error causes error accumulation.  
- Reviewer notes appreciation that per-timestep re-optimization enables using larger timesteps.  
- Minor request: cite one or two specific references for “similar to recent approaches” (l153).  
- Minor typo: l89 “to unify.”  
- Overall impression: results are impressive and implementation sound, but reviewer is not fully confident assessing novelty and broader significance due to being outside the subarea.
