- Trains RNN policies as real-time feedback controllers using offline trajectory optimization.
- Uses an iterative alternating optimization that jointly refines optimal trajectories and policy parameters.
- Injects noise during training to improve policy robustness.
- Novel technique to extract optimal feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer so the network learns local optimal reactions to perturbations.
- Demonstrated on a diverse set of 3D characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) with a single architecture.
- Technically sophisticated integration of Contact-Invariant Optimization, gain extraction, and stochastic training; significantly better than naive static dataset training.
- Original contribution: scales trajectory-optimization-based policy learning to complex, contact-rich 3D locomotion and introduces robust gain-based targets.
- High potential impact: automated alternative to hand-engineered controllers and model-free RL for realistic, stable behaviors in graphics and robotics.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well organized, with an informative ablation study.
- Weakness: lacks quantitative or qualitative comparisons to state-of-the-art reinforcement learning or policy-search baselines.
- Weakness: policy execution involves a single-timestep optimization whose computational cost and real-time limitations are not fully analyzed.
- Weakness: scalability of the trajectory optimizer (time horizon, character complexity, task constraints) needs more discussion.
- Overall recommendation: outstanding paper; recommend acceptance.
