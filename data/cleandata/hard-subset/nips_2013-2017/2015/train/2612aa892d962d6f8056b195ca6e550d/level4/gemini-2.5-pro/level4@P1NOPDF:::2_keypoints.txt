- Paper demonstrates that a small, fast neural network can be trained via trajectory optimization to control diverse, semi-cyclic simulated characters.  
- Main contribution appears to be showing a single network architecture works across a variety of characters and behaviors.  
- The work largely applies established graphics techniques (trajectory optimization + policy approximation) and needs clearer positioning relative to prior art.  
- Missing detailed comparison to recent work by Levine, Abbeel, and colleagues; clarify differences and advantages (training speed, generalization, runtime efficiency, robustness).  
- Clarify the claim that the method “can produce very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains,” including when and why this happens.  
- Recommend moving the LQG discussion until after the main method is presented so readers first understand the proposed approach.  
- Fix confusing notation around lines 226–240 (e.g., s(X)=\bar{s} and cost defined as s(X)-\bar{s}), using more distinct symbols to avoid implying zero cost.  
- Explain what is meant by “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ,” including the baseline comparison and a precise definition of “softness.”  
- Justify the motivation and benefit for modifying the initial state as part of the training procedure.  
- Overall: technically sound but needs stronger delineation of novel contributions and clearer explanations addressing the points above.
