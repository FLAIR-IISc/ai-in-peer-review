## Review: Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks

This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as feedback controllers for a variety of dynamic systems, including swimming, flying, and walking characters with different morphologies. The approach aims to automate controller design, moving beyond traditional methods reliant on motion capture or state machines. It combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization – CIO), leveraging the optimizer to generate training data for the neural network. The key contribution lies in demonstrating stable and realistic locomotion in 3D, a feat previously challenging for similar approaches, achieved through several technical improvements including noise injection and optimized feedback gain calculation.

The work builds upon a rich history of neural network control, harking back to early applications while benefiting from modern computational power. It connects to recent efforts in learning control policies from trajectory optimization [9, 11], but extends this work to significantly more complex, three-dimensional systems. The authors also acknowledge related work in traditional control methods for specific character types like bipeds [18, 21] and quadrupeds [13], aiming for a unified approach.  This unification is particularly valuable, given the fragmented nature of control architectures historically used for different character types [7, 8].

**Strengths:**

* **Broad Applicability:** The method demonstrates successful control across a diverse range of character types and tasks, showcasing its generality. The accompanying video is crucial in conveying this strength.
* **Stable 3D Locomotion:** Achieving stable locomotion in 3D space is a significant technical accomplishment and a clear advancement over previous work that was largely limited to 2D.
* **Combined Approach:** The synergy between trajectory optimization and neural network learning is well-motivated. Leveraging CIO for data generation addresses the challenge of obtaining sufficient training data for complex controllers.
* **Technical Details:** The paper provides a relatively detailed explanation of the training procedure, including the stochastic optimization process, noise injection strategy, and feedback gain calculation.
* **Scalability:**  The authors acknowledge and address the computational demands of the approach, utilizing cloud computing and a GPU implementation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Model Dependence:** The method relies heavily on having a complete and accurate dynamical model of the character (Equation 6). This is not explicitly highlighted early in the paper and represents a significant limitation for application to real-world robotics.
* **Uneven Detail:** Sections 4 (Neural Network Policy Regression) and 6 (Distributed Training Architecture) feel disproportionately detailed compared to other sections. A more balanced presentation would be beneficial.
* **Recurrent Units:** The role of recurrent units, mentioned in the abstract, isn’t clearly elaborated upon within the main body of the paper. The explanation of the state representation (Eq. 2) focuses primarily on feedforward aspects.  A deeper discussion of how recurrence contributes to memory and stability would be valuable.
* **Stochasticity Clarification:** The description of stochasticity (l. 405) is somewhat unclear. While the *training data* is stochastic, the network architecture itself appears deterministic. Clarification of how stochasticity manifests within the network’s operation would strengthen the description.
* **Limited Comparative Evaluation:**  The comparison to direct methods (e.g., [3, 6, 14]) remains impressionistic. A more systematic comparison, even if preliminary, would be valuable.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a compelling approach to automated controller design with a strong empirical demonstration.  The ability to generate stable and realistic locomotion for diverse characters is a notable achievement. While the reliance on a complete dynamical model is a limiting factor, the overall contribution is significant and potentially impactful. The paper is generally well-written, but could benefit from restructuring and a clearer explanation of the role of recurrent units and stochasticity.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Demonstrates a significant advance in the automated design of complex controllers.
* Achieves stable 3D locomotion, exceeding the capabilities of prior work.
* Combines established techniques in a novel and effective manner.
* Presents a thorough experimental evaluation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Reliance on a complete dynamical model limits applicability to real-world scenarios.
* Uneven level of detail in different sections of the paper.
* Lack of a more rigorous comparison to alternative control methods.



**Recommendation:**  **Accept with Minor Revisions.**  I recommend acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly clarifying the role of recurrence, stochasticity, and the model dependence, and providing a more balanced presentation of the technical details.  A more thorough discussion of the limitations and potential future work would also be beneficial.
