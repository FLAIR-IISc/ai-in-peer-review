- Proposes a novel method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers producing stable, realistic behaviors.
- Method combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injects noise during training, and uses the optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on diverse tasks (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) and varied body morphologies.
- Differs from prior work by training a network to mimic an optimizer’s feedback behavior online rather than hand-designing controllers.
- Addresses challenges of neural network controllers, including need for precise real-valued outputs and risks of overfitting and poor generalization.
- Strength: learns robust, realistic behaviors that generalize across morphologies and tasks.
- Strength: flexible and potentially suitable for real-time interactive control.
- Key innovation: noise injection during training to reduce overfitting and improve generalization.
- Evaluation is thorough, including comparisons to other methods and ablation studies.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and precise resource requirements.
- Overall assessment: well-written, clearly organized, and technically sound with appropriate mathematical exposition.
- Originality: presents a distinct and innovative approach building on but different from prior neural control and trajectory optimization work.
- Significance: has potential for wide applicability and substantial impact on autonomous systems.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clear explanation, thorough evaluation, and potential high impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing computational complexity details and some readers may find the mathematics challenging.
