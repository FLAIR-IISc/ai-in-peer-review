This paper proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning to enable interactive control of complex characters. The core idea is to jointly learn trajectories and the policy, with the practical insight that decoupling the problem into alternating subproblems both enables reuse of well-established methods and works well empirically. I was able to follow the paper, but I am not immersed enough in this domain to confidently assess its potential impact. On the surface, it represents a strong technical achievement, though it does not appear to offer novel theoretical insights.

The work demonstrates an impressive level of technical sophistication—from conceptual design through implementation—and the results are equally compelling. The claimed advantages of (i) injecting sensory noise during training and (ii) jointly learning trajectories with the policy are clearly supported by the experiments.

The paper is clearly written, with an appropriate level of detail given the complexity of the problem and the system. Each section addresses a distinct component, and the cross-references are helpful.

That said, I think the motivation for the additional optimization step in Section 7 could be better explained. By that point, based on the setup in earlier sections, I expected the learned policy to run without requiring extra optimization. Is the additional step necessary because there is always a residual trajectory cost or policy regression error, such that executing the policy alone in the physical simulator would accumulate drift? I would have thought the forward dynamics would enforce physical constraints and prevent implausible behavior, but I may be missing an important point. I do appreciate the remark that re-optimizing at every timestep allows for larger timesteps.

l153: “similar to recent approaches...” Could you provide one or two representative references? Also, the claim that keeping updates small in parameter space is generally desirable seems counterintuitive to me. I understand its usefulness here, where you alternate between subproblems and want stability across iterations, but some justification or pointers to supporting work would help.

Overall, the results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and implementation seem sensible. However, because this area is slightly outside my expertise, I find it difficult to be fully confident about the paper’s novelty and significance.
