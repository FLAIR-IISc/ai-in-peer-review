- Proposes an algorithm training a neural network to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs with alternating joint training between CIO and NN.  
- Uses noise injection via data augmentation and additive noise in hidden layers to improve generalization.  
- Experiments show the network yields realistic and stable real-time control policies across various character models.  
- Joint training and noise injection are significant contributions beyond simply training NN to predict CIO.  
- Dataset is small (100–200 trials, 5 branched segments), raising overfitting concerns, especially for the "no noise" variant.  
- The "no joint" variant may also suffer from insufficiently diverse training trajectories.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak—only a brief comparison to MPC; broader comparisons to other methods are needed.  
- Mathematical explanations are generally clear, but the CIO step (Eq. 6) is treated superficially and needs more references.  
- Requesting code would aid reproducibility and community validation.  
- Section 3.2 (generating optimal actions for noisy inputs) has confusing notation and unclear treatment of the time index t; needs clarification.  
- Should report runtime/time measurements and scaling with model complexity for real-time control use cases.  
- Minor issues: clarify "biped location" as locomotion and correct phrasing ("unity" → "unite"/"unify").  
- Target specification seems to omit heading angle; discuss ease of adding a target orientation.  
- Term "noise injection" is misleading—it's data augmentation rather than denoising autoencoders.  
- Unclear justification for using the same hyperparameter eta in both steps of Alg. 1; several minor notation and grammar errors throughout.  
- Overall: well-written and innovative with promising results, but practical applicability and broader validation remain to be established.
