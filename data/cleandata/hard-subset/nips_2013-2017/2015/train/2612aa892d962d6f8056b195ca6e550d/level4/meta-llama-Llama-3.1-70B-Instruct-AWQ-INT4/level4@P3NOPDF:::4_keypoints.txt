- Proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning to enable interactive control of complex characters.  
- Main contribution is joint learning of trajectories and policies, with empirical insight that alternating (decoupled) subproblem optimization works well.  
- Alternating subproblem approach allows reuse of existing methods and yields satisfactory practical results.  
- Reviewer not an expert but finds the work a technical tour-de-force showing strong technical sophistication.  
- Experimental results are impressive and clearly demonstrate benefits of training with sensory noise and joint trajectory-policy learning.  
- Paper is well-written and provides appropriate implementation and methodological detail, with helpful cross-references.  
- Motivation for the extra optimization step in Section 7 is unclear; reviewer expected the policy to run without additional optimization.  
- Reviewer suspects the extra step is needed to correct residual trajectory cost or policy regression error that accumulates when actions are simulated.  
- Notes that forward dynamics impose physical constraints, but re-optimizing each step may allow use of larger time steps.  
- Suggests adding one or two relevant references where the approach is compared to recent methods (line 153).  
- Initially found the idea of keeping parameter-space updates small counterintuitive but accepts its utility for block-alternating optimization.  
- Minor typo on line 89 ("to unify"); reviewer is hesitant to judge novelty/significance due to not being a domain specialist.
