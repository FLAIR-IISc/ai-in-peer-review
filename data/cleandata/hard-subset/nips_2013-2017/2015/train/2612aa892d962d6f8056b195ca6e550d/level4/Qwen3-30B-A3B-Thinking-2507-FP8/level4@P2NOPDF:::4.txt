

This paper introduces a method for interactive character control by jointly learning trajectories and policies through alternating optimization. The key insight is that decoupling the problem into alternating subproblems—trajectory optimization followed by policy learning—enables reuse of established techniques while achieving strong empirical performance. The approach leverages sensory noise during training and demonstrates clear advantages in learning trajectories alongside policies, which the authors effectively substantiate.

The paper exhibits exceptional technical depth and clarity. The implementation integrates sophisticated concepts seamlessly, and the results are compelling. The writing is precise, maintaining an appropriate level of detail for the problem's complexity, with well-structured sections and helpful cross-references. The method's advantages—particularly the use of sensory noise and joint trajectory-policy learning—are convincingly demonstrated.

However, the rationale for the additional optimization step in Section 7 requires clarification. Given the prior presentation of the policy as a direct controller, the necessity of this step is unclear. Specifically, it is not evident why the policy alone cannot generate valid actions without residual trajectory cost or regression error. If physical constraints inherently prevent implausibility during forward dynamics simulation, why is iterative re-optimization needed? The benefit of enabling larger timesteps is noted, but the underlying motivation for this extra step warrants stronger justification.

The claim at line 153 ("similar to recent approaches...") would be strengthened by citing one or two relevant works. While the strategy of small parameter updates aligns with the block-alternating framework, the general intuition that restricting updates in parameter space is beneficial is counterintuitive without context—this should be explicitly linked to the paper's specific optimization structure.

A minor typo appears at line 89 ("to unify" should be "to unify"). 

The paper’s technical execution is robust and its implementation sensible, yielding impressive results. Nevertheless, as this domain is outside my primary expertise, I cannot confidently assess the novelty or broader significance of the contribution. The work demonstrates strong engineering and empirical rigor, but its theoretical novelty appears limited. While the alternating optimization framework is practical and effective, it builds directly on established techniques rather than introducing new theoretical foundations. The impact hinges on whether this specific combination unlocks capabilities beyond existing trajectory-based policy learning approaches, a point that requires deeper domain expertise to evaluate.

Overall, the paper presents a well-executed, technically sound method with clear empirical advantages. Its primary value lies in the practical integration of trajectory optimization and policy learning for interactive control, supported by thorough evaluation. To enhance its contribution, the authors should clarify the necessity of the Section 7 optimization step and provide context for the parameter update strategy. The work would benefit from stronger articulation of its distinctiveness relative to prior art in the introduction or related work. Given the paper’s technical quality and results, I recommend acceptance pending these clarifications.
