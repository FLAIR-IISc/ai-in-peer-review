The authors propose a novel approach to training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers, capable of generating stable and realistic behaviors for a range of dynamical systems and tasks. The method combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, and using the trajectory optimizer to obtain optimal feedback gains. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various tasks, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking, with different body morphologies.

The paper relates to previous work in the field, including the use of neural networks for control and the application of trajectory optimization methods. However, the authors' approach differs from previous work in its use of a neural network to learn from the optimizer and generate similar behaviors online. The authors also discuss the challenges of training neural network controllers, including the need for precise real-valued outputs and the potential for overfitting and poor generalization.

The authors' approach has several strengths, including its ability to learn robust and realistic behaviors, its flexibility in handling different body morphologies and tasks, and its potential for real-time interactive control. The use of noise injection during training is also a key innovation, as it helps to prevent overfitting and improve generalization. The authors' evaluation of their approach, including comparisons with other methods and ablation studies, is thorough and well-executed.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach. While the authors mention the use of cloud computing and GPU implementation, it would be helpful to have a more detailed discussion of the computational resources required to implement the method.
