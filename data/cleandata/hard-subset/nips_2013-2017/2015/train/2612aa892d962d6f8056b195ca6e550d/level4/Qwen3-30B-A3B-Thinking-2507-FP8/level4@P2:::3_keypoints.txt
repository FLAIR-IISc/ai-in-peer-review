- Introduces an alternating joint optimization of RNN training and Contact-Invariant Optimization with data augmentation and hidden-layer noise to produce near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D characters.  
- Generates stable, realistic behaviors across swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal morphologies without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Clear motivation, well-structured methodology, and compelling empirical results; outperforms naive static-trajectory NN training by ~100x.  
- Demonstrates that noise injection is crucial to prevent overfitting and produce generalizable controllers.  
- Comparative evaluation is insufficient: only compares to MPC and lacks systematic comparisons to other neural-network control methods (e.g., refs [3,6,14]).  
- Training dataset is small (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), which likely causes overfitting; larger datasets needed to support generalization claims.  
- Ablation study is weak: "no noise" should be split into "no data augmentation" and "no hidden noise injection" to isolate contributions.  
- Mathematical exposition is confusing (Section 3.2 drops time index t without explanation); notation and time-index relationships need clarification.  
- Missing runtime measurements for real-time execution; should report computation times to assess feasibility for applications like games.  
- Several minor issues and wording fixes: "biped location" → "biped locomotion", "unity" → "unify"/"unite", clarify target orientation constraints, and note that "noise injection" is essentially data augmentation.  
- Methodological clarifications needed: justify using the same hyperparameter η in both optimization steps and better connect the asynchronous scheme in Sec.6 to Algorithm 1.  
- High significance and broad applicability to animation, robotics, and biomechanics; promising contribution that is acceptable for publication pending the suggested evaluations and clarifications.
