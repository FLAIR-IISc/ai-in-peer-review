- Paper shows a compact, fast neural-network policy learned via trajectory optimization that generalizes across diverse cyclic behaviors and body types.  
- Many key components (trajectory optimization producing realistic controls; NNs approximating step-wise policies) are established—authors must better position their contribution relative to prior work.  
- The closest related line (Pieter Abbeel & Sergey Levine) is not sufficiently compared—clarify whether this method improves learning speed, body-shape generality, runtime cost, or robustness.  
- Clarify the statement “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains”: what it means and its implications.  
- Consider reordering: postpone the rationale for not using LQG until after presenting the proposed method.  
- Notation in lines 226–240 is confusing (definition of s(X)=\bar{s} then cost in terms of s(X)-\bar{s}); simplify or rephrase.  
- Explain “our approach produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ”: specify “softer” relative to what, define it, and justify why it helps.  
- Justify why changing the initial state is included as part of the procedure.  
- Overall assessment: sound work, but the novelty and contribution relative to recent literature remain inadequately clarified.
