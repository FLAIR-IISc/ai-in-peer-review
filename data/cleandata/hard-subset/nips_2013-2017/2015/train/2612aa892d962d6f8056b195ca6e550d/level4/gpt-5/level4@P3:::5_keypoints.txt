- Proposes alternating Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) for trajectory/gain generation with supervised learning of a neural controller that outputs desired velocities, contact forces, and recurrent memory updates.  
- Builds on guided policy search and prior trajectory-optimization + function-approximation work, targeting higher-dimensional 3D locomotion/flight/swimming characters for animation-style control.  

Strengths:
- Single unified method/architecture works across diverse morphologies (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds) without mocap or task-specific features.  
- Reuses CIO Gauss–Newton Hessian to derive local feedback gains for noise-adjusted supervision in an elegant and efficient manner.  
- Injecting noise at inputs and hidden layers with joint training yields improved robustness and reduces overfitting (ablation shows large gaps).  
- Practical distributed asynchronous training pipeline with proximal regularization that empirically converges stably.  
- Real-time execution with a simple T=1 optimization that stabilizes dynamics and contacts; behaviors appear stable and visually plausible.

Weaknesses / Clarifications needed:
- Heavy dependence on full and accurate model knowledge (EoM, contact/friction constraints) which limits applicability; this assumption should be stated up front.  
- Terminology confusion: paper calls the policy “stochastic” but the deployed network appears deterministic—clarify stochastic training vs. stochastic policy.  
- Recurrent component is poorly described: specify whether there are true recurrent weights and BPTT, the size of r, and how ṙ is trained.  
- The T=1 execution step is akin to MPC/trajectory tracking with soft constraints; calling it “near-optimal feedback control” may be overstated and needs discussion of failure modes and guarantees.  
- Evaluation is mainly qualitative; add quantitative metrics (tracking error, robustness to perturbations/model mismatch, energy/torque, contact violations, timing) and comparisons to MPC and model-free baselines.  
- Generalization is unclear since training data come from CIO with fixed cost—need held-out tests for new goals, terrains, parameter changes, and stronger perturbations.  
- Paper organization is uneven; Sections 4 and 6 should be rebalanced and recurrent/architectural details consolidated in Section 4.  
- Confusing action/state wording: clarify that the network learns a policy under a specific action parameterization (desired velocities/forces) rather than the system dynamics.  
- References and notation need tightening and completion.

Quality:
- Methodologically sound with a principled coupling of trajectory optimization, local gains, and supervised learning, but empirical evaluation lacks rigorous quantitative analysis and broader ablations.

Clarity suggestions:
- State full-model assumption early, clarify stochastic vs deterministic policy, detail recurrent design/training, and standardize notation for states/poses/sensory/memory.

Originality:
- Moderate; combines known components (CIO, noise injection, alternating optimization) but contributes a notable gain-computation reuse of the CIO Hessian.

Significance:
- High relevance to graphics and interactive character control; moderate broader impact for general RL/control.

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong diverse qualitative results and unified approach.  
- Elegant and efficient gain computation with robust training via noise.  
- Scalable, practical training pipeline with real-time execution.

Cons for acceptance:
- Reliance on accurate models and soft runtime dynamics.  
- Limited quantitative evaluation and comparisons.  
- Ambiguities around recurrence and stochastic-policy claims.  
- Organizational issues and incomplete references.

Key questions for authors:
- How many recurrent units are used, and are there true recurrent connections with BPTT?  
- Is the deployed policy deterministic—if so, avoid calling it stochastic?  
- What are runtime timings per frame for the T=1 solve across tasks?  
- How sensitive is performance to λ in gain computation and noise scales (σε, σγ)?  
- How does the policy handle model mismatch (changed mass/friction) and larger perturbations?

Recommendation:
- Promising work with practical impact; lean toward acceptance conditional on clearer exposition of recurrence/model assumptions and stronger quantitative evaluation.
