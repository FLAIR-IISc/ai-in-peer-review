

The author introduces a strategy for embedding feedback gains within neural network policies to enhance robustness against disturbances and regression errors. Trajectory optimization and policy optimization are softly coupled through a cost penalty for deviation, enabling the policy to learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories that are both simpler and more robust than those generated solely by MPC-based trajectory optimization. While sophisticated parallelization techniques are employed for task optimization, this serves as a supporting method rather than the paper's central contribution. The trained feedback controllers successfully govern a diverse array of robotic systems.  

This paper is of the highest quality, exhibits exceptional clarity, represents a tour de force of technical execution, and delivers striking results across its applications. Bravo. It is a beautiful synthesis of graphics, control theory, and neural networks, achieving inspiring controller generation for varied body morphologies and tasks.
