- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers using trajectory optimization (CIO) and supervised learning for diverse dynamical characters.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability and robustness across swimming, flying, and 3D walking with different morphologies.  
- Novel combination of trajectory optimization and neural-network control enabling high-quality, real-time execution.  
- Key technical improvements (noise injection, stochastic optimization, efficient feedback gain computation) are crucial for stable 3D behaviors.  
- Clearly articulates learning challenges: need for precise outputs, closed-loop operation, and risks of overfitting.  
- Provides detailed implementation of distributed training architecture, aiding reproducibility.  
- Lacks a clear explanation of recurrent units and the role/impact of the memory state r in the control architecture.  
- Unclear description of stochasticity: training data is stochastic but the deployed network appears deterministic, needing clarification on how stochasticity shapes the learned policy.  
- Assumes full model knowledge (Eq. 6) without stating implications up front, limiting discussion of real-world applicability with imperfect models.  
- Section imbalance: Sections on policy regression and distributed training are overly detailed relative to other parts, hurting paper flow.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective approach with compelling results, substantial technical contributions, and reproducible detail.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear recurrent/stochastic explanations, limiting model-assumption for real-world use, and organizational/clarity issues.  
- Overall: strong, impactful paper recommended for acceptance pending revisions to clarify recurrent units, stochasticity, and model-knowledge assumptions.
