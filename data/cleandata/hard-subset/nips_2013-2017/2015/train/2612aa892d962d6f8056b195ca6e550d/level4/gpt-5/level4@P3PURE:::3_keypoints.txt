- Proposal: train a neural network offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs for real-time 3D character control, with interleaved CIO and NN training and noise injection (data augmentation + hidden-layer noise).

- Main contributions: alternating joint training and noise injection — nontrivial steps that improve generalization according to experiments.

- Experimental strength: resulting policy produces realistic, stable control across diverse character models.

- Dataset limitation: base training trajectories are small (100–200 trials with 5 branches), likely causing overfitting in ablations.

- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief MPC comparison at the end, no citation for that MPC variant, and no systematic comparison to direct methods.

- Mathematical exposition mostly clear but not fully self-contained; replication requires external references.

- CIO step unclear: Eq. 6 has undefined notation and is treated superficially.

- Section 3.2 is confusing: notation degenerates, time index t is dropped, and time dependencies/optimization procedure are unclear.

- Request to release code to aid replication and community adoption.

- Missing run-time and scaling analysis: report real-time control performance and dependence on model complexity.

- Terminology nit: recomputing supervision for perturbed inputs is standard data augmentation, not “noise injection”/denoising.

- Algorithm 1 concerns: do initial Xs use random θ or are they computed without the regularization term? justification requested.

- Hyperparameter question: why use the same η in both steps of Algorithm 1?

- Clarify asynchronous vs alternating optimization: Section 6’s asynchronous joint optimization differs substantially from Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme and should be flagged earlier.

- Suggest finer ablations: split “no noise” into “no data augmentation” and “no hidden-layer noise” to disambiguate effects.

- Question on objective design: can α be annealed to zero in CIO (curriculum vs maintaining proximity to network representable trajectories)?

- Notation/typos and clarity issues throughout (missing articles, acronyms before definition, misplaced symbols, missing words) that need fixing.

- Specific line queries: misplaced star in argmin/ȳs missing in C̃, “sX and aX are subsets of ϕX” justification, whether “single pass over the data” refers to Algorithm 1’s objective, Line 315 f vs f^{des}.

- Clarify modeling detail: what “root forces” arise from soft enforcement of dynamics constraints and under what conditions.

- Minor wording fixes suggested: “biped location”→locomotion; “unity”→unite/unify; other phrasing/grammar fixes.

- Table/figure inconsistency: Table 1a caption says 4 layers but architecture appears to have 3.

- Overall summary: promising, well-motivated method with demonstrable benefits from joint training and noise, but needs broader empirical comparisons, clearer CIO/Section 3.2 exposition, and run-time analysis to establish practical applicability.
