- Paper presents training recurrent neural network controllers from trajectory optimization to enable interactive real-time control of diverse simulated characters.
- Key contribution: single network architecture produces realistic, stable behaviors across varied morphologies and cyclic tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal).
- Needs clearer positioning relative to recent work (e.g., Levine & Abbeel) and explicit statement of advantages (learning speed, morphological adaptability, runtime efficiency, robustness).
- Should clarify whether benefits are in learning speed, adaptability to new morphologies, computational efficiency at runtime, or robustness to environment changes.
- The claim about “large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” needs explanation and justification for why the proposed method addresses it.
- Rationale for not using LQG should be reorganized and presented after introducing the proposed alternative for logical flow.
- Notation around lines 226–240 is confusing (use of bars and squiggles); simplify to improve readability.
- Explanation of “softer feedback gains” and the role of parameter λ is insufficient—define “soft” gains and why modifying the initial state is desirable.
- Strength: Versatility — controls many character types and locomotion modes without task-specific tuning or hand-designed controllers.
- Strength: Scalability — extends methods to complex 3D environments beyond prior 2D-focused work.
- Strength: Integration of techniques — supervised learning + trajectory optimization + noise injection is sensible for robustness and generalization.
- Strength: Real-time performance — neural policy enables interactive control suitable for animation and robotics.
- Weakness: Limited novelty — approach mainly applies existing techniques to broader problems and needs clearer articulation of new contributions.
- Weakness: Lacks thorough comparative analysis with recent reinforcement learning and policy optimization methods.
- Weakness: Explanations around feedback gain optimization and “soft” gains are unclear and require more detail.
- Weakness: Section 3.2 notation hampers readability and should be improved.
- Argument for acceptance: practical, effective method with demonstrated versatility and scalability.
- Argument against acceptance: incremental contribution, weak comparative evidence, and unresolved clarity issues.
- Overall assessment: solid, promising work but incremental; recommend weak accept contingent on stronger comparisons and clearer explanations.
