- Proposes an algorithm for real-time 3D character control where a neural network is trained offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs via alternating joint training.  
- Core contribution is alternating CIO and NN training, where each informs the other's optimization criterion.  
- Uses noise injection through data augmentation (perturbations) and additive hidden-layer noise to improve robustness.  
- Experiments show the network yields realistic, stable control across diverse character models.  
- Joint training and noise injection are significant contributions that appear to improve generalization.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: training data is small (100–200 trials with 5 branches each), risking overfitting.  
- The “no noise” variant’s overfitting is unsurprising given limited data; the “no joint” variant may also suffer from limited trajectory diversity.  
- Comparison to competing methods is limited to a brief, unreferenced MPC analysis; a systematic comparison to direct methods is missing.  
- Mathematical exposition is mostly clear but some parts (notably the CIO step, Eq. 6) are superficial or use undefined notation, hindering replication.  
- Releasing code would significantly aid reproducibility and community use.  
- Section 3.2 (optimal actions for noisy inputs) is confusing: notation is unclear and the time index t is inconsistently handled.  
- Include runtime measurements for real-time control and analysis of scaling with model complexity, as CPU cost can be a practical bottleneck.  
- Terminology and minor wording fixes needed (e.g., “biped location” → locomotion; “unity” → unite/unify; grammar on “asynchronous and stochastic setting”).  
- Clarify whether targets include only x,y,z coordinates and whether adding a target facing angle is supported or straightforward.  
- Prefer describing the procedure as “data augmentation” rather than “noise injection” since targets are recomputed for perturbed inputs.  
- Clarify Alg. 1 initialization: are initial X computed using random network weights θ or computed without the regularization term?  
- Justify using the same hyperparameter η in both steps of Alg. 1.  
- Define acronyms before use (LQG) and fix phrasing to “a Linear Quadratic Gaussian (LQG) pass.”  
- Correct mathematical/notation errors (misplaced star in argmin, missing \bar{s} in \tilde{C}, missing words in several sentences).  
- Explain claims like “sX and aX are subsets of φX”: what guarantees this?  
- Clarify that “a single pass over the data to reduce the objective in (4)” refers to optimizing only θ.  
- Note that the asynchronous joint optimization in Section 6 differs substantially from the alternating scheme in Alg. 1 and this should be flagged earlier.  
- Fix specific line errors (e.g., l.315: f → f^{des}).  
- Clarify the sentence about dynamics constraints being enforced softly and possible root forces in simulation.  
- Split the “no noise” experimental variant into separate “no data augmentation” and “no hidden noise injection” ablations.  
- Suggest investigating decreasing α to zero during CIO-only optimization to test whether benefits come from curriculum learning or continual closeness to the network’s capabilities.  
- Caption error: Table 1a states 4 layers but the network appears to have 3.  
- Overall assessment: promising application of NNs to character control, but more extensive comparisons, clearer exposition, and additional experiments are needed to establish practical benefits.
