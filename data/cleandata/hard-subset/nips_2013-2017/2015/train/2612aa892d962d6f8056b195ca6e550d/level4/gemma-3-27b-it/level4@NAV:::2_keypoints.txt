- Paper demonstrates a compact neural-network policy for real-time physics-based control across diverse cyclic motions and character morphologies.  
- Method uses trajectory optimization during training to produce control targets for the network.  
- Authors should better situate their work relative to existing trajectory-optimization + NN methods in prior literature.  
- Provide a direct comparison to recent RL/robotics work (e.g., Pieter Abbeel and Sergey Levine) and clarify distinguishing benefits (faster learning, body-type generality, runtime cost, robustness).  
- Clarify the claim that the process "can produce very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains."  
- Consider delaying the argument against using LQG until after presenting the implemented control strategy.  
- Fix unclear notation around lines 226–240 (definition s(X)=s̄ and cost expressed in s(X)−s̄).  
- Explain precisely what is meant by “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ,” what is being compared, and how “soft” is defined vs. “not soft.”  
- Justify and explain the rationale for modifying the initial state as part of the procedure.  
- Emphasize and clarify the paper’s novelty and clear distinctions from recent related research.
