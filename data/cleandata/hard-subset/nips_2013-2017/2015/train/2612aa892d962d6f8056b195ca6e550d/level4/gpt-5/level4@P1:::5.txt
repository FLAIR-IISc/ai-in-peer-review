This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks as near‑optimal feedback controllers by alternating trajectory optimization with supervised learning, and demonstrates real‑time, interactive control across diverse character morphologies. The results are promising.

Comments and suggestions:

- State the modeling assumptions upfront. The approach requires full knowledge of the dynamics and contact model (Eq. 6), with constraints enforced via a physics engine. This strong model‑based assumption should be clearly mentioned at the beginning (e.g., in the abstract or introduction) to set expectations and distinguish the method from model‑free approaches.

- Balance the exposition across Sections 4 and 6. As written, these sections are disproportionate. Section 6 (distributed training architecture) is detailed, while Section 4 (neural network policy regression), which is central to the contribution, is comparatively brief. Rebalancing would improve clarity.

- Clarify the role of recurrence in the policy. The abstract emphasizes “a small number of recurrent units that implement memory states beyond the physical system state,” but Section 4 does not describe the recurrent units or how the recurrent state r is updated and trained. Please explain the recurrent architecture, how recurrence is used during training and execution, and how it interfaces with the velocity/force outputs.

- Line 405: clarify in what sense the network is “stochastic.” The training data and inputs are stochastically perturbed (sensor noise ε and hidden‑unit noise γ) during training, but the policy itself appears deterministic at test time. If the network is intended to be stochastic, please specify the source of stochasticity at execution and its purpose; otherwise, consider rephrasing to avoid confusion.
