- Proposes training a recurrent neural network as a feedback controller to imitate an offline trajectory optimizer for real-time control of complex simulated characters.
- Demonstrates a unified method that automatically generates high-quality controllers across diverse morphologies (bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, flyers), removing need for hand-crafted controllers.
- Introduces extracting optimal feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer to produce richer supervised training targets.
- Uses principled noise injection into sensory inputs and hidden activations during training to improve robustness; ablation study shows joint optimization and noise are essential.
- Empirically produces stable, realistic locomotion and natural gaits without explicit encoding, supported by strong results and video.
- Relies on access to a full dynamics model and derivatives (strong assumption) which should be explicitly stated up front.
- Lacks clarity on the recurrent network architecture and how recurrent states (r, r_dot) are implemented — critical for reproducibility.
- Terminology is misleading: calling the policy a "stochastic neural network" is confusing since execution is deterministic and stochasticity is only during training.
- Paper organization is imbalanced—trajectory optimization is detailed while the neural network regression and distributed training sections omit important implementation/training details (optimizers, learning rates, batch sizes).
