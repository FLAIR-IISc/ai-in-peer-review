The authors propose a strategy for embedding feedback gains within neural network policies, resulting in robust performance despite disturbances and regression errors. They softly couple trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for deviation, enabling the policy to learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories – approximations that are, in practice, simpler and more robust than those produced by model predictive control (MPC)-based trajectory optimization alone. The tasks are optimized using sophisticated parallelization, though this is presented as a means to an end rather than a primary contribution. Ultimately, the trained feedback controllers successfully control a diverse set of robots.

This paper is of the highest quality, exceptionally clear, and represents a significant technical achievement. The results are particularly compelling in their applications. This is a beautiful and inspiring work that effectively merges graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers for a wide range of body morphologies and tasks. Bravo.
