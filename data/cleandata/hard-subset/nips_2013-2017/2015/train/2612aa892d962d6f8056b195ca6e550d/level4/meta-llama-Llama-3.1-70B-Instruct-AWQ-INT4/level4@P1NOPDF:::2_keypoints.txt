- Demonstrates that a small, fast neural network can represent interactive control policies learned via trajectory optimization and that the same architecture works across varied cyclic behaviors and body types.  
- Paper’s positioning relative to prior trajectory-optimization + neural network work is unclear and must be clarified.  
- Should explicitly compare to recent work by Abbeel and Levine: address speed of learning, applicability to varied body shapes, runtime computation, and robustness to environmental variability.  
- The novelty/contribution relative to recent related work is not clearly stated.  
- The claim about producing “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” needs more context and explanation.  
- Recommend moving the discussion of why LQG was not used to after the proposed method to better motivate the approach.  
- Notation around lines 226–240 (s(X) = \bar s and costs using s(X) - \bar s) is confusing; revise bars/squiggles for clarity.  
- The statement that the approach yields “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” requires definition of “softer,” comparison to other methods, and justification; also explain benefits of changing the initial state during the procedure.
