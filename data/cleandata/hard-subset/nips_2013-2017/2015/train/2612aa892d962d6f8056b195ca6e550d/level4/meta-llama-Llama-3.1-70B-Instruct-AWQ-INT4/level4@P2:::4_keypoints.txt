- Proposes a novel method for interactive control of complex characters by jointly learning trajectories and policy via alternating subproblem optimization.  
- Decoupling into alternating trajectory optimization and policy learning enables reuse of established methods and practical implementation.  
- Demonstrates strong technical sophistication across conceptual framing and implementation details.  
- Empirical results are impressive and show the approach works well in practice.  
- Shows clear benefits of using sensory noise during training and of learning trajectories jointly with the policy.  
- Paper is well-written with appropriate detail and useful cross-references between sections.  
- Motivation for the extra optimization step in Section 7 is unclear and needs clearer explanation.  
- There may be residual trajectory cost or policy regression error motivating Section 7, but clarification is required.  
- Provide one or two references to support the claim "similar to recent approaches" (line 153).  
- Caution that keeping updates small in parameter space is not generally sensible, though it is useful here due to block-alternating optimization.  
- Results and optimization scheme appear sensible, but the reviewer lacks domain expertise to fully assess novelty and significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong technical execution, impressive results, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: uncertain novelty/significance without further expertise, missing references, and unclear motivation for Section 7.
