- Introduces a novel method that trains neural-network controllers by interleaving trajectory optimization (CIO) and network training rather than doing them sequentially.  
- Demonstrates real-time controllers for diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without task-specific features, state machines, or motion capture.  
- Uses noise injection during training to improve robustness and generalization of policies.  
- Proposes a principled method to compute optimal feedback gains for noisy inputs, improving robustness without extra cost terms.  
- Presents a distributed training architecture that scales to complex 3D characters and speeds up training.  
- Technical approach is well-motivated and clearly explained (joint optimization algorithm and feedback gain computation).  
- Experimental results convincingly show diverse, stable behaviors and generalization across morphologies.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate the necessity of both joint optimization and noise injection for good performance.  
- Comparison with alternative methods is limited — MPC comparison is only a single figure without comprehensive analysis or timing data.  
- Lacks broader comparisons to reinforcement learning baselines (e.g., TRPO, PPO) to support claims of superiority.  
- Some technical notation (Section 3.2) is confusing, especially the time index "t" and perturbation vs. feedback gain relationship.  
- Execution details for real-time policy runtime (computational complexity and timing) are insufficiently specified.  
- The claim about softly enforced dynamics constraints and possible root forces in simulation needs elaboration.  
- Paper is original and significant, extending trajectory optimization methods to diverse 3D neural controllers.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on expanded comparisons, clarification of Section 3.2, and inclusion of timing/real-time execution data.
