- Presents a method for real-time control of diverse 3D characters by training neural nets to mimic Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs.  
- Key innovations are an alternating CIO/network training loop and strategic noise injection via data augmentation and hidden-layer additive noise.  
- Demonstrates realistic, stable controllers across swimmers, flyers, bipeds, and quadrupeds.  
- Joint training and noise strategies substantially improve generalization beyond the straightforward supervise-CIO baseline.  
- Training data is limited (100–200 trials × 5 branches), likely causing overfitting in the "no noise" variant; more trajectories are needed.  
- The "no joint" variant also needs more diverse trajectories to fairly assess its shortcomings.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief MPC comparison is shown and lacks proper citation and systematic baselines.  
- Mathematical exposition is mostly clear but omits important details; replication requires consulting external references, especially for CIO implementation.  
- Equation 6 and other CIO-related notation lack proper definitions and clarity.  
- Section 3.2 has significant notational confusion, notably inconsistent handling or dropping of the time index t.  
- Authors should release source code to aid reproducibility and community adoption.  
- Report real-time control computation times (CPU) to assess practical feasibility for applications like games.  
- The term "noise injection" is misleading for the target-recomputed perturbations and should be described as "data augmentation."  
- Clarify whether initial network weights are used in iteration 1 of Algorithm 1 and justify reusing the same hyperparameter η in both algorithm steps.  
- Fix multiple minor wording/typo issues (e.g., "biped location" → "biped locomotion", "unity" misuse) and grammatical errors.  
- Clarify modeling choices: current target seems to be position only (x,y,z); consider adding orientation constraints.  
- Resolve notation inconsistencies: time index t, \bar{s} in \tilde{C}, relation between sX and aX as subsets of φX, and correct f^{des} formatting.  
- Explain discrepancy between asynchronous optimization in Section 6 and the alternating scheme in Algorithm 1.  
- Clarify statement about dynamics constraints being enforced softly and whether α can be annealed to zero (curriculum effect).  
- Split "no noise" ablation into "no data augmentation" and "no hidden noise injection" for clearer analysis.
