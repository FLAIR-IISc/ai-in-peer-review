- Summary: Alternates Contact-Invariant trajectory optimization with supervised recurrent NN training, coupling them via penalties and noisy-data imitation to produce real-time controllers for diverse 3D characters.

- Key technical contributions: perturbation-based extraction of local feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer and an asynchronous distributed training scheme.

- Execution: Online T=1 optimization enforces dynamics while tracking NN outputs (velocities, memory, contact forces) for real-time control.

- Strength: Clean, practical integration of trajectory optimization and policy learning that regularizes both components and improves robustness.

- Strength: Stochastic training with input-noise + corrective actions and hidden-layer noise reduces compounding errors in closed-loop rollouts.

- Strength: Novel, efficient method to compute feedback gains from trajectory optimizers, likely of independent interest.

- Strength: Single network architecture and shared hyperparameters succeed across multiple morphologies and behaviors.

- Strength: Useful ablations demonstrate the importance of joint training and noise injection.

- Strength: Distributed, asynchronous implementation with GPU training is a practical systems contribution.

- Weakness: Limited comparative evaluation — no quantitative baselines vs. contemporary RL or direct methods and only a brief qualitative MPC comparison.

- Weakness: Small training dataset (100–200 trials) raises concerns about overfitting and limits assessment of generalization.

- Weakness: Section 3.2 derivation is confusing: dense notation, missing time indices, and unclear time-dependent quantities impede reproducibility.

- Weakness: Several implementation choices lack justification or clarity (same proximal weight η, trajectory initialization, orientation treatment, T=1 runtime/scaling).

- Weakness: Asynchronous training in practice departs from the algorithmic alternation; convergence behavior under stale updates is not discussed.

- Quality: Technically sound with thoughtful engineering, but CIO setup and key derivations are only sketched; more explicit definitions, pseudocode, or code would aid reproducibility.

- Clarity: Generally clear writing, but overloaded notation and missing symbol definitions (notably in Sec.3.2 and Eq.6) need fixing.

- Originality: Novel combination of known components for 3D character control; feedback-gain extraction from direct optimizers is a notable original element.

- Significance: Promising, practical results across morphologies, but impact is limited by sparse quantitative comparisons and small training sets.

- Pros for acceptance: elegant optimization-learning integration, robust multi-morphology behaviors, and practical training/feedback-gain innovations.

- Cons against acceptance: limited quantitative baselines, clarity/derivation issues, and missing runtime/scaling measurements for real-time claims.

- Requested clarifications: add quantitative comparisons to MPC and policy optimization baselines; report task metrics (completion, energy, robustness).

- Requested clarifications: rewrite Sec.3.2 with time indices, full symbol definitions, dimensionalities, and clear role of λ for conditioning.

- Requested clarifications: state initial trajectory optimization procedure, justify shared η, report sensitivity, and separate no-noise ablations into input vs hidden-layer effects.

- Requested clarifications: report online per-step runtime, CPU/GPU usage, and scaling with model complexity and contact count.

- Editorial: fix typos (e.g., “biped location” → “biped locomotion”), define LQG, correct Eq.6/argmin notation, and note asynchronous updates earlier.
