- Trains RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, walking) across varying morphologies.  
- Uses trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data and distill optimized behaviors into a learnable policy.  
- Introduces soft coupling of trajectory optimization and policy learning via a cost penalty, improving robustness and generalization.  
- Demonstrates a distributed training architecture to manage the approach’s high computational demands.  
- Positions work within control/ML literature and extends prior trajectory-optimization + NN methods from 2D to challenging 3D locomotion tasks.  
- Explicitly addresses historical neural-network-control challenges (data scarcity, real-valued outputs, closed-loop operation, overfitting).  
- Strength — Technical soundness: well-motivated, rigorous formulation with noise injection and computation of optimal feedback gains.  
- Strength — Clarity: exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible with clear algorithm descriptions.  
- Strength — Originality: novel combination of methods; computing feedback gains as a byproduct is an insightful innovation.  
- Strength — Significance: generates stable, realistic locomotion across morphologies and has cross-disciplinary impact potential.  
- Weakness — Computational cost: requires substantial cloud compute and long training times, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks detailed sensitivity analysis to understand parameter impacts.  
- Weakness — Limited comparative analysis: needs broader comparisons to MPC, direct RL, and other state-of-the-art control methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a hard problem with significant, well-supported technical contributions and excellent presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to cost and insufficient comparative evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: Outstanding paper; strengths substantially outweigh limitations.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
