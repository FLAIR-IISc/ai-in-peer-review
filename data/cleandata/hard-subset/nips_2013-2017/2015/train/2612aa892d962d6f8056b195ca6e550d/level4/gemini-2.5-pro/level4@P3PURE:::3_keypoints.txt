- Proposes a real-time 3D character control algorithm where a neural network mimics a Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) planner and is trained jointly with the planner in an alternating scheme.
- Core contribution: joint alternating training of CIO planner and neural network, where each component’s output guides the other.
- Core contribution: noise injection via data augmentation (input perturbations) and additive noise in hidden layers.
- Experiments show the network produces realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.
- Joint training and noise injection are identified as crucial for achieving good generalization.
- Training data is limited (≈100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), making the results less convincing and explaining overfitting in the "no noise" ablation.
- The "no joint" ablation might perform better with a larger, more diverse dataset.
- Comparative evaluation is limited; only a brief, uncited comparison to an MPC-based method is provided.
- Mathematical formulation is generally clear but some components are described superficially, impeding reproducibility.
- Notations in the CIO step (Eq. 6) are not fully defined and Section 3.2 is difficult to follow due to confusing notation and omitted time indices.
- Releasing source code would greatly benefit reproducibility and the community.
- Computation times for the real-time control phase are not reported; scaling with model complexity is critical for practical applications.
- Typos and wording issues: "biped location" → "biped locomotion"; "unity" → "unite/unify", and other grammatical errors.
- Question whether the control target includes final orientation; orientation constraints appear missing and should be addressed for practical use.
- The term "noise injection" for recomputed-target perturbations is imprecise; this is better described as "data augmentation" and distinct from denoising autoencoders.
- Ambiguity in Algorithm 1: whether initial random network weights are used to compute the first trajectories or initial trajectories are computed without network regularization.
- Unclear justification for using the same hyperparameter (eta) for both optimization steps in Algorithm 1.
- Several small editorial/math errors: missing "the" in Section 5, LQG acronym used before definition, missing articles in Section 3.2, misplaced star in argmin (line 231), possibly missing \bar{s} in cost \tilde{C}.
- Unclear statements in Section 3.2 (e.g., incomplete phrase "the minimizer of a quadratic around optimal trajectory") and questionable claims (why sX and aX are subsets of φX).
- In Section 4, reference to "a single pass over the data to reduce the objective in (4)" likely refers to the Algorithm 1 objective; this should be clarified.
- The asynchronous joint optimization scheme (Section 6) differs substantially from the alternating scheme in Algorithm 1 and this distinction should be made earlier.
- Possible typo in line 315: 'f' should be 'f^{des}'.
- Sentence about dynamics constraints being enforced softly and including root forces is unclear and needs clarification.
- The "no noise" ablation should be split into two variants: "no data augmentation" and "no hidden-layer noise" for clearer analysis.
- Suggestion for future work: annealing alpha to zero during CIO to test whether network regularization acts as a curriculum or remains beneficial.
- Caption mismatch: Table 1a states a 4-layer network while text describes a 3-layer network.
- Overall: promising application of neural networks to character control, but requires larger datasets, better comparisons, clearer notation, runtime reporting, and editorial fixes to establish practical benefits.
