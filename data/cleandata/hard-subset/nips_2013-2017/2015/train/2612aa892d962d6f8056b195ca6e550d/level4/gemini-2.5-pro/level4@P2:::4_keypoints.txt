- Proposes training an RNN as a real-time feedback controller by alternating joint optimization of optimal trajectories and a policy network, combining trajectory optimization with supervised policy learning.  
- Introduces principled noise injection during training to improve robustness.  
- Presents a technique to extract optimal feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer to guide learning.  
- Demonstrates a single unified architecture producing stable, realistic locomotion for diverse morphologies (bipeds, quadrupeds, swimmers, fliers) without task-specific engineering.  
- Strong technical execution: combines Contact-Invariant Optimization with a stochastic RNN and distributed alternating optimization.  
- Strength: emergence of natural gaits and generalization across character types, outperforming hand-crafted controllers.  
- Strength: convincing empirical validation and ablations showing joint optimization and noise are critical.  
- Strength: novel integration where policy regularizes trajectories and improving trajectories produce better policy data.  
- Clarification requested: why execution requires solving an optimization per timestep rather than direct feed-forward policy output (physics constraints vs. implicit integrator/practicality)?  
- Comment: missing specific citations for the claim linking quadratic proximal regularization to recent stochastic optimization approaches.  
- Minor typo: "to unity these disparate approaches" should be "to unify these disparate approaches."  
- Originality: builds on prior work but makes a significant contribution by scaling to complex 3D locomotion and providing a novel method for extracting feedback gains.  
- Overall: excellent, technically significant, visually impressive, and likely influential — recommend acceptance.
