The author begins by proposing a strategy for embedding feedback gains into neural network policies, which makes them robust against disturbance and regression error. trajectories are gradually added by cost penalties for optimization and policy optimization deviations, allowing the policy to learn reasonable estimates of optimal trajectories that are actually simpler and more robust than the products of MPC-based trajectory optimization on their own. Tasks are optimized using sophisticated parallelism, although this is not the central focus of the paper and is an end means. Finally, a diverse set of robots is controlled using trained feedback controllers.

This paper is of the highest quality, incredibly clear, an excellent demonstration of technology, and presents quite beautiful results in its applications. Bravo. It is a beautiful body of work that combines graphics, control, neural networks, and the production of controllers for various types of body geometry and functions. It is inspiring.
