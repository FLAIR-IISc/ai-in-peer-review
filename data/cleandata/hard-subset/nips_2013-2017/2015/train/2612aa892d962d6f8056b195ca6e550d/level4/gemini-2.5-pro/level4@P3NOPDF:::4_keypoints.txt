- Proposes jointly learning optimized trajectories and a policy for interactive control of simulated characters, using alternating optimization of trajectory optimization and policy learning, producing impressive results.
- Technical sophistication and strong implementation lead to visually compelling, fine-grained control over complex characters.
- Empirical validation clearly supports key design choices, notably the use of sensory noise during training and joint trajectory-policy learning.
- Paper is well-written and well-structured, providing appropriate detail and helpful cross-references.
- Motivation for the additional online optimization step (Section 7) is unclear; the reviewer asks whether it’s merely to correct residual errors/drift and requests a clearer discussion of trade-offs and necessity.
- Line 153’s claim that small parameter updates are “similar to recent approaches” needs one or two specific citations for grounding.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed system with strong results, but appears to be an effective combination of existing techniques rather than a fundamental theoretical breakthrough; reviewer unsure of long-term impact given limited subfield expertise.
- Minor comment: possible typo or missing word at Line 89 after “…to unify”.
