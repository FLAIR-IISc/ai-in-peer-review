- Core contribution: joint learning of trajectories and policies for interactive control of complex characters via alternating subproblem optimization.
- Decoupling trajectories and policies allows reuse of existing methods and is effective in practice.
- Reviewer not sufficiently familiar with the domain to assess impact; views the work as a technical achievement without novel theoretical insights.
- High technical sophistication in concepts and implementation with impressive results.
- Empirical demonstration that training with sensory noise and learning trajectories jointly with the policy are beneficial.
- Paper is well-written, appropriately detailed, and sections are well-structured with helpful cross-references.
- Motivation for the additional optimization step in Section 7 is unclear; expected the policy to be executable without extra optimization.
- Reviewer questions whether residual trajectory cost or policy regression error necessitates re-optimization, noting dynamics should enforce physical constraints.
- Acknowledges that re-optimizing at every time step can enable larger time steps.
- Suggests adding one or two references where similarity to recent methods is claimed (line 153).
- Finds the claim that small parameter-space updates are beneficial counterintuitive but understandable in the alternating-subproblem context.
- Notes a minor typo on line 89 ("to unify").
- Overall: results and optimization scheme seem sensible and impressive, but novelty and significance are uncertain.
