- Paper proposes an NN that mimics Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) for real-time 3D character control, with alternating joint training between CIO and the NN and noise injection/data augmentation.
- Joint training and hidden-layer noise injection are substantive contributions that empirically improve generalization.
- Experiments use a small training set (≈100–200 trials × 5 branches), raising concerns that results (and ablations like “no noise”) may reflect overfitting.
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief, unreferenced comparison to MPC and no systematic comparisons to other methods.
- Mathematical exposition is mostly clear but key parts (especially the CIO step and Eq. 6) lack definition and detail needed for replication.
- Section 3.2 is confusing: notation drops the time index and it’s unclear which quantities depend on time or what is computed.
- The authors should release code to aid reproducibility and clarify implementation details.
- Timing and runtime scaling with model complexity are not reported but are critical for real-time/game settings.
- Terminology and presentation issues: several typos, missing words, misordered/missing notation (LQG, argmin/star placement, Table 1a layer count), and awkward phrasing.
- Clarify conceptual distinctions: data augmentation vs. noise injection (denoising autoencoder claim is misleading) and split the “no noise” ablation into “no data augmentation” vs “no hidden-layer noise.”
- Algorithmic questions remain: how Alg.1 initializes X (with random θ or without regularizer), why the same η is used in both steps, and differences between alternating Alg.1 and the asynchronous joint scheme in Section 6.
- Suggest experiment: test annealing α to zero to see if benefits are from curriculum learning or persistent regularization.
- Overall assessment: strong and visually compelling application, but practical benefits, broader applicability, and comparative/replication evidence need strengthening.
