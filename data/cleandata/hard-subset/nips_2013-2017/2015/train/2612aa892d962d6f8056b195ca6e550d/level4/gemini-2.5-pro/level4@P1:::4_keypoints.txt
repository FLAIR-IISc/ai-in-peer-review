- Combines offline trajectory optimization with online policy learning via a block-alternating joint optimization of trajectories and a neural policy.  
- Injects noise during training and uses the trajectory optimizer to compute optimal feedback gains.  
- Impressive technical execution and high-quality results controlling diverse characters (swimmers, flyers, bipeds, quadrupeds) with stable, realistic behaviors.  
- Thorough empirical validation: ablations show benefits of joint trajectory-policy optimization and noise injection; convincing comparison to static-dataset training.  
- Well-written and organized; complex system is clearly decomposed with helpful cross-references.  
- Clarify motivation for performing a single-timestep optimization at test time instead of using the learned policy directly (correction of regression errors vs enforcing physical constraints; larger timesteps noted but primary reason unclear).  
- Contribution is largely an engineering/integation advance of existing techniques rather than new theoretical insight; repositioning could help.  
- Minor: add citations for the proximal regularization claim (line 153).  
- Minor typo: "unity" should be "unify" (line 89).  
- Overall: compelling, well-executed paper that advances the state of the art in interactive character control; recommend acceptance.
