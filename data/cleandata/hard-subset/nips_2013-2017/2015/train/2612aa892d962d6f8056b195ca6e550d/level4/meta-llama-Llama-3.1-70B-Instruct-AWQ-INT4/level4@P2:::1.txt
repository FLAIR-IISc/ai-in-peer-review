This paper proposes a novel approach to training neural networks as feedback controllers for a wide range of dynamical systems and tasks, including swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking with different body morphologies. The method combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO), to generate stable and realistic behaviors. The authors also introduce a technique for injecting noise during training to improve robustness and prevent overfitting.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The use of figures and equations helps to illustrate the key concepts and makes the paper easier to follow. The authors also provide a detailed description of their experimental setup and results, which demonstrates the effectiveness of their approach.
