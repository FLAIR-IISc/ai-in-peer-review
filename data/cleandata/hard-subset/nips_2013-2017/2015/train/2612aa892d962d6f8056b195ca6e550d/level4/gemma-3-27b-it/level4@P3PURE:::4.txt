This paper proposes to combine trajectory optimization with policy learning to enable interactive control of complex characters. The central idea is to jointly learn trajectories and a control policy, with the empirical finding that decoupling the two optimization problems into alternating subproblems – trajectory optimization and policy update – is both effective in practice and allows for the reuse of well-established methods. While I followed the paper’s development well, my limited familiarity with this specific domain prevents me from fully assessing its potential impact. However, the work certainly demonstrates a significant degree of technical skill, though it does not present novel theoretical insights.

The authors combine an impressive level of technical sophistication, spanning both conceptual design and implementation, with correspondingly strong experimental results. I found the claimed benefits of i) incorporating sensory noise during training and ii) learning trajectories in conjunction with the policy to be clearly demonstrated.

The paper is also clearly written, providing an appropriate level of detail given the complexity of the problem and its implementation. Each section focuses on a distinct aspect of the work, and the cross-references between sections are particularly helpful. 

I believe the motivation for the additional optimization step described in Section 7 could be more thoroughly explained. Given the preceding sections, I initially anticipated that the learned policy would operate without requiring further optimization. Is this extra step necessary due to a residual trajectory cost, or policy regression error, such that directly applying the policy’s actions to the physical simulator would lead to accumulated errors? I understand that the forward evolution of the dynamics incorporates physical constraints to prevent implausible behavior, but I may be overlooking a key consideration. I do appreciate, however, that re-optimizing at each timestep allows for the use of larger timesteps. 

On line 153, the phrase “similar to recent approaches…” would benefit from one or two relevant citations. Furthermore, it is initially counterintuitive that small updates in parameter space would generally be desirable; however, I came to understand that this approach is beneficial in the context of your block-alternating optimization scheme.

The paper’s results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and its implementation appear sensible. However, this research area is somewhat outside my primary expertise, making it difficult for me to confidently evaluate the novelty and overall significance of the work.
