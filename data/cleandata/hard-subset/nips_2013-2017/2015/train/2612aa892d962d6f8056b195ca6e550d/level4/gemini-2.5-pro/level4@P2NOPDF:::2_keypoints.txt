- Paper proposes a fast neural-network policy trained offline from trajectory-optimized example motions to control diverse simulated characters.  
- Contribution appears to be an application of known techniques rather than a fundamental advance in trajectory optimization or neural control.  
- Core components (trajectory optimization discovering controls; networks approximating state-action mappings) are already explored in prior work.  
- Paper needs clearer articulation of specific novel contributions and positioning relative to closely related work (e.g., Levine, Abbeel).  
- Authors should explicitly state advantages (e.g., faster learning, broader applicability, lower runtime cost, improved robustness) and provide evidence.  
- Clarify and elaborate on the claim about producing “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains”: when it occurs and how the method mitigates it.  
- Consider reorganizing: present the proposed method first, then justify design choices like not using LQG as comparative rationale.  
- Improve notation around lines 226–240: similar symbols (bars, squiggles) make equations hard to parse—use more distinct symbols.  
- Explain the statement that the method “produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ”: define “softer,” specify baseline, and justify why desirable.  
- Clarify the motivation and benefits of modifying the initial state during training for final policy quality and learning.  
- Overall: technically sound with compelling results, but weakened by insufficient explanation of novelties and comparisons to recent related work.
