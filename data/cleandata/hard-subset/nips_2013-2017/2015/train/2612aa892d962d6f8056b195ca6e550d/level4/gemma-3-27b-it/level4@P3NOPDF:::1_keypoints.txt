- Proposes embedding feedback gains directly inside neural network policies to achieve robust control against disturbances and model error.
- Soft-couples trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a penalty for deviation, allowing policies to learn simplified approximations of optimal trajectories.
- Learned policies demonstrate greater robustness than traditional MPC-based approaches.
- Parallelization techniques are used to handle optimization compute, presented as an implementation detail rather than the main contribution.
- Successfully applies the method to a diverse set of robotic morphologies and tasks, showing broad applicability.
- High technical quality and clear presentation throughout the paper.
- Novelty comes from learning feedback gains within the policy rather than fixing them as parameters.
- The soft-coupling mechanism prevents overconstraining policies to brittle optimized trajectories.
- Strong experimental evaluation with compelling visual results that intuitively demonstrate learned behaviors.
- No significant weaknesses identified; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
