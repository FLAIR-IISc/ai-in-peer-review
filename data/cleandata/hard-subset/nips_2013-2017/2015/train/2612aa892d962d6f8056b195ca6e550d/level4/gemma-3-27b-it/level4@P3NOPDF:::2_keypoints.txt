- Concern that the work mainly applies existing trajectory optimization + neural network ideas and needs clearer articulation of specific novel contributions beyond prior literature.  
- Recommend a detailed comparison to Pieter Abbeel and Sergey Levine’s work and explicit statement of how this method differs (e.g., learning speed, generality, runtime cost, robustness).  
- Claim that a single network generalizes across cyclic behaviors and body types is promising but requires stronger justification relative to existing methods.  
- “Very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” needs quantitative definition and examples to clarify the issue.  
- The rationale for not using an LQG approach should be presented after the proposed method to better justify that choice.  
- Notation in lines 226–240 (s(X)=\bar s and cost expressed in s(X)-\bar s) is visually confusing and should be revised for clarity.  
- The phrase “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” is ambiguous; define “soft” vs “hard” gains and explain why softer gains are desirable.  
- The purpose and rationale for intentionally perturbing the initial state in the procedure must be explained more clearly.  
- Overall: technically sound with notable generalization results, but needs stronger comparisons to related work and clearer definitions/notations to improve impact and readability.
