- Proposal: train NNs offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs for real-time 3D control using an alternating CIO↔NN training loop.  
- Key technique: interleaving CIO and NN training so each step informs the other’s optimization criterion.  
- Noise strategy: inject noise via small perturbation data augmentation and additive noise in NN hidden layers to improve generalization.  
- Empirical result: produces realistic, stable control policies across a diverse set of character models.  
- Architecture strength: single network architecture successfully handles varied character morphologies.  
- Dataset limitation: training set (≈100–200 trials with 5 branches each) is relatively small and may cause overfitting (notably in the no-noise variant).  
- Comparative evaluation weak: brief, uncited MPC comparison and lack of systematic comparisons to RL and other direct methods.  
- Reproducibility concerns: CIO step (e.g., Eq. 6) and Section 3.2 are unclear; some notation (missing time index t) and definitions are omitted—code release recommended.  
- Runtime/latency missing: no measurements of computational cost or scaling with model complexity, limiting practical assessment.  
- Algorithm clarity issues: discrepancy between asynchronous optimization discussion and Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme; unclear justification for shared hyperparameter η and weight initialization in Algorithm 1.  
- Notational and editorial issues: ambiguous phrase about dynamics constraints being enforced softly and other clarity problems in Sec. 3.2.  
- Minor corrections: wording fixes (“biped location”→locomotion, “unity”→unify), typos (lines 231, 315, table captions), and grammatical errors need addressing.  
- Ablation suggestion: test decreasing α to zero during optimization to evaluate the importance of CIO-learned trajectories.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel CIO+NN alternating training, technically sound formulations, significant potential impact, and strong cross-morphology generalization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons to baselines, small dataset risking overfitting, clarity issues in key sections, and missing runtime/practical considerations.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept pending expanded comparative experiments, larger/more diverse training data, clearer technical exposition, and code or runtime details.
