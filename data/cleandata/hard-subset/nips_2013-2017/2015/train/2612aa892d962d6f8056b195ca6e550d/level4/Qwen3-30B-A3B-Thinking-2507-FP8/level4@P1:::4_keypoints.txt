- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal).  
- Key innovation: jointly optimizes trajectories and neural policies via an alternating procedure interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization.  
- Avoids traditional reinforcement learning; uses trajectory optimization to guide policy learning.  
- Demonstrates stable, realistic behaviors across multiple morphologies without motion capture, task-specific features, or hand-crafted state machines.  
- Controller architecture uses feed-forward and recurrent units, with actions defined as velocity rather than a direct control policy.  
- Builds on Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) and extends prior 2D-limited methods to successful 3D locomotion.  
- Technical contribution 1: principled noise injection during training to improve robustness.  
- Technical contribution 2: technique to obtain optimal feedback gains as a byproduct of trajectory optimization.  
- Technical contribution 3: distributed training architecture for efficient scaling to complex problems.  
- Method handles unexpected task changes and maintains stability despite limited training data.  
- Strong theoretical analysis, including detailed derivations of feedback gains and noise injection rationale.  
- Comprehensive experiments demonstrating effectiveness across multiple character types in 3D.  
- Ablation studies clearly demonstrate the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection for stable, generalizable policies.  
- Comparison with MPC shows the method produces more predictable, less chaotic behaviors via trajectory regularization.  
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with effective figures (e.g., diverse behaviors in Figure 1).  
- Originality: novel combination of trajectory optimization with neural policy learning for interactive control; distinguishes from related 2D-limited work.  
- Significance: enables interactive control across morphologies without task-specific engineering, with broad robotics, animation, and biomechanics applications.  
- Minor weakness: needs clearer explanation of why physical state evolution is optimized each timestep rather than using policy outputs directly.  
- Minor weakness: limited discussion of computational trade-offs between trajectory optimization and neural network training.  
- Could further emphasize potential robotics applications with more specific examples.  
- Overall assessment: strong technical contribution, robust implementation, compelling results; recommendation for acceptance.
