This paper proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning to enable interaction control of complex characters. The main proposal is to learn both the trajectory and the policy simultaneously, along with additional empirical insights that, if the two optimization problems are divided into sequential sub-problems, i) existing, well-documented methods can be reused, and ii) it works reasonably well in practice. Although I understand the paper well, I am not proficient enough to comment on the potential impact of this paper, as it appears to be a technical skill externally, even though it lacks new theoretical insights.

The authors have combined impressive levels of technical sophistication at all stages, from concept to implementation, with equally impressive results. I clearly saw the benefits claimed of using sensory noise during training and learning the trajectory in conjunction with the policy.

I also saw that the paper is clearly written, and considering the complexity of the problem and its implementation, I think it is adequately explained in detail. Each section discusses a separate aspect of the problem, and there are very useful cross-references between the sections.
