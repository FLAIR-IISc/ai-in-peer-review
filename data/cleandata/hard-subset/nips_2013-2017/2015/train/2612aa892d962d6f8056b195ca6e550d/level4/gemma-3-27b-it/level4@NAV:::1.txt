The authors introduce a novel approach to incorporating feedback gains directly into neural network policies, enhancing robustness to external disturbances and mitigating regression errors. This is achieved through a soft coupling of trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a deviation cost, enabling the policy to learn effective, and importantly, simpler and more robust control solutions than those typically generated by Model Predictive Control (MPC). While the work leverages advanced parallelization techniques to facilitate optimization, this aspect serves primarily as an implementation detail. The resulting feedback controllers are successfully demonstrated across a range of robotic platforms.

This is an exceptionally strong paper, characterized by its clarity, technical depth, and compelling results. The work represents a significant achievement, elegantly integrating techniques from graphics, control theory, and neural networks to generate controllers adaptable to diverse robot morphologies and tasks. It is a truly inspiring contribution.
