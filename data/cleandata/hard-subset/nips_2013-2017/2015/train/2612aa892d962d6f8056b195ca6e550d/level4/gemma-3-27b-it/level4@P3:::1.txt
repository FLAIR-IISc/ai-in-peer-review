This paper presents a compelling method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a diverse range of dynamical systems, including swimming, flying, and walking robots with varying morphologies. The core idea is to leverage trajectory optimization (specifically, Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data for a neural network, effectively distilling complex, optimized behaviors into a learnable policy. A key innovation lies in the soft coupling of trajectory optimization and policy learning via a cost penalty, enhancing robustness and allowing the neural network to generalize effectively. The authors also demonstrate a sophisticated distributed training architecture to handle the computational demands of this approach.

This work builds upon a significant body of research in control, reinforcement learning, and deep learning. Traditional methods for generating complex controllers often rely on manual design, motion capture, or task-specific state machines – limitations the authors aim to overcome. The paper correctly positions itself within the context of recent work combining trajectory optimization and neural networks [9, 11], but importantly demonstrates success in significantly more challenging three-dimensional locomotion tasks where prior work was limited to two dimensions. It also draws connections to earlier neural network control approaches [3, 6, 14] and highlights the benefits of their trajectory-optimization-based method in producing more realistic and purposeful behaviors. The historical context of neural network control and the challenges of applying deep learning to control problems (data scarcity, real-valued outputs, closed-loop operation, overfitting) are well articulated.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The method is well-motivated and rigorously described. The combination of trajectory optimization and neural network learning is cleverly implemented, and the inclusion of noise injection and optimal feedback gains demonstrates a careful consideration of practical challenges. The mathematical formulation and algorithmic description are clear and concise.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The authors effectively explain complex concepts in an accessible manner. The inclusion of a detailed overview, algorithm description, and clear explanations of the individual components (trajectory optimization, noise injection, distributed optimization) significantly enhances readability.
* **Originality:** While building on existing techniques, the paper presents a novel and effective combination of methods. The application of this approach to a diverse set of locomotion tasks in 3D space, and the demonstrated success in generating stable and realistic behaviors, represent a significant contribution. The method for computing optimal feedback gains as a byproduct of trajectory optimization is a particularly insightful innovation.
* **Significance:** The results are highly significant. The ability to generate stable and realistic locomotion controllers for various body morphologies using a single, unified approach is a major advance. This work has the potential to impact fields such as robotics, animation, biomechanics, and neuroscience. The scalability enabled by the distributed training architecture further enhances the practical relevance of the method.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** The paper acknowledges the high computational cost of the training process, requiring cloud computing and hours of training time. While the distributed architecture mitigates this to some extent, it remains a significant barrier to entry for researchers without access to substantial computational resources.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors provide specific values for algorithmic constants, a more detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity would be valuable. Understanding the impact of different parameter settings on performance could further improve the robustness and generalizability of the method.
* **Limited Comparative Analysis:** The paper includes a brief comparison to static neural network training and ablation studies, but a more comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art control methods (e.g., MPC, direct reinforcement learning) would strengthen the evaluation.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a challenging problem with a novel and effective solution.
* The technical contributions are significant and well-supported by experimental results.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.
* The results demonstrate a significant advance in the field of control and have the potential to impact multiple disciplines.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The high computational cost may limit the accessibility of the method.
* A more thorough comparative analysis with other state-of-the-art control methods would be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is an outstanding paper that represents a significant contribution to the field. The authors have successfully combined trajectory optimization and neural network learning to create a powerful and versatile method for generating complex controllers. The clarity of the writing, the technical soundness of the approach, and the significance of the results all strongly support acceptance. Despite minor weaknesses regarding computational cost and comparative analysis, the strengths of this work far outweigh its limitations.

**Recommendation:** Strong Accept.
