“Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks” presents a method for learning control policies for physically simulated characters using recurrent neural networks. The approach leverages trajectory optimization during a training phase to generate data for training a neural network, which then enables interactive, real-time control. The key contribution lies in demonstrating a single network architecture’s ability to generate realistic and stable behaviors across a diverse set of character morphologies and cyclic tasks – swimming, flying, and both bipedal and quadrupedal locomotion.

**General Comments**

This paper applies deep learning techniques to the established field of physically-based character control, building upon prior work in trajectory optimization and neural network policy learning. While trajectory optimization has proven effective in generating realistic motions, and neural networks have been used to approximate control policies from optimized trajectories (e.g., [9]), this work attempts to scale these approaches to more complex, high-dimensional problems. The authors should more explicitly position their contribution relative to recent advancements, particularly the work by Levine and Abbeel. Clarifying the advantages of this approach – whether in terms of learning speed, morphological adaptability, computational efficiency at runtime, or robustness to environmental variations – would strengthen the paper’s impact.

**Specific Comments**

The paper effectively demonstrates the versatility of the proposed method across a range of characters and tasks. However, certain aspects could benefit from further elaboration. The statement regarding “large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” requires clarification; providing more detail on this issue and why the proposed method addresses it would be beneficial. The rationale for not utilizing Linear Quadratic Gaussian (LQG) control could be presented more logically by postponing the discussion until after the introduction of the proposed alternative. 

The notation used around lines 226-240 is somewhat confusing. The use of both bars and squiggles (s(X) =  s̄, and the subsequent difference s(X) – s̄) is visually distracting and could be simplified. Furthermore, the description of “softer feedback gains” in relation to parameter λ lacks sufficient explanation. What does it mean for gains to be “soft,” and why is modifying the initial state a desirable aspect of the optimization process?

**Strengths**

*   **Versatility:** The method successfully controls a wide variety of characters and locomotion types without requiring task-specific tuning or hand-designed controllers.
*   **Scalability:** The approach appears to scale to complex, three-dimensional environments, overcoming limitations of previous work that focused on two-dimensional characters.
*   **Integration of Techniques:** Combining supervised learning with trajectory optimization and noise injection is a well-motivated strategy for improving robustness and generalization.
*   **Real-time Performance:** The resulting neural network policy enables interactive, real-time control, making the method practical for applications such as animation and robotics.

**Weaknesses**

*   **Limited Novelty:** The paper builds upon existing techniques, and the core contribution – applying these techniques to a broader range of problems – could be more clearly articulated.
*   **Lack of Comparative Analysis:** A more thorough comparison with related work, particularly recent advancements in reinforcement learning and policy optimization, is needed.
*   **Clarity of Explanation:** Certain aspects of the method, such as the feedback gain optimization and the concept of “soft” gains, require more detailed explanations.
*   **Notation:** The notation in section 3.2 is somewhat confusing and could be improved for readability.

**Arguments for Acceptance**

*   The paper presents a practical and effective method for generating control policies for complex characters.
*   The results demonstrate the versatility and scalability of the approach.
*   The combination of techniques is well-motivated and contributes to a more robust and generalizable solution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance**

*   The paper’s contribution is incremental and lacks significant novelty compared to recent advances in the field.
*   The lack of a thorough comparative analysis weakens the paper’s claims of superiority.
*   Certain aspects of the method require further clarification and explanation.

**Overall Assessment**

This paper represents a solid piece of work that demonstrates the effectiveness of combining trajectory optimization and neural networks for character control. However, its impact is limited by its incremental nature and the lack of a strong comparative analysis. While the method is valuable and demonstrates promising results, the paper needs to better articulate its contribution relative to existing work to justify acceptance. I recommend **weak accept** pending the authors addressing the points raised above, particularly strengthening the comparison to related work and improving the clarity of explanations.
