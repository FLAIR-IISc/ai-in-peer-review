- Proposes a novel method combining supervised learning with Contact-Invariant Optimization to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical tasks.  
- Method requires no motion capture or task-specific features; neural controller learns complex state-action mappings.  
- Distinct contribution: integrates trajectory optimization with supervised policy learning to handle complex dynamics and high-dimensional spaces.  
- Paper provides clear, detailed descriptions of network architecture, trajectory optimization, and training procedure.  
- Uses noise injection during training to reduce overfitting and improve generalization.  
- Strong empirical results controlling swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped characters; supplementary video supports realism and purposeful behaviors.  
- Major limitation: assumes full model knowledge (Eq. 6), reducing applicability to real-world settings with unknown dynamics.  
- Section 4 on neural network policy regression is brief and needs expansion.  
- Recurrent units are mentioned in the abstract but not discussed in sufficient detail in the paper.  
- More detail requested on neural network architecture and training procedure.  
- Scores well on originality and significance, with clear relevance to robotics and animation.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address clarity and methodological detail.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, impressive results, well-written.  
- Rejection arguments: reliance on full model knowledge and insufficient detail in key sections.
