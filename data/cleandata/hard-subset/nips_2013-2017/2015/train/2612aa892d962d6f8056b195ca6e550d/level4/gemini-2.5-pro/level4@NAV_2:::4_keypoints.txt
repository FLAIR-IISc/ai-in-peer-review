- Paper trains an RNN controller to reproduce high-quality trajectories from an offline trajectory optimizer (CIO) for real-time control of simulated characters.  
- Main contribution is a joint optimization framework that iteratively refines policy and trajectories, extending prior trajectory-optimization+policy-learning work to 3D locomotion (walking, swimming, flying).  
- Technical improvements include principled noise injection during training and a novel method for extracting optimal feedback gains from the trajectory optimizer.  
- Strength: high technical quality and sophisticated integration of simulator, optimizer, and NN training pipeline.  
- Strength: impressive, visually compelling results demonstrating stable, plausible control across diverse morphologies and movement styles.  
- Strength: ablation study clearly shows the importance of joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clearly organized, with helpful section cross-references.  
- Weakness: originality is limited—mainly an effective engineering synthesis of existing techniques rather than a new theoretical concept.  
- Argument for acceptance: powerful, well-engineered framework that achieves interactive controllers for complex 3D characters.  
- Argument for acceptance: empirical results and ablations strongly support the claims.  
- Argument for acceptance: high potential impact for animation, gaming, and robotics.  
- Argument against acceptance: contribution is largely engineering/scale-up of prior ideas rather than new theoretical insight.  
- Question: clarify motivation for the additional execution-time single-step optimization—why not directly apply the policy outputs to the simulator?  
- Question: provide key references for the claim that quadratic proximal regularization is similar to recent stochastic optimization approaches.  
- Minor comment: typographical error on line 89 ("to unity" → "to unify").
