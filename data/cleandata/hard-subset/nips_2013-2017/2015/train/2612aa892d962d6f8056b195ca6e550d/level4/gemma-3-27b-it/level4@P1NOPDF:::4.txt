This paper proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning to enable interactive control of complex characters. The core idea is to jointly learn trajectories and the policy, with the empirical finding that decoupling the two optimization problems into alternating subproblems—first optimizing trajectories, then the policy—allows for the reuse of existing, well-established methods and performs effectively in practice. While I was able to follow the paper’s arguments, I am not deeply familiar with this domain and therefore hesitate to comment on its potential impact. However, it certainly demonstrates technical skill, even if it doesn't present novel theoretical insights.

The authors demonstrate an impressive level of technical sophistication, both in the concepts and their implementation, and achieve equally impressive results. I found the claimed advantages of i) training with sensory noise and ii) learning trajectories jointly with the policy to be clearly demonstrated through their experiments.

The paper is also clearly written, with a level of detail appropriate for the complexity of the problem and its implementation. Each section addresses a distinct aspect of the work, and the cross-references between sections are particularly helpful. 

I believe the motivation for the additional optimization step described in Section 7 could have been better explained. Given the information presented in previous sections, I initially expected the learned policy to operate without requiring further optimization. Is this because a residual trajectory cost—or policy regression error—remains such that directly applying the policy’s action to the physical simulator would lead to accumulated errors? I would expect the forward evolution of the dynamics to inherently respect physical constraints, preventing physically implausible results, but I may be overlooking something. I do appreciate, however, that re-optimizing at each timestep allows for the use of larger timesteps.

Line 153: “similar to recent approaches…” Could you provide one or two references here? It seems counterintuitive to me that limiting updates in parameter space would generally be a sensible approach, although I ultimately understood its usefulness in the context of your block-alternating subproblems.

Typo: Line 89: “to unify” 

Overall, the paper’s results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and its implementation seem sensible. However, this research area is somewhat outside my area of expertise, making it difficult to confidently assess the novelty and significance of the work.
