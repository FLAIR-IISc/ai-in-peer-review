- Proposes embedding feedback gains into neural network policies to improve robustness to disturbances and approximation errors.
- Core method softly couples trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a cost penalty on deviation.
- This coupling drives the policy to learn simpler, more robust approximations than MPC-based trajectory optimization alone.
- Uses sophisticated parallelization for optimization, presented as an implementation detail rather than the main contribution.
- Demonstrates effectiveness of the trained feedback controllers across a diverse set of robots and tasks.
- Paper judged highest quality: exceptionally clear, a technical tour de force that synthesizes computer graphics, control theory, and neural networks to produce powerful, general controllers.
