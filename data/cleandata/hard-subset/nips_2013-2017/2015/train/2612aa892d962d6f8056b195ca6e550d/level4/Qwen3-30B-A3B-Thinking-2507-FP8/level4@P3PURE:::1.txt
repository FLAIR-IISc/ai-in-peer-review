

The authors propose a novel strategy for embedding feedback gains within neural network policies, enhancing robustness against disturbances and neural network approximation errors. By softly coupling trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for trajectory deviation, the approach enables the policy to learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories—simpler and more robust than those generated by MPC-based trajectory optimization in isolation. The method leverages sophisticated parallelization techniques to optimize tasks efficiently, though this aspect serves primarily as an enabling technical enabler rather than the paper's core contribution. The trained feedback controllers successfully control diverse robotic platforms across multiple tasks. This work represents a significant advancement, demonstrating exceptional technical depth, clarity, and elegance. It constitutes a tour de force in integrating graphics, control theory, and neural networks to generate versatile controllers for varied body morphologies and tasks. The results are compelling, and the synthesis of these fields is truly inspiring. This is a high-quality, impactful contribution to the field.
