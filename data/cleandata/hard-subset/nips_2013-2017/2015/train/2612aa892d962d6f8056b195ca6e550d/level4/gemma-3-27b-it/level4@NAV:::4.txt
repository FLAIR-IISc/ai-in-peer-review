This paper presents a method for interactive control of complex characters by integrating trajectory optimization and policy learning. The core idea is to jointly learn trajectories and a control policy, leveraging an alternating optimization approach that allows for the application of established techniques and demonstrates practical effectiveness. While the paper was technically clear, my limited expertise in this specific field prevents a comprehensive assessment of its potential impact, though it appears to represent a significant technical achievement without introducing fundamentally new theoretical concepts.

The authors demonstrate a remarkable degree of technical skill throughout, both in the conceptual design and the implementation details, accompanied by compelling results. The benefits of both incorporating sensory noise during training and the joint learning approach for trajectories and policy are convincingly shown.

The paper is exceptionally well-written, providing an appropriate level of detail considering the problem's complexity and its implementation. The organization is logical, with each section focusing on a distinct aspect of the work and benefiting from helpful cross-references.

The motivation behind the additional optimization step described in Section 7 could be clarified. Based on the prior presentation, I anticipated the policy operating independently of further optimization. Is this additional step necessitated by persistent trajectory costs or policy inaccuracies that would accumulate over time when applied to the physical simulation? I would expect the dynamics simulation to inherently enforce physical constraints, preventing unrealistic behavior, although I may be overlooking a critical consideration. I do recognize, however, that this re-optimization allows for the use of larger timesteps. 

Line 153 ("similar to recent approaches...") would benefit from one or two relevant citations. The rationale for maintaining small parameter updates is initially counterintuitive, but the explanation relating to the block-alternating optimization scheme ultimately clarified its utility.

Typo: Line 89: “to unify…” 

Overall, the results presented are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and its implementation seem sound. However, due to my limited familiarity with this research area, it is difficult to fully evaluate the novelty and significance of this work.
