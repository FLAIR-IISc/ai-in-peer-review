- Demonstrates a compact, efficient neural network policy for interactive character control in a physics simulator.  
- Uses trajectory optimization during training and shows versatility across various cyclic behaviors and body types.  
- Paper is essentially an application of deep learning to existing computer graphics control methods, building on recent advances.  
- Trajectory optimization’s ability to produce realistic control signals is already established and cited by the authors.  
- Prior work has used neural networks to approximate step-by-step policies that trace trajectories from trajectory optimization (e.g., [9]).  
- Authors need to clarify the paper’s positioning relative to preceding approaches and prior art.  
- Should compare and contrast with Abbeel & Levine’s recent work (learning speed, support for diverse bodies, runtime efficiency, robustness).  
- The statement about producing “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” requires clarification.  
- Recommend presenting the proposed strategy before explaining why LQG was not used.  
- Notation in lines 226–240 (s(X) = \bar s and cost based on s(X) - \bar s) is confusing due to similar symbols.  
- The claim that the approach yields “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” needs further explanation and comparison to alternatives.  
- Provide the rationale for altering the initial state as part of the procedure.  
- Although the work is sound, the significance of the contribution relative to recent related research is unclear.
