- Presents a novel real-time control algorithm: a NN trained offline to replicate Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) with intertwined CIO and NN training and noise injection.  
- Joint training and noise injection are significant contributions that improve generalization beyond naive “learn CIO output.”  
- Experiments show the network produces realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.  
- Training data is limited (100–200 trajectories with 5 branched segments), risking overfitting, especially for the "no noise" variant.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: needs broader comparisons to competing methods beyond a brief MPC comparison, and the MPC reference is missing.  
- Mathematical exposition is generally clear but some parts are superficial or under-specified (notably the CIO step/Eq.6 and undefined notation); additional references and code would aid replication.  
- Section 3.2 is unclear due to confusing notation and omission of the time index t, which affects interpretation of quantities.  
- Real-time computational cost measurements are missing and should be reported, as runtime could be a bottleneck for applications like video games.  
- Minor wording/notation issues: clarify “biped location” (locomotion), fix “unity” → “unite/unify,” report facing angle target, and correct misuse of “noise injection” term, initial weights theta use, eta hyperparameter choice, typos, and acronyms.  
- The asynchronous joint optimization in Section 6 differs substantially from the alternating scheme in Alg.1 and should be mentioned earlier for clarity.  
- Experimental ablations could be improved: split "no noise" into "no data augmentation" vs. "no hidden noise injection," and test decreasing alpha to zero to probe curriculum-learning effects.  
- Overall: an impressive application of NNs to character control, but practical benefits and broader applicability need stronger empirical support.
