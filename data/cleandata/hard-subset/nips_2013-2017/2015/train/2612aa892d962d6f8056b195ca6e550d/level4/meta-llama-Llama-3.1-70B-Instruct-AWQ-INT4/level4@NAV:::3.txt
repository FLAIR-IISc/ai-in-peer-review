The authors of this submission propose a novel algorithm for real-time control of 3D models, leveraging a neural network (NN) to generate animations by mimicking the output of Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) after offline training. Notably, the CIO and NN training processes are intertwined, with each influencing the optimization criterion of the other. Another crucial aspect of this method is the introduction of noise through data augmentation via minor perturbations and additive noise in the NN's hidden layers. Experimental results demonstrate that this network can produce realistic and stable control policies across diverse character models.

This approach is particularly intriguing given the challenges associated with real-time control of arbitrary models. While the proposed method may initially seem straightforward, the incorporation of joint training and noise injection constitutes significant new contributions that enhance generalization, as evidenced by experimental results.

However, the experimental results could be further strengthened by comparisons with a larger dataset. The limited number of base training trajectories (100-200 trials with 5 branched segments) may contribute to the "no noise" variant's tendency to overfit. Similarly, more diverse training trajectories could benefit the "no joint" variant.

The comparative evaluation is the paper's primary weakness, with the introduction acknowledging the need for a systematic comparison with more direct methods. The sole comparison to a competing method, Model Predictive Control (MPC), is brief and lacks a reference. 

The mathematical explanations are generally clear and well-motivated, although some points, such as the CIO step (Eq. 6), are only superficially addressed and may require additional references for replication. Providing code would be a valuable contribution to the community. Section 3.2, which discusses generating optimal actions for noisy inputs, is somewhat unclear due to confusing notations and the omission of the time index "t."

Including time measurements for computations during real-time control would be beneficial, as this could be a significant bottleneck, particularly in applications like video games where CPU availability is limited. 

Minor remarks include: 

- Clarifying the term "biped location" to locomotion
- Correcting "unity" to "unite" or "unify" in the sentence "It is our aim to unity these disparate approaches"
- Confirming whether the target is limited to x, y, z coordinates without constraints on the character's angle and exploring the feasibility of adding an extra target angle
- Replacing "noise injection" with "data augmentation" in the context of generating additional data from noisy inputs
- Clarifying the use of initial network weights theta in the first iteration of Alg. 1
- Justifying the use of the same hyperparameter eta in the two steps of Alg. 1
- Addressing minor grammatical errors and inconsistencies throughout the paper
- Providing clarification on the asynchronous joint optimization scheme described in Section 6 and its differences from the alternating scheme in Alg. 1
- Exploring whether alpha can be decreased to zero during optimization for CIO and its implications on the learning process.

Overall, this submission presents an innovative application of neural networks to character control, with practical benefits and applicability awaiting further establishment.
