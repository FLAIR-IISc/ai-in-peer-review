- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers for diverse dynamic characters using trajectory optimization (CIO) to generate training data.  
- Demonstrates stable, realistic 3D locomotion across swimming, flying, and walking morphologies, showing broad applicability.  
- Combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization, leveraging noise injection and optimized feedback gains for improved stability.  
- Provides relatively detailed training procedure and addresses computational scalability via cloud computing and GPU implementation.  
- Extends prior work on learning control from trajectory optimization to more complex 3D systems, aiming for a unified control approach.  
- Heavily depends on a complete and accurate dynamical model (Eq. 6), a significant limitation for real-world robotics applications.  
- Sections 4 and 6 are disproportionately detailed compared to other parts of the paper, creating an imbalanced presentation.  
- The role and benefits of recurrent units are not clearly explained in the main text despite mention in the abstract.  
- The description of stochasticity is unclear—training data is stochastic but the network appears deterministic; this needs clarification.  
- Comparative evaluation to direct or alternative control methods is impressionistic and lacks systematic benchmarking.  
- Overall assessment: strong empirical contribution with important technical advances, but constrained by model dependence and presentation issues.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant advance in automated controller design, stable 3D locomotion, effective combination of techniques, thorough experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on full dynamical model, uneven detail across sections, and insufficient rigorous comparisons to alternatives.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on clarifying recurrence, stochasticity, model dependence, balancing technical detail, and discussing limitations/future work.
