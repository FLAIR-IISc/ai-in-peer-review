This work presents a method for the interactive control of complex characters by integrating trajectory optimization with policy learning. The central contribution is the joint optimization of both the trajectories and the control policy. A key practical finding is that separating this into two alternating subproblems is an effective strategy, as it allows for the use of well-established methods and yields strong empirical results. While the paper was clear and easy to follow, my expertise is not directly in this subfield, making it difficult for me to assess its potential impact. From my perspective, the work stands as a significant technical accomplishment, even if it does not introduce new theoretical contributions.

The authors demonstrate a high degree of technical skill at every stage, from the conceptual framework to the final implementation, leading to compelling results. The paper successfully substantiates its primary claims regarding the benefits of both incorporating sensory noise during training and the joint learning of the policy and trajectories.

The manuscript is exceptionally well-written, striking an appropriate balance of detail for such a complex system. Its structure is logical, with each section addressing a distinct component of the method, and the cross-referencing between sections is helpful for the reader.

The rationale for the additional optimization step described in Section 7 could be clarified. After reading the preceding sections, my expectation was that the learned policy could be deployed directly without requiring further optimization at runtime. Is this step necessary because of a residual trajectory cost or a policy regression error that would otherwise lead to error accumulation when integrated through the physics simulator? My assumption was that the forward dynamics model would enforce physical plausibility, but perhaps I am overlooking a key detail. I do, however, recognize and appreciate the stated benefit that this re-optimization allows for the use of larger simulation timesteps.

Line 153: The text mentions the approach is "similar to recent approaches." Could the authors please provide one or two citations to support this? The strategy of keeping parameter updates small was initially counterintuitive, but I later understood its value within the context of the paper's block-alternating optimization scheme.

Line 89: A minor typographical error is present in the phrase "to unify".

In summary, the paper's results are impressive, and the proposed optimization framework and its implementation appear sound. However, as this research area is outside my primary area of expertise, I cannot confidently evaluate the work's novelty and overall significance.
