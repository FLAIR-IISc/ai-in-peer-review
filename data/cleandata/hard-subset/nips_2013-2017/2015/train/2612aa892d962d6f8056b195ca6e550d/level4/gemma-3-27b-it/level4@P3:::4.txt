This paper proposes a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for complex characters, enabling interactive control. The core idea is to jointly learn trajectories and a control policy, leveraging trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data for the neural network. The authors demonstrate that decoupling the optimization into alternating subproblems – trajectory optimization and neural network regression – allows them to utilize existing methods effectively and achieve practical success. While the paper doesn't present fundamentally novel theoretical insights, it showcases a strong combination of technical sophistication and impressive results.

The authors demonstrate a sophisticated approach at multiple levels – from the underlying concepts to their implementation – with correspondingly strong empirical results. The benefits of incorporating sensory noise during training and learning trajectories in conjunction with the control policy are clearly articulated and convincingly demonstrated. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized, providing an appropriate level of detail considering the complexity of the problem and its implementation. Each section addresses a distinct aspect of the work, and cross-references are thoughtfully included, enhancing understanding.

A potential area for improvement lies in the explanation of the final optimization step described in Section 7. After establishing the framework for learning a policy, the introduction of a real-time optimization process seems somewhat counterintuitive. It would be beneficial to more clearly articulate *why* this step is necessary. Is it to address residual trajectory cost or policy regression error, preventing error accumulation during simulation? While the benefit of enabling larger timesteps is mentioned, a more detailed explanation of the underlying rationale would strengthen the paper. The reviewer notes a potential expectation that the policy should function without continuous optimization, and addressing this directly would be helpful. 

On line 153, the phrase "similar to recent approaches..." could be strengthened by including one or two relevant citations. The concept of keeping updates small in parameter space initially seemed counterintuitive, but the explanation of its utility within the block-alternating optimization scheme was ultimately clear. A minor typographical error exists on line 89 ("to unify"). 

The results presented are visually compelling (and the accompanying video is highly recommended), and the proposed optimization scheme appears sensible. However, this research area falls somewhat outside the reviewer’s primary expertise, making it difficult to confidently assess the novelty and overall significance of the contribution relative to the broader field.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Strong empirical results:** The method successfully controls diverse, complex characters in 3D environments, a challenging task that previous work often addressed only in 2D.
*   **Clear and well-written:** The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly explains a complex system.
*   **Practical approach:** The decoupling of trajectory optimization and policy learning allows the authors to leverage existing tools and algorithms effectively.
*   **Demonstrated benefits of key components:** The positive impact of sensory noise injection and joint trajectory/policy learning is convincingly demonstrated.
*   **Reproducibility:** The level of detail provided should facilitate reproduction of the results.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited theoretical novelty:** The paper primarily combines existing techniques rather than introducing fundamentally new theoretical advances.
*   **Domain expertise required for full assessment:** The reviewer lacks deep expertise in this specific area, hindering a complete evaluation of the work's significance.
*   **Motivation for final optimization step could be clearer:** The rationale behind the real-time optimization (Section 7) requires further clarification.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with impressive results and a clear presentation. While the theoretical novelty is limited, the practical contribution and technical execution are excellent. Despite a slight reservation regarding the assessment of novelty due to limited domain expertise, I believe the paper is a valuable addition to the literature and warrants acceptance. I recommend a score of **7/10** (Strong Accept).
