- Proposes combining trajectory optimization with policy learning via an iterative, block-alternating refinement of trajectories and control policy.  
- Joint learning enables reuse of established methods and yields effective practical results.  
- Demonstrates high technical sophistication in both concepts and implementation.  
- Shows clear benefits of injecting sensory noise during training.  
- Shows clear benefits of jointly learning trajectories with the policy.  
- Experiments successfully control a diverse set of character morphologies in complex 3D environments, indicating versatility.  
- Paper is clearly written with appropriate detail and helpful cross-references.  
- Lacks novel theoretical contributions—primarily a combination/engineering of existing techniques.  
- Needs clarification on the motivation and necessity of the additional optimization step in Section 7 (is it required at execution due to residual trajectory cost or policy regression error?).  
- Line 153: request to add one or two relevant references for “similar to recent approaches”; limiting parameter updates initially seemed counterintuitive but makes sense for block-alternating optimization.  
- Typo: Line 89 “to unify” → should be “to unify these disparate approaches.”  
- Reviewer’s limited domain expertise makes it hard to fully assess the work’s novelty and significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong technical execution, effective results, clear presentation, tackles a challenging problem, and demonstrates advantages of noise injection and joint optimization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, requires domain expertise to evaluate significance, and unclear motivation for real-time/re-optimization at execution.
