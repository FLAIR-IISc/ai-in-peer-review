- Demonstrates feasibility of using a compact neural network as a control policy for interactive characters in a physics simulator.  
- Training leverages trajectory optimization to learn the neural network policy.  
- Main contribution is showing the architecture works across diverse cyclic behaviors and body types.  
- Appears to be an application of deep learning to established trajectory-optimization techniques in graphics.  
- Effectiveness of trajectory optimization for realistic control signals is already known and cited.  
- Neural network approximation of step-by-step actions overlaps with prior work (e.g., [9]); positioning relative to that work is unclear.  
- Should compare and contrast with Abbeel & Levine’s recent work on learning-based control (learning speed, body shape support, runtime cost, robustness).  
- Claim that the process produces “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” needs clarification and evidence.  
- Recommend reorganizing the LQG discussion to follow presentation of the proposed strategy for clarity.  
- Notation in lines 226–240 (e.g., s(X)=\bar s and cost definition) is confusing due to similar symbols and should be clarified.  
- Statement that the approach produces “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” is vague—define “softer,” compare to alternatives, and justify desirability.  
- The rationale for changing the initial state during the procedure is not explicitly stated.  
- Overall work is sound but the novelty and contribution relative to recent related work require clearer clarification.
