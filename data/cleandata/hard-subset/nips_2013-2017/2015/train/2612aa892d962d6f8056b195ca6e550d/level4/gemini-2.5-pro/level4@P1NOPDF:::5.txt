This paper shows promising results, but its overall quality would be significantly improved with substantial restructuring. The following points should be addressed to enhance the paper's clarity and organization.

First, a critical assumption of the proposed method—the requirement of full model knowledge (as specified in Eq. 6)—should be stated explicitly at the beginning of the paper. Readers should be made aware of this important prerequisite from the outset, rather than discovering it later in the text.

The paper's organization also needs attention. There is a noticeable disproportion in the length and level of detail between Section 4 and Section 6, which disrupts the flow and balance of the manuscript. Furthermore, there is a discrepancy between the abstract and the main text; Section 4 fails to discuss the recurrent units mentioned in the abstract, leaving the reader searching for a connection that is never made.

Finally, a point of terminology requires clarification. On line 405, the authors describe their model as a "stochastic NN." However, the network architecture itself appears to be deterministic, with stochasticity originating from the training data rather than the model's operations. The authors should clarify why this term is used or justify how the network itself is stochastic.

In summary, while the technical contributions are promising, the paper is hindered by these structural and clarity issues. Addressing them would substantially strengthen the submission.
