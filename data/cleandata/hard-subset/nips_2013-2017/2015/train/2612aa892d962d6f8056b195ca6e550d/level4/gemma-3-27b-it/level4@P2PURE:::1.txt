The authors propose a strategy for embedding feedback gains within neural network policies, enhancing robustness to disturbances and mitigating regression error. They achieve a soft coupling between trajectory and policy optimization via a deviation cost, enabling the policy to learn simplified, yet robust, approximations of optimal trajectories—often exceeding the performance of trajectories generated by model predictive control (MPC) alone. While sophisticated parallelization is employed to optimize these tasks, it serves primarily as an enabling technique rather than a core contribution. The efficacy of the approach is demonstrated through successful control of a diverse set of robotic systems.

This paper is exceptionally well-written and technically sound, representing a significant achievement in the field. The results are compelling and beautifully demonstrate the successful integration of graphics, control, and neural networks for generating controllers applicable to a wide range of robot morphologies and tasks. This work is truly inspiring.
