This paper presents promising results, but there are several areas that require attention. 

Firstly, it would be beneficial to explicitly state from the outset that full model knowledge (as presented in Equation 6) is a prerequisite for the proposed approach. 

Upon examining the paper's structure, it becomes apparent that Sections 4 and 6 are disproportionate in terms of their content and depth. Specifically, Section 4 does not mention the recurrent units that were highlighted in the abstract, which may lead to confusion for readers. 

Furthermore, the description of the neural network as "stochastic" on line 405 seems misleading. While the training data may be stochastic, the network itself appears to be deterministic. This discrepancy warrants clarification.

In response to the authors' rebuttal, which provided additional information, I offer the following assessment: the paper demonstrates potential but would benefit from restructuring to address the aforementioned concerns and improve overall coherence.
