- Presents a small, fast neural-network controller trained with trajectory optimization that generalizes across cyclic behaviors and body types.  
- Reviewer views this as applying deep learning to existing graphics/trajectory-optimization techniques and asks for clearer positioning relative to prior work.  
- Authors should compare and contrast their method with recent work by Abbeel and Levine (e.g., speed of learning, body-shape generality, runtime cost, robustness).  
- The claim about producing “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” is unclear and needs a detailed explanation.  
- Discussion of why LQG was not used should be presented after the authors’ strategy to provide clearer context.  
- Notation around lines 226–240 (s(X)=\bar s and cost definition) is confusing due to similar symbols and needs clarification.  
- The statement that the approach produces “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” needs definition of “softer,” comparisons to alternatives, and justification of desirability.  
- Unclear why changing the initial state is beneficial in the procedure; paper’s contribution relative to recent related research is not convincingly established.
