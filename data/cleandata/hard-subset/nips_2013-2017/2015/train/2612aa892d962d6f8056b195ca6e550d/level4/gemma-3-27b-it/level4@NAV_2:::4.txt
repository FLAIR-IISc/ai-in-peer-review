This paper proposes a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as near-optimal feedback controllers for a diverse range of dynamical systems, enabling interactive control of complex characters. The core idea is to jointly learn optimal trajectories and a control policy through an iterative process, leveraging trajectory optimization (specifically, Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data for the neural network. The authors demonstrate strong empirical results across swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal locomotion tasks, showcasing the method's ability to synthesize stable and realistic behaviors without relying on motion capture or task-specific features. While the paper does not present novel theoretical insights, it successfully integrates existing techniques in a way that tackles challenging control problems in high-dimensional spaces.

The authors demonstrate a commendable level of technical sophistication throughout the work, from the conceptual framework to the implementation details. The combination of supervised learning with trajectory optimization, the use of sensory noise during training, and the alternating optimization scheme are all well-motivated and clearly explained. The empirical validation, with demonstrations of locomotion for various character morphologies, is particularly compelling. The clear writing style and well-organized structure enhance the paper’s accessibility despite the complexity of the subject matter. Each section focuses on a distinct aspect of the problem, with helpful cross-references facilitating understanding.

One area where further clarification could be beneficial is the motivation for the real-time optimization step described in Section 7. While the preceding sections establish a framework for learning a control policy from optimized trajectories, the need for continued optimization at each timestep was not immediately clear. Specifically, it would be helpful to elaborate on whether this step is required to address residual trajectory cost or policy regression error, and how it interacts with the physics-based simulation's inherent constraints. Understanding why this optimization is necessary, despite the apparent capabilities of the learned policy, would strengthen the paper's narrative.

On line 153, the phrase “similar to recent approaches…” would benefit from specific citations to provide context and allow readers to explore related work. Furthermore, the rationale behind keeping updates small in parameter space, while ultimately understood in the context of the block-alternating optimization scheme, initially felt counterintuitive and could be more explicitly explained. A minor typographical error exists on line 89 (“to unify”).

Despite these minor points, the paper presents a strong and well-executed contribution. The results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and its implementation appear sensible and effective. However, due to my limited expertise in this specific research area, I find it difficult to fully assess the novelty and significance of this work relative to the broader field.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a technically sound and well-implemented approach to interactive control.
*   **Empirical Results:** The demonstrated results are compelling, showcasing the method’s ability to generate stable and realistic behaviors across a diverse range of characters and tasks.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized, making it accessible to a broad audience.
*   **Practicality:** The method combines established techniques in a practical and effective manner, enabling interactive control without relying on extensive manual tuning or motion capture data.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Novelty:** The paper does not introduce significant theoretical advancements but rather focuses on the effective integration of existing techniques.
*   **Domain Specificity:** Assessing the true novelty and impact requires deeper expertise in the field of character control and robotics.
*   **Motivation for Real-Time Optimization:** The rationale for the continued optimization step at runtime could be more thoroughly explained.
