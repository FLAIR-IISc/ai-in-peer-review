- Proposes joint learning of trajectories and policy for interactive control of complex characters by combining trajectory optimization with policy learning.
- Key empirical insight: alternating (block-decoupled) subproblem optimization is effective, enabling reuse of existing methods and strong practical performance.
- Reviewer’s domain expertise is limited, making it difficult to judge the paper’s broader impact; the work reads as strong engineering without clear theoretical novelty.
- Technical implementation is sophisticated and results are impressive.
- Empirically demonstrates benefits of training with sensory noise and of learning trajectories jointly with the policy.
- Paper is clearly written, appropriately detailed, and well organized with useful cross-references.
- Questioning motivation for the additional optimization step in Section 7—reviewer expected the policy to run without test-time optimization and wonders if it’s needed due to residual trajectory cost or policy regression error; notes re-optimizing each timestep enables larger time steps.
- Minor request: add references where the text says "similar to recent approaches" (l153) to clarify and justify the small parameter-space update strategy.
- Minor typo: "to unify" at l89.
- Overall: results and optimization scheme are sensible and impressive, but novelty/significance remain uncertain to the reviewer.
