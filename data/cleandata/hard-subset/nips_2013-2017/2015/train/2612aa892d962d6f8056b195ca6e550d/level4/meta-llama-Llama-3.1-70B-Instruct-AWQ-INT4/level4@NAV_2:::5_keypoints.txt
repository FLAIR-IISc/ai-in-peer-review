- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers using supervised learning combined with trajectory optimization and noise injection.  
- Uses the trajectory optimizer to obtain both optimal actions and optimal feedback gains.  
- Introduces a neural policy mapping sensory states to optimal actions and a distributed stochastic optimization to jointly find trajectories and a reconstructing policy.  
- Claims the training method scales to larger and harder control problems.  
- Key contributions: novel controller training approach, distributed stochastic optimization algorithm, and noise-injection during training.  
- Demonstrates results across diverse tasks: swimming, flying, biped and quadruped walking with varied morphologies.  
- Strengths: novel, clear, thorough exposition and impressive empirical results.  
- Weakness: assumes full model knowledge, limiting practical applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison to alternative methods such as model-predictive control (MPC).  
- Weakness: unclear what is meant by the network being “stochastic” versus stochastic training data — network appears deterministic.  
- Suggests originality in combining components, but needs clearer relation to prior work.  
- Significance: shows promising multi-task performance but needs broader discussion of applications and impact.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, interesting distributed/noise techniques, strong empirical demonstrations.  
- Rejection arguments: reliance on full model knowledge, insufficient comparisons, and ambiguity about network stochasticity.
