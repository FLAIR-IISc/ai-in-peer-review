- Proposes real-time 3D model control by training a neural network offline to mimic Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs.  
- Introduces iterative alternating training where CIO and the NN inform each other’s optimization criteria.  
- Adds noise injection via input perturbations and additive hidden-layer noise as key components for generalization.  
- Experimental results show realistic, stable control across diverse character models.  
- Joint training and noise injection are highlighted as significant contributions enabling improved generalization.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: limited training data (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments) risks overfitting.  
- Comparison to other methods is insufficient: only a brief MPC comparison without proper citation.  
- Mathematical exposition is generally clear but lacks detail for replication; some CIO notation (e.g., Eq. 6) is superficial.  
- Section 3.2 (optimal actions for noisy inputs) is confusing due to notation issues and missing time index t.  
- Providing code would greatly aid reproducibility.  
- Runtime measurements for real-time control are missing and needed to assess practical applicability and CPU constraints.  
- Multiple minor corrections needed: wording (“locomotion,” “unify”), grammar, and typographical errors (lines 231, 315).  
- Clarify technical points: initial network weights θ in Alg.1, justification for shared hyperparameter η, meanings of subsets sX and aX, and the statement about objective minimization relative to Alg.1.  
- Highlight that the asynchronous joint optimization in Section 6 differs significantly from the alternating scheme in Alg.1 and should be noted earlier.  
- Suggest splitting the “no noise” ablation into “no data augmentation” and “no hidden noise injection” for finer analysis.  
- Recommend future work: test adding orientation target, investigate reducing α to zero during optimization (role of CIO as curriculum), and correct Table 1a caption (network has 3 layers, not 4).
