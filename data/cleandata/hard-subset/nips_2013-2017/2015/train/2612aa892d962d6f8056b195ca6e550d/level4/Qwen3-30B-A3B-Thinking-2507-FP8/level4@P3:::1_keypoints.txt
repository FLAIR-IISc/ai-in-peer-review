- Presents a compelling method to train neural controllers for diverse 3D characters without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.  
- Combines trajectory optimization with neural network training in a novel distributed stochastic optimization framework.  
- Jointly optimizes trajectories and network parameters while injecting noise to improve robustness and prevent overfitting.  
- Introduces an efficient perturbation-based method to compute optimal feedback gains during trajectory optimization, extending Contact-Invariant Optimization without LQG passes.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental validation across swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal characters with interactive real-time control.  
- Shows substantial empirical gains over naive training on static trajectories (≈ two orders of magnitude in test performance).  
- Ablation studies convincingly show the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection for stability and robustness.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, mathematically precise, and reproducible, with an effective supplementary video.  
- Original contribution: solves substantially harder 3D locomotion problems and unifies control approaches across multiple morphologies.  
- Significant impact: advances generalizable controllers with potential applications in robotics and robust real-world control.  
- Main weakness: limited discussion and analysis of computational complexity, scalability, and training time relative to character complexity.  
- Additional weakness: insufficient discussion of potential failure modes in highly dynamic or unpredictable environments.  
- Overall verdict: technically strong, significant, and well-presented — recommendation to accept (NIPS).
