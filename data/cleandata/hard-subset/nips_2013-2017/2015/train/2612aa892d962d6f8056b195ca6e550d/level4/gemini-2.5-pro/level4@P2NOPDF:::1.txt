This paper presents a novel strategy for training robust neural network control policies by embedding feedback gains directly within the network architecture. This approach enhances robustness against disturbances and regression errors. The core of the method is a soft coupling between trajectory optimization and policy optimization, enforced by a cost penalty for deviating from the reference trajectory. This framework enables the policy to learn effective approximations of optimal trajectories that are simpler and more robust than those generated by traditional MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. While the optimization process leverages sophisticated parallelization, this is presented as a means to an end rather than a central contribution. The effectiveness of the method is demonstrated through the successful control of a diverse set of robots performing various tasks.

This is an outstanding paper of the highest quality. The work is exceptionally clear, represents a formidable technical achievement, and delivers compelling and impressive results. The authors have produced a beautiful body of work that masterfully integrates concepts from machine learning, optimal control, and robotics to generate controllers for a wide variety of body morphologies and tasks. The technical depth, clarity of presentation, and the quality of the results are truly inspiring.
