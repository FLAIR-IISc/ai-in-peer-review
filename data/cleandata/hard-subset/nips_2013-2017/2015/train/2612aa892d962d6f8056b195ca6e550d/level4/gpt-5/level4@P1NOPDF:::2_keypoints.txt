- Paper demonstrates a small, fast neural network can learn interactive control policies via trajectory optimization for diverse character types and behaviors.  
- Authors must better position the contribution relative to prior work showing trajectory optimization generates realistic control signals.  
- Prior work using neural nets to imitate trajectory-optimized policies (e.g., [9]) is not sufficiently distinguished from this paper’s approach.  
- The manuscript should directly compare and contrast with Abbeel and Levine’s line of work: intended advantages (speed, morphology generality, runtime cost, robustness) are unclear.  
- Clarify the statement about producing “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains”: define “ill-conditioned” here and give practical magnitude.  
- Consider moving the discussion of why LQG was not used to a later section after the proposed strategy is presented.  
- Fix confusing notation around lines 226–240 (e.g., s(X)=s̄ and cost defined in terms of s(X)−s̄); simplify symbols to avoid ambiguity.  
- Explain what “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” means: softer relative to which baseline, operational definition of “soft,” and why changing the initial state is desirable.  
- Overall assessment: sound work but the novelty and contribution relative to recent related work are not made sufficiently clear.
