- Presents a novel method training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal).  
- Core innovation: interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization), injecting noise during training, and using trajectory optimization to obtain optimal feedback gains.  
- Demonstrates substantially more complex 3D locomotion behaviors than previous neural-network + trajectory-optimization work.  
- Provides a unified framework that replaces disparate control architectures across character types with a single network architecture and parameters.  
- Single network controls a range of characters, producing stable, realistic behaviors without task-specific hand-crafting.  
- Technically sound with careful experimental validation and thorough ablation studies showing joint optimization and noise injection are essential.  
- Achieves interactive real-time control in high-dimensional continuous spaces, a rare technical accomplishment.  
- High significance and potential impact for animation, robotics, and biomechanics.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and supported by a compelling supplementary video.  
- Major limitation: method requires full model knowledge (Eq. 6), which is not emphasized early and limits real-world applicability.  
- Imbalance in technical detail: neural network policy section is too short (insufficient discussion of recurrent units) while distributed training section contains excessive implementation specifics.  
- Misleading terminology: calling the learned policy “stochastic” is inaccurate—training data is stochastic but the network appears deterministic; this should be clarified.  
- Lacks thorough comparison with alternative approaches (e.g., MPC and other neural network control methods) on stability, efficiency, and behavior quality.  
- Meets NIPS quality criteria overall (quality, clarity, originality, significance) and is recommended for acceptance with conditions: explicitly state model-knowledge requirement early, condense distributed training details and expand network architecture discussion, clarify stochastic vs deterministic policy distinction, and provide more comprehensive comparisons with alternatives.
