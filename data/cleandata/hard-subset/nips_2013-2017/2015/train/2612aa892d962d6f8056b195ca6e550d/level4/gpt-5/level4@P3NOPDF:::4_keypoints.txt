- Proposes an alternating framework that jointly learns trajectories and a policy by decoupling optimization into reusable subproblems; advocates training with sensory noise.  
- Strong empirical performance with convincing demonstrations; experiments and ablations support benefits of noise injection and joint trajectory–policy optimization.  
- Thoughtful system design: the alternating scheme leverages established solvers, improving practicality without overcomplicating the pipeline.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with adequate implementation detail.  
- Limited theoretical novelty—contribution is primarily engineering/integration rather than new theory or guarantees.  
- Motivation for the additional online optimization (Section 7) is unclear and needs strengthening.  
- Need clarification whether online re-optimization corrects residual trajectory cost, policy regression error, or both.  
- Request concrete enumeration of failure modes that online optimization prevents (e.g., drift from reference, instability at contact, sensitivity to modeling error).  
- Ask for an ablation comparing policy-only vs policy+online optimization across tasks/timesteps to quantify trade-offs in accuracy, stability, and compute.  
- Request quantification of claim that re-optimizing allows larger control timesteps and discussion of interaction with simulator integration step size and contact handling.  
- Suggest clearer positioning relative to closely related alternating/guided policy search methods to justify novelty/significance.  
- Recommend including a schematic summarizing the alternating loop, data flow, and where noise is injected.  
- Request citations for “similar to recent approaches” (e.g., TRPO, Conservative Policy Iteration, Natural Policy Gradient, Guided Policy Search, iLQG/LQR) and clarification of how small parameter updates are enforced (KL/trust regions/penalties).  
- Suggest additional experiments: focused ablation on alternation vs end-to-end policy learning and pure trajectory optimization with MPC; report stability, sample efficiency, wall-clock time, final performance.  
- Recommend discussing generalization, sensitivity to trajectory distribution coverage, failure cases, and robustness to modeling error, noise levels, and timestep choices.  
- Minor: fix typo at line 89 (“to unify”) and ensure all similarity claims are supported by citations.  
- Overall: strong empirical/engineering paper with high-quality results; main weaknesses are limited theory and incomplete justification for online optimization—would favor acceptance if clarifications and targeted ablations are added.
