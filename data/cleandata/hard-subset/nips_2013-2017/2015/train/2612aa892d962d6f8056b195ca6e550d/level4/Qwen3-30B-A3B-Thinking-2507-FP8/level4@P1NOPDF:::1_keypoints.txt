- Introduces a novel strategy to embed feedback gains directly into neural network policies, improving robustness to disturbances and regression errors.  
- Softly couples trajectory optimization with policy learning via a cost penalty for trajectory deviation.  
- Enables policies to learn simpler, more robust approximations than trajectories produced solely by MPC-based optimization.  
- Uses sophisticated parallelization to speed task execution, but this is an enabler rather than the main contribution.  
- Demonstrates successful control across diverse robotic platforms and a range of tasks using trained feedback controllers.  
- Exceptionally well-executed work with outstanding technical rigor and clarity.  
- Represents a significant advancement in integrating control theory, neural networks, and robotic system design.  
- Results are compelling and show versatility across varied body morphologies and tasks.  
- Elegant synthesis of neural policy learning and feedback-driven robustness, offering a strong framework for future research.  
- Thorough experimental validation and exceptionally clear presentation, making the contributions broadly accessible.  
- Overall an inspiring contribution that meaningfully advances the state of the art.
