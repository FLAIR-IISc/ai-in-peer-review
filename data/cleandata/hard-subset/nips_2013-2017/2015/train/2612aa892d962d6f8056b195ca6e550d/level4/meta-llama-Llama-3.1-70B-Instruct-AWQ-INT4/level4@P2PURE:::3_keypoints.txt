- Proposes a novel real-time 3D character control algorithm where a neural network is trained offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs.
- Key technical contributions are alternating/joint training of CIO and NN and noise injection via data augmentation and additive hidden-layer noise to improve generalization.
- Experiments show the network can produce realistic, stable control policies for multiple character models.
- Joint training and noise injection are significant and nontrivial steps that substantially help avoid overfitting.
- Experimental evaluation is limited by a small training set (100–200 base trials with 5 branches each), which likely causes overfitting in the "no noise" variant.
- The "no joint" variant might also perform better with more diverse training trajectories; dataset size/diversity is a concern.
- Comparative evaluation is weak—only a brief MPC comparison is presented and lacks a proper reference or broader benchmarking against competing methods.
- Some mathematical explanations are clear, but key parts (notably the CIO step and Eq. 6) are superficially treated and use undefined notation.
- Section 3.2 (optimal actions for noisy inputs) is confusing: notations and the role of time index t are unclear and need clarification.
- Providing code would benefit reproducibility and adoption in the community.
- Real-time computation timings and scaling with model complexity are missing but important, especially for CPU-limited applications like games.
- Minor phrasing and terminology fixes needed (e.g., "biped location" → "locomotion"; "unity" → "unite/unify").
- Clarify practical modeling choices: target currently seems only xyz positions without facing angle—consider discussing adding a target angle.
- "Noise injection" term may be misleading since it refers to data augmentation with re-computed targets rather than denoising autoencoders.
- Clarify algorithmic details: whether initial random weights theta are used in the first iteration of Alg. 1 and why the same hyperparameter eta is used in both steps.
- Paper contains several minor errors and inconsistencies (missing articles, incorrect acronyms, typos) that should be fixed.
- Overall assessment: promising approach, but practical benefits, broader comparisons, clearer exposition, and additional experiments are needed to establish applicability.
