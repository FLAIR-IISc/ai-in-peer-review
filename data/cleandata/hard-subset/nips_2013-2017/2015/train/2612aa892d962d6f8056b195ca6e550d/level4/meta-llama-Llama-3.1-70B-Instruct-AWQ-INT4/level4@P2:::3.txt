The paper "Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks" proposes a novel approach for real-time control of 3D models using a neural network (NN) trained offline to reproduce the output of Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO). The core elements of the method include alternating CIO and NN training, injection of noise through data augmentation and additive noise in the NN's hidden layers, and the use of a distributed training architecture.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a range of complex character models, including 3D swimming, flying, biped, and quadruped walking tasks. The experimental results show that the proposed method can produce realistic and stable control policies, outperforming a naive neural network training approach and a model-predictive control (MPC) method.

The paper is well-written, and the mathematical derivations are clearly explained and motivated. However, some points are only addressed superficially, and additional references are needed to fully understand the CIO step and other technical details. Providing code would be highly appreciated by the community.

One of the weaknesses of the paper is the limited comparative evaluation. The authors acknowledge that a systematic comparison with more direct methods remains to be done. The only comparison to a competing method is a brief one with MPC at the end of the paper. Additionally, the experimental results would be stronger if comparisons were made with a larger dataset.

Some minor remarks include the use of unclear notation in section 3.2, the lack of time measurements for computations during real-time control, and the need for clarification on some technical details, such as the target angle constraint and the use of initial network weights.

Overall, the paper presents a promising approach for real-time control of complex character models using neural networks. However, further evaluation and comparison with other methods are needed to establish its practical benefits and applicability.

Strengths:
- Novel approach for real-time control of 3D models using neural networks
- Effective in producing realistic and stable control policies for complex character models
- Well-written and clearly explained mathematical derivations

Weaknesses:
- Limited comparative evaluation
- Experimental results would be stronger with a larger dataset
- Some technical details are only addressed superficially
- Minor notation and clarity issues

Arguments for acceptance:
- The paper presents a novel and effective approach for real-time control of complex character models.

Arguments against acceptance:
