- Proposes interactive control by jointly learning target trajectories and a tracking policy, combining trajectory optimization with policy learning.  
- Key insight: joint optimization can be decoupled into alternating subproblems, enabling reuse of established methods and practical effectiveness.  
- Demonstrates high technical sophistication and strong empirical results, emphasizing engineering and system implementation.  
- Practical contributions are well-supported; sensory noise during training and joint trajectory-policy learning show clear experimental benefits.  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and provides appropriate detail with useful cross-references.  
- Main confusion: unclear motivation for the additional online re-optimization step (Section 7) — why the learned policy cannot be used directly at runtime.  
- Reviewer suspects re-optimization may prevent accumulation of trajectory cost or regression error and notes it allows larger timesteps, but requests clarification.  
- Minor requests: add citations for the "similar to recent approaches" update rule (Line 153) and fix a typo at Line 89 ("to unify").  
- Overall judgment: strong technical contribution with compelling results, though novelty and long-term significance are hard to assess given the reviewer’s partial expertise.
