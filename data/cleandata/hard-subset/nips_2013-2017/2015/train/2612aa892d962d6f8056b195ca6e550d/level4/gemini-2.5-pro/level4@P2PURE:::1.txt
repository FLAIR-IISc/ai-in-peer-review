The authors propose a strategy for embedding feedback gains within neural network policies to enhance their robustness against disturbances and approximation errors. The core of their method involves softly coupling trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for deviation. This coupling encourages the policy to learn effective approximations of optimal trajectories that are simpler and more robust than those generated by MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. While the approach leverages sophisticated parallelization for optimization, this is presented as an implementation detail rather than a central contribution. The effectiveness of the trained feedback controllers is demonstrated on a diverse set of robots and tasks.

This is a paper of the highest quality, written with exceptional clarity, and representing a technical tour de force with impressive application results. The work is an inspiring synthesis of techniques from computer graphics, control theory, and neural networks, culminating in a powerful method for generating controllers for a wide variety of robot morphologies and tasks.
