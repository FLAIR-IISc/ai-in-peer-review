This paper presents a novel and effective method for learning robust neural network control policies. The core contribution is a strategy that embeds feedback gains directly within the policy network, significantly enhancing its robustness to both external disturbances and internal approximation errors. The authors achieve this through a soft coupling of trajectory optimization and policy optimization, mediated by a cost penalty for deviation. This unique formulation encourages the policy to learn effective approximations of optimal trajectories that are demonstrably simpler and more robust than those generated by traditional Model Predictive Control (MPC) methods alone. The framework is validated on a diverse and challenging set of simulated robotic systems, where the trained feedback controllers produce remarkably stable and natural-looking motions.

This is a paper of the highest quality. It is a comprehensive, exceptionally well-executed, and inspiring body of work that will be of great interest to the NIPS community.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Technical Contribution and Novelty:** The central idea of softly coupling trajectory and policy optimization is both elegant and highly effective. By penalizing deviation rather than enforcing strict adherence, the system allows for the discovery of simpler, more natural, and ultimately more robust control strategies. This represents a significant conceptual advance over methods that treat trajectory optimization and policy learning as separate, sequential steps.

2.  **Exceptional Empirical Results:** The empirical validation is a tour de force. The method is successfully applied to a diverse set of robotic systems with varied morphologies and tasks, providing compelling evidence of the controllers' robustness and versatility. The quality of the resulting motions is outstanding and demonstrates a state-of-the-art capability in physics-based character animation and control. While not the primary scientific contribution, the sophisticated use of parallelization for optimization is indicative of the high level of technical execution throughout the work.

3.  **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear. The authors do an excellent job of motivating the problem, explaining their technical approach, and presenting the results. The work represents a beautiful synthesis of ideas from machine learning, control theory, and computer graphics, and it is presented in a manner that is both accessible and inspiring.

**Weaknesses:**

I find no significant weaknesses in this work.

**Conclusion:**

This is a landmark paper that masterfully integrates concepts from control theory and machine learning to solve challenging problems in robotic locomotion. The work is technically profound, empirically impressive, and presented with exceptional clarity. I strongly recommend acceptance.
