- Combines Contact-Invariant trajectory optimization with supervised learning of a recurrent stochastic policy via alternating optimization.  
- Policy outputs desired velocities and contact forces, includes small recurrent state, and is trained with injected sensory and hidden-unit noise for robustness.  
- Derives stabilizing feedback gains from trajectory optimization using a perturbation-based approach, avoiding a separate LQG pass.  
- Training implemented in a distributed, asynchronous GPU/EC2 pipeline with proximal regularization to stabilize alternating updates.  
- Positions work relative to learning-from-trajectory-optimization literature and contrasts with model-free RL and hand-crafted controllers.  
- Claimed advance: scalable, single-architecture control for diverse high-DOF 3D behaviors (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped locomotion).  
- Technically sophisticated and well-engineered end-to-end system from trajectory generation to distributed training.  
- Clear empirical evidence that noise injection and joint alternating optimization substantially improve test-time robustness (ablation results).  
- Strong, diverse experimental results producing stable, plausible behaviors without motion capture or task-specific state machines.  
- Paper is well organized and sufficiently detailed with modular sections and helpful cross-references.  
- Question about execution-time per-timestep optimization: need clearer motivation and trade-off discussion (stability, accuracy, computational cost).  
- Paper delivers engineering and empirical contributions but lacks novel theoretical insights, making broader impact/novelty assessment harder.  
- Request to add references for stochastic/proximal regularization viewpoint (trust-region/proximal methods) mentioned in Line 153.  
- Evaluation: method is technically sound with supported claims; feedback-gain reuse and asynchronous proximal training are practical contributions.  
- Pros for acceptance: robust, general real-time control across morphologies; clear evidence for joint optimization and noise benefits; practical system others can build on.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical novelty; unclear necessity/implications of execution-time optimization; relative novelty vs. recent work uncertain.  
- Minor edits: fix typo “to unity” → “to unify” (Line 89) and add proximal/stochastic reference(s) at Line 153.  
- Overall recommendation: positive with reservations—compelling systems contribution but clarify execution-time optimization and strengthen novelty/contextualization.
