- Paper proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped walking) using supervised learning + trajectory optimization with noise and task changes.  
- Main contribution: a general synthesis method applicable to arbitrary behaviors, morphologies, online objective changes, noise, and modeling errors.  
- Method: interleave Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) to find optimal trajectories with supervised learning of a neural network from those trajectories.  
- Results are impressive: demonstrate stable, realistic behaviors across multiple characters and tasks.  
- Major concern: the paper assumes full model knowledge but does not state this assumption early or discuss real-world implications.  
- Section 4 is too short and lacks sufficient detail on the neural network architecture, especially the recurrent units referenced in the abstract.  
- Unclear claim about using a stochastic neural network—training data is stochastic but the network appears deterministic; authors must clarify.  
- Paper structure and flow are unclear; sections should be more distinct and reorganized for readability.  
- NIPS criteria: strong on significance, originality, and quality of results; weak on clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective method; impressive results; addresses an important control/robotics problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unstated/full model knowledge assumption, poor clarity/structure, missing methodological details (e.g., network architecture).  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify assumptions, expand methodological details, and improve paper organization.
