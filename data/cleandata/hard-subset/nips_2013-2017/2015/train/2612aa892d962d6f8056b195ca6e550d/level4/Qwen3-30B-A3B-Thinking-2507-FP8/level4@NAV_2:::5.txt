

The paper introduces a method for training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical systems including swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal locomotion. It combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization (specifically Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate stable, realistic behaviors without requiring motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines. The key innovation lies in the neural network's ability to learn both optimal controls and a model of dynamics simultaneously, with a small number of recurrent units implementing memory states beyond the physical system state.

Strengths:
- The approach successfully demonstrates interactive control across a wide range of 3D character types with a single architecture, showing significant generalization capabilities beyond previous 2D work.
- The ablation studies (Section 8.1) provide strong empirical evidence for the necessity of the proposed techniques, particularly the joint optimization approach and noise injection.
- The method produces realistic, purposeful behaviors rather than the chaotic movements often seen in direct learning approaches.

Weaknesses:
- The paper would benefit from explicitly stating at the beginning that full model knowledge (Eq. 6) is required, as this is a significant assumption not clearly articulated upfront.
- Section 4 (Neural Network Policy Regression) is disproportionately short compared to Section 6 (Distributed Training Architecture), and fails to adequately address the recurrent units mentioned in the abstract.
- The description of the stochastic neural network (line 405) is confusing; while the training data is stochastic, the network itself appears deterministic, requiring clarification.

Quality: The technical approach is sound with well-designed experiments, but the requirement for full model knowledge limits applicability to scenarios where such models are available. The authors are careful in evaluating both strengths and weaknesses.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-organized but would benefit from better balance between sections and clearer articulation of the recurrent units' role. The description of the stochastic nature of the neural network requires clarification.

Originality: While the components (trajectory optimization, neural networks) are not novel, the specific integration and combination of these techniques for interactive control of diverse characters represents a significant advancement over previous work limited to 2D or simpler character types.

Significance: The work demonstrates a promising path toward universal character controllers with potential applications in animation, robotics, and biomechanics. The unified framework for diverse locomotion tasks advances the state of the art in interactive character control.

Pro:
- Demonstrates impressive generalization across diverse 3D character types with a single architecture
- Provides strong empirical evidence for the necessity of the proposed techniques
- Addresses a significant gap in the literature for interactive control of complex characters

Con:
- Requires full model knowledge (Eq. 6), limiting applicability to scenarios where such models exist
- Section 4 inadequately addresses recurrent units from the abstract
- Stochastic nature of the network is not clearly explained

Overall, the paper presents a technically sound approach with significant potential impact, but would benefit from better organization, clearer articulation of key technical points, and addressing the imbalance between sections. The results are compelling, but the manuscript would be stronger with a more balanced presentation and clearer discussion of the method's requirements and limitations.
