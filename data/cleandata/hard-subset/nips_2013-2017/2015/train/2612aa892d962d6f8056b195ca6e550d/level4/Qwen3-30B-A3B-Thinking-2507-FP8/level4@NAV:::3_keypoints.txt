- Paper proposes training an NN offline to replicate CIO outputs for real-time 3D model control.  
- Key innovation is iterative, interdependent training of CIO and NN where each informs the other's optimization.  
- Novel noise injection via data augmentation (small perturbations and additive hidden-layer noise) improves generalization.  
- Experiments show realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.  
- Approach is compelling for real-time control of arbitrary models, addressing a significant challenge.  
- Core idea is straightforward, but joint training and noise injection are substantial, validated contributions.  
- Experimental validation is weak due to small training dataset (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments each).  
- Overfitting in the "no noise" variant is unsurprising given limited training trajectories.  
- The "no joint" variant might underperform because of insufficiently diverse training data.  
- Comparative evaluation is the paper’s weakest aspect; only a brief, unreferenced MPC comparison is provided.  
- Paper acknowledges lack of systematic comparisons with more direct methods but does not provide them.  
- Mathematical exposition is generally clear but lacks references needed for replication, especially for the CIO step (e.g., undefined notations in Eq. 6).  
- Section 3.2 has confusing/ambiguous notation regarding optimal action generation for noisy inputs (time index t dropped unclear).  
- Code availability is requested to aid replication.  
- Time measurements for real-time computation (CPU constraints) are missing and would be valuable for practical applications.  
- Terminology/language issues: "biped location" likely means locomotion; "unity these disparate approaches" should be unite/unify.  
- Target specification unclear: only x,y,z targeted; angle constraint appears missing.  
- "Noise injection" is a misnomer here: the data augmentation recomputes targets for modified inputs rather than denoising autoencoder-style noise.  
- Algorithm 1 questions: are initial network weights θ used for the first X computation?; justification for identical hyperparameter η in both steps?  
- LQG acronym is used before definition; phrase "use Linear Quadratic Gaussian (LQG) pass" missing "a".  
- Several technical typos/errors: misplaced star in argmin (l.231); \bar{s} missing in \tilde{C}; "minimizer of a quadratic around optimal trajectory" missing word.  
- Question about guarantee that sX and aX are subsets of φX—need justification.  
- Clarify reference to "single pass over the data to reduce the objective in (4)"—should reference Algorithm 1 objective.  
- Section 6’s asynchronous scheme differs significantly from Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme and should be noted earlier.  
- Suggest splitting "no noise" variant into separate "no data augmentation" and "no hidden noise injection" ablations.  
- Investigate whether α can be reduced to zero during CIO optimization (curriculum learning vs. persistent constraint).  
- Table 1a caption mismatch: states "4 layers" but appears to describe 3 layers.  
- Typo: l.315 f should be f^{des}?  
- Clarify phrase "dynamics constraints are enforced softly... may include some root forces" — currently unclear.
