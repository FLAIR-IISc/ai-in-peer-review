- Introduces a real-time 3D character control method where a neural network is iteratively trained to mimic a Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) planner, with joint optimization and noise injection.  
- Joint training scheme and hidden-layer plus input-noise (data augmentation) injection are claimed as key innovations that enable strong generalization.  
- Experiments show realistic, stable control across diverse character models but use a limited training set (100–200 trials), which likely causes overfitting in ablations.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief, uncited comparison to MPC is provided; more baselines are needed.  
- Mathematical derivations are mostly clear but omit important details; replication would require consulting additional references.  
- CIO step has notation problems (Equation 6 undefined) and Section 3.2 is confusing, with unclear procedure and missing time index t.  
- Performance metrics (computation time and scaling with model complexity) are missing but essential for real-time/CPU-constrained applications.  
- Releasing source code would significantly aid reproducibility and community uptake.  
- Terminology concern: calling recomputed-target perturbations “noise injection” is misleading—this is better described as data augmentation.  
- Ablation design could be improved; “no noise” should be split into “no data augmentation” and “no hidden noise injection.”  
- Unclear implementation details / questions: whether initial trajectories use random network weights, why the same hyperparameter eta is used for both Algorithm 1 steps, and differences between asynchronous joint optimization and alternating Algorithm 1.  
- Multiple minor typographical and notation issues throughout (e.g., “biped location” vs locomotion, “unity” vs unite/unify, misplaced LQG acronym, missing articles, misplaced star in argmin, etc.).  
- Specific technical queries: whether the target includes orientation, why sX and aX are subsets of φX, whether f should be f^{des}, and the meaning of soft-enforced dynamics/root forces.  
- Useful future investigations suggested: annealing alpha to zero during CIO to test for curriculum effects, and providing computation-time scaling experiments.  
- Overall assessment: promising and compelling approach but requires stronger experiments, clearer presentation, and more comparisons to establish practical benefits.
