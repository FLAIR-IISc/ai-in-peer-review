- Proposes a novel approach combining trajectory optimization with policy learning via a joint alternating optimization framework.
- Main contribution: joint learning framework that alternates between optimizing trajectories and policy parameters, leveraging existing solvers for each subproblem.
- Demonstrates strong technical expertise in concept, implementation, and exposition.
- Paper is well-organized with clear sections and useful cross-references.
- Empirically shows benefits of using sensory noise during training.
- Empirically shows benefits of learning trajectories jointly with the policy.
- Results are impressive and the optimization scheme and implementation appear sensible.
- Additional optimization step in Section 7 is under-explained; motivation and necessity need clearer articulation.
- Possible rationale noted: policy actions alone may not ensure physically plausible behavior due to residual trajectory costs or regression errors—this should be clarified.
- Claim “similar to recent approaches” (line 153) needs one or two supporting references.
- The guideline to keep parameter updates small seems counterintuitive, but the authors’ specific justification is convincing.
- Minor typo: change “to unify” to “to unite” (line 89).
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of approach, technical execution, strong results, demonstrated training benefits.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear impact/novelty to non-experts, unclear Section 7 explanation, missing references on update-size claim.
