- Paper shows interactive control of simulated characters using a compact neural-network policy trained offline via trajectory optimization; single architecture works across diverse morphologies and quasi-cyclic behaviors.
- Work appears to apply deep learning to established trajectory-optimization techniques rather than introduce a clearly novel methodology.
- Authors must better articulate novelty relative to prior work and explicitly compare to relevant recent research (e.g., Pieter Abbeel, Sergey Levine).
- Clarify claimed advantages over prior methods: learning speed, generality to character models, runtime cost, and robustness to perturbations.
- Explain what is meant by "very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" and provide evidence or examples.
- Move the justification for not using LQG to after the proposed method is fully described for clearer narrative flow.
- Fix confusing notation in lines 226–240 (s(X) = \bar s and the cost involving s(X) - \bar s); bar/tilde visual similarity impedes parsing.
- Justify and explain the claim that the approach "produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ," including the baseline for comparison and a definition of "soft."
- Provide a rationale for modifying the initial state as part of the proposed procedure.
- Overall evaluation: technical execution is sound, but the paper lacks clarity about its contributions relative to recent related literature.
