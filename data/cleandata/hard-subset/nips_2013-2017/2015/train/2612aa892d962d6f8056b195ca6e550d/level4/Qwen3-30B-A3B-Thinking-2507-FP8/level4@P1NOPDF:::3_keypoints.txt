- Proposes a real-time 3D character control algorithm using an NN trained offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs with alternating joint training.  
- Key innovations are alternating CIO/NN training and noise injection (data augmentation + additive hidden-layer noise) that improve generalization.  
- Experiments show realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models, demonstrating the method’s promise.  
- The core idea is straightforward, but the synergistic integration of joint training and noise is the main novel contribution.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: training data is limited (100–200 trials with 5 branches), risking overfitting in baselines.  
- Comparisons are insufficient: only a brief MPC evaluation with no strong baselines or detailed analysis.  
- A larger, more diverse dataset and rigorous benchmarks against trajectory-optimization and other baselines are needed.  
- Important replication details and references (especially for CIO) are missing; code release would help reproducibility.  
- Section 3.2 is unclear: notation becomes opaque (time index t dropped) and needs clearer exposition.  
- No real-time computational measurements or scaling analysis by 3D model complexity are provided—critical for practical use.  
- The target specification seems limited to x,y,z; no discussion of adding a facing angle which is practically important.  
- The term “noise injection” conflates standard data augmentation (recomputing targets for perturbed inputs) with denoising; clarify terminology.  
- Algorithm 1 ambiguities: does first iteration use random θ to compute X or unregularized X, and why use the same η in both steps?  
- Section 6’s asynchronous optimization differs significantly from Algorithm 1’s alternating scheme and should be highlighted earlier.  
- The “no noise” variant conflates no data augmentation and no hidden-layer noise; these should be separated in experiments.  
- Key experimental question left open: can α be reduced to zero during CIO optimization, testing reliance on curriculum vs. network proximity?  
- Many mathematical/notation issues and typos: undefined notation in Eq.6, misplaced star in argmin (Line 231), LQG used before definition, and f vs. f^{des} (Line 315).  
- Several unclear claims/definitions: justification that sX and aX are subsets of φX, and “a single pass over the data to reduce the objective in (4)” needs precise reference.  
- Minor editorial fixes: “Biped location”→locomotion, “Unity these disparate approaches”→unite/unify, clarify sentence about enforcing dynamics constraints softly, and Table 1a caption (4 vs 3 layers).
