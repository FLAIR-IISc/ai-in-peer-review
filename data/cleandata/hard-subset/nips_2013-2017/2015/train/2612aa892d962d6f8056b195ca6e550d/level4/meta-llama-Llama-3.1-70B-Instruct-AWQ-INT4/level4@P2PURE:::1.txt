The author proposes a strategy for embedding feedback gains in neural network policies, enhancing their robustness to disturbances and regression errors. By softly coupling trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for deviation, the policy can learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories that are simpler and more robust than those obtained through MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. The use of sophisticated parallelization to optimize tasks is a notable aspect of the paper, although it serves as a means to an end rather than the primary focus. The trained feedback controllers are successfully applied to a diverse set of robots.

This paper is of exceptional quality, boasting clarity, technical mastery, and impressive results in its applications. The work seamlessly integrates graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers for a wide range of body morphologies and tasks, making it a truly inspiring contribution to the field.
