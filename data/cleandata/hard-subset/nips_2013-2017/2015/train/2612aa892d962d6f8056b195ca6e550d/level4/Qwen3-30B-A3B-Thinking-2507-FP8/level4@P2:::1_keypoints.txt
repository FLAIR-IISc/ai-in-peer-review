- Proposes a novel joint optimization framework combining trajectory optimization with neural network policy learning for robust, real-time character locomotion.  
- Uses recurrent neural networks that learn control policies and implicit dynamics models, incorporating optimal feedback gains.  
- Introduces a principled noise injection strategy to enable learning of optimal feedback within the network.  
- Soft coupling via a quadratic penalty between trajectory optimization and network training stabilizes learning and produces realistic behaviors without hand-crafted tuning.  
- Extends prior methods (e.g., CIO) to challenging 3D locomotion tasks, unlike earlier 2D-limited work.  
- Single architecture successfully controls diverse characters (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without task-specific state machines or mocap.  
- Technically sound with solid theoretical grounding and clear mathematical derivations.  
- Thorough experimental validation including ablation studies showing necessity of key components and robustness to perturbations.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and supported by effective figures and a supplementary video.  
- Original contribution: integrating optimal feedback gains into policies via noise injection and scaling to diverse 3D morphologies.  
- Significant impact for animation, robotics, biomechanics, and interactive real-time applications.  
- Well-designed distributed training enabling large-scale experiments and reproducibility.  
- Main limitations: limited analysis of computational complexity/scaling and a thin exploration of connections to biological systems.  
- Overall recommendation: outstanding, highly influential work with a strong recommendation for acceptance.
