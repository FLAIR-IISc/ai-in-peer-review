## Review: Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks

This paper presents a method for training recurrent neural networks to act as feedback controllers for a variety of dynamical systems, demonstrating success in controlling locomotion for swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal characters. The approach combines supervised learning with trajectory optimization (specifically, Contact-Invariant Optimization – CIO) and employs techniques like noise injection and stochastic optimization to improve robustness and generalization. This work builds upon a rich history of neural network control, dating back to early applications in control theory (e.g., [21, 18, 13, 8, 7]) but aims to scale these approaches to more complex, high-dimensional control problems, addressing challenges similar to those tackled in Reinforcement Learning and stochastic optimal control. Recent related work leveraging neural networks and trajectory optimization exists ([9, 11]), but this paper distinguishes itself by achieving stable and realistic 3D locomotion – a feat not previously demonstrated in prior work.

The core idea is to use a neural network to *learn from* a trajectory optimizer, effectively distilling the optimizer’s knowledge into a form suitable for real-time, interactive control. The network directly outputs velocity commands, learning a mapping from state to action rather than a discrete policy. The authors highlight the importance of interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injecting noise during training, preparing for unexpected task changes, and leveraging trajectory optimization for optimal feedback gain estimation. The paper also details a distributed stochastic optimization architecture utilizing cloud computing and GPU acceleration.

**Strengths:**

* **Broad Applicability:** The method successfully controls a diverse range of characters and locomotion types with a single network architecture and parameter set, demonstrating impressive generality.
* **Stable 3D Locomotion:** Achieving stable and realistic locomotion in 3D space is a significant accomplishment, overcoming limitations of previous work in this area.
* **Novel Combination:** The combination of trajectory optimization and neural network learning, along with the specific techniques employed (noise injection, feedback gain estimation), presents a novel approach to complex control.
* **Detailed Implementation:** The paper provides a comprehensive description of the training process, distributed architecture, and physics simulation details, enhancing reproducibility.
* **Clear Presentation of Challenges:** The authors clearly articulate the difficulties of learning neural network controllers compared to classification tasks (e.g., precise real-valued outputs, closed-loop dynamics, overfitting).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Model Knowledge Requirement:** The method fundamentally relies on having a complete and accurate dynamical model of the character (Equation 6), a point not sufficiently emphasized in the introduction. This limits applicability to systems where such a model is available.
* **Disproportionate Section Lengths:** Sections 4 (Neural Network Policy Regression) and 6 (Distributed Training Architecture) are relatively detailed, while key aspects of the architecture like the recurrent units mentioned in the abstract are not elaborated in Section 4. A more balanced presentation would be beneficial. Specifically, the role of the recurrent units and how their state is incorporated into the control loop deserves more discussion.
* **Stochasticity Clarification:** The description of stochasticity (l. 405) is slightly unclear. While the *training data* is stochastic due to noise injection, the resulting neural network appears deterministic at execution time. Clarifying this point would be helpful.
* **Limited Comparative Analysis:** While the ablation study in Section 8.1 provides some comparative insights, a more thorough comparison with alternative control methods (e.g., Model Predictive Control (MPC) as suggested in the paper) would strengthen the evaluation.
* **Clarity of Contributions:** While the overall approach is novel, a more explicit delineation of *specific* contributions beyond simply combining existing techniques could enhance the paper's impact.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a significant advance in automated control of complex characters. The method's ability to generate stable and realistic locomotion across a diverse set of morphologies is impressive. While the reliance on full model knowledge is a limitation, the demonstrated results and the novel combination of techniques justify serious consideration. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Solves a challenging problem (stable 3D locomotion) that has proven difficult for previous approaches.
* Demonstrates strong performance across a wide variety of characters and tasks.
* Presents a novel and well-implemented combination of trajectory optimization and neural network learning.
* Includes a detailed description of the methodology and implementation, fostering reproducibility.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The method requires a complete and accurate dynamical model, limiting its applicability.
* The paper lacks a comprehensive comparative analysis with alternative control methods.
* The level of detail is uneven across different sections.




**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** I recommend acceptance contingent upon addressing the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, the authors should: 1) Emphasize the requirement for a complete dynamical model more prominently. 2) Expand the discussion of the recurrent units in Section 4. 3) Clarify the nature of stochasticity in the control policy. 4) Consider including a more thorough comparative analysis with MPC or other relevant control methods. 5) Rebalance the section lengths for better clarity and flow.
