

The author proposes a strategy for embedding feedback gains within neural network policies, enhancing robustness to disturbances and regression errors. Trajectory optimization and policy optimization are softly coupled through a deviation cost penalty, enabling the policy to learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories that are inherently simpler and more robust than those produced by MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. While the approach employs sophisticated parallelization for task optimization, this serves merely as a means to an end rather than the paper's central contribution. The trained feedback controllers successfully govern a diverse set of robots. This paper is of the highest quality, exhibits exceptional clarity, represents a tour de force in technique, and presents striking results in its applications. Bravo. This is a beautiful body of work that integrates graphics, control, and neural networks to enable controller generation for diverse body morphologies and tasks. It is deeply inspiring.
