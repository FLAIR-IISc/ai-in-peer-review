This paper presents a method for interactive control of complex characters by combining trajectory optimization with policy learning. The central contribution is a framework for jointly learning a control policy and the reference trajectories it aims to follow. The authors propose an effective empirical strategy: decoupling the joint optimization into two alternating subproblems. This approach has the dual advantage of allowing the use of established, well-understood optimization methods for each subproblem while demonstrating strong performance in practice.

**Strengths:**
The paper is commendable for its technical sophistication, which is evident from the high-level concepts down to the implementation details, and is supported by impressive results. The authors provide clear empirical evidence for their key claims, particularly the benefits of using sensory noise during training and the advantages of learning the policy and trajectories jointly.

The paper is also very well-written and structured. The authors provide an appropriate level of detail to navigate a complex problem, and the use of cross-references between sections is helpful for the reader.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**
One area that could be clarified is the motivation for the additional online optimization step described in Section 7. After the joint training procedure, I expected the learned policy to be sufficient for control at test time. The necessity of this extra optimization step was not immediately obvious. Could the authors elaborate on why this is necessary? Is it to correct for residual errors from the policy regression or trajectory cost, which might otherwise accumulate when interacting with the physics simulator? While the physics engine would prevent physically implausible states, it is unclear what kind of errors this step is designed to mitigate. I do recognize the paper's point that this online re-optimization allows for the use of larger timesteps, which is a clear practical benefit.

On line 153, the paper mentions that keeping parameter updates small is "similar to recent approaches". It would be helpful to include one or two citations here to ground this claim. While the rationale for this choice becomes clearer in the context of the paper's alternating optimization scheme, providing references would strengthen the argument.

**Minor Comments:**
Line 89: A word appears to be missing after "to unify".

**Conclusion:**
In summary, this paper presents a technically impressive and well-executed project. The proposed optimization framework appears sensible, and the results are compelling. My main reservation in assessing the work stems from my limited expertise in this specific subfield, which makes it difficult for me to confidently evaluate the novelty and potential impact of the contributions relative to the state of the art. Despite this, I believe the work is of high quality.
