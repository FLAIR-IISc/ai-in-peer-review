- Paper proposes an algorithm for real-time 3D character control using a neural network trained offline to mimic Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs.  
- Key novelty: alternating, interdependent training of CIO and NN so each influences the other's optimization criterion.  
- Key novelty: noise injection via data augmentation and additive noise in NN hidden layers to improve generalization.  
- Experiments show the network can produce realistic, stable control policies across multiple character models.  
- The approach is intriguing and practically challenging; joint training and noise injection are meaningful contributions.  
- Experimental dataset is small (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), weakening empirical claims and explaining overfitting in the "no noise" variant.  
- The "no joint" variant may also suffer from limited diversity and could benefit from more training trajectories.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief MPC comparison late in the paper, lacking proper references and systematic baselines.  
- Mathematical formulation is mostly clear but some parts (e.g., CIO step / Eq. 6) omit definitions and require consulting external refs.  
- Section 3.2 on generating optimal actions for noisy inputs is hard to follow; notational confusion and unclear time-indexing hinder reproducibility.  
- Releasing code would substantially help replication and community use.  
- Real-time computation times and scaling with 3D model complexity are missing and important for practical deployment (e.g., games).  
- Terminology and wording issues: "biped location" likely means "locomotion"; "unity" should be "unite" or "unify".  
- Clarify target specification: are only x,y,z coordinates used and can a target facing angle be added practically?  
- The term "noise injection" is misleading here; the data perturbation is better described as "data augmentation" distinct from denoising autoencoders.  
- Clarify whether initial random weights θ are used to compute initial X's in Algorithm 1 or if X's are computed without the regularizer initially.  
- Explain rationale for using the same hyperparameter η in both steps of Algorithm 1.  
- Multiple minor editorial/notation issues: missing "the", LQG introduced before definition, "a pass" grammar, misplaced star in argmin, possible missing \bar{s} in \tilde{C}, and other typos.  
- Questionable/missing derivation details: missing word in "minimizer of a quadratic...", why sX and aX are subsets of φX, and whether objective (4) reference is precise.  
- Asynchronous joint optimization in Section 6 differs notably from alternating Algorithm 1 and this distinction should be mentioned earlier.  
- Specific technical clarifications requested: should f be f^{des} at Line 315; unclear sentence about soft dynamics constraints and root forces; split "no noise" into "no data augmentation" vs "no hidden noise injection."  
- Suggest investigating reducing α to zero during optimization to test whether benefits come from curriculum learning or always maintaining trajectories near learnable network outputs.  
- Caption error: Table 1a claims 4 layers but likely reports 3.  
- Overall: impressive application of NN to character control, but practical benefits, comparisons, and broader applicability need stronger evidence and clearer exposition.
