The authors propose a strategy to embed feedback gains directly within neural network policies, resulting in robust control performance in the presence of disturbances and regression error. This approach softly couples trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a cost penalty for deviation, enabling the policy to learn reasonable, and importantly, simpler and more robust approximations of optimal trajectories than those typically produced by model predictive control (MPC). The optimization process benefits from sophisticated parallelization, though this is presented as an implementation detail supporting the core contribution rather than a primary focus. The efficacy of the method is demonstrated through control of a diverse set of robotic morphologies and tasks.

This is a paper of exceptional quality, exhibiting remarkable clarity and representing a significant technical achievement. The results are compelling and beautifully demonstrate the synergy between graphics, control, and neural networks in the generation of controllers for a wide variety of body morphologies and tasks. It is truly inspiring work.
