“Interactive Control of Diverse Complex Characters with Neural Networks” demonstrates that a compact, fast neural network can encode a feedback policy for interactive control of physically simulated characters. The method uses trajectory optimization during training to supervise a neural policy, and the main empirical claim is that a single architecture suffices across a variety of largely periodic behaviors and diverse morphologies.

General Comments
My interpretation is that this is an application of modern deep learning to a recently established pipeline in computer graphics and control. Prior work has already shown that trajectory optimization can synthesize realistic control signals, and that neural networks can be trained to reproduce action sequences that track optimized trajectories (e.g., [9]). Given this context, the paper should more clearly situate its contributions relative to these precedents. In particular, the closest related line appears to be recent work by Pieter Abbeel and Sergey Levine. How does the present approach compare along concrete axes: faster training convergence, broader coverage of body morphologies, lower computational cost at runtime, or improved robustness to environmental perturbations?

Specific Comments
- What precisely is meant by “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains”? Please quantify (e.g., norms, condition numbers), and clarify under what objectives/linearizations this arises.
- Consider moving the rationale for not using LQG to follow your own feedback-gain computation method, so that the contrast is clearer after the proposed approach is fully specified.
- The notation around lines 226–240 is hard to follow: you define s(X) = s̄ and then write the cost using s(X) − s̄. The visual similarity of overbars and tildes makes the derivation difficult to parse; consider simplifying symbols or adding a brief glossary.
- “Softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” needs clarification. Softer than what baseline (e.g., LQG with hard constraints)? What operationally characterizes “soft” here (magnitude, smoothness, bandwidth), and why is relaxing the initial-state constraint beneficial in your setting?
- Sound work, but the contribution relative to other recent related work is not made sufficiently explicit.
