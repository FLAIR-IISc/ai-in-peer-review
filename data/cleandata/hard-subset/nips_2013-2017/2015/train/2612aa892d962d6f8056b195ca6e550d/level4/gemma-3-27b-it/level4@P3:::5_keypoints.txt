- Presents a method to train recurrent neural networks as feedback controllers for diverse dynamical characters, distilling trajectory optimizer knowledge into real-time control.
- Demonstrates broad applicability by controlling swimming, flying, bipedal, and quadrupedal locomotion with a single architecture and parameter set.
- Achieves stable and realistic 3D locomotion across multiple morphologies, addressing a major limitation of prior work.
- Combines trajectory optimization (CIO) with supervised learning, noise injection, and stochastic optimization for robustness and generalization.
- Uses a distributed stochastic optimization architecture with cloud and GPU acceleration and provides detailed implementation and simulation specifics.
- Emphasizes interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization and leveraging trajectory optimization for feedback gain estimation.
- Builds on a long history of neural network control and situates the work relative to recent related methods while claiming novel 3D results.
- Strength: novel combination of techniques and clear presentation of learning challenges specific to control tasks.
- Strength: detailed exposition enhances reproducibility.
- Weakness: method requires a complete and accurate dynamical model, limiting applicability and underemphasized in the introduction.
- Weakness: uneven section detail—Sections 4 and 6 are long while the recurrent unit design and its role are underexplained.
- Weakness: unclear description of stochasticity—training data is stochastic but the deployed network appears deterministic, needing clarification.
- Weakness: limited comparative analysis; more thorough comparisons with alternatives like MPC are needed.
- Weakness: contributions could be more explicitly delineated beyond combining existing techniques.
- Overall assessment: significant advance worthy of acceptance but constrained by model-dependence and evaluation gaps.
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a hard 3D locomotion problem, shows strong multi-character performance, and provides a well-implemented, reproducible approach.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on full model knowledge, lack of comprehensive comparisons, and uneven detail across sections.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address model requirement prominence, expand recurrent unit discussion, clarify stochasticity, add comparative analysis, and rebalance section lengths.
