- Proposes a joint optimization framework that combines trajectory optimization with neural network training to learn near-optimal feedback controllers.  
- Interleaves supervised learning with trajectory optimization, injects noise during training, and trains for unexpected task changes to produce stable closed-loop controllers.  
- Uses trajectory optimization to obtain optimal feedback gains used in network training.  
- Demonstrates success across diverse 3D locomotion tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without motion capture, task-specific features, or state machines.  
- Extends prior trajectory-optimization-based character control work to substantially more challenging 3D scenarios where previous methods struggled.  
- Distinguishes from RL approaches by using trajectory optimization for training data rather than direct reinforcement learning.  
- Technical approach is sound with rigorous theoretical analysis of feedback gains (Sec 3.2) and extensive experimental validation.  
- Ablation studies show key components (noise injection, joint optimization) are crucial; full method outperforms static training by ~100x.  
- Experimental design is thorough with appropriate baselines and evaluations across multiple morphologies.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and reproducible, with effective figures and a supplementary video.  
- Originality is strong: specific combination of techniques and successful application to varied morphologies is a notable advance.  
- High significance: enables general controllers for diverse characters with applications in robotics, animation, and biomechanics.  
- Limitation: lacks systematic comparison with direct RL methods (e.g., DDPG, TRPO).  
- Limitation: computational requirements (hours on cloud infrastructure) may limit accessibility.  
- Limitation: limited discussion of generalization to completely novel character morphologies not seen during training.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, well-executed contribution to character control and neural-network-based feedback control.
