The authors propose a strategy to embed feedback gains within neural network policies, enhancing their robustness to disturbances and regression error. Trajectory optimization and policy optimization are softly coupled via a cost penalty for deviation, which allows the policy to learn effective approximations of optimal trajectories. The authors demonstrate that these learned trajectories are often simpler and more robust than those generated by MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. While the optimization leverages sophisticated parallelization, this is framed as a means to an end rather than a core contribution. The resulting feedback controllers are successfully used to control a diverse set of robots.

This paper is of the highest quality. It is exceptionally clear, represents a technical tour de force, and presents compelling and beautifully rendered results. The work is an inspiring synthesis of graphics, control, and neural networks, brought together to generate controllers for a diversity of body morphologies and tasks.
