- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for complex characters to enable interactive control.
- Core idea: jointly learn trajectories and a control policy using trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization) to generate training data.
- Decoupling into alternating trajectory optimization and neural network regression allows effective use of existing methods and practical success.
- Not a fundamentally novel theoretical contribution, but a strong combination of techniques with impressive empirical results.
- Sophisticated approach and implementation across conceptual and practical levels with strong empirical performance.
- Incorporating sensory noise during training and joint trajectory/policy learning yields clear, demonstrated benefits.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- The necessity and rationale for the final real-time optimization step (Section 7) need clearer explanation (e.g., residual costs, regression error, timestep issues).
- Recommend adding citations for the phrase “similar to recent approaches...” (line 153).
- Minor typographical error on line 89 ("to unify").
- Results are visually compelling and the accompanying video is highly recommended.
- Reviewer’s limited domain expertise reduces confidence in assessing novelty and significance relative to the broader field.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clarity, practical decoupling approach, demonstrated benefits of components, and reproducibility.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, lack of reviewer domain expertise, and unclear motivation for the final optimization step.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with excellent execution; recommend acceptance, score 7/10 (Strong Accept).
