- Presents a method to train RNNs as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse physical characters without motion capture or task-specific features.  
- Core approach interleaves supervised learning with Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO), injects noise during training, and uses trajectory optimization to derive feedback gains.  
- Enables interactive real-time control across multiple body morphologies in 3D, extending prior work that was limited to 2D.  
- Uses a unified neural-network architecture to approximate both dynamics and control, producing stable, realistic behaviors where direct RL often fails.  
- Strong technical quality with rigorous ablation studies showing a 100× performance gain over static NN training and robustness to noise.  
- Trajectory optimization and feedback gain derivation are well-motivated and implemented as a byproduct of CIO.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear notation overall, thorough experimental validation across diverse characters, and an adequately described distributed training setup.  
- Originality: combining CIO with neural network regression for 3D locomotion without mocap or morphology-specific tuning is a novel unification of control approaches.  
- Significance: enables real-time control for diverse physical systems, generalizes across morphologies, handles online goal changes, and has clear robotics/animation applications.  
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from prior 2D work [9, 11]; needs explicit quantitative comparison to justify claimed advances.  
- Weakness: unclear/inconsistent notation in the feedback gain derivation (e.g., s vs. s̄) and vague wording like “softer feedback gains” that require clarification.  
- Weakness: LQG discussion should precede the perturbation method; claims that LQG produces ill-conditioned gains are unquantified.  
- Pro for acceptance: solves a hard 3D locomotion problem, demonstrates broad applicability, strong empirical validation, and unifies disparate control paradigms.  
- Con for acceptance: not sufficiently differentiated from related work and has clarity/notation issues around feedback gains.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to clarify novelty relative to [9, 11] and improve notation/explanations.
