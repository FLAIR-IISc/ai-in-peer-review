- Paper shows a compact neural network can encode an interactive control policy learned via trajectory optimization that generalizes across cyclic behaviors and multiple body types.  
- The method uses trajectory optimization during training, but the novelty over prior trajectory-optimization + neural network approaches is not clearly articulated.  
- Prior work (e.g., Abbeel & Levine) appears closely related; the authors should explicitly compare to that line of work (learning speed, morphology generality, runtime cost, robustness).  
- The claim "very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" needs clarification.  
- Suggest moving the discussion of why LQG was not used to after the core strategy presentation.  
- Notation in lines 226–240 is ambiguous (using s(X)= s̄ then a cost with s(X)-s̄ is visually confusing).  
- Explain what "softer feedback gains according to parameter λ" means, define "soft" vs "non-soft" gains, and justify altering the initial state as part of the procedure.  
- Overall assessment: sound work but the contribution relative to other recent related work lacks sufficient clarity.
