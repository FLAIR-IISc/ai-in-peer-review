- Paper proposes a real-time control algorithm that trains a neural network offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs, using alternating training where NN and CIO inform each other.  
- Key contributions include joint/alternating training of NN and CIO and noise injection (data augmentation + hidden-layer additive noise).  
- Experiments show the network can produce realistic, stable control policies across diverse character models.  
- Main weakness: comparative evaluation is limited — small training dataset (100–200 trials) likely causes overfitting, and only a single, under-referenced MPC comparison is provided.  
- Need more extensive comparisons to other direct methods and MPC baselines to establish advantages.  
- Reproducibility issues: CIO step and some equations (e.g., Eq. 6) use undefined notation and require consulting external references.  
- Section 3.2 is hard to follow due to confusing notation and disappearance of the time index t.  
- Request to release code to aid replication and community use.  
- Runtime performance during real-time control is not measured; computational cost could be a major bottleneck for applications like games.  
- Clarify whether network weights θ are initialized and whether X’s in Algorithm 1 are computed with or without regularization in the first iteration.  
- Justify using a single uniform hyperparameter (η) for both steps of Algorithm 1.  
- The asynchronous joint optimization in Section 6 differs significantly from the alternating scheme in Algorithm 1 and this discrepancy should be mentioned earlier.  
- Minor errors and unclear phrasing throughout: typos (“biped location” → “locomotion”, “unity” → “unify”), misplaced/missing symbols (argmin star, missing \bar{s} in \tilde{C}), and incomplete sentences/claims.  
- Specific notation/terminology fixes: define LQG before use, change phrasing to “a Linear Quadratic Gaussian (LQG) pass,” and correct “asynchronous and stochastic setting we have.”  
- Clarify whether the “no noise” variant mixes two effects; split into “no data augmentation” and “no hidden noise injection.”  
- Clarify and justify the guarantee that sX and aX are subsets of φX, and complete the statement about “the minimizer of a quadratic around optimal trajectory.”  
- Consider adding orientation targets (not just x,y,z) in objectives, and investigate whether α can be reduced to zero during CIO (possible curriculum learning effect).  
- Table/figure inconsistencies: Table 1a caption says 4 layers but appears to be 3 — fix.
