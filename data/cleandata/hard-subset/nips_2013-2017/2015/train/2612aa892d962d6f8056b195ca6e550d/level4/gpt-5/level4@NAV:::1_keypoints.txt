- Introduces embedding feedback gains into neural network policies to improve robustness to disturbances and regression error.
- Softly links trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a deviation penalty, enabling policies to learn faithful, simpler, and more robust approximations of optimal trajectories than MPC alone.
- Uses advanced parallelization for task optimization, but primarily as an implementation/efficiency detail rather than a core contribution.
- Demonstrates trained feedback controllers across a broad spectrum of robots.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, technically strong, and presents striking empirical results.
- Effectively fuses graphics, control, and neural networks to produce controllers for diverse body morphologies and tasks.
- Overall an inspiring, high-quality, tour-de-force contribution.
