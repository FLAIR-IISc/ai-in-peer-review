The authors propose a strategy for integrating feedback gains into neural network policies, making them robust to disturbances and regression error. Trajectory optimization and policy optimization are softly coupled via a deviation penalty in the cost, allowing the policy to learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories that are, in practice, simpler and more robust than the results of MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. The tasks are optimized using sophisticated parallelization—an effective means to an end rather than the central contribution. Finally, the trained feedback controllers are demonstrated on a diverse set of robots.

This paper is of the highest quality, exceptionally clear, and represents a tour de force of technique, with beautiful application results. Bravo. It is a compelling body of work that marries graphics, control, and neural networks in pursuit of generating controllers for a wide range of body morphologies and tasks. It is inspiring.
