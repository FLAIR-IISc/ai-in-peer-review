- Demonstrates a compact, fast neural network encoding a feedback policy for interactive control of simulated characters using trajectory optimization, with a single architecture for diverse periodic behaviors and morphologies.  
- Appears to apply modern deep learning to an existing graphics/control pipeline; paper should better situate itself relative to prior trajectory-optimization + neural reproduction work.  
- Needs explicit comparison to Abbeel & Levine-style work on concrete axes: training convergence speed, morphology coverage, runtime cost, and robustness to perturbations.  
- Request to quantify “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” (e.g., norms, condition numbers) and state the objectives/linearizations where this arises.  
- Suggest moving the rationale for not using LQG to after the feedback-gain computation description so the contrast is clearer.  
- Notation around lines 226–240 is confusing (e.g., s(X)=s̄ then using s(X)−s̄); simplify symbols or add a brief glossary.  
- “Softer feedback gains according to parameter λ” needs clarification: what baseline is “softer” than, how is it characterized (magnitude/smoothness/bandwidth), and why relaxing the initial-state constraint helps.  
- Overall sound work but the paper does not make its contribution relative to recent related work sufficiently explicit.
