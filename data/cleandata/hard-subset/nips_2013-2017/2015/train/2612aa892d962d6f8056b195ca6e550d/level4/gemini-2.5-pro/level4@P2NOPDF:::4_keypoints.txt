- Presents a method for interactive control by jointly learning a control policy and reference trajectories combining trajectory optimization with policy learning.  
- Main contribution: a framework that jointly learns policies and their reference trajectories via an alternating optimization scheme.  
- Empirical strategy: decouples joint optimization into two alternating subproblems, allowing use of established optimization methods for each.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance and practical effectiveness of the approach.  
- Technical sophistication and detailed implementation are strengths, with impressive results.  
- Provides clear empirical evidence for key claims, notably benefits of sensory noise during training and advantages of joint learning.  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and uses helpful cross-references and appropriate detail.  
- Request clarification on the motivation for the additional online optimization step (Section 7) and what residual errors it is intended to mitigate.  
- Notes practical benefit of online re-optimization (enables larger timesteps), but asks for explanation why the learned policy alone is insufficient.  
- Suggests adding citations when claiming similarity to recent approaches regarding small parameter updates (line 153).  
- Minor typo: missing word after "to unify" on line 89.  
- Reviewer uncertain about novelty/impact relative to state of the art due to limited subfield expertise but considers the work high quality.
