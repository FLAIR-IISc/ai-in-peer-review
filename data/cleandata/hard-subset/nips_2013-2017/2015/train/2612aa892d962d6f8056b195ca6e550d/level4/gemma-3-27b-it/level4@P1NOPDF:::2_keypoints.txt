- Paper shows a small, fast neural network can represent control policies for diverse, cyclic character behaviors using trajectory optimization during training.  
- The technique largely applies established trajectory optimization and neural-network approximation methods; novelty over prior work is not clearly positioned.  
- Reviewer asks for explicit comparison to recent Abbeel & Levine work (e.g., learning speed, generality across body types, runtime cost, robustness).  
- Authors should clarify what specific advantages their approach offers over existing methods.  
- Explain the claim about producing “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” with more detail and evidence.  
- Move the LQG discussion to after presenting the proposed strategy to improve exposition and flow.  
- Fix confusing notation around s(X) = \bar{s} and the cost defined in terms of s(X) - \bar{s}; overbars and squiggles are easily misread.  
- Define “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ”: specify softer than what baseline, what “soft” means, and implications.  
- Justify why altering the initial state is beneficial in the procedure and how it affects training or performance.
