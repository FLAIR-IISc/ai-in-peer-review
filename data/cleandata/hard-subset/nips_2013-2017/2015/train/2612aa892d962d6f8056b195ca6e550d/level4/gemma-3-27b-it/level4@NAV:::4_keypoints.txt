- Introduces a method combining trajectory optimization and policy learning for interactive control of complex characters via alternating optimization.  
- Core idea: jointly learn trajectories and a control policy; alternating optimization lets established techniques be applied and shows practical effectiveness.  
- Paper is technically clear and appears to be a significant technical achievement, though not introducing major new theoretical concepts.  
- Authors demonstrate strong technical skill in both design and implementation, with compelling empirical results.  
- Shows clear benefits from training with sensory noise and from the joint trajectory-policy learning approach.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with appropriate levels of detail and useful cross-references.  
- Seeks clarification on Section 7: why is an additional optimization step needed instead of relying solely on the policy—is it due to persistent trajectory costs, policy inaccuracies, or to enable larger timesteps?  
- Suggests adding one or two citations for the claim at line 153.  
- Notes that the initial counterintuitiveness of small parameter updates is resolved by the block-alternating optimization explanation.  
- Typo: Line 89 (“to unify…”).  
- Overall results are impressive, but the reviewer’s limited familiarity with the field makes it hard to fully assess novelty and impact.
