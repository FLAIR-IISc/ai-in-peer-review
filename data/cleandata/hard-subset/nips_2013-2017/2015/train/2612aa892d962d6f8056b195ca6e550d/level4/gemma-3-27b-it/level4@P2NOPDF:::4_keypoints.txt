- Proposes joint learning of trajectories and policies via an alternating (block-alternating) optimization scheme for interactive control of complex characters.
- Decoupling trajectories and policies lets the authors leverage established methods for each subproblem and yields effective practical performance.
- Paper is technically sound and well-implemented, demonstrating impressive technical skill and compelling results.
- Experimental results clearly substantiate benefits of incorporating sensory noise during training and of joint trajectory-policy learning.
- Writing is clear and well-organized, with good balance of detail and helpful cross-references.
- The additional optimization step in Section 7 needs clearer motivation—uncertain why the policy alone isn’t sufficient and whether it addresses persistent trajectory costs or policy regression errors.
- The re-optimization advantage (e.g., enabling larger timesteps) is acknowledged but could be more explicitly justified.
- Line 153’s claim “similar to recent approaches…” should be supported with one or two relevant citations.
- Restricting updates within parameter space initially seems counterintuitive but is understandable within the block-alternating optimization context.
- Minor typographical error on line 89 (“to unify”).
- Overall work is strong and results impressive, but reviewer is uncertain about novelty and broader impact due to limited domain expertise.
