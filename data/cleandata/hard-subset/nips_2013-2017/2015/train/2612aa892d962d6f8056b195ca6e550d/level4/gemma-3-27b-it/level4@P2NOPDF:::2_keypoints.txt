- Demonstrates a small, fast neural-network policy for interactive character control trained with trajectory optimization, but novelty vs prior work is unclear.
- Authors must explicitly delineate how this approach differs from existing trajectory-optimization + NN methods (e.g., [9]).
- A direct comparison to reinforcement learning work (notably Abbeel & Levine) is needed to clarify advantages (faster learning, broader applicability, lower runtime cost, or greater robustness).
- Clarify the statement about producing “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains” and explain its implications.
- Reorganize the paper so the reason LQG was not used appears after the proposed method is fully presented to improve narrative flow.
- Fix confusing notation around s(X)=\bar s and the cost function—use distinct symbols to improve readability.
- Define what is meant by “softer feedback gains according to parameter λ,” including the baseline for “softer” and its practical meaning.
- Explain why perturbing the initial state is included in the procedure and how this modification is beneficial.
- Overall: work is sound but the contribution relative to recent related research is not sufficiently emphasized; stronger, direct comparisons would improve impact.
