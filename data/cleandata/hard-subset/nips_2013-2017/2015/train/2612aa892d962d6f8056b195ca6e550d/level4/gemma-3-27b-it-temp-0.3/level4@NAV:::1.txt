The authors introduce a novel approach to incorporating feedback gains directly into neural network policies, resulting in enhanced robustness to external disturbances and reduced susceptibility to regression errors. This method employs a soft coupling between trajectory optimization and policy optimization via a deviation cost, enabling the policy to learn effective, and importantly, simpler and more robust control strategies compared to traditional Model Predictive Control (MPC)-derived trajectories. While the work leverages advanced parallelization techniques to facilitate optimization, this is presented as an implementation detail supporting the core contribution. The efficacy of the approach is demonstrated through successful control of a variety of robotic systems. 

This is an exceptionally strong paper, characterized by its clarity, technical sophistication, and compelling results. The work represents a significant achievement in integrating graphics, control theory, and neural networks to generate controllers applicable to diverse robot morphologies and tasks – a truly inspiring contribution.
