- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse 3D locomotion using supervised learning combined with Contact-Invariant Optimization.
- Key innovation: joint learning of optimal controls and a dynamics model with a small number of recurrent units providing memory beyond physical state.
- Demonstrates strong generalization: a single architecture controls a wide range of 3D character types, extending prior 2D work.
- Ablation studies convincingly show the necessity of joint optimization and noise injection for performance.
- Produces realistic, purposeful behaviors rather than chaotic motions typical of direct learning methods.
- Major limitation: requires full model knowledge (Eq. 6), a significant assumption not clearly stated up front.
- Section imbalance: Section 4 is too short relative to Section 6 and does not adequately discuss the recurrent units highlighted in the abstract.
- The description of the stochastic neural network is unclear—training data is stochastic but the network appears deterministic and needs clarification.
- Technical quality is sound with well-designed experiments, but applicability is limited where full models are unavailable.
- Paper is generally well-organized but would benefit from better balance between sections and clearer articulation of recurrent unit roles.
- Originality lies in the specific integration of trajectory optimization and neural networks for interactive control across complex 3D characters.
- Significance: promising step toward universal character controllers with applications in animation, robotics, and biomechanics.
- Overall: technically solid and impactful work that needs clearer exposition of assumptions, recurrent architecture, and stochasticity.
