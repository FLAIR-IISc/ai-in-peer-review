- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers using supervised learning combined with trajectory optimization.  
- Demonstrates the method on diverse 3D characters (swimming, flying, biped, quadruped) with varied morphologies.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and clearly explains the method and its components.  
- Provides a thorough background and related work discussion on neural control and learning challenges.  
- Experimental results are impressive, showing successful controllers across multiple characters and tasks.  
- Main contribution: a single neural network architecture can learn control policies for a wide range of complex characters and tasks.  
- Strength: clarity and organization with logical flow and clear section separation.  
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation including comparisons and performance analysis across characters/tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear, detailed comparison to other recent work, making relative contribution unclear.  
- Weakness: some technical details are unclear—notation around lines 226–240 is confusing.  
- Weakness: explanation of the perturbation method for producing feedback gains could be clearer.  
- Overall: technically sound and significant, with potential impact in graphics and robotics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, demonstrated effectiveness, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparison to recent work and unclear technical exposition in places.  
- Suggestions: add clearer comparisons to recent work, clarify the perturbation/feedback-gain details, and improve confusing notation (lines 226–240).
