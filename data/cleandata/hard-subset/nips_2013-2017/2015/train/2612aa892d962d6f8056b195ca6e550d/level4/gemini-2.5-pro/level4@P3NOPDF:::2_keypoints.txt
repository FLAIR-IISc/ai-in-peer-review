- Presents a method using offline trajectory optimization to generate motions and a small neural network to represent interactive control policies for simulated characters.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across diverse characters and cyclic behaviors (walking, running, swimming) with visually compelling results.  
- Core components (trajectory optimization + policy distillation via neural nets) are well-established; novelty and significance are unclear.  
- Paper needs clearer articulation of what is specifically novel (optimization formulation, network architecture, or demonstrated scalability/generality).  
- Requires direct comparisons to contemporary related work (e.g., Levine, Abbeel) to contextualize contributions.  
- Suggested comparison axes: sample efficiency/training time, generality across morphologies/skills, runtime performance for real-time use, and robustness to perturbations.  
- Clarify statement about "very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains": explain what “ill-conditioned” means and why large gains are problematic.  
- Move justification for not using LQG (lines 190–192) to after the methodology is presented for better narrative flow.  
- Improve notation in the cost function (s(X) = s̄ and related terms); current symbols are visually confusing.  
- Explain what is meant by "produces softer feedback gains according to parameter λ": softer than what, technical meaning, and why advantageous.  
- Clarify motivation for modifying the initial state during optimization and why this step is beneficial or necessary.  
- Overall: solid technical work but reads as incremental without clearer novelty, explicit comparisons to recent work, and answers to the specific clarification questions.
