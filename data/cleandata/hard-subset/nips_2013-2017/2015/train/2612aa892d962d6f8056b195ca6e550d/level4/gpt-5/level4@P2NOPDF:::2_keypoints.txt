- Novelty unclear: paper appears to apply deep nets to an established trajectory-optimization control pipeline and needs a clearer statement of what is new beyond integration.  
- Positioning vs prior work: explicitly compare and position relative to Abbeel/Levine (guided policy search) and similar trajectory-optimization–to–policy methods.  
- Practical advantages: clarify whether the approach yields faster training, less supervision, better generalization, lower runtime/latency, increased robustness, or simpler implementation.  
- Quantify breadth claim: if the key novelty is a single compact network for many behaviors/morphologies, quantify this and compare to strong baselines.  
- Ill-conditioned gains: clarify which process produces “very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains,” under what conditions, how ill-conditioning is diagnosed, provide quantitative ranges, and describe mitigation.  
- Reorder LQG discussion: move the explanation of why LQG was not used until after the method presentation to improve flow and trade-off understanding.  
- Notation clarity: fix confusing notation around lines 226–240 (use distinct symbols for target vs. function) to make the objective unambiguous.  
- Define “softer” gains: precisely define “softer feedback gains,” explain relative baseline, detail how λ affects magnitude/conditioning/stability, and provide empirical evidence.  
- Initial state handling: justify and clarify why initial-state alteration is used, how states are chosen/adapted, its effect on bias/reproducibility/robustness, and quantify benefits for training or exploration.  
- Overall: technically solid with compelling demos, but needs sharper positioning against related work, clearer exposition of design choices (LQG avoidance, gain regularization, initial-state handling), and quantitative comparisons to strengthen the contribution.
