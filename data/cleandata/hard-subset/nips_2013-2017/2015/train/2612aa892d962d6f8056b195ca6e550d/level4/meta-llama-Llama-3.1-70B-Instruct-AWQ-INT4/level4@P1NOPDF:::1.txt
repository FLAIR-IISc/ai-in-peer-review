The author presents a strategy for embedding feedback gains in neural network policies, rendering them robust to disturbances and regression errors. By softly coupling trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for deviation, the policy learns reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories. Notably, these approximations are simpler and more robust than those produced by MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. The tasks are optimized using sophisticated parallelization techniques, which, although not the central focus of the paper, serve as a means to an end. Ultimately, the trained feedback controllers are successfully applied to a diverse set of robots.

This paper is of the highest quality, boasting exceptional clarity, a mastery of technique, and stunning results in its applications. The work is a testament to the authors' skill and dedication. This beautiful body of work seamlessly integrates concepts from graphics, control, and neural networks to generate controllers for a wide range of body morphologies and tasks, making it a truly inspiring contribution.
