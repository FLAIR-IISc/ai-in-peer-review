This paper presents a method for interactive control of complex characters by integrating trajectory optimization and policy learning. The core idea is to jointly learn trajectories and a control policy, leveraging the practical benefit of decomposing the optimization into alternating subproblems – a strategy that allows for the application of established techniques and demonstrably yields strong performance. While the paper was technically clear, my limited expertise in this specific area prevents a comprehensive assessment of its potential impact, though it appears to represent a significant technical achievement without introducing novel theoretical foundations.

The authors demonstrate a remarkable level of technical skill throughout, from the conceptual design to the implementation details, and support this with compelling results. The benefits of both incorporating sensory noise during training and the joint learning approach are convincingly illustrated. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written, providing an appropriate level of detail considering the problem's complexity and the intricacies of the implementation. The structure is logical, with each section focusing on a distinct aspect of the work, and the inclusion of cross-references enhances clarity.

The rationale behind the additional optimization step detailed in Section 7 could be further clarified. Given the preceding presentation, I anticipated the policy would operate independently of further optimization. Is this additional step necessary due to inherent trajectory cost or policy inaccuracies that accumulate over time when applied to the physical simulation? While I expect the dynamics simulation to enforce physical plausibility, I may be overlooking a key consideration. I do acknowledge the advantage of this re-optimization in enabling larger timesteps.

On line 153, the statement “similar to recent approaches…” would benefit from one or two supporting citations. The concept of small parameter updates initially seemed counterintuitive, although I ultimately understood its utility within the block-alternating optimization framework.

A minor typographical error exists on line 89 (“to unify”). Overall, the results are impressive, and the proposed optimization scheme and its implementation appear sound. However, due to the specialized nature of this research area, I am hesitant to definitively assess the novelty and overall significance of this work.
