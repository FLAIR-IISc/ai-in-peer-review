The author proposes a strategy for embedding feedback gains in neural network policies, enabling them to be more robust to disturbances and regression errors. By softly coupling trajectory optimization and policy optimization through a cost penalty for deviation, the policy can learn reasonable approximations of optimal trajectories that are simpler and more robust than those produced by MPC-based trajectory optimization alone. The tasks are optimized using sophisticated parallelization, a means to an end rather than the central focus of the paper. Ultimately, the trained feedback controllers are applied to a diverse set of robots.

This paper is of the highest quality, exceptionally clear, and showcases a remarkable mastery of techniques. The results presented in the applications are impressive and aesthetically pleasing. The authors deserve accolades for this outstanding work, which seamlessly integrates computer graphics, control theory, and neural networks to generate controllers for a wide range of body morphologies and tasks. The paper is truly inspiring, presenting a beautiful body of work that sets a high standard in the field.
