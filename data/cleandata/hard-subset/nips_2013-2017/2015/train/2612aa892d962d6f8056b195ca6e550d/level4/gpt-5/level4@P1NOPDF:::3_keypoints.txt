- Proposes a real-time 3D character control method where a neural network is trained offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs, with CIO and NN training interleaved.
- Introduces data augmentation and additive noise in hidden layers; alternating training plus noise substantially improves generalization.
- Experiments show the learned controller produces realistic, stable behaviors across diverse character models.
- The alternating training and noise strategies are substantive, nontrivial contributions beyond the basic idea of learning CIO outputs.
- Experimental dataset is small (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), limiting confidence in results and explaining overfitting in the “no noise” variant.
- The “no joint” ablation may also suffer from limited trajectory diversity and needs more data to assess effects.
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief MPC baseline is provided and no references or systematic comparisons to other methods are given.
- Mathematical presentation is mostly clear but leaves key details superficial; reproducing results will require consulting additional references.
- CIO derivation has unclear/undefined notation (e.g., Eq. 6) and other formal gaps that impede reproducibility.
- Section 3.2 (generating optimal actions for noisy inputs) is confusing: notation and handling of time index t are unclear.
- Releasing code would greatly aid reproducibility and verification.
- Paper should report runtime/timing and scalability with model complexity, since real-time CPU budgets are critical in practical applications.
- Terminology and phrasing issues and many minor errors/typos need correction (e.g., “biped location” vs locomotion, unite/unify, missing articles, LQG undefined).
- Multiple specific technical/notation queries and corrections are raised (Algorithm 1 initialization and η, misplaced asterisk, sX and aX subsets, f vs f^{des}, Table 1a layer count, etc.).
- Methodological suggestions: split “no noise” into “no data augmentation” vs “no hidden-layer noise,” test annealing α in CIO, and clarify whether target orientation is handled.
- Overall assessment: strong and appealing application with promising qualitative results, but needs better comparative evaluation, clearer derivations (CIO and 3.2), runtime measurements, and fixes to minor issues to firmly establish practical benefits.
