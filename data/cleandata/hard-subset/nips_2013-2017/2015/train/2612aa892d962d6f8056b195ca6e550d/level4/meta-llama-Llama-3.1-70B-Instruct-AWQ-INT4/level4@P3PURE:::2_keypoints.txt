- Demonstrates a small, fast neural network policy trained via trajectory optimization that controls diverse cyclic behaviors and body types in a physics simulator.  
- The approach largely applies deep learning to established trajectory optimization/control techniques; prior work has shown similar capabilities and must be better distinguished.  
- Authors should explicitly position and compare their method to recent work by Abbeel and Levine, highlighting potential benefits (faster learning, broader body support, lower runtime cost, greater robustness).  
- Clarify the claim about producing "very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" and consider presenting the LQG discussion after introducing the proposed strategy.  
- Fix confusing notation around lines 226–240 (the use of s(X) = \bar s and cost defined as s(X) - \bar s is visually and conceptually unclear).  
- Explain what is meant by "softer" feedback gains with respect to parameter λ, how this compares to alternatives, and why it is desirable.  
- Justify why changing the initial state as part of the procedure is beneficial.  
- Overall assessment: the work is sound but its novelty and contribution relative to recent related work need clearer articulation.
