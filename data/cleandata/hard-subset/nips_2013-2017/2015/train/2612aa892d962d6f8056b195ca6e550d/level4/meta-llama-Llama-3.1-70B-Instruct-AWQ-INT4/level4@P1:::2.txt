"Interactive control of diverse complex characters with neural networks" demonstrates how a small, fast neural network can be used to represent a control policy for interactive control of a character in a physics simulator. The approach utilizes trajectory optimization in a training phase to learn a neural network policy. The contribution of this paper is to show that the same architecture works across a range of more-or-less cyclic behaviors and a range of body types.

General Comments
----------------

This paper applies deep learning to existing techniques in computer graphics, building upon recent work in the field. The ability of trajectory optimization to find realistic control signals has been established by previous work, some of which is cited here. The use of neural networks to approximate the step-by-step actions required of a policy in order to trace out trajectories similar to the ones found by trajectory optimization has also been developed in previous work (such as e.g. [9]). Therefore, the authors must clearly position their work relative to these previously-published approaches.

A comparison to the recent line of work by Pieter Abbeel and Sergey Levine would be beneficial. Specifically, the authors should discuss whether their approach is designed to learn faster, support a wider variety of body shapes, require less computation at runtime, or be more robust to certain environmental variabilities.

Specific Comments
-----------------

The statement "we found the resulting process can produce very large and ill-conditioned feedback gains" is unclear. The authors should provide more context or explanation for this finding.

It would be helpful to move the discussion of why LQG was not used until after presenting the proposed strategy. This would improve the flow of the paper and make the justification for the chosen approach clearer.

The notation around lines 226-240 is confusing. The introduction of s(X) = \bar s, followed by the definition of a cost in terms of s(X) - \bar s, uses visually similar bars and squiggles. The authors should consider revising this notation for clarity.

The statement "our approach produces softer feedback gains according to parameter \lambda" is unclear. The authors should specify what is meant by "softer" and how this compares to other approaches. Additionally, they should explain why it is beneficial to change the initial state as part of their procedure.

Overall, the paper presents sound work, but the contribution relative to other recent related work is not clear enough. The authors should strive to provide more context and comparisons to establish the significance of their approach.

Strengths:
- The paper demonstrates the ability to learn a control policy for interactive control of a character in a physics simulator using a neural network.
- The approach shows promise for a range of body types and behaviors.

Weaknesses:
- The contribution relative to previous work is not clearly established.
- The paper could benefit from more context and comparisons to recent related work.
- Some notation and explanations could be clarified for better understanding.

Acceptance:
- The paper is a good candidate for acceptance, but the authors should address the mentioned weaknesses to strengthen their contribution.

Arguments For Acceptance:
- The paper presents a novel application of deep learning to computer graphics.
- The approach shows promise for a range of body types and behaviors.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
- The contribution relative to previous work is not clearly established.
- The paper could benefit from more context and comparisons to recent related work.
