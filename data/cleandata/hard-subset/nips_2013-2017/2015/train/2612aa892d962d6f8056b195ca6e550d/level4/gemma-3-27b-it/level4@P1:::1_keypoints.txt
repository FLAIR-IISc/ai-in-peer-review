- Proposes training recurrent neural networks as near-optimal feedback controllers for diverse dynamical tasks (swimming, flying, biped/quadruped locomotion).
- Controller architecture: feed-forward units learn state-action mappings, recurrent units provide memory beyond system state.
- Novel training paradigm: network learns dynamics under an implicit policy rather than directly learning a control policy.
- Key training techniques: interleaving supervised learning with trajectory optimization (Contact-Invariant Optimization), noise injection, training for unexpected task changes, and using the optimizer to derive feedback gains.
- Method avoids motion capture, task-specific features, and state machines.
- Scales established ideas (supervised learning + trajectory optimization) to more complex, stable 3D locomotion tasks than prior work.
- Builds on a substantial body of control, RL, and stochastic optimal control research and acknowledges recent related work.
- Technical soundness: well-founded integration of trajectory optimization and network training with attention to overfitting and stability.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and uses consistent mathematical notation.
- Originality: novel combination of CIO with neural network training and emphasis on learning feedback gains.
- Significance: demonstrates a single network controlling diverse characters with realistic, stable behaviors; potential impact on robotics, animation, biomechanics, neuroscience.
- Practical insight: more compute is spent on data generation than on learning.
- Weakness — computational cost: high training and cloud computing requirements may limit accessibility.
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: several algorithmic constants are used without detailed sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness — comparisons: lacks a systematic, quantitative comparison to direct neural network controller training methods.
- Recommendation: reviewer judges the paper an outstanding, significant advance and recommends Strong Accept.
