- Summary: Trains a single RNN controller via alternating offline trajectory optimization (CIO) and network training, extracting feedback gains and injecting noise to produce real-time, robust locomotion for varied 3D characters.

- Strength — Generality: Same framework and network successfully applied across diverse morphologies and locomotion modes without task-specific engineering.

- Strength — Novel techniques: Introduces extraction of optimal feedback gains from a direct trajectory optimizer and principled noise injection to transfer offline solutions to robust online policies.

- Strength — Empirical evidence: Thorough experiments and ablations show large performance gains and natural gait emergence; comparison to MPC highlights regularizing effect of the network.

- Weakness — Strong model assumptions: Method requires a complete differentiable dynamics/contact model (CIO), limiting applicability to real-world robotics; this assumption should be highlighted earlier.

- Weakness — Recurrent network detail lacking: Paper omits architecture, recurrent state dimensionality, update rules, and analysis of learned memory, leaving a core claim unexplained.

- Weakness — Misleading terminology: Calling the approach a “stochastic neural network” is misleading since stochasticity is only in training (noise injection) while the executed policy is deterministic.

- Weakness — Imbalanced presentation: Excessive engineering detail on distributed training (Section 6) versus sparse description of the neural policy (Section 4); suggest condensing implementation details to expand RNN discussion.

- Overall assessment: Powerful, general, and empirically strong contribution, but clarity and impact would improve by explicitly stating model-based assumptions and thoroughly describing/evaluating the recurrent components.
