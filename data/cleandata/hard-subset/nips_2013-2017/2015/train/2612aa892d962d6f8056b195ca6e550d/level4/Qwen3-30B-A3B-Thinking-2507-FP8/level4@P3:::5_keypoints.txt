- Combines trajectory optimization with neural network policy learning to produce stable, realistic locomotion controllers.  
- Demonstrates method across diverse 3D tasks (swimming, flying, bipedal, quadrupedal) without task-specific initialization.  
- Joint optimization and noise injection yield large performance gains over naive neural training (two orders of magnitude improvement in comparisons).  
- Distributed training architecture is well-described and scales effectively; proximal regularization stabilizes asynchronous training.  
- Requires full model knowledge (Eq. 6), which limits applicability to real-world robotics and should be explicitly acknowledged and discussed.  
- Section 4 (Neural Network Policy Regression) is too short and lacks detail on recurrent units central to the controller.  
- Paper mischaracterizes the method as using “stochastic neural networks”; stochasticity arises from noise in training data, not network architecture.  
- Method is technically sound with strong experimental validation and thorough ablation studies.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, though Section 4 needs clarification on recurrent units and stochasticity.  
- Original contribution: extends trajectory optimization–based control to harder 3D problems and presents a unified framework for learning controllers.  
- Significant for animation/character control, but limited broader robotics impact due to model knowledge requirement.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address model-knowledge limitation, Section 4 detail imbalance, and stochasticity mischaracterization.
