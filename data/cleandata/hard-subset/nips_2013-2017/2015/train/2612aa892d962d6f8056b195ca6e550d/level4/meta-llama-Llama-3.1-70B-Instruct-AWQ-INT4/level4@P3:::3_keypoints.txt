- Proposes a novel algorithm for real-time control of 3D characters by training a neural network offline to reproduce Contact-Invariant Optimization (CIO) outputs.  
- Key innovation: alternating CIO and NN training where each method’s output is used in the other’s optimization criterion.  
- Noise injection (data augmentation and additive noise in hidden layers) is crucial for generalization.  
- The approach is interesting and valuable given the difficulty of real-time control for arbitrary models.  
- Joint training plus noise injection are significant contributions beyond a straightforward NN imitation approach.  
- Experimental results are promising across several character models.  
- Training data is small (100–200 trials with 5 branched segments), risking overfitting and limiting generalization.  
- The “no noise” and “no joint” ablations likely overfit and might improve with more diverse trajectories.  
- Comparative evaluation is weak: only a brief comparison to MPC and no systematic comparison to more direct methods.  
- Some mathematical parts are clear, but key steps (CIO Eq. 6) and Section 3.2 (handling noisy inputs and time index t) are underexplained or confusing.  
- Providing code would benefit reproducibility and the community.  
- Time measurements for real-time control are missing and necessary given potential CPU bottlenecks in applications like games.  
- Target specification lacks orientation (only x,y,z), so adding a target angle would be important in practice.  
- Terminology and wording issues: “biped location” → “locomotion”; “It is our aim to unity” → “unite/unify”; “noise injection” is misleading (really data augmentation).  
- Unclear justification for reusing the same hyperparameter eta in both steps of Algorithm 1.  
- Several minor notation and formatting errors throughout the paper.
