- Tackles maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models by constraining parameters θ to a "fast-mixing" convex set Θ.  
- Main contribution: proves projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS under the fast-mixing assumption.  
- Provides separate non-asymptotic convergence and complexity bounds for unregularized and ridge-regularized cases with explicit schedules for K, M, and v.  
- Strength: rigorous, detailed theoretical analysis of gradient descent with biased, noisy gradients.  
- Strength: proposing tractability via parameter-induced fast mixing (rather than structural restrictions) is a compelling idea.  
- Major weakness: insufficient engagement with extensive stochastic approximation literature, reducing novelty.  
- Major weakness: relies on strong and impractical assumptions, notably re-initializing the MCMC chain from a fixed distribution each iteration.  
- Major weakness: assumes uniform geometric mixing for all parameters in Θ, which is a strong condition rarely required in prior work.  
- Practical critique: re-initialization is computationally inefficient compared to common warm-starting; warm-start analysis is well-studied.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and organized; notation table is helpful.  
- Clarity improvement suggested: state theorem assumptions explicitly within the theorem statements for self-containment.  
- Originality concern: proposed algorithm is a special case of classic stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics; prior works cover more general conditions.  
- Significance tempered: theoretical finite-sample analysis is valuable, but originality and practicality issues limit impact.  
- Recommendation: reviewer urges literature review and reframing relative to stochastic approximation, extension to practical variants (e.g., warm-start), and leans toward rejection.
