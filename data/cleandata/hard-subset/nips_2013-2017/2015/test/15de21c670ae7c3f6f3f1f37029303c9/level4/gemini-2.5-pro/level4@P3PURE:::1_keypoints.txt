- Manuscript shows limited engagement with relevant literature, notably Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.  
- Authors should compare their method to existing work to properly situate the contribution.  
- Much of the theoretical analysis already exists in more general form; results are not novel.  
- Convergence results in prior work do not require the authors' uniform ergodicity assumption.  
- Reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is an unnecessarily restrictive and unrealistic assumption.  
- Initializing with the previous sample is likely more effective and has been extensively studied in the literature.  
- Presentation is suboptimal: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated within the theorems.  
- Strong assumptions used for results like Theorem 6 appear unnecessary; more general results (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) exist.  
- Overall, due to lack of awareness of prior work, the manuscript's contributions appear only marginally novel.
