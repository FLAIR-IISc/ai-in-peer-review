The primary concern with this manuscript is an insufficient engagement with the existing literature. The proposed algorithm is closely related to Monte Carlo EM and, more broadly, to stochastic-approximation methods with Markovian dynamics that have long been used to optimize intractable likelihoods. There is a substantial body of prior work in this area, and the paper should situate its contributions relative to these results. Much of the established theory already covers scenarios at least as general as those considered here. For example, uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains is not necessary, and it is not required to reinitialize the chain from a fixed distribution r at every iteration (while the authors note this is done for analytical convenience, this rationale is insufficient—moreover, warm-starting from previous states, as discussed briefly in the manuscript, is a common and often preferable strategy that has been extensively analyzed). The presentation could also be improved: for Theorems 1 and 2 it is unclear exactly what assumptions are in force; readers should not have to infer these from surrounding text. Additionally, the strong conditions imposed to derive results such as Theorem 6 are not needed (see, for instance, Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret for more general treatments). Overall, the paper appears to lack awareness of key prior work, and the contributions seem only marginally novel.
