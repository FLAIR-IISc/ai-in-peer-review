- Presents a novel computational approach to efficient MLE in exponential families by using fast-mixing MCMC to approximate integrals for proximal gradient descent.  
- By combining the fast-mixing assumption with Schmidt et al. (2011), the paper provides a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Distinguishes convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood-error guarantees in the former and parameter-error guarantees in the latter.  
- Provides a simple experimental demonstration supporting the theoretical claims.  
- Theoretical analyses appear sound and non-trivial, mainly combining MCMC mixing bounds, concentration inequalities, and existing optimization results; detailed proof checking is pending.  
- Offers a well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and converts one hard problem (integrals) into another (fast-mixing MCMC).  
- Authors give an honest discussion of limitations, the pessimism of assumptions, and directions for future work.  
- Provides useful insights related to Contrastive Divergence (CD)-type algorithms despite analyzing a different algorithm.  
- Introduction is well-written with substantial references to prior work.  
- Technical clarity issues: fails to explicitly state that the log-likelihood is convex, which is central to the argument.  
- Recommends a clear summary of key assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ) to guide readers.  
- Practical value may be limited by the difficulty of proving fast mixing for MCMC in many models, though the theoretical contribution remains valuable.  
- Minor editorial corrections: Line 185 (",w here" → ", where"), Line 192 ("this paper that" → "this paper assumes that"), Line 207 ("simple example fast-mixing" → "simple example of a fast-mixing"), Line 281 ("of of" → "of"), and revise Lines 311–312 for clarity.
