- Main issue: manuscript shows poor awareness of related literature, especially Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.
- Authors must compare their algorithm and results to extensive existing work and established theory that already covers similar or broader scenarios.
- Paper studies “fast-mixing parameters” as an alternative tractable set for MCMC inference, an interesting concept with potential for high-treewidth undirected graphical models.
- The authors should explicitly relate their work to prior research on MCMC methods and stochastic approximation algorithms.
- Presentation is weak: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated and should be made explicit in the theorem statements.
- Provide more context and clearer exposition so readers need not search the surrounding text for key assumptions or conditions.
- Convergence guarantees rely on strong assumptions; the authors must discuss limitations and compare robustness with existing methods.
- The paper omits analysis of computational complexity and scalability; include discussion of runtime and how performance scales with problem size.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: the single illustrative example does not demonstrate performance on complex or realistic problems.
- Include more empirical results to validate effectiveness and practical utility of the algorithm.
- Technically sound but lacks clarity, context, and a comprehensive literature framing; revise to improve readability and positioning.
- Originality is limited: offers a new perspective on tractable sets but the algorithm itself is not clearly novel—authors should delineate specific novel contributions.
- Potential significance exists for inference in high-treewidth models, but the impact and applicability need clearer discussion.
