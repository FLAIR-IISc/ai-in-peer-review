- Paper proposes constraining parameters to "fast-mixing sets" so MCMC converges rapidly for ML in high-treewidth graphical models.  
- Core theoretical contribution—convergence guarantees for gradient descent with MCMC-based gradient approximation—lacks sufficient novelty versus established literature.  
- Algorithm is essentially related to Monte Carlo EM and general stochastic approximation methods for intractable likelihoods, which are well-studied.  
- Existing foundational theory (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret 1990) already provides convergence under much weaker assumptions than those used here.  
- The authors’ simplifying assumption of reinitializing chains from a fixed distribution r each iteration is unnecessary and suboptimal.  
- Initializing MCMC from the previous sample, noted by the authors, has rigorous prior analysis (e.g., contrastive divergence techniques).  
- Presentation suffers from poor clarity: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not self-contained and require cross-referencing.  
- Strong assumptions used (uniform mixing bounds, fixed reinitialization) are stronger than needed and not justified.  
- Failure to engage with foundational stochastic approximation literature undermines the perceived novelty; contributions appear marginal rather than substantive.
