Comments on Quality:
- The paper presents convergence-rate bounds for learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, and these bounds appear meaningful and useful.
- My main concern is that the lower bound (Theorem 7) is difficult to interpret. A more detailed explanation of this theorem—and, more broadly, a discussion of the lower bounds relevant to the problem—would be valuable.

Comments on Clarity:
- The presentation is clear.

Comments on Originality:
- Both the problem formulation (via the “fast-mixing” model class) and the theoretical results appear original.

Comments on Significance:
- The overall significance is not entirely clear to me. However, the work seems to fill a gap in the literature on learning intractable models using MCMC, and in that sense it appears significant.
- The submission would be strengthened by further discussion of its potential impact, including possible extensions and opportunities to build on these results.
- The paper introduces a novel class of tractable models based on the quality of MCMC-based approximations to expectations. The theoretical results providing convergence rates for learning these models appear novel and interesting. On this basis, I recommend acceptance.
