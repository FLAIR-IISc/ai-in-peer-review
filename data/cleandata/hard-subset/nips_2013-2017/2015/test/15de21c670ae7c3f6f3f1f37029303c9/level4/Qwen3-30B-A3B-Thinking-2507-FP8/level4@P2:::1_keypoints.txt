- Proposes ML learning in high-treewidth graphical models by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set and using MCMC-sampled gradients for gradient descent.  
- Provides provable approximation guarantees and complexity bounds: unregularized O(1/ε^3) and ridge-regularized O(1/ε^2), yielding a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme.  
- Technical analysis and main theorem proofs are sound and correct.  
- Assumptions are unnecessarily restrictive—requires uniform ergodicity (Eq.2) and fixed initialization each iteration.  
- More general stochastic approximation theory (e.g., Benveniste et al. 1990) removes those restrictive assumptions and could yield similar results.  
- Authors acknowledge fixed initialization is a simplification but do not justify why it’s preferable to reusing previous samples for initialization.  
- Presentation lacks clarity: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are scattered rather than listed upfront.  
- The relation between mixing parameters (C, α) and target accuracy ε is not clearly stated, reducing practical applicability of bounds.  
- Originality is limited: approach is essentially a variant of Monte Carlo EM and applies existing stochastic approximation ideas to graphical models.  
- Paper fails to adequately contextualize and compare to prior work (e.g., Andrieu et al. 2005, Welling & Teh 2011).  
- Practical significance is limited; authors admit bounds are conservative and the work does not advance state-of-the-art ML learning for graphical models.  
- Recommendation: reject—work is technically correct but lacks sufficient novelty, significance, and literature contextualization for NIPS.  
- Required revisions if resubmitted: thorough literature review, justification of restrictive assumptions, explicit upfront statement of assumptions, and demonstration of how results extend/improve existing theory.
