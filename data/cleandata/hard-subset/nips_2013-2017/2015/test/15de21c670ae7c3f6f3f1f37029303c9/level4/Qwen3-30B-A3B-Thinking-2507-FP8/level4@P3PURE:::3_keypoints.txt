- Addresses weight learning in Markov networks and the need for approximate gradient computation via MCMC since exact gradients are NP-hard.
- Main theoretical claim: if parameters guarantee rapid MCMC mixing, MCMC-based gradient learning is an FPRAS.
- Provides separate analyses and bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases.
- Paper is generally well-written with only minor typographical errors.
- Proofs appear plausible, though the reviewer could not fully verify them.
- Major practical concern: the rapid-mixing parameter condition is essentially untestable/impossible to guarantee in practice.
- Reviewer asks whether the mixing condition could be enforced artificially; it resembles a form of regularization.
- Combining the artificial constraint with ridge regularization may lead to over-regularization and degraded models.
- Overall: solid theoretical formalization of when MCMC-based learning is FPRAS, but limited practical relevance and non-trivial open challenges to make it applicable.
