I believe the primary weakness of this manuscript is a limited engagement with relevant prior work. The authors’ algorithm bears a strong relationship to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, and more broadly, the class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics that have been widely proposed for optimizing intractable likelihood functions. A substantial body of literature exists on this topic, and the authors should thoroughly compare their approach to existing methods. Indeed, a theoretical framework encompassing many of the authors’ results already exists and addresses more general scenarios. For example, the requirement of uniform ergodicity for the Markov chains is not necessary in existing work, nor is the re-initialization of the Markov chain from a fixed distribution *r* at each iteration – a practice the authors themselves acknowledge is for analytical simplification. However, this simplification isn’t adequately justified; re-initializing with the previous sample, as the authors discuss at the manuscript’s conclusion, may be a preferable and already well-studied alternative. 

The presentation of the results also needs improvement. Specifically, the assumptions underlying Theorem 1 and Theorem 2 are unclear. While referenced elsewhere in the text, the reader should not have to piece them together from surrounding paragraphs. Furthermore, the strong assumptions used to derive results like those in Theorem 6 are not necessary; more general results are available (see, for example, Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret). 

Overall, I feel the authors demonstrate a lack of awareness of the existing literature, and the contributions, as presented, are only marginally novel.
