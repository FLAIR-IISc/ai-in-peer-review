- Addresses maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models by constraining parameters to a convex "fast-mixing" set to ensure rapid MCMC convergence.  
- Proposes projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated likelihood gradients and proves this yields an FPRAS for the constrained ML problem.  
- Provides explicit non-asymptotic complexity bounds (MCMC steps, gradient steps, sample counts) for both unregularized and ridge-regularized cases.  
- Main strength: derives concrete, non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for a practical fast-mixing parameter constraint.  
- Main weakness: limited engagement with the extensive stochastic approximation (SA) literature, especially methods driven by Markovian dynamics.  
- Relation to prior theory: the algorithm is a specific instance of Markovian SA methods and should be compared to classical results (e.g., Benveniste et al.).  
- Strength of assumptions: requiring uniform geometric ergodicity for all parameters is overly strong compared to typical SA conditions.  
- Practicality critique: re-initializing MCMC from a fixed distribution each iteration simplifies analysis but is nonstandard; warm-start schemes are common and well-studied.  
- Novelty unclear: unclear whether the new non-asymptotic bounds are tighter or offer new insights beyond existing SA theory, including for the strongly convex case.  
- Presentation issue: key theorems should state all necessary assumptions explicitly rather than scattering them through the text.  
- Overall verdict: interesting approach with valuable bounds but insufficiently situated within prior SA literature, so novelty is marginal and a major revision is needed.
