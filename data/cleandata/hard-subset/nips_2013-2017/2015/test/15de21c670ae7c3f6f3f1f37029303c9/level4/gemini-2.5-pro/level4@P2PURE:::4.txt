**Quality:**
The paper derives meaningful and useful convergence rates for learning algorithms by relating them to MCMC mixing times. The primary weakness is the lower bound presented in Theorem 7, which is difficult to interpret in its current form. The paper would be substantially strengthened by an expanded discussion of this theorem and, more generally, of the lower bounds applicable to this problem.

**Clarity:**
The paper is well-written and the presentation is clear.

**Originality:**
The contributions appear to be original, both in the framing of the problem around the "fast mixing" model class and in the theoretical results themselves.

**Significance and Recommendation:**
While the paper successfully fills a gap in the literature on learning intractable models with MCMC, its broader significance could be better articulated. The submission would be improved by a more detailed discussion of the work's potential impact, including future extensions and opportunities for researchers to build upon these results.

Overall, the paper introduces a novel class of tractable models based on the efficient approximability of expectations via MCMC. The theoretical results providing convergence rates for learning these models are novel and interesting. This is a valuable contribution, and I recommend acceptance.
