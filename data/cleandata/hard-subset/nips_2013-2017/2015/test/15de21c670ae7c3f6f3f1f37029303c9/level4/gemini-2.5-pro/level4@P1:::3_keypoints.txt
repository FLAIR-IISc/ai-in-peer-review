- Addresses max-likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models where exact gradient computation is intractable.  
- Common practical approach uses MCMC samples to approximate gradients, but lacks theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Main contribution: proves that projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS when parameters lie in a known fast-mixing set.  
- Provides explicit convergence bounds for both the convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases.  
- Paper is well-written and presents novel, significant theoretical results; core arguments appear plausible.  
- Reviewer did not verify all proof details but found the formal justification important for a widely used method.  
- Primary weakness: practical applicability is limited by the assumption that one can define and project onto a fast-mixing parameter set.  
- Authors discuss projection for Ising models, but extension to many complex models is unclear and likely difficult.  
- Open question: how to enforce fast-mixing constraints more generally and whether the constraint acts as an implicit regularizer.  
- Concern that combining the fast-mixing constraint with explicit regularization (e.g., L2) could lead to over-regularization.  
- If practical methods to enforce fast-mixing were found, the work would move from theoretical interest to substantial practical impact.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with limited direct practical relevance; further research needed to bridge theory and broadly applicable algorithms.
