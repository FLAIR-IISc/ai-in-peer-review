

The manuscript's primary limitation stems from insufficient engagement with the established literature on stochastic optimization for intractable likelihoods. The proposed algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM framework and more broadly to stochastic approximation methods employing Markovian dynamics—well-studied approaches for optimizing complex likelihoods. The authors should explicitly compare their work to this extensive body of literature. Crucially, a comprehensive theoretical foundation already exists that encompasses their results while addressing far more general scenarios. For instance, existing theory does not require Markov chains to be uniformly ergodic, and reinitializing the chain at a fixed distribution $r$ at each iteration (a simplification the authors acknowledge but justify only as a technical convenience) is unnecessary. Moreover, reinitializing using the previous sample—as noted in the manuscript's conclusion—has been rigorously analyzed in prior work and may yield better performance.  

The presentation of results also requires significant improvement. For Theorems 1 and 2, the assumptions are unclear; readers should not need to cross-reference surrounding text to understand them. Furthermore, the strong assumptions underpinning Theorem 6 are unjustified, as more general results—such as those in Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret's seminal work—already cover similar ground without such constraints. The authors' technical contributions thus offer only marginal novelty relative to the existing literature. Their analysis fails to acknowledge key prior developments, particularly regarding the flexibility of Markov chain initialization and the broader theoretical guarantees available. This lack of contextualization weakens the paper's contribution and undermines its significance for the community. The work would benefit substantially from a thorough revision that situates the method within the established theoretical landscape, clarifies all assumptions, and rigorously contrasts results with prior art.
