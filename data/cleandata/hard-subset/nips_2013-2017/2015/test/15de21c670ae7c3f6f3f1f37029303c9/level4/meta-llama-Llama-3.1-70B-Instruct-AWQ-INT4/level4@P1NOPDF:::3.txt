A key problem in Markov networks is weight learning, which typically involves finding the optimal setting of weights attached to features such that the likelihood is maximized. To achieve this, a gradient descent algorithm is often employed, and a crucial sub-task within this algorithm is computing the gradient. However, since computing the gradient is NP-hard in general, MCMC algorithms are frequently used as an alternative to exact algorithms.

The paper presents theoretical results demonstrating that if the weights (parameters) are such that MCMC converges rapidly to the stationary distribution on them, then the weight learning algorithm (which utilizes MCMC to compute the gradient) is a Fully Polynomial-Time Randomized Approximation Scheme (FPRAS). The authors consider two distinct cases: the unregularized case (convex case) and the ridge-regularized case (non-convex case), deriving separate bounds for each.

The paper is generally well-written, with a few minor typos scattered throughout. Although I was unable to verify all the proofs, they appear plausible.

While the theoretical results are impressive, they have limited practical relevance, a point that the authors acknowledge. I believe it is virtually impossible to ensure that the parameters satisfy the constraints required by the theorems. It would be beneficial for the authors to consider whether it is possible to artificially enforce this condition, as it can be viewed as a form of regularization. Furthermore, using ridge regression in conjunction with this condition may result in over-regularized models. If this issue can be addressed, the theoretical results presented in the paper could be significantly strengthened.

Overall, the paper provides a reasonably well-written formalization of the conditions under which using MCMC for weight learning (with and without regularization) yields a FPRAS. However, the practical relevance of these conditions is limited. Before the results derived in the paper can be translated into a practical and accurate algorithm for weight learning, several non-trivial research problems need to be addressed.
