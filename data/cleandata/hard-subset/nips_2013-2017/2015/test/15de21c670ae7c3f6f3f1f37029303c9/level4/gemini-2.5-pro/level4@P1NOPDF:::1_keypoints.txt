- Limited engagement with relevant literature; authors must position their work relative to extensive prior research.
- Proposed algorithm closely related to Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics, which are well-studied.
- Many theoretical results are already established in the literature, often under more general conditions than those used here.
- Unnecessary assumptions: theory does not require uniform ergodicity or re-initializing the chain from a fixed distribution r at each iteration.
- Re-initializing from the previous sample—a potentially better strategy—is already extensively analyzed in prior work.
- Theorems (e.g., 1 and 2) are presented without explicitly enumerated assumptions, hurting clarity and self-containment.
- Some theoretical claims use overly strong assumptions; results like Theorem 6 can be derived under weaker conditions (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret).
- Overall, limited literature engagement and marginal novelty weaken the manuscript’s contributions.
