- Paper introduces a novel, computationally efficient MLE approach for exponential families using the assumption of rapid MCMC mixing.  
- Key insight: rapid MCMC mixing allows accurate approximation of integrals for proximal gradient descent, yielding an FPRAS for MLE via combination with Schmidt et al. (2011).  
- Analysis covers both convex and strongly convex settings, giving likelihood error bounds (convex) and parameter error bounds (strongly convex).  
- Provides a simple empirical demonstration supporting the theoretical claims.  
- Well-motivated alternative to restrictive small-treewidth assumptions in prior work.  
- Theoretical framework is sound, combining MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and existing results.  
- Authors transparently discuss limitations, especially the difficulty of verifying fast MCMC mixing in practice.  
- The practical utility is limited by replacing MLE hardness with the hard problem of proving fast mixing.  
- Paper offers useful insights into CD-type algorithms despite differences from standard CD.  
- Improve clarity by explicitly stating convexity of the log-likelihood and summarizing key assumptions (e.g., convex parameter set Θ).  
- Minor grammatical and typographical errors need correction (several line-specific examples provided).  
- Overall assessment: significant, original theoretical advance that merits publication at NIPS.
