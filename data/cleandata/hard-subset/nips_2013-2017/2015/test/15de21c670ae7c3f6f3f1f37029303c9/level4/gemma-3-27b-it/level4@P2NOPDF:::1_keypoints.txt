- Algorithm closely relates to existing Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation methods for intractable likelihood optimization.  
- Manuscript shows substantial similarity to previously developed and analyzed algorithms in this literature.  
- Authors fail to engage adequately with the established literature, limiting the paper’s novelty.  
- A robust theoretical framework already exists that subsumes the authors’ results in a more general setting.  
- Current theory often removes the uniform ergodicity requirement and avoids re-initializing the Markov chain each iteration, unlike the authors’ approach.  
- Re-initialization from a fixed distribution r is used for analytical convenience but is a notable limitation.  
- Initializing from the previous sample is a better and well-studied alternative that the authors do not consider sufficiently.  
- Theorem statements lack explicit assumptions; critical conditions are only discoverable by cross-referencing surrounding text.  
- Assumptions used to prove Theorem 6 are unnecessarily strong; weaker conditions in works like Benveniste, Métivier and Priouret can yield similar results.  
- Overall contribution is incremental given prior work; a thorough comparison and discussion of relevant prior literature is necessary to establish significance.
