The main issue with this manuscript is its limited engagement with the existing literature. The proposed algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm and, more broadly, to the class of stochastic approximation methods with Markovian dynamics that have long been used to optimize intractable likelihoods. This area is well developed, and the authors should position their contribution relative to prior work. In particular, existing theory already encompasses several of the manuscript’s results and in more general settings.

For example, uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains is not required in standard analyses, and there is no need to reinitialize the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r at each iteration. While the authors state that they adopt this strategy to simplify the analysis, this rationale is not sufficient. Moreover, as they note at the end of the manuscript, reinitializing from the previous sample (i.e., warm starts) may be preferable; such approaches have also been extensively analyzed in the literature.

The presentation could be significantly improved. For Theorems 1 and 2, it is unclear what the precise assumptions are; one can infer them from the surrounding text, but the theorem statements should be self-contained. In addition, the strong assumptions invoked to obtain results such as those in Theorem 6 are unnecessary; see, for example, Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret for more general results that cover related cases.

Overall, the manuscript appears to lack awareness of key prior work, and the novelty of the results is, at best, marginal. I recommend that the authors thoroughly review the literature on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics, clearly articulate how their approach differs from or extends existing methods, relax unnecessary assumptions where possible, and present all assumptions explicitly within the theorem statements.
