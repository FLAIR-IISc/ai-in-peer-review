## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission: [Paper Title - *Please fill in*]

This paper addresses the fundamental problem of weight learning in Markov networks, typically formulated as maximizing the likelihood function via gradient-based optimization. A core challenge within this process lies in efficiently computing the gradient, which is NP-hard in the general case. Consequently, Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods are frequently employed as approximations. 

The authors present compelling theoretical results demonstrating that, under specific conditions on the network weights, a weight learning algorithm leveraging MCMC for gradient estimation achieves a Fully Polynomial-Time Randomization Approximation Scheme (FPRAS). The analysis is carefully divided into two scenarios: the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (non-convex) case, with distinct bounds derived for each.

The paper is, for the most part, well-written and clearly presented. I observed a few minor typographical errors, but these do not significantly detract from the overall readability. While a complete verification of all proofs was beyond the scope of my review, the arguments appear logically sound and plausible.

The primary strength of this work is the formalization of conditions guaranteeing a FPRAS for MCMC-based weight learning. However, the practical relevance of these conditions is a significant concern. The authors themselves acknowledge this limitation, which is commendable. Specifically, the requirement for rapid MCMC convergence to the stationary distribution imposes constraints on the weight parameters that seem difficult, if not impossible, to ensure in practice. 

This raises a critical question: is it possible to *enforce* these convergence conditions artificially, perhaps through a tailored regularization scheme? Viewing the convergence requirement as a form of regularization raises the possibility of incorporating it directly into the learning process. However, combining this new regularization with existing techniques like ridge regression could lead to overly regularized models, potentially hindering performance.  It would be valuable if the authors discussed this potential interaction and explored strategies to mitigate it. 

Addressing this practical limitation would dramatically strengthen the paper. Currently, while theoretically interesting, the derived results do not translate directly into a practical algorithm for weight learning. Further research is needed to bridge this gap and explore methods for approximating or achieving the necessary conditions in realistic scenarios.

**Summary:**

This is a reasonably well-written paper that provides a valuable theoretical contribution by formalizing the conditions under which MCMC-based weight learning yields a FPRAS, both with and without regularization. However, the practical applicability of these conditions remains a significant issue. Substantial further investigation is required before the results can lead to a practical and accurate algorithm for weight learning. 

**Recommendation:** Weak Reject. While the theoretical results are noteworthy, the lack of clear path toward practical implementation prevents a stronger recommendation at this time. I encourage the authors to address the concerns regarding the enforceability of the convergence conditions and explore potential strategies for making their results more applicable to real-world problems.
