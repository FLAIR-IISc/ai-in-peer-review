Comments on Quality:
The paper presents convergence rates of learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, and the provided bounds appear to be meaningful and useful. However, the lower bound in Theorem 7 is somewhat challenging to interpret. To enhance the paper's quality, it would be beneficial to include a more detailed discussion of this theorem, as well as a broader exploration of the lower bounds applicable to this problem.

Comments on Clarity:
The presentation of the paper is clear and well-organized.

Comments on Originality:
The problem formulation, specifically the introduction of the "fast mixing" model class, and the accompanying theoretical results appear to be original contributions.

Comments on Significance:
While the significance of the paper is not immediately apparent to the reviewer, it does seem to address a gap in the existing literature on learning intractable models using MCMC. In this context, the paper's contribution is noteworthy. To further strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to include a more in-depth discussion of the potential impact of this work, including potential future extensions and opportunities for building upon these findings. The introduction of a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations is a valuable contribution. The theoretical results providing convergence rates for learning these models are novel and interesting, making a strong case for the paper's acceptance.
