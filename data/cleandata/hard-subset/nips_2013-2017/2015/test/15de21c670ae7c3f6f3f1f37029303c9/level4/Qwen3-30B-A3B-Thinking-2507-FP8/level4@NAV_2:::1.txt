

The paper addresses maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models by proposing to constrain parameters to "fast-mixing sets" where MCMC inference converges rapidly. While the approach is framed within exponential families, the core contribution—proving convergence guarantees for gradient descent with MCMC-based gradient approximation—lacks sufficient novelty relative to established literature. The authors' algorithm is directly related to Monte Carlo EM and broader classes of stochastic approximation methods for optimizing intractable likelihoods, which have been extensively studied. Notably, existing theory (e.g., Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret, 1990) provides convergence guarantees under significantly weaker assumptions: Markov chains need not be uniformly ergodic, and reinitialization at a fixed distribution \(r\) at every iteration (a simplifying assumption the authors acknowledge) is unnecessary and suboptimal. In fact, initializing chains from the previous sample—discussed as a potential improvement in the paper—has been rigorously analyzed in prior work (e.g., using contrastive divergence techniques). The presentation of key results also suffers from poor clarity: the assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not self-contained, forcing readers to cross-reference text before and after, which violates the expectation of a well-structured manuscript. Crucially, the strong assumptions employed (e.g., fixed reinitialization, uniform mixing bounds) are not required for the results; general stochastic approximation theory already covers these cases with minimal constraints. The authors’ failure to engage with this foundational literature undermines the perceived novelty of their work, which offers only marginal improvements over established frameworks rather than a significant advancement.
