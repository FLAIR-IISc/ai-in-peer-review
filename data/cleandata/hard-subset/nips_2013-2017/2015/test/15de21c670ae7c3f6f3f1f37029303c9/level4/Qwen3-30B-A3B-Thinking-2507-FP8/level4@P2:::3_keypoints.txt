- Addresses intractability of maximum likelihood learning for undirected graphical models due to partition function computation.
- Reviews prior tractable approaches (Chow-Liu, pseudolikelihood, score matching, high-girth) and their limitations in sample complexity, model misspecification, or inference tractability.
- Proposes "fast-mixing parameter sets": parameter constraints guaranteeing polynomial-time MCMC convergence to stationarity.
- Proves that gradient descent with MCMC gradient approximations under these constraints yields an FPRAS for maximum likelihood learning.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis for both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases with precise complexity bounds.
- Carefully analyzes trade-offs between gradient steps, MCMC samples, and chain transitions, accounting for mixing rates and gradient errors.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with consistent notation and accessible mathematical exposition.
- Contributions are novel and significant theoretically, establishing first guarantees for MCMC-based maximum likelihood under specific parameter constraints.
- Main practical limitation: fast-mixing constraints are highly restrictive and difficult to enforce in real-world models.
- No practical mechanism is provided to identify or enforce these constraints without risking over-regularization and degraded predictive performance.
- Illustrative example uses an artificial constraint (|θij| ≤ 0.2) that likely does not generalize to real applications.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution and clarity, but limited practical significance unless methods to enforce fast-mixing constraints without harming model quality are provided.
