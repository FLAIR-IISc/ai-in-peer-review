- Limited engagement with extensive literature on stochastic approximation for intractable likelihoods (especially Monte Carlo EM); manuscript must situate and compare to existing methods.  
- Proposed algorithm appears to be a specific instance of stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics, so novelty is questionable.  
- Existing theoretical frameworks already cover many of the authors' results under weaker conditions than those assumed here.  
- Requiring uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains is unnecessarily strong and not typical in prior analyses.  
- Re-initializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is a significant and restrictive simplification.  
- Warm-starting the chain from the previous state is standard, often yields faster convergence, and has been thoroughly analyzed but is ignored.  
- Presentation issues: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated inside the theorems, hindering clarity.  
- Theoretical analysis (e.g., Theorem 6) uses unnecessarily strong assumptions; more general results are available in foundational texts (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret).  
- Overall: failure to connect to a large relevant body of prior work leads to marginal novelty and re-derivation of known results for a specialized case.
