The primary weakness of this manuscript is a limited engagement with relevant prior work. The authors’ algorithm bears a strong resemblance to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, and more broadly to the established class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics used for optimizing intractable likelihood functions. A substantial body of literature exists in this area, and the authors should thoroughly compare their approach to existing methods. Indeed, a theoretical framework encompassing the authors' results already exists and addresses more general cases; for instance, it does not require uniformly ergodic Markov chains, nor does it necessitate re-initializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution *r* at each iteration – a simplification the authors themselves acknowledge. While they justify this re-initialization for analytical convenience, it is insufficient, and updating with the previous sample, as they suggest, may be preferable; algorithms employing such strategies have also been extensively studied. The presentation of the results could be significantly improved; specifically, the assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are unclear and require the reader to cross-reference the surrounding text. Furthermore, the strong assumptions imposed to derive results like those in Theorem 6 are unnecessary, as more general results are available (e.g., Benveniste, Metivier and Priouret). Overall, the manuscript demonstrates a lack of awareness of the existing literature, and the technical contributions, as presented, appear only marginally novel.
