- Addresses weight learning in Markov networks and the NP-hardness of exact gradient computation, motivating MCMC approximations.
- Main theoretical contribution: conditions under which MCMC-based weight learning yields a FPRAS.
- Analysis splits into two cases with separate bounds: unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex).
- Proofs appear logically sound and plausible, though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Paper is generally well-written and clear, with only minor typographical errors.
- Primary strength is formalizing convergence conditions that guarantee an FPRAS for MCMC-based learning.
- Major practical concern: the required rapid MCMC convergence imposes weight constraints that are hard to ensure in real-world settings.
- Raises the possibility of enforcing convergence conditions via a tailored regularization scheme, but notes potential over-regularization.
- Suggests the authors should discuss interactions between the new convergence-based regularizer and existing techniques like ridge regression and strategies to mitigate negative effects.
- Current results are theoretically interesting but do not yet translate into a practical weight-learning algorithm.
- Recommendation: Weak Reject; encourages addressing enforceability of convergence conditions and exploring practical approaches to make the results applicable.
