This paper presents a theoretical analysis of learning algorithms for a novel class of models defined by the "fast mixing" property of their corresponding MCMC samplers. The results provide rates of convergence for learning in terms of these mixing times, and the derived bounds appear both meaningful and useful.

The primary strengths of the work lie in its originality and technical contributions. The framing of the problem around a "fast mixing" model class is a novel and insightful perspective. The theoretical results establishing convergence rates for learning these models appear to be original and interesting. In this sense, the paper successfully fills a gap in the literature on learning intractable models with MCMC-based methods. The overall presentation is clear and well-written.

The main area for improvement concerns the lower bound presented in Theorem 7, which is very difficult to interpret in its current form. The manuscript would be significantly strengthened by an expanded discussion that clarifies the implications of this theorem and, more generally, the lower bounds applicable to this problem setting.

Furthermore, the broader significance of the work could be better articulated. While the contribution is evident from a theoretical standpoint, the paper would have a greater impact with a more thorough discussion of its potential applications, future extensions, and opportunities for other researchers to build upon these results.

In summary, the paper introduces a novel class of tractable models based on the efficiency of MCMC-based expectation approximation. The theoretical results on convergence rates are a significant and interesting contribution. Despite the need for greater clarity on the lower bound and a more detailed discussion of the work's impact, the submission is strong and should be accepted.
