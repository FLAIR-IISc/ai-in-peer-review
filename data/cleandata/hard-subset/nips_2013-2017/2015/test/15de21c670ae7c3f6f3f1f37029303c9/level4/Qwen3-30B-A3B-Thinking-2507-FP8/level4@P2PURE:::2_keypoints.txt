- Proposes a novel approach for computationally efficient MLE in exponential families by assuming rapid MCMC mixing for all allowed parameters.  
- Shows that integrals for proximal gradient descent can be approximated with sufficient precision under the fast-mixing assumption.  
- Combines the above with Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for the MLE.  
- Analyzes both convex and strongly convex cases, providing likelihood-error guarantees for the former and parameter-error guarantees for the latter.  
- Includes a simple experimental demonstration of the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions; theoretical analyses appear sound and the combination of techniques is nontrivial.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, note overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions, and suggest future directions.  
- Provides insights relevant to CD-type algorithms, despite not directly analyzing contrastive divergence.  
- Introduction is well-written with good references, but technical presentation has minor clarity issues.  
- Significant omission: the paper never explicitly states the convexity of the log-likelihood (A(θ) convex because its Hessian is the covariance of sufficient statistics).  
- Missing a clear summary of all paper assumptions; e.g., convexity of the parameter set Θ is not stated in the main text.  
- Practical impact limited because proving fast MCMC mixing is typically hard—paper replaces one difficult problem (MLE) with another (finding a fast-mixing chain), though this reduction is still valuable.  
- Minor editorial/typo corrections suggested (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312).
