- Paper addresses weight learning in Markov networks and the NP-hardness of computing gradients, motivating MCMC use.
- Main contribution: theoretical results showing that when MCMC mixes rapidly under given weights, MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.
- Separate analyses and bounds are provided for the unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases.
- Paper is generally well-written with only minor typos.
- Proofs appear plausible but were not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Major limitation: practical relevance is limited because the required fast-mixing parameter conditions are nearly impossible to guarantee in practice.
- Reviewer asks whether the authors can enforce the fast-mixing condition (e.g., via regularization) and warns that combining such enforcement with ridge regularization may cause over-regularization.
- Addressing how to satisfy or enforce the necessary conditions would substantially strengthen the paper’s impact.
- Overall: solid formal characterization of when MCMC yields an FPRAS, but low immediate practical applicability and non-trivial further research required for practical algorithms.
