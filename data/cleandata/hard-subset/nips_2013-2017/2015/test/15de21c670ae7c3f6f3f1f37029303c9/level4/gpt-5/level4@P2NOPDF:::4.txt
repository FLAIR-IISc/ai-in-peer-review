Comments on Quality:
The paper provides convergence rates for learning algorithms expressed in terms of mixing times, and the bounds seem both meaningful and useful. My main concern is that the lower bound (Theorem 7) is difficult to interpret. A more detailed explanation of this theorem and, more broadly, of the applicable lower bounds would strengthen the paper.

Comments on Clarity:
The exposition is clear and well organized.

Comments on Originality:
Both the problem formulation (via the “fast-mixing” model class) and the theoretical results appear original.

Comments on Significance:
The overall significance is somewhat uncertain, but the work appears to address an important gap in the literature on learning intractable models using MCMC, which makes it potentially impactful. The paper would benefit from additional discussion of its potential impact, including likely extensions and avenues for future work. By defining a new class of tractable models based on MCMC-approximable expectations and providing novel convergence-rate guarantees for learning in this class, the submission makes a compelling contribution. I recommend acceptance.
