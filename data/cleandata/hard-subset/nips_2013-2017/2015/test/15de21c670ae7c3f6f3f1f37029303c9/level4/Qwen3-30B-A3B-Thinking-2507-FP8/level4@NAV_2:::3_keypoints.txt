- Learning Markov network parameters requires gradient computation, which is NP-hard to do exactly, so MCMC is commonly used to approximate gradients.  
- The paper proves that if parameters lie in a "fast-mixing" set (where MCMC mixes rapidly), MCMC-based weight learning yields a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS).  
- The authors provide separate theoretical convergence bounds for both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.  
- The manuscript is well-written with minor typographical errors; the proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- The main limitation is practical applicability: ensuring parameters satisfy the fast-mixing constraints may be infeasible in real problems.  
- The reviewer requests discussion on whether fast-mixing constraints can be enforced artificially (e.g., via regularization) and whether that plus ridge regularization risks over-regularization.  
- Overall assessment: rigorous and mathematically impressive formalization of when MCMC-based learning is an FPRAS, but limited practical relevance and significant open research challenges remain.
