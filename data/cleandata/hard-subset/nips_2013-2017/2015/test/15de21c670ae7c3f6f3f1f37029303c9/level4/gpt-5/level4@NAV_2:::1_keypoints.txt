- Insufficient engagement with existing literature on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic-approximation methods with Markovian dynamics.  
- Proposed algorithm is closely related to long-established methods for optimizing intractable likelihoods and lacks clear novelty.  
- The paper fails to situate its contributions relative to a substantial body of prior work that already covers similarly general scenarios.  
- Uniform ergodicity assumptions are unnecessarily strong and not required for the considered results.  
- Reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is unjustified; warm-starting from previous states is common and better analyzed.  
- Presentation is unclear: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not explicitly stated and must not be left to inference.  
- Strong conditions used for results like Theorem 6 are likely unnecessary; more general treatments (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) should be considered.  
- Overall lack of awareness of key prior work leads to results that appear only marginally novel.
