## Review

The paper provides convergence rates for learning algorithms, expressed in terms of mixing times. The derived bounds appear meaningful and offer practical value. 

My primary concern lies with the interpretability of the lower bound presented in Theorem 7. A more detailed discussion of this theorem, and of lower bounds applicable to this problem in general, would be highly beneficial.

The presentation is clear and well-structured.

Both the problem framing – specifically, the definition of the “fast mixing” model class – and the theoretical results presented appear original. 

The significance of this work is somewhat unclear at present. However, it does appear to address a gap in the literature concerning learning intractable models using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods, and in that respect, it seems potentially significant.

To strengthen the submission, I recommend a more thorough discussion of the work’s potential impact, including possible future extensions and opportunities for building upon these results. The paper introduces a novel class of tractable models grounded in the good MCMC-based approximability of expectations. Given that the theoretical results providing convergence rates for learning these models are novel and interesting, I recommend acceptance.
