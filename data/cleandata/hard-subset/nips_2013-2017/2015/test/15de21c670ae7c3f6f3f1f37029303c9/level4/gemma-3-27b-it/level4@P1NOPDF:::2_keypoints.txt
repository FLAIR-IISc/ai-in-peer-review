- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient approach to maximum likelihood learning in exponential families using a fast-mixing MCMC assumption.  
- Shows integrals for proximal gradient descent can be approximated with sufficient precision under fast-mixing, yielding a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (with Schmidt et al. 2011).  
- Treats both convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood-error guarantees for the former and parameter-error guarantees for the latter.  
- Theoretical analysis appears solid and is a nontrivial combination of MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and existing optimization results.  
- Includes a simple experiment to demonstrate the theoretical claims.  
- Provides an honest, thoughtful discussion of limitations, possible over-pessimism in assumptions, and future work directions.  
- Offers insights into CD-type algorithms, though the analyzed algorithm is not strictly contrastive divergence.  
- Main practical limitation: proving fast mixing for MCMC is very challenging, possibly limiting real-world applicability.  
- Suggests explicitly stating that the log-likelihood f(θ) is convex (A(θ) convex because its Hessian is a covariance).  
- Recommends a clear, explicit summary of the paper’s assumptions, including that the parameter set Θ is convex (currently unstated).  
- Writing and introduction are strong with good references, but some technical presentation could be clarified for readers.  
- Minor editorial corrections noted (l185, l192, l207, l281) and a suggested rewording for the nonsensical sentence at l311–312.
