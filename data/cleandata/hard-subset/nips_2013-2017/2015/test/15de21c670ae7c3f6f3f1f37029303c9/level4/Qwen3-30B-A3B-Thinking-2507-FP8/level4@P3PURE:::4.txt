

Comments on Quality:  
The paper provides meaningful convergence rates for learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, with bounds that appear theoretically sound and practically useful. The primary concern lies with Theorem 7, which establishes a lower bound that is challenging to interpret. An expanded discussion of this theorem—including its implications and context relative to existing lower bounds for the problem—would significantly strengthen the manuscript.  

Comments on Clarity:  
The exposition is clear and well-structured, facilitating comprehension of the technical contributions.  

Comments on Originality:  
The problem framing, particularly through the lens of the "fast mixing" model class, and the associated theoretical results demonstrate clear originality.  

Comments on Significance:  
While the paper addresses a meaningful gap in the literature on learning intractable models via MCMC, the significance would be enhanced by a more thorough discussion of the work’s potential impact. Specifically, elaborating on specific future directions, extensions, and opportunities for building upon these results would better contextualize the contribution. Despite this, the novel class of tractable models defined by efficient MCMC-based approximability of expectations, coupled with the original convergence rate guarantees, constitutes a substantial advance. The theoretical results are both novel and compelling, and the paper should be accepted.
