**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

The paper "Maximum Likelihood Learning With Arbitrary Treewidth via Fast-Mixing Parameter Sets" explores an alternative approach to tractable learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models. Instead of restricting parameters to a tree-structured set, the authors consider a set of "fast-mixing parameters" where Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference can quickly converge to the stationary distribution. This idea is related to previous work on graphical models, but the authors' result is more general, applying to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.

The paper builds upon existing work on MCMC algorithms and their convergence properties. The authors cite relevant literature on mixing times and fast-mixing parameter sets, including results on the Ising model and pairwise Markov random fields. The paper's contribution is to establish a theoretical guarantee for maximum likelihood learning in these models, providing a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme.
