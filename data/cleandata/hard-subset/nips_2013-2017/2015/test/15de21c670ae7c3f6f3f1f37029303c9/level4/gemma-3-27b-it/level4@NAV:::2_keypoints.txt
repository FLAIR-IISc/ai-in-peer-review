- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient method for MLE in exponential families by approximating required integrals via fast-mixing MCMC and proximal gradient descent.  
- Shows that under a fast-mixing MCMC assumption the integrals can be approximated to sufficient accuracy, yielding a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Provides separate theoretical analyses for convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood error bounds for the former and parameter error bounds for the latter.  
- Analysis combines the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and results from Schmidt et al. (2011); proofs appear rigorous though not exhaustively checked by the reviewer.  
- Offers a simple experimental validation consistent with the theoretical claims.  
- Presents a compelling alternative to approaches requiring small-treewidth assumptions.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, acknowledge pessimism in assumptions, and suggest directions for future work.  
- Provides useful insights related to contrastive divergence algorithms, even though it does not directly analyze contrastive divergence.  
- Technical presentation needs clarification: the paper should explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood (convexity of A(θ) via covariance should be noted).  
- Main assumptions should be consolidated and clarified (e.g., explicitly state that the parameter set Θ is convex).  
- Practical applicability is limited by the difficulty of proving fast mixing for MCMC across parameter sets, effectively shifting one hard problem to another.  
- Minor typographical and wording errors throughout the manuscript that should be corrected.
