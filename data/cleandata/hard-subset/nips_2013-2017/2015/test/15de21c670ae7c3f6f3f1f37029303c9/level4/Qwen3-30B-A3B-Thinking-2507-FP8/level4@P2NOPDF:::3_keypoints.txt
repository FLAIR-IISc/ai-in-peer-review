- Paper studies weight learning in Markov networks where exact gradients are NP-hard and MCMC is used for approximation.  
- Authors derive theoretical conditions under which an MCMC-based weight learning algorithm is an FPRAS, with separate bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases.  
- Results are clearly presented; minor typographical errors exist and some technical details in proofs were not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- The core condition requiring rapid MCMC convergence under the learned parameters severely limits practical relevance.  
- Ensuring rapid MCMC mixing for arbitrary parameter settings is implausible without prior knowledge of network structure or parameters.  
- The paper does not explore methods to enforce the convergence condition during learning (e.g., additional regularization or adaptive MCMC).  
- Enforcing the condition via regularization risks over-regularization and degraded model performance.  
- There is a circular dependency: the required convergence rate must hold for the exact weights being learned, which the algorithm is trying to find.  
- Theoretical formalization of MCMC limits is valuable, but absence of a practical pathway to satisfy assumptions undermines utility.  
- Recommended future work: develop ways to enforce the convergence condition during learning and avoid over-regularization so results can inform practical algorithms.
