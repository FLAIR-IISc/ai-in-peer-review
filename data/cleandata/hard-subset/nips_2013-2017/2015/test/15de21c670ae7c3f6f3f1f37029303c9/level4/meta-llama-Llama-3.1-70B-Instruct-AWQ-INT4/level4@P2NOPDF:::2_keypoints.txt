- Proposes a novel method for computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing and using proximal gradient descent plus Schmidt et al. to obtain a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS).  
- Presents separate analyses for convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood-error guarantees for the former and parameter-error guarantees for the latter.  
- Includes a simple experiment that demonstrates the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions; combination of existing techniques is nontrivial and valuable.  
- Theoretical analysis appears solid, relying on the fast-mixing MCMC assumption, concentration inequalities, and prior optimization results.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, overly pessimistic assumptions, and directions for future work; provide insight into Contrastive Divergence–type algorithms (though the algorithm analyzed is not exactly CD).  
- Introduction is well-written with substantial references to prior work.  
- Technical presentation lacks an explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex and a brief justification that A(θ) is convex (Hessian = covariance of sufficient statistics, PSD).  
- Paper should clearly summarize all assumptions (e.g., that parameter set Θ is convex), as some assumptions are omitted from the main text.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast mixing for MCMC is extremely challenging—this shifts one hard problem (MLE) to another (designing fast-mixing chains).  
- Minor editorial issues and typos (specific line edits provided) and at least one sentence that needs rephrasing for clarity.
