- Paper proposes a stochastic-approximation algorithm with an embedded Markov chain for optimizing intractable likelihoods but substantially overlaps MCEM and stochastic-approximation EM literature.
- Strength: addresses a relevant, challenging problem using a principled stochastic-approximation framework and aims to provide convergence guarantees.
- Major concern: limited novelty and insufficient positioning relative to MCEM/MCMC-SAEM and existing stochastic-approximation-with-Markovian-noise theory.
- Major concern: overly strong assumptions, notably uniform ergodicity, which are not necessary and are stronger than typical prior work.
- Major concern: reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is unnecessary and impractical; persistent chains are standard and analyzed.
- Theorems 1 and 2: assumptions are not stated clearly and not placed adjacent to theorem statements.
- Theorem 6: uses stronger-than-necessary conditions; existing frameworks (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) could yield similar results under weaker assumptions.
- Literature positioning: must explicitly compare and contrast with MCEM, MCMC-SAEM, and stochastic-approximation-with-Markovian-noise results.
- Assumptions on Markov chains: replace uniform ergodicity with drift/minorization or other verifiable weaker conditions where possible.
- Chain persistence: allow and analyze persistent chains; if reinitialization is retained, provide theoretical and empirical justification and quantify trade-offs.
- Theorem statements and proofs: restate all assumptions immediately before each theorem and define all symbols at first use.
- Proofs: cite exact standard lemmas/theorems used and clearly indicate which parts are novel adaptations versus standard results.
- For Theorem 6: either relax assumptions to match existing results or explicitly clarify the novel contribution.
- Empirical evaluation: compare against MCEM and persistent-chain SAEM baselines; test sensitivity to step sizes, batch sizes, initialization strategy, and mixing; report cost per effective update.
- Clarity and presentation: improve notation consistency, add a dedicated “Assumptions” subsection, and provide a concise discussion (with citations) on why persistence is preferable and when reinitialization might be used.
- Overall assessment: contribution appears marginal relative to established work; recommend substantial revision to broaden literature comparison, relax assumptions, allow persistent chains, clarify theorems/proofs, and provide empirical comparisons.
