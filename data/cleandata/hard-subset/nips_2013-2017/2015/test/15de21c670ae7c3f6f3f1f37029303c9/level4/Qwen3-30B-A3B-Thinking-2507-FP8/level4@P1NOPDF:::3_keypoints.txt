- Addresses weight learning in Markov networks and the NP-hardness of exact gradient computation, motivating MCMC gradient approximations.
- Main contribution: theoretical conditions under which an MCMC-based weight learning algorithm is an FPRAS.
- Key result: if model parameters ensure rapid MCMC mixing to stationarity, the learning algorithm attains FPRAS guarantees.
- Analysis covers both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases, with separate theoretical bounds.
- Paper is generally well written with only minor typographical errors.
- Proofs appear plausible and logically structured, though the reviewer could not fully verify all details.
- Critical limitation: the rapid mixing condition required is practically unrealistic for most real-world models.
- Authors note this gap but do not investigate ways to enforce the mixing condition (e.g., via additional regularization).
- Potential remedy via embedding constraints into regularization is suggested, but risks excessive regularization and degraded performance; this trade-off is unexplored.
- Overall, the work precisely characterizes theoretical limits of MCMC-based learning but currently lacks a viable path to practical implementation or actionable algorithmic guidance.
