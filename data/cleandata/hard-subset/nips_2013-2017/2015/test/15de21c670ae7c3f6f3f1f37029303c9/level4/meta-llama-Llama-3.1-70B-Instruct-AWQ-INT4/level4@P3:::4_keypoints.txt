- Proposes a novel notion of tractable parameter sets: "fast-mixing parameters" where MCMC provably mixes quickly, enabling inference in high-treewidth undirected models.  
- Approach is fundamentally different from traditional tractable restrictions (e.g., tree-structured parameters).  
- Proves that for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, constraining parameters to a fast-mixing set yields that gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients approximates the maximum-likelihood solution with high probability.  
- Provides detailed analysis of mixing times, characterization of fast-mixing parameter sets, and convergence rates of the proposed algorithm.  
- Introduces a new class of tractable models defined by good MCMC-based approximability of expectations.  
- Demonstrates significance by enabling learning in high-treewidth graphical models, addressing a challenging problem.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and contains thorough explanations and related-work discussion.  
- Suggests the paper could be strengthened by more discussion of potential impact, future extensions, and opportunities for follow-up work.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept (rating 8/10, reviewer confidence 8/10).
