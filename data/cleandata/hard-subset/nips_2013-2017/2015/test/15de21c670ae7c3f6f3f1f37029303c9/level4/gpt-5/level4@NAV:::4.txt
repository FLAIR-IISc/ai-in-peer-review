Review- Comments on Quality:
The paper provides convergence rates for learning algorithms in terms of mixing times. The bounds seem meaningful and useful. My only major concern is that the lower bound (Theorem 7) is very hard to interpret. A more detailed discussion of this theorem and, more generally, of the lower bounds relevant to the problem would be desirable.

Comments on Clarity:
The exposition is clear.

Comments on Originality:
Both the problem framing (via the "fast mixing" model class) and the theoretical results appear original.

Comments on Significance:
The significance is not entirely clear to this reviewer. However, the work seems to address a gap in the literature on learning intractable models using MCMC, and in that sense it appears significant. The submission would benefit from further discussion of the potential impact (including future extensions and opportunities to build upon it). The paper introduces a new class of tractable models based on strong MCMC-based approximability of expectations. The theoretical results that provide convergence rates for learning these models appear novel and interesting, and the submission should therefore be accepted.
