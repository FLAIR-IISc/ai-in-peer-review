- Insufficient engagement with extensive existing literature on related algorithms and theory.
- Proposed algorithm closely resembles Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.
- Manuscript needs a detailed comparison against well-established prior work.
- Existing theoretical frameworks already cover many of the authors' results and often in more general settings.
- Re-initializing the Markov chain each iteration is unjustified and unnecessary; prior work analyzes alternate initializations.
- Authors themselves mention initializing from previous samples, a strategy already extensively studied in the literature.
- Presentation is poor: theorem assumptions are not clearly stated within the theorems and are scattered in the text.
- The manuscript uses unnecessarily strong assumptions (e.g., Theorem 6) whereas weaker, existing conditions suffice (see Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret).
- Overall conclusion: lack of awareness of prior work yields contributions of marginal novelty.
