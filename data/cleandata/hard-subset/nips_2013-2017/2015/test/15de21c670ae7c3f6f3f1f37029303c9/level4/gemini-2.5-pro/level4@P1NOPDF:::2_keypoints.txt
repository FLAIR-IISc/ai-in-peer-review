- Presents a novel method for computationally efficient MLE in exponential families by assuming a fast-mixing MCMC sampler to approximate integrals needed for proximal gradient descent, yielding an FPRAS and including a simple experiment.

- Provides guarantees for both convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases, leveraging Schmidt et al. (2011).

- Core theoretical contribution is a nontrivial synthesis of fast-mixing MCMC assumptions, concentration inequalities, and stochastic proximal-gradient convergence results.

- Offers a well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth approaches and gives useful insights related to CD-type algorithms.

- Thoughtful, honest discussion of limitations and how theoretical assumptions may be pessimistic in practice, plus directions for future work.

- Major practical limitation: the fast-mixing MCMC assumption is difficult to verify for most models, effectively replacing one hard problem (MLE) with another (proving fast mixing).

- Technical presentation needs clearer statements: explicitly justify log-likelihood convexity (e.g., Hessian of A(θ) is covariance of sufficient statistics).

- Paper should include a centralized, explicit summary of all assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ).
