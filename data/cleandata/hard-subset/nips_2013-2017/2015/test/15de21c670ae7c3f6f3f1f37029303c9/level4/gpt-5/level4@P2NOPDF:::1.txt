The main weakness of the manuscript is its limited engagement with the existing literature. The proposed algorithm is closely related to Monte Carlo EM and, more generally, to stochastic approximation methods with Markovian dynamics that have long been used to optimize intractable likelihoods. This area is well developed, and the paper needs to position its contribution relative to prior work. Much of the theory presented here already exists in more general form, and the paper should explain clearly what is new and why the additional assumptions or constraints are warranted.

In particular, the analysis relies on unnecessarily strong conditions. For instance, uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains is not required in the general theory, and there is no theoretical need to restart the chain from a fixed distribution r at each iteration. While the authors state this choice simplifies their analysis, that is not a sufficient justification—especially since continuing the chain from the previous state (as they note at the end of the manuscript) is often preferable in practice and has already been extensively studied. Existing results cover these scenarios without reinitialization and under weaker ergodicity or mixing conditions.

The presentation could also be substantially improved. For Theorems 1 and 2, the assumptions are not stated alongside the theorems; forcing the reader to hunt for conditions before and after the statements hinders clarity. Each theorem should be self-contained, with all assumptions, notation, and requirements listed explicitly where the result is stated. Similarly, the assumptions invoked for Theorem 6 appear stronger than necessary. Classic results in stochastic approximation (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret) provide more general convergence guarantees under weaker conditions; the manuscript should either rely on these standard frameworks or clearly justify why stronger assumptions are essential here.

Overall, the paper underestimates the breadth of relevant prior work, and the novelty appears limited. To strengthen the contribution, the authors should:
- Provide a thorough comparison with MCEM and stochastic approximation with Markovian noise, clarifying what is genuinely new in their algorithm and analysis.
- Rework the theoretical results to align with established frameworks that do not require uniform ergodicity or repeated reinitialization, or justify convincingly why such restrictions are needed.
- Revise the exposition so that all theorems are self-contained and assumptions are stated transparently.
- Situate the results within the broader literature (including the classical SA theory of Benveniste–Métivier–Priouret), highlighting differences, advantages, and limitations.

As written, the lack of literature awareness and reliance on strong, nonessential assumptions make the contribution only marginally novel. Addressing the points above would substantially improve both the clarity and the impact of the work.
