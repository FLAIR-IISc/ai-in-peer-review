**Comments on Quality:**
This work establishes convergence rates for learning algorithms, expressing these rates in terms of mixing times. The derived theoretical bounds are both non-trivial and practically relevant.

The primary weakness identified is the difficulty in interpreting the lower bound presented in Theorem 7. The manuscript would be substantially strengthened by an expanded discussion clarifying this theorem and providing a broader context on lower bounds relevant to this problem.

**Comments on Clarity:**
The manuscript is well-written and the arguments are presented clearly.

**Comments on Originality:**
The work demonstrates considerable novelty. This is evident in the proposed problem formulation, which introduces the "fast mixing" model class, as well as in the theoretical results themselves.

**Comments on Significance:**
While the broader impact is not immediately obvious, the work makes a significant contribution by addressing a notable gap in the literature concerning the learning of intractable models via MCMC. The manuscript's impact could be better highlighted with an expanded discussion on its potential applications, future research directions, and how other researchers might build upon these findings.

In summary, the paper introduces a novel and tractable model class defined by the efficient MCMC approximability of expectations. The accompanying theoretical results on convergence rates for learning these models are original and compelling. Consequently, this submission warrants acceptance.
