- Introduces a method for computationally efficient MLE in exponential families by restricting parameters to a “fast-mixing” set enabling rapid MCMC stationarity.
- Uses projected (proximal) gradient descent with MCMC-based expectation estimates and leverages inexact-gradient results to get a polynomial-time randomized approximation scheme.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for both convex (log-likelihood suboptimality) and strongly convex (parameter-distance error) regimes.
- The synthesis of mixing-time assumptions, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al. convergence theory is nontrivial and well executed.
- Clear, well-referenced introduction and candid discussion of limitations, future directions, and relation to contrastive divergence–style methods.
- Small illustrative experiment included but practical impact may be limited.
- Main practical limitation: certifying fast mixing for a given model and sampler is difficult, so the approach trades one hard problem for another.
- Minor technical presentation issues: should explicitly state convexity of the (regularized) negative log-likelihood and summarize all assumptions up front (e.g., convexity of constraint set Θ).
- Several small typographical and phrasing errors noted (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312) that need correction.
