The manuscript's primary weakness is its limited engagement with the relevant literature. The proposed algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM (MCEM) algorithm and, more broadly, falls into the general class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics, which have been widely studied for optimizing intractable likelihood functions. Given the extensive literature on this topic, the authors should position their contributions by comparing their algorithm and analysis to this existing work.

Much of the theoretical groundwork for this type of algorithm has already been established, often under more general conditions than those considered here. For instance, existing theory already encompasses several of the results presented by the authors, but for more general scenarios. Established theoretical frameworks, for example, do not require the Markov chains to be uniformly ergodic, nor do they necessitate re-initializing the chain from a fixed distribution `r` at each iteration. The authors acknowledge this re-initialization is done to simplify their analysis, but this justification is insufficient given that more general results are already available. Furthermore, the practice of re-initializing using the previous sample—a potentially more effective strategy acknowledged by the authors at the end of the manuscript—has also been extensively analyzed in prior work.

The presentation of the theoretical results also lacks clarity. For example, Theorems 1 and 2 are stated without a clear enumeration of their underlying assumptions. While these assumptions may be inferred from the surrounding text, they should be stated explicitly with the theorems for clarity and self-containment. The reader should not have to search for them.

Additionally, some of the theoretical claims rely on unnecessarily strong assumptions. The results in Theorem 6, for example, can be derived under much weaker conditions, as demonstrated in the classic work of Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret.

In summary, the manuscript's limited engagement with the substantial body of existing literature significantly weakens its contributions. As a result, the novelty of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical analysis appears to be marginal.
