Summary: This paper proposes a novel approach to computationally efficient maximum-likelihood learning in exponential families. In general, maximum-likelihood estimation is intractable. From a convex optimization perspective, the bottleneck is evaluating an integral with respect to the current exponential-family parameter. Assuming the underlying MCMC is fast-mixing for all admissible parameters, the authors show that the integrals required by proximal gradient descent can be approximated to sufficient accuracy so that, combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), one obtains a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE. Both the convex and strongly convex settings are analyzed, yielding different guarantees: likelihood error in the former and parameter error in the latter. A simple experiment illustrates the theory.

Overall, this is a high-quality piece of work. The approach is well-motivated as an alternative to small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analysis appears solid, though I have not checked the proofs in detail. Conceptually, the proofs combine the fast-mixing MCMC assumption with concentration inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al.; while the components are known, the combination is nontrivial.

I appreciated the candid discussion of limitations. The conclusion thoughtfully analyzes weaknesses of the approach, ways in which the assumptions may be overly pessimistic, and directions for future work. The paper also sheds light on CD-type algorithms, even though the analyzed algorithm is not exactly contrastive divergence.

The introduction is well written and well referenced, but there are a few clarity issues in the technical presentation. One small but important omission is an explicit statement that the negative log-likelihood is convex: the second and third terms in Eq. 1 are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(θ) follows because the Hessian of A is the covariance matrix of the sufficient statistics and is therefore PSD. While this may seem minor, the entire paper relies on the convexity of f(θ), and stating it up front would help orient the reader. I would also welcome a clear summary of all assumptions used. For example, the main paper never states that the parameter set Θ must be convex.

Overall, I found the paper to be an original and substantial contribution. However, the practical impact seems limited because proving fast mixing of MCMC is extremely challenging. In a sense, the paper replaces one hard problem (finding the MLE) with another (exhibiting a fast-mixing Markov chain). That said, reductions of this kind are often valuable, and making this conversion explicit is useful to the community.
