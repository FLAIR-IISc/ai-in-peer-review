- Contribution: Introduces tractability for MLE by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set and proves projected gradient descent with MCMC-estimated gradients is an FPRAS for MLE in that set.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous, technically sound analysis combining MCMC mixing time theory, concentration inequalities, and inexact-gradient optimization results.  
- Originality: First formal theoretical guarantees linking MCMC mixing rates to optimization convergence for MLE with MCMC-estimated gradients.  
- Scope of analysis: Treats both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases, providing convergence guarantees for log-likelihood and parameter error respectively.  
- Significance: Bridges MCMC theory and learning, provides a theoretical lens on heuristics like contrastive divergence, and may inspire design of provably fast-mixing parameterizations.  
- Clarity issue 1: Paper never explicitly states or justifies convexity of negative log-likelihood; should note Hessian of A(θ) equals covariance to be self-contained.  
- Clarity issue 2: Assumptions (e.g., convexity and projectability of Θ) are not consolidated; an explicit itemized assumptions list is needed.  
- Practical limitation: Replacing intractable MLE with the requirement to find a nontrivial convex fast-mixing Θ is difficult in practice, limiting immediate applicability.  
- Overall assessment: Original, substantial theoretical contribution that merits acceptance despite limited practical utility.  
- Minor comments: Fix typos (e.g., "of of", ",w here"), clarify the mixing time bound sentence in Section 3, and rephrase the abstract to say "a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme."
