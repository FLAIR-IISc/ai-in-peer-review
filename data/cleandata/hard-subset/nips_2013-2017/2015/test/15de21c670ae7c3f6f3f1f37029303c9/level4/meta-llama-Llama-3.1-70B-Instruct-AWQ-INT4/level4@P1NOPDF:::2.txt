The paper proposes a novel approach to computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families. In general, finding the maximum likelihood solution is intractable due to the need for calculating an integral with respect to the currently proposed exponential family (EF) parameter. From a convex optimization perspective, this is the main obstacle. By assuming that Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters, the authors are able to show that the integrals needed for proximal gradient descent can be calculated with sufficient precision. When combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for calculating the Maximum Likelihood Estimate (MLE) can be obtained. Both the convex and strongly convex cases are considered, leading to different types of guarantees: the former on the likelihood error, and the latter on the parameter error. A simple experiment demonstrating the theory is also included.

Overall, this is a high-quality piece of work. The approach is well-motivated as an alternative to relying on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear solid, though I haven't checked the proofs in detail. The proofs essentially combine the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al. The paper thus combines existing techniques in a nontrivial way.

I appreciated the authors' honesty in discussing the limitations of the approach. In particular, the conclusion provides a thoughtful analysis of the weaknesses of the approach, the ways in which the theoretical assumptions are overly pessimistic, and directions for future work. The paper also offers some insight into Contrastive Divergence (CD)-type algorithms, even though the algorithm analyzed is not quite Contrastive Divergence.

The introduction is well-written, with substantial references to previous work, but there are a few issues with the clarity of the technical presentation. One important omission is the statement that the log-likelihood is in fact convex. The convexity of the log-likelihood follows from the fact that the 2nd and 3rd terms in eq. 1 are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(θ) follows from the fact that the Hessian of A is the covariance of the sufficient statistics, and thus is Positive Semi-Definite (PSD). While seemingly minor, the whole paper is premised on the convexity of f(θ), and discussing it will help guide the reader through the logic of the paper. A clear summary of the assumptions made in the paper would also be helpful. For example, one assumption never mentioned in the main paper is that the parameter set Θ must be convex.

Overall, I found the paper to be an original and substantial contribution. The results seem to be of limited practical value due to the fact that proving fast mixing of MCMC is extremely challenging. So, in a sense, the paper has replaced one hard problem (finding the MLE) with another (finding a fast-mixing Markov chain). That said, being able to convert one problem into another is often useful, and thus having the results of the paper available to the community is valuable.
