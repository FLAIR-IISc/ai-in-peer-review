The submission presents convergence rates of learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, providing bounds that appear meaningful and useful. However, the lower bound in Theorem 7 is challenging to interpret, and an expanded discussion of this theorem, as well as the lower bounds applicable to the problem, would be beneficial to enhance understanding.

The presentation is clear and effectively communicates the ideas and results. The framing of the problem in terms of the "fast mixing" model class, as well as the theoretical results, appear original and contribute to the novelty of the submission.

The significance of the submission is not entirely clear, but it seems to fill a gap in the literature on learning intractable models using MCMC. The introduction of a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations is a valuable contribution. To further strengthen the submission, a more in-depth discussion of its potential impact, including future extensions and opportunities to build upon it, would be desirable.

Overall, the submission presents novel and interesting theoretical results, providing convergence rates for learning the proposed models. Therefore, it is recommended for acceptance, with the suggestion to provide additional context and discussion on its significance and potential impact.
