- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient approach to MLE in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters.  
- Shows integrals for proximal gradient descent can be approximated precisely, yielding a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions, with solid theoretical analysis combining MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and existing results.  
- Original and substantial contribution offering new perspectives on contrastive divergence-type algorithms.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast mixing of MCMC is challenging, reducing immediate applicability.  
- Introduction and references are strong, but technical presentation needs clarity—explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood and summarize assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ).  
- Several minor typographical and wording errors that should be fixed (lines indicated by reviewer).  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Impact 8/10; overall recommendation: accept.
