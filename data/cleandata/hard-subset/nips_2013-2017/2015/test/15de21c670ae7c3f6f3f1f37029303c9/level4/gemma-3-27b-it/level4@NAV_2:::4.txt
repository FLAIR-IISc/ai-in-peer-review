This paper investigates maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models by restricting parameters to a “fast-mixing” set, where Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference converges quickly. This offers an alternative to traditional tractable sets like tree-structured parameters. The core contribution is proving that, for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients approximates the maximum likelihood solution within the fast-mixing set with high probability, providing fully-polynomial time randomized approximation schemes—cubic in 1/ε for unregularized cases and quadratic for ridge-regularized ones.

The paper’s quality is strong. The results establish meaningful and useful convergence rates for learning algorithms based on mixing times. The theoretical analysis appears sound, and the results are presented with appropriate mathematical rigor. A minor concern is the difficulty in interpreting the lower bound presented in Theorem 7. Expanding the discussion around this theorem, and generally providing more context regarding applicable lower bounds for this type of problem, would be beneficial.

The presentation is clear and well-organized. The notation is introduced carefully, and the overall flow of the paper is logical. The authors effectively relate their work to existing literature, including challenges with traditional methods like pseudolikelihood and composite likelihoods, and prior work on tractable parameter sets like tree-structured distributions.

The work demonstrates good originality. Both the framing of the problem – defining tractability through fast-mixing properties – and the theoretical results appear novel. This provides a new perspective on learning in intractable models. The paper appropriately references relevant prior work, and the authors clearly articulate how their contributions differ.

The significance of the work is promising, although further elaboration would strengthen the paper. The authors correctly identify a gap in the literature regarding theoretical guarantees for MCMC-based learning in intractable models. The introduction of a novel class of tractable models based on MCMC approximability is a significant contribution. However, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the potential impact of these findings, including future research directions and opportunities for practical application. Despite this, the theoretical results are interesting and suggest the work should be accepted.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel framing of tractability based on MCMC mixing times.
*   Strong theoretical guarantees for convergence of gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients.
*   Provides fully-polynomial time randomized approximation schemes.
*   Fills a gap in the literature on learning intractable models.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lower bound (Theorem 7) is difficult to interpret and could benefit from further discussion.
*   Potential impact and future research directions could be elaborated on for greater clarity.
