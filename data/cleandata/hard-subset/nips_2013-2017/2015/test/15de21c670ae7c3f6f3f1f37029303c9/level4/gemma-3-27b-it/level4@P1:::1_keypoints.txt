- Problem: Learning maximum likelihood in high-treewidth undirected graphical models is intractable due to partition function evaluation; paper studies this under fast-mixing parameter constraints.
- Core idea: Constrain parameters to a fast-mixing set so MCMC converges quickly, and use MCMC-approximated gradients in gradient descent to approximate MLE.
- Main theoretical result: Provides a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for MLE when using fast-mixing parameters.
- Convergence rates: Establishes convex and strongly-convex convergence with computational effort scaling as 1/ε³ and 1/ε² respectively (ignoring logs).
- Scope: Results are framed for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics and not limited to graphical models.
- Novelty strength: Shifts tractability focus from graph structure to MCMC convergence properties, offering a distinct alternative.
- Theoretical strength: Supplies rigorous FPRAS guarantees that justify a commonly used practical method (gradient descent with sampled gradients).
- Generality strength: Applicable beyond graphical models to broad exponential family settings.
- Presentation strength: Core theorems and proof sketches (Theorems 2, 5, 6, 8) are clearly stated and intuitive.
- Related-work weakness: Insufficient engagement with extensive literature on stochastic approximation and Markovian dynamics (e.g., Monte Carlo EM); misses many existing general results.
- Assumptions/clarity weakness: Theorems 1 and 2 rely on assumptions not explicitly stated within the theorem statements, reducing clarity.
- Conservatism weakness: Some assumptions (notably for Theorem 6) are overly restrictive and ignore broader theoretical frameworks (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier & Priouret).
- Re-initialization weakness: Authors dismiss re-initializing chains from previous samples despite substantial prior work showing its benefits.
- Projection complexity weakness: Analysis omits potentially significant computational cost of projecting parameters onto the fast-mixing set.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel theoretical approach, useful FPRAS guarantees, and broad applicability beyond graphical models.
- Arguments against acceptance: Weak literature awareness, restrictive assumptions, and unjustified dismissal of established strategies reduce impact.
- Overall assessment: Interesting and valuable theoretical contribution but major revision required to address related work and assumptions; recommend rejection now with encouragement to resubmit after substantial revisions.
