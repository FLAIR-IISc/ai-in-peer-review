The manuscript under review has several key issues that detract from its overall impact. The primary concern is the authors' apparent lack of awareness of the existing literature on the topic. The proposed algorithm bears a strong resemblance to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, as well as the broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics, which have been extensively explored in the context of optimizing intractable likelihood functions.

A thorough examination of the literature reveals that a well-established theoretical framework already encompasses many of the authors' results, and in fact, covers a more comprehensive range of scenarios. Notably, the existing theory does not require Markov chains to be uniformly ergodic, and there is no necessity to reinitialize the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r at each iteration. While the authors acknowledge that they employ this reinitialization strategy to simplify their analysis, this approach is not entirely justified. Furthermore, reinitializing with the previous sample, as discussed in the manuscript's conclusion, has also been extensively analyzed in prior work.

The presentation of the results is also suboptimal. For instance, the assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated. Readers should not be required to refer to preceding or subsequent text to understand the underlying assumptions. Additionally, the strong assumptions used to derive results such as those in Theorem 6 are unnecessary, as more general results have been established in the literature (e.g., Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret).

In light of these observations, it appears that the authors are not adequately familiar with the existing literature, and consequently, their results are only marginally novel. To strengthen the manuscript, the authors should engage more thoroughly with the relevant literature, comparing and contrasting their approach with existing work, and highlighting the unique contributions of their research.
