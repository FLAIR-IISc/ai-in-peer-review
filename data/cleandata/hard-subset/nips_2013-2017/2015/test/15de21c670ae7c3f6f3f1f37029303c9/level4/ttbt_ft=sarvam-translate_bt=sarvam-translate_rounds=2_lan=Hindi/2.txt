Summary: This paper proposes a new approach for computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families. Generally, finding a maximum likelihood solution is difficult. From a convex optimization perspective, the problem is that currently proposed EF parameters require the calculation of an integral. Assuming that MCMC is exponentially mixed for all permitted parameters, the authors are able to show that the integral required for proximal gradient descent can be computed with sufficient accuracy, which, combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), can generate a complete- बहुपद random approximation scheme for the calculation of MLE. Both convex and strongly convex cases are considered, providing different types of guarantees: one on the probability error, the other on the parameter error. A simple experiment demonstrating the theory is also included.

Overall, this is a high-quality work. This approach is well-motivated as an alternative to relying on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analysis seems solid, although I have not examined the evidence in detail. The proof essentially involves reconciling the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al. Thus, this paper is a combination of existing techniques, although the combination is non-trivial.

I appreciated the honesty with which the limitations of the approach were discussed. In particular, a thoughtful conclusion analyzed the weaknesses of the approach, the overly pessimistic methods of theoretical assumptions, and directions for future work. This paper also provides some insights into CD-type algorithms, even though the algorithm analyzed is not a complete reverse deviation.

The introduction was well-written with sufficient references to previous work, but there were some problems with the clarity of the technical presentation. A minor but significant omission was simply the statement that the log-probability is actually convex: the second and third terms in equation 1 are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(theta) follows from the fact that the Hessian of A is the covariance of the sufficient statistic, and thus the PSD. Although it seems trivial, the entire paper is based on the convexity of f(theta), and therefore discussing this will help guide the reader through the paper's arguments. A clear summary of the assumptions made in the paper. For example, a notion that is never mentioned in the main paper is that the parameter set theta should be convex.

Overall, I found this paper to be an original and important contribution. The results seem to be of limited practical value because proving the exponential mixing of MCMC is extremely challenging. Therefore, in a way, this paper has replaced a difficult problem (finding MLE) with another (finding exponentially mixed Markov chains). That being said, being able to convert one problem into another is often useful, and thus the results of a paper available to the community are valuable.
