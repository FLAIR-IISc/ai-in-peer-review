The paper presents convergence rates for learning algorithms expressed in terms of mixing times, and the derived bounds appear both meaningful and practically useful. 

My primary concern lies with the interpretability of the lower bound presented in Theorem 7. A more detailed discussion of this theorem, alongside a broader contextualization of applicable lower bounds for this problem, would be highly beneficial.

The presentation is clear and well-organized.

Both the problem formulation – specifically, framing the analysis within a “fast mixing” model class – and the resulting theoretical findings appear to be original contributions.

The significance of this work is not immediately apparent, though it does address a gap in the literature concerning learning intractable models via Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC). In this respect, the paper possesses potential significance. To strengthen the submission, I recommend a more thorough discussion of the work’s potential impact, including possible future extensions and avenues for building upon these results. The introduction of a novel class of tractable models grounded in strong MCMC-based approximation of expectations is a notable achievement. Given the novelty and interest of the theoretical convergence rate results for learning these models, I recommend acceptance.
