- Limited engagement with relevant prior work; manuscript ignores a substantial body of related literature.
- Proposed algorithm closely resembles Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation methods with Markovian dynamics.
- Authors fail to thoroughly compare their approach against existing methods and algorithms.
- Existing theoretical frameworks already cover more general cases than the paper claims (e.g., no uniform ergodicity requirement).
- Re-initializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is an unjustified simplification.
- Updating the chain with the previous sample is preferable and has been extensively studied in prior work.
- Presentation is unclear: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not stated transparently and require cross-referencing.
- Strong assumptions used for results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) exist.
- Overall technical contribution appears marginally novel due to lack of literature awareness and insufficient comparison to prior work.
