- Proposes a novel computational method for efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families.
- Leverages a uniform fast-mixing MCMC assumption to compute integrals needed for proximal gradient descent.
- Combines this MCMC approach with Schmidt et al. (2011) to produce a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for the MLE.
- Provides separate theoretical guarantees for convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.
- Includes a simple experimental demonstration validating the theoretical framework.
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and appears to have robust theoretical analysis.
- Effectively combines existing techniques (MCMC mixing, concentration inequalities, Schmidt et al.) in a non-trivial way.
- Authors transparently discuss limitations, pessimistic assumptions, and avenues for future work.
- Offers useful insights related to contrastive divergence–type algorithms, though it does not analyze CD directly.
- Introduction and references are strong, but some technical clarity is lacking (e.g., explicit statement of log-likelihood convexity).
- Recommends clearer presentation of assumptions (e.g., convexity of Theta, PSD Hessian from covariance of sufficient statistics).
- Practical impact may be limited because proving fast MCMC mixing for all parameters is difficult, though the theoretical reduction is valuable.
- Minor typos and wording errors noted (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312).
