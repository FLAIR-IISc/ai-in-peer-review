The main issue with the manuscript is insufficient engagement with the relevant literature. The proposed algorithm is closely related to Monte Carlo EM and, more broadly, to the class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics that have been proposed in various forms to optimize intractable likelihoods. There is a well-developed body of work on these methods, and the authors should compare their approach and results explicitly to existing analyses. In fact, a theory that already includes some of the authors’ results exists and covers substantially more general scenarios.

For example, standard analyses do not require the underlying Markov chains to be uniformly ergodic, nor do they require reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r at each iteration. Although the authors state that this reinitialization is used to simplify the analysis, this justification is not sufficient; moreover, as noted at the end of the manuscript, reinitializing from the previous sample is likely a better strategy, and such algorithms have already been extensively analyzed in the literature.

The presentation of the results also needs improvement. For Theorems 1 and 2, the assumptions are not clearly stated alongside the theorems. While one can infer them from the surrounding text, the reader should not be required to do so; the assumptions should be explicitly listed in the theorem statements. In addition, the strong assumptions imposed for results such as Theorem 6 are unnecessary; see, for example, Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret for more general results that render such assumptions superfluous.

Overall, the manuscript demonstrates limited awareness of prior work, and the contribution appears only marginally novel in light of existing theory and analyses.
