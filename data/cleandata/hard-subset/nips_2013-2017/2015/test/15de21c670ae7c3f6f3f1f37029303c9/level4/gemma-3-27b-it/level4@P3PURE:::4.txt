Comments on Quality:
The results provide convergence rate analyses for learning algorithms, expressed in terms of mixing times. The derived bounds appear meaningful and practically useful.
The primary concern is the limited interpretability of the lower bound presented in Theorem 7. Expanding the discussion of this theorem, and more broadly, the applicable lower bounds for this problem, would be beneficial.

Comments on Clarity:
The presentation is clear and well-structured.

Comments on Originality:
Both the problem framing – specifically, the introduction of the “fast mixing” model class – and the theoretical results appear original. 

Comments on Significance:
The significance of this work is somewhat unclear, though the paper does appear to address a gap in the literature regarding learning intractable models using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods, suggesting a degree of importance. 
The submission would be strengthened by a more detailed discussion of its potential impact, including possible future extensions and opportunities for building upon these findings. The introduction of a novel class of tractable models founded on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations is a notable contribution. Given the novelty and interest of the theoretical results providing convergence rates for learning these models, acceptance is recommended.
