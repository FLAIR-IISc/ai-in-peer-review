- Background: Weight learning in Markov networks uses gradient descent, but gradient computation is NP-hard so MCMC is commonly used instead of exact methods.  
- Main contribution: The paper proves that if MCMC mixes rapidly for the current parameters, then MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.  
- Cases analyzed: Separate theoretical bounds are derived for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (non-convex) case.  
- Writing and proofs: Paper is generally well-written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Practical relevance: The theoretical conditions (rapid mixing for the true parameters) are unlikely to hold in practice, limiting applicability.  
- Possible fix/suggestion: Authors should consider whether the rapid-mixing condition can be artificially enforced (as a form of regularization).  
- Regularization concern: Combining ridge regression with the enforced condition may lead to over-regularization and poor models.  
- Impact: Results provide a formal characterization of when MCMC yields an FPRAS for weight learning, but translating this into a practical, accurate algorithm requires solving several non-trivial research problems.
