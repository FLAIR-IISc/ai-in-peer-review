- Main idea: Proposes learning high-treewidth undirected graphical models by constraining parameters to "fast-mixing" sets so MCMC inference converges quickly.
- Generality: Results apply to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just specific models.
- Theoretical contribution: Establishes a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme and theoretical guarantees for maximum likelihood learning under fast-mixing conditions.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on literature on MCMC convergence and mixing times, citing results for Ising models and pairwise MRFs.
- Strength — novelty: Introduces a novel tractable-learning approach for high-treewidth graphical models.
- Strength — rigor: Provides a significant theoretical guarantee for ML learning and appears technically sound with correct proofs.
- Strength — clarity: The paper is well-written with clear explanations of ideas and technical results.
- Weakness — practicality: The required fast-mixing parameter condition may be difficult or unrealistic to ensure in practice.
- Weakness — limited impact: Authors acknowledge the results have little to no immediate practical relevance, reducing overall impact.
- Weakness — future work gap: Paper does not outline a clear path to solve the non-trivial research problems needed for practical application.
- Evaluation: Quality, clarity, and originality are high, but significance is tempered by practical limitations.
- Decision-level arguments: For acceptance — novel and theoretically significant; Against — impractical conditions, limited real-world applicability, and lack of roadmap.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with encouragement for authors to address the practical research challenges before application.
