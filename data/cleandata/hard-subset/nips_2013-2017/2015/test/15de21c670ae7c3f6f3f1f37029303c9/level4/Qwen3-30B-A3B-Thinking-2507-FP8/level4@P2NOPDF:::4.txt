

Comments on Quality:  
The paper establishes meaningful convergence rates for learning algorithms within the "fast mixing" model class, framed through mixing time analysis. The theoretical bounds are both mathematically sound and practically relevant, addressing a critical gap in the literature on MCMC-based learning for intractable models. The primary concern lies with Theorem 7 (the lower bound), which is challenging to interpret due to its technical complexity. An expanded discussion of this result—including its implications for the problem’s inherent difficulty and a comparison with existing lower bounds—would significantly enhance the paper’s rigor and accessibility.  

Comments on Clarity:  
The presentation is exceptionally clear, with logical flow and precise mathematical exposition. Key concepts, such as the "fast mixing" model class, are well-defined, and the structure facilitates easy comprehension of the theoretical contributions.  

Comments on Originality:  
The work introduces a novel framework for tractable learning via efficient MCMC approximability, distinguishing itself through both its problem formulation and theoretical results. The "fast mixing" model class represents a fresh perspective on learning intractable distributions, and the derived convergence guarantees are original to the best of the reviewer’s knowledge.  

Comments on Significance:  
The paper addresses a substantial gap in the theoretical understanding of MCMC-based learning, where prior work often lacks explicit convergence guarantees for complex models. While the current significance discussion is somewhat limited, the paper’s core contribution—providing rigorous rates for learning within a broad, practically relevant class of models—holds clear value for both theoretical and applied machine learning. To strengthen this aspect, the authors should elaborate on the potential impact: specifically, how their framework enables new applications (e.g., in Bayesian inference for large-scale systems), identifies limitations of current MCMC approaches, and suggests concrete pathways for future work (e.g., extensions to non-stationary settings or connections to deep learning). A brief mention of empirical validation opportunities would further contextualize the theoretical advances.  

Overall Recommendation:  
The paper makes a strong, original contribution to the theoretical foundations of MCMC-based learning. The minor issue with Theorem 7 is resolvable through minor revisions, and the proposed significance enhancements would solidify the work’s impact. Given the novelty of the framework, the technical soundness of the results, and the paper’s clear presentation, I recommend acceptance.
