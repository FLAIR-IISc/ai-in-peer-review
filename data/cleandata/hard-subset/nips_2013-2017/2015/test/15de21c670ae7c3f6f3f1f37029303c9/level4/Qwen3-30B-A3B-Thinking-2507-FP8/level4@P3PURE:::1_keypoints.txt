- Manuscript fails to engage sufficiently with established literature on stochastic approximation methods for intractable likelihood optimization.
- Proposed algorithm is closely related to Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics and requires an explicit comparison to that prior work.
- Existing theoretical foundations already cover more general settings (e.g., relaxing uniform ergodicity and not requiring per-iteration reinitialization).
- Reinitialization to a fixed distribution r is inadequately justified; reinitializing from the previous sample is more effective and has been rigorously analyzed previously.
- Presentation is poor: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated and must be inferred from the text.
- Some assumptions (e.g., in Theorem 6) are unnecessarily strong; general results (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) already handle these cases.
- Overall lack of awareness of relevant literature makes the contribution only marginally novel.
