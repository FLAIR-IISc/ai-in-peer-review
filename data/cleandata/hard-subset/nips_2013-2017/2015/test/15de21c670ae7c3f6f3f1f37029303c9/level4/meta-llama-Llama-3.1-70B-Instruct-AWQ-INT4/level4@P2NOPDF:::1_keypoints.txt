- Manuscript fails to engage with existing literature on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic-approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics.  
- The proposed algorithm strongly resembles Monte Carlo EM and prior stochastic approximation methods for intractable likelihoods; the authors should compare to those established methods.  
- A broader theoretical framework already exists that encompasses the authors' results and handles more general settings (e.g., no uniform ergodicity).  
- Reinitializing the Markov chain to the previous sample (rather than a fixed distribution r) is preferable and has been extensively studied in prior work.  
- Assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are not explicitly stated and should be included within the theorems.  
- The strong assumptions used for results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results are available (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret).  
- Overall, the paper shows limited awareness of prior work and offers only marginal novelty.
