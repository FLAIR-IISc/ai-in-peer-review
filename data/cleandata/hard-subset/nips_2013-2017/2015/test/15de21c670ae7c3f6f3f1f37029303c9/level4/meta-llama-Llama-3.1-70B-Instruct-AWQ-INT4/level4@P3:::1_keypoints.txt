- Paper studies "fast-mixing parameters" for tractable MLE in high-treewidth undirected graphical models using MCMC.  
- Authors prove gradient descent with sampled gradients approximates MLE inside a fast-mixing parameter set for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Problem is well-motivated and important for high-treewidth graphical models.  
- Paper provides a clear, concise introduction and is well-organized with understandable notation and assumptions.  
- Examples give useful insights into algorithm behavior.  
- Major concern: lack of awareness and comparison to existing literature (Monte Carlo EM, stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics).  
- Algorithm is closely related to well-studied Monte Carlo EM and other stochastic approximation methods.  
- Existing theory already covers more general scenarios than presented (e.g., no need for uniform ergodicity).  
- Reinitializing the Markov chain each iteration is unnecessary and prior work often reuses previous samples; this is not acknowledged adequately.  
- Presentation issues: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated alongside the theorems.  
- The paper uses overly strong assumptions (e.g., for Theorem 6) that are not needed given prior results.  
- The results are of only marginal novelty due to overlap with existing theory.  
- Reviewer recommends a thorough literature review and explicit comparison to related work.  
- Reviewer asks for clearer, self-contained statements of assumptions and results.  
- Reviewer suggests relaxing strong assumptions and extending analysis to more general settings.
