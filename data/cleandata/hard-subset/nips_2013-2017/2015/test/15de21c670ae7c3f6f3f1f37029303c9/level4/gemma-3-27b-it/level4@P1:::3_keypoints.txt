- Paper studies weight learning in Markov networks where MCMC approximates NP-hard gradient computations in gradient descent.
- Main theoretical result: if parameters lie in a set permitting fast MCMC mixing, then MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.
- Separate bounds derived for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.
- Proofs appear plausible and are generally well-structured, though not exhaustively verified by reviewer.
- Paper is generally well-written with minor typos.
- Practical relevance is limited because ensuring the fast-mixing parameter constraint is difficult in practice.
- Reviewer asks whether the authors considered enforcing the fast-mixing condition, e.g., via regularization.
- Combining ridge regularization with a fast-mixing constraint might cause over-regularization but is worth exploring.
- Addressing the enforceability of fast mixing would substantially strengthen the paper.
- Contribution formalizes conditions under which MCMC gradients yield polynomial-time approximations for learning.
- Novelty: introduces fast mixing as a tractability notion beyond traditional treewidth-based constraints.
- Strengths for acceptance: strong theoretical guarantees, novel tractability notion, clear presentation, and comprehensive regularized/unregularized analysis.
- Weaknesses against acceptance: limited practical applicability, unresolved open question about enforcing fast mixing, and somewhat incremental given reliance on existing techniques.
