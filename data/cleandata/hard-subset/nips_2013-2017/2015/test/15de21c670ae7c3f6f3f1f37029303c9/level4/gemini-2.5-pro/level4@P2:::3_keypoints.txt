- Problem: maximum-likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected models is intractable because gradients require expectations that are approximated by biased MCMC samples.  
- Main contribution: introduce "fast-mixing parameter set" and prove that gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS for ML within that set.  
- Conceptual shift: reframes tractability from graphical structure (e.g., treewidth) to algorithmic properties (fast mixing).  
- Generality: analysis applies to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics and treats both convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases.  
- Technical quality: provides rigorous, explicit bounds on computational effort and builds on established convex-optimization and concentration-inequality results.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, with precise setup, notation, and clear theorem statements.  
- Significance: offers the first formal convergence guarantees for the common MCMC-gradient heuristic, bridging theory and practice.  
- Practical limitation: strong assumption that one can efficiently identify and project onto a meaningful fast-mixing parameter set Θ, which may be as hard as the original problem for many models.  
- Modeling concern: enforcing Θ via hard projection may be too restrictive or lead to over-regularization; a relaxed approach (e.g., regularization to encourage fast mixing) is not explored.  
- Overall verdict: strong foundational theoretical contribution with limited immediate practicality but high value; recommended for acceptance.
