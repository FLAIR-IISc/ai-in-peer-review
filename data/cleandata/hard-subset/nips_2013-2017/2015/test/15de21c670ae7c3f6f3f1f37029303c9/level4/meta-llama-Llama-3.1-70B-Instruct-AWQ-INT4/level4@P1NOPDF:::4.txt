Comments on Quality:
The paper presents convergence rates of learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, providing bounds that appear meaningful and useful. However, a significant concern is that the lower bound (Theorem 7) is challenging to interpret. A more in-depth discussion of this theorem, as well as a broader examination of the lower bounds applicable to this problem, would be beneficial to enhance understanding.

Comments on Clarity:
The presentation is clear and well-organized, making it easy to follow the author's arguments.

Comments on Originality:
The problem framing in terms of the "fast mixing" model class, as well as the theoretical results, appear to be original contributions. The author's approach to addressing the challenges of learning intractable models using MCMC is novel and noteworthy.

Comments on Significance:
While the significance of the paper is not immediately apparent to the reviewer, it does seem to fill a gap in the literature on learning intractable models using MCMC. This contribution is significant in itself. To further strengthen the paper, the author could provide a more detailed discussion of the work's potential impact, including potential future extensions and opportunities for building upon this research.

Overall, the submission introduces a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations. The theoretical results providing convergence rates for learning these models are novel and interesting. Therefore, the submission is worthy of acceptance.
