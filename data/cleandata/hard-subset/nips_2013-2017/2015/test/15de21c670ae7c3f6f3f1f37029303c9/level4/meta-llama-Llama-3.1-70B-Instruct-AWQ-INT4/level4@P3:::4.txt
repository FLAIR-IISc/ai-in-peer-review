This paper presents a novel approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models, where inference is typically intractable. The authors propose an alternative notion of a tractable set, namely a set of "fast-mixing parameters" where Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference can be guaranteed to quickly converge to the stationary distribution. This approach is fundamentally different from the traditional method of restricting parameters to a tractable set, such as tree-structured parameters.

The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed approach, including a proof that for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, when parameters are constrained to a fast-mixing set, gradient descent with gradients approximated by sampling will approximate the maximum likelihood solution inside the set with high-probability. The authors also provide a detailed discussion of the mixing times and fast-mixing parameter sets, as well as the convergence rates of the proposed algorithm.

The results of the paper are significant, as they provide a new perspective on learning intractable models using MCMC. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be used to learn high-treewidth graphical models, which is a challenging problem in the field. The paper also introduces a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations.
