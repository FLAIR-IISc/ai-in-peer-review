**Comments on Quality:**

The paper establishes convergence rates for learning algorithms in terms of MCMC mixing times. The derived bounds appear to be both meaningful and useful.

My main criticism concerns the lower bound presented in Theorem 7, which is difficult to interpret. The paper would be significantly strengthened by an expanded discussion of this theorem, as well as a more general treatment of the lower bounds applicable to this class of problems.

**Comments on Clarity:**

The paper is well-written and the presentation is clear.

**Comments on Originality:**

The work demonstrates originality in both its formulation of the problem—specifically, the introduction of the "fast mixing" model class—and its theoretical results, which appear to be novel contributions to the field.

**Comments on Significance:**

The broader significance of this work is not immediately obvious from the manuscript. However, the paper makes a valuable contribution by filling a recognized gap in the literature on learning intractable models with MCMC. The submission would be strengthened by a more thorough discussion of its potential impact, including promising avenues for future work and opportunities for other researchers to build on these results.

Overall, the paper introduces a novel class of tractable models defined by their efficient approximability via fast-mixing MCMC methods. The theoretical results on convergence rates for learning these models are novel and interesting. Given these strong contributions, I recommend that the paper be accepted.
