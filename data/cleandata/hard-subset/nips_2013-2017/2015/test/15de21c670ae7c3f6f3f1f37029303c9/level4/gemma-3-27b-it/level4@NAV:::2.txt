## Review:

The paper introduces a novel and computationally efficient approach to maximum likelihood estimation within exponential families. Obtaining the maximum likelihood solution is generally intractable; the core difficulty lies in computing an integral involving the current exponential family parameter, viewed from a convex optimization perspective. By leveraging the assumption of fast-mixing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) for all permissible parameters, the authors demonstrate that the necessary integrals for proximal gradient descent can be approximated to sufficient accuracy. This, in conjunction with results from Schmidt et al. (2011), yields a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for computing the MLE. The analysis encompasses both convex and strongly convex cases, resulting in distinct theoretical guarantees – likelihood error bounds for the former and parameter error bounds for the latter. A simple experimental validation of the theory is also presented.

This is a high-quality contribution. The proposed method provides a compelling alternative to approaches reliant on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear rigorous, although a detailed verification of all proofs was beyond the scope of this review. The proofs primarily involve combining the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and the findings of Schmidt et al., representing a nontrivial, albeit synergistic, application of existing techniques. 

I particularly commend the authors’ candid discussion of the approach’s limitations. The conclusion thoughtfully analyzes the method’s weaknesses, acknowledges potential pessimism in the theoretical assumptions, and suggests promising avenues for future research. Furthermore, the work provides valuable insights into algorithms related to contrastive divergence, despite not directly analyzing a contrastive divergence algorithm itself.

The introduction is well-written and thoroughly references related work. However, some aspects of the technical presentation could benefit from clarification. Notably, the paper would be strengthened by explicitly stating the convexity of the log-likelihood function. The convexity of the second and third terms in equation 1 is evident, and the convexity of *A(θ)* follows from its Hessian being the covariance of the sufficient statistics – thus positive semi-definite.  While seemingly minor, the entire paper rests on the convexity of *f(θ)*, and a clear statement of this fact would greatly assist the reader. A consolidated summary of the paper’s key assumptions would also be beneficial; for instance, the requirement that the parameter set *Θ* be convex is not explicitly stated in the main text.

Overall, I view this paper as an original and significant contribution to the field. While the practical applicability of the results may be limited by the inherent difficulty of proving fast mixing for MCMC, transforming one challenging problem (MLE computation) into another (fast-mixing MCMC) is a valuable outcome.  Making these results available to the research community is therefore worthwhile.
