- Tackles weight learning in Markov networks where gradient estimation is NP-hard and MCMC is commonly used as an approximation.  
- Main contribution: proves that MCMC-based gradient computation yields an FPRAS under specific conditions on model weights.  
- Provides separate analyses and bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) settings.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear, with only minor typographical errors.  
- Reviewer could not fully verify all proofs but finds the presented arguments plausible and logically sound.  
- Strength is the rigorous theoretical framework and formal characterization of conditions guaranteeing FPRAS.  
- Major concern: the derived weight conditions (ensuring rapid MCMC convergence) appear difficult to satisfy in practice, limiting applicability.  
- Suggests authors consider methods to enforce convergence conditions (e.g., additional regularization), while noting this may cause over-regularization and hurt performance.  
- Recommends exploring the interaction between convergence-focused regularization and existing techniques like ridge regression.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution that establishes groundwork, but substantial further work is needed to make the results practical and deployable.
