- Introduces a novel, computationally efficient method for MLE in exponential families by assuming a fast-mixing MCMC sampler and approximating integrals for proximal gradient descent.  
- Builds a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE using the framework of Schmidt et al., with analyses for convex and strongly convex cases.  
- Provides guarantees on likelihood error (convex case) and parameter error (strongly convex case) and includes a simple experiment demonstrating the theory.  
- Well-motivated and well-executed work that offers an appealing alternative to restrictive assumptions like small treewidth.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid, synthesizing MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and existing optimization results.  
- Honest and thorough discussion of limitations, pessimism in theoretical assumptions, and clear directions for future research.  
- Offers useful insight into algorithms related to contrastive divergence, though the analyzed method is not strictly CD.  
- Technical presentation needs clarity improvements, notably an explicit statement/proof that the log-likelihood is convex (Hessian = covariance of sufficient statistics).  
- Paper should consolidate and state all assumptions clearly (e.g., convexity of the parameter set Theta).  
- Practical impact is limited because the fast-mixing MCMC assumption is difficult to prove for most interesting models, effectively swapping one hard problem for another.  
- Minor editorial issues and typos need correction (specific line corrections listed; sentence at l311-12 appears incomplete).
