The primary weakness of this manuscript is its limited engagement with the relevant literature. The proposed algorithm is strongly related to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm and, more broadly, to the class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics that have been developed for optimizing intractable likelihood functions. Given the well-developed literature on this topic, the authors should compare their method to existing work to properly situate their contribution.

A significant portion of the theoretical analysis presented here already exists in a more general form. For example, established convergence results for similar algorithms do not require the Markov chains to be uniformly ergodic. Furthermore, the authors' strategy of reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution *r* at each iteration, which they acknowledge is done to simplify the analysis, is an unnecessarily restrictive assumption. Prior work has extensively analyzed algorithms that do not require this step. The authors themselves note that initializing with the previous sample may be more effective, a strategy that has also been thoroughly studied in the literature.

The presentation of the results is also suboptimal. For instance, the assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated within the theorems themselves, forcing the reader to piece them together from the surrounding text. Additionally, the strong assumptions used to derive results such as those in Theorem 6 appear unnecessary, as more general results are available under weaker conditions (see, for example, Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret).

In summary, due to a lack of awareness of prior work, the contributions of this manuscript appear to be of only marginal novelty.
