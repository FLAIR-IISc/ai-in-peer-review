- Limited engagement with prior work: the manuscript fails to position itself relative to Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation methods with Markovian dynamics.  
- Existing theory already covers several of the manuscript’s results and does so in more general settings.  
- Uniform ergodicity is not required in standard analyses; the authors’ reliance on it is unnecessary.  
- Reinitializing the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r each iteration is not required and the stated rationale (simplifying analysis) is insufficient.  
- Warm-starting (reinitializing from the previous sample) is often preferable and has been extensively analyzed in the literature.  
- Presentation issues: Theorems 1 and 2 do not state precise assumptions within the theorem statements and should be self-contained.  
- Unnecessarily strong assumptions (e.g., in Theorem 6) should be relaxed; more general results exist (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret).  
- Overall novelty is marginal due to lack of awareness of key prior work; authors should review the literature, clarify differences/extensions, relax assumptions, and make assumptions explicit in theorem statements.
