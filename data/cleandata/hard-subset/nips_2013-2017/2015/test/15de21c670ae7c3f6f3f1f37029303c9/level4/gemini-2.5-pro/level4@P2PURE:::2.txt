### Review

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel method for computationally efficient maximum likelihood estimation in exponential families, where finding the exact MLE is generally intractable. The primary difficulty from a convex optimization standpoint is the calculation of an integral with respect to the current exponential family parameter. The authors address this by assuming that an MCMC sampler is fast-mixing for all parameters in the domain. Under this assumption, they demonstrate that the integrals required for proximal gradient descent can be approximated with sufficient precision. By combining this approach with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), they establish a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE. The analysis covers both the convex and strongly convex cases, providing guarantees on the likelihood error and parameter error, respectively. A simple experiment is included to demonstrate the theoretical results.

**Overall Assessment and Strengths**

This is a high-quality and well-executed paper. The approach is well-motivated, presenting a valuable alternative to methods that rely on restrictive structural assumptions, such as small treewidth. The theoretical analysis appears solid, although the proofs were not checked in detail. The paper's contribution lies in a non-trivial combination of existing techniques, namely the MCMC fast-mixing assumption, concentration inequalities, and the optimization results of Schmidt et al.

A significant strength of the paper is its transparent discussion of the approach's limitations; the conclusion provides a thoughtful analysis of weaknesses, overly pessimistic assumptions, and promising directions for future work. The work also offers useful insights into algorithms related to Contrastive Divergence.

**Critique and Suggestions for Improvement**

While the introduction is well-written with a thorough review of previous work, the clarity of the technical presentation could be improved in a few areas.

First, the paper never explicitly states *why* the negative log-likelihood is convex. Since the entire optimization framework is premised on this fact, a brief explanation—noting that the Hessian of the log-partition function A(theta) is the covariance matrix of the sufficient statistics and therefore positive semi-definite—would significantly guide the reader.

Second, the paper would benefit from a consolidated summary of its assumptions. For instance, the requirement that the parameter set Theta be convex is a critical assumption that does not appear to be mentioned in the main text.

**Critique of Practical Value**

Despite being an original and substantial contribution, the practical applicability of the proposed method appears limited. The core assumption of a fast-mixing MCMC sampler is extremely challenging to verify in practice for complex models. In essence, the paper trades one difficult problem (directly computing the MLE) for another (proving the existence of a fast-mixing Markov chain). Nevertheless, this reduction is a valuable theoretical contribution in its own right, and making these results available to the community is a worthwhile endeavor.
