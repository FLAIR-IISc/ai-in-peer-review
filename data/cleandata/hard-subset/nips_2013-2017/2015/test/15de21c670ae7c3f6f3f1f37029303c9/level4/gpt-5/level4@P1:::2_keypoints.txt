- Proposes restricting parameters to a fast-mixing set Θ and using MCMC-approximated projected gradient descent to obtain computationally efficient MLE in exponential families.  
- Proves FPRAS results: unregularized case achieves ε-accuracy in log-likelihood with total effort O(1/ε^3) (up to logs); ridge-regularized case achieves ε-accuracy in parameter distance with effort O(1/ε^2).  
- Analysis combines mixing-time bounds, concentration inequalities (Efron–Stein, Bernstein, Hoeffding), and inexact projected gradient methods (Schmidt et al.).  
- Results are general to arbitrary exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, not limited to specific graphical models.  
- Provides explicit schedules for iterations (K), samples (M), and mixing steps (v), and includes a small Ising-model experiment to illustrate theory.  
- Positions the work as an alternative to treewidth-based tractability and to surrogates (pseudolikelihood, score matching), and connects to MCMC-based ML and fast-mixing restart literature.  
- Strength: clear, compelling motivation to replace structural tractability with algorithmic tractability (fast mixing).  
- Strength: solid theoretical development with transparent assumptions and explicit complexity bounds.  
- Strength: useful discussion of limitations and relation to contrastive-divergence–type algorithms.  
- Weakness: paper does not explicitly state early that f(θ) is convex (A(θ) convex because Hessian = Cov(t(X))) — this should be made explicit.  
- Weakness: assumption that Θ is convex (needed for projection and mixing guarantees) is not clearly stated early and should be emphasized.  
- Weakness: presentation could be tightened—Theorem 1 (Lipschitz gradient) should be highlighted earlier and mixing assumptions should separate algorithm-agnostic vs chain-specific details.  
- Weakness: practical guidance needed—constants (e.g., L = 4R2^2 + λ) and resulting schedules may be pessimistic, as seen in the example.  
- Weakness: analysis assumes independent samples per iteration and does not cover common heuristics like sample-pooling or CD with hidden units.  
- Quality: technically sound under its assumptions, with careful use of concentration and mixing bounds and clear separation of convex vs strongly convex regimes.  
- Originality: novel synthesis yielding an FPRAS for MLE within fast-mixing parameter sets; the “algorithmic tractability via fast mixing” perspective is fresh.  
- Significance: limited practical impact due to difficulty certifying fast mixing and computing projections, but formalizes a useful trade-off that may guide design of regularizers/constraints.  
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually novel, general theoretical results with explicit bounds, transparent limitations, and community relevance.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited practical applicability without verifiable fast-mixing/projection methods, potentially loose constants, and omission of common practical heuristics.  
- Minor comments/typos: multiple small typos (l185, l192, l207, l281) and an unclear sentence at l311–312; recommend stating convexity of f(θ) and Θ early.
