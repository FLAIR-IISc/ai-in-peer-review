- Weight learning in Markov networks is a key challenge; exact gradient computation is NP-hard, so MCMC is the practical alternative.  
- Main theoretical result: if weights ensure rapid MCMC convergence, MCMC-based weight learning achieves a FPRAS.  
- Separate theoretical bounds are derived for the unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) settings.  
- Paper is generally well-written, with only minor typos.  
- Proofs appear plausible to the reviewer, though not all were verified.  
- Theoretical results are impressive but of limited practical relevance (acknowledged by the authors).  
- The parameter constraints required by the theorems are virtually impossible to guarantee in practice.  
- Reviewer asks if the authors considered enforcing those constraints artificially, noting they resemble regularization.  
- Combining enforced constraints with ridge regression risks over-regularization.  
- Resolving the practical enforceability of the constraints would significantly strengthen the paper.  
- Overall: clear formalization of conditions for MCMC-based FPRAS, but the conditions currently have little practical utility and much further work is needed.
