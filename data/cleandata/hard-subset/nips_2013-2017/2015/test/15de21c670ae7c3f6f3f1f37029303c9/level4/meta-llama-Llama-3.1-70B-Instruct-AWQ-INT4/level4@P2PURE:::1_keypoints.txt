- Manuscript shows poor engagement with existing literature on related algorithms.
- Proposed algorithm closely resembles Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.
- Authors should compare their method to a well-established body of prior work on optimizing intractable likelihoods.
- A broader theoretical framework already exists that covers many of the authors’ results.
- Existing theory handles more general cases (e.g., non-uniformly ergodic Markov chains) without the authors’ restrictions.
- Reinitializing the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r each iteration is unjustified and unnecessary.
- Reinitializing with the previous sample is likely preferable and has been extensively analyzed in prior work.
- Presentation is unclear: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not stated cleanly and force readers to search the text.
- Strong assumptions used for results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) exist.
- Overall contribution appears only marginally novel due to lack of awareness and comparison with relevant prior work.
