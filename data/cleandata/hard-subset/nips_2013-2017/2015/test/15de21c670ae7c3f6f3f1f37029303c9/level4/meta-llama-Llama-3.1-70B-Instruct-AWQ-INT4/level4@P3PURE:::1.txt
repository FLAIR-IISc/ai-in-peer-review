I think the main issue with the manuscript is a lack of awareness of the existing literature on the topic. The authors' algorithm bears a strong resemblance to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, and more broadly, to the general class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics that have been proposed in various forms to optimize intractable likelihood functions. A well-developed body of literature exists on this topic, and the authors should compare their work to existing research. In fact, a theory that encompasses some of the authors' results already exists and covers much more general scenarios. For instance, Markov chains are not required to be uniformly ergodic, and there is no need to reinitialize the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r at each iteration. While the authors acknowledge that they reinitialize the chain to simplify their analysis, this justification is insufficient. Furthermore, reinitializing with the previous sample, as discussed at the end of the manuscript, may be a better approach, and these algorithms have been extensively analyzed previously. 

The presentation of the results is also suboptimal. For example, the assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are unclear. Readers should not have to refer to the preceding and following text to understand these assumptions. Additionally, the strong assumptions used to develop results such as those in Theorem 6 are unnecessary. For instance, the work of Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret presents more general results. Overall, I believe that the authors lack awareness of the literature, and their results are only marginally novel.
