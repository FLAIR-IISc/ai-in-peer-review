- Establishes meaningful convergence rates for learning algorithms in the "fast mixing" model class, using mixing times as a central metric.  
- Upper bounds are well-motivated and technically sound, providing a strong theoretical foundation.  
- Theorem 7 (the lower bound) is difficult to interpret; presentation and implications are unclear.  
- Authors should substantially expand discussion of Theorem 7 and relate it explicitly to existing MCMC-based lower bounds.  
- Paper is well-structured and generally clear; technical concepts and argument flow are coherent.  
- Minor presentation refinements (especially for Theorem 7) would enhance clarity.  
- Introduces a novel "fast mixing" framing that makes learning intractable models tractable via MCMC.  
- Convergence-rate results are original and represent a significant contribution absent from prior work.  
- Addresses an important literature gap: theoretical guarantees when exact inference is intractable but MCMC approximations are feasible.  
- Should more explicitly discuss broader impact and concrete future directions (e.g., non-stationary settings, deep generative models, active learning).  
- Recommend elaborating on how to bridge theoretical guarantees with practical MCMC implementations.  
- Overall: compelling, novel, and technically strong contribution; recommend acceptance pending clarifications on the lower bound and significance discussion.
