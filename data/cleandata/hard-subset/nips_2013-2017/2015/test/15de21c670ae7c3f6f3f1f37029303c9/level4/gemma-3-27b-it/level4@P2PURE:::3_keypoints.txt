- Problem context: Weight estimation in Markov networks relies on gradient descent, but exact gradient computation is NP-hard so MCMC is used to approximate gradients.  
- Main contribution: Theoretical results showing that when weights satisfy conditions ensuring rapid MCMC mixing, MCMC-based weight learning admits an FPRAS.  
- Analysis: Two cases are treated separately—an unregularized (convex) case and a ridge-regularized (non-convex) case—with different derived bounds.  
- Writing and proofs: Paper is generally well-written with minor typos; proofs appear logically sound though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Practicality concern: The required weight conditions for the theoretical guarantees are unlikely to hold in practice, limiting real-world relevance (an issue acknowledged by the authors).  
- Suggestion: Authors should consider methods to enforce the mixing condition artificially—this resembles a regularization term but may cause over-regularization if combined with ridge.  
- Overall verdict: The paper provides a formal characterization of when MCMC-based learning yields FPRAS guarantees, but substantial further work is needed to make the results practically applicable.
