- Studies maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models by restricting parameters to a “fast-mixing” set where MCMC converges quickly.  
- Proposes framing tractability via MCMC mixing properties as an alternative to tree-structured parameter restrictions.  
- Main theoretical result: gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients approximates MLE within the fast-mixing set with high probability for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Provides fully-polynomial time randomized approximation schemes: cubic in 1/ε for unregularized cases and quadratic for ridge-regularized cases.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and presented with appropriate mathematical rigor.  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and notation is introduced carefully.  
- Authors situate the work well relative to prior literature, including pseudolikelihood, composite likelihoods, and tractable parameter sets.  
- The framing and theoretical results are original and offer a novel perspective on learning in intractable models.  
- The significance is promising, addressing a gap in theoretical guarantees for MCMC-based learning; practical impact and future directions need more elaboration.  
- Minor concern: Theorem 7’s lower bound is difficult to interpret and requires expanded discussion and context.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel tractability framing, strong convergence guarantees, polynomial approximation schemes, fills a literature gap.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear interpretation of the lower bound (Theorem 7) and insufficient elaboration on practical impact and future work.
