- Weight learning in Markov networks requires gradient computation, which is NP-hard, so MCMC is commonly used as an approximation.
- The paper shows that if parameters yield rapid mixing of MCMC, then MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.
- Separate theoretical bounds are derived for the unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases.
- The manuscript is generally well-written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Theoretical results are impressive but have limited practical relevance because the required parameter constraints are unlikely to hold in practice.
- The reviewer suggests exploring ways to enforce the rapid-mixing condition artificially, effectively as a form of regularization.
- There is concern that combining the rapid-mixing constraint with ridge regularization could lead to over-regularized models.
- If enforcement of the condition can be addressed, the theoretical results could form the basis of a strong contribution.
- Overall, the paper formalizes when MCMC yields an FPRAS for weight learning, but non-trivial additional research is needed to make it practically useful.
