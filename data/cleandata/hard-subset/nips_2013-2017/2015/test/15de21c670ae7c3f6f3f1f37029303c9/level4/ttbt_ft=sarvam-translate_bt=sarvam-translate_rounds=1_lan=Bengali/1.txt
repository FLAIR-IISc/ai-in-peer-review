I think the main problem with the manuscript is the lack of literature on this subject. The authors' algorithm is strongly related to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm (and indeed to the general class of stochastic approximation algorithms, including Markov dynamic, which has been proposed in various forms to optimize complex probability functions). There is a well-developed literature on this subject, and the authors should compare what they are doing with existing work. A theory that includes some of the authors' results already exists and covers many more common situations. For example, Markov chains do not need to be ergodic uniformly, and it is not necessary to restart the Markov chain in a specific distribution r at each iteration (the authors acknowledge that they do this to simplify their analysis, but this is not enough - in addition, restarting with a previous sample might be a good idea, as discussed at the end of the manuscript, and these algorithms have been widely analyzed before). The presentation of the results is far from optimal: for example, it is not clear what the assumptions are for theorems 1 and 2? One can mention the before and after text, but the reader does not need to do that. Also, note that there is no need for strong assumptions used to develop results like theorem 6 (see for more general results, for example, Benvenisti, Mateveira, and Prioret). I think there is a lack of literature by the authors, and the results are only marginally new.
