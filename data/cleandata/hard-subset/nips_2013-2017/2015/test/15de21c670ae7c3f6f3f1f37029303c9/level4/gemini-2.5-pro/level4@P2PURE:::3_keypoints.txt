- Problem context: weight learning in Markov networks uses gradient-based optimization but gradient computation is generally NP-hard, so MCMC is used to approximate it.
- Main contribution: proves that if model parameters ensure rapid MCMC mixing, then gradient-based learning is an FPRAS.
- Analysis scope: provides separate theoretical performance bounds for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized case.
- Writing and proofs: paper is well-written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not all were verified in detail.
- Practical limitation: the rapid-mixing parameter condition required by the theorems is essentially impossible to guarantee in practice.
- Authors’ acknowledgement: the paper candidly recognizes the limited practical relevance of the central condition.
- Suggested direction: consider algorithmically enforcing rapid-mixing (e.g., as a regularizer), but this risks over-regularization and interaction issues with existing methods like ridge regression.
- Overall assessment: formally strong theoretical result that establishes when MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS, but its practical applicability is currently weak and bridging that gap is a non-trivial open problem.
