- Establishes convergence-rate guarantees for ML learning in exponential families with parameters constrained to a fast-mixing set, using MCMC-approximated gradients; bounds in terms of mixing times are meaningful and useful.
- Main concern: Theorem 7’s lower bound is hard to interpret in practice and needs more extensive discussion.
- Request for intuition on when the lower bound is tight, comparison to upper bounds in typical regimes, and guidance on constant factors.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized; assumptions, definitions, projection, and algorithmic setup are presented cleanly.
- Proof sketches provide good intuition for the main results.
- Original formulation: learning within a “fast-mixing” parameter class is novel and moves beyond treewidth-based tractability.
- Technical contributions: original convergence guarantees with explicit schedules for iterations, samples, and chain lengths.
- Significance: fills a gap in learning intractable models with MCMC-based gradients, but practical impact is unclear.
- Suggests more discussion of which model families and mixing-time bounds make guarantees useful, trade-offs between projection and sampling cost, and possible extensions (alternative chains, better projections, reuse of samples).
- Overall: novel, interesting results and recommended for acceptance.
