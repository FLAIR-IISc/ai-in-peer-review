- Introduces learning under "fast-mixing parameter sets" instead of tractable structural families like trees.  
- Shows gradient descent with MCMC-based gradient approximations can converge efficiently when parameters ensure fast MCMC mixing.  
- Extends results to general exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, giving an FPRAS for learning in constrained parameter sets.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.  
- Derives computational complexity scaling as O(1/ε³ log(1/ε)) for convex and O(1/ε² log(1/ε)) for strongly convex settings.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound with careful treatment of mixing times, gradient approximation errors, and convergence rates.  
- Gives explicit parameter schedules (Theorems 4–5 and 8–9) that offer practical guidance.  
- Empirical demonstration via an Ising model shows theoretical bounds are conservative but reasonable in practice.  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and mathematically precise with consistent notation and structured proofs.  
- Lower bounds (Theorem 7) are hard to interpret; paper needs more intuitive discussion of their importance and relation to upper bounds.  
- Would benefit from more explicit discussion of potential applications and extensions (e.g., contrastive divergence variants, latent-variable models).  
- Original and significant: reframing tractability by mixing times is novel and complements prior work; overall recommendation is acceptance.
