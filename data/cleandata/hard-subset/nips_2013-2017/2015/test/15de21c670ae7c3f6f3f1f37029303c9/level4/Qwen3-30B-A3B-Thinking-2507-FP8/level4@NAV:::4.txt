

Comments on Quality:
The results provide substantive rates of convergence for learning algorithms measured via mixing times, with bounds that are meaningful and applicable.  
The primary concern is the limited interpretability of the lower bound (Theorem 7). A more detailed exposition of this theorem and a broader discussion of relevant lower bounds for the problem would be beneficial.  

Comments on Clarity:  
The presentation is clear and well-structured.  

Comments on Originality:  
The problem formulation—centered on the "fast mixing" model class—and the theoretical contributions demonstrate clear novelty.  

Comments on Significance:  
The significance requires clarification. While the work addresses a gap in learning intractable models via MCMC, its broader impact remains underdeveloped. The submission would be strengthened by a more explicit discussion of potential applications, future extensions, and opportunities for building upon these results. Despite this, the paper introduces a novel tractable model class based on efficient MCMC-based approximation of expectations, with theoretically interesting and novel convergence rate guarantees. The work merits acceptance.
