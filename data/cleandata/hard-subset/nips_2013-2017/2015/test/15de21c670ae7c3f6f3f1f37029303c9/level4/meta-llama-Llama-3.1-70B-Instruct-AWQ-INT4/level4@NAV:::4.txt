Comments on Quality:
The paper provides convergence rates of learning algorithms, framed in terms of mixing times, yielding meaningful and useful bounds. However, the lower bound presented in Theorem 7 is somewhat challenging to understand. A more detailed explanation of this theorem, along with a broader discussion of applicable lower bounds, would enhance the overall quality of the paper.

Comments on Clarity:
The presentation of the paper is well-organized and easy to follow.

Comments on Originality:
The formulation of the problem within the "fast mixing" model class, as well as the theoretical results, appear to be novel and innovative.

Comments on Significance:
While the significance of the paper is not immediately apparent, it does seem to fill a gap in the existing literature on learning intractable models using MCMC. To further strengthen the submission, it would be beneficial to include a more in-depth discussion of the work's potential impact, including potential future extensions and opportunities for building upon this research. The introduction of a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations, along with the novel theoretical results on convergence rates for learning these models, makes this a compelling submission that warrants acceptance.
