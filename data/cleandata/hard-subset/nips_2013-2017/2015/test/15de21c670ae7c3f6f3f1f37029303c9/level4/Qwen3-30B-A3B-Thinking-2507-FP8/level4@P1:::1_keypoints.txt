- Proposes restricting parameters to "fast-mixing sets" for MCMC-based maximum likelihood in exponential families.  
- Shows gradient descent with MCMC approximations yields an FPRAS for convex and strongly convex cases.  
- Technical results are mathematically sound.  
- Fails to engage adequately with extensive literature on stochastic approximation and Monte Carlo EM for intractable likelihoods.  
- Approach is fundamentally related to existing Monte Carlo EM and Markovian stochastic approximation methods but lacks explicit comparison.  
- Unnecessary assumption that Markov chains be reinitialized each iteration; reusing previous samples (contrastive divergence) is common and often more efficient.  
- No meaningful comparison to prior analyses of MCMC-based learning algorithms.  
- Presentation is weak: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated in the main text.  
- Theorem 6 relies on strong, possibly unjustified mixing-rate assumptions while more general results (e.g., Benveniste et al.) exist.  
- Claims of novelty are overstated; working in the exponential family is standard and contributions are marginal relative to existing theory.  
- Authors do not demonstrate necessity of their assumptions or practical advantages of their method.  
- Example section and practical contribution are unconvincing; overall lack of contextualization undermines the paper's significance.
