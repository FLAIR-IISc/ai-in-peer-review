

Quality: The paper establishes meaningful convergence rates for learning algorithms within the "fast mixing" model class, using mixing times as a key metric. The upper bounds are well-motivated and technically sound, providing a valuable theoretical foundation. However, Theorem 7 (the lower bound) presents significant interpretational challenges. The current presentation does not sufficiently clarify the theorem's implications or its relationship to existing lower bounds in the literature. The authors should substantially expand the discussion of Theorem 7, explicitly connecting it to the broader context of lower bounds for MCMC-based learning problems. This would greatly strengthen the paper’s theoretical contribution.

Clarity: The paper is well-structured and generally clear in its exposition. Technical concepts are introduced logically, and the flow of arguments is coherent. Minor refinements to the presentation of Theorem 7 (as noted above) would enhance overall clarity.

Originality: The paper introduces a novel problem framing centered on the "fast mixing" model class, which provides a tractable framework for learning intractable models using MCMC. The theoretical results—particularly the convergence rates for learning algorithms within this class—are original and represent a significant contribution to the literature on statistical learning with MCMC. This framing and its associated results are not present in prior work.

Significance: The paper addresses a clear and important gap in the literature: the lack of theoretical guarantees for learning models where exact inference is intractable, yet MCMC-based approximation of expectations is feasible. While the significance of this gap is implied, the paper would benefit from a more explicit discussion of its broader impact. Specifically, the authors should elaborate on: (1) how this framework enables practical learning in complex models (e.g., Bayesian networks, graphical models with high treewidth) where standard methods fail; (2) concrete future directions, such as extensions to non-stationary settings, applications to deep generative models, or connections to active learning; and (3) the potential to bridge theoretical learning guarantees with practical MCMC implementations. Articulating these points would solidify the paper’s importance and demonstrate its potential to influence both theory and practice.

Overall, the paper makes a compelling theoretical contribution by establishing a new class of tractable models and providing rigorous learning guarantees for it. The core results are novel, technically strong, and address a relevant problem in modern machine learning. While the current discussion of significance and the interpretation of the lower bound require strengthening, these are fixable issues. The work clearly advances the state of the art in learning with MCMC and provides a foundation for future research. For these reasons, and given the paper’s technical merits, I recommend acceptance.
