- Proposes an efficient MLE method for exponential families by assuming an MCMC sampler is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters.  
- Shows integrals needed for proximal gradient descent can be approximated accurately under this assumption, yielding an FPRAS for the MLE.  
- Gives guarantees in both general convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) settings.  
- Technical contribution synthesizes fast-mixing MCMC, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al.'s optimization analysis.  
- Includes a simple illustrative experiment supporting the theoretical results.  
- Reviewer finds the theoretical analysis high-quality and seemingly solid, though proofs were not independently verified.  
- Paper candidly discusses limitations, noting it replaces one hard problem (MLE computation) with another (proving fast mixing), limiting immediate practicality.  
- Provides useful insights into CD-style algorithms despite not analyzing contrastive divergence directly.  
- Presentation could be clearer; notably, it omits an explicit justification that the negative log-likelihood is convex.  
- Should consolidate and explicitly state all assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ) in one place.  
- Several minor typographical and grammatical errors identified for correction.
