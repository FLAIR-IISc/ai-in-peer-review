- Paper proposes an efficient MLE method for exponential families by approximating intractable integrals using a fast-mixing MCMC sampler.  
- Assumes an MCMC sampler is fast-mixing for all parameters in the domain and uses this to approximate integrals for proximal gradient descent.  
- Combines MCMC approximation with Schmidt et al. (2011) to establish an FPRAS for the MLE.  
- Analysis provides likelihood error guarantees in the convex case and parameter error guarantees in the strongly convex case.  
- Includes a simple experiment demonstrating the theoretical results.  
- Strength: well-motivated, high-quality, and a valuable alternative to structurally restrictive methods (e.g., small treewidth).  
- Strength: solid theoretical analysis that non-trivially combines fast-mixing MCMC, concentration inequalities, and existing optimization results.  
- Strength: transparent discussion of limitations and connections/insights to Contrastive Divergence.  
- Suggestion: explicitly state why the negative log-likelihood is convex (Hessian of A(theta) is covariance of sufficient statistics → PSD).  
- Suggestion: provide a consolidated summary of assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Theta is critical but not stated in main text).  
- Practical critique: core fast-mixing MCMC assumption is hard to verify in practice, limiting practical applicability and effectively trading one hard problem for another.
