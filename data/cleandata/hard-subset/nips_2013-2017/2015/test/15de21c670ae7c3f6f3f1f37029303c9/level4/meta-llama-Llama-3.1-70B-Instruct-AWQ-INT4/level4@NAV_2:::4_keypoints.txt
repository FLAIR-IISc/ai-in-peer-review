- Introduces "fast-mixing parameters" enabling MCMC to rapidly converge for high-treewidth undirected graphical models.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of convergence rates for learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, giving upper and lower bounds.  
- Establishes a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme for maximum likelihood learning in exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Analyzes gradient descent with MCMC and quantifies trade-offs among iterations, samples, and chain transitions (Theorems 2, 4, 6, 8).  
- Presentation is clear, notation is well-defined, and Figure 1 effectively illustrates the algorithm.  
- Novel framing: defines a tractable model class via fast-mixing and uses it to derive learning convergence guarantees.  
- Significance is somewhat unclear; the work fills a gap on learning intractable models with MCMC but broader impact needs clarification.  
- Theorem 7 (lower bound) is difficult to interpret and requires more detailed discussion.  
- Authors should elaborate on potential impact, future extensions, and opportunities to build on the results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, thorough convergence analysis, strong technical merit, fills a literature gap.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear significance, opaque lower bound, and insufficient discussion of implications and extensions.
