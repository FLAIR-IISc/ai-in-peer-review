The primary weakness of this manuscript is its limited engagement with the extensive existing literature on stochastic approximation and Monte Carlo methods for optimizing intractable likelihood functions.

The proposed algorithm is closely related to, and could be considered a variant of, the Monte Carlo EM (MCEM) algorithm. More broadly, it falls into the well-established class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics. A significant body of work has been developed on this topic, and the authors should provide a thorough comparison of their method to existing approaches to clearly delineate their novel contributions.

Similarly, the theoretical analysis overlooks a well-developed body of theory that already covers the results presented here, often in much more general settings. For example:
1.  Existing convergence analyses for this class of algorithms do not typically require the strong assumption that the Markov chains are uniformly ergodic.
2.  The requirement to re-initialize the Markov chain from a fixed distribution `r` at each iteration is a significant practical and theoretical limitation. While the authors acknowledge this is a simplifying choice for their analysis, it is an outdated one. More efficient and practical strategies, such as "warm-starting" the chain using the sample from the previous iteration, have been proposed and extensively analyzed in prior literature.

The presentation of the theoretical results also needs substantial improvement. For key results such as Theorems 1 and 2, the underlying assumptions are not explicitly stated within the theorems themselves. While these conditions may be found in the surrounding text, for the sake of clarity and rigor, each theorem or proposition should be a self-contained statement. The reader should not have to search for the required assumptions.

Furthermore, some of the assumptions used in the analysis appear to be unnecessarily strong. For instance, results similar to those in Theorem 6 have been established under much weaker and more general conditions in seminal works on stochastic approximation (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret, "Adaptive Algorithms and Stochastic Approximations"). Relying on less restrictive assumptions would significantly strengthen the paper's theoretical contribution.

In summary, due to the lack of connection to extensive prior work, the novelty of both the algorithm and its theoretical analysis appears to be marginal. The manuscript would need a major revision to properly situate the work within the existing literature and to demonstrate a more substantial and novel contribution.
