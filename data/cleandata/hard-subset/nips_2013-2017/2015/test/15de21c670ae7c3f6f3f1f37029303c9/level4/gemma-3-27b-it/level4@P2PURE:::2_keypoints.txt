- Introduces a novel, computationally efficient approach to maximum likelihood learning in exponential families using an MCMC rapid-mixing assumption.  
- Shows integrals needed for proximal gradient descent can be accurately approximated under rapid mixing, yielding a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Provides separate theoretical guarantees for convex (likelihood error bounds) and strongly convex (parameter error bounds) cases.  
- Analysis combines MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and results from Schmidt et al. (2011), representing a nontrivial synthesis of existing techniques.  
- Includes a basic experiment that validates the theoretical findings.  
- Well-justified alternative to small-treewidth methods and offers insight relevant to contrastive-divergence–type algorithms, despite not analyzing CD directly.  
- Paper is overall high-quality with a clear introduction and comprehensive literature references.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, potential overstringency of assumptions, and directions for future research.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast MCMC mixing for relevant models is difficult, effectively trading one hard problem for another.  
- Missing explicit statements of key assumptions and properties—e.g., explicit convexity of the log-likelihood f(θ) and convexity of the parameter set Θ—which harms clarity.  
- Minor technical/typo issues throughout (specific line edits suggested: l185, l192, l207, l281, l311-12).  
- Overall an original and substantial contribution whose practical applicability depends on future work establishing fast-mixing chains for target models.
