- Weight learning in Markov networks is hard; gradient-descent requires computing gradients which is NP-hard in general.  
- MCMC is commonly used as a substitute to approximate the gradient.  
- The paper proves that if weights ensure rapid MCMC mixing, MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.  
- The authors treat both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases, deriving separate bounds.  
- The manuscript is well-written with only minor typographical errors.  
- Proofs appear plausible, though the reviewer could not fully verify their correctness.  
- Theoretical results are impressive but have limited practical relevance, as the authors acknowledge.  
- The feasibility of ensuring the parameter constraints required by the theorems is questionable.  
- The reviewer suggests exploring ways to enforce those conditions (e.g., additional regularization) but warns of potential over-regularization.  
- If the enforceability issue is addressed, the results could form the basis of a strong, practical paper.
