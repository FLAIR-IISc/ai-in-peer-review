**Summary and Technical Soundness**

The paper proposes a novel approach to computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families. By assuming that Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters, the authors are able to show that the integrals needed for proximal gradient descent can be calculated with sufficient precision. This leads to a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for calculating the maximum likelihood estimate (MLE). The approach is well-motivated as an alternative to relying on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear solid, combining MCMC mixing assumptions with concentration inequalities and existing results.

**Originality and Impact**

The paper is an original and substantial contribution to the field. Although the results may be of limited practical value due to the challenge of proving fast mixing of MCMC, the ability to convert one problem into another is often useful. The paper provides valuable insights into contrastive divergence-type algorithms and offers a new perspective on maximum likelihood learning.

**Clarity and Presentation**

The introduction is well-written, with substantial references to previous work. However, there are some issues with the clarity of technical presentation. A clear statement on the convexity of the log-likelihood would be beneficial, as it is a crucial assumption. Additionally, a summary of the assumptions made in the paper, such as the convexity of the parameter set Θ, would be helpful.
