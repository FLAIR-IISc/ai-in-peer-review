- Introduces a novel "fast-mixing parameter set" framework enabling maximum likelihood learning when MCMC mixes rapidly.  
- Proves that gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients can approximate the ML solution with high probability under these parameter constraints.  
- Establishes convergence rates: unregularized case O(ε^-3) and ridge-regularized case O(ε^-2), yielding an FPRAS-like result.  
- Extends results to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.  
- Theoretical results are technically sound with comprehensive, well-supported proofs and clear proof sketches.  
- Main concern: Theorem 7 (lower bound) is hard to interpret and needs more discussion on implications and optimality.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation; examples (Section 6) aid understanding.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: state the relationship between convex and strongly convex convergence results earlier.  
- Original perspective reframes tractability via fast-mixing MCMC rather than treewidth, marking a novel contribution.  
- Clearly differentiates from related work (Chow-Liu, pseudolikelihood, score matching) and cites recent relevant literature.  
- Addresses an important gap by providing theoretical guarantees for MCMC-based learning in high-treewidth models.  
- Significance would be strengthened by more discussion of practical applications and construction of fast-mixing sets beyond the Ising example.  
- Needs further exploration of extensions to hidden variables and other real-world complexities.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with substantial value to the community; recommendation for acceptance.
