- Insufficient engagement with established literature on stochastic optimization for intractable likelihoods.  
- Proposed algorithm is closely related to Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation methods with Markovian dynamics.  
- Authors should explicitly compare their method to this extensive prior work.  
- A comprehensive theoretical foundation already exists that encompasses and generalizes the manuscript’s results.  
- Existing theory does not require uniformly ergodic Markov chains; reinitializing the chain at a fixed distribution r is unnecessary.  
- Reinitializing using the previous sample has been rigorously analyzed in prior work and may yield better performance.  
- Presentation needs improvement: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are unclear and require self-contained statements.  
- Strong assumptions in Theorem 6 are unjustified; Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret (and others) provide more general results.  
- Technical contributions offer only marginal novelty relative to prior literature and fail to acknowledge key developments.  
- Paper should be revised to situate the method within the theoretical landscape, clarify assumptions, and rigorously contrast with prior art.
