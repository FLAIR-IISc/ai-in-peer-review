- Proposes a novel method for computationally efficient MLE in exponential families using proximal gradient descent with MCMC-based integral estimates.  
- Assumes a fast-mixing MCMC for all admissible parameters and combines this with concentration inequalities and Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain an FPRAS.  
- Provides separate theoretical guarantees: likelihood-error bounds in the convex case and parameter-error bounds in the strongly convex case.  
- Analysis is a nontrivial synthesis of existing tools; proofs appear solid though not exhaustively checked by the reviewer.  
- Includes a simple experiment to illustrate the theoretical results.  
- Introduction is well written but fails to explicitly state that the log-likelihood f(θ) is convex, which is important for the paper’s logic.  
- Recommends adding a clear summary of all assumptions, e.g., convexity of the parameter set Θ, which is used but not stated.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is itself difficult—this trades one hard problem (MLE) for another (fast mixing).  
- Authors are praised for a frank discussion of limitations and for outlining future work; the paper also offers insight into CD-type algorithms.  
- Notes several minor typos and a nonsensical sentence (l185, l192, l207, l281, l311–12).  
- Overall assessment: original and substantial theoretical contribution, but of seemingly limited practical value.
