Summary: This paper presents a novel method for computationally efficient maximum likelihood estimation in exponential families. Exact MLE is generally intractable due to the need to compute expectations (integrals) with respect to the current parameter. Assuming the underlying MCMC sampler is fast-mixing for all admissible parameters, the authors show that the expectations required by proximal gradient descent can be approximated to sufficient accuracy. Combining this with results from Schmidt et al. (2011) yields a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE. Both the convex and strongly convex settings are analyzed, leading to different guarantees: in the convex case, bounds are provided on the likelihood error; in the strongly convex case, bounds are provided on the parameter estimation error. A simple experiment illustrates the theory.

Overall assessment: This is a high-quality and well-motivated contribution, offering an alternative to small-treewidth assumptions. The analyses appear sound (though I did not check every proof in detail). The technical core combines a fast-mixing MCMC assumption with concentration inequalities and the framework of Schmidt et al.; while composed of known ingredients, the combination is nontrivial and useful.

I especially appreciated the thoughtful discussion of limitations, the potential pessimism of some assumptions, and concrete directions for future work. The paper also sheds light on the behavior of contrastive-divergence-like methods, even though the analyzed algorithm is not exactly CD.

Clarity and presentation: The introduction is well written and well referenced, but there are a few points where clarity could be improved.

- It would help to state explicitly that the optimization objective f(θ) used in the paper is convex. While the convexity of the terms is implicit (e.g., the convexity of A(θ) via its Hessian being the covariance of the sufficient statistics, hence PSD), the entire development relies on convexity, so making this explicit early would guide the reader.

- Please provide a clear summary of all assumptions in one place. For example, the convexity of the parameter set Θ is required but is not stated in the main text.

- A brief, intuitive discussion of why fast mixing across the entire parameter set is plausible (or how it might be enforced/diagnosed in practice) would strengthen the practical interpretation of the results, even if formal guarantees remain hard.

Significance and practicality: The main limitation is the strength of the fast-mixing assumption, which is notoriously difficult to verify or guarantee in practice. In this sense, the method exchanges one hard problem (solving MLE exactly) for another (proving fast mixing of a Markov chain). Nevertheless, formalizing this reduction is valuable: it clarifies what is needed for efficient MLE and can guide algorithm design and analysis. As such, the paper makes an original and substantial theoretical contribution, even if immediate practical impact may be limited.

Overall: A novel approach to identifying conditions under which approximate MLE in exponential families is tractable. Well written and theoretically solid, though the strong fast-mixing assumption may limit practical applicability.
