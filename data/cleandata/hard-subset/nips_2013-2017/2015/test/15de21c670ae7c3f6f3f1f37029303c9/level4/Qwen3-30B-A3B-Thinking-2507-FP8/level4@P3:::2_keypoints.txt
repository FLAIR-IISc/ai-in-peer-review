- Presents a rigorous MLE learning framework for exponential families using MCMC on constrained “fast-mixing” parameter sets.  
- Core result: gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients yields an FPRAS for MLE under fast-mixing constraints.  
- Complexity bounds: cubic in 1/ε for unregularized convex case, quadratic in 1/ε for ridge-regularized strongly convex case.  
- Framework applies to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, avoiding treewidth-based limitations of prior work.  
- Methodologically ties MCMC mixing-time guarantees to learning guarantees, combining Schmidt et al. (2011) noisy-gradient analysis with mixing bounds.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound: establishes Lipschitz continuity of the gradient and rigorously bounds convergence for convex and strongly convex settings.  
- Proof strategy is robust, using mixing-time assumptions, concentration inequalities, and existing gradient-descent error bounds.  
- Minor technical omission: explicit justification of convexity for the regularized log-likelihood f(θ) (though follows from covariance PSD).  
- Important implicit assumption: the constraint set Θ must be convex and projection costs may be nontrivial for large models.  
- Section 6 example shows practical feasibility but highlights that the theoretical bounds are conservative.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized, notation consistent, with minor grammatical errors that do not impede understanding.  
- Originality: introduces “fast-mixing parameter sets” as a new tractability criterion, distinct from treewidth-based approaches.  
- Significance: primarily theoretical—formalizes when MCMC-based MLE is tractable, but verifying fast mixing remains challenging in practice.  
- Contrasts and improves over prior MCMC learning work by providing formal FPRAS guarantees where previous methods lacked them.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to state convexity of Θ explicitly and clarify the log-likelihood convexity argument.
