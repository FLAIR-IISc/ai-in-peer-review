## Review of NIPS_2015 Submission

This manuscript presents an algorithm for optimizing intractable likelihood functions. While the approach demonstrates potential, the primary weakness lies in a limited awareness of, and engagement with, the existing literature on stochastic approximation and Monte Carlo Expectation-Maximization (EM) methods. The proposed algorithm bears a strong resemblance to established techniques within this field, and a more thorough comparison to related work is crucial. 

The core methodology aligns with the broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms designed for intractable likelihood optimization, a topic with a well-developed theoretical foundation. The authors should explicitly position their work within this context, detailing how it differs from, and potentially improves upon, existing methods. Specifically, the manuscript would significantly benefit from a discussion of the relevant theoretical results that already encompass, and even generalize, aspects of the authors’ findings. 

For instance, the presented analysis relies on assumptions regarding uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains. However, a substantial body of literature relaxes this requirement, offering more general theoretical guarantees. Furthermore, the authors acknowledge the simplifying assumption of reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution *r* at each iteration, framing it as a convenience for their analysis. While understandable, this choice is not adequately justified given the availability of alternative approaches – such as reinitializing with the previous sample – that have been extensively studied and offer potential benefits, as even noted by the authors themselves. A deeper investigation of these alternatives, and a comparison to existing analyses, is warranted.

Beyond the connection to the broader literature, the presentation of the theoretical results requires improvement. The assumptions underlying Theorem 1 and Theorem 2 are not explicitly stated within the theorems themselves. While the reader *can* piece this information together by referencing surrounding text, a clear and concise statement of assumptions directly within each theorem would significantly enhance readability and understanding. Similarly, the strong assumptions required for Theorem 6 appear unnecessary; related work, such as that by Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret, demonstrates equivalent results under more general conditions. This suggests the possibility of strengthening the theoretical contributions by either relaxing these assumptions or explicitly acknowledging the broader scope of existing results.

In summary, while the manuscript presents a technically sound algorithm, its novelty is limited by the lack of a comprehensive literature review and a clear positioning of the work within the established theoretical framework. The results, as presented, appear marginally novel and would benefit substantially from a more nuanced discussion of related work and a more self-contained and rigorous presentation of the theoretical contributions. I recommend that the authors significantly expand the related work section, address the points raised regarding the reinitialization strategy, and ensure complete and clear statements of assumptions within their theorems.
