- Introduces learning MLE in exponential families by constraining parameters to fast-mixing sets so MCMC converges quickly, avoiding treewidth-based restrictions.  
- Proposes combining mixing-time results with gradient-descent analysis to enable MCMC-based gradient estimates for learning.  
- Proves that for bounded sufficient statistics and convex parameter constraints, gradient descent with MCMC yields an FPRAS for MLE.  
- Provides separate theoretical guarantees for convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (regularized) cases: likelihood error vs. parameter error.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous, leveraging mixing-time bounds, concentration inequalities, and gradient-descent error analysis.  
- Key assumptions (bounded sufficient statistics, convexity of Θ, convexity of negative log-likelihood f(θ)) are sensible but not always explicitly stated in the main text.  
- Originality: introduces a new tractability notion (fast-mixing parameter sets) instead of structural constraints like treewidth.  
- Significance: strong theoretical advance clarifying when MLE is tractable, but practical applicability is limited by difficulty of verifying fast mixing.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, technically solid, honest treatment of limitations, and useful connection to contrastive divergence.  
- Weaknesses: limited practical demonstrations, and clarity could be improved by explicitly stating convexity assumptions and projection details.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — high-quality theoretical contribution despite practical verification challenges.
