- Introduces a novel, computationally efficient MLE method for exponential families by approximating intractable integrals using fast-mixing MCMC.  
- Approximates required expectations to enable proximal gradient descent, yielding a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (building on Schmidt et al. 2011).  
- Provides separate analyses for convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood error guarantees (convex) and parameter error guarantees (strongly convex).  
- Supports theoretical claims with a simple experimental validation.  
- Technique avoids restrictive small-treewidth assumptions, offering a valuable alternative.  
- Theoretical analysis appears rigorous and leverages a non-trivial combination of MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and existing results.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations and acknowledge potentially pessimistic assumptions and practical challenges.  
- Connects to contrastive divergence (CD)-type algorithms, though analyzed algorithm differs from standard CD.  
- Practical impact is limited because demonstrating fast-mixing behavior for MCMC across parameter values is difficult.  
- Effectively reduces one hard problem (MLE computation) to another (finding fast-mixing Markov chains), which is still valuable.  
- Writing: introduction is clear and well-cited, but the technical presentation needs minor clarity improvements.  
- Recommend explicitly stating convexity of the log-likelihood and of A(θ) (Hessian = covariance of sufficient statistics) to support the analysis.  
- Suggest a consolidated summary of key assumptions (e.g., explicitly state Θ is convex).  
- Minor typos and wording issues to fix: l185, l192, l207, l281, and revise the sentence at l311–312.
