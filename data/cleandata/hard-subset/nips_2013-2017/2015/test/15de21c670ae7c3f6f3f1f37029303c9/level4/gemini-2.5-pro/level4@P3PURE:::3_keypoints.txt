- Addresses weight learning in Markov networks where exact gradient computation is NP-hard, so MCMC approximations are used.
- Main contribution: theoretical results showing that if model weights ensure rapid MCMC mixing, then MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.
- Provides separate performance bounds for the unregularized (convex) case and the L2 ridge-regularized (non-convex) case.
- Paper is generally well-written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Theoretical results are impressive but have limited practical relevance, a limitation the authors acknowledge.
- Major concern: the rapid-mixing parameter conditions required by the theorems are essentially impossible to guarantee or enforce in practice.
- Reviewer asks whether the authors considered enforcing rapid-mixing during learning (as a form of regularization) and notes potential interaction/over-regularization with the ridge penalty.
- If enforcing rapid-mixing during learning were solved, the theoretical results would become a strong and practical contribution.
- Overall verdict: paper formalizes when MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS but relies on an impractical condition; significant further research is needed for practical algorithms.
