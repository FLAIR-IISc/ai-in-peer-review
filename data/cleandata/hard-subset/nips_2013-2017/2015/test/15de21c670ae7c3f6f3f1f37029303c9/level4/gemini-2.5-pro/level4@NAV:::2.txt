**Summary**

This paper introduces a novel method for computationally efficient maximum likelihood estimation in exponential families, where direct computation of the MLE is generally intractable. The primary computational bottleneck from a convex optimization perspective is the need to evaluate an integral with respect to the current parameter iterate. The authors address this by assuming that a fast-mixing MCMC sampler exists for all valid parameters. This assumption allows the integrals required for proximal gradient descent to be approximated with sufficient precision. When combined with the framework of Schmidt et al. (2011), this leads to the development of a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for the MLE. The analysis considers both convex and strongly convex cases, providing guarantees on the likelihood error and parameter error, respectively. The paper includes a simple experiment that demonstrates the theory in practice.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

This is a high-quality and well-executed piece of work. The approach is well-motivated, presenting a compelling alternative to methods that rely on restrictive assumptions like small treewidth. The theoretical analyses appear to be solid, resting on a non-trivial synthesis of existing techniques, including MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and the results of Schmidt et al. While I have not checked the proofs in detail, the core theoretical argument seems sound.

I commend the authors for their honest and thorough discussion of the method's limitations. The conclusion thoughtfully analyzes the weaknesses of the approach, ways in which the theoretical assumptions might be overly pessimistic, and clear directions for future research. The paper also provides valuable insight into algorithms related to contrastive divergence, even though the analyzed method is not strictly a CD algorithm.

While the introduction is well-written and references prior work extensively, the technical presentation could be improved for clarity in a few areas.
*   A key omission is an explicit statement that the log-likelihood function is convex. Since the entire paper is built upon this property, a brief explanation—for example, noting that the Hessian of the log-partition function A(theta) is the covariance matrix of the sufficient statistics and is therefore positive semi-definite—would help guide the reader and reinforce the paper's logical foundation.
*   The paper would benefit from a consolidated summary of all its assumptions. For example, the requirement that the parameter set Theta must be convex appears to be an unstated assumption in the main text.

**Overall Assessment**

I found this paper to be an original and substantial contribution. Its immediate practical value may be limited, as the core assumption of fast MCMC mixing is extremely challenging to prove for most interesting models. In this sense, the paper effectively replaces one hard problem (finding the MLE) with another (proving the existence of a fast-mixing Markov chain). That said, the ability to convert one difficult problem into another is often a valuable theoretical step, and the results presented here are a worthwhile contribution to the community.
