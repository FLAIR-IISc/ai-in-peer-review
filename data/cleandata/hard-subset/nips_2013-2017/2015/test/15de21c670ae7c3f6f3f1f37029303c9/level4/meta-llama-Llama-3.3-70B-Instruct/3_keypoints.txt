- Paper addresses weight learning in Markov networks, focusing on computing optimal feature weights to maximize likelihood.
- Gradient computation for weight learning is NP-hard and typically approximated using MCMC.
- Main theoretical result: if weights guarantee rapid MCMC convergence, then MCMC-based gradient learning yields a FPRAS.
- Analysis treats both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases, deriving different bounds for each.
- Proofs are plausible but were not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Paper is generally well-written with only minor typos.
- Theoretical results are impressive but of limited practical applicability, as acknowledged by the authors.
- The required parameter conditions for the theorems are essentially impossible to guarantee in practice.
- Reviewer notes it is unclear whether authors attempted to artificially enforce the rapid-mixing condition (a form of regularization) or studied interactions with ridge regularization.
- Overall: solid formalization of conditions for FPRAS via MCMC, but limited real-world utility and significant open research questions remain.
