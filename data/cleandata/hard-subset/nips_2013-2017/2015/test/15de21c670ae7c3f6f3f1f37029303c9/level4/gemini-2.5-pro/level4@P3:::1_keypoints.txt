- Proposal: constrain parameters to a convex "fast-mixing" set so MCMC inference mixes rapidly and enable ML learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.

- Main contribution: rigorous analysis proving projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS under the fast-mixing assumption, with explicit non-asymptotic rates for unregularized and ridge-regularized cases.

- Strength: tackles an important practical problem by providing formal, explicit convergence guarantees for a commonly used MCMC-based learning heuristic.

- Strength: gives a concrete recipe for setting iterations, sample sizes, and MCMC lengths, and connects MCMC mixing-time theory to learning.

- Weakness: insufficient engagement with the extensive stochastic approximation (SA) literature; close relations to MCEM and classic SA results (e.g., Benveniste et al.) are not discussed.

- Weakness: analysis assumes uniform geometric ergodicity across the constraint set Θ, whereas SA literature supports convergence under weaker, non-uniform ergodicity.

- Weakness: impractical assumption of restarting MCMC from a fixed distribution at each gradient step instead of using warm starts, reducing practical relevance.

- Weakness: presentation/rigor issues — key theorems do not explicitly state all required assumptions.

- Weakness: limited novelty — results appear marginally new given prior SA work, especially given the restrictive assumptions used.

- For acceptance: important problem, rigorous non-asymptotic rates, and the fast-mixing parameter constraint is an interesting perspective.

- Against acceptance: poor connection to prior SA work, questionable novelty, and reliance on strong/inefficient assumptions.

- Conclusion: reviewer leans to reject unless authors thoroughly situate results within SA literature and justify or improve upon their restrictive assumptions.
