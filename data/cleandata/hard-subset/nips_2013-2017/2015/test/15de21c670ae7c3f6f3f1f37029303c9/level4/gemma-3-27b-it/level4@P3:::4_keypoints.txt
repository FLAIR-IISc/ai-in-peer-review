- Proposes a novel tractability criterion: restrict parameters to a “fast-mixing” set to guarantee rapid MCMC convergence, instead of tree-structured restrictions.  
- Proves a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood learning within the fast-mixing set.  
- Provides convergence-rate guarantees for gradient descent using MCMC-approximated gradients, covering both regularized and unregularized cases.  
- Analysis is rigorous, with detailed proofs (sketched in the main text and deferred to the appendix).  
- Framework is general: applies to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.  
- Includes both convex and strongly-convex analyses, adding completeness to theoretical results.  
- Clearly positions contribution relative to existing methods (pseudolikelihood, score matching) and articulates limitations of prior work.  
- Includes a simplified example that helps illustrate the practical implications of the theory.  
- Theorem 7 (lower bound on computational effort) is hard to interpret and needs more explanation and intuition relative to the upper bounds.  
- Discussion of practical impact, applications, and future directions is thin; needs more on implications for specific model classes.  
- Lacks guidance on identifying or constructing fast-mixing parameter sets and practical challenges in doing so.  
- Convergence analysis may be conservative; authors appropriately acknowledge this.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant; recommendation is acceptance pending clarification of the lower bound and expanded discussion of practical implications.
