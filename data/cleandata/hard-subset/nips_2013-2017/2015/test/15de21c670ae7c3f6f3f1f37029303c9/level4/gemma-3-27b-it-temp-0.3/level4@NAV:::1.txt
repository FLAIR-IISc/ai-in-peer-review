The primary weakness of this manuscript lies in its limited engagement with existing literature. The proposed algorithm shares strong connections with the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, and more broadly, with the established field of stochastic approximation algorithms employing Markovian dynamics for intractable likelihood optimization. A substantial body of work already exists in this area, and a thorough comparison to related methods is necessary. Notably, a theoretical framework encompassing the authors’ findings already exists and addresses a wider range of scenarios. Specifically, current theory does not require uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains, and the practice of reinitializing the chain from a fixed distribution *r* at each iteration – acknowledged by the authors as a simplification for analysis – is not essential. Indeed, reinitialization using the previous sample, as the authors themselves suggest, and algorithms employing this approach, have been extensively studied. 

The presentation of the results also requires improvement. The assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated within the theorems themselves, forcing the reader to cross-reference surrounding text. Furthermore, the strong assumptions used to derive results like those in Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results are available (e.g., Benveniste, Metivier and Priouret). 

Overall, the manuscript demonstrates a lack of awareness of relevant prior work, resulting in contributions that are only incrementally novel.
