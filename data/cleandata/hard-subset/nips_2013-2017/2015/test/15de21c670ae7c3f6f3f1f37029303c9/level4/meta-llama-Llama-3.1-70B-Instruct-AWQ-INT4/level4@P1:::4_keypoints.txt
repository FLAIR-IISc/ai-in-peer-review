- Proposes "fast-mixing parameter" sets for undirected graphical models to guarantee quick MCMC convergence.  
- Shows that restricting parameters to these sets lets gradient descent with sampling approximate the MLE with high probability for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Establishes a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for MLE under these conditions.  
- Provides comprehensive convergence-rate analysis and a lower bound on the number of Markov transitions needed for a given accuracy.  
- Introduces a novel class of tractable models based on MCMC-approximability of expectations; theoretical results are original and interesting.  
- Paper is well-presented and the arguments are thorough.  
- Theorem 7 (the lower bound) is difficult to interpret and needs expanded explanation.  
- Recommends discussing potential impact, future extensions, and practical applications.  
- Suggests adding more context on how results relate to prior work.
