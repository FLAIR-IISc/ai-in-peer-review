- Problem: Weight learning in Markov networks requires gradient computation, which is NP-hard, so MCMC is commonly used as an approximate alternative.
- Contribution: The paper proves that if MCMC mixes rapidly for the given parameters, then MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.
- Cases analyzed: Separate theoretical bounds are derived for the unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) settings.
- Writing and proofs: Paper is generally well-written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Practical relevance: The parameter conditions required by the theorems are unlikely to hold in practice, limiting applicability.
- Suggestion: The authors should explore methods to artificially enforce the rapid-mixing condition, potentially via regularization.
- Caveat: Enforcing the condition (e.g., via ridge) may lead to over-regularization and degraded models.
- Conclusion: The paper formalizes conditions under which MCMC gives an FPRAS for weight learning, but significant work remains to make the results practically useful.
