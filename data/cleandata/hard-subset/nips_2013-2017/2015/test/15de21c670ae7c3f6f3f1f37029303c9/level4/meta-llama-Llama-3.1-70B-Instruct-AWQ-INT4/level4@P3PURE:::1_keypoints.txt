- Manuscript shows poor awareness of existing literature on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.  
- The authors' algorithm closely resembles Monte Carlo EM and broader stochastic approximation methods for intractable likelihoods.  
- There exists a well-developed body of work and a theory that already encompasses and generalizes many of the authors' results.  
- Prior results do not require uniform ergodicity of Markov chains, contradicting the authors' restrictive assumptions.  
- Reinitializing the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r each iteration is unnecessary and inadequately justified.  
- Reinitializing with the previous sample is a better approach and has been extensively analyzed in prior work.  
- Presentation is suboptimal: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are unclear and should be stated self-containedly.  
- Theorem 6 relies on strong, unnecessary assumptions; more general results (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) already exist.  
- Overall, the paper lacks novelty and should compare and position itself against the extensive existing literature.
