- Paper provides convergence rates for learning algorithms expressed in terms of mixing times; derived bounds are meaningful and practically valuable.
- Main concern: interpretability of the lower bound in Theorem 7; calls for a more detailed discussion and broader lower-bound analysis.
- Presentation is clear and well-structured.
- The “fast mixing” model class framing and the theoretical results appear original.
- Significance is somewhat unclear but the work addresses a gap on learning intractable models via MCMC and is potentially significant.
- Recommends expanding discussion of the work’s potential impact, future extensions, and opportunities for follow-up.
- Introduces a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations.
- Theoretical convergence-rate results for learning these models are novel and interesting.
- Overall recommendation: acceptance.
