This paper explores a novel notion of tractability in exponential families, namely, a guarantee that Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling will quickly converge to the stationary distribution. The authors propose an algorithm for maximum likelihood learning with arbitrary treewidth via fast-mixing parameter sets. The algorithm uses MCMC to generate samples and approximate the gradient, and the authors provide theoretical guarantees for the convergence of this algorithm.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme for maximum likelihood learning in exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics. The authors show that their algorithm can achieve high-probability guarantees for the convergence of the estimated parameters to the true parameters.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, with clear explanations of the algorithm and the theoretical results. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence of their algorithm and discuss the implications of their results.

However, I have some concerns regarding the novelty of the results. The authors' algorithm is strongly related to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm and other stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics. There is a well-developed literature on these topics, and the authors should compare their work to existing results. In particular, the authors should discuss how their algorithm differs from existing methods and what advantages it offers.

Additionally, the presentation of the results could be improved. For example, the assumptions for Theorem 1 and 2 are not clearly stated, and the reader must refer to the text before and after the theorems to understand the assumptions. Furthermore, the authors could provide more context for their results and discuss the implications of their findings in more detail.
