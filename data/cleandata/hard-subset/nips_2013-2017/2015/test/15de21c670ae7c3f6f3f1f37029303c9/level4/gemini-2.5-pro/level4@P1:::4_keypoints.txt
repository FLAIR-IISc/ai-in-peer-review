- Addresses maximum likelihood learning in intractable graphical models by defining tractability via a "fast-mixing" parameter set ensuring rapid MCMC convergence.  
- Introduces a novel perspective: tractability based on MCMC mixing properties rather than graphical structure.  
- Main contribution: formal analysis proving that gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients yields an FPRAS for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics constrained to a fast-mixing set.  
- Provides explicit convergence rates and schedules for gradient steps, MCMC samples, and MCMC transitions for both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.  
- Rigorous theoretical guarantees place a common practical heuristic (MCMC-based gradient estimation) on solid footing by treating it as projected gradient descent with biased/noisy gradients.  
- Paper is technically strong, appears sound, and is clearly and well-written.  
- Weakness: could better articulate practical impact and guidance for practitioners, and outline new research directions enabled by the framework.  
- Weakness: Theorem 7’s lower bound is hard to interpret and needs expanded discussion and contextualization.  
- Overall assessment: novel, valuable theoretical contribution that fills an important gap; recommend acceptance.
