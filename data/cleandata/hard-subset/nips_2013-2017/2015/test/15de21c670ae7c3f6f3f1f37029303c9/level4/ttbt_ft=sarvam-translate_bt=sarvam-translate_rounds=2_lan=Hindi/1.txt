I think the main issue with the manuscript is the lack of awareness of the literature on this topic. The authors' algorithm strongly relates to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm (and indeed to the general class of Stochastic Approximation Algorithms, including Markov Dynamics, which has been proposed to optimize intractable probability tasks in various forms). There is well-developed literature on this subject, and the authors should compare what they are doing with existing work. A theory that includes the results of some authors already and covers much more general scenarios exists. For example, the Markov chain does not need to be equally ergodic, and it is not necessary to restart the Markov chain on a certain distribution r at each iteration (the authors admit that they do this to simplify their analysis, but this is not enough - it might be a better idea to restart with the previous sample, as discussed at the end of the manuscript, and these algorithms have been previously analyzed on a large scale). The presentation of the results is far from optimal: for example, it is not clear what assumptions are made for theorems 1 and 2? Anyone can refer to the before and after text, but the reader should not do so. Also note that there is no need for strong assumptions used to develop results like theorem 6 (see Benavisti, Mateevier, and Prioret for more general results). I think there is a lack of awareness of the literature among the authors, and the results are only marginally new.
