- Paper studies ML learning for exponential families by constraining parameters to a “fast-mixing” set Θ where MCMC mixes geometrically, and using projected gradient descent with MCMC gradients.  
- Proves an FPRAS for constrained MLE with bounded sufficient statistics, giving complexity/schedules for iterations, samples, and mixing steps.  
- Separate analyses for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases: roughly cubic in 1/ε for convex log-likelihood accuracy and quadratic in 1/ε for parameter error in the strongly convex case (up to logs).  
- Results are general to exponential families (not just graphical models) and build on mixing-time theory and inexact-gradient optimization literature.  
- Clear theoretical contribution with rigorous high-probability guarantees and careful use of concentration inequalities.  
- Strength: separates convex vs. strongly convex settings and provides explicit schedules for K, M, v.  
- Strength: well written, plausible claims, and careful technical development.  
- Major practical weakness: hard in practice to ensure or verify that parameters lie in Θ; mixing constants (C, α) are rarely known and bounds are likely conservative.  
- Projection onto Θ may be computationally heavy or infeasible for general models and could introduce significant bias versus unconstrained MLE.  
- Risk of over-regularization when combining fast-mixing constraints with ridge regularization; no guidance on joint/adaptive tuning of Θ.  
- Analysis assumes fresh independent MCMC samples per iteration and does not cover persistent chains, sample pools, or contrastive-divergence heuristics commonly used in practice.  
- Questions/suggestions: provide data-driven ways to choose/adapt Θ, robustness to unknown mixing constants, computational complexity of projection in general settings, and extensions to persistent/warm-start sampling.  
- Suggest exploring hybrid strategies (contrastive divergence + fast-mixing constraint) and empirical diagnostics to adapt v, and clarify Lipschitz constant derivation and dimensional dependence.  
- Overall: solid and original theoretical contribution formalizing MCMC-based ML guarantees, but limited immediate practical impact without methods to enforce/verify Θ or accommodate standard training heuristics.
