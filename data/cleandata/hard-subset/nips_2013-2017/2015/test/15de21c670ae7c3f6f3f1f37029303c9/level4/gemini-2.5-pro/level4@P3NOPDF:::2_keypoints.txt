- Proposes a computationally efficient approach to approximate MLE in exponential families by approximating the log-partition integral via sampling.  
- Assumes access to a fast-mixing MCMC sampler for all parameters in the allowed set to enable accurate integral estimates.  
- Shows that sampling-based approximations can provide sufficient precision for proximal gradient descent, yielding an FPRAS for the MLE.  
- Provides convergence guarantees for both convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Includes a simple experiment to illustrate the theoretical results.  
- Novel combination of fast-mixing MCMC, concentration inequalities, and proximal gradient analysis to link sampler precision to optimization convergence.  
- Strong and apparently sound theoretical analysis connecting sampler mixing time to overall complexity of approximate MLE.  
- Clear and candid discussion of limitations and pessimistic assumptions, with suggestions for future work.  
- Offers broader insights into related algorithms (e.g., Contrastive Divergence) by formalizing the role of sampler accuracy.  
- Suggests explicitly stating and justifying convexity of the negative log-likelihood (Hessian = covariance of sufficient statistics).  
- Recommends consolidating all key assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ) in one clear, enumerated list.  
- Main practical limitation: proving fast-mixing for a given model class is often difficult, so the paper shifts one hard problem to another.  
- Valuable theoretical contribution that gives an off-the-shelf result for researchers who can establish fast-mixing for new model classes.  
- Minor editorial issues: typos and phrasing errors at l185, l192, l207, l281, and an unclear sentence at l311–312.
