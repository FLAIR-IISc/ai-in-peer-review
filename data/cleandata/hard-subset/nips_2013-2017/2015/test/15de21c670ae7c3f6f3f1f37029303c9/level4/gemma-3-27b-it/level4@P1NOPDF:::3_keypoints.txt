- Problem: weight learning in Markov networks requires gradient computation, which is NP-hard, so MCMC is used to approximate the gradient.  
- Contribution: proves that if MCMC mixes rapidly for the given weights, then MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.  
- Scope: derives separate theoretical bounds for the unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases.  
- Presentation: paper is generally well-written with only minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Practicality concern: the required parameter/weight constraints for fast MCMC mixing are unlikely to hold in practice.  
- Question: unclear whether the mixing condition can be enforced artificially and whether it has been considered as an explicit regularizer.  
- Risk: combining the mixing-based constraint with ridge regularization may lead to over-regularized (poor) models.  
- Overall assessment: formal and impressive theoretical results but limited practical applicability; further non-trivial research is needed to make the approach practical and accurate.
