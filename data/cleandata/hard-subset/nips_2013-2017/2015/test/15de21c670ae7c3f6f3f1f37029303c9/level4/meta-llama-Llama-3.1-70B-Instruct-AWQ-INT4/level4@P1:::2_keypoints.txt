- Proposes a novel approach for computationally efficient MLE in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters.  
- Shows that, under the fast-mixing assumption, integrals for proximal gradient descent can be approximated to yield an FPRAS for the MLE, using results from Schmidt et al. (2011).  
- Provides separate guarantees for convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Presents a simple experiment that demonstrates the theoretical claims.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid and nontrivially combines existing techniques (MCMC mixing, concentration inequalities, Schmidt et al.).  
- Main limitation: practical value is limited because proving fast mixing of MCMC is typically very difficult, shifting one hard problem to another.  
- Paper offers useful insights into CD-type algorithms, though the analyzed algorithm is not exactly contrastive divergence.  
- Authors honestly discuss limitations, overly pessimistic assumptions, and directions for future work.  
- Introduction is well-written with good references, but technical presentation sometimes lacks clarity and concision.  
- Important omissions: the paper should explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood and clearly summarize all assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ).  
- Several minor typographical and phrasing errors noted (lines cited for corrections).  
- Strengths: original/substantial contribution, avoids small-treewidth reliance, solid theory, and thoughtful conclusions.  
- Weaknesses: limited practical applicability, unclear statement of assumptions, and some technical clarity issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and explicitly state key assumptions.
