The primary concern with the manuscript is its apparent lack of engagement with existing literature on the topic. The authors' algorithm bears a strong resemblance to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, as well as the broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics that have been proposed in various forms to optimize intractable likelihood functions. A well-established body of literature on this topic exists, and the authors would benefit from comparing their work to these existing methods. Notably, a theoretical framework that encompasses some of the authors' results already exists and is applicable to more general scenarios. For instance, Markov chains are not required to be uniformly ergodic, and there is no need to reinitialize the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r at each iteration. Although the authors acknowledge that this reinitialization is done to simplify their analysis, this justification is insufficient. Furthermore, reinitializing with the previous sample, as discussed at the end of the manuscript, may be a more effective approach, and such algorithms have been extensively analyzed in previous work. The presentation of the results could also be improved. For example, the assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are unclear and require the reader to refer to preceding and subsequent text, which is not ideal. Additionally, the strong assumptions used to develop results such as those in Theorem 6 are unnecessary, as more general results have been established (e.g., by Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret). Overall, the authors appear to lack awareness of the relevant literature, and their results are only marginally novel.
