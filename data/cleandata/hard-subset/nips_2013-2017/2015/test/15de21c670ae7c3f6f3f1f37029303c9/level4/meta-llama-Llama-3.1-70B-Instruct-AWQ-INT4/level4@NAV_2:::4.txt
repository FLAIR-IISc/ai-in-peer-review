This paper presents an innovative approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models by introducing the concept of "fast-mixing parameters" where Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference can be guaranteed to quickly converge to the stationary distribution. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, offering both upper and lower bounds. The paper's main contribution lies in its ability to establish a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme for maximum likelihood learning in exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.

The paper's quality is evident in its rigorous theoretical analysis, which provides a comprehensive understanding of the problem. The authors' use of a common existing strategy for maximum likelihood learning, namely gradient descent with MCMC, is well-motivated and allows for a clear comparison with previous work. The paper's results, including Theorems 2, 4, 6, and 8, demonstrate a clear understanding of the trade-offs between the number of iterations, samples, and Markov chain transitions required to achieve a desired level of accuracy.

The presentation is clear, with the authors providing a concise introduction to the problem and a well-structured outline of their approach. The use of a cartoon in Figure 1 to illustrate the gradient descent strategy is helpful in visualizing the algorithm. The paper's notation is also well-defined, making it easy to follow the mathematical derivations.

The originality of the paper lies in its novel framing of the problem in terms of the "fast mixing" model class. The authors' use of this concept to establish convergence rates for learning algorithms is a significant contribution to the field. The paper's results appear to be novel and interesting, and the submission should be accepted based on its technical merit.

The significance of the paper is somewhat unclear, as the reviewer notes. However, the submission does fill a gap in the literature on learning intractable models using MCMC. The introduction of a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations is a valuable contribution. The paper's potential impact lies in its ability to provide a foundation for future research in this area, and the authors should consider discussing potential extensions and opportunities to build on their work.

To strengthen the submission, the authors could provide a more detailed discussion of the lower bounds applicable to the problem, as the reviewer notes that Theorem 7 is difficult to interpret. Additionally, the authors could elaborate on the potential impact of their work, including future extensions and opportunities to build on their results.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of learning algorithms in terms of mixing times.
