Comments on Quality:
The paper provides convergence-rate guarantees for learning algorithms expressed in terms of mixing times. The resulting bounds seem meaningful and practically useful. The primary concern is that the lower bound (Theorem 7) is hard to parse. A more extensive explanation of this theorem—and a broader discussion of relevant lower bounds for the problem—would improve accessibility.

Comments on Clarity:
The manuscript is clearly written and well structured.

Comments on Originality:
Both the problem formulation (restricting to a “fast-mixing” parameter class) and the theoretical developments appear original.

Comments on Significance:
The overall impact is somewhat difficult to assess, but the work appears to address a notable gap in the literature on learning intractable models with MCMC, which suggests significance. The paper would benefit from expanded discussion of potential impact, future directions, and ways the community might build on these results. Given that the submission introduces a new tractable class defined via rapid MCMC approximability of expectations and supplies novel convergence-rate guarantees for learning within this class, I recommend acceptance.
