- Defines tractable parameter sets by requiring MCMC samplers to be fast-mixing for maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected models.
- Main contribution: proves projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS when parameters lie in a convex fast-mixing set, giving explicit schedules for K, M, and v.
- Gives separate convergence analyses for convex and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases, showing faster rates with strong convexity.
- Rigorous bounds on error from biased MCMC gradient estimates and how this error affects overall optimization convergence.
- Provides explicit hyperparameter schedules to achieve desired accuracy ε with high probability 1−δ.
- Careful distinction between convex and strongly convex results, highlighting benefits of regularization.
- Theorem 7 (lower bound for the strongly convex case) is dense and hard to interpret; needs more intuition and discussion.
- Well-written and organized with clear motivation, notation, and separation of analyses.
- Includes a simple example that instantiates theoretical bounds as a useful sanity check.
- Novel idea: reframes tractability in terms of algorithmic (mixing-time) properties rather than structural constraints.
- First rigorous, non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for MCMC-based maximum likelihood learning under parameter constraints.
- Significant because it provides a formal framework for analyzing common MCMC gradient heuristics and motivates designing models with good mixing.
- Suggests need for more discussion on practical implications and methods for identifying or projecting onto fast-mixing parameter sets; conclusion is brief.
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptual novelty, important theoretical guarantees, technical quality, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical discussion and unclear presentation/intuition for lower bounds.
- Final recommendation: strong accept—novel and significant theoretical contribution that warrants acceptance.
