- Paper proposes a novel, computationally efficient approach to MLE in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters.  
- Under that fast-mixing assumption, the authors derive a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE using projected gradient descent.  
- Analysis leverages Schmidt et al. (2011), concentration inequalities, and the MCMC mixing assumption to bound integral estimation error.  
- Both convex and strongly convex settings are treated, giving likelihood-error guarantees (convex) and parameter-error guarantees (strongly convex).  
- A simple experiment is included to demonstrate the theoretical results.  
- The approach is a well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and offers insight into CD-type algorithms (though it is not strictly CD).  
- Theoretical analyses appear solid but are essentially a nontrivial combination of existing techniques.  
- Authors honestly discuss limitations, potential pessimism in assumptions, and future work directions in the conclusion.  
- Practical value is limited because proving fast mixing for MCMC is difficult, effectively replacing one hard problem (MLE) with another ( finding a fast-mixing chain).  
- Paper should explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood (A(θ) convex via covariance Hessian) since the results rely on it.  
- The assumption that the parameter set Θ is convex is never explicitly mentioned and should be clarified.  
- Several minor typographical and clarity issues need fixing (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312; last sentence is nonsensical and should be rewritten).
