- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient method for approximate MLE in exponential families using proximal gradient descent with MCMC-based integral estimates.  
- Assumes rapid mixing of MCMC for all permissible parameters to ensure accurate integral estimation.  
- Combines MCMC mixing assumption, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al. (2011) to yield a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme.  
- Provides separate theoretical guarantees for convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Includes a simple experiment that supports the theoretical claims.  
- Theoretical analysis appears robust and is a nontrivial, innovative synthesis of existing techniques.  
- Offers useful insights into contrastive divergence–type algorithms, though the analyzed algorithm is not strictly CD.  
- Candidly discusses limitations and conservatism of assumptions, and outlines directions for future work.  
- Main practical limitation: requiring provable fast mixing of MCMC chains, which is difficult and may limit applicability.  
- Intro is well-written with extensive references, but technical presentation lacks clarity in places.  
- Omits an explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex and should state convexity of A(θ) explicitly to aid readers.  
- Fails to explicitly summarize key assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ) in the main text.  
- Minor editorial issues: l185 ",w here"→", where"; l192 "this paper that"→"this paper assumes that"; l207 "simple example fast-mixing"→"simple example of a fast-mixing"; l281 "of of"→"of"; l311-12 unclear sentence — rewrite for clarity.  
- Overall assessment: original and significant contribution, well-written, but practical utility may be limited by the fast-mixing requirement.
