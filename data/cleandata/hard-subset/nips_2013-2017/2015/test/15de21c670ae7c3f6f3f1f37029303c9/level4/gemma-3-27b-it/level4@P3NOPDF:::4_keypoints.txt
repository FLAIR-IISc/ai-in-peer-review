- Presents convergence-rate guarantees for learning on a novel class of models defined by efficient MCMC approximability (“fast mixing” models).  
- Demonstrates meaningful bounds on convergence rates framed in terms of mixing times.  
- Results are original, fill a gap in the literature, and warrant acceptance.  
- Theorem 7 (lower bound) is unclear and needs much clearer presentation and interpretation.  
- Recommend deeper discussion comparing the new lower bound to existing lower bounds.  
- Technical arguments appear sound, but the lower-bound analysis needs improved accessibility.  
- Overall presentation is generally clear and well-structured; math derivations are followable.  
- Novelty: framing tractability via “fast mixing” is insightful and not merely an application of existing techniques.  
- Significance could be better articulated by discussing practical applications and how theory guides algorithm design.  
- Suggest exploring extensions (more complex MCMC beyond Metropolis–Hastings, applications beyond parameter estimation).  
- Overall high-quality work with potential to stimulate further research.
