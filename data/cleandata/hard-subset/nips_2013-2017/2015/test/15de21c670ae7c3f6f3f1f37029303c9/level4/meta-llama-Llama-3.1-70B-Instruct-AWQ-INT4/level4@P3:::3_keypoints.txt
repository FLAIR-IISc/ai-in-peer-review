- Addresses maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models where inference is intractable.  
- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC inference converges quickly.  
- Analyzes gradient descent using MCMC-estimated gradients with thorough convergence proofs.  
- Derives separate bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases.  
- Shows the algorithm is an FPRAS when parameters lie in the fast-mixing set.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear sound.  
- Primary contribution is theoretical; practical relevance is limited.  
- Hard to ensure parameters satisfy the fast-mixing constraints in practice.  
- Paper does not offer a clear way to enforce the fast-mixing condition (e.g., via regularization).  
- Results are promising but non-trivial research is needed to make them practical.  
- Suggests authors discuss practical implications and ways to enforce fast-mixing.  
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage addressing practical relevance; rating 7/10.
