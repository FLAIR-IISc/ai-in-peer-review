- Problem context: weight learning in Markov networks is hard because exact gradient computation is NP-hard, so MCMC is commonly used.
- Main contribution: shows that if MCMC mixes rapidly for given parameters, then MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.
- Analysis: provides separate theoretical bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) settings.
- Writing and proofs: paper is generally well written with a few typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully checked.
- Practicality concern: the central rapid-mixing condition seems to have little practical relevance, as it’s hard to ensure in practice.
- Question raised: authors should consider whether the mixing constraints can be enforced artificially.
- Regularization issue: enforcing the condition plus ridge regression may lead to over-regularized models, potentially harming accuracy.
- Recommendation: results are theoretically strong but further non-trivial research is needed to make them practical and accurate.
