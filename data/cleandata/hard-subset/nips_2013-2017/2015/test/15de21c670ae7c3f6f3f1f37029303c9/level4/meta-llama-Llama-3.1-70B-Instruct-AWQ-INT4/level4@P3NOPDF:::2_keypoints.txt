- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient approach to MLE in exponential families by assuming fast-mixing MCMC to approximate required integrals.  
- Shows that under the fast-mixing assumption, proximal gradient descent integrals can be estimated to sufficient precision, yielding a FPRAS for MLE when combined with Schmidt et al. (2011).  
- Analyzes both convex and strongly convex cases, providing likelihood-error guarantees for the former and parameter-error guarantees for the latter.  
- Includes a simple experiment that demonstrates the theoretical results.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid and nontrivially combines MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and prior optimization results.  
- The approach is an interesting alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and offers insight into CD-type algorithms, though it is not exactly contrastive divergence.  
- Paper honestly discusses limitations, notes overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions, and suggests useful future directions.  
- Practical value is limited because proving fast mixing for MCMC is hard, effectively replacing one difficult problem (MLE) with another (designing fast-mixing chains).  
- The introduction is well-written with good references, but some technical clarity issues remain in the presentation.  
- Important omissions: the paper never explicitly states the convexity of the log-likelihood or clearly summarizes all assumptions (e.g., that the parameter set Θ is convex).  
- The work is largely a combination of existing techniques, which nonetheless constitutes an original and substantial contribution.  
- Several minor typographical and wording errors should be corrected (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312).
