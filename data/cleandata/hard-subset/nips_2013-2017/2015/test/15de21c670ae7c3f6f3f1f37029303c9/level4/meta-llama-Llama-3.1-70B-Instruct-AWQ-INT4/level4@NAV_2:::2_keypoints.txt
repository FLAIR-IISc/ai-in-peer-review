- Proposes a novel approach yielding a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for MLE in exponential families under a fast-mixing MCMC assumption.  
- Shows proximal gradient descent integrals can be approximated with sufficient precision using MCMC mixing plus concentration bounds and Schmidt et al. (2011).  
- Provides separate guarantees for convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Includes a simple experiment that demonstrates the theoretical results.  
- Combines existing techniques (MCMC mixing, concentration inequalities, proximal optimization) in a nontrivial and useful way.  
- Thoughtful and honest discussion of limitations, overly pessimistic assumptions, and future work directions.  
- Offers insights relevant to contrastive-divergence–type algorithms, though analyzed algorithm is not exactly CD.  
- Main practical limitation: proving fast mixing for MCMC across allowed parameters is extremely challenging, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Paper replaces one hard problem (MLE) with another (design/proof of fast-mixing chains), which may nonetheless be valuable.  
- Some clarity issues in technical presentation—omits explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex and should state why A(theta) is convex.  
- Fails to clearly summarize key assumptions in the main text, e.g., convexity of the parameter set Theta.  
- Theoretical analyses appear solid though proofs were not checked in detail by the reviewer.  
- Well-written introduction with substantial prior-work references, but contains minor typos and a few nonsensical sentences.  
- Overall judgment: original and substantial contribution worth publishing despite limited practical value; balance of acceptance and rejection arguments noted.
