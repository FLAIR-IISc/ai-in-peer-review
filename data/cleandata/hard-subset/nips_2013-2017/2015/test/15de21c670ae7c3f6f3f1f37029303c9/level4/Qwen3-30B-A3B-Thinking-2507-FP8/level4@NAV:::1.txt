

The primary concern with this manuscript is a significant lack of engagement with the established literature on the topic. The authors' proposed algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm and falls within the broader class of stochastic approximation methods with Markovian dynamics, which have been extensively developed for optimizing intractable likelihood functions. A well-established body of literature already addresses these methods, and the authors must explicitly compare their approach to existing work. A theoretical framework encompassing the authors' results already exists, covering substantially more general scenarios—specifically, it does not require Markov chains to be uniformly ergodic, and it avoids the need to reinitialize the Markov chain at a fixed distribution $r$ at every iteration (a simplification the authors acknowledge but do not sufficiently justify; as noted in the manuscript's conclusion, reinitializing using the previous sample may be preferable, and such strategies have been thoroughly analyzed previously). Furthermore, the presentation of results is suboptimal: for Theorems 1 and 2, the assumptions are unclear and force readers to consult surrounding text rather than having them explicitly stated. Additionally, the strong assumptions employed for results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results are available (e.g., in Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret), rendering the authors' approach only marginally novel. The authors' insufficient awareness of the literature undermines the contribution.
