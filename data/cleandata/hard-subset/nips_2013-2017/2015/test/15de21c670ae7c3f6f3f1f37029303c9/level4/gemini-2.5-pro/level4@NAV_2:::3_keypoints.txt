- Paper proves that projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS for maximum likelihood in exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics under a fast-mixing parameter constraint Θ.
- Central idea: constrain parameters to a known convex “fast-mixing” set Θ where MCMC mixes rapidly, enabling rigorous convergence analysis.
- Provides separate, detailed convergence and complexity analyses for the unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.
- Originality/significance: gives the first formal theoretical justification for a widely used practical heuristic, reframing tractability via mixing properties instead of graph structure.
- Technical quality: strong, technically sound derivations that quantify trade-offs between optimization error, sample complexity, and MCMC mixing time.
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, with clear setup, assumptions, and main theorems.
- Main weakness: the assumption of a known, convex, efficiently projectable fast-mixing set Θ is very strong and limits practical applicability.
- Unclear how to define or enforce Θ for complex real-world models beyond simple examples (e.g., Ising models).
- Potential risk of over-regularization: interaction between implicit constraint-induced regularization and explicit ridge penalty is not analyzed and could harm generalization.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical contribution, first formal FPRAS result for the heuristic, and comprehensive complexity bounds.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive central assumption reduces immediate practical impact and the paper lacks broader discussion or methods for satisfying constraints in practice.
- Overall: strong and original theoretical work with limited practical relevance until methods for identifying/projecting onto fast-mixing parameter sets are developed.
