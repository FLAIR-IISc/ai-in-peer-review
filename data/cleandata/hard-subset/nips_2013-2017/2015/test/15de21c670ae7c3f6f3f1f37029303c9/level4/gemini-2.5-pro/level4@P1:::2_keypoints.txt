- Proposes constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC converges quickly for learning in high-treewidth exponential families.  
- Shows projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients gives an FPRAS for maximum likelihood within the fast-mixing set.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for both convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (regularized) cases on log-likelihood sub-optimality and parameter error.  
- Analysis combines MCMC mixing time bounds, concentration inequalities, and convergence of gradient descent with inexact gradients.  
- Novel and non-trivial combination of existing techniques, offering a compelling alternative to low-treewidth approaches like Chow-Liu.  
- Gives useful theoretical insight into contrastive divergence, despite differences from standard CD implementations.  
- Includes a simple Ising model experiment to illustrate theoretical results.  
- Paper is generally well-written and the authors candidly discuss limitations and future directions.  
- Fails to explicitly state or justify convexity of the negative log-likelihood (f(θ)), which should be mentioned for completeness.  
- Does not summarize core assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ needed for projection) prominently enough.  
- Main practical limitation: identifying and projecting onto a parameter set with provably fast MCMC mixing remains very challenging.  
- Overall assessment: substantial, original theoretical contribution; recommends acceptance.
