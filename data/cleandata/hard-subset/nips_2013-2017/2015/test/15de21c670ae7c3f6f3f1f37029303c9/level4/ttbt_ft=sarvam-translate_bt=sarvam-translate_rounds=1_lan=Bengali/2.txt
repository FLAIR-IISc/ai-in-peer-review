Summary: This paper proposes a new method of computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families. Generally, finding maximum likelihood solutions is difficult. From a convex optimization perspective, an integral needs to be computed with respect to the currently proposed EF parameters. The authors are able to show that the integrals required for proximal gradient descent can be computed with sufficient accuracy, assuming that MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters. When this computation is combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), a complete-multivariate randomized approximation scheme can be obtained for the MLE computation. Both convex and strongly convex cases are considered, which guarantees different types: the first on probability error, and the second on parameter error. A simple experiment to demonstrate the theory is also included.

Overall, this is a high-quality work. This method is well-motivated as an alternative to relying on small-tree-with assumptions. The theoretical analyses seem difficult, although I have not examined the proofs in detail. The proofs are mainly about combining the MCMC mixing assumption with density inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al. Therefore, this paper is a combination of existing techniques, although this combination is not trivial.

I appreciate that the limitations of the method are discussed with honesty. In particular, the method's weakness in a thoughtful conclusion, the way the theoretical assumptions are overly pessimistic, and the direction of future work are analyzed. This paper provides some insights into CD-type algorithms, although the algorithm analyzed here is not entirely contrastive divergence.

The introduction is well-written with sufficient reference to previous work, but there were a few issues with the clarity of the technical presentation. A small but important flaw was simply the statement that log-probability is actually convex: the 2nd and 3rd terms of equation 1 are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(theta) comes from the fact that the Hessian of A is the covariance of the sufficient statistic, and therefore it is PSD. Although seemingly small, the entire paper is based on the convexity of f(theta), and therefore discussing it will help guide the reader through the paper's arguments. A clear summary of the assumptions made in the paper is provided. For example, an assumption not mentioned in the main paper is that the parameter set Theta must be convex.

Overall, I see this paper as a fundamental and important contribution. The results seem to have limited practical value, as MCMC's fast-mixing is extremely difficult to prove. So in a sense, this paper has replaced a difficult problem (finding the MLE) with another problem (finding the fast-mixing Markov chain). It can be said that being able to transform one problem into another is often useful, and therefore it is valuable for the community to have the results of this paper available.
