Summary: The paper proposes a novel approach to computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families. In general, finding the maximum likelihood solution is intractable. From a convex optimization perspective, the sticking point is the need to calculate an integral with respect to the currently proposed exponential family parameter. By assuming that Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters, the authors are able to show that the integrals needed for projected gradient descent can be calculated with sufficient precision that, when combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for calculating the maximum likelihood estimate (MLE) can be obtained. Both the convex and strongly convex cases are considered, leading to different types of guarantees: the former on the likelihood error, the latter on the parameter error. A simple experiment demonstrating the theory is also included.

Overall, this is a high-quality piece of work. The approach is well-motivated as an alternative to relying on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear solid, though I haven’t checked the proofs in detail. The proofs are essentially a matter of combining the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al. The paper is thus a nontrivial combination of existing techniques.

I appreciated the honesty with which the limitations of the approach were discussed. In particular, the conclusion thoughtfully analyzes weaknesses, potential pessimism in the theoretical assumptions, and directions for future work. The paper also offers some insight into contrastive divergence (CD)-type algorithms, even though the algorithm analyzed is not strictly CD.

The introduction was well-written and contained substantial references to previous work. However, there were a few issues with the clarity of the technical presentation. One small but important omission was explicitly stating that the log-likelihood is convex: the second and third terms in eq. 1 are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(θ) follows from the fact that its Hessian is the covariance of the sufficient statistics and thus is positive semi-definite. While seemingly minor, the entire paper is premised on the convexity of f(θ), so discussing this point will help guide the reader. A clearer summary of the assumptions would also be beneficial. For example, the paper never explicitly mentions the assumption that the parameter set Θ must be convex. 

Overall, I found the paper to be an original and substantial contribution. The results seem to have limited practical value due to the inherent difficulty of proving fast mixing for MCMC. Thus, the paper potentially replaces one hard problem (finding the MLE) with another (finding a fast-mixing Markov chain). Nevertheless, converting one problem into another is often useful, making the availability of these results valuable to the community.
