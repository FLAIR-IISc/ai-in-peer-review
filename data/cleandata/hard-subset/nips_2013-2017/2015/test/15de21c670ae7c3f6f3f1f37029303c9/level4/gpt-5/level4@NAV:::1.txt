I believe the manuscript’s primary weakness is insufficient engagement with the relevant literature. The proposed algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM method (and, more broadly, to the class of stochastic approximation schemes with Markovian dynamics that have been introduced in various forms to optimize intractable likelihoods). This area has an extensive existing literature, and the authors should situate and compare their approach against prior work. A theoretical framework encompassing several of the authors’ results already exists and applies to significantly more general settings. For instance, uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains is not required, and it is unnecessary to restart the chain from a fixed distribution r at every iteration (the authors note they adopt this for analytical convenience, but that is not adequate—moreover, warm-starting from the previous sample may be preferable, as they mention at the end, and such algorithms have already been thoroughly analyzed). The exposition also needs improvement: for Theorems 1 and 2, the assumptions are not clearly stated; while one can piece them together from the surrounding text, the reader should not be forced to do so. Likewise, the strong assumptions used to obtain results like Theorem 6 are not needed (see, for example, Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret for more general treatments). Overall, the paper shows limited awareness of prior work, and the novelty of the results appears only marginal.
