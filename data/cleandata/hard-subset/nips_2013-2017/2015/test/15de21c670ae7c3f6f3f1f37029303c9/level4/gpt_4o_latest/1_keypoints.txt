- Insufficient engagement with existing literature on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.
- Proposed algorithm is closely related to well-studied Monte Carlo EM and broader stochastic approximation methods.
- Significant prior work and theoretical frameworks already cover many of the authors’ results in more general settings.
- Unnecessary restrictive assumptions: uniform ergodicity of Markov chains is not required in the literature.
- Reinitializing the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r each iteration is unjustified and overly restrictive.
- Reinitializing with the previous sample is a more natural and better-analyzed alternative in prior work.
- Theorems 1 and 2 lack explicit statement of their underlying assumptions, forcing readers to infer them.
- Results such as Theorem 6 rely on unnecessarily strong assumptions while more general results exist (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret).
- Overall lack of awareness of relevant prior work leads to only marginal novelty in the manuscript.
