- Proposes a novel approach for computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families using MCMC-based estimates for integrals.  
- Assumes fast-mixing MCMC for all allowed parameters to bound integral approximation error in proximal gradient descent.  
- Combines MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for MLE.  
- Provides separate guarantees for convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Includes a simple experiment that demonstrates the theoretical claims.  
- Theoretical analysis appears solid and nontrivially combines existing techniques, though proofs were not checked in detail.  
- Paper is well-motivated as an alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and offers insight into CD-type algorithms (though not exactly CD).  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations and future work, noting the practical difficulty of proving fast MCMC mixing.  
- Main practical limitation: replacing the hard problem of finding the MLE with the equally hard problem of proving fast-mixing Markov chains.  
- Clarity issues: fails to state explicitly that the log-likelihood is convex (needs explanation via A(θ) Hessian being the covariance).  
- Missing clear summary of assumptions (e.g., the parameter set Θ must be convex is not stated in the main paper).  
- Minor writing errors and typos listed (several line-specific corrections) and one nonsensical sentence that should be rewritten.
