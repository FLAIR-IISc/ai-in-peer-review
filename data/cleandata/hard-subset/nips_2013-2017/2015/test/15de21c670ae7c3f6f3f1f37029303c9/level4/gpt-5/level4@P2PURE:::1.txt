The primary issue with this manuscript is its limited engagement with the relevant literature. The proposed algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm and, more generally, to stochastic approximation methods with Markovian dynamics that have been introduced in various forms to optimize intractable likelihoods. This area has a substantial existing literature, and the authors should compare their approach with prior work. In fact, a theory encompassing several of the authors’ results already exists and addresses much more general settings.

For example, uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains is not required, and there is no need to reinitialize the chain at a fixed distribution r at each iteration. Although the authors note that they make this assumption to simplify their analysis, this justification is not sufficient. Moreover, as discussed at the end of the manuscript, reinitializing from the previous sample may be preferable, and algorithms of this type have already been extensively analyzed.

The presentation of the results also needs improvement. For Theorems 1 and 2, the assumptions are unclear; while one can infer them from the surrounding text, the reader should not have to do so. Additionally, the strong assumptions used to establish results such as Theorem 6 are unnecessary; see, for example, Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret for more general results.

Overall, the authors appear insufficiently aware of the existing literature, and the contributions seem only marginally novel.
