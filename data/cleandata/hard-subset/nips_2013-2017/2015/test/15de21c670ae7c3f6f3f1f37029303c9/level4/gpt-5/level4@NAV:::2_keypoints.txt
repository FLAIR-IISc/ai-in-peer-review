- Paper proposes a method for computationally efficient MLE in exponential families by assuming fast-mixing MCMC for all admissible parameters.
- Shows integrals for proximal gradient descent can be estimated accurately, yielding a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme (using Schmidt et al. 2011).
- Treats both convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood-error guarantees for convex and parameter-error guarantees for strongly convex settings.
- Theoretical analysis combines MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and known optimization results; integration is nontrivial though components are familiar.
- Includes a simple experiment that illustrates the theoretical results.
- Introduction is well written with strong references, but the technical presentation has some clarity issues.
- Important omission: the paper never explicitly states convexity of the log-likelihood or that the parameter set Θ is convex, which should be clarified.
- Authors candidly discuss limitations and potential pessimism of assumptions, and suggest directions for future work.
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast mixing for MCMC is very challenging, so the work trades one hard problem (MLE) for another (fast-mixing chains).
- Overall evaluation: original, substantial contribution and well written, but of limited practical utility.
- Minor errors and typos present (several line edits suggested, plus one nonsensical sentence at l311–12).
