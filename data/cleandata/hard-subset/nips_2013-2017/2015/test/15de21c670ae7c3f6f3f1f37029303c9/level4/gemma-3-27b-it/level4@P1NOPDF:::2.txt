Summary: The paper proposes a novel and computationally efficient approach to maximum likelihood learning in exponential families. Generally, finding the maximum likelihood solution is intractable. From a convex optimization perspective, the key challenge lies in calculating an integral with respect to the current exponential family parameter. By assuming fast-mixing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) for all allowed parameters, the authors demonstrate that the integrals required for proximal gradient descent can be computed with sufficient precision. When combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), this yields a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for calculating the MLE. Both the convex and strongly convex cases are considered, leading to different types of guarantees: the former on the likelihood error, and the latter on the parameter error. A simple experiment is included to demonstrate the theory.

Overall, this is a high-quality piece of work. The approach is well-motivated as an alternative to methods relying on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear solid, although I haven’t checked the proofs in detail. These proofs largely involve combining the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al.; therefore, the paper represents a nontrivial combination of existing techniques. 

I particularly appreciated the authors’ honest discussion of the approach’s limitations. The conclusion thoughtfully analyzes the weaknesses, potential over-pessimism in the theoretical assumptions, and avenues for future work. The paper also offers some insights into CD-type algorithms, even though the analyzed algorithm isn’t strictly contrastive divergence.

The introduction is well-written and provides substantial references to prior work. However, there are a few areas where the technical presentation could be improved for clarity. One minor omission is an explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex. The second and third terms in equation (1) are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(θ) follows from the fact that its Hessian is the covariance of the sufficient statistics, and is therefore positive semi-definite. While seemingly minor, the entire paper rests on the convexity of f(θ), and explicitly stating this would help guide the reader through the paper’s logic. A clear summary of the paper's assumptions would also be helpful. For instance, the requirement that the parameter set Θ be convex is never mentioned in the main text.

Overall, I found this paper to be an original and substantial contribution. However, the practical value of the results may be limited, as proving fast mixing for MCMC is extremely challenging. In a sense, the paper replaces one difficult problem (finding the MLE) with another (finding a fast-mixing Markov chain). Nevertheless, transforming one problem into another can often be useful, and making these results available to the community is valuable.
