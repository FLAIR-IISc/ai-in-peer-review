- Problem: Weight learning in Markov networks requires gradients of log-likelihood, which are NP-hard to compute, so MCMC is used to approximate them.
- Contribution: The paper gives a theoretical analysis showing conditions under which MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.
- Key assumption: The FPRAS guarantee requires rapid mixing of the MCMC sampler for the model parameters.
- Results: Guarantees are provided separately for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (claimed non-convex) case, with explicit bounds.
- Writing and proofs: The paper is generally well-written, with a few typos, and proofs appear sound on initial inspection.
- Main weakness: The rapid-mixing condition is stringent, practically unverifiable, and limits real-world applicability.
- Suggestion: Authors should consider methods to enforce rapid mixing during learning (a form of regularization) and study interactions with standard regularizers like ridge to avoid over-regularization.
- Overall: Valuable theoretical contribution but limited practical utility unless the rapid-mixing assumption can be made enforceable or verifiable; more work needed to bridge theory and practice.
