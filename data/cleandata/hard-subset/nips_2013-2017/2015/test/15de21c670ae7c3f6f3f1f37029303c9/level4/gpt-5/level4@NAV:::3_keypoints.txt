Central challenge: weight learning in Markov networks needs gradient computation, which is NP-hard, so MCMC is often used as a practical subroutine.

Main contribution: proves that if parameters induce rapid MCMC mixing, then the MCMC-based weight-learning algorithm is an FPRAS.

Two settings analyzed: unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex), with separate theoretical bounds for each.

Manuscript quality: generally well written with minor typographical errors; proofs appear plausible though not fully checked.

Practical concern: the rapid-mixing parameter condition is virtually impossible to guarantee in practice, limiting practical relevance.

Question raised: can the rapid-mixing condition be enforced artificially (i.e., treated as a form of regularization)?

Risk noted: combining an enforced mixing condition with ridge regularization could lead to over-regularized models.

Overall verdict: solid theoretical delineation of conditions for FPRAS via MCMC, but substantive research is needed to make the results practical and accurate.
