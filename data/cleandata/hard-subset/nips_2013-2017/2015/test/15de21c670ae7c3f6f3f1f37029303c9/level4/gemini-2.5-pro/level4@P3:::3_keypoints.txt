- Proposes learning high-treewidth undirected models by constraining parameters to a convex "fast-mixing" set Θ where MCMC converges rapidly.  
- Shows gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS for MLE within Θ for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Main theoretical results: unregularized case — objective convergence with total effort O(1/ε^3) (ignoring logs); ridge-regularized case — parameter convergence with effort O(1/ε^2).  
- Derives explicit schedules for number of gradient steps, MCMC samples per step, and MCMC chain lengths to achieve ε-accuracy with high probability.  
- Strength: Novel reframing from structural (treewidth) to dynamic (mixing-time) tractability, opening new research directions.  
- Strength: Provides the first rigorous proof that finite-length MCMC gradient approximation can be provably efficient under clear conditions.  
- Strength: Clear, well-written, and mathematically precise presentation with transparent assumptions and plausible proofs.  
- Weakness: Central assumption requires a known, convex fast-mixing set Θ, but practical methods for constructing such sets for general models are unclear.  
- Weakness: Assumes access to an efficient projection oracle ΠΘ and omits projection cost from complexity bounds; projection may be computationally heavy (e.g., SVD).  
- Weakness: Interaction between the fast-mixing constraint and explicit regularization (ridge) is not fully analyzed and could cause over-regularization or redundancy.  
- Conclusion: Strong theoretical contribution likely to spur follow-up work, but practical impact depends on developing methods to identify and project onto fast-mixing parameter sets.
