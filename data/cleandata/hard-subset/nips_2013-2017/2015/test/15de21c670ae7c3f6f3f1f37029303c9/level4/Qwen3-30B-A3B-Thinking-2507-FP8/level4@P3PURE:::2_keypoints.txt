- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient method for maximum likelihood learning in exponential families using proximal gradient descent with MCMC-based integral estimates.  
- Assumes rapid MCMC mixing across the parameter domain to compute required integrals with sufficient precision.  
- Combines the MCMC mixing assumption with Schmidt et al. (2011) to yield a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Provides separate analyses for convex and strongly convex cases: the convex case bounds likelihood error, the strongly convex case bounds parameter error.  
- Includes a simple experimental demonstration to illustrate the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and synthesizes existing techniques in a nontrivial way.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound (relies on mixing assumption, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al.), though proofs were not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Authors are candid about limitations, discuss weaknesses, and suggest promising future directions.  
- Offers useful insights into contrastive divergence (CD)-type algorithms, despite not analyzing exactly CD.  
- Introduction is strong with substantial prior-work references.  
- Technical presentation has clarity issues: convexity of the log-likelihood and of A(θ) should be explicitly stated and justified.  
- The convexity of the parameter set Θ and a clear summary of all assumptions are not explicitly presented and should be added.  
- Practical value is limited by the difficulty of verifying the fast-mixing MCMC assumption, effectively replacing one hard problem with another.  
- Nonetheless, reframing the problem in terms of mixing conditions is still a valuable contribution.  
- Minor typographical and phrasing corrections suggested (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312).
