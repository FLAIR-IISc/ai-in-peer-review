## Review:

The paper presents convergence rates for learning algorithms, expressed in terms of mixing times, and these bounds are both meaningful and potentially highly useful. 

My primary concern lies with the interpretability of the lower bound presented in Theorem 7. A more detailed discussion of this theorem, and a broader contextualization of applicable lower bounds for this problem, would greatly strengthen the work.

The presentation is clear and well-structured.

The framing of the problem – utilizing a “fast mixing” model class – and the associated theoretical results appear to be original contributions.

The significance of this work is somewhat unclear, though it does appear to address a gap in the literature concerning learning intractable models via Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods, suggesting a notable level of significance.  

The submission would benefit from a more thorough discussion of the potential impact of this work, including possible future research directions and opportunities for building upon these findings. The introduction of a new class of tractable models grounded in strong MCMC-based expectation approximation is a valuable contribution. Given the novelty and interest of the theoretical convergence results for learning these models, I recommend acceptance.
