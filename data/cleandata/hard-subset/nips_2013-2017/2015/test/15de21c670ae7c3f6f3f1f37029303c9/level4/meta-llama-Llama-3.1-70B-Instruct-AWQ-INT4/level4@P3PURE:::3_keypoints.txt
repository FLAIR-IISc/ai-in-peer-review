- Problem: Weight learning in Markov networks requires computing gradients, which is NP-hard, so MCMC is commonly used as an approximate substitute.  
- Contribution: Paper proves that if parameters make MCMC rapidly mixing, then MCMC-based weight learning is a Fully Polynomial-Time Randomized Approximation Scheme (FPRAS).  
- Cases analyzed: Separate bounds are derived for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (non-convex) case.  
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-written with a few typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Practical relevance: The required parameter conditions for the theorems are difficult or impossible to ensure in practice.  
- Recommendation: Authors should explore whether the rapid-mixing condition can be enforced artificially (viewed as a form of regularization).  
- Concern: Applying ridge regularization in addition to enforcing the condition may cause over-regularization.  
- Overall assessment: Theoretical formalization is valuable but has limited practical impact until non-trivial research addresses making the conditions enforceable and yielding a practical algorithm.
