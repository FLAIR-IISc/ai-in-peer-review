- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient approach to MLE in exponential families using fast-mixing MCMC to approximate integrals for proximal gradient descent, yielding an FPRAS.  
- Analyzes both convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood-error guarantees in the former and parameter-error guarantees in the latter.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid and nontrivially combines fast-mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al. (2011).  
- Well-motivated as an alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and sheds light on CD-type algorithms (though not exactly CD).  
- Includes a simple experiment that illustrates the theoretical results.  
- Candid discussion of limitations and thoughtful analysis of overly pessimistic assumptions and future directions.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is difficult, effectively swapping one hard problem (MLE) for another (designing fast-mixing chains).  
- Clarity issues in technical presentation: omission of an explicit statement that the negative log-likelihood is convex and that the parameter set Θ must be convex.  
- Requests a clear summary of all assumptions used.  
- Notes specific clarity/grammar problems (e.g., l311–312 nonsensical) and several minor typos.  
- Editorial suggestions: replace “wrt” with “with respect to” and ensure consistent hyphenation of “fast-mixing.”
