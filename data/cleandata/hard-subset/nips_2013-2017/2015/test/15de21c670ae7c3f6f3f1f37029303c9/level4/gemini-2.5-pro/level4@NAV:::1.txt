The primary weakness of this manuscript is its insufficient engagement with the extensive existing literature on the topic. The proposed algorithm bears a strong resemblance to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm and the broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics, which have been developed in various forms to optimize intractable likelihood functions. The manuscript would be significantly strengthened by a detailed comparison of the proposed method against this well-established body of work.

A substantial theoretical framework that includes several of the authors' results already exists and often applies to more general settings. For example, existing theory does not necessitate the Markov chains to be uniformly ergodic, nor does it require re-initializing the chain from a fixed distribution `r` at every iteration. While the authors justify this re-initialization as a means to simplify their analysis, this is not a sufficient rationale. Moreover, alternative strategies, such as initializing from the previous sample (as the authors themselves suggest), have already been extensively analyzed in prior literature.

The presentation of the results is suboptimal. For instance, the assumptions for Theorem 1 and 2 are not clearly stated within the theorems themselves, requiring the reader to search the surrounding text to understand the conditions under which the results hold. Additionally, the manuscript employs unnecessarily strong assumptions to derive certain results. The conclusions of Theorem 6, for example, can be achieved under much weaker conditions, as demonstrated in more general treatments of the topic (see, for instance, Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret).

In conclusion, the apparent lack of awareness of relevant prior work leads to contributions that are of marginal novelty.
