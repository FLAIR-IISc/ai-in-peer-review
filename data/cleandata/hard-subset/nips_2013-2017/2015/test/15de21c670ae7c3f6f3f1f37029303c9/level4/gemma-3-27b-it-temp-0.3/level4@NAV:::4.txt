The paper presents convergence rates for learning algorithms, expressed in terms of mixing times, and these bounds appear both relevant and practically valuable. A key area for improvement lies in the interpretability of the lower bound presented in Theorem 7; a more detailed explanation of this theorem, and a broader discussion of relevant lower bounds for this problem, would be beneficial. 

The presentation is clear and well-structured.

The work demonstrates originality both in its formulation of the problem – specifically, framing it within a “fast mixing” model class – and in the theoretical results themselves.

The significance of this work is somewhat unclear, though it appears to address a gap in the literature concerning learning intractable models via Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC). The submission would benefit from a more thorough discussion of its potential impact, including possible future research directions and opportunities for building upon these findings. The introduction of a new class of tractable models grounded in strong MCMC-based expectation approximation is a notable contribution. Given the novelty and interest of the theoretical convergence rate results for learning these models, I recommend acceptance.
