This manuscript presents an algorithm with strong connections to existing work in Monte Carlo Expectation-Maximization (EM) and, more broadly, stochastic approximation methods for intractable likelihood optimization. The primary weakness of the paper is a significant lack of engagement with this established literature. 

The authors’ approach shares substantial similarities with algorithms already developed and analyzed in this area. A robust theoretical foundation already exists which encompasses the authors’ results within a more general framework. Specifically, current theory often relaxes the requirement of uniform ergodicity for the Markov chains involved and avoids the need for re-initialization of the Markov chain at each iteration from a fixed distribution *r*, as employed here. While the authors acknowledge the re-initialization is for analytical convenience, this simplification is a notable limitation. Previous research suggests initializing with the previous sample might be a more effective strategy, and these alternatives have received considerable analytical attention. 

The presentation of the results also requires improvement. The assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are not explicitly stated within the theorem statements themselves. While discoverable through cross-referencing with surrounding text, critical assumptions should be clearly articulated directly within each theorem for clarity and readability. Furthermore, the strong assumptions utilized to prove Theorem 6 appear unnecessarily restrictive; more general results, such as those found in Benveniste, Metivier and Priouret, demonstrate that similar conclusions can be reached under weaker conditions.

Overall, the manuscript’s novelty is limited by the authors’ apparent unfamiliarity with the related literature. While the work is technically sound, the contribution feels incremental given the existing body of research. A thorough comparison to, and discussion of, relevant prior work is crucial to establish the distinctiveness and significance of this contribution.
