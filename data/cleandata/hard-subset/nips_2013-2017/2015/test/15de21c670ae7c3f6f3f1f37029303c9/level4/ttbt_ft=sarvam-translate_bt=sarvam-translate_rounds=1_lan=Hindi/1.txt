I think the main issue with the manuscript is the lack of awareness of the literature on this topic. The authors' algorithm strongly relates to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm (and indeed the general class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics, which have been proposed to optimize intractable probability tasks in various forms). There is well-developed literature on this subject, and the authors should compare what they are doing with existing work. A theory that includes some of the authors' results already and covers much more common scenarios exists. For example, Markov chains do not need to be uniformly ergodic, and there is no need to restart the Markov chain at a certain distribution r on each iteration (the authors admit that they do this to simplify their analysis, but that is not enough – it might be a better idea to restart with the previous sample, as discussed at the end of the manuscript, and these algorithms have been extensively analyzed before). The presentation of the results is far from optimal: for example, it is not clear what the assumptions are for theorems 1 and 2? One can refer to the before and after text, but the reader should not have to do that. Also note that there is no need for strong assumptions used to develop results like theorem 6 (see Benvenisti, Mateevier, and Prioret for more general results). I think there is a lack of awareness of the literature among the authors, and the results are only marginally new.
