The primary weakness of this manuscript lies in its limited engagement with relevant existing literature. The proposed algorithm shares strong connections with the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, and more broadly, with the established field of stochastic approximation algorithms employing Markovian dynamics for intractable likelihood optimization. A substantial body of work already exists in this area, and a thorough comparison to these methods is necessary. Specifically, a theoretical framework encompassing the authors’ findings already exists and addresses a wider range of conditions; for instance, it relaxes the requirement for uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains. Furthermore, the practice of reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution *r* at each iteration – acknowledged by the authors as a simplification for analysis – is potentially suboptimal; previous research suggests leveraging the prior sample, and has already extensively analyzed such approaches. 

The presentation of the results also requires improvement. The assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated within the theorems themselves, forcing readers to cross-reference surrounding text. Similarly, the strong assumptions made to derive results like those in Theorem 6 are unnecessary, as more general results (e.g., Benveniste, Metivier and Priouret) are available. 

Overall, the manuscript demonstrates a lack of awareness of the broader literature, resulting in results that, while technically sound, offer only incremental novelty.
