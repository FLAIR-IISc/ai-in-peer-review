- Studies maximum-likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models by restricting parameters to a “fast-mixing” set where MCMC converges quickly.  
- Proves that for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients yields a FPRAS to the ML solution.  
- Provides convergence rates: ε-accuracy in log-likelihood with complexity cubic in 1/ε, and ε-accuracy in parameter distance with complexity quadratic in 1/ε under ridge regularization.  
- Core idea is original and compelling, giving theoretical guarantees for a common but previously unrigorous MCMC-based learning practice.  
- Results are meaningful, with clear, well-organized presentation and rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Paper fills a literature gap and offers a foundation for exploring other tractable model classes beyond tree-structured distributions.  
- Significance and practical impact are not fully articulated; more discussion of application scenarios (e.g., model misspecification or post-learning inference needs) is needed.  
- Theorem 7 (lower bound on computational effort) is hard to interpret and lacks sufficient explanation and implications.  
- The paper needs a more detailed discussion of the limitations and practical applicability of the derived bounds.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel problem framing, strong theoretical guarantees, addresses a practical gap, and enables future work.  
- Rejection arguments: unclear practical significance, insufficient explanation of Theorem 7, and limited discussion of bounds’ applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—significant theoretical contribution likely to influence future research; minor weaknesses can be addressed with improved discussion.
