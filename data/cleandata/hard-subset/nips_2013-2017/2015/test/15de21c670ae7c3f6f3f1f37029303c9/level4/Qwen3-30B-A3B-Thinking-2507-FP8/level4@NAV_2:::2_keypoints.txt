- Paper proposes a novel FPRAS for maximum likelihood in exponential families by assuming rapid MCMC mixing over a constrained parameter set.
- Key technical tools: fast-mixing MCMC assumption, concentration inequalities, and results from Schmidt et al. (2011).
- Provides separate guarantees for convex (log-likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter distance error) cases.
- Includes a simple illustrative example that supports the theoretical results.
- Offers a well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions in graphical models.
- Analysis appears robust and non-trivially combines existing techniques, though proofs were not fully checked by the reviewer.
- Honest and thorough discussion of limitations, acknowledging potentially pessimistic theoretical assumptions.
- Gives useful insights into contrastive divergence–type algorithms despite analyzing a different specific algorithm.
- Introduction is well-written with strong references to prior work.
- Minor clarity issues in technical presentation, notably omission of the straightforward convexity observation for the negative log-likelihood.
- Lacks a clear summary of key assumptions (e.g., convexity of the constraint set Θ).
- Practical impact is limited because verifying fast MCMC mixing can be as hard as the original problem, though the theoretical transformation is valuable.
- Paper is an original, substantial contribution with meaningful value to the research community.
- Minor editorial corrections suggested (several small typos/phrasing fixes).
