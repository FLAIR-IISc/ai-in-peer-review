- Manuscript fails to engage with existing literature on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.  
- The proposed algorithm strongly resembles Monte Carlo EM and related stochastic approximation methods and needs explicit comparison to them.  
- Existing theoretical frameworks already cover some of the authors' results and handle broader cases (e.g., without uniform ergodicity).  
- Reinitializing the Markov chain to a fixed distribution r each iteration is unjustified and unnecessary.  
- Reinitializing with the previous sample is likely more effective and has been extensively analyzed in prior work.  
- Presentation flaws: assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated and should be made explicit.  
- The strong assumptions used for results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results (e.g., Benveniste, Métiivier, Priouret) exist.  
- Overall contribution appears only marginally novel due to insufficient familiarity with and comparison to prior work.
