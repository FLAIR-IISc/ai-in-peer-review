- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient MLE method for exponential families using the assumption that MCMC mixes rapidly to approximate integrals for proximal gradient descent.  
- Combines the mixing assumption with Schmidt et al. (2011) to give a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for MLE.  
- Provides separate guarantees: likelihood-error bounds for the convex case and parameter-error bounds for the strongly convex case.  
- Presents a simple empirical demonstration that supports the theoretical results.  
- High-quality, original contribution offering an alternative to small-treewidth assumptions via a nontrivial synthesis of MCMC mixing, concentration inequalities, and prior work.  
- Authors are transparent about limitations and connect results to insights about contrastive divergence, despite methodological differences.  
- Technical exposition needs clarity: must explicitly state log-likelihood convexity (via A(θ) convexity) and that the parameter set Θ is assumed convex.  
- Primary practical limitation: verifying fast-mixing MCMC for arbitrary EF parameters is difficult, so the work replaces one hard problem (MLE) with another (fast-mixing verification).  
- Authors note some theoretical bounds may be overly pessimistic and outline promising future directions.  
- Minor typographical corrections needed (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-motivated, important theoretical step; recommendation to accept.
