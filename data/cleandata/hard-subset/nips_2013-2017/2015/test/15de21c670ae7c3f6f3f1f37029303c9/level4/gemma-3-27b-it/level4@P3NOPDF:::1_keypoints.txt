- Algorithm addresses optimization of intractable likelihoods but resembles existing stochastic approximation/Monte Carlo EM methods.
- Main weakness: limited engagement with and comparison to the substantial existing literature in stochastic approximation and MC-EM.
- Authors should explicitly position their method within the stochastic approximation framework and state how it differs or improves on prior work.
- The manuscript omits discussion of broader theoretical results that already encompass or generalize the authors’ findings.
- The analysis assumes uniform ergodicity of Markov chains, despite literature providing weaker, more general conditions.
- Reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is unjustified; alternatives (e.g., reusing previous samples) are standard and should be analyzed.
- Theorems 1 and 2 lack clear, self-contained statements of their underlying assumptions.
- The strong assumptions in Theorem 6 appear unnecessary given related results (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) that hold under milder conditions.
- Recommendations: expand the related work section, compare to existing analyses, justify or revise reinitialization strategy, and state/relax assumptions in theorems.
- Overall: technically sound but marginally novel due to insufficient literature contextualization and presentation shortcomings.
