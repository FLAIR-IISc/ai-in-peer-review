- Weight learning in Markov networks is hard; gradient computation is NP-hard, so MCMC is commonly used to approximate gradients.
- Paper proves that if model parameters guarantee rapid MCMC mixing, MCMC-based weight learning becomes an FPRAS.
- Two cases analyzed with separate convergence bounds: unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex).
- Theoretical contributions are strong and proofs appear plausible, though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Paper is generally well-written but contains minor typographical errors.
- Major limitation: the parameter conditions required for the theorems are practically unrealistic and hard to guarantee.
- Authors should address whether those conditions can be enforced (e.g., via regularization) and whether adding ridge regularization risks over-regularization.
- Overall, the paper formalizes conditions for FPRAS via MCMC but offers negligible immediate practical utility; significant further research is needed to make it applicable.
