- Problem: weight learning in Markov networks is hard because exact gradient computation is NP-hard, so MCMC approximations are used.  
- Contribution: proves that if MCMC mixes rapidly for the given weights, MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.  
- Cases analyzed: separate theoretical bounds for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (non-convex) case.  
- Writing: paper is generally well-written with only minor typos.  
- Proofs: proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Practical relevance: theoretical results are impressive but have limited practical applicability.  
- Main concern: it is difficult in practice to guarantee the parameter conditions required for the theorems to hold.  
- Suggestion: consider enforcing the fast-mixing condition via additional regularization (e.g., ridge), but this risks over-regularization.  
- Impact: addressing how to ensure or enforce the required parameter conditions would significantly strengthen the paper.  
- Conclusion: paper formalizes conditions for FPRAS using MCMC, but turning this into a practical weight-learning algorithm requires substantial further research.
