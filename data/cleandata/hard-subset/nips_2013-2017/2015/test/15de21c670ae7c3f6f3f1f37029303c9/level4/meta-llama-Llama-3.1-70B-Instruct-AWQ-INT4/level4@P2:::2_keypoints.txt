- Proposes a novel approach for computationally efficient maximum likelihood estimation in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters.  
- Shows that integrals required for proximal gradient descent can be computed with sufficient precision to yield a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Provides separate analyses for convex and strongly convex cases, yielding likelihood-error guarantees and parameter-error guarantees respectively.  
- Includes a simple experiment that demonstrates the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions; theoretical analyses appear solid and combine existing techniques nontrivially.  
- Proof strategy: combine the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and results of Schmidt et al. (2011).  
- Paper candidly discusses limitations, overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions, and directions for future work.  
- Offers insight into contrastive-divergence-type algorithms, though analyzed algorithm is not exactly CD.  
- Major assumption (fast-mixing MCMC) is strong and hard to verify in practice, limiting practical impact—transforms one hard problem into another.  
- Clarity issues: fails to explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood and should present a clear summary of all assumptions.  
- Omits mention in main text that the parameter set Θ must be convex.  
- Several minor typos and a nonsensical sentence noted (specific line corrections provided).  
- Overall assessment: original and substantial contribution; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
