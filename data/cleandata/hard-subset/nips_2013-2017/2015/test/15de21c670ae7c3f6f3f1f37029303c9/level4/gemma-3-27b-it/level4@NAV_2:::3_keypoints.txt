- Problem: Weight estimation in Markov networks is hard; gradient descent with MCMC is commonly used to approximate intractable gradients.
- Contribution: Proves that if MCMC mixes rapidly on the model parameters, MCMC-based gradient learning is an FPRAS.
- Scope: Analysis covers both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases with separate bounds.
- Writing: Paper is well-written, clearly structured, with only minor typos.
- Proofs: Full verification not performed, but arguments appear plausible and logically sound.
- Conceptual link: Effectively connects fast-mixing parameter regimes to theoretical guarantees for gradient-based learning.
- Main limitation: The fast-mixing-on-learnt-parameters assumption is questionable in practical settings.
- Recommendation: Authors should explore methods to enforce fast-mixing (e.g., regularization) and evaluate risks like over-regularization.
- Impact: Solid theoretical contribution that formalizes when MCMC-based learning attains FPRAS guarantees.
- Practical gap: The applicability of the theoretical conditions to real-world algorithms remains an open problem.
- For acceptance: Strengths include theoretical rigor, clear presentation, novelty (fast-mixing parameters), and good literature context.
- Against acceptance: Weaknesses are limited practical relevance and lack of empirical exploration of enforcing the fast-mixing condition.
