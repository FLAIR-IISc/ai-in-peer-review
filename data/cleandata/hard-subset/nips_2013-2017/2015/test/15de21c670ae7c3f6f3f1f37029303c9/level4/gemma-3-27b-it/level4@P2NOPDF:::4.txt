This paper presents convergence rates for learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, offering bounds that appear both meaningful and useful. The core contribution lies in framing the problem within a “fast mixing” model class and the resulting theoretical developments. Both this framing and the derived theoretical results appear original.

My primary concern relates to the interpretability of Theorem 7, the lower bound presented. While mathematically sound, its practical implications are not immediately clear. Expanding the discussion surrounding this theorem – and, more broadly, providing a more comprehensive overview of relevant lower bounds in this problem space – would significantly strengthen the paper. 

The presentation is generally clear and well-structured. However, the significance of this work, while potentially substantial, is not fully articulated. The paper demonstrably addresses a gap in the literature regarding learning intractable models using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods, suggesting inherent significance. To enhance this aspect, I recommend a more detailed discussion of the potential impact of this work. Specifically, exploring future research directions and opportunities for building upon these results would be beneficial. 

The introduction of a novel class of tractable models, defined by their efficient approximability of expectations via MCMC, is a compelling aspect of this submission. Coupled with the novel and interesting theoretical results concerning convergence rates for learning these models, I believe this paper warrants acceptance. Further clarifying the practical implications of the lower bound and expanding upon the potential impact would elevate the submission further, but the existing work represents a valuable contribution to the field.
