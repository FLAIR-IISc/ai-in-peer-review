The paper proposes a novel approach to computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families, tackling the intractability of finding the maximum likelihood solution. From a convex optimization perspective, the primary challenge lies in calculating an integral with respect to the currently proposed exponential family (EF) parameter. By assuming that Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters, the authors demonstrate that the integrals required for proximal gradient descent can be calculated with sufficient precision. This, combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), yields a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for calculating the maximum likelihood estimator (MLE). The authors consider both convex and strongly convex cases, leading to different types of guarantees: the former on the likelihood error and the latter on the parameter error. A simple experiment is also included to demonstrate the theory.

Overall, this is a high-quality piece of work. The approach is well-motivated as an alternative to relying on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear solid, although I have not checked the proofs in detail. The proofs essentially involve combining the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al. While the paper combines existing techniques, the combination is nontrivial.

I appreciated the authors' honesty in discussing the limitations of their approach. The conclusion provides a thoughtful analysis of the weaknesses of the approach, the pessimism of the theoretical assumptions, and potential directions for future work. The paper also offers insight into CD-type algorithms, even though the analyzed algorithm is not quite contrastive divergence.

The introduction is well-written, with substantial references to previous work. However, there were a few issues with the clarity of the technical presentation. A notable omission is the explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex. Specifically, the 2nd and 3rd terms in equation 1 are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(θ) follows from the fact that the Hessian of A is the covariance of the sufficient statistics, which is positive semi-definite (PSD). While this may seem minor, the entire paper relies on the convexity of f(θ), and discussing it would help guide the reader through the paper's logic. A clear summary of the assumptions made in the paper would also be beneficial. For example, the assumption that the parameter set Θ must be convex is never mentioned in the main paper.

Overall, I found the paper to be an original and substantial contribution. However, the results seem to be of limited practical value due to the extreme difficulty of proving fast mixing of MCMC. In a sense, the paper has replaced one hard problem (finding the MLE) with another (finding a fast-mixing Markov chain). Nevertheless, being able to convert one problem into another is often useful, and having the paper's results available to the community is valuable.
