- Paper proposes a computationally efficient MLE approximation for exponential families using the fast-mixing MCMC assumption to approximate intractable integrals.
- Combines MCMC mixing assumption, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al. (2011) to yield a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme.
- Provides convergence guarantees: bounds on likelihood error for convex case and parameter error for strongly convex case.
- Includes a simple experiment demonstrating the theoretical findings.
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution that avoids restrictive small-treewidth assumptions.
- Theoretical analysis appears robust, though the reviewer did not fully verify all proofs.
- The paper skillfully integrates established techniques and offers insights related to CD-type algorithms.
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, potential conservativeness of assumptions, and future research directions.
- Strength: novelty and significance in offering a new perspective on approximating MLE without structural model assumptions.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor with clear convergence results.
- Strength: honest discussion of limitations and clear motivation and context.
- Weakness: technical clarity could be improved—explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood and justification for A(θ) convexity.
- Weakness: core assumptions (e.g., Θ convexity) should be summarized explicitly in the main text.
- Weakness: practical impact limited by difficulty of verifying the fast-mixing property of MCMC, effectively shifting one hard problem to another.
- Specific edits: several minor typos and a suggested rephrasing for a confusing sentence (l311-312).
- Recommendation: accept, given the substantial theoretical contribution despite practical challenges.
