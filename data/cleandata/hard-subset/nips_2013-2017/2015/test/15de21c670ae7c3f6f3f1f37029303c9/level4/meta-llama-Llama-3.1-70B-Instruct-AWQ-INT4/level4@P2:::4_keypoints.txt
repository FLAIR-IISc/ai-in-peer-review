- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set to guarantee fast MCMC convergence in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.
- Proves that for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients approximates the ML solution within the fast-mixing set with high probability.
- Provides a thorough analysis of convergence rates and derives bounds that relate learning rates to mixing times.
- Builds on prior MCMC-based expectation approximability work but introduces the novel "fast-mixing" tractable model class and tighter convergence analysis.
- Strength: Innovative approach for maximum likelihood learning in intractable/high-treewidth models.
- Strength: Rigorous and detailed convergence-rate analysis.
- Strength: Demonstrates effectiveness of the algorithm in approximating the ML solution.
- Weakness: Theorem 7 (lower bound) is difficult to interpret and needs expanded discussion.
- Weakness: The paper’s broader significance and potential impact (extensions, practical use) could be clarified further.
- Quality: Results appear meaningful and useful, though the lower-bound clarity reduces interpretability.
- Clarity: Presentation and analyses are clear and thorough.
- Originality: Framing and theoretical results are original.
- Recommendation: Accept, provided authors address the lower-bound explanation and clarify significance.
