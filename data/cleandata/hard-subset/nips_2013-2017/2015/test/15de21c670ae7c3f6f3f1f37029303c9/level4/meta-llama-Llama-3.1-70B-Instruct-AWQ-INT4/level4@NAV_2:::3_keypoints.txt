- Paper studies "fast-mixing parameter" sets where MCMC provably mixes quickly for tractable maximum likelihood learning.  
- Proves that for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, gradient descent using sampled gradients approximates the MLE inside the fast-mixing set with high probability.  
- Establishes a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE restricted to fast-mixing parameters.  
- Relates to prior work (pseudolikelihood, score matching, composite likelihoods) and highlights their drawbacks (worse sample complexity, model misspecification assumptions).  
- Provides thorough background on mixing times and fast-mixing parameter sets, with clear theorem statements and proofs.  
- Offers a rigorous analysis of gradient descent convergence with noisy/estimated gradients.  
- Main strength: significant theoretical advance in understanding MLE with MCMC and convergence guarantees.  
- Main weakness: practical relevance is limited because enforcing the fast-mixing parameter constraint is difficult in real problems.  
- Authors acknowledge the practical limitation but do not propose concrete solutions to ensure parameters lie in the fast-mixing set.  
- Writing and structure are good, but clarity could be improved with more explanations and examples.  
- Technical quality is high (8/10), clarity moderate (7/10), originality strong (8/10), overall significance limited by practicality (6/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous convergence analysis, significant theoretical contribution, well-structured and clearly written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical relevance, hard-to-satisfy assumptions, lack of methods to enforce fast-mixing constraints in practice.
