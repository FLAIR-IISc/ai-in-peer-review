- Proposes a novel approach to computationally efficient MLE in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters.  
- Shows that under the fast-mixing assumption, integrals for projected gradient descent can be approximated precisely enough to yield a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Analyzes both convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood-error guarantees for the former and parameter-error guarantees for the latter.  
- Theoretical analysis combines MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and results from Schmidt et al. (2011); proofs appear solid though not checked in detail.  
- Provides a simple experiment demonstrating the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and offers insight into CD-type algorithms, though the analyzed algorithm is not strictly CD.  
- Authoritatively discusses limitations and potential pessimism in theoretical assumptions, and suggests directions for future work.  
- Main practical limitation: proving fast mixing for MCMC is very challenging, so the work trades one hard problem (MLE) for another (designing/guaranteeing fast-mixing chains).  
- Technical clarity issues: paper fails to explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood and should summarize assumptions more clearly (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ not explicitly stated).  
- Several minor writing/typo errors (specific line corrections provided) and one nonsensical sentence noted around l311–312.
