- Paper addresses weight learning in Markov networks and the challenge of computing gradients via MCMC due to NP-hardness.
- Derives theoretical results showing that fast MCMC mixing on current parameters yields an FPRAS for weight learning.
- Analyzes two cases separately: unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex), with distinct bounds for each.
- Proofs appear plausible and the theoretical framework is clearly and well-structured, though some typos exist.
- Main strength: rigorous analysis of conditions under which MCMC can be used to compute gradients.
- Main weakness: the parameter conditions required by the theorems are practically impossible to ensure.
- The authors acknowledge limited practical relevance but do not provide a clear method to enforce the required mixing conditions.
- Enforcing the condition artificially could act as a form of regularization and combined with ridge regression may lead to over-regularization.
- Theoretical results are impressive but currently have little to no practical applicability without additional work.
- Significant non-trivial research problems remain before the results can yield a practical and accurate weight-learning algorithm.
- Recommendation: accept the paper but urge authors to address practical relevance and propose ways to enforce the required conditions.
- Overall: formalizes when MCMC-based gradient estimation gives an FPRAS, but practicality and enforcement of assumptions are unresolved.
