

The primary concern is the manuscript's insufficient engagement with the established literature on stochastic approximation methods for intractable likelihood optimization. The proposed algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM framework and the broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics, which have been extensively studied to address intractable likelihoods. A well-developed theoretical foundation already exists for these methods, and the authors must explicitly compare their approach to prior work. Crucially, the existing theory encompasses significantly more general scenarios—such as relaxing the requirement for uniformly ergodic Markov chains and eliminating the need for reinitializing the chain at a fixed distribution *r* at each iteration. While the authors acknowledge their reinitialization simplifies analysis, this choice is insufficient justification; reinitializing with the previous sample (as noted in the manuscript's conclusion) represents a more effective strategy and has been rigorously analyzed in prior work. The presentation of results is suboptimal: for Theorems 1 and 2, the assumptions are not clearly stated, forcing readers to infer them from surrounding text. Furthermore, the strong assumptions underpinning results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; general results covering these cases are available (e.g., in Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret). Overall, the authors' lack of awareness of this literature renders their contributions only marginally novel.
