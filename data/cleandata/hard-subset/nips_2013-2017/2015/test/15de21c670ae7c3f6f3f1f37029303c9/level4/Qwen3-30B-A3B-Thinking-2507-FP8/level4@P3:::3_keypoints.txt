- Addresses MLE in high-treewidth undirected models by restricting parameters to "fast-mixing" sets where MCMC mixes rapidly.  
- Extends beyond graphical models to the general exponential family, increasing applicability.  
- Main theoretical contribution 1: proves gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients gives an FPRAS when parameters lie in a fast-mixing set.  
- Main theoretical contribution 2: derives precise computational complexity bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.  
- Complexity results: unregularized log-likelihood ε-accuracy in O(1/ε^3) total effort; ridge-regularized parameter ε-accuracy in O(1/ε^2) effort.  
- Situates work relative to prior foundations (Jerrum & Sinclair) and contrasts with tree-based tractable approaches like Chow-Liu.  
- Engages relevant MCMC/fast-mixing literature (e.g., Liu & Domke; Domke & Liu) and recent MCMC-based learning methods.  
- Paper quality: technically rigorous proofs, well-motivated results, and careful mathematical derivations.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: excellent exposition, clear notation, effective figures, and sufficient detail for experts to reproduce.  
- Originality: introduces a novel tractability notion based on fast-mixing rather than structural restrictions (e.g., trees).  
- Significance: primarily theoretical — establishes when MCMC-based learning is provably sound, but limited immediate practical impact.  
- Main weakness: enforcing or ensuring fast-mixing parameter constraints in practice is challenging and not sufficiently explored.  
- Review error noted: the draft review incorrectly labeled the ridge-regularized case as non-convex, undermining that criticism.  
- Recommendation: accept as a strong theoretical contribution advancing understanding of MCMC-based maximum likelihood learning.
