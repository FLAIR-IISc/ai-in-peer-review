- Core idea: restrict parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC gives efficient inference and enables theoretical guarantees for gradient-based maximum likelihood learning.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proof of a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood within the fast-mixing set.  
- Results apply broadly to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.  
- Mathematical treatment and theorems are rigorous and clearly stated, with a helpful illustrative example.  
- Paper situates its work relative to classical approximation results (e.g., Jerrum and Sinclair, 1993).  
- Weak literature engagement: insufficient discussion of stochastic approximation and Monte Carlo EM bodies of work that are relevant.  
- Missing connection to broader theoretical frameworks (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) that may subsume or relate to the results.  
- Methodological assumptions (uniform ergodicity, chain re-initialization) need stronger justification and discussion of alternatives (e.g., reusing previous samples).  
- Clarity issue: assumptions are not made explicit within each theorem statement, forcing readers to cross-reference earlier text.  
- Novelty concern: limited comparison to prior work leaves the degree of advancement over existing techniques unclear.  
- Significance: provides theoretical guarantees for a widely used practical approach (MCMC-based gradient descent).  
- Acceptance arguments: strong theoretical guarantee (FPRAS), broad applicability, tackles a hard inference problem, and offers clear math.  
- Rejection arguments: inadequate literature coverage, unclear novelty relative to prior results, and presentation could better highlight assumptions.
