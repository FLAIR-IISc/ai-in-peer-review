- Paper proposes tractability notion for exponential families based on fast-mixing MCMC and an algorithm for ML learning with arbitrary treewidth using fast-mixing parameter sets.
- Algorithm uses MCMC to approximate gradients and comes with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Main contribution: a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme for ML learning in exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.
- Authors prove high-probability convergence of estimated parameters to true parameters.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a thorough analysis of algorithm convergence.
- Concern: novelty is unclear because the algorithm is closely related to Monte Carlo EM and other stochastic approximation methods with Markovian dynamics.
- Authors should compare to the well-developed literature on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation and clarify differences and advantages.
- Presentation issues: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated within the theorems and require reading surrounding text.
- Authors should provide more context and discussion of the implications of their results.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Originality 6/10, Clarity 8/10, Impact 7/10.
- Acceptance arguments: novel algorithm, theoretical guarantees, and strong writing; Rejection arguments: limited novelty, presentation weaknesses, and insufficient literature comparison.
