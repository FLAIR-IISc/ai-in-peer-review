- Paper studies weight learning in Markov networks using MCMC to approximate expectations for likelihood gradient computation.
- Main contribution: theoretical guarantees that MCMC-based learning is an FPRAS when parameters lie in a rapid-mixing regime.
- Authors analyze two settings separately: unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex), providing distinct bounds.
- Paper is generally well written with minor typos; proofs appear sound though not exhaustively checked by reviewer.
- Primary concern: practical relevance is limited because rapid-mixing conditions are hard to verify, maintain, or approximate in realistic models.
- Reviewer asks whether fast-mixing constraints can be enforced during learning (e.g., via projections, penalties on strong couplings, or weight constraints).
- Reviewer questions how enforcing fast-mixing would interact with ridge regularization and whether it could cause over-regularization.
- Reviewer requests tractable, a priori certifiable sufficient conditions or diagnostics to monitor during optimization to avoid leaving the fast-mixing regime.
- If exact certification is intractable, reviewer asks for practical proxies or empirical tests to adaptively guide the algorithm.
- Addressing these feasibility and enforcement issues would substantially increase the paper’s impact; as is, the central assumption limits applicability.
