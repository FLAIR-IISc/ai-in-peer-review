- Summary: Analyzes projected gradient descent for MLE in exponential families constrained to a fast-mixing parameter set Θ, proving high-probability convergence when gradients are approximated by MCMC and giving explicit schedules for iterations, samples, and chain length.  
- Relation to prior work: Complements pseudolikelihood/score-matching and structure-restricted MLE approaches and connects to practical MCMC/contrastive divergence methods under controlled mixing.  
- Main theoretical results: Lipschitz gradient bound, explicit K/M/v schedules, high-probability convergence for convex and strongly convex cases, and near-optimal lower bounds up to logs.  
- Complexity rates: Unregularized objective accuracy ε requires total sampling ≈ O(ε−3); with ridge regularization (strong convexity) parameter accuracy ε requires ≈ O(ε−2) sampling (up to logs).  
- Applicability: Applies to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics and to known fast-mixing constructions (e.g., bounded-interaction Ising models).  
- Projection: Projection onto Θ is feasible in key examples (clipping, convex optimization), but cost is not included in complexity accounting.  
- Strength — framework: Novel, clear formulation of tractability via fast-mixing parameter sets rather than graph structure.  
- Strength — guarantees: Strong, high-probability convergence guarantees with explicit dependence on α, C, and ε and accompanying lower bounds.  
- Strength — generality: Results are broadly stated beyond specific graphical models to general exponential families with bounded statistics.  
- Strength — connections: Concrete ties to known fast-mixing regimes and practical projection procedures.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Requires known mixing constants (C, α) and efficient projection onto Θ, which can be hard to verify or compute in large models.  
- Weakness — experiments: Minimal empirical validation (single small Ising example) with conservative constants, limiting insight into practical tightness and scaling.  
- Weakness — Theorem 7: Lower-bound statement is hard to interpret and needs more intuition, special cases, or worked examples for clarity.  
- Weakness — sampling practice gap: Theory assumes independent samples each iteration and does not cover persistent/pool sampling commonly used in practice.  
- Weakness — computation accounting: Projection costs are excluded; paper lacks guidance on when projection is negligible versus dominant.  
- Quality: Technical development appears careful and sound, using appropriate concentration and inexact-gradient convex optimization tools.  
- Clarity: Generally well written and organized, but Theorem 7 and centralized statement of assumptions (bounded t(x), projection oracle, known mixing bounds) need improvement.  
- Originality: Novel idea of constraining to fast-mixing parameter sets for provable MCMC-based MLE and the combination of upper/lower bounds with explicit schedules.  
- Significance: Theoretically fills a gap by giving provable guarantees for MCMC-based learning under verifiable mixing assumptions; most impact on theory and tractable model classes.  
- Suggestions: Add intuition and special cases for Theorem 7; provide guidance on estimating/bounding (C, α) and R2; clarify projection costs for common Θ; discuss persistent sampling extensions; include more experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Conceptually novel, general framework with clear theoretical guarantees and near-optimal complexity bounds.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical applicability limited by need to certify mixing and efficient projection, plus limited empirical evidence and opaque lower-bound presentation.  
- Recommendation: Accept — a well-executed, original theoretical contribution likely to stimulate further work on tractable, learnable model classes and mixing certification.
