- Presents a novel approach for computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families using fast-mixing MCMC parameter sets.  
- Assumes rapid MCMC mixing for all parameters in a constrained set Θ to approximate gradient integrals for gradient descent.  
- Combines MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al. (2011) to yield an FPRAS for ML within Θ.  
- Provides complementary guarantees: likelihood-error bounds for convex case and parameter-distance bounds for strongly convex case.  
- Simple empirical demonstration supports the theoretical findings.  
- Argues fast-mixing parameter sets as a compelling alternative to treewidth-based approaches.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound and non-trivial, though proofs were not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Introduction is well-written with good references but lacks an explicit statement that A(θ) is convex.  
- Omits explicit statement that Θ is convex, which is essential for projection and optimization guarantees.  
- Authors honestly discuss limitations: projection computational cost, arbitrary MCMC initialization, and possibly conservative theoretical assumptions.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast mixing for a given parameter set is difficult, exchanging one hard problem for another.  
- Discusses connections to contrastive divergence, offering theoretical insights even without directly analyzing that algorithm.  
- Overall judgment: substantial, original theoretical contribution deserving consideration for acceptance, pending minor clarifications.
