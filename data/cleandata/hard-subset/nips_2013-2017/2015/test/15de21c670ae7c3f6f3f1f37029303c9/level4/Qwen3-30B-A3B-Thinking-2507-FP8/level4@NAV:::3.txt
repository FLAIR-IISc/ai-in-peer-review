

A key challenge in Markov networks is weight learning, defined as optimizing feature weights to maximize likelihood. Gradient descent is commonly employed, with the critical sub-task being gradient computation. As exact gradient calculation is NP-hard in general, MCMC algorithms are typically used as a practical alternative.

The paper establishes theoretical results demonstrating that if weights ensure rapid MCMC convergence to the stationary distribution, the weight learning algorithm (using MCMC for gradient computation) achieves a FPRAS. Separate bounds are derived for the unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases.

The paper is generally well-written, though it contains minor typos. While I could not verify all proofs, they appear plausible. Although the theoretical results are impressive, they lack practical relevance (a point the authors acknowledge). It is virtually impossible to guarantee the parameter constraints required by the theorems; have the authors considered enforcing these conditions artificially? Noting that such constraints resemble regularization, combining them with ridge regression could lead to over-regularized models. Resolving this issue would significantly strengthen the paper.

Overall, the paper provides a clear formalization of conditions under which MCMC-based weight learning (with and without regularization) yields a FPRAS. However, the required conditions are of little practical utility. Substantial research challenges must be addressed before these results can yield a practical or accurate weight learning algorithm.
