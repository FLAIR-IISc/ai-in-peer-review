- Introduces a novel "fast-mixing parameter sets" framework restricting parameters so MCMC inference mixes rapidly for maximum likelihood in exponential families.  
- Contrasts this approach with traditional tractable sets (e.g., Chow-Liu trees) and methods like pseudolikelihood/score matching that fail under misspecification or keep inference intractable.  
- Generalizes prior mixing-time results (e.g., Domke & Liu 2013) to general exponential families, not just Ising models.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for gradient descent using MCMC-approximated gradients with explicit convergence rates.  
- Builds on foundational mixing-time analyses and applies results (e.g., Schmidt et al.) to handle noisy gradients in learning contexts.  
- Theoretical results are technically sound with rigorous convergence bounds for both unregularized (Theorems 2–5) and ridge-regularized cases (Theorems 6–9).  
- Main technical concern: Theorem 7’s lower bound uses abstract parameters (a, b, c, α) lacking clear practical interpretation.  
- Suggests expanding discussion to relate those parameters to model traits (interaction strengths, graph density) to improve practical relevance.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear: consistent notation, logical flow, effective examples, and intuitive proof sketches.  
- Original framing is novel—applying mixing-time constraints to learning is distinct from contrastive divergence or pseudolikelihood and extends beyond graphical models.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: first guarantees for MCMC-based maximum likelihood in high-treewidth/intractable models, though immediate practical impact may be limited.  
- Acceptance recommendation: accept — novel, rigorous, fills a gap; minor revisions needed on practical interpretation and concrete application discussion.
