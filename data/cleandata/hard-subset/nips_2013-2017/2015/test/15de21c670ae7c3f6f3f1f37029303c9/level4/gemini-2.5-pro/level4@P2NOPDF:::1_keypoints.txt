- Limited engagement with extensive literature on stochastic approximation and Monte Carlo methods for intractable likelihood optimization.  
- Proposed algorithm closely resembles MCEM and falls into well-established stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.  
- Authors must compare to existing approaches to clearly delineate novel contributions.  
- Theoretical analysis overlooks established, more general results in prior work.  
- Assuming uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains is unnecessarily strong and not typically required.  
- Re-initializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is a practical and theoretical limitation.  
- Warm-starting (using the previous iteration’s sample) is a more efficient, well-studied alternative.  
- Theorems (e.g., Theorems 1 and 2) should be self-contained, explicitly stating all assumptions within each statement.  
- Some assumptions (e.g., those for Theorem 6) are stronger than necessary; similar results exist under weaker conditions.  
- Due to weak literature connection and marginal novelty, the manuscript needs major revision to establish significance.
