## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission

**Summary:** This paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to computationally efficient maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) within exponential families. Obtaining the MLE is generally intractable, with the primary computational bottleneck being the evaluation of integrals required for proximal gradient descent. The authors circumvent this challenge by leveraging the assumption of fast-mixing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) for all parameter values within the allowed set. This allows them to approximate the required integrals to sufficient precision, and, when combined with the randomized approximation scheme of Schmidt et al. (2011), yields a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for computing the MLE. The work provides convergence guarantees for both convex and strongly convex cases, resulting in bounds on likelihood error and parameter error, respectively. A simple experiment is included to demonstrate the theoretical findings.

**Overall Assessment:** This is a high-quality contribution. The proposed method offers a compelling alternative to approaches reliant on restrictive small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analysis appears robust, although a complete verification of all proofs was beyond the scope of this review. The core of the work lies in a non-trivial combination of established techniques – the MCMC mixing assumption, concentration inequalities, and the results of Schmidt et al. – skillfully integrated to address a fundamental problem in statistical inference. 

I particularly appreciated the authors’ candid discussion of the approach's limitations. The conclusion thoughtfully analyzes potential weaknesses, identifies scenarios where the theoretical assumptions might be overly conservative, and suggests promising avenues for future research. Furthermore, the paper offers valuable insights into the broader landscape of contrastive divergence (CD)-type algorithms, despite analyzing a slightly different procedure.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty and Significance:** The paper provides a new perspective on approximating MLE in exponential families, moving away from reliance on structural assumptions about the model.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The analysis appears mathematically sound, building upon well-established results and offering clear convergence guarantees.
*   **Honest Discussion of Limitations:** The authors acknowledge the practical challenges associated with verifying the MCMC mixing assumption and thoughtfully discuss its implications.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The paper effectively establishes the context and motivation for the proposed approach, referencing relevant prior work.

**Weaknesses & Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   **Technical Clarity:** While the introduction is well-written, certain aspects of the technical presentation could benefit from increased clarity. Specifically, the paper would be strengthened by explicitly stating that the log-likelihood function is convex. The convexity of the second and third terms in equation (1) is readily apparent, and the convexity of A(θ) follows directly from the positive semi-definiteness of its Hessian (which represents the covariance of the sufficient statistics). Highlighting this convexity upfront would better guide the reader through the logic of the paper.
*   **Assumption Summary:** A clear and concise summary of the paper's core assumptions would enhance readability. Notably, the requirement that the parameter set Θ must be convex is not explicitly stated in the main text.
*   **Practical Value:** While theoretically significant, the practical impact of this work may be limited by the difficulty of verifying the fast-mixing property of MCMC. The paper essentially transforms one challenging problem (MLE computation) into another (MCMC mixing analysis). However, framing it as such – and making this transformation available to the research community – remains a valuable contribution.

**Specific Comments:**

*   l185: “w here” should be “where”
*   l192: “this paper that” should be “this paper assumes that”
*   l207: “simple example fast-mixing” should be “simple example of a fast-mixing”
*   l281: “of of” should be “of”
*   l311-312: The sentence "An novel approach to developing conditions under which finding the (approximate) MLE is tractable. Well-written, though of seemingly limited practical value." is somewhat nonsensical and could be rephrased for clarity. For example: "This work presents a novel approach to making MLE computation tractable under certain conditions, offering theoretical insights despite potential limitations in practical application."

**Recommendation:** I recommend accepting this paper. While proving fast mixing of MCMC remains a significant challenge, the theoretical contributions of this work are substantial and represent a valuable addition to the literature on approximate inference in exponential families. The honest assessment of limitations and the clear articulation of future research directions further enhance the paper's merit.
