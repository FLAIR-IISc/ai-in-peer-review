- Manuscript shows limited engagement with relevant prior work, especially Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics.  
- Authors’ algorithm strongly resembles existing Monte Carlo EM / stochastic approximation methods and needs explicit comparison.  
- A substantial body of literature already provides theoretical frameworks covering more general scenarios than presented.  
- The uniform ergodicity requirement for Markov chains is unnecessary according to existing work.  
- Re-initializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is unjustified and used only for analytical simplification.  
- Re-initializing from the previous sample is a preferable, well-studied alternative the authors should consider.  
- Presentation is unclear: assumptions for Theorem 1 and Theorem 2 are scattered and should be explicitly stated.  
- Strong assumptions in results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) exist.  
- Overall lack of awareness of existing literature leads to contributions that are only marginally novel.
