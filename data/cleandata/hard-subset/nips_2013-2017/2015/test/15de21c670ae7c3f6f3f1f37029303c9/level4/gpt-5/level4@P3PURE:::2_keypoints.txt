- Proposes a novel approach to computationally efficient MLE in exponential families by using fast-mixing MCMC to estimate integrals for proximal gradient descent, yielding an FPRAS under assumptions.  
- Analyzes both convex and strongly convex cases, providing likelihood error bounds (convex) and parameter error bounds (strongly convex).  
- Assumes MCMC is fast-mixing for all admissible parameters—a strong and hard-to-verify requirement driving the results.  
- Theoretical analysis combines fast-mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al. (2011) in a nontrivial way; proofs appear solid.  
- Offers insight into CD-type algorithms, though the analyzed algorithm is not exactly contrastive divergence.  
- Provides a simple experiment to illustrate the theoretical findings.  
- Strength: well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions for tractable learning.  
- Strength: paper is candid about limitations and discusses pessimistic assumptions and future directions.  
- Strength: introduction is clear and well-referenced.  
- Main weakness: practical impact is limited because proving fast mixing across all parameters is extremely challenging, effectively trading one hard problem for another.  
- Clarity issue: should explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood (A(θ) convex because its Hessian is a covariance).  
- Presentation issue: provide a consolidated summary of all assumptions (e.g., that parameter set Θ is convex).  
- Minor typos to fix: l185 “,w here”→“, where”; l192 “this paper that”→“this paper assumes that”; l207 “simple example fast-mixing”→“simple example of a fast-mixing”; l281 “of of”→“of”; l311–312: sentence nonsensical—revise.  
- Overall: high-quality, original contribution combining existing techniques meaningfully, but with limited practical value due to the difficulty of verifying the key fast-mixing assumption.
