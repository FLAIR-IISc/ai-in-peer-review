- Presents a novel method giving a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for MLE in exponential families by approximating proximal-gradient expectations with fast-mixing MCMC.  
- Analyzes both convex and strongly convex settings: convex case yields likelihood-error bounds; strongly convex gives parameter-estimation bounds.  
- Technical contribution combines fast-mixing MCMC assumptions, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain nontrivial, useful guarantees.  
- Empirical illustration: a simple experiment supports the theoretical development.  
- Paper thoughtfully discusses limitations, potential pessimism of assumptions, and concrete future directions.  
- Provides insight into contrastive-divergence-like behavior, though the analyzed algorithm is not exactly CD.  
- Main practical limitation: the strong fast-mixing assumption is hard to verify or guarantee in practice, trading one hard problem for another.  
- Reviewers found the analyses sound overall, though proofs were not checked in full detail.  
- Clarity issues: explicitly state convexity of the objective early and summarize all assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Θ) in one place.  
- Suggest adding an intuitive discussion of why fast mixing across the entire parameter set might hold or how to enforce/diagnose it in practice.  
- Minor typos and wording fixes needed (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312).
