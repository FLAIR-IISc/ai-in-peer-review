- Addresses MLE in high-treewidth undirected graphical models where exact inference is intractable.  
- Proposes restricting parameters to "fast-mixing sets" so MCMC converges rapidly instead of using tree-structured constraints.  
- Proves gradient descent with MCMC gradient estimates is an FPRAS under fast-mixing constraints for both unregularized and ridge-regularized cases.  
- Complexity: unregularized case requires effort cubic in 1/ε; ridge regularization reduces this to quadratic in 1/ε.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates itself appropriately within exponential family and prior tractable-inference work.  
- Formalization of fast-mixing parameter sets and connections to established mixing time bounds is strong.  
- Proofs are technical but appear sound and logically derived from mixing assumptions.  
- Main contribution is the rigorous theoretical guarantee that MCMC-based learning can be made an FPRAS under fast-mixing constraints.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge the limited practical relevance of enforcing fast-mixing constraints.  
- Paper fails to adequately address how to enforce fast-mixing constraints in practice during optimization.  
- Unclear interaction between enforcing constraints (e.g., projection/clipping) and ridge regularization; potential for over-regularization not analyzed.  
- Does not explore adaptive enforcement strategies (e.g., adjusting β) or alternative, more feasible fast-mixing guarantees.  
- Empirical example uses artificial constraints (|θ_ij| ≤ 0.2) and does not show how such constraints could be derived from data.  
- Overall: technically sound and important theoretically, but practical enforcement of fast-mixing constraints is unresolved and limits impact.
