Comments on Quality:
- The paper provides rates of convergence for learning algorithms expressed in terms of mixing times. The bounds appear meaningful and useful.
- The only significant concern is that the lower bound (Theorem 7) is difficult to interpret. An expanded discussion of this theorem—and, more generally, of the lower bounds applicable to the problem—would be very helpful.

Comments on Clarity:
- The presentation is clear.

Comments on Originality:
- Both the framing of the problem (in terms of the “fast mixing” model class) and the theoretical results appear original.

Comments on Significance:
- The significance is somewhat unclear to the reviewer. That said, the paper seems to fill a gap in the literature on learning intractable models using MCMC and is therefore likely to be significant.
- The submission would benefit from additional discussion of the work’s potential impact, including future extensions and opportunities for others to build on it.
- The paper introduces a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations. The theoretical results providing convergence rates for learning these models appear novel and interesting, and the submission should therefore be accepted.
