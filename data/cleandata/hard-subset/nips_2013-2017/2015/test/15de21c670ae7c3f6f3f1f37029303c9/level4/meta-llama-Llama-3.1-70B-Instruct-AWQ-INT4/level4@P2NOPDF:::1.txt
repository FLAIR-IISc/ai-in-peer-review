The manuscript's primary issue is its apparent lack of engagement with the existing literature on the topic. The authors' algorithm bears a strong resemblance to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, as well as the broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics that have been proposed in various forms to optimize intractable likelihood functions. A well-developed body of literature exists on this subject, and the authors would benefit from comparing their work to these established methods.

Furthermore, a theoretical framework that encompasses some of the authors' results already exists, and it covers more general scenarios than the one presented in the manuscript. For instance, the Markov chains in this framework do not require uniform ergodicity, and there is no need to reinitialize the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r at each iteration. Although the authors acknowledge that they reinitialize the chain to simplify their analysis, this approach is not sufficient. In fact, reinitializing with the previous sample, as discussed at the end of the manuscript, may be a more effective strategy, and this approach has been extensively analyzed in previous studies.

The presentation of the results could also be improved. For example, the assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated. While the reader can refer to the surrounding text for clarification, it would be more effective to explicitly state these assumptions within the theorems themselves. Additionally, the strong assumptions used to develop results such as those in Theorem 6 are not necessary, as more general results have been established by authors such as Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret.

Overall, the manuscript appears to lack awareness of the existing literature, and the results presented are only marginally novel.
