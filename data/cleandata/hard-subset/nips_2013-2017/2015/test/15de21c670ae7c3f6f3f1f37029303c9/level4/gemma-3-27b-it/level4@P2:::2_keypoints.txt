- Proposes a novel approach for computationally efficient MLE in exponential families by constraining parameters to a fast-mixing MCMC set, yielding a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS).
- Leverages stochastic gradient descent results (Schmidt et al., 2011) and carefully analyzes error from approximating gradients with MCMC samples.
- Considers both convex and strongly convex cases, providing likelihood-error guarantees and parameter-distance guarantees respectively.
- Includes a practical example to illustrate the theoretical results.
- Well-motivated as an alternative to restrictive treewidth-based methods (e.g., Chow-Liu).
- Theoretical analyses appear solid, though the reviewer could not verify all proofs in detail.
- Core contribution is the non-trivial combination of fast-mixing MCMC assumptions, concentration inequalities, and optimization results.
- Paper candidly discusses limitations, possibly over-pessimistic assumptions, and outlines promising future research directions.
- Provides useful insights related to contrastive divergence (CD)-type algorithms, despite focusing on a different sampling strategy.
- Introduction is well-written and places the work in context with relevant references.
- Technical presentation needs clearer exposition; the paper omits an explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex and should more prominently summarize core assumptions (e.g., convex parameter set Θ).
- Practical applicability may be limited because proving MCMC fast-mixing is difficult in real problems.
- The paper reframes MLE into the problem of proving fast-mixing, which shifts but does not eliminate a fundamental difficulty—still a valuable perspective.
- Reviewer identified minor clarity issues and copyediting errors (several line-specific corrections suggested).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretically-grounded approach, well-motivated, honest about limitations, non-trivial technical combination, addresses a fundamental inference problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical impact due to hard-to-verify fast-mixing assumption, reliance on a challenging prerequisite, and minor technical clarity problems.
