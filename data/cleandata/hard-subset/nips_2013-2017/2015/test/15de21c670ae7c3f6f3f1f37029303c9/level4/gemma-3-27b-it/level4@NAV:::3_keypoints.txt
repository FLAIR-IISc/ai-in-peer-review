- Weight learning in Markov networks is challenging; gradient computation is often NP-hard and typically approximated using MCMC.  
- The paper proves that if weights ensure rapid MCMC mixing, then MCMC-based weight learning yields an FPRAS.  
- Separate analyses and bounds are provided for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) scenarios.  
- The manuscript is clearly written, with only minor typographical errors.  
- Proofs appear logically sound, though the reviewer did not fully verify every detail.  
- Theoretical results are significant but have limited practical applicability, a limitation acknowledged by the authors.  
- Major concern: the practicality of enforcing the parameter constraints required by the theorems is questionable.  
- Reviewer suggests exploring methods to artificially enforce those conditions (e.g., additional regularization), noting potential over-regularization when combined with ridge regression.  
- Addressing enforcement of conditions would substantially strengthen the paper.  
- Overall: well-structured formal characterization of FPRAS conditions for MCMC-based weight learning, but more work is needed to make it practically viable and accurate.
