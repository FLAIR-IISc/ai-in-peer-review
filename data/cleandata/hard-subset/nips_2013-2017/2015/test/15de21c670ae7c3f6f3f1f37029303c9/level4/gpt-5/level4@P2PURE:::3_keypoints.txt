- Problem context: Weight learning in Markov networks uses gradient descent, but gradient computation is NP-hard so MCMC is often used as an estimator.  
- Main theoretical result: If MCMC mixes rapidly for the given parameters, then using MCMC to estimate the gradient yields an FPRAS for weight learning.  
- Separate analyses: The paper treats unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) settings separately, deriving distinct bounds for each.  
- Writing and proofs: Paper is generally well written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Practical relevance concern: The required rapid-mixing condition is practically hard or impossible to guarantee for real models.  
- Enforceability question: Reviewer asks whether the condition can be enforced artificially and notes it effectively acts as a form of regularization.  
- Over-regularization risk: Combining the mixing condition with ridge regression may lead to over-regularized, less accurate models.  
- Overall assessment: The paper formalizes when MCMC-based learning is an FPRAS, but the key condition limits practical applicability and further research is needed to make it usable.
