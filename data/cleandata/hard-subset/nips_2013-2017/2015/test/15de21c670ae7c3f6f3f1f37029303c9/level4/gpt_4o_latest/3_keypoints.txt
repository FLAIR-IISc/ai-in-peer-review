- Problem context: weight learning in Markov networks is hard because gradient computation is NP-hard, so MCMC is commonly used.
- Main claim: If MCMC rapidly mixes under the current parameters, then MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.
- Technical contribution: Theoretical analysis for two cases—unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex)—with different bounds derived.
- Writing quality: Paper is generally well-written with a few minor typographical errors.
- Proofs: Proofs appear plausible, though the reviewer did not fully verify all details.
- Practicality concern: The practical utility of the theoretical results is minimal to nonexistent as the required parameter conditions are impractical to ensure.
- Question raised: Can the rapid-mixing condition be enforced artificially, and would that act as an implicit regularizer?
- Risk noted: Combining the mixing-condition regularization with ridge regression may lead to over-regularization.
- Suggestion: Addressing how to enforce or mitigate the condition in practice could substantially strengthen the paper.
- Overall summary: The paper formalizes conditions for MCMC-based learning to be FPRAS but currently lacks practical relevance; non-trivial work is needed to make it a practical, accurate algorithm.
