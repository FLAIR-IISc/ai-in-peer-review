This paper addresses the challenge of maximum likelihood learning in intractable graphical models, such as those with high treewidth. Instead of relying on common structural restrictions (e.g., tree-structured models), the authors introduce a novel notion of a tractable parameter set: a "fast-mixing" set where Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference is guaranteed to converge rapidly. The central contribution is a formal analysis of the widely used practice of performing gradient descent with gradients approximated by MCMC samples. The authors prove that for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics constrained to a fast-mixing set, this procedure provides a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the maximum likelihood solution within that set. The paper provides explicit convergence rates for both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) optimization, detailing how the number of gradient steps, MCMC samples, and MCMC transitions should be scheduled to achieve a desired accuracy with high probability.

This is a strong theoretical paper that fills an important gap in the literature. The main strengths are:

*   **Originality:** The key conceptual contribution is framing tractability in terms of fast MCMC mixing rather than graphical structure. This is an original and insightful perspective that offers a new way to think about tractable learning in complex models.
*   **Quality:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for the common heuristic of using MCMC to approximate gradients in maximum likelihood learning. By analyzing this procedure as projected gradient descent with biased and noisy gradients, the work places a popular practical method on solid theoretical footing. The convergence bounds are meaningful and appear to be technically sound.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and the technical setup and results are presented clearly.

While the paper is of high quality, it could be strengthened in a couple of areas:

*   **Significance:** The theoretical significance of providing convergence guarantees for MCMC-based learning is clear. However, the paper could do more to articulate the work's potential practical impact. The discussion could be expanded to explore how these findings might guide practitioners (even if the bounds are conservative), what new research avenues this framework opens, and the potential for building upon these results.
*   **Clarity of Lower Bound:** My only significant technical complaint is that the lower bound presented in Theorem 7 is very difficult to interpret. The paper would be improved by an expanded discussion of this theorem, providing more intuition about its implications and perhaps placing it in the context of more general lower bounds for this class of problems.

Overall, the submission introduces a novel and valuable perspective on tractable learning by defining a class of models based on the approximability of expectations via MCMC. The theoretical results establishing convergence rates for learning within this class are novel and interesting. The paper makes a solid contribution, and I recommend acceptance.
