- Limited engagement with relevant literature; the method closely relates to Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics and should be compared to existing work.
- An established theoretical framework already covers many of the paper’s results and addresses more general scenarios.
- Unnecessary reinitialization of Markov chains from a fixed distribution r weakens the paper; prior work handles non-uniform ergodicity without this step.
- Initializing from the previous sample (instead of reinitializing) is a known alternative that has been extensively analyzed in the literature.
- Presentation issues: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated within the theorems, forcing readers to search the surrounding text.
- Theorem 6 and similar results rely on strong assumptions that appear unnecessary given existing results under weaker conditions (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret).
- Overall lack of awareness of prior work significantly diminishes the manuscript’s contribution and makes its novelty marginal.
