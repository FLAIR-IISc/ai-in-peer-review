I believe the primary issue with the manuscript is its apparent lack of engagement with the existing literature on the topic. The authors' algorithm bears a strong resemblance to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm, as well as the broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics that have been proposed in various forms to optimize intractable likelihood functions. A well-established body of literature exists on this subject, and the authors would benefit from comparing their approach to existing work. In fact, a theoretical framework that encompasses some of the authors' results already exists, and it covers a wider range of scenarios. For instance, the Markov chains in this framework do not require uniform ergodicity, and there is no need to reinitialize the Markov chain at a fixed distribution r at each iteration. While the authors acknowledge that they reinitialize the chain to simplify their analysis, this justification is insufficient. Moreover, reinitializing with the previous sample, as discussed towards the end of the manuscript, may be a more effective approach, and these algorithms have been extensively analyzed in previous studies.

The presentation of the results could also be improved. For example, the assumptions underlying Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated. Although the reader can refer to the preceding and following text, the necessary information should be readily available. Furthermore, the strong assumptions used to derive results such as those in Theorem 6 are unnecessary. More general results can be found in the work of Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret, for instance. Overall, I think the authors' lack of familiarity with the literature has resulted in contributions that are only marginally novel.
