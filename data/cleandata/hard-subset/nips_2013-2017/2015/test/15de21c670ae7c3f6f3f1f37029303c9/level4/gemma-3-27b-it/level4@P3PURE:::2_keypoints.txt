- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient ML learning approach for exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters and using proximal gradient descent to compute required integrals.  
- Combines MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and results of Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Provides separate analyses and guarantees for the convex case (likelihood error) and the strongly convex case (parameter error).  
- Includes a simple experiment that illustrates the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions and gives insight into CD-type algorithms (though the analyzed algorithm is not strictly contrastive divergence).  
- Theoretical analyses appear solid and represent a nontrivial combination of existing techniques.  
- Major limitation: practical value is limited because proving fast mixing of MCMC for all allowed parameters is typically difficult, effectively replacing one hard problem with another.  
- Authors honestly discuss limitations, potentially pessimistic assumptions, and directions for future work.  
- Technical presentation issues: the paper never explicitly states the convexity of the log-likelihood (A(θ) convexity should be noted since its Hessian is the covariance of sufficient statistics).  
- Key assumptions are not clearly summarized in the main text; e.g., convexity of the parameter set Θ is not explicitly stated.  
- Overall assessment: original and substantial contribution, but limited practical applicability due to the strong mixing assumption.
