The primary weakness of this manuscript is its limited engagement with the relevant literature. The proposed algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM (MCEM) algorithm and, more broadly, to the general class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics that have been developed for optimizing intractable likelihood functions. A well-developed body of literature exists on this topic, and the authors should compare their method to existing work.

An established theoretical framework already encompasses some of the paper's results and addresses much more general scenarios. For instance, existing literature handles cases where the Markov chains are not uniformly ergodic and does not require reinitializing the chain from a fixed distribution `r` at each iteration. The authors acknowledge this reinitialization is done to simplify their analysis, but this justification is insufficient. Moreover, alternatives such as initializing from the previous sample—which the authors mention as a potentially better approach—have already been extensively analyzed in prior work.

The presentation of the results is also suboptimal. For example, the assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated within the theorems themselves, forcing the reader to search the surrounding text to understand the conditions under which the results hold. Similarly, the strong assumptions used to develop results like those in Theorem 6 appear unnecessary, as more general results have been established under weaker conditions (see, for example, Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret).

In summary, the manuscript's lack of awareness of the extensive prior literature significantly diminishes its contribution, and as a result, the novelty of the work appears to be marginal.
