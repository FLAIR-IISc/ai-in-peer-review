- The proposed algorithm is a direct special case of Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics, so its novelty is unsubstantiated.  
- The manuscript fails to engage with foundational literature (e.g., Benveniste et al., 1990), which already provides comprehensive convergence theory.  
- The uniform ergodicity assumption is unnecessarily strong; existing theory proves convergence under weaker (non-uniform/geometric) ergodicity.  
- The authors’ fixed reinitialization of the Markov chain to a distribution r is an unjustified simplification and is inferior to adaptive reinitialization using the previous sample.  
- Prior work (e.g., Andrieu & Roberts, Douc et al.) shows adaptive reinitialization yields better theory and practice, which the manuscript ignores.  
- Theoretical results are poorly presented: assumptions are not clearly stated in theorems and must be inferred from surrounding text.  
- Theorems rely on restrictive conditions (e.g., in Theorem 6) that are not required by existing frameworks, making results redundant.  
- The algorithm is essentially a constrained MCEM variant; noting adaptive reinitialization is preferable but omitting it without comparison is a major oversight.  
- The manuscript omits comparisons to prior explicit error bounds and convergence rates for MCEM (e.g., Fessler & Hero; Wei & Tanner).  
- Technical execution is weak: Theorem 3’s proof sketch is incomplete, and Theorem 4 lacks clarity on dependence on problem-specific parameters like mixing time.  
- Empirical evaluation is inadequate—no comparisons to standard MCEM or adaptive reinitialization baselines, so claims of improvement are unsupported.  
- Overall, the paper shows a profound disconnect from the literature, uses artificial constraints without justification, and thus offers marginal contribution.  
- Required revisions: thoroughly compare to MCEM/stochastic approximation literature, adopt standard/adaptive reinitialization, and clearly state all theorem assumptions.
