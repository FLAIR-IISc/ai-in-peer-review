This paper explores an alternative notion of a tractable set, namely a set of “fast-mixing parameters” where Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference can be guaranteed to quickly converge to the stationary distribution. The authors prove that for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, when parameters are constrained to a fast-mixing set, gradient descent with gradients approximated by sampling will approximate the maximum likelihood solution inside the set with high-probability. 

The paper relates to previous work on maximum likelihood learning, including the pseudolikelihood, score matching, composite likelihoods, and other methods. However, these methods have some drawbacks, including inferior sample complexity to the likelihood and the assumption of a well-specified model. 

The authors consider a fundamentally different notion of tractability, namely a guarantee that Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling will quickly converge to the stationary distribution. They establish the existence of a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the maximum likelihood solution inside the set of fast-mixing parameters.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough background on mixing times and fast-mixing parameter sets, and their main results are presented in a clear and concise manner. The use of theorems and proofs adds to the paper's technical soundness.

The paper's main strength is its contribution to the theoretical understanding of maximum likelihood learning with MCMC. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the convergence of gradient descent with errors in estimated gradients, and their results have implications for the design of efficient algorithms for maximum likelihood learning.

However, the paper's practical relevance is limited. The condition required by the theorems, namely that the parameters satisfy the constraints of a fast-mixing set, is difficult to ensure in practice. The authors acknowledge this limitation, but do not provide a clear solution to this problem.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors' use of theorems and proofs adds to the paper's rigor. However, the paper's clarity could be improved with additional explanations and examples. The paper's significance is also limited by its practical relevance.
