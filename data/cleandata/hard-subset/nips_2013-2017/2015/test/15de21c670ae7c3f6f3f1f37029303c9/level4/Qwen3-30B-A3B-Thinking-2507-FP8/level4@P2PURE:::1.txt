

The primary issue with the manuscript is a lack of engagement with the relevant literature. The authors' algorithm is closely related to the Monte Carlo EM algorithm and the broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics, which have been extensively developed for optimizing intractable likelihood functions. A well-established literature exists on this topic, and the authors should explicitly compare their approach to prior work. Existing theory already encompasses many of the authors' results and handles significantly more general scenarios. For instance, uniform ergodicity of the Markov chains is not required, and there is no need to reinitialize the Markov chain at a fixed distribution $r$ at each iteration (the authors acknowledge this simplification but note that reinitializing with the previous sample—a strategy also extensively analyzed in prior work—would be preferable). Furthermore, the presentation of results is suboptimal: for Theorems 1 and 2, the assumptions are unclear and require the reader to consult surrounding text, which is unacceptable. Additionally, the strong assumptions underlying results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results are available (e.g., in Benveniste, Metivier, and Priouret). The authors' lack of awareness of this literature renders their contributions only marginally novel.
