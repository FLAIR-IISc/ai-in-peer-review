- Proposes a novel approach for computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families using an MCMC fast-mixing assumption.  
- Identifies the core challenge as computing an integral w.r.t. the current EF parameter and addresses it for proximal gradient descent.  
- Shows that under fast-mixing MCMC, required integrals can be approximated to sufficient precision.  
- Combines MCMC mixing, concentration inequalities, and Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the MLE.  
- Provides separate guarantees: likelihood-error bounds for the convex case and parameter-error bounds for the strongly convex case.  
- Includes a simple experiment to illustrate the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions with solid theoretical analysis; combination of techniques is nontrivial.  
- Authors are candid about limitations and pessimism of assumptions; paper gives insight into CD-type algorithms despite not analyzing CD itself.  
- Introduction and related work are strong, but some parts of the technical presentation lack clarity.  
- Omits an explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex and should clarify convexity of A(θ) via its Hessian (covariance of sufficient statistics).  
- Should explicitly summarize key assumptions (e.g., convexity of the parameter set Θ), which is not stated in the main text.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast mixing for MCMC is typically very difficult, effectively replacing one hard problem with another.  
- Contains several minor editorial issues/typos to fix (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, and an unclear sentence at 311–312).
