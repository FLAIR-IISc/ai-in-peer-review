- Limited engagement with relevant prior work; many related methods and theory are not cited or compared.  
- Algorithm closely resembles Monte Carlo EM and broader class of stochastic approximation algorithms with Markovian dynamics.  
- Authors should thoroughly compare their approach to existing methods and literature.  
- A general theoretical framework already exists that covers the authors’ results.  
- Requirement of uniform ergodicity for the Markov chains is unnecessarily restrictive.  
- Reinitializing the chain from a fixed distribution r each iteration is suboptimal; restarting from the previous sample is preferable and well-studied.  
- Presentation issues: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not stated within the theorems, forcing cross-referencing.  
- Strong assumptions used for results like Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) are available.  
- Overall: technically sound but only marginally novel due to lack of literature awareness and limited contribution.
