Review- Summary: The paper introduces a new method for computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families. In general, computing the MLE is intractable. From a convex optimization viewpoint, the bottleneck is evaluating an integral with respect to the current exponential-family parameter. By assuming that MCMC is fast-mixing for all admissible parameters, the author(s) show that the integrals needed for proximal gradient descent can be estimated accurately enough that, when combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), one obtains a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme for the MLE. Both the convex and strongly convex cases are treated, leading to distinct guarantees: the former on likelihood error and the latter on parameter error. A simple experiment illustrating the theory is included.

Overall, this is a high-quality piece of work. The approach is well-motivated as an alternative to relying on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear sound, though I did not check the proofs in detail. The proofs essentially combine the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al.; while the components are known, their integration here is nontrivial.

I appreciated the candid discussion of limitations. In particular, the conclusion thoughtfully analyzes weaknesses of the approach, ways in which the theoretical assumptions may be overly pessimistic, and directions for future work. The paper also provides insight into CD-style algorithms, even though the algorithm analyzed is not exactly contrastive divergence.

The introduction is well written with substantial references to prior work, but there are a few clarity issues in the technical presentation. A small but important omission is an explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex: the second and third terms in Eq. 1 are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(θ) follows from the fact that the Hessian of A is the covariance of the sufficient statistics and thus PSD. While seemingly minor, the paper’s logic rests on the convexity of f(θ), so making this explicit will help guide readers. Please also include a clear summary of the assumptions made; for example, the requirement that the parameter set Θ be convex is not stated in the main paper.

Overall, I found the paper to be an original and substantial contribution. The practical value of the results seems limited, however, because proving fast mixing of MCMC is extremely challenging. In that sense, the work trades one hard problem (finding the MLE) for another (establishing a fast-mixing Markov chain). That said, such reductions can be useful, and making these results available to the community is valuable.
