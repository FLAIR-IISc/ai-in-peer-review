- Introduces a novel approach for efficient MLE in exponential families using fast-mixing MCMC to evaluate integrals in proximal gradient descent.  
- Under the fast-mixing assumption, shows integrals can be approximated sufficiently to yield a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (with Schmidt et al. 2011).  
- Analyzes both convex and strongly convex settings, giving likelihood error bounds (convex) and parameter error bounds (strongly convex).  
- Provides a simple experimental validation of the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to restrictive small-treewidth assumptions; integration of existing techniques is nontrivial.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound though proofs were not verified in detail; relies on mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and prior results.  
- Gives useful insights into contrastive divergence–type algorithms, noting the analyzed algorithm differs slightly from standard CD.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions, and directions for future work.  
- Practical utility is limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is itself difficult, effectively replacing one hard problem with another.  
- Introduction and references are strong, but the technical presentation has minor clarity issues.  
- Critical omissions: does not explicitly state convexity of the log-likelihood (convexity of A(θ)) and omits a clear summary of all assumptions, including that Θ must be convex.  
- Minor editorial/typo corrections recommended (l185, l192, l207, l281, l311-12).
