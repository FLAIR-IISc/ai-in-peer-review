- Context: Weight learning in Markov networks requires gradient computation, which is NP-hard, so MCMC is commonly used to approximate gradients.  
- Contribution: The paper proves that if parameters yield rapid MCMC mixing, then MCMC-based gradient learning is an FPRAS.  
- Analysis: Two cases are analyzed separately—unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex)—with distinct bounds for each.  
- Writing/proofs: The paper is generally well written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Practicality concern: The key rapid-mixing condition is difficult or impossible to verify/guarantee in practice, limiting real-world relevance.  
- Suggested direction: The reviewer asks whether the authors can enforce the mixing constraints during learning or otherwise ensure them.  
- Regularization tradeoff: Treating the mixing condition as regularization may help, but combining it with ridge regularization risks over-regularization and degraded performance.  
- Overall assessment: The paper formally characterizes when MCMC gradient estimation is an FPRAS but currently has limited practical impact; substantial further work is needed to make it practical.
