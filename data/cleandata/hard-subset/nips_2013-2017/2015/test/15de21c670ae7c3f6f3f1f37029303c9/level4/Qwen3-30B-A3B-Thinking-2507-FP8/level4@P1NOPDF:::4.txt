

Comments on Quality:  
The paper establishes meaningful convergence rates for learning algorithms within the framework of mixing times. The provided bounds are tight and practically relevant, offering valuable theoretical insights. A minor concern is the interpretability of the lower bound (Theorem 7), which is challenging to unpack due to its technical complexity. The authors should elaborate on the theorem’s implications and contextualize the lower bounds within the broader landscape of this problem class.  

Comments on Clarity:  
The presentation is well-structured and largely accessible, with logical flow and clear notation.  

Comments on Originality:  
The framing of the problem—introducing the "fast mixing" model class—and the accompanying theoretical results represent a novel contribution. The identification of a tractable model class defined by efficient MCMC-based approximability of expectations, coupled with the convergence guarantees for learning these models, demonstrates genuine originality.  

Comments on Significance:  
The paper addresses a meaningful gap in the literature on learning intractable models via MCMC, which is a significant contribution. However, the broader impact and potential applications require clearer articulation. The authors should expand on future extensions, real-world applicability, and how this work enables subsequent advances in scalable Bayesian inference or related domains. While the theoretical novelty is strong, the significance would be substantially reinforced by a more detailed discussion of these opportunities.  

Overall, the technical merits are compelling, and the paper makes a worthwhile advance in the field. With minor revisions to clarify the lower bound and strengthen the impact discussion, this work would be a strong candidate for acceptance. Given the novelty of the model class and the rigor of the convergence analysis, the paper merits acceptance pending these clarifications.
