Comments on Quality:
The results provide convergence rates of learning algorithms in terms of mixing times, and the obtained bounds appear to be meaningful and useful. However, a significant concern is that the lower bound presented in Theorem 7 is challenging to interpret. An expanded discussion of this theorem, as well as the lower bounds applicable to the problem in general, would be highly desirable to facilitate a deeper understanding of the results.

Comments on Clarity:
The presentation is clear and effectively conveys the main ideas and technical contributions of the paper.

Comments on Originality:
The framing of the problem in terms of the "fast mixing" model class, as well as the theoretical results, appear to be original and contribute new insights to the field.

Comments on Significance:
While the significance of the paper is not entirely clear to the reviewer, it seems to fill a gap in the literature on learning intractable models using MCMC. In this sense, the paper appears to be significant. To further strengthen the submission, it would be beneficial to include a more in-depth discussion of the work's potential impact, including potential future extensions and opportunities to build upon the presented results. The introduction of a novel class of tractable models based on good MCMC-based approximability of expectations is a notable contribution. The theoretical results providing convergence rates for learning these models are novel and interesting, making a strong case for the acceptance of the submission.
