Summary: This paper proposes a novel approach to computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families. Generally, finding the maximum likelihood solution is intractable. From a convex optimization perspective, the key challenge lies in the need to calculate an integral with respect to the currently proposed exponential family parameter. By assuming that MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters, the authors demonstrate that the integrals required for proximal gradient descent can be calculated with sufficient precision. When combined with the results of Schmidt et al. (2011), this leads to a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for calculating the MLE. Both the convex and strongly convex cases are considered, resulting in different types of guarantees: the former on the likelihood error, and the latter on the parameter error. A simple experiment illustrating the theory is also included.

Overall, this is a high-quality piece of work. The approach is well-motivated as an alternative to relying on small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear solid, although I haven’t checked the proofs in detail. The proofs essentially involve combining the MCMC mixing assumption with concentration inequalities and the results of Schmidt et al.; thus, the paper represents a nontrivial combination of existing techniques.

I appreciated the honesty with which the limitations of the approach were discussed. In particular, the conclusion provides a thoughtful analysis of the method’s weaknesses, potential for overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions, and directions for future work. The paper also offers some insight into CD-type algorithms, even though the algorithm analyzed is not strictly contrastive divergence.

The introduction is well-written and includes substantial references to prior work. However, there are a few areas where the technical presentation could be improved. One minor, yet important, omission is the explicit statement that the log-likelihood is convex. The second and third terms in equation (1) are clearly convex, and the convexity of A(θ) follows from the fact that its Hessian is the covariance of the sufficient statistics and is therefore positive semi-definite. While seemingly minor, the entire paper rests on the convexity of f(θ), and explicitly stating this will better guide the reader through the paper’s logic. Additionally, a clearer summary of the key assumptions would be beneficial. For example, the assumption that the parameter set Θ must be convex is never explicitly mentioned in the main text. 

Overall, I found this paper to be an original and substantial contribution. The results appear to have limited practical value, however, due to the significant challenge of proving fast mixing of MCMC. In a sense, the paper replaces one difficult problem (finding the MLE) with another (finding a fast-mixing Markov chain). Nevertheless, being able to transform one problem into another is often valuable, and thus making these results available to the community is worthwhile.
