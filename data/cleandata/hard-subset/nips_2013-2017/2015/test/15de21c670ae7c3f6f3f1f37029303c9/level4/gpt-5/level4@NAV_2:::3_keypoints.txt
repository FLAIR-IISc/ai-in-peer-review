- Shows that if parameters lie in a fast-mixing set, gradient descent with MCMC-based gradient estimates is an FPRAS for maximum-likelihood learning in Markov networks.  
- Provides separate theoretical bounds for two regimes: unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized learning.  
- Paper is mostly clearly written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not exhaustively checked.  
- Main practical limitation: the fast-mixing parameter assumption is difficult to ensure in real training.  
- Raises the idea of imposing fast-mixing constraints during training (akin to regularization), but combining this with ridge penalties could over-regularize.  
- Overall assessment: a careful formalization of when MCMC-driven learning yields an FPRAS, but the key assumption limits practical applicability and further research is needed to bridge the gap.
