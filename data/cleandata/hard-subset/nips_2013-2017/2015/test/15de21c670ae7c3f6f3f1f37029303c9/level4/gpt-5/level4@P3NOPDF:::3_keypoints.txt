- Main contribution: The paper proves that if MCMC mixes rapidly along the learning trajectory, gradient-based weight learning in Markov networks is an FPRAS for the learning objective, with separate analyses for unregularized and ridge-regularized cases.

- Strength: Clear motivation that exact gradients are NP-hard and MCMC is a standard workaround.

- Strength: Clean theoretical statement linking rapid MCMC mixing at encountered parameters to polynomial-time accuracy guarantees.

- Strength: Helpful separation of unregularized and ridge-regularized settings with technically plausible derivations.

- Limitation: Central assumption that iterates remain in a fast-mixing region is typically infeasible to certify or enforce in practice.

- Limitation: Mixing conditions invoked (spectral gap, contractivity, high-temperature) are rarely checkable from data or known a priori, limiting practical relevance.

- Limitation: Sensitivity to hidden constants in mixing-time bounds is unclear; large constants could make guarantees impractical despite polynomial dependence.

- Limitation: Presentation needs clearer statement of what the FPRAS guarantees (objective value vs parameter closeness) and explicit dependence on model size, graph structure, feature norms, and accuracy/confidence.

- Suggestion: Consider designing constrained or projected optimization that enforces a known fast-mixing set (e.g., via Dobrushin-type bounds or L∞ projections) and discuss feasibility/statistical trade-offs.

- Suggestion: Discuss interaction between any fast-mixing constraint and ridge regularization, and provide principled tuning guidance to avoid over-regularization.

- Suggestion: Propose practical diagnostics/adaptive heuristics (autocorrelation tests, Gelman–Rubin, coupling-based checks) to detect mixing violations and adapt training accordingly.

- Suggestion: Clarify scope by stating whether bounds extend to other regularizers (e.g., L1) or approximate objectives (e.g., pseudolikelihood).

- Suggestion: Explicitly state polynomial dependence of runtime/sample complexity on 1/ε, log(1/δ), number of variables, max degree, and feature/parameter norms, and note effects of warm starts.

- Suggestion: Provide a small synthetic experiment showing a projection/constraint that guarantees fast mixing does not unduly harm predictive performance.

- Minor: Proofread to fix typos and minor grammatical slips.

- Minor: Collect and restate mixing assumptions in one place with concrete sufficient conditions (e.g., Dobrushin) and relate them to parameter norms.

- Overall: Solid, well-written theoretical contribution clarifying when MCMC-based learning admits FPRAS guarantees, but limited practical impact unless the authors provide mechanisms or diagnostics to enforce/verify fast mixing during training.
