- Addresses MLE in high-treewidth undirected models by constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing" set enabling rapid MCMC convergence.  
- Proposes and analyzes gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients, proving it is an FPRAS for maximum likelihood within the fast-mixing set.  
- Results apply to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.  
- Provides separate complexity results: unregularized case needs effort cubic in 1/ε for ε-accurate log-likelihood; ridge-regularized case needs effort quadratic in 1/ε for ε-accurate parameters.  
- Technically sound proofs (Theorems 2 and 6) leveraging projected gradient descent with errors and concentration inequalities.  
- Gives explicit, actionable schedules for iterations (K), samples (M), and chain length (v) to achieve target accuracy with high probability.  
- Minor weakness: Theorem 7 (lower bound) is complex and lacks intuition or discussion linking it to the upper-bound schedules.  
- Well-written and organized with clear motivation, notation table, and helpful proof sketches.  
- Example in Section 6 serves as a useful sanity check illustrating theoretical application.  
- Original conceptual shift: defines tractability by algorithmic property (fast MCMC mixing) rather than graph structure.  
- Novelty in providing the first rigorous, non-asymptotic FPRAS guarantees for learning with MCMC-approximated gradients.  
- Significant theoretical contribution that justifies MCMC-based learning when mixing is fast and suggests new directions for designing fast-mixing parameterizations.  
- Limited practical contribution: projection complexity and exclusion of common heuristics (e.g., sample pooling) restrict immediate applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framing, rigorous guarantees, comprehensive technical analysis, and high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear interpretation of lower bounds and limited discussion of practical implications.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
