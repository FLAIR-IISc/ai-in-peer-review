- Problem: ML learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models where exact inference is intractable, using MCMC for approximate gradients.  
- Novel idea: Define tractability via a "fast-mixing" parameter set Θ that guarantees rapid MCMC convergence instead of restricting model structure.  
- Core contribution: Prove that gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS for ML within Θ for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Theoretical results: Separate analyses for unregularized convex and ridge-regularized strongly convex cases with explicit schedules for K (steps), M (samples), and v (transitions).  
- Complexity rates: Convex case requires effort cubic in 1/ε (up to logs) for ε-accurate log-likelihood; strongly convex case requires effort quadratic in 1/ε for ε-accurate parameter distance.  
- Strength — Originality: Creative reframing of tractability based on MCMC mixing rather than graph structure.  
- Strength — Significance: First formal convergence guarantees connecting MCMC gradient approximation to ML learning, including convergence to the constrained M-projection under misspecification.  
- Strength — Quality: Sound, thorough theoretical analysis with explicit algorithmic schedules and separate convex/strongly-convex treatment.  
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, well-organized exposition with precise technical setup and clear theorems.  
- Weakness — Theorem 7: Lower bound presentation is dense and hard to interpret; its tightness relative to upper bounds is not discussed.  
- Weakness — Practical impact: Limited discussion of real-world applicability and examples of nontrivial, projectable fast-mixing parameter sets beyond Ising models.  
- Weakness — Projection cost: Complexity analysis ignores potential computational cost of projecting onto Θ, which may be expensive for some models (e.g., SVD-based projections).  
- Recommendation: High-quality, novel theoretical contribution; minor exposition and discussion issues; clear accept.
