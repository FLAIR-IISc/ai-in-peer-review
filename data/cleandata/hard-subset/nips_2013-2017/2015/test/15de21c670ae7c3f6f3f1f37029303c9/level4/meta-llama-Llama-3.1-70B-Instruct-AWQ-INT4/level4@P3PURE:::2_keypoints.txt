- Proposes a novel approach for computationally efficient maximum likelihood learning in exponential families.  
- Core challenge: MLE is intractable because it requires integrating w.r.t. the current EF parameter.  
- Key assumption: MCMC is fast-mixing for all allowed parameters, enabling accurate integral estimates.  
- Combining MCMC mixing with concentration inequalities and Schmidt et al. (2011) yields a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for the MLE.  
- Analysis covers both convex and strongly convex cases, giving likelihood-error guarantees (convex) and parameter-error guarantees (strongly convex).  
- Includes a simple experiment to demonstrate the theoretical claims.  
- Approach is well-motivated as an alternative to small-treewidth assumptions.  
- Theoretical analysis appears solid and the technique combination is nontrivial, though proofs were not independently checked.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, pessimistic assumptions, and future work directions.  
- Provides useful insights into Contrastive Divergence–type algorithms, despite not analyzing CD exactly.  
- Introduction is well-written with substantial related-work citations.  
- Clarity issues: paper omits explicit statement/proof that the log-likelihood is convex (Hessian = covariance PSD).  
- Missing a clear, centralized summary of assumptions (e.g., convexity of parameter set Theta is not stated).  
- Practical value is limited because proving fast MCMC mixing is hard—this shifts one hard problem (MLE) to another (fast-mixing chain).  
- Minor copyediting issues and one nonsensical sentence pointed out by the reviewer.
