- Presents a novel framework for efficient MLE in high-treewidth exponential families by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set enabling rapid MCMC-based gradient approximation.
- Proves that MCMC-approximated gradients with sufficient samples yield an FPRAS for MLE under the constrained set, with analyses for both convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases.
- Supports theoretical results with a small-scale Ising model experiment.
- Strength: reframes tractability via dynamic properties (mixing time) rather than structural constraints like treewidth.
- Strength: provides a rigorous, non-trivial synthesis of MCMC mixing bounds, concentration inequalities, and inexact-gradient convergence to justify a common heuristic.
- Strength: paper is well-written and transparent about limitations and conservative assumptions.
- Strength: analysis sheds formal insight on related heuristics (e.g., Contrastive Divergence), helping explain when MCMC-based learning can work.
- Weakness: practical applicability is limited because defining and projecting onto fast-mixing parameter sets is itself difficult for nontrivial models.
- Weakness: key assumptions could be stated more clearly and earlier (e.g., convexity of negative log-likelihood, convexity of Theta, bounded sufficient statistics).
- Conclusion: overall original and substantial theoretical contribution linking sampling dynamics to learning complexity; recommends acceptance.
- Minor comments: several small wording and typographical edits suggested (lines 192, 207, 281, 311–312).
