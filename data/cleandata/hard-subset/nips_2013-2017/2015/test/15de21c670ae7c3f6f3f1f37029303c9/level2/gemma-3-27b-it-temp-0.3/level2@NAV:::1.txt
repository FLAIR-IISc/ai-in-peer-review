## Review of "Maximum Likelihood Learning With Arbitrary Treewidth via Fast-Mixing Parameter Sets"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of maximum likelihood learning in graphical models with high treewidth, proposing a novel approach based on restricting parameters to “fast-mixing” sets. The core idea – leveraging MCMC convergence guarantees to enable efficient learning – is a compelling alternative to traditional methods like Chow-Liu or pseudolikelihood, which often suffer from limitations in model expressiveness or statistical efficiency. The authors establish a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood estimation within these fast-mixing sets, providing theoretical guarantees for gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the drawbacks of existing approaches and positioning their work as a general framework applicable to exponential families beyond graphical models. The theoretical results are significant, demonstrating convergence rates for both regularized and unregularized cases. The analysis appears rigorous, building upon established results in Markov chain Monte Carlo theory and optimization. The inclusion of a concrete example with an Ising model, while brief, helps illustrate the practical implications of the proposed method.

However, the paper suffers from a lack of empirical evaluation. While the authors acknowledge the limited practical contribution, demonstrating the performance of their approach on even a small set of benchmark datasets would significantly strengthen the work. The theoretical analysis relies heavily on the assumption of known mixing rates and the ability to project onto the fast-mixing set, which may not always be straightforward in practice. The discussion of initialization strategies and their impact on convergence is also limited. Furthermore, the connection to existing work on contrastive divergence, while mentioned, could be explored in more detail.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound approach to maximum likelihood learning.
*   Provides FPRAS guarantees for a broader class of models than traditional methods.
*   Clear presentation and rigorous analysis.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of empirical validation.
*   Reliance on potentially difficult-to-obtain mixing rate guarantees.
*   Limited discussion of practical considerations like initialization.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to the field of graphical model learning. While the lack of empirical evaluation is a significant weakness, the novelty and rigor of the analysis warrant consideration. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically encouraging the authors to include some preliminary experimental results and expand the discussion of practical challenges.
