Strengths:
- Introduces fast-mixing parameter sets as a novel tractability notion beyond treewidth, applicable to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.
- Provides rigorous proofs that gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients converges to the MLE with high probability under these constraints.
- States clear complexity bounds (cubic in 1/ε unregularized, quadratic in 1/ε ridge-regularized) with explicit optimization schedules.
- Addresses a major gap by giving provable convergence guarantees for MCMC-based learning in high-dimensional graphical models.

Weaknesses:
- Practical utility is unclear: experiments are small (4x4 Ising) and scalability to real-world problems is not demonstrated.
- Assumes availability of fast-mixing parameter sets without empirical evidence or methods to identify them in practice.
- Projection onto the fast-mixing set may be computationally expensive, but its cost is not analyzed.
- Limited empirical validation—single example insufficient to support practical feasibility.

Suggestions:
- Extend empirical evaluation to larger, more complex graphical models to demonstrate scalability.
- Provide heuristics or guidance for identifying fast-mixing parameter sets in real-world applications.
- Analyze the computational complexity of the projection step and its impact on overall efficiency.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on addressing practical limitations and adding experimental support.
