- Contribution: Provides a rigorous analysis of ML learning with MCMC-approximated gradients under a novel "fast-mixing set" constraint.  
- Main theoretical result: Shows exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics yield an FPRAS for ML when parameters lie in a fast-mixing set.  
- Complexity bounds: Derives precise computational bounds—cubic in 1/ε for convex (unregularized) cases and quadratic for strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases.  
- Strength: Sound, well-motivated theoretical framework that addresses lack of convergence guarantees in prior MCMC-based learning.  
- Strength: Solid proofs (Lipschitz gradient, convergence guarantees) with clear sketches and technical rigor.  
- Strength: Clear distinction and analysis between convex and strongly convex settings, with thorough complexity theorems.  
- Strength: Proper contextualization with prior treewidth-based tractability work, shifting focus to mixing-time tractability.  
- Weakness: Assumes an efficient projection operator onto the fast-mixing set but provides no general algorithm or complexity analysis for computing it beyond trivial cases.  
- Weakness: Empirical validation is weak—only a 4×4 synthetic Ising model, no comparisons to baselines like pseudolikelihood or contrastive divergence.  
- Weakness: Omits relevant related work (Steinhardt & Liang 2014; Domke & Liu 2013) that address fast-mixing and projection methods.  
- Weakness: Overstates practical significance of the FPRAS claim by ignoring potentially dominant projection costs.  
- Suggestion: Add analysis and references for projection algorithms and their computational cost relative to sampling.  
- Suggestion: Include empirical comparisons to standard baselines to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Suggestion: Clarify that the FPRAS result depends on efficient projection and revise abstract/conclusion accordingly.  
- Overall assessment: Strong and novel theoretical contribution but limited practical relevance due to unaddressed projection complexity and weak empirical evidence—recommendation: weak accept (borderline).
