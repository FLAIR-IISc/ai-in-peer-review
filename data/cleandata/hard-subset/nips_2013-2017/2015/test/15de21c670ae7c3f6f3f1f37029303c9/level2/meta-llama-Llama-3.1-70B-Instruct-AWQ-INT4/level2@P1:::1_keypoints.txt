- Proposes constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing" set to enable provably fast MCMC for maximum likelihood in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.  
- Introduces a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme under the fast-mixing parameter constraint.  
- Paper is well-written with clear introduction, organized technical sections, and thorough proofs.  
- Provides a useful illustrative example demonstrating the method.  
- Novel approach addressing a challenging setting—significant theoretical contribution.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees for MCMC convergence and learning under the proposed constraints.  
- Limited practical contribution: method not evaluated on real-world data.  
- Relies on strong assumptions (existence of fast-mixing parameter set and tractable projection) that may not hold in practice.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity and practical runtime considerations.  
- Original and significant theoretically, but practical applicability is uncertain.  
- Clarity, organization, and writing quality are high.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical guarantees, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation, strong assumptions, and absent complexity analysis.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10.
