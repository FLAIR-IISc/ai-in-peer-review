- Proposes "fast-mixing parameter sets" enabling MCMC inference to converge quickly for undirected graphical models.  
- Shows that constraining parameters to these sets yields an FPRAS for maximum likelihood learning in exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Provides computational complexity bounds: O(1/ε^3) for unregularized and O(1/ε^2) for ridge-regularized cases.  
- Generalizes beyond graphical models to the full exponential family, broadening applicability.  
- Effectively contrasts the approach with existing tractable methods (e.g., tree-structured models, pseudolikelihood).  
- Connects and builds on prior work about fast-mixing models (e.g., Steinhardt and Liang).  
- Technical quality is strong with rigorous proofs for convex and strongly convex cases and clear theorem statements.  
- Mixing time analysis is well-grounded in the literature and provides explicit, justified bounds.  
- Ising model example concretely illustrates results but is small-scale (4×4 grid), limiting practical insight.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with helpful background on mixing times for non-experts.  
- Honest discussion of limitations, notably ignoring projection complexity and not modeling sample-pool strategies.  
- Main originality is shifting focus from treewidth to mixing-time guarantees for tractable learning.  
- Significance lies in the theoretical guarantee (FPRAS) and distinct cubic vs. quadratic accuracy trade-offs.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of how to identify or project onto fast-mixing sets for complex model classes.  
- Weakness: lack of larger-scale experiments to demonstrate practical feasibility, though authors frame the work as theoretical.  
- Recommendation: accept as a strong theoretical contribution valuable to NIPS and theoretical ML.
