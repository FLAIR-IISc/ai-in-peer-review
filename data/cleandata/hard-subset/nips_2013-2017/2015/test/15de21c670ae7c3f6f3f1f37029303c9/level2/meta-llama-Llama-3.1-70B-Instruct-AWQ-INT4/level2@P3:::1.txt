**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a novel approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models. The authors propose a new notion of tractability, called "fast-mixing parameters," where Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference can be guaranteed to quickly converge to the stationary distribution. They prove that for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, when parameters are constrained to a fast-mixing set, gradient descent with gradients approximated by sampling will approximate the maximum likelihood solution inside the set with high probability.

**Strengths of the Paper**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper introduces a new notion of tractability, which is different from the traditional approach of restricting parameters to a tractable set, such as tree-structured parameters.
2. **Theoretical guarantees**: The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the convergence of the proposed algorithm, including a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme.
3. **General applicability**: The results are applicable to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, making it a general framework for maximum likelihood learning.

**Weaknesses of the Paper**

1. **Complexity of projection**: The paper ignores the complexity of projection, which might be significant in certain cases.
2. **Initialization of samples**: The authors assume that the initial distribution is arbitrary, but in practice, the choice of initialization can affect the mixing time.
3. **Limited experimental evaluation**: The paper only provides a simple example to illustrate the proposed algorithm, and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation would be beneficial.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper presents a novel approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models, which is a significant contribution to the field. The theoretical guarantees provided by the authors make the proposed algorithm a promising approach for practical applications.

**Clarity and Organization**

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The notation is well-defined, and the mathematical derivations are thorough and accurate.
