- Tackles maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models by restricting to “fast-mixing” parameter sets that enable rapid MCMC inference.  
- Proposes gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients and proves a Fully Polynomial Time Randomized Approximation Scheme (FPRAS) for ML estimation in these sets.  
- Results extend beyond graphical models to general exponential family distributions.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with convex and strongly convex convergence guarantees and explicit computational bounds for given accuracy.  
- Addresses practical concerns such as projection complexity and sample initialization in the analysis.  
- Includes an illustrative example to ground the theory, while noting caveats about the Lipschitz constant.  
- Main practical weakness: the paper does not provide concrete methods for identifying or constructing fast-mixing parameter sets in complex models.  
- Analysis depends on mixing time bounds and constants (L, C, α) that are often hard to compute or estimate in practice.  
- Empirical example shows theoretical bounds are conservative, revealing a discrepancy between theory and observed convergence.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novel approach, strong theoretical contribution, mathematically sound FPRAS guarantees.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: limited immediate practical applicability and sensitivity to hard-to-estimate parameters.  
- Recommendation: accept as a strong theoretical contribution, with suggested future work on practical strategies to identify fast-mixing parameter sets.
