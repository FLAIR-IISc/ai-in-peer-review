**Review of "Maximum Likelihood Learning With Arbitrary Treewidth via Fast-Mixing Parameter Sets"**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models. The authors introduce the concept of "fast-mixing parameter sets," which allows for efficient Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference. They prove that, under certain conditions, gradient descent with MCMC approximated gradients can converge to the maximum likelihood solution with high probability. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the convergence rates and computational complexity of the proposed algorithm.

**Claims and Support**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The proposed algorithm can efficiently learn maximum likelihood parameters in high-treewidth graphical models.
2. The algorithm converges to the maximum likelihood solution with high probability.
3. The computational complexity of the algorithm is fully polynomial in the model parameters.

The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the convergence rates and computational complexity of the algorithm, using a combination of theoretical results and empirical evidence. The support for the claims is strong, with detailed proofs and simulations that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

**Usefulness**

The ideas presented in this paper are practically useful, as they provide a novel approach to learning maximum likelihood parameters in high-treewidth graphical models. The algorithm is particularly useful when the model is well-specified, and the target distribution falls within the assumed family. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the algorithm, which is essential for practitioners.

**Limitations**
