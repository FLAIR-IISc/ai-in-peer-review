- Proposes "fast-mixing parameters" as a new notion of tractable sets guaranteeing rapid MCMC convergence.  
- Shows constraining parameters to a fast-mixing set lets gradient descent with sampled gradients approximate the MLE with high probability.  
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis, including bounds on iterations needed for a target accuracy.  
- Applicable to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, giving broad generality.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to non-experts.  
- Strength: novel alternative to traditional tree-structured parameter restrictions.  
- Strength: significant technical contributions and thorough convergence analysis.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation—only a single example provided.  
- Weakness: potentially high computational complexity in high-dimensional models; needs discussion of costs and improvements.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address empirical evaluation and computational complexity.
