- Proposes using “fast-mixing parameter” sets so MCMC inference converges quickly as an alternative to tractable-structure constraints.  
- Proves a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood learning using MCMC-approximated gradients.  
- Provides convergence analysis for both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases with different rates.  
- Results extend beyond graphical models to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with helpful visual aids (Figure 1).  
- Situates work relative to pseudolikelihood and composite likelihoods, offering theoretical guarantees those lack.  
- Practical implications are underexplored; the provided example is limited and the Lipschitz constant appears conservative.  
- Complexity of projecting onto the fast-mixing parameter set is not fully addressed and could be a practical bottleneck.  
- The bounded sufficient-statistics assumption may not hold in all applications and sensitivity to violations isn’t analyzed.  
- Runtime depends strongly on mixing-rate constants (C and α), which are hard to estimate in practice.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to established methods (pseudolikelihood/composite likelihood) on diverse datasets.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically strong approach with a significant FPRAS result and broad applicability.  
- Against acceptance: practical relevance unproven, projection complexity and assumptions are concerns, and empirical evidence is thin.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing practical relevance, projection costs, and expanding experiments.
