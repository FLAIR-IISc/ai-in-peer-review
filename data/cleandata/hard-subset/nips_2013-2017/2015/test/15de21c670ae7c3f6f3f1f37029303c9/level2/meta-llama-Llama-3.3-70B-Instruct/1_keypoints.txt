- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set to guarantee rapid MCMC convergence and claims this yields an FPRAS for maximum likelihood in exponential families.  
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis of gradient descent using MCMC-based gradient estimates for both convex and strongly convex cases.  
- Proves that with enough iterations, samples, and MCMC transitions, the algorithm approximates the maximum likelihood solution with high probability.  
- Supplies explicit schedules for the number of iterations, samples, and Markov chain transitions needed to reach a target accuracy.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis clarifying convergence properties and conditions for the algorithm.  
- Strength: emphasizes the practical importance of fast-mixing parameter regimes and includes an illustrative example.  
- Limitation: requires parameter constraints to a fast-mixing set, which may not hold in many practical problems.  
- Limitation: lacks clear empirical or theoretical comparison to existing methods like contrastive divergence.  
- Limitation: approach may not generalize or be applicable to all types of graphical models.  
- Paper is well-written and discusses limitations and future work clearly.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address limitations and provide clearer comparisons to existing methods in future work.
