- Introduces a theoretical framework restricting parameters to "fast-mixing sets" so MCMC inference converges quickly for maximum likelihood in exponential families.  
- Main claim: gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients gives an FPRAS for ML with bounded sufficient statistics for both unregularized and ridge-regularized cases.  
- Provides rigorous proofs for convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (regularized) cases with explicit computational complexity bounds.  
- Establishes complexity rates: cubic in 1/ε for unregularized and quadratic in 1/ε for regularized settings.  
- Connects mixing-time theory to optimization using tools like Bernstein’s inequality and Schmidt et al.’s analysis of gradient descent with errors.  
- Properly contextualizes the work relative to prior methods (Chow-Liu, pseudolikelihood, Steinhardt & Liang) and clarifies its theoretical contributions.  
- Novelty: proposes fast-mixing parameter sets as a more flexible tractable alternative to tree-structured parameter restrictions.  
- Writing is clear and math is well-organized, with detailed proofs in the appendix supporting reproducibility.  
- Main weakness: no empirical validation beyond a simple theoretical example, leaving real-world applicability uncertain.  
- Additional weakness: theoretical bounds appear conservative (worst-case), limiting practical significance despite strong theoretical results.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality theoretical contribution within NIPS scope, moderate field significance—pros: rigor and novelty; cons: lack of experiments and limited practical impact.
