- Proposes restricting parameters to a “fast-mixing” set so MCMC inference converges quickly for maximum likelihood learning in graphical models.
- Proves a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood learning within fast-mixing parameter sets.
- Analyzes gradient descent using MCMC-approximated gradients and provides theoretical guarantees for this procedure.
- Extends beyond graphical models to arbitrary exponential families.
- Builds on prior methods (e.g., Chow-Liu, pseudolikelihood) but supplies theoretical guarantees those methods lack.
- Clear, well-organized writing, careful notation, and progressive concept introduction.
- Includes a concrete Ising model example to illustrate applicability.
- Provides extensive related-work discussion and analyzes both regularized and unregularized cases.
- Practical impact is unclear due to potentially large hidden constant factors in the polynomial-time bounds (dependence on Lipschitz constant L and mixing parameters C, α).
- Limited empirical evaluation; more experiments on complex graphical models are needed to validate practicality.
- Projection onto the fast-mixing set may be computationally expensive and is a potential bottleneck.
- Paper does not deeply address how to choose or identify suitable fast-mixing sets for specific problems.
- Heavy reliance on existing mixing-time bounds (e.g., Ising); runtime dependence on these bounds and their computability is insufficiently discussed.
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution worthy of acceptance, but should expand empirical evaluation and discuss constant factors and projection complexity.
