- Paper studies MLE for exponential families constrained to a “fast-mixing” convex set Θ and proves projected-gradient-with-MCMC-gradients yields an FPRAS: O(ε−3) sampling for ε-accurate log-likelihood (convex) and O(ε−2) for parameter error (ridge-regularized), with explicit schedules and near-matching lower bounds.  
- Analysis gives Lipschitz gradient bounds, explicit iteration/sample/mixing schedules, and high-probability end-to-end guarantees for biased MCMC gradients.  
- Framework is general beyond graphical models (applies to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics).  
- Connects to prior work on projection to fast-mixing sets, inexact proximal-gradient methods, and learning fast-mixing models; contrasts with tree-structured, pseudo/composite likelihood, and score-matching approaches.  
- Strength: clear, general theoretical framework with concrete linkage to mixing-time literature and candid discussion of limitations.  
- Strength: provides explicit complexity results, scheduling, and accompanying lower bounds.  
- Strength: potentially unifies practical MCMC-based learning with principled guarantees and a theoretical view on contrastive divergence.  
- Weakness: practicality depends on access to a convex fast-mixing set Θ and a projection oracle, which is often hard or problem-dependent to construct.  
- Weakness: mixing-time constants are loose, so the prescribed computational work may be prohibitive in practice; empirical evaluation is minimal and toy-scale.  
- Weakness: no empirical comparisons to pseudo/composite likelihood or score matching, and no treatment of persistent chains or hidden-variable models.  
- Weakness: some clarity issues—notation for bounds on sufficient statistics is inconsistent and projection algorithmic details are lacking for general families.  
- Quality: technically sound with plausible proofs sketched in the main text and detailed in the appendix, leveraging standard concentration and inexact-gradient analyses.  
- Clarity: generally well organized but would benefit from a catalog of known Θ/projection methods and a summary table of constants and their roles.  
- Originality: novel end-to-end FPRAS for MLE over fast-mixing sets in general exponential families with near-optimal bounds.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of when MCMC-based MLE is computationally tractable; practical impact hinges on feasibility of defining/projecting onto Θ.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong, general theory with clear novelty and careful bounds that bridge common practice and theory.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited empirical validation and uncertain practical applicability due to projection and mixing-constant requirements.  
- Suggestion: add experiments on moderate-sized MRFs comparing to pseudo/composite likelihood and score matching.  
- Suggestion: provide guidance or algorithms for constructing Θ and estimating mixing constants C and α for common models; discuss adaptive schedules, persistent chains, and variance reduction.  
- Suggestion: clarify notation and present a concise algorithm box summarizing assumptions, parameters, and explicit projection procedures.
