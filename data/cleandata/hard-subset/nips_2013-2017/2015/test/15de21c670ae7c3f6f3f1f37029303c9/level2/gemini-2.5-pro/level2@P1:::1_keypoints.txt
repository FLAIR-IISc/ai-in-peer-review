- Proposes restricting parameters to a convex "fast-mixing" set Θ so MCMC is provably fast, instead of restricting graph structure for tractable inference.  
- Main contribution: rigorous analysis showing projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS for MLE over Θ under fast-mixing assumptions.  
- Analysis applies to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- For convex (unregularized) MLE, achieving ε-accurate log-likelihood requires O(1/ε^3) total work.  
- For strongly convex (e.g., ridge-regularized) MLE, achieving ε-accurate parameter distance requires O(1/ε^2) work.  
- Connects MCMC mixing-time theory with optimization guarantees for a commonly used heuristic learning method.  
- Strength: novel and significant conceptual shift—defining tractability via fast-mixing parameters rather than graph structure.  
- Strength: rigorous, sound proofs leveraging optimization with inexact gradients and concentration inequalities; assumptions are stated explicitly.  
- Strength: paper is very clear, well-organized, with helpful proof sketches and consistent notation.  
- Weakness: relies on knowing a convex fast-mixing set Θ and an efficient projector, which may be hard to obtain for many MRFs (higher-order or non-binary variables).  
- Weakness: theoretical schedules for iterations, samples, and MCMC steps are conservative; practical constants are much smaller in experiments.  
- Weakness: analysis assumes independent fresh chains each iteration and does not cover practical schemes like persistent contrastive divergence (PCD).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel concept, first FPRAS proof for this MCMC-based learning scheme, high-quality theory and presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong assumptions limit practical applicability; complexity bounds likely too loose for direct practical use.  
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution with minor practical limitations; recommend acceptance.
