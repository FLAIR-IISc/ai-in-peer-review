- Paper studies MLE in exponential families by restricting parameters to a convex “fast-mixing” set Θ where a chosen MCMC kernel mixes geometrically fast (constants C, α).  
- Analyzes projected (proximal) gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients and bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Proves negative log-likelihood has L-Lipschitz gradient under assumptions.  
- In the convex case, averaged iterate achieves εf-suboptimality with total MCMC work KMv = Õ(1/ε^3).  
- In the strongly convex (ridge) case, final iterate achieves ∥θK − θ∗∥ ≤ εθ with total work Õ(1/ε^2).  
- Provides explicit schedules for iterations K, samples per iteration M, and chain steps v, claimed near-optimal up to logs.  
- Results apply to general exponential families, not just graphical models, given bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Connects Θ design to mixing-time literature (e.g., Dobrushin/spectral-norm bounds, bounded couplings, Swendsen–Wang conditions).  
- Builds on classical MCMC-MLE and prior work (Domke & Liu), offering high-probability end-to-end guarantees and an FPRAS view for MLE in Θ.  
- Distinct from methods enforcing fast mixing via restarts; focuses on projecting to mixing-certified sets.  
- Strength: clear, general theoretical contribution with high-probability convergence for biased MCMC gradients in both convex and strongly convex settings.  
- Strength: transparent, interpretable complexity statements and explicit K, M, v schedules with matching lower bounds.  
- Strength: broad applicability beyond graphical models and useful synthesis of mixing-time conditions with projection discussion.  
- Weakness: practical reliance on known mixing constants (C, α) which are often unknown or extremely conservative in real models.  
- Weakness: analysis assumes independent fresh chains each iteration and does not cover common persistent-chain/sample-pool practices that can reduce cost.  
- Weakness: very limited empirical validation (single 4×4 Ising example) leaving practical tightness and runtime benefits unclear.  
- Weakness: some technical/notation clarity issues (e.g., TV bound use, R2 notation, missing Algorithm 1 details and step-size policy).  
- Weakness: scope and computability of Θ (convexity, projection, computing α) not fully developed for example chains like subgraphs-world.  
- Weakness: complexity ignores projection cost which may be dominant (e.g., repeated SVDs for spectral-norm constraints).  
- Quality: arguments plausible and based on standard tools, but some proofs/constants need fuller justification or citations.  
- Clarity: generally organized and intuitive but needs tightened notation, explicit algorithm box, and consolidated assumptions.  
- Originality: novel framing of optimizing MLE over a fast-mixing parameter set with provable MCMC-based gradient convergence; FPRAS perspective is new.  
- Significance: strong theoretical contribution linking learning, mixing-time analysis, and inexact optimization; practical impact limited by assumptions.  
- Suggestions: include a clear algorithm box, discuss/estimate C and α in practice, consider adaptive v or diagnostics, analyze/reuse persistent chains, expand experiments on larger graphs versus baselines, and quantify projection cost.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, principled theoretical framework with strong links to mixing-time literature and inexact gradient methods.  
- Cons for acceptance: strong/unknown constants, limited practical guidance, and minimal empirical support.  
- Recommendation: weak accept for a theory-focused paper, stronger with added experiments and clarification of constants/assumptions.  
- Reviewer confidence: moderate.
