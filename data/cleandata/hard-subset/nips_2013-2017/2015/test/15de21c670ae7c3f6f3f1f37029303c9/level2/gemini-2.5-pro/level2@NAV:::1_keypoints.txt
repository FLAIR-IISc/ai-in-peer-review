- Addresses ML in high-treewidth undirected models by restricting parameters to a convex fast-mixing set enabling provably fast MCMC.
- Main claim: projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients in this set gives an FPRAS for ML in exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.
- Novelty/significance: introduces tractable model class via fast-mixing parameters rather than structure and gives the first formal convergence guarantees for MCMC-based SGD.
- Generality: results apply broadly to exponential families.
- Technical quality: rigorous, technically sound analysis for both convex and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases.
- Concrete results: derives explicit schedules and complexity bounds for gradient steps, MCMC sample counts, and chain lengths to achieve target accuracy with high probability.
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, well-motivated, and well-situated in the literature.
- Practical limitations: admits no significant practical contribution; derived bounds are conservative in examples and may not be tight.
- Implementation caveat: analysis assumes an efficient projection operator onto the fast-mixing set, whose complexity is not included and could be a bottleneck.
- Scope limitation: does not cover common practical heuristics like persistent chains or contrastive divergence.
- Overall judgement: excellent theoretical contribution with minor practical-scope weaknesses; strongly recommended for acceptance.
