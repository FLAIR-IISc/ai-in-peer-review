- Proposes approximating MLE in exponential families by restricting to a "fast-mixing" parameter set so MCMC converges rapidly.  
- Combines fast-mixing assumption with proximal gradient descent to obtain an FPRAS for maximum likelihood within that set.  
- Provides convergence guarantees for both convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Analysis leverages and combines existing techniques (e.g., Schmidt et al. 2011) in a nontrivial way.  
- Includes a simple experiment that qualitatively supports the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to low-treewidth parameter restrictions, offering a different tractability route.  
- Authors are candid about limitations and suggest meaningful future research directions.  
- Discusses connections to Contrastive Divergence (CD)-type algorithms, noting analysis isn’t directly applicable but related.  
- Fails to explicitly state fundamental assumptions: log-likelihood convexity (Hessian positive definite) and convexity of parameter set Θ.  
- Lacks a concise summary of all key assumptions at the start of relevant sections.  
- Practical impact limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is itself difficult and may replace one hard problem with another.  
- Some clarity issues and several minor typographical/wording errors (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311-312).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and does not strongly demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on explicitly stating convexity assumptions, summarizing key assumptions, and fixing typos.
