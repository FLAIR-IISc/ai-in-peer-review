- Introduces a framework for maximum likelihood learning by restricting parameters to "fast-mixing sets" guaranteeing MCMC convergence.  
- Formalizes MCMC-approximability of expectations and provides convergence guarantees for MCMC-approximated gradients.  
- Proves convergence rates for gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients, tied to mixing times.  
- Shows that any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics admits a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme under this approach.  
- Technical quality is high with rigorous proofs and meaningful convergence-rate bounds.  
- Results are original and extend beyond graphical models to general exponential families.  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and detailed enough for reproduction.  
- Main weakness: insufficient discussion of practical significance, applications, and real-world impact.  
- Theorem 7 lower bound is underdeveloped and hard to interpret; needs expansion to assess tightness of upper bounds.  
- Paper should compare computational efficiency with existing methods and outline specific future research directions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; accept with minor revisions to clarify lower bound and expand significance discussion.
