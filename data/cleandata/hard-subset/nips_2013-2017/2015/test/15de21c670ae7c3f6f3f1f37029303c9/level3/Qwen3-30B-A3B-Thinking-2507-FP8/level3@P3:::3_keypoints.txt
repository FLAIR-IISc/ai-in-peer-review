- Proposes constraining parameters to "fast-mixing parameter sets" so MCMC inference converges quickly for maximum likelihood learning.  
- Main theoretical result: under fast-mixing constraints, MCMC-based gradient descent is an FPRAS for maximum likelihood.  
- Provides separate complexity bounds: unregularized (convex) case has cubic dependence on 1/ε; ridge-regularized (strongly convex) case has quadratic dependence.  
- Builds on prior mixing-time theory (e.g., Jerrum & Sinclair, Liu & Domke) but applies it novelly to learning guarantees.  
- Generalizes results to exponential families beyond graphical models.  
- Technical quality: proofs are sound, well-separated for convex vs. strongly convex, and plausible though not fully verified by reviewer.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear notation and intuitive proof sketches; only minor typos.  
- Originality: first to formally link mixing times to convergence guarantees for MCMC-based learning.  
- Significance: provides valuable theoretical insight but practical impact is limited by stringent parameter constraints.  
- Main weakness: parameter constraints required for guarantees are unlikely to hold in real-world applications.  
- Practical gap: paper does not show how to enforce the fast-mixing condition in practice or resolve combining mixing enforcement with ridge regression.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid theoretical contribution that would benefit from more discussion on making the mixing condition practical.
