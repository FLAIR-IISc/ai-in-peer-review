- Proposes maximum-likelihood learning in exponential families by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing set" so MCMC converges quickly and claims polynomial-time ε-accuracy for gradient descent with MCMC gradients.  
- Important problem, but the paper shows poor awareness of prior literature on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics (e.g., Benveniste et al., 1990).  
- Fails to compare adequately with existing methods for intractable likelihoods, such as contrastive divergence.  
- Theoretical contributions largely overlap with existing work; novelty appears marginal and incremental.  
- The mixing-time assumption (Eq. 2) is overly restrictive—uniform ergodicity is not necessary in general.  
- Reinitializing chains from a fixed distribution r is unnecessary; using previous samples has been extensively analyzed and is not addressed.  
- Presentation of assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 is unclear and they should be stated explicitly near the theorems.  
- The strong conditions required in Theorem 6 are unnecessary; more general results exist that avoid these restrictive assumptions.  
- Technical content is sound but does not provide significant new theoretical advances beyond known theory.  
- Paper does provide a clear analytical framework for MCMC-based learning, but this does not compensate for the lack of novelty.  
- Insufficient acknowledgement and discussion of related work is a significant oversight.  
- Overall significance is limited and the paper does not meet the bar for NIPS due to marginal novelty and inadequate literature grounding.
