- Tackles maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models where inference is intractable.  
- Focuses on “fast-mixing parameters” for which MCMC converges rapidly instead of restricting to tractable graph structures.  
- Main contribution: proves MCMC-based gradient descent is an FPRAS for maximum likelihood under the fast-mixing condition.  
- Provides analysis for both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases.  
- Builds on known NP-hardness of exact gradients and common use of MCMC approximations.  
- Positions method against pseudolikelihood/composite likelihood, arguing potential sample-complexity and inference limitations of those alternatives.  
- Connects to existing mixing-time literature and gives model examples (e.g., Ising) where fast mixing can be guaranteed.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical formalization of when MCMC-based learning yields an FPRAS.  
- Strength: clear presentation and plausible proofs, with separate treatments for convex and non-convex settings.  
- Major weakness: stringent mixing-condition assumptions likely limit practical applicability.  
- Concern: enforcing mixing via regularization (e.g., ridge) may cause over-regularization and degrade performance.  
- Omission: computational cost and feasibility of verifying the mixing condition are not addressed.  
- Omission/weakness: projection complexity and how to implement constraints in practice are not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Recommendation: theoretically valuable but limited in practice; leans weak accept pending authors’ responses and further work to bridge theory and practice.
