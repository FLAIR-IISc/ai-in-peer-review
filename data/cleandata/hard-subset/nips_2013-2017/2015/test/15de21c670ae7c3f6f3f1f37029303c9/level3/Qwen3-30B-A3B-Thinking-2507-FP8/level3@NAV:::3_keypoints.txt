- Addresses NP-hardness of exact gradient computation for Markov network ML learning and reliance on MCMC without guarantees.  
- Main contribution: proves MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS when parameters lie in a fast-mixing set.  
- Provides separate analyses for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases with complexities O(1/ε³) and O(1/ε²).  
- Novel focus on fast-mixing parameter sets rather than structural constraints like tree models.  
- Theoretical framework and proofs are plausible and sound but would benefit from more rigorous verification.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, with only minor typos.  
- Strength: elegant theoretical unification giving FPRAS guarantees across exponential families.  
- Major weakness: practical relevance limited by stringent, potentially unrealistic parameter constraints for mixing (e.g., uniform interaction bounds).  
- Proposal to enforce mixing via regularization is promising but may cause over-regularization, especially combined with ridge regression, and is not well addressed.  
- Lacks empirical demonstrations showing how to enforce fast-mixing conditions or that bounds are not overly conservative in real settings.  
- Overall verdict: strong theoretical contribution but limited practical significance; recommendation to reject.
