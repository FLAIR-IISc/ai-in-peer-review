- Proposes a new notion of tractable models: "fast-mixing parameter sets" where MCMC converges rapidly, replacing structural constraints like treewidth.  
- Analyzes gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients and proves it is an FPRAS for maximum likelihood within a convex fast-mixing set.  
- Results apply to general exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Provides explicit prescriptions for numbers of gradient steps, MCMC samples, and chain lengths to guarantee accuracy with high probability.  
- Derives separate convergence/complexity bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.  
- Original and insightful reframing connecting practical MCMC-based learning to rigorous theory.  
- Technically rigorous analysis that quantifies trade-offs and handles biased/noisy gradient estimates using inexact gradient descent theory.  
- Clear and well-organized exposition with explicit assumptions and helpful proof sketches.  
- Significant practical relevance: fills a theoretical gap for MCMC-based training of intractable models and may guide algorithm design.  
- Weak explanation and interpretation of the lower bound (Theorem 7); needs more intuition, comparisons to upper bounds, and relation to known lower bounds.  
- Discussion and conclusions are brief; should expand on practical applications, how to handle violated fast-mixing assumptions, and open problems (e.g., projection operators, adaptive MCMC).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel concept, first formal FPRAS guarantee for this setting, rigorous results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient contextualization of the lower bound and limited discussion of broader impact/future directions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and significant paper; recommend acceptance subject to addressing presentation and discussion weaknesses.
