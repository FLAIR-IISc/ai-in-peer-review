- Proposes a novel approach to approximate MLE in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing.  
- Combines proximal gradient descent with Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS).  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Includes a simple experiment that demonstrates the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated alternative to the common small-treewidth assumption in graphical models.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid but largely a nontrivial combination of existing techniques.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations and propose directions for future work.  
- Offers insight into CD-type algorithms, though the analyzed algorithm is not exactly contrastive divergence.  
- Omits explicit statement that the log-likelihood (log-partition A(θ)) is convex and should show Hessian = covariance.  
- Fails to summarize all assumptions, notably that the parameter set Θ must be convex (not stated in main text).  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is generally hard, shifting rather than resolving difficulty.  
- Contains minor typos and wording errors at several lines (l185, l192, l207, l281, l311-12).  
- Scores: Originality 8/10, Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Significance 6/10.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address omissions, clarity, and errors.
