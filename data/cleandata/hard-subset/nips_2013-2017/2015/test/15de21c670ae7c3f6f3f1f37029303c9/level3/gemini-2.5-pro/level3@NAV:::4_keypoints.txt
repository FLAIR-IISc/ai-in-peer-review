- Addresses maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models where exact inference is intractable.  
- Notes prior work often restricts models to tractable structures (e.g., Chow-Liu) or uses pseudolikelihood with weak guarantees under misspecification.  
- Introduces a novel notion of tractability via "fast-mixing" parameter sets guaranteeing rapid MCMC convergence.  
- Main contribution: formal proof that for exponential families constrained to fast-mixing sets, SGD with MCMC-approximated gradients yields an FPRAS for the maximum likelihood solution.  
- Provides theoretical grounding for a commonly used MCMC-based heuristic.  
- Framing tractability through fast-mixing parameter sets is original and elegant.  
- Theoretical results are technically sound and give convergence rates linking learning guarantees to MCMC mixing time.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, making complex analysis accessible.  
- Primary weakness: interpretation and discussion of theoretical bounds (especially Theorem 7 lower bound) is unclear and under-explored.  
- Recommendation to expand discussion of Theorem 7 and place it in the broader context of lower bounds for the problem class.  
- Broader significance and potential impact of the work are not fully articulated.  
- Suggestion to add discussion of future extensions and follow-on research opportunities enabled by the framework.  
- Overall assessment: original, technically strong contribution that fills an important theoretical gap; should be accepted.
