- Summary: Analyzes ML in exponential families using fast-mixing parameter sets and inexact projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated expectations, proving high-probability convergence under bounded statistics and projection onto a convex Θ.  
- Summary: Shows ε-accuracy in log-likelihood with total MCMC effort O((1/ε)^3) for convex case and ε-accuracy in parameter distance with O((1/ε)^2) for λ-strongly convex (ridge) case, yielding an FPRAS for ML constrained to Θ.  
- Relation to prior work: Provides nonasymptotic guarantees for MCMC-approximated ML, building on classical fast-mixing conditions and prior projection ideas for Ising/MRFs.  
- Relation to prior work: Complements Steinhardt & Liang by handling general exponential families and giving explicit schedules for sampling/iterations.  
- Strength: Introduces a clear, principled tractability notion via fast-mixing sets applicable to general exponential families with bounded statistics.  
- Strength: Delivers solid technical development combining mixing bounds, concentration, and inexact projected gradient theory to produce explicit complexity schedules and FPRAS guarantees.  
- Strength: Treats both convex and strongly convex cases with interpretable complexity tradeoffs (cubic vs quadratic in 1/ε).  
- Strength: Honest discussion of limitations and relations to contrastive divergence and practical sampling.  
- Weakness: Practical impact limited by difficulty of certifying/designing Θ with known mixing constants C, α and efficient projection beyond special families.  
- Weakness: Constants may be pessimistic and the single toy example does not validate tightness or competitiveness against standard baselines.  
- Weakness: Assumptions and requirements are scattered; would benefit from a concise assumptions summary (bounded t(x), convex Θ, projection oracle, known C, α, etc.).  
- Quality: Technically sound and careful; main theorems plausibly follow from assembled standard tools, but rely on strong assumptions about known mixing rates and projection availability.  
- Clarity: Well written and organized with helpful symbol list, but should state convexity/strong convexity earlier and consolidate assumptions in one place.  
- Originality: Novel combination of biased MCMC gradients with fast-mixing constraints and explicit FPRAS complexity schedules for exponential families.  
- Significance: Conceptually significant by providing an alternative tractability axis and blueprint for end-to-end guarantees with approximate MCMC; practical significance limited until certifiable Θ and scalable projections exist.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Clear theoretical contribution, explicit complexity bounds, broad applicability, and transparent limitations that seed future work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Hard to apply in practice due to verification/projection challenges, minimal empirical validation, and some clarity/assumption presentation issues.  
- Suggestions: Add a concise “Assumptions” section, state convexity properties up front, clarify Lipschitz constants and estimation, and provide more empirical comparisons (Ising/Potts, pseudolikelihood, CD).  
- Suggestions: Discuss how to estimate/upper-bound C, α in practice, sensitivity to misspecification, and clarify sample independence and reuse strategies.  
- Minor issues: Fix typos/wording, check consistency of constants/symbols (e.g., R2 vs R^2), and justify example parameter choices (L=10 vs theoretical 97).  
- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept — conceptually original and technically solid but limited practical validation and strong assumptions; reviewer confidence medium.
