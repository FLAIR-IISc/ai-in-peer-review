- Proposes a novel notion of tractable parameter sets defined by "fast-mixing" MCMC behavior rather than graphical structure like treewidth.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis showing that constraining parameters to a fast-mixing set yields an FPRAS for maximum likelihood in exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Proves explicit convergence rates and schedules relating mixing rate (C, α), number of gradient steps (K), MCMC samples (M), and transitions (v) to target accuracy.  
- Separately analyzes unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases with distinct, rigorous results.  
- Theoretical results formalize and justify the common heuristic of using MCMC-based approximate gradients for learning energy-based models.  
- Technical development is solid with detailed proofs culminating in Theorems 5 and 9; analysis is thorough and meaningful.  
- Paper fills an important theoretical gap for MCMC-based learning methods and could guide practical algorithm design.  
- Writing and organization are strong; background on mixing times aids readers unfamiliar with the literature.  
- Main weakness: Theorem 7 (lower bound for the strongly convex case) is complex and poorly explained, making its implications and tightness unclear.  
- Suggest providing clearer interpretation or simplification of the lower bound under assumptions and relating it to known stochastic optimization lower bounds.  
- Suggest expanding discussion of practical impact, including how results might influence algorithm design and extensions to sample-reuse or more complex MCMC strategies.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — novelty, technical quality, and significance outweigh addressable presentation weaknesses.
