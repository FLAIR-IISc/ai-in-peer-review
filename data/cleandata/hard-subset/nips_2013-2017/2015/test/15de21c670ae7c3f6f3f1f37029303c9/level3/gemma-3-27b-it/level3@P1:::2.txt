This paper proposes a novel approach to approximate Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) in exponential families. Instead of restricting parameters to low-treewidth graphical models, the authors assume a “fast-mixing” parameter set, guaranteeing rapid convergence of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling. They combine this with proximal gradient descent, leveraging results from Schmidt et al. (2011) to establish a Fully Polynomial Time Randomized Approximation Scheme (FPRAS) for finding the maximum likelihood solution within the specified set. The analysis provides convergence guarantees for both the convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases, complemented by a simple experimental validation.

The work is well-motivated, offering an alternative to the common approach of restricting model complexity to achieve tractability. The theoretical analyses appear solid, though largely rely on combining existing techniques in a nontrivial manner. The authors demonstrate a commendable level of honesty in discussing the limitations of their work, including potentially overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions and avenues for future research. They also offer insights into potential connections with Contrastive Divergence (CD)-type algorithms, noting that their analysis isn’t directly applicable but provides related considerations.

However, the paper suffers from some clarity issues. Crucially, it doesn’t explicitly state that the log-likelihood is convex, a fundamental property underpinning the analysis. The authors should explicitly acknowledge this (through the Hessian being positive definite) and provide a concise summary of all key assumptions at the beginning of the relevant sections. A hidden assumption, that the parameter set Θ must be convex, is also not stated in the main text, which detracts from the paper’s completeness.

The practical impact of this work appears limited. While theoretically sound, proving fast-mixing for MCMC is a notoriously difficult problem in itself. This effectively replaces one challenging computational hurdle with another. Nevertheless, the reformulation of the problem and the associated theoretical framework may still prove valuable for certain applications. Several minor typos and wording errors were identified throughout the paper (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311-312) and should be addressed.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Presents a fresh perspective on approximate MLE, moving beyond traditional treewidth constraints.
* **Theoretical Rigor:** Provides solid theoretical guarantees for convergence, establishing an FPRAS.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the key concepts and results.
* **Honest Discussion:** Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest future research directions.
* **Connection to Practice:** Includes a simple experiment demonstrating the theoretical results.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practicality:** The requirement of proving fast-mixing for MCMC limits real-world applicability.
* **Clarity Issues:** Omission of key assumptions (log-likelihood convexity, Θ convexity) and a lack of a summary of assumptions.
* **Minor Errors:** Contains some typographical and wording errors.
* **Limited Experimental Evaluation:** The experiment is simple and doesn't thoroughly demonstrate the practical benefits.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound contribution to the field of approximate MLE.
* The FPRAS guarantee is a significant result, even if the practical challenges remain.
* The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of the relevant literature and clearly articulate the contributions of their work.
* The honest discussion of limitations and future directions is commendable.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The practical relevance of the work is questionable, as proving fast-mixing for MCMC is often intractable.
* The clarity issues and missing assumptions detract from the overall quality of the paper.
* The experimental evaluation is limited and doesn't provide strong evidence of practical benefits.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a substantial and original contribution. While the practical impact is limited by the difficulty of verifying the fast-mixing assumption, the theoretical results are significant, and the paper is well-written. I recommend acceptance, contingent upon the authors addressing the clarity issues by explicitly stating the convexity of the log-likelihood and the parameter set and providing a concise summary of key assumptions. Addressing the minor typos would also improve the manuscript.
