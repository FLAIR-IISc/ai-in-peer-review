- Problem: Maximum likelihood learning in undirected graphical models is often intractable due to impossible direct gradient computation.
- Proposal: Leverage fast-mixing MCMC to define parameter sets where MCMC converges rapidly and obtain an FPRAS for learning.
- Novel focus: Restrict parameter space by MCMC mixing-rate (not by model structure like trees) to make learning tractable.
- Main theoretical claim: Gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients approximates the MLE with high probability on fast-mixing sets.
- Cases analyzed: Separate convergence analyses for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) objectives.
- Convergence rates: Derived effort bounds are cubic in 1/ε for unregularized and quadratic in 1/ε for ridge-regularized cases.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on and contrasts with pseudolikelihood, score matching, composite likelihood, and previous MCMC-learning results.
- Generality: Framework applies to exponential families and cites rapidly mixing chain results (e.g., Ising model literature).
- Theoretical rigor: Provides rigorous proofs, clear mathematical framework, and a helpful proof sketch.
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well-written and includes a concrete example illustrating the theory.
- Practical limitation: Fast-mixing constraint is hard to verify/enforce in real-world scenarios, limiting applicability.
- Regularization trade-off: Enforcing mixing via regularization may over-constrain models and harm learning; needs further study.
- Parameter estimation issue: Dependence on Lipschitz constant L and sufficient statistic norm Ra, which are difficult to estimate in practice.
- Sampling assumption: Assumes independent MCMC samples each iteration and omits discussion of sample-pooling techniques common in practice.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel theoretical contribution, FPRAS guarantee, clear exposition, and relevance to a core problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: Practical enforceability of mixing constraint, parameter-estimation challenges, and simplifying sampling assumptions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, urging exploration of regularization to enforce mixing and more discussion on parameter estimation.
