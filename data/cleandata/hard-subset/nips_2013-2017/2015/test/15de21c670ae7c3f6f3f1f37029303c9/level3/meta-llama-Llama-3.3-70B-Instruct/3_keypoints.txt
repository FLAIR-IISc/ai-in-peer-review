- Proposes a novel top-down tree method for multiclass classification that aims for logarithmic-time training and testing using binary classifiers at each node trained online.  
- Introduces a new objective that promotes pure and balanced splits, which yields logarithmic tree depth.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise exposition of the approach.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including a boosting statement that guarantees effectiveness under certain conditions.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, tested on multiple benchmarks (including ImageNet and ODP).  
- Strength: objective function that balances purity and balance to produce shallow trees.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees (boosting statement) supporting the method.  
- Strength: strong experimental results across several large-scale datasets.  
- Weakness: theorem condition is difficult to ensure in practice and lacks guidance for enforcement.  
- Weakness: unclear applicability—no clear criteria for when the approach suits particular data types.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to other state-of-the-art methods; benchmarking is insufficiently broad.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the theoretical-condition, applicability guidance, and comparison limitations.
