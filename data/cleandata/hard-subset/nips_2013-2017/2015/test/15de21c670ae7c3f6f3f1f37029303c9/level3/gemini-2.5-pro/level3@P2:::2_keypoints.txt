- Main contribution: propose tractable parameter sets defined by MCMC fast-mixing and prove that projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is an FPRAS for ML within such convex fast-mixing sets.  
- Theoretical results: provide explicit convergence guarantees and complexity bounds for both convex (log-likelihood convergence) and ridge-regularized/strongly-convex (parameter-distance convergence) cases.  
- Novelty/significance: introduces a new, well-motivated notion of tractability based on dynamic mixing properties rather than static low-treewidth structure.  
- Practical insight: gives the first formal FPRAS guarantee for widely used MCMC-based gradient heuristics (including contrastive divergence variants).  
- Technical quality: detailed, apparently solid proofs leveraging inexact proximal-gradient results; authors honestly discuss limitations.  
- Main practical weakness: framework shifts difficulty to defining and projecting onto fast-mixing sets, which is often as hard as the original inference problem and may require overly restrictive parameter constraints.  
- Projection/computation issue: the complexity and feasibility of computing the projection operator Π_Θ onto the fast-mixing set are not addressed and may be prohibitive.  
- Pessimistic bounds: theoretical bounds appear potentially loose/overly pessimistic compared to experiments.  
- Clarity concern: key assumptions (convexity of negative log-likelihood, convexity of Θ, bounded sufficient statistics, geometric mixing) are not clearly summarized up front and are introduced late in the text.  
- Suggested fix: explicitly state convexity of the objective and Θ and provide a consolidated assumptions paragraph in the setup.  
- Acceptance pros: novel perspective, substantial technical contribution, and first formal FPRAS for MCMC-gradient learning justify acceptance.  
- Acceptance cons: limited immediate practical value and reliance on combining existing optimization/MCMC tools could argue against acceptance.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance despite practical challenges.  
- Minor editorial fixes: several phrasing and typo corrections (l185, l192, l207, l281, l311-12) and a missing Corollary 9 title to be fixed.
