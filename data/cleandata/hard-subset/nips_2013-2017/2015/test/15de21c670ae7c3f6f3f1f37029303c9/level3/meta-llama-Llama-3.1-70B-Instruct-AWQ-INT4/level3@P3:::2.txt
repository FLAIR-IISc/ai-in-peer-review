This paper proposes a novel approach to approximate maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) in exponential families by assuming that Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is fast-mixing and combining proximal gradient descent with the technique of Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain a fully polynomial-time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS). The authors provide guarantees for both convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases and include a simple experiment demonstrating the theory.

The paper is well-motivated, and the approach is an interesting alternative to small-treewidth assumptions. The theoretical analyses appear solid, although they are largely a non-trivial combination of existing techniques. The authors candidly discuss limitations, overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions, and directions for future work, offering insight into CD-type algorithms (although the analyzed algorithm is not exactly CD).

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a clear effort to explain the technical details of their approach. However, there are a few important clarity omissions. For example, the paper never explicitly states that the log-likelihood is convex, which should be noted via the Hessian = covariance. Additionally, the authors should summarize all assumptions, including the unstated assumption that the parameter set Θ must be convex.

The practical impact of the paper is limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is generally hard, effectively replacing one difficult problem with another. However, the reformulation may still be useful.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work. However, the paper is not a complete piece of work, as it relies heavily on existing techniques and does not provide a significant practical contribution.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and well-organized. The authors provide enough information for an expert reader to reproduce the results. However, the paper could benefit from a clearer summary of assumptions and a more explicit statement of the convexity of the log-likelihood.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel approach to approximate MLE in exponential families. While the approach is not entirely new, the combination of techniques and the application to exponential families is original. The paper adequately references related work and clearly explains how the approach differs from previous contributions.

In terms of significance, the results are important, but the practical impact is limited. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research, but the approach is not widely applicable due to the difficulty of proving fast-mixing for MCMC. However, the paper provides a unique theoretical perspective on CD-type algorithms and offers insight into the limitations of existing approaches.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to approximate MLE in exponential families.
* The authors provide guarantees for both convex and strongly convex cases.
* The paper is well-written and well-organized.
* The authors candidly discuss limitations and directions for future work.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The practical impact of the paper is limited due to the difficulty of proving fast-mixing for MCMC.
* The paper relies heavily on existing techniques and does not provide a significant practical contribution.
* The paper could benefit from a clearer summary of assumptions and a more explicit statement of the convexity of the log-likelihood.

Minor typos and wording errors were noted on lines 185, 192, 207, 281, and 311-312.

Overall, the paper is an original and substantial contribution to the field, but its practical value is limited.
