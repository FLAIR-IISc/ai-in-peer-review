## Review of "Maximum Likelihood Learning With Arbitrary Treewidth via Fast-Mixing Parameter Sets"

This paper proposes a novel approach to approximate Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) in exponential families by leveraging the concept of “fast-mixing parameters.” The core idea is to constrain parameters to a set where Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference converges quickly, and then combine this with a proximal gradient descent method inspired by Schmidt et al. (2011) to achieve a Fully Polynomial Time Randomized Approximation Scheme (FPRAS). This offers an alternative to traditional approaches reliant on low-treewidth assumptions, a significant motivation given the intractability of MLE in high-treewidth graphical models.

The authors provide theoretical guarantees for both convex and strongly convex cases, demonstrating a solid understanding of optimization theory. The inclusion of a simple experimental validation, while limited, is a positive step towards demonstrating the practical relevance of the proposed method. The paper is well-written and the authors are commendably candid about the limitations of their work, including potentially pessimistic theoretical assumptions and avenues for future research. This honesty, along with the insight into CD-type algorithms (despite noting the analyzed algorithm isn’t strictly CD), is a strength.

However, the practical impact of this work appears limited. While the reformulation is valuable, the reliance on proving fast-mixing for MCMC effectively shifts the computational burden to a notoriously difficult problem. The theoretical analysis, while sound, largely builds upon existing techniques, and doesn’t represent a breakthrough in those areas. Several clarity issues detract from the presentation; the paper never explicitly states the convexity of the log-likelihood (which hinges on the Hessian being positive semi-definite, equivalent to the covariance matrix), and a consolidated summary of all assumptions would be beneficial. A crucial, unstated assumption – the convexity of the parameter set Θ – is also missing from the main text. 

Minor typos and wording errors (l185, l192, l207, l281, l311-12) are present, but do not significantly impact understanding.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel approach to MLE approximation.
*   Solid theoretical foundation with FPRAS guarantees.
*   Well-written and honest discussion of limitations.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited practical impact due to the difficulty of proving fast-mixing.
*   Clarity issues regarding assumptions and convexity.
*   Theoretical analysis largely a combination of existing techniques.



**Overall:** This is an original and substantial contribution, demonstrating a strong technical understanding. However, the limited practical value and clarity issues prevent a strong recommendation for acceptance at this time. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the clarity concerns and a more thorough discussion of the practical challenges associated with verifying the fast-mixing assumption.
