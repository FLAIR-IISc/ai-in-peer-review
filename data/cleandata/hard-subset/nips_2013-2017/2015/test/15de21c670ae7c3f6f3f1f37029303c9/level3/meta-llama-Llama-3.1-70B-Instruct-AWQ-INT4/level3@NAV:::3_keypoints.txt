- Novel contribution: proves MCMC-based weight learning is a Fully Polynomial-Time Randomized Approximation Scheme (FPRAS) when parameters are restricted to a fast-mixing set.  
- Theoretical result: shows gradient descent with gradients approximated by MCMC converges to the maximum likelihood solution inside the fast-mixing set with high probability.  
- Separate analyses: derives distinct theoretical bounds for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (non-convex) case.  
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses clear, well-defined notation.  
- Proofs: proofs are plausible and thorough, though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Significance: provides a meaningful theoretical guarantee for convergence of MCMC-based learning algorithms.  
- Practical relevance concern: required parameter constraints for the theorems are unlikely to hold in practice, limiting applicability.  
- Open question: the paper does not address whether or how the fast-mixing condition can be enforced or achieved in practice.  
- Follow-up work needed: results are theoretically significant but require non-trivial additional research to become practically useful.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of approach; thorough convergence analysis; significant theoretical guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unrealistic parameter constraints; no method to enforce mixing condition; practical utility not yet demonstrated.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
