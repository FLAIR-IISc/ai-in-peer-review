- Proposes constraining parameters to a “fast-mixing” set Θ and analyzing projected gradient descent with MCMC-based gradient estimates for exponential-family MLE.  
- Shows FPRAS-style convergence: O(1/ε^3) for convex (unregularized) and O(1/ε^2) for ridge-regularized (strongly convex) objectives, with explicit schedules for iterations, samples, and chain steps.  
- Translates known geometric mixing (TV) control into provable convergence rates for biased MCMC gradients, complementing prior structure- and surrogate-based tractability work.  
- Builds on prior fast-mixing parameter projection and restart-chain literature, but provides new end-to-end sample/iteration/chain complexity guarantees.  
- Assumptions (bounded sufficient statistics, known mixing constants C, α, and projectability onto Θ) are clearly stated and used; Lipschitz gradient bound is standard.  
- Convergence analyses are technically sound, using concentration inequalities and inexact-gradient descent results; complexity claims match derived upper bounds.  
- Proofs are sketched plausibly but full proofs are deferred; dependence on constants C, α, R, L, λ, δ is transparently stated.  
- Theorem 7 (lower bound) is valuable but hard to interpret; needs more intuition, simplification, and direct comparison to the upper bound (Theorem 8).  
- Practical acquisition of tight mixing constants C and α and the computational cost of projection (e.g., spectral-norm constraints) can dominate and need more discussion.  
- Analysis assumes fresh independent MCMC runs each iteration; common persistent/pool sampling practices are not covered and may affect v.  
- Empirical validation is minimal (small Ising example); additional sanity checks showing scaling with v, M, K would strengthen confidence.  
- Clarity improvements suggested: make Theorem 7 interpretable, standardize notation for statistic bounds (Ra vs R2), and define constants once.  
- Add a summary table/paragraph of known projection operators for common models (Ising box constraints, spectral-norm, pairwise MRFs) with computational costs.  
- Originality: novel framing of tractability via fast-mixing parameter sets and provision of explicit FPRAS-like guarantees for biased MCMC gradients.  
- Significance: fills a theoretical gap by providing principled guarantees for MCMC-based likelihood learning and may guide choice of constraints/regularizers for mixing.  
- Pros: principled framework linking mixing-time control to optimization guarantees; general applicability; explicit schedules and rates; timely and original.  
- Cons: lower bound hard to parse; limited practical guidance on estimating C, α; projection costs abstracted; minimal empirical exploration.  
- Suggested additions/questions: provide practical recipes/refs for estimating C and α, analyze effect of misspecified constants, extend analysis to persistent sampling or restart chains, simplify Theorem 7 via a corollary, and compare projection vs sampling costs.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — original, technically solid contribution with suggested improvements around lower bounds and practical constants.
