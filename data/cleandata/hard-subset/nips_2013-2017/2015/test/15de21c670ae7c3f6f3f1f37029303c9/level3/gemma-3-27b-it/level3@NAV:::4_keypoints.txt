- Introduces a novel "fast-mixing" parameter set to enable maximum likelihood learning in intractable models by guaranteeing rapid MCMC convergence.  
- Provides a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood estimation under the fast-mixing constraint.  
- Analyzes convergence rates for both regularized and unregularized cases with explicit, practically meaningful bounds.  
- Builds on and contrasts with existing heuristics (pseudolikelihood, score matching) by offering theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Connects to tractable model classes (e.g., tree-structured) by using mixing properties rather than graph structure as the tractability criterion.  
- Presentation is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and technically thorough, with appropriate literature positioning.  
- Strengths: originality of the fast-mixing concept and the theoretical FPRAS guarantees.  
- Theoretical analysis appears sound and carefully derived.  
- Significance/impact is currently understated; reviewer unsure of the framework’s breadth and practical importance beyond the Ising example.  
- Lacks concrete examples or applications demonstrating where the approach is especially advantageous.  
- The lower-bound analysis (Theorem 7) is difficult to interpret and its practical implications are not clearly articulated.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on expanding discussion of impact, practical limitations, extensions (other MCMC algorithms, adaptive mixing), and providing an intuitive explanation of the lower bound results.
