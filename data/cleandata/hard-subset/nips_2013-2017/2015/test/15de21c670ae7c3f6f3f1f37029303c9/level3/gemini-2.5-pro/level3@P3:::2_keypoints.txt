- Addresses MLE in exponential families with intractable inference by constraining parameters to a “fast-mixing” set Θ where MCMC mixes quickly.  
- Proposes and analyzes projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients, combining proximal-gradient-with-errors analysis and MCMC mixing bounds.  
- Proves the procedure is an FPRAS for MLE over Θ, with separate guarantees: log-likelihood error for convex (unregularized) case and L2 parameter error for strongly convex (ridge-regularized) case.  
- Supports theory with a simple Ising-model experiment.  
- Novel conceptual contribution: defines tractability via fast-mixing parameter sets instead of structural constraints like treewidth.  
- Technically strong: non-trivial combination of optimization and MCMC theory yielding rigorous convergence guarantees and an FPRAS.  
- Authors are candid about limitations and relate their theory to practical heuristics (e.g., contrastive divergence) and sample-pool methods.  
- Generally clear writing and organization, with helpful informal proof sketches and detailed appendices.  
- Major practical limitation: requires a known fast-mixing set Θ with mixing constants C and α and an efficient projection operator, which is hard to obtain for many models.  
- Framework may simply replace one hard problem (intractable inference) with another (proving fast mixing and finding Θ), limiting immediate practical impact.  
- Important omitted/unstated assumptions: convexity of negative log-likelihood f(θ) not explicitly stated, and convexity of constraint set Θ (needed for projection) is not mentioned.  
- Suggestion to include a consolidated list of assumptions (bounded sufficient statistics, geometric mixing rate, convexity of Θ, etc.).  
- Minor issues: several typos and awkward phrasings identified with line references and suggested rewordings.  
- Overall: original and technically significant theoretical work that advances understanding of learning with intractable models, but practical applicability is limited by strong assumptions; suitable for NIPS.
