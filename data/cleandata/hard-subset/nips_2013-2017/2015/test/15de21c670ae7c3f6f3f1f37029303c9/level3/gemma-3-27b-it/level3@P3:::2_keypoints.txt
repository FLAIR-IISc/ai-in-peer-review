- Proposes a novel MLE approximation for exponential families combining fast-mixing MCMC with projected/proximal gradient descent, yielding an FPRAS.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for both convex and strongly convex cases, building on Schmidt et al. (2011).  
- Offers a well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth methods and reformulates the problem in a useful way.  
- Technical contribution is original and largely sound, with nontrivial application of existing techniques.  
- Combines fast-mixing parameter sets with proximal gradient descent in a valuable way.  
- Analysis is solid and authors are candid about limitations and pessimistic assumptions.  
- Insightful discussion relating the approach to Coordinate Descent–type algorithms despite not being strict CD.  
- Demonstrates the theory with a simple experiment (limited empirical validation).  
- Major clarity issues: never explicitly states that the log-likelihood is convex (only implied via Hessian = covariance).  
- Omits a vital assumption that the parameter set Θ must be convex and lacks a consolidated summary of all assumptions.  
- Practical impact limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC can be as hard as the original problem.  
- Theoretical complexity remains high, possibly limiting real-world applicability.  
- Minor typos and wording errors (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312) reduce polish.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, provides FPRAS guarantees, addresses a hard problem, authors acknowledge limitations, gives insights into MCMC+optimization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: difficulty verifying fast-mixing, clarity gaps about convexity and Θ, minor technical errors, limited practical applicability.  
- Overall: original and substantial contribution but limited immediate practical value; recommendation = Weak Accept, conditional on clarifying convexity and fixing typos.  
- Ratings summary: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 6/10.
