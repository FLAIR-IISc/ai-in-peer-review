- Problem: Addresses maximum-likelihood learning in high-treewidth Markov networks where exact gradient computation is NP-hard and MCMC approximations lack theoretical guarantees.  
- Main contribution: Proves MCMC-based gradient descent is an FPRAS for maximum-likelihood within parameter sets whose MCMC chains mix rapidly.  
- Originality/significance: Provides a novel theoretical bridge between a widely used heuristic and computational learning theory.  
- Technical quality: Thorough analysis with separate complexity bounds for unregularized and ridge-regularized cases; proofs appear plausible and formalization is sound.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and clearly presents the problem, framework, and results.  
- Practical relevance (weakness): Results rely on strong assumptions—restricting parameters to a fast-mixing set (e.g., small interaction strengths) that are unlikely in many real applications.  
- Constructive feedback: Explore algorithmic enforcement of the fast-mixing condition (e.g., specialized regularization) while avoiding over-regularization; correct minor typos.  
- Overall assessment: A solid theoretical first step offering the first formal guarantees for a common learning algorithm, but significant work is needed to relax assumptions for practical impact.
