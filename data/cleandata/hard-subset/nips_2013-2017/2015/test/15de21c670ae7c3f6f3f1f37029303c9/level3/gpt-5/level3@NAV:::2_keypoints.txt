- Proposes an efficient, principled method for approximate MLE in exponential families by restricting parameters to a fast-mixing set and using projected proximal gradient with inexact MCMC gradients.  
- Provides FPRAS-style guarantees: O(ε^-3 polylog) work for ε suboptimality (convex) and O(ε^-2 polylog) for ε parameter error under ridge regularization (strong convexity).  
- Analysis applies to general exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics and includes a simple Ising-grid experiment illustrating the theory.  
- Theoretical development is careful and technically sound, combining mixing-time bounds, concentration, and inexact proximal gradient analysis.  
- Assumptions are somewhat pessimistic but are acknowledged and discussed by the authors.  
- Experimental validation is illustrative but minimal, limiting empirical support.  
- Important clarity omissions: must state negative log-likelihood convexity (A(θ) convex) and explicitly summarize all assumptions in one place.  
- Paper should state convexity of the constraint set Θ (currently missing) and clarify step-size/schedule and sampling independence assumptions.  
- Originality: novel FPRAS framing for constrained MLE under fast mixing, synthesizing existing techniques; connects to CD-type algorithms.  
- Significance: substantial conceptual/theoretical contribution for theory audiences, but limited practical impact because certifying fast mixing for real models is hard.  
- Pros: rigorous explicit rates in convex and strongly convex regimes; broad exponential-family scope; honest limitations discussion with connections across literatures.  
- Cons: limited practical utility without verifiable fast-mixing certificates; minimal experiments; missing clarity on core assumptions and convexity statements.  
- Overall recommendation: original and substantial theoretical work; lean weak accept for a theory track contingent on addressing clarity issues.
