- Proposes learning exponential-family parameters constrained to a “fast-mixing” set to get an FPRAS for maximum likelihood.
- Core idea (approximating likelihood gradient with MCMC samples) is standard practice and not novel.
- Paper fails to situate contribution within stochastic-gradient/MCMC optimization literature (e.g., Monte Carlo EM, stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics).
- Comparison to other intractable-likelihood methods (score matching, composite likelihoods, pseudolikelihood) is superficial and lacks sample-complexity/convergence analysis.
- Theoretical guarantees claimed are largely subsumed by existing stochastic approximation theory (Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret).
- Assumptions (uniform ergodicity, fixed re-initialization distribution r) are unnecessarily strong and not well justified.
- Re-initialization with the previous sample (e.g., contrastive divergence) and other sampling strategies are ignored despite existing analysis.
- The claim that projection onto Θ is necessary oversimplifies—alternative techniques like sample pools can avoid explicit projection.
- Presentation is poor: key assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not stated near the theorems, reducing clarity.
- Theorem 6 relies on overly strong assumptions that seem unwarranted given prior work.
- Related work section lists many papers but lacks critical analysis of their relation to the contribution.
- The illustrative example is useful but acknowledges that theoretical bounds are conservative.
- Strength: addresses the important problem of learning with intractable likelihoods.
- Strength: provides a rigorous theoretical analysis (though not novel).
- Strength: includes a practical example to illustrate the method.
- Weakness: major lack of awareness of prior literature on related algorithms and theory.
- Weakness: results are incremental and largely covered by existing theory.
- Weakness: unnecessary/incorrect assumptions about ergodicity and re-initialization undermine applicability.
- Weakness: poor presentation and unclear statement of assumptions hurt readability.
- Weakness: insufficient empirical/theoretical comparison to alternative optimization methods.
- For acceptance: addresses a relevant problem and delivers a rigorous (if not novel) analysis.
- Against acceptance: significant literature blind spots, marginal novelty, problematic assumptions and presentation limit impact.
- Overall: not suitable for acceptance without major revision to address literature, relax assumptions, and improve presentation.
