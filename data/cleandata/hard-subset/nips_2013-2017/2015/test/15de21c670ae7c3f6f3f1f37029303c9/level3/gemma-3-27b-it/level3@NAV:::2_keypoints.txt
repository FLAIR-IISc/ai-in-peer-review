- Proposes constraining parameters to a “fast-mixing” set to enable MLE approximation with MCMC-approximated gradients.  
- Establishes FPRAS for both convex and strongly convex cases using projected gradient descent error analysis (building on Schmidt et al. 2011).  
- Offers an alternative to low-treewidth assumptions for handling high-treewidth graphical models.  
- Conceptually interesting connection to projected gradient descent and MCMC with clear theoretical framing.  
- Authors are transparent about limitations and outline future directions, including links to Contrastive Divergence methods.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast-mixing for parameter sets is often as hard as the original MLE problem.  
- The approach effectively shifts computational difficulty from MLE to characterizing mixing properties of the parameter set.  
- Clarity issues: convexity of the log-likelihood and convexity of the parameter set Θ are not explicitly stated, and assumptions lack a concise summary.  
- Experimental results are encouraging but basic and do not demonstrate benefits across a wider range of problems.  
- Several minor typos and wording errors need proofreading (e.g., lines l185, l192, l207, l281, l311-12).  
- Arguments for acceptance: original, substantial theoretical contribution and a new perspective on MLE.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical value, clarity problems about assumptions/convexity, and insufficient experimental breadth.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and, if possible, expand experimental evaluation.
