- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC inference provably mixes quickly in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.  
- Proves gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients can approximate the maximum likelihood solution inside the fast-mixing set with high probability.  
- Gives a bound on total computational effort required to achieve a target log-likelihood accuracy.  
- Fails to adequately cite or compare to prior literature (e.g., Monte Carlo EM, stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics).  
- Imposes unnecessary assumptions (uniform ergodicity and reinitialization from a fixed distribution r) instead of reusing previous samples.  
- Theorem assumptions and conditions are unclearly presented and should be stated explicitly near the theorems.  
- Novelty is marginal: results are not clearly distinct from existing work and the advancement over prior art is unclear.  
- Paper is technically sound but has poor presentation, limited theoretical/experimental support for claims, and insufficient comparison to related research.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection but notes the authors could address the issues and resubmit.
