- Proposes "fast-mixing parameter sets" and shows MCMC with guaranteed fast convergence yields an FPRAS for maximum likelihood in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.  
- Main contribution: proof that under certain conditions MCMC-based weight learning is a FPRAS.  
- Derives separate theoretical bounds for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (non-convex) case.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear plausible, though reviewer did not fully verify them.  
- Results provide a significant theoretical foundation for MCMC-based weight learning.  
- Practical relevance is limited: required parameter constraints are unlikely to hold in real-world settings.  
- Open question: whether the mixing condition can be enforced artificially as a form of regularization (possibly combined with ridge), with risk of over-regularization.  
- Conclusion: solid theoretical formalization but substantial additional research needed to make results practically useful.
