- Proposes learning in intractable graphical models by constraining to “fast-mixing parameters” where MCMC converges quickly.  
- Shows that restricting to fast-mixing sets yields a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood learning.  
- Proves gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients attains approximate maximum likelihood within the constrained set with high probability.  
- Derives convergence rates: cubic effort (1/ε^3) for regularized and quadratic effort (1/ε^2) for unregularized cases.  
- Results apply beyond graphical models to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Technical presentation is sound, well-organized, and includes clear notation and helpful symbol summaries.  
- Proofs are detailed and mostly in the appendix, with intuitive sketches in the main text.  
- Thorough treatment of MCMC gradient estimation challenges, providing rigorous guarantees despite bias and noise.  
- Includes analysis for both convex and strongly convex settings, strengthening robustness.  
- Novel reframing of tractability from graph structure to MCMC mixing properties, offering a new analytical lens.  
- Theorem 7 provides a lower bound on computational effort, but its interpretation needs more elaboration.  
- Practical significance and direct applicability to real domains (e.g., vision, NLP) are not fully articulated.  
- Limited discussion on limitations (sensitivity to mixing-time parameters) and on computational cost of projecting onto fast-mixing sets.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution, clear writing, and justification for acceptance despite needing more on practical impact and future directions.
