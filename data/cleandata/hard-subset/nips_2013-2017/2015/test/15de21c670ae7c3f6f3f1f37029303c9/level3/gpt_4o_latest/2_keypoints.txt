- Proposes a novel FPRAS for MLE in exponential families by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set enabling efficient MCMC gradient approximation.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for likelihood error in the convex case and parameter error in the strongly convex case.  
- Synthesizes MCMC mixing assumptions, concentration inequalities, and prior optimization results (e.g., Schmidt et al. 2011).  
- Technically rigorous with detailed proofs and convergence analyses.  
- Addresses the challenging problem of MLE for high-treewidth graphical models and offers a tractable alternative to tree-structured constraints.  
- Well-written introduction that situates the work within the literature.  
- Authors transparently discuss limitations, notably the difficulty of proving fast-mixing for MCMC in practice.  
- Outlines useful future directions like initialization strategies and extending to practical settings.  
- Strong assumption: requires fast-mixing MCMC for all parameters, which is hard to verify in real-world problems.  
- Limited empirical validation — only a toy experiment is provided, reducing practical appeal.  
- Some technical details (e.g., convexity of log-likelihood and parameter set) are not explicitly stated, hurting reproducibility.  
- Minor typographical errors and unclear sentences that detract from clarity.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for its theoretical contribution, but request improved clarity and more practical experiments or discussion to boost impact.
