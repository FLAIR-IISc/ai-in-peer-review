- Addresses maximum likelihood learning in Markov networks where exact gradients are intractable, using MCMC.
- Main theoretical contribution: proves MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS under a fast-MCMC-mixing condition.
- Provides separate theoretical bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly-convex) cases.
- Novel tractability notion: focuses on fast-mixing parameter sets rather than tree structure or high-girth assumptions.
- Results generalize beyond graphical models to exponential families.
- Appropriately cites relevant mixing-time literature and adapts bounds to the learning setting.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with well-defined notation and helpful proof sketches.
- Minor typos exist but do not impede understanding; full proof verification not performed but analysis seems plausible.
- Major weakness: the fast-mixing parameter assumption is unlikely to hold in many practical settings, limiting applicability.
- Empirical Ising-model example offers some support but is simplistic and relies on a modified Lipschitz constant, questioning generalizability.
- Open and important question: how to enforce or regularize for fast mixing in practice without introducing bias or heavy computational costs.
- Discussion of initializing MCMC with contrastive divergence is interesting but leaves how to adapt the analysis unresolved.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novelty of the fast-mixing concept, generality, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical applicability, lack of concrete enforcement strategies for mixing, and risk of over-regularization.
- Overall assessment/recommendation: solid theoretical contribution but practical gap remains; recommend weak accept with caveat that further work is needed to bridge theory and practice.
