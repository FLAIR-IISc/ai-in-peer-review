- Proposes an approach to approximate MLE in exponential families by assuming fast-mixing MCMC and combining proximal gradient descent with Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain an FPRAS.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for both convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Includes a simple experiment that demonstrates the theoretical results.  
- Well-motivated as an interesting alternative to small-treewidth assumptions.  
- Theoretical analyses are solid but largely a non-trivial combination of existing techniques.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, pessimistic assumptions, and future work; provide insight into CD-type algorithms (though the analyzed algorithm is not exactly CD).  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides enough detail for expert reproduction.  
- Important clarity omissions: never explicitly states log-likelihood convexity (Hessian = covariance) and fails to summarize all assumptions.  
- Unstated assumption noted: parameter set Θ must be convex.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is generally hard, effectively shifting the difficulty.  
- Reformulation may still be theoretically useful despite limited practical applicability.  
- Relies heavily on existing techniques and does not offer a significant practical contribution.  
- Originality: combination and application to exponential families is novel, though components are not entirely new.  
- Significance: provides important theoretical perspective but limited real-world applicability due to fast-mixing requirement.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid guarantees for both cases, clear writing, candid discussion of limitations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical impact, heavy reliance on prior techniques, unclear summary of assumptions and convexity.  
- Minor typos/wording errors noted on lines 185, 192, 207, 281, and 311–312.  
- Overall: an original and substantial theoretical contribution with limited practical value.
