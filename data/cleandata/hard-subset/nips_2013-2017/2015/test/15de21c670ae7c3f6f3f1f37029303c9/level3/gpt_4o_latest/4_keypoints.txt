- Introduces a novel "fast-mixing parameters" framework enabling efficient MCMC-based maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees showing gradient descent attains ε-accurate solutions with polynomial computational effort.  
- Delivers convergence rate bounds for both regularized (strongly convex) and unregularized (convex) cases expressed in terms of mixing times.  
- Fills a literature gap by offering a formal tractable parameter set based on mixing times rather than heuristics or tree structure.  
- Paper is technically sound with thorough, rigorous analysis and meaningful complexity insights.  
- Clearly written and well-organized, with useful explanations of mixing times, fast-mixing sets, and proof sketches.  
- Original contribution that could guide future research on learning intractable models with MCMC.  
- Potentially significant for probabilistic modeling and inference, with broad applicability to exponential family models.  
- Theorem 7’s lower bound is hard to interpret and needs more explanation and practical intuition.  
- Broader significance and impact could be better articulated, including applications, hidden-variable extensions, and links to methods like contrastive divergence.  
- Practical relevance is under-explored: theoretical bounds are conservative and the paper lacks empirical comparison to heuristic approaches.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on clarifying Theorem 7 and expanding discussion of impact and practical implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous guarantees, novel framework, and general applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: interpretability of Theorem 7 and insufficient exploration of practical significance.
