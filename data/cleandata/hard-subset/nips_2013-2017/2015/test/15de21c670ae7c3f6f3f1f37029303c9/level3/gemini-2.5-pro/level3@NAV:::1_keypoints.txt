- Paper studies ML learning for high-treewidth graphical models by constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing" set where MCMC is provably efficient.
- Authors analyze gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients and claim an FPRAS guarantee for ML within the constrained set.
- Strength: attempts to provide rigorous theoretical guarantees for a commonly used heuristic.
- Major weakness: poor awareness of extensive prior work on stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.
- Theoretical results appear to be special cases of established theorems (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret) rather than novel contributions.
- Paper makes overly strong or incorrect assumptions, notably requiring uniform ergodicity.
- Reinitializing the Markov chain from a fixed distribution each iteration is unnecessary and less efficient than warm-starting, which is standard and well-studied.
- Clarity problems: key assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated alongside the results, hindering verification.
- Overall contribution is marginal; the authors largely re-derive known results in a narrow context without proper citation of foundational work.
- Recommendation: perform a thorough literature review, situate the work within stochastic approximation and Monte Carlo EM, and clearly identify any genuinely novel analysis.
