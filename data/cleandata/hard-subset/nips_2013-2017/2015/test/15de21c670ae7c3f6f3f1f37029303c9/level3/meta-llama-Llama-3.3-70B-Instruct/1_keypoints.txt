- Proposes a novel approach for multiclass classification with many classes that achieves logarithmic-time training and testing.
- Introduces a new decision-tree objective that optimizes for pure and balanced splits at each node.
- Provides theoretical analysis showing effective reduction of average Shannon entropy across the tree.
- Proposes an online algorithm, LOMtree, that builds a logarithmic-depth tree via recursive splits using the new objective.
- Empirical results on benchmarks show LOMtree outperforms other logarithmic-time methods and narrows the gap with one-against-all.
- Clear motivation and significant practical potential for large-scale applications (e.g., NLP, search, detection).
- Manuscript lacks awareness and discussion of related literature on stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics and Monte Carlo EM connections.
- Presentation of theoretical results is suboptimal; assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are unclear or buried in the text.
- Some theoretical assumptions (e.g., in Theorem 6) are unnecessarily strong; existing literature offers more general results.
- Needs more detailed comparisons with existing methods and deeper theoretical analysis of the proposed approach.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to expand literature review, clarify theorem assumptions and presentation, and strengthen comparisons.
