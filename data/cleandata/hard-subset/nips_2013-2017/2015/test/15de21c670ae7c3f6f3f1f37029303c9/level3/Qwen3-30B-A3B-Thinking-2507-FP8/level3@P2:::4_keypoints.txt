- Problem: Addresses intractability of maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models due to difficult inference.  
- Approach: Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC converges quickly, enabling tractable learning.  
- Novelty: Introduces a new notion of tractable models based on fast-mixing properties, extending beyond treewidth-based approaches.  
- Theoretical result: Proves gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients converges to the ML solution within the fast-mixing set with high probability.  
- Complexity bounds: Unregularized case requires effort O(1/ε^3) for ε-accuracy; ridge-regularized (strongly convex) case requires O(1/ε^2).  
- FPRAS: Establishes a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme for ML learning in these exponential-family models with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Technical quality: Theoretical results and proofs are rigorous and carefully distinguish convex vs. strongly convex analyses.  
- Weakness: Theorem 7 (lower bound) is hard to interpret and lacks sufficient discussion linking it to the upper bounds.  
- Empirical validation: Section 6 example supports the theory and shows solutions can be tighter than specified accuracy.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, with consistent notation and effective figures.  
- Practical implications: Paper lacks detailed discussion of practical implementation, applications, and real-world limitations.  
- Significance: Fills an important theoretical gap by providing guarantees for MCMC-based learning, though practical impact needs more elaboration.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel framework, rigorous guarantees, clear presentation, and fills a literature gap.  
- Cons for acceptance: Need stronger discussion of practical impact, interpretation of lower bound, and implementation considerations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify the lower bound and expand significance/practical discussion.
