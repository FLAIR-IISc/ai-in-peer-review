- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set for maximum likelihood learning, guaranteeing rapid MCMC convergence and proving FPRAS plus convergence for gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients.  
- Conceptually appealing attempt to move beyond tree-structured parameter constraints by leveraging fast-mixing parameter sets.  
- Lacks awareness and citation of closely related methods (e.g., Monte Carlo EM, stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics).  
- Fails to compare against alternative approaches for intractable likelihoods such as score matching, pseudolikelihood, and composite likelihoods.  
- Results are insufficiently contextualized; many claims overlap with existing theory on SGD and noisy estimates.  
- Theorem 6 relies on unnecessarily strong/restrictive assumptions; should cite Benveniste, Métivier, and Priouret (1990) for more general stochastic approximation results.  
- Recommendation to reinitialize at a fixed distribution is unnecessary; reinitializing with the previous sample is standard, better, and well-analyzed.  
- Presentation and clarity issues: assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated near the theorems, hurting readability.  
- Claims of novelty are marginal—techniques and theoretical results are largely well-trodden even if the combination is somewhat new.  
- Practical example does not convincingly demonstrate a significant advantage over existing methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: appealing idea of fast-mixing parameter sets and provision of theoretical guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: major lack of prior-work awareness, overlap with existing theory, questionable/strong assumptions, weak novelty, and poor presentation.  
- Overall assessment: recommend rejection; needs substantial revision to address literature engagement, assumptions, clarity, and demonstration of contribution.
