- Proposes maximum-likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected models by restricting parameters to a “fast-mixing” set and using projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients.  
- Derives nonasymptotic, high-probability convergence rates for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics tied to mixing constants (C, α).  
- Shows unregularized (convex) case requires cubic effort in 1/ε, while ridge-regularized (strongly convex) case requires quadratic effort, giving FPRAS-style guarantees.  
- Provides explicit schedules for MCMC/run-length parameters (K, M, v) and frames “tractability via fast mixing,” which is original and valuable.  
- Connects clearly to prior work on tractable restrictions, surrogate estimators, MCMC-based learning, and prior fast-mixing projection/learning results.  
- Analysis is technically careful and sound, using Lipschitz/strong convexity, concentration inequalities, and inexact gradient descent bounds.  
- Main theoretical concern: Theorem 7 is hard to parse—role of γ and logarithmic factors, tightness versus Theorem 8 and Theorem 3 need deeper clarification.  
- Manuscript is well organized and clearly written; the example is helpful but lacks guidance on how to obtain/estimate mixing constants C and α in practice.  
- Originality is high: the fast-mixing parameter class abstraction and convergence-rate results fill a notable theoretical gap.  
- Potentially significant, but practical impact is unclear due to reliance on availability/efficiency of projection operators and realistic mixing constants.  
- Paper would benefit from broader discussion of application domains, comparisons to pseudolikelihood/score matching in sample/computational complexity, and follow-on directions (adaptive constraints, other chains).  
- Pros: original, technically solid convergence guarantees tied to mixing times; general exponential-family scope with explicit schedules and FPRAS characterization; clear presentation and prior-work connections.  
- Cons: Theorem 7 needs clearer interpretation and empirical intuition; practical significance and impact are insufficiently articulated; limited guidance on projection operators beyond Ising and on estimating C, α.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on clarifying Theorem 7 and adding discussion of impact, extensions, and practical guidance.
