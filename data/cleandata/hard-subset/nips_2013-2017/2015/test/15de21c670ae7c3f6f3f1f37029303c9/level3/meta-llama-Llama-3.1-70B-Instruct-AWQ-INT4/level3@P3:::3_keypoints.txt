- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing set" so MCMC rapidly reaches stationarity for high-treewidth undirected graphical models.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proving that when MCMC mixes rapidly, MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS.  
- Derives separate convergence bounds for the unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (non-convex) cases linking iterations, samples, and chain transitions to accuracy.  
- Proofs are plausible and the analysis appears rigorous, though not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Paper is well-written, with clear explanations, examples, and visualizations that aid accessibility.  
- Strength: provides a solid formalization of conditions under which MCMC-based learning yields polynomial-time approximation guarantees.  
- Practical relevance is limited because the parameter constraints required by the theorems are unlikely to hold in real applications.  
- Authors acknowledge practical limitations and call for further research to make the approach more applicable.  
- Open question: whether the fast-mixing condition can be enforced (e.g., via regularization), which could improve practicality but risks over-regularization.  
- Originality: novel approach distinct from tree-structured restrictions, offering new insights into MCMC mixing and learning.  
- Significance: addresses a challenging problem with potential impact, but practical limitations reduce immediate applicability.  
- Overall scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid theoretical FPRAS result, tackles a hard ML problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions limit practical impact, no clear practical solutions offered, limited generalizability.
