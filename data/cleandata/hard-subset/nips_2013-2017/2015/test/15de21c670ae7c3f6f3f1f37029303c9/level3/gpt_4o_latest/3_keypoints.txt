- The paper provides a theoretical framework showing MCMC-based weight learning converges efficiently under specific conditions, making learning an FPRAS if MCMC mixes rapidly.  
- Separate convergence bounds are derived for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases with rigorous proofs.  
- Introducing a "fast-mixing set" constraint on parameters is a novel generalization beyond tree-structured or variational methods.  
- Results apply broadly to exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.  
- The paper is well-written, logically organized, and includes clear definitions, proof sketches, and an illustrative Ising-model example.  
- Practical relevance is limited because fast-mixing and parameter-constraint assumptions are hard to verify or enforce in real applications.  
- The work does not analyze whether enforcing fast-mixing constraints artificially (e.g., with regularization) unduly degrades model performance.  
- Empirical validation is insufficient: a single illustrative example does not demonstrate broader practical utility.  
- Projection onto the fast-mixing set may be computationally expensive, but the paper does not analyze this complexity in detail.  
- Recommendation: pursue methods to enforce fast-mixing constraints and provide empirical evidence, plus deeper analysis of projection complexity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novelty in addressing high-treewidth intractability, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions limit applicability, lack of empirical validation, and insufficient exploration of projection complexity.  
- Final decision by reviewer: Weak Accept, contingent on conference valuing theoretical contributions.
