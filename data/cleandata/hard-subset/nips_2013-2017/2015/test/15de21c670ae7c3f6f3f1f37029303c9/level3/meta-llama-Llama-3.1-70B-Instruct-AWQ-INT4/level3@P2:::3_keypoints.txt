- Proposes constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC inference converges quickly for high-treewidth undirected models.  
- Proves MCMC-based gradient descent yields an approximate MLE with high probability when parameters lie in the fast-mixing set.  
- Main theoretical contribution: shows MCMC-based weight learning is an FPRAS under the fast-mixing constraint.  
- Provides separate bounds for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (non-convex) case.  
- Paper is well-written with plausible proofs and a clear, logically presented exposition.  
- Technical soundness is good, but the proofs were not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Practical relevance is limited because the required parameter constraints are unlikely to hold in real applications.  
- No empirical experiments are provided to demonstrate practical utility or validate theoretical claims.  
- Open question: whether the mixing condition can be enforced (e.g., via regularization) and how that would interact with ridge regularization.  
- Risk that combining mixing-enforcing constraints with ridge regularization could over-regularize and hurt accuracy.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution but significant further work needed to make results practically useful.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, well-motivated, clear presentation, meaningful theoretical implications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, lack of empirical evidence, unclear regularization interactions.
