- Paper studies maximum-likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set to enable efficient MCMC gradient estimation.
- Authors prove an FPRAS for likelihood maximization within the constrained set, for both regularized and unregularized cases.
- Approach is positioned as an alternative to pseudolikelihood, score matching, and composite likelihoods, and as providing guarantees for MCMC-based gradient approximations.
- Strength: problem formulation is clear and well-motivated.
- Strength: theoretical development is rigorous, with quantifiable computational bounds and treatment of convex vs. strongly convex cases.
- Strength: results are given in a general exponential-family framework, broadening applicability.
- Strength: detailed convergence and complexity analysis with supporting theorems and proofs.
- Weakness: insufficient engagement with prior work, particularly MCEM and stochastic approximation literature (e.g., Benveniste et al.).
- Weakness: assumption of uniformly ergodic Markov chains is overly restrictive and not reflective of many practical MCMC algorithms.
- Weakness: reinitialization strategy using a fixed distribution r is unnecessarily restrictive compared to common alternatives (e.g., reusing previous samples).
- Weakness: presentation issues—key assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2 are not clearly stated near the results and notation is dense.
- Weakness: novelty is marginal since similar convergence guarantees exist in prior stochastic approximation/MCMC literature.
- For acceptance: provides rigorous guarantees for a commonly used MCMC-based optimization and applies fast-mixing theory to ML learning.
- Against acceptance: neglects relevant literature, uses restrictive assumptions, offers limited novelty, and could improve clarity.
- Overall recommendation: reject—technically sound but diminished by poor literature engagement, restrictive assumptions, and limited contribution; revise to address related work, relax assumptions, and clarify presentation.
