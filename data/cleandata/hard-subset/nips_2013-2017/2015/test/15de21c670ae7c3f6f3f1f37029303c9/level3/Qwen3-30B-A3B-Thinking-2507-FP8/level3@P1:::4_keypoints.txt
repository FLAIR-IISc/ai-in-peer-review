- Proposes a novel theoretical framework for maximum likelihood in exponential families based on "fast-mixing parameter sets" guaranteeing rapid MCMC convergence.  
- Shows gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients can recover the MLE inside a fast-mixing set with high probability for bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) with convergence rates cubic in 1/ε for unregularized and quadratic for ridge-regularized learning.  
- Extends prior work (e.g., Steinhardt & Liang) by providing rigorous convergence guarantees for standard MCMC-based learning used in practice.  
- Introduces a new notion of tractability based on mixing times rather than structural constraints like treewidth.  
- Technical quality is high: Theorems 1–6 give precise bounds on transitions, gradient iterations, and sample complexity.  
- Theorem 1 (Lipschitz gradient) is important for the theoretical foundation; analysis separates convex and strongly convex cases with different rates.  
- Section 6 example demonstrates practical applicability and suggests the theoretical bounds are reasonably conservative.  
- Presentation and clarity are excellent: notation, mixing-time background, and proof sketches are well explained.  
- Originality is strong: fills a gap by establishing convergence rates for MCMC-based learning previously lacking in the literature.  
- Significance is underdeveloped: paper lacks sufficient discussion of applications, impact, computational savings, and domains where fast-mixing sets arise.  
- Theorem 7 (lower bound) is mathematically correct but hard to interpret; needs expanded discussion and comparison to other lower bounds.  
- Suggests future extensions (e.g., integrating modern MCMC techniques, applying to latent-variable models) and a more comprehensive limitations/future-work section.  
- Main strength: rigorous theoretical guarantees for a widely used practical learning approach.  
- Main weaknesses: limited discussion of practical impact/significance and unclear interpretation of the lower bound.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, original, and significant enough for acceptance.
