- Proposes tractability via constraining parameters to a fast-mixing set Θ where an MCMC kernel mixes geometrically, enabling MCMC-based projected gradient descent for ML in exponential families.  
- Proves end-to-end FPRAS-style guarantees: convex (unregularized) case ε-likelihood with Õ(ε−3) sampling, strongly-convex (ridge) case ε-parameter with Õ(ε−2).  
- Analysis applies to general exponential families, with the Ising model as a canonical example and known projection strategies.  
- Complements existing tractable learning alternatives and relates to MCMC-MLE, contrastive divergence, and classical FPRAS results.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated alternative to treewidth-based tractability applicable to bounded-statistic exponential families.  
- Strength: solid, careful theory combining mixing-time bounds, concentration inequalities, and inexact projected gradient methods.  
- Strength: provides explicit schedules for iterations, sample sizes, and chain lengths with near-matching lower bounds.  
- Strength: honest discussion of limitations and conceptual connections to CD-type algorithms.  
- Weakness: practical impact limited because certifying fast mixing and obtaining constants (C, α) is hard in practice.  
- Weakness: assumptions and convexity not summarized prominently; convexity of A(θ) and f(θ) should be stated early.  
- Weakness: convexity of Θ (needed for projection/analysis) should be emphasized up front.  
- Weakness: dependence on unknown/pessimistic constants (L, TV mixing) — need guidance on estimation and sensitivity.  
- Weakness: limited empirical validation — only a small Ising example and no comparisons to pseudolikelihood, CD, or pooled MCMC-MLE.  
- Weakness: practical algorithmic choices like persistent chains/pools are out of scope of the proof technique and should be discussed more.  
- Quality: technically sound with careful proofs and precise guarantees; empirical section minimal but consistent with theory.  
- Clarity: generally well written, but improve by summarizing assumptions, highlighting guarantee differences, and explaining projection operators beyond Ising.  
- Originality: new and interesting tractability notion with a thoughtful synthesis of existing techniques.  
- Significance: conceptually significant but limited by difficulty of certifying mixing and unknown constants; may inspire new regularizers or analyses.  
- Suggested fixes: explicitly state convexity/assumptions, discuss projection choices for general families, provide guidance for estimating constants, add empirical comparisons, and fix minor typos/notation.  
- Recommendation: original, substantial theoretical contribution deserving weak accept if clarity and assumption summaries are addressed.
