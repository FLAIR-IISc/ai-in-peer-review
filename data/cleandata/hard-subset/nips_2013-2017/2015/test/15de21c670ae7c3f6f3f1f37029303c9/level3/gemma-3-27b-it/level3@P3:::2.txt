This paper proposes a novel approach to approximate maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) in exponential families by leveraging fast-mixing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) and combining it with a projected gradient descent framework, building upon Schmidt et al. (2011) to achieve a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS). The work is a well-motivated alternative to traditional methods relying on small-treewidth assumptions, offering a theoretical guarantee for both convex and strongly convex cases. The authors demonstrate their theory with a simple experiment.

The paper’s strengths lie in its originality and technical soundness. The combination of fast-mixing parameter sets with proximal gradient descent is a valuable contribution. The theoretical analysis appears solid, though largely a nontrivial application of existing techniques, and the authors are commendably candid about the limitations of their approach, identifying overly pessimistic assumptions and potential directions for future research. The discussion of how this relates to Coordinate Descent (CD) type algorithms, despite not being strictly CD, is insightful. 

However, the paper suffers from several clarity issues. Critically, it never explicitly states that the log-likelihood is convex, a crucial detail that is only implicitly understood via the Hessian being equal to the covariance. A consolidated summary of all assumptions would significantly improve readability. Furthermore, the paper omits a vital assumption: that the parameter set Θ must be convex. 

The practical impact of this work is limited. While the theoretical guarantees are appealing, the difficulty of proving fast-mixing for MCMC effectively replaces one hard problem with another. Nevertheless, the reformulation of the problem and the associated analysis may still be useful in specific scenarios. Minor typos and wording errors were observed (lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311-312), which detract from the overall polish.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound approach to MLE in exponential families.
*   Provides FPRAS guarantees under reasonable assumptions.
*   Addresses a challenging problem with a well-defined framework.
*   Authors demonstrate self-awareness and discuss limitations honestly.
*   Offers insights into the interplay between MCMC and optimization.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited practical impact due to the difficulty of verifying the fast-mixing assumption.
*   Clarity issues regarding convexity and missing assumption about Θ.
*   Minor technical errors throughout the manuscript.
*   The theoretical complexity remains high, potentially limiting real-world applicability.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is an original and substantial contribution to the field, presenting a well-written, technically rigorous approach to a difficult problem. However, its seemingly limited practical value prevents a strong recommendation for acceptance at this time. While the theoretical framework is solid, the reliance on the often-unprovable fast-mixing assumption significantly diminishes its immediate applicability. 

**Quality:** 8/10 – Technically sound, rigorous analysis, complete work.
**Clarity:** 6/10 – Some ambiguity, missing assumptions, minor typos.
**Originality:** 9/10 – Novel approach, valuable combination of techniques.
**Significance:** 6/10 – Theoretically significant, but limited practical impact.



**Recommendation:** Weak Accept. I recommend acceptance with the caveat that the authors address the clarity issues identified, specifically explicitly stating the convexity of the log-likelihood and including the missing assumption regarding the convexity of Θ. Addressing the minor typos would also improve the overall quality of the manuscript. While the practical limitations are acknowledged, the theoretical contribution is substantial enough to warrant acceptance, contingent upon addressing the identified issues.
