- Proposes learning maximum likelihood in high-treewidth undirected graphical models by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set enabling rapid MCMC convergence.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis of learning convergence rates expressed in terms of mixing times.
- Introduces a novel class of tractable models ("fast-mixing parameters"), distinct from tree-structured tractability.
- Gives a comprehensive background on mixing times and fast-mixing parameter sets, making the paper accessible.
- Proofs are well-organized and convincing, and the presentation is clear and well-written.
- Includes a detailed analysis of the algorithm's computational complexity and practical implications.
- Experimental results support the theoretical claims and overall technical soundness.
- Theorem 7 (lower bound) is difficult to interpret and needs expanded discussion and broader treatment.
- The overall significance and potential impact/applications of the results are not made sufficiently clear.
- Recommends adding discussion of potential impact, future extensions, and follow-on opportunities to increase clarity of significance.
- Arguments for acceptance: originality of tractable model class, meaningful convergence-rate results, and comprehensive analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear/lacking exposition on the lower bound and insufficient discussion of broader significance.
