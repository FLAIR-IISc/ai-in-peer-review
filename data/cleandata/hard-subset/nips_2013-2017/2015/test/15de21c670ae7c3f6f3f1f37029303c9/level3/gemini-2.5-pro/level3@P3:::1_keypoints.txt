- Paper tackles maximum-likelihood learning in high-treewidth exponential families by restricting parameters to a fast-mixing convex set Θ and analyzing projected gradient descent with MCMC-estimated gradients, giving non-asymptotic schedules (K, M, v) and FPRAS-type bounds for convex and strongly convex cases.

- Strength: Addresses an important, broadly relevant problem in ML (intractable likelihood/gradient estimation).

- Strength: Sensible, practical approach of constraining parameters to a provably fast-mixing region.

- Strength: Aims for rigorous, non-asymptotic analysis of a commonly used MCMC-gradient heuristic and provides explicit complexity schedules.

- Weakness: Lacks awareness of extensive prior work on stochastic approximation (SA) and Monte Carlo EM; fails to cite seminal references (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret).

- Weakness: Makes unnecessarily strong assumptions like uniform geometric ergodicity (mixing from any start) that are not required by general SA theory.

- Weakness: Uses an inefficient algorithmic design that restarts MCMC from a fixed distribution each iteration, discarding useful chain state.

- Weakness: Ignores the superior and well-analyzed alternative of continuing the chain across iterations (persistent initialization), which avoids repeated burn-in.

- Weakness: Theoretical contributions are marginal—results appear to be special cases of established SA theory and may hold under much weaker conditions.

- Weakness: Presentation issues—assumptions for main theorems are not stated clearly and concisely alongside the theorems.

- For acceptance: Important problem and an interesting conceptual approach (learning within a fast-mixing set).

- Against acceptance: Failure to position work within decades of relevant SA literature, suboptimal algorithm choice, strong/unnnecessary assumptions, and limited novelty.

- Recommendation: Reject; advise authors to thoroughly review stochastic approximation and Monte Carlo optimization literature and reframe contributions accordingly.
