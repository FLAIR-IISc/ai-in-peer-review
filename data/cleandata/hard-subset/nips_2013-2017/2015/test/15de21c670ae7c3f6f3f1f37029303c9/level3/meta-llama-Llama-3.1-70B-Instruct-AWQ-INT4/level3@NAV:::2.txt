This paper proposes a novel approach to approximate Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) in exponential families by leveraging fast-mixing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) and combining proximal gradient descent with existing techniques. The authors provide theoretical guarantees for both convex and strongly convex cases, demonstrating the efficacy of their approach.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors candidly discuss limitations, overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions, and directions for future work. The theoretical analyses appear solid, although they are largely a nontrivial combination of existing techniques. The paper offers insight into CD-type algorithms, and the authors provide a simple experiment to demonstrate their theory.

However, there are some important clarity omissions. For instance, the paper never explicitly states that the log-likelihood is convex, which should be noted via the Hessian's equality to the covariance. Additionally, the authors should summarize all assumptions made in the paper. A missing assumption not stated in the main text is that the parameter set Θ must be convex.

The practical impact of this paper is limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is generally hard, effectively replacing one difficult problem with another. Nevertheless, the reformulation may still be useful.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their approach. However, there are some minor typos and wording errors noted in the text.

In terms of originality, the paper makes a substantial contribution to the field by proposing a novel approach to approximate MLE in exponential families. The paper is also significant, as it addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. However, the practical value of the paper is limited due to the difficulty of proving fast-mixing for MCMC.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper, but with some revisions to address the clarity omissions and minor errors.
