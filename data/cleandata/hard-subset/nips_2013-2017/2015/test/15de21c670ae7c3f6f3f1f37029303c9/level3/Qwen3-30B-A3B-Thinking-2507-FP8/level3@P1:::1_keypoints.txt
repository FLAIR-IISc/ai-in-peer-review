- Paper proposes constraining parameters to a fast-mixing set so MCMC-approximated gradients yield provable ML learning in high-treewidth graphical models.  
- Authors prove an FPRAS for maximum likelihood inside the fast-mixing set for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Theoretical results and proofs are technically sound.  
- Major lack of novelty: core claims (convergence with MCMC-approximated gradients) are already known from stochastic optimization and stochastic approximation literature.  
- Paper fails to adequately cite or compare to key prior work (Monte Carlo EM; Benveniste et al. 1987; Chen 1995; contrastive divergence literature).  
- Critical assumptions are unnecessary or incorrect: uniform ergodicity is assumed but non-uniform ergodicity suffices in prior work.  
- Unnecessary requirement to reinitialize MCMC from a fixed distribution each iteration; initializing from previous sample is more natural and analyzed in literature.  
- Theorems are poorly presented: assumptions are scattered and not stated clearly alongside theorems.  
- Strongly convex case (Theorem 6) relies on unnecessarily strong/high-probability error bounds that prior work avoids.  
- Lack of meaningful comparison to alternative methods for intractable likelihoods (pseudolikelihood, score matching, composite likelihood).  
- Experimental evaluation is minimal (simple Ising example) and shows no practical advantage; paper admits no practical contribution.  
- Presentation and organization are weak; relationship between theory and implementation is not well explained.  
- Overall originality is limited; the paper restates known results with more restrictive assumptions.  
- Net assessment: technically correct but marginal contribution, insufficient engagement with prior work, recommend rejection.  
- Suggested improvements: cite and compare stochastic approximation literature, relax ergodicity/reinitialization assumptions, state assumptions clearly per theorem, provide stronger practical demonstrations, compare to alternative estimators.
