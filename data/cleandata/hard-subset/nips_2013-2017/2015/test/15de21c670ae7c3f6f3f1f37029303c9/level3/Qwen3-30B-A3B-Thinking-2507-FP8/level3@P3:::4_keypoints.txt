- Introduces a novel approach: restrict parameters to a "fast-mixing" set to guarantee rapid MCMC convergence for maximum likelihood learning in undirected graphical models.  
- Reframes tractability from treewidth to mixing times, defining a new class of tractable exponential-family models.  
- Main theoretical result: with bounded sufficient statistics and fast-mixing parameters, gradient descent using MCMC-approximated gradients converges to the ML solution inside the set with high probability.  
- Provides convergence-rate bounds: achieving ε-accuracy in log-likelihood requires O(1/ε^3) effort unregularized and O(1/ε^2) with ridge regularization.  
- Strength: original, significant theoretical contribution introducing MCMC-approximable expectations as a tractable model class.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation that connects theory to practical considerations.  
- Strength: meaningful convergence rates yielding a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme for learning.  
- Strength: fills a literature gap by providing theoretical guarantees for commonly used MCMC-based learning strategies.  
- Weakness: Theorem 7 (lower bound) is hard to interpret and needs substantial expansion and contextualization.  
- Weakness: Significance and broader impact are not fully articulated and need stronger justification.  
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed comparison with related work on mixing times in learning, especially recent NIPS developments.  
- For acceptance: novel tractability notion, rigorous guarantees for MCMC-based gradient descent, meaningful computational bounds, and technically sound analysis.  
- Against acceptance: underdeveloped lower bound interpretation, insufficient articulation of impact, and inadequate discussion of practical implications.  
- Evaluation — Quality: technically sound and rigorous but needs improved lower-bound discussion.  
- Evaluation — Clarity: paper is clear and accessible.  
- Evaluation — Originality: represents a significant conceptual advance beyond treewidth-based approaches.  
- Evaluation — Significance: potentially important but currently under-argued; needs better connection to applications.  
- Recommended revisions: expand and interpret Theorem 7, add detailed discussion of impact and future extensions, and strengthen links between theory and practical applications.
