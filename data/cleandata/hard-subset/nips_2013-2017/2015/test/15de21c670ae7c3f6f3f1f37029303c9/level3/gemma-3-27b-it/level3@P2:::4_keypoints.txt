- Introduces a novel framework restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set to guarantee rapid MCMC convergence for learning in intractable models.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for gradient descent with sampled gradients, establishing FPRAS for maximum likelihood estimation.  
- Original idea leveraging fast-mixing as a tractability notion, applicable to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Connects mixing-time concepts from statistical physics with learning-theory convergence rates.  
- Clear, systematic presentation with rigorous mathematical derivations and well-introduced notation.  
- Includes a concrete Ising model example (4x4 grid) that illustrates practical implications despite conservative bounds.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations such as independent-sample assumptions and potential complexity of the projection step.  
- Discusses future work directions like sample pools and initialization strategies.  
- Strongly justified connection to related methods (pseudolikelihood, score matching, composite likelihoods).  
- Major weakness: Theorem 7 lower bound is hard to interpret and its implications are opaque.  
- Reviewer requests deeper discussion and intuitive reformulation of the lower bound and comparisons to existing lower bounds.  
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous proofs and clearly stated assumptions.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written and organized; example enhances understandability.  
- Originality: novel problem formulation and contribution distinct from prior NIPS work.  
- Significance: potentially high theoretical impact, though immediate practical impact is unclear and could use expansion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong theoretical guarantees, clarity, fundamental relevance, and generality.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear lower bound interpretation and limited discussion of practical impact.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for NIPS 2015 despite the lower-bound issue.
