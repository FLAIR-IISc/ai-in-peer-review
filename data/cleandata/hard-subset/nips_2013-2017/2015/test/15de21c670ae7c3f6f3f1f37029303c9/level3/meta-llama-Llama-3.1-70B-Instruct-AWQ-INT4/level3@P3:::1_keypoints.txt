- Paper studies "fast-mixing parameters" for exponential families to guarantee rapid MCMC convergence during ML learning.
- Main theoretical claim: gradient descent with sampling-approximated gradients can approximate the MLE inside the fast-mixing parameter set with high probability.
- Paper is well-motivated and frames the problem clearly.
- Presentation is poor in places, with unclear assumptions for Theorems 1 and 2.
- Authors assume uniform ergodicity and reinitialization at a fixed distribution r, assumptions that are unnecessary and potentially incorrect.
- Reinitializing from the previous sample is often preferable and has been analyzed in prior work, which the authors ignore.
- The paper fails to acknowledge related literature, notably connections to Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation with Markovian dynamics.
- The authors do not compare their method to existing approaches for optimizing intractable likelihoods.
- Results are not particularly novel; existing theory already covers these results and more general scenarios.
- Theoretical claims rely on strong assumptions (e.g., Theorem 6) that could be improved by citing more general results (Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret).
- Positive contributions: using a convex parameter set to guarantee fast mixing is an interesting idea.
- Positive contribution: the analysis of gradient descent convergence with errors in estimated gradients is thorough.
- Overall assessment: technical soundness is good but novelty is marginal, and poor citation and clarity reduce impact.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 6/10, Clarity 5/10, Originality 4/10, Significance 6/10.
