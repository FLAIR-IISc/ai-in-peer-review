- Novel approach: restrict parameters to a "fast-mixing set" and use fast-mixing MCMC for MLE in exponential families instead of small-treewidth assumptions.  
- Algorithmic contribution: combine proximal gradient descent with Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain an FPRAS for MLE within the fast-mixing parameter set.  
- Theoretical guarantees: convergence for convex (log-likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases with complexity bounds O((1/ε)^3) and O((1/ε)^2) respectively.  
- Technical quality: analysis is technically sound, well-motivated, and a nontrivial combination of existing techniques.  
- Originality/significance: extends beyond graphical models to general exponential families and meaningfully builds on mixing-time and optimization literature.  
- Empirical support: simple experiment successfully validates the theoretical claims.  
- Major omission: does not explicitly state log-likelihood convexity (Hessian = covariance) nor explicitly assume Θ is convex.  
- Practical limitation: proving fast-mixing for MCMC is difficult in practice, so the method swaps one hard problem for another, limiting practical utility.  
- Minor issues: contains distracting typos and wording errors and would benefit from clearer exposition regarding convexity and Θ.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and insightful theoretically, but practical impact is diminished by challenges in verifying fast-mixing conditions.
