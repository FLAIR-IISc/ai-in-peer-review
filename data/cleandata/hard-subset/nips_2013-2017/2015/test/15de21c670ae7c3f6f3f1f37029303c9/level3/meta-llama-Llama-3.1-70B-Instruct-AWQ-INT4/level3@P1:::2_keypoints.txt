- Proposes a novel approach to approximate MLE in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing and restricting parameters to a fast-mixing set.  
- Combines proximal gradient descent with results from Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS).  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for both convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases.  
- Includes a simple experiment that empirically demonstrates the theory.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions; fast-mixing restriction is more generally applicable.  
- Theoretical analysis is solid but mainly a nontrivial combination of existing techniques.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, notably that proving fast-mixing for MCMC is generally hard.  
- Practical impact is limited because the fast-mixing assumption is difficult to verify in practice.  
- Offers important insights into contrastive divergence (CD)-type algorithms.  
- Clarity omissions: the paper never explicitly states that the log-likelihood is convex.  
- Fails to summarize all assumptions, e.g., the convexity requirement of the parameter set Θ is not clearly stated.  
- Minor typos and wording errors present throughout the paper.  
- Overall assessment: original and substantial theoretical contribution; recommended accept with minor revisions to address clarity and typos.
