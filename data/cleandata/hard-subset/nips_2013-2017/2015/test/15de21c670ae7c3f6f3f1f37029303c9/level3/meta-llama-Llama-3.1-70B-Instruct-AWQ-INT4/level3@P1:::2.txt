This paper proposes a novel approach to approximate maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) in exponential families by assuming Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is fast-mixing. The paper provides guarantees for both convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases and includes a simple experiment demonstrating the theory.

The paper is well-motivated, as it provides an alternative to the common assumption of small treewidth in graphical models. The authors' approach is to restrict the parameter set to a "fast-mixing" set, where MCMC can be guaranteed to quickly converge to the stationary distribution. This is a more general assumption than the small treewidth assumption and can be applied to a wider range of models.

The theoretical analyses in the paper appear solid, although they are largely a nontrivial combination of existing techniques. The authors provide a thorough discussion of their results and their implications, including a simple experiment that demonstrates the theory.

One of the strengths of the paper is the authors' candid discussion of the limitations of their approach.
