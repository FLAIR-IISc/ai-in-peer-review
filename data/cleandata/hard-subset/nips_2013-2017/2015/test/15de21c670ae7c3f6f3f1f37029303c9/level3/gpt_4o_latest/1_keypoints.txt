- Introduces a framework for maximum likelihood in exponential families by constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing set" ensuring rapid MCMC convergence.
- Provides theoretical guarantees for gradient descent with MCMC-based approximate gradients, showing polynomial-time convergence in convex and strongly convex settings.
- Offers a fully polynomial-time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) and rigorous convergence bounds with explicit schedules for steps, samples, and transitions.
- Framework is general, applying to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.
- Fails to engage key prior work on Monte Carlo EM and stochastic approximation (e.g., Benveniste, Métivier, Priouret), weakening theoretical grounding.
- Lacks empirical comparisons to established methods (e.g., pseudolikelihood, variational approaches) to demonstrate practical advantages.
- Unnecessary and unrealistic reinitialization assumption for MCMC; using previous samples would be more practical and efficient.
- Theorems lack explicit assumptions and some results rely on overly strong conditions that existing literature already addresses more generally.
- Contributions are incremental; leveraging fast-mixing chains for optimization is not substantially novel without stronger empirical or theoretical differentiation.
- Presentation is unclear in places, making it hard to assess applicability and validity of the results.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical rigor, generality, and novel framing of fast-mixing parameter sets for exponential families.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient literature awareness, limited novelty, unnecessary assumptions/oversimplifications, and weak empirical validation.
- Overall recommendation: Reject; mathematically interesting but lacks originality, clarity, practical comparisons, and significance for a top-tier conference.
