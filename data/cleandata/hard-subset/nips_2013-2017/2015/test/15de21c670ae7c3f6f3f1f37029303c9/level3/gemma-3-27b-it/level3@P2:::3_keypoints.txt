- Problem: Maximum likelihood learning in graphical models is intractable; authors study MCMC-approximated likelihood gradients.
- Main contribution: Prove that under a “fast-mixing parameters” condition, gradient descent with MCMC gradients yields an FPRAS for constrained-parameter MLE.
- Analysis: Derives convergence rates for both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.
- Theoretical context: Builds on intractability results (e.g., Jerrum & Sinclair) and contrasts with pseudolikelihood, composite likelihoods, and tree-restricted approaches.
- Relation to literature: Leverages prior MCMC mixing-time results and stochastic gradient analyses.
- Strength — novelty: The FPRAS result for MCMC-based learning under a clear condition is a significant theoretical contribution.
- Strength — rigor and clarity: Provides a rigorous analysis, clear presentation, thorough references, and a simple illustrative example.
- Weakness — core assumption: The fast-mixing parameter condition is restrictive and unlikely to hold in many real-world models.
- Weakness — practicality: Paper gives little concrete guidance on enforcing fast-mixing (beyond constraining parameters) and risks over-regularization with proposed remedies.
- Weakness — verification: Proofs are sketched and rely on complex mixing-time results, making full verification difficult.
- Weakness — empirical gap: Limited empirical validation and only a simple example, so scalability and robustness are not demonstrated.
- Overall assessment: Interesting and solid theoretical work but of questionable practical relevance; reviewer recommends weak accept pending better treatment of enforcing fast-mixing (e.g., practical regularization strategies).
