- Summary: Proposes defining tractable parameter sets by MCMC fast-mixing rates to enable efficient maximum likelihood learning in otherwise intractable probabilistic models.  
- Method: Analyzes projected gradient descent where gradients are estimated by MCMC, proving convergence within the fast-mixing set.  
- Main theoretical results: Shows the procedure is an FPRAS with explicit schedules; unregularized (convex) case requires O(1/ε^3) total work for ε log-likelihood accuracy, ridge-regularized (strongly convex) case O(1/ε^2) for ε parameter accuracy.  
- Strength — Originality: Novel framing of tractability via MCMC mixing rather than structural constraints like treewidth.  
- Strength — Theoretical significance: First formal convergence guarantees for MCMC-approximated likelihood gradients with meaningful, non-asymptotic bounds and trade-offs.  
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and connects to relevant prior work with clear proofs and sketches.  
- Weakness — Lower bound interpretability: Theorem 7’s lower bound expression is complex and hard to parse or compare to the upper bound.  
- Suggestion: Provide asymptotic simplification for Theorem 7, directly compare it to the upper bound, and contextualize it with known complexity results.  
- Weakness — Understated impact: Paper downplays practical implications; needs clearer discussion of applicable model families and practical relevance.  
- Suggestion: Expand discussion on potential impact (e.g., deep energy-based models), extensions to advanced MCMC samplers or adaptive procedures, and projection algorithms for complex fast-mixing sets.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept; authors should revise to clarify the lower bound and expand discussion of impact and future work.
