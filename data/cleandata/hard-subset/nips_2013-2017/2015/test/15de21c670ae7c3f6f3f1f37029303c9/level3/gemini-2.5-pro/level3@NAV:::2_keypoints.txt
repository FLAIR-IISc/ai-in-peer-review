- Proposes a novel framework: constrain parameters to a "fast-mixing" set and use proximal gradient + MCMC to obtain an FPRAS for MLE in exponential families.  
- Offers a new alternative to methods like Chow-Liu and pseudo-likelihood for handling intractable inference in high-treewidth graphical models.  
- Main strength: rigorous theoretical analysis giving high-probability guarantees for the convex case (likelihood error) and the strongly convex regularized case (parameter error).  
- Notable contribution: theoretically grounds the common heuristic of using MCMC for gradient steps by combining existing techniques (e.g., Schmidt et al. 2011).  
- Authors are transparent about limitations and future directions; writing is generally clear and a simple experiment supports the theory.  
- Major weakness: the core fast-mixing assumption is often as hard to verify as the original inference problem, limiting practical applicability.  
- Clarity issues: should explicitly state that the negative log-likelihood is convex and summarize all assumptions in one place.  
- Important omission: the convexity of the parameter set Θ is crucial but not stated in the main text.  
- Minor issues: several typos noted (e.g., lines 185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312).  
- Overall: original and substantial theoretical work, but practical impact is constrained by difficulty of verifying the fast-mixing condition.
