- Introduces a novel "fast-mixing" parameter-set approach for maximum likelihood in intractable graphical models.  
- Provides original theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients.  
- Delivers fully-polynomial randomized approximation schemes (FPRAS) for the proposed learning procedure.  
- Clear presentation, well-defined notation, and intuitive proof sketches.  
- Extends results beyond graphical models to the broader exponential family, increasing applicability.  
- Effectively positions work relative to prior methods (pseudolikelihood, score matching, composite likelihood) and known mixing-time results.  
- Theorem 7 (lower bound) is hard to interpret and lacks sufficient discussion and practical connection.  
- Overall significance and practical impact of learning with fast-mixing parameter sets versus alternatives is under-explored.  
- Limited discussion of practical implementation issues (projection algorithm complexity, initialization strategies like contrastive divergence).  
- Theoretical bounds appear conservative in the provided example and need acknowledgment/examination.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous approach with meaningful theoretical guarantees that fill a literature gap.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear lower-bound interpretation and weak practical demonstration of advantage over existing methods.  
- Recommendation: expand and illustrate Theorem 7, add a section on impact and future extensions, and address conservatism/tightening of bounds.  
- Overall conclusion: solid theoretical contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and practical relevance.
