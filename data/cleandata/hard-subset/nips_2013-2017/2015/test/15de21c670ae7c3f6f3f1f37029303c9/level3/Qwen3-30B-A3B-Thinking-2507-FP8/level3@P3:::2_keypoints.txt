- Proposes using fast-mixing parameter sets so MCMC-approximated gradients yield tractable MLE, offering an alternative to small-treewidth assumptions.  
- Proves a FPRAS for MLE in exponential families when parameters are constrained to fast-mixing sets.  
- Provides computational bounds: O(1/ε³) for likelihood error and O(1/ε²) for parameter error.  
- Establishes a clear link between MCMC mixing time and learning guarantees, replacing treewidth-based tractability.  
- Treats both convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases with theoretical guarantees.  
- Builds on Schmidt et al. (2011) and existing mixing-time literature; strong theoretical foundation.  
- Omits key assumptions in the main text: convexity of the log-likelihood and convexity of the parameter set Θ (only in appendix).  
- Theoretical bounds are conservative; experiments converge faster than predicted.  
- Practical applicability limited because verifying fast-mixing and knowing mixing constants (C, α) is typically infeasible.  
- Analysis does not cover common contrastive-divergence variants that reuse sample pools between iterations.  
- Provides a simple Ising-model experiment that empirically supports the approach.  
- Notes minor typographical errors at several line numbers.  
- Overall: important and original theoretical contribution worthy of acceptance, but of moderate practical significance.
