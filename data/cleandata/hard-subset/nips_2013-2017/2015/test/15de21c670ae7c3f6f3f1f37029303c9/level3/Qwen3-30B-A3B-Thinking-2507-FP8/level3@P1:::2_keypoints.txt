- Introduces a novel MLE approach by constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing set" where MCMC converges quickly, providing theoretical guarantees.  
- Proves gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients yields an FPRAS: O(1/ε³) unregularized and O(1/ε²) for ridge-regularized cases.  
- Combines fast-mixing assumptions with proximal gradient descent analysis (Schmidt et al., 2011) to provide rigorous convergence guarantees for convex and strongly convex cases.  
- Framework generalizes beyond graphical models to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Offers an original alternative to tree-structured restrictions by focusing on tractable parameter sets via fast mixing.  
- Empirical demonstration on a simple Ising model confirms theoretical bounds but highlights practical limitations.  
- Proofs are technically sound, using appropriate concentration inequalities and mixing time bounds.  
- Major omission: does not explicitly state that the log-likelihood is convex (via convexity of A(θ)), a crucial assumption.  
- Major omission: does not state that the constraint set Θ must be convex for the projection operator and analysis to be valid.  
- These missing assumptions undermine technical rigor and clarity; the paper should summarize all assumptions up front.  
- Minor issues: typographical errors noted at several line numbers (185, 192, 207, 281, 311–312).  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is difficult, so finding applicable parameter sets remains challenging.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations and relate their analysis to contrastive divergence, while clarifying their algorithm is not exactly CD.  
- Strengths: novel theoretical contribution, rigorous analysis, clear ties to practical MCMC methods, and honest discussion of limitations.  
- Weaknesses: missing key technical assumptions and limited real-world applicability.  
- Recommendation: substantial theoretical contribution suited to NIPS acceptance despite limited practical impact.
