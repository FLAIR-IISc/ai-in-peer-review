- Introduces "fast-mixing parameter sets" to enable maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.
- Provides a theoretical framework relating learning convergence rates to MCMC mixing times.
- Restricts parameters to a fast-mixing set so MCMC inference provably converges quickly to stationarity.
- Main theoretical results: Theorems 2 and 6 give high-probability convergence guarantees for convex and strongly convex cases.
- Supplies explicit schedules for iterations, sample sizes, and Markov chain transitions to reach desired accuracy.
- Claims applicability to learning misspecified models, addressing a common practical issue.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented, with enough detail for expert reproduction.
- Original contribution: novel concept and a new combination of familiar techniques.
- Technical quality: sound theoretical analysis; not merely a position paper.
- Significance: advances state of the art on a difficult problem with demonstrable theoretical progress.
- Weakness: Theorem 7's lower bound is hard to interpret and needs expanded discussion and broader treatment.
- Weakness: More discussion needed on potential impact and directions for future extensions.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
