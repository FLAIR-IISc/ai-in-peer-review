- Introduces a novel framework: constrain parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC-based gradient descent yields a FPRAS for MLE in exponential families.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees for both convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (regularized) cases.  
- Shows total computational effort is polynomial in 1/ε for ε-accuracy.  
- Demonstrates the approach with a simple Ising-model example linking theory to practice.  
- Strong technical quality: careful convergence analysis of gradient descent with approximate gradients; proofs appear sound though some details are deferred.  
- Authors transparently discuss limitations, including conservative bounds and practical difficulties.  
- Important omission: the convexity of the parameter set Θ is assumed but not stated in the main text (only in the appendix).  
- Clarity is generally good, but missing explicit statements (e.g., log-likelihood convexity), several minor typos, and a clearer upfront assumptions summary are needed.  
- Original contribution: first theoretical guarantees for MCMC-based learning under a fast-mixing assumption, distinct from treewidth-based approaches.  
- High theoretical significance: rigorously connects MCMC mixing time to convergence of gradient-based learning.  
- Limited practical impact: verifying fast-mixing is difficult, so the framework may not yield a directly usable new algorithm.  
- Strengths: novel theory, rigorous guarantees, practical illustration, and candid limitation discussion.  
- Weaknesses: omitted key assumption in main text, limited applicability, conservative bounds, and minor typographical errors.  
- Recommendation: accept — substantial theoretical advance with clear novelty and sound technical quality despite limited immediate practicality.
