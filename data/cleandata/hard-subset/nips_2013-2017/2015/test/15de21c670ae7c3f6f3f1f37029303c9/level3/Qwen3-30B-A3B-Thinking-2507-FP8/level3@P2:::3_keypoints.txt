- Addresses gradient intractability for high-treewidth undirected graphical models.
- Introduces a novel tractability notion: restrict parameters to sets that guarantee fast MCMC mixing.
- Proves that for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients yields an FPRAS for maximum likelihood.
- Leverages and extends existing MCMC mixing-time literature to the ML learning context, filling a theoretical gap.
- Provides separate convergence bounds for unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.
- Theoretical arguments and proofs are solid, with clear notation and careful discussion of limitations.
- Main practical limitation: the fast-mixing parameter constraints (e.g., |θ_ij| ≤ β) are unlikely to hold in real-world models and severely restrict the parameter space.
- The paper does not offer practical methods to enforce or verify the mixing condition in applications.
- Combining fast-mixing constraints with ridge regularization may cause over-regularization; this interaction is underexplored.
- Significance is limited: important theoretical insight but poor practical applicability without further work.
- Recommendation: reject—valuable theoretical contribution but insufficient practical impact for acceptance.
- Suggested improvement: address enforcement of mixing conditions in practice or demonstrate meaningful applications where constraints hold.
