- Introduces a novel notion of tractability ("fast-mixing parameters") for learning intractable models via MCMC.  
- Provides a new framework and thorough background on MCMC mixing-time analysis.  
- Main theoretical contributions are convergence guarantees stated in Theorems 2, 4, 6, and 8.  
- Offers a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Technical claims are sound and supported by rigorous analysis.  
- Originality comes from defining a new tractable model class and novel convergence-rate results.  
- Lower bound in Theorem 7 is hard to interpret and needs expanded discussion and broader treatment.  
- Significance and practical impact of the results are not fully clear and require more discussion.  
- Related work is adequately cited and the distinction from prior MCMC-based learning methods is explained.  
- Recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing the noted weaknesses.  
- Suggested improvements: expand lower-bound discussion, clarify impact/future extensions, add examples/experiments, and discuss relations to other MCMC algorithms.
