- Proposes restricting parameters to a “fast-mixing” set and using proximal gradient descent to obtain a fully polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for approximate MLE in exponential families.  
- Provides a theoretically grounded alternative to small-treewidth assumptions for tractable inference in graphical models.  
- Situates work well within existing literature (pseudolikelihood, score matching, composite likelihoods, tree-structured restrictions).  
- Leverages established techniques (notably Schmidt et al. 2011) to give convergence guarantees; presents separate analyses for convex and strongly convex cases with different error bounds and complexities.  
- Empirical validation is limited but provides a simple preliminary demonstration supporting theoretical claims.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, including the shifted burden of proving fast-mixing for MCMC and connections to contrastive divergence methods.  
- Notes practical concerns about the computational cost of projection and potential for optimizing that step.  
- Major clarity omission: the convexity of the log-likelihood is never explicitly stated (Hessian positive definite/covariance should be noted).  
- Missing concise summary/list of key assumptions; parameter set Θ convexity is implicitly used but not explicitly stated.  
- Contains minor typos and awkward phrasing in several places that need revision.  
- Practical impact is limited because proving fast-mixing remains difficult, so applicability depends on cases where fast-mixing can be guaranteed.  
- Quality: technically sound and rigorous; combines existing results insightfully for a novel application.  
- Originality: approach of leveraging fast-mixing parameters with gradient methods is novel, though tools are largely existing.  
- Significance: addresses an important inference challenge; theoretical contribution is substantial and could inspire future work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, novel approach, clear motivation, and candid limitations discussion.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical applicability due to fast-mixing verification, clarity omissions about assumptions/convexity, and limited empirical evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to explicitly state convexity, list key assumptions, and fix typos.
