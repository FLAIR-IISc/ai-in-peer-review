- Proposes using parameter sets that guarantee fast-mixing to make MCMC-based maximum likelihood learning tractable within those sets.
- Shows gradient descent with MCMC gradient estimates (FOPD) yields an FPRAS for maximum likelihood within the constrained fast-mixing set.
- Provides convergence guarantees and complexity analysis for both convex and strongly convex cases.
- Framework applies to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.
- Positions work relative to pseudolikelihood, score matching, composite/local learning, Chow-Liu, and high-girth models.
- Connects appropriately to mixing time theory (Jerrum & Sinclair, Hayes, Dyer et al.) and to Schmidt et al.’s analysis of projected gradient descent with errors.
- Novel perspective: focusing on computationally efficient parameter sets rather than tractable model structures.
- Theoretical analysis is comprehensive and appears mathematically sound.
- General approach has broad applicability beyond graphical models.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful notation/symbols.
- Includes an illustrative Ising model example and discusses projection complexity.
- Practical applicability hinges on identifying parameter sets that are both fast-mixing and expressive enough to model real data.
- Projection steps onto the constrained set may be computationally expensive and a practical bottleneck.
- Theoretical bounds are likely conservative; observed Lipschitz behavior in examples is faster than theory predicts.
- Dependence on parameters like R (norm of sufficient statistics) could be problematic for large-norm data.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a single illustrative example; broader experiments are needed.
- Lacks an explicit comparison to variational inference and a discussion of trade-offs between the approaches.
- Role and selection of the regularization parameter λ need more nuanced discussion.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically rigorous, general, well-written, and likely to inspire new research.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear practical applicability, conservative theoretical bounds, and limited empirical validation.
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical contribution; novelty and rigor justify acceptance despite practical concerns.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested minor revisions: expand discussion of practical considerations and projection challenges.
- Requested minor revisions: investigate/tighten theoretical bounds and dependencies.
- Requested minor revisions: add a brief discussion comparing to variational inference.
- Requested minor revisions: include a paragraph on limitations and future directions.
