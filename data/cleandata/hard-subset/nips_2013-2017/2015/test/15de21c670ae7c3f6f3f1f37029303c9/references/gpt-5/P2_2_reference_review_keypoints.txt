- Paper studies ML learning in exponential families constrained to a convex “fast-mixing” parameter set Θ and analyzes projected gradient descent using short MCMC runs for gradient estimates.
- Provides non-asymptotic convergence guarantees in two regimes: convex (objective suboptimality) and strongly convex (parameter distance).
- Derives constructive schedules for iterations, MCMC runs, and mixing time (K, M, v) with total work bounds and near-matching lower bounds, claiming an FPRAS-like result for MLE in Θ.
- Results are stated for arbitrary exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, not just specific graphical models.
- Concrete complexity: O(εf^−3 log(1/εf)) in the convex case and O(εθ^−2 polylog) in the strongly convex case.
- Good placement in literature: connects to prior MCMC-MLE work, contrastive/pseudolikelihood methods, and fast-mixing parameter set results for Ising/MRFs.
- Honest discussion of limitations such as projection cost, necessity of independent samples, and conservatism of bounds; includes a small illustrative example.
- Major practicality concern: requires a convex Θ with known projection operator and known mixing constants C, α, which are often unknown or conservative in practice.
- Analysis assumes fresh independent MCMC chains per iteration, unlike common persistent-chain practice, and does not analyze reuse/persistent sampling.
- Terminology issue: calling the result an “FPRAS” is misleading for optimization; approximation notion (additive vs relative) should be clarified or terminology adjusted.
- Technical concern: the TV-Lipschitz gradient bound ∥pθ−pφ∥TV ≤ 2 R2 ∥θ−φ∥2 needs more detailed justification and constants may be loose; notation R2 is confusing.
- Experimental validation is weak: only a toy 4×4 Ising example and no comparisons to alternatives or larger-scale empirical evaluation.
- Scope concern: unclear how broadly one can construct useful Θ with efficient projection and valid mixing certificates beyond pairwise Ising-like models.
- Algorithmic-model concern: resetting chains each iteration is analytically convenient but potentially inefficient; no partial analysis for persistent/pool-based sampling is provided.
- Theoretical development appears sound, leveraging concentration and inexact gradient descent results, but some proof steps are sketched and full appendix proofs are needed.
- Clarity issues: overloaded notation (R2), FPRAS usage, and ambiguous references to which MCMC chain is assumed in examples should be fixed.
- Originality: novelty lies in a unified, non-asymptotic analysis of projected gradient with biased MCMC gradients and near-matching complexity lower bounds for general exponential families.
- Significance: moderate-to-good theoretical significance by providing finite-sample guarantees for a common heuristic, but practical impact limited by strong assumptions and conservative mixing bounds.
- Suggestions: clarify approximation notion/FPRAS terminology, provide full TV-Lipschitz proofs and tighten constants, discuss estimating/adapting unknown C, α, add larger experiments and comparisons, and analyze persistent-chain sampling.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid, general theoretical contribution worth publication after addressing terminology, proof detail, and experimental/practical concerns.
