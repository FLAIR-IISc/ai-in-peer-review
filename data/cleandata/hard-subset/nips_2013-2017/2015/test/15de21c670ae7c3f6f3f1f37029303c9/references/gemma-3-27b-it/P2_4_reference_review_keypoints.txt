- Proposes using "fast-mixing parameter sets" plus projection to obtain an FPRAS for maximum likelihood via MCMC-based approximate gradient descent.  
- Provides theoretical convergence and computational complexity guarantees for both convex and strongly convex cases.  
- Frames the results in the general exponential family setting, increasing applicability beyond specific graphical models.  
- Novel contribution linking fast-mixing Markov chains to FPRAS for likelihood learning.  
- Rigorous mathematical analysis of iterations, sample complexity, and Markov chain transitions needed for desired accuracy.  
- Includes practical discussion and an Ising model example; notes limitations relative to common MCMC heuristics like contrastive divergence.  
- Generally well-organized and clearly motivated; notation is consistent.  
- Practical impact unclear due to potential overhead of projecting onto fast-mixing parameter sets in many models.  
- Strong assumption: requires known fast-mixing parameter sets and efficient projection operators, which may be hard to identify.  
- Analysis depends on constants (e.g., C and α) that are difficult to estimate in practice.  
- Complexity bounds involve many parameters (ε, R, L, etc.), making scalability and practical runtime intuition difficult.  
- Dependence on sufficient-statistics norm bound (R) could be problematic in high-dimensional settings.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (primarily a single Ising example) and lacks diverse datasets/models.  
- Some sections (notably Section 3.1) are dense and the connection to standard mixing-time results could be clearer.  
- Technically sound and well-motivated, but strong assumptions reduce immediate applicability.  
- Original and potentially significant theoretically, but practical significance is currently limited.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, generality to exponential families, and rigorous guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong/hard-to-verify assumptions, limited empirical validation, and complex parameter-dependent bounds.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution with potential, but requires more practical methods for finding fast-mixing sets and broader empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept — accept but encourage addressing practical identification of fast-mixing sets and expanded experiments.
