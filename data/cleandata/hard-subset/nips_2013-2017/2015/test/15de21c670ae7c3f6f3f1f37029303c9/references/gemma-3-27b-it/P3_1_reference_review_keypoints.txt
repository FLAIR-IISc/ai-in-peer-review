- Proposes restricting learning to parameter sets that guarantee fast MCMC mixing to enable approximate MLE for high-treewidth models.  
- Proves an FPRAS for maximum likelihood within such fast-mixing parameter sets, with theoretical guarantees for gradient descent plus MCMC.  
- Analysis covers both convex and strongly convex cases and provides computational bounds for desired accuracy.  
- Frames results for general exponential families, extending beyond graphical models.  
- Includes an illustrative Ising model example to ground the theory.  
- Positions work relative to pseudolikelihood, score matching, and structure-learning methods, highlighting differences.  
- Connects to prior MCMC-based learning literature and mixing-time results for graphical models.  
- Novel idea: defining tractability via mixing time rather than explicit model structure.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with careful, rigorous analysis and clear presentation.  
- Well-written, clearly notated paper with comprehensive related work.  
- Practical relevance is uncertain because knowing or bounding mixing rates a priori is difficult in real-world models.  
- Projection onto the tractable parameter set Θ may be computationally expensive and is not deeply analyzed.  
- The geometric mixing-rate assumption is strong and may not hold for real MCMC chains; robustness to slower mixing is not explored.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a single example, leaving practical viability untested.  
- Theoretical bounds appear conservative, reducing immediate practical impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, general, and well-presented contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical limitations around mixing-rate knowledge, projection complexity, and limited empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for expanded discussion on projection complexity, robustness to mixing rates, an additional empirical example, and clarification on how to find fast-mixing parameter sets.
