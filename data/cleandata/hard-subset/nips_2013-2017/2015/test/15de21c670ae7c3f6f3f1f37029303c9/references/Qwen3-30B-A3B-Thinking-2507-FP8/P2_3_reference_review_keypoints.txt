- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set Θ so MCMC sampling mixes quickly, enabling MCMC-based gradient descent to find the ML solution within Θ.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: total computation scales as (1/ϵ)^3 log(1/ϵ) (convex) and (1/ϵ)^2 log(1/ϵ) (strongly convex), giving an FPRAS under Θ.  
- Establishes Lipschitz continuity of the gradient and rigorous convergence results (Theorems 1–9) combining optimization and MCMC mixing analysis.  
- Proof techniques and bounds are carefully derived and logically sound, with attention to constants and conservatism.  
- Acknowledges conservative theoretical bounds and that the example achieved tighter performance than the theory predicted.  
- Assumes the fast-mixing set Θ is known, which is a strong and potentially limiting assumption.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear notation, intuitive proof sketches, and full proofs in the appendix.  
- Original contribution: introducing fast-mixing parameter sets as a tractability condition distinct from tree- or high-girth restrictions.  
- General applicability to exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics and clear distinction from pseudolikelihood/score matching approaches.  
- Significant theoretical advance showing ML learning can be polynomial-time under fast-mixing, informing when MCMC-based learning is feasible.  
- Practical relevance is limited; theoretical results are not strongly emphasized as non-practical and may be conservative for practitioners.  
- Projection onto Θ is treated as "trivial" for some cases but the computational complexity of projection for complex/large models is not addressed.  
- No empirical comparisons to alternative methods (pseudolikelihood, score matching); a theoretical sample-complexity comparison would help context.  
- Relies on a strong geometric mixing-time assumption (Equation 2) without detailed comparison to existing graphical-model mixing-time bounds.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contribution, rigorous and well-presented despite practical limitations and conservative bounds.
