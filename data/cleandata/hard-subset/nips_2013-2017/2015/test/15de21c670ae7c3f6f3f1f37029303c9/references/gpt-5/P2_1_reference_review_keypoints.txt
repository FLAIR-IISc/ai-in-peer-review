- Paper studies MLE for exponential families using projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients over a convex “fast-mixing” parameter set Θ, giving finite-time high-probability convergence bounds.  
- Main theoretical results: convex case achieves additive ε_f-optimality with total transitions O(ε^{-3} log(1/ε)); strongly convex (ridge) case achieves parameter error ε_θ with O(ε^{-2} log(1/ε)); includes step-size/schedule choices and near-optimal lower bounds.  
- Connects to prior work on MLE with intractable partition functions (Jerrum–Sinclair; Geyer; Gu & Zhu), surrogate estimators (pseudolikelihood, score matching), and fast-mixing parameter sets (Hayes; Dyer–Goldberg–Jerrum; Domke & Liu), and relates to Steinhardt & Liang.  
- Technical approach is sound: uses standard smoothness/convexity and inexact projected gradient analyses, with bias/variance control via concentration inequalities and mixing-time bounds.  
- Key assumptions: bounded sufficient statistics, convex Θ with geometric mixing parameters (C, α), and efficiently computable Euclidean projection — reasonable for some models but potentially conservative/hard in general.  
- Proof completeness concerns: main text sketches proofs; needs an explicit proof or citation for the TV-distance-to-parameter-distance Lipschitz bound that underlies gradient smoothness, and Algorithm 1 should be fully specified.  
- Empirical validation is minimal: only a toy Ising example provided; more experiments (e.g., Potts models, different Θ and chains) would strengthen the paper.  
- Clarity is generally good and organization helpful, but notation (e.g., R2) should be standardized, references completed, Algorithm 1 included, and “FPRAS” definition clarified (additive vs multiplicative).  
- Originality: offers a novel synthesis giving finite-time MLE guarantees for general exponential families under fast-mixing constraints, with matching lower bounds.  
- Significance: conceptually important as an alternative to low-treewidth and surrogate methods, showing polynomial tractability under verifiable fast-mixing constraints; practical impact depends on non-vacuous Θ and projection efficiency.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled finite-time analysis of MCMC-based MLE, concrete schedules with near-matching lower bounds, and potential to influence model restriction practices.  
- Cons for acceptance: practicality limited by conservative/unknown mixing constants and projection costs, some deferred technical steps (TV-Lipschitz), minimal empirical support, and FPRAS terminology ambiguity.  
- Suggested fixes: define approximation notion precisely, provide TV-to-parameter bound proof or citation and constant, state Algorithm 1 with step-size/projection/sampling details, discuss projection complexity beyond pairwise Ising and approximate projections, address adaptation to unknown C and α (e.g., doubling schedules), consider persistent chains/pools, and add more experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
