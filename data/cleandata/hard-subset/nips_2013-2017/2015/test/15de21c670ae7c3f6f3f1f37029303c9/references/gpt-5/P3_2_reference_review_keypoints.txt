- Paper studies ML in exponential families under a novel tractability notion: parameters constrained to Θ where an MCMC kernel mixes geometrically fast with known constants.  
- Analyses projected gradient descent using v-step chain restarts and M samples per gradient estimate.  
- Provides a Lipschitz-gradient bound for regularized negative log-likelihood under bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Gives high-probability convergence rates: convex case (λ=0) with objective suboptimality and total sampling work O(εf^-3 log(1/εf)).  
- Gives high-probability rates: strongly convex case (λ>0) with parameter error and work O(εθ^-2 polylog(1/εθ)).  
- Presents schedules for K, M, v achieving these rates up to logs and gives lower bounds showing near-optimality in their model.  
- Instantiates Θ for fast-mixing conditions in Ising/MRFs and shows a small illustrative experiment.  
- Main message: with a projection oracle and uniform geometric mixing guarantees on Θ, MCMC-based ML yields a polynomial-time randomized approximation scheme.  
- Positions work relative to low-treewidth and likelihood-alternative approaches and connects to MCMC-ML, contrastive divergence, and prior projection-to-fast-mixing research.  
- Technical development appears sound, using TV contraction, concentration inequalities, and inexact projected gradient analysis.  
- Request to clarify/define “FPRAS” precisely for optimization, including input encoding, problem size, and bit-complexity of Θ’s oracle.  
- Ask for full proof or clear citation for the Lipschitz constant ∥pθ−pφ∥TV ≤ 2R^2∥θ−φ∥2 and cleaner, unambiguous notation for bounded statistics.  
- Note that assumed uniform mixing constants (C, α) are often loose in practice; request discussion of dependence on N, degree, and graph structure and effect on theorem constants.  
- Point out projection costs (e.g., SVD for spectral-norm constraints) may dominate KMv sampling cost; request quantification of projection vs sampling cost.  
- Challenge independence assumption of M restarts per iteration; suggest extending analysis to dependent samples or giving cost/gap tradeoffs when reusing chains.  
- Recommend adding statistical/population-risk guarantees showing how dataset size D trades off with optimization error.  
- Ask for explicit algorithmic details: step-size, projection norm, handling λ=0, and fixing inconsistencies/typos in the example section.  
- Praise originality: combines fast-mixing constraints, nonasymptotic MCMC-based gradient descent rates, and near-matching lower bounds in general exponential families.  
- Acknowledge theoretical significance but note practical applicability limited by required certified mixing constants and projection oracles; constants may be large.  
- Suggest future directions: designing Θ with better mixing–projection tradeoffs, adaptive v/M choices, and analysis of persistent/contrastive-divergence variants.  
- Clarity is generally good but request a concise pseudocode box for Algorithm 1 and fixes for notation/typos.  
- Pros: principled analysis with explicit rates, works for general exponential families, near-matching bounds, honest limitations discussion.  
- Cons: ambiguous FPRAS terminology, loose/large constants dependent on N, need known mixing constants and efficient projections, assumes independent per-iteration samples, minor clarity issues.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on clarifying the FPRAS claim, tightening notation, and expanding discussion of constants and practical considerations.
