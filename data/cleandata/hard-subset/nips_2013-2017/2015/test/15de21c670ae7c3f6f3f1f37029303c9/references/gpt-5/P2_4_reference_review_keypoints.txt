- Core idea: Analyze MLE in exponential families by restricting parameters to a convex fast-mixing set Θ and running projected gradient descent with MCMC-estimated gradients, giving high-probability convergence and work bounds.
- Constraint: Parameters are restricted to a convex Θ that satisfies a uniform geometric mixing bound (C, α) and admits efficient projection.
- Results: Derive Lipschitzness of the gradient, explicit schedules for iterations K, samples per iteration M, chain steps v, and polynomial total work (KMv) in the desired accuracy; provide near-matching lower bounds.
- Claim: Argue a randomized polynomial-time approximation scheme for constrained MLE (terminology caveat noted).
- Technical soundness: Uses standard tools (concentration, TV mixing bounds, Lipschitz A(θ), inexact projected gradient descent) and is carefully reasoned.
- Crucial assumption: The convex Θ with uniform (C, α) mixing and efficient projection is explicit and central to all guarantees.
- Gap — TV bound: The bound ∥pθ−pφ∥TV ≤ 2R^2∥θ−φ∥2 is strong; needs a precise lemma or tightened/constants (Pinsker-type arguments may introduce dimension/curvature dependence).
- Gap — mixing constants: FPRAS/FPTAS-style guarantees hinge on knowing (C, α); obtaining or certifying these constants is difficult or impossible in practice.
- Gap — sampling independence: Analysis assumes independent MCMC samples each iteration (no persistence), differing from common practice and affecting practical relevance.
- Gap — statistical error: The paper focuses on optimization only and does not address statistical/generalization error.
- Gap — constants in examples: The illustrative example uses an L smaller than theoretical bounds; formal guarantees require the conservative constants.
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized with clear setup and proof sketches; the path from mixing bounds to gradient bias to optimization is easy to follow.
- Clarity — notation: Notational inconsistencies (R, R2, R22) should be standardized; Algorithm 1 should be described inline.
- Originality: Novel combination of projecting into fast-mixing parameter sets, MCMC gradient estimates, and end-to-end high-probability optimization and work complexity guarantees with lower bounds.
- Significance: Conceptually advances when MCMC-based MLE can be provably efficient via parameter restrictions, but practical impact is limited by strong assumptions about Θ and mixing.
- Suggestion — terminology: Clarify FPTAS vs FPRAS and whether approximations are additive vs multiplicative and in objective vs parameters.
- Suggestion — dependence on problem size: Make KMv dependence on dimension N, dataset size D, and graph structure explicit through R and L, with examples for Ising/Potts.
- Suggestion — computing (C, α) and projection cost: Discuss how to bound/compute mixing constants in practice, effects of loose bounds, and complexity of projections (when they are bottlenecks).
- Suggestion — broader models: Comment on extensions to continuous or unbounded sufficient statistics (e.g., Gaussians) where bounded-statistics assumptions fail.
- Suggestion — experiments: Add at least a small empirical comparison (e.g., pseudolikelihood or contrastive divergence on toy Ising) to illustrate practical behavior and constants.
- Pros: Clear principled framework, high-probability convergence with explicit near-optimal work bounds, general exponential-family treatment with bounded statistics, and clarification of when MCMC gradients are sound.
- Cons: Strong/hard-to-verify assumptions (known mixing rates, efficient projection), independence assumption across iterations and large constants reduce applicability, some technical/terminology details need tightening, limited empirical validation.
- Recommendation: Lean accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate to high.
