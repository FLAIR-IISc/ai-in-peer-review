- Proposes defining tractable parameter sets Θ by fast-mixing MCMC behavior to enable maximum likelihood learning for arbitrary treewidth models.  
- Shows that given Θ and a projection operator, an FPRAS exists for finding the maximum likelihood solution within Θ.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of gradient descent with MCMC-estimated gradients for both convex and strongly convex cases.  
- Frames the approach within the exponential family for broad applicability beyond graphical models.  
- Bridges mixing-time theory with optimization, leveraging prior work on Markov chain mixing and noisy-gradient optimization.  
- Offers detailed bounds on required MCMC steps and iterations (Theorems 7 and 8) for desired accuracy.  
- Supplies a simple Ising model example to illustrate feasibility in practice.  
- Includes a comprehensive background on mixing times and specific Ising-model bounds for readers.  
- Novelty: shifts tractability focus from graph structure to mixing properties of parameter sets.  
- Theoretical rigor: strong FPRAS guarantee and careful analysis of optimization under sampling noise.  
- Generality: exponential-family framing increases potential impact and use cases.  
- Weakness: lacks in-depth discussion of practical challenges in finding and projecting onto fast-mixing sets Θ.  
- Weakness: theoretical bounds appear loose with potentially large multiplicative constants.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation—only a single Ising example insufficient for practical validation.  
- Weakness: assumes known mixing constants (C and α), which are hard to obtain in practice.  
- Weakness: projection computational cost and algorithms for general Θ are not thoroughly addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically solid, FPRAS result, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical support, loose bounds, and unanswered practical issues regarding Θ and mixing parameter estimation.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution likely to stimulate research, but practical concerns remain.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, including expanded practical discussion, more experiments, tighter-bound investigation, and strategies for estimating mixing parameters.
