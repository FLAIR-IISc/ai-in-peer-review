- Studies MLE for exponential families constrained to a convex Θ where a chosen MCMC kernel mixes rapidly and projection onto Θ is available.  
- Analyzes projected gradient descent with MCMC-estimated gradients and gives finite-sample, high-probability convergence guarantees.  
- Provides separate results for convex (additive objective error) and strongly convex (parameter error) regimes.  
- Bounds bias from finite mixing and variance from finite sampling, leveraging Lipschitz smoothness of the regularized log-likelihood.  
- Uses inexact projected gradient theory (Schmidt et al. NIPS’11) and produces explicit schedules for iterations K, samples per iteration M, and mixing steps v.  
- Proves lower bounds and argues near-optimality up to constants/log factors.  
- Connects to practical Θ constructions (box or spectral-norm constraints) relevant for Ising/MRFs and to prior fast-mixing projection work.  
- Builds on mixing-time literature (Gibbs dynamics results) and prior projection and MCML analyses.  
- Distinct from pseudolikelihood and composite-likelihood methods by targeting the M-projection, not a surrogate.  
- Clear conceptual contribution reframing tractability via mixing-time-constrained parameter sets.  
- General applicability to arbitrary exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics and not reliant on treewidth.  
- Careful finite-sample analysis with explicit, interpretable bounds and constructive schedules.  
- Honest discussion of limitations and inclusion of a small illustrative example.  
- Strong assumption: existence and efficient projection onto a practically useful fast-mixing convex Θ is nontrivial beyond pairwise discrete models.  
- FPRAS claim is unclear/misleading: paper gives additive guarantees, uses treated-as-given constants and oracles, and lacks a precise computational model.  
- Limited empirical validation: only a tiny toy example, no larger experiments or comparisons with alternatives (pseudolikelihood, CD, persistent chains).  
- Insufficient discussion of related analyses for MCMC-biased gradients and contrastive divergence under mixing constraints.  
- Some technical clarity issues: inconsistent notation (R2 vs R2^2), incomplete references, and TV-to-expectation steps need clearer constants/conditions.  
- Theoretical development appears sound, with reasonable proofs and supportive lower/upper bound pairing, but the FPRAS statement needs qualification.  
- Recommend clarifying FPRAS/approximation notion, stating oracle assumptions, discussing estimation/mis-specification of mixing constants, and including projection-costed runtimes.  
- Suggest extending analysis to persistent chains and clarifying applicability to continuous or higher-order models.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept (6) — solid theoretical advance with moderated claims and limited practical impact without further empirical/constructive work.
