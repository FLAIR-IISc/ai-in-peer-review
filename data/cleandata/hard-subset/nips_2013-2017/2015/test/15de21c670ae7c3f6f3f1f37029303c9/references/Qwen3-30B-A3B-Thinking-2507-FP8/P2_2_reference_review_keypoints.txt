- Addresses ML learning in graphical models by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set Θ so MCMC-based gradient descent can efficiently estimate gradients with guarantees.  
- Proves convergence and gives precise computational complexity bounds for both convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (regularized) cases using MCMC estimates.  
- Novel contribution: focuses on mixing properties (fast-mixing parameter sets) rather than structural constraints (e.g., treewidth), providing first theoretical guarantees under these assumptions.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous with well-structured proofs and clearly stated assumptions (including mixing time bound).  
- Strong Lipschitz-gradient analysis (Theorem 1) with clear proof sketch conveying key insights.  
- Empirical example (Section 6) matches theoretical predictions, showing practical convergence in the tested case.  
- Authors acknowledge and reasonably justify ignoring projection complexity as typically negligible compared to sampling.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and helpful proof sketches for intuition.  
- Some technical constants in mixing-time bounds may be hard for readers not expert in MCMC theory, though sufficient context is provided.  
- Significant theoretical value: clarifies trade-offs among gradient steps, samples, and chain transitions and bridges theory toward practical algorithms.  
- Limited immediate practical impact: experiments use a small (4x4) Ising model and practical implications could be discussed more.  
- Strengths summarized: novel theoretical framework, rigorous convex/strongly-convex analysis, clear complexity bounds, concrete example, careful theory/practice distinction.  
- Weaknesses summarized: ignores projection complexity, practical implications under-discussed, small-scale experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: accept (Score 8/10, Strong Accept).
