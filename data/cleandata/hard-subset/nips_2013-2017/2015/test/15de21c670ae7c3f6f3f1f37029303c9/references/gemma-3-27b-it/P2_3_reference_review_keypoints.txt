- Proposes tractable maximum likelihood learning by restricting parameters to fast-mixing MCMC parameter sets and proving an FPRAS for that constrained problem.
- Framework applies broadly to exponential families and provides convergence guarantees for gradient descent with MCMC-based gradient estimates in convex and strongly convex cases.
- Novel conceptual shift: defines tractability via MCMC mixing rates rather than graph structure or classical approximations.
- Strong theoretical contributions: rigorous analysis of convergence, mixing-rate dependence, and computational cost (samples, transitions, gradient steps).
- Practical awareness: discusses projection onto tractable sets and gives an Ising-model example with concrete parameter choices.
- Well-written and organized with clear notation and sketched proofs for intuition.
- Effectively contrasts and motivates the approach versus pseudolikelihood and score matching.
- Main limitation: primary contribution is theoretical; computational costs may be prohibitive for very large models.
- Framework depends on existence and efficient projection onto fast-mixing parameter sets, which may be hard to identify for complex models.
- Projection complexity can be nontrivial and may dominate overall computational cost.
- Theoretical bounds appear conservative; gap between guarantees and practical performance is not fully quantified.
- Quality: technically sound and rigorous within stated assumptions.
- Clarity: generally clear, though some mathematical details need more inline explanation.
- Originality: novel framing of tractability via mixing rates; combination of known techniques is original.
- Significance: important theoretical contribution, but practical impact hinges on projection algorithms and identifying fast-mixing sets for more models.
- Pros for acceptance: novel, general, rigorous, and addresses a fundamental problem.
- Cons for acceptance: limited immediate practical impact, reliance on identifying fast-mixing sets, and potential projection overhead.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: expand discussion/analysis of projection complexity, outline strategies to find fast-mixing parameter sets, and investigate the gap between theoretical bounds and empirical performance.
