- Summary: Proposes restricting parameters to a fast-mixing set Θ so MCMC mixes rapidly, proving a FPRAS for maximum likelihood within Θ and showing gradient descent with MCMC yields provable convergence.  
- Relation to prior work: Positions the approach relative to pseudolikelihood, score/composite likelihoods, tractable families (Chow-Liu), and MCMC convergence theory (e.g., Jerrum & Sinclair).  
- Novelty: Introduces a new theoretical framework linking fast mixing to FPRAS-based ML estimation rather than approximating the partition function.  
- Generality: Results are presented for general exponential families, not just specific graphical models.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides detailed convergence analysis for gradient descent with MCMC in convex and strongly convex settings, with explicit rates.  
- Practical grounding: Includes an Ising model example and discusses initialization strategies like contrastive divergence.  
- Presentation: Generally well-structured with helpful notation and a clear algorithmic outline (Figure 1).  
- Main practicality concern: Identifying parameter sets Θ that guarantee fast mixing is difficult for complex models, limiting applicability.  
- Conservative bounds: Theoretical bounds appear overly conservative, with strong dependence on constants like R and L.  
- Projection cost understated: The paper does not fully assess the computational cost of projecting onto Θ, which may be nontrivial.  
- Clarity issues: Some mathematical derivations (e.g., Theorem 6) are terse and need more intuition and detail.  
- Empirical limitations: Lacks extensive empirical validation beyond the Ising example to demonstrate practical usefulness.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, general, and theoretically sound solution to an important inference problem with rigorous convergence guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited practical applicability due to difficulty finding Θ, overly conservative bounds, and insufficient empirical evidence.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution likely impactful, but practical limitations and clarity issues temper its immediate utility.  
- Recommendation and revisions: Accept with minor revisions—expand discussion of finding fast-mixing sets, run sensitivity analyses (R, L, α), improve exposition of dense proofs, and add broader empirical evaluation.
