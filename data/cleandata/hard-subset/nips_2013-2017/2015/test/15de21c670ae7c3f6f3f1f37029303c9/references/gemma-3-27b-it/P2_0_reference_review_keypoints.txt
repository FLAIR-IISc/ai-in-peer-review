- Tackles intractable maximum likelihood learning by restricting parameters to sets that guarantee fast MCMC mixing rather than bounding treewidth.  
- Proposes a framework that yields a Fast Polynomial Randomized Approximation Scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood within those fast-mixing parameter sets.  
- Provides convergence guarantees for both convex and strongly convex cases and relates computational complexity to mixing rate and desired accuracy.  
- Framework applies to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.  
- Demonstrates practical considerations via an Ising model example and discusses initialization strategies like contrastive divergence.  
- Offers a detailed analysis of required MCMC steps and gradient descent iterations, with explicit parameter schedules.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation with rigorous analysis and an FPRAS result.  
- Strength: broad generality beyond graphical models.  
- Strength: addresses a fundamental problem—exact maximum likelihood in intractable settings.  
- Strength: clearly positions work relative to pseudolikelihood, score matching, and related methods.  
- Strength: practical awareness shown by example and initialization discussion.  
- Strength: thorough complexity analysis with explicit theorems.  
- Weakness: practical applicability unclear due to potentially large constants and high cost for high accuracy.  
- Weakness: projection onto the tractable set Θ is not included in complexity bounds and may be expensive.  
- Weakness: core fast-mixing assumption requires knowing or bounding constants C and α, which is hard for complex models.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation and lack of comparisons to existing methods on benchmarks.  
- Weakness: dense writing in places; some sections need clearer, more intuitive exposition.  
- Assessment summary: quality Very Good, clarity Good, originality Good, significance Good but practical impact uncertain.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous theoretical contribution with broad applicability and a notable FPRAS result.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical cost/mixing estimation concerns, projection complexity omission, limited experiments, dense exposition.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss projection complexity in detail; expand empirical evaluation across models/datasets; clarify practical implications and strategies for estimating mixing; improve clarity and flow in theoretical sections.
