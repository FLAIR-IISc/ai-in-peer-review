- Proposes restricting parameter space to "fast-mixing parameter sets" to make MCMC-based maximum likelihood learning tractable.  
- Proves gradient descent with MCMC gradient estimates converges with total Markov transitions scaling as (1/ε)^3 log(1/ε) (convex) and (1/ε)^2 log(1/ε) (strongly convex).  
- Frames results in the general exponential family context, broadening applicability beyond graphical models.  
- Provides rigorous, well-structured theoretical analysis handling MCMC bias and deriving precise sample/step/transition bounds.  
- Introduces the novel conceptual contribution of fast-mixing parameter sets as an alternative to low-treewidth restrictions.  
- Paper is exceptionally well organized, with clear notation and helpful proof sketches.  
- Offers concrete guidance for practitioners on setting algorithm parameters via explicit complexity bounds.  
- Authors honestly acknowledge limitations and conservative nature of their bounds, illustrated by an Ising model example.  
- Lacks empirical validation and comparisons to existing methods on real-world datasets.  
- Does not provide concrete evidence that projection onto the fast-mixing set is negligible for large-scale or complex models.  
- Insufficient guidance on how to choose the fast-mixing parameter set Θ in practice for complex model classes.  
- Limited discussion of connections to and improvements over existing MCMC-based learning methods like contrastive divergence.  
- Relies on geometric mixing time bounds that may not hold for all samplers or models, with limited robustness analysis.  
- Technically sound, clear, original theoretical contribution with potentially significant impact on learning in graphical models.  
- Recommendation: accept, as a strong theoretical contribution despite lack of empirical results.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss adapting Θ for other model classes, methods to estimate mixing constants (C, α), expand connection to contrastive divergence, and compare empirical iterations/samples to theoretical bounds.
