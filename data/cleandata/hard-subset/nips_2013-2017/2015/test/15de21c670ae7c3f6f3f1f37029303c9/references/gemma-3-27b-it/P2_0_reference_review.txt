## Review of "Maximum Likelihood Learning With Arbitrary Treewidth via Fast-Mixing Parameter Sets"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of maximum likelihood learning in graphical models, which is generally intractable. It proposes a framework based on restricting parameters to a “tractable” set defined not by treewidth directly, but by guaranteeing fast mixing of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling within that set.  The key result is a Fast Polynomial Randomized Approximation Scheme (FPRAS) for maximum likelihood estimation within such a set, leveraging gradient descent and MCMC. The authors provide convergence guarantees for both convex and strongly convex cases, and analyze the computational complexity, relating it to the mixing rate and desired accuracy.  The work builds on existing techniques like pseudolikelihood and score matching, but distinguishes itself by aiming for true maximum likelihood estimation and providing theoretical guarantees, even when the model is misspecified.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the proposed approach, including convergence guarantees and complexity bounds. The use of FPRAS is a powerful result.
* **Generality:** The framework extends beyond graphical models, applying to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics. This broadens the potential impact of the work.
* **Addresses a Core Problem:** Maximum likelihood learning is a fundamental problem in statistical modeling, and the paper offers a potentially valuable approach when direct optimization is intractable.
* **Clear Connections to Existing Work:** The authors carefully position their work relative to existing approaches like pseudolikelihood, score matching, and Chow-Liu, highlighting the advantages of their method.  The detailed discussion of mixing times and related bounds in the background section is impressive.
* **Practical Considerations:** The inclusion of an example (Ising model) and discussion of initialization strategies (contrastive divergence) demonstrates awareness of practical challenges and potential applications.
* **Detailed Analysis of Complexity:** The careful analysis of the number of MCMC steps, gradient descent iterations, and their relationship to desired accuracy is a significant strength. The theorems providing explicit schedules for parameter settings are helpful.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Practicality Concerns:** While the theoretical results are compelling, the practical applicability remains somewhat unclear. The cost of achieving high accuracy appears substantial, and the constant factors in the complexity bounds could be large.  The example shows faster convergence than theoretically predicted, suggesting conservatism in the bounds but also a gap between theory and practice.
* **Projection Complexity:** The paper acknowledges that the complexity of projecting onto the tractable set Θ is not considered in the overall complexity analysis.  In some cases, this projection could be computationally expensive, potentially offsetting the benefits of the fast-mixing guarantee.
* **Mixing Time Assumptions:**  The fast-mixing assumption (Equation 2) is crucial, but obtaining concrete bounds on C and α can be challenging in practice. The examples provided rely on relatively simple models and established mixing time results. Applying this framework to more complex models might require significant effort to characterize the mixing rate.
* **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The example provides some validation, but a more comprehensive empirical evaluation with diverse models and datasets would strengthen the paper.  Comparing the performance (accuracy and runtime) to existing methods on benchmark tasks would be highly valuable.
* **Writing Style:** The paper is dense and occasionally difficult to follow. While thorough, some sections could benefit from more intuitive explanations and a clearer flow of ideas.

**Assessment of Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** Very Good. The paper is technically sound, with well-supported claims and a rigorous analysis. However, the practical implications are less clear.
* **Clarity:** Good. The paper is generally well-written, but some sections are dense and could benefit from simplification. The notation is clearly defined, but the overall flow could be improved.
* **Originality:** Good. The approach of leveraging fast-mixing parameter sets to achieve a FPRAS for maximum likelihood learning is novel.  It offers a different perspective on tractability compared to traditional treewidth-based approaches.
* **Significance:** Good.  The paper addresses a fundamental problem and offers a potentially valuable theoretical framework. However, the practical impact remains to be demonstrated.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Provides a strong theoretical foundation for a challenging problem.
* Offers a novel approach with potential for broad applicability.
* Rigorous analysis and clear connections to related work.
* The FPRAS result is significant.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Practical concerns regarding computational cost and mixing time estimation.
* Limited empirical evaluation.
* Projection complexity is not considered.
* Dense writing style.

**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions. The paper presents a solid theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of maximum likelihood learning in intractable models. While the practical implications remain somewhat uncertain, the rigor and originality of the work justify acceptance. I suggest the authors address the following in a revision:

1.  **Discuss projection complexity:**  Provide a more detailed discussion of the computational cost of projection onto Θ, and how this might impact the overall efficiency of the algorithm.
2.  **Expand empirical evaluation:** Include more comprehensive experiments with diverse models and datasets, comparing the performance to existing methods.
3.  **Clarify practical implications:**  Discuss strategies for mitigating the computational cost and estimating mixing rates in practice.
4.  **Improve clarity:**  Revise the writing style to improve readability and flow, particularly in the theoretical sections.
