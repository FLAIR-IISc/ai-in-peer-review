- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set Θ so MCMC mixes rapidly, enabling tractable maximum likelihood learning for arbitrary treewidth models.

- Shows gradient descent with MCMC-based gradient estimates converges within Θ with guarantees: convex case O((1/ε)^3 log(1/ε)), strongly convex O((1/ε)^2 log(1/ε)).

- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and proofs (Lipschitz gradient, convergence theorems for convex and strongly convex cases).

- Proof sketches balance intuition and mathematical rigor and carefully separate convex vs. strongly convex behaviors.

- Acknowledges limitations: ignores projection cost, derives conservative bounds, and does not bridge fully to contrastive divergence practice.

- Presentation is clear and well-organized with consistent notation and a helpful symbol summary.

- Includes an illustrative Ising model example showing empirical convergence faster than theoretical bounds.

- Novel viewpoint: tractability via fast-mixing parameter sets rather than restricting graph structure (treewidth/girth).

- Significant contribution: fills a theoretical gap for MCMC-based ML learning and offers guidance for hyperparameter selection.

- Weakness: limited empirical validation beyond a single Ising example; lacks benchmark comparisons.

- Weakness: does not analyze why theoretical bounds are conservative or how practical strategies (e.g., sample pools) affect results.

- Weakness: assumes existence of fast-mixing sets with known projection but gives limited guidance on constructing them for general models.

- Recommendation: strong accept—high-quality, original, and significant theoretical work meeting NIPS criteria.

- Suggested improvements: expand discussion on constructing fast-mixing parameter sets for complex models.

- Suggested improvements: add comprehensive empirical evaluations and comparisons (pseudolikelihood, score matching) and discuss extensions to sample-pool techniques.
