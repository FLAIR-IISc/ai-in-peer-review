- Presents an FPRAS for maximum likelihood learning in exponential families constrained to fast-mixing parameter sets.  
- Framework restricts parameters to ensure fast MCMC mixing and provides convergence guarantees for MCMC-estimated-gradient descent.  
- Supplies schedules for iterations, sample sizes, and MCMC transitions to reach target accuracy.  
- Generalizes beyond graphical models to the exponential family, increasing applicability.  
- Situates work relative to intractability results and recent fast-mixing literature (e.g., Steinhardt & Liang).  
- Provides detailed analysis for both convex and strongly convex cases with theorem sketches.  
- Offers practical guidance via schedules, though bounds are acknowledged to be conservative.  
- Includes an Ising model example to illustrate theoretical results.  
- Limited practical contribution: large constant factors likely make the method less competitive in practice.  
- Critical reliance on knowing mixing-time constants (C, α), which are hard to obtain in real problems.  
- Projection onto the fast-mixing parameter set Θ is underanalyzed; computational cost may be substantial.  
- Theoretical bounds appear conservative; more empirical evaluation is needed.  
- Effect of initialization (θ0) on convergence is understated and deserves deeper treatment.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous theoretical result and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable practical applicability, reliance on mixing-time knowledge, and lack of comprehensive experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution but practical impact uncertain; lean accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: expand discussion of projection complexity, initialization strategies, conservatism of bounds, and potential alternative MCMC algorithms.
