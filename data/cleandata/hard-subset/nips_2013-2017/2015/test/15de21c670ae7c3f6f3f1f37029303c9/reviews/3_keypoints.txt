- Problem: weight learning in Markov networks requires computing gradients for likelihood maximization, but exact gradient computation is NP-hard so MCMC is used.
- Contribution: proves that if MCMC mixes rapidly for the given parameters, then the MCMC-based weight learning algorithm is a FPRAS.
- Cases analyzed: derives separate theoretical bounds for the unregularized (convex) case and the ridge-regularized (non-convex) case.
- Writing and proofs: paper is generally well written with minor typos; proofs appear plausible though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Practical relevance concern: the parameter constraints required for the theorems are unlikely to be satisfied in practice, limiting applicability.
- Open question/criticism: asks whether the authors can enforce the mixing condition artificially (a form of regularization) and warns that combining this with ridge regression might over-regularize.
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical formalization of when MCMC-based weight learning yields a FPRAS, but significant non-trivial research is needed to make the results practically useful.
