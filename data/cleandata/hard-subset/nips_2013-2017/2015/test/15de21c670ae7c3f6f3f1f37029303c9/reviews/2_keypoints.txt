- Proposes a novel, computationally efficient approach to approximate MLE in exponential families by assuming MCMC is fast-mixing and combining proximal gradient descent with Schmidt et al. (2011) to obtain an FPRAS.  
- Provides guarantees for both convex (likelihood error) and strongly convex (parameter error) cases and includes a simple experiment demonstrating the theory.  
- Well-motivated alternative to small-treewidth assumptions; theoretical analyses appear solid though largely a nontrivial combination of existing techniques.  
- Authors candidly discuss limitations, overly pessimistic theoretical assumptions, and directions for future work; offers insight into CD-type algorithms (algorithm analyzed is not exactly CD).  
- Important clarity omissions: never explicitly states that the log-likelihood is convex (should note A(θ) convexity via Hessian = covariance) and should summarize all assumptions.  
- Missing assumption not stated in main text: parameter set Θ must be convex.  
- Practical impact limited because proving fast-mixing for MCMC is generally hard, effectively replacing one difficult problem with another, though the reformulation may still be useful.  
- Minor typos and wording errors noted (lines l185, l192, l207, l281, l311-12).  
- Overall verdict: original and substantial contribution, well-written, but of seemingly limited practical value.
