- Introduces a new tractability notion: restrict parameters to a convex "fast-mixing" set Θ where MCMC is guaranteed to mix quickly.
- Studies projected gradient descent on negative log-likelihood with MCMC-approximated gradients and provides the first non-asymptotic convergence guarantees.
- Proves the procedure is an FPRAS for MLE within Θ for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.
- Gives two analyses: convex (unregularized) case requiring computational effort ~1/ε^3, and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) case with effort ~1/ε^2.
- Provides explicit schedules for gradient steps (K), samples per step (M), and MCMC transitions per sample (v) to achieve target accuracy with high probability.
- Bridges MCMC mixing-time theory and practical ML by formalizing how fast mixing enables efficient learning with biased gradient estimates.
- Technical quality is high: detailed proofs, clear assumptions, and careful separation of convex vs. strongly convex analyses.
- Clarity is excellent: well-written, organized, with intuitive proof sketches and formal theorem statements.
- Originality is strong: novelty lies in defining tractability via mixing speed and providing the first rigorous FPRAS analysis for this heuristic.
- Theoretical significance is high: foundational result likely to inspire follow-up work on MCMC-based learning and projection operators for fast-mixing sets.
- Practical utility is limited: bounds have large/conservative constants and schedules depend on problem-specific, often unknown constants (L, C, α).
- Assumes efficient projection onto Θ and negligible projection cost, which may be unrealistic for complex constraint sets.
- Acceptance arguments: fundamental contribution, high originality, strong theoretical significance, and exceptional clarity.
- Rejection arguments: limited immediate practical impact and reliance on potentially unavailable/expensive projection operators.
