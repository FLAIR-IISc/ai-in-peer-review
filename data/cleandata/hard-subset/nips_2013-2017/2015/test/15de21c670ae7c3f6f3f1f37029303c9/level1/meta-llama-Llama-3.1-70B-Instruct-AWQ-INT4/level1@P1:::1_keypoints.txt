- Proposes a novel tractability notion: restrict parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC inference converges quickly.
- Approach contrasts with traditional tree-structured parameter restrictions for tractability.
- Proves that for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, gradient descent with sample-based gradients approximates the MLE inside the fast-mixing set with high probability.
- Provides explicit schedules for iterations, sample counts, and Markov transitions to reach a target accuracy.
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and well-organized technical sections.
- Proofs are thorough and clearly explained.
- Includes a useful discussion of related work and limitations.
- Strength: novel method for maximum likelihood in high-treewidth graphical models.
- Strength: rigorous and well-explained technical contributions.
- Strength: clarity and concise presentation.
- Weakness: requires a fast-mixing parameter set, which may not always exist.
- Weakness: high computational complexity—large numbers of samples and Markov transitions needed.
- Weakness: lacks empirical results demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Suggested improvements: add empirical validation, analyze/reduce computational complexity, and explore broader applicability.
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS 2015; overall rating 8/10 with confidence 8/10.
