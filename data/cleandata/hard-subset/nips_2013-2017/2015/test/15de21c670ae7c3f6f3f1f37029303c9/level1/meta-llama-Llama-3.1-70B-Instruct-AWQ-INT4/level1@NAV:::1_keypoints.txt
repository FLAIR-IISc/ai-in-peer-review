- Proposes a novel maximum likelihood learning approach for high-treewidth undirected graphical models by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set.  
- Shows that when parameters lie in a fast-mixing set, MCMC gradients allow gradient descent to approximate the MLE with high probability for exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of convergence rates and proves a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS).  
- Empirical results support the theoretical claims.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are well-supported.  
- Well-written with clear notation and derivations, but assumes strong background in graphical models, exponential families, and MCMC.  
- The idea of parameter restriction to ensure fast mixing is original and potentially broadly applicable.  
- Results have significant implications for learning high-treewidth models in applications like vision, NLP, and bioinformatics.  
- Pros: novel approach, thorough convergence analysis, and significant potential impact.  
- Cons: limited accessibility to non-experts and potential computational impracticality for very large models due to MCMC cost.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance and suggests adding more explanations and examples to improve accessibility.
