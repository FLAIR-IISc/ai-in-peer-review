- Proposes "fast-mixing parameters" for exponential families to guarantee rapid MCMC convergence.
- Shows gradient descent with sampled (approximate) gradients recovers the ML solution inside the fast-mixing set with high probability.
- Relates work to existing tractable models and highlights limitations of pseudolikelihood, score matching, and composite likelihoods.
- Strength: introduces a new notion of tractability that yields theoretical MCMC convergence guarantees.
- Strength: provides a fully-polynomial-time randomized approximation scheme for maximum likelihood learning.
- Strength: analyzes convergence of gradient descent under noisy/estimated gradients, matching common practice.
- Weakness: requires parameters to be constrained to a fast-mixing set, which may be impractical.
- Weakness: lacks discussion of the computational complexity of projecting onto the fast-mixing set.
- Weakness: analysis is limited to exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.
- Pro-accept: offers a novel perspective and strong, well-supported theoretical guarantees that could inspire new approaches.
- Con-accept: restrictive assumptions and limited model class may limit practical applicability; more complexity discussion needed.
- Overall: well-written and thorough with significant theoretical contributions, but practical limitations should be carefully considered.
