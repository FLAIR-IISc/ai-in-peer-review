- Proposes restricting parameters to "fast-mixing sets" so MCMC-based gradient descent can provably find MLEs in high-treewidth undirected models.
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees (convex and strongly convex cases) and a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme.
- Novel notion of tractability based on mixing-time guarantees, distinct from treewidth-based or prior fast-mixing work.
- Applies broadly to exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, not just specific graphical models.
- Well-organized, clearly written paper with helpful examples and well-structured proofs addressing MCMC approximation error.
- Significant theoretical contribution that fills a gap by giving guarantees for a commonly used but previously unproven practice.
- Lacks empirical validation beyond a tiny 4x4-grid example, so practical scalability and performance are untested.
- Does not analyze the computational cost or practical methods for projecting onto the fast-mixing set, a potential bottleneck.
- Omits practical implementation details (MCMC initialization strategies, selecting the fast-mixing set) needed for real-world use.
- No detailed comparison of computational complexity or empirical performance against alternatives like pseudolikelihood or variational methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: first rigorous guarantees for MCMC-based MLE, novel and general concept, technically sound and well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: purely theoretical with limited practical demonstration, unresolved projection complexity, and unclear practical selection of parameter sets.
- Overall: strong, high-quality theoretical paper with broad implications; recommend acceptance but authors should acknowledge limitations and address projection complexity in future work.
