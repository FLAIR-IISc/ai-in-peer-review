- Paper studies maximum likelihood learning for exponential families under the constraint that MCMC samplers mix rapidly (fast-mixing parameter sets).  
- Main theoretical claim: gradient descent with gradients approximated by MCMC sampling converges to the ML solution inside the fast-mixing set with high probability.  
- Introduces a new tractability notion for exponential families based on MCMC mixing time.  
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis, including a bound on the total number of Markov transitions needed for a target accuracy.  
- Builds on and situates itself within prior work on mixing times and ML learning in graphical models.  
- Strength: comprehensive related-work review and formal theoretical contributions.  
- Strength: novel perspective tying tractability to sampler mixing properties.  
- Weakness: assumes parameters can be constrained to a fast-mixing set, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness: no empirical results to validate algorithm effectiveness.  
- Weakness: omits discussion of computational complexity of the projection operator, a potential practical bottleneck.  
- Originality: offers a novel, mixing-time–based approach with rigorous analysis.  
- Significance: could enable more efficient/scalable ML learning across domains (vision, NLP, biology) if practical issues are addressed.  
- Overall: well-written; main concern is lack of experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add empirical validation and discuss projection complexity.  
- Rating: 8/10.
