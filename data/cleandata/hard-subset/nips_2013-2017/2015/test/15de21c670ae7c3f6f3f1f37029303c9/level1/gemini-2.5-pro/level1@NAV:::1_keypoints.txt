- Problem: Addresses maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models where exact gradient computation is intractable.  
- Approach: Constrains model parameters to a convex "fast-mixing set" guaranteeing geometric MCMC convergence.  
- Main theoretical result: Shows projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients is a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for the constrained ML objective.  
- Error analysis: Rigorous account of both sampling error (finite samples) and mixing error (finite chain length).  
- Coverage: Provides complete analysis for both convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases using inexact-gradient optimization results.  
- Novelty: First non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for MCMC-based ML by defining tractability via mixing rates rather than structural model restrictions.  
- Significance: Gives a theoretical foundation for widely used MCMC learning heuristics and clarifies trade-offs among optimization steps, sample sizes, and MCMC transitions.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is exceptionally clear, well-organized, with precise notation and helpful proof sketches.  
- Limitations: Practical schedules are likely conservative; complexity of projecting onto the fast-mixing set is not fully incorporated into final complexity bounds.  
- Overall judgment: Highly original and technically strong work with minor practical limitations; strong candidate for acceptance.
