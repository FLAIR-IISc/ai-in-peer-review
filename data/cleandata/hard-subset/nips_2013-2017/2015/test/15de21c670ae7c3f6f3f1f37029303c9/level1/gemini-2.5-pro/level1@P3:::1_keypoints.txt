- Main idea: Reframe tractability by restricting parameters θ to a convex fast-mixing set Θ so MCMC gradients converge geometrically and enable provable learning.
- Methodology: Analyze projected gradient descent with MCMC-based inexact gradients, decomposing gradient error into MCMC bias and sampling variance.
- Generality: Results hold for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.
- Main theoretical results: Unregularized MLE achieves ε_f accuracy in log-likelihood with total MCMC effort O(1/ε_f^3) (ignoring logs).
- Strongly convex case: Ridge-regularized MLE reaches ε_θ parameter accuracy with total effort O(1/ε_θ^2).
- Practical guidance: Provide explicit schedules for gradient steps, MCMC samples, and chain lengths to attain desired accuracy with high probability.
- Technical quality: Analysis is rigorous, builds on concentration inequalities and inexact-gradient optimization results, and is well-supported by proofs.
- Clarity and presentation: Exceptionally well-written, with clear setup, precise theorems, and helpful proof sketches and examples.
- Originality: Novel reframing of tractability via fast-mixing parameter sets, connecting MCMC mixing theory with optimization for the first time.
- Significance: Provides the first FPRAS-style guarantees for MCMC-based MLE, bridging theory and practice and guiding hyperparameter trade-offs.
- Weakness — fast-mixing sets: Reliance on known, convex, projectable fast-mixing sets (e.g., Dobrushin conditions) may be overly restrictive and limit expressiveness.
- Weakness — conservative bounds: Theoretical schedules for K, M, v are likely very loose and computationally pessimistic compared to practice.
- Weakness — limited experiments: Single small-scale experiment is insufficient; broader empirical comparison (e.g., PCD, pseudolikelihood) would be valuable.
- Weakness — MCMC model scope: Analysis assumes independent chains from a fixed start per iteration and does not cover persistent CD, limiting applicability to common heuristics.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — significant, original, and rigorous theoretical contribution despite practical gaps that invite future work.
