- Addresses intractable inference in high-treewidth undirected graphical models by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing set" where MCMC converges quickly.
- Proposes maximum likelihood learning using MCMC-approximated gradients constrained to the fast-mixing parameter set.
- Proves gradient descent with MCMC gradients converges to the ML solution within the fast-mixing set with high probability for bounded exponential families.
- Derives computational complexity bounds: O(1/ε^3) for unregularized learning and O(1/ε^2) for ridge-regularized learning.
- Presents a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) for learning under fast-mixing conditions.
- Situates work relative to prior methods (Chow-Liu, pseudolikelihood, score matching) and mixing-time results, extending theoretical guarantees for MCMC-based learning.
- Novelty: introduces the fast-mixing parameter set concept and provides first NIPS-level theoretical guarantees for fast-mixing MCMC learning.
- Technical soundness: rigorous analysis with clear proof sketches for both convex and strongly convex cases and comparisons to lower bounds.
- Clarity: well-structured paper, clear notation, logical flow, and illustrative example that aids understanding.
- Significance: broadens tractable learning beyond tree-structured models and theoretically justifies a common practical approach (MCMC-based learning).
- Weakness — limited practical demonstration: the empirical example is minimal and doesn't compare practical utility to existing methods.
- Weakness — projection complexity: projecting onto fast-mixing sets may be non-trivial in large models and receives no practical solution.
- Weakness — initialization assumptions: analysis assumes arbitrary MCMC initialization, whereas practical methods often use non-arbitrary initializations that affect mixing.
- Concern that theoretical results and bounds may be conservative relative to practical implementations.
- Overall recommendation: accept — paper makes a significant, rigorous theoretical contribution with high quality, clarity, and originality, and medium-high significance.
