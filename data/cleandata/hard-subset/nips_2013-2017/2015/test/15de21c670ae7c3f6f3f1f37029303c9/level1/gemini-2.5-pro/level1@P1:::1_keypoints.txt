- Proposes restricting parameter space Θ to fast-mixing sets (w.r.t. a given MCMC) as a new notion of tractability for high-treewidth undirected models.
- Analyzes projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients and proves it is an FPRAS for finding the constrained MLE.
- Provides two main convergence guarantees: convex case achieves ε-accurate log-likelihood with cost ≈ O(1/ε³) (ignoring logs).
- Provides a strongly convex (ridge) result achieving ε-accurate parameter distance with cost ≈ O(1/ε²).
- Results apply to general exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.
- Situates work relative to prior tractable methods (Chow-Liu, pseudolikelihood, score matching) and MCMC-based heuristics like Contrastive Divergence.
- Connects and leverages existing MCMC fast-mixing theory and prior work on learning fast-mixing models.
- Strength: highly original and significant conceptual shift from structural to parameter-based tractability.
- Strength: technically rigorous proofs using convex optimization with noisy gradients and concentration bounds.
- Strength: paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and provides intuitive proof sketches.
- Strength: results are general and potentially applicable to many exponential-family models.
- Weakness: practical usefulness depends on finding non-trivial convex fast-mixing sets Θ, which may overly restrict model expressiveness.
- Weakness: analysis omits the potentially significant computational cost of projecting onto complex sets Θ at each iteration.
- Weakness: theoretical bounds are likely conservative and may demand impractically large sample/chain lengths in practice.
- For acceptance: first formal convergence guarantees for widely used MCMC-MLE approaches and a novel, impactful perspective likely to inspire follow-up work.
- Against acceptance: limited practical utility given restrictive fast-mixing conditions and overlooked projection costs.
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution, well-written and technically sound, recommended for acceptance despite practical caveats.
