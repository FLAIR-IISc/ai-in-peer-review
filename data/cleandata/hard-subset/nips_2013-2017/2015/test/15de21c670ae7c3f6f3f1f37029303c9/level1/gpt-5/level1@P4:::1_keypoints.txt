- Paper studies MLE for exponential families when exact inference is intractable by restricting parameters to a convex “fast-mixing” set Θ guaranteeing uniform geometric mixing for a chosen MCMC kernel.  
- Proposes projected gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients and proves high-probability convergence to the MLE within Θ under bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Provides explicit effort bounds: unregularized objective ε-accuracy needs total Markov transitions O(ε−3 polylog(1/ε)); ridge-regularized parameter ε-accuracy needs O(ε−2 polylog(1/ε)).  
- Gives lower bounds matching the upper bounds up to constants/log factors and instantiates the approach for Ising/pairwise MRFs with known projection methods.  
- Leverages Lipschitz gradient continuity, concentration bounds, and inexact projected gradient descent analysis to decompose error into sampling noise and incomplete-mixing bias.  
- General applicability to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not limited to specific graphical structures.  
- Clear theoretical guarantees with explicit schedules for iterations, samples, and chain length, plus near-optimal lower bounds.  
- Careful integration of mixing-time theory and inexact gradient descent with a clean error decomposition.  
- Concrete examples for Ising/MRFs, including convex fast-mixing sets and projection procedures.  
- Provides rigorous foundation for common practice of using sampling-based gradients under fast-mixing constraints.  
- Practical viability depends on having a convex Θ with known uniform mixing constants C, α and an efficient projection operator, which is often hard to obtain.  
- Total work scaling (ε−3 or ε−2) may be large in practice and includes unfavorable constants and logs.  
- Empirical validation is minimal (single 4×4 Ising example) with no comparisons to baselines like pseudolikelihood, CD, or persistent CD.  
- Analysis assumes independent sampling per iteration and does not cover common persistent-chain/sample-pool practices.  
- Informal use of “FPRAS” for optimization is imprecise; additive vs multiplicative approximation should be clarified.  
- Projection cost is excluded from effort bounds, yet projection (e.g., spectral-norm constraints) can be costly in large models.  
- Technical soundness is high; assumptions and derivations (Lipschitz bound, error decomposition, schedules) are correct and consistent.  
- Notational clarity could be improved (e.g., R2 vs R for norm bounds) and reliance on uniform C, α is a strong assumption.  
- Paper is well organized, references relevant literature, and provides proof sketches with full proofs deferred.  
- Original contribution lies in a general convergence and complexity theory for MLE with MCMC gradients over fast-mixing sets, extending prior model-specific work.  
- Theoretical significance is notable for providing first general high-probability optimization guarantees under fast-mixing constraints; practical impact is limited by conservative mixing bounds and projection costs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid, general theoretical results that bridge practice and theory with near-optimal effort bounds.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical support, strong/possibly unrealistic assumptions, and omission of persistent-sampling analyses.  
- Suggested improvements: add empirical comparisons to pseudolikelihood/CD, clarify FPRAS usage, discuss estimating C/α and MCMC kernel choices, analyze persistent chains, and include projection cost analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution with clear assumptions and results, but limited practical validation and applicability due to conservative constants and projection/mixing requirements.
