- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set for tractable maximum likelihood learning with MCMC, with FPRAS guarantees.
- Well-motivated approach addressing intractability in high-treewidth graphical models and distinguishing from pseudolikelihood/composite likelihood.
- Novelty: introducing fast-mixing parameter sets as a tractable constraint for maximum likelihood learning.
- Theoretical rigor: provides rigorous FPRAS guarantees and complexity bounds for gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients.
- Generality: results apply to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, with clear setup, notation, and helpful proof sketches.
- Completeness: analyzes both unregularized (convex) and ridge-regularized (strongly convex) cases.
- Practical relevance is underexplored; projecting onto fast-mixing sets in real problems is not fully addressed and the example feels contrived.
- Computational cost of projecting onto the fast-mixing set is not analyzed in detail and may offset MCMC gains.
- Analysis depends on knowing MCMC mixing constants (C and α), which are hard to estimate in practice.
- Limited empirical evaluation: the single example does not show clear advantage over existing methods.
- Literature review could be deeper, missing recent work in variational inference and related MCMC-based approaches (e.g., score-based models).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically strong, general results with significant FPRAS contributions.
- Arguments against acceptance: uncertain practical applicability, projection cost and mixing-constant estimation issues, and weak empirical validation.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution warranting acceptance but needs more practical discussion and empirical validation.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions asking for expanded discussion of practical limitations and more extensive experiments.
