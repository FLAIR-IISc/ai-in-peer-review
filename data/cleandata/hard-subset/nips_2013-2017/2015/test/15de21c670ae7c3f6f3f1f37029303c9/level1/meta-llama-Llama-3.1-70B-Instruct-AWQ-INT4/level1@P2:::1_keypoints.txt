- Proposes restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC quickly converges, enabling efficient maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models.  
- Uses gradient descent with MCMC approximations and provides theoretical convergence guarantees, including a fully-polynomial time randomized approximation scheme.  
- Paper is well-written and technically clear, with well-organized technical sections.  
- Thorough analysis of convergence: detailed discussion of MCMC mixing times and gradient approximation error bounds.  
- Presents a unified framework applicable to a range of models (e.g., Ising models, pairwise MRFs).  
- Provides new insights showing MCMC mixing time can be bounded by a geometric decay rate.  
- Lacks a clear, detailed empirical or theoretical comparison to existing maximum likelihood methods for high-treewidth models.  
- Does not sufficiently discuss practical limitations, notably the impact of assuming bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Assumes high technical expertise, which may limit accessibility for non-expert readers.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address weaknesses.  
- Suggested revisions: add clear comparisons to existing methods, detail limitations and practical applicability, and include more introductory material for non-experts.
