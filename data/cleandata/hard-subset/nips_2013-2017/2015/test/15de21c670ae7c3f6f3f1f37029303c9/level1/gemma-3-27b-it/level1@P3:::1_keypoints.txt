- Novel approach: restrict parameters to a "fast-mixing" set and use MCMC for gradient estimation to enable ML learning in high-treewidth graphical models.
- Main contribution: a fully-polynomial randomized approximation scheme (FPRAS) with provable convergence rates for ML estimation.
- Theoretical coverage: provides guarantees for both convex (unregularized) and strongly convex (ridge-regularized) cases.
- Generality: extends beyond graphical models to exponential families, broadening applicability.
- Rigorous analysis: builds on established Markov chain convergence and optimization results with clear presentation and lower bounds.
- Practical clarity concerns: the definition of "fast-mixing" depends on unknown constants (C, α) that are hard to estimate in practice.
- Mixing time discussion: connections to existing mixing time bounds are referenced but lack depth on practical applicability across model structures.
- Example section issues: examples feel disconnected from theory; parameter choices (e.g., θij bound .2) and the faster-than-predicted L=10 vs L=97 convergence are unexplained.
- Initialization and sampling: brief mention of initialization (e.g., contrastive divergence) but insufficient exploration of initialization strategies and their effect on convergence.
- Projection step: algorithm relies on projection but the computational cost and potential bottlenecks for complex parameter spaces are not thoroughly examined.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong theoretical rigor, generality to exponential families, and significance of an FPRAS for high-treewidth ML learning.
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability issues due to unknown mixing constants, limited discussion of mixing time bounds, superficial limitations discussion, and unaddressed projection cost.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address estimation of mixing constants, deepen mixing time discussion, analyze projection cost, and expand limitations/future work.
