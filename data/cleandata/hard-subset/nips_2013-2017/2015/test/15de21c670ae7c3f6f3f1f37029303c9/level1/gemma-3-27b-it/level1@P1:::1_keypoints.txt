- Proposes using “fast-mixing” parameter constraints so MCMC-approximated gradients yield an FPRAS for maximum likelihood learning.  
- Extends beyond tree-structured models to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics.  
- Positions approach against prior methods (Chow-Liu, pseudolikelihood, score matching) and cites relevant MCMC learning analyses.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with rigorous guarantees and separate convergence analyses for convex and strongly convex cases.  
- General results increase impact beyond graphical models to broad exponential families.  
- Paper is clearly written with helpful notation and illustrative figures.  
- Tackles the fundamental problem of efficient learning when exact inference is intractable.  
- Demonstrates practicality with an Ising model example, showing the framework can operate in practice.  
- Practicality concern: verifying the fast-mixing property and efficiently projecting onto the constraint set is nontrivial and potentially computationally expensive.  
- Theoretical bounds may be conservative; empirical example indicates looser Lipschitz constants can yield faster convergence.  
- Strong assumption of a known mixing rate (Equation 3); determining this rate in practice is difficult.  
- Does not study sensitivity to initialization or propose practical initialization strategies (e.g., contrastive divergence).  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to a single example; broader experiments are needed to validate real-world performance.  
- Overall assessment: significant and theoretically solid contribution; recommend acceptance (7/10) subject to addressing practical verification/projection issues and expanding empirical discussion.
