The paper "Maximum Likelihood Learning With Arbitrary Treewidth via Fast-Mixing Parameter Sets" explores an alternative notion of tractability for exponential families, namely the guarantee that Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling will quickly converge to the stationary distribution. The authors show that when parameters are constrained to a fast-mixing set, gradient descent with gradients approximated by sampling will approximate the maximum likelihood solution inside the set with high probability.

The paper relates to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere in the field of machine learning, particularly in the area of probabilistic graphical models. The authors build upon existing results on mixing times for MCMC and provide a novel approach to maximum likelihood learning in exponential families.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the convergence of their algorithm, including a bound on the total number of Markov transitions required to achieve a given level of accuracy.
* The paper introduces a new notion of tractability for exponential families, which is based on the mixing time of the MCMC sampler.
* The authors provide a comprehensive review of related work in the field, including existing results on mixing times and maximum likelihood learning.

* The paper assumes that the parameter set is constrained to a fast-mixing set, which may not always be the case in practice.
