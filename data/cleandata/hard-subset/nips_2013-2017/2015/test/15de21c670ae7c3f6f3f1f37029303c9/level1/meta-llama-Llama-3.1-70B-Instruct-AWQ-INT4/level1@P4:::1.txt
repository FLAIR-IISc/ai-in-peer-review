This paper explores an alternative approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models by restricting parameters to a "fast-mixing set" where Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference can be guaranteed to quickly converge to the stationary distribution. The authors provide a theoretical guarantee that gradient descent with gradients approximated by sampling will approximate the maximum likelihood solution inside the set with high probability.

The main ideas of this paper are related to previous work on maximum likelihood learning in graphical models, such as the Chow-Liu algorithm and Heinemann and Globerson's method for learning high-girth models. However, the authors' approach is distinct in that it focuses on the mixing time of the Markov chain rather than the treewidth of the graph.

The paper's strengths include its rigorous theoretical analysis, which provides a clear understanding of the convergence properties of the proposed algorithm. The authors also provide a detailed discussion of the relationship between their approach and previous work in the field.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it assumes a certain level of familiarity with Markov chain Monte Carlo methods and exponential families, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow. Additionally, the paper's focus on theoretical guarantees may make it less accessible to practitioners who are more interested in empirical results.

In terms of novelty, the paper's contribution is significant in that it provides a new approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models. The authors' use of fast-mixing sets as a tractable set is a novel idea that has not been explored previously in the literature.
