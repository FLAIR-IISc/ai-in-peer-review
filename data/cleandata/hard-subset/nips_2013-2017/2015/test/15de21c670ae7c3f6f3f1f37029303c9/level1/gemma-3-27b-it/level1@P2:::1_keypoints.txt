- Core idea: Restrict parameters to a “fast-mixing” set to enable efficient MCMC-based gradient optimization and provable learning.
- Main contribution: Proves an FPRAS for maximum likelihood learning under fast-mixing parameter constraints with convergence guarantees for unregularized and ridge-regularized cases.
- Motivation: Clearly motivated by intractable inference in high-treewidth graphical models and limitations of existing approximations.
- Theoretical rigor: Provides solid theoretical analysis with proof sketches and appropriate grounding in prior work (e.g., Schmidt et al.).
- Novelty: Introduces “fast-mixing parameters” as a novel, tractable alternative to tree-structured parameter constraints.
- Generality: Extends results to the broader exponential family, increasing applicability beyond graphical models.
- Presentation: Well-written and organized with clear notation and helpful illustrative figures.
- Background: Extensive and detailed background on mixing times, Ising models, and related literature showing strong domain knowledge.
- Comprehensive analysis: Thorough treatment of both convex and strongly convex cases with distinct convergence and complexity bounds.
- Practical relevance: Limited practical contribution; empirical example is not convincingly superior to existing methods.
- Mixing-rate assumptions: Reliance on strong assumptions about mixing constants (C and α) may limit real-world applicability.
- Bound tightness: Computational complexity bounds appear loose and potentially conservative.
- Projection complexity: Underestimates potential computational cost of projecting onto the fast-mixing set; needs deeper discussion.
- Initialization sensitivity: Role of initial distribution r on MCMC convergence is not fully explored and could affect results (contrastive divergence noted but not analyzed).
- Background length: Background section is overly long and could be condensed or moved to an appendix for readability.
- Acceptance rationale: Novel, theoretically sound FPRAS result with broad implications and deep literature understanding argues for acceptance.
- Rejection concerns: Limited practical impact, restrictive mixing assumptions, and loose bounds/projection costs argue against acceptance.
- Overall assessment: Reviewer rates the paper as a strong accept for its significant theoretical contribution despite practical limitations.
