- Introduces a novel framework constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing" set to obtain ML learning guarantees for exponential families via MCMC.
- Proves convergence guarantees: ε-accurate log-likelihood in O(1/ε^3) work and parameter-distance in O(1/ε^2) under fast-mixing constraints.
- Extends beyond graphical models to general exponential families with bounded sufficient statistics.
- Establishes rigorous control of MCMC bias and gradient-estimation variance in the optimization analysis.
- Connects to and builds on mixing-time literature (e.g., Jerrum–Sinclair, Domke–Liu) and provides an Ising-model example.
- Main strength is the elegant, technically sound theoretical contribution giving the first rigorous guarantees for MCMC-based maximum likelihood learning.
- Key limitation: assumes the fast-mixing parameter set Θ is known and projection onto Θ is efficient, which is unrealistic in many practical settings.
- Does not address how to identify Θ for complex models or handle non-convex Θ, weakening practical applicability.
- Fails to discuss how to estimate mixing parameters (C, α) in practice.
- Computational complexity bounds (O(1/ε^3)) are higher than some alternatives (e.g., pseudolikelihood O(1/ε^2)) and may be conservative.
- Limited empirical/practical discussion—only a simple Ising example; broader implementation guidance is missing.
- Does not treat model misspecification (true distribution outside assumed family), an important practical omission.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear notation and helpful proof sketches; minor clarity improvement: more on identifying fast-mixing sets.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to (1) expand discussion on identifying fast-mixing sets, (2) compare computational complexity to other methods, and (3) discuss handling misspecified models.
