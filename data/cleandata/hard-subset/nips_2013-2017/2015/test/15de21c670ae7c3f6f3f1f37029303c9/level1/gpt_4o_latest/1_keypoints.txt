- Introduces learning by constraining parameters to a "fast-mixing" set so MCMC inference converges quickly in high-treewidth undirected models.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees that gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients converges to the maximum likelihood solution with high probability.  
- Derives fully polynomial-time randomized approximation schemes (FPRAS) for the learning problem.  
- Analysis applies generally to any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, not just graphical models.  
- Distinguishes and provides detailed convergence bounds for convex and strongly convex cases.  
- Shows total computational effort scales polynomially with desired accuracy.  
- Novel perspective compared to traditional tractability approaches (e.g., tree-structured parameters).  
- Includes a numerical Ising-model example demonstrating practical feasibility.  
- Authors acknowledge the method is conservative and may not yield major practical improvements in current form.  
- Assumes projection onto the fast-mixing set is negligible, which may not hold for large or complex models.  
- Does not analyze practical sampling strategies like sample pools or contrastive divergence, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Empirical validation is limited to a single Ising example; broader experiments are lacking.  
- Dense mathematical notation and detailed proofs may reduce accessibility for non-experts.  
- Argument for acceptance: significant, rigorous theoretical advance addressing ML learning in high-treewidth models.  
- Argument for acceptance: results are general and could inspire future research on tractability via parameter constraints.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited practical contributions and empirical evidence reduce immediate impact.  
- Argument against acceptance: questionable assumptions about projection complexity and initialization hinder scalability.  
- Argument against acceptance: presentation density may limit the paper's reach; clarity improvements needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, especially improving clarity and discussing extensions to practical settings.
