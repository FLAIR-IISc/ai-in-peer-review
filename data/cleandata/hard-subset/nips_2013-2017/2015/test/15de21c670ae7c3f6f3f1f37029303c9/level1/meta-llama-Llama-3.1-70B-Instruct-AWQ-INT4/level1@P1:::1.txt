The paper "Maximum Likelihood Learning With Arbitrary Treewidth via Fast-Mixing Parameter Sets" presents a novel approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth undirected graphical models. The authors propose a new notion of tractability, where the parameter set is restricted to a "fast-mixing" set, ensuring that Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference can quickly converge to the stationary distribution. This approach is fundamentally different from traditional methods that restrict parameters to tree-structured distributions.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and their approach. The technical sections are well-organized, and the proofs are thorough and well-explained. The authors also provide a useful discussion of related work and the limitations of their approach.

The main contribution of the paper is the proof that, for any exponential family with bounded sufficient statistics, when parameters are constrained to a fast-mixing set, gradient descent with gradients approximated by sampling will approximate the maximum likelihood solution inside the set with high-probability. The authors also provide explicit schedules for the number of iterations, samples, and Markov transitions required to achieve a desired level of accuracy.

The paper's strengths include:

* A novel approach to maximum likelihood learning in high-treewidth graphical models
* A thorough and well-explained technical section
* A clear and concise introduction to the problem and approach
* A useful discussion of related work and limitations

The paper's weaknesses include:

* The approach requires a fast-mixing parameter set, which may not always be available
* The computational complexity of the approach is high, requiring a large number of samples and Markov transitions
* The paper does not provide empirical results to demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper scores well on:

* Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis.
