- Problem: Maximum likelihood learning for high-treewidth graphical models via parameter constraints.  
- Core contribution: Proposes constraining parameters to a fast-mixing set and proves an FPRAS for ML learning using gradient descent with MCMC-approximated gradients.  
- Method: Uses MCMC convergence theory to guarantee efficient sampling and gradient approximation within the fast-mixing parameter set.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Chow-Liu, high-girth learning, and classic MCMC convergence and sampling-based gradient methods.  
- Theoretical strength: Rigorous analysis and detailed proofs demonstrating strong guarantees for the proposed approach.  
- Generalization: Extends beyond graphical models to general exponential families, increasing applicability.  
- Empirical support: Provides a simple example offering some practical validation.  
- Practical concerns: Hidden constant factors in polynomial runtime and unaccounted projection complexity may limit real-world use.  
- Assumptions: Requires known mixing rate and an algorithm to project onto the fast-mixing set, which may be hard to verify or implement.  
- Initialization discussion: Treatment of initialization strategies (e.g., contrastive divergence) is brief and underexplored.  
- Writing: Generally clear but sometimes overly technical and dense.  
- Recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify practical mitigation strategies.
