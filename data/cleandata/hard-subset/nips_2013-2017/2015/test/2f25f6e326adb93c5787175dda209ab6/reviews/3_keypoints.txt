- Proposal: approximate inference for DPPs that avoids spectral decomposition by using inducing inputs to bound the likelihood; presents both variational inference and approximate MCMC and shows empirical speedups.
- Overall assessment: paper has merit but method description is rough and empirical evaluation is insufficient.
- Major question: need comparison of computational complexity and wall-clock time between the two proposed methods.
- Major question: need comparisons to other approximate and exact alternatives in terms of approximation quality and runtime.
- Major question: variational inference shows large parameter estimation error on toy data and relies on approximate MCMC—clarify when variational inference is expected to work or fail and why.
- Major question: for the toy example, how do methods perform when inducing inputs are chosen to be the actual sampled points?
- Major question: provide rule-of-thumb guidance for selecting (a) number of inducing points and (b) initialization/placement of inducing points.
- Minor comment: matrices K and L are used without definition—briefly explain marginal kernel and L-ensembles for uninformed readers.
- Minor comment: discussion of advantages of directly learning marginal kernel parameters is inconsistent with the paper’s approach and could be reduced.
- Recommendation: if accepted, improve clarity of method description and strengthen empirical evaluation and write-up.
