- DPPs model repulsion via a positive-definite kernel, but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant summing over all cardinalities and configurations.
- Prior work [7] estimated the kernel spectrum to get incremental upper/lower bounds and enabled provably-correct retrospective MCMC for kernel parameters.
- The paper proposes alternative bounds that avoid eigenvalue estimation, usable for MCMC, and also supports a variational learning approach.
- The method draws useful connections to inducing-point techniques for scalable Gaussian process inference.
- The paper is technically strong and well written; Proposition 1’s lower-bound inequality is a key novel insight.
- The variational-inference capability and GP connections are particularly appealing and extend the paper’s impact.
- Concern: Replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension may not be a clear computational win.
- Concern: Increasing the inducing/cardinality may not guarantee convergence of bounds due to coupled, likely non-convex optimizations; local minima could prevent valid MCMC steps.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels; the main proof (Proposition 1) should be in the main text rather than the appendix; minor wording/typo ("the variational lower is ...").
- Overall: A clever approach to bounding the DPP partition function that could enable faster inference and links to other model classes.
