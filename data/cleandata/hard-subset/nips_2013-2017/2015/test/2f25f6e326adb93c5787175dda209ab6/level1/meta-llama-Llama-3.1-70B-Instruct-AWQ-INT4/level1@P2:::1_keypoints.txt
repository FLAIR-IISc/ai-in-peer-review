- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate nonspectral bounds on determinants in DPP likelihoods for both finite and continuous domains.  
- Provides a clear, concise introduction to DPPs, their properties, and inference challenges.  
- Main technical contribution: determinant bounds that avoid spectral decomposition, derived from a nontrivial determinant inequality.  
- Demonstrates how to use these bounds for parameter inference via variational inference and MCMC.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear, accessible exposition for readers with ML/statistics background.  
- Theoretical analysis and experimental results support the claims; the work is technically sound.  
- Authors are careful and honest in evaluating strengths and weaknesses; not merely a position paper.  
- Sufficient detail is given for expert readers to reproduce results.  
- The approach is original, combining familiar techniques in a novel way and clearly distinguishing from prior work.  
- Results are significant: address a difficult problem better than prior work and likely useful to practitioners/researchers.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel solution, clear presentation, solid theory and experiments.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: requires high mathematical sophistication, limiting accessibility.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: unclear guidance on real-world application and limited experimental evaluation on few datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
