- Proposes a novel inference approach for determinantal point processes (DPPs) that does not require spectral knowledge.  
- Derives computable bounds on the DPP likelihood for both finite and continuous domains.  
- Bounds are cheap to evaluate and avoid approximating large matrix/operator spectra.  
- Bounds can be used for variational inference and MCMC methods.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized overall.  
- Provides solid mathematical foundations and proofs (in supplementary material).  
- Experiments are well-designed and show effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- High originality and potential broad applicability across ML, statistics, and physics.  
- High significance demonstrated through empirical results.  
- Minor clarity issues: some technical terms are used without definitions.  
- Some sections (notably the introduction) could be shorter and more focused.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel spectrum-free inference, originality, empirical effectiveness, clear writing.  
- Arguments for rejection: potential difficulty for non-experts due to undefined terms and a verbose introduction.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and tighten the introduction.
