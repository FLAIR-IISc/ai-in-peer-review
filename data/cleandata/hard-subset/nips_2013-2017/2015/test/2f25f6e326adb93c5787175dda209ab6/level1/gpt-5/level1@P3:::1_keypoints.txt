- Paper introduces nonspectral upper/lower bounds on det(I+L) for finite and continuous DPPs using GP-style inducing-point low-rank approximations Q.
- Extends finite-domain bounds to continuous Fredholm determinants and proposes variational objectives plus retrospective MH that adaptively refines m.
- Contribution is novel: general-purpose, cheap nonspectral determinant bounds applicable to continuous DPPs.
- Theory: matrix/operator bounds are plausible and hinge on L−Q ⪰ 0 via Schur complements when Z are inducing points in the RKHS.
- Critical sign inconsistency: Proposition 1 vs. Eq. (6) appears to have wrong sign for the trace term; continuous case may have similar slips.
- Assumptions: continuous result requires trace-class L and tractable Ψij = ∫ L(zi,x)L(x,zj)dµ(x); practical scope limited beyond Gaussian–Gaussian or when quadrature is used.
- Algorithms: variational objective has attractive O(n m^2) scaling; retrospective MH idea is sensible but needs explicit justification that adaptive m and re-optimizing Z preserve detailed balance.
- Experiments: 1D synthetic validates MH and shows identifiability issues; neuropathy dataset demonstrates bound convergence and stable summaries.
- Empirical limits: no large-scale discrete benchmark (n large) to showcase nm^2 advantage; no runtime/accuracy comparisons to spectral-bound MH [7] or EM/fixed-point baselines.
- Clarity: generally well written, but fix sign errors, equation-number mismatches, and explicitly state conditions for L−Q ⪰ 0 when Z ∉ Y.
- Reproducibility: provide gradients or at least efficient derivative outlines for F(θ,Z), tr(L−Q) and det(Q+I) instead of relying on black-box optimizers.
- Discrete applicability: clarify requirement that kernel L(x,z) be defined for arbitrary z so Z ⊄ Y is meaningful for finite DPPs.
- Originality: high — applying inducing-point GP ideas to derive nonspectral determinant bounds (including Fredholm extension) is new in DPP literature.
- Significance: moderate-to-high — provides practical alternative to eigen-based methods, especially for continuous DPPs, but needs stronger empirical validation to maximize impact.
- Pros: novel elegant bounds, clear computational benefits (O(n m^2)), retrospective MH validated on known-spectrum case, insightful L vs. K discussion.
- Cons: potential sign error in variational bound, limited empirical breadth and comparisons, continuous-case reliance on closed-form Ψ, informal discussion of m→∞ convergence, unclear MH detailed balance with adaptive Z/m.
- Suggestions: fix sign/trace issue, add proof sketches for Propositions 1–2 emphasizing L−Q ⪰ 0 conditions, and clean equation numbering.
- Suggestions: include at least one large discrete-domain experiment (n ≥ 10^4) with runtime/accuracy comparisons to [7] and EM/fixed-point methods.
- Suggestions: provide gradient expressions for θ and Z, report sensitivity to Z initialization and m, and discuss numerical stabilization of LZ (jitter).
- Suggestions: formalize convergence of bounds as m→∞ under mild conditions or state limitations; rigorously justify MH accept/reject correctness when adapting m/Z and cite delayed-acceptance/retrospective MH literature.
- Recommendation: weak accept — promising and novel but needs sign fixes and stronger empirical/theoretical support.
