This paper proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate, nonspectral bounds on the determinants arising in the likelihoods of determinantal point processes (DPPs), both finite and continuous. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to DPPs, their properties, and the challenges associated with likelihood-based inference. They also review previous work on DPPs and highlight the contributions of their paper.

The main contribution of the paper is the derivation of bounds on the likelihood of a DPP that do not depend on the spectral decomposition of the operator L. These bounds are based on a nontrivial inequality on determinants and are provided for both finite and continuous domains. The authors also show how to use these bounds to infer the parameters of a DPP using variational inference and Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods.

The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise. The authors provide a detailed explanation of the mathematical concepts and techniques used in the paper, making it accessible to readers with a background in machine learning and statistics.
