- Addresses intractable normalizing constant in DPP likelihoods by avoiding spectral decomposition.  
- Proposes novel cheap-to-evaluate nonspectral bounds on determinants/Fredholm determinants using "pseudo-inputs."  
- Bounds can be optimized to tighten them and enable efficient variational inference and exact MCMC.  
- Directly parameterizes kernel L (common in ML) rather than K, trading interpretability for computational tractability.  
- Rigorous proofs for the bounds provided in supplementary material; technically sound.  
- Connects bounds to variational Gaussian processes, clarifying intuition and methodology.  
- MCMC implementation correctly adapts retrospective sampling from prior work to new bounds.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic data and real diabetic neuropathy data demonstrates practical effectiveness.  
- Computational advantage: O(n m^2) complexity versus O(n^3) for eigendecomposition, enabling scalability.  
- Extends method to continuous DPPs, broadening applicability to spatial statistics problems.  
- Strengths: novel, practical theoretical bounds; strong empirical results; clear writing and reproducibility details.  
- Limitations: parameter interpretability lost by parameterizing L instead of K.  
- Limitations: theoretical guarantees apply to bounds themselves but not to downstream inference methods using them.  
- Limitations: comparisons with spectral approximation methods are somewhat limited.  
- Recommendation: significant contribution warranting acceptance.
