- Problem: Inference in DPPs without spectral decompositions by deriving likelihood bounds for finite and continuous domains to enable variational inference and MCMC.  
- Motivation/context: Well-motivated, ties to diverse applications and clearly explains limitations of existing spectral-based inference.  
- Method: Proposes likelihood bounds inspired by GP approximations that avoid spectral computations, introducing pseudo-inputs Z.  
- Technical soundness: Mathematical derivations appear logically sound, though full details are in the supplementary material.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, and separates finite and continuous cases clearly.  
- Originality: Novel application of bounding the DPP likelihood to circumvent spectral decomposition.  
- Significance: Could enable scalable DPP inference for larger problems; validated on synthetic and one real dataset.  
- Computational cost weakness: Optimizing pseudo-inputs may introduce substantial computational burden; trade-off vs spectral methods needs analysis.  
- Identifiability weakness: Experiments show identifiability issues (e.g., kernel κ) when parameterizing L directly rather than K.  
- Limited experiments: Evaluation limited to a synthetic example and a single real dataset; needs broader datasets and comparisons.  
- Connection to prior work: Relation to quadrature-based bounds is noted late and should be discussed earlier alongside the bounds.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, offers a practical non-spectral alternative, has sound derivations and promising results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Unclear computational trade-offs, identifiability concerns, and insufficient experimental breadth.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: Provide detailed computational trade-off analysis vs spectral decomposition; discuss identifiability and remedies; expand experiments/comparisons; move up/elaborate connection to quadrature-based bounds.
