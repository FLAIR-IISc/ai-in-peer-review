- Paper tackles likelihood-based parameter inference for DPPs, focusing on the expensive normalizing constant (determinant/Fredholm determinant).  
- Main contribution: non-spectral upper and lower bounds on the log-likelihood using a low-rank inducing-point approximation Q of kernel L.  
- Computation reduces to O(n m^2) using m inducing inputs, improving over O(n^3) direct determinant calculations.  
- Two inference schemes: a cheap variational inference (VI) maximizing a lower bound over parameters and inducing locations, and an asymptotically exact MCMC using bounds in a retrospective Metropolis–Hastings sampler.  
- Framework applies to both finite and continuous DPPs, extending beyond prior spectral-only methods.  
- Experiments: MCMC recovers posterior on a 1D continuous toy; VI distinguishes patient classes on a 2D nerve-fiber dataset.  
- Major technical concern: Proposition 1 / Eq. (6) appears to use an incorrect inequality sign for log det bounds, potentially invalidating the variational lower bound F(θ,Z).  
- The apparent contradiction with standard matrix/Jensen inequalities must be resolved or corrected; if a typo, the rest of VI derivation needs re-verification.  
- Reported poor VI performance on the toy example raises further concerns about the variational objective’s robustness or model identifiability.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and the GP-inducing-points intuition aids clarity.  
- Idea is novel and significant: adapting sparse GP approximations to DPP inference is an original, computationally appealing departure from spectral methods.  
- If technically sound, the method could substantially broaden scalable DPP applications (high potential impact).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, computational advantage, generality across domains, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments for rejection: potential critical technical flaw in the variational bound, inconsistent VI performance, and limited experimental baseline comparisons.  
- Recommended fixes: double-check and correct inequalities in Proposition 1, verify tractable variational bound, update experiments if corrected.  
- Minor suggestions: provide more detail on optimizing inducing inputs Z and add direct empirical comparison to spectral-based MCMC (e.g., Affandi et al. 2013).
