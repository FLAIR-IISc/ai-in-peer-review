- Proposes novel nonspectral bounds for DPP likelihoods that avoid costly spectral decomposition and enable efficient variational inference and exact MCMC for finite and continuous domains.
- Technical contribution: derivation of nonspectral bounds is significant, computationally cheaper than spectral approximations and adaptable via pseudo-input optimization.
- Broad applicability: methods work for both finite and continuous domains, useful across tasks from search-result diversity to spatial biological data.
- Experimental validation: synthetic and real-world (diabetic neuropathy) experiments demonstrate scalability, correctness of MCMC, and practical utility.
- Clarity of presentation: paper is well-organized with clear technical derivations and effective figures (e.g., pseudo-input placement).
- Interpretability trade-off: parametrizing kernel L instead of K reduces interpretability of model parameters.
- Variational inference limitations: results vary across independent runs, raising robustness concerns; authors recommend MCMC but do not fully explore the issue.
- Limited related-work discussion: needs a more detailed comparison with recent DPP inference advances addressing similar computational challenges.
- Scalability concerns: unclear how the approach scales to very large datasets (e.g., millions of points); discussion of limitations is missing.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful nonspectral bounds; strong experimental validation; addresses a key computational bottleneck in DPP inference.
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable robustness of variational inference and reduced interpretability limit broader applicability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address variational robustness and expand discussion of related work and scalability.
