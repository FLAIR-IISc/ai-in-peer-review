- Derives nonspectral upper and lower bounds on the DPP normalizing determinant for finite and continuous domains.
- Finite-domain bounds use an inducing-point low-rank surrogate Q (inspired by sparse GP methods) and a Seiler–Simon determinant inequality.
- Continuous-domain bounds use LZ and Ψij = ∫ L(zi,x)L(x,zj)dµ(x) to bound the Fredholm determinant.
- Enables a variational lower bound on the log-likelihood optimized jointly over kernel parameters and inducing locations.
- Enables a retrospective Metropolis–Hastings scheme that adaptively tightens accept/reject bounds without eigen-decompositions.
- Contrasts with prior DPP learning approaches that require spectra or Nyström approximations and connects to GP variational inducing-point methods.
- Experiments: 1D Gaussian–Gaussian DPP validates retrospective MH vs ideal MH; diabetic neuropathy data shows plausible class overdispersion and bounds tighten with m.
- Technical claims are sound, proofs provided, inference constructions correct, and complexity claims (O(n m^2)) are reasonable.
- Empirical validation supports correctness but is limited in scope and lacks broad comparative benchmarks.
- Paper is well organized and readable, but assumptions for the continuous case (existence/computability of Ψ) need clearer discussion.
- More practical guidance is needed on selecting/initializing inducing points.
- Originality: novel nonspectral determinant bounds for continuous DPPs and nontrivial application of GP inducing-point ideas to DPP normalization.
- Significance: removing spectral requirements broadens applicability to continuous spaces; impact would increase with larger-scale benchmarks and head-to-head comparisons.
- Pros: novel general bounds, practical variational and retrospective-MH instantiations, solid theoretical underpinnings and GP connections.
- Cons: limited experimental breadth and no direct comparisons to key baselines; practical Ψ computation not fully explored; limited guidance on inducing-point placement and bound tightness vs m; observed identifiability issues not deeply analyzed.
- Suggestions: add finite-dataset comparisons to spectral/MH baselines; analyze bound tightness as a function of m and Z; discuss approximate Ψ (quadrature, random features); provide implementation details and code.
