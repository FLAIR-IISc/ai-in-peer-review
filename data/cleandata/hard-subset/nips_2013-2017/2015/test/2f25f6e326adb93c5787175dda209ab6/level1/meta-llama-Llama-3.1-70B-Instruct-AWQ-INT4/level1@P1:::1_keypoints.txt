- Main contribution: novel method for DPP inference that removes need for spectral knowledge by deriving likelihood bounds usable in variational inference and MCMC.  
- Theoretical core: bounds are based on a non-trivial determinant inequality, with a clear, concise proof.  
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written with a clear introduction to DPPs and their applications.  
- Experiments: demonstrated effectiveness on a toy Gaussian continuous experiment and a real-world nerve-fiber spatial dataset for parameter inference and approximate MLE.  
- Strength: avoids spectral knowledge requirement, which is a substantive methodological advance.  
- Strength: bounds are directly applicable to common inference tools (variational inference and MCMC).  
- Strength: experimental results support practical utility.  
- Weakness: assumes kernel L is parametrized, which may limit applicability in some practical settings.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparison to other methods that also avoid spectral knowledge.  
- Weakness: does not analyze or discuss computational complexity of the proposed approach.  
- Originality: presents a novel non-spectral likelihood-bounding approach for DPP inference.  
- Significance: advances methods for DPP parameter estimation and approximate training, with demonstrated real-data utility.  
- Quality: proofs and experiments are clear and credible.  
- Clarity: methodology and results are communicated clearly.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing weaknesses—add comparisons to other non-spectral methods and discuss computational complexity.
