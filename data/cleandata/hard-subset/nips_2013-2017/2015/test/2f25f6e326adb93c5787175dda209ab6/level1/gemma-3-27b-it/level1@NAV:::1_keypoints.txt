- Addresses the practical bottleneck of spectral decomposition for likelihood-based inference in Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs).  
- Proposes rigorous upper and lower bounds on the DPP likelihood that avoid spectral computation for both finite and continuous domains.  
- Builds on and extends Gaussian Process literature results to the DPP setting.  
- Treats both finite and continuous DPPs, providing a comprehensive theoretical framework.  
- Connects to potential future work using quadrature-based Fredholm determinant approximations.  
- Discusses the modeler trade-off between parametrizing kernel K (interpretability) versus L (tractability).  
- Derives bounds enabling cheaper Variational Inference (VI) and Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods.  
- Provides both VI and MCMC algorithms leveraging the proposed bounds.  
- Experimental validation includes a toy example and a real dataset, showing effectiveness of the MCMC method and potential of VI.  
- Comparison with existing methods is helpful but limited in scope.  
- Honest discussion of parameter identifiability issues (e.g., scaling parameter κ).  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is relatively limited and needs broader datasets and stronger baselines.  
- Weakness: Section 4 (continuous case) is mathematically dense and could use more intuitive explanation.  
- Weakness: notation is dense and occasionally hinders readability.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, novel contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experiments.
