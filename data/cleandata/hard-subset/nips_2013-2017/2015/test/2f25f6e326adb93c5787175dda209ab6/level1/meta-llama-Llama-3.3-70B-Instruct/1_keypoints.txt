- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate bounds on DPP likelihoods for finite and continuous domains that avoid spectral approximations.  
- Demonstrates use of these bounds for variational inference and MCMC, enabling scalable parameter learning for DPPs.  
- Bounds do not require approximating the spectrum of large matrices or operators, a significant technical advantage.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough introduction to DPPs and their applications.  
- Authors clearly explain learning challenges for DPPs and how the proposed bounds address them.  
- Experimental evaluation includes a toy example and a real-world diabetic neuropathy dataset, showing effectiveness.  
- Strength: novel bounds that are cheap to evaluate and avoid spectral methods.  
- Strength: practical demonstrations for variational inference and MCMC.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation and clear writing.  
- Weakness: assumes the kernel L is parametrized, potentially limiting interpretability of model parameters.  
- Weakness: requires optimization of pseudo-inputs, which can be computationally expensive.  
- Weakness: lacks direct comparison to other methods (e.g., spectral approximations) to clarify accuracy vs. cost trade-offs.  
- Pro-accept: significant contribution with practical, scalable bounds and demonstrated effectiveness.  
- Con-accept: interpretability concerns, computational cost of pseudo-input optimization, and missing method comparisons.
