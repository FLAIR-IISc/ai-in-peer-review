- Main contribution: Novel non-spectral upper and lower bounds on det(L+I) for finite and continuous DPPs using inducing-variable ideas, avoiding eigendecompositions.
- Inference enabled: Bounds facilitate a cheap variational inference (VI) method and an asymptotically exact MCMC algorithm with retrospective sampling.
- Significance: Removes a major bottleneck for scalable DPP likelihood-based inference and unifies treatment of finite and continuous domains.
- Originality: Novel and elegant application of inducing-point approximations, connecting DPPs to sparse GP literature.
- Technical quality: Bounds are derived from solid matrix inequalities; experiments include synthetic ground-truth validation and a real neuropathy dataset showing practical utility.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, logically organized, and clearly separates finite and continuous cases.
- VI weakness: VI produced poorly consistent results on the synthetic experiment, attributed to parameter non-identifiability; deeper analysis of VI optimization is needed.
- Empirical comparison missing: A direct runtime comparison to spectral-based bounding methods (including inducing-point optimization overhead) would better quantify practical advantages.
- Overall verdict: High-quality, significant, and original work whose strengths outweigh minor weaknesses and is likely to inspire future research.
