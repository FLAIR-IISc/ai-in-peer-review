- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate nonspectral bounds on determinants in DPP likelihoods for both finite and continuous cases.
- Derives likelihood bounds that do not require spectral decomposition of operator L, a significant technical contribution.
- Provides a clear and accessible introduction to DPPs, their properties, and likelihood-inference challenges.
- Uses pseudo-inputs/inducing inputs to approximate the likelihood, which is a clever and effective idea.
- Thorough theoretical analysis of the bounds and their properties is provided.
- Experimental results on synthetic and real datasets convincingly demonstrate the method's effectiveness.
- Balances technical rigor with accessibility, making the work useful to non-specialists and experts alike.
- Raises future research directions, e.g., exploring kernel connections between L and K without spectral knowledge.
- Suggests further study on optimal choice of inducing inputs and accuracy vs. computational trade-offs.
- Main weakness: limited discussion of broader implications, applications, and limitations of the proposed method.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with high quality (9/10), clarity (9/10), originality (8/10), and significance (8/10).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective method with convincing experiments and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: overly technical focus with insufficient discussion of broader applications and limitations.
