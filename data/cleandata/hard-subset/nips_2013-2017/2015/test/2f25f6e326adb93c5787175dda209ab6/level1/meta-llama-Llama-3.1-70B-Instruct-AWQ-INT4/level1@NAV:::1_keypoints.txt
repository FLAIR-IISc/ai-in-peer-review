- Novel approach to DPP inference that does not require spectral knowledge.
- Derived likelihood bounds for DPPs on finite and continuous domains usable for variational inference and MCMC.
- Bounds are cheap to evaluate and avoid approximating large matrix/operator spectra.
- Thorough theoretical analysis of the bounds and their properties.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction to DPPs and applications.
- Assumes reader familiarity with DPPs, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.
- Lacks comprehensive empirical comparison with existing methods.
- Needs more experimental results, particularly for the continuous case.
- The bounds are original and previously unpublished.
- Work has significant potential impact for machine learning and statistics.
- Overall high quality; recommended for acceptance pending revisions to address weaknesses.
