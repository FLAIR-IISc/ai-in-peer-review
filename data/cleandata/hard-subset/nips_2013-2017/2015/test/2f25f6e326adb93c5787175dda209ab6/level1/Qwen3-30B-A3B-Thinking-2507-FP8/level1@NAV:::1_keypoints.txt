- Addresses intractable DPP normalizing constant by proposing nonspectral likelihood bounds that avoid spectral decomposition.
- Enables efficient variational inference and MCMC for DPPs by eliminating need for spectral approximations.
- Extends prior spectral-approximation work (Affandi et al. 2013, Gillenwater et al. 2012) to both finite and continuous domains without spectral knowledge.
- Introduces pseudo-inputs as the key innovation to avoid spectral decomposition.
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees via Propositions 1 and 2 with detailed proofs.
- Carefully discusses trade-off between interpretability (parametrizing K) and tractability (parametrizing L).
- Demonstrates empirical utility on synthetic and real diabetic neuropathy datasets, showing convergence of overdispersion as inducing variables increase.
- Continuous DPP extension is a notable contribution since most prior work targeted finite domains.
- Presentation is clear and well-structured, making technical content accessible.
- Appropriately cites relevant prior work and clearly distinguishes novel contributions.
- Originality is strong: nonspectral bounds materially advance DPP inference methods.
- Significance is high: methods make likelihood-based DPP inference feasible for larger datasets and broader applications.
- Limitation: interpretability loss when parametrizing L instead of K remains an issue for some applications.
- Limitation: continuous-DPP section requires background in operator theory, posing a higher entry barrier.
