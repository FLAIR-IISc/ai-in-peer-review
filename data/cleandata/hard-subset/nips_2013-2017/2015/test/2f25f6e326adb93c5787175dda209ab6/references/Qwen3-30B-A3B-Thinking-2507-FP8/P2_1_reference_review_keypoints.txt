- Proposes nonspectral likelihood bounds for DPPs that avoid kernel spectral decomposition, inspired by sparse Gaussian process approximations.
- Derives nonspectral bounds for both finite and continuous DPPs (Propositions 1 & 2) using a determinant inequality.
- Enables likelihood-based variational inference and MCMC for DPPs without spectral knowledge.
- Theoretical development is sound and proofs are provided in the supplementary material.
- Acknowledges the trade-off: parametrizing L improves computation but reduces interpretability versus parametrizing K.
- Demonstrates practical utility on synthetic Gaussian continuous data and a real diabetic neuropathy dataset.
- Clearly situates the work relative to prior spectral methods (e.g., [7]) and GP literature.
- Novel contribution: optimizing pseudo-inputs to tighten bounds and make inference feasible.
- Exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with consistent notation and logical progression.
- Minor clarity issue: connection between pseudo-input placement and the process’s marginal distribution could be made more explicit.
- Limited empirical comparison of computational efficiency against existing spectral-based methods.
- Needs deeper exploration of how pseudo-input selection affects inference quality.
- Treatment of the interpretability trade-off between L and K parametrizations is superficial.
- Retrospective sampling MCMC is presented but not directly compared to other MCMC approaches.
- Overall assessment: technically solid, significant practical contribution to DPP inference, recommended for acceptance.
