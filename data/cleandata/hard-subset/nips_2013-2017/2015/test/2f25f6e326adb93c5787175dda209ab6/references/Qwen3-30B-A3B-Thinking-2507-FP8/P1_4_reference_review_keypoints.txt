- Proposes nonspectral likelihood bounds for DPPs that avoid expensive spectral decomposition, enabling more efficient inference.  
- Provides theoretical results: Proposition 1 (finite DPPs) and Proposition 2 (continuous DPPs) with proofs based on determinant inequalities.  
- Leverages connections to Gaussian processes and sparse GP inducing-variable approximations to derive bounds.  
- Applies bounds to both variational inference and MCMC methods for DPP parameter estimation.  
- Validates approach on synthetic data (with ground truth) and a real diabetic neuropathy dataset, demonstrating practical relevance.  
- Technically sound proofs and supplementary material supply necessary theoretical details.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, and accessible presentation with consistent notation and useful figures.  
- Novel contribution: first nonspectral bounds for DPP inference and nontrivial extension from finite to continuous cases.  
- Addresses a significant computational bottleneck, potentially enabling likelihood-based DPPs on larger datasets.  
- Acknowledges interpretability trade-off from parametrizing L instead of K but does not fully resolve it.  
- Lacks comprehensive computational benchmarks directly comparing to spectral-based methods to substantiate complexity claims.  
- Insufficient analysis of how inducing point selection (Z) affects bound tightness and performance.  
- Limited comparison to alternative (non-spectral) inference approaches beyond spectral methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen computational comparisons and discuss inducing point selection strategy.
