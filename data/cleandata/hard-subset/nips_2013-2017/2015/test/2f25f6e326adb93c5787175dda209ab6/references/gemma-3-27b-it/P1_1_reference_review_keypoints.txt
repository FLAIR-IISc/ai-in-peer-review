- Proposes likelihood bounds for DPPs that avoid spectral decomposition, inspired by variational GP methods.  
- Derives bounds applicable to both finite and continuous DPPs.  
- Implements bounds within variational inference and MCMC frameworks.  
- Validates approach on a synthetic Gaussian example and a real nerve-fiber dataset.  
- Claims complete elimination of the need for spectral knowledge, expanding DPP applicability.  
- Builds on existing DPP theory and connects to Gaussian Process inducing-input techniques.  
- Theoretical derivations appear rigorous and supported by relevant literature.  
- Extension to continuous domains is a notable technical contribution.  
- Experiments show strong agreement between retrospective MCMC and ideal likelihood methods.  
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to GP-literate readers.  
- Acknowledges limitations around parameter interpretability when directly parametrizing L.  
- Parameter identifiability (e.g., κ) is a significant concern and needs deeper discussion or fixes.  
- Optimization of pseudo-inputs Z introduces nontrivial computational cost and scaling concerns.  
- Experimental scope is limited—primarily Gaussian kernels and base measures—reducing generality.  
- Some notation and exposition in the continuous DPP section are hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: address identifiability, analyze pseudo-input optimization cost, and broaden experimental evaluation.
