- Tackles expensive spectral decomposition in DPP likelihood inference by proposing nonspectral bounds inspired by GP variational methods.  
- Technical contribution: derives novel nonspectral likelihood bounds (Propositions 1 & 2) using a determinant inequality and demonstrates effectiveness empirically.  
- Practical impact: enables efficient variational inference and MCMC for DPPs without spectral knowledge, shown on synthetic and real (diabetic neuropathy) data.  
- Clarity and organization: well-structured paper with clear derivations and a logical extension to continuous domains.  
- Insufficient differentiation from prior work: claims of avoiding eigendecomposition are unclear since [7] needs only a few eigenvalues; lacks direct complexity comparison with [7].  
- Identifiability issues: parametrizing L leads to identifiability problems (e.g., κ not well-learned) that are acknowledged but not adequately addressed.  
- Lack of theoretical analysis: no formal study of bound tightness or convergence rate as a function of the number of pseudo-inputs m.  
- Interpretability trade-off: paper notes but does not explore how parametrizing L vs K affects interpretability in downstream applications.  
- Suggestion: add a subsection on identifiability issues and how the method mitigates or is affected by them.  
- Suggestion: provide detailed computational complexity analysis and empirical runtime comparison with [7].  
- Suggestion: include theoretical analysis of bound tightness/convergence as m increases.  
- Suggestion: discuss practical implications of choosing L vs K parametrization for specific applications.  
- Overall: technically sound and practically valuable but needs stronger justification versus prior work and deeper treatment of limitations.  
- Pros summary: novel nonspectral bounds, applicable to finite and continuous domains, demonstrated on real data, clearly presented.  
- Cons summary: weak comparison to [7], insufficient treatment of identifiability, missing theoretical bound analysis, limited exploration of interpretability trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept (major revisions requested).
