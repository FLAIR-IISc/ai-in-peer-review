- Eliminates the need for spectral decomposition in parametric DPP inference by deriving likelihood bounds that avoid eigendecomposition.
- Proposes bounds inspired by variational GP techniques that approximate the likelihood using pseudo-inputs and determinant inequalities.
- Provides rigorous theoretical foundations (Propositions 1 and 2) with proofs in the supplement.
- Extends the bounds and methods to both discrete and continuous DPPs.
- Demonstrates practical applicability via variational inference and MCMC (including retrospective sampling).
- Empirically validated on a 1D Gaussian example and a diabetic neuropathy dataset, showing feasibility and effectiveness.
- Clearly written and well-organized, with standard notation and concise explanations.
- Connects to prior work on DPPs and sparse GP approximations, and avoids power-iteration methods used in earlier approaches.
- Main novelty is applying known determinant inequalities to DPP inference and deriving practical algorithms rather than new inequalities.
- Loss of interpretability when parametrizing L directly, making parameters harder to relate to underlying phenomena.
- Performance depends on the choice and number of pseudo-inputs Z; selection strategies need more systematic study.
- Optimizing over pseudo-inputs introduces computational costs that are not thoroughly compared to power-iteration methods.
- Potentially significant impact by making DPP inference tractable for larger datasets and continuous domains.
- Reviewer recommends strong accept, citing theoretical soundness, practical demonstration, and clear exposition despite minor weaknesses.
