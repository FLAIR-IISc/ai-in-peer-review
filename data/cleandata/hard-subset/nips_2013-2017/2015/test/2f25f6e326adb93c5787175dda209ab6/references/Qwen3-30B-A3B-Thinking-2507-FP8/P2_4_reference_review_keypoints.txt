- Summary: Proposes novel non-spectral likelihood bounds for DPPs using pseudo-inputs, enabling variational inference and MCMC without spectral decomposition for finite and continuous cases.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous mathematical derivations supported by supplementary proofs and appropriate use of determinant inequalities.  
- Experimental validation: Thorough experiments on synthetic (known-spectrum) and real diabetic neuropathy data demonstrating convergence and practical utility.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear notation and helpful connections to Gaussian process literature.  
- Originality: First work to provide non-spectral DPP likelihood bounds and to extend these ideas to continuous DPPs.  
- Significance: Addresses a major computational bottleneck, making DPP inference feasible at larger scales and widening application potential.  
- Key strength: Eliminates expensive spectral computations with theoretically sound, practically effective bounds.  
- Additional strengths: Good linkage to GP methods, effective use of pseudo-inputs, and comprehensive experimental analysis.  
- Weakness: Parametrizing L instead of K reduces interpretability and no clear remedy is provided.  
- Weakness: Continuous DPP extension may have limited relevance to practitioners focused on finite domains.  
- Weakness: Could include more direct comparisons to other approximate DPP inference methods.  
- Overall recommendation: High-quality, original, and significant work; recommended for acceptance (meets NIPS criteria: quality, clarity, originality, significance).
