- Summary: Proposes non-spectral bounds on DPP likelihoods to enable inference (variational + MCMC) without eigendecomposition, with theory and experiments on synthetic and real data.

- Strength: Tackles a major practical limitation by avoiding spectral decomposition, enabling scalability to larger/continuous DPPs.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation—bounds derived rigorously using determinant inequalities and linked to GP variational inference.

- Strength: Method applies to both finite and continuous DPPs, increasing applicability.

- Strength: Empirical validation demonstrates effectiveness, convergence to known results, and real-data utility (diabetic neuropathy).

- Strength: Clear, well-organized writing that makes the ideas accessible.

- Weakness: Direct parametrization of kernel L (rather than K) causes interpretability and identifiability issues (e.g., κ not well-identified).

- Weakness: Optimizing pseudo-inputs Z during MCMC/variational inference adds computational overhead; scalability analysis is limited.

- Weakness: Core inference techniques (variational inference, MCMC) are not novel; novelty is mainly in adapting them and deriving DPP-specific bounds.

- Weakness: Lacks comparison to alternative DPP approximation methods (e.g., Nyström, other low-rank approximations).

- Qualitative—Quality: Technically sound with theoretical and empirical support; limitations are acknowledged.

- Qualitative—Clarity: Notation and exposition are consistent and clear.

- Qualitative—Originality: Novel application of non-spectral bounds to DPP inference, effectively combining existing techniques for a new problem.

- Qualitative—Significance: Addresses a key bottleneck and could broaden practical use of DPPs.

- For acceptance: Addresses practical DPP inference problem, is theoretically grounded, empirically validated, and well-written.

- Against acceptance: Interpretation issues from parametrization, added computational cost for pseudo-inputs, limited methodological novelty, and missing baseline comparisons.

- Overall assessment: Strong accept — valuable, practical, and well-executed contribution to DPP literature despite acknowledged limitations.
