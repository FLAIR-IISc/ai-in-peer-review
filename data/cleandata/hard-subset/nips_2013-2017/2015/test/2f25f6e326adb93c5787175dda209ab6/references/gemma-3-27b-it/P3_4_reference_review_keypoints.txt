- Proposes bounds on DPP likelihoods that avoid explicit spectral decomposition, using variational GP ideas for finite and continuous DPPs.
- Addresses a major practical bottleneck in DPP inference, enabling scalability to larger or continuous domains.
- Builds on prior DPP and GP literature, extending spectral-free approaches and operator-theoretic results to continuous cases.
- Derivations (Propositions 1 & 2) are theoretically grounded, leveraging determinant inequalities and trace-class operator theory.
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, progressing from finite to continuous cases for reader clarity.
- Empirical validation includes a synthetic 1D Gaussian example and a real-world diabetic neuropathy dataset, showing method feasibility.
- Connects neatly to variational sparse GP methods and incorporates retrospective sampling within MCMC.
- Parameter identifiability issues observed with L-parametrization (notably κ) are acknowledged but not fully addressed.
- Optimization over pseudo-inputs (Z) introduces computational overhead; the paper lacks a detailed complexity comparison to spectral methods.
- Experimental scope is limited and lacks direct, competitive comparisons to existing spectral methods (e.g., [7]) on comparable datasets.
- Continuous-case assumptions (Mercer’s theorem, trace-class kernels, base measure µ) are noted but could use more discussion regarding practical implications.
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a key practical problem, provides rigorous bounds, and shows empirical promise.
- Arguments against acceptance: identifiability concerns, unclear cost of pseudo-input optimization, and limited experimental validation.
- Overall recommendation: accept; reviewer considers it a strong contribution despite noted weaknesses.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
