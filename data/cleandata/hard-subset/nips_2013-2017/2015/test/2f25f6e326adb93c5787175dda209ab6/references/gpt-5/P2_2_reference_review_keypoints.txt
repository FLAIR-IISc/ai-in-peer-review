- Proposes nonspectral upper and lower bounds on det(I+L) for finite DPPs using inducing inputs Z and a low-rank approximation Q, avoiding eigendecompositions.
- Extends these bounds to continuous DPPs via analogous Fredholm determinant bounds involving LZ and Ψ.
- Uses the bounds for (i) a variational lower bound for approximate MLE jointly optimizing θ and Z, and (ii) a retrospective MH sampler that refines Z until acceptance is resolved.
- Connects DPP inference to sparse GP methodology (inspired by Titsias 2009), enabling application where spectral methods are infeasible or expensive.
- The nonspectral bounds are conceptually novel and address a practical bottleneck in DPP inference.
- Theoretical development is sound, leveraging the Seiler–Simon determinant inequality and trace-class assumptions for the continuous case.
- Complexity O(n m^2) and memory O(n m) are practical when m ≪ n and avoid storing n×n matrices or computing eigenpairs.
- Retrospective MH scheme is asymptotically exact despite using approximate likelihood bounds.
- Empirical validation: toy 1D Gaussian–Gaussian DPP shows MH sampler matches an “ideal” MH baseline.
- Empirical validation: real neuropathy dataset demonstrates convergence of bounds, reasonable runtimes, and useful parameter estimates.
- Experimental scope is limited: no finite discrete large-scale experiments or comparisons against spectral methods like Affandi et al. (2014).
- No quantitative analysis of how the bound gap decays with m or principled guidance for selecting/optimizing inducing points Z.
- Sensitivity to Z is noted but gradients, initialization strategies, and analysis of failure modes (local optima, sensitivity to m) are lacking.
- Continuous-case practicality is underexplored: computing Ψ beyond Gaussian–Gaussian requires discussion of quadrature, stability, and cost.
- Bayesian inference diagnostics (mixing, acceptance rates, average m, autocorrelations, ESS) are lightly discussed and lack detail.
- Parameter identifiability issues (e.g., instability for κ) are observed but no remedies (priors, reparametrizations, K/L hybrid) are proposed.
- Overall quality: core claims are theoretically grounded with correct adaptation of inference schemes, but broader experiments would improve confidence.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written with minor notation/typo issues; more explicit derivations (gradients, complexity for tr(L−Q)) would aid reproducibility.
- Originality: nonspectral determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods and extension to continuous DPPs are novel and creatively adapt sparse GP ideas.
- Significance: removing eigen-decomposition dependence broadens DPP applicability, particularly for continuous domains and large discrete sets.
- Suggested improvements include adding large-scale finite experiments vs spectral baselines, analyzing bound tightening with m, providing gradients for Z, discussing Ψ computation beyond Gaussian settings, and reporting MH diagnostics.
- Pros: novel nonspectral bounds, applicability to continuous DPPs, solid GP connection, asymptotically exact retrospective MH, and practical demonstrations.
- Cons: limited experiments and baselines, lack of quantitative bound/Z-selection analysis, underexplored Ψ computation for general continuous settings, and unaddressed variational instability for some parameters.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — clear, novel, and practical contribution that would be strengthened by expanded empirical comparisons and guidance on Z and bound tightness.
