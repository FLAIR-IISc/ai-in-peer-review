- Problem: Evaluates the DPP normalizer det(I+L) without spectral decompositions using nonspectral, inducing-point-based bounds.  
- Finite-setting method: Proposes bounds sandwiching 1/det(I+L) between det(Q+I)^{-1} and det(Q+I)^{-1} exp(tr(L−Q)) using inducing inputs Z and Seiler–Simon inequalities.  
- Continuous-setting method: Extends bounds to trace-class operators/Fredholm determinants with Ψij = ∫ L(zi,x)L(x,zj)dµ(x); Ψ analytic for Gaussian kernel + Gaussian base.  
- Inference toolkit: Develops a variational lower bound F(θ,Z) and a retrospective MH scheme that tightens acceptance by increasing m and optimizing Z.  
- Computational cost: Claims O(n m^2) via matrix determinant lemma when m ≪ n, avoiding eigenvalue computations.  
- Experiments (1D): Retrospective MH matches exact Fredholm-based MH; variational inference recovers α and lengthscale but not κ (non-identifiability).  
- Experiments (real data): Diabetic neuropathy study shows converging bounds with increasing m, sensible inducing-point grids, and stable overdispersion measures separating classes.  
- Relation to prior work: Adapts variational sparse GP inducing-variable bounds (Titsias 2009) to DPP normalizers and contrasts with spectral truncation and Nyström methods.  
- Continuous links: Connects to numerical Fredholm determinant literature (Bornemann 2010) and cites future connections.  
- Strength — novelty: Provides a clean, general nonspectral approach with rigorous upper/lower bounds for finite and continuous DPPs.  
- Strength — practicality: Substantially reduces computation when m ≪ n and applies to continuous domains where spectra are unknown.  
- Strength — inference: Supplies both VI and an (asymptotically) exact retrospective MH procedure.  
- Strength — validation: Continuous 1D example validates correctness; real data illustrates practical behavior.  
- Weakness — theoretical guarantees: Lacks formal results on monotonicity/tightness of bounds as m increases and Z is optimized.  
- Weakness — optimization guidance: Insufficient practical advice on initializing and optimizing Z, gradient use, regularization, and numerical stability (jitter).  
- Weakness — complexity accounting: Ignores cost of det(LYt) per realization (O(|Yt|^3)); needs clarity on impact for large realizations.  
- Weakness — continuous generality: Ψ analytic only in Gaussian–Gaussian case; broader kernel/base-measure handling requires quadrature/approximation and more experiments.  
- Weakness — empirical comparisons: No comparisons to spectral MH or stochastic log-det approximations (Lanczos/SLQ + Hutchinson), which would contextualize performance.  
- Weakness — identifiability: Observed κ non-identifiability needs systematic discussion and practical remedies (priors, reparameterization).  
- Quality: Theory appears sound and carefully extended to trace-class operators; algorithms are well motivated though experiments are limited.  
- Clarity: Generally well written; suggests streamlining notation, clarifying matrix-determinant lemma use, fixing typos, and adding pseudocode for retrospective MH.  
- Originality: Novel adaptation of variational GP determinant bounds to DPP normalizers, especially for continuous domains and nonspectral MH.  
- Significance: Useful alternative to spectral methods for large finite and continuous DPPs; likely to spur further nonspectral inference work.  
- Suggested improvements: Add formal convergence/monotonicity results, implement gradient-based Z optimization and jitter, compare runtimes/accuracy to spectral and stochastic methods, broaden continuous experiments, clarify costs and adaptive m selection, and release code/pseudocode.  
- Pros summary: Eliminates spectral computations, provides rigorous simple bounds, offers practical VI and MH algorithms, and demonstrates correctness and real-data utility.  
- Cons summary: Limited empirical breadth, no formal tightness guarantees, strong dependence on Z optimization, and under-discussed practical/stability details.  
- Recommendation: Leaning toward acceptance pending stronger empirical comparisons and clearer theoretical/convergence discussion.
