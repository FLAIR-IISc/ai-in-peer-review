- Paper proposes nonspectral, computable upper and lower bounds on det(I+L) using a low-rank Q built from m pseudo-inputs Z, applicable to finite and continuous DPPs.  
- Bounds are used to form a variational lower bound on the log-likelihood and a retrospective MH scheme that tightens bounds by increasing m and re-optimizing Z.  
- Experiments: 1D Gaussian–Gaussian DPP for validation against exact Fredholm results, and a 2D neuropathy dataset for variational parameter estimation.  
- Builds on prior DPP work (Gillenwater, Mariet & Sra), Affandi’s spectral and Nyström methods, and Titsias’ sparse GP variational framework; complements Bornemann’s Fredholm quadrature.  
- Conceptual novelty: spectral-free determinant bounds with an inducing-point interpretation that generalize to operators.  
- Generality: construction does not require Z ⊂ Y, avoids storing full n×n matrices, and extends to continuous domains when Ψ is tractable.  
- Versatility: bounds support both fast variational inference and retrospective (asymptotically exact) MCMC.  
- Empirical validation: retrospective MH is cross-checked against “ideal” MH in 1D; neuropathy results show scalable variational training and convergence with m.  
- Clarity: analogy to Titsias’ sparse GP helps intuition and frames Q as a rank-m projector with tr(L−Q) correction.  
- No formal theoretical guarantees on tightness/convergence; claims that bounds can be arbitrarily refined by increasing m lack precise conditions or rates.  
- Finite-case exactness requires Z=Y (m=n); with Z ∉ Y exact recovery may be impossible and this is not fully characterized.  
- Retrospective MH correctness is not justified when Z is adaptively optimized per proposal; detailed-balance/invariance argument or citation is missing.  
- Computational complexity is incompletely discussed: computing det(LYt) still costs O(|Yt|^3), and the claimed O(n m^2) ignores this and trace computation costs.  
- Numerical stability and conditioning (e.g., need for jitter on LZ) and storage/implementation details are not addressed.  
- Scope of Ψ computability in continuous case is unclear beyond Gaussian–Gaussian; comparison to Bornemann’s quadrature method is missing.  
- Missing comparisons to modern spectral-free log-det approximations (stochastic Lanczos, Hutchinson) that could be competitive alternatives.  
- Empirical scope limited: no discrete-domain experiments (e.g., summarization, image diversity) or comparisons to EM/fixed-point learners and spectral-bounds MCMC.  
- Presentation issues: inconsistent equation numbering, misaligned footnotes, and informal statements (e.g., GP posterior variance link) needing formalization or removal.  
- Technical quality: determinant bounds derivation via Seiler–Simon is sound, but theoretical claims (convergence, MH validity) require proofs or precise conditions.  
- Reproducibility: manuscript needs clearer complexity accounting, regularization/gradient details, and implementation notes for Z and θ optimization.  
- Originality: high — nonspectral determinant bounds with inducing points and an operator extension are novel in the DPP context.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for scalable DPP inference without eigendecompositions, contingent on stronger theory and broader experiments.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, general, practical method; applies to finite and continuous DPPs; intuitive sparse-GP analogy and some empirical validation.  
- Cons against acceptance: lacks theoretical guarantees on tightness/MH correctness, limited experiments especially on discrete problems, and incomplete complexity/stability discussion.  
- Suggested improvements: provide convergence/monotonicity theorem for bounds, justify retrospective MH with adaptive Z, add discrete experiments and method comparisons, discuss numerical stability and gradient/autodiff details, compare to stochastic log-det methods, and fix presentation issues.
