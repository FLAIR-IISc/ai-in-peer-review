The paper proposes approximate inference techniques for determinantal point processes (DPPs) that circumvent the need for spectral knowledge of the kernel defining the DPP. Instead, inducing inputs are leveraged to bound the likelihood, thereby reducing computational cost. Both variational inference and approximate Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) techniques are described, and their efficiency is demonstrated empirically.

I believe this paper presents a valuable contribution, however, I found the method descriptions somewhat lacking in detail and the empirical evaluation not sufficiently comprehensive. To aid clarification and strengthen the work, I suggest addressing the following points:

1) What is the computational complexity and overall runtime comparison between the two proposed methods (variational inference and approximate MCMC)?
2) How do the proposed methods compare to other available alternatives – both approximate and exact – in terms of both the quality of the approximation achieved and computation time?
3) The empirical results in Section 5.1 reveal a substantial parameter estimation error when using variational inference, necessitating a switch to approximate MCMC. Under what conditions is the proposed variational inference expected to perform well, and when is it expected to fail? A discussion of the underlying reasons for these limitations would be beneficial.
4) In the toy example, a 13-point sample is generated. How do the proposed methods perform when the inducing inputs are specifically chosen to be these sampled points?
5) Are there guidelines or rules of thumb for selecting a) the number of inducing points and b) their initial placement?

Minor comments:

The matrices K and L are referenced throughout the introduction without prior definition. A brief explanation of the marginal kernel and L-ensembles would enhance readability for readers unfamiliar with these concepts. 
The paper discusses the advantages of directly learning the marginal kernel parameters, but does not implement this approach. I suggest streamlining this discussion, as it doesn’t directly contribute to the core contributions of the paper.

The introduction repeats the summary statement: "Inducing inputs are utilized to bound the DPP likelihood, which reduces the computational cost. Both variational inference and approximate Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) techniques are described and their efficiency is demonstrated empirically.I believe there is merit to this paper, however I found the method description rather rough and empirical evaluation not thorough enough." This repetition can be removed.

If accepted, I strongly encourage the authors to refine the presentation and address the points raised above.
