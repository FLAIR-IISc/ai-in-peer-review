- Introduces nonspectral determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods that avoid eigendecomposition using pseudo-inputs/inducing variables.  
- Extends variational and MCMC inference for DPPs by adapting Titsias’ variational GP bounds to the DPP setting.  
- Significant technical advance over prior spectral approximation methods (e.g., Affandi et al. 2013), removing a key computational bottleneck.  
- Achieves improved scalability (O(n m²) vs. O(n³) for spectral methods) while maintaining theoretical rigor.  
- Proofs are sound and provided in the supplementary material.  
- Experiments convincingly demonstrate efficacy: retrospective MCMC yields consistent posteriors and variational inference captures overdispersion.  
- Real-data application (diabetic neuropathy dataset) validates practical utility.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivates pseudo-inputs as optimization variables.  
- Appropriately trades interpretability (parameterizing L instead of K) for tractability, though this discussion is slightly overemphasized.  
- Continuous-domain extension depends on Mercer’s theorem; limitations for non-Mercer or heavy-tailed kernels should be briefly noted.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, original, and impactful contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
