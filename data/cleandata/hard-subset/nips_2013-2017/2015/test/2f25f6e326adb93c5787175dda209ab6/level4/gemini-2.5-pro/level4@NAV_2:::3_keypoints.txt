- Paper derives novel non-spectral upper and lower bounds on DPP likelihoods for finite and continuous domains using inducing-variable ideas.  
- Bounds enable two inference schemes: a fast approximate variational inference (VI) and an asymptotically exact but costlier MCMC with retrospective sampling.  
- Strength: addresses an important bottleneck by avoiding kernel eigendecomposition, improving scalability to kernels without tractable spectra.  
- Strength: original adaptation of inducing-point/sparse GP methods to DPP likelihoods, including extension to continuous domains.  
- Strength: provides practical trade-off between cost and accuracy by offering both VI and MCMC options for practitioners.  
- Weakness: insufficient empirical comparisons — paper lacks runtime and accuracy comparisons between proposed VI and MCMC methods.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to existing alternatives (e.g., Affandi et al. 2014 spectral-bound MCMC, Nyström approximations) on both approximation quality and runtime.  
- Weakness: variational inference gave inconsistent results on the toy dataset, but the paper does not analyze when VI is expected to succeed or fail.  
- Weakness: no analysis of the VI lower bound or optimization landscape to explain poor VI performance or provide guidance for reliability.  
- Weakness: inadequate practical guidance on choosing inducing inputs Z, including number of inducing points m and initialization strategies.  
- Weakness: missing experiments on natural baselines, e.g., setting inducing inputs equal to observed data in small examples.  
- Minor clarity: kernels K and L are referenced before definition; briefly explain marginal kernel K vs. L-ensemble L earlier for readers unfamiliar with DPPs.  
- Minor clarity: introductory discussion emphasizes learning interpretable K but then parametrizes L; condense K discussion to focus on inference contributions for L-ensembles.
