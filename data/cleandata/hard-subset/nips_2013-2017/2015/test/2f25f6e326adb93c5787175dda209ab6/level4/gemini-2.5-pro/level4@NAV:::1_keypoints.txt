- DPPs model repulsion via a positive definite kernel, but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant over all set configurations.  
- Prior work [7] estimated the kernel spectrum to build incremental bounds enabling retrospective MCMC for parameters.  
- This paper proposes a new bounding technique that avoids eigenvalue estimation and is compatible with MCMC and variational inference.  
- The framework draws useful connections to inducing-point methods from scalable Gaussian process inference.  
- The paper is technically sound and well-written; Proposition 1’s inequality is an effective, novel tool that circumvents prior estimation difficulties.  
- Unclear computational advantage: trading power iterations for a sequence of increasing-dimensional nonlinear optimizations is not convincingly justified.  
- Convergence concern: bounds may not tighten with increasing cardinality because the coupled optimization is highly non-convex and may converge to local minima, potentially stalling MCMC.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels.  
- Presentation issues: the proof of the main proposition should be moved to the main text rather than the appendix.  
- Presentation issues: typo on Page 7, Line 361 ("... the variational lower is ...").
