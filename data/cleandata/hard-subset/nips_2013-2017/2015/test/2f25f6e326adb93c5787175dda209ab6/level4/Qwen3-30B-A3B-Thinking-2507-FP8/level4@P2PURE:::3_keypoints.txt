- Proposes approximate inference for DPPs using inducing inputs to bound the likelihood, avoiding spectral knowledge; presents variational inference and approximate MCMC with empirical efficiency.  
- Method description is rough and the empirical evaluation is not thorough.  
- Clarify computational complexity and wall-clock runtime comparison between the variational and MCMC methods.  
- Compare approximation quality and computational time against other approximate and exact DPP inference alternatives.  
- Explain when variational inference is expected to succeed or fail, since VI showed large parameter error on toy data necessitating MCMC.  
- Report performance when inducing inputs are exactly the sampled points (toy example uses 13 points).  
- Provide rules of thumb for choosing (a) the number of inducing points and (b) initial placement of inducing points.  
- Minor: briefly define matrices K and L (marginal kernel and L-ensembles) for readability.  
- Minor: shorten or remove the discussion advocating direct learning of marginal kernel parameters since the paper does not adopt that approach.  
- Overall: paper has merit but requires significant rewrite and addressing of the above points if accepted.
