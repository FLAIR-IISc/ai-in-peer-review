This paper introduces new upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant, demonstrating their utility in bounding the likelihood function of a determinantal point process. While the core concept is straightforward, the work is presented clearly and offers valuable insights, opening avenues for future research and refinement.

The authors cleverly leverage pseudo-inputs to establish bounds on functionals of positive definite kernels. This is a pertinent problem with broad appeal within the machine learning community. 

The proposed methodology is generally applicable, extending beyond the specific context of determinantal point processes. The experimental results are thoughtfully designed and effectively highlight important considerations, notably limitations of a variational approximation under specific circumstances. The chosen application area is both interesting and highly pertinent to the investigated problem.

Minor comments:

- The emphasis on parameterizing the kernel *K* (rather than *L*) in the abstract and throughout the paper is somewhat unclear, as the authors primarily characterize the problem without attempting to resolve this parameterization challenge.
- Should the paper be accepted, I recommend providing a dedicated supplementary document, rather than simply uploading an extended version of the manuscript.

Suggestions for future work:

- The discussion surrounding the interpretability of the *K* parameterization is intriguing.  Could the authors investigate directly bounding the determinant in terms of the *K* matrix? Furthermore, what are the scalability implications of this approach when employing the pseudo-input method?
