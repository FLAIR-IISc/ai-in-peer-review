The authors propose new upper and lower bounds on the determinant of a matrix, which would be useful for limiting the potential for determinant-point approximation. The core idea is simple, but the paper is well-written and educational, and it paves the way for several potential improvements.

The authors propose a creative use of pseudo-inputs to functionally constrain the positive definite kernel. The proposed problem is timely and will be of interest to many in the community.

Such an approach is quite general with potential applications beyond determinant-point approximation. The experimental evaluation is well-designed to clarify several key points, including the failure of variable approximation under specific conditions. I found the selected application area to be attractive and highly relevant to the problem being studied.

Small comments:
- It is not clear why the parameterization of K (instead of L) is so abstract and prominently featured in the rest of the paper, as the authors primarily focused on describing the problem and made no attempt to solve it.
- If accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement, and not just upload an extended version of the paper as a supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- On the other hand, a discussion on the interpretability of K parameterization would make such an approach more appealing. Could the authors consider directly constraining the determinant in terms of the K matrix? What is the scalability of such an approach using the pseudo-input method?
