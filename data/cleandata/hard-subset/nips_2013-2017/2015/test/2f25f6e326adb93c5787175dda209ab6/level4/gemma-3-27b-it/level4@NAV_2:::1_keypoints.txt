- DPPs model repulsion via a kernel but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.
- Prior work ([7]) estimates the spectrum to get incremental bounds for provably-correct retrospective MCMC of kernel parameters.
- This paper proposes new bounds usable for MCMC that avoid eigenvalue estimation and enable variational inference.
- The method connects DPP inference to inducing-point approaches from scalable Gaussian process models.
- The paper is technically strong, well-written, and leverages a useful inequality (Proposition 1) that avoids spectral estimation.
- Proposition 1’s inequality is a key, novel insight and a major contribution of the paper.
- Concern: unclear whether replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimension yields a real computational advantage.
- Concern: increasing inducing-point cardinality may not guarantee convergence of the bounds because the coupled optimization is likely highly non-convex and prone to poor local minima.
- A local minimum could prevent reaching the Bernoulli threshold required for MCMC, so convergence guarantees or mitigation strategies are needed.
- Presentation suggestion: add axis labels to Figure 1.
- Presentation suggestion: move the proof of Proposition 1 from the supplement into the main paper.
- Presentation suggestion: fix grammatical error on P7, L361 (“the variational lower is…”).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important DPP inference problem, offers novel mathematically sound bounds, and provides practical benefits via inducing-point and variational connections.
- Arguments against acceptance: practical computational benefits are not definitively demonstrated and convergence properties need further investigation.
- Overall: valuable contribution that would be strong with minor revisions addressing the computational and convergence concerns.
