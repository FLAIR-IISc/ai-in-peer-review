- DPPs model repulsion via a PSD kernel, but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.  
- Prior work [7] estimates the spectrum to get incremental bounds for retrospective MCMC of kernel parameters.  
- This paper proposes new bounds for MCMC that avoid eigenvalue estimation and also enable variational learning.  
- The approach connects to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian process inference.  
- The paper is technically strong and well written; Proposition 1’s lower-bound inequality is a clever central insight.  
- The GP-inducing-point and variational-inference connections are particularly noteworthy.  
- Concern: Replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension may not be a clear computational win.  
- Concern: Increasing cardinality may not guarantee convergence of bounds because the coupled optimization is highly nonconvex; local minima could block bounding the Bernoulli threshold and prevent MCMC steps.  
- Presentation: Figure 1 needs axis labels for clarity.  
- Presentation: The proof of the main proposition should be in the main text rather than the appendix.  
- Minor wording: rephrase "the variational lower is" (page 7, line 361) for clarity.  
- Overall: With technical clarifications and presentation fixes, the paper could make a significant contribution.
