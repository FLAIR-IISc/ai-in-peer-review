- Presents novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants with applications to bounding DPP likelihoods.  
- Core idea: use pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels—simple, elegant, and clearly executed.  
- Proposed bounds enable tighter likelihood approximations for DPPs and highlight limitations of existing variational methods.  
- Timely and well-motivated, directly relevant to ML practitioners working with kernel-based probabilistic models.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous, accessible, and clearly derived.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: confirms bound tightness and systematically identifies variational failure modes (e.g., skewed kernel eigenvalues).  
- Approach generalizes beyond DPPs to Gaussian processes, kernel approximation, and Bayesian optimization.  
- Choice of experimental settings (controlled synthetic and real-world DPPs) effectively demonstrates practical utility.  
- Minor issue: paper emphasizes K vs L parameterization but does not address how to handle K-parameterization in practice.  
- Minor issue: supplementary material should be a concise supplement (proofs, extended experiments) rather than an extended main paper.  
- Suggested future work: derive determinant bounds directly in terms of K (not L) for better interpretability.  
- Suggested future work: provide deeper analysis of scalability and computational complexity of the pseudo-input approach (e.g., dependence on n and m).  
- Overall: strong, theoretically sound, experimentally compelling contribution with minor, easily addressable issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
