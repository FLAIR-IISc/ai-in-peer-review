- Problem: DPP likelihood inference is bottlenecked by a large determinant requiring costly eigendecomposition of the kernel matrix.  
- Contribution: Derives novel upper and lower bounds on the determinant that require no spectral information using pseudo-inputs for low-rank approximation.  
- Scope: Presents bounds for both finite-set and continuous DPPs and develops two inference schemes: fast variational inference and exact retrospective MCMC.  
- Originality/Significance: Non-spectral bounds are a novel, elegant solution that avoids eigendecompositions and broadens applicability to larger-scale problems.  
- Technical quality: Sound derivations, clear application to VI and MCMC, and well-designed experiments including a case showing VI failure and MCMC robustness.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized presentation, introducing the discrete case before the continuous case for intuition.  
- Weakness: Framing mismatch — paper emphasizes K vs L interpretability but does not directly address K-parametrization, focusing instead on inference for L.  
- Weakness: Minor revision suggestion — provide a standard supplement rather than an extended main paper.  
- Future work: Investigate adapting the pseudo-input framework to bound the likelihood in terms of the K matrix and analyze scalability of that approach.
