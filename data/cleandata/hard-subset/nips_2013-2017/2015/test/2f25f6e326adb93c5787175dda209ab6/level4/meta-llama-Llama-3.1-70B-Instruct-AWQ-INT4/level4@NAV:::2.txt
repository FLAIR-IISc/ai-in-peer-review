The authors present innovative upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant, which have significant implications for bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes. The core concept is straightforward, yet the paper is skillfully crafted and insightful, laying the groundwork for multiple potential advancements.

The authors demonstrate a creative application of pseudo-inputs in bounding functionals of positive definite kernels, addressing a timely problem that will likely resonate with the community. This approach exhibits considerable generality, with potential applications extending beyond determinantal point processes.

The experimental evaluation is well-designed, effectively highlighting key aspects, including the limitations of variational approximations under specific conditions. The chosen application area is engaging and highly pertinent to the problem under investigation.

Minor comments:
- The emphasis on parameterizing K (as opposed to L) in the abstract and the rest of the paper is unclear, as the authors primarily focus on problem description without attempting to address this issue.
- If accepted, it is recommended that the authors prepare a standard supplement rather than uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion on the interpretability of K parameterization makes this approach more appealing. Could the authors consider directly bounding the determinant in terms of the K matrix? What are the scalability implications of using the pseudo-input method for such an approach?
