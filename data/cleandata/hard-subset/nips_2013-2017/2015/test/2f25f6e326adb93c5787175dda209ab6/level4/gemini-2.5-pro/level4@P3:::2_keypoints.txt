- Addresses the computational bottleneck of computing the normalizing constant/determinant in likelihood-based inference for finite and continuous DPPs.
- Main contribution: novel, computationally efficient upper and lower bounds on the determinant that require no spectral information, inspired by inducing-point (sparse GP) methods.
- Bounds are integrated into a fast approximate variational inference scheme and an exact MCMC algorithm using retrospective sampling.
- Methods are developed for both finite and continuous DPPs and validated on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Strength: high significance and originality—adapting inducing-point ideas to non-spectral DPP bounds is creative and opens new inference avenues.
- Strength: strong technical quality with rigorous derivations and clear application to VI and MCMC.
- Strength: insightful experiments, including a toy 1D ground-truth validation, transparent demonstration of VI limitations, and real-data diabetic neuropathy results showing controllable approximation quality via more inducing points.
- Strength: excellent clarity and organization; paper is well-written and the connection to sparse GPs aids understanding.
- Weakness: framing mismatch—paper emphasizes K vs L parameterization interpretability but ultimately works with the standard L-parametrization, making that setup feel disconnected from the core contribution.
- Minor question: could the bounding technique be applied to K-based formulations (e.g., det(I−K)) and what challenges would arise?
- Minor suggestion: format the supplementary material as a standalone document for easier navigation if accepted.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, technically solid and practically impactful—recommend acceptance.
