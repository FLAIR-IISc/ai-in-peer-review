- Proposes new non-spectral bounds for DPP partition functions that enable MCMC and variational inference without eigenvalue estimates, with connections to GP inducing points.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-written, and uses a clever inequality (Proposition 1) central to the contribution.  
- Replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension may not constitute a clear computational improvement.  
- Increasing the number of inducing points may not guarantee convergence of the bounds due to coupled, likely highly non-convex optimization and risk of getting stuck in local minima.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels, proof of Proposition 1 should be in the main paper, and a minor typo (“the variational lower is …”) at line 361.  
- Overall assessment: a nice, clever approach that could speed inference and links to other models.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and technically sound method, avoids expensive spectral decompositions, clear GP connections, well-written, supported by experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear computational advantage, unestablished convergence guarantees for inducing-point refinement, and potential optimization/local optimum problems.
