

The paper introduces approximate inference methods for determinantal point processes (DPPs) that eliminate the need for spectral knowledge of the kernel defining the process. The approach leverages inducing inputs to establish bounds on the likelihood function, thereby reducing computational complexity. The authors present both variational inference and approximate Markov chain Monte Carlo techniques, with empirical validation demonstrating their efficiency.

I recognize the paper's contribution to the field, though I found the methodological exposition somewhat superficial and the empirical validation insufficiently comprehensive. To strengthen the paper, I recommend addressing the following points:

1) A detailed comparison of the computational complexity and actual runtime performance between the variational inference and approximate MCMC approaches would be valuable.
2) A thorough comparison with existing alternatives (both approximate and exact methods) in terms of approximation quality and computational efficiency would better establish the method's advantages.
3) The empirical results in Section 5.1 indicate significant parameter estimation errors with variational inference, necessitating a switch to approximate MCMC. It would be helpful to clarify the specific conditions under which variational inference performs well versus when it fails, and why.
4) The toy example uses a 13-point sample. It would be insightful to examine performance when the inducing inputs correspond to these sampled points.
5) Practical guidance on selecting both the number of inducing points and their initial placement would be beneficial for practitioners.

Minor comments:
The introduction refers to matrices K and L without sufficient explanation of their roles. Briefly defining the marginal kernel and L-ensembles would improve accessibility for readers unfamiliar with DPPs.
The paper contains extensive discussion about the benefits of directly learning marginal kernel parameters, despite the authors ultimately adopting the L-parametrization approach. This section could be condensed as it does not directly support the main contribution.
