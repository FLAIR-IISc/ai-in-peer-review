- Paper proposes approximate DPP inference using inducing inputs to bound the likelihood, avoiding spectral kernel knowledge; presents variational inference and approximate MCMC with empirical efficiency results.
- Methodological exposition is underdeveloped and empirical evaluation is insufficiently comprehensive.
- Need a clear comparison of computational complexity and runtime between the two proposed methods.
- Need evaluation versus existing exact and approximate methods on approximation quality and computational efficiency.
- Variational inference shows significant parameter-estimation error on the toy dataset; authors should state conditions when it will succeed or fail and explain why.
- For the toy example, evaluate performance when inducing inputs are the sampled 13 points.
- Provide heuristics/guidelines for selecting the number of inducing points and initial placement.
- Minor: Matrices K and L are referenced without explaining marginal kernel and L-ensembles; add brief definitions for accessibility.
- Minor: Reduce or remove discussion about directly learning marginal kernel parameters since it is not pursued in the methods.
- Recommendation: paper has merit but needs clearer presentation and deeper empirical validation before acceptance.
