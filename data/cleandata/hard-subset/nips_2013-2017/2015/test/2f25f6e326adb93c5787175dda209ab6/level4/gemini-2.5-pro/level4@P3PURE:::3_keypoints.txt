- Proposes inducing-input bounds to avoid spectral decomposition for DPPs, with two methods: variational inference (VI) and approximate MCMC.
- Manuscript is rough: method descriptions lack clarity and empirical evaluation is not thorough—needs improved write-up.

Main concerns:
- No direct comparison between VI and approximate MCMC; lacks computational-complexity analysis and empirical runtime comparison on the same tasks.
- Experiments omit comparisons to relevant alternative approximate and exact DPP inference methods for both approximation quality and computational cost.
- VI shows large parameter estimation errors in experiments; paper needs discussion of when VI is expected to work, when it fails, and reasons for its failure here.
- Recommend diagnostic: set inducing points to the actual sampled points in the toy example to assess bound tightness and approximation behavior.
- Need practical guidance on choosing the number and initial placement of inducing points, plus sensitivity analysis or principled selection rules.

Minor comments:
- Define matrices K (marginal kernel) and L (L-ensemble) for readers not familiar with DPPs.
- Streamline or reduce discussion about directly learning marginal kernel parameters since the proposed methods do not follow that approach.
