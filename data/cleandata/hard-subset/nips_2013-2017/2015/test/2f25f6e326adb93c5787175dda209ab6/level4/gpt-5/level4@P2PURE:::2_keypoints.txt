- Novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant are presented, applied to bounding determinantal point process (DPP) likelihoods.  
- The central idea is simple, well executed, and illuminating, opening the door to potential improvements.  
- Using pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels is a creative contribution.  
- The problem is timely and should interest many in the community.  
- The approach appears general and may have applications beyond DPPs.  
- Experimental evaluation is carefully designed and highlights key behaviors, including failure cases of variational approximations.  
- The chosen application area is interesting and highly relevant.  
- Minor: It is unclear why the paper emphasizes parameterizing K rather than L, since the paper does not attempt to address this.  
- Minor: Recommend providing a standard supplementary document instead of uploading an extended paper as the supplement.  
- Future work suggestion: Explore bounding the determinant directly in terms of K and analyze the scalability of the pseudo-input method for that parameterization.
