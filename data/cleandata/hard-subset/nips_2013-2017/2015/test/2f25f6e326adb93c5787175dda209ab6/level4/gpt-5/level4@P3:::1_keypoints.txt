- Proposes nonspectral upper/lower bounds on DPP likelihoods for finite and continuous domains using an inducing-point approximation Q and a trace correction.

- Enables two inference schemes: (i) variational lower bound for approximate MLE and (ii) retrospective MH that tightens bounds until accept/reject, with continuous extensions using Fredholm determinants and computable Ψ in Gaussian settings.

- Originality: introduces clean, nonspectral determinant bounds (building on Seiler & Simon) and ties them elegantly to GP inducing-point methods.

- Quality: bounds are theoretically motivated, easy to evaluate, lead to practical inference, and the retrospective MCMC is correct-by-construction; continuous extension has sound operator-theoretic framing.

- Significance: removes need for eigenvalue computations, broadens DPP applicability and scalability, and is particularly valuable for continuous/spatial applications.

- Clarity: generally well written with helpful connections to GP variational intuition.

- Concern: computational advantage unclear since method requires nonlinear optimization over increasing inducing points; lacks head-to-head runtime comparison with spectral-bounds approaches.

- Suggestion: include ablations showing time per iteration, convergence of bound with m, and sensitivity to number of Z-optimization steps.

- Concern: convergence under optimized Z may stall due to nonconvexity; need explicit statement that expanding Z to Y yields equality and practical strategies (nested Z, restarts, jitter, warm starts) to avoid local optima.

- Concern (continuous): provide theorem/citation or empirical evidence that bounds tighten as m increases and Z becomes dense; show monotone tightening for nested Z and multiple initializations.

- Identifiability issue: κ is weakly identified when parameterizing L directly; paper should offer practical guidance (informative priors, reparameterizations, diagnostics comparing VI vs MCMC).

- Numerical details: state when Ψ is available in closed form (e.g., Gaussian–Gaussian) and advise on numerically stable inversion of LZ (add jitter).

- Quality assessment summary: technically strong and original, clarity good, significance good-to-high.

- Suggestion: provide runtime comparisons with baseline [7] across n, m, and accuracy targets, report average inducing points per MH decision and acceptance rates.

- Suggestion: describe practical strategy for increasing m and optimizing Z (e.g., doubling m, limited L-BFGS, warm starts, restarts) and report restart frequency.

- Suggestion: include plots of bound tightness vs m for nested Z in both finite and continuous cases and report monotonicity.

- Suggestion: move or expand the finite-domain proof (Proposition 1) into main text and define Ψ and all notation where first used.

- Suggestion: discuss initialization of Z (k-means, sampling from base measure) and consider releasing code or pseudocode for reproducibility.

- Presentation issues: add axis labels to Figure 1, fix typo “variational lower is” → “variational lower bound is,” and ensure consistent notation for submatrices.

- Pros for acceptance: novel, simple, effective nonspectral bounds applicable to finite and continuous DPPs enabling variational training and correct retrospective MCMC without eigen computations.

- Cons for acceptance: limited quantitative comparison to spectral-bounds baselines and potential fragility of convergence under nonconvex Z-optimization.

- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on strengthening empirical comparisons, clarifying convergence behavior, and elevating the core proof.
