This paper introduces new upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant, demonstrating their utility in bounding the likelihood function of a determinantal point process. While the core concept is straightforward, the work is presented clearly and provides valuable insights, opening avenues for further research.

The authors cleverly leverage pseudo-inputs to establish bounds on functionals of positive definite kernels. This is a pertinent problem with broad appeal within the machine learning community.

The methodology is generally applicable, extending beyond the specific context of determinantal point processes. The experimental validation is thoughtfully designed, effectively illustrating key aspects, including limitations of a variational approximation under specific scenarios. The chosen application area is both interesting and well-aligned with the presented problem.

Minor comments:
- The emphasis on parameterizing the kernel *K* (rather than *L*) in the abstract and throughout the paper is somewhat unclear, given that the authors primarily focus on problem formulation without attempting to resolve this parameterization issue.
- Should the paper be accepted, a dedicated supplementary document, rather than simply an extended manuscript, would be preferable.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion surrounding the interpretability of the *K* parameterization is intriguing. Exploring direct bounding of the determinant in terms of the *K* matrix could be a fruitful direction. Furthermore, investigating the scalability of this approach when employing the pseudo-input method would be valuable.
