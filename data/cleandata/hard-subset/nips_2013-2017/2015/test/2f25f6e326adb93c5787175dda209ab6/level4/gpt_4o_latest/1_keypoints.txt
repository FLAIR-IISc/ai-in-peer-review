- Paper tackles learning DPP kernel parameters, a hard problem due to an intractable normalization constant.
- Prior work [7] estimates the spectrum and uses incremental eigenvalue bounds to enable retrospective MCMC.
- Main contribution: a novel bound that enables MCMC inference without eigenvalue estimation.
- Method also supports variational learning and draws connections to inducing-point methods for scalable GP-like inference.
- Paper is technically strong and well-written.
- Proposition 1 uses an unfamiliar but well-suited inequality to derive the lower bound.
- Innovative way to avoid spectrum estimation challenges in [7].
- Technical concern 1: unclear that replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimensionality is a computational win.
- Technical concern 2: unclear that increasing cardinality guarantees convergence of bounds due to coupled, likely highly non-convex optimization; local minima could block MCMC steps.
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 (page 7) lacks axis labels.
- Presentation issue: the critical proposition proof should be in the main text rather than the appendix.
- Presentation issue: incomplete phrase on page 7, line 361 ("... the variational lower is ...").
- Overall assessment: excellent, clever technique with potential for faster inference and useful connections to other modeling approaches.
