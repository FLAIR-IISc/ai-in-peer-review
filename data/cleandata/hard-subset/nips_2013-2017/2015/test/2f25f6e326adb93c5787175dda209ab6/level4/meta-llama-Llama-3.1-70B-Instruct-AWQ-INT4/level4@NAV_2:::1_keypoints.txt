- Problem: Learning DPP kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant (determinant).
- Contribution: Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate nonspectral bounds on determinants for finite and continuous DPPs.
- Use cases: Bounds enable exact-but-expensive MCMC and cheap-but-approximate variational inference.
- Connection: Draws links to GP inducing-point methods for scalable inference.
- Technical merit: Leverages a strong inequality (Proposition 1) that avoids estimation issues in prior work.
- Strength: Allows variational inference and connects DPP inference to established GP techniques.
- Concern — computational gain: Unclear if replacing power iterations with growing-dimension nonlinear optimization yields real computational benefit.
- Concern — convergence: Coupled, nonconvex optimization may not converge to a global minimum, risking failure to bound away the Bernoulli threshold for MCMC steps.
- Presentation: Figures need axis labels (page 7) for clarity.
- Presentation: Proposition proof should be moved from supplementary material into the main paper.
- Overall assessment: Technically strong, well-written, original, and significant with well-supported claims and experiments.
- Acceptance pros: Novel bounding approach, valuable GP and variational connections, addresses an important DPP inference challenge.
- Acceptance cons: Unclear computational advantage and potential convergence/local-minima issues in optimization.
