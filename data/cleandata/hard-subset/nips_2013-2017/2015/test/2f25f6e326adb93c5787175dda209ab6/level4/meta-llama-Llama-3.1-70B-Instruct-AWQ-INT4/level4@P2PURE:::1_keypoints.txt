- DPPs model repulsion via a kernel but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.
- Prior work [7] estimates the spectrum to get incremental eigenvalue-based bounds for retrospective MCMC of kernel parameters.
- This paper proposes alternative bounds usable for MCMC that avoid eigenvalue estimation.
- The new approach enables a variational learning framework and connects to GP inducing-point methods for scalable inference.
- The paper is technically strong, well-written, and uses a clever inequality in Proposition 1 to avoid spectrum estimation.
- The connections to inducing points and the ability to perform variational inference are notable strengths.
- Concern: Replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension may not be a clear computational win.
- Concern: Increasing cardinality may not guarantee bound convergence because the coupled optimization appears highly non-convex and may get stuck in local minima.
- Critical issue: Local minima could prevent bounding away the Bernoulli threshold, stopping valid MCMC moves.
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 lacks axis labels.
- Presentation issue: The proof of the central proposition should be moved from the appendix into the main text for accessibility.
- Presentation issue: Rephrase awkward wording on page 7, line 361 ("...the variational lower is...").
- Overall: A well-written paper offering a clever bounding trick with potential for faster inference and model connections.
