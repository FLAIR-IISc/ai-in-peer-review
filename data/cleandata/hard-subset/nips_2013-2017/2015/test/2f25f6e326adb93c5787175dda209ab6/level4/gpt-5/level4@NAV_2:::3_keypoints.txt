- Paper proposes DPP inference that avoids spectral decompositions by using inducing inputs to bound the likelihood, applied in a variational objective and a retrospective-acceptance MCMC sampler, with empirical efficiency demonstrations.

- Contribution is promising but methodological details are under-specified and empirical evaluation lacks sufficient depth.

- Request for direct comparison of computational complexity and wall-clock runtime between variational and MCMC approaches, including scaling with data size n and number of inducing points m.

- Request for quantitative comparison against existing alternatives (approximate and exact/spectral), reporting both approximation quality and computational time on shared benchmarks.

- Ask for analysis of when the variational approach succeeds or fails (dependence on data size, kernel smoothness, identifiability, and m) and explanation of observed large parameter errors in Section 5.1.

- Question about the toy experiment: how methods behave when the inducing set is chosen to be the observed points.

- Request for practical guidance: heuristics for selecting m and strategies for initializing/placing inducing inputs.

- Minor: K and L are used before being clearly defined—add an early explanation of marginal kernel K and L-ensembles.

- Minor: manuscript motivates learning the marginal kernel but ultimately uses L-ensemble parameterization—suggest trimming or refocusing that discussion.

- Overall: inducing-input approach and empirical illustrations are promising, but presentation needs tightening and substantially deeper experiments; recommend major revision if accepted.
