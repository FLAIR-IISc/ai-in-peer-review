- DPPs model repulsion via a PD kernel but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.  
- Prior work [7] estimates the spectrum to get incremental eigenvalue-based bounds enabling retrospective MCMC.  
- This paper proposes alternative bounds that avoid eigenvalue estimation and can be used for MCMC.  
- The new approach admits a variational learning scheme and draws connections to GP inducing-point methods.  
- The paper is technically strong and well written.  
- Proposition 1’s lower-bound inequality is a central, clever insight.  
- Variational inference and GP-inducing-point connections are noteworthy contributions.  
- Concern 1: Unclear if replacing power iterations with growing-dimension nonlinear optimizations is a real computational win.  
- Concern 2: Increasing cardinality may not guarantee bound convergence because the coupled, highly nonconvex optimization can get stuck in local minima, preventing valid MCMC steps.  
- Presentation: Figure 1 lacks axis labels.  
- Presentation: The proof of the main proposition should be in the main text rather than the appendix.
