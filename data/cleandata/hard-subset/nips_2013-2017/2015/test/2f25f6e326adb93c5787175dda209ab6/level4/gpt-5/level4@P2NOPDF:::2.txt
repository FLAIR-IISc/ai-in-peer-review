The paper introduces new upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants and applies them to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs). Although the central idea is straightforward, the execution is careful and the insights are valuable, opening avenues for several extensions. The work makes creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. The problem is timely and should interest a broad segment of the community. The approach appears quite general, with potential applications well beyond DPPs.

The empirical evaluation is thoughtfully designed and clarifies several key issues, including conditions under which a variational approximation fails. The chosen application domain is well aligned with the technical contributions and demonstrates their practical relevance.

Minor comments:
- It is unclear why parameterizing K (rather than L) is emphasized in the abstract and throughout the paper, since the manuscript primarily describes the issue but does not propose a concrete solution.
- If accepted, please provide a conventional supplementary document rather than simply uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion on the interpretability of the K parameterization is compelling. Could the authors develop bounds that operate directly on the K matrix? What would be the computational and memory scalability of such bounds when implemented via the pseudo-input technique?
