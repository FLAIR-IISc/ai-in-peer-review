- Technical novelty: Proposition 1 gives an elegant lower bound avoiding spectrum estimation for DPP partition functions.  
- Practical alternative: Combined lower and upper bounds offer a usable substitute for retrospective MCMC that relies on spectral approximations.  
- Conceptual link: The method clarifies tight connections between DPPs and inducing-point approaches for scalable GPs.  
- Clarity: Paper is well written, coherent, and clearly motivates methodological contributions.  

- Computational trade-off concern: Unclear that solving growing nonlinear optimization problems outperforms power-iteration-based spectral methods in practice.  
- Complexity comparison requested: Provide per-iteration complexity and memory comparisons versus power-iteration spectral estimation.  
- Sensitivity to inducing points: Quantify how the number of inducing points scales with dataset size and kernel smoothness.  
- Empirical trade-offs: Report runtime vs. bound tightness and compare accuracy of bounds against spectral approaches.  
- Ablations needed: Separate effect of bound type from optimization heuristics (initialization, step sizes, stopping tolerances).  

- Convergence worry: Increasing inducing-set cardinality may not guarantee monotone bound improvement due to nonconvex inner optimization.  
- Conditions for monotonicity: Clarify when increasing inducing points yields monotone improvement if inner problems are solved to global optimality.  
- Global optimality certification: Explain how global optimality is certified or approximated in practice (convex subproblems, dual gaps, certificates).  
- Effect of local minima on MCMC: Assess whether poor local optima can prevent Bernoulli-threshold decisions and stall mixing.  
- Practical mitigations: Recommend warm starts, restarts, trust-region/annealing/continuation strategies, or incremental updates to ensure robustness.  
- Empirical diagnostics: If theory is infeasible, provide diagnostics (bound progress vs restarts, seed variance) and robust hyperparameter settings.  

- Regime guidance: Provide a concise statement of statistical/computational regimes where this method outperforms [7] (e.g., slow spectral decay, unstable eigenvalue estimation, need for variational learning).  
- Inducing-point selection: Clarify selection method (greedy, k-DPP, leverage scores, joint learning) and its impact on tightness and runtime.  
- Mini-batching: State whether the variational objective and bounds support stochastic mini-batching and remain valid under subsampling.  

- Presentation fixes: Add axis labels/units to Figure 1; move or summarize the proof of Proposition 1 into the main text; fix typo on p.7 (“variational lower bound is …”); briefly summarize the key inequality in the main text.  

- Overall assessment: Technically strong and promising; main reservations are practical computational benefits versus [7] and robustness of bound tightening; leans positive conditional on addressing these concerns.
