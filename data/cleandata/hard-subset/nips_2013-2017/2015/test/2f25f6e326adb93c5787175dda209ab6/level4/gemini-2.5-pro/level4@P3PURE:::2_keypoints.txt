- Introduces novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, applied to bounding DPP likelihood.  
- Core idea uses pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels—simple yet powerful.  
- Paper is well-executed and enlightening, enabling several potential improvements.  
- The problem is timely and of interest to the community.  
- Approach is general and could apply beyond DPPs.  
- Experimental evaluation is thoughtful and highlights when standard variational approximations fail.  
- Choosing DPPs as the application is relevant and appropriate.  
- Minor concern: emphasis on parameterizing K (vs Cholesky L) is prominent but the paper describes the problem more than resolves it, creating a slight disconnect.  
- Suggestion: provide a standard supplement rather than uploading an extended paper if accepted.  
- Future work: consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of K and evaluate scalability when combined with the pseudo-input method.
