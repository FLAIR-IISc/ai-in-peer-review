- Introduces a novel method for learning kernel parameters in DPPs that tackles the intractable normalization constant.
- Proposes an elegant partition-function bound that avoids eigenvalue estimation, enabling efficient MCMC and variational learning.
- Central technical contribution is a new inequality (Proposition 1) giving a lower bound; it's sophisticated and well-suited to the problem.
- Connects DPP inference to Gaussian process inducing-point methods, offering a valuable perspective for scalable inference.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and represents a significant contribution.
- Claim that replacing power iterations with cardinality-increasing nonlinear optimization is a “huge computational win” is unconvincing; need concrete computational comparisons.
- Convergence of bounds as cardinality increases is not guaranteed due to non-convexity; local minima may prevent bracketing the partition function needed for valid MCMC.
- Authors should explain how global optimality is achieved or how local minima are mitigated (e.g., restarts or convex relaxations).
- Proof of Proposition 1 should be moved from the appendix into the main body.
- Presentation fixes: add axis labels to all subfigures in Figure 1 (page 7) and correct typo on page 7, line 361 ("the variational lower is" → "the variational lower bound is").
- With the requested clarifications and fixes, the work would be a strong contribution to the conference.
