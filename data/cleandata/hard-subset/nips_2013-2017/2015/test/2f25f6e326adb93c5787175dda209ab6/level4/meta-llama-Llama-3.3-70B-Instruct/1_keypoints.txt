- DPPs model repulsion via a kernel whose parameter learning is hard because the partition function sums over all point configurations and cardinalities.
- Prior work estimated the spectrum to build incremental eigenvalue-based bounds enabling retrospective MCMC for kernel parameter inference.
- This paper proposes a novel inequality that yields lower bounds avoiding eigenvalue estimation, enabling MCMC without spectrum computation.
- The method also supports a variational learning approach and connects to inducing-point techniques from scalable Gaussian process inference.
- The paper is technically sound, well-written, and presents a fitting novel bound in Proposition 1.
- Concern: Replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimension may not offer practical computational savings.
- Concern: Increasing cardinality may not guarantee convergence of bounds because the coupled optimization can be highly non-convex and may require global minima.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels, the proof of the proposition is placed in the appendix instead of the main text, and a phrasing on line 361 needs clarification.
- Overall assessment: Clever approach with potential for faster inference and useful model connections, but technical and presentation issues should be addressed.
