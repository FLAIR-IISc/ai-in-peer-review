- Presents novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant with a compelling application to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs).
- Core idea is conceptually straightforward but carefully and insightfully executed, opening avenues for future research.
- Cleverly leverages pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels; a timely contribution for ML.
- Proposed approach is general and likely applicable beyond DPPs, broadening impact.
- Experimental evaluation is well-designed and effectively illustrates key findings, including limitations of a variational approximation under certain conditions.
- Ambiguity in emphasizing parameterizing the kernel matrix K (vs. L, its Cholesky decomposition); motivation is unclear and not resolved.
- Recommend clarifying the motivation for focusing on K and discussing interpretability of the K parameterization.
- Suggest exploring direct determinant bounds expressed in terms of K as a valuable extension.
- Recommend assessing scalability of the pseudo-input method for larger problem instances to evaluate practical utility.
- If accepted, prepare a dedicated supplementary-material document per standard conference guidelines rather than uploading an extended manuscript.
