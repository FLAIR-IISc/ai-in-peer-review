- Addresses learning kernel parameters of DPPs where the partition function is intractable.
- Introduces a novel inequality (Proposition 1) that gives a tight lower bound on the DPP partition function.
- The new bound avoids spectral/eigenvalue estimation used in prior work [7].
- Enables an exact retrospective MCMC inference scheme without spectral estimation.
- Extends the bounding framework to a variational inference method for scalable DPP learning.
- Draws insightful connections to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian Processes.
- Paper is technically strong, well-written, and the contributions are significant and well-motivated.
- Concern: unclear computational trade-offs—nonlinear optimization sequence may not always be faster than power iterations; needs empirical/runtime comparison.
- Concern: convergence and non-convexity—optimizing inducing points could get stuck in local minima, potentially stalling the MCMC sampler; request discussion/empirical or theoretical evidence and initialization strategies.
- Presentation issue: add axis labels to all plots in Figure 1 (Page 7).
- Presentation issue: move a proof sketch or key steps of Proposition 1 into the main paper for visibility.
- Presentation issue: fix typo on Page 7, Line 361 ("the variational lower is...").
- Overall recommendation: strong paper and recommend acceptance if the authors satisfactorily address the technical concerns.
