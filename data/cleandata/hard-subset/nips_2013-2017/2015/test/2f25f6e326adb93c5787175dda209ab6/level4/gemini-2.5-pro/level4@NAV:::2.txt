This paper introduces novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant, which are then applied to the problem of approximating the likelihood of a determinantal point process. While the core concept is conceptually straightforward, its execution is rigorous and insightful, opening promising avenues for future enhancements.

The work presents an innovative application of pseudo-inputs for deriving bounds on functionals of positive definite kernels. The problem addressed is highly relevant and is poised to attract considerable interest within the research community.

This methodology is broadly applicable, with clear potential for use in domains beyond determinantal point processes. The experiments are thoughtfully designed to demonstrate key properties of the proposed bounds, including insightful examples where a standard variational approximation fails. The choice of application is compelling and serves as an excellent testbed for the problem under investigation.

Minor comments:
- The emphasis on parameterizing the kernel matrix K (as opposed to its Cholesky factor L) in the abstract and introduction is somewhat confusing, given that the paper describes this as a challenge but does not offer a direct solution.
- Should the paper be accepted, it is recommended that the authors provide a formally structured supplement rather than simply submitting a longer version of the manuscript.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion regarding the interpretability of a direct K parameterization is compelling. A valuable direction for future research would be to develop bounds on the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix itself. It would be particularly interesting to analyze the scalability of such a method, especially when combined with the proposed pseudo-input technique.
