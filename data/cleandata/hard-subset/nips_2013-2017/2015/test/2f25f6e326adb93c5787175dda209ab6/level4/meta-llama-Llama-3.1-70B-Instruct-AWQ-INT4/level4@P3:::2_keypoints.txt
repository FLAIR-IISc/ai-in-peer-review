- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants to bound DPP likelihood without spectral knowledge.  
- Main idea is simple but well executed and paves the way for improvements.  
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels; approach is general and may apply beyond DPPs.  
- Experimental evaluation is well constructed, elucidating key points and showing failures of a variational approximation under some conditions.  
- Thorough literature review situating the contribution within existing DPP work.  
- Technical aspects are sound and the proposed bounds are well justified.  
- Good balance between theoretical and experimental results; experiments provide valuable insights.  
- Minor critique: prominent emphasis on parameterizing K (instead of L) is not addressed or resolved in the paper.  
- Suggestion to provide a standard supplement (not just an extended paper) to keep the main paper concise.  
- Future work suggested: derive bounds directly in terms of K and investigate scalability of the pseudo-input method.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with high scores (Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, broad applicability, balanced theory and experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of treatment of K vs L parameterization and limited discussion of method limitations.
