- DPPs model repulsion via a PD kernel but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.
- Prior work [7] estimates the spectrum to get incremental bounds and enables retrospective MCMC for kernel parameter inference.
- The paper proposes an alternative bounding strategy that avoids eigenvalue estimation and supports MCMC.
- The proposed approach naturally extends to variational inference and is connected to inducing-point methods, suggesting scalable GP inference.
- The method is technically strong and well-written; Proposition 1’s inequality is a clever and useful contribution.
- The connections to GP inducing points and the potential for variational inference are particularly appreciated.
- Concern: unclear whether replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimension provides a real computational advantage.
- Concern: increasing cardinality may not guarantee convergence of bounds because the coupled optimization is likely highly non-convex and may get stuck in local minima.
- Risk: local minima could prevent the procedure from bounding away the Bernoulli threshold and thus block MCMC moves.
- Presentation: Figure 1 needs axis labels for clarity.
