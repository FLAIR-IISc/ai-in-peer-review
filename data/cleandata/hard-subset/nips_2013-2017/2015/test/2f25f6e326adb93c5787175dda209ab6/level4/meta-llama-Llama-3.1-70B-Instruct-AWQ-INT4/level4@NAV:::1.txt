This paper addresses the challenge of learning determinantal point process (DPP) kernel parameters from example point realizations, which is hindered by the intractable normalization constant that sums over all possible cardinalities and point configurations.
A recent approach [7] tackled this issue by estimating the spectrum and using it to derive incremental upper and lower bounds for Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference.
This work proposes an alternative bounding method that does not require eigenvalue estimation and enables both MCMC and variational inference, drawing connections to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian process inference.
The paper is technically sound and well-written, with a novel application of the inequality in Proposition 1 that effectively avoids the estimation issues of [7].
The connections to Gaussian process inducing points and the potential for variational inference are particularly noteworthy.

Second, the coupled optimization problem raises concerns about the convergence of the bounds with increasing cardinality, particularly given the potential non-convexity of the optimization problem.
Ensuring that a global minimum is achieved is crucial to prevent local minima from hindering the bounding procedure and MCMC inference.
