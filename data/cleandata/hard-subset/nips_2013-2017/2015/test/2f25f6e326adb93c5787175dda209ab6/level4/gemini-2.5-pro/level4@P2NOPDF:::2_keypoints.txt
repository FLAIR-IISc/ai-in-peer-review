- Introduces novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinant with a direct application to bounding DPP likelihoods.  
- Central idea uses pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels; elegant and simple.  
- Paper is well-executed, provides valuable insights, and opens multiple future research directions.  
- Problem is timely and relevant; methods likely of wide interest in machine learning.  
- Bounding technique is general and potentially applicable beyond DPPs.  
- Experimental evaluation is thoughtful and elucidates key properties of the proposed bounds.  
- Includes useful analysis showing when standard variational approximations can fail, highlighting practical utility.  
- Minor comment: excessive emphasis on the difficulty of parameterizing K vs its Cholesky L may misframe the core contribution, which operates on L.  
- Minor comment: authors should prepare a standard, self-contained supplement rather than an extended main paper.  
- Future work suggestion: extend pseudo-input methodology to derive bounds that operate directly on K and analyze scalability and tightness.
