- Introduces a novel inequality (Proposition 1) that gives a lower bound on the DPP partition function, avoiding eigenvalue estimation and enabling alternative MCMC and variational inference approaches with a connection to inducing-point GP methods.

- Paper is technically strong and well written; central contribution (Proposition 1) is insightful and valuable.

- Concern 1: Claim of computational advantage is unclear—avoiding power iterations is replaced by a sequence of growing non-linear optimization problems; need detailed complexity and convergence comparison.

- Concern 2: Convergence of the bounds is questionable because the tightening relies on solving non-convex, growing-dimensional optimizations; poor local minima could prevent sufficient tightening and stall the retrospective MCMC—requires discussion or guarantees.

- Minor: Figure 1 (Page 7) is missing axis labels.

- Minor: Proof of Proposition 1 is central and a proof sketch or key steps should be moved into the main text.
