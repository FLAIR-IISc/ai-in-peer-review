- DPPs model repulsion via a kernel but parameter learning is hard due to an intractable normalization constant summing over cardinalities and configurations.
- Authors propose novel lower and upper bounds on the DPP likelihood that avoid eigenvalue estimation, improving computational efficiency.
- Bounds are inspired by inducing-variable sparse Gaussian process approximations and connect DPP inference to scalable GP methods.
- The bounds can be used within variational inference and MCMC frameworks.
- Paper is technically strong, well-written, and the connection to inducing-point methods is noteworthy.
- The lower bound uses a clever inequality (Proposition 1) that sidesteps prior estimation issues.
- Concern: unclear whether replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension is actually a computational win.
- Concern: the coupled optimization is likely highly non-convex, and increasing bound cardinality may not guarantee convergence.
- Reviewer asks for direct discussion and more insight into the optimization process and convergence behavior.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels.
- Presentation issue: the central proposition proof is relegated to the supplement and should be in the main paper.
- Overall assessment: novel and valuable contribution, but acceptance concerns remain due to computational/optimization clarity and presentation fixes.
