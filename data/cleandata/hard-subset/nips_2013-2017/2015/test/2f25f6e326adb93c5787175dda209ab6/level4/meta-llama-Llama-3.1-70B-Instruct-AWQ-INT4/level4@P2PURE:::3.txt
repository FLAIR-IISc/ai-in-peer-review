The paper proposes approximate inference techniques for determinantal point processes (DPPs) that do not require spectral knowledge of the kernel defining the DPP. Instead, inducing inputs are utilized to bound the likelihood, which reduces the computational cost. Both variational inference and approximate Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) techniques are described, and their efficiency is demonstrated empirically.

I believe there is merit to this paper; however, I found the method description to be somewhat rough, and the empirical evaluation could be more thorough. To clarify, I would like to raise the following points:

1. A comparison of the computational complexity and overall computation time of the two proposed methods would be beneficial. How do they compare in terms of efficiency?

2. It would be helpful to compare the proposed methods to other alternatives, both approximate and exact, in terms of the quality of approximation and computation time. This would provide a clearer understanding of their strengths and limitations.

3. The empirical results on the toy data in Section 5.1 show that the parameter estimation error using variational inference is too large, and the authors resort to approximate MCMC instead. Could the authors elaborate on the expected settings in which the proposed variational inference is likely to work and when it is expected to fail? What are the underlying reasons for this?

4. A 13-point sample is generated for the toy example. I would like to see an analysis of how the methods perform when the inducing inputs are actually these sampled points.

5. Are there any rules of thumb for selecting a) the number of inducing points and b) the placement of the initial inducing points? Providing guidance on these choices would enhance the practical applicability of the proposed methods.

Minor comments:

The matrices K and L are referred to throughout the introduction without explanation. Briefly mentioning the marginal kernel and L-ensembles may make it easier for an uninformed reader to follow.

The discussion about the advantages of directly learning the marginal kernel parameters is repeated throughout the paper, but this approach is not taken. Some of this content could be removed, as it does not contribute to the rest of the paper.

If accepted, I strongly encourage the authors to improve the write-up, as the current method description and empirical evaluation could be more detailed and thorough.
