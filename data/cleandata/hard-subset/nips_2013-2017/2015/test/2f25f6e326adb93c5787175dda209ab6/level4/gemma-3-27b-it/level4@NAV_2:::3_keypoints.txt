- Paper proposes approximate DPP inference using inducing inputs to bound likelihoods, avoiding spectral decomposition.  
- Presents both variational inference and MCMC approaches, evaluated empirically.  
- Addresses an important computational bottleneck, potentially enabling DPPs on larger and continuous domains.  
- Connection to variational inference in Gaussian processes is well-motivated and provides useful intuition.  
- Methodological presentation lacks clarity and sufficient detail in places.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and needs stronger benchmarking against existing DPP inference algorithms.  
- Missing detailed comparison of computational complexity and runtime between proposed variational and MCMC methods.  
- Variational inference shows substantial parameter estimation error in experiments, prompting a switch to MCMC.  
- Paper needs a deeper discussion of when variational inference is expected to succeed or fail and why.  
- Should evaluate performance when inducing inputs are initialized as data points (e.g., in the toy example).  
- Practical guidelines for choosing the number and placement of inducing points are absent and necessary.  
- Minor clarity issues: brief explanation of marginal kernel K and L-ensembles is needed for accessibility.  
- Discussion about learning marginal kernel parameters is somewhat tangential and should be streamlined.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant problem, provides novel bounds, offers viable inference methods, and shows feasibility on synthetic and real data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear methodology, limited empirical benchmarking, unclear conditions for variational success, and missing practical guidance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions.
