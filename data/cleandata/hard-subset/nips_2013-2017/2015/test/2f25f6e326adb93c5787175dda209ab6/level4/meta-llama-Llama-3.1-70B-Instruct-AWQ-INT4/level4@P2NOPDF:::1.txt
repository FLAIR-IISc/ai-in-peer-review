Determinantal point processes (DPPs) have garnered significant attention in the machine learning literature due to their ability to model repulsion within realizations of a set of points, specified via a positive definite kernel function. However, learning the appropriate parameters of this kernel from example point realizations poses a challenge, primarily due to the difficult-to-compute normalization constant that sums over all possible cardinalities and point configurations.

A recent proposal [7] addressed this issue by estimating the spectrum and using incremental upper and lower bounds to create a provably-correct scheme for "retrospective" Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) of the kernel parameters. The present paper proposes an alternative type of bound that can be used for MCMC inference without requiring eigenvalue estimation. This approach lends itself to a variational method for learning and draws connections to inducing-point methods for scalable inference in Gaussian process models.

The paper is technically strong and well-written, and the inequality providing the lower bound in Proposition 1 is an excellent fit for this problem. It cleverly avoids the estimation issues present in [7]. The connections to Gaussian process inducing points and the ability to perform variational inference are also notable strengths.

However, two technical concerns need to be addressed. Firstly, while the proposed approach is appealing, it is unclear whether replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization problems of increasing dimension results in significant computational savings. Secondly, it is not immediately apparent that simply increasing the cardinality guarantees convergence of the bounds, particularly considering the coupled optimization problem. This optimization problem appears to be highly non-convex, and increasing the cardinality may not ensure achievement of a global minimum. This could potentially prevent the procedure from bounding away the Bernoulli threshold and making it impossible to take an MCMC step.

Regarding presentation, a few minor issues were noted. Firstly, axis labels should be included for the figures, such as Figure 1 on page 7. Additionally, the proof of the proposition, which is the central insight of the paper, would be better placed in the main body rather than the appendix. Finally, a minor typo was found on page 7, line 361, where the phrase "the variational lower is" could be rephrased for clarity.

Overall, the paper presents a clever trick for bounding the partition function of a DPP, leading to potentially faster inference and connections to other models.
