- Paper proposes approximate inference for DPPs using inducing inputs to bound the likelihood, with variational and approximate MCMC schemes and empirical efficiency claims.  
- Reviewer finds method descriptions unclear and the empirical evaluation insufficiently thorough.  
- Request (Q1): Provide a direct comparison of computational complexity and overall runtime between the variational and MCMC methods.  
- Request (Q2): Compare the proposed methods to other approximate and exact alternatives in both approximation quality and computation time.  
- Request (Q3): Explain when variational inference is expected to perform well or fail (toy example showed large parameter error) and why.  
- Request (Q4): Report performance when inducing inputs are chosen to be the toy example’s 13 sampled points.  
- Request (Q5): Provide guidelines or rules of thumb for selecting the number of inducing points and their initial placement.  
- Minor: Matrices K and L are referenced without prior definition; briefly introduce marginal kernel and L-ensembles for readability.  
- Minor: Reduce discussion of directly learning marginal-kernel parameters since that approach is not pursued.  
- Recommendation: Improve the write-up and clarify method descriptions if the paper is accepted.
