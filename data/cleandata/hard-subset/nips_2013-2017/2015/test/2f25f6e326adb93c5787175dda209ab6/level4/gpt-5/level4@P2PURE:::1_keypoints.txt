- Context: DPPs model repulsion via a PD kernel; learning kernel parameters is hard because the partition function sums over all cardinalities and configurations.
- Prior work: Recent method [7] estimates the spectrum to build incremental bounds and enables a “retrospective” MCMC for kernel inference.
- Contribution: Proposes a new class of bounds that enable MCMC inference without estimating eigenvalues.
- Contribution: Method also supports variational learning and connects to inducing-point techniques from scalable GP models.
- Strength: Paper is technically strong, well written, and applies a clever inequality (Proposition 1) to avoid spectrum estimation.
- Strength: The inequality underpinning the lower bound is novel in this context and is a key insight.
- Concern (computational): Not convinced replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization (whose dimension grows) is a clear computational win.
- Concern (convergence): Increasing cardinality may not guarantee convergence of bounds due to coupled, highly non-convex optimization and possible local minima blocking MCMC decisions.
- Presentation: Figure 1 (P7) needs axis labels.
- Presentation: Proof of the key proposition should be moved from the appendix into the main text.
- Presentation: Typo on P7, L361 (“… the variational lower is …”).
- Overall: Strong paper offering a clever bound for the DPP partition function, potentially enabling faster inference and broader modeling connections.
