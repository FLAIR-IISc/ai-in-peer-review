- Introduces novel upper and lower determinant bounds that enable practical bounds on determinantal point process (DPP) likelihoods.
- Central idea is straightforward; execution is careful, illuminating, and opens avenues for promising refinements.
- Inventive use of pseudo/inducing inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels is a notable contribution.
- Addresses a timely problem of broad interest to the community.
- Approach appears general and may have utility beyond DPPs.
- Experimental section is thoughtfully designed and reveals key insights, including cases where variational approximations fail.
- Chosen application area is engaging and well aligned with the technical contributions.
- Minor concern: prominence of the K-versus-L parameterization discussion is confusing since the paper frames the issue but does not resolve it.
- Minor request: if accepted, supply a standard supplementary document rather than an extended paper as the supplement.
- Suggested future work: explore determinant bounds stated directly in terms of K and study scalability when using pseudo-inputs.
- Overall: presents novel two-sided determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods with a simple central idea, strong presentation, and clear potential for extensions.
