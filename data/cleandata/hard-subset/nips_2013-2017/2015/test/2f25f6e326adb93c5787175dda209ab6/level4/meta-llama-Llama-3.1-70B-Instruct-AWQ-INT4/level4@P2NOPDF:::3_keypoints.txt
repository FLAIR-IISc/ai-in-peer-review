- Paper proposes approximate inference for DPPs using inducing inputs to avoid spectral knowledge, with variational and approximate MCMC methods and empirical demonstrations.
- Reviewer finds the paper meritorious but with a rough method description and insufficient empirical evaluation.
- Request a comparison of computational complexity and wall-clock compute time between the variational and MCMC methods to clarify trade-offs.
- Request comparisons to other approximate and exact alternatives in both approximation quality and computation time.
- Ask for discussion of when variational inference succeeds or fails (given observed large parameter-estimation error on toy data) and explanation of underlying reasons.
- Suggest evaluating the case where inducing inputs are set to the actual sampled points in the toy example (13-point sample).
- Request a practical rule of thumb for choosing the number and initial placement of inducing points.
- Minor: Introduce/define matrices K and L (marginal kernel and L-ensembles) for reader accessibility.
- Minor: Remove or reduce discussion on directly learning marginal kernel parameters since that approach is not pursued in the paper.
- Overall recommendation: paper has potential but needs improved method description and stronger empirical evaluation; revise accordingly.
