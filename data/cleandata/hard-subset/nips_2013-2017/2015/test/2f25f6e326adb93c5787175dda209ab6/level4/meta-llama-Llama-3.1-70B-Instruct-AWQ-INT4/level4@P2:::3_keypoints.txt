- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate nonspectral bounds on determinants in DPP likelihoods for both finite and continuous cases.  
- Bounds avoid spectral decomposition and are computationally cheaper than spectrum-based approaches.  
- Demonstrates use of bounds for parameter inference via expensive-but-exact MCMC and cheap-but-approximate variational inference.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a thorough literature review and clear explanation of the inference problem without spectral knowledge.  
- Main contribution: derive likelihood bounds that do not depend on the spectral decomposition of operator L.  
- Method description is rather rough and needs clearer exposition.  
- Empirical evaluation is not thorough enough and lacks detailed comparisons.  
- Asks for comparison of computational complexity and total compute time between the proposed methods.  
- Requests comparison to other approximate and exact alternatives in terms of approximation quality and runtime.  
- Notes variational inference shows large parameter estimation error on toy data; asks when variational is expected to succeed or fail and why.  
- Questions performance when inducing inputs are the actual sampled points used in the toy example.  
- Requests rule-of-thumb guidance for selecting number and initialization/placement of inducing points.  
- Minor: matrices K and L are referenced without explanation; suggest briefly defining marginal kernel and L-ensembles.  
- Minor: discussion of learning marginal kernel parameters is unnecessary since that approach is not pursued in the paper.  
- Strengths: novel nonspectral inference approach, thorough literature review, clear motivation.  
- Weaknesses: rough method description, insufficient empirical evaluation, lacks detailed comparisons to alternatives.  
- Originality: presents new nonspectral likelihood bounds for DPPs.  
- Writing: generally well-written but needs clearer method description and explanation of K and L.  
- Impact: potential for significant impact across ML and statistics applications using DPPs.  
- Recommendation: reviewer would accept but strongly urges improvements to write-up and addressing raised points.
