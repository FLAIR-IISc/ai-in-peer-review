This paper presents novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant, with a compelling application to bounding the likelihood function of determinantal point processes (DPPs). The core idea is conceptually straightforward, yet the paper demonstrates a careful and insightful execution, opening avenues for future research.

The authors cleverly leverage pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. This is a timely contribution likely to be of significant interest to the machine learning community. The generality of the proposed approach suggests potential applicability beyond DPPs, broadening its impact. 

The experimental evaluation is well-designed and effectively illustrates key findings, including limitations of a variational approximation under specific conditions. I found the chosen application area to be both interesting and highly relevant to the theoretical problem being addressed. 

However, a point of ambiguity arises regarding the prominence given to parameterizing the kernel matrix *K* (rather than *L*, its Cholesky decomposition) in the abstract and throughout the paper. While the authors clearly define the problem, they do not attempt to resolve this parameterization issue. Clarifying the motivation behind focusing on *K* would strengthen the work.

Should this paper be accepted, I recommend that the authors prepare a dedicated supplementary material document, adhering to standard conference guidelines, rather than simply uploading an extended version of the manuscript.

Looking forward, the discussion surrounding the interpretability of the *K* parameterization hints at further potential. Exploring the possibility of directly bounding the determinant in terms of the *K* matrix could be a valuable extension. Furthermore, assessing the scalability of the pseudo-input method—particularly for larger problem instances—would provide a more complete understanding of its practical utility.
