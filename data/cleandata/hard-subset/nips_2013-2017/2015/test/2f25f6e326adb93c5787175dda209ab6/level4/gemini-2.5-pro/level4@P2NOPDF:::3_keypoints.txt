- Introduces VI and MCMC approximate inference for DPPs using inducing inputs to avoid spectral decomposition and improve scalability.
- Promising core idea but manuscript lacks methodological clarity and sufficient empirical validation.
- Missing direct comparison between VI and MCMC, including computational complexity and wall-clock runtime trade-offs.
- Experimental evaluation omits comparisons with state-of-the-art approximate and exact DPP inference baselines, including quality vs runtime metrics.
- VI shows a significant failure on the toy dataset; the paper needs deeper analysis of why VI fails and when it can be expected to succeed.
- Sensitivity to the placement of inducing points is unclear; experiments (e.g., using sampled data points as inducing points) are needed.
- No practical guidance or heuristics for choosing the number and initialization of inducing points; an ablation on these hyperparameters is requested.
- Minor: matrices K and L are not defined on first mention and should be briefly introduced for clarity.
- Minor: discussions on learning the marginal kernel feel extraneous since that approach is not followed and could be condensed or removed.
- Overall: promising direction but requires clearer exposition and a far more comprehensive experimental validation to be convincing.
