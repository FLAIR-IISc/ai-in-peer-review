- Novel method for learning DPP parameters that avoids requiring spectral (eigenvalue) knowledge.  
- Derives nonspectral bounds on the likelihood via a nontrivial determinant inequality.  
- Applies the approach to both finite and continuous DPPs with demonstrated effectiveness.  
- Provides clear, concise introduction to DPPs, their properties, and inference challenges.  
- Contrasts with prior work ([7]) by offering bounds usable for MCMC without spectrum estimation.  
- Connects the method to inducing-point techniques for scalable Gaussian process inference.  
- Paper is well-written and explains advantages clearly.  
- Experimental results, including a real-world dataset, show the method’s potential.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity and comparisons to existing approaches.  
- Optimization is highly non-convex; more insight is needed on ensuring convergence to a global minimum.  
- Suggests moving the central proposition’s proof into the main paper for clarity and emphasis.
