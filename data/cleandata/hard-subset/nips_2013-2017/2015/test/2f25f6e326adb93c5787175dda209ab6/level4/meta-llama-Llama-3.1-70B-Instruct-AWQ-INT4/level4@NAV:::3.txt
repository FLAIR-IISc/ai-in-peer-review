The paper introduces approximate inference methods for determinantal point processes (DPPs) that bypass the need for spectral knowledge of the defining kernel. By leveraging inducing inputs to bound the likelihood, the authors successfully reduce computational costs. The manuscript presents both variational inference and approximate Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) techniques, demonstrating their efficiency through empirical evaluations.

Despite the paper's potential, I found the method descriptions to be somewhat lacking in clarity and the empirical assessment to be insufficient. To further strengthen the manuscript, I recommend addressing the following points:

1. A detailed comparison of the computational complexity and overall computation time between the two proposed methods would be beneficial.
2. It would be helpful to compare the proposed methods to existing alternatives, including both approximate and exact techniques, in terms of approximation quality and computation time.
3. The empirical results in Section 5.1 indicate that variational inference yields large parameter estimation errors, necessitating the use of approximate MCMC. The authors should discuss the expected performance and limitations of the proposed variational inference method.
4. The toy example in Section 5.1 utilizes a 13-point sample. I suggest evaluating the methods' performance when the inducing inputs are these sampled points.
5. Providing guidelines for selecting the number of inducing points and their initial placement would enhance the practicality of the proposed approach.

Minor suggestions:

* Briefly introducing the marginal kernel and L-ensembles in the introduction would improve the manuscript's accessibility for unfamiliar readers.
* The discussion on directly learning marginal kernel parameters could be condensed, as it does not significantly contribute to the paper's core content.
* If accepted, I strongly encourage the authors to refine the writing to improve clarity and thoroughness.
