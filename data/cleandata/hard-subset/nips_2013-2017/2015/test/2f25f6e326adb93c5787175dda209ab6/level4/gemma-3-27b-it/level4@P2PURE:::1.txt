Determinantal point processes (DPPs) have recently gained significant attention in machine learning due to their ability to model repulsive interactions between point realizations, governed by a positive definite kernel function. A key challenge lies in learning the kernel parameters from observed point realizations, a task complicated by a computationally intractable normalization constant that requires summation over all possible cardinalities and configurations. A recent approach [7] addresses this by estimating the kernel spectrum and deriving incremental upper and lower bounds, enabling provably-correct retrospective Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) for kernel parameter estimation. This paper presents an alternative bounding strategy for MCMC inference that avoids explicit eigenvalue estimation. Furthermore, this approach naturally extends to variational inference and establishes connections with inducing-point methods for scalable inference in Gaussian process models. 

This is a technically sound and well-written contribution. Proposition 1’s underlying inequality was previously unknown to me and appears ideally suited to this problem, offering a clever alternative to the estimation challenges posed by [7]. The connections to Gaussian process inducing points and the possibility of variational inference are particularly appealing. Overall, this paper is a pleasure to read.

However, I have two technical concerns. First, while I appreciate this approach, I am not convinced that replacing power iterations with potentially costly nonlinear optimization problems of increasing dimensionality constitutes a substantial computational improvement. Second, the convergence of the bounds achieved through simply increasing the cardinality is not immediately clear, given the coupled nature of the optimization problem. This optimization appears highly non-convex, and increasing cardinality alone may not guarantee convergence to a global minimum. This point requires further discussion, as a local minimum could prevent effective bounding of the Bernoulli threshold and hinder MCMC step acceptance.
