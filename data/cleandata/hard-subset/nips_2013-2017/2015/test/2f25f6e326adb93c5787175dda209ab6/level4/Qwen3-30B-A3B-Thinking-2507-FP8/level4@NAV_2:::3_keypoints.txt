- Paper introduces inducing-input-based approximate inference for DPPs that avoids spectral kernel knowledge, presenting variational inference and approximate MCMC with empirical efficiency claims.
- Methodological exposition is somewhat superficial and needs more depth.
- Empirical validation is not comprehensive enough to fully support the claims.
- Provide a detailed comparison of computational complexity and actual runtime between the variational inference and approximate MCMC approaches.
- Compare thoroughly with existing alternatives (approximate and exact) on approximation quality and computational efficiency.
- Clarify the conditions under which variational inference yields good parameter estimates versus when it fails, and explain why (motivated by errors in Section 5.1).
- Test the toy example when inducing inputs coincide with the 13 sampled points to assess performance implications.
- Give practical guidance on selecting the number of inducing points and their initial placement for practitioners.
- Define matrices K and L and briefly explain marginal kernels and L-ensembles to improve accessibility.
- Condense the extended discussion on directly learning marginal kernel parameters since the paper ultimately uses the L-parametrization.
