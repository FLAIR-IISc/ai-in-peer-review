The authors have proposed new upper and lower bounds on the determinant of matrices, which will be useful in limiting the potential of the determinant-point approximation. The core idea is simple, but the paper is well-written and instructive, and it paves the way for several potential improvements.

The authors have proposed a creative use of pseudo-inputs to limit the functional of positive definite kernels. The proposed problem is timely and will be of interest to many in the community.

Such an approach is quite general with potential applications beyond determinant-point approximations. The experimental evaluation is well-designed to clarify several key points, including the failure of variational approximations under specific conditions. I found the selected application area to be interesting and highly relevant to the problem being studied.

Small comments:
- It is unclear why the parameterization of K (instead of L) is so abstract and prominently featured in the rest of the paper, as the authors are primarily focused on describing the problem and have made no attempt to solve it.
- If accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement, and not just upload an extended version of the paper as a supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- On the other hand, a discussion of the interpretability of K parameterization makes such an approach more appealing. Could the authors consider directly constraining the determinant in terms of K matrices? What is the measurability of such an approach using the pseudo-input method?
