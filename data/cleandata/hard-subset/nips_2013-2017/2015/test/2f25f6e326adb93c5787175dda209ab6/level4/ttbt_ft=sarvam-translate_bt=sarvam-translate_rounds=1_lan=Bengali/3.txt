This paper proposes an approximate estimation technique for Determinantal Point Process (DPP), where spectral knowledge of the kernel is not required to define DPP. Instead, inducing inputs are used to constrain probabilities, which reduces computational cost. Both variational inference and approximate Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) techniques are described, and their effectiveness has been experimentally demonstrated.

I believe this paper has some merit, but I found the description of the method to be quite vague and the empirical evaluation not sufficiently thorough. A few points might help clarify:

1) How can the computational complexity and overall computing time of the two proposed methods be compared?
2) How do the proposed methods compare in terms of approximation and computing time compared to both approximate and exact alternatives?
3) Experimental results on toy data of section 5.1 show that the error in parameter estimation using variational inference is very large, and approximate MCMC is instead used. Under what circumstances is the proposed variational inference expected to work, and when is it expected to fail? Why?
4) A 13-point sample is created for the toy example. How do the methods perform when the inducing inputs are these sample points?
5) Are there any general rules to follow in setting the number of inducing points and the initial inducing point?

Small comments:
The entire introduction mentions matrices K and L, but they are not explained. A brief mention of the marginal kernel and L-ensemble might make it easier for an inexperienced reader to follow.

The entire paper discusses the advantages of directly learning the parameters of the marginal kernel, but this is not the method that is taken. I think some of this can be omitted, as it does not contribute to the rest of the paper.
