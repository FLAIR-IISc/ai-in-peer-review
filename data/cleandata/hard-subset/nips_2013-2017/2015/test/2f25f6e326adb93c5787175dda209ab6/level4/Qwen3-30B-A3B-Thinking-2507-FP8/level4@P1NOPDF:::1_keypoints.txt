- Paper addresses kernel learning for DPPs by proposing a new bound that enables MCMC inference without eigenvalue estimation.  
- Bound also supports a variational learning approach and connects DPP inference to GP inducing-point methods for scalability.  
- Technical contributions are strong and well-executed; Proposition 1 provides a novel, effective lower-bound inequality.  
- The linkage to inducing points and extension to variational inference are insightful and valuable.  
- Concern 1: The computational advantage over prior spectral methods ([7]) is unclear—need explicit complexity comparison or empirical speedup evidence.  
- Concern 2: Convergence via increasing cardinality may be jeopardized by non-convex, coupled optimization and local minima; authors should justify global convergence or propose safeguards.  
- Presentation note: Add axis labels to Figure 1 for clarity.  
- Presentation note: Move the Proposition 1 proof from the appendix into the main body.  
- Presentation note: Fix typo on Page 7, line 361 (“the variational lower is” → “the variational lower bound is”).  
- Overall assessment: High-quality contribution that advances DPP inference, contingent on addressing the two technical concerns.
