- DPPs model repulsion via a positive definite kernel; learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.
- Prior work [7] estimates the spectrum to build incremental bounds and a retrospective MCMC for kernel parameters.
- This paper proposes new bounds that avoid eigenvalue estimation while still supporting MCMC inference.
- The method also enables a variational learning framework and connects to inducing-point methods from scalable Gaussian process inference.
- The paper is technically solid, well-written, and uses a fitting inequality (Proposition 1) to sidestep spectrum estimation.
- Proposition 1’s inequality is a neat, central contribution that strengthens the paper’s approach.
- Concern: replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension may not yield a clear computational advantage.
- Concern: increasing cardinality may not guarantee bound convergence due to coupled, likely highly non-convex optimization and risk of poor local minima blocking MCMC transitions.
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 (P7) needs axis labels.
- Presentation issue: the proof of the main proposition should be in the main body rather than the appendix/index.
- Presentation issue: fix typo/phrase at P7 L361 ("... the variational lower is ...").
- Overall: strong paper offering a clever device to bound the DPP partition function, potentially enabling faster inference and useful connections to other frameworks.
