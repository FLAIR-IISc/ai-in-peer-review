- Methodological description is rough and needs clearer, more complete exposition.
- Empirical evaluation is insufficiently thorough to support claims.
- Need theoretical and empirical comparison of computational complexity and wall-clock time between the VI and approximate MCMC methods.
- Require comparisons against existing exact and approximate DPP inference methods in terms of approximation quality and runtime.
- Clarify when VI succeeds versus fails (toy experiment shows large parameter error and fallback to MCMC) and explain underlying reasons.
- For the toy example, report performance when inducing inputs are set exactly to the sampled points.
- Provide practical rules of thumb for choosing (a) number of inducing points and (b) inducing point initialization/placement.
