- Introduces kernel-free DPP inference using inducing inputs and proposes variational inference and approximate MCMC; idea is promising and empirically validated.  
- Method description lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Computational complexity and runtime trade-offs between variational and MCMC approaches are not reported.  
- No comparative time/space analysis, especially as a function of number of inducing points.  
- Empirical evaluation omits benchmarks against relevant alternatives (e.g., greedy sampling, Nyström) and exact DPP where feasible.  
- Missing quantitative reporting of approximation quality (e.g., log-likelihood, diversity metrics) and computational efficiency.  
- Variational inference fails on the toy example (large parameter errors) and the paper does not analyze when or why it succeeds or fails.  
- No theoretical or empirical characterization of conditions under which variational inference is valid or its failure modes.  
- Sensitivity to inducing point selection is untested (e.g., using sampled points as inducing inputs).  
- No practical guidance on hyperparameters: how many inducing points to use and how to initialize them (k-means, random, kernel-based).  
- Minor issues: matrices K and L are referenced but not defined.  
- Claim about directly learning marginal kernel parameters is emphasized but not implemented, creating a disconnect.  
- Overall assessment: lacks technical depth, empirical rigor, and implementation guidance; recommend rejection pending substantial revision.
