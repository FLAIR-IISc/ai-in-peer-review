- Proposes novel upper and lower non-spectral bounds on matrix determinants to bound DPP likelihoods, avoiding explicit spectral decomposition.  
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels, inspired by variational GP inference.  
- Extension of the approach to continuous domains is well-presented and broadens applicability.  
- Method is general and likely applicable beyond DPPs, opening avenues for further improvements.  
- Technical contributions appear sound with proofs referenced in supplementary material.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and situates the work within existing methods (variational inference, MCMC).  
- Experimental evaluation is thoughtfully designed and shows limitations of a variational approximation under certain conditions.  
- Application area (modeling diversity) is timely and relevant to the community.  
- Insufficient treatment of the K versus L parameterization issue—raised repeatedly but no proposed solutions.  
- Supplementary material plan is suboptimal; authors should provide a standard supplement with detailed proofs and extra experiments rather than an extended duplicate manuscript.  
- Experiments could be deeper: lacking comprehensive comparisons to spectral DPP inference methods and quantitative cost vs. accuracy analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant DPP inference bottleneck, novel and sound bounds, practical potential, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no solution for K parameterization, inadequate supplement format, and limited experimental comprehensiveness.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revision, requesting a proper supplement and additional experimental analysis.
