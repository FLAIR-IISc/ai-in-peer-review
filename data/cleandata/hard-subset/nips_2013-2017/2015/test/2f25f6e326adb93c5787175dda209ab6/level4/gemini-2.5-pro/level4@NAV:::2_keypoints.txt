- Introduces novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant and applies them to approximating determinantal point process likelihoods.
- Core idea is conceptually straightforward but executed rigorously and insightfully.
- Presents an innovative use of pseudo-inputs to derive bounds on functionals of positive definite kernels.
- Addresses a highly relevant problem likely to attract substantial interest in the community.
- Methodology is broadly applicable and has potential beyond determinantal point processes.
- Experiments are well designed, demonstrating key properties of the bounds and showcasing cases where standard variational approximations fail.
- The chosen application (DPPs) is compelling and serves as a strong testbed for the method.
- Minor critique: emphasis on parameterizing K (vs its Cholesky factor L) in abstract/introduction is confusing since no direct solution is provided.
- Recommendation: if accepted, authors should submit a formally structured supplement rather than an extended manuscript.
- Future work suggestion: develop bounds on the determinant directly in terms of K and analyze scalability, especially when combined with pseudo-inputs.
