- Context: DPPs model repulsion via a kernel, but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.
- Prior work [7]: estimated the kernel spectrum to get incremental eigenvalue-based upper/lower bounds enabling retrospective MCMC.
- Contribution: paper proposes an alternative bounding strategy that avoids explicit eigenvalue estimation.
- Method: approach naturally extends to variational inference and connects to GP inducing-point methods for scalable inference.
- Novelty: introduces a useful inequality (Proposition 1) that circumvents spectrum estimation; proof currently in the appendix.
- Strengths: technically strong, well-written, and provides insightful connections to related models.
- Concern 1: unclear if replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension offers a real computational advantage.
- Concern 2: increasing cardinality may not guarantee convergence of bounds due to likely non-convex, coupled optimization and risk of local minima blocking correct MCMC acceptance.
- Presentation notes: add axis labels to Figure 1 (P7), move the proof of Proposition 1 into the main text, and fix typo at P7, L361 (“… the variational lower is…”).
- Overall: a compelling paper using a clever trick to bound the DPP partition function with potential for faster inference.
