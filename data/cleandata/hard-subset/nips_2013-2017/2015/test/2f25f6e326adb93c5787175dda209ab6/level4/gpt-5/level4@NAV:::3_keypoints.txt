- Introduces approximate DPP inference that avoids spectral info by using inducing points to bound the likelihood; presents variational and approximate MCMC methods with empirical efficiency claims.
- Reviewer finds the contribution promising but says the methodological description is rough and the empirical evaluation is not comprehensive.
- Requests direct comparison of the two proposed methods in terms of computational complexity and wall-clock time.
- Asks for comparisons to alternative methods (both approximate and exact) on approximation quality and runtime.
- Notes variational inference showed large parameter estimation error in the toy experiment and asks when variational will succeed versus fail, and why.
- Asks how methods perform if inducing inputs are chosen to be exactly the 13 sampled points in the toy example.
- Requests practical rules of thumb for (a) selecting the number of inducing points and (b) initializing their locations.
- Minor: matrices K and L are referenced without definition; suggest briefly introducing marginal kernel and L-ensembles.
- Minor: paper emphasizes benefits of learning marginal-kernel parameters but does not adopt that approach; suggest trimming or aligning discussion.
- Overall recommendation: promising idea but needs clearer presentation and more thorough evaluation; encourage authors to improve the write-up if accepted.
