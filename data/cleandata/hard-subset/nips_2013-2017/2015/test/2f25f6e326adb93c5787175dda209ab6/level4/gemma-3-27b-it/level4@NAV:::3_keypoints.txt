- Introduces inducing-input-based approximate inference for DPPs that avoids kernel spectral decomposition, with variational and approximate MCMC algorithms and empirical validation.  
- Provide a direct comparison of computational complexity and runtime between the two proposed methods.  
- Benchmark the methods against existing approximate and exact DPP inference techniques for both approximation quality and computational cost.  
- Explain conditions under which the variational inference performs well or poorly, and justify reliance on approximate MCMC given observed parameter estimation error.  
- Evaluate using the 13-point sample from the toy example as inducing inputs to assess method performance.  
- Give practical guidance or a heuristic for selecting the number of inducing points and their initial placement.  
- Define matrices K and L and briefly introduce marginal kernels and L-ensembles to improve readability for a broader audience.  
- Streamline or remove discussion of directly learning marginal kernel parameters since that approach is not implemented.  
- Overall: promising approach but manuscript needs more detailed methodological exposition and a more comprehensive empirical evaluation before acceptance.
