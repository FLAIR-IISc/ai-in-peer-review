- Introduces novel, computationally efficient upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants using pseudo-inputs for DPP likelihoods.
- Key idea of using pseudo-inputs to approximate determinants is simple, insightful, and practically useful.
- Bounds avoid explicit spectral decomposition, enabling more scalable likelihood-based inference for DPPs.
- Approach applies beyond DPPs to other problems requiring determinantal computations.
- Experimental results convincingly demonstrate advantages and show MCMC can outperform variational inference in this context.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and builds logically from problem to solution.
- Authors show strong knowledge of related work across point processes, Gaussian processes, and numerical linear algebra.
- Theoretical results are well-motivated with derivations; more detailed proofs are in supplementary material.
- Strength: technical soundness of the proposed bounds and derivations.
- Strength: significant computational efficiency by avoiding spectral decomposition.
- Strength: clarity and accessibility for readers not expert in DPPs.
- Strength: solid experimental validation supporting the method’s effectiveness.
- Weakness: limited focus on parameterizing K versus L—paper mainly bounds likelihood given L and lacks direct treatment for K.
- Weakness: supplementary material appears to be an extended paper rather than a concise collection of proofs; needs standardization.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, technically sound solution with practical computational advantages and strong experiments.
- Argument against acceptance: needs deeper exploration of K vs L parameterization implications and improved supplementary format.
- Overall recommendation: accept—valuable contribution that would benefit from minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses.
