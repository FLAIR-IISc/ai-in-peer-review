This paper proposes an approximate estimation technique for Determinantal Point Process (DPP), where spectral knowledge of the kernel is not required to define DPP. Instead, induced inputs are used to constrain probabilities, which reduces computational cost. Both variational inference and approximate Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) techniques are described, and their effectiveness has been experimentally demonstrated.

I believe this paper has some merit, but I find the description of the method quite vague and the empirical evaluation insufficient. A few points might help clarify:

1) How can the computational complexity and overall computing time of the two proposed methods be compared?
2) How do the proposed methods compare in terms of approximate and computing time compared to approximate and exact alternatives?
3) Experimental results on toy data of section 5.1 show that the parameter estimation error using variational inference is very large, and approximate MCMC is used instead. Under what circumstances is the proposed variational inference expected to work, and when is it expected to fail? Why?
4) A 13-point sample is created for the toy example. How do the methods work if the induced inputs are these sample points?
5) What general rules are there to follow in determining the number of induced points and initial induced points?

Small comments:
The entire introduction mentions matrices K and L, but they are not explained. Brief mentions of the kernel and L-ensemble can make it easier for an inexperienced reader to follow.

The entire paper discusses the advantages of learning the parameters of the kernel directly, but this is not the method that is taken. I think some of this can be omitted, as it does not contribute to the rest of the paper.
