- Novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant for bounding DPP likelihood; simple idea, well executed and enlightening.  
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive-definite kernels; approach is general and has potential beyond DPPs.  
- Clear derivations and thorough introduction to determinantal point processes; technical challenges in likelihood-based inference well explained.  
- Connections to variational inference and MCMC are clearly articulated.  
- Experiments are well designed, demonstrating effectiveness and revealing failures of variational approximations in some cases.  
- Paper is timely, relevant, and makes a significant contribution to DPP research.  
- Minor concern: prominent emphasis on parameterizing K (vs L) is not actually addressed or solved in the paper.  
- Suggestion to provide a standard conference-style supplement rather than an extended paper as the supplement.  
- Future work recommended: bound the determinant directly in terms of K and evaluate scalability of the pseudo-input method.  
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel bounds with DPP application, strong experimental evaluation, broader applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no solution for K vs L parameterization, limited discussion of L-parameterization drawbacks, and scalability of pseudo-input approach not fully explored.
