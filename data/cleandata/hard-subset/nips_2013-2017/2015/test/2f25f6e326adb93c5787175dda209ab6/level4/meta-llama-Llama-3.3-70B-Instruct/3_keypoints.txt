- Proposes approximate inference for DPPs that avoids spectral knowledge by using inducing inputs to bound the likelihood and reduce computational cost.
- Presents both variational inference and approximate MCMC formulations and provides empirical evidence of improved efficiency.
- Methods and empirical evaluation need clearer, more comprehensive descriptions.
- Provide a detailed comparison of computational complexity and wall-clock time between the two proposed methods.
- Compare proposed methods against alternative approaches (approximate and exact) on both approximation quality and computation time.
- Explain why variational inference showed large parameter estimation error in Section 5.1 and specify scenarios where VI is expected to succeed or fail.
- Investigate performance when inducing inputs coincide with sampled points (e.g., the 13-point toy example).
- Offer practical guidance for choosing the number of inducing points and their initial placement.
- Intro should briefly explain matrices K and L (marginal kernel and L-ensembles) for accessibility.
- Streamline discussion of directly learning marginal kernel parameters to keep focus on the core methodology.
- Recommendation: refine methodological explanations and strengthen empirical evaluations if the paper is accepted.
