

Determinantal point processes (DPPs) have gained significant traction in machine learning for modeling repulsion among point sets via positive definite kernel functions. A key challenge lies in learning kernel parameters from example realizations, complicated by a normalization constant requiring summation over all possible point configurations and cardinalities. While a recent approach [7] addresses this via spectral estimation for provable retrospective MCMC of kernel parameters, this paper introduces an alternative bound enabling MCMC inference without eigenvalue estimation. The method further supports variational learning and draws meaningful connections to inducing-point techniques for scalable Gaussian process inference. The paper is technically robust, well-written, and features a novel lower bound (Proposition 1) that elegantly circumvents the estimation challenges of [7]. The linkage to Gaussian process inducing points and the viability of variational inference are particularly compelling.  

However, two technical concerns require clarification:  
1. The claimed computational advantage of replacing power iterations with higher-dimensional nonlinear optimization is not strongly supported; the benefit over iterative methods must be more rigorously justified.  
2. Convergence of the bounds via increasing cardinality is not guaranteed due to the coupled non-convex optimization. Ensuring global minima (not just local) is critical to avoid failure in bounding away the Bernoulli threshold during MCMC steps.
