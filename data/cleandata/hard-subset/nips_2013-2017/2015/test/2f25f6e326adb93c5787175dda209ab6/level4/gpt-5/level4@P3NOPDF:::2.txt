This paper introduces novel upper and lower bounds on the determinant of positive definite matrices and applies them to bound the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs). The core idea—leveraging pseudo-inputs to approximate and bound functionals of positive definite kernels—is conceptually simple yet executed with care. The approach is broadly applicable beyond DPPs, and the paper is clear, informative, and likely to spur further advances.

Strengths:
- The use of pseudo-inputs to bound determinants is creative and well motivated. It provides a principled way to control approximation quality while maintaining computational tractability.
- The results are timely and relevant to the community working on kernels, probabilistic modeling, and scalable inference for DPPs.
- The experimental evaluation is thoughtfully designed. It highlights key aspects of the method, including regimes where a common variational approximation can fail, and clarifies when the proposed bounds are most useful.
- The application to DPP likelihoods is compelling and demonstrates practical impact. The connection to kernel methods suggests broader utility.

Minor comments and questions:
- The paper repeatedly emphasizes parameterizing the DPP via K rather than L in the abstract and body, but does not substantively tackle this issue. If this perspective is central to the paper’s framing, it would help to either (i) provide concrete methodological or theoretical advances specific to K-parameterization, or (ii) de-emphasize it and clearly delineate it as motivation for future work.
- If accepted, please provide a standard, self-contained supplementary material (proofs, additional experiments, algorithmic details), rather than uploading an extended version of the paper. This will make it easier for readers to find what they need without duplication across documents.

Suggestions for future work:
- The paper’s discussion of the interpretability benefits of K-parameterization is persuasive. It would be valuable to investigate whether the determinant can be bounded directly in terms of K, and to articulate the trade-offs compared to working with L. In particular:
  - Are there analogous upper and lower bounds that operate naturally on K, and do they retain the same tightness and stability properties?
  - What is the computational complexity and scalability of such K-based bounds when using pseudo-inputs? Are there regimes (e.g., sparse kernels, low effective rank) where K-based bounds provide advantages?
- Given the generality of the pseudo-input strategy, exploring applications beyond DPPs (e.g., log-determinant regularization, kernel learning objectives, or Gaussian process marginal likelihoods) could broaden the impact and test the robustness of the approach.

Overall assessment:
This is a solid and well-executed paper with a clear main contribution and a convincing empirical narrative. While the conceptual advance is succinct, the work is enlightening, practically relevant, and opens several promising avenues for improvement and extension. Clarifying the role of K-parameterization and providing a standard supplement would strengthen the final version. I recommend acceptance.
