- Problem: parameter inference for DPPs is hard because the normalizing constant (determinant/Fredholm determinant) is intractable at scale.  
- Contribution: novel non-spectral upper and lower bounds on the determinant using inducing points (via Seiler & Simon inequality), analogous to sparse GP approximations.  
- Methodological advances: enables a retrospective MCMC sampler avoiding eigen-solvers and introduces a variational inference framework for DPPs.  
- Scope and validation: methods developed for discrete and continuous DPPs and validated on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Strength: originality and significance of the non-spectral bounding approach and the new variational inference for DPPs.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized exposition with a useful connection to inducing-point sparse GPs.  
- Strength: technically sound derivations and convincing experiments, including a 1D Gaussian-Gaussian sanity check.  
- Weakness: practical computational trade-off — optimizing inducing points Z is a non-linear/non-convex task and may offset the benefit of avoiding power iterations; need detailed complexity comparison to computing leading eigenvalues.  
- Weakness: MCMC correctness concerns — non-convex optimization of Z may yield poor local minima that prevent bounds from tightening, risking chain stalling; unclear if guarantees hold that increasing m will always bracket acceptance thresholds regardless of optimization quality.  
- Minor: Figure 1 missing axis labels.  
- Minor: key proof (Proposition 1) is in the supplement; include a brief proof sketch or intuition in the main text.  
- Minor: typo on page 7 ("the variational lower is..." → "the variational lower bound is...").  
- Recommendation: strong, novel, and significant paper; recommend acceptance.
