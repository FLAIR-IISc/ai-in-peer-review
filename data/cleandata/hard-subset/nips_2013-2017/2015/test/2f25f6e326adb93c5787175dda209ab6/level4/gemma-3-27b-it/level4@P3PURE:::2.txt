The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. The core idea is straightforward, yet the paper is well-executed and insightful, opening avenues for several potential improvements.

The authors present a creative application of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. This problem is timely and likely to be of interest to a broad segment of the research community. 

The proposed approach is quite general and holds potential for applications extending beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-designed and effectively illuminates key points, including limitations of a variational approximation under specific conditions. I found the chosen application area to be both interesting and highly relevant to the problem under investigation.

Minor comments:

- The prominence given to the issue of parameterizing K (rather than L) in the abstract and throughout the paper is unclear, as the authors primarily focus on problem formulation without attempting to address this parameterization challenge.
- Should the paper be accepted, I recommend the authors prepare a dedicated supplementary document, rather than simply uploading an extended version of the manuscript.

Suggestions for future work:

- The discussion regarding the interpretability of the K parameterization actually enhances the appeal of this approach. Could the authors explore bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? Furthermore, what is the scalability of this method when employing pseudo-inputs?
