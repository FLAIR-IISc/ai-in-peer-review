Introduces non-spectral upper and lower determinant bounds using inducing inputs Z and a low-rank proxy Q to avoid eigencomputations.

Bounds apply to both finite and continuous domains (via Fredholm determinants), producing tractable DPP likelihoods.

Enables two inference schemes: a fast variational approach and a retrospective Metropolis–Hastings sampler.

Validated on a 1D Gaussian–Gaussian DPP (with ground-truth spectra) and on real spatial epidermal nerve-fiber data.

Positions itself against spectral methods and adapts sparse GP inducing-variable ideas (Titsias 2009) to determinant bounds.

Broad applicability and practical impact by removing the need for partial eigendecompositions.

Strength: clear, well-executed idea likely to stimulate further developments beyond DPPs.

Strength: experiments are thoughtful and show convergence of bounds with increasing number of pseudo-inputs m.

Weakness: emphasizes the K-versus-L parameterization tension but does not attempt to resolve it; methods operate solely in L.

Weakness: supplement should be a conventional, self-contained appendix with proofs and extended experiments for reproducibility.

Suggestion: derive similar inducing-point determinant bounds directly in terms of K for interpretability and assess scalability.

Suggestion: explore broader applications of the determinant-bounding framework beyond DPPs.

Pro-acceptance: novel, simple, general technique with solid experimental evidence and clear exposition.

Con-acceptance: lack of progress on K-vs-L and absence of bounds formulated in K may disappoint some readers.

Quality (NIPS criteria): technically sound; retrospective MH construction appropriate; experiments convincing.

Clarity (NIPS criteria): generally clear and organized, but K vs L narrative should be tightened; provide a proper supplement.

Originality (NIPS criteria): fresh extension of sparse GP ideas to nonspectral determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods.

Significance (NIPS criteria): high potential impact enabling practical variational and MCMC inference for DPPs without eigendecompositions.

Minor: clarify why K-vs-L is emphasized in abstract/introduction if no bridging technique is proposed.

Minor: if accepted, include a standard supplementary document containing all proofs, implementation details, and extended results.
