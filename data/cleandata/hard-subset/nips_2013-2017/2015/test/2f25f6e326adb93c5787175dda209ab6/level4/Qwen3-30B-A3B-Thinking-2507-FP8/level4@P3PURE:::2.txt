

The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant with applications to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes. The main idea is simple yet well-executed, offering clear insights and paving the way for several potential improvements. The creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels addresses a timely problem of significant interest to the community. This approach is sufficiently general to have potential applications beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-designed to demonstrate key insights, including the failure modes of variational approximations under specific conditions. The chosen application area is both interesting and highly relevant to the problem studied.

Minor comments:  
- It is unclear why parameterizing the kernel matrix \(K\) (rather than its Cholesky factor \(L\)) is emphasized so prominently in the abstract and main text, given that the authors focus primarily on problem formulation without addressing this parameterization choice.  

- If accepted, the authors should provide a standard supplemental document rather than uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.  

Suggestions for future work:  
- The discussion of interpretability associated with \(K\)-parameterization makes the approach particularly appealing. Could the authors explore bounding the determinant directly using the \(K\) matrix? Additionally, what is the scalability of this approach when using pseudo-inputs?
