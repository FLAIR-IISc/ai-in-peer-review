This paper proposes novel bounds on the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs) that do not require spectral knowledge of the kernel defining the DPP. The authors derive these bounds for both finite and continuous domains and demonstrate their use in variational inference and Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods. The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of DPPs, as likelihood-based inference is not straightforward due to the intractable normalizing constant and the need for spectral decomposition.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The use of inducing inputs to bound the DPP likelihood reduces the computational cost, making the methods more efficient. The empirical results demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed methods, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the results.

However, there are a few areas that require clarification and improvement. Firstly, the computational complexity and overall compute time of the two proposed methods are not thoroughly compared. A detailed analysis of the computational cost and time complexity would strengthen the paper. Secondly, the authors do not provide a comprehensive comparison of the proposed methods with other alternatives, both approximate and exact. A more detailed comparison would help to establish the superiority of the proposed methods.

Furthermore, the empirical results on the toy data in Section 5.1 show that parameter estimation error using variational inference is too large, and the authors resort to approximate MCMC instead. The authors should provide more insight into when the proposed variational inference is expected to work and when it is expected to fail. Additionally, the authors should investigate the performance of the methods when the inducing inputs are actually the sampled points.

Minor comments include the need for a brief explanation of the matrices K and L in the introduction, as they are referred to throughout the paper without clear explanation. Some discussion about the advantages of directly learning the marginal kernel parameters could be cut back, as it does not contribute to the rest of the paper.

In terms of the quality of the paper, I would rate it as follows:

* Quality: 8/10
* Clarity: 8/10
* Originality: 9/10
* Impact: 8/10

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of DPPs, and with some clarifications and improvements, it has the potential to be a strong paper.
