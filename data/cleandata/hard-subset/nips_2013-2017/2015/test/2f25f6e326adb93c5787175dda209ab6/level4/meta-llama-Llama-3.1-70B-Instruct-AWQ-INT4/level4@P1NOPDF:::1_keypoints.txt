- Problem: DPP parameter learning is hard because the normalization constant sums over all cardinalities and configurations, making it computationally intractable.  
- Prior work [7]: estimates the kernel spectrum and uses incremental eigenvalue-based upper/lower bounds to enable retrospective MCMC inference.  
- Contribution: paper proposes new bounds for MCMC inference that avoid eigenvalue estimation.  
- Additional methods: approach supports a variational learning method and connects to GP inducing-point techniques for scalable inference.  
- Writing and technical quality: paper is well-written and technically strong.  
- Key technical insight: the inequality in Proposition 1 gives an excellent lower bound and sidesteps the estimation issues in [7].  
- Concern 1 (computational): replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension may not be a practical computational win.  
- Concern 2 (convergence/nonconvexity): unclear that increasing cardinality guarantees convergence of bounds—coupled, likely highly nonconvex optimizations may get stuck in local minima.  
- Critical risk: a local minimum could prevent bounding away the Bernoulli threshold, making MCMC steps impossible.  
- Presentation suggestion: Figure 1 lacks axis labels and needs them for clarity.  
- Presentation suggestion: move the proof of the central proposition from the appendix into the main text for accessibility.  
- Presentation suggestion: rephrase “the variational lower is” on page 7, line 361, for clarity.  
- Overall assessment: a clever, promising approach for bounding the DPP partition function with potential for faster inference and useful model connections.
