## Review of NIPS 2015 Submission: [Paper Title - *Please insert title here*]

This paper presents novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant, with a compelling application to bounding the likelihood function of a determinantal point process (DPP). The core idea is elegantly simple, yet the paper demonstrates a thorough and insightful execution, laying the groundwork for potentially significant advancements in the field. The work addresses a timely problem and is likely to be of broad interest to the machine learning community, particularly those working with Gaussian processes and kernel methods.

The authors’ creative utilization of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels is particularly noteworthy. This approach exhibits a promising degree of generality, extending beyond the specific context of DPPs and offering potential benefits in other applications where determinantal calculations are crucial. The experimental evaluation is well-designed and effectively illustrates key aspects of the proposed bounds, including illuminating conditions under which a variational approximation may fail. I found the chosen application area – determinantal point processes – to be both interesting and highly relevant to the theoretical problem being addressed. 

The paper is generally well-written and the mathematical derivations are clear. The authors effectively communicate the intuition behind their approach, making the results accessible to a broad audience. The connection between the theoretical bounds and the empirical results is convincingly demonstrated.

However, I have a few minor points for consideration. The repeated emphasis on the challenges of parameterizing the kernel matrix *K* (rather than *L*, its Cholesky decomposition) throughout the abstract and paper feels somewhat misplaced. While acknowledging the issue is reasonable, the authors primarily *describe* the problem without proposing any solutions or further investigation into alternative parameterizations. This creates a slight disconnect, as it raises a question that remains unanswered.

Furthermore, should the paper be accepted, I strongly recommend that the authors prepare a dedicated supplementary material document, conforming to the standard NIPS format.  Uploading an extended version of the paper itself as the supplement is not ideal for readability and accessibility. A dedicated supplement allows for more focused presentation of additional details, proofs, and experimental settings.

Finally, the discussion regarding the interpretability of the *K* parameterization paradoxically strengthens the appeal of directly bounding the determinant in terms of the *K* matrix itself.  I would encourage the authors to explore this direction further.  Additionally, understanding the scalability of the pseudo-input method – particularly as the size of *K* grows – is important. Investigating the computational complexity and potential optimizations would significantly strengthen the practical relevance of the work. 

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution. The theoretical results are valuable, the application is relevant, and the experimental evaluation is convincing. Addressing the minor comments regarding the *K* parameterization and the supplementary material would further enhance the quality of the paper. I believe this work has the potential to significantly impact the field and recommend acceptance.



**Strengths:**

*   Novel and insightful theoretical results.
*   Clear and well-motivated application to determinantal point processes.
*   Well-designed experimental evaluation.
*   Potential for broad applicability beyond DPPs.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Discussion of *K* parameterization feels incomplete.
*   Supplementary material should adhere to standard NIPS format.
*   Scalability of the pseudo-input method warrants further investigation.
