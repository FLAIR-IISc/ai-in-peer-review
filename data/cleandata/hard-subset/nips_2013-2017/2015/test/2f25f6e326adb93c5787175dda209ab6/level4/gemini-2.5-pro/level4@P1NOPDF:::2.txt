This paper introduces novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant and applies them to the problem of bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. While the core idea is straightforward, the paper is well-executed and insightful, opening up several avenues for future work.

The authors' creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels is a key contribution. The problem itself is timely and should be of broad interest to the machine learning community. Furthermore, the proposed approach is quite general, with clear potential for applications beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-designed, clearly demonstrating several key points, including the failure modes of variational approximations under certain conditions. The chosen application is both interesting and highly relevant to the problem under consideration.

**Minor Comments:**

*   It is unclear why the challenge of parameterizing the kernel matrix K (as opposed to its Cholesky factor L) is featured so prominently in the abstract and introduction. This issue is not directly addressed in the main body of the paper, which focuses primarily on the bounding technique itself.
*   If the paper is accepted, I suggest the authors prepare a standard supplement rather than simply uploading an extended version of the main paper.

**Suggestions for Future Work:**

*   The paper's discussion of the interpretability of parameterizing K directly is compelling. This raises the question: could the authors' method be adapted to bound the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix, rather than its factors? It would also be interesting to analyze the scalability of such an approach when combined with the pseudo-input method.
