The authors present novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes. The central idea is simple, but the paper is well executed and illuminating, and it opens the door to several potential improvements. The use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels is creative. The problem is timely and should interest many in the community. The approach appears quite general, with potential applications beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is carefully designed to highlight several key points, including cases where a variational approximation fails under certain conditions. I found the chosen application area both interesting and highly relevant to the problem studied.

Minor comments:
- It is unclear why parameterizing K (rather than L) is emphasized in the abstract and throughout the paper, given that the authors primarily describe the issue and make no attempt to address it.
- If accepted, I recommend providing a standard supplementary document rather than uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion of the interpretability of the K parameterization makes this direction particularly appealing. Could the authors consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? What would be the scalability of such an approach when using the pseudo-input method?
