The authors propose upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, which can be used to limit the possibility of the determinant point process. The main idea is simple, but the paper is well-executed and insightful, and paves the way for several potential improvements.

The authors propose the creative use of pseudo-inputs to limit the functional properties of the positive definite kernel. The proposed problem is timely and will be of interest to many in the community.

Such an approach with potential applications beyond determinant point processes is quite general. Experimental evaluation has been well-conducted to clarify several key points, including the failure of variable approximations under certain conditions. I found the selected application area interesting and highly relevant to the problem studied.

Minor comments:
- It is unclear why the issue of parameterizing K (instead of L) is so prominent in the abstract and the rest of the paper, as the authors primarily focus on describing the problem and make no attempt to address it.
- If accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement, and not upload an extended version of the paper as a supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- On the other hand, the discussion of the explanation of K parameterization makes such an approach even more appealing. Could the authors consider limiting the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? What is the scalability of such an approach when using the pseudo-input method?
