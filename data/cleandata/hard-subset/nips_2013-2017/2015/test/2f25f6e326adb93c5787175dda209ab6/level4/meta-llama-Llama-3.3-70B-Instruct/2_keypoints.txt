- Presents novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant with implications for bounding determinantal point process (DPP) likelihoods.
- Core concept is straightforward; paper is well-organized, informative, and lays groundwork for further improvements.
- Introduces a novel use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels, addressing a timely problem.
- Approach is fairly general and could apply beyond DPPs.
- Experimental evaluation is carefully designed and highlights key aspects, including limits of variational approximations under certain conditions.
- The chosen application area is intriguing and highly relevant to the problem.
- Minor critique: emphasis on parameterizing K (vs L) seems unjustified, as the paper does not address this issue in depth.
- Minor critique: if accepted, authors should provide standard supplementary material rather than an extended paper as the supplement.
- Suggestion for future work: directly bound the determinant in terms of the K matrix and explore interpretability of the K parameterization.
- Suggestion for future work: investigate scalability of the method using pseudo-inputs.
