- Introduces novel upper and lower bounds on determinants of positive definite matrices and applies them to bound DPP likelihoods.  
- Core idea: use pseudo-inputs to approximate and bound functionals of positive definite kernels; conceptually simple and carefully executed.  
- Approach is broadly applicable beyond DPPs and connects to kernel methods.  
- Creative, well-motivated use of pseudo-inputs that controls approximation quality while keeping computation tractable.  
- Timely and relevant to kernels, probabilistic modeling, and scalable DPP inference.  
- Experiments are well-designed, highlighting failure regimes of common variational approximations and when proposed bounds help.  
- Application to DPP likelihoods is compelling and demonstrates practical impact.  
- Paper overemphasizes K-parameterization versus L without providing substantive methodological or theoretical advances on K.  
- Request to provide a standard, self-contained supplementary material (proofs, extra experiments, algorithmic details) rather than an extended paper version.  
- Future work: investigate bounding determinants directly in terms of K and compare tightness, stability, and trade-offs with L.  
- Future work: analyze computational complexity and scalability of K-based bounds with pseudo-inputs, and explore regimes (sparse kernels, low rank) where K-based bounds help.  
- Future work: apply the pseudo-input strategy to other problems (log-determinant regularization, kernel learning, GP marginal likelihoods) to test robustness.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed, enlightening, and practically relevant work that opens promising avenues; recommends acceptance.
