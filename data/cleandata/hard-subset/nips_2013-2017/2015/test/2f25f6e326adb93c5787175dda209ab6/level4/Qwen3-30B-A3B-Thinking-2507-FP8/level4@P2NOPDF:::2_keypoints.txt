- Introduces novel upper and lower bounds for matrix determinants with applications to bounding DPP likelihoods.  
- Core idea: use pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels—simple, insightful, and potentially widely applicable.  
- Well-executed, mathematically rigorous derivations with clear exposition.  
- Strong empirical evaluation demonstrating key insights and limitations of variational approximations.  
- Application domain (DPP likelihood estimation) is well-motivated and increases the paper's impact.  
- Main strength: generality of the approach and successful bridging of theory with empirical validation.  
- Minor concern 1: paper emphasizes K-parameterization over Cholesky L but does not clarify or resolve why this choice is central.  
- Minor concern 2: recommend submitting a standard supplementary material file instead of an extended main paper for accessibility.  
- Future work suggestion 1: explore bounding the determinant directly in terms of K to improve interpretability and implementation.  
- Future work suggestion 2: investigate scalability of the pseudo-input method and computational cost for large-scale problems.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution, minor issues are addressable, recommend acceptance.
