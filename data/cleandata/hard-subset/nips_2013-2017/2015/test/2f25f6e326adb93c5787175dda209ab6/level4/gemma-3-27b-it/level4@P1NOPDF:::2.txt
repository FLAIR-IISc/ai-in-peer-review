The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. The core idea is straightforward, but the paper is well-executed and insightful, opening avenues for several potential improvements.

The authors present a creative application of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. This is a timely problem that will likely be of interest to many in the machine learning community.

The approach is quite general and has potential applications extending beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-designed and effectively illustrates key points, including the limitations of a variational approximation under specific conditions. I found the chosen application area both interesting and highly relevant to the problem under investigation.

Minor comments:

- The prominence given to the issue of parameterizing K (rather than L) in the abstract and throughout the paper is unclear. While the authors clearly describe the problem, they don’t attempt to address this parameterization choice.
- If accepted, I recommend the authors prepare a standard supplementary document, rather than simply uploading an extended version of the paper.

Suggestions for future work:

- Conversely, the discussion surrounding the interpretability of the K parameterization makes this approach particularly appealing. Could the authors consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? Furthermore, what is the scalability of this approach when employing the pseudo-input method?
