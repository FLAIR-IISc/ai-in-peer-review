- Proposes novel likelihood bounds for DPPs that avoid spectral decomposition of the kernel.  
- Bounds derived for both finite and continuous domains and applied to variational inference and MCMC.  
- Uses inducing inputs to bound DPP likelihoods, reducing computational cost.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear problem statement and solution exposition.  
- Empirical results demonstrate efficiency and practical utility of the proposed methods.  
- Computational complexity and total runtime of the two methods are not thoroughly compared.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparison against other approximate and exact alternatives.  
- Variational inference shows large parameter estimation error on toy data, leading to reliance on approximate MCMC.  
- Needs guidance on when variational inference is expected to succeed or fail.  
- Should investigate performance when inducing inputs coincide with sampled points.  
- Minor: introduce and briefly explain matrices K and L in the introduction.  
- Minor: trim or justify discussion on advantages of directly learning marginal kernel parameters.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Impact 8/10.
