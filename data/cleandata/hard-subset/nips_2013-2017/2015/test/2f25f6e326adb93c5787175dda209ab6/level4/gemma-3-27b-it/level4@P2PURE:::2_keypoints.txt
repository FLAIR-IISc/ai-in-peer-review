- Presents novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant with applications to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs).  
- Core idea is straightforward but well-executed, well-written, and opens avenues for further research.  
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.  
- Addresses a timely problem with likely broad interest to the machine learning community.  
- Approach is general and has potential applications beyond DPPs.  
- Experimental evaluation is well-designed and effectively illustrates key points, including limitations of a variational approximation under certain conditions.  
- Minor concern: prominence of parameterizing the kernel matrix K (rather than L) is unclear since the paper does not resolve that challenge.  
- Minor suggestion: if accepted, provide a standard supplementary material document instead of uploading an extended manuscript.  
- Future work suggestion: explore directly bounding the determinant in terms of the K matrix.  
- Future work suggestion: evaluate the scalability of the method when using pseudo-inputs.
