This paper proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs). The core idea is simple, but the execution is careful and illuminating, and the work opens several avenues for improvement.

The authors make creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. The problem is timely and of broad interest, and the approach appears quite general, with potential applications beyond DPPs. The experimental evaluation is well designed and clarifies several key points, including cases in which a variational approximation fails. I found the chosen application area both interesting and highly relevant to the problem studied.

Minor comments:
- It is unclear why the issue of parameterizing K (rather than L) features so prominently in the abstract and throughout the paper. The manuscript largely describes the issue without attempting to address it. Either de-emphasize this point or clarify concrete steps taken toward resolving it.
- If accepted, please prepare a conventional supplementary document rather than simply uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion highlighting the interpretability of the K-parameterization makes that direction particularly appealing. Could the proposed determinant bounds be developed directly for the K matrix? If so, what would the scalability look like when using the pseudo-input method?
