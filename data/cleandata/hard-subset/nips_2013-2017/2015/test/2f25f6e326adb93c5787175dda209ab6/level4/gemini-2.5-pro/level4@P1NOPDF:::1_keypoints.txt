- Paper introduces a new bound for DPP kernel learning that avoids eigenvalue estimation, enabling retrospective MCMC and a variational inference framework with connections to GP inducing points.

- Technically strong and well-written; Proposition 1 uses a clever inequality, and the variational/inducing-point connections are insightful.

- Major concern: claimed computational advantage is unconvincing — replacing power iterations with growing-dimension nonlinear optimizations may not yield a net gain; a more thorough empirical/runtime comparison is needed.

- Major concern: convergence of bounds as inducing-set cardinality increases is not rigorously shown; non-convex optimization may get stuck in local minima, preventing bounds from tightening and stalling MCMC — discuss practical impact and mitigation.

- Presentation issues: Figure 1 (page 7) needs axis labels.

- Presentation issue: move the proof of Proposition 1 from the appendix into the main body given its centrality.

- Presentation issue: fix typo on page 7, line 361 ("... the variational lower is ...").
