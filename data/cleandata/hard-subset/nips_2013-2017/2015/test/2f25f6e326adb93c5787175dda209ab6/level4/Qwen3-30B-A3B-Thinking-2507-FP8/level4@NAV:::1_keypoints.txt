- Paper introduces a novel lower bound (Proposition 1) for DPP partition functions that avoids eigenvalue estimation, enabling MCMC inference without spectral methods.  
- Method also supports variational learning and draws meaningful connections to inducing-point techniques for scalable Gaussian process inference.  
- The work is technically robust, well-written, and the lower bound is a clever solution to the estimation challenges of prior work [7].  
- Claim that replacing power iterations with higher-dimensional nonlinear optimization offers computational advantage is not convincingly supported and needs stronger justification.  
- Convergence of the bounds by increasing cardinality is not guaranteed due to coupled non-convex optimization; ensuring global (not just local) minima is critical to avoid MCMC failures.  
- Presentation fixes requested: add axis labels to P7 Figure 1.  
- Move the proof of Proposition 1 from the appendix into the main text since it is central.  
- Typo correction requested: change "variational lower is" to "variational lower bound" on P7, L361.  
- Overall assessment: a strong paper with a clever bounding technique that enables faster inference and useful cross-model connections.
