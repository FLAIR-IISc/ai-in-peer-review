- DPPs model repulsion via a kernel, but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.
- Prior work [7] used spectral estimation to enable retrospective MCMC for kernel inference.
- This paper proposes an alternative bounding strategy for MCMC that avoids eigenvalue estimation.
- The approach naturally supports variational inference and connects to GP inducing-point methods for scalable inference.
- Proposition 1 leverages a novel inequality that is a central and valuable technical insight.
- The method is technically strong and the paper is well written and pleasant to read.
- Concern: replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization problems of increasing dimension may not be a clear computational win; complexity needs further discussion.
- Concern: convergence of bounds as cardinality increases is unconvincing due to non-convex optimization and risk of local minima affecting Bernoulli thresholds and MCMC steps; theoretical or empirical support/mitigation is needed.
- Presentation issues: add axis labels to Figure 1, move the proof of Proposition 1 into the main text, and fix a minor grammatical error on page 7.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions addressing the computational complexity and convergence concerns.
