- Main contribution: derivation of novel non-spectral upper and lower bounds on the DPP normalizing constant (determinant) using a determinant inequality and pseudo-inputs/inducing points.
- Key insight: replaces spectral estimation (prior work [7]) with inducing-point-based bounds, drawing a parallel to variational sparse approximations in Gaussian processes.
- Inference algorithms: proposes a cheap approximate variational inference method and a more expensive but exact retrospective MCMC sampler leveraging the bounds.
- Originality/quality: idea is novel, technically sound, and a clever alternative to spectral techniques.
- Significance: enables scalable DPP inference and extends applicability to larger problems where MCMC is infeasible.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and handles extension from finite to continuous domains smoothly.
- Computational concern: avoids power iterations but requires repeated non-linear optimization over pseudo-input locations Z, which may grow with problem size—trade-offs not fully analyzed.
- MCMC convergence concern: optimization over Z is non-convex; poor local minima could prevent tightening bounds, potentially stalling the retrospective MCMC and affecting correctness.
- Missing/incomplete exposition: Proposition 1 is central and should include a high-level proof sketch in the main paper rather than only the supplement.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels.
- Typo: Page 7, Line 361 reads "the variational lower is..." (needs correction).
