- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds for determinants of positive definite matrices, applied to bounding DPP likelihoods.  
- Leverages a creative, conceptually simple use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.  
- Paper is well-executed, enlightening, and opens promising avenues for future research.  
- Addresses a timely problem of clear interest to the machine learning community.  
- Method is general and potentially applicable beyond DPPs to any setting where scalable determinant computation is a bottleneck.  
- Experiments are well-designed and convincingly show conditions where standard variational approximations fail, highlighting the proposed bounds’ utility.  
- Suggests clarifying or better integrating the motivation about difficulty parameterizing K versus its Cholesky factor L, since the main contribution targets determinant bounds.  
- Asks authors to comment on extending the framework to bound determinants directly in terms of K and discuss scalability with pseudo-inputs.  
- Recommends providing a standard supplementary document with additional details/proofs rather than an extended main paper if accepted.
