- Paper proposes new bounds for DPP partition functions that avoid eigenvalue estimation, enable MCMC inference, and yield a variational learning approach with links to GP inducing points.  
- The approach is technically strong, well-written, and uses a clever inequality (Proposition 1) particularly well-suited to the problem.  
- Reviewer appreciated the connection to inducing-point methods and the ability to perform variational inference; overall enjoyable to read.  
- Major concern 1: Unclear computational advantage—need complexity analysis and empirical wall-clock vs. accuracy comparisons to power-iteration spectral methods like [7].  
- Major concern 2: Convergence of bounds as cardinality increases is not guaranteed due to nonconvex optimization; reviewer asks for (a) theoretical conditions for convergence, (b) optimization strategies or guarantees to avoid poor local minima, and (c) analysis of how failing to reach global optimum affects correctness/mixing of retrospective MCMC.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 (page 7) needs axis labels.  
- Presentation issue: The proof of the key proposition should be moved into the main body rather than the appendix.  
- Presentation issue: Typo on page 7, line 361—“the variational lower is” should be “the variational lower bound is.”  
- Overall assessment: A very nice, clever contribution that would be significantly strengthened by addressing the computational trade-offs and convergence/optimization concerns.
