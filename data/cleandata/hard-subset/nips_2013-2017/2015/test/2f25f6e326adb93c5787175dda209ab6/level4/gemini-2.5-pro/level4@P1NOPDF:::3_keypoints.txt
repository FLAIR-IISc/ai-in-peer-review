- Core idea has merit (inducing-input bound for DPP inference) but the methodology is unclearly written and empirical evaluation is insufficient.  
- Missing direct comparison of computational complexity and empirical runtimes for VI versus MCMC to understand trade-offs.  
- Need broader empirical comparison to other exact and approximate DPP inference methods, evaluating approximation quality and computational time.  
- VI shows large parameter estimation errors on the toy dataset; authors must discuss when VI is expected to succeed or fail and state its theoretical/practical limitations.  
- For the toy example, using the exact 13 sampled points as inducing inputs is a natural baseline that should be tried.  
- Paper should provide guidance on selecting inducing points: recommended number and initial placement strategies.  
- Minor: introduce and define the marginal kernel K and L-ensemble L in the introduction for reader clarity.  
- Minor: condense or remove tangential discussion about directly learning marginal kernel parameters since it is not the adopted approach.
