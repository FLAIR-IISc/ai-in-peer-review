- Proposes a new bounding strategy for MCMC in DPPs that avoids eigenvalue estimation.
- Enables variational learning and draws connections to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian process inference.
- Technically rigorous and clearly written; the inequality in Proposition 1 is novel and well-suited to the problem.
- The connection to GP inducing points and resulting variational inference capability is particularly appreciated.
- Concern: unclear whether replacing power iterations with potentially complex, high-dimensional nonlinear optimization is a real computational win.
- Concern: convergence of bounds by increasing cardinality is not guaranteed because the coupled optimization is highly non-convex and may get stuck in local minima.
- Recommendation: provide strategies or guarantees to ensure global minimum attainment for the optimization used in bounding.
- Presentation fixes: add axis labels to Figure 1, move the proof of the proposition from the appendix into the main text, and correct the typo on Page 7 ("the variational lower is...").
- Overall: strong paper that cleverly bounds the DPP partition function, with potential for faster inference and meaningful links to related modeling paradigms.
