This paper introduces novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with an application to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process (DPP). The core idea, which involves a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels, is simple yet powerful. The paper is well-executed and enlightening, paving the way for several potential improvements. The problem is timely and will be of interest to many in the community.

The proposed approach is quite general, with potential applications beyond DPPs. The experimental evaluation is thoughtfully constructed, effectively elucidating key aspects of the method and highlighting conditions under which a standard variational approximation fails. The choice of DPPs as an application area is both interesting and highly relevant to the problem being studied.

**Minor Comments:**
- The issue of parameterizing the kernel matrix K (as opposed to its Cholesky factor L) features prominently in the abstract and the main text. However, the paper focuses on describing the problem rather than addressing this specific challenge, creating a slight disconnect between the stated motivation and the core contribution.
- If the paper is accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement rather than uploading an extended version of the paper.

**Suggestions for Future Work:**
- The paper’s discussion on the interpretability of the K parameterization makes a direct approach highly appealing. Could the authors consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? What would be the scalability of such an approach when combined with the proposed pseudo-input method?
