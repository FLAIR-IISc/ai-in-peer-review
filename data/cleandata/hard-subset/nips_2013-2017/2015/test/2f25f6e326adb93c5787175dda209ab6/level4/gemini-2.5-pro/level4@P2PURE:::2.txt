This paper proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with an application to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process (DPP). The central idea, a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels, is simple yet enlightening. This timely contribution will be of interest to many in the community. The paper is well-executed, and the proposed approach is general, with potential applications beyond DPPs. The experimental evaluation is thoughtfully constructed to elucidate key properties of the bounds, including conditions under which a competing variational approximation fails. The chosen application is interesting and highly relevant to the problem studied.

Minor comments:
- The paper prominently discusses the issue of parameterizing the kernel matrix K directly, as opposed to its Cholesky factor L. However, it is unclear why this receives so much emphasis, as the proposed method does not address this challenge.
- If the paper is accepted, I suggest the authors prepare a standard supplement rather than submitting an extended version of the main paper.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion of the interpretability of K-parameterization makes such a direction appealing. Could the authors' pseudo-input method be adapted to bound the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? An analysis of the scalability of such an approach would be a valuable contribution.
