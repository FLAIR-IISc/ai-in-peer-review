Determinantal point processes (DPPs) have received significant recent attention in the machine learning literature for their ability to model repulsion among points in a set, specified via a positive definite kernel. A key challenge is learning the kernel parameters from observed point configurations, which is difficult due to the intractable normalizing constant (partition function) that effectively sums over all possible cardinalities and configurations. A recent proposal [7] addresses this by estimating the spectrum to construct incremental upper and lower bounds, enabling a provably correct “retrospective” MCMC scheme for kernel parameters.

This paper proposes a different type of bound that supports MCMC inference without requiring eigenvalue estimation. The approach also leads naturally to a variational learning method and draws compelling connections to inducing-point techniques for scalable Gaussian process (GP) inference. The paper is technically strong and clearly written. I was not aware of the inequality used to obtain the lower bound in Proposition 1, and it appears to be an excellent fit for this problem—a clever way to avoid the spectral estimation challenges in [7]. I especially appreciate the connections to GP inducing points and the ability to perform variational inference. Overall, the paper was a pleasure to read.

I have two technical concerns I would like to see addressed:

1) While I like the approach, I am not convinced it represents a substantial computational win to replace power iterations with nonlinear optimization problems of increasing dimension. The trade-off is not obviously favorable.

2) It is not clear that simply increasing the cardinality guarantees convergence of the bounds, given the coupled optimization problem. Although I am not certain, I believe this optimization is highly non-convex, so one must both increase cardinality and ensure attainment of a global minimum. A local minimum could prevent the procedure from bounding away the Bernoulli threshold, making it impossible to accept or reject an MCMC step. This should be addressed directly.

Presentation issues:
- Page 7, Figure 1: Please include axis labels.
- The proof of Proposition 1 is the central insight that enables the paper. I would prefer to see it in the main body rather than the appendix.
- Page 7, line 361: “… the variational lower is …” → “… the variational lower bound is …”

In sum, this is a very nice paper that uses a clever inequality to bound the DPP partition function, enabling potentially faster inference and drawing useful connections to other models.
