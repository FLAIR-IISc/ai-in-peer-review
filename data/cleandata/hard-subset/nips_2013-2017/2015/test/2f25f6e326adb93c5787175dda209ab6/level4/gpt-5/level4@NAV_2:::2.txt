The paper introduces new upper and lower bounds on determinants, enabling practical bounds on the likelihood of determinantal point processes. The core idea is straightforward, but the execution is careful and illuminating, and it opens the door to several promising refinements.

A notable contribution is the inventive use of pseudo/inducing inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. The problem addressed is timely and should be of broad interest to the community.

The approach appears quite general, with potential utility beyond DPPs. The experimental section is thoughtfully designed to surface several key insights, including situations where a variational approximation can fail. The chosen application area is engaging and well aligned with the technical contributions.

Minor comments:
- The prominence of the K-versus-L parameterization issue in the abstract and main text is somewhat confusing, as the paper primarily frames the problem without attempting to solve it.
- If accepted, please prepare a standard supplementary document rather than submitting an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- Given the interpretability advantages of parameterizing K, it would be valuable to explore determinant bounds stated directly in terms of K. How would such a strategy scale if implemented with pseudo-inputs?

In summary, the paper presents novel two-sided determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods. The central idea is simple, the presentation is strong and informative, and it sets the stage for multiple useful extensions.
