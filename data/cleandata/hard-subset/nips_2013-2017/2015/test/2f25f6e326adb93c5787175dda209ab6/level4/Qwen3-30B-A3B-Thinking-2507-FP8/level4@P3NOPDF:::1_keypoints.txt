- Novel approach to learning DPP kernel parameters that avoids the intractable normalization constant.  
- Proposes a new lower bound for the partition function that removes the need for eigenvalue estimation used in prior work [7].  
- Enables both MCMC inference and variational learning for DPPs.  
- Insightful connection to inducing-point methods in Gaussian processes, offering a path to scalable DPP inference.  
- Strong technical contribution: Proposition 1 gives a clever, effective lower bound derived from a novel inequality.  
- Well-written and clearly motivated presentation.  
- Core innovation replaces spectral estimation with a variational bound, sidestepping a key bottleneck in prior methods.  
- Synergy with scalable GP inference techniques suggests broader applicability beyond DPPs.  
- Concern 1: Computational advantage over [7] is not fully justified—avoids power iterations but introduces high-dimensional non-convex optimization whose cost scales with inducing set size.  
- Request to quantify trade-offs: per-iteration cost and convergence rate versus power iterations for given problem sizes and accuracies.  
- Concern 2: Convergence guarantee unclear as inducing set size increases; Equation 11 is highly non-convex and local minima may degrade bound tightness.  
- Authors should address risk of local minima, mitigation strategies (e.g., restarts, initialization), and conditions for global-optimum convergence.  
- Presentation fixes requested: add axis labels to Figure 1, move proof of Proposition 1 into main paper/appendix, and correct typo on Page 7 Line 361.  
- Overall: technically strong with significant potential, but must address computational and convergence concerns (especially for MCMC validity) before acceptance.
