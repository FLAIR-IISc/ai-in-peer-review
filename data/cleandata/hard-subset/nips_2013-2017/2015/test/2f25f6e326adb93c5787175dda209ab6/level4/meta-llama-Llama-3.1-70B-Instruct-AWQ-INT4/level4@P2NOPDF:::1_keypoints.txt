- DPPs model repulsion but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant summing over cardinalities and configurations.
- Prior work [7] estimated the spectrum and used incremental eigenvalue bounds for provably-correct retrospective MCMC of kernel parameters.
- This paper proposes an alternative bound for MCMC inference that avoids eigenvalue estimation.
- The approach enables variational learning and links to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian process inference.
- Paper is technically strong and well-written; Proposition 1’s lower bound is a central, clever insight that avoids estimation issues in [7].
- Concern: unclear if replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimizations of increasing dimension actually reduces computation.
- Concern: increasing cardinality may not guarantee convergence of the bounds because the coupled optimization is highly non-convex and may fail to reach a global minimum, potentially preventing MCMC steps.
- Presentation issues: figures need axis labels (e.g., Figure 1), the proof of the proposition should be moved to the main text, and there is a minor typo ("the variational lower is").
- Overall: a clever method for bounding the DPP partition function with potential for faster inference and connections to other models.
