- Addresses learning kernel parameters for DPPs by introducing a novel bound on the normalization constant that avoids eigenvalue estimation and enables MCMC and variational inference, with connections to inducing-point GPs.  
- Central contribution: an elegant inequality (Proposition 1) giving a lower bound on the partition function.  
- Method avoids eigenvalue estimation and naturally extends to scalable variational learning, linking to GP inducing-point methods.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-motivated, clearly written, and presents a significant contribution.  
- Concern: unclear computational advantage—replacing eigenvalue estimation with a sequence of non-linear optimizations of increasing dimensionality may not be cheaper; need explicit discussion of trade-offs.  
- Concern: convergence guarantees of the bounds are not established—incrementally increasing set cardinality may not ensure convergence due to non-convexity and risk of local minima, which could prevent valid MCMC steps.  
- Presentation: Figure 1 is missing axis labels.  
- Presentation: Proof of Proposition 1 should be moved to the main body or include a proof sketch given its centrality.  
- Presentation: Minor typo on page 7 ("the variational lower is ...").  
- Conclusion: Novel and valuable technique with important practical concerns about computational cost and convergence, but core ideas are significant.
