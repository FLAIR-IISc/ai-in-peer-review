- Paper proposes novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants to bound DPP likelihoods, avoiding expensive spectral decompositions.
- Main idea is simple, well executed, and paves the way for improvements.
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.
- Approach is general and potentially applicable beyond determinantal point processes.
- Experimental evaluation is well constructed and highlights strengths and limitations, including failure modes of a variational approximation.
- Theoretical results are clearly presented with solid derivations and links to GP variational inference.
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and uses consistent notation.
- Proposed bounds are original and address a significant computational bottleneck in DPP inference.
- Demonstrations on synthetic and real data support the method’s effectiveness and practicality.
- Limited discussion and lack of solutions regarding parameterizing K instead of L.
- Experimental comparisons could be more comprehensive against additional existing methods.
- Minor comment: prominence of K parameterization in the paper is unclear given it is not resolved.
- Minor comment: authors should provide a standard supplement rather than an extended paper version.
- Suggestion for future work: explore direct determinant bounds in terms of K and assess scalability with pseudo-input methods.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
