- Contribution: Proposes approximate DPP inference that avoids spectral kernel knowledge by using inducing inputs to bound the likelihood; presents variational and approximate MCMC approaches with empirical efficiency claims.  
- Presentation: Method description is somewhat superficial and needs more detail.  
- Empirical evaluation: Not sufficiently thorough; stronger experiments and comparisons required.  
- Clarification 1: Provide computational complexity and actual runtime comparisons between the two proposed methods.  
- Clarification 2: Compare approximation quality and runtime against other approximate and exact alternatives.  
- Clarification 3: Explain when and why variational inference succeeds or fails (Section 5.1 toy example) and conditions prompting the switch to approximate MCMC.  
- Clarification 4: Report performance when inducing inputs are exactly the 13 sampled points used in the toy example.  
- Clarification 5: Give practical guidelines for selecting the number of inducing points and their initial placement.  
- Minor: Define matrices K and L in the introduction (marginal kernel and L-ensembles) for readability.  
- Minor: Remove or reduce discussion about directly learning marginal kernel parameters since the paper does not adopt this approach.  
- Minor: Remove redundant sentence about inducing inputs being used to bound the DPP likelihood.  
- Overall: Paper has merit but requires substantial improvements to write-up and empirical validation for acceptance.
