- DPPs model repulsion via a PD kernel; learning kernel parameters is hard because the normalization constant is intractable.  
- Prior work [7] estimated the kernel spectrum and derived incremental bounds enabling provably correct retrospective MCMC.  
- This paper proposes an alternative bounding strategy for MCMC that avoids spectral estimation.  
- The method naturally supports variational inference and connects to GP inducing-point approaches for scalable inference.  
- The paper is technically sound, well-written, and a valuable contribution.  
- Proposition 1 relies on an elegant inequality that sidesteps the estimation difficulties of [7].  
- Concern: replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimizations of increasing dimension may not be a clear computational advantage and needs stronger justification.  
- Concern: convergence of bounds by increasing cardinality is unclear due to coupled, highly non-convex optimization and risk of getting stuck in local minima, which could block effective bounding and MCMC acceptance.  
- Presentation: add axis labels to Figure 1 (page 7).  
- Presentation: move the proof of Proposition 1 from the appendix into the main paper for visibility.  
- Presentation: fix incomplete phrase on page 7, line 361 (“… the variational lower is …”).  
- Overall: strong, clever technique with promising connections; addressing computational cost and convergence concerns would strengthen the contribution.
