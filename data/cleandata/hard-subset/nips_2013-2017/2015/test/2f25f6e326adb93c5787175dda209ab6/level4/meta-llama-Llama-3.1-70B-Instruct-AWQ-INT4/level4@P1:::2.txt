The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. The main idea is simple, but the paper is well executed and enlightening, and paves the way for several potential improvements.

The authors propose a creative use of the pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of a positive definite kernels. The proposed problem is timely, and will be of interest to several in the community. Such an approach is quite general with potential applications beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well constructed to elucidate several key points, including failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions. I found the selected application area interesting and highly relevant to the studied problem.

The paper provides a thorough introduction to determinantal point processes, and the authors do a good job of explaining the technical challenges involved in likelihood-based inference. The proposed bounds on the likelihood are derived in a clear and concise manner, and the connections to variational inference and Markov chain Monte Carlo methods are well explained. The experiments are well designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

However, I have some minor comments. It is unclear why the issue of parameterizing K (instead of L) features so prominently in the abstract and the rest of the paper, as the authors mainly focus on describing the problem, and make no efforts to address it. While the authors mention the potential drawbacks of parameterizing L directly, they do not provide a clear solution to this problem.

Additionally, if accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement, and not just upload an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

In terms of future work, I would like to see the authors consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix. What is the scalability of such an approach if using the pseudo-input method? This could potentially address the issue of parameterizing K instead of L, and provide a more interpretable model.

Overall, I think the paper is well written and provides a significant contribution to the field of determinantal point processes. With some minor revisions, I believe it has the potential to be a strong paper.
