The authors present novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood function of a determinantal point process. While the core idea is straightforward, the paper is well-written and provides valuable insights, opening avenues for further research. 

They demonstrate a creative application of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. This work addresses a timely problem and is likely to be of broad interest to the machine learning community. 

The proposed approach is quite general and holds potential for applications extending beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-designed and effectively illustrates key points, including limitations of a variational approximation under specific circumstances. I found the chosen application area to be both interesting and highly relevant to the problem under investigation.

Minor comments:
- The prominence given to the issue of parameterizing the kernel matrix *K* (rather than *L*) in the abstract and throughout the paper is unclear, as the authors primarily describe the problem without attempting to resolve this parameterization challenge.
- Should the paper be accepted, I recommend the authors prepare a standard supplementary material document, rather than simply uploading an extended version of the manuscript.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion regarding the interpretability of the *K* parameterization actually strengthens the appeal of this approach. Could the authors explore directly bounding the determinant in terms of the *K* matrix? Furthermore, what is the scalability of this method when employing pseudo-inputs?
