1. Need clearer analysis of trade-offs between variational inference and MCMC, including why VI can fail, computational complexity, performance guarantees, and guidance on method selection.

2. Experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to alternative DPP inference methods (e.g., Affandi et al. 2014 spectral bounds, Nyström), measured by approximation quality and wall-clock time.

3. More practical guidance required on inducing points: principled/heuristic choice of number m, sensitivity to placement and optimization, and experiments using observed data points as inducing points.

4. Introduction should more clearly define kernels K and L and their roles early to aid readers less familiar with DPPs.

5. Streamline discussion on parametrizing K versus L, since the paper ultimately parametrizes L and the K discussion is tangential.

6. Summary: the non-spectral likelihood bounds are promising, but the paper is weakened by insufficient empirical evaluation and lack of detailed practical/trade-off analysis; adding baselines and VI vs MCMC analysis would strengthen it.
