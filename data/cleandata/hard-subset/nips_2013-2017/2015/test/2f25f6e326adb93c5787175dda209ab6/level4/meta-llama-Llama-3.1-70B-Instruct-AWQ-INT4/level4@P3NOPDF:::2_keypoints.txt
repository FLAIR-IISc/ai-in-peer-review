- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant with applications to bounding determinantal point process (DPP) likelihoods.  
- Main idea is simple, well-executed, and enlightening, enabling several potential improvements.  
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive-definite kernels.  
- Approach is timely, quite general, and likely applicable beyond DPPs.  
- Experimental evaluation is well-constructed, illustrating failures of variational approximations and using a relevant application area.  
- Unclear why parameterizing K (instead of L) is emphasized; paper describes the issue but does not address it—needs clearer explanation or deeper discussion.  
- If accepted, authors should provide a standard supplement rather than an extended paper uploaded as the supplement.  
- Suggested future work: explore interpretability of the K parameterization and derive bounds directly in terms of K.  
- Suggested future work: investigate scalability of the pseudo-input method.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution that, with minor revisions and further exploration, could significantly impact the field.
