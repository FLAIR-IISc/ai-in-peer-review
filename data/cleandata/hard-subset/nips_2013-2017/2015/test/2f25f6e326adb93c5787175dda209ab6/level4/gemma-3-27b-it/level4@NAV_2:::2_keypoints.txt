- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants to bound DPP likelihoods.
- Main idea is simple, well executed, and opens avenues for further improvements.
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.
- Approach is timely, of broad interest, and potentially applicable beyond DPPs.
- Experimental evaluation is well-constructed and highlights key behaviors and limitations.
- Experiments effectively show limitations of a variational approximation under certain conditions.
- Application area is relevant and well chosen for the problem studied.
- Overemphasis on parameterizing K vs. L is unclear, since the paper describes the issue but does not resolve it.
- Recommend providing a dedicated supplementary document rather than an extended paper version if accepted.
- Suggested future work: derive determinant bounds directly in terms of K and test scalability of the pseudo-input method.
- Interpreting the K parameterization motivates further investigation into the proposed approach.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses avoiding spectral decomposition, provides novel and seemingly efficient bounds, and pairs theory with synthetic and real-data validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: does not offer a complete parameter inference solution (K vs. L issue) and experimental comparisons are limited beyond reference [7].
