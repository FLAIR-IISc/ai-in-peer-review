- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants, applied to bounding determinantal point process (DPP) likelihoods.
- Main idea is straightforward but well-executed, enlightening, and enables potential future improvements.
- Introduces a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.
- Approach is general and potentially applicable beyond DPPs.
- Experimental evaluation is well-constructed and effectively highlights several key phenomena.
- Experiments reveal failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions.
- Chosen application area is interesting and highly relevant to the problem.
- Minor concern: emphasis on parameterizing K (instead of L) is unclear since the paper mainly describes the problem rather than solving it.
- Minor suggestion: submit a standard supplement rather than an extended paper as the supplement.
- Future work: explore interpretability and direct determinant bounds in terms of K.
- Future work: evaluate the scalability of the pseudo-input method.
