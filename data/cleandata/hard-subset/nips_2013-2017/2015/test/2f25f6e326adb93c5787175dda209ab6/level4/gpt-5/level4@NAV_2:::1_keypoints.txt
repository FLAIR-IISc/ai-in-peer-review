- Paper introduces alternative bounds for DPP partition functions that avoid eigenvalue estimation, support variational learning, and connect to inducing-point GP techniques.  
- Manuscript is technically solid and clearly written.  
- The determinant inequality used in Proposition 1 is novel and a strong fit for this problem.  
- The approach cleverly sidesteps the spectral-estimation difficulties of prior work.  
- Parallels to GP inducing points and the potential for variational inference are valuable.  
- Technical concern 1: it’s unclear that replacing power iterations with a sequence of nonlinear optimization problems of increasing size is a clear computational win.  
- Technical concern 2: increasing the number of inducing points may not guarantee bound convergence because the coupled, highly nonconvex optimization over inducing locations Z and kernel parameters can get stuck in poor local minima, potentially preventing interval shrinkage needed for retrospective MH decisions — authors should discuss this failure mode and practical safeguards.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 (P7) needs axis labels.  
- Presentation issue: the proof of the key proposition should be moved into the main text rather than only the appendix.  
- Presentation issue: typo on P7, L361 (“… the variational lower is …”).  
- Overall verdict: a nice paper leveraging a neat determinant inequality to enable potentially faster inference and useful connections to other frameworks.
