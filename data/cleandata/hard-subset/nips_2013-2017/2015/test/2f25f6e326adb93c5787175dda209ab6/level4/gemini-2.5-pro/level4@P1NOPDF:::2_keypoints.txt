- Introduces novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant and applies them to bounding determinantal point process (DPP) likelihoods.
- Core idea is straightforward but well-executed, insightful, and opens avenues for future work.
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels is a key contribution.
- Problem is timely and likely of broad interest to the machine learning community.
- Proposed approach is general and has clear potential applications beyond DPPs.
- Experimental evaluation is well-designed and convincingly demonstrates key points, including failure modes of variational approximations.
- Chosen application is interesting and highly relevant to the paper’s aims.
- Minor concern: unclear why parameterizing K (vs its Cholesky factor L) is emphasized when the main text focuses on the bounding technique rather than this issue.
- Minor suggestion: if accepted, provide a standard supplement rather than an extended main paper.
- Future work suggestion: adapt the method to bound determinants directly in terms of K (not its factors) and analyze scalability when combined with pseudo-inputs.
