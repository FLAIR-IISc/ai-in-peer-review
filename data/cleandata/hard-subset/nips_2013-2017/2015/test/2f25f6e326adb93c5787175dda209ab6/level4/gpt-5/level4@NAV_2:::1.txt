Determinantal point processes (DPPs) have seen increased attention in machine learning because they naturally model repulsion among points within a realization. This repulsion is encoded by a positive definite kernel. A core challenge is learning kernel parameters from observed point configurations. Likelihood-based estimation is hard due to an intractable normalizing constant that aggregates over all cardinalities and configurations. Prior work [7] addressed this by estimating parts of the spectrum to obtain incremental upper and lower bounds, enabling a provably correct “retrospective” MCMC scheme for kernel-parameter inference. The current paper introduces an alternative class of bounds that avoid eigenvalue estimation entirely. These bounds also support a variational learning approach and connect neatly to inducing-point techniques used for scalable Gaussian process inference.

This paper is technically solid and clearly written. The determinant inequality underpinning the lower bound in Proposition 1 was new to me and appears to be a very apt tool for this setting. It provides a clever way to sidestep the spectral estimation difficulties in [7]. I especially appreciate the parallels to GP inducing points and the resulting possibility of variational inference. Overall, the paper was a pleasure to read.

I have two technical reservations I would like the authors to address. First, although I like the idea, I am not fully persuaded that replacing power iterations with a sequence of nonlinear optimization problems of increasing size is necessarily a major computational win. Second, it is not obvious that simply increasing the number of inducing points guarantees convergence of the bounds, given the coupled optimization over Z and kernel parameters. The objective appears highly non-convex, so one would need both to increase cardinality and to find global optima. A poor local minimum could in principle keep the interval from shrinking past the Bernoulli threshold in retrospective MH, potentially preventing an acceptance/rejection decision. Please discuss this failure mode and any practical safeguards.
