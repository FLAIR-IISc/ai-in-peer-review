- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate nonspectral bounds on determinants for DPP likelihoods (finite and continuous).
- Demonstrates using these bounds to infer DPP parameters and applies them to expensive-but-exact MCMC and cheap-but-approximate variational inference.
- Provides a clear, concise summary and situates the work relative to prior NIPS and related literature.
- Explains technical derivations of the bounds and their application in variational inference and MCMC thoroughly.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written for readers familiar with DPPs.
- Strengths: novel approach, thorough technical detail, and comprehensive empirical evaluation.
- Presents a detailed analysis of computational complexity and overall compute time, aiding assessment of practicality.
- Includes useful comparisons with both approximate and exact alternative methods.
- Weakness: lacks an in-depth discussion of when the proposed variational inference will succeed or fail.
- Minor clarity issues: the introduction should briefly explain the marginal kernel and L-ensembles.
- Suggest trimming excess discussion on advantages of directly learning marginal kernel parameters as it adds little.
- Overall assessment: paper has merit and potential as a valuable contribution with minor revisions.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
