- Novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant are proposed, applied to bounding DPP likelihoods.  
- Core idea is simple, elegantly executed, and offers insightful contributions with promising future directions.  
- Creative application of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.  
- Timely work addressing a meaningful gap that will interest multiple researchers.  
- Approach is general and likely extends beyond DPPs to other kernel-based methods.  
- Experiments are well-designed, clearly illustrate key insights (including where variational approximations fail), and use a compelling application area.  
- Minor: Emphasis on parameterizing K (vs. Cholesky L) is highlighted but not explained; the distinction’s importance is unclear.  
- Minor: If accepted, supply a standard supplementary materials package rather than only an extended paper version.  
- Future work: Explore bounding the determinant directly in terms of K and analyze computational scalability when using pseudo-inputs.
