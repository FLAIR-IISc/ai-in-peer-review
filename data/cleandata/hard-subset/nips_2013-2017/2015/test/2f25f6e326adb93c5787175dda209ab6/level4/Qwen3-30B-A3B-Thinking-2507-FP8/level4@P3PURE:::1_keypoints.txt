- Paper addresses learning DPP kernel parameters despite an intractable normalization constant.  
- Prior work [7] used spectral estimation and retrospective MCMC with eigenvalue bounds.  
- Proposed alternative bound avoids eigenvalue estimation and supports variational learning.  
- Draws compelling connections to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian process inference.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-written, and presents a clever solution.  
- Proposition 1’s inequality is a central, elegant technical insight that circumvents spectral estimation challenges.  
- Extension to variational inference is an excellent contribution.  
- Concern: Unclear computational advantage of replacing power iterations with increasing-dimensional nonlinear optimization.  
- Concern: Unclear convergence guarantees for bounds as cardinality increases; coupled non-convex optimization may get stuck in local minima and block MCMC.  
- Suggested remedies: provide guarantees or strategies (e.g., multiple restarts, convex relaxations) to avoid local minima.  
- Presentation issue: Add axis labels to all plots in Figure 1.  
- Presentation issue: Move the proof of Proposition 1 from the appendix into the main body.  
- Presentation issue: Fix typo on page 7, line 361 (“the variational lower is” → “the variational lower bound is”).  
- Overall: High-quality paper with significant merit and promising ML connections; addressing concerns would substantially strengthen it.
