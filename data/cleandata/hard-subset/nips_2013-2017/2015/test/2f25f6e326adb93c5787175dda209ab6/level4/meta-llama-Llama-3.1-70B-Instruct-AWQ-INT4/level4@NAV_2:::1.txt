Determinantal point processes (DPPs) have been receiving significant attention in the machine learning literature due to their ability to model repulsion within realizations of a set of points, specified via a positive definite kernel function. However, learning the appropriate parameters of this kernel, given example point realizations, poses a challenge due to a difficult-to-compute normalization constant.

The authors address this challenge by proposing novel, cheap-to-evaluate, nonspectral bounds on the determinants arising in the likelihoods of DPPs, both finite and continuous. These bounds can be used for expensive-but-exact Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) and cheap-but-approximate variational inference. The paper draws connections to inducing-point methods for scalable inference in Gaussian process models.

The proposed approach is technically strong and well-written, leveraging an excellent inequality that provides the lower bound in Proposition 1. This inequality cleverly avoids the estimation issues present in previous work, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

The connections to GP inducing points and the ability to perform variational inference are notable strengths of the paper. However, two technical concerns need to be addressed. Firstly, while the approach is a significant improvement, it is unclear whether replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization problems of increasing dimension results in a substantial computational win.

Secondly, the coupled optimization problem raises concerns about convergence. Increasing the cardinality of the bounds may not guarantee convergence due to the non-convex nature of the optimization problem. Ensuring a global minimum is achieved is crucial to prevent local minima from hindering the procedure's ability to bound away the Bernoulli threshold and take an MCMC step.

To improve the presentation, including axis labels for the figures on page 7 would enhance clarity. Additionally, considering the central insight of the paper, it would be beneficial to include the proof of the proposition in the main body of the paper rather than the supplementary material.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field, offering a clever trick to bound the partition function of a DPP, leading to potentially faster inference and connections to other kinds of models.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors carefully evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of their approach and discuss its limitations.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach and its connections to related work. However, some minor improvements to the presentation, such as adding axis labels to figures, would enhance clarity.

Originality: The paper presents a novel approach to bounding the determinants arising in the likelihoods of DPPs, which is a significant contribution to the field. The connections to GP inducing points and variational inference are also original and valuable.

Significance: The paper addresses an important challenge in the field of DPPs and provides a potentially faster and more efficient approach to inference. The results have implications for various applications, including machine learning and statistics.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and technically strong approach to bounding the determinants arising in the likelihoods of DPPs.
* The connections to GP inducing points and variational inference are valuable and original.
* The paper addresses an important challenge in the field and provides a potentially faster and more efficient approach to inference.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The computational win of replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization problems of increasing dimension is unclear.
* The coupled optimization problem raises concerns about convergence, and ensuring a global minimum is achieved is crucial to prevent local minima from hindering the procedure's ability to bound away the Bernoulli threshold and take an MCMC step.
