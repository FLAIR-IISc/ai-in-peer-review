The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. The main idea is simple, but the paper is well executed and enlightening, and paves the way for several potential improvements.

The authors propose a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. The problem addressed is timely and likely to be of interest to researchers in the community. This approach is quite general with potential applications extending beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-constructed, effectively illustrating key points, including the limitations of a variational approximation under certain conditions. I found the chosen application area interesting and highly relevant to the studied problem. 

A minor point is that the prominence given to the issue of parameterizing K (instead of L) in the abstract and throughout the paper is somewhat unclear, as the authors primarily describe the problem without attempting to address it. If accepted, I suggest the authors prepare a dedicated supplementary document rather than simply uploading an extended version of the paper. 

Suggestions for future work include investigating whether the determinant can be directly bounded in terms of the K matrix, and assessing the scalability of such an approach when using the pseudo-input method. The discussion regarding the interpretability of the K parameterization makes exploring this direction particularly appealing.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The work addresses a well-defined and practical challenge in DPP inference – avoiding spectral decomposition.
*   The proposed bounds are novel and appear to be computationally efficient.
*   The theoretical results are supported by experimental validation on both synthetic and real-world data.
*   The paper is clearly written and well-organized, demonstrating a strong understanding of the related literature.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The paper primarily focuses on bounding the likelihood and does not offer a complete solution for parameter inference, particularly addressing the K vs. L parameterization issue.
*   The experimental section, while thorough, could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods beyond [7].
