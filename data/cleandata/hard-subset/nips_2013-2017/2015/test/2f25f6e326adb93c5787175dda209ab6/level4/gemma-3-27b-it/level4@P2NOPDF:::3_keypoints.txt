- Paper proposes approximate DPP inference that avoids spectral decomposition by using inducing inputs to bound the likelihood, with both VI and approximate MCMC implementations.  
- Methodological descriptions are insufficiently detailed and need clarification.  
- Empirical evaluation is not comprehensive enough to assess the contributions.  
- Require comparative analysis of computational complexity and runtime between the proposed VI and MCMC methods.  
- Need stronger comparisons to existing exact and approximate DPP inference techniques on both approximation quality and computation time.  
- VI shows substantial parameter estimation error in experiments, forcing reliance on the costlier approximate MCMC.  
- Authors should delineate when VI is expected to work or fail and provide theoretical or intuitive explanations for these limitations.  
- Evaluate performance when using the observed sampled points as inducing inputs as a baseline inducing-point strategy.  
- Provide practical guidance (rules of thumb/heuristics) for selecting the number and initial placement of inducing inputs.  
- Minor clarity fixes: define matrices K and L up front and briefly introduce marginal kernel and L-ensembles for accessibility.  
- The discussion on directly learning marginal-kernel parameters is not implemented and should be removed or actually implemented to avoid distraction.  
- Overall: approach is promising but requires substantial refinement in methodological clarity and more thorough, comparative empirical validation.
