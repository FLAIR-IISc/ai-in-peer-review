- Paper proposes inducing-input-based approximate inference for DPPs that avoids spectral knowledge of the kernel, with both variational inference and approximate MCMC described and shown empirically to be efficient.

- Reviewer believes the paper has merit but finds the method description rough and the empirical evaluation insufficiently thorough.

- Request a direct comparison of computational complexity and wall-clock runtime between the proposed variational and approximate MCMC methods.

- Request comparison against other baselines (both approximate and exact) on approximation quality and computation time to clarify strengths and limitations.

- Notes variational inference shows large parameter estimation error on the toy data and asks for analysis of when variational inference is expected to succeed or fail, with underlying reasons.

- Asks for analysis of method performance when inducing inputs are chosen to be the actual sampled points (13-point toy sample).

- Requests practical rules of thumb for choosing (a) the number of inducing points and (b) placement/initialization of inducing points.

- Minor: Matrices K and L are used without explanation; briefly define marginal kernel and L-ensembles for uninformed readers.

- Minor: Repeated discussion about advantages of learning marginal kernel parameters should be trimmed if that approach is not pursued.

- Strong recommendation to improve the write-up and expand the empirical evaluation and methodological detail if the paper is accepted.
