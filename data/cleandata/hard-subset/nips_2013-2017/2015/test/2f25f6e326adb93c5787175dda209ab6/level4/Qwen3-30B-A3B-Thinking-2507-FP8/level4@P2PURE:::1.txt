

Determinantal point processes (DPPs) have garnered significant recent attention in machine learning for their ability to model repulsion among point sets. This repulsion is specified via a positive definite kernel function. A key challenge lies in learning the kernel parameters from example point realizations, as estimation is challenging due to an intractable normalization constant that sums over all possible cardinalities and configurations. A recent approach [7] addresses this by estimating the kernel spectrum to derive incremental upper and lower bounds, enabling a provably correct retrospective Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) scheme for kernel parameter inference.  

The present paper proposes an alternative bound for MCMC inference that avoids eigenvalue estimation. This approach also facilitates a variational learning method, with the paper drawing meaningful connections to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian process inference. The paper is technically strong, well-written, and presents a compelling solution. I was unaware of the inequality establishing the lower bound in Proposition 1, which appears exceptionally well-suited to this problem and cleverly circumvents the estimation challenges in [7]. The connections to Gaussian process inducing points and the enabling of variational inference are particularly compelling. Overall, this is a pleasure to read.  

I have two technical concerns requiring clarification:  
1. While I appreciate the approach, I question whether replacing power iterations with higher-dimensional nonlinear optimization problems constitutes a significant computational advantage.  
2. It is unclear whether simply increasing cardinality guarantees convergence of the bounds, given the coupled optimization problem. Given the likely non-convexity of this optimization, achieving a global minimum must be ensured alongside cardinality increases. Failure to do so could trap the procedure at a local minimum, preventing bounds from being established away from the Bernoulli threshold and thereby blocking MCMC steps. This must be explicitly addressed.  

Presentation issues:  
- P7 Figure 1: Include axis labels.  
- The proof of Proposition 1 is the central insight enabling this work; it should appear in the main body, not the appendix.  
- P7 L361: "... the variational lower bound is ..."  

This is a well-executed paper that cleverly bounds the DPP partition function, enabling faster inference and connections to broader modeling frameworks.
