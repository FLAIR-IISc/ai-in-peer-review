This paper proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process (DPP). The main idea is simple, yet the paper is well executed and illuminating, and it paves the way for several potential improvements. The authors make a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of a positive definite kernel. The problem is timely and should be of interest to many in the community. The approach is quite general and has potential applications beyond DPPs. The experimental evaluation is well designed to elucidate key points, including failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions. I found the chosen application area interesting and highly relevant to the problem studied.

Minor comments:
- It is unclear why parameterizing K (instead of L) features so prominently in the abstract and throughout the paper, as the authors primarily describe the issue but make no attempt to address it.
- If accepted, I suggest that the authors provide a standard supplementary document rather than uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion of the interpretability of the K parameterization makes this direction particularly appealing. Could the authors consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? What would be the scalability of such an approach when using the pseudo-input method?
