- Tackles DPP inference bottleneck by proposing likelihood bounds that avoid expensive spectral decompositions using inducing inputs.  
- Core methods enable both variational inference and MCMC by approximating the DPP likelihood with inducing points.  
- Well-motivated situating within DPP literature and clearly connects to variational inference for Gaussian Processes for intuition.  
- Main technical contribution: derivation of tractable bounds on the DPP likelihood that scale to larger datasets.  
- Empirical validation shows feasibility but is limited in scope and not fully convincing.  
- Methodological description is terse and lacks detail needed for straightforward implementation and replication.  
- Needs detailed computational complexity and runtime analysis (comparisons versus methods like power iteration).  
- Requires more thorough comparisons to alternative DPP inference/approximation techniques for accuracy and cost trade-offs.  
- Variational inference performed poorly in experiments; authors should clarify when VI is expected to work and discuss sensitivity to initialization and hyperparameters.  
- Inducing point placement is crucial but guidance is insufficient; recommend heuristics, initialization strategies, and experiments with data-selected inducing points.  
- Initial explanation of kernels K and L is brief; add an intuitive description of their roles in DPPs for clarity.  
- Discussion on parametrizing K vs L is repetitive and should be streamlined to focus on core contributions.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending substantial revisions—add methodological detail, broaden empirical evaluation and comparisons, analyze VI limitations, and provide practical inducing-point guidance.
