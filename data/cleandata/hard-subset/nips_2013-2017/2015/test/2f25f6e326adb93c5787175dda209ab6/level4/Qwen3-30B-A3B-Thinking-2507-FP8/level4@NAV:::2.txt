

The authors present novel upper and lower bounds for matrix determinants, with applications to bounding the likelihood in determinantal point processes. The core idea is straightforward, yet the paper is meticulously executed, insightful, and opens avenues for multiple future improvements.  

The authors introduce a novel application of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. The problem is timely and holds significant interest for the broader community. This approach exhibits strong generality, suggesting applicability far beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental design effectively demonstrates key insights, including limitations of variational approximations under specific conditions. The chosen application area is both compelling and highly pertinent to the theoretical contributions.  

Minor comments:  
- The prominence of K (vs. L) parameterization in the abstract and main text appears disproportionate to the paper's focus, which centers on problem formulation without addressing this aspect.  
- If accepted, the authors should provide a standard supplement rather than uploading an extended version of the manuscript.  

Suggestions for future work:  
- The interpretability discussion of K parameterization enhances the appeal of the approach. Could the authors explore bounding the determinant directly using the K matrix? Additionally, what are the scalability implications of this method when combined with pseudo-inputs?
