- Introduces inducing-point-based approximate DPP inference that avoids spectral decomposition, using variational inference and approximate MCMC with empirical efficiency claims.
- Paper is promising but unclear in methodological description.
- Empirical evaluation is insufficiently thorough.
- Provide direct comparison of computational complexity and empirical runtimes between the variational and MCMC methods.
- Compare against other approximate and exact DPP inference methods on both approximation quality and computation time.
- Analyze when the variational approach is expected to work or fail, given observed high parameter estimation error in the toy example.
- In the toy example, evaluate performance when the 13 sampled points are used as the inducing points.
- Provide practical guidance for selecting inducing points: recommendations for number and initial placement.
- Define matrices K and L (marginal kernel and L-ensemble) when first mentioned for reader clarity.
- Streamline or remove the discussion on directly learning marginal kernel parameters since that approach is not pursued.
