- Paper is technically strong and well-written and tackles learning kernel parameters for DPPs despite an intractable partition function.  
- Prior method [7] used kernel spectrum estimates to form incremental bounds for retrospective MCMC.  
- The paper proposes an alternative bounding strategy that avoids estimating eigenvalues while enabling similar MCMC inference.  
- The approach naturally facilitates a variational inference framework and draws connections to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian processes.  
- Proposition 1’s inequality is a central, elegant insight that circumvents the estimation challenges in [7].  
- Concern: The claimed computational advantage is not fully convincing—replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization problems of increasing dimensionality may not yield a net efficiency gain and requires further discussion.  
- Concern: Increasing the inducing set size may not guarantee bounds convergence because the required non-convex optimizations can get stuck in local minima, potentially failing the Bernoulli test and stalling the MCMC.  
- Presentation: Figure 1 (page 7) is missing axis labels.  
- Presentation: The proof of Proposition 1 should be moved from the appendix into the main paper.  
- Presentation: Typo on page 7, line 361 ("... the variational lower is ...").
