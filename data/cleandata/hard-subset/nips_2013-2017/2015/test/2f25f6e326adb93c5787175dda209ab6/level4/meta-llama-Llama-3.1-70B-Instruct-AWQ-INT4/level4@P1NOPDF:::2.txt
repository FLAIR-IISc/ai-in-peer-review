The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. The core concept is straightforward, yet the paper is well executed, enlightening, and lays the groundwork for several potential improvements.

The authors introduce a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of a positive definite kernel, a timely problem that will likely be of interest to several members of the community. This approach is quite general, with potential applications extending beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well constructed, effectively highlighting several key points, including the failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions. I found the selected application area to be interesting and highly relevant to the studied problem.

Minor comments:
- The emphasis on parameterizing K (instead of L) in the abstract and the rest of the paper is unclear, as the authors primarily focus on describing the problem without making any attempts to address it.
- If accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement, rather than simply uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion of the interpretability of the K parameterization makes this approach even more appealing. Could the authors consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? What is the scalability of such an approach when using the pseudo-input method?
