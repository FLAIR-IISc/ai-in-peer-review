- DPPs model repulsion via a positive definite kernel, but learning kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable normalization constant.
- The paper proposes novel bounds on the DPP likelihood for both finite and continuous domains.
- The proposed bounds avoid approximating the spectrum of large matrices or operators, making evaluation cheaper.
- The method is inspired by variational bounds for Gaussian processes and extends inducing-point ideas to continuous DPPs.
- The approach enables variational inference for DPPs, which is an added advantage.
- The paper is technically strong, well-written, and draws clear connections to scalable GP inference.
- Concern: unclear whether replacing power iterations with increasingly large nonlinear optimization is a real computational win.
- Concern: increasing bound cardinality may not guarantee convergence because the coupled optimization is highly non-convex and may get stuck in local minima.
- Suggestion: add axis labels to the figures on page 7 to improve clarity.
- Suggestion: move the proof of the central proposition from the supplementary material into the main paper.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, significant contribution; technical strength; clear GP/inducing-point connections; supports variational inference.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear computational benefits; lack of guaranteed convergence due to non-convex optimization; presentation could be improved.
- Reviewer rating: 8/10, confidence: 8/10.
