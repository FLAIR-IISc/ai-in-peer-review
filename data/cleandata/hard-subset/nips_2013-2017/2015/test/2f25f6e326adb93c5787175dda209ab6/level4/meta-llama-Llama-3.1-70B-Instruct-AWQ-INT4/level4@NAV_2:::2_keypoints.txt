- Novel bounds: Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants for bounding DPP likelihoods.  
- Simplicity and execution: Main idea is simple, well executed, and enlightening.  
- Pseudo-inputs use: Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.  
- Generality: Approach is general and potentially applicable beyond determinantal point processes.  
- Experimental evaluation: Experiments are well designed and highlight key behaviors, including variational approximation failures under certain conditions.  
- Technical quality: Paper is technically sound with clear derivations of the bounds.  
- Clarity: Well written, easy to follow, consistent notation, and clear figures.  
- Originality: Approach is novel and timely for the community.  
- Significance: Potentially significant applications beyond DPPs; application choice is relevant.  
- Minor concern — K vs L: Unclear why parameterizing K (instead of L) is emphasized despite not being addressed.  
- Minor suggestion — supplement: Recommend preparing a standard supplementary material rather than an extended paper as the supplement.  
- Future work suggestion — K-based bounds: Consider developing bounds directly in terms of the K matrix and discuss interpretability.  
- Future work suggestion — scalability: Need clarity on scalability of the pseudo-input method for larger problems.  
- Acceptance pro: Novelty, strong experiments, and relevant application motivate acceptance.  
- Acceptance con: Lack of treatment of K vs L parameterization and unclear scalability argue against acceptance.
