The paper introduces new upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants, with applications to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes. The central idea is straightforward, but the work is well executed and illuminating, and it opens the door to several potential refinements.

The authors make a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. The problem addressed is timely and will be of interest to many in the community.

The approach appears quite general, with potential uses beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental study is thoughtfully designed to clarify several key aspects, including cases where a variational approximation fails under certain conditions. I found the chosen application area engaging and highly pertinent to the problem at hand.

Minor comments:
- It is unclear why the emphasis on parameterizing K (instead of L) features so prominently in the abstract and throughout the paper, given that the work mainly describes the issue without attempting to tackle it.
- If accepted, I recommend that the authors provide a conventional supplementary document, rather than simply uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- Conversely, the discussion on the interpretability of the K parameterization makes that direction particularly attractive. Could the authors consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? How would such an approach scale when using the pseudo-input method?
