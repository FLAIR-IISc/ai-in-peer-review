- Proposes nonspectral upper and lower determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods using inducing points and low-rank proxies, avoiding eigenvalue computations.  
- Extends bounds to continuous DPPs via Fredholm determinant bounds using LZ and Ψ, tractable for Gaussian kernels with Gaussian base measures.  
- Derives a variational lower bound for approximate MLE and a retrospective Metropolis–Hastings sampler that tightens bounds by increasing |Z|.  
- Positions method as an alternative to spectral approaches, reducing memory/time costs and enabling continuous-domain inference.  
- Builds on sparse GP inducing-point ideas and adapts them to DPP likelihoods in a novel way.  
- Finite-domain (Proposition 1) and continuous-domain (Proposition 2) bounds follow from a determinant inequality; proofs deferred to supplement.  
- Computational complexity is attractive: variational objective evaluates in O(n m^2) with m inducing points.  
- Experiments: 1D Gaussian–Gaussian validates retrospective MH against exact MH and highlights identifiability issues under L-parameterization.  
- Real-data spatial nerve fiber study replicates prior findings and shows monotone tightening of bounds as m increases.  
- Suggested improvements: compare to spectral approximations in finite domains (accuracy and wall-clock), provide guidance on initializing/optimizing Z, and discuss theoretical conditions for tightness/monotonicity with m.  
- Clarity: generally well written; recommend moving K vs L parameterization emphasis to limitations or adding experiments, and state Proposition 2 assumptions more prominently.  
- Request to provide a standard supplementary document with full proofs and experimental details if accepted.  
- Originality: solid novelty in inducing-point determinant bounds for DPPs and adapting retrospective MH to nonspectral bounds.  
- Significance: practically useful for settings where spectra are infeasible, offering tools for both approximate and exact inference and impacting other log-determinant problems.  
- Strengths: nonspectral bounds for finite/continuous DPPs, clear GP connection, practical algorithms, thoughtful experiments, and optimized inducing inputs.  
- Weaknesses: overemphasis on K vs L without resolution, limited empirical comparison to spectral baselines, insufficient discussion of Z sensitivity and m-selection, and unclear Ψ tractability beyond Gaussian–Gaussian or in higher dimensions.  
- Reproducibility concern: request to release code and scripts for finite and continuous cases.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a bottleneck in DPP learning, demonstrates both inference modes, extends to continuous DPPs, and provides credible empirical validation.  
- Cons for acceptance: lacks direct empirical comparison to spectral baselines, K vs L discussion lacks technical contribution, and theoretical tightness/monotonicity with m is not formalized.  
- Questions for authors: conditions for monotone bound tightening with m/Z optimization; possibility of bounding det(I−K) for K-parameterization; sensitivity and heuristics for Z initialization; strategies for computing Ψ in non-Gaussian/high-dimensional settings.  
- Overall: well-executed, practical, and likely acceptable pending clarifications on assumptions, K vs L emphasis, and an empirical spectral baseline comparison.
