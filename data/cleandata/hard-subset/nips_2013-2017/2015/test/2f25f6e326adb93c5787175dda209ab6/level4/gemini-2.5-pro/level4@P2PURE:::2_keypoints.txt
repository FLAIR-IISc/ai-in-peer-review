- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, applied to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs).  
- Central idea: creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels—simple, enlightening, and core to the contribution.  
- Timely and of broad interest to the community.  
- Well-executed method that is general and has potential applications beyond DPPs.  
- Experimental evaluation is thoughtful and elucidates key properties of the bounds.  
- Experiments identify conditions under which a competing variational approximation fails.  
- Chosen application (DPP likelihood) is interesting and highly relevant.  
- Minor concern: paper emphasizes K-parameterization vs Cholesky L, but it’s unclear why since the method doesn’t address that challenge.  
- Minor editorial suggestion: prepare a standard supplement if the paper is accepted rather than an extended main paper.  
- Suggested future work: adapt the pseudo-input method to bound the determinant directly in terms of K and analyze the scalability of that approach.
