- Proposes novel non-spectral bounds on DPP likelihoods that avoid expensive spectral decompositions.
- Leverages the connection between DPPs and Gaussian processes, adapting variational/inducing-point techniques from GP literature.
- Key contribution: likelihood bounds that depend only on a set of pseudo-inputs (inducing points), enabling large-scale applicability.
- Theoretical development is sound with careful derivations, proofs, and relevant literature grounding.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, moving logically from finite to continuous domains.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic and real-world datasets (including diabetic neuropathy) validate practical relevance.
- Shows computational efficiency gains while maintaining accuracy; variational bounds converge to true likelihood as pseudo-inputs increase.
- Provides clear comparisons to existing spectral approaches and demonstrates computational advantages.
- Originality: meaningful advancement by applying GP techniques to DPPs and removing spectral bottleneck.
- Significance: broadens applicability of DPPs for large-scale problems and spatial-pattern modeling.
- Minor weakness: emphasizes interpretability issues of parametrizing L instead of K but lacks theoretical or empirical analysis of this effect.
- Minor weakness: does not provide detailed computational complexity comparison versus spectral-based methods.
- Minor weakness: some experimental findings (e.g., variational approach's poor consistency) require deeper analysis.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address interpretability discussion and provide more detailed computational complexity analysis.
