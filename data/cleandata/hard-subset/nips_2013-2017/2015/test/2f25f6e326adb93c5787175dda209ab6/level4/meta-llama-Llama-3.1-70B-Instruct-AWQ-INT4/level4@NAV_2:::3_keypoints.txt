- Novel approach: inference for DPPs that avoids requiring spectral knowledge of the kernel.  
- Main contribution: derived cheap-to-evaluate likelihood bounds for finite and continuous DPPs usable in variational inference and MCMC.  
- Motivation and problem statement are clear and well-presented.  
- Literature review is thorough and contributions are clearly stated.  
- Technical content is sound and proofs are provided in the supplementary material.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow.  
- Method uses inducing inputs to derive bounds and applies to both VI and MCMC.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real data are convincing and show effective parameter estimation.  
- Variational inference sometimes yields large parameter estimation error on toy data, leading authors to use approximate MCMC.  
- Introduction should briefly define the matrices K and L, which are referenced without explanation.  
- Some discussion (advantages of directly learning marginal kernel parameters) is unnecessary and could be shortened.  
- Method description lacks detail, particularly about computational complexity and overall runtime comparison of the two proposed methods.  
- Empirical evaluation lacks thorough comparisons to other approximate and exact alternatives in quality and computation time.  
- Missing guidance on selecting the number and initial placement of inducing points (rule of thumb requested).  
- Authors do not clearly state when the proposed variational inference is expected to succeed or fail.  
- Overall assessment: paper has merit and impact but needs methodological and empirical clarifications; recommendation: accept with minor revisions (scores ~7–8/10).
