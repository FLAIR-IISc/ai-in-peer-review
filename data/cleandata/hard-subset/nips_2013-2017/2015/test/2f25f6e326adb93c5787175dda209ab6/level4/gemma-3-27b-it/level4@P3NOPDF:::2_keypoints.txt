- Presents novel upper and lower bounds for the matrix determinant with a compelling application to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs).
- Core idea is simple and well-executed, offering groundwork for significant future advances.
- Tackles a timely problem of broad interest to researchers in Gaussian processes and kernel methods.
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels; method appears generalizable beyond DPPs.
- Experimental evaluation is well-designed and effectively demonstrates where variational approximations can fail.
- Paper is well-written; mathematical derivations are clear and the intuition is communicated accessibly.
- Repeated emphasis on parameterizing K (vs. its Cholesky L) feels misplaced: the issue is raised but no solution or deeper investigation is provided.
- Strong recommendation to supply a dedicated supplementary material in standard NIPS format rather than an extended paper PDF.
- Suggests further exploration of directly bounding the determinant in terms of K to improve interpretability.
- Calls for investigation of scalability and computational complexity/optimizations of the pseudo-input method as K grows.
- Overall: strong, valuable theoretical contribution with convincing experiments; recommend acceptance.
