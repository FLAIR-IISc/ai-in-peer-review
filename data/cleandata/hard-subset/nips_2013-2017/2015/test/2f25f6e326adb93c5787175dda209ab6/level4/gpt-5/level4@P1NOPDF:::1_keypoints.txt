- DPPs model repulsion via a kernel; learning kernel parameters is hard because the partition function is intractable.
- Prior work [7] estimates the spectrum to get incremental bounds and a retrospective MCMC for kernel parameters.
- This paper proposes bounds that avoid eigenvalue estimation, supporting MCMC without spectral estimates.
- The approach naturally yields a variational learning method and connects to inducing-point techniques for scalable GP inference.
- The paper is technically strong, clearly written, and uses a clever inequality (Proposition 1) central to the contribution.
- Concern 1: Unclear if replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimension is a true computational win.
- Concern 2: Increasing cardinality may not guarantee bound convergence due to likely non-convex coupled optimization and potential local minima preventing MCMC decisions.
- Presentation request: add axis labels to Figure 1 (page 7).
- Presentation request: move the proof of Proposition 1 from the appendix into the main body.
- Presentation request: fix typographical error (“variational lower is” → “variational lower bound is”).
- Overall: a very nice paper that enables potentially faster inference and draws useful model connections.
