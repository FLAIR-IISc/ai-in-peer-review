- Introduces novel, non-spectral bounds for determinants in DPP likelihoods using "pseudo-inputs," applicable to finite and continuous domains.  
- Leverages analogy to inducing variables in sparse Gaussian processes to build cheap upper and lower bounds on intractable determinant terms.  
- Shows how these bounds integrate into standard inference: an approximate variational scheme and an exact Metropolis-Hastings MCMC sampler.  
- Strength: creative, elegant use of pseudo-inputs; insightful connection to sparse GP approximations.  
- Strength: addresses a timely, significant scalability bottleneck for DPP inference with practical applicability.  
- Strength: bounding technique is general and may extend to other large-determinant problems beyond DPPs.  
- Strength: thorough experiments demonstrating MCMC success and exposing variational approach pitfalls and parameter identifiability issues.  
- Weakness: framing mismatch—paper motivates K-parameter interpretability but the technical contribution works only with the L kernel, creating a disconnect.  
- Minor: recommend providing a standard, self-contained supplementary document if accepted.  
- Future work suggestion: extend the pseudo-input bounding approach to the K kernel and discuss potential challenges and scalability.
