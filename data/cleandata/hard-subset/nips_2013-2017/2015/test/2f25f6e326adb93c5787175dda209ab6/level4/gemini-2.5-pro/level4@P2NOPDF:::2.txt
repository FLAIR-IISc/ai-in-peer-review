This paper introduces novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with a direct and compelling application to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process (DPP). The central idea, which involves a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels, is elegant in its simplicity. The paper is well-executed, providing valuable insights and opening up several avenues for future research.

The problem addressed is both timely and relevant, and the proposed methods will likely be of significant interest to many in the machine learning community. The generality of the bounding technique suggests potential applications well beyond DPPs, which further strengthens the contribution. The experimental evaluation is thoughtfully constructed to elucidate key properties of the proposed bounds. I particularly appreciate the inclusion of an analysis showing the conditions under which standard variational approximations can fail, which highlights the practical utility of this work. The chosen application area is interesting and highly relevant to the problem being studied.

**Minor Comments:**

*   The paper repeatedly emphasizes the challenge of parameterizing the kernel matrix K directly, as opposed to its Cholesky factor L. While this is a well-motivated and important problem, its prominence in the abstract and introduction feels somewhat disconnected from the paper's core technical contribution. The proposed bounds operate on L, and the paper focuses on describing this challenge rather than directly addressing it. Clarifying this framing could prevent potential reader confusion about the paper's primary focus.
*   If the paper is accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard, self-contained supplement rather than uploading an extended version of the main paper. This typically makes it easier for readers to locate supplementary proofs, derivations, and experimental details.

**Suggestions for Future Work:**

Building on the paper's motivation regarding the interpretability of a direct K parameterization, a compelling direction for future work would be to develop bounds that operate directly on the K matrix. Could the pseudo-input methodology be extended to this setting? An analysis of the scalability and tightness of such a direct approach would be a valuable contribution and a natural follow-up to the present work.
