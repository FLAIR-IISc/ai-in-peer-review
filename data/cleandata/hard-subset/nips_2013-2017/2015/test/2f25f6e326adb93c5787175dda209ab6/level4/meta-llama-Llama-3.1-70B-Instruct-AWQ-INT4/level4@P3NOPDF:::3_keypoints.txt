- Paper proposes inducing-input–based approximate inference for DPPs that avoids spectral knowledge, presenting variational and approximate MCMC approaches and showing empirical efficiency.  
- Addresses a significant challenge in DPP inference and has merit.  
- Method description is somewhat rough and needs clarification.  
- Empirical evaluation is not thorough enough and should be strengthened.  
- Compare computational complexity and wall-clock runtime of the variational and MCMC methods, and analyze their scaling with dataset size for large-scale applicability.  
- Benchmark the proposed methods against other approximate and exact DPP inference techniques in both approximation quality and computation time.  
- Explain when variational inference is expected to work or fail (toy results show large parameter estimation error), and give underlying reasons.  
- Evaluate robustness when inducing inputs coincide with the sampled points (e.g., the 13-point toy sample).  
- Provide practical guidelines or rules of thumb for selecting the number and placement of inducing points.  
- Minor: Define matrices K and L and briefly mention marginal kernel and L-ensembles for accessibility.  
- Minor: Condense or remove the discussion on advantages of directly learning marginal kernel parameters, as it is not central to the paper’s approach.  
- Overall: Paper has potential but needs clearer exposition and stronger empirical validation; revise write-up accordingly.
