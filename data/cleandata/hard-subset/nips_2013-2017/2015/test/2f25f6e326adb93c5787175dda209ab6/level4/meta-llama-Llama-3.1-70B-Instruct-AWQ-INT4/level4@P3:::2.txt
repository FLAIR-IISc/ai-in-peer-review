The paper "Inference for determinantal point processes without spectral knowledge" proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process (DPP). The main idea is simple, but the paper is well executed and enlightening, and paves the way for several potential improvements.

The authors propose a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of a positive definite kernel. The proposed problem is timely, and will be of interest to several in the community. Such an approach is quite general with potential applications beyond DPPs. The experimental evaluation is well constructed to elucidate several key points, including failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions. The selected application area is interesting and highly relevant to the studied problem.

The paper provides a thorough review of the existing literature on DPPs, and the authors' contribution is clearly explained in the context of previous work. The technical aspects of the paper are sound, and the proposed bounds are well justified. The experiments are also well designed and executed, providing valuable insights into the performance of the proposed method.

The paper's main strength is its ability to provide a simple yet effective solution to the problem of bounding the likelihood of a DPP without requiring spectral knowledge. The use of pseudo-inputs is a clever idea that allows the authors to derive tight bounds on the determinant of the kernel matrix. The paper also provides a good balance between theoretical and experimental results, making it a well-rounded contribution to the field.

One minor comment is that the issue of parameterizing K (instead of L) features prominently in the abstract and the rest of the paper, but the authors do not make any efforts to address it. It is unclear why this issue is emphasized, as the authors mainly focus on describing the problem rather than proposing a solution.

If accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement, and not just upload an extended version of the paper as the supplement. This will help to keep the main paper concise and focused on the main contributions.

Suggestions for future work include considering bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix. This could potentially provide a more interpretable solution, as the K parameterization is more natural for many applications. The scalability of such an approach using the pseudo-input method is also an interesting direction for future research.

Overall, the paper is well written, and the authors have made a significant contribution to the field of DPPs. The proposed method has the potential to be widely applicable, and the paper provides a good starting point for future research in this area.
