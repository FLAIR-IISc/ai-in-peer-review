- Proposes alternative bounds for DPP normalization that avoid eigenvalue estimation, enable MCMC and variational inference, and connect to GP inducing-point methods.

- Introduces a novel inequality (Proposition 1) that is well-suited to DPPs and circumvents spectral estimation issues.

- Paper is technically strong, clearly written, and the inducing-point/variational perspective is appreciated.

- Concern 1 — computational trade-offs: need theoretical and/or empirical evidence comparing optimization-based approach to prior spectrum estimation ([7]) in wall-clock time, scaling with dataset size and effective rank, and sensitivity to initialization.

- Clarify when the sequence of nonlinear optimization problems is faster or more stable than power-iteration spectral estimation.

- Concern 2 — convergence of bounds: unclear that enlarging the inducing set guarantees convergence given coupled non-convex optimization; global minima may be required.

- Request discussion of conditions that ensure global optimality or why local optima suffice, and suggestions for convex relaxations or certificates.

- Ask for practical strategies to avoid poor local minima (warm starts, continuation, multiple restarts, diagnostics), since suboptimal solutions could prevent separation from the Bernoulli acceptance threshold and stall retrospective MCMC.

- Presentation fixes: add axis labels to Figure 1 (page 7), move or summarize the key proposition proof from the appendix into the main text, and correct typo “the variational lower is” → “the variational lower bound is.”

- Overall verdict: strong paper leveraging a clever inequality to bound the DPP partition function, with promising inference advantages and useful connections to related models.
