The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. The main idea is straightforward, yet the paper is well-executed and enlightening, paving the way for several potential improvements.

The authors propose a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of a positive definite kernel, addressing a timely problem that will likely be of interest to several members of the community. This approach is quite general, with potential applications extending beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-constructed, effectively elucidating several key points, including the failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions. I found the selected application area interesting and highly relevant to the studied problem.

Minor comments:
- The emphasis on parameterizing K (instead of L) in the abstract and the rest of the paper is unclear, as the authors primarily focus on describing the problem without attempting to address it.
- If accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement, rather than uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion of the interpretability of the K parameterization makes this approach even more appealing. It would be interesting for the authors to consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix. Additionally, what is the scalability of such an approach when using the pseudo-input method?
