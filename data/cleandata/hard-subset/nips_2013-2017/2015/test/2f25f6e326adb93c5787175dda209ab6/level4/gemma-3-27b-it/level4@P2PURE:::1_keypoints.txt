- Paper proposes an alternative bounding strategy for DPP MCMC inference that avoids explicit eigenvalue estimation and enables provably-correct retrospective MCMC.  
- Approach naturally extends to variational inference and connects to inducing-point methods for scalable Gaussian process inference.  
- Technically sound and well-written; Proposition 1 presents a novel and useful inequality central to the contribution.  
- The connection to inducing points and potential for variational inference are particularly appealing strengths.  
- Concern: Replacing power iterations with potentially costly nonlinear optimization of increasing dimensionality may not be a clear computational improvement.  
- Concern: Unclear convergence of bounds when increasing cardinality due to the coupled, highly nonconvex optimization; local minima may prevent effective bounding and hurt MCMC acceptance.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 (P7) needs axis labels.  
- Presentation issue: The proof of the proposition should be moved from the appendix to the main text, as it is central to the paper.  
- Presentation issue: Typo/awkward phrasing at P7, L361 ("... the variational lower is ...").
