- Proposes an alternative MCMC bound for DPPs that avoids eigenvalue estimation, enables variational learning, and connects to inducing-point Gaussian process methods; technically strong and well-written.  
- Proposition 1 gives a novel lower-bound inequality that cleverly circumvents the eigenvalue-estimation challenges of prior work [7].  
- Establishes meaningful connections to inducing-point methods for scalable GP inference and enables variational inference for DPP kernels.  
- Technical concern 1: Replacing power iterations with higher-dimensional nonlinear optimization may not yield a computational advantage—need clarification and runtime/complexity comparison.  
- Technical concern 2: Simply increasing cardinality may not guarantee convergence of the bounds because the coupled, likely non-convex optimization can get stuck in local minima, which could block MCMC; authors must address global-optimum guarantees or mitigation strategies.  
- Presentation: Figure 1 (p7) should include axis labels.  
- Presentation: The proof of Proposition 1 is central and should be moved from the appendix into the main body.  
- Presentation: Fix wording/typo at P7 L361 (“... the variational lower bound is ...”).  
- Overall: A well-executed paper that cleverly bounds the DPP partition function, enabling faster inference and broader modeling connections.
