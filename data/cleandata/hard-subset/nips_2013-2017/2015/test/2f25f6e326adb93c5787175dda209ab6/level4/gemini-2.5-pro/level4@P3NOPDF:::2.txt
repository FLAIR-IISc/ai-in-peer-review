This paper introduces novel upper and lower bounds for the determinant of a positive definite matrix, with a primary application to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process (DPP). The core idea, which leverages a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels, is conceptually simple yet powerful. The paper is well-executed and enlightening, and the proposed method opens up several promising avenues for future research.

The problem addressed is timely and will be of significant interest to many in the machine learning community. The proposed approach is quite general, with potential applications extending well beyond DPPs to any domain where scalable computation of matrix determinants is a bottleneck.

The experimental evaluation is thoughtfully constructed to elucidate several key aspects of the method. In particular, the experiments effectively highlight the conditions under which standard variational approximations can fail, providing a compelling demonstration of the proposed bounds' utility. The selected application area is interesting and highly relevant to the problem being studied.

**Minor Comments and Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   One point that could be clarified is the emphasis on the difficulty of parameterizing the kernel matrix K directly, as opposed to its Cholesky factor L. This issue is featured prominently in the abstract and introduction, yet the paper's primary contribution is on bounding the determinant, which is a related but distinct problem. The manuscript would benefit from either better integrating this motivation with the proposed solution or tempering its emphasis to improve the paper's focus.

*   Regarding the interpretability of the K parameterization, which the authors correctly note makes direct modeling appealing: could the authors comment on the possibility of extending this framework to bound the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix? What would be the scalability implications of such an approach, especially if combined with the pseudo-input method? A brief discussion of this could strengthen the paper.

*   If the paper is accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement with additional details or proofs, rather than uploading an extended version of the main paper. This format is generally more convenient for readers.
