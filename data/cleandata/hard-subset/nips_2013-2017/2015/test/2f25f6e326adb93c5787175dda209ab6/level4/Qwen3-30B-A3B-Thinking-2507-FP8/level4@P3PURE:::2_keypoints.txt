- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant with application to bounding DPP likelihoods.  
- Main idea is simple, well-executed, and provides clear insights enabling future improvements.  
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels addresses a timely, important problem.  
- Approach is sufficiently general and may apply beyond determinantal point processes.  
- Experimental evaluation is well-designed and demonstrates key insights, including failure modes of variational approximations under certain conditions.  
- Chosen application area is interesting and highly relevant to the methods studied.  
- Minor concern: unclear why K-parameterization (vs Cholesky factor L) is emphasized despite no detailed treatment of this choice.  
- Minor suggestion: provide a standard supplemental document rather than an extended paper as the supplement.  
- Future-work queries: explore bounding the determinant directly using the K matrix and assess the scalability of the pseudo-input approach.
