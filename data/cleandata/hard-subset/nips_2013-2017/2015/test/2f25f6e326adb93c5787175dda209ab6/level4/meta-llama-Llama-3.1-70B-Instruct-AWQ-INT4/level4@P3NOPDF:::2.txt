The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. The main idea is simple, yet the paper is well-executed and enlightening, paving the way for several potential improvements.

The authors propose a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. The proposed problem is timely and will be of interest to several members of the community. This approach is quite general, with potential applications beyond determinantal point processes.

The experimental evaluation is well-constructed to elucidate several key points, including the failures of variational approximations under certain conditions. The selected application area is interesting and highly relevant to the studied problem.

There are a few minor comments that can be addressed to further improve the paper. Firstly, it is unclear why the issue of parameterizing K (instead of L) features so prominently in the abstract and the rest of the paper, as the authors mainly focus on describing the problem without making any efforts to address it. A clearer explanation or a more in-depth discussion on this aspect would be beneficial.

Additionally, if the paper is accepted, it would be more suitable for the authors to prepare a standard supplement rather than uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work include exploring the interpretability of the K parameterization, which makes this approach even more appealing. The authors could consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix. Furthermore, investigating the scalability of such an approach when using the pseudo-input method would be valuable.

Overall, the paper presents a well-executed and enlightening approach to bounding the matrix determinant, with potential applications to determinantal point processes and beyond. With some minor revisions and further exploration of the proposed ideas, this work has the potential to contribute significantly to the field.
