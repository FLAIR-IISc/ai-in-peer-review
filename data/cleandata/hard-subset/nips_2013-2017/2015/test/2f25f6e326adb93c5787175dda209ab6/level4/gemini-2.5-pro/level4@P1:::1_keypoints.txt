- Problem: Learning DPP kernel parameters is hard due to an intractable likelihood normalizing constant (large determinant).  
- Main contribution: Introduces non-spectral determinantal-inequality-based bounds parameterized by inducing points, avoiding eigenvalue computations and enabling variational inference.  

Strengths:
- Novel and elegant use of a determinantal inequality to form non-spectral likelihood bounds.  
- Insightful connection to inducing-point approximations in Gaussian processes, linking DPPs and scalable GP methods.  
- Enables variational inference for DPPs, offering a faster, scalable alternative to MCMC.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and supported by convincing experiments for finite and continuous domains.  

Weaknesses / Concerns:
- Computational trade-offs unclear: replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization over inducing-point locations may not always be faster as optimization dimensionality grows; direct complexity/runtime comparisons to [7] are needed.  
- Convergence and non-convexity risk: optimizing inducing points is likely non-convex, so local minima could prevent bounds from tightening sufficiently in retrospective MCMC, potentially trapping the sampler or harming correctness — authors should address practical impact or guarantees.  

Minor comments:
- Include a brief proof sketch or core intuition for Proposition 1 in the main text.  
- Figure 1 needs axis labels.  
- Minor typo: "the variational lower is..." (page 7, line 361).  

Overall: Strong, novel contribution that avoids spectral methods and enables variational DPP inference; addressing computational comparisons and the impact of non-convex optimization would strengthen the paper.
