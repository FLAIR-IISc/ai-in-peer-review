The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. Although the main idea is straightforward, the paper is well-executed, enlightening, and paves the way for several potential improvements.

The authors propose a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of a positive definite kernel, a timely and interesting problem that will likely attract attention from the community. This approach is quite general, with potential applications extending beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-constructed, effectively highlighting key points, including the failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions. The selected application area is interesting and highly relevant to the studied problem.

Minor comments:
- The emphasis on parameterizing K (instead of L) in the abstract and the rest of the paper is unclear, as the authors primarily focus on describing the problem without attempting to address it.
- If accepted, I suggest that the authors prepare a standard supplement rather than uploading an extended version of the paper as the supplement.

Suggestions for future work:
- The discussion on the interpretability of the K parameterization makes this approach even more appealing. A potential direction for future research is to consider bounding the determinant directly in terms of the K matrix. Additionally, it would be interesting to investigate the scalability of this approach when using the pseudo-input method.
