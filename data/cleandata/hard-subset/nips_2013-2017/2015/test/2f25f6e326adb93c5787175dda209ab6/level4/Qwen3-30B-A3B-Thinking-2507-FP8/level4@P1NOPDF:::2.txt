

The authors present novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of determinantal point processes (DPPs). The core idea is conceptually simple yet elegantly executed, offering insightful contributions that open promising avenues for future work.  

The paper introduces a creative application of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. This problem is timely and addresses a meaningful gap, likely to interest multiple researchers in the field. The approach is sufficiently general to extend beyond DPPs, suggesting broader applicability to kernel-based methods. The experimental evaluation is well-designed, clearly illustrating key insights—including scenarios where variational approximations fail—and the chosen application area is both compelling and highly relevant to the theoretical framework.  

Minor comments:  
- The paper emphasizes parameterizing the kernel matrix $K$ (rather than its Cholesky factor $L$) prominently in the abstract and main text, yet the authors primarily describe the problem without addressing this choice. It remains unclear why this distinction is central to the narrative.  
- If accepted, the authors should provide a standard supplementary material package rather than merely uploading an extended version of the paper.  

Suggestions for future work:  
- The discussion of $K$'s interpretability further enhances the appeal of this approach. Could the authors explore bounding the determinant directly in terms of $K$? Additionally, what is the computational scalability of such a method when using pseudo-inputs?
