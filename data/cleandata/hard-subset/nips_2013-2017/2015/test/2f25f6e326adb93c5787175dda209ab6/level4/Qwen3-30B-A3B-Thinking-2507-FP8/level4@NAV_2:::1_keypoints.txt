- Tackles likelihood-based parameter inference for DPPs, avoiding costly spectral decomposition of the kernel.
- Key contribution: novel nonspectral determinant inequality (Proposition 1) enabling efficient variational inference and exact MCMC.
- Links DPP inference to Gaussian process inducing-point methods, extending inducing-point techniques to DPPs.
- Well-motivated and technically sound approach with clear exposition for finite and continuous DPP cases.
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets shows practical utility of the proposed methods.
- Offers a viable alternative to spectral methods, particularly for large-scale or continuous domains where spectra are intractable.
- Unclear computational advantage: nonlinear optimization over inducing points may not be faster than spectral power iterations and can scale with pseudo-inputs.
- Convergence concerns: inducing-point optimization is highly non-convex and convergence to a global minimum is not addressed, risking MCMC acceptance issues.
- Presentation issues: figures lack axis labels, key proof is placed in the appendix rather than main text, and there's a typo in line 361.
- Pro-accept: technically novel, theoretically elegant nonspectral bounds with practical connections to established GP methods and empirical support.
- Con-accept: insufficient evidence for computational benefits and inadequate treatment of optimization convergence, limiting practical applicability.
