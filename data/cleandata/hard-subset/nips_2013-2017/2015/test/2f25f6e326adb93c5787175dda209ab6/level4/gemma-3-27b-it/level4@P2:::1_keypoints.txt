- Paper proposes a novel non-spectral bounding method for DPP partition functions, avoiding eigenvalue estimation.  
- Method enables variational inference and connects to inducing-point techniques from Gaussian processes.  
- The determinant inequality in Proposition 1 is a key technical contribution and appears well-suited to the problem.  
- Paper is technically strong, well-written, and enjoyable to read.  
- Potential computational advantage over spectral methods where eigen-decomposition is expensive.  
- Main technical concern: replacing power iterations with high-dimensional nonlinear optimization may not be a clear computational win.  
- Optimizing inducing points could become costly as their number increases to tighten bounds.  
- Convergence concern: increasing inducing points may not guarantee tighter bounds due to non-convexity and local minima.  
- Need discussion of strategies for ensuring global optima or stopping criteria based on bound tightness.  
- Limited empirical validation: experiments do not fully demonstrate computational efficiency gains versus spectral methods.  
- Minor presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels.  
- Important proof (Proposition 1) is relegated to the supplement and should be in the main text.  
- Typo/incomplete sentence on page 7, line 361 ("the variational lower is...").  
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, novelty, links to existing work, potential for computational improvement, and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear practical computational benefits, unresolved convergence issues, and limited empirical evidence.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on addressing computational cost and convergence discussions.
