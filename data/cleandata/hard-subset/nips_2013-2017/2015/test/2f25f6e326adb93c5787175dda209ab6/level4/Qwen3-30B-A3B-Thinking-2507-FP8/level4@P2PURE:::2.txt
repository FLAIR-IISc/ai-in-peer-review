

The authors propose novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant, with applications to bounding the likelihood of a determinantal point process. The main idea is simple yet well-executed and insightful, opening avenues for future improvements.  

The authors introduce a creative application of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels. This problem is timely and will interest multiple researchers in the community. The approach is sufficiently general, with potential applications extending beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-designed to illustrate key points, including the failure modes of a variational approximation under specific conditions. The chosen application area is compelling and highly relevant to the problem studied.  

Minor comments:  
- It is unclear why parameterizing the kernel as $K$ (rather than $L$) features prominently in the abstract and main text, given that the authors primarily describe the problem without addressing this aspect.  
- If accepted, the authors should prepare a standard supplement rather than merely uploading an extended version of the paper.  

Suggestions for future work:  
- The discussion of the interpretability of the $K$ parameterization enhances the appeal of the approach. Could the authors explore bounding the determinant directly in terms of the $K$ matrix? What is the scalability of this approach when using the pseudo-input method?
