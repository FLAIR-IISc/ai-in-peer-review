- Novel contribution: derives nonspectral bounds for DPP likelihoods that avoid expensive spectral decomposition by using inducing inputs and determinant bounds.
- Methodological advantage: bounds depend only on a subset of inducing points, enabling practical variational inference and MCMC for finite and continuous domains.
- Relation to prior work: avoids eigenvalue approximation approaches (e.g., power iteration) and extends variational sparse GP techniques to DPPs.
- Experiments: demonstrated on a toy Gaussian continuous example and a real diabetic neuropathy dataset with clear class separation.
- Strength: significant and well-motivated theoretical contribution avoiding spectral decomposition.
- Strength: extension to continuous domains shows method generality beyond prior finite-domain approaches.
- Strength: empirical validation on real data supports practical utility.
- Strength: connection to variational sparse GP methods provides a familiar framework for ML practitioners.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation lacks direct comparison to spectral-based methods—needs runtime, approximation error, and convergence comparisons.
- Weakness: insufficient explanation for variational inference failure on the toy example; lacks guidance on when to prefer VI vs MCMC.
- Weakness: no systematic analysis of how the number of inducing points affects accuracy and computational cost; needs convergence analysis as m increases.
- Weakness: toy experiment is very small (13 points); needs larger-scale experiments and tests using actual data points as inducing inputs.
- Weakness: confusing discussion of kernel parametrization (parametrizing L vs K) without clarifying impact on inference and interpretability.
- Writing suggestions: define K and L early in the introduction and condense tangential interpretability discussion.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, specifically add comparative analysis with spectral methods, clarify VI vs MCMC conditions, include inducing-point convergence analysis, and clarify kernel parametrization.
