- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant with applications to bounding DPP likelihoods.
- Main idea is simple, well executed, enlightening, and enables potential future improvements.
- Introduces a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.
- Approach is general and potentially applicable beyond determinantal point processes.
- The problem is timely and of interest to the machine learning community.
- Experimental evaluation is well designed and highlights key behaviors, including failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions.
- Application choice (DPPs) is relevant and well matched to the method.
- Builds on prior work (sparse GP inducing variables, previous determinant bounds) and extends bounds to continuous-domain DPPs.
- Paper is technically sound with a clear, concise exposition and thorough analysis of bound properties.
- Strengths: clarity, technical soundness, relevance, and discussion of applications and limitations.
- Minor weakness: does not address parameterizing K instead of L despite its prominence in the abstract.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Minor comments: clarify importance of K-parameterization in the paper; provide a standard supplement rather than an extended paper.
- Suggested future work: investigate bounding the determinant directly in terms of K and assess scalability of pseudo-inputs for such an approach.
