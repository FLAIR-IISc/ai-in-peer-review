- Introduces novel, non-spectral pseudo-input-based upper and lower bounds for the DPP likelihood determinant, avoiding costly eigendecompositions.  
- Bounds are inspired by sparse Gaussian process approximations and use inducing points to construct tractable approximations.  
- Develops two inference schemes: a fast variational method maximizing an ELBO and an exact but costlier retrospective-sampling MCMC.  
- Framework is presented for both finite (discrete) and continuous DPPs and validated on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Technical derivations are rigorous, with clear connections to variational sparse GP methods and a strong, honest experimental evaluation.  
- Experiments show the variational approach can fail to recover parameters in some cases, motivating the robust MCMC alternative.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-structured, progressing from finite to continuous cases for accessibility.  
- The determinant-bounding technique may have applicability beyond the DPP community.  
- Framing around the K vs. L parameterization is potentially misleading since the work operates solely on L; this should be reframed as future motivation.  
- A valuable extension would be adapting the pseudo-input approach to the interpretable K parameterization; discussing challenges and scalability would strengthen the paper.  
- Suggests providing proofs and extra details in a standard supplement (camera-ready) rather than an extended main paper.  
- Overall recommendation: well-executed, insightful work with strong contributions and a recommendation for acceptance.
