- Presents novel upper and lower bounds for matrix determinants with applications to bounding likelihoods in determinantal point processes.  
- Core idea is straightforward but the paper is meticulously executed and insightful.  
- Introduces a novel use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.  
- Problem is timely and of significant interest to the broader community.  
- Approach shows strong generality and potential applicability beyond determinantal point processes.  
- Experimental design effectively demonstrates key insights and highlights limitations of variational approximations under certain conditions.  
- Chosen application area is compelling and well-aligned with the theoretical contributions.  
- Minor concern: prominence of K (vs. L) parameterization in abstract/main text seems disproportionate to the paper’s focus.  
- Minor request: if accepted, authors should provide a standard supplementary material rather than uploading an extended manuscript.  
- Suggestion for future work: explore bounding the determinant directly using the K matrix and analyze scalability when combining this method with pseudo-inputs.
