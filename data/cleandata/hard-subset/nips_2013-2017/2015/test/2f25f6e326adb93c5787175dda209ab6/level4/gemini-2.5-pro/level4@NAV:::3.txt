This paper introduces approximate inference methods for determinantal point processes (DPPs) that circumvent the need for spectral knowledge of the underlying kernel. The approach relies on inducing inputs to construct a bound on the likelihood, thereby reducing computational complexity. The authors develop both a variational inference framework and an approximate Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) scheme and provide an empirical demonstration of their performance.

While the direction of this work is promising, the description of the methodology could be clearer, and the empirical evaluation is not sufficiently comprehensive. The following points could help strengthen the paper:

1) A direct comparison between the two proposed methods in terms of theoretical computational complexity and empirical runtime is currently missing.

2) The paper would benefit from a comparative analysis against other existing alternatives, including both exact and approximate baselines. This evaluation should address both the quality of the approximations and the associated computational costs.

3) As noted in Section 5.1, the variational inference approach results in a high parameter estimation error on the toy dataset, prompting the use of approximate MCMC. It is crucial to discuss the settings in which the proposed variational method is expected to perform well and the conditions under which it is likely to fail, along with the underlying reasons.

4) For the toy example, which uses a 13-point sample, it would be insightful to see an experiment where the inducing inputs are set to be the sampled points themselves.

5) The manuscript would be more useful to practitioners if it offered some practical guidance or rules of thumb for two key hyperparameters: a) the number of inducing points to select, and b) the strategy for their initial placement.

**Minor Comments:**

*   In the introduction, the matrices K and L are mentioned without definition. Briefly introducing them in the context of the marginal kernel and L-ensembles would improve clarity for a broader audience.
*   The paper repeatedly discusses the advantages of directly learning the marginal kernel parameters. Since this is not the approach ultimately adopted, this discussion could be condensed to improve the paper's focus on its core contributions.
