- Proposes novel upper and lower likelihood bounds for DPPs that avoid expensive spectral decompositions.
- Bounds are cheap to compute and applicable to both finite and continuous domains.
- Main technical idea: use pseudo-inputs/inducing points to construct the determinant bounds.
- Bounds integrate directly into variational inference and MCMC for DPP parameter estimation.
- Theoretical development is sound; Propositions 1 and 2 follow from nontrivial determinant inequalities with sufficient supplementary detail.
- Computational cost is O(n m²), a substantial improvement over O(n³) spectral methods.
- Connects naturally to sparse Gaussian process approximations, making the approach familiar to ML researchers.
- Experiments are thorough: toy Gaussian validates MCMC vs exact inference; diabetic neuropathy case shows real-world applicability.
- Analysis of pseudo-inputs reveals they form grids reflecting the DPP marginal distribution.
- Writing and exposition are clear and accessible to the NIPS audience.
- Significance is strong: practical inference for DPPs with potential broader applicability to kernel models.
- Weakness: limited discussion of scalability to extremely large n (e.g., n > 10⁵) and how choice of m affects performance.
- Weakness: could better compare to other approximate DPP methods (e.g., Nyström approaches).
- Clarification: paper correctly acknowledges the K vs L parameterization trade-off and works within the standard ML convention; it does not claim to resolve interpretability issues.
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, novel, well-validated, and recommended for acceptance.
