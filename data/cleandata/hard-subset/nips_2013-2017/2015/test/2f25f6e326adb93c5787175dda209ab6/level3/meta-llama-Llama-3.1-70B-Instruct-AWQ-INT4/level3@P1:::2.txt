This paper proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant with applications to bounding determinantal point process (DPP) likelihoods. The main idea is simple, well-executed, and enlightening, paving the way for further improvements. The authors creatively use pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels, which is a timely problem likely to be of interest to the community.

The approach is general and has potential applications beyond determinantal point processes. The experimental evaluation is well-constructed and illustrates key behaviors, including failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions. The chosen application area is interesting and highly relevant.

The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to DPPs, their properties, and the challenges associated with likelihood-based inference. The authors explain how the proposed bounds can be used for variational inference and Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods, and they provide a detailed analysis of the bounds' properties.

The experimental evaluation is thorough and well-presented, with a clear description of the datasets, experimental setup, and results. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on both synthetic and real-world data, and they provide a detailed analysis of the results.

One minor point is that it is unclear why parameterizing K (instead of L) is emphasized, since the paper does not address it in depth. However, this does not detract from the overall quality of the paper.

In terms of originality, the paper provides a novel approach to bounding DPP likelihoods, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels is creative and well-executed.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a timely problem that is likely to be of interest to the community. The approach has potential applications beyond DPPs, and the experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach on both synthetic and real-world data.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The experimental evaluation is well-presented, with a clear description of the datasets, experimental setup, and results.
