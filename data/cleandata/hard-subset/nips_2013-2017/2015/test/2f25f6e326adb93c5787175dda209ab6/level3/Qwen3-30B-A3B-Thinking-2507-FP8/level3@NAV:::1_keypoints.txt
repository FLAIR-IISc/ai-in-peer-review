- Addresses intractable DPP normalization constant by avoiding spectral decomposition.
- Proposes nonspectral bounds using inducing-point techniques analogous to sparse Gaussian processes.
- Enables efficient variational inference and retrospective MCMC without eigenvalue estimates.
- Extends prior work [7] by providing cheap-to-evaluate bounds applicable to finite and continuous DPPs.
- Main technical contribution: Proposition 1—tight lower bound via determinant inequalities.
- Paper is technically strong, well-structured, and clearly written.
- Variational and MCMC frameworks are elegantly derived; GP connections broaden impact.
- Experiments on synthetic and diabetic neuropathy data validate convergence of bounds and parameter estimation.
- Concern: computational advantage unclear—optimizing over growing inducing sets may not be faster or scalable compared to power iterations.
- Concern: non-convex optimization of inducing points and kernel parameters risks local minima and lacks convergence guarantees, possibly invalidating MCMC.
- Presentation issues: unlabeled axes in Figure 1, main proof relegated to appendix (should be in main text), and minor wording errors.
- Overall verdict: novel, technically sound, and significant; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
