- Addresses intractable normalizing constants in DPP likelihoods by avoiding spectral decompositions.  
- Proposes novel nonspectral bounds on det(L+I) (finite) and Fredholm determinants (continuous) using pseudo-inputs Z.  
- Bounds are computationally efficient: O(n m^2) vs O(n^3) for spectral methods.  
- Enables likelihood-based inference via variational lower bounds and MCMC without spectral knowledge.  
- Builds on sparse Gaussian process approximations, extending them nontrivially to DPPs.  
- Theoretical contributions (Propositions 1–2) are sound and proved using a nontrivial determinant inequality.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates computational advantages and limitations (e.g., identifiability issues).  
- Paper is very clear, well-written, and explains connections to prior work accessibly.  
- Original application of pseudo-input approximations to DPP likelihoods; bounds are broadly applicable to kernel-based models.  
- Significant impact for scalable DPP inference in practical applications modeling diversity/repulsion.  
- Minor weakness: paper emphasizes L-parameterization but does not deeply justify not parameterizing K, which may be more interpretable.  
- Minor weakness: supplementary material is provided as an extended paper rather than a standard, more accessible supplement.  
- Suggested future work: derive bounds directly in terms of K and evaluate scalability of pseudo-input methods on very large datasets.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound, novel, and practically useful paper — recommendation: accept.
