- Introduces novel nonspectral upper and lower bounds on determinants for matrices and operators with DPP applications.  
- Proposes using pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels for efficient likelihood-based DPP inference.  
- Avoids spectral decomposition, reducing computational cost and enabling applicability to finite and continuous domains.  
- Addresses timely, relevant challenges in DPP inference with potential impact across ML, spatial statistics, and physics.  
- Novelty and impact: nonspectral bounds offer a practical alternative to spectral methods and can spur further research.  
- Broad applicability: methods may extend to other problems where determinant evaluation is a bottleneck.  
- Experimental validation: well-designed experiments, including a compelling diabetic neuropathy dataset application.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear exposition and rigorous derivations of the pseudo-input approach.  
- Future directions: suggests exploring K–L kernel connections without spectral knowledge and quadrature-based bounds.  
- Weakness — parameterization: insufficient discussion of interpretability trade-offs when parameterizing L instead of K.  
- Weakness — supplemental reliance: heavy dependence on supplementary material for proofs reduces main-text accessibility.  
- Weakness — scalability: scalability with respect to number of pseudo-inputs m needs further analysis for large datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a challenging problem with a novel, validated, and broadly applicable solution aligned with conference goals.  
- Arguments against acceptance: interpretability trade-offs and reliance on supplementary material remain unresolved concerns.  
- Suggestions for improvement: elaborate on K vs L parameterization implications, use a standard supplementary document, and test scalability on larger/high-dimensional datasets.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: high-quality contribution with minor issues; recommend acceptance with revisions.
