- Proposes nonspectral determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods using a low-rank surrogate Q built from m inducing inputs Z, avoiding eigen-decompositions.
- Derives a variational lower bound jointly optimizable over kernel parameters and Z, and a retrospective MCMC scheme that refines bounds by increasing m until MH decisions are decisive.
- Draws an explicit analogy to inducing-point sparse GP methods (Titsias ’09) and contrasts with spectral DPP methods (e.g., Affandi et al. ICML’14).
- Extends the finite-matrix bounds to continuous DPPs (trace-class operators) and provides a closed-form Ψ-term for Gaussian–Gaussian settings.
- Strength: conceptually novel nonspectral bounds applicable to both finite and continuous DPPs.
- Strength: practical—avoids eigen-decompositions and costs O(n m^2) for the variational bound when m ≪ n.
- Strength: unified treatment of finite and continuous DPPs, addressing a rarely scaled continuous case.
- Strength: offers both a cheap variational method and a principled retrospective MH alternative.
- Strength: empirical sanity checks show retrospective MH matches ideal MH on a toy and intuitive inducing-input structure on real data.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is thin; missing head-to-head comparisons to spectral-bound MCMC, EM/fixed-point L-ensemble methods, and log-det approximations.
- Weakness: lacks wall-clock runtime and memory comparisons; needs complexity table and scaling plots vs n and m.
- Weakness: variational instability on the toy (large parameter error, especially κ); unclear if due to identifiability or loose bounds.
- Weakness: insufficient guidance on choosing m and initializing Z, and no comparisons of candidate selection heuristics (k-means, k-DPP, greedy, posterior variance).
- Weakness: does not quantify bound tightness (upper–lower gap) as a function of m and Z, nor connect gap to inference error or MH stopping criteria.
- Weakness: early notation confuses K vs L; paper needs a concise primer on marginal kernel K and L-ensembles for readers new to DPPs.
- Weakness: continuous-case practicality unclear—Ψ requires integrals; need list of kernels/measures with closed-form Ψ or efficient approximations (quadrature, RFF, inducing measures).
- Weakness: key determinant-inequality proofs deferred; request for high-level proof sketch and conditions for equality (e.g., Z dense).
- Technical soundness: bounds and operator extension appear reasonable, but missing quantitative analysis of tightness and benchmark comparisons.
- Experimental support: suggestive but limited—toy confirms correctness, real-data results lack comparisons and wall-clock studies.
- Clarity requests: add primer on K vs L, pseudo-code for variational optimization and retrospective MH, complexity and notation tables, and trim extraneous K-parameter interpretation.
- Originality: nonspectral determinant bounds, operator extension, and retrospective MH are novel; connection to inducing-point GPs insightful.
- Significance: potentially broad impact if bounds are tight and deliver competitive accuracy–speed trade-offs versus alternatives.
- Specific revision requests: add runtime/accuracy comparisons to strong baselines, wall-clock scaling plots, bound-gap analyses, heuristics for m/Z, and stability strategies for variational inference.
- Acceptance pro: elegant, general nonspectral approach with continuous-DPP extension and simple implementation.
- Acceptance con: insufficient empirical validation, limited inducing-input guidance, variational instability, and clarity gaps.
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending stronger empirical evidence, clearer guidance, and improved exposition.
