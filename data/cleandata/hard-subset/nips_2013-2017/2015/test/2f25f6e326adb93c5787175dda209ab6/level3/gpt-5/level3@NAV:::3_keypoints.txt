- Summary: Proposes nonspectral DPP likelihood bounds using inducing inputs Z and low-rank Q, giving a tractable variational lower bound and retrospective-MH for finite and continuous DPPs that avoid eigencomputations and connect to inducing-point GP methods.

- Strength: Conceptually neat, broadly applicable bounds that sidestep spectral decompositions and extend to continuous domains.

- Strength: Clear derivation using determinant inequalities and a carefully constructed retrospective MH from bracketing log-likelihoods.

- Strength: Practical promise with O(n m^2) variational complexity and empirical evidence that bound gaps shrink with m.

- Weakness: Presentation is rough in places; key objects (K vs L, L-ensembles) are used before being defined.

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited, missing principled baselines and direct head-to-head comparisons.

- Weakness: Variational inference appears unstable on the toy example; paper relies on approximate MCMC without clarifying when VI is appropriate.

- Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance: Theoretical contributions are plausible and novel, clarity is mixed, and significance is promising but depends on stronger experimental evidence.

- Major request: Provide direct complexity and wall-clock comparisons between VI and retrospective MH, including cost/memory vs n, m, d and number of Z-optimizations per MH step.

- Major request: Compare against exact small-n and approximate alternatives (spectral MH, Nyström/Krylov, determinant approximations, fixed-point/EM) with approximation quality and runtime.

- Major request: Clarify when VI is expected to work or fail and explain why VI mis-estimates parameters on the toy data (sensitivity to Z placement or κ/σ scales).

- Major request: Toy study: evaluate performance when inducing inputs are set to observed points and to k-means centers.

- Major request: Provide rules of thumb for choosing m (e.g., based on bound gap or tr(L−Q)) and for initializing/placing Z (e.g., k-means, samples from marginal intensity, grid for Gaussian bases).

- Minor comment: Define K (marginal kernel) and L-ensembles early, state K = L(I+L)^{-1} and the role of det(L_Y)/det(I+L).

- Minor comment: Reconcile or trim the discussion advocating direct learning of K’s parameters versus parametrizing L.

- Recommendation: Borderline accept conditional on improved clarity (definitions, algorithmic details) and a significantly strengthened empirical section with comprehensive baselines and runtime analyses.
