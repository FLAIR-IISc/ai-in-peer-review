- Proposes novel nonspectral bounds on DPP likelihoods that avoid expensive spectral decompositions, enabling more efficient inference.
- Derives determinant bounds based on "pseudo-inputs" (inducing-variable style) rather than spectral information.
- Bounds apply to both finite and continuous DPPs and can be tightened by optimizing pseudo-inputs.
- Enables both variational inference and exact MCMC for DPPs without spectral approximations.
- Theoretical results (Propositions 1 & 2) are rigorous, derived using determinant inequalities.
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real (diabetic neuropathy) data shows practical effectiveness and trade-offs between accuracy and cost.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with a clear connection to Gaussian process inducing-variable methods.
- Originality: novel application of inducing-variable style nonspectral bounds to DPPs and extension to continuous domains.
- Significance: removes a key inference bottleneck for DPPs, improving scalability and practical applicability.
- Strengths: conceptually simple, strong theoretical foundation, practical algorithms (variational & MCMC), convincing experiments.
- Weaknesses: parameterizing K instead of L is under-justified and not fully explored; supplement is presented as a long extended paper rather than a concise supplement.
- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS — strong technical contribution and clear exposition; minor issues are addressable in final version.
