- Proposes a novel inference method for DPPs that does not require spectral knowledge.  
- Derives likelihood bounds for DPPs usable in variational inference and MCMC, based on a non-trivial determinant inequality.  
- Avoids approximating the spectrum of large matrices or operators.  
- Paper is technically strong, clearly written, and concisely presents main ideas.  
- Provides a thorough literature review and motivation for the new approach.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness with experiments on synthetic and real-world data.  
- Draws useful connections between DPPs and other models (e.g., Gaussian processes) and explains implementation for VI and MCMC.  
- Computational concern: replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of increasing dimension may not be a clear computational win.  
- Optimization concern: increasing inducing/cardinality may not guarantee bound convergence due to coupled, likely non-convex optimizations and local minima that could invalidate MCMC steps.  
- Presentation suggestions: Figure 1 needs axis labels and the main proof (Proposition 1) should be moved from the appendix into the main text.  
- Overall assessment: significant and impactful contribution; rating 8/10, confidence 9/10.
