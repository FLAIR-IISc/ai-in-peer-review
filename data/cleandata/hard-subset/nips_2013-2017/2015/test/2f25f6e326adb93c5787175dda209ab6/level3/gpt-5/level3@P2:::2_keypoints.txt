- Proposes nonspectral upper and lower determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods using low-rank approximations with pseudo-inputs Z for finite and continuous domains.  
- Bounds avoid eigenvalue computations by approximating L with Q built from pseudo-inputs and applying trace corrections (finite) or Fredholm-determinant analogs (continuous).  
- Method is conceptually related to inducing-variable sparse variational GP methods but applied to determinants rather than predictive conditionals.  
- Uses the bounds for (i) a variational lower bound to train kernel parameters and pseudo-inputs, and (ii) a retrospective Metropolis–Hastings sampler that adaptively tightens bounds.  
- Continuous extension leverages computable Ψ integrals for Gaussian kernels with Gaussian base measures and connects to Fredholm determinants.  
- Experiments: 1D synthetic validates posterior recovery and reveals a VI identifiability issue; 2D diabetic neuropathy dataset shows convergence behavior and class separation.  
- The determinant inequalities are technically sound, based on Seiler & Simon’s inequality, and the retrospective MH adaptation is correctly applied.  
- Continuous-domain assumptions are reasonable and practical quantities are computable for common kernel/measure pairs.  
- Synthetic results expose an identifiability parameter κ where variational inference struggles but MCMC succeeds.  
- Empirical evaluation is solid but scalability to high dimensions and very large n is not fully assessed.  
- Tightness of bounds depends critically on pseudo-input locations Z, with no quantitative gap control beyond increasing m.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and explains motivation and finite vs. continuous cases effectively.  
- Recommends adding practical guidance: Z initialization/optimization recipes, bound-gap monitoring, and gradient expressions for Z.  
- Novel use of pseudo-inputs for nonspectral determinant bounds distinct from spectral or Nyström approaches; retrospective MH adaptation is impactful.  
- Approach addresses the central DPP bottleneck det(I+L) and enables both fast approximate training and exact inference; broadly useful beyond DPPs.  
- Strengths: simple, broadly applicable idea; clear GP connection; both variational and exact MCMC instantiations; honest experiments.  
- Weaknesses: no theoretical guarantees on gap vs. m, limited guidance on Z selection, scalability comparisons lacking, and Ψ tractability limited for non-Gaussian measures.  
- Questions for authors: provide empirical bound-gap vs. m curves, sensitivity of results to Z initialization, possibility of stochastic/mini-batch VI for large n, and combining bounds with randomized log-det estimators.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, principled, practical, well-written, and likely to influence follow-up work.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical characterization of bound tightness, limited Z/scale guidance, and incomplete treatment of K vs. L parameterizations.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept with minor presentation and supplementary improvements.  
- Minor suggestions: add a standard supplement with proofs/gradients/algorithms and experiments or discussion connecting K-parameterized models to det(I+L) bounds and scalability.
