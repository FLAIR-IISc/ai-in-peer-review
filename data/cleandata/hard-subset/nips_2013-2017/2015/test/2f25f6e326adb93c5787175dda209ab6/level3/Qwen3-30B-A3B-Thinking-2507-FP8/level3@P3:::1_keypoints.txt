- Addresses the intractable DPP normalization constant that normally requires spectral decomposition.  
- Proposes novel non-spectral bounds (Proposition 1) inspired by inducing-point techniques to avoid eigenvalue estimation.  
- Proposition 1 gives cheap-to-evaluate bounds enabling variational inference and exact MCMC without spectral knowledge.  
- Derives non-spectral bounds for both finite and continuous DPPs; Proposition 1 is technically elegant and novel.  
- Connects DPP inference to scalable inducing-point methods from Gaussian processes, offering transferable insights.  
- Develops variational and MCMC methods that effectively leverage the new bounds.  
- Rigorous proofs and clear exposition, though the main proof of Proposition 1 should be moved into the main text.  
- Experiments (synthetic and diabetic neuropathy data) show bounds converge to true likelihood as inducing points increase; variational estimates match MCMC.  
- Strength: removes computational bottleneck of spectral decomposition, extending DPP applicability to larger datasets.  
- Strength: substantial technical contribution with broad applicability for determinant bounds.  
- Concern: claimed computational advantage over Affandi et al. [7] is not clearly demonstrated or analyzed in complexity terms.  
- Concern: potential non-convergence and optimization issues from coupled non-convex inducing-point optimizations are not fully addressed.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels, main proof in appendix, and minor wording errors need fixing.  
- Limited discussion on scenarios where the proposed bounds are most advantageous.  
- Overall significance high and reviewer recommends acceptance.
