- Addresses likelihood-based inference for DPPs by tackling the intractable det(L+I) normalizer.
- Introduces novel non-spectral upper and lower bounds on det(L+I) for finite and continuous DPPs.
- Leverages pseudo-inputs from sparse Gaussian Processes (Titsias, 2009) to build computationally efficient bounds.
- Enables fast approximate variational inference using the bounds.
- Supports exact but more expensive MCMC via retrospective sampling enabled by the bounds.
- Contribution is simple, original, well-executed, and provides a new perspective connecting sparse GPs and DPPs.
- Bounding approach is general and may apply beyond DPPs to other kernel-matrix determinant problems.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and experimentally strong on synthetic and real data.
- Experimental evaluation includes a failure case for the variational method and shows the utility of the MCMC alternative.
- Minor weakness: paper emphasizes K-kernel parameterization challenge but proceeds with L; motivation/clarification would help.
- Minor accessibility issue: recommend a standard, self-contained supplementary document rather than only an extended version.
- Suggested future work: extend the framework to bound the determinant directly in terms of the K kernel.
