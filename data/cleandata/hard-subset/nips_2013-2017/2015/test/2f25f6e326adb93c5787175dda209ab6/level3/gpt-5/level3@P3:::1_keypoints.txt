- Problem: Proposes nonspectral determinant bounds for DPP normalization using inducing points Z and a low-rank surrogate Q to avoid eigenvalue estimation.
- Method: Bounds yield cheap O(n m^2) evaluations and extend to continuous DPPs via Fredholm determinants using LZ and Ψ.
- Inference: Bounds support a variational lower bound for learning and retrospective MCMC by refining bounds as needed.
- Conceptual link: Connects DPP inference to inducing-point sparse GP methods, especially the Titsias variational construction.
- Empirics (1): In a 1D Gaussian setting with known spectrum, retrospective MCMC with the proposed bounds matches an ideal exact-determinant sampler.
- Empirics (2): On a spatial neuropathy dataset, variational learning recovers coherent overdispersion estimates and class differences across inducing-point budgets.
- Strength — novelty: Proposition 1 provides a simple, nonspectral lower-bound inequality that parallels variational sparse GP bounds.
- Strength — extension: The construction is extended to continuous DPPs, which is a significant contribution.
- Strength — practicality: Bounds are inexpensive (no eigen computations) and usable for both variational learning and provably-correct retrospective MCMC.
- Strength — writing: Paper is clear, technically careful, and well organized.
- Weakness — computational tradeoff: Optimizing Z (and θ) in growing dimension may offset benefits of avoiding power iterations; wall-clock comparisons to spectral methods are missing.
- Weakness — bound refinement: Joint, nonconvex optimization over Z may not guarantee monotonic tightening as m increases, risking practicality of retrospective MH.
- Weakness — experimental scope: Limited experiments; needs more systematic finite-domain DPP tests and broader scalability/runtime evaluations.
- Quality: Theoretical propositions are sound and MCMC construction follows established retrospective logic; empirical results are supportive but need more robustness analysis.
- Clarity issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels; Proposition 1 proof should be in main text with intuition; minor typos; suggest adding retrospective MH pseudocode.
- Originality: New nonspectral determinant bounds for finite and continuous DPPs and the GP-inducing connection are original and insightful.
- Significance: Potentially broadens DPP applicability by removing spectral reliance and offers scalable variational and exact MCMC routes.
- Suggested experiments: Head-to-head comparisons with spectral baselines (time, memory, accuracy, acceptance rates) and ablations on optimizer choices and initialization strategies.
- Suggested analysis: Study refinement behavior (m needed per MH iteration), sensitivity to Z initialization, and conditions/strategies for bound convergence or enforced monotonicity.
- Suggested application: Add a discrete-domain task (e.g., document summarization or subset selection) to demonstrate broader utility.
- Pros for accept: Strong theoretical contribution, removes spectral reliance, enables both variational and MCMC inference, and links to influential GP methodology.
- Cons for accept: Unclear practical computational advantage, no guarantees on bound tightening with local optimization, and limited experimental breadth.
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on stronger empirical comparisons and discussion/analysis of optimization robustness.
