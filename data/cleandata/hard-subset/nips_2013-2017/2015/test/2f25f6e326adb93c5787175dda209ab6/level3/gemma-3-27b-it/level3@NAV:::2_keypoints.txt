- Proposes likelihood-based DPP inference without costly spectral decompositions via novel upper and lower bounds.  
- Enables both variational inference and MCMC by avoiding spectral computations.  
- Core idea: adapt GP variational-inference-style bounds to DPPs using pseudo-inputs.  
- Mathematical derivations appear sound, with full proofs deferred to the supplement.  
- Bounds are general and applicable to both finite and continuous DPPs.  
- Paper is well-positioned in related work, citing key DPP and inference literature.  
- Experiments (synthetic Gaussian and real nerve-fiber data) validate the approach and highlight cases where variational inference struggles.  
- Emphasis on parameterizing L instead of K is noted, but the implications need deeper discussion.  
- Supplement is lengthy/structured like an extended paper, potentially less accessible for reviewers.  
- Suggested future work: derive determinant bounds directly in terms of K.  
- Suggested evaluation extension: assess scalability of pseudo-input method with increasing data dimensionality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, practical significance, theoretical soundness, and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of L vs K parameterization and supplement accessibility.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept.
