- Addresses likelihood-based parameter inference for DPPs and the challenge of computing the normalizing constant det(L+I) (or Fredholm determinant).
- Proposes novel non-spectral upper and lower bounds on the DPP likelihood using m inducing "pseudo-inputs" Z, avoiding eigenvalue computations.
- Connects the inducing-point bounds to sparse Gaussian Process approximations and optimizes Z to tighten bounds.
- Integrates the bounds into two inference schemes: retrospective MCMC (avoiding spectral decompositions) and a novel variational inference (VI) framework for DPPs.
- Methods apply to both finite-set DPPs and continuous-domain DPPs.
- Technical contribution is strong; the derivation (notably Proposition 1) is non-trivial and well-supported by experiments.
- Experiments on synthetic and real diabetic neuropathy data demonstrate utility and reveal issues like parameter non-identifiability with L-kernel parameterization.
- VI experiments show convergence of log-likelihood bounds and parameter estimates as m increases.
- Paper is well-written and organized, presenting finite case first then continuous case.
- Weak clarity issues: main proof relegated to supplement (should include a sketch in main text), Figure 1 lacks axis labels, and minor typos exist.
- Original and impactful idea: first VI framework for DPPs and a novel non-spectral alternative to prior spectral methods.
- Significance tempered by computational concerns: optimizing inducing points Z introduces a potentially costly non-convex optimization that may offset spectral-method savings.
- Reliability concern for retrospective MCMC: non-convex optimization and local minima might prevent bounds from tightening sufficiently, harming exactness or speed.
- Overall recommendation: accept — novelty and contributions outweigh weaknesses, but authors should better discuss computational trade-offs and optimization risks.
