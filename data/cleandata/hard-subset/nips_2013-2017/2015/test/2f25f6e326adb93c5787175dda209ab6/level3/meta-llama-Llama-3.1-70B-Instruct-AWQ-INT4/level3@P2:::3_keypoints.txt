- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate nonspectral bounds on determinants for likelihoods of finite and continuous DPPs.  
- Avoids spectral decomposition by using inducing inputs, inspired by sparse Gaussian process approximations.  
- Bounds are applicable to both exact (MCMC) and approximate (variational) inference and do not rely on approximating large spectra.  
- Paper is well-motivated, technically solid, and includes proofs in the supplement.  
- Writes a clear introduction to DPPs, their properties, and inference challenges.  
- Method description assumes strong background in DPPs, GPs, and operator theory, making it hard to follow for some readers.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks thorough comparisons to other approximate and exact alternatives.  
- Variational inference shows large parameter estimation errors on toy data and appears to depend on approximate MCMC; failure modes are unclear.  
- No guidance on when variational inference will succeed or fail and why.  
- Authors do not provide rule-of-thumb guidance for selecting number, initialization, or placement of inducing points.  
- Missing comparisons of computational complexity and wall-clock time between proposed methods.  
- Significant and original contribution with potential impact, demonstrated on several datasets including a real-world spatial dataset.  
- Recommendations: improve method clarity, strengthen empirical comparisons and complexity/runtime analysis, and add practical guidance on inducing point selection.
