- Proposes nonspectral, computable upper and lower bounds on log det(I+L) for DPP likelihoods using an inducing-point construction and a determinant inequality.
- Uses Q = L_YZ L_Z^{-1} L_ZY and Seiler & Simon (1975) inequality to get det(Q+I) e^{-tr(L−Q)} ≤ det(I+L) ≤ det(Q+I).
- Derives tractable bounds enabling (i) a variational lower bound for approximate MLE and (ii) retrospective MH with bracketing of the acceptance prob., tightened by increasing m and optimizing Z.
- Extends approach to continuous DPPs via Ψ_{ij} = ∫ L(zi,x)L(x,zj)dµ(x), yielding bounds involving det(L_Z+Ψ) and avoiding Fredholm spectrum approximations.
- Builds on variational sparse GP ideas (Titsias, 2009) and contrasts with spectral/Nyström, EM/fixed-point, and Fredholm-det approximation methods.
- Key novelty: bounding DPP normalizers without explicit spectrum approximation; makes continuous-domain case practical when Ψ is tractable.
- Strength: conceptually simple, elegant bounds that avoid expensive eigendecompositions and maintain correctness via retrospective MH.
- Strength: creative use of pseudo-inputs to control bound tightness, with a clear inducing-point analogy to GPs.
- Strength: general framework covering both finite and continuous DPPs, with particular value for continuous spectra.
- Strength: experiments validate MH on a toy case and show useful variational behavior on a real spatial dataset.
- Strength: candid discussion of VI failure modes (e.g., κ identifiability) and presenting MH as a robust alternative.
- Strength: timely and relevant for ML work on repulsive point processes and diversity modeling.
- Weakness: no quantification of convergence rates or practical guidance for choosing m; lacks empirical diagnostics like gap vs. m curves.
- Weakness: dependence on optimized Z not deeply analyzed—initialization, optimization stability, and cost need more study and comparisons to heuristics.
- Weakness: continuous case practicality depends on Ψ; paper lacks broader guidance on when Ψ is available or how to approximate it with error control.
- Weakness: limited empirical comparisons to strong finite-DPP baselines (Nyström, EM), lacking wall-clock and accuracy benchmarks.
- Weakness: brief treatment of K vs L parameterization; could explore expressing bounds in terms of K and pros/cons.
- Weakness: numerical stability and complexity for multiple realizations with large |Y_t| (cost of det(L_{Y_t})) need more detail.
- Minor: request for a standard supplementary PDF with proofs, algorithmic details, and gradients.
- Theoretical development is sound; extensions to continuous DPPs are carefully stated under appropriate assumptions.
- Algorithms and retrospective MH are correctly derived; experiments validate correctness but need broader scaling comparisons.
- Clarity: generally well written; suggests adding intuition for the determinant inequality, pseudo-code, and guidance on m and Z.
- Originality: novel application of determinant inequalities with inducing-point constructions to DPP normalizers, especially for continuous domains.
- Significance: lowers a major barrier (spectral costs) for DPP inference and may extend to other log-det normalizer models.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, general idea with solid theory, practical algorithms, and demonstrated relevance and limitations.
- Cons against acceptance: limited empirical scaling evidence, dependence on tractable Ψ, and limited guidance on selecting m and optimizing Z.
- Minor suggestions: clarify κ identifiability under L, explore inducing-point selection strategies, and consider experiments bounding determinant in terms of K.
- Overall recommendation: leaning toward acceptance due to clear, broadly useful contribution likely to stimulate follow-up work.
