- Tackles likelihood-based inference for DPPs where the normalization constant is intractable.  
- Novel non-spectral bounds that avoid estimating the kernel spectrum entirely.  
- Key insight: connecting DPPs to inducing-point methods from sparse Gaussian Processes.  
- Enables a variational inference framework for DPPs for the first time, allowing scalable approximate learning.  
- Proposition 1’s lower-bound inequality is a central, novel technical contribution.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and the approach is well-motivated and elegant.  
- Major contribution: variational extension that can be faster and more scalable than MCMC.  
- Concern: unclear if replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimension is a net computational win—needs practical trade-off analysis.  
- Concern: joint optimization over kernel parameters and inducing points is likely non-convex; local minima could prevent bounds from tightening and harm retrospective MCMC efficiency.  
- Minor presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels, the proof of Proposition 1 should be moved to the main text, and fix a typo ("the variational lower is").  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, impactful work with strong acceptance likelihood.
