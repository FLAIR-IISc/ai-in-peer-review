- Addresses intractable DPP likelihood normalization by avoiding spectral decomposition.  
- Prior work used power-iteration spectral approximations for retrospective MCMC, which scale poorly.  
- Proposes nonspectral lower and upper bounds on the determinant term using inducing points inspired by sparse GPs.  
- Key theoretical contribution: Proposition 1 gives tight determinant bounds without eigenvalues, enabling variational inference and MCMC.  
- Bounds derived via a nontrivial determinant inequality and extended to continuous domains.  
- Experiments (synthetic and diabetic neuropathy data) validate approach: variational inference shows parameter convergence as inducing points increase; retrospective MCMC matches ideal inference.  
- Strong technical quality and clear exposition; insightful connection to Gaussian process inducing points.  
- Computational advantage over prior spectral methods not conclusively demonstrated—optimization over inducing points and kernel parameters may offset gains.  
- Convergence under coupled optimization of inducing points and kernel parameters is not rigorously addressed; sensitivity to initialization and local minima not quantified.  
- Presentation issues: key proof placed in appendix, Figure 1 missing axis labels, and minor typographical errors.  
- Original and significant: avoids eigenvalue estimation, potentially applicable to other models with intractable partition functions.  
- Overall recommendation: technically strong and suitable for acceptance, though further empirical validation of computational trade-offs is needed.
