- Presents a novel inference method for DPPs that does not require spectral decomposition.
- Derives new likelihood bounds for both finite and continuous DPP domains.
- Bounds enable practical variational inference and MCMC for DPPs.
- Technique is inspired by sparse Gaussian process inducing-variable approximations.
- Relies on a nontrivial determinant inequality and is cheap to evaluate.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough DPP introduction.
- Experimental evaluation is well-constructed and demonstrates effectiveness.
- Addresses a timely, significant limitation in DPP inference with high potential impact.
- Original contribution: new bounds and connection to variational sparse GPs.
- Minor weakness: unclear justification for parameterizing kernel L instead of K.
- Minor weakness: lacks detailed discussion of limitations and future work.
- Suggestion: clarify importance of L vs K and expand limitations/future directions.
- Suggestion: consider additional experiments to further validate the approach.
- Overall recommendation: accept (rating 9/10).
