- Introduces non-spectral bounds for DPP likelihoods that avoid expensive spectral decomposition.  
- Enables variational inference and MCMC for DPP parameters using the proposed bounds.  
- Draws a strong and useful connection to inducing-point techniques from scalable Gaussian Process inference.  
- Proposition 1 provides a key lower-bound inequality and is a notable theoretical contribution.  
- Technical derivations are strong and the paper is generally well-written and clear.  
- Correctly frames the computational bottleneck as the intractable normalization constant and builds on prior work to circumvent eigenvalue estimation.  
- Discusses trade-offs between parametrizing K versus L and notes loss of interpretability with L.  
- Variational inference capability and GP links increase the method’s potential impact and hybrid modeling use cases.  
- Experiments, though simple, validate feasibility and show MCMC matches exact solution in a 1D Gaussian case.  
- Practical applicability demonstrated via a diabetic neuropathy dataset experiment.  
- Computational cost concern: optimizing pseudo-inputs Z may not be a clear win over power iterations for large-scale problems.  
- Convergence concern: increasing inducing points doesn’t guarantee bound convergence and non-convex optimization may trap in local minima, affecting MCMC validity.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels and the proof of Proposition 1 should be moved to the main text.  
- Minor wording correction needed ("the variational lower is...").  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses significant DPP inference bottleneck, strong theoretical novelty, and useful GP connections.  
- Rejection concerns: unclear computational advantage, potential convergence issues, and some presentation shortcomings.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve presentation, include the proof in main text, label figures, and discuss complexity and optimization behavior.
