- Proposes novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants with applications to bounding DPP likelihoods.  
- Main idea is simple, well-executed, and illuminating, enabling future improvements.  
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.  
- Approach is general and potentially applicable beyond determinantal point processes.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, well-presented, and demonstrates key behaviors including variational approximation failures.  
- Clear and concise exposition of DPPs, properties, and likelihood-inference challenges.  
- Authors explain applications to variational inference and MCMC and analyze bound properties in detail.  
- Paper is original, significant, and timely for the community.  
- High quality and clarity: well-written, easy to follow, and thoroughly analyzed.  
- Minor unclear point: emphasis on parameterizing K instead of L is not fully justified or explored.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; overall scores ~9/10 across quality, originality, significance, and clarity.  
- Suggested future work: bound determinant directly via K and assess scalability, explore kernel connections between L and K without spectral knowledge, and consider DPP variants conditioning on sample size for classification priors.
