- Presents a novel method to bound DPP likelihoods without requiring spectral knowledge.  
- Uses a non-trivial determinant inequality and pseudo-inputs to derive upper and lower matrix determinant bounds.  
- Applies the bounds to DPP likelihoods for both finite and continuous domains.  
- Creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.  
- Clear and concise introduction to DPPs and thorough review of prior work and limitations.  
- Well-written exposition with effective figures and tables.  
- Experimental evaluation includes a toy Gaussian continuous experiment and a real-world nerve-fiber spatial dataset.  
- Results show accurate and efficient inference for DPPs in experiments.  
- Approach is general and may have applications beyond DPPs; suggestion to bound determinant directly in terms of K as future work.  
- Minor criticism: insufficient in-depth discussion on parameterizing K versus L and trade-offs.  
- Minor criticism: supplement provided only as an extended paper version instead of a standard supplement.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-executed, and likely of interest to the community.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novelty, creative pseudo-input approach, thorough experiments, and clear writing.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: limited discussion on K vs L parameterization and supplement format.  
- Rating: 9/10; Confidence: 9/10.
