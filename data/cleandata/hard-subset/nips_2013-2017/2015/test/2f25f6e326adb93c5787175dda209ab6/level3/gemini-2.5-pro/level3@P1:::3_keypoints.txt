- Proposes non-spectral upper and lower bounds on det(L+I) using m inducing inputs, avoiding costly eigendecomposition and paralleling sparse GP approximations.  
- Introduces two inference schemes: a fast variational inference (VI) maximizing the lower bound, and an asymptotically exact MCMC using the bounds for retrospective Metropolis–Hastings.  
- Methods developed for both finite-set and continuous-domain DPPs.  
- Theoretical derivations (Propositions 1 and 2) appear sound, based on a known determinantal inequality.  
- Novel connection between sparse GPs and DPPs; moving away from spectral approximations is a significant conceptual contribution.  
- Potential to scale DPPs by avoiding O(n^3) eigendecomposition, offering practitioners a speed/accuracy trade-off.  
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient to demonstrate practical advantages; experiments show methods run but lack comparative analysis.  
- No direct internal comparison between VI and MCMC; missing quantitative trade-off of wall-clock time versus parameter accuracy.  
- No comparisons to external baselines (e.g., Nyström-based approximations or spectral-bound MCMC), so practical significance is unclear.  
- VI yields poor, inconsistent results on a toy example and fails to identify parameter κ, undermining confidence in the VI approach.  
- Unclear when VI will succeed or fail; need analysis whether failures stem from loose bounds, nonconvex optimization, or identifiability.  
- Method performance hinges on number and placement of inducing inputs Z, but paper gives little guidance on choosing m or initializing Z.  
- Recommends providing rules-of-thumb or sensitivity analysis for m and discussing initialization strategies for reproducibility.  
- Missing experiment comparing Nyström-style choice of inducing inputs (observed data) versus joint optimization to connect with existing schemes.  
- Clarity issues: distinction between marginal kernel K and L-ensemble kernel L is not well explained, and intro motivation (parametrizing K) is disconnected from the method (parametrizing L).  
- Overall recommendation: promising but currently a proof-of-concept; not ready for publication without substantially stronger experiments, comparisons, and analysis.
