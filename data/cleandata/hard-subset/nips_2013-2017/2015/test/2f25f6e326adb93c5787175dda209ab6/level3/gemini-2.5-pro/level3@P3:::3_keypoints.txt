- Proposes a novel non-spectral, inducing-point-based approximation for DPP likelihoods that avoids costly spectral decomposition.  
- Develops two inference algorithms: a fast approximate variational inference (VI) and an asymptotically exact but heavier MCMC with retrospective sampling.  
- Method applies to DPPs on both finite and continuous domains and shows feasibility on toy and spatial datasets.  
- Strength: original and significant idea extending sparse-GP inducing input ideas to DPPs.  
- Strength: technically elegant connection to sparse Gaussian processes (Titsias, 2009) providing intuition and justification.  
- Strength: offers practical impact by providing both fast VI and more robust MCMC within one framework.  
- Weakness: methodological description is rough and exposition lacks clarity.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is insufficient to characterize performance and limitations.  
- Major issue — Lack of comparative analysis: no direct VI vs MCMC comparison, no big-O or wall-clock runtimes provided.  
- Major issue — Lack of comparative analysis: no benchmarks against alternative approximations (e.g., Nyström) or exact inference to assess quality vs runtime trade-offs.  
- Major issue — Failure of VI: VI gives unstable/poor results (up to 100% parameter error) on the toy problem with inadequate analysis or explanation.  
- Major issue — VI uncertainty: authors must clarify when VI should work or fail (e.g., loose bounds, unstable joint optimization).  
- Major issue — Guidance on inducing inputs: insufficient practical advice on choosing number m and placement of inducing inputs Z.  
- Major issue — Suggestion to test sanity case: evaluate using inducing inputs equal to sampled points to check bound tightness.  
- Minor issue — Clarity: key DPP concepts (marginal kernel K, L-ensemble, relation K = L(I+L)^{-1}) are not clearly defined for readers.  
- Minor issue — Inconsistency: paper discusses learning K but technical work focuses on parameterizing L, creating a disconnect.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on substantial revision addressing empirical comparisons, VI failure analysis, inducing-input guidance, and clearer exposition.
