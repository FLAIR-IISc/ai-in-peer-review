- Addresses inference in DPPs by proposing novel non-spectral bounds for finite and continuous domains to avoid expensive spectral decomposition and enable variational and MCMC parameter learning.  
- Paper is well-motivated and well-situated in DPP and GP literature, with a useful connection to variational GP/inducing point techniques.  
- Proposition 1 (non-spectral bounds) is a significant technical contribution and the derivations appear sound.  
- Originality: avoiding spectral decomposition is novel and addresses a major bottleneck in existing DPP inference methods.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, consistent notation, and the continuous-domain extension demonstrates generality.  
- Significance: proposed bounds could substantially speed DPP inference and make models more practical at scale; supports both variational and MCMC approaches.  
- Computational gains not fully established: unclear whether optimizing inducing inputs (Z) and the resulting optimization/MCMC cost is always cheaper than power-iteration-based spectral methods for large n.  
- Convergence concerns: retrospective MCMC’s reliance on increasing inducing set size lacks clear convergence guarantees and may suffer from local minima, risking validity of samples.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels, proof of Proposition 1 is only in the supplement, and there are minor wording errors.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound solution with promising scalability implications and supportive (if limited) experiments.  
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient empirical demonstration of computational advantages, unresolved MCMC convergence issues, and presentation/parameterization concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—move Prop.1 proof to main text, label Figure 1 axes, analyze computational complexity and conditions for efficiency, and discuss strategies to mitigate MCMC convergence issues.
