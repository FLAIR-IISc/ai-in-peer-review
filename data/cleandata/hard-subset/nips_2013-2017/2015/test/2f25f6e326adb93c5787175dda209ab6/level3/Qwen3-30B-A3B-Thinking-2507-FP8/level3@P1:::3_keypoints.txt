- Novel contribution: derives nonspectral bounds for DPP likelihoods, avoiding costly eigendecomposition.  
- Significant practical goal: reduces computational overhead for large-scale DPP inference by leveraging inducing inputs Z.  
- Well-motivated theory: connects sparse Gaussian process techniques to DPPs and extends to continuous domains.  
- Empirical positives: shows convergence with increasing inducing points and retrospective MCMC recovers parameters in the toy example.  
- Reproducibility issues: insufficient detail on initializing and optimizing Z.  
- Missing guidance: no rule-of-thumb or justification for choosing the number of inducing points m.  
- Unexplained design choice: no rationale for placing Z away from data points (Figure 1a).  
- Insufficient comparisons: no wall-clock or accuracy comparisons between VI, retrospective MCMC, and prior spectral methods [7]/[8].  
- VI failure unexplained: variational inference completely fails to recover parameters in the toy example without analysis of when it should succeed.  
- Crucial omission: did not test using actual data points as Z (e.g., m = |Y|) to assess bound tightness and practical utility.  
- Complexity/scale unclear: paper fails to explicitly contrast proposed O(nm²) cost with spectral alternatives, hindering scalability claims.  
- Runtime not contextualized: reports (e.g., 24 minutes for m=1200) without comparison to eigendecomposition costs.  
- Terminology confusion: inconsistent definitions/use of L versus marginal kernel K and missing early definitions for DPP newcomers.  
- Originality mixed: bound derivation is novel, but MCMC adaptation via retrospective sampling is not.  
- Recommendation/overall judgment: theoretically sound but empirically weak and unclear—recommend reject pending stronger empirical validation and clarity.
