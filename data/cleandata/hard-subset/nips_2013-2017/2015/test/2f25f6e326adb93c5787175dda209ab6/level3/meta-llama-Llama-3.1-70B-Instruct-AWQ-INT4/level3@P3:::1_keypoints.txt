- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate, nonspectral bounds on determinants in DPP likelihoods for finite and continuous cases.  
- Derives bounds using inducing-variable ideas from sparse Gaussian process approximations, avoiding spectral decompositions.  
- Main technical contribution: two propositions providing upper and lower likelihood bounds that are usable for variational inference and MCMC.  
- Bounds are computationally cheaper to evaluate than spectral methods and connect well to GP and variational inference literature.  
- Experimental validation includes a toy Gaussian continuous example and a real-world spatial nerve-fiber dataset, showing effectiveness.  
- Paper is technically strong, clearly written, well-organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Authors provide thorough related-work discussion and acknowledge limitations.  
- Strength: enables learning DPP parameters without spectral knowledge, improving scalability for large problems.  
- Concern: replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimension may not be an obvious computational win for large datasets.  
- Concern: increasing inducing set/cardinality may not guarantee bound convergence due to coupled, likely non-convex optimizations and risk of local minima affecting MCMC validity.  
- Minor presentation issues: missing axis labels in Figure 1, main proof (Proposition 1) placed in appendix, and a small typo (“the variational lower is ...”).  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution, recommend accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
