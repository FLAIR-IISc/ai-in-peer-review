- Paper presents novel upper and lower bounds on matrix determinants to bound DPP likelihoods.  
- Main contribution: using "pseudo-inputs" to derive bounds that avoid spectral decomposition, enabling efficient variational inference and exact MCMC.  
- Addresses a key challenge in DPP inference (intractable spectral decomposition) with a simple, effective approach.  
- Builds on sparse GP approximations and extends them to DPPs, offering a general framework beyond DPPs.  
- Theoretical contributions are elegant and technically sound, with rigorous proofs in the supplement.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: synthetic experiments and real-world diabetic neuropathy data.  
- Experiments show bounds are effective and reveal limitations of variational methods in some settings.  
- Writing and organization are clear; the continuous case is well-motivated for non-expert readers.  
- Strengths: originality, significance for practical DPP inference, solid theory, and well-constructed experiments.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient justification for parameterizing K instead of L (Section 1).  
- Weaknesses: supplement formatting should follow standard style rather than read like an extended paper.  
- Suggested future work: explore direct bounds in terms of K and clarify interpretability trade-offs of L parameterization.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution with practical implications; recommended for acceptance.
