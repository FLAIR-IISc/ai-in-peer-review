- Addresses learning DPP parameters by avoiding intractable normalization det(L+I) using novel non-spectral bounds.
- Constructs low-rank approximation Q via m inducing (pseudo-)inputs, yielding tractable upper and non-trivial lower bounds (Prop.1) on the partition function.
- Bounds avoid eigenvalue computations and apply to both finite and continuous DPPs.
- Enables two inference frameworks: a fast variational inference scheme and an exact retrospective MCMC sampler.
- Major originality: non-spectral bounds and Prop.1 represent a significant theoretical advance over prior spectral methods.
- Expands inference capabilities by making scalable, deterministic variational inference for DPPs feasible and allows joint optimization of kernel and inducing points.
- Elegant theoretical connection drawn between DPP inference and sparse/inducing-point GP methods, encouraging cross-pollination.
- Strong technical quality and clarity; rigorous derivations and convincing experiments on synthetic and real data.
- Concern: replacing spectral methods with non-convex optimization over inducing points may not always be computationally superior; complexity scales with m (e.g., O(n m^2)).
- Concern: retrospective MCMC depends on re-optimizing inducing points; non-convexity risks poor local minima and potential sampler stalling—practical robustness and initialization strategies are needed.
- Presentation issues: key proof relegated to supplement (should include a sketch), Figure 1(a) axes unlabeled, and several typos/referencing errors that need proofreading.
- Overall assessment: excellent, original, technically strong work that substantially advances DPP inference; recommended for acceptance despite practical/formatting concerns.
