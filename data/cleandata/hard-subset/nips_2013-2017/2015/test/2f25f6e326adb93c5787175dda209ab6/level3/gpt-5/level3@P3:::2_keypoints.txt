- Tackles intractable normalizing constants in DPPs by deriving non-spectral upper and lower determinant bounds using low-rank pseudo-input approximations.
- Bounds apply to both finite and continuous DPPs and replace spectral methods with inducing-point based determinant inequalities.
- Continuous-domain bounds require only small Gram matrices on inducing inputs Z and kernel–measure integrals Ψ for common kernels.
- Enables a variational lower bound on the log-likelihood and a retrospective Metropolis–Hastings scheme with provably correct decisions by refining Z.
- Novel and clean determinant bounds that are simple to evaluate and broadly applicable beyond DPPs.
- Creative and effective transfer of inducing-point / sparse GP ideas to bound functionals of positive definite kernels.
- Empirical evaluation is solid, including a continuous 1D validation and a realistic neuropathy dataset, and honestly shows variational failure modes.
- Method is timely and likely to interest the DPP and spatial statistics communities.
- Limited guidance on how bound tightness scales with number of inducing points m and quality of Z; more quantitative analysis is needed.
- For continuous DPPs, availability of closed-form Ψ beyond Gaussian–Gaussian kernels is unclear; numerical quadrature costs/stability should be discussed.
- Paper emphasizes K-parameterization early but mainly operates on L; this mismatch should be clarified or toned down.
- Details on optimizing Z (stability, initialization, per-iteration cost, conditioning) are lacking and important for large m.
- Empirical comparisons to spectral truncation and Nyström baselines are sparse; stronger accuracy-runtime baselines would strengthen claims.
- Supplement presentation (pointing to an “extended version”) hinders reproducibility; a standard supplementary PDF with proofs/experiments is recommended.
- Technically sound with clear propositions and correct retrospective MH adaptation.
- Generally well written and organized; practical recipe for selecting m, initializing Z, and computing Ψ would improve clarity.
- Original contribution: eigen-free determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods using inducing-point machinery is creative and nontrivial.
- Significant impact potential: removes spectral barrier for scalable DPP inference and could transfer to other kernel models involving det(I+L).
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, generality, elimination of spectral requirements, and convincing empirical demonstrations.
- Cons for acceptance: limited quantitative tightness analysis, sparse baseline comparisons, and missing practical implementation details.
- Suggested future work: visualize bound gaps vs m/Z, explore K-parameterized bounds, test scalability in higher dimensions, compare with Fredholm/quadrature and hybrid eigen+pseudo-input approaches, and release code with standard supplement.
- Minor fixes: clarify K vs L emphasis and provide a standard supplementary PDF rather than only an extended version.
