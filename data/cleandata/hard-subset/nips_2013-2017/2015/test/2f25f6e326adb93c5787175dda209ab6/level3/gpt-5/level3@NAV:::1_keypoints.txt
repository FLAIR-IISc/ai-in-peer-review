- Tackles intractable DPP normalization det(I+L) with nonspectral inducing-point determinant bounds.
- Proposition 1 gives tight upper/lower determinant bounds using inducing points plus a trace correction; extended to continuous Fredholm determinants.
- Bounds support exact retrospective MH and a variational objective, drawing a connection to sparse GP inducing methods.
- Technical approach is strong and largely sound, leveraging Seiler–Simon inequalities; continuous-domain Ψ computable.
- Retrospective MCMC adaptation is principled and experiments validate correctness on 1D and real-data examples.
- Replacing spectral power iterations with nonlinear optimization over growing inducing sets may be computationally expensive.
- No guarantee bounds tighten monotonically as m increases due to coupled nonconvex optimization; local minima could stall MH decisions.
- Paper is well written and organized, but Figure 1 lacks axis labels and Proposition 1 should be placed in the main text; minor typos present.
- Originality is high: nonspectral determinant bounds for DPPs and variational usage are novel and insightful.
- Potentially significant: broadens applicability to large finite and continuous DPPs and related kernel models.
- Pros: general bounds enabling VI and provably correct retrospective MCMC without spectral info; clear GP connections; continuous-domain treatment.
- Cons: unresolved practical computational tradeoffs versus spectral methods; lack of monotonic tightening guarantees; potential MCMC deadlocks; presentation issues.
- Recommendations: provide wall-clock and accuracy comparisons to spectral retrospective MCMC; clarify conditions for m→∞ tightening; discuss Z optimization strategies (initialization, regularization, stochastic gradients); document failure modes and diagnostics; fix figure labels and move Proposition 1 proof.
