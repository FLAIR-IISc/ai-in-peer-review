- Proposes novel determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods using pseudo-inputs, avoiding costly spectral decomposition.  
- Leverages variational/inducing-variable ideas from sparse GPs to approximate kernel operators for DPPs.  
- Theoretical contributions (Propositions 1 & 2) are sound and grounded in known determinant inequalities.  
- Method addresses a key computational bottleneck in DPP inference, enabling more efficient likelihood-based methods.  
- Approach is original in applying pseudo-input bounds directly to DPP determinants.  
- Paper is timely and potentially widely applicable beyond DPPs.  
- Writing and notation are clear, with appropriate contextualization in related work.  
- Experimental validation is solid: 1D Gaussian example verifies MCMC behavior and identifiability issues; diabetic neuropathy study demonstrates practical utility and compares to prior work.  
- Claims of generality and broader impact are plausible and supported by experiments.  
- Weakness: choice to parameterize L rather than K is insufficiently justified and its implications are not deeply explored.  
- Weakness: supplement presented as an "extended paper version" rather than a standard organized supplement for proofs/experiments.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed scalability analysis with respect to dataset size, dimensionality, and number of pseudo-inputs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel, theoretically sound contribution with empirical support and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of L vs K parameterization, suboptimal supplement format, and limited scalability analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper—recommend accept with minor revisions to supplement format, scalability discussion, and clarification of the L vs K choice.
