- Problem addressed: efficient likelihood-based inference for DPPs that avoids costly spectral decompositions.  
- Core idea: derive bounds on the DPP likelihood inspired by Gaussian process variational inference to bypass eigenvalue computations.  
- Technical contributions: Propositions 1 and 2 provide bounds for finite and continuous DPPs; pseudo-inputs are used to tighten bounds.  
- Theoretical grounding: connections to sparse variational Gaussian processes and matrix theory; derivations appear mathematically sound.  
- Practical relevance: removes a major computational bottleneck, enabling broader DPP applications (diversity modeling, recommendations, spatial stats).  
- Experimental validation: toy Gaussian continuous MCMC demonstrates identifiability issues and MCMC implementation.  
- Real-data experiment: diabetic neuropathy dataset shows convergence with more inducing variables and reproduces prior findings.  
- Methods compared/evaluated: both variational inference and MCMC are used, strengthening empirical evaluation.  
- Clarity and presentation: well-written, logically organized, and clear exposition with good literature connections.  
- Originality and significance: novel, practical approach; pseudo-input technique is creative and effective.  
- Weakness — parameterization discussion: treatment of trade-offs between parameterizing L vs K is superficial and needs deeper exploration.  
- Weakness — supplementary material: referencing an "extended paper version" is non-standard; recommend a proper PDF supplement with proofs, extra results, and code.  
- Future work suggestion: exploring bounds expressed directly in terms of K is promising but requires discussion of challenges and scalability of pseudo-inputs.  
- For acceptance: technically sound, clear, strong experiments, and likely impactful—recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Against acceptance: incomplete discussion of kernel parameterization, non-standard supplement, and lack of demonstration on larger-scale problems beyond spectral methods.
