- Proposes a novel image restoration method that combines generative and discriminative models via a discriminatively trained gating network.  
- Gating network predicts latent variables for a generative Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) within a feed-forward architecture.  
- Achieves significant computational cost reduction while preserving generative model modularity and accuracy.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise explanation of the approach and advantages.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the method's effectiveness.  
- Comparison to state-of-the-art (including BM3D and discriminative methods) shows comparable performance with greater efficiency.  
- Strength: novel hybrid approach that leverages strengths of both model types.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments validating the method.  
- Strength: clarity and accessibility of the presentation.  
- Weakness: reliance on a specific generative model (GMM) may limit generality.  
- Weakness: current implementation needs further optimization and parallelization for larger speedups.  
- Recommendation: overall contribution is significant and the paper should be accepted, given its potential for wide applicability and efficient performance.
