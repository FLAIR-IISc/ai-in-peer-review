- Proposes a novel inference approach for DPPs that avoids spectral decomposition.  
- Main technical contribution: derivation of likelihood bounds for DPPs without spectral knowledge.  
- Uses those bounds to develop variational inference (VI) and MCMC algorithms for DPPs.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear background and motivation.  
- Technical details and related-work discussion are thorough.  
- Method description is rough in places and needs clearer exposition.  
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient and needs strengthening.  
- Lacks detailed comparison of computational complexity and wall-clock runtimes between proposed methods.  
- No comparisons to other approximate or exact alternatives in terms of approximation quality and runtime.  
- VI shows large parameter estimation error on toy data and depends on approximate MCMC.  
- Authors should clarify conditions when VI is expected to succeed or fail and explain why.  
- Suggestion to evaluate methods when inducing inputs are chosen as actual sampled points.  
- Needs a more detailed discussion of implications and potential applications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
