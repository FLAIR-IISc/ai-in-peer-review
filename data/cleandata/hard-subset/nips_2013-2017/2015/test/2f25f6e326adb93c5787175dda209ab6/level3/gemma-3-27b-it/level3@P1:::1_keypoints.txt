- Introduces novel bounds on DPP likelihoods that avoid spectral decomposition.  
- Bounds apply to both finite and continuous DPPs, enabling variational and MCMC inference.  
- Proposition 1 provides the core, technically sound bounds, inspired by variational GP approximations.  
- Leverages inducing-point techniques, drawing a clear connection to sparse Gaussian process methods.  
- Extension to continuous DPPs broadens the method's applicability.  
- Avoiding eigenvalue computations is theoretically attractive and could improve scalability.  
- Practical advantages over existing methods are not conclusively demonstrated, especially in high dimensions.  
- Optimizing inducing-point locations may increase computational cost and introduce difficult nonlinear optimization.  
- Convergence behavior as inducing-point count increases is not fully addressed; risk of local minima.  
- Retrospective MCMC reliability depends on bound tightness and proposal choice, which are under-discussed.  
- Figure 1 lacks axis labels, reducing interpretability.  
- Proof of Proposition 1 should be moved into the main text rather than supplementary material.  
- Minor wording/typo issues need correction.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel paper worth acceptance with minor revisions on presentation and discussion of trade-offs/convergence.
