- Problem: DPP inference is bottlenecked by expensive spectral decomposition for likelihood-based methods.  
- Contribution: Proposes novel bounds on the DPP likelihood that avoid eigenvalue/spectral estimation.  
- Methodological insight: Leverages and connects inducing-point methods from Gaussian Processes to DPP inference.  
- Core result: Proposition 1 provides a spectral-free lower bound enabling variational inference and MCMC.  
- Extension: Framework generalized to continuous DPPs (Section 4).  
- Theoretical/expository quality: Technically strong, clear exposition, and good related-work coverage.  
- Practical claim: Potential for faster inference compared to spectral methods.  
- Empirical support: Demonstrated on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Computational concern: Replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimension may not yield practical speedups.  
- Convergence risk: Increasing inducing-point/cardinality can introduce local minima that may impede valid MCMC moves.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 lacks axis labels; the proof of Proposition 1 should be in the main text; minor wording errors.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address presentation and provide more discussion/analysis of computational trade-offs.
