- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate, nonspectral bounds on determinants in DPP likelihoods for finite and continuous cases.  
- Bounds avoid approximating the spectrum of large matrices/operators, making them useful for VI and moderately expensive exact MCMC.  
- Identifies two core inference difficulties: the likelihood involves a transformed kernel via intractable spectral decomposition and an intractable normalizing constant.  
- Suggests directly parametrizing the L-kernel (instead of marginal K), making the likelihood numerator cheap but reducing interpretability of parameters.  
- Derives nonspectral likelihood bounds using inducing inputs to approximate determinants without spectral decompositions.  
- Shows the bounds can be used for both exact-but-slower MCMC and cheap-but-approximate variational inference.  
- Introduces pseudo-inputs (inducing points) whose optimization tightens bounds; optimizing them is crucial for parametric DPP inference.  
- Empirical demonstration includes a toy Gaussian continuous experiment and real spatial nerve-fiber dataset, showing reasonable parameter recovery.  
- Method description is rough and needs clearer exposition of algorithms and steps.  
- Empirical evaluation is insufficient: lacks detailed computational complexity, wall-clock times, and comparisons to other approximate/exact methods.  
- Raises open questions about when variational inference will succeed or fail and the theoretical/empirical regimes for its reliability.  
- Asks how the method performs when inducing inputs are set to the actual sampled points and requests guidance on that scenario.  
- Requests rule-of-thumb guidance for choosing number of inducing points and their initialization/placement.  
- Clarity issues: add brief explanations of marginal kernel and L-ensembles for non-expert readers and reduce inconsistent discussion about learning marginal kernel parameters.  
- Recommendation: if accepted, improve clarity of method, strengthen empirical comparisons and runtime reporting, and address the raised questions and practical guidance.
