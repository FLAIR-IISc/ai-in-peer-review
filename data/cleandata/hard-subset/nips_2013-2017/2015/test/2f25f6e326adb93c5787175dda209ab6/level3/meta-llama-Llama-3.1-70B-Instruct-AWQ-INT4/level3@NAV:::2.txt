This paper proposes novel upper and lower bounds on the matrix determinant with applications to bounding DPP likelihoods. The main idea is simple, well-executed, and enlightening, paving the way for further improvements. The authors provide a creative use of pseudo-inputs to bound functionals of positive definite kernels, which is a timely problem likely to be of interest to the community. The approach is general and has potential applications beyond determinantal point processes.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, making it easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental evaluation. The experimental evaluation is well-constructed and illustrates key behaviors, including failures of a variational approximation under certain conditions.

The chosen application area is interesting and highly relevant, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work. However, there are a few minor issues with the paper. For example, it is unclear why parameterizing K (instead of L) is emphasized, since the paper does not address it in depth. Additionally, the authors recommend providing a standard supplement rather than only an extended paper version as the supplement.

Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field, and the results are important and likely to be of interest to other researchers. The paper advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way, and the authors provide unique insights and conclusions on existing data.
