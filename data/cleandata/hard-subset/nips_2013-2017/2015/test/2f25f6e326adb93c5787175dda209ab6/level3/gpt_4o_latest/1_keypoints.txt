- Addresses the intractable normalization constant in DPP likelihoods by deriving nonspectral bounds that avoid eigenvalue estimation.
- Introduces bounds enabling efficient variational inference and exact MCMC without spectral methods.
- Uses pseudo-inputs (inducing-point style) to tighten bounds, linking the approach to Gaussian process inducing methods.
- Derivations are mathematically rigorous, leveraging determinant inequalities.
- Method applies to both finite and continuous DPPs, extending prior applicability.
- Validated on synthetic and real-world data (including a diabetic neuropathy dataset) with competitive empirical performance.
- Unified framework supports both variational inference and MCMC, offering flexible trade-offs between speed and accuracy.
- Major concern: computational efficiency and convergence guarantees of the pseudo-input optimization are unclear.
- Non-convex optimization for pseudo-inputs risks local minima and could undermine MCMC reliability.
- Variability in variational inference results suggests parameter identifiability issues, especially when directly parameterizing kernel L.
- Scalability to very large datasets (e.g., millions of points) is not demonstrated or thoroughly discussed.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 missing axis labels, main proposition proof should be in the main body, and there are minor text errors.
- Pros: novel, practical solution that avoids eigenvalue bottleneck, strong theory, and cross-field connections.
- Cons: unresolved scalability and convergence questions plus presentation clarity problems.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing presentation issues and discussing scalability and convergence concerns.
