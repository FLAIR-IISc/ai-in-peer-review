- Paper addresses intractable DPP likelihood normalization by deriving nonspectral bounds that avoid spectral decomposition.  
- Main contributions: nonspectral lower/upper likelihood bounds for finite DPPs and extension to continuous domains.  
- Key insight (Proposition 1): use arbitrary inducing (pseudo-)points to bound the likelihood without eigen decomposition.  
- Connects DPP inference to sparse inducing-point Gaussian process methods.  
- Enables both variational (efficient) and MCMC (exact but costly) inference using the bounds.  
- Theoretical contribution is novel, technically strong, and well-explained.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real data (including diabetic neuropathy) convincingly validate the method.  
- Variational method empirically improves as number of inducing points increases.  
- Strength: eliminates need for expensive spectral methods like power iterations.  
- Major concern 1: claim of being "cheap to evaluate" is not fully supported—optimization over inducing points may match or exceed prior computational costs and lacks runtime comparison to [7].  
- Major concern 2: potential nonconvexity and convergence issues—no guarantees that increasing inducing points tightens bounds, problematic for MCMC correctness.  
- Minor issues: Figure 1 missing axis labels; Proposition 1 proof is placed in appendix rather than main text; small typo in text.  
- Quality: rigorous and validated; Clarity: generally good but could move key proof to main text; Originality: clear and novel; Significance: substantial potential impact.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing presentation issues and adding detailed computational comparisons and discussion of convergence guarantees.
