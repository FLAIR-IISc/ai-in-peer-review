- Proposes a novel, spectrum-free approach to DPP inference by deriving cheap-to-evaluate bounds on the likelihood/partition function.  
- Focuses on parametrizing kernel L directly to avoid intractable spectral decompositions of K.  
- Derives likelihood bounds for both finite and continuous domains inspired by sparse GP inducing-variable approximations.  
- Demonstrates how to use the bounds for variational inference and MCMC parameter learning for DPPs.  
- Paper is technically strong and well written, with clear presentation of ideas and results.  
- Connections to inducing-point techniques for scalable Gaussian process inference are appealing and extend impact.  
- Potential to enable faster DPP inference and link DPPs to other model classes.  
- Main computational concern: replacing power iterations with nonlinear optimization of growing dimension may not be a clear efficiency win.  
- Increasing inducing set/cardinality may not guarantee bound convergence due to coupled, likely non-convex optimizations.  
- Non-convexity and local minima could invalidate MCMC transitions or hinder reliable inference.  
- Minor presentation issues: missing axis labels in Figure 1 and main proof (Proposition 1) placed in the appendix rather than the main text.  
- Overall reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10; arguments for and against acceptance summarized accordingly.
