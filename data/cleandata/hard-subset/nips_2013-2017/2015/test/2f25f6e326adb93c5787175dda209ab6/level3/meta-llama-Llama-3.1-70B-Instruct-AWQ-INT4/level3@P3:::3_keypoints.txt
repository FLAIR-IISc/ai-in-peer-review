- Proposes novel likelihood bounds for DPPs that avoid spectral decomposition, enabling more efficient inference.  
- Derives bounds for both finite and continuous domains and leverages inducing inputs to approximate the likelihood.  
- Uses the bounds to develop variational inference and MCMC methods for DPPs.  
- Well-motivated work addressing the challenge of intractable normalizing constants in likelihood-based DPP inference.  
- Technical derivations are thorough and appear sound.  
- The inducing-inputs idea is clever and effective in experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to only two experiments, weakening empirical claims.  
- Toy Gaussian experiment: MCMC recovers true parameters, but variational inference performs poorly.  
- Real diabetic neuropathy dataset: method effectively distinguishes different point-process classes.  
- Lacks comprehensive comparisons to existing approximate and exact methods (only brief mention of [7]).  
- Insufficient discussion of how inducing inputs are chosen and their impact on bound quality.  
- Notation is sometimes unclear; key terms like K, L, marginal kernel, and L-ensembles need definitions for uninformed readers.  
- Originality: applying inducing inputs to DPP inference is a significant, though incremental, contribution.  
- Significance: addresses an important, broadly applicable problem in machine learning and statistics.  
- Recommendations: improve method clarity/notation, provide detailed comparisons, expand experiments across datasets, and give guidance on selecting/initializing inducing inputs.
