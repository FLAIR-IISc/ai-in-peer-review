- Addresses intractable likelihood-based inference for DPPs by avoiding costly spectral decomposition.  
- Core contribution: introduces inducing-input non-spectral upper/lower bounds on det(L+I) to enable scalable inference.  
- Proposes two inference schemes using these bounds: a fast variational method and a more exact approximate MCMC.  
- The inducing-point approach is original, significant, and well-motivated by an elegant connection to sparse Gaussian processes.  
- Major issue: experiments lack critical baselines — no comparisons to existing approximate or exact DPP methods and no runtime/complexity comparison between VI and MCMC.  
- Major issue: the VI method shows poorly consistent results on a toy example; the paper lacks analysis of failure modes and conditions for VI reliability.  
- Major issue: no practical guidance on the number and placement of inducing inputs or sensitivity analysis; initialization strategies (e.g., using observed points) are not explored.  
- Minor issue: exposition needs brief definitions of marginal kernel K and L-ensembles for non-expert readers.  
- Minor issue: introduction’s discussion of K vs L parameterization is inconsistent with the paper’s L-parameterized methods and should be streamlined.  
- Recommendation: promising idea but requires major revisions — strengthen empirical evaluation and provide deeper methodological analysis, especially of the VI approach.
