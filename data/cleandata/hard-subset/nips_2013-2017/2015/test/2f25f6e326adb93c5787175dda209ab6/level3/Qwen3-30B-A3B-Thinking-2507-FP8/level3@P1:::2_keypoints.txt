- Addresses intractable normalizing constants in DPP likelihoods by avoiding expensive spectral decompositions.  
- Proposes novel nonspectral determinant bounds using pseudo-inputs (inducing variables) for finite and continuous DPPs.  
- Core theoretical contributions are Propositions 1 and 2, derived from determinant inequalities and technically sound.  
- Bounds eliminate spectral computation, enabling efficient variational inference and retrospective MCMC (O(nm²) vs O(n³)).  
- Experimental validation includes synthetic Gaussian experiments (matches spectral methods) and a diabetic neuropathy dataset (practical utility).  
- Empirically exposes variational inference failure modes (high parameter variability) and contrasts with MCMC robustness.  
- Method is broadly applicable beyond DPPs (e.g., GP inference) and adapts pseudo-input techniques to kernel functionals.  
- Novelty over prior work: nonspectral framework and inducing-point tightening differ from spectral and Nyström approximations.  
- Continuous-domain extension is a significant contribution compared to literature focused on finite sets.  
- Main weaknesses: superficial treatment of K vs L parametrization and lack of resolution on interpretability trade-offs.  
- Presentation issues: supplement should follow a standard format (not an extended paper) for clarity.  
- Experimental shortcomings: limited quantitative runtime comparisons with prior spectral methods.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, timely contribution with high technical quality and robust experiments — Accept.
