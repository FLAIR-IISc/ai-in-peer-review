- Proposes combining generative and discriminative models via a discriminatively trained gating network that predicts GMM latent variables.
- Gating network reduces computational cost while preserving generative-model modularity and flexibility.
- Paper is well-motivated and provides a clear overview of prior image-restoration approaches.
- Technical contributions are sound, with a detailed description of the gating network and training procedure.
- Method description is somewhat rough and needs clearer exposition.
- Empirical evaluation is not thorough enough.
- Computational complexity of the approach needs clearer quantification and comparison to alternatives.
- Effectiveness and failure modes of the proposed variational inference are not fully explained.
- Performance when using sampled points as inducing inputs is unknown and requires investigation.
- Authors should provide guidance (rule of thumb) for selecting number and placement of inducing points.
- Key terms (e.g., matrices K and L) lack clear explanations; discussion of directly learning marginal kernel parameters is unnecessary.
- Demonstrates potential: achieves state-of-the-art performance with significant computational savings.
- Overall writing is good but technical details and evaluations should be improved.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to address the noted limitations.
