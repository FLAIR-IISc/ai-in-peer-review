- Novel contribution: derives bounds on the DPP likelihood using inducing inputs that avoid spectral decomposition.  
- Theoretical soundness: Propositions 1 and 2 are well-founded and extend GP inference techniques to DPPs.  
- Extension: method applies to both finite and continuous DPPs, broadening applicability.  
- Empirical demonstration: validated on a toy Gaussian example and a real diabetic neuropathy dataset.  
- Practical tradeoff noted: parametrizing L directly favors tractability over interpretability compared to learning K.  

- Missing baseline comparisons: no evaluation against other approximate methods (e.g., [7]) for accuracy or runtime.  
- Poor parameter recovery: variational inference shows large estimation errors (up to 100%) on the toy example without explanation.  
- No guidance on inducing points: lacks recommendations for number of inducing points or initialization strategies.  
- Incomplete complexity analysis: claims of being "cheap" lack wall-clock time comparisons to alternatives.  
- Bounds' behavior unclear: paper does not demonstrate when bounds are tight or compare bound quality to existing methods.  

- Presentation issues: K and L are not properly defined, risking confusion for readers unfamiliar with DPPs.  
- Inconsistent discussion: contradictory points about learning K versus parametrizing L create confusion about trade-offs.  
- Clarity gaps: connection between inducing inputs and likelihood bounds and practical implementation details need clearer explanation.  
- Recommendation: major revision—add method comparisons, inducing-point guidance, analysis of variational failures, clarify K/L relationship, and include wall-clock timings.
