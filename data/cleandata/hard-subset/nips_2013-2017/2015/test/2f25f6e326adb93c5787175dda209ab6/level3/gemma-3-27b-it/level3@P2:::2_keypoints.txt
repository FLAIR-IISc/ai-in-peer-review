- Introduces novel, efficient determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods that avoid spectral decomposition.  
- Leverages variational Gaussian Process ideas and pseudo-inputs to derive bounds and approximate kernel functionals.  
- Technical core: Propositions 1 and 2 provide clear, well-justified determinant bounds inspired by sparse GP approximations.  
- Bounds are integrated into variational inference and MCMC, enabling practical DPP parameter learning.  
- Extension to continuous DPPs increases the generality and applicability of the approach.  
- Toy Gaussian experiment validates the MCMC implementation and highlights limitations of the variational approach.  
- Real-world diabetic neuropathy experiment demonstrates practical utility and provides a compelling comparison to prior work.  
- Shows convergence behavior with varying numbers of pseudo-inputs and visualizes optimized pseudo-inputs for intuition.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and accessible even to readers unfamiliar with DPP nuances.  
- Strengths: technically sound derivations, originality in avoiding spectral methods, and significant computational advantage.  
- Weakness: discussion of parameterizing K versus L is underdeveloped and needs deeper analysis.  
- Weakness: supplementary material is presented as an extended paper rather than a concise supplemental document focused on proofs and experimental details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: original, technically sound solution with substantial computational improvements and thorough experimental validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient K vs L discussion and suboptimal supplement format.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the K vs L discussion and supplement formatting.
