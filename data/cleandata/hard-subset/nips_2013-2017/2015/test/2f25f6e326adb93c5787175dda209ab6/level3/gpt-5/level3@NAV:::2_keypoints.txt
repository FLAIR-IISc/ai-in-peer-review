- Proposes nonspectral upper and lower determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods using pseudo-inputs Z to form a low-rank surrogate Q, applicable to finite and continuous domains.  
- Extends bounds to Fredholm determinants via computable kernel integrals Ψ, enabling tractable continuous-DPP computations without eigendecompositions.  
- Uses bounds to derive a variational lower bound for learning and a retrospective Metropolis–Hastings MCMC that avoids eigenvalue computations.  
- Approach is inspired by inducing-point methods in sparse GPs and replaces spectral bracketing (Affandi et al. ICML’13), broadening applicability.  
- Connects to numerical analysis of Fredholm determinants (Bornemann) and determinant inequalities (Seiler & Simon).  
- Theory is technically sound with clear finite- and infinite-dimensional statements and proofs (supplement contains details).  
- Variational and MCMC constructions correctly employ the bounds; experiments probe both success and failure modes (e.g., 1D Gaussian–Gaussian variational failures, identifiability issues).  
- Some practical guidance on tightness vs. m and optimization of Z is provided, but more quantitative diagnostics are needed.  
- Exposition is generally clear and well-organized, progressing helpfully from finite to continuous settings.  
- Manuscript overemphasizes the K vs L parameterization tension early on but does not follow up; either downplay or expand the discussion.  
- Supplement format should be standardized as a PDF with proofs and implementation details rather than only an extended version.  
- The determinant bounds via pseudo-inputs are novel, conceptually elegant, and extend GP-style variational ideas to DPPs and Fredholm determinants.  
- Method is timely, general, and likely to be adopted by practitioners who want to avoid spectral computations.  
- Pros: simple, general idea; creative pseudo-input mechanism; strong experiments including relevant application (neuropathy data); extends to continuous DPPs without spectral knowledge.  
- Cons: limited quantitative guidance on tightness, scalability, and choice/optimization of Z; unclear high-dimensional behavior.  
- Cons: comparisons to spectral methods and Nyström baselines are limited and should be expanded.  
- Cons: K vs L discussion underdeveloped and supplement format nonstandard.  
- Suggestion: add scaling plots for n, m, and d and report wall-clock times to assess scalability.  
- Suggestion: compare against spectral bracketing and Nyström where feasible.  
- Suggestion: explore bounding determinants directly in terms of K and investigate scalable/automated pseudo-input selection.  
- Suggestion: clarify computational costs of Ψ in higher dimensions and provide implementation details.
