- Tackles the computational bottleneck in DPP likelihood evaluation (det(L + I)).
- Proposes novel, non-spectral bounds on the DPP likelihood, avoiding costly spectral approximations.
- Draws a connection to sparse Gaussian Process approximations using inducing points.
- Derives cheap-to-evaluate bounds applicable to both finite and continuous DPPs.
- Develops two inference schemes using the bounds: a fast variational inference (VI) method and an exact retrospective MCMC sampler.
- Method is original, significant, technically sound, and clearly presented.
- Demonstrates computational efficiency that can extend DPPs to larger-scale problems.
- Experiments: MCMC is correctly benchmarked on a synthetic problem; VI shows practical utility on a real-world dataset.
- Limitation: VI performed poorly on the synthetic experiment but the cause (optimization vs. bound limitations) is not analyzed.
- Limitation: Use of the L-kernel parameterization leads to parameter non-identifiability, which needs explicit discussion.
- Recommendation: Accept, with revisions to address the VI failure analysis and discuss the L-kernel trade-offs.
