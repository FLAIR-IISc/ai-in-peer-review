- Addresses DPP inference without spectral decomposition by deriving nonspectral likelihood bounds for finite and continuous domains.  
- Leverages these bounds for variational inference and MCMC, drawing clear connections to sparse GP variational inference.  
- Propositions 1 and 2 (finite and continuous cases) are novel and offer a practical alternative to spectral methods.  
- Theoretical development is sound and grounded in existing DPP and inference literature.  
- Continuous-case derivations are mathematically involved but reasonably well explained.  
- Experiments include a toy Gaussian continuous DPP and a diabetic neuropathy dataset, showing applicability.  
- Presentation is verbose; introduction and background should be more concise and focused.  
- Experimental section lacks details on hyperparameters and implementation choices.  
- Quantitative comparisons to existing (even more expensive) methods are missing and would strengthen validation.  
- Computational cost vs. number of inducing points m is only discussed qualitatively; runtimes/tabled results are needed.  
- Direct parameterization of kernel L raises identifiability and interpretability concerns that require deeper discussion.  
- Practical implications of adaptive selection of m in retrospective MCMC are under-discussed.  
- Notation (especially in Section 4) is dense and can be hard to follow without substantial background.  
- Overall recommendation: technically strong and novel work worthy of acceptance pending major revision for clarity and expanded experiments.
