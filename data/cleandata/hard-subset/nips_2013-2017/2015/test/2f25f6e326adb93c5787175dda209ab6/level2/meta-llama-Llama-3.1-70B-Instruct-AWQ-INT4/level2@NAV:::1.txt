**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate, nonspectral bounds on the determinants arising in the likelihoods of Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs), both finite and continuous. DPPs are point process models that encode diversity between points in a realization through a positive definite kernel. The authors derive bounds on the likelihood of a DPP that do not depend on the spectral decomposition of the operator L, which is often intractable. These bounds are used for variational inference and Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference methods for DPPs.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper provides a novel solution to the problem of likelihood-based inference for DPPs, which is a challenging task due to the intractable spectral decomposition of the operator L.
* The proposed bounds are cheap to evaluate and do not rely on approximating the spectrum of a large matrix or an operator.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on both synthetic and real datasets.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the kernel L is parametrized, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The authors do not provide a thorough comparison with existing methods, such as spectral bounds, to demonstrate the superiority of their approach.
* The optimization of the variational parameters Z is not thoroughly explored, and the authors rely on a simple EM-like scheme or black-box optimizers.
