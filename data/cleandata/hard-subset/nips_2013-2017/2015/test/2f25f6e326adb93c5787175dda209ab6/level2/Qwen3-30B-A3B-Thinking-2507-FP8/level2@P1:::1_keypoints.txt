- Addresses intractable normalizing constant in DPP likelihoods (large determinant/Fredholm determinant) that hinders likelihood-based inference.
- Prior methods rely on spectral decomposition of the kernel, which is computationally expensive and limits scalability.
- Proposes spectral-free likelihood bounds using "pseudo-inputs" (inducing variables) to avoid eigen-decomposition.
- Derives two main rigorous bounds (Propositions 1 and 2) for finite and continuous domains.
- Shows how these bounds enable variational inference and MCMC without spectral computation.
- Theoretical proofs are nontrivial and provided (in supplementary material), indicating technical soundness.
- Method builds on intuition from Gaussian process inducing-point techniques, making it accessible to GP-literate readers.
- Experimental validation includes synthetic (with known spectrum) and real diabetic neuropathy data, demonstrating practical utility.
- Acknowledges interpretability trade-offs when parametrizing kernel L instead of K, showing thoughtful limitations.
- Novel contribution: spectral-free bounds for DPP likelihoods, including extension to continuous domains not previously addressed.
- Significant potential to improve DPP scalability and enable broader real-world applications (summarization, search, spatial stats).
- Paper is well-written, organized, and clearly explains definitions, challenges, and the GP connection.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- For acceptance: novel scalable approach, rigorous theory, clear GP connection, real-world applicability, solid experiments.
- Against acceptance: lacks detailed computational complexity comparison to existing methods.
- Against acceptance: motivation/discussion for parametrizing L instead of K is brief and could be expanded.
