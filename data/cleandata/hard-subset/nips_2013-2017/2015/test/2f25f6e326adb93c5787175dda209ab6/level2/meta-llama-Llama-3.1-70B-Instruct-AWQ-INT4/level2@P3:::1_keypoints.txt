- Proposes novel nonspectral, cheap-to-evaluate bounds on determinants in DPP likelihoods for both finite and continuous settings.
- Derives bounds using a nontrivial determinant inequality, avoiding spectral decomposition.
- Bounds enable likelihood-based inference without spectral knowledge and apply to variational inference and MCMC.
- Demonstrates use for expensive-but-exact MCMC and cheap-but-approximate variational inference.
- Strength: novel solution to likelihood inference for DPPs that is computationally inexpensive.
- Strength: thorough theoretical derivation and supportive experimental results.
- Strength: clear exposition, consistent notation, and well-organized figures and tables.
- Weakness: assumes the kernel is parametrized, which may not hold in all practical cases.
- Weakness: bounds are approximations (not exact), potentially affecting inference accuracy.
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive empirical comparison with spectral and other competing methods.
- Quality: technically sound with well-supported claims and detailed derivations.
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution with potential applications across spatial statistics, computer vision, and NLP.
