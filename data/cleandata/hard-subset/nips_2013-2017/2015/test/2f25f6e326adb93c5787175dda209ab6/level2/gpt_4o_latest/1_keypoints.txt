- Paper introduces novel nonspectral bounds for DPP likelihoods enabling likelihood-based inference without spectral decomposition.
- Bounds are derived for both finite and continuous domains, permitting efficient variational inference and exact MCMC.
- Uses pseudo-inputs to approximate the kernel, avoiding spectral computations and reducing computational complexity.
- Validated on synthetic and real-world datasets, showing utility in both approximate and exact inference settings.
- Novel contribution: addresses a key limitation in DPP inference with a computationally efficient alternative to spectral methods.
- Theoretical rigor: derivations are mathematically sound with proofs and clear connections to Gaussian processes and variational inference.
- Practical utility: methods perform effectively in synthetic and real-world experiments (e.g., diabetic neuropathy dataset).
- Scalability: bounds scale well with the number of pseudo-inputs; variational results converge as pseudo-inputs increase.
- Comprehensive evaluation: compares MCMC results to ideal likelihood methods and analyzes pseudo-input count impact.
- Parameter interpretability: parametrizing L instead of K reduces interpretability, hindering adoption in some applications.
- Variational inference instability: results show variability in synthetic experiments, raising robustness concerns.
- Limited related-work discussion: could expand coverage of recent advances in DPP inference and related kernel methods.
- MCMC computational overhead: retrospective MCMC requires iterative pseudo-input refinement, which may be costly for very large datasets.
- Pro acceptance: addresses a critical DPP inference bottleneck with strong theoretical and experimental support.
- Con acceptance: interpretability issues and inconsistent variational inference weaken impact and robustness.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand related-work discussion and clarify variational approach limitations.
