- Main contribution: novel non-spectral upper and lower bounds on det(I+L)/det(L+I) for continuous and finite DPPs using m inducing pseudo-inputs.
- Computational efficiency: bounds computed in O(n m^2) for the finite case, far cheaper than spectral approaches.
- Method inspiration: adapts sparse Gaussian Process inducing-point ideas to DPP likelihood bounding.
- VI application: lower bound used as a fast, jointly optimized variational objective over parameters and pseudo-input locations.
- MCMC application: bounds used for asymptotically exact retrospective Metropolis-Hastings by tightening bounds (increasing m) until accept/reject is decided.
- Theoretical basis: derivations rely on a determinant inequality by Seiler and Simon and are technically sound.
- Validation (synthetic): tested on a 1D continuous “Gaussian–Gaussian” model where the true Fredholm determinant is known, validating MCMC.
- Validation (real data): variational method applied to 2D nerve-fiber dataset, reproducing prior findings and showing parameter convergence as m increases.
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing and strong connection to sparse GP literature for accessibility.
- Strength: figures (e.g., optimized pseudo-input locations) provide useful visual insight into method behavior.
- Weakness: main bounds (Propositions 1 and 2) lack an intuitive sketch in the main text; proofs deferred to supplement.
- Minor issue: small typo in section reference (mentions equation (13) instead of (8)).
- Originality: first non-spectral inducing-point bounds for DPP likelihoods and novel extension to continuous domains.
- Limitation: parametrizing the L kernel directly sacrifices interpretability compared to K-kernel parameterizations.
- Limitation: observed instability of the variational inference method on the toy experiment, raising robustness concerns.
- Significance: makes parametric DPP inference more practical and scalable, enabling larger datasets and continuous-domain applications.
- Practical trade-off: provides a fast approximate VI and a slower exact MCMC option for different use cases.
- Recommendation: reviewer favors acceptance — novel, impactful, technically sound, and well-presented, with acknowledged but non-fatal limitations.
