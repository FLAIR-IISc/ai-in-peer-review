- Presents novel inference approach for DPPs that does not require spectral knowledge.  
- Derives likelihood bounds for both finite and continuous domains usable in VI and MCMC.  
- Uses a non-trivial determinant inequality with clear mathematical derivations and proofs.  
- Introduces a retrospective Metropolis-Hastings algorithm leveraging the likelihood bounds for acceptance decisions.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness through experiments on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Well-written and well-organized with clear background, methodology, and results.  
- Technically sound derivations and thorough explanations.  
- Main contribution: avoiding spectrum approximation for likelihood evaluation.  
- Limitation: parametrizing kernel L instead of K can harm interpretability.  
- Novelty is strong but builds on prior work using likelihood bounds.
