**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate, nonspectral bounds on the determinants arising in the likelihoods of determinantal point processes (DPPs), both finite and continuous. DPPs are point process models that encode diversity between points through a positive definite kernel. The likelihood of a DPP involves the evaluation and spectral decomposition of an operator related to the kernel, which is often intractable. The authors derive bounds on the likelihood that do not require spectral knowledge, using a nontrivial inequality on determinants. These bounds can be used for variational inference and Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The paper provides a novel solution to the problem of likelihood-based inference for DPPs without requiring spectral knowledge.
* The bounds derived are cheap to evaluate and can be used for both variational inference and MCMC methods.
* The paper demonstrates the use of the bounds for expensive-but-exact MCMC and cheap-but-approximate variational inference.
* The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work and the limitations of their approach.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the kernel is parametrized, which may not always be the case in practice.
* The bounds derived are not exact, but rather approximations, which may affect the accuracy of the inference results.
* The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison with other methods, such as spectral methods, to evaluate the performance of the proposed approach.
