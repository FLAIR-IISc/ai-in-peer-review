- Paper proposes nonspectral upper and lower bounds on det(I+L) for L-ensemble DPPs on finite and continuous domains, avoiding eigen-decompositions.  
- Introduces a low-rank inducing-point proxy Q based on m inducing points Z with bounds det(Q+I) e^{−tr(L−Q)} ≤ 1/det(I+L) ≤ 1/det(Q+I).  
- Provides analogous Fredholm determinant bounds in the continuous case using LZ and Ψij = ∫ L(zi,x)L(x,zj) dµ(x) under trace-class assumptions.  
- Derives a cheap variational lower bound on the log-likelihood (O(n m^2) compute, reduced memory) using these bounds.  
- Develops an exact Metropolis–Hastings scheme with retrospective acceptance tests that tighten bounds by increasing m and optimizing Z.  
- Method draws inspiration from sparse Gaussian processes (Titsias 2009) and leverages determinant inequalities (Seiler & Simon 1975).  
- Empirical validation: 1D Gaussian example with known spectrum validates MH sampler and shows L-parameterization identifiability issues.  
- Empirical validation: 2D diabetic neuropathy dataset shows converging overdispersion estimates with increasing m and class separation, with reasonable runtimes.  
- Strength: conceptually novel nonspectral determinant bounds applicable to finite and continuous DPPs.  
- Strength: practical—avoids large eigendecompositions and full n×n storage, using only n×m and m×m matrices.  
- Strength: continuous-domain treatment is general for Gaussian/Gaussian setups where Ψ is closed-form.  
- Strength: provides both variational and exact MCMC inference tools and connects bound tightening to optimizing inducing points.  
- Weakness: no theoretical characterization of the rate at which the bound gap shrinks with m or guidance on choosing m and Z beyond heuristics.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope and no runtime/accuracy comparisons to spectral methods or other scalable DPP approaches on large finite sets.  
- Weakness: limited guidance for continuous cases beyond Gaussian/Gaussian kernels—computability and numerical stability issues not fully addressed.  
- Weakness: variational optimization can struggle due to parameter identifiability; no diagnostics, priors, or reparameterizations proposed to mitigate this.  
- Weakness: continuous-domain assumptions (trace-class, eq. (9)) lack practical checks or sufficient conditions for common ML setups; convergence as m→∞ is informal.  
- Weakness: computational cost accounting is incomplete—O(n m^2) claim omits costs of optimizing Z and increasing m in MH.  
- Quality: technically sound with clear finite/continuous bounds and correct use in VI and MH; some proofs deferred to supplement.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, but minor notational inconsistencies and limited practical guidance for selecting/initializing Z.  
- Originality: high—nonspectral determinant bounds for DPP likelihoods, especially continuous domains, are novel in ML.  
- Significance: moderate-to-high—broadens DPP applicability by removing spectral requirements and is likely useful for continuous DPPs.  
- Key suggestions: provide monotonicity/tightness results in m and Z, practical strategies for initializing/optimizing Z, and tractability guidance for non-Gaussian kernels.  
- Key suggestions: report wall-clock comparisons vs. spectral MH methods and consider reparameterizations or priors to improve identifiability.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, general, implementable nonspectral approach enabling VI and exact MCMC with unified finite/continuous treatment.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited experiments/comparisons, lack of theoretical guarantees on bound tightness/rates, and unresolved variational instability.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept—original and useful contribution, would be strengthened by more experiments and theoretical insights on bound behavior.
