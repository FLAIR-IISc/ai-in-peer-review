- Proposes novel, cheap-to-evaluate nonspectral bounds on determinants for DPP likelihoods (finite and continuous).  
- Bounds avoid spectral decomposition of operator L, addressing an intractability in DPP inference.  
- Uses these bounds for variational inference and MCMC for DPPs.  
- Strength: Provides a novel solution to likelihood-based inference for DPPs.  
- Strength: Bounds are computationally cheap and do not require approximating the spectrum.  
- Strength: Empirical validation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrates effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Assumes kernel L is parametrized, which may not hold in all settings.  
- Weakness: Lacks thorough comparison with existing methods such as spectral bounds.  
- Weakness: Optimization of variational parameters Z is underexplored; relies on simple EM-like or black-box optimizers.  
- Quality: Paper is technically sound with correct mathematical derivations and well-designed experiments.  
- Clarity: Well-written, consistent notation, and helpful figures.  
- Originality: Novel approach representing a significant contribution to DPP inference.  
- Significance: Addresses a challenging problem with potential applications across ML and statistics.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, effective method with empirical support and clear presentation.  
- Argument against acceptance: Parametrization assumption, insufficient method comparisons, and incomplete Z optimization treatment.  
- Recommendations: Add thorough comparisons to spectral bounds, more detailed exploration of Z optimization, and additional application demonstrations.
