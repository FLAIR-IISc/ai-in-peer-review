- Main contribution: derives novel non-spectral upper and lower bounds on det(I+L) for both finite and continuous DPPs using inducing points (pseudo-inputs).
- Methods: proposes a fast variational inference maximizing a lower bound on log-likelihood and an asymptotically exact retrospective MH MCMC using the bounds for acceptance.
- Validation: experiments on a synthetic 1D Gaussian-Gaussian model (exact likelihood available) and a real 2D diabetic neuropathy spatial dataset.
- Strength: technically solid end-to-end contribution from theory to practical inference algorithms.
- Strength: well-designed experiments—synthetic case validates MCMC; real data demonstrates variational utility and reproduces prior findings.
- Major concern: potential sign error in the variational lower bound (Equation 6)—trace term may have wrong sign per the cited inequality, requiring clarification.
- Impact of concern: if unresolved, the correctness of the variational method is called into question; MCMC remains sound.
- Clarity: paper is clear and well-organized, with helpful connection to sparse GP literature.
- Minor clarity suggestions: add intuition linking Psi (continuous) to Q (finite) and fix Figure 1 legend missing the green prior line.
- Originality: novel and non-trivial application of inducing-point machinery to DPP determinantal structure, advancing beyond spectral approximations.
- Significance: high potential impact by making DPP inference more scalable and applicable to both finite and continuous domains.
- Practical note: authors honestly report variational inconsistency on toy problem; a brief discussion on causes (e.g., local optima or bound looseness) and diagnostics would help practitioners.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel non-spectral method, applicable to finite and continuous DPPs, well-written and technically strong, and potentially broad practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: the critical unresolved technical issue in the variational bound’s derivation/sign.
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance conditional on satisfactory clarification/correction of the variational bound derivation (Equation 6).
