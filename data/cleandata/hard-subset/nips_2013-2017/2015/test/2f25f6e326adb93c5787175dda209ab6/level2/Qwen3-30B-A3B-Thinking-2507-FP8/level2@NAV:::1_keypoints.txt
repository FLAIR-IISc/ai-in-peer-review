- Addresses major computational bottleneck in likelihood-based DPP inference by deriving cheap nonspectral likelihood bounds for finite and continuous domains.  
- Avoids expensive spectral decompositions, enabling inference without spectral knowledge.  
- Enables variational inference and MCMC for DPP parameter estimation using the new bounds.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical bounds (Propositions 1 and 2) that generalize sparse GP approximations to DPPs.  
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real (diabetic neuropathy) data shows practical utility and convergence as inducing variables increase.  
- Toy experiment comparison with ideal likelihood methods supports correctness of the approach.  
- Paper is clear and well-structured, carefully distinguishing finite and continuous cases.  
- Connection to sparse Gaussian process methods is well-motivated and helpful.  
- Authors demonstrate strong familiarity with DPP literature and appropriate citations (e.g., Affandi et al. 2014).  
- Novelty is significant: avoids spectral methods while maintaining theoretical guarantees and scalability.  
- Minor limitation: interpretability trade-off when parametrizing L instead of K is acknowledged.  
- Variational inference showed inconsistent results across runs in the toy experiment, suggesting MCMC may be preferable.  
- Overall: sound theory, thorough experiments, high-quality contribution; recommendation to accept for NIPS.
