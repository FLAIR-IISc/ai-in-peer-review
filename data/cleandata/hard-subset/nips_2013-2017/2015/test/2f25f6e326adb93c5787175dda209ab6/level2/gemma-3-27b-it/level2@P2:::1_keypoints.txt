- Paper addresses DPP inference bottleneck by deriving likelihood bounds that avoid spectral decomposition for finite and continuous domains.  
- Builds on prior DPP and Gaussian process variational inference literature, extending bounds to the continuous case via Fredholm determinant connections.  
- Derivations are technically sound and the finite-to-continuous extension is well-justified.  
- Avoiding spectral decomposition is practically relevant and can enable DPPs on larger datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written, with consistent notation and logical organization.  
- Empirical validation on synthetic and real (diabetic neuropathy) datasets supports effectiveness; pseudo-input analysis is insightful.  
- Explicit connections to variational inference and MCMC provide a clear implementation/use path.  
- Weakness: parametrizing kernel L instead of K reduces interpretability of learned parameters.  
- Weakness: the bounds require optimizing over pseudo-inputs Z, but the computational complexity of this optimization isn’t fully analyzed.  
- Weakness: comparisons to other DPP inference methods are limited; lacks thorough speed vs. accuracy tradeoff evaluation.  
- Minor clarification needed in Section 4.2 to better connect Proposition 1 and the continuous equivalent (Fredholm determinant).  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses significant practical challenge, offers computationally cheaper theoretically sound alternative, and shows empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reduced interpretability, incomplete complexity analysis, and limited method comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper; accept with minor revisions to analyze Z-optimization complexity, expand comparisons, and clarify Proposition connections.
