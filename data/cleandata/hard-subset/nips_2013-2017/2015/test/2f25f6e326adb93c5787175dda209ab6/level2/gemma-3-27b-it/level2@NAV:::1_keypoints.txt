- Addresses computational bottleneck in DPP likelihood inference by removing need for kernel spectral decomposition.
- Proposes novel non-spectral bounds on DPP likelihoods applicable to both finite and continuous domains.
- Bounds enable efficient variational inference and MCMC methods that avoid eigendecomposition.
- Derivations build on Gaussian Process literature; some technical details are deferred to the supplement.
- Inference methods (variational and MCMC) are standard but appropriately adapted to use the new bounds.
- Experiments include a toy Gaussian example and a real-world diabetic neuropathy dataset.
- Results: MCMC performs comparably to spectral approaches in the toy setting while avoiding expensive eigendecompositions.
- Variational inference yields comparable results to prior work, demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Strengths: novel, theoretically solid contribution; clear presentation; addresses a major computational issue in DPPs.
- Weaknesses: experimental validation is limited in scope and simplicity, reducing demonstration of practical benefits.
- Missing detailed computational analysis of actual speedups from avoiding spectral decomposition.
- Discussion of interpretability trade-offs between parameterizing L versus K is cursory and needs deeper exploration.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting more detailed computational performance analysis and expanded discussion of interpretability implications.
