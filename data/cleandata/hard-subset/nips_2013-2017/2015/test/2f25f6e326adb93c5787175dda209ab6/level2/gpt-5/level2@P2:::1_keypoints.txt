- Proposes nonspectral upper and lower bounds on the DPP likelihood normalization term for finite and continuous domains, avoiding eigenvalue computations.  
- For finite domains, derives bounds det(Q+I) exp(−tr(L−Q)) ≤ 1/det(L+I) ≤ 1/det(Q+I) using Nyström-style Q built from inducing inputs Z.  
- For continuous domains, obtains analogous bounds on Fredholm determinants involving det(LZ), det(LZ+Ψ), and exponential trace corrections, with Ψij = ∫ L(zi,x)L(x,zj)dµ(x).  
- Bounds are inexpensive to evaluate (O(n m^2) for finite case) and do not require spectral approximation.  
- Develops two inference strategies: a variational method maximizing a tractable lower bound over kernel parameters and Z, and a retrospective MH sampler that adaptively refines m and Z.  
- Connections to sparse GP variational bounds (Titsias, 2009) are leveraged; contrasts with prior spectral truncation approaches.  
- Finite-domain inequality aligns with Seiler–Simon determinant inequality and PSD ordering Q ≤ L is justified via Schur complement; continuous case consistent via det(I+Q)=det(LZ+Ψ)/det(LZ).  
- Variational objective is cheap when observation sizes are modest; retrospective MCMC is standard and correct provided bounds remain valid for any Z.  
- 1D Gaussian–Gaussian validation convincingly checks the sampler against exact likelihood; diabetic neuropathy study shows meaningful over-dispersion estimates and monotone tightening with m.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: no large-scale finite-domain benchmarks, no runtime/accuracy comparisons to spectral methods, and limited sensitivity analysis on m and Z.  
- Paper is generally well-written with helpful motivation and links to GP literature, though contains minor notational glitches and typos.  
- Main contribution is novel and nontrivial: nonspectral determinant bounds for DPPs and extension to continuous domains, elegantly reusing inducing-point GP ideas.  
- Significance is high because bounds remove eigen-computation bottlenecks and enable both variational and exact MCMC inference, especially for continuous DPPs.  
- Pros: conceptually clean nonspectral bounds, applicability to continuous Fredholm determinants, enables fast variational and exact retrospective MCMC, solid theoretical grounding, empirical validations.  
- Cons: limited experiments and scalability demos, no formal theorem on convergence/tightness as m→∞, dependence on Z optimization without practical guidance, identifiability issues when parameterizing L (e.g., κ).  
- Requests: provide conditions/theorem for convergence as m→∞; detail operator ordering and Seiler–Simon applicability in trace-class setting; report sensitivity to Z initialization and optimization method; compare against spectral methods on finite data (time/accuracy/memory); report uncertainty on diabetic dataset parameters and discuss κ identifiability; clarify O(n m^2) complexity assumption about diagonal L.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos/index inconsistencies, ensure consistent equation numbering, explicitly justify PSD property of Q when Z∉Y, and state bounds hold for any Z with optimization only tightening them.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel, and broadly useful contribution that avoids spectral computations for DPPs; recommend acceptance pending clarifications and at least one comparative experiment.
