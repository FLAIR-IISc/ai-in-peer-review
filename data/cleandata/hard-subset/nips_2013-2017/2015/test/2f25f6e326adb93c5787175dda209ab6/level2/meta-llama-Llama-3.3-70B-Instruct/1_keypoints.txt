- Main contribution: derives novel bounds on DPP likelihood (finite and continuous) that avoid spectral decomposition of operator L.
- Bounds are inexpensive to evaluate and applicable to variational inference and MCMC for learning DPP parameters.
- Paper is well-written, with clear introduction to DPPs and organized technical sections; proofs in supplementary material.
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
- Strength: novel likelihood bounds that remove need for spectral computation.
- Strength: application of these bounds to variational inference and MCMC methods.
- Strength: empirical validation on both synthetic and real-world data.
- Weakness: assumes direct parametrization of kernel L, which may be less interpretable.
- Weakness: optimization of pseudo-inputs is critical but lacks a detailed analysis of the optimization process.
- Weakness: no comparison with alternative DPP learning methods (e.g., spectral methods or other variational approaches).
- Pro-accept arguments: significant and novel contribution; useful likelihood bounds; strong experimental results.
- Con-accept arguments: interpretability and parametrization concerns; insufficient analysis of pseudo-input optimization; missing method comparisons.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting more detailed optimization analysis and comparisons with other methods.
