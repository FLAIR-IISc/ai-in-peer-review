- Proposes bounds on DPP likelihoods for finite and continuous domains that avoid spectral decomposition, usable in variational inference and MCMC.
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear introduction and relevant citations.
- Theoretical contributions (Propositions 1 and 2) appear rigorous, building on determinant inequalities and variational GP ideas.
- Novel practical alternative to spectral methods, especially beneficial for large-scale DPP inference.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with logical separation of finite and continuous cases.
- Experiments (synthetic Gaussian and diabetic neuropathy) demonstrate method effectiveness, MCMC performance, and variational convergence with more inducing points.
- Good positioning within related work and appropriate citation of GP, DPP, and spectral literature.
- Optimizing pseudo-inputs (Z) introduces new computational costs; trade-offs and scaling with problem size are not fully analyzed.
- Direct parameterization of L reduces interpretability compared to parameterizing K, and the paper does not explore ways to recover interpretability.
- Limited justification and discussion of prior choices in the MCMC experiments.
- Continuous-case results rely on trace-class and Mercer assumptions that need clearer, more accessible explanation of practical implications.
- Lacks direct experimental comparison to existing spectral-based DPP inference methods, limiting benchmarking.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant bottleneck, presents novel and sound bounds, demonstrates empirical effectiveness, and is well-written.
- Arguments for rejection: unresolved cost of pseudo-input optimization, interpretability concerns, and insufficient comparison to spectral alternatives.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution recommended for acceptance, with requested revisions to analyze pseudo-input costs, add method comparisons, justify priors, and clarify continuous-case assumptions.
