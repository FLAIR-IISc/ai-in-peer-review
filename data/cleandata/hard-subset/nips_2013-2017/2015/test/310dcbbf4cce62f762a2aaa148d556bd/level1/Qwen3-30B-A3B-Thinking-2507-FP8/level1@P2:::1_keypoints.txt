- Problem addressed: constrained contextual bandits with time and budget limits; proposes combining adaptive linear programming (ALP) with UCB to reduce regret.  
- Gap identified: existing budget-constrained bandit results do not generalize to contextual settings; prior work ([17]) attains only O(√T) regret with restricted benchmark and inefficiencies.  
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm achieving logarithmic regret (O(JK log T)) and ALP giving O(1) approximation except at boundary cases.  
- Technical novelty: new concentration and estimation-error analysis techniques for adaptive algorithms under random contexts.  
- Theoretical rigor: comprehensive proofs and careful handling of context coupling; strong theoretical guarantees.  
- Clarity and presentation: well-organized, clearly written, careful notation, and clear unit-cost vs heterogeneous-cost distinction; supplementary material detailed.  
- Originality: first work to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits; novel ALP approximation and ordering property exploitation.  
- Practical significance: relevant to applications (clinical trials, recommendations); computationally efficient and decouples information acquisition from decisions.  
- Improvement over prior work: substantial advance from O(√T) to O(log T) regret and more natural regret definition than some prior studies.  
- Weakness — experiments: lacks experimental validation and main-text comparisons with baselines; simulations only mentioned in supplement.  
- Weakness — scope: main-text focus on unit-cost systems may obscure practical applicability; needs clearer guidance on heterogeneous-cost relevance.  
- Weakness — related work: insufficiently detailed comparison with related extensions ([21,22]) and how results differ.  
- Weakness — boundary cases: boundary scenarios yield O(√T) regret and are not convincingly argued to be rare or mitigable in practice.  
- Practical concerns: some heterogeneous-cost technicalities and implementation challenges are relegated to supplementary material.  
- Overall verdict: significant theoretical advance, rigorous and well-written; recommended for acceptance despite limited empirical validation and some clarity gaps.
