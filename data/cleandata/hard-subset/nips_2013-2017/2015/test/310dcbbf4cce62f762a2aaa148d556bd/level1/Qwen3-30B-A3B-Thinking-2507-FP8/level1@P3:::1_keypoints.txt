- Paper addresses constrained contextual bandits (budget and time constraints) and achieves major theoretical advances.
- Key idea: approximate the oracle by Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that relaxes hard budget constraints to average budget constraints.
- ALP achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) regret in boundary cases, improving over prior O(√T) results.
- Combine ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, which attains O(log T) regret for non-boundary cases.
- Analysis shows ALP tolerates estimation errors and can be effectively combined with UCB; analysis is elegant and insightful.
- Extensions to unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs are provided, showing robustness of the approach.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides rigorous theoretical analysis with detailed proofs in the supplement.
- Assumption of known context distribution (until Section 5) is restrictive and makes later analysis (EALP) more complex with less clean bounds.
- Experimental validation is minimal (only in supplementary material) and insufficient to confirm theoretical results.
- Comparison to recent related work ([21,22]) is inadequate; the paper should better explain why it attains better regret in dynamic contexts.
- Originality: novel application/implementation of ALP for constrained contextual bandits, advancing beyond prior non-contextual or restricted-context results.
- Technical soundness: rigorous proofs and careful handling of exploration-exploitation under budget constraints.
- Significance: high — logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits is a major theoretical advance with many practical applications.
- Main weaknesses: limited experiments, restrictive known-distribution assumption, and need for more thorough comparison to recent works.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, makes significant progress on an important open problem — recommend acceptance.
