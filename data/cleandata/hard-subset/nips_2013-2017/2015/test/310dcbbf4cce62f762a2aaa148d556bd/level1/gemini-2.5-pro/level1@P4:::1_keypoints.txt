- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with time horizon T and budget B, where standard DP oracle is intractable.
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that relaxes hard budget into an adaptive average based on remaining budget and time.
- ALP needs only relative ordering of expected rewards and achieves O(1) regret except O(√T) on specific "boundary" parameter values.
- Extends ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, which learns rewards and achieves O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) in boundary cases.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, using a hypergeometric sampling-without-replacement view and concentration inequalities.
- Clear, well-structured exposition that introduces ideas progressively from known statistics to unknown rewards.
- Algorithms are computationally efficient, requiring simple LPs or sorting at each step unlike DP or Mixture Elimination methods.
- Main limitation: model simplifies to finite discrete contexts, single budget, and initially unit costs—less general than prior RCB frameworks.
- Boundary cases are identified but sensitivity when the budget-to-time ratio is near a boundary is not analyzed.
- Extensions for unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs are brief and rely on supplementary material, leaving details underdeveloped.
- No empirical validation or simulations to illustrate practical performance or behavior near boundaries.
- Overall verdict: strong and novel theoretical contribution (first logarithmic regret in this constrained setting) despite simplifying assumptions and lack of experiments.
