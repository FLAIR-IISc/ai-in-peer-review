- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that achieves near-optimality using only ordering of expected rewards.  
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP for unknown expected rewards.  
- UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases.  
- Technical contributions are significant and novel (ALP+UCB combination).  
- Provides thorough theoretical regret analysis supporting claims.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clear for expert reproduction.  
- Results are impressive and the significance of contributions is clearly explained.  
- Assumes a simplified model, raising questions about generalization to more complex scenarios.  
- No experimental results provided to validate theoretical analysis.  
- Focus is specific, so applicability to other problems is unclear.  
- Overall recommendation: accept based on strong technical contributions and clarity.
