- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to solve constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, achieving logarithmic regret except in certain boundary cases.
- ALP approximates the oracle via linear programming and tolerates some estimation errors in system parameters.
- UCB-ALP leverages exploration (UCB) plus ALP’s allocation to balance learning and budgeted decision-making.
- Algorithms are computationally efficient and straightforward to implement.
- Assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint, which may limit real-world applicability.
- UCB-ALP shows suboptimal regret behavior in specific boundary scenarios.
- Paper lacks a fully rigorous analysis of regret bounds for the UCB-ALP algorithm.
- ALP and UCB-ALP are original contributions to constrained contextual bandits.
- Results are potentially significant for applications like online advertising and clinical trials.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
