- Tackles contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, extending classic multi-armed bandits.
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle via linear programming, achieving near-optimal behavior.
- Proposes UCB-ALP to handle unknown expected rewards by combining ALP with UCB exploration.
- Shows ALP yields O(1) regret in most cases; UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret except in boundary cases.
- Identifies boundary cases where regret degrades to O(√T), leaving optimality in those cases open.
- Extends methods to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and concentration-based regret bounds.
- Novelty: addresses an underexplored constrained contextual bandit problem with computationally efficient algorithms.
- Theoretical rigor: clear regret analysis and distinction between boundary and non-boundary cases.
- Practical relevance: motivated by real-world applications like clinical trials and recommendations.
- Clarity: offers insights on decoupling information acquisition and decision-making via adaptive linear relaxation.
- Presentation is dense and may be hard for readers unfamiliar with contextual bandits or LPs.
- Limited empirical validation; lacks extensive real-world experiments demonstrating practical performance.
- Assumptions of finite discrete contexts and fixed costs may limit generalizability.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve exposition and add empirical results.
