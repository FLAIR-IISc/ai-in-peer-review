- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for constrained contextual bandits and shows it approximates the oracle under known statistics.
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, handling unknown expected rewards.
- Proves UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret generally and sublinear regret in boundary cases.
- Builds on prior work in contextual/multi-armed bandits and bandits with knapsacks but claims novelty in achieving logarithmic regret.
- Cites relevant literature and positions the contribution relative to resourceful contextual bandits.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with proofs provided (mostly in supplementary material).
- Paper is well-organized and presents intuition and algorithms clearly.
- Some technical proofs and details are deferred to the supplement, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.
- Writing is clear with consistent notation, figures, and tables, though some paragraphs are overly long.
- Novel combination of ALP and UCB yields computationally efficient algorithms for constrained contextual bandits.
- Significant practical implications for budget-constrained applications like clinical trials and online recommendations.
- Limitation: model assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint, which may reduce realism.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address accessibility and model-scope concerns.
