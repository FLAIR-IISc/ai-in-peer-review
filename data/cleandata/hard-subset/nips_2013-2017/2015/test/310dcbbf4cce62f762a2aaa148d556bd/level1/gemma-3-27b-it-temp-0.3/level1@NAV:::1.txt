## Review of "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of constrained contextual bandits, introducing a novel approach to achieve logarithmic regret – a significant advancement over existing work. The authors begin by analyzing a simplified setting with known context distributions and derive an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that approximates the oracle solution with near-optimality. They then cleverly combine ALP with the UCB method to handle the more realistic scenario of unknown rewards, resulting in the UCB-ALP algorithm.  The work builds upon existing contextual bandit literature [1, 2, 3, 7] and extends budget-constrained bandit results [11, 12, 13] to the contextual setting, addressing a gap identified in prior research [17].

The paper’s strengths lie in its technical soundness and the clear progression of ideas. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of both ALP and UCB-ALP, demonstrating logarithmic regret (with exceptions for boundary cases). The introduction of ALP as a means to approximate the oracle is insightful, and the adaptive budget constraint adjustment is a key contribution. The extension to heterogeneous costs (Section 6) further demonstrates the algorithm’s versatility. The writing is generally clear and well-organized, with sufficient detail to understand the core concepts and analysis.  The authors are careful to acknowledge limitations and boundary cases, showcasing intellectual honesty.

However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the practical implications of the boundary cases. While acknowledged, the impact of these cases on real-world performance isn’t fully explored.  Furthermore, the analysis relies on several assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs) which are explicitly stated, but a more nuanced discussion of how the algorithms might perform under relaxation of these assumptions would be valuable. While the related work section is comprehensive, it could benefit from a more direct comparison of the computational complexity of UCB-ALP with existing algorithms like Mixture Elimination [17], even if only qualitatively. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:**  The first work to demonstrate logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.
*   **Technical Soundness:** Rigorous analysis and clear derivation of regret bounds.
*   **Significance:** Addresses a practically relevant problem with potential applications in various domains.
*   **Clarity:** Well-written and organized, making the core ideas accessible.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Boundary cases and their practical impact are not fully explored.
*   Limited discussion of performance under relaxed assumptions.
*   Lack of detailed computational complexity comparison with alternative approaches.

**Overall:** I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of constrained contextual bandits, offering a theoretically sound and practically promising approach. Addressing the points raised above would further strengthen the work and enhance its impact.
