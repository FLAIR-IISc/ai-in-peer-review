- Problem setting: studies contextual bandits with both a time horizon and a budget constraint, where decisions are coupled over time due to limited resources.  
- Main idea: approximate the intractable optimal oracle via an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approach that adjusts decisions using the remaining-budget/remaining-time ratio.  
- Known-statistics result: ALP is shown to be near-optimal in the known-reward-distribution case.  
- Unknown-rewards algorithm: proposes UCB-ALP that combines ALP with UCB to handle unknown rewards.  
- Improvement over prior work: achieves logarithmic (O(log T)) regret under its assumptions, improving on prior O(√T) results for more general settings.  
- Strength — originality/significance: first algorithm to obtain O(log T) regret for constrained contextual bandits under the given assumptions.  
- Strength — technical quality: elegant, low-complexity decomposition (ALP then UCB) with a seemingly rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Strength — clarity: well-written, organized exposition that builds intuition from simple to complex cases.  
- Weakness — restrictive assumptions: logarithmic regret requires finite contexts and “non-boundary” budget-to-time ratios; boundary cases incur O(√T) regret.  
- Weakness — unexplored sensitivity: sensitivity to being near boundary cases is not analyzed.  
- Weakness — limited main-paper scope: main analysis focuses on unit-cost and known context distribution; extensions (unknown distributions, heterogeneous costs) are only briefly discussed in the supplement.
