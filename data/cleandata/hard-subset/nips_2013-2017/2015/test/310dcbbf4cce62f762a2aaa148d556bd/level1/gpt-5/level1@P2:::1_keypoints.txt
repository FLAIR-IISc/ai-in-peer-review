- Problem: studies contextual bandits with a single hard budget over finite horizon, i.i.d. finite contexts, fixed (initially unit) costs; oracle requires coupling across time via remaining budget/time.
- Main idea: introduce an LP relaxation replacing hard budget with average constraint, yielding a closed-form static LP upper bound on the oracle reward.
- Algorithm: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) re-solves LP each round with average remaining budget bτ/τ and samples actions per-context with computed probabilities.
- Key technical insight: ALP’s budget consumption ≡ sampling without replacement, so remaining budget concentrates via hypergeometric tails.
- Regret (known stats): ALP’s expected reward is within O(1) of the oracle in non-boundary cases; O(√T) regret when ρ=B/T equals a cumulative context probability (boundary).
- Unknown rewards: combine ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP); non-boundary regret O(JK log T), boundary regret O(√T + JK log T).
- Unknown context distribution: replace π with empirical estimates; rigorous bounds require truncating updates.
- Heterogeneous costs: generalized LP yields O(1) regret with known stats; with unknown stats propose epsilon-first ALP (no log-regret analogue given).
- Relation to prior work: contrasts with BwK/RCB and primal-dual methods that obtain O(√T); novelty is instance-dependent logarithmic regret in finite stochastic-context setting.
- Technical soundness: LP bound, ALP design, and regret proofs are solid; budget-concentration argument is neat and central.
- Completeness/honesty: assumptions (finite contexts/actions, i.i.d. contexts, known costs, uniqueness) are stated and limitations acknowledged.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized; oracle/ALP and sampling-without-replacement mapping are clearly explained.
- Clarity suggestions: explicitly handle ties in u*, provide ALP pseudocode, give geometric intuition for v(ρ), and state per-round computational cost.
- Originality: adaptive LP tailored to hard-budget contextual bandits and O(1) approximation is a notable conceptual contribution.
- Significance: practically relevant (recommendation systems, clinical trials) and likely to influence constrained contextual bandit work; decoupling estimation from decision via rank-only ALP is reusable.
- Strength: simple, computationally efficient algorithm (thresholded LP with adaptive bτ/τ).
- Strength: strong instance-dependent regret guarantees (O(1) known stats; O(JK log T) unknown stats in non-boundary).
- Strength: clear separation of regret sources and clean concentration analysis; extensions to unknown π and heterogeneous costs discussed.
- Weakness: limited scope—finite i.i.d. contexts, single resource, known costs assumption; O(JK log T) dependence can be large.
- Weakness: boundary-case √T regret may be significant and no remedy is provided (e.g., smoothing/randomization).
- Weakness: unknown π analysis requires truncation; gap between analyzed truncated variant and natural continuous update not fully discussed.
- Weakness: heterogeneous-cost unknown case uses epsilon-first; no UCB-style log-regret extension provided.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation and comparisons to primal-dual BwK/RCB are lacking in the main text.
- Suggestions: add experiments vs. Mixture Elimination and primal-dual BwK, discuss practical tie/boundary handling (budget randomization), explore structured contexts to reduce O(JK) dependence, and tighten/empirically validate unknown-π analysis without truncation.
- Acceptance case: novel, elegant adaptive LP with strong theoretical guarantees and first log-regret result for this setting.
- Rejection case: restricted setting, boundary-case √T regret, truncated-π analysis, and limited empirical validation.
- Overall recommendation: Accept (borderline to clear accept).
