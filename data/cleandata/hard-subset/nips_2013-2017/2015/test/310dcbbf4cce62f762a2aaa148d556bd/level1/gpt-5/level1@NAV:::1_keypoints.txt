- Proposes an adaptive linear-programming (ALP) relaxation that replaces the hard knapsack constraint with an average-constraint LP driven by remaining budget ratio bτ/τ.  
- When rewards are known, ALP yields an O(1) regret gap to the oracle in unit-cost systems except on measure-zero “boundary” parameters.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP to handle unknown rewards; combining ALP with UCB gives O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.  
- Extends ALP to unknown context distribution via a truncated empirical variant (EALP) and to heterogeneous costs via a generalized LP and an ε-first variant.  
- Main novelty: first to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits with finite contexts/actions and i.i.d. arrivals via an adaptive LP that decouples estimation and control.  
- Theoretical development is careful and technically sound: LP upper bound benchmark, hypergeometric concentration for remaining budget, and regret decomposition are convincing.  
- Assumptions are clearly stated: unique best action per context, strict ordering of u* j, i.i.d. contexts, and unit costs for core results.  
- Boundary-case analysis is weaker (√T regret) and lacks a matching lower bound.  
- Several important proofs are deferred to the supplement.  
- Empirical validation is minimal and lacks comparisons to related work ([17,20–22]).  
- Clarity is generally good and Algorithm 1 is clear, but notation is heavy and an illustrative example beyond J=2 would help.  
- Practical considerations (per-round LP complexity and implementation for large J,K) are not discussed.  
- Originality: high — improves on prior O(√T) results in constrained contextual bandits.  
- Significance: strong — computationally light approach likely to influence budgeted decision designs, with useful extensions though some are conservative (ε-first).  
- Pros: first logarithmic regret guarantees, clean/modular analysis, computationally efficient and simple.  
- Cons: strong simplifying assumptions, boundary cases retain √T regret, limited experiments and partial extensions.
