- Tackles constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits, motivated by crowdsourcing and clinical trials.
- Proposes approximating the oracle via Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and combining ALP with UCB for unknown rewards.
- Analysis is rigorous, using concentration inequalities and bounding estimation errors.
- Builds on contextual bandit and budget-constrained bandit literature, highlighting gaps for contextual settings.
- Distinguishes from prior work (e.g., Resourceful Contextual Bandits) via finite contexts, fixed costs, random context arrivals, and adaptive budget constraint.
- Achieves logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits, a previously open and significant result.
- Quality: technically sound (9/10); core results well-developed though some extensions rely on supplementary material.
- Clarity: generally well-written (8/10) but mathematical analysis is dense and could use more intuition and smoother transitions.
- Originality: highly novel (9/10) due to ALP+UCB approach and adaptive budget constraint.
- Significance: high impact (9/10) with practical relevance and computational efficiency.
- Arguments for acceptance: first to achieve logarithmic regret, rigorous analysis, high practical/significant impact, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: dense mathematical exposition, Sections 5–6 underdeveloped, and √T regret in boundary cases.
- Overall: strong paper with compelling contributions; recommend acceptance and suggest more intuition and empirical details.
