- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits and proposes ALP to approximate the oracle using only the ordering of expected rewards.
- Authors combine ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to handle unknown expected rewards and prove near-optimality.
- UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret in general, with sublinear regret in certain boundary cases.
- Builds on prior contextual bandit and budget-limited bandit work but focuses on hard budget constraints and logarithmic regret.
- Strength: novel ALP algorithm that approximates the oracle and requires only reward ordering.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical regret guarantees for ALP and UCB-ALP.
- Strength: demonstrated practical relevance via applications (clinical trials, online hiring).
- Weakness: simplifying assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget) may limit real-world applicability.
- Weakness: boundary cases yield only sublinear, not logarithmic, regret.
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale use.
- Quality: technically sound, well-organized, and rigorously analyzed, but needs more complexity discussion.
- Clarity: well-written with clear notation, but could use more intuitive explanations and practical discussion.
- Originality: novel combination of ALP and UCB with new guarantees, though building on existing literature.
- Significance: meaningful contribution to constrained contextual bandits with practical implications.
- Pro acceptance: novel algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, and real-world applicability.
- Con acceptance: restrictive assumptions, missing complexity analysis, and need for clearer intuition/practical implications.
- Recommendation: accept subject to minor revisions addressing the noted concerns.
