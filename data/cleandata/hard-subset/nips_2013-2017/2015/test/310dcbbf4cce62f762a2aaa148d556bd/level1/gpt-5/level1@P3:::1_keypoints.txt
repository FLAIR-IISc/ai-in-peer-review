- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that relaxes a hard budget to an average budget and recomputes using remaining budget-to-time ratio, achieving O(1) regret (non-boundary) vs oracle.  
- Introduces UCB-ALP: combine UCB estimates per context-action with ALP ordering to learn and allocate, giving O(JK log T) regret (non-boundary) and O(√T + JK log T) on boundaries.  
- Extends to unknown context distributions via empirical/truncated estimates and to heterogeneous costs via a generalized LP and an epsilon-first variant.  
- Main novelty: first to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits under given assumptions, using ALP and ordering-only insight.  
- Key analytic tool: maps ALP remaining-budget dynamics to sampling without replacement (hypergeometric) to tightly control bτ/τ concentration.  
- Regret decomposition separates within-context action mistakes (O(log T)) from context-ranking and budget-fluctuation components; event-based analysis around thresholds is careful.  
- Strong non-boundary O(1) gap of ALP to the dynamic oracle is a notable theoretical result.  
- Assumptions simplify analysis: unit-cost, finite contexts/actions, i.i.d. contexts, known costs, strict unique ordering per context.  
- Boundary cases (ρ equal to cumulative context probability) incur O(√T) regret; these are measure-zero but practically relevant since B and T are integers/rational.  
- Unknown-π guarantees rely on a truncated empirical variant; full algorithmic details and proofs are deferred to the supplement.  
- Minor rigor concern: claim that bτ under UCB-ALP concentrates as in ALP needs a clearer coupling argument in the main text.  
- Several proofs are deferred; some key arguments (bounded differences for unknown π, coupling for budget concentration) are only sketched.  
- Clarity is generally good: ALP intuition, threshold j̃(ρ), and v(ρ) are well presented; regret decomposition and event definitions are clear.  
- Missing/weak points: no per-round complexity analysis, some typos/notation issues, and boundary-case bounds need clearer parameterization.  
- Computationally efficient: ALP is simple (threshold over J contexts per round); heterogeneous-cost LP grows to JK variables but remains structured.  
- Empirical evaluation is lacking in the main text; constants, sensitivity to small gaps, and comparison to prior baselines are not shown.  
- Pros: simple, insightful algorithm with strong theory, hypergeometric budget concentration analysis, and useful extensions.  
- Cons: restrictive assumptions, boundary-case performance, reliance on truncated estimates for unknown π, and deferred empirical/technical details.  
- Suggestions: add per-round complexity analysis, a short main-text lemma for the UCB-ALP coupling, discuss tie/near-tie sensitivity, and include experiments vs Mixture Elimination and primal-dual baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept (borderline strong) — solid theoretical contribution that would benefit from clearer coupling arguments and empirical validation.
