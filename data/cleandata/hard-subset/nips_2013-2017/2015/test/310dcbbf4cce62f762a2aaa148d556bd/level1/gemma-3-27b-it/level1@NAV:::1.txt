## Review of "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of constrained contextual bandits, where decisions must balance reward maximization with budget and time limitations. The authors make a significant contribution by presenting algorithms achieving logarithmic regret, a novel result in this setting. The work builds upon existing contextual bandit literature [1, 2, 3] and extends budget-constrained bandit approaches [11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16] to the contextual case. They appropriately position their work relative to the more general Resourceful Contextual Bandit framework [17], highlighting their focus on a more tractable, practically relevant setting.

The core idea is to approximate the optimal policy using Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP), leveraging its desirable properties – near-optimality with only reward ordering information. This is cleverly combined with UCB [4] to handle the unknown reward scenario, resulting in the UCB-ALP algorithm.  The analysis is rigorous and provides clear regret bounds, showing logarithmic regret under most parameter settings. The extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs further broaden the applicability of the proposed framework. The paper’s structure is logical, progressing from simplified settings to more general cases, enhancing clarity.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Achieving logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits is a significant advance.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, with careful consideration of various factors influencing regret.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the core ideas accessible.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs) align with many real-world applications, and the algorithm appears computationally feasible.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Boundary Cases:** The O(√T) regret in boundary cases is a limitation. While acknowledged, further investigation into mitigating this could be beneficial.
*   **Empirical Evaluation:** The paper lacks empirical results to validate the theoretical findings and demonstrate performance compared to baseline algorithms.  This is a notable omission.
*   **Discussion of Trade-offs:** A deeper discussion on the trade-offs between the complexity of the algorithm and the achieved regret bounds would be valuable.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound solution to a challenging problem.
*   The results advance the state-of-the-art in constrained contextual bandits.
*   The clear writing and logical structure enhance the paper’s impact.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of empirical validation limits confidence in the practical performance of the proposed algorithms.
*   The O(√T) regret in boundary cases, while acknowledged, could be a concern.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the lack of empirical results, I believe this paper is a **strong accept**. The theoretical contributions are significant, and the proposed algorithms represent a valuable step forward in the field of constrained contextual bandits. Addressing the weaknesses through future empirical validation would further strengthen the work.
