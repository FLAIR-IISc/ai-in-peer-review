- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, filling a gap in prior work.  
- Main contribution: first demonstration of logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual bandit setting.  
- Method: combines Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the optimal policy with UCB to handle unknown rewards (UCB-ALP).  
- Novelty: adaptive LP for the budget constraint is a new contribution compared to prior LP relaxations.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on unconstrained contextual bandits and budgeted bandits, simplifying RCB to achieve stronger bounds.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous analysis using concentration inequalities and careful regret partitioning.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, and presents ideas stepwise (unit cost → known/unknown rewards → heterogeneous costs).  
- Practical relevance: problem and approach are applicable to real-world domains (clinical trials, advertising, crowdsourcing).  
- Computational efficiency: UCB-ALP is relatively simple and implementable compared to more general methods.  
- Thoroughness: discusses extensions to unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs.  
- Weakness: boundary cases exist where only √T regret is achieved; more analysis of their frequency/size is needed.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical evaluation; simulations or real-data experiments would strengthen the claims.  
- Weakness: logarithmic bounds may hide large constant factors that are not characterized.  
- Weakness: initial assumption of known context distribution and brief treatment of variable costs limit applicability; estimating context distribution within UCB-ALP is suggested.  
- Recommendation: overall strong and novel—recommend acceptance with minor revisions to include preliminary experiments and discussion of boundary cases.
