- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with hard budget and time limits, proposing an adaptive LP (ALP) that enforces budget in expectation via remaining average bτ/τ.  
- For unit-cost known-context-distribution and known rewards, ALP is near-optimal: O(1) regret except O(√T) in boundary cases.  
- Combining ALP with UCB yields UCB-ALP with O(JK log T) regret (and O(√T+JK log T) at boundaries) for unknown rewards.  
- Extensions include unknown context distributions (EALP/UCB-EALP via truncated estimator) and heterogeneous costs (generalized ALP and ε-first variant).  
- Compared to prior work (Mixture Elimination, BwK, concave/convex bandits), this achieves logarithmic regret by exploiting discrete contexts/actions and an adaptive LP, with lower computation.  
- Strength: elegant ALP reduction viewing decisions as sampling without replacement, enabling concentration analysis.  
- Strength: strong regret guarantees—O(1) for ALP (non-boundary) and first-known logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under stochastic contexts.  
- Strength: clear decomposition of regret sources (action-ranking, context-ranking, budget fluctuation) and careful probabilistic control.  
- Strength: practical per-round computation (simple threshold/randomization or modest LP) and only requires ordering of expected rewards.  
- Strength: meaningful extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs with reasonable analysis.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on non-boundary assumption; boundary cases common in discrete settings and lead to O(√T) regret.  
- Weakness: core guarantees assume i.i.d. contexts, known π (later relaxed); unknown-π analysis is sketched and lacks detail on truncation timing and constants.  
- Weakness: regret benchmark uses relaxed oracle Û(T,B); translation to true oracle regret for UCB-ALP should be emphasized or stated as a corollary.  
- Weakness: UCB-ALP may deviate from bτ/τ due to ranking errors; concentration claims for budget under UCB-ALP are deferred to supplement and need main-text visibility.  
- Weakness: missing formal lower bounds for this budgeted discrete setting; order optimality claim is plausible but not proved.  
- Minor issues: notation/typos (e.g., Θ(o)√T), dangling footnotes, and need for pseudocode and discussion of gap-dependent constants.  
- Quality: technical development appears sound; ALP analysis via hypergeometric concentration is neat though some proofs are deferred.  
- Clarity: generally well-organized and readable; would benefit from more main-text intuition and key lemmas.  
- Originality: adaptive LP tied to remaining budget and sampling-without-replacement coupling appears novel and contrasts prior O(√T) results.  
- Significance: high—logarithmic regret with a simple algorithm under realistic stochastic contexts is impactful for resource-constrained applications.  
- Suggested improvements: add proof sketch of UCB-ALP budget concentration, quantify truncated EALP (schedule and regret), discuss robustness to non-i.i.d. contexts and unknown T, and handle boundary cases via regularization/tie-breaking.  
- Suggested improvements: include empirical comparisons to Mixture Elimination, BwK, and concave/convex UCB on synthetic and boundary/heterogeneous-cost settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first logarithmic-regret algorithm for hard-budget constrained contextual bandits, elegant analysis, computationally efficient, and impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on non-boundary assumption, deferred analyses for unknown π and UCB-ALP concentration, and lack of experiments; overall leaning toward acceptance.
