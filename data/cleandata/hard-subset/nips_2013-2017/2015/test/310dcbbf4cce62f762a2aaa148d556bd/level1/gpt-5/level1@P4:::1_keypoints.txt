- Studies contextual bandits with finite horizon T and budget B; proposes an adaptive LP (ALP) giving a threshold policy and proving O(1) regret except O(√T) at budget-ratio boundary values.  
- Extends to unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs; combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to get O(JK log T) regret (non-boundary) and O(√T + JK log T) (boundary); also proposes EALP/UCB-EALP and an ε-first variant.  
- Core technical novelty: map remaining budget under ALP to sampling-without-replacement and use hypergeometric concentration to bound budget fluctuation.  
- Algorithmic idea is simple and computationally efficient (threshold structure; only ordering of u* j required).  
- First logarithmic-regret guarantees (to reviewer’s knowledge) for constrained contextual bandits with discrete stochastic contexts.  
- Regret decomposition into action-ranking and context-ranking/budget-fluctuation is clear and leverages standard UCB analysis.  
- Technical analysis is solid and convincing; proofs sketched in main text with details in supplement.  
- Strong/limiting assumptions: finite i.i.d. contexts, known horizon, single budget, unit costs initially, and uniqueness/strict ordering of best actions.  
- Boundary-case performance degrades to O(√T); robustness as ρ approaches context-probability thresholds is not quantified beyond asymptotics.  
- Unknown-context rigorous analysis relies on a truncated empirical estimator; the gap to the fully adaptive empirical variant used in practice is unresolved.  
- Heterogeneous-cost + unknown-reward case uses ε-first exploration rather than a fully adaptive UCB-like scheme, raising practicality and constant-factor concerns.  
- Main text omits instance-dependent/gap-dependent constants; the “JK log T” term hides potentially large dependence on reward gaps and arm counts.  
- Limited empirical evidence in the main text; lacks comparisons to RCB [17] and BwK [12] baselines and sensitivity tests near boundary regimes.  
- Clarity is generally good; problem, benchmarks, and algorithms are well presented, but recommend stating per-round complexity, tie-breaking/near-tie behavior, and gap dependence.  
- Originality: adaptive LP relaxation and sampling-without-replacement analysis are novel relative to prior BwK/RCB/concave-knapsack work.  
- Significance: advances theory by showing logarithmic regret is achievable under budget constraints with contexts and offers lightweight algorithms for finite-context applications.  
- Pros for acceptance: simple, efficient algorithms with strong regret guarantees and novel analysis linking contextual bandits and resource constraints.  
- Cons for acceptance: restricted/strong assumptions, partial theory for unknown-context and heterogeneous-cost cases, limited experiments, and no tailored lower bounds.  
- Suggested improvements: add experiments comparing to RCB/BwK and sensitivity to ρ near q_j; clarify ties/near-ties and provide gap-dependent constants; investigate fully adaptive UCB for heterogeneous costs and tighten analysis for the fully empirical-context version.
