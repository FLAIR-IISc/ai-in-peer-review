- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that needs only ordering of expected rewards and is near-optimal.
- Introduces UCB-ALP by combining ALP with UCB for unknown rewards, achieving logarithmic regret except in boundary cases.
- Technical content is sound with a rigorous regret analysis.
- Paper is well-written and situates the work within related literature.
- Strength: novel ALP algorithm requiring only reward ordering.
- Strength: UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret in most cases.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the results.
- Weakness: assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint—may be unrealistic.
- Weakness: no experimental results to validate empirical performance.
- Weakness: analysis is complex and may be difficult for some readers to follow.
- Pro-acceptance: novel solution, rigorous analysis, and clear presentation.
- Con-acceptance: restrictive assumptions, lack of experiments, and analytical complexity.
- Overall: significant contribution and deemed worthy of acceptance.
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
