- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits under a simplified model (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget).  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) achieving O(1) regret when statistics are known.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP achieving O(log T) regret with unknown rewards, with O(√T) regret in certain boundary cases.  
- Novel connection between dynamic programming oracle and linear programming relaxation.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis with careful proofs and a strong concentration result for remaining budget (Lemma 2).  
- Clear distinction and analysis of boundary vs non-boundary cases, with explanation why O(log T) is impossible in boundary scenarios.  
- Computationally efficient: ALP requires only ordering of expected rewards, practical for implementation.  
- UCB-ALP naturally combines with standard UCB exploration techniques.  
- Lacks empirical validation or experiments comparing UCB-ALP to prior methods.  
- Insufficient quantification or practical guidance on how often boundary cases occur in real applications.  
- Extension to heterogeneous costs is mentioned but not developed in technical detail.  
- Needs clearer discussion of when the simplified model assumptions hold or fail in practice.  
- Comparison to prior work is strong (improves on O(√T) results of [17,20–22]) but could better explain why adaptive budget yields logarithmic regret versus prior approaches.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, well-written theoretical contribution with minor practical gaps.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant advancement for constrained contextual bandits despite limited empirical evaluation.
