- Studies constrained contextual bandits with time and single budget constraints under finite discrete contexts and fixed costs.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for known statistics achieving O(1) regret except boundary cases.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP for unknown statistics achieving O(log T) regret for non-boundary cases.  
- Key idea: approximate the oracle with linear programming and combine with UCB to tolerate estimation errors.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with clear lemmas, theorems, and proofs; handles boundary cases explicitly.  
- Novel contribution over prior work (including [17]) by achieving logarithmic instead of O(√T) regret in this model.  
- Significant and practical: relevant to clinical trials, wireless networks; algorithms are computationally efficient.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; main definitions and theorems are clearly presented.  
- Concern: boundary cases (where regret is O(√T)) may be non-negligible for finite T and need clearer treatment.  
- Concern: analysis for unknown context distribution is truncated and less complete than the known-distribution case.  
- Concern: lacks detailed comparison with very recent related work [21,22] that extends [20].  
- Review scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first to achieve logarithmic regret, rigorous analysis, practical and efficient algorithms, clear contributions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: prevalence of boundary cases, incomplete unknown-distribution analysis, limited comparison to recent papers.  
- Recommendation: accept (strong contribution, minor issues to address).
