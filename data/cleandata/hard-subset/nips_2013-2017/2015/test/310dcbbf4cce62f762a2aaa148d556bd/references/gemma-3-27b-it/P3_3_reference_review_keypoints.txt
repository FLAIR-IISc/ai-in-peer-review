- Tackles contextual bandits with time and budget constraints, a realistic and under-addressed setting.
- Main contribution: algorithms (UCB-ALP) achieving logarithmic regret except in boundary cases.
- Method: LP relaxation to an average budget constraint, adaptive budget allocation (ALP), combined with UCB exploration.
- Positions itself against prior work (Badanidiyuru et al., Agrawal et al.), focusing on a simplified finite-context/fixed-cost model to get stronger guarantees.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, using concentration inequalities and adaptive algorithm techniques.
- ALP algorithm is computationally efficient compared to more complex approaches like mixture elimination.
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a logical presentation of ideas.
- Generalizations to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs are discussed but mainly deferred to supplementary material.
- Key weakness: logarithmic regret fails in boundary cases (√T regret), with limited exploration of their frequency or mitigation.
- Simplified model assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs) limit immediate applicability to some real-world scenarios.
- Lacks empirical evaluation on simulated or real datasets and comparisons with existing constrained bandit algorithms.
- Does not discuss hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., UCB exploration constant) or provide guidance for tuning.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Minor revision requests: analyze/mitigate boundary cases, add empirical experiments and comparisons, discuss hyperparameter sensitivity, and expand on generalization details.
