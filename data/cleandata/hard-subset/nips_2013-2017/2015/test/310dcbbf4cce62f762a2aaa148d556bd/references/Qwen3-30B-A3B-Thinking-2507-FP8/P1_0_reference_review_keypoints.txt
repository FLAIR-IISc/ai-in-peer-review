- Summary: Proposes ALP (known statistics) and UCB-ALP (unknown) for constrained contextual bandits, achieving O(1)/O(log T) regret except O(√T) in boundary cases via LP-based budget-aware policies.

- Technical quality: Rigorous theoretical analysis with precise definitions, novel techniques to handle budget randomness and ranking errors, and well-constructed proofs.

- Originality: Introduces LP approximation that decouples information acquisition from decision making, addressing a gap for observable-context constrained bandits.

- Significance: Achieves logarithmic regret where prior contextual methods only gave O(√T), advancing theory with practical implications for budget-limited applications.

- Clarity: Well-structured and clearly written, with pedagogical progression from unit-cost to heterogeneous-cost systems and intuitive proof sketches.

- Boundary cases: Boundary parameter settings yield O(√T) regret; claim they are measure-zero but lack practical justification of rarity.

- Computational complexity: Claims computational efficiency but omits detailed complexity analysis; solving LPs each timestep may be costly for large J or K.

- Empirical validation: No experiments presented; empirical results would strengthen claims and show practical performance, especially in boundary cases.

- Generalization: Focuses on single budget constraint; limited discussion on extension to multiple resource constraints.

- Recommendation: Overall accept — strong theoretical contribution deserving NIPS with minor issues fixable in revision.

- Pro-accept: First to achieve logarithmic regret (except boundary), rigorous treatment of boundary cases, novel LP technique, clear advancement over prior work.

- Con-accept: Lacks empirical validation, insufficient computational-cost detail, and limited discussion on multi-constraint generalization.
