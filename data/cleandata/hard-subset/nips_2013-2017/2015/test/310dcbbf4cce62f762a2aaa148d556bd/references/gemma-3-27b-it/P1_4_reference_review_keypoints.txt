- Overall: Valuable contribution on constrained contextual bandits; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Problem & approach: Studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints; introduces Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and UCB-ALP to handle unknown rewards.
- Main result: Provable logarithmic regret for UCB-ALP under reasonable assumptions (√T in boundary cases).
- Novelty/positioning: Extends UCB and LP-relaxation ideas to constrained contextual bandits, distinguishes from RCB and recent √T-results.
- Strength: Addresses an important, practical problem (budget constraints) with principled methods.
- Strength: Strong theoretical guarantees and rigorous regret analysis.
- Strength: Clear, well-structured presentation and accessible notation.
- Strength: ALP is a clever, adaptive relaxation of hard budget constraints.
- Strength: Novel analytical techniques (concentration under adaptive algorithms, estimation error bounds).
- Strength: Brief discussion of extensions (unknown context distributions, heterogeneous costs).
- Weakness: Simplifying assumptions (discrete contexts, fixed costs) limit direct applicability.
- Weakness: Boundary cases yielding √T regret need further investigation and clarification on prevalence/impact.
- Weakness: Limited empirical evaluation—needs simulations and comparisons to baselines.
- Weakness: Logarithmic bounds may hide large constants; practical impact unclear.
- Weakness: Computational cost of repeatedly solving LPs could be high; complexity/optimizations not fully discussed.
- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Acceptance arguments: Strong theory, novel ALP approach, clear presentation, practical relevance.
- Rejection arguments: Restrictive assumptions, √T boundary behavior, lack of experiments.
- Requested revisions: Add simulations, analyze boundary-case prevalence/impact, discuss ALP computational complexity and optimizations, and comment on constant factors in regret bounds.
