Summary
The paper studies contextual bandits with a single hard budget and finite horizon, focusing on finite discrete contexts and actions. It proposes: (i) an LP relaxation of the dynamic-programming oracle by replacing a hard budget with an average-budget constraint, yielding a simple static upper bound Û(T,B) = Tv(ρ); (ii) an Adaptive LP (ALP) policy that plugs in the instantaneous remaining average budget bτ/τ, shown to achieve O(1) regret w.r.t. the oracle in non-boundary cases (ρ ≠ qj) and O(√T) in boundary cases; (iii) UCB-ALP for unknown rewards, combining UCB estimates with ALP, with regret O(JK log T) in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) on the boundary; (iv) extensions to unknown context distributions (via empirical estimates with truncation) and to heterogeneous costs (via a generalized LP and an ε-first approach). The work positions itself against RCB/CBwK-type algorithms which achieve O(√T) regret under more general assumptions.

Strengths
- Clear and appealing high-level idea: exploit the structure of LP relaxation and adapt to the remaining average budget, decoupling learning from constrained decision-making.
- Technically nontrivial but intuitive analysis: mapping ALP’s consumption to sampling without replacement to get concentration of bτ/τ; good regret decomposition for UCB-ALP.
- Achieves instance-dependent logarithmic regret in a constrained contextual setting under explicit structural assumptions; appears to be novel relative to prior O(√T) results with dynamic contexts.
- Computationally simple in the unit-cost, finite-context setting (essentially sorting the û∗j and applying a threshold rule each round).

Weaknesses and concerns
- Clarity/presentation issues:
  - Several equations are malformed or incorrect. Example: Eq. (4) is incomplete (third case missing) and poorly typeset; Theorem 1 bounds read “u∗1 − u∗J − e−2δ2” and “Θ(o) √T” which are nonsensical as written; Section 4.1 defines ūj,k(t) incorrectly (multiplies by Cj,k rather than divides). Algorithm 1 contains “û∗j (t)← û∗j,k∗j (t)(t)” which looks like a typo. These errors make parts of the analysis hard to follow or verify.
  - The ALP algorithm is presented as “solve LP” each round; for the unit-cost case, the solution is a simple threshold with at most one fractional context, but the text does not give the explicit operational rule in pseudocode form (compute j̃(b/τ) and pj̃+1(b/τ)). This hinders reproducibility.
  - The “boundary cases” are repeatedly dismissed as measure-zero; in practice, B/T is discrete and equality ρ = qj can be common (and known a priori). The text should avoid the “measure-zero” language and instead discuss robustness or a tie-handling strategy.
- Assumptions:
  - Main theorems assume known context distribution π; the unknown-π case is relegated to a truncated empirical variant with claims deferred to the supplement. This weakens the main narrative.
  - Uniqueness assumptions: a unique best action per context and strictly ordered u∗j across contexts. The paper claims these are for simplicity, but the algorithms depend on correct orderings. Tie-breaking and robustness aren’t specified in the main text.
- Technical gaps or places needing clarification:
  - The coupling showing that under UCB-ALP, the remaining budget still enjoys the hypergeometric concentration (Lemma 2) is asserted but not substantiated in the main text. Given that UCB-ALP may mis-rank contexts early on, a short proof sketch explaining why the LP remains tight and the per-step consumption probability equals b/τ would improve confidence.
  - Computational complexity is not discussed. Even if the unit-cost solution is O(J) per round, with sorting O(J log J), this is repeated T times. Practical considerations (e.g., incremental maintenance of the ordering of û∗j) should be addressed.
  - The final regret statements suppress gap dependencies. The O(JK log T) hides 1/Δ^2 factors inherited from UCB. If the contribution is “logarithmic regret,” please state instance-dependent bounds explicitly (with Δ’s) and compare to known lower bounds in this constrained setting.
  - The extension to heterogeneous costs is too brief in the main paper: the LP should be stated explicitly (variables p_{j,k}, constraints ∑k p_{j,k} ≤ 1 and ∑j,k πj cj,k p_{j,k} ≤ ρ) and the precise regret results listed (known vs unknown rewards). Using ε-first rather than UCB is a notable design choice that deserves justification (e.g., why UCB-ALP does not extend cleanly).
- Empirics: The paper alludes to “observed in numerical simulations,” but provides no experiments in the main text. For NeurIPS, even for a theory paper, a small, targeted experimental section comparing ALP/UCB-ALP to CBwK/RCB baselines (e.g., Mixture Elimination, BwK) on synthetic finite-context problems would materially strengthen the submission and validate constants and robustness to boundary cases and mis-specified π.
- References and formatting: Many citations are incomplete (no years, venues, pages), and some important related work is insufficiently discussed (e.g., contextual BwK variants). The writing has numerous minor grammatical issues.

Quality
Conceptually sound and technically plausible, but the current draft has enough mathematical typos and clarity issues that an expert cannot reliably verify some claims from the main text alone. Important parts (unknown π, heterogeneous costs) are relegated to the supplement.

Clarity
Moderate. The overarching idea is clear, but execution suffers from notation errors, missing cases, and under-specified algorithms. Rewriting key equations, providing exact pseudocode, and expanding the unknown-π and heterogeneous-cost sections would help a lot.

Originality
Within the restricted setting (finite contexts/actions, unit costs, known π), achieving instance-dependent O(log T) regret under hard budgets appears novel relative to prior O(√T) results with dynamic contexts. However, the restriction is substantial.

Significance
Potentially impactful for practitioners facing budgeted personalization with finitely many segments. Yet the narrow scope, boundary caveats, and lack of experiments limit immediate practical impact.

Detailed suggestions and questions
- Fix all mathematical typos (Eq. (4), Theorem 1 bounds, empirical mean definition, Algorithm 1 line).
- Provide explicit per-round ALP rule without “solve LP” (compute j̃ and p for j̃+1).
- State and prove (in main or appendix) that the LP in UCB-ALP is always tight, preserving the Bernoulli(b/τ) consumption and Lemma 2.
- Provide instance-dependent bounds with explicit gaps Δj,k in Theorem 2.
- Discuss and implement tie-handling (both across actions within a context and across contexts) and show the impact on regret.
- Add a short experimental section.
- Expand the unknown-π main-text theorem statement (even if for EALP-Trunc) and clarify how truncation time is chosen.
- For heterogeneous costs, give the LP and full theorem statements.

Pros
- Simple, elegant algorithmic idea with near-oracle performance under structure.
- Nontrivial regret analysis via budget concentration and ranking-error accounting.
- Potentially first logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits with dynamic contexts under these assumptions.

Cons
- Numerous presentation/notation errors; some derivations hard to verify.
- Heavy reliance on simplifying assumptions; boundary cases common in practice.
- No experiments; extensions lack detail in main text.

Recommendation
Lean reject in current form. The core idea is strong, but the paper needs substantial polishing, clearer algorithms and proofs, explicit instance-dependent bounds, and at least minimal experiments. With these improvements, it could become a solid contribution.
