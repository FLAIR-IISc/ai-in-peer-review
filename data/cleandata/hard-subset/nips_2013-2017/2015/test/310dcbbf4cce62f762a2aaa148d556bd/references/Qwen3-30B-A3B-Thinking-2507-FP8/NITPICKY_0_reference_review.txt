

# Review of "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

This paper investigates constrained contextual bandits with budget constraints, proposing an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that achieves O(1) regret (except for boundary cases) when statistics are known, and a UCB-ALP algorithm that achieves O(JK log T) regret for non-boundary cases when statistics are unknown. The work builds on prior research in contextual bandits [1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 7] and budget-constrained bandits [11-16, 17], with the key novelty being the use of linear programming approximations to handle the budget constraint.

## Strengths
The paper presents a theoretically sound approach to a challenging problem. The ALP approximation provides a novel way to handle the complex oracle algorithm in constrained contextual bandits. The UCB-ALP algorithm effectively combines the desirable properties of ALP (tolerance to estimation errors) with UCB (efficient exploration), achieving the claimed logarithmic regret for non-boundary cases. The paper demonstrates clear technical depth in analyzing the regret, with careful treatment of context ranking errors and budget fluctuations.

## Weaknesses

### Formatting and Technical Errors
1. Multiple LaTeX formatting inconsistencies: In Theorem 1, the expression "u ∗ 1−u ∗ J −e−2δ2" should be "u^*_1 - u^*_J - e^{-2δ^2}". Similarly, in Theorem 2, "O ( JK log T )" should have consistent spacing as "O(JK log T)".

2. In section 3.1, equation (5) has a formatting error: "v(ρ) = j̃(ρ)∑ j=1 πju ∗ j" should be "v(ρ) = ∑_{j=1}^{j̃(ρ)} π_j u^*_j + ..."

3. The phrase "the contextXt is observable" (section 2) contains a missing space between "context" and "Xt".

4. In Lemma 3, the inequality "P{ûj,k(t) ≥ ûj′,k′(t)|Cj,k(t− 1) ≥ `j,k} ≤ 2t−1" should be "P{...} ≤ 2t^{-1}".

### Theoretical and Methodological Concerns

1. The paper claims to be the first to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits, but fails to adequately distinguish its approach from [21, 22] which also address dynamic contexts. The paper states "we consider random context arrivals and use an adaptive budget constraint to achieve logarithmic regret" but doesn't clearly explain why their approach achieves logarithmic regret while [21, 22] only achieve O(√T) regret. This is a critical gap in the theoretical justification.

2. The claim that "bτ follows the hypergeometric distribution" (section 3.2) appears incorrect. The hypergeometric distribution models sampling without replacement from a finite population, while the paper's setup involves a continuous budget constraint. The paper should either correct this statement or provide a rigorous justification for this approximation.

3. The paper states "the best action under each context is unique" but doesn't discuss how this assumption affects the practical applicability of their results. In real-world applications, ties in expected rewards are common and would require special handling.

4. In the UCB-ALP algorithm, the coefficient in the exploration term is given as "√log t / 2Cj,k(t-1)" but the standard UCB formula uses "√(2 log t)/Cj,k(t-1)". The paper should justify this specific coefficient choice, as it directly impacts the regret bound.

5. The paper claims "a set of parameters of zero Lebesgue measure" for boundary cases but in Theorem 1, boundary cases are defined as "ρ = qj for some j ∈ {1, 2, ..., J-1}". These are not "zero Lebesgue measure" cases but rather discrete boundary points, which is a significant theoretical misstatement.

6. In section 6, the phrase "an -First ALP algorithm" contains a clear typo that should be "epsilon-First ALP".

### Clarity and Presentation

1. The paper mentions "a series of novel techniques" but fails to clearly identify or highlight these novel methodological contributions. The techniques mentioned (e.g., "method of showing concentration properties under adaptive algorithms") are not sufficiently distinguished from existing methods in the literature.

2. The paper claims to be the first to achieve logarithmic regret but doesn't provide empirical validation of their theoretical claims. For a NIPS paper, this is a significant omission.

3. The paper repeatedly references "supplementary material" for details on generalizations (Sections 5-6), which is acceptable, but the main text should more clearly indicate what specific technical challenges these generalizations present that necessitate supplementary material.

## Overall Assessment

While the paper makes a theoretically interesting contribution to constrained contextual bandits, the numerous minor technical errors, formatting inconsistencies, and theoretical misstatements significantly undermine the paper's credibility. The paper's claim of being the first to achieve logarithmic regret is not sufficiently justified against recent related work [21, 22], and the theoretical justification for key claims (like the hypergeometric distribution) is flawed.

The paper's main contribution is promising, but the numerous minor errors and unaddressed theoretical concerns prevent it from meeting NIPS's standard for technical rigor. The authors should carefully revise these issues before reconsideration.

## Recommendation

Reject. The paper has significant technical flaws that need to be addressed before it can be considered for publication. The numerous formatting errors, theoretical misstatements, and unaddressed gaps in the comparison with recent work [21, 22] make it difficult to assess the true novelty and contribution of the work.
