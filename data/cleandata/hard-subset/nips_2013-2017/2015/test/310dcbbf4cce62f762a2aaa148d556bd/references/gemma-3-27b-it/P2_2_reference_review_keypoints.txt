- Tackles contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, focusing on achieving low regret with observable contexts.
- Proposes an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm with O(1) regret in known-statistics setting.
- Extends ALP to unknown statistics via UCB-ALP, achieving O(log T) regret except for boundary cases with O(√T).
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis using concentration bounds and careful regret partitioning.
- Addresses a practically important problem with applications like clinical trials and online advertising.
- Novel combination of LP and UCB methods filling a gap in the constrained contextual bandit literature.
- UCB-ALP is presented as computationally tractable and implementable under simplifying assumptions.
- Main weakness: √T regret in boundary cases is significant and its practical impact/frequency is unclear.
- Reliance on simplified assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs) may limit generalizability to realistic settings.
- Comparison to recent √T-regret work [21,22] is incomplete and downplays their contributions (finite policy set restriction).
- Some sections (notably 3.2 and 4.3) are mathematically dense and lack intuitive explanations.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a literature gap, strong theoretical guarantees, practical relevance, rigorous analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: boundary-case √T regret, limiting assumptions, insufficient comparison to recent work.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Requested revisions: expand discussion on limitations and extensions (infinite contexts, heterogeneous costs).
