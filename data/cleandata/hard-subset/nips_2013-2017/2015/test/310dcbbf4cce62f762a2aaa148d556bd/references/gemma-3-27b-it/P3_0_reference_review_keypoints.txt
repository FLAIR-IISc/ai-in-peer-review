- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with time and budget limits and develops algorithms achieving logarithmic regret under reasonable assumptions.
- Main algorithms: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) using LP relaxations and UCB-ALP combining ALP with UCB for unknown rewards.
- Theoretical analysis is thorough, rigorous, and includes boundary-case treatment and generalizations (unknown context distribution, heterogeneous costs).
- Achieves a significant improvement over prior work by obtaining logarithmic rather than √T regret in many settings.
- Correctly positions and differentiates itself from prior contextual and budgeted bandit literature.
- Novel use of LP relaxations as a proxy for the optimal policy is a key technical contribution.
- Clear and well-organized presentation with useful breakdown of known vs. unknown statistics analyses.
- Addresses several important extensions and demonstrates strong awareness of related work.
- Primary limitation: assumes finite contexts and fixed costs, which restricts applicability to some real-world scenarios.
- Regret analysis is complex and may be inaccessible to non-expert readers; more intuitive explanations would help.
- Boundary cases still incur √T regret; impact and mitigation strategies for these cases are not fully explored.
- Major empirical weakness: no simulations or real-data experiments to validate theoretical claims or compare baselines.
- Recommendation: strongly accept based on significant theoretical contribution and technical rigor, with suggestion to add empirical validation in future versions.
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
