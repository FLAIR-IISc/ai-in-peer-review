- Paper studies contextual bandits with hard budget and finite horizon using an LP relaxation and an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) policy that adapts to average remaining budget bτ/τ.  
- ALP replaces the DP oracle with a static LP upper bound and a per-step adaptive LP solved with current bτ/τ and randomized thresholding.  
- Known-statistics, unit-cost, finite-contexts: ALP achieves O(1) regret off “boundary” ρ values and O(√T) regret at boundary values (ρ equals a cumulative context probability).  
- ALP’s key property: next-spend probability equals bτ/τ, inducing a sampling-without-replacement structure and strong concentration of the remaining budget.  
- Unknown rewards, known context distribution: UCB-ALP combines UCB with ALP and attains O(JK log T) regret off-boundary and O(√T + JK log T) at boundaries.  
- Regret for UCB-ALP is decomposed into within-context action-ranking errors and cross-context thresholding errors; only O(log T) pulls per context-action are needed to recover rankings.  
- Unknown context distribution: EALP uses empirical frequencies with a truncated update, yielding similar concentration and O(1) regret off-boundary (proofs in supplement).  
- Heterogeneous costs: extension formulates an LP over context-action pairs with “one action per context per round”; known-statistics yields O(1) off-boundary and O(√T) at boundaries; ε-first variant for unknown rewards is outlined.  
- Novelty: first logarithmic regret guarantees for constrained contextual bandits with i.i.d. contexts and finite discrete actions/costs, leveraging bτ/τ adaptation.  
- Relation to prior work: improves on general BwK/RCB and concave-knapsack results (which obtain O(√T)) by exploiting finite i.i.d. structure to get log T rates.  
- Quality: high-level proofs are plausible and technically interesting; the adaptive LP and budget-concentration argument are elegant and central to results.  
- Technical correctness: regret decomposition is standard but well adapted; some arguments (hypergeometric claim, exchangeability) need clearer justification.  
- Clarification needed: assumptions on unique best action per context and strict ordering are strong — need algorithmic tie-handling and explicit effect on regret.  
- Clarification needed: Theorem 1 has typographical/statement issues and should be restated with explicit constants and δ-dependence.  
- Clarification needed: the claim that bτ has an exact hypergeometric distribution under ALP needs a short self-contained argument or replacement via martingale inequalities.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but defers important technical details to the supplement; main text should better state conditions for concentration and role of known context distribution.  
- Practical details: Algorithm 1 contains typos and the paper should state per-round computational complexity (sorting UCBs and solving the simple LP) and heuristics for large J,K.  
- Pros: simple, efficient algorithms (ALP/UCB-ALP), novel adaptive LP idea, budget concentration tools, and extensions to unknown contexts and heterogeneous costs.  
- Cons: strong simplifying assumptions (i.i.d. finite contexts, known costs, unique best actions), boundary/tie regimes incur √T without matching lower bounds, and some proofs/details are deferred.  
- Empirical/robustness concerns: limited experiments in main text; need validation near boundary values, sensitivity to πj misspecification, and behavior with small gaps between u∗j’s.  
- Suggestions/questions: provide instance-dependent lower bounds for boundary regime or smoothing fixes; analyze sensitivity to πj misspecification and ties; consider KL-UCB for better constants; provide per-round complexity and main-text guarantees for heterogeneous-cost unknown-reward case.  
- Recommendation: generally positive—lean accept conditional on clarifying technical statements (Theorem 1, hypergeometric argument), handling ties/boundaries, and tightening presentation.
