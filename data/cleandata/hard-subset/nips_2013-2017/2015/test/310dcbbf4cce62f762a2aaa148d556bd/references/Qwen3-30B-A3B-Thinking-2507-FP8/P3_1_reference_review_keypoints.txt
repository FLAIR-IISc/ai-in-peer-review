- Addresses constrained contextual bandits (budget/time) and shows non-applicability of prior non-contextual results.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle and combines it with UCB for unknown statistics.  
- Achieves O(1) regret for ALP with known statistics and O(JK log T) regret for UCB-ALP with unknown statistics in non-boundary cases.  
- Analysis assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous with careful treatment of boundary vs. non-boundary cases and novel proof techniques.  
- ALP approximation (average budget LP) is a novel, elegant insight requiring only reward orderings.  
- Paper is very clearly written, well-organized, and builds intuition from simple to complex settings.  
- Results are significant—first to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits with practical implications.  
- Limitation: strong simplifying assumptions (discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget) and limited discussion on generalization.  
- Limitation: no empirical validation or simulations to illustrate practical regret behavior.  
- Limitation: boundary-case practical frequency not sufficiently discussed despite theoretical measure-zero claim.  
- Limitation: insufficient detailed comparison with recent related work ([21,22]) and extensions of [20].  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution suitable for NIPS with the main shortcoming being lack of experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestion to add discussion of boundary-case frequency and a simple simulation to strengthen the paper.
