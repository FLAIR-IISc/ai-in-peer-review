- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, proposing ALP and UCB-ALP to achieve logarithmic (or sublinear) regret.  
- Uses LP to approximate the DP oracle, a novel approach that avoids DP’s computational complexity.  
- Analyzes remaining budget evolution via hypergeometric distribution properties (Lemma 2), a significant technical contribution.  
- Provides rigorous regret bounds for ALP (Theorem 1) and UCB-ALP (Theorem 2) with proofs in the supplement.  
- Unit-cost assumption is restrictive and limits applicability to settings with variable action costs.  
- Boundary cases (ρ = qj) still incur O(√T) regret and are left as an unresolved open problem.  
- Comparison with recent works [21,22] is inadequate; differences in theoretical guarantees and practical implications are not clearly explained.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear problem statement and structured lemmas/theorems.  
- Needs more intuitive explanation of why the LP approximation works and clearer, concrete examples of budget effects.  
- Original contribution: first to claim logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under the given assumptions via LP relaxation.  
- Claim of being first to achieve logarithmic regret is weakened by insufficient discussion of [21,22] that also handle dynamic contexts.  
- Significance: substantial theoretical improvement over prior O(√T) results, relevant to clinical trials and recommendation systems.  
- UCB-ALP is computationally efficient and practical for real-world deployment.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparisons with [21,22], boundary-case discussion, and unit-cost limitation.
