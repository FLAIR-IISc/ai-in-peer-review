- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits and proposes ALP (known stats) and UCB-ALP (unknown stats) achieving O(1) and O(log T) regret respectively (with √T in boundary cases).
- Introduces an LP-based approximation of the oracle, improving over prior RCB framework and achieving the first logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Strong technical quality with rigorous proofs, careful regret decomposition, and well-structured lemmas supporting claims.
- Key insight: use linear programming to approximate dynamic programming oracle, avoiding DP computational complexity.
- Honest acknowledgment and analysis of boundary cases where regret degrades to O(√T).
- Elegant analysis of remaining budget evolution using hypergeometric distribution properties (Lemma 2).
- Robustness demonstrated through extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs (Sections 5–6).
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized exposition, with precise problem formulation and helpful proof sketches for main theorems.
- Novel contribution relative to prior work: avoids finite-policy benchmarks and improves regret bounds from O(√T) to O(log T).
- Significant practical relevance for applications with budget constraints (e.g., clinical trials, recommendations) and computationally efficient algorithms.
- Strengths: rigorous theory, substantial improvement over prior bounds, novel ALP approach, clear presentation, and thorough extensions.
- Weaknesses: lacks empirical experiments to validate practical performance.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on how common or impactful boundary cases are in practice.
- Weaknesses: some technical concentration results (Lemma 2) could use more intuitive explanation for broader accessibility.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically sound, novel, significant, and well-written despite minor empirical and exposition shortcomings.
