- Problem studied: contextual bandits with a single hard budget over finite horizon, i.i.d. finite contexts, finite actions, bounded stochastic rewards, unit (or fixed) costs, B/T = ρ.

- Main method: adaptive linear programming (ALP) that uses instantaneous remaining-budget ratio bτ/τ to relax the DP.

- Key insight: under ALP, budget consumption mimics sampling without replacement, causing bτ to concentrate around ρτ and enabling tight performance control.

- Main theoretical results: ALP achieves O(1) regret (non-boundary) vs oracle in unit-cost known-π setting; UCB-ALP attains O(JK log T) regret non-boundary and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.

- Extensions: empirical-π estimator (EALP) for unknown context distribution and a sketch for heterogeneous costs (and an exploration-first variant).

- Comparison to prior work: improves over general BwK/RCB O(√T) guarantees by exploiting finite-structured setting to get logarithmic regret with low per-round complexity.

- Strength: clear, principled algorithmic idea that sidesteps DP while nearly matching the oracle.

- Strength: elegant analysis coupling budget dynamics to hypergeometric-like concentration.

- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees with low computational cost, especially in unit-cost known-π settings.

- Strength: practical relevance for applications with budget constraints (clinical trials, crowdsourcing).

- Weakness: strong assumptions — known finite π (partially relaxed), fixed costs, unique best action per context, strict ordering across contexts.

- Weakness: boundary cases (ρ = qj) are not as rare in practice and cause O(√T) regret; need simple safeguards.

- Weakness: several crucial proofs and arguments deferred to supplement, weakening main-paper rigor (e.g., Lemma 2, LP–oracle gap, budget concentration under context-dependent spending).

- Weakness: unclear explicit bound on LP upper bound Û(T,B) − U*(T,B); needs isolation or direct analysis against U*.

- Weakness: unknown-π analysis relies on truncation; fully online π̂ concentration is not analyzed.

- Weakness: heterogeneous-cost extension is sketched only; no UCB analogue or detailed regret constants provided.

- Weakness: no experiments — small simulations vs Mixture Elimination/BwK baselines would strengthen claims and illustrate boundary behavior.

- Questions for authors: bound Û − U* for unit costs? Is bτ distribution provably hypergeometric or concentrated under ALP/UCB-ALP? Simple parameter-free fix for boundary cases? Can fully online π̂ concentrate without truncation? Can UCB-ALP extend to heterogeneous costs and what regret?

- Minor fixes: correct Eq. (4) piecewise case for pj(ρ), clarify uniqueness/tie assumptions, unify time notation t vs τ, fix missing references/typos.

- Overall assessment: high theoretical quality and originality but proofs need strengthening and the empirical/unknown-π/heterogeneous-cost aspects require completion.

- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on clarifying LP vs oracle gap, tightening budget-concentration proofs, and adding at least small experiments.
