- Summary: Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) combined with UCB for constrained contextual bandits, achieving O(log T) regret except for boundary cases, with rigorous analysis and clear exposition.  
- Relation to prior work: Clearly positions the paper within contextual, budget-constrained, and resourceful bandit literature and builds on LP relaxation with a novel adaptive approach.  
- Strength — Problem formulation: Tackles a practically relevant constrained contextual bandit problem with motivating applications.  
- Strength — Novel approach: Introduces ALP and adapts budget constraints, providing a practical oracle approximation.  
- Strength — Rigorous analysis: Provides detailed theoretical regret bounds, concentration results, and treatment of boundary/estimation errors.  
- Strength — Clear presentation: Well-organized and accessible writing.  
- Strength — Computational efficiency: Algorithms are computationally efficient for practical use.  
- Strength — Generalization: Extends to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs (additional details in supplement).  
- Weakness — Assumptions: Relies on simplifying assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs) that limit direct applicability and need further discussion.  
- Weakness — Boundary-case regret: Suffers O(√T) regret in boundary cases, limiting performance in specific scenarios.  
- Weakness — Empirical evaluation: Lacks extensive simulations or comparisons to baseline algorithms to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Weakness — Complexity of analysis: Theoretical analysis is dense and could use more intuitive explanations for key steps.  
- Pro acceptance arguments: Significant, well-motivated contribution with novel algorithmic design, strong theory, and clear presentation.  
- Con acceptance arguments: Simplifying assumptions, boundary-case regret, limited empirical validation, and potentially hard-to-follow analysis.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant contributions; limitations are acknowledged and do not outweigh merits.  
- Recommendation & required revisions: Accept with minor revisions — add empirical evaluations, expand discussion of assumptions and extensions, and provide more intuitive explanations in the analysis.
