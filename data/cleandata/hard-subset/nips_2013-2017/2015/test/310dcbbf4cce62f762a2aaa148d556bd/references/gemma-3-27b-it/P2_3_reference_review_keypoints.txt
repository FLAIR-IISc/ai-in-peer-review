- Tackles contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, a realistic and challenging setting.  
- Proposes ALP (Adaptive Linear Programming) and UCB-ALP algorithms using LP relaxation to approximate the oracle.  
- Proves logarithmic regret under mild assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget), with O(√T) in boundary cases.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous and carefully handles boundary cases.  
- ALP is computationally efficient and well-motivated.  
- Novelty: achieves logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits, extending prior constrained-bandit work to the contextual setting.  
- Clear exposition and logical paper structure from oracle approximation to UCB-ALP.  
- Assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs) limit applicability to some real-world scenarios.  
- O(√T) regret in boundary cases is a notable limitation needing further study.  
- Empirical evaluation is lacking; simulations comparing UCB-ALP to recent baselines are recommended.  
- Discussion of related work (especially [21,22]) is somewhat dismissive and should be more nuanced.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to strong technical contribution and practical relevance.  
- Suggested revisions: expand related-work comparison, add simulations, and further characterize/mitigate boundary cases.
