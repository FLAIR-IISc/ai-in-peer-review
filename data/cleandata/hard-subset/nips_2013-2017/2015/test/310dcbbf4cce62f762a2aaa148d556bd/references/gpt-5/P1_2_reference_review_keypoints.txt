- Problem and approach: Studies contextual bandits with a single budget B over horizon T (i.i.d. discrete contexts, bounded rewards, fixed costs) and introduces an adaptive LP (ALP) that replaces the hard budget by the average remaining budget bτ/τ.

- ALP guarantees (known rewards): Achieves O(1) regret relative to the oracle in non-boundary cases and O(√T) regret at boundary cases (ρ = qj).

- UCB-ALP (unknown rewards): Combines ALP with UCB to get O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.

- Extensions: Sketches empirical ALP for unknown context distributions (with truncation) and a heterogeneous-cost LP over context-action pairs, preserving O(1)/O(√T) in known-parameter settings.

- Comparison to prior work: Shows that for finite discrete contexts and fixed costs, adaptive average-budget relaxation can yield logarithmic regret vs the √T rates in more general BwK/RCB settings.

- Conceptual novelty: Clean adaptive LP relaxation and hypergeometric “sampling without replacement” analysis for budget evolution.

- Theoretical contributions: Sharp regret bounds and a reusable decomposition into action-ranking and context-ranking regret components.

- Practicality: ALP requires only ordering of per-context best rewards, tolerates moderate estimation errors, and unit-cost case has a simple threshold structure.

- Positioning: Clearly differentiates from RCB/BwK and concave-knapsack work and highlights the adaptive-budget insight.

- Boundary-case concern: ρ = qj is measure-zero but can occur in practice (e.g., integral budgets), causing √T regret; mitigation (bias/dithering) is not provided.

- Experimental gap: Simulations are referenced but not shown; need empirical validation of boundary vs non-boundary scaling, robustness to misspecified πj, and heterogeneous-cost scenarios.

- Restrictive assumptions: Reliance on i.i.d. contexts, fixed known costs, finite discrete sets, and known πj limits applicability; unknown-π treatment needs truncation.

- Computational complexity: Per-round complexity not explicitly bounded; heterogeneous-cost LP over JK variables may be costly and lacks incremental/greedy alternatives.

- Missing lower bounds: No formal instance-dependent lower bound under budget constraints to confirm optimality of O(JK log T).

- Minor issues: Typos and unclear notation (e.g., garbled algorithm lines, ambiguous constants) need fixing and clearer statement of constants in theorems.

- Quality assessment: Analysis appears technically sound using appropriate tools (hypergeometric concentration, UCB bounds), with full proofs deferred to the supplement.

- Clarity assessment: Well-written and structured overall, but would benefit from clearer notation, boundary-case constants, a small running example, and a per-round complexity discussion.

- Originality assessment: Novel adaptive LP relaxation and mapping to sampling-without-replacement producing improved regret rates in this setting.

- Significance assessment: Advances constrained contextual bandits for finite discrete settings; potential broader impact if scalability and robustness are demonstrated.

- Suggested improvements: Add comprehensive experiments, discuss computational optimizations for per-round LP, explore boundary smoothing/randomization, fix typos/notation, and consider alternative exploration methods like Thompson sampling.

- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theoretical novelty, elegant analysis, and interpretable algorithmic design with improved regret rates.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited experimental validation, restrictive assumptions, and unresolved boundary-case degradation.

- Overall verdict: Solid theoretical contribution that would be stronger with empirical support and minor clarifications before being a top-tier conference paper.
