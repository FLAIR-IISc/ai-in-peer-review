- Paper proposes an adaptive LP (ALP) using average remaining budget bτ/τ for contextual bandits with hard budget B and horizon T, terminating on budget/time exhaustion.  
- For known statistics and unit costs, ALP achieves O(1) regret off a measure-zero set of boundary ρ=B/T values, and O(√T) regret at boundary parameters.  
- Authors combine ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) for unknown rewards, proving O(JK log T) regret off-boundary and O(√T + JK log T) at boundary (J contexts, K actions).  
- Extensions to unknown context distribution (empirical estimates with truncation/coupling) and heterogeneous costs are sketched but not fully developed.  
- Technical approach is sound: LP relaxation and adaptive bτ/τ are natural, and mapping budget evolution to sampling without replacement with hypergeometric concentration is elegant.  
- Regret decomposition for UCB-ALP into action-ranking and context-threshold errors is reasonable and UCB tail bounds are appropriately used.  
- Rigor issues: many details deferred to supplement, some main-text statements are terse or contain typos (e.g., Eq. (4), Theorem 1) that obscure exact bounds.  
- Key claims (e.g., budget concentration under UCB-ALP) are asserted but not fully formalized in the main text.  
- Extensions (unknown π, heterogeneous costs) are mainly sketches; precise regret statements and analyses are missing from the body.  
- No empirical results in the main paper; small simulations would help illustrate robustness and constants.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, with helpful intuition for ALP needing only rankings and bτ behavior.  
- Suggested clarity fixes: correct typos/incomplete equations, explicitly state assumptions (unique best actions, strict orderings, iid contexts), and address ties.  
- For UCB-ALP the reviewer requests a formal lemma on budget consumption concentration under learning.  
- Recommend summarizing at least one empirical figure/table or concrete qualitative observations in the main text.  
- Originality: adaptive LP using bτ/τ and proof of O(1) off-boundary appear novel in this setting; achieving O(log T) in finite-context unit-cost case improves on prior √T results.  
- Conceptual contribution: ALP only needs reward rankings, enabling tight coupling with UCB.  
- Significance: advances BwK family by showing log-type regret in finite-context settings; algorithms are computationally simple and implementable.  
- Limitations: strong assumptions (known π for main results, unit costs, single resource, unique gaps), boundary cases with √T regret, and immature extensions to more general settings.  
- Pros: simple algorithms with strong theoretical guarantees, elegant hypergeometric-based analysis, good positioning relative to RCB/BwK/convex-knapsack literature.  
- Cons: reliance on simplified assumptions, no lower bound shown for boundary √T necessity, presentation issues and dependence on supplement, lack of main-text empirical validation.  
- Reviewer questions: can authors give a lower bound for boundary √T or remove boundary effect via smoothing; sensitivity to small reward gaps and instance-dependent constants; formalize budget-consumption concentration under UCB-ALP; can online π̂ be used without truncation while preserving concentration?  
- Overall recommendation: accept (novel theoretical advance); reviewer confidence: high on novelty/main results, medium on extensions.  
- Scores: Quality 7/10; Clarity 6/10; Originality 8/10; Significance 7/10.
