- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with time and budget constraints; proposes ALP (known statistics) and UCB-ALP (unknown statistics) achieving O(log T) regret for non-boundary cases and O(√T) for boundary cases.
- Strong technical quality with rigorous proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) and novel concentration arguments for adaptive algorithms.
- Advances prior work by reducing regret from O(√T) (e.g., [17]) to O(log T) in many cases and clearly distinguishes from recent related extensions.
- Exceptionally clear, well-organized writing with consistent notation, helpful proof sketches, and adequate supplementary material for reproducibility.
- Original methodological contribution: ALP approximation applied to constrained contextual bandits and the novel UCB-ALP combination for adaptive budget constraints.
- High practical and theoretical significance: relevant to real-world constrained decision problems and computationally efficient.
- Strengths: novel theory, clear non-boundary vs. boundary distinction, practical algorithmic efficiency, strong presentation, and solid comparisons to prior work.
- Weaknesses: simplifying assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs) limit generality; boundary-case regret explanation could be deeper; heterogeneous-cost analysis is less complete.
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, original, and significant; minor limitations do not outweigh contributions.
