- Problem studied: stochastic contextual bandits with a hard single-knapsack budget, finite horizon, i.i.d. finite contexts, fixed known action costs (initially unit cost).

- Main idea: relax hard budget to average budget and solve a per-round LP; Adaptive LP (ALP) replaces ρ=B/T by instantaneous bτ/τ, yielding a simple thresholding policy.

- ALP property: in unit-cost systems ALP depends only on ordering of per-context best rewards and is computationally light.

- Known-statistics guarantee: ALP achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) on the boundary.

- Unknown rewards: UCB-ALP pairs UCB estimation with ALP, giving O(JK log T) non-boundary and O(√T + JK log T) on the boundary.

- Unknown context distribution: empirical EALP/UCB-EALP procedures (with a truncated variant) are proposed and sketched to retain O(1) regret in non-boundary cases.

- Heterogeneous costs: an ε-First ALP extension is sketched, claiming similar regret rates.

- Relation to prior work: improves on BwK/RCB √T bounds under stronger assumptions; differs from RL log-regret Markov results by treating i.i.d. contexts; leverages adaptivity to beat √T in this specialized setting.

- Strength — conceptual: ALP is simple, insightful, and only requires ordering of context utilities.

- Strength — theoretical: achieves strong guarantees (constant or logarithmic regret) and clearly identifies/analyzes the boundary phenomenon.

- Strength — analysis: maps budget evolution to sampling without replacement (hypergeometric concentration) and cleanly decomposes regret into action-ranking and threshold errors.

- Strength — practicality: per-round decisions reduce to thresholding with simple randomization and low complexity O(J + observed actions).

- Weakness — assumptions: results rely on restrictive conditions (finite i.i.d. contexts, known horizon/budget, fixed costs, uniqueness/strict ordering, and known π).

- Weakness/question — ties: need clarification which proofs require strict ordering and how ties can be handled without altering rates.

- Weakness/question — boundary lower bound: no matching Ω(√T) lower bound is provided; establishing one or empirical evidence is requested.

- Weakness/question — J,K dependence: RUCB-ALP = O(JK log T) may be pessimistic; ask for instance-dependent gap-based bounds (e.g., sum Δ−2 log T).

- Weakness/question — truncated EALP: analysis is only sketched; need explicit truncation window vs T and dependence on min πj, and behavior when some πj are small.

- Weakness/question — robustness: sensitivity to mild nonstationarity or random costs is not analyzed; discussion of tracking/sliding-window approaches would help.

- Weakness/question — heterogeneous-costs detail: extension lacks algorithmic specifics, per-round complexity, and learning-regret guarantees.

- Weakness — experiments: no empirical results in main text to assess constants, boundary behavior, or compare to BwK/RCB baselines.

- Quality: technical core is sound and novel; some proofs deferred and main text would benefit from more intuition on concentration under UCB-ALP.

- Clarity: generally clear and well organized; fix minor typesetting/notation issues and restate tie-handling, removing strict ordering from main statements if only for exposition.

- Originality: adaptive LP and the resulting O(1)/O(log T) guarantees in this constrained contextual setting are novel.

- Significance: advances constrained contextual bandits under practical simplifications and introduces techniques likely useful more broadly.

- Suggestions: add instance-dependent gap bounds, provide boundary lower bound or empirical evidence, expand heterogeneous-costs algorithmic detail and complexity, include experiments versus baselines, and clarify tie-handling.

- Recommendation: Accept (7/10).
