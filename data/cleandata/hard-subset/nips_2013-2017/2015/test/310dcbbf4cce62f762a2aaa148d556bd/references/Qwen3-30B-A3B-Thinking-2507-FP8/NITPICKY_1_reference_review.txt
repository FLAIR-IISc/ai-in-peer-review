

# Review of "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

## Summary
This paper addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, proposing an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm and its UCB-based extension (UCB-ALP). The authors claim that ALP achieves O(1) regret and UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret (except for boundary cases), representing a significant improvement over prior work that achieved O(√T) regret. The paper builds on the Resourceful Contextual Bandits framework [17] but makes simplifying assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget constraint) to enable efficient algorithms.

## Major Weaknesses

### 1. Fundamental Flaw in Regret Bound Claim (Critical Issue)
The paper's most significant flaw is a fundamental mathematical error in Theorem 1, which claims ALP achieves O(1) regret for non-boundary cases. The stated bound is:
"RALP(T,B) ≤ u^*_1 - u^*_J - e^{-2δ^2}"

This is mathematically invalid because:
- Regret RALP(T,B) = U*(T,B) - UALP(T,B) must be non-negative by definition
- The right-hand side (u^*_1 - u^*_J - e^{-2δ^2}) can be negative (e.g., when u^*_1 - u^*_J < e^{-2δ^2})
- A bound that can be negative for the regret is meaningless and contradicts the definition of regret

This is a critical flaw that undermines the paper's main claim. The authors appear to have confused the regret bound with some other quantity. For the bound to be valid, it should be RALP(T,B) ≤ u^*_1 - u^*_J + e^{-2δ^2} or similar, but as stated, it's mathematically incorrect.

### 2. Inconsistent Regret Bound Claims
The paper repeatedly claims "logarithmic regret" but Theorem 2 states "RUCB-ALP(T,B) = O(JK log T)". This is a significant discrepancy:
- The paper claims "O(log T) regret" but the actual bound is O(JK log T), which is logarithmic in T but depends on problem dimensions J and K
- The authors should clarify that the regret is logarithmic in T but depends on the problem size (J and K), rather than claiming "logarithmic regret" without qualification

### 3. Inconsistent Mathematical Notation
The paper inconsistently uses:
- "u ∗ j" (with space between u and *) versus "u^*_j"
- "O( √ T )" (with space before the square root) versus "O(√T)"

This inconsistency appears throughout the paper and significantly hinders readability and understanding of the technical content.

### 4. Inadequate Comparison with Related Work
The paper states "to the best of our knowledge, this is the first work that shows how to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits" but fails to adequately distinguish its approach from recent work [20, 21, 22]. The paper mentions these works but doesn't clearly explain why they don't achieve logarithmic regret in the random context case, which is critical for establishing novelty.

## Minor Weaknesses

### 1. Ambiguous Terminology
The paper uses "unit-cost systems" without clear definition. It's unclear whether costs are normalized to 1 or if all costs are exactly 1, which affects the interpretation of the model.

### 2. Unclear Justification for Simplifications
While the paper justifies its simplifications (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget constraint), it doesn't sufficiently explain why these are reasonable for the applications cited (clinical trials, wireless networks) or how they might limit the applicability of the results.

### 3. Formatting Inconsistencies
- Inconsistent spacing in mathematical expressions (e.g., "O( √ T )" vs "O(√T)")
- Inconsistent use of "context" vs "contexts"
- Unclear explanation of why "dummy action" At=0 is needed

### 4. Overstated Claims
The paper claims "ALP achieves O(1) regret" but Theorem 1 shows a bound that depends on δ (which depends on ρ), meaning the bound isn't truly constant. This should be clarified as "constant with respect to T" rather than simply "O(1)".

## Strengths

### 1. Clear Problem Formulation
The paper provides a well-structured problem formulation of constrained contextual bandits, clearly defining the objective, constraints, and regret metric.

### 2. Insightful Algorithm Design
The ALP approach to approximating the oracle with linear programming is conceptually sound and represents a reasonable approach to the problem.

### 3. Good Experimental Motivation
The paper provides good motivation for constrained contextual bandits through practical applications (clinical trials, crowdsourcing).

## Verdict

The paper presents a theoretically interesting approach to constrained contextual bandits, but the fundamental mathematical error in Theorem 1 (the invalid regret bound) is a critical flaw that undermines the paper's main contribution. The inconsistent regret bound claims (O(log T) vs O(JK log T)) and inconsistent mathematical notation further weaken the paper.

While the problem is significant and the approach is potentially valuable, these technical errors make the paper unsuitable for NIPS. The authors should correct the fundamental mathematical error and clarify the regret bound claims before the paper can be considered for publication.

I recommend rejection due to the fundamental mathematical flaw in the main theoretical result.
