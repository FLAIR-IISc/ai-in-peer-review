- Paper addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, using LP relaxation and adaptive budgeting (ALP) with UCB (UCB-ALP).
- Achieves logarithmic regret (except boundary cases) under finite contexts and fixed costs.
- Problem formulation is relevant and practically motivated (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing).
- Technical innovation: LP approximation + adaptive budget (ALP) is insightful and tractable.
- UCB-ALP is a natural, effective extension for unknown rewards.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and regret guarantees with careful proof sketches.
- Clear and well-organized presentation with mostly clear notation and intuition.
- Thorough literature review connecting to contextual, constrained bandits and resource allocation.
- Simplifying assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs) limit applicability to complex real-world settings.
- Logarithmic regret depends on avoiding “boundary cases,” whose practical impact is not fully explored.
- Analysis is complex and the paper’s proof sketches lack full detail, making verification difficult without supplements.
- No empirical evaluation; experiments would strengthen claims and illuminate boundary-case behavior.
- Comparison to recent related work ([21,22]) is brief and could be more nuanced.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution deserving acceptance, but needs experiments and better discussion of assumptions/boundary cases.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem, provable logarithmic regret, strong analysis, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, conditional guarantees, lack of empirical validation, insufficient comparison to recent work.
- Reviewer recommendation: Strong Accept (7/10), contingent on adding experiments and clarifying boundary-case handling.
