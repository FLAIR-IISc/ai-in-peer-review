- Studies contextual bandits with hard budget and finite horizon; introduces LP relaxation and Adaptive LP (ALP) policy.
- ALP solves LP each round using average remaining budget bτ/τ and induces a “sampling without replacement” budget evolution.
- For unit-cost, known context distribution, ALP needs only ordering of context-wise optimal rewards and can combine with UCB.
- ALP achieves O(1) regret to the oracle in non-boundary cases (ρ ≠ cumulative qj) and O(√T) regret in boundary cases.
- UCB-ALP (unknown rewards) plugs UCB estimates into ALP and gets O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases, O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.
- Handles unknown context distributions via a truncated estimator and concentration arguments (details deferred to supplement).
- Extends to heterogeneous costs with a generalized ALP and an ε-first variant (some analysis sketched).
- LP upper bound Û(T,B)=Tv(ρ) is a clean benchmark; hypergeometric concentration of budget is a key technical insight.
- Regret decomposition separates within-context action-ranking errors (O(log T)) and context-ranking/budget-fluctuation errors (O(JK log T)).
- Assumptions are clear: finite J,K; iid contexts; bounded rewards; fixed costs; strict ordering u*1>…>u*J used for exposition.
- Paper is technically sound with plausible analyses, but many proofs and key theorem details are relegated to appendices.
- Concern: reliance on non-boundary condition—boundary cases occur in discrete settings and yield O(√T) regret.
- Suggestion: discuss tie-breaking/smoothing/dithering to avoid boundary regimes or improve the √T term.
- Mild concern: use of a smaller UCB exploration coefficient—main text should justify Lemma 3 and constants more explicitly.
- Practical limitations: heavy dependence on finite discrete contexts/actions, JK exploration factor may be large, and no experiments in main text.
- Clarity issues: some equations/notation (e.g., Eq. (4)) are hard to parse and several typos; main-text statements for truncated EALP/heterogeneous-cost results would help.
- Originality/significance: first to show log regret in constrained contextual bandits under these finite, fixed-cost assumptions; algorithm is simple and computationally efficient.
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
