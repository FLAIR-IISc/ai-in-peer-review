- Problem: Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget/time limits and observable contexts.  
- Algorithms: Proposes ALP (known statistics) and UCB-ALP (unknown statistics).  
- Main theoretical result: Achieves O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- ALP performance: ALP gives O(1) (near-optimal) regret in non-boundary cases by adapting budget constraints over time.  
- UCB-ALP performance: UCB-ALP combines UCB with ALP to learn ordering and attains O(log T) regret.  
- Key insight: Uses LP relaxation to tightly approximate the oracle (DP) solution for regret analysis.  
- Novel techniques: Develops concentration analysis for remaining budget under adaptive policies.  
- Clarity and formulation: Problem is clearly defined with justified simplifying assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs).  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on and significantly improves prior constrained/RCB frameworks and recent extensions.  
- Novel insight: Illuminates relationship between LP relaxation and dynamic programming for constrained bandits.  
- Weakness — experiments: No empirical validation provided to support practical performance claims.  
- Weakness — boundary cases: Treatment of boundary cases (where regret becomes O(√T)) is brief and lacks deeper intuition.  
- Weakness — complexity: Computational complexity of solving LPs and practical efficiency analysis is insufficiently detailed.  
- Weakness — DP connection: The connection between the LP approximation and the true DP oracle could be more thoroughly explained.  
- Overall assessment: Technically strong, original, and significant theoretical contribution; recommendation to accept despite missing empirical validation.
