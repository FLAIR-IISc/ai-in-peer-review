- Tackles constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits, relevant to real-world applications (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing).  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the optimal policy under budget constraints.  
- Combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to handle unknown rewards.  
- Achieves logarithmic regret in most cases, with O(√T) regret only in boundary cases.  
- Assumes finite contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget—simplifying but practical in some settings.  
- Provides rigorous analysis using novel techniques (bounding estimation errors under random contexts, concentration under adaptive algorithms).  
- Clearly situates work within existing literature and highlights novelty over prior approaches.  
- Extends framework to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs (mainly in supplementary material).  
- Strength: addresses an important, challenging problem with significant theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: ALP is a novel, effective method for handling budget constraints in contextual bandits.  
- Weakness: simplifying assumptions limit immediate applicability to more complex/continuous settings.  
- Weakness: boundary-case O(√T) regret lacks intuitive explanation and raises practical concerns.  
- Weakness: analysis is dense and can be hard to follow; presentation could be clearer.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical validation—no simulations or real-world experiments shown.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and significant; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Reviewer recommendations: provide intuitive explanation of boundary cases, include simulation results, and simplify presentation of analytical results.
