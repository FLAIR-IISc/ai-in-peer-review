- Summary: Tackles contextual bandits with budget/time constraints and proposes ALP + UCB-ALP algorithms achieving logarithmic regret under finite contexts and fixed costs; clear motivation and strong theoretical analysis.  
- Relation to previous work: Builds on budget-constrained and contextual bandit literature, offers a simpler/efficient alternative to Resourceful Contextual Bandits and obtains logarithmic vs. √T regret compared to recent dynamic-context results.  
- Novelty: First significant advance achieving logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under practical assumptions.  
- Technical soundness: Rigorous analysis using LP relaxation and ALP, with a natural UCB extension for unknown rewards.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written, organized, and mathematically clear with good intuition.  
- Practicality: Assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs) are reasonable for many real applications.  
- Detailed analysis: Thorough treatment of known/unknown statistics, boundary cases, and extensions (heterogeneous costs in supplement).  
- Computational efficiency: Algorithms appear tractable and implementable.  
- Simplifying assumptions: Fixed costs and finite contexts limit generality and need discussion of sensitivity to violations.  
- Boundary cases: Some cases incur √T regret, which is a notable limitation requiring mitigation strategies.  
- Empirical validation: Lacks experiments on synthetic or real datasets to support theoretical claims.  
- Connection to [17]: Relationship to Resourceful Contextual Bandits needs clearer exposition of trade-offs and possible integrations.  
- Unknown context distribution: Generalization discussion is brief and deferred to supplement; main paper should provide more detail.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an open problem with rigorous theory, practical algorithms, clarity, and likely impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited generality from assumptions, missing empirical results, and √T regret in boundary cases.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation — discuss assumptions: Add sensitivity analysis and potential avenues for generalization.  
- Recommendation — empirical validation: Include experiments on synthetic and/or real datasets.  
- Recommendation — mitigate boundary regret: Explore adaptive tuning or alternative exploration to reduce √T cases.  
- Recommendation — strengthen connections: Provide deeper comparison and trade-offs with [17].  
- Recommendation — expand unknown-context discussion: Move a clearer high-level treatment into the main paper.
