- Tackles contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, achieving logarithmic regret via LP approximation and adaptive budget (ALP), extended to unknown rewards with UCB-ALP.  
- Correctly situates work relative to prior literature, improving on [17]’s √T regret and distinguishing from resource-constrained bandit papers.  
- Novelty/significance: first to achieve logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual setting, addressing a practically important problem.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous analysis supporting regret bounds; LP relaxation and adaptive budget are well-motivated and proven.  
- Clarity and organization: well-written progression from known to unknown statistics with clear definitions and notation.  
- Practicality: algorithms are computationally tractable compared to Mixture Elimination; finite contexts and fixed costs make results immediately applicable in some settings.  
- Thoroughness: addresses boundary cases, generalizes to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, with detailed proofs in the supplement.  
- Key assumption limitation: finite discrete contexts and fixed costs restrict immediate applicability.  
- Boundary-case weakness: some boundary scenarios still incur √T regret; practical impact and frequency are unclear.  
- Supplement reliance: heavy use of supplementary material makes it harder to quickly assess full rigor.  
- Empirical gap: lacks experiments or simulations to validate UCB-ALP and demonstrate boundary-case prevalence.  
- Comparison gap: brief treatment of recent work [21,22]; needs deeper comparison on why log T result is a significant improvement over √T approaches.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem with strong theoretical results, significant log-regret improvement, and relatively simple practical algorithms.  
- Against acceptance: simplifying assumptions, √T boundary cases, and absence of empirical validation are concerns.  
- Overall: strong paper; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommend expanding discussion/intuition on boundary cases and estimating their real-world prevalence.  
- Recommend adding empirical validation or simulations to demonstrate UCB-ALP performance and boundary effects.  
- Recommend a detailed comparison to [21,22] covering regret bounds, computational complexity, and assumptions.  
- Recommend improving organization and clarity of the supplementary material and moving key insights into the main paper.
