- Tackles contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, a realistic and understudied problem.  
- Proposes LP relaxation to approximate the optimal policy and derives an adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm.  
- Extends ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to handle unknown rewards.  
- Achieves logarithmic regret in most cases, with O(√T) regret in boundary cases.  
- Strong technical novelty in using LP relaxation and adaptive budget adjustment.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis addressing interplay of exploration, exploitation, and budget limits.  
- Careful handling and decomposition of regret, with concentration and estimation-error bounds.  
- Solid connections and positioning relative to existing literature on bandits and resource allocation.  
- Discussion of extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs shows broader applicability.  
- Limitation: assumption of fixed costs and a single budget constraint reduces generality.  
- Boundary cases yielding O(√T) regret are analyzed but their practical prevalence is unclear.  
- Presentation is dense and technical; more intuitive explanations would improve accessibility.  
- Lacks empirical evaluation to demonstrate practical performance on simulated or real environments.  
- Overall quality: very good technical contributions with significant theoretical results.  
- Originality: strong—novel combination of LP relaxation, adaptive budgeting, and UCB.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested additions of empirical results and clearer discussion of boundary cases.
