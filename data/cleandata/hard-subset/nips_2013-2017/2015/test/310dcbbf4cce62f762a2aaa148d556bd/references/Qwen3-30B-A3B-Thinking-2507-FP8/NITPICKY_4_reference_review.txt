

# Review: "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

This paper studies constrained contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, proposing an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that achieves O(1) regret (except for boundary cases) when system statistics are known, and a UCB-ALP algorithm that achieves O(log T) regret (except for boundary cases) when expected rewards are unknown. The authors claim this is the first work to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits, contrasting with previous O(√T) regret results for related problems.

**Strengths:**
1. The problem is well-motivated with practical applications in clinical trials and crowdsourcing.
2. The authors make a clear distinction between their work and related literature, properly citing [17, 20, 21, 22] and highlighting the novelty of their approach.
3. The ALP approximation provides a natural benchmark for regret analysis, which is a significant technical contribution.
4. The paper provides a clear algorithmic structure and a reasonable theoretical analysis framework.
5. The results are theoretically sound in the non-boundary cases, and the paper correctly identifies boundary cases as a limitation.

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Notation inconsistencies:** The paper contains multiple notational errors that undermine technical precision. In Equation (4), the expression "ρ− ∑j̃(ρ) j'=1 πj' πj̃(ρ)+1" is missing a closing parenthesis. In Equation (6), the notation "`j,k = 2 log T(uj',k'-uj,k)^2" uses an unconventional symbol (` instead of a standard notation), which creates confusion. The paper should use consistent and standard mathematical notation throughout.

2. **Incorrect technical claim in proof sketch:** In the sketch of Theorem 1's proof, the paper claims "E[v(bτ/τ)] = v(ρ) if the threshold j̃(bτ/τ) = j̃(ρ) for all possible bτ's." This is mathematically incorrect. Since v(·) is a piecewise linear function (as defined in Equation 5), E[v(bτ/τ)] would equal v(ρ) only if v(·) is linear over the relevant range, which is not guaranteed. The paper should correct this fundamental error in the proof sketch.

3. **Lack of concrete regret bounds:** While the paper claims O(log T) regret for non-boundary cases, it doesn't provide a concrete bound with an explicit constant factor (e.g., C·JK·log T). Without this, the practical significance of the logarithmic regret is unclear, as the constant factor could be prohibitively large.

4. **Ambiguous boundary cases:** The paper states "except for certain boundary cases" multiple times but never clearly defines what constitutes a boundary case. The paper should provide a precise mathematical characterization of these cases and explain why they're problematic for the algorithm.

5. **Inconsistent regret definition:** The paper claims "the regret is defined more naturally as the performance gap between the proposed algorithm and the oracle," but the oracle is never properly defined. The oracle should be explicitly defined as the optimal algorithm with known statistics, not just mentioned.

6. **Missing computational complexity analysis:** The paper claims UCB-ALP is "quite computationally efficient" but provides no analysis of its computational complexity, which is a significant omission for a paper claiming to address "computationally efficient algorithms."

7. **Inconsistent use of "bτ":** The paper uses "bτ" to denote both the remaining budget at time τ (in the main text) and a different variable (in Equation 5), causing confusion.

**Recommendation:**
While the paper makes a theoretically interesting contribution to constrained contextual bandits, the technical flaws in the proof sketch, notation inconsistencies, and lack of concrete regret bounds undermine the paper's scientific rigor. The fundamental error in the proof sketch of Theorem 1 is particularly concerning, as it suggests potential issues with the core technical contribution. The paper would benefit from a complete revision to correct these issues.

The authors' claim of "logarithmic regret" is not sufficiently supported by the current analysis, as the paper fails to provide a rigorous, error-free proof of this claim. Without this, the paper cannot make a strong case for being the first to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.

**Pro/Con Summary:**
- Pro: Well-motivated problem with practical relevance; clear distinction from prior work; novel ALP approximation technique.
- Con: Fundamental error in proof sketch; inconsistent notation; lack of concrete regret bounds; ambiguous boundary cases; missing computational complexity analysis.

**Decision: Reject**

The paper's technical flaws are too significant to overlook, especially given the paper's claim of a major theoretical contribution. The authors should address these issues before the paper can be considered for publication at NIPS.
