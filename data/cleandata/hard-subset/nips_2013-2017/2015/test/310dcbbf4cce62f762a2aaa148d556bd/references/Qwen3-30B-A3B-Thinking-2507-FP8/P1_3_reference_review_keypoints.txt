- Problem: Studies constrained contextual bandits with time horizon T and budget B where action costs must not exceed B; previous non-contextual budget results don't apply.  
- Main contributions: Proposes ALP (known statistics) achieving O(1) regret (O(√T) at boundaries) and UCB-ALP (unknown statistics) achieving O(JK log T) regret (boundary O(√T + JK log T)).  
- Novelty: Introduces ALP as a novel LP-based approach that decouples information acquisition and decision making, yielding logarithmic regret vs prior O(√T).  
- Technical quality: Rigorous theoretical analysis with complete proofs that account for context arrival, budget fluctuations, and estimation errors.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized with clear problem formulation, consistent notation, and adequate supplementary material for reproducibility.  
- Significance: Theoretical and practical impact — major improvement in regret bounds and computationally efficient algorithms applicable to real-world budgeted problems.  
- Limitations: Boundary cases with O(√T) regret unresolved; core analysis assumes unit costs and known context distribution; extensions placed in supplementary material.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-executed theoretical contribution appropriate for NIPS.  
- Pros: Addresses a key gap, achieves logarithmic regret, efficient algorithms, rigorous analysis, and proper literature positioning.  
- Cons: Unresolved boundary behavior, restrictive main assumptions, and limited integration of extensions into the main text.
