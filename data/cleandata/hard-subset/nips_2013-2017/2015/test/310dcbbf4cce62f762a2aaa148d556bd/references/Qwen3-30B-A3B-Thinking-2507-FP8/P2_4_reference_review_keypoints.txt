- Proposes UCB-ALP, combining Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) with UCB to handle constrained contextual bandits and achieve logarithmic regret in non-boundary cases.  
- Shows ALP yields O(1) regret with known statistics and UCB-ALP yields O(JK log T) regret for unknown statistics.  
- Key insight: ALP approximates the oracle and tolerates estimation errors, enabling effective integration with UCB.  
- Builds on prior contextual and constrained bandit work and distinguishes itself by achieving logarithmic regret under finite discrete contexts and fixed costs.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with clear theorems and detailed proofs, handling action/context ranking errors, budget fluctuations, and context randomness.  
- Authors acknowledge boundary cases where regret degrades to O(√T) and characterize when these occur.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with helpful intuition and logical separation between unit-cost and generalized settings.  
- Some technical sections (notably Section 4.3) could use more intuitive explanation; reproducible for experts but lacks empirical validation.  
- Novel contribution: first logarithmic regret bound for constrained contextual bandits via ALP approximation and UCB integration.  
- Significant practical relevance for applications with budget constraints and offers computationally efficient algorithms.  
- Main weaknesses: reliance on unit-cost assumption, no empirical validation, unresolved practical guidance for boundary cases, and unclear scalability to large context/action spaces.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; accept for publication despite limitations.
