- Studies budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions and proposes an LP-approximation algorithm achieving logarithmic regret.  
- Main contributions: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm and UCB-ALP (ALP + UCB) combination.  
- ALP achieves near-optimal performance with known system statistics and tolerates certain estimation errors.  
- UCB-ALP addresses unknown expected rewards and attains logarithmic regret except in boundary cases.  
- Provides thorough regret analyses for both ALP and UCB-ALP and insights on budget-constraint challenges.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and technically sound with clear presentation of technical details.  
- Novel and significant contribution: an efficient improved algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with near-optimal regret.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Suggestion: consider parametric reward models for scalability when contexts are numerous.  
- Suggestion: clarify the rationale for combining UCB with ALP, explicitly stating optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty and relation to UCRL2.  
- Suggestion: include numerical experiments in an extended version to show practicality.
