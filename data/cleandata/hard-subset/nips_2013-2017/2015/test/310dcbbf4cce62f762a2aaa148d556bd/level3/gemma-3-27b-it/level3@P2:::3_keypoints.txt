- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, complicating exploration-exploitation.
- Introduces Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for known context distributions and unit costs, approximating the oracle.
- Proposes UCB-ALP combining ALP with UCB to handle unknown rewards, achieving logarithmic regret except in boundary cases.
- Extends methods to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.
- First work to demonstrate logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits, a notable novelty.
- Technical approach is sound: LP approximation plus UCB is well-designed with thorough analysis.
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and well-organized.
- Results are significant and applicable to real-world problems (clinical trials, advertising, crowdsourcing).
- Thorough treatment covering multiple settings (known/unknown contexts, unit/heterogeneous costs).
- Limitation: logarithmic regret fails in certain boundary cases, degrading to O(√T).
- Restrictive assumption u*1 > u*2 > ... > u*J in the unknown context case may limit generality.
- More detailed analysis of computational complexity for solving the LPs (especially for large spaces) is needed.
- Strong arguments for acceptance based on novelty, rigor, clarity, and practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance focus on boundary-case guarantees, model assumption restrictions, and complexity analysis gaps.
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
