This paper proposes an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions, and provides a logarithmic regret bound. The problem is well-motivated by real-world applications, and the authors provide a thorough review of related work.

The main contribution of the paper is the design of an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that approximates the oracle algorithm with known statistics. The ALP algorithm achieves near-optimal performance and only requires the ordering of expected rewards. The authors then combine ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method to propose a UCB-ALP algorithm for the general case where expected rewards are unknown. The UCB-ALP algorithm achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases.

The paper is very well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem, the proposed algorithms, and the theoretical analysis. The organization of the paper is logical, and the notation is consistent throughout.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions.
* The provision of a logarithmic regret bound for the proposed algorithm.
* The thorough review of related work and the clear explanation of the problem and the proposed algorithms.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The assumption of discrete contexts and actions may not be realistic in all applications.
* The boundary cases where the regret is O(√T) may be significant in practice.
* The paper does not include numerical experiments to validate the theoretical results.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Consider parametric reward models for scalability when contexts are numerous.
* Clarify the rationale for combining UCB with ALP in Section 4.1, and explicitly state the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle and its relation to UCRL2 policy updates.
* Include numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper.
* Consider referencing arXiv:1506.03374, which appears to be relevant to the problem.

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of constrained contextual bandits, and the proposed algorithm is efficient and effective. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the theoretical analysis is thorough and convincing. The weaknesses of the paper are minor, and the suggestions for improvement are intended to strengthen the paper further.

Recommendation: The paper merits acceptance. It provides an efficient, improved algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with near-optimal regret, and the theoretical analysis is thorough and convincing. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the suggestions for improvement are minor.
