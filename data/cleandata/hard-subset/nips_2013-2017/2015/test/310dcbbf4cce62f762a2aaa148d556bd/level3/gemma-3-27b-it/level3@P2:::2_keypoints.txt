- Problem: studies contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, addressing a practically important setting.
- Main contribution: shows logarithmic regret is achievable in this constrained contextual bandit setting, a previously unknown result.
- Key idea: proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that relaxes hard budget constraints to adaptive average constraints for tractability.
- Algorithm: develops UCB-ALP by combining ALP with UCB to handle unknown rewards while relying on reward ordering.
- Theoretical strength: rigorous, convincing proofs with lemmas (e.g., concentration of remaining budget) and detailed analysis.
- Extensions: considers unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, but these sections are brief and less thorough.
- Assumptions: relies on finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint, limiting broader applicability.
- Clarity and writing: paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and has a strong related-work section with recent citations.
- Main weakness: complete absence of experimental results prevents assessment of practical performance and sensitivity.
- Analysis limitations: some results depend on truncated algorithms or assumed known context distributions.
- Boundary cases: Theorem 2 identifies boundary cases with O(√T) regret that need further investigation and mitigation strategies.
- Novelty caution: claim of novelty is likely but should be tempered given rapid developments and related √T results in the literature.
- Recommendation: overall strong theoretical contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance but urges addition of experiments or deeper practical discussion.
