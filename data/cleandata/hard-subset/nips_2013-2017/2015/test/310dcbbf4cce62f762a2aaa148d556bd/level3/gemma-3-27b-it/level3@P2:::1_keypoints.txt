- Tackles constrained contextual bandits with time and budget limits and proposes algorithms achieving logarithmic regret under certain assumptions.  
- Core approach: approximate the oracle policy using Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and combine with UCB for unknown rewards (UCB-ALP).  
- Well-motivated, clearly presented problem with reasonable extensions (unknown context distributions, heterogeneous costs).  
- First paper to claim logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual setting, which is a significant claim.  
- Authors correctly identify key challenges: context coupling over time and difficulty approximating the optimal policy.  
- Technical development and analysis are generally solid and thorough.  
- Originality concern: problem can be reframed as a time/budgeted bandit with J×K arms, making logarithmic regret less surprising.  
- Paper should better articulate why constrained contextual bandits are fundamentally different from the JK-arm formulation.  
- Regret claims depend on restrictive assumptions (finite contexts/actions, proportional growth of T and B); implications of relaxing them are not discussed.  
- Clarity issue: regret analysis (especially Theorem 2) is dense and needs more intuitive explanation linking UCB ordering events to regret.  
- Ambiguity in Theorem 2’s rate: the √T term’s inclusion is unclear—might be √(K T) or require stronger justification.  
- Provided regret bounds (supplement) appear pessimistic; a cleaner, arm-gap-based problem-dependent bound is needed.  
- Major weakness: absence of matching lower bounds (problem-dependent and problem-independent) to establish optimality.  
- Authors hint at a rework of problem-dependent bounds but need to present tighter, interpretable bounds and lower bounds (e.g., via Chernoff information).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel logarithmic regret result, solid motivation, clear algorithms, and reasonable ALP approximation.  
- Rejection arguments: questionable originality, lack of lower bounds, pessimistic/tightness concerns for regret bounds, unclear Theorem 2, and insufficient discussion of relaxed assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: weak reject pending a rebuttal that clarifies novelty, tightens/simplifies regret bounds, addresses Theorem 2, and provides preliminary lower-bound discussion.
