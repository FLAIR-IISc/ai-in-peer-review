- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits, aiming for logarithmic regret — a meaningful goal.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for known context distributions that approximates the oracle and is near-optimal.  
- Extends to unknown rewards with UCB-ALP, claiming logarithmic regret up to boundary case caveats.  
- Central idea: approximate the oracle via LP and adapt over time to handle context-induced coupling.  
- Analysis is mathematically rigorous, using concentration inequalities and event decomposition.  
- The claim that contexts decouple into independent bandit instances for large horizons lacks thorough justification; context arrival order effects aren’t fully addressed.  
- Regret upper bound presentation is opaque; should be restated as a clearer, problem-dependent bound based on arm gaps.  
- Matching lower bounds (both problem-dependent leveraging context structure and problem-independent) are missing and needed.  
- Novelty is unclear given similarity to time/budgeted multi-armed bandits with JK arms; authors must clarify fundamental differences and required new techniques.  
- Theorem 2’s √T term appears high and potentially unnecessary; a dependence like O(√(K T)) or tighter rates may be achievable.  
- Proof reliance on UCB ordering events may be suboptimal; alternative analyses (e.g., summing inverse arm gaps) could tighten bounds.  
- Current lower bounds do not adequately capture complexity from context structure — this is a notable weakness.  
- Overall: promising approach but neutrality maintained until decoupling justification, clearer and tighter bounds, stronger lower bounds, and clearer statement of novelty are provided.
