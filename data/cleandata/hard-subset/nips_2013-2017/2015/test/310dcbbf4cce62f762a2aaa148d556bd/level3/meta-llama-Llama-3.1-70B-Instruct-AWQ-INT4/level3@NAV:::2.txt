This paper studies the problem of constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints. The authors propose an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle, which achieves near-optimality and only requires the ordering of expected rewards. They then combine ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method to propose a UCB-ALP algorithm for the general case where the expected rewards are unknown a priori. The authors show that the proposed UCB-ALP algorithm achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear motivation for the problem and a thorough analysis of the proposed algorithms. The theoretical contributions are solid, and the proofs are convincing. The authors also provide a good discussion of the related work and the significance of their contributions.

However, the practical aspects of the paper are thinly treated, and no experimental results are provided. The authors only provide a brief discussion of the potential applications of their algorithms and the challenges of implementing them in practice.

Overall, I would rate this paper as a good contribution to the field of contextual bandits. The authors provide a novel solution to the problem of constrained contextual bandits and demonstrate its theoretical properties. However, more work is needed to demonstrate the practicality and effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.
