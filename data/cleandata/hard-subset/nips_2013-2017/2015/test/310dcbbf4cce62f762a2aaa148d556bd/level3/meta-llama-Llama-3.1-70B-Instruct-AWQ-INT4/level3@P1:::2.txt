This paper addresses the problem of contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, referred to as constrained contextual bandits. The authors propose an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle, which achieves near-optimal performance while tolerating certain estimation errors of system parameters. They also introduce a UCB-ALP algorithm that combines ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method to achieve O(log T) regret except for certain boundary cases, where it achieves O(√T) regret.

The paper is well-written, clear, and well-documented, with explicit motivation and relevant citations. The theoretical contributions are solid, and the proofs are convincing. However, the practical aspects of the algorithms are thinly treated, and no experimental results are provided. The authors acknowledge that the analysis is limited to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint, which is a simplification but practically reasonable.

The paper's main contributions are the introduction of the ALP approximation and the UCB-ALP algorithm, which achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The authors claim that this is the first work to obtain logarithmic regret for this problem, and the paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for further research in this area.
