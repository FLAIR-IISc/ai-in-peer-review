- Focus: Tackles budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions, extending classic bandit frameworks.  
- Methods: Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and UCB-ALP algorithms for efficient exploration-exploitation under budget/time constraints.  
- LP approximation: Uses LP approximations to simplify the oracle solution and guide policy design.  
- Regret guarantees: Achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases for ALP and O(log T) regret for UCB-ALP in most cases.  
- Boundary cases: Obtains O(√T) regret in boundary cases, acknowledged as a remaining challenge.  
- Technical soundness: Provides detailed theoretical analysis and novel theoretical guarantees for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and well-organized with clear motivation and explanations.  
- Originality: Novel combination of ALP and UCB, offering the first logarithmic regret bounds in this setting.  
- Practical significance: Results are computationally efficient and relevant for applications like recommendations, clinical trials, and crowdsourcing.  
- Missing experiments: Lacks numerical simulations to validate theory and demonstrate empirical performance.  
- Algorithm rationale: Needs clearer justification for combining UCB with ALP and connections to methods like UCRL2.  
- Scalability: Does not address extensions to large or continuous context spaces; parametric models suggested for future work.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing strong theoretical contributions and practical potential.
