- Tackles contextual bandits under both budget and time constraints.
- Introduces UCB-ALP algorithm that achieves logarithmic regret.
- Develops an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) framework to approximate the intractable oracle.
- ALP adapts to the remaining budget-to-time ratio, unlike fixed-budget baselines.
- Combines ALP with UCB-style exploration to handle unknown rewards.
- Demonstrates logarithmic regret in most scenarios; theoretical analysis appears sound.
- Primary strength: novel, significant result—first logarithmic regret bounds for this setting.
- ALP is an elegant, powerful analytical tool of independent interest and a strong adaptive baseline.
- Main weakness: weak exposition and lack of intuition for the boundary cases (ρ = q_j).
- Analysis shows degradation to O(√T) at those thresholds, but the paper doesn't explain why they are fundamentally harder.
- Recommendation: accept, with a strong suggestion to clarify the intuition behind the boundary-case analysis.
