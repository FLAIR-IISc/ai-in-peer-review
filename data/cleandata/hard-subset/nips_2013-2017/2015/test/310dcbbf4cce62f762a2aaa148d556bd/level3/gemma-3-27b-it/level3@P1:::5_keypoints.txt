- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with time and budget limits, a practically important and challenging extension.  
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm achieving logarithmic regret, claimed first to do so.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written with strong motivation and problem framing.  
- System model and assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget) are clearly stated and justified for tractability.  
- Core idea: approximate oracle with Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and combine with UCB for unknown rewards.  
- ALP shown near-optimal when statistics known; UCB-ALP leverages strengths of both methods.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, providing logarithmic regret bounds and addressing fluctuating budgets and contexts.  
- Extensions include handling unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, demonstrating flexibility.  
- Suggest exploring parametric reward models for scalability in high-dimensional context spaces.  
- Recommend clearer articulation of UCB+ALP rationale, explicitly linking to “optimism in the face of uncertainty” and UCRL2-style policy updates.  
- Minor weakness: no numerical experiments to validate practical performance.  
- Suggest adding reference to related work arXiv:1506.03374 addressing similar constrained bandit problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel log-regret result, significant practical relevance, rigorous theory, clarity, and original LP+UCB approach.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of empirical validation and limited generality due to modeling assumptions.  
- Overall: strong paper with significant contribution; merits acceptance despite minor limitations.
