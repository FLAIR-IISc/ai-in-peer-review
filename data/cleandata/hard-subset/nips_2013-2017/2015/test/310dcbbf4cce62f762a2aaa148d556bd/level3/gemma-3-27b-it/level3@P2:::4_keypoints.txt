- Tackles constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, achieving logarithmic regret previously unachieved.  
- Proposes ALP (adaptive LP) and UCB-ALP algorithms combining LP relaxation, adaptive budgeting, and UCB exploration.  
- Analysis appears rigorous, leveraging concentration inequalities and bounded differences.  
- Novelty: first to obtain logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual setting.  
- Computationally efficient and relevant to contextual bandits, RL, and resource allocation communities.  
- Positions work well in literature, justifies simplified model (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget) and relates to prior resourceful contextual bandits.  
- Thoroughness: addresses known/unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Strength: clear writing and organized presentation.  
- Weakness: boundary cases (ρ = qj) yield O(√T) regret; explanation for why these are inherently harder is insufficient.  
- Weakness: LP-based oracle benchmark feels artificial and may not reflect realistic agent behavior.  
- Weakness: lacks empirical evaluation—needs experiments versus baselines (e.g., budgeted LinUCB variants).  
- Weakness: heterogeneous-costs extension is brief and mostly in supplement; should be integrated into main text.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: provide intuitive explanation for boundary-case regret, add empirical results, and expand heterogeneous-costs discussion in the main paper.
