- Addresses constrained contextual bandits by introducing budget and time constraints into the contextual bandit framework.  
- Argues existing budget-constrained MAB solutions do not apply to contextual settings.  
- Main contributions: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and UCB-ALP algorithms.  
- Claims logarithmic regret for ALP/UCB-ALP under proportional scaling of budget and horizon, except boundary cases.  
- Introduces oracle analysis with ALP as an LP-based approximation benchmark.  
- Extends to unknown rewards via UCB-ALP with natural, well-justified methodology.  
- Theoretical analysis appears rigorous, using concentration and bounded averaged differences.  
- Carefully handles both unit-cost and heterogeneous-cost scenarios, showing generality.  
- Discusses extension to unknown context distributions using empirical estimates.  
- Strength: novel problem and innovative LP-based approach adapted to UCB.  
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees and detailed analysis.  
- Strength: practical relevance due to common budget/time constraints.  
- Strength: clarity and well-structured presentation.  
- Weakness: logarithmic regret fails at boundary cases (ρ = qj), with unsatisfying intuition and √T regret claimed.  
- Weakness: ALP benchmark may be stronger than reasonable baselines, making comparisons unclear.  
- Weakness: no empirical evaluation to validate practical performance, especially in boundary cases.  
- Weakness: lengthy supplementary proofs may hinder reviewer accessibility; key arguments could be highlighted in the main text.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify boundary-case intuition and, if possible, add preliminary empirical results.
