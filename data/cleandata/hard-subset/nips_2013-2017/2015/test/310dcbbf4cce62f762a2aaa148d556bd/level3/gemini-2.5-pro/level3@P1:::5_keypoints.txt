- Problem: studies constrained contextual bandits with time horizon T and budget B, where the budget creates temporal dependency making the optimal policy a complex DP.  
- Contribution: develops a computationally efficient algorithm that approximates the intractable oracle and achieves near-optimal regret.  
- ALP (known stats): solves an LP each step using remaining-budget/remaining-time ratio; achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) in boundary cases.  
- UCB-ALP (unknown rewards): plugs UCB estimates into ALP; because ALP relies on reward ordering, UCB learns efficiently and yields O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases.  
- Novelty: first logarithmic regret bound for this budget-constrained contextual bandit setting, improving on prior O(√T) results.  
- Extensions: discusses handling unknown context distributions and heterogeneous action costs.  
- Strength — significance: addresses a practical, important problem (budget constraints) with a major theoretical advance.  
- Strength — technical quality: elegant two-step design (ALP then UCB) with sound regret decomposition and novel bounding techniques.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written and logically organized, clearly presenting formulation, algorithms, and results.  
- Weakness: limited to finite discrete contexts/actions; lacks discussion on extensions to large or continuous feature spaces (e.g., parametric function approximators).  
- Weakness: Section 4.1 should more explicitly connect UCB-ALP to "optimism in the face of uncertainty" and related RL approaches.  
- Weakness: entirely theoretical with no empirical validation; simulations could clarify constants and boundary vs. non-boundary behavior.  
- Weakness: brief comparison to concurrent work (Agrawal et al.); a more detailed contrast in the main text is recommended.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — significant, novel, and technically solid contribution despite minor limitations.
