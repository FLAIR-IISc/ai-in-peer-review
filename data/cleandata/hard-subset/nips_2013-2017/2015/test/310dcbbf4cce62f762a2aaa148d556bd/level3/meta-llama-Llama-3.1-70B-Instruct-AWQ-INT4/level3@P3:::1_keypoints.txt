- Proposes ALP and UCB-ALP algorithms for constrained contextual bandits (budget/time) and claims near-optimality.  
- Main theoretical result: UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret except in certain boundary cases.  
- Algorithmic contribution: computationally efficient approach combining LP approximation with UCB for unknown rewards.  
- Unclear justification for treating context occurrences as coupled or why they cannot be treated as independent bandit instances.  
- Problem-dependent upper bound is expressed with a KJ factor; reviewer questions why it isn’t in terms of sums of inverse arm gaps.  
- Proof technique using UCB ordering events appears overly complicated; reviewer asks why a simpler proof isn’t possible.  
- Missing matching lower bounds in both problem-dependent and problem-independent settings.  
- Novelty questioned: result may be unsurprising given reduction to time/budgeted bandits with JK arms and action constraints.  
- Paper is theoretically sound and meets quality guidelines but lacks clarity in explanations and proofs.  
- Originality is unclear and needs stronger positioning relative to existing contextual bandit work.  
- Recommendation: clarify context-occurrence modeling, simplify/justify proof approach, and provide matching lower bounds.  
- Overall stance: neutral on acceptance — significant contribution but requires clarification and stronger novelty evidence.
