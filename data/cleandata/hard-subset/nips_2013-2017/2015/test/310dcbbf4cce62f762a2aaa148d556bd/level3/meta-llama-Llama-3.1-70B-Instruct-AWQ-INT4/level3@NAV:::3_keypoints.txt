- Proposes an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm for constrained contextual bandits that is near-optimal when system statistics are known.
- Extends ALP with an Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) method (UCB-ALP) to handle unknown expected rewards.
- ALP achieves O(1) regret in general, with O(√T) regret in certain boundary cases.
- UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret except in certain boundary cases.
- Novel use of linear programming to approximate the oracle algorithm for constrained contextual bandits.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and provides a thorough performance analysis.
- Provides a detailed comparison with existing work and highlights advantages of the approach.
- Relies heavily on the assumption of a known context distribution, which may limit practical applicability.
- The performance analysis is complex and may be difficult for non-experts to follow.
- Lacks a clear comparison with other state-of-the-art constrained contextual bandit algorithms.
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance (Rating: 9/10, Confidence: 8/10).
