- Paper studies contextual bandits with both time horizon T and budget B, where budget couples decisions over time.  
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm achieving O(log T) regret for unit costs with known context distribution.  
- ALP approximates the intractable dynamic program by solving an LP each step using remaining budget/time ratio and attains O(1) regret in most cases.  
- Combining ALP with UCB handles unknown rewards, yielding UCB-ALP and extending results to the bandit setting.  
- Paper discusses extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Significance: first work to prove logarithmic regret for a constrained contextual bandit, improving over prior O(sqrt(T)) results.  
- Technical quality: ALP + concentration analysis is elegant, and decomposition of regret sources is convincing.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and logically structured from oracle to ALP to UCB-ALP.  
- Limitation: O(log T) guarantee is conditional — “boundary” cases (specific budget-to-time ratios) incur O(sqrt(T)) regret.  
- Suggest formalizing hardness of computing the exact DP/oracle to justify the LP approximation.  
- Clarify treatment when context distribution is unknown, since ordering contexts by true rewards may not be directly learnable.  
- Ask about extending the framework to unknown or infinite horizon settings and adding intuition for Lemma 1’s LP upper-bound.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor caveats; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
