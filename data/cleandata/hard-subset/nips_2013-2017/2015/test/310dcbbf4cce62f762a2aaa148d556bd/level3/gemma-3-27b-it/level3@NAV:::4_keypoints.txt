- Tackles constrained contextual bandits and presents algorithms achieving logarithmic regret, advancing the field.
- Identifies limitations of prior work and motivates need for new approaches for constrained settings.
- Core idea: approximate the oracle via Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP), which underpins proposed algorithms.
- Proposes UCB-ALP, well-motivated and shown to achieve logarithmic regret under certain conditions.
- Paper progression is clear, moving from known-statistics cases to unknown rewards and context distributions.
- Regret analysis is careful, with particular attention to boundary cases and estimation errors.
- Brief extension to heterogeneous costs indicates potential for broader applicability.
- Clearly articulates connection to Resourceful Contextual Bandits [17] and positions contribution as more computationally tractable with stronger regret guarantees in a specific setting.
- Weakness: justification for treating ρ = B/T as a special “boundary case” and its worse regret is somewhat hand-wavy and needs more intuition.
- Weakness: lacks an explicit computational-complexity comparison to the stronger baseline in [17], which would strengthen claims of efficiency.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty (first to show logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits), sound theoretical analysis, practical significance, and creative ALP-based solution.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited explanation of boundary-case behavior and missing complexity comparison to prior adaptive baselines.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address boundary-case justification and provide complexity comparison.
