- Addresses a practically important problem (budgeted contextual bandits) relevant to advertising, trials, and recommendations.  
- Main contribution: algorithmic framework achieving O(log T) regret for finite contexts/actions — a substantial improvement over typical O(√T).  
- Key technical idea: approximate the dynamic-programming oracle with an Adaptive Linear Program (ALP) that requires only correct ordering of expected rewards.  
- UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB to handle unknown rewards and attains O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) otherwise; extensions handle unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Concern about novelty: reviewer questions whether the formulation is essentially a time-varying “bandits with knapsacks” or J*K-armed bandit and asks authors to clarify technical distinctions and why existing methods fail.  
- Regret bound form is coarse (O(JK log T)); reviewer requests a gap-dependent bound (e.g., Σ (log T / Δ²)) instead of scaling with all context-action pairs.  
- Lack of lower bounds: no matching lower bounds are provided to demonstrate optimality, especially capturing context–budget interaction beyond K-armed reductions.  
- Clarify dependence in Theorem 2’s boundary-case regret (O(√T+...)): reviewer asks whether J and K dependencies are hidden and requests explicit rates.  
- Overall: elegant ALP-based approach and strong positive results, but neutrality maintained pending clarifications on originality and tighter/more complete theoretical analysis.
