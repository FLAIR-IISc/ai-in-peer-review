- Problem & approach: Studies contextual bandits with finite budget B and horizon T (ρ = B/T), proposing an adaptive LP (ALP) that relaxes budget to an average constraint and UCB-ALP for unknown rewards.  
- Main theoretical results: ALP attains O(1) regret (non-boundary); UCB-ALP attains O(JK log T) regret, with an extra O(√T) term only in boundary cases ρ = qj.  
- Key technical tools: Models budget evolution as sampling without replacement and proves hypergeometric concentration for bτ.  
- Relation to RCB: RCB gives O(√T) for more general settings but with finite policy sets and higher complexity; this paper focuses on computationally light algorithms achieving O(log T) in most cases.  
- Relation to BwK and concave-knapsack work: Those achieve sublinear regret under budgets but do not yield logarithmic regret in this dynamic-context setting.  
- Benchmark choice: Uses a stronger adaptive LP benchmark (steering by bτ/τ), making comparisons conservative and meaningful.  
- Strength: Elegant, practical adaptive-LP needing only reward ordering and the sampling-without-replacement insight yielding clean concentration bounds.  
- Strength: Tight theoretical guarantees and clear decomposition of regret sources (action-ranking vs. context-ranking/budget fluctuation).  
- Extensions: Outlines unknown-context-distribution handling (EALP/UCB-EALP) and heterogeneous costs with principled arguments.  
- Weakness: Boundary-case behavior (ρ = qj) is unresolved—unclear if O(√T) is intrinsic or an artifact; mitigation or lower bound is missing.  
- Weakness: Main results depend on known context distribution π; truncation/coupling approach for unknown π lacks explicit guarantees and guidance.  
- Weakness: Practical complexity details are sparse—per-round LP cost, heterogeneous-cost LP size, and explicit complexity bounds (e.g., O(J log J)) should be stated.  
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation should compare to stronger baselines (BwK variants, static-budget UCB, Mixture Elimination) to demonstrate adaptivity benefits.  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized but should move key lemmas/figures into main text and clarify tie-handling and the per-round LP solution procedure.  
- Originality: Novel adaptive-LP approximation and sampling-without-replacement perspective; combining with UCB is simple and effective.  
- Significance: Achieving logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits with random contexts is a meaningful advance with likely broader impact.  
- Suggested improvements: Provide sharper intuition or a lower bound for boundary cases, a smoothed/robust policy variant, explicit truncation schedules for unknown π, discuss constant factors in O(JK log T), and add empirical baselines.  
- Quality & recommendation: Technically sound with convincing analysis; boundary-case gap and practical details need addressing — overall recommendation: lean accept.
