- Problem addressed: studies constrained contextual bandits with per-action costs under fixed budget B and time horizon T.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves O(log T) regret for unit-cost systems with known context distribution under mild conditions (improving on prior O(√T)).  
- Key method: introduces an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle policy that is near-optimal (O(1) regret) in non-boundary cases.  
- Practical estimation advantage: ALP requires only ordering of expected rewards, making it compatible with UCB-style estimators.  
- Algorithm: combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, achieving O(log T) regret for non-boundary cases and O(√T) for boundary cases.  
- Technical quality: rigorous analysis disentangling context ranking errors and budget fluctuations; key Lemma 1 (LP relaxation upper bound) validated after rebuttal.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, clear problem setup, intuitive presentation, and accessible proof sketches.  
- Originality: first to achieve O(log T) regret for constrained contextual bandits under mild conditions; novel use of adaptive LP to decouple exploration and exploitation.  
- Significance: strong theoretical and practical impact; computationally efficient algorithm applicable to real-world problems (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing).  
- Main weakness: O(log T) guarantee excludes boundary cases where ρ equals a cumulative context probability q_j, reverting to O(√T); stronger universal guarantees would be desirable.  
- Open question: whether computing the exact optimal action sequence is NP-hard remains unresolved.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — important contribution with solid theory, clarity, and practical relevance despite minor limitations.
