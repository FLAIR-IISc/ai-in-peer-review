- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, focusing on the unexplored logarithmic regret regime.  
- Main contribution: algorithms achieving O(log T) regret under practical assumptions, improving on prior O(√T) results.  
- Core method: approximate the oracle policy via linear programming (LP) relaxations to handle budget constraints.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that adapts to remaining resources.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP for unknown expected rewards by combining ALP with UCB.  
- Provides rigorous regret analysis for known and unknown reward settings, including boundary-case treatment.  
- Extends results to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Novelty: first to achieve logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual bandit model under reasonable assumptions.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and connects effectively to prior work.  
- Technical soundness: mathematical derivations are detailed and reviewer concerns (e.g., Lemma 1) were satisfactorily addressed.  
- Limitations: logarithmic regret holds only under a “mild condition”; boundary cases yield √T regret.  
- Limitation: assumption that expected rewards can be ordered in the unknown context distribution case may be restrictive.  
- Limitation: limited discussion of computational complexity for finding optimal offline action sequences.  
- Future direction: extending results to unknown horizons (T) suggested.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; paper is a substantial, practical, and theoretically solid contribution.
