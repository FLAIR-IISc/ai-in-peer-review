- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits, proposing Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and UCB-ALP for unknown rewards.  
- ALP achieves near-optimal performance when system statistics are known.  
- UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB to handle unknown expected rewards.  
- Main contribution: algorithms achieving logarithmic or sublinear regret for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Relates to prior work on contextual bandits, budget-limited MABs, and resourceful contextual bandits.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses consistent mathematical notation.  
- Provides detailed proofs and thorough performance analysis, including boundary-case discussion.  
- Strength: novel combination of linear programming with UCB to tackle constrained contextual bandits.  
- Weakness: comparison to a stronger, nonstandard baseline (adaptive LP skipping a fixed percent of rounds) may be unfair.  
- Weakness: boundary cases (ρ = q_j) are analyzed but lack clear intuition on why they are special.  
- Weakness: needs more context on how proposed algorithms relate to other works in the field.  
- Overall: significant contribution likely to impact the field; reviewer rating 8/10, confidence 9/10.
