- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with time and budget limits and proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the dynamic oracle.
- In unit-cost, finite discrete contexts with known context distribution, ALP achieves O(1) regret for non-boundary instances and O(√T) for boundary instances.
- UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB learning to attain O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.
- Extensions are sketched for unknown context distributions (empirical estimation/truncation) and heterogeneous known costs, with analogous guarantees.
- Novelty: first to show logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under finite contexts, fixed costs, and single budget using an adaptive LP relaxation.
- Strength: ALP is simple, computationally light, decouples learning from control, and provides a clean benchmark.
- Strength: Strong theoretical guarantees and elegant analysis using hypergeometric concentration for remaining budget.
- Strength: Paper is well-written, well-structured, and clearly separates cases and assumptions.
- Limitation: Results rely on restrictive assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget), excluding many practical scenarios.
- Limitation: Boundary instances degrade to O(√T); no matching lower bounds or detailed treatment of near-boundary behavior.
- Limitation: Practical validation is missing—no experiments to assess constants, robustness, or runtime.
- Limitation: Unknown context-distribution analysis is cursory and relies on truncation without quantifying practical overhead.
- Limitation: Heterogeneous-cost algorithm (-First ALP) may be suboptimal and lacks comparison to UCB/Thompson-style methods.
- Quality: Technical development is solid and convincing, though proofs were not fully checked line-by-line by the reviewer.
- Clarity suggestions: add pseudocode for variants, a running example, and clarify per-round computational complexity.
- Questions for authors include sensitivity to near-boundary ρ, handling heavy-tailed/sub-Gaussian rewards, non-i.i.d. contexts, UCB-style heterogeneous-cost variants, and lower bounds for boundary cases.
- Suggestions: add experiments against BwK/RCB baselines, guidance on hyperparameters, heuristics for boundary mitigation, and discussion of multiple resources/continuous contexts.
- Arguments for acceptance: timely problem, elegant ALP idea, first logarithmic regret result under stated assumptions, careful analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: no empirical validation, restricted setting, boundary-case weaknesses, and deferred/partial extensions.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with clear writing; reviewer leans accept but urges empirical studies and boundary-robustness discussion.
