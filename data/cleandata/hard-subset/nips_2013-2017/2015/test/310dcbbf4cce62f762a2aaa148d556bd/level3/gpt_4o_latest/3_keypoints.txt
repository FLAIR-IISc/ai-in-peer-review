- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, focusing on maximizing cumulative reward.
- Motivated by practical applications like clinical trials and online recommendations.
- Introduces Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for known context distributions and uniform costs.
- ALP attains near-optimal performance with O(1) regret in most settings.
- Proposes UCB-ALP, combining ALP with UCB, for unknown context distributions and arbitrary costs.
- UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases, improving over prior O(√T) results.
- Paper is well-written and provides rigorous theoretical analysis; Lemma 1 issues were addressed in rebuttal.
- Novel contribution: first known logarithmic-regret result for constrained contextual bandits under certain conditions and an original ALP+UCB approach.
- Demonstrates computational efficiency, making the methods practical for real-world use.
- Limitations: results depend on assumptions (e.g., context distribution ordering) and the generality of O(log T) is uncertain.
- Open questions include NP-hardness of computing the optimal action sequence and extension to unknown time horizons.
- Strong recommendation for acceptance based on significance and technical contribution.
