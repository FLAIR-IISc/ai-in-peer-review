- Introduces Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with budget and time horizon.
- Challenges prior special treatment of boundary cases (ρ = q_j) and motivates LP-based oracle approximation.
- Compares proposed ALP to a stronger adaptive-LP baseline that skips a fixed percent of suboptimal rounds.
- Assumes unit-cost system with known context distribution to isolate the exploration–exploitation tradeoff.
- Proposes a novel hybrid of ALP and UCB for handling parameter estimation.
- Shows near-optimal regret: O(1) except for certain boundary cases.
- Develops novel technical tools: concentration analysis under adaptive algorithms and bounding estimation errors with random contexts.
- Analysis is rigorous and reproducible for expert readers.
- Writing is clear and well-organized, accessible to a broad audience.
- Original use of linear programming to approximate the oracle and combine with UCB.
- Potentially significant applications (clinical trials, recommendations, crowdsourcing).
- Limitation: unit-cost and known-context assumptions may limit applicability to more general settings.
- Limitation: regret analysis is complex and may be difficult for non-experts to follow.
- Limitation: comparison to existing approaches could be more comprehensive.
