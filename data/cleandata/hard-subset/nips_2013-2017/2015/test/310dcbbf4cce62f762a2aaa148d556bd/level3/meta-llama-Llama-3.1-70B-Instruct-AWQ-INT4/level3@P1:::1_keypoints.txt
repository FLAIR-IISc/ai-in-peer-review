- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) achieving near-optimal performance when system statistics are known (O(1) regret except boundary cases).  
- Extends ALP to unknown rewards via UCB-ALP, claiming logarithmic regret except for boundary cases (sublinear regret there).  
- Provides a proof sketch for the UCB-ALP regret bound.  
- Strength: tackles a challenging constrained bandit problem.  
- Strength: develops a novel algorithmic approach (ALP and UCB-ALP) with strong performance guarantees.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation balancing technical detail and intuition.  
- Strength: results have potential real-world impact for budgeted decision problems.  
- Weakness: originality concerns — problem can be seen as a time/budgeted bandit variant with JK arms and action constraints.  
- Weakness: proof approach based on UCB ordering events may be suboptimal; alternative analyses suggested.  
- Weakness: paper needs more discussion of implications and potential real-world applications.  
- Recommendation/requests: clarify novelty vs. existing work, consider alternative proof techniques, and expand discussion of practical implications.
