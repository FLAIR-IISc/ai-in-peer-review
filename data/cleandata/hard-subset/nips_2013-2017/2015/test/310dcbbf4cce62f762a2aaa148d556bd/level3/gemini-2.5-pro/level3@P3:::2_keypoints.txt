- Problem: studies contextual bandits with both a time horizon T and a global budget B, where the budget couples decisions across contexts and time.  
- Main contribution: proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that re-solves an LP each step using remaining-budget-to-time ratio as an average constraint.  
- Known-statistics result: ALP is near-optimal, achieving O(1) regret generally and O(√T) regret in boundary cases.  
- Unknown-rewards algorithm: UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB estimates and attains O(log T) regret outside boundary cases.  
- Novelty/significance: first work to establish logarithmic regret for a constrained contextual bandit variant, improving on prior O(√T) results.  
- Technical quality: rigorous analysis with clear decomposition (ALP near-optimality, budget concentration via Lemma 2, action/context-ranking error bounds).  
- Clarity: well-written and well-organized, with a logical progression from known to unknown settings and good exposition.  
- Weakness: no experimental validation—no empirical comparison to RCB algorithms or assessment of boundary-case prevalence.  
- Weakness: extensions (unknown context distribution, heterogeneous costs) are brief, heuristic, and lack rigorous analysis.  
- Weakness: simplifying assumptions (finite discrete contexts, unit/fixed costs, known context distribution) restrict practical applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution worthy of acceptance at a theory-focused venue, but authors should add experiments for camera-ready.  
- For/against summary: arguments for—novelty, significance, technical quality, clarity; arguments against—no experiments and limited scope due to assumptions.
