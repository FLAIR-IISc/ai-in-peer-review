- Problem & proposal: Studies contextual bandits with time and budget constraints and proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and UCB-ALP for known/unknown statistics.

- ALP idea: Relax hard budget to an average constraint, solve an LP per context using instantaneous average budget bτ/τ, and adapt decisions to remaining time and budget.

- ALP guarantee: With known rewards, ALP achieves O(1) regret except in boundary cases.

- UCB-ALP idea: Combine ALP with UCB learning to handle unknown rewards.

- UCB-ALP guarantee: Attains O(JK log T) regret (and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases), with J contexts and K actions.

- Analysis technique: Maps budget consumption to sampling without replacement and uses hypergeometric concentration to control bτ.

- Relation to prior work: Improves on Mixture Elimination/RCB (O(√T)) and knapsack-style bandits by providing instance-dependent logarithmic regret and simpler computation.

- Novelty: Adaptive LP pacing (adaptive pacing of the budget) is the key algorithmic novelty enabling O(log T) regret.

- Strength — conceptual: Decouples learning and resource allocation; “only ordering matters” property is insightful and practical.

- Strength — theoretical: First logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits (non-boundary) with interpretable O(JK log T) dependence.

- Strength — analysis: Sampling-without-replacement view and hypergeometric concentration are elegant and reusable.

- Strength — computation: Per-round ALP is simple (threshold structure for unit cost) and remains tractable for heterogeneous costs.

- Weakness — boundary cases: ρ = qj causes discontinuities leading to Θ(√T) regret; need clearer intuition, possible margin/dithering remedies, or a lower bound.

- Weakness — robustness: Sensitivity when ρ is within O(1/√T) of a boundary needs discussion and principled stabilizing policies.

- Weakness — baseline clarity: Must state whether regret bounds are relative to true oracle U* or the LP upper bound Û and bound Û − U* when used.

- Weakness — assumptions/ties: Uniqueness assumptions (strict ordering, unique best action) may be fragile; need tie-handling or ε-margin extension.

- Weakness — unknown context distribution: Truncated EALP relegated to supplement; main text should summarize algorithm, truncation epoch scaling, and explicit bounds.

- Weakness — heterogeneous costs: Extension promising but under-specified in main text; include LP formulation and resulting threshold/marginal structure.

- Weakness — experiments: Limited empirical validation; should empirically probe boundary sensitivity, scaling in J,K, heterogeneous costs, and comparisons to baselines.

- Assessment — quality: Technically sound; proofs use standard UCB concentration plus novel control of bτ, but boundary regime optimality is unclear.

- Assessment — clarity: Generally well-written but needs clearer treatment of Û(T,B), boundary intuition, and unknown-π algorithm in main text.

- Assessment — originality & significance: Method and guarantees are novel and significant for budgeted applications; likely to influence follow-up work.

- Suggestions: Provide formal/better intuition for boundary regimes or lower bounds, clarify regret baseline, elevate unknown-π results to main text, add experiments, and discuss per-round complexity.

- Arguments for acceptance: First logarithmic-regret result under practical assumptions, simple and efficient algorithm, and reusable analytical techniques.

- Arguments against acceptance: Unresolved boundary-case optimality, key details deferred to supplement, and limited empirical validation.
