- Tackles contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, a practical but underexplored setting.  
- Establishes logarithmic regret bounds under assumptions: finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Introduces the Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation as a clever oracle approximation.  
- Develops the UCB-ALP algorithm building effectively on the ALP framework.  
- Theoretical contributions are solid and proofs appear convincing in outline.  
- Claim of being first to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits seems accurate given cited literature.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and thoroughly motivated with good examples of applications.  
- Authors demonstrate strong awareness of related work and appropriately cite prior work.  
- Use of LP relaxation is standard, but the adaptive application in ALP is novel and insightful.  
- Major weakness: no experimental validation—no simulations or empirical results presented.  
- Sections 5 and 6 (unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs) are treated superficially and pushed to the supplement.  
- Simplifying assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs) limit applicability to more complex real-world problems.  
- Recommendation: accept for theoretical novelty and rigor, but strongly encourage adding empirical evaluation (e.g., small-scale simulations vs simple baselines).
