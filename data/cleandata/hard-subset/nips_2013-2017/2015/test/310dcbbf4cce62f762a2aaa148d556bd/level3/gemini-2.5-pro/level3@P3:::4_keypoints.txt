- Problem: studies constrained contextual bandits with both time horizon T and resource budget B, introducing coupling across time and planning complexity.  
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm achieving logarithmic regret (O(log T) in non-boundary cases), improving over prior O(sqrt(T)) results.  
- Key idea: develop a near-optimal known-statistics policy (ALP) that adaptively solves a simple LP using remaining budget and time to decide actions.  
- ALP performance: achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and O(sqrt(T)) regret in specific "boundary" cases.  
- UCB-ALP: combines UCB exploration with ALP budget management and attains O(log T) regret (non-boundary) and O(sqrt(T)) in boundary cases.  
- Extensions: discusses handling unknown context distributions (via a truncated variant) and heterogeneous action costs.  
- Technical novelty: using static LP as an upper bound and ALP as a tractable near-optimal oracle is a clever decoupling enabling tight analysis.  
- Analysis strength: rigorous regret decomposition into action-ranking and context-ranking/budget-fluctuation errors, comparing to a strong ALP oracle baseline.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, structured, and provides intuitive proof sketches progressing from known-statistics to bandit setting.  
- Weakness: insufficient intuitive explanation for why boundary cases (ρ = q_j) cause regret to jump and why system is sensitive at those thresholds.  
- Weakness: simplifying assumptions (finite discrete contexts, i.i.d. arrivals, unit costs) limit direct applicability versus more general prior frameworks.  
- Question 1 for authors: provide clearer intuition why expected reward v(·) and budget dynamics are sensitive at ρ = q_j and distinctions among q_j (e.g., why ρ = q_1 is non-boundary).  
- Question 2 for authors: explain technical obstacles preventing direct analysis of the fully online UCB-EALP (updating empirical context distribution every step) instead of the truncated version.  
- Recommendation: overall high-quality, original, and significant work; strong accept recommendation.
