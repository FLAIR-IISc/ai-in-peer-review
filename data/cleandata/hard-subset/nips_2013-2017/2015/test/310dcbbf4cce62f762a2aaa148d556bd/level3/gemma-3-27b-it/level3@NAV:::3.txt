## Review of "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, a setting with significant practical relevance. The authors make a strong contribution by developing algorithms that achieve logarithmic regret under specific conditions, a substantial improvement over prior work in this area, particularly the O(√T) regret bound of Badanidiyuru et al. [17]. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly motivated, making it easy to understand the problem setup, proposed solutions, and their theoretical guarantees.

The core idea of approximating the oracle using adaptive linear programming (ALP) is both interesting and intuitive. The authors demonstrate a clever approach to handle the exploration-exploitation tradeoff under budget constraints by relaxing the hard budget constraint into an average constraint. Extending this to the unknown reward case with the UCB-ALP algorithm further strengthens the contribution. The analysis, though complex, appears rigorous and is supported by detailed proofs (referenced in the supplementary material). The extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs showcase the versatility of their approach.

However, the logarithmic regret guarantee does come with a caveat: a "mild condition" that, if violated, results in an Ω(√T) lower bound. While acknowledged by the authors, this limitation somewhat diminishes the universality of their results. Stronger universal guarantees would undoubtedly enhance the paper's significance. The reliance on Lemma 1 was initially a concern, as noted in the rebuttal, but the authors convincingly addressed this. 

Several interesting open questions remain. The computational complexity of computing the exact optimal action sequence is not discussed, and it would be valuable to know if this is NP-hard. Furthermore, exploring extensions to unknown horizon T and investigating the assumption of ordered expected rewards in the unknown context distribution case would be worthwhile future directions. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** First work to achieve O(log T) regret for this constrained contextual bandit setting.
*   **Significance:** Addresses a difficult and practically relevant problem with a significant improvement over existing approaches.
*   **Quality:** Technically sound with rigorous analysis and well-supported claims.
*   **Clarity:** Exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Generality:** The logarithmic regret guarantee is conditional, and the boundary cases revert to O(√T) regret.

**Overall:**

Despite the limitation of the conditional logarithmic regret, this is a high-quality paper that makes a substantial contribution to the field. The novelty, clarity, and potential impact of the work strongly outweigh the drawbacks. I **strongly recommend acceptance** to the conference.
