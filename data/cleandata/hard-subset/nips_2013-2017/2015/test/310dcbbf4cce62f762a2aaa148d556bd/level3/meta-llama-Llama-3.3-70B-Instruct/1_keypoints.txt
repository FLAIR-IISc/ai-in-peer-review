- Paper presents robustness analysis for iterative stochastic policy optimization and derives a novel inequality handling unbounded change-of-measure likelihood ratios, yielding high-confidence performance/safety certificates.  
- Manuscript is well-written and results are thoroughly presented.  
- Concern about originality: contextual bandits can be reduced to budgeted/time-constrained finite-arm bandits, a heavily studied area; need clearer differentiation from prior work.  
- Reviewer requests a cleaner, problem-dependent upper bound expressed in terms of arm gaps.  
- Reviewer asks for matching lower bounds in both problem-dependent and problem-independent settings to assess optimality.  
- Theorem 2’s regret bounds appear suboptimal; reviewer suspects they can be tightened to include the sum of inverse arm gaps.  
- Results are not surprising to the reviewer—log(T) regret is standard in contextual bandits literature.  
- Request for more detailed related-work discussion to clarify novelty and complexity of the setting.  
- Strength: clear motivation and demonstrated applicability to a simple robot control scenario.  
- Strength: use of concentration-of-measure inequalities to bound expected future cost and violation probability, with detailed analysis.  
- Overall verdict: paper has merits but requires more work on originality, tighter bounds, and lower bounds to justify significance.  
- Pro-accept points: quality of writing, thorough results, clear motivation, and novel use of concentration inequalities.  
- Con-accept points: perceived lack of novelty, potentially suboptimal regret bounds, unsurprising results, and missing lower bounds.
