- Problem: Contextual bandits with a hard budget and known horizon, starting with discrete contexts, unit costs, and known context distribution.
- Main idea: Introduce an adaptive linear-programming (ALP) approximation that relaxes the hard budget to an average budget and uses a threshold structure based on remaining average budget bτ/τ.
- Known-reward result: ALP is near-optimal with O(1) regret off boundary and O(√T) regret at boundary cases.
- Learning reward rankings: Combine ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, using only ordering of expected rewards to learn online.
- UCB-ALP regret: Achieves O(JK log T) off boundary and O(√T + JK log T) on boundaries.
- Unknown context distribution: Propose EALP/UCB-EALP using empirical estimates with truncated estimators and coupling/concentration analysis.
- Heterogeneous costs: Extend to general LP over context-action pairs and an ε-first learning variant for unknown rewards/costs.
- Key analysis tool: Map remaining budget behavior to sampling without replacement (hypergeometric) to obtain tight concentration and separate budget vs ranking errors.
- Upper bound justification: LP upper bound (Lemma 1) supports using Û(T,B) as the benchmark; authors address initial concerns about static LP vs dynamic oracle.
- Relation to prior work: Improves over resourceful contextual bandits [17] by achieving logarithmic regret (off boundary) against the oracle with simpler algorithms.
- Comparison to non-contextual BwK: Prior BwK techniques don't handle context coupling; this work addresses that gap.
- Comparison to concave/convex knapsack bandits: Sharpens rates from typical O(√T) to O(log T) off boundary in dynamic-context settings.
- Clarity: Paper is well written, organized, and explains key ideas intuitively; some technical details are in the supplement.
- Originality: First to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits with hard budgets and random contexts; ALP surrogate and decoupling of learning/budget are novel.
- Practical significance: Algorithms are computationally simple, require only rank information, and apply to real-world budgeted decisions.
- Strengths: Clear conceptual contribution, strong regret guarantees, scalability, extensions to unknown π and heterogeneous costs, and solid connections to literature.
- Weaknesses: Regret degrades to O(√T) at boundaries; unknown-π analysis requires truncation; heterogeneous-costs solution needs an ε-first phase rather than a fully adaptive method.
- Questions/suggestions: Clarify complexity of exact oracle, anytime variants for unknown horizon, tie-breaking/ordering assumptions, practical heuristics to reduce constants, and add empirical comparisons/ablations.
- Acceptance pros: First logarithmic-regret result for this setting, simple implementable algorithms, strong theory, and broad relevance.
- Acceptance cons: Boundary-case deterioration and lack of matching lower bounds; some deferred technical details could be expanded.
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
