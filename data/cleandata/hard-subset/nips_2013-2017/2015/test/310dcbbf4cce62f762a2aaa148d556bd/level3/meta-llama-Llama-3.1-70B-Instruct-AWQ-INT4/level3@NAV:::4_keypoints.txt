- Proposes algorithms with logarithmic or sublinear regret for constrained contextual bandits by decoupling information acquisition and decision making via adaptive linear relaxation, tolerant to parameter estimation errors.
- Raises valid concern about special treatment of boundary cases (why ρ = q_j is special, especially for j > 1) since current analysis focuses on non-boundary cases.
- Introduces a stronger baseline using adaptive linear programming that skips a fixed percent of suboptimal rounds (“budget”), enabling nuanced exploration–exploitation tradeoffs.
- Main contribution is UCB-ALP, combining UCB with adaptive linear programming.
- UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret generally, but only O(√T) regret in certain boundary cases.
- Strength: interesting setup and unique solutions that advance constrained contextual bandits.
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis of UCB-ALP’s regret bounds.
- Weakness: analysis of boundary cases is weaker and needs more insight and treatment.
- Weakness: assumes known context distribution, which may be unrealistic in practice and requires discussion or extensions.
- Weakness: insufficient numerical simulations to validate practical performance of UCB-ALP.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address boundary-case analysis and handling of unknown context distributions.
- Overall rating: 8/10.
