- Main contribution: first work to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under budget and time constraints.  
- Technical insight: use Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle, requiring only reward orderings rather than exact values.  
- Algorithm: combine ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, achieving O(log T) regret except in boundary cases.  
- Clarity and organization: problem formulation and progression from unit-cost to general cases are clear and well-structured.  
- Key theoretical element: Lemma 2 gives concentration properties for remaining budget, crucial to analysis.  
- Technical quality: rigorous regret bounds and careful analysis of ranking errors and budget fluctuations; proofs are convincing.  
- Originality: novel application of LP/ALP to constrained contextual bandits and the ordering-only requirement is innovative.  
- Significance: high practical relevance (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing) with computationally efficient, near-optimal performance.  
- Suggestions: explicitly state how UCB-ALP uses optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty, discuss integrating parametric reward models, add numerical experiments, and cite arXiv:1506.03374.  
- Weakness: limited discussion/analysis of computational complexity for LP solving with large context sets.  
- Remaining open issue: boundary cases where regret degrades to O(√T) are not fully resolved.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — significant, novel, and well-analyzed contribution despite minor concerns.
