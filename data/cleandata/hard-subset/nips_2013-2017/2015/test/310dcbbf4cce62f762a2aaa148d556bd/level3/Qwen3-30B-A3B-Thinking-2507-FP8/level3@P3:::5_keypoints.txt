- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits under a simplified model (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget).  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation that adapts budget/time and attains O(1) regret except at boundary cases.  
- Develops UCB-ALP by combining ALP with UCB, achieving O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases (J contexts, K actions).  
- Presents the first known logarithmic regret result for constrained contextual bandits under the paper’s model assumptions.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong and novel, with careful handling of context-ranking errors and remaining-budget fluctuations to bound regret.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and connects effectively to prior NIPS work, distinguishing itself from [17] and [20].  
- Main strengths: clean theoretical framework, rigorous analysis, practical relevance, and elegant LP + exploration combination.  
- Limitation: simplified model assumptions may not scale when contexts are numerous.  
- Suggestion: consider parametric/linear reward models to improve scalability for many contexts.  
- Suggestion: clarify how the optimism principle is applied in UCB-ALP and its relation to UCRL2 policy updates.  
- Suggestion: include numerical experiments to validate theoretical results in an extended version.  
- Suggestion: add citation to Agrawal et al. (arXiv:1506.03374) on related linear contextual bandit work.  
- Recommendation: accept—significant, near-optimal theoretical contribution with practical implications.
