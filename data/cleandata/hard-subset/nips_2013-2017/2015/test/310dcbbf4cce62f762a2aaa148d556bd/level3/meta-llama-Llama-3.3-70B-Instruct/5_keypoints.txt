- Presents a significant contribution to stochastic decision-making under uncertainty by providing probably-approximate robustness bounds for decision variables.
- Uses a statistical-learning viewpoint and concentration-of-measure inequalities to derive high-confidence bounds on expected cost and probability of constraint violation.
- Demonstrates the approach on a simple robot navigation scenario and provides initial steps toward aerial vehicle navigation applications.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with a strong motivation for robustness guarantees.
- Technical analysis is sound, thorough, and well-structured.
- Major strength: delivers high-confidence guarantees on cost and constraint-violation probabilities, improving reliability under uncertainty.
- Applicable to discrete-time stochastic control and promising for aerial vehicle navigation tasks.
- Improvement needed: additional numerical experiments, particularly for aerial vehicle navigation, to better support claims.
- Improvement needed: clearer explanation of the rationale and differences from existing methods in the field.
- Future work: suggest using parametric reward models for scalability when handling many contexts.
- Overall assessment: novel, well-motivated, and thoroughly analyzed work with potential broad impact; strong candidate for acceptance.
- Potential limitation: may not be directly comparable to existing works due to its novel approach.
