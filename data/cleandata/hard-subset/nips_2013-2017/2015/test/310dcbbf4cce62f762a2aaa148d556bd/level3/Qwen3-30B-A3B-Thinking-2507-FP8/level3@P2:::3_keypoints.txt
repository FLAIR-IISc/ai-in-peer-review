- Problem: Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget.  
- Motivation: Targets practical applications like clinical trials and crowdsourcing where time and resource limits matter.  
- Main result: Develops algorithms achieving O(log T) regret under certain conditions, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Contribution 1: Proposes an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) oracle approximation that yields O(1) regret for known statistics (non-boundary cases).  
- Contribution 2: Combines ALP with UCB to create UCB-ALP, achieving O(log T) regret when statistics are unknown.  
- Contribution 3: Extends results to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Literature positioning: Fills gap between contextual bandits and budgeted bandits and distinguishes from prior work (e.g., [17]) that only achieved O(√T).  
- Theoretical quality: Rigorous, well-structured proofs with a clear upper bound (Lemma 1) and careful treatment of boundary cases.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Exceptionally clear writing, logical flow, and sufficient technical detail (with supplementary material) for experts to reproduce results.  
- Originality: Novel design and analysis of ALP in constrained contextual bandits and its combination with UCB to obtain logarithmic regret.  
- Significance: Addresses an important practical problem, gives substantial theoretical improvement, and provides computationally efficient algorithms.  
- Main limitation: O(log T) guarantee requires a non-boundary condition (ρ ≠ any cumulative q_j); boundary cases incur O(√T) regret.  
- Open questions: Whether a universal guarantee avoiding boundary restrictions is possible, extension to unknown horizon T, and NP-hardness of computing exact optimal sequence.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
