- Problem studied: constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.  
- Special-case result: for known context distribution and unit costs, ALP algorithm achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extension: UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB to handle unknown context distribution and arbitrary costs, achieving logarithmic regret except in boundary cases.  
- Novel approach: uses linear programming to approximate the oracle algorithm.  
- Technical contribution: substantial and rigorous regret analysis for ALP and UCB-ALP.  
- Significance: improves prior results (from O(√T) to O(log T)) in constrained contextual bandits.  
- Clarity/quality: paper is well-written, well-organized, with thorough related-work review.  
- Limitation: O(log T) guarantee depends on a mild condition; boundary cases have an Ω(√T) lower bound.  
- Dependence on Lemma 1: core results rely heavily on Lemma 1; reviewer initially skeptical but convinced after rebuttal.  
- Open questions: complexity of computing exact optimal action sequence (NP-hard?), extension to unknown horizon T, and assumptions about ordering u^1>u^2>... in unknown-distribution case.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, novel algorithmic idea, strong theoretical results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on condition/Lemma 1 and remaining technical open issues.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
