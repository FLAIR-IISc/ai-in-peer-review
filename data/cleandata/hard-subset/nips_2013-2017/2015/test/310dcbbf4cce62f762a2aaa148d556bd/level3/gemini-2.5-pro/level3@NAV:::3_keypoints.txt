- Paper studies contextual bandits with finite time horizon T and budget B, where budget couples decisions across time and complicates exploration-exploitation.
- Main contribution is UCB-ALP, the first algorithm to achieve O(log T) regret under a mild non-boundary condition, improving on prior O(√T) results.
- Approach: develop an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) offline approximation and combine it with a UCB strategy for online learning.
- Theoretical advance is significant for budgeted bandits and has potential practical applications.
- Analysis is technically sound, with clear logical progression from known-statistics to full online learning; Lemma 1’s role is central and defended convincingly.
- Authors transparently state limitations and acknowledge Ω(√T) lower bound in boundary cases.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and the exposition (unit-cost to general cases) makes core ideas accessible.
- Main weakness: reliance on the “non-boundary” condition for O(log T) limits universality of guarantees.
- Open technical question: is computing the exact offline optimal action sequence NP-hard, which would justify the approximation approach?
- Practical question: can results be extended to unknown time horizon T?
- Minor clarification: in the unknown context distribution case, clarify whether the ordering u*1 > u*2 > ... > u*J is assumed without loss of generality.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—strengths outweigh limitations.
