- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with budget/time limits and proposes an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation and UCB-ALP, claiming first logarithmic regret for this setting.  
- Relationship to recent contextual-bandit-with-constraints work ([20-22]) and the dynamic context case is unclear and needs clarification.

Strengths:
- ALP provides a clean theoretical benchmark for regret analysis in constrained contextual bandits.  
- UCB-ALP is computationally efficient and practical for exploration–exploitation under budget constraints.  
- ALP analysis shows near-optimality (O(1) regret except boundary cases), offering insights into problem structure.  
- Addresses important applications (clinical trials, crowdsourcing, recommendations).

Weaknesses:
- Novelty is questionable since the problem can be reduced to a standard JK-arm time/budgeted bandit with action constraints.  
- Theorem 2’s regret O(√T + JK log T) is dubious; standard bounds suggest O(√(JK T)) and the paper doesn’t justify the √T dominance.  
- Problem-dependent bound is expressed as a JK-sum rather than in terms of standard arm gaps; a gaps-based bound is needed.  
- No matching lower bounds are provided, so tightness of regret results is uncertain.  
- The claim that context coupling vanishes for large horizons needs justification; treating contexts as independent bandit instances is not addressed.

Specific comments:
- Must explicitly connect and contrast with [20-22] to substantiate novelty.  
- The “context ranking events” proof structure seems overly complex; a standard UCB regret decomposition might suffice.  
- Supplementary material should rework problem-dependent regret to use arm gaps instead of a KJ factor.  
- Boundary-case analysis is unclear: why are boundary cases O(√T) when general cases are logarithmic?

Recommendation and required fixes:
- Recommend weak rejection due to overstated novelty and insufficient theoretical justification.  
- Authors should (1) rigorously justify novelty vs prior work, (2) correct or justify regret bounds (e.g., O(√(JK T) + ...)), (3) derive gap-based problem-dependent bounds, and (4) provide matching lower bounds for both settings.
