- Tackles constrained contextual bandits aiming for logarithmic regret via ALP and UCB-ALP.  
- Proposes LP relaxation and Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle when statistics are known.  
- Extends ALP to unknown rewards by combining with UCB (UCB-ALP) and analyzes regret.  
- Claims O(log T) regret under certain conditions and generalizes to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Strength: addresses a practically important and more complex problem than unconstrained bandits.  
- Strength: ALP is a novel approximation approach for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear motivation and presentation.  
- Strength: analysis is comprehensive, covering multiple realistic scenarios.  
- Strength: appropriately connects to prior work in contextual bandits, knapsack, and resource allocation.  
- Weakness: claim that context coupling vanishes with large horizon is unconvincing and needs stronger justification.  
- Weakness: upper bound is loose (O(JK log T)); paper needs a clean problem-dependent bound in terms of arm gaps.  
- Weakness: lacks matching lower bounds to establish optimality of the logarithmic regret claim.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2’s O(√T + …) rate is questionable and may need dependence on K (e.g., O(√(KT))).  
- Weakness: reliance on UCB ordering events feels ad-hoc within the LP-based framework; more principled connections or alternatives needed.  
- Weakness: overall contribution may be incremental—extensions (unknown contexts, heterogeneous costs) are natural from ALP.  
- Pro-accept: important problem, novel ALP approach, and thorough theoretical treatment.  
- Con-accept: insufficient theoretical justification, missing lower bounds, questionable rates, and incremental novelty.  
- Overall: reviewer neutral—technically sound but needs stronger theoretical justification, tighter bounds, and clarifications for acceptance.
