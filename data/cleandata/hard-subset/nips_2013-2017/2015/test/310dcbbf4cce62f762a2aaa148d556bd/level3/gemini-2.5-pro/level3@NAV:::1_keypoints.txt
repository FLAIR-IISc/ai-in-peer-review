- Topic: studies contextual bandits with time and budget constraints causing coupling across contexts.
- Method: proposes UCB-ALP combining Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation with UCB learning.
- Main result: proves O(log T) regret for large horizon T in "non-boundary" cases with finite contexts/actions.
- Relation to prior work: positioned against bandits with knapsacks and resourceful contextual bandits, claiming stronger logarithmic regret under a restricted setting.
- Strength: tackles a significant practical problem with an intuitive ALP framework and clear progression from known-parameter to learning settings.
- Originality concern: claim that coupling vanishes for large T may reduce problem to a JK-arm MAB with knapsack, questioning novelty.
- Modeling question: authors should clarify why each context occurrence cannot be treated as an independent bandit instance.
- Lower bounds requested: provide matching problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds that account for context arrival complexity.
- Upper bounds requested: rework problem-dependent upper bounds into standard sum of inverse-squared gaps (ΣΔ⁻²) instead of a coarse KJ factor.
- Technical clarity: ambiguity in Theorem 2—verify whether the O(√T + …) term should instead be O(√(KT) + …) or include K dependency.
- Recommendation: reviewer is neutral pending clearer novelty justification and more complete theoretical analysis; urge authors to address these in rebuttal.
