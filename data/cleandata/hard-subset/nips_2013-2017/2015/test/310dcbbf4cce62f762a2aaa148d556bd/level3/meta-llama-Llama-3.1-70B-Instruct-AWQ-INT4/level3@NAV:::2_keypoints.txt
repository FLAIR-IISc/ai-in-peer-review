- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.
- Proposes an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle achieving near-optimality.
- ALP only requires the ordering of expected rewards, not their exact values.
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP for unknown expected rewards.
- UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases.
- Theoretical contributions and proofs are solid and convincing.
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and thorough analysis.
- Provides a good discussion of related work and the significance of contributions.
- Practical aspects are thinly treated and implementation challenges are briefly discussed.
- No experimental results or practical evaluations are provided.
- Overall assessment: good contribution but needs more work to demonstrate practicality and effectiveness.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel solution, thorough theoretical analysis, significant contribution to contextual bandits.
- Arguments for rejection: lack of experiments, technical difficulty for non-experts, combination of ALP with UCB not entirely novel.
