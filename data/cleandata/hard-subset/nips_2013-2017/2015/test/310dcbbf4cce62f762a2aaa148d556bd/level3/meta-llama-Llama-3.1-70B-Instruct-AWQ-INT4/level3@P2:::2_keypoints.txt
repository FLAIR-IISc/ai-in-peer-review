- Studies constrained contextual bandits (budget and time constraints) and introduces adaptive linear programming (ALP) and UCB-ALP algorithms.  
- Analysis limited to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- ALP approximates the oracle and achieves O(1) regret except for boundary cases with O(√T) regret.  
- UCB-ALP handles unknown expected rewards and achieves O(log T) regret except for boundary cases with O(√T) regret.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and proofs are convincing.  
- Paper is well-structured, clear, and well-documented with explicit motivation and relevant citations.  
- Practical aspects are underdeveloped; Sections 5 and 6 are brief and lack experimental validation.  
- Empirical ALP (EALP) is claimed to use empirical context distribution, but rigorous analysis is challenging due to dependency issues.  
- Cites related work (e.g., Resourceful Contextual Bandits) and justifies simplified model for applications like clinical trials and wireless rate selection.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, proposes a novel ALP, and provides solid regret analysis and theoretical contributions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical evaluation, restrictive modeling assumptions, and insufficient discussion of relation to existing work.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Overall: strong theoretical paper with clear presentation but needs more practical exploration and experiments.
