- Problem: Addresses contextual bandits with coupled decisions under budget and time constraints, complicating exploration-exploitation.  
- Method: Introduces an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) framework to approximate the intractable oracle policy.  
- Algorithm: Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP for settings with unknown expected rewards.  
- Main theoretical claim: UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret, improving on prior O(√T) bounds.  
- Novelty/strength: First known work to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits; ALP is an elegant, effective technique.  
- Presentation: Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and situates contributions within prior literature; proofs and sketches appear sound.  
- Main weakness: Complete absence of experimental validation.  
- Modeling limitations: Analysis relies on simplifying assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget).  
- Empirical concerns: No experiments makes practical performance, hidden constants, and boundary-case O(√T) behavior unclear; extensions in Sections 5–6 feel preliminary without empirical support.  
- Recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance but strongly encourage adding experiments in the final version.
