- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with time horizon T and budget B, proposing ALP and UCB-ALP to aim for O(log T) regret.  
- ALP approximates the oracle by adapting budget constraints based on remaining time and budget.  
- UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB for unknown rewards and claims logarithmic regret in non-boundary cases.  
- Builds on prior work (RCB and bandits with knapsack constraints) but focuses on simplified assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget).  
- Strength: ALP offers an elegant way to decouple context-budget coupling and Theorem 1 analysis supports its near-optimality.  
- Strength: UCB-ALP is well-motivated and the sketch of regret analysis gives useful insight into exploration under constraints.  
- Strength: Practical connections (clinical trials, wireless networks) and empirical EALP for unknown context distributions are useful contributions.  
- Originality concern: The reduction to JK context-action arms makes the logarithmic-regret claim questionable since standard UCB already yields O(log T) for finite arms.  
- Theorem 2 concern: Regret bound stated as O(JK log T) is overly pessimistic and should be problem-dependent (sum over inverse gaps) rather than a simple JK factor.  
- Proof issues: UCB ordering event analysis may overestimate regret from context ranking errors; supplementary material appears inconsistent with the claimed bound.  
- Missing lower bounds: No matching problem-dependent Ω(Σ (gap)^{-1} log T) or problem-independent Ω(√(JK T)) lower bounds are provided.  
- Boundary cases: Analysis for ρ = q_j is insufficient; unclear whether the √T term is necessary or should be √(K T) as standard results suggest.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and organized, but the link between ALP and standard bandit reduction is not clearly explained, weakening novelty claims.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on authors addressing novelty concerns and providing a clean, problem-dependent regret bound and clarifications on boundary-case rates.
