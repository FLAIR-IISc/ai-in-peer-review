- Problem and contributions: studies contextual bandits with a single budget and finite horizon, proposes LP-based ALP and learning UCB-ALP; ALP: O(1) regret off-boundary, O(√T) on-boundary; UCB-ALP: O(JK log T) plus √T on-boundary.

- Relation to prior work: positioned against RCB/BwK and convex-knapsack contextual bandits; finiteness and unit-cost simplify structure enabling LP thresholding/adaptation.

- ALP quality: analysis is crisp and uses a neat hypergeometric (sampling-without-replacement) concentration argument.

- UCB-ALP quality: proof strategy (decomposing action vs. context-ordering errors) is reasonable but resulting bounds are not cleanly instance-dependent.

- Modeling clarification requested: if bτ/τ concentrates as T grows, why not treat each context occurrence as an independent bandit with its own budget share—need to pinpoint where coupling persists beyond boundary cases.

- Problem-dependent bounds: supplementary bounds should be rewritten explicitly in terms of gaps Δ_{j,k} with standard ∑(1/Δ)-type terms instead of a blanket JK factor.

- Technical clarity: UCB “ordering events” argument can be streamlined to avoid potential double-counting.

- Originality concern: result may be unsurprising when viewed as a time/budgeted bandit with JK arms and action constraints; novelty relative to BwK/RCB and recent dynamic-context knapsack works should be sharpened.

- Significance/requested strengthenings: provide matching lower bounds (both problem-dependent and -independent) that capture context complexity rather than reducing to classical bandits.

- Theorem 2 question: clarify boundary-rate scaling—whether the √T term is truly O(√T + JK log T) or should scale with √(KT) or depend on J due to fluctuation and ranking complexity.

- Pros: simple, computationally light algorithms; nice LP–UCB synthesis; clear concentration-based ALP analysis; addresses a relevant constrained setting.

- Cons: novelty versus BwK/RCB unclear; no matching lower bounds; gap-dependent bounds not clean; ambiguity about context coupling/decoupling and boundary-rate scaling.

- Overall verdict: Neutral—encourage rebuttal to clarify novelty, tighten gap-dependent bounds, and add lower bounds.
