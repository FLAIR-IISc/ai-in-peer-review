- Problem: constrained contextual bandits with i.i.d. discrete contexts, hard budget B and horizon T; goal maximize reward before time/budget runs out.  
- Main idea: adaptive linear program (ALP) replaces hard budget with average remaining budget bτ/τ to derive a threshold policy.  
- Core results (unit-cost, known π): ALP attains O(1) regret for non-boundary budget ratios ρ and O(√T) on boundary cases.  
- Learning extension: UCB-ALP learns rewards and achieves O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases (O(√T + JK log T) on boundary).  
- Unknown π extension: empirical-estimate-based ALP with truncated analysis; heterogeneous costs: generalized LP and exploration-then-ALP.  
- Regret characterization: instance-dependent logarithmic rates are achieved except for inevitable √T behavior on boundary instances.  
- Novelty: first known logarithmic-regret result for budgeted contextual bandits with i.i.d. discrete contexts and hard budget.  
- Strength: conceptual simplicity—ALP decouples resource allocation from learning and yields an implementable threshold policy.  
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees and clear separation of boundary vs. non-boundary behavior.  
- Strength: computational efficiency—per-round computation reduces to small LP/sorting and thresholding.  
- Strength: tight oracle upper bound Û(T,B) enables clean regret decomposition and analysis.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing and motivation.  
- Limitation: main logarithmic guarantee requires a non-boundary condition; boundary instances degrade to O(√T).  
- Limitation: heavy reliance on LP upper bound (Lemma 1); coupling between hard constraint and average-constraint LP could be highlighted more.  
- Limitation: extensions (unknown π, heterogeneous costs) are sketched and mostly in the supplement; more main-text detail or empirical validation needed.  
- Technical quality: careful, convincing analysis using hypergeometric concentration and standard UCB tools; Lemma 1 validated post-rebuttal.  
- Clarity suggestions: state per-round complexity explicitly and quantify constants in boundary-regret to show when JK log T dominates √T.  
- Clarity suggestions: in unknown-π section, state when empirical distribution is frozen and summarize main concentration lemma; in heterogeneous-costs, include LP and regret theorem.  
- Clarification needed: explicit handling of assumed ordering u*1 > u*2 > … when π is unknown and how empirical ordering/ties are treated.  
- Open questions: complexity of exact oracle, adapting UCB-ALP to unknown T, possible perturbations to avoid boundary issues, detection of near-boundary ρ in practice.  
- Empirical suggestion: add experiments comparing ALP/UCB-ALP to RCB and convex-knapsack methods on synthetic and small real datasets.  
- Overall judgment: high originality and significance; recommended for acceptance (strong accept) despite boundary-case and extension presentation limitations.
