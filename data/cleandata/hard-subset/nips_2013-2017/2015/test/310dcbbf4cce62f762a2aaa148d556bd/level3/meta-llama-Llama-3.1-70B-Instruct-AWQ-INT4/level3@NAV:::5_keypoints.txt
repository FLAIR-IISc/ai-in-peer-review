- Paper proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to solve constrained contextual bandits with budget/time constraints.
- ALP approximates the oracle and achieves near-optimal performance for known expected rewards.
- ALP is extended to unknown expected rewards by combining with UCB, yielding the UCB-ALP algorithm.
- Theoretical analysis provides regret bounds for both ALP and UCB-ALP.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with thorough explanations.
- Related work is reviewed thoroughly and contributions are clearly highlighted.
- Strength: novel algorithm achieving near-optimal performance for constrained contextual bandits.
- Strength: successful extension to the unknown-reward setting.
- Strength: detailed theoretical regret analysis.
- Weakness: assumes discrete contexts and actions, which may be unrealistic in some applications.
- Weakness: lacks numerical experiments to empirically validate algorithm performance.
- Recommendation: accept for publication.
- Suggested revision: add numerical experiments to validate performance.
- Suggested revision: discuss limitations of discrete-context/action assumption and possible extensions.
- Suggested revision: clarify rationale for combining UCB with ALP, state the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle, and relate to UCRL2 updates.
- Suggested revision: consider parametric reward models for scalability with many contexts.
- Suggested revision: include citation to arXiv:1506.03374.
