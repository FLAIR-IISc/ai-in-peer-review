- Paper addresses budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions and presents a computationally efficient algorithm achieving logarithmic regret.  
- Main contribution: first O(log T) regret result for this problem, improving over prior constrained contextual bandits (RCB) which had O(√T).  
- Key technical idea: use an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle that attains near-optimality (O(1) regret except boundary cases).  
- For unknown statistics, combine ALP with UCB to obtain a learning algorithm.  
- Theoretical guarantee: UCB-ALP achieves O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases, which is order-optimal.  
- Paper provides rigorous regret analysis showing ALP tolerates estimation errors, enabling combination with UCB.  
- The work is computationally efficient and advances the state of the art significantly beyond previous approaches.  
- Writing and structure are clear and the paper is technically sound, original, and significant.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested clarifications: explicitly state how the UCB+ALP combination implements optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty and relate it to UCRL2 policy updates (Section 4.1).  
- Suggested citations: add arXiv:1506.03374 [21] which extends related work to dynamic contexts.  
- Scalability note: for many contexts, consider exploring parametric reward models (left reasonable for future work).  
- Empirical suggestion: include numerical experiments in an extended version to strengthen practical relevance.  
- Caveat: ALP has boundary cases where O(1) performance does not hold and these require special handling.
