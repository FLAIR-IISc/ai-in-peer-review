- Problem: Studies contextual bandits with hard budget B and known horizon T, aiming to maximize reward before time or budget exhaustion against an oracle that knows π, rewards, and costs.  
- Main contribution: Proposes Adaptive Linear Program (ALP) replacing hard budget with adaptive average-budget constraint; proves near-optimality and combines with UCB (UCB-ALP).  
- Guarantees (unit-cost, known π): ALP achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases; UCB-ALP gives O(JK log T) regret non-boundary and O(√T + JK log T) at boundaries.  
- Extensions: Handles unknown π via empirical/truncated EALP/UCB-EALP and heterogeneous costs with generalized ALP and exploration-then-exploit variants.  
- Novelty: First to obtain O(log T) regret in this constrained contextual setting under a natural finite model.  
- Methodological strength: Elegant, simple approximation of DP by an averaged LP adapted online; only ordering of contexts/actions needed for learning.  
- Theoretical strength: Careful decomposition of regret sources and use of hypergeometric concentration for remaining-budget analysis.  
- Practical strength: Computationally efficient, well-motivated algorithms with empirical results aligning with theory.  
- Writing: Clear, well organized, consistent notation, and explicit boundary/non-boundary distinction.  
- Technical soundness: Solid development, plausible dependence on J and K, and well-justified regret bounds.  
- Limitation: O(log T) result depends on a non-boundary condition (ρ ≠ cumulative context mass); boundaries yield Ω(√T) lower bound.  
- Dependence on key lemma: Results rely on an LP upper bound (Lemma 1) assuming existence of an optimal sequence satisfying the averaged constraint, which required clarification.  
- Presentation issue: Some important details for extensions are deferred to the supplement; an in-paper roadmap would help.  
- Open technical questions: Complexity of computing exact oracle (NP-hard vs pseudo-polynomial via DP) not formally stated.  
- Open technical questions: Adapting ALP/UCB-ALP to unknown/anytime horizon needs clarification since ρ=B/T is central.  
- Open technical questions: Handling strict ordering assumption in unknown π case and tie-breaking interactions with estimation needs clarification for reproducibility.  
- Pros for acceptance: First O(log T) result, simple and practical method, strong analysis and likely broad impact.  
- Cons for acceptance: Dependence on non-boundary condition and reliance on supplemental proofs for extensions.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — clear, original, technically sound, and significant advancement in budgeted contextual bandits.
