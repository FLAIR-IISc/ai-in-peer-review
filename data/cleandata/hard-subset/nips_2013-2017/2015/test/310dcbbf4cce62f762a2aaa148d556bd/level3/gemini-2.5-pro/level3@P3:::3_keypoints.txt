- Addresses budgeted contextual bandits with time horizon T and budget B where agent can play costly actions or skip until time or budget runs out.
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild non-boundary condition, improving on prior O(√T) results.
- Introduces ALP (Adaptive Linear Programming) for the offline problem, approximating optimal DP by solving a simple LP with adaptive budget constraint.
- ALP shown to be near-optimal, yielding O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and providing a strong foundation for the online algorithm.
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP; key insight is ALP needs only ordering of expected rewards, not exact values.
- Analysis successfully decouples estimation errors, random context arrivals, and budget fluctuations to prove logarithmic regret.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and structured to build intuition from simple to general cases (unit costs → unknown distributions → heterogeneous costs).
- Significant theoretical advance and technically sound; fills a challenging open problem in the literature.
- Limitation: O(log T) guarantee requires a "non-boundary" condition on ρ=B/T; at boundary points regret degrades to O(√T).
- Boundary-case O(√T) is tight due to known Ω(√T) lower bounds, but a universal or adaptive guarantee would strengthen the result.
- Suggest adding intuitive explanation of Lemma 1 in main text to preempt reader confusion about LP upper bound vs. non-stationary optimal policy.
- Ask whether computing the exact optimal offline policy is known to be NP-hard to better motivate the ALP approximation.
- Request comment on extending results to unknown or infinite horizon settings.
- Clarify the reward-ordering assumption when moving from known to unknown context distributions and empirical estimation.
- Recommendation: Strongly recommend acceptance.
