- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, motivated by clinical trials and online advertising.
- Introduces UCB-ALP algorithm achieving O(log T) regret for most instances and O(√T) regret only in boundary cases.
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation that adjusts budget to average remaining budget, yielding O(1) regret when statistics are known.
- Key insight: ALP needs only the ordering of expected rewards, enabling combination with UCB to handle unknown statistics.
- Elegant decoupling of information acquisition (UCB) and decision making (ALP), addressing the core challenge in constrained bandits.
- Uses LP relaxation as a benchmark instead of intractable DP oracle, simplifying analysis.
- Provides rigorous regret analysis handling interactions between context arrivals, budget fluctuations, and estimation errors.
- Novel proof techniques for concentration properties of remaining budget bτ/τ under adaptive algorithms.
- Clear explanation of boundary cases (ρ = q_j) causing threshold ambiguity and resulting in O(√T) regret; these cases are measure-zero but could occur in practice.
- Comprehensive comparison to related work, showing improvement over prior O(√T) results and addressing an open problem about computationally efficient algorithms.
- Presentation is clear and well-structured; experimental validation not detailed in provided text but theoretical results are compelling.
- Significant and practical contribution with computationally efficient approach; recommendation for acceptance given technical novelty and impact.
