- Problem and contribution: Studies constrained contextual bandits and proposes ALP (adaptive LP) and UCB-ALP (ALP + UCB) algorithms.

- Main theoretical claim: ALP is near-optimal with known statistics; UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret except for boundary cases.

- Technical soundness: Generally technically sound with thorough analysis.

- Major flaw: Proof of Theorem 2 appears incorrect — regret rate should be O(√(K T) + …) rather than O(√T + …).

- Clarity issues: Paper is well-organized overall but parts (notably the proof of Theorem 1) are hard to follow.

- Originality: Combination of ALP and UCB is novel, though work builds on existing contextual and budgeted bandit literature.

- Significance: Results are significant and potentially applicable to clinical trials, online recommendation, and crowdsourcing hiring.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel ALP+UCB approach, clear analysis of algorithms, and practically significant results.

- Arguments against acceptance: Incorrect/misstated theorem rate, incremental contribution over prior work, and some unclear exposition.

- Recommendation: Reviewer neutral; requests corrected/clarified proof of Theorem 2 and improved presentation for accessibility.
