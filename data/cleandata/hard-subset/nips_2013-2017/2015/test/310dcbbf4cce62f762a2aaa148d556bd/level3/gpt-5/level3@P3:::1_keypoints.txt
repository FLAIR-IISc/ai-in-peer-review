- Studies contextual bandits with global budget/time horizon, finite contexts/actions, unit costs, known context distribution; proposes LP relaxation and adaptive LP (ALP) using bτ/τ.

- ALP is within O(1) of the DP oracle for non-boundary parameters; O(√T) gap on measure-zero “boundary” parameters where ρ equals a cumulative context probability.

- For unknown rewards, UCB-ALP attains O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.

- Sketches extensions to unknown context distribution (empirical EALP) and heterogeneous costs.

- Setting is a special case of RCB/BwK; prior work gives O(√T) regret but with high computational cost and policy restrictions, while this work targets a simpler, efficient algorithm.

- Claimed novelty: achieving logarithmic regret by adaptively tracking bτ/τ in the LP relaxation; ALP idea is intuitive and related in spirit to BwK/RCB relaxations.

- Theoretical quality: clean LP decomposition and concentration of bτ; O(1) non-boundary optimality gap is technically interesting; UCB-ALP learning term is standard.

- Experiments: limited discussion and empirical evaluation appears secondary.

- Clarity: generally well-written; suggest emphasizing early that ALP only needs ordering and give cleaner supplement statements in terms of arm gaps.

- Originality concern: with finite J,K and large T, coupling across contexts may vanish, reducing problem to JK-armed bandit with an activity constraint—making O(log T) unsurprising.

- Question why one cannot treat each context occurrence as an independent bandit with per-context budget share and run standard UCB per context; request formal comparison or counterexample.

- Clarify boundary-case regret: is the √T term truly independent of K, or should it be O(√(K T)) when learning is active?

- Request instance-dependent bounds in terms of action gaps Δj,k and context threshold gap Δctx instead of crude JK prefactors.

- Suggest reworking analysis to count pulls of each suboptimal action and only account for context-ranking mistakes near the LP threshold to yield a sum-of-inverse-gaps form.

- Ask for lower bounds: problem-dependent Ω(Σ(log T)/Δj,k plus threshold term) and problem-independent nontrivial Ω(√T) or Ω(√(K T)) capturing context and budget coupling.

- “Coupling vanishes” claim needs a precise statement: specify necessary concentration rates and conditions, and address heterogeneous-cost extensions.

- EALP analysis for unknown context distribution appears ad hoc; quantify bias from stopping updates and show fully online UCB-EALP can match rates.

- Positioning vs RCB/BwK and budgeted MABs needs clarification: explain why result is not simply running UCB over JK context-action arms with an activity constraint.

- Strengths: simple, efficient algorithms; neat concentration argument yielding small oracle gap; achieves O(log T) in a constrained contextual setting; thoughtful extension discussion.

- Weaknesses: uncertainty on novelty relative to reductions; bounds are problem-independent and potentially loose; lack of matching lower bounds and some informal claims.

- Requested revisions: provide instance-dependent upper bounds, add matching lower bounds, clarify per-context reduction impossibility, justify √T dependence on K, and characterize heterogeneous-cost instance-dependent bounds.

- Overall evaluation: borderline — well-executed and potentially useful but originality unclear; a convincing rebuttal and tightened analysis could change the recommendation.
