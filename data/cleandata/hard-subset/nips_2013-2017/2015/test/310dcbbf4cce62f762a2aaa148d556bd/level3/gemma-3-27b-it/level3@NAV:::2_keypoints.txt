- Tackles constrained contextual bandits (budget and time limits), a practically important problem.  
- Main contribution: algorithms claiming O(log T) regret, asserted as first such result for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Core technique: approximate oracle via adaptive linear programming (ALP) combined with UCB to form UCB-ALP.  
- Theoretical analysis appears solid with convincing proofs (not fully vetted by reviewer).  
- Assumptions: finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget constraint.  
- Novel adaptive LP relaxation is key to theoretical guarantees.  
- Extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs included.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates contributions within related work.  
- Major weakness: no experimental validation; lack of empirical results undermines practical assessment.  
- Sections 5 and 6 (extensions) are thin on detail and practical insight.  
- Strong simplifying assumptions may limit robustness and applicability in real-world scenarios.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding at least limited experimental validation to demonstrate practical viability.
