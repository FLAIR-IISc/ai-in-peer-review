- Paper studies contextual bandits with time and hard budget constraints and proposes an LP relaxation, an adaptive LP (ALP) using remaining-average bτ/τ, and UCB-ALP for learning.  
- ALP approximates the dynamic-programming oracle and is claimed to be near-optimal with O(1) regret in non-boundary regimes and O(√T) on measure-zero boundary cases.  
- Combining ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) yields claimed regret O(JK log T) in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) on the boundary.  
- Main novelty claimed: adaptive LP relaxation enabling instance-dependent, logarithmic regret for finite contexts/actions with unit or heterogeneous costs.  
- Strength: clear modeling of budget-constrained contextual bandits with practical applications (clinical trials, recommendation, wireless).  
- Strength: ALP is simple, computationally efficient, and achieves a tight O(1) gap from the oracle; hypergeometric concentration argument is neat.  
- Strength: UCB-ALP cleanly separates estimation and allocation by leveraging only reward orderings.  
- Strength: Extensions to unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs indicate robustness.  
- Weakness: Need formalization of the claimed “decoupling” — budget imposes global coupling and the paper should make precise in what sense coupling vanishes.  
- Weakness: Clarify why the problem is not reducible to J independent bandits plus a shared budget controller and where cross-context dependencies enter the regret analysis.  
- Weakness: Sharpen comparisons to prior work (BwK, RCB, concave/convex knapsack bandits) and explain what structural trick yields O(log T) vs prior O(√T).  
- Weakness: Provide gap-dependent upper bounds (e.g., Σ (log T)/Δj,k) and isolate threshold-gap/ slack terms instead of the coarse JK log T bound.  
- Weakness: Provide matching lower bounds — problem-dependent (Ω(Σ (log T)/Δj,k)) and problem-independent (Ω(√T) at boundary) — and clarify J,K dependence.  
- Weakness: Clarify Theorem 2 scaling: whether the √T term depends on J,K or margin parameters and where it arises from.  
- Weakness: The ordering-events analysis may be loose; replace JK log T counting with per-pair gap-based counts and isolate threshold-crossing mistakes.  
- Weakness: Boundary cases can be frequent in discrete settings; discuss practical mitigations (randomizing ρ, smoothing) and high-probability bounds.  
- Weakness: Move concrete finite-time guarantees for unknown context distribution into the main text with explicit constants and parameters.  
- Weakness: Discuss computational complexity of heterogeneous-cost LP per round and whether ε-first exploration can be avoided using UCB-style confidence sets.  
- Quality: Analysis appears technically sound but current bounds are mostly problem-independent and potentially loose.  
- Clarity: Recommend an upfront “decoupling via concentration” lemma and clearer, earlier comparison to prior BwK/RCB works; move key Section 5 results into main paper.  
- Originality: Adaptive-LP approach and its near-oracle/logarithmic regret claims appear new but novelty relative to straightforward reductions to sleeping JK-armed bandits is not fully convincing.  
- Significance: If tightened (gap-dependent bounds, lower bounds, clarified scaling), the simple, efficient algorithm would be a valuable practical contribution.  
- Recommendation: Neutral — encourage authors to address novelty positioning, provide gap-dependent and lower bounds, clarify Theorem 2, and strengthen unknown-context and heterogeneous-cost analyses.
