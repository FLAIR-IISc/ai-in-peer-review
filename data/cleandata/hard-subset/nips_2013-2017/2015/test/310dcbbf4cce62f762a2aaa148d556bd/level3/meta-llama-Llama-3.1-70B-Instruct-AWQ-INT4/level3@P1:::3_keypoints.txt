- Problem setup: contextual bandit with budget B and known horizon T, costly actions or skip, stochastic rewards in [0,1].
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm for known context distribution and unit costs achieving logarithmic regret under a mild condition.
- Generalization: extensions to unknown context distribution π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.
- Novelty: achieves logarithmic regret for budgeted contextual bandits, improving on prior O(√T) results.
- Method: uses a simple, interesting approximation algorithm for the offline optimal action sequence.
- Analysis: thorough theoretical analysis with a proof of the logarithmic regret bound; paper is well-written.
- Key technical reliance: results depend heavily on Lemma 1 (initially doubted but validated by rebuttal).
- Limitation: the logarithmic regret guarantee requires a mild condition that may not always hold.
- Limitation: heavy dependence on Lemma 1 may limit generalizability.
- Open question: whether computing the exact optimal action sequence is NP-hard remains unresolved.
- Open question: unclear if results extend to unknown horizon T.
- Assumption concern: the claim that u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J can be assumed without loss of generality in the unknown-π case may not always hold.
- Strengths summary: novel algorithm, strong theoretical guarantees, clear motivation and presentation.
- Weaknesses summary: condition-dependence, Lemma reliance, and potentially unjustified assumption in unknown-π case.
- Recommendation: strongly recommended for acceptance as a significant contribution with practical implications.
