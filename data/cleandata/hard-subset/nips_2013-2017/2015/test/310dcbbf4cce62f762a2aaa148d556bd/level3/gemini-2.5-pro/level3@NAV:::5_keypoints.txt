- Addresses budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions, where decisions are coupled over time by resource constraints.
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm combining an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) oracle approximation with UCB exploration.
- Achieves near-optimal O(log T) regret in most settings, improving on prior O(sqrt(T)) results for resourceful bandits.
- Theoretical advance is significant and the algorithm is computationally efficient.
- Key technical insight: adaptive LP relaxation decouples decision-making from information acquisition.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and the analysis is accessible.
- Clear progression from known-statistics ALP to unknown-statistics UCB-ALP strengthens the results.
- Primary weakness: absence of numerical experiments to validate practical performance and regret tightness.
- Suggestion: make the Section 4.1 rationale more explicit by referencing optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty and connections to UCRL2.
- Suggestion: discuss scalability to many contexts, e.g., extensions to parametric reward models.
- Suggestion: cite related work by Agrawal et al. (arXiv:1506.03374) on contextual bandits with global constraints.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper that merits acceptance; minor weaknesses can be addressed in revision.
