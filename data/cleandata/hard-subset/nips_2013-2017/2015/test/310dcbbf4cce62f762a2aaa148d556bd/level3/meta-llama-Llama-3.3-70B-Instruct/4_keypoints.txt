- Presents a novel framework for robustness analysis in iterative stochastic policy optimization problems.  
- Derives a concentration-of-measure inequality to compute probabilistic bounds on expected cost and constraint-violation probability.  
- Provides high-confidence guarantees for future performance and safety of control algorithms.  
- Introduces a unique experimental setup analyzing contextual bandits against a stronger baseline that uses adaptive linear programming on suboptimal rounds.  
- Proposes a robust estimation technique to handle potentially unbounded change-of-measure likelihood ratios from iterative policy adaptation.  
- Applicable to a wide range of stochastic decision-making problems, with potential impact on robust model-predictive control.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with a simple illustrative example and reproducible technical detail.  
- Technically sound theoretical analysis and supportive experimental results.  
- Originality: combines concentration-of-measure inequalities with robust estimation for robustness analysis.  
- Significant practical relevance for safety-critical domains (robotics, autonomous systems, finance, healthcare).  
- Weakness: special treatment of boundary cases (particularly when rho = q_j) is under-explained and needs further justification.  
- Weakness: paper lacks detailed comparisons and explicit relationships to existing robustness-analysis methods.  
- Weakness: approach needs further development and evaluation on more complex stochastic decision-making problems.
