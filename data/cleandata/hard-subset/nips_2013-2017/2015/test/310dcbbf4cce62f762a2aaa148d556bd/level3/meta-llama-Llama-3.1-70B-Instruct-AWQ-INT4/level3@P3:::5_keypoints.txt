- Proposes an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions, with practical motivation and thorough related work.

- Main contribution: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that approximates the oracle (known-statistics) solution and only needs the ordering of expected rewards.

- Extends ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP for unknown rewards, achieving logarithmic regret in general.

- Notes boundary cases where regret degrades to O(√T).

- Paper is very well-written, logically organized, and easy to follow with consistent notation.

- Theoretical analysis is thorough and convincing, supporting the paper’s claims.

- Strength: efficient algorithm design tailored to constrained contextual bandits with near-optimal regret.

- Strength: provision of logarithmic regret bounds for the proposed approach.

- Strength: comprehensive literature review and clear explanations of methods.

- Weakness: assumption of discrete contexts and actions may limit applicability in many real-world settings.

- Weakness: boundary cases with O(√T) regret could be significant in practice.

- Weakness: lack of numerical experiments to validate theoretical results.

- Suggestions: consider parametric reward models for scalability with many contexts.

- Suggestions: clarify rationale for combining UCB with ALP, explicitly state optimism-in-face-of-uncertainty and relation to UCRL2 updates.

- Suggestions: include numerical experiments in an extended version and consider citing arXiv:1506.03374.

- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, well-supported contribution with minor weaknesses.

- Scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
