- Introduces ALP and UCB-ALP for constrained contextual bandits, achieving logarithmic regret in most cases.
- Significant theoretical advancement over prior work (e.g., Mixture Elimination for RCB) under simplified but practical assumptions.
- Clear, well-documented problem formulation, methodology, and theoretical results.
- Rigorous proofs supporting effectiveness of ALP and UCB-ALP.
- Novel: first work to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Practical relevance to domains like clinical trials and online recommendation systems.
- ALP is elegant, computationally efficient, and offers insights into decoupling exploration and exploitation under constraints.
- Properly situates contributions within related literature and cites relevant prior work.
- Lacks empirical validation and detailed discussion of practical applications, limiting assessment of real-world performance.
- Assumes simplified settings (fixed costs, known context distributions), which may limit generalizability.
- Mentions extensions to heterogeneous costs and unknown context distributions but does not explore them in depth.
- Recommendation: accept as a strong theoretical paper, with a suggestion to add experimental validation and more application discussion in future work.
