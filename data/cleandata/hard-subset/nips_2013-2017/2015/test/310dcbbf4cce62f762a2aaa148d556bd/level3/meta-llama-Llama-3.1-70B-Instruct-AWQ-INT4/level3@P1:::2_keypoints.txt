- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.  
- Proposes an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle that is near-optimal and tolerant to certain parameter estimation errors.  
- Introduces UCB-ALP, combining ALP with UCB, achieving O(log T) regret except for boundary cases where regret is O(√T).  
- Claims to be the first work obtaining logarithmic regret for this constrained contextual bandit problem.  
- Theoretical contributions are solid and proofs are convincing.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, well-documented, and cites relevant prior work.  
- Analysis is restricted to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Practical aspects and implementation details of the algorithms are thinly treated.  
- No experimental results or empirical evaluations are provided.  
- Lacks a comprehensive evaluation of algorithm performance across different scenarios.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, with a request for more experimental results and broader empirical evaluation.
