- Problem studied: contextual bandits with time horizon T and a hard budget B, creating coupling across rounds.  
- Method (known statistics): introduce LP relaxation replacing hard budget with average budget ρ=B/T and propose Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that uses remaining budget bτ/τ.  
- ALP performance: achieves O(1) regret to the oracle in non-boundary cases and O(√T) in boundary cases (measure zero).  
- Method (unknown rewards): UCB-ALP uses per (context,action) UCBs and solves ALP with optimistic estimates each round.  
- UCB-ALP regret: O(JK log T) in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.  
- Key analysis insight: remaining-budget evolution maps to sampling without replacement, enabling hypergeometric concentration around ρ.  
- Extensions sketched: unknown context distribution (UCB-EALP via empirical frequencies) and heterogeneous costs (ALP with per-context single-action constraint and ε-first learning).  
- Relation to prior work: contrasts with BwK/RCB which obtain O(√T) regret in more general settings; here discrete contexts/actions and fixed costs allow lighter algorithms and near-oracle O(log T) regret.  
- Clarifies prior static-budget UCB limits: shows why static budget approaches incur √T regret and why adaptive budget control is crucial for log regret.  
- Technical soundness: LP relaxation, ALP, hypergeometric mapping, and regret decomposition are well motivated and rigorously handled.  
- Assumptions: unique best action per context and strict ordering of context values; boundary cases are explicitly acknowledged.  
- Unknown-context analysis: reasonable via truncation and coupling, though some technical details are deferred.  
- Completeness/honesty: claims are properly qualified; lack of experiments noted as a gap.  
- Computational efficiency: decision step reduces to thresholding with O(J log J) or O(J) if incremental, per-step LP trivial.  
- Clarity: paper is well written, notation consistent, and proofs sketches convey main ideas.  
- Originality: ALP with adaptive budget and hypergeometric viewpoint are novel; first to get O(log T) regret under hard budget in this discrete setting.  
- Significance: practical, fast algorithm with near-optimal guarantees; modularity suggests applicability to other estimators/models.  
- Suggestion — scalability: consider parametric reward models (e.g., linear/GLM) to replace per-pair UCBs and analyze how estimation error affects ALP and regret.  
- Suggestion — clarify optimism: explicitly connect UCB+ALP to optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty and optimistic planning analogies (e.g., UCRL2).  
- Suggestion — experiments: add empirical results comparing ALP vs oracle/static-LP, UCB-ALP scaling, boundary behavior, and BwK/RCB baselines.  
- Suggestion — related work: discuss Agrawal–Devanur–Li papers more prominently, clarifying differences in assumptions and dynamic vs static contexts.  
- Suggestion — minor: state per-round complexity, justify exploration coefficient, and confirm concentration constants.  
- Pros: clear formulation, strong theory, novel ALP/hypergeometric analysis, computationally simple UCB-ALP, useful extensions, good exposition.  
- Cons: no experiments, restrictive discrete-context/action assumptions and tie conditions, boundary cases incur √T regret, scalability to large/continuous contexts not empirically addressed.  
- Questions for authors: can the boundary-case √T term be improved under margin/gap conditions?  
- Questions for authors: for unknown context distribution, can truncation be avoided in practice while preserving guarantees?  
- Questions for authors: are there lower bounds showing instance-optimality (up to constants) for discrete contexts/actions?  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution and clarity outweigh missing experiments; empirical section would strengthen the paper.
