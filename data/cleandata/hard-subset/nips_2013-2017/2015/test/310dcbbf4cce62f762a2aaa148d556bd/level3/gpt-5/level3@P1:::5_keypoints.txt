- Problem: Constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions and a hard budget over a finite horizon.  
- Main idea: Approximate the DP oracle by a linear program imposing an average budget constraint (Adaptive Linear Program, ALP).  
- Known-statistics result: ALP attains O(1) regret except at boundary parameter values (ρ equals a cumulative context mass), where regret is O(√T).  
- Unknown rewards: Combine ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to get O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) on boundaries.  
- Unknown context distribution: Addressed via empirical estimates and a truncated EALP analysis to handle dependence issues.  
- Heterogeneous costs: Extend to a generalized LP with single-action-per-context constraint and propose an exploration-first variant for unknown rewards.  
- Technical novelty: Mapping budget evolution to sampling-without-replacement (hypergeometric) for concentration is a key technical device.  
- Practical insight: ALP only requires reward orderings, reducing computational requirements compared to DP or policy-elimination approaches.  
- Improvement over prior work: Achieves logarithmic regret for random context arrivals, improving on prior O(√T) RCB results by exploiting discrete contexts and ALP structure.  
- Theoretical quality: Technical development is sound; LP upper bound and concentration-based ALP analysis are elegant.  
- Regret analysis: Clean decomposition isolating action-ranking vs. context-ranking errors in UCB-ALP.  
- Clarity: Well written and easy to follow; narrative from oracle approximation to learning is compelling.  
- Notational issues: Minor glitches (e.g., Eq. (4)) and boundary-case role could be highlighted earlier.  
- Computational concerns: Would like explicit constants and per-round complexity analysis for solving LPτ,b.  
- Missing experiments: No empirical validation; experiments would illustrate boundary behavior and comparisons to baselines.  
- Scalability concern: Discrete/tabular treatment may not scale when J, K are large; structured models could help.  
- Heterogeneous-cost limitation: Exploration-first approach may be suboptimal and lacks stronger adaptive regret guarantees.  
- Suggestions: Clarify optimism principle behind UCB-ALP, add per-round complexity/tie-handling discussion, propose mitigation for boundary cases, consider structured reward models, and include numerical experiments.  
- Minor suggestions: Tighten notation in Eq. (4) and consider moving core concentration lemma proof sketch into main text.  
- Recommendation: Accept — computationally efficient approach with strong theoretical guarantees and high confidence from the reviewer.
