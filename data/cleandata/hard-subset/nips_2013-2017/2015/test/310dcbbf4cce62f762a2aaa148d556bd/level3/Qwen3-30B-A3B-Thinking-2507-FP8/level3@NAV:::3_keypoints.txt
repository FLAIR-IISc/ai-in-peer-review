- Addresses contextual bandits with budget constraints, modeling real-world resources (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing).
- Proposes UCB-ALP algorithm combining UCB with an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation.
- ALP approximation tolerates estimation errors and links linear programming to bandit exploration.
- Achieves O(log T) regret for unit-cost systems with known context distribution under a mild non-boundary condition.
- Shows ALP attains O(1) regret except in boundary cases.
- Prior work only achieved O(√T); this is the first to establish logarithmic regret in this setting.
- Provides rigorous analysis handling interactions among budget constraints, context arrivals, and exploration.
- Clarity and motivation are strong, with well-structured exposition of challenges and techniques.
- Dependence on the non-boundary condition is the main limitation; boundary cases require O(√T) regret.
- Lemma 1 concerns were addressed and justified in the rebuttal.
- Properly handles the assumption u^1 > ... > u^*_J in the known-context case.
- Algorithms are computationally efficient, enhancing practical relevance.
- Leaves extensions to unknown horizon T as an open direction for future work.
- Highly original, technically sound, and recommended for acceptance.
