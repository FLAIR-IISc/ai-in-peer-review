- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with budget/time constraints, proposing ALP for known statistics and ALP+UCB for unknown statistics, claiming O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases.
- Main technical contribution is the ALP approximation for the oracle, claimed to yield near-optimality with O(1) regret.
- Novelty is questionable: problem can be seen as a time/budgeted bandit with JK arms and action constraints, so contextual coupling vanishes for large T (Sec. 3.2).
- Logarithmic regret result is unsurprising and a natural extension of known budgeted bandit results (e.g., prior work [12,17]).
- Technical concern 1: problem-dependent upper bound in the supplement should be expressed in terms of arm gaps (sum of inverse gaps) rather than involving a multiplicative KJ factor.
- Technical concern 2: Theorem 2’s regret term stated as O(√T + ...) should more correctly be O(√(KT) + ...) per standard bandit analysis.
- Technical concern 3: Paper lacks matching lower bounds that account for context complexity beyond trivial reductions to classical bandits.
- Claiming to be first to achieve logarithmic regret appears overstated given connections to simpler, existing problems.
- Strengths: clear problem formulation and a well-structured algorithmic approach.
- Main weakness: insufficient evidence of originality; results appear to be a straightforward extension of existing techniques.
- Recommendations: clarify fundamental differences from prior budgeted/contextual bandit work, rework problem-dependent bounds using arm gaps, and provide nontrivial lower bounds before acceptance.
