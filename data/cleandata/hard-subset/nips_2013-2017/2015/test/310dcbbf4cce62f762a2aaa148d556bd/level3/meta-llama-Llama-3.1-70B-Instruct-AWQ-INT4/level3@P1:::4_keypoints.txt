- Problem studied: constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.  
- Main algorithm: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that relaxes hard budget to average budget to approximate the oracle.  
- Performance of ALP: near-optimal, with regret within a constant of the optimum except for certain boundary cases.  
- Handling unknown rewards: UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB to learn expected rewards.  
- Performance of UCB-ALP: achieves logarithmic regret generally, sublinear regret in boundary cases.  
- Strength: novel and efficient approach for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Strength: ALP empirically/theoretically achieves near-optimal regret.  
- Strength: UCB-ALP handles unknown rewards and attains strong regret bounds.  
- Weakness: unclear analysis of boundary cases, specifically why ρ = q_j is special for j > 1.  
- Weakness: comparison uses a strong baseline that skips a fixed percent of suboptimal rounds, possibly unfair.  
- Weakness: model assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget) may limit real-world applicability.  
- Originality: novel use of adaptive linear programming and its combination with UCB.  
- Significance: results have practical implications (clinical trials, recommendations, crowdsourcing).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear explanations and well-defined notation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — clarify boundary-case analysis, justify baseline comparison, and discuss limitations/extensions.
