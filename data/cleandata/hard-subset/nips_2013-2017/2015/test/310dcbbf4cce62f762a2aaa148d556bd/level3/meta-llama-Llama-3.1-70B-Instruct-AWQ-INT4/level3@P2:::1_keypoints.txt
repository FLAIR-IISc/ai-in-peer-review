- Problem studied: constrained contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T, maximize reward under budget/time limits.
- Main algorithm: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that needs only ordering of expected rewards and is near-optimal.
- ALP regret: achieves O(1) regret except boundary cases where regret is O(√T).
- Unknown-reward algorithm: UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB to handle unknown rewards.
- UCB-ALP regret: achieves logarithmic regret for non-boundary cases.
- Regret analysis: partitioned into action-ranking errors and budget/context-fluctuation errors; shows E[T(s)] = O(JK log T).
- Non-boundary regret bound: UCB-ALP regret shown to be O(JK log T).
- Extensions: handles unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs via EALP (empirical πj) and τ-First ALP (explore-then-exploit).
- Strengths: ALP’s minimal information requirement and near-optimality, plus the UCB-ALP combination and thorough theoretical analysis.
- Novelty concern: reviewer views problem as reducible to a time/budgeted bandit with JK arms and action constraints, questioning originality.
- Theorem-rate concern: reviewer doubts Theorem 2’s rate, expecting O(√(KT)+…) rather than O(√T+…).
- Problem-dependent bounds concern: reviewer argues bounds should use sums of inverse arm gaps instead of a multiplicative JK factor.
- Proof-method concern: reviewer questions the reliance on UCB ordering-event-based proofs and asks for cleaner arguments.
- Technical fixes requested: rework supplementary-material bounds and provide a clean problem-dependent upper bound in terms of arm gaps.
- Overall verdict: neutral recommendation pending clarification of novelty and stronger problem-dependent/ lower-bound results.
