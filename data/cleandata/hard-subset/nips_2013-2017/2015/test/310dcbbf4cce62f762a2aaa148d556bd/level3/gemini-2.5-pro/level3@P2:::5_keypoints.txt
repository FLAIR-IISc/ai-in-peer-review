- Problem: Studies constrained contextual bandits with both time horizon T and budget B, coupling decisions over time via a shared budget.  
- Main contribution: Proposes UCB-ALP, the first computationally efficient algorithm achieving O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases.  
- Oracle simplification: Analyzes known-distribution (oracle) case and shows optimal DP is intractable.  
- ALP idea: Introduces Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that solves a simple per-step LP using remaining-budget-to-time ratio as the constraint.  
- ALP performance: Proves ALP is near-optimal, with only a constant or O(√T) gap to the true optimum in the oracle setting.  
- UCB-ALP algorithm: Combines ALP with UCB reward estimates to handle unknown expected rewards.  
- Regret improvement: Shows UCB-ALP attains O(log T) regret, improving over prior O(√T) results (e.g., Badanidiyuru et al.).  
- Technical analysis: Uses nontrivial tools like mapping budget evolution to sampling-without-replacement and decomposing regret sources.  
- Clarity: Manuscript is exceptionally well-written and structured, building logically from ALP to UCB-ALP.  
- Significance & originality: Addresses an important variant and introduces a novel adaptive LP relaxation for DP in bandits.  
- Scalability concern: Analysis assumes finite/discrete contexts and actions, limiting applicability to high-dimensional or continuous contexts.  
- Conceptual motivation: Could better link UCB+ALP to broader RL principles (e.g., optimism in face of uncertainty, UCRL2-style optimism).  
- Lack of experiments: Purely theoretical work; empirical validation would strengthen practical claims and demonstrate observed regret.  
- Related work: Positions well relative to prior art and recent preprints, with helpful discussion/footnote.  
- Acceptance case: First near-optimal algorithm, novel and efficient framework, rigorous analysis, and clear presentation argue for acceptance.  
- Rejection concerns: Limited applicability due to finite-context assumption and absence of empirical evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance.
