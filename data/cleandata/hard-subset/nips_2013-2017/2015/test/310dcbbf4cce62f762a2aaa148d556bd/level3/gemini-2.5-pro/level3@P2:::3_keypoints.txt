- Problem: Studies contextual bandits with both time horizon T and budget B, introducing complex temporal resource constraints.  
- Main contribution: Proposes UCB-ALP algorithm that attains O(log T) regret, improving on prior O(√T) results.  
- Method (oracle): ALP (Adaptive Linear Programming) approximates the DP by solving an LP adapting to remaining budget/time, achieving O(1) regret in most cases.  
- Method (practical): Combines ALP with UCB estimation (UCB-ALP) to learn unknown rewards while making near-optimal budget-aware decisions.  
- Extensions: Discusses handling unknown context distributions and heterogeneous action costs.  
- Strength — significance/originality: First to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits and decouples learning and planning via ALP.  
- Strength — technical quality: ALP relies only on reward ordering (robust to estimation errors); analysis uses concentration of remaining budget and is technically sound.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written, logically organized, and presents simple, intuitive algorithms.  
- Weakness — conditional guarantee: O(log T) bound holds only in non-boundary cases; boundary cases revert to O(√T), weakening universality of the result.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, efficient, and elegant algorithm with strong theoretical improvement and clear presentation.  
- Argument against acceptance: Regret guarantee is not universal due to boundary-case degradation.  
- Request for authors: Clarify whether computing the optimal DP policy is NP-hard to strengthen motivation for the LP approximation.  
- Request for authors: Provide intuition for Lemma 1 in the main text, as it is central to the analysis.  
- Request for authors: Confirm and clarify that contexts are ordered by current UCB estimates in the online unknown-context-distribution setting.  
- Request for authors: Discuss challenges and possible extensions to unknown or infinite time-horizon settings.
