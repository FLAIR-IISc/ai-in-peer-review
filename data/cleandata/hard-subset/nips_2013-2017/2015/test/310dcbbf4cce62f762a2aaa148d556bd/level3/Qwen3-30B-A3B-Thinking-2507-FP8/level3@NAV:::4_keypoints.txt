- Studies constrained contextual bandits with time and budget constraints and proposes algorithms achieving logarithmic regret in non-boundary cases.  
- Introduces Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) oracle for known statistics that attains O(1) regret except for boundary cases where it is O(√T).  
- Proposes UCB-ALP (combining ALP with UCB) achieving O(JK log T) regret for non-boundary (ρ ≠ q_j) and O(√T + JK log T) for boundary cases.  
- Represents a significant improvement over prior work (e.g., [17] and extensions of [20]) which only achieve O(√T) regret and/or use more restrictive benchmarks and higher computational cost.  
- Strong technical quality with rigorous analysis and novel concentration properties for the adaptive budget constraint.  
- Key insight: ALP requires only reward ordering (not exact values), enabling an elegant UCB combination.  
- Presentation is clear and well-structured, with careful distinction between boundary and non-boundary cases and appropriate references.  
- Originality: first work to show logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits under the considered assumptions.  
- High practical significance for budget-constrained applications (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing) with theoretical guarantees and practical algorithmic insights.  
- Primary weakness: boundary case analysis (ρ = q_j) is under-justified, especially why cases with j > 1 are special and whether they are a meaningful fraction of the parameter space.  
- Secondary concern: comparison to an ALP-based baseline may be misleading since the baseline already incorporates similar techniques.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
