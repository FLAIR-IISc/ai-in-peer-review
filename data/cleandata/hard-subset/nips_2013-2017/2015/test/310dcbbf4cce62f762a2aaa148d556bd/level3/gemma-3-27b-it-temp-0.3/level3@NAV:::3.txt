## Review of "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, a setting highly relevant to numerous real-world applications. The authors make a significant contribution by presenting algorithms that achieve logarithmic regret under certain conditions, a substantial improvement over the previously known √T regret bounds for this problem. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly motivated, making it easy to follow the complex ideas presented. 

The core idea is to approximate the optimal policy using adaptive linear programming (ALP), which is then combined with an upper confidence bound (UCB) approach for the unknown reward case. This approach is particularly interesting due to its simplicity and the fact that it only requires ordering the expected rewards. The extension to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs further strengthens the work. The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature, highlighting the limitations of prior approaches like the Mixture Elimination algorithm [17] and demonstrating a clear advancement in achievable regret bounds.

A key strength is the thoroughness of the analysis. The authors carefully consider boundary cases where the logarithmic regret guarantee doesn't hold, providing a clear understanding of the algorithm's limitations. The technical details are well-explained, and the reliance on Lemma 1, initially a concern, was convincingly justified in the rebuttal. 

However, the logarithmic regret guarantee *does* rely on a mild condition, and the Ω(√T) lower bound in boundary cases is a notable limitation. While acknowledged by the authors, stronger universal guarantees would significantly enhance the paper's impact.  Several open questions are raised, including the computational complexity of finding the exact optimal action sequence (NP-hardness?) and the possibility of extending the results to unknown horizons.  The assumption u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J in the unknown context distribution case also feels somewhat restrictive and warrants further discussion regarding its necessity.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Achieves O(log T) regret, a significant improvement over existing work.
*   **Significance:** Addresses a practically important problem with a strong theoretical foundation.
*   **Clarity:** Exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.
*   **Technical Soundness:** Rigorous analysis with careful consideration of limitations.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   **Conditional Guarantee:** Logarithmic regret relies on a mild condition.
*   **Model Assumptions:** The assumption on the ordering of expected rewards in the unknown context case.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Strongly recommend acceptance.** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of constrained contextual bandits. The clear writing, strong theoretical analysis, and significant improvement in regret bounds outweigh the limitations. The open questions raised by the authors also provide avenues for future research.
