- Novel algorithm for contextual bandits with budget and time constraints achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.
- Problem setup: constrained contextual bandits with known horizon T and budget B; actions have costs and stochastic rewards.
- Approach: ALP for unit-cost/known-distribution case; UCB-ALP for unknown context distributions and arbitrary costs.
- Claimed novelty: first to achieve O(log T) regret in this problem setting, improving over prior O(√T) results.
- Paper is very well-written, clearly motivated, and uses consistent, helpful notation.
- Technical analysis is rigorous, building logically from known-statistics to unknown cases and leveraging standard UCB results.
- Methodological insight: approximating the offline oracle via linear programming and adapting it with UCB is clever and broadly applicable.
- Comprehensive treatment includes extensions, boundary cases, and discussion of limitations.
- Main weakness: O(log T) guarantee depends on a condition; if violated, lower bound becomes Ω(√T).
- Concern about Lemma 1’s correctness was raised (later addressed by rebuttal), suggesting the lemma needs clearer/intuitive presentation.
- Computational complexity analysis (particularly LP-solving costs) is underdeveloped.
- Empirical evaluation is limited or absent; simulations/experiments would strengthen the claims.
- Originality: high — novel combination of adaptive LP and UCB under budget constraints yielding improved regret.
- Significance: strong practical relevance for online advertising, clinical trials, and resource allocation; opens avenues for future research.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical improvement, clear/technical soundness, novel and practically relevant approach.
- Arguments against acceptance: conditional nature of the main guarantee and lack of extensive empirical validation.
- Open questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard; can results extend to unknown horizon T; necessity of ordering assumption in unknown-context case.
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
