## Review of "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions, a relevant extension of standard contextual bandit models with practical applications in resource allocation scenarios. The authors make a significant contribution by developing a computationally efficient algorithm, UCB-ALP, and proving a logarithmic regret bound – a first for this problem setting. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and highlights the limitations of existing approaches. The progression from analyzing the oracle with LP approximation (ALP) to extending it to the unknown reward case (UCB-ALP) is logical and well-explained. The mathematical derivations appear sound, and the notation is consistent throughout. The authors appropriately position their work within the existing literature, referencing key papers in contextual bandits and constrained optimization. 

A key strength lies in the adaptive nature of the proposed ALP algorithm, which intelligently adjusts to the remaining budget and time. This allows for a more nuanced exploration-exploitation trade-off than fixed-constraint approaches. The analysis, while complex, is clearly presented and leverages techniques like concentration inequalities and bounded differences effectively.

However, the paper would benefit from several extensions. The authors acknowledge the limitation to discrete contexts and actions. Exploring parametric reward models, as suggested by the supervisor, would be a valuable direction for future work, enabling scalability to scenarios with numerous contexts. Furthermore, the rationale for combining UCB with ALP in Section 4.1 could be more explicitly articulated. A clearer connection to the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle and a direct comparison to UCRL2 policy updates would strengthen the argument. Finally, including numerical experiments, even on a small scale, would provide empirical validation of the theoretical results and enhance the paper's impact. Referencing arXiv:1506.03374, as suggested, would also be beneficial.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Provides a novel and efficient algorithm (UCB-ALP) for constrained contextual bandits.
*   Achieves a logarithmic regret bound, a significant improvement over existing results.
*   The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and technically sound.
*   Addresses a practically relevant problem with potential for real-world impact.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited to discrete contexts and actions.
*   Lacks empirical validation through numerical experiments.
*   The connection between UCB and ALP could be more thoroughly explained.

**Overall Recommendation:**

Despite the minor weaknesses, this paper represents a significant advancement in the field of constrained contextual bandits. The theoretical contributions are substantial, and the paper is of high quality. I **recommend acceptance** with the suggestion that the authors address the points raised above in an extended version.
