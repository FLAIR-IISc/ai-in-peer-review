- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions, a meaningful extension of standard formulations.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP, a computationally efficient algorithm with a proven logarithmic regret bound.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation and precise problem definition.  
- Technical exposition is rigorous and accessible, with a gradual, effective buildup from oracle approximations to the full algorithm.  
- Establishes strong connections to prior work in contextual bandits and knapsack problems.  
- Core contribution is the Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) technique that approximates the oracle and adapts constraints based on remaining budget.  
- Combining ALP with UCB yields a practical, theoretically sound algorithm; analysis is robust and handles boundary cases.  
- Suggestion: consider parametric reward models to improve scalability when contexts are numerous or high-dimensional.  
- Suggestion: clarify the rationale in Section 4.1 for combining UCB with ALP, explicitly explaining UCB’s “optimism” and links to algorithms like UCRL2.  
- Major weakness: lacks numerical experiments and empirical validation of practical performance.  
- Inclusion of arXiv:1506.03374 in related work is a useful citation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and efficient algorithm, near-optimal regret, excellent writing, and rigorous analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: absence of empirical results, potential scalability issues, and unclear UCB–ALP connection.  
- Overall verdict: significant advance in constrained contextual bandits that merits conference acceptance despite minor improvements needed.
