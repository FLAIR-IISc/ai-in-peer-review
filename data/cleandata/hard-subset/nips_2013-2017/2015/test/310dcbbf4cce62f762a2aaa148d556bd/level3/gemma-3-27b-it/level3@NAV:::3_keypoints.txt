- Tackles contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, a practically relevant and challenging setting.  
- Main contribution: algorithms achieving logarithmic regret under specific mild conditions, improving on prior O(√T) bounds.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.  
- Core technique: approximate the oracle via adaptive linear programming (ALP) by relaxing hard budget into an average constraint.  
- Extension to unknown rewards via UCB-ALP strengthens the approach for realistic settings.  
- Analysis is complex but appears rigorous and supported by detailed proofs in the supplement.  
- Method further extended to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, showing versatility.  
- Limitation: logarithmic regret holds only under a “mild condition”; violating it yields an Ω(√T) lower bound.  
- Initial concern about reliance on Lemma 1 was raised but convincingly addressed in the rebuttal.  
- Open question: computational complexity of finding the exact optimal action sequence (possible NP-hardness) is not discussed.  
- Open question: handling unknown horizon T and the ordered-expected-rewards assumption merits future work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty (first O(log T) in this setting), significance, technical quality, and clarity.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited generality due to conditional nature of the logarithmic guarantee.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance despite conditional limitation.
