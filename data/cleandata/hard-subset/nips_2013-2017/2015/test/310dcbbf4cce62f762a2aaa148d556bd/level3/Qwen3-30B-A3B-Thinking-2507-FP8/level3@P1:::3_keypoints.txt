- Problem: Tackles constrained contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints.  
- Main contribution: Proposes UCB-ALP, the first algorithm achieving O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Key insight: Uses Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) oracle approximation that needs only ordering of expected rewards, decoupling exploration and exploitation.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides rigorous regret analysis for known and unknown statistics, with careful handling of boundary cases and a justified Lemma 1.  
- Generality and robustness: Extends to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs; algorithms are computationally efficient and implementable.  
- Originality and significance: Novel approach with substantial practical implications (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing, recommendations).  
- Limitation: Logarithmic regret requires a non-boundary condition; boundary cases revert to O(√T) but are well-characterized and measure-zero.  
- Minor gaps: Could briefly address unknown time horizon T; the unique-best-action ordering assumption is standard and acceptable.  
- Evaluation: Quality — Strong; Clarity — Excellent; Originality — High; Significance — High; strongly recommended for acceptance.
