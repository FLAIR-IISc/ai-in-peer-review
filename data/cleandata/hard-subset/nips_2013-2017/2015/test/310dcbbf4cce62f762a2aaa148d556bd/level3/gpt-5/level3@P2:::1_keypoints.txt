- Paper studies contextual bandits with a single knapsack resource and proposes ALP (known params) and UCB-ALP (unknown rewards) with claims: O(1) regret off-boundary, O(√T) on boundary, and O(JK log T) for learning.
- Relation to prior work: builds on BwK/RCB literature but claims improved instance-dependent (log T) regret in a discrete, computationally light setting.
- Strength: clear algorithmic idea using adaptive LP and a sampling-without-replacement view yielding budget concentration and tractable benchmark v(ρ).
- Strength: careful ALP analysis with clean O(1) non-boundary guarantees and explanation for O(√T) boundary behavior.
- Strength: computational efficiency—per-round LP simplifies to sorting and thresholding.
- Strength: plausible extensions outlined for unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs.
- Weakness: Upper bounds are largely problem-independent (O(JK log T)) and lack clean gap-dependent, context-frequency-dependent forms (e.g., ∑1/Δ).
- Weakness: No matching lower bounds provided, leaving tightness of O(JK log T) and boundary O(√T) unclear.
- Weakness: The √T boundary term needs clarification—possible hidden dependence on J, K, π, and margin δ is not made explicit.
- Weakness: Unknown-context (EALP) analysis is only sketched; explicit bounds and dependence on πmin, J, and truncation are missing.
- Clarity issue: Strong assumptions (strict ordering, unique best actions) and tie handling are not addressed; bounds under small margins are unclear.
- Clarity issue: Constants and dependencies in O(1) regret (on δ and reward ranges) and complexity per round should be stated explicitly in the main paper.
- Originality concern: Novelty depends on restrictive setting (finite contexts/actions, single budget); need clearer argument why existing BwK/RCB cannot yield similar rates.
- Significance: Potentially useful if sharpened with instance-dependent upper and matching lower bounds; currently feels incremental.
- Rebuttal requests: Provide clean gap-dependent upper bounds that replace opaque JK factors with ∑(log T)/Δ terms and include f(π,δ).
- Rebuttal requests: State explicit dependence on πmin and show sample complexity scales like 1/πj for rare contexts.
- Rebuttal requests: Clarify and tighten Theorem 2’s boundary rate with explicit constants and parameter dependence (variance, δ, u* range).
- Rebuttal requests: Supply matching lower bounds both problem-dependent (Ω(∑(log T)/Δ)) and problem-independent (Ω(√T)) including context complexity factors.
- Rebuttal requests: Formalize why contexts are not independent bandit instances and quantify ALP’s budget-concentration effect and threshold-change probabilities.
- Rebuttal requests: For unknown context distribution, provide a full UCB-EALP theorem with explicit dependence on J, πmin, truncation schedule, and δ; discuss fully online estimation.
- Rebuttal requests: For heterogeneous costs, give a UCB-ALP-style algorithm (not just ETC) with log(T) regret off-boundary and clarify per-round LP complexity and threshold structure.
- Rebuttal requests: Empirically and theoretically compare against RCB, BwK, budgeted Thompson sampling, and static LP baselines across boundary/non-boundary and unknown-π regimes.
- Pros: simple, efficient framework; elegant near-oracle ALP analysis; potential to improve understanding of budgeted contextual bandits.
- Cons: insufficiently sharp problem-dependent guarantees, missing lower bounds, unclear novelty vs. BwK/RCB, and several technical gaps (unknown-π, boundary precision, πmin dependence).
- Overall recommendation: Borderline, leaning negative unless rebuttal supplies gap-dependent bounds with π/δ dependence, matching lower bounds, and clearer novelty positioning.
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
