- Problem: studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints where actions consume budget and process stops when budget or time runs out.  
- Oracle analysis: for unit-cost, finite discrete contexts with known distribution, introduce a static LP relaxation replacing hard budget by average budget B/T to upper-bound oracle reward.  
- ALP algorithm: Adaptive LP solves LP each round using current average remaining budget bτ/τ and maps budget evolution to sampling without replacement.  
- ALP regret: achieves O(1) regret vs. oracle in non-boundary cases, and O(√T) regret in boundary cases where ρ equals cumulative context probabilities.  
- UCB-ALP: combines ALP with UCB estimates for unknown rewards; ranks contexts by estimated best reward and runs ALP on estimates.  
- UCB-ALP regret: RUCB-ALP(T,B) = O(JK log T) in non-boundary cases, and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.  
- Analysis techniques: decomposes regret into action-ranking and context-ranking errors, uses UCB concentration and hypergeometric concentration for remaining budget.  
- Extensions: unknown context distribution via empirical estimates (EALP, UCB-EALP) with truncated updates to recover concentration guarantees.  
- Extensions: heterogeneous costs handled by extending LP to all context-action pairs with per-context single-action constraint; propose explore-then-ALP for unknown stats.  
- Novelty: adaptive use of remaining-budget ratio combined with UCB to obtain logarithmic regret in this model appears novel.  
- Relation to prior work: improves on RCB and primal-dual approaches which typically yield O(√T) regret or restrict policies; tailored to bandit-with-budget structure.  
- Technical soundness: main claims and proof sketches are convincing; LP bound and hypergeometric mapping are elegant and plausible.  
- Completeness: core results for unit costs and known π are fully developed; extensions sketched and deferred to supplementary material.  
- Missing details: some proofs deferred, need verification of constants, gap dependence, and exact JK log T coefficients.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; algorithms and LP threshold structure are clear.  
- Suggested clarifications: define "boundary cases" early, state computational cost per round, and explain truncation horizon choice for unknown π.  
- Strengths: clear formulation, simple/effective ALP design, strong concentration analysis, and meaningful theoretical gains.  
- Weaknesses: no experiments, restrictive assumptions (i.i.d. finite contexts, fixed costs, unique best action), and boundary cases unresolved with no lower bounds.  
- Questions for authors: request lower bounds for boundary cases, sensitivity to near-boundary ρ, practical truncation selection for unknown π, and runtime for heterogeneous-cost LP.  
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution likely acceptable despite lack of experiments; reviewer leans toward acceptance and requests empirical validation and clarifications.
