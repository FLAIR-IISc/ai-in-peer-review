

This paper addresses the challenging problem of budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions, presenting a computationally efficient algorithm with logarithmic regret. The authors make a significant contribution by showing how to achieve O(log T) regret for this problem, which is the first such result in the literature. Previous work on constrained bandits, such as Resourceful Contextual Bandits (RCB) [17], achieved only O(√T) regret, while non-contextual budget-constrained bandits have been studied extensively [11-16]. The key insight is using an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle solution, which achieves near-optimality with O(1) regret except for boundary cases, and then combining it with UCB for the unknown statistics case.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly structured, making it easy to follow the technical contributions. The authors provide rigorous regret analysis, demonstrating how the ALP approximation tolerates estimation errors, enabling the combination with UCB. The theoretical results are compelling, with the paper showing that UCB-ALP achieves O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases, which is order-optimal.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper would benefit from clarifying the rationale for combining UCB with ALP in Section 4.1 by explicitly stating how it implements the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle and relates to UCRL2 policy updates. Additionally, the authors should consider citing arXiv:1506.03374 [21], which extends related work to dynamic contexts. For scalability when contexts become numerous, the authors might explore parametric reward models, though this is reasonable to leave for future work. While the paper is strong theoretically, including numerical experiments in an extended version would further strengthen the practical relevance.

The work makes a clear contribution to the field by providing an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with near-optimal regret, advancing the state of the art significantly beyond previous approaches. The paper meets all four key criteria for quality: technically sound, clear, original, and significant.
