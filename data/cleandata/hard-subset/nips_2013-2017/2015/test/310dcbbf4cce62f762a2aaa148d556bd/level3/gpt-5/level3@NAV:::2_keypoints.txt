- Paper studies contextual bandits with time and budget constraints for finite discrete contexts, fixed per-action costs, and a single budget.
- Proposes an adaptive linear program (ALP) that enforces the budget on average via the remaining budget/time ratio.
- Shows ALP achieves near-oracle performance with O(1) regret off an LP upper bound, except measure-zero boundary cases (O(√T)).
- Combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to learn action rankings, proving O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) otherwise.
- Sketches extensions to unknown context distributions (empirical frequencies) and heterogeneous costs.
- Novelty: first to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits by leveraging finite discrete settings and adaptive LP relaxation.
- Technical quality: solid, carefully argued analyses; elegant mapping of remaining budget dynamics to sampling without replacement and resulting concentration bounds.
- Regret decomposition for UCB-ALP is well-motivated and appears sound (proofs not fully verified by reviewer).
- Clarity: paper is clear, organized, well referenced; suggests clarifying boundary-case measure-zero claim and surfacing which extension results are in main text vs. supplement.
- Requests reporting per-round LP complexity and constants in regret bounds.
- Originality: high — ALP construction and regret analysis are novel compared to prior BwK/RCB work.
- Significance: advances theory with computationally light algorithms but limited practical impact due to assumptions and no experiments.
- Pros for acceptance: first log-regret guarantees under clear assumptions, computationally efficient algorithms, strong positioning relative to related literature.
- Cons for acceptance: no experiments, thin practical treatment and boundary-case details, assumptions exclude multiple constraints/random costs/infinite contexts, some extensions deferred to supplement.
