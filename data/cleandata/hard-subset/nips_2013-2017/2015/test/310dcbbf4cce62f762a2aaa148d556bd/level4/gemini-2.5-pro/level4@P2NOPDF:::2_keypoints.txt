- Tackles contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints under simplified but justified assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed action costs, single budget).  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured with explicit motivation and thorough prior-work discussion.  
- Main technical contribution: an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.  
- Develops algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret — claimed first logarithmic regret result for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Proof sketches are convincing, though not every detail was verified by the reviewer.  
- Major limitation: emphasis on theory with minimal practical evaluation; Sections 5–6 discuss practical aspects only briefly.  
- No experimental results included to validate or demonstrate empirical performance.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical novelty and significance; more detailed practical discussion or experiments would strengthen the paper.
