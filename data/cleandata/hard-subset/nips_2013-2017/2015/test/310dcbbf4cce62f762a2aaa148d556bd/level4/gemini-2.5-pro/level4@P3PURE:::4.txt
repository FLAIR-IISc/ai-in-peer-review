This paper analyzes the contextual bandit problem against a stronger baseline that uses adaptive linear programming to pass on a percentage of suboptimal rounds, a concept the authors term a "budget." The problem formulation is interesting and the proposed solutions are unique.

My primary question concerns the analysis of the boundary cases. Why do these cases necessitate a separate treatment? It is not clear from the text why the condition ρ = q_j should be considered a special case, particularly for j > 1.
