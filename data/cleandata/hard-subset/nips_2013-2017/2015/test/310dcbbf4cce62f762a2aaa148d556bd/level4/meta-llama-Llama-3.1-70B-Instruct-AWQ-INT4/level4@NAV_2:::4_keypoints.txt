- Studies constrained contextual bandits with both budget B and time-horizon T, highlighting complex coupling across time.  
- Time and budget constraints complicate exploration–exploitation tradeoffs due to inter-temporal context coupling.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that approximates the oracle and only requires ordering of expected rewards, achieving near-optimality.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP (ALP + UCB) when expected rewards are unknown; achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases.  
- Differentiates from prior contextual/resourceful bandit work by focusing specifically on budget and time constraints.  
- Points out limitations of prior Mixture Elimination approach (high complexity, no logarithmic regret).  
- Strength: novel LP-based approximation of the oracle algorithm.  
- Strength: achieves logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.  
- Strength: provides thorough analysis and regret bounds for the proposed algorithms.  
- Weakness: boundary cases are treated differently but lack clear explanation for the different regret behavior.  
- Weakness: paper would benefit from more numerical simulations to validate empirical performance.  
- Weakness: assumes a known context distribution, which may be unrealistic in some settings.
