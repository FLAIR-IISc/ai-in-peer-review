- Setting similar to budgeted, time-constrained bandits; for large horizon context coupling vanishes and each context could be treated as a separate bandit instance.  
- Paper would be significantly strengthened by a clean problem-dependent upper bound expressed in terms of arm gaps (supplementary bound needs reworking) and matching lower bounds.  
- Key result: for finite context size J and action size K and large T, O(log T) regret is achievable — novel but unsurprising given reduction to JK arms.  
- Authors should clarify the paper’s novelty relative to action-/budget-constrained bandits in the rebuttal.  
- Concern 1: Theorem 2 Case 2 reports O(√T + …) regret — should this be at least O(√(K T) + …)?  
- Concern 2: Theorem 2’s bounds (O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T)) may be improvable to the usual sum-of-inverse-arm-gaps problem-dependent form; is the UCB-order-events proof technique optimal?  
- Concern 3: Lower bounds (problem-dependent and problem-independent) are missing; trivial classical lower bounds exist but should be refined to reflect context complexity.  
- Overall verdict: Setting closely resembles known budgeted/action-constrained bandits, originality is unconvincing, so reviewer maintains a neutral recommendation pending clarifications.
