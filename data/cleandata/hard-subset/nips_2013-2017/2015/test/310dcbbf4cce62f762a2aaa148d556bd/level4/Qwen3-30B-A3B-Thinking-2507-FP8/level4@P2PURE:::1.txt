

Review after rebuttal: I agree the setting is not identical, but it appears sufficiently similar to me: if the horizon is large enough, context coupling vanishes (and given this, I do not understand why each context instance cannot be treated as a separate bandit instance). Regarding the paper's contribution, it would be significantly strengthened by a clean problem-dependent upper bound in terms of arm gaps (the supplementary material's bound requires reworking), ideally accompanied by matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent cases.  

The authors address a budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandit problem. Their key result establishes that for finite context set size $J$ and action set size $K$, and sufficiently large time horizon $T$, $\mathcal{O}(\log T)$ regret is achievable—a novel result for contextual bandits. While the results are thorough and the writing precise, the findings are not highly surprising. Specifically, since $J$ and $K$ are finite, the problem reduces to a time-constrained, budgeted bandit with $JK$ arms (where only $K$ arms are accessible per context). Given the finite arm count, $\mathcal{O}(\log T)$ regret for large $T$ aligns with established results for action-constrained bandits. The authors should clarify the paper's novelty in the rebuttal.  

I have three specific concerns:  
1. In Theorem 2, Case 2 reports regret $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{T} + \cdots)$. Should this not be $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{KT} + \cdots)$ at minimum?  
2. The regret bounds in Theorem 2 are $\mathcal{O}(KJ \log T)$ or $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{T} + KJ \log T)$, depending on arm configuration. Problem-dependent bounds typically use the sum of inverse arm gaps; is a refined bound achievable here? While the proof relies on UCB order events rather than gap concentration, is this approach optimal?  
3. Lower bounds (problem-dependent and problem-independent) would strengthen the paper. Trivial lower bounds match classical bandits (cost $=0$, static context), but can they be refined to incorporate context complexity?  

The setting closely resembles a budgeted bandit with action constraints, and I remain unconvinced of the paper's originality. I therefore maintain a neutral recommendation pending clarification in the rebuttal.
