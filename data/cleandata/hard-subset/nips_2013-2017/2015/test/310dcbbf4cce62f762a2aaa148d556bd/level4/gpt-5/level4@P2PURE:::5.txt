This paper studies a relatively simple budget-constrained contextual bandit problem with discrete contexts and actions. Its main contribution is an algorithm that is computationally efficient—via a linear programming approximation—and achieves a log(T) regret bound. The paper is very well written and easy to follow.

Suggestions:
- For future work, consider adopting a parametric reward model, which is useful when the number of contexts is large.
- In Section 4.1, when combining UCB with ALP, clarify the rationale behind the UCB-ALP algorithm. My understanding is that it follows the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle, and it is probably equivalent to the policy update in UCRL2.
- It would be valuable to include numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper.
- You may want to cite this related work: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

Overall assessment: This paper merits acceptance because it provides an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with optimal regret in most cases. It is interesting and represents an improvement over existing work.
