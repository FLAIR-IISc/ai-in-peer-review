- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with finite discrete contexts, fixed (possibly heterogeneous) costs, single budget, proposing an adaptive LP (ALP) and UCB-ALP for unknown rewards.  
- Main theoretical results: ALP yields O(1) regret (O(√T) at measure-zero boundaries); UCB-ALP gives O(log T) regret (O(√T + log T) at boundaries).  
- Extensions sketched for unknown context distribution (EALP/UCB-EALP) and heterogeneous costs, with similar regret guarantees claimed.  
- Technical development is solid: LP relaxation as an upper bound, implementable policy, and convincing regret proofs handling budget-context coupling.  
- Analysis leverages concentration of remaining budget and UCB ordering to control coupling and randomness.  
- Writing and organization are clear; boundary cases are explained but need more intuition for practice.  
- Originality: novel adaptive LP tailored to remaining budget and integration with UCB; first to obtain logarithmic regret in this setting while remaining computationally efficient.  
- Significance: important advance—logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits and lightweight algorithms; techniques (e.g., sampling-without-replacement mapping) could generalize.  
- Major weakness: core results depend on simplified assumptions (finite contexts, single budget, known context distribution).  
- Boundary-case behavior (O(√T)) lacks practical diagnostics or mitigation strategies; more guidance needed.  
- Empirical evaluation is missing; simulations comparing to Mixture Elimination and knapsack baselines, and sensitivity analyses, are recommended.  
- Practical guidance is limited: provide per-round complexity, constants in regret bounds, and real-world mapping examples (e.g., clinical trials, ad allocation).  
- Recommendation: accept—strong theoretical contribution and novel ideas—conditional on adding empirical evaluation and more discussion on robustness and boundary handling.
