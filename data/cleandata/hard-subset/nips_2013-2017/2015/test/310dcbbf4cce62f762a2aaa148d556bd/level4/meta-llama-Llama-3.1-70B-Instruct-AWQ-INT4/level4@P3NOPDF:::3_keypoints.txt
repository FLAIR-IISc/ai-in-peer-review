- Paper studies contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T where actions incur known costs and skipping is allowed.
- Focuses on special case with known context distribution π and unit costs c{j,k}=1, presenting an algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild condition.
- Extends the algorithm to the general case with unknown π and arbitrary costs c{j,k}.
- Prior work [16] considers a more general setting but only achieves O(√T) regret; this paper may be first to achieve O(log T) under its condition.
- Algorithm is simple, interesting, and based on an approximation algorithm for the optimal action sequence when statistics are known.
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.
- Major caveat: the O(log T) regret guarantee depends on a mild condition; it would be stronger if it held universally.
- Suggested complementary result: prove an Ω(√T) lower bound for the boundary cases where the mild condition fails.
- Reviewer initially questioned the correctness of Lemma 1 but was convinced after the authors' rebuttal.
- Minor questions for future work: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard?
- Minor questions: can results be extended to unknown T, and can action-value ordering u^1>u^2>...>u^*_J be assumed without loss of generality in the unknown π case?
- Strong recommendation for acceptance.
