The paper "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits" presents a significant contribution to the field of contextual bandits by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle, which achieves O(log T) regret in the presence of budget and time constraints. The authors restrict their analysis to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint, which, although simplified, are reasonable assumptions in practice.

The paper is well-written, clear, and concise, with relevant work properly cited and motivation explicitly stated. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, including proofs that are convincing, although not all were checked in full detail. The practical aspects of the proposed algorithms are briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6, but no experimental results are provided.

The main contribution of the paper lies in the design and analysis of the adaptive linear programming (ALP) algorithm, which approximates the oracle and achieves near-optimal performance while tolerating certain estimation errors of system parameters. The authors also propose a UCB-ALP algorithm that combines ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method, achieving O(log T) regret except for certain boundary cases.

The paper's strengths include its solid theoretical foundation, clear presentation, and significant contribution to the field. However, the lack of experimental results and in-depth practical discussions may limit the paper's impact. Nevertheless, this is a good paper that makes appropriate simplified assumptions to obtain O(log T) regret algorithms, and its contributions are noteworthy.

To improve the paper, the authors could consider providing more practical discussions and experimental results to demonstrate the effectiveness of their proposed algorithms. Additionally, exploring the extension of their results to more general settings, such as infinite contexts or multiple budget constraints, could further enhance the paper's impact.
