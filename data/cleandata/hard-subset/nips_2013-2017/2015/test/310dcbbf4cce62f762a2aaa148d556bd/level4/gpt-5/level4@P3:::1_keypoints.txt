- Problem & regime: Studies contextual bandits with hard budget B and horizon T in the proportional regime B/T = ρ, approximating the DP oracle via an LP relaxation.  
- Core methods: Proposes a static LP benchmark Û(T,B)=T v(ρ) and an Adaptive LP (ALP) policy that uses instantaneous remaining-budget ratio bτ/τ.  
- Known-statistics results: ALP achieves O(1) regret off boundary ρ and O(√T) regret at boundary cases (ρ equals certain cumulative context probabilities).  
- Unknown rewards: Combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to get O(JK log T) off boundary and O(√T + JK log T) on boundary.  
- Extensions: Sketches handling unknown context distributions (empirical estimates) and heterogeneous costs (LP over context-action pairs, ε-first learning variant).  
- Relation to prior work: Positions contribution against knapsack/budgeted MAB, RCB, and convex-knapsack bandits, claiming a new efficient instance-dependent log(T) result for finite contexts/actions.  
- Main strength — principled LP: Clear, principled LP approximation yielding a simple benchmark and adaptive policy.  
- Main strength — instance-dependent guarantees: Notable O(1) (ALP) and O(JK log T) (UCB-ALP) instance-dependent bounds under constraints.  
- Main strength — technical novelty: Interesting analysis mapping budget evolution to sampling without replacement (hypergeometric concentration) to control bτ/τ.  
- Main strength — computational efficiency: ALP depends only on ordering of context-wise best rewards, making per-round computation lightweight and learnable by UCB.  
- Main strength — useful extensions: Reasonable algorithmic adaptations for unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Key question on Theorem 2: Clarify source of the O(√T) term and whether it truly is independent of K or should scale like √(KT) when learning is included.  
- Need for gap-dependent analysis: Reviewer requests problem-dependent (gap-based) bounds for both within-context action ranking and cross-context ordering (e.g., sums over 1/Δ).  
- Need for lower bounds: Asks for problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds that capture dependence on J, π, and margins between context-wise u*.  
- Clarity issues: Make the "measure zero boundary" precise (use margin assumptions), state boundary conditions in main text, and move key supplemental lemmas/constants into the main paper.  
- Complexity transparency: Explicitly state per-round computational complexity (e.g., sorting û*j and thresholding) for ALP and UCB-ALP.  
- Originality concern: Argues setting resembles knapsack bandits; requests sharper discussion of why prior reductions don't yield log(T) regret and what is fundamentally new in adaptive-LP analysis.  
- Significance: Praises practical appeal and potential wider applicability, but notes broader impact would be clearer with gap-dependent bounds and matching lower bounds.  
- Suggested improvements: Provide gap-dependent upper bounds, add lower bounds accounting for context complexity, clarify the √T term origin, and strengthen empirical comparisons to knapsack-style baselines.  
- Pros for acceptance: Clean, efficient algorithm; new log(T) guarantee in constrained contextual setting; solid technical ideas.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited novelty vs action-constrained knapsack, lack of gap-dependent bounds and tight constants, unclear boundary-case impact.  
- Post-rebuttal stance: Reviewer remains neutral; requests clean gap-dependent upper bounds and matching lower bounds to be convinced of the paper’s contribution.
