- Reviewer views the setting as essentially a budgeted bandit with action constraints, arguing contexts decouple for large horizons.  
- Main claim: paper studies budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandits and shows O(log T) regret for finite contexts/actions when T is large.  
- Results are thorough and writing is precise, but the finding is interesting yet not surprising.  
- With J contexts and K arms, the problem can be seen as a JK-arm action-constrained bandit, undermining originality.  
- Questions about Theorem 2: the O(√T + ...) term should perhaps be O(√(K T) + ...).  
- Theorem 2’s problem-dependent bound is coarse (O(KJ log T)); reviewer asks if bounds can be refined to sums over inverse arm gaps.  
- Concern that the proof technique (ordering of UCBs) prevents gap-refined bounds and may be suboptimal.  
- Requests lower bounds: both problem-dependent and problem-independent, beyond trivial classical-bandit cases.  
- Suggests refining lower bounds to account for context complexity.  
- Conclusion: reviewer remains neutral and recommends improving the paper with gap-based upper bounds and matching lower bounds.
