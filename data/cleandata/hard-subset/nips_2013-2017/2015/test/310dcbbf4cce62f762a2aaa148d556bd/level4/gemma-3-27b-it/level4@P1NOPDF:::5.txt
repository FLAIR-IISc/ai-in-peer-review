This paper considers a relatively simple budget-constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The main contribution lies in the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm, achieved through a linear programming (LP) approximation, and the resulting log(T) regret bound.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. 

Suggestions for future work include exploring the use of a parametric model for the reward function, which would be particularly beneficial when dealing with a large number of contexts. In Section 4.1, when combining Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) with the Approximate Linear Program (ALP), clarifying the rationale behind the UCB-ALP algorithm would be helpful. For example, the approach appears to leverage optimism in the face of uncertainty, and may be equivalent to the policy update used in UCRL2. Including some numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would further strengthen the results. 

The authors may also find the following paper relevant: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374. 

Overall, this paper merits acceptance. It provides an efficient algorithm for constrained CB with near-optimal regret performance in many cases. The work is interesting and represents a genuine improvement over existing approaches.
