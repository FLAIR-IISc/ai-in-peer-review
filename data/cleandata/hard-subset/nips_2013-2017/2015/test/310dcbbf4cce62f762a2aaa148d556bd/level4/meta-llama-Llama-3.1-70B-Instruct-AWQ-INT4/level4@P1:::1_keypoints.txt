- Main result: For finite contexts J and actions K, and sufficiently large T, the paper shows O(log T) regret for budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandits.  
- Presentation: Results are thorough and writing is precise.  
- Surprise/novelty: Result interesting but not surprising given finite J,K — problem can be viewed as a JK-arm time/budgeted bandit.  
- Accessibility constraint: At each time only K arms (those for the current context) are available, making it an action-constrained JK-arm problem.  
- Concern on originality: Reviewer questions how innovative the setting is and asks authors to clarify originality in rebuttal.  
- Theorem 2, Q1: Doubt whether the O(√T + ...) term should instead be O(√(K T) + ...).  
- Theorem 2, Q2: Regret bounds given as O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T); reviewer asks if bounds can be refined to problem-dependent forms using arm gaps (sum of inverse gaps).  
- Proof technique concern: Current proof uses UCB ordering events rather than gap-based concentration, raising question of optimality of approach.  
- Lower bounds, Q3: Reviewer requests problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds beyond trivial special-case bounds.  
- Recommendation stance: Reviewer remains neutral due to unresolved originality concerns.  
- Strengths summarized: New log(T) result under conditions; thorough results; precise writing.  
- Weaknesses summarized: Questionable originality, coarse regret bounds (no gap dependence), and lack of lower bounds.  
- Suggestions: Clarify originality, refine regret bounds to incorporate arm gaps, and provide both problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds.
