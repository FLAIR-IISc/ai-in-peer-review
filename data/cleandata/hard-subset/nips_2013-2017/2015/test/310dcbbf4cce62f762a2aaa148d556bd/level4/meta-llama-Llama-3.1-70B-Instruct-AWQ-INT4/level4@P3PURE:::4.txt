This paper presents an interesting setup and unique solutions for analyzing contextual bandits against a stronger baseline. The authors propose a baseline that utilizes adaptive linear programming to decide whether to pass on a certain percentage of suboptimal rounds, referred to as the "budget." A question arises, however, regarding the analysis of boundary cases. Specifically, it is unclear why the case of rho = q_j requires special treatment, particularly for j > 1. This distinction does not appear to be inherently justified, as rho = q_j does not seem to possess any unique properties that would necessitate a separate analysis.
