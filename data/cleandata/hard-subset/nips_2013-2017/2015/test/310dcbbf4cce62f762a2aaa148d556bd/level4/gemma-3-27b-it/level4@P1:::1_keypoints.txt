- Paper studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints; main claim: O(log T) regret attainable for finite contexts/actions when T is large.
- Main contribution: novel demonstration of logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual setting.
- Writing is thorough and generally precise; results are interesting but not wholly surprising.
- Observation: with finite J contexts and K actions, problem can be seen as JK-armed bandit constrained by observed context, so O(log T) is expected.
- Request: authors should clarify the paper’s novelty in the rebuttal.
- Question: In Theorem 2 case 2, regret given as O(√T + ...); shouldn’t it be at least O(√(K T)) + ...?
- Question: Theorem 2 gives O(JK log T) or O(√T + JK log T); can this be refined to problem-dependent bounds (sums of inverse gaps)?
- Note: Current proof uses event-based UCB ordering instead of gap concentration bounds—question whether this is optimal.
- Suggestion: Add lower bounds (both problem-dependent and problem-independent); trivial reductions exist but contextual complexity may allow refinement.
- Reviewer stance: originality seen as somewhat incremental; reviewer is neutral pending responses.
- Pros listed: novel constrained contextual analysis, reasonable UCB-ALP algorithm combining LP and UCB, comprehensive analysis including unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.
- Cons listed: log regret might be expected given finite spaces, boundary-case √T term appears suboptimal, absence of lower bounds weakens optimality claims.
