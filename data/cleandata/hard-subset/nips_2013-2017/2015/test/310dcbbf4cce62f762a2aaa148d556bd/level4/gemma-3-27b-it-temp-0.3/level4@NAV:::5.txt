This work addresses a constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions, focusing on computational efficiency through LP approximation and achieving a log(T) regret bound. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.

Suggestions for future research include exploring a parametric reward model, which would be particularly beneficial when dealing with a large number of contexts. In Section 4.1, the motivation behind integrating UCB with the ALP algorithm could be clarified; the connection to optimism in the face of uncertainty, and its potential equivalence to the policy update in UCRL2, would be valuable to highlight. Furthermore, the inclusion of numerical experiments would strengthen the extended version of this paper. 

The authors may also find the work in [http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374](http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374) relevant. Overall, this paper merits acceptance due to its efficient algorithm for constrained CB problems, its near-optimal regret performance, and its contribution as an improvement over current state-of-the-art methods. It presents an interesting and valuable advancement in the field.
