- Problem: contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T; actions include skipping (k=0) or taking action k>0 with known cost c_{j,k} and reward in [0,1].
- Special case: with known context distribution π and c_{j,k}=1, authors give an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a specific condition.
- Extension: algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.
- Novelty: prior related work [16] only achieved O(√T), so this may be the first to obtain O(log T) regret (conditional on the stated assumption).
- Method: algorithm approximates the optimal action sequence assuming known statistics; simple and compelling approach.
- Writing: paper is well-motivated and extremely clearly written.
- Impact caveat: stronger impact if O(log T) held universally or if an Ω(√T) lower bound were shown when the condition fails.
- Technical concern: reviewer initially questioned Lemma 1 (validity of averaging the constraint), but was convinced by the rebuttal.
- Minor question: is computing the optimal action sequence exactly NP-hard?
- Minor question: can results be extended to unknown time horizon T?
- Minor question: can one assume u^1 > u^2 > … > u^J without loss of generality when context distribution is unknown?
- Recommendation: strong accept.
