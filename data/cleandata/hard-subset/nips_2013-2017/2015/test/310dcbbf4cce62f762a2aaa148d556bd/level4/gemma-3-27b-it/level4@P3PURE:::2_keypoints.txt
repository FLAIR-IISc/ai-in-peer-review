- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, focusing on finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.
- Simplifying assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget) are reasonable for many applications.
- Framework enables algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by using an adaptive linear programming approximation of the optimal policy.
- Claims a strong theoretical contribution, reportedly the first to establish logarithmic regret bounds for constrained contextual bandits.
- Paper is clearly written, well-documented, and provides adequate citations and motivation.
- Proofs appear convincing, though the reviewer did not verify them in full detail.
- Practical discussion is limited (mainly Sections 5 and 6) and no experimental evaluation is provided.
- Recommendation: overall a good paper; expanding discussion of practical considerations would strengthen it.
