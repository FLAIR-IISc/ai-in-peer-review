- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with both time horizon T and budget B, where temporal dependencies make the oracle optimal policy intractable.
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for known statistics, relaxing the hard budget into a dynamically updated average constraint.
- Shows ALP achieves O(1) regret (constant gap to oracle) except for "boundary cases" where regret is O(√T).
- Proposes UCB-ALP for unknown rewards by combining ALP with UCB estimation; ALP only needs correct ordering of expected rewards.
- Proves UCB-ALP attains O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) in boundary cases, claimed as the first logarithmic regret bound for this setting.
- Sketches extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous action costs.
- Strength: Significant and original — first log-regret result and practical relevance to crowdsourcing and clinical trials.
- Strength: Strong technical contribution — elegant adaptive LP relaxation and nontrivial concentration analysis of remaining budget (Lemma 2).
- Strength: Clear exposition with a pedagogical progression from known-statistics ALP to UCB-ALP.
- Weakness: Insufficient intuition and explanation for performance degradation in boundary cases and whether it’s an artifact of the LP relaxation.
- Weakness: Reliance on simplifying assumptions (finite contexts, known context distribution) with limited discussion on relaxing them.
- Weakness: Extensions (unknown contexts, heterogeneous costs) are treated briefly and most details are deferred to the supplement.
- Overall conclusion: Strong, technically sound paper that advances the field and is recommended for acceptance.
