

The paper addresses contextual bandits under budget and time constraints, restricting analysis to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Though simplified, these assumptions remain practically relevant. Crucially, the approach yields algorithms achieving O(log T) regret through an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle. The paper is clearly written and well-documented, with appropriate citations and explicit motivation. It presents a solid theoretical contribution. However, practical considerations are only briefly covered in Sections 5 and 6, with no experimental results provided. Despite this, the contributions are significant: to the best of my knowledge, the authors are the first to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs are convincing (though I did not verify all details exhaustively). This is a strong paper that appropriately leverages simplified assumptions to achieve O(log T) regret, though deeper discussion of practical implications would be beneficial.
