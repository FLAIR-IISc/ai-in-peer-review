- Problem studied: constrained contextual bandits with budget/time limits, discrete contexts and actions.
- Main contribution: computationally efficient UCB-ALP algorithm using an LP approximation with logarithmic regret.
- Writing and presentation: exceptionally clear and well-situated within existing literature.
- Theoretical analysis: sound proofs and reasonable approach; strong theoretical foundation.
- LP approximation: clever technique to handle constrained optimization and reduce computational complexity.
- Computational efficiency: LP-based method makes the algorithm practical compared to naive approaches.
- Generalizations: includes discussion of unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, but with limited detail.
- Strength: clear articulation of assumptions, limitations, and treatment of known vs unknown parameters.
- Weakness: lack of empirical validation—no numerical experiments to demonstrate practical performance.
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of the LP vs dynamic programming connection and why DP is intractable beyond complexity claims.
- Weakness: the rationale for combining UCB with ALP needs clearer exposition (e.g., optimism principle, parallels to UCRL2).
- Weakness: limited analysis of sensitivity to errors in the context distribution estimate for Empirical ALP.
- Related work: should reference arXiv:1506.03374, which explores related ideas.
- Recommendation: overall high-quality and recommend acceptance, with requests to add experiments and address the noted clarifications.
