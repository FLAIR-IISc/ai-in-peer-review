Summary:
This paper studies a budget-constrained contextual bandit problem with discrete contexts and actions. The main contribution is an efficiently implementable algorithm based on a linear-programming (LP) relaxation of the oracle, combined with UCB for learning. The approach yields a logarithmic regret bound, O(log T), in most cases, and is computationally attractive. Overall, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Strengths:
- Proposes a simple, computationally efficient algorithm via LP approximation (ALP) that requires only the ordering of expected rewards.
- Combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to handle unknown rewards, achieving O(log T) regret in most cases.
- The writing is clear and the presentation is well organized.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Consider, as future work, adopting a parametric model for rewards. This would be particularly useful when the number of contexts is large.
- In Section 4.1, when combining UCB with ALP, clarify the rationale behind the UCB-ALP design. It appears to follow the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle, and may be closely related to the policy updates used in UCRL2; making this explicit would help readers.
- It would be valuable to include numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper to complement the theoretical results.
- You may want to cite: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374.

Pros:
- Efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits.
- Regret guarantees are optimal (logarithmic) in most cases.
- Clear exposition.

Cons:
- Limited discussion of extensions to high-dimensional or large-context settings (e.g., parametric models).
- The motivation for UCB-ALP could be made more explicit.
- Experimental validation is currently missing.

Recommendation:
This paper merits acceptance. It provides an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with optimal regret in most cases, and constitutes a meaningful improvement over existing work.
