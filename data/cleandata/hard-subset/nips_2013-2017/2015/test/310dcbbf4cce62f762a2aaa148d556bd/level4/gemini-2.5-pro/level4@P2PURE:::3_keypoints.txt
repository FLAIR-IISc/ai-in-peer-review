- Problem setup: contextual bandit with horizon T and budget B; contexts j~π; actions 0..K with skip action 0 (no cost/reward); other actions yield stochastic rewards in [0,1] and incur known costs c_{j,k}; objective is to maximize cumulative reward under the budget constraint.

- Special-case result: for known π and unit costs (c_{j,k}=1), the proposed algorithm achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition.

- Generalization: the algorithm is extended to handle unknown π and arbitrary costs.

- Method: uses a simple, theoretically interesting approximation to compute the optimal policy when parameters are known.

- Novelty: appears to be the first work achieving O(log T) regret for this problem; prior work (e.g., [16]) attained O(√T) regret.

- Limitation/suggestion: impact would increase if O(log T) could be shown universally or if an Ω(√T) lower bound were provided for the boundary cases not covered.

- Dependence on Lemma 1: main results hinge on Lemma 1; reviewer initially questioned it but found the authors' rebuttal clarification satisfactory.

- Minor open questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard when statistics are known? Can results extend to unknown T? Can one assume u1>u2>...>uJ w.l.o.g. when π is unknown?

- Overall verdict: strong paper; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
