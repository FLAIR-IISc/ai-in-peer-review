This paper studies a contextual bandit with a budget constraint B and a known time horizon T. In each round, the agent observes a context j drawn from a fixed distribution π over {1,…,J} and chooses an action k from {0} ∪ {1,…,K}, where k=0 denotes “skip.” If k=0, the agent receives zero reward and incurs no cost. If k>0, the agent receives a reward y and pays cost c{j,k}, where y is drawn from a fixed distribution on [0,1] that depends on j and k, and c{j,k}>0 is known to the agent. The objective is to maximize cumulative reward subject to the cumulative cost never exceeding B.

The main part of the paper focuses on the special case where π is known and c{j,k}=1 for all j and k, and proposes an algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild condition. The paper then extends the method to the general case with unknown π and arbitrary c{j,k}’s.

A more general formulation is treated in [16], but only achieves O(√T) regret. Thus, this work is likely the first to obtain O(log T) regret, albeit under the stated mild condition. The method builds on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when all statistics are known. The algorithm is simple yet quite compelling.

The paper is well motivated and very clearly written. It would be even more interesting and impactful if O(log T) regret held unconditionally; otherwise, the Ω(√T) lower bound established for boundary cases clarifies the limits.
