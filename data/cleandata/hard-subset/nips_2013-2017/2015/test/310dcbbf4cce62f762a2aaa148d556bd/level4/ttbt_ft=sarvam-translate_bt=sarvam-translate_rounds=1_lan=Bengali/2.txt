(Discussion Review)

This paper considers relevant bandits with budget and time constraints. The analysis is limited to isolated contexts, specific costs, and a single budget constraint. Although the context is simplified, these assumptions are reasonably valid in practice. Importantly, it creates an algorithm that achieves O(log T) regress through the introduction of Oracle's Adaptive Linear Programming approximation.

The paper is clear and well-documented. Relevant work is cited, and the purpose is clear. It is a reliable theoretical paper. Practical aspects are only briefly discussed in sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are provided. However, the contributions are significant: to my knowledge, the authors are the first to achieve logarithmic regress in the context of limited bandits. The evidence is credible (although I have not fully detailed it). It is a good paper, which makes suitable simplified assumptions for obtaining an O(log T) regress algorithm. Practical aspects can be discussed in more detail.
