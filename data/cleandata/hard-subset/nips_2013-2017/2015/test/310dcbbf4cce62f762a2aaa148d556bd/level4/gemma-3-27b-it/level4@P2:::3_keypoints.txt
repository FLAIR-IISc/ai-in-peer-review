- Addresses contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, motivated by crowdsourcing and clinical trials.  
- Main contribution: approximating the optimal policy via adaptive linear programming (ALP) combined with UCB to handle unknown rewards.  
- Achieves logarithmic regret in many cases, a significant improvement over prior √T bounds.  
- ALP requires only reward ordering, enabling effective combination with estimation methods.  
- Algorithmic contribution (UCB-ALP) appears computationally efficient and addresses an open problem.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and makes complex concepts accessible.  
- Strong technical justification for using ALP + UCB and positioning within related work.  
- Extends results from known context/unit costs to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Logarithmic regret guarantee fails on certain boundary cases, where regret is √T.  
- Lemma 1 raised initial correctness concerns, later clarified, highlighting need for rigorous justification.  
- Analysis of the general setting (unknown contexts, heterogeneous costs) is more complex and less detailed.  
- Assumption of known context distribution in initial analysis could be relaxed for greater generality.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with strong practical relevance and excellent writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: non-universal logarithmic guarantee and weaker analysis/detailing for the general case; overall recommendation strongly accepts despite these.
