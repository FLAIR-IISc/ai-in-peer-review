(Light Review)

This paper studies contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints. The analysis is restricted to finite, discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While simplified, these assumptions are reasonable in practice and enable algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret via an adaptive linear-programming approximation to the oracle.

The paper is clear and well organized. Relevant prior work is properly cited and the motivation is explicit. This is a solid theoretical contribution. The practical aspects, however, are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are provided. That said, the contributions are significant: to the best of my knowledge, this is the first work to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs are convincing, although I did not verify every detail.

Overall, this is a good paper that makes appropriate simplifying assumptions to obtain O(log T) regret algorithms. I would encourage the authors to discuss the practical implications in greater depth and, if possible, provide empirical validation.
