The paper addresses the problem of contextual bandits with budget and time constraints. The analysis focuses on finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While these are simplifying assumptions, they remain reasonable in many practical scenarios. Crucially, they allow the authors to develop algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the optimal policy (the oracle).

The paper is clearly written and well-documented. The authors appropriately cite relevant work and provide explicit motivation for their approach. This is a solid theoretical contribution. The discussion of practical aspects is limited to Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are presented. Nevertheless, the contributions are significant: to the best of my knowledge, this is the first work to obtain logarithmic regret guarantees for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear convincing (though I did not verify them in their entirety). Overall, this is a strong paper that makes appropriate simplifying assumptions to achieve O(log T) regret algorithms. A more detailed discussion of practical considerations would be beneficial.
