- Studies budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions and proposes an LP-based, computationally efficient algorithm.
- Main contribution: an LP approximation combined with exploration that achieves logarithmic regret in T.
- Presentation is clear and easy to follow.

- Strength: clear problem formulation and motivation for budget constraints.
- Strength: elegant LP relaxation yielding computationally efficient policy.
- Strength: theoretical guarantee of near-optimal log(T) regret.
- Strength: strong writing and well-structured paper.

- Weakness: rationale behind the UCB-ALP combination (Section 4.1) needs clearer explanation and explicit optimism interpretation.
- Weakness: limited to discrete, not-too-large context spaces; scalability and generalization are not addressed.

- Suggestion: consider parametric or generalized linear reward models to enable generalization across contexts and reduce sample complexity.
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed derivation/intuitions for UCB-ALP and clarify similarities/differences with UCRL2.
- Suggestion: add empirical validation (sensitivity to budget/horizon, scaling with contexts/actions, baselines, robustness to misspecification/nonstationarity).
- Suggestion: discuss related work (e.g., arXiv:1506.03374) and position the contribution relative to constrained/resource-aware bandit algorithms.

- Significance: combines LP approximation with principled exploration to yield practical, theoretically strong results, improving or unifying prior approaches.
- Clarity: results and assumptions are clearly stated; adding noted clarifications would aid readers unfamiliar with optimism-based methods.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated, and theoretically careful paper; recommendation to accept with suggested clarifications, scalability discussion, and experiments.
