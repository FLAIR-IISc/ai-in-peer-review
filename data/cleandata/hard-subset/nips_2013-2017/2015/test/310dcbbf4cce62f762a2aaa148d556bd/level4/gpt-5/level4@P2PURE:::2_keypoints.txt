- Studies contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints.
- Analysis restricted to finite, discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.
- These simplifying assumptions are reasonable in practice and enable tractable analysis.
- Introduces an adaptive linear-programming approximation of the oracle.
- Algorithms achieve O(log T) regret.
- Claimed to be the first work (to reviewer’s knowledge) obtaining logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Paper is clear, well-documented, cites relevant prior work, and states motivation explicitly.
- Theoretical contribution is solid and proofs are convincing (not fully checked).
- Practical aspects are only briefly discussed (Sections 5 and 6) and no experiments are provided.
- Practical implications should be discussed in greater depth.
