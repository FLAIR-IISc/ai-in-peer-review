- Studies contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T where actions can be "skip" or incur known costs and stochastic rewards, aiming to maximize cumulative reward under the budget constraint.  
- Focuses on the special case with known context distribution π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, and proposes an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extends the algorithm to settings with unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Novel contribution: appears to be the first work to obtain O(log T) regret (prior work [16] only gives O(√T)), though the bound holds under a condition and may not be universal.  
- Algorithmic approach builds on an approximation method for computing the optimal action sequence when all statistics are known; the method is simple and interesting.  
- Results critically depend on Lemma 1; reviewer initially questioned its validity but was convinced by the authors’ rebuttal.  
- Minor question: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard?  
- Minor question: can the results be extended to unknown horizon T?  
- Minor question: for unknown context distribution, can one assume u^1 > u^2 > … > u^*_J without loss of generality?  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
