- Introduces an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation achieving O(log T) regret for constrained contextual bandits with budget/time constraints.
- Analysis restricted to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint (reasonable but simplified).
- Paper is well-written, clear, concise, with proper citations and explicit motivation.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis and convincing proofs (not all checked in detail).
- ALP tolerates estimation errors and approximates the oracle to achieve near-optimal performance.
- Proposes UCB-ALP combining ALP with UCB, achieving O(log T) regret except for certain boundary cases.
- Strengths: solid theoretical foundation, clear presentation, and significant contribution to the field.
- Weakness: lacks experimental results and in-depth practical discussion, limiting empirical validation.
- Suggested improvements: add experiments, more practical discussion, and extend analysis to infinite contexts or multiple budget constraints.
- Overall assessment: good paper, rated 8/10, recommended for acceptance (confidence 8/10).
