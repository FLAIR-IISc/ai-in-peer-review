- Reviewer acknowledges authors’ distinction but finds the setting fundamentally similar to existing models; coupling of contexts should diminish for large horizons, so each context might be treated as an independent bandit.

- Unclear why context occurrences cannot be treated as independent bandit instances for large T.

- Paper needs a refined problem-dependent upper bound (supplementary version requires revision) and ideally matching problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds.

- Main claim: for finite J contexts and K actions and large T, O(log T) regret is achievable; authors present this as novel.

- Analysis is rigorous and clear, but novelty is questionable because the problem can be reframed as a budgeted bandit with JK arms and K-availability constraints, where O(log T) is expected.

- Concerns about conceptual innovation: contextual aspect may only act as an action constraint rather than a fundamentally new setting.

- Question on Theorem 2 case 2: regret bound O(√T + ...) should likely scale with number of arms, e.g., O(√(K T) + ...).

- Question on Theorem 2 problem-dependent bound O(KJ log T): typically such bounds depend on sums of inverse gaps (1/Δ); ask whether a more granular, tighter gap-dependent bound can be derived.

- Request to include lower bounds (both problem-dependent and problem-independent) that explicitly capture complexity from the contextual element rather than relying on trivial classic-bandit bounds.

- Primary reservation: setting appears very close to budgeted bandit with action constraints, so originality is not convincing; reviewer remains neutral.
