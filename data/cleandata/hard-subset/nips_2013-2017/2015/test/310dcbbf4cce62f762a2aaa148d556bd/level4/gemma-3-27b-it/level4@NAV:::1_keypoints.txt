- Paper shows a log(T) regret is achievable for budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandits with finite contexts and actions when T is large relative to problem parameters.
- Result is novel within contextual bandits but not entirely surprising given finite J contexts and K actions can be viewed as JK arms with context-specific constraints.
- Presentation is comprehensive and clear, but the novelty/originality needs stronger justification or discussion in rebuttal.
- Theorem 2: suspected typo/omission — regret term stated as O(√T + ...) likely should be O(√(K T) + ...).
- Theorem 2 bounds use O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T); reviewer suggests deriving more precise problem-dependent bounds expressed via sums over inverse arm gaps.
- Current proof relies on UCB-ordering events instead of concentration on gaps; optimality and tightness of bounds should be reconsidered.
- Reviewer requests lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent settings, possibly refining classical bandit lower bounds to account for context complexity.
- Strong connection to budgeted bandits with action constraints raises doubts about originality — reviewer is unconvinced the contextual setting isn’t reducible to known formulations as T grows.
- Recommend refining upper bounds in terms of arm gaps, providing matching lower bounds, and revising the supplementary material’s current bound.
