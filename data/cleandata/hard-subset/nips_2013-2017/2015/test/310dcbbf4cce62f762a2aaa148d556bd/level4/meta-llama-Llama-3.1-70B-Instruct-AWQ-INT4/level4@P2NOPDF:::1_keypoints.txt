- Reviewer remains unconvinced the setting is novel, viewing it as essentially a budgeted bandit with action constraints, especially for large horizons where context coupling is negligible.
- Argues each context occurrence might be treated as a separate bandit instance, questioning the need for the proposed coupling.
- Requests a clean problem-dependent upper bound expressed in terms of arm gaps (sum of inverse gaps) rather than coarse KJ log(T) terms.
- Asks for matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent regimes.
- Acknowledges the paper’s main contribution: showing log(T) regret for finite contexts/actions and large T.
- Finds results thorough and writing precise but not particularly surprising given reduction to a JK-arm action-constrained bandit.
- Questions the paper’s originality and asks for clearer justification of the innovation in the proposed setting.
- Technical question on Theorem 2: challenges the O(√T + ...) term and suggests it should be at least O(√(KT) + ...).
- Notes Theorem 2 provides either O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T) and asks if more refined, gap-dependent bounds are achievable.
- Encourages establishing refined lower bounds that account for context complexity beyond trivial reductions to classical bandits.
