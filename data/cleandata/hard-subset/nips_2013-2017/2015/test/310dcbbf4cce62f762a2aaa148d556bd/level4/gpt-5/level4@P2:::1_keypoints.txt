- Problem studied: constrained contextual bandits with finite contexts J and actions K, budget B and horizon T, focusing on proportional regime B/T = ρ.
- Known-statistics result: ALP approximation yields O(1) regret off measure-zero boundary values of ρ, and O(√T) regret on boundaries.
- Unknown rewards: UCB-ALP achieves O(JK log T) regret (non-boundary) and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.
- Extensions: authors discuss empirical ALP for unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs.
- Claimed novelty: instance-dependent O(log T) regret for discrete contexts/actions via adaptive budget allocation, contrasted with prior O(√T) results.
- Strength: clear, practically motivated model and simple, computationally efficient ALP that needs only reward ordering.
- Strength: UCB-ALP design with careful regret decomposition into action-ranking, context-ranking, and budget fluctuation components.
- Strength: technical contributions include concentration of remaining budget under adaptive decisions and a sampling-without-replacement mapping.
- Main originality concern: with finite J and K the problem may reduce to a budgeted/time-constrained MAB with JK arms and action constraints; novelty relative to that reduction is not fully clarified.
- Boundary-case question: need justification whether the √T term is tight and whether it should scale with K (e.g., √(K T)) or is independent of action set size.
- Missing theory: no gap-dependent (problem-dependent) upper bounds like classic UCB inverse-gap sums; supplementary bounds need tightening and reworking.
- Missing theory: lack of problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds specific to the constrained contextual setting and LP threshold structure.
- Clarity request: make explicit the measure of boundary parameters and whether boundary rates can be improved with alternative exploration or budget control.
- Quality: technical development is largely sound but some supplementary proofs (context-ranking errors, boundary analysis) need clearer gap-dependent constants and streamlined presentation.
- Clarity: paper is well written overall but should crisply explain how the model differs from budgeted/action-constrained MABs.
- Originality: adaptive LP relaxation and decoupling learning from budget control is appealing, but extent beyond action-constrained bandits is unclear without refined bounds and lower bounds.
- Significance: results are practically relevant and computationally attractive but need refined/gap-dependent bounds and lower bounds to be more definitive.
- Pros summary: simple efficient algorithms, near-optimal ALP with known stats, clear regret decomposition and novel concentration tools.
- Cons summary: limited problem-dependent theory, no lower bounds, and unclear novelty relative to finite-arm budgeted MABs; boundary-case dependence on K/J needs sharpening.
- Suggestions: provide gap-dependent upper bounds separating within-context and cross-context terms; add problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds; clarify non-reducibility to budgeted/action-constrained bandits and necessity of adaptive LP.
