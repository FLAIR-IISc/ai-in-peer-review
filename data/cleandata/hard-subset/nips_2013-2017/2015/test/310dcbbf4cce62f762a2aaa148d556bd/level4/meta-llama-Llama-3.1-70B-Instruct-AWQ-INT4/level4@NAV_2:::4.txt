This paper studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, referred to as constrained contextual bandits, where the agent is given a budget B and a time-horizon T. The time and budget constraints significantly complicate the exploration and exploitation tradeoff because they introduce complex coupling among contexts over time. The authors propose an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that achieves near-optimality and only requires the ordering of expected rewards. When the expected rewards are unknown, the authors combine ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method to propose a UCB-ALP algorithm, which achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases.

The paper relates to previous work on contextual bandits and resourceful contextual bandits, but differs in its focus on budget and time constraints. The authors also discuss the limitations of previous work, such as the Mixture Elimination algorithm, which suffers from high complexity and does not achieve logarithmic regret.

The strengths of the paper include its novel approach to approximating the oracle algorithm using linear programming, and its ability to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the regret bounds for the proposed algorithms.

However, the paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the boundary cases and why they need to be analyzed differently. The authors mention that the boundary cases have a different regret bound, but do not provide a detailed explanation of why this is the case. Additionally, the paper could benefit from more numerical simulations to demonstrate the performance of the proposed algorithms.

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of contextual bandits and resourceful contextual bandits, and its novel approach to approximating the oracle algorithm using linear programming is a major strength.
