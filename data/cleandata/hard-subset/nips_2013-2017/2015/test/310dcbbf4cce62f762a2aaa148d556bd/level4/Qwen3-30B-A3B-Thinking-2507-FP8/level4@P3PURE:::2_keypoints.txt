- Problem setting: contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Assumptions: simplified but well-motivated for practical applications.  
- Main technical contribution: algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret via an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.  
- Novelty: appears to be the first work establishing logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under these assumptions.  
- Writing and presentation: clear, well-documented, with relevant prior work cited and motivation explicit.  
- Theoretical rigor: proofs are convincing, though not independently verified in full detail.  
- Limitations: brief treatment of practical aspects (Sections 5–6) and no experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: would benefit from a more detailed exploration of practical implementation and empirical evaluation.
