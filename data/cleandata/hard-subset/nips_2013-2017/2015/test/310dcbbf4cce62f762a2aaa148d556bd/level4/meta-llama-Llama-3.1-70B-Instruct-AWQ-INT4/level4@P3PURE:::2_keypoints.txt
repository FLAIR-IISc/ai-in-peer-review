- Studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints in a simplified setting: finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Simplifying assumptions are restrictive but reasonable in practice.  
- Introduces an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle enabling algorithm design.  
- Algorithms achieve O(log T) regret in this constrained contextual bandit setting.  
- Appears to be the first work to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and thoroughly documented with proper citations and motivation.  
- Proofs are convincing, though not every detail was checked by the reviewer.  
- Practical aspects are only briefly discussed (Sections 5 and 6) and lack experimental validation.  
- Suggestion: expand discussion of practical implications and provide experiments.
