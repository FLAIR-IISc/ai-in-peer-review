- Tackles budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions.  
- Proposes a computationally efficient algorithm using LP approximation.  
- Establishes a logarithmic regret bound O(log T).  
- Technical correctness and clear exposition make contributions accessible.  
- Main strengths: algorithmic efficiency and tight regret analysis.  
- LP-based approximation effectively handles the budget constraint while preserving guarantees.  
- O(log T) regret aligns with optimal performance in similar settings.  
- Writing and organization are excellent, improving readability.  
- Suggests extending to parametric reward models for scalability to large context spaces.  
- Requests clarification of the rationale for combining UCB with ALP and connection to UCRL2-style optimism.  
- Recommends adding numerical experiments for empirical validation and citing related work (e.g., arXiv:1506.03374).  
- Overall assessment: novel, significant, and clear work that merits acceptance to NIPS.
