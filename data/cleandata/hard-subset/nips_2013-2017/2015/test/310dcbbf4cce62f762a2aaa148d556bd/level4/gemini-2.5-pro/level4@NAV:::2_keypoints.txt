- Studies contextual bandits with finite discrete contexts, fixed action costs, and a single budget constraint under time limits.
- Assumptions simplify the problem but are pragmatically reasonable.
- Main contribution: algorithms achieving O(log T) regret via an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.
- Manuscript is clear, well-documented, and well-situated in related work with explicit motivation and citations.
- Primary limitation: Sections 5 and 6 treat practical aspects briefly and lack experimental validation.
- Claims originality: to the reviewer’s knowledge, first work achieving logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual bandit setting.
- Proofs are convincing overall, though not checked line-by-line by the reviewer.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper that could be improved with a more detailed discussion of practical applications.
