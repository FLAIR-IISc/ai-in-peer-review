This paper addresses the budget-constrained contextual bandit problem in a setting with discrete contexts and actions. The paper's main contributions are a computationally efficient algorithm, based on a linear programming approximation, and a corresponding O(log T) regret bound. The paper is well-written and the presentation is easy to follow.

I have a few suggestions for the authors to consider for future work and potential revisions:

*   For future work, the authors might consider using a parametric model for the rewards. This extension would be particularly useful in settings with a large number of contexts, where the current tabular approach may not scale.
*   In Section 4.1, the paper would benefit from a clearer explanation of the rationale behind the proposed UCB-ALP algorithm. It appears to be guided by the "optimism in the face of uncertainty" principle, and it would be helpful to state this explicitly. Clarifying if the policy update is analogous to those in algorithms like UCRL2 would also strengthen the exposition.
*   The paper would be significantly strengthened by the inclusion of numerical experiments. These could perhaps be added to an extended version of the paper to validate the algorithm's empirical performance.
*   The authors may also find the following related work of interest: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

This paper merits acceptance. It provides a novel and efficient algorithm for the constrained contextual bandit problem that achieves near-optimal regret in many standard cases. The work is interesting and represents a clear improvement over existing literature.
