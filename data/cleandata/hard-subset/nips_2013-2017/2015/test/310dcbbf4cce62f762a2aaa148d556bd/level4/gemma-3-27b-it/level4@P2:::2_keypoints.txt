- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints in a finite discrete-context, fixed-cost, single-budget setting.  
- Proposes algorithms using an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation of the optimal policy.  
- Main theoretical result: algorithms achieve O(log T) regret.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presenting problem, approach, and results.  
- Literature review and positioning within existing constrained contextual bandit work are appropriate.  
- Proofs are convincing and the analysis appears rigorous (full verification not performed by reviewer).  
- Core idea of approximating the oracle with ALP is insightful and significant.  
- Extensions to unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs are included but treated briefly.  
- Lacks empirical evaluation; no experiments to validate practical performance.  
- Limited discussion of practical considerations and real-world applicability.  
- Novelty and significance: first work known to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.  
- Recommendation: strong accept despite minor drawbacks due to theoretical contribution and clarity.
