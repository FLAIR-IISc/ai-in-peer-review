- Addresses budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions and proposes a computationally efficient solution.  
- Main contribution: an approximate linear programming (ALP) algorithm that attains near-optimal O(log T) regret.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear problem formulation and logical, accessible technical exposition.  
- Strength: significant improvement over prior methods by combining computational efficiency with strong theoretical regret guarantees.  
- Suggestion: Clarify the rationale behind UCB-ALP, explicitly connect UCB/OFU to the ALP step, and relate the update to RL methods like value/policy iteration or UCRL2.  
- Suggestion: Propose extending to parametric/function-approximation reward models (e.g., linear) for large or continuous context spaces as future work.  
- Suggestion: Add numerical experiments comparing to baselines to empirically validate performance; can be placed in an appendix if space is limited.  
- Suggestion: Include additional related work (reference provided: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374).  
- Recommendation: Paper merits acceptance — technically sound, advances the state of the art, and would be strengthened by the suggested minor revisions.
