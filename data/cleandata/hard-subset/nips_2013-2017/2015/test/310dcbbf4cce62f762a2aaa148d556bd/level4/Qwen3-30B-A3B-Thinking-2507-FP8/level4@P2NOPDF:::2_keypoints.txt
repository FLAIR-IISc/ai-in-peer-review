- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget/time limit, assumptions claimed practically motivated.  
- Main contribution: achieves O(log T) regret via an adaptive linear programming approximation to the oracle.  
- Novelty: first to establish logarithmic regret bounds in constrained contextual bandits (to reviewer’s knowledge).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and proofs appear sound, leveraging simplifying assumptions to get strong guarantees.  
- The adaptive LP approach is elegant and effective, enabling the logarithmic regret result where prior work had polynomial bounds.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and thoroughly documented with a comprehensive literature review.  
- Practical implications and implementation details are underdeveloped; Sections 5–6 lack empirical validation.  
- Absence of experiments limits assessment of real-world applicability, performance trade-offs, and scalability.  
- Suggested future work: empirical evaluation, scalability to larger context spaces, and extensions to multiple budget constraints.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution, suitable for acceptance with minor concerns about the empirical gap.
