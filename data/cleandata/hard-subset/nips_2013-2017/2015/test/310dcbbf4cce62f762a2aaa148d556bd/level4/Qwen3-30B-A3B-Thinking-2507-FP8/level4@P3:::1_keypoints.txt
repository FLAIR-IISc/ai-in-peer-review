- Studies constrained contextual bandits with time and budget constraints; proposes algorithms achieving logarithmic regret.  
- Main contributions: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for known statistics and UCB-ALP for unknown statistics.  
- Proven regret: O(log T) generally, with O(√T) regret in boundary cases.  
- Relates appropriately to prior work on budgeted (non-contextual) bandits and Resourceful Contextual Bandits.  
- Simplified model (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget) is justified by practical applications and enables stronger bounds.  
- Technical quality is strong: rigorous regret decomposition and novel techniques for budget-context-reward interactions.  
- Supplementary material is detailed but the regret decomposition presentation could be clearer/streamlined.  
- Paper is clearly written; algorithms and proofs are accessible and well-structured.  
- Originality: key insight is decoupling via adaptive linear relaxation (ALP) enabling logarithmic regret; ALP is a novel approximation.  
- UCB-ALP effectively handles exploration-exploitation under budget constraints.  
- Significant for applications (clinical trials, recommendations) and advances theory for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Main concern: novelty vs naive reduction to a budgeted bandit with JK arms needs clearer articulation.  
- Crucial distinction: the algorithm’s adaptive adjustment of budget/time is what yields logarithmic regret and must be emphasized.  
- Regret bounds: clarify whether O(√T) boundary term is tight and consider providing problem-dependent (gap-based) bounds.  
- Recommend adding gap-dependent bounds and lower-bound discussion to strengthen comparisons and contributions.  
- Overall: solid, technically sound contribution that needs clearer novelty exposition and more refined regret analysis to fully establish impact.
