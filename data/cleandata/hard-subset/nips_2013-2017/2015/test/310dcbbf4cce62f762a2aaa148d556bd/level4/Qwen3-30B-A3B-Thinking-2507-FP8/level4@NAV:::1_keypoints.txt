1. Doubts about novelty: setting appears very close to budgeted, time-constrained bandits with action constraints.  
2. Main claim: with finite contexts and actions and large T, log(T) regret is achievable — novel but not highly surprising.  
3. Reduction argument: problem reduces to a time-constrained, budgeted bandit with JK arms where only K arms are accessible per context.  
4. For finite arms and large T, log(T) regret is standard in action-constrained bandits, so novelty must be clarified.  
5. Request for a clean problem-dependent upper bound in terms of arm gaps; supplementary bound needs revision.  
6. Theorem 2, case 2: reported O(√T + …) regret may be missing a √K factor—should it be O(√(K T) + …)?  
7. Problem-dependent bounds (O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T)) should be refined to sums of inverse arm gaps; current UCB-order-event proof may not be optimal.  
8. Missing lower bounds: ask for matching problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds that possibly incorporate context complexity.  
9. Reviewer unconvinced that large horizon context coupling precludes treating each context as a separate bandit instance.  
10. Overall neutral recommendation: results are thorough and well written but authors must address novelty and relation to existing budgeted/action-constrained bandit work in rebuttal.
