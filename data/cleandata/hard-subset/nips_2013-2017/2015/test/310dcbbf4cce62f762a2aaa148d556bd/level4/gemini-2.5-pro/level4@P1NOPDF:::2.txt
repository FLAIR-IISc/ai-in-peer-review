This paper addresses the problem of contextual bandits under both budget and time constraints. The analysis focuses on a specific setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While this framework represents a simplification of the general problem, the assumptions are reasonable for many practical applications. Crucially, this approach allows the authors to develop algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

The paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and thoroughly cites relevant prior work, presenting a solid theoretical contribution. The main results are significant: to my knowledge, the authors are the first to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear sound and are convincing, although I have not verified every step in full detail.

The primary weakness of the paper is its limited discussion of practical aspects. The treatment in Sections 5 and 6 is brief, and the paper lacks any experimental results to validate the proposed algorithms. While this is a theoretical paper, it would be strengthened by a more thorough exploration of the practical implications of the proposed methods.

In summary, this is a strong paper that makes an important theoretical advance. By making judicious simplifying assumptions, the authors are the first to develop algorithms with O(log T) regret for this constrained bandit setting. Despite the limited practical discussion, the theoretical results are a significant contribution to the field.
