- Problem studied: contextual bandits with finite contexts/actions under time horizon T and budget B.
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm achieving O(log T) regret, improving on prior O(sqrt(T)) results under finite-context assumption.
- Key technique: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) as a near-optimal oracle approximation that needs only relative ordering of expected rewards.
- Estimation approach: Combine ALP with UCB estimates to handle unknown expected rewards.
- Strength — significance: Addresses practical budget-constrained bandits with computationally efficient algorithm and strong guarantees.
- Strength — technical quality: ALP decouples temporal budget dependencies from reward estimation, a key technical insight.
- Strength — originality: Novel O(log T) regret bound in this setting, showing improved possibilities under finite contexts.
- Strength — clarity: Well-written progression from known-statistics ALP to unknown-statistics UCB-ALP provides good intuition.
- Weakness — novelty of setting: Problem can be viewed as finite-armed bandit with sleeping arms; authors should better justify novelty of budget-induced coupling across contexts.
- Weakness — regret bound refinement: Theorem 2 bounds depend on JK; tighter, instance-dependent bounds (sum of inverse gaps) may be possible.
- Weakness — lack of lower bounds: No derived lower bounds specifically accounting for budget-context interaction to establish optimality.
- Weakness — clarity of sqrt(T) term: Need clarification whether the O(sqrt(T)) term in boundary case depends on J or K.
