- Studies constrained contextual bandits (budget + time) and proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP).
- ALP needs only the ordering of expected rewards and achieves near-optimality.
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP for unknown expected rewards.
- UCB-ALP attains logarithmic regret in general, with exceptions at boundary cases.
- Detailed regret analysis: ALP has O(1) regret (non-boundary) and O(√T) (boundary).
- Detailed regret analysis: UCB-ALP has O(log T) regret (non-boundary) and O(√T + log T) (boundary).
- Discusses extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.
- Assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint (limitation).
- No experimental results provided to validate theoretical findings (limitation).
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically sound with thorough regret analysis.
- Novel contribution: ALP algorithm that requires only reward ordering.
- Significant potential impact for applications like clinical trials, recommendations, and crowdsourcing.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, comprehensive theoretical analysis, and discussed generalizations.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions and lack of empirical validation.
