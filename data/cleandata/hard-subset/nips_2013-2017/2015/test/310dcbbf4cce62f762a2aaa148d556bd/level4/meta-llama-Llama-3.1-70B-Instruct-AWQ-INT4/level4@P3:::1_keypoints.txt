- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with budget/time constraints and aims for logarithmic or sublinear regret.  
- Main contribution: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that is near-optimal when system statistics are known and requires only reward ordering.  
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, achieving logarithmic regret except in certain boundary cases.  
- Proposes Empirical ALP (EALP) and a “-First ALP” variant to handle unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Provides thorough regret analysis and bounds for the proposed algorithms.  
- Reviewer finds results interesting and insightful for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Reviewer questions originality, noting similarity to budgeted bandits with action constraints.  
- Requests regret bounds be expressed more finely (e.g., sum of inverse arm gaps).  
- Notes absence of lower bounds for the problem and recommends including them.  
- Strengths: detailed theoretical analysis, interesting results, and treatment of unknown contexts and heterogeneous costs.  
- Weaknesses: perceived limited originality, need for refined regret expressions, and missing lower bounds.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify originality, refine regret bounds, and consider adding lower bounds.
