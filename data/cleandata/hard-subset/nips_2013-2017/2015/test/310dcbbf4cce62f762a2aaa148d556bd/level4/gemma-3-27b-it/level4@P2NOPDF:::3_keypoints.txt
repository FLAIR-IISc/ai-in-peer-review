- Studies contextual bandits with a budget constraint where actions include skipping, and the goal is to maximize cumulative reward under total cost B.  
- Focuses on a simplified setting with known context distribution π and uniform costs c_{j,k}=1.  
- Proposes an algorithm that achieves O(log T) regret under a specified condition.  
- Extends the algorithm to the general setting with unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Related work obtains O(√T) regret; this paper may be the first to obtain O(log T) but applicability depends on the stated condition.  
- Algorithm is built on an approximation technique for computing the optimal action sequence when parameters are known, yielding a simple and insightful method.  
- Paper is well-motivated and exceptionally clear in presentation.  
- Impact would be greater if the O(log T) bound were shown to hold universally or if a complementary Ω(√T) lower bound were proved for cases violating the condition.  
- Reviewer’s initial concerns about Lemma 1 and the averaging relaxation were satisfactorily addressed by the authors’ rebuttal.  
- Remaining minor questions: is finding the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard?  
- Remaining minor questions: can the results be extended to unknown time horizon T?  
- Remaining minor questions: in the unknown context distribution case, is the assumption u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^J without loss of generality justified?  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
