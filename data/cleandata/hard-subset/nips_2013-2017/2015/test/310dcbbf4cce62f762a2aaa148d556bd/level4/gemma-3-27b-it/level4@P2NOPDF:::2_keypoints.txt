- Addresses contextual bandits under budget and time constraints, a relevant and challenging RL problem.  
- Studies a simplified setting: finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget constraint; authors acknowledge and justify these restrictions.  
- Simplification enables a strong theoretical result: algorithms achieving O(log T) regret.  
- Technique: adaptive linear programming approximation to the optimal oracle policy.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly presented, and well-cited; motivation is clearly articulated.  
- Primary strength is theoretical novelty—apparently first to show logarithmic regret bounds for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Proofs appear convincing though the reviewer did not fully verify them.  
- Authors make a justifiable trade-off using simplifying assumptions to obtain the theoretical result.  
- Practical implications are discussed only briefly (mainly Sections 5 and 6).  
- Major omission: no experimental/empirical validation; experiments would strengthen and illuminate real-world performance.  
- Suggestion: include more detailed discussion of practical considerations and implementation challenges in future work.
