- Problem: contextual bandits with known horizon T and fixed budget B; contexts j ~ π, actions k (plus skip), action k>0 gives stochastic reward and known positive cost, objective maximize reward subject to hard budget constraint.

- Simplified setting: with known context distribution π and unit costs, authors propose an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild technical condition.

- Extension: algorithm generalized to unknown context distribution and arbitrary known costs.

- Method: core is a simple, elegant approximation algorithm for the optimal offline policy when parameters are known.

- Novelty/significance: first work (to reviewer’s knowledge) achieving logarithmic regret for this problem, improving over prior O(√T) results.

- Clarity: paper is well-motivated and clearly written.

- Suggestion: strengthen significance by proving O(log T) regret universally or providing an Ω(√T) lower bound for cases not covered by the condition.

- Lemma 1: reviewer’s initial doubts about Lemma 1 and the relaxation of the hard budget constraint were resolved by the authors’ rebuttal; reviewer now convinced.

- Minor comment 1: discuss computational complexity — is computing the optimal offline policy NP-hard?

- Minor comment 2: could results be extended to unknown horizon T?

- Minor comment 3: question about assumption u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J — can this ordering be made w.l.o.g. when π is unknown?

- Recommendation: strong accept.
