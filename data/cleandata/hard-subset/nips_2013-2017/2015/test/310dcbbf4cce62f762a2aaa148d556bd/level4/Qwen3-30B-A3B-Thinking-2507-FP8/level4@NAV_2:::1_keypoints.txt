- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints and proposes the UCB-ALP algorithm.  
- Authors claim O(log T) regret for finite contexts/actions when the horizon is large, with O(√T) regret in boundary cases.  
- Analysis appears technically sound and the writing is clear and precise.  
- Main novelty: extending logarithmic regret guarantees to constrained contextual bandits (distinct from prior budgeted bandits).  
- Reviewer questions distinctness: with finite J and K the problem can be seen as a budgeted bandit with JK arms and only K accessible per step.  
- Concern that logarithmic regret for large T is expected given the action-constrained perspective, reducing perceived novelty.  
- Theorem 2’s regret term O(√T + JK log T) may be incorrect — reviewer expects at least O(√(KT) + JK log T) considering number of arms.  
- Problem-dependent bounds use JK rather than standard sum of inverse arm gaps; reviewer requests refined bounds expressed via arm gaps.  
- Reviewer asks for lower bounds (including context complexity effects) to establish tightness and strengthen the contribution.  
- Supplementary material’s upper bound analysis needs reworking for greater rigor.  
- Overall recommendation: provide clean problem-dependent (gap-based) bounds and matching lower bounds; without these refinements novelty remains questionable.
