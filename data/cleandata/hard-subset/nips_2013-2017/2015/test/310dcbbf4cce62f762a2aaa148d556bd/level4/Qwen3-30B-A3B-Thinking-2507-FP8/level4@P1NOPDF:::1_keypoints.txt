- When T is large the context coupling vanishes, so each context can appear separable—this contradicts the authors’ claim that contexts cannot be treated as separate bandits.  
- The main technical result (log T regret) can be obtained by viewing the problem as a bandit with JK arms (J contexts × K arms) with per-context accessibility constraints.  
- The paper does not convincingly distinguish its setting from the standard reduction to a JK-armed action-constrained bandit, calling novelty into question.  
- Theorem 2 (Case 2) is misstated: the regret should scale as O(√(K T) + …) to account for K arms per context, not O(√T + …).  
- Problem-dependent bounds O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T) are suboptimal—standard contextual bounds refine dependence using gap terms (Σ 1/Δ_i), which the authors do not provide.  
- The proof technique based on UCB-order events leads to poor KJ dependence; a refined gap-based analysis is recommended.  
- The paper lacks matching lower bounds beyond trivial cases; refined lower bounds that incorporate context complexity are needed.  
- Results are technically sound and well-written but the contribution seems marginal unless the authors explicitly contrast with the JK-armed reduction and demonstrate new insights.  
- Recommended fixes: (i) provide clean problem-dependent bounds using arm gaps, (ii) derive matching lower bounds that reflect context structure, (iii) precisely justify novelty relative to the JK-armed reduction.
