- Problem: studies constrained contextual bandits with both time horizon T and budget B under discrete contexts and fixed known action costs.  
- Assumptions: finite discrete contexts, single budget constraint, fixed known costs for actions.  
- Known-statistics contribution: proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that re-solves an LP each step to approximate the optimal DP.  
- ALP performance (known rewards): achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) regret in boundary cases.  
- Unknown-statistics contribution: combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP), leveraging that ALP needs only the correct ordering of expected rewards.  
- UCB-ALP theoretical result: attains O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases—the first logarithmic bound for this problem.  
- Technical strengths: rigorous, elegant analysis with insightful proof sketches and a useful sampling-without-replacement analogy for budget evolution.  
- Originality: novel adaptive LP approximation and its O(1) regret proof; non-trivial integration with UCB for learning.  
- Extensions: discusses handling unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written and organized, with effective "Sketch of Proof" sections.  
- Major weakness: complete lack of empirical validation or experiments to support practical performance and constants.  
- Recommended improvements: add simulations to illustrate boundary vs non-boundary behavior and compare to prior algorithms.  
- Significance: theoretically significant advance likely to influence future work, though practical impact is limited by assumptions and missing experiments.  
- Verdict: reviewer recommends acceptance, with a minor suggestion to discuss practical implications and frequency of boundary cases.
