This paper investigates the problem of contextual bandits under both budget and time limitations. The analysis focuses on a finite, discrete context space, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Although these are simplifying assumptions, they remain practical and allow for the development of algorithms achieving O(log T) regret through an adaptive linear programming approximation of the optimal solution. 

The manuscript is well-written and clearly presented, with thorough citations of related work and a well-defined motivation. It represents a strong theoretical contribution. While practical considerations are touched upon in Sections 5 and 6, the paper lacks experimental validation. Nevertheless, the work is significant; to the best of my knowledge, this is the first result demonstrating logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear sound, though a complete verification was beyond the scope of this review. Overall, this is a valuable paper that appropriately simplifies the problem to achieve a compelling theoretical result – algorithms with O(log T) regret. Further discussion of practical implications would be beneficial.
