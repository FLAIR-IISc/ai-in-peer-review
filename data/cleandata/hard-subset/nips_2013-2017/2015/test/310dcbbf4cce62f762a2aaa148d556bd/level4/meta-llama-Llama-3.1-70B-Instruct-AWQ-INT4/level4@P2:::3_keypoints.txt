- Paper studies contextual bandits with a budget B and horizon T where actions can be "skip" and costs/rewards depend on context-action pairs.  
- Main contribution: algorithm for the special case with known context distribution π and unit costs achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extension: algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Novelty: potentially first work achieving O(log T) regret (prior work [16] only achieves O(√T)).  
- Algorithm is simple, based on an approximation for the full-information optimal action sequence.  
- Paper is well motivated and clearly written.  
- Limitation: O(log T) result depends on a mild condition that may not always hold; boundary cases may have Ω(√T) lower bounds.  
- Major concern: correctness of Lemma 1 — unclear that the optimal action sequence always satisfies condition (2) and why the average constraint relaxes the hard constraint.  
- Missing: no lower bound provided for the boundary cases.  
- Minor questions: is finding the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard? Can results handle unknown T? Can we assume ordered utilities u^1>u^2>...>u^J* without loss of generality when π is unknown?  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance, but authors should address the mild-condition concern and provide lower bounds for boundary cases.
