As this is a brief review, I pose one question: why do the boundary regimes require separate treatment? It is not clear to me why ρ = q_j is intrinsically special, especially for j > 1. The paper evaluates contextual bandits against a stronger comparator: an adaptive linear programming strategy that opts to skip a certain fraction of less favorable rounds (what the authors refer to as budget). The problem formulation is engaging, and the solutions are distinctive.
