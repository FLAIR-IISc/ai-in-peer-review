- Main contribution: shows O(log T) regret for contextual bandits with budget and time constraints for finite contexts and actions; well-written and thorough results.

- Novelty concern: with finite J contexts and K arms, problem can be reframed as a time- and budget-constrained bandit with JK arms, reducing apparent novelty.

- Context coupling concern: for large T, context coupling diminishes, so each context might be treated as a separate standard bandit instance, questioning unique contribution.

- Upper-bound refinement: question whether the problem-independent O(sqrt(T) + ...) term should scale with number of arms, e.g., O(sqrt(KT) + ...).

- Gap-dependent bounds: current problem-dependent O(KJ log T) bound is less informative than standard sum-of-inverse-gaps formulations; suggest reworking analysis to obtain cleaner gap-dependent bounds.

- Proof technique critique: reliance on UCB-ordering events rather than direct concentration on gaps may be suboptimal and drives the coarse bounds.

- Lower bounds missing: paper lacks problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds specific to this setting; these should ideally match upper bounds and reflect context-budget-time interactions.

- Overall judgement: result is novel but fundamental originality is unclear and analysis needs refinement; reviewer remains neutral.
