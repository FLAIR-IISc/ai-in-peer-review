- Problem and setting: Studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints in a simplified but reasonable setting (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget).
- Method and idea: Introduces an adaptive linear programming approximation of an oracle to design algorithms.
- Theoretical result: Algorithms achieve O(log T) regret.
- Writing and literature: Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and cites relevant work thoroughly.
- Novelty/significance: Appears to be the first work to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Proofs: Proofs appear convincing though not fully checked by the reviewer.
- Limitation: Practical aspects are discussed only briefly (Sections 5 and 6) and there are no experimental validations.
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical contribution that uses judicious simplifying assumptions to derive novel algorithms with an impressive regret bound.
