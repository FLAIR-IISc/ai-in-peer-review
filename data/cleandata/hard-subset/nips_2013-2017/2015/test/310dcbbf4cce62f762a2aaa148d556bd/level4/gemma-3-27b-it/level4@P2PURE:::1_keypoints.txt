- Reviewer acknowledges authors’ clarification but still questions why each context occurrence cannot be treated as an independent bandit instance when T is large enough to reduce contextual coupling.  
- Requests a tighter problem-dependent upper bound on regret expressed in terms of arm gaps (sum of inverse gaps) instead of the coarse KJ factor.  
- Asks for matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent settings, refined to capture contextual complexity beyond trivial classical-bandit bounds.  
- Notes the paper’s main contribution: proving O(log T) regret for finite J (contexts) and K (actions) when T is sufficiently large.  
- Points out that O(log T) regret is unsurprising because the setting can be viewed as a JK-arm budgeted/action-constrained bandit with only K arms accessible per context.  
- Questions the novelty/innovation of the work given its reducibility to known action-constrained bandit results and requests clarification in rebuttal.  
- Raises a technical question on Theorem 2: whether the stated O(√T + …) term should instead be at least O(√(K T) + …).  
- Observes that the KJ term in proofs comes from events ensuring correct UCB ordering rather than gap-concentration arguments, and questions whether this is optimal.  
- Emphasizes that providing refined problem-dependent bounds and lower bounds would significantly strengthen the paper.  
- Concludes with a neutral overall assessment due to remaining doubts about originality and relation to budgeted/action-constrained bandits.
