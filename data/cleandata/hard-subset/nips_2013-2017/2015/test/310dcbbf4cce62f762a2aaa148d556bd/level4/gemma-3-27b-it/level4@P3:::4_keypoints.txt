- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with time and budget limits, a practically important and challenging setting.
- Main contribution: first demonstration of logarithmic regret in this complex constrained setting.
- Core method: approximate the oracle with Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and combine it with UCB to handle unknown rewards.
- ALP key insight: only the ordering of expected rewards is required, enabling efficient approximation.
- Paper structure: builds intuition with unit-cost case, then generalizes to unknown statistics and heterogeneous costs.
- Theoretical rigor: provides detailed regret bounds and solid analysis using LP relaxation and concentration inequalities.
- Novelty: significant advance over prior work that typically attains only √T regret or uses restrictive assumptions.
- Practical relevance: handles common constraints (budget and time), increasing applicability to real-world problems.
- Clear presentation: well-written and logically organized, with comprehensive related work discussion.
- Insightful algorithm design: ALP analysis offers valuable design principles for constrained contextual bandits.
- Weakness: boundary cases (ρ = qj) incur √T regret and lack an intuitive, satisfying explanation.
- Weakness: extension to heterogeneous costs is relegated to the supplement and needs clearer exposition in the main text.
- Weakness: no empirical evaluation or simulations to validate theoretical results and illustrate boundary-case behavior.
- Acceptance arguments: novel, theoretically sound, significant improvement in regret bounds, and likely influential.
- Rejection concerns: unsatisfactory treatment of boundary cases, missing empirical validation, and terse coverage of heterogeneous costs.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but request clearer intuition for boundary cases and addition of empirical results (even simple simulations).
