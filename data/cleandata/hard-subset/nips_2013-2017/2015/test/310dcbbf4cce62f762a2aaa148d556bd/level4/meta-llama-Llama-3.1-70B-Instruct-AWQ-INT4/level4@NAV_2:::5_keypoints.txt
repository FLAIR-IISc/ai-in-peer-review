- Problem & contribution: Studies a budget-constrained contextual bandit with discrete contexts/actions; main contributions are LP-based computational efficiency and a log(T) regret bound.  
- Algorithm: Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and combines it with UCB as a natural extension.  
- Theoretical analysis: Provides thorough analysis and proofs (in supplement), showing log(T) regret in most cases.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with consistent notation.  
- Strength — efficiency & regret: Computationally efficient algorithm achieving log(T) regret in most cases.  
- Strength — thoroughness: Comprehensive treatment of budget-constraint challenges and prior-work limitations.  
- Strength — clarity: Clear, concise explanation of the algorithm and its performance analysis.  
- Weakness — problem simplicity: Problem formulation is simple and may not capture real-world complexity.  
- Weakness — empirical validation: Lacks numerical experiments to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Suggestion — modeling: Consider parametric reward models for settings with many contexts.  
- Suggestion — motivation: Clarify rationale for combining UCB with ALP, including optimism-in-face-of-uncertainty.  
- Suggestion — experiments: Add numerical experiments in an extended version to show empirical behavior.  
- Quality: Technically sound; claims are supported by theoretical analysis.  
- Originality: Proposal is original; ALP+UCB is natural but the performance analysis is novel.  
- Significance: Valuable contribution with potential real-world applicability; log(T) regret is significant.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Efficient algorithm, strong regret guarantees, and thorough analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Oversimplified formulation and missing empirical evaluation.
