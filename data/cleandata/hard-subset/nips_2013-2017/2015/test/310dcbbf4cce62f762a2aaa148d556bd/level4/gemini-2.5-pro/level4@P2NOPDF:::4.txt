This paper presents a novel and interesting approach to the contextual bandit problem by introducing a budget. This budget allows the algorithm to strategically pass on a percentage of rounds that are likely to be suboptimal, a decision managed through adaptive linear programming. The setup is compelling and the proposed solutions are unique.

My primary question concerns the theoretical analysis of the boundary cases. Could the authors clarify why these scenarios require a separate treatment? The motivation is not immediately obvious, as the condition ρ = q_j, especially for j > 1, does not appear to be fundamentally different from the general case. Elucidating the reasoning behind this distinction would strengthen the paper's theoretical exposition.
