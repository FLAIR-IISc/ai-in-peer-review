- Studies budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts and actions.  
- Main contribution: an LP-relaxation-based, efficiently implementable algorithm (ALP) needing only ordering of expected rewards.  
- Combined ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to learn unknown rewards, yielding O(log T) regret in most cases.  
- Algorithm is computationally attractive and the paper is clearly written.  
- Suggest exploring parametric reward models for scalability to many contexts.  
- Request clearer rationale for UCB-ALP design, noting its optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty flavor and potential relation to UCRL2 updates.  
- Recommend adding numerical experiments to complement theoretical results.  
- Suggest citing related work: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374.  
- Pros: efficient algorithm, optimal (logarithmic) regret in most cases, clear exposition.  
- Cons: limited discussion of high-dimensional/large-context extensions, unclear motivation for UCB-ALP, and lack of experiments.  
- Recommendation: paper merits acceptance as a meaningful improvement over existing work.
