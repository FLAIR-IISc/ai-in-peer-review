This paper explores contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, focusing on finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Although the setting is simplified, these assumptions remain reasonable in practice, ultimately yielding algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by leveraging the adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

The paper is well-written, clear, and thoroughly documented, with relevant work properly cited and motivation clearly stated. This is a solid theoretical contribution. However, the practical aspects are only briefly touched upon in Sections 5 and 6, and unfortunately, no experimental results are provided. Nevertheless, the contributions are substantial, as the authors appear to be the first to achieve logarithmic regret in the context of constrained contextual bandits. The proofs presented are convincing, although a detailed examination of each proof was not conducted. Overall, this is a good paper that makes suitable simplified assumptions to derive O(log T) regret algorithms. To further enhance the paper, a more in-depth discussion of the practical aspects would be beneficial.
