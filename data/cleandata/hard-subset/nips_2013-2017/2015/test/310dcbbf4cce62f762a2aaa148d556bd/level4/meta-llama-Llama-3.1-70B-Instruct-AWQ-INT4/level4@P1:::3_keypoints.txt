- Problem setting: contextual bandits with budget B, horizon T, skip action, context distribution π, known costs c_{j,k}, and reward distributions per (j,k); objective maximize cumulative reward subject to cumulative cost ≤ B.  
- Main contribution: algorithm for the special case (π known, c_{j,k}=1) achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition, with extensions to unknown π and arbitrary costs.  
- Novelty/significance: possibly first work to obtain O(log T) regret for this budgeted contextual bandit setting (prior work gives O(√T)).  
- Method: algorithm built on an approximation algorithm that computes the optimal action sequence when statistics are known; the algorithm is simple and interesting.  
- Strength: well-motivated, clearly written, and produces a near-optimal (logarithmic) regret bound in the main case.  
- Weakness: O(log T) guarantee depends on a mild condition that may not always hold; boundary cases can have Ω(√T) lower bounds.  
- Major concern: heavy reliance on Lemma 1 — reviewer is not convinced of its correctness and questions whether the optimal action sequence always satisfies condition (2) and whether the average constraint legitimately relaxes the hard constraint.  
- Requested clarifications: brief explanation/proof of Lemma 1’s correctness and justification that the average constraint is a valid relaxation of the hard constraint.  
- Minor/technical questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard? Can results be extended to unknown T? Can one assume u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J WLOG when π is unknown?  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance, contingent on clarifications about Lemma 1 and the generality of the results.
