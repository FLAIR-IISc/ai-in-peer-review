- Main theoretical concern: unclear why boundary cases when ρ = q_j for j > 1 are analyzed separately.
- Critique: the paper does not justify why ρ = q_j is special for j > 1, since this equality arises naturally in the formulation.
- The separate treatment of these cases lacks explanation and leaves the analysis ambiguous.
- Strong evaluation: the paper compares to a more powerful baseline using adaptive linear programming that skips a fraction of suboptimal rounds ("budget").
- This baseline comparison is well-motivated and adds rigor to the empirical assessment.
- The proposed method effectively leverages the adaptive-budget baseline, producing a compelling, practical framework.
- Technical contribution: integrating adaptive budgeting into contextual bandits is a meaningful advancement.
- Experimental validation is thorough and supports the practical value of the approach.
- Remaining ambiguity around the boundary-case analysis weakens the theoretical foundation and should be addressed.
- Overall: a valuable, novel methodology offering a clear perspective on contextual bandit optimization.
