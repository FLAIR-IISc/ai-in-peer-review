This paper addresses the problem of contextual bandits under budget and time constraints. The analysis is restricted to a setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While this setting is simplified, the assumptions are reasonable for many practical applications. The key contribution is the development of algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret, a result enabled by an adaptive linear programming approximation of an oracle policy.

The paper is a solid theoretical contribution. It is well-written, clearly motivated, and properly situated within the relevant literature. The theoretical contributions are significant; to my knowledge, this is the first work to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear convincing, although I have not verified them in full detail.

The primary weakness is the limited treatment of practical aspects. The discussion in Sections 5 and 6 is brief, and the paper does not provide any experimental results. The work could be strengthened by a more detailed discussion of its practical implications.

In summary, this is a good paper that makes judicious simplifying assumptions to derive a novel and important O(log T) regret bound.
