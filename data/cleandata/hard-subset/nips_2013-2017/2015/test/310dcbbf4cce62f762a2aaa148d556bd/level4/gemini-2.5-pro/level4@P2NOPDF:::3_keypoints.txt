- Problem: Contextual bandits with a hard budget B over horizon T, contexts j∈J drawn from π, actions k∈K plus skip, costs c_{j,k}, rewards in [0,1], aim to maximize reward under budget constraint.  
- Main result: Algorithm for known context distribution and unit costs achieves O(log T) regret under a mild non-boundary condition.  
- Extension: Authors extend the approach to unknown π and arbitrary costs.  
- Strength: Significant theoretical advance—first to achieve logarithmic O(log T) regret vs prior O(√T) results.  
- Strength: Paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with accessible exposition of algorithm and analysis.  
- Strength: Algorithm is novel, simple, and based on approximating the offline optimal policy.  
- Weakness: Main O(log T) guarantee relies on the optimal policy not lying on a “boundary”; consequences when this fails are not explored.  
- Suggestion: Recommend investigating boundary cases, e.g., proving a lower bound (Ω(√T)) or otherwise characterizing necessity of the condition.  
- Clarification resolved: Reviewer is convinced by the authors’ rebuttal validating Lemma 1 (relaxing per-trial budget to an average constraint).  
- Remaining questions: What is the offline computational complexity (is it NP-hard)? Can the algorithm handle unknown horizon T? Is the assumed utility ordering still justifiable when π is unknown and must be learned?  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—novel, important contribution despite minor clarifications requested.
