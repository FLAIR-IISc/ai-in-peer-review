- Setting closely resembles existing budgeted bandit problems with action constraints; for large horizons context coupling may be negligible.  
- Reviewer urges deriving a clean problem-dependent upper bound expressed in terms of arm gaps.  
- Also recommends proving matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent cases.  
- Key claimed contribution: with finite contexts J and actions K and sufficiently large T, regret of order log(T) is achievable.  
- Results and writing are precise, but the log(T) finding is unsurprising since finite J,K can be seen as a JK-arm action-constrained bandit.  
- The paper’s originality/innovativeness is unconvincing; authors should clarify novelty in rebuttal.  
- Technical question: Theorem 2’s case 2 bound O(√T + ...) might need to be at least O(√(K T) + ...).  
- Concern that problem-dependent bounds are stated as O(KJ log T) rather than a refined sum over inverse arm gaps; current proof uses UCB-order events causing KJ instead of gap-sum.  
- Request for refined lower bounds that account for contextual complexity beyond trivial classical-bandit lower bounds.
