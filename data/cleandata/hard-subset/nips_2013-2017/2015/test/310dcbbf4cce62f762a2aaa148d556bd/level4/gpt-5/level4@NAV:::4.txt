Given the brevity of this review, I have a single question: why must the boundary cases be analyzed separately? It is not clear to me that ρ = q_j is in any way special, especially for j > 1. The paper studies contextual bandits against a stronger baseline—one that uses adaptive linear programming to forgo a certain percentage of suboptimal rounds (which the authors refer to as the budget). The setup is compelling, and the solutions are distinctive.
