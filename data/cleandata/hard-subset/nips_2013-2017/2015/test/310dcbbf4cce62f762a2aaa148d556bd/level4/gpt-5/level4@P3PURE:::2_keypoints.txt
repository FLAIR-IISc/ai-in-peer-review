- Studies contextual bandits under joint budget and time constraints.
- Analysis assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed action costs, and a single budget constraint; assumptions are simplified but reasonable.
- Introduces an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.
- Proposes algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret.
- Paper is clearly written, well documented, and cites related work appropriately.
- Motivation is explicit and the theoretical contribution is solid.
- Reviewer believes the authors are the first to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Proofs appear convincing, though the reviewer did not verify all details.
- Practical aspects are only briefly treated (Sections 5 and 6) and there are no experimental results.
- Additional discussion of practical implications and empirical validation would strengthen the paper.
- Overall, a good paper with significant contributions.
