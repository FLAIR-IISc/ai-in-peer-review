- Paper addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T, proposing UCB-ALP to handle budget coupling across time.  
- Main contribution: computationally efficient UCB-ALP algorithm achieving O(log T) regret (non-boundary) and O(sqrt T) in boundary cases.  
- Method: introduce Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) as a tractable approximation to the DP oracle when statistics are known.  
- ALP needs only the relative ordering of expected rewards, enabling combination with UCB for unknown rewards.  
- Analysis shows ALP is near-optimal with O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and leverages sampling-without-replacement concentration arguments.  
- Strength: first to achieve logarithmic regret in this constrained setting, improving on prior O(sqrt T) results.  
- Strength: technically sound and novel LP-based approximation with a clean decoupling of ranking errors in the regret analysis.  
- Strength: paper is clear, well-organized, and builds intuition from known-statistics to bandit settings.  
- Strength: UCB-ALP is simple, computationally efficient, and practical (small LP each step).  
- Weakness: boundary cases where rho equals some q_j degrade regret to O(sqrt T); practical sensitivity near boundaries is not analyzed.  
- Weakness: need stronger justification for LP relaxation — a formal statement of computational hardness of the DP oracle is missing.  
- Weakness: Lemma 1 (static LP upper bounds oracle) is central but lacks an intuitive explanation in the main text.  
- Questions: clarify if assumed ordering u*_1 > u*_2 > ... > u*_J is w.l.o.g. in the bandit setting and whether analysis depends on learning this ordering.  
- Questions: can the framework be extended to unknown or random time horizon T?  
- Recommendation: strong accept — significant theoretical advance with minor issues around boundary cases and exposition.
