My primary question concerns the analysis of boundary cases: could the authors clarify why these cases necessitate a separate treatment? It is not immediately apparent why the condition rho = q_j, particularly for j > 1, is considered special. 

Overall, the paper investigates contextual bandits against a formidable baseline, which uses adaptive linear programming to selectively pass on a percentage of suboptimal rounds (defined as the "budget"). The problem formulation is compelling and the proposed solutions are novel.
