- Addresses constrained contextual bandits using adaptive linear programming (ALP) to balance exploration and exploitation under budget/time constraints.  
- Proposes combining ALP with UCB for unknown rewards to approximate the oracle.  
- Major theoretical contribution: first to establish logarithmic regret bounds in this setting.  
- Analysis is rigorous, using concentration inequalities and careful regret decomposition.  
- Extensions cover heterogeneous costs and unknown context distributions, showing framework adaptability.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, progressing from known-statistics to realistic scenarios.  
- Algorithms are computationally efficient and practical to implement.  
- Weakness: explanation for boundary cases (where regret becomes O(√T)) lacks sufficient intuition.  
- Specifically unclear why parameter settings with ρ = q_j cause degradation beyond the logarithmic bound.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation—more experiments would strengthen practical claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant open problem, offers novel and technically sound solutions with clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate intuition for boundary-case behavior and absence of empirical validation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper meriting acceptance with minor revisions to clarify boundary-case discussion.
