- Presents an intriguing analysis of contextual bandits against a stronger baseline that uses adaptive linear programming to pass on a percentage of suboptimal rounds ("budget").  
- The experimental/setup idea is interesting and the proposed solutions are unique.  
- Unclear why boundary cases are treated differently; the rationale for distinct treatment needs clarification.  
- The specific choice rho = q_j for j > 1 lacks clear significance and requires explanation.  
- Comparing against a more robust baseline is commendable and provides a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Incorporation of adaptive linear programming to optimize budget allocation adds sophistication to the analysis.  
- Recommends addressing the boundary-case concern to strengthen clarity and the paper’s arguments.  
- Overall, a promising contribution to contextual bandits that could have greater impact after minor revisions.
