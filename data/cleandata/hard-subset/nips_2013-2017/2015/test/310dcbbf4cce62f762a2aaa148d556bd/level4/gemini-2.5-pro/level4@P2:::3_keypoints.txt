- Problem: contextual bandits with both time horizon T and budget B introduce complex temporal dependencies, complicating exploration–exploitation trade-offs.

- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm that combines UCB learning with an adaptive LP relaxation and achieves O(log T) regret.

- ALP (known-statistics, unit-cost) insight: solve an LP with average budget constraint at each step; yields O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and approximates the intractable oracle.

- Extensions: treatment of unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.

- Originality/significance: first known work to achieve logarithmic regret under hard budget constraints; adaptive LP decoupling planning and learning is novel and potentially influential.

- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, building intuition from simplified to full problem.

- Technical quality: algorithm is simple and computationally efficient; analysis leverages a sampling-without-replacement mapping and concentration inequalities.

- Weakness: Lemma 1 (LP upper bound) needs stronger justification or a proof sketch in the main text since the LP uses an average constraint versus the oracle’s hard constraint.

- Weakness: boundary cases (ρ equal to q_j) are excluded from the main O(log T) result; need discussion of performance as ρ approaches boundaries and whether O(√T) is optimal there.

- Weakness: assumption of contexts ordered by optimal rewards is unclear for unknown-reward setting—does the algorithm need prior ordering or learn it, and how are ordering errors handled?

- Minor: clarify computational complexity of the true oracle (is it NP-hard?) to further motivate ALP.

- Minor: discuss extension to unknown time horizon T.

- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; paper is an excellent and impactful contribution.
