The paper considers contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, focusing on a simplified setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Although this setting is restrictive, the assumptions are still reasonable in practice, and they enable the development of algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret. Notably, this is achieved by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

The paper is well-written and clearly documented, with relevant work properly cited and motivation explicitly stated. This is a solid theoretical paper that makes significant contributions to the field. The authors are, to my knowledge, the first to obtain logarithmic regret in the case of constrained contextual bandits. The proofs provided are convincing, although I did not verify them in full detail.

One notable limitation of the paper is that practical aspects are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are provided. Nevertheless, the theoretical contributions are substantial, and the authors' decision to make simplified assumptions is justified by the resulting O(log T) regret algorithms. Overall, this is a good paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field, and I believe it would benefit from a more detailed discussion of practical aspects.
