- Problem setting: contextual bandits under budget and time constraints with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget (simplified but reasonable).
- Method: introduces an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle to design algorithms.
- Main theoretical result: algorithms achieve O(log T) regret.
- Novelty: apparently the first work to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Writing and scholarship: paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and thoroughly cites relevant prior work.
- Proofs: theoretical arguments appear sound and convincing (not every step independently verified by reviewer).
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical aspects; Sections 5 and 6 are brief.
- Missing validation: no experimental results to demonstrate practical performance; empirical study would strengthen the paper.
- Overall assessment: strong and important theoretical contribution despite limited practical exploration.
