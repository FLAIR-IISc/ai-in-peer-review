- Problem: Studies constrained contextual bandits with both budget and time limits, finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.
- Setting: Focuses on a simplified but practically relevant model that enables rigorous analysis.
- Method: Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle policy.
- Exploration strategy: Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP when expected rewards are unknown.
- Main result: Achieves logarithmic regret O(log T) in most cases and O(√T) in boundary cases.
- Theoretical contribution: First to demonstrate logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under reasonable assumptions.
- Analysis quality: Thorough theoretical analysis with careful treatment of boundary cases and challenges.
- Comparison to prior work: Extends beyond prior budgeted and contextual bandit work and differs from RCB approaches that obtain O(√T).
- Practical relevance: Assumptions are argued to be reasonable for applications like clinical trials and wireless networks.
- Writing and exposition: Well-written, clearly organized, and comprehensively referenced.
- Main weakness: No empirical validation or experiments demonstrating practical performance.
- Practical discussion: Sections on implementation/practical aspects are brief and could be strengthened with concrete examples.
- Literature gap: Does not sufficiently address two recent related papers [21, 22] on dynamic contexts.
- Recommendation: Strong theoretical paper suitable for NIPS, with empirical validation needed to broaden practical impact.
