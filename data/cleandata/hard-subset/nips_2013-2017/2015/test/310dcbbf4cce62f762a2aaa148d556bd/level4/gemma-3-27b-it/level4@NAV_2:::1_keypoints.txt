- Studies contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, extending the classical setting.
- Main contribution: shows logarithmic regret is achievable for finite action and context spaces under these constraints.
- Proposes UCB-ALP, an algorithm combining adaptive linear programming (ALP) with upper confidence bounds.
- Presents a detailed, rigorous theoretical analysis of UCB-ALP’s performance, including boundary cases.
- ALP approximates the oracle solution effectively and adapts to remaining budget and time.
- Addresses both known and unknown context distributions, broadening applicability.
- Algorithm is computationally efficient.
- Paper is well-written and clearly situates itself relative to related work.
- Novelty claim is tempered by strong connections to prior work on budgeted/action-constrained bandits.
- Regret bounds in boundary cases (O(√T + log T)) could be tightened; arm-gap based analysis might improve them.
- Absence of lower bounds is a limitation, leaving optimality unconfirmed.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking authors to address tightness of bounds and include or discuss lower bounds.
