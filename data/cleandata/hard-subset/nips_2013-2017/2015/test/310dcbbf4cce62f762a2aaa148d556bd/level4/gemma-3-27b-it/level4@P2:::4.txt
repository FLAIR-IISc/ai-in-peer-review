This paper addresses constrained contextual bandits, a relevant extension of the standard contextual bandit framework incorporating budget and time constraints. The core idea is to leverage adaptive linear programming (ALP) to approximate the optimal policy, and combine it with an upper confidence bound (UCB) approach for the unknown reward case. This work is a significant contribution as it establishes logarithmic regret bounds – a first for this constrained setting. The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and the technical approach appears sound.

The authors effectively position their work within the existing literature, referencing key papers in contextual bandits, constrained bandits, and linear programming. They clearly articulate the challenges presented by the constraints and explain how their approach addresses them. The use of ALP to approximate the oracle is a clever idea, and the extension to heterogeneous costs and unknown context distributions demonstrates the versatility of the approach. 

A major strength is the rigor of the analysis. The authors provide detailed proofs and carefully consider various scenarios, including boundary cases where the regret bound degrades to sublinear. The paper thoroughly investigates and provides insights into the intricacies of the proposed algorithms. The discussion of how the work relates to and improves upon prior work (e.g., [17], [20], [21], [22]) is also strong. 

One minor point requiring clarification is the detailed justification for the separate analysis of “boundary cases” (where ρ = q<sub>j</sub>). While the authors acknowledge these cases have different regret characteristics, the explanation for *why* they require distinct treatment could be more intuitive. It isn’t immediately obvious why these specific values of ρ are fundamentally different in terms of algorithm performance. Expanding on this aspect would strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** This is the first work to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.
*   **Significance:** The problem setting is practically relevant and the results are likely to be impactful.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed algorithms are well-designed, and the analysis appears rigorous.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
*   **Originality:** The combination of ALP and UCB is a novel approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The justification for separate analysis in boundary cases could be more detailed.
*   While the extensions to heterogeneous costs and unknown context distributions are valuable, the analysis in these sections appears less detailed and relies on deferred supplementary material.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field. The technical approach is novel, the analysis is rigorous, and the results are significant. Despite a minor clarification needed regarding the boundary case analysis, I recommend acceptance. This work advances the state-of-the-art in constrained contextual bandits and opens up new avenues for research.
