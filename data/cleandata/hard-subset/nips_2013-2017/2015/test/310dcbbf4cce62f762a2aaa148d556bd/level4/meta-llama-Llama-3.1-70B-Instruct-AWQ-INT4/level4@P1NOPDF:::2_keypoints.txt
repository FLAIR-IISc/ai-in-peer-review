- Studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints in a simplified setting: finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget constraint.
- Assumptions are restrictive but reasonable in practice and allow strong theoretical results.
- Introduces an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle to design algorithms.
- Algorithms achieve O(log T) regret — claimed first to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Paper is well-written, clearly documented, properly cites related work, and has explicit motivation.
- Proofs are convincing, though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Major limitation: practical aspects are briefly discussed and there are no experimental results.
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution that would benefit from a more detailed discussion of practical implications.
