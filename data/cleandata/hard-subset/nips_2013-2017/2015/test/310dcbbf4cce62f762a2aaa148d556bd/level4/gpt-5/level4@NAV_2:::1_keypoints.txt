- As horizon grows, inter-context coupling fades, so each context arrival could be treated as an independent bandit instance.  
- The paper would be stronger with a clean, problem-dependent upper bound expressed in terms of arm gaps; the supplementary bound needs reworking.  
- Ideally provide matching lower bounds in both instance-dependent and instance-independent regimes.  
- Main claim: with finite J contexts and K actions and large T, O(log T) regret is attainable — a new guarantee but not surprising.  
- The setting is essentially a budgeted bandit with JK arms and an action-access constraint, so novelty relative to existing action-constrained bandits is unclear.  
- Question on Theorem 2 case (2): the stated O(sqrt(T)+…) may be missing a dependence on K (e.g., O(sqrt(KT)+…)).  
- Instance-dependent bounds in Theorem 2 use O(KJ log T); request a more refined gap-dependent form (sums over inverse gaps) instead of the KJ aggregate.  
- Ask whether the UCB-ordering based proof (which yields KJ factors) is optimal or could be improved to gap-based bounds.  
- Request for lower bounds sharpened to reflect contextual complexity beyond trivial reductions to classical bandit lower bounds.  
- Overall recommendation: neutral due to perceived closeness to existing budgeted/action-constrained bandit settings and unclear novelty.
