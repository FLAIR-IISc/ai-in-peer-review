This paper addresses a budget-constrained contextual bandit problem within a setting of discrete contexts and actions. The primary contribution lies in the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm, which leverages a linear programming approximation to achieve a log(T) regret bound. The manuscript is commendably well-written and its arguments are easy to follow.

For future work, the authors could consider employing a parametric model for the reward, which would be particularly advantageous in scenarios with a large context space. Additionally, in Section 4.1, the rationale for integrating UCB with ALP in the UCB-ALP algorithm could be more explicitly stated. It seems to be an application of the "optimism in the face of uncertainty" principle, and clarifying its potential equivalence to the policy update in algorithms like UCRL2 would be beneficial. Including numerical experiments in an extended version of this work would also significantly enhance its impact.

The authors may also find the following related work of interest: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

This paper warrants acceptance as it introduces an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits that achieves optimal regret in many cases. The work is interesting and presents a clear improvement over existing literature.
