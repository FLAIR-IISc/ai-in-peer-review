This paper presents an intriguing analysis of contextual bandits against a stronger baseline, one that utilizes adaptive linear programming to strategically pass on a certain percentage of suboptimal rounds, referred to as the "budget." The setup is interesting, and the proposed solutions are unique. However, one aspect of the analysis warrants further clarification: the distinct treatment of boundary cases. Specifically, it is unclear why the case of rho = q_j necessitates separate analysis, particularly for j > 1, as this value does not appear inherently special.
