This paper analyzes contextual bandits against a strong baseline – one employing adaptive linear programming to strategically abstain from acting on a certain percentage of suboptimal rounds (referred to as a “budget” by the authors). The setup is interesting and the proposed solutions are unique. However, a key question remains unanswered: what necessitates analyzing boundary cases differently? The parameter ρ = q<sub>j</sub> does not appear to possess any special properties, particularly for j > 1, to warrant separate consideration.
