This paper studies a contextual bandit setting with a hard budget B and a known horizon T. In each round, a context j is drawn i.i.d. from a fixed distribution π over {1,…,J}, and the agent selects an action k from {0} ∪ {1,…,K}, where k=0 denotes “skip.” If k=0, no reward or cost is incurred; if k>0, the agent receives a reward y ∈ [0,1] drawn from a context–action–specific distribution and pays cost c_{j,k}>0 (known to the agent). The objective is to maximize cumulative reward subject to the constraint that cumulative cost never exceeds B.

The main focus is the unit-cost, known-context-distribution case (c_{j,k}=1 and π known), for which the authors propose an algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild non-boundary condition. They then extend the approach to unknown π and to heterogeneous costs c_{j,k}.

Compared to more general formulations such as [16], which achieve O(√T) regret, this work appears to be the first to obtain O(log T) regret for constrained contextual bandits (again, outside boundary cases). The approach builds on an approximation to the oracle policy when statistics are known, via an adaptive linear programming (ALP) relaxation. The resulting algorithm is simple yet quite compelling.

The paper is well motivated and very clearly written. It would be even stronger if O(log T) regret could be shown without exception, or if an Ω(√T) lower bound were established for the boundary regimes.

Much of the development hinges on Lemma 1, and I was initially unconvinced. Specifically, it was unclear why the average-budget LP constraint truly relaxes the hard budget constraint, since the optimal sequence of actions need not satisfy (2) at every round. A brief justification would help. => After reading the rebuttal, I am convinced by the authors’ clarification. Thank you.

Minor comments:
- Is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard?
- Can the results be extended to unknown T (e.g., anytime versions)?
- Can we assume u*_1 > u*_2 > … > u*_J without loss of generality even when the context distribution is unknown?

I strongly recommend acceptance.
