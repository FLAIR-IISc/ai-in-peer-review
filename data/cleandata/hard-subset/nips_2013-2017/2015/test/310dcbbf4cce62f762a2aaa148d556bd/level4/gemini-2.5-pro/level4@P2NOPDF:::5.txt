This paper considers the budget-constrained contextual bandit problem in a setting with discrete contexts and actions. The primary contribution is a computationally efficient algorithm, which leverages a linear programming approximation, that is shown to achieve a logarithmic (log(T)) regret bound. The paper is exceptionally well-written and the technical arguments are easy to follow.

I offer the following suggestions for improvement and future work:

*   In Section 4.1, the rationale for the proposed UCB-ALP algorithm could be made more explicit. Clarifying how the algorithm's design follows the "optimism in the face of uncertainty" principle would strengthen the presentation. It might also be beneficial to discuss the connection between the proposed policy update and related mechanisms in the literature, such as the one employed in UCRL2.
*   A valuable direction for future work would be to extend this approach to use a parametric model for the reward function. Such an extension would significantly broaden the applicability of the method, particularly to problems with a large number of contexts.
*   While perhaps beyond the scope of this conference version, including numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would provide valuable empirical validation of the theoretical results and offer practical insights into the algorithm's performance.
*   The authors may also want to consider the following related work in their discussion: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

This paper merits acceptance. It presents an interesting and elegant solution to an important problem, providing an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits that achieves near-optimal regret in most cases. The work represents a clear and significant improvement over existing methods.
