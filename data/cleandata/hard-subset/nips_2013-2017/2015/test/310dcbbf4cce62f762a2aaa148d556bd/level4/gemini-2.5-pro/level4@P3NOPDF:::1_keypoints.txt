- Paper examines contextual multi-armed bandits with budget and time constraints for finite contexts (J) and actions (K), claiming O(log T) regret for large T; well-written and thorough.  
- Main novelty questioned: setting can be viewed as a standard budgeted bandit with JK arms and action constraints, so O(log T) is unsurprising.  
- Paper does not sufficiently distinguish the “contextual” problem from existing frameworks or explain fundamental differences.  
- Theorem 2’s problem-independent bound is unclear: likely should depend on K (e.g., O(√(KT))) rather than just O(√T).  
- The problem-dependent bound O(KJ log T) is coarse; a gap-dependent bound expressed via sums of 1/Δ_i is expected or an explanation why it’s hard to obtain.  
- Paper lacks lower bounds: beyond the trivial J=1 case, it should provide bounds that capture context–budget–time interactions and ideally matching lower bounds.  
- After rebuttal, reviewer remains unconvinced that context coupling yields a fundamentally different problem for large T; treating context-action pairs as independent arms may suffice.  
- Recommendations: present a clean, gap-dependent upper bound and matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and independent cases; otherwise the contribution appears incremental.
