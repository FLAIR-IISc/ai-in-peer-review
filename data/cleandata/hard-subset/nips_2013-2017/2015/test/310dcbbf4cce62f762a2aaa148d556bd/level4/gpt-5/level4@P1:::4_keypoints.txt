- Studies contextual bandits with time and a single budget constraint and proposes an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation to the oracle when statistics are known.  
- Shows ALP achieves O(1) regret except on measure-zero “boundary” instances where ρ equals a cumulative context probability qj.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP that combines ALP with UCB to learn reward ordering and obtains O(log T) regret for non-boundary cases and O(√T) regret on boundary cases.  
- Focuses on finite contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget, yielding computationally simple algorithms and stronger, instance-dependent guarantees compared to prior work.  
- Contrasts with prior resourceful contextual bandit results that typically give O(√T) regret under more general settings and higher complexity.  
- Reviewer asks why boundary cases must be analyzed separately and what makes ρ = qj intrinsically special, especially for j > 1.  
- Requests intuition for the √T term—suggests stochastic fluctuations of bτ/τ around the threshold might cause frequent switches in the optimal LP solution (“thrashing”).  
- Asks whether smoothing or tie-breaking schemes (margin, randomized thresholding, biasing) could mitigate boundary effects without harming non-boundary correctness.  
- Asks if there are lower bounds proving √T regret is unavoidable on boundary instances for policies relying only on reward ordering, or if the rate is an artifact of the analysis.  
- Overall assessment: setup is interesting, ALP backbone is clean, UCB combination is appealing and computationally efficient.
