- Studies contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T; contexts from distribution π, actions include skip (0) with zero cost; goal is to maximize cumulative reward subject to budget.

- Main focus: special case with known π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1; proposes an algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild condition.

- Extends the algorithm to the general setting with unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.

- Compared to prior work [16] (which attains O(√T) regret), this may be the first to achieve O(log T) regret, conditional on the mild assumption.

- Algorithm uses a simple approximation to compute the optimal action sequence when statistics are known; approach is intriguing.

- Importance would increase if O(log T) bound were universal; otherwise need Ω(√T) lower bounds for boundary cases.

- Key technical result relies on Lemma 1; reviewer initially doubted whether eq.(2) holds for all trials (average vs hard constraint), but the rebuttal resolved the concern.

- Minor suggestions: check NP-hardness of obtaining the exact optimal action sequence; extend results to unknown T; justify assuming u^1>u^2>...>u^*_J w.l.o.g. when π is unknown.

- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
