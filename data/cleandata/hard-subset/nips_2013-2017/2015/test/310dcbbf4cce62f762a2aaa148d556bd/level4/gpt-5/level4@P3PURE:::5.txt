This paper studies a budget-constrained contextual bandit problem with discrete contexts and actions. Its main contribution is an algorithm that is computationally efficient via a linear programming (LP) approximation and achieves a log(T) regret bound. The paper is well written and easy to follow.

Suggestions:
- For future work, consider using a parametric model for the reward, which is especially useful when the number of contexts is large.
- In Section 4.1, when combining UCB with ALP, please clarify the rationale underlying the UCB-ALP algorithm. It appears to follow the “optimism in the face of uncertainty” principle and is probably equivalent to the policy update in UCRL2.
- It would be valuable to include numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper.
- You may want to cite this related work: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

Overall, this paper merits acceptance. It provides an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with optimal regret in most cases, and it is an interesting and meaningful improvement over existing work.
