- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T, extending classic MAB to include context and cost constraints.  
- Main contribution: algorithm achieving logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits, improving over prior O(√T) bounds.  
- Algorithm derived from an approximation for the known-statistics optimal action sequence and extended to unknown-statistics settings.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with detailed lemmas, theorems, and a comprehensive regret bound proof.  
- Paper clearly written, well-motivated, and carefully explains problem formulation and algorithm implementation.  
- Computational complexity and implementation details are provided and discussed.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review and positions the contribution relative to existing research.  
- Extends algorithm to practical cases where statistics are unknown, increasing applicability.  
- Assumes known context distribution (minor limitation) but offers a heuristic to handle unknown context distributions.  
- Assumes known costs (minor limitation) but proposes a generalization for unknown costs.  
- Lacks experimental results to empirically validate algorithm performance.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept due to novelty, significance, and technical soundness.
