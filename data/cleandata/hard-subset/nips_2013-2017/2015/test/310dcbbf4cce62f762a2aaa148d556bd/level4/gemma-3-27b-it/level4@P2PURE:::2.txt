This paper investigates contextual bandits under budget and time constraints, focusing on the case of finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget limitation. While these assumptions represent a simplification of more general settings, they remain practically relevant and enable the development of algorithms achieving O(log T) regret through an adaptive linear programming approximation of the optimal policy.

The paper is clearly written and well-documented, with appropriate citations to related work and a well-defined motivation. It presents a strong theoretical contribution. Discussion of practical considerations is limited to Sections 5 and 6, and the absence of experimental validation is noted. Nevertheless, the authors appear to be the first to demonstrate logarithmic regret bounds for constrained contextual bandits, representing a significant advance in the field. The proofs presented are convincing, though a complete verification was not performed. Overall, this is a valuable paper that makes sensible simplifying assumptions to achieve provably efficient, O(log T) regret algorithms. Further discussion of the practical implications and potential extensions would be beneficial.
