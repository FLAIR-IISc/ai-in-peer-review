After rebuttal: I appreciate the authors’ clarifications. I agree the setting is not identical to standard budgeted/action-constrained MABs, yet it still feels quite close: as T grows large, the coupling across contexts diminishes, and it is not obvious why each context occurrence cannot be treated as an (action-constrained) instance. The contribution would be stronger with a clear, gap-dependent upper bound (the supplementary bound needs tightening/reworking) and, ideally, matching lower bounds in both problem-dependent and problem-independent regimes.

Summary and relation to prior work:
- The paper studies contextual bandits with both a budget B and horizon T (“constrained contextual bandits”), where actions incur costs and the process ends when budget or time is exhausted. The focus is on finite contexts J and actions K and on the proportional regime B/T = ρ.
- With known statistics (unit costs, known context distribution), the authors approximate the dynamic oracle via a linear program with an adaptive average budget constraint (ALP). They show ALP is near-optimal with O(1) regret except for measure-zero “boundary” values of ρ, where the regret is O(√T).
- With unknown rewards, they combine ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) and prove O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases. They also discuss extensions to unknown context distribution (empirical ALP) and heterogeneous costs.
- Relative to Resourceful Contextual Bandits (Mixture Elimination, O(√T) against a finite policy set) and bandits with knapsacks (and concave/convex knapsack formulations achieving O(√T)), the main claimed novelty is an instance-dependent O(log T) regret for discrete contexts/actions with adaptive budget allocation.

Strengths:
- Clear formulation of a practically motivated constrained contextual bandit model.
- A simple, computationally efficient ALP approximation that only needs reward ordering and yields O(1) regret off boundaries.
- A clean UCB-ALP design achieving O(JK log T) regret (non-boundary) with careful decomposition of regret sources (action-ranking vs. context-ranking vs. budget fluctuations).
- Useful technical insights: concentration of the remaining budget under adaptive decisions and mapping to sampling-without-replacement.

Weaknesses and questions:
- Originality/novelty: With finite J and K, the setting can be viewed as a budgeted, time-constrained MAB with JK arms and action constraints (only K arms available per round due to the context). For large T, O(log T) regret is not surprising if one can correctly rank arms and manage budget adaptively. Please clarify more sharply what aspect cannot be reduced to an action-constrained budgeted bandit with an adaptive knapsack controller, beyond the context-coupling.
- Theorem 2 (boundary case): The stated regret is O(√T + JK log T). Is the √T term tight and independent of K, or should it scale as √(K T) in worst case? Please justify the dependence carefully (e.g., whether the √T originates solely from budget fluctuations rather than action set size).
- Problem-dependent bounds: The current O(JK log T) bound is problem-independent. For classic UCB, refined bounds sum inverse gap terms. Can you provide a gap-dependent regret bound reflecting (i) within-context action gaps and (ii) cross-context threshold gaps induced by the LP? A sharper, gap-based bound would strengthen both theory and interpretability. The supplementary bound would benefit from a cleaner, gap-dependent statement and proof.
- Lower bounds: It would be valuable to include problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds tailored to the constrained contextual setting (beyond reductions to classical bandits with zero cost or a single context). Can the lower bounds reflect the complexity introduced by context distribution and the LP threshold structure (e.g., boundary vs. non-boundary regimes)?
- Scope of “logarithmic regret”: The abstract claims logarithmic regret “except for certain boundary cases.” Please make explicit the measure of boundary parameters and whether the boundary rate can be improved using alternative exploration schedules or refined budget control.

Quality:
- The technical development is largely sound. The ALP analysis is insightful and the coupling/concentration arguments are well-motivated. Some proofs in the supplement (especially those underpinning the context-ranking error counts and the boundary-case analysis) would benefit from clearer gap-dependent constants and streamlined presentation.

Clarity:
- The paper is generally well written and organized. Explanations of the threshold j̃(ρ), adaptive budget ratio bτ/τ, and the event partition for ranking errors are clear. A crisper discussion on how and why the model differs from budgeted/action-constrained MABs would help readers assess novelty.

Originality:
- The adaptive LP relaxation and its use to decouple learning and budget control in contextual settings is appealing. However, given finite J and K, the extent to which the contribution goes beyond action-constrained budgeted bandits is not entirely convincing without gap-dependent bounds and lower bounds.

Significance:
- The results are practically relevant and computationally attractive. If complemented with refined bounds and lower bounds, the work would provide a more definitive step toward understanding constrained contextual bandits.

Pros:
- Simple, efficient algorithms (ALP, UCB-ALP) with strong guarantees.
- Near-optimality of ALP with known statistics (O(1) off boundaries).
- Clear regret decomposition and novel concentration tools for adaptive budgets.

Cons:
- Limited problem-dependent theory; no lower bounds.
- Novelty relative to budgeted/action-constrained MABs with finite arms remains somewhat unclear.
- Boundary-case understanding and dependence on K/J could be sharpened.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide gap-dependent upper bounds separating within-context and cross-context terms; revise the supplementary proofs accordingly.
- Add problem-independent and problem-dependent lower bounds that account for context complexity and boundary vs. non-boundary regimes.
- Clarify the precise relation (and non-reducibility) to budgeted/action-constrained bandits and why the adaptive LP structure is essential.

Overall: Promising approach with solid techniques and practical algorithms. I remain neutral pending stronger evidence of novelty and sharper (gap-dependent and lower-bound) theory.
