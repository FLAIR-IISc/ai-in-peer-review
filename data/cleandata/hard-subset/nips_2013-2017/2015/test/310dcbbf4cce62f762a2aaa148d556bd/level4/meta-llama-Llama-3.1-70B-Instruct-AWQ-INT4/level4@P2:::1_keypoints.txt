- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for constrained contextual bandits with near-optimal performance when system statistics are known.
- Introduces UCB-ALP combining ALP with UCB to handle unknown expected rewards, achieving logarithmic regret except O(√T) in boundary cases.
- Provides detailed regret analyses for both ALP and UCB-ALP.
- Extends results to settings with unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs.
- Writing is precise and results are interesting.
- Reviewer questions originality, suggesting the problem reduces to a time-constrained, budgeted, action-constrained bandit with JK arms.
- Requests clearer statement of the paper's novel contributions and innovations.
- Challenges the regret bound in Theorem 2, suggesting it should be O(√(KT)+...) rather than O(√T+...).
- Recommends expressing problem-dependent bounds more finely, e.g., as sums of inverse arm gaps.
- Notes absence of lower bounds and asks for both problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds.
- Overall verdict: neutral pending clarifications on innovation and technical bounds.
