

Since this is a short review, I pose a single question: what is the justification for treating boundary cases differently? The condition ρ = q_j does not appear particularly special, especially for j > 1. The paper analyzes contextual bandits against a stronger baseline—specifically, an adaptive linear programming approach that skips a fraction of suboptimal rounds (referred to as "budget" by the authors). The setup is interesting, and the solutions presented are novel.
