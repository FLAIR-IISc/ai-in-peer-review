- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits, focusing on computational efficiency and logarithmic regret.  
- Core contribution: LP relaxation to approximate the oracle policy, yielding the Adaptive LP (ALP) algorithm and UCB-ALP for unknown rewards.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.  
- Novel result: first to achieve logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual bandit setting.  
- Emphasizes computational efficiency via LP-based algorithms suitable for practical use.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with clearly stated regret bounds.  
- Problem is well-motivated with real-world applications (e.g., crowdsourcing, clinical trials).  
- Weakness: simplified setting assumes discrete contexts and actions, limiting applicability to continuous/complex domains.  
- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation—no numerical experiments to validate practical performance.  
- Weakness: rationale for combining UCB with ALP could be more explicitly connected to algorithms like UCRL2.  
- Related-work note: cites relevant literature but should acknowledge Agrawal et al. (2015) on bandits with concave rewards and convex constraints.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, computationally efficient, clearly presented, and an improvement over prior approaches.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited immediate impact due to simplified setting and lack of empirical validation.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommend acceptance (NIPS 2015).  
- Suggestions for future work: extend to continuous contexts/actions, use parametric reward models for high-dimensional contexts, run extensive experiments, and further relate UCB-ALP to UCRL2.
