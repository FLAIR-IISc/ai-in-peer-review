- Problem: Contextual bandits with budget B and known horizon T; contexts j ~ π, actions k∈{0,1..K} with costs c_{j,k}, goal maximize reward under cumulative cost ≤ B.  
- Core contribution (special case): For known π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, authors propose an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extension: Results generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Significance: First to obtain O(log T) regret for this constrained bandit problem, improving on prior O(√T) results.  
- Method: Algorithm uses an approximation of the optimal action sequence under full knowledge; notable for simplicity.  
- Writing and rigor: Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and technically sound.  
- Condition caveat: O(log T) bound currently requires a mild condition; universal validity would be stronger but condition is reasonable.  
- Reliance on Lemma 1: Analysis depends heavily on Lemma 1; reviewer’s initial concern about Eq. (2) was resolved by the rebuttal and Lemma 1 is endorsed.  
- Minor suggestion 1: Clarify whether computing the exact optimal action sequence is NP-hard to contextualize the approximation.  
- Minor suggestion 2: Discuss extension of results to unknown T.  
- Minor suggestion 3: In the unknown π case, address whether the ordering u^{(1)} > u^{(2)} > … > u^{(J)} can be assumed WLOG.  
- Recommendation: Minor concerns addressed; strong contribution and strong accept recommendation.
