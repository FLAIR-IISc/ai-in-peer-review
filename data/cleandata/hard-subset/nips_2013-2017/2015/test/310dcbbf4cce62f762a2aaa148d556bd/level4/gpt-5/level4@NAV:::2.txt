Review- (Light Review)
The paper studies contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints. The analysis is limited to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Although the setting is simplified, these assumptions are still reasonable in practice. Notably, it yields algorithms with O(log T) regret by introducing the adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

The paper is clear and well documented. Relevant work is cited and the motivation is explicit. This is a solid theoretical paper. Practical aspects are only briefly covered in Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are presented. That said, the contributions are significant: to the best of my knowledge, the authors are the first to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs are convincing (though I did not check all of them in full detail). Overall, this is a good paper, making appropriate simplifying assumptions to obtain O(log T) regret algorithms. The practical aspects could be discussed in more detail.
