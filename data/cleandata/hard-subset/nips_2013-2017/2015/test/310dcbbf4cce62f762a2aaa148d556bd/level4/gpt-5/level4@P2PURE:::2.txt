(Light Review)
This paper studies contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints. The analysis is limited to finite, discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While simplified, these assumptions are still reasonable in practice and lead to algorithms achieving O(log T) regret by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

The paper is clear and well documented, with relevant work appropriately cited and the motivation made explicit. This is a solid theoretical contribution. The practical aspects are discussed only briefly in Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are provided. That said, the contributions are significant: to the best of my knowledge, the authors are the first to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs are convincing (though I did not check every detail). Overall, this is a good paper that adopts appropriate simplifying assumptions to obtain O(log T) regret algorithms. The practical implications could be discussed in greater depth.
