This paper considers a budget-constrained contextual bandit problem with discrete contexts and actions. The main contributions are a computationally efficient algorithm, which leverages a linear programming approximation, and a corresponding logarithmic regret bound. The paper is very well-written and easy to follow.

This paper merits acceptance, as it provides an efficient algorithm for the constrained contextual bandit problem that achieves optimal regret in most cases. The work is interesting and represents a clear improvement over existing methods.

Suggestions for improvement:
*   In Section 4.1, the rationale for combining UCB with the ALP in the UCB-ALP algorithm could be clarified. The current approach appears to follow the "optimism in the face of uncertainty" principle, and it might be beneficial to explicitly state this. Furthermore, drawing a connection to similar policy updates, such as the one in UCRL2, could provide additional insight.
*   The inclusion of numerical experiments, perhaps in an extended version of the paper, would be a valuable addition to complement the theoretical results.
*   For future work, the authors might consider using a parametric model for the reward function. This extension would be particularly useful for settings with a large number of contexts.
*   The authors may also want to consider citing the following related work: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374
