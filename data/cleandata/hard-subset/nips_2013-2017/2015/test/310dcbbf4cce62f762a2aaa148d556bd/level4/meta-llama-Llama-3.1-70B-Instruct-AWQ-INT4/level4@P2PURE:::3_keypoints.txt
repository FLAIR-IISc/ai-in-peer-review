- Problem: Studies a contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T where actions can be skipped, rewards depend on context-action pairs, and costs constrain total spending.  
- Paper focus: Primarily analyzes the special case with known context distribution π and unit costs c{j,k}=1.  
- Main result: Presents an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a stated mild condition.  
- Extension: Extends the algorithm to unknown π and arbitrary costs c{j,k}.  
- Novelty: Potentially first to attain O(log T) regret for this setting, improving on prior work [16] which achieves O(√T).  
- Method: Algorithm built on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when statistics are known; described as simple and intriguing.  
- Writing and motivation: Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.  
- Limitation/concern: The O(log T) bound is conditional; it would be stronger if it held universally or if an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases were shown.  
- Lemma 1: Reviewer initially doubted Lemma 1 but accepts its correctness after the authors' rebuttal.  
- Minor/future-work questions: Is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard? Can results handle unknown T? Can we assume u^1>u^2>...>u^*_J without loss of generality for unknown π?  
- Recommendation: Strongly recommend acceptance.
