- Studies a contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T where each context j appears with known distribution π and actions k incur known costs c_{j,k} and stochastic rewards.
- Focuses on the special case π known and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, proposing an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.
- Extends the approach to the general case with unknown π and arbitrary costs.
- Novelty: first to obtain O(log T) regret (under the stated condition), improving over prior work [16] which only achieved O(√T).
- Algorithm builds on an approximation algorithm for the full-information optimal sequence and is simple and interesting.
- Paper is well motivated and clearly written.
- Concern: would be more impactful if O(log T) could be shown without the extra condition, or alternatively an Ω(√T) lower bound established when the condition fails.
- Lemma 1 was initially unconvincing to the reviewer but is now accepted after the authors' rebuttal clarification.
- Minor questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard?
- Minor questions: can results extend to unknown T (e.g., via doubling trick) while preserving regret rates?
- Minor questions: can we assume u^1 > u^2 > … > u^*_J WLOG even when π is unknown?
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
