- Problem: contextual bandits with budget B over horizon T, contexts j∈{1..J} drawn from known π, actions k∈{0..K} (0 = skip), rewards in [0,1], costs c_{j,k}, goal maximize cumulative reward under cumulative cost ≤ B.  
- Paper focuses on special case: known π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1.  
- Main result: proposed algorithm achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition for that special case.  
- Extension: algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Strength: well-motivated and clearly written.  
- Strength: first work (apparently) to obtain logarithmic regret for this problem under the stated condition, improving on prior O(√T) bounds.  
- Strength: algorithm is simple and based on an offline optimal-policy approximation; insightful design.  
- Critique: initial doubt about correctness of Lemma 1 and the relaxation from hard cumulative to average cost constraint was raised but satisfactorily addressed in rebuttal.  
- Main limitation: O(log T) regret relies on a specific condition; broader applicability is unclear — paper would be stronger if logarithmic regret held more generally or if an Ω(√T) lower bound were shown for cases outside the condition.  
- Minor question: is computing the exact optimal action sequence (when distributions known) NP-hard — comment on computational complexity requested.  
- Minor question: can the algorithm and analysis be extended to unknown horizon T?  
- Minor question: the ordering assumption u^1>u^2>...>u^*_J — can this be assumed WLOG when π is unknown?  
- Overall: strong paper with an interesting result; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
