This paper addresses a constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions, focusing on computational efficiency through LP approximation and achieving a log(T) regret bound as its primary contribution. 

The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented.

Suggestions for future work include exploring a parametric reward model, which would be particularly beneficial when dealing with a large number of contexts. In Section 4.1, the motivation behind combining UCB with ALP in the UCB-ALP algorithm could be further clarified; the connection to optimism in the face of uncertainty and its potential equivalence to the policy update in UCRL2 would be valuable to highlight. Including numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would also strengthen the results. 

Related work to consider includes http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374. Overall, this paper merits acceptance. It offers an efficient algorithm for constrained CB problems, achieving near-optimal regret performance and representing a notable improvement over existing approaches. The work is both interesting and well-executed.
