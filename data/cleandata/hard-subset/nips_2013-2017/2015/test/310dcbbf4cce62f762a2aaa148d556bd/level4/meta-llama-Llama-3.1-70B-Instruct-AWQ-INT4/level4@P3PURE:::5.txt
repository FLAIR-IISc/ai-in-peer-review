This paper addresses a fairly simple budget-constrained contextual bandit (cb) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The main contribution lies in the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm, achieved via linear programming (LP) approximation, and the logarithmic regret bound of O(log(T)) it achieves.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read. 

To further enhance the work, the authors may consider incorporating a parametric model for the reward in future research. This approach would be particularly useful when dealing with a large number of contexts. 

In Section 4.1, when combining Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) with Approximate Linear Programming (ALP), it would be beneficial to provide a clearer explanation of the underlying rationale behind the UCB-ALP algorithm. For instance, it appears that the authors are employing the principle of optimism in the face of uncertainty, which is likely equivalent to the policy update in UCRL2. A more explicit discussion of this connection would be appreciated.

Including numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would also be a valuable addition. Furthermore, the authors may find the following paper relevant to their work: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374.

Overall, this paper merits acceptance as it presents an efficient algorithm for constrained cb with optimal regret in most cases, making it an interesting and notable improvement over existing work.
