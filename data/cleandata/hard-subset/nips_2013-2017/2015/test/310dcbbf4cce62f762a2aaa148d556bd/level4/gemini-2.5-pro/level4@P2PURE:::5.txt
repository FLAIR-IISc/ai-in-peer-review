This paper addresses the budget-constrained contextual bandit problem in a setting with discrete contexts and actions. The primary contributions are a computationally efficient algorithm, which leverages a linear programming approximation, and a corresponding regret bound of O(log T). The paper is exceptionally well-written and the presentation is clear and easy to follow.

Suggestions for improvement:
- For future work, consider extending the framework to use a parametric model for the rewards. This would be particularly valuable in scenarios with a large number of contexts.
- In Section 4.1, the rationale for combining UCB with the approximate linear program (ALP) in the UCB-ALP algorithm could be stated more explicitly. It appears the algorithm follows the "optimism in the face of uncertainty" principle, and it would be helpful to clarify this. Furthermore, it seems the policy update may be equivalent to that in UCRL2; discussing this potential connection would be valuable.
- The inclusion of numerical experiments, perhaps in an extended version of the paper, would significantly strengthen the empirical validation of the proposed algorithm.
- The authors may also want to consider the following related work: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

This paper merits acceptance. It provides an efficient algorithm for the budget-constrained contextual bandit problem that achieves optimal regret in most cases. The work is interesting and represents a clear improvement over existing approaches.
