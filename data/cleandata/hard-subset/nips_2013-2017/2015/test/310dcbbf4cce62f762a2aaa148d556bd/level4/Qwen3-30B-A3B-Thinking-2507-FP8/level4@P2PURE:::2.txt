

The paper addresses contextual bandits under budget and time constraints, focusing on finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While these assumptions simplify the setting, they remain reasonable in practical applications. Crucially, the authors derive algorithms achieving O(log T) regret through an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle. The paper is clear, well-documented, and properly contextualizes prior work with explicit motivation. It presents a solid theoretical contribution. However, practical considerations are only briefly addressed in Sections 5 and 6, with no experimental results provided. Despite this, the contributions are significant: to the best of my knowledge, the authors are the first to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs are convincing (though I did not verify all details exhaustively). This is a strong paper that employs reasonable simplifications to achieve O(log T) regret, though a more detailed discussion of practical implications would be beneficial.
