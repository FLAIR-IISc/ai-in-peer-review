- Studies a contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T where actions incur known costs and skipping is allowed.  
- Main contribution: for known context distribution π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, proposes an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extends the algorithm to the case of unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Novelty: appears to be the first to achieve O(log T) regret (under the stated condition), improving over prior O(√T) results (e.g., [16]).  
- Algorithmic approach uses an approximation to compute the optimal action sequence under full statistical knowledge; the method is simple and insightful.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.  
- Limitation: would be stronger if the O(log T) bound held universally, since Ω(√T) lower bounds exist for boundary cases.  
- All results rely on Lemma 1; reviewer initially doubted its correctness (whether Eq.(2) holds for all trials) but was convinced after the rebuttal.  
- Minor/questions: is computing the optimal action sequence exactly NP-hard? can results generalize to unknown T? can we assume u^1>u^2>... without loss of generality when π is unknown?  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
