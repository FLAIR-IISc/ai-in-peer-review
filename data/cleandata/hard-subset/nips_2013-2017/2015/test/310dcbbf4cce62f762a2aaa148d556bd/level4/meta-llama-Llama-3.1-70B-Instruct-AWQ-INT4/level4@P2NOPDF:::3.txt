This paper addresses a contextual bandit problem with a budget constraint B and a known time horizon T. In each trial, the agent observes a context j drawn from a fixed probability distribution π over a context set {1, ..., J} and selects an action k from {0} ∪ {1, ..., K}, where 0 denotes "skip." If k = 0, the agent receives no reward and incurs no cost. If k > 0, the agent receives a reward y and incurs a cost c{j, k}, where y is drawn from a fixed probability distribution over [0, 1] that depends on j and k, and c{j, k} > 0 is a known parameter. The agent's goal is to maximize the cumulative reward while ensuring that the cumulative cost does not exceed the budget B.

The paper focuses on a special case where π is known and c{j, k} = 1 for all j and k, presenting an algorithm with a regret bound of O(log T) under a mild condition. The algorithm is then extended to the general case where π is unknown and c{j, k} values are arbitrary. A more general setting is considered in [16], but it yields an algorithm with O(√T) regret. This paper may be the first to achieve O(log T) regret, although the mild condition must be satisfied. The algorithm is based on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when all statistics are known. While the algorithm is simple, it is very interesting.

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written. It would be more significant if the O(log T) regret bound held without the mild condition; otherwise, proving an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases would be valuable.

Initially, I was skeptical about the correctness of Lemma 1, as I did not believe the optimal action sequence always satisfies equation (2) for all trials. However, the rebuttal has addressed my concerns, and I am now convinced of the lemma's validity.

Some minor comments and suggestions for future work include:

* Is it NP-hard to exactly obtain the optimal action sequence?
* Can the results be generalized to the case where T is unknown?
* Can we assume u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J without loss of generality, even for the unknown context distribution case?

I strongly recommend this paper for acceptance.
