This work investigates contextual bandits under budget and time limitations, focusing on a setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While this setup represents a simplification, the assumptions are pragmatically sound. The key result is the development of algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret, a feat accomplished by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

The manuscript is clear, well-documented, and effectively situated within the existing literature with explicit motivation and relevant citations. It stands as a solid theoretical paper. The primary limitation is the brief treatment of practical aspects in Sections 5 and 6 and the absence of experimental validation.

Despite this, the contributions are significant: to my knowledge, this is the first work to obtain logarithmic regret in the constrained contextual bandit setting. The proofs are persuasive, although I did not conduct a full line-by-line verification. In summary, this is a strong paper that leverages sensible simplifying assumptions to achieve its O(log T) regret guarantees. The work could be further enhanced by a more detailed discussion of its practical applications.
