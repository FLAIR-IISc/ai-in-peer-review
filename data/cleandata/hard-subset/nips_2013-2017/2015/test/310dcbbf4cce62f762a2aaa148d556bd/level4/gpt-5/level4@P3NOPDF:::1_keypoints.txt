- Reviewer doubts the setting is substantively different from standard budgeted bandits for large T; wants a precise explanation or counterexample showing the JK-arm reduction fails.  
- Paper studies contextual bandits with a budget and horizon; main claim: O(log T) regret for finite J and K when T is sufficiently large.  
- Writing and technical analysis are clear and thorough.  
- Conceptual novelty is unclear because the problem can be viewed as JK “composite” arms with only K available per context, so O(log T) is unsurprising.  
- Paper needs a sharper delineation of what is genuinely new: when context fundamentally changes attainable regret, algorithms, or lower bounds versus a JK-arm reduction.  
- Theorem 2 (case 2) should clarify dependence on K and J — question whether regret should be O(√(KT)+…) rather than O(√T+…).  
- Request for refined gap-dependent upper bounds (e.g., Σ Δ^{-1} log T per context or composite arm) instead of coarse KJ factors.  
- Supplementary material’s gap-dependent bound requires reworking for correctness, clarity, and explicit constants/dependence on J, K, budget, and gaps.  
- Strongly request lower bounds: problem-independent (e.g., Ω(√T)) and problem-dependent (matching Σ Δ^{-1} log T) to show tightness and regimes where budget-induced coupling matters.  
- Ask to formalize conditions for O(log T): make explicit how “sufficiently large T” depends on J, K, budget, context process, and gaps; state any context-frequency or independence assumptions.  
- Strengths summarized: clear writing, thorough analysis, practically relevant setting, and favorable O(log T) rates under finite J,K.  
- Weaknesses summarized: insufficiently argued novelty relative to JK reduction, coarse problem-dependent analysis, flawed supplementary gap result, and missing lower bounds.  
- Recommendation: Neutral; would be strengthened if authors (1) provide corrected/refined gap-dependent bounds, (2) add problem-independent and problem-dependent lower bounds, and (3) clearly delineate regimes where contextual coupling prevents a naive JK reduction.
