- Main result: Shows O(log T) regret is achievable for finite context and action spaces under large T; technically sound and well-written.
- Novelty concern: Contribution seems incremental because the finite contextual bandit can be recast as a JK-armed action-constrained bandit.
- Literature connection: Paper does not sufficiently discuss relation to existing action-constrained bandit work.
- Theorem 2 issue: Case 2 regret stated as O(√T + …) seems low — should it be at least O(√(K T) + …)?
- Problem-dependent bounds: Current bounds (O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T)) are not expressed in gap-dependent form; authors should provide sharper bounds in terms of arm gaps.
- Proof technique concern: Relying on UCB ordering events instead of gap concentration may be suboptimal for obtaining tight problem-dependent bounds.
- Lower bounds: Presented lower bound is trivial/weak; need refined problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds that account for contextual complexity.
- Post-rebuttal point: Even if not exactly equivalent, the setting appears close enough to separate bandits per context when T is large; the coupling rationale remains unclear.
- Recommendation: Provide gap-based upper bounds and matching tighter lower bounds to strengthen novelty and theoretical contribution; current stance is neutral.
