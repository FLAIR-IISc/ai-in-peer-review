(Light Review)

This paper studies contextual bandits under joint budget and time constraints. The analysis focuses on finite, discrete contexts, fixed action costs, and a single budget constraint. While simplified, these assumptions are reasonable in practice. Within this setting, the authors introduce an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle and obtain algorithms with O(log T) regret.

The paper is clearly written and well documented. The related work is appropriately cited, and the motivation is explicit. This is a solid theoretical contribution. As far as I know, the authors are the first to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear convincing, although I did not verify all details.

On the downside, the practical aspects are treated only briefly in Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are provided. These practical considerations could be discussed in greater depth.

Overall, this is a good paper that makes appropriate simplifying assumptions to enable O(log T) regret algorithms in a constrained contextual bandit setting. The main contributions are significant, though additional discussion of practical implications and empirical validation would strengthen the work.
