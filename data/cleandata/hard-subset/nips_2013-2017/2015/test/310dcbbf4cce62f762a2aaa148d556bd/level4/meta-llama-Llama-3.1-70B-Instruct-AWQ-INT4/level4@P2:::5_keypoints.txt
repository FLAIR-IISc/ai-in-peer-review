- Addresses budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions; main contribution is a computationally efficient LP-approximation algorithm with logarithmic regret.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Key insight: relax hard budget constraint to an average budget via LP approximation to enable efficient near-optimal policies.  
- Proposes the Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm which is simple and effective.  
- Combining ALP with UCB yields a logarithmic regret bound.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, leveraging concentration inequalities and tracking remaining budget evolution.  
- Algorithm achieves logarithmic (near-optimal) regret in most cases.  
- Suggest exploring parametric reward models for large context spaces to compactly represent rewards.  
- Clarify the rationale for combining UCB with ALP in Section 4.1 and relate it to optimism/UCRL2 updates.  
- Recommend adding numerical experiments to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Limitation: problem setting is relatively simple (discrete contexts/actions); extensions to more complex scenarios would strengthen the work.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution, accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
