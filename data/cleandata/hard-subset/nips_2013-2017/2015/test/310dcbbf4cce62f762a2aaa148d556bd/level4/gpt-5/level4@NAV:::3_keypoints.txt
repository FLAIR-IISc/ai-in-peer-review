- Studies a contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T where contexts j~π, actions k∈{0,1..K} (0=skip), rewards y∈[0,1] depend on (j,k), costs c_{j,k} known, objective: maximize reward subject to cumulative cost ≤ B.

- Main focus: special case with known π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1; proposes an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.

- Extends method to the general case with unknown π and arbitrary costs.

- Prior work [16] handles a more general formulation but only achieves O(√T); this paper is likely first to get O(log T) (under the stated condition).

- Method builds on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when statistics are known; the algorithm is simple and compelling.

- Paper is well motivated and clearly written; would be stronger if O(log T) regret held unconditionally, though an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases shows limits.

- All results depend critically on Lemma 1; reviewer was initially unconvinced that the optimal action sequence satisfies (2) every round and why the average constraint relaxes the hard constraint, but was convinced after rebuttal.

- Minor questions: Is exact computation of the optimal action sequence NP-hard? Can results be extended to unknown T? Can we assume u^1>u^2>…>u^*_J without loss of generality when π is unknown?

- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
