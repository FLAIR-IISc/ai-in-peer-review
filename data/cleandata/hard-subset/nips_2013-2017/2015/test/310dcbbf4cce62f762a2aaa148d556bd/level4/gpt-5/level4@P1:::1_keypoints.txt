- Setting seems close to budgeted/action-constrained bandits; need clearer justification why contexts cannot be treated as separate bandit instances as T grows.
- Novelty unclear: authors should clarify what fundamentally prevents reduction to standard action-constrained budgeted bandits and where context coupling matters asymptotically.
- ALP relaxation and adaptive LP benchmark are elegant and yield strong non-boundary guarantees (O(1) with known rewards).
- UCB-ALP is simple, efficient, and achieves O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases.
- Technical contribution: concentration of remaining budget via sampling-without-replacement view enables clean regret decomposition.
- Boundary cases produce a √T term; reviewer questions whether this √T is tight and whether dependence on K should be √(K T) instead.
- Request clarification and possible tightening of the √T term’s dependence on K in boundary regimes.
- Current bounds are largely problem-independent; reviewer asks for gap-dependent (problem-dependent) upper bounds expressed in arm/context gaps.
- Supplementary problem-dependent bound is messy and should be reworked into a cleaner gap-based form.
- Lack of tailored lower bounds: reviewer requests matching or near-matching problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds that account for context variability and budget/time coupling.
- Need clearer, explicit characterization of when boundary regimes occur and sensitivity to small perturbations in ρ.
- Comparison to prior work should be sharpened, especially to budgeted/action-constrained bandits, bandits with knapsacks, and Resourceful Contextual Bandits.
- Overall technically sound and well-written, but originality and tightness of results are not fully convincing given proximity to known finite-armed settings.
- Acceptance would be supported by clearer novelty claims, gap-dependent upper bounds, tightened boundary-case analysis, and appropriate lower bounds.
