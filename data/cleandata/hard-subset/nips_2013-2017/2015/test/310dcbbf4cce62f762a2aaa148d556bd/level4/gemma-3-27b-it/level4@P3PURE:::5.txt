This paper considers a relatively simple budget-constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The primary contribution lies in the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm, achieved through a linear programming (LP) approximation, and the resulting log(T) regret bound. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow. 

Suggestions for future work include exploring the use of a parametric model for the reward function, which would be particularly beneficial when dealing with a large number of contexts. In Section 4.1, the rationale for combining Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) with the Active Learning Policy (ALP) – specifically, the reasoning behind its implementation in the UCB-ALP algorithm – could be clarified. The approach appears to leverage optimism in the face of uncertainty and may be equivalent to the policy update used in UCRL2; explicitly stating this connection would be valuable. Furthermore, the inclusion of numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would greatly strengthen the presentation.

The paper http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374 may be of interest to the authors. Overall, this paper merits acceptance. It provides an efficient algorithm for constrained CB with near-optimal regret performance in many scenarios and represents a genuine improvement over existing work.
