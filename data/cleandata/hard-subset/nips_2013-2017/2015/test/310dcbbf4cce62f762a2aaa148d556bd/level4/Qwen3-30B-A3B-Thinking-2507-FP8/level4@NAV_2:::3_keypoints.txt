- Problem: studies constrained contextual bandits with hard budget and time constraints, maximizing cumulative reward under budget.  
- Main improvement: achieves logarithmic regret in non-boundary cases vs prior O(√T) results.  
- Contribution 1: Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approximation yields O(1) regret for known statistics in non-boundary cases.  
- Contribution 2: UCB-ALP (ALP + UCB) achieves O(log T) regret for unknown expected rewards.  
- Contribution 3: Extensions handle unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Theory: provides rigorous regret analysis, novel concentration techniques, and careful decomposition, with attention to boundary cases.  
- Technical quality: sound theoretical results and clear characterization of applicability and limitations.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written and organized; reproducible for experts with clear definitions and algorithms.  
- Originality: novel ALP approximation and first demonstration of logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual setting; clearly differentiated from prior work.  
- Significance: meaningful practical implications (e.g., clinical trials, recommendations) and advances constrained bandit theory.  
- Strengths: technically sophisticated, computationally efficient ALP, and impressive regret analysis handling context ranking and budget dynamics.  
- Weaknesses: logarithmic regret only in non-boundary cases (O(√T) on boundaries); needs more discussion on practical occurrence of boundary cases and comparison with recent extensions [21,22].  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify boundary-case implications and comparisons.
