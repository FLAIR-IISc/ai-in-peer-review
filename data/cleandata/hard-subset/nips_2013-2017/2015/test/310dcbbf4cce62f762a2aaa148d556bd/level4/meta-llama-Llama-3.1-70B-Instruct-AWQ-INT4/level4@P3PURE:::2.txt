The paper considers contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, focusing on a simplified setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Although this setting is restricted, these assumptions remain reasonable in practice. Notably, this simplification enables the development of algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

The paper is well-written, clear, and thoroughly documented, with relevant work properly cited and motivation clearly stated. This is a solid theoretical contribution. However, the practical aspects are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6, and unfortunately, no experimental results are provided. Nevertheless, the contributions are significant, as the authors appear to be the first to achieve logarithmic regret in the context of constrained contextual bandits. The proofs provided are convincing, although I did not verify every detail. Overall, this is a good paper that makes appropriate simplified assumptions to obtain O(log T) regret algorithms. To further enhance the paper, the authors may consider discussing the practical aspects in more detail.
