

This paper addresses a contextual bandit problem with budget constraint $B$ and known time horizon $T$. At each trial, the agent observes a context $j$ drawn from a fixed distribution $\pi$ over $\{1, \dots, J\}$ and selects an action $k \in \{0\} \cup \{1, \dots, K\}$, where $k=0$ denotes "skip." For $k=0$, the agent receives no reward and incurs no cost. For $k>0$, the agent receives reward $y$ (drawn from a fixed distribution over $[0,1]$ depending on $j,k$) and incurs cost $c_{j,k} > 0$, which is known. The objective is to maximize cumulative reward subject to cumulative cost never exceeding $B$.  

The main contribution focuses on the special case where $\pi$ is known and $c_{j,k} = 1$ for all $j,k$, presenting an algorithm achieving $O(\log T)$ regret under a mild condition. The approach extends to the general case with unknown $\pi$ and arbitrary $c_{j,k}$. While a more general setting in [16] yields $O(\sqrt{T})$ regret, this work appears to be the first with $O(\log T)$ regret (provided the mild condition holds). The algorithm leverages an approximation method for optimal action sequences under known statistics and is both simple and insightful.  

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written. Its significance would be enhanced if the $O(\log T)$ bound held universally, with a matching $\Omega(\sqrt{T})$ lower bound established for boundary cases. All results rely on Lemma 1, which initially raised concerns about whether the optimal action sequence always satisfies (2) for all trials and why the average constraint is a relaxed version of the hard constraint. However, I am now convinced of Lemma 1's correctness following the rebuttal.  

Minor comments:  
1. Is obtaining the optimal action sequence exactly NP-hard?  
2. Can results be generalized to unknown $T$?  
3. Can we assume $u^1 > u^2 > \dots > u^*_J$ without loss of generality for the unknown $\pi$ case?  
I strongly recommend acceptance.
