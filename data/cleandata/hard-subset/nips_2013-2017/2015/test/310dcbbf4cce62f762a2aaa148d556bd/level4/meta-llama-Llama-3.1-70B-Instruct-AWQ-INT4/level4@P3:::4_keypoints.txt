- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for constrained contextual bandits, using LP relaxation of budget to an average constraint.
- ALP requires only the ordering of expected rewards and achieves near-optimality when system statistics are known.
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP for the case of unknown expected rewards.
- Shows UCB-ALP attains logarithmic regret in general, with higher regret only in specific boundary cases.
- Identifies and analyzes boundary cases where budget/time ratio equals a context cumulative probability, causing indistinguishability of actions.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with detailed proofs of regret bounds.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of problem, algorithms, and boundary-case reasoning.
- Key contribution: ALP’s reduced information requirement (ordering only) compared to prior methods needing accurate reward estimates.
- Limitation: assumes known context distribution, which may be unrealistic in practice.
- Limitation: lacks experimental results to empirically validate the algorithms.
- Recommendation: accept, but encourage adding experiments and addressing unknown context distribution.
- High reviewer ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
