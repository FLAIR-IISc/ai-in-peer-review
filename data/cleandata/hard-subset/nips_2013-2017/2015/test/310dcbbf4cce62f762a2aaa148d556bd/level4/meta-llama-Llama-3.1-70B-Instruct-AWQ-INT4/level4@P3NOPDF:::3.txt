This paper considers a contextual bandit problem with a budget constraint B and a known time horizon T. In this setting, the agent observes a context j chosen according to a fixed probability distribution π over a context set {1, ..., J} and takes an action k from {0} ∪ {1, ..., K}, where 0 denotes "skip". If k = 0, the agent receives no reward and incurs no cost. If k > 0, the agent receives a reward y and incurs a cost c{j, k}, where y is chosen according to a fixed probability distribution over [0, 1] that depends on j and k, and c{j, k} > 0 is a parameter known to the agent. The goal of the agent is to maximize the cumulative reward under the condition that the cumulative cost never exceeds the budget B.

The main body of the paper focuses on a special case where π is known and c{j, k} = 1 for all j and k, and presents an algorithm with a regret bound of O(log T) under a mild condition. The paper then extends this algorithm to the general case where π is unknown and c{j, k} values are arbitrary.

A more general setting is considered in [16], but it yields an algorithm with O(√T) regret. Therefore, this paper is possibly the first work to achieve O(log T) regret, although the mild condition must be satisfied. The algorithm is based on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when all statistics are known. The algorithm is simple yet very interesting.

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written. However, it would be more interesting and significant if the O(log T) regret bound held in all cases, rather than being subject to a mild condition. Otherwise, proving an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases would be valuable.

Initially, I had concerns regarding the correctness of Lemma 1, as I did not believe the optimal action sequence always satisfies equation (2) for all trials. However, after reviewing the rebuttal, I am now convinced of the lemma's validity.

Some minor comments and suggestions for future work:

1. Is it NP-hard to exactly obtain the optimal action sequence?
2. Can the results be generalized to the case where T is unknown?
3. Can we assume u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J without loss of generality, even for the unknown context distribution case?

Overall, I strongly recommend this paper for acceptance.
