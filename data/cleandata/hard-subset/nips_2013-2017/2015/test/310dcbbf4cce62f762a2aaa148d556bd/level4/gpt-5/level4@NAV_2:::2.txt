(Light Review)
This paper studies contextual bandits under both budget and time (horizon) constraints. The analysis focuses on finite, discrete contexts, fixed action costs, and a single budget constraint. Although simplified, these assumptions are realistic in many applications. Crucially, they enable algorithms with O(log T) regret via an adaptive linear programming approximation to the oracle.

The manuscript is clearly written and well organized, with appropriate citations and a clear motivation. It is a solid theoretical contribution. Practical considerations are treated only briefly in Sections 5 and 6, and there are no empirical results. Nonetheless, the contributions are noteworthy: to the best of my knowledge, this is the first work to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs are persuasive (though I did not verify every detail). Overall, this is a strong paper that makes sensible simplifying assumptions to deliver O(log T) regret algorithms; expanding the discussion of practical implications would further strengthen it.
