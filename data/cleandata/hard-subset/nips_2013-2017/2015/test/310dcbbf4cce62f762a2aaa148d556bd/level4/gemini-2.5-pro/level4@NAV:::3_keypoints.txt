- Problem: contextual bandit with known horizon T, fixed budget B, contexts from distribution π, actions include K active actions and a zero-reward skip; active actions yield stochastic rewards and incur costs, with cumulative cost constrained by B at all times.  
- Focus: specializes to known context distribution π and uniform action costs (c_{j,k}=1).  
- Contribution: proposes an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild assumption for the specialized setting.  
- Extension: generalizes the algorithm to handle unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Novelty: contrasts with prior work [16] which achieved O(√T) regret; this is the first to claim O(log T) regret (under conditions).  
- Methodology: builds on an offline approximation algorithm; resulting online algorithm is simple and conceptually elegant.  
- Clarity & motivation: strong motivation and exceptionally clear exposition.  
- Limitations / open problems: would be stronger if O(log T) held unconditionally or if an Ω(√T) lower bound were proved for cases violating the assumption.  
- Technical note: Lemma 1 is central; initial concerns about its proof and the average-constraint relaxation were raised but satisfactorily resolved in rebuttal.  
- Minor questions: is computing the exact offline optimal action sequence NP-hard?  
- Minor questions: can the results be extended to unknown horizon T?  
- Minor questions: can the assumed ordering u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J be made w.l.o.g. when π is unknown?  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
