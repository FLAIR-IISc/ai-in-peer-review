The paper considers contextual bandits with budget and time constraints. The analysis is restricted to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs and a single budget constraint. While the setting is simplified, these assumptions are still reasonable in practice. Importantly, it results in algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by introducing the adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

The paper is clear and well documented. Relevant work is cited and motivation is explicit. This is a solid theoretical paper. The practical aspects are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6 and no experimental results are provided. This being said, the contributions are significant: as far as I know, the authors are the first to obtain logarithmic regret in the case of constrained contextual bandits. The proofs are convincing (although I did not check them all in full detail). This is a good paper, making appropriate simplified assumptions to obtain O(log T) regret algorithms. The practical aspects might be discussed into more detail.

Strengths:
- The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of contextual bandits by introducing an algorithm that achieves O(log T) regret in the presence of budget and time constraints.
- The authors make reasonable assumptions about the problem setting, which allows them to obtain a tractable solution.
- The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.

Weaknesses:
- The analysis is restricted to a simplified setting, which might not be directly applicable to more complex scenarios.
- There is a lack of experimental results to validate the theoretical findings.
- The practical aspects of the algorithms are only briefly discussed.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. The authors provide a complete piece of work, and the evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of the work is careful and honest.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and adequately informs the reader. The authors provide enough information for an expert reader to reproduce the results.

Originality: The paper introduces a novel algorithm for constrained contextual bandits, which achieves O(log T) regret. The combination of adaptive linear programming and UCB is new and provides a significant contribution to the field.

Significance: The results are important, and the paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The algorithms proposed in the paper advance the state of the art in a demonstrable way and provide unique insights into the problem of constrained contextual bandits.
