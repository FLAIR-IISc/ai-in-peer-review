The authors consider the problem of a contextual bandit with budget and time constraints. The main contribution of their paper is demonstrating that, under finite context and action spaces, a logarithmic regret of O(log T) is achievable when the time horizon T is sufficiently large, a novel result for this problem setting. 

The paper appears thorough and the writing is generally precise. The results are interesting, though perhaps not entirely surprising. Given a finite number of contexts J and actions K, the problem can be viewed as a time and budget-constrained bandit with JK arms, further constrained by the context observed at each time step. Consequently, for sufficiently large T, a regret of O(log T) is expected, mirroring results from constrained bandit problems. Therefore, the paper’s novelty could be further clarified in the rebuttal. 

I have a few questions and suggestions:

*   In Theorem 2, the regret in case 2) is O(√T + ...). However, wouldn't a more appropriate bound be O(√KT) + ... at least?
*   Also in Theorem 2, the regret is either O(JK log T) or O(√T + JK log T), depending on the problem configuration. Typically, problem-dependent bounds are expressed more precisely using a sum of inverse arm gaps. Is a similar refinement possible here? While I understand the current proof relies on event-based characterization of UCB ordering rather than concentration bounds on gaps, it’s worth considering if this approach is optimal.
*   It would strengthen the paper to include lower bounds for this problem, ideally both problem-dependent and problem-independent bounds. While trivial lower bounds can be derived from classical bandit problems (setting cost to zero and a constant context), it would be valuable to investigate if these can be refined to account for the complexity introduced by the contextual aspect.

I currently view the originality of this setting as somewhat incremental, and therefore remain neutral regarding this paper. I am looking forward to the authors’ response to these points.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel analysis of constrained contextual bandits, demonstrating a logarithmic regret bound under specific conditions.
*   The proposed UCB-ALP algorithm is a reasonable approach combining linear programming and UCB, offering a potentially efficient solution.
*   The paper provides a comprehensive analysis, including extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The core result, logarithmic regret, feels somewhat expected given the finite action/context space and connections to existing constrained bandit literature.
*   The regret bound in the boundary cases (Theorem 2) includes a √T term, which is suboptimal compared to potential logarithmic bounds.
*   The lack of lower bounds weakens the claim of optimality and limits understanding of the problem’s fundamental limits.
