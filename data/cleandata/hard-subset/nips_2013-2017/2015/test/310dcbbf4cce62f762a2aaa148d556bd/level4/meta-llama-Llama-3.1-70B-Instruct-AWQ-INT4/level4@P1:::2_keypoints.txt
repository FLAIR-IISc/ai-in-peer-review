- Problem addressed: constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits, relevant to clinical trials, recommendations, and crowdsourcing.
- Modeling assumptions: finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget constraint — simplified but reasonable in practice.
- Main technical contribution: adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle that tolerates parameter estimation errors.
- Algorithmic contribution: UCB-ALP — combines ALP with UCB to handle unknown expected rewards.
- Regret guarantees: UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret in general, with O(√T) regret in certain boundary cases.
- Novelty: decoupling information acquisition and decision making via adaptive linear relaxation offers a new perspective.
- Quality and clarity: paper is well-written with convincing proofs, though some sections could be clearer.
- Significance: achieves logarithmic regret for a challenging constrained contextual bandit problem.
- Practical evaluation gap: lacks experimental results and detailed analysis of practical aspects.
- Extensions: brief discussion of unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, but deeper analysis is needed.
- Recommendation and ratings: reviewer recommends acceptance; rates quality, clarity, originality, and significance each 9/10.
- Acceptance arguments: significant, innovative contribution with strong theoretical support; Rejection arguments: insufficient practical discussion, no experiments, and some clarity issues.
