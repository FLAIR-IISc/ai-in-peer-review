This paper studies the problem of constrained contextual bandits, which is an extension of the classic multi-armed bandit problem. The authors propose an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that achieves near-optimal performance when the system statistics are known. They also combine ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method to propose a UCB-ALP algorithm for the case where the expected rewards are unknown. The authors show that UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases, where it achieves O(√T) regret.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the regret bounds for both ALP and UCB-ALP algorithms. The authors also discuss the extension of their results to more general settings, including bandits with unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs.

The writing is precise, and the results are interesting. However, the reviewer questions the originality of the paper, suggesting that the problem can be seen as a time-constrained and budgeted bandit problem with JK arms, which is also action-constrained. The reviewer also asks for clarification on the innovation of the paper.

Regarding the technical aspects, the reviewer asks for clarification on the regret bound in Theorem 2, suggesting that it should be O(√(KT) + ...) instead of O(√T + ...). The reviewer also suggests that the problem-dependent bounds could be expressed in a more refined way, similar to the sum of inverse of arm gaps. Additionally, the reviewer asks for lower bounds for this problem, ideally problem-dependent and problem-independent.

Overall, the reviewer remains neutral about the paper, awaiting further clarification on the innovation and technical aspects.
