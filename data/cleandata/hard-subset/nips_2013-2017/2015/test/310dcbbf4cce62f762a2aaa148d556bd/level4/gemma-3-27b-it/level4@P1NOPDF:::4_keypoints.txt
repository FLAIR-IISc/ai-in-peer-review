- Primary concern: No clear justification for treating boundary cases (ρ = q_j) specially, especially for j > 1.  
- Paper analyzes contextual bandits against a strong adaptive-LP baseline that abstains on a percentage of rounds ("budget").  
- Setup is interesting and proposed solutions are novel.  
- Motivation for the budget constraint is well-explained and tied rigorously to adaptive linear programming.  
- Authors clearly articulate the exploration–exploitation trade-off under the budget constraint.  
- Problem statement and baseline are defined with good clarity.  
- Theoretical analysis feels limited because constant factors hidden in asymptotic notation are not discussed.  
- Need discussion of how the required budget affects observed regret in practice.  
- Empirical evaluation is promising but lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art contextual bandit algorithms without a budget.  
- Possible that the budget introduces unnecessary overhead; this should be investigated empirically.  
- Presentation is generally good but some notation and terminology are unclear or inconsistent.  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel paper with a strong adaptive-LP baseline; addressing constants, empirical comparisons, and the boundary-case justification would significantly strengthen it.
