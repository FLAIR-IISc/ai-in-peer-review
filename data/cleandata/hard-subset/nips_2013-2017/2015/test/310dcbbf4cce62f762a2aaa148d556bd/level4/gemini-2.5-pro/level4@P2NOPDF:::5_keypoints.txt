- Studies budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions.
- Main contribution: a computationally efficient LP-approximation algorithm (UCB-ALP) achieving logarithmic regret.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear, easy-to-follow technical arguments.
- Suggestion: make UCB-ALP rationale explicit and link its design to the "optimism in the face of uncertainty" principle and UCRL2-like updates.
- Future work: extend the approach to parametric reward models to handle many contexts.
- Suggestion: include numerical experiments in an extended version to validate theoretical results and show practical performance.
- Suggestion: consider adding related work (http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374) to the discussion.
- Overall recommendation: merits acceptance — interesting, elegant, and significantly improves existing methods with near-optimal regret.
