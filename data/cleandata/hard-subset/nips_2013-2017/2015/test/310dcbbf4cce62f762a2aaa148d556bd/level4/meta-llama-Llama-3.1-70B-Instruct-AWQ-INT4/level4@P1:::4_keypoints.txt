- Problem: Studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints (constrained contextual bandits).
- Algorithm: Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP), near-optimal and only requires ordering of expected rewards.
- Unknown rewards: Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, achieving logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases.
- Analysis: Provides comprehensive analysis, addressing budget-induced challenges and limits of existing algorithms.
- Comparison: Includes detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods.
- Key insight: Decouples information acquisition and decision making via adaptive linear relaxation.
- Strengths: Clear, concise, well-organized presentation.
- Weakness/Question: Boundary cases (rho = q_j) are handled separately; it’s unclear why these are special, especially for j > 1.
- Recommendation: Novel and efficient approach; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Rating: 8/10.
