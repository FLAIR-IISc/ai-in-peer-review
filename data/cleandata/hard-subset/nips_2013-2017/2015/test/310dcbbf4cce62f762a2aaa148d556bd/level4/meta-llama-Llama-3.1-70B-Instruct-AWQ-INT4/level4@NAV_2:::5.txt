This paper considers a fairly simple budget constrained contextual bandit (cb) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The main contribution is the computational efficiency of the algorithm (via LP approximation) and the log(T) regret bound it achieves.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem, starting with the introduction of the constrained contextual bandit problem and its significance in various applications. They then delve into the details of the problem, discussing the challenges introduced by budget constraints and the limitations of existing approaches. The proposed Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm is well-motivated, and its combination with the Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) method is a natural extension.

The paper is very well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem, the proposed algorithm, and the analysis of its performance. The use of mathematical notation is consistent throughout the paper, and the proofs are provided in the supplementary material.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a computationally efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits, which achieves a log(T) regret bound in most cases.
* A thorough analysis of the problem, including the challenges introduced by budget constraints and the limitations of existing approaches.
* A clear and concise explanation of the proposed algorithm and its performance analysis.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The simplicity of the problem formulation, which may not capture the complexity of real-world applications.
* The lack of numerical experiments to demonstrate the performance of the proposed algorithm.

Suggestions for future work include:

* Considering a parametric model for the reward, which can be useful when the number of contexts is large.
* Providing a clearer explanation of the rationale behind combining UCB with ALP, including the optimism in the face of uncertainty principle.
* Including numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper to demonstrate the performance of the proposed algorithm.

Overall, this paper provides an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with optimal regret in most cases, making it a valuable contribution to the field. The paper is well-written, and the analysis is thorough. However, the simplicity of the problem formulation and the lack of numerical experiments are limitations that should be addressed in future work.
