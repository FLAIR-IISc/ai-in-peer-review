- The proposed setting still appears very similar to a budgeted bandit with action constraints; context coupling may vanish and each context occurrence might be treated as a separate bandit instance.
- The paper should derive a clean problem-dependent upper bound in terms of arm gaps and establish matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent cases.
- The authors show log(T) regret for finite contexts and actions when T is large, but the result is thorough yet not particularly surprising.
- With finite J and K, the problem can be seen as a JK-arm action-constrained bandit yielding log(T) regret, raising doubts about the novelty; the rebuttal did not resolve these concerns.
- Questioning Theorem 2: the stated O(√(T)+...) regret may be missing an O(√(KT)) factor and needs clarification.
- Theorem 2’s problem-dependent bound should ideally be refined to a sum of inverse arm gaps rather than KJ log(T).
- Stronger, nontrivial lower bounds that account for context complexity (both problem-dependent and independent) are needed beyond trivial bounds.
- Overall verdict: neutral due to reservations about originality.
