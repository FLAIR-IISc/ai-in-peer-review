(Light Review)

This research paper examines relevant bandits with budget and time constraints. The analysis is limited to discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a budget constraint. Although the setting is simple, these assumptions are valid in practice. Importantly, it yields results in an algorithm that achieves O(logT), which presents Oracle's adaptive linear programming approximation.

The research paper is clear and well-documented. Relevant tasks are mentioned, and the motivation is clear. It is a solid theoretical paper. Practical aspects are briefly discussed in sections 5 and 6 only, and no experimental results are provided. Nevertheless, the contributions are significant: as far as I know, the authors are the first to obtain logarithmic regret in the case of bound bandits. The evidence is convincing (although I have not fully verified all of them). It is a good paper, making reasonable simplified assumptions to achieve O(logT). More detail could be provided on practical aspects.
