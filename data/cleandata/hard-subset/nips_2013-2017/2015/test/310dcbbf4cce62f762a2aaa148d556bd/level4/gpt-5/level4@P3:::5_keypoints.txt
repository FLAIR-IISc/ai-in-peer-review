- Problem & challenge: Studies contextual bandits with time and hard budget constraints where optimal policy couples decisions across time and contexts, making DP intractable.

- Main approach: Relax hard budget to an average-budget LP yielding a threshold structure; propose ALP replacing ρ=B/T with instantaneous ratio bτ/τ.

- ALP performance (known rewards & contexts): ALP achieves O(1) regret except at boundary ρ values (regret O(√T)).

- UCB-ALP: Combining ALP with UCB yields O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) on boundaries.

- Extensions: Discuss handling unknown context distribution via empirical estimates and heterogeneous costs via an LP over context–action pairs and an ε-first variant.

- Relation to prior work: Improves on RCB and concave-reward/convex-knapsack lines by achieving logarithmic regret for finite contexts/actions with lower computational cost.

- Technical quality: Sound analysis mapping ALP budget dynamics to sampling without replacement and using hypergeometric concentration (Lemma 2) for tight control of bτ/τ.

- Regret decomposition: Carefully separates within-context UCB errors (classical O(log T) per pair) from context-ranking/remaining-budget fluctuations to obtain overall bounds.

- Boundary behavior: √T regret at boundaries attributed to threshold instability when ρ equals cumulative context mass; authors retain both √T and log T terms.

- Unknown π analysis: Extension to unknown context distribution is reasonable but relies on truncating the estimator, making the analysis conservative.

- Heterogeneous costs: Section outlines a plausible LP approach and ε-first learning but lacks detailed complexity and gap-dependence analysis.

- Clarity: Paper is clearly written; LP and ALP are well explained, but Section 4 should state explicitly that UCB-ALP is an optimism-under-uncertainty method and why only ordering of u* matters.

- Originality: Novel combination of adaptive LP relaxation and UCB to get O(log T) regret in constrained contextual bandits; event-based decomposition is a useful analytic tool.

- Significance: Advances theoretical understanding and provides a computationally efficient algorithm with potential practical applications.

- Pros (condensed): Clear formulation, elegant and simple ALP, strong UCB-ALP guarantees, useful extensions, and better computational efficiency than prior methods.

- Cons (condensed): Boundary √T regret and sensitivity not quantified, conservative treatment for unknown π, limited treatment of ties/near-ties, and no empirical evaluation.

- Suggested improvements: Clarify optimism connection, provide gap-dependent/sensitivity analysis, address ties/near-ties explicitly, analyze fully online π via martingale concentration, detail heterogeneous-costs LP and ε-first schedule, and add numerical experiments.

- Overall recommendation: Accept.
