The paper "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits" studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, referred to as constrained contextual bandits. The main contribution of the paper is the design and analysis of algorithms that achieve logarithmic or sublinear regret for this problem.

The authors first study approximations of the oracle algorithm when the system statistics are known. They propose an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that achieves near-optimality and only requires the ordering of expected rewards. The ALP algorithm is then combined with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method to handle the case where the expected rewards are unknown. The resulting UCB-ALP algorithm achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases.

The paper also discusses the generalization to systems with unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs. The authors propose a modified ALP algorithm called Empirical ALP (EALP) that uses the empirical distribution to estimate the context distribution. They also propose an -First ALP algorithm for the unknown statistics case.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the regret bounds for the proposed algorithms. The results are interesting, but the reviewer questions the originality of the paper, suggesting that the setting is close to a budgeted bandit problem with action constraint. The reviewer also asks for clarification on the regret bounds, suggesting that they should be expressed in a more refined way with the sum of inverse of arm gaps.
