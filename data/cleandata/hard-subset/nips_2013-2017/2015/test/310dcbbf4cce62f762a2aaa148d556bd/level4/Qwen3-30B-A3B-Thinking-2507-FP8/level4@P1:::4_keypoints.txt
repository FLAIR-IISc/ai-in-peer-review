- Establishes logarithmic regret guarantees (O(log T) for non-boundary cases) for constrained contextual bandits, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Proposes a novel Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approach that approximates the optimal oracle and remains computationally efficient.  
- Combines ALP with UCB to handle unknown reward expectations, decoupling exploration (UCB) from decision-making (ALP).  
- Key insight: ALP tolerates estimation errors in reward rankings, enabling clean separation of information acquisition and allocation.  
- Achieves O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) regret in boundary cases (ρ = q_j for some j).  
- Substantial concern: boundary-case analysis lacks sufficient intuition, especially for j > 1, and may be a technical artifact rather than a fundamental problem property.  
- Authors should clarify why boundary cases arise and whether they reflect a meaningful structural distinction or an analysis artifact.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous with careful handling of budget constraints via adaptive adjustments to remaining budget and time.  
- Properly situates and compares the work to prior literature (notably [17]), clearly demonstrating improvement.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated for applications with budget constraints (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing).  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and mathematically precise for readers familiar with contextual bandits.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; accept with minor revisions to address the boundary-case explanation.
