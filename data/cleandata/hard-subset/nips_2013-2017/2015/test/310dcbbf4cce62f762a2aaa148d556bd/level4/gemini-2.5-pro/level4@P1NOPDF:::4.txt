My primary question is: why must the boundary cases be analyzed differently? It is not clear why the condition ρ = q_j is considered a special case that warrants a separate analysis, particularly for j > 1.

Overall, this paper analyzes the contextual bandit problem against a stronger-than-usual baseline. This baseline uses adaptive linear programming to strategically pass on a percentage of suboptimal rounds, a mechanism the authors refer to as a 'budget'. The problem formulation is interesting, and the proposed solutions are unique.
