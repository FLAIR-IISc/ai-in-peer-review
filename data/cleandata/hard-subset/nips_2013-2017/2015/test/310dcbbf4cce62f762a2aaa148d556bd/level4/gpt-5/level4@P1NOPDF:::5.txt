This paper studies a fairly simple budget-constrained contextual bandit problem with discrete contexts and actions. The main contribution is an algorithm that is computationally efficient via a linear-programming (LP) approximation and achieves a log(T) regret bound. The paper is very well written and easy to follow.

Suggestions:
- For future work, consider a parametric reward model. This can be especially useful when the number of contexts is large, enabling generalization across contexts and improved sample efficiency.
- In Section 4.1, when combining UCB with ALP, please clarify the rationale behind the UCB-ALP algorithm. For example, my understanding is that you are following the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle; this seems akin to the policy update in UCRL2. Making this connection explicit would help readers understand the design choices and their theoretical grounding.
- It would be great to include numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper to complement the theoretical results and illustrate practical performance and scalability.
- You may want to cite the following related work: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

Overall, this paper merits acceptance. It provides an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with optimal regret in most cases. The results are interesting and represent a clear improvement over existing work.
