- Paper studies contextual bandits with time (T) and budget (B) constraints for finite contexts/actions and proposes UCB-ALP that decouples learning and decision-making.  
- Authors introduce an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) oracle for known statistics and combine it with UCB to handle unknown rewards.  
- Main theoretical claim: UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret in most cases, a novel positive result for this formulation.  
- Paper is well-written and technically thorough; the problem is practically important and logarithmic regret is a meaningful advance.  
- Concern about originality: the setting may reduce to a JK-armed action-constrained bandit, so O(log T) regret may not be surprising; authors should better explain unique challenges of their formulation.  
- Regret bounds need refinement: clarify whether the O(sqrt(T)) term depends on J and K (e.g., O(sqrt(JK T))) and replace coarse O(JK log T) with gap-dependent instance-specific bounds.  
- Missing lower bounds: the paper lacks problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds tailored to the constrained contextual setting to validate optimality.  
- Overall verdict: solid work but neutral recommendation pending clearer novelty positioning, tighter gap-dependent upper bounds, and matching lower bounds.
