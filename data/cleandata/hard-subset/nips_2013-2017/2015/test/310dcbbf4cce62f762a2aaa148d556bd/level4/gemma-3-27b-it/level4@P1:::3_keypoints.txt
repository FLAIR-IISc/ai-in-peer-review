- Problem: contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T; contexts drawn from distribution π; action 0 = skip yields no reward/cost; non-skip yields reward y and cost c_{j,k}; objective maximize cumulative reward subject to cost ≤ B.  
- Main contribution: for π known and c_{j,k}=1, algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under certain conditions.  
- Extension: algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Novelty: prior related work attains O(√T) regret, so this may be first to get O(log T) in this setting (conditioned on assumptions).  
- Method: algorithm based on approximating the optimal action sequence when statistics are known; described as simple and interesting.  
- Clarity: paper is well-motivated and clearly written.  
- Major caveat: result hinges on Lemma 1; initial doubts about relaxing hard constraint to an average constraint were resolved by rebuttal, convincing the reviewer.  
- Suggested strengthenings: either prove O(log T) regret always holds or provide an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases.  
- Minor questions: is finding the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard? can results generalize to unknown T? can one assume u^1>u^2>...>u^J without loss when context distribution is unknown?  
- Recommendation: strong accept.
