- Reviewer accepts results are close but questions why contexts can't be treated as separate bandits when horizon is large enough to decouple them.  
- Requests a clear problem-dependent upper bound expressed in terms of arm gaps, and ideally matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent cases.  
- Paper addresses a budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandit and claims O(log T) regret for finite contexts/actions when T is sufficiently large.  
- Results are thorough and the writing is precise.  
- Findings are not surprising: with J contexts and K arms the problem can be seen as a JK-armed action-constrained bandit, so log(T) regret for large T is expected; novelty is unclear.  
- Asks authors to clarify and defend the paper’s novelty in the rebuttal.  
- Questions Theorem 2’s second case: is O(√T + …) correct, or should it be at least O(√(K T) + …)?  
- Questions whether the problem-dependent bound can be refined to a sum of inverse arm gaps instead of the KJ factor and whether the current UCB-ordering proof approach is optimal.  
- Requests lower bounds (both problem-dependent and independent); notes trivial lower bound reduces to classical bandit and asks for refinement to capture contextual complexity.  
- Remains unconvinced about the originality of the setting and maintains a neutral overall assessment.  
- Impression that the setting is very similar to budgeted bandits with action constraints and asks for clarification on the degree of innovation.
