This paper studies a relatively simple budget-constrained CB problem with discrete contexts and actions. The main contribution is the algorithm’s computational efficiency (via an LP approximation) together with a log(T) regret guarantee.

The paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Suggestions: For future work, consider a parametric reward model, which is valuable when the number of contexts is large. In Section 4.1, when combining UCB with ALP, clarify the rationale behind the UCB-ALP algorithm; for example, it appears to follow the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle, likely akin to the policy update in UCRL2. It would be great to include some numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper. You may want to see this paper: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

This paper merits acceptance since it provides an efficient algorithm for constrained CB with optimal regret in most cases. It is interesting and improves upon existing work.
