- Problem setup: contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T, contexts j ~ π, actions k∈{0,…,K} where k=0 skips (no reward/cost), other actions give reward in [0,1] and cost c_{j,k}, objective maximize cumulative reward subject to hard budget constraint.  
- Main contribution: for known π and unit costs (c_{j,k}=1), proposes an algorithm that achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extensions: approach generalized to unknown π and arbitrary positive costs.  
- Comparison to prior work: prior algorithm in [16] attains O(√T) regret; this is (to reviewer’s knowledge) the first to get O(log T) in this budgeted contextual bandit setting under the stated condition.  
- Methodology: leverages an approximation routine for the offline problem (computing optimal action sequence when statistics known); method described as simple and insightful.  
- Presentation: paper is well motivated and clearly written.  
- Limitation/weakness: O(log T) guarantee depends on a “mild condition”; would be stronger if the result held without it or if an Ω(√T) lower bound were shown for boundary cases.  
- Technical note: most results hinge on Lemma 1; reviewer was initially unconvinced about condition (2) but was convinced after rebuttal.  
- Minor question 1: Is computing the exact offline optimal action sequence NP-hard?  
- Minor question 2: Can results be extended to unknown/unbounded T (anytime or doubling-trick) without weakening regret guarantees?  
- Minor question 3: Can contexts be relabeled so that u^1 > u^2 > … > u^*_J even when context distribution is unknown?  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
