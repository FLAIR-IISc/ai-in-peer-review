- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints under finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint—simplifying but practically reasonable assumptions.
- Main technical contribution: algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret using an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.
- Paper is clear, well-documented, and properly situates itself within prior work with explicit motivation.
- Presents a solid theoretical contribution and (to the reviewer’s knowledge) is the first to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Proofs are convincing overall, though not exhaustively verified by the reviewer.
- Practical considerations are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are provided.
- Overall assessment: strong paper using reasonable simplifications to obtain O(log T) regret, but would benefit from a deeper discussion or experiments on practical implications.
