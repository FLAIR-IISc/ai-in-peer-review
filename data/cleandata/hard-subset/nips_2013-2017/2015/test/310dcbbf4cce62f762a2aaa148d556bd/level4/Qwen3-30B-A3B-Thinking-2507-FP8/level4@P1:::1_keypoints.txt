- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits and proposes an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm.
- ALP gives a clean benchmark and achieves O(1) regret except in boundary cases.
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP for unknown statistics.
- UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases—the first logarithmic regret bound reported for constrained contextual bandits.
- Boundary cases incur O(√T) regret and are noted to have zero Lebesgue measure; practical relevance needs more discussion.
- Model assumptions (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget) are justified as practical and enable efficient algorithms.
- Novel connection between constrained contextual bandits and linear programming that decouples exploration and exploitation.
- Rigorous regret analysis with careful treatment of budget fluctuations and context ranking interactions.
- Clear presentation of algorithms and supplementary material appears to support reproducibility.
- Computationally efficient and practically implementable algorithm design.
- Novelty claim should be more precisely framed relative to viewing the problem as contextual bandit with JK arms.
- Regret bound O(JK log T) (Theorem 2) is correct but could be refined via problem-dependent arm-gap analysis.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant contribution, recommendation for acceptance.
