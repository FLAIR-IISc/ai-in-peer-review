- Claim: when horizon is large, context coupling disappears and each context could be treated as a separate bandit instance.
- Suggestion: include a clean problem-dependent upper bound in terms of arm gaps and ideally matching lower bounds (both problem-dependent and problem-independent).
- Paper studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints and claims log(T) regret when contexts J and arms K are finite and T is large.
- Result assessment: thorough and well-written but not very surprising given finite J, K can be seen as JK arms with availability constraints.
- Novelty concern: setting may be very close to time-/budget-constrained bandits with action constraints; authors should clarify originality.
- Question on Theorem 2 case (2): regret stated as O(√T + ...) — should this be at least O(√(KT) + ...)?
- Question: Theorem 2 gives O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T); typically problem-dependent bounds use sums of inverse gaps — can a similar refinement be obtained here?
- Methodological concern: proof relies on UCB index ordering leading to KJ terms rather than refined gap-based sums — is this optimal?
- Request: provide nontrivial lower bounds that capture the complexity added by contexts beyond classical single-context bandit lower bounds.
- Overall verdict: remains unconvinced about originality and stays neutral; asks authors to clarify novelty relative to budgeted/action-constrained bandits.
