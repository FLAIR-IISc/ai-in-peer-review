- Problem setup: contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T; contexts j ~ π, actions k∈{0,1…K} with skip action, stochastic rewards in [0,1], known costs c_{j,k}, maximize cumulative reward under budget constraint.  
- Main focus: special case with known π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, algorithm achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extension: algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Novelty: improves on [16] (which had O(√T) regret); possibly first O(log T) result, though reliant on the mild condition.  
- Method: algorithm built on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when statistics are known; simple and interesting.  
- Writing and motivation: paper is well-motivated and clearly written.  
- Limitation/concern: significance would increase if O(log T) held without the mild condition or if an Ω(√T) lower bound were shown for boundary cases.  
- Lemma 1: reviewer initially doubted correctness of Lemma 1 and equation (2) but was convinced after the rebuttal.  
- Open question: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard?  
- Open question: can results be extended to unknown horizon T?  
- Open question: can we assume u^1 > u^2 > … > u^*_J WLOG in the unknown context distribution case?  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
