- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, under finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Assumptions are simplified but remain practically relevant.  
- Proposes algorithms achieving O(log T) regret via an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-documented, and properly motivated with appropriate citations.  
- Presents a solid theoretical contribution and appears to be the first to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Proofs are convincing, though not exhaustively verified by the reviewer.  
- Practical considerations are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6 and no experiments are provided.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper that would benefit from deeper discussion of practical implications.
