- Problem/setup: studies contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T, actions include skipping, rewards in [0,1], costs c_{j,k}; objective is maximize reward under budget constraint.

- Main result: for known context distribution π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, proposes an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.

- Extension: algorithm is extended to unknown π and arbitrary costs.

- Novelty/significance: potentially the first O(log T) result for this problem (prior work [16] had O(√T)), but the logarithmic bound is conditional on the stated mild condition.

- Method: uses an approximation approach to compute the optimal action sequence given full statistics; algorithm is simple and insightful.

- Concern/limitation: significance would be stronger if the O(log T) bound held unconditionally or if a lower bound Ω(√T) were shown for cases violating the condition.

- Technical point resolved: reviewer’s initial doubt about Lemma 1 and the averaging relaxation of the hard constraint was addressed by the rebuttal; reviewer is convinced of Lemma 1’s validity.

- Minor/future questions: is exact optimal action sequencing NP-hard; can results handle unknown horizon T; can one assume u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^J w.l.o.g. when π is unknown.

- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
