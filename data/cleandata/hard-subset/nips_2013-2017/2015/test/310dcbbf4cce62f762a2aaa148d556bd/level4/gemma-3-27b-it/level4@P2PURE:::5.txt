This paper addresses a constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions, focusing on computational efficiency through a linear programming (LP) approximation, and achieving a log(T) regret bound. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear. 

Suggestions for future work include exploring a parametric model for the reward function, which would be particularly beneficial when dealing with a large number of contexts. In Section 4.1, the rationale behind combining UCB with the Accelerated Linear Program (ALP) in the UCB-ALP algorithm could be elaborated upon. The approach appears to leverage optimism in the face of uncertainty, and a connection to the policy update in UCRL2 might be insightful. Furthermore, the inclusion of numerical experiments would strengthen the paper.

The paper http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374 may be of interest. Overall, this work merits acceptance due to its provision of an efficient algorithm for constrained CB problems with near-optimal regret performance; it represents a valuable and demonstrable improvement over existing approaches.
