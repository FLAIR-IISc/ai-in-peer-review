

This paper addresses a well-motivated contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions under a budget constraint. The primary contributions are the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm—achieved through linear programming (LP) relaxation—and the logarithmic regret bound it attains. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow.  

Suggestions for improvement:  
1. In future work, consider employing a parametric reward model, which would be beneficial when the number of contexts becomes large.  
2. In Section 4.1, clarify the rationale for combining UCB with ALP in the UCB-ALP algorithm. Specifically, it appears the approach follows the "optimism in the face of uncertainty" principle, which resembles the policy update mechanism in UCRL2. Explicitly connecting these ideas would strengthen the exposition.  
3. Including numerical experiments in an extended version would further validate the algorithm’s practical efficacy.  

The following paper is relevant for comparison: arXiv:1506.03374.  

This work merits acceptance, as it provides an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with optimal regret guarantees in most settings. It represents a meaningful improvement over prior approaches and is both technically sound and interesting.
