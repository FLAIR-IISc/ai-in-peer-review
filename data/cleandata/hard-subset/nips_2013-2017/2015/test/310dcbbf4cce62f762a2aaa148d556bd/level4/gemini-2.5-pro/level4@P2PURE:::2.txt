This paper addresses the problem of contextual bandits under budget and time constraints. The analysis is restricted to a simplified, yet practically reasonable, setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. This focused setting allows the authors to introduce an adaptive linear programming approximation of an oracle, leading to algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret.

The paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and provides thorough citations of relevant work. It is a solid theoretical contribution. The primary significance of this work is that, to my knowledge, it is the first to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear convincing, although I did not verify every detail.

The main limitation is the brief discussion of practical aspects in Sections 5 and 6, along with the absence of experimental validation. While the paper's theoretical merits are clear, a more detailed discussion of its practical implications would strengthen it further.

In summary, this is a strong paper that makes judicious simplifying assumptions to derive novel algorithms with an impressive O(log T) regret bound.
