This paper studies a budget-constrained contextual bandit setting with discrete contexts and actions. Its primary contribution is a computationally efficient method based on an LP relaxation (used adaptively) and a UCB-based learning layer that together yield a logarithmic-in-T regret bound.

The paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Suggestions:
- For future work, consider parametric reward models, which can be advantageous when the context space is large.
- In Section 4.1, please clarify the principle underlying the UCB-ALP combination. My understanding is that it follows optimism under uncertainty, likely analogous to the policy update mechanism in UCRL2.
- It would be valuable to include numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper.
- You may wish to cite and compare more explicitly with: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

This paper deserves acceptance: it provides an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with optimal (logarithmic) regret in most cases, and represents an interesting and meaningful improvement over prior work.
