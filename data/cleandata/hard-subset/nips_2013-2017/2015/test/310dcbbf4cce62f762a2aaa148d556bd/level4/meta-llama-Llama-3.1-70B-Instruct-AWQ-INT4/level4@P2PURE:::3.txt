This paper addresses a contextual bandit problem with a budget constraint B and a known time horizon T. In each trial, the agent observes a context j drawn from a fixed probability distribution π over a context set {1, ..., J} and selects an action k from {0} ∪ {1, ..., K}, where 0 denotes "skip". If k = 0, the agent receives no reward and incurs no cost. If k > 0, the agent receives a reward y and incurs a cost c{j,k}, where y is drawn from a fixed probability distribution over [0,1] that depends on j and k, and c{j,k} > 0 is a known parameter. The agent's objective is to maximize the cumulative reward while ensuring that the cumulative cost never exceeds the budget B.

The paper primarily focuses on a special case where π is known and c{j,k} = 1 for all j and k, presenting an algorithm with a regret bound of O(log T) under a mild condition. The paper then extends this algorithm to the general case where π is unknown and c{j,k}'s are arbitrary. Notably, a more general setting is considered in [16], but with an algorithm that achieves O(√T) regret. This paper may be the first to achieve O(log T) regret, although this is contingent upon satisfying the mild condition. The algorithm is based on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when all statistics are known. The algorithm is simple yet intriguing.

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written. However, it would be more interesting and significant if the O(log T) regret bound held universally, rather than being conditional. Alternatively, proving an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases would be valuable.

Initially, I had concerns regarding the correctness of Lemma 1, as I did not believe the optimal action sequence always satisfies equation (2) for all trials. Consequently, I struggled to understand why the average constraint is a relaxed version of the hard constraint. However, the rebuttal has alleviated my concerns, and I now accept the validity of Lemma 1.

Some minor comments and suggestions for future work: (1) Is it NP-hard to exactly obtain the optimal action sequence? (2) Can the results be generalized to the case where T is unknown? (3) Can we assume u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J without loss of generality, even for the unknown context distribution case? I strongly recommend this paper for acceptance.
