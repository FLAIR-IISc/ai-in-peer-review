- Studies a contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T where action 0 skips, rewards y in [0,1], and costs c_{j,k} are known.
- Main focus: special case with known context distribution π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1.
- Presents an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild technical condition.
- Extends the algorithm to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.
- Prior work achieved O(√T) regret, so this may be the first O(log T) result (contingent on the mild condition).
- Algorithm is simple and based on an approximation for the offline optimal action sequence; overall approach is interesting.
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.
- Reviewer wished the O(log T) bound held without the mild condition, or that an Ω(√T) lower bound be proven for boundary cases.
- All results rely on Lemma 1; reviewer was initially skeptical about its correctness but was convinced after the rebuttal.
- Minor questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard; can results handle unknown T; can one assume u^1>u^2>...>u^*_J without loss of generality for unknown context distributions?
- Strong recommendation for acceptance.
