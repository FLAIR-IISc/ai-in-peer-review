- Problem and contribution: Studies constrained contextual bandits (finite contexts, fixed costs, single budget) and proposes an Adaptive LP (ALP) relaxation and UCB-ALP for learning.

- Benchmark and regret notion: Regret measured against an oracle that knows all statistics.

- ALP idea: Relax hard budget to adaptive average budget bτ/τ and use ordering of expected rewards to decide actions.

- ALP guarantee: ALP achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary instances and O(√T) on measure-zero boundary instances.

- UCB-ALP: Combines ALP with UCB estimation to learn rankings and attains O(JK log T) regret off-boundary and O(√T + JK log T) at boundaries.

- Extensions sketched: Unknown context distribution via empirical plug-in and truncation, and heterogeneous costs via an LP over context-action pairs and explore-then-ALP.

- Novelty vs prior work: Improves on prior √T results for resource-constrained bandits, giving logarithmic regret under stated finite/discrete assumptions and an oracle benchmark.

- Technical novelty: Maps budget evolution under ALP to sampling without replacement and uses hypergeometric concentration; careful regret decomposition for UCB-ALP.

- Computational efficiency: Requires solving a small per-round LP with threshold structure; avoids dynamic programming.

- Positioning: Well-situated in literature and likely the first to show logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits against an oracle.

- Empirical validation lacking: No experiments; small-scale simulations recommended to show constants, boundary behavior, and robustness.

- Boundary-case concerns: Performance can degrade near boundaries; recommend quantifying regret vs margin δ and adding practical safeguards (slack or randomized tie-breaking).

- Unknown context distribution underdeveloped: Truncated empirical approach is only sketched—needs truncation schedule, dependence on πmin and T, and high-probability guarantees.

- Heterogeneous-cost extension underdeveloped: Clarify LP size, per-round complexity, regret dependence on cost ranges, and feasibility of a fully online UCB-style method.

- Assumptions and generality: Paper assumes i.i.d. contexts and fixed costs; discuss adaptability to nonstationary π, unknown horizon, multiple resources, or continuous contexts.

- Comparative baselines missing: Provide theoretical/empirical comparisons to RCB/Mixture Elimination and BwK-style algorithms to highlight when ALP yields log vs √T regret.

- Clarity improvements: Add tie-breaking rules for equalities, collect pseudocode for ALP/UCB-ALP/EALP, and include a running example and figure for threshold structure.

- Quality: Technically solid with convincing proofs and careful arguments.

- Clarity assessment: Generally well written but would benefit from examples, figures, and expanded heterogeneous-cost details.

- Originality: Adaptive LP relaxation and ranking-reduction appear novel and insightful.

- Significance: Advances state of the art for constrained contextual bandits with computationally efficient algorithms and strong guarantees.

- Pros for acceptance: First log-regret guarantees under oracle benchmark; simple implementable algorithms; new analysis techniques of independent interest.

- Cons for acceptance: No experiments; incomplete treatment of boundary behavior and unknown-context analysis; heterogeneous-costs lightly treated.

- Key questions for authors: Request high-probability bounds, sensitivity to margin δ, a fully online UCB-ALP for heterogeneous costs, per-round LP cost and threshold structure in heterogeneous case, and robustness/adaptation to misspecified or drifting π.

- Recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on adding brief experiments and expanding boundary and heterogeneous-cost discussions.
