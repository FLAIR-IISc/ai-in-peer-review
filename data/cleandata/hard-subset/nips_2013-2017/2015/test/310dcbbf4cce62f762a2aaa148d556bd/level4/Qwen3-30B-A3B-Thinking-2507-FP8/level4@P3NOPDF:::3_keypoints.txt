- Problem: contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T, contexts from π, actions include a "skip" with costs c_{j,k} and stochastic rewards in [0,1].  
- Main result: algorithm achieving O(log T) regret for the special case of known π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1 under a mild condition.  
- Extension: approach generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs via a known-statistics approximation for optimal action sequences.  
- Novelty/significance: first to obtain O(log T) regret in this setting; prior work only achieved O(√T).  
- Key insight: relax the hard budget constraint to an average (over T) constraint to enable efficient optimization.  
- Lemma 1: central technical claim that the relaxed average constraint matches the hard budget under the mild condition; reviewer convinced after rebuttal.  
- Limitation: O(log T) guarantee depends on the mild condition; would be stronger if proven unconditionally or paired with a lower bound in boundary cases.  
- Open/minor questions: whether computing the optimal action sequence is NP-hard, extension to unknown T, and necessity of the ordering assumption in the unknown-π case.  
- Writing and presentation: well-motivated, clearly written, technically sound after clarification.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — valuable, novel contribution to contextual bandits with budget constraints.
