- Problem setup: budgeted contextual bandits with contexts j∈{1..J}, actions k∈{0..K}, cost constraint B over horizon T; core focus on known context distribution π and unit costs.

- Core contribution: algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild technical condition for the specialized (known π, unit-cost) setting.

- Extension: algorithm and analysis are extended to the general case with unknown context distribution and arbitrary positive costs.

- Novelty/significance: first-known result to obtain logarithmic regret for this budgeted contextual bandit setting, improving on typical O(√T) bounds.

- Clarity/presentation: paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and logically progresses from simplified to general settings.

- Algorithmic idea: simple, insightful algorithm based on an approximation to the offline problem; key analysis uses Lemma 1 to relax path-dependent budget constraints to expected-average-cost constraints.

- Limitation: O(log T) bound depends on a technical condition; more discussion is needed on cases where the condition fails.

- Suggestion: proving an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases would better characterize problem difficulty and justify the technical condition.

- Question (offline complexity): is finding the optimal action sequence (oracle) NP-hard when all statistics are known?

- Question (unknown T): can the algorithm and guarantees be adapted to an unknown time horizon T?

- Question (ordering assumption): is the assumed ordering of optimal expected rewards valid without loss of generality when π is unknown and must be learned?

- Recommendation: strong paper with significant results and elegant approach; strongly recommended for acceptance.
