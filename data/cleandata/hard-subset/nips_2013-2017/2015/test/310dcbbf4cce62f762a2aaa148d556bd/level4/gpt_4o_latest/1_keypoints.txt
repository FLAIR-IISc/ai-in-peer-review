- Reviewer thinks the context-coupled problem is effectively similar to treating each context as an independent bandit when horizon T is large, and questions why contexts aren’t treated independently.  
- Requests a more rigorous problem-dependent upper bound expressed in terms of arm gaps, and ideally matching problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds.  
- Notes the main contribution is showing O(log T) regret for finite contexts/actions and large T, but questions the novelty since the problem reduces to a JK-arm time-constrained bandit.  
- Finds the paper comprehensive and well-written technically, but the results are unsurprising given finite J and K.  
- Points out the supplementary-material upper bound needs revision.  
- Questions Theorem 2’s form: asks whether the sqrt(T) term should be O(sqrt(KT)) instead.  
- Criticizes Theorem 2’s use of O(KJ log T) rather than a refined sum over inverse arm gaps, and asks if the UCB-ordering-based proof is optimal.  
- Asks for refined lower bounds that capture context-induced complexity beyond trivial classical-bandit lower bounds.  
- Remains unconvinced about the originality of the setting and maintains a neutral recommendation, requesting clarification from the authors.
