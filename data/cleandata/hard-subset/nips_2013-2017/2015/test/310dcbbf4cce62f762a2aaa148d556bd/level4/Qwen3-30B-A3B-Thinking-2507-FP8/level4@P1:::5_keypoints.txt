- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with time and budget limits, focusing on a simplified model with finite contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Justifies model relevance with applications like clinical trials and wireless networks.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for known statistics, achieving near-optimal O(1) regret except at boundary cases.  
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP for unknown statistics, achieving O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases.  
- Key insight: ALP requires only reward orderings, enabling efficient combination with UCB for exploration.  
- First known result achieving logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits (per authors/reviewer).  
- Extends and improves over prior work (Badanidiyuru et al. 2013: O(√T) with different benchmark and high complexity).  
- Contrasts with Agrawal et al. (2015), which assumes static contexts and yields O(√T) in this setting.  
- Technical exposition is precise, well-organized, and accessible, with detailed proofs and clear intuition.  
- Regret analysis is rigorous, carefully handling boundary cases, ranking errors, and budget fluctuations.  
- Originality is strong: novel ALP design and its combination with UCB represent a meaningful innovation.  
- Results are significant and computationally efficient, with practical relevance to many budgeted applications.  
- Clear statement of limitations and applicable regime (B and T growing proportionally).  
- Minor weaknesses: lacks explicit connection to the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle and limited discussion on unknown context-distribution extensions.  
- Suggested future work: extend to continuous contexts or parametric models and include numerical experiments to demonstrate practical performance.  
- Recommendation: paper merits acceptance due to theoretical novelty, strong results, efficiency, and high-quality presentation.
