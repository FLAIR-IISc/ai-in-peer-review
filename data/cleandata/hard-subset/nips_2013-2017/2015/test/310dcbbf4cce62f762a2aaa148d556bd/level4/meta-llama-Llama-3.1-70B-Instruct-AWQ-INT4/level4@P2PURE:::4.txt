This paper presents an interesting setup and unique solutions for analyzing contextual bandits against a stronger baseline, which utilizes adaptive linear programming to decide on passing over a certain percentage of suboptimal rounds, referred to as the "budget." However, I have one major question regarding the analysis: what is the justification for treating boundary cases differently? Specifically, it is unclear why the case where rho equals q_j, particularly for j greater than 1, warrants special consideration, as it does not appear to possess any unique properties.
