

This paper addresses a constrained contextual bandit problem with discrete contexts and actions, featuring a budget constraint. The primary contribution lies in the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm (achieved via linear programming approximation) and its log T regret bound. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.  

Suggestions for future work: (1) Consider employing a parametric reward model when the context space is large; (2) In Section 4.1, clarify the rationale for combining UCB with ALP in the UCB-ALP algorithm. Specifically, the approach appears to follow the optimism principle under uncertainty, which may be equivalent to policy updates in UCRL2; (3) Include numerical experiments in an extended version.  

For related work, see: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374. The paper merits acceptance as it delivers an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with near-optimal regret in most cases, representing a meaningful advancement over prior work.
