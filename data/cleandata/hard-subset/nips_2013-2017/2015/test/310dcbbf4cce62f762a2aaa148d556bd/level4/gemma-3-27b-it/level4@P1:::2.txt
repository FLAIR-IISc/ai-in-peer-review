The paper considers contextual bandits with budget and time constraints. The analysis is restricted to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While the setting is simplified, these assumptions are still reasonable in practice and allow for a tractable analysis. Importantly, the authors introduce an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation of the oracle, leading to algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret.

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, with relevant work cited and strong motivation provided. This is a solid theoretical contribution. While the practical aspects are discussed briefly in Sections 5 and 6, experimental results are not included. Nevertheless, the contributions are significant; to the best of my knowledge, the authors are the first to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear convincing, although a full verification was not possible within the scope of this review. Overall, this is a good paper that makes appropriate simplifying assumptions to obtain O(log T) regret algorithms. Further discussion of practical considerations and potential empirical validation would be beneficial. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novelty: The paper presents the first work demonstrating logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.
*   Theoretical soundness: The analysis is rigorous and provides strong theoretical guarantees.
*   Clarity: The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
*   Significance: The problem of constrained contextual bandits is practically relevant, and the proposed solution is a significant step forward.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of experimental validation: The absence of experimental results limits the assessment of the algorithm’s practical performance.
*   Simplified assumptions: While reasonable, the assumptions limit the direct applicability of the results to more complex scenarios.
