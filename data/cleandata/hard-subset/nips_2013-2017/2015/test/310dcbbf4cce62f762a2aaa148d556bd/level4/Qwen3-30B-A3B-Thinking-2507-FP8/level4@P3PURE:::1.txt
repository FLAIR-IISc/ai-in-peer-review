

Review after rebuttal: I acknowledge the distinction, but the setting appears sufficiently close that the context coupling vanishes for large horizons (I remain unclear why this is not addressed). Consequently, I question why each context occurrence cannot be treated as an independent bandit instance.  

The paper's core contribution—achieving $\mathcal{O}(\log T)$ regret for a budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandit with finite contexts $J$ and arms $K$ when $T$ is sufficiently large—is valid. However, the novelty requires clarification: this problem reduces to a time-constrained, action-constrained bandit with $JK$ arms (where only $K$ arms are accessible per context), and finite-armed bandits with such constraints achieve $\mathcal{O}(\log T)$ regret for large $T$. This reduction suggests the result is less surprising than claimed.  

Key concerns remain:  
1. **Theorem 2 (Case 2)**: The regret bound $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{T} + \cdots)$ appears incorrect. It should be $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{KT} + \cdots)$ at minimum, given the $K$-arm complexity.  
2. **Problem-dependent bounds**: The current bound $\mathcal{O}(KJ \log T)$ or $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{T} + KJ \log T)$ lacks refinement. Standard problem-dependent bounds express regret via sums of inverse arm gaps (e.g., $\sum_{i} \frac{1}{\Delta_i}$). While the proof relies on UCB order events rather than gap concentration, this approach may not yield optimal bounds. A refined bound incorporating arm gaps would significantly strengthen the paper.  
3. **Lower bounds**: The paper lacks matching lower bounds (both problem-dependent and independent). Trivial lower bounds (e.g., classical bandit bounds with cost=0 and fixed context) are insufficient; bounds should incorporate context complexity.  

The supplementary material's upper bound requires reworking. Without a clean problem-dependent bound with matching lower bounds, the paper's technical contribution remains limited. The setting is closely related to budgeted bandits with action constraints, and the authors must clarify the novelty gap in their rebuttal. I remain neutral pending these clarifications.
