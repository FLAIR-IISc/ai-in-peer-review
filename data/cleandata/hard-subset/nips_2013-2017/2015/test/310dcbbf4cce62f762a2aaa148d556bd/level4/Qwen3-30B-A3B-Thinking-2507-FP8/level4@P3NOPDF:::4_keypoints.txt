- Introduces a novel contextual bandit comparison using adaptive linear programming to strategically forgo a fraction of suboptimal rounds ("budget").  
- Setup is interesting and addresses a meaningful gap in the literature.  
- Core contribution—using adaptive LP to manage a skip-budget of rounds—is compelling and models a realistic learning scenario.  
- Theoretical guarantees and empirical validation are strong.  
- Critical ambiguity: the paper analyzes cases with ρ = q_j separately for j > 1 without clear justification.  
- Argument: ρ = q_j for j > 1 does not seem to introduce unique structure or computational issues, so the separate treatment appears arbitrary.  
- Request: authors must justify the segmentation by showing a mathematical discontinuity, computational artifact, or algorithmic constraint specific to those points.  
- Suggestion: if unnecessary, subsume the boundary analysis into a unified framework to simplify exposition and strengthen the argument.  
- Minor clarification: define "budget" precisely in the introduction (e.g., "a user-specified fraction of rounds to be skipped").  
- Recommendation: paper is suitable for acceptance pending clarification of the boundary-case treatment.
