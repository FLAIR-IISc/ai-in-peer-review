- Problem: contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T where agent observes context j ~ π, chooses action k∈{0..K} (0 = skip), receives reward and incurs cost, aim to maximize cumulative reward under budget.  
- Main contribution: for known π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, propose an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a specific condition, extended to unknown π and arbitrary costs.  
- Comparison to prior work: related paper [16] only achieves O(√T) regret, so this may be the first O(log T) result for this problem under the stated condition.  
- Method: uses an approximation technique to compute the optimal action sequence with full statistical knowledge; the approach is simple and compelling.  
- Presentation: paper is well-motivated and exceptionally clearly written.  
- Limitation/impact: broader impact would increase if the O(log T) guarantee were universal or if a matching Ω(√T) lower bound were provided for boundary cases.  
- Lemma 1: initial concerns about condition (2) and using an average-constraint relaxation were raised, but the rebuttal clarified these and the reviewer is satisfied.  
- Minor questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence computationally hard (NP-hard)? can results generalize to unknown horizon T? is the ordering assumption u^1>u^2>... WLOG when π is unknown?  
- Recommendation: strong accept.
