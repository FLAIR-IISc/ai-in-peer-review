This paper addresses a relatively simple budget-constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The primary contribution of the paper is the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm, achieved through linear programming (LP) approximation, and the logarithmic regret bound it obtains.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read. 

One potential direction for future work is to consider using a parametric model for the reward function. This could be particularly useful when dealing with a large number of contexts, as it would allow for more efficient generalization.

In Section 4.1, when combining Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) with the Approximate Linear Program (ALP), it would be beneficial to provide a clearer explanation of the underlying rationale behind the UCB-ALP algorithm. For instance, it appears that the approach follows the principle of optimism in the face of uncertainty, which is similar to the policy update in UCRL2. Clarifying this connection would strengthen the presentation.

Including numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would be valuable in further demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Readers may also find the following paper relevant: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374.

Overall, this paper merits acceptance, as it provides an efficient algorithm for constrained CB with optimal regret in most cases. The work is interesting and indeed an improvement over existing research in the field.
