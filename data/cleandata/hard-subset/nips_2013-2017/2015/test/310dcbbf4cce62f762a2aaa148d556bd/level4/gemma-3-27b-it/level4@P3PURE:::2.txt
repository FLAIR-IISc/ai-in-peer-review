This paper addresses the problem of contextual bandits under budget and time constraints. The analysis focuses on finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While these are simplifying assumptions, they remain reasonable for many practical applications. Crucially, this framework allows the authors to develop algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by leveraging an adaptive linear programming approximation of the optimal policy.

The paper is clearly written and well-documented. The authors provide adequate citations to relevant prior work and clearly articulate their motivation. This is a strong theoretical contribution. The discussion of practical implications is limited to Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental evaluation is presented. Nevertheless, the contributions are significant; to the best of my knowledge, this work is the first to establish logarithmic regret bounds for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear convincing, although I did not verify them in their entirety. Overall, this is a good paper that makes appropriate simplifying assumptions to achieve algorithms with O(log T) regret. Expanding the discussion of practical considerations would further strengthen the work.
