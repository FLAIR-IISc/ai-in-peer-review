This paper tackles a relatively straightforward budget-constrained contextual bandit (cb) problem, characterized by discrete contexts and actions. The primary contribution lies in the algorithm's computational efficiency, achieved through LP approximation, and its accomplishment of a log(T) regret bound.

The paper is exceptionally well-written, making it easy to comprehend and navigate.

For future research directions, the authors may consider incorporating a parametric model for the reward, particularly beneficial when dealing with a large number of contexts. In Section 4.1, a clearer explanation of the rationale behind combining UCB with ALP in the UCB-ALP algorithm would be beneficial. Specifically, it appears that the approach follows the principle of optimism in the face of uncertainty, which is analogous to the policy update in UCRL2. Including numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would be a valuable addition.

The authors may also find the following reference relevant: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

This paper warrants acceptance due to its provision of an efficient algorithm for constrained cb with optimal regret in most scenarios, making it an intriguing and notable improvement over existing works.
