- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with both budget and time limits, claiming logarithmic regret in most cases.  
- Key contribution: shows logarithmic regret is achievable for constrained contextual bandits, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Introduces Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that relaxes hard budget constraints to an average constraint, achieving O(1) regret in non-boundary cases.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP (ALP + UCB) achieving O(JK log T) regret for non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) for boundary cases.  
- Extends methods to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, demonstrating robustness.  
- Technical presentation is rigorous with clear definitions, proofs, and detailed regret bounds.  
- Writing is precise and well-organized; experiments (not fully shown) purportedly support theory.  
- Situates contribution within literature, distinguishing from Resourceful Contextual Bandits and other budgeted bandit work.  
- Strength: novel LP-based approach decouples budget-information coupling and is computationally efficient for practice.  
- Weakness: connection to standard bandit formulations with action constraints (JK arms, K accessible per step) is not made sufficiently clear.  
- Weakness: regret bounds are O(JK log T) rather than refined gap-dependent expressions common in bandit literature.  
- Weakness: lacks matching lower bounds to establish tightness of the provided upper bounds.  
- Weakness: boundary cases require more thorough analysis; they may be more frequent than implied.  
- Suggested fixes: clarify relation to standard action-constrained bandits, explore gap-dependent bounds, provide lower bounds, and analyze boundary cases more deeply.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution with practical significance; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
