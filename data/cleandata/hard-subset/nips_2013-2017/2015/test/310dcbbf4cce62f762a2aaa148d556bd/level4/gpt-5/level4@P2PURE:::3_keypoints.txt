- Studies a contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T where actions incur known costs and rewards depend on context-action distributions.  
- Focuses on special case with known context distribution π and unit costs c{j,k}=1 for all j,k.  
- Proposes an algorithm that achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extends the approach to the general setting with unknown π and arbitrary costs c{j,k}.  
- Likely first to obtain O(log T) regret for this problem setting; prior work [16] only achieved O(√T).  
- Method builds on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when all statistics are known.  
- Algorithm is simple and interesting; paper is well motivated and clearly written.  
- Would be more compelling if O(log T) regret could be guaranteed universally, or alternatively an Ω(√T) lower bound in boundary cases.  
- All results depend critically on Lemma 1; reviewer was initially skeptical but convinced by the rebuttal.  
- Minor questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard; can results extend to unknown T; can we assume ordered utilities u^1>u^2>…>u^*_J when π is unknown?  
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
