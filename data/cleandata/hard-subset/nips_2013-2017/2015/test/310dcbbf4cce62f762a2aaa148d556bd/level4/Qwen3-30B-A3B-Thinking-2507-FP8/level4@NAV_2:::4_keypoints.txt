- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget/time constraints, relevant to clinical trials and recommendations.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) for known statistics and UCB-ALP for unknown rewards.  
- Achieves logarithmic regret in most cases; boundary cases yield O(√T) regret.  
- Improves over prior work (e.g., [17]) that achieved only O(√T) with a restricted benchmark.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and novel techniques for bounding regret under adaptive algorithms and random contexts.  
- Justifies simplifying assumptions (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget) as practical and enabling computational efficiency.  
- ALP requires only the ordering of expected rewards, facilitating combination with UCB methods.  
- Introduces a new framework that, to the authors’ knowledge, is first to achieve logarithmic regret in this setting.  
- Effectively relates to and compares with prior NIPS work and related literature.  
- Notes a weakness: boundary cases (ρ = q_j) need deeper analysis and clearer justification despite being measure-zero.  
- Well-written, reproducible, and experimentally supportive of theoretical claims.  
- Key insight: constrained contextual bandits can be decoupled via adaptive linear relaxation, yielding practical and theoretical benefits.
