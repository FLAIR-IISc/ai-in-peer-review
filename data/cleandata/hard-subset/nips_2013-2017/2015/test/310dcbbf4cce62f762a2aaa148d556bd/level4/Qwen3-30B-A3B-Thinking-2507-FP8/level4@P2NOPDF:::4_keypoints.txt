- Presents a novel contextual bandits framework evaluated against a stronger baseline using adaptive linear programming to forgo a fraction of suboptimal arm selections ("budget").  
- Adaptive budgeting baseline is a compelling and innovative contribution.  
- Main criticism: analysis treats the boundary case ρ = q_j as a distinct scenario without justification.  
- The special treatment of ρ = q_j is particularly questionable when j > 1.  
- No structural or algorithmic reason is provided why ρ = q_j should trigger different analysis.  
- If ρ = q_j is merely a continuous transition point, isolating it undermines the paper’s generalizability.  
- The boundary case could be a technical artifact of the LP formulation or a design choice, but this is not clarified.  
- Without justification, the treatment appears ad hoc and risks weakening the theoretical claims.  
- Addressing the unresolved boundary-case issue is essential for full validation of the work.
