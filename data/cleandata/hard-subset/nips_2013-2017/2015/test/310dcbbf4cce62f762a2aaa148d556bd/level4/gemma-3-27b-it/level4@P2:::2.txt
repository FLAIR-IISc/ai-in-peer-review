This paper addresses the problem of contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, a challenging extension of the classical contextual bandit setting. The authors focus on a simplified, yet practical, setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Their key contribution is the development of algorithms achieving O(log T) regret, leveraging an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation of the optimal policy. 

The paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem, proposed approach, and theoretical results. The motivation for the work is well-explained, and the related literature is appropriately cited. The authors correctly position their work within the existing body of research on contextual bandits and constrained optimization. This is a theoretically sound paper with convincing proofs – although a complete verification of all proofs was beyond the scope of this review. The core idea of approximating the oracle with ALP is insightful and appears to be a significant step towards solving constrained contextual bandit problems.

A potential weakness lies in the limited discussion of practical considerations. While Sections 5 and 6 extend the framework to more general settings (unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs), these extensions receive relatively brief treatment.  Furthermore, the absence of experimental results is notable. While theoretical contributions are valuable, empirical validation would strengthen the claims and demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed algorithms. 

However, the authors convincingly demonstrate a significant theoretical advance: to the best of my knowledge, this is the first work to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits. The simplified assumptions made are justified and lead to a valuable contribution to the field. The analysis is rigorous and demonstrates a strong understanding of the underlying principles.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of contextual bandits.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The proposed algorithms are rigorously analyzed, and the theoretical results (O(log T) regret) are convincing.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand.
*   **Significance:** Achieving logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits is a substantial advance with potential for practical applications.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Lack of Empirical Evaluation:** The absence of experimental results limits the assessment of the algorithms' practical performance.
*   **Limited Practical Discussion:** The discussion of practical aspects and extensions is relatively brief.



**Overall assessment:** This is a strong paper that makes a valuable theoretical contribution to the field of constrained contextual bandits. While the lack of experimental results is a minor drawback, the significance of the theoretical results and the clarity of the presentation warrant acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper.
