Given the brevity of this review, I have one main question: Why must the boundary cases be analyzed separately? Specifically, the condition ρ = q_j does not seem inherently special, particularly for indices j > 1. Clarifying what fails in the general-case arguments at this boundary—or providing intuition for any structural change that occurs—would be helpful.

The paper studies contextual bandits against a stronger baseline that uses adaptive linear programming to allocate a “budget,” allowing the algorithm to skip or defer a certain fraction of potentially suboptimal rounds. The problem formulation is compelling, and the proposed solutions appear novel and well-motivated.
