- Reviewer accepts authors’ clarification that the setting differs from budgeted bandits with action constraints but remains unconvinced overall, especially about diminishing coupling of contexts with large horizon.  
- Questions why each occurrence of a context cannot be treated as a separate bandit instance, which undermines perceived novelty.  
- Requests a clear problem-dependent upper bound on regret in terms of arm gaps and ideally matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent cases; notes current supplementary bound needs reworking.  
- Acknowledges main contribution: proof that O(log T) regret is achievable for finite contexts/actions when T is large, but finds the result unsurprising given a JK-armed constrained-bandit framing.  
- Asks authors to address the degree of innovation given the equivalence to a time-constrained/budgeted bandit with JK arms and K-access constraints.  
- Questions Theorem 2: whether the stated O(√T + …) term should instead be O(√(KT) + …).  
- Challenges the use of coarse KJ factors in problem-dependent bounds and asks if refinement to sums of inverse arm gaps (more precise gap-dependent bounds) is possible.  
- Requests lower bounds that capture context complexity beyond trivial classical-bandit lower bounds.  
- Overall rating: neutral due to unresolved concerns about originality and relation to budgeted/action-constrained bandits.
