- Paper studies a contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T where contexts follow distribution π, actions include "skip", rewards in [0,1], and costs c_{j,k}.

- Focuses on the special case with known π and unit costs (c_{j,k}=1), providing an algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild condition.

- Extends the algorithm to the general case with unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.

- Prior work ([16]) handles a more general setting but only achieves O(√T) regret; this paper may be first to achieve O(log T) (subject to the mild condition).

- Algorithm is based on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when statistics are known; the method is simple and interesting.

- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written.

- Limitation: O(log T) guarantee depends on a mild condition; removing this condition or proving an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases would increase significance.

- Reviewer was initially skeptical about Lemma 1 but was convinced by the authors' rebuttal.

- Minor suggestions/future work: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard? can results extend to unknown T? can one assume a sorted ordering u^1 > u^2 > ... without loss of generality in the unknown-π case?

- Recommendation: Strong accept.
