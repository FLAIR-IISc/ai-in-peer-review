- Problem setup: contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T, actions include "skip" (no reward/cost); rewards in [0,1], costs c_{j,k}, objective maximize cumulative reward subject to cost ≤ B.  
- Core contribution: for known context distribution π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, the paper presents an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extension: authors generalize the approach to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Novelty: appears to be the first work achieving O(log T) regret in this setting (conditioned on the mild assumption), improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Method: algorithm uses an approximation of the optimal action sequence under full statistical knowledge; approach is simple and elegant.  
- Writing and motivation: paper is well-motivated and clearly written.  
- Limitations: significance would increase if the O(log T) bound held universally rather than only under the mild condition, given Ω(√T) lower bounds at boundaries.  
- Technical concern resolved: Lemma 1 is critical and initially seemed questionable, but the reviewer is convinced of its validity after the rebuttal.  
- Minor questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard; can results extend to unknown T; in unknown π case can one assume u_1>u_2>…>u_J* without loss of generality.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution to contextual bandits under budget constraints; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
