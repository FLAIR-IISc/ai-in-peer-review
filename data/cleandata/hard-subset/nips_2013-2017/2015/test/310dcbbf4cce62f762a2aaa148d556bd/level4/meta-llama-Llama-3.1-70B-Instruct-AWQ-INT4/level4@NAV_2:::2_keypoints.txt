- Considers contextual bandits with budget and time constraints under finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Uses reasonable simplified assumptions that make analysis tractable though limit generality.  
- Introduces an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle combined with UCB to design algorithms.  
- Main theoretical result: algorithms achieve O(log T) regret for the constrained contextual bandit setting.  
- Claims novelty: first to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Proofs are convincing and technically sound, though not exhaustively checked by the reviewer.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and easy for experts to reproduce results from the exposition.  
- Practical aspects are only briefly discussed (Sections 5 and 6) and need more detail.  
- No experimental results are provided to validate the theoretical findings.  
- Primary strength: significant theoretical contribution advancing state of the art for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Primary weaknesses: restricted/simplified setting, lack of experiments, and limited practical discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses.
