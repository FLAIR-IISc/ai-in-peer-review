Summary
The paper studies a budget-constrained contextual bandit problem with discrete contexts and actions. Its main contribution is an algorithm that is computationally efficient via a linear-programming (LP) approximation and achieves logarithmic regret in the time horizon T under the stated assumptions. The presentation is clear and easy to follow.

Strengths
- Clear problem formulation and motivation for budget constraints in contextual bandits.
- Elegant use of an LP relaxation to obtain a computationally efficient policy.
- Theoretical guarantees with a log(T) regret bound, which is near-optimal for this setting.
- The writing is strong; the paper is well structured and accessible.

Weaknesses / Areas for clarification
- The rationale behind the UCB-ALP combination in Section 4.1 could be explained more explicitly. The algorithm appears to follow the “optimism in the face of uncertainty” principle; making this explicit would improve readability. It would also be helpful to clarify whether the policy updates are formally equivalent (or closely related) to those in UCRL2, and to delineate similarities and differences.
- The approach assumes a discrete and not-too-large context space. When the number of contexts is large, the LP and learning updates may become less practical without generalization. A brief discussion of scalability or feature-based generalization would strengthen the paper.

Suggestions for improvement
- Consider a parametric (or generalized linear) reward model to enable generalization across contexts and reduce sample complexity in large context spaces. Even a short discussion or an outline of how the LP-based policy would adapt to a parametric setting would be valuable.
- In Section 4.1, provide a more detailed derivation or intuition for the UCB-ALP update rule, explicitly tying it to optimism-based planning and clarifying the conditions under which it coincides with (or differs from) UCRL2-style updates.
- Empirical validation: while the theory is the main contribution, a set of numerical experiments (even if deferred to an extended version) would help illustrate practical performance and robustness. Useful diagnostics might include:
  - Sensitivity to budget level and horizon.
  - Scaling with the number of contexts and actions.
  - Comparison to natural baselines (e.g., greedy LP with plug-in estimates, standard UCB without budgeting, primal-dual or inventory-constrained heuristics).
  - Impact of model misspecification or nonstationary rewards, if relevant.
- Related work: you may wish to discuss and cite related results such as arXiv:1506.03374, positioning your contribution relative to closely related constrained or resource-aware bandit algorithms.

Significance and novelty
The combination of an LP approximation for computational efficiency with a principled exploration mechanism that yields logarithmic regret is both interesting and practically relevant. To my knowledge, the regret guarantees for this specific constrained contextual bandit formulation improve upon or cleanly unify prior approaches in most regimes considered. The work fills a useful gap between theoretically sound resource-constrained bandit methods and algorithms that are computationally tractable.

Clarity and presentation
The paper is generally very well written and easy to follow. The theoretical results are stated clearly, and the assumptions are understandable. Adding the clarifications noted above would further improve accessibility, especially for readers less familiar with optimism-based methods and their connections to LP-based planning.

Overall assessment
This paper provides an efficient algorithm for budget-constrained contextual bandits with strong theoretical guarantees, including a log(T) regret bound. The method is well motivated, the analysis is careful, and the presentation is strong. With the suggested clarifications on the UCB-ALP rationale, a short discussion of scalability to large context spaces (possibly via parametric models), and some empirical illustrations in an extended version, the paper would be even stronger.

Recommendation: Accept.
