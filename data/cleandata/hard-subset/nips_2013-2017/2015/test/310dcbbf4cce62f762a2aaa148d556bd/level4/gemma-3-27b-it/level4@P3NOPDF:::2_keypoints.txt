1. Problem setup: studies contextual bandits with budget/time constraints under finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.

2. Main contribution: develops algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret using an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.

3. Novelty claim: to the reviewers' knowledge, first work to achieve logarithmic regret bounds for constrained contextual bandits.

4. Writing quality: paper is clearly written, well-organized, with explicit motivation and appropriate related work.

5. Theoretical analysis: solid and convincing theoretical development; arguments appear logically consistent though not fully verified by reviewer.

6. Modeling choices: simplifying assumptions are reasonable and enable rigorous analysis.

7. Major weakness: complete absence of experimental validation on synthetic or real-world datasets.

8. Practical concerns: limited discussion of implementation details and computational cost of the adaptive LP, especially scaling with number of contexts.

9. Impact assessment: theoretical contributions are substantial and potentially impactful despite practical gaps.

10. Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to add experiments and a more detailed discussion of practical/computational considerations in future revisions.
