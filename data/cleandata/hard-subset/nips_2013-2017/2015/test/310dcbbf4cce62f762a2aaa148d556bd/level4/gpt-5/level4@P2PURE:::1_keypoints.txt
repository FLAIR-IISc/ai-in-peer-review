- Reviewer notes the setting is very close to action-constrained (budgeted) bandits and for large T the context coupling vanishes, so each context could be treated as a separate bandit instance.  
- Requests a cleaner problem-dependent upper bound expressed in arm gaps and ideally matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent regimes.  
- Summarizes main result: for finite contexts J and arms K and sufficiently large T, O(log T) regret is achievable; finds result thorough but not surprising given reduction to JK arms.  
- Questions the paper's novelty and originality, suggesting the setting may not be substantially different from budgeted bandits with action constraints.  
- Asks whether Theorem 2’s √T term should be at least √(K T) instead.  
- Notes Theorem 2 gives O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T) and asks whether bounds can be refined to sums of inverse gaps (more typical problem-dependent form).  
- Points out the proof uses UCB ordering events leading to a KJ factor instead of gap-sum bounds and asks if that approach is optimal.  
- Urges providing nontrivial lower bounds that reflect context complexity, beyond trivial classical-bandit lower bounds when cost is zero and context constant.  
- Concludes neutral recommendation due to doubts about originality and requests clarification.
