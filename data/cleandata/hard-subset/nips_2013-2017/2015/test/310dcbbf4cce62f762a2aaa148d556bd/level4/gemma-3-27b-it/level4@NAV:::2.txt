This paper investigates the problem of contextual bandits under both budget and time limitations. The authors focus their analysis on a finite, discrete action space, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint – a simplification that, nonetheless, maintains practical relevance. A key contribution is the development of algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret through an adaptive linear programming approximation of the optimal policy. 

The presentation is lucid and thoroughly documented, with appropriate citations to related work and a clear statement of motivation. Overall, this is a strong theoretical contribution. While practical considerations are touched upon in Sections 5 and 6, the paper lacks empirical validation. Despite this, the results represent a significant advancement; to the best of my knowledge, this work is the first to demonstrate logarithmic regret bounds for constrained contextual bandit problems. The proofs appear sound, though a complete verification was beyond the scope of this review. In summary, this is a valuable paper that skillfully leverages simplifying assumptions to achieve provably efficient, O(log T) regret algorithms. Further discussion of the practical implications would be beneficial.
