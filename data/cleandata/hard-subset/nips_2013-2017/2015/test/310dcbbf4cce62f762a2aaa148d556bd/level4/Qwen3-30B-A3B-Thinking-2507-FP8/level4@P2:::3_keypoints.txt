- Paper addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget/time limits and maximizes reward under resource constraints.  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) that relaxes hard budget constraints to an average constraint to approximate the oracle.  
- Introduces UCB-ALP combining ALP with UCB for unknown reward statistics.  
- Achieves logarithmic regret O(log T) in most cases, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Works under a simplified model: finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- Builds on and extends prior contextual and constrained bandit literature, offering a more natural regret definition.  
- Algorithms are computationally efficient compared to dynamic programming approaches.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous with detailed proofs and novel techniques handling information–decision coupling.  
- Identifies boundary cases where regret is O(√T) and analyzes them; these cases are argued to be measure-zero.  
- Originality is high: first to obtain logarithmic regret under the given assumptions and introduces a novel ALP approximation.  
- Strong practical significance for settings with budget constraints (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing).  
- Main limitations: limited discussion of how common boundary cases are in practice and insufficient treatment of unknown horizon T.  
- Assumes strict ordering u*1 > u*2 > ... > u*J without fully justifying necessity.  
- Clear, well-structured writing and presentation; overall recommendation: strong candidate for acceptance.
