- Studies constrained contextual bandits with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint (motivated by clinical trials and wireless networks).
- Introduces Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle solution, achieving near-optimal performance with O(1) regret except for boundary cases.
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, which attains O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(JK log T) specifically.
- Makes a significant theoretical advance by achieving logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits, improving over prior O(√T) results.
- Correctly positions the work relative to prior contextual-bandit and budgeted-bandit literature and RCB [17] limitations.
- Technical analysis is rigorous and well-structured; ALP and the regret analysis carefully handle budget fluctuations, context arrivals, and ranking errors.
- Proofs appear solid but supplementary material should more explicitly discuss concentration arguments.
- Writing, organization, and explanations (including unit-cost intuition) are clear and effective.
- Originality: novel implementation of LP-based DP approximation and first to show logarithmic regret in this setting.
- Significance: high practical relevance and computationally efficient algorithms useful for applications and future work.
- Weaknesses: main text lacks experimental validation; empirical results relegated to supplement.
- Weaknesses: discussion of boundary cases (where regret is O(√T)) is brief and should clarify prevalence in practice.
- Weaknesses: extension to unknown context distributions (Section 5) is underdeveloped with insufficient implementation detail.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand on boundary cases and practical implementation details.
