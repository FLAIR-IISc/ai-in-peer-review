- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints in a simplified but practical finite-context, fixed-cost, single-budget setting.  
- Proposes adaptive linear programming (ALP) algorithms approximating the oracle to handle constraints.  
- Main theoretical claim: first to achieve O(log T) regret for constrained contextual bandits under reasonable assumptions.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and motivates the problem effectively.  
- Positions work relative to prior resourceful contextual bandits literature and explains simplifications for tractability.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough with detailed proofs and sound LP-based regret analysis.  
- Demonstrates extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, indicating practical relevance.  
- Major weakness: no experimental validation to assess empirical performance or tightness of bounds.  
- Limited discussion of practical implementation, computational complexity, and scalability concerns.  
- Regret guarantee has "boundary cases" where performance degrades to O(√T); implications and frequency of these cases are underexplored.  
- Comparison to more recent related work (e.g., dynamic context extensions) is insufficient.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical advance, significant O(log T) result, clear writing, and rigorous analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of experiments and limited practical discussion weaken impact.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; accept with encouragement to add experiments and fuller comparison to recent work.
