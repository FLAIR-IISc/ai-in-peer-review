- Paper studies contextual bandits with a budget over horizon T where contexts j ~ π and actions k ∈ {0,...,K} have rewards y and known costs c_{j,k}, maximizing reward under cost budget B.
- Core focus is the simplified case with known context distribution π and unit costs (c_{j,k}=1), under which the authors present an algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild condition.
- The algorithm is extended to handle unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.
- Achieving O(log T) regret (conditional on the mild assumption) is a notable improvement over prior O(√T) results (e.g., [16]).
- The algorithm is conceptually simple and connects to approximation algorithms for computing optimal action sequences when statistics are known.
- The paper is very well written and clearly motivated.
- A valuable future direction is to either prove the O(log T) bound universally or establish an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases where the condition fails.
- Reviewer’s initial concern about Lemma 1 (validity of averaging the cost constraint) was raised but satisfactorily addressed by the rebuttal.
- Minor questions for authors: is finding the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard (to justify approximations)?
- Minor question: can the results be extended to unknown time horizon T?
- Minor question: in the unknown context-distribution case, is assuming u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^J without loss of generality valid—please clarify.
- Reviewer strongly recommends acceptance given the clear exposition, insightful algorithm, and potentially significant regret improvement.
