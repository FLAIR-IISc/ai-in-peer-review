

The paper addresses contextual bandits under budget and time constraints, constrained to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. While these assumptions simplify the setting, they remain well-motivated for practical applications. Crucially, the authors derive algorithms achieving $O(\log T)$ regret by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle. The paper is clearly written and well-documented, with relevant prior work cited and motivation explicitly stated. This constitutes a strong theoretical contribution. The primary limitation lies in the brief discussion of practical aspects (Sections 5–6) and the absence of experimental validation. Nevertheless, the authors appear to be the first to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under these assumptions. The proofs are convincing (though not fully verified in detail), and the paper makes appropriate simplified assumptions to achieve its key result. The work would benefit from a more detailed exploration of practical implementation considerations.
