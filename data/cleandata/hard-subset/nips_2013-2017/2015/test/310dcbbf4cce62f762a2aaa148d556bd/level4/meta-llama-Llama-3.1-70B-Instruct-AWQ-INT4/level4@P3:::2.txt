This paper studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, referred to as constrained contextual bandits. The authors consider a simplified setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint, which is reasonable in practice and allows for the design of easily-implementable algorithms.

The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm, which approximates the oracle and achieves near-optimal performance while tolerating certain estimation errors of system parameters. When the expected rewards are unknown, the proposed UCB-ALP algorithm combines ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method and achieves O(log T) regret except for certain boundary cases, where it achieves O(√T) regret.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their work. Relevant literature is cited, and the motivation for the research is explicitly stated. The theoretical contributions are significant, as the authors are the first to obtain logarithmic regret in the case of constrained contextual bandits.

However, the practical aspects of the research are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are provided. It would be beneficial to include more details on the practical implications of the algorithms and to provide experimental results to validate the theoretical findings.

In terms of the quality of the paper, it is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their work. The writing is clear, and the paper is easy to follow.

Regarding originality, the paper presents a novel combination of familiar techniques, specifically the use of ALP and UCB to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits. The related work is adequately referenced, and the authors clearly explain how their work differs from previous contributions.

In terms of significance, the results are important, and other researchers or practitioners are likely to use these ideas or build upon them. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

Overall, this is a solid theoretical paper that makes significant contributions to the field of contextual bandits. While the practical aspects could be discussed in more detail, the theoretical contributions are substantial, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
