- Studies contextual bandits with both budget and time (horizon) constraints.
- Assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed action costs, and a single budget constraint—simplifying but realistic.
- These assumptions allow algorithms with O(log T) regret via an adaptive linear programming approximation to the oracle.
- Manuscript is clearly written, well organized, and properly cited with a clear motivation.
- Makes a solid theoretical contribution and appears to be the first to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Proofs are persuasive, although the reviewer did not verify every detail.
- Practical considerations are treated only briefly (Sections 5 and 6) and there are no empirical results.
- Recommendation: expand discussion of practical implications to further strengthen the paper.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with sensible simplifying assumptions.
