- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits; shows O(log T) regret is achievable for finite context/action spaces.
- Proposes the Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm combined with UCB (UCB-ALP) to attain the logarithmic regret, with O(√T) regret in certain boundary cases.
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and situates the work well within related literature.
- The result’s novelty is limited: connection to finite-armed bandits with action constraints makes the O(log T) bound somewhat predictable.
- Authors address contextual coupling challenges, but the work doesn’t convincingly show a major departure from constrained finite-armed bandit techniques.
- Theorem 2’s O(√T) regret in case 2 may be loose; authors should revisit and consider dependencies on K or J·K.
- Suggest exploring problem-dependent regret bounds expressed in terms of arm gaps for tighter analysis.
- Recommend investigating concentration bounds on gaps as an avenue to improve regret bounds.
- Paper lacks lower bounds; even trivial lower bounds from classical bandits would strengthen theoretical claims and optimality arguments.
- Strength: relevant problem, clear algorithm (UCB-ALP), comprehensive related work, and positive theoretical result (logarithmic regret).
- Weakness: incremental novelty, potentially improvable regret bounds, absence of gap-based bounds, and missing lower bounds.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically correct solution, reasonable algorithm, thorough analysis, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, potential for improved regret bounds, and insufficient theoretical grounding (no lower bounds).
- Overall assessment: Neutral — solid and well-executed but incremental; addressing tighter regret analyses and lower bounds could make the contribution more compelling.
