- Main contribution: shows log(T) regret is achievable for budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandits when number of contexts J and actions K is finite and T is large.

- Results are thorough and proofs precise; writing quality is good.

- Reviewer finds the main result unsurprising: with finite J and K the problem reduces to at most JK arms, so log(T) regret follows from known action-constrained bandit results.

- Concern about originality: author should clarify novelty relative to treating the problem as a finite JK-arm bandit with restricted accessibility.

- Theorem 2 question: regret stated as O(√T + ...) — reviewer asks whether it should be O(√(K T) + ...) at least.

- Theorem 2 structure: regret given as O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T); reviewer suggests more refined, problem-dependent bounds (sums of inverse gaps) may be possible and asks if current UCB-ordering proof approach is optimal.

- Request for lower bounds: reviewer asks for problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds beyond trivial reductions (e.g., cost=0, fixed context), possibly incorporating context complexity.

- Suggestion: provide more intuitive explanations, visualizations, and examples to illustrate algorithms and results.

- Suggestion: expand discussion of implications, applications, and practical significance of the algorithms.

- Overall stance: contribution is valuable and well-presented but originality doubts leave the reviewer neutral; asks for clarifications in rebuttal.

- Arguments for acceptance: significant effort on constrained contextual bandits, clear writing, and potentially wide applicability.

- Arguments for rejection: unclear originality, need for deeper insights and additional discussions (bounds, lower bounds, and interpretations).
