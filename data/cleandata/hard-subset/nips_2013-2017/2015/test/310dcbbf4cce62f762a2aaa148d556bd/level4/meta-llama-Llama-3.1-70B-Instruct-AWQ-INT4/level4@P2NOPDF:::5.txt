This paper addresses a relatively simple budget-constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The primary contribution of the paper lies in the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm, achieved through linear programming (LP) approximation, and the logarithmic regret bound it achieves.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read. The authors have done an excellent job in presenting their ideas clearly.

One potential direction for future work is to consider using a parametric model for the reward function. This approach would be particularly useful when dealing with a large number of contexts, as it would provide a more efficient way of modeling the reward structure.

In Section 4.1, the authors propose combining Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) with their Approximate Linear Program (ALP) algorithm. To improve clarity, it would be beneficial to provide a more detailed explanation of the rationale behind this combination. Specifically, it appears that the authors are following the principle of "optimism in the face of uncertainty," which is similar to the policy update in UCRL2. A more explicit discussion of this connection would strengthen the paper.

Including numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would also be valuable, as it would provide further insight into the performance of the proposed algorithm.

Readers may also find the following paper relevant: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374. Overall, this paper merits acceptance due to its contribution of an efficient algorithm for constrained CB with optimal regret in most cases. The work is interesting and represents an improvement over existing research in the field.
