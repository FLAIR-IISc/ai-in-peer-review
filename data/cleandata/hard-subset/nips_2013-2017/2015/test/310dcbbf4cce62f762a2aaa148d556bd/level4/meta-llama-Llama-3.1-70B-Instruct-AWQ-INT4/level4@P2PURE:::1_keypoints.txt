- Reviewer still views the setting as essentially a standard bandit, arguing that for large T the context coupling vanishes and each context occurrence could be treated as a separate bandit instance.  
- Requests a clean problem-dependent upper bound expressed in terms of arm gaps, and ideally matching lower bounds for both problem-dependent and problem-independent cases; current supplementary bounds need reworking.  
- Summarizes the paper’s main contribution: proving log(T) regret is achievable for finite contexts/actions and sufficiently large T in a budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandit.  
- Notes results are thorough and writing is precise but not surprising, since finite J and K imply a JK-arm action-constrained bandit where log(T) regret is expected for large T.  
- Questions the paper’s novelty, suggesting the setting is very close to a budgeted bandit with action constraints and asking for clearer justification of originality.  
- Theorem 2 concern: case 2) states O(sqrt(T)+...), but the reviewer expects at least O(sqrt(KT)+...).  
- Asks whether problem-dependent bounds can be refined to sums of inverse arm gaps rather than the current UCB-ordering event approach, and whether the current approach is optimal.  
- Emphasizes the need for lower bounds tailored to the contextual complexity (not just trivial reductions to classical zero-cost constant-context bandits).  
- Concludes unconvinced about originality and remains neutral on the paper.
