

The paper addresses constrained contextual bandits, where the agent faces both budget and time constraints while making decisions based on observed contexts. The authors focus on a simplified but practically relevant setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. They propose a novel Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) approach to approximate the oracle policy, enabling them to achieve logarithmic regret (O(log T)) in most cases, except for certain boundary cases where the regret is O(√T).

This work builds upon prior research in contextual bandits [1-7] and budget-constrained bandits [8-16], but makes a significant contribution by addressing the constrained contextual bandit problem, which has remained largely unexplored despite its practical importance in applications like clinical trials and online recommendation systems. The authors clarify that while Resourceful Contextual Bandits (RCB) [17] has been studied, it focuses on more general settings with O(√T) regret and restricted benchmarks, whereas their work provides a more natural regret definition and achieves logarithmic regret under simplified assumptions.

The paper's primary strength lies in its elegant theoretical contribution: the ALP approximation provides a near-optimal solution with O(1) regret in non-boundary cases, and the UCB-ALP algorithm cleverly combines ALP with upper-confidence-bound methods to achieve logarithmic regret when expected rewards are unknown. The analysis is thorough, with careful consideration of boundary cases and a clear explanation of the challenges in the constrained setting. The paper is well-written, with precise definitions, good organization, and comprehensive literature review that properly situates their work within the broader bandit literature.

The main limitation is the lack of empirical validation. While the theoretical results are strong, the paper would benefit from experimental results demonstrating the practical viability of the proposed algorithms. The discussion of practical aspects in Sections 5-6 remains brief, and the connection to real-world applications could be strengthened with more concrete examples beyond the general descriptions provided. Additionally, the authors could better address the two recent papers [21, 22] that extend previous work to dynamic contexts.

The paper makes a significant theoretical contribution by being the first to demonstrate how logarithmic regret can be achieved in constrained contextual bandits under reasonable assumptions. The ALP approximation provides a valuable framework for addressing the complex trade-offs between exploration and exploitation in constrained settings. While the simplifying assumptions are necessary for the theoretical results, they are well-justified through practical examples like clinical trials and wireless networks.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel contribution: First paper to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.
- Theoretical significance: Well-motivated, rigorous analysis with clear results.
- Practical relevance: Assumptions are reasonable for many real-world applications.
- Quality of writing: Clear, well-organized, and well-documented.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Lack of empirical validation to support theoretical claims.
- Limited discussion of practical implementation challenges.
- Simplifying assumptions might limit applicability to more complex real-world scenarios.

Overall, this is a strong theoretical contribution that advances the state of the art in constrained bandit algorithms. The paper's main limitation is the absence of experimental validation, but given the focus on theoretical results and the fact that this is a foundational paper in an emerging area, this is not a major weakness. The work is well-suited for NIPS as it makes a significant theoretical contribution to the field of bandit algorithms, providing both a novel approach to constrained contextual bandits and valuable insights into the relationship between exploration and exploitation under budget constraints. The paper's clear exposition and rigorous analysis make it a valuable addition to the literature.
