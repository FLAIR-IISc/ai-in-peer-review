- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits; proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) + UCB to achieve improved regret.  
- Builds on Resourceful Contextual Bandits [17] which had O(√T) regret and high computational cost.  
- Contribution 1: ALP gives a near-optimal LP-based oracle approximation with O(1) regret when statistics are known.  
- Contribution 2: UCB-ALP (ALP + UCB) achieves O(log T) regret for most parameter settings.  
- Contribution 3: Extends approach to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous regret analysis that accounts for action/context ranking errors and budget fluctuations.  
- Novel proof techniques for concentration under adaptive algorithms represent a methodological contribution.  
- Clarity: Well-written, precise, and organized; supplementary material provides necessary technical depth.  
- Key insight: ALP tolerates certain estimation errors while maintaining near-optimal performance.  
- Originality: First to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions; ALP differs from prior LP approaches.  
- Significance: Substantial practical relevance (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing) and advances state of the art.  
- Weakness 1: Boundary cases where regret rises to O(√T) need more detailed discussion.  
- Weakness 2: Connection to existing optimism-based algorithms like UCRL2 could be clarified.  
- Weakness 3: Lacks empirical validation; even limited simulations would strengthen the paper.  
- Related work: Effectively positions contributions relative to [17, 20, 21] and distinguishes improvements.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — computationally efficient, near-optimal regret, and significant theoretical advance.
