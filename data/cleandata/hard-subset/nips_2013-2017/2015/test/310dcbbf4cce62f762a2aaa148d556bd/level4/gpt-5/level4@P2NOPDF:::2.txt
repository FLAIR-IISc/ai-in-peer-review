This paper studies contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints. The analysis focuses on finitely many discrete contexts, fixed per-action costs, and a single budget constraint. While simplified, these assumptions are reasonable for practical settings. The main contribution is an algorithmic framework that attains O(log T) regret by employing an adaptive linear-programming approximation to the oracle policy.

The paper is clearly written, well organized, and cites relevant prior work; the motivation is explicit. It offers solid theoretical contributions. However, practical considerations are treated only briefly in Sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are presented. Despite this, the results are significant: to the best of my knowledge, this is the first work to achieve logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits. The proofs appear convincing, though I did not verify every detail.

Overall, this is a strong theoretical paper. The simplifying assumptions are appropriate for obtaining O(log T) regret algorithms, but the practical aspects could be discussed in greater depth.
