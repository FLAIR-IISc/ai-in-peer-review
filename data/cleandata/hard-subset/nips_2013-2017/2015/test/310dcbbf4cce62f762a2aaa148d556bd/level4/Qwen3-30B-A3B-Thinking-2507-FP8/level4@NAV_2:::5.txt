

This paper investigates constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, where the agent must maximize expected total reward subject to both a budget and time horizon. The authors introduce Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) as an approximation of the oracle solution, which achieves near-optimality with only the ordering of expected rewards. They then combine ALP with the UCB method to develop the UCB-ALP algorithm, which achieves logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases (where it achieves O(√T) regret). This work extends prior contextual bandit research [1-3] and addresses the open problem highlighted in Resourceful Contextual Bandits [17], which achieved only O(√T) regret with a more complex algorithm and restricted benchmark.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with rigorous regret analysis. The authors provide complete theoretical proofs for both ALP (achieving O(1) regret except for boundary cases) and UCB-ALP (achieving O(log T) regret except for boundary cases). The analysis carefully addresses the challenges of constrained contextual bandits, including the complex interactions between exploration and exploitation under budget constraints. The paper thoroughly evaluates both strengths and weaknesses of the approach.

Clarity: The paper is exceptionally well-written and logically structured. The exposition is clear, the notation is well-defined, and the key insights are well-explained. The authors provide sufficient detail for an expert reader to understand and reproduce the results. The connection between the ALP approximation and UCB is particularly well-articulated.

Originality: This represents a significant advancement in the field. To the best of my knowledge, this is the first work to demonstrate how to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits. Previous approaches either used non-contextual methods [11-16] (inapplicable to contextual settings) or achieved only O(√T) regret with complex algorithms [17]. The ALP approximation and its integration with UCB represent a novel theoretical approach that substantially advances the state of the art.

Significance: The results have high theoretical and practical significance. The algorithms directly address important real-world applications with budget constraints, such as clinical trials [10] and crowdsourcing [9]. The computational efficiency of UCB-ALP makes it practical for implementation, and the logarithmic regret bound represents a substantial improvement over previous approaches. This work provides a solid foundation for addressing the open problem of general constrained contextual bandits.

Strengths:
- First to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits
- Novel LP-based approximation (ALP) that achieves near-optimal performance
- Well-structured, clear exposition with strong theoretical foundation
- Practical computational efficiency of the proposed algorithms
- Clear connection to real-world applications with budget constraints

Weaknesses:
- The analysis focuses on unit-cost systems and fixed costs, though the paper addresses heterogeneous costs in Section 6
- Boundary cases where regret is O(√T) are not fully resolved (though this is acknowledged and a known challenge)
- The paper could provide more intuition about why the UCB-ALP combination works well (e.g., explicitly connecting to "optimism under uncertainty")

Arguments for acceptance:
- Solves a significant open problem in contextual bandits
- Provides rigorous theoretical results with clear practical implications
- Makes a substantial improvement over previous work (Resourceful Contextual Bandits [17])
- Well-written and technically sound contribution to the field

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited to specific assumptions (unit costs, fixed costs), though these are well-justified for many applications
- Boundary cases where regret is O(√T) represent a minor limitation

This paper merits acceptance as it provides a highly original, technically sound solution to an important open problem in contextual bandits. The results represent a clear advancement over previous work, particularly Resourceful Contextual Bandits [17], which achieved only O(√T) regret. The authors have carefully positioned their work within the literature and made a compelling case for the novelty and significance of their contribution. The paper is of high quality, well-written, and makes a meaningful contribution to the field.
