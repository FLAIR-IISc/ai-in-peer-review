- Problem setup: contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T, contexts drawn from distribution π, actions include skip (0) or k>0 with known costs c_{j,k}, aim to maximize reward under budget constraint.

- Main focus: special case with known π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, where authors propose an algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild condition.

- Extensions: algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.

- Novelty: prior work achieves O(√T) regret, so this paper may be first to obtain O(log T) regret (subject to the mild condition).

- Method: algorithm derived from an approximation for computing the optimal action sequence when parameters are known; approach is simple yet interesting.

- Presentation: paper is well-motivated and clearly written.

- Concern/request: desirable that O(log T) bound holds universally; otherwise proving an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases would strengthen the work.

- Technical concern: initially questioned correctness of Lemma 1 (condition (2) and average vs hard constraint), but rebuttal resolved this.

- Minor questions: is computing the optimal action sequence NP-hard? can results extend to unknown T? is assuming u^1>u^2>...>u^*_J w.l.o.g. valid when context distribution is unknown?

- Recommendation: strong accept.
