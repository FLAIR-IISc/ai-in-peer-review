- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints in a simplified practical setting (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget).  
- Introduces the Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that approximates the oracle and tolerates certain parameter estimation errors.  
- Proposes UCB-ALP (ALP + UCB) for unknown expected rewards, achieving O(log T) regret except boundary cases with O(√T) regret.  
- First work to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits, marking a significant theoretical advance.  
- Theoretical claims are well-supported and the analysis is technically sound.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and cites relevant literature.  
- Novel combination of familiar techniques (ALP and UCB) tailored to the constrained setting.  
- Results are likely to be influential and useful for further research or practice.  
- Practical implications are only briefly discussed (Sections 5 and 6) and need expansion.  
- No experimental results are provided to validate theoretical findings.  
- Some proofs were not checked in full detail by the reviewer.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on significant theoretical contributions despite limited practical evaluation.
