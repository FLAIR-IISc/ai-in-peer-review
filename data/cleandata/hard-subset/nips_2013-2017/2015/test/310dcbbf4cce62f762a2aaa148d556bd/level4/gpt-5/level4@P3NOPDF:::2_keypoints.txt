- Paper studies contextual bandits with time and single-budget constraints for finite discrete contexts and fixed per-action costs, proposing an adaptive LP approximation achieving O(log T) regret.
- Presentation is clear, motivation strong, and related work appropriately cited.
- Theoretical development appears solid and convincing, though not every proof was checked in full detail.
- Strength: clear problem formulation with reasonable assumptions that enable sharp theoretical guarantees.
- Strength: novel theoretical contribution—first to achieve logarithmic regret under these constrained contextual bandit assumptions.
- Strength: elegant adaptive LP–based approach linking oracle design to an implementable approximation while controlling regret.
- Strength: paper is well written and organized, making technical ideas accessible.
- Limitation: minimal practical validation—no experiments and only brief application discussion in Sections 5 and 6.
- Limitation: scope restricted to finite contexts, fixed costs, and single budget; extensions to multi-budget, stochastic/context-dependent costs, continuous contexts, and nonstationary or unknown horizons are not addressed.
- Limitation: computational aspects underexplored—no LP complexity analysis, update frequency, or practical speed-up strategies discussed.
- Limitation: unclear trade-offs between LP approximation tightness and regret/performance.
- Limitation: positioning relative to bandits-with-knapsacks literature could be sharpened, especially which assumptions enable O(log T) versus prior sublinear rates.
- Limitation: regret bounds lack explicit dependence on problem parameters (contexts, actions, gaps, budget, costs) and clarity on constant sizes.
- Limitation: algorithmic behavior near budget exhaustion is unclear—how exploration is scheduled, adaptive adjustments, and whether results are high-probability or in expectation.
- Suggestion: add small synthetic experiments showing empirical regret scaling, budget utilization, computational overhead, and robustness to assumption violations.
- Suggestion: expand discussion/sketches of extensions to multiple budgets, stochastic costs, and larger context spaces, including main technical obstacles and possible approaches.
- Suggestion: provide explicit comparisons with related constrained bandit frameworks to highlight assumption sets and regret contrasts.
- Suggestion: include a brief complexity analysis or implementation notes for LP updates (LP size, re-solve frequency, and practical accelerations).
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper with novel O(log T) result and clear writing; recommend acceptance contingent on adding empirical illustration and expanded discussion of computational aspects and generalizations.
