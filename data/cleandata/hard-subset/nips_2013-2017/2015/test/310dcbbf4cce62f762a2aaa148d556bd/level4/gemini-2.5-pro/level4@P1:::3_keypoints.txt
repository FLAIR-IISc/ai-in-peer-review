- Studies contextual bandits with a hard budget B and known horizon T; agent may skip contexts; objective is max cumulative reward under budget constraint.  
- Focuses on unit costs and known context distribution; proposes UCB-ALP achieving O(log T) regret for non-boundary cases and O(√T) for boundary cases.  
- Extends the approach to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Strength: significant and original — first to achieve O(log T) regret for constrained contextual bandits (previous works gave O(√T)).  
- Strength: strong technical contribution — replaces intractable dynamic programming with an Adaptive LP relaxation combined with UCB-style exploration.  
- Strength: paper is well-motivated and clearly written, building from known-statistics to unknown-statistics cases.  
- Main limitation: O(log T) regret is not universal; would be stronger if it held for all cases or if an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases were provided.  
- Key technical concern: Lemma 1 uses an LP with an average budget constraint to upper-bound the oracle, but the justification for why this relaxations valid under a hard budget is unclear.  
- Minor question: Is computing the exact optimal policy (oracle) known to be computationally intractive (e.g., NP-hard)?  
- Minor question: Can the methods be generalized to unknown time horizon T?  
- Minor question: The analysis assumes u*_1 > u*_2 > ... > u*_J; can this ordering be assumed without loss of generality when rewards and context distribution are unknown?  
- Overall recommendation: novel and technically sound work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
