- Studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints under finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget.  
- Introduces an adaptive linear programming (ALP) approximation to the oracle policy.  
- Relaxes the hard budget constraint to an adaptive average budget constraint.  
- Combines ALP with UCB when rewards are unknown.  
- Proves O(log T) regret for the proposed algorithms.  
- Claims to be the first work obtaining logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and properly motivated with appropriate related work.  
- Simplifying assumptions are reasonable for applications like clinical trials and crowdsourcing.  
- Proofs appear convincing, though not exhaustively verified by the reviewer.  
- Practical aspects are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6.  
- No experimental results or empirical evaluation are provided.  
- Recommendation: solid theoretical contribution; should expand discussion of implementation and practical trade-offs.
