- Persistent concern that the setting is nearly equivalent to an action-constrained bandit with J·K arms, so context-action pairs might be treated as separate arms for large T.  
- Paper studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints and claims O(log T) regret for finite J and K—a novel claim in this setting.  
- Technical analysis is thorough and writing is precise.  
- Result is unsurprising to the reviewer because finite J and K can be reduced to a time-constrained, budgeted bandit with J·K arms, where logarithmic regret for large T is expected.  
- The fundamental novelty of the problem setting is questioned and needs clearer justification.  
- Question 1: Theorem 2’s sqrt(T) term should perhaps scale with the number of arms (e.g., O(sqrt(KT))).  
- Question 2: Request for a more granular, gap-dependent regret bound (sum of inverse gaps); current UCB-ordering proof may not be optimal.  
- Question 3: Stronger contribution would include lower bounds (problem-dependent and problem-independent), ideally reflecting context-structure complexity rather than trivial single-context bounds.  
- Supplementary material’s problem-dependent bound appears to need revision.  
- Overall recommendation: neutral stance due to concerns about originality and missing/insufficient bounds.
