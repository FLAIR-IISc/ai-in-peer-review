This paper examines contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, focusing on finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Although these assumptions simplify the setting, they remain reasonable in practical scenarios. Notably, the authors achieve O(log T) regret by introducing an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle, resulting in efficient algorithms.

The paper is well-written, clear, and thoroughly documented, with relevant citations and explicit motivation. As a theoretical contribution, the paper is solid. However, the practical aspects are only briefly discussed in Sections 5 and 6, and unfortunately, no experimental results are provided. Nevertheless, the contributions are significant, as the authors appear to be the first to attain logarithmic regret in the context of constrained contextual bandits. The proofs are convincing, although I did not verify every detail. Overall, this is a good paper that makes suitable simplified assumptions to derive O(log T) regret algorithms, with the potential for more in-depth discussion of practical aspects.
