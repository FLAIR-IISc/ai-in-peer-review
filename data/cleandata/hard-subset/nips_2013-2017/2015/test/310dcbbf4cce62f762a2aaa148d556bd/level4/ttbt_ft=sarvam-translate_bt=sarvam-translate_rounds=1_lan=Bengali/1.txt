The authors consider a budgeted and time-bound contextual bandit problem. The main finding of their paper is that if the context and the number of actions are limited, and if the time constraint T is large enough (relative to the current problem), they prove that it is possible to achieve a regret of order log(T), which is a new result for contextual bandits.
The results seem thorough and the writing is precise. The results are interesting but not too surprising. In fact, since the number of context J and arm K are limited, this bandit problem can be viewed as a time-bound and budgeted bandit problem with arm K, which is activity-bound, because at each time, not all arm K are accessible (only the K arm related to the context). Since the number of arms is limited, if T is large enough, the regret is log(T), as is the case in activity-bound bandit problems. So I'm not sure how innovative this paper is - perhaps the authors will be able to clarify this in their refutation.
