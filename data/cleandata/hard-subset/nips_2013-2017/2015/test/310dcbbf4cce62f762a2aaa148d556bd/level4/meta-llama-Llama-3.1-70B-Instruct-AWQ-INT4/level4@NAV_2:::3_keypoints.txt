- Problem: contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T; contexts drawn from π; actions include skip (0); nonzero actions yield stochastic rewards and known positive costs; goal maximize cumulative reward without exceeding budget.

- Main result: algorithm with O(log T) regret for the special case π known and unit costs c_{j,k}=1 under a mild condition.

- Extension: algorithm generalized to unknown context distribution π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.

- Novelty: possibly first work achieving O(log T) regret for this setting (prior work [16] achieves O(√T)).

- Method: algorithm built on an approximation algorithm for the optimal action sequence when statistics are known; simple and easy to implement.

- Significance caveat: O(log T) guarantee holds only under a mild condition; boundary cases admit an Ω(√T) lower bound, so logarithmic regret may not always be attainable.

- Technical concern: initial doubt about Lemma 1 — whether the optimal action sequence satisfies equation (2) and whether the average constraint truly relaxes the hard constraint.

- Resolution: after re-reading, reviewer accepts Lemma 1 and commends the clear, concise proof.

- Clarity: paper is well motivated and very clearly written.

- Minor questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard? can results extend to unknown T? can we assume u^1>u^2>...>u^*_J w.l.o.g. in the unknown π case?

- Strengths: novel algorithm, simple implementation, clear proofs.

- Weaknesses: logarithmic regret only under a condition; lack of provided Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases.

- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
