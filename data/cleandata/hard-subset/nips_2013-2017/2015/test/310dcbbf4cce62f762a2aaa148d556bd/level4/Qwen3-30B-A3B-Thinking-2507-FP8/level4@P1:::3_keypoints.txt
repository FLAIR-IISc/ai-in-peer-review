- Addresses constrained contextual bandits where each action has reward and cost and the agent must respect a hard budget.  
- Problem is motivated by practical applications like clinical trials and online recommendations with limited resources.  
- Main contribution: achieves logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Introduces adaptive linear programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle, yielding O(1) regret in non-boundary cases.  
- Combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to handle unknown expected rewards, achieving O(log T) regret except in boundary cases.  
- Boundary cases incur O(√T) regret but are characterized as rare (zero Lebesgue measure) and thoroughly analyzed.  
- Correctly compares to prior work (e.g., Resourceful Contextual Bandits and related extensions) and highlights differences in benchmarks and regret bounds.  
- Key technical insight: ALP requires only the ordering of expected rewards, enabling effective combination with UCB.  
- Extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs are discussed, though technically more challenging.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with rigorous proofs and sufficient detail for reproduction; supplementary materials are comprehensive.  
- High significance and practical impact; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
