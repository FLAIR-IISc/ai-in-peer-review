This paper presents an intriguing analysis of contextual bandits against a stronger baseline that utilizes adaptive linear programming to strategically pass on a certain percentage of suboptimal rounds, referred to as the "budget." The setup is interesting, and the proposed solutions are unique.

However, upon closer examination, one aspect of the analysis warrants further clarification. Specifically, it is unclear why boundary cases require distinct treatment. The choice of rho = q_j, particularly for j > 1, does not appear to hold any special significance. A clear explanation for this design choice would strengthen the paper's overall argument.

The authors' decision to compare their approach against a more robust baseline is commendable, as it provides a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's effectiveness. The incorporation of adaptive linear programming to optimize the budget allocation adds a layer of sophistication to the analysis. Overall, the paper offers a fresh perspective on contextual bandits, and its contributions have the potential to impact the field positively.

To further enhance the paper's clarity and impact, it would be beneficial to address the aforementioned concern regarding the boundary cases. Providing a concise explanation for the distinct treatment of these cases would not only alleviate potential confusion but also reinforce the paper's key findings.

In conclusion, the paper demonstrates a promising approach to contextual bandits, and its unique setup and solutions make it a valuable contribution to the field. With some minor revisions to address the identified concern, the paper has the potential to make an even more significant impact.
