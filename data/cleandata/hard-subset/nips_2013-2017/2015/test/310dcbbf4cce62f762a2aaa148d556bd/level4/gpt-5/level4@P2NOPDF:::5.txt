This paper studies a budget-constrained contextual bandit with discrete contexts and actions. Its main contributions are an efficient algorithm based on an LP approximation and a regret bound of order log(T). The paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

Strengths:
- Computational efficiency via an LP-based approach.
- Strong theoretical guarantees, including logarithmic regret in time.
- Clear presentation and organization.

Suggestions for improvement and future work:
- Consider a parametric reward model to improve scalability when the number of contexts is large.
- In Section 4.1, clarify the rationale behind combining UCB with ALP. It appears to follow the optimism-in-the-face-of-uncertainty principle and may be closely related to the policy update mechanism in UCRL2; making this connection explicit would aid understanding.
- Include numerical experiments (e.g., in an extended version) to validate the practical performance and compare against baselines.
- You may want to cite: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374

Overall assessment: The paper merits acceptance. It provides an efficient algorithm for budget-constrained contextual bandits with near-optimal regret in most regimes and represents a meaningful improvement over existing work.
