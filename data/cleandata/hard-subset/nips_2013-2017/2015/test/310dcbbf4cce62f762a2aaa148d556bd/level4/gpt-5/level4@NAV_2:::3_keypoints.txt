- Problem setup: contextual bandit with hard budget B and horizon T, actions include skip (no cost/reward) and context–action–specific stochastic rewards and known costs, objective maximize cumulative reward subject to budget constraint.  
- Main result: for unit-cost and known context distribution, propose an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild non-boundary condition.  
- Extensions: method extended to unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs.  
- Novelty/impact: appears to be first work to obtain O(log T) regret for constrained contextual bandits (outside boundary cases), improving on prior O(√T) results.  
- Technique: builds on an adaptive linear programming (ALP) relaxation approximating the oracle policy when statistics are known; resulting algorithm is simple and compelling.  
- Writing and motivation: paper is well motivated and very clearly written.  
- Limitation/desired strengthening: would be stronger if O(log T) regret could be shown without exceptions or if an Ω(√T) lower bound were given for boundary regimes.  
- Technical concern resolved: reviewer was initially unconvinced by Lemma 1 (average-budget LP relaxing hard budget), but was convinced after the authors’ rebuttal clarification.  
- Minor questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard? can results extend to unknown T (anytime)? can one assume u*_1 > u*_2 > … > u*_J w.l.o.g. when context distribution is unknown?  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
