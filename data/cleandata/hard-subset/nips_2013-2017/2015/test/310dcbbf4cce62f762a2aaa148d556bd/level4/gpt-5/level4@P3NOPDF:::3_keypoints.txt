- Problem and focus: Studies contextual bandits with a hard budget B and horizon T, focusing on unit-cost/known-π where a simple algorithm achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition, and extends to unknown π and arbitrary costs.  
- Novelty: Potentially the first O(log T) regret result in the budgeted contextual bandit setting versus prior O(√T) results (e.g., [16]).  
- Strengths: Clear formulation and motivation, simple algorithm with strong regret guarantee in the special case, broader extensions, and clear writing.  
- Main caveat: The O(log T) guarantee relies on a “mild condition”; stronger contribution would come from removing it or proving Ω(√T) lower bounds for boundary cases.  
- Dependence on Lemma 1: Many arguments hinge on Lemma 1; include a brief, self-contained proof sketch or intuition in the main text.  
- Oracle/approximation concerns: Need to state computational complexity and approximation guarantees of the oracle routine and how these propagate into regret bounds.  
- Question on hardness: Is computing the exact optimal action sequence under the hard budget NP-hard? If so, cite results; if not, clarify complexity and necessity of approximation.  
- Unknown horizon: Can the algorithm and guarantees be adapted to unknown T (e.g., via doubling or anytime bounds), and what are the extra constants/overhead?  
- Context ordering: If indexing contexts by decreasing utility is used, clarify whether this can be assumed when π is unknown and how to maintain/update ordering without bias.  
- Mild-condition clarity: Explicitly state the condition in the main theorem, interpret it (e.g., margin/separability), discuss frequency in practice, and give examples where it fails.  
- Arbitrary-cost sensitivity: In the general case, clarify how regret constants scale with cost range/heterogeneity and whether large cost variation causes sensitivity issues.  
- Presentation suggestions: Add Lemma 1 intuition in main text, summarize oracle approximation guarantee and its impact on regret, and provide a concise comparison with [16] highlighting applicable regimes.  
- Overall evaluation: Well-motivated, clear, and meaningful contribution with notable progress; recommended for acceptance despite the noted clarifications and caveats.
