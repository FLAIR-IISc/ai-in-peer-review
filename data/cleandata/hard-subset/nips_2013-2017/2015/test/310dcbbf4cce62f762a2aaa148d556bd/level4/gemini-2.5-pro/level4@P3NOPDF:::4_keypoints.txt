- Introduces a contextual bandit framework with a budget that lets the algorithm skip rounds.  
- Proposes a novel algorithm using adaptive linear programming to manage the budget.  
- Evaluates performance against a stronger, more practical adaptive baseline than usual.  
- Problem formulation is interesting, well-motivated, and addresses realistic costs of committing to actions.  
- The solution is unique, technically sound, and moves beyond standard regret frameworks.  
- Analysis against an adaptive baseline is a valuable contribution to the bandits literature.  
- Major question: unclear why boundary cases are treated separately—specifically why ρ = q_j needs different analysis for j > 1.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, valuable contribution, acceptable pending clarification on the technical distinction.
