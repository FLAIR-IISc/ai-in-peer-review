- Studies budget-constrained contextual bandits with discrete contexts/actions; main contributions are computational efficiency via LP approximation and a log(T) regret bound.  
- Paper is well written, clear, and concise.  
- Suggest future work: use parametric reward models to handle large numbers of contexts and reduce dimensionality.  
- In Section 4.1, clarify UCB-ALP rationale—explicitly state it uses optimism in the face of uncertainty, give intuition, and note equivalence to UCRL2 policy updates.  
- Recommend adding numerical experiments in an extended version to compare empirical performance with other methods.  
- Suggest citing Agrawal et al., "Contextual Bandits with Global Constraints and Objective" (arXiv:1506.03374).  
- Overall recommendation: accept—provides an efficient algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with optimal regret in most cases and is a significant, well-presented improvement.
