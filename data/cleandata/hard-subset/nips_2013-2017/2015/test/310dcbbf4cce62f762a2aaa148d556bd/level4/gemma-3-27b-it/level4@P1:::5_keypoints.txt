- Main contribution: computationally efficient algorithm for budget-constrained contextual bandits using LP approximation with logarithmic regret.
- Problem setting: contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, discrete contexts and actions.
- Algorithmic approach: ALP (Adaptive Linear Programming) for known statistics and UCB-ALP combining ALP with UCB for unknown rewards.
- Theoretical result: provable logarithmic regret in most cases, sublinear regret in boundary cases.
- Computational efficiency: LP approximation yields practical computational gains.
- Clarity: paper is very well written and easy to follow.
- Novelty: novel combination of ALP and UCB for constrained contextual bandits.
- Extensions: analysis covers unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and practical relevance to real-world applications.
- Weakness: simplifying assumptions (discrete contexts/actions) limit applicability to complex settings.
- Weakness: boundary cases where only sublinear (not logarithmic) regret is achieved.
- Weakness: lack of empirical validation / numerical experiments to demonstrate practical performance.
- Weakness: analysis remains focused on relatively simple scenarios despite some extensions.
- Suggestions: explore parametric reward models for large context spaces in future work.
- Suggestions: clarify rationale for combining UCB with ALP (e.g., optimism/UCRL2-like policy update).
- Suggestions: include numerical experiments in an extended version to strengthen results.
- Related work: reviewer recommends considering arXiv:1506.03374.
- Recommendation: paper merits acceptance; reviewer recommends acceptance.
