- Argues that with large horizon the coupling across contexts vanishes, so each context occurrence might be treatable as a separate bandit instance — questions why this reduction isn’t valid.  
- Requests a clean problem-dependent upper bound in terms of arm gaps (supplementary bound needs reworking) and ideally matching lower bounds in both problem-dependent and problem-independent regimes.  
- Notes the main result (log(T) regret for finite J and K when T large) appears new for contextual bandits and the paper is thorough and precise.  
- Finds the results unsurprising given a reduction to a budgeted bandit with JK arms and an action constraint; questions the conceptual novelty and asks for clarification on fundamental differences from that reduction.  
- Questions Theorem 2, case (2): the stated O(sqrt(T)+...) regret may be missing a dependence on K (e.g., O(sqrt(KT)+...)).  
- Suggests deriving finer, gap-dependent bounds (sums of inverse gaps) instead of coarse KJ log T bounds; questions whether the current UCB-ordering proof is optimal.  
- Calls for lower bounds tailored to the multi-context setting (beyond trivial classical-bandit lower bounds) to reflect added complexity from multiple contexts.  
- Asks authors to explicitly state any inherent obstacles preventing treating each context occurrence as an independent bandit instance.  
- Overall verdict: well-written and solid guarantees but originality is uncertain; would be improved by polished gap-dependent upper bounds, matching lower bounds, and clearer explanation of contextual coupling — reviewer remains neutral.
