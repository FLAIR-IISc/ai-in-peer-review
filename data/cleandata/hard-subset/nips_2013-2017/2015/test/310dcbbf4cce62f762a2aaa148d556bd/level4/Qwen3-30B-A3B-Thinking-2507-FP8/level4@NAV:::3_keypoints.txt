- Problem: contextual bandit with budget B and horizon T, contexts j∼π, actions k∈{0,1..K} with skip action 0, rewards in [0,1], and known costs c_{j,k}; objective maximize cumulative reward subject to cost ≤ B.  
- Main contribution: for π known and unit costs c_{j,k}=1, an algorithm achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extension: the approach generalizes to unknown π and arbitrary costs.  
- Novelty: appears to be the first work achieving O(log T) regret (when the mild condition holds), improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Methodology: leverages a simple, insightful approximation for optimal action sequences under known statistics.  
- Caveat/significance: impact would be stronger if the O(log T) bound held universally and if a matching Ω(√T) lower bound were shown for boundary cases.  
- Technical concern resolved: all results hinge on Lemma 1 (whether the optimal sequence always satisfies condition (2) and why the average constraint relaxes the hard constraint); the reviewer was convinced after rebuttal.  
- Minor questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard; can results handle unknown T; can one assume u^1>u^2>…>u^*_J WLOG in the unknown-π case?  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
