

This paper addresses a budget-constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The primary contributions are the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm (achieved via linear programming approximation) and its logarithmic regret bound. The paper is well-written and clearly presented.  

Key suggestions for improvement:  
1. In future work, consider adopting a parametric reward model, which would be particularly valuable for large context spaces.  
2. In Section 4.1, clarify the rationale for combining UCB with ALP in the UCB-ALP algorithm. Specifically, the approach appears to follow optimism under uncertainty, and this may be equivalent to the policy update mechanism in UCRL2—elaborating this connection would strengthen the presentation.  
3. Including numerical experiments in an extended version would further validate the practical effectiveness of the method.  

We note the related work: [http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374].  

This work merits acceptance. It provides an efficient algorithm for constrained CB problems with near-optimal regret in standard settings, offering a meaningful improvement over prior approaches. The technical contributions are sound, and the clarity of the exposition enhances the paper’s impact.
