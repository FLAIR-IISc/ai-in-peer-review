- Problem: contextual bandits with both budget and time constraints, complicating exploration–exploitation.
- Main contribution: UCB-ALP algorithm that achieves logarithmic regret (except boundary cases) using LP approximations for computational efficiency.
- Positioning: properly situated in contextual and constrained bandit literature, identifying a gap for budgeted contextual settings.
- LP/ALP approach: LP approximates the oracle; ALP is well-motivated and combined with UCB to handle unknown rewards.
- Theoretical rigor: rigorous analysis with clear theorems/lemmas and treatment of boundary cases, unknown context distributions, and heterogeneous costs.
- Novelty strength: first logarithmic regret bound for constrained contextual bandits.
- Computational efficiency strength: LP-based approach makes the algorithm practical to compute.
- Clarity strength: paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.
- Generalization strength: framework extended to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.
- Simplifying-assumptions weakness: discrete contexts and actions may limit real-world applicability.
- Boundary-case weakness: O(√T) regret in boundary cases; prevalence and mitigation are unclear.
- Empirical-evaluation weakness: no numerical experiments to validate practical performance.
- Related-work weakness: needs explicit comparison to concurrent works [21,22].
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, efficient algorithm, clear exposition, and useful extensions.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited applicability due to assumptions, lack of experiments, and suboptimal boundary-case regret.
- Overall assessment: reviewer recommends accept, citing valuable theoretical results despite concerns.
- Minor suggestions: compare explicitly to [21,22]; clarify UCB+ALP rationale (optimism/UCRL2); include experiments in future; discuss practical handling of boundary cases.
