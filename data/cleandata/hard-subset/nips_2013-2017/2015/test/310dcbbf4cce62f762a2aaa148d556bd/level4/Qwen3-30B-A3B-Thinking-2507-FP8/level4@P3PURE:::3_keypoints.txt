- Problem: contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T; contexts j ~ π, actions include "skip" (k=0), costs c_{j,k}>0 known; objective maximize cumulative reward subject to cumulative cost ≤ B.

- Main result: for known π and unit costs (c_{j,k}=1), the proposed algorithm achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition.

- Extension: approach generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.

- Novelty: prior work [16] only attains O(√T) regret, so this may be the first O(log T) result, contingent on the mild condition.

- Caveat: significance depends on whether O(log T) holds universally; otherwise Ω(√T) lower bounds in boundary cases remain unaddressed.

- Method: leverages an approximation for computing the optimal action sequence; algorithm is simple and conceptually interesting.

- Technical point: all results rely on Lemma 1; reviewer initially questioned it but accepted it after the authors' rebuttal.

- Open/minor questions: is finding the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard?

- Open/minor question: can the results be extended to unknown T?

- Open/minor question: is the assumption u^1 > u^2 > … > u^*_J WLOG even when π is unknown?

- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
