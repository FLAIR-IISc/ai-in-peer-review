- Problem: Contextual bandits with time horizon T and hard budget B, actions can be skipped, rewards in [0,1], costs >0; objective maximize expected cumulative reward under budget.  
- Contribution (unit-cost, known π): Proposes ALP using an adaptive LP with average remaining budget b_τ/τ; achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) in boundary cases.  
- Contribution (unknown rewards): Proposes UCB-ALP combining ALP with UCB estimates; gets O(JK log T) regret non-boundary and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.  
- Extensions: Extends approach to unknown context distributions via empirical π and to heterogeneous costs using an ε-First ALP variant.  
- Prior work relation: Prior constrained bandit work typically achieved O(√T) or focused on finite policy classes; this paper appears first to obtain O(log T) (instance-dependent) regret for constrained contextual bandits under non-boundary conditions.  
- Strength — clarity: ALP is simple, intuitive, and computationally efficient by tracking average remaining budget.  
- Strength — theoretical guarantees: Proves O(1) regret for ALP (known rewards) and O(JK log T) for UCB-ALP with careful boundary analysis.  
- Strength — technical novelty: Connects budget dynamics to sampling-without-replacement concentration and decomposes regret into budget fluctuation and ranking-error components.  
- Strength — practical relevance: Setting and algorithms motivated by realistic budgeted applications (e.g., clinical trials, crowdsourcing).  
- Weakness — boundary cases: Regret is O(√T) at boundary values of ρ; need either improved algorithms (O(log T)) or a matching Ω(√T) lower bound.  
- Weakness — ordering assumption: Analysis assumes strict ordering u*_1 > … > u*_J; need clarification on enforcing this without loss of generality, especially when π is unknown/estimated.  
- Weakness — oracle tractability: Paper argues DP is prohibitive but lacks formal NP-hardness or references to justify the approximation approach.  
- Weakness — unknown horizon: Unclear whether results extend to unknown T (anytime/doubling) without degrading regret.  
- Quality: Technical soundness appears strong; Lemma 1 (average-budget LP upper bound) is critical and should have a brief intuitive explanation in the main text.  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized and readable; include a short proof sketch or intuition for Lemma 1 to aid readers.  
- Originality: Adaptive LP relaxation to decouple decision-making from learning is novel and yields new O(log T) guarantees (non-boundary).  
- Significance: Advances state of the art for constrained contextual bandits and may inform broader resource-constrained learning.  
- Pros for acceptance: First O(log T) guarantees in this setting; simple, efficient algorithms with rigorous analysis; extensions to unknown π and heterogeneous costs.  
- Cons for acceptance: O(√T) boundary cases without matching lower bound; assumptions on strict ordering need more discussion; lack of formal hardness results for the oracle.  
- Minor comments: Clarify computational complexity or NP-hardness of the exact oracle (DP); discuss making algorithms horizon-free; clarify tie-breaking/ordering when π is unknown.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
