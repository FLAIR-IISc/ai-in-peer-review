- Studies contextual bandits with both budget and time limits.
- Analysis restricted to finite discrete actions, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint (simplifying but practically relevant).
- Main contribution: algorithms achieving O(log T) regret via an adaptive linear programming approximation of the optimal policy.
- Presentation is clear, well-documented, and properly cited with a clear motivation.
- Strong theoretical contribution and significant advancement in the area.
- Claims to be the first work to show logarithmic regret bounds for constrained contextual bandits (to reviewer’s knowledge).
- Proofs appear sound, though full verification was not performed by the reviewer.
- Paper lacks empirical validation despite sections on practical considerations.
- Recommendation: include further discussion of practical implications.
