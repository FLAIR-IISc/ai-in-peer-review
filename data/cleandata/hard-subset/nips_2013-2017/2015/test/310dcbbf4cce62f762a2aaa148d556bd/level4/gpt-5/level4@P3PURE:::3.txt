This paper studies a contextual bandit problem with a budget constraint B and a known time horizon T. In each round, the agent observes a context j drawn i.i.d. from a fixed distribution pi over {1, ..., J} and selects an action k from {0} ∪ {1, ..., K}, where k = 0 denotes “skip.” If k = 0, the agent receives no reward and incurs no cost. If k > 0, the agent receives a reward y drawn from a fixed distribution over [0,1] that depends on (j, k), and incurs a known positive cost c(j,k). The objective is to maximize cumulative reward subject to the hard constraint that cumulative cost never exceeds the budget B.

The main technical focus is a special case where pi is known and c(j,k) = 1 for all j and k. For this case, the paper proposes a simple and interesting algorithm with regret O(log T) under a mild condition. The approach is extended to the general case where pi is unknown and the costs c(j,k) are arbitrary.

Compared to prior work, a more general setting is considered in [16], but that paper provides an algorithm with regret O(sqrt T). Hence, this work appears to be the first to achieve O(log T) regret (under the stated mild condition). The algorithm builds on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when all statistics are known.

The paper is well motivated and clearly written. It would be even more compelling if O(log T) regret could be shown to hold without additional conditions, or, failing that, if an Omega(sqrt T) lower bound were established for the boundary cases where the condition does not hold.

A central technical point is Lemma 1. Initially, I was not convinced of its correctness. In particular, I did not believe the optimal action sequence necessarily satisfies condition (2) in every round, and therefore I did not see why the average constraint constitutes a relaxation of the hard budget constraint. After reading the rebuttal, I am now convinced of Lemma 1. Thank you for the clarification.

Minor comments and questions:
- Is it NP-hard to compute the exact optimal action sequence?
- Can the results be generalized to the setting where T is unknown?
- Can we assume, without loss of generality, u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J even when the context distribution is unknown?

I strongly recommend this paper for acceptance.
