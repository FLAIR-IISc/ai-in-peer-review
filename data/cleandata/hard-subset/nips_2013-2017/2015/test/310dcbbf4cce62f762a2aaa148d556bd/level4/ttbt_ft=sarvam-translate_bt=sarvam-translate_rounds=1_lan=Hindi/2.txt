(Light Review)

This research paper examines relevant bandits with budget and time constraints. The analysis is limited to discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint. Although the setting is simplified, these assumptions are still valid in practice. Importantly, it results in algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by introducing Oracle's adaptive linear programming approximation.

The research paper is clear and well-documented. Relevant tasks are cited, and the motivation is clear. It is a solid theoretical paper. Practical aspects are only briefly discussed in sections 5 and 6, and no experimental results are provided. Despite this, the contributions are significant: as far as I know, the authors are the first to achieve logarithmic regret in the case of constrained relevant bandits. The evidence is convincing (although I did not examine all of them in full detail). It is a good paper, making reasonable simplified assumptions to achieve an O(log T) regret algorithm. More detail could be provided on practical aspects.
