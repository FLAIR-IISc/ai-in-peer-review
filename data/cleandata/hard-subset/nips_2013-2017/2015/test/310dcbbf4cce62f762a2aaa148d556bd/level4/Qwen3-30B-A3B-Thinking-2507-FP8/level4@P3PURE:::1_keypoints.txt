- Reviewer questions why context coupling disappears for large horizons and why each context occurrence cannot be treated as an independent bandit instance.  
- Core claim (O(log T) regret for budgeted, time-constrained contextual bandits with finite J, K for large T) is valid.  
- Novelty concern: the problem reduces to a time-/action-constrained bandit with JK arms (K accessible per context), so the result may be less surprising.  
- Theorem 2 (Case 2) appears incorrect: the regret should be at least O(√(K T) + …), not O(√T + …).  
- Problem-dependent bounds are insufficiently refined: current O(KJ log T) or O(√T + KJ log T) should be expressed in gap-dependent form (e.g., sums of 1/Δ_i).  
- Proof reliance on UCB order events rather than gap-concentration may prevent achieving optimal problem-dependent bounds; a gap-based refinement is needed.  
- Missing matching lower bounds (both problem-dependent and independent) that incorporate context complexity; trivial classical lower bounds are inadequate.  
- Supplementary material’s upper bound needs reworking; without clean problem-dependent bounds and matching lower bounds, the technical contribution is limited.  
- Authors must clarify the novelty gap in rebuttal; reviewer remains neutral pending these clarifications.
