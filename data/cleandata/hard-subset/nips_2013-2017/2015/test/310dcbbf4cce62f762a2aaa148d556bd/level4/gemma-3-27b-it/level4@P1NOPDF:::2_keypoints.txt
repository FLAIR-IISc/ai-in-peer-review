- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.  
- Analysis assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint.  
- These simplifying assumptions are reasonable for many practical scenarios.  
- Assumptions enable development of algorithms achieving O(log T) regret.  
- Introduces an adaptive linear programming approximation of the optimal policy (the oracle).  
- Paper is clearly written and well-documented.  
- Authors appropriately cite related work and provide explicit motivation.  
- Solid theoretical contribution; appears to be first to obtain logarithmic regret guarantees for constrained contextual bandits.  
- Proofs appear convincing (not fully verified by reviewer).  
- Practical discussion is limited to Sections 5 and 6 and no experiments are presented.  
- A more detailed discussion of practical considerations would be beneficial.
