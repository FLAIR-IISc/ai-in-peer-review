

The paper presents an interesting setup for contextual bandits with a unique solution approach. However, I have one critical question regarding the analysis: why must boundary cases be treated separately, particularly when ρ = q_j for j > 1? This case does not appear special in the problem structure, and its distinct handling lacks clear justification. Additionally, the paper analyzes contextual bandits against a stronger baseline—a method employing adaptive linear programming to skip a certain percentage of suboptimal rounds (referred to as the "budget"). Despite this potentially complicating comparison, the work's framework and solution remain noteworthy.
