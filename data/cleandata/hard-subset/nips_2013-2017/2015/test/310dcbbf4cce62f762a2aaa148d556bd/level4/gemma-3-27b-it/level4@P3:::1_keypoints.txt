- Tackles constrained contextual bandits with budget and time limits and shows logarithmic regret for finite contexts/actions — a novel result for this setting.  
- Proposes ALP to approximate the oracle using only ordering of expected rewards, combined with UCB for unknown rewards to form UCB-ALP.  
- Analysis is rigorous and thorough, with clear algorithm presentation and theoretical foundations.  
- Extends method to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous action costs, increasing applicability.  
- Paper is well-written and logically organized.  
- Novelty concern: the connection to existing constrained-bandit work needs clearer articulation to show the significance of the contextual adaptation.  
- Regret bounds concern: Theorem 2’s O(√T) term in boundary cases appears potentially improvable; consider problem-dependent bounds using arm gaps.  
- Missing lower bounds: lack of lower bounds limits assessment of optimality; exploring lower bounds that incorporate context complexity is recommended.  
- Acceptance arguments: solid contribution, provable logarithmic regret, practical relevance, and computational efficiency justify acceptance.  
- Rejection arguments: perceived incremental novelty and unresolved regret/lower-bound issues could warrant rejection or stronger justification.  
- Revision suggestions: elaborate on novelty/related work, attempt refined/regret bounds, discuss lower-bound challenges and future work, and clarify supplementary proofs.
