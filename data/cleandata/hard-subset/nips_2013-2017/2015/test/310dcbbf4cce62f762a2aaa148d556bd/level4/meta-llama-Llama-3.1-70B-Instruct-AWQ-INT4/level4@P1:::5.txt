This paper considers a fairly simple budget-constrained contextual bandit (cb) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The main contribution is the computational efficiency of the algorithm (via LP approximation) and the log(T) regret bound it achieves.

The paper is very well written and easy to follow. The authors have done a great job in presenting their ideas and results in a clear and concise manner.

One potential direction for future work is to consider using a parametric model for the reward. This is particularly useful when the number of contexts is large, as it can help to reduce the dimensionality of the problem and improve the efficiency of the algorithm.

In Section 4.1, when combining UCB with ALP, the authors may want to provide more clarity on the rationale behind the UCB-ALP algorithm. Specifically, it appears that the algorithm is following the principle of "optimism in the face of uncertainty", which is a common approach in bandit problems. It may be helpful to explicitly state this and provide more intuition on why this approach is effective in this setting. Additionally, the authors may want to note that this approach is equivalent to the policy update in UCRL2.

It would also be great to include some numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper. This would help to provide further insight into the performance of the algorithm and how it compares to other existing methods.

Finally, the authors may want to consider citing the paper "Contextual Bandits with Global Constraints and Objective" by Agrawal et al. (arXiv:1506.03374), which appears to be relevant to the work presented in this paper.

Overall, this paper merits acceptance as it provides an efficient algorithm for constrained cb with optimal regret in most cases. It is an interesting and significant improvement over existing work, and the results are well presented and easy to follow.
