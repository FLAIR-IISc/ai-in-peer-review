This work presents an interesting setup and novel solutions for contextual bandit problems, evaluating performance against a strong baseline employing adaptive linear programming with a “budget” – a mechanism for strategically skipping suboptimal rounds. My primary question concerns the justification for treating boundary cases with distinct analysis; the role of ρ = q<sub>j</sub> doesn't appear fundamentally different, particularly for j > 1. Specifically, could the authors elaborate on the reason a separate analysis is required for these cases?
