- Introduces a novel contextual bandit framework incorporating both budget and time constraints, addressing complex temporal coupling in decisions.  
- Central idea: approximate the intractable oracle policy with a tractable adaptive linear program (LP) benchmark.  
- Proposes ALP for unit-cost, known-context-distribution setting; ALP adapts to average remaining budget.  
- ALP achieves near-optimal performance, with O(1) regret in most scenarios.  
- Extends to unknown expected rewards by combining ALP with UCB into UCB-ALP.  
- UCB-ALP attains logarithmic regret generally, with O(√T) regret only in specific “boundary cases.”  
- Analysis is further extended to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Technical strengths: LP approximation gives a clean analyzable benchmark; concentration analysis of remaining budget is key to regret bounds.  
- Presentation is clear and logical, building intuition via the simplified known-statistics case first.  
- Main concern: need clarification on the significance of boundary cases (ρ = q_j)—whether they are proof artifacts or indicate fundamental changes in problem structure/policy behavior.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original contribution with important theoretical results; addressing the boundary-case motivation would improve clarity and impact.
