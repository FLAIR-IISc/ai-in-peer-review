This paper addresses the constrained contextual bandit (CB) problem with discrete contexts and actions, focusing on computational efficiency achieved through a linear programming (LP) approximation. The primary contribution lies in the development of an algorithm that offers a log(T) regret bound, making it a valuable addition to the field.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly presented, making it easy to follow the proposed methodology and its theoretical underpinnings. The authors have done a commendable job in articulating the problem and their solution.

I have a few suggestions for potential future work. When dealing with a large number of contexts, employing a parametric model for the reward function could significantly improve scalability and performance. This approach could allow for generalization across contexts and reduce the computational burden associated with estimating rewards for each individual context. 

Specifically, in Section 4.1, the rationale for combining Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) with the Active Learning Policy (ALP) algorithm could benefit from further clarification. While the authors likely leverage the principle of optimism in the face of uncertainty, explicitly stating this connection – and noting its equivalence to the policy update mechanism in UCRL2 – would strengthen the explanation and enhance understanding for the reader. 

The inclusion of numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would be highly beneficial. These experiments would provide empirical validation of the theoretical results and demonstrate the algorithm’s performance in practical scenarios. They could also offer insights into the algorithm’s sensitivity to different parameters and dataset characteristics.

Furthermore, the authors may find the related work presented in http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374 relevant to their research. 

Overall, this paper presents a significant advancement in the area of constrained contextual bandits. The proposed algorithm is efficient and achieves near-optimal regret bounds in many practical settings. The work is both interesting and represents a clear improvement over existing approaches. Therefore, I recommend acceptance of this paper.
