- Problem setup: contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T, contexts from π, actions include skipping, rewards in [0,1], known costs c_{j,k}, objective maximize cumulative reward under budget.  
- Main contribution: for π known and c_{j,k}=1, presents an algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild condition.  
- Extensions: algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.  
- Novelty vs prior work: contrasts with [16], which achieves O(√T) regret; this work may be first to get O(log T) regret when the condition holds.  
- Algorithmic approach: based on an approximation algorithm for the optimal action sequence with full-statistics knowledge; algorithm is simple and interesting.  
- Desirability: paper is well-motivated and clearly written; stronger impact if O(log T) guarantee were universal or an Ω(√T) lower bound were shown when the condition fails.  
- Technical concern resolved: initial doubts about Lemma 1 and the average-constraint relaxation were addressed in rebuttal and the reviewer is satisfied.  
- Minor questions: is exact optimal action sequence NP-hard? can results extend to unknown T? can one assume ordering u^1>u^2>…>u^J w.l.o.g. when π is unknown?  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
