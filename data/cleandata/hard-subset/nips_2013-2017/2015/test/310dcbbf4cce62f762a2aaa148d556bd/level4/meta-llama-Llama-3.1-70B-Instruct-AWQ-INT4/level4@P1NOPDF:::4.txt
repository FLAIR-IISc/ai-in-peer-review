This paper presents an analysis of contextual bandits against a stronger baseline, one that utilizes adaptive linear programming to decide whether to pass on a certain percentage of suboptimal rounds, referred to as the "budget." The setup is interesting and the solutions proposed are unique. However, I have one major question regarding the analysis: what is the rationale behind treating boundary cases differently? Specifically, it is unclear why rho = q_j appears to be a special case, particularly for j > 1.
