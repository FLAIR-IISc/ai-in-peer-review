- Problem: studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints relevant to clinical trials and online hiring.
- Model: simplified setting with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint justified for some real applications.
- Method: proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the optimal oracle solution.
- Main algorithm: combines ALP with UCB to create UCB-ALP for unknown statistics.
- Regret result: ALP achieves near-optimal O(1) regret except boundary cases; UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret for non-boundary cases.
- Novelty: first work to establish logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits under their model.
- Comparison to prior work: contrasts with Resourceful Contextual Bandits (RCB) which has O(√T) regret and uses a finite policy-set benchmark; this paper benchmarks against an optimal oracle.
- Technical strength: rigorous regret analysis handling action/context ranking errors, remaining budget fluctuations, and context arrival randomness.
- Robustness: ALP tolerates certain estimation errors, enabling effective combination with UCB.
- Extensions: discusses unknown context distributions (Section 5) and heterogeneous costs (Section 6).
- Presentation: clear, well-organized, and properly contextualized with sound proofs (not fully verified by reviewer).
- Limitation 1: insufficient discussion of practical implications of simplifying assumptions and limited development of application connections.
- Limitation 2: no experimental results, making practical performance unclear.
- Limitation 3: focuses on asymptotic regime (T and B grow proportionally) and does not address finite-horizon fixed-budget settings.
- Suggested improvements: add experiments comparing UCB-ALP to baselines, expand discussion of practical implications of assumptions, and analyze finite-horizon fixed-budget cases.
