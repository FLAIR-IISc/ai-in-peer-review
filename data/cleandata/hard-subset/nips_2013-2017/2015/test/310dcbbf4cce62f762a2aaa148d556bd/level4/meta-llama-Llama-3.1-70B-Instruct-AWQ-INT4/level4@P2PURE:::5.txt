This paper addresses a relatively simple budget-constrained contextual bandit (cb) problem with discrete contexts and actions. The primary contribution of the paper lies in the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm, achieved through linear programming (LP) approximation, and the log(T) regret bound it attains.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it a pleasure to read. A potential direction for future work is to consider employing a parametric model for the reward function, which would be particularly useful when dealing with a large number of contexts.

In Section 4.1, the authors combine Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) with their Approximate Linear Program (ALP) algorithm. To improve clarity, it would be beneficial to provide a more detailed explanation of the underlying rationale behind the UCB-ALP algorithm. Specifically, it appears that the approach follows the principle of optimism in the face of uncertainty, which bears resemblance to the policy update in UCRL2. Elucidating this connection would enhance the reader's understanding of the proposed method.

Including numerical experiments in an extended version of the paper would further strengthen the authors' claims. Additionally, the authors may find the following paper relevant to their work: http://arxiv.org/abs/1506.03374. Overall, this paper deserves acceptance, as it presents an efficient algorithm for constrained cb problems with optimal regret in most cases, making it an interesting and notable improvement over existing work.
