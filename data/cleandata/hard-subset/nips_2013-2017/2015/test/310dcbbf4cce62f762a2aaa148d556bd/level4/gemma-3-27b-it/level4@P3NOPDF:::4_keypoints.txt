- Studies contextual bandits against an adaptive linear-program baseline that sacrifices performance on a fraction of rounds (“budget”); setup is interesting and solutions compelling.

- Main concern: unclear treatment of boundary cases—specifically why ρ = q_j requires separate analysis, especially for j > 1.

- Motivation for a budget-constrained, strategically adaptive baseline is well-articulated and a meaningful, stronger benchmark than fixed/random baselines.

- Competing with this adaptive baseline necessitates careful exploration strategy design; the paper highlights this challenge.

- Technical contributions are sound: analysis appears rigorous and proofs are well-structured.

- Regret bounds are informative and clearly show dependence on budget size and problem complexity.

- Algorithmic modifications to compete with the adaptive LP baseline are clearly presented.

- Experimental evaluation is limited; more empirical results (e.g., performance curves against the adaptive baseline) would strengthen the paper.

- Paper lacks details on parameter tuning for both the proposed algorithm and the adaptive baseline, which harms reproducibility.

- Overall a valuable contribution; resolving the boundary-case explanation and expanding experiments would make it suitable for acceptance with minor revisions.
