- Problem setting: contextual bandits with finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint (simplified but practically reasonable).
- Main result: algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret by leveraging an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.
- Novelty: appears to be the first work achieving logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits.
- Writing and presentation: well-written, clear, thoroughly documented, with relevant citations and clear motivation.
- Theoretical contribution: solid and convincing proofs (not exhaustively checked by reviewer).
- Practical evaluation: practical aspects are only briefly discussed (Sections 5 and 6) and no experimental results are provided.
- Recommendation: overall a good paper that could be improved with a deeper discussion of practical implications and experiments.
