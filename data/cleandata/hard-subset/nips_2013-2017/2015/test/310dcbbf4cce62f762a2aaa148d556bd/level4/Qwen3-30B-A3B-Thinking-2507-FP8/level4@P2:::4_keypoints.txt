- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with budget constraints, important for real applications (clinical trials, recommendations).  
- Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate oracle solutions under budget constraints.  
- Simplifies model to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget—well-motivated and practical.  
- Shows ALP achieves O(1) regret when statistics are known (except boundary cases).  
- Extends to unknown statistics with UCB-ALP, achieving O(log T) regret (except boundary cases).  
- Appears to be first work achieving logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.  
- Technical analysis is rigorous, with novel proof techniques for adaptive algorithms and random contexts.  
- Properly generalizes to unknown context distributions (EALP, UCB-EALP) and heterogeneous costs.  
- Paper clarity is excellent; problem formulation, algorithms, and insights are well-presented.  
- Originality is strong—novel design and analysis of ALP compared to prior LP-based approaches.  
- Significance is substantial—computationally efficient algorithm with practical impact.  
- Main weakness: treatment and intuition for boundary cases (ρ = q_j) is abrupt and underexplained.  
- Could improve comparison and discussion relative to recent works [21,22] that handle dynamic contexts with O(√T) regret.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify boundary-case analysis; meets NIPS criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance).
