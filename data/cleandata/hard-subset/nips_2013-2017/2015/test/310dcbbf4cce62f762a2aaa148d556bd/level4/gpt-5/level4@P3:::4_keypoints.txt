- Studies constrained contextual bandits (time + budget); proposes LP relaxation and ALP that uses bτ/τ; ALP achieves O(1) regret off boundary cases when rewards known.  
- Combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) for unknown rewards, proving O(JK log T) regret off boundaries and O(√T + JK log T) at boundaries.  
- LP relaxation replaces hard budget with average budget constraint and is solved adaptively each round.  
- Analysis leverages sampling-without-replacement / hypergeometric concentration to obtain tight O(1) regret off breakpoints.  
- Regret decomposition separates within-context (action-ranking) and across-context (context-ranking/threshold) errors and controls both in UCB-ALP.  
- Extensions: unknown context distributions handled via empirical estimates (truncated EALP) and heterogeneous costs treated via normalized rewards and structured LP.  
- Novelty: adaptive LP control of budget via bτ/τ and logarithmic regret guarantees in this finite-context/action constrained setting appear new.  
- Relation to prior work: contrasts with √T guarantees from RCB and concave/convex-knapsack bandits and offers a simpler, computationally efficient alternative.  
- Quality: technical approach is sound; LP upper bound Û(T,B) is a clear benchmark and analyses are carefully done.  
- Clarity issues: need earlier/clearer definition of “boundary cases,” explicit Algorithm 1 behavior for b=0 and UCB tie-breaking, unified notation, and clearer piecewise-linear v(ρ) exposition.  
- Main question: at ρ = qj (breakpoints) small fluctuations in bτ/τ frequently flip the LP threshold, causing constant-probability threshold errors and Θ(√T) regret.  
- Paper does not provide matching lower bounds at boundary cases or fully explore sensitivity to ties/near-ties in u*.  
- Unknown-context analysis is truncated and deferred; main-text summary and assumptions should be added for self-containment.  
- Heterogeneous-costs unknown-reward solution relies on an ε-first approach; unclear if a fully adaptive UCB-style method can match known-case guarantees.  
- Suggestions: add intuition/lower-bound discussion for boundary cases, discuss robustness to ties/near-ties, summarize EALP assumptions/results in main text, detail per-round complexity and implementation, and include empirical results illustrating boundary vs non-boundary regimes.  
- Pros: principled reduction from DP to adaptive LP, first log-regret results in this setting, computationally simple, and provides useful analytical tools.  
- Cons: relies on simplifying assumptions (finite contexts/actions, fixed costs, i.i.d. contexts, strict ordering), boundary √T regret unresolved by lower bounds, truncated unknown-context analysis, and adaptivity gap in heterogeneous-costs learning.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
