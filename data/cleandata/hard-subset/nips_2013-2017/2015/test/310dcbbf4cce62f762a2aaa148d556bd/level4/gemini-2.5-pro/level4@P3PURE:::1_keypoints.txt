- Paper studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints and claims a novel O(log T) regret for finite contexts (J) and actions (K) with large T.

- Reviewer finds the contribution incremental since the setting closely resembles a standard time-constrained/budgeted bandit with JK arms where context merely restricts available arms.

- Questions novelty: context-induced coupling may vanish as T grows, so the problem might effectively decompose into J separate bandit problems.

- Theorem 2’s case 2 regret bound O(sqrt(T)+...) seems to omit dependence on number of arms — should likely be O(sqrt(KT)+...); needs clarification.

- Problem-dependent bound O(KJ log T) appears coarse; reviewer asks if a tighter gap-dependent bound (sum of 1/Δ terms) is attainable rather than the KJ factor from UCB-ordering arguments.

- Paper lacks lower bounds; reviewer requests both problem-dependent and problem-independent lower bounds that reflect context-driven complexity beyond trivial classical bounds.

- Overall: limited novelty and incomplete theoretical treatment; recommend refined gap-dependent upper bounds (fix supplementary material) and matching lower bounds; reviewer remains neutral.
