- Problem: studies contextual bandits with horizon T and hard budget B where actions have known positive costs and stochastic rewards, goal maximize reward under budget.  
- Main idea: introduce an adaptive linear program (ALP) that relaxes the hard budget to an average constraint and adapts via remaining budget-to-time ratio.  
- Unit-cost known-context result: ALP achieves O(1) regret w.r.t. the oracle except on boundary parameters (O(√T) there).  
- Learning rewards: UCB-ALP (ALP + UCB) gets O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.  
- Unknown context distribution: propose empirical ALP (EALP) with truncated updates and concentration analysis to obtain analogous guarantees.  
- Heterogeneous costs: generalize LP and propose an ε-First ALP variant for learning under varying costs.  
- Novelty: first to achieve logarithmic regret (outside measure-zero boundaries) for discrete stochastic contextual arrivals under a single budget constraint with computationally light algorithms.  
- Relation to prior work: improves on Bandits with Knapsacks / RCB which typically give O(√T); contrasts with policy-class approaches that are computationally heavier.  
- Technical quality: proofs are sound; LP upper bound (Lemma 1) and hypergeometric concentration analysis for ALP are convincing after rebuttal clarification.  
- Analysis clarity: clean UCB-ALP decomposition into action- and context-ranking errors; boundary behavior is explicitly characterized.  
- Clarity suggestions: add a short intuitive justification of why LP relaxation upper-bounds the oracle and a small running example (e.g., J=3) to illustrate thresholds and boundary effects.  
- Practical significance: approach is computationally efficient, practically implementable, and relevant for resource-constrained applications.  
- Empirical concerns: experiments are brief — need more evaluation on sensitivity near boundaries, mis-specified context distributions, and heterogeneous costs.  
- Open questions / cons: boundary-case optimality (is Ω(√T) intrinsic?), performance/analysis of non-truncated EALP/UCB-EALP, and empirical guidance for truncation.  
- Specific suggestions: clarify oracle computational complexity/hardness, anytime/unknown-horizon extensions (doubling trick), tie-handling assumptions and effect on constants, tuning of UCB coefficient, and include a Lemma 1 proof sketch.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — well-motivated, technically solid, original, and significant contribution.
