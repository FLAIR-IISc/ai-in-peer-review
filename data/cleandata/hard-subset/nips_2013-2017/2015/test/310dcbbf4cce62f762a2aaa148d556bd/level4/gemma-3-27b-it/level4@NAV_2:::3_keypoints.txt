- Problem: Contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T where actions incur known costs and skipping yields no reward/cost.  
- Special case: Focuses mainly on known context distribution π and unit costs c_{j,k}=1.  
- Goal: Maximize cumulative reward while ensuring cumulative cost never exceeds B.  
- Main contribution: Algorithm achieves O(log T) regret under a mild condition for the special case.  
- Extension: Algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs; prior work [17] only achieved O(√T).  
- Novelty: Potentially first work to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits (subject to the mild condition).  
- Method: Uses an approximation algorithm for the full-information oracle combined with UCB-style exploration.  
- Technical soundness: Core ideas (LP approximation + UCB) appear rigorous and well-analyzed, centered on Lemma 1.  
- Clarification: Reviewer was initially unsure about Lemma 1 (average vs. hard constraint) but became convinced after authors’ clarification.  
- Limitations: O(log T) guarantee does not hold in boundary cases, where regret can be Θ(√T).  
- Suggested improvements: Provide a lower bound showing impossibility in boundary cases or an algorithm avoiding them.  
- Practicality concern: Parts of analysis assume known context distribution, which may limit applicability despite an extension to unknown π.  
- Minor questions: Complexity of computing exact optimal action sequence (NP-hard?), handling unknown T, and ordering utilities u*_1 > u*_2 > ... without loss of generality.  
- Impact & clarity: Paper is well-written, technically strong, and addresses an important, practical problem; potential high impact.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses, with the √T boundary cases as the main caveat.
