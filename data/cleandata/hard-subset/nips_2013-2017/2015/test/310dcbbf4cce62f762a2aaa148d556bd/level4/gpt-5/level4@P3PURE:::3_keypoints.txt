- Studies contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T; actions include "skip" with zero cost/reward and paid actions with known costs and stochastic rewards.
- Focuses on special case with known context distribution and unit costs c(j,k)=1, proposing a simple algorithm with O(log T) regret under a mild condition.
- Extends the approach to the general case where the context distribution is unknown and costs are arbitrary.
- Claims to be the first to achieve O(log T) regret (under the stated condition), improving on prior work [16] which achieves O(√T).
- Algorithm builds on an approximation algorithm for computing the optimal action sequence when statistics are known.
- Paper is well motivated and clearly written.
- Desirability for stronger results: either remove the extra condition for O(log T) regret or prove an Ω(√T) lower bound for boundary cases where the condition fails.
- Central technical point is Lemma 1; reviewer was initially unconvinced but convinced after rebuttal clarification.
- Asks whether computing the exact optimal action sequence is NP-hard.
- Asks whether results generalize to unknown T.
- Asks if one can assume without loss of generality that u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J when the context distribution is unknown.
- Strong recommendation for acceptance.
