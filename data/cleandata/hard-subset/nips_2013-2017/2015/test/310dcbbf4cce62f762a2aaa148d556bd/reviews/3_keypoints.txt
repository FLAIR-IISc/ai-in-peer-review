- Problem setup: contextual bandits with budget B and known horizon T, contexts j ~ π, actions include skip (0) and costly actions k>0 with known costs c_{j,k} and stochastic rewards in [0,1].
- Main contribution: algorithm for the special case (π known, c_{j,k}=1) achieving O(log T) regret under a mild condition.
- Extension: algorithm generalized to unknown π and arbitrary costs c_{j,k}.
- Novelty: likely first work obtaining O(log T) regret for this budgeted contextual bandit setting (prior work [16] achieves O(√T)).
- Method: algorithm based on an approximation algorithm for the offline optimal action sequence; described as simple and interesting.
- Writing and motivation: paper is well motivated and very clearly written.
- Limitation: O(log T) guarantee requires a mild condition; without it an Ω(√T) lower bound holds in boundary cases — stronger universal guarantees would increase significance.
- Technical concern: results rely heavily on Lemma 1; reviewer initially doubted correctness (optimal sequence satisfying (2)) but was convinced after rebuttal.
- Open/technical questions: is computing the exact optimal action sequence NP-hard?
- Practical/generalization questions: can results be extended to unknown horizon T?
- Model assumption question: can one assume u^1 > u^2 > ... > u^*_J w.l.o.g. in the unknown context distribution case?
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
