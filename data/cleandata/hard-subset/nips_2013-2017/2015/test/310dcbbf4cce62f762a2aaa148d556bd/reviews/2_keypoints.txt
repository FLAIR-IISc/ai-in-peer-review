- Addresses contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.
- Analysis limited to finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint — simplified but practically reasonable.
- Introduces an adaptive linear programming approximation of the oracle.
- Proposes algorithms that achieve O(log T) regret.
- Claims novelty: first to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits (to the reviewer’s knowledge).
- Paper is clear, well documented, cites relevant work, and has explicit motivation.
- Theoretical contributions are solid and proofs are convincing (not fully checked).
- Practical aspects are thinly treated (Sections 5 and 6) and no experimental results are provided.
- Overall assessment: good paper using appropriate simplifying assumptions to obtain strong theoretical results.
