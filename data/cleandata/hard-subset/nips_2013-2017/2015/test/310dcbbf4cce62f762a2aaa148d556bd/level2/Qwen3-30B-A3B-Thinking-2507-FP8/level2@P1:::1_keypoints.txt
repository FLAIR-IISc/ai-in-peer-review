- Problem: Addresses constrained contextual bandits with both budget and time limits, a realistic but under-studied setting.  
- Method: Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle and combines it with UCB, yielding the UCB-ALP algorithm.  
- Main result: UCB-ALP achieves O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Theoretical rigor: Provides detailed regret bounds for known and unknown statistics and a structured analysis from unit-cost to general cases.  
- Novelty: ALP is a new, elegant approach to handle budget/time coupling in contextual bandits.  
- Comparison to prior work: Clarifies improvements over Resourceful Contextual Bandits (RCB) [17] and related papers [17,20-22], especially in regret and computational profile.  
- Practical insight: Demonstrates decoupling information acquisition and decision making as a useful principle.  
- Implementation: ALP is simple to implement; reviewer recommends briefly discussing computational complexity in the main text.  
- Scope limitation: Main text focuses on unit-cost systems; general cases are deferred to supplementary material.  
- Writing and reproducibility: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and supplementary material provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Significance: High practical and theoretical significance for applications like clinical trials, recommendations, and resource allocation.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revision to include a short discussion of algorithmic/computational complexity in the main text.
