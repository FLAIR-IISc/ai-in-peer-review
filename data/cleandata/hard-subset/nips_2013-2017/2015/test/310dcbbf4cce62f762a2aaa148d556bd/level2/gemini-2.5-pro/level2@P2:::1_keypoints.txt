1. Problem: studies contextual bandits with both time-horizon T and budget B, where the budget couples decisions across time.  
2. Main contribution: computationally efficient algorithms that for the first time achieve logarithmic regret in this constrained setting.  
3. Key challenge: budget constraint makes exploration-exploitation significantly more complex than standard contextual bandits.  
4. Oracle model: proposes ALP (Adaptive Linear Programming) for known expected rewards and context distribution with unit costs as a tractable DP approximation.  
5. ALP results: achieves O(1) regret in non-boundary cases and O(sqrt(T)) regret in boundary cases.  
6. ALP insight: decisions depend only on the ordering of expected rewards, not their exact values.  
7. Unknown-rewards solution: UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB estimation and achieves O(log T) regret (non-boundary) and O(sqrt(T)) (boundary).  
8. Improvement over prior work: logarithmic regret is a significant advance versus prior O(sqrt(T)) bounds in related settings.  
9. Technical quality: solid analysis, novel mapping of remaining budget to sampling-without-replacement and use of concentration inequalities.  
10. Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, progressing from oracle to practical settings with helpful proof sketches.  
11. Weakness — simplifying assumptions: results rely on finite discrete contexts, known context distribution, and unit costs; robustness to misspecification or nonstationarity is underexplored.  
12. Weakness — boundary cases: regret degrades at rho equal to cumulative context probability q_j; practical behavior near these boundaries needs more intuition/plots and possible algorithmic smoothing.  
13. Weakness — underdeveloped extensions: treatments of unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs are brief or rely on truncated/alternative algorithms without full justification.  
14. Minor clarity issue: Algorithm 1 should clarify re-indexing/thresholding when estimated u_hat values change ordering to ensure reproducibility.
