- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with both budget and time limits, relevant to clinical trials and recommender systems.  
- Main contribution: achieves logarithmic regret in this constrained setting, improving on prior O(√T) results.  
- Proposes the Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm for known statistics, achieving O(1) regret except at boundary cases.  
- Combines ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP, achieving O(JK log T) regret in non-boundary cases (J contexts, K actions).  
- Correctly positions contribution relative to prior work that ignored contexts or only achieved O(√T) regret.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous proofs supporting the results.  
- Novel analysis of remaining budget evolution and concentration properties under adaptive algorithms.  
- Paper acknowledges and explains boundary cases where regret becomes O(√T).  
- Excellent clarity and organization; notation and algorithms are well defined and reproducible.  
- Effectively treats unit-cost and heterogeneous-cost cases and handles unknown context distributions carefully.  
- Original insight: LP relaxation with adaptive budget constraints decouples information acquisition from decision making.  
- More computationally efficient and better regret bounds than dynamic programming or static-budget approaches.  
- Suggestion: provide more intuition and practical examples for when boundary cases occur and their impact.  
- Suggestion: include a more explicit comparison with recent dynamic-context papers [21, 22].  
- Justified focus on unit-cost systems before generalizing to heterogeneous costs, with real-world examples.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant contribution, well-suited for NIPS.
