- Problem & contributions: Tackles constrained contextual bandits with budget/time limits and proposes ALP and UCB-ALP, claiming O(1) regret (non-boundary, known stats) and O(log T) regret (unknown rewards) except O(√T) in boundary cases; first to report logarithmic regret in this setting.
- Strength — Novelty and significance: Introduces computationally efficient, practically relevant algorithms and an innovative adaptive linear programming approach.
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: Provides detailed regret analyses and proofs addressing exploration–exploitation and constraint interactions.
- Strength — Practical relevance: Algorithms are computationally efficient, scalable, and extensions to heterogeneous costs and unknown context distributions are discussed.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-organized with clear explanations, examples, and structured algorithms.
- Weakness — Boundary cases: Gives only O(√T) regret in boundary cases and offers no clear path to improvement.
- Weakness — Empirical validation: Lacks experimental results to corroborate theoretical claims and performance vs. alternatives.
- Weakness — Assumptions: Relies on finite discrete contexts and fixed costs, limiting generality; extensions to more complex settings are only briefly discussed.
- Suggestion: Add experiments to validate theory and compare against existing methods.
- Suggestion: Explore strategies (e.g., hybrids or alternative relaxations) to improve boundary-case regret.
- Suggestion: Provide deeper discussion and analysis of assumption limitations and extensions.
- Recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above suggestions.
