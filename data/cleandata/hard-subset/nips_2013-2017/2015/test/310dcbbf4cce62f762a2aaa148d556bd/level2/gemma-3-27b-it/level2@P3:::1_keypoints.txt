- Addresses constrained contextual bandits with goal of achieving logarithmic regret, a relevant and important extension.
- Introduces ALP (adaptive linear programming) as an approximation to the oracle by relaxing hard budget to an average constraint.
- Analysis shows near-optimality of ALP and provides substantial theoretical contribution.
- Extends ALP to unknown rewards via UCB-ALP, achieving logarithmic regret except for boundary cases.
- Further extensions cover unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, broadening applicability.
- Positions work well within related literature and compares to methods like Mixture Elimination and constrained linear contextual bandits.
- Analysis is careful and complex, leveraging concentration properties and handling context ranking errors.
- Boundary cases yield √T regret; implications and example instances for these cases are not fully clarified.
- The connection between LP relaxation and the optimality gap could be stated more explicitly and visualized.
- Theoretical exposition is dense; more intuitive explanations of key steps would improve accessibility.
- Paper lacks empirical evaluation or simulations to validate performance and compare with existing methods.
- Suggest revising language such as “order-optimal” in Theorem 2 given the √T boundary-case regret.
- Strengths: novel problem formulation, solid theory, ALP algorithm, and useful extensions.
- Weaknesses: no experiments, dense theory, and insufficient discussion of boundary-case practical significance.
