- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits with budget/time constraints and proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and UCB-ALP algorithms.  
- ALP approximates the oracle and achieves near-optimal performance when system statistics are known.  
- UCB-ALP combines ALP with UCB to handle unknown expected rewards, achieving logarithmic or sublinear regret in various settings.  
- Authors claim extensibility to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.  
- Rigorous regret analysis is provided using linear programming, concentration inequalities, and coupling arguments.  
- Numerical simulations are included to support theoretical results.  
- Algorithms are practically useful for applications like clinical trials, recommendations, and crowdsourcing.  
- The paper shows strong field knowledge and a comprehensive related-work review.  
- Novelty stems from adaptive linear programming for budget constraints and its combination with UCB.  
- Sufficient methodological detail is provided for reproducibility.  
- Limitations include assumptions of finite discrete contexts and fixed costs, and acknowledged challenges in generalization.  
- UCB-ALP analysis is complex and could use more intuition and clearer explanations.  
- More numerical experiments would strengthen evidence of practical performance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, as the work offers a significant and rigorous contribution to contextual bandits.
