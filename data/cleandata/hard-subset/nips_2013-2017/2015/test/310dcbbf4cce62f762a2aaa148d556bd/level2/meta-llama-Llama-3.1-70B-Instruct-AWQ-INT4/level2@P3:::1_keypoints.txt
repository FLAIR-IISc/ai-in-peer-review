- Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget/time constraints and proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) to approximate an oracle while tolerating parameter estimation errors.
- Combines ALP with UCB to create UCB-ALP for settings with unknown expected rewards.

- Introduces a novel ALP-based approach distinct from dynamic programming methods.
- Provides theoretical guarantees including regret bounds and concentration properties.
- Addresses practical budget/time-constrained problems relevant to applications like clinical trials and recommendations.
- Discusses extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs.

- Assumes finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint that may limit real-world applicability.
- ALP may incur high computational complexity for large instances.
- Regret bounds include additional terms for boundary cases, potentially degrading performance in some scenarios.

- Novel use of ALP to approximate the oracle algorithm.
- New combination of ALP with UCB to form UCB-ALP.

- Contributes a new methodological direction to constrained contextual bandits.
- Relevant to practical applications with budget/time limitations.
- Opens future research directions for more general constrained settings.

- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow.
- Explanations of ALP and UCB-ALP and their theoretical results are clear.
- Includes technical details, proofs, and derivations useful for researchers.

- Technically sound with rigorous proofs and derivations.
- Appears complete, discussing assumptions, limitations, and extensions.
- Highly relevant to the contextual bandit literature.

- Recommendation: Accept for publication at NIPS 2015, while suggesting authors address limitations and assumptions in future work.
