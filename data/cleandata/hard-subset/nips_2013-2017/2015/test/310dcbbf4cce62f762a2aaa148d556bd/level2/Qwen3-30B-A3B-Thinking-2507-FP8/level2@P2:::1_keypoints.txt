- Paper proposes ALP and UCB-ALP for constrained contextual bandits, achieving O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) in boundary cases.  
- Authors claim this is the first work to obtain logarithmic regret for constrained contextual bandits, improving on prior O(√T) results.  
- ALP approximation is well-motivated; theoretical analysis shows O(1) regret with known statistics and UCB-ALP yields logarithmic regret with unknown rewards.  
- Regret analysis is rigorous and carefully handles boundary vs non-boundary technicalities.  
- Paper is well-written, well-structured, and situates itself clearly with respect to prior work [17,20]; supplementary material aids reproducibility.  
- Main results assume unit-cost systems and known context distribution, limiting practical applicability.  
- Extensions to unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs are discussed but not as fully developed or practically convincing.  
- The handling and practical relevance of boundary cases (where regret is O(√T)) are insufficiently justified; “zero Lebesgue measure” is not shown to imply practical rarity.  
- Literature survey may be incomplete; reviewer asks for verification that no prior work achieved logarithmic regret, especially recent works.  
- Computational complexity comparison between UCB-ALP and prior algorithms is lacking.  
- Significance is high due to relevance for applications (clinical trials, recommendations) and the theoretical improvement.  
- Suggested improvements: discuss practical implication/frequency of boundary cases, compare computational complexity, expand main-text treatment of extensions, and re-check literature for prior logarithmic-regret results.
