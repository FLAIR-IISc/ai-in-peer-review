- Summary: Studies constrained contextual bandits with budget B and horizon T, proposes an adaptive LP (ALP) approximation and UCB-ALP to learn unknown rewards, achieving O(log T) regret in non-boundary cases and O(√T) in boundary cases.  
- Strength: Significance & originality — addresses a practical generalization (budgeted contexts) and attains the first logarithmic regret for this setting.  
- Strength: Technical quality — rigorous analysis linking ALP to a hypergeometric concentration, and a credible decomposition of UCB-ALP regret sources.  
- Strength: Clarity — well-organized exposition that builds intuition via the known-statistics case before treating learning.  
- Weakness: Extensions under-discussed — treatments of unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs are too brief and mostly deferred to the supplement.  
- Weakness: Boundary-case implications — paper should better discuss practical sensitivity when the budget ratio ρ is near boundary points (dependence on 1/δ^2).  
- Weakness: Computational comments lacking — per-step LP solves are small but need explicit runtime/complexity remarks (analytic solution and O(J log J) sorting).  
- Argument for acceptance — tackles a significant problem, introduces an elegant ALP framework, provides state-of-the-art logarithmic regret, and offers sound analysis.  
- Argument against acceptance — main-text presentation of key extensions is condensed and practical implications of boundary cases are underexplored.  
- Conclusion: High-quality, technically strong, original work with minor presentation gaps; strong candidate for acceptance.
