## Review of "Algorithms with Logarithmic or Sublinear Regret for Constrained Contextual Bandits"

This paper tackles the challenging problem of constrained contextual bandits, a crucial extension of standard contextual bandits with practical relevance in resource-limited scenarios. The authors make a significant contribution by presenting the first algorithms achieving logarithmic regret in this setting, a notable improvement over existing work that typically yields sublinear regret or is limited to simpler cases. The core idea of approximating the oracle using Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) and combining it with UCB is clever and well-motivated.

The paper is well-structured, starting with a clear problem definition and motivation, followed by a detailed presentation of the proposed algorithms and their analysis. The progression from the known-statistics case (ALP) to the unknown-statistics case (UCB-ALP) is logical and easy to follow. The extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs further demonstrate the versatility of the approach. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, with careful consideration given to the complexities introduced by the constraints and the adaptive nature of the algorithms. The inclusion of lemmas and detailed proofs (referenced to supplementary material) strengthens the technical soundness.

A key strength is the acknowledgment of limitations, particularly the boundary cases where the regret degrades to sublinear. The authors also appropriately position their work within the existing literature, highlighting the limitations of previous approaches and the novelty of their contributions. The discussion of related work is comprehensive, referencing relevant papers in contextual bandits, resource allocation, and online learning.

However, the paper could benefit from more extensive empirical evaluation. While the theoretical results are compelling, demonstrating the practical performance of the algorithms on simulated or real-world datasets would further bolster the claims. Additionally, the discussion of the boundary cases could be expanded, perhaps with insights into how to mitigate their impact in practice. The notation is dense and could be made more accessible with clearer definitions and explanations.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** First algorithms to achieve logarithmic regret in constrained contextual bandits.
*   **Significance:** Addresses a practically important problem with broad applications.
*   **Technical Soundness:** Rigorous theoretical analysis and well-motivated algorithmic design.
*   **Clarity:** Generally well-written and logically organized.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** Lack of experimental results to validate the theoretical findings.
*   **Dense Notation:** Can be challenging for readers unfamiliar with the field.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of contextual bandits. Despite the minor weaknesses, the novelty, significance, and technical quality of the work justify acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically requesting the inclusion of empirical results and improvements to the clarity of the notation.
