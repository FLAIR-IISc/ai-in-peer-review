- Tackles constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, a challenging and important problem.
- Proposes the Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm and UCB-ALP combination to achieve logarithmic (or sublinear in boundary cases) regret.
- Claims to be the first work to obtain logarithmic regret guarantees in this constrained contextual bandit setting.
- ALP for known rewards is well-developed and forms a solid basis for the UCB-ALP extension.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, using concentration inequalities and careful regret decomposition.
- Extensions to unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs demonstrate algorithmic versatility.
- ALP’s reliance only on reward ordering is an appealing property facilitating combination with UCB.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates itself within the literature with an extensive reference list.
- Main limitation: boundary cases are acknowledged but their practical conditions and impacts are not sufficiently explored.
- Empirical validation is limited; more comprehensive simulations and comparisons to existing contextual bandit algorithms are needed.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestions to elaborate on boundary cases and strengthen empirical results.
