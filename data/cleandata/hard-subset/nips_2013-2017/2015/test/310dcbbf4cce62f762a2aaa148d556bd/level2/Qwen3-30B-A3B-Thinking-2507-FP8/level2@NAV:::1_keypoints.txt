- Problem: constrained contextual bandits with budget and time constraints, relevant to clinical trials and crowdsourcing.
- Novelty: first work claiming logarithmic regret for this problem versus prior O(√T) results.
- Algorithms: proposes ALP (known statistics) and UCB-ALP (unknown statistics).
- Regret guarantees: both achieve logarithmic regret except boundary cases where regret is O(√T).
- Core insight: approximate the oracle with linear programming to decouple information acquisition from decision making.
- Theoretical results: ALP achieves O(1) regret (non-boundary); UCB-ALP achieves O(JK log T) regret and is order-optimal (non-boundary).
- Technical quality: rigorous, well-structured proofs and detailed supplementary material.
- Practicality: algorithms are computationally efficient and easily implementable.
- Extensions: addresses unknown context distributions and heterogeneous costs, showing flexibility.
- Strengths: clear theoretical contribution, strong motivation, elegant treatment of exploration–exploitation, and clear writing.
- Weaknesses: main analysis focuses on unit-cost systems (extensions only), and boundary-case practical implications (O(√T) regret) are not fully addressed.
- Recommendation: high-quality paper; accept with minor revisions to better discuss boundary-case implications.
