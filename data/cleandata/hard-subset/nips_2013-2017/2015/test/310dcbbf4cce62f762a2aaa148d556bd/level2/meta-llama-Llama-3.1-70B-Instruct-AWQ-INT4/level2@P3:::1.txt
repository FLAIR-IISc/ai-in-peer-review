**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper studies constrained contextual bandits, a variant of the classic multi-armed bandit problem with additional budget and time constraints. The authors propose a novel approach using adaptive linear programming (ALP) to approximate the oracle algorithm, which achieves near-optimal performance while tolerating certain estimation errors of system parameters. They also combine ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method to design a UCB-ALP algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with unknown expected rewards.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel approach**: The paper introduces a new approach to tackle constrained contextual bandits using ALP, which is different from traditional dynamic programming methods.
2. **Theoretical guarantees**: The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the performance of ALP and UCB-ALP algorithms, including regret bounds and concentration properties.
3. **Practical relevance**: The paper addresses a practical problem with budget and time constraints, making it relevant to real-world applications such as clinical trials and online recommendation systems.
4. **Extension to more general settings**: The authors discuss the possibility of extending their approach to more general constrained contextual bandits with unknown context distribution and heterogeneous costs.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumptions**: The paper makes several assumptions, such as finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, and a single budget constraint, which may not hold in all real-world scenarios.
2. **Computational complexity**: The ALP algorithm may have high computational complexity, especially for large problem instances.
3. **Boundary cases**: The regret bounds for UCB-ALP algorithm have an additional term that depends on the boundary cases, which may affect the performance in certain scenarios.
