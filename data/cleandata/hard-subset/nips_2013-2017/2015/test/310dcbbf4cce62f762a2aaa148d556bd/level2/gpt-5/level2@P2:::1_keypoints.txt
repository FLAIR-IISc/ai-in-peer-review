- Paper studies constrained contextual bandits (single knapsack budget, finite horizon) and proposes an LP relaxation and Adaptive LP (ALP) with O(1) regret (non-boundary) and O(√T) in boundary cases.  
- Combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) for unknown rewards achieving O(JK log T) regret (non-boundary) and O(√T + JK log T) in boundary cases.  
- Extends methods to unknown context distribution (empirical/truncated updates) and heterogeneous costs (ε-first learning) with analogous guarantees.  
- Key technical insight: remaining budget under ALP evolves like sampling without replacement, enabling hypergeometric concentration bounds.  
- Regret decomposition separates action-ranking and context-ranking errors, aiding analysis.  
- Novelty: achieves logarithmic regret in this constrained contextual setting under finite i.i.d. contexts and single budget, improving over prior O(√T) results.  
- Strength: simple, computationally efficient adaptive LP that decouples learning from budget management.  
- Strength: strong, mostly tight theoretical guarantees and careful technical treatment.  
- Strength: clean technical contributions (budget concentration) and useful extensions.  
- Weakness: boundary cases produce unavoidable O(√T) regret; practical frequency and mitigation strategies are not sufficiently addressed.  
- Weakness: O(JK log T) hides gap-dependent constants (e.g., Δ−2); explicit gap dependencies are needed for interpretability.  
- Weakness: limited guidance on choosing truncation points and practical trade-offs for unknown context distribution updates.  
- Weakness: heterogeneous-cost setting relies on ε-first; lacks a UCB-style adaptive alternative or justification for ε-first.  
- Weakness: restrictive assumptions (finite, i.i.d. contexts, single budget) limit applicability versus RCB/BwK; robustness to nonstationarity not discussed.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is sparse; more experiments vs. BwK/RCB baselines and simple heuristics are needed, especially near boundaries and with heterogeneous costs.  
- Quality: technical development is careful and convincing, though some proofs and extensions are deferred to the supplement.  
- Clarity: generally well written; suggestions include reporting per-round computational complexity, explicit gap-dependent terms, and an intuitive figure for thresholding.  
- Originality: adapting LP relaxations to get log regret here and the budget concentration argument are novel contributions.  
- Significance: practically relevant and implementable approach for budgeted contextual decision-making in applications.  
- Suggestions: add broader experiments, propose/Analyze schemes to avoid boundary √T regret (e.g., randomized thinning), make gap dependence explicit, give concrete truncation rules and sensitivity analysis, and explore UCB-ALP for heterogeneous costs.  
- Acceptance case: novel, strong theoretical guarantees and simple practical algorithms.  
- Rejection case: limited empirical validation, boundary √T degradation without mitigation, and restrictive assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
