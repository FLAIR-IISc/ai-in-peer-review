This paper studies the problem of constrained contextual bandits, where the agent is given a budget B and a time-horizon T. The authors propose an Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm that achieves near-optimality and only requires the ordering of expected rewards. They then combine ALP with the upper-confidence-bound (UCB) method to propose a UCB-ALP algorithm for constrained contextual bandits with unknown expected rewards.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed algorithms, and the theoretical analysis. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work and clearly explain how their work differs from previous studies.

The main contribution of the paper is the proposal of the ALP and UCB-ALP algorithms, which achieve logarithmic regret except for certain boundary cases. The authors also provide a rigorous analysis of the regret bounds for these algorithms.
