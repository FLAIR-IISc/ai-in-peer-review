- Problem: Studies contextual bandits with budget and time constraints.  
- Main contribution: Proposes Adaptive Linear Programming (ALP) algorithm requiring only ordering of expected rewards.  
- Extension: Combines ALP with UCB (UCB-ALP) to handle unknown expected rewards.  
- Theoretical results: ALP is near-optimal; UCB-ALP achieves logarithmic regret except for boundary cases.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous regret analysis and sound technical content.  
- Writing: Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Related work: Provides thorough literature review and clear explanation of contributions.  
- Originality: ALP and its combination with UCB are novel contributions.  
- Significance: Addresses an important problem with potential impact on applications like advertising and recommendations.  
- Limitation: Assumes simplified model (finite discrete contexts, fixed costs, single budget); unclear generalizability to complex settings.
