- Addresses selecting optimal unit-sphere graph embeddings for graph transduction, focusing on orthonormal representations.
- Derives a novel PAC-based generalization bound that depends on the spectral norm of the embedding's kernel matrix rather than VC dimension.
- Proposes SPORE (SPectral regularized ORthonormal Embedding), a convex formulation combining empirical loss with a spectral-norm regularizer over the elliptope.
- Develops IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal), a scalable first-order algorithm for optimizing over elliptopes with an O(1/√T) convergence guarantee.
- The PAC bound is original and provides fresh theoretical insight that directly motivates the method.
- SPORE is a novel, principled formulation that advances graph transduction methodology.
- IIP is a valuable practical contribution enabling scalability to graphs with thousands of nodes, outperforming SDP solvers.
- Empirical results on UCI and MNIST show SPORE outperforms strong Laplacian-based baselines.
- Paper is well-written and logically structured, making technical content accessible.
- Clarification needed: theoretical hyperparameter choices (C and β) depend on Lovász θ, which is hard to compute, so the disconnect with cross-validation should be discussed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel PAC bound, principled SPORE formulation, scalable IIP algorithm with guarantees, and strong empirical performance.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient discussion of practical hyperparameter selection relative to the theoretical analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
