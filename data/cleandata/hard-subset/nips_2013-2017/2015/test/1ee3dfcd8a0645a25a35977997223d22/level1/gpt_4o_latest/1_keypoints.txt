- Proposes SPORE: orthonormal embeddings on a unit sphere for graph transduction.
- Introduces a PAC learning framework to handle infinite VC dimension, with a generalization bound tied to the Lovász θ function.
- Derives the SPORE optimization formulation.
- Develops IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal), a scalable solver with O(1/√T) convergence for the SPORE problem.
- Extends SPORE to multiple graphs via an MKL-style approach, claiming improved bounds and noise robustness.
- Strength: Strong theoretical rigor—PAC learnability of orthonormal embeddings and a VC-independent generalization bound.
- Strength: Algorithmic innovation—novel, scalable IIP algorithm for non-smooth convex problems over intersecting convex sets.
- Strength: Practical relevance—addresses scalability and robustness issues relevant to real-world graph transduction.
- Strength: Empirical validation—consistent improvements on synthetic and real-world datasets vs. baselines.
- Weakness: Clarity—the paper is dense and the PAC-to-Lovász θ connection lacks intuitive explanation.
- Weakness: Comparative baselines—experiments omit comparisons to recent methods like Graph Neural Networks.
- Weakness: Scalability claims—computational complexity analysis is insufficiently detailed versus state-of-the-art.
- Weakness: Multiple-graph setting—practical applicability to diverse real-world multi-graph datasets is underexplored.
- Argument for acceptance—significant theoretical extension and algorithmic contributions with compelling empirical results.
- Argument against acceptance—accessibility issues and incomplete experimental comparisons/dataset diversity.
- Recommendation—accept, with revisions to improve exposition clarity and expand experimental evaluation.
