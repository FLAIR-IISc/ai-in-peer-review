- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embedding for graph transduction, addressing optimal embedding selection.  
- Establishes PAC learnability of orthonormal representations with improved sample complexity bounds.  
- Improves prior sample complexity from Ω(ϑ/n)^{1/3} to Ω(ϑ/n)^{1/2}.  
- Formulates SPORE as a spectral-norm-regularized optimization over the elliptope.  
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) algorithm with O(1/√T) convergence for scalable computation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on UCI and synthetic graphs, including scalability to 2000+ vertices.  
- Extends method to multiple graph transduction via MKL-SPORE, increasing practical utility.  
- Strength: clear theoretical foundation and meaningful improvements over prior NIPS work.  
- Strength: practical algorithmic contribution that overcomes SDP computational bottlenecks.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation on small and large graphs.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical implementation details of IIP.  
- Weakness: potential limitations of spectral-norm regularization are not fully explored.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis of the sample complexity improvement could be more detailed.  
- Weakness: connection between Lovász θ function and practical graph properties needs strengthening.  
- Overall: well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically effective work; strong candidate for acceptance.
