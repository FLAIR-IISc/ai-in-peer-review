- Paper proposes SPORE for orthonormal graph embeddings and derives a PAC-based generalization bound for such representations.  
- Uses the Lovász θ function to avoid VC-dimension limitations and justify spectral-norm regularization.  
- Formulates SPORE as a non-smooth convex optimization problem and introduces the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) method for solving it.  
- Provides convergence analysis for IIP with rate O(1/√T).  
- Demonstrates scalability to graphs with thousands of vertices, addressing SDP bottlenecks.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over Laplacian-based baselines on single and multiple graphs.  
- Presents an extension of the method to multiple graphs, increasing applicability.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Parameter sensitivity (choice of C and β) is not thoroughly analyzed; robustness to parameter variation is unclear.  
- Practical implementation and tuning of IIP (approximate projection via FISTA) may be non-trivial; cost terms cp and c0 are not fully elaborated.  
- Comparisons are limited to basic baselines; missing evaluations against recent graph embedding/transduction methods (e.g., GNNs, advanced kernels).  
- PAC bound relies on assumptions about graph structure (relationship between ϑ(G) and n); applicability to diverse graphs needs clearer discussion.  
- Synthetic multiple-graph experiment is simplistic; more complex synthetic or real multi-view datasets would strengthen validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address parameter sensitivity and provide a more detailed IIP complexity/implementation analysis (e.g., table showing influence of C and β and expanded cost analysis).
