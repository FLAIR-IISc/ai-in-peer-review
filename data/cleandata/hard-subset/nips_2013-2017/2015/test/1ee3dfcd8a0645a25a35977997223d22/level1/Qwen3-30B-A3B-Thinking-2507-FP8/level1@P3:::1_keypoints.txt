- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction, proves PAC learnability, gives a spectral-norm-based generalization bound, and introduces IIP optimization; extends to multi-graph (MKL-style).

- Connects to prior work by addressing gaps: uses Rademacher analysis instead of VC-dimension and provides an efficient method to compute orthonormal embeddings.

- Theoretical contribution: Rademacher-based PAC bounds, elegant link between spectral norm and Lovász θ (Lemma 2), and improved sample complexity over prior work.

- Algorithmic innovation: IIP handles infeasible approximate projections via a dual approximation criterion, achieves O(1/√T) convergence, and scales to thousands of vertices unlike SDPs.

- Experimental strength: Empirical gains on UCI and synthetic datasets, large-scale scalability demonstrated, and multi-graph experiments show robustness to noisy graphs.

- Theoretical clarity issue: Confusing use of RBF kernels in experiments versus the orthonormal-embedding framework (K = U^T U) — needs clarification.

- Algorithmic detail lacking: Algorithm 1 insufficiently explained, unclear role of inner FISTA iterations and the approximation/convergence criteria.

- Practical sensitivity missing: No analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters C and β; performance claims need robustness checks over parameter choices.

- Theoretical scope limitation: Analysis assumes simple graphs, but experiments use RBF-constructed graphs that may violate assumptions; theoretical guarantees should be qualified.

- Overall: Strong theoretical and practical contribution with minor, addressable weaknesses.

- Recommendation: Accept.
