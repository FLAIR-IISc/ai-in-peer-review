- Addresses graph transduction by leveraging orthonormal representations and spectral norm regularization to improve generalization.
- Proposes SPORE, a novel algorithm to compute spectral-norm-regularized embeddings on the unit sphere.
- Introduces the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm to scalably solve the resulting non-smooth convex optimization with approximate projections.
- Derives a PAC-learnability bound for orthonormal representations that is independent of VC dimension.
- Extends the framework to handle multiple graph transduction problems.
- Empirical results show significant improvements over baselines and scalability to graphs with thousands of vertices.
- Theoretical presentation is dense and requires substantial prior knowledge in PAC learning and convex optimization.
- Experimental section lacks detailed ablation studies, e.g., sensitivity to IIP parameters and projection approximation criteria.
- Limited comparison against broader state-of-the-art graph kernels and other relevant baselines.
- The practical computational connection between the Lovász theta function and spectral norm computation is not fully clarified.
- Writing could be clearer; some sentences and explanations are overly complex.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental validation (more ablations and baselines).
