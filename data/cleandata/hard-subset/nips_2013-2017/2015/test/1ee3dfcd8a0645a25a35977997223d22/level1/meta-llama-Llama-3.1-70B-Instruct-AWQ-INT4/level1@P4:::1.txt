This paper presents a novel approach to graph transduction using orthonormal representations. The authors show that these representations are PAC learnable and propose a spectral norm regularized formulation, SPORE, for graph transduction. They also develop an efficient algorithm, IIP, to solve the SPORE formulation. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their contributions.

1. A generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings, which suggests that graph embeddings with low spectral norm and empirical error lead to better generalization.
2. A spectral norm regularized formulation, SPORE, for graph transduction, which is derived from the generalization bound.
3. An efficient algorithm, IIP, to solve the SPORE formulation, which is based on an inexact proximal method.
4. An extension of the SPORE formulation to the multiple graph transduction setting, MKL-SPORE.
