- Tackles graph transduction by proposing SPORE to compute orthonormal (unit-sphere) graph embeddings with spectral-norm regularization.
- Core idea well-motivated: unit-sphere embeddings for better generalization and a scalable inexact proximal method (IIP) to avoid SDP bottlenecks.
- Theoretical contributions include a novel generalization bound (Theorem 1) and PAC learnability analysis with improved sample complexity over prior work.
- Connects the formulation to the Lovász θ function and naturally extends to multiple graphs (MKL-SPORE).
- SPORE formulation is logically derived from the PAC bound and addresses efficient computation of orthonormal embeddings.
- IIP offers significant scalability improvements in experiments but its description is dense and relies heavily on supplementary material.
- Convergence rate for IIP is provided, but the paper lacks an intuitive explanation of why inexactness is acceptable.
- Experimental results are promising on synthetic and real datasets and show benefits in multi-view/noisy settings.
- Limited experimental detail: datasets, parameter tuning, and comparisons to more recent baselines need expansion.
- Heavy reliance on supplementary material for technical details and proofs hinders quick understanding.
- Motivation for spectral-norm regularization could be clarified beyond its derivation from the PAC bound.
- Paper is technically sound, rigorous, and generally well-written, though readability suffers where IIP and proofs are outsourced.
- The combination of orthonormal representations, spectral norm regularization, and IIP is novel and original.
- The approach is significant due to improved scalability and theoretical grounding, likely useful for graph-based learning.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel and scalable algorithm, positive experimental results, useful multi-graph extension.
- Arguments against acceptance: complex IIP presentation, reliance on supplements, and limited experimental comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand IIP explanation in the main text and provide more comprehensive experimental details and baselines.
