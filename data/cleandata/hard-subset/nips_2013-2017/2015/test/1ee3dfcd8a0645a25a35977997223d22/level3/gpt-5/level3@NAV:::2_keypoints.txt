- Paper proposes SPORE: convex program over the elliptope that jointly learns vertex labels and unit-sphere orthonormal embeddings with a spectral-norm regularizer on the graph kernel.
- The spectral-norm choice is motivated by a high-probability bound (Theorem 1) that places λ1(K) in the generalization term.
- The authors derive asymptotic upper bounds on transductive error (Theorem 4) and labeled sample-complexity guarantees scaling favorably when Lovász theta = o(n).
- SPORE can be formulated as an SDP, and the paper introduces an infeasible inexact proximal (IIP) method with O(1/√T) guarantees that scales to thousands of nodes.
- Experiments on UCI, MNIST, and synthetic multi-graph settings show consistent gains over Laplacian baselines and K-scaling.
- Optimization contribution and inexact proximal analysis are sound, practically useful, and well-motivated for elliptopes.
- Empirical comparisons are favorable and the method demonstrates scalability.
- Theorem 4 lacks clear high-level intuition and missing/unused “similarity” assumptions—“edges link similar instances” is not formalized or used in the proof sketch.
- The sample-complexity implication (e.g., m = Ω(n^{3/4}) for ER graphs with θ(G)=Θ(√n)) needs clearer articulation and comparison to prior bounds.
- Some algorithmic details and notation (e.g., projection step) are confusing and need clarification.
- Novelty lies in coupling orthonormal embeddings with spectral-norm regularization and the IIP solver relative to prior graph SSL work.
- Potentially impactful if theoretical claims are clarified and validated; optimization and empirical results are solid.
- Pros: principled formulation linking generalization to spectral norm, scalable first-order solver with analysis, strong empirical performance and multi-graph extension.
- Cons: missing/unused similarity assumption in main theorem, insufficient intuition and clarity for Theorem 4, minor algorithmic and parameter-clarity issues.
- Verdict: Unable to judge the main theoretical claim due to gaps; leaning reject pending precise formalization of the similarity assumption and expanded proof-level intuition for Theorem 4.
