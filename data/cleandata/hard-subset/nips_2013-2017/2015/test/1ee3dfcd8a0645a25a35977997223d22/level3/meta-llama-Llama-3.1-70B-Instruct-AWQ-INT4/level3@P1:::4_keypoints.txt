- Proposes SPORE: a spectral-norm-regularized framework to learn orthonormal graph embeddings for transduction.  
- Introduces an inexact proximal (IIP) algorithm to efficiently approximate the SPORE solution.  
- Provides theoretical results: a generalization bound and a labeled sample complexity estimate.  
- Demonstrates SPORE empirically on real-world and synthetic graphs, including a multiple-graph transduction setup.  

- Benefits of orthonormal embedding are not strongly justified; more rigorous analysis and comparisons are needed.  
- Experiments lack ablation studies isolating the impact of SPORE and the orthonormal constraint (Eq. 3).  
- No evaluation of the IIP approximation accuracy relative to the true SPORE solution.  
- Multiple-graph transduction extension is described superficially and needs more motivation, details, and experiments.  
- Some experimental findings (e.g., Table 4 noisy case) are counterintuitive—the single graph outperforming multiple graphs is unexplained.  
- Paper is dense and hard to read, overly reliant on supplementary material; needs reorganization for clarity and self-containment.  

- Quality: technically sound with supported claims, but several key aspects need deeper analysis.  
- Clarity: currently poor due to density and heavy dependence on appendices.  
- Originality: framework and algorithm are novel and elegant, though some components (spectral norm regularization) are not entirely new.  
- Significance: promising potential but requires more thorough justification and analysis to demonstrate full impact.  

- For acceptance: novel framework and algorithm, theoretical guarantees, and empirical validation across datasets.  
- For rejection: weak justification for orthonormal embedding, missing ablations and approximation analysis, dense presentation, and unexplained counterintuitive results.
