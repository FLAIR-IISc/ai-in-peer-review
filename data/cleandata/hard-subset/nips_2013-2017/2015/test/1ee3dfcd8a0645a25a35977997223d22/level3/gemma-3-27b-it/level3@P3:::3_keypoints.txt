- Problem & contribution: Proposes SPORE (spectral norm regularization) and IIP (inexact proximal algorithm) to optimize orthonormal graph embeddings for improved graph transduction.

- Gap in prior work: Identifies lack of guidance for selecting optimal embeddings and argues kernel normalization alone is insufficient.

- Theoretical novelty: Derives a PAC-based generalization bound using the kernel matrix maximum eigenvalue and links the problem to the Lovász θ function.

- Optimization formulation: Casts the embedding selection as a convex optimization over the elliptope, avoiding VC-dimension bounds.

- Algorithmic advance: Introduces IIP, an efficient, scalable inexact proximal method to avoid costly SDP solves.

- Convergence guarantees: Provides theoretical convergence analysis for IIP (Theorems 5 & 6).

- Sample complexity: Presents improved sample complexity results over prior work (Theorem 4).

- MKL extension: Extends SPORE to multi-kernel learning (MKL-SPORE) to combine multiple views robustly.

- Empirical validation: Demonstrates significant performance gains over baselines (Un-Lap, N-Lap, KS) on UCI and MNIST datasets.

- Scalability evidence: Shows IIP scales to larger graphs (up to 2000 nodes) and MKL experiments on synthetic graphs illustrate robustness to noise.

- Presentation quality: Paper is well-written, organized, and clearly explains concepts.

- Strengths summary: Strong theoretical foundation, methodological novelty, scalability, empirical support, and clear presentation.

- Weaknesses summary: Limited analysis of parameter sensitivity (C, β), insufficient detailed computational cost breakdown versus SDP solvers, and limited comparisons to recent GNN methods.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, theoretically sound, scalable, empirically validated, and accessible to a broad audience.

- Arguments against acceptance: Need for deeper parameter sensitivity and computational cost analyses.
