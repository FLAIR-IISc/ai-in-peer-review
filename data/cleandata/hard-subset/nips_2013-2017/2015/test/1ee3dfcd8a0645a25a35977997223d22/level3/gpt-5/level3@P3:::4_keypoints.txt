- Paper proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embedding over the elliptope K(G) for graph transduction, with generalization bound depending on λ1(K) and links to Lovász θ.
- Introduces IIP, an infeasible inexact proximal algorithm using approximate projections (FISTA inner loop) with O(1/√T) convergence; extends to MKL-style multi-graph formulation with bound depending on min θ.
- Situates work among transductive Laplacian regularization, orthonormal embedding consistency, SDP relaxations, and first-order/inexact proximal methods.
- Conceptual strength: clear connection between generalization, spectral norm, and θ(G) motivating spectral-norm regularization.
- Theoretical strength: PAC-style bound avoiding VC dimension, improved labeled-sample scaling O((θ/n)1/2), and connection θ(G)θ(Ḡ)=n explaining density-dependent rates.
- Algorithmic strength: elegant inexact-projection scheme with provable convergence for non-smooth objectives over PSD+affine constraints.
- Practical strength: SPORE scales beyond standard SDP solvers and shows empirical gains over Laplacian baselines on UCI/MNIST and synthetic data.
- Weakness: lack of ablations isolating benefits of orthonormal constraints (Kii=1, Kij=0) and the spectral-norm term.
- Suggestion: ablate β=0, relax zero constraints (e.g., ℓ1 penalty or diag-normalized PSD kernels), and correlate λ1(K) with train/test error.
- Weakness: no evaluation of IIP approximation fidelity versus true SPORE SDP on small graphs.
- Suggestion: report objective gaps g(K_IIP)−g(K_SDP), feasibility residuals, PSD violations, effect of inner-loop length S, and runtime–accuracy curves.
- Weakness: multi-graph extension underdeveloped; missing optimization details, stopping criteria, complexity, and intuition about min_k θ(G(k)).
- Suggestion: detail alternating optimization, prox for max_k λ1(K(k)), behavior when graphs conflict, η dynamics, and real multi-view experiments.
- Concern: unintuitive empirical result where best single graph outperforms MKL-SPORE in noisy case; needs analysis of learned η, underweighting, over-regularization, or per-graph β.
- Clarity issue: paper is dense with key algorithmic details and proofs in supplement; suggest moving IIP pseudocode, proximal operators, and intuition for Lemmas/Theorems into main text.
- Baselines/scope: compare to stronger graph SSL baselines (label propagation, manifold regularization) and scalable SDP/low-rank methods (Arora–Kale, Hazan FW, Burer–Monteiro); compare spectral norm to trace/Frobenius penalties.
- Quality assessment: sound theory and algorithms but empirical analyses currently do not validate crucial claims (orthonormal benefit, IIP fidelity, multi-graph robustness).
- Clarity assessment: technically solid but overly compressed; needs more intuition, ablations, and implementation diagnostics for reproducibility.
- Originality: high — novel generalization analysis tied to θ and a new inexact-projection framework.
- Significance: potentially significant if empirical benefits and practicality of IIP are more convincingly demonstrated, especially for multi-graph cases.
- Pros for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, elegant inexact proximal method, and promising scalable empirical results.
- Cons for acceptance: missing ablations and approximation diagnostics, underdeveloped multi-graph section, and dense presentation with key details in supplement.
- Recommendation: borderline accept pending ablations, SDP comparison diagnostics, expanded multi-graph experiments, and clearer exposition.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity of results to β and C and validation curves for theoretically suggested values.
- Questions for authors: what is the feasibility gap at termination and do you post-project to exact feasibility before prediction?
- Questions for authors: can you report objective gaps and feasibility residuals vs SDP for n ≤ 200?
- Questions for authors: in the noisy multi-graph case, what are the learned η values and can sparsity priors or per-graph β fix the degradation?
