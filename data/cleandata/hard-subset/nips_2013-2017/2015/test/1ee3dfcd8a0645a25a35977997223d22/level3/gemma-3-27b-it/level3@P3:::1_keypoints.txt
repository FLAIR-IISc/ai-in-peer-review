- Strong theoretical foundation connecting orthonormal embeddings, spectral norm regularization, and the Lovász number with a novel error bound.  
- Novel SPORE algorithm that principledly applies the theory to graph transduction.  
- Practical Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) solver addressing SDP scalability.  
- MKL extension enabling multi-graph / multi-view applicability.  
- Convincing empirical results showing SPORE outperforms baselines across datasets.  
- Clear positioning relative to prior work, building on the Zhang and Ando framework.  

- Paper is dense and could be streamlined for readability; many contributions packed into one paper.  
- Equation (3) needs clarification to show SSL reduces to supervised learning on labeled samples S.  
- Notation in Section 2 (the equals sign before ω_C in eq. (5)) is confusing and needs explanation.  
- Embedding recovery from K (how U is obtained, factorization choices) is insufficiently discussed.  
- PSD enforcement via eigendecomposition in Alg.1 (steps 8–9) may be a computational bottleneck; alternative factorizations could be more efficient.  
- Minor error: Zhang and Ando citation year is incorrect (should be NIPS 2005).  

- Add a sentence clarifying eq. (3) as a kernel learning formulation using only labeled samples S.  
- Rephrase the introduction to eq. (5) to clarify the meaning of the equals sign and ω_C.  
- Expand on embedding recovery methods (e.g., SVD) and their computational costs.  
- Quantify the eigendecomposition cost in Alg.1 and compare to optimizing over a factorization like K = LL^T.  
- Correct the Zhang and Ando citation year to 2005.  

- Overall: strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; minor clarity and implementation detail issues do not undermine the work’s importance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theory and novel error bound; innovative algorithm with strong empirical performance; scalable solver; advances prior literature; wide applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility hindered by density; some implementation and computational-cost details need elaboration.  

- Recommendation: Strong Accept, subject to addressing the noted clarifications and minor corrections.
