- Introduces SPORE: Spectral Norm Regularized Orthonormal Embedding for graph transduction, reformulating SSL as a kernel-based supervised problem.  
- Main contributions: PAC-based error bound, spectral norm regularization framework, and an efficient proximal solver for large-scale optimization.  
- Extends method to multiple-graph learning (MKL-SPORE), showing robustness to noisy graphs.  
- Theoretical novelty: links the Lovász number of the data graph to error convergence, advancing understanding of spectral regularization.  
- Algorithmic innovation: proposes the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) method enabling scalable optimization for problems otherwise requiring SDPs.  
- Convergence guarantees for IIP are provided (O(1/√T)) and are well-supported.  
- Experimental validation: strong empirical results on real and synthetic datasets showing SPORE outperforms existing methods.  
- Scalability: method can handle graphs with thousands of vertices, making it practical for large-scale problems.  
- Clarity issues: paper is dense with unclear notation (notably Equations (3) and (5)) and lacks explicit explanation of how embeddings U are recovered from K.  
- Computational concerns: reliance on expensive PSD checks (eigendecomposition) in the proximal solver could be a scalability bottleneck; alternatives like Cholesky are not explored.  
- Practical implications: trade-offs between spectral norm regularization and other regularizers are not thoroughly discussed for practitioners.  
- Reference error: Zhang and Ando citation should be NIPS 2005, not 2006.  
- Acceptance stance: recommends acceptance with minor revisions to improve notation clarity and address computational efficiency concerns.
