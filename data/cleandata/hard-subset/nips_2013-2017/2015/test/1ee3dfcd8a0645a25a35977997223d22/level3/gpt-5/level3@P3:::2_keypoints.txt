- Paper proposes jointly learning an orthonormal unit-sphere embedding and labels by minimizing the SVM dual plus a spectral-norm penalty over the elliptope K(G).  
- Theorem 1 gives a high-probability generalization bound depending on the kernel spectral norm, motivating spectral-norm regularization.  
- The theory connects to Lovász’s theta (ϑ(Ḡ)) and yields asymptotic error and labeled sample complexity bounds with improved scaling over prior orthonormal-representation work.  
- Theorem 4 and its assumptions are unclear: the role of the informal “edges link similar instances” assumption is not defined or located in the proof.  
- Equation (6) is syntactically ambiguous; the intended rate and its sample-complexity implications (e.g., for random graphs with ϑ(G)=Θ(√n)) need clarification.  
- Optimization: proposes an infeasible inexact proximal (IIP) method using subgradient updates and approximate projections (FISTA on support functions) with O(1/√T) convergence.  
- The inexact prox via support-function proximal operators is an interesting, technically sound approach for the elliptope PSD constraints.  
- The method scales to graphs with thousands of nodes and enforces PSD feasibility before SVM calls.  
- Experiments: SPORE/MKL-SPORE consistently outperform Laplacian and K-scaling baselines across UCI, MNIST subsamples, and synthetic graphs.  
- Experimental ablations are present (β=0, fixed embeddings) but stronger baselines (harmonic functions, game-theoretic transduction) and runtime/SDP comparisons are missing.  
- Clarity: paper is dense but organized; definitions for “similarity” and explicit statement of assumptions in Theorem 4 are needed.  
- Suggest disambiguating (6), providing corollaries for G(n,p) and SBMs, and summarizing the Rademacher-based improvements over prior work.  
- Provide intuition for why spectral-norm penalty via ϑ(Ḡ) is the appropriate regularizer.  
- Originality: jointly optimizing embeddings with a PAC-derived spectral-norm penalty is novel and well-motivated.  
- Methodological novelty: inexact proximal approach for nonsmooth objective over the elliptope and the multiple-graph max-spectral-norm extension are useful contributions.  
- Significance: if Theorem 4 is clarified, the work meaningfully links generalization to graph parameter ϑ and provides a scalable algorithm; current ambiguity limits assessment.  
- Key questions for authors: formalize the “edges connect similar instances” assumption, show where it’s used in the proof, disambiguate (6) with corollaries, provide ablations (β=0/fixed embeddings), sensitivity to C and β, and runtime/memory comparisons vs SDP solvers.  
- Pros: principled spectral-norm regularization from a high-probability bound, elegant use of Lovász ϑ, scalable inexact-prox method, consistent empirical gains, and a theory-backed multi-graph extension.  
- Cons: unclear/possibly unused assumptions in Theorem 4, ambiguous rates, limited baselines/ablations and runtime comparisons, and heavy reliance on the supplement with some unreadable notation.  
- Recommendation: Borderline/uncertain—promising ideas and optimization, but central theoretical claims need clarification and more targeted empirical validation.  
- Reviewer confidence: Moderate.
