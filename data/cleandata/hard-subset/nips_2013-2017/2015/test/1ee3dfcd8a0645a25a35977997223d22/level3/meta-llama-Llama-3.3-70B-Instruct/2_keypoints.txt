- Proposes a novel optimization formulation that jointly learns vertex embeddings and labels with a spectral-norm-based regularization term.
- Regularizer derived from an upper bound (Theorem 1) on generalization error, yielding an asymptotic upper bound with favorable scaling and a labeled sample complexity bound.
- Optimization can be cast as an SDP; authors advocate an inexact proximal method for scalability to graphs with thousands of vertices.
- Builds on prior graph learning and optimization work and provides a clear related-work overview.
- Method is novel, technically sound, and has potential to advance the state of the art in graph learning.
- Experimental results claim superiority over previous methods and are supported by thorough analysis.
- Paper is well-organized and includes sufficient details for expert reproduction of results.
- Presentation of Theorem 4 is unclear due to ambiguous assumptions and terminology.
- Needs clearer explanations and definitions of technical terms throughout.
- Requires more detailed empirical and theoretical comparisons with prior work and a discussion of method limitations.
- Overall: promising and significant contribution but necessitates revisions to improve clarity and address limitations.
