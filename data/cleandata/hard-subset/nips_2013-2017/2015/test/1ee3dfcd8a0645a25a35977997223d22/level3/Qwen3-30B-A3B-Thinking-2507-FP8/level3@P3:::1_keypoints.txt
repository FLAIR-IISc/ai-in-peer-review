- Novel reduction of SSL to a kernel-based supervised problem via Zhang-Ando, establishing a connection between spectral regularization and orthonormal representations (note: Zhang-Ando is NIPS 2005, not 2006).
- Main theoretical result: PAC generalization bound (Theorem 1) showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.
- SPORE formulation: convex optimization over the elliptope that links error convergence to the Lovász θ function of the graph.
- IIP algorithm: an Infeasible Inexact Proximal method handling cone–polytope intersections with approximate projection, scaling to thousands of vertices.
- IIP has provable O(1/√T) convergence and addresses scalability limits of standard SDP solvers.
- Extension to multiple graph transduction with an MKL-style approach, supported by experiments.
- Experiments demonstrate clear improvements over existing methods across multiple datasets.
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous PAC analysis and convergence proofs.
- Paper is dense and contains many contributions, which makes it challenging to follow; authors should explicitly state the supervised reduction and clarify sums over labeled set S.
- Notational/confusing issue: Equation (5) should remove the stray equals sign before ω_C to clarify the definition.
- Need clarification on how embedding U is recovered from K and practical choices for factorization/dimension of U when K = U^T U.
- Computational concern: ensuring K_r is PSD via eigendecomposition (Algorithm 1, steps 8–9) may be the main cost; consider discussing factorized optimization (K = L L^T) to avoid repeated decompositions.
- Incorrect citation: explicitly reference Zhang and Ando’s 2005 NIPS paper.
- Originality: the link between spectral norm regularization, orthonormal embeddings, and Lovász θ is novel and extends prior foundational work.
- Significance: provides new theoretical insights and a scalable algorithmic solution for graph transduction; MKL extension broadens applicability.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, fix notation/citation, and address factorization/complexity details.
