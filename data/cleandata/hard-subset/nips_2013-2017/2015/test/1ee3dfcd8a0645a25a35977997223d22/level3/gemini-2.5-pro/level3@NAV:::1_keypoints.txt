- Problem and approach: Reduces graph transduction/SSL to an equivalent supervised kernel learning problem, building on Zhang and Ando (NIPS 2005).
- Main theoretical contribution: Novel PAC-based error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.
- Core proposal: SPORE — a spectral-norm-regularized formulation for learning an optimal orthonormal embedding.
- Theoretical link: Connects error convergence rate to the Lovász θ number of the data graph.
- Algorithmic contribution: Proposes a scalable Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) solver to handle the resulting non-smooth convex optimization.
- Extension: Generalizes the approach to a multiple kernel learning setting.
- Empirical results: Experiments on several datasets show convincing improvements over existing methods.
- Strengths: Original, significant theoretical insight with a complete pipeline from theory to an efficient, scalable algorithm and strong empirical support.
- Main weakness: Paper is dense and occasionally unclear, needing clarifications in several places.
- Clarification requests: Explicitly state the reduction from SSL to supervised learning (eq. (1) → eq. (3)); resolve confusing equals-sign/notation in eq. (5).
- Implementation clarifications: Explain how orthonormal embedding U is recovered from learned K and discuss practical matrix-factorization choices (e.g., K = LL^T).
- Computational concern: Comment on PSD-enforcement via eigendecomposition in Alg.1 (steps 8–9) as a potential bottleneck and whether optimizing over a factorization would avoid repeated decompositions.
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando paper appeared in NIPS 2005 (not 2006).
- Overall recommendation: Strong paper; recommend acceptance.
