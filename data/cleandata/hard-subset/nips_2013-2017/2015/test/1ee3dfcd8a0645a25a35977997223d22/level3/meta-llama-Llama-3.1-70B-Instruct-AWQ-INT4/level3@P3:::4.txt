This paper proposes a novel spectral regularized embedding framework, called SEER, for graph transduction. The authors also introduce an original algorithm, called IIP, to approximately solve the SEER problem. The experiments suggest benefits of the proposed method, but the paper lacks ablations showing the advantages of SEER or the orthonormal representation.

The main contribution of the paper is the SPORE formulation, a spectral norm regularized orthonormal embedding for graph transduction. The authors derive a generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings and show that the class of orthonormal representations is efficiently PAC learnable. The SPORE formulation is posed as a non-smooth convex function over an elliptope, which can be solved using semi-definite programs (SDPs). However, the authors propose an inexact proximal method, called IIP, which is more scalable than SDP and has a convergence rate of O(1/√T).

The paper also extends the analysis to the multiple graph setting and proposes an MKL style extension of SPORE. The authors show that combining multiple graphs improves the error convergence rate and hence the labelled sample complexity. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on both real-world and synthetic graphs.
