## Review of "Spectral Norm Regularization of Orthonormal Representations for Graph Transduction"

This paper presents a compelling investigation into spectral regularization for graph transduction, framing the problem through the lens of kernel methods and orthonormal representations. The core idea – reducing semi-supervised learning (SSL) to a supervised problem via the Zhang & Ando formulation – is a strong starting point, and the subsequent theoretical development, particularly the error bound relating spectral norm minimization to generalization via the Lovász number, is a significant contribution. The proposed SPORE algorithm, coupled with the novel Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) solver, addresses the scalability issues inherent in standard SDP approaches. The MKL extension and experimental validation on several datasets further strengthen the work.

The paper is ambitious in scope, packing multiple contributions into a single submission. While this demonstrates the depth of the research, it also leads to a density that occasionally hinders clarity. The authors successfully establish a strong theoretical foundation, linking spectral norm regularization to improved generalization, and provide a practical algorithm with promising empirical results. The connection to the Lovász number is particularly insightful, offering a graph-theoretic perspective on the regularization process.

However, the paper isn't without its weaknesses. The authors should explicitly state that the correspondence between equations (3) and (1) establishes SSL as a supervised learning problem operating solely on labeled samples, to avoid potential misinterpretations regarding the summation over *S* only. In Section 2, the notation in equation (5) is unclear; the equals sign preceding ω_C is confusing and requires clarification. Furthermore, Section 3 would benefit from a more detailed explanation of how the embedding *U* is recovered from the kernel *K*, along with a discussion of practical factorization choices. Regarding Algorithm 1, the authors should address the computational cost of repeatedly performing eigendecomposition to ensure *K_r* is PSD. Exploring alternatives, such as optimizing directly over a factorization (e.g., *K=LL^T*), could significantly improve efficiency. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel theoretical insights into spectral regularization for graph transduction.
*   Strong connection to the Lovász number, providing a meaningful theoretical interpretation.
*   Practical and scalable algorithm (SPORE with IIP solver).
*   Convincing experimental results on multiple datasets.
*   Extension to the multiple graph setting.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Density and occasional lack of clarity in presentation.
*   Potential for misinterpretation of the SSL reduction.
*   Computational cost of PSD enforcement in Algorithm 1.



**Overall:** This is a high-quality paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of graph transduction. Despite some minor clarity issues and potential computational bottlenecks, the theoretical novelty, algorithmic innovation, and empirical validation make it worthy of acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above. I also note the authors correctly cite Zhang and Ando as NIPS 2005.
