This paper addresses the problem of graph transduction by first reducing semi-supervised learning (SSL) to an equivalent supervised kernel learning problem, building on the framework of Zhang and Ando (NIPS 2005). The main theoretical contribution is a novel PAC-based error bound which demonstrates that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization. This insight motivates the paper's central proposal: SPORE, a spectral norm regularized formulation for learning an optimal orthonormal embedding. The authors further connect the error convergence rate to the Lovász θ number of the data graph, a significant theoretical link. To solve the resulting non-smooth convex optimization problem, they propose a scalable Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) solver. The work is extended to a multiple kernel learning setting, and experiments on several datasets show convincing improvements over existing methods.

The primary strengths of the paper are its originality and significance. The theoretical connection between the spectral norm, generalization in graph transduction, and the Lovász θ number provides a novel and valuable perspective. This work is a complete and substantive contribution, presenting a clear pipeline from theory to an efficient algorithm and demonstrating its practical utility. The proposed IIP solver is a strong algorithmic contribution that makes the approach scalable, and the experimental results are compelling.

The paper’s main weakness is its density, which occasionally compromises clarity. A few points could be clarified to improve the manuscript:
*   The authors should explicitly state that the correspondence between eq. (1) and eq. (3) effectively reduces the SSL problem to a supervised one (learning only on labeled samples S), as the summation over S in eq. (3) might otherwise cause confusion.
*   In Section 2, the notation in equation (5) is slightly confusing with an equals sign appearing just before ω_C.
*   In Section 3, it would be helpful to clarify how the orthonormal embedding U is recovered from the learned kernel K and to discuss practical choices for the matrix factorization.
*   Regarding the proposed algorithm (Alg.1, steps 8–9), ensuring the kernel estimate is PSD via eigendecomposition can be computationally expensive. The authors should comment on whether this is the main bottleneck and if optimizing over a factorization (e.g., K=LL^T) would be a feasible alternative to avoid repeated decompositions.

Finally, a minor correction: the cited Zhang and Ando paper appeared in NIPS 2005, not 2006.

Overall, this is a strong paper that provides new theoretical insights on spectral regularization for graph transduction, supported by a novel, scalable algorithm and convincing empirical results. I recommend acceptance.
