This paper presents a novel approach to graph transduction by reducing it to an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem using Zhang and Ando's formulation. The main theoretical contribution is an error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization. The authors propose a spectral-norm regularizer and link error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph. They also present an efficient algorithm, called Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP), to solve the resulting optimization problem.

* The paper presents a novel and interesting approach to graph transduction, which is a significant contribution to the field.
