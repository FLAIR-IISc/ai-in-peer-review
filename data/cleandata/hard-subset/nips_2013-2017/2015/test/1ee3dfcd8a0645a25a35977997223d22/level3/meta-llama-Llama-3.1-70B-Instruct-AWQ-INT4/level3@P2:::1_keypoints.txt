- Proposes a novel reduction of graph transduction to an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem.  
- Builds on Zhang and Ando and makes significant theoretical and algorithmic contributions.  
- Main theoretical result: derives an error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.  
- Introduces a proximal solver handling cone–polytope intersection via an approximate projection step (novel and efficient).  
- Uses the Lovász number of the data graph to bound error convergence — a novel spectral-regularization insight.  
- Provides thorough analysis of the algorithm and its convergence properties.  
- Experimental results are convincing and demonstrate effectiveness on several datasets.  
- Paper is well-written and explanations are generally clear.  
- Notation issues: the equals sign in equation (5) may cause confusion.  
- Clarify correspondence between equations (3) and (1) to make the semi-supervised→supervised mapping explicit.  
- Clarify how the embedding U is recovered from K and discuss practical factorization choices.  
- Discuss cost of ensuring K_r is PSD (e.g., via eigendecomposition) in Section 4.  
- Paper is dense with many contributions, which may be heavy for a single submission.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant; Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10.
