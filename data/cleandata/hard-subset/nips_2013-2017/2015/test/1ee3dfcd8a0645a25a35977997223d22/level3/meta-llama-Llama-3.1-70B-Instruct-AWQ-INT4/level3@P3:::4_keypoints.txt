- Proposes SEER/SPORE: a spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embedding formulation for graph transduction.  
- Introduces IIP: an inexact proximal algorithm to solve SPORE more scalably than SDP with O(1/√T) convergence.  
- Derives generalization bounds and shows orthonormal representations are efficiently PAC-learnable.  
- Frames SPORE as a non-smooth convex problem over the elliptope, solvable by SDP but addressed via IIP for practicality.  
- Extends to multiple-graph setting with an MKL-style extension, showing improved error convergence and labeled sample complexity.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real graphs demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Lacks ablation studies to isolate the benefits of SEER and the orthonormal representation.  
- Paper is dense and relies heavily on supplemental material; needs more intuitive explanations and main-text detail.  
- Technically sound (Quality 8/10) but would benefit from additional analyses.  
- High originality (9/10) and significance (9/10) in addressing a challenging graph-transduction problem.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and add ablations/analyses.
