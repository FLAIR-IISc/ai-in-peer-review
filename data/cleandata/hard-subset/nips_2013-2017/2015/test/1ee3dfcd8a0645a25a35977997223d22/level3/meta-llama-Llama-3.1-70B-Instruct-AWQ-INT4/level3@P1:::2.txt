This paper proposes a novel approach to graph transduction by learning orthonormal representations of graphs. The authors provide a generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings and show that the class of orthonormal representations is efficiently PAC learnable. They then introduce a spectral norm regularized formulation, called SPORE, which jointly optimizes embedding and labels. The paper also presents an efficient algorithm to solve SPORE using an inexact proximal method.

The main contributions of the paper are:

1.  A generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings, which suggests that penalizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel leads to better generalization.
2.  A spectral norm regularized formulation, SPORE, which jointly optimizes embedding and labels.
3.  An efficient algorithm to solve SPORE using an inexact proximal method, which scales to graphs with thousands of nodes.

The paper also presents experimental results on real-world and synthetic graphs, which suggest that the proposed method outperforms prior methods.

However, the reviewer raises some concerns about the paper:

1.  The main result (Theorem 4) is not clearly understood at a high level.
2.  The paper claims that learning is impossible without assumptions, but the assumption that "edges link similar instances" is not clearly defined or incorporated into Theorem 4's assumptions.
3.  The proof of Theorem 4 does not seem to use the "similar" assumption.

Overall, the reviewer is unable to judge the main theoretical claim due to these confusions.
