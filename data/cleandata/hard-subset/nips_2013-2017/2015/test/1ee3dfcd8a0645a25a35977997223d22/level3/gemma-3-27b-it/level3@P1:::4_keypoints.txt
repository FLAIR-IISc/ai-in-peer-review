- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embedding for graph transduction plus an Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm for efficient computation.
- Builds on unit-sphere embedding literature and derives a PAC bound using the Lovász θ function instead of VC dimension.
- Core contribution: SPORE formulation with IIP algorithm that scales beyond SDP-based approaches to graphs with thousands of nodes.
- Extends the method to multiple graph transduction (multi-view) settings.
- Empirical results on UCI and MNIST show SPORE often outperforms baseline methods.
- Novel combination of spectral regularization, orthonormal embeddings, and a practical IIP optimization algorithm.
- Strong theoretical foundation via a PAC bound and connection to Lovász θ.
- IIP algorithm is a significant practical contribution enabling scalability.
- Multiple-graph extension is a valuable and natural direction for real-world multi-view data.
- Weak justification for choosing orthonormal embeddings specifically; more motivation needed versus other embeddings.
- Insufficient ablation studies to separate effects of spectral regularization from orthonormal embeddings.
- No quantitative analysis of IIP’s approximation accuracy—only convergence rate is given.
- Multiple-graph extension is described at a high level; lacks intuition, implementation details, and explanation of counterintuitive noisy-case results.
- Paper is dense, relies heavily on supplementary material, and uses heavy notation—reducing clarity and accessibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound framework; scalable IIP algorithm; promising empirical results and multi-graph extension.
- Arguments for rejection: weak embedding justification, missing ablations and approximation analysis, underdeveloped multi-graph treatment, and poor clarity.
- Overall: strong contribution but weakened by analysis and presentation gaps; needs more experiments and approximation quantification.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on added ablation studies, quantification of IIP approximation error, expanded multi-graph details and explanations, and improved clarity.
