- Proposes SPORE: a spectral-norm–regularized orthonormal embedding framework for graph transduction (acronym inconsistent with SEER in draft).
- Derives a PAC-style generalization bound tied to the Lovász theta θ(G), motivating spectral regularization and graph-structure–dependent labeled sample complexity.
- Poses SPORE as convex optimization over the elliptope and introduces an Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm using approximate projections (FISTA) with O(1/√T) convergence.
- Sketches an MKL-style extension for multiple graphs.
- Experiments on UCI/MNIST and synthetic multi-graph settings show gains over Laplacian baselines and scalability beyond standard SDP solvers.
- Positions work relative to Laplacian/spectral SSL, orthonormal representations/Lovász theta, elliptope/SDP methods, and inexact proximal algorithms.
- Strength: original theoretical bridge from PAC bound to convex spectral-regularized formulation via θ(G).
- Strength: IIP algorithm is elegant, broadly applicable with a well-motivated dual-criterion approximation and convergence analysis.
- Strength: Demonstrates practical scalability and promising empirical performance versus common graph SSL baselines.
- Strength: Multi-graph bound implies robustness when at least one view is “good,” consistent with MKL intuition.
- Weakness: Need stronger empirical justification for enforcing orthonormal/unit-sphere embeddings—request ablations (no spectral term; spectral vs trace/nuclear/∞-norm; orthonormal vs generic PSD kernels).
- Weakness: Missing evaluation of IIP approximation accuracy—compare to exact/SDP on small graphs to quantify objective gaps, solution distance, accuracy/runtime, and sensitivity to projection tolerance and inner FISTA iterations.
- Weakness: Multi-graph extension is high-level—requires explicit optimization details (variables, subgradients, proximal steps for max-λ1), computational cost, and intuition for η behavior.
- Weakness: Some experimental findings are unintuitive (single graph outperforming multiple graphs under noise); ask for analysis varying noise and number of graphs M to explain when the “one good graph” advantage fails.
- Weakness: Exposition dense and over-reliant on the supplement; recommend streamlining Section 4, define operators inline, and add a practical algorithm box with hyperparameter guidance and complexity.
- Pros summary: novel theory–algorithm link, general-purpose IIP, solid convergence analysis, promising results, scalability.
- Cons summary: missing ablations and approximation validation, thin multi-graph details, empirical inconsistencies, clarity/exposition issues.
- Overall: Novel and potentially impactful contribution but borderline accept—should address the above empirical, theoretical, and exposition concerns.
