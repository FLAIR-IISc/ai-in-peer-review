- Proposes learning orthonormal graph representations and provides a generalization bound linking spectral-norm penalization of the graph kernel to better generalization.  
- Introduces SPORE: a spectral-norm-regularized formulation that jointly optimizes embeddings and labels.  
- Presents an efficient inexact-proximal algorithm for SPORE that scales to graphs with thousands of nodes.  
- Experimental results on real and synthetic graphs show SPORE outperforms prior methods.  
- Reviewer finds the paper technically sound with well-supported claims, but has key clarity concerns.  
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 4) is not explained at a high level and is hard to interpret.  
- The claimed necessary assumption that “edges link similar instances” is neither clearly defined nor incorporated into Theorem 4.  
- The proof of Theorem 4 appears not to use the “similar” assumption, undermining the theoretical claim.  
- Because of these issues, the reviewer is unable to judge the main theoretical contribution.  
- Strengths cited: novelty of orthonormal representation approach, SPORE formulation, and scalable algorithm.  
- Weaknesses cited: unclear statement/use of assumptions in theory and potential gaps in the proof.  
- Suggestions: clarify and give a high-level explanation of Theorem 4, formally define and incorporate the “edges link similar instances” assumption, revise the proof to use that assumption, add experimental setup/dataset details, and expand theoretical analysis and limitations.
