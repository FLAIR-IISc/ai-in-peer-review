- Reduces semi-supervised learning to an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem building on Zhang and Ando.  
- Main theoretical contribution: error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.  
- Introduces a spectral-norm regularizer and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Proposes a proximal solver that handles cone–polytope intersection via an approximate projection step.  
- Presents an MKL extension enabling combination of multiple graphs/multi-view learning.  
- Experimental results on several datasets are convincing and show strong performance.  
- Paper is dense with many contributions, which may hamper readability and accessibility.  
- Recommend explicitly stating the correspondence between equations (3) and (1) to clarify the SSL→supervised reduction.  
- Some notation and equation formatting need fixes (e.g., equals sign in equation (5)).  
- Authors should discuss how to recover the embedding U from K and give practical factorization guidance.  
- Computational cost concern: ensuring K_r is PSD via eigendecomposition is expensive; suggest optimizing over factorizations (e.g., K = LL^T).  
- Quality: technically sound with well-supported claims and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Originality/significance: novel spectral-norm regularization, Lovász-number link, proximal solver, and MKL extension with potential high impact.
