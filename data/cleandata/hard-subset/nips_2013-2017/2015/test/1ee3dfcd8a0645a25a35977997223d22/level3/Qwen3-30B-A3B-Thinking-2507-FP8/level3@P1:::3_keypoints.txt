- Addresses optimal orthonormal graph embeddings and efficient computation for graph transduction, building on prior work that left embedding selection open.
- Identifies gap: previous consistency results lacked a practical method to choose or compute optimal embeddings.
- Main theoretical contribution: a new generalization bound (Theorem 1) linking prediction error to the spectral norm of the kernel matrix instead of VC dimension.
- Proposes spectral-norm-regularized formulation (SPORE, Eq.5) that minimizes a convex objective over the elliptope motivated by the new bound.
- Develops an Inexact Infeasible Proximal (IIP) algorithm to solve the non-smooth convex SPORE problem, avoiding O(n^6) SDP costs.
- IIP achieves O(1/√T) convergence and scales to graphs with thousands of nodes, where SDP-based methods fail beyond a few hundred vertices.
- Extends approach to multiple graphs (MKL-SPORE) with improved generalization bounds for multi-view settings.
- Theoretical novelty: spectral-norm regularization and PAC analysis avoid VC-dimension issues of prior work.
- Algorithmic innovation: practical and provably convergent projection method onto the elliptope.
- Practical impact: experiments show SPORE significantly outperforms baselines and scales to large graphs (2000+ nodes).
- Theoretical depth: connections to the Lovász θ function (Lemmas 2–3) illuminate graph-dependent transduction difficulty.
- Weaknesses: no principled method for choosing regularization parameter β (cross-validation used in experiments).
- Weaknesses: analysis assumes known graph structure and does not address noisy/uncertain graphs.
- Weaknesses: experiments focus mainly on binary classification; multi-class/regression tasks are not evaluated.
- Overall verdict: technically strong, well-written, significant practical impact, and recommended for acceptance.
