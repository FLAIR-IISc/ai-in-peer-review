- Problem: Studies graph transduction for orthonormal graph embeddings and how to select an optimal embedding from a large class.
- Theoretical contribution: Presents a novel PAC-style generalization bound for orthonormal representations that avoids VC-dimension and ties error to the spectral norm of the kernel.
- Graph-theoretic insight: Connects the spectral-norm-based bound to the Lovász θ function of the graph complement, motivating spectral regularization.
- Algorithmic contribution: Proposes SPORE, which minimizes empirical hinge loss plus a spectral-norm regularizer over the elliptope of valid graph kernels.
- Optimization method: Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal), a projected subgradient method using approximate projections to handle expensive feasible-set projections.
- Convergence: IIP is shown to converge at O(1/√T) despite inexact/approximate projections.
- Extensions: Provides a multi-graph extension (MKL-SPORE).
- Experiments: Convincing empirical results on real and synthetic datasets, outperforming baselines and demonstrating scalability on large graphs.
- Quality & significance: Paper is high-quality, technically sound, and provides principled justification for spectral regularization missing in prior work.
- Originality: Highly original—new PAC analysis, Lovász θ connection, and a novel inexact-projection optimization method.
- Clarity concerns: Paper is dense; some parts need clearer exposition for broader readership.
- Clarify reduction: Explicitly state that the transductive-to-supervised reduction treats the problem as supervised on labeled set S with sums only over labeled examples.
- Notation fix: Move the definition g(K)=ωC(K,yS)+βλ1(K) out of the min expression in Section 3 to improve readability.
- Embedding recovery: Clarify how (or whether) the orthonormal embedding U is recovered from the optimized kernel K* and practical factorization choices.
- IIP complexity: Provide more detailed complexity analysis—address cost of PSD projection (eigendecomposition) in step 8 and whether factorized optimization (K=LL^T) was considered to avoid repeated eigencomputations.
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando should be cited as the 2005 NIPS paper, not 2006.
