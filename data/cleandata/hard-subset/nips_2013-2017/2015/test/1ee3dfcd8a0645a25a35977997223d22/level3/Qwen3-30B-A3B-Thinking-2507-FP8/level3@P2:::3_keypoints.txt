- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal graph embeddings for graph transduction.  
- Presents a new generalization bound (Theorem 1) tying error to the spectral norm λ₁(K) instead of VC dimension.  
- Motivates SPORE formulation (eq. 5) by showing minimizing spectral norm improves generalization.  
- Introduces the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm to solve the non-smooth convex problem over the elliptope.  
- Proves IIP convergence rates of O(1/√T) (Theorems 5 and 6).  
- Demonstrates scalability: IIP handles graphs with thousands of nodes where SDPs fail.  
- Extends method to multiple-graph transduction (MKL-SPORE) with stronger theoretical guarantees.  
- Connects graph structure to sample complexity via the Lovász θ function and graph density (Theorem 4).  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over multiple baselines on small and large datasets (Tables 1–2).  
- Provides implementation details and Algorithm 1 to aid reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and maintains a strong theory–algorithm–experiment link.  
- Significance: offers a principled solution to optimal embedding choice and advances scalable graph transduction.  
- Minor weaknesses: requires tuning of hyperparameters (β, C) and could give more intuition why spectral-norm regularization outperforms others.  
- Suggests clarifying connections to related spectral graph theory work.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept.
