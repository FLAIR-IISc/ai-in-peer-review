- Reduces graph transduction to a kernel-based supervised learning problem via the Zhang & Ando formulation.  
- Main contribution: shows minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization with an error bound tied to the Lovász number.  
- Proposes SPORE, a practical algorithm solved with an inexact proximal method (IIP) to address SDP computational challenges.  
- Empirical results on standard and large-scale datasets show SPORE often outperforms Laplacian-based methods and K-scaling.  
- Extends the approach to multiple graph transduction using an MKL-style formulation.  
- Paper is ambitious and substantive but dense, which may reduce accessibility.  
- Connections to prior work are well-established; theoretical analysis is rigorous and experimental validation is convincing.  
- Clarification needed: explicitly state that the equivalence of Eqs. (3) and (1) constitutes a reduction to supervised learning on labeled samples.  
- Notation issue in Section 2: the “=” preceding ω_C in Eq. (5) is unclear and should be clarified.  
- Section 3 needs more detail on how the embedding U is recovered from the learned kernel K and practical factorization considerations.  
- Algorithmic concern: address computational cost of eigenvalue decompositions in steps 8–9 of Algorithm 1 and consider optimizing over a factorization (e.g., K = LL^T).  
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando reference should be NIPS 2005, not 2006.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, scalable solver, convincing empirical performance, and valuable multi-graph extension.  
- Arguments against acceptance: paper density and the need for clarifications to improve understanding and reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: significant advancement and worthy of acceptance pending the requested clarifications.
