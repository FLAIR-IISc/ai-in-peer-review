- Addresses learning optimal orthonormal graph embeddings for graph transduction, motivated by limitations of prior Laplacian-based bounds.  
- Derives a new PAC-style generalization bound that depends on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix λ1(K).  
- Proposes SPORE, a spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embedding formulation motivated by the new bound.  
- Develops an efficient, scalable Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm to solve the resulting non-smooth convex problem.  
- Extends the framework to a multiple-kernel learning variant (MKL-SPORE) for multi-view settings.  
- Novel shift from trace-based to spectral-norm regularization is technically sound and addresses an open question.  
- IIP algorithm is a significant practical contribution enabling scalability beyond SDP solvers.  
- Empirical results show substantial performance improvements, indicating likely high impact.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, theory, and algorithmic exposition.  
- Minor weakness: lacks deeper analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity for C and β.  
- Overall recommendation: strong acceptance; high-quality, novel, and impactful contribution.
