- Problem: Learning optimal graph embeddings for transduction via orthonormal representations (equivalent to PSD kernels with unit diagonal and graph non-edge sparsity).
- Theoretical contribution: New PAC-style generalization bound (Theorem 1) that depends on the kernel spectral norm λ1(K) instead of the trace.
- Methodological contribution: SPORE — a convex objective (Eq. 5) combining empirical hinge loss with spectral-norm regularization over the elliptope.
- Algorithmic contribution: IIP — a scalable infeasible inexact proximal (projected subgradient) method with approximate projection onto the elliptope and O(1/√T) convergence (Theorem 5).
- Extension: MKL-SPORE for multi-graph transduction, showing robustness to noisy graphs.
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrating significant performance gains over prior graph transduction methods and scalability to ~2000 nodes.
- Quality: High technical quality with rigorous theoretical support and a complete theory-to-algorithm-to-experiment pipeline.
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; notation and motivations are clear and accessible to experts.
- Originality: Novel spectral-norm-based generalization bound, SPORE formulation, and the IIP prox-approximation technique that is of independent interest.
- Significance: Principled, practical solution for kernel selection in graph semi-supervised learning with potential broader impact (e.g., max-cut relaxations, sensor localization).
- Strengths: Strong theoretical grounding, novel scalable algorithm with convergence guarantees, and state-of-the-art empirical results.
- Weaknesses: Reviewer reports no significant weaknesses.
- Arguments for acceptance: Theoretically grounded novel bound, principled convex formulation, scalable and novel optimization algorithm with guarantees, strong empirical performance, and excellent presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: Reviewer found none.
