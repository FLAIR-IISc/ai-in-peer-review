The authors propose an alternate convex optimization criterion (eqn. 5) involving λ1(K), which is nondifferentiable but convex. The paper also introduces an extension of SPORE, called MKL-SPORE, which is a multiple-kernel variant. The authors show that combining multiple graphs can improve the error convergence rate and labelled sample complexity.

The experiments presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of SPORE and MKL-SPORE on various tasks, including graph transduction and multiple graph transduction. The results show significant performance improvements over prior approaches.
