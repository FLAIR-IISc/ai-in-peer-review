- Problem: tackles graph transduction by learning graph-dependent kernels from orthonormal representations (unit-diagonal, zeros for non-adjacent vertices).
- Main theory: introduces a new PAC-style generalization bound that depends on the kernel's spectral norm rather than its trace.
- Insight: links generalization, spectral norm, and the Lovász θ number, explaining why denser graphs may be easier to learn.
- Proposal: SPORE — spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embedding objective motivated by the new bound.
- Optimization: formulates a convex but non-smooth problem over the elliptope and develops the IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) projected subgradient method.
- Scalability: IIP uses approximate projections to make the method practical for graphs with thousands of nodes.
- Extensions and empirical results: extends SPORE to multiple-graph learning and shows strong empirical performance across synthetic and real datasets, outperforming baselines up to 2000 nodes.
- Strength: provides a complete research story from novel theory to a practical, convergent first-order solver with significant empirical validation.
- Clarity issue: paper is dense; the supervised framing (eq. 3) and why sums are over labeled set S should be made more explicit.
- Embedding recovery: should discuss how to recover the orthonormal embedding U from the learned kernel K* (e.g., via eigendecomposition) and practical considerations.
- Computational concern: clarify cost of PSD projection (possible O(n^3) eigendecomposition), whether it dominates runtime, and consider alternatives like optimizing over low-rank factorization K=LL^T.
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando [19] is NIPS 2005, not 2006.
