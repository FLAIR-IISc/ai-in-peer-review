- Reduces graph transduction to a kernel-based supervised learning problem, building on Zhang & Ando.  
- Key theoretical insight: an error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization, leading to SPORE.  
- Links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Proposes an efficient infeasible inexact proximal (IIP) algorithm that scales to thousands of vertices vs. standard SDP.  
- Extends method to multiple graph transduction (MKL-SPORE) with convincing experimental results.  
- Strength: strong theoretical depth combined with practical applicability.  
- Novel connection between spectral regularization and the Lovász number addresses a gap in prior work.  
- IIP algorithm is well-motivated and demonstrates impressive scalability.  
- Experiments across multiple datasets validate the theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: paper is dense, packing several substantial contributions makes it difficult to follow.  
- Notational issue: Section 2 (eq.5) has a confusing/unnecessary equals sign before ω_C.  
- Clarify that correspondence between eqs (3) and (1) makes SSL a supervised problem (sums over S only).  
- Section 3 should explain how embedding U is recovered from K and discuss practical factorization choices.  
- Computational concern: ensuring PSD via eigendecomposition in Algorithm 1 (steps 8–9) is costly; optimizing over factorization K = L L^T may be more efficient.  
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando is NIPS 2005, not 2006.  
- Overall: high-quality paper whose strengths (novel theory, scalable algorithm, strong experiments) outweigh weaknesses; strong candidate for acceptance.
