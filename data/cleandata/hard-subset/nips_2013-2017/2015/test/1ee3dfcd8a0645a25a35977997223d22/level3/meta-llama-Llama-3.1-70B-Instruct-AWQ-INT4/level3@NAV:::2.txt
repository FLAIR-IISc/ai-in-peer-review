This paper presents a novel approach to learning vertex labels on graphs via joint embedding and label learning. The authors build upon the idea of Ando and Zhang that orthonormal unit-sphere embeddings improve generalization, but lacked a concrete embedding selection method. The main contribution of the paper is a new optimization formulation that jointly optimizes embedding and labels with a regularizer, specifically the spectral norm of the graph kernel.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their approach, including a generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings. They also derive a labelled sample complexity estimate, which is a significant improvement over existing analysis. The paper also presents an efficient algorithm, Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP), to solve the proposed formulation, which can scale to graphs with thousands of nodes.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, outperforming prior methods on various datasets. The authors also extend their approach to multiple graph transduction, showing improved performance over individual graphs and other combination methods.
