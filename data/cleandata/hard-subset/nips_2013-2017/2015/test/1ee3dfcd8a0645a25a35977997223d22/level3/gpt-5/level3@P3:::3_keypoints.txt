- Paper introduces SPORE: convex optimization over the elliptope to learn orthonormal graph embeddings by regularizing the maximum eigenvalue λ1(K).
- Derives a new high-probability PAC-style generalization bound depending on λ1(K), linking transductive error to spectral geometry and Lovász θ.
- Improves labeled sample complexity scaling to m̃/n = Ω((ϑ/n)1/2) for orthonormal embeddings, sharper than prior Ω((ϑ/n)1/3).
- Proposes MKL-SPORE: principled multi-graph extension using convex kernel combinations with per-graph max spectral-norm penalty and supporting bound.
- Develops an infeasible inexact proximal (IIP) method to handle non-differentiable λ1 and intersection of psd cone and affine constraints using prox-of-support-function and FISTA.
- Provides convergence guarantees (O(1/√T) inner rate) for the non-smooth optimization with approximate projections.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over Laplacian kernels and K-scaling on UCI, MNIST, and synthetic multi-graph datasets.
- Demonstrates scalability of the solver to graphs with up to ~2000 nodes and robustness to noisy graph views in multi-graph settings.
- Clear derivations, subgradient computations, stepsizes, complexity analysis, and thorough supplementary material.
- Strength: strong theoretical contribution connecting λ1(K) and θ, with interpretable dependence on graph density.
- Strength: novel convex criterion that operationalizes the bound and a principled multi-graph formulation.
- Strength: meaningful optimization contribution tackling an SDP-like problem with scalable first-order tools.
- Strength: solid empirical validation and explicit links between theory and algorithm.
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices (C, β) depend on θ(G) and θ(Ḡ), which are expensive to estimate in practice.
- Weakness: empirical baselines are limited to Laplacian variants and K-scaling; missing standard transductive methods and alternative optimizers.
- Weakness: demonstrated scale is only up to thousands of nodes; lacks discussion of memory footprint and large-scale extensions (≥10^5 nodes).
- Weakness: missing ablation on β and diagnostics showing how reductions in λ1(K) correlate with error improvements.
- Weakness: limited discussion on using smooth surrogates for λ1 and their empirical trade-offs.
- Weakness: minor notation/naming inconsistencies (SPORE) and need to clarify hinge vs. 0-1 loss in bounds/experiments.
- Quality: technically sound with proofs and a notable IIP convergence analysis for non-smooth objectives.
- Clarity: generally well-organized; suggestions include intuition box for prox trick and schematic of feasible set/projection geometry.
- Originality: novel combination of PAC bounds, spectral-norm regularization, and tailored inexact proximal approach; original multi-graph max spectral-norm penalty.
- Significance: advances theory and practice of graph transduction beyond Laplacian regularization and is likely to influence future work.
- Pros for acceptance: strong theory, principled convex criterion, nontrivial optimization contribution, solid empirical gains, and clear writing.
- Cons for acceptance: limited baseline diversity and runtime comparisons, dependence on hard-to-estimate θ, and limited demonstrated scalability.
- Questions for authors: propose practical proxies for θ to set C/β without CV; sensitivity of performance to β and relation between λ1(K) and generalization; adaptation of IIP to stochastic/block variants; runtime/accuracy comparison of IIP vs ADMM or Frank–Wolfe.
