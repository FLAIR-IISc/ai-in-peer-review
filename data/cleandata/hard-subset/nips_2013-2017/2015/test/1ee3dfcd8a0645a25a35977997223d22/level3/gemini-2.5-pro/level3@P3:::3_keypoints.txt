- Problem: learning optimal orthonormal graph embeddings for transduction; prior sphere-embedding work lacked a principled selection method.

- Key theoretical contribution: new PAC-style generalization bound depending on the spectral norm (maximum eigenvalue) of the kernel K (Theorem 1).

- Practical implication of bound: motivates minimizing empirical error plus the spectral norm of K when choosing embeddings.

- Deeper theory: connects the bound to the Lovász θ function and yields improved sample-complexity estimates.

- SPORE formulation: minimizes a convex combination of SVM hinge loss and the spectral norm over the elliptope of valid orthonormal representations (eqn. 5).

- Optimization challenge: objective includes a non-differentiable spectral norm and complex PSD+affine constraints, making SDP intractable for large graphs.

- Algorithmic solution: Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) — a novel, scalable first-order method using approximate projections with proven convergence.

- Extension: MKL-SPORE generalizes the framework to multiple graphs and comes with its own generalization bound.

- Empirical results: SPORE and MKL-SPORE significantly outperform Laplacian-based and kernel-scaling baselines on real-world and synthetic tasks.

- Strengths: strong theoretical soundness, originality (spectral-norm bound, SPORE, IIP), technical depth, clear presentation, and broad potential interest of IIP.

- Weakness — hyperparameters: recommended C and β rely on Lovász θ (computationally expensive); need sensitivity analysis or practical heuristics beyond cross-validation.

- Weakness — complexity details: complexity estimate uses cp and c0 but should explicitly state scaling in number of vertices n (e.g., principal eigenvector cost ~O(n^2) for dense matrices).

- Weakness — MKL-SPORE implementation: algorithmic details for handling the simplex variable η via IIP are deferred to the supplement and should be sketched in the main text.

- Overall verdict: minor clarifications needed but high-quality, original, and significant work; strong accept.
