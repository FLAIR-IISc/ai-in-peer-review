- Paper proposes SPORE: spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embedding for learning vertex labels on graphs, building on Ando & Zhang.
- Theorem 1 gives a PAC-style generalization bound motivating spectral-norm regularization.
- Introduces IIP, an efficient inexact proximal algorithm using FISTA for approximate projections to avoid expensive SDPs.
- IIP has convergence guarantees (O(1/√T)) and is designed to scale to large graphs.
- Experimental results show SPORE outperforms existing methods in accuracy and scalability; MKL-SPORE is robust to noisy graphs.
- Significant theoretical and algorithmic contributions with practical scalability, relevant to real-world applications.
- Theorem 4 is unclear: assumptions (e.g., definition of “similar” instances) and presentation are insufficient for evaluation.
- Paper presentation is dense and may be hard for non-experts; more intuition and simplified exposition are needed.
- Lacks detailed comparison to recent graph transduction methods, especially deep learning approaches.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying Theorem 4 and improving exposition to increase accessibility.
