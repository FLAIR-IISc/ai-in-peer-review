- Addresses graph transduction by improving label prediction via orthonormal embeddings and efficient computation.  
- Establishes a PAC learnability bound for orthonormal representations, filling a gap in sample-complexity analysis.  
- Derives a new generalization bound (Theorem 1) based on the Lovász θ function and the kernel matrix's maximum eigenvalue.  
- Proposes spectral norm regularization (SPORE) to balance empirical error and spectral penalties, motivated by the new bound.  
- Develops the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm as a scalable alternative to solving the SPORE SDP.  
- Provides O(1/√T) convergence analysis for the IIP method, supporting its theoretical soundness.  
- Extends the approach to multiple graphs (MKL-SPORE) with accompanying theoretical results.  
- Empirical evaluation shows performance improvements on UCI and MNIST datasets, supporting practical effectiveness.  
- Novelty: introduces a new spectral norm regularization criterion addressing limitations of prior methods.  
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation combining PAC analysis and spectral arguments.  
- Strength: algorithmic contribution (IIP) that improves scalability compared to SDPs.  
- Weakness: reliance on the Lovász θ function, which is computationally challenging and needs practical approximation discussion.  
- Weakness: insufficient ablation studies to isolate the specific impact of spectral norm regularization.  
- Weakness: sensitivity and practical selection of hyperparameters (β and C), especially in MKL, are underexplored.  
- Weakness: dense notation and many proofs relegated to the supplement reduce accessibility and readability.  
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions (clarify Lovász θ approximations and add ablation experiments).
