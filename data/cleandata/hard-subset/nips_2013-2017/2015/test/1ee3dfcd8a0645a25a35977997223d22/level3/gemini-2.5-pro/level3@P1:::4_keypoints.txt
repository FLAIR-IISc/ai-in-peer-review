- Addresses graph transduction by focusing on orthonormal representations and deriving a PAC-style generalization bound linking generalization to the spectral norm λ1(K) rather than VC dimension.  
- Proposes SPORE: learns an optimal kernel by minimizing the SVM objective plus a spectral norm regularizer over the elliptope of orthonormal representation kernels.  
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal), a scalable first-order projected subgradient method using approximate projections for intractable feasible sets.  
- Provides an O(1/√T) convergence guarantee for IIP in the inexact projection setting.  
- Applies IIP to SPORE where the feasible set is the intersection of the PSD cone and a polytope, enabling scalability to large graphs.  
- Extends SPORE to multiple graph transduction using an MKL-style approach to combine multiple graph views.  
- Experiments show SPORE outperforms Laplacian-based baselines and scales to graphs with thousands of nodes.  
- Strength: Theoretical justification for spectral norm regularization (Theorem 1) fills an open question about selecting optimal normalized graph kernels.  
- Strength: IIP is a novel, general, and elegant optimization contribution addressing inexact projection problems.  
- Strength: Practical scalability and superior classification accuracy demonstrated empirically.  
- Weakness: Paper is dense and relies heavily on the supplement, making it hard to read and not self-contained, especially the multi-graph section.  
- Weakness: Missing ablation studies to isolate the effect of spectral norm regularization versus optimizing over the elliptope alone.  
- Weakness: No empirical evaluation comparing IIP solutions to true SDP optima on small graphs to assess approximation quality.  
- Weakness: MKL-SPORE results are counter-intuitive (worse than best single graph in noisy case), contradicting theoretical expectations and needing explanation.  
- Pro-accept: Introduces a novel, theoretically-motivated framework (SPORE) and a significant optimization algorithm (IIP) with strong empirical results.  
- Con-accept: Exposition and experimental gaps (clarity, ablations, approximation evaluation, MKL inconsistency) reduce impact and require further work.
