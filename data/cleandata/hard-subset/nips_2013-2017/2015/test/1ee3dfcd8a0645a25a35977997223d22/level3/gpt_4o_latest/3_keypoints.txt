- (Strength) Strong theoretical advance: new PAC-based generalization bound using the Lovász θ function and spectral norm regularization for unit-sphere embeddings.
- (Strength) Novel optimization: formulates SPORE as a non-smooth convex problem over the elliptope and introduces IIP with inexact proximal updates and O(1/√T) convergence guarantees.
- (Strength) Scalability: IIP scales to graphs with thousands of vertices, overcoming SDP-based methods' computational limits.
- (Strength) Empirical validation: SPORE and MKL-SPORE show substantial accuracy, robustness to noise, and scalability improvements across synthetic and real datasets.
- (Strength) Clarity and reproducibility: well-organized exposition with supplementary proofs and implementation details.
- (Weakness) Dense presentation: PAC analysis and IIP algorithm sections are technically heavy and may be inaccessible to non-experts.
- (Weakness) Limited comparisons: lacks comprehensive evaluation against recent graph transduction/embedding methods, especially graph neural networks.
- (Weakness) Practical cost unclear: runtime and memory overhead of IIP (e.g., inner FISTA iterations) not thoroughly analyzed for very large graphs.
- (For acceptance) Substantial theoretical and practical contribution that advances graph transduction methodology.
- (For acceptance) Rigorous analysis and convincing empirical improvements over baselines.
- (For acceptance) Scalability of IIP addresses a key field limitation, making the work relevant for large-scale applications.
- (Against acceptance) Dense theoretical presentation may limit the paper’s accessibility and impact.
- (Against acceptance) Missing detailed runtime/memory analysis and comparisons with recent embedding techniques weaken practical evaluation.
- (Recommendation) Recommend acceptance: strengths outweigh minor weaknesses; paper is a high-quality contribution.
