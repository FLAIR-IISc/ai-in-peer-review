- Paper proposes spectral-norm-regularized selection of orthonormal representations (SPORE) for graph transduction, minimizing an SVM-style dual plus a spectral penalty over the elliptope.
- Main technical contributions: (i) high-probability generalization bound scaling with the kernel spectral norm (Theorem 1).
- (ii) PAC-style sample-complexity analysis for SPORE that improves prior OR-based transduction rates.
- (iii) An “infeasible inexact proximal” (IIP) projected subgradient algorithm using proximal support functions and FISTA to approximate projection onto PSD ∩ linear constraints with O(1/√T) rate.
- (iv) Extension to multi-graph (MKL-SPORE) with bounds that depend on the best graph/view.
- Builds on graph SSL, ORs/Lovász theta, Rademacher/transductive analysis, and projection/proximal methods; spectral-norm regularization is novel vs. trace/diagonal normalizations.
- IIP’s use of proximal support functions and FISTA is related to proximal splitting, but needs positioning against ADMM, Douglas–Rachford, and Chambolle–Pock.
- Strength: well-motivated convex SPORE formulation linking spectral norm to a complexity bound and θ-related graph-dependent analysis.
- Strength: thoughtful algorithm design and a reasonable convergence rate for non-smooth objectives.
- Strength: experiments show consistent gains over Laplacian-kernel baselines and illustrate multi-graph selection behavior.
- Major concern: incorrect use of θ(G) θ(Ḡ) = n — only an inequality holds in general, undermining Theorem 4’s stated bound.
- Concern: Lemma 3’s label-independent bound minK ωC(K,yS) ≤ θ(G)/2 omits label dependence and lacks a proof sketch; may be loose or misleading.
- Concern: Theorem 1’s transductive complexity argument and its scaling in m, n, λ1(K) are not clearly or fully derived; reported rate is unconventional and needs clarification.
- Algorithm–theory mismatch: Algorithm 1 projects iterates back to PSD for SVM calls, which may break the convergence analysis for A ∩ B — needs justification or a revised scheme.
- Experimental gaps: no runtime/iteration comparisons to SDP/ADMM/PDHG baselines, modest graph sizes (≤2000), and missing standard graph SSL baselines like label propagation/harmonic methods.
- Practicality issue: hyperparameters C, β depend on θ(G), θ(Ḡ) which are costly to compute; sensitivity analysis or simple proxies is needed.
- Related work coverage: optimization section should compare with established splitting/primal-dual methods and recent inexact proximal results.
- Overall: promising and original idea but theoretical precision (especially about Lovász theta), alignment between algorithm and analysis, and stronger empirical/optimization comparisons are required; reviewer recommendation: weak reject.
