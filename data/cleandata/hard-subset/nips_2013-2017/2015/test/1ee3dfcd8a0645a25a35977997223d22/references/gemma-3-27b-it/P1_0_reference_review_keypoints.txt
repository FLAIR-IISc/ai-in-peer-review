- Proposes SPORE: spectral norm regularization for orthonormal graph embeddings to address kernel/embedding selection in graph transduction.  
- Introduces an efficient Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm to solve the SPORE optimization without expensive SDP solvers.  
- Extends the method to multiple-graph (multi-view) transduction settings.  
- Provides theoretical results: a generalization bound (Theorem 1), PAC learnability analysis (Theorem 4), and convergence guarantees for IIP (Theorems 5 & 6).  
- Claims improved sample complexity over prior orthonormal-embedding work (Shivanna & Bhattacharyya, 2023).  
- Empirically validated on UCI and MNIST datasets and scaled experiments up to 2000 nodes, showing competitive performance versus baselines.  
- Grounded in prior literature on graph transduction, PAC learning, orthonormal embeddings, proximal algorithms, and multi-view learning.  
- Strength: novel, theoretically grounded formulation that links spectral norm regularization to kernel selection.  
- Strength: algorithmic innovation—practical, convergent IIP method avoiding SDPs.  
- Strength: valuable extension to multiple-graph scenarios and clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Weakness: IIP implementation and tuning appear complex; inner FISTA iterations and parameter choices need clearer guidance.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (β, C) not fully analyzed beyond cross-validation.  
- Weakness: PAC analysis depends on graph-structure assumptions; robustness to assumption violations is not discussed.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to recent graph neural network (GNN) methods.  
- Weakness: theoretical gaps—PAC derivation relies on lemmas with only sketch proofs; full derivations are needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting more implementation details, sensitivity analysis, GNN comparison, and full PAC proofs in the supplement.  
- Confidence of reviewer: 8/10.
