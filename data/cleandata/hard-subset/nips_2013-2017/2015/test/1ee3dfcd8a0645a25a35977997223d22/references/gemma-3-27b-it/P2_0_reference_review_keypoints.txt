- Proposes SPORE: minimize empirical error plus kernel spectral norm for orthonormal graph embeddings to improve graph transduction.
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) algorithm to solve the non-smooth convex SPORE problem efficiently.
- Provides theoretical results: generalization bounds, PAC learnability, improved sample complexity, and links to Lovász θ.
- Extends method to multiple graphs via an MKL-style approach with accompanying theory.
- Empirically validates SPORE and IIP on benchmark single- and multi-graph datasets, showing scalability on larger graphs.
- Well-motivated problem that builds on recent theoretical work about orthonormal representations.
- Strong theoretical contributions, notably Theorem 1 and PAC/sample complexity improvements over prior work.
- Novel algorithmic contribution with convergence guarantees for the challenging projection step.
- MKL extension increases practical applicability and theoretical depth.
- Clear writing and organization with consistent notation and logical flow.
- IIP algorithm may be complex to implement and tune, relying on parameters like sigma and epsilon.
- Limited empirical comparison against stronger recent baselines (e.g., deep learning or advanced kernel methods).
- Experimental scope restricted to standard datasets; more diverse graph types and features would strengthen claims.
- Hyperparameter selection details (ranges, cross-validation setup) are insufficiently described.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and potentially significant but would benefit from stronger empirical evaluation and implementation details.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve empirical breadth and clarify practical IIP/hyperparameter guidance.
