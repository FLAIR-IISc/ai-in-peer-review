- Paper studies spectral-norm-regularized convex optimization over the elliptope for graph transduction and links generalization to λ1(K) and Lovász θ.  
- Theorem 1’s spectral complexity term lacks explicit 1/√m decay; dependence on m and constants need clarification and possible tightening.  
- Theorem 4 contains inconsistent parameter choices for β (algebra/scale mismatch); full derivation and corrected statement required.  
- Central lemmas (max λ1(K)=θ(Ḡ) and min ωC ≤ θ(G)/2) need short proofs or precise citations in the main text.  
- IIP projection via prox of σX and Moreau identity is interesting, but implementational details (proxσA, proxσB) must be explicitly stated.  
- Position the IIP method relative to existing inexact proximal and projection algorithms (Dykstra, Douglas–Rachford, ADMM, PDHG) and clarify unique advantages.  
- Provide justified complexity constants (L, R, R̂), stopping criteria, and report wall-clock/runtime comparisons against SDPs and first-order baselines.  
- Graph construction in experiments is ad-hoc (RBF mean threshold, fixed cosine threshold); sensitivity to thresholds/σ and kNN alternatives should be studied.  
- Include stronger baselines such as label propagation, local/global consistency, and orthonormal/θ-based methods; add ablations (no λ1, nuclear norm).  
- Clarify how hyperparameters C and β are selected using only labeled data; report more trials and standard deviations (more than 5 for UCI).  
- MKL-SPORE experimental details are thin: specify optimization procedure (alternating updates?), regularization on η, and convergence behavior; test on real multi-graph datasets.  
- Paper is dense with typos and inconsistent citations; clean up references and unify citation style.  
- Move key derivations (esp. Theorem 4) to main text and present a compact algorithm box for IIP with explicit prox formulas.  
- Clarify Theorem 1 assumptions (loss, margin) and provide transparent constants and m-dependence in statements.  
- Originality: novel combination of spectral-norm regularization over elliptope tied to θ(G) and a dual-accuracy inexact proximal scheme, though parts build on known proximal splitting.  
- Significance: could influence orthonormal embedding choices and multi-graph transduction if theoretical and algorithmic issues are addressed.  
- Pros: clear motivation for spectral-norm regularization, convex elliptope formulation linked to θ, practical first-order algorithm with convergence guarantee, multi-graph extension.  
- Cons: theoretical inconsistencies (Theorems 1 and 4), obscured optimization/prox details, loose complexity constants, limited baselines and sensitivity analyses.  
- Recommendation: weak reject pending corrections to Theorems 1 and 4, clearer optimization/prox operators, and improved experimental methodology; likely accept if addressed.
