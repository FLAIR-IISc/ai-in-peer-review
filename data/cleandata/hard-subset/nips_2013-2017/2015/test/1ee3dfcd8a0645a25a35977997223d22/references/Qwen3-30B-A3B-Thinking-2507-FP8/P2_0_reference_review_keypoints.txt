- Studies graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings that place graph vertices on a unit sphere.  
- Theorem 1: low spectral norm orthonormal embeddings yield improved generalization bounds over prior work.  
- Proposes SPORE: a convex spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embedding formulation over the elliptope.  
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) algorithm to solve SPORE efficiently with O(1/√T) convergence.  
- Extends to multiple graph transduction via MKL-SPORE, combining multiple graph views.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorously proven, including generalization, sample complexity, and convergence results.  
- Experiments on UCI and MNIST show SPORE consistently outperforms baselines, especially on larger graphs.  
- Authors acknowledge SDP scalability issues and address them with the IIP algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and provides clear notation and reproducible method details.  
- Connection to Lovász ϑ function is mentioned but not fully exploited in main theorems.  
- Lacks discussion comparing the method to recent graph neural network approaches (e.g., GCNs, GATs).  
- Technical components (e.g., inexact proximal method) would benefit from a simple illustrative example.  
- Novelty: SPORE is a new spectral-norm regularization approach for orthonormal embeddings.  
- Novelty: IIP algorithm and the analysis of inexact proximal methods for this setting are original.  
- Novelty: MKL-SPORE and its improved PAC bounds for multiple graph views are a meaningful extension.  
- Significance: Addresses an important problem with applications in web classification, bioinformatics, and social networks.  
- Significance: Theorem 4 gives improved sample complexity over prior work when ϑ = o(n).  
- Significance: IIP scales to graphs with thousands of vertices, mitigating prior computational limits.  
- Significance: Multiple-graph extension is relevant for multi-view learning and shows robustness to noisy graphs.  
- Overall: Strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; recommendation to accept.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, novel SPORE formulation, scalable IIP algorithm, strong empirical gains, improved sample complexity.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to GNNs, underutilized Lovász ϑ connection, and limited context versus state-of-the-art graph representation learning.
