- Proposes SPORE: a convex formulation that explicitly penalizes the spectral norm of the kernel to learn optimal orthonormal embeddings.  
- Provides PAC learnability analysis and a new generalization bound, improving sample complexity over prior work.  
- Introduces IIP: an Infeasible Inexact Proximal projected subgradient method with an approximate projection step and convergence guarantees for scalability.  
- Extends the framework to multiple graphs (MKL-SPORE) and demonstrates benefits on synthetic multi-graph data.  
- Empirically evaluated on UCI and MNIST, including large-scale (2000-node) experiments showing scalability and competitiveness.  
- Correctly positions work relative to prior orthonormal embedding and proximal algorithm literature, emphasizing learning embeddings rather than using fixed ones.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation linking spectral norm regularization to generalization via Lovász θ.  
- Strength: novel convex optimization viewpoint explicitly optimizing spectral norm.  
- Strength: practical algorithm with proven convergence that bridges theory and implementation.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation, including multi-graph scenarios.  
- Weakness: IIP algorithm description is dense and relies on supplementary material; needs clearer exposition and pseudocode.  
- Weakness: parameter sensitivity (C, β, α) is handled by cross-validation but lacks theoretical guidance.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to modern graph kernels (e.g., Weisfeiler-Lehman, GCNs) in larger-scale experiments.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on the Lovász θ function, which can be computationally expensive in practice.  
- For acceptance: addresses a fundamental open problem with significant theoretical and practical contributions.  
- Against acceptance: algorithm complexity and θ dependence may limit adoption; comparisons could be more comprehensive.  
- Overall: strong paper with major contributions; recommendation is acceptance.  
- Recommendations: simplify IIP explanation and include pseudocode; analyze parameter sensitivity; compare to more state-of-the-art kernels; discuss practical estimation/bounding of Lovász θ.
