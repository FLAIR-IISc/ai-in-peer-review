- Paper proposes SPORE: convex kernel selection over graph elliptope with spectral-norm penalty and MKL extension, optimized by an “infeasible inexact proximal” (IIP) projected subgradient method; shows empirical gains over Laplacian baselines.

- Theoretical claims: generalization bound depending on λ1(K) (Theorem 1), PAC-style labeled-sample complexity tied to Lovász θ, and multi-graph bounds.

- Connects to prior work on Laplacian-based SSL, kernel learning with structural constraints, orthonormal graph representations and θ, and inexact proximal methods.

- Strength: clear motivation isolating spectral norm as a generalization factor and unified convex SPORE formulation with simple subgradients.

- Strength: PAC analysis linking labeled-sample complexity to graph structure via θ and an intuitive multi-graph extension.

- Strength: empirical improvements on UCI/MNIST graphs and synthetic mixtures; MKL-SPORE performs well in experiments.

- Major theoretical concern: repeated use of θ(G) θ(Ḡ) = n is incorrect in general (only ≥ n); Theorem 4 and parameter choices appear to rely on equality.

- Major concern: Lemma 2 claiming maxK∈K(G) λ1(K) = θ(Ḡ) is not standard and lacks rigorous proof or proper reference.

- Major concern: Lemma 3 claiming minK∈K(G) ωC(K,yS) ≤ θ(G)/2 independent of C and yS is strong and nonstandard, needs detailed derivation.

- Loss/duality mismatch: Theorem 1 uses squared hinge loss but bounds and dual ωC(K,yS) appear tied to standard hinge/L2-SVM dual; consistency must be clarified.

- Practicality issue: Theorem 4 requires C and β depending on θ and θ̄, but θ is SDP-hard to compute—no efficient proxies or practical parameter-setting guidance provided.

- Optimization novelty unclear: IIP’s contribution relative to existing inexact-prox and projection-free SDP methods (Frank–Wolfe/Hazan, ADMM, etc.) is not established and lacks comparisons.

- Complexity statements incomplete: iteration counts given but no explicit ε-dependence or comparison of complexity to alternatives.

- Technical gaps: key proximal derivations (prox of σ over S+ and affine mask) and other proofs are deferred to supplement; some prox/support-function claims require careful justification.

- Experimental gaps: missing strong SSL baselines (label propagation, manifold regularization), projection-free SDP solvers, ablation on spectral penalty β, sensitivity to graph construction, runtime/memory and rank analysis.

- Practical disconnect: experiments tune parameters by cross-validation rather than using theoretically prescribed C, β, weakening the theory–practice link.

- Notational and clarity issues: confusing use of Sn notation, missing proofs and key derivations in main text, minor typos and missing references.

- Acceptance pros: well-motivated convex formulation, interesting PAC link to θ, scalable first-order method with promising empirical gains, multi-graph extension.

- Acceptance cons: core theoretical claims hinge on questionable θ identities/lemmas, unclear optimization novelty, and insufficient experimental baselines and analyses.

- Key author actions requested: provide rigorous proofs or references for Lemmas 2–3 and fix θ usage; clarify hinge vs squared-hinge loss; give computable proxies for θ to set C,β; compare to projection-free SDP methods and add runtimes/ablations; include explicit prox formulas and proof sketches in main text.

- Overall verdict: promising direction but substantial theoretical, experimental, and clarity issues need correction before acceptance.
