- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; theoretically strong and technically sound contribution.
- Problem addressed: graph transduction using orthonormal representations and the challenge of choosing an optimal embedding.
- Main idea: propose SPORE — spectral norm regularization of the kernel to select suitable orthonormal embeddings.
- Theoretical contributions: rigorous PAC learnability bounds and improved sample complexity over prior work.
- Algorithmic contribution: IIP, an efficient first-order algorithm with inexact proximal computations to scale beyond SDP solvers.
- Convergence result: O(1/√T) convergence guarantee for IIP.
- Multi-graph extension: MKL-SPORE extends SPORE to multiple graphs and multi-view settings.
- Empirical results: experiments on benchmark and synthetic datasets show promising performance over baselines.
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees, novel formulation, practical algorithm, multi-graph extension, and empirical validation.
- Weakness: paper is dense and hard to read; needs clearer intuition and flow.
- Weakness: experimental setup lacks detail on baseline parameter choices and hyperparameter selection for SPORE.
- Weakness: scalability still a concern for very large graphs; runtime complexity limitations should be discussed.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis depends on graph-structure assumptions (e.g., power-law, random graphs) and may not generalize.
- Question: sensitivity of generalization bound to choice of loss function and implications of different losses.
- Question: clarify the link between spectral norm minimization and approximating the Lovász theta function.
- Question: compare IIP's convergence to standard proximal methods and quantify the impact of the approximation parameter ε.
- Question: detail how MKL-SPORE combines predictions from multiple graphs and whether tuning was used.
- Experimental request: provide kernel parameter choices for baselines and confidence intervals (e.g., standard deviations) for accuracy.
