- Proposes SPORE: spectral norm regularization for orthonormal representations in graph transduction.  
- Introduces IIP: an "Infeasible Inexact Proximal" algorithm for scalable SPORE optimization with convergence guarantees.  
- Extends the approach to multiple-graph transduction (MKL-SPORE) with algorithmic and generalization results.  
- Strong theoretical foundation drawing on PAC learning, spectral graph theory, and convex optimization; provides generalization bounds (Theorems 1 & 4).  
- Novelty: uses spectral norm regularization to principledly guide the choice of orthonormal embeddings.  
- IIP is a practical, scalable alternative to solving the SPORE SDP, with proven convergence rate.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets show consistent improvements over baselines; large-scale tests validate IIP’s utility.  
- Paper is generally well written and supported by extensive supplementary material.  
- Presentation is dense and notation-heavy, demanding strong background knowledge.  
- Practical parameter tuning (C, β) lacks detailed guidance beyond cross-validation.  
- Impact of IIP’s approximation quality (epsilon) on empirical performance is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Baseline comparisons are limited (Un-Lap, N-Lap, KS); missing comparisons to recent state-of-the-art methods (e.g., GNNs).  
- Synthetic datasets may not fully reflect real-world graph complexity.  
- Overall technically sound with convincing experiments and rigorous analysis.  
- Contribution is original and significant for embedding selection and scalable graph transduction methods.  
- For acceptance: strong theory, novel formulation and algorithm, and compelling empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: dense presentation, limited modern baselines, and missing sensitivity analysis of IIP.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; suggest simplifying notation, adding comparisons to recent methods, conducting epsilon sensitivity analysis, and providing practical tuning guidelines.
