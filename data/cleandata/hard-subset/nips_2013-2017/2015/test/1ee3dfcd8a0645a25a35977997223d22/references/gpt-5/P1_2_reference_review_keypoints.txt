- Paper proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized kernel learning over the elliptope for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings.
- Theorem 1: high-probability generalization bound for SVM-like transduction highlighting dependence on the spectral norm λ1(K).
- Connects feasible-set extremal spectral norms to Lovász theta: maxK∈K(G) λ1(K) = θ(Ḡ) and relates min spectral quantities to θ(G)/2.
- Derives error bound er = O((1/m (√(n θ(G)) + log(1/δ)))1/2) and improves labeled sample complexity to m̃/n = Ω((θ/n)1/2) under stated choices.
- Formulates convex SPORE objective minK∈K(G) ωC(K,yS) + β λ1(K) over PSD cone with affine constraints.
- Develops an Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) projected subgradient method using FISTA-approximated proximal projections to scale beyond SDPs.
- Proves O(1/√T) convergence for the inexact proximal scheme and shows composition rules for proximal operators over intersections.
- Extends SPORE to multi-graph settings via MKL-style combination with a max spectral penalty; bound depends on mink θ(G(k)).
- Empirically outperforms Laplacian-kernel baselines and K-scaling on UCI and MNIST (500–2000 nodes) and shows robustness in synthetic noisy multi-graph tests.
- Builds on prior graph transduction, orthonormal embedding consistency, and Lovász theta literature, improving PAC rates via Rademacher complexity and spectral penalty.
- Strength: clear conceptual insight that generalization depends on λ1(K), motivating spectral-norm regularization.
- Strength: strong theoretical contributions tying bounds to θ(G), θ(Ḡ) and improving sample complexity rates.
- Strength: optimization contribution of an inexact proximal framework with practical FISTA-based decomposition and closed-form proxes for PSD/affine sets.
- Strength: solid empirical gains and demonstrated scalability to a few thousand nodes, including multi-graph robustness.
- Weakness: PAC parameter choices for C and β depend on θ(G)/θ(Ḡ), which are hard to compute and make learnability non-constructive in practice.
- Weakness: Theorem 4 relies on informal graph similarity assumptions—needs precise statements (e.g., stochastic block/homophily) and intuitive derivation of the unusual rate.
- Weakness: Lemma 3’s claim minK ωC(K,yS) ≤ θ(G)/2 is strong and needs clarification on dependence (uniform vs worst-case labels, role of m,C).
- Weakness: proximal operator feasibility and closed-form derivations are central but largely in the supplement; move key derivations into main text.
- Weakness: experiments lack wall-clock runtimes, eigendecomposition counts, comparison to fast SDP baselines, and ablations on β (including β=0).
- Weakness: limited baselines—add label propagation and possibly GNN transductive baselines to better situate results.
- Weakness: sensitivity to graph construction thresholds and hyperparameters (β,C) not sufficiently explored; multi-graph alternatives not compared.
- Presentation: paper is dense, relies heavily on supplement, has incomplete references and minor typos; notation and proof sketches should be tightened in the main text.
- Suggestions: provide practical θ surrogates (e.g., degree/eigenvalue-based), runtime/memory plots, β and graph-threshold ablations, visualization of learned spectra/embeddings, and expanded baselines.
- Overall: novel, principled, and theoretically interesting work with practical optimization advances; reviewer leans weak accept subject to stronger empirical comparisons and clarification of technical details.
