- Introduces SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction to improve label prediction on unlabeled vertices.
- Proves a new high-probability generalization bound (Theorem 1) linking spectral norm regularization to better generalization.
- Proposes the IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) algorithm to efficiently solve the SPORE optimization, with O(1/√T) convergence (Theorem 5).
- Extends the approach to multi-graph settings via an MKL-style framework (MKL-SPORE).
- Builds on prior work [16] by addressing optimal embedding choice and improving sample complexity from Ω(ϑ/n)^{1/3} to Ω(ϑ/n)^{1/2}.
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and the PAC learning results show orthonormal embeddings are efficiently learnable for many graph families (e.g., power-law, random graphs).
- Experimental results (Tables 1-4) are comprehensive and show SPORE outperforming existing methods on small and large graphs and in multi-graph/noisy settings.
- IIP algorithm is theoretically sound and offers a practical alternative to SDP solvers for scalability.
- The connection between Lovász ϑ and spectral norm (Lemma 2) is stated without sufficient intuition for readers.
- Computational complexity analysis of IIP is vague (mentions "T^{3/2}·c_p + T·c_0") and lacks concrete comparisons or scaling of constants.
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (C and β) is not thoroughly analyzed beyond cross-validation guidance.
- Paper is well-organized, with clear notation and reproducible experimental setup.
- Some key proofs (e.g., Theorem 1) are in the supplement, which may hinder accessibility for readers who don't consult it.
- The benefit of spectral norm regularization could use more intuitive explanation beyond the formal bound.
- Novel contributions include PAC analysis for orthonormal embeddings, the SPORE formulation, and the IIP solver.
- The MKL-SPORE extension is a valuable, practical contribution for multi-view learning.
- The IIP algorithm is novel but not sufficiently contrasted with other inexact proximal methods in the literature.
- The paper does not fully explore how SPORE differs from other spectral-norm-regularized graph-learning approaches.
- Significance: provides a theoretically grounded, efficient method with meaningful sample-complexity improvements and practical scalability gains.
- Limitations: unclear scaling to extremely large graphs or other graph types, and the practical impact of the sample-complexity improvement is not concretely illustrated.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical and algorithmic contributions; minor issues do not detract from its quality.
- Recommendation: Accept.
