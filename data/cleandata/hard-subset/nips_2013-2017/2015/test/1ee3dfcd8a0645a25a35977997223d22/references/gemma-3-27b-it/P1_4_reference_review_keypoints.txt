- Overall: Strong accept — novel SPORE regularization with solid theory and a practical IIP solver; experiments promising but limited.  
- Problem/summary: Addresses graph transduction by selecting optimal orthonormal embeddings via spectral norm regularization (SPORE) and connects to Lovász θ.  
- Algorithm: Proposes the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm to solve SPORE for larger graphs.  
- Theory: Provides rigorous analysis including generalization bounds (Theorem 1) and PAC learnability (Theorem 4) with improved sample complexity over prior work.  
- Regularization novelty: Spectral norm regularization offers a compelling geometric interpretation (unit-sphere embedding) for choosing embeddings.  
- Multiple graphs: Extends method to multiple graph transduction (MKL-SPORE), demonstrating versatility.  
- Practicality/convergence: IIP is well-motivated with convergence analysis (Theorem 5), enabling scalability beyond SDPs.  
- Writing: Generally clear and well-organized with consistent notation.  
- Experiments weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited to small datasets and mostly Laplacian baselines; needs larger, diverse real-world graphs and scalability tests.  
- Parameter sensitivity: Lacks detailed analysis or guidance on choosing hyperparameters (C, β) and their sensitivity.  
- IIP clarity: Description and supplementary details of IIP need clearer step-by-step pseudocode and exposition.  
- Computational cost: Cost analysis is insufficiently explicit about scaling with graph size.  
- Quality/originality/significance: High technical quality, original combination of ideas, and significant potential impact for future work.  
- Acceptance pros: Strong theory, novel regularizer, practical solver, promising results, and MKL extension.  
- Acceptance cons: Experimental scope, minor clarity issues in IIP, and parameter sensitivity discussion need improvement.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify algorithm/parameter details.
