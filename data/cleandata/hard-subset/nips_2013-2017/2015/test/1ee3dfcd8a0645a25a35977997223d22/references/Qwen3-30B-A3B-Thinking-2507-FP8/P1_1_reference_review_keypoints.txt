- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction with convex formulation over the elliptope.
- Provides PAC learning analysis showing orthonormal representations are efficiently PAC learnable for many graph families.
- Derives a generalization bound linking spectral norm of the kernel to prediction error (Theorem 1).
- Improves PAC sample complexity for SPORE (Theorem 4) over prior work [16].
- Develops an Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm to solve SPORE with O(1/√T) convergence.
- Extends the method to multiple graph transduction via an MKL-style approach.
- Experimental results show consistent improvements over baselines on several datasets, including 2000-node graphs.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous with detailed proofs in the supplement.
- The IIP algorithm offers a practical scalable solution for large graphs.
- The connection between spectral norm and graph structure beyond Lovász ϑ is underdeveloped.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to standard benchmarks (UCI, MNIST); lacks more challenging/complex graphs.
- Computational complexity claims for IIP are vague; constants in the cost estimate are not specified.
- Sensitivity to key hyperparameters (C and β) is not thoroughly analyzed.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and uses consistent notation.
- Link between theoretical guarantees and practical implementation needs clearer exposition.
- Some technical derivations in the supplement could be more accessible to non-experts.
- Minor typos (e.g., "orhonormal") should be corrected.
- Core idea of orthonormal representations builds on prior work, so novelty is incremental in that aspect.
- IIP adapts existing inexact proximal methods, limiting the algorithmic novelty.
- Paper addresses an important problem with potential real-world impact and improved theoretical foundations.
- Lacks clear comparison to other spectral-based regularization methods (e.g., Laplacian regularization) and recent methods like GNNs.
- Recommendation: accept, subject to addressing intuition, empirical scope, and clarity issues.
- Suggested improvements: add intuition/visualizations for spectral-norm benefits, expand datasets and sensitivity studies, clarify IIP complexity constants, relate to other spectral methods, fix typos, and compare to recent graph transduction approaches.
