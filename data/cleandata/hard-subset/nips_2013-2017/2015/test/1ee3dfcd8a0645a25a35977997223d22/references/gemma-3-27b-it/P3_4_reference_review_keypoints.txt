- Overall: Strong theoretical contribution (SPORE + IIP) for graph transduction; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem & approach: Addresses label prediction on partially labelled graphs by regularizing the spectral norm of orthonormal embeddings, deriving SPORE from tighter PAC bounds and solving it with a convex formulation and the IIP algorithm.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on and tightens existing generalization/sample-complexity results (e.g., [16]), contrasts with Laplacian and kernel-scaling methods, and adds an MKL-style multi-graph extension.  
- Strength — theory: Provides a solid theoretical foundation (Theorems 1 & 4) giving generalization guarantees for spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embeddings.  
- Strength — algorithm: Proposes a novel IIP algorithm with a theoretical convergence guarantee (Theorem 5) that avoids scaling issues of direct SDPs.  
- Strength — extensions & analysis: Includes a useful multiple-graph extension and improved sample complexity estimates, with thorough supplementary proofs.  
- Weakness — accessibility: Paper is very dense with heavy notation and requires substantial background, reducing accessibility.  
- Weakness — experiments: Empirical evaluation uses limited datasets and lacks deeper analysis (e.g., embedding visualizations, broader real-world benchmarks).  
- Weakness — algorithm practicality: IIP appears complex to implement and the computational overhead of inexact proximal steps is not well characterized.  
- Weakness — hyperparameters: Practical guidance on tuning important parameters (C, β, α) and sensitivity analysis is insufficient.  
- Specific question: Justify or analyze sensitivity of β = ϑ(G)/ϑ(Ḡ).  
- Specific question: Clarify trade-offs and choices related to the approximation criterion R̂²/S² in Eq.(11).  
- Specific question: Provide computational-cost estimate per iteration of Algorithm 1, including the FISTA step.  
- Specific question: Explain the impact or learning strategy for ηk in MKL-SPORE (Eq.(17)).  
- Specific question: Report runtime of IIP on larger datasets (Table 2).  
- For acceptance: Significant theoretical advance, novel regularizer, provably sound algorithm, better bounds, and multi-graph capability.  
- Against acceptance: High technical density, limited empirical validation, and potentially prohibitive implementation complexity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on improving clarity, expanding experiments (including runtimes and sensitivity analyses), and addressing the listed specific questions.
