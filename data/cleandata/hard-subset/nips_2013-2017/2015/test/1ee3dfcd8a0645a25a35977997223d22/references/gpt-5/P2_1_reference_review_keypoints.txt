- Proposal: Introduces SPORE — select orthonormal graph embeddings by minimizing a convex trade-off between the SVM dual objective and the kernel spectral norm.  
- Theoretical contributions: high-probability generalization bound depending on λ1(K), PAC-style sample complexity bounds via Lovász theta (ϑ(G),ϑ(Ḡ)), an IIP optimization algorithm, and an MKL extension with improved bounds.  
- Theory strength: Clear connection from complexity term to λ1(K) and to ϑ(Ḡ); lemmas relating λ1 and ϑ are plausible and the final error bound yields improved labeled sample complexity.  
- Theory concerns: Theorem 1’s complexity term lacks an explicit 1/√m unless C is tied to m; dimensional inconsistency in m̃ derivation and missing ε-dependence; practical tuning requires ϑ(G)/ϑ(Ḡ) which needs SDPs.  
- Optimization strength: IIP is well-motivated using Moreau’s identity and infimal convolution, with a FISTA inner loop and sound inexact-projection criterion and clear complexity characterization.  
- Optimization suggestion: Compare IIP numerically to Dykstra/ADMM and other projection schemes.  
- Empirics strength: SPORE and MKL-SPORE outperform Laplacian baselines on UCI/MNIST and synthetic multi-graph experiments up to 2000 nodes.  
- Empirics concerns: Need convergence plots, wall-clock times, sensitivity to β, ablations removing spectral-norm term, and comparisons to stronger graph SSL baselines (label propagation/harmonic functions) and modern methods.  
- Clarity: Paper is dense; key identities (e.g., σA∩B via infimal convolution) and proof sketches (Theorem 1) should be included in the main text; fix typos/notation (C, m̃).  
- Originality: Novel synthesis of spectral-norm regularization on the elliptope tied to Lovász theta for PAC rates; IIP for inexact projections is thoughtful though related to existing literature.  
- Significance: Clarifies how graph structure via ϑ controls transductive complexity and provides reusable optimization for elliptope constraints, but practical impact limited by parameter tuning and incomplete empirical/runtimes.  
- Pros: Improved graph-aware generalization analysis, convex operationalization via spectral norm, implementable inexact-projection algorithm with guarantees, and MKL extension with theory and robust experiments.  
- Cons: Clarity and typographical issues, reliance on unknown ϑ for parameter selection, incremental optimization-rate novelty, limited comparison to alternative projections and stronger baselines, and relatively small-scale experiments without ablations or runtimes.  
- Questions/suggestions: Provide practical ways to set C/β without ϑ, correct sample-complexity derivation with ε-dependence, add Dykstra/ADMM and label-propagation baselines, report runtimes/convergence, and include ablations and scalability/sparsity discussions.  
- Overall assessment: Solid theoretical and algorithmic contribution to graph transduction with orthonormal embeddings; recommend acceptance conditional on clarifications, corrected derivations, stronger empirical comparisons, and practical tuning guidance.
