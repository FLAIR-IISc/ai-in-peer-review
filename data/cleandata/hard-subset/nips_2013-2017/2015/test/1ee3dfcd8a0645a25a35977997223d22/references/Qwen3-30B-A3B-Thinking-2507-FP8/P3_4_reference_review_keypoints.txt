- Paper proposes spectral-norm regularization of orthonormal graph embeddings with four contributions: PAC analysis, SPORE formulation, IIP solver, and MKL-SPORE extension.

- Theoretical contribution: strong PAC/Rademacher-based generalization bounds and improved sample complexity; connects orthonormal embeddings to kernel matrices (K = U^T U).

- Algorithmic innovation: IIP algorithm adapted for SPORE with a practical approximation criterion and O(1/√T) convergence; natural MKL extension.

- Experimental validation: consistent improvements over traditional baselines on small and large graphs; MKL-SPORE robust to noisy graphs.

- Weakness: No comparisons to modern GNN methods (e.g., GCN, GAT, GraphSAGE), limiting assessment of practical relevance.

- Weakness: Theoretical results rely on Lovász theta (ϑ), whose computation requires an SDP and is impractical for large graphs, creating a theory–practice disconnect.

- Weakness: Empirical gains are modest (≈1–5% accuracy), which may not justify added complexity versus current GNNs.

- Weakness: Limited implementation detail on the proximal inner iterations and lack of evidence that the approximation criterion (eq. 11) is efficiently satisfiable.

- Overall assessment: Well-written paper with valuable theoretical insights and a scalable algorithm, but needs better positioning relative to modern graph learning.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address practical comparisons and implementation details.

- Revision request 1: Add comparison with at least one state-of-the-art GNN on the same datasets.

- Revision request 2: Discuss computational complexity of Lovász theta and its impact on applying the theoretical results.

- Revision request 3: Provide more implementation detail and empirical evidence about the efficiency of meeting the IIP approximation criterion.

- Revision request 4: Discuss how spectral-norm regularization could be integrated into GNN frameworks.
