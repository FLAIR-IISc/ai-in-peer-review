- Proposal: Introduces SPORE — spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction — plus an IIP optimizer and multi-graph MKL extension, with PAC learnability and improved sample complexity.

- Theoretical contribution: Proves orthonormal graph embeddings are PAC learnable and derives stronger generalization/sample-complexity bounds using Rademacher averages (Theorems 1 and 4).

- Algorithmic innovation: Develops the IIP algorithm for inexact proximal optimization with careful approximation criteria, offering O(1/√T) convergence for the non-smooth convex problem.

- Experimental validation: Shows consistent empirical improvements over Unnormalized/Normalized Laplacian and K-Scaling on UCI, MNIST, synthetic graphs, and scalability to 2000-node graphs with few iterations.

- Broad applicability: Extends SPORE to multiple-graph transduction (MKL-SPORE) with theoretical and empirical benefits when combining graphs, including robustness to noisy graphs.

- Weakness — limited baselines: Lacks comparisons to modern graph-learning methods (e.g., GCNs, GATs), weakening claims of practical competitiveness.

- Weakness — complexity analysis: Provides O(1/√T) convergence but omits clear comparative computational complexity versus SDPs or other first-order methods.

- Weakness — implementation details: Insufficient practical details on IIP implementation choices (e.g., inner FISTA iterations, parameter settings) and their effects.

- Weakness — reliance on supplement: Key proofs and analyses are relegated to supplementary material, making evaluation of theoretical claims harder within the main paper.

- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical and algorithmic contributions with supportive experiments, but significance would improve with comparisons to recent graph neural methods.

- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS based on novel theory, algorithms, and empirical results.

- Suggestion 1: Add experimental comparisons with recent graph-based methods (GCNs, GATs).

- Suggestion 2: Provide more implementation details and guidance on parameter selection for the IIP/FISTA inner loop.

- Suggestion 3: Include a computational-complexity comparison between IIP and alternative optimization approaches.

- Suggestion 4: Move some key proofs from the supplementary material into the main paper for accessibility.

- Suggestion 5: Clarify earlier the relationship between the kernel formulation and orthonormal embedding representation.

- Final comment: Well-written and structured paper that makes a meaningful contribution to graph transduction, with suggested revisions likely to strengthen its impact.
