- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction with a convex formulation and an IIP optimization algorithm, plus an MKL-style extension.

- Establishes PAC learnability and a generalization bound (Theorem 1) motivating spectral norm regularization.

- Provides improved sample complexity (Theorem 4) using Rademacher averages, improving over prior work [16].

- Connects orthonormal embeddings to graph kernels (Lemma 2) and links spectral norm to graph structure via Lovász ϑ.

- Introduces a novel Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm with convergence analysis (Theorems 5 and 6).

- Empirical results show SPORE outperforming traditional graph kernel baselines across datasets and scale reasonably with few outer iterations.

- Clear motivation for spectral norm regularization and sound theoretical development.

- Lacks comparisons to modern GNN methods (e.g., GCN, GAT, GraphSAGE), limiting relevance to current graph learning research.

- Overstates novelty relative to [16] and does not fully clarify how spectral regularization improves upon [16]’s consistency result.

- Focuses narrowly on graph transduction and evaluates only on small-to-medium graphs (≤2000 nodes), limiting practical scope and scalability claims.

- Theorem 4 depends on the ϑ(G)ϑ(Ḡ)=n assumption (holds for perfect graphs); paper does not discuss implications for non-perfect real-world graphs.

- Algorithmic presentation of IIP lacks intuitive explanation of practical advantages over standard proximal methods.

- Proofs and experiments appear rigorous and well-conducted, with clear writing and consistent notation.

- Paper could better connect theoretical results to the practical algorithm and give more intuition for spectral-norm benefits.

- Novelty: spectral norm regularization and IIP are meaningful extensions but build on prior inexact proximal literature and MKL is a straightforward extension.

- Significance is limited by narrow problem focus, missing GNN comparisons, and limited demonstrated scalability.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on significant revisions addressing comparisons with GNNs and scalability.

- Revision suggestions: add GNN comparisons, discuss ϑ-assumption limitations, situate work in broader graph learning context, provide more IIP intuition, evaluate larger graphs, and clarify improved sample complexity over [16].
