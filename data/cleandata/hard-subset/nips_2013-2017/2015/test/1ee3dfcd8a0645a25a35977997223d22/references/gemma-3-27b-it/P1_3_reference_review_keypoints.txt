- Paper proposes SPORE: spectral norm regularization for selecting orthonormal embeddings in graph transduction.
- Introduces IIP, an Infeasible Inexact Proximal algorithm as an efficient alternative to SDP solvers.
- Extends the method to multi-graph transduction and analyzes benefits of combining views.
- Provides theoretical analysis: generalization bound (Theorem 1), PAC learnability (Theorems 4 and 6), and sample complexity estimates.
- Empirical validation on UCI and MNIST shows SPORE outperforming existing methods and scales with IIP.
- Well-situated within prior work, building on Shivanna & Bhattacharyya (2023) and linking to classic graph transduction literature.
- Novel use of spectral norm regularization applied specifically to orthonormal representations.
- IIP algorithm is a practical, computationally efficient contribution enabling larger-graph applications.
- Multi-graph extension is useful and relevant to applications like bioinformatics and spam detection.
- Paper is generally well-written, clearly presented, and uses consistent notation.
- IIP retains complexity: multiple steps and parameters (e.g., α, approximation parameter) with unclear sensitivity.
- Parameter tuning details (cross-validation strategy) are insufficiently described.
- Comparisons are limited; lacks benchmarks against recent graph neural network or other modern methods.
- Scalability limitations of the IIP algorithm are not thoroughly discussed; practical bottlenecks are unclear.
- Connection to the Lovasz Theta function is noted but not fully exploited; approximation/initialization strategies absent.
- Multi-graph experiments rely on synthetic data; real-world multi-graph evaluations are missing.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, practical algorithm, multi-graph extension, convincing empirical results, clear presentation.
- Arguments for rejection: algorithmic complexity and tuning sensitivity, limited comparisons, potential scalability concerns.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with solid theory and promising empirical results.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing parameter tuning, sensitivity analysis, expanded comparisons, scalability discussion, Theta approximations, and real-world multi-graph results.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
