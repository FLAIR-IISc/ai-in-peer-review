This paper proposes a new framework for spectral regular embedding applied to graph translation. This framework, and in particular the benefits of being an orthonormal embedding, can be described more concretely, but it is not a serious issue.

The authors then propose an interesting and elegant algorithm to almost solve the SEEER problem. This is a fundamental contribution and provides a good option for solving complex learning problems.

The experimental results demonstrate the benefits of the proposed solution. It would be great if the results showed the benefits of the SEEER framework, or (3) the orthonormal representation defined by (3), first, and also the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution for the SEEER problem. This is not visible in the current results, and it would be very important to place the actual contribution in a more global context.

Many graph translations are unfortunately described at a very high level. This is certainly an interesting extension, but more detail, motivation, and intuition are needed as to how and why the proposed method is also interesting for many graphs, and especially to understand how that algorithm is applied in such settings. In its current form, it is difficult to understand the main ideas in the case of many graphs, and in this case it is difficult to really appreciate the experimental results. In that regard, the last column of Table 4 (the noisy case) is not very intuitive - it seems that one graph is better than many graphs, which would contradict the motivation for dealing with many graphs.

Finally, this paper is not always easy to read and is very dense in some places. It also mentions supplementary material in many places. This is probably a sign that the authors want to convey too much information in the same paper, which actually penalizes accessibility and perhaps the visibility of the work. This paper proposes a new framework for orthonormal embedding, SEEER, which is applied to graph translation. SEEER proposes an interesting algorithm based on IIP for solving learning problems, and relatively concrete results are proposed.
