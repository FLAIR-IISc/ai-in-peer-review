- Paper investigates spectral regularization's effect on error bounds for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings, with new theory and convincing experiments.  
- Strong contribution linking large-scale graph topology (Lovász number) to convergence of embedding estimation (eq. 5).  
- Builds on Zhang and Ando (2005) to recast SSL as an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem.  
- Section 2 and appendix: derive an error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.  
- Section 3: introduce a spectral norm regularization term and connect error convergence to the graph's Lovász number.  
- Section 4: propose a proximal solver with an approximate projection handling constraints from intersecting a cone and a polytope.  
- Section 5: extend the framework to Multiple Kernel Learning (MKL); Section 6: experimental validation on several datasets.  
- Suggest explicitly stating (after eq. 3) that the formulation reduces SSL to supervised learning on labeled samples to clarify summation indices over S only.  
- Point out confusing notation in eq. (5), specifically an unclear equal sign before ω_C that should be revisited.  
- Recommend briefly discussing how embedding U is recovered from K and mentioning practical factorization choices.  
- Raise concern that the PSD projection step (Alg.1 steps 8–9) via eigendecomposition may be a runtime bottleneck for dense matrices.  
- Suggest exploring alternatives like optimizing directly on a factorization (e.g., Cholesky K = LL^T) and enforcing feasible-set structure on the factor to keep K PSD.  
- Overall assessment: strong, ambitious paper with dense material that could benefit from clarifications suggested above.
