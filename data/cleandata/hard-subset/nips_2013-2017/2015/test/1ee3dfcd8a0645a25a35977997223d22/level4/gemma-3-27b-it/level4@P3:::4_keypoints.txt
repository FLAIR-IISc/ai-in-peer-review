- Proposes SPORE, a novel framework for spectral norm regularization of orthonormal graph embeddings on the unit sphere.  
- Derives a PAC-based generalization bound linking spectral norm regularization to learning guarantees.  
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal), a scalable algorithm to solve the non-smooth convex SPORE problem instead of an SDP.  
- Provides convergence analysis for IIP with rate O(1/√T).  
- Extends the framework to multiple graph transduction using an MKL-style approach.  
- Situates work relative to kernel methods and Laplacian regularization, building on recent orthonormal embedding results.  
- Highlights a technically deep connection between the spectral norm and the Lovász θ function.  
- Experimental results show SPORE consistently outperforms baselines (Un-Lap, N-Lap, KS) on UCI and MNIST datasets.  
- Demonstrates scalability to graphs with thousands of vertices.  
- Motivation for why orthonormal embeddings improve generalization is insufficiently intuitive.  
- Paper should more clearly separate the benefits of orthonormal representations from the specific SPORE spectral regularization.  
- Experiments lack ablation isolating the impact of the spectral norm regularization (results with/without the term).  
- Multiple graph transduction section is underdeveloped and the motivation is weaker than for the single-graph case.  
- Counter-intuitive drop in performance with multiple graphs under noise requires deeper analysis (parameter sensitivity, noise properties, MKL limitations).  
- Paper is dense and over-relies on supplementary material, disrupting the main-text flow.  
- Key proofs and lemmas (e.g., Lemma 2 and Lemma 3) should be expanded in the main paper for clarity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, scalable algorithm, strong empirical performance, useful multiple-graph extension, rigorous analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear intuition for orthonormal embeddings, missing ablation of spectral regularization, underexplained multiple-graph results, dense presentation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand motivation for orthonormal embeddings, and analyze the noisy multiple-graph case further.
