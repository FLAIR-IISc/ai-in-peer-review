- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embedding for graph transduction to choose optimal orthonormal representations for better generalization.
- Shows orthonormal representations are PAC learnable via Theorem 1, giving a high-probability generalization bound tied to the spectral norm of the graph kernel.
- Identifies low spectral norm as a concrete criterion for selecting better embeddings (differs from prior expected-bound results).
- Formulates SPORE as a convex optimization with spectral-norm regularization and introduces the Inexact Infeasible Proximal (IIP) solver.
- Proves theoretical guarantees for the method, including convergence rates and improved sample complexity (Ω(ϑ/n)^{1/2} vs prior Ω(ϑ/n)^{1/3}).
- Provides an MKL-style extension (MKL-SPORE) to handle multiple graphs, with supporting theory and experiments.
- IIP algorithm is scalable and positioned as a practical alternative to SDP-based approaches, with stated O(1/√T) convergence.
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets (up to 2000 vertices) show SPORE outperforms baselines and scales to large problems where SDPs fail.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis linking graph structure (Lovász θ) to generalization and concrete improvements over prior bounds.
- Strength: Well-motivated, scalable algorithmic contribution with thorough empirical validation across scales.
- Weakness: The notion of "similar" vertices is vague; the paper lacks a precise definition of similarity and its relation to graph structure and embeddings.
- Weakness: Practical implementation details of IIP (e.g., integration with FISTA for projection) are under-specified, affecting reproducibility.
- Weakness: Comparisons to other spectral-regularization methods beyond standard graph kernels are limited.
- Overall assessment: Strong, novel theoretical and practical contributions; recommendation to accept.
