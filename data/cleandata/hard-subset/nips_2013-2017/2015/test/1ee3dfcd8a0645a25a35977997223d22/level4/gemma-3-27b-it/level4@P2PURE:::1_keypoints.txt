- Paper frames contributions and builds on Zhang and Ando (2005) showing SSL ≡ kernel-based supervised learning.
- Sections 2 and appendix present an error bound: minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.
- Section 3 adds spectral-norm regularization to Zhang & Ando’s formulation and links error convergence to the data graph’s Lovász number.
- Section 4 proposes a proximal solver with an approximate projection to handle cone–polytope intersection constraints.
- Section 5 gives an MKL extension; section 6 reports experiments on several datasets validating the approach.
- Strength: the connection between large-scale graph topology (Lovász number) and embedding convergence is insightful and bridges multiple areas.
- Suggest explicitly state after Eq.3 that the correspondence with Eq.1 converts the SSL problem into a supervised one focusing on labeled samples (why sums are over S only).
- Notation issue: Eq.5 is unclear—the equal sign immediately before ω_C is confusing and needs fixing.
- Request clarification in Sec.3 on how the embedding U is recovered from K; enumerate practical factorization choices.
- Algorithmic concern: ensuring K_r is PSD via eigendecomposition each iteration is likely expensive—ask if this is the main bottleneck.
- Suggest alternative optimization over a factorization (e.g., K = L L^T) to guarantee PSD and simplify projection (zeroing upper-triangular).
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando was published in NIPS 2005, not 2006.
- Overall: compelling and substantial work, potentially dense for one paper, with novel theory and convincing experimental support.
