This paper presents a novel method for optimizing the orthonormal embedding of a graph and its impact on learning functions on the graph. The authors' main contribution is the derivation of a generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings, which suggests that penalizing the spectral norm of K would lead to better generalization. They propose a spectral norm regularized algorithm, SPORE, which can be posed as a semi-definite program (SDP). However, SDPs do not scale well beyond a few hundred vertices, so they develop an inexact proximal method, IIP, to solve SPORE efficiently.

The IIP algorithm is based on approximating the proximal operator for the support function of the feasible set within a given precision. The authors prove that IIP converges to the optimal minimum of a non-smooth convex function with a rate of O(1/√T) in T iterations. They also apply IIP to the case when the set of interest is composed of the intersection of two convex sets, where the proximal operator for the support function of the set of interest can be obtained using the FISTA algorithm.

The authors evaluate their method on several datasets, including UCI and MNIST, and demonstrate significant performance improvements over existing methods. They also tackle large-scale graph transduction problems and show that their method can handle graphs with thousands of vertices.

The paper also extends the SPORE formulation to multiple graph transduction, proposing an MKL-SPORE formulation that combines multiple graphs. They show that combining multiple graphs improves the error convergence rate and the labelled sample complexity.
