This paper proposes a new framework for spectral regularized embedding applied to graph translation. This framework, and in particular the benefits of being an orthonormal embedding, could be more concretely described, but it is not a serious issue.

The authors then propose an interesting and elegant algorithm to almost solve the SEER problem. This is a fundamental contribution and seems to offer a good alternative to solving complex learning problems.

The experimental results demonstrate the benefits of the proposed solution. It would be great to show results that demonstrate the benefits of the SEER framework, or (3) demonstrate the orthonormal representation defined by (3) first, and also demonstrate the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution for the SEER problem. This is not visible in the current results, and it would be very important to place the actual contribution in a more global context.

Multiple graph translation has unfortunately been described at a very high level. This is certainly an interesting extension, but more details, motivation, and intuition are needed to understand how and why the proposed method is also interesting for multiple graphs, and especially to understand how that algorithm is applied in such settings. In its current form, it is difficult to understand the main ideas in the case of multiple graphs, and it is difficult to truly appreciate the experimental results in this case. In that regard, the last column of Table 4 (the noisy case) is not very intuitive – it seems that one graph is better than many graphs, which would contradict the motivation for dealing with multiple graphs.

Finally, this paper is not always easy to read and is very dense in some places. It also mentions supplementary material in many places. This is probably an indication that the authors want to convey too much information in a single paper, which actually penalizes the reach and perhaps the visibility of the work. This paper proposes a new framework for orthonormal embedding, SEER, which is applied to graph translation. SEER proposes an interesting algorithm based on IIP to solve the learning problem, and relatively concrete results are proposed.
