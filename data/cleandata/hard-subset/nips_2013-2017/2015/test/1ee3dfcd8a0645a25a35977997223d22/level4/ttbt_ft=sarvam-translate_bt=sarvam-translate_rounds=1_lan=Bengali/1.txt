This paper begins by presenting the background and showing the contributions. Then, the authors, using the results of Zhang and Andou, demonstrate that SSL reduces to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem. In Section 2 and the appendix, they provide an error bound that shows that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix is a good way to improve generalization. In Section 3, they add a spectral norm regularization term to Zhang and Andou's formula and provide a link between the error convergence of the data graph and the Lovelace number. Section 4 proposes a proximal solver, where the specialty is that it often works with projection steps to handle the constraint of a cone-polyhedron intersection. Section 5 proposes an MKL extension, and Section 6 concludes with experimental results on several datasets.

Overall, the paper is interesting and contains a lot of material (perhaps a bit too dense for a single paper), with several important contributions. The large-scale topological features (Lovelace number) of the data graph, which reflects the organization of a space or manifold, and the link between the convergence of the embedding estimate (Equation 5) are particularly instructive and connect several fields.
