The authors investigate the optimization of orthonormal embeddings of a graph and the resulting impact on learning functions defined on the graph. 

The paper begins by reviewing prior work that utilizes a Laplacian and its corresponding generalization bound (eqn. 2). The authors then introduce their bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4), which leads to an alternate optimization criterion in equation (5). Although \lambda_1(K) is not differentiable, the resulting optimization criterion is convex, being the sum of convex functions.

The authors employ inexact proximal methods to solve the optimization problem of SEER. 

In the experiments section, the authors apply SEER and MKL-SEER to multiple problems and demonstrate significant performance improvements.

Overall, this paper is very solid, with good theoretical justification. The authors exhibit cleverness in their approach through multiple intuitive leaps, including the derivation of the new optimization criterion, their willingness to tackle the difficult optimization problem, and the successful application to real-world problems. This paper represents a nice advancement over prior approaches.

The authors address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction, deriving a novel method and demonstrating its effectiveness in practice. The paper is complete and well-explained, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
