- Addresses learning optimal orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction and their effect on learning functions over graphs.  
- Identifies gap in prior Laplacian-based work: unit-sphere embeddings are suggested but not how to choose optimal embedding.  
- Proposes a novel PAC generalization bound (Theorem 1) that depends on the spectral norm of the kernel matrix.  
- Key theoretical insight: embeddings with low spectral norm and low empirical error generalize better.  
- Introduces SPORE: a spectral-norm-regularized convex objective over the elliptope to find optimal embeddings.  
- Formulates SPORE as a non-smooth convex optimization problem minimizing empirical error plus spectral norm regularizer.  
- Develops the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm to avoid costly SDP projections via approximate, possibly infeasible projections.  
- IIP guarantees an O(1/√T) convergence rate and is presented as a broadly applicable scalable optimization method.  
- Extends framework to multiple-graph learning with MKL-SPORE.  
- Empirical results show SPORE and MKL-SPORE outperform baselines and scale to graphs with thousands of nodes.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical motivation, novel algorithmic contribution, clear exposition, and thorough empirical validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled generalization bound, scalable convergent algorithm, state-of-the-art empirical performance, and well-written complete contribution.
