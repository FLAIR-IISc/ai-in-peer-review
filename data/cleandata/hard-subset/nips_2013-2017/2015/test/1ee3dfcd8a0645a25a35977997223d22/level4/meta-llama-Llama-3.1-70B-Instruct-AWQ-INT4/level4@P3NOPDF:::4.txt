The paper proposes a new framework for spectral regularized embedding (SEER), applied to graph transduction. While the framework itself is a valuable contribution, its benefits, particularly those of having an orthonormal embedding, could be described more convincingly to enhance the paper's impact.

The authors propose an interesting and elegant algorithm to solve the SEER problem approximately using the Inexact Inverse Power (IIP) method. This is an original contribution and provides a good alternative for solving the complex learning problem. The algorithm's novelty and effectiveness make it a notable addition to the field.

Experimental results tend to demonstrate the benefits of the proposed solution. However, to strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to show results that highlight the advantages of the SEER framework and the orthonormal representation defined by equation (3). Additionally, evaluating the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution to the SEER problem would provide valuable insights. Currently, these aspects are not clearly visible in the results, and including them would help position the actual contribution in a more global context.

The extension of the framework to multiple graph transduction is an interesting concept, but unfortunately, it is described at a very high level. To fully appreciate the proposed method's value in multiple graph settings, more details, motivation, and intuitions are necessary. The current description makes it difficult to grasp the main ideas and appreciate the experimental results in this case. For instance, the last column of Table 4 (noisy case) is not very intuitive, as it seems to suggest that one graph is better than multiple ones, which contradicts the motivation for dealing with multiple graphs.

The paper's readability could be improved. At times, the text is dense and difficult to follow, and it frequently refers to the supplementary material. This may indicate that the authors are trying to convey too much information in a single paper, which can negatively impact the accessibility and visibility of their work.

Overall, the paper proposes a new framework, SEER, for orthonormal embeddings applied to graph transduction. The authors also propose an interesting algorithm based on IIP to solve the learning problem in SEER, and the results are relatively convincing. With some revisions to address the mentioned concerns, the paper has the potential to make a more significant impact in the field.
