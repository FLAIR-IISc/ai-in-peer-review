- Recasts SSL objective via Zhang–Ando into an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem that underpins the analysis.
- Section 2 and appendix: derive a generalization bound showing that controlling the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.
- Section 3: introduce a spectral-norm regularizer in the Zhang–Ando formulation and relate the resulting error behavior to the graph’s Lovász number.
- Section 4: propose a proximal algorithm that approximates the projection step to handle intersection of a cone with a polytope.
- Section 5: extend the method within a multiple-kernel-learning (MKL) framework.
- Section 6: present empirical results on several datasets supporting the claims.
- Overall assessment: compelling, dense paper with multiple substantive contributions; explicit link between global graph structure (Lovász number) and embedding convergence is particularly illuminating.
- Section 1 (post-Equation 3): clarify that Eq. (1)→Eq. (3) maps the SSL problem to a supervised one on labeled samples, since the restriction of sums to S may seem surprising.
- Section 2: fix confusing notation in Eq. (5) — explain why there is an equals sign directly before ωC.
- Section 3: clarify in practice how embedding U is recovered from K and state which factorization is used.
- Section 4, Alg. 1 (Steps 8–9): ensuring K_t is PSD via eigendecomposition each iteration may be expensive — is this the dominant cost in practice?
- Section 4 suggestion: consider reparameterizing K = L L^T (e.g., Cholesky/factorization) so PSD is guaranteed and projections act on L.
- Bibliography correction: Zhang and Ando is NIPS 2005, not 2006.
