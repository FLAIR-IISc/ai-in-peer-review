This paper addresses the problem of learning labels on the vertices of a graph. It builds upon the work of Endo and Zhang, which suggested that embedding a graph on a unit sphere, through an orthonormal representation, yields better generalization error, i.e., error on the test set. However, Endo and Zhang did not present a concrete solution on how to choose the optimal embedding. The contribution of the present paper is the proposal of a new optimization formulation that jointly optimizes the embedding and the learned labels by introducing a regularization term. The regularization term, the spectral norm of the corresponding graph kernel matrix, is chosen based on insights derived from Theorem 1 in the paper, which provides an upper bound on the generalization error. Further results in the paper propose an asymptotic upper bound on the generalization error of the proposed method, with adaptive scaling compared to previous results. This upper bound can also be translated into an upper bound for the labeled sample complexity. While the proposed optimization can be inserted as an SDP, the complexity of solving the SDP is not desirable. Therefore, the paper suggests optimizing this problem using a scaleable, exact proximal method for graphs with 1000 vertices. Some experimental results indicate the superiority of the proposed method over previous methods.
