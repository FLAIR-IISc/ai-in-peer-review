- Addresses semi-supervised vertex label learning on graphs, building on Ando and Zhang's spherical embedding idea.
- Fills prior gap by proposing a concrete joint optimization for embedding and labels via a novel regularized formulation.
- Introduces a regularizer: the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix, motivated by Theorem 1 giving a generalization-error upper bound.
- Derives asymptotic upper bounds on generalization error with favorable scaling versus prior work, and translates this to labeled sample-complexity bounds.
- Notes the formulation can be written as an SDP, but SDPs are computationally costly.
- Proposes an inexact proximal optimization method that scales to graphs with thousands of vertices.
- Empirical results claim the proposed method outperforms existing approaches.
