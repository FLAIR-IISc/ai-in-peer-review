The authors investigate optimizing orthonormal embeddings of graphs and the consequent benefits for learning functions defined on those graphs. 
They begin by reviewing related work utilizing Laplacians and their associated generalization bounds (eqn. 2), before presenting their own bound derived from the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4). This bound motivates a novel optimization criterion (eqn. 5); while the maximum eigenvalue, λ<sub>1</sub>(K), is not differentiable, the resulting criterion is convex due to being a sum of convex functions.
The authors employ inexact proximal methods to solve the resulting Scalable Embedding via Eigenvalue Regularization (SEER) problem.
The experimental section demonstrates significant performance improvements achieved by applying SEER and MKL-SEER to several problems.
Overall, this is a strong paper with solid theoretical foundations. 
The work displays considerable ingenuity through several key insights – the development of the new optimization criterion, the willingness to address the challenging optimization problem it presents, and the successful application to real-world datasets.
This paper represents a valuable advancement over existing approaches.
The authors tackle the problem of optimal graph transduction embedding, deriving a novel method and demonstrating its practical effectiveness; the paper is complete and clearly explained.
