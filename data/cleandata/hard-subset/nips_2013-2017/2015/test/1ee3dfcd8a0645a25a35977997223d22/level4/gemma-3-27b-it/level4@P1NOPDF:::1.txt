The paper begins by introducing the necessary background and outlining its contributions. The authors then leverage the result from Zhang and Ando (2005) – demonstrating that semi-supervised learning (SSL) can be reduced to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem – as the foundation for the remainder of the work. In sections 2 and the appendix, they present an error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix leads to improved generalization. Section 3 adds a spectral norm regularization term to the Zhang and Ando formulation and establishes links between error convergence and the Lovász number of the data graph. Section 4 proposes a proximal solver, with a key feature being its approximate handling of the projection step to address the constraint of intersecting cone-polytope projections. Section 5 extends the approach to a multiple kernel learning (MKL) framework, and section 6 concludes with experimental results on several datasets.

Overall, this is an interesting and substantial paper – perhaps even somewhat dense for a single publication – with several important contributions. The connection between large-scale topological properties of the data graph (captured by the Lovász number, which reflects the graph’s organization within a space or manifold) and the convergence of the embedding estimation (eq. 5) is particularly insightful, bridging multiple fields.

In section 1, following equation (3), it would be helpful to explicitly state that the correspondence between equations (3) and (1) means the semi-supervised learning problem is transformed into a supervised learning problem, effectively focusing learning on the labelled samples. Otherwise, the indices appearing only over *S* (without *¬S*) in the sums within equation (3) are somewhat surprising, given that the regularizer in equation (1) is applied to both labelled and unlabelled samples while the loss is computed only on labelled data.

In section 2, the notation in equation (5) is confusing – the equal sign immediately preceding *ω<sub>C</sub>* is unclear.

In section 3, around line 162, it would be beneficial to mention how the embedding (U) is recovered from K. There are several practical choices for this factorization.

This is a valuable paper demonstrating how spectral regularization impacts error bounds on graph transduction using orthonormal embedding. It provides novel theoretical insights and is supported by compelling experimental results.
