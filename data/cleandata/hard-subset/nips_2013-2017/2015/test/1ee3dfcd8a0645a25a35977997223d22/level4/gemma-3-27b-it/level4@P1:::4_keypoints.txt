- Proposes SPORE, a novel framework for spectral norm regularization of orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction.  
- Motivation for using orthonormal embeddings is underdeveloped and could be stronger.  
- Introduces the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm—an original, elegant method to approximately solve the resulting optimization.  
- IIP appears scalable and offers a practical alternative to SDP-based approaches for larger graphs.  
- Provides a significant theoretical contribution: a PAC-learnability bound for orthonormal representations.  
- Extends the approach to multiple graph transduction, a promising direction but currently underexplained.  
- Experimental results show empirical performance gains overall.  
- Experiments do not sufficiently isolate performance gains attributable to SPORE, the orthonormal representation (Eq. 3), or the IIP algorithm.  
- Lacks quantitative evaluation of IIP’s approximation accuracy relative to the true SPORE solution.  
- Multiple-graph discussion is high-level—needs clearer motivation, intuition, and implementation details.  
- The “noisy case” result (Table 4 last column) is counterintuitive—multiple graphs perform worse than a single graph and needs explanation.  
- Paper is dense, hard to read, and over-relies on supplementary material; main text should be streamlined.  
- For acceptance: novelty of SPORE, scalable IIP algorithm, strong theoretical grounding, multi-graph extension, and empirical validation.  
- Against acceptance: weak motivation for orthonormal embeddings, experimental design issues, insufficient multi-graph detail, and presentation density.
