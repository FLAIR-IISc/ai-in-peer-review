- Introduces a novel framework (SEER) for spectral-regularized embedding applied to graph transduction, but the core concept and advantages of orthonormal embeddings are underexplained.  
- Presents an original and elegant IIP-based algorithm to approximate the SEER solution, offering a compelling alternative for the problem.  
- Experimental results generally support the method but lack explicit demonstration of the SEER framework's benefits and the orthonormal representation from Equation (3).  
- Fails to show the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution versus the exact SEER problem, leaving the fidelity of the approximation unclear.  
- Extension to multiple graph transduction is promising but treated superficially, missing implementation details, motivation, and intuitive explanation of applicability.  
- Counterintuitive experimental outcomes (e.g., single graph outperforming multiple graphs in Table 4) are unexplained and make the multiple-graph claims hard to interpret.  
- Manuscript is dense and relies excessively on supplementary material, harming readability, accessibility, and potential impact.
