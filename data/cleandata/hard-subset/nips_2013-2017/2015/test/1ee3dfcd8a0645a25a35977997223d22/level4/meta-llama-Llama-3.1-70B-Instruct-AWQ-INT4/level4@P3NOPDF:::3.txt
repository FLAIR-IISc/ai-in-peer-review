The authors investigate the optimization of orthonormal embeddings of a graph and their impact on learning functions defined on the graph. They begin by reviewing relevant prior work, which utilizes a Laplacian and its corresponding generalization bound (eqn. 2). Building upon this foundation, the authors introduce their own bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4). This bound leads to an alternative optimization criterion, as presented in equation (5). Although \lambda_1(K) is not differentiable, the optimization criterion is convex, being the sum of convex functions.

To solve the optimization problem, the authors employ inexact proximal methods, specifically tailored for the proposed approach, referred to as SEER. In the experiments section, the authors apply both SEER and its multi-kernel variant, MKL-SEER, to a range of problems, demonstrating significant performance improvements over existing methods.

Overall, this paper is very solid, with good theoretical justification. The authors exhibit cleverness in their approach through multiple intuitive leaps, including the derivation of the new optimization criterion, their willingness to tackle the challenging optimization problem, and the successful application of their method to real-world problems. This paper represents a nice advancement over prior approaches.

The authors effectively address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction by deriving a novel method and demonstrating its efficacy in practice. The paper is well-structured and clearly explained, making it easy to follow.
