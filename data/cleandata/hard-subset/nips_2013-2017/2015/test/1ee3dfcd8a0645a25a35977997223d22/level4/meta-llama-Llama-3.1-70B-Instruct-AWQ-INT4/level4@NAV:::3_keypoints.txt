- Paper optimizes orthonormal graph embeddings and studies their effect on learning functions on the graph.
- Reviews prior work using the Laplacian and its generalization bound (eqn. 2).
- Introduces a novel generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4).
- Derives an alternative optimization criterion from that bound (eqn. 5).
- Notes λ1(K) is non-differentiable but the proposed criterion remains convex as a sum of convex functions.
- Solves the resulting optimization problem using inexact proximal methods.
- Experiments show SEER and MKL-SEER yield notable performance improvements on real tasks.
- Paper is theoretically solid, inventive in derivations, and represents a significant advancement over prior approaches.
