- Clear introduction and stated contributions.
- Leverages Zhang and Ando to cast self-supervised learning as an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem.
- Section 2 and appendix provide an error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.
- Section 3 adds spectral-norm regularization to the Zhang & Ando formulation and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.
- Proposes a proximal solver (Section 4) that approximately handles the projection for a cone–polytope intersection constraint.
- Extends the approach to multiple kernel learning in Section 5 and validates experimentally in Section 6.
- Paper is substantial and insightful, notably bridging large-scale graph topology (Lovász number) and embedding convergence.
- Request to clarify in Section 1 (after Eq.3) that the correspondence between Eqs. (3) and (1) turns a semi-supervised problem into a supervised framework, and explain why summation indices in Eq.3 only run over S.
- Notation issue in Section 2: Eq.5’s use of an equal sign immediately before ω_C is unclear.
- In Section 3, the paper should explain how the embedding U is recovered from the kernel K and discuss factorization choices.
- Section 4’s Step 8–9 enforces PSD via repeated eigendecompositions, which may be a costly performance bottleneck.
- Suggestion to consider alternative optimization on kernel factorizations (e.g., K = LL^T) to avoid expensive PSD projections.
- Correction: Zhang and Ando was published at NIPS 2005, not 2006.
- Overall conclusion: noteworthy contribution clarifying spectral regularization’s effect on graph transduction error bounds with solid theoretical and experimental support.
