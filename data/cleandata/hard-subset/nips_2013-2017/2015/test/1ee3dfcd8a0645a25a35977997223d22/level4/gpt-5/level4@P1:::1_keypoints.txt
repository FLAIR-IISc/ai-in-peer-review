- Paper develops generalization bounds for graph transduction with orthonormal (unit-sphere) embeddings, showing spectral-norm minimization improves generalization.
- Introduces SPORE: a kernel-learning objective augmented with a spectral-norm regularizer and theoretical links to the Lovász θ function.
- Proposes an infeasible inexact proximal (IIP) method using approximate projections via FISTA to solve nonsmooth convex optimization over the elliptope with O(1/√T) convergence.
- Extends approach to multiple graphs with an MKL-style combination and improved theoretical bounds.
- Empirical results on UCI, MNIST, and synthetic multi-graph experiments show accuracy gains and scalability beyond standard SDP solvers.
- Theorem 1 gives a high-probability bound motivating spectral-norm regularization and yields graph-dependent sample-complexity rates tied to ϑ(G) and ϑ(Ḡ).
- IIP + approximate-projection strategy is algorithmically novel and avoids expensive exact elliptope projections.
- Solver scales to graphs with thousands of vertices, demonstrating practical impact where SDPs fail.
- MKL-SPORE is theoretically supported and empirically robust to noisy graphs.
