- Clear introduction and use of Zhang & Ando reduction showing SSL reduces to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem.  
- Sections 2 and appendix: present an error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.  
- Section 3: incorporate spectral-norm regularization into Zhang & Ando formulation and link error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Section 4: propose a proximal solver addressing the approximate projection for the cone–polytope intersection constraint.  
- Section 5: propose a multiple kernel learning (MKL) extension.  
- Section 6: provides experimental results across several datasets validating the approach.  
- Paper is interesting, substantial, and potentially dense for a single publication.  
- Notable insight: connects large-scale topological properties (Lovász number) of the data graph to embedding convergence, bridging multiple fields.  
- Request: after Equation (3) explicitly state that the reduction means learning occurs only on labeled samples to avoid index-summation confusion.  
- Issue: Equation (5) in Section 2 uses a confusing equality symbol immediately before ω_C and needs clarification.  
- Request: Section 3 should specify how to recover the embedding U from K, since multiple factorization choices exist.  
- Concern: Algorithm 1 (steps 8–9) must ensure K_r is PSD; current eigendecomposition per step may be the dominant cost.  
- Suggestion: consider optimizing over a factorization (e.g., K = L L^T) to guarantee PSD and potentially reduce projection cost.  
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando citation year should be 2005, not 2006.  
- Overall: offers novel theoretical insights into spectral regularization for graph transduction with compelling experimental validation.
