- Addresses graph transduction by learning an optimal orthonormal graph embedding with a PAC-based generalization bound that avoids VC-dimension arguments.  
- Derives a novel PAC generalization bound motivating spectral-norm regularization for orthonormal embeddings.  
- Proposes SPORE (SPectral regularized ORthonormal Embedding), which regularizes the embedding matrix spectral norm to improve generalization.  
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal), a first-order projected subgradient method for settings where exact projection (onto an elliptope) is expensive.  
- Provides convergence analysis for IIP, proving an O(1/√T) rate for this inexact projection method.  
- Demonstrates scalability: SPORE+IIP scales to graphs with thousands of vertices and outperforms several baselines empirically.  
- Algorithmic contribution is original and significant, offering a practical, scalable alternative to SDP solvers for relevant problems.  
- Paper clarity suffers from density and heavy reliance on supplementary material; main text needs more intuition and a more self-contained presentation.  
- Experimental analysis should better disentangle contributions: compare IIP solutions to exact SDP on small graphs to validate approximation quality.  
- Also compare SPORE against a non-optimized orthonormal representation within the same framework to isolate SPORE’s benefit.  
- Multiple-graph transduction section is underdeveloped and lacks implementation/intuition details; the "Noisy" result (MKL-SPORE worse than single best graph) is counterintuitive and needs discussion.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and algorithmic contributions but would benefit from clearer exposition and more targeted experiments.
