Review- The paper introduces a new framework for spectral-regularized embedding, applied to graph transduction. The rationale for using an orthonormal embedding could be articulated more convincingly, but this is not a major concern.

The authors then present an elegant and interesting algorithm to approximately solve the SEER problem. This is an original contribution and appears to offer a solid alternative for addressing the complex learning task.

The experimental results generally support the benefits of the proposed approach. It would be valuable to include results that specifically demonstrate the advantages of the SEER framework, or of the orthonormal representation defined in (3), as well as to quantify the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution to the SEER objective. These aspects are not evident in the current experiments, and clarifying them would help position the contribution in a broader context.

The multiple-graph transduction extension is unfortunately described at a very high level. It is indeed an interesting direction, but more details, motivation, and intuition are needed to clarify how and why the method is suitable for multiple graphs, and especially how the algorithm is implemented in that setting. In its current form, the main ideas in the multi-graph case are hard to grasp, making it difficult to fully appreciate the corresponding experimental results. In this regard, the last column of Table 4 (noisy case) is not very intuitive—it seems that a single graph outperforms multiple graphs, which appears to contradict the motivation for using multiple graphs.

Finally, the paper is at times difficult to read and quite dense. It also defers too frequently to the supplementary material. This likely indicates an attempt to include too much in a single paper, which ultimately harms the accessibility and likely the visibility of the work. In sum, the paper introduces SEER, a framework for orthonormal embeddings applied to graph transduction, proposes an IIP-based algorithm to solve the SEER learning problem, and presents relatively convincing empirical results.
