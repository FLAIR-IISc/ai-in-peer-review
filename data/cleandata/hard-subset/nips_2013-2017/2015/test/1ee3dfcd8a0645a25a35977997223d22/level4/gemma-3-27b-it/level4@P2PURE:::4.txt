The paper introduces a novel framework for spectral regularized embedding, specifically applied to graph transduction. While the advantages of this framework, and particularly the use of an orthonormal embedding, could be more thoroughly justified, this is a minor concern. 

The authors subsequently present an interesting and elegant algorithm for approximately solving the resulting Spectral Embedding with Orthonormal Regularization (SEER) problem. This represents an original contribution and appears to offer a strong alternative for addressing this challenging learning task. 

The experimental results generally support the effectiveness of the proposed solution. However, it would be valuable to include results that specifically demonstrate the benefits of the SEER framework itself, or of the orthonormal representation defined in equation (3), and to evaluate the approximation accuracy of the Iterated Intrinsic Proximal (IIP) solution with respect to the original SEER problem. Currently, these aspects are not evident in the provided results, and clarifying them would help better situate the contribution within the broader context of the field.

The extension to multiple graph transduction is unfortunately described at a high level of abstraction. Although potentially interesting, more detailed explanations, motivation, and intuition are needed to fully understand how and why the proposed method is beneficial for multiple graphs, and specifically how the algorithm is implemented in this setting. As it stands, the core ideas behind the multiple graph case are difficult to grasp, diminishing the impact of the associated experimental results. In this regard, the final column of Table 4 (noisy case) is counterintuitive – the observation that a single graph performs better than multiple graphs seems to contradict the motivation for exploring multiple graph approaches.

Finally, the paper’s readability is hampered by its density and frequent references to the supplementary material. This suggests the authors may be attempting to include excessive information in a single paper, which ultimately reduces accessibility and potentially limits the work’s visibility. In summary, the paper proposes a new SEER framework for orthonormal embeddings in graph transduction, along with an interesting IIP-based algorithm for solving the associated learning problem, and presents reasonably convincing experimental results.
