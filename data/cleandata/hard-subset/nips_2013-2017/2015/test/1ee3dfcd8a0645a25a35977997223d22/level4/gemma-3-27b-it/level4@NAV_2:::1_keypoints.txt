- Introduces spectral-norm regularization of orthonormal representations for graph transduction and proposes SPORE.
- Leverages Zhang and Ando’s framework to cast semi-supervised learning as a kernel-based supervised problem.
- Derives a generalization bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.
- Connects the Lovász number to embedding-estimation convergence (eq. 5), linking graph theory, learning, and optimization.
- Proposes an inexact proximal method to scale the algorithm to larger graphs.
- Extends the approach to multiple-graph transduction and validates performance empirically.
- Suggests explicitly stating after eq. (3) that it transforms the semi-supervised problem into a supervised one and clarifying why sums index only S.
- Notes awkward notation in eq. (5); recommends rewriting the equal sign preceding ω_C for readability.
- Requests a brief description in section 3 on how to recover embedding U from the optimized kernel K and practical factorization choices.
- Flags PSD enforcement in Algorithm 1 (steps 8–9) as a potential computational bottleneck due to required eigendecomposition.
- Recommends investigating alternative positivity-preserving approaches (e.g., optimizing a factorization K = L L^T) to improve performance.
- Minor factual correction: Zhang and Ando’s NIPS paper is from 2005, not 2006.
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation with rigorous analysis and clear literature connections.
- Strength: novel and valuable insight linking the Lovász number to embedding convergence.
- Strength: practical algorithmic contribution addressing scalability.
- Strength: extension to multiple graphs increases applicability.
- Strength: compelling experimental results that support theoretical claims.
- Weakness: computational cost of PSD enforcement step in the algorithm.
- Weakness: minor notation clarity issues in eqs. (3) and (5).
- Weakness: lacks implementation detail on recovering U from the optimized K.
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper; accept with minor revisions.
