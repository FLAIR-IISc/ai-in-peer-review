- Investigates optimizing orthonormal graph embeddings and their impact on learning functions defined on the graph.
- Reviews related work and uses spectral graph theory, including Laplacian-based generalization bound (eqn. 2).
- Proposes a new generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4).
- Derives a novel optimization criterion (eqn. 5) motivated by the new bound; although λ1(K) is nondifferentiable, the criterion is convex as a sum of convex functions.
- Solves the resulting Scalable Embedding for Efficient Regression (SEER) problem using inexact proximal methods.
- Experiments show SEER and MKL-SEER achieve significant performance improvements across multiple tasks compared to existing methods.
- Paper offers sound theoretical justification and several insightful contributions (new criterion, tackling difficult optimization, practical applicability).
- Presents a novel, convincing method for optimal embeddings in graph transduction; the manuscript is complete and clearly explained.
