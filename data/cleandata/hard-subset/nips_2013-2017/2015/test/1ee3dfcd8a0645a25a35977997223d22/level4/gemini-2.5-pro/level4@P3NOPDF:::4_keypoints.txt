- Proposes SEER, a spectral regularized embedding framework applied to graph transduction.
- Core contribution is a novel, elegant IIP-based algorithm to approximately solve the SEER optimization.
- Evaluated on single- and multi-graph transduction tasks with generally promising results.
- Strength: IIP-based solver is original and a significant technical contribution.
- Strength: Experimental results support the method’s effectiveness compared to existing techniques.
- Weakness: Motivation for enforcing an orthonormal embedding is insufficiently explained.
- Suggestion: Add ablation comparing orthonormal representation to a non-orthonormal variant.
- Suggestion: Quantify IIP’s approximation quality by comparing to a direct (slower) solver on small problems.
- Weakness: Multi-graph extension is described too high-level and lacks intuition for why SEER suits it.
- Suggestion: Provide detailed algorithmic implementation and clearer motivation for the multi-graph setting.
- Suggestion: Explain counterintuitive multi-graph results (e.g., Table 4 noisy case) that conflict with the section’s motivation.
- Weakness: Paper is dense and over-relies on supplementary material, harming readability and accessibility.
- Suggestion: Move core details into the main text and relegate secondary material to the appendix.
- Overall: Promising framework and algorithm but needs stronger motivation, ablation studies, clearer multi-graph exposition, and improved presentation.
