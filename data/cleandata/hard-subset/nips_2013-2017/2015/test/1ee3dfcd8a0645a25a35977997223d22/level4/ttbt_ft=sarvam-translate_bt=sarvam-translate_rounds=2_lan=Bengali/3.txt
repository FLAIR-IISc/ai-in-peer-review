The authors discuss the meaning of graphs, optimizing embedding, and the resulting effects of learning functions on graphs.

The authors begin by reviewing elements of previous work using Laplacian and related generalization bounds (Equation 2). They then discuss their bounds based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Equation 4). The authors use an inexact proximal method for the SEER solution.

In the experimental section, the authors apply SEER and MKL-SEER to multiple problems and show significant performance improvements.

Overall, this paper is very strong.

It has a good theoretical justification.

Also, as far as the paper goes, there are multiple intuitive ideas that show cleverness in the method – new optimization criteria, the willingness to tackle difficult optimization problems, and satisfactory applications to real problems.

This paper is a great advancement compared to previous methods.

The authors are looking at the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction. They have created a new method and shown that it works well in practice – this paper is complete and well-explained.
