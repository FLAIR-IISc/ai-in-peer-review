1. Builds on Ando & Zhang: prior claim that orthonormal graph embeddings improve generalization but lacked a method to choose an optimal embedding.  
2. Main contribution: optimization formulation that jointly optimizes embedding and labels via a regularizer (spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix) motivated by Theorem 1.  
3. Provides an asymptotic upper bound on generalization error with improved scaling over prior results, yielding a labeled sample complexity bound.  
4. Optimization can be formulated as an SDP, but SDPs are computationally expensive; authors propose an inexact proximal method scalable to graphs with thousands of vertices.  
5. Experiments show the proposed method outperforms previous approaches.  
6. Confusion about Theorem 4: the paper states learning is impossible without assumptions and assumes edges link “similar” instances, but “similar” is not formally defined nor explicitly used in the theorem or its proof.  
7. Theorem 4 (for random graphs) claims er^{0-1}[\hat{y}] = O(1/m (n^{3/4} + log(1/δ))^{1/2}), implying recovery with very few labels—seemingly counterintuitive without strong graph-label assumptions.  
8. Due to the ambiguity around the assumptions and Theorem 4’s basis, the reviewer cannot assess the significance of the theoretical contributions.
