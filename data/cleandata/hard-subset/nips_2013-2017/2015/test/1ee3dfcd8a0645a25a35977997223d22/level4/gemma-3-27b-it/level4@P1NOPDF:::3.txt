The authors investigate optimizing orthonormal embeddings of a graph and the resulting impact on learning functions defined on that graph.

The paper begins with a review of related work, leveraging concepts from spectral graph theory including the Laplacian and its corresponding generalization bound (eqn. 2). They then present their own bound, based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4). This bound motivates a novel optimization criterion – equation (5); while \lambda_1(K) is not differentiable, the proposed criterion is convex, being a sum of convex functions. 

The authors employ inexact proximal methods to solve the resulting Scalable Embedding for Efficient Regression (SEER) problem.

The experimental section demonstrates the effectiveness of SEER and MKL-SEER across multiple tasks, showcasing significant performance improvements compared to existing methods.

Overall, this is a strong paper with sound theoretical justification. The work exhibits several insightful and clever ideas – the derivation of the new optimization criterion, the authors’ willingness to address a challenging optimization problem, and the successful application to real-world problems are all particularly noteworthy. This paper represents a notable advancement over prior approaches.

The authors tackle the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction. They develop a novel method and convincingly demonstrate its practical effectiveness. The paper is complete and clearly explained.
