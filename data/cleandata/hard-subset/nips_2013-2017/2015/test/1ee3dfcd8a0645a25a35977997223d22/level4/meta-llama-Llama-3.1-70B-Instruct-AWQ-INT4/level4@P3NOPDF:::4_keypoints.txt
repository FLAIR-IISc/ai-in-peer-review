- Proposes a valuable new framework (SEER) for spectral regularized orthonormal embeddings applied to graph transduction.  
- The benefits of orthonormal embeddings are asserted but not convincingly demonstrated; need clearer motivation and empirical support.  
- Introduces an original, elegant Inexact Inverse Power (IIP) algorithm to approximate the SEER solution, a notable methodological contribution.  
- Experimental results broadly support the method, but lack targeted experiments that isolate advantages of SEER and the orthonormal representation (Eq. 3).  
- Missing evaluation of the IIP approximation accuracy to SEER—quantitative analysis of approximation quality is needed.  
- Multiple-graph extension is interesting but described too high-level; lacks detail, motivation, and intuition to interpret results.  
- Some reported results (e.g., Table 4 noisy case) are counterintuitive and need explanation for why a single graph may outperform multiple graphs.  
- Paper readability is hindered by dense text and heavy reliance on supplementary material; should streamline content for accessibility.  
- Overall promising contribution with convincing results, but requires revisions (better empirical validation, clearer multi-graph treatment, and improved clarity) to increase impact.
