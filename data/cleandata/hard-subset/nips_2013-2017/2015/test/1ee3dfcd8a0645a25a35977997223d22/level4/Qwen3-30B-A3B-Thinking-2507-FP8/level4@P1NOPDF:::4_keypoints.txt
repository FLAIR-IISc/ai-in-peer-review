- Proposes SEER (spectral regularization with orthonormal embeddings) but fails to justify why orthonormality is preferable to non-orthonormal alternatives.  
- The IIP approximation algorithm is an elegant, original solution that effectively reduces computational complexity.  
- Experiments show competitive results but do not isolate SEER’s contribution or the benefit of orthonormal embeddings.  
- Missing ablation studies: (1) SEER vs non-orthonormal baseline, and (2) IIP approximation accuracy vs exact SEER.  
- Multiple-graph extension is underdeveloped: insufficient motivation, detail, and intuition about scaling/adaptation.  
- Results in Table 4 (noisy case) where a single graph outperforms multiple graphs contradict the paper’s stated motivation and need explicit discussion.  
- Writing is dense and over-relies on supplementary material, which reduces accessibility and obscures key contributions.
