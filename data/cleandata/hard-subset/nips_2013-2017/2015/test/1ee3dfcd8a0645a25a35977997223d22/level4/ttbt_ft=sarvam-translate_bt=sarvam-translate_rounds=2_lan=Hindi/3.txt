The authors look at optimizing the orthonormal embedding of a graph and the resulting impact of learning tasks on the graph.

The authors begin by reviewing the material from previous work using the Laplacian and related generalization bound (Equation 2). They then discuss their bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Equation 4). This bound leads to an alternative optimization criterion – Equation (5); \lambda_1 (K) is not differentiable, but the optimization criterion is convex (the sum of convex functions).

The authors use exact near methods to solve the SEER.

In the experiment section, the authors apply SEER and MKL-SEER to several problems and show significant performance improvements.

Overall, this paper is very solid.

It has good theoretical justification.

Furthermore, as the paper progresses, there are several intuitive leaps that demonstrate cleverness in the approach – a new optimization criterion, the desire to tackle a difficult optimization problem, and satisfactory applications to real problems.

This paper is a good improvement over previous approaches.

The authors address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction. They derive a new method and demonstrate that it works well in practice – this paper is complete and well-explained.
