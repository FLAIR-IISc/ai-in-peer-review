- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized optimization over orthonormal-embedding kernels for graph transduction with a new PAC-based generalization bound.
- Bound depends on the spectral norm of the kernel matrix λ1(K) (and relates to Lovász θ) instead of VC dimension, enabling finite-sample control for this function class.
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) algorithm using FISTA-based approximate projections onto the elliptope with O(1/√T) convergence and empirical scalability to thousands of nodes.
- Extends to MKL-SPORE for multi-graph settings and proves improved PAC guarantees when at least one view is strong.
- Empirically outperforms Laplacian-based baselines and K-scaling on UCI, MNIST, and synthetic multi-graph experiments.
- Connects and extends prior Laplacian-regularization analyses by focusing on orthonormal embeddings and spectral-norm control.
- Links embedding geometry to Lovász theta, complementing θ-based and orthonormal-representation literature.
- Advances inexact proximal and first-order method literature by analyzing non-smooth objectives with infeasible approximate projections.
- Theoretical strength: clear high-probability generalization bound tying performance to λ1(K) and θ(G), with improved labeled sample complexity.
- Algorithmic strength: well-motivated IIP with practical approximate projection and proven O(1/√T) convergence under inexactness.
- Scope strength: natural multi-graph extension with meaningful theoretical benefits in multi-view settings.
- Empirical strength: consistent gains over baselines and demonstrated scalability to a few thousand nodes.
- Practical weakness: parameter choices for C and β rely on θ(G)/θ(Ḡ), which require SDPs and are tuned by cross-validation in experiments.
- PAC assumptions: paper should more explicitly state where similarity-graph consistency assumptions are used and implications for real-world graph construction.
- Projection/inexactness concerns: need clearer stopping criteria for inner FISTA, empirical R̂ values, and impact of projection errors on PSD feasibility and convergence.
- Computational profiling lacking: more detailed time/memory breakdown and comparisons to strong first-order SDP or ADMM elliptope methods are needed.
- Baseline and sensitivity gaps: missing comparisons to other graph kernels (random-walk, heat), ablations for β, and sensitivity to graph construction choices.
- Presentation issues: dense notation, minor typos, and need for consolidated assumptions and clearer algorithmic pseudo-code.
- Suggested fixes: provide practical heuristics or spectral surrogates for C/β, runtime/scalability plots, sensitivity studies, and explicit FISTA stopping rules.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel θ- and spectral-norm-based theory, scalable elliptope optimization algorithm, and strong empirical results including multi-graph extension.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on θ for theory without efficient substitutes, limited empirical baselines/sensitivity analysis, and clarity/reproducibility issues for IIP.
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance contingent on clarifying parameter selection, expanding experiments, and polishing IIP exposition and stopping criteria.
