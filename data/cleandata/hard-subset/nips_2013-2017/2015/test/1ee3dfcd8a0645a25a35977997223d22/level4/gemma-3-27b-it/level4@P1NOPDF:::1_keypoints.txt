- Leverages Zhang & Ando (2005) to reduce semi‑supervised learning to an equivalent kernel‑based supervised problem and builds the paper on this foundation.  
- Derives an error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.  
- Adds a spectral‑norm regularizer and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph, connecting global graph topology to embedding convergence.  
- Proposes a proximal solver that approximately handles projection onto intersecting cone‑polytope constraints.  
- Extends the approach to a multiple kernel learning (MKL) framework and provides experimental validation on several datasets.  
- Paper is interesting and substantial but somewhat dense for a single publication.  
- Suggest explicitly state after eq.(3) that the SSL problem is transformed into a supervised problem focusing learning on labelled samples (clarify why sums index only over S).  
- Notation in eq.(5) is confusing—the equal sign immediately preceding ω_C should be clarified.  
- In section 3, clarify how the embedding U is recovered from K and discuss practical factorization choices.  
- In Algorithm 1 (steps 8–9), enforcing K_r PSD via eigendecomposition each iteration may be a major computational bottleneck; ask whether optimizing on a factorization (e.g., K = LL^T) is feasible.  
- Correct Zhang & Ando’s publication year to NIPS 2005 (not 2006).  
- Overall assessment: valuable theoretical contributions linking spectral regularization to error bounds with compelling experimental support.
