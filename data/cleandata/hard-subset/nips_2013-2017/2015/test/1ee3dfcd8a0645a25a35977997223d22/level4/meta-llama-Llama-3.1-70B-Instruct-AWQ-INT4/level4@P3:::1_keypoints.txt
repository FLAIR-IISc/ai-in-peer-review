- Establishes a novel connection between graph transduction generalization bounds and the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix.  
- Shows minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization (Section 2 + appendix).  
- Introduces spectral-norm regularization to the Zhang and Ando formulation (key methodological contribution, Section 3).  
- Links error convergence to the Lovász number and discusses recovering the embedding U from the optimal kernel K.  
- Proposes a proximal solver (SPORE) using an approximate projection to handle cone–polytope intersection constraints.  
- Extends SPORE to a multiple-kernel-learning (MKL) setting and shows improved error convergence and labeled sample complexity.  
- Experimental results are convincing, showing the method outperforms existing approaches in several cases (Section 6).  
- Paper is comprehensive, well-organized, and makes a significant contribution to graph transduction.  
- Paper is dense and may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with the topic.  
- Some notation and terminology are unclear for non-experts.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical considerations and limitations (computational complexity, hyperparameter choices).  
- Suggested improvements: add a concise summary of contributions, clarify notation, discuss practical/complexity issues and hyperparameter selection, and include more baseline comparisons.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept (rating 9/10, confidence 9/10).
