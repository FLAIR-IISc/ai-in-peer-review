- Paper reduces semi-supervised learning to a kernel-based supervised problem using Zhang and Ando (2005) and builds work in that framework.  
- Presents an error bound (Section 2 and appendix) showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.  
- Adds spectral-norm regularization to the Zhang-Ando formulation and links error convergence to the Lovasz number of the data graph (Section 3).  
- Proposes a proximal solver (Section 4) that approximately handles the projection for a cone–polytope intersection constraint.  
- Notes an MKL extension (Section 5) and validates claims with experiments on multiple datasets (Section 6).  
- Strength: insightful connection between global graph topology (Lovasz number) and embedding estimation convergence, bridging multiple fields.  
- Suggestion: clarify after Eq. (3) that it represents the reduction of SSL to a supervised learning problem, since sums over labeled indices only are surprising.  
- Suggestion: fix confusing notation in Eq. (5), specifically the equal sign immediately before ω_C.  
- Suggestion: explain how the embedding U is recovered from K and discuss practical factorization choices.  
- Concern: Algorithm 1 ensures K_r is PSD via eigendecomposition each iteration, which is likely a primary computational bottleneck.  
- Suggestion: consider optimizing over a factorization of K (e.g., K = LL^T) to avoid repeated PSD projections.  
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando was published in NIPS 2005, not 2006.
