The paper commences by introducing the background and highlighting the contributions. The authors then utilize the Zhang and Ando result, which demonstrates that semi-supervised learning (SSL) can be reduced to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem, as the foundation for the remainder of the paper. In sections 2 and the appendix, they derive an error bound, showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix is an effective approach to improving generalization. Section 3 introduces a spectral norm regularization term to the Zhang and Ando formulation, establishing a connection between error convergence and the Lovasz number of the data graph. Section 4 proposes a proximal solver, distinguished by its approximate handling of the projection step to accommodate the constraint of a cone-polytope intersection. Section 5 extends the work to Multiple Kernel Learning (MKL), and section 6 concludes with experimental results on several datasets.

Overall, the paper is interesting and encompasses a substantial amount of material, perhaps even too dense for a single paper, with several significant contributions. The link between large-scale topological properties of the data graph, as reflected by the Lovasz number, and the convergence of embedding estimation (eq. 5) is particularly insightful, bridging multiple fields.

A minor clarification is suggested in section 1, following equation 3, to explicitly state that the correspondence between equations (3) and (1) implies that a semi-supervised learning problem is effectively transformed into a supervised learning problem. This would help clarify why the sums in equation (3) only contain indices over S, whereas equation (1) includes a regularizer over non-labeled samples in addition to the loss over labeled data.

In section 2, the notation in equation 5 is somewhat confusing, specifically the equal sign preceding \omega_C. A brief explanation would be beneficial to enhance readability.

In section 3, around line 162, it would be helpful to mention how the embedding (U) is recovered from K, as there are multiple practical choices for factorization.

Regarding section 4, Algorithm 1 steps 8-9 involve ensuring that K_r is positive semi-definite (PSD), which can be computationally expensive for general dense square symmetric matrices. The current implementation performs an eigendecomposition every time (supplementary D.3.2). It is likely that this step dominates the algorithm's runtime. An alternative approach would be to optimize over a factorization of K, such as Cholesky decomposition (K=LL^T), where projection to the feasible set would result in a matrix with all-zeros in the upper triangular part, thereby ensuring that K is PSD by construction.

Additionally, the Zhang and Ando reference should be corrected to NIPS 2005 instead of 2006.

This paper presents an interesting exploration of how spectral regularization affects error bounds on graph transduction using orthonormal embedding, providing new theoretical insights and convincing experimental results.
