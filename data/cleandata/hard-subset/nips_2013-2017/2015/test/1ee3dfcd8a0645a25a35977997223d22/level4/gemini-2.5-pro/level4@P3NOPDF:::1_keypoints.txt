- Introduces spectral norm regularization for graph-based semi-supervised learning, framing SSL as a kernel-based supervised problem (building on Zhang & Ando).
- Main contributions: derives an error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.
- Establishes a theoretical link between embedding error convergence and the Lovasz number of the data graph.
- Proposes an efficient proximal solver with an approximate projection step to handle complex constraints.
- Demonstrates method effectiveness via an MKL extension and experiments on several benchmark datasets.
- Strength: novel and significant theoretical insights, particularly the Lovasz-number connection bridging spectral graph theory and learning.
- Strength: theory is supported by a practical optimization algorithm and convincing experimental results.
- Suggestion: clarify how Equations (1)→(3) transform the semi-supervised problem into a supervised one, since (3) sums only over labeled set S.
- Suggestion: fix or clarify a likely typo in Equation (5) (extraneous equals sign before ω_C).
- Suggestion: explain how the low-dimensional embedding U is recovered from K in practice (e.g., specify eigen-decomposition or factorization).
- Suggestion: report runtime impact of Algorithm 1’s PSD enforcement (steps 8–9 perform full eigendecomposition each iteration).
- Suggestion: consider alternative optimization (e.g., parametrize K = L L^T) to avoid repeated expensive PSD projections.
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando citation should be NIPS 2005, not 2006.
- Overall: excellent paper with strong theory, algorithm, and experiments; addressing clarity and computational-cost points would further strengthen it.
