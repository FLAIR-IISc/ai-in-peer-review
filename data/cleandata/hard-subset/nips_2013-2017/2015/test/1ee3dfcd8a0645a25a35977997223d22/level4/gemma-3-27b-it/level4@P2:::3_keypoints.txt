- Addresses graph transduction via optimal orthonormal (unit-sphere) embeddings and the challenge of computing them efficiently with guarantees.  
- Proposes SPORE: a spectral norm regularization criterion motivated by a new generalization bound based on the kernel matrix maximum eigenvalue.  
- Shows SPORE is convex despite the non-differentiability of the spectral norm.  
- Introduces an Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm to solve SPORE efficiently, avoiding SDP scalability issues.  
- Extends the approach to multiple graphs with MKL-SPORE and provides corresponding algorithmic and generalization results.  
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis for the IIP method.  
- Empirically, SPORE and MKL-SPORE outperform baselines (Un-Laplacian, Normalized Laplacian, K-Scaling) on benchmark and synthetic datasets.  
- Demonstrates encouraging scaling behavior on graphs with up to 2000 nodes.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation (novel generalization bound and convergence proofs).  
- Strength: Novel and clever combination of spectral norm regularization with orthonormal embeddings, plus useful multi-graph extension.  
- Strength: Complete work — well-defined problem, proposed solution, theory, and experiments.  
- Strength: Practical IIP algorithm enabling scalability beyond SDP methods.  
- Strength: Clear and well-written presentation.  
- Weakness: Notation could be improved for consistency and readability.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of parameter sensitivity (e.g., effect of regularization parameter β).  
- Weakness: Related work section could better connect to literature on inexact proximal methods and approximation criteria.  
- For acceptance: Significant advance with strong theory, practical algorithm, convincing empirical results, and scalability.  
- Against acceptance: Minor issues with notation and insufficient discussion of parameter sensitivity and proximal-method literature.  
- Overall: High-quality paper with easily addressable minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
