- Addresses vertex label learning on graphs, building on Ando and Zhang’s embedding-on-sphere/orthonormal representation idea.
- Notes Ando and Zhang lacked a concrete method for choosing an optimal embedding.
- Proposes a joint optimization of embedding and labels by adding a regularizer equal to the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix, motivated by Theorem 1.
- Presents asymptotic upper bounds on the method’s generalization error and corresponding labeled sample complexity with favorable scaling.
- Shows the optimization can be cast as an SDP but argues SDPs are impractical, proposing an inexact proximal method that scales to thousands of nodes.
- Provides experiments that indicate the proposed method outperforms previous methods.

- Gives a rough interpretation of Theorem 4 for random graphs (error bound ~ O(1/m (n^{3/4} + log(1/δ))^{1/2})) but remains unconvinced of its significance or correctness due to unclear proof.
