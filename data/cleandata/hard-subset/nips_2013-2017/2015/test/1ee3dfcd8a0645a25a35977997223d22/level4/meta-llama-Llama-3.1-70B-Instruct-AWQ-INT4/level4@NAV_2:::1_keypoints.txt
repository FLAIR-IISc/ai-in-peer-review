- Paper uses Zhang & Ando reduction to cast semi-supervised learning as an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem.  
- Section 2 and appendix: derive an error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.  
- Section 3: introduce spectral-norm regularization in the Zhang & Ando formulation and relate error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Section 4: propose a proximal solver that handles the projection step approximately to manage a cone–polytope intersection constraint.  
- Section 5: propose a multiple-kernel-learning (MKL) extension.  
- Section 6: present experiments on several datasets that support the claims.  
- Overall assessment: interesting, dense paper with several important contributions that bridge multiple fields.  
- Highlighted contribution: the link between large-scale graph topology (Lovász number) and embedding-convergence is particularly enlightening.  
- Suggestion: after Eq. (3) clarify that the correspondence to Eq. (1) means SSL reduces to supervised learning on labeled samples.  
- Critique: notation in Eq. (5) is confusing — unclear why there is an equal sign just before ω_C.  
- Suggestion: in Section 3 explain how the embedding U is recovered from K and discuss practical factorization choices.  
- Critique: Algorithm 1 (steps 8–9) requires PSD projection via eigendecomposition, which is expensive — ask if this dominates runtime and whether optimizing over a factorization (e.g., K=LL^T) could avoid repeated decompositions.  
- Correction: Zhang & Ando should be cited as NIPS 2005, not 2006.  
- Final summary: provides new theoretical insights on spectral regularization for graph transduction and convincing experimental results.
