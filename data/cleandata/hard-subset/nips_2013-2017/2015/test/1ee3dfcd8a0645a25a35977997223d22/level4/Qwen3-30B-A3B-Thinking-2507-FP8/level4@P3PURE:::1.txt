

Overall, the paper presents a compelling theoretical framework for semi-supervised learning (SSL) by establishing an equivalence between SSL and kernel-based supervised learning, leveraging the Zhang and Ando result. The authors derive an error bound in Section 2 (and the appendix) demonstrating that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix enhances generalization. Section 3 introduces spectral norm regularization within the Zhang and Ando formulation, linking error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph—a connection that bridges spectral graph theory and statistical learning, offering valuable insights into how large-scale topological properties of the data graph influence embedding estimation (Equation 5). Section 4 proposes a proximal solver handling cone-polytope constraints via approximate projection, while Section 5 extends the approach to Multiple Kernel Learning (MKL). Section 6 validates the method with experimental results across multiple datasets.  

The paper is intellectually rich and makes several significant contributions, though its scope may be somewhat dense for a single publication. The theoretical insights, particularly the link between the Lovász number and error convergence, are especially noteworthy.  

Specific suggestions for improvement:  
- **Section 1 (after Equation 3):** Clarify that the correspondence between Equations (3) and (1) implies SSL reduces to a supervised learning problem over labeled samples. This is essential to explain why Equation (3) sums only over \(S\) (labeled indices), unlike Equation (1) which regularizes over all samples.  
- **Section 2, Equation (5):** The equals sign preceding \(\omega_C\) is confusing; revise the notation for clarity.  
- **Section 3 (line 162):** Explicitly describe how the embedding matrix \(U\) is recovered from \(K\) (e.g., via factorization choices like Cholesky or eigen-decomposition), as this is critical for practical implementation.  
- **Section 4 (Algorithm 1, steps 8–9):** The requirement to enforce \(K_r\) as positive semi-definite (PSD) via eigendecomposition at each iteration (as noted in Supplementary D.3.2) may dominate computational cost. Consider whether optimizing over a factorized \(K\) (e.g., \(K = LL^\top\) via Cholesky) would streamline the projection step—projecting to a matrix with zeros in the upper triangular part would inherently preserve PSDness.  
- **Citation correction:** Zhang and Ando (2005), not 2006.  

This work provides novel theoretical insights into spectral regularization for graph-based SSL, supported by rigorous analysis and empirical validation. The paper is well-motivated and makes a meaningful contribution to the field.
