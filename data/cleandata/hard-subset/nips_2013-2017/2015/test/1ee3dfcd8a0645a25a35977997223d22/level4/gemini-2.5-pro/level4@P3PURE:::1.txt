This paper presents an interesting approach to graph transduction, showing how spectral regularization of the graph kernel impacts error bounds. The authors begin by framing the semi-supervised learning (SSL) problem as an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem, based on the work of Zhang and Ando. They then derive an error bound demonstrating that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization. This leads to a new formulation with a spectral norm regularization term, where the authors establish a compelling link between error convergence and the Lovasz number of the data graph. The paper proposes a proximal solver to handle the resulting optimization problem, extends the method to Multiple Kernel Learning (MKL), and validates the approach with convincing experimental results on several datasets.

Overall, this is a strong paper containing a significant amount of material and several important contributions. The primary strength lies in its novel theoretical insights. The connection established between the convergence of the embedding estimation (Equation 5) and the Lovasz number of the data graph is particularly enlightening. This insight bridges graph theory and machine learning, providing a deeper understanding of how the large-scale topological properties of the data manifold influence learning performance. The theoretical work is well-supported by convincing experimental results.

Below are some points for clarification and potential improvement:

*   In Section 1, following Equation (3), it would be beneficial to explicitly state that the correspondence with Equation (1) transforms the semi-supervised problem into a purely supervised one. This would clarify why the sums in Equation (3) only iterate over the labeled set S, which might otherwise seem surprising given that the regularizer in Equation (1) involves unlabeled samples as well.

*   In Section 2, the notation in Equation (5) is confusing. There appears to be a misplaced equals sign just before the $\omega_C$ term, which should be corrected.

*   In Section 3 (around line 162), the paper discusses the embedding U but does not specify how it is recovered from the learned kernel K. Clarifying the factorization method used would be helpful, as several practical choices exist.

*   In Section 4, the proposed proximal solver (Algorithm 1, steps 8-9) requires projecting K_r onto the cone of positive semi-definite (PSD) matrices in each iteration. As noted in the supplement, this is accomplished via a full eigendecomposition, which can be computationally expensive for dense matrices. Could the authors comment on whether this step is the primary computational bottleneck? Furthermore, have the authors considered optimizing directly over a factorization of K (e.g., K = LL^T via a Cholesky decomposition)? This might offer a more efficient alternative, as the PSD constraint would be implicitly satisfied, and projection to the feasible set could be simplified.

*   Minor correction: The citation for Zhang and Ando should be NIPS 2005, not 2006.
