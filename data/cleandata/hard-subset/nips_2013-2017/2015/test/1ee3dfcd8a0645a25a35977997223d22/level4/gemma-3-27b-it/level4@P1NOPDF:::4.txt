The paper proposes a novel framework for spectral regularized embedding, applied to graph transduction. While the benefits of this framework, and particularly of employing an orthonormal embedding, could be articulated more persuasively, this is not a major concern.

The authors then present an interesting and elegant algorithm to approximately solve the resulting SEER problem. This is an original contribution and appears to offer a valuable alternative for addressing this complex learning challenge.

Experimental results generally demonstrate the advantages of the proposed solution. However, it would be beneficial to include results that specifically highlight the benefits of the SEER framework itself, or of the orthonormal representation defined in equation (3), as a first step. Additionally, demonstrating the approximation accuracy of the Iterative Independent Projection (IIP) solution with respect to the SEER problem would be highly valuable. This is not currently evident in the presented results, and would help to better contextualize the core contribution.

The extension to multiple graph transduction is unfortunately described at a very high level. While it represents an interesting direction, more detailed explanations, motivation, and intuition are needed to fully understand how and why the proposed method is also effective in this setting, and particularly how the algorithm is implemented. In its current form, it’s difficult to grasp the key ideas behind the multiple graph approach, and consequently, to fully appreciate the experimental results. Related to this, the final column of Table 4 (the noisy case) is not immediately intuitive – the observation that performance with a single graph is superior to that with multiple graphs seems to contradict the motivation for considering multiple graphs in the first place.

Finally, the paper can be challenging to read and feels dense in places. It also relies heavily on references to the supplementary material. This likely indicates that the authors are attempting to include too much information in a single paper, which ultimately hinders accessibility and potentially the visibility of the work. 

In summary, the paper introduces a new framework, SEER, for orthonormal embeddings applied to graph transduction. An interesting algorithm based on IIP is proposed to solve the learning problem within SEER, and the results are relatively convincing.
