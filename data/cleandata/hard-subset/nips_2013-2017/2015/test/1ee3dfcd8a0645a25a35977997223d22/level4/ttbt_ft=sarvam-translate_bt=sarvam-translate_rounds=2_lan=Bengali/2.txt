This paper discusses the problem of learning labels at the vertices of a graph. It builds upon the work of Ando and Zhang, who suggested embedding the graph onto a single sphere through an orthonormal representation, resulting in better generalization error, i.e., error on the test set. However, Ando and Zhang did not present a specific solution on how to choose an optimal embedding. The contribution of the present paper is to propose a new optimization formulation that jointly optimizes the embedding and learning labels through the introduction of a regularization term. The regularization term, the spectral norm of the corresponding graph kernel matrix, is chosen based on insights from the first theorem of the paper, which specifies an upper bound on the generalization error. Further results of the paper mention an infinite upper bound on the generalization error of the proposed method, which is optimally scaled compared to previous results. This upper bound can be translated into an upper bound for the complexity of the labeled sample. Although the proposed optimization can be considered an SDP, the complexity of SDP solutions is not desirable. Therefore, the paper suggests optimizing this problem using an implicit proximal method, which can be scaled with graphs with more than 1000 vertices. Some experimental results indicate the superiority of the proposed method over the previous method.
