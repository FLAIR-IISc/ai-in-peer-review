This paper addresses the problem of optimizing the orthonormal embedding of a graph to improve the performance of functions learned on it. The authors propose a novel and effective method for graph transduction, which they support with both theory and strong empirical results.

The paper begins by reviewing prior work that uses the graph Laplacian and its corresponding generalization bound (Eq. 2). From this foundation, the authors derive their own generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Eq. 4). This new bound motivates an alternative optimization criterion, given in Equation (5). While the maximum eigenvalue term, λ_1(K), is non-differentiable, the authors note that the overall optimization criterion is convex, being a sum of convex functions. To solve their proposed formulation (SEER), they employ inexact proximal methods.

In the experiments section, the authors apply SEER and a multiple kernel learning variant, MKL-SEER, to several problems, demonstrating significant performance improvements over prior approaches.

Overall, this is a very solid and complete paper that presents a clear advancement in the field. The work has strong theoretical justification, and the authors show cleverness in their approach through several intuitive steps: formulating the new optimization criterion, tackling the resulting non-differentiable optimization problem, and successfully applying the method to real-world problems. The paper is well-explained and presents a compelling case for the proposed method.
