- Proposes a novel SEER framework for spectral regularized orthonormal embeddings in graph transduction.  
- Benefits of using an orthonormal embedding are not clearly explained or justified.  
- The SEER framework description could be clearer, though this is not a fatal flaw.  
- Presents an original, elegant iterative inference procedure (IIP) algorithm to approximately solve SEER.  
- IIP appears viable and offers a meaningful alternative for this learning problem.  
- Experiments generally support the proposed method’s effectiveness.  
- Missing experiments isolating the benefits of the SEER framework or the orthonormal representation independent of IIP.  
- Lacks evaluation of the IIP approximation accuracy relative to the true SEER solution.  
- The multiple-graph transduction extension is described too high-level and needs more detail, motivation, and implementation intuition.  
- Results for the multiple-graph/noisy case (Table 4 final column) are hard to interpret and sometimes counterintuitive.  
- Paper readability suffers from density and overreliance on supplementary material, reducing accessibility and impact.
