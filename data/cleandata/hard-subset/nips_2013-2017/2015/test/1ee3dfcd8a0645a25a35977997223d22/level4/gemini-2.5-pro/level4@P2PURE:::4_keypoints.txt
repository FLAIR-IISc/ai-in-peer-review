- Introduces SEER, a spectral regularized orthonormal embedding framework and a novel iterative algorithm to approximately solve its optimization problem.  
- The iterative algorithm is a novel and promising alternative for the complex learning task.  
- Motivation for enforcing an orthonormal embedding is weak and not convincingly justified.  
- Experimental results show effectiveness but lack targeted analyses that isolate core contributions.  
- Recommend experiments to (1) evaluate benefits of the SEER framework and orthonormal representation separately from the solver, and (2) measure IIP approximation accuracy versus the true SEER solution.  
- The multi-graph transduction extension is described at a high level and lacks sufficient motivation, intuition, implementation details, and clarity.  
- Counterintuitive result in Table 4 (noisy case) where a single graph outperforms multiple graphs raises concerns about the multi-graph approach.  
- Paper is dense, relies heavily on supplementary material for key details, and would benefit from clearer, more focused presentation to improve accessibility and impact.
