- Introduces SEER, a spectral-regularized embedding framework enforcing an orthonormal constraint for graph transduction.  
- Proposes a novel, elegant Inverse Power (IIP)-based algorithm to approximately solve the SEER optimization.  
- Experimental results are generally promising and show benefits over existing methods.  
- Needs stronger motivation and clearer explanation of the orthonormal constraint (Eq. 3) and its theoretical/practical benefits.  
- Experiments conflate the value of the SEER formulation with the performance of the IIP approximation; should decouple and evaluate them separately.  
- Should compare SEER to a non-orthonormal variant or other spectral methods and quantify the IIP approximation accuracy.  
- Multi-graph transduction extension is presented too superficially—lacks motivation, intuition, and implementation details.  
- Counter-intuitive noisy multi-graph results (Table 4) where a single graph outperforms multiple graphs require clarification or further investigation.  
- Paper is dense and relies heavily on supplementary material; clarity and self-contained presentation need significant improvement.  
- Overall: a valuable and original algorithmic contribution but needs clearer motivation, better experimental separation of contributions, and a fuller treatment of the multi-graph case.
