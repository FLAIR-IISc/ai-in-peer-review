- Recasts semi-supervised learning as an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem building on Zhang and Ando.  
- Derives a generalization error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix can improve generalization.  
- Introduces a spectral-norm regularizer and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Proposes a proximal solver that handles the cone–polytope intersection via an approximate projection step.  
- Presents an MKL extension for the proposed framework.  
- Reports empirical results on several datasets supporting the approach.  
- Overall: interesting, dense paper with substantial contributions; the Lovász-number connection is particularly insightful and interdisciplinary.  
- Clarify after Eq. (3) that the correspondence to Eq. (1) reduces the problem to supervised learning on the labeled set S.  
- Fix notation in Eq. (5) (use “:=” or clarify the equals sign).  
- Explain how the embedding U is recovered from K in practice and discuss factorization choices (eigendecomposition, Cholesky, low-rank/Nyström) and their implications.  
- Algorithm 1 concern: ensuring K_r PSD via eigendecomposition each iteration is potentially expensive—state if this is the main bottleneck.  
- Suggest exploring optimization over a factorization K = L L^T to enforce PSD by construction and discuss how this interacts with cone–polytope constraints and the approximate projection.
