The paper addresses the problem of learning labels on the vertices of a graph. It builds on the work of Ando and Zhang which suggested that embedding the graph on a unit sphere, by means of an orthonormal presentation, results in better generalization error, i.e., error on the test set. Ando and Zhang however, did not present a concrete solution on how to choose an optimal embedding. The current paper's contribution is the proposal of a new optimization formulation which jointly optimizes the embedding and the learned labels by introducing a regularization term. The regularization term, the spectral norm of the corresponding graph kernel matrix, is chosen based on the insight obtained from Theorem 1 in the paper which states an upper bound on the generalization error.

The paper presents a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method, including a generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings, a PAC analysis of the proposed SPORE formulation, and a labelled sample complexity estimate. The authors also propose an efficient algorithm to solve the SPORE formulation using inexact proximal methods, which can scale to graphs with 1000s of vertices.

The experimental results indicate the superiority of the proposed method compared to previous methods. The paper also presents a multiple graph transduction formulation, MKL-SPORE, which can handle multiple graphs and provides improved PAC bounds.

The main contribution of the paper is the proposal of the SPORE formulation and the efficient algorithm to solve it. The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the proposed method and demonstrates its effectiveness through experimental results.
