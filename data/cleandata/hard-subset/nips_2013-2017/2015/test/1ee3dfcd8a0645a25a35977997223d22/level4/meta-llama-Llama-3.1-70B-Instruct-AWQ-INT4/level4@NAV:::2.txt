This paper tackles the problem of learning vertex labels in a graph by building upon the work of Ando and Zhang, who demonstrated improved generalization error through orthonormal graph embedding on a unit sphere. However, their work lacked a concrete solution for optimal embedding selection. The current paper addresses this by introducing a novel optimization formulation that jointly optimizes embedding and learned labels through a regularization term. This term, the spectral norm of the corresponding graph kernel matrix, is informed by Theorem 1, which provides an upper bound on generalization error.

The paper further establishes an asymptotic upper bound on the proposed method's generalization error, exhibiting favorable scaling compared to previous results. This upper bound can be translated into an upper bound for labeled sample complexity. Although the proposed optimization can be cast as a semi-definite program (SDP), the complexity of solving SDPs is undesirable. As an alternative, the paper suggests using an inexact proximal method, which can scale to graphs with thousands of vertices. Experimental results indicate the superiority of the proposed method over previous approaches.
