The authors discuss optimizing the embedding of the meaning of a graph and the resulting effects of learning functions on the graph.

The authors begin by reviewing the elements of previous work using the Laplacian and related generalization bound (Equation 2). They then discuss their bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Equation 4). This bound leads to an alternative optimization criterion – Equation (5); \lambda_1(K) is not differentiable, but the optimization criterion is convex (a set of convex functions).

The authors use an inexact proximal method for the SEER solution.

In the experimental section, the authors apply SEER and MKL-SEER to multiple problems and show significant performance improvements.

Overall, this paper is very strong.

It has a good theoretical justification.

Also, as the paper progresses, there are multiple intuitive ideas that show cleverness in the method – new optimization criteria, the willingness to tackle difficult optimization problems, and satisfactory applications to real problems.

This paper is a great advancement compared to previous methods.

The authors are looking at the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction. They have created a new method and shown that it works well in practice – this paper is complete and well-explained.
