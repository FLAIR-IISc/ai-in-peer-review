- Introduces SEER, a framework emphasizing orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction; idea is promising but under-justified compared to standard spectral methods.  
- IIP (Iterative Inverse Projection) is an elegant technical contribution for approximating SEER, but its treatment of computational challenges is imprecise.  
- Paper lacks clear explanation of how IIP addresses optimization landscape and convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental results are competitive but fail to isolate which component (SEER, orthonormal constraint, or IIP) drives gains.  
- No ablation comparing SEER to non-orthonormal spectral methods to demonstrate the framework’s advantage.  
- No ablation evaluating the specific benefit of the orthonormal representation from Equation (3).  
- The approximation accuracy of IIP versus the exact SEER solution is not quantified, leaving practical validity unclear.  
- Multi-graph extension is presented superficially with weak motivation for why multi-graph is more challenging or beneficial.  
- Algorithmic details for the multi-graph case are sparse: generalization of orthonormal constraints and IIP adaptation are not explained.  
- Table 4 shows a noisy case where single-graph outperforms multi-graph, contradicting the claimed benefits and not addressed.  
- Overreliance on supplementary material harms readability; key definitions, derivations, and algorithmic steps are deferred from the main text.  
- Overall: technically sound and promising, but necessity and specific advantages of SEER/orthonormality are not convincingly established and multi-graph claims remain underdeveloped.  
- Recommended fixes: provide theoretical/empirical justification for orthonormality, quantify IIP approximation error, expand multi-graph motivation and algorithms, resolve Table 4 anomaly, and move essential supplement content into the main paper.
