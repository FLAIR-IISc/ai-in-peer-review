- Derives a PAC-style generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal embeddings, suggesting minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.  
- Proposes SPORE, a formulation that regularizes the spectral norm of the kernel to operationalize that insight.  
- Shows a compelling link between the method's error convergence rate and the graph's Lovász θ number, connecting graph theory and ML.  
- Develops the IIP (infeasible inexact proximal) algorithm to solve the resulting non-smooth convex problem over the elliptope, aiming for better scalability than standard SDP solvers.  
- Extends the framework to a multiple-kernel learning setting for multi-graph transduction.  
- Presents experiments on several datasets that convincingly demonstrate practical benefits over existing methods.  
- Strength: theoretically dense, well-motivated work that bridges fundamental graph properties and learning, with solid empirical validation.  
- Suggestion: clarify the transition from the semi-supervised formulation (1) to the supervised kernel learning formulation (3), specifically why the objective sums only over the labeled set S.  
- Suggestion: state how the orthonormal embedding U is recovered from the optimal kernel K* in practice and note the non-uniqueness (e.g., via eigendecomposition).  
- Suggestion: discuss the computational cost of the PSD projection (eigendecomposition) performed each IIP iteration and consider factorized parametrizations (e.g., Cholesky) as alternatives to avoid repeated decompositions.  
- Minor correction: the citation for Zhang and Ando [19] should be NIPS 2005.
