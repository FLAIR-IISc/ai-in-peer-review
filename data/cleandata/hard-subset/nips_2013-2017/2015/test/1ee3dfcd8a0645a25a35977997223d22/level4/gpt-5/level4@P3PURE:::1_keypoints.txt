- Reduces semi-supervised learning to an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem using Zhang & Ando, then builds remainder of paper on that reduction.
- Provides an error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.
- Augments Zhang & Ando with a spectral-norm regularizer and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.
- Proposes a proximal solver whose key feature is an approximate projection step to handle the cone–polytope intersection constraint (Section 4).
- Presents an MKL extension and reports experiments on several datasets validating the approach (Sections 5–6).
- Paper is interesting and dense, with several notable theoretical and practical contributions.
- Insightful connection between large-scale graph topology (via the Lovász number) and convergence of embedding estimation (Eq. 5).
- Request to explicitly state that Eq. (3) corresponds to a supervised learning problem on labeled samples, clarifying sums over S only.
- Section 2 notation issue: clarify the equals sign immediately before ω_C in Eq. (5).
- Section 3 question: clarify how embedding U is recovered from K in practice and state the chosen factorization(s).
- Section 4 concern: ensuring K_r is PSD via eigendecomposition every iteration may be a main computational bottleneck.
- Suggestion: consider optimizing over a factorization K = L L^T (e.g., Cholesky) so PSD is enforced by construction and projections may be cheaper.
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando citation should be NIPS 2005, not 2006.
- Clarifying the above points—especially supervised reduction, Eq. (5) notation, U-from-K recovery, and PSD projection cost—would strengthen the paper.
