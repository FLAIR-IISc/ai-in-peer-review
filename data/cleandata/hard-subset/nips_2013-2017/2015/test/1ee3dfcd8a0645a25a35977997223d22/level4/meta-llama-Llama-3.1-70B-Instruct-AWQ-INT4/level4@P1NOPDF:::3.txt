The authors investigate the optimization of orthonormal embeddings of a graph and their impact on learning functions on the graph.

The paper begins by reviewing prior work that utilizes a Laplacian and its corresponding generalization bound (eqn. 2). The authors then present their own bound, based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4), which leads to an alternative optimization criterion (eqn. 5). Although the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix, \lambda_1(K), is not differentiable, the resulting optimization criterion is convex, being the sum of convex functions.

To solve the optimization problem, the authors employ inexact proximal methods, specifically designed for the solution of SEER.

The experimental section demonstrates the application of SEER and MKL-SEER to multiple problems, yielding significant performance improvements.

Overall, this paper is very solid, with good theoretical justification. The authors exhibit cleverness in their approach through multiple intuitive leaps, including the derivation of the new optimization criterion, their willingness to tackle the challenging optimization problem, and the successful application to real-world problems.

This paper presents a nice advancement over prior approaches. The authors address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction, derive a novel method, and demonstrate its effectiveness in practice. The paper is complete and well-explained, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
