- Addresses learning vertex labels on graphs, building on Ando & Zhang’s unit-sphere orthonormal embedding idea to improve generalization.
- Main contribution: a joint optimization formulation that simultaneously learns the graph embedding and labels via a new regularizer.
- Regularizer is the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix, motivated by Theorem 1 which gives an upper bound on generalization error.
- Presents asymptotic upper bounds on generalization error with improved scaling over prior work, which translate into labeled sample-complexity bounds.
- Formulates the optimization as an SDP but notes SDPs are costly, so proposes an inexact proximal method that scales to graphs with thousands of nodes.
- Empirical results show the proposed method outperforms previous approaches.
- Reviewer cannot understand Theorem 4 at a high level and finds the explanatory paragraph unclear.
- The paper asserts an assumption that “an edge links similar instances” without defining “similar” or formally including it in the theorem assumptions or proof.
- Reviewer paraphrases Theorem 4’s claim for random graphs as er^{0-1} = O(((n^{3/4}+log(1/δ))^{1/2})/m), implying ω(n^{3/4}) labeled nodes suffice, but is unsure of its correctness or significance due to unclear assumptions.
