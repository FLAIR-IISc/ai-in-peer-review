- Studies optimization of orthonormal graph embeddings and their effect on learning graph-based functions.
- Reviews prior work centered on the graph Laplacian and its generalization bound (Eq. 2).
- Introduces a novel generalization bound based on the maximal eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Eq. 4).
- Derives an alternative optimization objective that incorporates the maximal eigenvalue term (Eq. 5).
- Notes the maximal eigenvalue term is non-differentiable but the overall criterion remains convex as a sum of convex functions.
- Proposes SEER and optimizes the objective using inexact proximal methods.
- Extends method to MKL-SEER and demonstrates significant empirical improvements over existing approaches.
- Paper is strong, well-executed, theoretically grounded, and presents clever insights.
- Effectively tackles non-differentiable optimization and demonstrates practical utility on real-world tasks; presentation is complete and well-articulated.
