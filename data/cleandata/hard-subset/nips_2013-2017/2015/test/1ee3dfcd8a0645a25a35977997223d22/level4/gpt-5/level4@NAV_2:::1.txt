The paper begins by laying out the background and key contributions. It then relies on the Zhang–Ando result to recast the SSL objective as an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem, which underpins the rest of the analysis. In Section 2 and the appendix, the authors derive a generalization bound indicating that controlling the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization. In Section 3, they augment the Zhang–Ando formulation with a spectral-norm regularizer and connect the resulting error behavior to the Lovász number of the graph. Section 4 introduces a proximal algorithm that purposely approximates the projection step to handle the intersection of a cone with a polytope. Section 5 extends the method in an MKL framework, and Section 6 presents empirical results on several datasets.

Overall, the paper is compelling and quite packed (arguably a bit dense for a single submission), with multiple substantive contributions. The explicit link between global graph structure (via the Lovász number), which captures organization in an underlying space/manifold, and the convergence of the learned embedding in Eq. (5) is particularly illuminating and helps bridge several areas.

- Section 1 (post-Equation 3): It would help to spell out more explicitly that the mapping from Eq. (1) to Eq. (3) turns the semi-supervised problem into a supervised one on labeled samples. Otherwise, the restriction of sums to indices in S in Eq. (3) may look surprising compared to Eq. (1), where the loss is on labeled data but the regularizer involves all nodes (including unlabeled ones).

- Section 2: The notation in Equation (5) is confusing—why is there an equals sign directly preceding ωC?

- Section 3 (around line 162): Please clarify how the embedding U is recovered from K in practice. There are multiple viable factorizations, and a brief note on the chosen approach would be useful.

- Section 4:
  • Algorithm 1, Steps 8–9: Ensuring Kt is PSD can be expensive for dense symmetric matrices; the supplementary material (D.3.2) suggests an eigendecomposition is performed at each iteration. Is this the dominant computational cost in practice? Would it be feasible to reparameterize and optimize over a factorization K = LL^T (e.g., Cholesky)? Then projection to the feasible set could act on L (e.g., enforcing a structured form such as zeroing appropriate entries in the upper-triangular part), which would guarantee PSD by construction.
  • Bibliography detail: Zhang and Ando is NIPS 2005, not 2006.

This is a strong paper that elucidates how spectral regularization shapes error bounds for graph transduction via orthonormal embeddings, offering new theoretical insights alongside convincing empirical results.
