- Addresses graph transduction via orthonormal representations and derives a novel PAC generalization bound tied to the kernel spectral norm.
- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embedding that minimizes empirical error plus kernel spectral norm.
- Connects SPORE convergence to the graph's Lovász θ function, linking topology to learning performance.
- Introduces IIP, an inexact projected subgradient method with approximate projection for scalable optimization over the elliptope.
- Extends SPORE to multi-graph settings with an MKL-style approach.
- Empirically validates methods on synthetic and real datasets, showing SPORE outperforms Laplacian-based baselines.
- Strength: strong theoretical contributions (Theorem 1) motivating spectral-norm regularization for embeddings.
- Strength: principled and novel formulation addressing an open problem in selecting orthonormal embeddings.
- Strength: scalable algorithm (IIP) with O(1/√T) convergence and practical scalability to thousands of nodes.
- Strength: comprehensive scope—theory, algorithm, multi-graph extension, and convincing experiments.
- Weakness: clarity—explicitly state transformation of transductive problem to supervised learning over labeled set S after Eq. (3).
- Weakness: algorithmic efficiency—PSD-cone projection via eigendecomposition may be costly; discuss practical cost and whether it bottlenecks scalability.
- Weakness: suggest considering factorized parametrization (K = UUᵀ) as a potentially more efficient PSD-enforcing alternative.
- Weakness: minor clarifications—explain how embedding vectors U are recovered from K* and correct Zhang & Ando NIPS year to 2005.
- Overall conclusion: technically sound, significant contributions, convincing experiments, and recommendation for acceptance.
