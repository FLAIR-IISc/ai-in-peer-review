- Paper studies learning vertex labels on graphs and extends Ando & Zhang by seeking a concrete method to choose graph embeddings.
- Ando & Zhang showed orthonormal-sphere embeddings improve generalization but did not give a procedure for selecting an optimal embedding.
- Main contribution: a joint optimization framework that simultaneously optimizes the embedding and predicted labels via a new regularizer.
- The proposed regularizer is the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix, motivated by Theorem 1 which gives a generalization-error upper bound.
- The paper provides an asymptotic upper bound on generalization error with better scaling than prior work; this can be read as a labeled sample-complexity bound.
- The joint optimization can be written as an SDP, but SDPs are computationally expensive for large graphs.
- To scale, the authors propose an inexact proximal method that can handle graphs with thousands of vertices.
- Experiments indicate the proposed method outperforms existing approaches.
- Reviewer could not fully understand the main theoretical result (Theorem 4) at a high level.
- The paper asserts an assumption that edges link “similar” instances, but “similar” is not precisely defined or included in Theorem 4’s assumptions.
- Reviewer finds no use or reference to the “similarity” assumption in the proof of Theorem 4.
- Reviewer paraphrases Theorem 4 (for random graphs) as er^{0-1}[\hat{y}] = O((1/m) sqrt(n^{3/4} + log(1/δ))) and interprets it to mean a diminishing fraction (~ω(n^{3/4})) of observed labels suffices to recover the rest with small error, but cannot definitively assess due to lack of clarity.
