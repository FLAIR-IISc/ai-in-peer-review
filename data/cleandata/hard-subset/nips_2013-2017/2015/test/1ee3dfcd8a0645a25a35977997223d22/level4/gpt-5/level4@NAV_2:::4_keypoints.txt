- Introduces a new SEER framework for spectrally regularized, orthonormal embeddings in graph transduction.  
- Rationale for using orthonormal embeddings needs stronger, more persuasive argumentation (minor concern).  
- Proposes an appealing, elegant, and original algorithm (IIP-based) to approximately solve the SEER objective.  
- Empirical results generally support the proposed approach.  
- Missing experiments isolating the benefit of the SEER framework and the orthonormal representation (eq. 3) in isolation.  
- Needs quantitative evaluation of the IIP solver’s approximation accuracy for the SEER problem.  
- Multiple-graph transduction extension is described at a high level and lacks sufficient details, motivation, and intuition.  
- Interpretation and instantiation of the multi-graph algorithm and experiments are hard to follow.  
- Table 4 (noisy case) is counterintuitive: a single graph outperforming the multi-graph model contradicts the motivation for combining graphs.  
- Manuscript is sometimes dense and hard to read, with excessive reliance on supplementary material, reducing accessibility and visibility.  
- Overall: presents a novel SEER framework, an IIP-based solver, and reasonably convincing empirical evidence, but needs clearer motivation, more targeted experiments, and better exposition.
