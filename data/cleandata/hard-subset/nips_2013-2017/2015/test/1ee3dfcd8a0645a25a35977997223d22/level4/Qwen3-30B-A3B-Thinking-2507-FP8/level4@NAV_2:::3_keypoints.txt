- Addresses optimal orthonormal embedding selection for graph transduction, filling a gap left by prior consistency results.
- Derives a novel generalization bound (Theorem 1) highlighting the need to minimize the spectral norm of the kernel matrix.
- Proposes the Spectral Norm Regularized Orthonormal Embedding (SPORE) formulation (Equation 5).
- Proves orthonormal representations are efficiently PAC learnable for various graph families including power-law and random graphs.
- Introduces the Inexact Infeasible Proximal (IIP) method for efficient solution of the SPORE optimization.
- Connects the analysis to the Lovász θ function, linking graph structure to sample complexity.
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and avoids infinite VC dimension issues of earlier work.
- Empirical results show SPORE significantly outperforms existing methods and scales to thousands of vertices.
- SPORE outperforms SDP-based approaches which fail beyond a few hundred vertices.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible with consistent notation and comprehensive supplements.
- Original contribution: novel combination of PAC theory, spectral regularization, and proximal methods for embedding selection.
- Extends to multiple graph transduction via MKL-SPORE, demonstrating versatility and improved bounds.
- Strengths: novel PAC analysis, scalable practical algorithm with guarantees, and thorough experimental validation.
- Weaknesses: limited intuition on why spectral norm regularization is superior and incomplete comparison with recent GNNs.
- Weaknesses: theoretical analysis of IIP could give more guidance on choosing approximation parameters.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advance, practical scalability and performance, and clear alignment with conference scope.
- Arguments against acceptance: needs broader comparison with state-of-the-art graph methods and deeper practical interpretation of Lovász θ connections.
