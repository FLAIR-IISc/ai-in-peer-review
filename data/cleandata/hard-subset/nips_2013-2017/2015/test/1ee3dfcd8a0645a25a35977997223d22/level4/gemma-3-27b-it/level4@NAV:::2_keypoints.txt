- Paper addresses graph vertex label learning, building on Ando and Zhang’s unit-sphere orthonormal embeddings to improve generalization.
- Ando and Zhang showed benefit of such embeddings but did not provide a method to choose an optimal embedding.
- Main contribution: a novel optimization formulation that jointly learns the embedding and vertex labels with a regularizer motivated by Theorem 1’s generalization-error upper bound.
- Provides asymptotic upper bounds on the proposed method’s generalization error with favorable scaling, and derives an upper bound on labeled sample complexity.
- Recognizes the SDP computational cost and proposes an inexact proximal optimization method to scale to graphs with thousands of vertices.
- Experiments show the proposed method outperforms prior approaches in empirical evaluation.
- Reviewer found Theorem 4 difficult to understand and the exposition around it unclear.
- The paper invokes an informal “similarity” assumption (edges connect similar instances) but does not define “similarity” or state it explicitly as an assumption in Theorem 4.
- The notion of similarity also appears absent from the proof of Theorem 4, creating a gap between claim and justification.
- Theorem 4’s stated claim for random graphs suggests recovery of remaining labels when observed labels are roughly ω(n^{3/4}), but the reviewer could not verify this due to the preceding ambiguities.
- Due to the difficulty in understanding the core theorem, the reviewer could not form a definitive assessment of the paper’s overall contribution.
