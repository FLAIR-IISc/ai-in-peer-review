This paper introduces the background and main contributions, then builds on the Zhang and Ando result to reduce semi-supervised learning (SSL) to an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem for the remainder of the work. In Section 2 (and the appendix), it provides an error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix is a principled way to improve generalization. Section 3 augments the Zhang and Ando formulation with a spectral-norm regularizer and connects error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph. Section 4 proposes a proximal solver whose key feature is an approximate projection step to handle the cone–polytope intersection constraint. Section 5 presents an MKL extension, and Section 6 reports experiments on several datasets.

Overall, the paper is interesting and dense (perhaps slightly too dense for a single paper) and contains several notable contributions. The link between large-scale topological properties of the data graph (via the Lovász number), which reflect its organization in a space or manifold, and the convergence of the embedding estimation (Eq. 5) is particularly insightful and helps bridge several areas.

Comments and questions by section:
- Section 1 (after Eq. 3): Please make explicit that the correspondence between Eqs. (3) and (1) implies that the semi-supervised learning problem becomes a supervised learning problem, with learning performed on labeled samples. Otherwise, it is surprising to see sums over S only (with no S̄) in Eq. (3), whereas in Eq. (1) the regularizer involves unlabeled samples while the loss is on labeled data.
- Section 2: The notation in Eq. (5) is confusing—why is there an equals sign immediately before ω_C?
- Section 3 (around line 162): Please clarify how the embedding U is recovered from K in practice. There are several possible factorizations, and it would help to state the choice(s) you use.
- Section 4:
  - Algorithm 1, Steps 8–9: Ensuring K_r is PSD for general dense symmetric matrices can be expensive; as written (Supplement D.3.2), an eigendecomposition is performed at every iteration. Is this the main computational bottleneck? Would it be feasible to optimize directly over a factorization K = L L^T (e.g., Cholesky), so that PSD is enforced by construction? In that parameterization, projection to the feasible set would reduce to a projection on L (e.g., zeroing the upper-triangular part), which might be more efficient.
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando is NIPS 2005, not 2006.

This is an interesting paper that elucidates how spectral regularization affects error bounds for graph transduction with orthonormal embeddings, offering new theoretical insights supported by convincing experiments. I found the theoretical links to the Lovász number and the practical solver contributions valuable. Clarifying the points above—especially the supervised reduction in Eq. (3), the notation in Eq. (5), the recovery of U from K, and the computational profile of the PSD projection—would further strengthen the paper.
