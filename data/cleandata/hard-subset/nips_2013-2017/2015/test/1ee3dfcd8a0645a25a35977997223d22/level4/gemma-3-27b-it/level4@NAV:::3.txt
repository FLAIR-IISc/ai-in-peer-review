This paper investigates optimizing orthonormal graph embeddings and their effect on learning functions defined on graphs.

The authors begin by reviewing related work grounded in Laplacian-based approaches and their associated generalization bounds (eqn. 2). They then present their own bound, predicated on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4), which motivates a novel, convex optimization criterion (eqn. 5) – despite the non-differentiability of λ₁(K). 

The proposed solution, SEER, is addressed using inexact proximal methods. 

Empirical evaluation demonstrates substantial performance gains achieved by applying SEER and MKL-SEER across a range of problems.

Overall, this is a strong contribution. The work is well-supported by theoretical analysis and showcases insightful problem-solving through innovative steps – namely, the development of the new optimization criterion, the ambition to address the challenging optimization problem, and successful application to real-world datasets. This represents a notable improvement over existing methods for graph transduction. The paper is complete and clearly articulated, presenting a novel and effective method with compelling practical results.
