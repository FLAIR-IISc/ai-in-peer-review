The paper proposes a novel framework for spectral regularized embedding, applied to graph transduction, which is a valuable contribution to the field. However, the benefits of having an orthonormal embedding could be more convincingly explained to further justify the framework. This is a minor issue that does not detract from the overall quality of the paper.

The authors' proposed algorithm for solving the SEER problem approximately using Iterative Improvement Procedure (IIP) is an original and elegant contribution, offering a viable alternative for tackling the complex learning problem. The experimental results generally demonstrate the benefits of the proposed solution, although it would be beneficial to provide additional results that showcase the advantages of the SEER framework and the orthonormal representation defined by equation (3). Furthermore, evaluating the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution to the SEER problem would provide valuable context for understanding the actual contribution of the paper.

The extension of the framework to multiple graph transduction is an interesting concept, but it is described at a very high level, making it challenging to grasp the main ideas and appreciate the experimental results in this context. More details, motivation, and intuitions are necessary to clearly explain how and why the proposed method is suitable for multiple graphs. Specifically, it would be helpful to elaborate on how the algorithm is implemented in such settings. The results presented in the last column of Table 4 (noisy case) are not intuitive, as they suggest that using one graph is better than using multiple graphs, which contradicts the motivation for dealing with multiple graphs.

The paper's readability is sometimes compromised by its density and the frequent references to supplementary material. This may indicate that the authors are trying to convey too much information in a single paper, which can negatively impact the accessibility and visibility of their work. Overall, the paper proposes a new framework, SEER, for orthonormal embeddings applied to graph transduction, along with an interesting IIP-based algorithm for solving the learning problem, and presents relatively convincing results.
