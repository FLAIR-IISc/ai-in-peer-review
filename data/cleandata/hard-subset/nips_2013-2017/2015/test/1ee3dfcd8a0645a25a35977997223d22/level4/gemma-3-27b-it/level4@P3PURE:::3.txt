The authors investigate the optimization of orthonormal embeddings for graphs and the subsequent impact on learning functions defined on those graphs.

The paper begins by reviewing related work utilizing Laplacians and their associated generalization bounds (eqn. 2). It then presents the authors’ bound, which is based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4). This bound motivates a novel optimization criterion – equation (5); while \lambda_1(K) is not differentiable, the resulting criterion is convex, being a sum of convex functions. 

To solve the resulting Spectral Embedding via Eigenvalue Regularization (SEER) problem, the authors employ inexact proximal methods.

The experimental section demonstrates the application of SEER and MKL-SEER to several problems, showcasing significant performance improvements.

Overall, this is a very strong paper with solid theoretical justification. The work exhibits cleverness in several areas – the derivation of the new optimization criterion, the willingness to address the challenging optimization problem it presents, and the successful application to real-world problems. This paper represents a noteworthy advancement over prior approaches to graph transduction. The authors tackle the problem of finding optimal embeddings for graph transduction, derive a novel method, and convincingly demonstrate its practical effectiveness; the paper is both complete and well-explained.
