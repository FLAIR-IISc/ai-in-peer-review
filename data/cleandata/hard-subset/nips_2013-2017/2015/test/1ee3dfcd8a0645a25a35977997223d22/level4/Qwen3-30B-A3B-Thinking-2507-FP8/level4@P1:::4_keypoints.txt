- Paper introduces SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction and formulates it as a convex optimization over the elliptope.  
- Authors prove PAC learnability for orthonormal embeddings and show equivalence to graph kernel matrices with normalized diagonals.  
- Theorem 1 gives a generalization bound suggesting spectral norm regularization improves generalization.  
- Main technical contribution is the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm that scales beyond standard SDP solvers.  
- IIP has rigorous convergence guarantees (Theorems 5 and 6) with an O(1/√T) rate.  
- Paper extends the framework to multiple graph transduction via an MKL-style MKL-SPORE formulation.  
- Work connects well to prior NIPS papers: fills gaps left by [1] and [16] on optimal embedding selection and efficient computation.  
- PAC analysis is technically sound, using Rademacher averages appropriately and improving sample-complexity estimates over prior VC-based analyses.  
- IIP algorithm is novel, well-motivated, and addresses a key computational bottleneck for large graphs.  
- MKL-SPORE provides a principled approach to combining multiple graph views.  
- Clarity issue: connection between orthonormal representations and kernel learning (Eq. 3) is under-motivated.  
- Experimental weakness: lacks comparison to the unregularized version of the same kernel learning approach, making it hard to isolate the benefit of spectral regularization.  
- Multiple-graph section is too brief and insufficiently motivated; preference over simpler weighted-kernel baselines is not clearly justified.  
- Results on the “noisy” multiple-graph case are confusing and need explanation for why adding a worse graph improves performance.  
- Paper over-relies on supplementary material; key proofs/derivations should be moved to the main text for accessibility.  
- Notational inconsistency/typo: use of “SEER” instead of “SPORE” is confusing and should be corrected.  
- Theoretical contributions, SPORE formulation, and IIP algorithm are high quality and practically significant for large-graph transduction.  
- Originality is strong: spectral-norm regularization for orthonormal embeddings and the IIP solver are novel.  
- Significance: advances sample-complexity understanding and offers scalable methods for multi-view graph learning.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve experimental isolation of spectral regularization, expand/motivate the multiple-graph section, fix notation, and move key proofs into the main text.
