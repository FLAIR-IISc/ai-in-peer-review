- Problem: optimizes orthonormal/unit-sphere embeddings for graph transduction.  
- Main contribution: introduces SPORE, a spectral-norm regularizer to find optimal embeddings.  
- Theory: derives a PAC-based generalization bound (Theorem 1) that avoids VC-dimension issues.  
- Algorithm: proposes IIP, an efficient, scalable solver for the resulting non-smooth convex problem.  
- Extension: develops MKL-SPORE to handle multiple graphs with supporting theory and experiments.  
- Positioning: clearly situated within unit-sphere embedding literature and highlights prior limitations in embedding choice and computation.  
- Strength — theory: strong PAC-based theoretical foundation linking bound to the regularizer.  
- Strength — novelty: spectral norm regularization and the IIP algorithm are novel contributions.  
- Strength — scalability: IIP scales significantly better than SDP-based approaches.  
- Strength — presentation: well-written with consistent, effective mathematical notation.  
- Strength — experiments: empirical validation on real and synthetic datasets demonstrates effectiveness.  
- Strength — versatility: multiple-graph extension addresses a practical, relevant scenario.  
- Weakness — parameter tuning: cross-validation details for C and β and sensitivity analysis are insufficient.  
- Weakness — baselines: comparison set is limited; broader SOTA comparisons beyond kernel-based methods are needed.  
- Weakness — complexity: computational complexity analysis of IIP is limited and lacks per-step cost breakdown.  
- Weakness — assumptions: the similarity-graph assumption in the multi-graph setting needs clearer discussion of implications and limits.  
- Acceptance case: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically strong, and practically scalable — recommend acceptance.  
- Revision needs: address parameter sensitivity, expand baseline comparisons, and provide a more detailed complexity analysis.
