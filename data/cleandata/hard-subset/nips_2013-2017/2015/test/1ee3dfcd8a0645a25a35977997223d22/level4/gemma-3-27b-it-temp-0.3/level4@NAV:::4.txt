The paper introduces a novel framework for spectral regularized embedding, focusing on its application to graph transduction. While the advantages of employing an orthonormal embedding within this framework could be further emphasized, this is a minor point.

The authors subsequently present a compelling and well-designed algorithm for approximately solving the resulting Spectral Embedding with Orthonormal Regularization (SEER) problem. This represents a significant and original contribution, offering a promising alternative for tackling this challenging learning task.

The experimental results generally support the effectiveness of the proposed solution. However, it would strengthen the paper to include results specifically illustrating the benefits of the SEER framework itself, or of the orthonormal representation defined in equation (3), independently. Furthermore, demonstrating the approximation accuracy of the Iterative Improvement Procedure (IIP) solution relative to the SEER problem would be valuable for contextualizing the contribution. Currently, this aspect is not clearly evident in the presented results.

The extension to multiple graph transduction is described at a rather abstract level. While conceptually interesting, greater detail, motivation, and intuition are needed to fully understand the rationale for applying the method to multiple graphs and how the algorithm is implemented in such scenarios. The current presentation makes it difficult to fully grasp the key ideas and appreciate the experimental findings in this setting. Specifically, the results in the noisy case (last column of Table 4) are counterintuitive, suggesting a potential advantage to using a single graph, which seems to conflict with the motivation for considering multiple graphs.

Overall, the paper is somewhat dense and can be challenging to follow, with frequent references to supplementary material. This may indicate an attempt to include an excessive amount of information within a single paper, potentially hindering its accessibility and impact. Nevertheless, the paper introduces a new framework, SEER, for orthonormal embeddings applied to graph transduction, and proposes an interesting IIP-based algorithm with reasonably convincing results.
