- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction linking Lovász θ and PAC learnability.  
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) algorithm to solve the non-smooth convex SPORE problem efficiently for large graphs.  
- Extends method to multiple graphs (MKL-style) with theoretical and experimental validation.  
- Builds on prior work (Zhang & Ando 2005) and provides a strong theoretical analysis connecting graph topology to generalization.  
- IIP offers scalability advantages over traditional SDP-based approaches.  
- Experiments are convincing, showing SPORE outperforms existing methods on multiple datasets.  
- Paper is well-motivated and addresses an important problem in semi-supervised graph learning.  
- Paper is dense and could be reorganized for clarity; notation and presentation need simplification.  
- Equation (5) uses an unusual equality sign before ω_C and should be rewritten as a clear definition or assignment.  
- After equation (3), explicitly state that the supervised transformation focuses on labeled samples S to avoid index confusion.  
- Add brief discussion on recovering embedding U from optimal kernel K* and note multiple factorization choices.  
- PSD projection in Algorithm 1 (steps 8–9) requires eigendecomposition each iteration and may be a computational bottleneck.  
- Suggest exploring alternatives (Cholesky factorization, maintaining PSD via factorization, projecting upper-triangular constraints) to reduce cost.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, theoretically sound paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify notation, embedding recovery, and improve PSD projection efficiency.
