- Paper proposes a new joint optimization formulation that learns graph vertex labels while optimizing a unit-sphere embedding via a spectral-norm regularizer.  
- Regularizer choice is motivated by Theorem 1, which gives an upper bound on generalization error; asymptotic bounds show favorable scaling and yield labeled sample-complexity bounds.  
- The optimization can be cast as an SDP, but authors propose an inexact proximal method to scale to graphs with thousands of nodes.  
- Experiments report that the proposed method outperforms prior approaches on tested tasks.  
- Reviewer could not understand the high-level meaning or implications of Theorem 4.  
- Theorem 4’s preceding claim that “edges link similar instances” lacks a formal definition of “similar” and this assumption is not stated or used explicitly in the theorem or its proof.  
- Reviewer summarizes Theorem 4 (for random graphs) as giving an error bound er^{0-1}[\hat{y}] = O(1/m · (n^{3/4} + log(1/δ))^{1/2}), implying recovery with a diminishing fraction (~ω(n^{3/4})) of observed labels.  
- The paper needs clearer exposition of Theorem 4, its assumptions, and how “similarity” is formalized and used.  
- The inexact proximal method is presented as practical, but the reviewer requests more detailed explanation of the algorithm and its advantages over solving the SDP directly.  
- Strengths: novel formulation, better asymptotic/sample-complexity scaling, scalable optimization approach, supporting empirical results.  
- Weaknesses: unclear main theorem and missing definition/use of “similar,” and insufficient detail on the proximal optimization method.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel interesting formulation, favorable theoretical scaling, and empirical superiority.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of clarity around Theorem 4 and the “similarity” assumption, and need for more algorithmic detail on the inexact proximal solver.
