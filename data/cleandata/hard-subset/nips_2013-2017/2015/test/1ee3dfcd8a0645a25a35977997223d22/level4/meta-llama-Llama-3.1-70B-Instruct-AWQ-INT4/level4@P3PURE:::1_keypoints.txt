- Paper uses Zhang and Ando’s reduction to cast SSL as a kernel-based supervised learning problem and builds the work on that foundation.
- Section 2 derives an error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.
- Section 3 adds a spectral-norm regularizer to the Zhang-Ando formulation and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.
- Section 4 proposes a proximal solver that approximates the projection step to handle cone–polytope intersection constraints.
- Section 5 presents a multiple-kernel learning (MKL) extension; Section 6 reports experiments on several datasets validating the approach.
- Strength: insightful connection between large-scale graph topology (Lovász number) and embedding-convergence, bridging multiple fields.
- Clarify after Eq. (3) that the correspondence with Eq. (1) reduces SSL to supervised learning on labeled samples to avoid confusion about index sets.
- Notation in Eq. (5) is confusing—specifically the equal sign before ω_C should be clarified.
- In Section 3, explain practical choices for recovering the embedding U from K (factorization methods).
- Algorithm 1 (Section 4) may be computationally heavy due to repeated eigendecompositions to enforce PSD; confirm if this is the main cost.
- Suggest considering optimization over a factorization of K (e.g., K = LL^T / Cholesky) to enforce PSD by construction and potentially reduce computation.
- Correct the Zhang and Ando citation to NIPS 2005 (not 2006).
