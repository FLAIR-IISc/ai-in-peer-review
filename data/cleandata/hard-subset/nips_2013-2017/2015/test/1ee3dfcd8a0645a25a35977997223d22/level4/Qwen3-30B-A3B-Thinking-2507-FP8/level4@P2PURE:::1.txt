

The paper begins with a clear introduction of background and contributions. The authors subsequently employ the Zhang and Ando result, establishing that semi-supervised learning (SSL) reduces to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem for the remainder of the work. Sections 2 and the appendix present an error bound demonstrating that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix provides a principled approach to enhancing generalization. Section 3 incorporates a spectral norm regularization term into the Zhang and Ando formulation, establishing connections between error convergence and the Lovász number of the data graph. Section 4 introduces a proximal solver, specifically addressing the challenge of approximately handling the projection step for the cone-polytope intersection constraint. Section 5 proposes a multiple kernel learning (MKL) extension, and Section 6 concludes with experimental results across several datasets.

Overall, the paper is interesting and presents substantial material (potentially slightly dense for a single publication), featuring several significant contributions. The linkage between large-scale topological properties of the data graph (via the Lovász number, reflecting its organization within a space or manifold) and the convergence of the embedding estimation (Equation 5) is particularly insightful, bridging multiple research fields.

In Section 1, following Equation 3, it would be beneficial to explicitly state that the correspondence between Equations (3) and (1) implies a semi-supervised learning problem reduces to a supervised learning problem, where learning occurs solely on labeled samples. This clarification is needed to avoid confusion regarding the summation indices over $S$ only in Equation 3 (without $\bar{S}$), contrasting with Equation 1 where the regularizer involves non-labeled samples while the loss is confined to labeled data.

In Section 2, Equation 5 uses an equality symbol immediately preceding $\omega_C$, which is confusing and requires clarification.

In Section 3, around line 162, the method for recovering the embedding $U$ from $K$ should be specified, as multiple practical factorization choices exist.

In Section 4, Algorithm 1 steps 8–9 require ensuring $K_r$ is positive semi-definite (PSD), typically computationally expensive for general dense symmetric matrices. The current approach performs an eigendecomposition at each step (Supplementary D.3.2). Is this the dominant computational cost? Would it be feasible to optimize directly on a factorization of $K$ (e.g., via Cholesky decomposition $K = LL^T$), where projection to the feasible set would set the upper triangular part to zero, thereby guaranteeing PSD $K$ by construction?

Note: Zhang and Ando is cited as NIPS 2005, not 2006.

This is an interesting paper that elucidates the impact of spectral regularization on error bounds in graph transduction using orthonormal embedding, providing novel theoretical insights and compelling experimental validation.
