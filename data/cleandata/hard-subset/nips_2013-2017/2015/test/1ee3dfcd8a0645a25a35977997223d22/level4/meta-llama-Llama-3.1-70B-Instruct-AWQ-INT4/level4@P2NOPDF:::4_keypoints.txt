- Proposes a novel spectral-regularized embedding (SEER) framework for graph transduction; valuable contribution.
- Need stronger justification for the benefits of enforcing orthonormal embeddings.
- Introduces an original and elegant Iterative Improvement Procedure (IIP) to approximately solve SEER.
- Experimental results generally support the proposed solution but more experiments are needed to highlight SEER’s and the orthonormal representation’s advantages (eq. 3).
- Should evaluate the approximation accuracy of IIP relative to the true SEER solution.
- Multiple-graph extension is interesting but described too high-level; requires more details, motivation, and implementation specifics.
- Noisy-case results (Table 4) are counterintuitive—using one graph outperforming multiple—needs explanation.
- Paper readability suffers from density and heavy reliance on supplementary material.
- Overall, SEER and the IIP algorithm are promising and present relatively convincing results.
