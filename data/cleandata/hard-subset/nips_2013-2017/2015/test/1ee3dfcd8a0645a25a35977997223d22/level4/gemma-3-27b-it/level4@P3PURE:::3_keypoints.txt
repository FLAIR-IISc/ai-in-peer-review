- Investigates optimization of orthonormal embeddings for graphs and their effect on learning functions over graphs.
- Reviews related work using graph Laplacians and existing generalization bounds (eqn. 2).
- Introduces a new generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4).
- Motivates a novel optimization criterion derived from that bound (eqn. 5).
- Notes λ1(K) is non-differentiable but the resulting criterion is convex as a sum of convex functions.
- Formulates the SEER (Spectral Embedding via Eigenvalue Regularization) problem.
- Solves SEER using inexact proximal methods.
- Extends the approach to MKL-SEER (multiple kernel learning) in experiments.
- Empirical results show significant performance improvements on several tasks.
- Paper offers strong theoretical justification, clever derivations, and represents a notable advance in graph transduction; well-explained and complete.
