- Addresses vertex label learning on graphs, extending Ando & Zhang’s sphere embedding idea to improve generalization.
- Main contribution: a joint optimization that simultaneously learns the graph embedding and vertex labels with a regularizer.
- Regularizer: spectral norm of the induced graph kernel matrix, motivated by Theorem 1 bounding generalization error.
- Provides asymptotic upper bounds on generalization error and corresponding labeled sample complexity, claiming favorable scaling vs prior work.
- Optimization can be cast as an SDP, but the authors propose an inexact proximal method to scale to graphs with thousands of nodes.
- Experiments indicate the proposed method outperforms previous approaches.
- Reviewer reports difficulty grasping Theorem 4 at a high level and assessing the core result.
- Paper claims learning is impossible without assumptions; follows literature by assuming edges link “similar” instances but never defines “similar.”
- The “similar” assumption is not formally stated in Theorem 4’s assumptions and appears unused in its proof, per the reviewer.
- Reviewer paraphrases Theorem 4 roughly: on a large random graph with random {0,1} labels, observing a diminishing fraction of labels (e.g., ω(n^{3/4})) suffices to recover remaining labels with small error w.h.p.
- Due to the ambiguity around key assumptions and the opaque presentation of Theorem 4, reviewer cannot render a comprehensive judgment.
