- Proposes SEER, a new framework for regularized spectral embedding applied to graph transduction.
- Motivation for enforcing an orthonormal embedding (benefits) is not convincingly described.
- Introduces an interesting, elegant, and original approximate algorithm to solve the SEER optimization.
- The proposed algorithm is a promising alternative for tackling this complex learning problem.
- Experimental results are encouraging and show benefits of the proposed solution.
- Evaluation needs strengthening by isolating and demonstrating advantages of SEER, particularly the orthonormal representation (eq.3).
- Missing analysis quantifying the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution relative to the true SEER objective.
- Multi-graph extension is described at too high a level and lacks detail, motivation, intuition, and implementation specifics.
- Counter-intuitive result in Table 4 (noisy case) where a single graph outperforms multiple graphs undermines the multi-graph motivation.
- Presentation issues: writing is dense and the paper over-relies on supplementary material for key details.
- Overall: valuable framework and compelling algorithm, but needs more thorough experimental validation and clearer exposition of the multi-graph extension.
