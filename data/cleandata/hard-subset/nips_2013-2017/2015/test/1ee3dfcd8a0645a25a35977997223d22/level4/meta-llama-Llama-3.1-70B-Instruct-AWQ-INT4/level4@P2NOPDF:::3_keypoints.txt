- Paper studies optimization of orthonormal graph embeddings and their effect on learning functions over graphs.
- Reviews prior Laplacian-based generalization bound (eqn. 2) as foundation.
- Introduces a novel generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4).
- Derives an alternative convex optimization criterion from that bound despite the non-differentiability of the max eigenvalue (eqn. 5).
- Uses inexact proximal methods to solve the convex problem, specifically the SEER algorithm.
- Proposes an extension, MKL-SEER, for multiple-kernel settings.
- Experiments show significant performance improvements across several tasks.
- Paper is well-structured, theoretically sound, and demonstrates clever derivations and intuition.
- Represents a meaningful advancement in finding optimal embeddings for graph transduction and is comprehensive and well-explained.
