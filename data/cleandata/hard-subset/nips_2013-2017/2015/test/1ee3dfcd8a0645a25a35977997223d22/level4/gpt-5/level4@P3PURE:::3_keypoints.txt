- Studies optimization of an orthonormal graph embedding and its effect on learning functions, focusing on graph transduction.
- Reviews prior work that uses the graph Laplacian and its associated generalization bound (eqn. 2).
- Introduces a new generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4).
- Motivates an alternative optimization criterion derived from the new bound (eqn. 5).
- Notes λ1(K) is nondifferentiable but the overall objective remains convex as a sum of convex functions.
- Solves the resulting optimization for SEER using inexact proximal methods.
- Empirical results show SEER and MKL-SEER produce significant performance improvements across multiple tasks.
- Paper offers strong theoretical justification and several intuitive, clever contributions (new criterion, tackling difficult optimization).
- Represents a meaningful advancement over prior approaches to optimal embedding for graph transduction.
- Presentation is complete and well-explained, and the method is demonstrated to work well in practice.
