The authors investigate the optimization of orthonormal embeddings of graphs and their impact on learning functions defined on these graphs. They begin by reviewing prior work that utilizes the Laplacian and its corresponding generalization bound (eqn. 2). The authors then introduce their own bound, based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4), which leads to an alternative optimization criterion (eqn. 5). Although the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix, λ₁(K), is not differentiable, the resulting optimization problem is convex, being the sum of convex functions. To solve this problem, the authors employ inexact proximal methods for the SEER algorithm. The experimental section demonstrates the application of SEER and MKL-SEER to multiple problems, yielding significant performance improvements. Overall, this paper is well-founded, with a strong theoretical justification. The authors exhibit cleverness in their approach through several intuitive leaps, including the derivation of the new optimization criterion, their willingness to tackle the challenging optimization problem, and the successful application to real-world problems. This paper presents a valuable advancement over prior approaches. By addressing the problem of optimal embedding for graph transduction, the authors develop a novel method and demonstrate its efficacy in practice, resulting in a complete and well-explained paper.
