- Summary: Introduces SEER, a spectral regularization framework with an IIP-based solver for orthonormal graph embeddings; algorithmic elegance and generally positive empirical results.

- Strength: Novelty and elegance — SEER formulation and IIP solver are original, well-motivated, and offer a practical alternative to complex exact solvers.

- Strength: Empirical promise — Experiments indicate competitive performance on standard graph transduction tasks.

- Concern 1 — Orthonormality motivation: The paper fails to convincingly justify why orthonormal constraints are crucial or how they improve transduction versus unconstrained/alternative regularizations.

- Concern 1 suggestion: Provide ablations (with/without orthonormality or alternative normalizations), theoretical/qualitative intuition (stability, de-correlation, avoiding trivial solutions), and a synthetic example illustrating benefits.

- Concern 2 — IIP approximation quality: The manuscript does not quantify how closely the IIP solver approximates the SEER solution or characterize its convergence behavior.

- Concern 2 suggestions: Report optimality/duality gaps on small instances, plot objective vs iterations/time, compare to alternative solvers, and show sensitivity to initialization and hyperparameters plus convergence discussion.

- Concern 3 — Multi-graph transduction underdeveloped: The multi-graph extension is presented high-level, lacking clarity on combination strategy, weighting, complexity, inductive biases, and when multiple graphs help.

- Concern 3 suggestions: Clarify how graphs are combined and weights set/learned, analyze noise effects (why single graph can beat multiple), show learned weights, sensitivity to noise, statistical significance, and provide implementation details for reproducibility.

- Concern 4 — Positioning and context: Needs clearer comparisons to established graph transduction/spectral methods and multi-view approaches, plus explicit cases where SEER outperforms and runtime/complexity comparisons.

- Concern 5 — Clarity and organization: Paper is dense with key details in the supplement; move core objective, notation, algorithm pseudocode, complexity analysis, and proof sketches into the main text and add experiment takeaways.

- Experimental recommendations: Run ablations isolating (i) orthonormal vs non-orthonormal, (ii) IIP vs alternative solvers, (iii) single- vs multi-graph with diagnostics on learned weights and noise robustness; report mean±std, paired tests, convergence curves, and include a small-scale near-exact benchmark.

- Overall assessment: Promising and technically interesting but borderline — needs stronger justification for orthonormal embeddings, quantitative evidence of IIP approximation quality, a deeper multi-graph treatment, and a more streamlined presentation to fully support its claims.
