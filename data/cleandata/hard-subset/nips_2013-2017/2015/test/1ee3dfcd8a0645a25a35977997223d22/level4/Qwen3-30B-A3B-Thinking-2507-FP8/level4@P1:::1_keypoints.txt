- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction, motivated by generalization analysis.  
- Proves orthonormal graph embeddings are PAC learnable, filling a gap in prior work.  
- Derives a generalization bound (Theorem 1) showing minimizing spectral norm of the kernel matrix improves generalization.  
- Formulates SPORE that regularizes the spectral norm while enforcing orthonormal embedding constraints.  
- Provides improved sample complexity (Theorem 4): Ω(ϑ/n)^{1/2}, substantially better than prior Ω(ϑ/n)^{1/3}.  
- Develops an efficient Inexact Infeasible Proximal (IIP) algorithm to solve SPORE, avoiding SDP bottlenecks.  
- Demonstrates scalability: IIP works on graphs with thousands of vertices where standard SDP solvers fail.  
- Extends framework to multiple graph transduction via an MKL-style approach (MKL-SPORE) and shows robustness in noisy settings.  
- Empirical results (Tables 1–4) show SPORE outperforming normalized/unnormalized Laplacian and K-scaling baselines across datasets.  
- Theoretically connects graph structure (Lovász θ function) to generalization error, giving insight into embedding performance.  
- Paper is well-structured and technically sound, with clear contextualization against prior NIPS work (noting [1] focuses on Laplacians).  
- Requires clarifications: explicit correspondence between Eq.(3) and Eq.(1) regarding labeled vs unlabeled data.  
- Notational issues: Eq.(5) has a confusing equals sign before ω_C and needs consistency.  
- Needs clarity on recovering orthonormal embedding U from kernel K (multiple factorizations exist).  
- Implementation concern: PSD projection via eigendecomposition (Alg.1 steps 8–9) may dominate runtime; consider optimizing over factorization K = LL^T.  
- Writing is generally clear but some sections (preliminaries on orthonormal representations) are dense and less accessible.  
- Minor reference correction: Zhang and Ando should be cited as 2005, not 2006.  
- Evaluation summary: Quality — excellent; Clarity — generally good with minor issues; Originality — high; Significance — significant.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address notational and implementation clarifications.
