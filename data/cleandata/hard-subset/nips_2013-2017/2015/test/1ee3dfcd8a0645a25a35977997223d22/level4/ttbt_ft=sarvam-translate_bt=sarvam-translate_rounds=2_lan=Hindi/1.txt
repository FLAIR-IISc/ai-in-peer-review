The paper begins with an outline of the background and contributions. Then, the authors use the results of Zhang and Endo, which show that SSL reduces to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem for the rest of the paper. In Section 2 and the appendix, they provide an error bound, suggesting that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix is a good way to improve generalization. In Section 3, they add a spectral norm regularization term to the Zhang and Endo formulation, which provides a link between error convergence and the Lovelace number of the data graph. Section 4 proposes a proximal solver, where the uniqueness is that it approximately handles the bottleneck of the cone-polytope intersection from the estimation step. Section 5 proposes an MKL extension, and Section 6 concludes with experimental results on several datasets.

Overall, this paper is interesting and contains a lot of material (perhaps a bit dense for a single paper), with many important contributions. The relationship between the large-scale topological properties (Lovelace number) of the data graph, which reflects its organization in a space or manifold, and the convergence of the embedding estimate (Equation 5) are particularly insightful and connect several areas.

In Section 2, the notation of Equation 5 is confusing - why is there an equals sign just before \omega_C?
