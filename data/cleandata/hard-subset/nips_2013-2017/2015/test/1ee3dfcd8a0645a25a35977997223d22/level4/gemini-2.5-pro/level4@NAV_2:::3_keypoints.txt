- Problem: learning an optimal orthonormal (unit-sphere) embedding for graph transduction; selecting the best embedding from this class was open.  
- Theoretical contribution: novel PAC-style generalization bound for orthonormal embeddings that depends on the kernel spectral norm λ₁(K) instead of the trace.  
- Formulation contribution: SPORE—a convex but non-smooth optimization over the elliptope that minimizes empirical hinge loss plus a spectral-norm regularizer.  
- Algorithmic contribution: IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) method—projected subgradient with approximate projection, proven O(1/√T) convergence, scalable versus SDP.  
- Extension: approach generalized to multi-graph learning.  
- Strength (quality): technically sound, complete pipeline from theory to experiments with convincing empirical validation.  
- Strength (originality): novel spectral-norm bound, new SPORE formulation, and an important practical IIP optimization algorithm.  
- Strength (significance): answers the open embedding-selection question, achieves state-of-the-art results, and provides a broadly useful scalable optimizer.  
- Strength (clarity): well-written and well-organized; logical progression from theory to method to algorithm.  
- Weakness: theoretical hyperparameter choices (C, β) depend on the computationally expensive Lovász θ; experiments use cross-validation and lack discussion linking practical choices to theory.  
- Acceptance summary: strong theory motivating SPORE, a novel scalable algorithm with guarantees, and significant empirical improvements on large datasets.
