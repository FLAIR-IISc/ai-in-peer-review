- Focus: optimizing an orthonormal graph embedding to improve learning functions defined over graphs.
- Background: revisits prior Laplacian-kernel-based generalization bound (eqn. 2).
- Novel theory: introduces a new generalization bound depending on the top eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4).
- Objective: proposes an alternative objective motivated by the new bound (eqn. 5); λ1(K) is non-differentiable but the total objective remains convex.
- Optimization method: employs inexact proximal techniques to optimize SEER.
- Extension: presents an MKL-SEER variant (multiple-kernel learning extension).
- Empirical results: SEER and MKL-SEER demonstrate substantial performance gains across several tasks/datasets.
- Strengths: provides solid theoretical support and contains several insightful, ingenious steps (new objective and tackling a challenging optimization).
- Contribution: represents a meaningful improvement over prior approaches for learning optimal graph embeddings for transduction.
