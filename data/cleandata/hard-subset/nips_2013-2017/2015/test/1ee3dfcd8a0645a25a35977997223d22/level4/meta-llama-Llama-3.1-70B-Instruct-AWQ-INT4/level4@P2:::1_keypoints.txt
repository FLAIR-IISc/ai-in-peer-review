- Proposes a novel graph transduction approach using orthonormal representations with spectral norm regularization.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis including a generalization bound and PAC learnability results.  
- Leverages Zhang and Ando’s reduction that converts semi-supervised learning to an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem.  
- Shows that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Adds a spectral-norm regularization term to the Zhang-Ando formulation and analyzes its impact on error convergence.  
- Introduces a proximal solver that handles the projection step approximately to manage a cone–polytope intersection constraint.  
- Presents an MKL extension for multiple graph transduction as an interesting practical extension.  
- Experimental results are convincing with thorough comparisons to existing methods.  
- Paper is well-written and explains technical details clearly, but is somewhat dense for non-experts.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of practical implications and real-world application scenarios.  
- Minor: clarify that eq.(3) ↔ eq.(1) means semi-supervised reduces to supervised.  
- Minor: equation (5) notation is confusing—why an equals sign before ω_C?  
- Minor: state how the embedding U is recovered from K and discuss practical factorization choices.  
- Minor: Algorithm 1’s PSD projection (eigendecomposition) may be expensive—ask whether optimizing over a factorization (e.g., Cholesky/LL^T) is feasible as an alternative.
