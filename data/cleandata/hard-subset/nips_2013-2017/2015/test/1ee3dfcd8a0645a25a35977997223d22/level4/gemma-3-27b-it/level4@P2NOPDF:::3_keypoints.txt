- Addresses optimizing orthonormal embeddings for graphs to improve function learning and graph transduction.
- Grounds the contribution in Laplacian-based methods and existing generalization bounds (Eq. 2).
- Proposes a novel generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Eq. 4).
- Derives a new optimization criterion from that eigenvalue-based bound (Eq. 5).
- Shows the criterion is convex despite the maximum eigenvalue being non-differentiable, enabling tractable optimization.
- Formulates the SEER (Structured Embedding via Eigenvalue Regularization) problem.
- Solves SEER using inexact proximal methods, a practical choice for the optimization complexity.
- Extends the approach to an MKL-SEER (multiple kernel learning) variant.
- Provides comprehensive experiments demonstrating consistent, significant performance gains over existing methods.
- Overall strong paper with solid theory, clever technical ideas, and clear, accessible writing.
