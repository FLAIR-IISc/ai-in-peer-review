- Main contribution: PAC-style bound linking generalization error to spectral norm and Lovász θ motivates spectral-norm regularization for orthonormal unit-sphere embeddings (SPORE).  
- Algorithmic contribution: SPORE optimized with an Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) method using approximate proximal steps (FISTA) instead of SDPs, with O(1/√T) convergence.  
- Scalability: IIP provides a first-order approach over the elliptope that scales to thousands of nodes, avoiding expensive SDPs.  
- Multi-graph extension: Method and analysis extended to multiple graphs in an MKL-style framework.  
- Empirical results: Competitive to superior performance on UCI, MNIST, and synthetic multi-graph experiments versus Laplacian baselines and k-scaling.  
- Originality: Novel combination of PAC analysis tied to spectral norm/Lovász θ and the IIP proximal optimization approach.  
- Technical quality: Nontrivial, sound theoretical arguments (including connection max_K λ1(K)=θ(Ḡ)) and proven convergence for IIP.  
- Practical significance: Offers a theoretically grounded, scalable alternative for learning over the elliptope with potential broad impact.  
- Motivation/framing weakness: Paper should better justify SPORE beyond the bound and include ablations isolating the effect of spectral-norm regularization.  
- IIP fidelity weakness: Lacks empirical assessment of approximation error versus SDPs or high-accuracy solvers and the effect of FISTA projection accuracy on performance.  
- Multiple-graph clarity weakness: Presentation is high-level; needs details on joint optimization of η-weights and K(k), spectral penalty handling, overhead, and explanation of unintuitive noisy-case results.  
- Clarity and organization: Paper is dense and relies heavily on the supplement; core theorems, intuition, and pseudocode (IIP/FISTA) should be moved into the main text.  
- Experimental recommendations: Add convergence plots and runtime vs accuracy trade-offs and small-scale validation of IIP solution quality.  
- Constants/parameters clarity: Need clearer narrative on the role and interpretation of θ(G), θ(Ḡ), and parameters C and β.  
- Pros for acceptance: Elegant, novel optimization framework with convergence proof; principled PAC-to-regularizer motivation; promising empirical performance.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited empirical analysis of IIP fidelity, unclear/misaligned multiple-graph findings, and dense presentation harming accessibility.  
- Overall assessment: Principled, original, and potentially impactful work; recommend clarifying multi-graph method/results, validating IIP approximation, and improving readability.
