- Proposes a new spectral-regularized embedding framework (SEER) for graph transduction; orthonormal embedding benefit needs clearer elaboration.
- Presents an original, elegant algorithm (IIP) to approximately solve the SEER problem; algorithmic development is a major strength.
- Experimental results generally support the method, but more experiments are needed to showcase SEER’s advantages and the orthonormal representation in Eq.(3).
- Should evaluate and report the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution relative to the true SEER solution.
- Multi-graph extension is described too high-level, lacking sufficient details, motivation, and intuition.
- Counterintuitive result in Table 4 (noisy case) suggests one graph outperforms multiple graphs, which contradicts the multi-graph motivation and needs explanation.
- Readability is hurt by paper density and heavy reliance on supplementary material, reducing accessibility and clarity.
- Overall: novel SEER framework and an interesting IIP-based algorithm with relatively convincing empirical evidence.
