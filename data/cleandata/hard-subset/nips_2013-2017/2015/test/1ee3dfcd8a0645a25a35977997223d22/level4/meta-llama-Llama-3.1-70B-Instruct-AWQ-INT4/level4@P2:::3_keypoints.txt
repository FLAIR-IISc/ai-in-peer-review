- Proposes SPORE (Spectral regularized ORthonormal Embedding) for finding optimal embeddings in graph transduction.
- Derives a new generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix leading to an alternate convex optimization criterion (eqn. 5) over the elliptope.
- Notes the spectral norm is nondifferentiable but the overall optimization criterion is convex as a sum of convex functions.
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal), an inexact proximal method approximating the proximal operator for the feasible set.
- Proves IIP converges to the optimal minimum for non-smooth convex functions with rate O(1/√T).
- Extends SPORE to multiple graph transduction via MKL-SPORE, using convex combinations of graph kernels.
- Shows MKL-SPORE improves classification accuracy and robustness to noise across views/graphs.
- Empirical results on real and synthetic graphs show SPORE outperforms unnormalized/normalized Laplacian and K-Scaling.
- Demonstrates scalability of the algorithm to large-scale graph transduction problems.
- Strengths: novel, theoretically justified method with provable optimization and practical effectiveness; clear explanations and intuitive insights.
- Weaknesses: assumes background in graph transduction/semi-supervised learning; some theoretical results (e.g., IIP rate) may be hard to follow without convex optimization expertise.
- Acceptance arguments: novel, well-justified, effective method with meaningful multi-graph extension.
- Rejection arguments: accessibility and clarity for non-experts and complexity of theoretical exposition could hinder broader impact.
