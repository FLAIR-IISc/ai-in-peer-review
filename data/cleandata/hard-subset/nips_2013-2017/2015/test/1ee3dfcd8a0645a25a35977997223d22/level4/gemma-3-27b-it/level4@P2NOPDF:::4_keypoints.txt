- Introduces a novel spectral-regularized embedding framework for graph transduction; motivation for orthonormal embedding could be stronger.  
- Main contribution is an elegant, original algorithm to approximately solve the SEER problem, offering a compelling alternative to existing methods.  
- Experimental results generally support the proposed solution’s effectiveness.  
- Evaluation should first demonstrate benefits of the SEER framework or the orthonormal representation (Eq. 3) before assessing the IIP solver.  
- Needs quantitative analysis of how well the IIP solution approximates the original SEER problem.  
- Extension to multiple-graph transduction is presented too abstractly; requires clearer motivation, implementation details, and algorithmic changes.  
- Multiple-graph results are hard to interpret; counterintuitive findings (e.g., Table 4 noisy case where single graph outperforms multiple) need explanation.  
- Paper is dense and sometimes unclear, with excessive reliance on supplementary material that harms readability and visibility of core contributions.  
- Overall: promising framework and algorithm with encouraging results, but needs deeper analysis and a more accessible presentation.
