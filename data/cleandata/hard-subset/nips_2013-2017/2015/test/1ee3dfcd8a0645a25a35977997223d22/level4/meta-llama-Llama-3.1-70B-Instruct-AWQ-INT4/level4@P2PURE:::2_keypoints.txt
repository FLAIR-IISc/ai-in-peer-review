- Paper addresses graph vertex label learning and builds on Ando and Zhang’s embedding-on-sphere idea but provides a concrete method to choose embeddings.
- Main contribution: a joint optimization formulation that simultaneously learns an orthonormal embedding and vertex labels using a regularizer.
- Regularizer: the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix, motivated by Theorem 1 which gives an upper bound on generalization error.
- The paper derives asymptotic upper bounds on generalization error that scale favorably relative to prior results and translate to labeled sample complexity bounds.
- The joint optimization can be formulated as an SDP, but SDPs are computationally expensive for large graphs.
- Practical solver: an inexact proximal method is proposed to scale the approach to graphs with thousands of vertices.
- Experiments show the proposed method outperforms previous methods on some tasks.
- Reviewer cannot understand the main result (Theorem 4) at a high level and therefore cannot assess its validity.
- The paper references an assumption that edges link “similar” instances but never defines “similar” or includes it in Theorem 4’s assumptions or proof.
- The reviewer finds no use of a similarity assumption in the theorem’s proof, creating a gap between stated intuition and formal results.
- Reviewer’s rough interpretation of Theorem 4 for random graphs: error scales as O(1/m * (n^{3/4} + log(1/δ))^{1/2}), implying recovery with high probability when an ω(n^{3/4}) fraction of labels are observed, but the reviewer is unsure of this interpretation.
