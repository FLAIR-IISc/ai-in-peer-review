The authors study the optimization of an orthonormal embedding of a graph and its impact on learning functions defined on the graph. They begin by reviewing prior work using a Laplacian and the corresponding generalization bound (Eq. 2). They then introduce their bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Eq. 4). This leads to an alternative optimization criterion (Eq. 5). Although lambda_1(K) is not differentiable, the objective is convex because it is a sum of convex functions. The authors solve SEER using inexact proximal methods. In the experiments, SEER and MKL-SEER are applied to multiple problems and show significant performance improvements.

Overall, this paper is very solid. The theoretical justification is strong. The paper also makes several insightful moves as it progresses—the new optimization criterion, the willingness to tackle a difficult optimization problem, and a convincing application to real problems. This work represents a meaningful advancement over prior approaches. The authors address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction, derive a novel method, and demonstrate that it works well in practice; the paper is complete and clearly explained.
