This paper studies the optimization of an orthonormal embedding of a graph and the resulting impact on learning functions defined on the graph, with a focus on graph transduction.

The authors begin by reviewing prior work that relies on a Laplacian and its associated generalization bound (eqn. 2). They then introduce their own bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4). This bound motivates an alternative optimization criterion (eqn. 5). Although λ1(K) is not differentiable, the overall objective is convex because it is a sum of convex functions. The authors solve the resulting problem for SEER using inexact proximal methods.

In the experimental section, SEER and MKL-SEER are applied to multiple problems and exhibit significant performance improvements.

Overall, this paper is very solid. It provides strong theoretical justification and features multiple intuitive leaps that demonstrate cleverness in the approach: the new optimization criterion, the willingness to tackle a challenging optimization problem, and a satisfying application to real problems. This represents a meaningful advancement over prior approaches. The authors address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction, derive a novel method, and demonstrate that it works well in practice. The paper is complete and well-explained.
