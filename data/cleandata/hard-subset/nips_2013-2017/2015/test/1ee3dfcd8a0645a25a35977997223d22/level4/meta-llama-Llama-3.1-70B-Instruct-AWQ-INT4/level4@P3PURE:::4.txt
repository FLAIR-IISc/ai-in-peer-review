The paper proposes a novel framework for spectral regularized embedding, applied to graph transduction. While the framework itself is intriguing, the benefits of having an orthonormal embedding could be more convincingly described, which might strengthen the authors' argument.

The authors then propose an interesting and elegant algorithm to solve the SEER problem approximately. This is an original contribution and appears to provide a good alternative for solving the complex learning problem.

Experimental results tend to demonstrate the benefits of the proposed solution. However, it would be beneficial to show results that highlight the advantages of the SEER framework or the orthonormal representation defined by (3) initially. Additionally, demonstrating the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution to the SEER problem would provide valuable insights. Currently, these aspects are not clearly visible in the results, and including them would help position the actual contribution in a broader context.

The extension to multiple graph transduction is unfortunately described at a very high level. Although this extension is interesting, more details, motivation, and intuition are necessary to clearly understand how and why the proposed method is also suitable for multiple graphs. Specifically, it would be helpful to explain how the algorithm is implemented in such settings. In its current form, it is challenging to grasp the main ideas in the multiple graph case, making it difficult to fully appreciate the experimental results. For instance, the last column of Table 4 (noisy case) is not very intuitive, as it suggests that one graph is better than multiple ones, which contradicts the motivation for dealing with multiple graphs.

Finally, the paper is not always easy to read and is dense in some areas. It also frequently refers to the supplementary material, which may indicate that the authors are trying to convey too much information in a single paper. This approach may compromise the accessibility and visibility of the work. Overall, the paper proposes a new framework, SEER, for orthonormal embeddings applied to graph transduction, along with an interesting algorithm based on IIP to solve the learning problem in SEER, and presents relatively convincing results.
