- Builds on Zhang & Ando to reduce semi-supervised learning to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem.  
- Sections 2 and appendix derive an error bound showing that minimising the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalisation.  
- Section 3 introduces a spectral-norm regulariser and connects error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Section 4 proposes a proximal solver with an approximate projection to handle a cone–polytope intersection constraint.  
- Section 5 presents a multiple-kernel-learning (MKL) extension; section 6 reports experiments across datasets.  
- Notable insight: links large-scale topological graph properties (Lovász number) to embedding estimation convergence.  
- Suggestion: explicitly state after eq. (3) that the correspondence with eq. (1) transforms SSL into supervised learning on labelled samples.  
- Issue: notation in eq. (5) is ambiguous—specifically the equal sign before ω_C.  
- Request: clarify how the embedding U is recovered from K in section 3 (multiple factorisation choices exist).  
- Practical concern: Algorithm 1 (steps 8–9) enforces PSD via eigendecomposition each iteration, which is computationally expensive.  
- Suggestion: optimise over a factorisation (e.g., K = LL^T / Cholesky) so projections guarantee PSD and reduce cost.  
- Minor correction: cite Zhang and Ando as NIPS 2005 (not 2006).
