- Studies label learning on graph vertices, building on Ando & Zhang’s idea of embedding graphs on the unit sphere via orthonormal representations to improve generalization, addressing their lack of a concrete embedding selection method.

- Proposes a joint optimization of the embedding and labels with a new regularizer: the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix, motivated by Theorem 1 which gives an upper bound on generalization error.

- Derives asymptotic upper bounds on generalization error with favorable scaling versus prior work, and translates these into labeled sample complexity bounds.

- Notes the optimization can be formulated as an SDP (computationally expensive), and proposes an inexact proximal method claimed to scale to graphs with thousands of vertices.

- Presents experiments suggesting the proposed method outperforms previous approaches.

- Reviewer is unable to understand the main result (Theorem 4) at a high level; the paper invokes an undefined “similar” edge assumption and does not state or use this assumption in the theorem or its proof.

- Reviewer summarizes Theorem 4 (for random graphs) roughly as er^{0-1}[\hat{y}] = O((n^{3/4} + log(1/δ))^{1/2} / m), implying that m = ω(n^{3/4}) labeled samples suffice for small error, but is unsure of this interpretation.

- Because of the above confusion about Theorem 4, the reviewer cannot judge the paper’s primary theoretical contribution.
