The authors study the optimization of an orthonormal embedding of a graph and its effect on learning functions defined over the graph. They begin by reviewing prior work based on the graph Laplacian and its associated generalization bound (Eq. 2). They then introduce a new bound that depends on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Eq. 4), which leads to an alternative optimization objective (Eq. 5). Although λ1(K) is not differentiable, the overall objective remains convex as a sum of convex functions.

To solve the resulting problem (SEER), the authors employ inexact proximal methods. In the experimental section, they evaluate SEER and its multi-kernel variant, MKL-SEER, across several tasks and report substantial performance improvements.

Overall, the paper is strong. It provides solid theoretical justification and demonstrates practical impact. The progression of ideas is coherent and contains several insightful elements: the new optimization criterion, the willingness to address a challenging nonsmooth optimization problem, and the effective application to real-world datasets. The work represents a meaningful advance over prior approaches to graph transduction. The authors tackle the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction with a novel method and show it performs well in practice. The paper is thorough, well-presented, and complete.
