This paper begins by presenting the background and showcasing the contributions. Subsequently, the authors, using the results of Zhang and Andou, demonstrate that SSL (supervised learning) is reduced to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem. In Section 2 and the appendix, they provide an error bound that shows that reducing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix is a good way to improve generalization. In Section 3, they add a spectral norm regularization term to Zhang and Andou's formula and provide a link between the error convergence of the data graph and the Lovelace number. Section 4 proposes a proximal solver, where the specialty is that it often works with projection steps to handle the restriction of intersecting a cone-polyhedron. Section 5 proposes an MKL extension and concludes Section 6 with experimental results on several datasets.

Overall, the paper is interesting and contains a lot of material (perhaps a bit too dense for a single paper), with several important contributions. The large-scale topological features of the data graph (Lovelace number), which reflects the organization of a space or polygraph, and the link between the convergence of the embedding estimate (Equation 5) are particularly instructive and connect several areas.
