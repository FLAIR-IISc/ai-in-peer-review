- Leverages Zhang and Ando to show semi-supervised learning reduces to an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem.  
- Derives an error bound proving that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix improves generalization.  
- Introduces a spectral-norm regularizer and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Proposes a proximal solver addressing the approximate projection for the cone–polytope intersection constraint.  
- Extends the method to multiple kernel learning (MKL) and provides experimental validation on multiple datasets.  
- Notable insight: connects large-scale graph topology (Lovász number) with embedding estimation convergence.
