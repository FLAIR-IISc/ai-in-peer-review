The paper introduces the background and contributions, then builds on Zhang and Ando to recast semi-supervised learning as an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem. In Section 2 and the appendix, the authors derive a generalization error bound showing that minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix can improve generalization. Section 3 augments the Zhang–Ando formulation with a spectral-norm regularizer and links error convergence to the Lovasz number of the data graph. Section 4 proposes a proximal solver that handles the cone–polytope intersection via an approximate projection step. Section 5 presents an MKL extension, and Section 6 reports experimental results on several datasets.

Overall, the paper is interesting and quite dense, with several substantial contributions. The connection between large-scale topological properties of the data graph (Lovasz number), which reflect its organization in some latent space or manifold, and the convergence of the embedding estimation is particularly insightful and bridges multiple areas.

Comments and suggestions:
- Section 1, after Eq. (3): Please state explicitly that the correspondence between Eqs. (3) and (1) reduces the semi-supervised problem to a supervised one on the labeled set S. Otherwise, seeing sums over S only in Eq. (3) can be surprising given that in Eq. (1) the regularizer involves unlabeled samples while the loss is on labeled data.
- Section 2: The notation in Eq. (5) is confusing—why is there an equals sign immediately before omega_C? If this is definitional, consider using “:=” or clarifying in text.
- Section 3 (around line 162): How is the embedding U recovered from K in practice? There are multiple factorization choices with different computational and statistical properties (eigendecomposition, Cholesky, low-rank factorization, Nyström). A brief discussion of the chosen approach and its implications would help.
- Section 4, Algorithm 1 (steps 8–9): Ensuring K_r is PSD via an eigendecomposition at each iteration can be expensive for dense matrices (as noted in Supplement D.3.2). Is this the main computational bottleneck? Would it be feasible to optimize directly over a factorization K = L L^T (e.g., a Cholesky-like factor), which enforces PSD by construction, with projection to the feasible set implemented by zeroing the strict upper triangle of L? How would this interact with the cone–polytope constraints and the approximate projection?
- Citation correction: Zhang and Ando is NIPS 2005, not 2006.

This is an engaging paper that clarifies how spectral regularization influences error bounds for graph transduction with orthonormal embeddings, offering new theoretical insights and convincing empirical results. Addressing the clarity issues (especially around Eqs. (3) and (5)) and discussing the computational trade-offs of the PSD projection versus factorized optimization would further strengthen the work.
