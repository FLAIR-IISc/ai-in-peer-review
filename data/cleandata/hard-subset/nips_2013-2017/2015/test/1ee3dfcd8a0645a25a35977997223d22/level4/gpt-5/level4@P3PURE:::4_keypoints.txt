- Introduces SEER framework for spectral-regularized embedding in graph transduction with emphasis on orthonormal embeddings, but motivation/benefits for orthonormality are not convincingly articulated.
- Proposes an elegant, original IIP-based algorithm that approximately solves the SEER problem and is a solid alternative for a complex learning problem.
- Experimental results generally support the approach, but missing experiments that explicitly demonstrate the benefits of SEER and the orthonormal representation (eq. 3).
- Lacks quantitative evaluation of how well the IIP-based solution approximates the SEER objective.
- Multiple-graph transduction extension is potentially valuable but presented too high-level; needs more detail, motivation, intuition, and implementation specifics.
- Noisy-case results (Table 4) are counterintuitive: a single graph outperforming multiple graphs contradicts the motivation for multi-graph modeling and needs explanation.
- Paper is occasionally dense and hard to follow, with excessive reliance on supplementary material reducing accessibility and clarity.
- Overall contribution is promising but would be strengthened by clearer justification for orthonormal embeddings, ablation analyses, approximation-accuracy evaluation, and a more interpretable multi-graph treatment.
