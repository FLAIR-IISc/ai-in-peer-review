The paper studies the problem of predicting labels on the vertices of a graph. It builds on Ando and Zhang, who argued that embedding the graph on the unit sphere via an orthonormal representation improves generalization (test) error. However, Ando and Zhang did not provide a concrete procedure for choosing an optimal embedding. This paper’s contribution is a new optimization formulation that jointly optimizes the embedding and the labels by adding a regularization term. The regularizer—the spectral norm of the corresponding graph kernel matrix—is motivated by Theorem 1, which gives an upper bound on the generalization error. The paper further derives asymptotic upper bounds on the generalization error for the proposed method with more favorable scaling than previous results; these bounds also translate into upper bounds on the labeled sample complexity. Although the optimization can be written as an SDP, solving SDPs is computationally expensive; therefore, the paper proposes using an inexact proximal method that scales to graphs with thousands of vertices. Some experiments indicate that the proposed method outperforms prior approaches.

As far as I understand, this means that if the vertices of a large random graph are labeled in {0,1} at random and then a diminishing fraction, e.g., ω(n^{3/4}), of these labels is observed, the algorithm is guaranteed, with high probability, to recover the remaining labels with small error.
