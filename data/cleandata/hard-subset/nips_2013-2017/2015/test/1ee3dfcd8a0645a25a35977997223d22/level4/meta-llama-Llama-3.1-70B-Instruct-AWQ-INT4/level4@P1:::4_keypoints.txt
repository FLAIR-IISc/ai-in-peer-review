- Proposes a new framework for spectral regularized embedding applied to graph transduction.  
- Benefits of the orthonormal embedding are not explained convincingly.  
- Introduces an interesting, elegant, and original approximate algorithm (IIP) for solving the SPORE problem.  
- Experimental results show benefits of the proposed solution but lack targeted experiments demonstrating SPORE-specific and orthonormal-representation gains.  
- No quantitative evaluation of the IIP approximation accuracy to the SPORE problem is provided.  
- Multiple graph transduction extension is presented at a high level and lacks motivation, intuition, and implementation details.  
- Results in the noisy multiple-graph case (Table 4) are unintuitive and may contradict the stated motivation.  
- Paper is dense, often hard to read, and relies heavily on the supplementary material.  
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental support.  
- Originality: novel framework for spectral regularized embedding and an elegant approximate solver for SPORE.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem in graph transduction with a promising multiple-graph extension.  
- Reasons to accept: originality of framework and algorithm, demonstrated empirical benefits, and interesting multi-graph extension.  
- Reasons to reject/revise: poor readability, insufficient detail for multiple-graph method, and over-reliance on supplementary material.
