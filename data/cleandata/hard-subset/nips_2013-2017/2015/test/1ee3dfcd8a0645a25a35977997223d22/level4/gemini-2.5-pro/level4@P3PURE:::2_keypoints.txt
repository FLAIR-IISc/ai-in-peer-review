- Builds on Ando and Zhang: learns vertex labels by embedding graphs on the unit sphere via an orthonormal representation.  
- Prior work suggested embeddings improve generalization but did not provide a method to choose an optimal embedding.  
- Main contribution: novel optimization that jointly learns the embedding and vertex labels using a regularization term.  
- Regularizer choice: spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix, motivated by an upper bound on generalization error (Theorem 1).  
- Proves an asymptotic upper bound on generalization error that scales more favorably than previous results and yields a labeled sample complexity bound.  
- Optimization can be cast as an SDP, but SDPs are computationally heavy.  
- Practical algorithm: an inexact proximal method that scales to graphs with thousands of vertices.  
- Experiments indicate the proposed method outperforms previous approaches.  
- Reviewer finds the high-level justification for Theorem 4 unclear.  
- Key assumption ambiguity: “edge links similar instances” is not formally defined, nor included in Theorem 4’s statement or proof.  
- Theorem 4 claim (for random graphs): er^{0-1}[\hat{y}] = O( (1/m) (n^{3/4} + log(1/δ))^{1/2} ), implying m = ω(n^{3/4}) suffices for low error, but validity/significance is unclear due to missing assumptions.
