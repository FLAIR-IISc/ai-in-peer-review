

The paper introduces a novel framework for spectral regularized embedding (SEER) applied to graph transduction. While the framework's core concept and the advantages of orthonormal embeddings are not sufficiently elaborated, this represents a minor limitation rather than a critical flaw.  

The authors present an original and elegant algorithm leveraging IIP to approximate the SEER solution, offering a compelling alternative for this complex learning problem. Experimental results broadly support the proposed method's efficacy, though they would be significantly strengthened by explicitly demonstrating: (1) the benefits of the SEER framework itself or the orthonormal representation defined in Equation (3), and (2) the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution relative to the exact SEER problem. These aspects are currently not clearly illustrated, hindering a comprehensive assessment of the contribution's scope.  

The extension to multiple graph transduction is introduced at a superficial level. While the idea is promising, the section lacks necessary details, motivation, and intuitive explanations to clarify *how* and *why* the method applies to multiple graphs—particularly regarding implementation nuances and the rationale for this setting. Consequently, the experimental results in this context (e.g., the counterintuitive outcome in the last column of Table 4, where a single graph outperforms multiple graphs) remain difficult to interpret and fail to convincingly support the proposed extension.  

Additionally, the paper's density and excessive reliance on supplementary material references impair readability and accessibility. This suggests an overburdened manuscript that risks diminishing the work's visibility and impact.
