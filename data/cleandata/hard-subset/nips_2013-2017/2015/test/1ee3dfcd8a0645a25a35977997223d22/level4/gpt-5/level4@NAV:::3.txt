The authors investigate optimizing an orthonormal embedding of a graph and the ensuing effect on learning functions over the graph.
They begin by revisiting prior work using the Laplacian and the corresponding generalization bound (eqn. 2). They then present their bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4). This bound yields an alternative optimization objective (eqn. 5); while lambda_1(K) is not differentiable, the objective is convex (a sum of convex functions).
The authors employ inexact proximal methods to solve SEER.
In the experiments, the authors apply SEER and MKL-SEER to multiple tasks and show substantial performance gains.
Overall, this paper is very strong.
There is solid theoretical justification.
In addition, the paper makes several insightful leaps that showcase the approach’s ingenuity—the new optimization criterion, the willingness to tackle a difficult optimization problem, and the satisfying application to real problems.
This work is a clear advancement over prior approaches.
The authors address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction. They introduce a novel method and demonstrate that it performs well in practice—this paper is complete and well explained.
