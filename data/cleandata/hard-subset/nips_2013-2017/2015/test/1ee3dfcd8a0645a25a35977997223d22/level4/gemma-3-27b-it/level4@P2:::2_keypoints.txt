- Paper proposes SPORE: a novel optimization that jointly learns orthonormal graph embeddings and labels with spectral-norm regularization.
- Addresses an open problem from Ando & Zhang (2005) about choosing optimal orthonormal embeddings.
- Provides a generalization bound (Theorem 1) and PAC learnability analysis motivating the spectral-norm regularizer.
- Introduces IIP, an efficient algorithm to solve the non-smooth convex SPORE problem, scaling to graphs with thousands of vertices.
- Extends the approach to multiple-graph (multi-view) settings.
- Experiments on synthetic and real data show SPORE outperforms existing methods.
- Paper effectively summarizes related work and clearly states the problem and proposed solution.
- Major clarity issue: assumptions underpinning Theorem 4, particularly the undefined notion of “similarity,” are not specified or justified.
- The connection between the implicit similarity assumption and the proof of Theorem 4 is unclear, preventing assessment of the theorem’s validity.
- Some sections are densely technical and lack intuitive explanations, reducing accessibility for non-experts.
- Limited discussion of parameter tuning and sensitivity; cross-validation is mentioned but not deeply analyzed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach, scalable algorithm, and strong empirical results addressing a gap in the literature.
- Arguments for rejection: unclear/unsupported assumptions in Theorem 4 undermine theoretical credibility and technical density limits accessibility.
- Overall: promising and technically impressive work but major theoretical-clarity concerns; recommendation: Weak Reject, with openness to reconsideration after revision.
