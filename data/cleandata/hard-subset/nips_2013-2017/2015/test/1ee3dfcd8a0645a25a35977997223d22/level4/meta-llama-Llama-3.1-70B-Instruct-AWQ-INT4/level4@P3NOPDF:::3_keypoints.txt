- Paper studies optimization of orthonormal graph embeddings and their effect on learning functions on graphs.
- Reviews prior work using the graph Laplacian and its generalization bound (eqn. 2).
- Proposes a new generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4).
- Derives an alternative optimization criterion from that bound (eqn. 5).
- Notes λ1(K) is nondifferentiable but the overall criterion is convex as a sum of convex functions.
- Solves the optimization via inexact proximal methods, proposing the SEER algorithm.
- Introduces a multi-kernel variant, MKL-SEER.
- Experiments show significant performance improvements over existing methods across several tasks.
- Paper has strong theoretical justification and demonstrates several insightful derivations and methodological choices.
- Work is a clear, well-structured advancement addressing optimal embeddings for graph transduction.
