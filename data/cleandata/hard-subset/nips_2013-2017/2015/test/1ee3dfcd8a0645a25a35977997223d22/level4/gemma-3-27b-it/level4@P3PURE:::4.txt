The paper proposes a novel framework for spectral regularized embedding, applied to graph transduction. While the framework itself, and particularly the benefits of employing an orthonormal embedding, could be described with greater clarity and justification, this is not a critical weakness.

The authors then present an interesting and elegant algorithm to approximately solve the resulting Spectral Embedding with Orthonormal Regularization (SEER) problem. This represents an original contribution and appears to offer a viable alternative for tackling this complex learning problem.

Experimental results generally support the effectiveness of the proposed solution. However, it would significantly strengthen the paper to include results specifically demonstrating the benefits of the SEER framework, or of the orthonormal representation defined by equation (3), independent of the iterative inference procedure (IIP). Furthermore, demonstrating the approximation accuracy of the IIP solution relative to the SEER problem would be highly valuable, allowing for a more comprehensive understanding of the contribution within a broader context. Currently, these aspects are not clearly visible in the presented results.

The extension to multiple graph transduction is unfortunately described at a very high level. Although potentially interesting, more detail, motivation, and intuition are needed to fully understand how and why the proposed method is beneficial in this setting, and especially how the algorithm is implemented. In its current form, the main ideas behind the multiple graph case are difficult to grasp, hindering a proper appreciation of the corresponding experimental results. In this regard, the final column of Table 4 (noisy case) is not readily interpretable; the observation that performance on a single graph can exceed that on multiple graphs seems counterintuitive given the stated motivation for addressing multiple graph scenarios.

Finally, the paper’s readability is occasionally hampered by its density, and it relies too heavily on references to the supplementary material. This may indicate an attempt to present an excessive amount of information within a single paper, which ultimately diminishes accessibility and potentially limits the work’s impact. The paper introduces a new framework, SEER, for orthonormal embeddings applicable to graph transduction. An interesting algorithm based on iterative inference procedure (IIP) is proposed to solve the resulting learning problem, and reasonably convincing results are presented.
