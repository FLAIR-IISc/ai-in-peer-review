The authors investigate the optimization of orthonormal embeddings of a graph and their impact on learning functions defined on the graph. They begin by reviewing prior work that utilizes the Laplacian and its corresponding generalization bound (eqn. 2). Building upon this foundation, they introduce a novel bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4), which leads to an alternative optimization criterion (eqn. 5). Notably, although the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix is not differentiable, the resulting optimization criterion is convex, being the sum of convex functions.

The authors employ inexact proximal methods to solve the optimization problem, specifically the SEER algorithm. In the experiments section, they apply both SEER and its extension, MKL-SEER, to various problems, demonstrating significant performance improvements.

Overall, this paper is well-structured and theoretically sound. The authors exhibit cleverness in their approach through several intuitive leaps, including the derivation of the new optimization criterion, their willingness to tackle the challenging optimization problem, and the successful application of their method to real-world problems. This work represents a meaningful advancement over prior approaches.

The authors effectively address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction by deriving a novel method and demonstrating its efficacy in practice. The paper is comprehensive and well-explained, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
