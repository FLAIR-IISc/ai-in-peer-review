The paper commences by presenting the background and outlining its contributions. The authors then leverage the Zhang and Ando result, which recasts semi-supervised learning (SSL) as an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem, as the foundation for their work. Section 2, supported by the appendix, develops an error bound that demonstrates how minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix can improve generalization. In Section 3, a spectral norm regularization term is incorporated into the Zhang and Ando formulation, and a connection is established between error convergence and the Lovasz number of the data graph. Section 4 introduces a proximal solver, distinguished by its use of an approximate projection step to manage the cone-polytope intersection constraint. An MKL extension is proposed in Section 5, and Section 6 presents experimental results on several datasets to conclude the paper.

Overall, this is a compelling and substantial paper, albeit dense, that offers several significant contributions. The link established between the large-scale topological properties of the data graph (as captured by the Lovasz number) and the convergence of the embedding estimation (Equation 5) is particularly insightful, effectively bridging multiple research fields.

Specific comments:
*   In Section 1, after Equation (3), it would be helpful to explicitly state that the correspondence between Equations (3) and (1) transforms the semi-supervised problem into a fully supervised one. This would clarify why the summations in Equation (3) are only over the labeled set S, which is initially surprising given that the regularizer in Equation (1) also operates on unlabeled samples.
*   In Section 2, the notation in Equation (5) is confusing. Please clarify the purpose of the equals sign directly before the \omega_C term.
*   In Section 3, around line 162, consider mentioning how the embedding (U) is recovered from the kernel matrix K, as there are several practical factorization choices available.
*   In Section 4, regarding steps 8-9 of Algorithm 1: Ensuring K_r is positive semi-definite (PSD) via an eigendecomposition at each iteration (as detailed in supplementary D.3.2) is typically computationally expensive. Does this projection step represent the primary bottleneck of the algorithm? Would it be feasible to optimize over a factorization of K instead (e.g., Cholesky decomposition, K=LL^T)? Projection onto the feasible set would then involve enforcing a lower-triangular structure, which ensures the PSD property by construction and might be more efficient.
*   Minor correction: The Zhang and Ando reference is from NIPS 2005, not 2006.

In summary, this is a strong paper that demonstrates how spectral regularization impacts error bounds on graph transduction through orthonormal embedding, providing novel theoretical insights and persuasive experimental validation.
