- Paper reframes SSL as a supervised kernel problem on labeled samples and proposes spectral-norm regularization on the graph kernel, with a proximal solver and MKL extension; experiments are competitive.

Strengths
- Leverages the SSL→supervised reduction to introduce spectral-norm regularization meaningfully.
- Strong theoretical contributions: error bound and connection to the Lovász number give intuition on graph-scale effects.
- Proximal algorithm is carefully designed and MKL extension increases applicability.
- Empirical results support the theory and are convincing.

Major comments
1) Clarify that after Eq. (3) the SSL objective reduces to a supervised problem over labeled samples S, with unlabeled data influencing only the kernel/regularizer.  
2) Fix notation in Eq. (5): use “:=” for defining ω_C and add brief explanation for each symbol and dependencies.  
3) Make assumptions behind the generalization bound explicit (distributional, stability/Lipschitz, graph construction) and compare to trace/Frobenius-norm bounds.  
4) Explain how embedding U is recovered from K in practice (factorization choices, rank selection) and note if results depend on a particular factorization.  
5) Quantify the per-iteration cost and runtime breakdown for PSD projection, discuss factorized parameterizations or truncated eigensolvers as alternatives, and clarify convergence guarantees given approximate projections.  
6) Provide a brief, self-contained explanation of the Lovász-number connection: role of orthonormal representations, why Lovász is natural here, and tightness for practical graph classes.  
7) Expand MKL details: whether spectral regularization is per-base or on the combined kernel, how kernel weights are regularized (l1/l2), sparsity behavior, and extra computational/convergence costs.  
8) Strengthen experiments by reporting runtimes and iteration counts (especially PSD projection time), sensitivity analyses for regularization and projection accuracy, ablations (full vs truncated/factorized), and scalability vs n and graph sparsity.

Minor comments
- Add the supervised-reduction sentence after Eq. (3).  
- Fix Eq. (5) notation near ω_C.  
- Describe practical recovery of U from K around line 162.  
- Note computational cost of PSD enforcement in Algorithm 1 and Supplement D.3.2 and mention truncated eigendecompositions if used.  
- Correct Zhang and Ando citation to NIPS 2005.  
- Include a short proof sketch of the key bound in the main text for readability.

Overall assessment
- Interesting and ambitious work with valuable theory (Lovász connection), solid algorithm and experiments; recommend acceptance pending clarifications on reduction, notation, embedding recovery, and PSD-projection computational issues.
