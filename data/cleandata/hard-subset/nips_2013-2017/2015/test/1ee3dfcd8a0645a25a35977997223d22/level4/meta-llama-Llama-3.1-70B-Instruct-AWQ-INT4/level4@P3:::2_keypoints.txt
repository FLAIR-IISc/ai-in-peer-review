- Paper addresses learning vertex labels on graphs by jointly optimizing an orthonormal embedding and labels, building on Ando & Zhang.
- Main contribution: SPORE formulation that adds a spectral-norm regularizer on the graph kernel to improve generalization, motivated by Theorem 1.
- Presents comprehensive theoretical analysis: generalization bound for graph transduction with orthonormal embeddings.
- Provides PAC analysis of SPORE and a labelled sample complexity estimate.
- Proposes an efficient inexact proximal algorithm to solve SPORE, scalable to graphs with thousands of vertices.
- Introduces MKL-SPORE for multiple graphs with improved PAC bounds.
- Experiments show SPORE outperforms previous methods.
- Strength: joint optimization of embedding and labels via a novel regularized formulation.
- Strength: thorough theoretical treatment and scalability-focused algorithm design.
- Weakness: assumption that edges link similar instances is neither clearly defined nor integrated into the proof of Theorem 4.
- Weakness: Theorem 4 and its implications need clearer exposition.
- Weakness: computational complexity of the proposed algorithm requires more detailed analysis.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Theorem 4, formalize the edge-similarity assumption, and expand complexity analysis.
- Overall ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
