This paper addresses the problem of learning an optimal orthonormal embedding for a graph, with the goal of improving graph-based transduction tasks.

The authors begin by reviewing prior work based on the graph Laplacian and its associated generalization bound (Equation 2). They then introduce a novel bound derived from the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (Equation 4). This bound directly motivates an alternative optimization criterion, presented in Equation 5. While the maximum eigenvalue term, λ_1(K), is not differentiable, the authors correctly note that the overall objective function is convex, as it is a sum of convex functions. To solve this optimization problem, which they term SEER, they employ inexact proximal methods.

In the experimental evaluation, the authors apply their proposed method, SEER, and a multiple kernel learning variant (MKL-SEER), to a variety of problems. The results demonstrate significant performance improvements over competing methods.

Overall, this is a very solid paper. The work is founded on good theoretical justification and demonstrates considerable cleverness through several intuitive leaps. Key strengths include the formulation of the new optimization criterion, the willingness to tackle the resulting non-differentiable optimization problem, and the satisfying application to real-world tasks. This work represents a nice advancement over prior approaches. By deriving a novel method and demonstrating that it works well in practice, the authors have delivered a complete and well-explained contribution.
