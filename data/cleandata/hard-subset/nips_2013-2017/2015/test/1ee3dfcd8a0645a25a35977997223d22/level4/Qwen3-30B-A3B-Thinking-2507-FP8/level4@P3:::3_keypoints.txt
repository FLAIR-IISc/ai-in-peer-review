- Introduces SPORE: spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embedding for graph transduction leveraging orthonormal representations.  
- Shows orthonormal representations are PAC-learnable with a new generalization bound (Theorem 1) depending on the spectral norm λ₁(K) rather than VC dimension.  
- Formulates SPORE as a convex optimization over the elliptope (K(G)), avoiding poorly scalable SDP solvers.  
- Proposes the IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal) algorithm with O(1/√T) convergence and much better scalability than SDP-based methods.  
- Extends to multiple graphs via MKL-SPORE and demonstrates robustness to noisy graphs.  
- Theorem 4 links graph-dependent error convergence to structural properties (via Lovász θ), filling a gap in prior work.  
- Empirical results (Tables 1–4) show consistent improvements over Un-Lap, N-Lap, and KS on both small UCI datasets and large (2000-node) graphs.  
- Supplementary material supports reproducibility and the paper situates itself well within prior NIPS literature.  
- Strong originality: novel PAC-based bound, spectral-norm regularization formulation, and tailored IIP/FISTA approximate projection approach.  
- Significance: addresses scalability and theoretical understanding of graph transduction with practical performance gains.  
- Minor limitations: lacks explicit runtime/computational comparisons of IIP vs other first-order methods and more analysis of MKL-SPORE failure cases.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clear, original, significant contribution with a strong recommendation for acceptance.
