This paper studies how to optimize orthonormal embeddings for graph transduction and examines the impact of these embeddings on learning functions over graphs.

The authors begin by reviewing prior work based on Laplacian kernels and their associated generalization bound (equation 2). They then develop a new bound (equation 4) that depends on the maximum eigenvalue (spectral norm) of the kernel matrix. This motivates an alternative optimization criterion (equation 5): minimize a convex objective that combines the standard dual SVM objective with a spectral-norm penalty, βλ1(K). Although λ1(K) is non-differentiable, the overall objective is convex as a sum of convex functions.

To solve this problem efficiently, the authors use inexact proximal methods for the proposed SPORE formulation. They employ an inexact proximal approach (with approximate projections) tailored to the elliptope constraints, enabling scalable first-order optimization despite the nonsmooth term. In the experiments, the authors apply SPORE and its multi-graph extension, MKL-SPORE, to several datasets and demonstrate significant accuracy gains over baselines.

Overall assessment: this is a very solid paper. The theoretical development is well motivated and technically grounded. The proposed generalization bound based on the spectral norm provides a clear rationale for the new regularizer, and the resulting convex formulation is natural. The authors then go beyond the formulation to tackle the algorithmic challenges head-on, using inexact proximal techniques to handle approximate projections efficiently, which is key for scaling beyond a few hundred nodes. Finally, the empirical results are convincing and show clear performance improvements across multiple tasks, including the multi-graph setting.

Strengths:
- Clear theoretical progression from a new generalization bound (equation 4) to a principled convex optimization criterion (equation 5).
- Sensible use of the spectral norm penalty; the objective remains convex despite the non-differentiability of λ1(K).
- Algorithmic ingenuity: a practical inexact proximal method designed for the elliptope constraints, enabling scalability where SDP-based methods do not.
- Strong empirical performance of SPORE and MKL-SPORE with consistent gains over baselines.
- The paper reads as a complete contribution, from theory to algorithm to experiments, and is well explained.

Weaknesses:
- None substantive identified in my reading. The paper’s narrative is cohesive and the technical choices are justified. Any remaining concerns would be minor and editorial.

Overall, this work represents a meaningful advancement over prior Laplacian-based approaches: it introduces a theoretically justified and computationally viable criterion for learning orthonormal graph embeddings and demonstrates practical benefits in graph transduction. The authors address the problem of finding an optimal embedding for graph transduction, derive a novel method, and show it works well in practice. The paper is complete and well articulated.
