- Paper addresses node label prediction on graphs and builds on Ando and Zhang’s unit-sphere orthonormal embedding idea but adds a principled embedding selection.  
- Introduces a new optimization formulation that jointly selects an embedding and learns labels via a regularizer.  
- The regularizer is the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix and is motivated by Theorem 1 giving a generalization error upper bound.  
- Derives asymptotic generalization error bounds and corresponding labeled sample complexity results that improve on prior scaling.  
- Formulation can be written as an SDP (computationally expensive), but the paper proposes an inexact proximal method that scales to graphs with thousands of nodes.  
- Empirical results indicate the proposed method outperforms previous techniques.  
- Reviewer could not understand the high-level meaning of Theorem 4 and finds its exposition unclear.  
- The paper claims learning is impossible with unlabeled nodes without assumptions, cites “edge links similar instances,” but never defines “similar” nor includes it in Theorem 4’s hypotheses.  
- Reviewer could not find where (or whether) the “similarity” assumption is used in Theorem 4’s proof.  
- Reviewer’s rough interpretation of Theorem 4 for random graphs suggests surprisingly strong recovery from a vanishing fraction of labels, but due to confusion about the result the reviewer cannot make a definitive judgment.
