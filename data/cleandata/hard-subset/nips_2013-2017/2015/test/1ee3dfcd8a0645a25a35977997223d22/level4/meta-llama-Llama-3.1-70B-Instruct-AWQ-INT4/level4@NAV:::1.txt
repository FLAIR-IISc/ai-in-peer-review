The paper commences by providing a comprehensive background and highlighting its key contributions. The authors build upon the Zhang and Ando result, demonstrating that semi-supervised learning (SSL) can be reduced to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem, which serves as the foundation for the remainder of the paper. In sections 2 and the appendix, the authors derive an error bound, illustrating that minimising the spectral norm of the graph kernel matrix is an effective approach to enhance generalisation. Section 3 introduces a spectral norm regularisation term to the Zhang and Ando formulation, establishing connections between error convergence and the Lovasz number of the data graph. A proximal solver is proposed in section 4, featuring an approximate projection step to accommodate the constraint of a cone-polytope intersection. Section 5 presents a multiple kernel learning (MKL) extension, while section 6 concludes with experimental results on various datasets.

Overall, the paper is engaging and encompasses a substantial amount of content, with multiple significant contributions. The link between large-scale topological properties of the data graph (Lovasz number), reflecting its organisation in a particular space or manifold, and the convergence of embedding estimation (eq. 5) is particularly insightful, bridging multiple fields.

In section 1, following equation 3, it would be beneficial to explicitly state that the correspondence between equations (3) and (1) implies that a semi-supervised learning problem is transformed into a supervised learning problem, thereby focusing learning on labelled samples. This clarification would address the potential confusion arising from the indices over S only (without \bar{S}) in the sums of equation (3), whereas equation (1) includes a regulariser on non-labelled samples and a loss function on labelled data.

In section 2, the notation in equation 5 is ambiguous, specifically the equal sign preceding \omega_C. A clearer notation would enhance the readability of this section.

Around line 162 in section 3, it would be helpful to mention how the embedding (U) is recovered from K, as there are multiple practical choices for factorisation.

In section 4, Algorithm 1, steps 8-9, ensuring that K_r is positive semi-definite (PSD) can be computationally expensive for general dense square symmetric matrices. The eigendecomposition performed at every iteration (supplementary D.3.2) may be the primary contributor to the algorithm's computational time. A potential optimisation could involve optimising over a factorisation of K (e.g., Cholesky with K=LL^T), where projection to the feasible set would result in a matrix with all-zeros in the upper triangular, thereby ensuring K is PSD by construction.

Additionally, the Zhang and Ando reference should be corrected to NIPS 2005, rather than 2006.

This paper provides an interesting exploration of how spectral regularisation impacts error bounds on graph transduction using orthonormal embedding, offering novel theoretical insights and compelling experimental results.
