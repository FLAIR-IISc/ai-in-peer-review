- Paper proposes jointly optimizing orthonormal graph embeddings and labels using a novel spectral-norm regularized formulation (SPORE) motivated by a generalization bound (Theorem 1).  
- Provides asymptotic generalization and labeled sample complexity bounds with favorable scaling, and extends analysis to multiple graphs.  
- SDP formulation is possible but computationally expensive; authors introduce an inexact proximal (IIP) method that scales to graphs with thousands of nodes.  
- Experiments on synthetic and real datasets show the proposed method outperforms existing approaches.  
- Reviewer found Theorem 4 hard to interpret at a high level; the notion of “similarity” between connected nodes is not clearly defined or stated in the theorem.  
- Reviewer could not find explicit use of the node-similarity assumption in Theorem 4’s proof, undermining clarity of the main result.  
- Rough informal implication of Theorem 4 for random graphs: 0-1 error scales as O((1/m (√n + log(1/δ)))1/2), suggesting good recovery with few labels in large random graphs.  
- Strength: technically rigorous derivations and well-motivated generalization bound.  
- Strength: novel combination of spectral-norm regularization with orthonormal embeddings and scalable optimization via IIP.  
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation and useful extensions (e.g., multiple graphs).  
- Strength: empirical validation supports effectiveness.  
- Weakness: unclear and vaguely stated assumptions about node similarity, especially for Theorem 4.  
- Weakness: paper is dense and may be inaccessible to readers unfamiliar with PAC learning or spectral graph theory.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on IIP approximation criteria and its impact on convergence.  
- Weakness: experiments could include broader comparisons to more recent methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach with strong theory and scalable optimization that shows empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear assumptions in key theorem and high technical density limit accessibility.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution that outweighs the main clarity concern.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Theorem 4’s assumptions and expand explanations of IIP and related theory.
