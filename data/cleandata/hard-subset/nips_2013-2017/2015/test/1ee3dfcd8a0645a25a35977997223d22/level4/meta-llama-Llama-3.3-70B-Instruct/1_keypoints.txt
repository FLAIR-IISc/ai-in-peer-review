- Paper builds on Zhang and Ando to reduce SSL to an equivalent kernel-based supervised learning problem.  
- Section 2 and appendix derive an error bound showing minimizing the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalization.  
- Section 3 adds spectral-norm regularization and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.  
- Section 4 proposes a proximal solver with an approximate projection to handle a cone–polytope intersection constraint.  
- Section 5 extends the approach to Multiple Kernel Learning (MKL).  
- Section 6 presents experimental validation across several datasets.  
- Paper is content-rich and makes multiple significant contributions, though may be dense for a single paper.  
- Insightful connection between large-scale graph topology (Lovász number) and embedding estimation convergence.  
- Suggest clarifying in Section 1 (after eq.3) that the correspondence to eq.(1) transforms SSL into supervised learning, explaining sums over S only.  
- Clarify notation in Section 2, eq.5, specifically the equal sign before ω_C.  
- Discuss in Section 3 (around line 162) how embedding U is recovered from K and practical factorization choices.  
- Section 4: ensuring K_r is PSD via repeated eigendecompositions is expensive and likely runtime-dominant; suggest optimizing over a factorization (e.g., K=LL^T) to enforce PSD more cheaply.  
- Correct Zhang and Ando citation year to NIPS 2005 (not 2006).  
- Overall assessment: compelling theoretical and experimental exploration of spectral regularization in graph transduction with orthonormal embedding.
