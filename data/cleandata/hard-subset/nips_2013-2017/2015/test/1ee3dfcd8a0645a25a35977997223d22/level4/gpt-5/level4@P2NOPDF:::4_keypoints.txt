- SEER proposes orthonormal spectral embeddings for graph transduction, but the motivation and benefits of orthonormality are not strongly articulated.
- The paper needs more intuition, theoretical insight, or ablations contrasting orthonormal vs non-orthonormal embeddings to justify the design choice.
- The IIP-based approximate solver is elegant and original and a notable contribution.
- The paper lacks direct assessment of how well IIP approximates the true SEER objective—report optimality gaps, constraint satisfaction, convergence behavior, and comparisons to exact/strong baselines (on small instances).
- Experiments show effectiveness but do not isolate the contributions of SEER versus the orthonormal constraint; ablation studies and controlled comparisons are needed.
- The multi-graph transduction extension is interesting but described too briefly—requires more detail on motivation, graph combination/weighting, algorithmic implementation, complexity, and convergence.
- The noisy multi-graph result (Table 4 last column) is puzzling—clarification, diagnostic analyses, and sensitivity studies to noise and graph quality are needed.
- The paper is dense and hard to read with heavy reliance on supplementary material; move key definitions, algorithmic details, and essential proofs into the main text and streamline notation.
- Overall: meaningful framework and promising algorithm, but clarity, empirical isolation of components, approximation analysis, and fuller multi-graph treatment would significantly strengthen the contribution.
