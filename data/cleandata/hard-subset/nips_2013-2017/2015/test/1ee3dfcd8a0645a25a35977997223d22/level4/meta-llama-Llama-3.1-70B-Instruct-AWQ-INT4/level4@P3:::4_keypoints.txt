- Proposes a new spectral regularized embedding framework applied to graph transduction.  
- Benefits of the orthonormal embedding are not convincingly explained and need clearer motivation.  
- Introduces an original, elegant algorithm to approximately solve the SPORE problem.  
- Algorithm is a meaningful alternative for solving the complex learning problem.  
- Experimental results indicate benefits of the proposed solution.  
- Missing experiments showing (a) benefits of the SPORE framework or orthonormal representation and (b) approximation accuracy of the IIP solution.  
- Multiple-graph transduction is described at a high level and lacks details, motivation, intuitions, and implementation clarity.  
- Results for the noisy multiple-graph case (Table 4 last column) are unintuitive and potentially contradict the motivation.  
- Paper is dense, sometimes hard to read, and over-relies on supplementary material.  
- Strengths: original algorithm, supportive experimental results, novel spectral embedding framework.  
- Weaknesses: weak explanation of orthonormal benefits, insufficient multiple-graph details, poor readability.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address multiple-graph details and improve readability.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
