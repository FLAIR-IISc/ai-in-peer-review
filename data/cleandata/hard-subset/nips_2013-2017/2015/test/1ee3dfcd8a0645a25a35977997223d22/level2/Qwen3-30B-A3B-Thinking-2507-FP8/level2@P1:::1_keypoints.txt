- Proposes SPORE: a spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal-embedding formulation for graph transduction.  
- Proves orthonormal graph embeddings are PAC learnable and derives a generalization bound tied to the Lovász θ function.  
- Shows low spectral-norm embeddings yield better generalization, motivating the SPORE regularizer.  
- Introduces IIP (Infeasible Inexact Proximal), a projected subgradient method with inexact proximal projection.  
- IIP attains O(1/√T) convergence and is far more scalable than standard SDP solvers.  
- Demonstrates scalability to graphs with thousands of vertices (e.g., 2000 nodes) with few outer/inner iterations.  
- Extends method to multiple graphs via an MKL-style formulation, improving error rates and robustness to noise.  
- Experimental results consistently outperform Unnormalized Laplacian, Normalized Laplacian, and K-Scaling baselines across datasets.  
- Multiple-graph experiments validate theoretical claims and show robustness to noisy graph inputs.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and technically careful with correct references (Lovász θ, elliptopes).  
- Related work is well contextualized, clarifying improvements over prior work ([1, 16]).  
- Main strengths: theoretical novelty (PAC analysis), algorithmic contribution (IIP), practical scalability, and solid experiments.  
- Minor weaknesses: practical handling of Lovász θ (requires SDP) is not fully addressed in implementation details.  
- Minor weaknesses: experimental comparisons limited to three baselines; could include more recent graph transduction methods.  
- Minor weaknesses: O(1/√T) is standard for subgradient methods, so the paper should better emphasize what makes IIP novel beyond rate.  
- Overall significance is high; theoretical insights and scalable algorithm warrant acceptance.
