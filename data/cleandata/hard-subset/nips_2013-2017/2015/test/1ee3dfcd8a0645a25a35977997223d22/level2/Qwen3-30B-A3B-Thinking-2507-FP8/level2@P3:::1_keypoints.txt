- Proposes SPORE: orthonormal graph embeddings with spectral norm regularization for graph transduction.  
- Develops a PAC learning framework for orthonormal embeddings, deriving a generalization bound tied to Lovász θ instead of VC dimension.  
- Main theoretical result (Theorem 1): low spectral norm embeddings yield better generalization.  
- Provides a convex formulation over the elliptope and an efficient solver using an Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) method.  
- IIP approximates projections with FISTA, enabling scalability to graphs with thousands of vertices while retaining O(1/√T) convergence.  
- Extends approach to multiple graph transduction via an MKL-style formulation, showing robustness to noisy graphs.  
- Shows tighter sample complexity (Theorem 4), improving prior bounds by a factor ≈ ϑ/n^(1/2) vs ϑ/n^(1/3).  
- Theoretical analysis of IIP (Theorem 5) includes a novel approximation criterion that avoids Dykstra’s asymptotic issues.  
- Empirical results on UCI and synthetic datasets consistently outperform baselines (Un-Lap, N-Lap, KS), especially on larger graphs.  
- Original contributions: new PAC analysis for orthonormal embeddings, scalable SDP-alternative (IIP), and linking Lovász θ to spectral norm regularization.  
- Presentation is generally clear with good notation and supplementary reproducibility, but the Lovász θ–spectral norm link should appear earlier and IIP needs more intuitive explanation.  
- Minor weaknesses: limited comparison to recent GNN-based methods, scant hyperparameter/runtime details, and lack of explicit runtime advantage quantification over SDP solvers.
