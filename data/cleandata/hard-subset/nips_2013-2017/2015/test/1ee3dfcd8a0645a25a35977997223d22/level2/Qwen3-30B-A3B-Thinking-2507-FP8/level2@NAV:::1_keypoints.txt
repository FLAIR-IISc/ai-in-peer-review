- Proposes SPORE: a novel graph transduction method using orthonormal representations for graph embeddings.  
- Shows orthonormal representations are PAC learnable and derives a generalization bound tied to the Lovász θ function instead of VC dimension.  
- Formulates SPORE as a spectral-norm regularized optimization problem over the elliptope.  
- Introduces the Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) algorithm for efficient non-smooth convex optimization over elliptopes.  
- Theorem 1 provides a high-probability generalization bound supporting the approach.  
- Theorem 4 gives a sample complexity bound that improves on prior work for graphs with θ(G)=o(n).  
- Theorem 6 proves IIP converges at rate O(1/√T) with a detailed application-specific proof.  
- Experiments (Tables 1–4) show SPORE outperforms existing methods and IIP scales to 2000+ vertices where SDPs fail.  
- Novel contribution: bridges PAC learning theory and orthonormal embeddings, offering a principled way to choose embeddings beyond consistency results.  
- Extends to multiple graphs via MKL-SPORE, showing practical applicability.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, though supplement needs more implementation detail on IIP.  
- Limitations acknowledged: restricted to unit-sphere embeddings and MKL assumes convex kernel combinations.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical foundations, practical scalability, significant improvement over prior work; recommended for acceptance.
