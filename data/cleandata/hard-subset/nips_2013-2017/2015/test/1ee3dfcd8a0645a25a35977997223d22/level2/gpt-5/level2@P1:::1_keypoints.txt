- Paper links transductive graph learning with orthonormal embeddings to spectral-norm regularization and provides a PAC-style bound dependent on the kernel spectral norm.
- Main theoretical result: a high-probability Rademacher-style bound for transductive SVM under orthonormal embeddings avoiding VC-dimension and depending on λ1(K).
- The authors relate max_K∈K(G) λ1(K) to Lovász theta of the complement graph and derive a data-dependent error bound O((1/m (√(n θ(G)) + log(1/δ)))1/2).
- They propose SPORE: convex objective minimizing transductive SVM dual plus βλ1(K) over the elliptope K(G).
- Introduce IIP (infeasible inexact proximal) optimization using approximate projections via proximal operators of support functions and FISTA with O(1/√T) convergence.
- Extend to multiple graphs with MKL-style convex combination and max spectral penalty, yielding similar bounds and “one good view suffices” insight.
- Empirically, SPORE shows consistent gains over Laplacian-based baselines on UCI and MNIST and robust performance in synthetic multi-graph tests.
- Strength: clear conceptual connection between generalization and Lovász θ via spectral control of embeddings.
- Strength: SPORE is a convex, interpretable formulation tying empirical fit to a spectral capacity term.
- Strength: IIP is a generally applicable optimization approach when exact projections are hard but proxes of support functions are accessible.
- Strength: principled multi-graph extension and useful insight that the best graph dominates the bound.
- Strength: experiments indicate scalability to a few thousand nodes and improved accuracy over baselines.
- Weakness: theoretical parameter choices require θ(G) and θ(Ḡ), which are not scalable/available in practice; need discussion of data-driven tuning or upper bounds.
- Weakness: Lemma 2 and Lemma 3 are central but lack detailed proofs or precise references; clarity needed on novelty vs known θ formulations.
- Weakness: optimization uses infeasible iterates—clarify whether SVM subproblems see indefinite K, per-iteration complexity (top eigenvector, SVM, inner FISTA), memory footprint, and sensitivity to inner-outer schedule.
- Weakness: empirical baselines omit classic transductive methods (label propagation, harmonic functions) and an SDP/orthonormal baseline to isolate the spectral penalty effect.
- Weakness: graph construction choices (RBF thresholding, cosine threshold) need sensitivity analysis and comparisons to common kNN graphs.
- Weakness: scalability claims limited by experiments up to n=2000; request larger runs (10k–50k), wall-clock times, convergence plots, and comparisons to exact-projection methods.
- Weakness: ablation studies missing—effect of removing λ1 penalty (β=0) and replacing λ1 with other spectral surrogates (trace/nuclear norm) should be reported.
- Minor issues: notation/spacing ambiguities, incomplete references, and inconsistent symbols; proofreading and unification needed.
- Clarity: Section 4 mixes IIP generalities with SPORE specifics—add a roadmap and list of required prox operators (forms for σS+ and σP(G)) for reproducibility.
- Clarity: explicitly state proximal steps used in FISTA and the Lipschitz constant for the smooth term in Eq. (16); justify step-size and constants L, R, R̂.
- Originality: combination of θ-based bound, spectral-norm-regularized elliptope optimization, and inexact-projection method is novel and a meaningful NeurIPS contribution.
- Significance: with stronger experiments and clearer proofs/algorithms, SPORE could be a principled alternative to Laplacian regularization and a reusable IIP framework for SDP-like problems.
- Questions: can θ(G) or θ(Ḡ) be efficiently upper-bounded without SDP, or are there empirical proxies for setting C and β?
- Questions: how does SPORE perform with kNN graphs versus global thresholding and how sensitive is it to graph sparsity?
- Questions: what is the true computational bottleneck (top eigenvector vs SVM vs inner FISTA), and can warm-starts or adaptive schedules improve runtime?
- Questions: is Lemma 3 tight or are there graph families achieving ωC ≈ θ(G)/2?
- Pros for acceptance: solid theory linking transduction to Lovász θ, convex formulation, useful IIP optimization, principled multi-graph extension, promising empirical gains.
- Cons for acceptance: missing standard SSL baselines and scale/sensitivity analysis, limited practical guidance for θ-based parameters, and presentation/proof clarity issues.
