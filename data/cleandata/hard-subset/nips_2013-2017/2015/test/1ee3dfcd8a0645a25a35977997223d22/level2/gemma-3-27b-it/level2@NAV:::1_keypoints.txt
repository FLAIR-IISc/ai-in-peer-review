- Problem & gap: tackles graph transduction via unit-sphere orthonormal embeddings and identifies lack of guidance on choosing optimal embeddings.  
- Proposed methods: introduces SPORE (spectral-norm regularization of orthonormal representations) and IIP, an efficient algorithm to compute it.  
- Theoretical core: derives a PAC-based generalization bound for orthonormal representations that motivates the spectral regularizer.  
- Scalability: IIP uses an inexact proximal method to avoid SDP solvers, enabling application to graphs with thousands of vertices.  
- Extension: proposes an MKL-style extension for multiple-graph transduction.  
- Empirical findings: shows improvements over baseline methods on single- and multi-graph datasets and provides convergence analysis for IIP.  
- Strengths summary: strong theoretical rigor, novel formulation, scalable algorithm, and demonstrated empirical gains.  
- Weakness—theory: limited discussion of the practical impact and interpretation of the PAC bound.  
- Weakness—parameters: regularization parameters (β, C) chosen by cross-validation without clear theoretical justification.  
- Weakness—experiments: evaluation could be more comprehensive (missing recent baselines) and choice of RBF kernel on UCI datasets seems arbitrary.  
- Presentation issue: extensive supplementary material aids proofs but makes core ideas harder to quickly grasp.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—accept with minor revisions to clarify theory–practice links and to expand experimental comparisons.
