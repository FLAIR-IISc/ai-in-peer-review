- Proposes SPORE: spectral-norm regularized orthonormal embedding for graph transduction, with PAC learnability and a generalization bound (Theorem 1).  
- Formulates SPORE as a convex optimization over the elliptope and introduces an efficient Inexact Infeasible Proximal (IIP) solver.  
- Theoretical advance: improved sample complexity (Theorem 4) from Ω((ϑ/n)^{1/3}) to Ω((ϑ/n)^{1/2}) over prior work.  
- Convergence analysis for IIP is rigorous (Theorems 5–6) and connects to a novel proximal operator for the support function.  
- Empirically, SPORE outperforms existing methods across multiple datasets, especially at large scale (Tables 1–4, Table 2).  
- Extends to multi-graph transduction (MKL-SPORE), offering practical multi-view learning utility.  
- Weakness: Theorem 4 proof relies on ϑ(G)ϑ(Ḡ)=n (Lovász θ), which holds only for perfect graphs; applicability to studied graph families (e.g., power-law) is unclear.  
- Weakness: No guidance on choosing kernels within the normalized-kernel set or theoretical justification why spectral-norm regularization yields better embeddings.  
- Weakness: Experimental analysis lacks deeper diagnostics (embedding visualizations, ablation studies) to explain why SPORE outperforms baselines.  
- Minor issue: citation [1] is slightly mischaracterized regarding unit-sphere/kernel diagonal normalization and should be clarified.  
- Weakness: Scalability claim is supported by results but lacks precise runtime comparisons with SDP solvers on comparable graph sizes.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and supplementary material contains rigorous proofs; overall recommendation: accept with clarification on the Lovász θ assumption.
