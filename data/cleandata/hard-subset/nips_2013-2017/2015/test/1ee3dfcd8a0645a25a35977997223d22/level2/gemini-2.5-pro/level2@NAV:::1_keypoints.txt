- Addresses graph transduction by selecting optimal orthonormal representations (unit-sphere graph embeddings).  
- Contribution 1: New PAC-based generalization bound linking generalization to embedding spectral norm and Lovász θ, independent of VC dimension.  
- Contribution 2: Proposes SPORE, a spectral-norm-regularized convex optimization to find the optimal embedding.  
- Contribution 3: Introduces IIP, a scalable first-order solver for SPORE with proven O(1/√T) convergence.  
- Theoretical analysis cleanly motivates SPORE, tying learning theory to a practical objective.  
- IIP is a major practical contribution, overcoming SDP scalability limits and applicable to optimization over intersections of convex sets with expensive projections.  
- Empirical results: SPORE significantly outperforms established graph transduction methods across real-world datasets.  
- Large-scale experiments validate the practical advantages and scalability of the IIP solver.  
- Minor clarity issue: need more intuition for parameter choices (C and β) in Theorem 4.  
- Reproducibility concern: provide more details on cross-validation and derivation of algorithm constants (L, R, R̂).  
- Suggest adding discussion of limitations, e.g., sensitivity of IIP to constants and performance on extremely large graphs.  
- Overall assessment: excellent, novel, and important contribution advancing graph-based learning and optimization tools.
