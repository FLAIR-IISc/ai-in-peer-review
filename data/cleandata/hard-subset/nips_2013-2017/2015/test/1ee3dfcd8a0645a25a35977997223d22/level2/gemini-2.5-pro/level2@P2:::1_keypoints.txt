- Novel high-probability generalization bound for graph transduction using orthonormal representations that depends on the kernel spectral norm.  
- SPORE: a spectral-norm-regularized orthonormal embedding formulation balancing SVM dual loss and spectral-norm regularizer.  
- PAC analysis connecting SPORE generalization to the Lovász θ function (and its complement), yielding improved sample-complexity and relating learning rate to graph density.  
- Infeasible Inexact Proximal (IIP) method: a general first-order projected-subgradient framework using approximate (possibly infeasible) projections with proven O(1/√T) convergence.  
- Practical instantiation of IIP for SPORE using FISTA to compute the approximate projection onto the elliptope, producing a scalable solver for large graphs.  
- Extension to multi-graph transduction via an MKL-style formulation, with corresponding theoretical and algorithmic results.  
- Empirical results on UCI and MNIST showing SPORE outperforms standard graph transduction methods.  
- Large-scale experiments demonstrating IIP’s scalability to graphs with thousands of nodes.  
- Synthetic experiments showing the multi-graph extension is robust to noisy graph views.  
- Strength: strong theoretical motivation and novel bounds (Theorems 1 and 4) that improve prior work and yield insights via Lovász θ.  
- Strength: IIP is a novel, general, and technically sound algorithmic contribution applicable beyond this paper’s setting.  
- Strength: the approach bridges theory and practice, avoiding SDPs and scaling to realistic graph sizes.  
- Strength: the paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Weakness: theoretical hyperparameter choices (C, β) rely on Lovász θ, which is expensive to compute, while experiments use cross-validation — practical guidance or cheap approximations are needed.  
- Weakness: the projection/FISTA inner-loop is described at high level; including detailed pseudocode (e.g., Algorithm 1) in the main paper would improve clarity.  
- Weakness: lack of direct quantitative runtime comparison to standard SDP solvers on small-to-medium graphs where SDPs are feasible.  
- Weakness: minor clarifications requested — explicitly separate subgradients of ωC and βλ1 in Section 4.2 and sketch how IIP adapts to the MKL min-max structure.  
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper with minor issues; strong acceptance recommendation.
