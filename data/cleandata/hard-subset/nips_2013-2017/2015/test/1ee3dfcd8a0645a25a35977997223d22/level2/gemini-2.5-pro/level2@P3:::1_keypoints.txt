- Problem: selects optimal orthonormal graph embedding for transduction, building on spherical embeddings but addressing how to choose the best embedding.
- Contribution 1: new PAC-style generalization bound for orthonormal representations that depends on the spectral norm of the kernel and improves sample complexity over prior work.
- Contribution 2: proposes SPORE — a convex, non-smooth formulation minimizing hinge loss plus kernel spectral-norm regularization over the elliptope of orthonormal representations.
- Contribution 3: introduces IIP, a projected subgradient method using approximate (infeasible) projections with O(1/√T) convergence, and extends methods to multi-graph transduction.
- Empirical result: SPORE outperforms standard graph transduction baselines on UCI and MNIST and scales to graphs with thousands of nodes using IIP.
- Strength: strong theoretical grounding with a clear link from the new generalization bound to the SPORE formulation and improved PAC analysis.
- Strength: IIP is a novel, general-purpose optimization method for large-scale constrained non-smooth convex problems with provable convergence.
- Strength: practical significance — learns data-driven kernels rather than relying on fixed kernels, demonstrating improved performance.
- Strength: scalability — custom first-order solver enables application to much larger graphs than standard SDP solvers.
