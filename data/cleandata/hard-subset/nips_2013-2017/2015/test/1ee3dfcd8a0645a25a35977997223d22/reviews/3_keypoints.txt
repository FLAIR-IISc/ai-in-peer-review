- Problem: optimizing orthonormal graph embeddings and impact on learning functions for graph transduction.
- Background: reviews prior Laplacian-based generalization bound (eqn. 2).
- Contribution: derives a new generalization bound based on the maximum eigenvalue of the kernel matrix (eqn. 4).
- Optimization: proposes an alternate convex optimization criterion (eqn. 5) involving λ1(K), which is nondifferentiable but convex.
- Algorithm: solves the resulting problem (SEER) using inexact proximal methods.
- Extension: introduces MKL-SEER (multiple-kernel variant).
- Experiments: applies SEER and MKL-SEER to multiple tasks and shows significant performance improvements.
- Theoretical quality: paper offers good theoretical justification.
- Innovation: contains several intuitive and clever methodological leaps (new criterion, tackling hard optimization).
- Impact: represents a solid advancement over prior approaches.
- Presentation: complete, well-explained, and practically validated.
