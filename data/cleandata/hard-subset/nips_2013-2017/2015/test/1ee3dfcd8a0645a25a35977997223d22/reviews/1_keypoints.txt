- Paper reduces SSL to an equivalent kernel-based supervised problem using Zhang and Ando and builds theory/algorithms from that reduction.
- Main theoretical contribution: error bound showing minimising the spectral norm of the graph kernel improves generalisation.
- Adds a spectral-norm regularizer to the Zhang–Ando formulation and links error convergence to the Lovász number of the data graph.
- Proposes a proximal solver that handles cone–polytope intersection via an approximate projection step.
- Presents an MKL extension and experiments on several datasets showing convincing results.
- Paper is interesting and substantive but dense—contains many contributions for a single paper.
- Suggest explicitly state that the correspondence between eqs. (3) and (1) makes SSL a supervised problem (learning on labeled samples only), to avoid confusion about sums over S only.
- Section 2: notation in equation (5) is confusing—why is there an equals sign just before ω_C?
- Section 3: clarify how the embedding U is recovered from K and discuss practical choices for factorisation.
- Section 4 / Alg.1 (steps 8–9): ensuring K_r is PSD via eigendecomposition is expensive—ask if this is the main cost and whether optimising over a factorisation (e.g., K=LL^T) would be feasible to avoid repeated decompositions.
- Minor correction: Zhang and Ando is NIPS 2005, not 2006.
- Overall: provides new theoretical insights on spectral regularization for graph transduction with convincing experiments.
