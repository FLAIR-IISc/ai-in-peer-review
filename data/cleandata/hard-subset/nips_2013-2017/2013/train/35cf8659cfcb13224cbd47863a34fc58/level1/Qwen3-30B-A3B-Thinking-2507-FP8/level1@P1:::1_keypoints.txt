- BHCD models hierarchical communities as a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels and uses a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm learned in one pass.  
- The method scales as O(n² log n), independent of nested community count, making it much faster than IRM.  
- Extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data with a novel caching scheme to avoid expensive MCMC.  

- Technically sound: derives marginal likelihood via dynamic programming and provides clear O(n² log n) complexity analysis.  
- Algorithmic details are well-specified and experiments show efficiency and accuracy advantages over IRM.  
- Novel contribution: a scalable Bayesian nonparametric method for hierarchical community detection that fills gaps of prior methods.  
- Significant practical impact: roughly two orders of magnitude speedup over IRM with comparable or better accuracy; demonstrated on NIPS co-authorship network.  
- Clear writing and figures; experimental section gives sufficient detail for reproducibility (parameters and comparisons).  

- Limited empirical comparison: does not evaluate against other recent hierarchical methods (e.g., deep learning or newer Bayesian approaches).  
- Risk of merging disconnected components; sparse variant proposed but lacks detailed analysis of prevalence and conditions.  
- No theoretical guarantees on convergence or optimality of the greedy algorithm; potential failure modes not characterized.  
- Reproducibility concern: sparse details on random-seed handling for R=50 restarts and sensitivity to seed choices.  

- For acceptance: novel, scalable method with strong theory and experiments; high practical significance and well-written presentation.  
- Against acceptance: limited comparisons to recent methods, merging disconnected-component concerns, lack of theoretical guarantees, and incomplete reproducibility details.  

- Overall: strong, significant contribution that advances scalable hierarchical community detection; recommended for acceptance despite the noted limitations.
