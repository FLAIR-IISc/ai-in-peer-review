- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured Bayesian nonparametric model (mixture of stochastic blockmodels) for hierarchical community discovery in one pass.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on datasets including Sampson's monastery and NIPS co-authorship networks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with a clear introduction and thorough related work.  
- Technical exposition of the model and inference algorithm is clear.  
- Strength: novel and efficient approach for discovering hierarchical community structure.  
- Strength: model is well-motivated with a clear theoretical foundation.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation showing effectiveness and efficiency.  
- Weakness: model is limited to binary trees, potentially insufficient for complex networks.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity for very large networks.  
- Weakness: no comparisons to other state-of-the-art community detection methods.  
- Acceptance arguments mirror strengths: novelty, theory, and empirical results.  
- Rejection/concern arguments mirror weaknesses: binary-tree restriction, missing complexity analysis, and lack of SOTA comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address the listed weaknesses.
