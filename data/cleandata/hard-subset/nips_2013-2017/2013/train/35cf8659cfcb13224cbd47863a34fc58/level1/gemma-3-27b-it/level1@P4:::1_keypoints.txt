- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with greedy agglomerative inference for hierarchical community detection.  
- Key contribution: scalable, practical alternative to Bayesian nonparametric methods with worst-case O(n^2 log n) complexity and large speedups over IRM.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents the model, math, and algorithmic details.  
- Introduces greedy agglomerative algorithms and caching scheme that improve practical efficiency.  
- Empirical evaluation includes comparisons to IRM and experiments on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship networks.  
- Small qualitative example (Sampson’s network) aids understanding.  
- Strength — Scalability: addresses computational cost with provable complexity and reported runtime gains.  
- Strength — Efficiency: algorithmic design and caching yield practical speed improvements.  
- Strength — Clear presentation: notation and algorithm descriptions are detailed and readable.  
- Strength — Empirical validation: demonstrates effectiveness on small and large real-world datasets.  
- Strength — Novelty: combines tree-structured SBM mixture with greedy agglomeration focused on computational efficiency.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper lacks guidance and analysis on choosing α, β, δ, λ, γ and their impact.  
- Weakness — Limited theoretical analysis: insufficient discussion of convergence properties and statistical consistency.  
- Weakness — Model assumptions: reliance on stochastic blockmodel may not capture complex real-world network structures.  
- Weakness — Missing broader baselines: needs comparisons to non-Bayesian hierarchical methods (e.g., modularity-based approaches).  
- Weakness — Binary tree restriction: better predictive performance reported for binary trees but lacks analysis of underlying reasons and trade-offs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, offers substantial scalability improvements, shows practical effectiveness, and is well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks hyperparameter analysis, theoretical guarantees, may be limited by SBM assumptions, and omits broader baseline comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and practical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing hyperparameter sensitivity and broader experimental comparisons.
