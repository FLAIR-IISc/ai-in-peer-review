- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community detection.  
- Extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data with a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm.  
- Algorithm runs in O(n² log n) time and is independent of the number of communities.  
- Achieves comparable or better accuracy than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while being ~100x faster.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Sampson’s monastery network, NIPS co-authorship data, and the full NIPS dataset.  
- Positions method relative to prior work: extends stochastic blockmodels and improves greedy hierarchical clustering while avoiding expensive IRM inference.  
- Clear exposition and sufficient mathematical detail for reproducibility.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and well-supported experimental results.  
- Major practical contribution: scalability enabling hierarchical analysis on larger networks than prior methods.  
- Original combination of greedy model selection with Bayesian hierarchical detection to avoid computational bottlenecks.  
- Weakness: handling of extremely large sparse networks is not fully addressed (noted as future work).  
- Weakness: random tie-breaking for equal-score merges may yield inconsistent results and lacks justification.  
- Weakness: interpretability of discovered hierarchies for complex real-world networks could be explored more deeply.  
- Pro: compelling speed–accuracy tradeoff making hierarchical analysis feasible for larger networks.  
- Con: paper does not demonstrate scalability to networks with millions of vertices.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clearly written, significant and novel; recommendation for acceptance.
