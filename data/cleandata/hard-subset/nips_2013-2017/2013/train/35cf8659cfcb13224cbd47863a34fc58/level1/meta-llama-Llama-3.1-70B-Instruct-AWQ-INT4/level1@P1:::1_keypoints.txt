- Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that learn a probabilistic hierarchical community model in one pass.  
- Algorithms scale quadratically with the number of vertices and are independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Paper is well-written and provides a clear, concise introduction and thorough literature review.  
- Technical contribution is significant and novel within community discovery methods.  
- Experiments on Sampson's monastery, a NIPS co-authorship subset, and full NIPS co-authorship show BHCD discovers reasonable hierarchies.  
- BHCD makes predictions about two orders of magnitude faster than a leading Bayesian nonparametric baseline while achieving comparable performance.  
- Strength: novel model and algorithms that are significant to the field.  
- Strength: experimental validation demonstrates effectiveness on multiple datasets.  
- Strength: clarity and exposition are high.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art community detection methods.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to three datasets, raising questions about generality.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms.  
- Overall recommendation: accept (rating 8/10, confidence 8/10).
