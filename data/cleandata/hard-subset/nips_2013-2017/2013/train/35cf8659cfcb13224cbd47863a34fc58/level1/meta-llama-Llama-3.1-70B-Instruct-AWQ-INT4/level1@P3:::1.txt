The paper "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery" proposes an efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors present a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, which allows for the discovery of hierarchical community structure without requiring the number of communities to be known a priori. The model is learned using a greedy agglomerative algorithm that scales quadratically in the number of vertices of the network, but is independent of the number of nested communities.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the model and the algorithm. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several datasets. The experimental results show that the proposed method, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), is able to discover reasonable hierarchies and make predictions about two orders of magnitude faster than one of the fastest existing Bayesian nonparametric schemes, whilst attaining comparable performance.

The weaknesses of the paper are:
