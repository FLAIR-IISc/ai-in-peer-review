- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a greedy agglomerative algorithm for scalable hierarchical community discovery.  
- Demonstrates competitive accuracy with the IRM while achieving significantly improved speed, especially on large networks.  
- Builds on prior Bayesian community detection and hierarchical clustering work and correctly positions itself within related literature.  
- Primary strength is scalability: worst-case complexity independent of community count and an effective caching optimization.  
- Uses a Bayesian nonparametric probabilistic framework, offering principled uncertainty-aware community inference.  
- Provides theoretical justification via marginal likelihood derivation and recursive computation (eq. 10).  
- Empirically validated on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship data, showing practical utility and speedups.  
- Paper is clearly written with a concise and understandable algorithm description (Algorithm 1).  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity is insufficiently analyzed and choices are not well-justified.  
- Weakness: comparison between binary and non-binary tree structures is incomplete and trade-offs are under-discussed.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to more recent scalable hierarchical community detection methods (e.g., graph-embedding or approximate-inference approaches).  
- Weakness: link prediction procedure lacks practical detail on how predictive distributions are converted to predictions (thresholding/ranking).  
- Weakness: focuses on undirected graphs without fully exploring implications or limitations for directed networks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: large scalability gains, sound theory, clear algorithm, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient hyperparameter analysis, narrow method comparisons, and incomplete analysis of tree-structure trade-offs.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practically useful contribution worthy of acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the listed weaknesses.
