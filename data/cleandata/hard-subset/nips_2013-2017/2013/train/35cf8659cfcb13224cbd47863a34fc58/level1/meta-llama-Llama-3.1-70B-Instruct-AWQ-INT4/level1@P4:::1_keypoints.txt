- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community detection.  
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithms that learn a full probabilistic hierarchical model in one pass.  
- Algorithm scales quadratically in the number of vertices and is independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Uses a Bayesian nonparametric framework to avoid fixing the number of communities a priori.  
- Positions work relative to stochastic blockmodels, mixed-membership models, and Bayesian community inference.  
- Strength: novel and efficient algorithm for hierarchical community structure.  
- Strength: clear, detailed description of model and algorithm, easy to follow.  
- Strength: empirical demonstration on several datasets, including Sampson's monastery and NIPS co-authorship.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art community detection methods.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation on truly large-scale networks.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and their effect on performance.  
- Quality: well-written and clearly organized with helpful figures and tables.  
- Originality: introduces a novel Bayesian nonparametric approach for hierarchical communities.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential for wide practical use.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, efficiency, demonstrated performance, and clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: missing comprehensive baselines, limited large-scale evaluation, and need for hyperparameter study.  
- Recommended improvements: add thorough comparisons to baselines, evaluate on larger networks, analyze hyperparameters, and use additional metrics (e.g., modularity, NMI).
