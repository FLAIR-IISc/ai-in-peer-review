This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel and efficient Bayesian nonparametric method for inferring hierarchical community structures in networks. The core idea is to frame the problem as a greedy agglomerative model selection task. The model is a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, where the marginal likelihood of the data given a hierarchy can be computed exactly and efficiently via dynamic programming. This work extends the general framework of Bayesian hierarchical clustering, previously applied to i.i.d. data [13, 14], to the relational domain. It presents a compelling alternative to computationally intensive MCMC-based methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) [5, 7].

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The derivation of the recursive marginal likelihood (Eq. 10) and the use of the Bayes factor as a local merge score (Eq. 12) provide a principled probabilistic foundation for the greedy algorithm. The claims of speed and accuracy are well-supported by experiments on both classic (Sampson's monastery) and contemporary (NIPS co-authorship) network datasets. The authors are honest about the greedy nature of the algorithm and appropriately use multiple restarts to mitigate the risk of poor local optima.

**Clarity:** The manuscript is clearly written and well-organized. The progression from the basic stochastic blockmodel to the full hierarchical model is logical and easy to follow. Algorithm 1, combined with the description of the caching scheme for sufficient statistics, provides sufficient detail for an expert to understand the implementation. The figures are effective in conveying both the model's structure and the empirical results.

**Originality:** The primary contribution is the novel synthesis of Bayesian agglomerative clustering with a stochastic blockmodel likelihood for network data. While the algorithmic skeleton is adapted from prior work [14], its application to relational data, including the specific likelihood factorization and efficient caching, is original. This successfully transforms a common heuristic (agglomerative clustering) into a fully Bayesian model selection procedure.

**Significance:** The work is highly significant as it addresses the critical challenge of scalability in Bayesian network analysis. The demonstration of a two-orders-of-magnitude speedup over a widely used baseline like the IRM, while maintaining comparable predictive performance, is a substantial contribution. This makes sophisticated hierarchical Bayesian modeling accessible for medium-to-large scale networks where MCMC would be computationally prohibitive. The proposed method is likely to be used by practitioners and serve as a strong baseline for future research in this area.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   **Pro:** Presents a novel, principled, and extremely fast algorithm for a fundamental problem in network analysis.
*   **Pro:** Strong empirical results that clearly demonstrate a significant performance advantage over established methods.
*   **Pro:** The work is a complete and well-executed contribution.
*   **Con:** The greedy nature of the inference lacks theoretical guarantees, though this is a common and practical trade-off.

Overall, the strengths of this paper far outweigh its minor limitations. It is a high-quality submission with clear potential for impact.
