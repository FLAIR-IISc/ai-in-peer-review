

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks, called Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD). The authors extend stochastic blockmodels to a tree-structured mixture, enabling the discovery of hierarchical community structure. Their key contribution is a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm that learns the hierarchical community model in O(n² log n) time, where n is the number of vertices. The authors claim BHCD runs two orders of magnitude faster than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while achieving comparable or better accuracy.

The paper builds upon previous work in community detection, including stochastic blockmodels [1], hierarchical clustering methods [3,4], and Bayesian approaches like the IRM [5-7]. Unlike previous Bayesian hierarchical methods that require expensive iterative inference (e.g., MCMC), BHCD uses a greedy approach with a novel caching scheme that enables efficient computation. The paper clearly distinguishes itself from prior work by providing a full probabilistic account of the data while maintaining computational efficiency.

**Quality**: The technical contributions are sound and well-justified. The authors provide a clear mathematical formulation of the model, including the marginal likelihood calculation and the generative process. The dynamic programming approach for computing the marginal likelihood is well-explained, and the caching scheme for the greedy algorithm is novel and well-executed. The experimental validation is thorough, with appropriate datasets (Sampson's monastery network and NIPS co-authorship data) and metrics (log likelihood, predictive performance, accuracy, AUC). The paper acknowledges limitations, such as the need to prohibit merging disconnected communities (addressed in BHCD sparse) and the need for multiple restarts to handle ties. The claims are well-supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results.

**Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-explained, with appropriate figures illustrating key concepts (e.g., Figure 1 showing how a tree defines partitions). The paper provides sufficient detail for an expert to reproduce the results, including algorithm pseudocode (Algorithm 1) and implementation details. The structure flows logically from problem statement to model to algorithm to experiments.

**Originality**: The paper makes a clear and significant contribution to the field. The greedy agglomerative approach with the novel caching scheme is distinct from previous Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods. While hierarchical clustering has been used previously [3,4], BHCD provides the first Bayesian approach that combines hierarchical structure with computational efficiency. The paper thoroughly contextualizes its work relative to prior NIPS papers and other relevant literature, demonstrating that the approach is substantially different from previous contributions. The model's ability to scale quadratically with n while being independent of the number of communities is a novel technical achievement.

**Significance**: The results address a practical need for scalable community detection algorithms on large networks. The two orders of magnitude speed improvement over IRM while maintaining comparable accuracy makes BHCD particularly valuable for real-world applications. The method provides a full probabilistic account of the data, which is a significant improvement over purely greedy hierarchical clustering methods. The hierarchical structure discovered on the NIPS co-authorship network (Figure 5) is interpretable and meaningful, demonstrating the method's practical utility.

**Strengths**: 
- Clear theoretical foundation with sound mathematical formulation
- Significant computational efficiency gains over existing Bayesian approaches
- Thorough experimental validation across multiple datasets
- Well-organized and clearly written paper
- Novel caching scheme enabling O(n² log n) time complexity

**Weaknesses**:
- The greedy approach might not find the globally optimal solution, though the authors provide evidence that it performs well in practice
- The paper assumes undirected networks with no self-edges, which may limit applicability to some network types
- The comparison with non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods is limited (only mentioned in passing)

**Arguments for acceptance**: 
- The method provides a substantial efficiency improvement over existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection approaches
- The theoretical analysis is sound and well-justified
- The experimental results demonstrate clear practical advantages
- The approach is distinct from and advances the state of the art in hierarchical community detection

**Arguments against acceptance**: 
- The greedy approach's potential suboptimality could be a concern for some applications
- The assumption of undirected networks with no self-edges limits the model's applicability
- The paper could have included more comparisons with state-of-the-art non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of community detection. The technical innovation in the greedy algorithm with its novel caching scheme is compelling, and the empirical results demonstrate clear practical advantages. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and addresses an important problem in network analysis. The authors have clearly distinguished their work from previous contributions and provided thorough validation of their approach. This paper advances the state of the art in scalable Bayesian community detection and would be of interest to both practitioners and researchers in the field.
