- Proposes BHCD, a novel Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithms that learn the hierarchy in a single pass.
- Algorithms have worst-case time complexity O(n^2 log n).
- Paper is clearly written with well-explained technical details and related work review.
- Empirical evaluation on datasets (Sampson’s monastery, NIPS co-authorship) shows BHCD finds reasonable hierarchies.
- BHCD makes predictions up to two orders of magnitude faster than existing Bayesian nonparametric methods while achieving comparable performance.
- Strength: novel and efficient model for hierarchical community discovery.
- Strength: single-pass greedy algorithms enabling practical learning.
- Strength: demonstrated effectiveness across multiple datasets.
- Weakness: model assumes a specific hierarchical form that may not suit all network types.
- Weakness: algorithms may not scale to very large networks despite suggested improvements.
- Weakness: lacking more detailed comparisons to other related (especially non-Bayesian) community-detection methods.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestion that authors address scalability, model assumptions, and broader comparisons in future work.
