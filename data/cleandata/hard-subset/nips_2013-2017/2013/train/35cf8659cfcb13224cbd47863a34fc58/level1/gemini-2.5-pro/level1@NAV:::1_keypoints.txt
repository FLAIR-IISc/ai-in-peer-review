- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a fast Bayesian nonparametric method for inferring hierarchical community structure in networks.
- Frames inference as a greedy agglomerative model selection task using a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.
- Derives an exact, efficiently computable marginal likelihood via dynamic programming, and uses the Bayes factor as a local merge score.
- Extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering from i.i.d. data to relational (network) data, offering an alternative to MCMC-based methods like the IRM.
- Technically sound: recursive marginal likelihood (Eq. 10) and Bayes-factor merge score (Eq. 12) provide a principled probabilistic foundation for the algorithm.
- Empirical claims of speed and accuracy are well-supported by experiments on networks such as Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship.
- Authors acknowledge the greedy algorithm’s limitations and use multiple restarts to mitigate local optima risk.
- Manuscript is clear and well-organized, with a logical progression from SBM to hierarchical model and effective figures.
- Algorithm 1 plus the caching scheme provides sufficient implementation detail for experts to reproduce the method.
- Originality stems from synthesizing Bayesian agglomerative clustering with SBM likelihoods for relational data and the specific likelihood factorization and caching.
- Significant contribution to scalability: demonstrates roughly two-orders-of-magnitude speedup over IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance.
- Strong case for acceptance: novel, principled, fast algorithm with strong empirical gains; main drawback is lack of theoretical guarantees for greedy inference.
