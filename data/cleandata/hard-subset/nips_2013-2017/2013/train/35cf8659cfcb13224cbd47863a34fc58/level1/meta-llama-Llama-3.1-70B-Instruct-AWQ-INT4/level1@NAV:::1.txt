This paper proposes an efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The model, called Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), is a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels that can learn a fully probabilistic, hierarchical community model in a single pass through the data. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of BHCD on several data sets, including Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship network.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' argument. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the work, and the related work section is thorough and well-referenced. The technical sections are also well-written, with clear explanations of the model and the inference algorithm.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors propose a novel and efficient model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks.
* The model is well-motivated and has a clear theoretical foundation.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of the model on several data sets, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The model is limited to binary trees, which may not be sufficient to capture the complexity of some social networks.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the model, which may be a concern for very large networks.
* The authors do not compare their model to other state-of-the-art models for community detection, which may be a limitation of the evaluation.
