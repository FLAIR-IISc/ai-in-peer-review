- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for discovering hierarchical community structure without predefining the number of communities.  
- Learns model with a greedy agglomerative algorithm that scales O(n^2) in number of vertices and is independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Experiments show BHCD finds reasonable hierarchies and makes predictions ~100x faster than a leading Bayesian nonparametric baseline while achieving comparable performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a thorough discussion of related work.  
- Strength: novel and efficient Bayesian nonparametric model tailored to hierarchical community discovery.  
- Strength: scalable greedy agglomerative learning algorithm with favorable dependence on hierarchy depth.  
- Strength: empirical validation on multiple datasets demonstrating speed and competitive accuracy.  
- Weakness: assumes hierarchical community structure, which may not hold for all real-world networks.  
- Weakness: requires specification of several hyperparameters that may be hard to tune in practice.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of the algorithm’s computational complexity for large-scale networks.  
- Weakness: does not compare against a broad set of state-of-the-art community detection methods.  
- Quality score: 8/10 — strong paper but could improve complexity analysis and comparisons.  
- Clarity score: 9/10 — clear exposition and easy to follow.  
- Originality score: 8/10 — novel contribution but builds on existing tree-structured mixture ideas.  
- Significance score: 8/10 — fast and effective approach, but impact limited by missing analyses and broader comparisons.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel efficient model, scalable algorithm, thorough related work and empirical results.  
- Arguments for rejection: hierarchical assumption, hyperparameter tuning difficulty, missing complexity analysis, and limited comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.
