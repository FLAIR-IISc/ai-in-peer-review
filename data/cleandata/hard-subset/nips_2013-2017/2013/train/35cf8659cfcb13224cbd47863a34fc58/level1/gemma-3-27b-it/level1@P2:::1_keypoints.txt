- Proposes Bayesian hierarchical community discovery (BHCD) using a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with greedy agglomerative inference.  
- Aims to provide a computationally efficient Bayesian alternative to costly methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly introduces problem, related work, and proposed method.  
- Uses a tree prior on partitions to enable efficient computation and model selection.  
- Greedy agglomerative algorithm is detailed and reduces computational complexity (claimed quadratic worst-case).  
- Demonstrates substantial speedup (≈100x faster than one IRM iteration) with comparable or better accuracy.  
- Theoretical foundations and likelihood derivations are clearly presented.  
- Empirical validation on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship networks shows effective hierarchical structure discovery.  
- Novel combination of tree-structured mixtures and greedy inference provides a new, efficient approach for hierarchical community detection.  
- Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Does not analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ), leaving uncertainty about robustness.  
- Limited empirical comparisons — should evaluate against other methods like LFRM and ILA for a fuller assessment.  
- Tie-breaking in the greedy search is handled ad hoc; needs a principled strategy or analysis of its impact.  
- Assumes Beta-distributed edge probabilities without strong justification; this assumption should be better motivated.  
- Scalability in sparse graphs is not fully addressed; more discussion or solutions are needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel efficient algorithm, strong speed-accuracy tradeoff, solid writing and theory.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing hyperparameter analysis, limited method comparisons, and ad hoc tie-breaking affecting reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity, broader comparisons, and tie-breaking methodology.  
- Requested revisions: perform hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, add comparisons with methods like LFRM/ILA, and provide a principled tie-breaking approach or impact analysis.
