This paper proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The model, called Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), is a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels. The authors describe a family of greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that can learn a fully probabilistic, hierarchical community model in one pass through the data. The algorithms scale quadratically in the number of vertices of the network, but independently of the number of nested communities.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of community discovery in social networks. They also provide a thorough review of previous work in the field, including stochastic blockmodels, mixed membership stochastic blockmodels, and Bayesian approaches to community detection.
