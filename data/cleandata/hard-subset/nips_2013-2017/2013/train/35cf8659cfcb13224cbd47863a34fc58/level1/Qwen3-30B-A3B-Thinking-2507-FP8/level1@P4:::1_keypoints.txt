- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured extension of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm with a novel caching scheme achieving O(n² log n) runtime.  
- Claims BHCD is ~100× faster than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while attaining comparable or better accuracy.  
- Provides a full probabilistic model with dynamic programming for marginal likelihood computation.  
- Caching scheme avoids expensive iterative inference (e.g., MCMC) and enables practical scalability.  
- Experimental validation is thorough: Sampson’s monastery, NIPS co-authorship, metrics include log likelihood, predictive performance, accuracy, and AUC.  
- Addresses practical issues (e.g., prohibiting merges of disconnected communities via BHCD sparse) and uses multiple restarts to handle ties.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and reproducible, with consistent notation, figures, pseudocode, and implementation details.  
- Novelty: first Bayesian approach combining hierarchical structure with this level of computational efficiency and independence from number of communities.  
- Significance: addresses practical need for scalable community detection and produces interpretable hierarchical structure on real networks.  
- Strengths: sound theory, significant computational gains, novel caching scheme, thorough experiments, well-written presentation.  
- Weaknesses: greedy algorithm may be suboptimal globally; assumes undirected networks without self-edges; limited comparisons to non-Bayesian hierarchical methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial efficiency improvement, solid theoretical justification, strong empirical results, advances state of the art.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential suboptimality of greedy search, applicability limited by model assumptions, insufficient comparison to top non-Bayesian baselines.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound, and practically valuable contribution to scalable Bayesian community detection.
