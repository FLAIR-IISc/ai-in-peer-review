- Proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric model: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithms that learn a fully probabilistic hierarchical community model in a single pass.  
- Well-motivated with a clear overview of community-detection literature and limitations of existing methods (SBM, IRM).  
- Significant technical contribution: handles hierarchical community structure with an efficient algorithm distinct from MCMC-based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, with consistent notation, well-formatted equations, and a clear exposition of model and algorithm.  
- Thorough experiments on multiple real-world datasets showing the method is faster and more accurate than state-of-the-art (including IRM).  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution to community detection with strong scores (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance = 9/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, novel greedy learning approach, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: assumes hierarchical structure may not hold for all networks, greedy approach may miss the optimal solution.  
- Additional concern: paper lacks a detailed analysis of the algorithm’s computational complexity.  
- Suggestions for improvement: add detailed computational-complexity analysis, evaluate on more datasets to test robustness, and provide deeper comparisons to other state-of-the-art methods.
