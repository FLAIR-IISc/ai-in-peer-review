- Introduces BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model that infers hierarchical community structure by modeling the adjacency matrix as a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Computes marginal likelihood for a specific tree by summing over tree-consistent partitions, enabling efficient dynamic-programming likelihood calculation.  
- Uses a fast greedy agglomerative inference algorithm that merges subtrees based on a local Bayes factor, avoiding slow MCMC.  
- Presents an implementation with worst-case time complexity O(n^2 log n), independent of the number of communities.  
- Empirical results show BHCD is orders of magnitude faster than IRM while achieving comparable or better predictive performance.  
- Qualitative results (e.g., Sampson’s Monastery) demonstrate interpretable, meaningful hierarchies aligned with known structure.  
- Theoretical derivation (recursive marginal likelihood) and algorithmic choices are technically sound and well-explained.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with effective figures and reproducible-level detail.  
- Original contribution: novel synthesis of Bayesian hierarchical clustering and stochastic blockmodels for relational data, including non-binary merge types.  
- Significant practical impact: provides scalable, probabilistic hierarchical community detection useful for medium-to-large networks.  
- Main weaknesses: greedy point-estimate inference prone to local optima and lacking posterior uncertainty quantification.  
- Additional concerns: reliance on local merge criteria can produce global suboptimality, and limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., γ) in experiments.
