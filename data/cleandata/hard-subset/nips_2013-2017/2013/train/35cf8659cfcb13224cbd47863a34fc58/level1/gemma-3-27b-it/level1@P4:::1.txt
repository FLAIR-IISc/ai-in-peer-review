The paper proposes a Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD) method for uncovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The model is a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, and the authors introduce greedy agglomerative algorithms for efficient inference. The core contribution lies in providing a scalable (O(n^2 log n) worst-case) and relatively fast (two orders of magnitude faster than IRM) alternative to existing Bayesian nonparametric approaches for hierarchical community detection.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the model and algorithm. It begins with a relevant introduction, motivating the need for hierarchical community detection and outlining the limitations of existing methods. The mathematical formulation of the model is reasonably clear, building upon the stochastic blockmodel and extending it to a hierarchical setting. The description of the greedy agglomerative algorithm is detailed and includes considerations for computational efficiency. The experimental evaluation, including comparisons to IRM and results on real-world datasets (Sampson’s monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship network), is a strong point. The inclusion of a small qualitative example (Sampson’s network) aids understanding.

**Strengths:**

*   **Scalability:** The proposed method addresses a critical limitation of existing Bayesian nonparametric approaches for hierarchical community detection – computational cost. The claimed O(n^2 log n) complexity and reported speedup compared to IRM are significant.
*   **Efficiency:** The greedy agglomerative algorithm and caching scheme contribute to the method's practical efficiency.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The mathematical notation is generally clear, and the algorithm description is detailed.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate the method's effectiveness on both small (Sampson’s network) and large (NIPS co-authorship network) datasets. The comparison to IRM is particularly valuable.
*   **Novelty:** While building on existing concepts (stochastic blockmodels, Bayesian nonparametric methods), the combination of a tree-structured mixture model with a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm appears novel, particularly in its focus on computational efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions setting hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) but doesn't offer much guidance on how to choose these values or an analysis of their impact on the results. This could limit the applicability of the method in practice.
*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the complexity is stated, a more detailed theoretical analysis of the algorithm's convergence properties and statistical consistency would strengthen the paper.
*   **Model Assumptions:** The reliance on the stochastic blockmodel might be a limiting factor. Real-world networks often exhibit more complex structure than can be adequately captured by this model.
*   **Comparison to other baselines:** While comparison to IRM is provided, it would be beneficial to compare BHCD with other, non-Bayesian, hierarchical community detection methods (e.g., those based on modularity optimization) to provide a broader performance context.
*   **Binary Tree Restriction:** The discussion of the binary tree restricted versions suggests they yield better predictive performance, but the reasons for this are not explored in sufficient depth. A more detailed analysis of the trade-offs between tree structure and predictive accuracy would be valuable.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant problem in network analysis – efficient hierarchical community detection.
*   The proposed method offers a substantial improvement in scalability compared to existing Bayesian nonparametric approaches.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the method's effectiveness and practicality.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presents the model and algorithm.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The lack of detailed hyperparameter analysis and theoretical guarantees weakens the paper.
*   The reliance on the stochastic blockmodel might limit the method's applicability to certain types of networks.
*   The comparison to other non-Bayesian baselines is missing.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of network analysis. The proposed BHCD method offers a practical and efficient solution for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. While some theoretical and experimental limitations exist, the strengths of the paper – particularly its scalability and clear presentation – outweigh these weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above, particularly regarding hyperparameter sensitivity and a broader comparison to other hierarchical community detection methods.
