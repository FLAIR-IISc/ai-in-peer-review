- Proposal: BHCD — a Bayesian nonparametric model that models networks as tree-structured mixtures of stochastic blockmodels with a prior over tree-consistent partitions and a recursive exact marginal likelihood p(D|T).  
- Likelihood: Uses a Beta–Bernoulli likelihood adapted for relational data, building on Bayesian hierarchical clustering and Rose Trees.  
- Inference: Greedy agglomerative bottom-up algorithm that merges subtree pairs by largest increase in marginal likelihood (Bayes factor).  
- Efficiency: Novel caching of sufficient statistics yields O(n^2 log n) runtime, independent of number of communities.  
- Empirical speedup: Approximately two orders of magnitude faster than IRM while matching predictive performance.  
- Evaluation: Demonstrated meaningful hierarchical structure recovery on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship networks.  
- Strength — Significance/Scalability: Makes Bayesian hierarchical network modeling practical for much larger datasets, bridging fast non-probabilistic and slow Bayesian methods.  
- Strength — Methodological soundness: Principled Bayesian formulation, marginal-likelihood scoring, Bayes-factor merges, and a full generative model enabling link prediction.  
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, derivations and algorithm are clear, and prior work is properly credited.  
- Weakness — Originality: Core framework is a direct extension of prior hierarchical clustering methods; novelty is primarily in adapting them to relational SBMs and engineering for efficiency.  
- Weakness — Greedy inference limits: Single point estimate prone to local optima; multiple restarts don’t capture posterior uncertainty and can preclude globally optimal trees.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Several fixed hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) with no sensitivity analysis or justification; γ affects hierarchy depth.  
- Overall: Strong, practical contribution trading full posterior inference for large speedups; technically sound and recommended for acceptance.  
- Minor suggestions: Clarify complexity comparison in Table 1 (internal nodes vs K) and discuss network structures that could challenge the local merge criterion.
