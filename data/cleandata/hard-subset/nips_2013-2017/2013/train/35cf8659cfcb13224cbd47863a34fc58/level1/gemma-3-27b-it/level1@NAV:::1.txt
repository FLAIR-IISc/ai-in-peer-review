## Review of Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

This paper introduces a novel Bayesian nonparametric model, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), for uncovering hierarchical community structure within social networks. The core idea is to represent the network using a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, enabling inference of nested communities. The authors present a greedy agglomerative algorithm for model selection, highlighting its efficiency – achieving speedups of two orders of magnitude compared to the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while maintaining comparable accuracy. 

The work builds upon existing Bayesian approaches to community detection, specifically addressing the computational limitations of methods like the IRM and earlier hierarchical extensions. It acknowledges the shortcomings of purely greedy hierarchical clustering methods (lack of probabilistic accounts) and aims to bridge the gap. The paper clearly positions itself within the existing literature, referencing relevant works on stochastic blockmodels (Holland et al., 1983), mixed membership models (Airoldi et al., 2008), and Bayesian nonparametric approaches (Kemp et al., 2006, Ghahramani & Heller, 2013). 

**Strengths:**

*   **Efficiency:** The primary strength is the algorithm’s speed. The reported two orders of magnitude improvement over the IRM is significant and makes the method practical for larger networks. The theoretical analysis of O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) complexity is also a positive point.
*   **Probabilistic Framework:**  The Bayesian approach provides a principled way to quantify uncertainty in the discovered community structure.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-structured and explains the model and algorithm in a reasonably clear manner. The inclusion of illustrative examples (Sampson’s monastery network) is helpful.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both Sampson’s data and the NIPS co-authorship network demonstrate the method's ability to discover meaningful community structures and achieve good predictive performance.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper briefly mentions hyperparameter setting but doesn't delve into a thorough sensitivity analysis.  The choice of hyperparameters (α=δ=1.0, β=λ=0.2, γ=0.4) feels somewhat arbitrary and could significantly impact results.
*   **Comparison Depth:** While the comparison to IRM is useful, a more detailed comparison against other relevant hierarchical community detection algorithms (e.g., LFRM, ILA) beyond just speed and accuracy metrics would strengthen the paper.
*   **Model Assumptions:** The reliance on the stochastic blockmodel as the base unit could be limiting in certain network settings where more complex relationships exist. This isn’t explicitly discussed.
*   **Limited Theoretical Novelty:**  The core idea builds heavily on existing work in Bayesian hierarchical clustering and extends it to relational data. While the implementation details and speedups are valuable, the underlying theoretical contribution feels incremental.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Significant speed improvement over existing Bayesian approaches.
*   Provides a principled probabilistic framework for hierarchical community detection.
*   Demonstrated performance on relevant datasets.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited theoretical novelty.
*   Lack of extensive hyperparameter analysis.
*   Comparison to other recent algorithms could be more comprehensive.



**Overall:**

This is a solid contribution that addresses a practical challenge in community detection – scalability. While the theoretical novelty is moderate, the significant speed improvements and probabilistic framework make it a valuable addition to the field. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, specifically requesting a more thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and a more comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art algorithms.
