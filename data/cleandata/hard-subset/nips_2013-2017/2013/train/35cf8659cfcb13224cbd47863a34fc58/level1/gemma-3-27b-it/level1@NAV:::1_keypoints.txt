- Introduces BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.
- Proposes a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm achieving ~100x speedup over the IRM with comparable accuracy.
- The algorithm has theoretical complexity O(n^2 log n), making it practical for larger networks.
- Bayesian/probabilistic framework provides principled uncertainty quantification for discovered hierarchies.
- Paper situates work well within related literature (SBMs, mixed-membership, Bayesian nonparametrics).
- Clear presentation with illustrative examples (e.g., Sampson’s monastery) aiding understanding.
- Empirical validation on Sampson’s network and NIPS co-authorship shows meaningful communities and good predictive performance.
- Hyperparameter settings are reported but sensitivity analysis is lacking and choices appear somewhat arbitrary.
- Comparison depth is limited: primarily compared to IRM, with insufficient benchmarking against other hierarchical/community models (e.g., LFRM, ILA).
- Model assumption reliance on stochastic blockmodels may limit applicability to networks with more complex relations.
- Theoretical novelty is moderate—method extends existing Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data but is largely incremental.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, requesting more hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and broader comparisons to recent algorithms.
