## Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper introduces a novel and efficient Bayesian nonparametric model, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), for uncovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The core idea is to represent the network as a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, enabling probabilistic inference of nested communities. The authors propose greedy agglomerative algorithms for model selection, achieving significant speedups compared to existing methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while maintaining comparable accuracy.

The paper effectively positions its contribution within the existing literature on community detection. It clearly outlines the limitations of flat community models (SBM, MMSBM), greedy hierarchical clustering lacking probabilistic guarantees, and existing Bayesian approaches (IRM, extensions) suffering from computational bottlenecks. The proposed BHCD builds upon Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering, offering a compelling balance between model expressiveness and computational efficiency. The authors correctly identify the quadratic time complexity as a key advantage, particularly its independence from the number of nested communities.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of a tree-structured mixture of SBMs with a greedy agglomerative inference scheme is a novel approach to hierarchical community detection.
*   **Efficiency:** The reported speedups (two orders of magnitude faster than IRM) are significant and address a crucial limitation of existing Bayesian methods. The O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) complexity is well-defined and a clear benefit.
*   **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a clear mathematical formulation of the model and the inference procedure, including the derivation of the marginal likelihood and the Bayes factor score.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on Sampson’s monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and the full NIPS network demonstrate the effectiveness of BHCD in discovering meaningful community structures and achieving competitive predictive performance.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper lacks a detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (α, β, δ, λ, γ). While values are provided, the justification for these choices and their impact on the results are not thoroughly explored.
*   **Scalability on Sparse Networks:** While the algorithm scales well for dense networks, the authors acknowledge a potential bottleneck on sparse networks due to the O(n<sup>2</sup>) complexity. The proposed “sparse” variant addresses this, but a more detailed analysis of its performance on varying network densities would be beneficial.
*   **Limited Comparison:** The comparison to other hierarchical community detection methods is limited. Expanding the benchmark to include more recent and competitive algorithms would strengthen the evaluation.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel approach to a challenging problem.
*   The demonstrated efficiency gains are substantial and address a significant limitation of existing methods.
*   The empirical results are promising and support the claims made by the authors.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of detailed hyperparameter analysis and scalability discussion on sparse networks.
*   The limited comparison to other hierarchical community detection algorithms.



**Overall:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The work represents a significant contribution to the field of community detection, offering a compelling combination of model expressiveness, computational efficiency, and theoretical grounding. While some aspects could be further strengthened, the overall quality of the paper and the potential impact of the proposed method warrant its inclusion in NIPS.
