- Introduces BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric model that represents hierarchical community structure as a tree-structured mixture of SBMs.
- Key technical contribution: exact dynamic-programming evaluation of the marginal likelihood over all partitions consistent with a tree, composing on- and off-diagonal marginal likelihoods (beta–Bernoulli).
- Learning algorithm: greedy agglomerative rose-tree model selection using local Bayes factors with join and absorption merges; complexity O(n^2 log n) with cached sufficient statistics and a sparse variant.
- Experiments: on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship graphs show large speedups over IRM (MCMC and greedy) with comparable or better predictive performance and interpretable hierarchies.
- Relates to prior work: connects to SBMs, mixed-membership SBMs, IRM/hierarchical IRM, Bayesian hierarchical clustering/rose trees, LFRM/ILA, and Mondrian-process relational models.
- Quality: model derivation is sound and well motivated; recursion and local merge scoring are solid.
- Runtime comparison caveat: fairness of runtime comparisons to IRM is unclear (single Gibbs scans vs complete BHCD runs).
- Hyperparameters: hyperparameters are fixed with no sensitivity analysis or hyperparameter inference provided.
- Clarity: generally well written, but join vs absorption explanations and caching could be streamlined; pseudo-code and complexity proof sketch would help; some grammar/typos remain.
- Originality: novel and elegant use of rose-tree priors with SBM marginals and an exact tree-marginal recursion; should cite hierarchical random graphs and degree-corrected/nested SBMs for positioning.
- Significance: scalable (independent of number of communities) and interpretable hierarchical outputs useful for exploratory analysis and fast link prediction.
- Pros: principled Bayesian extension to networks with exact tree-marginal computation, strong empirical speedups, scalability to thousands of nodes, and a practical sparse variant.
- Cons: greedy search limits posterior uncertainty and consistency guarantees; limited comparisons to modern hierarchical/degree-corrected SBMs and modest benchmark scope; no hyperparameter learning or robustness checks.
- Overall: solid, in-scope NeurIPS contribution; reviewer leans toward acceptance pending minor revisions on comparisons and hyperparameter treatment.
