- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels to infer hierarchical communities in networks.
- Computes marginal likelihood p(D|T) exactly and efficiently via a recursive dynamic-programming formula.
- Core inference is a greedy agglomerative algorithm that merges tree nodes using a Bayes factor merge score.
- Algorithm is deterministic, single-pass, and claimed worst-case complexity O(n² log n) with O(1) inner-loop via cached sufficient statistics.
- Builds on Bayesian hierarchical clustering for non-relational data, adapting it to relational SBMs.
- Empirically much faster (≈100×) than IRM while achieving comparable or better predictive performance on NIPS co-authorship data.
- Experiments include qualitative (Sampson’s monastery) and quantitative evaluations, with clear visualizations and comparisons.
- Paper is technically sound: well-formulated model, clear derivations, and justified algorithmic claims.
- Presentation is clear and well-organized; figures effectively illustrate model and merge types.
- Originality: first successful extension of fast greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data (SBMs).
- Significance: makes Bayesian hierarchical community detection practical for medium-to-large networks, likely useful to practitioners.
- Arguments for acceptance: large practical speedup, novel adaptation of prior ideas to relational data, technical clarity, and strong empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: greedy heuristic may miss global optimum and requires restarts.
- Merge criterion is local (subgraph-only), which can yield globally suboptimal merges.
- Worst-case O(n² log n) is not ideal for very large sparse networks (m << n²), though a BHCD-sparse variant is proposed.
