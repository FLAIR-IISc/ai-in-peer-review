- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery in social networks.  
- Claims efficient inference and scalability via agglomerative clustering with likelihood updates adapted for network data.  
- Main technical novelty is adapting the likelihood and recursive sufficient-statistic updates to enable scalable inference.  
- Authors claim likelihood computation is O(nodes + edges), making it suitable for large networks.  
- Section 3’s dynamic programming and recursive updates are dense and hard to follow; more detail and examples are needed.  
- Experiments on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship show improvements over a flat IRM baseline and qualitative success.  
- Averaging over hierarchical hypotheses improves predictive performance.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to existing hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process), a major weakness.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable—paper reads more like model selection than true nonparametrics.  
- Scalability claims are not fully empirically demonstrated because tested networks are not especially large.  
- Paper is technically sound and integrates prior work well, but originality is limited.  
- Presentation is generally clear with well-defined notation, though some sections remain challenging for readers.  
- Overall recommendation mixed: novel and potentially significant but needs stronger exposition and comparative evaluation.
