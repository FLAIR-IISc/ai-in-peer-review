- Paper proposes BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model combining SBM, single-table IRM, and Bayesian Rose trees to discover hierarchical communities.
- Writing quality is good, but avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.
- Theoretical presentation is concise and consistent in notation.
- Novel combination of familiar techniques yields an original model extension.
- Main algorithm is a greedy agglomerative method with claimed O(n^2 log n) time via a novel caching scheme.
- Authors present a sparse BHCD variant that computes only on edges, running faster and performing better on sparse networks.
- Experiments on Sampson’s monastery, NIPS subset, and full NIPS show BHCD ≈ LFRM and better than IRM for predictive performance.
- BHCD can make reasonable predictions before IRM finishes a single Gibbs scan.
- AUC is used for evaluation but the computation method is unclear and must be explicitly explained.
- Recommend adding NMI as a standard metric for SBM comparisons.
- Experiments are small-scale; larger benchmark datasets used in recent SBM work should be included.
- Experimental work is illustrative rather than fully comparative; comparisons to semi-parametric and other state-of-the-art methods are needed.
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by analysis and experiments.
- Clarity is good and reproducible for experts, but more detailed algorithmic and experimental descriptions would help.
- Overall recommendation: accept, conditional on addressing AUC clarity, expanding experiments to larger datasets, and providing broader comparative evaluations.
