- Summary: Proposes BHCD, a tree-restricted hierarchical stochastic blockmodel enabling exact marginal likelihood p(D|T) via dynamic programming and a greedy O(n^2 log n) agglomerative tree-learning algorithm.
- Relation to prior work: Reuses Bayesian tree priors (Heller & Ghahramani; Blundell et al.) and adapts them to relational data with Beta-Bernoulli block likelihoods, positioned versus IRM and other hierarchical network models.

Strengths
- Clear technical idea: tree-consistent partition restriction yields exact marginal likelihoods and efficient local Bayes factor computations.
- Practical impact: reported two-orders-of-magnitude speedups over IRM with comparable or better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy.
- Interpretability: produces hierarchical community structure useful for social network analysis, beyond flat clustering.
- Algorithm engineering: O(1) inner-loop updates, sensible sparse variant for realistic networks.
- Experimental protocol: uses standard datasets (Sampson, NIPS-234), restarts, and predictive evaluation.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Objective ambiguity: unclear whether the greedy algorithm optimizes p(D|T), a lower bound, or approximates p(T|D); relationship to Bayesian model not justified.
- Sparse variant mismatch: BHCD sparse may change prior/support and bias inference without a Bayesian interpretation.
- Presentation issues: grammar, vague phrasing, unclear figures, and hard-to-follow Section 4 notation and derivations.
- Limited ablations: fixed hyperparameters with no sensitivity analysis, no memory/space complexity estimates, and limited stability/tie-breaking study.
- Comparisons: relation to other hierarchical models and fairness of runtime comparisons (implementations, sweep counts) are underdeveloped.

Quality
- Core modeling and dynamic programming recursion are sound and empirically supported, but the greedy training objective should be explicitly stated and theoretically tied to the generative model.

Clarity
- Significant editing needed: derive key equations step-by-step, define sufficient statistics once, provide small examples for join vs absorption, and improve figure captions and leaf ordering.

Originality
- Novel combination: applying Bayesian rose-tree machinery to relational blockmodels with tailored likelihoods and efficient learning is meaningfully different from IRM.

Significance
- High practical relevance: addresses speed in Bayesian nonparametrics for networks and yields useful hierarchical structure for social scientists.

Requests for clarification
- Objective: State explicitly whether the algorithm maximizes p(D|T) (and the prior on trees) or describe the approximation to posterior inference.
- Local score derivation: Show derivation of Eq. (12) from Eq. (11), cancellation assumptions, and whether greedy merges guarantee non-decreasing p(D|F).
- Absorb vs join: Provide intuition and formal justification for absorption merges and their relation to the tree prior πS.
- BHCD sparse: Offer a Bayesian interpretation or acknowledge it as a heuristic and quantify likelihood vs speed trade-offs.
- Hyperparameters: Report sensitivity to α, β, δ, λ, γ and consider empirical Bayes or sampling for hyperparameter treatment.
- Experimental details: Provide train/test splits, number of restarts and selection rules, exact IRM settings (iterations, burn-in), runtime/memory environment, and stability across tie-breaking.

Pros for acceptance
- Addresses an important problem with a principled, efficient solution.
- Clear technical insight enabling exact marginal likelihoods and fast learning.
- Empirically strong speedups with competitive accuracy.
- Produces interpretable hierarchical structures.

Cons against acceptance
- Ambiguity about the inference objective and Bayesian validity of the greedy procedure.
- Presentation and writing issues impede reproducibility and understanding.
- Insufficient sensitivity analyses and ablation studies; sparse variant status unclear.
- Limited and potentially unfair baseline comparisons and related-work discussion.

Overall recommendation
- Borderline accept leaning accept, contingent on clarifying the learning objective, strengthening theoretical ties to the generative model, improving exposition, and adding experimental details.
