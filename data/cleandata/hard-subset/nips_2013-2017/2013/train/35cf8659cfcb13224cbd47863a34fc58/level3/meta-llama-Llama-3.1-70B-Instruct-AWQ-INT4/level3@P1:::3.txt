The paper "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery" proposes an efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The model extends stochastic blockmodels and the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees, allowing for the discovery of hierarchical community structure. The authors provide a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm that scales quadratically in the number of vertices of the network, but independent of the number of nested communities.

The paper is well-written, with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations. However, there is a minor style issue with beginning sentences with citations, which is not in line with the NIPS style. The theory is succinct in parts, with consistent use of notation such as f() and g(). The authors extend stochastic block models and the single-table IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees, which is a novel contribution.

The posterior factorization over subtrees follows Koivisto & Sood (JMLR 2004), enabling greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, handling of sufficient statistics, and link prediction. The main algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative method, summarized in Table 1, which is efficient and scalable.

The evaluation uses AUC, but the method of computing it is unclear and needs explicit explanation. NMI is a more common metric for stochastic block model comparisons, and it would be beneficial to include it in the evaluation. The experiments are small-scale, with the full NIPS dataset being the largest, and should be expanded to include larger datasets used in recent stochastic block model evaluations.

The experimental work is illustrative rather than comparative, and thus inadequate. The authors should include comparisons to semi-parametric methods beyond Bayesian nonparametrics to provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their method. Additionally, the authors should consider including larger datasets and more extensive experiments to demonstrate the scalability and effectiveness of their method.
