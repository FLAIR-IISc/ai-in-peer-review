- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community detection.
- Extends the stochastic blockmodel into a tree-structured mixture to infer hierarchical community structure.
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm to learn a fully probabilistic hierarchical community model.
- Algorithm scales quadratically in the number of vertices, improving scalability.
- Demonstrates BHCD is faster and more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) on several datasets.
- Empirical evaluation includes datasets such as the NIPS co-authorship network.
- Provides a thorough literature review and clear connection to prior work on community detection and Bayesian nonparametrics.
- Writing quality needs improvement, with multiple spelling and grammar errors.
- Some sections (notably Section 4) require clearer explanations and more detailed examples.
- Figures and tables lack clarity and readability and should be improved.
- Paper should discuss limitations and potential future work more thoroughly.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommendation to accept with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses.
