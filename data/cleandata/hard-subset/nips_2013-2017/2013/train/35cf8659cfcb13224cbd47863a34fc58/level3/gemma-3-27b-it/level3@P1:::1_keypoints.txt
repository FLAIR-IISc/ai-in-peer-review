- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community structure in networks.  
- Extends the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) using a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels informed by Bayesian rose trees.  
- Introduces an efficient greedy agglomerative inference algorithm that requires a single pass through data.  
- Achieves substantially faster runtimes than IRM (≈100× faster on NIPS-234) while maintaining comparable or better accuracy.  
- Provides a fully probabilistic alternative to previous greedy hierarchical clustering methods.  
- Builds on prior work in stochastic blockmodels, mixed membership models, greedy clustering, and Bayesian nonparametrics.  
- Technical formulation and algorithm are sound and clearly presented.  
- Experimental validation on three datasets (Sampson’s monastery, NIPS-234, full NIPS) supports claims.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and sufficiently detailed for expert reproduction.  
- Originality in adapting Bayesian rose trees to relational data with a focus on computational efficiency.  
- Complexity improved to O(n^2 log n), a significant gain over existing Bayesian nonparametric methods.  
- Method is practically significant for scalable hierarchical community detection in social networks.  
- Limitation: quadratic complexity may still be prohibitive for very large networks (tens of thousands of nodes).  
- Additional empirical scalability evidence (e.g., timings on full NIPS and larger real-world networks) is needed.  
- Open question: quality of the greedy approximation and lack of theoretical guarantees (e.g., submodularity-based bounds).  
- Minor editorial fixes recommended (phrasing and clarity issues).  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept (strong endorsement given practical impact and technical contributions).
