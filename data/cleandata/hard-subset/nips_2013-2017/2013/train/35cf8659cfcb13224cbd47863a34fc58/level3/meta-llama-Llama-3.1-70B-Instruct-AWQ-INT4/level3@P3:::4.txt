The paper "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery" proposes a novel approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks using a Bayesian nonparametric model. The authors introduce a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, which allows for efficient inference of hierarchical community structure. The model is learned using a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm that scales quadratically in the number of vertices of the network, but independently of the number of nested communities.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear overview of the existing work on community discovery in social networks. The introduction effectively sets the stage for the proposed approach, highlighting the limitations of existing methods and the need for a more efficient and scalable solution.

The technical novelty of the paper lies in the adaptation of the likelihood and updating sufficient statistics efficiently for network data. The authors propose a dynamic programming approach to compute the marginal likelihood of the tree, which allows for efficient inference of the hierarchical community structure. The presentation of the technical details is generally clear, but some sections, such as Section 3, could benefit from more detailed explanations and examples.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing that it outperforms existing methods in terms of predictive performance and computational efficiency. The authors also provide a qualitative evaluation of the proposed approach on Sampson's monastery network, which highlights the ability of the model to capture hierarchical community structure.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. One major weakness is the lack of comparison to existing hierarchical network models, such as the multiscale community blockmodel and the Mondrian process. This makes it difficult to assess the relative strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach. Additionally, the use of the term "nonparametric" is questionable, as the model is framed as a model selection rather than a nonparametric approach.

The scalability claims of the paper are also not fully demonstrated, as the tested networks are not especially large. While the authors provide some insights into the computational complexity of the algorithm, more extensive experiments on larger networks would be necessary to fully evaluate the scalability of the approach.

Overall, the paper is technically sound, and the authors do a good job of integrating prior work well. However, the originality of the approach is limited, and the explanation and comparative evaluation could be strengthened.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks using a Bayesian nonparametric model.
* The authors provide a clear overview of the existing work on community discovery in social networks.
* The technical novelty of the paper lies in the adaptation of the likelihood and updating sufficient statistics efficiently for network data.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:

* The lack of comparison to existing hierarchical network models makes it difficult to assess the relative strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.
* The use of the term "nonparametric" is questionable, as the model is framed as a model selection rather than a nonparametric approach.
* The scalability claims of the paper are not fully demonstrated, as the tested networks are not especially large.

Recommendations for improvement:

* Provide more detailed explanations and examples in Section 3 to help readers understand the technical details.
* Compare the proposed approach to existing hierarchical network models to assess its relative strengths and weaknesses.
* Clarify the use of the term "nonparametric" and provide more justification for its use in the context of the proposed approach.
* Conduct more extensive experiments on larger networks to fully evaluate the scalability of the approach.

Acceptance:

Based on the strengths and weaknesses of the paper, I would recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper provides a novel approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. However, the lack of comparison to existing hierarchical network models and the questionable use of the term "nonparametric" are limitations that need to be addressed. With some revisions to address these limitations, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
