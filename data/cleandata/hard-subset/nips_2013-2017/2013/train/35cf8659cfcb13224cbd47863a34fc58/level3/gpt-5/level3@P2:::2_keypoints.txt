- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-constrained mixture of blockmodels with exact marginal likelihood per tree and a greedy agglomerative inference (join/absorb-left/absorb-right) scored by local Bayes factors; sparse variant prohibits merging disconnected subgraphs.  
- Claims scalability: likelihood O(n + m), greedy search O(n^2 log n) with caching, independent of number of communities; empirically faster than IRM with comparable predictive performance.

- Strength: Clear probabilistic formulation with conjugate priors and exact DP marginalization for a fixed tree.  
- Strength: Practical, well-engineered agglomerative search with caching and a sparse variant that improves speed/accuracy on sparse graphs.  
- Strength: Strong empirical speedups vs IRM and convincing qualitative hierarchies (NIPS co-authorship, Sampson’s network).  
- Strength: Complexity analysis and independence from number of communities are attractive.

- Weakness: Greedy procedure’s relationship to Bayesian inference is underspecified — appears to be MAP tree selection via local search, not full posterior inference; objective and guarantees need clarification.  
- Weakness: Merge score locality approximates true evidence change (ignores interactions with rest of forest); implications and pathological cases not discussed.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) are fixed with no sensitivity analysis; behavior on disassortative/bipartite graphs unclear.  
- Weakness: Comparisons limited mainly to IRM; need additional comparisons to other scalable methods (LFRM, ILA, hierarchical IRM variants, multiscale blockmodels).  
- Weakness: Evaluation details lacking (data preprocessing, multi-edge handling, train/test split, proportion of held-out edges, treatment of missing/negative ties).  
- Weakness: Multi-restart strategy (R=50) and tie-breaking may affect results; variance across restarts and topology frequency not reported.

- Clarity: Paper needs grammar/organization edits and cleaner notation.  
- Clarity: Section 4 should more explicitly connect merge score to the generative model, state the exact algorithm, data structures, and memory complexity.  
- Clarity: Improve figure legibility and captions; provide more readable NIPS tree in supplement and clearer cut criterion.  
- Clarity: Provide self-contained dataset descriptions (n, m, density) and splits; avoid unnecessary generality in notation.

- Originality: Novel combination of tree-constrained SBM mixtures and rose-tree-inspired greedy agglomeration; DP marginalization adaptation to relational blockmodels is useful.  
- Significance: Valuable scalable approach for hierarchical community discovery, likely useful as a baseline/component for medium-to-large graphs, but impact limited by focus on assortative structure and fixed hyperparameters.

- Suggested experiments: Clarify whether πS is fixed or learned; try optimizing/placing a prior on γ.  
- Suggested experiments: Ablations for merge types, sparse constraint, and effect of R restarts; report wall-clock times and hardware, and ensure fair IRM comparisons.  
- Suggested experiments: Synthetic experiments with known hierarchical ground truth to test recovery, noise sensitivity, degree heterogeneity, and disassortativity.  
- Extensions: Discuss directed/weighted graphs, overlapping communities, and handling non-conjugate likelihoods (approximations).  
- Reproducibility: Release code and data splits.

- Pros for acceptance: Practical, scalable algorithm with probabilistic basis; strong speedups and competitive predictive performance; interpretable hierarchies; builds on Bayesian hierarchical clustering literatures.  
- Cons against acceptance: Conceptual gap between Bayesian model and greedy inference; limited comparative and sensitivity evaluations; presentation and reproducibility need polishing.

- Overall recommendation: Weak accept conditional on clarifying Section 4, providing precise experimental details, and better explaining the merge score’s relation to Bayesian model selection.
