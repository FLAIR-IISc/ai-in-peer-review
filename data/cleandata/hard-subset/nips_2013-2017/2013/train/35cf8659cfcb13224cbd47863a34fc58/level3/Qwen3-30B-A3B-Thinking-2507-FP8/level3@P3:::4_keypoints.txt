- Proposes Bayesian hierarchical clustering for networks using tree-structured mixtures of stochastic blockmodels ("Bayesian rose trees") with an agglomerative clustering approach.  
- Main technical contribution: an O(n + m) likelihood computation via dynamic programming and recursive sufficient-statistic updates enabling scalable agglomerative inference.  
- Empirically, BHCD is reported to be ~100x faster than the IRM while maintaining comparable predictive accuracy, with qualitative success on Sampson’s monastery and speed gains on NIPS co-authorship data.  
- Strength: Model is well-motivated and technically sound; the dynamic-programming likelihood approach represents a meaningful efficiency improvement.  
- Strength: Paper is generally well-organized and the algorithm (Algorithm 1) is presented clearly.  
- Strength: Experiments demonstrate benefits over a flat IRM baseline and use multiple restarts/averaging for prediction.  
- Strength: The O(n + m) computational complexity claim is significant and supported by the algorithm description.  
- Weakness: Section 3 (esp. Eq. 9 and subsequent derivations) is dense and lacks sufficient detail, making the O(n + m) claim hard to verify.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to other hierarchical network models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012, Roy et al. 2009), so relative advantages are unclear.  
- Weakness: Misuses "nonparametric" terminology—method is greedy model selection, not a true nonparametric Bayesian approach.  
- Weakness: Scalability claims are unsubstantiated due to experiments on relatively small networks (e.g., NIPS-234).  
- Weakness: Limited originality—approach extends prior greedy agglomerative Bayesian clustering to relational data without a major conceptual leap.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound and well-executed but undermined by sparse technical exposition and terminology issues.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally clear except for dense mathematical derivations in Section 3 that need more explanation.  
- Originality assessment: Not highly original; novelty would be clearer with explicit comparisons to existing hierarchical network models.  
- Significance assessment: Efficiency improvements are useful, but limited comparative evaluation and small-scale experiments reduce overall impact.  
- Recommendation: Reject as written; authors should provide fuller technical derivations/pseudocode, stronger comparisons to hierarchical models, clearer terminology, and larger-scale experiments.
