## Review of “Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery”

This paper proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric model, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), for uncovering hierarchical community structure in networks. The core idea is to extend the stochastic blockmodel into a tree-structured mixture, enabling the inference of nested communities rather than flat clusters. A greedy, agglomerative clustering algorithm is introduced for efficient learning of this tree structure, circumventing the computational bottlenecks of existing methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). 

The authors correctly identify a significant challenge in Bayesian nonparametric network modeling – scalability – and offer a compelling solution by restricting the partition space to tree structures. This allows for efficient marginal likelihood computation, a key technical contribution. Empirical evaluation on two social network datasets demonstrates that BHCD achieves comparable or superior accuracy to the IRM, with substantial runtime improvements (orders of magnitude faster). This addresses a relevant problem for social scientists seeking to analyze complex network data. 

The strengths of the paper are clear: a novel and theoretically sound model, a practical and efficient algorithm, and strong empirical results. The speedup compared to IRM is particularly impressive. The model builds logically upon existing work in stochastic blockmodels and Bayesian nonparametric methods, appropriately referencing relevant literature.

However, the paper suffers from clarity issues. The connection between the greedy tree-learning procedure (Section 4) and the underlying Bayesian generative model remains somewhat opaque. It’s unclear whether the algorithm performs valid posterior inference or simply finds a MAP/ML estimate. A more detailed explanation of this aspect is crucial. Furthermore, the writing quality needs significant improvement. There are numerous spelling and grammatical errors, vague phrasing, and unclear descriptions of the datasets used. Some mathematical notation is unnecessarily complex, and the figures could be more informative. 

**Arguments for acceptance:** Novel approach to hierarchical community detection, strong empirical results demonstrating improved speed and comparable accuracy, addresses a relevant problem with practical implications.
**Arguments against acceptance:** Clarity of the connection between the algorithm and the Bayesian model is lacking, significant writing quality issues hinder understanding.

**Overall:** Despite the writing and clarity concerns, the core ideas and results are promising. If the authors address the conceptual confusion in Section 4 and significantly improve the presentation, this paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance contingent upon major revisions addressing these issues.
