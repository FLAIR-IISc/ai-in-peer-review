- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a tree-structured probabilistic model that marginalizes over all tree-consistent partitions for hierarchical community detection.  
- Uses a blockmodel likelihood with Beta-Bernoulli conjugacy to encourage dense within-community and sparse between-community edges.  
- Performs greedy agglomerative tree learning with three merge types (join and two absorptions) using local Bayes factor scores.  
- Introduces efficient caching of sufficient statistics yielding O(1) per local update and worst-case O(n^2 log n) overall; marginal likelihood p(D|T) claimed computable in O(n+m) by recursion.  
- Presents a sparse variant that forbids merging disconnected subtrees to respect graph sparsity.  
- Predictions are computed as a mixture along a single tree path using on/off-diagonal posteriors.  
- Positions BHCD relative to SBMs, IRM, and Bayesian rose trees, trading modeling richness for much faster one-pass agglomerative inference.  
- Technically sound probabilistic construction and sensible Bayes-factor merge score; dynamic program and caching appear justified.  
- Key derivations (σ¬chSS in eq. (9) and recursion in eq. (10)) are terse and need fuller exposition.  
- The O(n+m) likelihood and O(1) update complexity claims require more detailed proofs or explanations.  
- Experimental results show competitive link-prediction vs. IRM and qualitative recovery on Sampson’s data, but datasets are modest in scale.  
- Experimental comparisons omit existing hierarchical network models (HRG, multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian relational models).  
- Hyperparameters are fixed with no sensitivity analysis or empirical Bayes; robustness to α,β,δ,λ,γ is unreported.  
- Variability across restarts/tie-breaking (R=50) is not quantified; no variance bands or recurrence statistics reported.  
- Section 3 is dense with confusing notation swaps (S vs T) and under-explained blocks (DTT, σSS, σ¬chSS); worked examples/diagrams are needed.  
- Algorithm 1 lacks explicit pseudocode for cached quantities, update rules, memory complexity, and handling of directed/self-loop/missing edges.  
- Predictive recursion complexity is ambiguously stated (linear in depth vs Θ(n)); clarify as O(depth(T)).  
- Novelty is mainly engineering: adapting rose-tree model selection to relational blockmodel likelihood rather than introducing a fundamentally new paradigm.  
- Significance: fast, probabilistic hierarchical discovery with exact marginalization is valuable, but scalability evidence (large graphs, runtime scaling) is limited.  
- Suggested improvements: expand derivations, detail Algorithm 1 caching and memory, compare to hierarchical baselines, clarify “nonparametric” claims, add hyperparameter sensitivity and variability analysis, and strengthen scalability experiments.  
- Pros: clear probabilistic formulation, efficient inference, strong speedups, and intuitive hierarchies with sensible sparse variant.  
- Cons: limited originality versus rose trees, missing comparisons to key hierarchical models, dense/under-explained derivations hindering reproducibility, and limited empirical scalability and robustness analysis.  
- Overall: a solid, practically useful contribution that needs clearer derivations, broader comparisons, and stronger scalability/robustness evidence to maximize impact.
