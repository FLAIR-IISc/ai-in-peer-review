This paper proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric model, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The model is a tree-structured mixture of potentially exponentially many stochastic blockmodels, and it uses a greedy agglomerative procedure to learn a fully probabilistic, hierarchical community model.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key ideas, making it easy to follow. The authors provide a thorough review of previous work on community discovery, highlighting the limitations of existing approaches and motivating the need for a new method. The technical contributions of the paper are significant, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the model and the inference procedure.

The BHCD model is an extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM), but it scales much better, with a worst-case time complexity of O(n^2 log n), where n is the number of vertices. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of BHCD on several datasets, including Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship network. The results show that BHCD achieves comparable or better accuracy than the IRM, but with a significant reduction in computational time.
