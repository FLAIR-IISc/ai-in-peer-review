- Proposes a Bayesian nonparametric method extending the IRM to tree-structured hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces a hierarchical mixture of stochastic block models with partitions restricted to tree-consistent ones.  
- Enables exact marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming over tree-consistent partitions.  
- Develops a greedy agglomerative learning algorithm running in O(n² log n), far faster than IRM's exponential search.  
- Empirical results on two social networks show ~100× speedups with comparable or better AUC, log-likelihood, and accuracy.  
- Theoretical analysis of the marginal likelihood and algorithmic complexity is rigorous and well-motivated.  
- Novel contribution: restricting partition space to trees retains Bayesian flexibility while enabling efficient computation.  
- Significant for social scientists who need hierarchical network structure and for scaling hierarchical community discovery.  
- Writing quality needs major improvements: grammar, spelling, vague phrasing, and inconsistent mathematical notation.  
- Dataset descriptions and some figures (e.g., Figure 3, Sampson's network) are unclear or poorly labeled.  
- Major conceptual confusion in Section 4: unclear whether the greedy algorithm performs Bayesian posterior inference, computes marginal likelihood/MAP, or is merely heuristic.  
- Authors must clarify the relationship between the generative model (Section 3) and the agglomerative algorithm (Section 4), and state precisely what the algorithm optimizes.  
- Recommendation: strong technical and empirical contribution but requires substantial clarification and polishing (especially Section 4 and presentation) before publication.
