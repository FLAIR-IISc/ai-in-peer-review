- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel Bayesian nonparametric method for hierarchical community detection.
- Proposes a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels and a greedy agglomerative learning algorithm to reduce computational cost.
- Restricting the partition space to tree structures is a clever core insight that is effective in practice.
- Empirical results on two social network corpora show comparable or better accuracy (AUC, predictive log-likelihood) than IRM while being orders of magnitude faster.
- Offers a scalable solution addressing a clear limitation of previous Bayesian methods for large networks.
- Connection to prior Bayesian hierarchical clustering work ([13, 14]) is well-established.
- Crucial conceptual weakness: unclear relationship between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model—uncertain whether it performs valid posterior inference or only MAP/ML estimation.
- Needs a thorough discussion of assumptions made during the greedy search and clarification of the inference interpretation (posterior vs MAP/ML).
- Writing quality is poor: spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, and unclear dataset descriptions reduce clarity and impact.
- Mathematical notation is sometimes unnecessarily obscure and figures could better convey key information.
- Experimental descriptions lack sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of the tree-structured prior, strong empirical performance, significant speed improvements, and relevance to social network analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: conceptual ambiguity about inference and poor writing quality hindering clarity and reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: technically significant and promising, but should be accepted only after major revisions that clarify the inference procedure and substantially improve presentation.
