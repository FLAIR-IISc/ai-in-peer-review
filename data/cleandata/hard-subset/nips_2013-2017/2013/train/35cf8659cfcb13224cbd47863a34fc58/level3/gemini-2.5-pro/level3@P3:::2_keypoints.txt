- Paper introduces BHCD: a tree-structured prior extension of stochastic blockmodels and a DP to compute marginal likelihood by summing partitions consistent with a tree.  
- Proposes a greedy bottom-up agglomerative algorithm to search tree space for high-likelihood hierarchies.  
- Aims to keep Bayesian grounding while delivering large computational speedups over MCMC-based IRM.  
- Strength: addresses important problems — costly inference in Bayesian nonparametrics and discovery of hierarchical communities.  
- Strength: technical novelty — restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones makes marginal likelihood tractable via a dynamic program.  
- Strength: strong empirical results — orders-of-magnitude speedups vs IRM with comparable or better predictive performance and interpretable qualitative findings.  
- Weakness: unclear connection between the probabilistic model (p(D|T)) and the greedy agglomerative search; optimization objective not specified.  
- Question: is the algorithm seeking a MAP tree, and how does the local Bayes factor (Eq.12) relate to the global p(D|T)?  
- Question: is the greedy procedure valid posterior inference or a heuristic inspired by the Bayesian model?  
- Weakness: writing and presentation problems — grammar/spelling issues and vague phrasing reduce clarity.  
- Weakness: dataset description (Sampson monastery) lacks detail on how positive/negative/unknown affiliations map to edges and missing data.  
- Weakness: key mathematical steps (e.g., transition from Eq.7/8 to Eq.10) need clearer, more intuitive derivations.  
- Weakness: figures are cluttered or under-captioned (Fig.3 hard to parse; Fig.5 node labels unexplained).  
- Overall: novel, practical, and impactful idea with strong experiments but held back by conceptual ambiguity about inference and numerous presentation flaws.  
- Recommendation: borderline, leaning toward acceptance conditional on major revisions clarifying the inference objective and fixing presentation/reproducibility issues.
