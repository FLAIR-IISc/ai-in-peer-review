- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), using Bayesian Rose trees to model hierarchical community structure in networks.
- Extends SBM/IRM by replacing the CRP prior with a tree-based prior (eq. 7) and leveraging posterior factorization over subtrees.
- Main theoretical contribution: posterior factorization (following Koivisto & Sood 2004) enabling efficient marginal likelihood computation and sufficient-statistic handling.
- Main algorithmic contribution: greedy agglomerative inference (Algorithm 1) with complexity O(n² log n) and independence from number of nested communities.
- Demonstrates two orders of magnitude speedup over IRM with comparable accuracy in reported experiments.
- Clear, well-written manuscript with strong connections to prior NIPS work and consistent theoretical exposition (use of f() and g()).
- Strength: efficient inference algorithm and proper handling of hierarchical structure via tree-based prior.
- Strength: well-organized presentation and solid theoretical grounding.
- Weakness: evaluation methodology inadequate—AUC used for link prediction without explaining computation procedure.
- Weakness: lacks standard community-detection metric NMI; NMI scores should be reported.
- Weakness: experiments limited to small datasets (Sampson, small NIPS subset), not demonstrating scalability to large real-world networks.
- Weakness: insufficient comparative experiments—only compared to IRM; missing comparisons to semi-parametric (LFRM, ILA) and other hierarchical methods.
- Weakness: claims of "comparable or better accuracy" lack statistical significance testing.
- Significance is limited by experimental shortcomings despite solid theoretical and algorithmic contributions.
- Recommended improvements: explain AUC computation, include NMI, run experiments on larger datasets, and compare to broader baselines (including non-Bayesian hierarchical methods).
- Pro-acceptance: novel algorithmic approach, strong theory, large speedups, clear presentation, and proper hierarchical modeling.
- Con-acceptance: inadequate evaluation methodology, small-scale experiments, insufficient baseline comparisons, and illustrative (not conclusive) results.
- Overall recommendation: reject due to insufficient experimental validation, though the core idea is promising and should be strengthened with additional experiments.
