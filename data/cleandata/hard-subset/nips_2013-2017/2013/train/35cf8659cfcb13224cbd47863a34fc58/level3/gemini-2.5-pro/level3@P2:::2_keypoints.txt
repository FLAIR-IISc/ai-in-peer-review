- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with an efficient DP for marginal likelihood.  
- Limits partitions to tree-consistent ones, enabling exact and efficient marginal-likelihood computation (core technical contribution).  
- Uses a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm that merges subtrees by largest Bayes-factor increase, starting from singleton nodes.  
- Empirical results: discovers meaningful hierarchies on two social networks, matches or exceeds IRM predictive accuracy, and is orders of magnitude faster.  
- Derivation of marginal likelihood (Eq. 10) is technically sound and is the foundation for the method’s efficiency.  
- Agglomerative algorithm is well-defined and experiments compare against a relevant baseline (IRM).  
- Major conceptual weakness: unclear relationship between the probabilistic generative model (Section 3) and the greedy inference (Section 4)—is the algorithm MAP, an approximation to the posterior, or something else?  
- Unclear whether the Bayes factor score (Eq. 12) validly proxies the global posterior probability of the forest; theoretical grounding needs clarification.  
- Presentation and clarity issues: numerous spelling/grammar errors reduce readability.  
- Experimental descriptions are sometimes vague (e.g., treatment of negative vs. unknown affiliations in Sampson’s Monastery).  
- Some math and derivations lack intuition—need clearer explanation of the partition prior (Eq. 7) and its link to the recursive likelihood (Eq. 10).  
- Figures lack informative captions and context (e.g., why one tree is preferred despite another having better log-likelihood; more context for discovered communities needed).  
- Originality: novel and practical combination of hierarchical Bayesian clustering ideas with relational models; tree-consistency constraint is a significant contribution.  
- Significance: scalable, fast method that could enable Bayesian hierarchical analysis on much larger networks and is likely to be influential.  
- Recommendations for acceptance conditional on revisions: (1) clarify theoretical justification of the greedy inference, (2) thoroughly proofread and improve writing, and (3) improve experimental descriptions and figure captions.
