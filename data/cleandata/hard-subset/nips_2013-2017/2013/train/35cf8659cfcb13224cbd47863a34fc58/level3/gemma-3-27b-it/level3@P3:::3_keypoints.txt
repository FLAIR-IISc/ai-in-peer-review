- Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community detection in social networks.
- Extends SBMs and the IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees for hierarchical structure.
- Core contribution is a greedy agglomerative algorithm for learning hierarchies (Table 1).
- Claims computational efficiency and scalability compared to existing hierarchical Bayesian methods.
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical presentation and correct citations, though some sentences begin with citations contrary to NIPS style.
- Uses consistent notation (f() and g()) for marginal likelihoods and clearly connects to prior BN methods (IRM, Mondrian).
- Replaces the CRP IRM formulation with a simpler expression (eq. 7), enabling efficient computation and handling of sufficient statistics.
- Posterior factorization (Koivisto & Sood) supports efficient greedy search and marginal likelihood calculation.
- Algorithm 1 appears well-designed and claims O(n^2 log n) time complexity, a significant advantage.
- Evaluation methodology is weak: AUC computation is unclear and not reproducible, and NMI (a standard metric) is not reported.
- Experiments are limited in scale (subset of NIPS, then full NIPS) and lack larger, commonly used SBM benchmarks.
- Comparative analysis is incomplete: shows BHCD faster than IRM but lacks comparisons to other semi-parametric and non-Bayesian methods (e.g., spectral clustering, label propagation).
- Ablation on “sparse” variants is useful but insufficient to offset limited comparative evaluation.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and potentially impactful, but evaluation and comparisons are inadequate; reviewer recommends rejection with encouragement to resubmit after addressing specific weaknesses.
- Requested revisions: clarify AUC computation, evaluate on larger benchmarks, compare to a broader range of algorithms, and expand experimental robustness and generalizability analysis.
