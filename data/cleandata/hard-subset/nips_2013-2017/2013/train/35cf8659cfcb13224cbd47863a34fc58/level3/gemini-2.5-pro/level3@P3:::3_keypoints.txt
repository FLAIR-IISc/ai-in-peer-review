- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a nonparametric Bayesian model using Bayesian Rose trees to find hierarchical community structure.  
- Extends stochastic blockmodels and the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) by defining a tree-consistent prior over partitions.  
- Enables exact and efficient computation of marginal likelihoods via dynamic programming and subtree factorization.  
- Core algorithm is a greedy, bottom-up agglomerative clustering procedure that constructs the hierarchy in a single pass.  
- Empirically much faster than MCMC-based IRM inference (claims ~100x speedup) while achieving comparable predictive performance on a small co-authorship network.  
- Paper writing is clear and well-structured, with elegant theoretical presentation and useful generalization via functions f() and g().  
- Technically original and sound: replacing CRP with a tree-consistent prior is a key insight enabling efficient recursion and greedy search.  
- Algorithmic contribution (Algorithm 1) is well-justified and offers O(n^2 log n) complexity independent of the number of communities.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation is illustrative rather than conclusive, limiting evidence for practical utility.  
- Comparisons are too narrow (only against IRM); should include scalable SBM variants, spectral hierarchical methods, and non-probabilistic baselines like Louvain.  
- Scale concerns: experiments use small datasets (Sampson, NIPS co-authorship); larger benchmark networks are needed to validate scalability claims.  
- Evaluation clarity: AUC computation procedure is not specified (sampling of test links/non-links), and community metrics like NMI should be reported for comparability.  
- Minor stylistic issue: several sentences start with citations, violating NIPS style and hurting readability.  
- Overall: well-motivated, elegant, and efficient method, but needs more comprehensive experiments and broader baselines to demonstrate practical significance.
