- Proposes BHCD, a novel Bayesian nonparametric model extending SBMs with Bayesian Rose trees to simplify IRM's CRP formulation.  
- Uses subtree factoring from Bayesian network learning to enable efficient computations like greedy search and link prediction.  
- Claims comparable or superior accuracy to IRM while being two orders of magnitude faster.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations, consistent notation, and comprehensive citations (avoid starting sentences with citations).  
- Theoretical contribution is novel and well-motivated: Rose tree extension and dynamic programming for marginal likelihoods.  
- Greedy agglomerative algorithm scales as O(n^2 log n), with a sparse variant for sparse networks.  
- Experiments on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship data show meaningful hierarchical structures and efficiency.  
- Evaluation uses AUC instead of the more common NMI for SBMs and lacks clear justification.  
- Datasets used are relatively small, limiting generalizability; larger datasets are needed.  
- Experimental work is illustrative rather than a rigorous comparative study beyond IRM.  
- The model’s relative simplicity may limit applicability to more complex network structures.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient method with clear theory and practical potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evaluation depth, questionable metric choice, and limited novelty/impact relative to more sophisticated models.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing AUC justification, expanding experiments, and including larger benchmarking datasets.
