- Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model extending the stochastic blockmodel to infer hierarchical communities.  
- Introduces an agglomerative-clustering-based learning algorithm that selects a tree of valid partitions.  
- Restricting partitions to tree-structured ones enables efficient marginal likelihood computation versus IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social connectivity corpora shows BHCD is faster and slightly more accurate than IRM (better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, accuracy).  
- Results demonstrate orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements.  
- Writing quality is weak: contains spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, and obscure/unnecessary math.  
- Figures are confusing and need clearer presentation.  
- Dataset descriptions are unclear and require more detail.  
- The relationship between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model is unclear (uncertain if it does valid posterior inference or finds a MAP/ML estimate).  
- Section 4 needs clearer explanation to resolve conceptual confusion about inference.  
- The hierarchical extension and the agglomerative learning algorithm are novel and significant contributions.  
- Recommendation: publishable with revisions—improve writing, figures, dataset descriptions, and clarify the learning/inference connection.  
- Ratings: Quality 7/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10, Clarity/Presentation 5/10.
