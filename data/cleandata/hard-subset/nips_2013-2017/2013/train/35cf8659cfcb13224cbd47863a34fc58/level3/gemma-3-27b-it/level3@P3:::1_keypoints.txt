- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels extending Bayesian Rose Trees to relational data, learned via a greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Achieves substantial computational efficiency (reported ~100× faster than a single IRM iteration) with stated O(n^2 log n) complexity.  
- Uses dynamic programming for likelihood computation as a key optimization.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, accessible math, and thorough experimental setup.  
- Situates work well in literature (IRM, SBMs, BRTs) and clearly explains nontrivial adaptations for relational data.  
- Technically sound: solid theoretical foundation combining SBMs and Bayesian hierarchical clustering.  
- Original contribution: novel application of BRT ideas to hierarchical community discovery and a greedy agglomerative algorithm.  
- Significant impact: addresses hierarchical community detection with competitive predictive performance and much-improved runtime, enabling larger-scale application potential.  
- Scalability concern: O(n^2 log n) claim and NIPS-234 timings leave open questions about performance on networks with tens of thousands of nodes.  
- Greedy algorithm optimality concern: no theoretical guarantees or empirical evaluation (e.g., exhaustive search on small instances or submodularity analysis) to bound solution quality.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks systematic analysis of impact of α, β, δ, λ, γ on results.  
- Requested actions: provide timings on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS) and more detailed discussion/experiments on scalability and greedy optimality.  
- Minor editorial corrections suggested for wording ("whilst… but" → "while… and"; "pair of trees" → "two trees"; "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step").  
- Overall verdict: despite open questions on scalability and greedy optimality, strongly recommend acceptance.
