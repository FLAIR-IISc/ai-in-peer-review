- Introduces BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Proposes an efficient greedy agglomerative algorithm to infer hierarchy, targeting computational limits of IRM.  
- Strong theoretical grounding, connecting to prior work (e.g., Koivisto & Sood) and posterior factorization.  
- Mathematical notation is concise and consistently applied, aiding clarity of model components.  
- Algorithmic contribution notable: caching scheme and reported complexity O(n^2 log n).  
- Claims substantial speed/scalability improvements over IRM and other Bayesian nonparametric methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and feels illustrative rather than rigorously comparative.  
- Only small datasets used (Sampson’s monastery, NIPS subset); larger standard SBM benchmarks are missing.  
- AUC evaluation methodology is unclear (uncertain how links were removed for testing).  
- Missing standard metrics like NMI; including them would strengthen evaluation.  
- Lacks comparisons to a broader range of semi-parametric and alternative community-detection methods.  
- Originality lies in combining established SBMs and tree-structured models with a practical inference procedure.  
- Significance: provides a practical, potentially faster method for hierarchical community detection with predictive capability.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on substantially improving experiments—clarify AUC, add NMI and larger benchmarks, expand comparisons, and discuss limitations.
