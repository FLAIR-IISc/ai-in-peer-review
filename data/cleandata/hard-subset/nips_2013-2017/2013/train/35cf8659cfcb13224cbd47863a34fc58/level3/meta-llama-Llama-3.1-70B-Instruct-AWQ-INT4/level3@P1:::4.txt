This paper proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks, called Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD). The model is a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, which allows for efficient inference and scalability to large networks. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on several datasets, including Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship network.

The main technical novelty of this paper is the adaptation of the likelihood and updating of sufficient statistics for network data, which enables scalable agglomerative clustering. The authors claim that their likelihood computation is O(nodes + edges), making it efficient for large networks. However, the explanation of the dynamic programming and recursive sufficient-statistic updates in Section 3 is dense and hard to follow, and more detail would be helpful.

The presentation of the paper is generally clear, but some sections, such as Section 3, could benefit from more explanation and examples. The experimental results show benefits over a flat IRM baseline and qualitative success on Sampson's monastery network, and averaging over hypotheses helps prediction. However, the experiments lack comparisons to existing hierarchical network models, such as the multiscale community blockmodel and the Mondrian process, which is a major weakness.

The use of the term "nonparametric" in the paper is also questionable, as the authors frame their approach as model selection rather than nonparametric. Additionally, while the scalability claims are discussed, the tested networks are not especially large, so empirical scalability is not fully demonstrated.

Overall, the paper is technically sound and integrates prior work well, but the originality is limited, and the explanation and comparative evaluation need strengthening.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the authors demonstrate a good understanding of the underlying concepts and techniques. However, the explanation of some sections, such as Section 3, could be improved. The experimental results are also well-presented, but the lack of comparisons to existing hierarchical network models is a major weakness.

**Clarity:** The presentation of the paper is generally clear, but some sections, such as Section 3, could benefit from more explanation and examples. The notation and terminology are well-defined, but some readers may find the paper challenging to follow due to the technical nature of the subject.

**Originality:** The originality of the paper is limited, as the authors build upon existing work on Bayesian nonparametric models and stochastic blockmodels. However, the adaptation of the likelihood and updating of sufficient statistics for network data is a novel contribution.

**Significance:** The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of network analysis and community detection. The proposed model and algorithm have the potential to be widely applicable in various domains, such as social network analysis, recommendation systems, and link prediction.
