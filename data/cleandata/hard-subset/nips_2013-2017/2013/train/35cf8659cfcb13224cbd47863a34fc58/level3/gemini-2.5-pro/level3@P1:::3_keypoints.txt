- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community detection.  
- Extends Bayesian Rose Trees to relational data, replacing IRM's CRP prior with a tree-structured prior that restricts partitions to hierarchical ones.  
- Derives an exact marginal likelihood for trees via dynamic programming using subtree factorization.  
- Introduces a greedy bottom-up agglomerative algorithm (single pass) with worst-case O(n² log n) time complexity.  
- Demonstrates major runtime improvements over MCMC-based IRM, achieving comparable link-prediction performance on tested datasets.  
- Strength: paper is very clear and well-written, with effective notation for on-/off-diagonal block likelihoods.  
- Strength: novel and well-motivated technical contribution linking Bayesian Rose Trees and relational SBMs.  
- Strength: exact tractability of marginal likelihood and the fast greedy search are significant algorithmic advances.  
- Strength: efficiency and scalability claim supported by empirical two-order-of-magnitude speed-up on NIPS co-authorship data.  
- Weakness: link-prediction metrics are used but evaluation lacks explicit methodology (e.g., held-out link procedure) and metrics for partition/hierarchy quality (e.g., NMI).  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons are limited—primary baseline is IRM; should include other scalable SBMs, fast hierarchical clustering (e.g., Clauset et al.), and varied parametric/semi-parametric methods.  
- Weakness: datasets are small; scalability claims need validation on much larger benchmark networks (tens of thousands of nodes).  
- Minor stylistic suggestion: avoid starting sentences with citations to conform to NIPS style.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and algorithmic results, but experimental evaluation needs broader, clearer, and larger-scale validation.
