- Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method extending the Infinite Relational Model with tree-structured partitioning.  
- Uses a Bayesian nonparametric model restricting partitions to tree-consistent structures, enabling dynamic-programming marginal likelihood computation.  
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative algorithm to build the hierarchical tree, claiming major speedups over IRM.  
- Empirical results show comparable or better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy than IRM, with runtime improvements of roughly two orders of magnitude.  
- Key technical insight: restricting to tree partitions avoids IRM's exponential partition space and enables efficient inference.  
- Problem is relevant to social scientists and meaningfully extends Bayesian nonparametric community detection.  
- Major weakness: conceptual confusion in Section 4—unclear whether the greedy algorithm does valid Bayesian posterior inference or only MAP/ML estimation.  
- Authors must clarify how the agglomerative procedure relates to the Bayesian generative model.  
- Presentation issues: poor writing, spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, and confusing figures detract from contributions.  
- Dataset descriptions are unclear; mathematical notation is sometimes unnecessary and muddles exposition.  
- Overall: high technical merit and practical value, but requires conceptual clarification and substantial writing/revision before publication.
