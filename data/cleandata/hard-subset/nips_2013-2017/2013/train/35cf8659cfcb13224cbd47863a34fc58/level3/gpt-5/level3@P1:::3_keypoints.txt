- Proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model using a tree prior and a mixture over tree-consistent SBMs to discover hierarchical community structure.
- Integrates out Bernoulli-Beta block parameters with different within-/between-community hyperparameters and factorizes the partition prior along the tree via stopping probabilities (Eq. 7).
- Computes exact marginal likelihood for a given tree by dynamic programming (Eq. 10).
- Uses a greedy bottom-up agglomerative inference with three merge types (join, absorb-left, absorb-right) scored by local Bayes factors (Eq. 12).
- Caches sufficient statistics for O(1) inner-loop updates and claims overall O(n^2 log n) runtime; offers a sparse variant that forbids merging disconnected subtrees.
- Predictive link probabilities are computed by a recursive mixture along root-to-leaf paths (Eq. 14).
- Empirical results show interpretable hierarchies and competitive or better link prediction than IRM on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship data, with much faster runtimes.
- Clear probabilistic model, conjugate integration, and consistent notation; DP recursion and scoring are conceptually sound.
- Complexity claims are plausible given the caching scheme and update formulas (Eq. 13); assumptions are explicit.
- Experimental evaluation is illustrative but limited in scale and omits strong semi-/parametric baselines like degree-corrected SBM.
- Reproducibility is mostly supported by provided algorithmic details, but the AUC/link-prediction protocol is under-specified.
- Paper is well written, organized, and clearly relates to SBM/IRM and Bayesian Rose Trees; minor stylistic notes only.
- Conceptually original in synthesizing tree-constrained SBM mixtures with exact DP evaluation and a pragmatic agglomerative search.
- Practical significance lies in a scalable, simple method producing reasonable hierarchies and fast, competitive link prediction.
- Strengths: exact marginal likelihood via DP, efficient agglomerative algorithm, strong runtime advantage, interpretable results, and good exposition.
- Weaknesses: limited datasets and baselines, under-specified predictive protocol, greedy search may be myopic, no uncertainty quantification or hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and worst-case search scaling remains O(n^2 log n).
- Suggested improvements: fully specify link-prediction protocol, add degree-corrected SBM and spectral/modularity baselines, report NMI/ARI on labeled benchmarks, evaluate larger datasets and scaling/memory, provide ablations and hyperparameter sensitivity, and release code.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled model with exact tree marginal likelihood, fast practical inference, competitive performance, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical scope and baselines, under-specified methodological choices, and potential brittleness of greedy search without uncertainty or sensitivity analyses.
- Overall judgment: promising, well-presented contribution with practical value that would benefit from strengthened empirical validation and clearer evaluation protocols.
