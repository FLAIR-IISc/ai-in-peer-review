- Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model that discovers hierarchical community structure by extending SBMs and the IRM with Bayesian Rose trees.  
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithms that learn a fully probabilistic hierarchy in one pass.  
- Learning algorithm is efficient with time complexity O(n^2 log n).  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation, correct citations, and generally clear theory.  
- Model is a significant contribution that addresses limitations of prior methods in handling hierarchical community structure.  
- Authors provide a clear and concise description of the model and its components.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness at discovering hierarchies and predicting unobserved connectivity.  
- AUC is used for evaluation but the method of computing AUC is unclear and needs explicit explanation.  
- Experiments are small-scale; larger datasets used in recent SBM work should be included.  
- Experimental comparisons are illustrative rather than thorough; should compare to semi-parametric methods beyond Bayesian nonparametrics.  
- Paper lacks discussion of model limitations and future work (e.g., non-conjugate likelihoods, hyperparameter inference).  
- Technically sound, but clarity could be improved with more detailed explanations of model components and the algorithm.  
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques offering a meaningful new approach.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and appears scalable to large dense networks.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address evaluation clarity, larger/ comparative experiments, and discussion of limitations.
