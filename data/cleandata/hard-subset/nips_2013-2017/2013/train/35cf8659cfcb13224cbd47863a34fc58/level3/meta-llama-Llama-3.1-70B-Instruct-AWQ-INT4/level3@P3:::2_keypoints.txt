- Proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric model: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm that merges trees using a Bayes factor score.  
- Derives a recursive marginal-likelihood formula computable efficiently via dynamic programming.  
- Algorithm scales quadratically in the number of vertices and is independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Experiments on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship subsets/full show BHCD finds reasonable hierarchies and is ~100× faster than IRM with comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper gives a clear, concise introduction and a useful review of prior models (SBM, MMSBM, IRM and extensions).  
- Strengths: novel model/algorithm, relevant problem, scalable inference, and convincing empirical results.  
- Weaknesses: writing quality suffers from spelling/grammar errors and unclear mathematical derivations.  
- Weaknesses: needs more explanations, illustrative examples, and a more detailed comparison with existing methods (e.g., IRM variants).  
- Originality: idea of tree-structured mixtures is not wholly new, but the specific model and greedy selection procedure are original contributions.  
- Significance: addresses an important social-network problem and produces a practically useful, scalable algorithm.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to improve writing, clarify derivations, add examples, and strengthen comparisons.
