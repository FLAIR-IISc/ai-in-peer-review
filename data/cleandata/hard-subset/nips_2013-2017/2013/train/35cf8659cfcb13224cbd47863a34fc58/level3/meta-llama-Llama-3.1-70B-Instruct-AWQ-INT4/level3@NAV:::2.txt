The paper "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery" proposes a novel approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks using a Bayesian nonparametric model. The authors extend the stochastic blockmodel to a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, allowing for the inference of hierarchical community structure. They also introduce a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm to learn a fully probabilistic, hierarchical community model.

The main ideas of the paper are well-motivated and clearly related to previous work on community detection and Bayesian nonparametric methods. The authors provide a thorough review of existing methods, highlighting their limitations and the need for a more efficient and scalable approach. The proposed method, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), is shown to be faster and more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) on several datasets.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The proposal of a novel and efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community detection.
* The introduction of a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm that scales quadratically in the number of vertices.
* The demonstration of the method's performance on several datasets, including the NIPS co-authorship network.
* The provision of a thorough review of existing methods and a clear explanation of the proposed approach.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The writing quality needs improvement, with several spelling and grammar errors throughout the paper.
* Some sections, such as Section 4, could benefit from clearer explanations and more detailed examples.
* The figures and tables could be improved for better clarity and readability.
* The authors could provide more discussion on the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future work.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of community detection and Bayesian nonparametric methods. With some revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above, the paper has the potential to be a strong contribution to the conference.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient approach to hierarchical community detection.
* The method is demonstrated to be faster and more accurate than existing methods on several datasets.
* The paper provides a thorough review of existing methods and a clear explanation of the proposed approach.

Arguments for rejection:

* The writing quality needs improvement.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
