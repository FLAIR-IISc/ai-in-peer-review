- Proposes BHCD: a novel Bayesian nonparametric method combining Bayesian Rose trees with SBM/IRM for hierarchical community discovery.
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm that replaces the CRP-based IRM inference with a simpler, single-pass formulation (eq. 7).
- Main theoretical contribution: posterior factorization over subtrees (following Koivisto & Sood) enabling greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, and handling of sufficient statistics.
- Algorithmic complexity: worst-case O(n² log n), independent of number of nested communities, a significant improvement over IRM's O(n²K²).
- Novel adaptation of Bayesian hierarchical clustering (Heller & Ghahramani) to relational data with a caching scheme for efficiency.
- Clear, well-written paper with correct citations and consistent use of f() and g() for block likelihoods.
- Algorithmic description well-structured (Table 1) and sufficient details for expert reproduction.
- Empirical evaluation limited: experiments only on NIPS subsets (234 and 2864 nodes), lacking larger datasets used in recent SBM work.
- Evaluation metrics unclear: AUC computation method not explained; should include standard SBM metrics like NMI.
- Experimental comparisons are illustrative rather than competitive; lacks comparison to semi-parametric and recent SBM methods.
- Significance: offers substantial computational speedups (reported two orders of magnitude faster than IRM) but impact reduced by limited experiments.
- Quality: technically sound with complete theoretical analysis and supported claims.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and accessible; algorithm explained clearly.
- Originality: novel combination of Bayesian Rose trees with hierarchical community detection and a new posterior factorization approach.
- Minor style issue: sentences beginning with citations (NIPS style) should be avoided.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments (larger datasets, NMI), clarify AUC computation, and add comparisons to recent methods.
