## Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper presents a novel and efficient Bayesian nonparametric model, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), for uncovering hierarchical community structure in networks. The approach extends Bayesian rose trees to relational data, leveraging a greedy agglomerative procedure to learn a probabilistic hierarchy of stochastic blockmodels. The core contribution lies in achieving computational efficiency – inference is significantly faster than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while maintaining comparable predictive performance. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex ideas. The motivation is well-established, clearly situating the work within the existing literature on community detection, particularly Bayesian approaches like the IRM and earlier attempts at hierarchical extensions. The authors thoroughly detail their model, the efficient inference algorithm, and demonstrate its effectiveness through qualitative results on Sampson's monks dataset and quantitative results on the NIPS-234 co-authorship network, alongside a more extensive analysis on the full NIPS dataset. 

A significant strength is the computational improvement over existing Bayesian nonparametric methods. The O(n^2 log n) complexity, while still quadratic, represents a substantial advance. The presented timings on NIPS-234 convincingly demonstrate the speedup relative to the IRM. However, the reviewer correctly points to the potential scalability limitations for very large networks (tens of thousands of nodes). The authors acknowledge this and suggest sparse variants as a potential solution. It would be valuable to see timings for the full NIPS dataset to further illustrate the practical performance at a larger scale and how it compares to NIPS-234.

Further strengthening the work would involve a more thorough investigation into the quality of the greedy agglomerative procedure. Evaluating how closely the greedy method approximates the optimal tree structure (perhaps through exhaustive search on smaller subsets) and seeking theoretical justification for its performance (e.g., exploring submodularity properties) could bolster confidence in the results. 

Minor corrections as noted by the reviewer (wording issues, "pair of trees," "A fruitful next steps") should be addressed. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Significant contribution in scaling Bayesian nonparametric network models.
*   Clear and well-written exposition of the method.
*   Convincing empirical results demonstrating speed and accuracy.
*   Addresses a challenging problem with a novel and effective approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:** 

*   Potential scalability limitations for very large networks.
*   Lack of theoretical guarantees regarding the quality of the greedy approximation.


**Overall:** Despite my familiarity with the underlying Bayesian rose tree ideas, I agree with the reviewer’s assessment: this is a significant contribution and the paper is well-deserving of acceptance to NIPS. The authors have developed a practical and efficient method for hierarchical community detection that advances the state of the art.
