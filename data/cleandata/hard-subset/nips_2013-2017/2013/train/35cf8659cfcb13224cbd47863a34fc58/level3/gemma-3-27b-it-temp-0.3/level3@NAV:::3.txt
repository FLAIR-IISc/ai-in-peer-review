## Review of “Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery”

This paper presents a novel Bayesian nonparametric model, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), for uncovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The core idea is to extend stochastic block models (SBM) using Bayesian Rose trees, offering a computationally efficient alternative to existing methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). The authors replace the CRP formulation of IRM with a simpler formulation and leverage posterior factorization for efficient inference via a greedy agglomerative algorithm. 

The paper is well-written and technically sound, demonstrating a clear understanding of relevant literature in Bayesian nonparametrics and community detection. The theoretical foundation is succinct, with consistent notation (f() and g()) aiding readability. The proposed method builds logically upon previous work, particularly Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering, and offers a compelling trade-off between model complexity and computational efficiency. The algorithm, summarized in Table 1, is a significant contribution, promising faster inference compared to existing Bayesian methods. 

However, the experimental evaluation is a key weakness. While the authors demonstrate the method on Sampson’s monastery network and a subset of the NIPS co-authorship dataset, the evaluation relies heavily on AUC, and the computation method is not clearly explained. NMI would be a more standard metric for SBM comparisons. More importantly, the experiments are small-scale and lack comparison to a broader range of semi-parametric methods beyond Bayesian nonparametrics. The claim of being “two orders of magnitude faster than the IRM” is impactful but needs to be substantiated with comparisons on larger, more challenging datasets commonly used in SBM evaluations. The experimental work feels illustrative rather than comparative, hindering a thorough assessment of the method’s performance.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel approach combining Bayesian Rose trees with SBMs.
*   Efficient greedy agglomerative algorithm with favorable computational complexity.
*   Clear and concise theoretical presentation.
*   Potentially significant speedup compared to existing Bayesian methods.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited and insufficiently detailed experimental evaluation.
*   Lack of comparison to a wider range of relevant methods.
*   Small-scale datasets hinder generalizability assessment.
*   AUC calculation method needs clarification.



**Overall:** This paper presents a promising approach to hierarchical community discovery. However, the limited experimental validation prevents a strong recommendation for acceptance in its current form. I recommend rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit after a more comprehensive experimental evaluation on larger datasets and with comparisons to a broader set of baseline methods. Addressing the clarity of AUC computation is also crucial.
