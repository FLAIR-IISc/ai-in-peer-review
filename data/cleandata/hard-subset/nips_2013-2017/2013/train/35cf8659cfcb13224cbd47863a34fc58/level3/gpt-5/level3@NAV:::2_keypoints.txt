- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model that infers hierarchical (tree-consistent) partitions with a tractable DP marginal likelihood recursion.  
- Learning via a single-pass greedy agglomerative model-selection procedure considering join/absorb merges scored by local Bayes factors.  
- Empirically matches or slightly outperforms IRM on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, while being orders of magnitude faster on two social connectivity corpora.  
- Situates work relative to IRM and hierarchical IRM, Bayesian hierarchical clustering/rose trees, and agglomerative community detection methods.  
- Technical strength: sound modeling of tree-consistent partitions and an efficient marginal likelihood recursion; clear computational advantage over IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Major concern: Section 4 does not clarify whether the greedy tree-building is MAP model selection, approximate posterior inference, or a heuristic — the global objective is unclear.  
- Needs articulation of monotonicity guarantees (if any), whether the merge prior over tree topologies is encoded, and the implications of the local Bayes factor objective.  
- Requests sensitivity analysis to γ and Beta hyperparameters and justification/reporting of tie-breaking/restarts and their impact on result variance.  
- Clarity problems: multiple grammar/spelling errors, vague/overloaded notation, unclear dataset descriptions (splits, preprocessing, sparsity), and hard-to-parse figures.  
- Suggests streamlining ancillary math, enlarging and labeling figures, and expanding Section 4 with a step-by-step derivation linking the score to the model.  
- Originality: novel and useful combination of known components to enable efficient tree-restricted marginal likelihood for relational data.  
- Significance: strong potential impact — fast, interpretable hierarchical structure discovery useful to social scientists and ML practitioners.  
- Pros: efficient marginal likelihood on trees, large speedups with comparable/better predictive performance, and practical hierarchical outputs for prediction.  
- Cons: conceptual gap between model and greedy learning, presentation issues hurting reproducibility, and limited baselines/ablation and hyperparameter sensitivity analyses.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, contingent on clarifying Section 4 and improving writing and presentation.
