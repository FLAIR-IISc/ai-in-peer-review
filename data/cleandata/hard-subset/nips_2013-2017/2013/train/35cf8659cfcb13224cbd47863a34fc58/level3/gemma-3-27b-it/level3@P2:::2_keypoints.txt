- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for nested communities.
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative algorithm to enable efficient inference and avoid exponential partition search.
- Exploits tree structure to compute marginal likelihoods efficiently, yielding major computational gains over full Bayesian partition methods.
- Demonstrates comparable or improved accuracy with significantly faster runtimes (up to ~100x faster than IRM) on synthetic and real datasets.
- Thorough empirical comparison including IRM and BHCD variants (sparse vs. non-sparse, binary vs. non-binary trees).
- Evaluation includes real-world networks such as Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship data, plus prediction experiments.
- Core technical contribution: restricting partition space to tree-consistent partitions to enable scalability.
- Well-motivated connection to prior Bayesian nonparametric work and comprehensive references.
- Main weakness: unclear relationship between the Bayesian generative model and the greedy tree-learning procedure (posterior inference vs. MAP/ML ambiguity).
- Writing quality issues: awkward phrasing, grammar errors, and overly complex notation hinder readability.
- Section 4 (inference procedure) is particularly hard to follow and needs clearer exposition.
- Figures (especially Figure 1) and captions are not sufficiently clear or informative.
- Hyperparameter discussion (α, β, δ, λ, γ) lacks justification and sensitivity analysis.
- Overall technically sound and significant for scalable hierarchical community detection.
- Novelty: distinct and practical combination of tree-structured prior with greedy agglomerative inference.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical speedups, novel and sound approach, clear motivation, and relevance to network analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity/writing issues, unclear inference-model link, and missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on substantial revisions to clarify inference, improve presentation, and address hyperparameter sensitivity.
