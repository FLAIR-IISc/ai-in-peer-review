- Proposes BHCD: extends stochastic blockmodels/IRM using Bayesian Rose trees to model hierarchical community structure.  
- Introduces an efficient agglomerative inference algorithm achieving significant speedups versus IRM.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear, succinct mathematical derivations.  
- Effectively connects to prior Bayesian hierarchical clustering work and motivates hierarchical community discovery.  
- Clever use of factorization over subtrees (inspired by Koivisto & Sood) enables greedy search and efficient marginal likelihood computation.  
- Complexity analysis is justified and BHCD sparse addresses practical sparse-network challenges.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scale and scope; needs comparisons to a wider range of methods (e.g., scalable SBM and semi-parametric approaches).  
- Use of AUC as primary metric is unclear; computation method not stated and NMI would be a more standard choice.  
- Sampson and NIPS experiments provide qualitative/ runtime validation but lack deeper quantitative analysis of discovered hierarchies.  
- Selection method for displayed communities (probability threshold rT) requires clarification.  
- Would benefit from more thorough quantitative evaluation using established hierarchical community metrics.  
- Discussion of limitations and future work (non-conjugate likelihoods, triangular representations) is brief and trade-offs between efficiency and expressiveness should be elaborated.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application of Bayesian Rose trees, efficient scalable inference, clear presentation, demonstrated speedups.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope, insufficient comparisons, unclear AUC reporting, inadequate quantitative hierarchy evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable and novel contribution; suggest acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify evaluation metrics.
