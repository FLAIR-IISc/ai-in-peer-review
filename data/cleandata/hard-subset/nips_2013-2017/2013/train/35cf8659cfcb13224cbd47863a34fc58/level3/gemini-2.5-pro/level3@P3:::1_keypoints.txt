- Introduces BHCD: a nonparametric hierarchical community model extending IRM with a prior over tree structures, adapting Bayesian rose trees to relational data.  
- Uses a fast greedy agglomerative bottom-up inference algorithm instead of MCMC.  
- Empirically orders-of-magnitude faster than IRM MCMC while achieving comparable predictive performance.  
- Significant practical contribution enabling scalable Bayesian nonparametric analysis for larger networks.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-written exposition linking stochastic blockmodels, tree mixtures, and inference.  
- Original technical work: novel likelihood and caching of sufficient statistics yielding O(n^2 log n) complexity.  
- Strong empirical validation: qualitative results (Sampson, full NIPS coauthorship) and quantitative comparison on NIPS-234 (log predictive probability, accuracy, AUC).  
- Scalability concern: O(n^2 log n) may still be prohibitive for very large graphs; the sparse variant needs further exploration.  
- Missing larger-scale timing comparisons: runtimes shown only for NIPS-234; should report full NIPS (n=2864) timings.  
- Greedy search optimality unclear: recommend experiments on very small graphs to compare to true MAP and discussion of theoretical guarantees (e.g., submodularity).  
- Minor edits: fix redundant phrasing in line 48, ensure consistent use of "pair of trees," and correct "fruitful next steps" grammar.  
- Overall verdict: strong, novel, well-executed paper; recommend acceptance.
