- Introduces BHCD: extends the Infinite Relational Model with a tree-based prior inspired by Bayesian Rose Trees to uncover hierarchical network structure.
- Tree-based formulation yields a mixture over consistent partitions and enables exact, efficient marginal likelihood computation.
- Uses a greedy agglomerative bottom-up inference algorithm instead of computationally intensive MCMC.
- Major strength: significant improvement in computational efficiency for Bayesian nonparametric network models.
- Empirically, BHCD is orders of magnitude faster than standard IRM and achieves comparable or better predictive accuracy on NIPS-234.
- Originality: non-trivial and successful adaptation of BRT/IRM ideas to relational data while preserving efficiency.
- Presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear and logical exposition of model and algorithm.
- Main weakness: scalability concern—O(n^2 log n) complexity may be prohibitive for very large networks (tens of thousands of nodes).
- Suggestion: discuss limitations and the sparse variant more thoroughly and report runtime on the full NIPS dataset for clearer scaling evidence.
- Concern: greedy inference may yield suboptimal hierarchies; recommend evaluation against optimal trees on small networks or theoretical guarantees (e.g., submodularity).
- Minor edits: fix awkward phrasing "whilst... but" (line 48) and rephrase "pair of trees" and "A fruitful next steps."
- Overall: technically sound, significant and practical contribution that merits acceptance.
