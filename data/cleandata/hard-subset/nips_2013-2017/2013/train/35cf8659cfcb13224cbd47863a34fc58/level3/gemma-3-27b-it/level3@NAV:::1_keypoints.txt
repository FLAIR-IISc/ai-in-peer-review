- Proposes BHCD, a novel Bayesian nonparametric model that extends Bayesian rose trees to relational data for hierarchical community detection.  
- Employs a greedy agglomerative procedure to learn a probabilistic hierarchy of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Achieves substantial computational speedup (O(n^2 log n)) over the Infinite Relational Model while maintaining comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex ideas and motivation.  
- Empirically validated with qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and quantitative results on NIPS-234 and full NIPS analyses.  
- Timings on NIPS-234 convincingly show speedups; timings on the full NIPS dataset would better illustrate large-scale practical performance.  
- Potential scalability limitations for very large networks (tens of thousands of nodes); authors propose sparse variants as a possible remedy.  
- The quality of the greedy agglomerative approximation needs further study (e.g., exhaustive search on small subsets, theoretical justification such as submodularity).  
- Lack of theoretical guarantees for the greedy approximation is a noted weakness.  
- Minor editorial corrections are required (wording issues like “pair of trees,” “A fruitful next steps”).  
- Overall recommendation: significant, practical contribution deserving acceptance.
