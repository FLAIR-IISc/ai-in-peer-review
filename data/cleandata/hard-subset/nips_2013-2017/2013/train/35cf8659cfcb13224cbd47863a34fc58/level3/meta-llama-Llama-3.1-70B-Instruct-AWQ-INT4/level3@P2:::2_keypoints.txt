- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community structure in networks.  
- Extends the stochastic blockmodel to a tree-structured mixture of potentially exponentially many blockmodels.  
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm for inference that scales quadratically in vertices and is independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Well-motivated problem and relevant/important application domain.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social corpora shows the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to tree-structured ones permits efficient marginal likelihood computation, improving over IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Unclear how the greedy tree-learning procedure relates to the Bayesian generative model and whether it yields valid posterior inference or a MAP/ML estimate.  
- Section 4 needs clearer explanation to resolve conceptual confusion about the inference procedure.  
- Results are strong: comparable/better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, accuracy, and orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements.  
- Writing quality is poor and needs fixing: spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, unclear dataset descriptions, unnecessary/obscure math, and confusing figures.  
- Scores well on originality, significance, and quality under conference guidelines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/insightful model and algorithm, thorough empirical evaluation, strong results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity and presentation issues plus uncertainty about the validity of the greedy inference relative to the Bayesian model.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify Section 4 and substantially improve writing and presentation.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10, confidence 8/10.
