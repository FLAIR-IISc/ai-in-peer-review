- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels using Bayesian rose trees for hierarchical community detection.
- Builds on prior Bayesian hierarchical clustering and extends it to relational data with block-model likelihood and agglomerative model selection.
- Claims efficient computation via dynamic programming with O(n + m) likelihood updates using recursive sufficient-statistic updates.
- Strength: technically sound integration of prior work with a clear presentation of the core algorithm.
- Strength: empirical results show BHCD matches IRM accuracy while being two orders of magnitude faster.
- Strength: qualitative success on Sampson’s monastery network demonstrates meaningful hierarchical recovery.
- Weakness: “nonparametric” claim is questionable—rose trees are framed as model selection rather than true nonparametric modeling.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks comparisons to existing hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process).
- Weakness: scalability claims not fully validated because tested networks are not particularly large.
- Weakness: Section 3 presentation is dense and hard to follow, with insufficient detail on Equation (9), dynamic programming, and sufficient-statistic updates.
- Overall: competent and useful method but limited originality and inadequate comparative evaluation weaken its contribution.
- Recommendation: add comparisons to established hierarchical models, clarify “nonparametric” terminology, and provide more computational detail.
