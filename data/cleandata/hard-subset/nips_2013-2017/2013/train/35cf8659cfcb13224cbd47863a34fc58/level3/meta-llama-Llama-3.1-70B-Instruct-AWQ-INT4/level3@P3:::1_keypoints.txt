- Proposes BHCD, a novel Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks.  
- Models a tree-structured mixture of potentially exponentially many stochastic blockmodels and learns it with a greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and well-structured, making ideas and inference easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough literature review that motivates the method by highlighting limitations of existing community discovery approaches.  
- BHCD is an extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) but scales much better in practice.  
- Worst-case time complexity is O(n^2 log n), substantially improving scalability over prior methods.  
- Empirical evaluation on datasets (Sampson’s monastery, NIPS co-authorship) shows comparable or better accuracy than IRM with much lower computation time.  
- Authors present a detailed analysis of BHCD’s time complexity.  
- Limitation: scalability to very large networks beyond the tested NIPS co-authorship graph (2,864 nodes) is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Limitation: sensitivity to hyperparameter choices is not thoroughly investigated or quantified.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on adding more analysis of large-network scalability and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
