- Proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric model (tree-structured mixture of many SBMs) for hierarchical community discovery in social networks.  
- Introduces a fast, greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm that learns a fully probabilistic hierarchical community model in one data pass.  
- Builds on stochastic blockmodels, mixed-membership SBMs, and Bayesian methods, extending IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees and a simpler CRP formulation (eq.7).  
- Uses posterior factorization (Koivisto & Sood 2004) to enable greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistic handling, and link prediction.  
- Main algorithmic contribution summarized in Table 1 (agglomerative method).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with correct citations and generally consistent notation.  
- Minor style issue: avoid beginning sentences with citations to match NIPS style.  
- Evaluation uses AUC but the computation method is unclear and must be explicitly explained.  
- Suggests using NMI as a more common metric for SBM comparisons.  
- Experiments are small-scale (largest is full NIPS dataset) and largely illustrative rather than comparative.  
- Lacks comparisons to other methods, especially semi-parametric approaches beyond Bayesian nonparametrics.  
- Recommends including larger datasets used in recent SBM evaluations to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Strengths: novel model combining SBMs and Bayesian Rose trees, efficient algorithm, clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: limited experimental evaluation, missing comparative baselines, unclear AUC methodology.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
