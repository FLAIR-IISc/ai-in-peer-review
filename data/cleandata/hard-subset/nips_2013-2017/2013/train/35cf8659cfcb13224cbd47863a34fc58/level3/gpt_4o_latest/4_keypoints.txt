- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), combining Bayesian rose trees with stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community detection.  
- Uses a greedy agglomerative clustering algorithm with claimed worst-case complexity O(n^2 log n) and practical runtimes much faster than the IRM.  
- Novel recursive likelihood updates enable efficient computation in O(Nodes + Edges) time.  
- Empirically shows comparable or better accuracy and qualitative hierarchy discovery on real datasets (Sampson's monastery, NIPS co-authorship).  
- Paper is well-organized with clear motivation and thorough methodological description overall.  
- Notation and explanation in Section 3 are dense and hard to follow; dynamic programming/recursive updates need simplification or examples.  
- Lacks comparisons to other hierarchical models (e.g., nested CRP, Mondrian Process), limiting contextual evaluation.  
- The use of the term "nonparametric" is debatable and not adequately justified relative to prior interpretations of Bayesian rose trees.  
- Scalability on very large networks is untested; experiments focus on small-to-medium datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel efficient method, strong computational advantages, and compelling experimental results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comparisons to alternative hierarchical models, clarity issues affecting reproducibility, and unverified large-scale scalability.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending improvements to Section 3 clarity and inclusion of broader experimental comparisons.
