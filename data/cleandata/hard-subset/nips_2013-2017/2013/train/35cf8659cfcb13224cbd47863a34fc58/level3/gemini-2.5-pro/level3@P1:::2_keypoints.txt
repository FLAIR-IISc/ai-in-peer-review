- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric model that restricts partitions to tree-consistent ones, modeling networks as a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Key technical insight: restricting to tree-consistent partitions enables exact, efficient marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming.  
- Inference uses a greedy bottom-up agglomerative algorithm that merges subtrees using a local Bayes factor as the merge criterion.  
- Technical contributions include the recursive marginal likelihood derivation and an efficient caching scheme allowing O(1) inner-loop updates.  
- Empirical results: BHCD is orders of magnitude faster than IRM MCMC and attains comparable or slightly better predictive performance on NIPS co-authorship and reasonable qualitative recovery on Sampson data.  
- Significance: addresses scalability of Bayesian nonparametric community detection, making hierarchical Bayesian network analysis practical for larger problems.  
- Main methodological weakness: unclear connection between the generative Bayesian model and the greedy inference—uncertainty whether the algorithm targets MAP, maximizes p(D|T), or guarantees local optimality.  
- Presentation weaknesses: numerous typos/grammar issues, sparse dataset descriptions, unexplained terminology (e.g., “rose trees”), non-self-contained figure captions (e.g., undefined “BHCD global”), and dense mathematical exposition needing more intuition.  
- Recommendation: clarify the relationship between model and inference (objective and guarantees) and substantially improve writing and exposition before acceptance.
