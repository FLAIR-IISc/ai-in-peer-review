## Review of Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

This paper proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks, building upon the stochastic blockmodel and the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). The core idea is to represent the community structure as a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, enabling efficient inference of nested communities. The authors present a greedy agglomerative algorithm for learning this hierarchical structure directly from the data, demonstrating significant speed improvements over existing methods like the IRM while maintaining comparable or better accuracy on both synthetic and real-world datasets.

The work addresses a significant challenge in network analysis: efficiently discovering hierarchical community structures, which are common in many real-world social networks. Existing Bayesian nonparametric approaches, like the IRM extended for hierarchies, often suffer from computational bottlenecks. The authors cleverly address this by restricting the space of possible partitions to those consistent with a tree structure, allowing for efficient marginal likelihood computation – a key technical contribution. This allows them to bypass the exponential complexity inherent in exploring all possible partitions, a major limitation of methods like the IRM. The proposed model and algorithm have potential applications in areas such as social network analysis, recommendation systems, and biological network modeling.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The model appears theoretically sound, leveraging well-established concepts from stochastic blockmodels and Bayesian nonparametric statistics. The reduction in computational complexity by restricting the search space to tree-structured partitions is a valuable contribution.
* **Originality:** While building on existing work, the combination of a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a greedy agglomerative learning algorithm represents a novel approach to hierarchical community discovery. 
* **Significance:** The significantly improved runtime compared to the IRM, without sacrificing accuracy, is a major benefit. This makes Bayesian hierarchical community detection practical for larger networks than previously possible. The demonstrated performance on both synthetic and real-world data strengthens the significance of the findings.
* **Empirical Results:**  The experiments on Sampson’s monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship data show promising results. The improved speed and competitive accuracy relative to the IRM are compelling.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity:** The paper suffers from several clarity issues. The writing quality could be improved to address grammar and spelling errors. Descriptions of the datasets and experimental setup are somewhat vague. Some mathematical notation and derivations could be presented more clearly. Figure 3, in particular, is difficult to interpret without further explanation.
* **Conceptual Gap:** A key weakness is the lack of a clear explanation of how the greedy tree-learning procedure relates to the underlying Bayesian generative model. It is unclear whether the algorithm performs valid posterior inference, finds a MAP estimate, or some other approximation. This relationship needs to be explicitly clarified to establish the theoretical foundations of the work. Specifically, it is unclear *how* the score function (12) is derived and whether it corresponds to a well-defined Bayesian quantity.
* **Dataset Details:** More details regarding the preprocessing of the NIPS dataset would be beneficial. For example, what criteria were used to select the 234 most connected individuals? 
* **Figure Quality:** Some figures, particularly Figure 3, are visually cluttered and difficult to interpret. Improving the presentation of these figures would enhance the reader's understanding.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a challenging problem in network analysis.
* The significant improvement in runtime compared to existing methods makes Bayesian hierarchical community detection practical for larger networks.
* The empirical results are compelling, demonstrating competitive accuracy with substantial speed gains.
* The work has the potential to be impactful in various fields, including social network analysis and biological network modeling.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper suffers from clarity issues that hinder understanding and reproducibility.
* The conceptual link between the greedy learning algorithm and the Bayesian generative model is not clearly established.
* Improvements in writing quality and figure presentation are necessary.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of network analysis. The proposed model and algorithm address a significant challenge – efficient hierarchical community discovery – and offer a compelling solution. However, the paper’s clarity and presentation need improvement. If the authors address the identified weaknesses, particularly by clarifying the theoretical foundations of their algorithm and improving the writing and figures, the paper has the potential to be a strong addition to the NIPS proceedings.  Currently, I would rate this as a **weak accept**. Addressing the clarity and conceptual gaps is crucial for a stronger recommendation.
