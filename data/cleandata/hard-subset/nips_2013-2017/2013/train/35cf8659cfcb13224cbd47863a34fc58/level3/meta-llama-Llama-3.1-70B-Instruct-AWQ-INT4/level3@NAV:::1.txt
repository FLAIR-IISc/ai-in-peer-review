This paper proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The model is a tree-structured mixture of potentially exponentially many stochastic blockmodels. The authors describe a family of greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that take just one pass through the data to learn a fully probabilistic, hierarchical community model. The algorithm scales quadratically in the number of vertices of the network, but independent of the number of nested communities.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of the work. The model is a novel combination of familiar techniques, and the authors provide a clear exposition of the key ideas.

**Clarity:** The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results. The authors use clear and concise language, making it easy to follow the paper.

**Originality:** The paper proposes a new approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. **Significance:** The results are important, and other people (practitioners or researchers) are likely to use these ideas or build on them. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

**Weaknesses:**

**Suggestions:**
