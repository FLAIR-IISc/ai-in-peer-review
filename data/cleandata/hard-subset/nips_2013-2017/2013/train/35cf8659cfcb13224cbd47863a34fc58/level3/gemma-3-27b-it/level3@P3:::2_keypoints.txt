- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian hierarchical extension of the stochastic blockmodel using tree-structured mixtures and a greedy agglomerative algorithm for efficient tree selection.  
- Claims speed improvements over the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) with comparable accuracy on social connectivity data.  
- Addresses a relevant need for scalable, interpretable hierarchical community detection, especially for social science applications.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partition space to tree structures enables efficient marginal likelihood computation, avoiding IRM’s exponential complexity.  
- Positions work within Bayesian nonparametrics and cites relevant prior work (e.g., Holland, Airoldi, Kemp, Xu); compares to LFRM and ILA on NIPS data.  
- Empirical strengths: orders-of-magnitude speedups vs IRM while maintaining comparable or better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy.  
- Demonstrates ability to recover meaningful hierarchies qualitatively (Sampson’s monastery) and scalability on the NIPS co-authorship network.  
- Introduces BHCD sparse as a practical improvement for sparse networks.  
- Major weakness: unclear connection between the greedy tree-learning procedure (Algorithm 1) and the Bayesian generative model—uncertain whether it performs valid posterior inference or only MAP/ML.  
- Section 4 needs clarification on how the “Bayes factor improvement” score relates to posterior probability and what approximations are used.  
- Presentation problems: numerous spelling/grammar errors and vague writing that hinder comprehension.  
- Specific clarity issues: unclear descriptions (e.g., tie-breaking in greedy search), insufficient dataset details (NIPS co-authorship), and obscure notation (e.g., σ¬chSS).  
- Figures are confusing—Figure 2 particularly requires careful study to understand merging options.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, relevance, and demonstrated scalability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity, lack of theoretical justification for the inference algorithm, and weak presentation quality.  
- Overall: promising ideas and results; recommend acceptance contingent on major revisions to clarify the greedy algorithm’s justification (especially Section 4) and substantially improve writing and presentation.
