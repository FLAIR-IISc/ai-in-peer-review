- Proposes BHCD: adapts Bayesian rose trees with a stochastic block-model likelihood for hierarchical community discovery in networks.  
- Main contribution is a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm that learns a hierarchy in a single pass as a computationally efficient alternative to MCMC.  
- Elegant integration of existing techniques; approach is well-motivated and overall presentation is clear.  
- Inference leverages dynamic programming and cached sufficient statistics to claim O(n + m) likelihood computation, yielding practical speed advantages.  
- Preliminary experiments demonstrate speed benefits and qualitatively sensible hierarchies, with competitive predictive performance versus a flat IRM baseline.  
- Fails to compare against existing hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian-process methods), limiting assessment of novelty and progress.  
- Scalability claims are under-supported because experimental networks are not large enough to validate large-scale performance.  
- Section 3 is overly dense; derivations for the marginal likelihood recursion and sufficient statistic updates lack detail, hindering reproducibility.  
- The label "nonparametric" is debatable since the rose tree framework may be better viewed as Bayesian model selection over discrete tree structures.  
- Overall: technically sound with a good core idea, but limited originality, insufficient experimental comparisons, and clarity issues prevent a strong acceptance recommendation.
