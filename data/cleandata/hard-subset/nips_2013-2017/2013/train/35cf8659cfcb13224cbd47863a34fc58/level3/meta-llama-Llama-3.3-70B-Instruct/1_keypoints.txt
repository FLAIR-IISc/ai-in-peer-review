- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the IRM using a mixture over consistent node partitions and a greedy agglomerative learning procedure.  
- Claims computationally cheaper inference than IRM while achieving similar predictive performance on evaluated datasets.  
- Adapts Bayesian rose tree ideas to relational (network) data, preserving computational complexity—novelty highlighted.  
- Significance: advances scalability of Bayesian nonparametric (BNP) models to network modeling, important for practical applications.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition, making the methodology easy to understand.  
- Empirical results are qualitative and quantitative across multiple datasets, supporting method effectiveness.  
- Strength: provides hierarchical representations of networks useful for interpretability and structure discovery.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees for the greedy algorithm’s optimality (no analysis of submodularity or similar).  
- Weakness: limited experimental evaluation on larger real-world networks—more timing/scale benchmarks needed.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparisons with other existing network modeling methods to fully demonstrate competitiveness.  
- Recommendation: promising novel and efficient approach; would be stronger with additional theory and larger-scale experiments.
