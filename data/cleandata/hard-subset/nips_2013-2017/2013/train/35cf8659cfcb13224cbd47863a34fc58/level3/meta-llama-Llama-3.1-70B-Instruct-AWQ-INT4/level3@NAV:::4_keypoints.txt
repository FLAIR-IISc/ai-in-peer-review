- Proposes a Bayesian nonparametric tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithms that learn a probabilistic hierarchy in a single pass through the data.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with technical details explained thoroughly.
- Claims algorithmic scaling is quadratic in the number of vertices and independent of the number of nested communities.
- Experimental results show the method finds reasonable hierarchies and predicts ~100× faster than a leading Bayesian nonparametric baseline with comparable accuracy.
- Empirical comparison is limited: only evaluated against the flat Infinite Relational Model, not other hierarchical models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian).
- The label "nonparametric" is questionable because the model depends on hyperparameters that require tuning.
- Use of conjugate priors and a specific likelihood reduces flexibility relative to some other nonparametric models.
- Overall assessment: promising and scalable approach but with notable methodological and comparative gaps (score 7/10).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel model, clear exposition, strong empirical speed/performance merits.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate comparisons to hierarchical baselines, ambiguous nonparametric claim, reliance on tunable hyperparameters.
- Suggestions: provide thorough comparisons to hierarchical network models, clarify/justify the “nonparametric” terminology, and consider more flexible nonparametric modeling choices.
