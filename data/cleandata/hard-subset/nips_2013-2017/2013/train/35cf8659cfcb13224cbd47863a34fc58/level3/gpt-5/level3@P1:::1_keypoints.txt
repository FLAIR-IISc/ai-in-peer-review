- Contribution: Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model that represents hierarchies as trees inducing mixtures over tree-consistent partitions with exact marginal likelihood via dynamic programming.
- Likelihood/prior: Uses a stochastic blockmodel with conjugate, separate priors for within- and between-cluster densities and per-node stopping probabilities (Bayesian rose tree style).
- Inference: Greedy agglomerative algorithm with three merge operations scored by local Bayes factors, O(1) cached updates and overall O(n^2 log n) time; sparse variant forbids merges between disconnected components.
- Prediction: Provides a recursive predictive distribution for link prediction by traversing the tree path between vertex pairs.
- Experiments: Qualitative recovery on Sampson’s monastery, competitive link prediction on NIPS-234 at much lower runtime than IRM, and a large-scale hierarchy for full NIPS coauthorship.
- Relation to prior work: Trades MCMC/variational inference of IRM and extensions for greedy model selection, leveraging Bayesian rose trees for exact marginal likelihood on trees.
- Relation to flat/mixed SBMs: Discovers nested structure without pre-specifying number of groups; extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering from i.i.d. to relational blockmodels.
- Strength — modeling: Principled mixture over tree-consistent partitions with exact marginalization and clear recursive p(D|T).
- Strength — efficiency: Thoughtful local Bayes factor scoring and caching yield scalable O(n^2 log n) inference independent of number of communities; sparse variant speeds up sparse graphs.
- Strength — empirical: Fast attainment of useful accuracy/AUC on NIPS-234 versus IRM; convincing qualitative hierarchies.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well written with clear derivations and algorithms.
- Weakness — scalability: O(n^2 log n) may be prohibitive for 10^4–10^5+ nodes; no systematic scaling or memory study and missing wall-clock/memory timings for datasets.
- Weakness — greedy optimality: No theoretical guarantees; needs benchmarking against exhaustive search on small n or conditions under which greedy is near-optimal.
- Weakness — sensitivity/robustness: Hyperparameters fixed; lacks sensitivity analyses for γ, Beta priors, ablations (binary vs rose, sparse vs dense), and effect of restarts R.
- Weakness — scope/generalization: Focuses on undirected, unweighted graphs without self-loops; needs discussion or experiments for directed/weighted networks and missing data.
- Weakness — empirical fairness: Clarify IRM implementation details (MCMC vs greedy), iterations, and convergence criteria for fair comparisons.
- Quality: Technical development and marginal likelihood derivation are sound; greedy inference is a reasonable engineering trade-off; speed claims against IRM supported.
- Clarity: Exposition and organization are very clear, with only minor language/typography issues.
- Originality: Novel and useful adaptation of Bayesian rose trees to relational blockmodels, local Bayes factor merges, and O(1) caching for sufficient statistics.
- Significance: Fills a useful niche for fast, interpretable hierarchical community discovery; O(n^2) barrier limits extreme-scale applicability.
- Specific suggestion: Provide wall-clock timings and memory for Sampson, NIPS-234, full NIPS, and a larger ≥10k-node network if feasible.
- Specific suggestion: Add sensitivity analyses for γ and priors and report impact of number of restarts R.
- Specific suggestion: Evaluate optimality gap on small synthetic datasets with known hierarchies or provide theoretical insight into greedy guarantees.
- Specific suggestion: Discuss or demonstrate directed/weighted extensions and handling of missing entries.
- Minor corrections: Fix wording/agreement errors, reference typos, and consistent citation/notation formatting.
- Arguments for acceptance: Principled model with exact tree marginal likelihood, substantial speedups over IRM with competitive performance, well-executed adaptation of BRT to relational data, and practical, code-friendly algorithm.
- Arguments against acceptance: Greedy inference lacks guarantees, limited scalability evidence, and insufficient exploration of sparsity/parameter sensitivity and robustness.
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
