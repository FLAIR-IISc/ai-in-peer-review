- Proposes a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method using a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Learns the model with a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm that scales quadratically in vertices and is independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Paper is well-motivated and provides a clear overview of prior community-discovery work.  
- Technical novelty in adapting likelihood computations and updating sufficient statistics efficiently for network data.  
- Uses dynamic programming to compute marginal tree likelihood, enabling efficient hierarchical inference.  
- Technical presentation is generally clear, but Section 3 needs more detailed explanations and examples.  
- Experiments show improved predictive performance and computational efficiency; qualitative case study (Sampson’s monastery) demonstrates hierarchical capture.  
- Lacks comparison to existing hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process), limiting assessment of relative strengths.  
- The label “nonparametric” is questionable since the approach is framed as model selection rather than a true nonparametric method.  
- Scalability claims are not fully demonstrated because tested networks are not especially large.  
- Originality is limited despite solid technical work and good integration of prior work.  
- Recommendations: expand Section 3, add comparisons to hierarchical baselines, clarify nonparametric terminology, and run larger-scale experiments.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing the noted limitations.
