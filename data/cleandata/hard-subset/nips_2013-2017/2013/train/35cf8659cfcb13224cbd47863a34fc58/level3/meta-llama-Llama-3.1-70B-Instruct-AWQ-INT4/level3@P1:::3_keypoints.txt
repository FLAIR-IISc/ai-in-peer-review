- Proposes a novel Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community discovery by extending stochastic blockmodels and the IRM with Bayesian Rose trees.  
- Presents an efficient greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm that scales quadratically with the number of vertices and is independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Posterior factorization follows Koivisto & Sood (2004), supporting greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, use of sufficient statistics, and link prediction.  
- Paper is well-written with correct citations; minor style issue: sentences beginning with citations violate NIPS style.  
- Theory is succinct and uses consistent notation (e.g., f() and g()); the extension to the single-table IRM is a novel contribution.  
- Main algorithmic contribution is summarized in Table 1 and is claimed to be efficient and scalable.  
- Evaluation relies on AUC, but the method for computing AUC is unclear and needs explicit explanation.  
- NMI (Normalized Mutual Information) should be included as it is a common metric for stochastic block model comparisons.  
- Experiments are small-scale (largest is the full NIPS dataset) and should be expanded to include larger, recent SBM benchmarks.  
- Experimental work is illustrative rather than comparative and lacks comparisons to semi-parametric methods beyond Bayesian nonparametrics.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing evaluation shortcomings and providing more extensive experiments.  
- Required fixes: clarify AUC computation, include NMI, add larger datasets, and perform more comprehensive comparative experiments.
