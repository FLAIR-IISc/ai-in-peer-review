- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric model combining rose-tree priors with a tree-structured mixture of SBMs and Beta-conjugate marginalization.  
- Introduces a tree-consistent partition prior (πS per internal node) encouraging non-binary trees when structure is weak and enabling subtree-wise marginal likelihood factorization.  
- Derives exact dynamic programming over tree-consistent partitions and efficient link-prediction posteriors.  
- Main algorithm is a greedy agglomerative model-selection procedure using local Bayes factors with cached sufficient statistics.  
- Per-iteration updates are O(1) with overall O(n^2 log n) runtime; a sparse variant forbids merging disconnected subtrees for speed.  
- Empirically produces sensible hierarchies (Sampson) and competitive link-prediction on NIPS coauthorship while being substantially faster than IRM.  
- Probabilistic construction and subtree factorization appear correct; three-way merge ops and cached statistic scheme are technically sound.  
- Greedy optimization is myopic and sensitive to tie-breaking; authors mitigate with restarts but this remains a limitation.  
- The sparse merge prohibition is practical but departs from the generative model and needs Bayesian justification or analysis of its effect on evidence.  
- Experimental section is illustrative but limited: comparisons concentrate on IRM and lack broader controlled baselines and protocol clarity.  
- Hyperparameters are fixed with no sensitivity analysis, leaving questions about robustness.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and notationally consistent; figures effectively show hierarchy/partition relations.  
- Minor style fix: avoid sentence-initial citations per NIPS style.  
- Evaluation details needed: AUC protocol (held-out positives fraction, negative sampling, class balance, ROC on dyads vs subsample, edge deletion preserving connectivity).  
- Recommend adding partition-quality metrics (NMI/ARI/VI) for tree cuts and calibration metrics (Brier/log-loss) for link prediction.  
- Novelty lies in combining rose-tree priors with SBMs and deriving a factorization enabling fast greedy inference; clarify contrasts to hierarchical IRM/Mondrian and multiscale blockmodels.  
- If validated on larger benchmarks and broader baselines (including degree-corrected SBMs), BHCD could offer a useful accuracy–speed trade-off.  
- Current evidence is promising but insufficient; include larger datasets, more baselines, and ablations (binary vs non-binary, sparse constraint).  
- Pros: principled model, exact subtree marginalization, scalable O(n^2 log n) inference, interpretable hierarchies, fast link prediction.  
- Cons: limited empirical scope and unclear AUC protocol, missing comparisons to stronger SBMs and hierarchical methods, sensitivity/tie-breaking concerns, and generative mismatch of sparse variant.  
- Suggested improvements: detail AUC protocol, report NMI/ARI, add larger datasets and baselines (degree-corrected SBM, hierarchical random graphs), perform hyperparameter sensitivity and restart stability analyses, and justify or quantify the sparse constraint.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid and clearly presented but empirically underspecified; leans toward weak reject pending strengthened experiments and clarified evaluation.
