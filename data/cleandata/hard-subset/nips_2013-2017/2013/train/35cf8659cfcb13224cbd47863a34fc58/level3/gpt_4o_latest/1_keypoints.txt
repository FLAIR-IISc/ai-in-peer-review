- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community structure in networks.  
- Extends the Infinite Relational Model by using Bayesian rose tree ideas to form a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Uses a greedy agglomerative learning procedure with computational complexity O(n^2 log n), independent of number of nested communities.  
- Claims significant scalability improvement over existing Bayesian nonparametric models, especially for dense networks.  
- Shows comparable predictive performance to IRM while being two orders of magnitude faster.  
- Empirical validation on Sampson's monastery and NIPS co-authorship datasets demonstrates meaningful hierarchies and accurate predictions.  
- Provides both qualitative and quantitative results and a detailed comparison of computational efficiency with other methods.  
- Highlights potential applications in social and biological network analysis and discusses limitations and future directions (e.g., sparse networks, non-conjugate likelihoods).  
- Lacks timing/scalability experiments on very large real-world networks (tens of thousands of nodes), limiting practical applicability assessment.  
- Greedy agglomerative procedure has no theoretical guarantees or analysis (e.g., submodularity or optimality bounds).  
- Minor issues: grammatical errors and no systematic analysis or guidelines for hyperparameter selection.  
- Overall recommendation: acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability evidence, theoretical analysis, grammar, and hyperparameter guidance.
