- Proposes a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels to discover hierarchical community structure, building on Bayesian rose trees.  
- Learns the hierarchy with a greedy agglomerative procedure adapted nontrivially for relational data while keeping computational complexity manageable.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on Sampson's monastery network and NIPS co-authorship networks (full NIPS and NIPS-234).  
- Achieves predictive performance similar to the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while being computationally cheaper.  
- Worst-case time complexity O(n^2 log n), making the method suitable for large dense networks.  
- Introduces a sparse version of the algorithm that could scale to larger networks.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and understandable presentation of key ideas.  
- Lacks runtime/timing comparisons on larger real-world datasets (e.g., full NIPS vs NIPS-234).  
- Does not address scalability to very large networks (tens of thousands of nodes) or practical inference scaling in such cases.  
- No evaluation of how close the greedy method comes to the optimal tree (e.g., via exhaustive search at feasible scales).  
- No theoretical support provided for the greedy procedure (e.g., submodularity or approximation guarantees).  
- Minor wording issues needing correction (e.g., "whilst... but", "pair of trees", "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step").  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; contributions significant for Bayesian nonparametric network models.
