- Proposal: Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a Bayesian nonparametric method that models hierarchies as trees of communities and sums exactly over tree-consistent partitions via dynamic programming.
- Likelihood/model: Uses conjugate Beta-Bernoulli blockmodels with different hyperparameters for within- vs between-community blocks, integrating out block parameters.
- Inference: Greedy agglomerative model selection merges subtree pairs using three local operations (join, absorb-left, absorb-right) scored by a local Bayes factor with cached sufficient statistics.
- Variants and prediction: Provides BHCD-sparse and binary-only variants and an efficient link-prediction routine via a recursive mixture down a tree path.
- Relation to prior work: Positioned between stochastic blockmodels and Bayesian nonparametrics (e.g., IRM); conceptually close to Bayesian hierarchical clustering/rose trees but applied to relational data.
- Tradeoffs: Trades full posterior inference for greedy model selection, gaining substantial speed over MCMC-based IRM and related models.
- Empirical results: Recovers interpretable groups on Sampson’s network, outperforms IRM in predictive log-likelihood/AUC on NIPS-234, is competitive with LFRM, and scales to the full NIPS graph (2864 nodes).
- Speed/scalability: Reports orders-of-magnitude speedups (useful accuracy in seconds vs hundreds/thousands of seconds for IRM) and worst-case learning time O(n^2 log n).
- Hyperparameters/reporting: Uses α=δ=1, β=λ=0.2, γ=0.4, R=50 restarts; however experimental details need clarification.
- Strength: Clear modeling insight—restricting to tree-consistent partitions enables exact marginalization and efficient dynamic programming.
- Strength: Principled, efficient local agglomerative learning with O(1) inner-loop updates via caching and overall O(n^2 log n).
- Strength: Practical algorithmic variants and a simple, linear-in-depth prediction routine.
- Strength: Strong empirical speedups with competitive accuracy and qualitative interpretability on real datasets.
- Strength: High relevance—addresses hierarchical community discovery and computational bottlenecks in Bayesian nonparametrics.
- Weakness: Inferential objective unclear—paper does not specify whether it maximizes p(D|T) or approximates a MAP with a prior p(T).
- Weakness: Role and sensitivity of γ (the partition prior parameter) are underexplained; need guidance and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: Merge locality assumption may fail with long-range dependencies; need discussion of backtracking or lookahead.
- Weakness: Lack of theoretical guarantees or bounds on approximation/consistency and known failure modes.
- Weakness: Incorrect statement on prediction complexity (claimed Θ(n) but should be linear in tree depth); need discussion of typical depths.
- Weakness: Experimental details incomplete—hardware, IRM/MCMC settings, normalization over restarts, consistent IRM comparisons, cross-validation/negative sampling protocols.
- Weakness: Modeling limitation—Beta-Bernoulli SBM ignores degree heterogeneity; compare to degree-corrected SBMs and test on synthetic hierarchical SBMs.
- Weakness: Presentation issues—typos, ambiguous notation, hard-to-read figures, and unclear binary vs non-binary tree description.
- Quality: Model and dynamic program are technically sound; greedy inference is reasonable but needs clearer probabilistic framing and limitations discussion.
- Clarity: Organization is good but prose, notation, and experimental descriptions require improvement.
- Originality: Novel combination of rose-tree–style hierarchical partitioning with blockmodel likelihood and agglomerative scoring for relational data; incremental but meaningful.
- Significance: Fast, scalable, and practical method likely to be adopted if clarified and released.
- Suggestions: Clarify inference objective and p(T), analyze γ and hyperparameter sensitivity, tighten experimental details, add synthetic recovery studies, fix notation/typos, and include pseudocode.
- Acceptance arguments: Strong speed-accuracy tradeoff, principled modeling, interpretable hierarchies, and practical impact.
- Rejection arguments: Unclear inference/prior specification, reproducibility/expt. detail gaps, and limited theoretical/robustness analysis.
- Overall recommendation: Lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying inference objective, improving Section 4 exposition, and tightening experimental details.
