

This paper proposes a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery (BHCD) method that extends the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) by introducing a tree-structured partitioning approach for inferring hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors develop a Bayesian nonparametric model where partitions are restricted to tree-consistent structures, enabling efficient marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming. They then introduce a greedy agglomerative algorithm to build the hierarchical tree, claiming orders-of-magnitude speed improvements over IRM while maintaining comparable accuracy.

The paper's key strengths include a significant technical insight—restricting partitions to tree structures avoids the exponential partition space of IRM, enabling efficient inference. The empirical results on social network datasets demonstrate strong performance: BHCD achieves comparable or better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy than IRM, with runtime improvements of two orders of magnitude. The problem addressed is highly relevant to social scientists seeking hierarchical network structures, and the method represents a meaningful extension of Bayesian nonparametric approaches to community detection.

However, the paper has notable weaknesses. The most significant issue is conceptual confusion regarding Section 4: it's unclear whether the greedy algorithm performs valid Bayesian posterior inference or merely finds a MAP/ML estimate. The authors need to clarify how the agglomerative procedure relates to the Bayesian generative model. Additionally, the writing quality requires substantial improvement—there are spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, unclear dataset descriptions, unnecessary mathematical notation, and confusing figures that detract from the otherwise strong technical contributions.

While the core algorithm and empirical results are insightful and the method has clear practical value, the presentation issues must be addressed before publication. If the authors clarify the conceptual relationship between the greedy algorithm and Bayesian inference, and improve the writing quality, this paper would make a valuable contribution to the field of network analysis. As it stands, the technical merit is high but the presentation undermines the clarity of the contribution. With revisions to address the conceptual and presentation issues, this paper would be a strong candidate for publication.
