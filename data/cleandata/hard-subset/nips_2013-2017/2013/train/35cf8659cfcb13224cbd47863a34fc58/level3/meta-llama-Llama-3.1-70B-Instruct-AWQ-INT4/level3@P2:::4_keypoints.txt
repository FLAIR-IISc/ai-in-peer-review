- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community detection.
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that learn a probabilistic hierarchy in a single pass.
- Worst-case runtime scales O(n^2) in number of vertices and is independent of number of nested communities.
- Claims likelihood computation and sufficient-statistic updates can be done in O(nodes + edges) enabling scalable clustering.
- Relates to prior work (stochastic blockmodels, mixed membership models, IRM) and compares to flat IRM baseline.
- Technical novelty is adapting likelihood and efficient recursive sufficient-statistic updates for network data.
- Presentation of dynamic programming and recursive updates is dense and hard to follow and needs more detail.
- Experiments show improvements over flat IRM and qualitative success on Sampson’s monastery network.
- Demonstrates speed and accuracy on a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network versus IRM.
- Lacks comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process).
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable given rose trees framed as model selection rather than true nonparametrics.
- Scalability claims are not fully demonstrated because tested networks are not especially large.
- Overall technically sound and well-integrated with prior work but shows limited originality.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve explanations, add comparative experiments, and clarify nonparametric terminology.
