- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): combines Bayesian rose tree priors with stochastic blockmodel likelihood for hierarchical network community detection.
- Adapts greedy agglomerative inference from rose trees to networks, enabling exact marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming over tree-structured partitions.
- Main technical contributions: likelihood adaptation for relational data and an efficient caching scheme for sufficient statistics supporting a bottom-up greedy tree-learning algorithm.
- Claims computational efficiency: marginal likelihood computable in O(n + m) and overall greedy search in O(n^2 log n).
- Strength: technically sound integration of established probabilistic models with a principled Bayesian framework for selection and prediction.
- Strength: computational efficiency—single-pass greedy algorithm with efficient updates is much faster than MCMC IRM and yields comparable or better predictive performance.
- Strength: generally clear writing and well-motivated introduction; qualitative results (Sampson monastery) are interpretable.
- Strength: significant practical contribution—fast Bayesian method for hierarchical network analysis applicable where MCMC is intractable.
- Weakness: Section 3 (core technical derivations) is dense and under-explained; dynamic programming and update rules need step-by-step explanation or pseudocode for reproducibility.
- Weakness: originality is incremental—main novelty is adapting existing rose-tree methods to network likelihoods rather than a fundamentally new hierarchical modeling approach.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons are inadequate—only compared to flat IRM, missing comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process).
- Weakness: scalability claims are not fully validated—datasets are modest (NIPS-234, ~2800 nodes) and O(n^2 log n) may not scale to very large graphs.
- Weakness: terminology issue—calling the model “Bayesian nonparametric” is debatable and should be more precisely framed.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on substantially improving Section 3 clarity and adding experimental comparisons to relevant hierarchical baselines.
