- Proposal: Introduces a Bayesian nonparametric tree-structured mixture of SBMs to discover hierarchical community structure with a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm running in O(n² log n).  
- Prior work: Builds on SBM, IRM, and Bayesian hierarchical clustering, citing relevant methods like the Mondrian process.  
- Key innovation: Replaces the CRP prior with a tree-based prior enabling dynamic programming and efficient marginal likelihood computation.  
- Theoretical quality: Sound derivation and posterior factorization; sufficient-statistic caching yields O(1) computation per merge.  
- Algorithmic advantage: Greedy inference is significantly faster (≈ two orders of magnitude) than IRM while maintaining comparable accuracy.  
- Experimental issues: Evaluation uses AUC but its computation is unclear; datasets are small (e.g., NIPS-234) and comparisons to broader baselines are limited.  
- Suggested metrics: Should include standard community-detection metrics like Normalized Mutual Information (NMI).  
- Clarity: Well-written and well-organized with clear notation and effective figures; minor citation-style issues.  
- Originality: Novel extension of SBM to hierarchical structures using Bayesian Rose trees and an efficient alternative to MCMC-based inference.  
- Significance: Addresses an important problem and offers practical scalability, but impact would be stronger with larger datasets and comparisons to non-Bayesian methods.  
- Strengths: Solid theory, elegant and efficient algorithm design, substantial speedup in experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: Limited experimental scope, unclear AUC methodology, lack of semi-parametric/non-Bayesian baselines, and missing standard evaluation metrics.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify evaluation methodology and expand experimental comparisons.
