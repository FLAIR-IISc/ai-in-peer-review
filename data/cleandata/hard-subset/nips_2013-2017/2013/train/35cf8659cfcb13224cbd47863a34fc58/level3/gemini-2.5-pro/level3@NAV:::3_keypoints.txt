- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a nonparametric model for hierarchical structure in relational data.  
- Extends stochastic blockmodel (SBM) and Infinite Relational Model (IRM) by replacing the CRP prior with a Bayesian Rose Tree prior over partitions.  
- Enables exact marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming, avoiding approximate MCMC/variational inference.  
- Proposes a greedy agglomerative algorithm to learn the tree structure (primary practical contribution).  
- Theoretical development is elegant and builds on Koivisto & Sood (2004) to enable posterior factorization.  
- Algorithm reportedly achieves large speedups (≈100×) over MCMC-based IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance.  
- Concise formulation using general f() and g() functions for on- and off-diagonal blocks.  
- Experimental evaluation is illustrative and insufficiently rigorous for a top-tier conference.  
- Use of AUC for link prediction is not clearly justified and held-out link selection is unspecified.  
- Missing standard community-detection metrics (e.g., NMI), making comparisons to SBM literature difficult.  
- Experiments are limited to small/modest datasets; scalability and state-of-the-art claims are not substantiated.  
- Comparisons are too narrow—need benchmarks against a broader set of scalable or semi-parametric hierarchical methods.  
- Minor stylistic issue: several sentences begin with citations, which conflicts with NIPS style.
