- Proposes a Bayesian tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm.
- Integrates stochastic blockmodel likelihood with rose-tree hierarchical prior in a technically sound way.
- Adapts likelihood and sufficient-statistic updates to enable efficient clustering for relational data.
- Claims theoretical O(n^2 log n) worst-case complexity and shows significant speed advantage over a single IRM iteration.
- Overall presentation is clear, with a well-defined experimental setup and a link-prediction application.
- Derivations in Section 3 (notably eq. (9)) are dense and need more intermediate explanation.
- Descriptions of dynamic programming and recursive statistic updates lack sufficient implementation detail.
- Experimental evaluation omits comparisons to established hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process).
- Calling the model “nonparametric” is misleading given the parametric nature of the rose-tree prior; better framed as Bayesian model selection.
- Scalability claims are not rigorously validated on truly large-scale networks; datasets used are modest.
- Multiple algorithm variants (BHCD, BHCD sparse, etc.) complicate interpretation of experimental results.
- Originality is limited—combines known Bayesian hierarchical clustering and SBM ideas—though adapting rose trees to relational data and efficiency gains are contributions.
- Significance is promising if scalability and robustness hold, but currently tempered by insufficient comparative validation.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound model, demonstrated speed improvements, generally clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of thorough comparisons, insufficient algorithmic detail, unvalidated scalability, and debatable terminology.
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution but recommend rejection pending clearer algorithmic exposition and more comprehensive comparative evaluation before resubmission.
