- Proposal: Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery with greedy agglomerative, single-pass model selection algorithms.  
- Scalability claim: algorithm scales quadratically in number of vertices and is independent of number of nested communities.  
- Quality: technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; evaluations are careful and honest.  
- Clarity: clearly written, well-organized, and sufficiently detailed for expert reproduction.  
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques and a significant departure from prior work.  
- Significance: meaningful advance likely to be used or built upon by researchers and practitioners.  
- Strengths: novel model, favorable scaling property, clear exposition, and balanced evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: may not suit very large networks (tens of thousands+ nodes); no timing experiments on large real networks; lacks theoretical performance guarantees for the greedy algorithm.  
- Suggestions: provide large-scale timing results, compare greedy solution to optimal tree where feasible, and seek theoretical support (e.g., submodularity) for algorithm performance.  
- Verdict: accept — significant, well-written contribution despite minor weaknesses.
