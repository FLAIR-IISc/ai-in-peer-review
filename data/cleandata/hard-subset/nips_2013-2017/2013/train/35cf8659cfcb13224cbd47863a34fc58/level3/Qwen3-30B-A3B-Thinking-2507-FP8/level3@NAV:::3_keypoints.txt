- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels extending the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).
- Replaces CRP formulation with a simpler prior (equation 7) and incorporates Bayesian Rose trees for hierarchical structure.
- Enables efficient greedy agglomerative search via posterior factorization over subtrees, following Koivisto & Sood (2004).
- Well-written paper with good grammar and correct citations (minor NIPS style issues: sentences beginning with citations).
- Clear, succinct theoretical development with consistent notation for on-diagonal and off-diagonal blocks.
- Main algorithmic contribution: quadratic-time agglomerative method that scales well and is ~100× faster than IRM.
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation building on SBM and IRM literature.
- Strength: novel use of Bayesian Rose trees to allow efficient greedy search.
- Strength: correct handling of sufficient statistics for marginal likelihoods.
- Weakness: Evaluation metric unclear—uses AUC without explaining computation; NMI is the standard for SBM comparisons.
- Weakness: Experiments limited to small NIPS datasets, lacking larger, commonly used SBM benchmarks.
- Weakness: Experimental work is illustrative and lacks comparisons to other semi-parametric methods beyond Bayesian nonparametrics.
- Conclusion: Technically sound with clear writing and speed advantage, but experimental shortcomings limit significance and suitability for NIPS.
- Recommendation: Require stronger comparative experiments on larger datasets and use standard metrics before publication.
