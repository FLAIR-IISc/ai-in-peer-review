- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): Bayesian rose trees + stochastic blockmodel for scalable agglomerative hierarchical community detection.
- Claims core novelty in adapting likelihood computation and sufficient-statistic updates to enable efficient hierarchical clustering with O(nodes + edges) complexity.
- Positions work within prior Bayesian hierarchical models (IRM, Mondrian) and greedy hierarchical clustering, arguing a pragmatic expressiveness–efficiency tradeoff.
- Sensible idea of reusing prior tree structure and agglomerative updates to improve scalability.
- Reports substantial speedup over IRM (two orders of magnitude) while achieving comparable or better accuracy.
- Qualitative success shown on Sampson’s monastery network; competitive predictive performance on NIPS co-authorship data.
- Averaging over multiple tree restarts improves robustness of results.
- Presentation clear overall but Section 3 is dense; derivations after eq.(9) are hard to follow.
- Dynamic programming and recursive sufficient-statistic updates lack detailed walkthroughs and clarity.
- Critical experimental omission: no comparison to established hierarchical models like multiscale community blockmodel (Ho et al. 2012) or Mondrian process (Roy et al. 2009).
- Use of “nonparametric” terminology is questionable given reliance on a prior framing of rose trees.
- Scalability claims are under-supported because tested networks are relatively small and larger-scale empirical validation is missing.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, well-motivated, integrates existing work, practical speedup over IRM.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited originality, insufficient algorithmic explanation, missing relevant comparisons, and unsupported scalability claims.
- Overall recommendation: rejection with encouragement to improve comparative evaluation and clarify algorithmic details.
