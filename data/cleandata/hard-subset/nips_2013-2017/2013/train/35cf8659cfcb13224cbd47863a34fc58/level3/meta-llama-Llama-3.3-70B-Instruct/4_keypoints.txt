- Proposes a novel hierarchical classification approach with a data-dependent generalization bound for multiclass hierarchical classifiers.  
- Introduces a meta-classifier to select taxonomy nodes to prune, improving classification performance.  
- Presents novel theoretical contributions: a hierarchical Rademacher data-dependent bound and an asymptotic approximation error bound for multiclass logistic regression.  
- Experiments demonstrate the pruning strategy’s effectiveness and align with prior studies.  
- Technical development is sound and the paper is well-structured.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: tested networks are small and comparisons to other hierarchical network models are missing.  
- Description of the new likelihood function and recursive updating of sufficient statistics lacks clarity.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable; approach appears closer to model selection.  
- Fails to cite or compare to other relevant Bayesian nonparametric models that could discover finer latent structures.  
- Claims would be better supported with more extensive experiments and broader model comparisons.
