- Paper proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones, enabling exact marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming and a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm that is much faster than IRM while matching predictive accuracy.

- Strength: Theoretical and empirical evidence supports substantial computational efficiency (O(n² log n) inference vs. IRM’s heavier cost) with validated runtime and competitive predictive performance on benchmarks.

- Strength: Original idea of restricting partitions to tree-structured sets to enable efficient inference is novel and clearly differentiated from prior MCMC or depth-constrained hierarchies.

- Strength: Highly relevant to hierarchical community detection in social networks, addressing scalability needs for Bayesian-style methods.

- Weakness: Conceptual misrepresentation of “Bayesian” — the greedy algorithm performs MAP-like tree selection via local Bayes factors rather than sampling or computing the posterior, disconnecting inference from the generative Bayesian model.

- Weakness: The generative model and the greedy inference procedure are not coherently connected; the paper does not compute full posteriors or marginal likelihoods for model comparison despite Bayesian framing.

- Weakness: Clarity and presentation problems — undefined terms (e.g., “rose trees,” “BHCD global”), vague algorithmic descriptions, unlabeled/misleading figures, and multiple spelling/grammar errors that reduce professionalism.

- Weakness: Missing analysis on why the greedy, locally optimal merges yield competitive results and lack of comparison of inferred tree interpretability (e.g., dendrogram quality) versus IRM.

- Recommendation: Authors should explicitly reframe the method as a fast MAP estimator or extend inference to compute posteriors, and substantially improve writing, figures, and explanations.

- Decision: Weak Accept conditional on major revisions to clarify the Bayesian interpretation and fix presentation issues; the work has significant potential if these are addressed.
