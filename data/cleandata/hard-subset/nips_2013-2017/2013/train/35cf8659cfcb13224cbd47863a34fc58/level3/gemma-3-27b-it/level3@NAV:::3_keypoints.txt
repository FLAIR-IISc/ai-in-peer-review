- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community detection.  
- Extends SBMs and the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees for hierarchical structure.  
- Proposes a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm using factorization techniques from Koivisto & Sood (2004) for computational efficiency.  
- Claims favorable scaling properties, notably inference complexity independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Clear theoretical foundation and consistent, readable notation (use of f() and g()).  
- Replaces the CRP formulation of IRM with a simpler approach (Eq. 7), improving practical applicability.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: primarily illustrative, not rigorous, and focused on speed comparisons with IRM.  
- AUC is reported but the computation method is unclear and needs detailed explanation; recommend using standard metrics like NMI.  
- Experiments use only a single, small NIPS dataset; larger and benchmark SBM datasets are needed for generalizability.  
- Lacks comparisons to broader methods (e.g., semi-parametric approaches), limiting assessment of relative performance.  
- Conclusion that BHCD achieves “good results quickly” is insufficiently supported by comparative evidence.  
- Recommendation: reject pending substantial expansion of experiments, clearer AUC methodology, and broader method comparisons before resubmission.
