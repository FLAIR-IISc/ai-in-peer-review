- Proposes BHCD: applies the Bayesian Rose Tree framework to network data using a stochastic blockmodel likelihood to discover hierarchical communities.  
- Main technical contribution is adapting Rose Tree marginal likelihood and greedy agglomerative search to relational data with efficient sufficient-statistic caching.  
- Derives recursive updates (Equation 13) enabling O(1) inner-loop merges and an overall O(n^2 log n) complexity claim.  
- Experiments show meaningful hierarchies on the Sampson dataset and large speedups over flat IRM on NIPS co-authorship data.  
- Major weakness: evaluation only compares to flat IRM rather than existing hierarchical network models, so claims about hierarchical performance are unvalidated.  
- Recommends adding comparisons to hierarchical baselines (hierarchical IRM, multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process).  
- Scalability claims need stronger empirical support on substantially larger graphs than NIPS-234 and 2864-node networks.  
- Clarity is generally good, but Section 3 (model recursion and likelihood derivation) is dense and needs a more detailed walkthrough or appendix.  
- Suggests clearer explanation of off-diagonal sufficient statistics and the dynamic-programming step for reproducibility.  
- Originality lies in a practical combination of existing components; conceptual novelty is limited since core parts are pre-existing.  
- Minor framing issue: calling the method “Bayesian nonparametric” is imprecise—Rose Trees are better described as model selection over discrete tree structures.  
- Overall: technically sound and fast, but significance depends on stronger hierarchical baselines and improved exposition.
