- Paper proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering for networks using a tree-constrained prior and a marginalised SBM likelihood computed by dynamic programming.  
- Algorithm uses local Bayes-factor merge scores and three merge types (join + two absorptions) with a sparse variant forbidding merges of disconnected subtrees.  
- Strength: clear methodological adaptation of Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data with an elegant recursion (Eq. 10) for p(D|T).  
- Strength: scalable greedy algorithm with cached sufficient statistics yielding O(n^2 log n) runtime (with restarts) and a sparse variant suited to real-world graphs.  
- Strength: practical local merge scores and flexible three-way merge that produces interpretable rose trees.  
- Strength: empirical results show fast link-prediction on NIPS-234, beating IRM on time–accuracy trade-offs and producing sensible hierarchies on Sampson and full NIPS.  
- Critical flaw: reported hyperparameters contradict modeling assumptions (text says α>β and δ<λ, but experiments use α=δ=1.0, β=λ=0.2), undermining the within-vs-between cluster prior claim.  
- Predictive complexity claim error: per-edge prediction stated as Θ(n) should be O(depth(T)); authors should correct and report typical depths.  
- Model expressiveness concern: homogeneous within-block density and single between-block prior limit handling of degree heterogeneity and disassortative/bipartite structures; no comparison to degree-corrected SBMs.  
- Positioning issue: insufficient relation to prior hierarchical network models (e.g., hierarchical random graphs, nested/multiscale SBMs); need clearer differences in modeling capacity and inference cost.  
- Evaluation breadth concern: main quantitative baseline is IRM; comparisons to LFRM/ILA are indirect—add fast baselines (modularity multilevel, degree-corrected SBM) and hierarchical IRM where feasible.  
- Experimental reporting gaps: no variance over restarts, unclear tie-breaking impact, and an undefined “BHCD global” variant shown in figures but not described.  
- Memory/caching omission: storing σIJ for all pairs is O(n^2) memory; need discussion of memory footprint and how sparse variant reduces it.  
- Computational claim gap: the DP cost p(D|T)=O(n+m) is stated but not justified; include a short proof or accounting of sufficient-statistic updates per edge.  
- Hyperparameter γ unexplored: fixed at 0.4 with no analysis of its effect on tree arity, depth, or branching; suggest Empirical Bayes or CV.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound overall but the hyperparameter inconsistency is a substantive flaw; empirical section supports speed but needs broader sensitivity and baselines.  
- Clarity: generally well written and derivations readable, but fix Θ(n) predictive complexity, define “BHCD global,” and complete references.  
- Originality: neat combination of BHC/rose trees with SBM marginalisation; three-way merge and sparse variant are practical contributions though related hierarchical models exist.  
- Significance: useful fast hierarchical community discovery tool and potential initializer for richer models if hyperparameter issues and evaluation breadth are addressed.  
- Suggestions: correct and analyze hyperparameters (sensitivity/Empirical Bayes), add degree-corrected/attribute-aware baselines, broaden hierarchical comparisons, report memory/wall-clock scaling and restart variability, and prove DP complexity.  
- Pros: principled Bayesian objective, efficient implementation, meaningful empirical speedups, and interpretable hierarchies.  
- Cons: hyperparameter inconsistency, limited baseline set and degree correction absence, and some methods/references clarity issues.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject / weak accept; fix hyperparameters and strengthen evaluation to make the submission strong.
