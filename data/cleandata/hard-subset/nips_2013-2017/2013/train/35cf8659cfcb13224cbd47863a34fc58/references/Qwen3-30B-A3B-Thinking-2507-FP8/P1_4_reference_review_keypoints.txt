- Summary: Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method extending stochastic blockmodels to relational data with an O(n² log n) inference algorithm and comparable predictive performance to IRM on multiple datasets.

- Strength — Computational efficiency: Presents an inference algorithm with O(n² log n) complexity, making hierarchical community detection feasible for much larger networks.

- Strength — Clear technical contribution: Provides a well-structured hierarchical Bayesian model and a novel caching scheme using conjugate priors and tree-structured partitions.

- Strength — Thorough experiments: Demonstrates comparable predictive performance to IRM while being two orders of magnitude faster on Sampson, NIPS-234, and full NIPS datasets.

- Strength — Well-written and organized: Paper is clear, detailed, and reproducible, with a well-motivated Algorithm 1.

- Strength — Practical implementation considerations: Offers variations (e.g., BHCD sparse) addressing real-world sparse networks.

- Weakness — Limited theoretical analysis: Lacks convergence proofs, statistical guarantees, or theoretical performance bounds.

- Weakness — Limited comparisons: Omits comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods (e.g., modularity-based or Girvan-Newman).

- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity not analyzed: Uses fixed hyperparameters without studying sensitivity or robustness.

- Weakness — Interpretability discussion weak: Provides insufficient evidence that discovered hierarchies are more interpretable or meaningful than alternatives.

- Overall assessment: High-quality, practically valuable contribution with significant computational gains despite some theoretical and comparative gaps.

- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance for NIPS based on novelty, soundness, and compelling empirical results.

- Pro — Clear technical contribution and efficiency gains: Novel algorithm with significant speedup and practical impact.

- Pro — Strong experimental validation: Convincing comparisons to IRM showing speed without loss of predictive quality.

- Pro — Novel O(n² log n) inference: Addresses a key scalability bottleneck in Bayesian hierarchical models.

- Pro — Good writing and presentation: Clear exposition and reproducible details.

- Con — Theoretical limitations: Needs deeper analysis of model properties and guarantees.

- Con — Missing non-Bayesian baselines: No broader contextual comparisons to classical hierarchical methods.

- Con — Hyperparameter analysis missing: No sensitivity study on chosen priors/hyperparameters.

- Con — Superficial interpretability analysis: Limited qualitative evidence for meaningful hierarchies.

- Final note: Paper would be strengthened by a brief theoretical discussion and comparisons to non-Bayesian methods, but these are improvements rather than prerequisites for acceptance.
