- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD) to infer nested community structure in networks using a dynamic-programming marginal likelihood and a greedy agglomerative algorithm.

- Theoretical strength: solid Bayesian formulation and an elegant DP derivation for marginal likelihood (Eq.10).

- Computational strength: efficient inference with claimed O(n² log n) complexity, enabling better scalability than existing Bayesian nonparametric methods.

- Empirical strength: qualitative recovery of meaningful hierarchies on Sampson’s monastery and comparable predictive performance on a NIPS co-authorship dataset.

- Weakness: lacks concrete runtime comparisons and empirical timing data to substantiate the “two orders of magnitude faster than IRM” claim.

- Weakness: limited comparison to recent scalable methods (e.g., Mondrian process) and other state-of-the-art approaches.

- Weakness: evaluation focuses on likelihood and efficiency but provides insufficient evidence that discovered hierarchies are qualitatively better or more interpretable than flat methods.

- Weakness: inadequate discussion of limitations—handling of overlapping communities, directed/multi-type edges, and sensitivity to hyperparameters is not addressed.

- Weakness: empirical results lack statistical significance testing to validate performance differences.

- Clarity strength: well-organized paper with clear mathematical derivations and helpful figures.

- Clarity weakness: Algorithm 1 and the three merger types (joining, absorption) are confusing and need clearer explanation or diagrams.

- Clarity weakness: update rules for sufficient statistics (Eq.13) are presented without sufficient justification or explanation.

- Clarity weakness: unclear mapping between tree nodes and communities at intermediate hierarchy levels; interpretation guidance is missing.

- Originality strength: novel extension of DP inference to relational data and a greedy Bayesian approach for hierarchical community detection.

- Originality weakness: contribution is somewhat incremental relative to prior Bayesian hierarchical clustering work and similar hierarchical SBMs.

- Originality weakness: mischaracterizes related work by overstating scalability issues and omitting comparisons to scalable Bayesian methods like the Mondrian process.

- Significance strength: addresses a real scalability bottleneck in Bayesian community detection and has practical utility for larger networks.

- Significance weakness: performance claims are based on limited datasets and the work does not demonstrate broader impact or clear advantages of hierarchical over flat structures.

- Overall assessment: theoretically sound and computationally useful, but empirical validation is limited and the contribution feels incremental without stronger comparisons and evaluations.

- Recommendation: weak accept conditioned on revisions to strengthen empirical evidence and clarify distinctions from prior work.

- Suggested improvements: add direct comparison to Mondrian, provide concrete runtimes, expand experiments and significance testing, clarify tree-to-community interpretation, discuss limitations and extensions (overlap, directed, edge types), provide hyperparameter guidance and sensitivity analysis, and clarify merger types in Algorithm 1.
