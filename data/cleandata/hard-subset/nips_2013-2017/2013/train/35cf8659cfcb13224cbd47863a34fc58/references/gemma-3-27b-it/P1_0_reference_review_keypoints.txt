- Proposes BHCD, a novel greedy Bayesian method for hierarchical community discovery that extends Bayesian greedy clustering to relational data.  
- Overall assessment: sound idea, well-written, and promising; reviewer leans Accept with minor revisions.  
- Major advantage: significant speedup (≈ two orders of magnitude vs. a single IRM iteration) and favorable complexity O(n^2 log n) or O(n + m) for sparse graphs.  
- Provides a probabilistic foundation allowing uncertainty quantification versus purely greedy methods.  
- Methodology is clear and intuitive: hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels with recursive marginal likelihood computation.  
- Practical implementation includes caching and a sparse-targeting variant to improve efficiency.  
- Experiments qualitatively recover known structure (Sampson) and show competitive performance on NIPS co-authorship with large speed gains.  
- Limited experimental comparisons: lacks quantitative benchmarking against other Bayesian nonparametric methods (e.g., LFRM, ILA) across more datasets.  
- No hyperparameter sensitivity analysis; impact of α, β, δ, λ is not explored.  
- Results lack statistical significance testing (no p-values or confidence intervals) to validate performance differences.  
- Implementation details under-specified: caching data structures and justification for number of restarts R need clarification for reproducibility.  
- Scalability concern: O(n^2 log n) may still be limiting for truly massive graphs (millions+ nodes); future optimizations should be discussed.  
- Originality/significance: practical and useful adaptation of existing techniques rather than a theoretical breakthrough, with moderate but meaningful impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions requesting expanded experiments, hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, statistical tests, more implementation detail, and discussion of scalability limits.
