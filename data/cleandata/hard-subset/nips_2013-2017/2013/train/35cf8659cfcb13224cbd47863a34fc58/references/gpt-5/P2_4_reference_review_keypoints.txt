- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a greedy Bayesian agglomerative method for inferring hierarchical community structure in networks.
- Models networks as a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a tree-induced set of tree-consistent partitions and conjugate beta–Bernoulli f/g likelihoods.
- Computes marginal likelihood given a tree via dynamic programming and scores merges using Bayes factors with cached sufficient statistics for O(1) local updates.
- Uses a bottom-up agglomerative search (join and absorb merges) inspired by BRT/BHC, achieving overall O(n^2 log n) complexity and a sparse variant that forbids zero-edge merges.
- Presents a simple, linear-in-depth predictive scheme mixing within- and between-cluster posteriors.
- Strength: clear probabilistic model separating within- and between-community likelihoods with exact marginalization over tree-consistent partitions.
- Strength: efficient, well-engineered inference with practical caching and explained complexity/implementation details.
- Strength: fast prediction procedure and competitive AUC/log-predictive performance versus IRM, with much lower wall-clock time.
- Strength: empirical demonstrations on small (Sampson) and medium (NIPS co-authorship) networks and a qualitative large-tree visualization.
- Strength: provides sensible practical variants (sparse BHCD; binary-only merges) and discusses their effects.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—largely an adaptation of BRT/BHC to relational data with blockmodel f/g and caching rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Weakness: limited experimental comparisons—missing direct, matched evaluations against HRG, degree-corrected/hierarchical SBMs, and no side-by-side runtime/memory matches for LFRM/ILA.
- Weakness: hyperparameters are fixed (α,β,δ,λ,γ) without sensitivity analysis or hyperparameter learning; strong priors may bias behavior and comparisons.
- Weakness: sparse BHCD heuristic breaks the generative model and introduces a model–inference mismatch without theoretical justification.
- Weakness: reproducibility concerns—missing implementation details (tie-breaking heap updates, memory complexity, code availability).
- Weakness: minor clarity issues and inconsistencies (predictive complexity described as linear in depth then as Θ(n); typos/dangling references).
- Assessment—Quality: technically sound marginal likelihood and merge scoring, plausible runtime claims, but empirical scope is modest and sparse heuristic departs from model.
- Assessment—Clarity: generally clear equations and recursion, but needs clearer contrast to BRT/BHC and hierarchical random graph models and to resolve complexity inconsistency.
- Assessment—Originality: moderate—thoughtful adaptation of existing machinery to blockmodels with engineering contributions.
- Assessment—Significance: practical and useful scalable Bayesian method for hierarchical communities, but contribution is incremental.
- Suggestions: add direct comparisons to HRG and degree-corrected/hierarchical SBMs and report wall-clock, AUC, and calibration.
- Suggestions: provide hyperparameter sensitivity analyses or empirical Bayes/MAP-II for key parameters and discuss identifiability across edge densities.
- Suggestions: clarify/justify the sparse constraint theoretically or present it as a post-hoc speed heuristic with an ablation study on its effect.
- Suggestions: expand experiments to additional benchmark networks, include variance over restarts and tie-breaking effects.
- Suggestions: release code and detail memory usage, exact tie-breaking policy, and heap invalidation strategy.
- Pros: simple, fast, interpretable Bayesian hierarchical community model with exact marginalization and efficient dynamic programming.
- Cons: incremental novelty, missing key baselines, fixed hyperparameters, heuristic sparse variant departs from generative story, limited experimental breadth.
- Recommendation: borderline accept—useful, practical adaptation with good engineering and speed advantages but needs stronger comparisons and hyperparameter/sensitivity work.
