- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian hierarchical extension of stochastic blockmodels that discovers nested community trees using a greedy inference algorithm.
- Key technical novelty: dynamic programming with caching enabling O(1) updates per merge and overall worst-case runtime O(n² log n).
- Positions BHCD between greedy clustering and fully Bayesian methods, combining computational efficiency with probabilistic rigor.
- Empirically, BHCD matches IRM predictive performance on NIPS co-authorship data while being ~100× faster.
- Qualitative results (e.g., Sampson's monastery) show meaningful hierarchical community structures discovered by BHCD.
- Paper is clearly written with effective figures and precise mathematical exposition.
- Practical implementation details are addressed, including a sparse variant and tie-breaking handling.
- Comparative analysis with Bayesian baselines is thorough and includes a helpful complexity table.
- Lacks deeper theoretical analysis such as convergence proofs or guarantees on greedy solution quality.
- Missing comparisons with state-of-the-art non-Bayesian hierarchical methods to establish relative advantages.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is underexplored; authors only report one working setting without systematic analysis.
- Limited interpretability: paper shows hierarchies but provides little qualitative explanation for their structure.
- Insufficient guidance on when to use BHCD vs. BHCD sparse and the trade-offs between variants.
- Suggestion: add theoretical justification or analysis of the greedy procedure to explain its effectiveness and failure modes.
- Suggestion: include at least one non-Bayesian hierarchical method comparison to strengthen empirical claims.
- Suggestion: run hyperparameter sensitivity experiments, especially for γ which controls tree structure.
- Suggestion: improve Figure 5 caption to specify which part of the hierarchy is shown and why it was chosen.
- Suggestion: clarify the discrepancy between per-likelihood O(n+m) computation and overall O(n² log n) algorithmic complexity.
- Overall assessment: significant, well-motivated contribution with impressive speedups and practical applicability.
- Recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses despite limited theory and some experimental gaps.
