The paper proposes a Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD) method for uncovering community structure in social networks. The core idea is to utilize a greedy algorithm combined with a Bayesian nonparametric framework based on stochastic blockmodels to infer a hierarchical clustering. The method aims to address the scalability issues of existing Bayesian approaches, particularly the IRM, while providing a probabilistic account of the discovered structure.

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear introduction of the problem and related work, followed by detailed descriptions of the stochastic blockmodel, the proposed Bayesian hierarchical model, the inference algorithm, and experimental results. The mathematical notation is generally clear, and the explanation of the model is easy to follow. The connection to existing literature, including the IRM and other Bayesian hierarchical models, is explicitly stated, demonstrating the authors' awareness of the field.

**Strengths:**

*   **Scalability:** The authors successfully address the scalability issues of existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods. The reported O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) complexity, along with the experimental results demonstrating a significant speedup compared to IRM, is a major strength. The algorithm’s runtime is independent of the number of communities, which is a substantial advantage.
*   **Probabilistic Framework:** The Bayesian formulation provides a principled way to model uncertainty and perform inference, offering a more complete picture than deterministic greedy approaches.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method. The inclusion of a detailed explanation of the inference algorithm and the connection to existing literature is valuable.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship data demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The comparison with IRM provides a clear benchmark, highlighting the speed advantage of BHCD.
*   **Variations & Insights:** The investigation of variations like BHCD sparse and binary tree restrictions provide useful insights into the method’s behavior and potential optimizations.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper lacks a discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity. The hyperparameters used in the experiments (α, β, λ, γ) are fixed without justification or exploration of their impact on the results. A sensitivity analysis would strengthen the findings.
*   **Limited Scope of Comparison:** While the comparison with IRM is valuable, comparing against more recent and relevant community detection algorithms (beyond those mentioned in the related work) would further contextualize the contributions of BHCD. For example, comparisons against Louvain or label propagation algorithms (though not Bayesian) would be insightful regarding speed and quality.
*   **Predictive Performance Discussion:** The discussion of predictive performance could be more detailed. While the results show BHCD performing comparably to LFRM and better than IRM, a more nuanced analysis of the types of predictions where BHCD excels or struggles would be beneficial.
*   **Figure Clarity:** Some of the figures, especially Figure 1, could benefit from clearer labeling and visual clarity to better illustrate the concepts being presented.
* **Computational Complexity Clarification:** While O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) is mentioned, the paper also states that the likelihood can be computed in O(n+m). The interplay between these complexities and under what network conditions each dominates is not fully articulated.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant challenge in community detection: scalability of Bayesian hierarchical models.
*   The proposed method achieves a significant speedup compared to existing Bayesian approaches without sacrificing predictive performance.
*   The paper is well-written, clearly presented, and provides a solid empirical validation of the proposed method.
*   The exploration of variations and the insights gained contribute to a better understanding of the algorithm's behavior.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is a significant omission.
*   The comparison against a limited set of baselines weakens the assessment of the method's novelty and significance.
*   The discussion of predictive performance could be more in-depth and nuanced.



**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian hierarchical community detection. The proposed method addresses a critical challenge in scalability while maintaining a probabilistic framework. While some weaknesses exist, the strengths of the paper, particularly its speed and clear presentation, outweigh the concerns. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically addressing the hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and expanding the comparison to include more recent community detection algorithms. The authors should also clarify the interplay of different computational complexities.
