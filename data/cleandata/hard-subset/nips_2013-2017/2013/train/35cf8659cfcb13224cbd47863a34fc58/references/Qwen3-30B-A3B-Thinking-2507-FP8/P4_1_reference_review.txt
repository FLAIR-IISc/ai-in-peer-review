

# Review: Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

## Summary of Main Ideas

This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a method for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors extend Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering to relational data, building upon the stochastic blockmodel and previous Bayesian nonparametric methods. BHCD organizes communities into a nested hierarchy T, where each vertex belongs to all communities along the path from the root to the leaf containing it. The key innovation is an efficient inference algorithm that computes the marginal likelihood through dynamic programming, achieving O(n² log n) time complexity, which is two orders of magnitude faster than a single iteration of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). The authors demonstrate BHCD's effectiveness on Sampson's monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and the full NIPS dataset.

The work relates to previous NIPS contributions including the stochastic blockmodel (Holland et al., 1983), the Infinite Relational Model (Kemp et al., 2006), and Bayesian hierarchical clustering (Heller & Ghahramani, 2005). It addresses limitations of prior Bayesian hierarchical approaches, which either require known hierarchy depth (e.g., [9, 10]) or scale poorly with network size (e.g., [8, 11]).

## Strengths

**Technical Soundness**: The paper presents a well-structured probabilistic model with clear mathematical formulations. The dynamic programming approach for computing the marginal likelihood (Equation 10) is elegant and correctly derived. The inference algorithm (Algorithm 1) is carefully designed with efficient caching of sufficient statistics, enabling O(1) inner loop operations. The theoretical analysis of time complexity (O(n² log n)) is sound and well-supported.

**Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical notation is consistent throughout, and the figures effectively illustrate key concepts like the tree structure and experimental results. The authors provide sufficient detail for an expert reader to reproduce the method, including clear explanations of the Bayes factor score (Equation 12) and predictive distribution (Equation 14).

**Significance**: BHCD addresses a practical problem in network analysis: scaling Bayesian hierarchical community detection to larger networks while maintaining reasonable predictive performance. The empirical results demonstrate that BHCD achieves comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art methods while being significantly faster (up to 1000x faster than IRM on the NIPS-234 dataset), making it suitable for real-world applications.

**Novelty**: The paper makes a clear contribution by introducing an efficient inference algorithm for hierarchical community discovery that does not depend on the number of communities (unlike previous Bayesian methods) and has a well-defined time complexity. The extension of Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data is a meaningful step beyond Heller & Ghahramani (2005) and Blundell et al. (2009).

## Weaknesses

**Incremental Novelty**: While technically sound, the paper's contribution appears somewhat incremental. The method builds directly on Heller & Ghahramani (2005) and Blundell et al. (2009), extending their framework to relational data. The paper could better distinguish its contribution from recent work on hierarchical community detection, such as Herlau et al. (2012).

**Limited Experimental Evaluation**: The empirical evaluation is somewhat limited to three datasets. The comparison with IRM focuses primarily on predictive performance, but the paper does not thoroughly compare the hierarchical structures discovered by BHCD versus IRM or other methods. The interpretability of the hierarchical structures (a key aspect of hierarchical community detection) is not adequately addressed.

**Theoretical Justification**: The claim that BHCD is "two orders of magnitude faster than a single iteration of the IRM" needs stronger empirical support across different network sizes. The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the approximation error introduced by the greedy approach, which could help understand the method's limitations.

**Sparse Network Treatment**: While the paper introduces BHCD sparse for sparse networks, it does not provide a thorough theoretical analysis of its performance guarantees for sparse graphs, which would be valuable given the practical importance of sparse networks.

## Pro/Con Acceptance

**Pro Acceptance**:
- Well-executed technical contribution with sound theoretical foundation
- Clear demonstration of significant computational advantages over state-of-the-art methods
- Excellent organization and writing quality
- Addresses a meaningful problem in network analysis with practical utility
- Provides sufficient detail for reproducibility

**Con Acceptance**:
- Novelty appears incremental compared to prior work on Bayesian hierarchical clustering
- Limited experimental evaluation with insufficient comparison of hierarchical structures
- Claims about computational speed require stronger empirical support
- Insufficient discussion of approximation error introduced by greedy approach
- Sparse network treatment lacks theoretical analysis

## Overall Assessment

This is a technically sound paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of community detection by providing an efficient Bayesian approach to hierarchical community discovery. The method is well-motivated, well-executed, and demonstrates clear practical advantages over existing approaches. While the novelty is somewhat incremental, the paper's strong technical execution, clear writing, and significant computational advantages make it a strong candidate for NIPS. The authors could strengthen their case by providing more comprehensive experimental comparisons and theoretical analysis of the greedy approximation. Nevertheless, the paper represents a solid contribution to the field that advances the state of the art in scalable Bayesian hierarchical community detection.
