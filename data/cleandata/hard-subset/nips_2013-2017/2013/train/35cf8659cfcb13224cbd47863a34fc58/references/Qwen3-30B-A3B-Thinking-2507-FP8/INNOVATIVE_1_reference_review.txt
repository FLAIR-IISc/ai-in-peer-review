

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors address a significant gap in the literature: while hierarchical community structure is common in real-world networks, most Bayesian approaches to community detection are limited to flat structures or suffer from poor scalability. BHCD provides a computationally efficient Bayesian method for hierarchical community discovery, with a runtime of O(n² log n) - two orders of magnitude faster than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while maintaining comparable predictive performance.

The key innovation lies in a greedy agglomerative model selection procedure that leverages a novel caching scheme for sufficient statistics. This allows BHCD to efficiently compute the marginal likelihood of hierarchical partitions without the prohibitive computational costs of existing Bayesian approaches. The paper builds on the stochastic blockmodel and Bayesian hierarchical clustering literature [13,14], but makes a significant leap by providing a method that is both theoretically grounded and practically scalable.

The authors demonstrate BHCD's effectiveness on Sampson's monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and the full NIPS dataset. Their results show that BHCD can discover meaningful hierarchical structures while achieving comparable predictive performance to more computationally expensive methods like IRM, but at a fraction of the cost. The sparse variant of BHCD (BHCD sparse) is particularly noteworthy for its speed on sparse networks.

**Originality (Strength):** This paper makes a genuinely novel contribution to the field. While hierarchical community detection has been studied before [3-4], and Bayesian approaches exist [5-12], BHCD is the first to provide an efficient Bayesian hierarchical clustering method that scales well to large networks. The authors clearly articulate how their approach differs from previous work, particularly in addressing the computational limitations of existing Bayesian hierarchical methods.

**Significance (Strength):** The computational efficiency of BHCD (O(n² log n) vs. O(n²K²) for IRM) is significant for large-scale network analysis. The paper demonstrates this with compelling experiments showing BHCD can make reasonable predictions before IRM completes a single Gibbs scan. For practitioners dealing with large networks, this represents a substantial practical advantage.

**Quality (Moderate Strength):** The theoretical foundation is solid, with clear mathematical formulation of the hierarchical mixture model and the greedy inference procedure. The algorithm is well-described (Algorithm 1) and the experimental results support the claims. However, the paper could have provided more theoretical justification for why the greedy approach works well for hierarchical community discovery, rather than simply demonstrating empirical success.

**Clarity (Strength):** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The figures (especially Figure 1 showing the tree structure) effectively illustrate key concepts. The mathematical notation is consistent and the pseudocode is clear.

**Weaknesses:** The paper acknowledges that BHCD is a greedy approximation rather than full Bayesian inference, which could potentially miss globally optimal solutions. While this is a common trade-off, the paper could have more thoroughly discussed the potential limitations of the greedy approach compared to full Bayesian methods. The tie-breaking strategy (random choice) could also be more thoroughly justified.

**Comparison to Prior Work:** The paper effectively situates its contribution within the literature, clearly differentiating BHCD from previous hierarchical community detection methods. The comparison to IRM and other Bayesian methods in Table 1 is particularly valuable for demonstrating the computational advantage.

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a bold and novel approach to a significant problem in network analysis. While not perfect (it's a greedy approximation rather than full Bayesian inference), the computational advantages are substantial and the method is well-grounded in Bayesian statistics. The authors have clearly identified a gap in the literature and made a meaningful contribution to filling it. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and demonstrates significant practical value.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold approaches, I find this paper's contribution to be highly significant. It opens up new possibilities for applying Bayesian hierarchical community detection to large-scale networks, which was previously computationally intractable. While the methodological approach is an approximation, the paper successfully demonstrates its effectiveness on real-world data and provides a clear path forward for future work.

**Recommendation:** Accept. This paper makes a strong contribution to the field with a novel, efficient approach to hierarchical community discovery that is both theoretically grounded and practically valuable. The computational speedup (two orders of magnitude over IRM) while maintaining comparable performance is particularly compelling for the NIPS community. The paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and provides significant value to the field of network analysis.
