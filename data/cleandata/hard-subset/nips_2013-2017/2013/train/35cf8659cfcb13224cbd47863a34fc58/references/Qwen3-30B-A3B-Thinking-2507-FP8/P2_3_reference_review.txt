

# Review: Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

## Summary
This paper proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel approach for inferring hierarchical community structure in social networks. BHCD extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data, building upon stochastic blockmodels with a hierarchical mixture structure. The key innovation is an efficient greedy inference algorithm that computes marginal likelihoods using dynamic programming, resulting in O(n² log n) time complexity—two orders of magnitude faster than existing Bayesian nonparametric methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). The paper demonstrates BHCD's effectiveness on Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship network, showing it achieves comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art methods while being significantly faster.

## Quality
The paper is technically sound with rigorous mathematical derivations. The authors clearly define the hierarchical stochastic blockmodel, derive the marginal likelihood calculation using dynamic programming (Equation 10), and establish the algorithm's computational complexity. The theoretical analysis is well-supported by empirical results across multiple datasets. The paper properly acknowledges limitations (e.g., BHCD may merge disconnected communities) and addresses them with the BHCD sparse variant. The experimental design is robust, including multiple variants of BHCD (binary and sparse versions) and comparisons to relevant baselines (IRM, LFRM, ILA). The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the trade-offs between computational efficiency and predictive performance.

## Clarity
The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly contextualizes the problem and differentiates BHCD from prior work. Mathematical notation is consistent and well-explained, with sufficient detail for replication. The algorithm description (Algorithm 1) is clear and pseudocode is well-structured. Figures 3-5 effectively visualize the hierarchical community structure and performance comparisons. The paper's flow from problem statement to model to algorithm to evaluation is logical and easy to follow. Minor improvements could include a more detailed explanation of the tie-breaking mechanism in Section 4.1, but this does not significantly impact understanding.

## Originality
BHCD makes a meaningful contribution to the literature by addressing a key limitation of existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods: computational scalability. While previous work (e.g., [5, 6, 7, 8, 12]) has explored Bayesian hierarchical models, these approaches typically suffer from poor scalability (O(n²K²) or worse). BHCD's O(n² log n) complexity with no dependence on the number of communities represents a significant advancement. The paper properly situates its contribution within the literature, clearly differentiating from related work on the Mondrian process [12] and hierarchical IRM [8]. The authors successfully combine Bayesian nonparametric methods with efficient greedy inference to create a practical solution for real-world networks.

## Significance
This work has substantial significance for network science and machine learning communities. Hierarchical community detection is a fundamental problem with applications in social network analysis, biology, and information retrieval. The computational advantage of BHCD makes hierarchical community discovery feasible for larger networks where previous Bayesian methods would be prohibitively expensive. The paper demonstrates that BHCD achieves comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art methods (like ILA) while being orders of magnitude faster—this practical advantage is highly valuable for practitioners. The method's ability to produce interpretable hierarchical structures (e.g., the NIPS co-authorship hierarchy in Figure 5) adds further practical utility.

## Strengths
- Clear theoretical contribution with a novel inference algorithm
- Strong empirical validation across multiple datasets
- Excellent computational efficiency (O(n² log n) complexity)
- Practical utility for real-world network analysis
- Well-organized and clearly written
- Comprehensive comparison to relevant baselines

## Weaknesses
- Limited exploration of theoretical guarantees for the greedy approach (e.g., how close it gets to the optimal solution)
- The assumption of undirected, symmetric networks without self-edges limits applicability to some network types
- Hyperparameter sensitivity analysis could be more thorough
- Could include more recent hierarchical community detection methods for comparison

## Recommendation
This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of network analysis. It addresses a meaningful problem with a technically sound solution that offers both theoretical and practical advantages over existing methods. The clarity of presentation and thoroughness of evaluation make it suitable for NeurIPS. The computational efficiency gains are particularly noteworthy, as they enable hierarchical community detection on networks where previous Bayesian methods would be intractable.

I recommend **acceptance**. The paper meets all four review criteria at a high level: it's technically sound (Quality), well-written (Clarity), makes a meaningful contribution (Originality), and solves a problem with practical significance (Significance). The weaknesses noted are minor and don't detract from the paper's overall contribution. The authors have successfully demonstrated that BHCD is both theoretically grounded and practically useful, advancing the state of the art in hierarchical community discovery.
