- BHCD: introduces a Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community discovery using a tree-based extension of stochastic blockmodels.
- Key innovation: an efficient greedy learning algorithm with O(n² log n) time complexity, roughly two orders of magnitude faster than the IRM.
- Model derivation: hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels with marginal likelihood computed via dynamic programming.
- Algorithmic techniques: novel caching scheme and use of a heap for efficient merge selection to enable scalability.
- Empirical results: on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship, BHCD finds meaningful hierarchies and matches IRM predictive performance.
- Sparse variant: BHCD sparse mitigates merging of disconnected communities and improves performance on sparse networks.
- Technical quality: mathematically sound formulations, clear derivations, and thorough complexity analysis.
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-written, with clear notation, easy-to-follow pseudocode, helpful visualizations, and implementation details.
- Originality: fills a gap in Bayesian hierarchical community discovery with a novel greedy DP algorithm distinct from IRM and Mondrian approaches.
- Significance: substantial practical impact by making hierarchical Bayesian community detection feasible for large-scale networks.
- Limitation 1: limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Limitation 2: lacks comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods.
- Overall judgment: strong submission—technically solid, practically valuable—recommend accept.
