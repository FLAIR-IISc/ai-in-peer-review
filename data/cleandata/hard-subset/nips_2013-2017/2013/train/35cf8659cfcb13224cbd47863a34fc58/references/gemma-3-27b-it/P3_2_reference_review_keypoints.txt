- Proposes BHCD: a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method combining Bayesian hierarchical clustering and stochastic blockmodels.
- Claims comparable performance to IRM with significantly faster runtimes (≈100× faster per iteration) and worst-case runtime independent of number of communities (O(n^2 log n)).
- Inference uses dynamic programming and caching to achieve computational efficiency.
- Introduces BHCD sparse tailored for sparse real-world networks.
- Evaluated on Sampson’s monastery, a NIPS co-authorship subset, and the full NIPS network showing qualitative and scaling results.
- Positions BHCD relative to flat community detection, greedy hierarchical clustering, and Bayesian nonparametrics, discussing trade-offs (conjugate vs non-conjugate priors).
- Strength: major computational speedup via clever caching and greedy DP.
- Strength: operates within a full probabilistic Bayesian framework enabling predictions.
- Strength: clear model presentation using a tree-based prior on partitions.
- Strength: empirical validation demonstrates recovery of known structures and competitive quantitative performance.
- Strength: practical consideration for sparsity through BHCD sparse.
- Weakness: lacks thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis for (α, β, δ, λ, γ).
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to faster non-Bayesian hierarchical methods (e.g., Leiden).
- Weakness: predictive performance not clearly superior to LFRM, suggesting room for likelihood/prior improvements.
- Weakness: greedy tree search risks local optima; restarts may be insufficient—alternative search strategies could help.
- Weakness: visualization and interpretation of very large hierarchies is challenging and under-addressed.
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial speed improvement, solid probabilistic grounding, reasonable benchmarks, practical sparse variant.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing hyperparameter study, limited baselines, not state-of-the-art predictive, greedy search limitations.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable computational contribution; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
