

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a method for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors build upon Bayesian approaches to hierarchical clustering and extend them to relational data using a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels. The key contribution is an efficient greedy inference algorithm with O(n² log n) time complexity, significantly faster than existing Bayesian nonparametric methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM), which scales poorly with network size. The method is evaluated on Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship dataset, demonstrating comparable predictive performance to existing methods while being two orders of magnitude faster.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with clear theoretical derivations of the model and inference algorithm. The authors provide a rigorous mathematical foundation for their approach, including the marginal likelihood calculation for tree-structured partitions and the dynamic programming scheme for efficient inference. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing BHCD to relevant baselines (IRM, LFRM, ILA) on multiple datasets. The authors address computational efficiency concerns by introducing a caching scheme that enables O(n² log n) complexity. However, the paper would benefit from more detailed runtime analysis on specific hardware configurations to substantiate the "two orders of magnitude faster" claim. The experimental design is appropriate, using standard metrics (log predictive probability, accuracy, AUC) for link prediction tasks.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and well-organized. The mathematical notation is consistently defined, and the algorithm is clearly presented (Algorithm 1). The figures effectively illustrate the hierarchical structure and experimental results. The authors provide sufficient detail for reproducibility, including model hyperparameters and implementation specifics. The connection between the proposed method and previous work is well-explained, with clear distinctions made from related approaches like the IRM and Mondrian process.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear contribution to the field by addressing the computational limitations of existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods. While hierarchical clustering has been studied before, the authors' specific approach to combining Bayesian methods with greedy hierarchical clustering for relational data represents a novel contribution. The caching scheme enabling efficient inference is particularly noteworthy. The paper properly situates its work within the literature, citing relevant NIPS and other conference papers on hierarchical community detection (e.g., [13, 14] for Bayesian hierarchical clustering, [1, 2] for stochastic blockmodels, [5-12] for Bayesian relational models). The method differs from previous work by being both Bayesian and computationally efficient, avoiding the computational bottlenecks of MCMC-based approaches.

**Significance:** The problem of hierarchical community discovery is important for network analysis, with applications in social sciences, biology, and information systems. The computational efficiency of BHCD (O(n² log n) complexity) makes it suitable for large networks, addressing a practical limitation of previous Bayesian approaches. The method achieves comparable predictive performance to existing methods while being significantly faster, making it valuable for real-world applications. The paper demonstrates this through thorough experiments on multiple datasets, showing that BHCD sparse variants perform particularly well on sparse networks like the NIPS co-authorship data.

**Strengths:**
- Novel efficient inference algorithm with strong theoretical grounding
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation on multiple datasets
- Clear connection to and differentiation from previous work
- Well-structured and clearly written with appropriate mathematical detail
- Demonstrated significant speed improvements over existing methods

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and principled methods for setting them
- The "two orders of magnitude faster" claim would be strengthened with more detailed runtime comparisons
- Limited discussion of method limitations in specific network structures
- Comparison to more recent methods beyond those cited in the paper (e.g., recent advances in scalable community detection)

**Arguments for acceptance:** BHCD makes a significant contribution to the field by providing a computationally efficient Bayesian approach to hierarchical community discovery. The method is theoretically sound, well-validated through experiments, and addresses a practical limitation of existing approaches. The paper is clearly written and makes a strong case for the novelty and significance of the work.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The paper would benefit from more detailed runtime analysis to substantiate the speed claims, and a more thorough discussion of the method's limitations in different network structures. However, these are minor concerns given the paper's overall quality and contribution.

The paper addresses a significant problem in network analysis with a technically sound and novel approach that offers substantial computational advantages over existing methods. The work is well-positioned within the literature and makes a clear contribution to the field. The method is both theoretically grounded and practically useful, with clear potential for adoption in real-world applications. I recommend acceptance at NIPS.
