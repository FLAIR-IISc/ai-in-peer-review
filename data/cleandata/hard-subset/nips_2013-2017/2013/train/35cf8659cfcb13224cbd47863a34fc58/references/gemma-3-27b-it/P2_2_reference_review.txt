## Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces a novel greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. The approach builds upon Bayesian methods for greedy hierarchical clustering, extending them to relational data. The authors propose a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels and develop an efficient inference scheme that leverages dynamic programming for marginal likelihood calculation. They demonstrate the method's speed advantage – two orders of magnitude faster than a single IRM iteration – and its quadratic worst-case runtime, independent of the number of communities.  The paper includes experiments on Sampson’s monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and the full NIPS co-authorship network, showcasing both qualitative results and predictive performance.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing landscape of community detection. It acknowledges the limitations of flat community models (SBM, mixed-membership SBM) and the computational cost of fully Bayesian nonparametric approaches like IRM and Mondrian process. It clearly distinguishes itself by offering a greedy, scalable Bayesian method that balances probabilistic rigor with computational efficiency.  The connection to previous Bayesian greedy hierarchical clustering work ([13, 14]) is also appropriately highlighted.

**Strengths:**

* **Computational Efficiency:** The core strength of this work is its efficiency. The claimed speedup over IRM is substantial and highly valuable for analyzing large networks.  The O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) runtime is a significant improvement over many existing Bayesian methods.  The caching scheme described appears to be a key enabler of this efficiency.
* **Probabilistic Framework:**  The method provides a fully probabilistic account of the data, which allows for uncertainty quantification and principled model comparison.  Unlike purely greedy approaches, it explicitly models the data-generating process.
* **Scalability:** The independence of runtime from the number of communities is a major advantage, making it suitable for networks with complex hierarchical structures.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on diverse datasets (Sampson's network, NIPS) demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  The comparison to IRM on the NIPS dataset provides a clear demonstration of the speed-accuracy tradeoff.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized.  The mathematical derivations are reasonably clear, and the algorithmic description is straightforward. The inclusion of algorithmic pseudocode is very helpful.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The experiments fix hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) without extensive sensitivity analysis.  The impact of these parameters on the resulting community structure and predictive performance is not fully explored.
* **Limited Comparison to Non-Bayesian Methods:** While comparison to IRM is valuable, a comparison to more scalable non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods (e.g., spectral clustering-based approaches) would strengthen the evaluation.
* **Binary Tree Restriction:** The discussion of binary vs. non-binary tree variations feels a little underdeveloped.  The rationale for favoring non-binary trees in some cases could be clarified.
* **Predictive Evaluation Focus:** The emphasis on predictive accuracy, while important, somewhat overshadows the potential for qualitative interpretation of the discovered hierarchical structure.  More detailed analysis of the discovered communities in the NIPS network, beyond simply presenting the tree, would be beneficial.
* **Theoretical Justification for Score:** The motivation behind the Bayes factor score (12) as focusing on “local” connectivity is reasonable, but a more formal justification or analysis of its properties would be desirable.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** Technically sound and well-supported. The dynamic programming approach for marginal likelihood calculation seems well-justified. However, the lack of hyperparameter sensitivity analysis weakens the claims of robustness.
* **Clarity:**  Mostly clear.  The notation can be dense at times, but the explanations are generally adequate.
* **Originality:**  The combination of Bayesian hierarchical clustering with a greedy inference scheme and the specific optimization for relational data are novel.
* **Significance:** The potential for fast and scalable Bayesian community detection is significant.  The method could be valuable for analyzing large-scale social networks and other relational data.



**Arguments for/against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Significant improvement in computational efficiency compared to existing Bayesian methods.
* Well-motivated and technically sound approach.
* Clear presentation and comprehensive experiments.
* Addresses an important problem in network analysis.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited hyperparameter analysis.
* Insufficient comparison to non-Bayesian baselines.
* Underdeveloped discussion of binary tree variations.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of community detection. The proposed BHCD method offers a compelling combination of probabilistic rigor and computational efficiency. While some weaknesses remain, they are not severe enough to warrant rejection. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing the following minor revisions:

1. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  Include a brief sensitivity analysis of the key hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) to demonstrate the robustness of the method.
2. **Baseline Comparison:** Add a comparison to one or two relevant non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods.
3. **Discussion of Tree Variation:** Elaborate on the tradeoffs between binary and non-binary trees and provide a more principled justification for the observed performance differences.
4. **Community Interpretation:** Provide a more in-depth analysis of the discovered communities in the NIPS network, going beyond simply presenting the hierarchical structure.
