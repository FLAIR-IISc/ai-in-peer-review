- Summary: Introduces BHCD, extending Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data with a greedy inference algorithm using dynamic programming and caching to achieve O(n² log n) complexity, yielding much faster inference with comparable predictive performance.

- Strength: Significant computational improvement for hierarchical community detection, enabled by a clever caching scheme (Equation 13) achieving O(n² log n).

- Strength: Empirically orders-of-magnitude faster than existing Bayesian nonparametrics (e.g., IRM) while maintaining similar predictive performance on real datasets.

- Strength: Well-situated in related work, clearly connecting to IRM, Bayesian hierarchical clustering, and stochastic blockmodels and articulating prior limitations.

- Weakness: Lacks theoretical analysis of statistical properties—no discussion of consistency, convergence rates, or guarantees for the greedy algorithm.

- Weakness: Comparisons are incomplete, focusing mainly on computational complexity and missing other hierarchical community methods (e.g., [3, 8, 11]) for contextual benchmarking.

- Weakness: Limited experimental validation—Sampson example lacks quantitative metrics and NIPS experiments do not analyze interpretability or hierarchical quality.

- Weakness: Ambiguous claims about finding the "most likely tree" without evidence that the greedy procedure reliably finds MAP rather than local optima; tie-breaking variability is unquantified.

- Quality (critical): Technically sound with strong empirical computational claims but weakened by limited theoretical/statistical validation.

- Clarity (critical): Well-written and organized; some sections (e.g., tree representation) could use more examples; algorithm description is clear.

- Originality (critical): Novel and distinctive inference algorithm/caching scheme; hierarchical model extension itself is less novel.

- Significance (critical): Practically significant due to enabling larger-scale hierarchical community detection, though not shown to produce superior hierarchical structures.

- Recommendation 1: Add a brief theoretical discussion of the greedy approach's statistical properties.

- Recommendation 2: Include empirical comparison with at least one other hierarchical community detection method beyond IRM.

- Recommendation 3: Provide more detailed quantitative analysis of the hierarchical structure found in the Sampson network.

- Final assessment: Strong submission recommended for acceptance with minor revisions — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
