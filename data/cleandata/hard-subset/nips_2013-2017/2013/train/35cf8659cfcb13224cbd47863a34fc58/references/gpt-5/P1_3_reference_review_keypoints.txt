- Proposes BHCD: a greedy Bayesian method that builds a tree-structured hierarchy and computes exact marginal likelihoods over tree-consistent partitions via dynamic programming.

- Likelihood uses an assortative Beta–Bernoulli stochastic blockmodel with different hyperparameters for within- vs between-cluster edges; tree prior arises from a stop/split generative process.

- Given a tree T, p(D|T) is computable in O(n + m) via a recursion; greedy agglomerative search with three merge types (join, absorb-left, absorb-right) learns T.

- Careful caching yields O(1) per candidate update and overall O(n^2 log n) runtime; variants include sparse and binary-only merges.

- Empirical evaluation: qualitative result on Sampson’s monastery, BHCD vs IRM on NIPS-234 showing much faster convergence with comparable predictive performance, and interpretable hierarchy on full NIPS 1–17 (2,864 nodes).

- Relates to IRM/hierarchical IRM, Bayesian Rose Trees, greedy modularity methods, and Mondrian process; trades modeling flexibility for speed and scalability.

- Strength: principled probabilistic model tying a tree prior to blockmodel likelihood with exact marginalization over tree-consistent partitions.

- Strength: elegant dynamic programming recursion for p(D|T) and practical greedy agglomerative inference with local Bayes factor scoring.

- Strength: strong focus on scalability with a careful caching scheme and favorable time complexity.

- Strength: empirical evidence that BHCD attains useful predictive performance orders of magnitude faster than an IRM implementation and yields interpretable hierarchies.

- Strength: sensible practical variants (sparse, binary) that improve applicability.

- Weakness: limited empirical evaluation (two datasets) and comparisons mainly to IRM; missing hierarchical random graph and strong non-Bayesian baselines (e.g., Louvain).

- Weakness: greedy local merge objective is heuristic with no analysis of approximation quality, stability, or sensitivity; multiple restarts needed.

- Weakness: assortative Beta-Bernoulli SBM may be brittle under degree heterogeneity or disassortative structure; no degree-correction or mixed-membership extension.

- Weakness: hyperparameters are fixed with no sensitivity analysis or hyperparameter learning (e.g., empirical Bayes).

- Weakness: minor clarity/consistency issues (predictive complexity statement, incomplete references, typos).

- Quality: technically sound recursion and sufficient-statistic updates; inference is transparent but lacks theoretical guarantees beyond restarts.

- Clarity: generally well-written and clear, though notation is dense and a worked example of the recursion would help.

- Originality: moderate novelty in adapting Bayesian Rose Trees to relational blockmodels with exact marginalization; useful combination.

- Significance: practically useful contribution offering fast, interpretable hierarchical community detection, trading some predictive performance for speed and interpretability.

- Suggestions: compare to hierarchical random graph models and multilevel Louvain, discuss/evaluate degree-corrected likelihoods, perform sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters or empirical Bayes.

- Suggestions: clarify memory complexity (σIJ storage O(n^2)), correct predictive complexity claim to O(tree depth), report variability across restarts and consider consensus trees, fix references/typos.

- Pros for acceptance: elegant formulation with exact marginalization, scalable inference, interpretable hierarchies, and useful predictive capabilities.

- Cons against acceptance: limited empirical breadth, greedy search without guarantees, simple (assortative, non-degree-corrected) modeling, and presentation issues.

- Recommendation: Weak accept (6) — useful, well-executed scalable method that would be stronger with broader comparisons and clarifications.
