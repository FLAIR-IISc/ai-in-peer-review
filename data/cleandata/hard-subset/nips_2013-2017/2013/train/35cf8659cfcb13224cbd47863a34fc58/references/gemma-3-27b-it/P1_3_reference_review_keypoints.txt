- Presents BHCD, a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method modeling communities as a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Key technical contributions: dynamic programming to compute marginal likelihoods and a caching strategy achieving O(n^2 log n) time complexity.  
- Empirical results show BHCD is much faster than IRM (≈ two orders of magnitude) and competitive on synthetic and real networks.  
- Situates work relative to flat models (SBM, IRM) and prior hierarchical Bayesian methods, extending ideas to relational data.  
- Comparison to related models (IRM, LFRM, ILA) helps contextualize strengths and limitations.  
- Strength: strong computational efficiency with an effective caching implementation.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian framework allowing uncertainty quantification and priors.  
- Strength: explicit hierarchical modeling yields richer community representations.  
- Strength: clear writing and accessible mathematical derivations.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation (Sampson, NIPS co-authorship) and discussion of practical variants like BHCD-sparse and tie-breaking.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter choices lack justification and no sensitivity analysis is provided.  
- Weakness: predictive performance is only comparable to LFRM and worse than ILA, raising accuracy vs. speed concerns.  
- Weakness: scalability claims are not empirically validated beyond networks of ~2,800 nodes.  
- Weakness: reliance on Beta-Bernoulli likelihood and priors is not well motivated; generalization to other families is cursory.  
- Weakness: some figures (notably Figure 5) need clearer captions and explanation of selection criteria.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant problem, offers large speed gains, principled Bayesian hierarchy, and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no demonstrable predictive superiority, missing hyperparameter sensitivity, and insufficient large-scale scalability evidence.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with significant computational advancement; weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, asking for hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, larger-scale scalability experiments, clearer prediction error analysis, and improved figure captions.
