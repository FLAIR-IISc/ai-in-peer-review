- Proposes BHCD: a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method for community detection in networks.  
- Extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data using stochastic blockmodels and a tree-based prior.  
- Introduces a sparse BHCD variant optimized for sparse graphs.  
- Claims computational complexity O(n^2 log n) with caching and empirical speedups over IRM.  
- Empirically comparable accuracy to IRM and LFRM, especially on larger datasets.  
- Demonstrates qualitative recovery of known communities (Sampson’s monastery) and quantitative results on NIPS co-authorship.  
- Builds on prior greedy and Bayesian clustering work (Heller & Ghahramani; Blundell et al.) and positions itself correctly in literature.  
- Methodology is theoretically sound with clear recursive likelihood computation and dynamic programming.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and equations are clearly presented.  
- Main strength: significant computational efficiency making Bayesian hierarchical approaches practical for larger networks.  
- Main weakness: no analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (α, β, δ, λ, γ) and robustness to their choices.  
- Limited baseline comparison: lacks evaluation against scalable non-Bayesian algorithms (e.g., Louvain, Leiden).  
- Predictive performance results lack details on the nature and density of held-out edges.  
- Complexity caveat: inference O(n^2 log n) vs. likelihood calculation O(n+m) may bottleneck extremely sparse networks.  
- Hierarchy summarization/filtering (rT condition) for visualization needs more justification.  
- Overall judgment: strong accept — significant contribution in scalable Bayesian hierarchical community detection with recommended minor revisions (sensitivity analysis, broader comparisons).
