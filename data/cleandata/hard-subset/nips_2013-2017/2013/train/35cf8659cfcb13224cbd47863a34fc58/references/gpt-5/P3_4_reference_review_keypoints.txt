- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method using a tree-induced partition prior and hierarchical Beta–Bernoulli blockmodel likelihood.
- Introduces an O(n + m) dynamic-programming recursion to compute p(D|T) exactly for a fixed tree T.
- Presents a greedy agglomerative search with three merge types and an O(1) local Bayes-factor score, yielding an overall O(n^2 log n) algorithm.
- Offers a sparse variant that forbids merges across components with no edges to speed inference on sparse graphs.
- Derives a recursive predictive distribution for fast link prediction and uses restarts to hedge against greedy optima.
- Builds on Bayesian rose trees and adapts hierarchical clustering ideas to relational blockmodels.
- Contrasts with IRM and hierarchical extensions by restricting partition space to tree-consistent partitions to enable exact marginalization and greedy search.
- Relates to HRG and nested SBM approaches, which are important missing baselines for empirical comparison.
- Strength: principled generative model with exact marginalization and efficient DP recursion.
- Strength: elegant, cached agglomerative procedure with attractive O(n^2 log n) complexity independent of community count.
- Strength: competitive and much faster link prediction performance on NIPS-234 compared to IRM, with sensible qualitative hierarchies on other datasets.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to key hierarchical baselines (HRG, nested SBMs) and re-runs of LFRM/ILA for fairness.
- Weakness: experimental depth and fairness of runtime comparisons are limited and require clearer definitions and controlled conditions.
- Weakness: the sparse merge heuristic breaks Bayesian coherence and should be formalized as a prior or justified theoretically.
- Weakness: no hyperparameter learning or sensitivity analysis for α, β, δ, λ, and γ; results may depend strongly on these.
- Weakness: ambiguities in complexity, memory costs, predictive complexity description, and variability across restarts are not addressed.
- Weakness: treats Sampson’s negative ties as absent edges instead of using a signed-edge model, which may be inappropriate.
- Modeling limitation: enforces a single laminar hierarchy and cannot represent overlapping communities like MMSB/LFRM/ILA.
- Quality: technically sound; recursion and merge score appear correct though derivations should be added for reproducibility.
- Clarity: generally well-written but needs derivations (Eq. 10), clarification of Bayes-factor correspondence (Eq. 12), and evaluation/protocol details.
- Originality: neat practical combination of rose-tree priors with blockmodel likelihoods, but hierarchical network models are well-studied.
- Significance: useful as a fast, interpretable baseline for hierarchical community detection but would benefit from stronger empirical grounding.
- Suggestions: add HRG/nested SBM comparisons, derive key equations, run ablations/sensitivity sweeps, formalize sparse constraint, and report variability across restarts.
- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising ideas and speed/accuracy but insufficient experimental comparison and analysis for acceptance.
