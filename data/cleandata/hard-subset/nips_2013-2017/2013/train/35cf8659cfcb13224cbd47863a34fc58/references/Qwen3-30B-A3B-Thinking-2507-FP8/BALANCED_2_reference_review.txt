

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

## Summary
This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a method for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors build upon Bayesian approaches to hierarchical clustering and extend them to relational data. BHCD models community structure as a tree where each internal node represents a community and leaves represent individual vertices. The key innovation is an efficient greedy inference algorithm that computes the marginal likelihood in O(n² log n) time through a novel caching scheme, making it two orders of magnitude faster than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) while achieving comparable predictive performance. The paper demonstrates BHCD on Sampson's monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and the full NIPS dataset.

The work relates to prior NIPS contributions including the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) [5-7], Bayesian hierarchical clustering [13,14], and the Mondrian process [12]. Unlike previous Bayesian methods, BHCD avoids the computational bottlenecks of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) or variational inference, making it suitable for larger networks.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Soundness and Quality**: The paper presents a well-motivated probabilistic model with clear theoretical foundations. The derivation of the marginal likelihood using dynamic programming is sound, and the inference algorithm is carefully designed with proper complexity analysis. The experimental results consistently support the claims about computational efficiency.

2. **Significant Computational Improvement**: The paper makes a compelling case for BHCD's computational advantages. The O(n² log n) complexity, which is independent of the number of communities, represents a meaningful improvement over existing Bayesian methods like IRM (O(n²K²IR)) and LFRM (O(n²F²IR)). The empirical results showing BHCD matching IRM's predictive performance in seconds rather than minutes are particularly convincing.

3. **Clear Presentation**: The paper is well-organized, with a logical flow from problem statement to model development to experimental validation. The pseudocode for Algorithm 1 is particularly helpful for understanding the novel caching mechanism. The supplementary materials (not included in the paper) appear to provide additional experimental details.

4. **Practical Utility**: The method is immediately applicable to real-world network analysis problems. The sparse variant (BHCD sparse) is particularly well-suited for large sparse networks, which are common in practice.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited Comparison to Non-Bayesian Methods**: The paper compares primarily to Bayesian methods but doesn't adequately contrast BHCD with popular non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering approaches (e.g., Girvan-Newman [3]). It's unclear whether the hierarchical structures discovered by BHCD are qualitatively better than those from simpler methods, which might be more interpretable.

2. **Insufficient Theoretical Justification for Greedy Approach**: While the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the greedy procedure empirically, it lacks theoretical justification for why this local search strategy converges to good hierarchical structures. A discussion of the optimality guarantees or potential pitfalls of the greedy approach would strengthen the paper.

3. **Parameter Sensitivity and Justification**: The choice of hyperparameters (α=δ=1.0, β=λ=0.2, γ=0.4) is not thoroughly justified. The paper mentions these values "work well" but doesn't explore how sensitive the results are to these choices, which could be problematic for practitioners.

4. **Limited Interpretability Analysis**: The paper demonstrates the hierarchical structure in the NIPS co-authorship network (Figure 5) but doesn't provide deeper analysis of the interpretability of the discovered hierarchy. For example, how do the hierarchical communities align with known research areas or subfields?

## Recommendations for Improvement

1. **Expand Comparative Analysis**: Include comparisons to non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods to better establish the added value of the Bayesian approach. Specifically, compare the quality of the hierarchical structure discovered (not just predictive performance) to methods like Girvan-Newman.

2. **Address Greedy Optimization Theoretically**: Add a brief discussion of the theoretical properties of the greedy procedure, including potential local optima and how the algorithm avoids them (e.g., via multiple restarts).

3. **Conduct Parameter Sensitivity Analysis**: Include a small ablation study showing how performance varies with different hyperparameter choices. This would help practitioners adapt the method to their specific domains.

4. **Improve Hierarchical Interpretability**: Provide a more detailed analysis of the hierarchical structure discovered in the NIPS co-authorship network, particularly for the top-level clusters. For example, how do the top-level clusters correspond to major research areas in machine learning?

5. **Clarify Limitations**: The paper mentions that the method "does not yield a full probabilistic account of the data" but doesn't explicitly discuss how this limitation affects interpretation compared to full Bayesian methods like IRM.

## Decision

This paper makes a significant contribution by providing a computationally efficient Bayesian method for hierarchical community discovery that bridges the gap between theoretical rigor and practical applicability. The method's speed advantage is substantial and well-demonstrated, and the technical details are sound. The paper is well-written and addresses an important problem in network analysis.

The main limitations relate to the lack of comparison to non-Bayesian methods and insufficient theoretical justification for the greedy approach. However, these are fixable with relatively minor revisions. Given the paper's strengths in computational efficiency, clear presentation, and practical relevance, I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points above.

The paper advances the state of the art in scalable community detection and would be valuable to the NeurIPS community, particularly for researchers working with large-scale network data.
