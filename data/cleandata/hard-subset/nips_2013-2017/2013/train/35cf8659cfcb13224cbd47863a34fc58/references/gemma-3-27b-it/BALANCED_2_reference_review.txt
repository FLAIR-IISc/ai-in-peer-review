This paper introduces a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. The core idea is to combine Bayesian approaches with greedy hierarchical clustering to achieve both probabilistic inference and computational efficiency. The method builds upon previous work in stochastic blockmodels and Bayesian nonparametric models, addressing limitations of existing approaches such as scalability issues with IRM and restrictive depth limits in other Bayesian hierarchical models. The authors demonstrate BHCD's performance on Sampson's monastery network and a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, showing significant speed improvements over IRM while maintaining competitive accuracy.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of greedy hierarchical clustering with a Bayesian framework, specifically tailored for relational data, is a valuable contribution.  This bridges the gap between probabilistic modeling and efficient computation in community detection.
* **Scalability:** The reported runtime improvements (two orders of magnitude faster than a single IRM iteration) are substantial and address a critical limitation of many Bayesian approaches to network analysis. The claimed O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) complexity is also promising.
* **Theoretical Foundation:**  The paper provides a clear mathematical formulation of the model, building on stochastic blockmodels and incorporating a tree prior for hierarchical structure. The derivation of the marginal likelihood and the explanation of the inference procedure are well presented.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on both Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship data demonstrate the practical effectiveness of BHCD. The comparison with IRM is particularly convincing, highlighting the speed advantage without significant loss of accuracy.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a logical flow of ideas.  The explanations are clear, and the notation is consistent.  The inclusion of the algorithm description is helpful.
* **Variations and Extensions:** Investigating variations like BHCD sparse and considering tie-breaking strategies demonstrates a thorough exploration of the method's properties and potential improvements.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions setting hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) to fixed values, but lacks a discussion of their sensitivity or a strategy for selecting optimal values.  A brief analysis of hyperparameter influence would strengthen the results.
* **Comparison Depth:** While the comparison with IRM is strong, the comparison with other hierarchical community detection methods beyond IRM (e.g., Mondrian process, methods mentioned in the introduction like [9, 10, 11]) could be more comprehensive. Specifically, comparing predictive performance against the LFRM and ILA models used in [10] with similar datasets would be valuable.
* **Model Assumptions:**  The assumption of Beta priors on edge probabilities might be restrictive for certain types of networks. A discussion of the implications of this assumption and potential alternatives would be beneficial.
* **Interpretability of Hierarchies:** The paper mentions the difficulty in interpreting the full hierarchy on the NIPS dataset. A more detailed analysis of the discovered communities, perhaps with qualitative examples or comparisons to known research areas within NIPS, would enhance the paper's impact.
* **Missing Implementation Details:** While the algorithm is described, some implementation details are lacking.  For example, the tie-breaking strategy is mentioned as random, but further details on this aspect (e.g., any safeguards against pathological behaviors) would be helpful for reproducibility.
* **Limited Exploration of Predictive Performance:** The predictive performance analysis focuses on link prediction. Exploring other predictive tasks, such as node classification, could demonstrate the broader utility of the discovered community structure.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound method for Bayesian hierarchical community detection.
* It addresses a significant practical challenge (scalability) in Bayesian network modeling.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the method and results.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters is lacking.
* The comparison to other hierarchical community detection methods is limited.
* The interpretability of the discovered hierarchies could be improved.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of community detection. The proposed BHCD method offers a compelling combination of Bayesian inference and computational efficiency.  While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.  I believe the paper is worthy of acceptance, with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.  Specifically, I recommend the authors include a brief hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and expand the comparison to other relevant hierarchical community detection methods, or at least discuss why those comparisons weren’t made.
