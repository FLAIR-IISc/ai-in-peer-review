

# Peer Review: "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

## Summary
This paper proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel method for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors address a significant gap in the literature: while previous work has focused on flat community structure (e.g., stochastic blockmodels) or hierarchical clustering (e.g., greedy approaches), few Bayesian methods exist for hierarchical community detection that scale well to large networks. BHCD extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering [13,14] to relational data, using a tree-based prior on partitions to define a hierarchical community structure. The key innovation is an efficient greedy inference algorithm that runs in O(n² log n) time—two orders of magnitude faster than the IRM—and avoids dependence on the number of communities. The authors demonstrate BHCD's effectiveness on Sampson's Monastery network, NIPS co-authorship networks, and show it achieves comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art methods while being significantly faster.

## Strengths

**Technical Soundness and Novelty (Quality):** The paper presents a technically rigorous approach with a solid Bayesian foundation. The authors clearly establish how their model extends previous work by using a tree structure to define partitions and deriving an efficient inference algorithm. The theoretical development is sound, with proper derivation of the marginal likelihood and careful complexity analysis. The key innovation—using a tree-based prior to enable efficient dynamic programming inference—addresses a real limitation in existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods.

**Computational Efficiency (Significance):** The computational advantage is the paper's strongest contribution. BHCD's O(n² log n) time complexity (with no dependence on the number of communities) makes it suitable for large-scale networks, unlike most Bayesian methods that scale poorly with community count. The empirical results demonstrate BHCD achieves predictive performance comparable to IRM after just 10 seconds, while IRM requires ~1000 seconds. This practical advantage is significant for real-world applications where scalability is critical.

**Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical development is systematic, with careful definitions of key concepts (e.g., tree-consistent partitions, sufficient statistics). The algorithm description (Algorithm 1) is precise and accompanied by clear pseudocode. The experimental results are presented logically, with appropriate comparisons to relevant baselines.

**Thorough Experimental Validation:** The authors provide multiple compelling experiments on both small and large networks. The Sampson's Monastery case study demonstrates the method's ability to recover meaningful hierarchical structure, while the NIPS co-authorship experiments quantitatively demonstrate the speed advantage. The ablation studies (comparing BHCD variants) and analysis of sparse vs. non-sparse implementations add valuable depth.

## Weaknesses

**Limited Theoretical Analysis of Performance:** While the computational complexity is thoroughly analyzed, the paper lacks deeper theoretical analysis of why BHCD performs well (beyond speed). The authors show it achieves comparable predictive performance to IRM but don't explore why the Bayesian hierarchical approach yields good results. A more detailed comparison of the underlying statistical assumptions would strengthen the theoretical contribution.

**Tie-Breaking Strategy:** The paper acknowledges that BHCD requires multiple restarts (R=50) to handle ties in merge scores, which is a practical limitation. While the authors provide a reasonable solution (averaging over R trees for predictions), this introduces additional computational overhead and potential variability in results. The paper doesn't explore whether more principled tie-breaking strategies could be developed.

**Limited Comparison to Non-Bayesian Hierarchical Methods:** The paper focuses on comparing to Bayesian methods (IRM, LFRM, ILA), but doesn't contrast BHCD with non-Bayesian hierarchical approaches like [3,4]. Given that many hierarchical clustering methods are computationally efficient, it would be valuable to see how BHCD compares in accuracy to these faster non-Bayesian methods.

## Recommendation

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The work represents a significant contribution to the field of community detection, particularly in addressing the scalability gap for Bayesian hierarchical community detection. The method is novel, well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically validated. The computational efficiency advantage is substantial and practically important for real-world network analysis.

The paper makes a clear contribution to the NIPS community by providing a method that bridges the gap between the theoretical elegance of Bayesian approaches and the practical need for scalability. The authors have clearly established how their work differs from previous contributions, with appropriate citations to related work.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. **Expand theoretical discussion of performance:** Add a brief section discussing why the hierarchical Bayesian approach might yield better predictive performance than flat models, even if the speed advantage is the main focus.

2. **Include comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical methods:** At least a brief comparison to standard hierarchical clustering methods would help contextualize the method's contribution.

3. **Clarify the tie-breaking strategy:** Consider discussing whether the random restarts are necessary or if there are alternative strategies that could reduce computational overhead while maintaining performance.

## Summary of Arguments for Acceptance

- **Novel contribution:** First scalable Bayesian method for hierarchical community detection with clear theoretical foundation
- **Significant practical advantage:** Two orders of magnitude faster than IRM while maintaining comparable accuracy
- **Strong empirical validation:** Demonstrated on multiple real-world datasets with appropriate comparisons
- **Well-written and technically sound:** Clear organization, precise mathematical development, thorough complexity analysis
- **Addresses a meaningful gap:** Fills the scalability gap in Bayesian hierarchical community detection

The paper makes a clear and important contribution to the field, with strong theoretical foundations and significant practical implications for analyzing large social networks. It represents a substantial advance over existing methods in terms of computational efficiency while maintaining competitive performance.
