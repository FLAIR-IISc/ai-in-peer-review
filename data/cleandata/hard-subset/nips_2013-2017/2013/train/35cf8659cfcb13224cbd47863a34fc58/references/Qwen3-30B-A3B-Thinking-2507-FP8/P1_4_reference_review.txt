

# Peer Review: "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

## Summary

This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel method for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors extend Bayesian hierarchical clustering approaches to relational data, building upon the stochastic blockmodel framework. BHCD organizes communities into a nested hierarchy using a tree structure, with a novel inference algorithm that achieves O(n² log n) time complexity—significantly faster than existing Bayesian nonparametric methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). The paper demonstrates BHCD on Sampson's monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network (NIPS-234), and the full NIPS dataset, showing that BHCD achieves comparable predictive performance to IRM while being two orders of magnitude faster. The work builds upon previous Bayesian hierarchical clustering work [13, 14] but extends it to relational data with a more efficient inference scheme.

## Strengths

1. **Computational efficiency**: The paper's most significant contribution is the efficient inference algorithm with O(n² log n) time complexity, a substantial improvement over existing Bayesian methods (e.g., IRM's O(n²K²) complexity). This makes hierarchical community detection feasible for larger networks, which is a practical contribution to the field.

2. **Clear technical contribution**: The authors provide a well-structured hierarchical Bayesian model with a novel caching scheme that enables efficient computation. The model's design (using conjugate priors and tree-structured partitions) is logically sound and well-motivated.

3. **Thorough experimental validation**: The paper demonstrates BHCD on multiple datasets with clear comparisons to IRM, showing that BHCD achieves comparable predictive performance while being significantly faster. The NIPS-234 results are particularly compelling, showing that BHCD makes reasonable predictions before IRM completes a single Gibbs scan.

4. **Well-written and organized**: The paper is clearly written, well-structured, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility. The algorithm description (Algorithm 1) is particularly clear and well-motivated.

5. **Practical implementation considerations**: The authors provide valuable variations like BHCD sparse for sparse networks, demonstrating practical awareness of real-world network properties.

## Weaknesses

1. **Limited theoretical analysis**: While the paper provides a clear algorithm and empirical results, it lacks deeper theoretical analysis. There's no discussion of convergence properties, statistical guarantees of the estimator, or theoretical bounds on performance. The paper would benefit from a more rigorous theoretical foundation.

2. **Comparison to non-Bayesian methods**: The paper focuses primarily on comparing to Bayesian methods (IRM, LFRM, ILA) but doesn't compare to non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods (e.g., hierarchical clustering based on modularity, Girvan-Newman algorithm). This comparison would help contextualize BHCD's advantages more broadly.

3. **Hyperparameter sensitivity analysis**: The paper sets hyperparameters to specific values (α = δ = 1.0, β = λ = 0.2, γ = 0.4) without analyzing how sensitive the results are to these choices. A more thorough investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity would strengthen the paper.

4. **Interpretability discussion**: While the paper shows BHCD finds reasonable hierarchies, it doesn't provide strong evidence that these hierarchies are more interpretable or meaningful than those found by other methods. The discussion of the Sampson's monastery network results is somewhat superficial.

## Overall Assessment

This is a high-quality technical contribution that addresses an important problem in network analysis. The computational efficiency gains are significant and well-demonstrated, making BHCD a practical tool for hierarchical community detection in large networks. The paper is well-written and the experimental results are convincing.

The main weaknesses are the limited theoretical analysis and lack of comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods. However, these do not undermine the paper's core contribution—the efficient inference algorithm—which is both novel and practically valuable.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance of this paper for NIPS. The contribution is significant, the technical content is sound, and the experimental results are compelling. The paper makes a clear and valuable contribution to the field of network analysis, particularly for applications where computational efficiency is critical.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Pro:**
- Clear technical contribution with significant computational efficiency gains
- Well-designed experiments demonstrating practical advantages over existing methods
- Novel inference algorithm with O(n² log n) complexity that addresses a real bottleneck in the field
- Strong comparison to IRM showing clear speed advantages without sacrificing predictive performance
- Good writing and clear presentation of results

**Con:**
- Limited theoretical analysis of the model's properties
- No comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods
- Insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis
- Superficial discussion of interpretability of the discovered hierarchies

The strengths of this paper significantly outweigh the weaknesses, making it a valuable contribution to the field. The computational efficiency gains are particularly compelling, and the paper provides a practical solution to an important problem in network analysis. The minor theoretical limitations do not detract from the overall quality and significance of the work.

## Final Note

This paper would be strengthened by adding a brief discussion of the theoretical properties of the estimator and including a comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods. However, these would be improvements rather than essential requirements for acceptance. The core contribution—efficient Bayesian hierarchical community discovery—is clearly articulated, well-justified, and empirically validated.
