## Peer Review: Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. Building upon Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering, the authors propose a model that organizes communities into a nested hierarchy, effectively a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels. The key contribution lies in an efficient inference scheme, leveraging dynamic programming to compute marginal likelihoods and a caching strategy to achieve favorable time complexity – O(n<sup>2</sup> log n), where n is the number of vertices.  The authors demonstrate the method's speed and accuracy on synthetic and real-world network datasets, comparing it favorably to the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper effectively situates itself within the landscape of community detection research. It acknowledges the limitations of flat community models (Stochastic Blockmodels, IRM) and previous hierarchical approaches (greedy clustering, Bayesian hierarchical IRM). It correctly points out the computational cost of existing Bayesian nonparametric methods for hierarchical structures. BHCD aims to bridge the gap by offering a computationally efficient Bayesian approach, drawing inspiration from [13, 14] but extending them to relational data.  The comparison to IRM, and mention of related work like LFRM and ILA, helps contextualize the contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Computational Efficiency:** This is the paper’s strongest point. The reported O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) time complexity, alongside empirical results showing significantly faster performance than IRM (two orders of magnitude), is a substantial advancement. This addresses a critical bottleneck in Bayesian hierarchical community detection.  The efficient caching scheme is a clever implementation detail.
* **Probabilistic Framework:** The Bayesian approach provides a principled way to reason about uncertainty in the discovered community structure and allows for incorporating prior knowledge.
* **Hierarchical Structure:** The method explicitly models and infers hierarchical relationships between communities, providing a richer and potentially more insightful representation of network organization.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and the contributions.  The mathematical derivations (Sections 2 & 3) are relatively easy to follow, even for those not deeply familiar with Bayesian nonparametrics.
* **Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments on Sampson’s monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship dataset provide evidence of the method’s effectiveness. The comparisons to IRM and other methods are valuable.
* **Variants and Analysis:** The introduction of BHCD sparse and the discussion of tie-breaking strategies demonstrate a thorough consideration of practical implementation details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The paper mentions setting hyperparameters α = δ = 1.0, β = λ = 0.2, and γ = 0.4 “which we found to work well.” This lacks justification. A sensitivity analysis to these hyperparameters would strengthen the results. How do changes in these values affect the resulting community structure and prediction performance?
* **Predictive Performance:** While the method is faster than IRM, the predictive performance is only “comparable” to LFRM and “worse than ILA”. This raises the question of whether the speed gain comes at a significant cost in accuracy. More detailed analysis of prediction errors would be helpful.
* **Scalability Claims:** While the theoretical complexity is stated, the experiments are limited to networks of size ~2800.  Extrapolating to much larger networks requires more evidence. Can the caching scheme effectively handle datasets with millions of nodes and edges?
* **Model Assumptions:** The assumption of a Beta-Bernoulli likelihood and specific prior distributions (Beta) is not deeply justified.  Exploring other likelihood families or priors could potentially improve performance. The discussion of generalizing to other exponential families feels cursory.
* **Clarity in Figure Descriptions:** Some figures, particularly Figure 5, could benefit from more detailed captions explaining the selection criteria for the displayed hierarchical structure (e.g., the 0.5 threshold for rT).



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant challenge in network analysis: efficient inference of hierarchical community structure.
* The proposed method offers a substantial improvement in computational speed compared to existing Bayesian nonparametric approaches.
* The probabilistic framework and the explicit modeling of hierarchy are valuable contributions.
* The empirical evaluation provides evidence of the method's effectiveness, although further validation is desirable.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The predictive performance is not demonstrably superior to existing methods.
* The sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters is missing.
* The scalability claims are not thoroughly validated with experiments on very large networks.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of community detection. The computational efficiency of BHCD is a significant achievement, and the method offers a principled Bayesian approach to hierarchical community discovery. While some weaknesses remain (particularly regarding hyperparameter sensitivity and scalability validation), the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept, with minor revisions.**  I recommend acceptance contingent upon the authors addressing the following:

1.  **Hyperparameter Sensitivity Analysis:** Conduct experiments to assess the impact of different hyperparameter settings on the results.
2.  **Scalability Demonstration:**  If possible, perform experiments on a larger network to demonstrate the practical scalability of the method.
3.  **Clarify Prediction Results:** Provide a more detailed analysis of prediction performance, including error analysis and comparison to state-of-the-art methods.
4.  **Refine Figure Captions:** Improve the clarity of figure captions, especially for Figure 5.
