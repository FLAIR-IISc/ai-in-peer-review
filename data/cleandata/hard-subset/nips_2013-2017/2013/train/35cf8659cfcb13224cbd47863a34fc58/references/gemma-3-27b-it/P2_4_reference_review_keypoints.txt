- Proposes BHCD: a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method using a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels, claiming ~100x speedup over IRM and runtime independent of community count.
- Positions BHCD relative to prior work, distinguishing it from flat models (SBM, IRM) and situating it among Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods.
- Core strength is computational efficiency with claimed O(n^2 log n) complexity and strong empirical speedups, especially using the sparse variant on sparse networks.
- Scales well: worst-case runtime independent of the number of communities, addressing a key limitation of many alternatives.
- Principled Bayesian framework allows probabilistic modeling of structure and uncertainty quantification.
- Clear writing and mathematical derivations; algorithm explained logically.
- Empirical validation on three datasets (Sampson, NIPS subset, full NIPS) shows competitive predictive performance and qualitative insights.
- Sparse variant is a practical improvement that boosts performance on sparse real-world networks.
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) with minimal justification or thorough sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to faster non-Bayesian hierarchical/community detection methods (e.g., modularity-based), leaving competitive position unclear.
- Weakness: predictive performance is only comparable (not markedly superior) to models like LFRM; more analysis of strengths/weaknesses is needed.
- Weakness: discovered hierarchies appear shallow on NIPS data; unclear if due to method limitations or dataset characteristics.
- Weakness: strong reliance on stochastic blockmodel assumptions without discussion of consequences if assumptions are violated.
- Overall assessment: solid, practically useful contribution emphasizing computational efficiency; recommended for acceptance (rating 7/10) with suggested revisions on hyperparameter analysis and broader comparisons.
