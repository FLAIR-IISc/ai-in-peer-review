

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a method for inferring hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors build upon Bayesian approaches to hierarchical clustering and extend them to relational data, addressing limitations in existing methods. Previous work on community detection has largely focused on flat structures (e.g., stochastic blockmodel [1], mixed membership stochastic blockmodel [2]), while hierarchical approaches (e.g., IRM extensions [5-8], Mondrian process [12]) often suffer from poor scalability. The paper positions BHCD as a solution that maintains Bayesian probabilistic modeling while achieving significantly better computational efficiency.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound model with clear mathematical formulations. The authors provide a rigorous derivation of the marginal likelihood for hierarchical community structures and develop an efficient inference algorithm (Algorithm 1) with O(n² log n) time complexity. The theoretical analysis is well-supported, and the experimental results demonstrate that BHCD achieves comparable predictive performance to the IRM while being approximately two orders of magnitude faster. The authors acknowledge limitations, including the need for multiple restarts to handle ties in merge scores and the potential for disconnected communities to be merged (addressed through the BHCD sparse variant). The experimental design is appropriate, with comparisons to relevant baselines on both synthetic and real-world networks (Sampson's monastery, NIPS co-authorship data).

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical formulations are clear, with careful notation and step-by-step explanations. The figures effectively illustrate the hierarchical structure and algorithmic process. The paper provides sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results, including pseudocode for the inference algorithm and specific hyperparameter choices. The connection between the hierarchical tree structure and the stochastic blockmodel is particularly well-explained.

**Originality:** The key contribution is the efficient inference algorithm for hierarchical community detection, which significantly improves upon the computational complexity of existing Bayesian approaches. While the paper builds on Bayesian hierarchical clustering [13, 14], it extends this to relational data and achieves better scalability. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from previous work, noting that existing Bayesian methods (e.g., [8, 9, 10, 11]) either have high computational costs or are limited to specific tree structures. The paper's novelty is well-established through comparison with recent NIPS proceedings (e.g., [18, 19, 21] are cited as relevant context).

**Significance:** The speed advantage of BHCD (2 orders of magnitude faster than IRM) is a substantial practical contribution for real-world applications on large networks. The method achieves comparable predictive performance to existing Bayesian methods while being significantly more scalable. This addresses an important gap in the field, as most state-of-the-art Bayesian community detection methods are computationally prohibitive for large networks. The hierarchical structure discovered on the NIPS co-authorship network provides meaningful insights into research collaboration patterns, demonstrating the method's practical utility.

**Strengths:**
- Novel efficient inference algorithm with clear theoretical analysis
- Comprehensive experimental validation across multiple datasets
- Clear exposition of the model and algorithm
- Demonstrated significant speed advantage over existing Bayesian methods
- Practical utility for large-scale network analysis

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (though hyperparameters were fixed based on empirical performance)
- The connection between the hierarchical structure and domain-specific interpretation could be strengthened, particularly for the NIPS dataset
- The comparison with non-Bayesian hierarchical methods (e.g., greedy hierarchical clustering [3, 4]) is somewhat limited
- The paper does not address how BHCD compares to recent nonparametric methods that might achieve similar scalability (e.g., recent work on scalable community detection)

**Recommendation:**
This paper makes a strong contribution to the field of community detection by providing an efficient Bayesian approach to hierarchical community discovery. The method is technically sound, well-justified, and demonstrates significant practical advantages over existing approaches. The paper meets all NIPS quality standards and addresses an important problem in network analysis. While there is room for deeper exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity and broader comparison with non-Bayesian methods, these do not detract from the paper's substantial contribution.

The paper is clearly within the scope of NIPS, as it addresses a fundamental problem in network analysis with a novel probabilistic approach that advances the state of the art. The combination of theoretical rigor, practical efficiency, and empirical validation makes this a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
