- Proposes BHCD, a scalable Bayesian method for hierarchical community detection in networks.
- Extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data using a tree-based prior on partitions.
- Introduces an efficient greedy inference algorithm with O(n² log n) time and no dependence on number of communities.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on Sampson’s Monastery and NIPS co-authorship networks.
- Achieves predictive performance comparable to IRM while being two orders of magnitude faster in practice.
- Theoretical development is rigorous: sound Bayesian foundation, marginal likelihood derivation, and complexity analysis.
- Key innovation: tree-based prior enabling dynamic programming and efficient inference.
- Computational efficiency makes BHCD suitable for large-scale networks (e.g., comparable IRM performance in ~10s vs ~1000s).
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with precise algorithm description and pseudocode.
- Experiments are thorough: multiple datasets, ablation studies, and sparse vs non-sparse analysis.
- Weakness: lacks deeper theoretical analysis explaining why BHCD attains good predictive performance beyond speed.
- Weakness: tie-breaking requires many restarts (R=50), adding overhead and potential result variability.
- Weakness: limited comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical methods; accuracy vs fast non-Bayesian baselines not evaluated.
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution bridging Bayesian theory and practical scalability.
- Suggested improvements: expand theoretical discussion of performance, compare to non-Bayesian hierarchical methods, and clarify/improve tie-breaking strategy.
