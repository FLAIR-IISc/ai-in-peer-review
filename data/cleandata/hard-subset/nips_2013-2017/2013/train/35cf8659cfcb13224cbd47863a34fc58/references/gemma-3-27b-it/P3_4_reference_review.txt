## Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. It builds upon Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering, framing the problem as a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels. The key contribution lies in an efficient inference scheme that avoids the computational bottlenecks of existing Bayesian nonparametric methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). The authors demonstrate that BHCD is significantly faster than IRM (two orders of magnitude faster per iteration) while maintaining competitive performance in terms of predictive accuracy, with a worst-case runtime independent of the number of communities.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the landscape of community detection algorithms. It highlights the limitations of traditional methods like flat stochastic blockmodels (requiring known K) and purely greedy hierarchical clustering (lacking a probabilistic framework). The authors accurately identify the challenges associated with Bayesian nonparametric approaches like IRM and the Mondrian process – their computational cost, particularly for large networks.  BHCD cleverly addresses these limitations by leveraging a greedy approach with a well-defined Bayesian framework and efficient computation of the marginal likelihood. The references cited (Holland et al., Airoldi et al., Girvan & Newman, Kemp et al., etc.) are relevant and provide a strong foundation for the work. The comparison to LFRM and ILA in the experiments is also appropriate given the recent advancements in this area.

**Strengths:**

* **Computational Efficiency:** This is the most significant strength of the paper. The authors clearly demonstrate that BHCD scales much better than existing Bayesian nonparametric alternatives, particularly for large networks. The claimed O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) complexity is a substantial advantage.
* **Probabilistic Framework:**  BHCD offers a complete probabilistic account of the data, unlike purely greedy methods.  This allows for principled model selection and uncertainty quantification.
* **Hierarchical Structure:** The method naturally discovers hierarchical community structure, providing a richer representation of network organization compared to flat community detection algorithms.
* **Clear Presentation:**  The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical derivations are relatively clear, and the algorithm description is detailed.  The inclusion of specific hyperparameters and experimental details increases reproducibility.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on Sampson's monastery network, the NIPS co-authorship dataset, and the full NIPS dataset provide compelling evidence for the effectiveness of BHCD. The comparison to IRM and the demonstration of speedup are particularly strong. The qualitative analysis of the NIPS hierarchy is insightful.
* **BHCD Sparse:** The introduction of the 'sparse' variant demonstrates a practical consideration for real-world network data and further improves performance on sparse graphs.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't extensively explore the impact of different hyperparameter settings (α, β, δ, λ, γ). While the chosen values worked well in their experiments, it's unclear how sensitive the results are to these choices, and a more thorough investigation would strengthen the findings.
* **Tie-breaking:** The random tie-breaking scheme could introduce variability in the results. While the authors address this by using multiple restarts (R), it would be helpful to explore more principled tie-breaking strategies.
* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:**  While the authors provide complexity analysis, a more detailed theoretical justification for the greedy approach and its convergence properties would be beneficial. The assumption of local community structure depending only on local connectivity, while practical, could be further explored.
* **Comparison to Non-Bayesian Methods:** The paper primarily focuses on comparisons to Bayesian methods. It would be valuable to compare BHCD to state-of-the-art non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection algorithms (e.g., Leiden algorithm) to provide a more comprehensive performance assessment.
* **Scalability of Likelihood Computation:** While the inference scales well, the O(n+m) complexity for likelihood computation in sparse networks is mentioned as a potential bottleneck. Further investigation into optimizing this step, perhaps through sampling strategies, would be worthwhile.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Significant improvement in scalability compared to existing Bayesian nonparametric methods.
* Sound probabilistic framework for hierarchical community detection.
* Compelling experimental results demonstrating effectiveness and efficiency.
* Clear and well-organized presentation.
* Addresses a significant challenge in network analysis.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Hyperparameter sensitivity is not thoroughly investigated.
* Limited theoretical analysis of the greedy approach.
* Comparison to non-Bayesian methods is missing.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field of community detection. The authors have developed a practical and efficient Bayesian method for discovering hierarchical community structure in networks. The speed and scalability advantages of BHCD are particularly impressive. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.  I believe this paper is a valuable addition to the NIPS conference and recommend acceptance.

**Rating:** 8.5/10 (Strong Accept)
