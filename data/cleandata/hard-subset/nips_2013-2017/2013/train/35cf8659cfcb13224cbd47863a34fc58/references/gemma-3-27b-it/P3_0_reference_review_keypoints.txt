- Proposes BHCD, a novel greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method for community detection offering scalability over IRM.
- BHCD models communities as a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a tree prior enabling efficient marginal likelihood computation.
- Empirical results on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS-234 show substantial speedups versus IRM while maintaining competitive predictive performance.
- Correctly positions itself relative to prior work, addressing limitations of parametric SBMs and costly nonparametric methods, and builds on prior Bayesian greedy clustering.
- References are comprehensive and demonstrate a solid understanding of related literature.
- Strength — Scalability: O(n^2 log n) complexity with caching yields significant speed improvements.
- Strength — Probabilistic framework: provides uncertainty quantification and principled model selection unlike purely greedy methods.
- Strength — Theoretical foundation: clear derivation of marginal likelihood and recursive computation scheme.
- Strength — Clear presentation: well-written, organized, and accessible mathematical notation.
- Strength — Empirical validation: qualitative insights on Sampson’s network and quantitative competitiveness on NIPS-234.
- Strength — Practical variants: BHCD sparse and binary/non-binary tree options show pragmatic optimization choices.
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: lack of thorough analysis on robustness to α, β, δ, λ, γ.
- Weakness — Limited comparisons: missing broader benchmarking against state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Louvain, Leiden) and their runtimes.
- Weakness — Scalability claims vs practice: empirical runtime on the full NIPS network and larger networks not reported.
- Weakness — Hierarchy interpretation: insufficient discussion on interpreting hierarchy levels and utility of probabilistic hierarchies.
- Weakness — Predictive gap: BHCD trails the best method (ILA) slightly without analysis of causes or remedies.
- Argument for acceptance: significant, scalable, and probabilistically sound contribution with strong theory and solid experiments.
- Argument against acceptance: needs broader comparisons, hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and larger-scale empirical evaluation.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor issues that can be addressed through additional experiments and analysis.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add broader algorithm comparisons, hyperparameter sensitivity study, full NIPS runtime, and expanded discussion on hierarchy interpretation.
