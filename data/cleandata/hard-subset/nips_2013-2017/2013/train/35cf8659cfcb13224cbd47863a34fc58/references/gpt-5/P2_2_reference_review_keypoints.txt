- Proposal: BHCD — a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering for networks using rose-tree priors + a Beta-Bernoulli blockmodel, dynamic-programming marginal likelihood, and local Bayes-factor agglomeration (with sparse and binary variants).
- Relation to prior work: Extends Bayesian rose trees to relational data and contrasts with IRM and latent-attribute models; misses citations/comparisons to hierarchical random graphs and nested SBMs.
- Strength — conceptual clarity: Clean mapping of rose-tree priors to relational data and an exact marginal-likelihood recursion.
- Strength — scalability: Cached sufficient statistics and a heap give O(n^2 log n) worst-case and O(n+m) per-tree likelihood; sparse variant practical for large sparse graphs.
- Strength — practicality: Greedy agglomeration with local Bayes factors is simple, fast, and easy to implement; predictive recursion is linear in tree depth.
- Strength — empirical support: Reaches reasonable link-prediction faster than IRM, sensible qualitative clusters on Sampson, and scales to full NIPS with interpretable clusters.
- Weakness — limited baselines: Comparisons only to IRM on NIPS-234; lacks evaluations against hierarchical random graphs, nested/degree-corrected SBMs, and stronger non-Bayesian hierarchical methods.
- Weakness — misleading speed claim: “Two orders of magnitude faster than a single iteration of the IRM” is inaccurate and should be clarified/toned down.
- Weakness — model expressivity: Single pair of Beta priors biases toward assortativity and cannot capture heterogeneous/disassortative patterns or degree effects without extensions.
- Weakness — evaluation gaps: No synthetic hierarchy recovery experiments, no ablations/sensitivity analyses for γ or Beta hyperparameters, limited evaluation of sparse variant, and no variability across restarts reported.
- Weakness — theoretical guarantees: No analysis of when local Bayes-factor merges match the global posterior/MAP or bounds on suboptimality from greedy merges.
- Weakness — reproducibility/clarity: Some incomplete references, unspecified code availability, and lacking full pseudocode and detailed complexity/heap-maintenance description.
- Quality: Technical core (partition prior, marginalization, recursion, Bayes-factor merges) appears sound and complexity claims plausible given caching.
- Clarity: Generally readable with clear notation and merge explanations, but certain claims and references need polishing and more algorithmic detail.
- Originality: Moderate — thoughtful adaptation of known rose-tree and blockmodel components to relational data with efficient caching, but not a fundamentally new model.
- Significance: Valuable for practitioners needing fast hierarchical community discovery; significance limited by incomplete empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art hierarchical network models.
- Suggestion: Clarify and properly quantify speed claims with matched wall-clock comparisons and implementations/hardware.
- Suggestion: Add baselines including hierarchical random graphs and nested/degree-corrected SBMs and possibly modularity-based hierarchical clustering.
- Suggestion: Include synthetic recovery experiments, hyperparameter sensitivity/ablation studies, restart variability, and fuller evaluation of the sparse constraint.
- Suggestion: Discuss extensions/applicability to directed/bipartite/weighted networks, consider degree correction or cluster-specific parameters, and provide code and full pseudocode.
