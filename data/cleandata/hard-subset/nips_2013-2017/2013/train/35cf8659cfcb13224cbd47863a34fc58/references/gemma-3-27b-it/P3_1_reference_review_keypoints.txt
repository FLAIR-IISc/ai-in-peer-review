- Presents BHCD: a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method for discovering nested community structures in networks.  
- Models networks as a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels with dynamic-programming likelihood and greedy tree search.  
- Claims computational complexity O(n^2 log n) with a sparse variant for real-world sparse networks.  
- Demonstrates speed and effectiveness on synthetic data, Sampson’s monastery, and NIPS co-authorship networks.  
- Empirical results show significant speedups over IRM and competitive link-prediction performance.  
- Positions work appropriately relative to stochastic blockmodels, greedy clustering, IRM, LFRM, and prior Bayesian greedy hierarchical clustering.  
- Strength: major computational efficiency advantage validated empirically.  
- Strength: solid Bayesian theoretical foundation and elegant marginal likelihood DP derivation.  
- Strength: flexible model without fixed community count or hierarchy depth.  
- Strength: clear presentation and helpful illustrative examples.  
- Strength: empirical validation across multiple datasets and inclusion of a sparse-optimized variant.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity not explored or justified beyond heuristic choices.  
- Weakness: predictive performance is comparable to LFRM and worse than ILA, not state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness: greedy search may yield suboptimal trees; restarts used but tie-breaking effects unexplored.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of scalability limits, especially memory use for very large/dense networks.  
- Weakness: discussion of connections to other hierarchical models is cursory and needs expansion.  
- For acceptance: significant efficiency improvement, sound theory, demonstrable effectiveness, and clear writing.  
- Against acceptance: subpar predictive accuracy relative to best methods, hyperparameter and scalability concerns, and greedy optimization risks.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution worth acceptance with minor revisions focused on hyperparameter analysis, clarifying predictive trade-offs, scalability discussion, and expanded related work.
