## Review of Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

This paper presents a novel Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. The authors address a well-studied problem – hierarchical community detection – offering a method that aims to be faster and more scalable than existing Bayesian nonparametric approaches like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). The core idea is to leverage a greedy approach with a Bayesian framework, building a hierarchy of communities from the bottom up.

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear introduction to the problem and related work. The authors correctly position their contribution within the existing literature, mentioning stochastic blockmodels, greedy hierarchical clustering, and various Bayesian approaches (IRM, Mondrian process). The mathematical formulation of the model in Section 3, building on stochastic blockmodels, is reasonably clear and follows a logical progression. The explanation of the greedy inference scheme in Section 4 is detailed, including considerations for computational efficiency through caching of sufficient statistics.  The experimental evaluation in Section 5 on Sampson’s monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and the full NIPS network demonstrates the method’s performance and speed.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** While Bayesian hierarchical community detection is not entirely new, the proposed greedy approach with efficient caching and explicit focus on scalability presents a novel contribution. The emphasis on avoiding computationally expensive MCMC methods and leveraging dynamic programming for marginal likelihood calculation is a key strength.
* **Scalability:** The reported O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) time complexity (and O(n+m) for likelihood calculation) is a significant advantage over existing Bayesian nonparametric methods, especially for larger networks. The experimental results on the NIPS dataset confirm the speed improvements compared to IRM.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the model and the inference procedure.  The inclusion of equations aids understanding. The algorithm is described in sufficient detail to allow for implementation.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on different datasets demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the method. Comparing against IRM provides a useful benchmark.  The inclusion of both qualitative and quantitative results is positive.
* **Variations and Analysis:** Exploring variations like BHCD sparse and binary trees, and discussing their trade-offs, adds depth to the analysis.



**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper lacks theoretical guarantees on the convergence or optimality of the greedy approach. While the authors acknowledge this implicitly, a discussion of potential suboptimality would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The choice of hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) is not thoroughly discussed. The authors state they found parameters that “work well,” but a sensitivity analysis or a principled method for hyperparameter selection would strengthen the work.
* **Comparison to Non-Bayesian Methods:** The comparison is largely focused on Bayesian approaches. Comparing BHCD against state-of-the-art non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods (e.g., spectral clustering-based approaches) would provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
* **Prediction Details:** While prediction is mentioned, the explanation could be more detailed.  How is the predictive distribution used for link prediction specifically? Providing more specifics on the evaluation of predictive performance would be helpful.
* **Figure Clarity:**  Figure 3, while showing results on Sampson's network, is difficult to interpret without more explanation of the colors/groupings.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses an important problem in network analysis with a novel and potentially impactful approach.
* The method demonstrates clear advantages in scalability compared to existing Bayesian nonparametric techniques.
* The paper is well-written, clearly explained, and supported by empirical validation.
* The authors demonstrate a strong understanding of related work.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of theoretical guarantees on the greedy approach is a concern.
* The paper lacks a comprehensive comparison to non-Bayesian methods.
* The hyperparameter sensitivity is not adequately addressed.


**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid contribution to the field of hierarchical community detection. While there are some weaknesses, the novelty, scalability, and clarity of the proposed method outweigh these concerns. The paper is of significant interest to the NIPS community, particularly those working in network analysis, Bayesian methods, and machine learning.  I believe the paper, with minor revisions addressing the weaknesses outlined above, is **accept**-worthy. Specifically, a discussion on theoretical limitations, a brief hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and more comprehensive benchmarking would strengthen the paper considerably.
