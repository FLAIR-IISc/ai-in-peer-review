- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a tree-based model with leaves as vertices and internal nodes as communities, plus an efficient greedy inference algorithm running in O(n² log n).
- Directly addresses computational inefficiency of prior Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods.
- Greedy approximation trades exact Bayesian inference for large speedups while maintaining comparable predictive performance.
- Model is well-formulated with clear mathematical definitions and a carefully derived inference algorithm.
- Provides detailed time-complexity analysis and compares favorably to IRM (O(n²K²)) and LFRM (O(n²F²)), summarized in Table 1.
- Experimental evaluation on Sampson’s network, NIPS subset, and full NIPS co-authorship network shows comparable predictive performance and ~1000× speed improvement.
- Produces insightful visualizations of discovered hierarchical structures.
- Limited theoretical guarantees: lacks rigorous analysis of when the greedy approach finds the optimal tree.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with consistent notation and clear algorithm description enabling reproducibility for expert readers.
- Discusses method variants (e.g., BHCD sparse) and trade-offs in detail.
- Original contribution: combining tree-based hierarchical structure with greedy dynamic programming and sufficient-statistics caching to achieve O(n² log n).
- Clarifies differences from prior work that were limited to specific tree structures or depths.
- Significant impact: makes hierarchical community detection feasible for much larger networks and demonstrates practical utility on a 2864-node dataset.
- Highlights the computational vs. model-complexity trade-off and avoids expensive MCMC/variational inference.
- Strengths: novel and significant contribution, strong theoretical foundation, comprehensive experiments, clear writing, practical implementation details, and solid baseline comparisons.
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical analysis, random tie-breaking with 50 restarts raises reproducibility concerns, uncertain scalability beyond 2864 nodes, and minimal hyperparameter sensitivity study.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Suggested improvements: discuss scaling to millions of vertices, provide hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, address limitations for networks lacking clear hierarchies, and explain the “out of date scores on its heap” failure mode.
