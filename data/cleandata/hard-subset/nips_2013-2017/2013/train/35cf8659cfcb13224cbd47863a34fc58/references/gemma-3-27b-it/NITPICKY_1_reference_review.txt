This paper presents a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. The core idea is to extend existing Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering to relational data, leveraging a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels. While the problem addressed – scalable Bayesian hierarchical community detection – is important, and the approach has potential, the paper suffers from a multitude of small but significant flaws in presentation, justification, and implementation details, leading to an overall impression of incompleteness and a lack of rigorous evaluation.

The introduction establishes the motivation reasonably well, but the literature review is surprisingly shallow. While key papers are cited (e.g., [1, 5, 8]), the discussion lacks nuance. For instance, the statement that methods like [9, 10] are “limited to hierarchies of depth two” is misleading; they are limited in *scalability* to deeper hierarchies, not inherently in depth itself. Similarly, the dismissal of greedy hierarchical clustering [3, 4] as lacking a “full probabilistic account” feels dismissive given its practical utility and speed – a point the authors later emphasize as a strength of their method.

Section 2 provides a standard presentation of stochastic blockmodels, but the notation feels inconsistent. The use of `φ` for both a partition *and* a set of communities within a tree (later) is confusing. The derivation of the likelihood (Equation 2) is correct, but lacks sufficient explanation for readers unfamiliar with the beta-Bernoulli model. The generalization to an exponential family (Equation 3) feels tacked on and doesn't significantly contribute to the paper. 

Section 3 introduces the Bayesian hierarchical community model. The core idea of using a tree to define a prior on partitions is sensible, but the notation becomes increasingly convoluted.  The explanation of Equation (7) is particularly weak; the authors state it is “motivated by” the generative process but fail to adequately connect the equation to that process. The choice of `πS = 1− (1− γ)|ch(S)|` is referenced as being motivated by [14], but the justification for this choice – specifically why it favors non-binary trees – is not clearly explained. The leap from this prior to the factorization in (8) is not well-motivated and requires more detailed explanation. 

Section 4 details the inference procedure. The greedy agglomerative approach is a reasonable strategy, but the Bayes factor score (Equation 12) feels arbitrary. Why this particular formulation?  The description of the different merger options (Figure 2) is helpful, but the discussion of sufficient statistic updates (Equation 13) is too concise. The claim of O(1) computation per inner loop, while potentially true, requires a much more detailed justification. The introduction of “BHCD sparse” feels ad hoc and lacks a strong theoretical underpinning. The discussion of tie-breaking and restarts is also weak; simply stating they use R=50 and pick the best tree is insufficient. Why 50? What is the sensitivity to this parameter?  The predictive distribution calculation (Equation 14) lacks sufficient explanation and feels disconnected from the earlier model description.

Section 5 presents experimental results. The qualitative results on Sampson’s monastery network are interesting, but the analysis is superficial. The comparison to IRM on the NIPS dataset is more compelling, but the presentation is flawed. Table 1 is insufficient; it doesn't provide any information about the size of the network, the number of edges, or the experimental setup. Figure 4 is difficult to interpret. The x-axis is not clearly labeled (what units are being used for “time”?). The AUC results are reported but not discussed in relation to the other metrics. The claim of “two orders of magnitude faster” compared to a *single iteration* of IRM is somewhat misleading; it doesn't address the iterative nature of Bayesian inference.  The visualization of the full NIPS network hierarchy (Figure 5) is intriguing but lacks context. The criteria for selecting which clusters to show (rT ∏ A∈ancestorT (1−rA) > 0.5) is somewhat arbitrary and not fully justified.

Numerous minor issues further detract from the paper. The formatting is inconsistent (e.g., varying spacing around equations). The writing style is often awkward and verbose. There are several instances of imprecise language. The figure captions are often too short and lack sufficient detail.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* **Lack of Rigor:** The paper lacks a level of mathematical rigor and detailed explanation expected of a NIPS submission. Key derivations are glossed over, and important choices (e.g., the Bayes factor score, the prior on tree structure) are not adequately justified.
* **Superficial Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation is insufficient. The results are presented without sufficient context or detailed analysis. The comparison to IRM is not comprehensive.
* **Presentation Issues:** The paper suffers from numerous minor flaws in presentation, including inconsistent notation, awkward writing, and inconsistent formatting.
* **Incremental Contribution:** While the proposed method is novel, its contribution feels incremental. It builds on existing work in a relatively straightforward manner and doesn't represent a significant breakthrough.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* **Interesting Problem:** The problem of scalable Bayesian hierarchical community detection is important.
* **Potential for Speed:** The greedy inference procedure offers a potential speed advantage over existing Bayesian methods.
* **Novelty:** The combination of Bayesian inference with greedy hierarchical clustering is a novel approach.



**Overall Assessment:** Despite the potential of the idea, the paper’s numerous shortcomings in rigor, evaluation, and presentation outweigh its strengths. The lack of detailed justification for key design choices, the superficial experimental evaluation, and the numerous minor flaws lead me to recommend **rejection**. A substantially revised version, with a more rigorous treatment of the theoretical foundations, a more comprehensive experimental evaluation, and a clearer and more polished presentation, might be reconsidered.
