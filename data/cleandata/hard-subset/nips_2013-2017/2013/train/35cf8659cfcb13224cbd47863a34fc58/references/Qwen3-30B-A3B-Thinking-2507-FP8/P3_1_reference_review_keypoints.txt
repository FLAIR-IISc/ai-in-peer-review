- Introduces BHCD: a scalable method for hierarchical community discovery using a hierarchical mixture of stochastic block models with Beta priors.  
- Key innovation is a greedy bottom-up Bayesian inference algorithm that merges trees to build the hierarchy.  
- Algorithm achieves O(n² log n) time complexity and is substantially faster than IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance.  
- Empirical validation on Sampson’s monastery, a NIPS co-authorship subset, and the full NIPS dataset shows practical effectiveness.  
- Clever caching scheme for sufficient statistics enables computational efficiency and is well-explained.  
- Clear problem formulation bridging flat community models and hierarchical clustering methods.  
- Well-structured presentation with helpful pseudocode and readable explanations.  
- Method is practically relevant for large-scale social network analysis.  
- Misleading use of “Bayesian”: model uses Bayesian priors but inference is greedy, not full Bayesian (no MCMC/variational inference).  
- Lacks comparisons with efficient non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods, weakening empirical positioning.  
- Limited theoretical analysis: no consistency or optimality guarantees and little discussion of failure modes.  
- Insufficient theoretical justification for preferring non-binary (rose) trees over binary trees.  
- Predictive performance comparisons lack statistical significance tests, error bars, or confidence intervals.  
- Mentions interpretability trade-off between binary and non-binary trees but doesn’t analyze how to balance it.  
- Model formulation and use of conjugate Beta priors are reasonable but implications for flexibility are not fully discussed.  
- Algorithm design is elegant; more intuition needed on why the three merger types are necessary and their effects.  
- Related work coverage is strong but omits some relevant recent hierarchical community detection papers.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on clarifying Bayesian claims and addressing comparisons and analyses.  
- Specific requested revisions: clarify “Bayesian” phrasing in title/abstract, compare to non-Bayesian methods, add theoretical discussion, include statistical significance testing, and expand on binary vs. non-binary trade-offs.
