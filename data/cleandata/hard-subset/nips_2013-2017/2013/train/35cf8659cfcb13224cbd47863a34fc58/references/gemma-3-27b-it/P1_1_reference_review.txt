## Peer Review: Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel, greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. The authors build upon existing Bayesian approaches to hierarchical clustering, specifically addressing the computational bottlenecks of methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  BHCD leverages a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels, utilizing a tree-based prior on partitions to enable efficient, scalable inference.  The paper demonstrates that BHCD achieves results two orders of magnitude faster than a single iteration of the IRM, with a worst-case runtime independent of the number of communities and quadratic in the number of vertices. Empirical evaluation on Sampson’s monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and the full NIPS network showcases both the speed and reasonable accuracy of the proposed method.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly positions itself within the landscape of community detection methods. It effectively contrasts BHCD with stochastic blockmodels (requiring known community numbers), greedy hierarchical clustering (lacking a probabilistic account), and Bayesian approaches like the IRM (computationally expensive). The authors acknowledge relevant work on Bayesian nonparametrics and the Mondrian process, and demonstrate an understanding of the trade-offs involved in different approaches.  The connection to prior work on Bayesian rose trees [14] is also clearly stated.

**Strengths:**

* **Computational Efficiency:** The primary strength of BHCD is its speed.  The reported two orders of magnitude speedup compared to IRM is significant, especially for larger networks. The quadratic time complexity is also a notable advantage. The caching scheme described is a clever optimization.
* **Scalability:**  The independence of runtime from the number of communities is a crucial benefit, particularly for complex networks with potentially deep hierarchical structures.
* **Probabilistic Foundation:** Unlike purely greedy methods, BHCD provides a fully probabilistic framework, allowing for principled inference and predictive modeling.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments on different datasets demonstrate the practical applicability of the method. The results on Sampson's monastery network provide qualitative insight, while the NIPS co-authorship experiments provide quantitative comparison to existing methods.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and provides a clear explanation of the model and inference procedure. The introduction and related work sections are well-structured.
* **Sparse Network Optimization:** The addition of BHCD sparse is a thoughtful addition, tailored to the characteristics of many real-world networks.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper briefly mentions the choice of hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) but doesn’t discuss sensitivity analysis or guidelines for selecting optimal values. While the authors found values that "work well," a more thorough investigation would strengthen the paper.
* **Limited Comparison:** While comparison to IRM is valuable, the comparison to other relevant methods (e.g., LFRM, ILA) is somewhat limited. While results are presented, a deeper analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of BHCD *relative* to these methods would be beneficial.
* **Predictive Performance:** While competitive, the predictive performance of BHCD is not state-of-the-art. Further investigation into factors limiting performance and potential improvements (e.g., exploring non-conjugate priors) could be explored.
* **Tree Visualization:** The visualization of the full NIPS hierarchy (Figure 5) is limited. Showing more levels of the tree and highlighting key communities would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the discovered structure.
* **BHCD global:** The description of why BHCD global performed poorly is a bit cursory. More details would be valuable.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper appears technically sound. The derivations and algorithmic descriptions are clear and logically presented. The claims are supported by experimental results. However, the lack of hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is a minor concern.
* **Clarity:** The paper is mostly clear and well-organized. The notation is generally consistent, but some sections (e.g., the recursive likelihood calculation) are dense and could benefit from further simplification.
* **Originality:** The approach of combining a hierarchical tree prior with stochastic blockmodels and a greedy inference procedure is novel. The caching scheme for efficient computation is also a significant contribution.
* **Significance:** The improved computational efficiency of BHCD makes it a valuable tool for analyzing large-scale networks. The ability to uncover hierarchical community structure is also significant, as many real-world networks exhibit this property. The paper provides a strong foundation for future research in this area.

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

*  Significant speedup over existing Bayesian nonparametric methods.
*  Scalable to larger networks.
*  Provides a probabilistic framework for hierarchical community detection.
*  Well-written and clearly explained.
*  Demonstrates promising empirical results.

**Against Acceptance:**

*  Limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
*  Somewhat limited comparison to other state-of-the-art methods.
*  Predictive performance is not state-of-the-art.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of community detection. The proposed BHCD method offers a compelling combination of efficiency, scalability, and probabilistic rigor. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, specifically addressing the hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and providing a more detailed comparison to related work.  I also suggest enhancing the visualization of the larger hierarchical structures discovered in the NIPS dataset.
