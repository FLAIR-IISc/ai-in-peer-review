

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

## Summary
This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a method for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors extend Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering to relational data, building upon the stochastic blockmodel framework. BHCD represents communities as a tree structure, where each internal node corresponds to a community and leaves represent vertices. The key innovation is an efficient greedy inference algorithm with O(n² log n) time complexity, significantly faster than existing Bayesian hierarchical methods (e.g., 2 orders of magnitude faster than IRM). The authors demonstrate BHCD on Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship network, showing reasonable performance with substantial computational advantages.

## Strengths

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with well-defined mathematical formulations. The inference algorithm leverages dynamic programming and clever caching of sufficient statistics to achieve efficient computation. The authors properly address limitations of their approach (e.g., handling disconnected components in BHCD sparse) and provide clear experimental comparisons to relevant baselines.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the algorithm description is thorough. Figures 1-5 effectively illustrate the concepts and results. The connection to previous work is adequately explained, and the paper properly cites relevant literature from NIPS and elsewhere.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear contribution by extending Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data with a focus on computational efficiency. While the hierarchical model builds on prior work (e.g., [14]), the specific inference algorithm and its computational advantages represent a novel contribution. The paper is distinct from previous NIPS papers on hierarchical community detection (e.g., [8], [9], [10], [11], [12]).

**Significance:** The computational efficiency of BHCD (O(n² log n) vs. O(n²K²) for IRM) represents a substantial practical advantage for large-scale network analysis. The method could enable Bayesian hierarchical community detection on networks where current approaches are computationally prohibitive. The results on NIPS-234 show BHCD achieves comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art methods while being significantly faster.

## Weaknesses

**Quality:** The theoretical analysis is somewhat limited. While the paper provides time complexity analysis, it lacks convergence guarantees or optimality analysis for the greedy inference procedure. The hyperparameter choices (α=δ=1.0, β=λ=0.2, γ=0.4) are not well-justified, which is particularly problematic since these choices significantly impact the model's behavior.

**Clarity:** The motivation for the specific prior structure (πS = 1−(1−γ)|ch(S)|) is not sufficiently explained. Additionally, the paper does not clarify how the method handles networks with disconnected components in the non-sparse variant, which is a limitation noted in the sparse variant.

**Significance:** The experimental evaluation is somewhat limited. The paper only evaluates on three datasets, with NIPS-234 being a small subset of the full NIPS dataset. The comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods (e.g., [3], [4]) is missing, which would strengthen the significance argument by showing how BHCD compares to the most common alternative approaches.

**Originality:** The paper could better articulate how BHCD differs from the Mondrian process [12], which also handles hierarchical relational data with a Bayesian approach. A more detailed comparison would clarify the unique contribution.

## Specific Suggestions for Improvement

1. **Expand theoretical analysis:** Provide more rigorous analysis of the greedy inference procedure, including convergence guarantees or at least a discussion of potential pitfalls in the greedy approach.

2. **Justify hyperparameters:** Conduct a small hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to demonstrate how performance varies with different choices of α, β, δ, λ, and γ.

3. **Expand experiments:** Include results on the full NIPS dataset (2864 vertices) to demonstrate scalability. Compare BHCD to non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods (e.g., [3], [4]) to better establish its significance.

4. **Clarify limitations:** More explicitly discuss the limitations of the greedy approach compared to full Bayesian inference (e.g., potential for local optima) and how this affects the interpretability of the resulting hierarchies.

5. **Improve comparison to Mondrian process:** Provide a more detailed comparison to [12] to better position the contribution of BHCD.

## Decision

This paper presents a technically sound and well-motivated approach to hierarchical community discovery with significant computational advantages over existing Bayesian methods. The method is clearly presented, and the experimental results demonstrate practical utility. While the theoretical foundation could be strengthened and the experimental evaluation expanded, the paper makes a valuable contribution to the field.

I recommend **acceptance** with the understanding that the authors address the suggested improvements in the camera-ready version. The computational efficiency of BHCD is particularly compelling for real-world applications, and the method could find significant use in network analysis where speed is a critical factor.
