This paper presents a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in networks. The core idea – leveraging a hierarchical tree prior within a Bayesian framework to enable efficient inference – is compelling and addresses a significant challenge in network analysis: scalable hierarchical community detection. The work builds logically from existing stochastic blockmodels and Bayesian approaches, and the authors correctly identify the computational bottlenecks of existing Bayesian nonparametric methods like IRM. The proposed approach demonstrably improves speed, achieving results two orders of magnitude faster than a single IRM iteration.

The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the model and algorithm. The mathematical notation is reasonably accessible, and the description of the greedy inference procedure is detailed. The experiments are well-chosen, starting with a qualitative analysis on Sampson’s monastery network, progressing to a quantitative comparison with IRM on a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and finally showcasing the method’s ability to uncover hierarchical structure in the full NIPS dataset. The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating a favorable speed-accuracy trade-off compared to IRM. The inclusion of BHCD sparse is a sensible extension catering to sparse network scenarios.

However, while the paper is strong, it doesn't entirely *revolutionize* the field. The core innovation lies in the efficient inference enabled by the greedy approach and tree prior, rather than a fundamentally new theoretical breakthrough. The reliance on a greedy algorithm, while providing scalability, inherently introduces suboptimality. While the authors acknowledge this, a more detailed discussion on the potential impact of the greedy approximation on the quality of the discovered hierarchy would be valuable. The connection to existing hierarchical clustering approaches (beyond the Bayesian framing) is somewhat underdeveloped; a clearer articulation of how BHCD differs from, and improves upon, purely greedy hierarchical clustering methods would strengthen the contribution. 

Furthermore, the hyperparameter choices (α = δ = 1.0, β = λ = 0.2, and γ = 0.4) appear somewhat arbitrary. Although the authors state they found these settings to work well, a more systematic exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity and their impact on the results is missing. The predictive performance on the NIPS dataset is good, but not dramatically superior to other methods. It would have been interesting to see a comparison with more recent, sophisticated link prediction baselines. While the paper shows good scalability, a discussion of memory requirements, particularly for very large networks, would also be beneficial.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of Bayesian hierarchical modeling with a greedy inference scheme for community detection is a novel and promising approach.
* **Scalability:** BHCD offers a significant speed improvement over existing Bayesian nonparametric methods, enabling analysis of larger networks.
* **Solid Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate the method's effectiveness on several datasets, including a comparison to a strong baseline (IRM).
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and explains the technical details clearly.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* **Greedy Approximation:** The greedy nature of the inference may lead to suboptimal solutions, and the impact of this approximation is not fully explored.
* **Limited Theoretical Depth:** The theoretical contributions are incremental; the paper primarily focuses on efficient implementation rather than fundamentally new theoretical insights.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Lack of a systematic hyperparameter study.
* **Incremental Improvement:** While effective, the improvement over state-of-the-art in terms of predictive performance is not substantial.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite minor weaknesses, I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of network analysis. The proposed BHCD method offers a compelling trade-off between scalability and accuracy, and the authors have demonstrated its effectiveness through thorough experimentation. The core idea of leveraging a hierarchical tree prior within a Bayesian framework is novel and has the potential to inspire further research. I lean towards **accepting** the paper, recognizing its significant practical value and potential for future development. While the paper doesn't present a paradigm shift, its contribution is substantial enough to warrant inclusion in NIPS, particularly given the emphasis on scalable methods for large-scale data analysis. I encourage the authors to address the points raised regarding hyperparameter sensitivity and the impact of the greedy approximation in a future version of the work.
