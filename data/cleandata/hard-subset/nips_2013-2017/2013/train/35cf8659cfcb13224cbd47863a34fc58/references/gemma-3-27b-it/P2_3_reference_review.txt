## Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. Building upon Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering, BHCD aims to address the computational limitations of existing Bayesian nonparametric methods (like the IRM) for hierarchical community detection. The authors propose a model based on a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels and an efficient inference scheme with a time complexity of O(n<sup>2</sup>log(n)), independent of the number of communities. They demonstrate BHCD’s speed and accuracy on several datasets, including Sampson’s monastery network and a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, showing significant speedups over the IRM while achieving comparable performance.

**Strengths:**

* **Computational Efficiency:** This is the primary strength of the paper.  The authors successfully address a major bottleneck in Bayesian hierarchical community detection – scalability.  The reported O(n<sup>2</sup>log(n)) complexity is a significant improvement over methods like the IRM, particularly for larger networks. The caching scheme described appears cleverly designed for efficiency.
* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The model is well-motivated, building on established concepts like stochastic blockmodels and Bayesian inference. The derivation of the likelihood and the recursive formula for marginal likelihood computation appear rigorous.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate a clear advantage in speed compared to the IRM, alongside competitive predictive performance. The application to the NIPS co-authorship network is particularly compelling, as it showcases the method's ability to handle real-world data.
* **Clear Explanation of Algorithm:** Algorithm 1 and the accompanying explanation provide a clear understanding of the inference procedure. The discussion of variations (sparse BHCD) and tie-breaking strategies adds to the practical usability of the method.
* **Addressing Limitations:** The authors acknowledge and address potential issues, such as the possibility of merging disconnected components, with the introduction of BHCD sparse.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Comparison to Non-Bayesian Methods:** While the comparison to the IRM is valuable, the paper lacks a comprehensive comparison to faster, non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering algorithms (e.g., those based on modularity optimization). This makes it harder to assess the trade-offs between accuracy, speed, and probabilistic modeling.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The impact of the hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) on the results is not thoroughly explored.  A more detailed sensitivity analysis would strengthen the paper. The justification for the chosen values seems somewhat arbitrary.
* **Predictive Performance Not State-of-the-Art:**  While competitive, the predictive performance on the NIPS dataset isn’t *better* than existing methods like ILA, as noted by the authors. More exploration of how to improve predictive capabilities (e.g., by using non-conjugate priors as suggested) would be beneficial.
* **Clarity of Hierarchy Visualization:** Figure 5, illustrating the hierarchy found in the full NIPS network, is somewhat difficult to interpret.  A more refined visualization technique might be helpful.
* **Discussion of Model Assumptions:** The paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the assumptions underlying the model, particularly the assumption of stochastic blockmodels.  How does the performance degrade when this assumption is violated?



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of community detection. The primary strength lies in its computational efficiency, which addresses a critical limitation of existing Bayesian nonparametric methods. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and well-motivated. While there are areas for improvement (e.g., more extensive comparisons, hyperparameter analysis, and better visualization), the strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses.  

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:**  Strong. The technical details are sound, and the authors demonstrate careful analysis and evaluation.
* **Clarity:** Very Good. The paper is generally well-written and organized, though some sections (e.g., the detailed mathematical derivations) could benefit from further simplification.
* **Originality:** Good. The combination of a Bayesian hierarchical model with a greedy inference scheme is novel and effectively addresses the scalability problem.
* **Significance:** Good. The method has the potential to be widely used for analyzing large-scale social networks. The improved efficiency makes Bayesian hierarchical community detection more practical.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Significant improvement in computational efficiency over existing Bayesian methods.
* Sound theoretical foundation and clear algorithmic description.
* Demonstrates competitive performance on benchmark datasets.
* Addresses an important problem in network analysis.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited comparison to non-Bayesian methods.
* Lack of thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
* Predictive performance is not state-of-the-art.



**Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept.** The paper's contributions in terms of efficiency and scalability are substantial, and the overall quality is high. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the work, but they do not detract from its overall value. I believe this paper would be a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.
