

# Review: Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel approach for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors propose a Bayesian nonparametric model that extends stochastic blockmodels to hierarchical structures, using a tree-based representation of community partitions. The key innovation is an efficient greedy algorithm for learning the hierarchy, with time complexity O(n² log n), which is two orders of magnitude faster than the IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance.

## Quality

The paper presents a technically sound approach with clear mathematical formulations. The authors carefully develop the hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels and derive the marginal likelihood using dynamic programming, which is a significant contribution. The algorithm (Algorithm 1) is well-justified and the complexity analysis is thorough, demonstrating the key advantage of BHCD over existing methods. The experimental section provides strong validation, with results on Sampson's monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship dataset showing that BHCD discovers meaningful hierarchical structure while achieving comparable predictive performance to the IRM. The authors acknowledge limitations, such as the potential for merging disconnected communities (addressed by the BHCD sparse variant), and provide a clear explanation of the trade-offs between binary and non-binary trees.

One minor weakness is the limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity. While the paper uses specific values (α = δ = 1.0, β = λ = 0.2, γ = 0.4), it doesn't thoroughly investigate how results might change with different hyperparameter settings, which could be important for practitioners.

## Clarity

The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly establishes the problem and relates it to prior work. The mathematical formulations are precise and well-motivated, with appropriate notation that facilitates understanding. The algorithm description is clear, and the pseudocode is easy to follow. The experimental results are well-presented with helpful visualizations (Figures 3-5) that effectively illustrate the hierarchical structures discovered. The paper provides sufficient detail for an expert to reproduce the results, including implementation specifics like the use of a heap for efficient merge selection and the caching of sufficient statistics.

## Originality

BHCD makes a meaningful contribution to the field by addressing a gap in existing Bayesian approaches to hierarchical community discovery. While previous work (like the IRM and Mondrian process) has explored hierarchical structures, these methods either scale poorly or are limited to specific tree structures. The authors explicitly identify these limitations in the introduction and position their work as filling this gap. The key novelty lies in the efficient greedy algorithm that uses dynamic programming with a novel caching scheme, enabling scalability to larger networks while maintaining the Bayesian framework. The paper's comparison with related work is thorough and well-referenced, clearly establishing how BHCD differs from previous approaches.

## Significance

The significance of this work is substantial. Hierarchical community discovery is an important problem in social network analysis, with applications in understanding scientific collaboration, political affiliations, and other social structures. BHCD's two orders of magnitude speed improvement over the IRM makes it practical for large-scale networks, which is crucial for real-world applications. The paper demonstrates this through experiments on the NIPS co-authorship network, showing that BHCD achieves comparable predictive performance to the IRM in a fraction of the time. The sparse variant (BHCD sparse) further improves efficiency on sparse networks like the NIPS dataset, making it particularly relevant for real-world applications where networks are often sparse. The method's ability to handle hierarchical structure while maintaining Bayesian probabilistic interpretation addresses a gap in the literature that has been recognized by previous work (e.g., [8, 9, 10]).

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong submission that makes a clear technical contribution to the field of community detection in networks. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and addresses an important problem with significant practical implications. The proposed BHCD algorithm offers a compelling balance between computational efficiency and model expressiveness, making it a valuable addition to the toolbox of network analysis methods.

The primary strength of this work is its practical significance: it solves a real problem (scalable hierarchical community discovery) with an elegant and efficient algorithm that can be applied to large networks. The paper's contributions are clearly differentiated from prior work, and the experimental validation is thorough and convincing.

I see two minor weaknesses: the limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity, and the lack of comparison with non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods (though the paper's focus is clearly on Bayesian approaches). However, these do not significantly detract from the paper's overall contribution.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance. This paper makes a significant contribution to the field with a technically sound approach that is both novel and practically valuable. The method's efficiency and comparable performance to state-of-the-art Bayesian methods make it particularly relevant for NIPS, where scalability to large datasets is increasingly important. The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a strong case for its contributions through both theoretical analysis and empirical validation.
