

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a method for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors extend Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data by modeling communities as a nested hierarchy of stochastic blockmodels. The key innovation is an efficient greedy agglomerative inference algorithm that computes marginal likelihoods using dynamic programming and sufficient statistics caching, resulting in O(n² log n) time complexity. This is orders of magnitude faster than existing Bayesian methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM), while achieving comparable predictive performance.

The work builds upon stochastic blockmodels [1], the Infinite Relational Model [5-7], and Bayesian hierarchical clustering [13,14]. Unlike previous Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods, which either have high computational costs [8,9,10] or are limited to specific hierarchical depths [9,10,11], BHCD efficiently handles arbitrary hierarchical structures. The paper also addresses limitations of non-probabilistic greedy hierarchical clustering by providing a full probabilistic account of the data.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with rigorous mathematical formulation. The inference algorithm is well-justified, and the complexity analysis is thorough. The experimental results support the claims about speed and performance, with clear comparisons to IRM on Sampson's monastery network and NIPS co-authorship data. The authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., potential merging of disconnected communities) and address them with the BHCD sparse variant. The model's probabilistic foundation is sound, and the marginal likelihood calculation via dynamic programming is well-executed.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. Mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined. Algorithm 1 provides a precise description of the inference procedure, and the figures effectively illustrate the hierarchical structure discovered. The explanation of the predictive distribution (Equation 14) is particularly clear. The paper provides sufficient detail for reproducibility, including hyperparameter choices and implementation specifics.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear contribution by providing an efficient Bayesian approach to hierarchical community detection. The key novelty lies in the inference algorithm's efficiency, which distinguishes it from previous Bayesian methods. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from related work, noting that previous methods either have high computational costs [8,9,10], are limited to specific depths [9,10,11], or are non-probabilistic [3,4]. The paper includes a thorough literature review that situates the work within the broader context of community detection.

**Significance:** The method addresses a significant practical challenge in network analysis by enabling scalable hierarchical community detection. The speed advantage (two orders of magnitude faster than IRM) makes the approach applicable to larger networks than previous Bayesian methods. The hierarchical structures discovered align with domain knowledge in the Sampson's monastery example, and the method produces reasonable predictive performance on the NIPS co-authorship network. The paper's focus on computational efficiency advances the state of the art in scalable community detection.

**Strengths:**
- Clear technical contribution with well-justified algorithmic improvements
- Comprehensive experimental validation across multiple datasets
- Thorough comparison with relevant baselines (IRM, LFRM, ILA)
- Practical significance for real-world network analysis
- Well-written with clear mathematical exposition

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity (α = δ = 1.0, β = λ = 0.2, γ = 0.4 are stated to "work well" without systematic analysis)
- Sparse variant (BHCD sparse) is not thoroughly evaluated beyond speed claims
- Comparison with non-Bayesian hierarchical methods (e.g., greedy hierarchical clustering) is limited
- The paper does not discuss potential limitations of the hierarchical structure assumption for certain network types

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

Pro:
- Provides a significant computational advance over existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods
- Technical contribution is clear, well-executed, and rigorously justified
- Experimental results convincingly demonstrate speed advantages without sacrificing predictive performance
- Well-written with sufficient detail for reproducibility
- Addresses a meaningful problem with practical applications in network analysis

Con:
- Hyperparameter selection lacks systematic evaluation
- Comparison with non-Bayesian methods is limited
- Sparse variant is not fully explored
- Does not address potential limitations of hierarchical structure for certain networks

Overall, this paper makes a substantial contribution to the field of community detection by providing an efficient Bayesian method for hierarchical community discovery. The technical soundness, clarity, and experimental validation are strong, and the method's speed advantage is particularly noteworthy for real-world applications. While the hyperparameter analysis and comparative evaluation could be strengthened, these do not undermine the paper's core contribution. The work advances the state of the art in scalable community detection and is well-suited for NeurIPS.
