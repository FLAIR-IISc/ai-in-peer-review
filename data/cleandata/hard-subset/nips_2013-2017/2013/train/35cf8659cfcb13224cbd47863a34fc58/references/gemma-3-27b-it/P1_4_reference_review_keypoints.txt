- Summary: Introduces BHCD, a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method for community detection that models networks as hierarchical mixtures of stochastic blockmodels and is much faster than alternatives like IRM while retaining competitive performance.

- Relation to Previous Work: Positions BHCD between flat stochastic blockmodels and fully Bayesian nonparametric approaches, building on prior greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering and extending it to relational data.

- Strength — Computational Efficiency: BHCD is significantly faster than IRM with claimed O(n^2 log n) complexity and an effective caching scheme.

- Strength — Bayesian Framework: Maintains a principled probabilistic approach to community discovery within a Bayesian framework.

- Strength — Hierarchical Structure: Naturally captures hierarchical community organization common in real-world networks.

- Strength — Clear Presentation: Paper is well-written with helpful figures and clear explanations of model and inference.

- Strength — Empirical Validation: Demonstrates speed and accuracy on Sampson’s network, a NIPS subset, and the full NIPS network.

- Strength — Practical Improvement (BHCD Sparse): Introducing a sparse variant reduces computation on non-existent edges, improving practicality for sparse networks.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Lacks exploration of sensitivity to hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) and their impact on results.

- Weakness — Limited Comparison to Non-Bayesian Methods: Does not compare to simpler non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering approaches (e.g., modularity-based methods).

- Weakness — Tie-Breaking Variability: Random tie-breaking during merges may introduce result variability; restarts are used but tie-breaking strategies are not systematically explored.

- Weakness — Scalability Beyond Density: O(n^2 log n) still limits applicability to extremely large, dense networks; further scaling strategies are needed.

- Weakness — Predictive Performance: Predictive performance on NIPS is competitive but not clearly superior; lacks statistical significance analysis.

- Argument for Acceptance: Presents a novel, efficient Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method with promising empirical results and clear writing.

- Argument for Acceptance: Demonstrates practical scalability improvements and thoughtful identification of limitations and future work.

- Argument Against Acceptance: Insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and limited baseline comparisons weaken empirical evaluation.

- Argument Against Acceptance: Random tie-breaking and only competitive (not superior) predictive performance raise concerns.

- Overall Assessment: Strong, technically sound, and empirically validated contribution with computational efficiency as the primary advantage; minor weaknesses do not outweigh merits.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 8/10.
