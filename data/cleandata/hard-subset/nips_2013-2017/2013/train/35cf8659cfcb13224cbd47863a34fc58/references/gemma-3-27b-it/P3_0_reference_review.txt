## Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method (BHCD) for discovering community structure in social networks. The authors build upon existing Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering, addressing limitations in scalability present in methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM). BHCD utilizes a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels, leveraging a tree structure to define a prior on partitions, enabling efficient marginal likelihood calculation. The authors demonstrate the speed and accuracy of BHCD on Sampson’s monastery network and a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, showing significant speedups compared to IRM while maintaining competitive predictive performance.

**Relationship to Prior Work:**

The paper effectively situates itself within the landscape of community detection methods. It correctly identifies the limitations of both parametric approaches (Stochastic Blockmodels, Mixed Membership Stochastic Blockmodels) requiring pre-defined community numbers, and fully Bayesian nonparametric methods (IRM, Mondrian process) suffering from computational cost.  The authors appropriately highlight previous Bayesian greedy hierarchical clustering approaches ([13, 14]) as a foundation for their work, focusing on extending these to relational data.  The comparison to existing methods, particularly IRM and LFRM, is crucial for establishing the contributions of BHCD. The referencing of relevant works (e.g., [1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21]) is comprehensive and demonstrates a solid understanding of the field.

**Strengths:**

* **Scalability:** The primary strength of BHCD is its computational efficiency. The claimed O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) complexity, combined with the caching scheme, is a significant improvement over many existing Bayesian approaches, allowing for application to larger networks. The empirical results on NIPS-234 clearly demonstrate this speed advantage.
* **Probabilistic Framework:**  BHCD provides a fully probabilistic account of the discovered community structure, unlike purely greedy hierarchical clustering methods.  This allows for uncertainty quantification and principled model selection.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The derivation of the marginal likelihood and the recursive computation scheme provide a strong theoretical foundation for the method.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the explanations are relatively easy to follow, even for readers unfamiliar with all the technical details.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The experiments on both Sampson’s monastery network and the NIPS co-authorship data provide compelling evidence of the method's effectiveness.  The qualitative results on Sampson’s network are insightful, and the quantitative results on NIPS-234 demonstrate competitive performance.
* **Practical Variants:** The introduction of BHCD sparse and the consideration of binary vs. non-binary trees show a pragmatic approach to optimizing the algorithm for different network types.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors set hyperparameters that "worked well," a more thorough investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity and its impact on performance would be beneficial.  How robust are the results to different choices of α, β, δ, λ, and γ?
* **Limited Comparison:**  Although the comparison to IRM and LFRM is valuable,  a more comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art community detection algorithms (e.g., Louvain, Leiden) would strengthen the paper. Specifically, including the runtime of these algorithms alongside BHCD in Table 1 would be helpful.
* **Theoretical Scalability vs. Practical Performance:** The theoretical complexity of O(n<sup>2</sup> log n) is promising, but the empirical runtime on the full NIPS network isn't reported. It would be useful to see how the algorithm scales to even larger networks.
* **Interpretation of Hierarchies:** While the visualizations of the discovered hierarchies are helpful, further discussion of how to interpret these hierarchies (e.g., what do the different levels of the hierarchy represent?) would add value. Specifically, the utility of having a probabilistic account of the hierarchy over purely deterministic methods isn't made explicit.
* **Predictive Performance:** The predictive performance on NIPS-234 is good, but still slightly behind ILA, which is mentioned. Further exploration of why BHCD isn't achieving comparable performance to the best methods and potential avenues for improvement would be beneficial.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*  BHCD presents a significant contribution to the field of community detection by offering a scalable and probabilistically sound method for discovering hierarchical community structures.
* The theoretical foundation is strong, and the empirical results demonstrate its effectiveness and efficiency.
* The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and adequately references related work.
* The practical variants (BHCD sparse, binary trees) showcase a thoughtful approach to optimizing the algorithm for real-world applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a more comprehensive comparison to other state-of-the-art community detection algorithms.
* The limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and the impact on performance.
* The limited empirical evaluation on very large networks.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential. The authors address an important problem (scalable hierarchical community detection) and propose a novel and effective solution. The weaknesses identified above are relatively minor and could be addressed with further experimentation and analysis.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.** I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions. Specifically, I suggest the authors:

1.  Include a more comprehensive comparison to other relevant community detection algorithms.
2.  Investigate and discuss the sensitivity of the results to different hyperparameter settings.
3.  Report the runtime of BHCD on the full NIPS network.
4.  Expand the discussion on interpreting the discovered hierarchies.
