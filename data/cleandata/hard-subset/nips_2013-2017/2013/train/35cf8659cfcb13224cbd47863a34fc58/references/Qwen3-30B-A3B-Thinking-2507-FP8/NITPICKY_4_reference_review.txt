

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper proposes a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery (BHCD) method that extends greedy hierarchical clustering to relational data. The authors claim BHCD is two orders of magnitude faster than IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance. While the paper addresses an important problem in network analysis, it contains multiple technical inconsistencies, unclear notations, and insufficient experimental support that significantly undermine its scientific contribution.

## Major Technical Issues

The paper suffers from critical notational inconsistencies that make key equations difficult to interpret. In Section 2, equation (1) defines "n1pq as the total number of edges in D between the vertices in clusters p and q," but for undirected graphs, this would double-count edges. The paper claims "σpq = σqp" but fails to clarify whether this applies to the counts (n1pq, n0pq) or the probabilities.

Section 3 contains the most severe notational problems. The prior on partitions (equation 7) uses undefined terms "ancestorT(φ)" and "subtreeT(φ)," while equation (9) states "σ¬chSS = σSS − ∑_{C∈ch(S)} σCC" without explaining why this subtraction would yield the correct sufficient statistics for edges between children. The marginal likelihood equation (10) appears to contain a typo with "g(σ¬chTT)" instead of the correct "g(σ¬chSS)" notation.

The paper claims "the marginal likelihood (6) may be calculated by dynamic programming, in O(n + m) time" (Section 3), yet later claims "Algorithm 1 has time complexity in O(n^2 log(n))" (Section 4). This contradiction is never resolved, and the paper fails to explain how the caching mechanism (mentioned in Section 4) actually enables the O(n^2 log n) complexity.

## Methodological Weaknesses

The experimental section contains several issues. The paper claims "BHCD sparse restricted to binary trees" but never defines what "binary" means in this context (e.g., whether it restricts the tree to binary structure or the merging process). The log-likelihood values reported for Figure 3 (−6.62 vs. −22.80) appear inconsistent with typical likelihood values for social network data.

The paper claims "BHCD is able to make reasonable predictions before the IRM has completed a single Gibbs scan" but provides no supporting data (e.g., time-to-accuracy curves). The comparison with IRM in Section 5 is limited to a single dataset (NIPS-234), and the paper fails to compare with other hierarchical community detection methods beyond IRM.

## Presentation and Clarity Issues

The paper contains multiple grammatical errors (e.g., "The Mondrian process [12] propose a flexible prior" should be "proposes"). The references exhibit inconsistent formatting, with some entries missing page numbers or volume information. The paper refers to "Figure 1" and "Figure 2" without providing them, making it impossible to assess the claims about these figures.

The authors claim their method "builds upon Bayesian approaches to greedy hierarchical clustering [13, 14]," but fail to clearly explain how their approach differs from these prior works. The paper would benefit from a more precise comparison of the technical innovations.

## Strengths and Weaknesses Summary

**Strengths:**
- Addresses an important problem in network analysis
- Provides a novel Bayesian approach to hierarchical community discovery
- Demonstrates good speed improvements over IRM in experiments

**Weaknesses:**
- Critical notational inconsistencies that undermine technical claims
- Contradictory complexity claims (O(n+m) vs. O(n^2 log n))
- Insufficient experimental evidence to support speed and performance claims
- Grammatical and formatting errors
- Ambiguous definitions of key terms

## Recommendation

While the paper addresses an interesting problem, the technical inconsistencies and lack of clarity in the core methodology make it difficult to assess the validity of the claims. The notational errors in key equations (Section 3) are particularly problematic as they prevent a proper understanding of the proposed model. The paper would require substantial revision to clarify the model, resolve the complexity contradictions, and provide more comprehensive experimental validation.

The paper also fails to adequately compare with other hierarchical community detection methods beyond IRM, which weakens the significance claim. The experimental results are insufficient to support the strong claims about speed and performance.

Given the paper's technical flaws and the fact that the core contribution is not clearly distinguishable from prior work due to the unclear presentation, I recommend rejection. The paper would benefit from a complete rewrite to clarify the notation, resolve the complexity contradictions, and provide more comprehensive experimental validation.
