

# Peer Review: Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery

## Summary of Main Ideas

This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a method for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors address a key limitation of existing Bayesian approaches to hierarchical community detection: computational inefficiency. While methods like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) and its extensions can infer hierarchical community structure, they often scale poorly with network size. BHCD proposes a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering approach that leverages a tree-based representation of community hierarchies and uses conjugate priors for computational efficiency.

The method builds upon the stochastic blockmodel framework, extending it to a hierarchical mixture model where each internal node of a tree represents a community, and leaves represent individual vertices. The key innovation is an efficient greedy algorithm for building the community hierarchy that runs in O(n² log n) time, significantly faster than previous Bayesian methods. The authors demonstrate BHCD's effectiveness on Sampson's monastery network, a subset of the NIPS co-authorship network, and the full NIPS network, showing that it achieves comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art methods while being orders of magnitude faster.

## Quality

The paper presents a technically sound approach with a clear mathematical foundation. The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods (particularly their computational inefficiency) and propose a well-motivated solution. The methodological development is rigorous, with proper treatment of the hierarchical mixture model and the marginal likelihood computation.

However, the paper lacks sufficient theoretical analysis of the method's properties. While the computational complexity is well-established, there's no analysis of the statistical properties of the proposed greedy approach—how close does it get to the optimal hierarchical community structure? The paper claims "good results" but doesn't provide theoretical guarantees or bounds on the quality of the solution relative to the optimal.

The experimental results are generally well-designed, but there are some gaps:
1. The paper doesn't adequately address the sensitivity of results to hyperparameter choices (α, β, δ, λ, γ). While they mention they "found these to work well," a more thorough exploration would strengthen the paper.
2. The comparison with non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods (e.g., HCS, HIER) is missing, which would help contextualize the method's performance.
3. The paper states BHCD is "two orders of magnitude faster than a single iteration of the IRM," but doesn't clarify whether this is for a single iteration or for convergence (which IRM may require many iterations to achieve).

## Clarity

The paper is generally well-written and well-organized. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the key concepts (stochastic blockmodels, hierarchical structure, marginal likelihood computation) are clearly explained. The algorithm description (Algorithm 1) is particularly clear, with well-explained steps and the caching mechanism that enables the O(n² log n) complexity.

One notable improvement would be to include a more detailed explanation of why the greedy approach works well for hierarchical community discovery. The paper states "the assumption that local community structure should depend only on the local connectivity structure" but doesn't elaborate on why this is a reasonable assumption for hierarchical community detection.

The experimental section is clear, but the description of the "BHCD global" variant (which "is lead astray by out of date scores on its heap") is confusing. It would be helpful to have a more precise explanation of what "out of date scores" means in this context.

## Originality

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field by addressing a significant practical limitation of Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods. While the hierarchical mixture model framework builds on previous work (e.g., [14] on Bayesian rose trees), the specific greedy algorithm with its caching mechanism for efficient computation is novel.

However, the paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate itself from more recent work on hierarchical community detection. For example, the paper doesn't cite or discuss the work by [21] on "On triangular versus edge representations" or the more recent methods like Hierarchical Dirichlet Process (HDP) for community detection, which have also addressed computational efficiency. The related work section would benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with recent advances.

The paper also doesn't clearly articulate how the hierarchical structure discovered by BHCD provides additional insights compared to flat community structure. While the authors mention that "within science, the community of physicists may be split into those working on various branches of physics," they don't demonstrate how BHCD's hierarchical structure yields more interpretable or useful insights than flat community structure on the datasets they examine.

## Significance

The computational efficiency claim is significant—being two orders of magnitude faster than existing Bayesian methods could make hierarchical community detection practical for larger networks. This is especially valuable for applications where computational resources are limited or where fast iterative analysis is needed.

However, the paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate that the hierarchical structure discovered by BHCD provides substantially better insights or predictive performance than flat community structure. The paper shows that BHCD finds a reasonable hierarchy on Sampson's network, but doesn't clearly explain how this hierarchy provides additional value compared to a flat community structure.

The claim that BHCD "attains comparable performance" to IRM is somewhat vague. The paper compares predictive performance using log predictive probability, accuracy, and AUC, but doesn't provide sufficient detail on how much better or worse BHCD performs relative to IRM. It would be helpful to know whether the predictive performance difference is statistically significant.

## Specific Comments and Suggestions

1. **Theoretical Justification for Greedy Approach:** The paper should provide stronger theoretical justification for why the greedy approach would work well for hierarchical community detection. What properties of the hierarchical structure make this greedy approach effective? Is there a connection to submodularity or other optimization properties?

2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity Analysis:** The paper should include a sensitivity analysis showing how the results change with different hyperparameter choices, particularly γ (which controls the tree structure). This is important for practitioners who will want to use the method.

3. **Comparison with Non-Bayesian Methods:** The paper should compare BHCD with state-of-the-art non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods (e.g., HCS, HIER) to better contextualize its performance.

4. **Interpretability of Hierarchical Structure:** The paper should provide more detailed analysis of the hierarchical structure discovered on the NIPS dataset. How does this hierarchy reflect the actual research areas and collaborations in the NIPS community? What insights does the hierarchy provide that flat community structure would miss?

5. **Implementation Details:** The paper claims "BHCD sparse" is faster for sparse networks, but doesn't provide specific details about how the sparsity is exploited in the algorithm. A more detailed explanation would be helpful.

6. **Connection to Recent Work:** The paper should be updated to include and discuss more recent work on hierarchical community detection, particularly methods that have also focused on computational efficiency.

## Overall Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Addresses a significant computational limitation in existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods
- Clear, well-organized presentation of the method
- Demonstrates substantial computational advantages over previous Bayesian methods
- Provides a practical implementation that can be applied to real-world networks
- Includes a useful "BHCD sparse" variant for sparse networks

**Weaknesses:**
- Lacks sufficient theoretical analysis of the method's properties
- Doesn't fully differentiate from more recent work in the field
- Doesn't clearly demonstrate the additional value of hierarchical structure beyond flat community structure
- Limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity
- Missing comparison with non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods

## Recommendation

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field by addressing a significant computational bottleneck in Bayesian hierarchical community detection. The method is well-motivated, technically sound, and demonstrates clear practical advantages. However, the paper would benefit from stronger theoretical justification for the greedy approach, more comprehensive comparison with recent work, and better demonstration of the added value of hierarchical structure.

I recommend **acceptance with minor revisions**. The paper has significant potential to be of interest to the NIPS community, but the revisions suggested above would strengthen the contribution and make it more compelling.

The authors should address the theoretical gaps, provide more detailed comparisons with recent work, and better demonstrate the value of the hierarchical structure discovered. These changes would make the paper significantly stronger while maintaining its core contribution of a computationally efficient Bayesian hierarchical community detection method.
