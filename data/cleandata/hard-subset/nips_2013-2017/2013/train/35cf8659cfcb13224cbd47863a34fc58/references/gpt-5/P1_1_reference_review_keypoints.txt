- Summary: Proposes BHCD, a greedy Bayesian agglomerative method adapting Bayesian hierarchical clustering/rose trees to stochastic blockmodel marginals, using a tree-constrained marginal likelihood and local Bayes-factor merges for scalable hierarchical community discovery.

- Relation to prior work: Extends BHC/Bayesian Rose Trees to relational data; connects to IRM, hierarchical blockmodels, Mondrian process, and hierarchical random graphs.

- Strength: Conceptual simplicity—clear adaptation of Bayesian hierarchical clustering to blockmodel data with a well-defined recursion and local scoring.

- Strength: Speed—bottom-up greedy agglomeration with cached sufficient statistics yields practical scalability and an O(n^2 log n) procedure; sparse variant helps on sparse graphs.

- Strength: Predictive distribution—derives an interpretable recursive predictive link distribution along tree paths.

- Strength: Empirical demonstration—qualitative results on Sampson, competitive predictive performance vs IRM on NIPS-234 with much lower wall-clock time, and a large hierarchy on full NIPS.

- Weakness: Prior inconsistency—the experiments use α=δ=1.0, β=λ=0.2 despite motivating α>β, δ<λ, undermining the claim that priors encode community structure and yielding a high prior mean.

- Weakness: Baselines—comparisons limited to IRM; missing comparisons/discussion with Hierarchical Random Graph and other hierarchical blockmodels, and some cited results (ILA/LFRM) are not directly replicated.

- Weakness: Runtime claims—statements about being “two orders of magnitude faster” than IRM are unclear/implausible as written and need hardware/implementation/stopping-criterion details.

- Weakness: Objective and guarantees—no discussion of consistency, approximation quality, or failure modes of the greedy local Bayes-factor merging; sensitivity to random restarts not reported.

- Weakness: Modeling limitations—single shared f/g likelihood across all nodes/levels prevents capturing community- or level-specific heterogeneity; sparse-merge heuristic may fail for disassortative/bipartite or missing-data settings.

- Weakness: Clarity gaps—terms like “BHCD global” and notation (e.g., DTT) are undefined or terse; more reproducibility details needed for dynamic programming and caching.

- Quality: Core derivations are technically sound and mirror BHC/rose trees, but hyperparameter inconsistency is a serious experimental flaw and reporting lacks reproducibility details (code, hardware, runtimes, restarts).

- Clarity: Generally clear with helpful equations/algorithms, but needs explicit definitions of variants, pseudocode for caching, and clearer complexity statements for the sparse variant.

- Originality: Moderate—novelty lies in applying Bayesian rose trees to blockmodel marginals and efficient local scoring/caching rather than a fundamentally new model.

- Significance: Moderate—a fast Bayesian hierarchical community method is useful for exploratory analysis and link prediction, but broader impact requires stronger baselines, hyperparameter learning, and larger evaluations.

- Pros for acceptance: Elegant, fast method with clear Bayesian semantics; practical recursion for predictions; principled bridge from BHC to relational data.

- Cons for acceptance: Inconsistent hyperparameters vs stated modeling intent; limited/incomparable baselines and unclear runtime claims; no sensitivity analysis to restarts/γ; limited expressivity due to shared f/g and fixed priors.

- Key questions for authors: Explain and justify prior settings or provide sensitivity/hyperparameter learning; provide hardware/implementation/stopping-criteria and candidate counts; compare vs Hierarchical Random Graph or discuss differences; report sensitivity to γ and restarts R and variance across runs; analyze impact of forbidding edge-less merges on disassortative/missing-data cases; discuss extending model to level/node-specific parameters.

- Minor issues: Incomplete citations/placeholders; define BHCD variants earlier; clarify O(n+m) computation claim with memory usage; provide pseudocode and reproducibility details.

- Recommendation: Weak reject pending revisions addressing hyperparameter inconsistencies, baseline comparisons, sensitivity analyses, and clarity/reproducibility improvements.
