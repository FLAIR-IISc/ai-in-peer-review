- Proposes BHCD: a greedy Bayesian method extending BHC/Rose Trees to relational data using an SBM with Beta-Bernoulli conjugate priors.  
- A tree T induces a prior over tree-consistent partitions; marginal likelihood p(D|T) computed exactly via a BHC-like dynamic program.  
- Inference uses a bottom-up agglomerative procedure with three merge types, local Bayes factor scoring, cached sufficient statistics, and O(n^2 log n) worst-case time.  
- A sparse variant forbids merges between disconnected components; link prediction mixes on-/off-diagonal posteriors along tree paths.  
- Strength: principled tree-induced prior and exact marginalization over tree-consistent partitions with a simple recursion.  
- Strength: efficient inference via caching and local scoring yielding practical scalability and a sparse option for large graphs.  
- Strength: practical results—reasonable predictive performance on NIPS-234 much faster than IRM, sensible Sampson network hierarchy, and large-scale NIPS hierarchy demonstration.  
- Strength: honest positioning acknowledging speed-oriented greedy inference sacrifices some predictive accuracy compared to models like ILA.  
- Weakness: merge score is local and ignores effects on edges outside the merged set, which can cause suboptimal merges under degree heterogeneity or long-range dependencies.  
- Weakness: uses standard (non-degree-corrected) Bernoulli SBM, problematic for heavy-tailed degree networks; degree correction could improve accuracy.  
- Weakness: hyperparameters (α,β,δ,λ,γ) are fixed and not inferred; no sensitivity or robustness analysis provided.  
- Weakness: limited experimental baselines—no comparison to hierarchical random graphs (Clauset et al. 2008), Mondrian relational models, or modern hierarchical SBMs.  
- Weakness: claim of being two orders of magnitude faster than a single IRM iteration seems extreme and needs clarification of measurement methodology.  
- Weakness/clarity: predictive complexity description is inconsistent—prediction should be O(depth) not Θ(n); needs clarification.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but notation gets dense; some symbols and equations should be standardized for readability.  
- Clarity: references contain typos/incomplete entries and should include Clauset-Moore-Newman (2008) for hierarchical random graphs.  
- Complexity: O(n+m) dynamic programming claim for a fixed tree is plausible but would benefit from a brief derivation or pseudocode.  
- Originality: novel combination of BHC-style tree-consistent priors with SBM likelihood and an agglomerative caching scheme for relational data.  
- Significance: provides a practical, fast Bayesian tool for hierarchical community detection useful for exploratory analysis and as a baseline despite not being SOTA for link prediction.  
- Suggestions: analyze sensitivity/learn γ (e.g., Empirical Bayes or cross-validation) and report robustness to within/between priors.  
- Suggestions: characterize sparse variant complexity in terms of m and explore data structures for o(n^2) behavior on sparse graphs.  
- Suggestions: consider integrating degree-corrected SBMs with conjugate priors into the f/g framework.  
- Suggestions: for NIPS-234, clarify whether results are single-run or averaged over R=50 restarts and report wall-clock times including restarts.  
- Suggestion: add comparisons to hierarchical random graphs and modern hierarchical SBMs on at least one dataset.  
- Pros summary: elegant Bayesian formulation, fast scalable inference, sensible hierarchies, clear reproducible algorithm.  
- Cons summary: limited hierarchical baselines, no degree correction, greedy local merges can be myopic with variance from tie-breaking, fixed hyperparameters, minor clarity/reference issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept—useful and efficient contribution but would be stronger with degree correction, clarified complexity/experimental details, and broader comparisons.
