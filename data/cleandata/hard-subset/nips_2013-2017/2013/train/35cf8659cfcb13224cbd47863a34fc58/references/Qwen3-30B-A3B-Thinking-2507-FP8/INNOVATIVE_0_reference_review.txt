

# Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors address a significant limitation in existing Bayesian methods for hierarchical community detection: computational intractability for larger networks. Previous work like the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) and its extensions [5-11] either require knowing the hierarchy depth in advance or suffer from poor scalability. BHCD provides a greedy Bayesian hierarchical clustering method that achieves two orders of magnitude faster inference than a single iteration of IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance.

The paper makes several key contributions: (1) a Bayesian hierarchical model that uses a tree structure to define partitions consistent with the hierarchy, (2) a dynamic programming approach to compute marginal likelihoods efficiently, and (3) an agglomerative inference algorithm with O(n² log n) complexity. The authors clearly articulate how their approach differs from previous work, particularly by avoiding the computational bottlenecks of existing Bayesian hierarchical methods.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The mathematical formulation is precise, and the experimental results demonstrate the practical advantages of BHCD. The authors compare their method to IRM on a co-authorship network, showing that BHCD achieves comparable predictive performance to IRM but in about 1/100th the time. The experiments on Sampson's monastery network demonstrate the method's ability to recover meaningful hierarchical community structure.

**Strengths:**

1. **Novel Computational Approach:** The key innovation is the efficient greedy inference algorithm that avoids the combinatorial explosion of previous Bayesian hierarchical methods. The caching scheme enabling O(1) inner loop operations is particularly elegant and represents a significant methodological advance.

2. **Strong Practical Impact:** The paper addresses a real bottleneck in network analysis - scalability of hierarchical community detection. The two orders of magnitude speedup makes hierarchical community discovery feasible for larger networks that were previously intractable for Bayesian methods.

3. **Clear Differentiation from Prior Work:** The authors thoroughly contextualize their contribution against existing methods, explicitly stating how BHCD overcomes limitations of previous approaches (e.g., [8-11] require known depth, [12] has poor scaling).

4. **Good Experimental Validation:** The experiments cover multiple datasets and demonstrate both the computational advantages and the quality of the discovered hierarchies.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Theoretical Guarantees:** While the paper provides strong empirical results, it doesn't offer theoretical guarantees about the quality of the discovered hierarchies or how the greedy approach compares to optimal hierarchical community detection.

2. **Comparison to Non-Bayesian Methods:** The paper primarily compares to other Bayesian methods but doesn't engage with non-Bayesian hierarchical community detection approaches like hierarchical clustering [3,4], which could provide context for the method's relative strengths.

3. **Scalability on Very Large Networks:** While the paper demonstrates good performance on networks with hundreds of vertices, it doesn't explore how BHCD scales to networks with thousands or millions of vertices, where the O(n² log n) complexity could become problematic.

4. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper uses fixed hyperparameters (α = δ = 1.0, β = λ = 0.2, γ = 0.4), but doesn't explore how sensitive the results are to these choices.

**Recommendation:**

This paper presents a significant methodological advance that addresses a real bottleneck in network analysis. The authors have developed a novel algorithm that achieves a remarkable speedup over existing Bayesian hierarchical community detection methods while maintaining comparable predictive performance. The paper's core contribution - the efficient greedy inference algorithm - is both technically sound and practically valuable.

While the paper has some limitations in theoretical guarantees and scalability on extremely large networks, these are common for a methodological contribution of this nature. The reviewers should recognize that the primary contribution here is a novel computational approach, not necessarily a theoretical breakthrough. The method's speed and practical applicability represent a meaningful advancement in the field.

The paper's novelty is clear and significant, and it makes a compelling case for a new direction in hierarchical community detection. It directly addresses a problem that has hindered the adoption of Bayesian hierarchical methods in practice. Given the NIPS emphasis on novel approaches that enable new directions in research, this paper aligns well with the conference's priorities.

**Pro Acceptance Arguments:**
- Novel greedy inference algorithm with significant computational advantages
- Clear differentiation from prior work with strong experimental validation
- Addresses a real bottleneck in network analysis
- Practical impact on hierarchical community detection for larger networks

**Con Rejection Arguments:**
- Limited theoretical guarantees
- Some comparison gaps (non-Bayesian methods)
- Scalability limits not fully explored

Given the emphasis on novelty and bold approaches, and the clear practical significance of the work, I recommend acceptance. The paper introduces a significant new concept that enables a previously intractable approach to become practical, which aligns with the conference's values. The minor methodological limitations do not outweigh the paper's novel contribution to the field.
