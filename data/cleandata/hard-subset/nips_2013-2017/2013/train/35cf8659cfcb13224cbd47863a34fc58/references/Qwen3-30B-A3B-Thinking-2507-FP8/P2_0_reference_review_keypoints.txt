1. Introduces BHCD: a Bayesian hierarchical model for discovering community hierarchies in networks using a stochastic blockmodel with Beta-Bernoulli priors.  
2. Represents communities as a nested tree where internal nodes are communities and leaves are vertices.  
3. Derives marginal likelihood via dynamic programming and gives clear mathematical formulation (Equations 2–10).  
4. Presents an efficient greedy inference algorithm (Algorithm 1) with O(n² log n) complexity.  
5. Claims two orders of magnitude faster runtime than IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance.  
6. Demonstrates results on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship networks, showing meaningful hierarchies.  
7. Paper is technically sound with reproducible details and thorough complexity analysis.  
8. Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing BHCD variants (sparse, binary, etc.) against IRM, LFRM, ILA on likelihood and runtime.  
9. Acknowledges limitations like tendency to merge disconnected communities and addresses them with BHCD-sparse.  
10. Strength: clear theoretical foundation and well-explained derivations.  
11. Strength: thorough experimental comparisons and algorithmic analysis.  
12. Strength: sufficient implementation/hyperparameter details for reproducibility.  
13. Weakness: insufficient demonstration of why hierarchical models are better than flat models for tested datasets.  
14. Weakness: limited analysis of interpretability or downstream utility comparing BHCD hierarchies to IRM flat structures.  
15. Weakness: claim that non-binary trees better explain data is under-supported beyond higher log likelihoods.  
16. Clarity: well-written, logically organized, effective figures, and standard algorithm presentation.  
17. Clarity weakness: would benefit from deeper discussion linking hierarchy to interpretability with concrete dataset examples.  
18. Originality: novel extension of Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data with an efficient inference algorithm.  
19. Originality weakness: contribution is incremental, building on prior hierarchical clustering work and not sharply distinguished from recent hierarchical community methods.  
20. Significance: addresses practical scalability for hierarchical community detection with major runtime savings.  
21. Significance weakness: does not convincingly show hierarchical necessity or superior utility over flat models.  
22. Pro-accept arguments: solid model, efficient algorithm, comprehensive experiments, significant speedup, and clear presentation.  
23. Con-accept arguments: limited evidence for hierarchical advantage, under-supported non-binary claim, incremental novelty, and limited interpretability comparison.  
24. Final recommendation: accept—paper makes a valuable, practical contribution despite some shortcomings.  
25. Suggested improvements: add interpretability discussion with NIPS examples, compare flat vs hierarchical models for predictive/interpretability gains, clarify hierarchy-data correspondence, and include a downstream-task experiment leveraging the hierarchy.
