This paper introduces a Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD) method for uncovering community structure in social networks. The core idea is to combine a greedy hierarchical clustering approach with a Bayesian framework, specifically utilizing a hierarchical mixture of stochastic blockmodels. The authors aim to address the computational limitations of existing Bayesian nonparametric methods for hierarchical community detection, such as the IRM, by proposing an efficient algorithm with a quadratic worst-case runtime independent of the number of communities. 

The paper is well-structured, starting with a clear introduction of the problem and related work. The mathematical formulation of the model is presented in a logical order, building from stochastic blockmodels to the hierarchical extension. The explanation of the inference procedure is detailed, and the inclusion of variations like BHCD sparse and the discussion of predictive performance add to the paper's completeness. The experimental evaluation demonstrates the speed and accuracy of BHCD compared to the IRM and other methods on both synthetic and real-world datasets (Sampson’s monastery network and NIPS co-authorship network).

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical derivations appear correct, and the algorithm builds upon established concepts in Bayesian modeling and hierarchical clustering. The complexity analysis is clearly stated and justified.
*   **Efficiency:** The reported speed improvements over IRM (two orders of magnitude for a single iteration) are significant and highlight a key contribution of the work. The quadratic time complexity is a notable advantage for large networks.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with a logical flow of ideas. The explanations of the model and algorithm are detailed and include helpful examples.
*   **Completeness:** The authors explore variations of the algorithm (sparse, binary) and demonstrate predictive performance, showcasing a comprehensive evaluation.
*   **Novelty:**  While building on existing work, the combination of a greedy Bayesian approach with a hierarchical stochastic blockmodel, along with the caching scheme for efficiency, represents a novel contribution. 

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Core Ideas:** The building blocks (stochastic blockmodels, Bayesian inference, greedy clustering) are well-established. The novelty lies more in the efficient combination and optimization for this specific problem than in fundamentally new theoretical concepts.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions setting hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) to specific values that worked well, but lacks a thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity or a systematic approach to hyperparameter tuning.
*   **Comparison Depth:** The comparison to other methods, while present, could be more in-depth. For example, a more detailed breakdown of the computational costs and memory requirements would be valuable. The justification for choosing certain IRM configurations (e.g., MCMC vs. greedy search) could be elaborated on.
*   **Predictive Evaluation:**  While predictive performance is shown, a more extensive comparison with state-of-the-art link prediction algorithms beyond those mentioned in the paper would strengthen the results.
*   **Minor Clarity Issues:**  Some equations (e.g., (13)) could benefit from further clarification regarding the meaning of the variables.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and efficient algorithm for a challenging problem in network analysis.
*   The demonstrated speed improvements over existing Bayesian nonparametric methods are significant.
*   The paper is well-written, clear, and complete, providing sufficient detail for reproducibility.
*   The experimental evaluation supports the claims made by the authors.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The core ideas are not groundbreaking, and the novelty lies primarily in the optimization and combination of existing techniques.
*   The lack of a thorough hyperparameter analysis is a limitation.
*   The comparison to other methods could be more extensive and detailed.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian hierarchical community detection. While the core ideas are not entirely novel, the authors successfully combine and optimize existing techniques to create an efficient and effective algorithm. The paper is well-written, clear, and thoroughly evaluated. I believe the strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the concerns regarding hyperparameter sensitivity and the depth of the comparison to other methods. Specifically, the authors should include a discussion of hyperparameter tuning and add a more comprehensive comparison of computational costs.
