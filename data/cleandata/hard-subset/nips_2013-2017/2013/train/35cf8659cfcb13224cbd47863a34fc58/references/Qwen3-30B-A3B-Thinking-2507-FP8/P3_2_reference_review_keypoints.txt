- Introduces BHCD, a greedy Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method using conjugate priors and a tree-based stochastic blockmodel.  
- Claims O(n² log n) runtime and shows orders-of-magnitude speedup over prior Bayesian methods on Sampson and NIPS networks.  
- Methodologically sound with clear mathematical formulation and efficient caching mechanism (Algorithm 1).  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of the greedy algorithm’s statistical quality or guarantees relative to optimal hierarchies.  
- Experimental gaps: no sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) despite reliance on defaults.  
- Missing comparisons to non-Bayesian hierarchical methods (e.g., HCS, HIER) for contextual performance evaluation.  
- Ambiguous performance claim about being “two orders of magnitude faster than a single iteration of the IRM” versus full IRM convergence.  
- Clarity is generally good, but justification for why the local greedy assumption is valid is underexplained.  
- Description of the “BHCD global” variant and “out of date scores on its heap” is confusing and needs clarification.  
- Novel contribution in combining hierarchical mixture framework with a novel greedy caching algorithm for efficiency.  
- Insufficient discussion and citation of recent related work (e.g., triangular vs edge representations, HDP-based approaches).  
- Paper doesn’t convincingly show that hierarchical outputs provide more insight or predictive advantage over flat community structure.  
- Claims about exploiting sparsity in “BHCD sparse” lack implementation details.  
- Specific recommendations: add theoretical justification for greedy approach, hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, non-Bayesian comparisons, deeper interpretability analysis on NIPS, and update related work.  
- Strengths: addresses a key computational bottleneck, clear presentation, practical implementation, and a sparse-network variant.  
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical guarantees, incomplete comparisons to recent work and non-Bayesian methods, unclear added value of hierarchy, and limited hyperparameter exploration.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical gaps, comparisons, and clearer demonstration of hierarchical value.
