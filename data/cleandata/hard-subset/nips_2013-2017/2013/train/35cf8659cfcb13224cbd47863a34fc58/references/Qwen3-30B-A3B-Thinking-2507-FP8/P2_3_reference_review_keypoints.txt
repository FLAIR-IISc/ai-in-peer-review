- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD) for inferring hierarchical community structure in networks.
- Extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data using a hierarchical stochastic blockmodel.
- Introduces an efficient greedy inference algorithm computing marginal likelihoods via dynamic programming.
- Achieves O(n² log n) time complexity, two orders of magnitude faster than methods like IRM.
- Demonstrates comparable predictive performance to state-of-the-art on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship datasets while being much faster.
- Technically sound with rigorous derivations and a clear marginal likelihood calculation (Equation 10).
- Theoretical analysis is supported by empirical results and includes a BHCD sparse variant to address merging of disconnected communities.
- Experimental design is robust, evaluating binary and sparse BHCD variants and comparing to relevant baselines (IRM, LFRM, ILA).
- Exceptionally well-written and organized with consistent notation, clear algorithm pseudocode, and effective visualizations (Figures 3–5).
- Minor clarity issue: tie-breaking mechanism in Section 4.1 could use more detail.
- Original contribution: solves scalability limitations of previous Bayesian hierarchical methods with no dependence on the number of communities.
- Properly situates contribution relative to prior work (e.g., Mondrian process, hierarchical IRM).
- Significant practical impact: makes hierarchical community discovery feasible for larger networks and yields interpretable hierarchies.
- Strengths: novel inference algorithm, strong empirical validation, excellent computational efficiency, practical utility, clear writing, comprehensive baselines.
- Weaknesses: limited theoretical guarantees for the greedy approach, restricted to undirected symmetric networks without self-edges, insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, could compare to more recent methods.
- Recommendation: accept — meets criteria for Quality, Clarity, Originality, and Significance; computational efficiency and practical utility are notable.
