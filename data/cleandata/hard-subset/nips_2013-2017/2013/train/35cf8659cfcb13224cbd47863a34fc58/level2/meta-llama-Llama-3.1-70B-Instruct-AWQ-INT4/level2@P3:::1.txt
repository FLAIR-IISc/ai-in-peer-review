**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The model, called Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), is a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels. The authors describe a family of greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that can learn a fully probabilistic, hierarchical community model in a single pass through the data. The algorithm scales quadratically in the number of vertices of the network, but independently of the number of nested communities.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on stochastic blockmodels, mixed membership stochastic blockmodels, and Bayesian approaches to community detection. The authors also mention related work on hierarchical community detection, including the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) and its extensions. However, the paper's approach is distinct in its use of a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels and a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm.

**Strengths**

1. **Efficient algorithm**: The paper's algorithm has a time complexity of O(n^2 log n), making it suitable for large dense networks.
2. **Good predictive performance**: The algorithm achieves comparable or better accuracy than the IRM, one of the fastest Bayesian nonparametric schemes, while being two orders of magnitude faster.
3. **Flexibility**: The model can be easily extended to incorporate non-conjugate likelihoods and hyperparameter inference.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited interpretability**: The non-binary hierarchies found by the algorithm can be difficult to interpret, although the binary-only variations of the algorithm tend to produce more interpretable results.
2. **Dependence on hyperparameters**: The algorithm's performance may depend on the choice of hyperparameters, which can be difficult to set in practice.
3. **Limited scalability**: While the algorithm is efficient for large dense networks, its scalability may be limited for very large sparse networks.

**Originality**

The paper's approach is novel in its use of a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels and a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm. The algorithm's ability to learn a fully probabilistic, hierarchical community model in a single pass through the data is also a significant contribution.

**Significance**

The paper's results have significant implications for the analysis of social networks and other relational data. The algorithm's ability to efficiently discover hierarchical community structure can be used to identify patterns and relationships in large networks, which can be useful in a variety of applications, such as link prediction, community detection, and network analysis.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

**Pro acceptance**:

* The paper presents a novel and efficient algorithm for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks.
* The algorithm achieves good predictive performance and is two orders of magnitude faster than the IRM.
* The paper provides a thorough evaluation of the algorithm on several datasets.

**Con acceptance**:

* The algorithm's performance may depend on the choice of hyperparameters, which can be difficult to set in practice.
* The non-binary hierarchies found by the algorithm can be difficult to interpret.
* The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the algorithm's limitations and potential biases.

**Recommendations for Revision**

1. **Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning**: The paper should provide more details on how the hyperparameters were set and how sensitive the algorithm is to their choice.
2. **Improve interpretability**: The paper should provide more guidance on how to interpret the non-binary hierarchies found by the algorithm.
3. **Discuss limitations and potential biases**: The paper should provide a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's limitations and potential biases, such as the dependence on the choice of hyperparameters.
