- Paper proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a novel Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community detection in networks, claiming quadratic scaling and better or comparable accuracy to IRM while being much faster.
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative single-pass algorithm that builds a hierarchical community tree and variants (sparse, binary-tree) for scalability and modeling flexibility.

Strengths:
- Novelty/significance: Extends Bayesian relational models to capture hierarchical community structure, improving over flat models like stochastic blockmodels and IRM.
- Efficiency: Algorithm has worst-case O(n^2 log n) complexity, uses caching and a sparse variant to enhance scalability for large/sparse networks.
- Empirical validation: Experiments on multiple datasets (Sampson, NIPS-234, full NIPS) show meaningful hierarchies and strong predictive performance, with large speedups over IRM.
- Clarity: Methodology, generative process, likelihood computations, and algorithmic variations are well explained.

Weaknesses:
- Limited theoretical analysis: Lacks deeper theory on properties, robustness to hyperparameters, or convergence guarantees.
- Evaluation metrics: Relies mainly on predictive metrics and qualitative analysis; could include metrics like modularity or hierarchy interpretability.
- Limited comparisons: Empirical comparison is mainly to IRM; missing direct comparisons to newer models like LFRM or ILA.
- Scalability concerns: Despite sparse variant, worst-case O(n^2 log n) complexity may limit applicability to extremely large sparse networks.

Suggestions:
- Discuss limitations more explicitly, including greedy search dependence and sensitivity to initialization.
- Investigate hyperparameter inference and non-conjugate likelihoods to potentially improve performance.
- Provide more detailed empirical comparisons with state-of-the-art hierarchical relational models on accuracy and efficiency.

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address theoretical gaps and broaden comparative evaluation.
