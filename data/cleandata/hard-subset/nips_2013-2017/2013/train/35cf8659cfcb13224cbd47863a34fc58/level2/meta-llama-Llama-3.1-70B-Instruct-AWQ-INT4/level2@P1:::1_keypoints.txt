- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community detection.
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that learn a fully probabilistic hierarchical community model in a single pass.
- Paper situates work well with a thorough review of related methods (SBM, MMSBM, Bayesian approaches) and highlights existing limitations.
- Technical description is clear and sound: prior on partitions, adjacency likelihood, recursive marginal likelihood derivation.
- Details an agglomerative algorithm with Bayes factor scoring and a caching scheme to improve computational efficiency.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and supported by helpful figures and tables.
- High originality: novel combination of tree-structured mixture and stochastic blockmodels for hierarchy discovery.
- High significance: demonstrated effectiveness on real networks (Sampson, NIPS coauthorship) and reported ~100× speedup over existing Bayesian nonparametric methods with comparable or better accuracy.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, strong empirical results and speed, clear exposition, and technical rigor.
- Arguments against acceptance: needs experiments on larger datasets to further demonstrate scalability.
- Evaluation could be strengthened with additional metrics (e.g., precision and recall) for more comprehensive assessment.
- Method relies on a specific partition prior that may not suit all datasets; alternative priors or more robust model-selection strategies could be explored.
