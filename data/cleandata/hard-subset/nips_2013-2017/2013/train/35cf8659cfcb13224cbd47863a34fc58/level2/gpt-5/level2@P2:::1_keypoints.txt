- Introduces BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric model and greedy agglomerative inference for hierarchical community discovery in networks.
- Model interprets a tree-structured mixture over exponentially many stochastic blockmodels, marginalizing over tree-consistent partitions.
- Key computational contribution: dynamic-programming recursion for marginal likelihood of a tree and a local Bayes-factor merge score enabling efficient greedy agglomeration.
- Algorithm achieves O(n+m) evaluation for fixed tree and O(n^2 log n) greedy search with cached sufficient statistics.
- Variants include a “sparse” constraint (forbid merges between disconnected subgraphs) and binary-only merges.
- Empirically produces interpretable hierarchies on Sampson’s network and scales to full NIPS 1–17 graph.
- Predictive performance: much faster than IRM with comparable AUC/log-probability on NIPS-234; competitive with LFRM and worse than ILA per reported numbers.
- Extends Bayesian rose-tree ideas to relational data via blockmodel marginals, contrasting with IRM, LFRM, ILA, and deterministic hierarchical clustering.
- The generative recursion is clear and ensures each edge is explained at the unique separation level.
- Local Bayes-factor merge score relies on local subgraph statistics—efficient but heuristically approximates global structure.
- The sparse constraint is a practical acceleration but changes the prior/model class and may fail on disassortative/bipartite structures.
- Complexity claims and caching strategy are plausible; sparse constraint offers practical speedups on real networks.
- Experiments lack breadth: only one small qualitative dataset and one predictive benchmark; no synthetic recovery or robustness tests.
- Missing comparisons to hierarchical network baselines (e.g., hierarchical IRM, multiscale blockmodels, hierarchical random graphs).
- Hyperparameters (α,β,δ,λ,γ) are fixed with no sensitivity analysis, empirical Bayes, or posterior inference; γ is not learned.
- Stability concerns: reliance on random tie-breaking and R=50 restarts with unclear runtime accounting and variability analysis.
- Clarity issues: undefined “BHCD global” term, missing DP derivation, unspecified memory requirements, and inconsistent variant definitions.
- Originality is moderate: novel adaptation of rose-tree ideas to blockmodels with efficient DP and generalized tree merges.
- Practical significance is good: scalable, probabilistic, interpretable hierarchical community discovery with competitive predictive performance.
- Recommendations: add synthetic hierarchical SBM recovery experiments, include at least one hierarchical baseline, and provide hyperparameter sensitivity or empirical Bayes.
- Suggest analyzing the sparse constraint’s impact, reporting runtime/memory scaling including restarts, and reducing restarts via informed initialization.
- Encourage clarifying ambiguous terms/figures, deriving the DP briefly, and releasing code for reproducibility.
- Pros for acceptance: principled model, elegant DP, fast inference, interpretable hierarchies, and useful practical variants.
- Cons for acceptance: limited experimental breadth, no hyperparameter learning, heuristic sparse constraint, and insufficient analysis of stability and restarts.
- Overall verdict: solid, scalable contribution with incremental theory; borderline accept contingent on stronger empirical validation and clearer discussion of limitations.
