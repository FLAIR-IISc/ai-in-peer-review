- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm for hierarchical community discovery without pre-specifying community count.  
- Key contributions: Bayesian nonparametric framing, a dynamic-programming inference algorithm, and empirical speedups over IRM with comparable or better accuracy.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and places BHCD in context relative to IRM, greedy clustering, and the Mondrian process.  
- Algorithm (Algorithm 1) is described in reproducible detail; experiments compare to relevant baselines.  
- Strength: mathematically sound model and inference, with careful marginal likelihood derivations.  
- Strength: empirical demonstration of ~100x speed improvement over IRM while maintaining competitive accuracy on real networks.  
- Strength: clear explanation of how BHCD avoids computational bottlenecks of prior Bayesian methods.  
- Strength: practical implementation details, including a sparse variant that improves performance on sparse graphs.  
- Weakness: claiming “Bayesian nonparametric” is inconsistent with fixing hyperparameters rather than placing priors—needs clarification.  
- Weakness: IRM comparison is incomplete—reporting speed relative to a single IRM iteration lacks context about IRM convergence and fairness of comparison.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of model limitations, e.g., stochastic blockmodels assume non-overlapping communities which restrict applicability.  
- Weakness: NIPS-234 experiment lacks detail on how many IRM iterations were needed to match BHCD performance; convergence behavior should be reported.  
- Weakness: claim that runtime is quadratic in vertices and independent of number of nested communities lacks sufficient theoretical justification.  
- Significance: moderate–high—practical and substantial speedups for hierarchical community detection, but unclear if discovered hierarchies give fundamentally new insights versus other methods.  
- Conclusion: main novelty is the efficient inference algorithm rather than the model; paper should clarify the nonparametric claim, improve IRM comparisons, and more thoroughly discuss limitations.
