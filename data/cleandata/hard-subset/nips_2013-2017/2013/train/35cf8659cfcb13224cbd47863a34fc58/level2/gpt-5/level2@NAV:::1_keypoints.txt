- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric model combining rose-tree hierarchical clustering with stochastic blockmodels and exact marginalization via conjugacy.
- Uses a greedy agglomerative algorithm with local Bayes factor merges and dynamic programming to compute exact marginal likelihoods per node.
- Claims two orders of magnitude speedup over IRM with comparable or better link prediction on NIPS co-authorship subset and scales to full NIPS graph.
- Builds on Bayesian hierarchical clustering and rose trees, adapted to relational blockmodels; contrasts with IRM, Mondrian, LFRM, ILA.
- Modeling assumptions are clear and inference is well specified, with O(1) cached updates in the inner loop.
- Experimental claims on speed and accuracy are supported by real-data results but lack statistical significance tests and broader baseline comparisons.
- Hyperparameters are fixed; no sensitivity analysis or hyperparameter inference is provided.
- Impact of multiple random restarts and robustness of the greedy search are not quantified.
- Writing is clear and well organized; key equations and merge scoring are explained crisply.
- Minor inconsistency in complexity statements: text claims worst-case quadratic but algorithmic analysis gives O(n^2 log n).
- Novelty lies in combining rose-tree Bayesian hierarchical clustering with blockmodels and efficient greedy selection for relational data.
- Method is practically useful: scales to thousands of nodes, yields interpretable hierarchies, and offers competitive prediction with large speedups.
- Pros: principled model with exact marginalization, scalable greedy algorithm, interpretable hierarchies and competitive link prediction.
- Cons: limited baselines and no statistical testing, no hyperparameter sensitivity or inference, potential instability of greedy search, complexity inconsistency, limited discussion of directed/weighted graphs.
- Suggestions: compare to hierarchical IRM and multiscale blockmodels with confidence intervals; provide hyperparameter sensitivity and variance across restarts.
- Suggestions: clarify complexity claims and BHCD-sparse complexity in terms of m; consider hyperparameter learning and non-conjugate likelihoods; extend to directed/weighted ties.
- Suggestion to release code for reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept — useful and efficient contribution but evaluation and analysis gaps remain.
