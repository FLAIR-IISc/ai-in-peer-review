- Proposal: BHCD, a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method modeling hierarchies as a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Inference innovation: greedy agglomerative algorithm with dynamic programming and sufficient-statistic caching for local computations.  
- Complexity claim: O(n² log n) runtime—about two orders of magnitude faster than IRM while maintaining comparable accuracy.  
- Theoretical quality: sound derivations for marginal likelihood and predictive distribution; well-specified algorithm and thorough complexity analysis.  
- Empirical results: recovers meaningful hierarchies on Sampson’s network and matches IRM predictive accuracy on NIPS co-authorship with drastically reduced runtime (10s vs 1000s).  
- Ablation study: sparse variant shown valuable for real-world sparse networks.  
- Contribution: provides a scalable Bayesian alternative to prior hierarchical methods, avoiding expensive MCMC and retaining probabilistic interpretation.  
- Positioning: clearly differentiated from non-Bayesian hierarchical methods and IRM-based hierarchical extensions, with appropriate citations.  
- Writing and reproducibility: well-written, clear figures (e.g., Figure 3), and sufficient code/experimental details for reproducibility.  
- Significance: fills practical gap enabling Bayesian hierarchical discovery on much larger networks, demonstrated on full NIPS co-authorship data.  
- Weakness: lacks direct empirical comparison to non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering baselines.  
- Weakness: predictive distribution (equation 14) and responsibility term r_S need more intuitive explanation.  
- Weakness: no sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ), important for practical use.  
- Weakness: should discuss trade-offs of greedy inference—potential to miss globally optimal hierarchies.  
- Recommendations: add comparisons to non-Bayesian methods, include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis in supplement, and clarify equation 14 with a concrete example.  
- Overall judgment: technically strong, novel, and impactful; minor addressable weaknesses—recommend acceptance.
