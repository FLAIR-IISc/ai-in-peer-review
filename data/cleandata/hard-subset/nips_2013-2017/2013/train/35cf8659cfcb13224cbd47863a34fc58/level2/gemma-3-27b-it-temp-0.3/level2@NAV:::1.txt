## Review of "Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery"

This paper presents a novel and efficient Bayesian nonparametric model, Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), for uncovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The core idea is to represent the hierarchy as a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, coupled with a greedy agglomerative algorithm for inference. The authors address a key limitation of existing Bayesian approaches – computational cost – by proposing an algorithm that scales favorably with network size, specifically independent of the number of nested communities.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the shortcomings of existing flat community detection methods and computationally expensive hierarchical Bayesian models like the IRM. The proposed model builds logically upon prior work in Bayesian hierarchical clustering and extends it effectively to relational data. The authors convincingly demonstrate the efficiency of BHCD, reporting speedups of two orders of magnitude compared to a single iteration of the IRM while maintaining comparable accuracy.  Experiments on Sampson’s monastery network provide qualitative validation, while quantitative results on the NIPS co-authorship dataset demonstrate competitive performance with other state-of-the-art methods. The inclusion of a visualization of the discovered hierarchy in the full NIPS dataset adds further insight.

A key strength is the focus on computational efficiency. The authors carefully analyze the complexity of their algorithm and identify opportunities for optimization, such as caching sufficient statistics. The introduction of BHCD sparse, tailored for sparse networks, further highlights this focus. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the choice of hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ, γ) and their impact on the results.  While values are provided, a sensitivity analysis or justification for these choices would strengthen the work. 

Furthermore, the comparison to other methods is somewhat limited. While IRM is used as a benchmark, a more comprehensive comparison to other hierarchical community detection algorithms (e.g., those based on modularity optimization) would be valuable.  Finally, the discussion of future work is promising, but could be expanded to include potential applications of the discovered hierarchical structures beyond link prediction.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and efficient approach to Bayesian hierarchical community discovery.
*   Significant speedup compared to existing Bayesian methods.
*   Competitive performance on benchmark datasets.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
*   Relatively narrow comparison to other hierarchical community detection methods.
*   Could benefit from a more detailed discussion of potential applications.

**Overall:** This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of community detection. The proposed method is both theoretically sound and practically efficient. Despite some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the work outweigh its limitations, and I recommend acceptance.  Encourage the authors to address the points raised above in a revised version.
