- Proposes BHCD: an efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community discovery in social networks.
- BHCD is a tree-structured mixture of potentially exponentially many stochastic blockmodels.
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that learn a fully probabilistic hierarchical model in a single pass.
- Algorithmic complexity scales quadratically with number of vertices and is independent of number of nested communities, making it suitable for large dense networks.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations, supportive figures, and helpful equations.
- Provides a thorough related-work review that situates the contribution in existing research.
- Balances theoretical derivation with practical considerations, including computational complexity and scalability discussion.
- Experiments evaluate algorithm performance across several datasets.
- Results show BHCD discovers reasonable hierarchies and makes predictions about two orders of magnitude faster than a leading Bayesian nonparametric method.
- Useful empirical comparisons to other methods such as the IRM.
- Key weakness: assumes a specific structure for the community hierarchy that may not hold in real-world networks.
- Authors acknowledge this limitation and suggest future work to relax the hierarchical assumption.
- Overall assessment: high quality and significant contribution; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
- Reviewer scores: originality 8/10, significance 9/10, clarity 9/10, quality 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and efficient approach, favorable scaling properties, strong experimental evaluation.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive hierarchical assumption and idea not entirely novel (builds on existing tree-structured SBM concepts).
- Requested revisions: expand discussion of limitations and assumptions, explore more flexible hierarchy models, and provide more detail on computational complexity and scalability.
