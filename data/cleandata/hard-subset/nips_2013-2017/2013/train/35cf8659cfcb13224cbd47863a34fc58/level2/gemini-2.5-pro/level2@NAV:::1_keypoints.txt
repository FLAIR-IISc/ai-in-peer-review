- Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model that infers hierarchical community structure via a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Learns structure with a greedy agglomerative algorithm, extending Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data.  
- Positions BHCD as a practical alternative to slow MCMC methods (e.g., IRM) and more principled than non-probabilistic greedy methods.  
- Strength: combines a principled probabilistic framework with a highly efficient inference algorithm.  
- Strength: strong experimental support for efficiency and accuracy, including a two-order-of-magnitude speedup vs IRM on NIPS with comparable predictive performance.  
- Strength: qualitative results (Sampson’s Monastery, full NIPS) show interpretable, non-binary hierarchical structures.  
- Strength: clearly written, well-motivated model with an elegant recursive marginal likelihood formulation.  
- Weakness: greedy inference is susceptible to local optima; multiple random restarts are used but more analysis of solution quality vs global methods is needed.  
- Weakness: BHCD sparse is empirically faster on sparse networks but worst-case inference complexity remains O(n² log n), indicating a scalability bottleneck.  
- Weakness: given that likelihood computation is O(n+m), the paper should discuss challenges and potential paths toward near-linear time inference for large sparse graphs.  
- Conclusion: high-quality, novel, and significant contribution; results are strong and the paper is a strong candidate for acceptance and likely to be influential.
