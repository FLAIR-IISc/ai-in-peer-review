- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.
- Introduces a family of greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that learn a probabilistic hierarchical community model in a single pass.
- Algorithmic complexity is O(n^2 log n), scaling quadratically with vertices but independently of number of nested communities.
- Builds on stochastic blockmodels, mixed membership models, and IRM-like Bayesian approaches, but differs via a tree-structured mixture and greedy agglomeration.
- Strength: efficient algorithm suitable for large dense networks.
- Strength: competitive or better predictive performance than IRM while being ~100× faster.
- Strength: flexible model that can extend to non-conjugate likelihoods and hyperparameter inference.
- Weakness: non-binary hierarchies can be difficult to interpret; binary variants are more interpretable.
- Weakness: performance may be sensitive to hyperparameter choices, which can be hard to set.
- Weakness: scalability may be limited for very large sparse networks despite efficiency on dense graphs.
- Originality: novel combination of tree-structured mixture of SBMs and greedy agglomerative model selection enabling single-pass probabilistic learning.
- Significance: useful for analyzing social and relational networks, with applications like link prediction and hierarchical community detection.
- Pro-accept: novel, efficient, fast, and thoroughly evaluated on multiple datasets.
- Con-accept: hyperparameter dependence, interpretability issues, and insufficient analysis of limitations and biases.
- Revision recommendations: provide more details on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity.
- Revision recommendations: offer guidance to improve interpretability of non-binary hierarchies and discuss limitations and potential biases.
