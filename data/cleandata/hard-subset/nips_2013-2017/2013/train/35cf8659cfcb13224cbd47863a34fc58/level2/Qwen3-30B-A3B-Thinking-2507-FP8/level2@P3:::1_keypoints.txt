- Proposes BHCD, a greedy agglomerative Bayesian method for hierarchical community discovery using a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Claims O(n² log n) time via dynamic programming and caching, independent of community count, improving over IRM’s O(n²K²).  
- Validated on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship data, showing qualitative hierarchy alignment and competitive predictive accuracy.  
- Strength: principled Bayesian framework that incorporates priors (e.g., Beta) tuned for within-community density.  
- Strength: dynamic programming for marginal likelihood (Eq. 10) is well-justified and enables claimed scalability.  
- Strength: empirical speed advantage—sparse BHCD reaches IRM-like accuracy on NIPS-234 in ~10s vs IRM’s ~1000s.  
- Strength: predictive model derivation (Eq. 14) is elegant and the method clearly differentiated from prior iterative or depth-limited IRM extensions.  
- Weakness: claim of complexity “independent of number of nested communities” is misleading—hierarchy can have O(n) communities and complexity scales with vertices, not communities.  
- Weakness: experimental omission of comparisons to non-Bayesian hierarchical methods (e.g., [3,4]) leaves BHCD’s speed/accuracy tradeoff undercontextualized, especially on sparse graphs.  
- Weakness: tie-breaking in Algorithm 1 is insufficiently justified; random ties and 50 restarts may affect consistency and hierarchy quality.  
- Weakness: predictive accuracy is comparable to IRM but not superior to non-Bayesian methods (LFRM/ILA), and the paper doesn’t discuss when BHCD’s speed or probabilistic output justifies slightly lower accuracy.  
- Revision request: clarify the precise meaning of “community-count independence” (complexity scales with vertices, not number of communities).  
- Revision request: add comparative experiments versus non-Bayesian hierarchical methods, focusing on sparse-network performance of sparse BHCD.  
- Revision request: justify or replace random tie-breaking with a deterministic rule (e.g., merge smallest clusters) and report stability metrics across restarts.  
- Revision request: discuss the speed vs accuracy tradeoff explicitly and summarize key supplementary results (e.g., tie-breaking stability) for reproducibility.
