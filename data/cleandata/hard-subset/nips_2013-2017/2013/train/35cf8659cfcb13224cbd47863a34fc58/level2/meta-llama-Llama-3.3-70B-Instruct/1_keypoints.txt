- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm that learns the hierarchy in a single pass.  
- Algorithm runs in O(n^2 log n) time (n = number of vertices).  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanation of model and algorithm.  
- Experiments show the method discovers reasonable hierarchies and makes predictions ~100x faster than a fast existing Bayesian nonparametric scheme while achieving comparable performance.  
- Strength: novel, efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community structure.  
- Strength: single-pass greedy algorithm enabling fast inference.  
- Strength: empirical results demonstrating effectiveness and speed.  
- Weakness: assumes hierarchical community structure, which may not hold for all real-world networks.  
- Weakness: O(n^2 log n) complexity may not scale to very large networks.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art community detection and link prediction methods.  
- Pro-accept: novel approach, strong empirical evidence, clear presentation.  
- Con-accept: restrictive assumptions, scalability concerns, insufficient benchmarking.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to address limitations and provide more comparisons.
