- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a Bayesian nonparametric tree-based model and greedy agglomerative algorithm for hierarchical community detection.  
- Defines a tree over vertices to induce a prior over tree-consistent SBM partitions and exactly marginalizes edge-probability parameters via a dynamic programming recursion.  
- Inference uses cached sufficient statistics and local Bayes-factor scoring for three merge types (join and two absorptions), yielding O(n^2 log n) worst-case runtime and O(n + m) likelihood cost.  
- Variants include a sparse version forbidding merges of disconnected subtrees and a binary-only version.  
- Experiments: qualitative analysis on Sampson’s monastery, link prediction on NIPS-234 (compared to IRM MCMC/greedy), and a large-scale run on full NIPS co-authorship showing interpretable hierarchies.  
- Empirical result: BHCD attains comparable/better predictive performance than IRM with much lower runtime; results similar to LFRM and below ILA (via published numbers).  
- Extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering and rose trees to relational data by restricting SBM partitions with a tree and integrating out parameters.  
- Contrasts with IRM and hierarchical IRM methods (MCMC-heavy), nonparametric latent feature models (LFRM, ILA), and HRG, while offering distinct tree-conditioned SBM marginalization.  
- Strength: elegant, efficient exact marginalization for p(D|T) with O(1) inner-loop updates via caching.  
- Strength: practical scalability independent of number of communities and well-motivated sparse variant for real networks.  
- Strength: clear modeling choices using conjugate on-/off-diagonal Beta–Bernoulli models embodying dense-within, sparse-between community assumption.  
- Strength: strong empirical utility—fast, accurate link prediction on NIPS-234 and sensible qualitative structures on other datasets.  
- Strength: simple, implementable greedy algorithm based on local Bayes factors and three merge types.  
- Weakness: limited baselines—only IRM run directly; HRG, degree-corrected SBMs, and direct runs of LFRM/ILA are missing.  
- Weakness: modeling lacks degree correction and cannot capture heavy-tailed degree distributions; degree-corrected SBM or node-activity correction recommended.  
- Weakness: fixed hyperparameters (α,β,δ,λ,γ) across datasets without sensitivity analysis or empirical Bayes/MCMC updates.  
- Weakness: greedy inference may be sensitive to local optima, tie-breaking, and early merges; restarts and post-processing (e.g., subtree reattachments) and diagnostic variance reporting needed.  
- Weakness: evaluation depth limited—Sampson analysis is qualitative; need quantitative clustering metrics (NMI/ARI) and consistent runtime/hardware comparisons and calibration for NIPS-234.  
- Weakness: minor clarity issues—ambiguous complexity phrasing (e.g., “linear in depth” vs “Θ(n)”), memory footprint of caches, and tie-breaking effects; some typographical inconsistencies.  
- Quality: model and inference are technically sound with well-reasoned dynamic programming and Bayes-factor scoring.  
- Clarity: exposition is clear and structured, with helpful descriptions of caching and merge types, aside from noted minor clarifications.  
- Originality: novel adaptation of rose-tree priors and exact partition marginalization to relational SBMs with an efficient local tree-building algorithm.  
- Significance: likely useful as a fast, scalable exploratory tool and link-prediction baseline; impact would increase with degree correction and richer likelihoods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, efficient framework with exact marginalization, strong scalability, clear algorithmic contributions, and useful variants.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing stronger baselines and quantitative clustering metrics, limited model expressiveness (no degree correction), and potential sensitivity to greedy search/local optima.  
- Essential revisions suggested: add at least one stronger baseline (e.g., HRG or degree-corrected SBM), report clustering metrics on Sampson, clarify runtime/complexity statements, and report hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Nice-to-have revisions: incorporate degree correction, add lightweight local refinement after agglomeration, and release code/scripts for reproducibility.
