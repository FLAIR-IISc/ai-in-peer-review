- Proposes BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric method for discovering hierarchical community structure in networks.
- Claims: efficient greedy agglomerative model selection, quadratic scaling in network size (independent of community count), and ~100x faster than IRM with comparable accuracy.
- Extends Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data, filling a gap where prior Bayesian methods scale poorly or lack full probabilistic accounts.
- Theoretical foundation builds on stochastic blockmodels and tree-structured partitions; marginal likelihood computed via dynamic programming in O(n + m).
- Provides a clear, reproducible algorithmic description (Algorithm 1).
- Experimental results on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship show effective structure discovery and much faster runtimes than IRM.
- Sparse variant is well-motivated for real networks and helps avoid merging disconnected components.
- Authors acknowledge limitations: potential to merge disconnected components (addressed by sparse variant) and binary vs. non-binary tree tradeoffs.
- Strengths: elegant theoretical development, clear comparisons to relevant baselines, and substantial practical runtime improvements.
- Areas for improvement: better motivation for tree vs. flat models, broader predictive comparisons beyond IRM, and stronger theoretical justification for chosen hyperparameters (α=δ=1.0, β=λ=0.2, γ=0.4).
- Overall: technically sound, practically useful, addresses a meaningful problem for large-scale applications, recommended for acceptance.
