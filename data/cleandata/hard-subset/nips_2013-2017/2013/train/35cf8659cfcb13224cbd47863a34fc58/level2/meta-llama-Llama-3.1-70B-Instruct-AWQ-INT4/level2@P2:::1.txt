This paper proposes an efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks, which is a significant contribution to the field of network analysis. The model, called Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), is a tree-structured mixture of potentially exponentially many stochastic blockmodels. The authors describe a family of greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that can learn a fully probabilistic, hierarchical community model in a single pass through the data. The algorithm scales quadratically in the number of vertices of the network, but independent of the number of nested communities, making it suitable for large dense networks.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the model and the algorithm. The use of figures and equations helps to illustrate the concepts and make the paper more readable. The authors also provide a thorough review of the related work in the field, which helps to situate their contribution in the context of existing research.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to balance theoretical rigor with practical considerations. The authors provide a detailed derivation of the model and the algorithm, but they also discuss the computational complexity and the scalability of the approach. The experiments section is also well-done, as it provides a clear evaluation of the performance of the algorithm on several datasets.

The results of the experiments are promising, as they show that BHCD can discover reasonable hierarchies and make predictions about two orders of magnitude faster than one of the fastest existing Bayesian nonparametric schemes. The comparison with other methods, such as IRM, is also useful, as it provides a baseline for evaluating the performance of BHCD.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it assumes a specific structure for the community hierarchy, which may not always be the case in real-world networks. The authors acknowledge this limitation and suggest that future work could involve relaxing this assumption.

Overall, I would rate the quality of the paper as excellent. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of network analysis, and their paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.
