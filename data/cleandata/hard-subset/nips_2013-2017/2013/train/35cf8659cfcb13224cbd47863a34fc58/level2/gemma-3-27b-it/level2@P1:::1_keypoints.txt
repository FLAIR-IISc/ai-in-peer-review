- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method using a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a greedy agglomerative learning algorithm.  
- Claims runtime is quadratic in vertices and independent of number of communities, yielding substantial speedups over IRM.  
- Motivated by the high computational cost of existing Bayesian nonparametric hierarchical community methods.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear positioning in literature and connections to prior work (e.g., [13,14]).  
- Exposition and intuitive explanation of the greedy agglomerative process are strong.  
- Provides theoretical justification for the algorithm’s runtime complexity.  
- Experimental results recover known structure on Sampson’s monastery network.  
- Demonstrates substantial speed advantage over IRM on NIPS co-authorship data and scales to the full NIPS dataset with useful visualizations.  
- Empirical speed comparisons combined with theoretical analysis are commendable.  
- Comparisons are limited — lacks evaluation against other state-of-the-art hierarchical community detection methods beyond IRM.  
- Hyperparameter choices (α, δ, β, λ, γ) are not well-justified and lack sensitivity analysis.  
- Explanation of the predictive “responsibility” parameter rS is unclear and needs expansion.  
- Sparse-network performance and scaling with sparsity are mentioned but not analyzed in depth.  
- Impact of tie-breaking strategies and restarts is insufficiently explored.  
- Code is not released, hindering reproducibility and further evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: solid, efficient contribution with convincing results; recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address comparisons, hyperparameter sensitivity, clarity, and code release.
