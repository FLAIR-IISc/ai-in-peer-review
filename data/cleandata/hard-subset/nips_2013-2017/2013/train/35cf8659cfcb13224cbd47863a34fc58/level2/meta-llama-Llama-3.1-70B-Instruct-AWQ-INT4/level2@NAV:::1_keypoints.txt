- Proposes BHCD, a novel Bayesian nonparametric model: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Model enables efficient inference of hierarchical community structure.  
- Evaluated on Sampson’s monastery, NIPS subset, and full NIPS co-authorship networks.  
- BHCD discovers reasonable hierarchies and makes predictions ~100× faster than a top existing Bayesian nonparametric method while achieving comparable performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of model and inference.  
- Experiments are thorough and results are clearly presented.  
- Technically sound: claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Notation and figures are clear and helpful.  
- Original contribution: tree-structured mixture of SBMs is a novel approach.  
- Significant problem addressed; approach applicable beyond social networks (e.g., biological networks).  
- Strengths: novelty, efficiency/scalability, and thorough experiments.  
- Weaknesses: assumes undirected, unweighted networks and lacks detailed computational complexity analysis for very large networks.  
- Suggestions: include a detailed complexity analysis and explore extensions to directed/weighted networks.  
- Rating/recommendation: 9/10 — accept with minor revisions.
