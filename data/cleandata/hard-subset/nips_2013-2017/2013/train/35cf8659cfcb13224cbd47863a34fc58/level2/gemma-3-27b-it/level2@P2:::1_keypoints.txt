- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method using a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels learned via a greedy agglomerative algorithm.  
- Core claim: efficient, fully probabilistic hierarchical community modeling with substantial speedup over methods like the IRM.  
- Algorithm scales well and is claimed to be independent of the number of nested communities.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and situates the work within relevant literature (SBMs, IRM, greedy clustering, Mondrian process).  
- Mathematical formulation and greedy algorithm are presented clearly and concisely, with step-by-step description.  
- Experiments show competitive accuracy and two orders of magnitude faster performance than IRM, and demonstrate meaningful hierarchies on real datasets.  
- Major strength: emphasis on computational efficiency with claimed O(n^2 log n) complexity and a faster sparse variant, supported by a clever caching scheme.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is narrow—focused on link prediction; lacks other community recovery metrics (e.g., NMI, adjusted Rand index) and comparisons to more methods.  
- Weakness: hyperparameters (α, β, δ, λ) are set empirically without sufficient sensitivity analysis or justification; choice of γ needs clearer explanation.  
- Weakness: presentation of the full NIPS dataset results is ad hoc (“cut the tree”); needs a principled method for selecting/displaying hierarchy levels or better visualizations.  
- Weakness: some algorithmic steps (Bayes factor SCORE, sufficient-statistics updates) assume reader familiarity; more intuitive explanations would help.  
- Minor issues: occasional inconsistent notation (e.g., S vs. T for trees) that affects readability.  
- Recommendation: overall strong contribution emphasizing efficiency; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing experimental breadth and hyperparameter sensitivity.
