- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm.  
- Claims runtime independent of number of nested communities and shows ~100x speedup over the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Well-motivated, clear extension of stochastic blockmodels and Bayesian clustering to hierarchical community detection.  
- Greedy algorithm is a major contribution that avoids iterative, expensive procedures used by methods like IRM.  
- Theoretical analysis indicates O(n^2 log n) computational complexity.  
- Empirical validation on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship datasets supports efficiency and includes both qualitative and quantitative evaluations.  
- Prior choices (α, β, δ, λ, γ) are reported but lack deeper justification and sensitivity analysis.  
- Experiments are somewhat limited; more diverse network types and comparisons to non-Bayesian hierarchical clustering methods are needed.  
- Descriptions of BHCD variants (sparse, binary) are brief and require more detailed trade-off analysis.  
- Discussion of scalability to extremely large sparse networks is insufficient despite efficiency claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient algorithm; significant speedup; clear presentation; empirical evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited prior sensitivity discussion; narrower experimental scope; insufficient analysis of method variants.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, accept with minor revisions to address prior sensitivity, broader experiments, variant analyses, and scalability concerns.
