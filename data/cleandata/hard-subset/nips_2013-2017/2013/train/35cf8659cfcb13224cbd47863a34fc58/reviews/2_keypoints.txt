- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchies instead of flat clusters.  
- Introduces an agglomerative-clustering-based learning algorithm to select a tree of valid partitions.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social connectivity corpora shows the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Addresses a relevant problem: speeding Bayesian nonparametric methods and providing hierarchical network discovery useful to social scientists.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to tree-structured ones enables efficient marginal likelihood computation versus IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Unclear how the greedy tree-learning procedure relates to the Bayesian generative model—uncertain whether it performs valid posterior inference or finds a MAP/ML estimate.  
- Requests clearer explanation of Section 4 to resolve the above conceptual confusion.  
- Results are strong: comparable or better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, with orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements.  
- Writing quality needs improvement: spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, unclear dataset descriptions, some unnecessary/obscure math, and confusing figures.  
- If clarity and presentation issues are fixed, the paper has sufficient novelty and strong results to merit publication.  
- Reviewer’s summary: the model is a Bayesian-style agglomerative clustering inspired by the IRM; core algorithm and experiments are insightful, but writing must be improved.
