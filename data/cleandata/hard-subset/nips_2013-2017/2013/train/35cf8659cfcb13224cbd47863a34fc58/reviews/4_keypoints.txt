- Proposes applying Bayesian rose trees with a block-model likelihood to hierarchically cluster networks, reusing prior tree prior and agglomerative structure.
- Main technical novelty is adapting the likelihood and updating sufficient statistics efficiently for network data.
- Likelihood computation claimed to be O(nodes + edges), enabling scalable agglomerative clustering.
- Presentation is generally clear, but Section 3 (especially equation (9) and subsequent derivations) is dense and hard to follow.
- Explanations of the dynamic programming and recursive sufficient-statistic updates are insufficient and need more detail.
- Experimental results show benefits over a flat IRM baseline and qualitative success on Sampson, and averaging over hypotheses helps prediction.
- Major weakness: experiments lack comparisons to existing hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel [Ho et al. 2012], Mondrian process [Roy et al. 2009]).
- Questionable use of "nonparametric" terminology given prior framing of rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric.
- Scalability claims are discussed but tested networks are not especially large, so empirical scalability is not fully demonstrated.
- Overall technically sound and integrates prior work well, but originality is limited and explanation and comparative evaluation need strengthening.
