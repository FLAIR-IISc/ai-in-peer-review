- Well-written paper with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations; minor style issue: avoid beginning sentences with citations in NIPS style.
- Theory is succinct in parts (consistent use of f() and g()).
- Extends stochastic block models (SBM) and the single-table IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees.
- Replaces the CRP formulation of IRM with the simpler formulation in equation (7).
- Posterior factorization over subtrees follows Koivisto & Sood (JMLR 2004), enabling greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, handling of sufficient statistics, and link prediction.
- Main algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative method (summarized in Table 1).
- Evaluation uses AUC but the method of computing it is unclear (presumably by deleting links) and needs explicit explanation; NMI is a more common metric for SBM comparisons.
- Experiments are small-scale (full NIPS dataset only); should include larger datasets used in recent SBM evaluations.
- Experimental work is illustrative rather than comparative and thus inadequate; need comparisons to semi-parametric methods beyond Bayesian nonparametrics.
