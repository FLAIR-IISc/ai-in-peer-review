- Paper proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model to infer hierarchies instead of flat clusterings from network data.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm to select a tree encoding valid partitions.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social connectivity corpora shows the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Problem is timely and relevant: Bayesian nonparametric methods are conceptually appealing but often slow, and social scientists increasingly use such models.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones enables efficient marginal likelihood computation versus the exponential search in IRM.  
- Unclear how the greedy tree-learning procedure connects to the Bayesian generative model—does not appear to perform posterior inference over trees or yield a clear MAP/ML estimate.  
- Recommendation to clarify Section 4 and the relationship between the algorithm and the generative/posterior model.  
- Empirical gains in predictive metrics are modest, but runtime improvements are orders of magnitude over IRM.  
- Writing and clarity need improvement: spelling/grammar errors and vague prose (e.g., lines 47–50).  
- Datasets (Monastery networks and NIPS authorship) are not well described and may confuse unfamiliar readers.  
- Some mathematical exposition is opaque or unnecessary (e.g., Eq. 5), and Figure 3 is confusing for readers unfamiliar with the Monastery dataset.  
- Suggest including a more familiar dataset to help readers interpret results.  
- Overall: novel model and insightful core algorithm with strong experiments, but acceptance should depend on addressing clarity and explanation issues.
