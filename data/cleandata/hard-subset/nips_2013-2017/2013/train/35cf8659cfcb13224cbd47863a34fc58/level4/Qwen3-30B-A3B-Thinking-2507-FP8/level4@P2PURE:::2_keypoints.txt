- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchical clustering instead of flat partitions.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering-based learning algorithm to select a tree representing valid partitions.  
- Restricting partitions to tree structures enables efficient marginal likelihood computation compared to the IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Empirical results on two social datasets show comparable or slightly better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy than IRM.  
- The method runs orders of magnitude faster than IRM, a major practical advantage.  
- The paper addresses a timely problem relevant to both Bayesian nonparametrics and social network analysis.  
- Unclear how the greedy tree-learning procedure aligns with the Bayesian generative model—cannot be true posterior inference or yield MAP/ML without clarification.  
- Performance gains are modest in metrics, but runtime improvements are substantial and compelling.  
- Writing quality needs substantial improvement: spelling and grammatical errors distract.  
- Several passages are vague (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”) and need clearer context (are these MCMC iterations?).  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficient for readers unfamiliar with them.  
- Some mathematical exposition (e.g., Equation 5) is unnecessarily obtuse and hard to follow.  
- Figure 3 is confusing without knowledge of the monastery dataset; a more familiar dataset or clearer explanation would help.  
- Section 4, which presents the core contribution, is hard to parse and requires clearer exposition.  
- Despite writing issues, the approach is novel, empirically validated, and merits publication after revisions.
