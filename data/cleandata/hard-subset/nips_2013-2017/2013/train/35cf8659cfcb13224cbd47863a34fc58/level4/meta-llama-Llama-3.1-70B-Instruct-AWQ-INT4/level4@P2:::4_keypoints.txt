- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian (claimed nonparametric) tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithms that learn a fully probabilistic hierarchical community model in a single pass.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound; Section 3 is particularly clear though some notation/explanations could be simplified.  
- Provides a good overview of prior work on community discovery and Bayesian approaches.  
- Extends Bayesian greedy hierarchical clustering to relational data by using a tree prior that restricts partitions, allowing exact marginal likelihood evaluation.  
- Describes an efficient inference scheme similar in structure to Bayesian rose trees, requiring cached sufficient statistics.  
- Algorithmic complexity reported as O(n^2 log n) in the number of vertices.  
- Empirical demonstration on datasets (Sampson’s monastery, NIPS co-authorship subset) and comparison to IRM; BHCD produces reasonable predictions faster than IRM.  
- Strengths: novel model, clear/concise presentation, and effective experimental results.  
- Weaknesses: lacks comparisons to other hierarchical network models; insufficient detail on the dynamic programming for marginal likelihood; questionably labelled “nonparametric.”  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clarity of exposition, and demonstrated effectiveness.  
- Arguments for rejection: missing comparative baselines, terminology concerns, and insufficient algorithmic detail.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to include more comparisons, clarify “nonparametric” terminology, and expand details on the dynamic programming approach.
