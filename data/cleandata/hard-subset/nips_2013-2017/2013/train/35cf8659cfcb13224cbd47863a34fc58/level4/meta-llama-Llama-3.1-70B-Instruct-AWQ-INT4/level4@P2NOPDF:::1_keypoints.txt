- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) inspired by Bayesian rose trees.  
- Uses a hierarchy to define a mixture over consistent node partitions and applies a greedy agglomerative learning procedure.  
- Provides qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and the full NIPS dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Inference is computationally more efficient than the IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.  
- Paper is well-written and key ideas are clearly presented.  
- Originality: adapts Bayesian rose tree ideas to relational data, maintaining the same computational complexity.  
- Significance: scaling BNP network models is crucial; the basic algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may be prohibitive for large networks.  
- Sparse version likely feasible for larger graphs, but the paper should report runtimes on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS, gene/social networks).  
- Recommend evaluating how well the greedy method approximates the optimal tree (compare to exhaustive search where possible) and providing theoretical support (e.g., submodularity).  
- Minor corrections: replace "whilst... but" (Line 48), fix "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees," and "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step."  
- Overall judgment: contribution is significant despite familiar ideas; paper should be accepted.
