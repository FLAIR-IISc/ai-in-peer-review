- Writing is clear with correct grammar/citations, but avoid starting sentences with citations in NIPS style.  
- Theoretical contributions are partly clear, but notation (abstract functions like f() and g()) should be clarified for broader accessibility.  
- Main contribution: extends single-table IRM by integrating Bayesian Rose trees, yielding a simplified CRP formulation (Eq. 7).  
- Method leverages structural factorization ideas (e.g., Koivisto & Sood 2004) to enable efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistic handling, and link prediction.  
- Theoretical foundation and algorithmic presentation are sound and clearly described.  
- AUC-based evaluation methodology is ambiguous—paper does not explain how AUC is computed for SBM (e.g., edge deletion/prediction procedure).  
- Experimental validation is limited to the small NIPS dataset and lacks scalability evidence against larger, modern SBM benchmarks.  
- Comparisons are narrow (mainly Bayesian nonparametric); the paper should include recent semi-parametric and state-of-the-art SBM methods.  
- The agglomerative algorithm (Table 1) is the core contribution and is concisely described.  
- Empirical section lacks rigorous benchmarking: results are illustrative rather than comparative, with no ablation studies or SOTA solver comparisons.  
- Overall: solid theoretical advance but experimental evaluation is insufficient for NIPS standards; addressing AUC methodology, dataset size, and comparative analyses is necessary.
