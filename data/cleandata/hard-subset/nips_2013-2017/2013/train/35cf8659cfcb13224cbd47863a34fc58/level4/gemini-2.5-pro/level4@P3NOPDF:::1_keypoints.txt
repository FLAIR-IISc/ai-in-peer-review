- Introduces a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) using Bayesian Rose Tree ideas to define mixtures over consistent partitions for network modeling.  
- Proposes an efficient greedy agglomerative procedure for learning node hierarchies.  
- Empirical results show similar predictive performance to IRM but with significantly better computational efficiency (e.g., inference in tens of seconds on NIPS-234).  
- Qualitative evaluations on Sampson's monks and full NIPS, and quantitative evaluation on NIPS-234, support the method's effectiveness.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation and logical connections to IRM and BRT.  
- Adapting BRT concepts to relational data is non-trivial and represents a solid original contribution, especially preserving greedy algorithm complexity.  
- Work is significant for scaling Bayesian nonparametric models and making them more practical for real-world network analysis.  
- Scalability concerns: core algorithm scales O(n^2), unclear runtime on full NIPS, and unclear performance/projections for much larger networks (e.g., tens of thousands of nodes); more experiments or detailed scaling analysis needed.  
- Optimality concerns: reliance on greedy agglomeration warrants empirical comparisons to optimal trees on small datasets and/or theoretical analysis (e.g., submodularity) to justify greedy performance.  
- Minor corrections requested: fix redundant phrasing ("whilst... but"), "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees", and "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step" or "Fruitful next steps".  
- Overall conclusion: well-executed, valuable contribution that should be accepted pending suggested scalability and optimality clarifications.
