- Paper proposes a fast greedy agglomerative algorithm for hierarchical community discovery using Bayesian rose trees with a stochastic blockmodel likelihood.  
- Method achieves single-pass bottom-up learning and, with cached sufficient statistics, worst-case time O(n² log n).  
- Well-written and technically sound integration of rose trees and stochastic blockmodels producing a practical algorithm.  
- Efficient caching scheme for recursive updates of sufficient statistics is a key technical contribution enabling scalability.  
- Empirical results show BHCD is orders of magnitude faster than the IRM while achieving comparable predictive accuracy.  
- Lacks comparison (discussion and experiments) to other hierarchical Bayesian models (e.g., Multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian Process).  
- Section 3 is dense and omits important details (e.g., how marginal likelihood in O(n+m) is computed), reducing verifiability.  
- Update equations for sufficient statistics (Eq. 13) need clearer derivation or explanation for reproducibility.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is misleading; method is better framed as Bayesian model selection over nested tree-structured models.  
- Experiments use relatively small networks (Full NIPS < 3,000 nodes); larger-scale evaluations are needed to substantiate scalability claims.  
- Overall: an effective, fast algorithm that would be strengthened by clearer technical derivations and broader comparisons to hierarchical alternatives.
