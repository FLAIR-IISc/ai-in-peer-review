- Introduces BHCD, a hierarchical extension of the IRM using Bayesian rose tree ideas to define a mixture over consistent node partitions learned by greedy agglomeration.
- BHCD is a tree-structured mixture of many stochastic blockmodels with a single-pass greedy agglomerative model selection procedure.
- Worst-case runtime scales as O(n^2) in number of vertices and is independent of nested community count.
- Empirically reported to be ~100× faster than IRM while achieving comparable or better accuracy.
- Experiments: qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and full NIPS, quantitative results on NIPS-234 demonstrating hierarchical community discovery and link prediction.
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition, consistent notation, and thorough model/algorithm explanation.
- Originality: adapts Bayesian rose tree ideas to relational data with important algorithmic adjustments, maintaining computational complexity.
- Significance: advances scaling of Bayesian nonparametrics to larger networks; sparse variant could be feasible for bigger graphs.
- Improvement: provide timing results on larger real-world networks to better assess scalability.
- Improvement: analyze how close the greedy solution is to the optimal tree, e.g., via exhaustive search comparisons.
- Improvement: add theoretical support (e.g., submodularity arguments) to strengthen the method’s foundations.
- Minor corrections: fix phrasing ("whilst... but" → "while... but"; "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees"; "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step").
- Recommendation: contribution is significant despite prior related work and the paper should be accepted.
