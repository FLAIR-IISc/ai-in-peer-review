- Writing quality is high (good spelling, grammar, citations); minor stylistic note: avoid beginning sentences with citations in NIPS submissions.  
- Theory is presented succinctly, aided by consistent f() and g() notation.  
- Paper extends stochastic block models (SBM) / single-table IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees.  
- Replaces CRP formulation of the IRM with a simpler equation (7).  
- Factoring over subtrees parallels Koivisto & Sood’s work, enabling efficient greedy search and computations.  
- Benefits include efficient marginal likelihood calculation, handling of sufficient statistics, and link prediction.  
- Core algorithmic contribution is the agglomerative approach summarized in Table 1.  
- Underlying concepts and algorithms are clear but relatively straightforward.  
- Evaluation uses AUC rather than common SBM metrics like NMI; the procedure (likely link deletion/prediction) needs explicit explanation.  
- Experimental scale is small (e.g., NIPS dataset); results should be showed on much larger datasets.  
- Comparison set is limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; should include semi-parametric and other published baselines.  
- Experimental results are illustrative rather than fully convincing; a more comprehensive evaluation would strengthen the paper.
