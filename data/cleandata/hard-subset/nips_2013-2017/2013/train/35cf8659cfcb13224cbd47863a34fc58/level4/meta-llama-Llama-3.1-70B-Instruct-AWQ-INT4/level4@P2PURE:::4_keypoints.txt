1. Proposes a scalable agglomerative method for hierarchical community detection by extending a Bayesian rose tree prior with a network-specific likelihood.  
2. Core algorithm structure is largely unchanged from prior Bayesian rose tree work; main novelty is adapting the likelihood to network data.  
3. Modified likelihood enables efficient caching of sufficient statistics and can be computed in O(Nodes + Edges) time.  
4. The recursive updating of sufficient statistics and claimed dynamic-programming steps are under-explained and equations are opaque.  
5. Presentation is generally clear but Section 3 (particularly equation (9) and its developments) is dense and could use clearer notation/explanation.  
6. Experimental comparisons are limited to flat-community baselines (e.g., IRM), showing benefits of hierarchy but not benchmarking against hierarchical alternatives.  
7. No comparisons to existing hierarchical models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel Ho et al. 2012, Mondrian Process Roy et al. 2009), a significant omission.  
8. Scalability claims are weakened because empirical tests use relatively small networks.  
9. Use of the term "nonparametric" is questionable; the rose tree approach is better framed as model selection per Blundell et al. [12].  
10. Overall, a technically sound, incremental integration of rose trees with block-model likelihoods that yields hierarchical community discovery, but novelty and evaluation are limited.
