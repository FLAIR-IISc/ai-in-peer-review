- Well-written paper with correct grammar, spelling, and citations; notation (f(), g()) is clear.  
- Avoid beginning sentences with citations to conform to NIPS style.  
- Paper extends single-table IRM using Bayesian Rose tree theory, yielding a simpler formulation (Eq. 7) that replaces CRP-based IRM.  
- Posterior factorization over subtrees connects to prior work (Koivisto & Sood 2004) and supports efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient statistics, and link prediction.  
- Evaluation methodology is unclear: standard SBM uses gold partitions and NMI, but the paper reports AUC without explaining how it is computed (e.g., held-out edge prediction).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scale (small NIPS dataset); contemporary SBM work uses much larger datasets.  
- Comparisons are incomplete: only Bayesian nonparametric baselines are used; semi-parametric and other state-of-the-art alternatives should be included.  
- Agglomerative algorithm is well-summarized in Table 1 and conceptually straightforward; theoretical development is sound and clearly presented.  
- Experiments are illustrative rather than comparative and lack rigorous benchmarks against relevant baselines.  
- Empirical claims are weakened by limited experiments, leaving the proposed method’s advantages unquantified and reducing the paper’s impact.
