- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model using Bayesian rose tree concepts to define a mixture over consistent node partitions and a greedy agglomerative learning procedure.  
- Develops an efficient greedy agglomerative inference algorithm that substantially reduces computational cost compared to the IRM while retaining similar predictive performance.  
- Presents qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and the full NIPS dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234, demonstrating competitive predictive accuracy.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear and concise exposition.  
- Main ideas are adapted from Bayesian rose trees but are meaningfully tailored to relational data, preserving computational complexity.  
- Scaling BNP network models is important; the basic algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may limit large networks, though the sparse version is expected to be feasible.  
- Recommends including timing/benchmark results on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Suggests testing the greedy method on much larger networks (e.g., gene networks with tens of thousands of nodes).  
- Recommends assessing how close the greedy solution is to the optimal tree, e.g., via exhaustive search at small scales.  
- Advises providing theoretical justification for the method where possible (for example, submodularity analysis).  
- Minor editorial fixes: rephrase “whilst... but” (line 48), use “pair of trees” (not “pair of treeS”), and correct “A fruitful next steps.”  
- Overall conclusion: contribution is significant despite familiar ideas, and the paper deserves acceptance.
