The paper presents a novel approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks using a Bayesian nonparametric model. The model, called Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), is an extension of the stochastic blockmodel that incorporates the theory of Bayesian Rose trees. This allows for a simpler and more efficient formulation of the model, as seen in equation (7).

The paper is well-written, with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations. The theory is succinct and clearly presented, making use of functions f() and g() throughout the paper. The authors effectively build upon previous work, such as the CRP formulation of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) and the concept of Bayesian Rose trees.

The evaluation of the BHCD model is somewhat limited, as it primarily focuses on comparing the performance of the algorithm to other Bayesian nonparametric methods. While the results presented in Figure 4 are promising, it would be beneficial to include a more comprehensive evaluation of the model, including comparisons to semi-parametric methods and larger datasets.

One area that requires further explanation is the use of Area Under the Curve (AUC) as an evaluation metric. It is unclear how the authors used AUC to evaluate the performance of the BHCD model, particularly in the context of stochastic blockmodels. A clearer explanation of this evaluation method would be helpful.

The contribution of the paper lies in the agglomerative method presented in Table 1, which summarizes the time complexity of the BHCD algorithm. The algorithm is simple yet effective, and the use of a greedy approach allows for efficient computation.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and efficient approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. While the evaluation could be more comprehensive, the theory and algorithms presented are clear and well-executed.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks
* Simple and efficient formulation of the model
* Clear and well-executed theory and algorithms
* Promising results in the evaluation

Weaknesses:

* Limited evaluation, primarily focusing on comparisons to other Bayesian nonparametric methods
* Lack of explanation for the use of AUC as an evaluation metric
* Evaluation could be more comprehensive, including comparisons to semi-parametric methods and larger datasets
