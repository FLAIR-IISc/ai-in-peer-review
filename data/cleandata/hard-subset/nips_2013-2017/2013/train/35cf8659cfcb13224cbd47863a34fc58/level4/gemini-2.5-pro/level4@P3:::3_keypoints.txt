- Paper proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): tree-structured mixture of SBMs with a tree prior allowing exact, efficient marginal likelihood computation.
- Introduces a greedy bottom-up agglomerative inference algorithm that merges subtrees by local Bayes factor improvements with O(n² log n) worst-case time using cached sufficient statistics.
- Strength: Clear, elegant theoretical development using general on-/off-diagonal likelihood functions and a clean connection between tree prior and likelihood factorization.
- Strength: Novel combination of Bayesian Rose Trees with SBMs and a scalable greedy model-selection algorithm; synthesis is an original contribution.
- Strength: Significant computational efficiency; complexity independent of number of communities and demonstrated two-order-of-magnitude speedup over IRM on NIPS-234.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited in scope and rigor, insufficient to firmly establish state-of-the-art standing.
- Weakness: Missing standard clustering metrics (e.g., NMI, ARI) and lack of experiments on synthetic or benchmark datasets with ground truth.
- Weakness: Narrow baseline comparison (primarily IRM); should include other scalable hierarchical/community-detection methods.
- Weakness: Scalability claim under-supported—detailed time-vs-performance comparison shown only on a small 234-node network.
- Weakness: Unclear link prediction protocol (how training/test splits were constructed), harming reproducibility.
- Suggestions: Expand experiments to benchmarks with known community structure (e.g., Karate, LFR) and report NMI/ARI; broaden baselines to include other hierarchical and SBM methods.
- Suggestions: Clarify link prediction protocol, replicate quantitative comparisons on larger datasets, and fix minor stylistic issues (avoid sentence-initial citations).
- Overall: Sound, elegant, and computationally promising method, but requires substantially stronger and clearer empirical validation before top-tier acceptance.
