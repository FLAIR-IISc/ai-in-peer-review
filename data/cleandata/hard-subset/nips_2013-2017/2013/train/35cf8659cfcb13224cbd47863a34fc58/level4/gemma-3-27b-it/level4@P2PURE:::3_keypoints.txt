- Writing quality is excellent with strong attention to spelling, grammar, and citations; minor stylistic note to avoid beginning sentences with citations.  
- Theoretical development is concise and clear, aided by consistent f() and g() notation.  
- Main technical contribution: extends single-table IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose tree theory.  
- This yields a simplification of the CRP formulation to the more direct equation (7).  
- The posterior factorization over subtrees parallels prior work (e.g., Koivisto and Sood 2004) and enables greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistic handling, and link prediction.  
- Evaluation methodology is unclear: use of AUC versus more common NMI for SBM needs justification and a clearer description of the link-deletion/reinstatement procedure.  
- Empirical evaluation uses relatively small datasets; results on larger datasets (e.g., full NIPS) are recommended.  
- Comparisons are limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; inclusion of relevant semi-parametric baselines is necessary.  
- Key algorithmic novelty is the proposed agglomerative method, effectively summarized in Table 1.  
- Overall: a clear, theoretically sound, but conceptually simple extension whose experiments are illustrative rather than sufficiently comparative or deep.
