- Introduces a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model using Bayesian rose tree concepts to define a mixture over consistent node partitions.  
- Proposes a greedy agglomerative learning procedure for efficient inference.  
- Demonstrates qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and full NIPS, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Achieves predictive performance comparable to IRM at significantly lower computational cost.  
- Paper is very well written and clear (Quality 9/10), with only minor grammatical/phrasing fixes needed.  
- Originality is solid (7/10): adapts BRT ideas to relational data with non-trivial efficiency gains (O(n^2) dense, O(n log n) with sparsity).  
- Significant contribution (8/10) toward scaling Bayesian nonparametrics to networks, but scalability questions remain for larger real-world graphs.  
- Key scalability concerns: no timing reported for full NIPS (n≈1,500) and lack of experiments/timings on much larger networks (e.g., 10k–millions of nodes).  
- Methodological concern: greedy agglomerative procedure may be suboptimal—suggest exhaustive search on small n (e.g., <50) and theoretical analysis (e.g., submodularity) to bound suboptimality.  
- Minor corrections: fix “computational much cheaper” → “computationally cheaper”, “practioners” → “practitioners”, “whilst... but” → “while”, “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees”, “A fruitful next steps” → “A fruitful next step”.  
- Overall assessment: meaningful and practical contribution that advances scalable network modeling; recommended for acceptance.
