- Proposes a hierarchical extension of Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block models to infer tree-structured partitions instead of flat clusters.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm to construct valid partition trees.  
- Shows empirical improvements over the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) in both speed and accuracy on two social network datasets.  
- Addresses a meaningful problem by combining Bayesian nonparametrics with practical scalability for network modeling.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to tree structures enables efficient marginal likelihood computation, avoiding IRM’s exponential search.  
- Main methodological concern: the greedy tree-learning procedure is not clearly connected to the Bayesian generative model and does not perform true posterior inference or clearly correspond to MAP/ML.  
- Results are compelling: comparable or slightly better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, with orders-of-magnitude runtime speedups.  
- Primary weakness is writing quality: frequent spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, and dense prose that hinders comprehension.  
- Dataset descriptions and Figure 3 lack sufficient context for readers unfamiliar with the datasets.  
- Some mathematical notation (e.g., Equation 5) appears overly complex and should be streamlined.  
- Recommendations: clarify the theoretical grounding of the tree-learning procedure, improve exposition (define terms, contextualize datasets), and fix grammar/notation for clarity.  
- Overall: novel and practically valuable work with strong empirical validation, contingent on substantial clarity and presentation improvements.
