- Proposal: hierarchical generalization of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer entity hierarchies from link data.  
- Method: uses an agglomerative clustering algorithm to select a tree of valid partitions.  
- Claim: empirical results on two social connectivity corpora show the method is faster and more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Relevance: addresses scalability of Bayesian nonparametric methods and is timely for social-science applications.  
- Technical insight: restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones enables efficient marginal likelihood computation, avoiding the exponential partition space of IRM.  
- Concern about methodology: unclear connection between the greedy agglomerative tree learning and the generative model—does not appear to perform posterior inference or explicitly find a MAP/ML estimate.  
- Empirical findings: comparable or slightly better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy than IRM, with runtimes orders of magnitude faster.  
- Major weakness: paper clarity and presentation suffer from spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing (e.g., unclear “local changes” and “iterations”).  
- Exposition issues: Section 4 and the mathematical presentation are hard to follow; Equation 5 needs simplification or better explanation.  
- Data/figure problems: datasets (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are under-described and Figure 3 is hard to interpret; consider using a more recognizable dataset.  
- Overall: novel and impactful model with insightful learning algorithm and strong experiments, but publication should require thorough revision to fix clarity and presentation.
