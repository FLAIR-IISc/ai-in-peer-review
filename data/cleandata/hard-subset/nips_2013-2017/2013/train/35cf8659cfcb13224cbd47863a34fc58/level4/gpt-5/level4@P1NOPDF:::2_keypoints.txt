- Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model that infers a tree-based hierarchy of entities from link data.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm that selects a tree representing valid partitions.  
- Claims method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) on two social connectivity datasets.  
- Paper addresses a timely problem combining Bayesian nonparametrics and network analysis.  
- Key insight: restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones makes marginal likelihood computation efficient.  
- Confusion about how the greedy tree-learning procedure relates to the model’s generative Bayesian story and whether it approximates posterior, MAP, or ML.  
- Section 4 needs to clarify the connection between the proposed objective, search procedure, and the underlying Bayesian model.  
- Empirical results are strong: comparable/slightly better accuracy and predictive metrics, with orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements.  
- Writing and clarity are weak, with spelling/grammar errors and vague or hard-to-parse prose.  
- Datasets (monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are not described clearly for unfamiliar readers.  
- Some mathematics (e.g., Eq. 5) and Figure 3 are opaque or hard to interpret; figure choice and presentation should improve.  
- Overall: novel and promising contribution, but exposition must be significantly polished before publication.
