- Exceptionally well-written paper with strong language, grammar, and citation style; minor stylistic note to avoid beginning sentences with citations per NIPS guidelines.
- Theoretical development is concise and elegant, with consistent notation (e.g., f() and g()).
- Paper extends stochastic block models (SBM) / IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees, simplifying the CRP formulation (see Equation 7).
- The subtree factorization builds on prior work (Koivisto & Sood 2004) enabling posterior factorization and related efficient techniques.
- Primary algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative method, effectively summarized in Table 1.
- Evaluation metric choice (AUC) needs clear explanation—likely link-removal/reinstatement—but should be explicitly described.
- Experiments use relatively small datasets; larger-scale evaluations would strengthen the results.
- Comparison set is limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; broader comparisons including semi-parametric approaches are needed.
- Overall, the theory and algorithms are clear but conceptually straightforward, and the experiments are illustrative rather than fully convincing.
