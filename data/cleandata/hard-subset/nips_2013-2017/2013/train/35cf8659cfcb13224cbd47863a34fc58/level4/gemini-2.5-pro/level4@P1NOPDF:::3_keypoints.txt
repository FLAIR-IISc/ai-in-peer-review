- Paper is well-written with clear prose, correct citations, and especially succinct theoretical exposition.  
- Minor stylistic issue: sentences should not begin with citations in NIPS format.  
- Core problem: extends learning of stochastic block models (SBMs) from the IRM.  
- Main contribution: integrates Bayesian Rose Trees to replace CRP with a simpler objective (Eq. 7).  
- Factorization over subtrees is sound and leverages prior work (e.g., Koivisto & Sood 2004).  
- Factorization enables efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, and handling of sufficient statistics for link prediction.  
- Central algorithmic contribution: an agglomerative inference method summarized in Table 1.  
- Experimental concern 1: AUC is an unconventional primary metric for SBMs; authors must clarify how AUC is computed (likely held-out link prediction).  
- Experimental concern 2: datasets are small; scalability to larger graphs (e.g., full NIPS co-authorship) is not demonstrated.  
- Experimental concern 3: baselines are limited to Bayesian nonparametrics and omit relevant semi-parametric/high-performing methods already cited.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically sound and elegant but algorithmically straightforward; empirical evaluation is illustrative rather than comparative and insufficient to convincingly show advantages.
