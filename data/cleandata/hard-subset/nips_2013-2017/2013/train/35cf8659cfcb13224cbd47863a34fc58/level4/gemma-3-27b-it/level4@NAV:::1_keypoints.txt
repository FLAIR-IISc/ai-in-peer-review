- Proposes a hierarchical extension to IRM using Bayesian rose trees, defining a mixture over coherent node partitions and learning the hierarchy with a greedy agglomerative algorithm.  
- Provides qualitative analyses on Sampson’s monks and the full NIPS dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Inference procedure substantially reduces computational cost compared to standard IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains core concepts.  
- Adapting Bayesian rose tree ideas to relational/network data while preserving computational efficiency is a valuable contribution.  
- Scalability concern: quadratic algorithmic scaling may limit very large networks; sparse variant is promising but runtime on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS, biological/social networks) should be reported.  
- Suggests further evaluation of the greedy tree approximation versus optimal trees (via exhaustive search at feasible scales) and exploration of theoretical guarantees (e.g., submodularity).  
- Minor editorial corrections recommended: replace “whilst… but” with “while… but”; “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees”; “A fruitful next steps” → “Fruitful next steps.”  
- Despite related prior work, contribution is significant and the paper merits acceptance.
