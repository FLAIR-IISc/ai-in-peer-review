- Proposes a technically sound extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees.  
- Writing quality is excellent (grammar, spelling, citations); minor stylistic note: avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.  
- Theoretical development is concise and readable, using consistent notation (e.g., f() and g()).  
- Core contribution: augments standard single-table IRM with Bayesian Rose trees, simplifying the CRP formulation (see Eq. 7).  
- Correctly connects to Bayesian network structure learning (factorization over subtrees), enabling efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, and handling of sufficient statistics.  
- Evaluation focuses on Stochastic Block Models (SBM) but uses AUC instead of typical clustering metrics (e.g., NMI); the AUC computation procedure must be explicitly explained.  
- Experimental results (Figure 4) are a useful demonstration but use relatively small datasets (e.g., NIPS); larger-scale evaluations are needed.  
- Comparisons are limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; the paper should also benchmark against relevant semi-parametric approaches cited in related work.  
- Table 1 effectively summarizes the proposed agglomerative method and its functionality.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution and clear algorithms, but experiments are illustrative rather than comprehensive—clarify AUC and expand empirical comparisons to strengthen the paper.
