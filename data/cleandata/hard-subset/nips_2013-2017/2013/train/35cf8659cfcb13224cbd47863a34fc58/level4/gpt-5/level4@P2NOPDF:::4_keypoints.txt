- Proposes agglomerative hierarchical clustering for networks based on Bayesian rose trees with a new straightforward stochastic blockmodel likelihood.  
- Main technical advantage: likelihood allows recursive sufficient-statistic updates and can be computed in O(Nodes + Edges).  
- Paper is generally clear and technically sound but Section 3 (around equation (9)) is dense and needs clearer notation and explanation.  
- Dynamic-programming ideas and updates for sufficient statistics are presented too tersely; more derivation, intuition, pseudo-code, or a worked example is needed for reproducibility.  
- Experiments show improvement over a flat community baseline and useful qualitative analysis on the Sampson network, including comparison to IRM.  
- Major empirical omission: no comparisons to other hierarchical network models, so results primarily show hierarchy vs. no hierarchy rather than superiority over hierarchical alternatives.  
- Relevant missing related work includes the Multiscale Community Blockmodel (nested CRP) and the Mondrian Process; these should be cited and ideally compared empirically.  
- Scalability claims are weakly supported: datasets are not very large and wall-clock time, memory usage, and scaling behavior on massive graphs are not reported.  
- Terminology issue: labeling the method “nonparametric” is questionable given rose trees’ framing as model selection; the paper should avoid or justify this term.  
- Overall: approach is sound and promising, but stronger contributions require clearer algorithmic exposition, careful terminology, and empirical comparison to existing hierarchical models.
