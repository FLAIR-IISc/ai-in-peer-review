- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer tree-structured cluster hierarchies instead of flat partitions.  
- Introduces an agglomerative learning algorithm that exploits tree constraints to make marginal likelihood computation tractable.  
- Key insight: tree-structured partitions reduce inference complexity versus exploring exponentially many partitions (as in the IRM).  
- Empirical results on Monastery and NIPS authorship networks show orders-of-magnitude speedup over IRM with competitive accuracy (AUC, predictive likelihood, classification).  
- Addresses two timely issues: computational burden of Bayesian nonparametrics and increasing use of hierarchical models in social network analysis.  
- Conceptually sound innovation: a Bayesian analogue of agglomerative clustering offering a principled alternative to heuristics.  
- Critical gap: the greedy tree construction does not correspond to posterior inference or a MAP estimate, undermining the Bayesian interpretation.  
- Section 4 must clarify how the algorithm relates to the generative Bayesian model (e.g., what posterior or loss is being approximated).  
- Writing quality problems: spelling/grammar errors and ambiguous phrasing (e.g., unclear meaning of “iterations”).  
- Dataset descriptions are insufficient; Monastery and NIPS datasets need brief contextual definitions for readers.  
- Mathematical exposition is dense (notably Eq. 5 and parts of Section 4) and should be simplified or moved to an appendix.  
- Figure 3 is poorly labeled and hard to interpret; improve labels and consider more intuitive visualizations or datasets.  
- Overall verdict: novel and empirically strong method with substantial runtime benefits; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: reconcile greedy algorithm with Bayesian model, fix language issues, add dataset context and improve Figure 3, and streamline technical equations.
