- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric model adapting Bayesian Rose Trees to relational data for hierarchical community discovery.
- Model is a tree-structured mixture over stochastic blockmodels with a tree prior over partitions.
- Key advantage: marginal likelihood for a tree can be computed exactly and efficiently via dynamic programming.
- Inference uses a greedy bottom-up agglomerative algorithm starting from singleton communities.
- Algorithm complexity: worst-case O(n^2 log n) and independent of the number of communities.
- Empirical evaluation: qualitative on Sampson’s monastery and full NIPS co-authorship, quantitative on NIPS subset.
- Results show ~100x speedup over the IRM with comparable or better predictive accuracy.
- Technical quality: derivation is clear and correct; greedy algorithm justified by local Bayes factor score.
- Authors present and honestly compare several model variants (e.g., sparse, binary-restricted).
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and reproducible; figures (e.g., tree-consistent partitions) are helpful.
- Originality: adaptation of Rose Tree ideas to relational/community detection is novel and significant.
- Non-trivial technical contribution: block-specific sufficient statistics and a caching scheme enabling O(1) updates per merge.
- Significance: addresses scalability of Bayesian nonparametrics for networks and makes hierarchical Bayesian community detection more practical.
- Limitation: O(n^2 log n) worst-case scaling may still be problematic for very large networks; need more runtime reporting and discussion.
- Limitation: greedy inference has no optimality guarantees; suggest experiments comparing to optimal trees on small graphs.
- Limitation: lacking theoretical justification for the greedy merge criterion (e.g., connections to submodularity).
- Minor corrections: wording "whilst... but" (Line 48), "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees" (Alg.1 caption), "A fruitful next steps" → "fruitful next steps".
- Overall recommendation: strong, well-executed contribution warranting acceptance.
