- Proposes a hierarchical extension of IRM using ideas from Bayesian rose trees: hierarchy defines a mixture over consistent node partitions and is learned via a greedy agglomerative procedure.
- Demonstrates qualitative results on Sampson's monks and full NIPS datasets and quantitative results on NIPS-234.
- Inference is computationally much cheaper than IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well written with a clear exposition of core ideas.
- Originality: main concepts borrow from Bayesian rose trees but adapting them to relational data required substantial effort while preserving computational complexity.
- Significance: scaling BNP models to networks is important; n² scaling may be prohibitive but a sparse variant appears feasible.
- Requests additional experiments/timings on larger real-world networks (how long does full NIPS take; scalability to gene/social networks with tens of thousands+ nodes).
- Suggests evaluating how close the greedy method gets to the optimal tree (use exhaustive search at largest computable scale) and seeking theoretical support (e.g., submodularity).
- Minor corrections recommended: change "whilst"→"while", "pair of treeS"→"pair of trees", and "A fruitful next steps"→"A fruitful next step".
- Overall assessment: contribution is significant and the paper should be accepted.
