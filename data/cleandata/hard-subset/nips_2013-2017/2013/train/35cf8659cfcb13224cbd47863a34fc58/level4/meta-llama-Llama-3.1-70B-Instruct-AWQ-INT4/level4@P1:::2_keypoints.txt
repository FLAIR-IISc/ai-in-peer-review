- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchies instead of flat clusters.  
- Uses an agglomerative clustering algorithm to select tree-constrained partitions of entities.  
- Restricting partitions to tree structures enables efficient marginal likelihood computation compared to IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Empirical results on two social-network corpora show comparable or slightly better performance than IRM on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy.  
- Claimed runtime is orders of magnitude faster than IRM inference.  
- Unclear how the greedy tree-learning procedure relates to the Bayesian generative model—does not appear to perform true posterior inference or find a clear MAP/ML estimate.  
- Section 4 (core algorithm) is hard to follow and needs clarification.  
- Writing quality has spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing (e.g., lines 47–50, line 126).  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficient and may confuse readers unfamiliar with them.  
- Some mathematical exposition is obtuse or unnecessary (example: Eq. 5).  
- Figure 3 is confusing and does not clearly show which method performs best; consider using more familiar datasets or clearer visuals.  
- Strengths: novel model, insightful learning algorithm, strong experimental results.  
- Weaknesses: poor writing quality, obtuse math, unclear Section 4, confusing figure.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong results, importance of the problem domain.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity and presentation issues in writing and math.  
- Recommendation: Revise paper to fix clarity, presentation, and writing issues; if addressed, the paper merits publication.
