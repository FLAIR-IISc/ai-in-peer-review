- Proposes a Bayesian hierarchical approach for community discovery by adapting Bayesian rose trees and stochastic block models to networks.  
- Presentation is generally clear and technically sound, though Section 3 is dense and equations (9+) need simpler notation/explanation.  
- Using a tree prior over partitions enables exact evaluation of the marginal likelihood — a notable contribution.  
- Claims efficient likelihood computation (O(n + m)), but the description and recursive update of sufficient statistics need clearer exposition.  
- Experiments show favorable results vs. a flat community baseline and qualitative results (e.g., Sampson's monastery) are convincing.  
- Comparison to the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) highlights benefits of averaging over hypotheses for prediction.  
- Main limitation: insufficient comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012 multiscale blockmodel, Roy et al. 2009 Mondrian Process).  
- Significance lies in efficient hierarchical community modeling, but experiments mainly demonstrate benefits of hierarchy vs. flat models, not superiority over other hierarchical methods.  
- Terminology concern: labeling the approach as “nonparametric” is debatable; it may be better framed as model selection (per Blundell et al. 2012).  
- Scalability discussion is limited—the tested networks are not large and broader scalability claims are not well substantiated.  
- Overall: solid contribution with novel adaptation and efficient inference, but needs clearer technical exposition and more comprehensive empirical comparisons.
