- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the IRM for networks, leveraging BRT ideas: hierarchy as a mixture over consistent partitions and greedy agglomerative learning.  
- Provides qualitative results (Sampson’s monks, full NIPS) and quantitative results (NIPS-234); inference is much cheaper than IRM with similar predictive performance.  
- Paper is very well written with clear exposition.  
- Originality: adapting BRT ideas to relational data required nontrivial effort to preserve computational complexity.  
- Significance: scaling BNP models for network data is important; basic algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may be prohibitive, though a sparse version might be feasible.  
- Requests: include timings on larger real-world networks (how long full NIPS vs NIPS-234; scalability to gene/social networks with tens of thousands+ nodes).  
- Requests: evaluate how close the greedy method gets to the optimal tree (compare at the largest scale where exhaustive search is possible) and provide any theoretical support (e.g., submodularity guarantees).  
- Minor corrections: remove redundant “whilst … but”; fix “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees”; fix “A fruitful next steps” → singular/plural.  
- Overall assessment: contribution is significant despite familiar ideas from BRT; recommendation: accept.
