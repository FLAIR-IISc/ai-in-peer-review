- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to discover tree-structured partitions instead of flat clusters.  
- Introduces an efficient agglomerative clustering learning algorithm to identify a partition tree.  
- Restricting to tree-structured partitions enables efficient marginal likelihood computation compared to the IRM’s exponentially large search space.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social network datasets shows comparable or slightly better accuracy (AUC, predictive log-likelihood, accuracy) than the IRM.  
- Reports orders-of-magnitude faster runtime than IRM inference.  
- Addresses an important, timely problem at the intersection of scalable Bayesian nonparametrics and social network analysis.  
- The connection between the greedy tree-learning algorithm and the Bayesian generative model is unclear—need to clarify whether it performs posterior inference or approximates MAP/ML.  
- Recommend clarifying Section 4 to explain the inference objective and algorithmic guarantees.  
- Presentation suffers from spelling/grammar errors and vague prose that hinder comprehension.  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) lack sufficient detail for unfamiliar readers.  
- Some mathematical notation (e.g., Eq. 5) and Figure 3 are unnecessarily complex or difficult to interpret.  
- Overall contribution is novel and promising and likely publishable after writing and clarity improvements.
