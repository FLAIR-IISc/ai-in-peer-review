- Paper is well written with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations, exceeding average standards.  
- Minor stylistic suggestion: avoid starting sentences with citations to comply with NIPS style.  
- Theoretical presentation is succinct in parts and uses clear, consistent notation (e.g., f() and g()).  
- Extends stochastic block models (SBM) and a single-table Infinite Relational Model (IRM) by integrating Bayesian Rose trees.  
- Integration permits replacing the CRP formulation of IRM with a simpler equation (7).  
- Factoring over subtrees is reminiscent of Koivisto and Sood (JMLR 2004), where posterior factorization enables efficient greedy search and marginal likelihood computation.  
- Evaluation choices are unclear: AUC is reported but its application to SBM is not explained—NMI and other clustering metrics are more standard.  
- Presumably link deletion/recovery was used for AUC, but the paper needs a clearer explanation of the evaluation protocol.  
- Dataset size is small (Figure 4); results on larger datasets should be included as recent SBM work uses bigger datasets.  
- Comparisons are insufficient: more semi-parametric and other Bayesian nonparametric methods should be included.  
- Main contribution is the agglomerative algorithm (summarized in Table 1) and the conceptual extension of IRM with Bayesian Rose trees.  
- Overall: clear theory and algorithms based on a simple concept, but experimental work is mostly illustrative rather than adequately comparative.
