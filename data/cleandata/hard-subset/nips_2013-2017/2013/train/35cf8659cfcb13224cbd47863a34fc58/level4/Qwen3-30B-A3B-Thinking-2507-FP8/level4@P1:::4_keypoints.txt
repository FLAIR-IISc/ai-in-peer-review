- Proposes BHCD: adapts Bayesian rose trees to networks using a stochastic blockmodel likelihood.  
- Key innovation: caching sufficient statistics for recursive updates yielding O(n² log n) runtime.  
- Empirically, BHCD matches or exceeds IRM accuracy while being ~100× faster.  
- Presentation is generally clear with strong empirical results.  
- Experiments on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship show interpretable hierarchical structures.  
- Comparison to IRM highlights advantages of hierarchical modeling for relational data.  
- Related work omission: fails to cite directly comparable hierarchical models (Ho et al. 2012 Multiscale Community Blockmodel; Roy et al. 2009 Mondrian Process).  
- Misuses the term “nonparametric”: method is greedy model selection with a tree prior, not a true nonparametric Bayesian approach.  
- Explanation of recursive sufficient-statistics updates (eqs. 13) is confusing and lacks intuition or examples.  
- Section 3’s notation is dense and cumbersome; σ¬chSS and other terms need clearer connection to the tree structure.  
- Scalability claim is limited by small datasets used; larger-network experiments are needed.  
- Contribution is technically sound and computationally useful, but novelty is overstated due to omitted prior work and mischaracterization.  
- Recommendations: include missing related work, correct “nonparametric” labeling, simplify notation, clarify/update examples, and evaluate on larger networks.
