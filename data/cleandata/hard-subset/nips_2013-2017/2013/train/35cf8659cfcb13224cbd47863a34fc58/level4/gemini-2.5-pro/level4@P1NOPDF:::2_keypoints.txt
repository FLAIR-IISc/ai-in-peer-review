- Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model to infer entity hierarchies rather than flat clusters.
- Uses an agglomerative clustering algorithm to build a tree that restricts the space of valid partitions.
- Restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones enables efficient computation of the marginal likelihood, unlike the exponential space IRM must consider.
- Empirical evaluation on two social-network datasets shows the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).
- The method addresses a timely problem: making Bayesian nonparametric network models more computationally practical for social-science applications.
- The main technical insight is computational efficiency via tree-structured partition restrictions.
- The connection between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the model’s generative posterior is unclear.
- It is not evident whether the greedy algorithm performs true posterior inference or corresponds to a MAP/ML estimator; Section 4 needs theoretical clarification.
- Experimental results are strong on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, with especially large speedups over IRM.
- Primary weakness is presentation: writing quality, grammar, and clarity need significant improvement.
- Several specific presentation issues: ambiguous prose (e.g., “local changes”/“iterations”), insufficient dataset descriptions, overly complex notation (Eq.5), and a confusing Figure 3.
- Overall judgment: novel, insightful, and computationally compelling work, but must fix theoretical clarity (Section 4) and major presentation problems before publication.
