- Proposal: hierarchical extension of the IRM for network modeling using Bayesian rose tree (BRT) ideas (hierarchy defines mixture over consistent node partitions; learned via fast greedy agglomerative procedure).
- Datasets/experiments: qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and full NIPS dataset; quantitative results on NIPS-234.
- Performance: inference is substantially cheaper than IRM while achieving comparable predictive performance.
- Quality/clarity: paper is very well written and ideas are communicated clearly.
- Originality: builds on BRT concepts but requires nontrivial adaptation to relational data while preserving computational complexity.
- Significance: scaling Bayesian nonparametric models for networks is important for practical use.
- Complexity concern: base algorithm scales O(n^2), which may be prohibitive for very large graphs.
- Sparse variant: sparse version should be viable for larger networks, but more evidence is needed.
- Timings requested: report runtimes on larger real-world networks (how long did full NIPS take versus NIPS-234’s tens of seconds?).
- Scalability question: how will inference scale to gene networks (tens of thousands of nodes) and large social networks?
- Greedy method assessment: evaluate how close the greedy tree is to the optimal tree at scales where exhaustive search is feasible.
- Theoretical support: consider providing theoretical justification (e.g., via submodularity) for the greedy procedure.
- Minor corrections: fix phrasing at Line 48 (“whilst … but”); address “pair of trees”; rephrase “A fruitful next step”.
- Recommendation: contribution is significant despite familiar ideas from BRT; recommended for acceptance.
