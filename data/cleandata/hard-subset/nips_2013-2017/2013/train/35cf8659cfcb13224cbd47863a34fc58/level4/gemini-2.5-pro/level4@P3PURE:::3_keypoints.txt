- Presentation is strong: well-written, clear prose, correct citations; minor style issue—sentences should not start with a citation in NIPS format.  
- Theoretical contribution: extends single-table IRM for SBMs by incorporating Bayesian Rose Trees, replacing CRP with the factorization in Eq. (7).  
- The factorization over subtrees is analogous to Koivisto & Sood (JMLR 2004) and enables greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, and efficient sufficient-statistics handling.  
- Algorithmic contribution: a straightforward agglomerative method is the core contribution and is well summarized in Table 1.  
- Evaluation metric is unclear: authors report AUC without explaining how it was computed (presumably link prediction), requiring clarification.  
- Experimental scale is limited: tests use a small NIPS co-authorship network; larger datasets common in recent SBM work are needed.  
- Comparisons are incomplete: evaluation focuses on Bayesian nonparametric baselines and omits many relevant semi-parametric methods.  
- Overall: elegant theoretical advance but experimental work is illustrative rather than rigorously comparative, so empirical advantages are not convincingly demonstrated.
