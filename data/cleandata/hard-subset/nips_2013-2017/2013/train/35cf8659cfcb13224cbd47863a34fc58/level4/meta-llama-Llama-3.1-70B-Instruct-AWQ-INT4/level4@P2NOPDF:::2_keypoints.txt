- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchical structure from link data.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm that selects a tree of valid partitions.  
- Restricting allowed partitions to tree-conforming ones enables efficient marginal likelihood computation vs. IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- The connection between the tree-learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model is unclear and needs clarification.  
- The greedy nature of the algorithm raises questions about whether it performs posterior inference or yields a point MAP/ML estimate.  
- Empirically evaluated on two social-network corpora, the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Performance metrics (AUC, predictive log-likelihood, accuracy) are comparable or modestly better than IRM.  
- Claimed runtime improvements are large—orders of magnitude faster than IRM inference.  
- Addresses a timely problem likely to appeal to NIPS audience (BNP scalability and social-science network analysis).  
- Writing quality needs improvement: correct spelling and grammar errors.  
- Prose is sometimes vague and hard to follow (e.g., “local changes”, “iterations”); Section 4 requires clearer exposition.  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficient for readers unfamiliar with them.  
- Some math (e.g., Eq. 5) and Figure 3 are confusing or unnecessary; consider simplification and using more familiar datasets.  
- Overall novelty and results are strong enough to warrant publication if the clarity and technical-exposition issues are addressed.  
- The model can be viewed as a Bayesian version of agglomerative clustering inspired by the IRM; the core learning algorithm is insightful.
