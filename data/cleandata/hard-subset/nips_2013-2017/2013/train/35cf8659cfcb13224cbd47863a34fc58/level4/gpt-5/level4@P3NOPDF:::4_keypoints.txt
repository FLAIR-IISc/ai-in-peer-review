- Summary: Proposes an agglomerative hierarchical community detection method combining Bayesian rose trees with an SBM-style likelihood; efficient via cached sufficient statistics but omits comparisons to existing hierarchical network models.

- Strength: Sensible integration of rose trees and SBM enabling hierarchical discovery in relational data.

- Strength: Clear overall approach linking to greedy agglomerative clustering.

- Strength: Efficient likelihood computation (O(nodes+edges)) with attractive caching for scalability.

- Strength: Empirical benefits over a flat IRM-like baseline and interpretable qualitative results.

- Weakness: Fails to compare against relevant hierarchical network models, weakening empirical claims about hierarchy.

- Weakness: Section 3 is dense and the notation around Eq. (9), the dynamic programming remark, and recursive statistic updates need clearer motivation and explanation.

- Weakness: Calling the method “nonparametric” is misleading; it functions more like model selection over trees per Blundell et al.

- Weakness: Scalability claims lack substantiation—datasets are modest and runtime/memory/caching impacts are not analyzed.

- Significance: Shows hierarchy can improve modeling/prediction versus flat models but needs direct comparisons to nested-CRP and Mondrian-process-based approaches.

- Originality: Main novelty is adapting SBM to rose trees and engineering recursive updates; technical contribution is incremental but useful.

- Clarity/Presentation: Needs clearer notation, a step-by-step derivation (or running example), explicit dynamic programming formulation, pseudocode, and complexity analysis.

- Terminology: Recommend removing or qualifying the “nonparametric” label to avoid confusion and align with prior work.

- Experiments: Recommend adding hierarchical baselines, scalability metrics (time/memory), sensitivity analyses, ablations, larger graphs, and clearer link-prediction evaluation protocols.

- Suggestions: Simplify Section 3, provide pseudocode and proofs/sketches for recursive updates, expand related work and benchmarks, and add reproducibility details.

- Overall assessment: Elegant and efficient approach with promising results, but impact is limited by missing hierarchical comparisons and exposition; revisions needed before publication.
