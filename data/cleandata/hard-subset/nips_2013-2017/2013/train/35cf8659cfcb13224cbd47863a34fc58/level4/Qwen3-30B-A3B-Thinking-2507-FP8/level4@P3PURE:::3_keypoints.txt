- Writing quality is high (good spelling/grammar/citations) but avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.  
- Theoretical presentation is succinct and benefits from consistent f() and g() notation.  
- Main technical contribution: extends single-table IRM using Bayesian Rose tree theory, replacing the CRP with a simpler formulation (Equation 7).  
- The model’s factorization over subtrees aligns with prior Bayesian network structure learning work (e.g., Koivisto & Sood 2004).  
- This factorization enables efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistic handling, and link prediction.  
- Core algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative algorithm, clearly summarized in Table 1.  
- Experiments report AUC, but the method for computing AUC is non-standard for SBM and needs explicit justification (e.g., link-deletion protocol).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (small dataset, Figure 4) and insufficient given larger benchmarks used in recent SBM work.  
- Comparative evaluation is inadequate: missing comparisons to semi-parametric and other published alternatives.  
- Overall, theory and algorithms are well-explained, but experiments are illustrative rather than rigorously comparative.
