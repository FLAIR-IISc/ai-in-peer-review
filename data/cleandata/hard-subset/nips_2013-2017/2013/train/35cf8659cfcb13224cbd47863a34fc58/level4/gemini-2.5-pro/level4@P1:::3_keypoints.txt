- Introduces BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric model using Bayesian Rose Trees to model hierarchical community structures as mixtures over tree-consistent partitions.  
- Uses a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm that merges subtrees by maximizing local Bayes factors for fast, deterministic inference claimed to be much faster than MCMC IRM with comparable predictive performance.

- Strength: Clear, well-written presentation with elegant theoretical development (recursive marginal likelihood and merge score) and a useful summary of computational advantages.
- Strength: Efficient inference — a practical, scalable agglomerative algorithm with O(n² log n) complexity and O(1) cached updates in the inner loop.
- Strength: Novel combination of stochastic blockmodel and Bayesian Rose Tree ideas enabling exact marginal likelihood calculation over hierarchical partitions.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited and not comprehensive enough to support claims about speed/accuracy trade-offs.
- Weakness: Insufficient baselines — comparisons are mostly against IRM; should include fast non-Bayesian hierarchical methods and other scalable SBM inference techniques.
- Weakness: Dataset scale concerns — main quantitative link-prediction results use a small 234-node network; predictive performance on larger graphs is not quantitatively evaluated.
- Weakness: Evaluation protocol under-specified — details on held-out data selection and AUC computation are unclear, hindering reproducibility; cluster-recovery metrics on synthetic data are also missing.

- Weakness: Context of originality understated — the greedy decomposable-score approach is related to established techniques (e.g., Bayesian network structure learning) and should be better acknowledged.

- Detailed comment: Presenting key equations explicitly for the Beta-Bernoulli case would improve clarity beyond the abstract f(·), g(·) notation.
- Detailed comment: Minor stylistic suggestion to avoid beginning sentences with citations to conform to NIPS style.

- Conclusion: Presents a clear, elegant, and computationally efficient hierarchical community detection method, but would be significantly strengthened by broader, clearer, and more rigorous experimental evaluation on larger networks.
