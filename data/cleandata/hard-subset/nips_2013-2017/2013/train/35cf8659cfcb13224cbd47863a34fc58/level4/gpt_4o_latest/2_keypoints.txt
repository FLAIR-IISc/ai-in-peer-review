- Introduces a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchies instead of flat clusterings from network links.  
- Proposes an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm to identify a tree of valid partitions.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social datasets shows the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Problem is timely and likely to appeal to the NIPS audience, bridging Bayesian nonparametrics and social-science applications.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to a tree permits efficient marginal likelihood computation versus IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- The connection between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model is unclear and needs clarification (posterior vs. greedy optimization, MAP/ML interpretation).  
- Experimental gains: comparable or slightly better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, with a noteworthy orders-of-magnitude runtime advantage.  
- Writing quality needs substantial improvement: spelling/grammar errors and occasionally unclear prose.  
- Several descriptions are vague (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”) and require clarification.  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficient and may confuse unfamiliar readers.  
- Some mathematical details are dense (e.g., Equation 5) and Figure 3 is hard to interpret; a more familiar dataset could aid accessibility.  
- Overall verdict: novel and strong results that justify publication if the authors address clarity, explanation of Section 4, and presentation issues.
