- Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model to infer hierarchical entity groupings using agglomerative clustering to select tree partitions.  
- Empirical results on two social datasets show the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Addresses an important and timely problem for both Bayesian nonparametrics and social-science network analysis.  
- Key technical insight: restrict partitions to tree structures to compute marginal likelihoods efficiently and avoid IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Major methodological concern: the greedy tree-learning procedure does not appear to perform proper Bayesian posterior inference or produce a MAP/ML tree—Section 4 needs clarification.  
- Performance: comparable or slightly better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy than IRM, with orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements.  
- Paper clarity is poor: numerous spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, insufficient dataset descriptions, obtuse math (e.g., Eq. 5), unclear Figure 3, and hard-to-follow Section 4.  
- Despite writing flaws, the approach is novel, empirically strong, and merits publication if clarity is improved.  
- Contribution summary: a novel graph-edge model as a Bayesian variant of agglomerative clustering inspired by IRM, with a solid core algorithm and strong empirical evidence.
