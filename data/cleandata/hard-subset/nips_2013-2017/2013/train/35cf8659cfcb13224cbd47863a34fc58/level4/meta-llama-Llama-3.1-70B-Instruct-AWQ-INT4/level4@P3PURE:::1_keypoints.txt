- Introduces a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model using Bayesian rose tree ideas to define a mixture over consistent partitions, learned via a greedy agglomerative procedure.
- Demonstrates qualitative results on Sampson's monks and full NIPS, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.
- Inference is much cheaper computationally than IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.
- Paper is clearly written and well-explained, though core ideas are adapted from Bayesian rose tree work.
- Significant contribution toward scaling Bayesian nonparametric network models to larger datasets.
- Concern: basic algorithm has O(n^2) scaling and may be prohibitive for large networks; sparse variant is likely more feasible.
- Request for experimental timings on larger real-world networks and explicit processing time for the full NIPS dataset.
- Suggest investigating scalability to very large networks (e.g., gene networks with tens of thousands of nodes or large social networks).
- Recommend evaluating how close the greedy tree gets to the optimal tree at sizes where exhaustive search is feasible.
- Suggest providing theoretical support for the greedy method (e.g., submodularity guarantees).
- Minor wording corrections: "whilst... but" → "while... but"; "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees"; "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step".
- Overall recommendation: contribution is significant despite familiar ideas; paper should be accepted.
