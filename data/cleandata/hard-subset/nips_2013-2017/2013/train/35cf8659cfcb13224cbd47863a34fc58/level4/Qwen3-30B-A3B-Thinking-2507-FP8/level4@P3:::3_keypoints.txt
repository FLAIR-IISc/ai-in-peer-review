- Introduces BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels extending Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data.  
- Key algorithmic contribution: a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm that runs in O(n² log n), much faster than IRM.  
- Theoretical development is solid with clear derivations of marginal likelihood (Eqs. 6–8) and a well-designed Algorithm 1.  
- Introduces a novel caching scheme that enables O(1) computation per inner loop for efficient sufficient-statistics updates.  
- Experimental results show BHCD achieves comparable accuracy to IRM while being ~100× faster; ablation studies and merger analysis add insight.  
- Paper clarity is excellent: concise notation, well-organized exposition, effective figures, and professional writing.  
- Originality is moderate: builds on Bayesian Rose trees and greedy agglomeration—core ideas not entirely new, but the relational extension and caching are meaningful.  
- Literature positioning is good but could more explicitly differentiate from related methods such as the Mondrian process.  
- Significance is limited by a narrow empirical evaluation focused on small datasets and a subset (NIPS-234) rather than large-scale networks.  
- Missing standard clustering metrics (e.g., NMI) and insufficient explanation of how AUC is computed for hierarchical structures.  
- Unclear handling of missing/negative affiliations (e.g., Sampson network) and inconsistent use of evaluation metrics across experiments.  
- Scalability claim needs stronger evidence on networks with >1000 vertices to validate beyond NIPS-234.  
- Main strengths: elegant theory, efficient algorithm design, and clear presentation.  
- Main weaknesses: limited empirical validation, lack of comparisons to recent methods, and unclear evaluation details.  
- Overall recommendation: recommend acceptance conditional on expanded experiments, clearer evaluation metrics/explanations, and comparisons to more recent methods.
