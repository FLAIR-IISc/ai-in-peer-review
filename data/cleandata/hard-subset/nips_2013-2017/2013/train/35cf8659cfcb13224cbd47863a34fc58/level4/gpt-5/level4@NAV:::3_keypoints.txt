- Presentation is strong (good writing, spelling, grammar, citations); follow NIPS style by not starting sentences with citations.  
- Theory is concise and consistent (e.g., use of f() and g() notation).  
- Main technical contribution: extend SBM / single-table IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees, replacing CRP-based IRM with Eq. (7).  
- Posterior factorizes over subtrees, echoing Bayesian network structure learning (Koivisto & Sood), enabling greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistics handling, and link prediction.  
- Evaluation metric unclear: reporting AUC needs an explicit protocol (e.g., held-out edges) since SBM work commonly uses NMI vs. ground-truth partitions.  
- Experiments are small (NIPS example); should be complemented by larger datasets used in recent SBM evaluations.  
- Baselines are limited: comparisons only to Bayesian nonparametric methods; should include semi-parametric approaches cited in the paper.  
- Algorithmic highlight is the agglomerative procedure (well summarized in Table 1).  
- Overall: clear extension of IRM via Bayesian Rose trees; methods are conceptually straightforward and experiments are illustrative but insufficiently comparative.
