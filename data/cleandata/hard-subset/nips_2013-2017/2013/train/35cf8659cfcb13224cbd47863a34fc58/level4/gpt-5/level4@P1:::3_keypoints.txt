- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a tree-structured mixture over SBMs with node-specific stopping probabilities, yielding a constrained partition prior.  
- Derives an exact dynamic-programming recursion for marginal likelihoods (f and g) with a Beta–Bernoulli instantiation for efficiency.  
- Uses a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm with three merge operators (join, two absorptions) and a local Bayes factor for merges.  
- Caches sufficient σ-statistics to enable O(1) inner-loop updates and worst-case O(n^2 log n) runtime independent of community count.  
- Introduces a “sparse” variant that forbids merging disconnected components to exploit network sparsity.  
- Provides fast link prediction via a top-down traversal that mixes f and g with subtree responsibilities rS.  
- Experiments on Sampson’s monastery, NIPS-234 subset, and full NIPS 1–17 show interpretable hierarchies and competitive link prediction vs IRM, with much faster runtime.  
- Positions BHCD between flat community models (SBM, MMSB) and more complex hierarchical models (IRM trees, Mondrian, ILA/LFRM) as a simpler scalable alternative.  
- Notes similarity to Bayesian network structure learning where constraints yield clean marginal decompositions supporting greedy search.  
- Strength: technical simplicity and efficiency with a clear derivation and well-engineered agglomerative search.  
- Strength: practical scalability, reported two orders of magnitude faster than IRM, plus a sparse variant for large sparse graphs.  
- Strength: coherent probabilistic formulation with exact marginalization per tree and principled link prediction.  
- Strength: careful algorithmic design including merge operators, tie-handling restarts, and cached statistics.  
- Weakness: Link prediction protocol is under-specified—need details on train/test splits, negative sampling, folds, and metric computation.  
- Weakness: Limited benchmarks—lacks gold-standard clustering metrics (NMI/ARI), comparisons to scalable baselines (Louvain/Infomap), and larger-scale tests.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to hyperparameters (α,β,δ,λ,γ) and tie-breaking restarts—needs sensitivity analyses and clarification of BHCD variants.  
- Weakness: Scope/generalization limited by focus on undirected unweighted graphs and conjugate priors; extension to non-conjugate likelihoods should be discussed.  
- Presentation: generally clear but has minor style/typo issues and unclear table notations (K, F, IR, complexity constants).  
- Assessment — Quality: technically sound but empirical validation breadth is limited and evaluation protocol must be clarified.  
- Assessment — Clarity: well written overall; expand evaluation methodology and differentiate model variants in figures/captions.  
- Assessment — Originality: neat practical combination of Rose Tree priors with SBMs; straightforward but fills a useful niche.  
- Assessment — Significance: promising for scalable hierarchical discovery and fast link prediction but would benefit from broader benchmarks and robustness analyses.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled, fast hierarchical model with exact per-tree marginalization and strong runtime advantages.  
- Cons for acceptance: empirical section is illustrative and incomplete—missing standard clustering metrics, unclear link-prediction methodology, and limited hyperparameter/restart analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept if authors substantially strengthen empirical evaluation (protocol clarity, broader benchmarks, sensitivity analyses); otherwise a solid methodological contribution with an incomplete empirical section.
