- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchies (not flat clusters) from link data.
- Develops an agglomerative-clustering-based learning algorithm to select a tree of valid partitions.
- Empirical evaluation on two social connectivity corpora shows the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).
- Addresses relevant problems: makes Bayesian nonparametric methods practical (speed) and provides hierarchical structure useful to social scientists.
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to tree-structured ones lets marginal likelihood be computed efficiently, avoiding IRM’s combinatorial partitioning.
- Major concern: the greedy tree-learning procedure’s relationship to the Bayesian generative model/posterior is unclear; it does not perform true posterior inference or a clear MAP/ML estimate.
- Results are strong across metrics (AUC, predictive log-likelihood, accuracy) and yield orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over IRM.
- Writing quality needs improvement: spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing hinder readability (examples cited).
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficiently explained for readers unfamiliar with them.
- Some mathematical exposition is unnecessary or obtuse (e.g., Eq. 5) and Figure 3 is visually unclear about which method is best.
- Section 4, presenting the core contribution, is hard to understand and requires clearer exposition.
- Overall: novel, insightful algorithm with solid results; recommend publication after addressing clarity, exposition, and presentation issues.
