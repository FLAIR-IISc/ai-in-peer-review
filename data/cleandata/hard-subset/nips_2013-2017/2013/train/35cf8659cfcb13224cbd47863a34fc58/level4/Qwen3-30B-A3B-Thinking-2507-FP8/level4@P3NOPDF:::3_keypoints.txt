- Writing quality is excellent and exceeds venue standards; avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.  
- Core contribution: extends IRM by replacing CRP with a rose-tree factorized formulation (Equation 7), leveraging Bayesian rose tree theory.  
- Factorization uses structural constraints from Bayesian network learning to enable efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, and sufficient-statistics handling for scalability.  
- Theoretical exposition and agglomerative algorithm are clear and well-motivated; Table 1 effectively summarizes the method.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: AUC is an unconventional metric for SBM and the AUC protocol (edge deletion/prediction strategy) is not clearly explained.  
- Reliance on only the small NIPS dataset is insufficient; scalability and generalization should be tested on much larger networks used in recent SBM work.  
- Comparative analysis is inadequate: paper compares only to Bayesian nonparametrics and omits several state-of-the-art semi-parametric, parametric, and deep-learning SBM methods.  
- Empirical results are illustrative rather than competitive, leaving practical value and scalability of the method unproven.  
- Recommendations: clarify AUC methodology and holdout strategy, expand experiments to large benchmark datasets, and compare against ≥3 additional modern baselines.  
- Reframe the contribution to emphasize practical benefits (e.g., runtime, scalability) enabled by the rose-tree factorization rather than only theoretical reformulation.  
- Conclusion: strong and rigorous theoretical contribution but currently underdeveloped empirical validation; needs substantial experimental strengthening to demonstrate real-world utility.
