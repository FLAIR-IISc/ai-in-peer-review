- Proposes a hierarchical extension of Bayesian nonparametric stochastic blockmodels to recover nested community structure via a greedy agglomerative tree-construction.  
- Introduces a greedy procedure that selects a tree of allowable partitions, enabling efficient marginal likelihood evaluation.  
- Evaluated on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship networks, achieving accuracy comparable to or better than IRM.  
- Reports substantial runtime advantages—orders of magnitude faster than IRM—while accuracy gains are modest.  
- Problem is timely and of broad interest; hierarchical Bayesian models are appealing and relevant to social scientists.  
- Constraining partitions to tree-consistent ones reduces the partition search space versus IRM’s exponential range.  
- The relationship between the greedy tree-construction and the paper’s generative model is unclear; it’s not shown to correspond to posterior sampling or an explicit MAP/ML estimator.  
- Section 4 (the core algorithm) needs clearer exposition and connection to the model.  
- Presentation issues: typos, grammatical errors, and vague terminology (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”) need fixing.  
- Dataset descriptions are insufficient for unfamiliar readers; Figure 3 and some mathematical exposition (e.g., Eq. 5) are hard to interpret or overly heavy.  
- Overall assessment: novel and empirically strong work that likely merits publication if clarity and presentation issues are addressed.
