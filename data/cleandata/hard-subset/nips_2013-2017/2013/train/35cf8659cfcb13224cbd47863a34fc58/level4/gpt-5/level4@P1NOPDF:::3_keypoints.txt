- Writing quality is excellent (spelling, grammar, citations), but avoid beginning sentences with citations per NIPS style.
- Theory is succinct and clear, with consistent notation (f() and g()).
- Paper extends SBMs by incorporating Bayesian Rose Trees to replace the IRM’s CRP with a simpler formulation (Eq. 7).
- Posterior factorization over subtrees follows ideas from Bayesian network structure learning, enabling clean greedy search and marginal likelihood computation.
- Method allows clean handling of sufficient statistics and link prediction under the constrained class of structures.
- Reviewer assumes AUC is used for link prediction but requests a clear description of the evaluation protocol (holdout rate, negative sampling, repeated splits).
- Suggests reporting NMI or related clustering metrics on datasets with known partitions, since NMI is standard for SBM evaluations.
- Current experiments are small-scale (NIPS corpus); recommends adding larger datasets common in recent SBM work.
- Comparison set is insufficient: include semi-parametric baselines in addition to Bayesian nonparametric methods for fair evaluation.
- Core contribution is the agglomerative method (well summarized in Table 1), but underlying ideas are relatively simple.
- Experiments are illustrative rather than comparative and do not yet substantiate empirical advantages; strengthening evaluation (clarify AUC, add NMI, scale up, include baselines) is needed.
