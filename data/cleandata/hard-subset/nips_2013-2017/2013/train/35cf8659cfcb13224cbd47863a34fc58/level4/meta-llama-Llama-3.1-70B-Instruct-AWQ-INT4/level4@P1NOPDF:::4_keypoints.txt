- Proposes an agglomerative approach for scalable hierarchical community clustering, reusing the prior and algorithmic structure from Bayesian rose trees.  
- Modifies the likelihood to handle networks and maintains efficient caching of sufficient statistics.  
- Runtime for likelihood computation is O(Nodes + Edges), which is desirable.  
- Presentation is generally clear, but Section 3 (especially Equation (9) and subsequent development) is dense and could use simpler notation/explanation.  
- The dynamic programming and recursive updating of sufficient statistics are alluded to but not explained in sufficient detail.  
- Experiments compare favorably to a flat community baseline (IRM) and a qualitative case (Sampson’s data), showing benefits of modeling hierarchy.  
- Major omission: no comparisons to other hierarchical network models, so superiority over existing hierarchical methods is not established.  
- Missing citations and discussion of related hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012 nested CRP; Roy et al. 2009 Mondrian process).  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is debatable given prior work (Blundell et al. [12]) framing; terminology should be clarified.  
- Scalability claims are limited by experiments on relatively small networks.  
- Overall: effectively integrates rose trees with stochastic block-model likelihoods for hierarchical discovery, but lacks comparative baselines and clearer algorithmic exposition.
