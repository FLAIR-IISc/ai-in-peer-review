- Proposes an agglomerative method for hierarchical community detection using a Bayesian rose-tree prior and a network-adapted likelihood, with efficient caching of sufficient statistics.  
- Presentation is generally clear but Section 3 is dense; equation (9) and subsequent derivations need simplification for accessibility.  
- Experiments show improvements over a flat community baseline (e.g., IRM) and a qualitative Sampson network result, demonstrating benefits of hierarchical modeling.  
- Major omission: no comparison to existing hierarchical network models (e.g., nested CRP by Ho et al. 2012, Mondrian Process by Roy et al. 2009).  
- Main contributions are adapting rose trees to networks and deriving efficient recursive updates; the claimed O(Nodes+Edges) complexity is valuable but insufficiently explained.  
- The recursive update equations are given but lack intuitive derivation or rigorous justification.  
- The use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable—rose trees are more akin to model selection than traditional nonparametric methods.  
- Scalability claims are limited by experiments on modestly sized networks, leaving performance on very large datasets unproven.  
- Overall: solid integration of prior methods and promising results, but weakened by missing comparisons to other hierarchical models and unclear explanation of computational efficiencies.
