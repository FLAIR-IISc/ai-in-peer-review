- Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model to infer hierarchies instead of flat clusters.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering-based learning algorithm that selects a tree representing valid partitions.  
- Key insight: restricting partitions to tree structures enables efficient marginal likelihood computation, avoiding exponential partition search.  
- Demonstrates empirical results on two social connectivity datasets (Monastery networks and NIPS authorship).  
- Outperforms the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) in speed and accuracy, with orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements.  
- Maintains comparable or slightly better performance on AUC, predictive likelihood, and accuracy.  
- Addresses a timely problem by improving practical efficiency of Bayesian nonparametric methods for network analysis.  
- Concern: the greedy tree-learning procedure is not true posterior inference and cannot be interpreted as MAP or ML; the relationship to the Bayesian generative model needs clarification (Section 4).  
- Results are compelling but absolute metric gains are modest despite large computational benefits.  
- Paper suffers from significant clarity and writing problems, including spelling/grammar errors (e.g., line 126).  
- Contains vague phrasing (e.g., lines 47–50) and insufficient dataset descriptions for unfamiliar readers.  
- Some mathematical exposition is obscure (e.g., Equation 5) and Figure 3 is confusing for the Monastery dataset.  
- Overall: novel and robust contribution that could be publishable if clarity, exposition, and dataset/figure descriptions are substantially improved.
