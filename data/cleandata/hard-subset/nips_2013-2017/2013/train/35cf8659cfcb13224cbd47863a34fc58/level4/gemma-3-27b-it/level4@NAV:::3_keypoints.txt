- Writing quality is excellent with strong spelling, grammar, and citation practices; minor stylistic note: avoid starting sentences with citations (NIPS guideline).  
- Theoretical presentation is concise and effective, with consistent notation (e.g., f() and g()).  
- Paper extends stochastic block models (SBM) by applying Invariant Risk Minimization (IRM) in a multi-table setting.  
- Uses Bayesian Rose trees to simplify the IRM formulation from a CRP to a more direct factorization (equation (7)).  
- Factorization across subtrees parallels Koivisto & Sood (JMLR 2004), enabling clean posterior factorization for constrained structure classes.  
- This structure facilitates efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, handling of sufficient statistics, and link prediction.  
- Evaluation methodology is unclear: authors report AUC rather than standard SBM NMI comparisons; likely involves link deletion/recovery but needs confirmation.  
- Experimental evaluation is performed on relatively small datasets; results on larger datasets commonly used in recent SBM work are needed.  
- Comparisons should include semi-parametric baselines in addition to Bayesian nonparametric methods acknowledged by the authors.  
- The primary technical contribution is the proposed agglomerative method, effectively summarized in Table 1.  
- Overall: clear, theoretically sound extension with well-defined algorithms, but empirical evaluation is more illustrative than sufficiently comparative.
