- Introduces BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model for tree-structured hierarchical community discovery in networks.
- Extends stochastic blockmodels to allow tree-structured partitions without pre-specifying the number of communities.
- Uses a greedy agglomerative model-selection procedure to learn hierarchies efficiently.
- Restricts partition space to tree-consistent partitions, enabling dynamic programming for marginal likelihoods.
- Achieves significant computational advantages over IRM: comparable/better accuracy while being ~100× faster.
- Worst-case time complexity O(n² log n), independent of the number of communities.
- Empirical results show competitive or superior AUC, predictive log likelihood, and accuracy versus IRM.
- Runtime advantage highlighted by BHCD requiring a single pass versus many IRM iterations.
- Unclear relationship between the greedy learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model (posterior vs MAP vs other).
- Section 4 (core algorithm) is hard to follow and needs clearer exposition.
- Paper contains distracting spelling/grammatical errors and some opaque elements (Equation 5, Figure 3).
- Dataset descriptions (Sampson’s monastery, NIPS co-authorship) and mathematical intuition need clearer, more accessible explanations.
