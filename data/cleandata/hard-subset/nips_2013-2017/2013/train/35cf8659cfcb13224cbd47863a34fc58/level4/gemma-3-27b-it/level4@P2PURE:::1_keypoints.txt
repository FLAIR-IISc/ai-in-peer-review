- Introduces a hierarchical extension to IRM using Bayesian rose tree (BRT) ideas to define a mixture over consistent node partitions.
- Uses an efficient greedy agglomerative procedure to learn the hierarchy adapted for relational/network data.
- Presents qualitative results on Sampson's monks and full NIPS datasets, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.
- Inference is substantially more computationally efficient than standard IRM while achieving comparable predictive performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains core concepts.
- Originality: builds on BRT foundations but required substantial adaptation to preserve computational complexity for relational data.
- Significance: addresses the important challenge of scaling BNP/network models to practical sizes.
- Limitation: core algorithm’s n^2 scaling may restrict applicability to very large networks; sparse variant is promising.
- Missing evaluation: timing/runtime on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS, gene/social networks) should be reported.
- Suggestion: assess quality of the greedy tree by comparison to exhaustive search at tractable scales and explore theoretical guarantees (e.g., submodularity).
- Minor corrections: “whilst... but” → “while but”; “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees”; “A fruitful next steps” → “Fruitful next steps”.
- Overall recommendation: contribution is significant despite familiar ideas from BRT work — recommend acceptance.
