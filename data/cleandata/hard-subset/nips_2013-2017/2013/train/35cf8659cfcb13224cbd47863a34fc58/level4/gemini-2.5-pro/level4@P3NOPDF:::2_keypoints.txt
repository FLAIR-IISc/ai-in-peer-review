- Addresses an important problem: inferring hierarchical structure from relational data with broad applications.
- Novel technical idea: restrict partitions to those consistent with a tree to enable efficient marginal likelihood calculation.
- Practical benefit: achieves comparable or better accuracy than the IRM while being orders of magnitude faster.
- Unclear inferential justification: the connection between the greedy agglomerative learning algorithm and principled Bayesian inference (MAP or heuristic?) is not explained.
- Section 4 needs clarification on the inferential goal and why the greedy approach is reasonable.
- Writing and presentation require improvement: grammatical errors, ambiguous phrasing, and unclear references to "iterations" and "local changes."
- Dataset and figure explanations are minimal: Monastery and NIPS datasets and Figure 3 need better description or more interpretable visuals.
- Some mathematical notation and expressions (e.g., Equation 5) are overly complex and could be simplified or better motivated.
- Overall assessment: promising and novel approach with strong runtime gains; recommend publication if inference clarity and presentation issues are addressed.
