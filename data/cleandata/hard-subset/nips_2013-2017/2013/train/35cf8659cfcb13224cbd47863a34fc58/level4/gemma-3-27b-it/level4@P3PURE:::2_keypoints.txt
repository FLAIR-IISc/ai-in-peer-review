- Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model to infer tree-structured clusters instead of flat partitions.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering-based learning algorithm to select a tree of valid entity partitions.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social datasets shows the method is much faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Addresses a relevant problem: making Bayesian nonparametric network models more computationally efficient and useful for social-science applications.  
- Key insight: restricting partitions to tree structures enables efficient marginal likelihood computation compared to the exponential partition space of IRM.  
- Unclear connection between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model—uncertain whether the method approximates the posterior or yields a point MAP/ML estimate.  
- Recommends clarifying Section 4 to explain whether learning performs posterior inference or a point estimate and how greediness affects Bayesian interpretation.  
- Results are compelling across AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, with runtime improvements of orders of magnitude, though absolute score gains are modest.  
- Writing quality and clarity need improvement: spelling/grammar errors and occasionally vague phrasing (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”).  
- Dataset descriptions are insufficiently detailed (Monastery networks and NIPS authorship), potentially confusing readers unfamiliar with them.  
- Some mathematical notation is convoluted/unnecessary (e.g., Eq. 5), and Figure 3 is hard to interpret without familiarity with the dataset.  
- Overall recommendation: novel and valuable contribution with strong results, contingent on addressing clarity, exposition, and methodological explanation issues.
