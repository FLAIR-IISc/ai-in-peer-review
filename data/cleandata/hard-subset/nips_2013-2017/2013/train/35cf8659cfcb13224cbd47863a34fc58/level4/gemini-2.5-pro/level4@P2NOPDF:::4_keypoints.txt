- Proposes adapting Bayesian rose trees with a stochastic block model likelihood for hierarchical community detection in networks.  
- Main novelty is an efficient greedy agglomerative inference scheme enabling recursive updates of network sufficient statistics.  
- Paper is technically sound overall, but some derivations and notations (notably around Eq. (9)) are dense and hard to follow.  
- The O(|V|+|E|) likelihood-computation claim is underexplained; the dynamic programming details are omitted.  
- Recursive updates of sufficient statistics are provided but lack intuitive explanation or brief derivations to aid understanding.  
- Experimental comparison is limited to a non-hierarchical baseline (IRM), showing benefits of hierarchy but not competitiveness against other hierarchical methods.  
- Fails to cite or compare to relevant hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric network models (e.g., nested CRP by Ho et al. 2012, Mondrian Process by Roy et al. 2009).  
- Scalability claims are not convincingly supported because experiments use relatively small networks; larger-scale tests are needed.  
- Terminology is imprecise: calling the approach “nonparametric” is questionable given the discrete model-selection framing of Bayesian rose trees.
