- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model using Bayesian rose tree ideas, with a hierarchy defining a mixture over consistent node partitions and a greedy agglomerative learning procedure.  
- Shows qualitative results on Sampson's monks and the full NIPS dataset and quantitative results on NIPS-234; inference is computationally cheaper than IRM while matching predictive performance.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Originality: significant nontrivial adaptation of Bayesian rose tree methods to relational data while preserving computational complexity.  
- Significance: scaling Bayesian nonparametric network models is important; the sparse version may be feasible but the basic n^2 algorithm could be prohibitive for large networks.  
- Requests more timing experiments on larger real-world networks and the full NIPS dataset to demonstrate scalability, noting gene and social networks can have tens of thousands of nodes.  
- Suggests evaluating how close the greedy method is to the optimal tree (via exhaustive search at feasible scales) and providing theoretical support (e.g., submodularity).  
- Minor corrections: change "whilst" to "while"; "pair of trees" to "pair of tree"; "A fruitful next steps" to "A fruitful next step."  
- Overall recommendation: accept — contribution is significant despite relying on familiar ideas.
