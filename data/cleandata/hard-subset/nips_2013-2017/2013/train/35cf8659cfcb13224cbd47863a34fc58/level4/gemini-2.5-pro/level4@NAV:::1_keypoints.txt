- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model using Bayesian rose tree ideas, defining a mixture over consistent node partitions learned with a greedy agglomerative algorithm.  
- Empirically shown qualitatively on Sampson's monks and full NIPS and quantitatively on NIPS-234; inference is much faster than standard IRM while achieving comparable predictive accuracy.  
- Manuscript quality: exceptionally well-written with a clear and effective exposition.  
- Originality: non-trivial adaptation of BRT concepts to relational data that preserves the original computational complexity.  
- Significance: advances scalable Bayesian nonparametrics for networks but the O(n^2) base complexity may limit applicability to large graphs; sparse variant is mentioned but not demonstrated.  
- Requested experiments: provide timing results on larger, real-world graphs (e.g., runtime for full NIPS vs NIPS-234) and evaluate scaling to tens of thousands of nodes or large social networks.  
- Greedy-optimality concern: empirically compare the greedy solution to exhaustive search on small instances and/or provide theoretical guarantees (e.g., links to submodularity).  
- Minor corrections: fix phrasing/typos such as Line 48 ("whilst... but"), "pair of trees", and "A fruitful next step."  
- Overall recommendation: despite familiar roots, contribution is significant and paper warrants acceptance.
