This paper proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks, building upon the stochastic blockmodel. The authors introduce a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels, which allows for efficient computation of the marginal likelihood. They also develop a greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm that scales quadratically in the number of vertices, making it suitable for large networks.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem of discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks is of significant interest to the NIPS audience. The authors' approach is novel and insightful, and their experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of their method.

However, there are several areas where the paper could be improved. The writing quality is sometimes hard to understand, with vague phrasing and distracting spelling and grammar errors. For example, the introduction could be clarified, particularly in lines 47-50, where the authors refer to "local changes" and "iterations" without clearly explaining what they mean in this context.

Additionally, the authors could provide more context and description of the datasets they use in their experiments. While the results are impressive, it is difficult to understand the significance of the improvements without a clearer understanding of the datasets and the tasks being performed.

The math in the paper is sometimes obtuse and unnecessary, such as Equation 5. The authors could consider simplifying or removing these sections to improve the overall clarity of the paper.

Furthermore, the authors could improve the presentation of their results. Figure 3 is confusing, particularly for readers who are not familiar with the monastery dataset. The authors could consider using a more familiar dataset or providing more context to help readers understand the results.

Finally, the authors could clarify the relationship between the learning procedure and the generative story behind the Bayesian model. As the reviewer notes, the greedy algorithm does not perform true posterior inference over a distribution of trees, and it is unclear what kind of point estimate is being obtained.

Overall, the paper has a lot of potential, but could benefit from improvements in writing quality, clarity, and presentation. With these improvements, the paper could be even stronger and more effective in communicating the authors' insights and results.

Strengths:

* Novel and insightful approach to discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks
* Effective greedy agglomerative model selection algorithm
* Impressive experimental results

Weaknesses:

* Writing quality could be improved
* Vague phrasing and distracting spelling and grammar errors
* Lack of context and description of datasets
* Math is sometimes obtuse and unnecessary
* Presentation of results could be improved
* Relationship between learning procedure and generative story behind Bayesian model could be clarified

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and insightful approach to a significant problem in social network analysis.
* The experimental results are impressive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.
* With improvements in writing quality and clarity, the paper could be even stronger and more effective in communicating the authors' insights and results.

Arguments for rejection:

* The writing quality is sometimes hard to understand, which could make it difficult for readers to follow the authors' arguments and results.
* The lack of context and description of datasets could make it difficult for readers to understand the significance of the results.
* The math is sometimes obtuse and unnecessary, which could make the paper less accessible to some readers.
