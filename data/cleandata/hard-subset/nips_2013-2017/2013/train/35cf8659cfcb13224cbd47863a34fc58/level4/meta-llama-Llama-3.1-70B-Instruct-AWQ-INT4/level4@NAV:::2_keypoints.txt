- Introduces a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchical clustering of entities from links.  
- Proposes an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm that outputs a tree of valid entity partitions.  
- Demonstrates empirical results on two social connectivity datasets showing better speed and comparable or improved accuracy versus the IRM.  
- Topic is relevant to the NIPS audience and to social scientists using Bayesian models.  
- Addresses a practical bottleneck: Bayesian nonparametric methods are conceptually appealing but often slow in practice.  
- Key technical contribution: efficient marginal likelihood computation by restricting partitions to tree-conforming partitions versus the IRM’s exponential search.  
- Unclear relationship between the greedy learning procedure and the generative Bayesian model; ambiguity about whether this is posterior inference or a point estimate.  
- Section 4 is hard to understand and needs clarification regarding its connection to the model.  
- Empirical performance: comparable or slightly better than IRM on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy.  
- Reported runtime is significantly faster than IRM inference, despite modest absolute asymptotic gains.  
- Writing and presentation issues: spelling/grammar errors, occasionally obscure prose, and confusing parts (e.g., lines 47–50, line 126).  
- Dataset descriptions need clarification (Monastery networks and NIPS authorship); Figure 3 is confusing and a more familiar dataset might help.  
- Recommend simplifying unnecessary mathematical complexity (e.g., Eq. 5) and improving exposition overall.  
- Conclusion: method is novel and insightful with strong experimental results, but publication should follow fixes to clarity and writing.
