- Quality/Clarity: exceptionally well written with clear and precise exposition.
- Originality: builds on Bayesian rose tree framework; adapting it to relational/network data while maintaining computational efficiency is a non-trivial, key contribution.
- Significance: tackles scaling of BNP models for networks, achieving much lower computation than IRM while keeping predictive performance.
- Limitation: base algorithm has n² scaling, which may limit use on very large networks.
- Sparse variant: promising, but paper lacks timing/benchmarking on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS, gene or social networks).
- Empirical questions: report inference time on the full NIPS dataset and assess scaling to tens of thousands (or more) nodes.
- Greedy solution: evaluate gap to optimal tree (exhaustive search at feasible scales) and provide theoretical justification for greedy choices (e.g., submodularity).
- Minor corrections: fix "whilst... but" choice, "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees", and "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step".
- Recommendation: accept — the adaptation to relational data and computational gains make a significant contribution.
