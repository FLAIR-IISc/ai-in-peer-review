- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) using Bayesian rose tree ideas to define a mixture over consistent node partitions.  
- Introduces an efficient greedy agglomerative procedure for learning the hierarchy.  
- Demonstrates qualitative results on Sampson's monks and full NIPS datasets, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Inference is computationally cheaper than the IRM while achieving comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition of key ideas.  
- Originality stems from adapting Bayesian rose tree concepts to relational data while retaining computational complexity.  
- Significant contribution for scaling Bayesian nonparametric models to larger networks.  
- Requests additional timing results on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS, gene networks, social networks) to validate scalability.  
- Suggests evaluating how close the greedy solution is to the optimal tree (e.g., exhaustive search on small instances) and providing theoretical support (e.g., submodularity).  
- Minor typographical corrections: "whilst... but" → "while... but", "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees", "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step".  
- Overall recommendation: contribution is significant and the paper should be accepted.
