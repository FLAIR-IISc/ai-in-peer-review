- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the IRM using Bayesian rose tree (BRT) concepts to model networks.  
- Uses the hierarchy to define a mixture over consistent node partitions.  
- Learns the hierarchy via an efficient greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Presents qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and the full NIPS dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Inference achieves similar predictive performance to the IRM at substantially lower computational cost.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear and concise exposition.  
- Originality stems from adapting BRT ideas to relational data while preserving computational complexity.  
- Significance: advances scalability of Bayesian nonparametric network models for practical use.  
- Concern: basic algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may be prohibitive for large networks; sparse version may be feasible.  
- Suggestion: include timing/results on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS, gene networks, large social networks) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Suggestion: evaluate greedy method versus the optimal tree (e.g., exhaustive search on small graphs) and explore theoretical support (e.g., submodularity).  
- Minor corrections: fix phrasing on line 48 page 1 ("whilst... but"), correct "pair of trees", and rephrase "A fruitful next steps".  
- Overall recommendation: contribution is significant and the paper deserves acceptance.
