- Introduces an agglomerative method for scalable hierarchical network clustering built on Bayesian rose tree methodology.  
- Adapts the rose tree prior and algorithmic framework to a stochastic block model likelihood for network data.  
- Retains efficient sufficient-statistic caching, enabling O(Nodes + Edges) time complexity for likelihood computation.  
- Paper is clearly written overall, though Section 3 is dense and equation (9) and its derivations need clearer notation and explanation.  
- Experimental results show improved performance over a flat community model (IRM) and demonstrate benefits of hierarchical structure.  
- Lacks comparison to other hierarchical network models, weakening claims about relative performance.  
- Does not engage with relevant Bayesian nonparametric hierarchical methods (e.g., nested CRP by Ho et al. 2012, Mondrian Process by Roy et al. 2009).  
- The claimed dynamic-programming optimization prior to equation (10) and the recursive sufficient-statistic updates are insufficiently explained and justified.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable and should be clarified per Blundell et al. [12].  
- Qualitative Sampson example is adequate, but experimental networks are not large-scale despite scalability claims.  
- Overall, the integration of rose trees with SBM is a valuable contribution, but missing comparisons to existing hierarchical approaches limit assessment of novelty and effectiveness.
