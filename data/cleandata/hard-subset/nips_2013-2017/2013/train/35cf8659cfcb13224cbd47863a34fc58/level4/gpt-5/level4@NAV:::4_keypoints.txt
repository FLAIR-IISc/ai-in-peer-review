- Proposes an agglomerative method for scalable hierarchical community detection closely mirroring Bayesian rose trees.  
- Adapts the likelihood for network data with efficient caching of sufficient statistics and O(Nodes + Edges) evaluation.  
- Exposition is generally clear but Section 3 is dense; equation (9), the DP reference, and subsequent derivations are hard to follow.  
- Main novelty is pairing a stochastic block-model likelihood with rose trees, changing the recursive sufficient-statistics updates.  
- Recursive update formulas lack intuition and justification, making the mechanics difficult to follow.  
- Describing the method as “nonparametric” is debatable given rose trees’ framing as model selection.  
- Experiments show hierarchy improves over a flat baseline (IRM) and include solid qualitative analysis on Sampson data.  
- Missing comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., nested CRP, Mondrian Process), so superiority over hierarchical alternatives is not established.  
- Scalability is discussed but evaluated networks are not large, so empirical scalability evidence is limited.  
- Overall: effective and efficient combination of prior work and SBM likelihood that yields hierarchical communities, but clarity and comparative evaluation need strengthening.
