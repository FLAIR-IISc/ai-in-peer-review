- Paper is well-written with excellent grammar, spelling, and citation accuracy.  
- Theoretical presentation is concise (e.g., consistent use of f() and g()), but avoid beginning sentences with citations per NIPS style.  
- Extends single-table IRM by integrating Bayesian Rose tree theory, allowing replacement of the CRP formulation with the simpler Eq. (7).  
- Subtree factorization matches Koivisto & Sood (JMLR 2004, Sec. 3) and enables posterior factorization over constrained structures.  
- Factorization facilitates efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistics handling, and link prediction.  
- Use of AUC for SBM evaluation requires explicit justification (likely link deletion/reconstruction) and clearer explanation.  
- Experimental evaluation relies on a small dataset; results should be validated on larger networks used in recent SBM work.  
- Comparisons are limited to Bayesian non-parametric methods; should include established semi-parametric alternatives cited in the paper.  
- Algorithmic contribution is the agglomerative approach summarized in Table 1; theory and algorithms are clear and simple, but experiments are illustrative rather than comparative, making the empirical evaluation insufficient.
