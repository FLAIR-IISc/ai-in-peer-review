- Proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model combining stochastic blockmodels with Bayesian Rose trees for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Replaces the IRM’s CRP prior with a tree-based prior (eq. 7), enabling efficient computation and greedy search.  
- Theoretical development is concise and elegant, with f() and g() functions streamlining notation.  
- Factorization over subtrees mirrors prior work (Koivisto & Sood) and supports effective marginal likelihood calculations.  
- BHCD demonstrates substantial speedups over the IRM while achieving comparable or better accuracy.  
- Use of cached sufficient statistics further improves performance on sparse networks (BHCD sparse).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates contributions within related literature.  
- Clearly identifies limitations of flat SBMs and prior hierarchical approaches, emphasizing computational cost.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and would benefit from broader benchmarks and larger datasets.  
- Use of AUC as an evaluation metric is unclear; the assumed link deletion/prediction setup should be explicitly stated.  
- omits comparisons to many semi-parametric/established methods and does not report standard clustering metrics like NMI.  
- Strengths: technical soundness, computational efficiency, clarity of writing, and a novel SBMs+Rose tree combination.  
- Weaknesses: limited experiments, unclear AUC methodology, and missing standard evaluation metrics and broader comparisons.  
- For acceptance: novel, technically solid approach with significant efficiency gains and elegant theory.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient experimental breadth and benchmarking weaken claims of superiority.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on expanding experiments and clarifying AUC methodology.
