- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchies instead of flat clusters.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm to select a tree of valid partitions.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social-network corpora shows the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Addresses a relevant problem for NIPS: improving practical performance of Bayesian nonparametric methods and extending network models for social scientists.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to tree-conforming ones enables efficient marginal likelihood computation versus IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Concerns about clarity: unclear how the greedy tree-learning relates to the Bayesian generative model and whether it represents posterior/MAP/ML inference.  
- Strong empirical results on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, with runtime improvements by orders of magnitude over IRM.  
- Writing quality needs improvement: spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, and confusing exposition in Section 4.  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks and NIPS authorship) are inadequately explained, and some math/figures (Eq.5, Figure 3) are confusing.  
- Overall assessment: novel and insightful algorithm with strong results, recommend publication if clarity and presentation issues are addressed.
