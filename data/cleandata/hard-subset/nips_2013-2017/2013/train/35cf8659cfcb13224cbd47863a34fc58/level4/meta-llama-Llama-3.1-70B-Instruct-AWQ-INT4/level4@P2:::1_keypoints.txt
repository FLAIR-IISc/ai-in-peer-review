- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) using ideas from the Bayesian rose tree paper for network modeling.  
- Uses the hierarchy to define a mixture over consistent node partitions and learns it via an efficient greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Demonstrates qualitative results on Sampson's monks and full NIPS datasets and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition of key ideas.  
- Builds on Bayesian rose tree concepts but adapts them importantly to relational data while maintaining computational complexity.  
- Significance: advances scaling Bayesian nonparametric models to network settings, important for practical use.  
- Concern: basic algorithm has O(n^2) scaling which may be prohibitive for large networks; sparse variant likely more feasible.  
- Suggestion: include runtime/ timing results on larger real-world networks to demonstrate efficiency.  
- Suggestion: evaluate how close the greedy method is to the optimal tree (via exhaustive search at feasible scales).  
- Suggestion: provide theoretical support for the method (e.g., submodularity or related guarantees).  
- Minor corrections: fix wording on line 48 ("while... but"), "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees", and "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step".  
- Overall recommendation: contribution is significant despite familiar ideas; paper should be accepted.
