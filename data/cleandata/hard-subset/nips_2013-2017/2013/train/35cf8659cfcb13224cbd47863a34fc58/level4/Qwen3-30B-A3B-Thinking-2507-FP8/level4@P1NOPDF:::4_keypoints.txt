- Proposes agglomerative hierarchical clustering for networks by adapting Bayesian rose trees with a network-specific likelihood.  
- Core model and algorithm largely unchanged from prior Bayesian rose trees work [12]; likelihood adaptation is straightforward.  
- Efficient caching of sufficient statistics enables O(nodes + edges) computational complexity.  
- Presentation is clear overall, but Section 3 is dense and Equation (9) and its extensions could be simplified.  
- Recursive update equations and the "dynamic programming" before Equation (10) are vague and lack intuitive justification, harming reproducibility.  
- Experiments show gains over a flat-community baseline (IRM) and a qualitative Sampson example, demonstrating benefits of hierarchical modeling.  
- Major limitation: no comparisons to existing hierarchical network models, so it's unclear if the method outperforms specialized hierarchical approaches.  
- Key omitted related work: Ho et al. (2012) multiscale community blockmodel and Roy et al. (2009) Mondrian Process, which weakens novelty claims.  
- Use of the term "nonparametric" is questionable and inconsistent with prior framing of rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric.  
- Scalability claims lack context because evaluated networks are not large-scale, undermining practical scalability evidence.
