- Introduces a fast Bayesian hierarchical community model using a tree prior and a blockmodel likelihood, with exact marginal likelihood via dynamic programming.  
- Uses a greedy agglomerative procedure (join/absorb) with cached sufficient statistics to select trees by local Bayes factors.  
- Builds on Bayesian hierarchical clustering / rose trees but replaces i.i.d. likelihoods with a conjugate stochastic blockmodel for relational data.  
- Experiments show substantial speed-ups and competitive predictive performance versus the flat Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Technical soundness: conjugate Beta-Bernoulli blocks enable tractable marginal likelihoods and efficient local merge scores.  
- Key recursion (Eq. 10) and sufficient-statistics updates (Eq. 13) are central but presented densely and need clearer derivation.  
- Recommend providing DP pseudo-code and a small worked example to improve accessibility.  
- Complexity claims (O(n + m) likelihood, O(n^2 log n) inference with caching) are plausible and supported by the algorithmic structure.  
- Empirical speed-ups over IRM are convincing, but comparisons are limited to flat models.  
- Paper is generally well written, but notation around σSS and σ¬chSS is convoluted and would benefit from diagrams or simplification.  
- A brief upfront recap of assumptions (undirected, no self-edges, missing edges) and hyperparameter roles would help readers.  
- Originality lies in adapting rose-tree model selection to networks with a conjugate blockmodel and an efficient caching scheme—an engineering contribution rather than a novel probabilistic model.  
- Method is practically significant: fast, interpretable hierarchical community discovery with exact marginal likelihood and good predictive performance.  
- Significance would be stronger with comparisons to hierarchical network baselines (Herlau et al., Ho et al., Roy & Teh).  
- The “Bayesian nonparametric” label is misleading; better framed as Bayesian model selection over an unbounded model class.  
- Experiments: Sampson results sensible; NIPS-234 shows speed and predictive advantages; full NIPS hierarchy is interesting but datasets are modest in size.  
- Scalability claim should be validated on much larger sparse graphs (e.g., >50K nodes).  
- Suggestions: clearer DP derivation, comparisons to hierarchical baselines, hyperparameter sensitivity or inference, clarify terminology, and discuss directed/weighted extensions.  
- Pros: simple, fast, exact marginal likelihood via DP; efficient greedy agglomeration with cached stats; competitive performance and interpretable hierarchies.  
- Cons: limited baseline comparisons to other hierarchical models; dense presentation of key derivations; scalability demonstrated only on modest networks; “nonparametric” terminology may mislead.
