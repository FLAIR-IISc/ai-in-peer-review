- Introduces a hierarchical extension to IRM for network modeling, borrowing BRT ideas: hierarchy as a mixture over node partitions and a greedy agglomerative learning procedure.  
- Evaluated on Sampson’s monks, full NIPS, and NIPS-234; inference is far more computationally efficient than standard IRM while retaining comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear, accessible, and logically structured exposition.  
- Main contribution is adapting BRT concepts to relational data, preserving comparable computational complexity.  
- Addresses the important challenge of scaling BNP models to network data; proposes a sparse version to improve feasibility.  
- Concern: the base algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may not suit very large networks; request timing results on larger real-world networks and the runtime for the full NIPS dataset.  
- Suggest empirically comparing the greedy hierarchical construction to the exhaustive optimal tree at the largest tractable scale and exploring theoretical guarantees (e.g., via submodularity).  
- Minor corrections: fix wording on line 48 (“whilst… but” → “although” or “while… but”), “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees”, and “A fruitful next steps” → “Fruitful next steps.”  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution adapting known ideas to a new domain with improved efficiency; recommend acceptance.
