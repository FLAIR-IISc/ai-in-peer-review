- Introduces BHCD: a scalable Bayesian nonparametric method for hierarchical community discovery in social networks.  
- Builds on Bayesian Rose Trees, using tree-structured mixtures of stochastic blockmodels and a greedy agglomerative model-selection procedure.  
- Novel caching scheme enables O(n² log n) inference (sparse variant O(n + m)), maintaining computational efficiency for relational data.  
- Demonstrates substantial computational advantages over IRM—comparable predictive performance at roughly two orders of magnitude faster.  
- Empirical validation on Sampson’s monastery, NIPS-234, and full NIPS dataset shows effectiveness of the approach.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly explained, and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.  
- Adaptation from Rose Trees to relational data is a non-trivial, original contribution that avoids MCMC/expensive greedy searches of prior hierarchical Bayesian models.  
- Significance: scaling Bayesian nonparametrics to larger networks is valuable; complexity claims are compelling, especially for dense graphs.  
- Limitations: more timing/scalability data needed for larger real-world networks (>10⁴ nodes) and the full NIPS runtime; theoretical analysis of the greedy procedure’s optimality (e.g., submodularity) would strengthen claims.  
- Minor corrections: replace “whilst” with “while” (Line 48 p.1), “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees”, and “A fruitful next steps” → “A fruitful next step”.  
- Overall assessment: significant, practically useful contribution and well-positioned for acceptance at NIPS.
