- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) for networks using ideas from the Bayesian Rose Tree (BRT).
- Leverages BRT concepts that a hierarchy defines a mixture over consistent node partitions and can be learned by a greedy agglomerative procedure.
- Presents qualitative results on Sampson's monks and full NIPS, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.
- Inference is much cheaper than standard IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.
- Paper is well-written with a clear exposition of core ideas.
- Adaptation of BRT to relational data is a non-trivial, original contribution, preserving BRT computational complexity.
- Scaling is important; basic algorithm’s O(n^2) may limit large-network use, though a sparse variant could be feasible.
- Requests for improvements: report timing on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS, tens of thousands of nodes) to assess scalability.
- Requests empirical comparison of the greedy solution to the optimal tree on small problems where exhaustive search is possible.
- Suggests providing theoretical support for the greedy method (e.g., links to submodularity or performance guarantees).
- Minor corrections: remove redundant phrasing ("whilst... but") and fix typos like "pair of treeS" and "A fruitful next steps".
- Overall recommendation: accept — the network adaptation is a significant contribution despite conceptual ties to BRT.
