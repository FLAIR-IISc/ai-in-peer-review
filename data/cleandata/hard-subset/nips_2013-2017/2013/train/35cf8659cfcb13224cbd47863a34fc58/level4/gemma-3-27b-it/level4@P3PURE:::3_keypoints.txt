- Writing is strong with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations; minor style note: avoid starting sentences with citations in NIPS style.
- Theory is succinct and consistent, notably the consistent use of f() and g() notation.
- Main technical contribution: extends single-table IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees, replacing the CRP formulation with a simpler factoring equation (7).
- The subtree factoring resembles Koivisto & Sood’s Bayesian network structure work, enabling efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, and handling of sufficient statistics.
- Evaluation metric choice (AUC) is unusual for SBM where NMI against gold-standard partitions is common; the link-deletion/prediction procedure needs clearer explanation.
- Datasets used are relatively small (smaller than many recent SBM evaluations, e.g., larger than full NIPS dataset); larger-scale experiments are needed.
- Comparison set is limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; should include semi-parametric and other published SBM approaches already cited by the authors.
- Primary algorithmic novelty is the agglomerative approach (Table 1), but the underlying concepts and algorithms are relatively simple.
- Experimental work is illustrative rather than comprehensive and is insufficient to fully validate the proposed method.
