- Proposes BHCD: a Bayesian nonparametric tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with a greedy agglomerative learning algorithm.  
- Demonstrates comparable or better accuracy than the IRM with up to two orders of magnitude faster runtime.  
- Targets the important problem of inferring hierarchical community structure and addresses scalability in Bayesian nonparametric models.  
- Key insight: constrain partitions with a tree structure to enable efficient marginal likelihood computation and reduced search space.  
- Experimental validation on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship datasets shows promising speed and accuracy; includes ablation study (sparse vs. non-sparse, binary vs. non-binary).  
- Algorithmic complexity reported as O(n^2 log n), a significant scalability improvement.  
- Strength: novel combination of tree-structured mixture and greedy learning for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding in Bayesian nonparametrics and efficient marginal likelihood computation.  
- Weakness: Section 4’s description of the learning procedure is dense and hard to follow.  
- Weakness: Connection between the greedy algorithm and true posterior inference is not fully articulated; approximations/limitations unclear.  
- Weakness: Several grammatical errors and awkward phrasings reduce readability.  
- Weakness: Dataset descriptions, particularly for the NIPS co-authorship data, are too brief.  
- Argument for acceptance: technically sound, novel, and provides substantial efficiency gains with competitive accuracy.  
- Argument for rejection: current clarity and discussion of Bayesian inference limits reproducibility and understanding.  
- Overall: Strong potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, clarify inference relation, and fix writing.
