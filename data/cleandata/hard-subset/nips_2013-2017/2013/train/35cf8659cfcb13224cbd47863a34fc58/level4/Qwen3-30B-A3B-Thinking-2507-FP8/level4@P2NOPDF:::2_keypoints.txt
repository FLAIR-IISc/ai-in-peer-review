- Introduces a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model for nested partitions instead of flat clusters.  
- Uses an agglomerative learning algorithm to construct a valid tree of nested partitions, avoiding exponential search over flat partitions.  
- Demonstrates comparable or slightly better performance than IRM (AUC, predictive likelihood, accuracy) on two social network datasets.  
- Achieves orders-of-magnitude faster inference than IRM, enabling scalability to larger networks.  
- Addresses a timely problem: computational inefficiency of Bayesian nonparametrics in practice.  
- Key technical insight: computing marginal likelihood efficiently by restricting partitions to tree-structured hierarchies.  
- Major conceptual concern: unclear relationship between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model.  
- Greedy algorithm cannot perform true posterior inference over trees or produce MAP/ML estimates—Section 4 needs clarification.  
- Empirical gains are modest in accuracy but practically significant due to dramatic runtime improvements.  
- Paper suffers from clarity issues: spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing (e.g., "local changes," "iterations").  
- Datasets are insufficiently described (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship), hindering comprehension for unfamiliar readers.  
- Mathematical exposition is sometimes dense and Figure 3 is hard to interpret without better explanation.  
- Writing quality needs substantial improvement: define technical terms, streamline redundant claims, and provide better dataset/figure context.  
- Overall judgment: novel and computationally valuable contribution that merits publication after revisions clarifying theory, improving presentation, and enhancing figures/dataset descriptions.
