- Proposes an efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for hierarchical community detection by extending the stochastic blockmodel with Bayesian Rose trees.  
- Paper is well-written with correct citations and good presentation.  
- Theory is succinct and clear, with effective use of f() and g() notation.  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Koivisto & Sood 2004) for Bayesian network structure learning.  
- Factoring over subtrees is a key technical contribution enabling greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistics handling, and link prediction.  
- Evaluation focuses on NIPS co-authorship data and predictive performance.  
- AUC is used as an evaluation metric, but its use and relation to NMI for SBM is not fully explained.  
- Contribution centers on an agglomerative, efficiently scalable community-detection method.  
- Table 1 effectively summarizes advantages over other Bayesian nonparametric methods.  
- Experimental work is primarily illustrative and lacks comprehensive benchmarking on larger or more varied datasets.  
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to state-of-the-art (including semi-parametric methods) and unclear AUC reporting.  
- Suggestions: include broader method comparisons, clarify AUC usage, evaluate on larger datasets, and analyze hyperparameter impacts.
