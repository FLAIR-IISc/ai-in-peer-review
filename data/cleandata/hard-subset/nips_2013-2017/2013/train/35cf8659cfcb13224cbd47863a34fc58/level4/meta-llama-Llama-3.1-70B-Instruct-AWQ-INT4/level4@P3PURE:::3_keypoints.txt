- Paper is well-written with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations; but starting a sentence with a citation violates NIPS style.
- Theoretical presentation is succinct with clear, consistent notation (e.g., f() and g()).
- Contribution: extends single-table IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees, replacing the CRP formulation with a simpler equation (7).
- Posterior factoring over subtrees follows Koivisto & Sood (JMLR 2004), enabling factorization by constrained structures.
- This formulation allows use of greedy search, marginal likelihoods, sufficient-statistics handling, and link prediction techniques.
- Evaluation choice is questionable: SBMs are usually evaluated with gold-standard partitions and metrics like NMI, but the paper uses AUC.
- The link-deletion-and-prediction evaluation needs clearer explanation of methodology.
- Experiments are small-scale (Figure 4); results on larger datasets are needed.
- Comparison set is limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; should include semi-parametric baselines mentioned in the paper.
- Main algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative method (Table 1); theory and algorithms are clear but relatively simple.
- Experimental work is illustrative rather than sufficiently comparative or rigorous.
