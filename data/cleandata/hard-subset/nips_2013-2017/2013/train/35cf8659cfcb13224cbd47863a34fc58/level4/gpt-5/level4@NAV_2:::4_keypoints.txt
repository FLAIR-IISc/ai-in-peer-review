- Proposes a scalable agglomerative method combining Bayesian rose trees with a stochastic blockmodel likelihood for hierarchical community detection in networks.
- Follows prior rose-tree framework closely; main contributions are adapting the likelihood to relational data and efficient caching of sufficient statistics.
- Claims marginal likelihood evaluation in O(Nodes + Edges) and efficient recursive updates, but the exposition and dynamic-programming reference before Eq. (10) are terse and hard to parse.
- Section 3 is dense; notation and explanation around Eq. (9) and dependent components need simplification or clarification.
- The recursive sufficient-statistic updates are stated but lack additional intuition or justification to build confidence in correctness.
- Experiments compare favorably to a flat community baseline (e.g., IRM) and qualitative analysis (Sampson’s monastery) is appropriate.
- Major empirical gap: no comparisons to existing hierarchical network models (e.g., Multiscale Community Blockmodel, Mondrian Process), so it’s unclear if it outperforms other hierarchical approaches.
- Scalability is claimed, but the datasets used are not especially large, weakening empirical support for scalability.
- Terminology concern: labeling the approach “nonparametric” is debatable given rose trees can be framed as model selection rather than nonparametric inference.
- Overall: technically sound and practically useful extension, but clarity, stronger empirical baselines, and more detailed algorithmic exposition are needed.
