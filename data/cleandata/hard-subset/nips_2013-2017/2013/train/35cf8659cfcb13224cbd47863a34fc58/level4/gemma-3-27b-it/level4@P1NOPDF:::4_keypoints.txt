- Proposes an agglomerative method for hierarchical community clustering on networks using a rose-tree prior and modified likelihood for networks.  
- Prior and algorithmic structure are essentially unchanged from Bayesian rose trees [12], limiting novelty.  
- Main technical contribution is integrating a stochastic blockmodel likelihood and efficient caching of sufficient statistics.  
- Likelihood computation can be done in O(Nodes + Edges) time, which is notable for efficiency.  
- Presentation is generally clear but Section 3 is dense; equation (9) and subsequent derivations are hard to follow.  
- Recursive updates for sufficient statistics are given but lack clear explanation or justification of correctness.  
- Brief allusion to dynamic programming before equation (10) is insufficiently detailed.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable and inconsistent with Blundell et al.’s framing.  
- Experiments show improvements over a flat community baseline (IRM) and qualitative results on the Sampson network.  
- Lacks comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., Multiscale Community Blockmodel, Mondrian Process) and omits those related works.  
- Scalability discussion exists but tested networks are not large, so empirical scalability evidence is limited.  
- Overall: effectively combines rose trees with SBM for hierarchical discovery, but clarity, novelty, and comparative evaluation are lacking.
