- Proposes an agglomerative method for scalable hierarchical clustering of networks, building on Bayesian rose trees.  
- Prior over (possibly non-binary) trees and overall algorithmic structure are essentially unchanged from Bayesian rose trees [12].  
- Likelihood is straightforwardly adapted to network (block-model) data, and sufficient statistics can be cached efficiently.  
- Claims O(Nodes + Edges) likelihood computation, which is a noteworthy computational strength.  
- Presentation is generally clear but Sections 3 and Eq. (9)–(10) are dense and hard to follow; dynamic-programming details are insufficient.  
- Recursive updates of sufficient statistics lack clear justification and detailed exposition, hindering verification and reproducibility.  
- Empirical comparisons show improvement over flat community models (e.g., IRM) and qualitative results on Sampson are solid.  
- Missing comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel / nested CRP, Mondrian process) weaken empirical claims.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable given prior framing of rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric.  
- Overall contribution integrates rose trees with block-model likelihoods effectively but would be stronger with clearer algorithmic exposition and broader empirical baselines.
