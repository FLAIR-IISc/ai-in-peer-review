- Proposes agglomerative hierarchical clustering for networks based on a prior over (possibly non-binary) trees, adapting Bayesian rose trees to network data.  
- Core model and algorithm are largely derived from Blundell et al. [12], with modifications to the likelihood and efficient caching of sufficient statistics.  
- Presentation is clear and experiments show advantages over flat-community baselines (e.g., IRM).  
- Major evaluation gap: no comparisons to established hierarchical network models, limiting claims of superiority.  
- Missing benchmarks include Ho et al. (2012) multiscale community blockmodel (nested CRP) and Roy et al. (2009) Mondrian Process.  
- Section 3 notation (Equation 9 and extensions) is dense and could be simplified for accessibility.  
- Main technical contribution is adapting sufficient statistics for recursive greedy agglomeration, achieving O(nodes + edges) runtime.  
- Description of the sufficient-statistics mechanism is inadequate: the dynamic-programming mention before Eq. (10) lacks concrete explanation.  
- Recursive update equations (Eqs. 11–12) are presented without sufficient derivation or justification.  
- The term "nonparametric" is misleading; the approach aligns more with model selection as discussed by Blundell et al.  
- Scalability claims are weak: experiments use small networks (Karate, Dolphins) and lack large-scale evaluation.  
- Overall: effective integration of rose trees with stochastic block models and benefits over flat models, but novelty and clarity are undermined by missing hierarchical baselines, unclear technical derivations, and terminology issues.
