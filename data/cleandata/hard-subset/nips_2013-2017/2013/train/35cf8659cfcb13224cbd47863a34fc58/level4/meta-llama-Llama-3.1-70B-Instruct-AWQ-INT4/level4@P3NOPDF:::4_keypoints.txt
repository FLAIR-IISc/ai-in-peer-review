- Proposes an agglomerative hierarchical clustering for networks using a Bayesian rose tree prior with a modified stochastic block model likelihood.  
- Prior and algorithm structure largely reuse Bayesian rose tree methods from prior work [12]; main novelty is the network-adjusted likelihood.  
- Successfully adapts caching of sufficient statistics to maintain computational efficiency in the agglomerative algorithm.  
- Presentation is clear overall, with experiments showing improved performance over a flat community baseline.  
- Major limitation: lack of comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012 multiscale blockmodel, Roy et al. 2009 Mondrian Process).  
- Section 3 is dense; equation (9) and its components need simpler notation and clearer explanation.  
- Model can be viewed as extending Bayesian rose trees with a standard block-model likelihood; innovations are likelihood modifications and recursive statistic updates.  
- Likelihood computation is efficient (O(Nodes + Edges)), but the dynamic programming and recursive update description needs more detail.  
- Questionable use of the term "nonparametric," since rose trees can be framed as model selection rather than strictly nonparametric.  
- Qualitative experiment on Sampson’s data is well done and shows benefits over IRM by averaging hypotheses.  
- Scalability claims are not fully validated because experimental networks are not particularly large; larger-scale evaluation is needed.  
- Conclusion: method effectively integrates rose trees and block models for hierarchical community discovery but requires broader comparative evaluation to fully assess strengths and limitations.
