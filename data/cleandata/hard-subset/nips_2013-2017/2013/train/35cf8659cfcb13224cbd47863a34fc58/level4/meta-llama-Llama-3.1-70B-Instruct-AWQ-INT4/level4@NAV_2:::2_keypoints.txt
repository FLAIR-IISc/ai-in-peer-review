- Proposal: hierarchical generalization of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchies from link data.
- Algorithm: agglomerative clustering–based learning procedure that selects tree partitions representing valid clusterings.
- Key insight: restricting partitions to tree structure enables efficient marginal likelihood computation.
- Empirical performance: comparable or slightly better than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy.
- Speed advantage: reported runtime is orders of magnitude faster than IRM inference.
- Unclear inference relation: unclear whether the greedy learning procedure approximates posterior inference or yields a MAP/ML point estimate—needs clarification (Section 4).
- Writing quality: multiple spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing reduce clarity and should be corrected.
- Math presentation: some equations (e.g., Eq. 5) are obtuse or unnecessary and could be simplified or removed.
- Exposition: Section 4 (core contribution) requires more explanation and contextualization to be understandable.
- Experimental details: paper should provide more information about datasets and include intuitive visualizations of results.
- Strengths summary: novel Bayesian agglomerative clustering model, insightful learning algorithm, and strong experimental validation.
- Recommendation summary: paper merits publication after revisions addressing clarity, math simplification, and the learning vs. generative-model relationship.
