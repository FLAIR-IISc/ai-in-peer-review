- Presentation is strong: well-written, clear prose, correct grammar, proper citations; minor stylistic fix — avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.
- Theoretical exposition is concise but sometimes overly terse, e.g., excessive use of generic f() and g() notation.
- Contribution: extends SBMs by applying Bayesian Rose Trees to the single-table IRM, replacing the CRP formulation with a simpler expression (Eq. 7).
- The model’s factorization over subtrees is analogous to prior work (Koivisto & Sood 2004) and enables efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistics management, and link prediction.
- Experimental metric choice is unclear: reporting AUC instead of standard clustering metrics (e.g., NMI) requires justification and explicit methodology (likely link prediction held-out links).
- Experimental scale is limited: evaluations should include much larger datasets typical in recent SBM literature.
- Baseline comparisons are insufficient: authors compare only to Bayesian nonparametric methods and should add relevant semi-parametric approaches already cited in the paper.
- Overall assessment: contribution is a clear, straightforward agglomerative method with clean theory, but simplicity plus an insufficiently rigorous, mostly illustrative experimental evaluation weakens the paper’s demonstrated value.
