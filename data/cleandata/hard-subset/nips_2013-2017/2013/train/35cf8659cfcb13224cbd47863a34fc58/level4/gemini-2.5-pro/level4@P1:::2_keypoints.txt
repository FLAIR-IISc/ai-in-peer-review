- Introduces BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model extending stochastic block models to infer nested hierarchical community structure.  
- Uses a greedy agglomerative algorithm to build a tree that constrains partition space for computational efficiency.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones lets marginal likelihood be computed efficiently via dynamic programming.  
- Evaluated on two social networks, BHCD is ~100x faster than the IRM while matching or slightly improving predictive accuracy (AUC, log-likelihood, accuracy).  
- Addresses an important problem and will interest researchers applying Bayesian methods to network analysis.  
- Provides a useful hierarchical extension to popular network models, valuable for computational social science.  
- Main clarity concern: the connection between the greedy tree-building algorithm and the probabilistic model is not well explained.  
- Unclear whether the algorithm performs full posterior inference, finds a MAP/ML estimate, or is purely heuristic.  
- Prose occasionally vague (e.g., references to "many small changes" or "iterations" without specifics) and contains spelling/grammar errors.  
- Datasets (Sampson’s Monastery, NIPS co-authorship) are insufficiently described for readers unfamiliar with them.  
- Presentation of results could be improved—Figure 3 is hard to interpret without prior knowledge of the dataset.  
- Some math (e.g., Equation 5) needs clearer, more intuitive explanation.  
- Overall assessment: novel and efficient method with strong empirical speedups, but paper clarity—especially around inference interpretation—must be improved.
