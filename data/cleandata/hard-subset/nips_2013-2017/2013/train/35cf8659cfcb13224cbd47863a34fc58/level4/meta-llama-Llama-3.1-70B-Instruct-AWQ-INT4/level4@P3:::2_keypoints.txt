- Proposes a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces an efficient computation of marginal likelihood for the tree-structured model.  
- Develops a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm that scales quadratically in the number of vertices.  
- Problem is well-motivated and relevant to the NIPS audience.  
- Approach is novel and offers insightful contributions to hierarchical community detection.  
- Experimental results are impressive and demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Writing quality is uneven, with vague phrasing and distracting spelling/grammar errors.  
- Intro lacks clarity (e.g., lines 47–50 reference “local changes” and “iterations” without clear definitions).  
- Datasets used in experiments are under-described, making result significance hard to assess.  
- Some mathematical exposition is obtuse or unnecessary (e.g., Equation 5 could be simplified or removed).  
- Presentation of results is confusing (Figure 3 and the monastery dataset are hard to interpret for unfamiliar readers).  
- The relationship between the greedy learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model is unclear; greedy search is not full posterior inference.  
- Strengths: novel approach, scalable greedy algorithm, strong empirical performance.  
- Weaknesses: poor writing clarity, insufficient dataset context, overly complex math, weak result presentation, unclear inference interpretation.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant novel contribution with strong experimental support, fixable presentation issues.  
- Rejection arguments: current writing, dataset/context, and math clarity problems hinder accessibility and evaluation.
