- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of SBMs using a Bayesian Rose Trees prior that lets the marginal likelihood factorize and enables a greedy bottom-up agglomerative algorithm.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with elegant theoretical exposition and a clearly described algorithm.  
- Key contribution is a fast O(n^2 log n) greedy agglomerative algorithm whose complexity is independent of the number of communities, offering large practical speedups over IRM.  
- Theoretical foundations and derivations (recursive marginal likelihood, cached sufficient statistics) are sound and technically solid.  
- Evaluation relies on link prediction (AUC, predictive probability), which is an indirect measure of community recovery; clustering metrics like NMI and clearer protocol description are needed.  
- Empirical comparisons are largely restricted to IRM; broader benchmarking against other scalable probabilistic, semi-parametric, and non-probabilistic hierarchical methods is missing.  
- Main quantitative experiments use a small network (234 nodes); scalability claims should be validated on much larger datasets.  
- Current experiments feel illustrative of properties rather than a rigorous state-of-the-art comparative analysis.  
- Originality is mainly in the effective synthesis and implementation of existing techniques (SBMs, agglomerative clustering, tree priors) rather than a fundamentally new modeling paradigm.  
- Minor: rephrase sentences that begin with citation numbers to improve readability per NIPS style.  
- Overall: a fast, theoretically sound, and clearly presented method whose experimental evaluation should be expanded to fully substantiate its contributions.
