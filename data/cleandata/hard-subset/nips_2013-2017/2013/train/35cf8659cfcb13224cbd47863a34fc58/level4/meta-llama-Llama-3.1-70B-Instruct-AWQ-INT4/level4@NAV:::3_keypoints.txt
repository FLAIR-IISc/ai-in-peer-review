- Writing is excellent (spelling, grammar, citations) but avoid starting sentences with citations in NIPS style.  
- Theoretical presentation is concise and clear, consistently using notations like f() and g().  
- Paper studies SBMs and augments the single-table IRM using Bayesian Rose trees, replacing the CRP formulation with equation (7).  
- Factoring over subtrees connects to prior work (Koivisto & Sood 2004), allowing posterior factorization that supports greedy search, marginal likelihoods, sufficient statistics, and link prediction.  
- Evaluation methodology is unclear: SBMs are typically compared to gold-standard partitions with metrics like NMI, but the paper reports AUC without sufficient explanation.  
- Presumed AUC procedure is link deletion and prediction, which should be explicitly described.  
- Experimental scale is limited: recent SBM work uses datasets much larger than the NIPS dataset, so results on larger datasets are needed.  
- Comparisons to Bayesian nonparametric and semi-parametric methods are insufficient given the literature.  
- Main algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative method summarized in Table 1.  
- Overall contribution: clear theory and algorithms extending IRM via Bayesian Rose trees, but the idea is simple and the experiments are illustrative rather than sufficiently comparative.
