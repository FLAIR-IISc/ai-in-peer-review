- Introduces BHCD, a nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.
- Proposes a greedy agglomerative clustering algorithm that learns the hierarchy in a single bottom-up pass.
- Algorithm is computationally efficient (worst-case quadratic time, caching yields O(n² log n)) and independent of number of communities.
- Restricting partitions to tree-consistent sets enables exact, efficient marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on two social network datasets, matching or outperforming the IRM in accuracy.
- Shows orders-of-magnitude speedup over IRM; produces useful predictions before IRM completes a single MCMC iteration.
- Significant practical contribution by addressing scalability bottlenecks in Bayesian nonparametric network methods.
- Novel technical contribution in adapting Bayesian agglomerative clustering to relational data with an efficient caching scheme.
- Main weakness: unclear theoretical connection between the probabilistic model and the greedy learning algorithm.
- Unclear whether the greedy tree approximates MAP/ML or is a heuristic; needs explicit discussion of what the algorithm optimizes.
- Presentation issues: vague phrasing about why prior methods are slow and several minor grammatical/spelling errors.
- Missing dataset descriptions (size, sparsity, relationship types) and some dense mathematical notation that needs clearer intuition.
- Figure interpretation is weak—visualizations (e.g., Fig. 3) need guided comparison or quantitative metrics to show which hierarchies are better.
- Recommendation: accept pending revisions to clarify the inference algorithm’s grounding and improve prose, dataset/context, and figures.
