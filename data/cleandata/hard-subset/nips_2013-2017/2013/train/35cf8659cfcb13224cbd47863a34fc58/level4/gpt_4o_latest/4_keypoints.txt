- Proposes an agglomerative method combining Bayesian rose trees with a block-model likelihood to cluster networks into hierarchical communities.  
- Prior over trees and overall algorithm structure largely unchanged from earlier Bayesian rose trees [12].  
- Likelihood adapted straightforwardly for networks with added engineering for efficient caching of sufficient statistics.  
- Paper is clearly presented, but Section 3 (Equation (9) and subsequent derivations) is dense and could use more intuitive notation/explanation.  
- Main technical contribution is the recursive updating of sufficient statistics enabling O(Nodes + Edges) likelihood computation; the dynamic programming mention before Eq. (10) lacks detail and justification.  
- Equations for recursive updates are given but their correctness and derivation are not sufficiently explained.  
- Terminology concern: calling the method "nonparametric" is debatable and potentially misleading given prior discussion in Blundell et al. [12].  
- Experiments show favorable results versus a flat community baseline (IRM) and qualitative Sampson dataset results are adequate.  
- Major experimental shortcoming: no comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., Multiscale Community Blockmodel [Ho et al. 2012], Mondrian Process [Roy et al. 2009]).  
- Scalability is mentioned but tested networks are not particularly large, so claimed scalability is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Overall: effectively integrates rose trees with block-model likelihoods for hierarchical community discovery, but lack of comparisons to other hierarchical methods and limited explanatory detail weaken the paper.
