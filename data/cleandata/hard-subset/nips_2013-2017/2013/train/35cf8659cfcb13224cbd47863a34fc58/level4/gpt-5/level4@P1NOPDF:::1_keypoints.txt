- Introduces a hierarchical extension of the IRM using BRT ideas: hierarchy defines a mixture over node partitions and is learned by a greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Empirical results: qualitative on Sampson’s monks and full NIPS, quantitative on NIPS-234; inference is much cheaper than IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.  
- Presentation is very clear and well written.  
- Originality: adapts BRT concepts to relational data in a nontrivial way while preserving computational complexity.  
- Significance: important step toward scaling BNP network models; basic algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may limit very large networks, though the sparse variant could remain feasible.  
- Requests more timing experiments on larger real-world networks (e.g., how long for full NIPS) and discussion of scaling to gene/social networks with tens of thousands+ nodes.  
- Methodological concern: evaluate how close the greedy solution is to the optimal tree (compare at scales where exhaustive search is possible) and consider theoretical guarantees (e.g., via submodularity).  
- Minor corrections: fix “whilst … but” construction (Line 48), “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees,” and “A fruitful next steps” → “A fruitful next step” or “Fruitful next steps.”  
- Recommendation: acceptance.
