- Paper extends Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block models to infer tree-structured hierarchies instead of flat clusters.
- Proposes a greedy agglomerative algorithm that searches over tree-induced partitions and exploits tree constraints for efficient marginal likelihood computation.
- Experiments on two social networks show comparable or slightly better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy than the IRM, with orders-of-magnitude faster runtime.

- Strength: Problem is relevant and impactful; hierarchical structure is natural and a scalable Bayesian solution is valuable.
- Strength: Technical insight that restricting to tree-induced partitions enables tractable marginal likelihood computation.
- Strength: Empirical results demonstrate similar or improved accuracy with dramatically reduced computation time.

- Weakness: Unclear relationship between the greedy algorithm and the model’s generative/posterior inference; objective being optimized is not specified.
- Weakness: Section 4 (agglomerative algorithm and scoring) is hard to follow and needs systematic presentation.
- Weakness: Presentation has spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”).
- Weakness: Dataset descriptions for monastery and NIPS authorship are insufficient for readers unfamiliar with them.
- Weakness: Some mathematical derivations (e.g., Eq. 5) are overly dense and obscure intuition.
- Weakness: Figure 3 is difficult to interpret; legends and captions are not sufficiently informative.

- Clarification requested: Precisely state the objective optimized by the agglomerative procedure (exact marginal likelihood, bound, or heuristic) and any guarantees.
- Clarification requested: Provide search details—initialization, merge criteria, stopping conditions, tie-breaking, and any backtracking.
- Clarification requested: Give a precise complexity analysis per merge and per dataset, plus matched-hardware wall-clock times and IRM implementation details for fair comparison.
- Clarification requested: Report sensitivity to hyperparameters and provide a brief sensitivity analysis.
- Clarification requested: Clarify evaluation protocol—train/test splits, link prediction setup, and whether results are averaged over multiple splits.

- Suggestion: Rewrite Section 4 with step-by-step description, pseudocode, and a precise definition of the merge scoring function tied to the model.
- Suggestion: Move lengthy derivations to an appendix and add high-level intuition in the main text.
- Suggestion: Define terminology early and use it consistently throughout the paper.
- Suggestion: Improve dataset summaries (nodes, edges, directed/undirected, density, prediction task) and consider adding a small well-known dataset.
- Suggestion: Redo Figure 3 to be self-contained with larger fonts, clearer legends, visual cues for best scores, and numeric summaries in captions.
- Suggestion: Thorough proofreading to remove typos and grammar issues.

- Overall evaluation: Novel and practical hierarchical extension with strong runtime advantages; main barrier is clarity—make algorithm/model relationship explicit and tighten exposition for acceptance.
