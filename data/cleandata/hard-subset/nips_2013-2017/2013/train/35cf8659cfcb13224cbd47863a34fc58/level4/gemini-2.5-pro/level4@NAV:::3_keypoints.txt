- Manuscript is very clear with strong writing, good grammar, and accurate citations.
- Minor stylistic suggestion: avoid sentences that begin directly with a citation (NIPS convention).
- Theoretical exposition is concise and consistent, e.g., using general notations like f() and g().
- Main technical contribution: extends single-table IRM for SBMs by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees.
- Replaces CRP formulation with a more direct expression (eq. 7) and factors over subtrees.
- Builds on prior work (Koivisto & Sood 2004) for factoring and constrained structure selection.
- Posterior factorization enables greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, sufficient-statistic management, and link prediction.
- Evaluation protocol is unclear: AUC is reported but the link-prediction/edge-holdout setup needs explicit explanation.
- Experimental scale is limited (NIPS corpus); larger-scale SBM benchmarks are recommended.
- Baseline comparisons are narrow; should include stronger semi-parametric and other non-Bayesian SBM methods.
- Core algorithm (novel agglomerative method) is well-presented (Table 1); theory is sound and implementation straightforward.
- Primary weakness: experimental validation is illustrative rather than rigorously comparative.
