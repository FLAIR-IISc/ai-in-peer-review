- Writing quality is high: well-written with correct spelling, grammar, and citations.
- Theoretical presentation is concise and uses consistent notation, but avoid beginning sentences with citations per NIPS style.
- Main contribution: extends single-table IRM by integrating Bayesian Rose tree theory, replacing the CRP formulation with the simpler Equation (7).
- Subtree factorization mirrors Bayesian network structure learning (Koivisto & Sood 2004), allowing posterior factorization under constrained structures.
- This factorization enables greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, reuse of sufficient statistics, and link prediction.
- Evaluation metric choice requires clarification: you report AUC but standard SBM evaluation uses gold-standard partitions and metrics like NMI; explain the AUC setup (e.g., edge deletion/recovery).
- Dataset scale is insufficient: recent SBM work uses larger datasets than the full NIPS dataset—evaluation should include larger datasets.
- Baseline comparisons are incomplete: restricting to Bayesian non-parametric methods is insufficient; include semi-parametric and other relevant methods cited in the paper.
- Algorithmic contribution is the agglomerative approach (summarized in Table 1); theory is clear and motivated, but the core methods are relatively simple.
- Experiments are illustrative rather than strongly comparative, making the empirical evaluation inadequate for a strong paper.
