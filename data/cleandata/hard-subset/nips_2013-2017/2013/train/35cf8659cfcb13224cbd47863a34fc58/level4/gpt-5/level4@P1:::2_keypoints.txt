- Summary: Proposes a tree-structured Bayesian nonparametric stochastic blockmodel that restricts partitions to tree-consistent ones, enabling efficient marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming; uses a greedy agglomerative tree search and is evaluated on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship networks, showing large speedups and comparable/better predictive performance than the IRM.

- Key insight/prior work relation: Restricting partitions to a tree allows tractable, exact marginal likelihood evaluation, contrasting with the IRM’s exponential partition space.

- Algorithmic contribution: Greedy agglomerative search with cached sufficient statistics yields O(n^2 log n) worst-case runtime independent of the number of communities.

- Empirical quality: Shows competitive AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy versus the IRM, with orders-of-magnitude speed improvements.

- Main concern about methodology: The greedy tree-learning procedure’s relationship to the Bayesian generative model is unclear—uncertain whether it performs posterior inference, approximates a MAP tree, or optimizes a model-selection criterion.

- Requested clarification: Section 4 should explicitly state the objective optimized by the greedy search and whether it approximates posterior inference or a MAP/ML solution.

- Clarity issues: Paper needs grammar/style revision, clearer prose (e.g., define “local changes” and “iterations”), and lighter mathematical presentation in places.

- Dataset/figure problems: Datasets (Sampson, NIPS) are insufficiently described and Figure 3 is hard to interpret without prior familiarity; clearer labeling and brief dataset summaries are needed.

- Exposition difficulty: Section 4 is hard to follow and would benefit from more intuitive explanation and a small running example.

- Originality: Novel combination of a tree-structured prior over partitions with blockmodel likelihood and an efficient agglomerative search; well-positioned relative to IRM and hierarchical extensions.

- Significance: Learning hierarchical community structure with much greater computational efficiency is valuable and practically useful.

- Strength: Tree-constrained partitions enabling efficient marginal likelihood computation is a clear, useful insight.

- Strength: The proposed algorithm is practical and scalable with provable O(n^2 log n) runtime.

- Strength: Strong empirical results demonstrating comparable or better performance than IRM at much lower runtime.

- Weakness: Unclear theoretical/practical connection between the greedy search and the Bayesian model undermines principled interpretation.

- Weakness: Writing, presentation, and heavy math hinder readability.

- Weakness: Poor dataset descriptions and figure interpretability for readers unfamiliar with datasets.

- Weakness: Core algorithmic section (Section 4) is difficult to parse and needs clearer exposition and examples.

- Recommendation: Revise Section 4 for clarity, explain the search–Bayesian objective connection, fix writing issues, and improve dataset/figure descriptions to merit publication.

- Overall assessment: Novel and efficient Bayesian model for hierarchical communities with strong empirical performance but requires substantial improvements in exposition and clarity.
