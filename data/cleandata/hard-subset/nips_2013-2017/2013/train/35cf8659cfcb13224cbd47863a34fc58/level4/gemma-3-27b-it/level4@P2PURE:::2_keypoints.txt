- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer entity hierarchies from link data.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm that produces a tree of possible partitions.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social networks shows comparable or improved accuracy versus the IRM with much faster runtimes.  
- Addresses a relevant problem: combines Bayesian nonparametrics and scalable inference for social network analysis.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to a tree enables efficient marginal likelihood computation compared to IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Relationship between the tree-learning procedure and the underlying Bayesian generative model is unclear; need to clarify whether the greedy algorithm performs posterior inference or a point estimate (MAP/ML).  
- Reported improvements in AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy are modest, but runtime gains are substantial.  
- Writing quality needs improvement: multiple spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing hinder readability.  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficiently detailed for unfamiliar readers.  
- Some mathematical expressions (e.g., Eq. 5) seem unnecessarily complex; Figure 3 is confusing without Monastery dataset context and a more common dataset might help.  
- Section 4 (core contribution) is hard to follow and requires clearer exposition.  
- Overall: novel and convincing approach with publishable results pending revisions to clarity and methodological explanation.
