- Introduces a Bayesian nonparametric, tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery (BHCD).
- Proposes an efficient greedy agglomerative algorithm to learn tree-consistent community structure directly from data.
- Restricting structures to trees enables tractable marginal likelihood computation, avoiding exponential partition complexity.
- Demonstrates comparable or better accuracy than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) on synthetic and real datasets.
- Shows substantial runtime improvements over IRM, sometimes by two orders of magnitude.
- Validated on Sampson’s monastery network, a NIPS co-authorship subset, and the full NIPS dataset, revealing meaningful hierarchies.
- Well-motivated problem with a strong potential audience in machine learning, Bayesian statistics, and network science.
- Grounded in existing work (stochastic blockmodels, IRM, Bayesian hierarchical clustering) and offers a novel framing.
- Unclear connection between the greedy algorithm and the Bayesian generative model—needs clarification on whether it approximates posterior inference or is heuristic.
- Presentation suffers from awkward phrasing, minor grammatical errors, and dense sections that need clearer language and organization.
- Some technical elements (e.g., Eq. 5) are overly concise and require more explanation for readability.
- Figure 3 and some visualizations are hard to interpret without prior dataset knowledge; clearer figures or comparisons to ground truth are needed.
- Recommendation: accept contingent on revisions to improve clarity and provide stronger justification of the greedy learning procedure.
