- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels using greedy agglomerative model selection as a probabilistic alternative to the IRM.  
- Claims favorable scaling (quadratic in vertices, independent of number of communities) and comparable or superior accuracy to IRM with much faster runtime.  
- Leverages Bayesian hierarchical clustering ideas and a tree prior to enable efficient marginal likelihood computation, connecting to Koivisto & Sood (2004).  

Strengths:
- Technical soundness: model is well-defined and derivations are logical, with dynamic programming for likelihood as a key optimization.  
- Efficiency: reported O(n^2 log n) complexity and empirical ~100x speedup over IRM.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written with consistent notation and concise likelihood representation.  
- Novelty: novel combination of Bayesian Rose trees with stochastic blockmodels and efficient agglomerative inference.  
- Motivation: introduction effectively motivates hierarchical community detection and limitations of prior methods.  

Weaknesses:
- Limited evaluation: experiments use small datasets (e.g., 234-node NIPS co-authorship) and need larger, more complex networks.  
- Unclear link-prediction methodology: AUC evaluation lacks explanation of how links are removed and reinserted.  
- Missing comparisons to semi-parametric alternatives, limiting understanding of relative strengths.  
- No analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (α, β, δ, λ, γ).  
- Minor writing issue: sentence beginning with a citation should be rewritten.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Addresses an important problem, offers substantial computational improvement, has solid theoretical foundation, and shows promising empirical results.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Empirical validation is not comprehensive; unclear link-prediction and hyperparameter analyses weaken empirical claims.  

Overall assessment:
- Solid, promising contribution that would be significantly strengthened by addressing the empirical and methodological clarifications.  

Recommendation (accept with minor revisions):
- Expand experiments to larger datasets and broader comparisons.  
- Provide a detailed explanation of the link-prediction evaluation procedure.  
- Analyze sensitivity to hyperparameter settings.  
- Fix the sentence that starts with a citation.
