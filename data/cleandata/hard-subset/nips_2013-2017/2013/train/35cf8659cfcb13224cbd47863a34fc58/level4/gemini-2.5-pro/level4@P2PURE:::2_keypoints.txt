- Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer entity hierarchies from relational data rather than flat clusters.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering-based learning algorithm that selects a tree to constrain partition space.  
- Claims the tree constraint enables efficient marginal likelihood computation, avoiding the exponential partition space of the IRM.  
- Empirical results on two social connectivity corpora show comparable or slightly better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy than the IRM.  
- Primary empirical advantage is computational: reported orders-of-magnitude speedup over IRM inference.  
- Paper addresses an important problem (scalability of BNP methods and hierarchical network analysis) and is timely for the community.  
- Unclear connection between the greedy agglomerative tree-learning procedure and the generative model or principled posterior inference (uncertain whether it yields a MAP/ML estimate).  
- Improvements in predictive metrics are modest in absolute terms despite being consistent.  
- Major weaknesses in clarity and writing quality, with distracting spelling/grammar errors.  
- Specific presentation issues: vague prose (e.g., terms like "local changes"/"iterations"), insufficient dataset descriptions, and obtuse mathematical notation (e.g., Eq. 5).  
- Visualization problems: Figure 3 is confusing for unfamiliar readers; choice of datasets or visuals should be improved.  
- Overall judgment: novel and empirically strong approach, but publication contingent on substantially improving exposition and clarifying the inference procedure.
