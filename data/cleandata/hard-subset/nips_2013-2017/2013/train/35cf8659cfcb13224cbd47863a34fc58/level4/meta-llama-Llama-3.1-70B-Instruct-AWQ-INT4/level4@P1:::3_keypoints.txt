- Introduces BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic blockmodel using Bayesian Rose trees to find hierarchical community structure.  
- Equation (7) provides a simpler and more efficient formulation of the model.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, succinct theory and correct citations.  
- Theory uses functions f() and g() effectively and builds on prior work (IRM/CRP and Bayesian Rose trees).  
- Main contribution is an agglomerative greedy algorithm summarized in Table 1 with favorable time complexity.  
- Empirical results (Figure 4) are promising but evaluation is limited.  
- Evaluation compares mainly to other Bayesian nonparametric methods, lacking semi-parametric and larger-dataset comparisons.  
- Use of AUC as an evaluation metric is unclear and needs better explanation for stochastic blockmodels.  
- Strengths: novel approach, simple/efficient formulation, clear theory/algorithms, promising results.  
- Weaknesses: limited evaluation scope and unclear AUC usage.  
- Arguments for acceptance emphasize novelty, simplicity, clarity, and empirical promise.  
- Arguments against acceptance emphasize insufficient evaluation breadth and unclear metric justification.
