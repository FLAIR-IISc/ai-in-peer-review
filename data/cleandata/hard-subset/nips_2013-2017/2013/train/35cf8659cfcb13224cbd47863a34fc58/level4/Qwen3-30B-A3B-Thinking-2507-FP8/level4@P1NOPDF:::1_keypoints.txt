- Paper introduces a hierarchical extension of the IRM using Bayesian rose tree ideas to specify a mixture over consistent node partitions and learns the hierarchy with a greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Demonstrates qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and full NIPS datasets, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Proposed inference is computationally cheaper than IRM while achieving comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains core ideas.  
- Adaptation of rose-tree concepts to relational data required significant effort to maintain computational efficiency.  
- Scaling BNP models to networks is important, but the O(n^2) algorithm may limit use on very large networks; sparse variant could be feasible.  
- Requests timing data on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS dataset and much larger gene/social networks) to assess practical scalability.  
- Asks evaluation of how close the greedy method is to the optimal tree, suggesting experiments where exhaustive search is tractable.  
- Requests theoretical justification (e.g., submodularity guarantees) for the greedy method.  
- Minor corrections: replace "whilst" with "while"; fix "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees"; change "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step."  
- Overall judgment: adaptation to relational data is a meaningful contribution and the paper merits acceptance.
