- Problem is timely and significant: hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension for network clustering is valuable and relevant to the NIPS community.
- Novelty: generalizes Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block models to infer hierarchies rather than flat clusterings.
- Main technical insight: restricting partitions to those consistent with a tree enables efficient marginal likelihood computation compared to the IRM’s exponential partition space.
- Algorithmic concern: the greedy agglomerative tree-learning procedure’s relationship to the model’s generative story is unclear—uncertain if it performs true posterior inference or a MAP/ML estimate.
- Requested clarification: Section 4 must state precisely what objective is optimized, how it connects to the Bayesian model, and in what sense the procedure approximates posterior inference.
- Empirical performance: the method is comparable to or slightly better than IRM on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy.
- Practical advantage: reported runtimes are orders of magnitude faster than IRM, a compelling benefit despite modest absolute score gains.
- Experimental description issues: datasets (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficiently described for unfamiliar readers.
- Writing and presentation problems: spelling/grammar errors, vague prose (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”), and opaque mathematics (e.g., Eq. 5).
- Figure and exposition issues: Figure 3 is confusing and the core Section 4 required multiple readings—both need clearer explanation or revision.
- Overall conclusion: with fixes to clarity, Section 4, dataset/figure descriptions, and writing, the novel model and strong empirical results would warrant publication.
