- Proposes agglomerative clustering of networks into hierarchical communities by combining Bayesian rose trees with an SBM-like likelihood.
- Prior over trees and algorithm structure are essentially unchanged from prior Bayesian rose tree work [12].
- Likelihood is straightforwardly modified for networks and allows efficient caching of sufficient statistics.
- Computation of the likelihood is claimed O(Nodes + Edges), a notable scalability feature.
- Presentation is generally clear but Section 3 (especially equation (9) and subsequent derivations) is dense and could use simpler notation/explanation.
- The dynamic programming and details of the O(Nodes + Edges) computation (allusion before eq. (10)) are not clearly explained.
- Recursive updates of sufficient statistics are given by equations but lack clear justification or explanation of correctness.
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable given prior framing of rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric inference.
- Experiments compare favorably to a flat community baseline (IRM) and qualitatively on the Sampson network, illustrating benefits of hierarchical modeling and averaging.
- Major limitation: experiments do not compare to existing hierarchical network models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012 nCRP, Roy et al. 2009 hierarchical K-d tree process) and thus do not show superiority over other hierarchical approaches.
- Scalability claims are discussed but tested networks are not particularly large.
