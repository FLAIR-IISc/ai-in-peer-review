- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model using Bayesian rose tree concepts to define a mixture over consistent node partitions with a greedy agglomerative learning procedure.
- Presents qualitative results on Sampson's monks and the full NIPS dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.
- Inference is computationally cheaper than the IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear and concise exposition.
- Originality: adapts Bayesian rose tree ideas to relational data and preserves computational complexity.
- Significance: scaling BNP network models is important; n^2 scaling may be prohibitive but a sparse variant could be feasible.
- Missing empirical timings for larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS processing time not reported) and unclear scalability to very large networks (gene or social networks).
- Suggests evaluating how close the greedy method is to the optimal tree (compare to exhaustive search at the largest feasible scale) and providing theoretical support (e.g., submodularity).
- Minor corrections: replace "whilst" with "but", change "pair of trees" to "pair of tree", and rephrase "A fruitful next steps".
- Overall recommendation: contribution is significant and the paper should be accepted.
