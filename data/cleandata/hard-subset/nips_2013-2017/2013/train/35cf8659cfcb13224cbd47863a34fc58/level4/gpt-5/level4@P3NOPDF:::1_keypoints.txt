- Summary: Proposes a hierarchical IRM using Bayesian rose tree ideas, learned via greedy agglomeration, achieving similar predictive performance to IRM at substantially lower inference cost; shown on Sampson’s monks, NIPS coauthorship, and NIPS-234.

- Quality & Clarity: Well written with clear exposition, succinct BRT connection, and accessible description of the agglomerative procedure; figures and experiments support claims.

- Originality: Nontrivial adaptation of BRT to relational data, preserving computational complexity and translating mixture-over-partitions to IRM likelihoods.

- Significance: Addresses scalability of Bayesian nonparametrics for networks; sparse variant and empirical results suggest practical tractability gains.

- Strengths: Principled tree-induced partition mixture, efficient greedy inference with large speedups, solid empirical evidence on standard datasets, and good exposition.

- Weakness: Scalability evidence is limited—need more timing/memory on larger and denser real-world networks (absolute runtimes for full NIPS unclear).

- Weakness: Basic algorithm’s O(n^2) complexity may be prohibitive; sparse implementation assumptions and empirical scaling need clarification (e.g., bounded average degree).

- Weakness: No quantification of greedy tree optimality—need small-scale exhaustive comparisons to assess how close greedy gets to optimal.

- Weakness: Lack of theoretical support—no guarantees or (approximate) submodularity results to justify greedy merges.

- Weakness: Evaluation details missing—specify predictive metrics, hyperparameter tuning, and uncertainty handling when comparing models.

- Suggestion: Add timing/memory plots across dataset sizes and sparsity, and report parallelization details.

- Suggestion: Evaluate on a larger real-world sparse network and report runtime on full NIPS to demonstrate broader applicability.

- Suggestion: Include a small-scale study comparing greedy trees to optimal or near-optimal solutions.

- Suggestion: Provide theoretical insights (e.g., monotonicity, sufficient conditions for greedy optimality, or bounds under likelihood assumptions).

- Suggestion: Give practical guidance on when to prefer the hierarchical model, hyperparameter handling, and sensitivity to initialization/merge criteria.

- Minor corrections: Fix phrasing “whilst ... but”, “pair of trees”, and “A fruitful next step”.

- Overall assessment: Strong, timely paper adapting BRT to networks with meaningful computational gains and competitive accuracy; recommend acceptance pending more empirical scaling and brief theoretical discussion.
