- Proposes a scalable agglomerative clustering method for hierarchical network communities based on Bayesian rose trees.  
- Main contribution is adapting the likelihood to network data and developing efficient caching of sufficient statistics.  
- Paper is generally well-written and technically sound, but Section 3 (around Eq. 9) is dense and hard to follow.  
- Time complexity claim O(|V| + |E|) for likelihood computation is important but the description and dynamic-programming details are unclear.  
- Recursive updates for sufficient statistics are provided but lack intuition and clear derivation for correctness.  
- Experiments show advantages over a non-hierarchical baseline (IRM) and reasonable qualitative results on the Sampson dataset.  
- Major experimental limitation: no comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., nested CRP multiscale blockmodel, Mondrian Process).  
- Scalability claim is not fully supported because experiments use only modestly sized networks.  
- Originality is limited: method is essentially applying Bayesian rose trees with a standard stochastic block-model likelihood.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable given prior characterization of Bayesian rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric Bayesian.  
- Overall, integrates prior work effectively and demonstrates hierarchical discovery but is weakened by presentation issues and insufficient benchmarking against hierarchical alternatives.
