- Proposes agglomerative clustering of networks into hierarchical communities by combining Bayesian rose trees with a block-model likelihood.  
- Prior over (possibly non-binary) trees and algorithm structure largely unchanged from Bayesian rose trees [12].  
- Likelihood adapted straightforwardly for networks, with additional work to keep sufficient statistics cacheable and updates efficient.  
- Likelihood computation runs in O(nodes + edges), which is a notable scalability claim.  
- Presentation is clear overall but Section 3 and Equation (9)/(10) are dense and lack simpler notation or explanation.  
- Recursive sufficient-statistic update equations are given but insufficiently explained to verify correctness.  
- Experiments show favorable results vs. a flat model (e.g., IRM) and a qualitative demo on Sampson, demonstrating benefits of hierarchical modeling and hypothesis averaging.  
- Major empirical gap: no comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012 nested CRP, Roy et al. 2009 Mondrian process).  
- Terminology concern: calling the approach "nonparametric" is questionable given prior framing by Blundell et al. [12].  
- Scalability claims are discussed but tested networks are not particularly large.  
- Overall: successfully integrates rose trees with stochastic block models to enable hierarchical community discovery, but novelty is limited to likelihood-driven sufficient-statistic updates and empirical comparisons to other hierarchical methods are missing.
