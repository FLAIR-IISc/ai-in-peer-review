- Introduces Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community detection.
- Key technical contribution: an efficient greedy agglomerative algorithm that builds the hierarchy in a single bottom-up pass.
- Restricts partitions to tree-consistent ones to enable exact marginal likelihood computation via dynamic programming.
- Presents an efficient caching scheme and algorithmic derivation that appear technically sound.
- Empirically much faster than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM), claiming up to two orders of magnitude speed-up on NIPS co-authorship data.
- Achieves comparable or superior predictive performance (accuracy, log predictive probability, AUC) to IRM while being significantly faster.
- Addresses a relevant problem and offers a valuable computational alternative to sampling-based Bayesian nonparametrics.
- The greedy agglomerative procedure is not full posterior inference; the paper should clarify the objective being optimized (MAP, ML, or heuristic) and implications.
- Manuscript clarity needs improvement: several typos and ambiguous phrasing that require proofreading and clearer terminology (e.g., "small changes," "iterations").
- Experimental datasets (e.g., Sampson's Monastery, NIPS co-authorship) are not described and should include brief descriptions of nodes and edges.
- Some mathematical definitions (e.g., Equation 5) lack intuitive explanation and would benefit from accompanying textual intuition.
- Figure 3 is hard to interpret for readers unfamiliar with the dataset; visualizations should be clarified or better explained.
- Section 4 (algorithmic details) is dense; expand the description of algorithm flow, merge types, and update steps (e.g., Eq. 13) for accessibility.
- Overall assessment: novel and efficient approach with strong empirical results; recommend acceptance pending improvements to exposition and clarity.
