- Introduces an agglomerative hierarchical clustering algorithm for SBMs by using Bayesian Rose Trees to extend the Infinite Relational Model, enabling a direct calculation (Eq.7) instead of CRP.  
- Key technical insight: posterior factorizes over subtrees, enabling a greedy search, efficient sufficient-statistic handling, and marginal likelihood computation.  
- Strength: very clear, well-written presentation with concise notation and correct citations.  
- Strength: elegant theoretical formulation connecting IRMs and Bayesian Rose Trees, grounded in prior work (e.g., Koivisto & Sood 2004); Table 1 effectively summarizes the algorithm.  
- Weakness: experimental metric (AUC) is unclear and unconventional for SBMs; methodology for computing AUC must be explicitly described or replaced with standard clustering metrics (e.g., NMI).  
- Weakness: experiments are small-scale (e.g., NIPS co-authorship) and do not demonstrate scalability to contemporary, larger benchmark graphs.  
- Weakness: limited baselines—comparison mostly to Bayesian nonparametric methods; should include stronger semi-parametric and spectral SBM inference methods.  
- Overall: theoretically sound and elegant, but empirical evaluation (metric clarity, dataset scale, baseline breadth) is insufficient to support practical claims.  
- Minor style: avoid starting sentences with citations to follow conference formatting conventions.
