- Proposes an efficient agglomerative method for discovering hierarchical communities by adapting Bayesian rose trees to relational data with a stochastic blockmodel likelihood.  
- Key innovation is maintaining efficient recursive updates of sufficient statistics during greedy agglomerative clustering for scalable inference.  
- Likelihood computation has favorable O(Nodes + Edges) time complexity.  
- Overall presentation is clear, but Section 3’s notation is dense and could be simplified.  
- Explanation of the dynamic programming and correctness of the recursive sufficient-statistic updates is insufficient and needs more detail.  
- The use of “nonparametric” terminology is questionable; the rose tree approach may be better seen as model selection.  
- Experiments show improved performance over a flat IRM baseline and provide a qualitative case study on Sampson’s monastery network.  
- Scalability claims are limited by the fact that tested networks are not particularly large.  
- Major shortcoming: missing comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., nested CRP multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and well written, effectively integrates rose trees with SBM and generalizes efficient agglomerative clustering.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, efficient inference, demonstrated improvement over flat community models.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of comparison to hierarchical baselines, incomplete explanation of dynamic programming/updates, and questionable terminology.
