- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD), a Bayesian nonparametric method for hierarchical community detection.  
- Builds on stochastic blockmodels (SBMs) and the Infinite Relational Model (IRM), modeling communities as a tree-structured hierarchical mixture of SBMs.  
- Introduces an efficient greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm with O(n² log n) runtime.  
- Provides a theoretical foundation including marginal likelihood computations via dynamic programming and a principled link-prediction approach.  
- Empirically achieves comparable or better accuracy than IRM while being roughly two orders of magnitude faster.  
- Experimental evaluation includes Sampson’s monastery, a NIPS-234 coauthorship subset, and the full NIPS network (2,864 vertices).  
- Uses AUC and predictive probability for evaluation but gives insufficient explanation of how AUC relates to hierarchical community detection.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized; abstraction via f(·) and g(·) and a clear complexity comparison (Table 1) improve readability.  
- Original contribution: novel combination of Bayesian hierarchical clustering with SBMs and a tree prior, but needs clearer differentiation from the Mondrian process and other Bayesian hierarchical methods.  
- Significance: addresses scalable hierarchical community discovery for realistic network sizes; computational efficiency is a practical advantage.  
- Main weaknesses: limited experimental scope (lack of recent semi-parametric baselines), insufficient metric explanation, and incomplete contextualization vs related work.  
- Key strengths: solid theoretical development, efficient inference algorithm, demonstrated computational advantage, and practical utility for large-scale networks.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify evaluation metrics and relation to related Bayesian methods.
