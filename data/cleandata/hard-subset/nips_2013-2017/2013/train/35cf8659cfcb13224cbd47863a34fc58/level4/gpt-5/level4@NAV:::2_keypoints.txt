- Paper introduces a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model to infer hierarchies instead of flat clusters.
- Authors propose an agglomerative-clustering-based learning procedure to select a tree encoding valid partitions.
- Empirical results on two social networks show the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).
- Problem is interesting and likely to appeal to the NIPS audience; hierarchical extension is relevant to social scientists using Bayesian models.
- Restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones enables efficient marginal likelihood computation compared to IRM’s exponential partition space.
- Unclear how the greedy tree-learning relates to the Bayesian generative model—does not appear to perform true posterior inference or yield a MAP/ML estimate.
- Recommend improving clarity of Section 4 to explain the learning procedure’s relation to the model.
- Results are strong across AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, with runtime improvements being orders of magnitude faster than IRM.
- Writing quality needs improvement: spelling/grammar errors, vague prose, and some mathematical parts feel opaque or unnecessary (e.g., Eq. 5).
- Datasets (Monastery networks and NIPS authorship) are not clearly described and Figure 3 is confusing for readers unfamiliar with the data.
- If clarity and presentation issues are addressed, the approach is novel and results are strong enough to warrant publication.
