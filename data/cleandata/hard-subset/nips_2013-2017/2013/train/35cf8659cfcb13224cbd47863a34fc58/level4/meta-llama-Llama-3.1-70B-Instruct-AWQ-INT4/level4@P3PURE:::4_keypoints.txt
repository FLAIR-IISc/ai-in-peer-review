- Proposes an agglomerative method for scalable hierarchical community detection in networks.  
- Prior over trees and algorithm largely unchanged from Bayesian rose trees [12].  
- Likelihood adapted for network data; sufficient statistics can still be cached efficiently.  
- Presentation is clear overall but Section 3 is dense and Equation (9) and its buildup are hard to follow.  
- Experiments only compare to flat community models, so benefits shown are limited to using hierarchy vs. non-hierarchy.  
- Missing comparisons and discussion of other hierarchical network models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012 multiscale blockmodel, Roy et al. 2009 Mondrian process).  
- Model is essentially a Bayesian rose tree combined with a standard block-model likelihood; novelty is mainly in updating sufficient statistics for this likelihood.  
- Likelihood computation in O(Nodes + Edges) is a useful computational property.  
- Description of the dynamic programming / recursive updates (around Eq. (10)) is unclear and lacks justification.  
- Questionable use of the term "nonparametric" given prior work framing rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric.  
- Qualitative Sampson results and IRM comparison are informative, but scalability claims are weak because tested networks are not very large.  
- Overall: effective integration of rose trees with stochastic blockmodels and promising results, but insufficient comparisons to other hierarchical methods.
