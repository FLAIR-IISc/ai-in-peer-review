- Paper proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchical entity structure from links instead of flat clustering.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm that selects a tree of valid partitions.  
- Key innovation: restricts allowed partitions to tree-conforming ones, enabling efficient marginal likelihood computation versus IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Empirical results on two social datasets show the method outperforms the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) in speed and often in accuracy (AUC, predictive log-likelihood, accuracy).  
- The problem is timely and relevant to the NIPS audience, bridging Bayesian nonparametrics and practical scalability for social scientists.  
- Concern: unclear relationship between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the model’s generative/posterior inference — is it true posterior inference or a point estimate?  
- Section 4 (core contribution) needs clarification; current description is hard to follow and may require reworking.  
- Results are strong but absolute asymptotic improvements are modest; the major benefit is substantially faster runtime than IRM.  
- Writing and clarity problems: spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing in the introduction, and obtuse mathematical notation (e.g., Eq. 5).  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) and Figure 3 are unclear or confusing; consider more familiar datasets or added context.  
- Overall assessment: novel model and insightful algorithm with strong empirical results, but publication should require revisions to improve clarity and address methodological exposition.
