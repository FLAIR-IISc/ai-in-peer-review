- Proposal: Hierarchical extension of the IRM using Bayesian rose tree ideas—mixture over consistent node partitions and greedy agglomerative hierarchy learning.  
- Empirical evaluation: Qualitative results on Sampson's monks and full NIPS, quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Performance: Inference is more computationally efficient than IRM while achieving comparable predictive performance.  
- Writing: Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear exposition.  
- Originality: Builds on Bayesian rose tree foundations, but adapting to relational data and preserving computational complexity is a nontrivial, significant effort.  
- Significance: Advances scalability of Bayesian nonparametric network models, important for practitioner adoption.  
- Scaling concerns: Base algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may limit very large networks; sparse variant may be feasible—need timings on larger real-world datasets (full NIPS, gene and social networks).  
- Greedy approximation: Suggest evaluating how close the greedy method is to the optimal tree (e.g., exhaustive search at tractable scale) and investigating theoretical guarantees (e.g., submodularity).  
- Minor corrections: "whilst" → "while"; "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees"; "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step".  
- Recommendation: Contribution is significant and paper should be accepted.
