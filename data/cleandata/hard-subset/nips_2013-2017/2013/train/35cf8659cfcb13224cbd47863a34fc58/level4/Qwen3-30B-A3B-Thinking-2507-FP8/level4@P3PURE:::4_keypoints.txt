- Proposes an agglomerative method for scalable hierarchical community detection by combining Bayesian rose trees with a block-model likelihood for networks.  
- Model structure and prior largely mirror prior Bayesian rose tree work; likelihood is a straightforward network adaptation.  
- Sufficient statistics can be cached efficiently with minor modifications, enabling O(Nodes + Edges) likelihood computation.  
- Presentation is clear overall, but Section 3 (especially Equation (9)) is dense and needs simpler notation or explanation.  
- The dynamic programming/allusion before Equation (10) and the recursive sufficient-statistic equations lack detailed explanation and intuitive justification.  
- Experiments show favorable results versus a flat community baseline (e.g., IRM) and a qualitative Sampson case study.  
- Comparisons to established hierarchical network models are missing, which is a critical omission.  
- Relevant hierarchical baselines (e.g., nested Chinese Restaurant Process multiscale blockmodels and Mondrian-process hierarchical models) are not cited or compared.  
- Scalability claims are discussed but not validated on particularly large networks in the experiments.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable since rose trees have been framed as model selection rather than nonparametric approaches.  
- Overall: solid integration of rose trees and stochastic block models enabling effective hierarchical clustering, but the lack of hierarchical baselines and clearer algorithmic exposition are major weaknesses.
