- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels with greedy agglomerative model selection.  
- Combines Bayesian rose-tree ideas with stochastic blockmodels and an efficient marginal likelihood computation.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound with clear model and algorithm presentation.  
- Uses conjugate priors and dynamic programming to compute tree marginal likelihood (DP details underexplained).  
- Experiments on datasets (Sampson’s monastery, NIPS co-authorship) show effectiveness and include held-out evaluations and IRM comparison.  
- Demonstrates scalability: greedy agglomerative algorithm runs in O(n^2 log n).  
- Introduces BHCD sparse variant for more efficiency on sparse networks.  
- Lacks comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian process).  
- Dynamic programming method is not explained in sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Questionable use of the term “nonparametric” — not clearly justified as meeting traditional nonparametric definitions.  
- Innovation is incremental: mainly a novel combination of prior work rather than wholly new theory.  
- Overall makes a significant contribution to hierarchical community detection but needs more comparative evaluation and algorithmic detail.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid presentation, empirical effectiveness, and scalability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing model comparisons, insufficient DP explanation, questionable nonparametric claim, and need for further evaluation.
