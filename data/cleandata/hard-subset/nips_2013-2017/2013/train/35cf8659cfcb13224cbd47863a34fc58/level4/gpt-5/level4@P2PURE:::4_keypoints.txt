- Proposes agglomerative clustering of networks into hierarchical communities by adapting Bayesian rose trees to a network likelihood.  
- Prior and algorithmic structure largely unchanged from Bayesian rose trees [12]; likelihood modified to handle networks.  
- Sufficient statistics can be cached efficiently; likelihood computed in O(nodes + edges) time.  
- Presentation is clear overall but Section 3 (equation (9) and subsequent parts) is dense and hard to follow.  
- Dynamic-programming/allusion before (10) and recursive sufficient-statistic updates are described unclearly with limited justification.  
- Experiments show benefits of modeling hierarchy and compare favorably to a flat IRM baseline (qualitative Sampson results are fine).  
- Major missing comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., nested CRP / Multiscale Community Blockmodel, Mondrian process).  
- Scalability discussion is limited and tested networks are not especially large.  
- Questionable use of the term “nonparametric,” given prior framing of rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric.  
- Overall, capably integrates prior work and generalizes agglomerative clustering to networks, but lacks comparison to related hierarchical methods.
