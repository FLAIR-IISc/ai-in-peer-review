- Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model to infer a hierarchy instead of a flat clustering.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm that selects a tree of valid partitions.  
- Empirical results on two social connectivity datasets show the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Addresses a relevant problem for the NIPS audience, combining scalable Bayesian methods with network discovery.  
- Key insight: constraining partitions to a tree enables efficient marginal likelihood computation versus the IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Unclear linkage between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model—needs clarification on whether it performs posterior inference or yields a point estimate.  
- Experimental gains are notable in runtime (orders of magnitude faster) and competitive on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, though absolute score improvements are modest.  
- Writing and clarity need improvement: spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing detract from readability.  
- Methodological exposition weaknesses: insufficient dataset descriptions, overly complex notation (e.g., Eq. 5), confusing Figure 3, and Section 4 requires clearer presentation.  
- Overall assessment: novel and strong enough for publication if the clarity and methodological-explanation issues are addressed.
