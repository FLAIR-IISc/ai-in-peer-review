- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the IRM for networks using Bayesian rose tree ideas to define mixtures over consistent partitions.
- Uses an efficient greedy agglomerative procedure to learn the hierarchy, adapted for relational data while preserving favorable computational complexity.
- Demonstrates qualitative results on Sampson’s monastery and full NIPS dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234 co-authorship subset.
- Inference is significantly faster than the IRM and achieves comparable predictive performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear and concise exposition of model and algorithm.
- Novelty comes from nontrivial adaptation of Bayesian rose tree methods to relational/network settings.
- Addresses an important scalability challenge for Bayesian nonparametrics on network data.
- Concern: basic algorithm scales O(n^2), which may limit applicability to very large networks; sparse variant is promising.
- Suggestion: report timing on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS) and on much larger networks (gene/social networks).
- Suggestion: evaluate greedy algorithm quality versus optimal tree (where feasible) and provide theoretical justification (e.g., submodularity arguments).
- Overall recommendation: accept — contribution is significant despite familiar underlying ideas.
- Minor corrections: wording fixes (“whilst… but”, “pair of treeS”→“pair of trees”, “A fruitful next steps”→“A fruitful next step”).
