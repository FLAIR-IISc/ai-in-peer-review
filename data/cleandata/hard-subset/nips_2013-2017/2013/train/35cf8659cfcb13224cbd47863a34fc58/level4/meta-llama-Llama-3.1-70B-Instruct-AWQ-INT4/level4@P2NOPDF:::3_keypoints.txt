- Presentation is strong: well-written, good spelling/grammar, correct citations; avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.
- Theory is succinct with clear, consistent notation (e.g., f() and g()).
- Extends SBMs/IRM using Bayesian Rose trees, replacing the CRP with equation (7).
- Factoring over subtrees parallels prior work (Koivisto & Sood 2004) and lets the posterior factorize neatly.
- Factorization supports greedy search, marginal likelihoods, sufficient statistics handling, link prediction, etc.
- Evaluation choice is questionable: AUC for SBMs is unconventional and the deleted-link prediction setup needs clearer explanation.
- Experiments are small-scale (Figure 4); should include larger datasets for recent SBM benchmarks.
- Comparisons are limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; semi-parametric methods should also be included.
- Main contribution is the agglomerative algorithm (Table 1); method and theory are clear but relatively simple.
- Experimental work is illustrative rather than comparative and is insufficient for a thorough evaluation.
