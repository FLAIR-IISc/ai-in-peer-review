- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the IRM that applies BRT ideas: the hierarchy induces a mixture over consistent partitions and is learned via a greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Provides qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and the full NIPS dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Inference is computationally much cheaper than the IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.  
- Paper is very well written with clear exposition.  
- Originality: adapting BRT ideas to relational data while preserving computational complexity required nontrivial work.  
- Significance: scaling BNP models for network data is important; the work advances practicability.  
- Concern: basic algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may be prohibitive for very large networks; sparse variant may help but needs validation.  
- Request to report runtimes on larger real-world networks (e.g., how long does full NIPS take compared to NIPS-234?) and discuss scaling to gene/social networks with tens of thousands of nodes.  
- Suggest evaluating how close the greedy method is to the optimal tree (test on sizes where exhaustive search is feasible) and provide theoretical support (e.g., submodularity-based guarantees).  
- Minor corrections: several typos/phrasing fixes (e.g., “whilst ... but”, “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees”, “A fruitful next steps” → “A fruitful next step”).  
- Overall recommendation: contribution is significant and the paper should be accepted.
