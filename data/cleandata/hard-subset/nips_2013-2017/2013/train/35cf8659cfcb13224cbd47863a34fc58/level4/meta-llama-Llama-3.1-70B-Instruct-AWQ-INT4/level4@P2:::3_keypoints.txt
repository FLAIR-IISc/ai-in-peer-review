- Proposes an efficient Bayesian nonparametric model: a tree-structured mixture of (potentially exponentially many) stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Introduces greedy agglomerative model selection algorithms that learn a fully probabilistic hierarchical model in a single pass through the data.  
- Extends the single-table IRM using Bayesian Rose trees, replacing the CRP formulation with a simpler equation (7) and enabling posterior factorization over subtrees.  
- Theory leverages ideas from Bayesian network structure learning for clean factorization of constrained structures.  
- Paper is well written with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations.  
- Presentation is succinct in places, using compact notation (f(), g()) for clarity.  
- Minor style issue: should avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.  
- Evaluation concern: authors report AUC for SBM without clear justification; reviewer expects clustering metrics like NMI against gold-standard partitions.  
- Experimental work is illustrative rather than adequately comparative.  
- Needs comparisons to published semi-parametric methods, not just Bayesian nonparametric baselines.  
- Main contribution is the agglomerative algorithm (summarized in Table 1).  
- Significance: provides a new, efficient, scalable method for discovering hierarchical community structure.  
- Originality: novel combination of IRM and Bayesian Rose trees plus a new agglomerative approach.  
- Quality: strong theory and writing, but empirical evaluation is weak.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient method that is well-presented and scalable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparative experiments and unclear SBM evaluation methodology.
