- Presentation is strong: well-written, good spelling/grammar/citations; avoid starting sentences with citations (NIPS style).
- Paper extends stochastic block models / single-table IRM by integrating Bayesian Rose trees, replacing the CRP formulation with a simpler equation (7).
- Factoring over subtrees is reminiscent of Koivisto & Sood (2004); posterior factorization enables efficient greedy search, marginal likelihoods, handling sufficient statistics, and link prediction.
- Main algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative method (summarized in Table 1); theory is clear and the algorithm is simple and effective.
- Evaluation metric choice (AUC) is nonstandard for SBM—readers expect NMI and other clustering metrics—so the link-deletion/guessing protocol needs clearer explanation.
- Experiments use relatively small data (full NIPS dataset); evaluations should include larger datasets used in recent SBM work.
- Comparisons are limited: good start with Bayesian nonparametric baselines, but many semi-parametric methods should also be included.
- Overall experiments are illustrative rather than comparative; more comprehensive evaluations and comparisons are needed to establish significance.
