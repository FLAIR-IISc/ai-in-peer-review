- Proposes an efficient agglomerative method for discovering hierarchical community structure by adapting Bayesian rose trees with a stochastic blockmodel likelihood.  
- Maintains efficient recursive updates of sufficient statistics during greedy clustering, claiming O(Nodes + Edges) time complexity for likelihood calculations.  
- Overall presentation is clear, but notation and explanations (especially around equation (9)) are dense and could be simplified.  
- Experiments show favorable performance versus a flat-community baseline and qualitative results on Sampson’s monastery network.  
- Lacks comparisons to other hierarchical network models, limiting contextualization of the contribution.  
- Omits discussion of relevant Bayesian nonparametric hierarchical methods (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel [18], Mondrian Process [19]).  
- The “nonparametric” label is questionable; rose trees are better framed as model selection rather than truly nonparametric.  
- The explanation of the claimed efficiency (dynamic programming allusion before Eq. (10)) is underdeveloped and needs more detail.  
- Recursive-update equations are given but their correctness and rationale are not thoroughly justified.  
- Experiments do not demonstrate scalability to very large networks; datasets used are not especially large.  
- Comparison to the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) effectively shows benefits of modeling hierarchy for predictive performance.  
- Primary novelty is adapting Bayesian rose trees to relational data and deriving efficient recursive statistic updates for greedy agglomeration.  
- Paper is technically sound and well written, with useful algorithmic efficiency and an extension of hierarchical clustering techniques to networks.  
- Major weaknesses: missing comparisons to competing hierarchical models, unclear efficiency derivation, potentially misleading terminology, and limited experimental scale.  
- Acceptance arguments: sound technical approach, efficient algorithm, effective empirical improvement over flat baselines, extension of hierarchical clustering to relational data.  
- Rejection arguments: absence of hierarchical-model comparisons, unclear description of efficiency, terminology concerns, and insufficient demonstration of scalability.
