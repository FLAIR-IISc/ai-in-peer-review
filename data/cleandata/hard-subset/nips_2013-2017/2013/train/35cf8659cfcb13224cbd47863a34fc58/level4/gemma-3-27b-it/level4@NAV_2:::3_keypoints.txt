- Well written with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations; avoid beginning sentences with citations per NIPS style.
- Theory presentation is succinct and consistent, e.g., consistent use of f() and g() notation.
- Extends stochastic block models / IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees to introduce hierarchy.
- Replaces the CRP formulation of the IRM with a simpler formulation (equation 7).
- Posterior factorization over subtrees follows prior work (Koivisto & Sood), enabling efficient greedy search and marginal likelihood computation.
- Handles sufficient statistics for tasks like link prediction through the subtree factoring.
- Evaluation uses AUC but the application of AUC to SBM/link prediction is unclear and needs clarification.
- Typical SBM evaluations use NMI against known partitions; deletion-and-prediction for AUC should be described if used.
- Experimental evaluation is small-scale (Figure 4) and should be complemented with larger datasets.
- Comparison is limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; semi-parametric baselines should be included.
- Primary algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative approach, well summarized in Table 1.
- Methods and algorithms are clearly presented but are relatively straightforward and incremental.
- Arguments for acceptance: sound technical contribution, clear explanation, and notable computational efficiency.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope, lack of AUC clarification, and incremental advance rather than a breakthrough.
