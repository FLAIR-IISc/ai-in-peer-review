- Proposes a Bayesian nonparametric model: tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery.
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm for efficient inference.
- Achieves large runtime speedups (≈ two orders of magnitude) over the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).
- Maintains comparable or slightly better predictive accuracy than IRM on benchmarks.
- Addresses an important, broadly relevant problem in network/community analysis for the NIPS audience.
- Extends greedy hierarchical clustering ideas to relational data in a novel way.
- Key insight: using a tree structure to constrain partition space enables efficient marginal likelihood computation.
- The link between the greedy learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model/posterior inference is underexplained and needs clarification.
- Section 4 should better articulate whether the algorithm corresponds to MAP/ML estimation or another form of model selection.
- Experimental evaluation is compelling, including results on Sampson’s monastery and the NIPS co-authorship datasets.
- Predictive performance gains are modest in absolute terms, though runtime improvements are substantial.
- Presentation and writing quality need improvement (grammatical/typographical errors and occasionally imprecise prose).
- Some explanations (e.g., “local changes”/“iterations”) require more specific detail for clarity.
- Mathematical formulations (e.g., Equation 5) could be better motivated or simplified.
- Figure 3 is visually dense and hard to interpret without prior knowledge of the dataset.
- Overall novelty and empirical results justify publication, contingent on revisions to improve clarity.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel Bayesian nonparametric approach, major computational speedup, competitive performance, and relevance to network analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear connection between algorithm and Bayesian inference, writing/presentation issues, and some complex math/figures needing simplification.
