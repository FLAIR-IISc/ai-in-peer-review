- Proposes an efficient agglomerative method adapting Bayesian rose trees with a stochastic blockmodel likelihood for hierarchical community discovery.  
- Core algorithm updates sufficient statistics recursively and claims O(Nodes + Edges) likelihood computation.  
- Presentation is generally clear but Section 3 (esp. equation (9) and subsequent derivations) is dense and could use simpler notation/explanation.  
- The brief allusion to dynamic programming before equation (10) lacks sufficient detail for full understanding of the efficient computation.  
- Main novelty is applying rose trees to relational data and modifying recursive updates; the correctness rationale for these updates needs clearer justification.  
- The use of the term “nonparametric” is debatable and should acknowledge prior framing of rose trees as model selection rather than truly nonparametric.  
- Experiments show favorable performance versus a flat-community baseline (IRM) and qualitative success on Sampson’s monastery network.  
- Paper lacks comparisons to other hierarchical network models (e.g., nested CRP multiscale blockmodel, Mondrian Process), weakening its positioning.  
- Scalability claims are discussed but evaluated on relatively small networks; larger-scale performance is not demonstrated.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-written overall.  
- Efficient likelihood computation is a notable technical contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, efficient hierarchical algorithm, improved predictive performance vs IRM, relevance to network analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing hierarchical baselines in experiments, insufficient detail on efficient computation, potentially misleading “nonparametric” terminology, limited experiment scale.
