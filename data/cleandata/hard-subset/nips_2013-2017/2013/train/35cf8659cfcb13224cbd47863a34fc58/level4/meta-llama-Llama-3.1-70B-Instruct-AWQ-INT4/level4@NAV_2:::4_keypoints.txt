- Proposes a novel method for hierarchical community discovery in networks using a Bayesian nonparametric-style model based on Bayesian rose trees adapted for network data.
- Extends prior Bayesian rose tree work by modifying the likelihood for networks and caching sufficient statistics for efficiency.
- Presentation is generally clear but Section 3 is dense; equations (9) onward need simpler notation and clearer explanation.
- Using a tree to define a prior on partitions allowing exact marginal likelihood evaluation is a notable contribution.
- Likelihood computation runs in O(Nodes + Edges) and recursive sufficient-statistics updates are an important innovation, though their description needs more justification.
- Experiments show favorable results against a flat IRM baseline and qualitative analysis on Sampson’s monastery network; averaging over hypotheses improves predictions.
- Comparisons to other hierarchical network models are limited, weakening empirical claims.
- Paper omits discussion and comparison to other hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric models (e.g., multiscale community blockmodel, Mondrian Process).
- Terminology is questionable: labeling the method "nonparametric" may be misleading since it resembles model selection per prior rose-tree work.
- Scalability discussion is limited and tested networks are not large, leaving performance on large graphs uncertain.
- Overall technically sound and original in adapting rose trees to networks, but requires clearer exposition, stronger empirical comparisons, and better justification of algorithmic choices.
