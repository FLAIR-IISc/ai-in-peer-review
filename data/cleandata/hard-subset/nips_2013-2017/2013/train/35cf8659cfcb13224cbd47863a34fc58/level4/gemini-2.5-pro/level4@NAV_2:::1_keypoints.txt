- Introduces BHCD, a Bayesian nonparametric model that discovers hierarchical community structure by modeling the hierarchy as a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels.
- Proposes an efficient greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm that learns the hierarchy in a single bottom-up pass.
- Demonstrates runtime improvements of about two orders of magnitude over IRM inference while achieving comparable or better predictive accuracy on benchmarks.
- Significance: advances scalability of Bayesian nonparametric methods for large networks, making hierarchical modeling more practical.
- Quality: solid technical adaptation of Bayesian hierarchical clustering to relational data, with a clever caching scheme and sound empirical evaluation (Sampson, NIPS-234).
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written and explains model, likelihoods, and inference clearly.
- Originality: notable novel adaptation of Bayesian rose-tree ideas to community discovery in networks, maintaining computational efficiency.
- Scalability concern: dense algorithm has O(n^2 log n) complexity; more empirical analysis on full NIPS and much larger, sparse networks is needed to clarify practical limits.
- Greedy optimality concern: greedy merges may yield local optima; suggest experiments on small synthetic networks with known optimal trees and theoretical discussion (e.g., submodularity) of the merge criterion.
- Minor corrections: wording fixes—“whilst... but” redundancy (p.1), “pair of tree” → “pair of trees” (Alg.1), and “A fruitful next steps” → “A fruitful next step” (Sec.6).
- Conclusion: strong, elegant, and efficient contribution to network analysis; convincing results and presentation make a strong case for acceptance.
