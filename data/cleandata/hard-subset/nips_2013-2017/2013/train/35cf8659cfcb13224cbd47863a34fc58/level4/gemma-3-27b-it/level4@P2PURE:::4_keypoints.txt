- Proposes an agglomerative method for scalable hierarchical network clustering, building on Bayesian rose trees.  
- Prior over (potentially non-binary) trees and overall algorithmic structure largely follow Blundell et al. [12].  
- Adopts a stochastic block model likelihood for network data and enables efficient caching of sufficient statistics.  
- Claims likelihood computation in O(Nodes + Edges) time, a notable computational advantage.  
- Presentation is generally clear but Section 3 is dense; equation (9) and subsequent derivations need simpler notation and explanation.  
- Description of the dynamic programming and recursive updating of sufficient statistics is unclear and lacks justification for correctness.  
- Experiments show favorable performance versus a flat community model (IRM) and qualitative results on the Sampson network are satisfactory.  
- Major omission: no comparisons to existing hierarchical network models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012 multiscale blockmodel, Roy et al. 2009 Mondrian process).  
- Because comparisons are limited to non-hierarchical baselines, it's unclear if the method outperforms other hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric approaches.  
- Use of the term “nonparametric” is questionable; rose tree framing may be better viewed as model selection, not nonparametric inference.  
- Scalability is claimed but tested networks are not particularly large, limiting empirical validation of scalability.  
- Overall: integrates rose tree priors with SBM likelihoods and generalizes efficient agglomerative clustering, but lacks clear exposition of key derivations and comparisons to related hierarchical methods.
