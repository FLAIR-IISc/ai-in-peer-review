- Excellent writing, spelling, grammar, and citation practices; avoid beginning sentences with citations per NIPS style.  
- Theory is concise and readable, aided by consistent notation (f(), g()), though occasionally terse.  
- Main contribution: extends the IRM using Bayesian Rose Trees to replace the CRP formulation with a simpler expression (Eq. 7).  
- Factorized posterior over subtrees parallels past Bayesian network results and allows reuse of sufficient statistics and efficient marginal likelihood computation.  
- Enables efficient greedy/agglomerative search; the agglomerative algorithm is the principal, well-presented contribution (Table 1).  
- Practical implications include straightforward procedures for link prediction due to the factorization.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: uses AUC in a nonstandard, unclearly specified protocol (train/test splits, negative sampling, class imbalance, uncertainty).  
- Experiments are small-scale (NIPS example) and lack larger benchmarks to demonstrate scalability and robustness.  
- Comparisons are limited to Bayesian nonparametric baselines and omit strong semi-parametric and other competitive methods.  
- Should report standard clustering metrics (NMI, ARI) when ground truth is available, in addition to link-prediction metrics (AUC/AP).  
- Recommended improvements: fully specify link-prediction protocol (holdout strategy, negatives, trials, CIs), add large SBM benchmarks, include stronger baselines, and report NMI/ARI.  
- Overall: clear, elegant agglomerative method with solid theory, but empirical validation is illustrative rather than sufficiently comparative.
