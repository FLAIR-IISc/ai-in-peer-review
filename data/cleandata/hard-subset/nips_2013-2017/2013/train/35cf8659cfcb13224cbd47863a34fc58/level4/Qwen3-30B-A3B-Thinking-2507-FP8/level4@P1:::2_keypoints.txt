- Proposes BHCD: a tree-structured mixture of stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community detection.  
- Extends Bayesian nonparametrics (builds on IRM) to the hierarchical setting by restricting partitions to tree-consistent ones.  
- Key technical insight: tree restriction enables exact marginal likelihood computation in O(n + m) instead of exponential time.  
- Introduces a greedy agglomerative model-selection algorithm (Algorithm 1) that merges by Bayes factor with O(n^2 log n) runtime.  
- Empirical results show comparable accuracy to IRM but roughly two orders of magnitude faster (e.g., NIPS-234: ~10s vs ~1000s).  
- Presents a sparse BHCD variant that avoids unnecessary computation on disconnected components, useful for large sparse networks.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and clear connections to prior work on stochastic blockmodels and hierarchical clustering.  
- Thorough computational complexity analysis and clear comparisons to existing methods (Table 1).  
- Well-designed experiments on Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship datasets.  
- Major weakness: unclear relationship between the greedy learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model (not true posterior/MAP inference).  
- Section 4 (core algorithm) is difficult to follow and needs reorganization and clarification.  
- Writing quality problems: grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and some unnecessary mathematical detail that obscures the main contribution.  
- Dataset descriptions lack sufficient context for readers unfamiliar with the domains (e.g., monastery dataset).  
- Recommended revisions: clarify greedy vs Bayesian relationship, improve Section 4 clarity, add dataset context, and fix writing.  
- Overall: technically valuable, empirically strong, and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
