- Proposes scalable agglomerative clustering for hierarchical communities by adapting Bayesian rose trees to network data.  
- Main technical contribution is modifying the likelihood for networks, enabling efficient caching of sufficient statistics.  
- Claims likelihood computation runs in O(nodes + edges) time.  
- Presentation is clear overall, but Section 3 is dense and Equation (9) and its development need simplification.  
- Description of the dynamic programming (before Eq. 10) and details/intuition for recursive sufficient-statistic updates are unclear.  
- Experimental results favor the model over a flat community baseline (IRM) and show benefits of averaging hypotheses.  
- Experiments use small datasets (e.g., Sampson monastery), so scalability claims are not empirically substantiated.  
- Major weakness: no comparisons to other hierarchical network models, so superiority over state-of-the-art hierarchical methods is unestablished.  
- Overlooks relevant hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric models (e.g., Multiscale community blockmodel / nested CRP, Mondrian process).  
- Originality is limited: essentially a Bayesian rose tree combined with a standard stochastic block-model likelihood.  
- Questionable use of the term "nonparametric" given prior framing of rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric.  
- Overall: technically sound integration of prior methods with good qualitative results, but lack of hierarchical baselines and implementation clarity significantly weaken the paper.
