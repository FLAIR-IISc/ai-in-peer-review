- Proposes a hierarchical generalization of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchical structure from observed links.
- Develops a greedy agglomerative-clustering learning algorithm that selects a tree of valid partitions.
- Demonstrates empirical results on two social connectivity corpora showing the method is faster and at least as accurate as the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).
- Argues the work is timely and relevant: Bayesian nonparametrics are useful but often slow, and a hierarchical extension is valuable for social-science network analysis.
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to a tree enables efficient marginal likelihood computation versus IRM’s exponential partition space.
- Concern that the greedy tree-learning procedure’s relationship to the Bayesian generative model is unclear; it does not appear to perform true posterior inference or a MAP/ML search.
- Empirical performance: comparable or slightly better than IRM on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy, with orders-of-magnitude faster runtime.
- Writing and clarity issues: spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”), and obtuse or unnecessary math (e.g., Eq. 5).
- Presentation problems: datasets (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) and Figure 3 are not clearly described or accessible to unfamiliar readers.
- Overall verdict: novel and insightful approach with strong results that merit publication if clarity, exposition, and methodological explanations (Section 4) are improved.
