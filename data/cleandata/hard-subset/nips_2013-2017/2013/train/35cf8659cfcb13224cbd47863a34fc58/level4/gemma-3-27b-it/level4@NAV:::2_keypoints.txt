- Presents a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric extension of the stochastic block model to uncover entity hierarchies from network data.
- Uses an agglomerative clustering algorithm to explore partitions represented as a tree structure.
- Leverages the tree to enable efficient marginal likelihood computation, avoiding the IRM’s exponential partition space.
- Empirical evaluation on two social networks shows comparable or improved accuracy (AUC, predictive log-likelihood, accuracy) versus IRM.
- Demonstrates substantially faster runtime than IRM, a significant practical advantage.
- Key innovation: tree structure enabling efficient inference/marginal likelihood computation.
- Concern: unclear connection between the tree-learning procedure and the underlying Bayesian generative model.
- Concern: greedy agglomerative algorithm raises questions about whether it performs true posterior inference or approximates MAP—Section 4 needs clarification.
- Presentation issues: numerous grammatical/spelling errors and vague wording (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”) that impede comprehension.
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficient for readers unfamiliar with them.
- Some math (e.g., Eq. 5) is overly complex and Figure 3 is hard to interpret; consider clearer exposition or more familiar datasets.
- Overall judgment: novel and promising method; recommend publication pending revisions to clarify methods and improve presentation.
