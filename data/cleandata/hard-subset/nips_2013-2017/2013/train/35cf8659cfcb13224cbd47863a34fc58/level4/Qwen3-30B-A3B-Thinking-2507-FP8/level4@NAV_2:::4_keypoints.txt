- Introduces BHCD: a greedy agglomerative method adapting rose trees to stochastic blockmodels for hierarchical community discovery with O(n² log n) runtime and accuracy comparable to IRM.  
- Technical contributions are sound; dynamic programming marginal likelihood derivation (Eq.10) is correctly derived.  
- Paper omits critical comparisons to hierarchical Bayesian models (Mondrian Process, Ho et al.’s hierarchical blockmodel) and only compares to flat baselines.  
- Scalability claims are weakly supported because the largest tested dataset (NIPS-234, 234 nodes) is too small to validate "large dense networks."  
- Exposition is generally clear but Section 3 (Eqs.9–10) is dense; recursive updates (Eq.13) are clear but would benefit from a concrete example.  
- Algorithm description is precise and correctly implements the dynamic programming scheme.  
- Some reported results (e.g., log-likelihoods for BHCD variants) lack context for interpretation.  
- Novelty: efficient integration of tree priors with stochastic blockmodels and a caching mechanism enabling O(1) inner-loop operations is a meaningful contribution.  
- Novelty concerns: understates similarity to prior hierarchical methods and mislabels the approach as "Bayesian nonparametric" despite greedy model selection.  
- Significance: computational efficiency and predictive performance on NIPS-234 are promising, but importance is limited without hierarchical-baseline comparisons and rigorous evaluation of discovered hierarchies.  
- Strengths: clear adaptation of rose trees to network data, strong runtime improvements over IRM, precise algorithm, and handling of sparse networks.  
- Weaknesses: missing comparisons to hierarchical baselines, misleading terminology, limited dataset scale for scalability claims, and underdeveloped evidence that hierarchies improve over flat models.  
- Recommendation: technically sound but borderline for acceptance; requires additional comparisons to Mondrian Process and Ho et al., and clearer distinction from nonparametric methods to strengthen the paper.
