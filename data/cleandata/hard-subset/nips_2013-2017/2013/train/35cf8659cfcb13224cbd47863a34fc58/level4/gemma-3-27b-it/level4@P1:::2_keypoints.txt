- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchies from observed links.
- Uses an agglomerative clustering-based learning algorithm to select a tree representing valid partitions.
- Restricting partitions to a tree enables efficient marginal likelihood computation compared to IRM’s combinatorial space.
- Reviewer unclear whether the greedy learning procedure performs full posterior inference or returns a point estimate (MAP/ML); Section 4 needs clarification.
- Empirical results on two social-network corpora (Sampson’s monastery, NIPS authorship) show comparable or slightly better AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy than IRM.
- Claimed runtime is orders of magnitude faster than IRM inference.
- Theoretical insight and relevance to Bayesian nonparametrics and social-network analysis are compelling.
- Writing and presentation require improvement: grammar/spelling errors, vague phrasing, and dense or unnecessary math (e.g., Eq.5).
- Figures and dataset descriptions are confusing (e.g., Figure 3, unclear description of datasets), reducing accessibility.
- Section 4 (core contribution) is hard to follow and needs clearer exposition connecting the algorithm to the generative model.
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, substantial speed improvement, solid empirical performance, and a strong theoretical idea.
- Rejection concerns: clarity/notation issues, unclear relation between greedy learning and Bayesian inference, and unclear figures.
