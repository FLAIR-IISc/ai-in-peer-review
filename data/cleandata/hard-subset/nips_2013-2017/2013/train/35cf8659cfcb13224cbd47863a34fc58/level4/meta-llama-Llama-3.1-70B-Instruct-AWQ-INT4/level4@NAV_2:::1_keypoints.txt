- Proposes Bayesian Hierarchical Community Discovery (BHCD): a hierarchical extension of the IRM using Bayesian rose-tree ideas (hierarchy defines a mixture over consistent partitions; greedy agglomerative learning).  
- Reports qualitative results on Sampson's monks and the full NIPS co-authorship dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Inference is much cheaper computationally than the IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and good relation to prior work.  
- Extends Bayesian greedy hierarchical clustering approaches to relational/network data.  
- Strength: scales Bayesian nonparametric models to larger networks—an important practical advance.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness empirically across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical support/analysis (e.g., no submodularity or other guarantees).  
- Weakness: inadequate reporting of computational complexity and runtimes for large networks; sparse variant mentioned but timings not provided.  
- Open question: how close does the greedy algorithm get to the optimal tree?  
- Open question: can any theoretical guarantees be provided for the greedy procedure?  
- Open question: how does inference scale to very large networks (tens of thousands to millions of nodes)?  
- Minor corrections: fix wording "whilst... but", "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees", and "A fruitful next steps".  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution to Bayesian nonparametrics for networks; cheaper than IRM with similar performance; empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacking theory; insufficient complexity/scale analysis; greedy method may not find optimal tree.  
- Overall verdict: ideas echo Bayesian rose-tree work but contribution is meaningful and the paper should be accepted.
