This paper proposes an efficient Bayesian nonparametric model for discovering hierarchical community structure in social networks. The authors extend the stochastic blockmodel by incorporating the theory of Bayesian Rose trees, allowing for a more efficient and scalable approach to community detection. The paper is well-written, with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations.

The theory presented in the paper is succinct and well-explained, particularly in the use of f() and g() throughout the paper. The authors draw on existing work on learning Bayesian network structures, such as Koivisto and Sood's 2004 JMLR paper, to develop their approach. The factoring over subtrees is a key contribution, enabling greedy search, marginal likelihoods, handling of sufficient statistics, link prediction, and other aspects of the model to be neatly addressed.

The evaluation of the model is primarily done using the NIPS co-authorship dataset, with a focus on predictive performance. However, the evaluation could be strengthened by including more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, including semi-parametric approaches. The use of AUC as an evaluation metric is not fully explained, and it would be helpful to clarify how this was used, particularly for those familiar with NMI evaluations for stochastic block models.

The contribution of the paper lies in the development of an agglomerative method for community detection, which is efficiently scalable to larger networks. Table 1 effectively summarizes the advantages of the proposed approach over other Bayesian nonparametric methods.

The experimental work is primarily illustrative, demonstrating the effectiveness of the proposed approach on a few datasets. However, a more comprehensive evaluation on larger datasets would be beneficial to fully assess the model's performance.

Strengths:

* Well-written paper with clear theory and algorithms
* Efficient and scalable approach to community detection
* Effective use of Bayesian Rose trees to extend the stochastic blockmodel

Weaknesses:

* Limited evaluation compared to other state-of-the-art methods
* AUC evaluation metric not fully explained
* Experimental work primarily illustrative, rather than comprehensive

Suggestions for improvement:

* Include more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, including semi-parametric approaches
* Clarify the use of AUC as an evaluation metric
* Evaluate the model on larger datasets to fully assess its performance
* Consider including more detailed analysis of the results, such as the impact of hyperparameter settings on performance.
