- Exceptionally well-written paper with impeccable spelling, grammar, and citation formatting; avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.  
- The theoretical framework is concise and uses clear notation (e.g., f() and g()).  
- Main technical advance: integrates stochastic block models (SBM) with Bayesian Rose trees, replacing the CRP IRM formulation with equation (7).  
- The subtree factorization aligns with prior work (Koivisto & Sood 2004) and enables greedy search, marginal likelihoods, sufficient-statistics handling, and link prediction.  
- Evaluation critique: using AUC is unconventional for SBM (which typically uses NMI against gold partitions); the link-deletion/prediction methodology needs clear explanation.  
- Dataset critique: experiments are small-scale (NIPS dataset); add larger datasets used in recent SBM work.  
- Baselines critique: comparisons are limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; include relevant semi-parametric and other published baselines.  
- Contribution summary: primary novelty is the agglomerative algorithm (Table 1); theory and algorithms are clear but relatively simple, and experiments are more illustrative than comprehensive.
