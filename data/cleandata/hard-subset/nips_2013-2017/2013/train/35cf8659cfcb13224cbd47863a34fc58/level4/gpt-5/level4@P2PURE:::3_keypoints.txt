- Writing quality is excellent (spelling, grammar, citations); follow NIPS style by avoiding sentence-initial citations.  
- Theory is concise with consistent notation (e.g., use of f() and g()).  
- Paper extends stochastic block models by replacing the IRM’s CRP with Bayesian Rose Trees (Eq. 7).  
- The factorization over subtrees parallels Koivisto & Sood (2004) and enables clean handling of greedy search, marginal likelihoods, sufficient statistics, and link prediction.  
- Evaluation metric choice is unclear: SBMs are typically evaluated with clustering metrics like NMI, but the paper reports AUC—make the link-prediction protocol explicit.  
- Dataset size is small (only the NIPS dataset); include results on larger datasets used in recent SBM evaluations.  
- Baselines are insufficient: compare not only to Bayesian nonparametric methods but also to established semiparametric methods cited in the paper.  
- The main contribution is the agglomerative procedure, well summarized in Table 1.  
- Overall, the paper presents clear theory and algorithms extending IRM with Bayesian Rose Trees, but the ideas are relatively simple and the experiments are illustrative rather than sufficiently comparative.
