- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) for network modeling.
- Adapts ideas from Bayesian rose trees to define a hierarchy that mixtures over consistent node partitions.
- Learns the hierarchy with an efficient greedy agglomerative procedure.
- Shows qualitative results on Sampson's monks and full NIPS datasets, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.
- Inference is computationally much cheaper than standard IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains key ideas.
- Originality: non-trivial adaptation of Bayesian rose tree ideas to relational data, with efforts to preserve computational efficiency.
- Significance concerns: basic algorithm scales O(n^2), which may be prohibitive for large networks; sparse version is promising but scalability should be demonstrated.
- Requests: report runtime on full NIPS and discuss scaling to much larger real-world networks (e.g., gene or social networks).
- Requests further analysis of the greedy method’s approximation quality, including exhaustive-search comparisons on small datasets and possible theoretical support (e.g., submodularity).
- Minor corrections: awkward phrasing "whilst... but", "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees", and "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step" or "Fruitful next steps".
- Recommendation: paper is significant despite drawing on prior work and should be accepted.
