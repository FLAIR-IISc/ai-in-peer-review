- Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchies instead of flat clusters from network links.  
- Addresses an important and timely problem in social network analysis where Bayesian nonparametrics are conceptually appealing but often slow.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to tree-structured hierarchies makes marginal likelihood computation efficient, improving over IRM’s combinatorial search.  
- Experimental results: performs comparably or slightly better than IRM on AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy.  
- Runtime advantage: claimed inference is orders of magnitude faster than IRM.  
- Novelty: presents a Bayesian version of agglomerative clustering inspired by IRM with an insightful core learning algorithm.  
- Main methodological concern: unclear relationship between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the model’s generative/posterior interpretation (posterior vs. MAP/ML ambiguity).  
- Suggestion: clarify Section 4 to explain whether the algorithm performs true posterior inference or a point estimate.  
- Writing and clarity issues: numerous spelling/grammar errors and vague phrasing that hinder readability.  
- Dataset descriptions are insufficient (e.g., Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) and may confuse readers unfamiliar with them.  
- Some mathematical exposition is overly obtuse or unnecessary (e.g., Eq. 5) and should be simplified or removed.  
- Figure 3 is confusing; consider using a more familiar dataset or clearer visualization to illustrate results.  
- Overall recommendation: sufficient novelty and strong experiments justify acceptance, contingent on improving writing and clarifying methodological points.
