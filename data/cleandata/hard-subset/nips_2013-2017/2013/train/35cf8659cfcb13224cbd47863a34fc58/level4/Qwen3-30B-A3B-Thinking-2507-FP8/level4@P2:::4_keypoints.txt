- Introduces BHCD: an agglomerative method extending Bayesian rose trees to relational data via a hierarchical stochastic blockmodel with greedy model selection and dynamic-programming marginal likelihood (O(n + m), worst-case O(n² log n)).  
- Core innovation is caching sufficient statistics for O(1) updates during tree merges, yielding large inference speedups.  
- Model formalization and inference (tree-structured mixture of SBMs, Algorithm 1, Equations 9–13) are technically sound and runtime analysis is rigorous.  
- Experiments show ~100x speedup over IRM with comparable accuracy on NIPS co-authorship data.  
- Lacks critical comparisons to hierarchical Bayesian baselines (Ho et al. 2012 Multiscale Community Blockmodel, Roy et al. 2009 Mondrian Process), weakening claims of novelty and significance.  
- Presentation is clear for core algorithms (good pseudocode and reproducibility); some notation (Equation 5, derivation of σ¬chSS Eq. 9) is dense and would benefit from simpler notation or examples.  
- Not novel in model design (combines rose trees with standard SBM); primary novelty is scalability from recursive sufficient-statistic updates.  
- Misuses the term "nonparametric" contrary to Blundell et al. [12] and should correct terminology.  
- Significance partially realized (improved scalability), but the paper fails to show that its hierarchical structure outperforms other hierarchical methods.  
- Pros: clear, sound algorithm; major speedup; real-world validation. Cons: missing hierarchical baselines, terminology misuse, limited discussion of limitations, minimal theoretical novelty.  
- Recommendation: weak rejection pending major revisions — add comparisons to Ho et al. and Roy et al., clarify "nonparametric" claim, and discuss how BHCD’s hierarchy differs from existing hierarchical Bayesian models.
