- Proposes a hierarchical extension of IRM using Bayesian rose tree ideas: a hierarchy defines a mixture over consistent node partitions and is learned via a greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Evaluated on Sampson's monks, full NIPS, and NIPS-234, showing much lower computational cost than IRM while maintaining comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear, precise technical exposition.  
- Originality lies in adapting BRT concepts to relational/network data and retaining IRM-like computational complexity—a non-trivial engineering and modeling effort.  
- Significance: addresses practical scalability of Bayesian nonparametric models for networks by reducing computational burden from O(n^2).  
- Open scalability questions: runtime on full NIPS unclear; lack of empirical evidence on much larger real-world networks (10^4–10^6 nodes); sparse variant promising but untested at scale.  
- Concern about how close the greedy method gets to the optimal tree; recommend comparisons on datasets where exhaustive search is feasible.  
- Suggestion to provide theoretical justification for the greedy approach (e.g., submodularity or approximation guarantees).  
- Minor corrections: wording fixes—"whilst... but" → "while"/"whereas"; "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees"; "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step".  
- Overall recommendation: accept—the adaptation to network modeling, computational efficiency, and clear presentation constitute a significant contribution despite remaining scalability questions.
