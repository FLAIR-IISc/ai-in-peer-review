- Proposes an agglomerative hierarchical community detection method combining Bayesian rose trees with a block-model likelihood; overall algorithm/ prior largely follows prior rose-tree work.  
- Adapts the likelihood for networks with efficient caching of sufficient statistics, claiming O(nodes + edges) complexity.  
- Presentation is generally clear but Section 3 (especially Equation (9)) is dense and would benefit from simpler notation/explanation.  
- The recursive dynamic-programming updates (around Equation (10)) are described inadequately and lack justification for correctness.  
- Use of the term "nonparametric" is questionable given prior work [12] that contrasts rose trees with nonparametric methods.  
- Experiments show gains versus a flat-community baseline and useful qualitative results (Sampson, IRM comparison) demonstrating hierarchy’s utility.  
- Major omission: no comparisons to established hierarchical network models (e.g., Ho et al. 2012, Roy et al. 2009), weakening claims of relative superiority.  
- Scalability claims are not strongly validated because evaluated networks are not particularly large.  
- Overall: technically sound and efficient integration of rose trees with SBM likelihood, but limited baseline comparisons and insufficient algorithmic detail reduce impact.
