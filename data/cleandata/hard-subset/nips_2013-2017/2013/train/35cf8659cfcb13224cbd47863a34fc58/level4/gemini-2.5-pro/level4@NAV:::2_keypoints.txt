- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer trees instead of flat partitions.  
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering-based learning algorithm that identifies a tree constraining the space of partitions.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social datasets shows performance comparable to or slightly better than the IRM in AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy.  
- Claims orders-of-magnitude faster runtime than IRM inference, a major practical advantage.  
- Tackles a timely and relevant problem for NeurIPS: reducing computational inefficiency in Bayesian nonparametrics while serving social-science applications.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to a tree enables efficient marginal likelihood computation, avoiding the exponential partition space.  
- Unclear relationship between the greedy tree-learning procedure and the model’s generative/posterior inference—uncertain if it performs formal posterior inference or a MAP/ML estimate; Section 4 needs clarification.  
- Empirical gains in metrics are modest, but the speed improvement is substantial and practically important.  
- Paper’s clarity is problematic: typographical/grammatical errors and ambiguous phrasing (e.g., lines 47–50) hinder understanding.  
- Dataset descriptions (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficient for unfamiliar readers.  
- Some mathematical formulations (e.g., Equation 5) are unnecessarily opaque and Figure 3 is confusing for readers unfamiliar with the dataset.  
- Overall: novel and insightful Bayesian analogue to agglomerative clustering with strong experiments, but presentation and clarity issues must be fixed for publication.
