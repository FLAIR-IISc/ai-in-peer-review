- Presentation is strong: well-written, good spelling/grammar/citations; minor style fix—avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style.  
- Theoretical development is succinct and uses notation effectively (e.g., f() and g()).  
- Extends single-table IRM by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees, yielding a simpler CRP formulation (eq. 7).  
- Factoring over subtrees is akin to Koivisto & Sood’s work, enabling factorized posteriors for efficient greedy search, marginal likelihoods, sufficient statistics handling, and link prediction.  
- Use of AUC for SBM evaluation is unclear; the paper should clarify how AUC is applied (likely link deletion/recovery).  
- Experiments should include larger datasets (beyond the full NIPS dataset) to demonstrate scalability.  
- Comparison set is insufficient: need broader comparisons to published semi-parametric methods in addition to Bayesian nonparametrics.  
- Main contribution is the agglomerative method (summarized in Table 1); theory and algorithms are clear but relatively simple.  
- Experimental evaluation is mainly illustrative rather than comparative and therefore inadequate.
