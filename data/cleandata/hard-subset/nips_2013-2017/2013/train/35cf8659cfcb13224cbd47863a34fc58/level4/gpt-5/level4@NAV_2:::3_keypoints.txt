- Paper is well written with good grammar/formatting and consistent notation; avoid opening sentences with a citation per NIPS style.
- Generalizes single-table IRM for SBMs by incorporating Bayesian Rose trees, replacing CRP-based IRM marginalization with the partition prior in Eq. (7).
- Posterior factorization over subtrees parallels Bayesian network structure learning, enabling greedy search, exact marginal likelihoods, efficient sufficient-statistics handling, and link prediction.
- Evaluation uses AUC (less standard for SBM clustering); if performing link prediction, the holdout protocol must be made explicit.
- Experiments are limited in scale (NIPS-234 informative but small); should include larger datasets used in recent SBM evaluations.
- Baseline comparison is insufficient—needs additional semi-parametric methods, not just Bayesian nonparametric baselines.
- Main technical contribution is the agglomerative procedure; Table 1 highlights clear computational advantages.
- Overall: theory and algorithms are clear and well presented but conceptually straightforward; experimental section is illustrative rather than broadly comparative or fully adequate.
