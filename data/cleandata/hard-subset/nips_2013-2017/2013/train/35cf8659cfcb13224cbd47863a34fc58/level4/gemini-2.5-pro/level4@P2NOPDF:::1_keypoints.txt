- Proposal: hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model using Bayesian Rose Tree ideas—hierarchy defines a mixture over consistent partitions and is learned via a greedy agglomerative procedure; shown on Sampson’s monks, full NIPS, and NIPS-234, achieving comparable predictive performance to IRM with much lower computation.

- Quality/Clarity: paper is well-written and the core ideas are clearly and accessibly presented.

- Originality/Significance: significant and non-trivial adaptation of BRT to relational data, addressing an important scalability challenge for Bayesian nonparametric network methods.

- Main scalability concern: basic algorithm scales O(n^2) and may not handle large networks; authors should provide empirical evidence (e.g., full NIPS timings and experiments on much larger, sparse real-world networks).

- Greedy optimality concern: need empirical comparison between the greedy agglomerative solution and the global optimum (via exhaustive search) on small networks to assess how close greedy gets to optimal trees.

- Theoretical justification: request for analysis of the objective (e.g., submodularity or related properties) that could provide performance guarantees for the greedy method.

- Minor corrections: fix wording issues—remove redundant "whilst... but"; check "pair of trees" vs. singular; change "A fruitful next steps" to "A fruitful next step" or "Fruitful next steps."

- Overall conclusion: valuable contribution with promising results; recommend acceptance pending stronger evaluation of scalability and optimality concerns.
