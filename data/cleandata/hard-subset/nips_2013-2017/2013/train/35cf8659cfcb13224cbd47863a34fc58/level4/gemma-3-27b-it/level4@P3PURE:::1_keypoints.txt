- Introduces a hierarchical extension of the IRM using BRT ideas: hierarchy defines a mixture over consistent partitions and is learned with a greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Shows qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and full NIPS, quantitative results on NIPS-234; inference is much cheaper than standard IRM while attaining similar predictive performance.  
- Paper is very well written and the exposition is clear.  
- Core ideas are adapted from Bayesian rose trees, but adapting them to relational data required non-trivial effort to preserve comparable computational complexity.  
- Scaling BNP models to networks is important; the basic algorithm’s n^2 scaling may be prohibitive though a sparse variant could be feasible.  
- Requests timings and scalability analysis on larger real-world networks (how long on full NIPS, behavior on gene/social networks with tens of thousands of nodes).  
- Suggests evaluating how closely the greedy method approximates the optimal tree (compare to exhaustive search where tractable) and seeking theoretical justification (e.g., submodularity).  
- Minor corrections: replace “whilst... but” with “while but”; “pair of treeS” → “pair of trees”; “A fruitful next steps” → “Fruitful next steps.”  
- Overall verdict: contribution is significant and the paper should be accepted.
