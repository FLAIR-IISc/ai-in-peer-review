- Proposes a clear extension of stochastic block models by integrating Bayesian Rose trees.  
- Provides a solid theoretical foundation, using functions f() and g() consistently to explain the method.  
- Factoring over subtrees (building on Bayesian network structure learning) yields a neat posterior formulation and enables greedy search, marginal likelihoods, and use of sufficient statistics.  
- Main algorithmic contribution is an agglomerative method (BHCD), effectively summarized in Table 1.  
- BHCD is shown to discover hierarchical community structures and is demonstrated on datasets like Sampson’s monastery and NIPS co-authorship.  
- Evaluation primarily uses AUC, which may be inappropriate for SBMs and lacks justification.  
- Recommends using gold-standard partition metrics (e.g., NMI) and other clustering measures typical for SBM evaluation.  
- Experimental section is illustrative but not comparative enough; needs broader and larger-scale experiments.  
- Should compare against a wider range of published methods, including semi-parametric approaches, to better demonstrate strengths/weaknesses.  
- Paper is well-written with good spelling, grammar, and correct citations.  
- Some theoretical sections would benefit from additional explanations or examples to improve clarity.  
- Overall a solid contribution to SBMs, but acceptance depends on strengthening the evaluation and experimental comparisons.
