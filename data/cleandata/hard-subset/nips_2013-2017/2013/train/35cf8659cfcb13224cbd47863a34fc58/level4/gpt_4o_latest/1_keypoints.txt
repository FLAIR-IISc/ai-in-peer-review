- Introduces a hierarchical extension of the IRM using Bayesian rose tree (BRT) concepts to model networks.  
- Uses the hierarchy to define a mixture over consistent node partitions and learns it via a greedy agglomerative procedure.  
- Provides qualitative results on Sampson’s monks and the full NIPS dataset, and quantitative results on NIPS-234.  
- Inference is much more computationally efficient than the IRM while achieving comparable predictive performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear exposition of core ideas.  
- Primary ideas derive from BRT, but authors made meaningful adaptations for relational data and preserved computational complexity.  
- Scaling Bayesian nonparametrics for networks is important; the base algorithm’s O(n^2) scaling may limit applicability to large networks.  
- Sparse version is expected to remain practical, but runtime benchmarks on larger real-world networks (e.g., full NIPS, gene networks, social networks) are missing.  
- Suggest evaluating how close the greedy method gets to the optimal tree on datasets where exhaustive search is feasible.  
- Request theoretical support or guarantees for the greedy method (e.g., submodularity-related results).  
- Minor corrections: fix phrasing ("whilst... but"), "pair of treeS" → "pair of trees", "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step".  
- Overall assessment: contribution is significant despite BRT roots, and the paper merits acceptance.
