- Paper introduces a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchical structure from observed links.  
- Proposes an agglomerative clustering-based learning algorithm to select a tree of valid partitions.  
- Empirical evaluation on two social connectivity corpora shows the method is faster and slightly more accurate than the Infinite Relational Model (IRM).  
- Topic is relevant: addresses practical speed issues of Bayesian nonparametrics and the needs of social-science network analysis.  
- Key technical insight: restricting partitions to a tree enables efficient marginal likelihood computation versus IRM’s exponential partition space.  
- Concern: the greedy tree-learning algorithm’s relation to the Bayesian generative model is unclear—does not appear to perform true posterior inference or find a MAP/ML estimate; Section 4 needs clarification.  
- Results: comparable or improved AUC, predictive log-likelihood, and accuracy relative to IRM, with orders-of-magnitude faster runtime.  
- Writing and presentation issues: spelling/grammar errors, vague phrasing, unclear dataset descriptions, some unnecessary or obtuse math (e.g., Eq. 5), and a confusing Figure 3.  
- Overall judgment: novel and strong experimental results warrant publication if clarity and presentation issues are addressed; core algorithm is insightful but writing must improve.
