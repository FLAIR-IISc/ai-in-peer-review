- Proposes BHCD: a scalable Bayesian hierarchical community discovery method using tree-structured mixtures of stochastic blockmodels.  
- Adapts Bayesian Rose Trees to relational data with a greedy agglomerative inference algorithm running in O(n² log n) (sparse variant O(n + m)).  
- Key implementation innovations: marginal likelihood recursion, efficient sufficient-statistic caching, and a greedy agglomerative merge procedure.  
- Claims two orders of magnitude speedup over IRM while maintaining comparable predictive accuracy.  
- Demonstrated on Sampson’s monastery, NIPS-234, and full NIPS (2864 nodes), showing clear hierarchy and scalability in practice.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, technically sound, and clearly explains the connection between hierarchical partitions and tree structures.  
- Experiments are well-designed with thorough comparisons to IRM and baselines; Algorithm 1 is well-motivated and justified.  
- Originality: non-trivial and novel adaptation of BRT to network data, avoiding O(K²) scaling pitfalls of prior hierarchical Bayesian methods.  
- Significance: addresses scaling of Bayesian nonparametric network models, enabling practical application to larger datasets than IRM.  
- Major empirical gap: timing for the full NIPS (2864-node) run is unreported, weakening scalability claims.  
- Concern that O(n²) scaling may still be prohibitive for very large networks (10k+ or millions); unclear if sparse variant attains linear scaling in practice.  
- Greedy algorithm’s suboptimality is not assessed—no empirical optimality gap or theoretical guarantees (e.g., submodularity) provided.  
- Tie-breaking via restarts (R=50) is mentioned but its effectiveness is not quantified.  
- Minor issues: a few grammatical fixes required (e.g., "treeS" → "trees"; "A fruitful next steps" → "A fruitful next step").  
- Recommendation: accept—valuable, well-executed contribution with minor addressable weaknesses (scalability analysis and optimality assessment).  
- Pro: first to combine greedy agglomerative inference with a full Bayesian hierarchical network model; clear computational advantage and solid experiments.  
- Con: limited large-network scalability analysis and lack of theoretical/empirical guarantees on greedy optimality.
