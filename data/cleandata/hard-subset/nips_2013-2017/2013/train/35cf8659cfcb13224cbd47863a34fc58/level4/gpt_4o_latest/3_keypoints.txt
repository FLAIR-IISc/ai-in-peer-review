- Writing quality is high (good spelling, grammar, citations), but avoid starting sentences with citations per NIPS style; some theoretical notation (f(), g()) is inconsistently concise.  
- Main methodological contribution: extend single-table IRM using Bayesian Rose trees to replace the CRP formulation with a simpler Equation (7).  
- Factorization over subtrees (inspired by Koivisto & Sood) yields elegant posterior factorization for constrained structures.  
- This factorization enables efficient greedy search, marginal likelihood computation, handling sufficient statistics, and link-prediction tasks.  
- Evaluation choice: paper reports AUC for link prediction, which is unusual for SBM work that typically uses NMI/clustering metrics and needs clearer explanation of the protocol (e.g., link removal/restoration).  
- Experimental scale: evaluations are small; results would be more compelling on much larger datasets commonly used in recent SBM work.  
- Baselines: comparisons are limited to Bayesian nonparametric methods; semi-parametric and other relevant methods should be included.  
- Overall: primary contribution is the agglomerative method (Table 1) with clear theory and algorithms, but experimental evaluation is illustrative rather than sufficiently comparative.
