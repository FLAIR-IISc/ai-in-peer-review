- Proposes agglomerative clustering for hierarchical network communities by combining Bayesian rose trees with stochastic block model likelihoods.  
- Prior tree structure and algorithm largely unchanged from Bayesian rose trees; likelihood adapted for networks and sufficient statistics can be cached with extra effort.  
- Presentation is generally clear, but Section 3 needs better notation and explanation around equation (9) and following components.  
- Experimental results outperform a flat community baseline, showing benefits of hierarchical modeling for relational data.  
- Missing comparisons to existing hierarchical network models, notably Ho et al. (multiscale community blockmodel) and Roy et al. (Mondrian Process).  
- Novelty is limited: essentially a Bayesian rose tree paired with a standard block-model likelihood and greedy agglomerative updates.  
- Runtime claim of likelihood computation in O(Nodes + Edges) is a notable strength.  
- Description of recursive updating of sufficient statistics is unclear and insufficiently justified; dynamic programming allusion before equation (10) lacks detail.  
- Use of the term "nonparametric" is questionable given prior framing of rose trees as model selection rather than nonparametric.  
- Qualitative Sampson experiment and IRM comparison are useful, but broader hierarchical baselines are absent.  
- Scalability discussion is present but evaluated on relatively small networks, limiting evidence of large-scale performance.
