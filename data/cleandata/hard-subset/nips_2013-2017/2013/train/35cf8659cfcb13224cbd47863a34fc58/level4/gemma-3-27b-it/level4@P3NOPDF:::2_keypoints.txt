- Proposes a hierarchical extension of the Bayesian nonparametric stochastic block model to infer hierarchical relationships instead of flat clusters.
- Introduces an agglomerative clustering–based learning algorithm to efficiently select a tree of entity partitions.
- Restricting partitions to a tree enables efficient marginal likelihood computation, avoiding the IRM’s exponential partition space.
- Empirical results on two social datasets show comparable or slightly better accuracy (AUC, predictive log-likelihood, accuracy) than the IRM.
- Reports orders-of-magnitude runtime improvements over IRM inference, a major computational advantage.
- The connection between the tree-learning procedure and the Bayesian generative model is unclear and needs clarification.
- The greedy/agglomerative algorithm’s status is ambiguous—likely a heuristic or point estimate rather than full posterior inference—so Section 4 should clarify this.
- Some reported accuracy gains are modest in absolute terms, though runtime benefits are compelling.
- Presentation has multiple issues: spelling/grammar errors and occasionally unclear prose that hinder comprehension.
- Key terms and processes (e.g., “local changes,” “iterations”) are vague and should be better defined.
- Datasets (Monastery networks, NIPS authorship) are insufficiently described for readers unfamiliar with them.
- Certain mathematical formulations (e.g., Equation 5) seem unnecessarily complex and could be simplified.
- Figure 3 is visually unclear and the Monastery dataset visualization may not effectively illustrate method differences; a more familiar dataset could help.
- Overall contribution is novel and significant; recommend publication contingent on addressing clarity and exposition concerns.
