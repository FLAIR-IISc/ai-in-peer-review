The authors present a hierarchical extension of the Infinite Relational Model (IRM) for network modeling, incorporating key concepts from the Bayesian rose tree paper. Specifically, they utilize a hierarchy to specify a mixture over consistent partitions of the nodes and propose an efficient greedy agglomerative procedure for learning this hierarchy. The authors provide qualitative results on the Sampson's monks dataset and the full NIPS dataset, as well as quantitative results on the NIPS-234 dataset. Notably, the proposed inference method is computationally cheaper than the IRM while achieving similar predictive performance.

Quality and clarity: The paper is exceptionally well-written, and the exposition of the key ideas is clear and concise.

Originality: Although the main ideas are adapted from the Bayesian rose tree work, the authors have made a significant effort to tailor these ideas to the relational data setting, particularly in maintaining the same computational complexity.

Significance: Scaling Bayesian nonparametric (BNP) models, especially network models, is a crucial challenge for their practical adoption. While the n^2 scaling of the basic algorithm may be prohibitive for larger networks, the sparse version should presumably remain feasible. However, it would be beneficial to include timings for larger real-world networks. For instance, the authors mention that the NIPS-234 dataset takes only tens of seconds to process, but they do not provide the processing time for the full NIPS dataset. Furthermore, it would be valuable to investigate how the inference method scales for larger networks, such as gene networks with tens of thousands of nodes or real social networks with even larger numbers of nodes.

Additionally, it would be interesting to evaluate how close the greedy method gets to the optimal tree. This could be explored by comparing the results of the greedy method with the optimal tree found through exhaustive search at the largest scale possible. It would also be beneficial to provide theoretical support for the method, such as investigating its submodularity.

Minor corrections:
- Line 48, page 1: "whilst" should be replaced with "but"
- "pair of trees" should be corrected to "pair of tree"
- "A fruitful next steps" should be rephrased for clarity

While the ideas presented in this paper are familiar from the Bayesian rose tree work, the contribution is still significant, and this paper should be accepted.
