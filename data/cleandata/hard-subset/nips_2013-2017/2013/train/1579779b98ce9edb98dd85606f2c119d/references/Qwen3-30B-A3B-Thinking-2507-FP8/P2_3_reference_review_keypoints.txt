- Paper proves the first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move games using ε-Hannan consistent selection functions.  
- Presents a general MCTS template that uses any ε-Hannan consistent regret-minimizer for matrix-game selection.  
- Main theoretical result: algorithm converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium under this framework.  
- Proof is rigorous and inductive, building from matrix games to full extensive-form games with careful handling of MCTS error.  
- Defines key concepts (ε-Hannan consistency, guaranteed exploration) clearly and uses them effectively in the analysis.  
- Complements theory with thorough empirical evaluation using Regret Matching and Exp3, comparing mean- and current-sample propagation.  
- Empirical results support theory: mean-propagation variants converge to approximate Nash equilibria, with slightly slower convergence as predicted.  
- Writing, organization, notation, and explanatory background are excellent; only minor clarity issue in Section 4.3’s definition of "repeated game with error."  
- Novel contribution connecting online learning (regret minimization) with MCTS and filling a gap left by prior sequential-game convergence results.  
- Significant impact: provides theoretical foundation for practical MCTS use in simultaneous-move games and suggests future directions (finite-time bounds, imperfect information, non-zero-sum).  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation could include more diverse games beyond worst-case examples.  
- Weakness: paper should more explicitly explain why RM and Exp3 guarantee exploration without modification.  
- Weakness: could strengthen explicit connections to ML/NIPS community and practical ML implications.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, well-written, significant contribution; recommendation: Accept.
