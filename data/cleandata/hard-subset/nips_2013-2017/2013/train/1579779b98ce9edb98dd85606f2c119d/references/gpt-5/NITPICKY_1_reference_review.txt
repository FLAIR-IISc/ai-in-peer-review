Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) in two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move extensive-form games. It proposes a general SM-MCTS template that uses any ε–Hannan-consistent (HC) selection rule (e.g., Regret Matching, Exp3) with guaranteed exploration, and proves almost-sure convergence of the average strategies to a subgame-perfect (ε D^2 + δ)-Nash equilibrium, where D is the game-tree depth. The analysis proceeds by: (i) establishing convergence properties for repeated matrix games under HC, (ii) extending to repeated matrix games with bounded payoff errors, and (iii) inductively composing these bounds up the tree, assuming the full tree is eventually built. Empirically, the paper compares variants that back up the current sample vs. node means and examines worst-case convergence on small games.

This situates the work among MCTS and online learning in games (Hart & Mas-Colell; Auer et al.), simultaneous-move search with alpha-beta/double oracle (Saffidine et al., Bosansky et al.), and previous empirical SM-MCTS (Finnsson & Björnsson; Perick et al.; Lanctot et al.). It also relates to CFR-based methods (Shafiei et al.) used in simultaneous games.

Strengths
- Addresses an under-theorized setting: simultaneous-move perfect-information games with MCTS, giving the first convergence-to-ε-NE guarantee for a broad template (under HC selection).
- Clear high-level proof strategy: base case at leaves, bounded-error repeated games, and induction up the tree.
- Reasonable empirical checks that mean backups slightly slow convergence but do not change the limiting behavior; small-scale “worst-case” stress tests.
- Emphasizes subgame-perfect convergence and explicitly handles exploration-induced bias.

Weaknesses and technical concerns
Quality/technical soundness
- Payoff handling for player 2 is inconsistent. The paper assumes u2 = 1 − u1 (range [0,1]), yet says “Player 2 does the same except using negated utilities.” Negating u1 yields −u1, not 1 − u1. This affects both updates and the zero-sum property used in proofs.
- Exp3 specification is nonstandard and underspecified. The algorithm sets η = γ/|A1| and mixes exploration with probability γ, but HC of Exp3 typically requires a learning rate schedule depending on horizon T (or an adaptive scheme). The paper claims HC for “variants of Exp3” but does not show that the concrete, fixed-η, fixed-γ instantiation is HC. The update xh,i ← xh,i + u1/σs(h,i) is unbiased only if exploration is incorporated consistently; interaction with the A* “no-update-on-exploration” variant is unclear.
- Guaranteed exploration vs. updates: Definition 4.3’s A* explores with probability γ and “without updating any variables.” Elsewhere (Regret Matching section) the γ-on-policy sampling suggests updates do occur on exploratory actions. These two choices lead to different stochastic processes and regret properties. Lemma 4.4 assumes A* still yields (ε+γ)-HC, but the proof sketch is absent and the mismatch with the RM section is unresolved.
- Convergence via “complete tree eventually”: The main text claims to prove with probability 1 that the tree is fully expanded, but no sketch is provided, and the expansion rule (only one new leaf added per visit to a node with unexpanded children) plus depth coupling makes this nontrivial. This is central to the reduction to fixed, finite repeated matrix games at each node.
- Lemma 4.5 (empirical vs. average strategies) cites a martingale SLLN without conditions. Because the action probabilities are history-dependent, the result needs explicit boundedness and adaptedness conditions. The current statement lacks assumptions and proof details.
- The final bound is O(D^2 ε) with constants that accumulate loosely (factors of 4 and 2(c+1) across lemmas). It is not clear whether tighter constants or linear-in-D dependency could be obtained with sharper coupling/averaging arguments. The discussion hints at “in the order of d,” “2d,” then sums to “D^2,” but this is heuristic in the main text.
- Empirics are limited: only tiny games, no comparison to strong baselines such as CFR/CFR+ adapted to simultaneous moves. Claims of “comparable to recently proposed MCTS algorithms” are not quantified. Convergence plots use exploitability measures that depend on v and br but do not detail how br is computed (oracle? exact solving?), nor confidence intervals.
- Finite-time behavior is not addressed theoretically, and empirical evaluation does not fill the gap (no rates, no sensitivity to γ, η, rollout policy).

Clarity and presentation
- Algorithm 1 is difficult to follow: RetVal is overloaded (returning u1, or Xh/nh, or Xh′/nh′), and the “M” variant is described only in prose. Line numbers 8, 14 “Update” are not fully specified per variant. The interaction between mean backups and selection updates is ambiguous.
- Notation drift and typos impede readability: A(h) vs. A1(h), r+h vs. r+, “oth.” in Eq. (3), wh,i = xh,i1 in Eq. (4) (typo?), σs(h,a) uses “a” for player-1 action, etc.
- Several claims are deferred to an appendix/arXiv with no sketch. For a NIPS paper, more proof detail is warranted for key steps (e.g., Lemma 4.9, Theorem 4.11 induction).

Originality and significance
- The convergence template under HC is a meaningful theoretical contribution for SM-MCTS. However, the practical impact is dampened by strong assumptions (perfect information, full tree eventually built, mean backups) and loose bounds.
- Lack of empirical comparison to CFR(-family), which is standard for extensive-form games, weakens the case for significance in practice.

Questions for the authors
- Precisely specify player-2 updates under the u2 = 1 − u1 model. Are you actually using −u1 in code/theory?
- For Exp3, what learning rate schedule ensures HC? Is γ fixed or decayed? Are exploratory steps included in updates?
- Provide a proof sketch (in main text) that the tree is fully expanded almost surely under your expansion policy and exploration.
- Can the depth dependence be improved to O(D ε)? What are tight constants?
- How are best responses computed for exploitability in experiments?
- Why not compare to CFR in simultaneous-move settings?

Formatting and minor issues (non-exhaustive)
- Many references are incomplete (missing years, venues, pages); some misspellings (“Eolutionary,” “Proceeding,” etc.).
- Inconsistent notation: A(h) vs. A1(h), σ0(h,i) definition.
- Equation (3) “oth.” should be “otherwise,” denominator should be |A1(h)|.
- Equation (4): wh,i = xh,i1 appears to be a typo; clarify stability trick (subtract max) cleanly.
- Several grammar issues: “This values corresponds,” “in the level,” “the once where action i was selected.”

Pros
- First convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS to ε-NE under HC selection rules.
- Clear high-level structure; handles exploration-induced biases.
- Empirically observes practical convergence for RM/Exp3 variants.

Cons
- Multiple technical inconsistencies (player-2 payoff handling, Exp3/updates), and missing details essential for correctness.
- Weak empirical evaluation and no comparison to CFR.
- Significant clarity/notation/formatting problems; many references incomplete.
- Loose depth-dependent bound and no finite-time rates.

Recommendation: Reject (borderline). The core idea is promising, but the accumulation of technical and presentation issues, plus limited empirics, makes the current submission below the bar. With corrected algorithms/assumptions, clearer proofs (or detailed sketches), tighter constants, and stronger experiments (including CFR baselines), I would be inclined to reconsider.

Confidence: 4/5.
