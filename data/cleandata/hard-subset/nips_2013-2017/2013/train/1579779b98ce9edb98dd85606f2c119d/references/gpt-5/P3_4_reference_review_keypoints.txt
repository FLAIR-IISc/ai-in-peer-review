- Main claim: SM-MCTS using node-wise ε–Hannan consistent (HC) selection with guaranteed exploration and mean backprop converges almost surely to a subgame-perfect (ε·D^2 + δ)-Nash equilibrium (D = tree depth).
- Proof strategy: builds from HC learner convergence in repeated matrix games, extends to bounded-payoff-error repeated games, and inductively composes errors up the tree for SM-MCTS-M.
- Modularity: clean abstraction separating “selection algorithm is ε–HC” from the SM-MCTS template.
- Empirics: RM and Exp3 at nodes empirically converge on small random/worst-case games; mean backprop slows but still converges.
- Novelty: first asymptotic convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS in simultaneous-move perfect-observation games; repeated-game-with-bounded-error lemma is a neat technical contribution.
- Conditionality: main theorem depends on each node using an ε–HC rule with guaranteed exploration—this is a strong, nontrivial assumption.
- RM mismatch: the specific regret-matching (RM) variant used is not shown to be HC under bandit feedback; paper must either prove RM is ε–HC under observed feedback or restrict formal claims to algorithms like Exp3/bandit-RM.
- Mean backprop requirement: proof requires backpropagation of node-level sample means, which is nonstandard in MCTS; no theoretical treatment shows sample-backprop also satisfies the bounded-error assumptions.
- Asymptotic-only: paper proves eventual convergence but provides no finite-time rates; composing known regret rates across depth is unexplored and would aid practicality.
- Loose bounds: the ε·D^2 dependence and intermediate constants appear pessimistic; tightness or potential improvements (e.g., O(D) or depth-independent under stronger assumptions) are not addressed.
- Exploration realism: requiring every joint action be sampled infinitely often is unrealistic in large games and forces a non-vanishing exploration floor; decaying or adaptive exploration schedules are not analyzed.
- Terminology/scope: calling the class “perfect information with simultaneous moves” can confuse information-set conventions; the information structure should be precisely defined.
- Empirical limitations: experiments are small-scale, lack comparisons to CFR or backward-induction baselines, omit standard benchmarks (e.g., Goofspiel), and lack ablations on exploration schedules and scaling.
- Clarity fixes: distinguish empirical frequencies vs. average strategies, state mean vs. sample backprop in plots, define “value returned” formally, and consider moving the “complete tree eventually” lemma into the main text.
- Suggested improvements: prove or cite HC for the RM variant or restrict claims, attempt finite-sample analyses, study decaying/adaptive exploration, clarify information-structure terminology, and strengthen experiments and baselines.
- Acceptance recommendation: weak accept if RM/HC mismatch is resolved and clarity improved; otherwise borderline due to theoretical novelty but practical limitations.
