- Tackles convergence of MCTS in simultaneous-move games and proves convergence to subgame-perfect ε-Nash under ε-Hannan consistent selection with exploration.  
- Proposes a general SM-MCTS template using regret-minimizing procedures for action selection.  
- Empirical validation using Regret Matching and Exp3 shows comparable performance to existing MCTS methods.  
- Positions the work against prior MCTS, online learning, and simultaneous-move literature, noting limitations of UCB-based MCTS.  
- Primary theoretical contribution: first rigorous convergence proof for simultaneous-move MCTS.  
- Framework is general and can accommodate any ε-Hannan consistent regret-minimizer.  
- Experiments provide initial empirical support but are not exhaustive.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and cites related work comprehensively.  
- Weakness: no analysis of convergence rate; O(D^2) term in Theorem 4.11 may cause poor scaling with depth.  
- Weakness: error bound term (η) is hard to estimate or control in practice.  
- Weakness: limited empirical scope—needs wider range of game structures and branching factors.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of practical implementation and guarantees of ε-Hannan consistency.  
- Weakness: proof assumes a complete tree, which may not reflect real-world limited-tree MCTS.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, sound theoretical results with potential high impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of concrete convergence rate and simplified experimental setup.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss O(D^2) implications and mitigations; detail practical considerations for ε-Hannan consistency; address incomplete tree-building impact; expand empirical evaluation.
