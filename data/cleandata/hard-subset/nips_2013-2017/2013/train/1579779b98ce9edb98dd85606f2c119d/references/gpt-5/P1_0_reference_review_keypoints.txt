- Paper studies SM-MCTS in two-player simultaneous-move perfect-information games and proposes an SM-MCTS template using independent ε-Hannan-consistent selectors with mean backups (SM-MCTS-M).  
- Main theoretical claim: almost-sure convergence of empirical frequencies to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium under ε-HC selection, guaranteed exploration, and mean backups.  
- Analysis approach: reduce HC in repeated matrix games to bounded-error repeated games and inductively compose up the tree to bound incentives, yielding a (D^2 + δ)-NE type bound for depth D.  
- Empirically instantiates template with regret matching and Exp3, showing convergence on random small games and comparing mean vs sample backups.  
- Novelty: first general convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS to subgame-perfect approximate NE under broad HC selection, linking online learning (HC) with MCTS.  
- Conceptual strength: simple, general recipe—plug any ε-HC selector with exploration and back up means—with a clean convergence proof.  
- Technical strength: clear reduction and bounded-error lemmas that may be of independent interest.  
- Generality: result applies to any ε-HC selector (e.g., RM, Exp3), not a bespoke algorithm.  
- Subgame perfection: limit strategies are shown to be subgame perfect, stronger than root-only optimality.  
- Empirical sanity checks corroborate theoretical claims and explore backup choices.  
- Main theorem statement/tightness issue: Theorem 4.11 omits explicit ε dependence and constants, giving a vacuous D^2 term that can exceed payoff range—needs precise constants and clarification if dependence is O(ε D^2) or similar.  
- Backup requirement concern: theory relies on mean backups (SM-MCTS-M), a departure from common sample-backup practice; need extension or discussion of sample-backup convergence (e.g., via martingale/bias control).  
- Exploration assumption unclear: require each joint action tried infinitely often—please clarify why independent per-player exploration yields tij → ∞ a.s., and whether standard RM/Exp3 with γ-mixing suffice; discuss efficiency trade-offs.  
- Experimental limitations: evaluation restricted to small random games and tiny exhaustive searches; needs larger realistic domains, stronger baselines (CFR/MCCFR, double-oracle, alpha–beta variants), exploitability metrics, and confidence intervals.  
- Finite-sample rates missing: only asymptotic guarantees given; even heuristic or empirical scaling with D, branching factor, γ would be valuable.  
- Presentation issues: typos, notational glitches, incomplete citations, and Algorithm 1 ambiguity (mean-backup variant and Select composition should be explicit).  
- Overall quality: theoretical core is sound if theorem constants/ε dependence are clarified; empirical results support convergence but lack breadth.  
- Clarity: generally readable but needs corrected theorem statement and explicit algorithmic details (backup and exploration).  
- Originality and significance: novel and potentially influential theoretical foundation for SM-MCTS in simultaneous-move games despite potentially loose asymptotic bounds.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel general guarantee, clean HC connection, practical template compatible with standard selectors, supporting experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: misstated/loose main bound missing ε, reliance on mean backups and strong exploration assumptions, limited experiments, and presentation problems.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on fixing Theorem 4.11 (constants and ε), explicitly specifying the required backup estimator, improving presentation, and expanding experiments/discussion of finite-time behavior.
