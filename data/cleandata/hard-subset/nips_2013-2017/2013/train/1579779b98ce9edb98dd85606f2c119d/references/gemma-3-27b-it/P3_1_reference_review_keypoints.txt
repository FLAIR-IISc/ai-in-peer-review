- Proves convergence of SM-MCTS using an ε-Hannan consistent selection function to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Proposes a general SM-MCTS framework leveraging regret-minimizing procedures for matrix games.  
- The framework is instantiable with any ε-Hannan consistent algorithm, providing flexibility.  
- Empirical results with Regret Matching and Exp3 show competitive convergence speeds to recent SM-MCTS methods.  
- Correctly identifies the theoretical gap for MCTS in simultaneous-move games and connects to regret minimization literature.  
- Appropriately avoids UCB due to known non-convergence and builds on established Hannan-consistency results.  
- Highlights advantage over alpha-beta/double-oracle backward-induction when domain knowledge is limited.  
- Theoretical contribution is rigorous, with a clear proof structure from matrix games to multi-stage games.  
- Presentation is clear, with thorough definitions and accessible background material.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and suggest reasonable future research directions.  
- Practical implications of requiring Hannan consistency are underexplored; relation between ε and real-world performance is unclear.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to simple/random or worst-case games and lacks complex real-world benchmarks and broader algorithmic comparisons.  
- Insufficient analysis of the exploration parameter γ and its influence on convergence speed and theoretical guarantees.  
- Numerical stability concerns (e.g., Exp3 modifications) are mentioned but not broadly discussed for the framework.  
- Tightness of Theorem 4.11’s bound (D^2 + δ) is unclear—may be loose or worst-case and not practically achievable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant open problem, strong theoretical contribution, supportive empirical evidence, and good writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation, unclear practical implications of theory, and insufficient discussion of γ and numerical stability.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand empirical evaluation on complex games and more SM-MCTS variants.  
- Suggested revisions: analyze the impact of γ experimentally and theoretically.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss tightness of the convergence bound in Theorem 4.11.  
- Suggested revisions: address potential numerical issues and mitigation strategies.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss practical limitations of the Hannan consistency requirement.
