- Paper proves that MCTS with an ε-Hannan consistent selection function converges to a subgame perfect ε-Nash equilibrium in zero-sum simultaneous move games.  
- Connects online learning theory (Hannan consistency) to MCTS convergence in simultaneous move games — a novel theoretical contribution.  
- Provides a rigorous formal framework and a main theorem (Theorem 4.11) establishing convergence from matrix games to multi-stage games.  
- Empirical results support theory: regret-matching variants with mean value propagation converge to approximate Nash equilibria.  
- Identifies a practical insight that propagating mean values (vs. current samples) is important for theory and has marginal impact on convergence speed.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear definitions, consistent notation, and logical progression of results.  
- Analysis assumes a complete tree is built before convergence analysis, which conflicts with typical incremental MCTS implementations and weakens practical relevance.  
- No finite-time or convergence rate bounds are provided; the paper only proves eventual convergence, limiting practical applicability.  
- Empirical comparisons are limited; missing comparisons to algorithms with known convergence guarantees (e.g., CFR variants).  
- Worst-case analysis is restricted to tiny games (depth 2, branching factor 2), raising questions about scalability to larger, realistic games.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution that fills a literature gap, despite practical limitations regarding rates and incremental tree building.  
- Recommendation: Accept — contribution is significant and publication-worthy despite noted omissions.  
- Specific suggestions: more thoroughly address incremental tree construction in analysis, state limitations of worst-case analysis and scalability, add comparisons to CFR-based algorithms, and clarify how theory guides practical MCTS design.
