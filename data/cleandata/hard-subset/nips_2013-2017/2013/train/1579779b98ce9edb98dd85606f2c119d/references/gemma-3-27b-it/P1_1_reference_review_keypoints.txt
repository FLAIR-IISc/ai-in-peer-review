- Paper proves convergence of SM-MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games: ε-Hannan consistent action selection yields subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium; instantiations with Regret Matching and Exp3 and empirical validation provided.
- Addresses a literature gap by providing theoretical convergence guarantees for SM-MCTS, contrasting prior empirical-only SM-MCTS work and non-convergent UCB-based methods.
- Leverages online learning (-Hannan consistency) to build the theoretical foundation and positions work against prior simultaneous-move and sequential MCTS research.
- Main strength: rigorous, general theoretical proof of convergence for a broad SM-MCTS template.
- Strength: framework generality allows any ε-Hannan consistent regret minimizer to be used.
- Strength: clear presentation of theory with stepwise inductive proofs and useful lemmas.
- Strength: empirical results support theoretical claims and include worst-case game experiments.
- Strength: authors acknowledge limitations and suggest future work; paper is well written and organized.
- Weakness: no analysis or bounds on convergence rate, limiting practical guidance.
- Weakness: practical implications of choosing ε are not discussed, leaving uncertainty about real-world performance.
- Weakness: technical complexity of proof may reduce accessibility to non-specialists.
- Weakness: experiments show limited novelty and do not clearly outperform existing SM-MCTS baselines or explore more complex scenarios.
- Weakness: minor formatting and reference inconsistencies (e.g., reference [17]).
- For acceptance: provides crucial theoretical foundation, general framework, rigorous proof, and supporting experiments addressing an open question.
- Against acceptance: lack of convergence rate analysis, limited experimental novelty, and potentially inaccessible proof complexity.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions to discuss ε implications, explore convergence rate avenues, and fix formatting issues.
