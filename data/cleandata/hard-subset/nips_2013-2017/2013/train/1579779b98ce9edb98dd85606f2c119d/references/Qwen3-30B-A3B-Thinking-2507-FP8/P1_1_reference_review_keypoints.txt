- Addresses gap: proves convergence of MCTS in two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move games.  
- Main result: MCTS with an ε-Hannan consistent selection function converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Novel connection: links online learning (Hannan consistency) to MCTS convergence in simultaneous-move settings.  
- Proof approach: builds from matrix games to extensive-form games via induction, handling bounded errors and composition across tree levels.  
- Empirical validation: experiments with regret matching and Exp3 support theoretical claims and show comparable convergence rates to existing MCTS variants.  
- Builds on prior work: extends MCTS convergence theory from sequential to simultaneous-move games and contrasts with negative results for UCT.  
- Strength — theoretical contribution: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Strength — rigorous proof: careful, structured proofs addressing key technical challenges.  
- Strength — empirical support: well-designed experiments analyzing value propagation and worst-case convergence.  
- Strength — clarity of goal: clearly identifies the literature gap and situates contributions.  
- Strength — honest limitations: authors acknowledge lack of finite-time convergence bounds.  
- Weakness — practical implications: limited discussion of real-world performance and time-constrained behavior versus UCT.  
- Weakness — definition clarity: needs more intuitive explanation why Hannan consistency is the right property.  
- Weakness — comparisons: lacks direct empirical comparison of convergence rates against best-performing UCT variants.  
- Weakness — supplementary proofs: critical proofs are in supplementary material not available to reviewers.  
- Weakness — speculative conjecture: conjecture about extending analysis to other algorithms feels unsupported.  
- Quality: technically sound, well-supported by theory and experiments, carefully distinguishes proved vs. unproved claims.  
- Clarity: generally well-written with consistent notation, but the induction step from matrix to extensive games needs more intuition.  
- Originality: novel application of Hannan consistency to MCTS and first convergence guarantees for simultaneous-move games.  
- Significance: important theoretical foundation with practical relevance for security, general game playing, and related domains.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance based on significance and soundness.  
- Pro acceptance summary: first formal guarantee, novel online-learning link, rigorous proofs, empirical support, fills literature gap.  
- Con acceptance summary: limited practical discussion, definition ambiguity, sparse comparisons to UCT, speculative conjecture.  
- Minor suggestions: add intuitive explanation of Hannan consistency, compare to UCT in experiments, clarify theoretical vs. practical rates, discuss computational overhead, and mention generalization to imperfect-information games.
