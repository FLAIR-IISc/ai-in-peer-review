- Paper proves convergence guarantees for Monte Carlo Tree Search in simultaneous-move games using a -Hannan consistent selection function, converging to a subgame-perfect -Nash equilibrium.  
- Presents a general SM-MCTS template that integrates regret-minimizing procedures as selection mechanisms.  
- Theoretical proof strategy builds from matrix games to extensive-form games and is well-structured.  
- Empirical validation with Regret Matching and Exp3 shows convergence rates comparable to existing SM-MCTS algorithms.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear definitions, background, and a concise algorithmic description.  
- Authors appropriately situate the work within existing literature on MCTS, regret minimization, and game theory.  
- Main weakness: theoretical results guarantee convergence but give little insight into convergence rates, limiting practical applicability.  
- Analysis relies on the simplifying assumption of a complete tree, raising concerns about how quickly the full tree is built in practice.  
- Impact of the exploration parameter γ on convergence speed and equilibrium quality is insufficiently investigated.  
- Worst-case empirical results may be biased by selecting the most difficult games per method and lack a rigorous worst-case analysis.  
- Potential numerical stability issues in regret calculations (especially for Exp3 in deep/complex games) are not fully addressed.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution that fills a critical gap and is worthy of publication despite practical limitations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical result, general provable framework, empirical validation, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing convergence rate analysis, reliance on complete-tree assumption, and limited exploration-parameter study.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept (7/10) with a request to address convergence rate and practical considerations in future work.
