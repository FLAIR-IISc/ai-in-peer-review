- Studies MCTS for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proposes an SM-MCTS template using ε-Hannan-consistent selection with exploration.
- Main theorem: with mean-value backups and guaranteed exploration, average strategies converge almost surely to a subgame-perfect (ε D^2 + δ)-Nash equilibrium.
- Proof approach: reduce to HC convergence in repeated matrix games, extend to repeated games with bounded payoff errors, and inductively compose errors up the tree.
- Empirical instantiations use Regret Matching and Exp3, showing convergence on random and small hard instances; mean-backup variants converge slightly slower but reach similar exploitability under persistent exploration.
- Novelty: first general asymptotic convergence guarantee tying HC selection to subgame-perfect approximate NE for simultaneous-move MCTS.
- Places work relative to prior art: fills a gap where UCT can fail in simultaneous moves, leverages online-learning results, and contrasts with exact solvers and CFR-style methods.
- Strength: technically sound asymptotic result with a natural reduction and induction, and clear identification of when mean backups and exploration are required.
- Strength: empirical results are consistent with theory and include analysis of worst-case small instances.
- Limitation: restrictive assumptions (two-player zero-sum, perfect information, persistent exploration, mean backups) reduce practical applicability.
- Limitation: approximation bound O(D^2 ε) is likely pessimistic and no finite-time convergence rates are provided.
- Limitation: experimental evaluation is modest (small depths/branching, only RM/Exp3) and lacks comparisons to stronger baselines like simultaneous alpha-beta, double oracle, or CFR.
- Clarity issues: some algorithmic details are ambiguous (Select returning joint vs independent actions, RetVal role, mean vs last-sample backups) and need clearer exposition in the main text.
- Suggestion: include more proof sketches in the main text and explicitly state exploration models for RM and Exp3 and their impact on asymptotic policies.
- Originality: moderate-to-high—repeated-game-with-error lemma and inductive composition are neat, natural contributions.
- Significance: provides much-needed theoretical support for SM-MCTS and will interest researchers, though practical impact is tempered by restrictive assumptions and lack of finite-time bounds.
- Pros summary: clear theoretical contribution, general template applicable to multiple HC learners, and empirical alignment with theory.
- Cons summary: asymptotic and potentially loose bounds, need for mean backups and persistent exploration, small-scale experiments, and clarity issues.
- Reviewer suggestions: try to reduce depth dependence (D^2 → O(D) if possible), show necessity or counterexamples for last-sample backups, analyze decaying exploration, provide finite-time rates, and expand empirical evaluations with confidence intervals and stronger baselines.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable theoretical step likely to spur follow-up work; reviewer confidence medium-high.
