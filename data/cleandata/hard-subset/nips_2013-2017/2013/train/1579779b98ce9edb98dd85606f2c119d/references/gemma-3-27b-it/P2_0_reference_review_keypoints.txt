- Summary: Proposes a general MCTS template using regret-minimizing selection for zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proves convergence to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium under ε-Hannan consistency and exploration.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant gap by providing the first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Strength: Template is general and allows plugging in various regret-minimizing procedures.  
- Strength: Convergence proof is mathematically rigorous, using ε-Hannan consistency with a clear inductive argument up the tree.  
- Strength: Empirical validation with regret matching and Exp3 supports theoretical findings and shows competitive convergence.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written with precise definitions and understandable algorithm descriptions.  
- Weakness: No theoretical analysis of convergence rate; only asymptotic ε-convergence is shown.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited to random and constructed worst-case games; lacks diverse, established benchmarks.  
- Weakness: Exploration parameter γ is fixed and there is no sensitivity analysis of its effect.  
- Weakness: Practical implications of converging to an ε-Nash equilibrium (nonzero ε) are under-discussed.  
- Weakness: Strong assumption of perfect information; extension to imperfect-information games is not addressed.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of how this work builds on or extends existing simultaneous-move MCTS algorithms (refs [11,12,20]) and how analysis might generalize.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable theoretical contribution with a significant convergence proof, though experiments and rate analysis need strengthening.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Fills an important theoretical gap and gives a rigorous, general, and practically relevant MCTS template with supporting experiments and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Lacks convergence-rate analysis, has limited experiments, offers little practical guidance on ε, and needs deeper connections to prior algorithms.  
- Rating: Accept (with minor revisions).  
- Recommendation: Accept but request revisions to discuss ε’s practical impact and selection, broaden experiments to more game instances, analyze γ sensitivity, and outline extensions to imperfect-information games and other algorithms.
