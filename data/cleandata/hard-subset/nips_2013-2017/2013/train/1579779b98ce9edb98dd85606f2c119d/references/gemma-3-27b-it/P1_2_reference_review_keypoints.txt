- Problem: Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Method: Proposes a general MCTS template using regret-minimizing selection functions; Hannan-consistent selection implies convergence to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirics: Validates with Regret Matching and Exp3, showing convergence speeds comparable to existing MCTS methods.  
- Prior work: Correctly situates within MCTS and regret-minimization literature and notes Shafiei et al.'s negative UCB result.  
- Strength — novelty: First formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Strength — generality: Template works with any Hannan-consistent regret-minimizer.  
- Strength — proof: Rigorous reduction to repeated matrix games and use of Hannan consistency; detailed appendix.  
- Strength — experiments: Empirical validation and worst-case analysis support the theory.  
- Strength — presentation: Generally clear writing and organization.  
- Weakness — rates: No theoretical bounds on convergence rate (only asymptotic convergence).  
- Weakness — approximation tightness: Guarantees a (D^2 + δ)-Nash equilibrium; D^2 factor may be large for deep games.  
- Weakness — empirical scope: Limited set of experiments; needs broader/complex game evaluations.  
- Weakness — practicality: Lacks discussion of implementation, memory, and computational complexity.  
- Weakness — exposition: Algorithm 1 is verbose and contains redundant explanations.  
- Pro arguments: Fundamental theoretical result, flexible template, strong theoretical grounding.  
- Con arguments: Missing convergence-rate analysis, potentially loose approximation factor, limited empirical evaluation.  
- Overall: Strong paper with significant contribution; weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: Discuss limitations and future directions for convergence rates; comment on tightness/improvability of the D^2 bound; streamline Algorithm 1.
