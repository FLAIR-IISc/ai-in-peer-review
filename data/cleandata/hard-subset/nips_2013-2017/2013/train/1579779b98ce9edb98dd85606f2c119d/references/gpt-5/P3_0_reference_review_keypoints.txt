- Proposes SM-MCTS: at each node select joint actions with any ε–Hannan-consistent (HC) algorithm with guaranteed exploration and back up either sample outcomes or node means.  
- Main theorem: empirical/average strategies converge a.s. to an (ε D^2 + δ)-Nash equilibrium for tree depth D (Theorem 4.11).  
- Proof strategy: reduce internal-node learning to repeated matrix games with bounded payoff error, show HC preserved under uniform exploration, and HC in bounded-error games implies approximate NE.  
- Empirical evaluation: compares mean- vs. sample-backup variants using regret-matching and Exp3 on random and small worst-case games; mean backups slow convergence modestly.  
- Novelty: first convergence guarantee for MCTS-style algorithms in simultaneous-move perfect-information games using local HC selection and mean backups.  
- Generality: result applies to any ε–HC selection rule (e.g., regret matching, Exp3) and emphasizes necessary guaranteed exploration.  
- Strength: clear, modular theoretical framing leveraging online learning/HC guarantees.  
- Strength: subgame-perfect convergence guarantee addressing a known gap for SM-MCTS.  
- Weakness: the (ε D^2 + δ) bound is likely loose; an O(D ε) bound appears plausible with refined induction.  
- Weakness: convergence proof depends on returning node means rather than standard sample backups; no theoretical guarantee for sample-backup variant.  
- Weakness: experiments are small-scale, lack standard benchmarks, wall-clock comparisons, and comparisons to strong baselines (UCT variants, alpha-beta, double oracle).  
- Weakness: the claim about lack of convergence guarantees should be qualified—CFR/MCCFR provide sampling-based convergence in general EFGs, though they differ from classic MCTS.  
- Weakness: limited modeling scope — excludes chance nodes, >2 players, and imperfect information without discussion of extensions.  
- Clarity issues: some pseudocode and notation are hard to parse (line numbers, RetVal/u1 consistency); Theorem 4.11 seems to omit ε in the bound (typo).  
- Technical gap: no proof for sample-backup variant; suggestions include importance weighting or martingale techniques to potentially restore guarantees.  
- Suggested improvements: tighten the D^2 bound to O(D ε) if possible, analyze sample backups, incorporate chance nodes, and extend experiments to benchmarks.  
- Related work: paper should better contrast with CFR/MCCFR (sampling, backups, complexity, assumptions) and cite relevant works (e.g., Zinkevich et al., Lanctot).  
- Quality assessment: theory is plausible and grounded in HC/online learning, but constants and sample-backup coverage need more rigor; experiments support asymptotic claims but lack practical assessment.  
- Significance: theoretically important for SM-MCTS design; practical impact limited by mean-backup requirement, loose bounds, and sparse experiments.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — fills a theoretical gap but needs stronger analysis, clarifications, and broader experiments.
