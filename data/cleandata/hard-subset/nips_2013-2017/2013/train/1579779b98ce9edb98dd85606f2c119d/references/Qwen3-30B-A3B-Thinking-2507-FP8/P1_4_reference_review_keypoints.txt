- Paper provides first formal convergence analysis of MCTS in zero-sum simultaneous-move perfect-information games, proving ε-Hannan consistent selection leads to subgame perfect ε-Nash equilibrium and empirically validates with Regret Matching and Exp3.

- Theoretical contribution: first formal convergence guarantees for simultaneous-move MCTS with an elegant induction-based proof from leaves upward.

- Generality/flexibility: template works with any ε-Hannan consistent selection procedure, making results broadly applicable.

- Empirical validation: experiments support theory and compare mean propagation vs. current sample value, showing implementation impacts convergence speed.

- Clear presentation: well-organized paper with precise definitions, logical flow, and effective figures.

- Appropriate scope: addresses a recognized gap in the literature relevant to algorithmic game theory and RL communities.

- Limited empirical scope: experiments are small-scale (depth ≤2, branching ≤2) and use random instances, lacking larger practical benchmarks.

- Lack of finite-time bounds: convergence is proven asymptotically but no finite-time guarantees are provided for practitioners.

- Missing practical considerations: paper omits discussion of implementation costs (regret table complexity) and effect of exploration parameter γ on speed.

- Limited comparison with state-of-the-art: empirical comparisons do not sufficiently benchmark against recent simultaneous-move MCTS algorithms.

- Limited discussion of limitations: lacks analysis of how game structure affects convergence rates or possible failure modes.

- Quality: technically sound with rigorous proofs and supportive empirical results.

- Clarity: clearly written with well-presented theorems and helpful figures linking theory and experiments.

- Originality: novel contribution bridging a known gap—prior MCTS work focused on sequential games.

- Significance: important theoretical implications that justify MCTS use in simultaneous-move games and likely influence future research.

- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance based on significance, sound analysis, and supporting experiments.

- Specific comment: add a direct empirical comparison table of convergence rates against specific algorithms to bolster claims.

- Specific comment: explain why many existing MCTS variants might also satisfy conditions to converge to ε-Nash equilibrium.

- Specific comment: extend or discuss worst-case analysis beyond depth-2 to indicate scaling behavior.

- Specific comment: provide a more detailed explanation of error accumulation across levels in the proof of Theorem 4.11.

- Specific comment: explicitly connect "value of the game" used in theory to the exploitability metric used in experiments.
