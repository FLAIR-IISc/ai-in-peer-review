- Studies SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum perfect-information games with simultaneous moves and proposes an SM-MCTS template using ε–Hannan-consistent selection with guaranteed exploration and mean or sample backups.  
- Main theorem: with ε–HC selection, guaranteed exploration, and mean backups, average strategies converge a.s. to a subgame-perfect (ε D^2 + δ)-Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof builds on no-regret convergence in matrix games, a robustness lemma for repeated matrix games with bounded payoff error, and induction over the game tree to control error propagation.  
- Empirical sanity checks compare mean vs sample backups and RM vs Exp3 on small random and worst-case depth-2 games; convergence observed in all cases with mean backup slightly slower.  
- Context: UCT lacks guarantees in simultaneous-move games; CFR offers different guarantees but is not MCTS-style; exact backward induction exists but is impractical for deep trees.  
- Claimed novelty: first general convergence guarantees for an MCTS-style method to approximate NE in simultaneous-move perfect-information zero-sum EFGs using HC selection.  
- Theory quality: high-level argument is sound; reduction to repeated bounded-error matrix games and induction is appropriate; D^2 dependence likely loose.  
- Important theoretical requirements identified: guaranteed exploration and mean-value backups.  
- Some key proofs deferred to the appendix; assertions about full-tree expansion and harmlessness of early statistics need careful justification.  
- Experiments are limited and correctly scoped as sanity checks, but lack larger benchmarks and comparisons to search baselines.  
- No finite-time/sample-complexity bounds are provided.  
- Paper is mostly clear and organized; notation is dense in places and a few typos/formatting issues exist.  
- Suggestion to state all assumptions (e.g., mean backup, exploration) explicitly in Theorem 4.11 and summarize constants in bounds.  
- Originality: novel combination of HC selection in SM-MCTS and use of bounded-error repeated-game lemma to control backup noise.  
- Significance: theoretically meaningful for a niche class of games, but practical impact limited by asymptotic results, loose D^2 scaling, and small experiments.  
- Pros: first SM-MCTS convergence guarantees, general HC template, clear identification of required ingredients, clean proof structure, and consistent empirical results.  
- Cons: asymptotic-only results, pessimistic/loose error constants and D^2 scaling, reliance on mean backups (slower and atypical), proof assumption of full-tree expansion not matching practical incremental MCTS, and limited experimental scope.  
- Key questions for authors: provide finite-time rates; clarify whether mean-backup is essential or if sample/smoothed backups can work; prove full-tree expansion under exploration; discuss updating internals during exploration; expand experiments to larger benchmarks and comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid and novel theoretical contribution with limited empirical scope and asymptotic guarantees.
