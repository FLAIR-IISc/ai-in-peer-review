- Studies two-player zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves (stacked matrix games) and proposes a general SM-MCTS template.  
- Selection policy at each node can be any ε-Hannan consistent (HC) algorithm with guaranteed exploration (e.g., Regret Matching, Exp3).  
- Main theoretical result: with mean-value backpropagation, SM-MCTS converges almost surely to a subgame-perfect ε·D^2 + δ Nash equilibrium after full tree exploration.  
- Proof strategy: (i) HC ⇒ empirical strategies approximate NE in repeated matrix games; (ii) extends to bounded-observation-error repeated games; (iii) induction over depth shows error accumulation.  
- Empirical comparison of RM and Exp3, with/without mean backprop; all variants converge, mean backprop slightly slower.  
- Novelty: first general convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS to approximate subgame-perfect NE under mild selector assumptions.  
- Connects online learning (HC algorithms) and MCTS, analogous to regret-to-NE links in normal-form/EFGs.  
- Theory is well-structured and plausible; mean-backprop requirement is clearly identified as necessary for analysis.  
- Assumption of eventual full tree expansion under guaranteed exploration is reasonable and argued in the appendix.  
- Experiments illustrate trends and backprop comparisons but are limited in scale and do not measure finite-time rates.  
- Clarity is good; concrete descriptions of RM and Exp3 instantiations provided.  
- Suggestion to clarify differences between “guaranteed exploration” variant (Section 3) and A* exploration wrapper (Section 4).  
- Suggest to state early that convergence theorem requires RetVal = Xh/nh and explain why sample returns cause unbounded bias.  
- Recommend tightening notation for empirical frequencies (σ̂) vs average strategies (σ̄) and clarify which is evaluated experimentally.  
- Practical significance: provides principled foundation for SM-MCTS in games like Goofspiel and simultaneous-move GGP variants.  
- Limitations: asymptotic only—no finite-time/sample-complexity bounds provided.  
- Weakness: ε·D^2 error scaling seems loose and discussion on tightening constants is limited.  
- Practical concern: mean backprop deviates from common MCTS practice; implications for variance, bias, and efficiency need deeper empirical study.  
- Experiments are small-scale and lack comparisons to strong simultaneous-move baselines (e.g., CFR/CFR+ adaptations).  
- Some implementation/exploration details deferred to appendix and inconsistent treatment between sections.  
- Open questions: can node-mean convergence rates yield explicit finite-time bounds?  
- Open question: is D^2 dependence tight, or can variance reduction or depth-dependent exploration reduce it to O(D)?  
- Open question: can low-variance estimators (importance-weighting/control variates) preserve proof while improving empirical speed?  
- Open question: sensitivity of results to exploration schedule γ, including annealing to zero.  
- Minor suggestions: clarify RM exploration vs A* usage, sketch full-expansion proof in main text, report wall-clock mean vs sample backprop, discuss CFR comparisons.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel general convergence guarantee, solid theoretical contribution, bridges online learning and MCTS, empirical support aligns with theory.  
- Cons for acceptance: no finite-time guarantees, reliance on mean backprop and small experiments limit practical impact, limited experimental baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept; paper advances theoretical understanding but would benefit from stronger finite-time/empirical analysis and clearer exploration/backprop details.
