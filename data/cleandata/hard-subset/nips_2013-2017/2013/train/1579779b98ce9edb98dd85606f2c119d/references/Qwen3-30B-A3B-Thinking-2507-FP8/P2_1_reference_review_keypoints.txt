- Addresses a theoretical gap: proves SM-MCTS with ε-Hannan consistent selection converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium in two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move perfect-information games.

- Three main contributions: formal SM-MCTS framework, convergence proof using ε-Hannan consistent algorithms, and empirical validation showing mean propagation converges (slower than current sample propagation).

- Quality: provides a rigorous, well-structured proof (lemmas 4.4–4.10, induction up the tree) and complements theory with experiments while acknowledging limits.

- Originality: novel application of ε-Hannan consistency from online learning to obtain the first convergence guarantees for simultaneous-move MCTS.

- Significance: fills an important theoretical gap, justifies empirical MCTS success in these games, and opens directions for future theoretical work.

- Clarity: paper is well-organized, with precise definitions, a clear algorithm description, and coherent progression from matrix to extensive-form games.

- Empirical limitation: experiments use uniformly random games and limited runs (100), so identified "worst-case" games and generality are not fully established.

- No finite-time bounds: convergence is asymptotic to an ε-NE, but the paper lacks practical finite-time convergence guarantees.

- Practical implications under-discussed: insufficient analysis of why mean propagation is slower and how this affects resource-constrained real-world use.

- Unsubstantiated conjecture: authors conjecture many existing MCTS variants also converge to ε-NE but provide no supporting evidence or sketches.

- Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution with solid writing and experiments; limitations are reasonable for a first analysis.

- Recommendation: accept — significant, high-quality, and original work that advances understanding of MCTS in simultaneous-move games.

- Final thought: paper successfully bridges practice and theory and motivates future work on convergence rates and broader empirical evaluation.
