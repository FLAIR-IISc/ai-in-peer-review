Summary and relation to prior work
The paper studies Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) in two-player zero-sum extensive-form games (EFGs) with perfect information and simultaneous moves (“stacked matrix games”). It proposes a template SM-MCTS in which both players at each node use a Hannan-consistent (HC) online learning algorithm (e.g., regret matching or Exp3) with explicit exploration. The main theoretical result states that if the algorithm returns mean rewards up the tree (rather than single-sample returns) and ensures each joint action is attempted infinitely often, then the empirical/average strategies converge almost surely to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium, with an asymptotic error bound that scales as O(ε D^2), where D is the tree depth. The empirical section compares RM vs. “RM with mean backups” and Exp3 variants on small random and adversarially selected games, showing apparent convergence, with mean-backup variants slightly slower.

This connects to classical HC guarantees in repeated matrix games (Hart & Mas-Colell; Auer et al.) and to MCTS in sequential perfect-information games (UCT; Kocsis & Szepesvári). It also relates to prior simultaneous-move MCTS variants and to results that UCT may not converge in simultaneous-move settings (Shafiei et al.). Compared to exact backward induction with LP/double-oracle pruning, the approach targets simulation-based anytime performance without domain evaluators.

Strengths
- Addresses an important gap: asymptotic convergence guarantees for MCTS-like methods in simultaneous-move perfect-information games.
- General template: any ε-HC selection rule with guaranteed exploration can be used; covers RM and Exp3.
- Subgame-perfect convergence (not just root value), with a clean inductive argument leveraging repeated game with bounded error.
- Empirical section, albeit limited, is consistent with the theory and checks the effect of mean backups.

Weaknesses and detailed concerns
Technical correctness and algorithmic details
- Algorithm 1 is underspecified and inconsistent:
  - RetVal(u1, Xh, nh) is defined to sometimes return u1 and sometimes Xh/nh, but line 9 returns RetVal(u1, Xh′, nh′) (child stats), and line 15 returns RetVal(u1, Xh, nh) (parent stats). This is not standard MCTS backup, and the intent (mean of all returns from the subtree vs. single sample) is unclear. Please precisely specify the backup rule used in the proof and in each experiment.
  - “Choose one of the previously unselected (i, j)” lacks a policy (uniform? fixed order?). The expansion policy affects whether every joint action is eventually sampled.
- Regret-matching update is dubious: x(h,i,j)=Xhij if (i,j)≠(i1,j2) else u1. Using cumulative sums Xhij (not means) mixes scales and depends on visit counts, which can blow up regrets and break HC properties. If the intention was to use the current estimate of the payoff for (i,j), it should be the mean Xhij/nhij or a last-sample estimate; please clarify.
- The Exp3 description deviates from standard Exp3: softmax on wh,i with an extra (1−γ) term plus γ-uniform is not the canonical Exp3 sampling rule; the learning-rate choice η=γ/|A| and the accumulation xh,i ← xh,i + u1/σ(h,i) resemble importance-weighting but the combination with softmax needs justification or a citation. As written, it is not obvious that the stated procedure is HC.
- “Guaranteed exploration” for joint actions requires limt→∞ tij=∞ for all (i,j). Because actions are sampled independently per player, this hinges on both marginals having support; but in a large branching-factor product space, visiting all joint actions in practice is unrealistic. The paper claims this holds “almost surely,” but the proof is deferred and not summarized; at minimum, clarify the conditions (fixed γ>0 at each node suffices) and the impact on sample complexity.
- The main theorem statement uses “average strategies (σ̂1(t), σ̂2(t)),” but earlier σ̂ denotes empirical frequencies and σ̄ denotes averages. Lemma 4.5 asserts they coincide asymptotically, but the notation mix-up is confusing and needs correction.
- The D^2 factor in the error bound is hand-wavy in the main text (“players can gain 2d by deviating”; “errors … in the order of d”) and the tightness is not discussed. A brief sketch quantifying how c accumulates per level and why it yields D^2 (versus, e.g., O(D)) would greatly help. If the D^2 is loose, say so.

Scope and completeness of theory
- The reliance on mean backups is critical to the proof but the empirics indicate it slows convergence; the practical recommendation is thus unclear. Can the convergence be established with single-sample backups (perhaps with delayed averaging at nodes) or with standard tree-policy backups?
- No finite-time bounds are given, despite invoking HC algorithms with known rates; an O(1/√t)-type finite-sample result (even at leaves) would strengthen the contribution.
- The assumption that the “tree will eventually be complete with probability 1” under the given expansion rule is asserted but not shown in the main text; please provide a lemma or a precise pointer.

Empirical evaluation
- Experiments are very small scale (depth ≤ 2 in exhaustive search; random games with BF up to small values). No error bars/confidence intervals are reported; figures are referenced but not visible in the manuscript.
- The “worst-case” analysis is only over tiny discrete utilities {0,0.5,1}; no tests on standard simultaneous-move benchmarks (e.g., Goofspiel variants, Tron variants used in prior work) with controlled seeds; no comparison to established baselines beyond RM/Exp3 variants (e.g., double-oracle backward induction with pruning, DUCT variants).
- Only player-1 exploitability is reported, while the theorem claims subgame-perfect convergence; please report both players’ exploitability and subgame exploitability metrics.

Clarity and presentation
- Numerous notation/formatting issues: missing ε symbols (“-Hannan consistent,” “-NE”), truncated text (“oth.”), footnote intruding into Section 4, inconsistent σ̂ vs σ̄, undefined variables (nhij), and unclear figure references. The pseudocode is difficult to follow and needs careful editing.
- References are incomplete (missing years/venues, inconsistent formatting). Please fix (e.g., AAMAS 2011 double-oracle security games; EvoApplications; CIG; ECML 2006 UCT; Econometrica 2000 RM; SIAM J. Comput. 2002 EXP3).

Pros
- Addresses a meaningful gap: convergence guarantees for SM-MCTS.
- General template covering multiple HC selection rules and achieving subgame-perfect convergence.
- Clear high-level proof strategy (induction with bounded error at each level).

Cons
- Algorithmic details are inconsistent/possibly incorrect (RM updates, backup rule, Exp3 variant).
- Heavy reliance on mean backups; practical relevance unclear.
- No finite-time rates; asymptotic bound is loose and not well justified in main text.
- Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks statistical rigor and meaningful baselines.
- Numerous clarity/formatting/reference issues impede reproducibility.

Questions for authors
1) Precisely specify the backup used in theory and experiments; fix Algorithm 1 accordingly.
2) Correct the RM update to use properly scaled payoff estimates; justify the Exp3 variant’s HC property.
3) Provide a clear lemma guaranteeing eventual visitation of all joint actions per node under your exploration scheme.
4) Can you derive finite-time exploitability bounds using known regret rates?
5) Is the D^2 factor tight? Provide an example or a sharpened bound.

Recommendation
Lean reject. The core idea is interesting and potentially impactful, but the current manuscript has too many technical and presentation issues to assess correctness confidently. With clarified algorithms, corrected updates, stronger empirical evaluation, and improved exposition, this could become a solid contribution.
