- Paper studies SM-MCTS with ε–Hannan-consistent selection rules and proves almost-sure convergence to subgame-perfect (ε·D^2 + δ)-NE at depth D for mean-backup MCTS.
- Proof strategy: analyze repeated matrix games, extend to repeated games with bounded error, and inductively compose errors up the tree.
- Authors provide two instantiations (regret matching and Exp3) and empirical evaluation on random and small worst-case games.
- Novelty lies in lifting HC-based selection to MCTS in stacked matrix games and giving subgame-perfect convergence guarantees for simultaneous-move perfect-information games.
- The high-level theoretical argument is sound and leverages standard online learning results, but some constants and assumptions need clarification.
- Lemma constants (e.g., factor 4(c+1)+δ) are not reconciled cleanly with the main ε·D^2+δ bound; an explicit end-to-end bound tracking ε, γ, branching, and depth is needed.
- Theoretical results require backing up mean values; sample-backup (standard in MCTS) is only shown empirically to converge and lacks theoretic coverage.
- The “guaranteed exploration” assumption via γ-mixing is crucial, but the dependence of equilibrium error on γ and its interaction with ε is left implicit.
- For the RM instantiation, the update uses stored Xh,ij estimates and it is unclear whether this concrete RM variant is provably HC under the induced MCTS feedback; the theory treats HC abstractly.
- The Exp3 instantiation with importance weighting is a correct HC algorithm under bandit feedback and is more theoretically justified.
- Experiments are careful on synthetic small games but lack evaluation on standard simultaneous-move domains (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and comparisons to strong baselines like UCT variants, alpha-beta, or double-oracle.
- No finite-time convergence characterization is provided empirically (e.g., dependence on depth, branching, γ); mean-backup being slower is noted but not deeply analyzed.
- Presentation is generally clear and self-contained but has notational and editorial issues (T used twice, inconsistent pseudocode, unclear node expansion and tracking).
- RM update description must clarify whether full-information payoffs are observed or estimated and what happens when Xh,ij is uninitialized.
- Several references are incomplete or improperly formatted and related-work overlaps (e.g., Lanctot et al.) should be clarified.
- The contribution is novel and timely, filling a theoretical gap for SM-MCTS and complementing CFR-style guarantees.
- Practical impact is limited by asymptotic nature, loose constants (D^2 scaling), and lack of finite-time rates or broad empirical validation.
- Pros: principled theoretical framework linking HC learning and SM-MCTS, asymptotic subgame-perfect guarantees, simple template with natural instantiations, and supporting empirical evidence.
- Cons: theory depends on mean-backup, no finite-time bounds, limited experiments, and unclear HC guarantees for the RM variant under MCTS feedback.
- Suggested improvements: tighten error propagation to reduce D^2 factor, prove HC property for the RM update or state it’s empirical only, extend analysis to sample-backup (e.g., via martingales), add standard benchmarks and baselines, and empirically study dependence on γ and branching.
- Reviewer recommendation: Weak accept — novel and relevant theory but needs clearer assumptions, stronger empirical validation, and improved presentation.
