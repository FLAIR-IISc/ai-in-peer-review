- Paper analyzes SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move perfect-information games, proving convergence to subgame-perfect approximate NE when nodes use ε-Hannan-consistent selectors with exploration and mean-backups.  
- Empirically compares sample-backup vs mean-backup with Regret Matching and Exp3 on small random and worst-case games, showing convergence but slower with mean-backups.  
- Fills a gap in theory for simultaneous-move EFGs where prior UCT-based methods lacked convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: clear reduction from SM-MCTS to repeated (noisy) matrix games and induction over depth; template is natural and general.  
- Strength: novel — first general convergence result for MCTS in simultaneous-move perfect-information games to subgame-perfect approximate NE.  
- Strength: guarantees hold in every subgame (subgame-perfectity).  
- Strength: empirical checks corroborate theoretical convergence and compare backup variants.  
- Strength: connects MCTS analysis to online learning (Hannan consistency) and known UCB pitfalls.  
- Main weakness: inconsistent/mistyped constants — several lemmas/theorems omit ε (and possibly γ) dependence, making bounds implausible.  
- Weakness: overly pessimistic D^2 error scaling; likely analyzable down to O(D(ε+γ)) with refined arguments.  
- Weakness: proof relies on mean-backup (returning Xh/nh), which is nonstandard and empirically slower; necessity is unclear.  
- Weakness: exploration handling (mixing uniform exploration without updating base learner) may slow learning and needs justification or alternative handling.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to tiny games with utilities {0,0.5,1}; larger/structured benchmarks and comparisons (e.g., Goofspiel, CFR, simultaneous alpha-beta) would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: several notational slips and terse algorithm description; some definitions (RetVal variants) need clarification.  
- Quality: high-level proof strategy is sound and natural, but constants appear inconsistent and bounds likely improvable.  
- Clarity: overall well-written but should explicitly track ε/γ dependence, clarify mean-backup role, and state which strategy averages are used in final guarantees.  
- Originality: provides genuinely new convergence guarantees for SM-MCTS using HC node selectors.  
- Significance: legitimizes a class of methods for simultaneous-move domains; current bounds are asymptotic and loose but a meaningful first step.  
- Key author action 1: correct and unify dependence on ε and γ in Lemma 4.9, Theorem 4.10, and Theorem 4.11.  
- Key author action 2: try to tighten propagated error to O(D(ε+γ)) and explain source of D^2 scaling.  
- Key author action 3: investigate whether sample-backup convergence can be proven (e.g., via law of large numbers) or clarify why mean-backup is essential.  
- Key author action 4: justify/update exploration handling and whether base learners should be trained on exploration steps.  
- Key author action 5: expand experiments to larger/structured simultaneous-move domains and compare to CFR/alpha-beta baselines.  
- Pros summary: first rigorous analysis, general HC-selector template, subgame-perfect guarantees, connections to online learning, simple instantiations (RM/Exp3).  
- Cons summary: apparent constant mistakes and loose D^2 bound, reliance on mean-backups with worse empirical performance, limited experiments, no finite-sample rates.  
- Overall: conceptually solid and relevant; fixing constants, clarifying mean-backups, and expanding experiments would substantially strengthen the paper.
