- Main contribution: introduces an SM-MCTS template showing that ε-Hannan-consistent, exploration-guaranteed selection rules converge almost surely to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move games.
- Key technical requirement: analysis needs mean-backpropagation of accumulated rewards (not latest samples) during backprop.
- Proof strategy: reduce selection to repeated matrix games, show HC learners’ empirical/average strategies converge to approximate NE (with bounded payoff errors), and inductively propagate guarantees up the tree.
- Error scaling: approximation error grows with depth, stated as O(D^2) with constants from lemmas (e.g., factor 4(c+1)), but some constants appear loose.
- Theorem clarity issue: Theorem 4.11 seems to omit an explicit ε factor in the bound and needs clarification of exact dependence on ε, D, and exploration γ.
- Relation to prior work: fills a gap between bandit/online learning and game-tree search by providing first asymptotic convergence analysis for SM-MCTS in this setting.
- Practical instantiations: experiments use regret matching (RM) and Exp3 to validate convergence and effect of mean-backprop; mean-backprop slows convergence modestly.
- Algorithmic note: RM and Exp3 match known HC algorithms, but mean-backprop is nonstandard for MCTS and diverges from usual practice.
- Pseudocode and update clarity: several places ambiguous—RM update x(h,i,j) description unclear, Select's joint-action vs independent sampling inconsistent, and RetVal/mean vs sample usage split between text and pseudocode.
- Exploration mechanism: the “A*” exploration that does not update learner state is important to proofs but needs stronger justification and discussion of alternatives.
- Experiments limited: empirical validation is small-scale, focuses on asymptotics, lacks comparisons to strong baselines (CFR, alpha-beta, double-oracle) and larger benchmarks.
- No finite-time rates: paper provides asymptotic guarantees only; combining known regret bounds to yield finite-time guarantees is suggested but not done.
- Clarity/notation issues: be explicit about empirical vs average strategies (Lemma 4.5 needs a sketch), fix typos/grammar, and clarify when means must be used.
- Originality: novel in combining HC selection, exploration guarantees, and inductive composition to yield SM-MCTS convergence results.
- Significance limited: impact constrained by asymptotic nature, loose constants/depth dependence, and nonstandard mean-backprop requirement.
- Strengths: first rigorous convergence analysis for SM-MCTS under HC selection, clear reduction to repeated games with bounded error, principled inductive proof, practical RM/Exp3 instantiations.
- Weaknesses: asymptotic-only results, O(D^2) error with loose constants, unclear algorithmic details, limited empirical scope, and unexplored alternative exploration/update schemes.
- Suggested improvements/questions: clarify Theorem 4.11’s ε/D/γ dependence and tightness of D^2 factor; consider extending analysis to sample-backprop; provide finite-time guarantees via RM/Exp3 regret bounds; compare empirically to CFR/double-oracle/alpha-beta; tighten pseudocode and update rules; discuss practical choice and impact of exploration γ.
- Recommendation: weak accept—novel and meaningful theoretical contribution but needs tightened statements/proofs, clearer algorithms, and expanded empirical evaluation.
