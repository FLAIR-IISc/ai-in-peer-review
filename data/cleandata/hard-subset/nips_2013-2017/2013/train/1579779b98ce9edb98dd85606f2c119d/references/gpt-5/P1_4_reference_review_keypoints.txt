- Studies MCTS for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games (stacked matrix games).  
- Proposes SM-MCTS template where selection at each node is any ε–Hannan-consistent bandit with guaranteed exploration (e.g., Regret Matching, Exp3).  
- Uses mean-return backups (SM-MCTS-M) instead of most-recent-sample for the analysis.  
- Main theoretical claim: under ε-HC selection and guaranteed exploration, average strategies converge almost surely to a subgame-perfect ε‑Nash equilibrium; bound stated as O(ε D^2) (text likely omits an ε).  
- Proof strategy: reduce to repeated matrix games, extend to bounded-payoff repeated games, then induct over tree depth.  
- Technical lemmas (4.6, 4.9) reasonably extend HC properties to bounded payoff perturbations; induction plausible but constants are loose.  
- Assumes guaranteed exploration and eventual full tree expansion; proof details deferred to appendix/arXiv, weakening self-containedness.  
- Empirics compare mean vs sample backups and RM vs Exp3 on small random games and worst-case depth-2 examples; mean backups converge but are slightly slower.  
- Experiments are limited in scale, use simple payoffs, lack comparisons to strong baselines (SM-UCT, alpha-beta variants), and provide limited sensitivity/statistical analysis.  
- No finite-time/sample-complexity bounds provided; translating known HC rates (e.g., O(1/√T)) into exploitability vs iterations would strengthen the paper.  
- Writing is generally clear and well organized though notation is heavy and some references/formatting are inconsistent.  
- Theorem 4.11 appears to miss ε; dependence on ε, γ, and D should be clarified and the tree-completion argument sketched.  
- Originality: novel asymptotic convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS in simultaneous-move perfect-information games; combining HC bandits per node with mean backups is a useful theoretical insight.  
- Significance: important theoretical justification for SM-MCTS methods, but asymptotic nature and loose D^2 constants limit immediate practical guidance.  
- Pros: clear theoretical framework linking HC bandits to SM-MCTS convergence; subgame-perfect guarantee; covers multiple selection algorithms; empirical sanity checks provided.  
- Cons: asymptotic-only results, likely loose constants and missing ε in main theorem, small-scale experiments, practical slowdown from mean backups, some proofs deferred and notation issues.  
- Suggestions: correct and precisely state Theorem 4.11 (include ε, γ); investigate reducing D^2 to O(D); provide finite-time corollary using HC regret rates; clarify empirical vs average strategies; expand experiments to standard benchmarks and baselines; sketch argument that tree becomes complete a.s. under exploration.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — novel and of interest but needs revisions on statements, bounds, experiments, and some proofs.
