- Studies SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proposes SM-MCTS using Hannan-consistent (HC) selection with guaranteed exploration.  
- Key design: back up mean returns (not single samples) at each node.  
- Main theoretical result: empirical/average strategies converge almost surely to a subgame-perfect ε-NE with approximation bound O(D^2 ε) (Theorem 4.11).  
- Analysis approach: HC convergence in matrix games → extension to bounded-error repeated games → induction up the tree.  
- Instantiates framework with Regret Matching and Exp3 as HC selection algorithms.  
- Connects to prior MCTS/UCT theory (Kocsis & Szepesvári) which doesn’t cover simultaneous moves and to negative UCB observations in simultaneous games.  
- Novelty: first convergence guarantee for MCTS to approximate NE in this game class via reduction to repeated games with bounded error.  
- Theory judged plausible and technically sound; mean-backup requirement is essential to control error accumulation.  
- Proof relies on eventual full tree expansion under guaranteed exploration (details in appendix).  
- Final bound is conservative; dependence on depth and exploration parameter discussed but loose.  
- Experiments show mean-backup converges slightly slower than sample-backup; both reach an exploration-imposed exploitability floor.  
- Empirical evaluation limited to small synthetic and worst-case games; no large or real benchmarks included.  
- Theoretical guarantee applies only to mean backups; practical SM-MCTS often uses sample backups and lacks a proof.  
- Clarity: overall well organized and clear pseudocode, but some notation inconsistencies, typos, and unclear RetVal/strategy reporting choices.  
- Suggested literature addition: regret-minimization in extensive-form games (e.g., CFR) for positioning.  
- Strength: general template applicable to any HC selection with exploration and clear modular proof structure.  
- Strength: provides practical guidance to use HC selection with exploration and mean backups for theoretical convergence.  
- Weakness: asymptotic-only guarantees; no finite-time rates provided.  
- Weakness: D^2 ε bound likely loose; need discussion if O(D ε) or node-dependent bounds possible.  
- Weakness: mean-backup requirement departs from common practice; either prove sample-backup convergence or give more empirical evidence.  
- Weakness: limited experimental scope; recommend adding benchmarks like Tron or Goofspiel and comparisons to alpha-beta/double-oracle.  
- Weakness: need to clarify dependence on exploration γ and that exploitability cannot drop below a γ-dependent floor.  
- Minor: ensure consistent definitions of empirical vs. average strategies, clean pseudocode, and fix typos/notation.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, technically sound contribution filling a theoretical gap for SM-MCTS.  
- Cons for acceptance: loose asymptotic bounds, mean-backup/practical mismatch, and limited experiments.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — meaningful theoretical advance despite conservative guarantees and modest empirical validation.
