- Addresses lack of theoretical guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move zero-sum games.  
- Presents a general MCTS template using regret-minimizing action selection and proves convergence to subgame perfect ε-Nash equilibria with ε-Hannan consistent selectors.  
- First formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, filling an important gap in the literature.  
- Strong technical quality: main theorem (4.11) proven via inductive argument from leaves to root using lemmas 4.4–4.10.  
- Links Hannan consistency in repeated matrix games to approximate Nash equilibrium convergence in full game trees.  
- Empirical evaluation supports theory: mean-propagation variants (RMM, Exp3M) converge to approximate equilibria with comparable speed and worst-case performance.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, consistent notation, logical flow from matrix games to extensive-form results; appendix contains full proofs.  
- Original contribution: novel bridge between online learning (Hannan consistency) and MCTS convergence in simultaneous moves.  
- Significant impact: provides theoretical justification for MCTS use in applications and suggests extensions (finite-time bounds, imperfect information).  
- Weaknesses: lacks finite-time convergence bounds, empirical tests limited to small games, and limited direct comparison with UCT.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
