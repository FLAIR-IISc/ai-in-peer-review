- Paper proves that SM-MCTS using any ε-Hannan consistent regret minimizer converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games.  
- Provides a general SM-MCTS template that integrates matrix-game regret minimizers as selection functions.  
- Builds on bandit/regret-minimization literature (Regret Matching, Exp3) and adapts these methods to SM-MCTS.  
- Addresses a gap in theoretical understanding of MCTS for simultaneous-move games where prior work lacked convergence guarantees.  
- Strong, rigorous theoretical analysis with proofs progressing from matrix games to multi-stage games by induction.  
- Template is general and flexible, allowing use of various regret minimization algorithms.  
- Paper is well written with precise definitions, consistent notation, and clear algorithm descriptions.  
- Empirical validation using Regret Matching and Exp3 shows practical convergence comparable to existing algorithms.  
- Theoretical results guarantee only approximate Nash equilibria and offer limited information about convergence rates; bounds may be loose for deep games.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and should include larger, more complex games and more detailed comparisons to existing MCTS methods.  
- Practical implementation issues (e.g., memory, computational cost) receive little discussion beyond numerical instability of Exp3.  
- The assumption of ε-Hannan consistency may be restrictive; robustness to approximate Hannan consistency is not explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, supported empirically, clearly written, and relevant to the community.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited convergence-rate analysis, insufficient empirical breadth, and incomplete practical considerations.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution; paper is worthy of acceptance.  
- Recommendation and scores: Accept; Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
