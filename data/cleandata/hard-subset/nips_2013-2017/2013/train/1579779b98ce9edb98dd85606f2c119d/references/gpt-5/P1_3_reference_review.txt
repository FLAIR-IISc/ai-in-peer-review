Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information extensive-form games with simultaneous moves (“stacked matrix games”). It proposes a general SM-MCTS template where, at each state, the joint move is sampled using any selection rule that is ε-Hannan consistent (HC) and guarantees exploration. The main theoretical contribution is an asymptotic convergence result: if the selection rule is ε-HC and every joint action is sampled infinitely often, then the induced SM-MCTS (with mean-value backups) converges almost surely to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium of the full extensive-form game. The proof proceeds by (i) analyzing repeated matrix games, (ii) extending to repeated matrix games with bounded payoff errors, and (iii) inductively composing these errors over the tree depth to obtain a global bound. Empirically, the paper compares two HC selection algorithms (Regret Matching and Exp3), with either current-sample or mean-value backpropagation, in random and small worst-case games, reporting convergence to low exploitability and noting that mean-propagation is slightly slower.

This builds on UCT/MCTS in perfect-information sequential games (Kocsis & Szepesvári), on simultaneous-move search (e.g., Saffidine et al.; Bosansky et al.) and on online-learning/HC methods such as Regret Matching and Exp3. Prior work (Shafiei et al.) shows UCB selection does not converge to NE in simultaneous-move games. To my knowledge, there was no prior convergence proof for MCTS in perfect-information simultaneous-move EFGs; CFR-style guarantees exist for imperfect-information EFGs but with very different updates. Thus the result fills a notable theoretical gap.

Quality:
- Theory: The proof strategy is sensible and technically careful: it leverages standard HC properties in repeated games, shows robustness to bounded payoff errors, and uses induction over depth to obtain subgame-perfect approximate equilibrium. The guaranteed-exploration modification A* is standard and the lemmas on empirical vs average strategies are appropriate. However, there are issues with constants and clarity in the final bound:
  - Theorem 4.11 as written shows “(D2 + δ)-NE” without explicitly multiplying by ε; earlier text hints at error accumulation roughly linear in depth and constants of the form 4(c+1). It is unclear whether the final bound is O(D ε), O(D^2 ε), or something else. Please state the final dependence cleanly and consistently (and include ε).
  - The theorem says “average strategies (σ̂1(t), σ̂2(t))”, but σ̂ was defined as empirical frequencies, whereas σ̄ denotes average strategies. This needs correction.
  - The step that the tree becomes fully expanded “eventually with prob. 1” is plausible under guaranteed exploration but should be stated more explicitly (e.g., a lemma that each node and joint action is visited infinitely often almost surely).
- Practical variant: The proof requires mean-backups (returning Xh/nh) instead of the current rollout payoff; this is nonstandard in MCTS and explains the need to control bias/variance for the bounded-error lemmas. The experiments suggest that both variants converge empirically, but it would help to discuss whether the theoretical requirements can be relaxed or whether a practical, provably convergent variant can keep some of the sample-backup benefits (e.g., weighted averaging).
- Experiments: The empirical section is limited (small depths/branching factors, random utilities, worst-case search at depth 2), with no larger benchmarks (e.g., Tron, Goofspiel). The results are consistent with the theory and adequately illustrate asymptotic convergence but do not test scalability, runtime, or robustness. A head-to-head with a strong SM-MCTS baseline (e.g., UCT variants known to work well empirically) would be informative, even if the baseline lacks guarantees.

Clarity:
- Generally clear and well organized, with a helpful algorithm template and precise definitions. However:
  - Fix notation inconsistencies (σ̂ vs σ̄), explicitly include ε in Theorem 4.11, and ensure the final constants are consistent throughout.
  - Clarify whether “guaranteed exploration” refers to joint-action exploration or independent exploration per player and why the latter induces infinite visits for all joint actions.
  - Slightly more intuition around why mean-backups are essential for the analysis (unbiasedness and bounded error propagation) would help the reader.
  - Several minor typos and missing details in references (some entries incomplete).

Originality:
- The core theoretical statement—convergence of SM-MCTS with HC selection to subgame-perfect approximate NE in perfect-information simultaneous-move games—appears novel and nontrivial. Using HC selection is known in other contexts (e.g., CFR), but adapting it to the SM-MCTS setting with a careful error-propagation analysis is original.

Significance:
- The result supplies a theoretical foundation for a widely used heuristic (MCTS) in a class of games where guarantees were missing, and it connects online learning (HC) with MCTS in simultaneous-move trees. This is likely to influence future algorithm design and analysis. The lack of finite-time convergence rates and limited empirical scope reduce immediate practical impact, but the asymptotic guarantee itself is important.

Pros:
- First convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS to subgame-perfect approximate NE.
- Clear, modular analysis (HC in repeated games → bounded error → tree induction).
- Applicable to multiple HC selection rules (Regret Matching, Exp3).
- Empirical results consistent with theory; shows practical variants converge.

Cons:
- Final bound unclear/inconsistent; constants likely loose (possible D vs D^2 factor).
- Requires mean-backups; theoretical/practical gap not fully resolved.
- Experiments are small-scale; no large domains or comparisons to strong baselines.
- Some notation/typo issues and incomplete references.

Questions and suggestions:
- Please state the final approximation bound precisely as a function of depth D and ε (and γ), and reconcile the D vs D^2 discussion.
- Can you provide a finite-time bound (even very loose) by composing known regret rates of RM/Exp3 with your error-propagation analysis?
- Is there a provably convergent variant that uses sample-backups but variance reduction (e.g., Polyak averaging) to retain practicality?
- Clarify the “tree fully expanded w.p.1” argument under guaranteed exploration. Does your exploration rule update statistics on exploratory steps or not? How sensitive are results to this choice?
- Consider adding results on a standard simultaneous-move benchmark (e.g., Tron, Goofspiel) and a comparison to UCT-based SM-MCTS to show practical relevance.

Overall assessment:
A solid theoretical contribution establishing convergence of SM-MCTS with HC selection in simultaneous-move perfect-information games. Despite some clarity issues in constants and limited experiments, the paper advances understanding in an area where guarantees were lacking. I lean toward acceptance, contingent on clarifying the bound, fixing notation/typos, and, if possible, modestly strengthening the empirical section.
