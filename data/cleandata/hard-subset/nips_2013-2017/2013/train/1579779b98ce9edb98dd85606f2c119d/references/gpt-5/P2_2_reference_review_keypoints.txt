- Paper analyzes SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and gives convergence guarantees using ε–Hannan-consistent selection with exploration.  
- Main contribution: a general SM-MCTS template proving almost sure convergence of average strategies to subgame-perfect approximate NE, with error scaling in game depth D.  
- Proof strategy: reduce to repeated matrix games with HC learners, extend to bounded-payoff-error games, and induct up the tree to control error propagation.  
- Key technical choice: backing up mean returns (SM-MCTS-M) instead of recent samples enables bounded-error recursion.  
- Theoretical result: subgame-perfect (ε·D^2+δ)-NE under guaranteed exploration and mean backups, derived by induction with per-level error accumulation.  
- Lemmas: two ε-HC players lead empirical strategies within v ± 2ε; bounded-error extension yields constants linear in the error.  
- Assumptions: two-player zero-sum, perfect information, finite tree, guaranteed/forced exploration, and use of mean backups.  
- Exploration rate γ degrades effective ε by +γ and creates an exploitability floor observed in experiments.  
- Experiments: modest but relevant tests comparing RM and Exp3 with/without mean backups on random and adversarial small games.  
- Empirical findings: mean backups converge slightly slower but still converge; exploitability floor matches exploration rate.  
- Novelty: first formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in perfect-information simultaneous-move settings to the reviewer’s knowledge.  
- Relation to prior work: complements UCT limitation studies and regret-minimization methods; different from CFR-style guarantees for imperfect-information games.  
- Clarity: generally well-structured with understandable proof skeleton; full proofs in appendix; some dense notation and minor editorial issues.  
- Practical limitations: asymptotic-only guarantees, no finite-time/sample complexity bounds, and likely loose O(D^2) depth dependence.  
- Theoretical practicality concern: requirement of full-tree eventual expansion and mean backups is less common in standard MCTS implementations.  
- Suggestions: add crude finite-time/rate bounds or discuss obstacles, clarify mean-backup necessity and possible alternatives, and state γ’s effect explicitly in the main theorem.  
- Experimental suggestions: include one or two domain benchmarks beyond synthetic small games to strengthen empirical validation.  
- Questions for authors: can D^2 be improved to O(D); does analysis extend to running-average action values without storing full matrices; can non-mean backup variants be analyzed under variance control?  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, sound, and general theoretical contribution with clear implications for game search and learning in games.  
- Rejection arguments: lack of finite-time bounds, loose asymptotic constants, practical gap from mean backups and exploration, and limited empirical scope.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical advance leaning toward acceptance if authors clarify mean-backup assumption and explicitly address γ’s impact in the main theorem.
