- Paper proves that SM-MCTS with ε-Hannan-consistent selection and mean backups converges a.s. to a subgame-perfect (D^2 ε + δ)-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move perfect-information games.  
- Proof strategy: (i) ε-HC learners converge to approx. minimax in repeated matrix games, (ii) extend to repeated games with bounded observation error, (iii) inductively compose bounds up the tree yielding D^2 factor.  
- Empirically compares mean-backup vs sample-backup using Regret Matching and Exp3 on random small games and tiny worst-case games, finding convergence in practice and slightly slower convergence for mean backups.  
- Main theoretical strengths: sound convergence result under assumptions, natural reduction to repeated games with bounded error, requirement of exploration is standard, and subgame-perfect conclusion strengthens prior work.  
- Notational/constant issues: several lemmas appear to omit ε factors (e.g., “v + 2” vs “v + 2ε”); constants must be clarified and made consistent.  
- Backup rule limitation: proof critically uses mean returns (SM-MCTS-M) and does not cover standard single-sample backups; either extend theory to sample backups or precisely state where the proof fails.  
- Depth dependence concern: the D^2 ε bound seems loose; authors should discuss whether O(D ε) is attainable or provide a lower bound showing D^2 is necessary.  
- Tree completion gap: the claim that guaranteed exploration implies the tree is eventually fully expanded is plausible but not formally shown in the main text; provide a short formal argument or appendix pointer.  
- Experimental limitations: small-scale experiments, no scalability tests, no comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., alpha-beta variants or CFR adaptations), and limited benchmark coverage.  
- Exploitability measurement issues: only player 1 exploitability was measured and a fixed γ creates a floor; report symmetric exploitability and study exploration annealing.  
- Clarity suggestions: enforce consistent use of ε throughout, clarify pseudocode about backup choice and independent per-player selection, and precisely define “guaranteed exploration” and how exploration updates (or not) affect learners.  
- Originality: likely the first formal asymptotic convergence guarantees for SM-MCTS with broad no-regret selectors; integration of repeated-game decomposition is novel.  
- Significance: fills an important theory/practice gap and can guide future work, but immediate practical impact is limited by asymptotic bounds and mean-backup requirement.  
- Pros: first convergence guarantee to subgame-perfect approx. NE, modular repeated-game analysis, covers common learners with exploration, experiments consistent with theory.  
- Cons: asymptotic-only results with no finite-time rates, requires mean backups (theory gap for standard variant), loose D^2 dependence, small-scale experiments and missing baselines, and some notational inconsistencies.  
- Suggested improvements: fix ε constants in lemmas, extend or delimit theory for sample backups, analyze/improve D-dependence or show lower bounds, strengthen experiments (larger games, benchmarks, baselines, symmetric exploitability, annealed exploration), and formalize the tree-expansion argument.  
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution with caveats; lean toward acceptance if presentation issues are fixed and limitations are clearly acknowledged.
