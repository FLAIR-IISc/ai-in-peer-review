- Paper proves convergence of an MCTS template in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games using -Hannan consistent selection.  
- Authors show MCTS converges to a subgame-perfect -Nash equilibrium when sufficient exploration is ensured.  
- The MCTS template is general and can incorporate various regret-minimizing procedures.  
- Theoretical approach leverages established Hannan consistency and online learning theory.  
- Empirical validation with Regret Matching and Exp3 shows convergence comparable to recent algorithms.  
- Correctly positions work relative to prior MCTS and simultaneous-move literature and double-oracle methods.  
- Main strength: formal convergence guarantee filling a theoretical gap for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Strength: method generality allows different regret-minimizers to be used within the template.  
- Strength: sound methodology grounded in Hannan consistency and clear presentation with thorough literature review.  
- Weakness: no concrete bounds on convergence rate, limiting practical applicability.  
- Weakness: strong assumption of -Hannan consistency may be unrealistic in practice and needs sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: error bound scales as D^2 with game depth, which may be prohibitive for deep games.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to random and worst-case games; more realistic domains and broader comparisons are needed.  
- Weakness: numerical stability concerns (e.g., Exp3 overflow) are noted but not deeply addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contribution, general template, empirical support, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing convergence-rate bounds, strong assumptions, and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable theoretical advance; weaknesses manageable and do not outweigh contributions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, addressing sensitivity to Hannan consistency, expanded experiments, preliminary convergence-rate analysis, and numerical-mitigation discussion.
