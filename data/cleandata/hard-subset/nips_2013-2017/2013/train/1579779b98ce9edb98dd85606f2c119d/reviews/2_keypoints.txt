- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (examples: goofspiel, Tron).  
- Studies MCTS implemented with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, with well-presented results; main contribution is new formal analysis.  
- Prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) treated the setup but provided more preliminary analysis.  
- Proof technique is inductive, assuming optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Major weakness: limited provable insights about inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Analysis fails to connect to subgame perfect Nash equilibria and other game-theoretic tools.  
- Suggestion: tie analysis to subgame-perfect concepts and cite Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games.
