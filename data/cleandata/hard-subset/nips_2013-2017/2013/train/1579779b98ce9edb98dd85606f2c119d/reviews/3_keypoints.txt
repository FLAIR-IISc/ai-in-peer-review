- Paper analyzes a class of MCTS algorithms using essentially any no-regret selection method.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proof of asymptotic convergence to ε-Nash for simultaneous-move games.  
- Convergence result is asymptotic only; no convergence rates are provided.  
- Lack of rates is a limitation but may be inevitable under only ε-Hannan consistency.  
- Reviewer asks whether concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T from EXP) yield explicit convergence rates.  
- Experiments compare propagating mean reward values versus propagating current sample values.  
- Empirical finding: mean-value propagation slightly underperforms sample-value propagation.  
- Experiments attempt to provide insight into empirical convergence rates.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and technically correct.  
- Mild reservation about impact due to absence of rates and incremental nature given known sequential-move MCTS results.  
- Nitpicks: typos/notation issues—Figure 1 "leafs"→"leaves"; Line 144 "negated" not "inverted"; Algorithm 1 line 10 (a1,a2) should be (i,j) or (σ1,σ2); other (i,j) vs a notation inconsistencies.
