**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper presents a formal analysis of the convergence of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) algorithms in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors introduce a general template of MCTS algorithms for these games, which can be instantiated using any regret-minimizing procedure for matrix games as a function for selecting the next actions to be sampled. They formally prove that if the algorithm uses an -Hannan consistent selection function, which assures attempting each action infinitely many times, the MCTS algorithm eventually converges to a subgame perfect -Nash equilibrium of the extensive form game. The authors also empirically evaluate this claim using two different -Hannan consistent procedures: regret matching and Exp3.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on MCTS in sequential games and extends the analysis to simultaneous move games. The authors mention that while MCTS has been successfully applied to various games, there is a lack of theory analyzing MCTS outside two-player perfect-information sequential games. The paper fills this gap by providing a formal analysis of MCTS in simultaneous move games.

**Strengths**

1. **Formal Analysis**: The paper provides a rigorous formal analysis of the convergence of MCTS algorithms in simultaneous move games. The authors prove that the algorithm converges to a subgame perfect -Nash equilibrium under certain conditions.
2. **General Template**: The authors introduce a general template of MCTS algorithms that can be instantiated using any regret-minimizing procedure for matrix games. This makes the analysis more general and applicable to various MCTS variants.
3. **Empirical Evaluation**: The paper includes an empirical evaluation of the proposed algorithm using regret matching and Exp3. The results show that the algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in various games.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Assumptions**: The formal analysis relies on the assumption that the selection function is -Hannan consistent and has guaranteed exploration. While the authors provide examples of algorithms that satisfy these conditions, it is unclear how general these assumptions are.
2. **Computational Complexity**: The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm. This is an important aspect, as MCTS can be computationally expensive, especially in large games.
3. **Limited Experimental Evaluation**: While the paper includes an empirical evaluation, it is limited to a small set of games and algorithms. More extensive experiments would be necessary to fully understand the performance of the proposed algorithm.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

**Pro Acceptance**

* The paper provides a rigorous formal analysis of MCTS in simultaneous move games, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The general template of MCTS algorithms introduced in the paper is a useful framework for analyzing and designing MCTS variants.
* The empirical evaluation shows promising results, indicating that the proposed algorithm can be effective in practice.

**Con Acceptance**

* The assumptions underlying the formal analysis may not be general enough, limiting the applicability of the results.
* The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm, which is an important aspect of MCTS.
* The experimental evaluation is limited, and more extensive experiments are necessary to fully understand the performance of the proposed algorithm.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the formal analysis is rigorous. However, the assumptions underlying the analysis may be too restrictive, and the computational complexity of the algorithm is not thoroughly analyzed.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm and the formal analysis. However, some sections, such as the proof of Theorem 4.11, are quite technical and may be difficult to follow for non-experts.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel formal analysis of MCTS in simultaneous move games, which is a significant contribution to the field. The general template of MCTS algorithms introduced in the paper is also a new and useful framework.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for the field of game theory and artificial intelligence. The proposed algorithm and the formal analysis can be used to improve the performance of MCTS in various applications, such as game playing and decision-making under uncertainty.
