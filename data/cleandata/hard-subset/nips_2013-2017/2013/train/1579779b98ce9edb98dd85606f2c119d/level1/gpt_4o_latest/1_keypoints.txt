- Paper introduces a novel theoretical and empirical analysis of MCTS for zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move EFGs and a general MCTS template using Hannan-consistent selection with exploration.
- Authors prove asymptotic convergence of the template to an approximate Nash equilibrium and validate this empirically using regret matching and Exp3.
- Strength: First formal proof of convergence for this MCTS setting, with rigorous derivations using -Hannan consistency.
- Strength: Strong empirical validation on random and worst-case games that complements the theoretical results.
- Strength: Template is general and can be instantiated with various selection methods, suggesting broad applicability and extensions.
- Strength: Results are clearly presented regarding convergence to approximate NE and trade-offs between selection methods.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation focuses on small games with limited depth/branching, leaving scalability untested.
- Weakness: Theoretical results are asymptotic only; no finite-time convergence bounds are provided.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed comparison to other state-of-the-art algorithms for simultaneous-move games.
- Weakness: Some formal analysis sections are dense and could use clearer explanations or visual aids for accessibility.
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses an important gap with rigorous theory and supporting experiments, likely to stimulate further research.
- Argument against acceptance: Practical utility is limited by missing finite-time guarantees and restricted empirical scope; clarity could be improved.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand empirical evaluation.
