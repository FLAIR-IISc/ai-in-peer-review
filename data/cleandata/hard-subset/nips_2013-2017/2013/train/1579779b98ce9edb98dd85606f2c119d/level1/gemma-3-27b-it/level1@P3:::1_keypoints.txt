- Core contribution: proves MCTS with a β-Hannan consistent selection method and appropriate exploration converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum simultaneous-move perfect-information games.
- Empirical validation: experiments with Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games support the theoretical result.
- Motivation: addresses a gap in theoretical understanding of MCTS for simultaneous-move games.
- Theoretical novelty: connecting MCTS convergence to Hannan consistency is insightful and rigorous.
- Algorithm clarity: Algorithm 1 clearly presents the MCTS template for simultaneous-move games.
- Empirical strengths: includes worst-case game tests, showing robustness of the approach.
- Literature context: paper situates itself well among prior work (backward induction, UCT, regret methods).
- Well-defined scope: tackles a relevant, clearly specified problem in game-playing and security domains.
- Proof complexity: the convergence proof is dense and long, especially the repeated matrix games with bounded error section.
- Accessibility issue: the dense proof needs more intuition and high-level summaries in the main text.
- Limited empirical scope: experiments use small depth and branching factors, leaving generalization to larger games unclear.
- Exploration sensitivity: paper lacks a systematic study of how the exploration parameter γ affects convergence rate.
- Comparative evaluation missing: no thorough experimental comparison with existing MCTS variants (e.g., Lanctot et al., 2014).
- Practical relevance: convergence bounds appear loose and their practical usefulness for rapid, near-optimal play is unclear.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add proof intuition, expand empirical evaluation and sensitivity analysis, compare to other MCTS variants, and discuss practical implications.
