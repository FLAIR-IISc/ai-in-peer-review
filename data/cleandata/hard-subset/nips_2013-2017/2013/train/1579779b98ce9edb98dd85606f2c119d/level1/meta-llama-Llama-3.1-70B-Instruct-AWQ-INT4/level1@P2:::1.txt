**Summary**

The paper "Convergence of Monte Carlo Tree Search in Simultaneous Move Games" studies the convergence of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) algorithms in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors present a general template of MCTS algorithms for these games and prove that if the selection method is -Hannan consistent and satisfies additional requirements on exploration, then the MCTS algorithm eventually converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium (NE) of the extensive-form game. They also empirically evaluate this claim using regret matching and Exp3 as the selection methods on randomly generated games and empirically selected worst-case games.

**Strengths**

* The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the convergence of MCTS algorithms in simultaneous move games, which is a significant contribution to the field of game theory and artificial intelligence.
* The authors present a general template of MCTS algorithms that can be instantiated with various selection methods, making the results applicable to a wide range of algorithms.
* The empirical evaluation of the algorithms on randomly generated games and worst-case games provides valuable insights into the performance of the algorithms in practice.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper assumes that the game has perfect information and simultaneous moves, which may not be the case in many real-world applications.
* The analysis is limited to zero-sum games, and it is unclear whether the results can be extended to non-zero-sum games.
* The empirical evaluation is limited to a specific set of games and may not be representative of all possible games.

**Originality**
