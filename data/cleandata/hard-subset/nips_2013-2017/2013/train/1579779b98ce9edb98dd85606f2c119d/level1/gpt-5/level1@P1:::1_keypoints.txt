- Paper studies SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move perfect-information games and proposes a general SM-MCTS template using ε–Hannan-consistent node selection with explicit exploration.
- Main theorem: with ε–HC selection and guaranteed exploration each node visited infinitely often, SM-MCTS converges almost surely to a subgame-perfect approximate NE; asymptotic error scales as O(ε D^2).
- Analysis requires returning mean values up the tree rather than immediate sample values.
- Empirically validates convergence using regret matching and Exp3 on random and hard small games, and compares mean vs sample propagation (mean causes small slowdown).
- Fills a gap: provides first convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS in simultaneous-move games, distinct from known negative results for UCT and from CFR/MCCFR methods.
- Theory is sound and standard in approach (matrix-game HC dynamics → bounded-error propagation → induction over depth), with plausible lemmas and proper citations.
- Concern: D^2 dependence and multiplicative constants appear loose; tighter accounting might reduce to O(D).
- Concern: Claim that the complete tree is eventually built under guaranteed exploration is stated but not proved in main text; a brief argument should be added.
- Algorithmic presentation is clear overall; regret-matching and Exp3 instantiations are described with exploration.
- Practical caveat: requirement to propagate means differs from typical MCTS practice; empirical section quantifies the slowdown.
- Experiments corroborate asymptotic claims but are limited to small games and exhaustive tiny worst cases.
- Experimental shortcomings: no comparison to (MC)CFR or pruning-based simultaneous-move solvers, and no error bars or confidence intervals.
- Paper is mostly well-written; notation is defined and algorithm spelled out, but some presentation/notation inconsistencies remain.
- Suggested clarity fixes: clearly name and distinguish SM-MCTS vs SM-MCTS-M, state which return rule is used in theory/experiments, and summarize tree-expansion argument.
- Originality: novel convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS via composition of no-regret dynamics and bounded-error propagation to subgame-perfectness.
- Significance: conceptually important for MCTS/game-theoretic planning communities; justifies SM-MCTS heuristics in a nontrivial class of games.
- Practical significance limited by asymptotic nature, reliance on uniform exploration and mean propagation; finite-time rates would increase impact.
- Pros: first convergence proof, general template for any ε–HC selector with exploration, solid links to online learning, and supportive experiments.
- Cons: asymptotic only without finite-sample rates, loose constants and D^2 dependence, limited experiments, and informal tree-expansion argument.
- Key suggestions: provide finite-time bounds combining known regret rates, tighten depth dependence analysis, add baselines ((MC)CFR, double-oracle), include error bars and deeper/larger games, and clarify exploration/expansion argument.
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution with empirical corroboration; recommend acceptance (possibly weak accept) contingent on addressing clarity and discussion on rates and depth dependence.
