- Addresses theoretical convergence of MCTS in two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games by proposing a general SM-MCTS template.  
- Main contribution: proof that if in-tree selection is ε-Hannan consistent with sufficient exploration, SM-MCTS converges to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof uses induction on tree depth and leverages online learning/regret-minimization results, starting from convergence in single matrix games.  
- Extends the leaf-level result to internal nodes by showing convergence still holds when payoffs have bounded error from estimated child values.  
- Empirical evaluation uses Regret Matching and Exp3 to validate theoretical convergence and compares mean-value propagation (RMM/Exp3M) with sample-propagation (RM/Exp3).  
- Clarifies that prior popular choices like UCB1 fail in simultaneous-move games, positioning this work as filling a theoretical gap.  
- Technical quality is high: proof strategy is sound, elegant, and the claims are well-supported.  
- Authors carefully note the requirement for a specific MCTS variant (mean-value propagation) and honestly compare it to standard practice.  
- A potential issue: Theorem 4.11 reports an O(D^2) dependency on depth D, but the proof sketch suggests a linear dependency, requiring clarification.  
- Limitation: analysis is asymptotic and does not provide finite-time convergence rates.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear algorithms and informative empirical graphs; the D vs D^2 point is the main clarity concern.  
- Originality: highly original synthesis connecting ε-Hannan consistency/regret minimization with recursive MCTS search.  
- Significance: high — provides formal foundations for widely used algorithms and can guide future MCTS designs in game theory and general game playing.  
- Recommendation: strong accept; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses that can be fixed with revision.
