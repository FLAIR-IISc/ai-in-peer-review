- Paper analyzes MCTS convergence in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.
- Proposes a general MCTS template that can use any regret-minimizing matrix-game procedure for selection.
- Main theoretical result: if the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent with guaranteed exploration, MCTS converges to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium.
- Provides a technically sound induction proof that extends matrix-game convergence to extensive-form games.
- Empirical validation uses Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games, showing convergence to approximate Nash equilibria.
- Observes that mean-propagation (RMM) variants converge slightly slower than current-sample variants.
- Novel contribution: fills a gap since prior MCTS convergence results addressed only sequential games without simultaneous moves.
- Strong connection to online learning literature (Hannan consistency) and clear theoretical grounding.
- Practical relevance for game-playing and security-domain applications; results useful for practitioners and theorists.
- Paper is well-written with precise definitions and sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Weakness: assumes complete tree construction, which may not hold in practical large-scale MCTS.
- Weakness: lacks finite-time convergence bounds.
- Weakness: empirical comparisons are limited to the theoretical scope and could include more game types and MCTS variants.
- Overall assessment: novel, technically solid work with strong empirical support; recommendation: accept.
