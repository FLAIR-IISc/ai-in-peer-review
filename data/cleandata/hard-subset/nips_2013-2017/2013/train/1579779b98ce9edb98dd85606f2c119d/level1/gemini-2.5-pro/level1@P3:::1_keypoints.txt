- Provides first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general SM-MCTS template and proves convergence to a subgame-perfect ε-NE if in-node selection is ε-Hannan consistent with sufficient exploration.  
- Bridges online learning in repeated matrix games with search in extensive-form games via an inductive depth-based proof.  
- Core proof: ε-Hannan consistent players converge in repeated noisy matrix games; MCTS nodes induce such matrix games when propagating mean child values.  
- Empirically instantiates framework with Regret Matching and Exp3, demonstrating convergence on random and worst-case games.  
- Strength — Significance/Originality: Fills a fundamental theoretical gap where prior popular methods (e.g., UCT) lacked guarantees.  
- Strength — Generality: Framing around ε-Hannan consistency makes results broadly applicable beyond specific algorithms.  
- Strength — Technical quality: Sound reduction to repeated matrix games with bounded error and logically structured lemmas.  
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, organized, precise definitions, and a clear algorithmic template.  
- Weakness — Proof clarity: Theorem 4.11’s sketch is brief; the D^2·ε error bound and how errors accumulate lack detailed justification and tightness discussion.  
- Weakness — Static tree assumption: Analysis assumes a fully built tree, sidestepping dynamic tree expansion and the time-varying nature of child-value errors.  
- Weakness — Mean vs. sample propagation: Theory requires mean-value propagation, but practice often uses single-sample updates (which empirically can converge faster); theoretical treatment of single-sample is missing.  
- Weakness — No finite-time rates: Paper proves asymptotic quality of the equilibrium but does not analyze convergence speed; empirical results only hint at practical rates.  
- Conclusion: Strong, novel theoretical contribution with supportive experiments, but minor issues in proof exposition and simplifying assumptions remain.  
- For acceptance: First formal proof for simultaneous-move MCTS; elegant ε-Hannan consistency framework; technically sound and well-presented.  
- Against acceptance: Relies on static-tree simplification and has an opaque main-theorem error-accumulation sketch.
