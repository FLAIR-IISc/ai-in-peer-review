- Presents a formal convergence analysis of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general MCTS template that uses any regret-minimizing matrix-game procedure as the action-selection function.  
- Proves that with an ε-Hannan consistent, guaranteed-exploration selection function, MCTS converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirically evaluates the theory using regret matching and Exp3, showing convergence to approximate Nash equilibria in tested games.  
- Extends prior MCTS analyses from sequential games to simultaneous-move games, filling a theoretical gap.  
- Strength: Provides a rigorous, formal proof of convergence under stated conditions.  
- Strength: The general template makes the analysis applicable to many MCTS variants and regret-minimizing procedures.  
- Strength: Empirical results support the theoretical claims for two example selection algorithms.  
- Weakness: Relies on the assumption of ε-Hannan consistency and guaranteed exploration, whose generality is unclear.  
- Weakness: Lacks a detailed computational-complexity analysis of the proposed algorithm.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is limited in scope (small set of games and algorithms).  
- Pro acceptance: Significant theoretical contribution by proving MCTS convergence in simultaneous-move games.  
- Pro acceptance: The general template is a useful framework for designing and analyzing MCTS variants.  
- Pro acceptance: Empirical evidence indicates practical promise.  
- Con acceptance: Restrictive assumptions may limit real-world applicability.  
- Con acceptance: Missing complexity analysis raises questions about scalability.  
- Con acceptance: Insufficiently extensive experiments to fully validate performance claims.  
- Quality: Technically sound and rigorous, but some assumptions may be too restrictive.  
- Clarity: Well-written overall, though some proofs (e.g., Theorem 4.11) are highly technical and hard for non-experts.  
- Originality: Novel formal analysis and a new general MCTS template for simultaneous-move games.  
- Significance: Results have important implications for game theory and AI, potentially improving MCTS in practice.
