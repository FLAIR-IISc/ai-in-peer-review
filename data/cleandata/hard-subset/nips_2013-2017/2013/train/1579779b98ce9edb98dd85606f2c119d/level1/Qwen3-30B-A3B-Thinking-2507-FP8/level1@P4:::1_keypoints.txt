- Presents formal convergence analysis of MCTS for zero-sum perfect-information games with simultaneous moves, via a general MCTS template using regret-minimization selection.  
- Proves that ε-Hannan consistent selection methods with guaranteed exploration converge to an approximate subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirically validates theoretical claims using regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games; examines mean propagation vs current sample values.  
- First formal convergence analysis for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, with rigorous induction-based proofs.  
- Clear definitions, logical flow, precise mathematical notation, and well-structured formal analysis.  
- Strong empirical support showing regret matching and Exp3 variants converge to approximate Nash equilibria.  
- Effectively situates contribution within prior work on regret minimization and MCTS, filling a notable literature gap.  
- Establishes asymptotic convergence but provides no finite-time convergence bounds, limiting practical applicability.  
- Theoretical scope is limited to zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games; does not address imperfect information or non-zero-sum settings.  
- Empirical worst-case analysis is limited by finite sampling (100 runs) and may not identify true worst cases.  
- Lacks concrete guidance on practical implementation choices (e.g., mean propagation vs current sample) for varying game sizes/complexities.  
- Strong case for acceptance: novel theoretical result, rigorous proofs, empirical validation, clear writing, and practical relevance where prior methods lacked guarantees.  
- Counterarguments: limited applicability beyond the specific game class, absence of finite-time bounds, and non-rigorous worst-case empirical analysis.  
- Overall: significant, well-supported theoretical contribution that warrants acceptance despite acknowledged limitations and directions for future work.
