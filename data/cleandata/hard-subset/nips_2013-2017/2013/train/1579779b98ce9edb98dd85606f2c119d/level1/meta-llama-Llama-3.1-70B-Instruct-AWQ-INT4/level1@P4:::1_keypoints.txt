- Main idea: Formal analysis proving MCTS with a -Hannan-consistent, exploration-satisfying selection method converges to an approximate NE in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games; includes a general MCTS template and empirical tests (regret matching, Exp3).  
- Relation to prior work: Extends MCTS convergence theory from sequential to simultaneous-move games and builds on Hannan consistency/Nash convergence literature.  
- Strength 1: Provides rigorous, formal convergence analysis filling a theoretical gap for simultaneous-move MCTS.  
- Strength 2: Offers a general MCTS algorithm template applicable to multiple selection methods.  
- Strength 3: Empirical evaluation on random and worst-case games gives practical performance insights.  
- Weakness 1: Assumes perfect information with simultaneous moves, limiting applicability to many real-world games.  
- Weakness 2: Analysis restricted to zero-sum games; extension to non-zero-sum is unclear.  
- Weakness 3: Empirical study is limited to specific games and selection methods, so generalizability is uncertain.  
- Quality: Technically sound and rigorous with a well-designed empirical evaluation.  
- Clarity: Well-written, clear notation, and helpful figures/tables.  
- Originality: Novel theoretical results on MCTS convergence for simultaneous-move games.  
- Significance: Provides a theoretical foundation influencing algorithm design and future research.  
- Pro acceptance: Rigorous analysis, practical implications for algorithm design, and supportive empirical results.  
- Con acceptance: Limited scope (zero-sum, specific empirical settings) raises questions about broader applicability.
