- Addresses a major theoretical gap by proving convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.
- Introduces a general MCTS template that can use any regret-minimizing procedure at nodes.
- Main theorem: if the node selection is ε-Hannan consistent and ensures exploration, the MCTS average strategy converges to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Extends online-learning results from repeated matrix games to extensive-form games with stochastic node payoffs from subgame simulations.
- Requires backpropagation of mean rewards (not last-sample values) as a key assumption for the proof.
- Significance: provides a rigorous foundation for using MCTS in general game playing and security games beyond empirical UCT justifications.
- Technical quality: sound, logically built proofs via extension to noisy payoffs and induction over game depth.
- Experiments: thorough and supportive, comparing provably convergent variants to heuristic counterparts.
- Weakness: convergence bound depends on game depth (D^2) and may be loose for deep games; lacks discussion on tightness and practical impact.
- Weakness: the provably correct variant (mean-value backpropagation) converges slightly slower than the standard last-sample approach.
- Recommendation: strong accept — novel, significant, technically solid work that outweighs minor theoretical and practical caveats.
