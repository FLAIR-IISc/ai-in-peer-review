- Proposes SM-MCTS template using ε–Hannan-consistent selection with guaranteed exploration and shows average strategies converge to a subgame-perfect (ε·D^2+δ)-NE when mean returns are backpropagated.  
- Proof strategy: no-regret → minimax in matrix games; extend to bounded payoff error; inductively compose errors up the tree.  
- Empirical instantiations (Regret Matching, Exp3) converge on random/small adversarial games; mean-return backpropagation is empirically slower than sample-return.  
- Positions work at the intersection of MCTS and online learning; complements negative UCT results and relates to CFR-style local regret ideas.  
- Theory is sound and lemmas align with standard no-regret facts, but constants (factor 4, linear error dependence) and the D^2 depth dependence appear conservative.  
- A formal lemma is needed to show eventual full-tree expansion and that every node/joint action is visited infinitely often under the SM-MCTS expansion rule.  
- Require a brief justification that independent per-player exploration implies positive joint-action visitation probability (e.g., Borel–Cantelli argument).  
- Lemma 4.5 (empirical frequencies vs average strategies) needs an explicit martingale SLLN sketch because strategy samples are adapted, not i.i.d.  
- The proof crucially requires mean-return backpropagation; extension to standard sample-return backprop is unclear and would be valuable.  
- Fixed exploration γ induces an irreducible exploitability floor; analysis of decaying γt (with ∑γt=∞) should be considered.  
- Experiments confirm qualitative asymptotic convergence but are small-scale and lack extensive sensitivity analyses (depth, branching, γ).  
- Missing stronger baselines and runtime/sample-complexity comparisons (e.g., simultaneous alpha–beta, CFR adaptations, double-oracle).  
- Writing is generally clear with a readable algorithmic template, though some notation is dense and minor typos exist.  
- Originality: first general convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS to subgame-perfect approximate NE under broad selection policies.  
- Significance: theoretically meaningful foundation for SM-MCTS, but asymptotic nature, conservative constants, and limited empirical validation limit immediate practical impact.  
- Pros: general template linking SM-MCTS to no-regret selection; subgame-perfect guarantee; applies to standard HC algorithms; explains UCT failures.  
- Cons: asymptotic result with loose O(D^2ε) bound; relies on mean-return backprop; fixed exploration bias; limited experiments; some technical steps under-detailed.  
- Suggested improvements: add formal tree-expansion lemma; analyze decaying exploration schedules; derive finite-time bounds using per-node regret rates; attempt extension to sample-return backprop; expand experiments and baselines.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
