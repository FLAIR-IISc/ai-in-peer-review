- Formal analysis proving MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Proposes a general MCTS template that can be instantiated with various selection methods.  
- Shows convergence when the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent and satisfies exploration requirements.  
- Paper is well-written with clear introduction, related work, and organized technical sections.  
- Thorough explanation of the proposed algorithm and formal proofs.  
- Empirical evaluation demonstrates effectiveness using regret matching and Exp3.  
- Strength: formal convergence proof is a significant contribution.  
- Strength: algorithm generality allows multiple selection method instantiations.  
- Weakness: assumption of perfect information limits practical applicability to imperfect-information games.  
- Weakness: no finite-time convergence bounds provided.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation limited to a narrow set of games.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to other state-of-the-art extensive-form game solvers.  
- Arguments for acceptance: formal proof, general algorithm, and empirical demonstrations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: perfect-information assumption, no finite-time bounds, limited empirical scope.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10; Clarity 9/10; Originality 8/10; Significance 8/10.
