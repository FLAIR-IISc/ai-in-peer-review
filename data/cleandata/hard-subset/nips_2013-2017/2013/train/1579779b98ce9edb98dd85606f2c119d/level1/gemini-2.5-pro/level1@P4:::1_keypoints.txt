- Paper proves that SM-MCTS with an ε-Hannan consistent, sufficiently exploring selection policy converges to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium in two-player, zero-sum, simultaneous-move perfect-information games; approximation error depends on ε and tree depth.  
- Provides a general SM-MCTS template and instantiations with Regret Matching and Exp3, connecting online learning and game-tree search.  

- Originality/significance: first formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in this game class, giving a general recipe based on Hannan consistency.  
- Technical soundness: clear, inductive proof from leaves upward modeling nodes as repeated matrix games with bounded error (Lemmas → Theorem).  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, with precise algorithm descriptions and logical development of formal results.  
- Empirical support: experiments validate convergence, compare mean vs. sample value propagation, and test worst-case games, showing robustness and practical insights.  

- Weakness: Theorem 4.11’s final error bound (ε_D^2 + δ) is not precisely defined or clearly derived, leaving the exact approximation guarantee unclear.  
- Weakness: Analysis is asymptotic with no finite-time or rate-of-convergence bounds, limiting practical interpretability.  
- Weakness: Proof assumes the full game tree is built beforehand, sidestepping analysis of the initial biased/tree-building phase.  

- Pro for acceptance: novel, general theoretical contribution linking Hannan consistency to provable MCTS convergence, with sound proofs and supportive experiments.  
- Con for acceptance: unclear statement/derivation of final error bound and lack of convergence-rate guarantees.
