- Proves MCTS converges to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move extensive-form games when selection is ε-Hannan consistent with guaranteed exploration.  
- Presents a general MCTS template instantiated with regret-minimizing algorithms (Regret Matching, Exp3) linking online learning to MCTS.  
- Proof models stochastic returns as bounded errors and uses induction on tree depth; the theoretical argument is logical and appears sound.  
- Key technical requirement: nodes must propagate the mean of all collected rewards (RMM) rather than the most recent sample.  
- Empirical evaluation tests both variants, showing the proof-required variant converges slightly slower but still converges.  
- Experiments include random games and carefully designed worst-case instances, strengthening empirical support.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with accessible formal analysis and informative figures.  
- Originality: first formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Insight: identifies Hannan consistency as the critical local property enabling global convergence.  
- Significance: fills an important theoretical gap for MCTS and invites future extensions (e.g., imperfect information, faster selection rules).  
- Pros for acceptance: novel foundational result, technically solid, connects disparate fields, thoughtful experiments, high clarity.  
- Cons against acceptance: no finite-time or convergence-rate bounds, potentially loose D^2 error dependence, experiments limited to small synthetic games.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance—the strengths and novelty outweigh the limitations.
