- Tackles convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with simultaneous moves, addressing a theoretical gap.  
- Presents a general MCTS template and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under -Hannan consistent selection and exploration conditions.  
- Connects clearly to bandit/regret-minimization literature and prior game-playing work (e.g., Goofspiel, general game playing).  
- Formal analysis is dense but appears rigorous, using an inductive proof structure based on Hannan consistency.  
- Template is general and can be instantiated with various selection methods.  
- Empirical validation uses Regret Matching, Exp3, and several MCTS variants on random and worst-case games, demonstrating convergence.  
- Inclusion of worst-case game tests strengthens the empirical evaluation.  
- Experimental section lacks thorough comparison to specialized algorithms for simultaneous-move games.  
- Paper focuses on empirical convergence rate but omits discussion of computational cost and iterations needed for a given approximation.  
- Acknowledges but insufficiently details challenges in extending the theoretical analysis to all MCTS variants.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical foundation, addresses important gap, general template, and solid empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparisons to specialized algorithms, missing complexity/scalability discussion, and need for deeper analysis of extension challenges.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
