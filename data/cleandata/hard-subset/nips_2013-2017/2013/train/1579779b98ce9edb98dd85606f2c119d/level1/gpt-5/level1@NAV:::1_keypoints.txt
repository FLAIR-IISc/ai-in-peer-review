- Paper studies SM-MCTS in two-player zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proposes a general SM-MCTS template using ε-Hannan-consistent selection rules with guaranteed exploration.  
- Main theoretical result: eventual convergence to a subgame-perfect ε-approximate Nash equilibrium with error roughly scaling as ε·D^2 + δ (Theorem 4.11), proven by induction over depth.  
- Proof technique leverages results on repeated matrix games with bounded observation error to lift guarantees up the tree.  
- Assumptions: selection rules are ε-Hannan-consistent, guaranteed exploration, eventual full-tree expansion, and the use of mean backups.  
- Theoretical development is sound and well-motivated; bounded-error lemmas and analysis of exploration effects on regret are clear.  
- Empirical instantiation uses regret matching and Exp3, evaluated on random and worst-case small games; experiments support asymptotic convergence and compare mean vs. sample backups.  
- Experiments are limited in scale and do not compare against CFR-style baselines for simultaneous-move settings.  
- Clarity is generally good with precise definitions and readable proof sketch, but the mean-backup requirement and the ε–D dependence should be emphasized earlier and stated unambiguously.  
- Minor notation and algorithm presentation could be streamlined for readability.  
- Originality: first convergence analysis of MCTS in perfect-information simultaneous-move games, novel template and bounded-error composition argument.  
- Significance: important theoretical advance linking online learning (Hannan consistency) with tree search, but results are asymptotic with loose D^2 dependence and no finite-time rates.  
- Pros: first general convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS, clean linkage to online learning, subgame-perfect approximate equilibrium guarantee, empirical confirmation for two HC algorithms.  
- Cons: only asymptotic (no finite-time bounds), requires mean backups and explicit exploration whose practical impact is underexplored, and modest experimental scale and breadth.
