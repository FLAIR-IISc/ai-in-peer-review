- Paper studies SM-MCTS with ε–Hannan-consistent per-node selection and mean backups, proving almost-sure convergence to a subgame-perfect (ε·D^2 + δ)-Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof strategy: (i) ε–HC dynamics → approximate NE in repeated matrix games, (ii) extend to repeated games with bounded payoff errors, (iii) induct up the tree bounding per-level error.  
- Empirical validation: RM and Exp3 variants converge on random and small “empirical worst” games; compares mean-backup vs sample-backup.  
- Fills a gap vs prior MCTS theory (UCT failures in simultaneous-move games) and connects HC/online-learning results to MCTS in stacked matrix games.  
- Theoretical assumptions are clear; subgame-perfect guarantee is stronger than root optimality; O(D^2 ε) bound is conservative but justified by per-level accumulation.  
- Reliance on mean-value backup is essential for the proof and explicitly discussed and tested empirically.  
- Experiments are modest but consistent with theory; no comparisons to non-HC MCTS baselines (e.g., UCT) or to CFR/alpha-beta/double-oracle methods on benchmarks.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized; definitions and algorithm details are clear though some proofs are deferred.  
- Potential confusion about exploration variants (off-policy A* vs on-policy mixing) needs clarification and unified HC-constant statements.  
- Originality: novel theoretical convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS and a unifying template for RM and Exp3 within MCTS.  
- Significance: important principled foundation for MCTS in simultaneous-move perfect-information games, but practical impact limited by asymptotic result, lack of finite-sample rates, and D^2 dependence.  
- Strengths: clear theoretical framework, subgame-perfect approximate NE, instantiation with known HC algorithms, and empirical corroboration.  
- Weaknesses: asymptotic-only result, mean-value backup requirement (unproven for sample-backup), small synthetic experiments, unclear exploration-treatment bounds, and restriction to perfect-information games.  
- Suggested improvements: add proof sketch for main theorem and D^2 origin, unify exploration treatment with explicit HC constants for on-policy mixing, expand experiments to standard simultaneous-move domains and baselines, report wall-clock/node-normalized metrics with confidence intervals, and discuss prospects for finite-time bounds and sample-backup extension.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, clean theoretical contribution bridging online learning and MCTS with empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks finite-time analysis, uses practical assumptions that limit immediacy, and has limited experimental benchmarking.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying exploration/backup details and expanding empirical comparisons.
