- Formal analysis of MCTS for zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium using a Hannan-consistent selection method with exploration.  
- Introduces a general MCTS template and extends prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) with a more rigorous theoretical treatment.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with precise definitions and a clear inductive proof based on optimal leaf strategies.  
- Empirical validation using regret matching and Exp3 demonstrates convergence in both random and constructed “worst-case” games.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and propose future work on imperfect-information games and tighter convergence bounds.  
- Key strength: formalization and convergence guarantee provide a theoretical foundation for practical MCTS performance.  
- Novel contribution over prior preliminary analyses, solidifying the paper’s significance.  
- Major weakness: limited insight into algorithm behavior at inner tree nodes and how strategies are refined there.  
- Missing connection to subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium concepts, which feels like a missed theoretical opportunity.  
- Proof depends on the complete-tree assumption and does not fully address effects of incremental tree construction.  
- Suggestion to add the Harris (1990) citation and tie the analysis to subgame-perfect concepts.  
- Recommendation: Accept — substantial, rigorous contribution suitable for NIPS despite noted weaknesses.
