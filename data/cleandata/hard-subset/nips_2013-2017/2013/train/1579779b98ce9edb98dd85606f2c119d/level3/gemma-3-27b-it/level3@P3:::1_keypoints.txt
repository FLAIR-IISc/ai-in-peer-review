- Problem: Convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.
- Contribution: Presents a general MCTS template and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium given ε-Hannan consistent selection and exploration.
- Empirics: Validated on random and worst-case games; compares mean vs. sample value propagation.
- Novelty/context: Extends MCTS theory beyond sequential-move games and connects to regret-minimizing procedures from algorithmic game theory.
- Related work: Properly cites backward induction, double-oracle, and prior MCTS successes but notes lack of prior convergence guarantees for simultaneous-move games.
- Strength — Theoretical soundness: Core convergence proof under stated conditions is rigorous and valuable.
- Strength — Clarity: Paper is well-written, with precise definitions and a clear algorithm template.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Experiments support theory and provide insights on value propagation choices.
- Strength — Useful sufficient condition: Establishing ε-Hannan consistency as a convergence criterion is practically useful.
- Weakness — Proof novelty: Proof technique relies on standard repeated-game/regret arguments and offers limited methodological novelty.
- Weakness — UCB/UCT omission: Paper avoids discussing widely used UCB/UCT methods and lacks comparison or explanation of their exclusion.
- Weakness — Mean vs. sample propagation: Theory covers mean propagation, but experiments show sample propagation works too without theoretical explanation.
- Weakness — Incremental contribution: Overall contribution seen as solid but not a major paradigm shift.
- Acceptance pro: Fills a theoretical gap, clearly presented, empirically supported, and relevant to AI and game theory communities.
- Acceptance con: Limited novelty of technique, notable omission of UCB/UCT discussion, and unexplained discrepancy between theoretical and experimental propagation methods.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with revisions to address the sample-vs-mean propagation gap and discuss relation to UCB/UCT algorithms.
