- Proves MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Provides empirical evidence showing the algorithm converges in practice, supporting the theoretical result.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of technical details.  
- Proof technique may not be novel; underlying ideas might be incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Does not analyze widely used UCB/UCT algorithms; needs comparison to existing UCB analyses.  
- Theoretical convergence is proven for mean-value propagation only, yet experiments show sample-value propagation works too; explanation for this discrepancy is lacking.  
- Offers a useful sufficient condition for convergence, but the result is somewhat unsurprising.  
- Reviewer recommends minor revisions to address UCB comparisons and the mean vs. sample-value propagation issue.  
- Rating: 7/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
