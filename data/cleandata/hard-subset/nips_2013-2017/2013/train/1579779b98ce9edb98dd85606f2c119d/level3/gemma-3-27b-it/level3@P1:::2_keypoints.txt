- Presents a formal analysis proving MCTS with Hannan-consistent selection (regret matching, Exp3) converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and presents results straightforwardly.  
- Properly situates work in algorithmic game theory and motivates the need for theoretical analysis beyond sequential games.  
- Definitions and background are thorough and provide necessary foundations.  
- Rigorous formal analysis: induction-based proof leveraging Hannan consistency appears sound.  
- Empirical evaluation supports theoretical results and includes experiments with both Regret Matching and Exp3, strengthening generality.  
- Key weakness: analysis primarily focuses on leaf nodes and gives limited provable insights for inner-node convergence.  
- Reliance on assuming optimal leaf strategies is limiting; would benefit from bounding errors from imperfect inner-node play.  
- Connection to subgame perfect Nash equilibria is underdeveloped; should discuss relation and cite relevant work (e.g., Harris 1990).  
- Empirical evaluation scope is limited; should test more game instances and compare to state-of-the-art algorithms.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, clear presentation, empirical support, addresses an important gap.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on optimal leaf assumptions, weak linkage to subgame perfection, and limited experiments.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing subgame perfection discussion and expanded empirical evaluation.
