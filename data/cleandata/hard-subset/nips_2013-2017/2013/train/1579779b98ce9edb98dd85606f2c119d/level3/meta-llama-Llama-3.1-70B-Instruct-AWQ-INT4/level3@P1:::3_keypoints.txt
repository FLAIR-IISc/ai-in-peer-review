- Analyzes MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves—an under-studied class.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proof of asymptotic convergence to ε-Nash equilibrium for simultaneous-move games.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent mathematical notation and clear exposition of problem and solution.  
- Proof is thorough, well-structured, and based on ε-Hannan consistency; key steps are clearly explained.  
- Experiments provide empirical insight; mean-value propagation slightly underperforms sample-value propagation.  
- Experimental convergence-rate findings are interesting but not conclusive.  
- Major limitation: only asymptotic convergence is shown; no explicit convergence rates.  
- Reviewer asks whether concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T from EXP) would yield explicit convergence rates.  
- Contribution is incremental—extension of known sequential-move MCTS results to simultaneous-move games.  
- Minor issues: typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., "leafs" vs "leaves", "negated" vs "inverted", (i,j) vs a).  
- Quality: technically sound and thorough; score 9/10.  
- Clarity: clear and concise writing; score 9/10.  
- Originality: valuable extension but not entirely novel; score 8/10.  
- Significance: significant for understudied game class and empirical insights; score 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, thorough analysis, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of convergence rates, incremental nature, minor typos/notation issues.
