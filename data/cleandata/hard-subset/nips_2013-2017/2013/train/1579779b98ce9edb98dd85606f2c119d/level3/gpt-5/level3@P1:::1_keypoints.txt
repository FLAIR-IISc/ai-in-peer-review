- Studies MCTS for two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move perfect-information games and proposes an SM-MCTS template using any ε-Hannan-consistent (HC) selection rule with exploration; proves almost-sure convergence to a subgame-perfect approximate NE with mean-value backups, error scaling with depth (Theorem 4.11).  
- Analysis strategy: standard regret arguments in repeated matrix games → extension to repeated games with bounded payoff error → induction over tree depth to aggregate errors.  
- Empirically instantiates the template with Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and hard instances; observes convergence for both mean-backup and sample-backup variants (mean slightly slower).  
- Novelty: composes no-regret dynamics up the game tree for simultaneous moves and gives the first formal convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS under broad HC conditions.  
- Strength: clear, practically useful sufficient condition (ε-HC + guaranteed exploration) yielding convergence to approximate subgame-perfect NE.  
- Strength: sound, modular, and transparent analysis that reduces to well-understood repeated-game arguments.  
- Strength: empirical validation with canonical HC algorithms and stress tests consistent with theory.  
- Strength: clear definitions, algorithm template, and careful proofs.  
- Weakness: theoretical contribution feels incremental, relying heavily on standard no-regret to equilibrium tools and a straightforward depth induction; final bound (quadratic in depth) seems loose.  
- Weakness: theory–practice mismatch — proofs require mean-value backups and a specific off-policy exploration scheme, yet experiments show sample backups and on-policy exploration also work without theoretical explanation.  
- Weakness: limited empirical breadth — mainly random/small worst-case games, no comparisons to exact backward induction (LP/DO) or CFR, no error bars or runtime reporting.  
- Weakness: missing analysis or experiments for UCT/UCB despite known failure modes in simultaneous-move settings; lack of discussion on adversarial-bandit variants.  
- Weakness: no finite-time/sample-complexity rates or high-probability bounds are provided.  
- Quality: technically sound and carefully stated; the guaranteed-exploration assumption is crucial and needs clearer alignment with practical on-policy exploration.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized, but should unify empirical vs average strategies earlier and state δ quantification in asymptotic claims.  
- Originality/significance: first formal SM-MCTS convergence result under broad HC rules, but high-level idea of composing no-regret dynamics is not entirely new.  
- Suggestions: extend theory to sample-value backups via concentration bounds, tighten depth dependence, clarify exploration necessity, add LP/DO and CFR baselines, report error bars/runtime, test named deeper benchmarks, analyze sensitivity to parameters, and show exploitability after removing forced exploration.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — useful, modular theoretical contribution with empirical support, but incremental novelty, loose bounds, and theory–practice gaps limit impact.
