- Introduces a general MCTS template for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games using any no-regret learner as the in-tree selection policy.  
- Proves that an ε-Hannan consistent selection algorithm with sufficient exploration yields asymptotic convergence to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Fills a gap in MCTS theory by extending convergence guarantees to simultaneous-move games where UCB-based approaches fail.  
- Empirically validates convergence using Regret Matching and Exp3 and compares practical implementation choices.  
- Technical quality is high: the inductive proof (from matrix games up the tree) is sound and carefully handles bounded stochastic estimation errors.  
- Major limitation: the convergence guarantee is purely asymptotic and provides no convergence rates.  
- Experiments also investigate the theory/practice discrepancy between mean-value propagation (needed for proof) and common sample-value propagation.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with appropriate background and logical flow from simple to full game analysis.  
- Minor clarity/notation issues: typo ("leafs" → "leaves"), wording ("inverted utilities" → "negated utilities"), and inconsistent action/strategy notation in Algorithm 1.  
- Originality: first formal framing and convergence proof for this class of MCTS in simultaneous-move games, though related to existing no-regret/CFR ideas.  
- Significance: provides an important theoretical foundation but practical impact is limited by lack of rates and slight mismatch between theoretical and empirically better implementations.  
- Recommended future work: derive explicit convergence rates using known regret bounds (e.g., O(√T) for Exp3) to make results practically informative.
