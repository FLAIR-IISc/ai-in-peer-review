- Paper proposes a general MCTS framework for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions.
- Theoretical guarantee is given for algorithms using ε-Hannan consistent selection methods (e.g., regret matching, Exp3).
- Empirical results validate theory and compare mean propagation vs. current sample value propagation; worst-case game analysis included.
- Work fills a theoretical gap in understanding MCTS in simultaneous-move games and complements practical success.

- Strength: Provides a rigorous theoretical foundation; using ε-Hannan consistency as a sufficient condition is novel in this context.
- Strength: Experimental validation supports theoretical claims and explores practical performance nuances.
- Strength: Paper is well-organized with clear definitions, algorithms, and proofs.

- Weakness: Proof techniques largely adapt established concepts (Hannan consistency, bounded error propagation), limiting methodological novelty.
- Weakness: Excludes UCB-based algorithms, which are common in MCTS, and lacks discussion/comparison with them.
- Weakness: Some empirical findings (other MCTS variants converging) are unexplained by the theory.
- Weakness: Technical significance is marginal—results are incremental and formalize existing practitioner expectations.

- For acceptance: Addresses an important theoretical gap; combines theory and experiments; relevant to game theory and RL communities.
- Against acceptance: Limited novelty and exclusion of UCB reduce generality; lacks finite-time convergence bounds; unexplained empirical behaviors indicate incomplete theory.

- Recommendation: Weak Accept, with suggestion to address noted weaknesses in future revisions.
