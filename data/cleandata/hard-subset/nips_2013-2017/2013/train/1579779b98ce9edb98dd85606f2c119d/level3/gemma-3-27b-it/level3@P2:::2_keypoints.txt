- Presents a formal analysis proving MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games given a -Hannan-consistent selection method with additional exploration.  
- Instantiates the template with regret matching and Exp3 and provides empirical validation on random and worst-case games.  
- Builds on prior work (e.g., Lanctot et al. 2013) and significantly advances theoretical understanding of MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Addresses an important gap: theoretical guarantees for a widely used practical method in a relevant game setting.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-structured, making technical contributions accessible.  
- Empirical results convincingly support the theoretical claims and are well-executed.  
- Key contribution: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- General template is flexible, allowing different selection methods to be used.  
- Related work is thoroughly discussed and contributions are well-positioned in the literature.  
- Weakness: analysis concentrates on leaf nodes and the inductive proof assumes optimal behavior at leaves, leaving behavior deeper in the tree less explored.  
- Weakness: relationship between the achieved approximate Nash equilibrium and subgame-perfect equilibria is not explicitly addressed; citing Harris (1990) and discussing implications would strengthen the paper.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of convergence rates and insufficient exploration of how exploration parameter (γ) affects speed of convergence.  
- Weakness: insufficient investigation of game-specific factors that make problems easier or harder for this MCTS approach.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address subgame-perfect connections and convergence-rate discussion.
