- Studies MCTS for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, giving a general MCTS template instantiable by selection methods.  
- Proves that if a selection method is ε-Hannan consistent in a matrix game and meets exploration requirements, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, with a thorough introduction and background.  
- Formal analysis is rigorous, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Empirical evaluation is well-designed and demonstrates the algorithm’s practical effectiveness.  
- Major contribution: provides a sufficient theoretical condition for MCTS convergence in simultaneous-move games.  
- Concern: proof technique may lack novelty, relying on existing ε-Hannan consistency and regret-minimization results.  
- Concern: paper does not analyze commonly used UCB/UCT algorithms, potentially limiting impact.  
- Concern: theoretical guarantees apply only to mean-value propagation, whereas experiments show sample-value propagation also works—this discrepancy is unexplained.  
- Overall assessment: technically interesting but incremental, not a major breakthrough.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical result, solid empirical evaluation, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty of proofs, omission of UCB/UCT analysis, and unresolved theory–experiment mismatch.  
- Rating: 7/10; Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor revision requests: clarify novelty relative to existing ε-Hannan/regret work; explain theory vs. experimental discrepancy; discuss implications for UCB/UCT algorithms.
