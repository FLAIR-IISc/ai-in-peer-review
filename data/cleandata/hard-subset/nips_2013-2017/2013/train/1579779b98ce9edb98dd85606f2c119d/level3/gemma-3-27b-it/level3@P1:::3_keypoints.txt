- Presents a theoretical analysis of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, filling a gap in guarantees for this setting.
- Core contribution: proof that MCTS with any Hannan-consistent selection method converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Empirical validation using Regret Matching and Exp3 demonstrates practical viability of the framework.
- Extends MCTS theory from sequential to simultaneous-move games, relevant to domains like Goofspiel and general game playing.
- Offers a more general and theoretically grounded approach versus domain-specific or LP-based methods.
- Appropriately cites foundational work in regret minimization and algorithmic game theory.
- Strength: rigorous, clearly presented theoretical analysis that advances understanding of MCTS convergence.
- Strength: generality of framework—applies to any Hannan-consistent selection algorithm.
- Strength: empirical results support theoretical claims, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Limitation: no explicit convergence rates provided, limiting practical impact.
- Suggestion: investigate whether stronger regret-rate assumptions (e.g., Exp3’s √T bound) could yield explicit convergence rates.
- Limitation: contribution is somewhat incremental relative to existing sequential MCTS results without rates.
- Limitation: empirical results do not markedly improve playing strength; mean-reward propagation underperforming sample value is counterintuitive and unexplained.
- Minor issues: stylistic and notation inconsistencies (e.g., "leafs" vs "leaves", misuse of "negated", inconsistent action/index notation).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix stylistic/notation issues; encourage future work on deriving convergence rates.
