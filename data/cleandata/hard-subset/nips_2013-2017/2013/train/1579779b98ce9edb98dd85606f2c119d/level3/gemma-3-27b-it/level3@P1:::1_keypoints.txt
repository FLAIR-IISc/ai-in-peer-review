- Studies convergence of MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proves convergence to an approximate NE under -Hannan consistent selection with specific exploration.
- Presents a general MCTS algorithmic template adaptable to different selection methods.
- Formal proof establishing a sufficient condition (-Hannan consistency) for convergence is a significant theoretical contribution.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and definitions/algorithm are precisely presented.
- Empirical evaluation on random and "worst-case" games supports theoretical claims and shows robustness.
- Builds on regret-minimization techniques (regret matching, Exp3) and connects to existing game-theoretic literature.
- Proof technique’s novelty is limited because it relies on established -Hannan consistency ideas from online learning.
- Excludes analysis of widely used UCB/UCT methods; lacks a detailed comparison or discussion of why they fail and possible fixes.
- Theory assumes propagating mean rewards, but experiments show propagating current samples also converges; this discrepancy is unexplained.
- Contribution is useful but incremental — a natural extension of existing regret-minimization and MCTS work rather than a breakthrough.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a theoretical gap, provides a formal sufficient condition, has empirical validation, and is clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, incomplete treatment of UCB/UCT, unexplained theory–experiment mismatch, and overall incremental impact.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution rated as weak accept; addressing the theory–experiment discrepancy, UCB/UCT discussion, and clarifying proof novelty would strengthen the paper.
