- Studies MCTS convergence in zero-sum, perfect-information, extensive-form games with simultaneous moves.  
- Presents a general MCTS template and the first formal proof of convergence to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a sufficient condition that node selection be ε-Hannan consistent and guarantee exploration.  
- Connects MCTS convergence to online-learning algorithms like Regret Matching and Exp3.  
- Experimental results on randomly generated and worst-case games support the theoretical claims.  
- Fills an important theoretical gap where MCTS was used empirically without formal guarantees.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; strength is the formal link between ε-Hannan consistency and MCTS convergence.  
- Concern: proof technique (induction from leaves up) may lack novelty, appearing as a straightforward extension from single-stage matrix games.  
- Major issue: theory–practice disconnect — proof requires propagating mean rewards, but experiments use sample-value propagation; this discrepancy is unexplained and undermines necessity of conditions.  
- Concern: UCB/UCT methods are dismissed too quickly; given their prevalence, deeper discussion or experimental comparison is needed.  
- Overall: technically sound and useful but potentially incremental; significance would increase if novelty and the theory–practice gap were addressed.
