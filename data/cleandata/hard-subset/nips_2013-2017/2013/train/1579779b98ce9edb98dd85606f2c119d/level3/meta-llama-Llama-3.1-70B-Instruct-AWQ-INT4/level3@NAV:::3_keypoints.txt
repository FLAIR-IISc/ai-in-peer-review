- First formal analysis proving convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Main theoretical result: any ε-Hannan consistent algorithm can produce an MCTS that converges to an approximate (ε-)Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly presents ideas and proofs.  
- Thorough and clear literature review that situates the contribution.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: asymptotic convergence to ε-Nash for simultaneous-move games.  
- Experiments validate empirical convergence behavior and compare propagating mean rewards vs. current sample values.  
- Comparison of mean vs. sample propagation yields useful insights about trade-offs in practice.  
- Limitation: no convergence rate bounds (possibly inherent under ε-Hannan consistency), acknowledged by authors.  
- Originality: novel combination of game-theory and AI techniques, not a straightforward extension of prior work.  
- High significance: provides foundation for applying MCTS in game-playing, security, and decision-making under uncertainty.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix typos and notation issues.  
- Pros for acceptance: significant contribution, technical soundness, thorough review, informative experiments.  
- Cons against acceptance: lack of convergence rates and minor presentation issues.  
- Overall ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
