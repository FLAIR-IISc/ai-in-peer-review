- Studies SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proposes a general template using any ε-Hannan-consistent selector.
- Main theoretical claim: with guaranteed exploration and mean-value backups, SM-MCTS converges almost surely to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium in the limit.
- Proof strategy: induction on depth — no-regret learners → robustness under bounded payoff errors → composition across the tree yields asymptotic convergence.
- Analysis requires propagating mean returns (mean-value backups) rather than single-sample returns.
- Empirical comparison of mean-value vs sample-value backups and of regret matching vs Exp3 on random and small adversarial games; all converge, mean-backup slightly slower.
- Fills a gap left by UCT analyses (which don’t cover simultaneous moves) and provides the first general asymptotic convergence result for SM-MCTS.
- Technical soundness: careful, standard no-regret tools adapted correctly; exploration wrapper and frequency vs average strategy distinctions are handled cleanly.
- Main limitation: only asymptotic convergence is shown; no finite-sample or iteration-complexity rates are provided.
- Assumes eventual full tree expansion, which is reasonable asymptotically but avoids practical sample-complexity concerns.
- Experiments are aligned with theory but limited to small games, which constrains empirical generality.
- Clarity: well written, reproducible for experts, with a clear proof roadmap; some minor notation inconsistencies and typos remain.
- Originality: conceptually novel to plug any ε-Hannan-consistent selector into SM-MCTS with convergence guarantees; technically a useful induction proof pattern.
- Significance: offers a unifying theoretical foundation for SM-MCTS in simultaneous-move domains, though practical impact is limited by lack of rates.
- Strengths: general/modular approach, first asymptotic guarantee, clean proof (matrix → noisy matrix → tree), sensible experiments, solid writing.
- Weaknesses: no finite-sample bounds, mean-backup slows empirical convergence, experiments small, and time-to-full-expansion is unquantified.
- Key questions for authors: can finite-sample bounds be derived from per-node regret R(T)? can a convex mix of mean/sample backups be analyzed? does updating during exploratory moves break results? how is exploitability computed? can time-to-full-expansion be bounded?
- Suggestions: report confidence bands for exploitability curves, show sensitivity to γ and learning rates, and discuss tightness of depth-dependent constants in the theorem.
- Noted minor errors: "leafs" → "leaves"; wording at Line 144 should be "negated utilities" not "inverted"; inconsistent (i,j) vs (a1,a2)/(σ1,σ2) action/strategy notation.
- Pros for acceptance: first general convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS, clean modular analysis, supportive empirical study.
- Cons for acceptance: lack of finite-time rates, small empirical domains, and some incremental aspects relative to known no-regret and sequential-move MCTS results.
- Overall: a valuable, well-written, technically solid contribution that answers an important open question for SM-MCTS, with mild reservations due to missing finite-time guarantees.
