- Paper proves that a general MCTS template with any ε-Hannan-consistent, guaranteed-exploration selection method converges asymptotically to an ε-Nash equilibrium in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games.  
- Fills a gap in prior MCTS theory by extending convergence analysis from sequential to simultaneous-move settings and addressing known UCT failures in simultaneous games.  
- Builds on online learning foundations (e.g., regret matching, Exp3) and situates contribution relative to prior NIPS work.  
- Significant and well-executed theoretical contribution: rigorous proof of asymptotic convergence to ε-Nash.  
- Paper is technically correct and well-written, with clear formal analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation is useful, notably showing mean-value propagation slightly underperforms sample-value propagation.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge limitations, emphasizing the asymptotic nature of results.  
- Main weakness: absence of convergence rates; reviewer suggests exploring explicit rates under concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T).  
- Impact limited by incremental nature versus sequential-move MCTS results and by lack of practical convergence rates.  
- Minor presentation issues and notation inconsistencies (e.g., "leafs" vs "leaves", misuse of "negated", inconsistent action notation).  
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous proof; empirical results support theory but lack rate information.  
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized, slightly reduced by notation issues.  
- Originality: addresses a distinct and understudied gap for simultaneous-move MCTS and properly cites related work.  
- Significance: provides an important theoretical foundation for simultaneous-move MCTS, but asymptotic-only results limit immediate practical impact.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution that fills an important gap; acknowledged limitations point to future work.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
