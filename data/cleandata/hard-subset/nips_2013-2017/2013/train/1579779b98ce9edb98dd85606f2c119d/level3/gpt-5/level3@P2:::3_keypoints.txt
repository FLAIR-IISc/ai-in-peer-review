- Paper studies SM-MCTS in two-player zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and defines a general SM-MCTS template using ε-Hannan-consistent (HC) selection with exploration.  
- Main theoretical result: almost-sure asymptotic convergence of SM-MCTS to a subgame-perfect ε'-approximate Nash equilibrium with a depth-dependent error bound (Theorem 4.11).  
- Proof strategy: (i) convergence in repeated matrix games under ε-HC, (ii) extension to repeated matrix games with bounded payoff error, (iii) induction over game-tree depth.  
- Empirics: experiments with Regret Matching and Exp3 on random/adversarial games support convergence claims and compare mean vs. sample return propagation.  
- Novelty: first general asymptotic convergence analysis for MCTS in simultaneous-move perfect-information games; reduction to ε-HC selectors and handling of exploration errors is original.  
- Technical soundness: proof roadmap is clear and plausible; assumptions (ε-HC, guaranteed exploration, eventual full-tree expansion) are stated and justified.  
- Limitation: results are asymptotic with no finite-time convergence rates, which limits practical guidance.  
- Suggestion: derive finite-time exploitability bounds by leveraging known regret rates (e.g., O(1/√T)) to get depth-dependent bounds.  
- Question on exploration: could an annealed exploration rate γ_t→0 preserve guarantees while reducing asymptotic bias?  
- Return propagation: theory requires mean propagation; question whether weaker schemes (e.g., exponential moving averages) suffice and better match practice.  
- Subgame perfection: request to explicitly quantify approximation dependence on depth D and ε and discuss tightness of the bound.  
- Tree growth: paper conditions on eventual full-tree expansion; a bound on expected time to full expansion under exploration would strengthen the argument.  
- Quality of exposition: generally well-written and organized; algorithmic template and theory are clearly explained despite some dense sections.  
- Experiments: consistent with theory but relatively small-scale and lacking comparisons to strong baselines like alpha-beta pruning.  
- Pros: first convergence guarantees for SM-MCTS, modular analysis, empirical validation, and practitioner-relevant exposition.  
- Cons: no finite-time rates, some constants and notation in Theorem 4.11 unclear, minor notation inconsistencies, and limited experimental scope.  
- Minor issues/typos: "leafs"→"leaves"; ensure joint action notation consistency; use "negated utilities" not "inverted"; clarify Eq.(4) and Definition 4.3 on exploratory updates.  
- Overall assessment: solid, timely theoretical contribution with supportive experiments; recommend acceptance if finite-time bounds or clearer discussion of regret-rate translation are added.
