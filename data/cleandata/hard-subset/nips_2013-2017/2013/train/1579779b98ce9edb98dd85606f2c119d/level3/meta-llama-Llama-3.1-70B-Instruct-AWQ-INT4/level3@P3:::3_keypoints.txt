- Presents convergence analysis of MCTS for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Proposes a general MCTS template usable with any no-regret selection method.  
- Proves that ε-Hannan consistent selection methods yield convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and clearly organized with consistent notation.  
- Main theoretical contribution: asymptotic convergence to ε-Nash for simultaneous-move games.  
- Provides empirical results showing practical convergence of the algorithm.  
- Limitation: only asymptotic convergence is proved; no explicit convergence rates are given.  
- Authors acknowledge rate limitation and suggest exploring concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T).  
- Empirical comparison: mean-value propagation slightly underperforms sample-value propagation.  
- Evaluation includes random games and empirically selected worst-case games to probe convergence behavior.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with implications for practical use of MCTS.  
- For acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, practical relevance, clear writing, and sound results.  
- Against acceptance: lack of convergence rates and not fully comprehensive empirical analysis.  
- Rating: 8/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Minor revision requests: fix typo ("leafs"→"leaves"), correct notation ("negated"→"inverted"), fix Algorithm 1 inconsistency ((a1,a2)→(i,j) or (σ1,σ2)).  
- Suggested revisions: provide more comprehensive empirical analysis and discuss practical implications of the results.
