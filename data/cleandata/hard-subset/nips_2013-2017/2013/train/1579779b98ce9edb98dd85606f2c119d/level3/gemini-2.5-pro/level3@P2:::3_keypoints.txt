- Main contribution: first proof that SM-MCTS with ε-Hannan-consistent, sufficiently exploring selection algorithms converges asymptotically to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Approach: models each node's action selection as a repeated matrix game and proves convergence by induction on tree depth.  
- Template: introduces a general SM-MCTS framework where the selection mechanism is modular and treated as a no-regret learner.  
- Experiments: empirical validation on random and worst-case games using Regret Matching and Exp3 confirms convergence.  
- Practical comparison: investigates propagating mean reward (required by proof) versus current sample reward (commonly used) and finds the latter empirically better.  
- Technical quality: rigorous, logically structured proof that extends no-regret convergence results to extensive-form MCTS and carefully handles estimation errors.  
- Limitations acknowledged: results are asymptotic with no finite-time convergence rates provided.  
- Experimental design: well-designed experiments and sensible search for worst-case instances to probe empirical convergence.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized presentation, clear motivation and background, with logical breakdown of the theoretical argument.  
- Minor clarity issues: caption typo ("leafs"→"leaves"), term preference ("inverted"→"negated"), and a notational inconsistency in Algorithm 1 (Select(h) returns (i,j) vs. actions denoted a or (a1,a2)).  
- Originality: highly novel—closes a known theoretical gap by synthesizing MCTS and no-regret learning for simultaneous-move games.  
- Generality: proof applies to a class of ε-Hannan-consistent algorithms, not just a single instantiation.  
- Significance: important for researchers and practitioners—provides formal asymptotic justification for MCTS in simultaneous-move games and a foundation for further theory.  
- Key open question: lack of finite-time rates—could stronger assumptions (e.g., known regret bounds for Exp3) yield explicit convergence rates for SM-MCTS?  
- Suggestion: authors should speculate on why sample-value propagation outperforms mean propagation empirically and discuss challenges in extending the proof to that variant.
