- Problem: The paper studies theoretical foundations of MCTS for two-player, zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves (stacked matrix games, e.g., Goofspiel).  
- Main contribution: Proves that MCTS with ε-Hannan consistent action selection and guaranteed exploration converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof approach: Inductive analysis—prove for single matrix games, extend to matrix games with bounded errors, then to the entire game tree.  
- Empirical support: Experiments on random and brute-force-derived worst-case games validate convergence and compare MCTS variants.  
- Novelty/significance: First formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, filling a major theoretical gap.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous, well-structured, and elegant analysis with precise definitions and well-supported claims.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; algorithm template, formal analysis, and experiments are clearly presented.  
- Weakness: Analysis assumes a fully built, fixed game tree, avoiding the incremental tree-growth dynamics central to practical MCTS and limiting insight into inner-node behavior.  
- Weakness: Weakened connection to subgame-perfection literature; paper should better contextualize results with foundational work (e.g., existence of subgame-perfect equilibria).  
- Weakness: Empirical scope is limited—worst-case instances found by brute force over a small class may not represent truly hardest cases.  
- Overall: Strong, novel, technically sound contribution with addressable weaknesses; recommendation: accept.
