- Presents formal analysis proving convergence of MCTS to approximate Nash equilibria in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move extensive-form games.  
- Proposes a general MCTS template that can be instantiated with different selection methods.  
- Shows convergence when the selection method is Hannan-consistent and satisfies exploration requirements.  
- Builds on prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) and provides thorough definitions, background, and algorithm descriptions.  
- Provides detailed, well-structured formal proofs with clear explanations and implications.  
- Demonstrates applicability by evaluating regret matching and Exp3, both converging to approximate Nash equilibria in tested games.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Original contribution: formal MCTS analysis in a new domain and novel combination of existing techniques.  
- Significant implications for AI and game-playing systems, with potential generalization to more complex games.  
- Limitation: cannot prove meaningful results about inner game-tree nodes (e.g., subgame perfect equilibria).  
- Suggests incorporating subgame-perfect concepts would give a more comprehensive understanding.  
- Would benefit from more experimental results across a wider range of games.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.
