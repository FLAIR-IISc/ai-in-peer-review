- Studies convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.
- Presents a general MCTS algorithm template and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium using a -Hannan consistent selection method with exploration.
- Theoretical connection to Hannan consistency grounds the convergence proof in established online learning theory.
- Empirical evaluation uses Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games to support theoretical claims.
- Clearly written, well-organized paper with consistent notation and strong relevance to the community.
- Technical contribution is significant in filling a theoretical gap for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.
- Proof technique is sound but feels incremental given prior work on Hannan consistency.
- Omits in-depth discussion and comparison of UCB/UCT variants despite their widespread use in MCTS.
- Theory applies to mean reward propagation, but experiments show current-sample propagation also converges, creating an unexplained discrepancy.
- Lacks analysis or tight bounds on convergence rates, limiting practical insight.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel correct analysis, bridges theory and practice, validated empirically, clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty of proof technique, missing UCB discussion, propagation-theory mismatch, no convergence rate analysis.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and valuable but not groundbreaking; recommended as a weak accept pending addressing noted weaknesses.
