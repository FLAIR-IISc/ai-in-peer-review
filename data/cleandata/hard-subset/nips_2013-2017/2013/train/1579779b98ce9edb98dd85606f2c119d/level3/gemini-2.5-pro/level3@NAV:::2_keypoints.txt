- Paper gives a formal convergence analysis of MCTS for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves, proving ε-Hannan-consistent selection rules converge to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- The class studied includes games such as Goofspiel and Tron.  
- This is the first convergence proof for MCTS in this game class, addressing a theoretical gap beyond prior work like Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- Main strength: novel, significant theoretical contribution with a clear, well-organized, and technically sound inductive proof.  
- Weakness: the inductive proof assumes optimal play at leaf nodes and gives limited insight into strategic dynamics at interior nodes.  
- Weakness: the claimed convergence to subgame perfect equilibrium is not connected to established extensive-form game-theoretic tools.  
- Suggestion: explicitly tie the analysis to literature on subgame perfection (e.g., cite Harris 1990) to strengthen theoretical grounding.  
- Overall: valuable and significant for the MCTS community, but would benefit from better contextualization within game theory.
