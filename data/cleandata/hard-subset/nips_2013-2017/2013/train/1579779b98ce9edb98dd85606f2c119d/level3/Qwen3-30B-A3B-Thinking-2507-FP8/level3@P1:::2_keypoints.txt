- Presents the first formal convergence analysis of MCTS for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general MCTS template that can use any regret-minimizing matrix-game solver as the selection method.  
- Proves that ε-Hannan consistent selection methods with exploration lead MCTS to converge to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Uses an inductive proof technique assuming optimal leaf strategies and extending up the game tree, a methodological advance.  
- Empirically validates theory using regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case game instances.  
- Experimental results confirm convergence to approximate NE and support reproducibility.  
- Novel contribution: links Hannan consistency in matrix games to NE convergence in extensive-form simultaneous-move games.  
- Differentiates clearly from prior work (e.g., Lanctot et al. 2013) by providing rigorous convergence guarantees.  
- High significance for both theory (algorithmic game theory) and practice (justifies MCTS in simultaneous-move games).  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with consistent notation and thorough technical detail.  
- Main weakness: does not explicitly connect convergence results to subgame perfect Nash equilibria, despite focusing on extensive-form games.  
- Misses citation/discussion of Harris (1990) regarding existence of subgame perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Lacks finite-time convergence bounds; provides asymptotic guarantees only and limited guidance for practical runtime choices.  
- Recommendation: valuable and technically sound work; reviewer recommends acceptance (NIPS 2013).
