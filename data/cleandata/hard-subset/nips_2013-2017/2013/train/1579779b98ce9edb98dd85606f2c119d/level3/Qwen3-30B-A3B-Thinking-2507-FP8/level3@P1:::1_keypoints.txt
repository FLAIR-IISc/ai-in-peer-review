- Presents first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games via a general MCTS template using ε-Hannan consistent selection with exploration.  
- Proves that ε-Hannan consistent selection methods that ensure exploration lead to approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibria, extending Hannan consistency results to multi-stage games.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound, using careful inductive error analysis across the game tree and building on prior work [15,16].  
- Empirically validates theory using regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games, showing both mean and current-sample propagation converge.  
- Empirical finding that current-sample propagation converges despite theory requiring mean propagation is valuable for practitioners.  
- Clear problem formulation, well-structured proofs, and comprehensive experiments with reproducible implementation details.  
- Contribution is useful but incremental—largely an extension of existing Hannan consistency theory rather than a major novel breakthrough.  
- Paper lacks discussion and analysis of UCT/UCB variants, which are widely used and known to fail in some simultaneous-move settings.  
- Authors should explain why UCT variants do not satisfy the Hannan consistency requirements and relate to Shafiei et al. [17].  
- Theoretical requirement for mean value propagation conflicts with empirical success of sample propagation; this discrepancy is not adequately explained.  
- Could strengthen impact by comparing more thoroughly to related game-theoretic and regret-minimization advances (e.g., [18,19] and recent work).  
- Overall high clarity and quality, but the incremental nature and omission of UCT analysis reduce the paper’s significance.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the mean vs. sample propagation discrepancy and to discuss UCT/UCB variants more fully.
