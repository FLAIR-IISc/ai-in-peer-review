- Proposes a general MCTS template for simultaneous-move perfect-information games that uses any regret-minimizing matrix-game procedure for action selection.  
- Proves that using an ε-Hannan-consistent selection function that samples each action infinitely often yields convergence to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Extends prior MCTS analyses from sequential to simultaneous-move games.  
- Uses regret-matching and Exp3 as well-motivated selection methods in experiments.  
- Empirical evaluation on random and worst-case games gives useful performance insights.  
- Presentation is clear, well-structured, and technically detailed.  
- Main contribution is a formal sufficient condition for convergence, but the proof technique is not highly novel.  
- Does not analyze widely used UCB/UCT algorithms, which is a notable omission.  
- Theoretical focus on propagation of the mean is justified, but experiments show sample-value propagation also works without explanation.  
- Discrepancy between theory and empirical findings on propagation of the mean is unexplained and warrants further study.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental, limiting novelty and potential impact.  
- Overall quality is high (technically sound), clarity is strong, and significance is moderate.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear presentation, useful sufficient condition, and informative empirical results.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited novelty of proof technique, omission of UCB/UCT analysis, and unexplained theory–experiment mismatch.
