- Studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Provides a general MCTS algorithm template and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium given a selection method that is Hannan-consistent in matrix games and has sufficient exploration.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Main contribution: formal convergence analysis and a sufficient condition for convergence.  
- Proof technique may lack novelty, as it builds on existing results.  
- Does not analyze widely used UCB/UCT algorithms; lacks comparison to these methods.  
- Theoretical results only prove convergence for propagation of the mean, while experiments show sample-value propagation also works—this discrepancy is unexplained.  
- Contribution appears incremental rather than highly original or significant.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, addressing novelty, inclusion/comparison of UCB/UCT, and explanation of the theory–experiment discrepancy.
