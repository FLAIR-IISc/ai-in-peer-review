- Proves asymptotic convergence of MCTS with any no-regret selection to ε‑Nash in simultaneous-move games; technically sound and well-written, though somewhat incremental.  
- Properly situates work in algorithmic game theory and simulation-based game playing, clearly motivating the theoretical gap.  
- Main strength is a rigorous theoretical analysis: defines a general SM‑MCTS template and shows convergence under ε‑Hannan consistency and guaranteed exploration with a sound proof strategy.  
- Major weakness is absence of explicit convergence rates; reviewer suggests analyzing concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T for EXP3) or proving difficulty of obtaining rates.  
- Experiments compare mean-value vs sample-value propagation (sample-value slightly better); empirical study is limited and doesn't compensate for missing theoretical rates or guarantee true worst-case games.  
- Minor presentation issues: typos and inconsistent notation (e.g., “leafs” vs “leaves”, “negated” vs “inverted”, inconsistent use of (a1,a2) vs (i,j)) that need standardization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first formal convergence guarantee for general MCTS in simultaneous-move games, rigorous proofs, clear writing, and strong relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of convergence rates, incremental advance over sequential-move results, and presentation errors.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions — fix notation/typos and discuss (even briefly) why tighter convergence rates are hard under the paper’s general assumptions.
