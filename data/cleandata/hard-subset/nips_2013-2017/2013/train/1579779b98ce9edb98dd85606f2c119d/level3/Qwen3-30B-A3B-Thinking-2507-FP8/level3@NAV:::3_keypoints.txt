- Presents a formal analysis of MCTS for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general MCTS template parameterized by any no-regret selection method.  
- Proves that ε-Hannan consistent selection with guaranteed exploration implies convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Extends prior MCTS convergence results from sequential-move games to simultaneous-move games, filling a literature gap.  
- Theoretical contribution is technically sound, well-structured, and grounded in online learning and game theory.  
- Empirical comparison between mean-value propagation (used in proof) and sample-value propagation (common in practice) shows only minor performance differences.  
- Major limitation: no convergence rates are provided, reducing practical impact.  
- Reviewer suggests exploring concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T from EXP3) to derive explicit convergence rates.  
- Contribution is incremental relative to known sequential-move MCTS results, limiting perceived novelty.  
- Paper is well-written and correct but contains minor notation inconsistencies and typos (e.g., "leafs"/"leaves", "negated"/"inverted", inconsistent (a1,a2) vs. (i,j)).  
- Recommendation: suitable for acceptance at a high-quality conference but would be stronger if rate questions or limitations were addressed more thoroughly.
