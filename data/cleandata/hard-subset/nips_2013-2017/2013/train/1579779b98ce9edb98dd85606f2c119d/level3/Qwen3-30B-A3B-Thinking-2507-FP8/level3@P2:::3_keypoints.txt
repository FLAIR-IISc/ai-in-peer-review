- Formal analysis of MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games with a general MCTS template using regret-minimizing matrix-game procedures.  
- Main theoretical result: if selection is ε-Hannan consistent and explores, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirical evaluation (regret matching, Exp3) on random and worst-case games supports the theoretical claims.  
- Fills a literature gap: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, extending prior sequential-game theory.  
- Elegant, general connection between regret minimization and MCTS showing transfer of convergence properties.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and carefully developed; proofs are well-motivated and technically correct.  
- Major limitation: convergence is asymptotic only — no finite-time or explicit convergence rates provided.  
- Could strengthen results by leveraging concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T for Exp3) to derive rates.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly structured with careful definitions; minor notational inconsistencies noted (Algorithm 1, "leafs" vs "leaves").  
- Original and appropriately cites related work; distinguishes itself from sequential MCTS literature.  
- Significance: provides important theoretical grounding for practical MCTS use in many domains, though impact is somewhat incremental.  
- Pro-accept points: first formal guarantee, elegant template, rigorous analysis, supportive experiments, clear presentation.  
- Con-accept points: only asymptotic convergence, incremental advance, minor notation issues, limited comparison to other theoretical approaches (e.g., CFR).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address notational inconsistencies.
