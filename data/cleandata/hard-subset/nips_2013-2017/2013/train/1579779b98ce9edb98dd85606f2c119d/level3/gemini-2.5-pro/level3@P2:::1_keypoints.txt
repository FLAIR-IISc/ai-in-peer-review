- Studies convergence of MCTS in two-player, zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Proposes a general SM-MCTS template that can be instantiated with different action selection mechanisms.  
- Main theoretical result: if the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent and ensures sufficient exploration, MCTS converges to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium.  
- Instantiates the general condition with two regret-minimizing procedures: Regret Matching (RM) and Exp3.  
- Empirical evaluation on random and hand-selected "worst-case" games confirms convergence and compares algorithm variants.  
- Fills an important theoretical gap by providing formal convergence guarantees for a widely used class of MCTS algorithms.  
- Technical analysis is sound: builds from repeated matrix games, handles bounded payoff errors, and applies induction on the game tree.  
- Experiments reasonably support theoretical claims and explore practical choices like mean-value versus sample-value propagation.  
- Key weakness: theoretical proof requires propagating mean rewards, but experiments show standard sample-reward propagation also converges without theoretical justification.  
- Paper is very clear and well-written with precise notation, a helpful high-level algorithm (Algorithm 1), and informative empirical figures.  
- Originality: the ε-Hannan consistency framing is novel and useful, though the proof technique largely extends existing online-learning results.  
- Significance: provides the first formal justification for these MCTS variants and a useful design condition, but impact is limited by scope and lack of convergence-rate analysis.  
- Limitations: does not cover popular UCT/UCB MCTS variants and offers only asymptotic guarantees; empirical convergence can be slow in worst-case games.  
- Strengths summary: first formal convergence proof for simultaneous-move MCTS, technically sound, clear writing, and supportive experiments.  
- Weaknesses summary: moderate novelty of technique, excludes UCT/UCB, theoretical gap on sample propagation, and incremental rather than groundbreaking contribution.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, valuable contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
