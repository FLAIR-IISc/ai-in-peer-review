- Proves that any ε-Hannan consistent algorithm can be used within MCTS to converge to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general MCTS template that can be instantiated with any regret-minimizing procedure for matrix-game action selection.  
- Theoretical result: ε-Hannan consistency ensures each action is sampled infinitely often and the algorithm converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirical evaluation uses regret matching and Exp3 on random and adversarially chosen games.  
- Empirical results show convergence speed comparable to recently proposed MCTS algorithms for these games.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- Provides a comprehensive and useful review of related work.  
- Technical contributions are substantial and rigorously analyzed.  
- Empirical results support the theoretical claims.
