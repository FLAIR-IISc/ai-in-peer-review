- Presents formal analysis of MCTS convergence in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games.  
- Proof technique and results offer a more comprehensive understanding than prior work.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and accessible to readers with game theory/AI background.  
- Provides thorough introduction and detailed description of MCTS and its variants.  
- Main contribution: formal proof that MCTS with regret-matching converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirical comparisons of MCTS variants demonstrate effectiveness of the proposed approach.  
- Proof relies on an inductive approach assuming optimal strategies at leaf nodes, aiding clarity.  
- Major limitation: limited provable insights about inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Analysis would be stronger if connected to subgame-perfect concepts and relevant literature (e.g., Harris 1990).  
- Originality: builds on prior work but contributes significant new proof techniques and results.  
- Quality/significance: technically sound and provides a theoretical foundation for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Suggestions: provide more insights about inner nodes and explicitly connect/cite subgame-perfect equilibrium literature.
