- Addresses a significant theoretical gap: MCTS convergence in zero-sum simultaneous-move games.  
- Proposes a general MCTS template that can use any ε-Hannan-consistent selection method with exploration.  
- Proves asymptotic convergence of the algorithm to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof uses a layered approach: matrix games → repeated matrix games with bounded error → composition for full game trees.  
- Leverages online learning theory and Hannan-consistent algorithms (e.g., regret matching, Exp3).  
- Formal analysis is sound, well-structured, and mathematically precise.  
- Empirical evaluation confirms convergence on random and worst-case games.  
- Compares mean-value (theoretical) vs sample-value (practical) propagation: both converge; sample slightly outperforms mean.  
- Writing is clear, proofs well-motivated, and experimental design appropriate.  
- Good contextualization relative to prior MCTS and game-theoretic literature.  
- Minor notational issues (e.g., "leafs" vs "leaves", inconsistent tuple notation, wording) but easily fixable.  
- Primary limitation: results are asymptotic with no finite-time convergence rates provided.  
- Reviewer recommends exploring concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., O(√T) for Exp3) to obtain explicit rates.  
- Practical impact somewhat limited by asymptotic nature and incremental relation to known sequential-game MCTS results.  
- Empirical hints that the theoretical framework may extend to other MCTS variants beyond those formally covered.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid, novel theoretical contribution, and appropriate for acceptance despite the rate limitation.
