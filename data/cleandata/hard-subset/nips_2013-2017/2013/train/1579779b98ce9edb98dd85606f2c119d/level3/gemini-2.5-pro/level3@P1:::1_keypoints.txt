- Paper proves that MCTS with an ε-Hannan consistent selection function and sufficient exploration converges to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move, perfect-information, zero-sum extensive-form games.  
- Provides a general MCTS template instantiable with different action selection mechanisms and demonstrates convergence using Regret Matching and Exp3.  
- Theoretical contribution: first formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, linking MCTS convergence to regret-minimization properties.  
- Clear, well-organized exposition that builds from single-matrix regret results to noisy-payoff inductive proofs for full game trees.  
- Thorough empirical validation on random and worst-case games, comparing mean-propagation and current-sample update rules and confirming convergence.  
- Strength: places MCTS on a firmer theoretical footing by leveraging online learning/regret-minimization theory.  
- Strength: experiments corroborate theory and explore practical convergence rates and challenges.  
- Weakness: proof technique is a relatively straightforward extension of known regret-learning results and may be seen as incremental.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis covers mean-propagation updates, while practice favors current-sample updates that converge faster; paper lacks theoretical explanation for this gap.  
- Weakness: minimal discussion of why UCB/UCT fails in this setting and how regret-based methods address those failures.  
- Overall assessment: solid, technically sound contribution that meaningfully advances theory for MCTS in this game class despite some limitations and gaps between theory and practice.
