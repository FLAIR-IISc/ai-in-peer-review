- Paper studies convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).
- Introduces a general MCTS template that can be instantiated with any regret-minimizing procedure for matrix games.
- Proves that using an ε-Hannan consistent selection method with guaranteed exploration yields convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Extends prior work (Lanctot et al. 2013) by providing a formal convergence proof rather than preliminary analysis.
- Main strength: clear theoretical contribution—the first formal convergence analysis for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.
- Writing is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with proper definitions and logical flow.
- Experiments (using regret matching and Exp3) effectively complement theory, showing convergence on random and worst-case games.
- Quality, originality, and significance are high, filling an important gap in the literature.
- Weakness: limited provable insights about inner (non-leaf) nodes of the game tree.
- Major shortcoming: inadequate connection between the inductive proof and subgame perfect Nash equilibrium claims.
- Recommendation: cite Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games to strengthen the argument.
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable paper but needs a clearer link to subgame perfection.
