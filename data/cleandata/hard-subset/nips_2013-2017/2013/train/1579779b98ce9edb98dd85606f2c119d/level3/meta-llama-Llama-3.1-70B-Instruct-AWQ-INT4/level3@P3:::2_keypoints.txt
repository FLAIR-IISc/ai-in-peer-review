- Presents a formal convergence analysis of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Provides a general MCTS template that can use any regret-minimizing matrix-game procedure for action selection.  
- Proves that an ε-Hannan consistent selection function (ensuring each action is sampled infinitely often) yields convergence to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with thorough background and definitions.  
- Formal analysis is rigorous and uses an inductive proof assuming optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Shows algorithm converges near optimal strategies at leaves and extends convergence up the tree inductively.  
- Empirical evaluations with regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games support theoretical claims.  
- Empirical convergence speed is comparable to recent MCTS algorithms for these games.  
- Builds on Lanctot et al. (2013) and offers a more comprehensive and rigorous treatment.  
- Major weakness: limited provable insights about inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Analysis does not adequately connect to subgame-perfect Nash equilibria and broader game-theoretic tools.  
- Suggestion: tie analysis to subgame-perfect concepts and cite Harris (1990) on existence of subgame-perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Technically sound and honest about strengths and weaknesses; claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Original and significant contribution that advances the state of the art.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and expand discussion of implications and applications.  
- Rating: 8/10.
