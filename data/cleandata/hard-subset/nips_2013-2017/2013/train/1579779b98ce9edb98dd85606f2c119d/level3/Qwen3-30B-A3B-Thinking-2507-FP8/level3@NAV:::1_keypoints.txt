- Studies MCTS for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proving convergence to approximate Nash equilibria under specific conditions.  
- Introduces a general MCTS framework where selection can use any regret-minimizing procedure and shows -Hannan consistent selection with guaranteed exploration yields convergence to subgame-perfect -Nash equilibria.  
- Empirical validation with regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games supports the theoretical claims.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides a useful sufficient condition for convergence that advances understanding in this domain.  
- Theoretical results are technically sound and properly formalized, with appropriate empirical validation.  
- Proof techniques largely build on established Hannan consistency results from online learning, leading to a weakness in originality.  
- Major omission: no analysis of UCB/UCT algorithms, which are widely used in MCTS applications.  
- Authors should explain how their framework relates to UCT and why convergence guarantees do not apply to UCT (as noted by prior work).  
- Discrepancy: proofs cover convergence for mean propagation, but experiments show convergence with current sample-value propagation without explanation.  
- Contribution is technically interesting but incremental and limited to a specific class of games, not a substantial advance for game theory or RL.  
- Recommendation: reject due to limited novelty and insufficient significance for publication at NIPS.
