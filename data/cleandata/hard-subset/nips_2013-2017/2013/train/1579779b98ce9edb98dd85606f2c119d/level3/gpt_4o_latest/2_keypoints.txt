- Paper analyzes MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron), extending theory beyond sequential games.
- Builds on Lanctot et al. (2013) and proves convergence to approximate Nash equilibria using ε-Hannan-consistent selection methods (regret matching, Exp3).
- Provides empirical evidence showing convergence on random and worst-case games, supporting the theoretical claims.
- Technical quality: rigorous, well-structured proofs using an inductive approach from leaf nodes to inner nodes.
- Clarity: clearly written and organized, with systematic presentation of definitions, algorithms, proofs, and visualized empirical results.
- Originality: novel formal analysis of MCTS in simultaneous-move settings, generalizing prior results.
- Significance: convergence guarantees justify MCTS use in these games and could influence future game-theory and AI research.
- Weakness: limited, less comprehensive analysis of inner-node dynamics and subgame play beyond bounded-error assumptions.
- Weakness: does not explicitly connect results to subgame perfect Nash equilibria or cite foundational related work (e.g., Harris 1990).
- Suggestion: expand the inner-node analysis to better characterize their role in convergence.
- Suggestion: explicitly link results to subgame perfect Nash equilibria and incorporate relevant citations (e.g., Harris 1990).
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
