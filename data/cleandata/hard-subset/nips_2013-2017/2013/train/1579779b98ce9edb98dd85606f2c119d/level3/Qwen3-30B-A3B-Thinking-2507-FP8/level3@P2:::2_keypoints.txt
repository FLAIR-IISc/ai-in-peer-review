- Addresses theoretical gap: convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Proposes a general MCTS template that can use any regret-minimizing matrix-game selection method.  
- Main theoretical claim: if the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent and ensures exploration, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirical validation using regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games supports the theoretical results.  
- Mean-propagation MCTS variants converge as expected; RMM converges slightly slower than RM but is effective.  
- Builds on Lanctot et al. (2013) and provides the first formal convergence guarantees for this game class.  
- Presentation strength: clear organization, precise definitions, well-structured proofs, and inductive proof of Theorem 4.11 from leaves upward.  
- Strength: formal convergence proof filling a significant theoretical gap.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation that corroborates theory.  
- Strength: clear distinction from prior work and strong originality.  
- Weakness: insufficient connection to subgame perfect Nash equilibria—Theorem 4.11's claim of subgame perfect ε-NE is not properly established and needs relevant citations (e.g., Harris 1990).  
- Weakness: limited provable insight about inner nodes—error propagation through the tree is underanalyzed and the D² + δ bound lacks intuitive justification.  
- Overall significance: high, providing theoretical justification for practical MCTS use in game-theoretic applications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the subgame perfection connection and clarify inner-node error propagation.
