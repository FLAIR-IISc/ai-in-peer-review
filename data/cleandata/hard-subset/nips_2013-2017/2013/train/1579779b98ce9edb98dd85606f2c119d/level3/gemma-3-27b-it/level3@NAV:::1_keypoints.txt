- Studies convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Proposes a general MCTS algorithm template and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under -Hannan consistent selection and sufficient exploration.  
- Empirically validates theoretical results using Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games.  
- Addresses an important theoretical gap in MCTS for simultaneous-move games.  
- Presentation, organization, and connections to related work are clear and effective.  
- Proof technique uses -Hannan consistency and induction, which is logical but appears straightforward.  
- Lacks discussion of similar proof techniques used in other domains, weakening the novelty claim.  
- Does not analyze or compare to widely used UCB/UCT algorithms, a notable omission given prior UCB analyses.  
- Fails to explain the discrepancy between theoretical guarantees for mean-value propagation and empirical success of sample-value propagation.  
- Contribution is technically sound but considered incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen novelty argument, include UCB/UCT comparison, and explain the mean vs sample propagation gap.
