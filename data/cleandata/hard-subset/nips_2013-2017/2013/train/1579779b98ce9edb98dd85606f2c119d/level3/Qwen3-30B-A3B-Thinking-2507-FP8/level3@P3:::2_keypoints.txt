- Presents a formal convergence analysis of MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Introduces a general MCTS template and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium when using ε-Hannan consistent selection (e.g., regret matching, Exp3).  
- Uses an inductive proof from leaf (matrix games) to root to establish approximate subgame perfect Nash equilibrium convergence.  
- Requires selection methods to be ε-Hannan consistent and to guarantee exploration.  
- Provides empirical validation on random and worst-case games confirming theoretical claims.  
- First formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, extending prior preliminary work (Lanctot et al., 2013).  
- Strong theoretical contribution bridging online learning (Hannan consistency) and game-theoretic MCTS analysis.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and mathematically precise with reproducible experimental detail.  
- Experimental section thoughtfully examines theoretical requirements (mean propagation vs. sample values) and worst-case performance.  
- Limited provable insight about inner nodes: inductive step assumes optimal leaf strategies and does not fully characterize error propagation upward.  
- Does not explicitly connect results to subgame perfect Nash equilibrium despite claiming subgame perfection; could leverage existing game-theoretic concepts more clearly.  
- Fails to cite Harris (1990), which proves existence of SPE in simultaneous-move games and would strengthen theoretical foundation.  
- Assumes complete tree building in analysis, which may not hold in practical, computationally-limited MCTS implementations.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant, and well-presented; minor weaknesses are addressable — reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
