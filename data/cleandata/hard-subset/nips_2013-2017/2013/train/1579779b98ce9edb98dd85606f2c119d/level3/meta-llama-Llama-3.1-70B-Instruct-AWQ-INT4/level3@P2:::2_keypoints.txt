- Presents formal convergence analysis of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Provides a general MCTS template instantiable by various selection methods.  
- Proves that if a selection method is ε-Hannan consistent in matrix games and meets exploration requirements, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Clear, concise introduction and background, including definitions like ε-Hannan consistency.  
- Uses an inductive proof assuming optimal strategies at leaf nodes to show convergence; proof is well-presented and easy to follow.  
- Includes empirical evaluations demonstrating practical effectiveness.  
- Major strength: high clarity and readability; complex ideas presented accessibly.  
- Limitation: analysis does not connect to subgame-perfect concepts or related game-theoretic literature (e.g., Harris 1990).  
- Limitation: limited provable insights about MCTS behavior at inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Originality: builds on Lanctot et al. (2013) but delivers a more comprehensive and rigorous analysis.  
- Significance: high—results are relevant for developing more effective MCTS algorithms.  
- Ratings summary: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant formal contribution, strong analysis, well-written.  
- Rejection concerns: lacks subgame-perfect connection and deeper inner-node analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to address the noted limitations in future work.
