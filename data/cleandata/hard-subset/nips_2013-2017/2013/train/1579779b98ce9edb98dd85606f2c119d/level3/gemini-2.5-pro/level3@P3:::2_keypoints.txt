- Formal analysis of MCTS for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general SM-MCTS template using regret-minimizers (focus on regret-matching and Exp3) for in-tree selection.  
- Main contribution: proof that SM-MCTS converges to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium.  
- Closes an important theoretical gap beyond prior, less-formal analyses (e.g., Lanctot et al. 2013).  
- Paper is technically sound, exceptionally well-written, and clearly organized.  
- Empirical results reasonably validate the theory and explore practical variants (propagating mean vs sample values).  
- Elegant inductive proof technique that establishes convergence in leaf subgames and propagates up the tree.  
- Weakness: limited insight into inner-node strategy dynamics during early and intermediate search stages.  
- Weakness: insufficient connection to broader game-theory literature on subgame perfection and related equilibrium results.  
- Suggest citation of foundational work (e.g., Harris 1990) and clearer comparison to classical backward-induction/LP-based solvers to highlight trade-offs.  
- Overall assessment: strong and novel theoretical contribution that would benefit from better situating within the game-theory community.
