- Addresses theoretical convergence of MCTS for two-player, zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general SM-MCTS template and proves convergence to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium.  
- Core theoretical condition: node action selection must be ε-Hannan consistent and ensure sufficient exploration.  
- Empirically validates theory using Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation with a stepwise proof (matrix games → bounded error → induction).  
- Theoretical contribution provides a practical, formal criterion for designing convergent MCTS variants and connects MCTS to online learning/regret minimization.  
- Experiments are well-designed, including comparison of mean-value propagation (theoretical) vs. sample-value propagation (practical) and worst-case game analysis.  
- Proof technique largely extends established online learning results (Hannan consistency), so technical novelty is moderate.  
- Paper dismisses UCB/UCT quickly without deeper analysis; lacks discussion on why UCB fails or whether it could be fixed to meet ε-Hannan consistency.  
- Gap between theory and experiments: convergence proof covers mean-value propagation only, yet standard sample-value propagation also converges (often faster) with no explanation or conjecture.  
- Overall evaluation: high quality and clarity, moderate originality, good significance; valuable contribution despite limited novelty and theory–experiment disconnect.
