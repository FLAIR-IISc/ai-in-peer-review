- The paper analyzes MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, both theoretically and empirically.  
- Proposes a general MCTS framework using no-regret selection methods (regret matching, Exp3) and proves asymptotic convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Extends convergence guarantees to simultaneous-move games, filling a gap in the literature.  
- Relies on ε-Hannan consistent selection methods, grounded in online learning and game theory.  
- Experiments compare mean reward propagation to standard sample value propagation and empirically confirm convergence to ε-NE.  
- Evaluation includes worst-case games, providing deeper insights into performance.  
- Paper is technically sound, clear, and well-structured with detailed algorithmic and proof explanations.  
- Main limitation: results are asymptotic and do not provide finite-time convergence rates.  
- No analysis of finite-time rates under specific regret bounds (e.g., √T), reducing practical relevance.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental, building on established MCTS and regret-minimization concepts.  
- Minor presentation issues: inconsistent notation and typographical errors (e.g., "leafs" vs "leaves").  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; strengths are rigor and novelty, weaknesses are lack of rates and incremental nature.
