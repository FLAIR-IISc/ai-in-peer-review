- Studies MCTS for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proposing a general MCTS template using regret-minimizing selection.
- Proves that an ε-Hannan consistent selection method with required exploration yields convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Empirically validates the theory using regret matching and Exp3 on randomly generated and worst-case games.
- Builds on prior MCTS and regret-minimization literature and fills a gap by providing convergence guarantees for simultaneous-move games.
- Technical analysis is rigorous and proofs are well-structured, but largely rely on established Hannan-consistency results, limiting novelty.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough but would benefit from more detailed analysis of convergence rates.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, with consistent notation and effective figures.
- Does not explain why empirical success with current sample-value propagation matches a theory that only covers mean propagation.
- Originality: extends convergence guarantees to a new game class, but the sufficient condition (ε-Hannan consistency) is not highly novel and common algorithms like UCB/UCT are not analyzed.
- Significance: provides useful theoretical grounding for MCTS in simultaneous-move games but is an incremental contribution with limited broader ML impact.
- Strengths: clear presentation, rigorous empirical support, appropriate comparison to game-theory work, and readable manuscript.
- Weaknesses: proof technique lacks novelty, omission of UCB/UCT analysis, theoretical scope limited to mean propagation while experiments use sample propagation, and overall incremental contribution.
- Recommendation: borderline accept/reject; accept only if authors address proof novelty, explain the propagation discrepancy, and discuss UCB/UCT variants.
