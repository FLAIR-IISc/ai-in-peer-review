- Presents a general MCTS template for zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proves convergence to a subgame perfect ε-Nash when using an ε-Hannan consistent selection function.  
- Empirically evaluates the theory using two ε-Hannan consistent methods: regret matching and Exp3.  
- Main contribution: theoretical proof of asymptotic convergence to ε-Nash for simultaneous-move games.  
- Empirical results support the theoretical convergence claim in practice.  
- Paper is well-written and technically correct; proofs are thorough and organized.  
- Empirical evaluation is well-designed and provides useful performance insights.  
- Limitation: no explicit convergence rates are provided—only asymptotic guarantees.  
- Suggestion: investigate whether concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T from EXP) yield explicit rates.  
- Empirical finding: propagating sample-value outperforms mean-value propagation slightly.  
- Brute-force search on small games (depth 2, branching 2, utilities {0,0.5,1}) shows similar convergence rates, with mean propagation slower.  
- Possible concern about novelty/incrementality relative to known sequential-move MCTS results.  
- Minor issues: typos and notation problems need correction.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to fix typos/notation.
