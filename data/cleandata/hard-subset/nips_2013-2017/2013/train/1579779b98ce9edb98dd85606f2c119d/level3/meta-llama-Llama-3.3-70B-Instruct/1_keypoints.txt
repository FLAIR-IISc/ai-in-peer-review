Paper proves any Hannan-consistent algorithm can be used to build an MCTS that converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
Formal analysis and proofs are rigorous, clearly explained, and convincing.  
Empirical evaluation shows algorithms converge to approximate Nash equilibria and compares convergence rates of different variants.  
Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and accessible with well-explained definitions and notation.  
Strength: excellent clarity, readability, organization, and logical flow.  
Weakness: novelty of the proof technique is unclear and needs more context and comparison to prior work.  
Weakness: limited analysis of the widely-used UCB algorithm; a detailed convergence analysis for UCB is lacking.  
Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address concerns about novelty and UCB analysis.  
Pro-accept points: rigorous formal results, valuable empirical insights, and clear presentation.  
Con-accept points: unclear contribution novelty and incomplete treatment of UCB.
