- Paper studies SM-MCTS in two-player zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proposes a template using ε-Hannan-consistent selection rules with guaranteed exploration.
- Main theoretical result: SM-MCTS with mean backups and such selection rules converges almost surely to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium with error O(ε D^2).
- Proof approach: reduce nodes to repeated matrix games, apply no-regret→approximate-NE results, and inductively compose bounded errors up the tree.
- Empirical validation uses RM and Exp3 on random and adversarial depth-2 games, comparing mean-backup to current-sample backup.
- Fills a gap between practical SM-MCTS success and lack of convergence guarantees, complementing pruning/backward-induction methods.
- Strength: clear problem statement and well-scoped setting.
- Strength: provides a general sufficient condition (ε-Hannan consistency + exploration) for convergence under mean backups.
- Strength: clean, modular analysis linking matrix games to tree-wide guarantees.
- Strength: empirical results consistent with theory and informative about mean vs sample backups.
- Strength: paper is clearly written with explicit definitions and assumptions.
- Weakness: proof technique leverages standard no-regret facts and is incremental; novelty mainly in composition/specialization.
- Weakness: no analysis or empirical comparison to UCT/UCB variants, despite their prevalence and known pathological behavior.
- Weakness: theoretical requirement of mean backups mismatches common sample-backup practice; discrepancy unexplained and unproven.
- Weakness: bounds (O(ε D^2) and constants) appear loose and asymptotic; no finite-sample rates linking regret to exploitability.
- Weakness: limited discussion of scope relative to CFR, double-oracle, and practical runtime/practicality comparisons.
- Weakness: experiments modest in scope; missing variability reporting, sensitivity to hyperparameters, scaling, and wall-clock performance.
- Quality: technically sound proofs aligning with online-learning theory; empirical evaluation consistent but limited.
- Clarity: generally clear; minor fixes needed (explicit ε in theorem, error-propagation intuition, unify experiment notation).
- Originality: moderate — useful consolidation rather than a fundamentally new technique.
- Significance: useful for SM-MCTS practitioners seeking convergence guarantees, but incremental without UCT analysis or finite-time guidance.
- Pros for acceptance: first formal convergence guarantee under broad practical conditions; subgame-perfect guarantee and empirical validation.
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, loose/asymptotic bounds, omission of UCT analysis, theory–practice backup mismatch, and small-scale experiments.
- Key questions for authors include tightening D^2 dependence, extending analysis to sample backups or providing counterexamples, effects of annealed exploration, empirical comparisons to UCT/fixes, and deriving finite-time exploitability bounds.
- Recommendation: technically interesting and clear but incremental; reviewer leans toward weak accept or borderline depending on community valuation of consolidation.
