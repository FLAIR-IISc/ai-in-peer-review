- Main contribution: proves MCTS with any ε-Hannan consistent selection converges asymptotically to an ε‑Nash equilibrium in zero‑sum, perfect‑information, simultaneous‑move games.
- Significance: advances theoretical foundations for MCTS in simultaneous‑move games beyond prior empirical work.
- Motivation/positioning: clearly motivated, explains limitations of backward induction, and situates contribution relative to prior MCTS and game‑theory results.
- Formalization and proofs: rigorous, sound theoretical development building on Hannan consistency and regret minimization.
- Generality: framework applies to any ε‑Hannan consistent selection method, enabling broad algorithmic integration.
- Experiments: empirical evaluation supports theoretical claims and compares propagation strategies (mean vs. sample), but does not provide convergence rates.
- Strength — theoretical rigor: convergence proof fills an important gap for MCTS in this game class.
- Strength — clarity: well‑written, logically structured, notation mostly clear and algorithm concise.
- Strength — empirical validation: experiments offer practical insight into algorithm behavior despite limited scope.
- Weakness — lack of convergence rates: paper does not provide explicit rates; stronger regret assumptions (e.g., √T) might help and are suggested for future work.
- Weakness — incremental contribution: extension from sequential to simultaneous moves is non‑trivial but may be seen as incremental without tighter rates.
- Weakness — minor technical issues: typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., “leafs”, “inverted”, inconsistent action notation) hurt polish.
- Weakness — limited experiments: experiments focus on random/worst‑case games and lack broader benchmarks or comparisons to existing algorithms.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix notation/typos and discuss potential for deriving convergence rates under stronger regret bounds.
- Overall assessment/rating: solid, technically correct contribution that warrants acceptance with minor revisions.
