- Paper studies SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proposes a general SM-MCTS template using ε-Hannan-consistent selection with guaranteed exploration.  
- Main theoretical result: with ε-Hannan-consistent, exploring selection, empirical strategies converge to an O(ε·poly(D))-subgame-perfect approximate NE (D = tree depth).  
- Proof sketch: analyze ε-Hannan-consistent play in matrix games → extend to bounded-payoff-error games → compose errors up the tree → require mean-value backups (SM-MCTS-M) to control bias.  
- Empirical evaluation: compares mean-value vs. sample-value backups and regret matching vs. Exp3 on random and adversarial small games; both converge, mean backups slightly slower.  
- Context: fills a theoretical gap where UCT lacks guarantees for simultaneous moves; builds on classical no-regret results and tailors them to tree-structured simultaneous moves.  
- Strength: first general asymptotic convergence guarantee for MCTS in simultaneous-move perfect-information games under broad selection rules.  
- Strength: clean, modular proof structure and reconciliation between empirical/average strategies and exploration mixing.  
- Strength: empirical validation supports theoretical trends and assumptions are clearly stated (bounded utilities, guaranteed exploration).  
- Weakness: only asymptotic guarantees — no finite-sample/exploitability rates to guide practice or parameter choices.  
- Weakness: approximation factor grows with depth (roughly ε·D^2 + δ); bound may be loose and pessimistic for deep trees.  
- Weakness: proof requires mean-value backups but sample-value backups perform better empirically; analysis for sample backups is missing.  
- Weakness: experiments are small-scale and synthetic (worst-case depth ≤ 2); lacks benchmarks like Goofspiel or Tron and deeper trees.  
- Weakness: exploration mixing causes an exploitability floor ~O(γ); guidance on choosing γ and interaction with depth/regret is lacking.  
- Quality: technically sound reduction from repeated games to tree induction; lemmas align with no-regret theory though D^2 constants could be better explained or tightened.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well organized; should explicitly state where mean backups are required and summarize source of D^2 dependence.  
- Originality: convergence theorem for SM-MCTS under no-regret selection appears new and addresses a recognized theoretical gap.  
- Significance: scientifically meaningful as a first general convergence guarantee, but practical impact limited by missing finite-time rates and backup mismatch.  
- Suggested extensions: derive finite-time exploitability bounds using known regret rates R(T), extend analysis to sample backups via concentration/martingale arguments, and test larger D and benchmarks.  
- Suggested algorithmic ideas: consider variance-reduced backups, non-uniform or decaying exploration schedules, or exploration tied to visit counts to reduce limiting exploitability.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel rigorous convergence result, solid theory grounded in no-regret learning, and supportive empirical evidence.  
- Cons against acceptance: lacks finite-time rates, depth-dependent bound likely loose, relies on mean backups that underperform in practice, and experiments are limited.  
- Minor comments: fix typos ("leafs" → "leaves"), unify notation for actions and strategies (i,j vs. a, σ̂ vs. σ̄), and clarify Algorithm 1 action notation and Player 2 utility description.
