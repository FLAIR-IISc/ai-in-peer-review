- Proves that MCTS with an ε-Hannan consistent selection method converges to an ε-Nash equilibrium in zero-sum simultaneous-move games.  
- Frames MCTS as a general template instantiable with different selection methods, broadening applicability.  
- Well-motivated positioning against backward induction and computationally expensive alternatives.  
- Clearly defines concepts and establishes a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Empirical evaluation compares mean-value versus sample-value propagation, showing slight underperformance for mean-value.  
- Experiments attempt to probe convergence rates but fail to provide clear characterization of speed.  
- Major weakness: no explicit convergence rates are given, limiting practical utility.  
- Reviewer suggests exploring concrete regret-rate assumptions (e.g., √T from EXP3) to derive explicit rates.  
- Contribution feels somewhat incremental relative to existing MCTS theory for sequential-move games.  
- Paper is technically sound and generally well-written.  
- Minor polish issues: typos (e.g., “leafs” → “leaves”, “inverted” → “negated”) and notation inconsistencies (Algorithm 1 uses (a1,a2) vs (i,j)).  
- Overall assessment: Accept with minor revisions; fix typos/notation and prominently acknowledge the convergence-rate limitation.
