- Presents formal analysis proving a general MCTS template with ε-Hannan consistent selection (with exploration) converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move extensive-form games.  
- Main theoretical result is Theorem 4.11 which formalizes this convergence.  
- Empirically validates the theory using regret matching (RMM) and Exp3 on random and worst-case games, showing convergence to approximate Nash equilibria.  
- Shows mean propagation variant (RMM) is consistent but slightly slower than current sample value variant.  
- Builds correctly on online learning (Hannan consistency) and game theory (Nash equilibrium) with appropriate citations.  
- Addresses related work, including prior negative result that UCB does not converge to Nash equilibrium, and positions contribution relative to prior research.  
- Significant contribution: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games used widely in practice without prior theoretical justification.  
- Minor limitation: analysis assumes the entire game tree is eventually built and disregards incremental tree building, leaving finite-time implications unaddressed.  
- Suggests future work to provide finite-time bounds for practical applicability.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, reproducible, and the overall recommendation is acceptance.
