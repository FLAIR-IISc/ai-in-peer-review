- First formal convergence analysis of MCTS for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.
- Proposes a general MCTS template instantiable with any regret-minimizing matrix-game procedure and proves convergence to subgame perfect ε-Nash under ε-Hannan consistency with guaranteed exploration.
- Empirical tests using regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games confirm the theoretical claims.
- Fills an important literature gap: previous MCTS convergence guarantees applied only to sequential games; applicable to domains like security and general game-playing.
- Strong, elegant theoretical contribution using induction over the game tree and bounded-error repeated matrix-game results.
- Precise formalization of required properties (ε-Hannan consistency and guaranteed exploration).
- Thorough and well-designed empirical evaluation, including analysis of mean vs sample value propagation and identification of worst-case games.
- Exceptionally clear writing, consistent notation, precise definitions, and sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Simplifying assumption that the full game tree is built before convergence weakens practical relevance; needs analysis of tree-building convergence rates.
- Theorem 4.11’s D² + δ bound is loose; paper lacks empirical evidence on how convergence scales with game depth and its practical impact.
- Unclear which MCTS variants fall outside the framework; should identify and discuss specific algorithms (e.g., UCT) and reasons for exclusion.
- Worst-case empirical evaluation is limited (depth-2, branching factor 2); testing on larger games needed to demonstrate scalability.
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution, accept with minor revisions to address the above weaknesses.
