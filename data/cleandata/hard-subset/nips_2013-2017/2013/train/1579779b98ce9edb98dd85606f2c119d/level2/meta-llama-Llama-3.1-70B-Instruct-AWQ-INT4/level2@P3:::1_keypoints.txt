- Paper analyzes convergence of MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, using a general MCTS template with regret-minimizing selection functions.  
- Proves that using an ε-Hannan consistent selection function guarantees convergence to a subgame perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirically evaluates convergence with two ε-Hannan consistent methods: regret matching and Exp3.  
- Novel contribution: first formal convergence analysis of MCTS for this game class.  
- Rigorous proof: provides a formal, step-by-step convergence proof.  
- Empirical evaluation: experiments give practical insight into algorithm performance.  
- Limited scope: results are restricted to a specific class of games and may not generalize.  
- Complex proof: mathematical complexity may be hard to follow for non-expert readers.  
- Limited experiments: empirical evaluation covers a narrow set of games and algorithms.  
- Quality: technically sound with a well-presented rigorous proof.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear introductions, though proofs are complex.  
- Originality: results are novel and significant for game theory and AI.  
- Significance: findings have important implications for improving MCTS in practice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous theory and informative experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow scope, proof complexity, and limited empirical generalization.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address proof accessibility and broaden empirical scope.
