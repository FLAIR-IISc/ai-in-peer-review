- Summary: Proposes SM-MCTS using any ε–Hannan consistent selection rule with exploration and proves almost-sure convergence to subgame-perfect approximate NE with bound O(D^2 ε); supports theory with experiments.  
- Main theoretical result: If per-node selection is ε–HC with exploration, SM-MCTS converges to an O(D^2 ε)-approximate subgame-perfect NE (Theorem 4.11).  
- Proof approach: Shows ε–HC convergence for repeated matrix games, bounds backup bias from mean values, and inducts up the tree.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments with regret matching and Exp3 on random and hard games confirm convergence and study mean vs. sample backups.  
- Strength — novelty: First general convergence guarantee for MCTS in perfect-information simultaneous-move games where UCT can fail.  
- Strength — generality: Analysis applies to any ε–HC selection rule with exploration (e.g., RM, Exp3) and is subgame-perfect without heuristics.  
- Strength — proof quality: Clean decomposition into repeated games with bounded error and explicit handling of exploration-induced bias.  
- Strength — experiments: Empirical results match theory and show modest slowdown from mean backups.  
- Strength — practical insight: Explains why mean backups aid analysis and why uniform exploration yields a nonzero exploitability floor.  
- Weakness — bound tightness: O(D^2 ε) seems loose; possibility of improving to O(D ε) via sharper error composition or weighted backups not explored.  
- Weakness — finite-time rates: Lacks explicit T-dependent convergence/exploitability bounds despite known regret rates for RM/Exp3.  
- Weakness — mean vs sample backups: Main theorem requires mean backups (SM-MCTS-M); no proof for sample backups despite empirical convergence.  
- Weakness — exploration schemes: Two exploration mechanisms differ in bias/variance; presentation should be unified and the scheme used in results/experiments clarified.  
- Weakness — scope/scalability: Theoretic assumption of full tree expansion is strong; experiments are small (depth-2 or random) and lack larger benchmarks or progressive widening discussion.  
- Weakness — related work: Needs clearer comparison to CFR and earlier SM-MCTS/decoupled UCT lines to situate contribution.  
- Quality: Theoretical claims are well-supported and technically sound; assumptions are explicit and experiments align with theory but are limited in scale.  
- Clarity: Overall clear; suggestions include consolidating notation, explicitly flagging mean-backup requirement, and clarifying Lemma 4.9 interpretation.  
- Originality: High — novel bridge between online learning and MCTS for simultaneous-move perfect-information games.  
- Significance: Moderate–high — fills a theoretical gap for SM-MCTS, though finite-sample bounds and tighter depth dependence would increase impact.  
- Questions for authors: Can D^2 be tightened? Can known regret rates yield T-dependent exploitability bounds? Do sample backups converge or is there a counterexample? How sensitive to non-uniform/annealing exploration?  
- Pros for acceptance: First convergence guarantees for SM-MCTS, modular analysis for multiple no-regret algorithms, clean proofs with empirical confirmation.  
- Cons for acceptance: Asymptotic-only guarantees, loose depth dependence and mean-backup requirement, limited experimental scale.
