- Introduces a general MCTS template for zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games.  
- Proves that any selection method that is ε-Hannan consistent in matrix games and has sufficient exploration yields MCTS convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirically evaluates the theory using regret matching and Exp3 on randomly generated and adversarial (worst-case) games.  
- Main contribution: formal convergence analysis and a general MCTS template that leverages ε-Hannan consistent algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides clear explanations of algorithms and proofs.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing convergence rates and showing MCTS with regret matching and Exp3 converges to approximate NE.  
- Originality: provides a new formal analysis of MCTS convergence in simultaneous-move games.  
- Significance: important contribution to algorithmic game theory with practical implications for game-playing agents.  
- Limitation: theoretical analysis is restricted to ε-Hannan consistent algorithms.  
- Limitation: no finite-time convergence bounds are provided.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept (score 9/10).
