- Addresses theoretical gap: convergence of MCTS in simultaneous-move zero-sum perfect-information games.  
- Proposes a general MCTS template showing ε-Hannan consistent selection with guaranteed exploration converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Provides a formal convergence proof using induction over game depth and error bounds in repeated matrix games.  
- Empirically validates theory using regret matching and Exp3 as selection methods.  
- First formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in this specific game class.  
- Theoretical analysis is clear, well-structured, and elegantly executed.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and shows theoretical predictions hold in practice.  
- Writing, notation, and literature positioning are clear and accessible.  
- Limitation: analysis only establishes asymptotic convergence, no finite-time bounds.  
- Limitation: empirical tests are confined to relatively small games (e.g., depth 2, branching factor 2).  
- Limitation: scope restricted to zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (not imperfect information).  
- Suggested future work: extend results to more general game classes (imperfect information) and derive finite-time convergence bounds.  
- Suggested future work: investigate convergence of other MCTS variants conjectured by the authors.  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS due to significant theoretical contribution, solid proofs, and supporting experiments.
