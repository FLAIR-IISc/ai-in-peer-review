- Studies MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information games with simultaneous moves, a setting lacking prior convergence guarantees.  
- Presents a general MCTS template and proves convergence to approximate Nash equilibria under Hannan consistency and adequate exploration.  
- Empirically validates theory using regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games.  
- Addresses an important theoretical gap by extending MCTS analysis beyond sequential games.  
- Focusing on Hannan-consistent selection methods links the work to established online learning theory.  
- Theoretical contribution is significant: rigorous, well-structured proofs with sensible decomposition (analyzing repeated matrix games with error first).  
- Inclusion of exploration guarantees alongside Hannan consistency is an important and often overlooked detail.  
- Proof sketch is sound but many technical details are deferred to appendix/arXiv.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive but lacks detailed statistical analysis (means, stddevs, confidence intervals).  
- Discussion comparing mean vs. current sample propagation is inconclusive; potential slowdown with mean is only marginally observed.  
- Connection to existing SM-MCTS implementations is mentioned but not thoroughly mapped or compared.  
- Identification of “worst-case” games is based on limited runs and lacks a systematic or theoretical justification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical guarantees, rigorous analysis, empirical support, and potential impact on MCTS design.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient statistical rigor in experiments, incomplete exploration of connections to prior SM-MCTS work, and weak justification for worst-case game selection.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed contribution deserving acceptance, with requests for stronger statistical analysis and clearer mapping to existing SM-MCTS implementations.
