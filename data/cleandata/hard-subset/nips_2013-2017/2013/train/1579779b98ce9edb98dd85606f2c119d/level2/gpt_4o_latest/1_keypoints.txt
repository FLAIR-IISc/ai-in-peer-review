- Presents the first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games.
- Proposes a general MCTS template usable with any ε-Hannan consistent selection method (e.g., regret matching, Exp3).
- Proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specified conditions using an inductive multi-level tree argument.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and leverages bounded-error repeated matrix game results.
- Empirical validation on random and worst-case games supports theoretical claims about convergence behavior.
- Template is general and potentially extensible to other MCTS variants, opening future research directions.
- Practical impact is unclear because the proposed methods converge slower than existing MCTS variants.
- Paper lacks finite-time convergence bounds, limiting practical applicability.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to small games with modest depth and branching, raising scalability questions.
- Presentation is dense and technical; key concepts (ε-Hannan consistency, regret matching) are insufficiently explained for non-experts.
- Argument for acceptance: significant theoretical advance with supporting experiments relevant to NeurIPS audience.
- Argument against acceptance: limited practical utility due to slow convergence, no finite-time bounds, and narrow experiments.
- Suggested improvements: derive or discuss finite-time bounds, expand experiments to larger games, and improve clarity and intuitive explanations.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing practical impact and clarity concerns.
