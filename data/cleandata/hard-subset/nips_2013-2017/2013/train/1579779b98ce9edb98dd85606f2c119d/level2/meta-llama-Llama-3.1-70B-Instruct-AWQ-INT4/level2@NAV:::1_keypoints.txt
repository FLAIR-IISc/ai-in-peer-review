- Formal analysis proving MCTS convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Proposes a general MCTS template that can be instantiated with various selection methods.  
- Shows convergence when the selection method is Hannan-consistent and satisfies exploration requirements.  
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical notation and explanations.  
- Empirical evaluations using regret matching and Exp3 demonstrate effective convergence to approximate NE.  
- Strength — originality: novel formal convergence result for this class of games.  
- Strength — technical soundness: rigorous proofs and theoretical analysis.  
- Strength — empirical validation supports theoretical claims.  
- Limitation — complexity: requires high mathematical sophistication, limiting accessibility.  
- Limitation — limited scope: focuses only on zero-sum extensive-form perfect-information simultaneous-move games.  
- Limitation — lacks finite-time convergence bounds.  
- Overall conclusion: significant contribution; strengths outweigh limitations.
