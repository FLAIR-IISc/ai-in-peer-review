- Presents a general MCTS template for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, giving the first formal convergence guarantees for this class.  
- Proves that MCTS converges to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium when the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent in matrix games and meets exploration requirements.  
- Proof strategy: sequence of lemmas establishing convergence for repeated matrix games (with and without bounded error) followed by an inductive argument for the full extensive-form game.  
- Empirical evaluation using regret matching and Exp3 on randomly generated and worst-case games supports the theoretical results.  
- Significant theoretical contribution filling a gap: prior MCTS work for simultaneous-move games lacked convergence guarantees.  
- Elegant conceptual connection established between online learning (Hannan consistency) and MCTS.  
- Formal analysis is technically sound, with careful definitions, well-structured proofs, and appropriate citations.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with precise algorithm descriptions and a comprehensive background section.  
- Practical relevance emphasized: applicable to security domains and general game-playing where simultaneous moves occur.  
- Main weakness: no discussion or intuition about the tightness of the convergence bound (D² + δ); quadratic depth dependence may be prohibitive and unexplored.  
- Minor limitation: empirical evaluation focuses mainly on depth-2, branching-factor-2 games; needs deeper and more varied experiments and more empirical evidence for broader convergence claims.  
- High significance and potential impact; opens new research directions at the intersection of online learning, game theory, and search.  
- Well-positioned for NIPS given its technical contribution and relevance to ML/AI communities.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound and novel work that should be accepted, with suggested improvements on bound tightness discussion and expanded experiments.
