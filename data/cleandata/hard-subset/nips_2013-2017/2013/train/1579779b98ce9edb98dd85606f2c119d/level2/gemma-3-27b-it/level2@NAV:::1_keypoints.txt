- Studies MCTS convergence in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, a setting lacking prior guarantees.  
- Proposes a general MCTS template and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under -Hannan consistency and required exploration.  
- Assumes a -Hannan consistent selection method with explicit exploration requirements as core theoretical conditions.  
- Empirically validates theory using Regret Matching and Exp3 on randomly generated and worst-case games.  
- Extends theoretical understanding of MCTS beyond two-player sequential games.  
- Formal analysis is rigorous and conditions are clearly defined and related to existing algorithms.  
- Empirical evaluation supports theoretical claims and is strengthened by inclusion of worst-case constructions.  
- Lacks discussion of practical convergence rate; proven convergence may be too slow for complex real-world problems.  
- Does not quantify approximation error or its dependence on game parameters.  
- Exploration parameter γ is set arbitrarily in experiments; sensitivity analysis is missing.  
- Weak connection between the theoretical completed-tree analysis and the practical iterative tree-building implementation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions requesting discussion of practical convergence rates and impact of key parameters.
