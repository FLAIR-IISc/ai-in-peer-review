- Formal analysis of MCTS convergence in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.
- Introduces a general MCTS template and proves selection methods that are ε-Hannan consistent with adequate exploration converge to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Well-organized and clearly written with a helpful introduction and clear explanation of contributions.
- Technical results are sound; Theorem 4.11 proof (average strategies form a subgame perfect (D^2+δ)-NE) is particularly strong.
- Empirical evaluations with regret matching and Exp3 support the theoretical claims.
- Good balance of theoretical and empirical contributions, appealing to both theoretical and applied researchers.
- Assumes perfect information and simultaneous moves, which may limit realism and applicability.
- Does not provide finite-time convergence bounds, limiting practical usefulness.
- Original and significant contribution to game theory and MCTS research.
- Potential to impact AI and game-theory algorithm development.
- May be too specialized for a general conference audience.
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS.
- Rating: 9/10; Confidence: 8/10.
