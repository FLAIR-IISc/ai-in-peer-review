- Proposes a general MCTS template for zero-sum extensive-form games with simultaneous moves, instantiable with any matrix-game regret minimizer.  
- Proves that if the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent with guaranteed exploration, MCTS converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Fills a theoretical gap: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Empirical evaluation (regret matching, Exp3) on random and worst-case games supports the theoretical convergence to approximate Nash equilibria.  
- Framework is general and can accommodate various selection methods, increasing applicability.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, with clear definitions and well-structured proofs grounded in online learning and game theory.  
- Exposition and experiments are clear and well-organized, suitable for expert readers.  
- Originality: significant application of Hannan consistency to MCTS in this game class, though core techniques are drawn from existing literature.  
- Significance: provides both theoretical foundations and practical justification for using MCTS in simultaneous-move domains (e.g., security games, card games).  
- Concern: paper does not address or experimentally compare with UCB-based selection methods widely used in practice.  
- Concern: theory requires mean-value propagation (RMM) but experiments show current-sample RM also converges; more explanation is needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include UCB comparisons and clarify why RM (current samples) empirically converges.
