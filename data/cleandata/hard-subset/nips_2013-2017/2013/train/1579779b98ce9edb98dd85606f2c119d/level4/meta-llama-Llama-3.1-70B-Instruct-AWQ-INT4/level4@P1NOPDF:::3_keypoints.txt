- Presents analysis of a class of MCTS algorithms using essentially any no-regret selection method (with mild restrictions).  
- Primary theoretical contribution: proof of asymptotic convergence to ε‑Nash for simultaneous-move games.  
- Major weakness: convergence is asymptotic only — no finite-sample or convergence rates are provided.  
- This limitation stems from assuming only ε‑Hannan consistency for the selection method.  
- Suggestion: investigate whether specific regret rates (e.g., √T from exponential weights) imply corresponding convergence rates.  
- Experiments compare propagating mean reward values (used in analysis) vs. propagating current sample value; mean values slightly underperform.  
- Experiments also attempt to empirically characterize convergence rates.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and technically correct, but significance is mildly limited by lack of rates.  
- Additional concern: result feels somewhat incremental since MCTS convergence is known for sequential-move games.  
- Nitpicks: Figure 1 “leafs” → “leaves”; Line 144 “inverted” → “negated”; Algorithm 1 Line 10 notation "(a1,a2)" confusing (should be "(i,j)" or "(σ1,σ2)"); pervasive notation inconsistencies between "(i,j)" and "a's".
