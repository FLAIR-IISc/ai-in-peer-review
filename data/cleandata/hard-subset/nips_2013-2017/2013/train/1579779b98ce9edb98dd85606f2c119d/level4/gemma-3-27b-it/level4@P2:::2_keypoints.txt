- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Investigates MCTS with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Main contribution: formal, more rigorous analysis of MCTS convergence compared to prior work (e.g., Lanctot et al. 2013).  
- Proof strategy uses induction with optimal strategies at leaf nodes and appears sound.  
- Key limitation: analysis struggles to produce strong results about inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Overlooks subgame perfect Nash equilibria; strengthening this connection would improve theoretical grounding.  
- Reviewer recommends citing Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Theoretical depth could be improved by analyzing convergence rates or providing tighter approximation bounds.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the cited limitations and adding the suggested reference.
