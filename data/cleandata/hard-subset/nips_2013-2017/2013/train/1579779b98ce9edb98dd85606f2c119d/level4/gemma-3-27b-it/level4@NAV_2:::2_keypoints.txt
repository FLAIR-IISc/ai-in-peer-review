- Paper analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and studies MCTS with regret-matching, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Main contribution is a new formal convergence analysis of MCTS in this game class, improving on prior preliminary work (e.g., Lanctot et al.).
- Paper is clear, well-written, and presents results in a well-motivated way.
- Proof technique uses an inductive approach with optimal strategies at leaf nodes and is technically sound.
- Analysis gives limited insight into inner nodes of the game tree and does not sufficiently address subgame perfect Nash equilibria.
- Paper would be strengthened by explicitly connecting the analysis to subgame perfect equilibrium concepts; Harris (1990) is a relevant citation.
- Argument for acceptance: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.
- Argument for acceptance: analysis is technically sound and well-motivated.
- Argument for acceptance: experimental results support the theoretical findings.
- Argument for acceptance: work builds on and extends existing MCTS and game-theory literature.
- Argument against acceptance: connection to subgame perfect Nash equilibria is not fully explored.
- Argument against acceptance: practical significance of the convergence rate remains unclear despite empirical evaluation.
- Argument against acceptance: analysis is restricted to zero-sum perfect-information games and lacks discussion of generalization to other game classes.
