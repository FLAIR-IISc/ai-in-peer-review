- Studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and analyzes MCTS augmented with regret-matching.  
- Main contribution: formal proof that this MCTS+regret-matching converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in this game class.  
- Paper is clearly written with cohesive presentation and a novel, rigorous inductive proof using optimal terminal-node strategies.  
- Advances theoretical rigor beyond prior preliminary work (Lanctot et al., 2013).  
- Primary strength is the computational demonstration that regret-matching effectively guides MCTS in these games.  
- Major limitation: the analysis ignores subgame perfect Nash equilibria (SPE) and focuses only on root-level equilibrium.  
- Omitting SPE is critical because SPE enforces sequential rationality and strategic consistency across all subgames.  
- Recommendation to integrate SPE: cite Harris (1990) and connect convergence results to SPE-related theory.  
- Suggested investigations: whether MCTS+regret-matching implicitly satisfies SPE conditions or where deviations from SPE occur.  
- Risk: current framing may isolate the work from broader game-theoretic literature and limit its interdisciplinary impact.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to deepen theoretical grounding by engaging with SPE.
