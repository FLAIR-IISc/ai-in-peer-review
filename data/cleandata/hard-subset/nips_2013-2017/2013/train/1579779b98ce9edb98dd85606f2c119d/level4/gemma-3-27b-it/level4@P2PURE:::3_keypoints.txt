- Proves convergence of a class of MCTS algorithms to an ε‑Nash equilibrium in simultaneous‑move games assuming an ε‑Hannan consistent or other no‑regret selection method (with mild restrictions).  
- Key contribution: provides convergence guarantees for simultaneous‑move MCTS, a setting lacking prior theoretical results.  
- Convergence result is asymptotic and does not provide explicit convergence rates.  
- The absence of rates follows from the minimal ε‑Hannan consistency assumption; authors should discuss rates for specific selection methods (e.g., Exp3’s √T regret).  
- Experiments compare propagating mean reward values (used in theory) versus propagating current sample values, finding mean propagation slightly worse.  
- Experiments attempt to investigate empirical convergence rates but do not resolve theoretical rate questions.  
- Overall: technically sound and well‑written; solid contribution but potentially incremental given lack of rates and existing sequential‑move MCTS guarantees.  
- Minor edits: Figure 1 “leafs” → “leaves”; Line 144 use “negated” instead of “inverted.”  
- Notation issues: Algorithm 1’s (a1,a2) is confusing (use (i,j) or (σ1,σ2)); inconsistencies between (i,j) and ‘a’ notation should be fixed.
