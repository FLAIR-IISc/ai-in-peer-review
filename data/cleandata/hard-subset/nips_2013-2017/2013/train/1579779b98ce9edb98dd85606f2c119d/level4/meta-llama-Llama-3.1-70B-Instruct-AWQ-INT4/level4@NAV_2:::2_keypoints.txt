- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Introduces a general MCTS template that uses any regret-minimizing matrix-game procedure for action sampling.  
- Formally proves that with an ε-Hannan-consistent selection function (ensuring infinite sampling of actions), the MCTS converges to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof is inductive from leaf nodes and builds on prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013); presentation is clear and detailed.  
- Empirically compares propagating mean values vs. current sample values in MCTS; both converge, with mean propagation slightly slower.  
- Paper is well-written and results are well-presented.  
- Main contribution: novel formal analysis of regret-matching strategies within MCTS for these games.  
- Weakness: limited explicit connection to the established concept of subgame-perfect Nash equilibria and related game-theoretic literature.  
- Recommendation: strengthen ties to subgame-perfect NE literature (e.g., Harris 1990) and further explore relationships to game-theory concepts.  
- Overall assessment: positive—accept, rated 8/10 with high reviewer confidence.
