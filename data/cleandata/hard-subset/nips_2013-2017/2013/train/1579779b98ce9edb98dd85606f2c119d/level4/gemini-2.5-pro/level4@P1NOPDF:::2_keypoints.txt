- Studies zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).
- Investigates MCTS with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Paper is clear, well-written, and results are effectively presented.
- Main contribution is a novel formal analysis building on prior work (Lanctot et al. [20]).
- Convergence proof uses induction with the assumption of optimal strategies at leaf nodes.
- Significant weakness: limited theoretical guarantees for inner (non-leaf) nodes.
- Analysis does not engage with subgame perfect Nash equilibria or related game-theoretic refinements.
- For perfect-information games, subgame perfection is essential to ensure optimality at every node.
- Recommendation: strengthen the paper by connecting to subgame perfect equilibrium literature (e.g., Harris 1990).
- Overall: valuable computational contribution but would be more complete and impactful with ties to established game-theoretic concepts.
