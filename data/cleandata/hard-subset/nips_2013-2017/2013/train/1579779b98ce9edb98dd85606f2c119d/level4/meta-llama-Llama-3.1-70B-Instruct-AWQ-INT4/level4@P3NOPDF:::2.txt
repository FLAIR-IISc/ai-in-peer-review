This paper provides a thorough analysis of the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous move games, which includes interesting examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors examine the application of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with regret-matching to this class of games and demonstrate convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, effectively presenting the results of the analysis. The primary contribution of the paper lies in its formal analysis, which offers new insights into this class of games. The authors build upon previous work, notably Lanctot et al. (2013), which explored a similar setup but presented more preliminary results. The proof presented in this paper is inductive, relying on optimal strategies at the leaf nodes of the game tree.

However, a significant limitation of this approach is that it does not provide meaningful insights into the inner nodes of the game tree. This oversight neglects key concepts in game theory, particularly subgame perfect Nash equilibria. To strengthen the analysis, it would be beneficial to tie the results to these concepts. A recommended reference for this purpose is "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990).

In conclusion, this paper presents novel analyses of the regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect information simultaneous move games. While the results are interesting from a computational perspective, the analysis falls short by not leveraging the powerful tools of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, which have been extensively studied in game theory. By addressing this limitation, the authors could further enhance the impact of their work.
