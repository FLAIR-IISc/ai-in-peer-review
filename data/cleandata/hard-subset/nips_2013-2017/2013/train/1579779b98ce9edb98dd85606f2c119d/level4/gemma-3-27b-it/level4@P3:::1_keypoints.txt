- Addresses convergence of MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, filling a noted theoretical gap.  
- Main contribution: proves convergence of a general MCTS template when using a Hannan-consistent selection method with appropriate exploration.  
- Empirical results on random and worst-case games validate the theoretical findings.  
- Well-framed motivation and clear connection to prior work and limitations of backward induction.  
- Strength: strong formal theoretical convergence result for simultaneous-move MCTS.  
- Strength: general framework allowing different Hannan-consistent selection methods.  
- Strength: empirical validation using regret matching and Exp3 supports theory.  
- Strength: clear writing, organization, definitions, and notation.  
- Strength: appropriate citation and relation to existing game-theory and MCTS literature.  
- Weakness: novelty of the proof technique could be discussed and compared more thoroughly to prior MCTS convergence proofs.  
- Weakness: does not analyze widely used UCB algorithms; lacks deeper justification or future directions for UCB exclusion.  
- Weakness: theoretical reliance on propagating the mean conflicts with experiments showing current-sample propagation performs similarly; needs more discussion.  
- Weakness: empirical analysis is somewhat limited in game variety and parameters.  
- For acceptance: fills an important theoretical gap, provides a rigorous convergence foundation, and is empirically supported and well-written.  
- Against acceptance: proof-novelty not emphasized, UCB omission is a limitation, and discrepancy between theory and empirical propagation method needs more investigation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, pending revisions to address proof novelty and the theory-vs-empirical propagation discrepancy.
