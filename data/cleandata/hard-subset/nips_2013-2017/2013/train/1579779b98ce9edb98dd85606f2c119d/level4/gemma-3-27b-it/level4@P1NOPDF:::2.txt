This paper analyzes the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, which includes interesting examples like Goofspiel and Tron. The authors analyze Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) implemented with regret matching for this class of games, and prove convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear, well-written, and the results are well-presented. The main contribution lies in its new formal analysis. Previous work by Lanctot et al. [20] (2013) explores a similar setup, but provides a more preliminary analysis. The proof relies on induction, building on optimal strategies at the leaf nodes.

In my opinion, the primary weakness of this approach is the limited scope of what can be proven about the inner nodes of the game tree. This overlooks some key concepts in game theory, particularly subgame perfect Nash equilibria. I believe the paper would benefit from connecting the analysis to these concepts. I recommend citing: Harris, C. (1990). “Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves.” 

This paper presents a novel analysis of regret-matching strategies within an MCTS framework for zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games. While the analysis is interesting from a computational perspective, it would be strengthened by a connection to the well-established tools of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, which have been extensively studied by game theorists.
