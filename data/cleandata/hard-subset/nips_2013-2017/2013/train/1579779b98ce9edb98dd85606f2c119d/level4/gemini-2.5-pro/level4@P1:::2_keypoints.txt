- Addresses convergence of MCTS in two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves.  
- Presents a general MCTS template instantiable with various action selection methods.  
- Main theoretical result: if selection is ε-Hannan consistent with sufficient exploration, MCTS converges to a subgame perfect approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Empirical validation using Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games supports the theory.  
- Novelty: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in this class of games, building on prior empirical work.  
- Technical quality: proofs are carefully constructed, extending from repeated matrix games to multi-stage extensive-form games.  
- Presentation: paper is generally clear, well-written, and logically structured; experiments are well-presented.  
- Weakness: argument that the result is “subgame perfect” is underdeveloped and the link between inductive convergence and subgame perfection needs clearer exposition.  
- Suggestion: more explicitly explain how inner-node behavior guarantees optimal play in every subgame and connect to game-theory literature on subgame perfection (e.g., Harris 1990).
