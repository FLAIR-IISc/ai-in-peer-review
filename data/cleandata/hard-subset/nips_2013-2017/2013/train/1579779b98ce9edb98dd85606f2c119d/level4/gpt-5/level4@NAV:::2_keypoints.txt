- Studies zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Analyzes MCTS augmented with regret matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Manuscript is clear, well-written, and presents results cleanly.  
- Main contribution is new formal analysis compared to prior work.  
- Prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) treated the setup more preliminarily.  
- Proof is inductive and assumes optimal play at leaf nodes.  
- Principal limitation: provides little insight into internal game-tree nodes.  
- Overlooks connection to subgame-perfect Nash equilibria and related game-theory concepts.  
- Recommends citing Harris (1990) on existence of subgame-perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Computationally compelling but insufficiently engages the subgame-perfect NE literature.
