This paper provides an in-depth analysis of the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous move games, which includes interesting examples such as goofspiel and Tron. The authors investigate the application of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) combined with regret-matching to this class of games and prove its convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear and well-written, and the results are well-presented. The main contribution lies in the new results from the formal analysis, which build upon previous work by Lanctot et al. (2013) [20]. Notably, the authors' proof is inductive, relying on optimal strategies at the leaf nodes.

However, I believe that the biggest weakness of this approach is its limited ability to make interesting claims about the inner nodes of the game tree. This oversight neglects key concepts in game theory, particularly subgame perfect Nash equilibria. I think the paper could be improved by integrating its analysis with these concepts. To this end, I recommend citing the work "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990).

In conclusion, this paper presents novel analyses of the regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect information simultaneous move games. While the analysis is interesting from a computational standpoint, it misses the opportunity to connect with the powerful tools of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, which have been well-studied by game theorists.
