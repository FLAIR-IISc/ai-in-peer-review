- Studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, providing convergence analysis to approximate Nash equilibria.  
- Introduces a general MCTS framework that can use any ε-Hannan consistent selection method and proves convergence to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium under appropriate conditions.  
- Provides the first rigorous theoretical foundation for MCTS convergence in this game class, extending prior preliminary analysis by Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- The formal analysis is technically sound and carefully argued.  
- Empirical validation with regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games supports the theoretical claims.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and presented with sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Novelty: represents the first formal convergence proof for MCTS in this setting.  
- Significance: offers important theoretical justification for using MCTS as an approximation algorithm with potential applications (e.g., security domains).  
- Primary weakness: limited integration of subgame-perfect equilibrium concepts and related game-theory literature (suggested citation: Harris 1990).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better connect the analysis to established subgame-perfect equilibrium theory.  
- Pro: significant theoretical contribution backed by thorough experiments.  
- Con: needs deeper theoretical integration with established game-theoretic frameworks.
