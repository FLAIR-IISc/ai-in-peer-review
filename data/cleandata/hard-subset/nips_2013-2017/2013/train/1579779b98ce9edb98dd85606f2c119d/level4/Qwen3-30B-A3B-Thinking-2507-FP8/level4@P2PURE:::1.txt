

This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, establishing that MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specific conditions, with empirical results supporting the theoretical claims.  

The following are my detailed comments:  
1. While the paper presents a clear and useful result providing a sufficient condition for MCTS convergence, the theoretical contribution is limited by the lack of novelty in the proof technique. Given that the primary contribution should be theoretical, the proof approach does not significantly advance existing methods.  
2. The paper omits analysis of UCB algorithms, which are widely used in the literature. Given prior work on UCB for trees, the authors should incorporate UCB algorithms, compare their performance with other methods, and discuss their theoretical convergence properties.  
3. The convergence proof is restricted to mean propagation, yet experiments indicate that propagating current sample values also yields strong empirical performance across various criteria. The authors should provide an explanation for this discrepancy between the theoretical analysis and experimental observations.  

Although the paper's findings are interesting, its technical contribution is modest due to the non-novel proof technique and the omission of key algorithmic comparisons.
