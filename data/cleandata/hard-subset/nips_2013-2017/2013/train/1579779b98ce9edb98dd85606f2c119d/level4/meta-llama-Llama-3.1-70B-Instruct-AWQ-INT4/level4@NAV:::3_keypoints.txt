- Contribution: Analysis of a class of MCTS algorithms using any no-regret selection method (with minor restrictions).
- Theoretical result: Proof of convergence to ε-Nash equilibria in games with simultaneous moves.
- Limitation: Convergence is only asymptotic; no convergence rates are provided.
- Cause of limitation: Asymptotic result follows from assuming only ε-Hannan consistency of the selection method.
- Suggestion: Investigate convergence rates under specific regret bounds (e.g., √T for EXP-type algorithms).
- Experiments: Compared propagating mean reward values vs. current sample values; mean values slightly underperformed.
- Experiments: Attempted empirical estimation of convergence rates.
- Assessment: Paper is technically sound and well-written.
- Significance concern: Impact limited by lack of convergence rates and somewhat incremental given existing sequential-move MCTS convergence results.
