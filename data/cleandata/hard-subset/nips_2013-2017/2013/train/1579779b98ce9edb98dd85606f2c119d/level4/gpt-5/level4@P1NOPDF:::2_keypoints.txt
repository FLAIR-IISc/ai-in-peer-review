- Analyzes zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Studies MCTS with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clear and well-written; main contribution is a formal analysis extending prior work (e.g., Lanctot et al., 2013).  
- Proof is inductive: assumes optimal strategies at leaf nodes and propagates properties up the tree.  
- Main weakness: provides limited insight about strategies at internal nodes and does not connect to subgame-perfect Nash equilibria.  
- Recommendation: strengthen connection to subgame perfection—either characterize when subgame-perfect outcomes arise or explain why they are unattainable.  
- Suggest adding discussion/results relating to subgame-perfect equilibria and cite Harris (1990) on existence in simultaneous-move games.  
- Overall: novel and valuable computational convergence results, but needs tighter ties to game-theoretic subgame-perfect concepts.
