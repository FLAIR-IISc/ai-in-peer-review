- Provides a convergence analysis for a class of MCTS algorithms using any mild-restriction no-regret selection method, proving asymptotic convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move extensive-form games.  
- Convergence result is asymptotic only; no convergence rates are provided.  
- This may be the first convergence proof for MCTS in simultaneous-move extensive-form games, making it a valuable contribution despite limited rates.  
- Reviewer requests discussion on whether convergence rates can be derived when assuming a specific selection method with known regret bounds (e.g., O(√T) regret).  
- Experiments compare two value propagation strategies: mean reward (theoretical) vs. current sample value (standard), finding mean propagation slightly underperforms the standard method.  
- Authors also include experiments aiming to give empirical insight into convergence rates.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound, but significance is questioned due to lack of rates and prior convergence results for sequential-move games, making the contribution feel somewhat incremental.  
- Minor editorial/notation issues: Figure 1 "leafs" → "leaves"; Line 144 "negated" likely better than "inverted."  
- Notational confusions in Algorithm 1 Line 10: use of (a1,a2) is confusing (should be (i,j) or (σ1,σ2)); inconsistencies between (i,j) and 'a' variables persist and should be fixed.
