This paper explores the application of Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors provide a theoretical analysis demonstrating the convergence of the MCTS algorithm to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specific conditions. Empirical results support these formal findings.

Detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, the paper offers a valuable contribution by establishing a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful, and the presentation is clear. However, since the primary contribution of the paper lies in its theoretical aspects, I have some concerns regarding the novelty of the proof technique employed.

2. UCB algorithms, widely used in the literature, are not analyzed in this paper. Given the existing analysis on UCB for trees, it would be beneficial for the authors to consider UCB algorithms and compare their performance with other algorithms presented in the paper.

3. The authors only prove the convergence of the mean propagation, but the experimental results also indicate good convergence of the current sample value propagation under different criteria. A more in-depth explanation for this observation would be helpful.

While this paper presents interesting findings, from a technical standpoint, it does not particularly stand out.
