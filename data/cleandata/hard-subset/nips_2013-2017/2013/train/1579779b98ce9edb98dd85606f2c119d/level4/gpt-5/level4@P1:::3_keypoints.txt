- Paper introduces an SM-MCTS template using ε-Hannan-consistent (with exploration) selection and proves asymptotic convergence to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium for simultaneous-move, zero-sum, perfect-information games.  
- Proof strategy: reduce to repeated matrix games (including bounded payoff errors) and apply induction over tree depth.  
- Empirically instantiates template with regret matching and Exp3, testing on random and hard game families and comparing mean-return vs sample propagation.  
- Theoretical results appear sound and assumptions (ε-Hannan consistency, guaranteed exploration) are standard and satisfied by the chosen algorithms.  
- Analysis is asymptotic only; no finite-time/sample-complexity rates are provided.  
- Experiments appropriately validate qualitative convergence and show mean propagation slightly underperforms in practice.  
- Paper is generally well written but contains notational inconsistencies and minor wording issues.  
- Novelty: first formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in simultaneous-move perfect-information games under broad no-regret selection.  
- Significance: gives theoretical justification for SM-MCTS under broad selectors but limited practical impact due to lack of rates and backward-induction alternatives.  
- Main weakness 1: no finite-time/exploitability bounds — can per-selection regret bounds be translated to root exploitability?  
- Main weakness 2: proof requires mean propagation while empirical sample propagation performs better — can the proof be adapted or conditions identified where they coincide?  
- Main weakness 3: sensitivity to exploration parameter γ is unclear; discussion on theoretical and empirical effects is needed.  
- Pros: first asymptotic convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS with broad selectors; general template; clear inductive proof; supportive experiments.  
- Cons: no finite-time rates; theoretical reliance on mean propagation conflicts with empirically stronger sample propagation; somewhat incremental relative to sequential MCTS results.  
- Minor issues/typos: “leafs”→“leaves”; “negated” vs “inverted”; inconsistent action notation (i,j vs a’s); clarify “exp” vs “Exp3.”  
- Conclusion: solid, well-executed asymptotic result that fills a gap for simultaneous moves but would be strengthened by rate analyses and clarification about propagation and exploration.
