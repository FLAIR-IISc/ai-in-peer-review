This paper analyzes the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, which includes interesting examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors analyze Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS), implemented with regret-matching, for this class of games and prove convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium. 

The paper is clear and well-written, and the results are well-presented. The main contribution lies in its new formal analysis. Previous work by Lanctot et al. [20] explored a similar setup, but provided more preliminary analysis. The proof relies on an inductive argument, leveraging optimal strategies at the leaf nodes. 

A significant weakness of this approach, however, is the limited ability to prove meaningful results about the inner nodes of the game tree. This overlooks key concepts in game theory, particularly subgame perfect Nash equilibria. The paper would be strengthened by connecting the analysis to these concepts. I recommend citing "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990). This paper presents a novel analysis of regret-matching strategies within an MCTS framework for zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games. While interesting from a computational perspective, the analysis would benefit from a stronger connection to the well-established tools of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, a topic extensively studied by game theorists.
