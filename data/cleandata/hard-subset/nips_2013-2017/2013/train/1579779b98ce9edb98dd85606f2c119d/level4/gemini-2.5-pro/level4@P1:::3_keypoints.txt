- Formal analysis of MCTS for zero-sum, perfect-information, extensive-form games with simultaneous moves.
- Introduces a general MCTS template instantiable with any ε-Hannan consistent selection method.
- Main theoretical contribution: proof that this family converges to a subgame perfect ε-Nash equilibrium.
- Proof technique is elegant (induction from leaves) and generalizes to any ε-Hannan consistent selector.
- Key limitation: convergence is only asymptotic; no explicit convergence rates are provided.
- Lack of rates weakens practical assessment and comparison to polynomial-time exact solvers and anytime performance.
- Experiments compare propagating mean rewards vs. sample values; standard sample-based propagation converges slightly faster.
- Empirical tests on random and worst-case games give useful insight into practical convergence behavior.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and technically sound with solid proofs.
- Originality/significance: first convergence proof for simultaneous-move extensive-form games, though somewhat incremental relative to sequential-game MCTS results.
- Open question: can explicit convergence rates be derived by instantiating with specific algorithms with known regret bounds (e.g., Exp3’s O(√T))?
- Conclusion: strong, novel, and correct work with mild reservations about impact due to lack of rates; leaning toward acceptance.
- Minor edits: change "leafs" to "leaves"; use consistent joint-action notation (e.g., (i,j)); consistently use "negated utilities."
