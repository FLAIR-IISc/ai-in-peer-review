This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. It is shown that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions. The empirical study corroborates the theoretical result.
The detailed comments are as follows.
1. Overall, I find this a solid paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful and the exposition is clear. However, since the principal contribution is theoretical, I am concerned about the novelty of the proof technique.
2. UCB algorithms are widely used in the literature, but they are not examined in this paper. Given that there exist analyses of UCB for Trees, it would strengthen the work if the authors also considered UCB algorithms and compared them with the other methods in the paper.
3. The paper only proves good convergence for the propagation of the mean. Yet in the experiments, propagating the current sample value also appears to perform well under various criteria. Can you provide an explanation?
