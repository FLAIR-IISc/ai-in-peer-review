This paper studies zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games, a class that includes interesting instances such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors analyze Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) implemented with regret matching for this class and prove convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear and well written, and the results are well presented. The main contribution is new formal analysis. Relative to prior work—specifically Lanctot et al. (2013) [ref. 20], which considered a similar setup but provided a more preliminary treatment—this paper delivers stronger theoretical guarantees. The proof proceeds inductively, with optimal strategies established at the leaf nodes.

The main weakness of this approach is that it yields limited insight into the internal nodes of the game tree. As a result, it misses an opportunity to connect with key game-theoretic concepts, notably subgame-perfect Nash equilibria (SPNE). The paper would be stronger if the analysis were tied to SPNE and related subgame concepts, which have been extensively studied in the game-theory literature. Recommended citation:
- Harris, C. (1990). “Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves.”

In summary, this paper presents novel analysis of the regret-matching strategy within MCTS for zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games and establishes convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium. While the computational perspective is interesting, the analysis would benefit from engaging more directly with the theory of subgame-perfect equilibria for this class of games.
