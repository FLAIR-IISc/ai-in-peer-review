- Paper studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.
- Main theoretical contribution: a sufficient condition for MCTS to converge to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Empirical validation supports the theoretical result.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-presented contribution that clarifies MCTS convergence theory.
- Primary concern: novelty of the proof technique is limited given the theoretical core of the paper.
- Analysis is restricted to mean-value propagation; widely used UCB/UCT algorithms are not analyzed.
- Recommendation: compare with UCB-based methods (UCB for Trees) to contextualize results and show advantages/limitations.
- Experimental finding: sample-value propagation also performs well, contradicting focus on mean-value theory.
- Request: explain whether convergence guarantees extend to sample-value propagation (possibly under modified conditions) to reconcile theory with experiments.
