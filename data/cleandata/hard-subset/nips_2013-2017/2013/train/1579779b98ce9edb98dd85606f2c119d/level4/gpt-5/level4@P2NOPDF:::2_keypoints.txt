- Studies zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves, using examples like Goofspiel and Tron.  
- Analyzes Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with regret matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Presentation is clear, results are well organized, and the main contribution is the formal theoretical analysis.  
- Core proof proceeds inductively: establish optimal strategies at leaves and extend them upward through the game tree.  
- Advances theoretical understanding beyond prior preliminary work (e.g., Lanctot et al., 2013).  
- Principal weakness: limited insight into algorithm behavior at internal nodes.  
- Does not fully engage with subgame-level solution concepts, especially subgame-perfect Nash equilibria.  
- Paper should clarify whether convergence guarantees produce approximately subgame-perfect strategies in every subgame, identify conditions for that, or provide counterexamples.  
- Recommended citation to support this direction: C. Harris (1990), “Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves.”  
- Overall: well-written and novel formal analysis of regret-matching within MCTS with solid convergence results, but would be stronger if tied explicitly to subgame-perfect equilibrium concepts and internal-node implications.
