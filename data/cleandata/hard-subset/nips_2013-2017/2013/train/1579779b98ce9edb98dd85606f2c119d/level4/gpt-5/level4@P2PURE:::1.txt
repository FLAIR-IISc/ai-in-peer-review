This paper investigates Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) in zero-sum, perfect-information extensive-form games with simultaneous moves. It proves that, under certain conditions, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium, and the empirical results support the theoretical findings.

Detailed comments:
1. Overall, this is a solid paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful and the presentation is clear. However, since the main contribution is theoretical, I am concerned about the novelty of the proof technique.
2. UCB-based algorithms are widely used but are not analyzed here. Given prior analyses of UCB for Trees, it would strengthen the paper to include UCB algorithms and compare them with the algorithms considered in this work.
3. The theory establishes good convergence only for mean propagation. Yet in the experiments, propagating the current sample value also appears to perform well across multiple criteria. Could the authors explain this?

In summary, while the findings are interesting, the paper is not particularly strong from a technical novelty standpoint.
