- Main contribution: Proves asymptotic convergence of MCTS with any mild-restriction no-regret selection method to ε-Nash equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Major limitation: Convergence is only asymptotic with no convergence rates provided.  
- Cause of limitation: Authors assume only ε-Hannan consistency for selection methods, which precludes rate guarantees.  
- Suggested extension: Assess whether rates can be derived under stronger regret bounds (e.g., √T rates from Exp3/exponential weighting).  
- Experiments: Compare mean-reward propagation (used in analysis) versus standard sample-value propagation.  
- Empirical findings: Mean-reward propagation slightly underperforms standard propagation; attempts to estimate convergence rates are inconclusive.  
- Overall evaluation: Well-written and technically sound, but impact is tempered by lack of rate results and partial incrementality relative to sequential-move MCTS results.  
- Minor issues/nitpicks: Typos and notation fixes needed — "leafs"→"leaves"; "negated" vs "inverted"; Algorithm 1 (a1,a2) should be (i,j) or (σ₁,σ₂); notation inconsistencies (i,j vs a's) throughout.
