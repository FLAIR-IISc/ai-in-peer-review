This paper analyzes Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) for zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors prove that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specific conditions, a theoretical result supported by an empirical study.

Detailed comments:
1.  Overall, this is a well-presented paper that provides a useful sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS. The results are valuable and the presentation is clear. However, since the primary contribution is theoretical, the novelty of the proof technique is a concern.
2.  The paper does not analyze UCB algorithms, which are widely used in the literature. Given the existing analysis of UCB for Trees, the paper would be strengthened by including a theoretical and empirical comparison of UCB-based methods against the algorithms presented.
3.  The convergence proof is limited to the propagation of the mean value. However, the experiments show that propagating the current sample value also performs well across different evaluation criteria. Could the authors provide some intuition or explanation for this empirical observation, which is not covered by the current theory?

In summary, while the paper presents interesting findings, its technical contribution appears limited.
