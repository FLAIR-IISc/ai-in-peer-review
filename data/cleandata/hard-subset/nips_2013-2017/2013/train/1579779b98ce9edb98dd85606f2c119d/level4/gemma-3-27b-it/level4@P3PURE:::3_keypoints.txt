- Presents analysis of an MCTS class and proves asymptotic convergence to an ε‑Nash equilibrium under mild restrictions.
- Main theoretical contribution: first convergence proof for MCTS in simultaneous‑move games using any no‑regret selection method.
- Convergence result is asymptotic with no explicit convergence rates provided.
- Lack of rates stems from assuming only ε‑Hannan consistency for selection methods.
- Importance of convergence rates noted; e.g., deriving rates for specific regret bounds like Exp3’s O(√T) would be valuable.
- Experiments compare propagating mean reward values (used in theory) versus propagating current sample values, finding mean propagation slightly worse.
- Authors also attempt an experimental assessment of convergence rates.
- Overall assessment: well‑written and technically sound work but potentially limited impact without rate results.
- Paper feels somewhat incremental given existing convergence results for sequential‑move MCTS.
- Nitpicks: typo “leafs” → “leaves”; Line 144 prefer “negated” over “inverted.”
- Notation issues: Algorithm 1’s (a1,a2) is confusing (suggest (i,j) or (σ1,σ2)); inconsistent use of (i,j) vs. ‘a’ notation throughout.
