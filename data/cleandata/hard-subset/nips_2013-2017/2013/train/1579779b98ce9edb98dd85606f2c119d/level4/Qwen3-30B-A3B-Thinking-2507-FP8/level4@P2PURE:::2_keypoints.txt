- Studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, with examples like Goofspiel and Tron.  
- Analyzes Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) using regret-matching for this game class.  
- Proves convergence of the proposed MCTS/regret-matching method to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and presents results effectively.  
- Main contribution is novel formal analysis; prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) provided more preliminary treatment.  
- Proof technique is inductive and leverages optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Major limitation: provides little insight into inner game-tree nodes and ignores subgame perfect Nash equilibria.  
- Suggests strengthening by explicitly connecting to subgame perfection and citing Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria.
