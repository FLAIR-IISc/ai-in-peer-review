- Provides a formal proof that SM-MCTS with an ε-Hannan consistent, sufficiently exploring selection method converges to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move games.  
- Introduces a general SM-MCTS template that can be instantiated with different action-selection algorithms.  
- Extends analysis from repeated matrix games to full extensive-form games via an inductive proof.  
- Shows the result applies broadly to any ε-Hannan consistent algorithm with guaranteed exploration (e.g., Regret Matching, Exp3).  
- Empirical evaluation on random and worst-case games confirms convergence of the proposed methods.  
- Strength: Fills an important theoretical gap by proving MCTS convergence in simultaneous-move games.  
- Strength: Clear, systematic presentation and development of the theoretical analysis.  
- Strength: The generalization offers a useful framework for designing future MCTS variants.  
- Weakness/Question: Technical novelty is unclear—proof mainly applies established online-learning concepts; authors should clarify any new technical tools.  
- Weakness/Question: Lack of detailed discussion and empirical comparison with UCB-based selection, which is popular in practice despite known theoretical issues.  
- Weakness/Question: Theory requires propagating mean subtree rewards, but experiments show single-sample propagation also converges—need intuition or theoretical extension explaining this discrepancy.  
- Summary: Solid and useful contribution that feels somewhat incremental; clarify novelty, relate to UCB methods, and explain strong empirical performance of standard updates.
