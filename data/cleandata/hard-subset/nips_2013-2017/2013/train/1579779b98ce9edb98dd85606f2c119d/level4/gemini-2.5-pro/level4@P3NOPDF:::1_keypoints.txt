- Paper proves a class of MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in two-player zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and supports this with experiments.
- Well-written and clearly presented; arguments and results are easy to follow.
- Main theoretical contribution: a sufficient condition for MCTS convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium in the target game class.
- Concern about novelty: the proof technique may rely on established methods (e.g., stochastic approximation or regret minimization) and needs clearer positioning relative to prior work.
- Major omission: no analysis of UCB-based tree policies, which are widely used in MCTS; including UCB analysis or explaining why it doesn't apply would strengthen the paper.
- Experiments show sample-value propagation performs well despite theory covering only mean-value propagation; the paper should discuss this discrepancy and provide intuition.
- Overall: interesting and valuable result but technical novelty and practical relevance are limited until issues about proof originality and UCB comparison are addressed.
