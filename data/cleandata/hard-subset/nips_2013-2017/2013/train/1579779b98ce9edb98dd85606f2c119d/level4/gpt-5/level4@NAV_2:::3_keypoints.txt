- Contribution: Analyzes a family of MCTS algorithms that can use any no-regret (Hannan-consistent) selection rule.  
- Main theoretical result: Proves asymptotic convergence to an ε–Nash equilibrium in perfect-information, zero-sum extensive-form games with simultaneous moves.  
- Major limitation: Guarantees are purely asymptotic — no finite-time convergence rates are provided.  
- Cause of limitation: Analysis depends only on ε–Hannan consistency of the selection rule, which precludes deriving explicit rates.  
- Open question: Can specific regret bounds (e.g., O(√T) for Exp3) be translated into concrete convergence/exploitability rates for the overall algorithm?  
- Empirical finding: Propagating mean rewards (as assumed in analysis) converges slightly more slowly than propagating the current sample, and experiments include hard instances to examine convergence behavior.  
- Overall assessment: Paper is clearly written and technically sound, but impact is tempered by lack of quantitative rates and incremental nature given known MCTS convergence in sequential-move settings.
