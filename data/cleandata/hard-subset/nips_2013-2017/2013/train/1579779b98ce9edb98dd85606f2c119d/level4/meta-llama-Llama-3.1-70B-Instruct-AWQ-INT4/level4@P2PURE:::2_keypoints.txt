- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., goofspiel, Tron).
- Studies MCTS combined with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents its results.
- Primary contribution is a formal analysis that builds on prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) and provides new insights.
- Proof technique is inductive and relies on optimal strategies at leaf nodes.
- Major limitation: provides little insight into inner nodes of the game tree and omits subgame-perfect equilibrium considerations.
- Suggests integrating analysis with subgame perfect Nash equilibrium literature (e.g., Harris 1990).
- Results are novel from a computational perspective but lack connection to established subgame-perfect game-theoretic tools.
