This paper investigates zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, a class that includes notable examples like Goofspiel and Tron. The authors present an analysis of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) utilizing regret-matching within this game class, and demonstrate convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clearly written and the results are presented effectively. Its primary contribution lies in novel formal analysis building upon prior work, such as the preliminary analysis presented in Lanctot et al. [20, 2013]. The proof employs an inductive approach, relying on optimal strategies at the terminal nodes of the game tree.

A significant limitation of this approach, however, is the limited scope of provable results concerning the internal nodes of the game tree. This restricts the connection to core game-theoretic concepts like subgame perfect Nash equilibria. Strengthening the paper would involve explicitly linking the analysis to these established concepts. I recommend considering "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990) for relevant background. In summary, this work offers a valuable computational analysis of regret-matching with MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, but would benefit from a more thorough engagement with the broader literature on subgame perfect Nash equilibria.
