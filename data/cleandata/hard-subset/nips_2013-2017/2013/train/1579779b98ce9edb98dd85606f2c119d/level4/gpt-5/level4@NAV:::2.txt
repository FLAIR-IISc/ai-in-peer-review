This paper studies the class of zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games. Notable examples include Goofspiel and Tron. The authors examine MCTS augmented with regret matching for this class and prove convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The manuscript is clear and well-written, with well-presented results. The main contribution is new formal analysis. Prior work cited by the authors, e.g., [20] Lanctot et al., 2013, considers the same setup but provides a more preliminary treatment. The proof is inductive, assuming optimal play at the leaf nodes.

I believe the principal limitation of this approach is that it yields little insight about the internal nodes of the game tree. This overlooks key concepts from game theory, namely subgame-perfect Nash equilibria. The paper would benefit from connecting the analysis to these ideas. Recommended citation:
"Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves." C. Harris, 1990. This paper offers novel analysis of regret matching within MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games. While compelling from a computational perspective, the analysis does not sufficiently engage with the powerful framework of subgame-perfect Nash equilibria for this class of games, which is well-established in the game-theory literature.
