This manuscript presents an analysis of Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in the context of perfect-information, simultaneous-move, zero-sum extensive form games. The authors prove that, subject to certain conditions, the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium. This formal result is corroborated by an empirical study.

Detailed comments are as follows:

1.  Overall, this is a solid paper that provides a valuable sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful and the manuscript is clearly presented. However, as the primary contribution of the paper is theoretical, I have reservations regarding the novelty of the proof methodology.

2.  Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) algorithms are widely utilized in related literature but are not analyzed in this work. Given the existing body of analysis on UCB for Trees, the paper would be significantly strengthened if the authors were to include UCB algorithms in their analysis and compare their performance against the other methods presented.

3.  The theoretical convergence proof is provided only for the propagation of the mean. In the experiments, however, the propagation of the current sample value also appears to yield strong results under various metrics. Could the authors offer an explanation for this empirical observation?

This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search for zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. Although the findings presented are interesting, from a technical standpoint, the paper's contributions are not especially outstanding.
