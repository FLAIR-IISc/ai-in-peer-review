

This paper analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, including notable examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors examine Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) implemented with regret-matching for this class of games and establish convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium. The paper is clear and well-written, with results presented effectively. The primary contribution lies in novel formal analysis; while prior work [20] (Lanctot et al., 2013) addresses a similar setup, it offers more preliminary analysis. The proof is inductive, leveraging optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  

The most significant limitation is that the approach yields limited insights regarding the inner nodes of the game tree, thereby overlooking key concepts in game theory: subgame perfect Nash equilibria. The paper would be strengthened by explicitly connecting its analysis to these concepts. Recommended citation: "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990). While the computational analysis is interesting, the work misses an opportunity to integrate well-established game-theoretic tools—specifically subgame perfect Nash equilibria—which have been extensively studied in the field.
