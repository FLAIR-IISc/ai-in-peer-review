- Main contribution: proof that a class of MCTS using any (mildly restricted) no-regret selection method converges asymptotically to an epsilon-Nash equilibrium in extensive-form simultaneous-move games.
- Major weakness: convergence guarantee is purely asymptotic with no convergence rates provided.
- The lack of rates seems inherent to the analysis because the selection method is assumed only epsilon-Hannan consistent.
- Practical concern: rates matter since backward induction yields an exact equilibrium, so asymptotic-only guarantees limit practical impact.
- Request: characterize convergence rates when the selection method has specific regret bounds (e.g., sqrt(T) regret as in EXP).
- Experiments: compare propagating mean rewards (as in analysis) vs. propagating most recent sample; mean propagation slightly underperforms.
- Experiments also attempt to provide empirical insight into convergence rates but do not fully resolve the rate question.
- Overall: paper is well written and technically sound and provides a worthwhile first proof for simultaneous-move MCTS, but impact is tempered by lack of rates and feels incremental relative to existing sequential-move results.
- Minor nitpicks: fix “leafs”→“leaves,” “inverted”→“negated,” Algorithm 1 line 10 notation (a1,a2→(i,j) or (σ1,σ2)), and standardize (i,j) vs. a’s notation around line 172.
