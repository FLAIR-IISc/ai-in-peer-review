- Main contribution: proves convergence of an MCTS algorithm to an approximate Nash equilibrium for zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games under certain conditions.
- Empirical results: experiments confirm the theoretical convergence findings.
- Writing and contribution: clear presentation and a useful sufficient condition for MCTS convergence.
- Concern: technical novelty is limited—proof technique appears to rely on established methods, which may reduce impact.
- Omission: no analysis of UCB-based selection strategies, despite UCB’s widespread use and existing theoretical work—paper should include UCB variants and comparisons.
- Theory–experiment gap: theory only covers mean-value propagation up the tree, but experiments show single-sample propagation also performs well; the paper needs discussion or intuition explaining this empirical success.
