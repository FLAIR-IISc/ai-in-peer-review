- Paper studies SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move perfect-information games and proposes an SM-MCTS template using ε-Hannan-consistent (HC) selection with guaranteed exploration, proving almost-sure convergence to subgame-perfect approximate NE (mean-backups → (ε·D^2 + δ)-NE).

- Analysis approach: establish HC dynamics in repeated matrix games, extend to bounded evaluation error, and induct over depth for stacked matrix games.

- Empirical evaluation: tests regret matching (RM) and Exp3 on random and small worst-case instances; both converge empirically, mean-backups slightly slower than current-sample, and hard small games show exploitability floor due to exploration γ.

- Strength: clear problem formulation and a clean algorithmic template linking MCTS to online learning in matrix games.

- Strength: general convergence theorem covering any ε-HC selection with guaranteed exploration, yielding subgame-perfect approximate equilibria.

- Strength: sensible empirical evaluation with two standard HC algorithms and discussion of backup choices.

- Strength: well-written, well-structured analysis and clear notation.

- Weakness: technical novelty limited—proof combines standard ingredients (HC→NE, bounded-error handling, depth induction) and needs clearer emphasis on what is new.

- Weakness: omission of UCB/UCT analysis or empirical comparison despite their ubiquity and known issues in simultaneous-move settings.

- Weakness: gap between theory and experiments on backup rules—proof requires mean-backups but current-sample backups work in practice; needs explanation or counterexample.

- Weakness: loose asymptotic bound O(D^2 ε) with no finite-time rates, limiting practical guidance and leaving error accumulation/tightening unexplored.

- Weakness: limited experimental scope (worst-case enumeration only for depth-2 BF-2 and small random games); larger benchmarks and time-based convergence plots absent.

- Weakness: exploration-induced exploitability floor noted experimentally; lacks analysis of decaying or alternative exploration schedules to reduce asymptotic exploitability.

- Quality: theoretical results are sound and carefully structured; some proofs deferred to appendix; empirical methodology reasonable but limited.

- Clarity: paper is mostly clear; should explicitly separate backup variants, state where mean-backups are required, and summarize assumptions before the main theorem.

- Originality: moderate—first formal convergence for SM-MCTS under HC selection, but proof components largely adapt known ideas.

- Significance: provides first formal convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS under broad conditions, but impact limited by lack of finite-time bounds, D^2 dependence, and missing UCB comparisons.

- Suggestions: clarify novelty relative to known HC→NE results, analyze/compare UCB/UCT variants, provide intuition or counterexample for current-sample backups, explore decaying exploration schedules, and expand experiments to larger benchmarks.

- Arguments for acceptance: first formal convergence result for SM-MCTS with practical selection rules, clear presentation and unifying template, and supportive empirical results.

- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty and loose bounds, lack of UCT analysis/comparison, and modest experimental scope.

- Overall: solid and clearly written paper with a useful convergence result for SM-MCTS, but moderate novelty and limited experiments; addressing UCB and backup-rule behavior would materially strengthen the work.
