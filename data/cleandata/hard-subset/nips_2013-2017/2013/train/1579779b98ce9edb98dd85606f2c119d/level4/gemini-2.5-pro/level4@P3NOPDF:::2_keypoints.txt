- Presents formal analysis of MCTS for zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games.
- Studies an MCTS variant using regret matching at each decision node.
- Proves convergence of this algorithm to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Targets an important game class that includes Goofspiel and Tron.
- Paper is clearly written and results are well-presented.
- Primary contribution: novel, rigorous theoretical analysis extending prior preliminary work (e.g., Lanctot et al., 2013).
- Core proof uses an inductive argument establishing optimal strategies at leaves and propagating them up the tree.
- Main weakness: analysis focuses only on whole-game Nash equilibrium and not on stronger solution concepts.
- Does not guarantee optimality at inner nodes and thus does not address Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium (SPNE).
- Recommendation: discuss relationship to SPNE and cite relevant work (e.g., Harris 1990) to strengthen theoretical contribution.
