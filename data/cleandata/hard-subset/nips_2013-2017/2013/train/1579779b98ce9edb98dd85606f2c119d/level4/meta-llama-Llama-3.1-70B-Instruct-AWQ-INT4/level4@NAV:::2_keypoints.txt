- Studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., goofspiel, Tron).  
- Employs MCTS with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Main contribution: novel formal analysis extending Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- Proof uses an inductive approach focused on optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Major limitation: method offers little insight into inner game-tree nodes, overlooking subgame perfect equilibria.  
- Suggests integrating subgame perfect Nash equilibrium tools (e.g., Harris 1990) to strengthen the work.
