- Paper proves asymptotic convergence of a class of MCTS algorithms (with mild restrictions) to an epsilon-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move extensive-form games.  
- Convergence guarantee is purely asymptotic; no finite-time convergence rates are provided.  
- Lack of rates appears inherent to the epsilon-Hannan consistency assumption on selection methods.  
- Practical impact unclear: need translation from specific regret bounds (e.g., O(√T) for EXP3) to exploitability/convergence rates at the root.  
- Experiments compare mean-value backups (used in analysis) versus propagating the most recent sample; mean-value backups perform slightly worse empirically.  
- Experimental attempts to infer convergence rates give qualitative insight but do not resolve the theoretical rate question.  
- Paper is clear and technically sound, and asymptotic convergence in this setting is a meaningful result given prior absence of such proofs.  
- Contribution may be viewed as incremental since finite-time convergence for sequential-move MCTS already exists.  
- Main reservation about impact stems from the absence of finite-time convergence rates.
