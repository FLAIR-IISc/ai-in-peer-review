

This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, establishing convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specified conditions, with empirical validation supporting the theoretical claims.  

The paper presents a technically sound contribution, though I have the following concerns:  

1.  The theoretical contribution—providing a sufficient condition for MCTS convergence—is valuable and well-motivated. However, the novelty of the proof technique requires stronger justification, as the core arguments appear to build upon established approaches rather than introducing a fundamentally new analytical framework.  

2.  The paper does not analyze UCB-based algorithms, despite their widespread use in MCTS literature and prior convergence analyses for tree search. It would significantly strengthen the work to include UCB variants, compare their performance against the proposed methods, and discuss their theoretical guarantees within the paper’s framework.  

3.  The theoretical analysis establishes convergence for mean reward propagation, yet the empirical results indicate that propagating the current sample value also yields effective performance under multiple evaluation criteria. The authors should clarify this discrepancy and discuss whether the theoretical framework can be extended to accommodate sample-value propagation.  

While the findings are interesting and the presentation is clear, the technical contribution is not sufficiently novel or impactful to be considered outstanding for NIPS. The paper makes a solid incremental advance but does not offer transformative insights.
