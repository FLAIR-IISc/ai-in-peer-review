This paper investigates two-player zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, including examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. It studies MCTS instantiated with regret matching in this setting and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The manuscript is clearly written and the results are presented well. Its primary contribution is new formal analysis. Prior work cited by the authors (Lanctot et al., 2013 [20]) considers a similar setup but remains more preliminary. The proof strategy is inductive, beginning from optimal behavior at the leaf nodes.

The main weakness of this line of analysis is that it yields little of interest about the interior nodes of the game tree, missing connections to key game-theoretic notions such as subgame-perfect Nash equilibria. The paper would benefit from tying the analysis to those concepts. Recommended citation: “Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves,” C. Harris, 1990. Overall, the paper offers novel analysis of regret matching combined with MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games. While compelling from a computational perspective, it does not adequately connect to the powerful tools surrounding subgame-perfect equilibria that have been extensively developed in game theory.
