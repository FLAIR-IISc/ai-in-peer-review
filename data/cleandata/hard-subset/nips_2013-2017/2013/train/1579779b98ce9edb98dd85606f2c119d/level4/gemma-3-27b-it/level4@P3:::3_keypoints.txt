- Core contribution: proof that MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games when the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent.  
- Importance: fills a gap in theoretical guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move games.  
- Context: well-positioned with relevant prior work on game theory, backward induction, regret minimization, and MCTS.  
- Theoretical rigor: formal convergence proof is the paper's primary strength.  
- Generality: framework supports various ε-Hannan consistent selection methods.  
- Presentation: paper is generally well-written and Algorithm 1 is clear and logical.  
- Empirical validation: experiments provide practical validation of the theoretical results.  
- Relation to prior work: correctly connects to literature on regret minimization and MCTS.  
- Weakness — convergence rates: result is asymptotic with no explicit convergence rates, limiting practical insight.  
- Weakness — incremental impact: contribution may be seen as incremental given existing sequential-move MCTS convergence results.  
- Weakness — notation: multiple notational inconsistencies (e.g., (i, j) vs. a’s) create potential confusion.  
- Weakness — minor clarifications: need clearer motivation for propagating mean values vs. current sample values and discussion of experimental underperformance of mean propagation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a theoretical gap, provides a formal convergence proof, offers a general framework, includes supporting experiments, and is well-presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of convergence rates, perceived incremental nature, and notational clarity issues.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution but limited by missing convergence-rate analysis; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions (fix notation and expand discussion on asymptotic implications and regret-rate vs. convergence-rate).  
- Specific edits: change "leafs" to "leaves" (Figure 1) and "inverted" to "negated" (Line 144).  
- Specific notation fix: use consistent action notation (prefer (i, j) over mixed forms) and review lines from 172 onward for inconsistencies.  
- Experimental suggestion: clarify rationale and implications for mean propagation vs. current sample propagation in Section 5.1.
