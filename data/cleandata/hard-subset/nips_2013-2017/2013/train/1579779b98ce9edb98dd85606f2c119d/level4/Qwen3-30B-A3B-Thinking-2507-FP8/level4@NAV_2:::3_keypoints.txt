- Primary contribution: first rigorous proof that MCTS with ε-Hannan consistent selection and guaranteed exploration converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves.  
- Novelty: fills a gap in MCTS literature by extending convergence guarantees from sequential to simultaneous-move extensive-form games.  
- Theoretical quality: technically sound inductive proof showing convergence to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium with careful error bounds.  
- Empirical support: experiments compare mean-reward propagation (required by the proof) to current-sample propagation and study convergence on worst-case games.  
- Strength: provides the first formal convergence guarantee for simultaneous-move extensive-form MCTS.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with careful handling of induction and error propagation.  
- Strength: well-executed empirical validation that complements the theoretical claims.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing and presentation.  
- Weakness: only asymptotic convergence is shown; no finite-time or convergence rate bounds are provided.  
- Weakness: does not analyze how specific regret minimizers (e.g., Exp3 with known √T regret) affect convergence rates.  
- Weakness: overall significance is somewhat limited compared to existing sequential-game MCTS convergence results, making the contribution appear incremental.  
- Practical impact: lack of finite-time bounds reduces practical usefulness given that backward induction provides exact solutions.  
- Revision suggestions: fix typo "leafs"→"leaves"; change "inverted"→"negated" on line 144; replace (a1,a2) with (i,j) in Algorithm 1 line 10; resolve notation inconsistencies between (i,j) and a’s.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; technically correct and an important step forward despite limited practical impact from missing rate bounds.
