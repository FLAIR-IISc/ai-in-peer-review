This paper analyzes the class of zero-sum perfect-information games, including games like Go and chess. The authors perform an MCTS-based analysis of this class of games, including regret-matching, and prove the convergence of an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear and well-written, and the results are well presented. Its main contribution is new results in formal analysis. The authors refer to a previous work [20] by Lencott et al., 2013, which looks at the setup but has more basic analysis. The proof is with optimal strategies in ascending-leaf nodes.

I think the biggest weakness of this approach is that it cannot prove anything very interesting about the internal nodes of the game tree. It excludes some key concepts of game theory, such as subgame perfect Nash equilibria. I think the paper could be improved by connecting this analysis to those concepts. Proposed citation:

"The existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with perfect information". C. Harris 1990. This paper presents new analysis with the regret-matching technique MTCS in zero-sum perfect-information games. Although interesting from a computational perspective, these analyses are excluded from being connected to the powerful tools of subgame perfect Nash equilibria for this class of games; which have been well studied by game theorists.
