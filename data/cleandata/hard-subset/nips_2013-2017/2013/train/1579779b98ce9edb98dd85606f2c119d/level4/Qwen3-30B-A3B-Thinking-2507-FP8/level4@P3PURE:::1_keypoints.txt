- Paper studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions and validating empirically.  
- Provides a valuable sufficient condition for MCTS convergence, but the novelty of the proof technique is limited since it builds on established methods.  
- Omits analysis of widely used UCB-based algorithms; should include UCB variants, empirical comparison, and theoretical discussion.  
- Theory covers convergence for mean-reward propagation, yet experiments show sample-value propagation also performs well; authors should clarify this mismatch and consider extending theory.  
- Presentation is clear and results are interesting.  
- Overall the contribution is a solid incremental advance but not sufficiently novel or impactful for a top-tier acceptance.
