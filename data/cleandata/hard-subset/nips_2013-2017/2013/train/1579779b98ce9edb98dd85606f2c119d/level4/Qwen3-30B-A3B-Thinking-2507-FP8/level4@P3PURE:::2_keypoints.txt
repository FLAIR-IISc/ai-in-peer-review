Paper studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (examples: Goofspiel, Tron).  
Analyzes MCTS implemented with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
Paper is clearly written with well-presented, novel formal analysis.  
Prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) considered a similar setup but with less formal analysis.  
Proof uses induction and leverages optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
Primary weakness: theoretical analysis is limited and gives only trivial insights about inner nodes.  
Analysis overlooks subgame perfect Nash equilibria and broader game-theoretic concepts.  
Recommendation: strengthen the paper by connecting to subgame perfect equilibria literature (e.g., Harris 1990).
