This paper analyzes the class of zero-sum perfect-information games. This class includes some interesting games, such as Goofiespeak and Tron. The authors have analyzed MCTS, which is applied with regress-matching for this class of games and proven convergence in an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear and well-written, and the results are well-presented. The main contribution is new results in formal analysis. The previous work cited by the authors [20] by Lanthotte et al, 2013, looks at the setup but has more preliminary analysis. The evidence is inductive – with optimal strategies on leaf nodes.

I think the biggest weakness in this type of approach is that it cannot prove very interesting things about the internal nodes of the game tree. It leaves out some key ideas in game theory: the Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium. I think the paper could be improved by connecting the analysis to those concepts. Recommended citation:

"The Existence of Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium in Interactive Games". C. Harris 1990. This paper presents a novel analysis of regress-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information interactive games. While interesting from a computational perspective, the analysis misses connecting to the powerful tools of Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium for this class of games; something that has been well studied by game theorists.
