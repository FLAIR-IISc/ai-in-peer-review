- Primary contribution: proves asymptotic convergence of a general Simultaneous Move MCTS (SM-MCTS) template to a subgame perfect ε-Nash equilibrium when the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent with guaranteed exploration.

- Method: SM-MCTS instantiated with any no-regret action-selection algorithm; analysis builds from repeated matrix games to bounded-payoff errors and an inductive multi-stage argument.

- Empirical support: experiments with Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games confirm the theoretical claims.

- Strength — originality/significance: first formal convergence proof for MCTS in extensive-form simultaneous-move games, closing an important theoretical gap.

- Strength — technical quality: proofs are rigorous and logically structured, combining general theory with instantiations and experiments.

- Strength — clarity: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and defines the setting and algorithm before analysis.

- Weakness — no finite-time rates: convergence is asymptotic only; lacking explicit rates limits practical assessment and could be improved by leveraging known regret bounds (e.g., O(√T) for Exp3).

- Weakness — incremental perspective: result extends existing MCTS theory from sequential to simultaneous moves, so may be seen as technically non-trivial but incremental.

- Weakness — theory/practice gap: theoretical requirement to propagate mean rewards yields slightly slower empirical convergence than single-sample propagation commonly used in practice.

- Minor: fix caption typo "leafs" → "leaves".

- Minor: player 2 utility note — using negated utilities is correct for zero-sum framing.

- Minor: notation inconsistency — use (i,j) consistently instead of mixing with a to improve readability.

- Overall: solid, novel theoretical contribution and a clear candidate for acceptance despite reservations about practical impact without rates.
