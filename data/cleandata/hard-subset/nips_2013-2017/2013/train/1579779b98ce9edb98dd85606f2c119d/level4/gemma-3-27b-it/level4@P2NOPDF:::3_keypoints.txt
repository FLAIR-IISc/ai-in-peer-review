- Proves MCTS converges to an ε-Nash equilibrium for a broad class of algorithms using any no-regret selection method under simultaneous moves.  
- Valuable because convergence guarantees for simultaneous-move extensive-form games are scarce.  
- Main strength is the generality: leveraging no-regret learning yields a result applicable to many MCTS variants.  
- Key limitation: the proof is asymptotic and does not provide explicit convergence rates.  
- Practical usefulness is unclear without rates, especially compared to exact backward induction.  
- Suggestion: investigate translating specific regret bounds (e.g., Exp3’s √T) into concrete convergence rates.  
- Experiments compare propagating mean rewards vs. current sample values; mean propagation shows slight performance disadvantage.  
- Experimental attempts to assess convergence speed are useful but limited in conclusiveness.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and well-written.  
- Main reservation: contribution is somewhat incremental given existing sequential-move convergence results.  
- Lack of convergence rates reduces the paper’s potential impact.  
- Minor issues/typos: “leafs” → “leaves”; “negated” preferred over “inverted”; clarify notation (a1,a2) vs (i,j) and ensure consistent use of action notation.
