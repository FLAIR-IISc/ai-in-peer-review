- Presents theoretical analysis of MCTS algorithms that use any no-regret selection method (with mild restrictions) and proves convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium in extensive-form games with simultaneous moves.
- Main contribution is a first convergence proof for MCTS in extensive-form simultaneous-move games.
- Convergence result is asymptotic only; no convergence rates are provided.
- Lack of rates follows from the general assumption of ε-Hannan consistency for the selection method.
- Convergence rates are critical for practical/anytime utility; authors should discuss achievable rates for specific selection methods with known regret bounds (e.g., EXP3 with O(√T)).
- Experiments compare propagating mean reward values (used in theory) versus single sample propagation; mean propagation shows slightly worse empirical performance.
- Experiments also attempt to provide empirical insight into convergence rates.
- Paper is well-written and technically sound, but the lack of convergence rates tempers the potential impact and makes the contribution somewhat incremental relative to sequential-move MCTS results.
- Minor typo: Figure 1 uses "leafs" instead of "leaves".
- Suggested wording change: Line 144, use "negated" rather than "inverted".
- Notational inconsistency: Algorithm 1 Line 10 uses (a1, a2) while other parts use (i, j) or (σ1, σ2), causing confusion with a_{i,j} payoff.
- Notational inconsistency persists elsewhere (e.g., Line 172) and should be corrected for clarity.
