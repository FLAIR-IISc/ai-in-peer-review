

This paper investigates zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, including notable examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors employ Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with regret-matching to analyze this class and establish convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium. The manuscript is clearly written, with results presented in a well-structured manner. The primary contribution lies in novel formal analysis; while prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) addresses the same setup, it offers less rigorous theoretical treatment. The proof employs an inductive approach, leveraging optimal strategies at terminal nodes.  

The principal limitation is the analysis's failure to address meaningful guarantees for inner game-tree nodes, thereby overlooking a fundamental concept in game theory: subgame perfect Nash equilibria. The authors should integrate their results with this established framework. I recommend citing Harris (1990): "Existence of Subgame Perfect Equilibria in Games with Simultaneous Moves." While the computational insights are valuable, the work would be significantly strengthened by connecting its analysis to subgame perfect Nash equilibria—a well-studied theoretical foundation for this game class.
