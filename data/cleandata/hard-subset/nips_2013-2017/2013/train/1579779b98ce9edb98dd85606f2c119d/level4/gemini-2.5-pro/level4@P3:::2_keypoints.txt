- Paper tackles theoretical convergence of MCTS for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games, proving convergence to a subgame perfect ε'-Nash equilibrium when selection is ε-Hannan consistent with sufficient exploration.  
- Proof strategy: establish convergence in single-stage matrix-game leaves, show propagated values have bounded error, extend inductively to the full tree; empirical validation uses regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games.  
- Originality/significance: first formal convergence proof for MCTS in this domain, bridging practical use and theoretical guarantees.  
- Technical quality: rigorous, elegant analysis leveraging online-learning results and careful error bounds.  
- Clarity: paper is very well written and organized, with clear definitions, algorithm description, and presentation of results.  
- Completeness: combines novel theory with solid empirical evaluation and useful practical insights (e.g., mean vs sample value propagation).  
- Weakness — game-theory connection: should better relate findings to classical literature on subgame perfection in simultaneous-move games.  
- Weakness — convergence rates: main theorem lacks finite-time convergence rates and the ε'-bound’s dependence on game depth D needs discussion.  
- Weakness — empirical subgame tests: experiments measure root exploitability only; should include exploitability in representative subgames to support subgame perfection claim.  
- Overall evaluation: strong, novel, technically sound contribution that merits acceptance; weaknesses are minor and suggest useful extensions.  
- For acceptance: first formal convergence result, rigorous and novel analysis, clear presentation, and complete theory-plus-experiment contribution.  
- Against acceptance: minor issues — stronger ties to classical game theory and finite-time analyses would improve the work.
