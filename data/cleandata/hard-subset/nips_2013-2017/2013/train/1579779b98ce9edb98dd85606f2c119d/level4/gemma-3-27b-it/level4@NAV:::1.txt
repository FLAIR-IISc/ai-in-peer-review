This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) within zero-sum extensive-form games featuring perfect information and simultaneous moves, demonstrating conditions under which the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium. The empirical results support the theoretical claims.

My detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, this is a solid paper that establishes a sufficient condition for MCTS convergence. The result is valuable and the presentation is clear. However, given the paper’s primary contribution lies in the theoretical development, I question the novelty of the employed proof techniques.

2. While the paper focuses on specific algorithms, UCB-based methods are commonly used in this field and have received prior analysis (particularly UCB for Trees). Including a discussion and comparison with UCB algorithms would strengthen the work.

3. The proof currently focuses on the convergence of mean propagation. However, the experimental results suggest that propagating current sample values also performs well under various criteria. Providing an explanation for this observed behavior would be beneficial.

In summary, while the paper presents interesting findings, the technical aspects do not represent a significant advancement in the field.
