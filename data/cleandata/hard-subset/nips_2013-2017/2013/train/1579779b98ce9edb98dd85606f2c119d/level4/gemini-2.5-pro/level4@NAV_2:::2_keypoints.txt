- Studies convergence of MCTS in two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves.  
- Proposes a general Simultaneous Move MCTS (SM-MCTS) template parameterized by selection mechanisms.  
- Main theoretical result: with ε-Hannan consistent selection and sufficient exploration, average strategies converge to a subgame perfect approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof strategy: inductive treatment of nodes as repeated matrix games, with bounded error from subgame value estimates.  
- Empirical validation using Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games supports theoretical claims and examines practical variants (mean vs. sample values).  
- Strength: first formal convergence proof for MCTS in this game class, addressing an important open question.  
- Strength: technically sound, clear analysis leveraging repeated game foundations extended to multi-stage settings.  
- Strength: well-written and well-organized presentation making the argument accessible.  
- Weakness: analysis is asymptotic and provides no finite-time or convergence-rate bounds.  
- Weakness: approximation bound depends quadratically on game depth D, which may be loose for deep games.  
- Weakness: proof assumes a complete game tree and lacks discussion of pre-asymptotic behavior during tree expansion.  
- Argument for acceptance: general framework applicable to any ε-Hannan consistent selector, backed by rigorous proof and solid experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: missing finite-time guarantees and potentially impractical depth-dependent approximation bounds.
