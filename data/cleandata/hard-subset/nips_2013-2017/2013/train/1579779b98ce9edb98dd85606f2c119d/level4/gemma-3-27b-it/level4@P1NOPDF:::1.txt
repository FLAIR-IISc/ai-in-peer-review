This paper studies Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. It proves that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specific conditions, and this result is supported by empirical study.

The following are my detailed comments:

1. Overall, I believe this is a good paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful, and the presentation is clear. However, given that the paper’s main contribution lies in its theoretical development, I have some reservations regarding the novelty of the proof technique.

2. UCB algorithms are widely used within the literature, but they are not analyzed in this paper. Considering existing analyses of UCB for Trees, it would strengthen the work to include a discussion of UCB algorithms and a comparison with the algorithms presented here.

3. The paper proves convergence for the propagation of the mean, but the experiments suggest that propagating the current sample value also performs well under different criteria. Could the authors provide an explanation for this observation?

In summary, this paper studies Monte Carlo tree search in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The findings are interesting, although, from a technical standpoint, the paper isn't particularly groundbreaking.
