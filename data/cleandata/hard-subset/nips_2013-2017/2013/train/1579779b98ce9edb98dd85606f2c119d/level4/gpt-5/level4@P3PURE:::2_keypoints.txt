- Studies zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Analyzes Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with regret matching for this game class.  
- Proves convergence of the method to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clear, well written, and results are well presented.  
- Main contribution is new formal analysis with stronger theoretical guarantees than prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013).  
- Proof uses an inductive approach, establishing optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Main weakness: limited insight into internal (non-leaf) nodes of the game tree.  
- Misses opportunity to connect the analysis to subgame-perfect Nash equilibria (SPNE) and related game-theoretic concepts.  
- Recommends citing Harris (1990) on existence of SPNE in simultaneous-move games.  
- Overall: novel theoretical analysis of regret-matching MCTS, but would be stronger if tied to SPNE/subgame concepts.
