- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Studies MCTS implemented with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Main contribution is a new formal analysis; extends prior preliminary work by Lanctot et al.  
- Proof relies on an inductive argument that leverages optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and results are well-presented.  
- Significant weakness: limited ability to prove meaningful results about inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Overlooks connections to subgame perfect Nash equilibria; needs stronger linkage to game-theoretic tools.  
- Recommends citing Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Interesting from a computational perspective but would be strengthened by incorporating subgame-perfect analysis.
