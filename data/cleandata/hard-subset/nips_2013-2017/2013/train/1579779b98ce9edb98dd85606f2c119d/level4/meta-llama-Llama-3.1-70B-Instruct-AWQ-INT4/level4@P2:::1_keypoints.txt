- Studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions.
- Proposes a general MCTS template that can be instantiated with various selection methods.
- Shows convergence when the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent and satisfies additional exploration requirements.
- Includes empirical evaluations using regret matching and Exp3, demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and well-structured.
- Provides a thorough analysis and a formal proof of convergence as the main contribution.
- Novelty concern: the proof technique and use of ε-Hannan consistency and bounded-error repeated matrix game analysis are not clearly new.
- Lacks comprehensive empirical/theoretical comparison with other popular algorithms (e.g., UCB).
- Experiments show good performance for current-sample-value propagation, but the paper only proves convergence for mean propagation—this discrepancy is unexplained.
- Overall assessment: interesting insights and solid experiments but not technically outstanding.
- Recommendation: add comparisons to existing methods (like UCB) and clarify what new proof techniques or insights are contributed.
- Review score: 6/10, reviewer confidence 8/10.
