- Studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proving conditions for convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium and providing empirical validation.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation that advances practical understanding of MCTS convergence.  
- Theoretical novelty is limited: proof technique appears incremental and builds on existing frameworks rather than offering a substantially new analytical perspective.  
- Major omission of UCB-based algorithms: lacks analysis/comparison with tree-based UCB variants and discussion of how the convergence guarantees relate to prior UCB analyses.  
- Analysis focuses only on mean-value propagation, yet experiments show raw sample-value propagation performs well; requires theoretical or empirical explanation for this discrepancy.  
- Overall technically sound but does not substantially exceed current state-of-the-art; addressing theoretical novelty and UCB/sample-value gaps would strengthen the paper’s impact.
