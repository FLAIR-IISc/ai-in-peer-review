- Paper studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proving convergence to approximate Nash equilibria and providing supporting empirical results.  
- Work is well-motivated, clearly presented, and makes a useful contribution to MCTS analysis.  
- Main strength: establishes a practically relevant and rigorously demonstrated sufficient condition for convergence, validated empirically.  
- Technical novelty is limited: proof techniques use standard concentration inequalities and martingale arguments without significant methodological advances.  
- Critical omission: the paper excludes UCT (a dominant practical MCTS variant) and provides no comparison or incorporation of UCT in theory or experiments.  
- Theory studies mean-value propagation only, while experiments show sample-value (most recent sample) propagation also performs strongly, with no theoretical explanation offered.  
- Authors should analyze or justify why sample-value propagation works (e.g., variance or sampling dynamics) to bridge theory–practice gap.  
- Overall: a solid incremental contribution but lacking originality and depth for top-tier publication; addressing UCT and sample-value propagation is needed to strengthen the paper.
