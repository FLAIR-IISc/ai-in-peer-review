- Presents convergence conditions for MCTS in two-player zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games and empirical results consistent with theory.
- Strength: Provides a formal convergence result in a less-studied simultaneous-move setting.
- Strength: States a clear sufficient condition for convergence with supporting experiments.
- Strength: Writing and organization are clear and accessible.
- Weakness: Novelty of the proof technique is not clearly differentiated from prior MCTS/UCT or learning-dynamics analyses.
- Weakness: Does not analyze or empirically compare UCB/UCT selection, limiting practical relevance.
- Weakness: Theory requires mean-value backups, but experiments show sample-value backups also work—this mismatch is unexplained.
- Weakness: “Certain conditions” for convergence are not made explicit or assessed for realism in practice.
- Weakness: No finite-sample bounds or convergence rates are provided; asymptotic dependencies and constants are unclear.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation lacks detail on domains, baselines, parameter tuning, seeds, and error bars.
- Suggestion: Clarify novelty and position relative to prior MCTS/UCT and simultaneous-move game literature.
- Suggestion: Either extend analysis to UCB/UCT or explain why the framework cannot handle UCB, and add empirical UCB/UCT comparisons.
- Suggestion: Provide intuition, partial analysis, or experiments explaining when sample-value backups work or fail versus mean backups.
- Suggestion: List all convergence assumptions explicitly and discuss their necessity and practical guidance for hyperparameters.
- Suggestion: Strengthen experiments with standard benchmarks, error bars, ablations, UCB/UCT baselines, and sensitivity analyses.
- Suggestion: Improve theorem statements by adding rates or qualitative parameter dependencies and clarify the exploitability metric used.
- Minor: Precisely define “propagation of the mean” and “propagation of current sample value” and keep terminology consistent.
- Minor: Define acronyms on first use and summarize notation in a table or appendix.
- Minor: Add citations to prior UCT analyses and simultaneous-move search work; improve figure readability and include confidence intervals.
- Minor: Consider releasing code or pseudocode for reproducibility.
- Overall: Interesting and useful sufficient condition, but currently feels technically incremental due to unclear novelty, missing UCB/UCT treatment, and unexplained backup-rule discrepancy.
