- Paper analyzes a class of MCTS algorithms using any no-regret selection method (with mild restrictions) and proves asymptotic convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium for simultaneous-move games.  
- Main theoretical limitation: convergence is asymptotic with no convergence rates provided.  
- Lack of rates follows from assuming only ε-Hannan consistency for the selection method.  
- Important open question: whether rates can be derived when assuming specific regret rates (e.g., √T for exp3).  
- Experimental comparison: propagating mean reward values (analysis choice) versus propagating current sample values (standard) — mean values slightly underperform.  
- Experiments also attempt to empirically probe convergence rates.  
- Overall assessment: technically correct and well-written but limited impact due to absence of convergence rates and incremental advance over known sequential-move MCTS results.  
- Minor editorial/nitpick issues: Figure 1 “leafs” → “leaves”; line 144 “inverted” → “negated”; Algorithm 1 line 10 use (i,j) or (σ1,σ2) not (a1,a2); line 172 notation switches from (i,j) to a’s and should be fixed.
