This paper provides an analysis of the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous move games, which includes notable examples such as goofspiel and Tron. The authors examine the application of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with regret-matching to this class of games and establish convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is well-written and clearly presents its results. The primary contribution lies in the formal analysis, which offers new insights into this area. The authors build upon previous work, including Lanctot et al. (2013), which explored a similar setup but presented more preliminary analysis. The proof technique employed is inductive, relying on optimal strategies at the leaf nodes.

A significant limitation of this approach is that it does not provide substantial insights into the inner nodes of the game tree. This omission overlooks crucial concepts in game theory, particularly subgame perfect Nash equilibria. To strengthen the paper, the authors could integrate their analysis with these concepts. A relevant reference that could inform this extension is "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990).

This paper presents novel analyses of regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect information simultaneous move games. While the results are interesting from a computational perspective, they lack a connection to the powerful tools of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, which have been well-studied in the game theory literature.
