- Main contribution: proves asymptotic convergence to ε-Nash equilibria for simultaneous-move games using MCTS with any no-regret selection method (under minor constraints).  
- Limitation: convergence is only asymptotic with no rate guarantees, due to assuming only ε-Hannan consistency of selection.  
- Concern/request: authors should clarify whether specific regret bounds (e.g., √T for exponential weights) yield explicit convergence rates.  
- Context/significance: valuable first convergence proof for MCTS in extensive-form simultaneous-move games, but feels incremental relative to established sequential-move MCTS guarantees.  
- Experiments: compare mean-reward propagation (used in analysis) versus standard sample-value propagation; mean propagation slightly underperforms.  
- Experiments: authors attempt to empirically infer convergence rates.  
- Overall evaluation: well-written and technically sound, with mild reservation about significance arising from lack of rates.  
- Nitpick: typo in Figure 1 — "leafs" → "leaves".  
- Nitpick: wording at Line 144 — use "negated" (not "inverted").  
- Nitpick: Algorithm 1 Line 10 — "(a₁,a₂)" is confusing; should be "(i,j)" or "(\sigma₁,\sigma₂)".  
- Nitpick: notation inconsistencies (e.g., (i,j) vs. a’s) around Line 172 and elsewhere need editing.
