- Paper proves MCTS convergence in two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games given ε-Hannan consistent selection and sufficient exploration, using a general MCTS template.  
- Connects to prior work noting practical success of MCTS but theoretical gaps, and that UCT is known not to converge.  
- Empirical validation with Regret Matching and Exp3 shows convergence on random and worst-case games.  
- High technical quality: rigorous, stepwise proof using induction treating parent nodes as bounded-error matrix games.  
- Experiments are well-designed to test proof assumptions (e.g., mean reward propagation).  
- Very clear writing, logical structure, consistent notation, and a clear Algorithm 1 template.  
- Original contribution is the novel synthesis of online learning concepts (Hannan consistency) with MCTS analysis; likely the first formal proof for this game class.  
- Significant impact: provides theoretical foundation for MCTS in this domain and a template for designing provably convergent variants.  
- Limitation: core proof techniques are adapted from online learning, so novelty is primarily in applying existing tools rather than inventing new ones.  
- Limitation: excludes UCB-based (UCT) algorithms without detailed explanation of why the proof framework fails for them.  
- Limitation: theory requires propagating mean child rewards, which empirically converges slower than standard sample propagation; no explanation or alternative analysis provided.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first formal convergence proof, rigorous analysis, clear presentation, and important implications for future work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of widely used UCB methods and reliance on existing mathematical tools slightly limit direct applicability and perceived novelty.  
- Overall conclusion: strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance with minor suggested improvements and future directions.
