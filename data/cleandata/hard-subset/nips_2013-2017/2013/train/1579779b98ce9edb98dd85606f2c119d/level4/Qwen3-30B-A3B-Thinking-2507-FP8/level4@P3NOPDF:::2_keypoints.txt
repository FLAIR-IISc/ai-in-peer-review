- Studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and applies MCTS with regret-matching to find approximate Nash equilibria.  
- Provides a technically sound inductive proof built from optimal strategies at leaf nodes, with clear, well-structured exposition.  
- Writing is precise and results are accessible to the NIPS audience.  
- Contribution: formalizes regret-matching for this game class and extends prior preliminary analysis (Lanctot et al. 2013).  
- Major limitation: analysis focuses exclusively on terminal (leaf) nodes and does not leverage recursive subgame structure.  
- Fails to connect convergence results to subgame perfect Nash equilibrium (SPE), a stronger and relevant solution concept for these games.  
- Existing theory (Harris 1990) establishes SPE existence in such settings; the paper should engage with this literature.  
- Current proof strategy does not naturally extend to inner nodes or subgame recursion, leaving a gap in game-theoretic grounding.  
- Recommended revisions: formalize how regret-matching can induce SPE recursively, compare the found equilibrium to SPE in stability and strategic depth, and cite Harris (1990).  
- Overall: solid computational contribution but theoretical impact is limited without SPE integration; recommend acceptance pending addressed SPE analysis.
