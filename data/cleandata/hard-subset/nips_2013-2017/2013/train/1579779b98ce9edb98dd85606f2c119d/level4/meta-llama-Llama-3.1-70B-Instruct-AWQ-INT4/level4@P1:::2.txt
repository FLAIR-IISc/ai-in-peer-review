This paper analyzes the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous move games, which includes interesting games such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors implement Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with regret-matching for this class of games and prove convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear and well-written, and the results are well-presented. The main contribution is new results in the formal analysis, which builds upon previous work, such as Lanctot et al. (2013), but provides a more comprehensive analysis. The proof is inductive, starting with optimal strategies at the leaf nodes.

However, the biggest weakness in this type of approach is that not very interesting things can be proved about the inner nodes of the game tree. This misses out on some key ideas in game theory, specifically subgame perfect Nash equilibria. To improve the paper, it would be beneficial to tie the analysis to these concepts. A recommended citation is "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990).

The paper presents novel analyses of regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect information simultaneous move games. While interesting from a computational standpoint, the analyses would be strengthened by incorporating the powerful tools of sub-game perfect Nash equilibria for these class of games, which have been well-studied by game theorists.

Strengths:
- Clear and well-written paper
- New results in formal analysis
- Builds upon previous work, such as Lanctot et al. (2013)

Weaknesses:
- Limited analysis of inner nodes of the game tree
- Misses out on key ideas in game theory, specifically subgame perfect Nash equilibria

Recommendations:
- Tie the analysis to subgame perfect Nash equilibria concepts
- Incorporate the powerful tools of sub-game perfect Nash equilibria for these class of games

Overall, the paper provides a valuable contribution to the field, but could be improved by incorporating more game-theoretic concepts and analysis.
