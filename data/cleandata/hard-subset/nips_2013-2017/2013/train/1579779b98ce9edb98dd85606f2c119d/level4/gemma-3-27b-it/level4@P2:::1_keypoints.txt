- Central contribution: proof of conditions under which MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games.  
- Empirical validation: experiments support the theoretical convergence result.  
- Presentation: clear and well-organized manuscript.  
- Concern about novelty: the proof technique’s originality is not fully established.  
- Missing UCB analysis: paper lacks discussion and comparative analysis of widely used UCB algorithms and their convergence in this setting.  
- Related work gap: should discuss existing UCB-for-trees analyses and limitations for simultaneous-move games.  
- Propagation methods: paper proves convergence with mean propagation but experiments show current sample value propagation also performs well—this discrepancy needs explanation.  
- Experimental rigor: empirical comparisons should include statistical significance tests between propagation methods.  
- Scalability and rates: paper needs deeper analysis of convergence rates and scalability to larger, more complex games.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novel theoretical analysis, sufficient convergence condition, clear presentation, and supportive empirical results.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: unclear proof novelty, no UCB analysis, limited explanation for sample-value propagation effectiveness, and insufficient convergence-rate/scalability evaluation.
