- Paper studies zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and analyzes MCTS with regret matching, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clear, well written, and results are well presented.  
- Main contribution is new formal analysis of MCTS with regret matching; prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) offered only preliminary analysis.  
- Core proof is inductive and assumes optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Primary weakness: analysis gives limited insights or guarantees for internal (non-leaf) game-tree nodes.  
- Overlooks important game-theoretic concept of subgame-perfect Nash equilibria and does not connect results to that literature.  
- Suggests improving the paper by relating analysis to subgame-perfect equilibrium results (recommended citation: Harris, 1990).  
- Summary: novel, computationally compelling analysis but insufficient engagement with subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium literature.
