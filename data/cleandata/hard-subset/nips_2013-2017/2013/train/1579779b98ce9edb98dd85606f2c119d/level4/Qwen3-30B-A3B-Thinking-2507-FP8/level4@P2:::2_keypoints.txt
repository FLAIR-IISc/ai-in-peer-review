- Studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Introduces a general MCTS template parameterized by selection methods.  
- Proves that with an ε-Hannan consistent selection method and sufficient exploration, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof proceeds by induction from leaf nodes upward, carefully handling stochastic error propagation.  
- Empirical evaluation (Regret Matching, Exp3) shows convergence on random and worst-case games.  
- First formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move extensive-form games.  
- Connects Hannan consistency from online learning to equilibrium convergence in this game class.  
- Paper is well written, clearly organized, and technically sound.  
- Addresses the important practical/theoretical distinction between using mean values versus current samples in updates.  
- Results are significant for both theoretical understanding and practical applications of MCTS in strategic domains.  
- Weakness: does not explicitly establish convergence to subgame perfect equilibrium despite defining it.  
- Weakness: lacks finite-time convergence bounds, limiting practical guidance on iteration counts.  
- Weakness: the link between the theoretical guarantees and practical implementation could be more explicitly drawn.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, valuable contribution, recommend acceptance.
