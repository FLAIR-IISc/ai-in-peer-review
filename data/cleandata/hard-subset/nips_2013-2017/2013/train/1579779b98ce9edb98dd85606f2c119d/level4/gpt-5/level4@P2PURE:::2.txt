This paper studies the class of zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games, which includes interesting examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors analyze Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) implemented with regret matching for this class and prove convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear and well written, and the results are well presented. The main contribution is new formal analysis. Prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013 [20]) considered a similar setup but provided only preliminary analysis. The core proof is inductive, with optimal strategies assumed at the leaf nodes.

The primary weakness of this approach is that it yields limited insights or guarantees for internal nodes of the game tree. This overlooks important game-theoretic concepts, particularly subgame-perfect Nash equilibria. The paper could be improved by connecting its analysis to these notions. Recommended citation: “Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves,” C. Harris, 1990.

In summary, the paper presents a novel analysis of regret-matching strategies combined with MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games. While compelling from a computational perspective, it does not adequately relate its results to the well-developed literature on subgame-perfect Nash equilibria for this class of games.
