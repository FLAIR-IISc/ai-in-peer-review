- Paper studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Authors present a general MCTS template and prove convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under -Hannan consistency of selection and sufficient exploration.  
- Empirical evaluation with Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games supports the theoretical convergence claim.  
- Experiments suggest other MCTS variants may also converge in practice.  
- Overall assessment: solid, valuable theoretical contribution with clear and well-organized presentation.  
- Main concern: the proof technique may lack substantial novelty, relying on established online learning and game-theory tools.  
- Missing deeper discussion of how this work extends or differs from existing theoretical frameworks.  
- Lack of discussion on UCB: why the paper’s theoretical tools cannot be applied to UCB or how UCB might be modified to gain convergence guarantees.  
- Suggestion: relate to existing UCT/UCB-for-trees analyses (e.g., Kocsis & Szepesvári) to inform this discussion.  
- Theoretical analysis requires propagating mean rewards, but experiments show propagating sample values also performs well; need explanation of this empirical finding.  
- Conclusion: not a radical breakthrough but a rigorous, useful result that fills an important theoretical gap.  
- Arguments for acceptance: provides convergence guarantee in a challenging setting, sound proof with supporting experiments, clear writing, addresses important gap.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty of proof technique, insufficient treatment of UCB-related questions, and lack of explanation for sample-value propagation performance.
