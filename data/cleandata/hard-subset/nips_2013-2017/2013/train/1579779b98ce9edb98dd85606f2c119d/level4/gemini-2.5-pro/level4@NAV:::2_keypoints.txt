- Studies zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and analyzes MCTS with regret-matching.  
- Provides a formal proof of convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium for this game class.  
- Manuscript is clear, well-written, and presents findings effectively.  
- Main novelty is a rigorous formal analysis extending prior preliminary work (e.g., Lanctot et al. [20]).  
- Proof is inductive, assuming optimal strategies at leaf nodes and building upward.  
- Key weakness: limited guarantees/insights about behavior at inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Misses connection to Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibria (SPNE); should engage with SPNE literature (e.g., Harris 1990).  
- Overall: compelling computational contribution but would be stronger by integrating game-theoretic SPNE analysis.
