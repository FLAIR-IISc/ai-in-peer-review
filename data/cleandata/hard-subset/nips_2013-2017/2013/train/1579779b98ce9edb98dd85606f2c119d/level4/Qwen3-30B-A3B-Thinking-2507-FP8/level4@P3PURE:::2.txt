

This paper analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, a class that includes interesting examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors study Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) implemented with regret-matching, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium for this class. The paper is clearly written, with well-presented results and a novel formal analysis. Previous work cited by the authors (Lanctot et al., 2013) examines a similar setup but provides less formal analysis. The proof employs induction, leveraging optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  

The primary weakness lies in the limited scope of the theoretical analysis: the approach yields only trivial insights about inner nodes of the game tree, overlooking key concepts in game theory, specifically subgame perfect Nash equilibria. The paper would be significantly strengthened by connecting its analysis to these established concepts. A relevant reference is Harris (1990): "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves." While computationally interesting, the analysis fails to leverage the well-developed framework of subgame perfect Nash equilibria for this class of games.
