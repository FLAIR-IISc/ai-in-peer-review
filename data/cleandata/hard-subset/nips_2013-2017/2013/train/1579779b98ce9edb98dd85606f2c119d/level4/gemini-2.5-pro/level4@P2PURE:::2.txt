This paper presents a formal analysis of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS), in conjunction with regret-matching, for the class of zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games, which includes examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors prove that this approach converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is well-written and its results are presented with clarity. The main contribution is a novel formal analysis that builds upon the preliminary work of Lanctot et al. [20], which examined a similar setting. The proof proceeds by induction, assuming optimal strategies at the leaf nodes.

The primary weakness of the analysis is that it offers limited insights into the strategy at the inner nodes of the game tree. Consequently, the paper misses an opportunity to connect its findings to the powerful game-theoretic concept of Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibria (SPNE). The work could be significantly strengthened by tying the analysis to this well-studied equilibrium concept. A relevant citation would be:

*   Harris, C. (1990). "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves."

In summary, this paper presents a novel analysis of a regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games. While interesting from a computational standpoint, the analysis does not connect with powerful and relevant tools like Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibria, a topic that has been well-studied by game theorists for this class of games.
