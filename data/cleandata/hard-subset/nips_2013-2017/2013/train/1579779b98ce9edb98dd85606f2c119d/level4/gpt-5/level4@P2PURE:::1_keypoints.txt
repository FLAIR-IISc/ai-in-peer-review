- Paper proves MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games, with supporting empirical results.
- Solid, clearly presented result giving a sufficient condition for convergence, but the novelty of the proof technique is questionable.
- UCB-based algorithms, commonly used and previously analyzed for trees, are not considered; including and comparing them would strengthen the work.
- The theory guarantees convergence mainly for mean propagation, yet experiments show current-sample propagation also performs well — this discrepancy needs explanation.
- Overall interesting and useful findings, but limited technical novelty.
