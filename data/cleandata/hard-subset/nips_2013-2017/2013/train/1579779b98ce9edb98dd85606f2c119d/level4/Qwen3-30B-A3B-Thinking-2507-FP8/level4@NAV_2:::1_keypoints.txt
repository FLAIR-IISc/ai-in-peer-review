- Paper proves convergence guarantees for MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move extensive-form games using ε-Hannan consistent selection with exploration, and empirically validates results.  
- Claims first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in this game class.  
- Technical approach and theoretical analysis are sound and supported by experiments.  
- Concern: Novelty of proof technique is limited—builds on existing regret-minimization/online-learning results; needs clearer differentiation from prior work and comparisons (e.g., Lanctot et al. 2014).  
- Concern: Paper omits analysis/discussion of UCT, which is widely used; should address UCT’s applicability to simultaneous-move games and compare empirical performance.  
- Concern: Theory requires mean-value propagation, but experiments show current-sample propagation also works; the paper fails to explain this theory–practice discrepancy.  
- Overall: Meaningful and significant contribution, but theoretical novelty and practical implications need stronger positioning within the literature and responses to the noted concerns.
