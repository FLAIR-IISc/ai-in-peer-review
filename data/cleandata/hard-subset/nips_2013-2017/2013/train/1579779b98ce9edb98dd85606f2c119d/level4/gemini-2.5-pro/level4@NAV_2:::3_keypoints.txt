- Studies convergence of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves.  
- Introduces a general Simultaneous Move MCTS (SM-MCTS) template that uses any regret-minimizing selection procedure.  
- Proves that if the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent with sufficient exploration, average strategies converge to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium (asymptotically).  
- Fills a literature gap by providing the first formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in this game class.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound, well-written, and uses clear induction over the game tree.  
- Generalization to any ε-Hannan consistent selector is a strong and broadly applicable aspect.  
- Main weakness: proof is purely asymptotic and gives no finite-time convergence rates.  
- Practical significance limited because exact equilibria can be computed in polynomial time for these games, and MCTS’s advantage (speed) isn’t theoretically quantified.  
- Open question: can the analysis yield explicit rates using known regret bounds (e.g., O(√T) for Exp3)?  
- Empirical study supports theoretical claims and compares mean-reward propagation (theoretically required) vs. sample-value propagation (common heuristic).  
- Experiments show the theoretically grounded mean-propagation is slightly slower in practice.  
- For acceptance: novel first proof of convergence, technical soundness, and broad applicability to no-regret selection methods.  
- Against acceptance: only asymptotic convergence and could be seen as an incremental extension from sequential MCTS results.  
- Overall: high-quality, valuable theoretical contribution with reservations about impact due to lack of finite-time rates.
