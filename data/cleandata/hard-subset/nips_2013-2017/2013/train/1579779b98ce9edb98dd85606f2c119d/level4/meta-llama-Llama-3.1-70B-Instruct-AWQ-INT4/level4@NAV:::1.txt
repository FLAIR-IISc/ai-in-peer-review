This paper explores the application of Monte Carlo tree search in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, demonstrating convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specific conditions. The empirical results support the theoretical findings.

Detailed comments are as follows:
1. Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a valuable sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. While the presentation is clear, and the result is useful, the novelty of the proof technique raises some concerns, as the theoretical contribution is the primary focus of the paper.
2. Given the widespread use of UCB algorithms in the literature, their absence in the analysis is notable. Considering the existing analyses of UCB for Trees, it would be beneficial for the authors to incorporate UCB algorithms into their comparison and analysis.
3. The proof only addresses the convergence of the mean propagation, but the experimental results also indicate good convergence of the current sample value propagation under different criteria. An explanation for this observation would be appreciated.

This paper examines Monte Carlo tree search in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, yielding interesting findings. However, from a technical standpoint, the paper's impact is somewhat limited.
