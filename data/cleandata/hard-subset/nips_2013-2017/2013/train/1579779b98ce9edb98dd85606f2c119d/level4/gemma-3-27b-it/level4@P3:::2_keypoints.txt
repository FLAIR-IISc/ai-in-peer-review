- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proves MCTS with regret-matching converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.
- Main contribution: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in this game class.
- Paper is clear and well-written with a logical inductive proof building from leaf-node optimal strategies.
- Technical analysis appears rigorous and the framework is well-defined.
- Empirical evaluation supports the theoretical results.
- Major weakness: limited insight into inner nodes of the game tree and neglect of subgame perfect Nash equilibria.
- Authors mention subgame perfection but do not integrate it into the analysis; connecting to this theory would strengthen the work.
- Recommend citing Harris (1990) on regret-matching and subgame perfect equilibria.
- Assumption of perfect information is restrictive and may limit practical applicability; robustness to imperfect information is unexplored.
- Paper proves convergence but does not analyze convergence rates, limiting practical guidance.
- Scope is relatively narrow, focusing only on simultaneous-move perfect-information games.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important open problem, provides novel theoretical results, empirical support, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow scope, lack of connection to subgame perfection, and missing convergence-rate analysis.
- Overall: solid and novel contribution; recommend acceptance with suggestions to address the identified limitations in future work.
