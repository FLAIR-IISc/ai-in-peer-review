This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors prove that MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specified conditions, and empirical results validate this theoretical finding.

My detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, this is a solid paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is valuable and the presentation is clear. However, given that the primary contribution of the work is theoretical, I have some reservations regarding the novelty of the proof technique employed.

2. Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) algorithms are frequently used in the literature, yet this paper does not analyze them. Considering existing analyses of UCB for Trees, it would strengthen the work to include a consideration of UCB algorithms and a comparison with the methods presented here.

3. The paper demonstrates convergence for the propagation of the mean, but the experiments suggest that propagating the current sample value also performs well under various criteria. Could the authors provide an explanation for this observation?

In summary, this paper studies an interesting topic, but technically, the contribution is not exceptionally groundbreaking.
