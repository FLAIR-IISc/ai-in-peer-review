- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, including examples like Goofspiel and Tron.  
- Proves convergence of MCTS with regret matching to an approximate Nash equilibrium for this class of games.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and results are well-presented.  
- Main contribution is a new formal analysis; extends prior, more preliminary work by Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- Proof relies on induction, building on optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Primary weakness: limited scope of provable results for inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Overlooks connections to subgame perfect Nash equilibria and related game-theoretic concepts.  
- Recommends citing Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria with simultaneous moves.  
- Suggests strengthening the paper by connecting the analysis to established tools for subgame perfect Nash equilibria.
