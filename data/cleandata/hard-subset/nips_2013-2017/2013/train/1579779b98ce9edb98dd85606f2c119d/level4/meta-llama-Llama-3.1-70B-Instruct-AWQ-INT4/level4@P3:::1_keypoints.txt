- Studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, providing a general MCTS template.  
- Proves that if a selection method is ε-Hannan consistent in matrix games and meets exploration requirements, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Empirical evaluation is solid and confirms the formal analysis.  
- Technical results are sound but not particularly outstanding.  
- Novelty concern: proof technique largely extends existing results and lacks clear new insights.  
- Originality: applying ε-Hannan consistency to MCTS in simultaneous-move games is new, but underlying methods are not.  
- Missing analysis/comparison of UCB algorithms, which are widely used and relevant.  
- Empirical finding: mean propagation converges well, but sample-value propagation also works—authors should explain this.  
- Some notation and definitions need improvement for readability.  
- Significance: interesting results suitable for a conference like NIPS but likely not a top-tier journal contribution.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address novelty claims, include UCB comparison, and clarify empirical observations and notation.
