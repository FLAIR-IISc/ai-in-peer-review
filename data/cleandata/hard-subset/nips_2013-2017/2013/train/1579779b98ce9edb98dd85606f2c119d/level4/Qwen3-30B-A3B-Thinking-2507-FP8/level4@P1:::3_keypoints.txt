- Contribution: First formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Framework: Introduces a general MCTS framework that uses any regret-minimizing selection method for matrix games.  
- Theoretical result: Shows that if the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent with guaranteed exploration, the algorithm converges to an ε-Nash equilibrium.  
- Proof technique: Sound induction proof propagating convergence from leaf nodes to root while controlling MCTS’s stochastic bounded errors.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments show mean value propagation converges to approximate Nash equilibria, with convergence rates comparable to existing MCTS variants.  
- Technical rigor: Rigorous, clear proofs that specifically address challenges posed by simultaneous moves.  
- Originality: Novel extension of MCTS analysis to simultaneous-move games where prior work lacked convergence guarantees.  
- Significance: Provides a theoretical foundation for practitioners using MCTS in games with simultaneous moves.  
- Limitation 1: No convergence rates are provided; only asymptotic convergence is proven.  
- Limitation 2: Mean propagation required by theory slightly underperforms in experiments, creating a discrepancy needing explanation.  
- Minor issues: A few notational inconsistencies (e.g., switching between (i,j) and (a1,a2)).  
- Recommendation: Paper is technically sound and significant; reviewer recommends acceptance despite the lack of convergence rates.
