- Main contribution: Analysis showing MCTS algorithms converge using any no-regret selection method (with mild restrictions), proving convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium for simultaneous-move games.  
- Limitation: Convergence result is asymptotic and does not provide explicit convergence rates.  
- Cause of limitation: Analysis assumes only ε-Hannan consistency of the selection method, which prevents deriving rates.  
- Open question: Important to determine achievable rates when specific regret rates (e.g., Exp3’s O(√T)) are assumed for the selection method.  
- Experiments: Compared propagating mean reward values (used in analysis) vs. standard propagation of current sample values; mean propagation performed slightly worse empirically.  
- Experiments: Authors also attempted to empirically assess convergence rates.  
- Significance: Paper is technically sound and well-written, but impact is tempered by lack of convergence rates and perceived incrementality given known results for sequential-move MCTS.
