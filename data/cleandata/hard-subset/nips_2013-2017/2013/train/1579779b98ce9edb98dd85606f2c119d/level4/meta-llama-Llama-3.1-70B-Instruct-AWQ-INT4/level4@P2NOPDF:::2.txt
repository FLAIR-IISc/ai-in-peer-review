This paper provides an in-depth analysis of the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous move games, which includes notable examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors focus on the application of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with regret-matching in this context and demonstrate convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, effectively presenting the results of the analysis. A significant contribution of this work is the formal analysis of MCTS with regret-matching, which builds upon previous research by Lanctot et al. (2013). The authors employ an inductive proof approach, assuming optimal strategies at the leaf nodes of the game tree.

A notable limitation of this approach is that it does not provide substantial insights into the inner nodes of the game tree. This omission overlooks crucial concepts in game theory, particularly subgame perfect Nash equilibria. To enhance the paper, the authors could explore integrating their analysis with these concepts. A recommended reference for this purpose is "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990).

In summary, the paper presents novel analyses of the regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect information simultaneous move games. While the computational aspects of this analysis are intriguing, the work would benefit from connecting its findings to the established game-theoretic framework of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, which has been extensively studied in the field.
