- Main contribution: proves convergence of a specific MCTS variant to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move extensive-form games under stated conditions.  
- Empirical validation: experiments support the theoretical results.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written and logically structured, making core ideas accessible.  
- Novelty concern: the proof technique’s originality is unclear and needs a deeper comparison to existing analyses.  
- Limited algorithm coverage: analysis excludes common UCB/UCT algorithms; should either include them or justify/explain extensibility.  
- Theory–experiment gap: theoretical analysis focuses on mean propagation, but experiments show sample-value propagation also performs well; this discrepancy should be addressed or theoretically extended.  
- Technical impact: results are correct and useful but not a substantial methodological breakthrough.  
- Requested clarifications: elaborate on proof novelty, explain omission/extension to UCB, account for sample-value propagation performance, and add limitations/future work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the points above.
