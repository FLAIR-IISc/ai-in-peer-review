- Paper studies SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move perfect-information games and proves that ε‑Hannan consistent, sufficiently exploring selection rules yield convergence to an approximate subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium; experiments with Regret Matching and Exp3 support this.

- Significant theoretical contribution: provides, to the reviewer’s knowledge, the first formal convergence proof for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, linking online learning (Hannan consistency) to tree-search convergence.

- Sound and rigorous analysis: methodical extension from repeated matrix games to bounded-payoff errors and inductive application to game trees; proof appears technically solid.

- Clarity and organization: paper is well-written, problem and algorithm are clearly presented, and experiments directly address theoretical claims.

- Novelty caveat: proof technique is mainly a careful combination of known tools (Hannan consistency, regret minimization, induction) rather than a fundamentally new method; authors should temper claims of originality of technical machinery.

- Limited scope on UCB methods: paper dismisses UCB variants based on prior negative results but should empirically compare to UCB-based SM-MCTS since UCB is popular and may perform well in practice.

- Theory–experiment gap: formal proof requires propagating mean values (M variants), yet experiments show single-sample propagation (standard variants) also converges and even faster; this discrepancy needs discussion or theoretical extension.

- Request for clarification: explain precisely why the proof requires propagating the mean—e.g., is it relying on law-of-large-numbers stabilization for bounded error in Lemma 4.9?

- Request for intuition: why does single-sample return propagation work well empirically despite not being covered by the proof; provide possible explanations or theoretical avenues.

- Practical implications of depth D: approximation bounds depend on tree depth, so for deep games the base ε may need to be very small—please comment on the feasibility and practical meaning of this dependence.
