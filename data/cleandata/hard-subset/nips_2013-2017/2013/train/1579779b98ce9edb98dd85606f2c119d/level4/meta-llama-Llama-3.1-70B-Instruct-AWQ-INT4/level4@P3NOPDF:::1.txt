This paper studies Monte Carlo tree search in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. It provides a theoretical analysis of the convergence of the MCTS algorithm to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions. The empirical study presented in the paper confirms the formal result, demonstrating the practical applicability of the findings.

The detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, I think this is a good paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful, and the presentation is clear. However, as the main contribution of the paper lies in the theoretical part, I have concerns regarding the novelty of the proof technique. It would be beneficial for the authors to elaborate on how their approach differs from existing methods and what new insights it offers.

2. UCB algorithms are widely used in the literature, but they are not analyzed in this paper. Given the existing analysis on UCB for Trees, it would be beneficial for the authors to also consider UCB algorithms and compare them with other algorithms presented in the paper. This would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.

3. The paper only proves the good convergence of the propagation of the mean. However, in the experiments, the propagation of the current sample value also seems to perform well under different criteria. It would be helpful if the authors could provide some explanation for this observation. Are there any underlying reasons why the propagation of the current sample value performs well in practice, despite the lack of theoretical guarantees?

In conclusion, while the paper presents some interesting findings, it is not particularly technically outstanding. The authors could strengthen the paper by addressing the concerns mentioned above and providing more insights into the theoretical and practical aspects of their approach.
