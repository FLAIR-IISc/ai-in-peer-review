This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) within zero-sum extensive form games featuring perfect information and simultaneous moves, demonstrating convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specified conditions, a result validated by empirical evaluation.

My detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, this is a solid contribution that establishes a sufficient condition for MCTS convergence. The result is valuable and the presentation is clear. However, given the paper’s primary contribution lies in its theoretical development, I have some reservations regarding the novelty of the proof methodology employed.

2. While the paper focuses on specific algorithms, UCB-based methods are prevalent in the field, and analyses of UCB for Trees already exist. It would strengthen the work to include a consideration of UCB algorithms and a comparative analysis against the approaches presented here.

3. The theoretical proof currently focuses on the convergence of mean propagation. However, the experimental results suggest that propagating current sample values also performs well across various criteria. Providing an explanation for this observation would be beneficial.

In summary, while the paper presents interesting findings, its technical contribution, while sound, is not exceptionally groundbreaking.
