- Studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Analyzes MCTS implemented with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clearly written and presents novel formal analysis in this area.  
- Extends preliminary analysis by Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- Proof uses an inductive argument relying on optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Major limitation: analysis provides limited insight into inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Omits discussion of subgame perfect Nash equilibria; linking to that theory would strengthen the work.  
- Recommends consulting Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria for relevant background.
