- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Studies Monte Carlo Tree Search with regret-matching and shows convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Primary contribution is a formal analysis providing new insights into this game class.  
- Builds on prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013) but offers more complete proofs.  
- Proof is inductive and depends on optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Major limitation: analysis gives little insight into inner (non-leaf) nodes of the game tree.  
- Neglects subgame-perfect Nash equilibrium concepts and related game-theoretic tools.  
- Recommendation: tie results to subgame-perfect equilibria (e.g., Harris 1990) to strengthen impact.
