- Studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., goofspiel, Tron).  
- Analyzes MCTS with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Primary contribution is novel formal analysis building on Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- Proof is inductive and relies on optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents results.  
- Major limitation: analysis gives little insight into inner game-tree nodes.  
- Misses connection to subgame perfect Nash equilibria and related game-theoretic tools.  
- Suggests citing Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Overall: interesting computational analysis but overlooks leveraging subgame-perfect equilibrium theory.
