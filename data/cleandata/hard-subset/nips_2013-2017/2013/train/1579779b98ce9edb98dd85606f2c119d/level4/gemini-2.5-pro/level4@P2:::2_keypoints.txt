- Paper presents a formal convergence proof for a general Simultaneous Move MCTS (SM-MCTS) template in two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information extensive-form games.  
- Main result: if the selection method is ε-Hannan consistent and ensures sufficient exploration, SM-MCTS converges to an approximate subgame perfect Nash equilibrium; regret matching and Exp3 are analyzed as instances.  
- Significance/originality: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in simultaneous-move settings, bridging practice and game-theoretic theory.  
- Technical quality: sound, well-structured proofs using induction from single-stage ε-Hannan consistent algorithms to multi-stage games with bounded error.  
- Clarity/organization: paper is clear, well-written, and breaks the analysis into understandable components; experiments validate theory and explore implementations.  
- Completeness: provides a general algorithmic framework, rigorous proofs, and empirical evaluation supporting theoretical claims.  
- Weakness — asymptotic proof: guarantees eventual convergence but provides no finite-time/sample-complexity bounds for achieving an ε-approximation.  
- Weakness — theory vs. practice: theoretical guarantees rely on propagating mean rewards (M variants), yet empirically the standard single-value propagation converges faster and lacks a formal proof here.  
- Weakness — limited scope: analysis restricted to perfect-information games; imperfect information would break the current proof structure and needs further tools.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, technically solid contribution that meaningfully advances theory for MCTS in simultaneous-move games and merits acceptance.
