This paper provides an in-depth examination of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous move games, including notable examples such as goofspiel and Tron. The authors employ Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) with regret-matching to analyze this class of games, successfully proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, effectively presenting its findings. The primary contribution lies in the novel formal analysis, which builds upon previous work by Lanctot et al. (2013) with more in-depth examination. The proof relies on an inductive approach, focusing on optimal strategies at the leaf nodes.

However, a significant limitation of this approach is its inability to provide meaningful insights into the inner nodes of the game tree. This oversight neglects crucial concepts in game theory, particularly subgame perfect Nash equilibria. To strengthen the paper, the authors could explore integrating their analysis with these concepts.

A recommended reference for this purpose is "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990). While the paper offers innovative analyses of regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect information simultaneous move games, its examination would benefit from incorporating the established tools of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, a well-studied area in game theory.
