- Studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and analyzes MCTS with regret-matching for this class.  
- Proves convergence of the proposed method to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Manuscript is well-written with clear exposition and well-presented results.  
- Main contribution is a novel formal analysis, improving on prior preliminary work (e.g., Lanctot et al. 2013).  
- Uses an inductive proof technique beginning from optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Main limitation: analysis gives little insight into inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Fails to sufficiently connect to game-theoretic concepts like subgame perfect Nash equilibria.  
- Recommends citing Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games.
