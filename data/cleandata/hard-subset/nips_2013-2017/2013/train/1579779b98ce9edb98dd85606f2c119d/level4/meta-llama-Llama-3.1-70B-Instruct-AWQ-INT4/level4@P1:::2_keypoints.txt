- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and implements MCTS with regret-matching, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and presents results effectively.  
- Main contribution is new formal analysis that builds on prior work (e.g., Lanctot et al. 2013) using an inductive proof starting from leaf-node strategies.  
- Provides novel computational analysis of regret-matching within MCTS for this game class.  
- Major weakness: limited provable insights about inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Major weakness: fails to engage with key game-theoretic concepts, notably subgame perfect Nash equilibria.  
- Recommendation: tie the analysis to subgame perfect Nash equilibrium concepts (e.g., Harris 1990).  
- Recommendation: incorporate subgame-perfect tools to strengthen and broaden the theoretical analysis.  
- Overall: valuable contribution but would be improved by integrating deeper game-theoretic analysis.
