This paper provides an in-depth analysis of the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous move games, which encompasses various interesting games such as goofspiel and Tron. The authors examine the performance of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) implemented with regret-matching in this class of games and prove its convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is well-written and clearly presents its results. The primary contribution lies in the novel formal analysis, which builds upon previous work by Lanctot et al. (2013) [20]. The proof is inductive, relying on optimal strategies at the leaf nodes.

However, a significant limitation of this approach is that it does not provide substantial insights into the inner nodes of the game tree. This omission overlooks key concepts in game theory, particularly subgame perfect Nash equilibria. To enhance the paper, it would be beneficial to connect the analysis to these concepts. A relevant reference that could be cited is "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990).

In conclusion, this paper presents novel analyses of regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect information simultaneous move games. Although the analysis is interesting from a computational perspective, it misses the opportunity to leverage the powerful tools of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, which have been extensively studied in game theory.
