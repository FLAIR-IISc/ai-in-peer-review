- Paper analyzes MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, providing a general algorithmic template.  
- Main theoretical result: MCTS converges asymptotically to an epsilon-Nash equilibrium if the selection method is epsilon-Hannan consistent and satisfies exploration requirements.  
- Convergence result is asymptotic only; no convergence rates are provided.  
- Lack of rates is a limitation but partly unavoidable under the weak epsilon-Hannan consistency assumption; rates merit future study when specific regret rates are assumed.  
- Empirical comparison: propagating mean reward values (used in analysis) slightly underperforms propagating current sample values.  
- Experimental attempts to infer convergence rates are inconclusive; more empirical/theoretical work is needed to understand rates.  
- Paper is well-written and technically correct.  
- Impact concern: result could be seen as incremental because convergence for sequential-move MCTS is already known.  
- Minor revisions needed for notation inconsistencies and typos (e.g., "leafs"→"leaves", "inverted"→"negated", clarify Line 10 of Algorithm 1).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance pending minor revisions; considers the contribution significant though not groundbreaking.
