This paper investigates the application of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors prove that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions, which is supported by empirical studies.

My detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, I believe this is a good paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful, and the presentation is clear. However, as the main contribution of the paper lies in the theoretical part, I have concerns regarding the novelty of the proof technique.

2. UCB algorithms are widely used in the literature, but they are not analyzed in this paper. Given that there have been some analyses on UCB for Trees, it would be beneficial for the authors to also consider UCB algorithms and compare them with other algorithms in the paper.

3. The authors only prove that the propagation of the mean has good convergence. However, in the experiments, the propagation of the current sample value also appears to be effective under different criteria. I would appreciate an explanation for this observation.

While the findings in the paper are interesting, technically speaking, the paper is not very outstanding.
