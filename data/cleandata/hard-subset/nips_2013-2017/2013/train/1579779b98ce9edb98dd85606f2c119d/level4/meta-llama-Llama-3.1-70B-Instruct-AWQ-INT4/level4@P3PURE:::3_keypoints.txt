- Analysis of MCTS using any no-regret selection method under mild restrictions; main theoretical contribution is a proof of asymptotic convergence to ε‑Nash in simultaneous-move games.  
- Convergence is only asymptotic with no rates provided, due to assuming only ε‑Hannan consistency for the selection method.  
- Important missing piece: rates of convergence when specific regret bounds are assumed (e.g., √T for Exp3).  
- Empirical comparison: propagating mean reward values (the analysis’ choice) slightly underperforms propagating current sample values.  
- Experiments also attempt to empirically estimate convergence rates.  
- Paper is well-written and technically correct.  
- Mild reservation about impact/significance because similar convergence is known for sequential-move MCTS and lack of rates limits practical relevance.  
- Nitpicks: typos/notation issues — “leafs”→“leaves”; “negated” better than “inverted”; Algorithm 1 and line 172 need consistent (i,j) vs (a1,a2)/a notation.
