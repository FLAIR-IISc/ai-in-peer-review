This paper investigates the application of Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors provide a theoretical analysis, proving that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions. The empirical study supports this formal result.

My detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, I believe this is a good paper that establishes a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful, and the presentation is clear. However, since the main contribution of the paper lies in its theoretical part, I have concerns regarding the novelty of the proof technique.

2. UCB algorithms are widely used in the literature, but they are not analyzed in this paper. Given that there have been some analyses of UCB for Trees, it would be beneficial for the authors to also consider UCB algorithms and compare them with other algorithms in the paper.

3. The paper only proves that the propagation of the mean has good convergence. However, in the experiments, the propagation of the current sample value also appears to perform well under different criteria. I would appreciate some explanation for this observation.

In conclusion, while this paper presents interesting findings, its technical contributions are not particularly outstanding.
