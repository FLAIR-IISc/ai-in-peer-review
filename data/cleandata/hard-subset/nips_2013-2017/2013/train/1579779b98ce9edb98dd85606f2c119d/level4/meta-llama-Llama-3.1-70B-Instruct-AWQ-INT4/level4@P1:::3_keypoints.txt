- Main contribution: Proves asymptotic convergence of a class of MCTS algorithms to ε-Nash in simultaneous-move games using (mildly restricted) ε-Hannan-consistent selection methods.
- Theoretical limitation: Convergence is asymptotic with no rates provided, a consequence of only assuming ε-Hannan consistency for the selection method.
- Open question: Important to know convergence rates when specific regret rates are assumed for selection methods (e.g., √T from Exp).
- Novelty: Extends convergence analysis of MCTS to extensive-form games with simultaneous moves, where prior proofs are lacking.
- Experimental comparison: Propagating mean reward values (analysis approach) slightly underperforms propagating current sample values in experiments.
- Experimental goal: Attempts to empirically investigate convergence rates but does not fully resolve the theoretical rate question.
- Impact assessment: Technically sound and a useful first step, but significance is tempered by lack of convergence rates and perceived incrementality relative to sequential-move MCTS results.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance subject to minor revisions to address presentation issues.
- Clarity/quality: Paper is well-written, organized, and technically correct, though some notation inconsistencies exist.
