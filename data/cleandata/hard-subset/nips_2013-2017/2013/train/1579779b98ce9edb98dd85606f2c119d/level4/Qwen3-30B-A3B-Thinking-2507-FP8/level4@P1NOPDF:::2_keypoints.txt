- Studies zero-sum perfect-information games with simultaneous moves (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Analyzes MCTS with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium for this class.  
- Paper is clearly written with effectively presented results.  
- Main contribution is a formal analysis extending prior preliminary work by Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- Proof uses induction and relies on optimal play at terminal nodes.  
- Major limitation: analysis gives no meaningful insights about intermediate game states.  
- Overlooks subgame perfect Nash equilibria, a key concept for strategic behavior at every decision point.  
- Recommends explicitly connecting regret-matching/MCTS convergence results to subgame perfection.  
- Points to Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria for simultaneous-move games as relevant theory.  
- Integrating subgame-perfect perspective would strengthen theoretical grounding beyond the computational contribution.  
- Current work is computationally novel but remains disconnected from established game-theoretic tools; authors should address this gap.
