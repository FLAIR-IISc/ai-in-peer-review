- Proves asymptotic convergence of MCTS with any no-regret selection method to ε-Nash equilibria in simultaneous-move extensive-form games—a novel theoretical gap fill.

- Main limitation: no convergence rates are provided, weakening practical relevance and impact.

- Lack of rates is inherent to the ε-Hannan consistency assumption used in analysis.

- Without rates, asymptotic convergence is less useful since exact equilibria can be obtained via backward induction.

- Suggestion: analyze specific regret bounds (e.g., √T for exponential weights) to derive concrete convergence rates; authors do not address this.

- Experiments compare mean-reward propagation to standard sample-value propagation and find mean propagation marginally worse.

- Experimental attempts to estimate convergence rates are inconclusive and do not resolve the core concern about rates.

- Paper is well-written and technically sound but the result is incremental relative to established sequential-move MCTS convergence results, reducing significance for NIPS.

- Recommendation: add rate analysis or stronger justification for prioritizing asymptotic results to increase impact.

- Nitpicks/editorial fixes: "leafs" → "leaves"; use "negated" not "inverted"; change "(a₁,a₂)" in Alg.1 to "(i,j)" or "(σ₁,σ₂)"; fix notation inconsistencies (e.g., around line 172).
