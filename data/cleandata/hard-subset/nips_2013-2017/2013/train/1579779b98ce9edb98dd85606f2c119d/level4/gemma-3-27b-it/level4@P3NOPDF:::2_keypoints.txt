- Presents formal analysis of MCTS combined with regret-matching for zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium, extending prior work (Lanctot et al. 2013).  
- Writing and presentation are clear, making the results and arguments accessible.  
- Main contribution is new analytical results on the behavior of regret-matching within the MCTS framework.  
- Proof technique relies on an inductive argument that assumes optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Key weakness: the analysis struggles to draw strong conclusions about algorithm behavior at inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Missing connection to subgame perfect Nash equilibria; focusing only on overall approximate Nash convergence feels incomplete.  
- Recommends citing Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria to relate the work to established theory.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to explicitly address and incorporate subgame perfect equilibrium considerations; clarity and rigor are commendable.
