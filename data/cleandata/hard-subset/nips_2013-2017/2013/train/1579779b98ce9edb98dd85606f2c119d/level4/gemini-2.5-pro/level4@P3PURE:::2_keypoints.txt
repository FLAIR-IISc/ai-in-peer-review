- Problem scope: analyzes zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Algorithm: introduces an MCTS algorithm incorporating regret-matching.  
- Theoretical result: proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium for this game class.  
- Presentation: paper is clearly written and results are well-presented.  
- Novelty: offers a novel formal analysis building on prior work (e.g., Lanctot et al. [20]).  
- Proof approach: convergence proof is inductive, assuming optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Weakness: provides limited guarantees about strategies at internal (inner) nodes of the game tree.  
- Missing connection: fails to tie results to Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibria (SPNE); suggests consulting C. Harris (1990) on subgame perfection.
