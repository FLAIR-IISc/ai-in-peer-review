- Addresses theoretical gap: provides MCTS analysis for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Presents a general MCTS template that can be instantiated with any regret-minimizing procedure for the matrix games at each node.  
- Proves convergence to an approximate equilibrium when using an ε-Hannan consistent selection method with sufficient exploration.  
- Uses an inductive proof strategy: establish optimality at leaves and extend upward through the tree.  
- Empirical validation with regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games supports the theoretical claims.  
- Demonstrates convergence speed comparable to existing MCTS variants.  
- Key contribution: first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in this specific game class.  
- Strong theoretical link drawn between Hannan consistency in repeated matrix games and convergence to Nash equilibria in extensive-form games.  
- Careful treatment of exploration requirements and adaptation of online learning algorithms to the MCTS framework.  
- Writing and presentation are clear, well-structured, and technically sound.  
- Main weakness: insufficient explicit engagement with the subgame perfect Nash equilibrium concept despite its relevance and use in definitions.  
- Suggestion: cite and discuss Harris (1990) and explicitly relate the convergence results to subgame perfection to strengthen the theoretical positioning.  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS-suitable) with revision to better connect to subgame perfect equilibrium literature.
