- Analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., goofspiel, Tron).  
- Applies MCTS with regret-matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and results are well-presented.  
- Main contribution: new formal analysis building on Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- Proof technique is inductive and relies on optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Major weakness: limited ability to make claims about inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Neglects subgame perfect Nash equilibria and related game-theoretic concepts.  
- Recommends integrating subgame-perfect analysis and citing Harris (1990).  
- Overall: computationally interesting but misses connections to subgame perfect equilibria literature.
