- Generality: Analysis applies to essentially any no-regret selection method, making the result modular and widely relevant.  
- Novelty: First proof of asymptotic convergence for MCTS in simultaneous-move extensive-form games, where sequential analyses don't apply.  
- Technical quality: Core arguments are correct, well structured, and clearly written.  
- Asymptotic-only guarantee: No finite-time/convergence rates are provided, limiting practical guidance.  
- Finite-sample question: Can exploitability bounds be derived from known regret rates (e.g., O(√T) or O(log T)) and how do tree depth, branching, and simultaneous nodes amplify regret?  
- Backup types: Unclear whether mean-value vs. single-sample backups separate in rates; experiments show mean backups slightly underperform, needing theory.  
- Exploration role: Need clarification on how exploration parameters affect finite-time bias and bounds.  
- Scope/incrementality: Without rates or concrete cases where simultaneous-move structure changes outcomes, the contribution risks feeling incremental versus sequential-move results.  
- Experiments — metrics: Report exploitability over time (or proxy) to connect experiments to the theoretical goal rather than just payoffs.  
- Experiments — robustness: Include confidence intervals and seed variability.  
- Experiments — algorithms: Test specific no-regret algorithms (EXP3, regret matching, Hedge) to compare empirical convergence to theoretical regret rates.  
- Experiments — structure: Vary tree depth and branching factor to probe structural effects on convergence.  
- Experiments — motivation: Include a domain where backward induction is infeasible to justify using MCTS despite exact methods.  
- Assumptions: Explicitly state all assumptions for the convergence theorem (bounded payoffs, independent randomization, exploration, tie-breaking).  
- Definitions: Define “ε–Hannan consistent” on first use and relate it to selection rules used in experiments.  
- Guarantee scope: Clarify whether the ε–Nash guarantee applies to the full extensive-form game or the explored subtree and how averaging is used to form strategies.  
- Typos/notation: Fix “leafs”→“leaves”, “inverted”→“negated”, and multiple confusing notation instances (Alg.1 line10, line172, action/profile consistency).  
- Overall: Solid, technically sound paper that advances understanding; main reservation is lack of finite-time rates — providing bounds or structural conditions would significantly strengthen impact.  
- Recommendation/status: Close to ready pending addressing rates and presentation issues; reviewer enjoyed reading.
