This paper studies Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. It proves that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specified conditions, and this claim is supported by empirical study.

Overall, this is a sound paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is valuable and the presentation is clear. However, given that the primary contribution of the paper lies in its theoretical analysis, I have some reservations regarding the novelty of the proof technique employed.

UCB algorithms are widely used within the MCTS framework, yet they are not analyzed in this work. Considering existing analyses of UCB for Trees, it would strengthen the paper to include a discussion of UCB algorithms and a comparison with the methods presented here.

The paper demonstrates convergence for the propagation of the mean, but the experimental results suggest that propagating the current sample value also performs well under various criteria. An explanation for this observation would be beneficial. 

While the findings are interesting, technically the paper does not represent a significant breakthrough.
