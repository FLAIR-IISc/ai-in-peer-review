This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, providing a convergence proof to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions. The empirical study supports the formal result.

Detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a useful sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The presentation is clear, but since the main contribution lies in the theoretical part, I have concerns regarding the novelty of the proof technique.

2. UCB algorithms, widely used in the literature, are not analyzed in this paper. Given the existing analysis on UCB for Trees, it would be beneficial for the authors to consider UCB algorithms and compare them with other algorithms in the paper.

3. The proof only shows good convergence for the propagation of the mean. However, the experimental results also indicate good convergence for the propagation of the current sample value under different criteria. A further explanation for this observation would be appreciated.

While the paper presents interesting findings, from a technical standpoint, it does not stand out significantly.
