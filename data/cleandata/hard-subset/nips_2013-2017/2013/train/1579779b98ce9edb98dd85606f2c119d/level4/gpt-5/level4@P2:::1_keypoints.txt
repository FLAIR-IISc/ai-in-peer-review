- Paper analyzes SM-MCTS for two-player zero-sum simultaneous-move games and proposes a general template using ε-Hannan-consistent selection rules with exploration.  
- Proves eventual convergence to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium, with a stated approximation bound scaling (claimed) quadratically with game depth D.  
- Proof approach: (i) HC ⇒ convergence in matrix games, (ii) extend to repeated games with bounded payoff error, (iii) induct over tree depth to account for error accumulation.  
- Experiments on random and adversarial instances support theory; regret-matching and Exp3 variants converge, mean backups slightly slower than sample backups.  
- Situates work against exact simultaneous-move solvers and highlights negative results for UCT/UCB in simultaneous-move settings; leverages known HC results.  
- Strength: first formal convergence guarantee for SM-MCTS to approximate NE in this class of games.  
- Strength: general, modular template—any ε-HC selection rule with exploration suffices.  
- Strength: clear separation of analysis into matrix games, bounded-error extension, and tree induction.  
- Strength: empirical evidence aligns with theoretical claims and includes adverse test cases.  
- Strength: establishes subgame-perfect convergence, not just root-level equilibrium.  
- Weakness: core proof techniques (HC ⇒ approachability and induction over depth) are conceptually standard; need clarity on novel technical steps (e.g., Lemma 4.9).  
- Weakness: approximation bound stated as O(D^2 ε) appears loose and missing ε in theorem statement; need corrections and discussion of tightness or lower bounds.  
- Weakness: treatment of UCB/UCT is limited—paper should explain precisely why UCT fails HC conditions and whether modifications (forced exploration, unbiased backups) could fit the framework; include empirical comparisons.  
- Weakness: theory requires mean backups while experiments show sample backups converge faster—need intuition or analysis (bias–variance, martingale properties) and partial theoretical results for sample backups.  
- Weakness: experimental scope is limited (mostly D≤2, small BF); recommend scaling depth/branching, exploring γ, reporting confidence intervals, runtime/memory, more baselines, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Presentation issue: Theorem 4.11 omits ε in the NE bound and needs restatement.  
- Presentation issue: unify terminology for empirical frequencies vs average strategies and state which is used in main theorem.  
- Presentation issue: clarify whether forced-exploration iterations update learner state (A* definition vs implementation); align theory and implementation.  
- Presentation issue: improve Algorithm 1 readability (line refs, consistent return value usage).  
- Quality: theoretical core appears sound for eventual convergence under stated assumptions, but finite-time bounds are absent.  
- Clarity: well-organized and readable overall, with minor notation slips and missing experimental variability reporting.  
- Originality: moderate—assembly of known HC tools into SM-MCTS is novel and useful but not technically groundbreaking.  
- Significance: meaningful for search in simultaneous-move domains; template guides algorithm design, impact would increase with tighter bounds and sample-backup theory.  
- Pros for acceptance: first convergence guarantee, general template practical for RM/Exp3, and supporting experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, loose depth-dependent bounds, exclusion of UCT in theory/experiments, and gap between mean-backup theory and sample-backup practice.  
- Suggested improvements: correct/tighten final bound and discuss tightness; expand experiments (scale, baselines including UCT, runtime/memory); provide intuition or partial theory for sample backups; clarify exploration handling and strategy averaging.
