- Provides a formal analysis of MCTS for zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Integrates regret-matching into MCTS and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and well-presented results.  
- Primary contribution: new formal analysis of regret-matching within MCTS for this game class.  
- Significantly extends prior preliminary analyses such as Lanctot et al. (2013).  
- Technical highlight: an inductive proof establishing convergence by reasoning from leaf-node optimal strategies up the tree.  
- Main weakness: disconnect between computational analysis and game-theoretic concepts, specifically Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium (SPNE).  
- Current proof does not characterize learned strategies at internal game-tree nodes (no SPNE guarantees).  
- Recommends connecting convergence results to approximate SPNE (e.g., see Harris 1990) to strengthen theoretical impact and appeal.
