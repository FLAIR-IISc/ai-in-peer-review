- Paper studies MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games (perfect information, simultaneous moves), proving MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions; experiments confirm the theory.
- Main theoretical contribution: provides a sufficient condition for MCTS convergence.
- Concern that the novelty of the proof technique is unclear.
- Suggestion to analyze and compare UCB-based tree algorithms, since UCB is widely used and has prior analyses.
- Paper proves good convergence for propagation of the mean, but experiments show current-sample propagation also performs well—needs explanation.
- Strengths: useful result, clear presentation, empirical confirmation of the formal result.
- Weaknesses: limited technical originality, omission of UCB analysis, and incomplete explanation of empirical observations.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7, Clarity 8, Originality 6, Significance 7.
