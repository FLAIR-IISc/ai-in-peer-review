- Primary contribution: proof that a class of MCTS algorithms using any (mildly restricted) no-regret selection method converges asymptotically to an ε-Nash equilibrium for simultaneous-move games.  
- Analysis assumption: selection methods are only ε-Hannan consistent, which forces asymptotic convergence without provided rates.  
- Main weakness: no convergence rates are given, leaving open practical questions about speed of convergence.  
- Suggestion/request: quantify rates when specific regret bounds are assumed (e.g., √T regret of exp3) and report achievable convergence rates.  
- Comparison to existing results: known convergence for sequential-move MCTS makes this simultaneous-move result incremental in impact.  
- Experiments: compare propagating mean rewards (used in analysis) vs. propagating sample values; mean propagation slightly underperforms.  
- Experiments also attempt to empirically assess convergence rates but do not fully resolve the theoretical rate question.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and technically correct, but mild reservation about ultimate significance due to lack of rates.  
- Minor corrections: "leafs" → "leaves"; "inverted" → "negated" (line 144); Algorithm 1 notation (a1,a2) should be (i,j) or (σ1,σ2); fix inconsistent switching between (i,j) and a's (line 172).
