- Central contribution: provides a theoretical proof that MCTS with a broad class of no-regret selection methods converges to an ε‑Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move games.  
- Limitation: the convergence guarantee is asymptotic and does not provide explicit convergence rates.  
- Cause of limitation: authors assume only ε‑Hannan consistency for selection methods, which prevents deriving rate bounds.  
- Suggestion: relating convergence speed to specific regret bounds (e.g., Exp3’s √T bound) or to backward induction would strengthen the work.  
- Experiments: compare propagating mean reward values (analysis choice) versus propagating current sample values (standard practice).  
- Experimental finding: mean-value propagation shows a slight performance disadvantage compared to sample-value propagation.  
- Empirical assessment: authors attempt to measure convergence rates empirically, though no theoretical rates are provided.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound, clearly written, and a solid contribution, but its impact is limited by the lack of convergence-rate results.  
- Significance concern: result feels somewhat incremental given existing MCTS convergence for sequential moves.  
- Minor comments: typos and notation issues—“leafs”→“leaves”; Line 144 use “negated” not “inverted”; Algorithm 1’s (a1,a2) is unclear (use (i,j) or (σ1,σ2)); persistent inconsistencies between (i,j) and ‘a’ notation.
