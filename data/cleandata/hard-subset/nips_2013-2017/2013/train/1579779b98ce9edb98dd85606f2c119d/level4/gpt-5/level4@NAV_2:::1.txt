This paper investigates Monte Carlo Tree Search in two-player zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. It establishes that the MCTS procedure converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specific conditions, and the empirical evaluation supports the theoretical claim.

Specific comments:
1. Overall, I find this a solid paper that identifies sufficient conditions for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful and the exposition is clear. That said, since the main contribution is theoretical, I am concerned about the degree of novelty in the proof techniques.
2. UCB methods are widely used in the literature, yet they are not addressed here. Given existing analyses of UCB for trees, it would strengthen the paper to also examine UCB algorithms and compare them with the methods considered.
3. The theory only covers propagating mean estimates, whereas the experiments indicate that propagating the current sample value also performs well across metrics. Some explanation for this empirical behavior would be helpful.

In summary, the paper presents interesting findings, but from a technical standpoint it is not particularly exceptional.
