This paper studies Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move extensive-form games. It proves that, under certain conditions, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium, and the empirical results support the theoretical findings.

Detailed comments:

1. Overall, this is a good paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. The result is useful and the presentation is clear. However, since the main contribution is theoretical, I am concerned about the novelty of the proof technique.

2. UCB-based algorithms are widely used in the literature, but they are not analyzed here. Given that there has been prior analysis of UCB for Trees, it would strengthen the paper to include UCB algorithms and compare them with the other algorithms considered.

3. The paper proves convergence only for propagation of the mean. However, in the experiments, propagating the current sample value also appears to perform well across different criteria. Can you explain this apparent discrepancy?

In summary, the topic is interesting and the results are relevant, but from a technical standpoint the paper is not particularly outstanding.
