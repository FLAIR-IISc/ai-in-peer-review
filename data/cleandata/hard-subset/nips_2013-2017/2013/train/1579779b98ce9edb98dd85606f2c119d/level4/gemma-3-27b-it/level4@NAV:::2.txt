This paper investigates zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, a class that includes well-known examples like Goofspiel and Tron. The authors provide an analysis of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) utilizing regret matching within this game class, and establish convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clearly written and presents results in a well-organized manner. Its primary contribution lies in novel formal analysis of the algorithm. While related work, such as Lanctot et al. [20], explored similar foundations, this paper builds upon that work with more conclusive results. The proof employs an inductive approach, relying on optimal strategies at terminal nodes. 

A notable limitation of this approach is the limited scope of provable results regarding internal nodes of the game tree. This restricts the connection to important game-theoretic concepts, specifically subgame perfect Nash equilibria. Strengthening the analysis by relating it to these established concepts would significantly enhance the work. I recommend the authors consider “Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves” by C. Harris (1990) as a relevant reference. This paper offers a valuable analysis of regret-matching strategies with MCTS in the studied game class, however, the analysis would benefit from a deeper engagement with the existing literature on subgame perfect Nash equilibria, a well-developed area within game theory.
