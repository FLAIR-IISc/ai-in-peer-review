This paper analyzes the class of zero-sum perfect-information games. This class includes some interesting games, such as Goofiespeak and Tron. The authors have analyzed MCTS, which has been applied with regression matching for games in this class and proven convergence in an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear and well-written, and the results are well-presented. The main contribution is new results in formal analysis. The previous work cited by the authors [20] by Lanithot et al, 2013, looks at the setup but is more of an initial analysis. The evidence is inductive – with optimal strategies on leaf nodes.

I think the biggest weakness in this approach is that it cannot prove very interesting things about the internal nodes of the game tree. It leaves out some key ideas in game theory: the subgame perfect Nash equilibrium. I think the paper could be improved by connecting the analysis to those concepts. Recommended citation:

"The Existence of the Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium in Interactive Games". C. Harris 1990. This paper presents a novel analysis of the regression-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information interactive games. Despite being interesting from a computational perspective, the analysis misses connecting to the powerful tools of the subgame perfect Nash equilibrium for games in this class; something that has been well studied by game theorists.
