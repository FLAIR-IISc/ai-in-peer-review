- The paper proves that a general class of MCTS algorithms with no-regret selection converge to an ε-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move extensive-form games.  
- The convergence proof is asymptotic and does not provide explicit convergence rates.  
- The lack of rates stems from assuming only ε-Hannan consistency for the selection method.  
- This appears to be the first convergence proof for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, representing a novel contribution.  
- The absence of rates limits impact, since other methods (e.g., backward induction) can compute exact equilibria.  
- The reviewer asks whether concrete regret bounds for the selection module would yield explicit rates (e.g., assuming O(√T) regret).  
- Experiments compare propagating mean rewards (used in theory) versus single-sample propagation; mean propagation shows slightly weaker empirical performance.  
- Additional experiments aim to provide empirical insight into the algorithms' convergence rates.  
- The paper is well-written and the technical claims appear correct.  
- Primary reservation: overall impact is tempered by missing theoretical convergence rates and potential perception as an incremental extension of existing sequential-move results.
