- Presents analysis of MCTS using essentially any no-regret selection method under mild restrictions, with primary theoretical contribution: proof of asymptotic convergence to ε-Nash in simultaneous-move games.

- Convergence result is asymptotic with no rates provided.

- Lack of rates follows from assuming only ε-Hannan consistency for the selection method.

- Important to analyze rates when a specific regret rate is assumed (e.g., √T for exp3); authors should state what rates would be achieved.

- Experiments compare propagating mean reward values (analysis) vs. propagating current sample values (standard); mean propagation slightly underperforms.

- Authors attempt to empirically estimate convergence rates in experiments.

- Overall assessment: well-written and technically correct, but impact is limited by lack of convergence rates and feels incremental given existing sequential-move MCTS results.

- Minor corrections: typo in Figure 1 ("leafs" → "leaves") and word choice at Line 144 ("negated" not "inverted").

- Notation issues: Algorithm 1 Line 10 should use (i,j) or (σ1,σ2) instead of (a1,a2); Line 172 and elsewhere inconsistently switch between (i,j) and a's.
