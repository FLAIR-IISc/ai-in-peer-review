This paper analyzes the class of zero-sum perfect-information games, including games like Go and chess. The authors perform an MCTS-based analysis of this class of games, which includes regret-minimization, and prove the convergence of an approximate Nash equilibrium.

The paper is clear and well-written, and the results are well-presented. Its main contribution is new results in formal analysis. The authors refer to the previous work of Lenkot et al, 2013 [20], which looks at the setup but has more fundamental analysis. The proof is with optimal strategies in the ascending-leaf node.

I think the biggest weakness of this approach is that it cannot prove anything very interesting about the internal nodes of the game tree. It excludes some key concepts of game theory, such as the subgame perfect-Nash equilibrium. I think the paper could be improved by connecting this analysis to those concepts. Proposed citation:

"The Existence of Subgame Perfect Equilibrium in Games with Perfect Information". C. Harris 1990. This paper presents new analysis in zero-sum perfect-information games with regret-minimization strategy MCTS. Although interesting from a computational perspective, these analyses are excluded from being connected to the powerful tools of subgame perfect-Nash equilibrium for this class of games; which have been well studied by game theorists.
