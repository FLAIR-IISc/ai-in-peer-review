- Formal analysis of MCTS with regret-matching for zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).
- Shows convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium in this game class, extending prior analyses like Lanctot et al. [20].
- Presentation is clear, well-structured, and results are effectively communicated.
- Core contribution: new theoretical underpinning for the observed performance of regret-matching MCTS.
- Proof technique uses induction and assumes optimal strategies at leaf nodes.
- Major limitation: little insight into the algorithm’s behavior at inner nodes of the game tree.
- Does not address or connect results to subgame perfect Nash equilibria, a key concept in game theory.
- Misses engagement with the broader literature on subgame perfection for simultaneous-move games.
- Recommendation: explicitly relate regret-matching MCTS dynamics to existence/computation of subgame perfect equilibria to strengthen the paper.
- Suggests citing Harris (1990) “Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves.”
- Summary: valuable and novel computational analysis but limited theoretical impact without addressing subgame perfection; filling this gap would broaden appeal.
