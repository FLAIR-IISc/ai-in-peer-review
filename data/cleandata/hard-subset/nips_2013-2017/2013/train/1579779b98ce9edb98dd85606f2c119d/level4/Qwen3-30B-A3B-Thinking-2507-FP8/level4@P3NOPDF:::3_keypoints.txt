- Presents a meaningful theoretical result: MCTS with general no-regret selection converges asymptotically to ε-Nash equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Proof leverages ε-Hannan consistency of selection methods, which inherently rules out non-asymptotic convergence rates under the paper’s assumptions.  
- Lack of convergence rates significantly reduces practical relevance and the paper’s impact.  
- Authors should analyze whether specific regret bounds (e.g., √T for exponential weights) can produce concrete convergence rates.  
- Without rates, it is unclear how MCTS compares computationally to exact methods like backward induction in finite games.  
- The contribution feels incremental because sequential-move MCTS already has established convergence rates; this work only adds asymptotic guarantees for a new setting.  
- Experiments compare mean-reward propagation (used in analysis) to standard sample-value propagation; mean-value slightly underperforms, aligning with prior results.  
- Experimental attempts to estimate convergence rates are not rigorous and are constrained by the absence of theoretical benchmarks.  
- Paper is clearly written and technically sound; core analysis and experiments are correctly executed.  
- Overall significance is limited: confirms convergence in a new setting but does not resolve computational efficiency or provide actionable quantitative guarantees.  
- Recommendation: explicitly discuss trade-offs between asymptotic guarantees and practical efficiency, and investigate whether particular regret bounds yield provable rates.  
- Nitpicks: fix typos and notation inconsistencies—"leafs"→"leaves"; "negated" vs "inverted"; resolve conflicting use of (a₁,a₂) vs a_{i,j} and inconsistent (i,j) notation.
