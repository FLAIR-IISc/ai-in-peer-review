This paper analyzes zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games, a class that includes notable examples like Goofspiel and Tron. The authors investigate MCTS, implemented with regret-matching, for this game class and demonstrate convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium. 

The paper is clearly written and well-presented, with the main contribution being novel formal analysis in this area. This work expands upon preliminary analysis presented in previous work by Lanctot et al. [20, 2013], which considered a similar setup. The proof relies on an inductive argument, leveraging optimal strategies at the leaf nodes.

A significant limitation of this approach, however, is the limited ability to derive meaningful results concerning the inner nodes of the game tree. This omission overlooks crucial concepts in game theory, such as subgame perfect Nash equilibria. Strengthening the connection between the analysis and these concepts would improve the paper. I recommend consulting "Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves" by C. Harris (1990) for relevant background. This paper offers a novel analysis of regret-matching within an MCTS framework for zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games. While interesting from a computational perspective, the analysis would benefit from a more explicit connection to established theoretical tools like subgame perfect Nash equilibria, which have been extensively studied by game theorists.
