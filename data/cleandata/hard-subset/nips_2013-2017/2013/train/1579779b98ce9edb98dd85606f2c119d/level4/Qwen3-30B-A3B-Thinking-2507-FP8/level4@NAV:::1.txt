

This paper investigates Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proving that MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specific conditions, with empirical results supporting the theoretical claims.  

Detailed comments follow:  
1. The paper presents a clear and useful sufficient condition for MCTS convergence, with a well-structured theoretical contribution. However, the novelty of the proof technique requires further justification, as the primary contribution centers on theoretical analysis.  
2. UCB-based algorithms are prevalent in the literature yet unaddressed in this work. Given existing analyses of UCB for trees, the authors should incorporate UCB algorithms and include comparative evaluations with other methods.  
3. While convergence is established for mean propagation, the experiments indicate that propagating current sample values also performs well across various criteria. An explanation for this discrepancy is warranted.  

The findings are technically sound and interesting, though the paper's overall technical contribution does not significantly advance the field beyond established results.
