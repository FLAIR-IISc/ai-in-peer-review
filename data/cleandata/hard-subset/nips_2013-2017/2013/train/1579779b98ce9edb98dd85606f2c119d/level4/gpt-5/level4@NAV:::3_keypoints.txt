- Contribution: analyzes a class of MCTS algorithms that can use essentially any no-regret selection method (with mild restrictions).  
- Main theoretical result: proof of asymptotic convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move settings.  
- Limitation: convergence is only asymptotic — the paper gives no convergence rates.  
- Cause of limitation: analysis assumes only ε-Hannan consistency of the selection method, which yields no rate information.  
- Context: this may be acceptable since, to the reviewer’s knowledge, no prior convergence proofs exist for MCTS in extensive-form games with simultaneous moves.  
- Importance of rates: convergence rates matter in practice (backward induction gives exact equilibrium), so rate bounds would strengthen the result.  
- Question raised: if one assumes specific regret rates (e.g., sqrt(T) for EXP), do corresponding MCTS convergence rates follow?  
- Experiments: compare propagating mean rewards (as in analysis) vs. propagating current sample; mean propagation performs slightly worse empirically.  
- Experiments also attempt to extract empirical convergence-rate behavior.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and technically sound, but impact is somewhat limited by absence of rates and feels incremental compared to sequential-move MCTS results.  
- Nitpicks: typos/notation issues — “leafs”→“leaves”; line 144 “negated” vs “inverted”; Algorithm 1 line 10 uses (a1,a2) instead of (i,j) or (σ1,σ2); notation inconsistently switches between (i,j) and a’s.
