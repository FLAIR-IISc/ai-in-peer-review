This paper studies Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) in the context of zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors prove that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions, a formal result that is supported by an empirical study. The findings are interesting and the paper is clearly presented. However, while the main contribution is theoretical, the technical novelty appears to be limited.

Detailed comments:

1.  The paper's primary contribution is a theoretical proof providing a sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS algorithms. While this result is useful and the presentation is clear, there are concerns regarding the novelty of the proof technique employed.

2.  The analysis does not include UCB-based algorithms, which are widely used in the literature. Given that prior theoretical analyses exist for UCB in trees, the paper would be significantly strengthened by an analysis of UCB algorithms and a comparison of their convergence properties to the methods studied here.

3.  The theoretical results only prove convergence for the propagation of the mean. In contrast, the experiments suggest that propagating the current sample value also performs well across different criteria. The authors should provide some explanation or intuition for this empirical observation, as it is not covered by their theoretical framework.
