This paper discusses Monte Carlo tree searches in zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. It is proven that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions. Empirical research confirms the formal results.

Detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, I think this is a good paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of the MCTS algorithm. The result is useful, and the presentation is clear. However, since the main contribution of the paper should be the theoretical part, I have concerns about the novelty of the proof technique.

2. The UCB algorithms are algorithms widely used in literature, but they are not analyzed in this paper. Since there is some analysis on UCB for trees, it would be good if the authors considered UCB algorithms and compared them with other algorithms.

3. Only it has been proven that the average expansion has good convergence. However, the expansion of the current sample values under various criteria in the experiment also seems good. Can you provide some explanation?
