- Main contribution: proves that using any no-regret selection method in MCTS converges to an ε-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move games, filling a literature gap.  
- Theoretical significance: first convergence proof for MCTS in simultaneous-move games; analysis is rigorous and well-presented.  
- Key weakness: convergence result is only asymptotic and provides no explicit convergence rates.  
- Reason for weakness: authors only assume ε-Hannan consistency, which precludes deriving specific rate bounds.  
- Open question: what convergence rates follow when assuming concrete regret rates (e.g., Exp3’s O(√T))?  
- Experiments: compare propagating mean rewards vs. sample values, finding slight performance differences and attempt empirical rate assessment.  
- Empirical support: experiments back the theoretical findings, though rate assessment is limited.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound and clearly written, representing a solid contribution.  
- Reservations: lack of rates and incremental nature relative to sequential-move MCTS work may limit impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fills gap, rigorous theory, supporting experiments, clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: asymptotic-only result without rates, somewhat incremental.  
- Nitpicks: typo “leafs”→“leaves”; use “negated” instead of “inverted”; clarify (a1,a2) notation in Algorithm 1; fix inconsistent notation between (i,j) and a’s.
