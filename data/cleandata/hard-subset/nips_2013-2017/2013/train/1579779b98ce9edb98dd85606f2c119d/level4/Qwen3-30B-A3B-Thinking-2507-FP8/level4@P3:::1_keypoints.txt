- Problem: Provides first formal convergence guarantees for MCTS in zero-sum perfect-information games with simultaneous moves.  
- Main theoretical contribution: Presents a general MCTS template and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium when using ε-Hannan consistent selection with proper exploration.  
- Proof approach: Reduces to Hannan consistency in matrix games and extends to extensive-form games via careful inductive arguments.  
- Empirical validation: Tests with regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst-case games, showing the theory holds in practice.  
- Strength: Solid, well-executed theoretical analysis building on established online-learning and game-theory results.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation that supports theoretical claims.  
- Strength: Clearly distinguishes contribution from prior work and cites relevant literature (e.g., Shafiei et al. 2011).  
- Weakness: Proof technique’s novelty is limited—conceptually straightforward extension of existing results.  
- Weakness: Fails to explain why current sample value propagation (RM) works well in practice despite theoretical preference for mean propagation (RMM).  
- Weakness: Omits analysis of UCT, despite its widespread practical use and discussion in the introduction.  
- Weakness: Results are asymptotic only; no finite-time convergence bounds are provided.  
- Technical clarity: Writing is generally clear, but connections between regret matching and the MCTS template and the transition from matrix to extensive-form games could be elaborated.  
- Originality/significance: Meaningful advance for a previously unanalyzed class of games, though not groundbreaking; appropriate for NIPS.  
- Recommendation: Accept—valuable theoretical foundation and empirical support, suitable for publication despite limited novelty and some empirical/technical gaps.
