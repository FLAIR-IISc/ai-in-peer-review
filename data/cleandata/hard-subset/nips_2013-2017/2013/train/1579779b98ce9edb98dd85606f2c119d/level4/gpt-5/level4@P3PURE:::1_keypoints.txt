- Proves MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves; experiments support the theoretical claim.
- Presentation is clear and the findings are interesting.
- Reviewer questions the novelty and strength of the proof techniques and asks authors to clarify which aspects of the analysis are genuinely new and the key technical ideas enabling convergence.
- UCB-type algorithms are not analyzed or compared; reviewer requests inclusion of UCB variants in theory/experiments or a clear explanation of their exclusion and relation to prior UCT/UCT-for-Trees work.
- Theory guarantees convergence for mean-value propagation, but experiments show sample-value propagation also performs well; reviewer asks for an explanation and whether the analysis can be extended to cover sample-value propagation.
- Overall technical novelty appears limited and the paper is not viewed as technically outstanding.
- Important algorithmic baselines (notably UCB variants) are missing from the analysis and empirical comparison.
