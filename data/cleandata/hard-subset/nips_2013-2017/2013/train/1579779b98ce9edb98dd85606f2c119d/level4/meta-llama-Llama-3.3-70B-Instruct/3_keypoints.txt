- Main contribution: proves convergence of a class of MCTS algorithms to ε-Nash equilibria for simultaneous-move games, compatible with virtually any no-regret selection method under minor constraints.  
- Key theoretical condition: analysis requires the selection method to be ε-Hannan consistent.  
- Limitation: convergence is asymptotic with no explicit convergence rates provided.  
- Open question: determining convergence rates when assuming specific regret rates (e.g., √T for exponential weights) would be valuable.  
- Experiments: comparing mean reward propagation (used in analysis) vs. standard sample-value propagation shows mean values perform slightly worse.  
- Experiments also attempt to empirically estimate convergence rates, but no definitive theoretical rates are produced.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and well-written but potentially incremental and of limited impact due to absence of convergence rates.  
- Minor corrections: fix "leafs"→"leaves"; use "negated" instead of "inverted"; clarify Algorithm 1 notation "(a1,a2)" (e.g., "(i,j)" or "(σ1,σ2)"); resolve notational inconsistencies like switching from "(i,j)" to "a's".
