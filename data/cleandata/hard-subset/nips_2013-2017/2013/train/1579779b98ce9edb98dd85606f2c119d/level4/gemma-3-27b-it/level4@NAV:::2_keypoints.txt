- Studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Analyzes Monte Carlo Tree Search with regret matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Primary contribution is novel formal analysis of the algorithm.  
- Builds on prior work (Lanctot et al. [20]) but delivers more conclusive results.  
- Proof uses an inductive approach based on optimal strategies at terminal nodes.  
- Limitation: provable results for internal game-tree nodes are limited.  
- This limitation weakens connections to subgame perfect Nash equilibria and related game-theoretic concepts.  
- Recommends citing Harris (1990) and deeper engagement with literature on subgame perfect equilibria.
