- Paper is well-written and gives a clear sufficient condition for MCTS convergence, but the novelty of the proof technique is unclear—authors should clarify what is genuinely new versus adapted from prior work.  
- Missing analysis of UCB/UCT-style selection: authors should either provide theoretical guarantees for UCB within their framework, empirically compare UCB variants, or discuss why extending the analysis to UCB is challenging.  
- Theory proves convergence for mean-value backups, yet experiments show current-sample backups perform well; this discrepancy needs explanation.  
- Request clarification of conditions under which current-sample propagation can behave like mean backups.  
- Suggest investigating whether implicit averaging from repeated visits or exploration reduces variance and explains empirical success of current-sample backups.  
- Ask authors to identify game structures or parameter regimes where current-sample backups would fail and relate those to the paper’s assumptions.  
- Overall: results are interesting but appear incremental; strengthening novelty claims and addressing UCB and the mean vs. sample backup gap would substantially improve the paper.
