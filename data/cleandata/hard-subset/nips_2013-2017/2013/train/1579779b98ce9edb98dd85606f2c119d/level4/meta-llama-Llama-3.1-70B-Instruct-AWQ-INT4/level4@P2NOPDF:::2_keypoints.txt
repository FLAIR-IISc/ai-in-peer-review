- Paper studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and MCTS with regret-matching.  
- Main contribution: formal analysis showing MCTS with regret-matching converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Builds on prior work by Lanctot et al. (2013) and uses an inductive proof technique.  
- Inductive proof assumes optimal strategies at leaf nodes of the game tree.  
- Major limitation: analysis gives little insight into inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Overlooks connections to subgame-perfect Nash equilibria and related game-theoretic concepts.  
- Recommendation: integrate the analysis with subgame-perfect equilibrium literature (e.g., Harris 1990).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and computational aspects are intriguing.
