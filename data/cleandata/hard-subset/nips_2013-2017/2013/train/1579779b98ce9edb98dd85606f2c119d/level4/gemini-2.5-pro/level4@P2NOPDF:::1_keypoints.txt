1. Paper studies MCTS for zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium under specific conditions.  
2. Empirical study corroborates the formal convergence result.  
3. Overall well-written and clear presentation.  
4. Main concern: novelty of the proof technique is unclear and should be better situated relative to prior MCTS convergence proofs.  
5. Important omission: no analysis or discussion of UCB/UCT selection strategies despite their prevalence and existing theoretical work.  
6. Theory–experiment gap: proofs cover mean-value propagation, but experiments show strong performance when propagating current sample values; this discrepancy needs explanation.  
7. Conclusion: results are useful but technical significance is uncertain until proof novelty and UCB-related gaps are addressed.
