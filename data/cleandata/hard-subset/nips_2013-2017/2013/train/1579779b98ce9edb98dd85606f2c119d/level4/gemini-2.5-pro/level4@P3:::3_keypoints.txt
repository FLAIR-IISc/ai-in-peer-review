- Paper analyzes MCTS for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves and introduces a general MCTS template using no-regret selection methods.
- Main theoretical contribution: proof that ε-Hannan consistent selection with sufficient exploration ensures convergence to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium.
- First formal convergence guarantee for MCTS in simultaneous-move games, filling an important theoretical gap.
- Empirical evaluation using Regret Matching and Exp3 supports the theoretical claims.
- Practical MCTS variant (propagating current sample value) is not covered by the proof but converges empirically in experiments.
- Strength: original and significant — novel formal proof of MCTS convergence in this game class.
- Strength: framework is general, applying to any ε-Hannan consistent selection method.
- Strength: technically sound and clearly written, with logical inductive proof from single-stage to multi-stage games.
- Weakness: analysis is asymptotic only — no convergence rates are provided, limiting practical assessment.
- Weakness: contribution may be seen as incremental, combining established online-learning concepts with MCTS.
- Key question: can explicit convergence rates be derived by instantiating the analysis with specific algorithms (e.g., Exp3 with O(√T) regret)?
- Conclusion: valuable and well-executed asymptotic result that justifies acceptance despite limitations regarding finite-time bounds.
- Minor: typo in Figure 1 caption ("leafs" → "leaves").
- Minor: prefer "negated" over "inverted" for player 2 utilities in a zero-sum setting.
- Minor: notational inconsistencies (e.g., actions (i,j) vs. 'a', σs(h,a) vs. rh[i']) — clarify action selection notation in Algorithm 1.
