This paper studies Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) for zero-sum extensive form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. The authors prove that their MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions, a formal result that is subsequently confirmed by empirical study.

Overall, this is a good paper that provides a useful sufficient condition for the convergence of MCTS. The presentation is clear, and the findings are interesting. However, my primary concern is that while the paper's main contribution is theoretical, its technical novelty is somewhat limited. While the findings are valuable, the paper is not technically outstanding.

Detailed comments are as follows:

1.  My main reservation relates to the novelty of the proof technique. Given that the theoretical convergence proof is the central contribution of the paper, the reliance on what appear to be established methods may limit its overall impact.

2.  A notable omission is the analysis of UCB-based algorithms. UCB is a widely-used selection strategy in MCTS, and prior theoretical analyses for UCB in trees already exist in the literature. The paper would be significantly strengthened by including an analysis of UCB-based variants and comparing their performance to the algorithms currently presented.

3.  The theoretical analysis exclusively proves convergence for the MCTS variant that propagates the mean value up the tree. However, the experiments suggest that propagating a single, current sample value also performs well across different criteria. The authors should provide some discussion or intuition to explain this empirical success, as it is not covered by the current theoretical framework.
