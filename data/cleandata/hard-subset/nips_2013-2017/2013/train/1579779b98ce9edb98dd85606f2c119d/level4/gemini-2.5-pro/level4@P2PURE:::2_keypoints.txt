- Paper presents a formal analysis of MCTS with regret-matching for zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Authors prove the approach converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written and results are clearly presented.  
- Main contribution builds on preliminary work by Lanctot et al. [20].  
- Proof proceeds by induction, assuming optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Primary weakness: limited insight into strategy behavior at inner (non-leaf) nodes.  
- Misses opportunity to connect results to Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibria (SPNE).  
- Suggestion to cite Harris (1990) on existence of SPNE in simultaneous-move games.
