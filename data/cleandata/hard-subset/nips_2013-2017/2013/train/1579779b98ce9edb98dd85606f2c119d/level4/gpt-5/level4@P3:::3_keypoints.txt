- Studies MCTS for two-player, zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves and introduces an SM-MCTS template using any ε–Hannan-consistent selection rule with guaranteed exploration.
- Main theoretical result: SM-MCTS converges asymptotically to a subgame-perfect approximate Nash equilibrium, with a bound that scales with game depth.
- Proof strategy: (i) show convergence for ε-HC in repeated matrix games, (ii) extend to repeated matrix games with bounded payoff errors from sampling/exploration, (iii) inductively propagate bounds up the game tree.
- Empirical evaluation with Regret Matching and Exp3 on random and hard instances supports the theory and compares returning current sample vs. running mean in backpropagation.
- Fills a theoretical gap (UCT-style methods lacked guarantees in simultaneous-move settings) and contrasts with negative results for UCB in these games.
- Positions simulation-based SM-MCTS as a practical alternative to exact methods (backward induction, alpha-beta, double oracle) that require evaluations or LPs and can be costly.
- Quality: proof strategy is sound and well-motivated; induction and bounded-error treatment are careful; guarantees are asymptotic and may be loose (no finite-time rates).
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and organized, but contains notational inconsistencies (i, j vs. aij), minor terminology slips, and pseudocode mismatches that should be fixed.
- Originality: first general convergence guarantee for MCTS in perfect-information simultaneous-move games; applicable to standard ε-HC learners like RM and Exp3.
- Significance: valuable for large-scale or knowledge-poor domains where MCTS is preferred, but impact is tempered by existence of exact polynomial-time methods and depth-dependent bounds.
- Question: can O(√T) regret of RM/Exp3 be translated into explicit finite-time exploitability bounds at the root as a function of T, depth D, and exploration γ?
- Question: is the depth dependence (O(D) per level, O(D^2) overall) tight, and could variance-reduction or weighted backups improve it?
- Question: proof requires mean backpropagation, but experiments show sample backpropagation behaves similarly—are there selection rules or averaging schemes where sample backpropagation still converges?
- Question: can the bias floor from fixed exploration γ be quantified in the asymptotic bound, and could adaptive/decaying exploration preserve guarantees while lowering exploitability?
- Question: do parts of the analysis extend to non–zero-sum or imperfect-information settings (e.g., via regret minimization in information sets)?
- Pros: first general asymptotic convergence proof for this class, broadly applicable template using standard no-regret learners, subgame-perfect guarantee, solid empirical support.
- Cons: no finite-time convergence rates; theoretical requirement of mean backpropagation differs from common practice; notational/presentation issues; limited to perfect-information zero-sum settings where exact methods exist.
- Minor comments/typos: “leafs”→“leaves”; line 144 “negated” not “inverted”; clarify algorithm notation for selected joint action ((i,j) vs (a1,a2)); standardize notation and clarify RetVal behavior and Exp3 action selection/normalization.
- Overall recommendation: accept, but adding coarse finite-time bounds or a clearer discussion of depth dependence would materially strengthen the contribution.
