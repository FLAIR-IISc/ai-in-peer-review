This paper studies Monte Carlo tree search in zero-sum games with perfect information and simultaneous moves. It is proven that the MCTS algorithm converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium under certain conditions. Empirical studies confirm the formal result.

Detailed comments are as follows:

1. Overall, I think this is a good paper that provides a sufficient condition for the convergence of the MCTS algorithm. The result is useful, and the presentation is clear. However, since the main contribution of the paper should be the theoretical part, I am concerned about the novelty of the proof technique.
2. UCB algorithms are widely used in the literature, but they are not analyzed in this paper. Given that some analyses have been done on UCBs for trees, it would be better if the authors also considered UCB algorithms and compared them with other algorithms in the paper.
3. It has only been proven that the spread of the mean is good convergence. However, in experiments, the spread of the current sample value also seems good under various criteria. Can you provide some explanations?
