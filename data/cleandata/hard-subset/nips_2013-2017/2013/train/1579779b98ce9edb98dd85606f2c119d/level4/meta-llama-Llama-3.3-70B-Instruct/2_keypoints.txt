- Paper studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., goofspiel, Tron) using MCTS with regret-matching.
- Main theoretical result: MCTS + regret-matching converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium for this game class.
- Manuscript is well-organized, clearly written, and presents results effectively.
- Primary contribution is a novel formal analysis building on prior work (e.g., Lanctot et al., 2013).
- Proof technique is inductive and depends on optimal strategies at leaf nodes of the game tree.
- Major limitation: analysis yields weak/unremarkable conclusions for inner nodes and misses concepts like subgame perfect Nash equilibria.
- Suggested improvement: integrate analysis with subgame perfect equilibrium concepts and related literature.
- Relevant recommended reference: C. Harris (1990) on existence of subgame perfect equilibria in simultaneous-move games and analyses of regret-matching with MCTS.
- Overall: computationally interesting but would be stronger by connecting results to established game-theoretic frameworks.
