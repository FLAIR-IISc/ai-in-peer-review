- Central result: SM-MCTS with any Hannan-consistent selection policy and guaranteed exploration converges almost surely to a subgame-perfect ε-Nash equilibrium when mean returns are backpropagated; error scales with game depth.
- Proof approach: reduce nodes to repeated matrix games with HC learners, extend to noisy payoff estimates from exploration/sampling, and inductively compose error bounds across depth.
- Key assumption: backpropagation of mean returns (not single samples) and guaranteed exploration are crucial for the theoretical guarantees.
- Relation to prior work: contrasts with exact but heavy backward-induction/alpha–beta methods and with negative UCT results for simultaneous moves.
- Links to online learning: builds on Hannan consistency, no-regret convergence to NE, and algorithms like regret matching and Exp3.
- Novelty: first rigorous convergence analysis of SM-MCTS to subgame-perfect approximate NE for a broad class of HC selection rules.
- Technical soundness: assumptions clearly stated; modular lemmas and induction lead to Theorem 4.11 asserting almost-sure convergence with O(D^2) error dependence.
- Empirical validation: experiments compare mean vs sample backpropagation and validate asymptotic behavior on random games and depth-2 worst cases.
- Empirical finding: regret matching and Exp3 variants show comparable convergence; mean-propagation is slightly slower in practice.
- Clarity: paper is generally well organized; algorithm template and updates are precise.
- Exposition suggestions: emphasize RetVal (mean vs sample) earlier and summarize assumptions immediately before the main theorem.
- Theoretical limitation: results are asymptotic with potentially loose O(D^2) depth dependence and no finite-time/sample-complexity bounds.
- Practical gap: theoretical variant requires mean backpropagation and fixed exploration, differing from standard MCTS practice that backpropagates single samples.
- Exploration issue: guaranteed exploration inflates effective regret and sets a floor on exploitability; analysis of decaying exploration schedules is missing.
- Empirical scope limitation: experiments are small-scale (random games, depth-2 worst cases); larger structured benchmarks would strengthen practical claims.
- Suggested extensions: provide theory or counterexamples for sample-return backpropagation, finite-time rates, and analysis of exploration schedules.
- Strengths summary: first rigorous guarantee for SM-MCTS, clean reduction to repeated noisy games, modular proof structure, and supportive experiments.
- Acceptance pros: novel, nontrivial subgame-perfect convergence result of interest to MCTS and game-theory communities.
- Acceptance cons: gap between theory and standard practice, lack of finite-time bounds, and limited empirical scale.
- Overall recommendation: lean accept.
