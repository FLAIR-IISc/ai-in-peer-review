- Main theoretical contribution: proof that MCTS with any ε‑Hannan‑consistent selection method (with mild restrictions) converges to an ε‑Nash equilibrium in simultaneous‑move extensive‑form games.
- Provides a comprehensive, well‑written analysis and a general MCTS template that can be instantiated by various selection methods.
- Formal proof: convergence holds if the selection method is ε‑Hannan consistent in matrix games and meets additional exploration requirements.
- Clear presentation of necessary game‑theoretic background (Nash equilibrium, Hannan consistency, regret matching).
- Empirical evaluation using regret matching and Exp3 on random and worst‑case games supports the theoretical claims.
- Additional MCTS variants are empirically shown to converge to approximate Nash equilibria on evaluated games.
- Key weakness: convergence is asymptotic with no rates provided, limiting practical significance.
- Reviewer recommends investigating convergence rates by assuming specific regret rates for selection methods.
- Experiments comparing mean reward propagation vs. current sample propagation find mean values slightly underperform experimentally.
- Authors attempt to empirically estimate convergence behavior, giving a useful starting point for future work.
- Overall judgment: technically correct and valuable, but somewhat incremental given existing MCTS convergence results for sequential moves.
- Nitpicks: typos and inconsistent notation (Figure 1 "leafs"→"leaves"; "negated" vs "inverted"; inconsistent use of (i,j) vs (σ1,σ2); notation switch from (i,j) to a's).
- Pro‑accept: comprehensive analysis, general template, and supporting experiments.
- Con‑accept: asymptotic results/no rates, questions about practical impact, and incremental contribution relative to sequential‑move results.
