- Main contribution: proves asymptotic convergence of MCTS with any no-regret selection method (under mild restrictions) to an ε‑Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move games.

- Major limitation: convergence is only asymptotic with no convergence rates provided, due to assuming only ε‑Hannan consistency for the selection method.

- Importance of rates: need to investigate whether convergence rates follow when a specific regret rate is assumed (e.g., √T for exponential weights), since backward induction already gives exact equilibria.

- Experiments: compare mean-reward propagation (used in analysis) vs. sample-value propagation; mean propagation slightly underperforms empirically and authors attempt to estimate empirical convergence rates.

- Overall assessment: well-written and technically sound, but ultimate significance is mildly limited by lack of rates and being somewhat incremental given existing MCTS convergence results for sequential moves.

- Nitpicks/editing: several typos and notation issues—Figure 1 “leafs”→“leaves”; Line 144 wording (“negated”); Algorithm 1 line 10 indexing (a₁,a₂→(i,j) or (σ₁,σ₂)); persistent (i,j)/a’s notation inconsistency around Line 172.
