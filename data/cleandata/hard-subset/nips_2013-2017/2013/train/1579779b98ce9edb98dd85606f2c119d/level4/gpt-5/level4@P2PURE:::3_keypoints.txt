- Main contribution: analysis of MCTS variants that can use any no-regret selection method and proof of asymptotic convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move games.
- Major limitation: convergence guarantee is purely asymptotic with no convergence rates provided.
- Reason for limitation: seems unavoidable under the paper’s assumptions since the selection method is only required to be ε-Hannan consistent.
- Open question: characterize convergence rates when the selection method has known regret rates (e.g., O(√T) for exponential-weights).
- Experiments: compare mean-reward propagation (as in analysis) to standard most-recent-sample propagation.
- Empirical result: mean-based propagation performs slightly worse than the standard approach.
- Authors also attempt to empirically infer convergence rates.
- Overall assessment: clear and technically sound work but mild concern about ultimate significance due to absence of rates and incremental contribution relative to sequential-move MCTS results.
- Nitpicks: typographical and notation issues—“leafs”→“leaves”; “negated” vs “inverted”; Algorithm 1 variable labeling ((a1,a2) vs (i,j)/(σ1,σ2)); inconsistent use of (i,j) vs a’s.
