- Paper studies MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games and proves conditions for convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium, supported by empirical validation.

- Strength: Identifies a sufficient condition for MCTS convergence in this game class, addressing a gap in analysis for simultaneous-move games.

- Strength: Theoretical results are clearly articulated and the empirical evaluation is well-designed and aligned with the formal claims.

- Concern: Proof technique appears to reuse established methods (e.g., martingale arguments) without sufficiently distinguishing novelty from prior MCTS convergence work.

- Concern: The paper omits analysis of UCB/UCT-style algorithms, which are widely used and theoretically important, leaving a significant comparative gap.

- Concern: Experiments show good performance for sample-value propagation despite theory proving convergence only for mean propagation; this discrepancy is unexplained.

- Overall: Competent and well-presented work but technically incremental; solid foundation yet not sufficiently innovative for a major contribution.

- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on revisions to (i) clarify proof novelty vs. prior work, (ii) include or justify omission of UCB analysis, and (iii) explain the empirical behavior of sample-value propagation.
