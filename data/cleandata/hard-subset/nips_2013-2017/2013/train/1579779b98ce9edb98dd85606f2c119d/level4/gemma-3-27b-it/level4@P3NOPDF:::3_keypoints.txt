- Proves convergence of MCTS using any no-regret selection method to an ε-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move extensive-form games — primary theoretical contribution.  
- Notable because there were no prior convergence guarantees for MCTS in this setting.  
- Main strength is generality: analysis applies to a broad class of no-regret learning algorithms.  
- Technical presentation is clear and the proof appears sound.  
- Major limitation: the convergence result is asymptotic with no convergence rates provided, limiting practical significance.  
- Reviewer suggests analyzing convergence rates under specific regret bounds (e.g., Exp3’s O(√T)) and comparing convergence speed to backward induction cost.  
- Experiments compare propagating mean rewards vs current sample values; mean propagation slightly underperforms in practice.  
- Experimental attempt to estimate convergence rate is appreciated but inconclusive; more conclusive evidence needed.  
- Overall: solid, well-written theoretical contribution but impact is muted without concrete convergence speeds and feels somewhat incremental relative to sequential-move MCTS results.  
- Minor fixes: change “leafs”→“leaves”; use “negated” instead of “inverted”; clarify and consistently use notation (avoid confusing (a1,a2) with (i,j) or (σ1,σ2)).
