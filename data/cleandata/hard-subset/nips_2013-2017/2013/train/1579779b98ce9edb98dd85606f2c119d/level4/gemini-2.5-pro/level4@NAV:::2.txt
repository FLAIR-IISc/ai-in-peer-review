This work investigates the class of zero-sum, perfect-information games featuring simultaneous moves, a domain that includes notable examples such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors analyze a Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) approach integrated with regret-matching and formally prove its convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium for this game class.

The manuscript is clear, well-written, and its findings are presented effectively. The main contribution lies in its novel formal analysis, which extends previous work like Lanctot et al. [20] that offered a more preliminary examination of this setting. The proof is structured inductively, beginning with the assumption of optimal strategies at the leaf nodes of the game tree.

The primary weakness of this methodology is its limited ability to provide strong guarantees or insights regarding the behavior at the inner nodes of the game tree. This overlooks a fundamental concept from game theory: Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibria (SPNE). The paper's impact could be significantly enhanced by connecting its analysis to these concepts. A relevant citation would be:
"Existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves". C. Harris 1990.

In summary, this paper presents a novel analysis of regret-matching within MCTS for zero-sum, perfect-information, simultaneous-move games. While compelling from a computational perspective, the analysis misses an opportunity to engage with the powerful and well-studied game-theoretic framework of subgame perfect Nash equilibria.
