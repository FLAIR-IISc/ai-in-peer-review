- Paper studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Uses Monte Carlo Tree Search with regret-matching to analyze this game class.  
- Establishes convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Manuscript is clearly written and well-structured.  
- Main contribution is novel formal analysis, more rigorous than prior work (Lanctot et al., 2013).  
- Proof uses an inductive approach leveraging optimal strategies at terminal nodes.  
- Major limitation: analysis does not provide guarantees for inner game-tree nodes, neglecting subgame perfect Nash equilibria.  
- Recommendation: integrate results with the subgame perfect Nash equilibrium framework and cite Harris (1990).  
- Computational insights are valuable but would be strengthened by connecting to subgame perfect NE theory.
