- Studies two-player zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron).  
- Analyzes MCTS instantiated with regret matching and proves convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Manuscript is clearly written and results are well presented.  
- Primary contribution is new formal analysis; builds on but extends prior preliminary work (Lanctot et al., 2013).  
- Proof strategy is inductive, starting from optimal behavior at leaf nodes.  
- Main weakness: analysis says little about interior nodes of the game tree.  
- Lacks connection to game-theoretic concepts like subgame-perfect Nash equilibria; recommend citing Harris (1990).  
- Overall: compelling computational contribution but insufficiently tied to established subgame-perfect equilibrium theory.
