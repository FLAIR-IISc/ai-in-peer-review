- Paper analyzes a class of MCTS algorithms that can use any no-regret selection method under mild restrictions.  
- Main theoretical result: proof of asymptotic convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move games.  
- Key limitation: convergence is only asymptotic—no explicit convergence rates are provided.  
- Cause of limitation: selection method is assumed only ε-Hannan consistent, so the framework does not yield rates.  
- Practical concern: absence of rates weakens practical implications; backward induction yields exact solutions, so rate comparisons matter.  
- Reviewer asks whether specific regret bounds (e.g., √T from exponential weights) could imply concrete convergence rates.  
- Experiments compare mean reward propagation (used in analysis) to propagating current sample values and find mean propagation slightly worse.  
- Experiments also attempt an empirical exploration of convergence rates.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and technically sound but significance is somewhat limited by the lack of rate analysis and feels incremental given existing sequential-move MCTS results.  
- Nitpicks: wording/notation issues — "leafs"→"leaves"; possibly "inverted"→"negated"; Algorithm 1 uses (a1,a2) but should be (i,j) or (σ1,σ2); notational inconsistencies between (i,j) and a's throughout.
