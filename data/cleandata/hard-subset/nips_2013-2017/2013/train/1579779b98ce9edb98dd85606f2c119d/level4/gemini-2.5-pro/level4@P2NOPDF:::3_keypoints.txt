- Proves asymptotic convergence of a class of MCTS algorithms (with Hannan consistent node selection) to an epsilon-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move extensive-form games.  
- Framework is general and appears to be the first to give convergence guarantees for MCTS in this game setting.  
- Major limitation: convergence is purely asymptotic with no rates provided due to only assuming Hannan consistency.  
- Practical utility unclear because without rates it’s hard to compare to exact methods like backward induction or quantify sampling advantages in large games.  
- Key open question: what convergence rates follow if a specific online learning algorithm with known regret bounds (e.g., EXP3 with O(sqrt(T)) regret) is used for node selection?  
- Experiments compare mean-reward propagation (used in theory) versus standard single-playout value propagation; mean propagation slightly underperforms in practice.  
- Empirical investigation of convergence rates provides initial insights but does not replace theoretical rates.  
- Overall: well-written and technically sound, fills a gap, but impact is tempered by lack of explicit rates and feels somewhat incremental relative to sequential-move results.  
- Recommendation: strengthen by deriving explicit convergence rates using specific regret bounds.
