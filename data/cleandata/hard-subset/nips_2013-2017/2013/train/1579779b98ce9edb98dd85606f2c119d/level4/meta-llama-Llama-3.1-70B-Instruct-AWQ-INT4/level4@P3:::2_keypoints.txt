- Presents a formal analysis and general template for MCTS in zero-sum extensive-form games with perfect information and simultaneous moves.  
- Proves that MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium if the selection method is -Hannan consistent and meets exploration requirements.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with detailed explanation of selection and update policies.  
- Use of regret-matching as a selection strategy is highlighted and well-explained.  
- Formal guarantee of convergence to an approximate NE is a significant theoretical contribution beyond prior empirical work.  
- Empirical evaluation on random and worst-case games supports the theoretical convergence results.  
- Main weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of performance with respect to subgame perfect Nash equilibria, despite mentioning it.  
- Recommendation: accept, but request additional analysis of subgame perfect NE behavior.  
- Arguments for acceptance: formal convergence guarantee, thorough algorithmic analysis, solid empirical evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of detailed subgame perfect NE analysis.  
- Reviewer score: 8/10, confidence 8/10.
