- Main contribution: Proof that MCTS with any (mildly restricted) no-regret selection method converges asymptotically to ε‑Nash equilibria in simultaneous-move games.  
- Key limitation: Convergence is asymptotic with no rates provided, weakening practical/significance claims.  
- Cause of limitation: Analysis assumes only ε‑Hannan consistency, which inherently prevents deriving convergence rates.  
- Requested clarification: Authors should state whether specific regret rates (e.g., √T from exponential weights) yield concrete convergence rates for their method.  
- Experimental comparison: Propagating mean reward values (used in theory) slightly underperforms standard sample-value propagation in practice.  
- Empirical convergence analysis: Authors attempt to infer rates, but the experimental results are preliminary and not conclusive.  
- Significance assessment: Technically solid and well-written, but the absence of rates makes the contribution feel incremental given existing sequential‑move MCTS guarantees.  
- Recommendation for editing: Fix wording and notation (Figure 1 "leafs"→"leaves"; Line 144 "negated" not "inverted"; Algorithm 1 Line 10 use clearer indices; resolve (i,j) vs. a's inconsistencies).
