- Paper analyzes MCTS variants that can use any no-regret selection method and proves asymptotic convergence to an ε-Nash equilibrium in simultaneous-move games.  
- Main theoretical limitation: guarantee is purely asymptotic with no convergence rates, since selection is assumed only ε-Hannan consistent.  
- Suggestion: derive explicit convergence rates when the selection method has known regret bounds (e.g., O(√T) for Exp3) and show how selection regret maps to overall convergence rate.  
- Experimental comparison: mean reward propagation (used in analysis) vs. standard most-recent-sample propagation; mean values perform slightly worse in practice.  
- Authors also attempt empirical investigation of convergence rates.  
- Overall verdict: well-written and technically sound, but impact is limited by absence of rate guarantees; contribution feels incremental given existing MCTS convergence results for sequential moves.  
- Nitpick: typo in Figure 1 (“leafs” → “leaves”).  
- Nitpick: wording at Line 144—use “negated” rather than “inverted.”  
- Notation issues: Algorithm 1 line 10 likely should use (i,j) or (σ1,σ2) instead of (a1,a2); inconsistent switching between (i,j) and a’s throughout.  
- Reviewer enjoyed the paper but retains reservations about its significance without convergence rates.
