- Paper studies zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games (e.g., Goofspiel, Tron) and analyzes MCTS with regret-matching, proving convergence to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Main contribution is a new formal convergence analysis; earlier work (Lanctot et al.) provided more preliminary results.  
- Proof uses an inductive approach starting from optimal strategies at leaf nodes.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and presents results comprehensibly.  
- Technical contribution appears rigorous and builds on online learning and game-theory concepts.  
- Experimental results support the theoretical findings and show practical applicability.  
- Potential weakness: the inductive approach may not provide deep insights about inner nodes of the game tree.  
- Missing explicit connection to subgame perfect Nash equilibria; should cite and discuss Harris (1990).  
- Scope is limited to zero-sum perfect-information games, possibly reducing broader impact.  
- No finite-time convergence bounds are provided, leaving practical efficiency questions unanswered.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution and recommend acceptance if authors address the subgame-perfection connection.
