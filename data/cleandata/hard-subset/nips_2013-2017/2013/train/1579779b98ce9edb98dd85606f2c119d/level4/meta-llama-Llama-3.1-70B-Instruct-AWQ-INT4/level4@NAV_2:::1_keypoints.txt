- Paper proves MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous-move games and gives a sufficient convergence condition.  
- Presentation is clear and empirical results confirm the theoretical findings.  
- Main contribution is theoretical, but novelty of the proof technique is unclear and a concern.  
- UCB algorithms, widely used and analyzed elsewhere, are not considered or compared in the paper.  
- Theoretical proof covers propagation of the mean, but the paper lacks explanation for why propagation of current sample values also performs well empirically.  
- Overall technical contribution is interesting but not outstanding.  
- Strength: useful convergence condition and clear presentation.  
- Weakness: unclear proof novelty, missing UCB analysis, and missing explanation for sample-value propagation behavior.  
- Reviewer leans toward rejecting due to novelty and UCB analysis concerns but may accept if authors address these issues.  
- Minor suggestions: clarify and highlight proof novelty, add UCB comparisons, explain sample-value propagation empirically, and update some references.
