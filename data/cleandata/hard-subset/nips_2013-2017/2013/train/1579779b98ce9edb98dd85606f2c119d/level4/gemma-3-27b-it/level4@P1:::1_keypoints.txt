- Paper proves MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium in zero-sum, perfect-information games with simultaneous moves under certain conditions, supported by experiments.  
- Main contribution is a sufficient theoretical convergence condition; presentation is clear and well-motivated.  
- Reviewer questions novelty of the proof technique and asks for stronger positioning relative to existing convergence analyses.  
- Suggests comparing and discussing UCB-based MCTS methods and clarifying why UCB is not covered by the theoretical framework.  
- Notes the theoretical benefit of propagating mean values, but empirical results show sample-value propagation also performs well and warrant explanation.  
- Recommends investigating whether convergence rates differ between mean and sample propagation or depend on game structure.  
- Asks for more specific citations to related convergence analyses of tree search algorithms beyond game-playing.  
- Requests discussion of practical implications and limitations, e.g., sensitivity of convergence to the Hannan-consistency parameter ε.  
- Wants identification of game types that pose the greatest challenges for the proposed approach.  
- Arguments for acceptance: formal convergence guarantee in a challenging setting, empirical validation, clear writing, and relevant connections to prior work.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear novelty of proof technique, omission of widely used algorithms like UCB, and limited empirical depth.  
- Overall assessment: a worthwhile, solid contribution (not groundbreaking); reviewer leans toward acceptance if concerns about proof novelty and UCB omission are addressed.
