- Provides a thorough convergence analysis of MCTS in zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games.  
- Introduces a general MCTS algorithm template that can be instantiated by various selection methods.  
- Proves that if a selection method is Hannan-consistent in matrix games and meets exploration requirements, MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly structured, making arguments easy to follow.  
- Empirical evaluations are provided and support the theoretical claims.  
- Effectively ties MCTS analysis to game-theoretic concepts like Nash equilibrium and subgame perfection.  
- Main limitation: analysis is restricted to leaf nodes of the game tree.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of implications for subgame perfect Nash equilibria and broader limitations.  
- Originality: novel analysis of regret-matching strategies combined with MCTS in these games.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address limitations and expand discussion.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: thorough analysis, novel contribution, clear presentation; Rejection arguments: leaf-node limitation and insufficient discussion of implications.
