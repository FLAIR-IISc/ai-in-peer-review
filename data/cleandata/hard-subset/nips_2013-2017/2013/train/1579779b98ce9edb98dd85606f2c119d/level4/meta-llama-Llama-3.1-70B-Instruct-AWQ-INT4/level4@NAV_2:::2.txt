This paper provides a thorough analysis of the class of zero-sum perfect-information simultaneous move games, including interesting games such as Goofspiel and Tron. The authors present a general template of Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) algorithms for these games, which can be instantiated using any regret minimizing procedure for matrix games as a function for selecting the next actions to be sampled. They formally prove that if the algorithm uses an -Hannan consistent selection function, which assures attempting each action infinitely many times, the MCTS algorithm eventually converges to a subgame perfect -Nash equilibrium of the extensive form game.

The paper is well-written, and the results are well-presented. The main contribution is new results in the formal analysis, building upon previous work by Lanctot et al. (2013). The proof is inductive, starting with optimal strategies at the leaf nodes, and the authors provide a clear and detailed explanation of the proof.

The paper also includes an empirical evaluation of the influence of propagating the mean values instead of the current sample value in MCTS to the speed of convergence to Nash equilibrium. The results show that both methods converge to the approximate Nash equilibrium of the game, with the mean propagation method converging slightly slower in all cases.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not fully explore the connection between the analysis and the concept of subgame perfect Nash equilibria, which is a well-studied topic in game theory. The authors could strengthen the paper by tying their analysis to this concept, as suggested by the work of Harris (1990) on the existence of subgame perfect equilibria in games with simultaneous moves.

Overall, the paper presents novel analyses of regret-matching strategy with MCTS in zero-sum perfect information simultaneous move games, and it provides a valuable contribution to the field of game theory and artificial intelligence.

Strengths:
- Well-written and clear presentation of results
- New results in formal analysis of MCTS algorithms
- Empirical evaluation of the influence of mean propagation on convergence speed

Weaknesses:
- Limited connection to subgame perfect Nash equilibria concept
- Potential for further exploration of the relationship between the analysis and game theory concepts

Recommendations:
- Tie the analysis to the concept of subgame perfect Nash equilibria
- Explore the relationship between the analysis and game theory concepts further
