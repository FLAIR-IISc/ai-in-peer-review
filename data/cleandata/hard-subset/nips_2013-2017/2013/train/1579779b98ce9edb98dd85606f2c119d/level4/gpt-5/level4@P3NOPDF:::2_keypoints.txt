- Paper analyzes zero-sum, perfect-information simultaneous-move games and proves regret-matching MCTS converges to an approximate Nash equilibrium via depth induction assuming optimal leaves.  
- Clear writing and accessible technical presentation.  
- Novelty: provides formal convergence guarantees for regret-matching MCTS beyond prior preliminary analyses (e.g., Lanctot et al., 2013).  
- Relevant to computational game theory and reinforcement learning communities.  
- Primary concern: analysis shows ε-NE at the root but does not establish subgame-perfect equilibrium (ε-SPE) properties at interior nodes.  
- Coordination and credibility issues in simultaneous-move subgames are not addressed; root convergence may not imply off-path consistency.  
- Suggest explicitly defining the equilibrium concept achieved at each history (ε-SPE vs ε-NE) and characterizing any gap.  
- Suggest relating the inductive proof to subgame analysis and whether ε-optimal leaves can propagate SPE-like guarantees.  
- Suggest discussing appropriate equilibrium refinements (e.g., trembling-hand, subgame-perfect correlated equilibrium) and compatibility with regret matching.  
- Suggest deeper comparison to Lanctot et al. (2013): differing assumptions, regret decomposition, and insights specific to simultaneous moves.  
- Recommend citing game-theoretic literature on simultaneous-move subgames, notably Harris (1990).  
- Request details on convergence assumptions: exploration strategies, tie-breaking, learning-rate schedules, and whether bounds are asymptotic or finite-time.  
- Request precise definition of “approximate” NE (exploitability metric or max deviation across histories) and whether results are root-based.  
- Ask for explicit dependence of error/regret on depth and branching factor, including any depth-dependent constants or mixing-time effects.  
- Clarify how guarantees extend when leaves are solved approximately rather than optimally.  
- Clarify whether regret is computed independently at each simultaneous-move node and how consistency across the tree is ensured (global profile vs sampled paths).  
- If experiments exist, report exploitability over time in Goofspiel and Tron, not only win rates.  
- Minor: tighten formal model definitions for histories, simultaneous action sets, and information structure.  
- Minor: provide a brief proof roadmap highlighting the induction and role of leaf optimality.  
- Minor: clarify equilibrium selection when multiple equilibria exist and how exploration/tie-breaking affects subgame consistency.  
- Overall: solid and useful theoretical contribution, but would be strengthened by connecting convergence results to subgame-perfect reasoning and clarifying assumptions and error metrics.
