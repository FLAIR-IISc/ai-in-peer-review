- Reviewer notes that the inequality |R_k| <= κ |X_k - X_{k+1}| was already proved in Suvrit Sra, NIPS 2012 (Lemma 4).  
- Paper studies proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization, extending linear convergence beyond strong convexity.  
- Authors follow Luo and Tseng’s framework and prove a local error bound enabling asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient.  
- Key technical novelty: an observation allowing simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the problem to the vector case.  
- Technical soundness: paper appears correct overall.  
- Identified gap: appendix line 085 — need justification why chosen κ3 (e.g., α < 1) implies R(X^k) <= κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Clarity: paper is readable but should state main claims up front and defer the technical local error bound proof, then present the linear-rate derivation.  
- Originality: moderate — simultaneous diagonalization is new/interesting, but most of the approach follows existing work.  
- Significance: mainly theoretical with limited algorithmic impact; asymptotic nature weakens practical significance.  
- Overall assessment: incremental contribution, moderately interesting theoretically.
