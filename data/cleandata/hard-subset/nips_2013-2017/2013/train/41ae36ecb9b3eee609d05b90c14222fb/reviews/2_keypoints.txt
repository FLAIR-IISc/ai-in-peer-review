- Proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss.  
- Result addresses an important problem for ML and signal processing applications.  
- Paper is carefully and clearly written and the main proof appears correct.  
- Builds on Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique [20]; extends an open question about applying that technique to the nuclear (trace) norm.  
- Reviewer requests a clear comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case (e.g., [20,22]) and discussion whether/why l1 techniques do not directly translate to trace norm.  
- Note that the l1-norm result should follow as a corollary of the trace-norm result; discuss perceived novelty vs. expected extension.  
- Suggest changing the title to avoid the word “structured,” since the main new contribution concerns the loss side (linear operator A) rather than the regularizer.  
- Minor issues: clarify whether P is a sampling or mask operator (sampling implies randomness but P is fixed).  
- Minor issues: broken reference at line 337.  
- Minor issues: briefly recall meanings of Q- and R- linear convergence for reader clarity.  
- Minor issues: relate residual R(X) defined in (6) to Tseng’s original definition (46) for interpretation.  
- Suggest adding more discussion/conclusions to justify the technical Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
