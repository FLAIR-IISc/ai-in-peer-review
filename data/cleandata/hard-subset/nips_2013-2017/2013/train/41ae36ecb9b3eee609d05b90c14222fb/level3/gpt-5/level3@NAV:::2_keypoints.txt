- Summary: Proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems via a Lipschitzian error bound linking distance to optimal set with a residual, extending Luo–Tseng framework beyond polyhedral regularizers.  
- Relation to prior work: Resolves open question of extending Tseng’s error-bound technique to the nonpolyhedral trace norm using simultaneous SVD and a restricted invertibility argument; covers common losses like square and logistic.  
- Quality: Theoretical development is careful with clear assumptions and a sound proof; asymptotic nature and level-set dependence are acknowledged and experiments support linear tails.  
- Clarity: Generally well written with clear definitions (residual, Q-/R-linear rates), though a few presentation issues remain.  
- Originality: Novel extension of Lipschitzian error bounds to the trace norm, with insightful simultaneous SVD and perturbation analyses.  
- Significance: Important for PGM users in matrix completion and multitask learning where strong convexity fails; may influence other nonpolyhedral matrix regularizer analyses.  
- Request: Add a clear comparison to the ℓ1/vector case, explain why ℓ1 techniques don’t transfer, and show how the ℓ1 error bound follows as a corollary (e.g., x ↦ Diag(x)).  
- Request: Strengthen intuition and discussion in Section 3, and clarify the restricted-invertibility step (Lemma 4.1(c)) including essential properties of A and local spectral structure.  
- Pros: Resolves meaningful open problem under weaker assumptions; largely self-contained and correct analysis; broad applicability with empirical support.  
- Cons: Experiments are limited (no baselines) and some clarifications/references are missing.  
- Minor issue: Clarify whether P in experiments is a fixed mask or random sampling operator.
