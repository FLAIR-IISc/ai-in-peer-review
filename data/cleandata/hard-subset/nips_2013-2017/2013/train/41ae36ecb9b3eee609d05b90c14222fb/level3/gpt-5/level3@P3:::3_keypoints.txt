- Studies proximal gradient for convex f(X)+τ||X||* and proves a local Lipschitz error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η||R(X)||F and asymptotic linear convergence under fixed step sizes.
- Main technical novelty: proposition that any optimum X̄ and −∇f(X̄) admit simultaneous SVDs, allowing matrix perturbation to reduce to the vector case.
- Extends the Luo–Tseng error-bound + linear-rate framework from polyhedral regularizers to the nonpolyhedral trace norm.
- Assumptions on h (smooth, strictly convex, strongly convex on compacts) cover common losses (squared, logistic) and linear A with nontrivial nullspaces.
- Experiments show linear decay near the solution on matrix completion and logistic matrix classification.
- Strength: provides a careful, technically sound local error bound without requiring strong convexity of f.
- Strength: perturbation and structure lemmas are carefully developed and the simultaneous-diagonalization argument is neat and effective.
- Gap: appendix claims ||R(Xk)||F ≤ κ3||Xk − Xk+1||F (eq.11) without full justification for varying step sizes αk; κ3’s dependence on (α, ᾱ, Lf) should be made explicit.
- Attribution: the residual-vs-step inequality is known (S. Sra, NIPS 2012, Lemma 4) and should be cited where (11) is used or derived.
- Limitation: the linear rate is local/asymptotic; the paper does not quantify the neighborhood size ε or provide explicit rate constants ρ, limiting practical guidance.
- Algorithmic impact is limited: no change to algorithm, no improved global rates, and no practical step-size or stopping-rule recommendations.
- Experiments are illustrative but limited: no comparison to accelerated methods (FISTA) or other solvers; missing iteration counts, runtimes, and rank behavior.
- Presentation/clarity: main claims should be foregrounded in the intro with proof outlines and technical lemmas deferred; summary of constants and dependencies would help readability.
- Suggestions: add a lemma or cite Sra (NIPS 2012) to justify (11) for variable αk and make κ3 explicit; clarify η and ε dependencies on ζ, α, ᾱ, Lf, τ, A, h.
- Suggestions: move detailed proofs to an appendix, present the Luo–Tseng linear-rate derivation in the main text once the error bound is stated.
- Suggestions: discuss when the local regime is entered in practice and how to detect it (e.g., via the residual norm), and expand experiments to include FISTA and proximal quasi-Newton comparisons.
- Pros: solid, careful theoretical extension of error-bound theory to trace norm; insightful spectral argument; applicable to common loss functions.
- Cons: key inequality is known and not fully justified, constants and neighborhood are unquantified, and empirical evaluation lacks strong baselines.
- Overall: a moderately original, technically solid incremental theoretical contribution that would be strengthened by fixing the κ3 gap, clarifying constants/scope, and improving presentation and experiments.
