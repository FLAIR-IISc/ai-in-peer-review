- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized convex problems without requiring strong convexity.
- Technical novelty: introduces a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm-regularized problems, central to the convergence proof.
- Theorem 3.1: provides a rigorous, sound proof establishing the paper’s main result.
- Writing and organization: paper is well-written, clear, and includes a thorough literature review.
- Experiments: numerical results are well-designed and demonstrate practical effectiveness of PGM.
- Originality: offers a novel theoretical advance in optimization for trace-norm regularization.
- Significance: result broadens applicability of PGM and could improve efficiency for machine-learning and large-scale problems.
- Weakness — background: lacks sufficient context on trace-norm regularization and its machine-learning applications for non-expert readers.
- Weakness — implications: needs more discussion of practical implications and how linear convergence can be leveraged in applications.
- Weakness — complexity: should provide more details on computational complexity and memory requirements of PGM.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution; minor revisions would make it excellent and broadly interesting.
- Recommendation: accept after addressing background, practical implications, and implementation cost details.
