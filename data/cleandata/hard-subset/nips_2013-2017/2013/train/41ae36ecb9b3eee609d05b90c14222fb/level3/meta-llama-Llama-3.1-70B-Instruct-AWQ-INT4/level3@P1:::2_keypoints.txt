- Paper proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems, a significant contribution to ML and signal processing.
- Result applies to practical problems like matrix completion and matrix classification.
- Paper is well-written and the main proof appears correct.
- Builds on Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique and extends an open question to the nuclear (trace) norm.
- Reviewer asks for an explicit comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case and explanation why l1 techniques don’t directly transfer to trace norm.
- The l1-norm result should follow as a corollary of the trace-norm result; authors should discuss perceived novelty versus expected extension.
- Suggest changing the title to avoid “structured,” since the main new contribution concerns the loss operator A rather than the regularizer.
- Minor clarifications needed: specify whether P is a sampling or mask operator and fix a broken reference.
- Recommend briefly recalling Q- and R-linear convergence definitions for reader clarity.
- Suggest relating the residual R(X) in (6) to Tseng’s original definition for better interpretation.
- Ask for more discussion/conclusions to justify the technical Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-organized, original, and significant with broad applicability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the issues above.
- Rating: 8/10.
