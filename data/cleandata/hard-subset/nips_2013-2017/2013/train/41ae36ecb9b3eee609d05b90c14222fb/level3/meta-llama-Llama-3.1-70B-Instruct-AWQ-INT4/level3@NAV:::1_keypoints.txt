- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for a class of trace norm-regularized problems covering a large class of loss functions.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is well-written, clearly organized with a thorough introduction and explanation of results.  
- Technical novelty: Non-trivial technical extension driven by a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems.  
- Applicability: Linear convergence result applies to many loss functions (e.g., square loss, logistic loss), making it broadly relevant to ML.  
- Empirical validation: Limited — experiments only on matrix completion and matrix classification; needs more extensive empirical evidence.  
- Dependence on regularization: Paper lacks clear explanation of how error bound constants depend on the regularization parameter and guidance on choosing it.  
- Originality: Builds on prior work (e.g., Tseng) but contributes significantly via the new error bound and extension to a wider loss class.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10; Clarity 9/10; Originality 7/10; Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add more experiments and clarify constants and regularization guidance.
