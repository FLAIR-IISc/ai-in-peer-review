- Paper studies proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization and proves asymptotic linear convergence without strong convexity.
- Analysis uses the Luo–Tseng error-bound framework: prove a local Lipschitzian error bound linking distance to solution set and proximal residual, then derive linear convergence for fixed stepsizes < 1/L.
- Main technical novelty: an observation that an optimum allows simultaneous diagonalization of X̄ and −∇f(X̄), reducing arguments to the vector case and enabling a nonpolyhedral error bound for the nuclear norm.
- Connects to prior error-bound and linear-rate results for polyhedral regularizers (ℓ1, group LASSO) and to works by Sra (NIPS 2012), Tseng, Zhang–Jiang–Luo.
- Quality: analysis is broadly correct and carefully executed; the simultaneous diagonalization is elegant and useful.
- Major gap: appendix uses a constant κ3 to claim ‖R(Xk)‖F ≤ κ3‖Xk − Xk+1‖F without rigorous justification or explicit dependence on stepsize αk.
- Fix suggestion: provide a short derivation using firm nonexpansiveness of prox and the descent lemma or cite an existing result (e.g., Sra 2012 Lemma 4) and align notation (residual τ vs algorithm αkτ).
- Clarity suggestions: state main claims early (error bound then linear rate), move detailed error-bound proof to appendix, present linear-rate derivation in main text.
- Notation issue: unify prox notation with and without stepsizes to avoid confusion.
- Originality: moderate — simultaneous diagonalization is new for nuclear-norm problems, but the convergence framework largely follows established work.
- Significance: mainly theoretical; asymptotic/local nature limits immediate algorithmic impact.
- Experimental suggestions: add comparisons to accelerated methods and discuss when the local linear regime is entered in practice.
- Pros: resolves a nontrivial error-bound question for trace norm, clean extension of Luo–Tseng, technically sound with a clear novelty.
- Cons: missing justification/citation for residual–step relation, local/asymptotic result with limited practical implications, exposition and notation need streamlining.
- Overall: incremental but solid theoretical contribution; moderately interesting.
