This paper investigates the convergence rate of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for solving trace norm regularized optimization problems, a common task in machine learning applications such as matrix completion and low-rank matrix recovery. The authors demonstrate linear convergence of the PGM without assuming strong convexity of the loss function, extending previous results which typically require this stronger condition. They achieve this by establishing a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the problem, leveraging a key observation that allows for simultaneous diagonalization of relevant matrices, simplifying the analysis. The work builds on the established framework of Luo and Tseng, applying it to the specific context of trace norm regularization.

The paper is well-motivated, highlighting the prevalence of trace norm regularization and the limitations of existing convergence analysis. It correctly positions the contribution within the existing literature, referencing related work on PGM convergence, error bounds, and matrix completion techniques (e.g., [6, 16, 19]). The authors clearly articulate the gap they aim to fill – proving linear convergence without strong convexity – and achieve a significant step in that direction. The inclusion of numerical experiments demonstrating the linear convergence behavior in practice further strengthens the findings.

A primary strength of this work lies in the technical novelty of the simultaneous diagonalization observation (Proposition 4.2). This allows the authors to circumvent the challenges posed by the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm and effectively analyze the spectral structure of points near the optimal solution. This approach is a clever and substantial contribution to overcoming a known obstacle in the field. The paper is generally technically sound, with a clear progression of lemmas and theorems leading to the main results.

However, there are some areas for improvement. While the paper is readable, the structure could be enhanced. Presenting the main theoretical contributions – the linear convergence result and its implications – upfront, followed by the detailed technical development of the local error bound, might improve clarity for the reader. The current structure buries the key result somewhat within the detailed proofs. Furthermore, the paper would benefit from explicitly acknowledging the related work of Suvrit Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4) which already establishes the inequality |R_k| <= κ |X_k - X_{k+1}|.

The originality of the work is moderate. While the simultaneous diagonalization technique is a novel contribution, much of the overall framework relies on existing results. The incremental nature of the contribution impacts the perceived significance of the work. The authors correctly acknowledge that the result is primarily theoretical and that the asymptotic nature of the convergence might limit its practical impact.  A more detailed discussion of potential practical implications, even if limited, would be valuable. A further point for clarification in the appendix (line 85) is the justification for the claim that choosing α < 1 implies R(X^k) <= κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F. Providing a more explicit explanation here would enhance the rigor of the proof.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Establishes linear convergence of PGM for trace norm regularization without strong convexity, expanding the applicability of the method.
*   Presents a novel and clever technical contribution – the simultaneous diagonalization technique – to overcome a key challenge in analyzing trace norm regularization.
*   Sound technical analysis and clear mathematical derivations.
*   Demonstrates empirical validation of the theoretical results.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The originality is moderate; the work builds heavily on existing frameworks.
*   The significance is primarily theoretical, with limited immediate practical impact due to the asymptotic nature of the convergence.
*   The paper's structure could be improved for clarity.
*   The relationship to related work (Sra, NIPS 2012) should be explicitly acknowledged.



**Overall, this paper presents a solid theoretical contribution to the understanding of PGM convergence for trace norm regularization. While not a breakthrough result, it is a worthwhile addition to the literature, particularly for researchers working on optimization theory and low-rank matrix recovery. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the clarity and related work points mentioned above.**
