- Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm regularized problems without assuming strong convexity.
- Establishes a novel Lipschitzian error bound using a simultaneous diagonalization observation to simplify analysis.
- Builds on Luo–Tseng framework and adapts it to trace norm regularization.
- Well-motivated and correctly positioned within related literature on PGM, error bounds, and matrix completion.
- Numerical experiments support the theoretical linear convergence behavior.
- Proposition 4.2 (simultaneous diagonalization) is a technically novel and key contribution that overcomes non-polyhedral epigraph challenges.
- Theoretical development is sound with a clear sequence of lemmas and theorems.
- Paper structure could be improved by presenting main theoretical results upfront before technical proofs.
- Should explicitly acknowledge related work by Suvrit Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4) regarding the inequality |R_k| <= κ |X_k - X_{k+1}|.
- Originality is moderate since much of the framework leverages existing results; contribution is somewhat incremental.
- Practical impact is limited due to the asymptotic nature of the convergence result; significance is primarily theoretical.
- Appendix requires clearer justification for the claim that choosing α < 1 implies R(X^k) <= κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.
- Arguments for acceptance: extends PGM applicability without strong convexity, introduces clever technical tool, sound analysis, and empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: moderate originality, limited practical impact, structural clarity and related-work citation issues.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing clarity and related-work acknowledgment.
