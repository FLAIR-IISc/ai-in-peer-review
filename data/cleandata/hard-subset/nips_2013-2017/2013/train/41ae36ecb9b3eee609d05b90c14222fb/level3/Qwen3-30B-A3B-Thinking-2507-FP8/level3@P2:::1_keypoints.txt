- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems via a new Lipschitzian error bound that removes the need for strong convexity of f.  
- Extends Luo/Tseng error-bound frameworks from ℓ1 and group LASSO to trace norm using matrix perturbation theory.  
- Theorem 3.1 (error bound) is a non-trivial technical contribution potentially useful beyond this paper’s convergence analysis.  
- Proofs are technically sound and numerical experiments (matrix completion, classification) empirically show linear convergence.  
- Main novelty is primarily a technical extension rather than a fundamental conceptual advance.  
- Insufficiently differentiated from Tseng [22]; theorem/lemma structure mirrors prior work without clear explanation of key differences.  
- Empirical validation is limited to standard convergence plots; missing a simulation demonstrating linear convergence when the loss is not strongly convex and discussion of empirical behavior when assumptions fail.  
- Dependence of constants κ1,...,κ4 on step-size parameters (α̲, ᾱ) is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Proof of Theorem 3.1 has a confusing sequence of inequalities before Eq. (13); final step follows from convexity and subgradient condition but intermediate steps are unclear.  
- Paper does not discuss potential extensions of the analysis to ADMM.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions; authors should (1) clearly differentiate from Tseng [22], (2) add non-strongly-convex simulation and discussion, (3) clarify constant dependencies, (4) correct Theorem 3.1 proof, and (5) discuss ADMM extensions.
