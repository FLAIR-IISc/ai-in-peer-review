- Main claim: Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm regularization when f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex on compact sets and Lipschitz.  
- Contribution: Generalizes error-bound based linear convergence results from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm setting.  
- Technical quality: Proofs are dense but generally clear and technically sound.  
- Novelty: Non-trivial extension but largely incremental, building heavily on prior work.  
- Presentation: Well written and includes numerical experiments.  
- Strength — theoretical: Provides a linear convergence guarantee under relatively weak assumptions, justifying PGM in practice.  
- Strength — scope: Fills a literature gap by extending sparse-regularization results to trace-norm problems.  
- Strength — rigor: Uses a clear theoretical foundation (Luo & Tseng framework) to derive results.  
- Strength — empirical: Numerical experiments offer some empirical support for theory.  
- Weakness — novelty: Contribution feels like a technical adaptation rather than a major breakthrough.  
- Weakness — experiments: Empirical validation is limited in scope and doesn’t test robustness across diverse instances.  
- Weakness — theory vs practice: Paper doesn’t discuss cases where theoretical assumptions fail but empirical linear convergence is observed.  
- Weakness — ADMM: Lacks even an outline for extending the analysis to ADMM despite requests.  
- Request — citation: More explicit citation and distinction from Tseng [20], clarifying specific adaptations for the trace-norm case.  
- Request — constants: Clarify how constants κ1–κ4 depend on step-size bounds α̲ and ᾱ for practical implementation.  
- Request — inequality: Fix/clarify the sequence of inequalities before (13); final step follows from convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||_* (p.289 in [20]).  
- Request — simulations: Discuss when theory fails (e.g., decreasing stability) and show behavior near theory’s limits.  
- Request — ADMM outline: Provide a brief outline of how the analysis might extend to ADMM to broaden impact.  
- Acceptance arguments: Technically rigorous, extends known results to a new setting, and has some numerical support.  
- Rejection arguments: Incremental novelty, limited empirical robustness, and missing discussion of limitations/extensions.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address empirical validation, limitations discussion, citation/clarity issues, and constant dependencies.
