- Main contribution: establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for a class of trace norm–regularized problems.
- Technical result: proves a local error bound for trace-norm regularized risk minimization leading to asymptotic linear convergence under a relaxed strong convexity condition.
- Key novelty: a new observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, the paper’s primary original contribution.
- Soundness: proofs are technically sound and follow a well-established framework.
- Gap: a minor unresolved issue in the appendix regarding the choice of a parameter requires clarification.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear but would benefit from reorganizing the presentation of main claims and technical proofs.
- Originality: moderate—most interest centers on the matrix diagonalization observation rather than broad new theory.
- Significance: mainly theoretical with limited direct impact on algorithm design.
- Limitation: the asymptotic nature of the convergence proof reduces practical significance.
- Empirical support: numerical experiments demonstrate linear convergence of PGM in practice.
- Overall: well-written and a meaningful theoretical contribution, but needs fixes to the appendix and improved exposition.
