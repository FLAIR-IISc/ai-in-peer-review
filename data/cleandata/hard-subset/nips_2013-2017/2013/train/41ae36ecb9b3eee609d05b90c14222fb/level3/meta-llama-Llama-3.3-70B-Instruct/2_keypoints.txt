- Establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems.  
- Proves linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of the loss function.  
- Uses a Lipschitzian error bound technique to analyze convergence of first-order methods.  
- Result applies to a large class of loss functions, including non-strongly-convex cases.  
- Well-written and clearly structured presentation of problem, results, and proofs.  
- Novel contribution that advances the state of the art and offers a new perspective via the error bound technique.  
- Potential significant impact for applications like matrix completion and matrix classification.  
- Lacks a clear comparison to the vector l1-norm regularization case.  
- Contains minor issues: notation inconsistencies and broken references that need fixing.  
- Needs more discussion and conclusions to balance the technical emphasis (notably around Lemma 3.2).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address the minor issues and add comparative discussion.
