- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization when f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex on compact sets, extending prior results that require f to be strongly convex.  
- Main technical novelty: establishing a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization using matrix perturbation theory to handle the trace norm’s non-polyhedral epigraph.  
- Builds on and generalizes error-bound based linear convergence results (Tseng [20], Tseng & Yun, Zhang et al.).  
- Proofs are generally clear, well-structured, and grounded in convex optimization theory.  
- Empirical validation is limited: numerical examples are insufficient to demonstrate practical impact across varied settings.  
- Recommend adding simulations where the loss is not strongly convex to show PG linear convergence under different stability levels.  
- Authors must discuss failure conditions—explicitly when the linear convergence guarantee breaks down.  
- Suggest extending discussion/analysis to ADMM, since ADMM is widely used in large-scale problems and would increase relevance.  
- Overlap with Tseng’s results (Theorem 3.1, Lemma 3.2) is notable; authors should explicitly cite and differentiate their contributions.  
- Clarify how constants κ1–κ4 depend on ᾱ and α̲.  
- The sequence of inequalities before equation (13) is confusing; the justification is correct but should be presented more clearly.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions—solid theoretical contribution but needs stronger experiments and expanded discussion of limitations and extensions.
