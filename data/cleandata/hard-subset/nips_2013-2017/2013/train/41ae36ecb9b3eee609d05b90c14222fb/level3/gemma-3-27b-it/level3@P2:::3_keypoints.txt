- Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems without assuming strong convexity.  
- Establishes a novel Lipschitzian error bound combined with Luo and Tseng’s framework to obtain linear convergence.  
- Key technical novelty: an observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices that simplifies analysis.  
- Builds on and correctly positions itself within prior work (Beck & Teboulle, Recht et al., Koltchinskii et al.).  
- Proofs appear technically sound and derivations are logically structured.  
- The simultaneous diagonalization insight is a genuinely interesting and useful technical contribution.  
- Extends linear convergence guarantees to non-strongly convex loss functions, a meaningful theoretical advance.  
- Paper is generally well written with mostly consistent notation and clear explanations.  
- Contribution feels incremental because it follows established frameworks and the results are asymptotic.  
- Presentation buries main results behind technical details; main convergence claim should be stated up front.  
- Appendix contains an unjustified statement (line 85) relating α, κ3, and the residual norm that needs explicit proof.  
- Limited empirical validation: experiments are few and do not thoroughly evaluate practical performance across settings.  
- Overlooked related work: should acknowledge and discuss Sra’s NIPS 2012 result proving a similar inequality.  
- Suggestions: provide the missing justification in the appendix, discuss Sra’s lemma, and reorganize to prioritize main results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, valuable extension of convergence theory, and novel diagonalization technique.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental nature, unclear presentation ordering, and insufficient empirical evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: weak accept contingent on addressing clarity, the appendix justification, and acknowledging related prior work.
