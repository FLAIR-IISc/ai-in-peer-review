- Problem addressed: convergence rate of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized optimization.  
- Main contribution: proof of linear convergence for f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex on compacts and Lipschitz gradient, without requiring f to be strongly convex.  
- Theoretical context: generalizes "error-bound" based convergence analyses previously used for lasso and group lasso.  
- Key technical ingredient: a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace-norm regularized objective (of independent interest).  
- Strength: rigorous, non-trivial, and well-presented theoretical proof establishing linear convergence for important applications (e.g., matrix completion).  
- Weakness: contribution is mainly a technical extension of an existing framework, somewhat limiting perceived originality.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is limited — experiments confirm theory but do not probe boundaries or limitations.  
- Recommendation 1: explicitly differentiate the proofs from prior work (e.g., Tseng [20]) and explain adaptations for trace norm specifics.  
- Recommendation 2: clarify how convergence-rate constants κ1…κ4 depend on step-size bounds α̲ and ᾱ.  
- Recommendation 3: clarify the sequence of inequalities before Eq.(13), noting the connection to convexity and the subgradient condition -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||_*.  
- Recommendation 4: expand empirical/theoretical scope — include experiments where the loss is not strongly convex (semilog plots of objective vs iterations), discuss cases where assumptions fail but linear convergence holds, and comment on extending analysis to other algorithms (e.g., ADMM).
