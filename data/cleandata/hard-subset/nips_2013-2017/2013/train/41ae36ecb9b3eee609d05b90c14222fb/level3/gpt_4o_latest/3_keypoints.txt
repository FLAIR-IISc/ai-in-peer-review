- Shows PGM achieves asymptotic linear convergence for trace norm-regularized problems under relaxed strong convexity.
- Introduces a Lipschitzian error bound framework extending PGM applicability to broader ML loss functions.
- Main technical novelty: simultaneous diagonalization of matrices to reduce matrix proofs to the vector case.
- Theorems 3.1 and 3.2 are clearly stated and supported by detailed proofs.
- Numerical experiments corroborate observed linear convergence in practice.
- Technical soundness is generally strong but appendix lacks clarity on the choice and role of constant κ3, risking reproducibility.
- Paper organization could be improved by presenting main claims earlier and deferring heavy technical details.
- Originality is limited: approach builds substantially on existing Luo–Tseng error bound methodology despite the diagonalization novelty.
- Practical significance is limited: results are asymptotic and immediate impact on algorithm design or applications is unclear.
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical advance, useful diagonalization technique, rigorous proofs and experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution, moderate originality, and clarity/organization issues for non-experts.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify κ3 in the appendix and reorganize presentation for readability.
