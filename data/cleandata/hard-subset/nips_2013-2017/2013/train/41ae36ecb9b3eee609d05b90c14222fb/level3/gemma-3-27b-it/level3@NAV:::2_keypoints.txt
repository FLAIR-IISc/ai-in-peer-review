- Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularization without requiring strong convexity, relevant for matrix completion and similar problems.  
- Addresses an important, widely encountered problem in low-rank recovery, collaborative filtering, and dimensionality reduction.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and makes core ideas accessible.  
- Proof appears correct, extending Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique to the nuclear (trace) norm and addressing an open question.  
- Authors situate the contribution well within existing literature and show strong understanding of related work.  
- Needs a more direct comparison with the vector l1-norm case and clearer explanation why l1 splitting techniques don’t translate to the trace-norm beyond singular-value dependencies.  
- Should state explicitly that the l1-norm result follows as a corollary and discuss the novelty of extending results to the trace norm.  
- Title is imprecise; suggest a title emphasizing linear convergence of PGM for trace-norm regularization with non-strongly convex loss.  
- Clarify operator P (line 337): whether it’s a sampling operator (random) or a fixed mask, and fix broken reference [337].  
- Briefly recall definitions of Q- and R-linear convergence for reader clarity.  
- Explicitly relate the residual R(X) in (6) to Tseng’s original definition (46) to aid interpretation.  
- Expand discussion/conclusions on technical Lemma 3.2 to justify its importance and highlight novel proof aspects.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical result with practical implications, well-written, technically sound, addresses an open problem, strong literature connection.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient l1-norm comparison and minor clarity/precision issues in title and notation.  
- Overall assessment: Strong Accept.
