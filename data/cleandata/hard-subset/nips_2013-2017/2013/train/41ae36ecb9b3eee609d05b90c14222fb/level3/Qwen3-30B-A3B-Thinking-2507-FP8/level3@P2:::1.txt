

**Review**

This paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems min_X {f(X) + τ||X||_*} where f(X) = h(A(X)), A is linear, and h is strongly convex with Lipschitz gradient. The key contribution is a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization, which enables linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of f. The authors extend prior error-bound frameworks developed by Luo and Tseng [10] and Tseng [22] from ℓ1-norm and group LASSO to trace norm, addressing an important gap in the literature.

**Strengths:** The paper provides a technically sound proof of linear convergence for a broader class of loss functions than previously known. The error bound (Theorem 3.1) is a non-trivial contribution that may be of independent interest beyond the specific convergence analysis. The numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification demonstrate linear convergence in practice, supporting the theoretical claims. The paper correctly identifies the limitations of previous approaches for trace norm regularization and provides a novel technique using matrix perturbation theory.

**Weaknesses:** The main contribution appears to be a technical extension of existing work rather than a fundamental conceptual advance. The connection to Tseng's work [22] is insufficiently differentiated, and the structure of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirrors prior results without adequate explanation of key differences. The paper lacks empirical validation beyond standard convergence plots; specifically, it should include a simulation showing linear convergence when the loss is not strongly convex (as requested in authors' feedback) and discuss when theoretical assumptions fail but empirical linear convergence still occurs. The dependence of constants κ1,...,κ4 on step size parameters α̲ and ᾱ is unclear. The proof of Theorem 3.1 contains a confusing sequence of inequalities before equation (13), where the final inequality follows from convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||_* rather than the intermediate steps shown. The paper does not discuss potential extensions to ADMM as requested.

**Pro and Con Arguments for Acceptance:**

**Pro:** The error bound for trace norm regularization is a valuable technical contribution that fills a significant gap in the literature. The linear convergence result is applicable to a wide range of machine learning problems including matrix completion and classification. The paper is well-written with clear organization.

**Con:** The contribution is primarily a technical extension rather than a novel conceptual advance. The insufficient differentiation from prior work weakens the perceived originality. The lack of empirical validation beyond standard convergence plots reduces the paper's significance. The unclear constant dependencies affect practical applicability.

**Recommendation:** I recommend acceptance with major revisions. The paper makes a technically sound contribution to convex optimization for matrix problems, but requires significant improvements to better position itself relative to prior work and provide more comprehensive empirical validation. Specifically, the authors should:

1. Clearly differentiate their error bound from Tseng's work [22], explaining key differences and why existing approaches for ℓ1-norm do not apply to trace norm.
2. Include a simulation demonstrating linear convergence when the loss is not strongly convex, and discuss the conditions under which theoretical assumptions fail but empirical convergence still occurs.
3. Clarify the dependence of constants κ1,...,κ4 on step size parameters α̲ and ᾱ.
4. Correct the proof of Theorem 3.1 to fix the confusing sequence of inequalities.
5. Discuss potential extension of the analysis to ADMM.

These revisions would strengthen the paper's significance by better demonstrating the practical implications of the theoretical contribution and addressing the reviewers' specific concerns. The paper would be significantly more compelling with these improvements.
