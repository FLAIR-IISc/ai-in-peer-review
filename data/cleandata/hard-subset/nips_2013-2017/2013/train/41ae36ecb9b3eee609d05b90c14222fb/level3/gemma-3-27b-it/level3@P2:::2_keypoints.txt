- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions much weaker than strong convexity.
- Importance: Addresses low-rank matrix recovery, a central problem in matrix completion, collaborative filtering, and subspace learning.
- Writing quality: Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to non-experts.
- Technical rigor: Proofs are technically sound, precise, and presented in a logical, easy-to-follow structure.
- Empirical support: Numerical experiments convincingly validate the theoretical findings.
- Literature awareness: Authors appropriately reference state-of-the-art work and motivate their contribution well.
- Strength — theoretical advance: Linear convergence under weak assumptions is a significant theoretical improvement for widely used PGM.
- Strength — presentation: Clear exposition and thorough introduction that situates the work within prior literature.
- Weakness — novelty vs. l1 results: Needs clearer comparison to l1-norm regularization results and explicit explanation why l1 techniques don’t directly extend to the trace norm.
- Weakness — title: Current title’s use of “structured” is misleading; better to emphasize the role of the linear operator A in the loss.
- Weakness — minor clarity issues: Ambiguous notation (e.g., P at line 337), a broken reference, and missing recalls of Q- and R-linear convergence hamper readability.
- Weakness — residual/definition links: Should relate the residual R(X) in (7) to Tseng’s original definition to aid interpretation.
- Weakness — Lemma 3.2: Key lemma needs more discussion on its implications and how it connects the error bound to linear convergence.
- Acceptance arguments: Addresses a fundamental optimization problem, presents a significant theoretical result, is well-written, and empirically supported.
- Rejection concerns: Novelty relative to l1-norm work is not fully articulated and minor clarity issues remain.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on expanding the l1-norm connection, clarifying notation, fixing references, defining Q/R-linear convergence, and elaborating Lemma 3.2’s role.
