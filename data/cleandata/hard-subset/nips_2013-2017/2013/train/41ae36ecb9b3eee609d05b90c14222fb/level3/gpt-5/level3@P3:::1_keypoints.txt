- Problem and result: Studies PG for min_X f(X)+τ||X||_* with f=h(A(X)), h C1 with Lipschitz gradient and locally strongly convex; proves a local Lipschitzian error bound linking distance to optimum with the PG fixed-point residual and derives Q-linear objective and R-linear iterate convergence via Luo–Tseng framework.
- Technical novelty: Extends error-bound-based linear-rate analyses from polyhedral regularizers to the non-polyhedral trace norm using a spectral-alignment lemma and matrix perturbation to get restricted invertibility.
- Strength: Establishes linear convergence of PG without global strong convexity of f, relevant for problems like matrix completion with non-injective A.
- Strength: The trace-norm error bound is novel and of independent interest; spectral-alignment plus perturbation analysis is nontrivial.
- Strength: Proofs are generally clear and well-organized, with a solid connection to Luo–Tseng’s framework.
- Weakness: Conceptual novelty is moderate — primarily a technical extension of established error-bound machinery.
- Weakness: Empirical validation is limited; more targeted experiments demonstrating the EB regime and observed linear slopes are requested.
- Weakness: Some constant dependencies and connections to prior proofs (Tseng; Tseng & Yun) are not fully spelled out.
- Quality: Technically sound; main claims follow from assumptions and the EB appears correct, though a few proof steps need clarification and constants should be explicit.
- Clarity: Overall clear with consistent notation; spectral arguments motivated, but a few minor confusing places remain.
- Originality: Moderate — main new component is the non-polyhedral EB via spectral alignment; linear-rate template for PG is standard.
- Significance: Useful to low-rank learning and first-order methods; informs step-size choices and can be a building block for analyses of related algorithms (e.g., ADMM).
- Experiment request: Add synthetic experiments (e.g., matrix completion with rank-deficient A) varying sampling ratio θ and spectral gaps, show semilog plots of F(Xk)−F* and ||Xk−Xk+1||F and report estimated linear rates.
- Failure-mode discussion request: Discuss when theory fails but linear PG is observed — non-C1 or non-strongly-convex-on-compacts h, degenerate spectra at threshold, unbounded level sets or bad stepsize choices — and relate to EB constants.
- ADMM extension request: Outline how the EB/metric subregularity can yield local linear convergence for ADMM, stating what carries over and extra assumptions or penalty/regularity requirements, with relevant citations.
- Constants request: Make dependencies of κ1…κ4 on α̲, ᾱ, Lf explicit (e.g., κ1 ≥ 1/(2ᾱ) − Lf/2; κ3 bounded by 1+ᾱLf up to derivation; κ2, κ4 depend on EB modulus and local Lipschitz constants).
- Proof overlap/citation request: Explicitly cite and align which lemmas/arguments are adapted from Tseng/Tseng & Yun and delineate new contributions (Prop.4.2 spectral alignment; non-polyhedral EB proof).
- Minor technical fix: Simplify the derivation leading to (18) using the standard subgradient inequality and avoid suggesting unnecessary intermediate steps.
- Exposition/notation fixes: Clarify relation between residual map using α=1 and algorithm using αk∈(α̲,ᾱ), and recap why compactness/level-set arguments in Section 2.2 hold (A(X) constancy implies boundedness).
- Pros summary: First linear-rate PG result for trace-norm without global strong convexity; new nontrivial EB; clear modular analysis with practical implications.
- Cons summary: Mainly a technical extension of known machinery; empirical evidence tied to theory is limited; some constants, proofs, and overlaps need clearer exposition.
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on adding requested experiments, clarifying constants and proof lineage, discussing failure modes, and outlining an ADMM extension.
