- Main contribution: proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems when f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex, without assuming strong convexity.
- Methodology: follows the Luo–Tseng error-bound framework and establishes a local Lipschitzian error bound specific to the trace norm setting.
- Key technical insight: shows X* and −∇f(X*) share singular vectors, allowing reduction from matrix analysis to singular-value (vector) analysis.
- Strength: positively answers whether linear convergence holds for trace-norm problems absent strong convexity, extending prior L1/group-LASSO results.
- Strength: provides a novel, non-trivial proof of a local error bound enabled by an elegant simultaneous diagonalization argument.
- Strength: theoretical derivations are carefully presented and appear technically sound.
- Weakness: overall approach is standard (Luo–Tseng style); novelty is mainly in extending known techniques to the trace-norm case.
- Weakness: practical impact is limited because the linear rate is asymptotic, initial sublinear phase may dominate, and no new algorithm or empirical comparisons with accelerated methods are provided.
- Weakness: organization and clarity could be improved—state Theorem 3.2 assuming the error bound first, defer the technical error-bound proof to a later section.
- Comment: inequality linking the residual to iterate progress (inequality (11)) is deferred to supplement and may overlap with Sra (2012); the relationship and justification should be clarified.
- Comment: request for intuition/analysis on the size of the neighborhood where linear convergence holds and for comparisons against accelerated proximal-gradient methods in experiments.
- Comment: ask whether the analysis can be extended beyond f=h(A(X)) to more general smooth convex f satisfying other regularity conditions (e.g., quadratic growth).
- Summary: a solid, technically sound theoretical contribution with a novel error-bound proof, but moderate originality and limited immediate practical significance.
