

# Review of "On the Linear Convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method for Trace Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates the convergence rate of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized optimization problems of the form min_X {f(X) + τ‖X‖_*}, where f(X) = h(A(X)) with h satisfying certain convexity/smoothness conditions. The authors establish that under these conditions, the PGM achieves asymptotic linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of the loss function f, which extends previous results that typically required strong convexity for linear convergence.

The paper builds upon Luo and Tseng's framework for establishing linear convergence via error bounds, and its main technical contribution is a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization. The key insight enabling this result is the observation that optimal solutions and their gradients share singular vectors, allowing for simultaneous diagonalization of matrices and reduction to the vector case. This resolves an open question about whether trace norm regularization admits error bounds similar to those known for ℓ1-norm regularization.

The paper appears technically sound overall, with correct proofs of the main theorems. However, I note that the inequality |R_k| ≤ κ |X_k - X_{k+1}| (equation 11) was already established in Suvrit Sra's NIPS 2012 paper (Lemma 4), which should be acknowledged. This is a significant point as it indicates the core inequality used in the convergence proof was previously known.

The paper's main weakness is in clarity. The main results should be stated upfront, followed by the technical development of the error bound. Currently, the paper presents the error bound proof before stating the main convergence result, which makes it harder for readers to grasp the big picture. The authors could improve clarity by restructuring the paper to state the main convergence result first, then present the error bound as the key technical ingredient.

There is also a technical gap in the appendix (line 085) where the authors state R(X^k) ≤ κ3‖X^k - X^{k+1}‖_F but fail to justify why the chosen κ3 (e.g., α < 1) implies this bound. This requires additional justification for the paper to be complete.

Regarding originality, the simultaneous diagonalization technique is novel and interesting, but the overall approach follows existing frameworks (Luo and Tseng, Sra). The problem setting and technical approach are not substantially different from prior work, so the contribution is incremental rather than transformative.

The significance is mainly theoretical. While the result is important for understanding the convergence behavior of PGM for trace-norm regularization, the asymptotic nature of the linear convergence (requiring iterates to be sufficiently close to the solution set) limits its practical impact. The numerical experiments demonstrate linear convergence in practice, but they don't provide new algorithmic insights or significantly outperform existing methods.

Strengths:
- Clear motivation for the problem
- Correct technical development of the error bound
- Good numerical experiments demonstrating linear convergence
- Well-written mathematical presentation (aside from structural issues)

Weaknesses:
- Failure to acknowledge Sra's prior result on the key inequality
- Poor paper structure (main result buried before technical development)
- Missing justification for the inequality in Appendix line 085
- Asymptotic convergence result limits practical significance

Pro arguments for acceptance:
- The paper makes a genuine theoretical contribution to understanding linear convergence for trace-norm regularization
- The simultaneous diagonalization technique is novel and potentially applicable to other matrix regularization problems
- The result extends beyond strong convexity assumptions, which is valuable for many machine learning applications

Con arguments for rejection:
- The main technical novelty is limited (the simultaneous diagonalization observation)
- The approach largely follows existing frameworks (Luo and Tseng, Sra)
- The paper doesn't provide significant algorithmic improvements or practical insights
- The convergence result is asymptotic, limiting practical utility

Overall, this is a well-written, technically sound paper making an incremental contribution to the theory of proximal gradient methods for trace-norm regularization. While the simultaneous diagonalization technique is interesting, the overall contribution is moderate in originality and significance. The paper would benefit from improved clarity and addressing the identified technical gap. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to acknowledge Sra's prior result, clarify the paper structure, and provide the missing justification for the inequality in the appendix.
