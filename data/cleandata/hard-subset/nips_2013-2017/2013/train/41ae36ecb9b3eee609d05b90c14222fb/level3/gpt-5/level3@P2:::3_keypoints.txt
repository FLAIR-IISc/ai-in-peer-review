- Main contribution: proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for nuclear-norm regularized convex risks f(X)=h(A(X)) without assuming strong convexity of f.
- Proof approach: uses Luo–Tseng error-bound framework, establishing a local Lipschitzian error bound linking distance to optimum and proximal residual, then deducing linear rates.
- Technical novelty: new error bound for the nuclear norm via the structural observation that X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share left/right singular vectors, allowing reduction to a vector-like argument.
- Empirical support: experiments (matrix completion, classification) show semilog-linear convergence near the solution, consistent with theory.
- Strength: addresses an important theoretical gap about linear rates for nuclear-norm problems without strong convexity.
- Strength: the simultaneous SVD/structural lemma is elegant and of independent interest.
- Strength: analysis is careful and leverages a robust, well-known framework; assumptions cover common losses (squared, logistic).
- Weakness: originality is moderate — aside from the new lemma, the rest follows established Luo–Tseng-style analyses and is asymptotic/local.
- Weakness: limited algorithmic impact — no new method, unspecified constants, and asymptotic nature weakens practical relevance.
- Technical gap: inequality ‖R(Xk)‖F ≤ κ3‖Xk − Xk+1‖F (Theorem 3.2, (11)) is under-justified and needs a correct proof or citation (e.g., Sra NIPS 2012, Lemma 4).
- Quality: overall technically sound; error-bound proof is involved but structured and convincing aside from the noted gap.
- Clarity issues: paper dives into technicalities too quickly; main claims, assumptions, and high-level proof sketch should be stated up front.
- Presentation suggestion: move long error-bound proof to appendix, keep a sketch in main text, and present Luo–Tseng derivation immediately after the error bound.
- Request: clarify how error-bound constants (η, ε) depend on problem data and singular-value spectral gap at optimum.
- Request: define R(X) earlier and relate it clearly to the PGM update, explaining τ vs ταk mismatch.
- Extension question: discuss whether the arguments extend to other nonpolyhedral spectral regularizers (e.g., Schatten p-norms).
- Experiments suggestion: report stepsizes (fixed vs line search) and illustrate transition from sublinear to linear phases.
- Pros for acceptance: resolves a nontrivial theoretical question, provides reusable structural insight, and gives solid analysis aligned with experiments.
- Cons for acceptance: mostly incremental beyond the lemma, asymptotic/local scope, unspecified constants, and one under-justified proof step.
- Overall recommendation: borderline — lean weak reject unless the authors fix the gap, clarify constants/dependencies, and improve presentation (would support if revised).
