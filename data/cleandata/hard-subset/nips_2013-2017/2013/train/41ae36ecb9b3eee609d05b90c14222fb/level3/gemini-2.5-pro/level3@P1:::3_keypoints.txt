- Main contribution: Proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) without assuming strong convexity.
- Analytical framework: Uses Luo and Tseng (1993) approach linking linear convergence to a local Lipschitzian error bound.
- Technical novelty: Establishes a nontrivial local Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm regularizer.
- Key structural insight: Shows optimal X* and -∇f(X*) share singular vectors, enabling spectral analysis of iterates near the optimum.
- Strength — theoretical relevance: Addresses an open question on linear convergence under weaker-than-strong-convexity conditions for an important problem class.
- Strength — proof technique: The simultaneous SVD observation (Proposition 4.2) is clever and effectively handles non-polyhedral trace-norm structure.
- Strength — soundness: Theoretical development is logical and appears correct from error bound to linear rate.
- Weakness — originality: Moderate overall novelty since it applies a standard Luo–Tseng framework and some intermediate results overlap prior work.
- Weakness — related work/citation: Misses discussion/citation of related results (e.g., Sra 2012) for inequalities like ||R(X^k)||_F ≤ κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.
- Weakness — clarity/organization: Presentation could improve by stating the main linear convergence theorem up front and deferring the technical error-bound proof.
- Weakness — practical significance: Result is asymptotic; linear rate holds only near the optimum, so practical gains may be limited if iterates spend long in sublinear phase.
- Weakness — technical gap: Inequality (11) is stated but conditions (e.g., step-size requirements) under which it holds are not fully derived.
- Overall verdict: Solid theoretical paper with a valuable error-bound proof for trace norm, but somewhat incremental, asymptotically limited in practice, and needs minor citation/technical clarifications.
