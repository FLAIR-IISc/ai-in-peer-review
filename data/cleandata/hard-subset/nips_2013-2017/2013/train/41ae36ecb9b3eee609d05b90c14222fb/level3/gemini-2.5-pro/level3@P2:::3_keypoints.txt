- Studies asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems where f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex but f not necessarily strongly convex.  
- Main technical contribution: a new local Lipschitzian error bound (Theorem 3.1) relating distance to the optimal set to the norm of a residual map.  
- Key technical insight: optimal solution X* and −∇f(X*) share singular vectors, allowing reduction to vector-based perturbation analysis (Proposition 4.2).  
- Analysis applies the Luo–Tseng (1993) framework to derive linear convergence (Theorem 3.2) from the established error bound.  
- Theoretical novelty: extends linear convergence guarantees beyond strong convexity for trace-norm regularization problems.  
- Elegant proof technique: the spectral argument for the error bound may be of independent interest.  
- Relevance: covers important ML applications (matrix completion, multi-task learning, matrix classification).  
- Dependence on prior framework: much of the strategy follows Luo–Tseng; the novelty is primarily in proving the error bound for this specific class.  
- Prior-art concern: inequality (11) (‖R(Xk)‖F ≤ κ3‖Xk − Xk+1‖F) appears similar to known results (e.g., Sra 2012); authors should clarify novelty or cite related work.  
- Practical impact limited: result is asymptotic and may not translate to wall-clock speedups for moderate-accuracy stopping criteria compared to accelerated sublinear methods.  
- Presentation could improve: state main convergence result earlier and treat the error-bound proof as a supporting technical section.  
- Minor proof gap: inequality (11) is asserted with details in the supplement; a brief main-text justification (e.g., role of step size αk) would make the proof more self-contained.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical paper with a novel technical contribution but somewhat incremental and with limited immediate practical benefit; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing citations and proof justification.
