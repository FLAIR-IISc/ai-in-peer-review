- Studies PGM for trace-norm regularized problems and proves a Lipschitzian error bound and linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Extends prior error-bound based linear convergence results (lasso/group lasso) to the trace-norm setting.  
- Main technical contribution: a Lipschitzian error bound for non-polyhedral trace-norm regularization.  
- Key technique: show optimal solutions and their gradients share singular vectors, enabling use of matrix perturbation theory.  
- Non-trivial because standard splitting approaches used for ℓ1/group lasso do not apply to the spectral nature of the trace norm.  
- Strength: sound theoretical contribution that fills an important gap, with generally clear presentation of proofs.  
- Weakness: primarily a technical extension rather than a fundamentally new algorithmic approach.  
- Empirical validation is insufficient; missing semilog plot of objective vs iterations to demonstrate claimed linear convergence.  
- Needs an experiment where the loss is not strongly convex but PGM still shows linear convergence (for different stability levels).  
- Should discuss cases where theoretical assumptions fail yet empirical linear convergence persists.  
- Proof of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirrors Tseng [20]; authors should more clearly cite and distinguish their approach.  
- Dependencies of constants κ1...κ4 on step-size bounds (α̲ and ᾱ) are unclear and must be clarified.  
- Minor technical correction: the inequality before (13) follows from convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖_* (p.289 in [20]) rather than the shown intermediate step.  
- Recommend outlining how the analysis might extend to ADMM, a commonly used method for trace-norm problems.  
- NIPS criteria summary: Quality — theory sound but empirical lacking; Clarity — generally good but clarify constant dependencies; Originality — important but incremental; Significance — relevant to trace-norm community.  
- Requested revisions: add semilog convergence plot; include non-strongly convex simulation; clarify constant dependencies; better differentiate from Tseng; discuss failure cases; outline ADMM extension.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to address empirical validation gaps and technical clarifications.
