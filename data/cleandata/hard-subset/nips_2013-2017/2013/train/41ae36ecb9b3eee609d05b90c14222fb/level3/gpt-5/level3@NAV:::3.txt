This paper studies proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization and establishes asymptotic linear convergence without assuming strong convexity of the loss. The analysis follows the Luo–Tseng error-bound framework: the authors prove a local Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to the solution set to the proximal residual, and then derive linear convergence of proximal gradient under fixed step sizes below 1/L. The main technical novelty is an observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of X̄ and −∇f(X̄) at an optimum, which reduces key arguments to a vector case and unlocks a nonpolyhedral error bound for the nuclear norm. The work connects to and extends prior results on error bounds for polyhedral regularizers (ℓ1, group LASSO) and linear rates for strongly convex objectives. It is also related to Sra (NIPS 2012), Tseng, and Zhang–Jiang–Luo on linear rates via error bounds.

Quality: The analysis appears broadly correct and carefully executed. The simultaneous diagonalization step is elegant and technically useful. However, there is a gap: in the appendix (line 085) you use a constant κ3 to assert ‖R(Xk)‖F ≤ κ3‖Xk − Xk+1‖F. Please justify this rigorously, including the dependence on the stepsize αk (e.g., αk ∈ (α, ᾱ) ⊂ (0, 1/L)). A short derivation via firm nonexpansiveness of the proximal map and the descent lemma, or a direct citation, would suffice. In fact, an inequality of this form was already proved in Sra, NIPS 2012 (Lemma 4) for proximal gradient; please cite and align notation (your residual uses τ while the algorithm uses αkτ).

Clarity: The paper is readable but would benefit from reorganizing: clearly state the main claims early (local error bound, then linear rate under standard stepsizes), defer the error-bound proof to the appendix, and present the linear-rate derivation in the main text. Unify notation for prox with and without stepsizes to avoid confusion.

Originality: Moderate. The simultaneous diagonalization argument is new and interesting for nuclear-norm problems; the convergence framework and much of the analysis otherwise follow established work.

Significance: Mainly theoretical. The result strengthens our understanding of PGM under trace-norm regularization, but the asymptotic and local nature limits immediate algorithmic impact. Experiments illustrate linear behavior near the solution; adding comparisons to accelerated methods and discussing when the linear regime is entered would help practitioners.

Pros:
- Resolves a nontrivial error-bound question for the trace norm
- Clean extension of Luo–Tseng to a widely used regularizer
- Technically sound overall; clear main novelty

Cons:
- Missing justification for the residual–step relation; prior art not cited
- Asymptotic/local result with limited practical implications
- Exposition could be streamlined and notation clarified

Overall: Incremental but solid theoretical contribution; moderately interesting.
