- Main contribution: proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for min f(X)+τ||X||* without assuming strong convexity, via a new local Lipschitzian error bound linking dist(X,X*) to the proximal residual.

- Technical novelty: derives an error bound for the nonpolyhedral nuclear norm by exploiting spectral structure — optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors — and a restricted invertibility argument.

- Framework: combines the new error bound with the Luo–Tseng analysis to obtain Q-linear objective convergence and R-linear iterate convergence under step sizes αk ∈ (α, ᾱ) ⊂ (0,1/Lf).

- Relation to prior work: extends error-bound-based linear convergence results beyond polyhedral nonsmooth terms (ℓ1/group lasso) and beyond strong-convexity assumptions.

- Soundness: overall proof strategy is solid; simultaneous SVD and restricted invertibility are nontrivial and appropriately address nonpolyhedral geometry.

- Empirical support: numerical experiments (matrix completion, logistic classification) show empirical linear convergence near solutions.

- Constants issue: need explicit dependence of κ1,…,κ4 on α, ᾱ, Lf, τ, norms of A and ∇h (L) and whether they can be chosen uniformly over level sets.

- Proof clarity: the inequalities leading to equation (18) are confusing; streamline the argument and cite Tseng for the subgradient inequality used.

- Locality: state explicitly that the error bound and resulting linear rate are local — they hold once iterates enter a neighborhood where the residual is below ε.

- Notation: unify notation for regularization parameter (τ vs μ) and for step-size policies (fixed α vs backtracking); ensure backtracking enforces αk ≤ 1/Lf as required.

- Luo–Tseng invocation: explicitly map and verify the precise Luo–Tseng conditions (descent, sufficient decrease, error bound) and how they correspond to inequalities (9)–(11).

- Presentation fixes: correct figure numbering/captions and label all axes and parameters (n, θ, τ) in plots.

- Originality nuance: spectral-structure and restricted invertibility are substantive; the convergence-theorem pipeline largely follows known templates — delineate which parts are new versus borrowed.

- Significance: explains empirically observed local linear convergence for PGM/SVT-type algorithms without strong convexity and provides an error bound likely useful for other methods.

- Requested experiment: add a simulation where f is not strongly convex (e.g., sampling operator A with nullspace or poor conditioning) with semi-log plots of objective and distance to X*, varying sampling/conditioning.

- Failure-mode discussion: discuss scenarios delaying/failing linear regime (h not strictly convex on compacts, step sizes >1/Lf, singular value multiplicity changes, flat solution-set directions weakening restricted invertibility).

- Attribution: make explicit which parts of the error-bound→linear-rate pipeline follow Luo–Tseng [10], Tseng [22], Tseng & Yun, Zhang et al. [25], with precise citations at mirrored arguments.

- Constants/assumptions detail: provide explicit formulas or bounds for κ1,…,κ4 if possible, and express the neighborhood size ε in Theorem 3.1 in terms of problem constants.

- Acceleration remark: comment whether accelerated variants (e.g., FISTA with restart) inherit the local linear rate under the established error bound and specify required restart conditions.

- ADMM extension: briefly outline how the error bound and restricted invertibility could be used to derive linear convergence of ADMM for nuclear-norm problems under standard assumptions (exact solves, bounded multipliers).

- Pros summary: establishes a new local error bound for trace norm, achieves asymptotic linear convergence without strong convexity, covers common losses, and provides supportive experiments with mostly clear proofs.

- Cons summary: novelty concentrated in the error bound only, experiments are limited (need non–strongly convex example), some proof steps and constant dependencies are opaque, and attribution to prior templates should be clearer.

- Recommendation: weak accept, conditional on clarifying proof dependencies and constant relationships, adding the requested experiment, and including discussion on ADMM and assumption gaps.
