- Presents linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of f, via a novel Lipschitzian error bound.  
- Builds on convex optimization and PGM literature, extending results to settings like matrix completion where loss need not be strongly convex.  
- Uses matrix perturbation theory to handle the non-polyhedral nature of the trace norm epigraph — a key technical distinction from ℓ1 analyses.  
- The Lipschitzian error bound is a valuable independent technical contribution.  
- Proofs are rigorous and assumptions are clearly articulated, demonstrating deep literature knowledge.  
- Empirical validation is limited: experiments show convergence but do not systematically test theoretical assumptions (e.g., non-strongly convex h).  
- Reviewer requests simulations showing linear convergence with a non-strongly convex loss and plots varying regularization/noise levels.  
- Relation to prior work (especially Tseng [20]) needs a more explicit, detailed comparison highlighting precise differences and novel insights.  
- Clarify dependence of constants κ1–κ4 in Theorem 3.2 on step-size bounds α̲ and ᾱ.  
- Minor technical concern: a non-obvious step in inequalities before Eq. (13) appears to omit use of convexity and the optimality condition −Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||*.  
- Recommend discussing cases where assumptions fail but empirical linear convergence persists, to probe robustness.  
- Suggest sketching how analysis might extend to ADMM to increase practical impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, removes strong-convexity requirement, rigorous presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical validation, insufficiently detailed comparison to related work, lack of robustness/ADMM discussion, minor proof issues.  
- Overall assessment: weak accept pending expanded experiments, clearer relation to prior work, and addressing minor technical gaps.
