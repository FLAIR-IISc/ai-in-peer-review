- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm problems via a new Lipschitzian error bound under weaker assumptions than strong convexity.  
- Novelty: resolves an open question by extending Tseng's technique to nuclear/norm-regularized problems.  
- Applicability: result covers important ML/signal-processing tasks (matrix completion, classification) with losses like square and logistic loss, without requiring strong convexity.  
- Technical quality: paper is well-written, technically sound, main proof appears correct and properly situated in the literature.  
- Title issue: remove "structured" from the title since novelty concerns the loss/operator side (A), not the regularizer.  
- l1-norm comparison: paper lacks clear comparison to l1 results (e.g., [20,22]); authors should show how l1 follows as a corollary and clarify novelty vs. expected extension.  
- Clarify operator P in Section 5: state whether P is a sampling (random) or fixed mask operator.  
- Fix references/notation: repair broken reference at line 337 and briefly define Q- and R-linear convergence.  
- Relate definitions: connect the residual R(X) used here to Tseng's original definition (eq. 46).  
- Lemma 3.2: provide more discussion or justification to strengthen this lemma.  
- Strengths: addresses an important open problem, rigorous, clear writing, significant theoretical advance on error bounds for trace-norm problems.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient l1-norm comparison, title wording, and several minor clarifications needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
