## Review of "On the Linear Convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method for Trace Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates the convergence rate of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized risk minimization, a problem prevalent in various machine learning applications like matrix completion and low-rank representation. The authors demonstrate linear convergence under relatively mild assumptions – specifically, without requiring strong convexity of the loss function – extending previous results that relied on this stronger condition. They achieve this by building upon the framework of Luo and Tseng, and crucially, by establishing a novel Lipschitzian error bound tailored to the trace norm regularization. 

The work correctly identifies a gap in the existing literature, where linear convergence for PGM with trace norm regularization was largely limited to strongly convex loss functions. The authors’ approach of leveraging a new error bound, stemming from an observation allowing simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, is a clever technical contribution that reduces the problem to a more tractable vector case. The technical soundness of the paper appears solid, with a clear presentation of the theoretical analysis. The numerical experiments, while limited, provide some empirical support for the derived linear convergence rate in matrix completion and classification tasks.

However, the paper has several weaknesses. The most significant is the incremental nature of the contribution. While the simultaneous diagonalization is interesting, the overall approach largely follows established methodologies. Furthermore, the asymptotic nature of the linear convergence weakens its practical impact. The reviewer notes that the inequality |R_k| <= κ |X_k - X_{k+1}| was already proven in Sra (NIPS 2012), which is a significant oversight. 

The clarity of the paper could be improved. The authors bury the main result – the linear convergence – within a dense technical presentation. A more effective structure would be to first state the key convergence claim prominently, then outline the roadmap to its proof, and finally delve into the technical details of the local error bound. A justification for the choice of κ3 in Appendix line 085 is also missing; the claim that α < 1 implies R(X^k) <= κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F needs further explanation.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses an open question regarding linear convergence of PGM for trace norm regularization.
*   Presents a technically sound analysis with a novel observation regarding simultaneous diagonalization.
*   Provides empirical validation through numerical experiments.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Incremental contribution, building heavily on existing work.
*   Asymptotic convergence rate limits practical applicability.
*   Oversight regarding prior work (Sra, NIPS 2012).
*   Clarity could be improved by restructuring the presentation.



**Overall Assessment:** This paper represents a moderately interesting theoretical contribution. While technically correct and addressing a relevant problem, its incremental nature and limited practical impact suggest it is not a standout submission for NIPS. I recommend **weak accept** with the request that the authors address the clarity issues and acknowledge the related work by Sra (2012).
