This paper studies the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for a class of trace norm-regularized problems. The authors extend the linear convergence of PGM beyond strong convexity by establishing a local error bound, which enables asymptotic linear convergence of the method. The key technical novelty of the paper is an observation that allows for the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the problem to the vector case.

The paper appears to be technically sound overall. However, there is a gap in the appendix (line 085) that needs to be addressed. Specifically, the authors need to provide justification for why the chosen value of κ3 (e.g., α < 1) implies that R(X^k) <= κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.

The paper is readable, but it would benefit from a clearer presentation of the main claims and results. The authors should consider stating the main claims upfront and deferring the technical proof of the local error bound. The derivation of the linear convergence rate could then be presented in a more straightforward manner.

The originality of the paper is moderate. While the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is a new and interesting observation, the overall approach follows existing work. The significance of the paper is mainly theoretical, with limited algorithmic impact. The asymptotic nature of the results also weakens their practical significance.

In terms of related work, the authors mention that the inequality |R_k| <= κ |X_k - X_{k+1}| was already proved in Suvrit Sra, NIPS 2012 (Lemma 4). However, the authors' contribution lies in extending this result to the case of trace norm-regularized risk minimization.

Overall, this paper makes an incremental contribution to the field of optimization. While the results are moderately interesting from a theoretical perspective, their practical significance is limited.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a new and interesting observation that allows for the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.
* The authors establish a local error bound that enables asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient method.
* The paper is technically sound overall.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper's originality is moderate, and the overall approach follows existing work.
* The significance of the paper is mainly theoretical, with limited algorithmic impact.
* The asymptotic nature of the results weakens their practical significance.

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but suggest revisions to improve the clarity and presentation of the results.
* Request that the authors provide justification for the chosen value of κ3 (e.g., α < 1) and its implications for the error bound.
* Encourage the authors to discuss the practical significance of their results and their potential impact on the field of optimization.
