- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularization without requiring strong convexity of f.
- Key technical tool: Introduces a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm problems using matrix perturbation theory and shared singular vectors.
- Framework: Builds on Luo and Tseng’s error-bound approach to obtain linear rates (Theorems 3.1 and 3.2).
- Novelty: Resolves an open question about applying error-bound techniques to the non-polyhedral trace norm, extending beyond prior ℓ1 results.
- Technical quality: Rigorous, carefully developed proofs and assumptions; numerical experiments (matrix completion/classification) support theory.
- Clarity: Well-written and organized, but needs brief definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence and clearer relation of residual R(X) to Tseng’s definition.
- Title and wording: Title should avoid "structured" since novelty lies on the loss/operator side rather than the regularizer.
- Experimental detail: Clarify whether P in matrix completion experiments is a sampling/mask operator; fix the broken reference at line 337.
- Comparison to ℓ1: Paper should explicitly discuss how the result extends the ℓ1 case and why ℓ1 techniques do not directly transfer to trace norm.
- Lemma motivation: Lemma 3.2 (Lemma 4.1) requires better motivation and explanation.
- Significance: Results are important for low-rank recovery tasks (matrix completion, multi-task learning), improving O(1/k) to linear rates in practice.
- Strengths summary: Resolves an important open problem, provides a clean theoretical framework, demonstrates practical relevance, and clearly states technical conditions.
- Weaknesses summary: Minor clarity/notation and exposition issues, and a need for deeper comparison to vector ℓ1 literature.
- Recommendation: Strong accept — substantial theoretical contribution; authors should address the minor clarifications to improve accessibility.
