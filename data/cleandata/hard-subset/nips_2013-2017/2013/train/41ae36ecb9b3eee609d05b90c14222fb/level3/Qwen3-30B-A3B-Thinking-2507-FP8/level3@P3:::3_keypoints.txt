- Establishes asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularization without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Builds on Luo and Tseng’s error-bound framework to derive linear convergence.  
- Main technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization.  
- Key insight: optimal solutions and their gradients share singular vectors, enabling simultaneous diagonalization and reduction to the vector case.  
- Resolves an open question about whether trace-norm regularization admits error bounds analogous to ℓ1-norm regularization.  
- Proofs and main theorems appear technically sound overall.  
- The inequality |R_k| ≤ κ |X_k - X_{k+1}| (Eq. 11) was previously shown by S. Sra (NIPS 2012) and should be acknowledged.  
- Paper structure reduces clarity: main convergence result is presented after the error-bound proof, making the narrative harder to follow.  
- Technical gap in the appendix (line 085): R(X^k) ≤ κ3‖X^k - X^{k+1}‖_F lacks sufficient justification for the chosen κ3.  
- Originality is moderate: simultaneous diagonalization is novel, but the overall approach follows existing frameworks (Luo & Tseng, Sra).  
- Significance is mainly theoretical; asymptotic nature limits direct practical impact.  
- Numerical experiments confirm linear convergence but offer no new algorithmic advances or superior empirical performance.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, correct error-bound development, solid numerical demonstrations, and well-written mathematics aside from structure.  
- Weaknesses: omission of prior work citation (Sra), poor paper organization, missing appendix justification, and limited practical significance.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to acknowledge prior work, improve structure, and fix the appendix justification.
