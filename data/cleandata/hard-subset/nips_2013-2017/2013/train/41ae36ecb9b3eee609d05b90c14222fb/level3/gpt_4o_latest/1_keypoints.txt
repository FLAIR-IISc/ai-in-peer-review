- Main contribution: Shows proximal gradient method (PGM) attains linear convergence for trace-norm regularized convex smooth losses without strong convexity via a new Lipschitzian error bound.
- Strength — theoretical extension: Generalizes linear convergence results from lasso/group lasso to trace-norm regularization despite the non-polyhedral epigraph.
- Strength — error bound: Introduces a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm problems, a significant technical advance with potential broader impact.
- Strength — clarity of proofs: Proofs are generally clear and well-structured, using the Luo–Tseng framework to establish linear convergence.
- Strength — numerical validation: Numerical experiments (semi-log plots for matrix completion and classification) support the theoretical linear convergence claims.
- Weakness — limited novelty: Contribution is primarily technical and builds heavily on existing frameworks, so novelty may be seen as incremental.
- Weakness — unclear constant dependencies: Paper does not clearly explain how constants (κ1–κ4) depend on parameters (α̲, ᾱ), limiting practical interpretability.
- Weakness — proof referencing: Proofs closely resemble prior work and need more precise referencing to separate new contributions from existing literature.
- Weakness — numerical example shortcomings: Experiments need deeper discussion and better-annotated plots to clarify implications.
- Weakness — exposition issue: The sequence of inequalities leading to (13) is confusing and requires clarification.
- Suggestion — additional simulation: Include a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but PGM still converges linearly, and discuss failure cases.
- Suggestion — extend to ADMM: Analyze or discuss extending results to the alternating direction method of multipliers to broaden applicability.
- Suggestion — clarify constants and (13): Explain dependencies of constants on problem parameters and improve exposition around inequality (13).
- Suggestion — improve citations: Provide more precise citations to clearly delineate the authors’ novel contributions.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing the clarity, simulation, and citation issues.
