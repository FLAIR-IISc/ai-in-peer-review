- Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularization without requiring strong convexity.
- Builds on Luo–Tseng framework and derives a Lipschitzian error bound for the trace-norm problem.
- Key technical novelty: a simultaneous diagonalization observation that reduces the problem to a vector case.
- Technical analysis is sound and logically builds on established convex optimization and proximal algorithm concepts.
- The main result (linear convergence guarantee) is buried in dense theory and should be stated upfront with a high-level proof outline.
- The paper should more explicitly acknowledge prior work: the error bound |R_k| ≤ κ |X_k − X_{k+1}| was already proved in Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4).
- Appendix justification is weak: the step claiming κ3 based on α < 1 implying R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F needs further elaboration.
- Originality is moderate—simultaneous diagonalization is interesting but overall strategy follows existing approaches.
- Significance is mainly theoretical; asymptotic convergence results and lack of practical guidance (e.g., parameter choices) limit algorithmic impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel observation, technically sound analysis, addresses an important open problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution, limited practical relevance, presentation and citation issues.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve presentation and acknowledge related work.
