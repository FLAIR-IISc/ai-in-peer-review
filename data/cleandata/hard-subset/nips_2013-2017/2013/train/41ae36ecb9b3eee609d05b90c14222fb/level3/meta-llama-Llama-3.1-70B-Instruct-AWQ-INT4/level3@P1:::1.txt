This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of convex optimization by establishing the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for a class of trace norm-regularized problems. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and offer a clear and well-structured presentation of their results.

The main claim of the paper is that the PGM converges linearly for problems of the form min_X {f(X) = h(A(X)) + τ ||X||_*}, where A is a linear operator and h is a strongly convex function with Lipschitz gradient. This result is a generalization of previous "error-bound" based linear convergence results for lasso/group lasso problems to the trace norm.

The authors' technical contribution is substantial, as they overcome the challenges of analyzing the spectral properties of points close to the optimal solution set. The introduction of a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem is a crucial ingredient in their proof.

The paper's strengths include the establishment of linear convergence of the PGM for trace norm regularization, which is a significant extension of previous results. The authors also provide a clear and well-structured presentation of their results, making the paper easy to follow.

However, the paper has some weaknesses. The contribution is perceived as primarily technical, and the authors could have provided more empirical validation of their results. The reviewer requests a numerical example demonstrating linear convergence, which would strengthen the paper's impact.

Additionally, the authors should clarify the dependencies of the constants κ1, …, κ4 on the choice of α̲ and ᾱ. A minor technical issue is the sequence of inequalities before (13), which is confusing and could be improved.

The authors should also discuss when the theoretical assumptions (e.g., strong convexity) fail but empirical linear convergence still occurs. An outline of how the analysis might extend to ADMM would be a valuable addition to the paper.
