- Main result: Proximal gradient method (PGM) for min f(X)+τ||X||* attains asymptotic linear convergence (Q-linear objective, R-linear iterates) without strong convexity of f.  
- Scope: Covers f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear and h smooth, strictly convex, Lipschitz-gradient, including matrix completion and logistic loss.  
- Step-size: Linear rate holds for constant step-size ≤1/Lf or with backtracking that enforces this bound.  
- Core proof idea: Establishes a local Lipschitzian error bound linking dist(X,X*) to residual R(X)=Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X, then applies Luo–Tseng machinery.  
- Novelty: First derivation of an error bound for nuclear-norm regularization despite its non-polyhedral epigraph.  
- Technical key: Proposition 4.2—at optimality X* and −∇f(X*) share singular vectors—enables spectral perturbation analysis.  
- Restricted invertibility: Uses simultaneous SVD and curvature of h to obtain a restricted invertibility inequality tying ||X−ΠX*||F to ||A(X−ΠX*)||2.  
- Main theorem: Theorem 3.1 gives the error bound; Theorem 3.2 yields Q-linear decrease of function values and R-linear convergence of iterates.  
- Relation to prior work: Improves on previous sublinear O(1/k)–O(1/k^2) PGM rates and parallels Luo–Tseng results for polyhedral/group-separable nonsmooth terms.  
- Complementarity: Complements algorithmic advances (accelerated PG, fixed-point/Bregman) by giving asymptotic linear rates for basic PGM under general losses.  
- Experiments: Matrix completion and classification experiments show linear slopes in semi-log plots once in the local region.  
- Quality: Assumptions on h and A are reasonable and analysis is carefully written and plausible.  
- Locality: The linear rate is local/asymptotic, relying on a residual threshold—consistent with error-bound analyses.  
- Clarity strengths: Succinct setup and intuitive residual-based optimality measure; generally well organized.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: Compare R(X) to Tseng/Luo–Tseng residual maps, recall Q-/R-linear definitions earlier, and give more intuition for restricted invertibility.  
- Notation: Ensure consistent notation for regularization parameter (τ vs. μ).  
- Suggestions/questions: Add a subsection comparing to the ℓ1 case and show ℓ1 as a special corollary via diagonal restriction.  
- Constants/region: Comment on estimating error-bound constants (η, ε) and dependence on A’s conditioning in practice.  
- Accelerated methods: Discuss whether the error-bound framework yields local linear rates for accelerated/proximal methods with restarts.  
- Experiments improvements: Add comparisons to accelerated variants (e.g., FISTA), alternative solvers, wall-clock times, and SVD ranks.  
- Minor issues: Clarify P as a fixed masking operator in matrix completion, fix a broken reference around line ~337, and relate R(X) to prior residual definitions.  
- Pros for acceptance: Resolves an open question, broad applicability, clean analysis, and supportive experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: Rate is asymptotic/local with unquantified constants, limited empirical baselines, and some presentation gaps.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
