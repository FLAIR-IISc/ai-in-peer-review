

# Review of "On the Linear Convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method for Trace Norm Regularization"

This paper makes a significant contribution to the theoretical understanding of proximal gradient methods for trace norm regularization. The authors establish a linear convergence rate for the proximal gradient method (PGM) applied to problems of the form min_X {f(X) + τ||X||_*}, where f(X) = h(A(X)) for a linear operator A and a strictly convex function h with certain smoothness properties. This result is established without requiring strong convexity of f, which is a notable improvement over prior work that typically required strong convexity for linear convergence. The key technical contribution is a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems, which the authors prove using matrix perturbation theory and the observation that optimal solutions and gradients share the same singular vectors.

The paper builds on the framework developed by Luo and Tseng [10] for establishing linear convergence rates through error bounds. Prior work on error bounds for composite minimization has successfully applied to ℓ1-norm regularization [22, 23, 25], but the trace norm case had remained open due to the non-polyhedral nature of the trace norm's epigraph. The authors successfully bridge this gap, resolving an important open question in the literature.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with a rigorous proof. The error bound (Theorem 3.1) and its application to establish linear convergence (Theorem 3.2) are carefully developed. The numerical experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification demonstrate linear convergence behavior as predicted by the theory, supporting the theoretical claims. The authors are appropriately careful in discussing the limitations of their assumptions, particularly regarding the form of the loss function f(X) = h(A(X)).

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and well-organized, with a clear exposition of the problem and the technical contributions. However, there are several minor improvements that could enhance clarity. The paper would benefit from briefly defining Q-linear and R-linear convergence (as these are standard but not universally familiar to all readers), and more explicitly relating the residual map R(X) defined in (7) to Tseng's original definition. The title could be improved to avoid the term "structured," as the main novelty concerns the loss function side (linear operator A) rather than the regularizer. The reference at line 337 appears broken, and the paper should clarify whether P in the matrix completion experiments represents a sampling or mask operator.

**Originality:** This work makes a clear and significant contribution to the literature. The authors address an open question about applying Tseng's error bound technique to trace norm regularization, which had been a known limitation compared to ℓ1-norm regularization. While the ℓ1-norm case can be viewed as a special case of the trace norm (with a diagonal operator A), the authors should explicitly discuss how their result extends the ℓ1-norm case and why the direct translation of ℓ1 techniques to trace norm fails. The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with the vector ℓ1-norm case (e.g., [22]) to clarify the novelty and the specific technical challenges that arise with the trace norm.

**Significance:** The result has important implications for many machine learning applications involving low-rank matrix recovery, including matrix completion, multi-task learning, and matrix classification. The linear convergence rate is a substantial improvement over the standard O(1/k) rate, making the proximal gradient method more practical for large-scale problems. The paper also identifies a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems, which may be of independent interest for other optimization techniques.

**Strengths:**
- Resolves an important open question about linear convergence of PGM for trace norm regularization
- Provides a clean theoretical framework using error bounds
- Demonstrates practical relevance with numerical experiments
- Carefully establishes the technical conditions under which the result holds

**Weaknesses:**
- Title could be more precise regarding the main contribution
- Some minor technical points need clarification (Q/R-linear convergence, residual definition)
- Could better articulate the relationship between the trace norm result and the ℓ1-norm case
- Lemma 3.2 (now Lemma 4.1 in the paper) could be more thoroughly motivated

**Recommendation:** This paper makes a significant theoretical contribution to the field of convex optimization for machine learning. It addresses an important open question with a clean and rigorous proof, and the result has clear implications for practitioners. The paper would be strengthened by the suggested clarifications, but these are minor compared to the substantial technical contribution. I recommend acceptance at NIPS.

The authors should address the minor points regarding the title, residual definition, and comparison to the ℓ1-norm case. These would make an already strong paper even more accessible and impactful. The theoretical contribution is substantial and advances our understanding of first-order methods for low-rank matrix optimization problems.
