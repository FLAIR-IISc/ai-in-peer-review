

This paper investigates the convergence properties of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems of the form min_X {f(X) + τ‖X‖_*}, where f(X) = h(A(X)), A is linear, and h is strongly convex with Lipschitz gradient. The authors establish a Lipschitzian error bound for this problem class, which they then use to prove linear convergence of PGM without requiring strong convexity of f. This extends prior error-bound based linear convergence results (Tseng [20], Tseng & Yun, Zhang et al.) from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm setting.

The main technical contribution is a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization, which is non-polyhedral (unlike ℓ1 or group LASSO). The authors overcome the challenge of the trace norm's spectral nature by showing optimal solutions and their gradients share the same singular vectors, enabling the application of matrix perturbation theory. This is a non-trivial technical extension of prior work, as standard splitting approaches (used for ℓ1 or group LASSO) cannot be applied to trace norm.

Strengths include the sound theoretical contribution that fills an important gap in the literature, and the generally clear presentation of the proofs. The paper correctly identifies the key challenge (non-polyhedral epigraph) and provides a novel solution using matrix perturbation theory.

Weaknesses include the primarily technical nature of the contribution, which is an extension rather than a fundamentally new approach. The empirical validation is insufficient: while Section 5 provides numerical experiments, it lacks a semilog plot of objective vs. iterations to demonstrate linear convergence as claimed. The authors should add a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but PG still converges linearly (for different stability levels), and discuss when theoretical assumptions fail but empirical linear convergence persists.

The proof of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirrors Tseng's work [20] without sufficient differentiation. The authors should more clearly cite and distinguish their approach from Tseng's framework. Additionally, the dependencies of constants κ1,...,κ4 on α̲ and ᾱ are unclear; the authors should clarify how these constants depend on the choice of step size bounds.

There is a minor technical issue in the sequence of inequalities before (13): the final inequality follows from convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||_* (see p.289 in [20]) rather than the intermediate step shown.

Regarding extensions, the authors should outline how their analysis might extend to ADMM, which is a commonly used method for trace-norm regularization.

In terms of NIPS criteria:
- Quality: Theoretical results are sound, but empirical validation is insufficient to fully support claims.
- Clarity: Generally well-written, but technical details about constant dependencies need clarification.
- Originality: Important technical extension of prior work, but not fundamentally new.
- Significance: Significant for trace-norm regularization community, but primarily incremental contribution.

The paper would be significantly strengthened by:
1. Adding a semilog plot demonstrating linear convergence
2. Including the requested simulation with non-strongly convex loss
3. Clarifying constant dependencies on step size bounds
4. Better differentiating from Tseng's work
5. Discussing failure cases of theoretical assumptions
6. Outlining extension to ADMM

While the paper makes a valuable technical contribution to the trace-norm regularization literature, the lack of empirical validation and primarily incremental nature of the theoretical contribution make it less compelling than it could be. With the requested revisions, this paper would be a strong contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with major revisions to address the empirical validation gap and technical clarifications.
