- Addresses an important problem by proving linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity.  
- Main technical contribution: establishes a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the nuclear (trace) norm, resolving a prior open question.  
- Broadens applicability of PGM to common ML losses (e.g., square and logistic) beyond previously required assumptions.  
- Builds on Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound framework and successfully extends it to the non-polyhedral trace norm case.  
- Technical approach leverages matrix perturbation theory to analyze spectral structure near optimal solutions.  
- Provides a detailed proof of the error bound and derives implications for linear convergence of PGM.  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) empirically validate the theoretical linear convergence results.  
- Lacks a direct comparison to the vector ℓ1-norm case, which would clarify differences and why ℓ1 techniques don’t directly transfer.  
- Title is slightly misleading—flexibility from the linear operator affects the loss, not the regularizer—so title revision is suggested.  
- Minor presentation issues: clarify terms like “sampling operator,” fix a broken reference, and give more intuitive explanation of Q- vs R-linear convergence.  
- The technical proof of Lemma 3.2 is thorough but could be more concise with clearer conclusions to balance complexity.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
