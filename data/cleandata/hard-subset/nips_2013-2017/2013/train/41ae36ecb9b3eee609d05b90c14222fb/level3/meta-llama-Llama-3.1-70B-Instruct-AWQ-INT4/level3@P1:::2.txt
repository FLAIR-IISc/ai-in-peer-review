This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of machine learning and signal processing by establishing the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems. The authors address an important problem in this area, and their result is applicable to various applications, including matrix completion and matrix classification.

The paper is well-written, and the main proof appears to be correct. The authors build upon the Lipschitzian error bound technique developed by Tseng, extending an open question about applying this technique to the nuclear (trace) norm. The reviewer suggests that the authors provide a clear comparison and analogy to the vector l1-norm case, as well as a discussion on why l1 techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm.

One potential issue with the paper is that the l1-norm result should follow as a corollary of the trace-norm result. The authors should discuss the perceived novelty of their contribution versus the expected extension of existing results. Additionally, the title of the paper could be changed to avoid the word "structured," as the main new contribution concerns the loss side (linear operator A) rather than the regularizer.

Some minor issues with the paper include clarifying whether P is a sampling or mask operator, fixing a broken reference, and briefly recalling the meanings of Q- and R-linear convergence for reader clarity. The authors could also relate the residual R(X) defined in (6) to Tseng's original definition for interpretation.

The reviewer suggests adding more discussion and conclusions to justify the technical Lemma 3.2 in Section 3. However, overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a significant contribution to the field.
