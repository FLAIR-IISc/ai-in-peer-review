- Presents a theoretical result showing linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of f.
- Core contribution is a novel Lipschitzian error bound enabling use of Luo and Tseng’s convergence framework for trace-norm problems.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and positions itself within relevant literature (Beck & Teboulle, Recht & Fazel, Tseng).
- Mathematical notation and proof outline are generally clear and the arguments appear technically sound.
- Novelty: extends linear convergence analysis to a broader class of problems (trace-norm regularization) where f need not be strongly convex.
- Technical soundness: rigorous problem setup and clever use of a Lipschitzian error bound.
- Relevance: important for matrix completion and low-rank learning applications.
- Clear presentation: main ideas and results are easy to follow.
- Weakness: limited empirical validation; experiments are narrow and do not show linear convergence when strong convexity of h fails.
- Weakness: proof outline for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 4.1 closely resembles Tseng [20]; need clearer differentiation of novelty and technical contributions.
- Weakness: dependencies of constants κ1–κ4 on step sizes α and ᾱ are not explicitly stated.
- Weakness: a sequence of inequalities before eq. (13) is unclear and relies on a convexity argument that should be corrected.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of scenarios where assumptions (e.g., strong convexity of h on compact sets) are violated but empirical linear convergence occurs.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel extension, and clever handling of non-polyhedral trace-norm epigraph.
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate empirical support and insufficient differentiation from existing work (Tseng).
- Overall assessment: valuable theoretical contribution but limited practical impact due to weak experiments and relation to prior work.
- Recommendation: Weak reject, conditional on addressing empirical demonstration, clearer differentiation from Tseng, clarification of constants’ dependencies, and brief discussion/extension to ADMM.
