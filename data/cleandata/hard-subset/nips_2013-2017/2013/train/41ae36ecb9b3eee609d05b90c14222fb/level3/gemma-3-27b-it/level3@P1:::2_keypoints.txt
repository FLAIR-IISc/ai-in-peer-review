- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems under conditions weaker than strong convexity.  
- Significance: Result is important for common ML tasks like matrix completion and classification.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is generally well-written, clear, and logically structured.  
- Technical soundness: Proofs appear rigorous and build on established techniques.  
- Core idea: Establishes a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization enabling Luo–Tseng convergence framework.  
- Technical novelty: Addresses the non-polyhedral nature of the trace-norm epigraph via spectral analysis of near-optimal solutions.  
- Relation to prior work: Paper situates itself carefully within literature on PGM, convex optimization, and low-rank recovery.  
- Empirical validation: Numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification corroborate theoretical linear convergence.  
- Requested clarification 1: Provide a more explicit comparison to ℓ1-norm results and explain why ℓ1 techniques do not directly extend to trace norm.  
- Requested clarification 2: State explicitly whether the ℓ1-norm result follows as a corollary or requires substantial modification.  
- Title suggestion: Consider revising title to emphasize the composite problem or linear operator A (e.g., "Linear Convergence of PGM for Composite Problems with Trace Norm Regularization").  
- Minor fixes: Clarify notation ‘P’ in matrix completion, fix incomplete reference [337], and briefly recall Q-linear vs R-linear convergence.  
- Technical clarifications: Explain relation between residual R(X) in (7) and Tseng’s definition (46 in [20]), and expand justification before Lemma 3.2.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept with minor revisions — novelty, significance, technical soundness, clarity, and empirical support outweigh minor issues.
