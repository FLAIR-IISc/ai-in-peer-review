- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems min_X h(A(X))+τ||X||_* under strong convexity and Lipschitz gradient assumptions.  
- Generalization: extends error-bound based linear convergence results from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm case.  
- Technical novelty: develops new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization and analyzes spectral properties near the optimal set.  
- Strength: substantial technical contribution with clear, well-structured presentation.  
- Weakness: contribution is mainly technical with limited empirical validation.  
- Request: include a numerical example demonstrating linear convergence to strengthen impact.  
- Clarification needed: specify how constants κ1…κ4 depend on the choices of α̲ and ᾱ.  
- Minor fix: clarify the confusing sequence of inequalities before equation (13).  
- Assumption discussion: authors should discuss cases where strong convexity fails but empirical linear convergence still occurs.  
- Extension: outline how the analysis might be adapted or extended to ADMM.  
- Clarity assessment: paper is well-written overall but has a few technical points to improve.  
- Originality note: clarify dependencies and distinctions from prior error-bound work, especially Tseng’s results.  
- Significance: results could impact efficient algorithms for trace-norm problems if empirical support is provided.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarifications, fixes, and add a numerical demonstration.
