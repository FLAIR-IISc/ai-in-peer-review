- Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized risk minimization without assuming strong convexity.
- Main technical contribution: a novel simultaneous diagonalization observation that reduces the matrix problem to a vector case.
- Builds on and follows the Luo & Tseng (2010) framework to extend error-bound techniques to the trace norm.
- Addresses a relevant problem with applications in matrix completion and low-rank approximation.
- Theoretical derivations appear rigorous and leverage existing theory effectively.
- Numerical experiments illustrate the theoretical results but do not show marked improvement over existing solvers.
- Novelty is moderate: the diagonalization step is non-trivial, but the overall approach is incremental relative to prior frameworks.
- Practical significance is limited because linear convergence is asymptotic and requires specific conditions that may be restrictive.
- Missing key justification in the appendix: the bound |R_k| <= κ |X_k - X_{k+1}| (Appendix line 085) lacks a clear explanation of the choice of κ3 and α < 1.
- Presentation could be improved: the main result (Theorem 3.2) is buried amid technical proofs and should be stated earlier for clarity.
- Section 4.1.2’s Cases A, B, C need clearer explanation of how ǫ guarantees equations (25) and (26) hold in each case.
- Notation can be inconsistent/confusing (e.g., use of X̄ vs. X̃k for related concepts).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important theoretical question, provides a valuable technical trick, and supports results with proofs and experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: moderate originality, limited practical impact, incomplete proof detail in the appendix, and presentation issues.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on fixing the appendix justification, restructuring to highlight main results, and clarifying limitations and future work.
