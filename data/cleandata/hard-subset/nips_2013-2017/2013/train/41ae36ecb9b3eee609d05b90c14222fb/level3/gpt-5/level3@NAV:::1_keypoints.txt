- Main contribution: proved a Lipschitzian error bound for nuclear-norm composite problems and, via Luo–Tseng framework, established Q-linear objective and R-linear iterate convergence of proximal gradient for f(X)=h(A(X)).  
- Technical novelty: spectral alignment property (Proposition 4.2) and simultaneous SVD/perturbation device enabling a restricted invertibility argument for the trace norm.  
- Relation to prior work: generalizes error-bound–based linear convergence results from ℓ1/group-Lasso to the trace norm, building on established Luo–Tseng/Tseng & Yun approaches.  
- Soundness: arguments appear technically sound and largely clear, with nontrivial technical devices.  
- Attribution: closely mirrors prior proofs (e.g., Luo–Tseng machinery and parts of Lemma 4.1); must cite exact lemmas from Tseng (2009) and related papers and explicitly separate standard versus new steps (isolate Proposition 4.2).  
- Clarity issue: sequence of inequalities before (13) is confusing — the final gap follows directly from convexity and −Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||_*; simplify/correct that step.  
- Constants: clarify how κ1,…,κ4 depend on α̲, ᾱ, and Lf, and state whether backtracking enforces 0<α̲≤αk≤ᾱ<1/Lf.  
- Originality: moderate — technically nontrivial extension to trace norm but methodologically builds on known error-bound analyses.  
- Significance: useful for understanding local PG behavior in nuclear-norm problems and for algorithm design, but empirical validation is limited.  
- Requested additions: include a numerical example (semi-log plot of objective gap vs iterations) in a non-strongly-convex setting (e.g., matrix completion) showing linear slopes under varying stability and discuss failure cases (lack of local curvature or breakdown of restricted invertibility).  
- Requested additions (ADMM): discuss extending the error-bound argument to ADMM, specifying conditions (penalty parameter choices, exact subproblem solutions) under which ADMM inherits linear convergence via metric subregularity/error bounds, and cite relevant ADMM linear-convergence literature.  
- Pros: clear, rigorous extension; new spectral argument of independent interest.  
- Cons: mainly a technical extension with thin empirical support and insufficiently precise attribution and constant-dependence statements.
