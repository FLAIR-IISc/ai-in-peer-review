- Establishes linear (R-linear) convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems and provides a new Lipschitzian error bound extending ℓ1/group LASSO results to trace norm.  
- Main theoretical claim: under stated assumptions (f = h∘A, h strongly convex on compacts with Lipschitz gradient), PGM converges R-linearly to the optimal set.  
- Strength: technically sound proof framework and clear presentation of main results.  
- Strength: novel error bound for trace-norm regularization that may be of independent interest.  
- Strength: numerical experiments (semi-log plots) show linear convergence for matrix completion and classification, partially addressing empirical validation.  
- Weakness: proof of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirrors Tseng [20]; need explicit citation and clear statement of differences from that framework.  
- Weakness: unclear how constants κ1,…,κ4 depend on step-size bounds α̲ and ᾱ; authors should clarify these dependencies.  
- Weakness: sequence of inequalities before (13) is confusing — the final inequality follows from convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖_* rather than the shown intermediate step; correct and clarify.  
- Major omission: insufficient discussion and experiments on when assumptions (e.g., strong convexity of h) fail but empirical linear convergence still occurs; add simulations with non-strongly-convex losses and discuss failure cases.  
- Request: discuss/outline how the analysis might extend to ADMM.  
- Overall assessment: technically non-trivial but primarily an incremental extension; needs clearer positioning relative to prior work, more empirical validation, and discussion of theoretical limitations to strengthen significance.
