This paper establishes the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems. This is a significant result, as it holds under assumptions on the loss function that are weaker than strong convexity, a common scenario in many machine learning and signal processing applications like matrix completion. The analysis builds upon the powerful Lipschitzian error bound framework developed by Tseng for vector l1-norm problems [20], answering an open question about its applicability to the non-polyhedral trace norm.

The paper’s primary strength is its solid theoretical contribution to an important and active area of research. Linear convergence is a highly desirable property, and proving it without strong convexity is a valuable achievement. The paper is carefully written, the technical arguments are laid out clearly, and the main proof appears correct. The authors successfully identify the key challenges in extending the error bound technique from the l1-norm to the trace norm—namely, that the entries of a matrix cannot be decoupled like the components of a vector—and cleverly use matrix perturbation theory to address this.

While the work is strong, its clarity and impact could be enhanced with a few revisions. The paper would benefit greatly from a more explicit discussion comparing this work to the vector l1-norm case (e.g., [20,22]). The authors should elaborate on why the existing l1-norm techniques do not directly translate and what core new insights were required. This would better frame the novelty of the contribution, especially since the l1-norm result can be seen as a corollary of this work.

I also suggest reconsidering the title. The term “structured” could be interpreted as referring to the regularizer, whereas the main new contribution concerns the structure of the loss function via the linear operator A. A more direct title might be more appropriate.

Finally, there are several minor points to address:
*   In the experiments, please clarify whether the operator P is a fixed mask or a random sampling operator.
*   A reference appears to be broken at line 337.
*   For reader clarity, it would be helpful to briefly recall the definitions of Q- and R-linear convergence.
*   Please relate the residual R(X) defined in (6) to Tseng’s original definition (46) to aid interpretation.
*   More discussion surrounding the technical Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 would help justify its necessity and role in the broader proof.
