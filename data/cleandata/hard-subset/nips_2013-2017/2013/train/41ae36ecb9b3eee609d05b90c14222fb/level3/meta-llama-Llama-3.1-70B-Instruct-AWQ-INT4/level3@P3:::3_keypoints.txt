- Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized risk minimization.  
- Extends linear convergence beyond strong convexity using Luo and Tseng’s framework and a local error bound.  
- Key technical novelty: observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing analysis to the vector case.  
- Main contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularized problems that does not require strong convexity.  
- Provides a comprehensive, detailed proof based on careful spectral analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically well-organized.  
- Limitation: primarily theoretical with limited immediate algorithmic or practical impact.  
- Originality is moderate—builds on existing frameworks though includes a novel diagonalization insight.  
- Significance is mainly theoretical; asymptotic convergence may not yield clear practical advantages.  
- Suggested future work: study error bounds for more general matrix-norm regularizers and their effects on first-order methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and practical significance.  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10, Overall 7.5/10.
