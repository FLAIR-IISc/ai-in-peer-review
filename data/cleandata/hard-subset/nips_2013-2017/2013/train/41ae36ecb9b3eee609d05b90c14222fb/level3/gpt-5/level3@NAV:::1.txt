Summary and relation to prior work: The paper studies linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PG) for trace-norm–regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)), where A is linear, ∇h is Lipschitz, and h is strongly convex on compact sets. The main technical contribution is a Lipschitzian error bound for the nuclear-norm composite that, combined with the Luo–Tseng framework, yields Q-linear convergence of the objective and R-linear convergence of iterates for fixed step sizes below 1/Lf. This generalizes error-bound–based linear convergence results known for ℓ1/group-Lasso (Tseng; Tseng & Yun; Zhang et al.) to the trace norm. The novelty hinges on a spectral alignment property (Proposition 4.2) enabling a restricted invertibility argument.

Quality: The results appear technically sound and the argument is largely clear. The simultaneous SVD/perturbation device is interesting and nontrivial. However, parts of the convergence proof (e.g., the use of the Luo–Tseng machinery in Theorem 3.2 and elements of Lemma 4.1) closely mirror prior work. Please cite the exact lemmas from Tseng (Math. Program., 2009) and related papers when reusing their outlines, and delineate precisely which steps are standard versus new (e.g., isolate Proposition 4.2 as the key new ingredient).

Clarity: Proofs are mostly well organized. One minor issue: the sequence of inequalities preceding (13) is confusing; the final inequality follows directly from convexity and the subgradient relation −Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||_* (cf. Tseng [20], p. 289), without the intermediate step shown. Please correct. Also clarify how constants κ1,…,κ4 depend on α̲, ᾱ, and Lf, and whether backtracking satisfies 0<α̲≤αk≤ᾱ<1/Lf.

Originality: Moderate. The extension from separable norms to the trace norm is technically nontrivial, but the overall approach builds on established error-bound analyses.

Significance: The result is useful for understanding local behavior of PG in nuclear-norm problems and may inform algorithm design. The empirical validation is limited.

Requested additions:
- Provide a numerical example (semi-log plot of objective gap vs iterations) explicitly in a non-strongly-convex setting (e.g., matrix completion with varying sampling/conditioning), showing linear slopes under different stability levels, and discuss when theory fails (e.g., loss without local curvature on compacts, or when restricted invertibility breaks due to A and subgradient geometry).
- Discuss how the error-bound argument might extend to ADMM, including conditions (penalty parameter, exact subproblems) under which standard ADMM inherits linear convergence via metric subregularity/error bounds; cite relevant ADMM linear-convergence literature.

Pros:
- Clear, rigorous extension establishing linear convergence for nuclear-norm regularization.
- New spectral argument of independent interest.

Cons:
- Primarily a technical extension; empirical support is thin.
- Insufficiently precise attribution and unclear constants.
