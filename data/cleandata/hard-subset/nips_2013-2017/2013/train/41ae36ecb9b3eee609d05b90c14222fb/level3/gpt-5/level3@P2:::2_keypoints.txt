- Main contribution: Proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization without strong convexity of f, via a new local Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η‖R(X)‖F.  
- Technical ingredient: Establishes a restricted invertibility/error bound using spectral alignment at optimality and matrix perturbation arguments.  
- Novelty vs prior work: Fills a gap left by ℓ1/group-LASSO analyses where polyhedrality is used; addresses non-polyhedral nuclear norm.  
- Scope: Covers common losses (least squares, logistic) and explains observed linear rates in settings like matrix completion with nontrivial nullspace.  
- Strength — novelty/usefulness: New error bound for nuclear norm under weak assumptions is an important and original advance.  
- Strength — analysis: Proofs are technically careful; key propositions (simultaneous SVD, Lemma 4.1) are insightful and plausible.  
- Strength — framework: Clean reduction to the Luo–Tseng error-bound framework yields Q-linear decrease of F and R-linear iterate convergence.  
- Strength — practical relevance: Experiments show linear convergence behavior consistent with theory.  
- Strength — presentation: Well-written, organized, and assumptions/scope clearly stated.  
- Weakness — ℓ1 comparison: Needs explicit comparison to ℓ1/group-LASSO error bounds, explanation why those techniques fail here, and whether ℓ1 is a corollary.  
- Weakness — locality: Theorem 3.1 is local; authors should discuss basin of attraction and dependence of constants (η, ε) on level ζ and conditions for eventual global-linear behavior.  
- Weakness — residual mapping: Relate R(X)=Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X to Luo–Tseng/Tseng residuals and clarify step-size scaling (unit step vs αk).  
- Weakness — role of h’s "strong convexity on compact sets": Provide intuition/examples and why this suffices for deriving key inequality (15).  
- Weakness — experiments: Small-scale experiments; report estimated linear-rate constants, relate them to theoretical quantities, and discuss accelerated methods (FISTA) applicability.  
- Weakness — title/wording: Avoid ambiguous use of “structured” for regularizer; emphasize novelty is handling f=h∘A and non-polyhedral spectral structure.  
- Minor clarity/fixes: Remind Q-/R-linear definitions earlier, fix broken/missing reference (line 337), ensure consistent equation references and residual notation, restate prox/shrinkage in appendix.  
- Quality assessment: Technical soundness appears high; main lemmas justified and reduction to Luo–Tseng is standard once error bound holds.  
- Clarity assessment: Generally clear and organized, but add intuition for dense technical Lemma 4.1(c).  
- Originality: High — new error bound for non-polyhedral nuclear norm using spectral perturbation is novel.  
- Significance: High — impacts first-order methods for spectral regularization and explains common empirical behavior.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Resolves open problem, rigorous analysis, clear exposition, relevant experiments, and broad impact potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Empirical evaluation limited, locality/dependence of constants underexplored, and positioning relative to ℓ1/group-LASSO literature needs sharpening.  
- Minor editorial/typos: Clarify P as deterministic masking vs random sampling, fix missing reference metadata, and report logistic-loss Lf/backtracking parameter choices.
