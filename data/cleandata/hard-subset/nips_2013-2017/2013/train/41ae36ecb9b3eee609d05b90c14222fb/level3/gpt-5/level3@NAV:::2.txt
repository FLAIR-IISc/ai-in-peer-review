Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proves asymptotic linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method (PGM) for nuclear (trace) norm regularized problems of the form f(X) = h(A(X)) + τ‖X‖*, under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of f. The key is a Lipschitzian error bound relating dist(X, X*) to the residual ‖Sτ(X − ∇f(X)) − X‖F, enabling application of the Luo–Tseng framework. This resolves an open question of extending Tseng’s error-bound technique beyond polyhedral regularizers to the nonpolyhedral trace norm, via new spectral arguments (simultaneous SVD at optimality and a restricted invertibility property). The result covers square and logistic losses common in ML and signal processing. The paper is clearly written; the main proof appears correct. Experiments corroborate linear tails.

Quality: The theoretical development is careful, with crisp assumptions, a plausible and verifiable error bound, and a sound rate proof. The asymptotic nature and dependence on level-set parameters are appropriately acknowledged; experiments are illustrative though not exhaustive.

Clarity: Generally very good. Some definitions are recalled (Q- and R-linear rates), and the residual is clearly defined. A few presentation issues remain (see minor comments).

Originality: The extension of Lipschitzian error bounds to the trace norm is genuinely novel and addresses a long-standing gap. The simultaneous SVD observation and perturbation-based analysis are insightful.

Significance: Important for practitioners using PGM in matrix completion, multi-task learning, and related problems where strong convexity fails. Likely to influence analyses of other nonpolyhedral matrix regularizers.

Requests and suggestions:
- Please add a clear comparison to the vector ℓ1-norm case and explain why ℓ1 techniques (polyhedral epigraph, separable group-splitting) do not directly transfer to the trace norm; explicitly state how your result implies the known ℓ1 error bound as a corollary (e.g., via a diagonal embedding x ↦ Diag(x)) and discuss the incremental novelty versus this corollary.
- Strengthen the discussion around Section 3 to provide intuition for Theorem 3.2 and for the technical restricted-invertibility step (Lemma 4.1(c)): what properties of A and the local spectral structure are essential?

Pros:

- Careful, largely self-contained analysis; correct main proof.
- Broad applicability; empirical support.

Cons:
