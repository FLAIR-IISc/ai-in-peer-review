Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper studies the composite optimization problem minX f(X)+τ||X||*, where f(X)=h(A(X)), A is linear, and h has Lipschitz continuous gradient and is strictly convex on any convex compact subset of its domain. The main result is an asymptotic linear convergence rate for the proximal gradient method (PGM) applied to this problem without assuming strong convexity of f. The proof hinges on establishing a new local Lipschitzian error bound that controls dist(X, X*) by the proximal residual ||Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X||F. Combining this bound with the Luo–Tseng analysis framework yields Q-linear convergence in objective values and R-linear convergence of iterates under standard step-size conditions (αk ∈ (α, ᾱ) ⊂ (0,1/Lf)).

This extends a line of results that obtain linear convergence of first-order methods via error bounds and metric subregularity in structured convex problems. Previous linear convergence results for PGM/coordinate descent largely required polyhedral nonsmooth terms such as ℓ1 or group lasso penalties (e.g., Tseng, Tseng & Yun, Zhang et al.) or strong convexity of f. In contrast, the nuclear norm has a nonpolyhedral epigraph, and the authors’ main technical novelty is to obtain an error bound in this nonpolyhedral setting by exploiting spectral structure: optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors (Proposition 4.2), enabling a perturbation analysis of nearby iterates. The results cover common losses (least squares, logistic) used in matrix completion/classification and connect to widely used algorithms for nuclear norm problems (e.g., proximal/SVT-based methods).

Quality:
- Technical soundness: The overall proof strategy is sound and well aligned with the Luo–Tseng framework. The new ingredients—simultaneous SVD at optimality and the restricted invertibility bound—are nontrivial and address the key obstacle posed by nonpolyhedral geometry. The local error bound is used appropriately to obtain asymptotic linear convergence. The numerical experiments (matrix completion with square loss, matrix classification with logistic loss) empirically support linear convergence near the solution set.
- Rigor/detail: Some dependencies and intermediate steps should be clarified:
  - The constants κ1,…,κ4 in Theorem 3.2: please make explicit their dependence on α, ᾱ, and Lf (and any other problem constants), and whether they can be chosen uniformly over level sets.
  - The sequence of inequalities leading up to equation (18) (before the items summarized around (13)) is confusing. The key step follows from convexity and −Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X̄||* (cf. the subgradient inequality; see also Tseng [22], p. 289 for an analogous argument). Please streamline and cite appropriately.
  - State clearly that the error bound (and hence linear rate) is local: it holds once iterates enter a neighborhood where the residual is below ε, and therefore the observed linear phase is asymptotic.

Clarity:
The paper is generally well organized. The statement of assumptions and the residual map are clear. Suggestions to improve clarity:
- Unify notation for the regularization parameter (τ versus μ used in the shrinkage operator and in experiments) and for step size choices (fixed α vs. backtracking). The theorem uses ᾱ<1/Lf; backtracking should enforce αk≤1/Lf to meet the assumptions—please state this explicitly.
- When invoking Luo–Tseng [10], specify the precise conditions verified (descent, sufficient decrease, error bound) and how they map to inequalities (9)–(11).
- Minor: check figure numbering/captions and ensure all axes and parameters (n, θ, τ) are labeled.

Originality:
The main novelty is the Lipschitzian error bound for nuclear norm regularization, a nonpolyhedral case where existing error-bound proofs for ℓ1/group lasso do not transfer. The convergence theorem is then an application of a known framework. While the high-level template follows prior work (Tseng, Tseng & Yun, Zhang et al.), the spectral-structure argument and restricted invertibility for the trace norm are substantive additions. The paper should more explicitly delineate which parts follow existing templates and which parts are new, with precise attributions and contrasts to [22, 23, 25].

Significance:
The result is relevant to a broad class of low-rank learning problems and helps explain the empirically observed linear local convergence of PGM/SVT-type algorithms without strong convexity. The error bound is likely of independent interest and may inform analyses of other first-order methods (e.g., coordinate variants, proximal Newton, ADMM). The impact is primarily theoretical but addresses widely used models.

Requested revisions and suggestions:
- Experiments:
  - Include an additional simulation where f is not strongly convex (e.g., matrix completion with a sampling operator A having a controlled nullspace or varying conditioning), and show semi-log plots of objective and distance to X*. Vary “stability” by changing the sampling ratio or conditioning of A to illustrate when and how quickly the iterates enter the local linear regime.
  - Discuss when the theory may fail or the linear regime is delayed: e.g., if h is not strictly convex on compacts (hinge-like losses), if step sizes exceed 1/Lf, near points with singular value multiplicity changes, or when the solution set has flat directions that weaken the restricted invertibility (16).
- Attribution and proof structure:
  - Make explicit which elements of the error-bound-to-linear-rate pipeline follow Luo–Tseng [10], Tseng [22], Tseng & Yun, and Zhang et al. [25], and add precise citations at the points where the arguments mirror prior work.
- Constants and assumptions:
  - Clarify the dependence of κ1,…,κ4 on α, ᾱ, Lf, τ, and norms of A and ∇h (L). If possible, provide explicit formulas or bounds, and state the size of the neighborhood (ε) in Theorem 3.1 in terms of these constants.
  - Comment on whether accelerated variants (e.g., FISTA with restart) inherit linear local rates under your error bound, and under what restart conditions.
- Extension to ADMM:
  - Briefly outline how your error bound and restricted invertibility could be used to derive linear convergence of ADMM for nuclear-norm regularized problems under standard assumptions (e.g., exact subproblem solves, bounded multipliers), referencing the role of error bounds/metric subregularity in ADMM analyses. Even a short discussion section would be valuable.

Pros:
- Establishes a new local error bound for trace norm regularization (nonpolyhedral).
- Delivers asymptotic linear convergence of PGM without strong convexity of f.
- Covers widely used losses (square, logistic) and shows supportive experiments.
- Proofs are mostly clear and technically competent.

Cons:
- Primarily a technical extension of known frameworks; novelty is concentrated in the error bound.
- Experimental section is limited; needs a targeted non–strongly convex example and discussion of failure modes.
- Some proof steps and constant dependencies are opaque; clearer attribution to prior templates is needed.

Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on clarifying the proof dependencies, detailing constant dependencies, and adding the requested experiment and discussion on ADMM and assumption gaps. The theoretical contribution (error bound for the nuclear norm) is meaningful and of interest to the NIPS community working on convex low-rank learning and first-order methods.
