- Addresses linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss.
- Shows asymptotic linear convergence for losses of the form f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex and smooth.
- Main technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization.
- Key insight: optimal solution X̄ and -∇f(X̄) share singular vectors, enabling spectral analysis near the optimum.
- Uses simultaneous diagonalization (Proposition 4.2) to circumvent difficulties extending ℓ1 error bound techniques to trace norm.
- Applies the Luo and Tseng convergence framework to establish the linear rate.
- Paper is technically sound and mathematically rigorous with clear exposition of main ideas.
- Minor gap: justification for constant κ3 in Theorem 3.2 (equation (11)) and the step-size α < 1/Lf needs explicit clarification.
- Presentation could be improved by stating the main contribution more prominently and reorganizing the technical proof placement.
- Theoretical significance is strong, but the asymptotic nature limits immediate practical/algorithmic impact.
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion, classification) validate the predicted asymptotic linear convergence.
- Properly relates to and cites relevant prior work (Sra 2012, Zhang et al. 2013) and positions contribution within literature.
- Strengths: novel simultaneous diagonalization insight, clear theoretical development, solid experimental validation, good connections to related work.
- Weaknesses: minor proof gap (κ3), convoluted placement of main result, limited practical significance due to asymptotic focus.
- Overall assessment: a valuable incremental theoretical contribution suitable for NIPS, advancing understanding of first-order methods for low-rank matrix problems.
