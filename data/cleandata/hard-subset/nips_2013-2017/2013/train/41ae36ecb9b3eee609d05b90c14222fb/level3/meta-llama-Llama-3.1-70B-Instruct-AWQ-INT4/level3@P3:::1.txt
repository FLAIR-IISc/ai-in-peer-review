This paper proposes a new error bound for the proximal gradient method (PGM) applied to a class of trace norm-regularized problems. The main contribution is the establishment of linear convergence of the PGM for a large class of loss functions, which generalizes prior results from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through numerical experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification problems.

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their results, making it easy for the reader to follow. The proofs are mostly clear, although some of the notation and terminology may require additional explanation for non-experts.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to establish linear convergence of the PGM without requiring strong convexity of the loss function. This is a significant contribution, as many loss functions used in machine learning are not strongly convex. The authors also provide a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem, which may be of independent interest.

However, the paper's contribution is primarily technical, and it may be perceived as an extension of existing work rather than a novel approach. The authors could benefit from providing more empirical validation of their results, as the numerical experiments presented are limited to two specific problems.

The paper's clarity is generally good, but some minor issues were noted. The sequence of inequalities before equation (13) is confusing, and the final inequality follows from convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||_* rather than the intermediate step shown. Additionally, the dependencies of the constants κ1, …, κ4 on the choice of α̲ and ᾱ are unclear and could be clarified.

The paper's originality is moderate, as it builds upon existing work in the field. However, the authors provide a clear discussion of how their work differs from previous contributions and adequately reference related work.

The significance of the paper's results is high, as they address a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The authors demonstrate the importance of their approach through numerical experiments and provide a clear discussion of the implications of their results.

In conclusion, this paper provides a significant contribution to the field of convex optimization, establishing linear convergence of the PGM for a large class of loss functions. While the paper's contribution is primarily technical, it has the potential to impact the development of fast algorithms for solving trace norm-regularized problems.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of convex optimization.
* The authors establish linear convergence of the PGM for a large class of loss functions.
* The paper's results have the potential to impact the development of fast algorithms for solving trace norm-regularized problems.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper's contribution is primarily technical, and it may be perceived as an extension of existing work rather than a novel approach.
* The authors could benefit from providing more empirical validation of their results.
* Some minor issues with notation and terminology may require additional explanation for non-experts.

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
