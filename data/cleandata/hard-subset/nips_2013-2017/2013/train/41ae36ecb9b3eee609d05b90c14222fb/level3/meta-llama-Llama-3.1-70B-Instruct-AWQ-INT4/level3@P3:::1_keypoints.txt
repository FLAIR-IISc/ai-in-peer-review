- Proposes a new error bound for the proximal gradient method (PGM) on trace norm-regularized problems.  
- Establishes linear convergence of PGM for a broad class of loss functions, generalizing lasso/group-lasso results to the trace norm.  
- Introduces a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems that may be of independent interest.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via numerical experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and claims are well supported.  
- Presentation is clear and well organized, making results easy to follow.  
- Proofs are mostly clear but some notation/terminology may confuse non-experts.  
- Key strength: linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of the loss.  
- Contribution is primarily technical and could be perceived as an extension of prior work rather than a novel paradigm.  
- Empirical validation is limited to two problems; more experiments would strengthen the paper.  
- Minor clarity issues: confusing sequence of inequalities before Eq. (13) and an incorrect intermediate justification.  
- Constants κ1…κ4 dependencies on α̲ and ᾱ are unclear and should be clarified.  
- Originality is moderate but the paper adequately situates and references related work.  
- Significance is high given the difficult problem addressed and potential impact on fast algorithms; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
