- Paper studies linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems.  
- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of PGM without assuming strong convexity, via a new Lipschitzian error bound.  
- Technical novelty: uses simultaneous diagonalization to reduce the matrix problem to a vector case and establish a local error bound.  
- Analysis framework: builds on Luo and Tseng’s framework for convergence analysis.  
- Gap: appendix lacks justification that chosen κ3 (e.g., α < 1) implies R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F; needs fixing.  
- Readability: paper is readable but should state main claims upfront and defer technical error-bound proofs for clarity.  
- Originality: moderate — simultaneous diagonalization is new, but much follows existing work.  
- Significance: mainly theoretical with limited algorithmic or practical impact; asymptotic result weakens practical relevance.
