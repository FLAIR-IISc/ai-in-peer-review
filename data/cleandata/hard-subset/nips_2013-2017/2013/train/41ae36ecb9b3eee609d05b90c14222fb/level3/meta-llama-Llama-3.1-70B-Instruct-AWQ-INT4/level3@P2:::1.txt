This paper proposes a new result on the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for a class of trace norm-regularized problems. The authors establish that when the loss function f takes the form f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is an arbitrary linear operator and h is strictly convex with certain smoothness and curvature properties, the PGM for solving the problem has an asymptotic linear rate of convergence.

The main contribution of this paper is the derivation of a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem, which is a key ingredient in the proof of linear convergence. The authors demonstrate that this error bound is a crucial extension of prior work on error-bound-based linear convergence results, which were limited to lasso/group lasso problems.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs are mostly clear. However, the reviewer notes that the outlines of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirror Tseng's work, and the authors should cite and differentiate from the original more precisely. Additionally, the reviewer requests clarification on how the constants κ1, …, κ4 depend on the choice of α̲ and ᾱ.

The reviewer also suggests that the authors provide a numerical example demonstrating linear convergence, as the paper's empirical validation is perceived as insufficient. The authors have since added a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but the PGM converges linearly, which addresses this concern.

A minor technical issue is noted in the sequence of inequalities before (13), which is confusing and can be simplified by citing the relevant result from Rockafellar's book.

The reviewer requests that the authors discuss when theoretical assumptions (e.g., strong convexity) fail but empirical linear convergence still occurs, and outline how the analysis might extend to the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM). This discussion would strengthen the paper by providing a more comprehensive understanding of the results and their limitations.

Overall, the paper is technically sound and makes a non-trivial contribution to the field. However, the reviewer suggests that the authors address the above concerns to improve the paper's clarity, completeness, and impact.

Strengths:

* Establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm for trace-norm regularization, extending previous results.
* Derives a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem, which is a key ingredient in the proof of linear convergence.

Weaknesses:

* Perceived as a mostly technical contribution without sufficient empirical validation.
* Minor technical issues and areas for clarification, as noted above.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper makes a non-trivial technical contribution to the field, extending previous results on error-bound-based linear convergence to the trace norm.
* The authors have addressed the reviewer's concerns by adding a numerical example demonstrating linear convergence and discussing the limitations of their theoretical assumptions.

Arguments for rejection:

* The paper's empirical validation is still limited, and the reviewer suggests that more comprehensive experiments would strengthen the paper.
* The minor technical issues and areas for clarification, as noted above, should be addressed to improve the paper's clarity and completeness.

Rating: 7/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
