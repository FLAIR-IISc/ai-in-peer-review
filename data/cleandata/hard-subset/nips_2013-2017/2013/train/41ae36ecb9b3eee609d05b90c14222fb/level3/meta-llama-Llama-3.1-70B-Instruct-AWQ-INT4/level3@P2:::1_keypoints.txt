- Proposes linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems where f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex and certain smoothness/curvature.  
- Main contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization, enabling the linear-convergence proof.  
- Extends error-bound-based linear convergence results beyond lasso/group lasso to trace-norm problems.  
- Proofs are mostly clear and the paper is well written.  
- Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirror Tseng’s work; authors should cite and differentiate more precisely.  
- Require clarification on how constants κ1,…,κ4 depend on the choice of α̲ and ᾱ.  
- Reviewer originally requested a numerical example demonstrating linear convergence; authors added a simulation showing linear convergence without strong convexity.  
- Minor technical confusion in inequalities before (13); suggest simplifying by citing Rockafellar.  
- Ask for discussion of when theoretical assumptions (e.g., strong convexity) fail but empirical linear convergence still occurs.  
- Recommend outlining how the analysis might extend to ADMM to broaden impact.  
- Paper is technically sound and makes a non-trivial contribution to the field.  
- Weakness: perceived as mostly technical with limited empirical validation (more experiments desired).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; rating 7/10.
