- Paper proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems without assuming strong convexity of f.  
- Analysis establishes a Lipschitzian error bound (LEB) linking distance to optimum with proximal residual, enabling Luo–Tseng framework application.  
- Novel spectral-structure arguments show optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors, permitting perturbation analysis despite nuclear norm non-polyhedrality.  
- Extends Tseng’s LEB program from polyhedral regularizers (ℓ1, group LASSO) to the nuclear norm, resolving a known open question.  
- Results apply to common losses (square, logistic) and arbitrary linear operators A, covering practical ML/sensing settings.  
- Writing and exposition are clear; main proof appears correct and carefully organized.  
- Strength: first linear-rate result for PGM on nuclear-norm problems without strong convexity—high significance for ML/signal processing.  
- Technical novelty: new LEB for trace-norm via spectral alignment and perturbation arguments.  
- Empirics are limited but consistent with theory (semilog plots showing linear regimes near solution).
