- Studies linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm-regularized problems, extending beyond strong convexity via a local error bound to obtain asymptotic linear convergence.  
- Main technical novelty: an observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the problem to the vector case.  
- Paper is technically sound overall.  
- Gap in appendix: authors must justify why the chosen κ3 (e.g., α < 1) implies R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Readable but would benefit from clearer presentation: state main claims upfront and defer technical proof of the local error bound.  
- Suggest presenting the derivation of the linear convergence rate more straightforwardly.  
- Originality is moderate: simultaneous diagonalization is new, but the overall approach follows existing work.  
- Significance is mainly theoretical with limited algorithmic/practical impact; asymptotic results weaken practical relevance.  
- Related work: the inequality |R_k| ≤ κ|X_k − X_{k+1}| was proved in S. Sra (NIPS 2012); this paper extends such results to trace norm-regularized risk minimization.  
- Overall assessment: incremental theoretical contribution to optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel diagonalization observation, established local error bound, and technical soundness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: moderate originality, limited algorithmic impact, and reduced practical significance due to asymptotic nature.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions — improve clarity, justify κ3 implication, and discuss practical significance.
