- Proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method for nuclear-norm-regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Central technical novelty: a local Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η‖Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X‖F established via spectral alignment and restricted invertibility.  
- Uses the Luo–Tseng framework to translate the error bound into Q-linear objective decrease and R-linear iterate convergence.  
- Addresses an open gap: extends error-bound theory from polyhedral nonsmooth terms (ℓ1/group LASSO) to the nonpolyhedral nuclear norm.  
- Technical development is careful and largely self-contained; core lemmas are clear though some proofs are deferred to the supplement.  
- Assumes step sizes bounded away from zero and ≤1/Lf; obtains local/asymptotic linear rates; numerical experiments corroborate linear behavior near optimality.  
- Paper is well organized and readable; assumptions on h are explicit and motivated by common losses (squared, logistic).  
- Originality: first known linear convergence result for PGM with nuclear-norm regularization without strong convexity; the error bound is likely of independent interest.  
- Significance: broadens theoretical understanding for first-order methods on low-rank problems and has practical implications (step-size/restart strategies, local guarantees).  
- Strengths: important local result, novel technique overcoming nonpolyhedral challenges, general framework use, applicability to common ML problems, empirical support.  
- Weaknesses: convergence constants (η, ε, contraction) are unquantified and the result is local/asymptotic.  
- Need clarifications: whether backtracking ensures a uniform positive lower step-size in practice, robustness to inexact prox (partial SVDs), and potential extensions to nonsmooth losses or smooth surrogates.  
- Experimental limitations: small-scale/synthetic experiments only; recommend a real larger-scale dataset and comparison with accelerated PGM/restart behavior.  
- Minor fixes: ensure key proofs (e.g., Proposition 4.3) in supplement, add an intuitive note on shared singular vectors, clarify uniqueness/compactness statements, and correct typographical/reference issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — significant, original, and well-presented contribution with suggested clarifications to improve practical guidance.
