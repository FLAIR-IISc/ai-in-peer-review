- Studies convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems, proving linear convergence for a broad class of losses without strong convexity.
- Main technical contribution: a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization enabling linear convergence.
- Resolves an open question from prior work about existence of error bounds for the non-polyhedral trace norm.
- Proof technique leverages matrix perturbation theory and the key observation that optimal solutions and gradients share singular vectors.
- Analysis overcomes technical challenges arising from the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm.
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion, classification) empirically demonstrate linear convergence consistent with theory.
- Writing and organization are clear, making technical content accessible.
- Highly original: first to show linear convergence of PGM for trace-norm problems without strong convexity.
- Technique may generalize to other matrix norm regularization problems, increasing potential impact.
- Significance is strong given widespread use of trace norm in ML (matrix completion, multi-task learning).
- Assumption f(X)=h(A(X)) is somewhat restrictive but covers common losses (square, logistic) and is reasonably justified.
- Strengths: novel error bound, rigorous proofs, theoretical and empirical validation; Weakness: limited loss-class assumption.
- Overall recommendation: substantial contribution, meets acceptance criteria, recommend acceptance.
