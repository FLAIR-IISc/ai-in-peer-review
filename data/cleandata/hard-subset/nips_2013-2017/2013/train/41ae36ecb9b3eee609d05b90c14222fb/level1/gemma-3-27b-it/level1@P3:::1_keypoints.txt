- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization without requiring strong convexity — a significant and novel result.  
- Well-motivated problem statement addressing a key limitation in prior convergence analyses (e.g., matrix completion).  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear introduction and appropriate related work coverage.  
- Core technical contribution is a Lipschitzian error bound (Theorem 3.1) enabling the convergence result.  
- Proof strategy leverages matrix perturbation theory to handle the non-polyhedral nature of the trace norm.  
- Use of Luo and Tseng’s framework is appropriate and aligns with the established error bound.  
- Overall approach appears technically sound and grounded in established matrix-analysis tools.  
- Detailed proofs (Lemma 4.1, Propositions 4.2 & 4.3) are placed in the supplement, limiting on-paper verifiability.  
- Supplementary material should be self-contained; more critical proof details belong in the main text.  
- Numerical experiments show linear convergence but are limited in scope (mainly matrix completion and classification).  
- Experiments do not fully test cases where strong convexity is violated nor compare against strong-convexity-dependent methods.  
- Parameter tuning (e.g., backtracking line search) is not fully described, reducing reproducibility.  
- Clarity can be improved: motivation for Assumption 2.1(b) and the intuitive link between residual map R and distance to the optimum need elaboration.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong novelty, broad significance to ML, apparent soundness, and formal theoretical rigor.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of main-text proof detail, limited numerical validation, and minor clarity issues.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, provided authors include more detailed proof sketches in the main text or a fully self-contained supplement and address minor clarifications.
