- Problem & main result: Proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient methods for min_X f(X)+τ||X||* with f=h(A(X)) without assuming strong convexity of f for common losses (square, logistic) and arbitrary linear A.
- Key technical contribution: Establishes a new local Lipschitzian error bound relating dist(X, X*) to the residual ||Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X||F.
- Technical mechanism: Uses a spectral alignment property at optimality (shared singular vectors) plus matrix perturbation and a restricted invertibility inequality to overcome the nuclear norm's nonpolyhedrality.
- Convergence guarantees: Derives Q-linear convergence of objective values and R-linear convergence of iterates using the Luo–Tseng framework for step sizes in (0, 1/Lf) bounded away from 0.
- Relation to prior work: Extends linear-rate understanding from strongly convex and polyhedral-regularizer settings to nuclear norm regularization where prior results were only sublinear.
- Assumptions on h: Requires C1, Lipschitz gradient, strict convexity, and strong convexity on compact subsets — sufficient to get positive curvature on bounded iterate sets.
- Technical soundness: Main theorems are carefully stated; the contradiction-based proof and use of Luo–Tseng are convincing.
- Locality of results: The error bound and linear rate are local — valid for points in bounded level sets with sufficiently small residual.
- Completeness/organization: Core arguments are present (Propositions 4.2/4.3) though many technical details are in the supplement; step-size issues are standard and addressable via backtracking.
- Empirics: Experiments are minimal and illustrative (small synthetic problems), showing local linear trends but lacking comparisons, scalability tests, and robustness analyses.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written; some technical parts (spectral alignment, case analyses) would benefit from more intuition about active rank manifold and Null(A) directions.
- Originality: Novel local error bound for nuclear norm and original use of spectral alignment to handle nonpolyhedral geometry.
- Significance: Addresses an open theoretical gap with practical relevance to matrix completion and related problems; error bound may inform analyses of other first-order methods.
- Strengths: Resolves open question on nuclear-norm error bounds; provides clean general statements for many losses and arbitrary A; solid application of established convergence framework.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: Clarify neighborhood size and explicit dependence of constants (η, ε, rate) on problem data; expand experiments (accelerated PGM, ADMM, larger scale, noise/τ sensitivity).
- Additional suggestions: Discuss relation to KL/Attouch–Bolte frameworks and partly smooth/identification results; clarify backtracking guarantee (αk ≥ α >0) and behavior once in attraction region; expand on non-uniqueness and active singular subspace identification.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, nontrivial theoretical contribution with broad applicability that fills a gap in convergence theory for a widely used regularizer.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited empirical validation and lack of quantified constants for local linear rates reduce immediate practical guidance.
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
