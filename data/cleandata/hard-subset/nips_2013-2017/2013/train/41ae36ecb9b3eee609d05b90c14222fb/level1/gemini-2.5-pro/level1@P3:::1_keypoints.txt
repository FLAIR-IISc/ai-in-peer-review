- Main contribution: Proves PGM attains asymptotic linear convergence for problems min_X h(A(X)) + τ||X||_* even when f(X)=h(A(X)) is not strongly convex, via a new Lipschitzian error bound.

- Technical innovation: Derives a novel error bound linking distance to the optimal set with a residual map, overcoming non-polyhedral and non-separable challenges of the trace norm.

- Key insight: Shows a shared singular-vector structure between an optimal solution X* and the gradient -∇f(X*) (Proposition 4.2), which is central to the analysis.

- Analytical framework: Leverages Luo and Tseng’s error-bound-to-linear-convergence machinery to establish linear convergence of PGM.

- Proof technique: Uses matrix perturbation theory and careful SVD analysis to handle global dependence of singular values.

- Empirical validation: Numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification confirm an initial sublinear phase followed by the predicted linear convergence.

- Quality: Rigorous, technically deep, and complete theoretical development with solid experimental support.

- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; notation and argument structure are clear and accessible.

- Originality: Highly original approach addressing an open problem for trace-norm regularization; non-incremental theoretical advance.

- Significance: High impact for optimization and ML communities—provides first formal explanation for empirically observed linear convergence without strong convexity.

- Weakness 1: Analysis assumes h is strictly convex (strongly convex on compacts); the necessity or possible relaxation of this assumption should be discussed.

- Weakness 2: Convergence is asymptotic/local (error bound requires small residual); the local nature should be more explicit in abstract/introduction.

- Weakness 3: The technical proof’s cases (A,B,C) lack intuitive explanation in the main text and could benefit from brief physical interpretation.

- Pros for acceptance: Solves an important theoretical problem, introduces a novel and powerful error bound, and is likely to influence future work.

- Cons for acceptance: No significant negatives; only minor clarifications and added intuition are suggested.

- Recommendation: Strong accept.
