- Shows linear convergence of Proximal Gradient Method (PGM) for trace-norm regularization without requiring strong convexity.  
- Main technical contribution: a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the trace-norm-regularized problem.  
- Motivates problem via key applications (matrix completion, multi-task learning) and identifies a literature gap.  
- Positions results against prior work (Beck & Teboulle 2009, Nesterov 2013) and claims to overcome their limitations.  
- Uses a clever matrix perturbation argument to handle the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm.  
- Proof is technically rigorous and builds on established techniques.  
- Empirical results on matrix completion and classification corroborate the theoretical linear convergence.  
- Writing is dense and heavy with technical detail, reducing readability for non-experts.  
- Suggests streamlining the presentation, especially the proof of the error bound.  
- Related-work discussion is limited; needs expanded comparison to existing error-bound techniques (e.g., Tseng 2001).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical soundness, practical significance, and empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity concerns and insufficient related-work comparison.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to improve clarity and related-work coverage.
