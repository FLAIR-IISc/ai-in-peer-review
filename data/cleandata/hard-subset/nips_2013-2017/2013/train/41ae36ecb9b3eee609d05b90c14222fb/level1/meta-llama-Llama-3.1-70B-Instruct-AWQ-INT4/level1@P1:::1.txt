This paper addresses the problem of minimizing a convex smooth loss function with trace norm regularization, a common problem in machine learning. The authors focus on the proximal gradient method (PGM), a popular method for solving this problem, and provide a new result on its convergence rate. Specifically, they show that for a large class of loss functions, the convergence rate of the PGM is linear, without requiring strong convexity of the loss function.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem, including its applications and the current state of the art. They also provide a detailed explanation of their approach and the key ingredients in their proof.

The main contribution of the paper is the establishment of a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem. This error bound is a crucial component in the proof of the linear convergence of the PGM. The authors also provide a detailed proof of the error bound, which involves a careful analysis of the spectral properties of the points close to the optimal solution set.

The paper also includes numerical experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the PGM in practice. The experiments show that the PGM converges linearly in both the objective value and the solution, which is consistent with the theoretical results.
