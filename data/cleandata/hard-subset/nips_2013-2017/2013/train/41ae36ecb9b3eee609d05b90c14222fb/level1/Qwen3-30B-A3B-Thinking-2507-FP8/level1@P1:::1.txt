

# Peer Review

This paper addresses an important question in optimization for machine learning: the convergence rate of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems. The authors establish that for a large class of loss functions (specifically, those of the form f(X) = h(A(X)) where A is a linear operator and h is strictly convex with certain smoothness properties), the PGM achieves linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of the loss function. The key technical contribution is a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems, which establishes that the distance to the optimal solution set is bounded by the residual norm ||prox_τ(X - ∇f(X)) - X||_F. This resolves an open question regarding error bounds for trace norm regularization, as prior work established similar bounds for ℓ1-norm regularization but not for trace norm due to its non-polyhedral epigraph.

The paper relates well to previous NIPS work. It correctly cites the standard O(1/k) convergence rate for PGM in the general case [19] and the O(1/k^2) rate with acceleration [19], while noting that linear convergence was previously established only under strong convexity assumptions [16]. The paper also correctly situates itself relative to the recent work on error bounds for structured convex optimization [23, 22, 25], which established similar results for ℓ1-norm and group LASSO regularization but not for trace norm.

**Strengths:**
- **Quality:** The paper is technically sound with a rigorous proof of the error bound and linear convergence. The assumptions are well-justified with examples from matrix completion and multi-task learning. The proof of Lemma 4.1 and Theorem 3.1 is novel and addresses a significant gap in the literature. The numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification problems support the theoretical findings, showing linear convergence in practice.
  
- **Originality:** This is the first linear convergence result for PGM applied to trace norm-regularized problems without strong convexity assumptions on the loss function. The error bound technique using matrix perturbation theory and the observation that optimal solutions and gradients share singular vectors is particularly novel.

- **Significance:** The results significantly broaden the applicability of linear convergence guarantees to many machine learning problems (matrix completion, multi-task learning, etc.) where the loss function is not strongly convex. The error bound itself could be of independent interest for other optimization problems involving trace norm.

- **Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The main contributions are introduced with appropriate motivation, and the technical details are presented logically. The relationship between the error bound and the linear convergence result is well-explained.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper could provide more concrete examples of loss functions that satisfy the assumptions. While matrix completion (square loss) and multi-task learning (logistic loss) are mentioned, more specific details about the conditions on h would be helpful.
  
- The numerical experiments, while supportive, are somewhat limited. The paper uses fixed step sizes for matrix completion (which is acceptable) but relies on backtracking for matrix classification. It would be valuable to include a comparison with other algorithms (e.g., accelerated PGM) to demonstrate the practical advantage of the linear convergence rate.

- The proof of Lemma 4.1(c) is quite dense, particularly the case analysis in Section 4.1.2. While the supplementary material is referenced, a more intuitive explanation of the key insight (that X̂ = X̃k + ǫQ̄ belongs to Xc) would help readers grasp the main idea.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Resolves an important open question in optimization for machine learning.
- Provides a novel technical contribution (Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm) that is of independent interest.
- The results are theoretically sound and supported by numerical experiments.
- Significantly extends the applicability of linear convergence guarantees to a broad class of machine learning problems.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
- The numerical experiments could be more comprehensive.
- The paper might benefit from more detailed discussion of the practical implications of the linear convergence rate (e.g., how much faster it is than sublinear convergence in practice).

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant theoretical contribution to the optimization literature. The main result is novel, well-justified, and has clear implications for machine learning applications. While the numerical experiments could be expanded, the theoretical contribution is substantial and meets the high standards of NIPS. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and addresses an important open question.

I recommend acceptance. The paper makes a clear contribution to the field of convex optimization for machine learning, and the results are of interest to a wide audience at NIPS. The error bound technique could be applicable to other matrix norm-regularized problems, potentially opening up new research directions.
