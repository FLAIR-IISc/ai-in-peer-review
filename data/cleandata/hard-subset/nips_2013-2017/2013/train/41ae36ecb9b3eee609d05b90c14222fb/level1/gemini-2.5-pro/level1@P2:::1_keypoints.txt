- Studies PGM for trace-norm regularized problems and proves asymptotic linear convergence when f(X)=h(A(X)) even without strong convexity.  
- Main technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to the optimal set to the residual ||R(X)||_F = ||prox(X−∇f(X))−X||_F.  
- Establishing the error bound is hard because the trace norm is non-separable, so prior L1/group-Lasso techniques do not apply.  
- Key insight: an optimal X* and −∇f(X*) share singular vectors, enabling matrix perturbation analysis near the optimum.  
- With this error bound, linear convergence of PGM follows from the Luo–Tseng (1993) framework.  
- Theoretical findings are validated by experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification.  
- Paper exhibits high technical quality; Theorems 3.1 and 3.2 are rigorously proved and form the core contribution.  
- The assumptions f(X)=h(A(X)) and Assumption 2.1 on h are reasonable and cover important ML losses (square, logistic).  
- The paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and guides the reader through a dense proof effectively.  
- The result is novel: first proof of linear convergence for first-order methods with trace-norm regularization without strong convexity.  
- The Lipschitzian error bound is an independent, potentially widely useful analytical tool.  
- Results are significant theoretically and practically, explaining fast PGM behavior and impacting low-rank learning.  
- Pros: resolves a long-standing question, introduces a novel error bound, uses an original proof technique, and is well-motivated and written.  
- Con (minor): convergence result is asymptotic and lacks explicit constants/region characterization, limiting direct practical tuning.  
- Overall verdict: excellent paper, strengths far outweigh minor weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
