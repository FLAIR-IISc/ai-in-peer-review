- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method for f(X)=h(A(X)) with trace-norm regularization without requiring strong convexity, resolving a notable open question.
- Key technical novelty: Establishes a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization using matrix perturbation theory and an observation about singular vectors of optimal solutions.
- Theorem 3.1: Provides a rigorous, carefully constructed proof of the Lipschitzian error bound—identified as the core novelty.
- Theorem 3.2: Derives linear convergence from the new error bound via standard Luo–Tseng techniques, giving a clean convergence result.
- Relation to prior work: Fills a gap left by ℓ1 and group LASSO results because trace-norm’s non-polyhedral epigraph prevented earlier extensions.
- Numerical validation: Experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification demonstrate the predicted linear convergence in practice.
- Writing and presentation: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with consistent notation and comprehensive supplementary proofs.
- Originality: The Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm appears novel and the use of matrix perturbation theory is innovative.
- Significance: Broadens applicability of first-order methods to important ML problems (matrix completion, multi-task learning) where strong convexity fails.
- Weakness — loss form: The assumed loss structure f(X)=h(A(X)) is somewhat restrictive and could use more discussion of generality and limits.
- Weakness — experiments: Empirical evaluation is limited to two problems; wider testing would strengthen claims.
- Weakness — computation: The paper omits discussion of proximal-operator computational cost for large-scale settings, which affects practical applicability.
