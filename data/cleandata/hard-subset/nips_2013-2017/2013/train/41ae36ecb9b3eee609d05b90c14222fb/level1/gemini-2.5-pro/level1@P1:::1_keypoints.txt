- Main contribution: Proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems under weak assumptions f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex and smooth, without strong convexity.

- Key technical innovation: Derives a new Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to optimal set to a residual map measuring first-order optimality violation for the trace norm.

- Novel proof strategy: Uses the shared singular vector space between an optimal X* and -∇f(X*) plus matrix perturbation theory to handle the non-separable trace norm.

- Theoretical payoff: Linear convergence of PGM follows from the error bound via the Luo–Tseng analysis framework.

- Significance: Addresses the gap between observed linear PGM behavior in practice and prior sublinear theory, covering important cases like underdetermined least squares.

- Originality: Theorem 3.1 (error bound) is highly original and resolves an open question about error bounds for trace-norm regularization.

- Technical quality: Assumptions are precise, proofs are rigorous with a convincing contradiction-based restricted invertibility argument (Lemma 4.1c).

- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear motivation, related work, main results up front, and a helpful proof sketch.

- Empirical support: Numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification illustrate the predicted linear convergence tail.

- Weakness — asymptotic result: Convergence is shown only asymptotically; the rate constant and basin size are not characterized, limiting practical guidance.

- Weakness — reliance on supplement: Several key proof steps (inequalities and Proposition 4.3) are deferred to supplementary material, hindering independent verification from the main text.

- Weakness — limited experiments: Experiments are illustrative but lack cases violating assumptions and analyses of how conditioning or sampling ratio affect the linear phase onset.

- Arguments for acceptance: First non-strongly-convex linear convergence guarantee for PGM on trace-norm problems, novel error bound, original techniques likely to inspire further work, and strong writing.

- Arguments against acceptance: Results are asymptotic with limited practical rate insight and experiments are not exhaustive.

- Overall verdict: Excellent, fundamental, and elegant contribution with strengths outweighing minor weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
