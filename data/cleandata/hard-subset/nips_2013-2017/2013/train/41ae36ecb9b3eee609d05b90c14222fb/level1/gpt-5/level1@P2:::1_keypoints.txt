- Paper proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient methods for min f(X)+τ||X||* without assuming strong convexity of f.  
- Main technical contribution: a new local Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to the optimal set to the proximal residual ||Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X||F.  
- Uses spectral structure at optimality (shared singular vectors of X̄ and −∇f(X̄)) plus a refined perturbation argument and a “restricted invertibility” inequality.  
- Combining the EB with the Luo–Tseng framework yields Q-linear objective-value convergence and R-linear iterate convergence for fixed step sizes bounded away from 0 and 1/Lf.  
- Fills a gap: EB-based linear rates were known for polyhedral regularizers (ℓ1, group LASSO) but not for the nonpolyhedral nuclear norm.  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion, logistic matrix classification) show asymptotic linear trends once iterates are near optimal.  
- Technical soundness: main theorems and spectral/perturbation propositions appear correct; EB is local, which is standard.  
- Assumptions: h is C1 with Lipschitz gradient and locally strongly convex on compacts (covers squared and logistic losses); optimal set nonempty; step sizes require 0<αk<ᾱ<1/Lf.  
- Completeness: core proofs included, some technicalities deferred to the supplement; experiments are small-scale but illustrative.  
- Clarity: well organized with helpful discussion of nuclear vs ℓ1 differences; minor issues with figure references and notation (τ vs µ).  
- Originality: the Lipschitzian EB for nuclear-norm regularization and the spectral-perturbation approach are novel.  
- Significance: theoretically and practically important for ubiquitous nuclear-norm models; may inform algorithm design and stopping criteria.  
- Strengths: resolves an open question, handles common losses and arbitrary A, provides explicit rate statements, and overcomes nonpolyhedral challenges.  
- Weaknesses: results are local/asymptotic—EB requires small residual and bounded level set; limited guidance on when linear regime is reached.  
- Suggestion: add a corollary for standard backtracking (argue αk doesn’t vanish), discuss inexact proximal computations (truncated SVDs) and extension to summable/relative errors.  
- Suggestion: quantify EB constants and dependence on spectral gap/rank to make rates more interpretable.  
- Experiments suggestion: scale up problems, add residual plots, and compare to accelerated PGM, ALM/ADMM, and quadratic-regularized methods.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, significant, technically sound contribution bridging prior EB results and nuclear-norm problems.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, no treatment of inexact proximal steps, and asymptotic results lack constant characterization.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept, subject to minor clarifications and expanded empirical support.
