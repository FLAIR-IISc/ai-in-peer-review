- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of Proximal Gradient Method for a class of trace-norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss.
- Key technical tool: Introduces a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm-regularized problems used in the convergence proof.
- Rigor and presentation: Technical development is rigorous, well-organized, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Numerical validation: Provides experiments demonstrating PGM effectiveness, but limited to two specific problems.
- Assumption limitation: Analysis assumes the loss has a specific form, potentially restricting applicability.
- Generalizability concern: The novel Lipschitzian error bound may be difficult to extend to other problems.
- Experimental generalization: Unclear how empirical results will generalize beyond the two tested problems.
- Originality: Novel result and proof technique (Lipschitzian error bound) constitute a genuine contribution.
- Significance: Advances understanding of PGM convergence for an important class of machine learning/optimization problems.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance despite limitations, citing significance and rigor.
