- Addresses minimizing convex smooth loss with trace-norm regularization and improves Proximal Gradient Method (PGM) convergence.  
- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of PGM for a broad class of losses without requiring strong convexity.  
- Introduces a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm–regularized problems, potentially broadly useful.  
- Theoretical results apply to many losses (e.g., squared loss, logistic loss), making them widely relevant.  
- Proofs are rigorous and leverage matrix perturbation theory and the Luo–Tseng framework.  
- Numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification corroborate theoretical findings.  
- Paper is dense; exposition and proofs need clearer presentation for non-experts.  
- Experiments are limited in scope; larger-scale tests and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods are lacking.  
- Practical implications of linear convergence (real-world computational gains) are not fully explored.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel and rigorous theoretical advance, broad applicability with empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity and experimental depth need improvement.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve readability and expand experimental evaluation.
