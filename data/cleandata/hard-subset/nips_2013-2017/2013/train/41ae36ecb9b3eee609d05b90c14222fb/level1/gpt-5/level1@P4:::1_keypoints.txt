- Main contribution: Shows proximal gradient methods achieve asymptotic linear convergence for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X))+τ||X||* without requiring global strong convexity of f.  
- Key technical tool: Proves a local Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η||R(X)||F on bounded level sets for small residuals, where R is the proximal residual.  
- Consequence: Using the error bound and Luo–Tseng framework yields Q-linear convergence of objective values and R-linear convergence of iterates for fixed step sizes in (0,1/Lf).  
- Novelty: Error bound is new for the nonpolyhedral nuclear norm and fills a gap left by prior error-bound results for polyhedral penalties (ℓ1, group LASSO).  
- Structural insight: At any optimum X̄, X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share left/right singular vectors, enabling spectral perturbation analysis (Propositions 4.2, 4.3).  
- Critical lemma: A “restricted invertibility” relation (Lemma 4.1(c)) links distances in variable space to measurement space via A, key for the error bound.  
- Assumptions: h has Lipschitz gradient and is strongly convex on convex compact subsets; A is linear; solution set is nonempty; iterates lie in bounded level sets.  
- Theorems: The local error bound (Theorem 3.1) and linear convergence result (Theorem 3.2) are precisely stated and follow from standard descent and residual bounds plus the new error bound.  
- Scope/limitations: Convergence is asymptotic/local (requires small residual), not providing global linear or explicit non-asymptotic rate constants.  
- Step-size remarks: Step-size condition (αk bounded away from 0 and <1/Lf) is standard, but connection to backtracking and ensuring a uniform lower bound on αk could be stated more explicitly.  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized with consistent notation; suggestion to highlight earlier the role of bounded level sets in ensuring local strong convexity.  
- Experiments: Numerical results on matrix completion and logistic classification illustrate asymptotic linear behavior but are limited in scale and lack comparisons to other methods.  
- Originality/significance: Technically solid and original advance for nuclear-norm problems, expanding when linear convergence of PGM can be expected beyond strong-convexity regimes.  
- Pros: Rigorous new error bound, tailored spectral techniques, assumptions cover common losses, addresses an important open question.  
- Cons: Results are local/asymptotic, no explicit constants or broader empirical validation, and extensions (acceleration, stochastic/inexact steps, other spectral regularizers) are not addressed.  
- Overall assessment: A meaningful, well-executed theoretical contribution relevant to ML optimization, with main weaknesses being the asymptotic nature and limited experiments.
