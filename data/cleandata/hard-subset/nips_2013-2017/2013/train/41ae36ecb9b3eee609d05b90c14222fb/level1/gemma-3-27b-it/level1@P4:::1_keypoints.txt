- Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized convex smooth losses without requiring strong convexity.
- Main technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace-norm-regularized problem used to establish the linear rate.
- Proof leverages established convex optimization techniques and appears rigorous.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and uses standard, mostly clear notation.
- Main results (Theorems 3.1 and 3.2) are concisely and clearly presented.
- Numerical experiments are included and support the theoretical findings.
- Novelty: extending linear convergence to non-strongly convex settings is a significant advance.
- Technical soundness: derivation of the error bound is a key technical achievement.
- Clarity: overall readable, though some detailed proofs are deferred to the supplement.
- Significance: results have practical implications (e.g., matrix completion) and can reduce computational costs.
- Weakness: Assumption 2.1(b) (local strong convexity of h) may be restrictive; discussion on relaxing it is needed.
- Weakness: Full proof complexity (matrix perturbation theory) may limit accessibility; more intuitive explanation in main text would help.
- Weakness: Numerical experiments are limited in scope and lack comparisons to other state-of-the-art algorithms.
- Weakness: Paper does not discuss practical strategies for step-size selection in non-strongly convex settings.
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound contribution, well-positioned in literature, and empirically supported.
- Against acceptance: technical complexity limits accessibility, experiments could be more comprehensive, and some assumptions may be restrictive.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to add intuition and expand experiments.
