- Establishes linear convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method for trace norm regularization without requiring strong convexity, via a novel Lipschitzian error bound using matrix perturbation theory.  
- Results apply to practical problems like matrix completion and multi-task learning, supported by numerical experiments.  
- Significant theoretical contribution expanding PGM applicability when strong convexity is absent.  
- Core novelty: Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm that handles non-polyhedral epigraph and entry dependencies.  
- Paper is very clear, well-structured, and situates itself comprehensively within related work.  
- Numerical experiments (including backtracking line search example) support the theory and validate linear convergence in practice.  
- Assumptions are well-justified and connected to common application scenarios.  
- Proofs (notably Lemma 4.1 and Appendix propositions) are technically dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanation.  
- Limited discussion of practical implications—e.g., actual speedups versus sublinear methods and the role of constant factors/Lipschitz constants.  
- Step-size assumption (αk bounded away from 1/Lf) is restrictive and its impact on convergence needs deeper analysis.  
- Proposition 4.2’s structural assumption on the optimal set may be restrictive; exploring weaker conditions is advisable.  
- Recommendation: strongly accept with minor revisions to address practical implications, step-size sensitivity, and proof intuition.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
