- Main contribution: proves a local Lipschitzian error bound for nuclear-norm regularized composite problems: dist(X, X*) ≤ η ||R(X)||F on bounded level sets near optimality.
- Main consequence: uses Luo–Tseng framework to obtain asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient (Q-linear objective, R-linear iterates) for fixed step sizes without assuming strong convexity of f.
- Technical novelty: establishes the error bound for the nonpolyhedral nuclear norm via spectral alignment at optimality and a restricted invertibility lemma.
- Relation to prior work: connects to accelerated/PGM and linear-rate literature for nuclear norm and fills an open gap where prior error-bound proofs relied on polyhedrality.
- Technical soundness: statements and assumptions are precise; proofs leverage spectral structure appropriately and the convergence argument follows standard recipe once the error bound holds.
- Completeness: core theorems are proved; several technical proofs (ineqs (9)–(11), Props. 4.2–4.3) are deferred to the supplement but main text provides plausible scaffolding.
- Empirics: experiments are small-scale and illustrative, showing asymptotic linear phase but not assessing constants, scalability, or comparisons to baselines.
- Clarity: paper is well organized with a clear proof roadmap, but some sections are dense and would benefit from more intuition and a worked toy example.
- Presentation issue: clarify how the unit-step residual R relates to algorithmic step sizes αk and how constants κ1–κ3 bridge this gap (currently in supplement).
- Minor edits: fix a few missing reference years and typos (e.g., “trace norm1”).
- Originality: high — spectral-alignment-based analysis for a nonpolyhedral regularizer is novel and nontrivial.
- Theoretical significance: high — the error bound closes an important theoretical gap and justifies observed linear behavior of first-order methods.
- Practical impact: moderate-to-high — results justify PGM locally, though constants are existential and rates are asymptotic; extensions to accelerated methods seem feasible.
- Reusability: restricted invertibility lemma and spectral arguments may extend to other spectral regularizers, indicating broader methodological value.
- Pros: resolves an open question, gives linear convergence without strong convexity, technically deep, and applicable to many ML problems (matrix completion, logistic/multitask).
- Cons: convergence is local/asymptotic with no explicit constants, experiments are minimal, key technical proofs deferred, and no discussion of inexact proximal steps or accelerated variants.
- Suggestions/questions: consider extensions to FISTA or inexact proximal steps (e.g., truncated SVD), clarify derivation relating residual and step size, add larger-scale comparisons (accelerated/ADMM), and discuss extensions to other spectral penalties.
- Overall evaluation: strong, original, and technically sound theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance after minor clarifications.
