- Main contribution: Proves a local Lipschitzian error bound for nuclear-norm regularized problems and uses it to show asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method (R-linear iterates, Q-linear objective) under simple step-size bounds.
- Technical approach: Key spectral-structure argument shows optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors; construct a restricted set Xc and a restricted-invertibility inequality via perturbation analysis.
- Problem setting and assumptions: Works for f = h∘A with smooth h (Lipschitz gradient and locally strongly convex on compacts), general linear A, and nuclear-norm regularization τ‖·‖*.
- Relation to prior work: Extends error-bound based linear rates from polyhedral nonsmooth terms (ℓ1/group LASSO) to the non-polyhedral nuclear norm and complements strong-convexity and KL-based analyses.
- Theoretical soundness: Main theorems are well stated and proofs are coherent; error bound is local and sufficient for asymptotic linear convergence.
- Proof/derivation issues: Several technical steps (e.g., Proposition 4.3 and inequalities (9)–(11)) are deferred to the supplement and should be complete for reproducibility.
- Step-size policy concern: The uniform step-size lower bound assumption (αk ≥ α > 0 with ᾱ < 1/Lf) is stronger than typical backtracking guarantees and requires clarification or proof that backtracking satisfies it.
- Locality and dependence: The restricted invertibility and error bound are inherently local (near solutions) and κ depends on problem data; global invertibility cannot hold when A has a kernel.
- Experiments: Small-scale numerical results validate the local linear regime but do not stress-test the theory or compare to accelerated or inexact-SVD methods.
- Clarity and presentation: Generally clear and well organized; however, some references lack bibliographic data and figure numbering is inconsistent.
- Originality: Novel and meaningful contribution—first Lipschitzian error bound for nuclear norm regularization leading to linear PGM rates without global strong convexity.
- Significance: Theoretical impact is strong for algorithm design and stopping rules, though practical relevance is limited by asymptotic/local nature and unspecified constants.
- Pros: Resolves an open question, clean general setting f = h∘A, clear proof strategy with a key spectral insight, and empirical consistency with theory.
- Cons: Rate is local/asymptotic with uncharacterized constants and region of attraction; step-size assumption may not cover standard backtracking; some technical details and experiments are limited.
- Suggestions: Clarify/adapt step-size policy or prove αk ≥ α for backtracking, discuss relation to KL analyses, provide guidance for detecting the linear regime, expand experiments (larger scale, comparisons, inexact SVD), and fix references/figures.
- Recommendation: Lean toward accept for a theory-oriented NeurIPS audience, subject to stronger discussion of step-size policies and presentation polish.
