- Proves a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularized problems, enabling linear convergence of the proximal gradient method without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Main technical insight: at optimal solutions X̄, X̄ and -∇f(X̄) share the same singular vectors, allowing matrix perturbation theory to be applied.  
- Resolves a previously open question by extending error-bound techniques beyond non-smooth terms with polyhedral epigraphs (e.g., ℓ₁).  
- The proof is rigorous and well-structured (notably Lemma 4.1 and Theorem 3.1).  
- Numerical experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification corroborate the theoretical linear convergence.  
- Assumption f(X) = h(A(X)) with h strictly convex is clear but may not cover every practical loss function.  
- Experiments are limited to two problems and do not compare computational complexity against other algorithms (e.g., accelerated proximal gradient, alternating minimization).  
- Paper is clearly written, logically organized, and uses consistent mathematical notation suitable for reproducibility.  
- The contribution is original and significant for ML tasks using trace-norm regularization (matrix completion, multi-task learning, etc.).  
- The established error bound could be of independent interest for future work on matrix norm regularization.  
- Recommendation: accept — meets NIPS criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Minor suggestions: discuss how the Lipschitz constant of ∇f affects practical step-size choice and include algorithmic comparisons in experiments.
