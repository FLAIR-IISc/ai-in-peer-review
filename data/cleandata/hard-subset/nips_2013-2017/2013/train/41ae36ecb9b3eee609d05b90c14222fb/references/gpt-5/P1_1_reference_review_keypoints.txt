- Problem: composite convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization F(X)=f(X)+τ‖X‖*, f(X)=h(A(X)), h strictly convex C1 with Lipschitz gradient and local strong convexity on compacts.

- Main theorem: Proximal gradient method (PGM) with stepsizes αk in (α, ᾱ) ⊂ (0,1/Lf) achieves asymptotic linear convergence—Q-linear objective values and R-linear iterates—without f being strongly convex.

- Key technical contribution: a Luo–Tseng-type local Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η‖R(X)‖F for nuclear-norm regularization on bounded level sets with small proximal residual.

- Novelty: establishing such an error bound for the non-polyhedral nuclear norm was previously open.

- Proof idea: uses simultaneous SVD of optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄), perturbation analysis, and a restricted invertibility estimate.

- Relation to prior work: extends Luo–Tseng framework, complements acceleration/sublinear results, and differs from strong-convexity-based linear rates; relates in spirit to KL-based analyses but is tailored to nuclear norm.

- Experiments: simple tests on matrix completion (square loss) and matrix classification (logistic loss) illustrate asymptotic linear behavior.

- Strength: clear, focused theoretical contribution with broad applicability to common ML losses and general linear operators A.

- Strength: technically nontrivial spectral insights and careful perturbation arguments.

- Strength: clean integration with established convergence machinery yielding tidy theorems.

- Strength: experiments are relevant and illustrate the asymptotic regime.

- Weakness: linear rate is local/asymptotic and requires bounded level sets and small residuals; constants (η, ε, rates) are unquantified and not easily estimable.

- Weakness: experimental section is minimal—no comparisons to accelerated PGM or other solvers, no wall-clock/runtime, and unclear definition of “solution error.”

- Weakness: stepsize assumption (bounded away from 0) needs clarification regarding practical backtracking rules and when αk ≥ αmin holds.

- Weakness: relation to KL-based linear convergence not discussed—unclear if error bound implies KL exponent 1/2 or how results compare.

- Weakness: some key technical statements are deferred to the supplement, reducing accessibility of main text.

- Quality: theoretical claims appear sound and the main novelty is carefully argued; auxiliary proofs should be sketched in the main text for completeness.

- Clarity: generally well-written and organized; residual map role is clear, but minor notation inconsistencies exist.

- Originality: deriving a Luo–Tseng-type error bound for the nuclear norm is novel; using simultaneous SVD quantitatively is new.

- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of PGM for low-rank problems without strong convexity; practical impact limited by locality and unknown constants.

- Questions for authors: relation to KL exponent, guarantee of positive lower bound under backtracking, extension to Schatten p-norms or other spectral regularizers, and how “solution error” is computed in classification experiments.

- Pros for acceptance: novel and technically interesting error bound; fills gap on linear convergence without strong convexity; clear exposition.

- Cons against acceptance: weak empirical validation, limited discussion of KL connections, and local/asymptotic nature with little guidance on when linear regime begins.

- Minor comments: ensure consistent notation (τ vs μ), specify Lf = L‖A‖2, and fix equation references/braces.

- Overall recommendation: accept (borderline to clear accept); confidence moderate.
