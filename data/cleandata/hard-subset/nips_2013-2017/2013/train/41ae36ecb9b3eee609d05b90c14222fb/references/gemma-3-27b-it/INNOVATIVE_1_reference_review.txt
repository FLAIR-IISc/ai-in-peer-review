This paper investigates the convergence rate of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm regularization, a common technique for low-rank matrix recovery. The central contribution is establishing linear convergence *without* requiring strong convexity of the loss function, a significant departure from prior work. This is achieved by proving a novel Lipschitzian error bound tailored to trace norm regularization, overcoming challenges related to the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm and the interdependence of its singular values. The paper clearly articulates the motivation, connects to a broad range of applications, and provides a rigorous theoretical analysis.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The linear convergence result without strong convexity is genuinely novel and addresses a key limitation in existing theory. This is a bold and impactful contribution.
*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The analysis appears sound, with careful consideration given to the unique properties of the trace norm. The proof of the error bound, while detailed (as evidenced by the length of section 4), seems meticulously constructed.
*   **Broad Applicability:** The work’s relevance extends to a wide spectrum of machine learning problems utilizing trace norm regularization, including matrix completion, multi-task learning, and more.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction effectively motivates the problem, and the notation is consistent.
*   **Empirical Validation:** Inclusion of numerical experiments, albeit basic, adds credence to the theoretical results and demonstrates practical applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** While the numerical experiments demonstrate linear convergence, they are relatively simple. Expanding the experiments to more complex datasets and comparing performance against state-of-the-art solvers would strengthen the validation. The experimental section feels somewhat tacked-on and could benefit from a more thorough analysis of parameter sensitivity.
*   **Technical Density:** The technical details, particularly in the proof of the error bound, are dense and may be difficult for a broader audience to follow without significant effort.
*   **Assumptions:** The assumption of strict convexity on *h* is a reasonable starting point, but exploring relaxations or alternative conditions could broaden the scope of the results. The paper acknowledges this limitation but doesn’t delve into potential extensions.
*   **Limited Discussion of Practical Implications:** While theoretically significant, the paper could benefit from a deeper discussion of how this linear convergence result might impact algorithm design or parameter tuning in practical applications.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a genuinely novel and impactful theoretical result that significantly advances our understanding of the convergence properties of PGM for trace norm regularization.
*   The technical analysis is rigorous and well-supported.
*   The broad applicability of the results makes it relevant to a wide range of researchers.
*   The paper tackles a challenging problem and provides a clear and insightful solution.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The empirical evaluation is limited and could be significantly strengthened.
*   The technical density may make the paper inaccessible to some readers.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant theoretical contribution. While the empirical validation is somewhat limited and the technical details are demanding, the novelty and potential impact of the results outweigh these concerns. This paper fills an important gap in the literature and represents a valuable addition to the field of optimization. I believe this paper is a clear candidate for acceptance, prioritizing the novelty of the theoretical result. Minor flaws are acceptable given the boldness of the approach.



**Rating:** Accept (Strongly Consider)
