- Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Establishes a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization using novel matrix perturbation theory.  
- Shows how this error bound enables application of Luo and Tseng’s convergence framework to the trace-norm setting.  
- Handles the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm, a case where ℓ1-based variable-splitting approaches fail.  
- Proofs are rigorous, well-structured, and highlight an elegant key Lemma (4.1).  
- The analysis covers important ML applications (matrix completion, matrix classification, multi-task learning).  
- Numerical experiments confirm linear convergence in practice for the considered tasks.  
- The class of loss functions f(X)=h(A(X)) is somewhat restrictive and excludes more general/nonconvex losses.  
- The paper does not address practical estimation of L_f or step-size selection for large-scale problems.  
- Lacks a more explicit comparison of the new error bound’s nature versus existing bounds for other regularizations.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant contribution that meets conference quality criteria.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves an important open question, weaker assumptions than prior work, broad applicability, rigorous novel techniques, validated experimentally.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted loss class and limited practical guidance on step-size/L_f estimation.
