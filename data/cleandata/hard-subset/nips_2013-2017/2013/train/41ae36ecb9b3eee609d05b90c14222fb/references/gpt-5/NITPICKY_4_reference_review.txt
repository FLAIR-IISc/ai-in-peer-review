Summary
The paper studies proximal gradient methods (PGM) for the convex problem min_X f(X)+τ||X||_* with f(X)=h(A(X)), where A is linear and h has a Lipschitz gradient and is strictly/locally strongly convex on compact subsets of its domain. The main technical contribution is a local Lipschitzian error bound: dist(X, X*) ≤ η||R(X)||_F for points in a fixed level set with sufficiently small residual, where R(X)=S_τ(X−∇f(X))−X. Using Luo–Tseng’s framework, this yields asymptotic linear (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) convergence of PGM without requiring strong convexity of f. The core proof develops a “restricted invertibility” property near the solution set by exploiting that at any solution X̄, X̄ and −∇f(X̄) can be simultaneously SVD-diagonalized, enabling a perturbation argument based on matrix shrinkage. Experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification illustrate linear convergence behavior.

Strengths
- Establishes a nontrivial local error bound specialized to the nuclear norm; to my knowledge this fills a gap vis-à-vis known results for polyhedral and group-structured regularizers.
- The spectral-alignment insight (Proposition 4.2) and subsequent perturbation analysis (Proposition 4.3) are interesting and technically careful.
- The assumptions on h cover common losses (square, logistic) and allow A to be non-injective, broadening applicability beyond strongly convex f.
- The convergence statement via Luo–Tseng is standard and appropriate once the error bound is available.

Weaknesses and concerns (technical and presentation)
- Step-size and residual inconsistency: R(X) is defined with unit step (R(X)=S_τ(X−∇f(X))−X), but the algorithm uses X^{k+1}=prox_{τα_k}(X^k−α_k∇f(X^k)). Then X^{k+1}−X^k equals R_{α_k}(X^k):=S_{τα_k}(X^k−α_k∇f(X^k))−X^k, not R(X^k). Inequality (11), which bounds ||R(X^k)|| by ||X^{k+1}−X^k||, is nontrivial under this mismatch and should either redefine the residual with α_k or prove the bound explicitly (with constants tracking α≤α_k≤ᾱ). As written, there is a notational/technical gap.
- Theorem 3.2 requires α_k<1/L_f strictly; in matrix completion experiments α=1 is used. For f(X)=1/2||P(X)−b||^2, L_f=||P||^2=1 (for an entry-sampling operator), so α=1 violates α_k<1/L_f. Either relax the theorem (α_k≤1/L_f is standard) or adjust experiments.
- The local strong convexity assumption for h is stated as “strongly convex on any convex compact subset of dom(h).” This is unusual wording; please formalize it (e.g., for any compact convex Z⊂dom(h) there exists σ(Z)>0 s.t. h is σ(Z)-strongly convex on Z) and make explicit how σ used in (15) depends on the level set and bounds (ties into κ, η).
- Boundedness of level sets: Section 2.2 claims from “X is bounded and F is convex” that all level sets {X | F(X)≤ζ} are bounded, citing Rockafellar [14, Cor. 8.7.1]. This statement is not generally true for arbitrary convex functions. In your setting, boundedness follows because τ||X||_*→∞ as ||X||_F→∞ and h is bounded below, but the given argument and citation are misleading; please correct.
- Proposition 4.2: The statement “X̄ and −∇f(X̄) have the same set of left and right singular vectors” needs precise phrasing. From −∇f(X̄)∈τ∂||X̄||_* one can choose Ū,V̄ extending the SVD of X̄ that simultaneously diagonalize −∇f(X̄) (block-diagonal in the Ū_⊥,V̄_⊥ subspace via the SVD of W), but the SVD of −∇f(X̄) is not unique. Please state and prove the exact simultaneous diagonalizability used (as you do later via block structure).
- The proof of Lemma 4.1(c) (restricted invertibility) is central yet many steps are deferred to the supplement (e.g., Proposition 4.3). A concise sketch in the main text would help assess correctness.
- Definition of Q- and R-linear convergence: you define Q-linear via squared norms. This is nonstandard; either use the standard definition or note the equivalence.
- Notation inconsistencies: τ is the regularization parameter, but Section 5 uses μ=1 for the same role (μ is also used earlier for shrinkage threshold). Use a single symbol. Also “prox_τ” in (3) vs “prox_{τα_k}” in (5) should be unified via prox_{λ‖·‖_*}.
- Typos/grammar: “lead”→“leads”; missing hyphens; inconsistent capitalization (e.g., “Proximal Gradient Method”); footnote marker “1” appears misplaced.
- Figures/captions: The text refers to “Figure 1” for matrix completion and “Figure 2” for classification, but only one figure block appears, and the caption “Figure 2: Matrix Classification” seems mismatched with the plots labeled n=40/θ which look like completion. Axes labels and units are minimal; please fix numbering, captions, and improve readability.
- Experimental details are very light: no runtimes, no ranks of iterates, no discussion of when the “linear regime” kicks in, no comparison to accelerated PG or alternative solvers, and no study of sensitivity to conditioning/operator A. Given the theoretical nature, this may be acceptable, but even simple diagnostics (e.g., plotting ||R_{α_k}(X^k)|| vs dist(X^k,X*)) would support the error bound.

Relation to prior work
- The paper appropriately leverages Luo–Tseng’s error-bound-based framework [10] and distinguishes from polyhedral or group-structured regularizers [22,25]. However, there is a broader literature on linear convergence of proximal methods under error bounds/Kurdyka–Łojasiewicz properties; please discuss connections and what is new specifically for the nuclear norm case (e.g., why prior general KL-based results do not already imply your rate).
- Cite and contrast with works on linear convergence of PG for structured convex problems (e.g., analyses for LASSO/group LASSO) and with nuclear-norm-specific algorithms that achieve linear rates under RIP/strong convexity assumptions.

Pros
- Novel local error bound for nuclear-norm-regularized problems.
- Reasonable assumptions covering important losses.
- Clear high-level proof strategy and insightful spectral argument.
- Provides an asymptotic linear rate for a widely used method.

Cons
- Several technical inconsistencies (residual/step-size mismatch; level-set boundedness argument).
- Heavy reliance on supplement for key steps.
- Presentation/notation/typo issues and incomplete references.
- Weak, somewhat inconsistent experimental section and figure/caption errors.

Questions for the authors
1. Please redefine the residual map to incorporate α_k or rigorously justify inequality (11) as stated.
2. Can the step-size requirement be relaxed to α_k≤1/L_f? If not, please adjust experiments for matrix completion.
3. Provide an explicit expression for L_f in terms of L and ||A||, and make all constants κ_i explicit (or at least their dependence).
4. Clarify the exact statement and proof of Proposition 4.2 (simultaneous diagonalization).
5. Is the error bound global on a level set or only local-in-residual? Could it be strengthened?

Recommendation
Borderline reject. The core idea is strong and likely correct, but the manuscript contains multiple technical and presentation issues that, in aggregate, undermine confidence and readability. With careful fixes (especially residual/step-size consistency, corrected boundedness argument, improved proofs/sketches, and cleaned-up experiments and references), this would be a solid contribution.
