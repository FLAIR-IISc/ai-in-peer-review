- Problem & scope: Analyzes proximal gradient method (PGM) for nuclear-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)) in the non-strongly convex case.  
- Main technical contribution: Proves a Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η·||Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X|| on bounded level sets with small residual.  
- Novelty vs prior work: Resolves an open gap for nonpolyhedral nuclear-norm regularization where l1/group-lasso proofs fail.  
- Convergence result: Uses the error bound + Luo–Tseng framework to obtain asymptotic linear convergence (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) for PGM with step-sizes in (α,1/Lf).  
- Key proof idea: Shows spectral alignment at optimality (X* and −∇f(X*) share singular vectors) and uses perturbation and a “restricted invertibility” to link ||X−projX*(X)||F to ||A(X−projX*(X))||.  
- Assumptions & scope: Assumptions on h (strictly convex, L-smooth, strongly convex on compacts) are clearly stated and cover common losses (square, logistic).  
- Experimental evidence: Modest experiments (matrix completion/classification) show asymptotic linear decrease but do not validate constants or compare methods.  
- Technical quality: Development appears sound and well-reasoned, though some proofs (e.g., Proposition 4.3) are deferred to the supplement.  
- Clarity strengths: Paper is well organized; motivation for nuclear-norm difficulties and the spectral-alignment observation are strengths.  
- Clarity weaknesses: Dependence of constants (η, ε, κ) and neighborhood size not discussed; some references/figure labels wrong; some proofs should be sketched in main text.  
- Practical limitations: Result is local/asymptotic with unspecified constants, limiting direct practical guidance.  
- Suggested improvements: Provide dependence of η/ε on A, τ, and spectrum; justify/relax h assumptions or relate to KL property; add empirical comparisons (FISTA/ADMM), wall-clock times, and checks of the error bound; clarify backtracking guarantees.  
- Significance: Theoretically important—closes a gap for nuclear-norm regularization and likely to influence future theoretical/algorithmic work despite asymptotic nature.  
- Acceptance recommendation: Weak accept — novel and significant theoretical contribution but with modest empirical validation and some clarity/technical-details issues.
