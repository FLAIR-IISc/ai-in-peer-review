- Shows linear convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method for trace-norm regularization when f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex, extending guarantees beyond requiring f itself to be strongly convex.  
- Main technical contribution is a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm using matrix perturbation theory, addressing the non-polyhedral epigraph challenge.  
- Correctly situates the work within existing PGM and error-bound literature, and relates to prior trace-norm and matrix-completion results.  
- The result is general and applicable to a broad class of losses of the form h(A(X)), relevant to many applications (e.g., matrix completion, multitask learning).  
- Paper is clearly written with consistent notation and a solid proof outline; full proofs are provided in the supplement.  
- Empirical results on matrix completion and classification support the theoretical linear convergence in practice.  
- Assumption that h is strongly convex on compact subsets (Assumption 2.1(b)) needs clearer discussion about practical implications and cases where the optimum may not lie in such a subset.  
- Theoretical convergence requires a carefully chosen step size; the paper lacks practical guidance on step-size selection beyond using backtracking in experiments.  
- No analysis of per-iteration computational cost, particularly the proximal operator for the trace norm, so overall runtime complexity is unclear.  
- Experiments are limited to two applications; a broader empirical evaluation would strengthen the claims.  
- Propositions 4.2 and 4.3 are technically dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanations to improve readability.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable and novel theoretical contribution with supporting experiments; minor weaknesses do not outweigh merits.  
- Reviewer decision: Strong Accept (8/10) with high confidence.
