- Problem studied: composite convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization F(X)=h(A(X))+τ||X||* under smooth, strictly convex, Lipschitz-gradient h that is locally strongly convex on compacts.  
- Main contribution 1: Prove a local Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η||R(X)||F for small residuals and bounded objective.  
- Main contribution 2: Using the Luo–Tseng framework, derive asymptotic linear convergence of PGM (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) with stepsizes bounded away from 0 and below 1/Lf.  
- Technical novelty: Circumvents non-polyhedral nuclear-norm epigraph via spectral alignment at optimality (shared singular vectors) and a perturbation-based restricted invertibility argument.  
- Experiments: Small-scale synthetic matrix completion and logistic classification show late-phase linear convergence in semi-log plots.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends EB-to-linear-rate analyses from polyhedral nonsmooth parts (ℓ1, group LASSO) to the non-polyhedral nuclear norm case.  
- Relation to prior work: Leverages Luo–Tseng framework and fills gap where f lacks strong convexity due to A’s nullspace.  
- Technical quality: Main theorems and central Lemma 4.1 are technically sound and convincingly argued via projection onto Xc and Propositions 4.2–4.3.  
- Assumptions: Reasonable for common losses (square, logistic); step-size condition is standard and practically enforceable via backtracking.  
- Limitations of result: The linear rate is local/asymptotic and requires being sufficiently close to X* (small residual).  
- Clarity: Paper is clear and well-organized, with helpful high-level intuition about spectral alignment.  
- Suggestion 1: Discuss Assumption 2.1(b) more concretely with sufficient conditions and which losses satisfy it.  
- Suggestion 2: Discuss how EB constants (η, ε) and contraction factor qualitatively depend on problem data (L, ||A||, τ).  
- Suggestion 3: Explicitly state that backtracking yields the required stepsize bounds and monotonic decrease to link theory and experiments.  
- Suggestion 4: Briefly compare with KL-based analyses (e.g., KL exponent 1/2) and justify preference for the EB approach.  
- Originality: High — simultaneous SVD property and projection-based restricted invertibility are novel in this context.  
- Significance: Moderate-to-high theoretical impact for understanding PGM behavior with nuclear-norm regularization and potential utility for other algorithms.  
- Weakness: No explicit radius of convergence or computable constants limits predictiveness about when linear behavior begins.  
- Weakness: Non-singleton optimal set; iterates converge R-linearly to some point in X* but no rank-identification or identification analysis.  
- Weakness: Experiments are minimal, synthetic, small-scale, and lack comparisons to accelerated methods (SVT, ADMM) or strong-convex cases.  
- Practical concern: Scalability and inexact prox (truncated SVD) are not addressed; interaction of EB with inexact proximal steps is left open.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel, solid EB result for nuclear norm, broad applicability without strong convexity, and reusable spectral insights.  
- Cons for acceptance: Local nature of result, missing explicit constants, limited experimental validation, and some assumptions need deeper discussion.  
- Recommendations to authors: Expand discussion of Assumption 2.1(b), address inexact prox/truncated SVD extensions, and add at least one larger-scale experiment with comparisons to accelerated methods.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept (borderline to clear); reviewer confidence: high.
