Summary
The paper studies proximal gradient methods (PGM) for trace/nuclear-norm regularized problems of the form min_X f(X)+τ||X||*, where f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear and h smooth and strictly convex with Lipschitz gradient and “local” strong convexity on compact subsets. The main technical contribution is a Lipschitzian error bound: near the solution set X and on a level set, dist(X, X) ≤ η||R(X)||F, where R(X)=Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X is the residual. This allows invoking the Luo–Tseng framework to prove asymptotic linear convergence (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) for PGM with stepsizes bounded away from 0 and strictly below 1/Lf, without assuming global strong convexity of f. The proof relies on a new spectral argument: at optimality, X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors, enabling a careful perturbation analysis to establish a “restricted invertibility” inequality ||X−X̄||F ≤ κ||A(X−X̄)||.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on Luo–Tseng error-bound based convergence analysis and complements earlier bounds for polyhedral epigraphs (ℓ1, group LASSO). It claims novelty for nuclear norm, whose epigraph is non-polyhedral. Please more clearly position the result vis-à-vis: (i) KL-inequality based linear convergence for proximal methods on semi-algebraic objectives (e.g., Bolte et al., Attouch–Bolte–Svaiter); (ii) more recent EB/MPEC analyses for low-rank regularizers (if any). The manuscript cites Zhang et al. (2013) but a sharper contrast would help.

Strengths
- Addresses an important and widely used regularizer; linear rates under non-strongly-convex f are practically and theoretically valuable.
- The spectral alignment observation and its exploitation (Proposition 4.2) are elegant and seem broadly useful.
- The error-bound proof is nontrivial and, if correct, fills a notable gap compared to ℓ1/group LASSO cases.
- Experiments qualitatively illustrate asymptotic linear behavior on matrix completion and classification.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical correctness and completeness
- Residual map and stepsize mismatch: The residual is defined as R(X)=Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X (Sec. 2.3), i.e., with implicit stepsize 1. However, the algorithm uses variable stepsizes αk in (5), and Theorem 3.2 assumes 0<α<αk<ᾱ<1/Lf. Inequality (11) uses R(Xk) (with α=1) and relates it to ||Xk−Xk+1||, but Xk+1−Xk=Sαkτ(Xk−αk∇f(Xk))−Xk. The link is not immediate and may be false without redefining Rα(X)=Sατ(X−α∇f(X))−X. Please either redefine the residual map throughout to include α or justify carefully how (11) holds with a residual defined at α=1 while the method uses αk≠1.
- Assumptions vs experiments: Theorem 3.2 requires αk<1/Lf strictly. In matrix completion with square loss and sampling/projection operator P, Lf=||P||^2=1, yet the experiments fix α=1 (Section 5). This violates the theorem’s stepsize condition. Similarly, the matrix classification uses backtracking; please verify αk<1/Lf strictly holds at all iterations (and not only ≤1/Lf).
- Lemma 4.1(c) (“restricted invertibility”) is a strong, global statement (“for all k≥0”) under very weak assumptions on A (arbitrary linear). The proof sketched relies on a specific projection onto Xc (Proposition 4.2) and a perturbation argument. I am concerned about generality: does κ depend on the level set (ζ) and local spectral gaps at X̄? If so, state it explicitly. As written, it reads uniform and global, which seems unlikely without additional regularity (e.g., nondegeneracy of singular values at solutions).
- Boundedness of level sets: The argument in 2.2 (“since X is bounded and F is convex, all level sets {F≤ζ} are bounded”) is not justified. Convexity alone does not imply level-boundedness. Please either prove level-boundedness of F directly (using h(A(X)) and τ||X||*) or correct the citation and conditions.
- Novelty claim: The claim of being the first linear convergence result for trace-norm regularized problems without strong convexity should be carefully qualified in light of KL-based linear convergence results for composite objectives with semi-algebraic nonsmooth parts (which include nuclear norm). Please cite and delineate the difference (EB vs KL, constants, assumptions on A, explicit rates, etc.).

Clarity and presentation
- Notational inconsistencies:
  - τ vs μ used for regularization parameter (μ in experiments).
  - Residual map missing stepsize as noted.
  - Dimensions: “A: R^{m×n}→R^{Tp}” written as RTp; please fix.
- Equation (3): The 1/2 factor in the proximal definition is garbled/missing in typesetting.
- Figures: Captions/labels are inconsistent (e.g., “Figure 2: Matrix Classification” appears under the matrix completion discussion). Axes, definitions of “error in solution,” and y-axis scales are not specified. Please define exactly what is plotted and include units/scales.
- Missing details: Constants κ1–κ3 (Eqns 9–11) are deferred to the supplementary; at least provide the key inequalities and their dependencies in the main text for readability.
- Proposition 4.2: The “simultaneous SVD” property follows from the fixed-point prox equation via soft-thresholding; a short proof sketch in the main paper would help.

Related work and citations
- The related work could be expanded to include KL-based linear convergence for proximal algorithms and any existing EB results for non-polyhedral regularizers.
- References are incomplete or malformed: many entries lack years, venues, page ranges; some have placeholders or duplicated punctuation. This is below NeurIPS formatting standards.

Experimental evaluation
- Only convergence traces are shown; no wall-clock or per-iteration cost discussion. Since PGM with nuclear norm requires SVD/shrinkage, indicate whether full or partial/truncated SVD is used.
- Step size choices do not conform to the theorem (see above). Please either adjust experiments or clarify Lf and the actual αk used.
- Stopping criteria and tolerance rationale should be discussed; 1e−8 objective decrement is very stringent and may not reflect practical performance.

Questions for the authors
1. Can you restate Theorem 3.1 with a residual map parameterized by α and then derive Theorem 3.2 under αk∈[α,ᾱ]? This would resolve the current mismatch.
2. Does κ in Lemma 4.1(c) depend on (ζ, the spectral gap at X̄, τ)? Is the result local to a neighborhood? Please state the dependence precisely.
3. How does your EB compare quantitatively to KL-exponent-based linear rates (e.g., constants, neighborhood size)?
4. For matrix completion, what is Lf in your setup, and how do you reconcile α=1 with α<1/Lf?

Pros and cons for acceptance
Pros: interesting and nontrivial EB; elegant spectral insight; addresses an important algorithmic question; potentially broadly impactful.
Cons: technical inconsistency around residual/stepsize; experiments not aligned with assumptions; presentation/notation issues; incomplete references; some claims appear too strong or insufficiently qualified.

Recommendation
Reject in current form. The core idea is promising, but the accumulation of technical and presentation issues (especially the residual/stepsize mismatch and experimental noncompliance with assumptions) undermines confidence. I encourage a revised submission addressing these points.
