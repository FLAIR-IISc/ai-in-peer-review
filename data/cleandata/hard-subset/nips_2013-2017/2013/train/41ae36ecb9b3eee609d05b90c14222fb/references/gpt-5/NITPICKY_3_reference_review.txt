Summary
The paper studies composite convex optimization with nuclear-norm regularization: minimize F(X)=f(X)+τ||X||*, where f(X)=h(A(X)), A is linear and h has Lipschitz gradient and is strictly convex (indeed strongly convex on compact subsets). The main technical contribution is a local Lipschitzian error bound near the solution set: dist(X, X*) ≤ η ||R(X)||F whenever F(X) ≤ ζ and ||R(X)||F ≤ ε, where R(X)=Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X is the fixed-point residual for the proximal mapping. Leveraging the Luo–Tseng framework, the authors deduce asymptotic linear convergence (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) of the proximal gradient method (PGM) with fixed stepsizes 0<αk<1/Lf, without requiring strong convexity of f. The proof develops a spectral perturbation argument using the fact that any optimal solution X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors (from the subdifferential of ||·||*), enabling a “restricted invertibility” bound with respect to A on the relevant manifold. Numerical illustrations on matrix completion (squared loss) and matrix classification (logistic loss) show linear decay in a neighborhood of the optimum.

Relation to prior work
The paper positions its error bound as the non-polyhedral analog of known bounds for l1 and (group) LASSO, where Luo–Tseng style analyses yield linear rates. It contrasts with results requiring strong convexity of f and with oracle-based optimal sublinear rates. While the idea that nuclear norm is non-polyhedral is standard, the specific local EB for nuclear norm appears less developed in the cited literature; the approach via simultaneous SVD and perturbation is plausible and potentially novel. However, the paper omits discussion of general KL-based linear convergence results for convex semi-algebraic objectives (which include nuclear norm) and of restricted strong convexity frameworks for decomposable regularizers. Positioning relative to these would strengthen claims of novelty.

Strengths
- A clear target: establishing a local EB and consequent asymptotic linear convergence for nuclear-norm regularized problems without strong convexity of f.
- The use of simultaneous SVD at optimality to control nearby spectral structure is insightful.
- The theoretical result covers widely used losses (squared and logistic via h∘A).
- Numerical evidence is consistent with asymptotic linear behavior.

Weaknesses and detailed comments

Quality (technical soundness)
- Error bound is local (requires small residual and bounded level set). This is fine, but the constants (η, ε) are unspecified and their dependence on problem data is opaque; a brief discussion would aid applicability.
- Assumptions: 2.1(b) “strongly convex on any convex compact subset” is unusual. It holds for squared loss (global) and logistic loss (on bounded sets), but the paper should explicitly state the modulus on the subset used (it appears as σ in Lemma 4.1(b)). Clarify how σ is chosen and how it affects κ’s in Theorem 3.2.
- The analysis and residual map R(X)=Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X are fixed to α=1, while the algorithm uses general αk. Theorem 3.2 bridges this with inequality (11) but all three key inequalities (9)–(11) are deferred to the supplement. Given the centrality of the mismatch (α vs 1), at least a sketch should appear in the main text to reassure that the EB with α=1 suffices for general αk.
- Step-size requirements vs experiments: Theorem 3.2 requires αk<1/Lf strictly. In matrix completion with projection operator P, Lf=||P||^2=1, but experiments use α=1 (violating ᾱ<1/Lf). In the logistic example, backtracking is used without a global Lf; this may produce αk≥1/Lf on some iterations. The authors should either adapt the theorem to standard backtracking conditions or ensure experimental αk satisfy the stated bounds.
- Some proof-critical claims are deferred to the supplement (Propositions 4.2, 4.3) without even a sketch. Given their novelty, more detail in the main paper would be helpful.

Clarity and presentation
- Notation inconsistencies:
  - Regularization parameter is τ throughout but experiments use μ=1.
  - The shrinkage operator sμ is defined as sμ: Rp_+→Rp_+ where p already denotes the dimension of A(X)∈Rp; here the length should be min(m,n) (later the authors set m≤n). Avoid overloading p.
  - Define L vs Lf cleanly; both appear.
- Prox operator definition (3) appears typeset incorrectly: τ||Z||* + 1 / ||X−Z||^2F. It should be τ||Z||* + 1/2 ||X−Z||^2F. As written, (6) would be incorrect. Fix.
- Residual map R(·): clarify whether it is defined for α=1 only, and define the general residual Rα(X)=proxτα(X−α∇f(X))−X (this is standard in error-bound analyses).
- Lemma 4.1 assumptions: “Rk/δk→0” is written as “Rk δk → 0”; should specify norms.
- Section 2.2: The claim that A(X) is invariant on X relies on strict convexity of h; a one-line proof would help readability.
- Figures: The text says “Figure 1” for matrix completion, but only “Figure 2: Matrix Classification” is shown. Axes labels/units are unclear; define what “error in solution” is (distance to X or to a particular optimizer?). Stopping tolerances differ across experiments (1e−8 vs 1e−6) without justification.
- Numerous typographical/formatting issues: inconsistent hyphenation (trace norm–regularized), spaces in symbols (R m×n), stray indices (‖·‖2 2), missing accents. References lack years, venues, pages in multiple entries and are inconsistently formatted.

Originality
- The local EB for nuclear norm via spectral perturbation and simultaneous SVD appears novel in this specific form. However, the paper should clarify differences with general KL property-based linear convergence (e.g., Attouch–Bolte–Svaiter) and with decomposable regularizer analyses (e.g., Negahban–Wainwright), both relevant to nuclear norm. These omissions weaken the positioning.

Significance
- The result is of theoretical interest, providing a clean sufficient condition for asymptotic linear rates without strong convexity of f. Practically, the local nature and unknown constants limit immediate algorithmic impact. Extending to variable step sizes/backtracking (as used in practice) and to accelerated methods would improve significance.

Pros
- Addresses an identified gap: EB for nuclear norm regularization.
- Solid high-level proof strategy with meaningful use of spectral structure.
- Applicable to common losses (square/logistic) and linear operators A.

Cons
- Several technical/expository gaps and inconsistencies (prox definition, residual vs step size, experiments not matching assumptions).
- Missing related work discussion on KL and decomposability.
- Minimal, sometimes inconsistent empirical evaluation; figures incomplete/mislabeled.
- Numerous formatting/typographical/reference issues.

Questions for authors
1) Can you adapt Theorem 3.2 to standard backtracking (Armijo) and state precise conditions ensuring the αk sequence satisfies the needed bounds? In particular, reconcile α=1 usage in matrix completion with Lf=1.
2) Please provide a sketch (in main text) of (9)–(11) and how R (with α=1) is related to the algorithmic residual when αk≠1.
3) Can you quantify η and ε in Theorem 3.1 in terms of problem data (σ from Lemma 4.1(b), ||A||, Lh, etc.)?
4) How does your result relate to KL-based linear convergence for convex semi-algebraic objectives? Does your EB imply KL exponent 1/2 locally?
5) Please fix the prox definition, notation overloads, and complete/standardize references.

Recommendation: Reject
While the core idea is promising and likely correct, the manuscript contains too many technical and presentation issues (including theory–experiment mismatches, notation errors, incomplete figures, and missing related work) for NeurIPS. With careful revision addressing the above points, it could become a strong submission. Confidence: high.
