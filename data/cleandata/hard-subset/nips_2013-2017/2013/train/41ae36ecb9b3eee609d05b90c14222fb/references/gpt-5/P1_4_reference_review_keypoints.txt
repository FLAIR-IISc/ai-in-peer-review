- Main result: Proves a local Lipschitzian error bound for nuclear-norm regularized composite problems, linking distance to the optimal set to the proximal residual and yielding asymptotic linear convergence of PGM without strong convexity of f.  
- Setting: f(X)=h(A(X)) with linear A and strictly convex, smooth h; result holds on bounded level sets for small residuals.  
- Novelty: First error bound tailored to trace-norm regularization, extending prior polyhedral-based results (ℓ1/group LASSO) to nonpolyhedral spectral regularizers.  
- Technical crux: Simultaneous SVD at optimality for X* and −∇f(X*) (Prop. 4.2) enables perturbation analysis and a restricted invertibility inequality relating X-space and A(X) distances.  
- Convergence implication: Combining the error bound with Luo–Tseng framework yields R-linear iterate convergence and Q-linear objective convergence for PGM with step sizes in (0,1/Lf).  
- Theoretical soundness: Theorem 3.1 is local but well-structured; assumptions on h are standard and satisfied by squared and logistic losses on bounded domains.  
- Proofs and presentation: Spectral arguments are plausible and proof architecture is clear, though some technical steps are deferred to the supplement.  
- Empirics: Small-scale experiments (matrix completion, logistic classification) illustrate the asymptotic linear phase but are qualitative and do not quantify constants or neighborhoods.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well organized; role of assumptions explained; some notation inconsistencies (τ vs µ) and a need for brief intuition for Prop. 4.2 via subdifferential would help.  
- Minor issue: Theorem 3.2 requires αk < 1/Lf, but an experiment uses α = 1 without clarifying whether this meets the strict inequality.  
- Originality: The simultaneous SVD approach is an original workaround for nonpolyhedral trace-norm geometry and differs from KL-based generic analyses.  
- Significance: Explains observed linear ISTA behavior on nuclear-norm models, broadens applicable losses beyond strongly convex cases, and may extend to other spectral regularizers.  
- Strength: Conceptually clean, nontrivial local error bound for a widely used nonpolyhedral regularizer.  
- Strength: Clear linkage to standard convergence frameworks with practical step-size conditions.  
- Strength: Broad applicability to squared and logistic losses with linear measurement operators.  
- Weakness: Local/asymptotic nature — no quantitative characterization of neighborhood size where the bound holds.  
- Weakness: No guidance on estimating constants (η, ε) or contraction factors, limiting practical predictiveness.  
- Weakness: Experiments lack comparisons to accelerated methods (FISTA), timing, sensitivity analyses, and cases with multiple optima.  
- Weakness: Some technical proofs are in the supplement; including a derivation of Prop. 4.2 from the nuclear-norm subgradient would improve self-containment.  
- Questions for authors: Can η and ε be bounded in terms of Lf, ||A||, and h’s curvature?  
- Questions for authors: Does the error bound extend to accelerated PGM (FISTA) to guarantee eventual linear function-value rates?  
- Questions for authors: How sensitive is the restricted invertibility to degeneracy when X* is non-unique; any pathological cases?  
- Questions for authors: Can the approach extend to other spectral regularizers (Schatten p-norms p∈(1,2]) or to affine constraints?  
- Minor comments: Unify τ/µ notation in theory and experiments; fix incomplete bibliographic details; explicitly state rates are asymptotic.  
- Reproducibility: Algorithmic settings and synthetic data generation are described; providing seeds and code would further help replication.  
- Recommendation: Accept (7/10) — solid, novel theoretical contribution with high confidence despite limited empirical breadth.
