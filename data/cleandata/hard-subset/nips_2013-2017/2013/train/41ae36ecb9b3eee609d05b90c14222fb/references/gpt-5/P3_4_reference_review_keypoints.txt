- Main contribution: proves a local Lipschitz error bound for nuclear-norm regularized problems and uses it to show PGM converges R-linearly in iterates and Q-linearly in objectives under mild stepsize conditions.
- Setting/assumptions: f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear, h convex C1 with Lipschitz gradient and locally strongly convex on compacts; f need not be globally strongly convex.
- Key technical tools: spectral-alignment at optimality (shared singular vectors) and a “restricted invertibility” inequality near the optimal set.
- Proof structure: coherent contradiction argument with asymptotic optimality, boundedness, and perturbation along a limit direction; main theorems are Theorem 3.1 (error bound) and Theorem 3.2 (linear rate).
- Relation to prior work: extends Luo–Tseng error-bound approaches beyond polyhedral nonsmooth terms to the non-polyhedral nuclear norm via spectral analysis.
- Novelty: deriving a verifiable local Lipschitz error bound for the trace norm without assuming strong convexity appears new in convex composite analyses.
- Connection to KL literature: complements KL/Lojasiewicz-based local linear rate results but provides a more concrete, verifiable pathway for common losses (e.g., square, logistic).
- Technical soundness: arguments are technically careful and well-motivated; some proofs (e.g., restricted invertibility, constants κ1–κ3) are deferred to the supplement.
- Suggestion: include concise proof sketches of the most novel steps (especially restricted invertibility) in the main text to aid auditability.
- Clarity/notation: generally well written, but unify notation for regularization parameter (τ vs µ) and fix minor reference/formatting issues.
- Stepsize remark: theorem requires αk < 1/Lf, yet the matrix completion experiment uses α = 1 — clarify or adjust the experiment to match theory.
- Experiments: small-scale experiments illustrate the predicted linear phase but should report Lf, stepsize, entry to linear regime, and compare to accelerated PGM with restart.
- Practical limitations: linear rate is asymptotic/local with implicit constants, offering limited practical guidance on detecting/ensuring the linear regime.
- Broader impact: result is valuable for stopping criteria, stepsize choices, and could extend to other algorithms (coordinate descent, proximal Newton, ADMM) relying on local error bounds.
- Open questions for authors: can the error-bound constant η be bounded from problem data; extend to other unitarily invariant norms or composite penalties; adapt analysis to accelerated PGM with restart?
- Minor issues: unify parameter notation, add missing years in references, and add a brief discussion linking the error bound to KL/Lojasiewicz perspectives.
- Recommendation: strong theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance contingent on addressing clarity and experiment issues.
