- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X))+τ‖X‖_* by establishing a Lipschitzian error bound without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Key technical novelty: shows optimal X̄ and -∇f(X̄) share singular vectors and uses matrix perturbation theory to overcome the trace norm’s non-polyhedral epigraph.  
- Builds on Luo and Tseng (1992) but develops new spectral techniques tailored to trace-norm structure.  
- Theorem 3.1 and supporting lemmas (notably restricted invertibility Lemma 4.1c) are rigorous and carefully proven.  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion, matrix classification) validate the theoretical linear convergence in practice.  
- Originality: first to derive such an error bound for trace norm, addressing a known gap where ℓ1-based techniques fail.  
- Significance: results apply to common losses (square, logistic) and important ML tasks, impacting practical use of first-order methods.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and precise, though some proofs could use more intuitive explanations.  
- Limitation: scope restricted to f(X)=h(A(X)); extension to broader trace-norm problems is not addressed.  
- Technical presentation: parts of the proof (Proposition 4.3 / Lemma 4.1) are condensed and would benefit from clearer intuition on singular-value cases.  
- Literature positioning: cites relevant prior work but should compare more directly to recent advances in non-strongly convex convergence (e.g., Lan 2015, recent NIPS papers).  
- Overall assessment/recommendation: significant, original, technically sound contribution — recommend accept.
