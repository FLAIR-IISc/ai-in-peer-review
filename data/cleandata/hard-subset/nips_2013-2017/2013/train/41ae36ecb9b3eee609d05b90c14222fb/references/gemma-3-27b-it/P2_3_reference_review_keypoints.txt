- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization without requiring strong convexity of the loss f.
- Core technical innovation: Derives a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm problems using matrix perturbation theory.
- Addresses a gap: Extends linear-convergence guarantees to a broader, practically relevant class of problems (e.g., matrix completion).
- Methodology: Leverages the Luo & Tseng convergence framework to translate the error bound into linear-rate results.
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-written, logically organized, and clearly motivates the problem.
- Technical density: Proofs (especially the error bound and lemmas) are dense and require strong background in matrix analysis and optimization.
- Experimental support: Includes numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification that corroborate theoretical claims.
- Limited experiments: Empirical validation is somewhat narrow; broader datasets, parameter sweeps, and solver comparisons are missing.
- Reliance on prior work: Convergence analysis heavily depends on Luo & Tseng; a more self-contained derivation would increase independence.
- Practical implications: Lacks detailed discussion of how the linear rate translates to real-world speedups and applications.
- Clarity caveat: Intuitive explanations of key proof steps would help make the work accessible to a wider audience.
- Originality: Novel result and error bound; adequately references related work but could contrast more with strong-convexity analyses.
- Significance: Result is impactful for researchers and practitioners using trace-norm regularization; error bound may have broader uses.
- Acceptance verdict: Reviewer recommends acceptance, viewing the paper as a strong, high-quality contribution despite minor weaknesses.
