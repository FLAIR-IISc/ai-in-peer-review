- Paper proves linear convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method for trace-norm regularized problems under weak assumptions, notably without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Key technical contribution is a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm problem, addressing challenges from the trace norm’s non-polyhedral epigraph.  
- Assumes f(x)=h(A(X)) with smoothness and curvature conditions on h rather than global strong convexity.  
- Theoretical claims are rigorously proved and the manuscript is generally well-organized with clear notation and proof structure.  
- Numerical experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification demonstrate the predicted linear convergence in practice.  
- The paper situates itself well within existing literature and clearly contrasts its contributions with prior work.  
- Significant advance: relaxes strong convexity requirement, broadening PGM applicability to problems like matrix completion.  
- Novel technical result: establishing an error bound that overcomes dependence on all matrix entries and non-polyhedrality.  
- Main weakness: high technical depth makes proofs hard to follow for readers without strong convex optimization/matrix analysis background.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope; more problem sizes, regularization parameters, and operators A would strengthen validation.  
- Assumptions on h (smoothness and curvature) are abstract; more concrete examples and implications for convergence rates would help.  
- Overall assessment: strong recommendation for acceptance based on originality, technical soundness, clarity, and empirical support.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality—Strong; Clarity—Good; Originality—Excellent; Significance—Excellent.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial theoretical contribution, original error bound, supportive experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: technical complexity limiting accessibility and somewhat limited numerical experiments.
