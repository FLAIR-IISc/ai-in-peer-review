- Main contribution: proves a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularized problems and uses it to show linear convergence of the proximal gradient method without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Key technical insight: at optimal X̄, X̄ and -∇f(X̄) share the same left/right singular vectors, enabling matrix perturbation theory to establish the error bound.  
- Resolves an open question: provides the first Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization despite its non-polyhedral epigraph.  
- Builds on prior work (Luo & Tseng) but makes essential advances specific to trace-norm problems.  
- Applicability: framework covers important ML problems (matrix completion, multi-task learning) where f is not strongly convex.  
- Theoretical rigor: proofs (Theorems 3.1 and 3.2) are thorough and assumptions are carefully handled.  
- Empirical support: experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification demonstrate linear convergence behavior in practice.  
- Presentation: paper is well-structured with consistent notation and clear progression from setup to results.  
- Restrictive assumption: assumes f(X)=h(A(X)), so extension to more general losses is not addressed.  
- Technical complexity: some proofs (Lemma 4.1, Proposition 4.3) are highly technical and may limit accessibility; more intuition would help.  
- Limited experiments: empirical validation is confined to two problems and could be broadened.  
- Practical gap: existence of constants in the error bound (η, ε) is shown but their magnitude/estimation in practice is not discussed.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 5/5, Clarity 4.5/5, Originality 5/5, Significance 5/5.  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS).
