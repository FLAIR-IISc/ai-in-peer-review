- Studies proximal gradient method (PGM) for nuclear norm–regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex, Lipschitz gradient, and locally strongly convex on compacts.  
- Main contribution: a new local Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η||R(X)||F for points in bounded level sets with small residual.  
- Uses the error bound and Luo–Tseng framework to prove asymptotic linear convergence of PGM (Q-linear for objectives, R-linear for iterates) without requiring global strong convexity of f.  
- Key technical insight: at an optimum X̄, X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share left/right singular vectors, enabling spectral-structure–aware perturbation analysis.  
- Derives a “restricted invertibility” inequality relating distances in X-space and A(X)-space, which underpins the error bound.  
- Generalizes prior linear convergence results beyond strongly convex f and beyond polyhedral nonsmooth regularizers.  
- Proof strategy is careful and nontrivial; simultaneous SVD at optimality plus matrix perturbation and a contradiction argument are well-motivated.  
- Fixed-point characterization via shrinkage is used effectively to handle thresholding cases.  
- Some technical steps and constants (Proposition 4.3, κ1–κ3, multiplicity handling) are deferred to the supplement and are critical to correctness.  
- Assumption that h is strongly convex on compact subsets is reasonable for common losses and is used to obtain curvature on bounded level sets.  
- The linear rate is local/asymptotic, requiring small residual and bounded level sets, consistent with error-bound analyses.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and readable; motivation, assumptions, algorithm, and residual map are clearly presented.  
- Minor clarity issues: inconsistent notation for regularization parameter, need to explicitly state locality of the rate and unquantified constants, and address singular-value multiplicities more clearly.  
- Suggest adding a brief comparison to KL property/partial smoothness frameworks to situate the result.  
- Originality: first local error bound for nuclear norm regularization leading to asymptotic linear convergence of PGM without strong convexity—novel and interesting.  
- Significance: advances understanding of first-order methods for low-rank learning and explains observed linear tail behavior, though constants are not provided.  
- Pros: clear theoretical contribution, broad applicability (square/logistic losses, arbitrary A, non-unique optima), and reusable proof ideas.  
- Cons: rate is local with unspecified constants, some proofs relegated to supplement, limited experiments (small, no baselines, no runtime comparisons), and subtlety in simultaneous SVD with multiplicities.  
- Suggestions: explicitly state locality and residual threshold, clarify multiplicity handling and link to partial smoothness literature, expand experiments (larger-scale, baselines, timings), and fix notation/references.  
- Recommendation: Accept (7/10) — strong theoretical contribution despite light empirical validation.  
- Reviewer confidence: 4/5.
