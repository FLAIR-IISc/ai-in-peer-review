- Main contribution: proves a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularized problems and derives asymptotic linear convergence (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) of proximal gradient under mild conditions.
- Problem setting: f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear and h smooth, Lipschitz-gradient, strictly convex and strongly convex on compact subsets; covers square and logistic losses even when A is rank-deficient.
- Key technical novelty: handling the nonpolyhedral nuclear norm via spectral alignment at optimality and matrix perturbation to establish restricted invertibility for near-optimal sequences.
- Algorithmic result: PGM converges linearly for step sizes αk in (0,1/Lf); analysis leverages the Luo–Tseng error-bound framework.
- Experiments: synthetic matrix completion (square loss) and matrix classification (logistic loss) show a clear linear phase in objective and iterate convergence near solutions.
- Relation to prior work: extends standard PGM rates and error-bound literature by providing a nuclear-norm-specific Lipschitzian error bound not covered by separability/polyhedral arguments.
- Strength: clear conceptual and technical contribution with broad applicability to common ML losses and noninjective A.
- Strength: technically nontrivial spectral analysis and clean use of a known convergence framework for interpretability and extensibility.
- Weakness: result is local/asymptotic with unquantified region of attraction and constants, limiting practical guidance.
- Weakness: step-size issue — theorem requires αk<1/Lf but experiments use α=1; needs clarification of Lf or justification at the boundary.
- Weakness: Assumption 2.1(b) (strong convexity on any compact subset) is unusual; include a lemma illustrating it for logistic loss and intended examples.
- Weakness: several proof constants/inequalities are deferred (kappa1–kappa3); add brief proofs/intuition and more detail for Proposition 4.2 to help non-experts.
- Weakness: experiments are limited (small scale, no wall-clock/time-to-accuracy, no comparisons, no sensitivity analysis to τ/rank/sampling/noise).
- Suggestion: add timing, comparisons (accelerated PGM, proximal quasi-Newton), sensitivity studies, and larger-scale or truncated-SVD experiments.
- Suggestion: relate/contrast results to KL/Łojasiewicz theory for semi-algebraic convex functions and explain advantages for nuclear norm.
- Minor issues: notational inconsistencies (τ vs μ, ζ vs z̄) to be cleaned up.
- Quality: technically sound and coherent but some steps deferred; spectral arguments are delicate and need more detail for full trust.
- Clarity: generally well written and organized; include suggested sketches/lemmas to improve accessibility.
- Originality: high — a new Lipschitzian error bound and linear PGM convergence for nuclear-norm regularization without strong convexity of f.
- Significance: meaningful theoretical advance explaining observed linear PGM phases and informing algorithm design.
- Questions for authors: can analysis cover backtracking step sizes, relate error-bound constants to problem data, extend to accelerated PGM/FISTA, and apply to other unitarily invariant norms?
- Pros: novel error bound, insightful spectral analysis, applicability to key loss models, clear linkage to established frameworks.
- Cons: local result with unquantified constants, limited experiments, minor step-size/theoretical mismatches.
- Recommendation: weak accept — valuable and original theoretical work that would benefit from stronger empirical validation and clarification of minor theoretical details.
