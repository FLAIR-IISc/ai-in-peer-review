- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization when f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex and smooth.  
- Main technical contribution: a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization resolving an open question.  
- Proves convergence rigorously using Luo–Tseng framework; Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 4.1 are particularly strong.  
- Carefully handles the non-polyhedral nature of the trace norm, distinguishing it from ℓ1/group LASSO analyses.  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) corroborate the theoretical linear convergence.  
- Authors acknowledge and clearly state the restrictive assumption f(X)=h(A(X)).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; background and proof structure are logical and accessible to experts.  
- Technical details in Lemma 4.1(c) (decomposition of Q̄) are dense and may challenge some readers.  
- Original insight: optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors (Proposition 4.2), enabling the error bound.  
- Distinguishes approach from prior work that required strong convexity, thus offering novel theoretical advance.  
- Results are significant and broadly applicable to common low-rank matrix learning problems.  
- Error bound is likely useful for future optimization research in matrix problems.  
- Strengths: resolves an important open problem, provides a novel error bound, solid experiments, good literature context.  
- Weaknesses: restrictive assumption scope, technically heavy proofs limit accessibility, dependence of constants on problem parameters not explored.  
- Recommendation: accept with confidence; the paper meets NIPS criteria and makes a substantial contribution.
