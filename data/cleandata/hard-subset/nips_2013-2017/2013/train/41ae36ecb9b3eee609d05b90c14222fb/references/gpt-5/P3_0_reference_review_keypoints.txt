- Paper studies composite convex minimization with nuclear-norm regularization and establishes a local Lipschitzian error bound for solutions in a bounded level set.  
- Shows this error bound implies asymptotic linear convergence (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) for the proximal gradient method with fixed steps in (0,1/Lf) without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Technical novelty: handles the non-polyhedral nuclear norm by exploiting spectral structure — at optimum X̄, X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular subspaces (Proposition 4.2), enabling a restricted invertibility argument.  
- Complements existing results (O(1/k), O(1/k^2) for PGM/APG and linear rates under strong convexity) and provides the first error bound for trace norm with non-polyhedral epigraph.  
- Relates to Luo–Tseng error bound analysis and to literature on polyhedral nonsmooth regularizers (ℓ1, group lasso).  
- High-level proof strategy is sound: establish local EB then apply Luo–Tseng framework to derive linear convergence.  
- Lemma 4.1(c) (restricted invertibility via spectral projections) is carefully constructed and convincing.  
- Proposition 4.2 is critical but strong and nonstandard; its proof is sketched and deferred to supplement, which hinders validation.  
- Treatment of spectral cases and shrinkage conditions is delicate but coherent; using local strong convexity of h on a compact set is appropriate.  
- Minor technical inconsistency: residual map R(X) defined for step size 1 while algorithm uses variable αk; recommend defining Rα(X) and using it consistently.  
- Numerical experiments are illustrative (show linear tails) but limited to small synthetic problems and lack robustness checks or method comparisons.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and readable; introduction and assumptions are clear, but notation needs tightening (τ vs µ, residual vs α, explicit Lf).  
- Bibliographic entries need completion (missing years/pages).  
- Originality: new and nontrivial error bound for nuclear-norm problems under general linear A and mildly curved h; spectral-alignment technique is novel.  
- Should discuss connections to Kurdyka–Łojasiewicz property and restricted strong convexity results in statistics, clarifying differences and advantages.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of proximal methods for nuclear-norm regularization and may inspire extensions to other spectral regularizers.  
- Pros: novel Lipschitzian EB, clean derivation of asymptotic linear convergence without strong convexity, solid spectral-geometry use, clear common-loss assumptions.  
- Cons: key spectral alignment proposition needs more explicit proof, residual/step-size notation inconsistency, minimal experiments, related work could be broader.  
- Suggested fixes: define residual with α consistently, include a succinct proof sketch of Proposition 4.2 in main text, clarify dependence of EB constants on problem parameters, expand related work on KL/RSC, and strengthen experiments.  
- Questions for authors: can approach extend to other spectral regularizers or structured nuclear norms; can η be made explicit via spectral gaps; does backtracking/variable αk preserve linear rates?  
- Overall assessment: solid and technically interesting contribution with minor fixes needed; recommend acceptance (high confidence).
