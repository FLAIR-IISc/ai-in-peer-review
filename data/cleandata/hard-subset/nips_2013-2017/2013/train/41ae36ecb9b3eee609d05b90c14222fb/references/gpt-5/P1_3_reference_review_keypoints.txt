- Problem: PGM for convex trace-norm regularized problems min_X f(X)+τ||X||* with f(X)=h(A(X)) in the non-strongly-convex setting.  
- Main result: local Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η||R(X)||_F for X in a bounded level set with small residual R(X).  
- Consequence: using Luo–Tseng framework, asymptotic linear convergence of PGM—Q-linear function values and R-linear iterates for fixed stepsizes in (0,1/Lf).  
- Key technical insight: at any optimum X̄, X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share left/right singular vectors, enabling spectral perturbation analysis.  
- Restricted invertibility: perturbation argument rules out directions where A loses information relative to solution geometry.  
- Assumptions: h has Lipschitz gradient and is strongly convex on compact sets (covers squared and logistic losses); A is arbitrary (f need not be strongly convex).  
- Experiments: matrix completion and classification show linear convergence after entering a neighborhood of the solution set.  
- Novelty: provides an error bound for the nuclear norm (nonpolyhedral), filling a gap compared to ℓ1/group-lasso results.  
- Relation to prior work: distinct from statistical restricted strong convexity approaches and complements KL-based analyses; needs clearer positioning relative to KL exponent 1/2/Luo–Tseng error bounds.  
- Technical quality: proofs are sound; simultaneous SVD and contradiction-based restricted invertibility are elegant and convincing.  
- Assumption 2.1(b) is reasonable and appropriate given bounded level sets for common losses.  
- The convergence theorem follows standard Luo–Tseng reasoning once the error bound is established.  
- Presentation issues: Proposition 4.3 is relegated to supplement—short sketch should appear in main text; notation τ vs μ inconsistent; figure numbering/captions and references need fixes.  
- Experimental issues: stated stepsize α=1 for matrix completion may violate α<1/Lf (clarify or adjust); logistic experiment uses backtracking though theory assumes fixed stepsizes—discuss line-search extension.  
- Missing quantification: neighborhood size and constants (η, ε) are not explicitly characterized.  
- Limited experiments: more comparisons (accelerated PG, ADMM), sensitivity to τ, onset of linear regime, and robustness to approximate SVDs would strengthen practical significance.  
- Cons: rates are local/asymptotic; key proofs absent from main text; no discussion of accelerated methods, inexact proximal computations, or explicit KL-links.  
- Suggestions: clarify stepsize conditions and notation, sketch Proposition 4.3 proof, discuss backtracking/accelerated/inexact proximal extensions, and improve experimental scope and figure clarity.  
- Recommendation: strong, novel theoretical contribution—recommend accept after addressing the noted clarifications and presentation/experimental improvements.
