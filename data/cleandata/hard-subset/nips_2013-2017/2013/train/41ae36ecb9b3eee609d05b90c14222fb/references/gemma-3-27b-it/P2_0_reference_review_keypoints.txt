- Summary: Establishes linear convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method for trace-norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of f.
- Main technical contribution: Derives a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization using matrix perturbation theory and shared singular structure of solutions/gradients.
- Empirical support: Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) demonstrate linear convergence in practice.
- Strength — Theoretical contribution: Broadens applicability of PGM by removing strong-convexity requirement on f.
- Strength — Novelty: Clever, nontrivial error bound addressing the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm.
- Strength — Presentation: Well-structured, clear introduction and consistent notation.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Experiments support theoretical findings.
- Strength — Literature: Well connected to and cites relevant prior work.
- Weakness — Proof complexity: Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 4.1 are dense and hard to follow; need clearer/streamlined exposition.
- Weakness — Limited experiments: Empirical evaluation is narrow (few sizes, ranks, sampling ratios) and lacks comparisons to other solvers.
- Weakness — Line search details: Sparse discussion of backtracking line search and its effect on observed convergence; raises sensitivity concerns about step-size choice.
- Weakness — Proposition 4.2: Lacks intuition and explanation for why optimal solutions share singular vectors.
- Overall assessment: Strong, significant, and rigorous paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to clarify proofs and expand experiments.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novelty, broad significance for ML, and technical soundness supported by experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: Accessibility of technical proofs and limited empirical evaluation.
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
