- Proves a Luo–Tseng type local Lipschitzian error bound for nuclear-norm regularized convex problems under linear A and smooth, locally strongly convex h.  
- Combines the error bound with standard PGM inequalities to obtain asymptotic linear convergence: Q-linear in objective and R-linear in iterates for fixed step-sizes in (α, ᾱ) ⊂ (0,1/Lf).  
- Main technical novelty: first error-bound proof for the nonpolyhedral nuclear norm using spectral structure and perturbation plus a “restricted invertibility” argument.  
- Leverages the fact that optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors via properties of the nuclear-norm proximal mapping.  
- Extends the Luo–Tseng/error-bound framework to nonpolyhedral spectral regularizers, beyond prior polyhedral (ℓ1/group LASSO) results.  
- Theorems are technically plausible and careful; fixed-point and descent arguments are standard and sound.  
- Error-bound proof is convincing via constructing an optimizer set sharing singular vectors and using projection/contradiction arguments.  
- Assumption of strong convexity on compact sets (Assump 2.1(b)) is unusual but holds for squared and logistic losses on bounded regions and yields a local modulus.  
- The restricted invertibility claim (ineq. 16) is subtle but handled elegantly by contradiction, avoiding extra nondegeneracy assumptions.  
- Paper is generally well written with a clear proof roadmap, though some technical details are deferred to the supplement.  
- Minor clarity issues: figure numbering mismatch, inconsistent notation (µ vs τ), and need to clarify backtracking/step-size bounds.  
- Originality: novel and nontrivial result—first to establish Lipschitzian error bound and linear convergence for nuclear-norm without strong convexity of f.  
- Significance: important to optimization/ML for justifying linear convergence in common low-rank problems, but the result is local/asymptotic and constants are unquantified.  
- Empirics: illustrative synthetic experiments show linear decay near solution but are minimal—no real-data tests or comparisons to other solvers.  
- Questions/suggestions posed: applicability to accelerated PGM/FISTA (with/without restarts), dependence of η/neighborhood on spectral gaps, inexact-prox extensions, and backtracking clarification.  
- Pros: novel, technically solid error bound; extends linear-rate guarantees to nuclear-norm problems; insightful spectral arguments.  
- Cons: asymptotic/local result with unspecified constants; minimal experiments; some key proofs only in supplement.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, with suggestions to clarify assumptions/constants and modestly strengthen experiments.
