- Problem studied: convergence rate of proximal gradient method for nuclear-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) + τ||X||* with h smooth, strictly convex, and locally strongly convex on compacts.

- Main contribution: a new local Lipschitzian error bound dist(X,X*) ≤ η||R(X)||F in bounded level sets for small proximal-gradient residual R(X).

- Consequence: leveraging Luo–Tseng framework, the paper proves asymptotic linear convergence of PGM (R-linear iterates, Q-linear objectives) for stepsizes bounded away from 0 and below 1/L.

- Key technical insight: spectral alignment — any optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share left/right singular vectors via the proximal characterization.

- Core lemma: a restricted invertibility inequality ||X − ProjX*(X)||F ≤ κ ||A(X − ProjX*(X))||2 derived by perturbation analysis around the aligned singular subspaces.

- Numerical validation: experiments on matrix completion and logistic matrix classification show the predicted final-phase linear convergence.

- Relation to prior work: extends Luo–Tseng-style EB to nonpolyhedral nuclear norm regularization, resolving an open question for f(X)=h(A(X)) without global strong convexity.

- Distinction from other analyses: different from restricted strong convexity and KL-based results; provides a problem-specific, geometric EB based on singular-subspace alignment.

- Quality: main theorem and Proposition 4.2 are convincing and technically nontrivial; the contradiction argument for restricted invertibility is elegant.

- Assumptions: requirements on h (Lipschitz gradient, local strong convexity on compacts) are reasonable and satisfied by square and logistic losses.

- Convergence proof: follows cleanly from Luo–Tseng once the EB is established.

- Required clarifications: treatment of singular value multiplicities and selection/stability of Ū,V̄ in simultaneous SVDs needs explicit discussion.

- Stepsize issue: paper should argue that standard backtracking yields a uniform positive lower bound on αk under global Lipschitz continuity of ∇f.

- Presentation/clarity issues: minor notational inconsistencies (τ/µ) and figure numbering, plus a reminder that prox is implemented via shrinkage would aid readability.

- Significance: important result for ubiquitous nuclear-norm problems; EB likely useful beyond PGM for other algorithms.

- Limitations: linear rate is local/asymptotic with no quantitative constants or guidance on when linear regime starts; experiments are modest.

- Suggestions for authors: clarify multiplicity handling, backtracking lower-bound, connections to KL/partial smoothness (KL exponent 1/2), and possible extensions to other unitarily invariant norms.

- Recommendation: strong accept conditional on minor clarifications.
