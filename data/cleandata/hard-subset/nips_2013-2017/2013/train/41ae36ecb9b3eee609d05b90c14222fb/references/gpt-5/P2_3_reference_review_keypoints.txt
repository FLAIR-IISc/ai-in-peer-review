- Problem & setting: Studies PGM for convex problems min_X f(X)+τ||X||_* with f(X)=h(A(X)), where h is smooth and locally strongly convex on compacts, covering losses like squared and logistic and operators A with nullspace.

- Main contribution: Proves a local Lipschitzian error bound that upper-bounds distance to optima by a proximal residual and, via the Luo–Tseng framework, yields asymptotic linear convergence of PGM without global strong convexity.

- Technical novelty: Shows a spectral structural property at optimality—X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors—enabling a perturbation argument and restricted invertibility to overcome the nuclear norm’s non-polyhedrality.

- Relation to prior work: Extends linear-rate analyses beyond strong-convexity/RIP assumptions and complements polyhedral error bounds (ℓ1/group LASSO); comparable local EB for nuclear norm under such general f was previously lacking.

- Proof & rigor: Theoretical development appears careful and sound, using contradiction with key lemmas/propositions and standard Luo–Tseng arguments to get Q- and R-linear rates.

- Assumptions: Assumption 2.1(b) (local strong convexity of h) is reasonable for common losses; boundedness of level sets is established and essential for local constants.

- Convergence scope: Result is asymptotic/local (not global), explicitly stated given absence of global strong convexity.

- Experiments: Small-scale experiments align with theory showing linear trends near solution but do not quantify convergence neighborhoods, constants, or include method comparisons.

- Clarity & presentation: Paper is well organized, but has minor notation/editing issues (duplicate figure numbering, typos, incomplete refs) and could give more intuition for Proposition 4.2 and which proofs are deferred.

- Originality: Novel and nontrivial spectral alignment and perturbation-based EB for nuclear-norm regularized problems; could better position relative to KL/EB frameworks (e.g., KL exponent 1/2).

- Significance: Advances theory for nuclear-norm regularization with broad applicability (matrix completion, multitask learning) and potential use in other algorithms beyond PGM.

- Pros summary: Clear motivation, novel local EB without strong convexity, elegant spectral argument, rigorous convergence translation, and supportive experiments.

- Cons summary: Convergence is local with no basin/constant estimates, limited experiments lacking baselines and scalings, missing discussion of KL/EB relations, and minor clarity issues.

- Suggested improvements: Compare to KL-based results, give practical guidance on estimating Lf and step-size bounds, run larger-scale comparisons (including accelerated methods), clarify Proposition 4.2/subdifferential links, and fix figures/references.

- Overall recommendation: Solid theoretical contribution with broad relevance; leans toward acceptance pending clarifications and expanded empirical evaluation.
