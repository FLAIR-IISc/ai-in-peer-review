- Main result: Proximal Gradient Method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems achieves linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of the loss.  
- Technical contribution: Introduces a novel Lipschitzian error bound tailored to trace norm regularization using matrix perturbation theory.  
- Insight: Optimal solutions share singular-vector structure, which the analysis leverages.  
- Applicability: Results cover a wide range of losses (square, logistic, etc.) and relevant ML problems like matrix completion.  
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-written, well-motivated, and assumptions are clearly stated.  
- Experiments: Numerical results support theory but are limited in scope.  
- Related work: Strong connections to convex optimization, proximal algorithms, and matrix regularization literature.  
- Weakness — accessibility: Proofs are dense and technically demanding, reducing accessibility for a broader audience.  
- Weakness — empirical validation: Limited experiments; need exploration across problem sizes, τ, operators A, and method comparisons.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Implications of assumptions (e.g., conditions on A, b in matrix completion) are not fully detailed.  
- Weakness — constants: Convergence rate constant and its dependence on problem parameters (condition number, rank) are not explicitly characterized.  
- Overall assessment: Strong, significant theoretical advance with solid technical approach despite proof density and limited experiments.  
- For acceptance: Novelty, rigorous analysis, broad significance, and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: High proof complexity and sparse experimental validation.  
- Recommendation & rating: Recommend accept (Strong Accept, 8/10).
