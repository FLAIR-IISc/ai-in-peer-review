

Added after authors' feedback:  
Please include a simulation demonstrating linear convergence of the PG algorithm when the loss is not strongly convex (for varying stability levels), and explicitly discuss the failure cases of the theory. Additionally, extend this discussion to the analysis of ADMM.  

This paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized learning when the loss function takes the form $f(X) = h(A(X))$, where $A$ is linear, $h$ is strongly convex on any compact set, and $h$ has a Lipschitz-continuous gradient.  

This work extends Tseng [20] and Tseng and Yun ("A coordinate gradient descent method for nonsmooth separable minimization") and Zhang et al. [22], which established linear convergence via the "error-bound condition" for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm. While this extension is non-trivial, the contribution appears to be purely technical.  

The presentation of the proofs is largely clear.  

Strengths:  
- Demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending the result of Tseng et al.  

Weaknesses:  
- The contribution is purely technical.  
- A numerical example showing linear convergence (e.g., a semilog plot of the function value versus the number of iterations) is missing.  

Additional details:  
1. The proof outlines for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirror those of Tseng. The authors should more precisely reference the original work to clarify their specific contribution.  
2. A numerical example verifying linear convergence is required.  
3. The dependence of constants $\kappa_1, \ldots, \kappa_4$ on $\underline{\alpha}$ and $\overline{\alpha}$ is unclear.  

Minor issue:  
4. The sequence of inequalities preceding (13) is confusing. The final inequality follows from the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that $-\overline{G} \in \tau \partial \|X\|_{\ast}$, not from the intermediate inequality (cf. p. 289 in [20]).
