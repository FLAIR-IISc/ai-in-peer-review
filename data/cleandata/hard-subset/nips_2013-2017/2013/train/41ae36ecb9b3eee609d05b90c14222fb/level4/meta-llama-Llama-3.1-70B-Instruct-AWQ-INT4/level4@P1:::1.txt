This paper proves the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method applied to trace-norm regularized learning problems when the loss function has the form of f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is linear and h is strongly convex on any compact set and has Lipschitz gradient. The paper is an extension of previous work by Tseng, Tseng and Yun, and Zhang et al., which established the same result using the "error-bound condition" for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm.

The presentation of the proofs is mostly clear, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem. The main contribution of the paper is the establishment of a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems, which is used to prove the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method.

A strength of the paper is that it shows the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending the result of Tseng et al. to the trace norm. However, the contribution seems purely technical, and the authors could have done more to explain the significance of their result and its implications for machine learning.

One weakness of the paper is the lack of numerical examples. The authors should have included a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but the PG algorithm converges linearly (for different levels of stability). This would have helped to illustrate the practical relevance of their result and demonstrate its effectiveness in real-world applications.

Furthermore, the authors could have discussed how their result can be extended to the analysis of ADMM. This would have provided a more complete picture of the implications of their work and its potential applications in machine learning.

In terms of minor issues, the sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing. The last inequality follows due to the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that -Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖∗, and not because of the intermediate inequality (see p289 in [20]).

Overall, the paper provides a technical contribution to the field of machine learning, but could benefit from more discussion of its practical implications and potential applications.

Strengths:

* Shows the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm extending the result of Tseng et al.
* Provides a thorough analysis of the problem.

Weaknesses:

* The contribution is purely technical.
* Lack of numerical examples.
* Could have discussed how their result can be extended to the analysis of ADMM.

Minor issues:

* The sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing.

Recommendation:

* Accept with minor revisions.

Additional comments:

* The authors should refer to the original work of Tseng more precisely to make their contribution clearer.
* The authors should include a numerical example that indeed shows the linear convergence (a semilog plot of function value vs. the number of iterations).
* The authors should clarify how the constants κ1, ..., κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ and ᾱ.
