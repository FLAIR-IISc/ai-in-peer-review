The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm–regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss, which is important for many machine learning and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows Tseng’s [20] Lipschitzian error-bound technique, whose applicability to the nuclear-norm case had remained an open question. To the best of my understanding, the main part of the proof is correct.

What I miss is a clear comparison or analogy to the vector case with ℓ1-norm regularization, as in [20, 22] and related work. It would help to relate your results and techniques to those in the ℓ1 setting, and to explain whether and why the ℓ1 techniques do not directly carry over to the trace norm. (Moreover, the ℓ1 case should now follow as a corollary of your trace-norm result.) In assessing the paper’s contribution, this context matters, since some readers may not find it very surprising that an ℓ1 result extends to the trace norm. I still consider the contribution valuable.

Separately, I suggest a slight title change. The main new contribution concerns the loss, rather than making the trace-norm regularizer itself “structured.” The additional flexibility provided by the linear operator A lies on the loss side, not in the regularizer.

Minor issues:
- Notation: Should P be called a sampling operator or a mask operator? “Sampling” may suggest randomness, whereas P is fixed here.
- Line 337: Broken reference.
- The meanings of Q-linear and R-linear convergence may not be widely known; please restate them for clarity.
- Please further interpret the residual R(X) defined in (6) by relating it to Tseng’s original definition (46) in [20].

In general, I would recommend adding more discussion and perhaps a brief conclusion to balance the long technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
