For trace norm regularized risk minimization, a popular method is the proximal gradient algorithm. The sublinear global convergence rate of this algorithm is well-understood, and it is also known that the rate is linear if the loss is additionally strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption by building upon the framework established by Luo and Tseng. Specifically, the authors prove a local error bound that allows them to demonstrate the asymptotic linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularization. The paper largely follows a standard approach to establish the local error bound, featuring a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.

**Quality:**
This paper appears to be technically sound. A minor clarification is needed in the supplementary material, line 85. Please explain why the choice of κ3 (assuming α < 1) leads to the claimed inequality ‖R(Xk)‖F ≤ κ3‖Xk − Xk+1‖F.

**Clarity:**
The paper is generally clearly written and I could follow the arguments without significant difficulty. To enhance readability, the authors might consider presenting the main claims upfront before delving into detailed proofs. Specifically, after stating the local error bound, postponing the technical proof and directly proceeding to demonstrate the linear convergence rate (which relies largely on established results) could improve the flow.

**Originality:**
The originality of this work is moderate. The observation facilitating simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is novel and potentially interesting. However, the core approach to establishing the local error bound largely follows existing methodologies. The subsequent steps are not particularly innovative.

**Significance:**
The primary significance of this paper lies in its theoretical contribution, with limited immediate impact on algorithm design. While extending the linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm to scenarios relaxing the strong convexity assumption is valuable, the asymptotic nature of the result somewhat diminishes its practical relevance. The technique employed for diagonalizing matrices might be of independent interest. This work rigorously demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed form of strong convexity. The authors predominantly leverage a well-established framework by Luo and Tseng, augmented by a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, thus largely reducing the problem to a vector case. Overall, this work is incremental but provides a solid theoretical advancement.

*   The simultaneous diagonalization observation is a novel contribution.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**
