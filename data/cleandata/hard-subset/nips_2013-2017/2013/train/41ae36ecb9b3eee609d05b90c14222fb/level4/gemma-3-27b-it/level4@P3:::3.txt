This paper investigates the convergence rate of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for minimizing a convex smooth loss function with trace norm regularization. The authors demonstrate linear convergence under conditions weaker than strong convexity of the loss function, a notable improvement over existing results. Their proof relies on establishing a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem, which itself may be of independent interest.

The paper is technically sound and builds upon established results in convex optimization and proximal algorithms. The core idea of leveraging a specific structure in the optimal solution set to derive the error bound appears well-motivated and executed. However, the reviewer rightly points out a missing justification in the appendix (line 85) regarding the relationship between the residual norm and the step size, which should be addressed. The presentation is generally clear, though the organization could be improved. Laying out the main results upfront, particularly the error bound and its implication for linear convergence, before diving into the detailed proof could enhance readability. Deferring the technical proof of the error bound until after outlining the convergence proof might also streamline the narrative.

The originality of the work is moderate. While the observation regarding simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is a valuable contribution, the overall approach largely follows the established framework of Luo and Tseng. The paper skillfully adapts existing techniques to a specific problem setting, demonstrating a solid understanding of the relevant literature. The significance of this work lies primarily in its theoretical contribution. Extending the linear convergence guarantee of the PGM to a broader class of loss functions is a worthwhile advancement. While the impact on practical algorithm design may be limited, the theoretical insight is valuable. The asymptotic nature of the convergence result is a potential limitation, but does not negate the overall contribution. The author’s comment regarding the connection to “Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting” by Sra et al. (NIPS'12) specifically addressing the bound |Rk| <= kappa |Xk - X_{k+1}|_F is a useful clarification.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a relevant and well-motivated problem in optimization and machine learning.
*   The theoretical results are sound and contribute to a better understanding of the convergence properties of the PGM.
*   The error bound established in the paper is a novel and potentially useful result.
*   The presentation is generally clear and well-organized, although improvements can be made.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The originality of the work is moderate, as it builds heavily on existing results.
*   The asymptotic nature of the convergence result might limit its practical impact.
*   A clarification is needed regarding a specific detail in the appendix regarding the residual norm bound.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid theoretical paper that makes a worthwhile contribution to the understanding of proximal gradient methods for trace norm regularization. The technical results are sound, and the paper is generally well-written. The minor weaknesses related to originality and the asymptotic nature of the convergence result are outweighed by the strengths of the work. Addressing the clarification request regarding the appendix would further strengthen the paper.

**Recommendation:**

Accept. I recommend accepting this paper, contingent on the authors providing a clear explanation for the inequality mentioned in the appendix (line 85) of their initial submission.
