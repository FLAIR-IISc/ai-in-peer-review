- Main result: Proves PGM has asymptotic linear convergence (R-linear iterates, Q-linear objective) for min f(X)+τ‖X‖* without requiring f to be strongly convex, via a new Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to optimal set to the proximal residual.

- Technical novelty: Extends error-bound based linear convergence results to the nonpolyhedral trace norm by exploiting optimality structure (shared singular vectors) and spectral-domain perturbation.

- Technical soundness: Analysis and high-level logic (derive local error bound, then invoke Luo–Tseng) appear correct; the spectral-structure argument is nontrivial.

- Presentation quality: Generally well written and organized but needs clearer citations and explanations in places and clarification of a confusing inequality chain.

- Clarity request: State explicitly how constants κ1…κ4 in Theorem 3.2 depend on step-size bounds and Lf.

- Originality: Meaningful technical advance adapting known error-bound frameworks to trace norm, but primarily technical rather than algorithmic innovation.

- Significance: Relevant for matrix completion, multi-task learning, and other ML problems using trace-norm where f lacks strong convexity.

- Pros: Establishes linear convergence for a widely used nonpolyhedral regularizer without f strong convexity; introduces a potentially broadly useful Lipschitzian error bound; leverages a clear Luo–Tseng pathway.

- Cons: Novelty is mainly technical; empirical section does not sufficiently illustrate the theoretical (non-strongly-convex) regime.

- Request 1 (attribution): Make links to prior Luo–Tseng/Tseng & Yun/Zhang et al. proofs explicit in the main text and clarify which parts are new.

- Request 2 (numerics): Add semilog plots of F(Xk)−F(X*) and dist(Xk,X*) vs iterations across instances and show onset of local linear regime.

- Request 3 (constants): Clarify or bound dependence of κ1–κ4 on α, ᾱ, and Lf, and give explicit/bounded values for common losses (squared, logistic).

- Request 4 (technical fix): Revise the inequality chain before (13); attribute the final step to convexity of the trace norm and −Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X̄‖* (per Tseng & Yun).

- Additional experiments: Include simulations with non-strongly-convex losses (e.g., matrix completion with non-injective A) and study how conditioning/stability affect onset and rate of linear convergence.

- Theory limitations: Discuss failure cases—if h lacks required curvature, step sizes violate bounds, or iterates never enter the neighborhood where the error bound holds.

- ADMM discussion: Briefly comment which parts of the error-bound analysis might carry to ADMM and what difficulties arise (dual residual control, over-relaxation, penalty selection).

- Overall assessment: Solid, careful technical contribution extending linear convergence guarantees to trace-norm regularization; would be strengthened by clearer attribution, explicit constants, targeted experiments, and an ADMM discussion.
