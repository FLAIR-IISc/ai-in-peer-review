- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)), where h is Lipschitz-smooth and strongly convex on compacts.  
- Extends prior error-bound based linear-convergence results (e.g., Tseng [20], Zhang et al. [22]) from lasso/group lasso to the trace-norm setting.  
- Contribution is primarily a non-trivial technical extension of existing convergence theory.  
- Proofs are generally clear and follow a logical structure.  
- Strength: Establishes linear convergence guarantees specifically for trace-norm regularization.  
- Strength: Theoretical analysis is rigorous and builds on established first-order method frameworks.  
- Weakness: Mostly technical contribution with limited discussion of practical implications.  
- Weakness: No compelling numerical experiments demonstrating the claimed linear convergence rate.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of how convergence constants (κ1–κ4) depend on step sizes α and ᾱ.  
- Detailed comment: Proofs of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 4.1 closely resemble Tseng [20]; need clearer citations and emphasis on novel aspects.  
- Detailed comment: Recommend a numerical semilog plot of objective vs iterations (including non-strongly convex cases and varying condition numbers) to validate theory.  
- Detailed comment: Clarify the sequence of inequalities before (13); justify the final step using convexity of the trace norm and -∇f(X̄) ∈ τ∂‖X̄‖∗.  
- Potential extension: Explore extending the analysis to ADMM and identify conditions under which ADMM achieves linear convergence for trace-norm problems.
