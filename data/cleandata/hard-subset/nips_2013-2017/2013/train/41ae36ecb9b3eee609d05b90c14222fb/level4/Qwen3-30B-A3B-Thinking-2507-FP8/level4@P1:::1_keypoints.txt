- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity when f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex and Lipschitz-gradient on compacts.  
- Main contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization, resolving an open question absent for trace norm.  
- Extends Luo & Tseng’s error-bound framework from ℓ₁ and group LASSO to trace norm; extension is non-trivial due to non-polyhedral epigraph and SVD interdependence.  
- Key technical insight: at optimal X̄, X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors, allowing matrix perturbation theory to establish the error bound.  
- Theoretical results are sound and technically sophisticated, proving linear convergence without strong convexity.  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) corroborate linear convergence in practice near the optimum.  
- Paper needs more detail on how convergence constants (κ₁…κ₄) depend on step-size bounds α̲ and ᾱ.  
- Generally clear and well-organized, but the inequality sequence leading to (13) should explicitly cite convexity of trace norm and the optimality condition.  
- Originality: meaningful and non-trivial technical extension, though primarily technical rather than conceptual.  
- Significance: broad applicability to ML tasks using trace-norm regularization; the error bound may be independently useful.  
- Strengths: broader loss class for linear convergence, novel error bound, supporting experiments, clear explanation of technical challenges.  
- Weaknesses: mainly technical novelty, missing semi-log plot for linear convergence, incomplete characterization of rate constant dependence, no ADMM connection.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Revision requests: add a semi-log convergence plot for non-strongly convex loss; clarify dependence of rate constants on α̲ and ᾱ; discuss extending the error bound to ADMM; provide precise citations relative to Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22].
