Added after authors’ feedback:
- Please include a simulation showing linear convergence of the proximal gradient (PG) algorithm when the loss is not strongly convex (across different stability levels), and explicitly discuss when the theory breaks down. Also, please discuss how your analysis could be extended to ADMM.

This paper proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm–regularized learning when the loss has the form f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is linear and h has a Lipschitz gradient and is strongly convex on any compact set. The work extends results of Tseng [20], Tseng and Yun (“A coordinate gradient descent method for nonsmooth separable minimization”), and Zhang et al. [22]—which established linear convergence via the error-bound condition for lasso and group lasso—to the trace-norm setting. While this is a nontrivial extension, the contribution appears to be mainly technical. The presentation of the proofs is mostly clear.

Strength:
- Establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm by extending the results of Tseng et al.

Weaknesses:
- The contribution seems purely technical.
- A numerical example demonstrating linear convergence is missing.

More details:
1. The outlines of the proofs of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 appear very similar to those in Tseng. The authors should more precisely reference the original arguments to clarify what is new.
2. Please include a numerical example that clearly demonstrates linear convergence (e.g., a semilog plot of objective value versus iteration count).
3. It is unclear how the constants \kappa_1, \ldots, \kappa_4 depend on the choices of \underline{\alpha} and \bar{\alpha}.

Minor issue:
4. The sequence of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is confusing. The final inequality follows from the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that -\bar{G} \in \tau \partial \|X\|_* (see p. 289 in [20]), rather than from the intermediate inequality.

Extension of prior linear-convergence results based on the error-bound condition from lasso and group lasso to the trace norm.
