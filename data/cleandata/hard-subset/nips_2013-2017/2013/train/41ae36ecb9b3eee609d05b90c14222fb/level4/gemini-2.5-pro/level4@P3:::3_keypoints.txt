- Shows PGM achieves asymptotic linear convergence for trace-norm regularized problems when f(X)=h(A(X)) with strictly convex h (covers square/logistic loss) without requiring strong convexity.  
- Main technical tool is a new local Lipschitzian error bound linking residuals to distance to the optimal set.  
- Analysis builds on the Luo & Tseng (1993) framework that converts such error bounds into linear convergence of descent methods.  
- Novel technical contribution: deriving an error bound for the trace norm (non-polyhedral epigraph) via spectral analysis and a simultaneous SVD of the solution and gradient (Proposition 4.2).  
- Proofs are detailed and technically sound; the paper carefully develops results from optimal-set properties to the main theorem.  
- Requested clarification: inequality (11) ‖R(Xk)‖F ≤ κ3‖Xk − Xk+1‖F is crucial and should be briefly derived or an explicit prior citation given (e.g., Sra NIPS 2012 Lemma 4).  
- Paper is well-written and organized; the introduction effectively motivates the problem and positions the contribution.  
- Suggested minor restructuring: after Theorem 3.1 immediately present Theorem 3.2 (linear convergence) and defer the technical error-bound proof to later for readability.  
- Originality is moderate at the framework level but significant in the nontrivial technical machinery used to handle singular-value dependence.  
- Significance is primarily theoretical—clarifies PGM behavior in practical (non-strongly convex) settings—but the result is asymptotic and lacks a characterization of when linear rates begin.  
- Analytical techniques for handling spectral structure near solutions may be of independent interest for other matrix optimization problems.  
- Recommendation: accept — novel and technically nontrivial contribution with rigorous analysis; weaknesses (framework familiarity and asymptotic scope) do not outweigh strengths.
