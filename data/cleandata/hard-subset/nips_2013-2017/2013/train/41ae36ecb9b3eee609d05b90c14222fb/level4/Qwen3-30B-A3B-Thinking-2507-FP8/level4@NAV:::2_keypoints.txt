- Establishes linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity; core proof appears sound.  
- Manuscript is well-written, clearly structured, and follows Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound framework, resolving the previously open nuclear-norm case.  
- Key omission: lacks clear comparison to the vector l1-norm case (e.g., [20,22]); should explain why l1 techniques don’t directly extend and show l1 as a corollary to assess novelty.  
- Title should be refined to emphasize that the main novelty is relaxing loss-function assumptions (via linear operator A) rather than structuring the trace norm; “structured” may be unnecessary.  
- Minor issues to fix: clarify notation (P as fixed mask operator), repair broken reference at line 337, define Q-/R- linear convergence, relate residual R(X) in (6) to [20]’s definition (46), and better contextualize Lemma 3.2’s technical proof.
