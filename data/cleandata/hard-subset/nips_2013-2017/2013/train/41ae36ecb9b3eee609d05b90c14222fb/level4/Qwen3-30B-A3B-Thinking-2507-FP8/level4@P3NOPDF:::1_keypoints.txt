- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient (PG) for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) under linear A, compact strong convexity of h, and Lipschitz gradient.  
- Contribution extends error-bound-based linear convergence results from lasso/group lasso to the trace-norm setting, but is primarily a technical adaptation rather than a novel concept.  
- Strength: Successfully generalizes the error-bound framework to trace-norm problems, addressing an important low-rank optimization class.  
- Strength: Proofs are generally well-structured and accessible with clear logical flow.  
- Weakness: Limited conceptual novelty—paper needs to explicitly explain how trace-norm structure changes the error-bound derivation (e.g., dual norm, singular value issues) versus prior work.  
- Weakness: Lacks empirical validation; a numerical example (e.g., semilog plot of f(X_k)-f(X*)) demonstrating linear convergence is required.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous dependence of constants κ1…κ4 on α_lower, α_upper and problem dimensions; scaling and tightness of the rate are not specified.  
- Minor issue: The inequality sequence before (13) is confusing; the argument should clarify reliance on trace-norm convexity and the subgradient condition -Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖_* rather than the intermediate steps.  
- Critical (post-feedback): Authors must supply a simulation with a non-strongly convex loss showing convergence behavior across stability levels and clearly state when the theory fails (e.g., particular τ or problem instances).  
- Critical (post-feedback): Authors must discuss how the analysis could be extended to ADMM for trace-norm problems, outlining key challenges (augmented Lagrangian, adapting error-bound) and possible approaches.  
- Overall recommendation: Major revision—technically sound but incremental and missing required simulations, clarity on constants and technical distinctions from prior work, and an ADMM discussion; not ready for acceptance.
