- Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss.  
- Main technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization, resolving an open question.  
- Extends Luo–Tseng error-bound framework and Tseng & Yun’s linear convergence results from ℓ1/group LASSO to trace norm.  
- Non-trivial extension because the trace norm has a non-polyhedral epigraph; authors use shared singular vectors and matrix perturbation theory.  
- Strength: technically sound proof that broadens linear convergence guarantees to a larger class of loss functions.  
- Strength: theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification demonstrating linear convergence.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and well structured; error bound result may be of independent interest.  
- Weakness: contribution is primarily technical/incremental; needs clearer distinction from prior Tseng/Yun work.  
- Weakness: lacks a specific numerical semilog plot demonstrating linear convergence for a non-strongly-convex loss.  
- Weakness: dependence of constants κ1–κ4 on step-size parameters α and ᾱ is not analyzed, limiting practical interpretability.  
- Weakness: confusing sequence of inequalities in the proof of Lemma 4.1(c) before equation (13) that needs correction.  
- Quality: technically rigorous and careful, but empirical validation could better illustrate linear-rate behavior.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, but should more explicitly connect and contrast with Tseng’s prior error-bound results.  
- Originality: non-trivial extension to trace norm but follows existing error-bound methodology, making it incremental.  
- Significance: important for low-rank matrix recovery applications, though the paper should better articulate advances over prior work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revisions: add a semilog convergence plot for a non-strongly-convex instance; explicitly distinguish contributions from Tseng/Yun; clarify step-size dependence of constants; fix Lemma 4.1(c) derivation.  
- Additional suggestion: state failure cases (when assumptions fail) and discuss possible extensions to other algorithms such as ADMM.
