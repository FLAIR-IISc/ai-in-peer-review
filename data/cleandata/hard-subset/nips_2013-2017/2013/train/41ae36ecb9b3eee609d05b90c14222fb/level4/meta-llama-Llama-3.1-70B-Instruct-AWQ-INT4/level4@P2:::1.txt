This paper proves the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method applied to trace-norm regularized learning problems when the loss function has the form of f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is linear and h is strongly convex on any compact set and has Lipschitz gradient.

The paper is an extension of previous works by Tseng, Tseng and Yun, and Zhang et al., which established the same result using the "error-bound condition" for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm. While this extension is non-trivial, the contribution seems purely technical.

The presentation of the proofs is mostly clear, but some minor issues need to be addressed. For instance, the sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing and could be clarified. Additionally, the authors should provide more details on how the constants \kappa1, ..., \kappa4 depend on the choice of \underbar{\alpha} and \bar{\alpha}.

One of the main strengths of the paper is that it shows the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending the result of Tseng et al. However, the paper would benefit from a numerical example that demonstrates the linear convergence, such as a semilog plot of function value vs. the number of iterations.

In terms of future work, the authors may want to consider extending their analysis to the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) and discussing the implications of their results for this algorithm. Furthermore, it would be interesting to see a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but the proximal gradient algorithm still converges linearly, and to discuss when the theory fails.

Overall, the paper provides a technical contribution to the field of optimization, but could benefit from additional context, examples, and discussion of future work.

Strengths:

* Shows the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm extending the result of Tseng et al.
* Provides a technical contribution to the field of optimization.

Weaknesses:

* The contribution is purely technical.
* Lacks numerical examples demonstrating the linear convergence.
* Minor issues with the presentation of the proofs.

Minor issues:

* The sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing.
* The authors should provide more details on how the constants \kappa1, ..., \kappa4 depend on the choice of \underbar{\alpha} and \bar{\alpha}.

Recommendations:

* Discuss the implications of the results for ADMM.

* Clarify the sequence of inequalities before inequality (13).
