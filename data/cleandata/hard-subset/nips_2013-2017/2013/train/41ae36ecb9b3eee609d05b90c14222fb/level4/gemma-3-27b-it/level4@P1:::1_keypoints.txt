- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)) under mild smoothness/strong-convexity-on-compact assumptions.
- Extends prior error-bound based linear convergence results (Tseng [20], Zhang et al. [22]) to the trace-norm setting; contribution is largely technical.
- Proofs are generally clear and well-structured.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical extension of linear convergence guarantees to trace-norm regularization.
- Weakness: Practical impact is unclear due to primarily technical contribution.
- Weakness: No numerical experiments demonstrating the linear convergence rate in practice.
- Weakness: Insufficient explanation of how derived constants depend on step-size parameters ᾱ and α.
- Request: More precise references and clearer delineation of novel contributions versus prior work (especially Tseng [20]).
- Request: Include a numerical example (e.g., semi-log plot of objective vs iterations) to empirically validate linear convergence.
- Request: Clarify dependence of κ1–κ4 on step-size choices and discuss how parameter selection affects convergence rate.
- Comment: The inequality sequence before (13) is confusing; the final step relies on convexity and subgradient membership, not the intermediate inequality as written.
- Overall: Solid theoretical result but incremental and weakened by lack of practical validation and parameter-sensitivity discussion.
- For acceptance: Addresses a relevant optimization problem with rigorous analysis and a useful extension to trace-norm regularization.
- Against acceptance: Lacks empirical validation, feels incremental, and provides limited practical guidance on parameter sensitivity.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept/reject; encourage authors to add numerical experiments, discuss failure conditions and parameter sensitivity, and consider extensions (e.g., ADMM).
