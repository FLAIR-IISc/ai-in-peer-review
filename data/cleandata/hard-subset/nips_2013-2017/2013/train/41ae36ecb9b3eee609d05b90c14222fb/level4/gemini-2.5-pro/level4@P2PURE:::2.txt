This paper establishes the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems. Crucially, this result is shown under assumptions weaker than the strong convexity of the loss function, a setting relevant to many machine learning and signal processing applications. The paper is clearly written and carefully follows the Lipschitzian error bound technique from Tseng [20], successfully applying it to the trace norm case, which the reviewer notes was an open question. To the best of my understanding, the main proof appears to be correct.

A key addition that would strengthen the paper is a more explicit comparison to the analogous vector case of `l1`-norm regularization (e.g., [20, 22]). It would be highly beneficial for the authors to discuss the relationship between the results and techniques used in the `l1` setting and to clarify why those techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm setting. Presumably, the `l1`-norm results should now follow as a corollary of the present work, which would be worth noting. This discussion is important for contextualizing the paper's contribution, as some readers might otherwise view the extension from `l1` to trace norm as straightforward. While I personally find this to be a valuable contribution, a clear explanation of the technical challenges would help all readers appreciate its significance.

Independent of the above, I suggest the authors reconsider the title. The primary contribution appears to be the analysis of the loss function, particularly the flexibility introduced by the linear operator A, rather than an analysis of a "structured" trace norm regularizer. A title that better reflects the focus on the loss term might be more accurate.

**Minor Issues**
*   **Notation:** The operator P is referred to as a "sampling" operator, which might imply randomness. Since P is fixed, "masking operator" could be a more precise term.
*   **Line 337:** There appears to be a broken reference.
*   The definitions of Q- and R-linear convergence may not be universally known. It would be helpful to briefly restate them for the reader's convenience.
*   To aid interpretation, the definition of the residual R(X) in equation (6) could be more explicitly linked to the original definition (46) in Tseng [20].

More broadly, the paper could be improved by expanding the discussion and conclusions. This might be achieved by shortening the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3, perhaps by moving some details to an appendix, to make space for higher-level insights.
