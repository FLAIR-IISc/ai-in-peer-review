This paper establishes the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, a significant result given the prevalence of these problems in machine learning and signal processing applications. Notably, the convergence guarantee holds under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, broadening the applicability of the method.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, building logically upon the Lipschitzian error bound technique introduced by Tseng [20]. The authors successfully extend this technique to the nuclear norm case, addressing an open question highlighted in previous work. Based on my understanding, the core of the proof appears sound. 

However, a stronger connection to the existing literature on l1-norm regularization would significantly enhance the paper’s impact. A detailed comparison or analogy to the vector case, referencing works such as [20, 22] and related studies, is currently missing. Discussing whether and why techniques developed for l1-regularization do not directly translate to the trace norm setting would provide valuable insight. Furthermore, demonstrating that the l1-norm result emerges as a corollary of the trace norm result would strengthen the paper’s completeness. Assessing the paper's novelty requires considering this connection; while I personally find the contribution valuable, some readers might perceive the extension from the l1-case as less surprising without this explicit discussion.

I also suggest reconsidering the paper’s title. The primary contribution appears to lie in the relaxation of assumptions on the loss function rather than in characterizing the trace norm regularization itself as “structured.” The flexibility gained through the linear operator A primarily impacts the loss function, not the regularization term.

Several minor points could further improve the manuscript:

* **Notation:** The operator *P* is currently described without sufficient clarity. While "sampling operator" might suggest randomness, *P* appears to be a fixed matrix. A more precise term, such as “mask operator” or simply “linear operator”, would be preferable.
* **Reference:** Line 337 contains a broken reference that requires correction.
* **Convergence Rates:** The definitions of “Q-linear convergence” and “R-linear convergence” are potentially unfamiliar to some readers. Briefly reiterating these definitions would enhance clarity.
* **Residual Explanation:** The residual *R(X)*, defined in equation (6), could benefit from a more explicit link to its original definition in [20] (equation (46)). This connection would aid in interpreting its meaning within the context of the trace norm problem.
* **Lemma 3.2 Discussion:** The technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 is lengthy. Including additional discussion or concluding remarks after the proof would improve readability and motivation.
