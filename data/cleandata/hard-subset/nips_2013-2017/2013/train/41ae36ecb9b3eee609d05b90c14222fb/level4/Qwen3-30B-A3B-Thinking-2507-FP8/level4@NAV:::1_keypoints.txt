- Reviewer requests adding a simulation showing linear convergence of PG when loss is not strongly convex (for varying stability levels), discussing theory failure cases, and addressing extension to ADMM.  
- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized learning when f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear, h strongly convex on compacts and Lipschitz gradient.  
- Work extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] results (lasso/group lasso) to the trace norm; extension is non-trivial but mainly technical.  
- Proofs are generally well-presented.  
- Strength: demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm for trace-norm regularization.  
- Weakness: contribution appears largely technical rather than conceptual.  
- Weakness: a numerical example confirming linear convergence (e.g., semilog plot of objective vs iterations) is required.  
- Detailed comment: Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirror Tseng’s work; authors should more precisely cite the original and clarify novel parts.  
- Detailed comment: A semilog numerical example showing linear convergence is essential.  
- Detailed comment: Dependence of constants κ1,…,κ4 on α̲ and ᾱ is unclear and should be clarified.  
- Minor issue: The inequality sequence before (13) is confusing; the final step relies on trace-norm convexity and −Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖* (per p.289 of [20]).  
- Summary: The paper extends the error-bound convergence framework from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm.
