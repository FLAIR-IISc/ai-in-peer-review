

This paper addresses the convergence rate of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems, establishing linear convergence under relaxed assumptions compared to previous work. The authors demonstrate that for loss functions of the form f(X) = h(A(X)) with h satisfying certain smoothness and convexity conditions, the PGM achieves linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of f. This is a notable extension of existing results, as many machine learning loss functions (e.g., square loss in matrix completion) are not strongly convex.

The paper follows the established framework of Luo and Tseng [10] for establishing linear convergence rates via error bounds. The key contribution is a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization, which the authors establish by proving that optimal solutions and their gradients share the same singular vectors (Proposition 4.2), enabling a simultaneous diagonalization approach. This insight appears novel and is the main technical contribution of the paper.

Quality: The paper is technically sound overall, though it requires clarification on a key inequality. Specifically, the justification for inequality (11) (R(Xk) ≤ κ3‖Xk - Xk+1‖F) in the main text is incomplete. The draft review correctly identifies that the appendix (line 085) does not sufficiently explain why the choice of κ3 (with α < 1) ensures this inequality holds. The authors should explicitly verify that their step size parameters guarantee this relationship. The connection to Sra's NIPS 2012 work [1] should be acknowledged in the main text to properly contextualize their contribution.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written, though the presentation could be improved. The authors state the main convergence result (Theorem 3.2) before establishing the key error bound (Theorem 3.1), creating a slight disconnect in the narrative flow. It would be helpful to first present the main convergence result and then explain how the error bound enables it, rather than proving the error bound before stating its consequences. The technical details of the error bound proof are quite dense and might be better placed in a supplementary section.

Originality: The paper's main contribution is the observation that optimal solutions and their gradients share the same singular vectors (Proposition 4.2), which enables the simultaneous diagonalization approach. This insight appears novel and is the key technical advance. While the overall framework follows Luo and Tseng, the specific adaptation to trace norm regularization addresses a gap in the literature. The authors should more clearly emphasize this contribution as the key novelty.

Significance: The theoretical result is significant for understanding the convergence behavior of PGM for trace norm regularization, which is widely used in machine learning applications. However, the linear convergence is established asymptotically (only when iterates are sufficiently close to the optimal solution), which limits immediate practical impact. The paper does not provide new algorithmic insights or practical improvements, but rather a deeper theoretical understanding of existing methods. This is valuable for the theoretical community but may have limited direct impact on practitioners.

Overall, this paper makes a solid theoretical contribution to understanding proximal gradient methods for trace norm regularization. The key technical insight about simultaneous diagonalization is novel and valuable, though the overall approach follows a well-established framework. The main weaknesses are the unclear justification for the constant κ3 and the presentation of the main result before the key technical contribution.

The paper should be accepted as it makes a meaningful theoretical contribution to an important problem in machine learning optimization. It advances our understanding of when first-order methods can achieve linear convergence for non-strongly convex problems, which is relevant to many machine learning applications.

Pro:
- Establishes linear convergence of PGM for trace norm regularization without strong convexity
- Novel insight about simultaneous diagonalization of matrices
- Theoretical contribution advances understanding of optimization for machine learning

Con:
- Asymptotic nature of convergence (not immediately applicable to early iterations)
- Incomplete justification for the constant κ3 in the error bound
- Limited practical implications despite theoretical significance
- The main result is established by following a well-known framework

The paper is suitable for NIPS as it makes a clear theoretical contribution to an important problem in machine learning optimization.
