- The inequality |Rk| ≤ κ |Xk − Xk+1| was already established in S. Sra, NIPS 2012 (Lemma 4).  
- Proximal gradient is the standard method for trace-norm regularized risk minimization with known sublinear global convergence.  
- Linear convergence typically requires strong convexity; this paper relaxes that assumption using the Luo–Tseng framework.  
- The authors prove a local error bound and use it to establish asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization.  
- The local error bound proof follows conventional techniques but includes a novel simultaneous diagonalization to reduce to the vector case.  
- Paper appears technically sound, but Appendix line 085 needs clarification on why choosing κ3 (with α < 1) implies R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Readability is good, but a clearer roadmap is recommended: state main claims (especially the local error bound) before detailed proofs.  
- Suggestion to defer heavy technical derivations so readers can first see the linear convergence result, which is straightforward from the bound.  
- Originality is moderate: simultaneous diagonalization is interesting, but the main approach adapts existing Luo–Tseng methodology.  
- Significance is modest and incremental: extends linear convergence to a relaxed condition for trace-norm problems, but result is asymptotic; diagonalization may have independent value.  
- Overall: a clean, incremental theoretical extension showing proximal gradient attains linear convergence under relaxed assumptions for trace-norm regularization.
