The paper proves the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function. This is a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal processing applications. 

The paper is well-written and clearly presented, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng [20]. The authors successfully apply this technique to the nuclear norm case, which was previously left as an open question. Upon reviewing the main part of the proof, it appears to be correct.

However, a clear comparison or analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as seen in [20,22] and related work, is missing. It would be beneficial to relate the results and techniques used in the l1-case to the trace norm case, explaining why the l1-case techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm case. Additionally, it should be noted that the l1-norm case should now follow from the trace norm case as a corollary.

When evaluating the value of the paper, this omission should be taken into account. Some readers might find it unsurprising that the l1-result also holds for the trace norm (although this reviewer still finds it a valuable contribution).

A minor suggestion is to consider revising the title of the paper. The main new contribution affects the loss part more than making the trace norm regularization a structured one, as the flexibility gained by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regularizer.

Minor issues include:
- Notation: The term "sampling or mask operator" could be clarified, as "sampling" might imply randomness, but P is fixed.
- Reference [l337] is broken.
- The meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence could be repeated for clarity, as they may not be widely known.
- The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be further explained in relation to the original definition (46) by [20] for better interpretation.

In general, it would be beneficial to include more discussion and conclusions in favor of the long technical part of the proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.

Update after author feedback:
The authors have addressed most questions. Regarding the title, this reviewer suggested removing the word "structured," which was deemed unnecessary in this context. However, the decision is left to the authors.
