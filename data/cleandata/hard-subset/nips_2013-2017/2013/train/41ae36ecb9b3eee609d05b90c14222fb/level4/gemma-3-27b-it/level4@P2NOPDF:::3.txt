This paper investigates the convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm for trace norm regularized risk minimization, relaxing the standard strong convexity assumption. Existing work demonstrates sublinear convergence in general and linear convergence when strong convexity holds. This work, building on the framework of Luo and Tseng, establishes a local error bound that leads to a proof of asymptotic linear convergence under a weaker convexity condition. The core contribution lies in a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of relevant matrices, which simplifies the analysis.

**Quality:**

The paper appears technically sound. I have one specific question regarding the appendix (line 85): Could the authors please elaborate on why choosing a particular value for kappa3 (specifically, alpha < 1) leads to the stated inequality, R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F? Clarification on this point would strengthen the presentation. 

**Clarity:**

The paper is generally well-written and accessible, though the technical nature of the subject matter demands careful attention. To enhance readability, I suggest restructuring the presentation to first clearly state the main results – particularly the local error bound and the resulting linear convergence rate – before delving into the detailed proof. After establishing the local error bound, the authors could postpone the complete technical proof and instead immediately proceed to demonstrate the linear convergence rate, as the latter builds directly on the former and contains relatively few novel elements.

**Originality:**

The originality of this work is moderate. The key innovation – the observation allowing simultaneous diagonalization of matrices – is both interesting and novel. However, the remainder of the approach to establishing the local error bound largely follows existing methodologies. Therefore, while the diagonalization technique is a valuable contribution, the overall approach is largely incremental.

**Significance:**

The significance of this paper resides primarily in its theoretical contribution. While extending the conditions for linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm to more practically relevant, less strongly convex scenarios is a positive step, the asymptotic nature of the result somewhat limits its immediate impact on algorithm design. The matrix diagonalization technique itself might be of independent interest to researchers. In summary, this work demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed form of strong convexity. The authors effectively leverage the established framework of Luo and Tseng, enhanced by their novel diagonalization observation, which effectively reduces the problem to a vector-valued case. Ultimately, this is an incremental contribution with moderate theoretical interest.
