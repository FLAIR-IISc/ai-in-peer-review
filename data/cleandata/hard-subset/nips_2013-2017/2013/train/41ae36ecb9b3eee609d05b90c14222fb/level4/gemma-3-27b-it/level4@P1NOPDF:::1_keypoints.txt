- Main claim: Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)), where h is locally strongly convex and has Lipschitz gradient.
- Contribution: Extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] linear-convergence/error-bound results from lasso/group lasso to trace-norm regularization.
- Novelty concern: The extension is non-trivial but appears primarily technical; authors should better emphasize original contributions.
- Presentation: Proofs are generally clear.
- Strength: Demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm for the trace-norm case.
- Weakness: Lacks a numerical example illustrating the claimed linear convergence behavior.
- Proof similarity: Outlines of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely resemble Tseng’s proofs; need more precise citation and clearer statement of specific novelties.
- Numerical recommendation: Include a semilog plot (function value vs iterations) to explicitly show linear convergence.
- Clarification needed: How constants κ1–κ4 depend on choices of α and ᾱ is unclear and should be explained.
- Minor issue: The inequality sequence before (13) is confusing; the final step follows from convexity of the trace norm and -̄G ∈ τ∂||X||*, not the stated intermediate inequality.
- Overall summary: Paper extends error-bound based linear-convergence results to trace-norm regularization but would benefit from clearer novelty claims and empirical validation.
- After authors’ feedback: Add a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but proximal gradient still shows linear convergence (varying stability), discuss where the theory fails, and comment on extending the analysis to ADMM.
