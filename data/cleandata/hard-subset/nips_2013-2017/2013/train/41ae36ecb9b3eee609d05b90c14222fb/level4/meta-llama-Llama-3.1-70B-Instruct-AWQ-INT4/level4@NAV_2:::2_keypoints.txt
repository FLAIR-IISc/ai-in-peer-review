- Proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity.  
- Paper is carefully and clearly written; main proof appears correct.  
- Main technical tool is a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm problems, of independent interest.  
- Result applies to many machine learning and signal-processing settings.  
- Reviewer requests a clear comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case and explanation why l1 techniques do or do not translate.  
- Some readers may find the extension from l1 to trace norm unsurprising, but the contribution is still valuable.  
- Suggests changing the title to reflect that the main novelty concerns the loss/operator A rather than the structure of the regularizer.  
- Minor notation issue: calling P a sampling/mask operator may misleadingly suggest randomness though P is fixed.  
- Broken reference at line 337 needs fixing.  
- Recommend repeating/clarifying meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence for broader readability.  
- Ask for additional explanation of the residual R(X) in (6) and its relation to Tseng’s original definition.  
- Recommend more discussion or conclusions to justify the lengthy technical Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication; contributions are significant and proofs are sound.
