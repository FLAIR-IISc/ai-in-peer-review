- Clarity: paper is well-written, but would benefit from presenting main claims up front and then detailing proofs.  
- Originality: moderate overall—main technique follows existing approaches except for the simultaneous diagonalization insight.  
- Significance: mainly theoretical with limited impact on algorithm design; asymptotic nature of the convergence claim reduces practical significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: detailed local error bound proof, novel diagonalization observation, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: moderate originality, limited algorithmic impact, and an asymptotic (not finite-time) convergence result.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to fix the appendix gap and improve accessibility for non-expert readers.
