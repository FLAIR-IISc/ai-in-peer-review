The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng, whose application to the nuclear norm case was still left as an open question. The main part of the proof seems correct as far as I can understand.

The authors establish the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for a class of trace norm-regularized problems. Their convergence result does not require the objective function to be strongly convex and is applicable to many settings in machine learning. The key technical tool in the proof is a Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems, which could be of independent interest.

However, I would like to see a clear comparison or analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as in related work. It would be very helpful to relate to the results and techniques used in that case and to explain if or why the l1-case techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm case.

As for judging the value of the paper, this should be taken into account, as some people might find it not extremely surprising that the l1-result also holds for the trace norm (I still find it a very valuable contribution though).

I would suggest a slight change of the title of the paper, as the main new contribution affects the loss part more than making the trace norm regularization a structured one. Since the newly gained flexibility by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regularizer.

Minor issues include notation, where calling P a sampling or mask operator might hint at randomness, but here P is fixed. Additionally, reference l337 is broken. The meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence are maybe not that widely known and could be repeated for clarity. The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be additionally explained in relation to the original definition by Tseng for better interpretation.

In general, I would vote to include a bit more discussion and maybe conclusions in favor of the long technical part of the proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proximal gradient method and establish its linear convergence for a class of trace norm-regularized problems. The paper is well-written, and the proof seems correct. However, I would like to see a clearer comparison to the vector case of l1-norm regularization and a more detailed explanation of the residual R(X).

The paper's contributions are significant, as it establishes the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for a class of problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss function. The key technical tool in the proof is a Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems, which could be of independent interest.

Overall, I would recommend this paper for publication, as it provides a valuable contribution to the field of machine learning and signal processing.
