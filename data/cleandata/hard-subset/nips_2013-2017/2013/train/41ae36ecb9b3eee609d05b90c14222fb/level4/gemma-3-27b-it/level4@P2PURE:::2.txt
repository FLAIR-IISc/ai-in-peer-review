This paper establishes linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss function—a significant challenge in many machine learning and signal processing applications. 

The paper is meticulously written and clearly presented. It builds directly upon the Lipschitzian error bound technique introduced by Tseng [20], extending its application to the nuclear norm case, which was previously an open question. The core argument of the proof appears sound, to the best of my understanding.

However, the paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison to, and analogy with, the vector-valued case of l1-norm regularization, as explored in [20, 22] and related works. Relating the results and techniques used in the l1-norm setting would be valuable, particularly an explanation of why existing l1-norm techniques do not directly generalize to the trace norm setting. (Conversely, the l1-norm result should naturally follow as a corollary of the presented trace norm result.) Assessing the paper’s novelty requires this context, as some readers might anticipate the extension from the l1-norm case. Nevertheless, I consider this a valuable contribution.

Furthermore, I suggest revising the title. The primary contribution concerns the conditions on the loss function rather than the structural properties of the trace norm regularization itself. The added flexibility stems from the linear operator A acting on the loss, not from characteristics of the regularizer.

- In general, adding more discussion or a concluding summary following the technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 would enhance the paper’s readability.
