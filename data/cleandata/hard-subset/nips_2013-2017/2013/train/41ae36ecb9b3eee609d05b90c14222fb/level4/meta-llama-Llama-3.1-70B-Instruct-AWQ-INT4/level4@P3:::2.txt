The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial problem for many machine learning (ML) and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng, whose application to the nuclear norm case was still left as an open question. The main part of the proof seems correct as far as I can understand.

One aspect that I think is missing in this paper is a clear comparison/analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as in related work. It would be very helpful to relate to the results and techniques used in that case and explain if/why the l1-case techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm case. Additionally, the authors should clarify that the l1-norm case should follow from the trace norm one as a corollary. This comparison is essential for judging the value of the paper, as some people might find it not extremely surprising that the l1-result also holds for trace norm (although I still find it a very valuable contribution).

I suggest a slight change of the title of the paper, as the main new contribution affects the loss part more than making the trace norm regularization a structured one. Since the newly gained flexibility by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regularizer, the title could be rephrased to better reflect this.

Minor issues include:
- Notation: Calling P a sampling or mask operator? (sampling might hint at randomness, but here P is fixed)
- Reference l337 is broken
- The meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence are maybe not that widely known and could be repeated for clarity
- The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be additionally explained in relation to the original definition by Tseng for better interpretation

In general, I would suggest including a bit more discussion and maybe conclusions in favor of the long technical part of the proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.

After receiving feedback from the authors, I appreciate the clarification on most points. Regarding the title, I was referring to the word "structured," which I found unnecessary in the context here, but I leave that to the authors' discretion.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a good effort to explain the technical details clearly.

Originality: The paper makes a significant contribution to the field by establishing linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function.

Significance: The results have important implications for many machine learning and signal processing applications, where trace norm regularization is commonly used.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
