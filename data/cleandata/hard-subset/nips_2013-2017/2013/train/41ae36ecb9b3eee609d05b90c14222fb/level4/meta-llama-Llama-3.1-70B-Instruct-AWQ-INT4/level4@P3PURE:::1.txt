This paper proves the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method applied to the trace-norm regularized learning problem when the loss function has the form of f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is linear and h is strongly convex on any compact set and has a Lipschitz gradient. 

The authors extend the result of Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], which established the same result using the "error-bound condition" for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm. Although this extension is non-trivial, the contribution appears to be purely technical.

The presentation of the proofs is mostly clear, but there are a few areas that require improvement. To strengthen the paper, we suggest adding a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but the proximal gradient algorithm converges linearly (for different levels of stability), and importantly discussing when the theory fails. Furthermore, it would be beneficial to discuss how this analysis can be extended to the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM).

The paper's main strength lies in showing the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending the result of Tseng et al. However, there are a few weaknesses that need to be addressed. The contribution seems purely technical, and the authors should consider adding a numerical example to demonstrate the linear convergence (e.g., a semilog plot of function value vs. the number of iterations).

Some additional comments and suggestions are as follows:

1. The outlines of the proofs of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 appear very similar to those of Tseng. To make their contribution clearer, the authors should refer to the original work more precisely.

2. A numerical example demonstrating the linear convergence would significantly enhance the paper.

3. It is not clear how the constants κ1, ..., κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ and α. The authors should provide more insight into this relationship.

Minor issue:

4. The sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing. The last inequality follows due to the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||* and not because of the intermediate inequality (see p289 in [20]).
