**Comments Following Author Rebuttal**

To further strengthen the paper, I recommend including a simulation where the loss function is not strongly convex, yet the proximal gradient algorithm still exhibits linear convergence (e.g., for different levels of stability). Crucially, this section should also discuss the conditions under which the presented theory fails. A discussion on how this analysis could be extended to ADMM would also be a valuable addition.

***

This paper establishes the linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized learning problems. The analysis applies to loss functions of the form f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is a linear operator, and h is strongly convex on any compact set and has a Lipschitz continuous gradient.

The work extends the results of Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], who used the "error-bound condition" to prove analogous linear convergence results for lasso and group lasso problems. While this extension to the trace norm setting is non-trivial, the contribution appears to be primarily technical in nature. The presentation of the proofs is generally clear.

**Strengths:**
-   The paper successfully extends the linear convergence analysis based on the error-bound condition from vector-based regularization (lasso, group lasso) to matrix-based trace-norm regularization.

**Weaknesses:**
-   The contribution, while mathematically sound, is incremental and highly technical.
-   The theoretical claims are not supported by any numerical experiments to demonstrate the predicted linear convergence in practice.

**Detailed Comments:**

1.  The proof strategies for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 appear very similar to those in Tseng [20]. To better delineate their novel contributions, the authors should provide a more precise and detailed comparison with the original proof techniques.

2.  The paper would be significantly strengthened by including a numerical example that demonstrates the claimed linear convergence rate. A semilog plot of the objective function value versus the iteration count would be particularly illustrative.

3.  It is not clear how the constants κ₁, ..., κ₄ depend on the choice of the bounds `α_min` and `α_max`. The authors should explicitly state this relationship for clarity.

**Minor Issue:**

4.  The sequence of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is difficult to follow. The final inequality appears to be a consequence of the convexity of the trace norm and the subgradient condition `-\bar{G} ∈ τ∂||X||_*`, rather than the intermediate inequality in the chain. Clarifying this derivation, which is analogous to a point made in [20] (p. 289), would improve the paper's readability.
