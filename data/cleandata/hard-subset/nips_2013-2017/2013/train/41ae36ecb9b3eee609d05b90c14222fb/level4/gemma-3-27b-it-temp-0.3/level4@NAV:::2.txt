This paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, a significant result given its relevance to numerous machine learning and signal processing applications, and achieved under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss function. The manuscript is well-written and clearly presented, building logically upon the Lipschitzian error bound technique introduced by Tseng [20]. The core proof appears sound based on my understanding.

However, the paper would benefit from a more explicit discussion connecting these findings to the established results for l1-norm regularization, as seen in [20, 22] and related work. A detailed comparison of techniques and an explanation of why methods successful in the l1-case do not directly generalize to the trace norm setting would strengthen the contribution. Ideally, the l1-norm result should emerge as a direct corollary of this work. Assessing the paper’s novelty is partially dependent on this connection, as the extension from l1 to trace norm might be perceived as unsurprising by some, although I still consider it a valuable contribution. Furthermore, I suggest reconsidering the paper’s title; the primary advancement lies in the relaxation of assumptions on the loss function rather than in characterizing the trace norm regularization as “structured.” The flexibility gained through the linear operator A impacts the loss term more directly than the regularizer itself.

Several minor points should be addressed: the notation for *P* could be clarified (using “mask operator” might be preferable to “sampling operator” given its deterministic nature); reference [l337] is currently broken; the definitions of “Q-” and “R-” linear convergence could be briefly reiterated for broader accessibility; and the residual *R(X)* defined in (6) could be further linked to the original definition in [20] (equation 46) to aid interpretation. Finally, expanding the discussion and potentially adding concluding remarks following the technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 would be beneficial.
