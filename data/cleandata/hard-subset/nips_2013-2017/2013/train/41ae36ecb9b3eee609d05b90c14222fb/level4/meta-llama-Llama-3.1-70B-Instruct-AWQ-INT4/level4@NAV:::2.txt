This paper establishes the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for problems regularized by the trace norm, under conditions weaker than strong convexity of the loss function - a crucial consideration in numerous machine learning and signal processing applications.

The manuscript is well-organized and clearly written, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound technique introduced by Tseng [20], whose application to the nuclear norm case remained an open question. Upon examination, the core of the proof appears to be correct.

However, a notable omission in this work is a clear comparison or analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as seen in [20, 22] and related studies. It would be beneficial to relate the results and techniques employed in that case and explain why the l1-case techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm case. Notably, the l1-norm case should now follow as a corollary from the trace norm case.

When evaluating the paper's value, this omission should be taken into account, as some readers might find the extension of the l1-result to the trace norm case somewhat expected. Nevertheless, this contribution is still highly valuable.

Furthermore, I suggest a slight modification to the paper's title, as the primary new contribution affects the loss function rather than the structured nature of the trace norm regularization. The increased flexibility introduced by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regularizer.

Minor issues include:

* Notation: The term 'P' is referred to as a sampling or mask operator, which might be misleading, as 'sampling' implies randomness, whereas 'P' is fixed.
* Reference [l337] is broken.
* The definitions of 'Q-' and 'R-' linear convergence might not be widely known and could be repeated for clarity.
* The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be further explained in relation to the original definition (46) by [20] for better interpretation.

In general, I recommend including more discussion and possibly conclusions to support the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.

After receiving author feedback, I appreciate the response to most of my concerns. Regarding the title, I suggested removing the word 'structured,' which I found unnecessary in this context, although I leave the final decision to the authors.
