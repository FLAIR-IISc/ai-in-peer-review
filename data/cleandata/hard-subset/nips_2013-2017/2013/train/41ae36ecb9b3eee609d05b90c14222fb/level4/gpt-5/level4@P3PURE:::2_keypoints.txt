- Proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm–regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity.  
- Addresses an important practical need in many machine learning and signal-processing applications.  
- Paper is carefully written and the main proof appears correct, closely following Tseng’s Lipschitzian error-bound technique; applying this to the nuclear norm was an open question.  
- Reviewer requests an explicit comparison/analogy to the vector ℓ1-norm case (as in [20,22])—relate results and proof techniques directly.  
- Explain why ℓ1 techniques do not directly carry over to the trace norm or how they must be modified; show the ℓ1 result as a corollary of the trace-norm analysis if possible.  
- Title suggestion: reconsider the word “structured,” since the novelty seems to come from relaxed loss assumptions (via linear operator A) rather than added structure in the trace norm.  
- Notation: suggest calling P a “mask” operator instead of “sampling” because P is fixed and “sampling” implies randomness.  
- Fix broken reference at line 337.  
- Briefly remind readers of the meanings of Q-linear and R-linear convergence to aid readability.  
- Better motivate the residual R(X) in (6) by explicitly relating it to Tseng’s original definition (Eq. (46) in [20]).  
- Add more high-level discussion and takeaways to balance the long technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.  
- After author feedback, most questions were addressed; reviewer reiterates the title concern and restates the main contribution (linear convergence under weaker assumptions).
