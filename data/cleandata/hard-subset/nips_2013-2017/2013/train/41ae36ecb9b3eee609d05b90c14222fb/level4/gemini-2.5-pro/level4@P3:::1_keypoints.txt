- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring global strong convexity, for f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex on compacts.  
- Technical novelty: establishes a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization (Theorem 3.1) using matrix perturbation theory and spectral analysis.  
- Overcomes challenge: handles the trace norm’s non-polyhedral, non-separable nature by exploiting shared singular vector structure of solutions and gradients.  
- Theoretical impact: fills an important gap and provides justification for fast PGM convergence observed in practice (e.g., matrix completion).  
- Presentation: paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a clear proof sketch (notably via Lemma 4.1).  
- Major weakness: entirely theoretical — lacks numerical experiments (e.g., semilog plots of F(X_k)-v* vs iterations) to validate predicted linear rates and dependence on problem parameters.  
- Clarity suggestion: more explicitly separate standard uses of the Luo–Tseng framework from the novel trace-norm-specific arguments to highlight the paper’s core innovation.  
- Scope suggestion: discuss practical implications and limitations of assumptions, when they might fail, expected behavior then, and possible extensions to other algorithms (e.g., ADMM).  
- Minor issue: state how constants κ1, κ2, κ3 depend on α, ᾱ, and Lf (relation to step size).  
- Minor issue: clarify the derivation of inequality (18) in Theorem 3.1 — the τ‖X̄k‖∗ step should be tied explicitly to the subgradient optimality condition.  
- Overall verdict: strong, novel theoretical contribution of independent interest; recommend acceptance subject to adding experiments and clearer exposition of novel proof elements.
