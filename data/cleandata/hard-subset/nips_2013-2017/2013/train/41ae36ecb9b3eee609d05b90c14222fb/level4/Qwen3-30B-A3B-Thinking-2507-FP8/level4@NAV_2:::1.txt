

This paper establishes the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized learning problems when the loss function takes the form f(X) = h(A(X)), with A being a linear operator and h being strongly convex on compact sets with Lipschitz continuous gradient.

This work extends the linear convergence results for the proximal gradient method from lasso and group lasso (previously established by Tseng [20], Tseng and Yun [22], and Zhang et al. [22]) to the trace norm setting. While this extension is non-trivial, the contribution appears primarily technical in nature.

The presentation of the proofs is generally clear.

Strengths:
- Demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending the results of Tseng et al.

Weaknesses:
- The contribution is largely technical in nature.
- A numerical example demonstrating linear convergence (e.g., a semi-log plot of objective value versus iteration count) is needed to support the theoretical claims.

More details:
1. The proof structure of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely parallels that of Tseng's work. The authors should more precisely cite the original contributions to clarify their specific advancements.
2. A numerical example showing linear convergence (a semi-log plot of objective value versus iterations) is required to substantiate the theoretical results.
3. The dependence of constants κ₁, ..., κ₄ on the step size bounds α̲ and ᾱ is not explicitly addressed.
4. The sequence of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is confusing. The final inequality follows from the convexity of the trace norm and the inclusion -Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖* rather than from the intermediate inequality (cf. p. 289 in [20]).

The extension of previous linear convergence results based on the "error-bound condition" from lasso and group lasso to the trace norm is the main contribution.
