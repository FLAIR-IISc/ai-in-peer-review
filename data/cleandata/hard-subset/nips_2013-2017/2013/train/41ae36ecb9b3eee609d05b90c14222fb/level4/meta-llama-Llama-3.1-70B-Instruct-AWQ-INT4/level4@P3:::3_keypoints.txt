- Main contribution: Establishes asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for a class of trace norm–regularized problems using a relaxed strong-convexity assumption (local error bound).  
- Technical tool: Introduces a Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm–regularized problems, a key proof ingredient of potential independent interest.  
- Analysis quality: Technically sound and thorough convergence analysis.  
- Novelty: Moderate originality—builds on Luo & Tseng but includes a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.  
- Experiments: Provides numerical experiments that demonstrate PGM’s practical effectiveness.  
- Clarity: Writing is good overall but paper structure should be improved by presenting main claims/results up front.  
- Significance: Primarily a theoretical contribution with limited direct impact on algorithm design, though it extends linear rates to more practical settings.  
- Practical limitation: The convergence result is asymptotic, which may limit immediate practical applicability.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-written, technically sound contribution likely of interest to optimization researchers.  
- Recommendation and score: Accept with revisions to improve clarity/structure; rating 7/10.
