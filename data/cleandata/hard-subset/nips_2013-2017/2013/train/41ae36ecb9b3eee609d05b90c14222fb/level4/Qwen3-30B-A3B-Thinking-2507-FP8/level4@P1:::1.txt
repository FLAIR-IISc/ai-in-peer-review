

This paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss function. The authors show that when the loss function takes the form f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is a linear operator and h is strictly convex with Lipschitz gradient on compact subsets of its domain, the PGM converges linearly. The key contribution is a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization, which resolves an open question about whether such bounds exist for trace norm (unlike ℓ₁-norm or group LASSO regularization where they are known).

The paper builds upon the error-bound framework established by Luo and Tseng [10], extending their results from ℓ₁-norm and group LASSO to trace norm. This extension is non-trivial due to the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm and the interdependence of matrix entries in singular value decomposition. The authors demonstrate that for optimal solutions X̄, both X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share the same singular vectors, enabling the use of matrix perturbation theory to establish the error bound.

**Quality:** The theoretical contribution is sound and well-supported. The proof of the error bound is technically sophisticated, addressing a significant gap in the literature. The authors establish linear convergence without strong convexity assumptions, which is a meaningful advancement over prior work. The numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification problems corroborate the theoretical findings, showing linear convergence in practice once iterates are sufficiently close to the optimal set. However, the paper would benefit from more detailed discussion of the constants in the convergence rate (e.g., how κ₁,..., κ₄ depend on the step size bounds α̲ and ᾱ).

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written. The technical exposition is precise, with appropriate notation and definitions. The connection between the error bound and the convergence analysis is well-explained. However, the sequence of inequalities leading to (13) could be clarified to explicitly state that the final inequality follows from convexity of the trace norm and the optimality condition (−Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X̄‖*), rather than the intermediate inequality.

**Originality:** The work extends the error-bound framework from ℓ₁-norm and group LASSO regularization to trace norm, which is non-trivial due to the different structural properties of these norms. While the overall approach follows Luo and Tseng [10], the adaptation to trace norm requires novel technical insights. This represents a meaningful contribution to the understanding of first-order methods for matrix completion and related problems, though the novelty is primarily technical rather than conceptual.

**Significance:** The results have important implications for machine learning applications where trace-norm regularization is used, such as matrix completion and multi-task learning. The linear convergence guarantee without strong convexity assumptions broadens the applicability of PGM to a wider range of problems. The error bound itself may be of independent interest to researchers working on convex optimization with matrix variables.

**Strengths:**
- Establishes linear convergence for a broader class of loss functions than previous work
- Provides a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization
- Includes numerical experiments demonstrating linear convergence in practice
- Clearly explains the technical challenges in extending the error bound from ℓ₁-norm to trace norm

**Weaknesses:**
- The contribution is primarily technical rather than conceptual
- The numerical experiments lack a semi-log plot showing linear convergence (as requested in the review)
- The dependence of convergence rate constants on step size bounds is not fully characterized
- The connection to ADMM is not explored, which would be a natural extension

**Recommendation:** Accept

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- The linear convergence result is theoretically significant and fills an important gap
- The error bound is a valuable technical contribution with potential applications beyond the specific convergence analysis
- The numerical experiments support the theoretical findings
- The paper is well-written and technically sound

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- The contribution is primarily technical rather than conceptual
- The numerical evidence for linear convergence could be more explicit
- The paper does not explore potential extensions to other algorithms like ADMM

The authors should address the following points in revision:
1. Include a semi-log plot showing linear convergence (objective value vs. iterations) for a specific example where the loss is not strongly convex
2. Clarify the dependence of convergence rate constants on the step size bounds α̲ and ᾱ
3. Discuss how the error bound might be extended to analyze ADMM for trace-norm regularization
4. Provide a more precise citation of prior work to clarify the technical contribution relative to Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22]

Overall, this is a solid theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of first-order methods for trace-norm regularization. While the novelty is primarily technical, the results are significant for practitioners and researchers working on low-rank matrix problems in machine learning. The paper would be strengthened by the suggested revisions, but as is, it represents a valuable contribution to the field.
