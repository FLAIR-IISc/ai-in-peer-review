- Main result: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity.
- Significance: extends Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique to the nuclear-norm case, addressing a practical ML/signal-processing problem.
- Presentation and correctness: paper is carefully written and the main proof components appear correct.
- Major omission: lacks explicit comparison to the l1-norm (vector) literature [20,22] and related works.
- Novelty clarification: should explain whether and why l1-norm techniques do not directly translate to the trace-norm setting.
- Framing: note that the l1 case may follow as a corollary—authors should frame contributions in this broader context.
- Title suggestion: “structured” may be misleading; the key novelty is the linear operator A in the loss, so consider retitling to reflect that.
- Notation: “sampling operator” for P is potentially misleading; “mask operator” recommended.
- Definitions: briefly restate Q-linear and R-linear convergence for broader audience clarity.
- Residual interpretation: explicitly connect the residual R(X) in (6) to Tseng’s formulation (46).
- Broken reference: the reference on line 337 appears to be broken and should be fixed.
- Content balance: consider condensing the technical proof of Lemma 3.2 to add more high-level discussion, interpretation, and a conclusion.
