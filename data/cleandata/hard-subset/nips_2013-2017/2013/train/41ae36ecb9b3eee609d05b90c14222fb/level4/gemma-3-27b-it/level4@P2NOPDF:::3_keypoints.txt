- Paper studies proximal gradient convergence for trace-norm regularized risk minimization, proving asymptotic linear convergence under a weaker convexity condition via a local error bound.  
- Main technical contribution: a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of relevant matrices, reducing the problem to a vector-valued case.  
- Paper appears technically sound overall.  
- Reviewer requests clarification in appendix on why choosing kappa3 with alpha < 1 yields R(X^k) <= kappa3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Suggests restructuring: state main results (local error bound and linear rate) upfront, then present proofs, and derive linear convergence immediately after the bound.  
- Originality rated moderate: diagonalization trick is novel/interesting, but the rest follows existing methodologies.  
- Significance: primarily theoretical; extends linear convergence to less restrictive convexity but is asymptotic, limiting immediate algorithmic impact.  
- Matrix diagonalization may be of independent interest to researchers.  
- After rebuttal, reviewer notes the |R_k| <= kappa ||X_k - X_{k+1}|| result is explicitly established in S. Sra, NIPS 2012 (Lemma 4).
