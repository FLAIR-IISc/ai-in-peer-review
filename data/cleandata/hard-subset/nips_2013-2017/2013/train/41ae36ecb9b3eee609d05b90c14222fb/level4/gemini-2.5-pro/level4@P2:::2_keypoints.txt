- Problem: Studies convergence rate of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems where standard PGM is sublinear and strong convexity is often unrealistic.

- Main result: Proves PGM achieves linear convergence under weaker assumptions f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex and A linear.

- Technical innovation: Develops a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace-norm objective, resolving an open question and extending Luo–Tseng analysis to the trace norm.

- Significance: Theoretical advance relevant to matrix completion and multi-task learning, improving understanding when the loss lacks strong convexity.

- Originality & quality: Novel, non-trivial proof handling global dependence of singular values; technically sound and supported by brief empirical results.

- Clarity: Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and places the contribution in context with a logical proof strategy.

- Weakness 1: Needs a more detailed comparison with l1-norm analyses (e.g., Tseng) to clarify which l1 proof steps fail for the trace norm.

- Weakness 2: Main text is proof-heavy; recommend moving technical derivations to the supplement to allow broader discussion and interpretation.

- Minor: Define Q-linear and R-linear convergence in Section 3 for a broader audience.

- Minor: Add a sentence linking the residual map R(X) in Eq. (7) to the fixed-point optimality condition in Eq. (4).

- Minor: Prefer the term "masking operator" over "sampling operator" for P to avoid ambiguity.

- Minor: Fix the broken reference at line 337.

- Recommendation: Strong theoretical contribution; improve impact by explicitly comparing to l1-norm case and addressing the minor clarity points.
