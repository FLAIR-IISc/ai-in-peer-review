

The paper establishes linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function—a crucial advancement for numerous machine learning and signal processing applications. The paper is well-written and clearly structured, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound technique from Tseng [20], whose application to the nuclear norm case remained an open question. The main proof appears correct as far as I understand it.  

* A key gap is the lack of a clear comparison to the vector case of $\ell_1$-norm regularization (e.g., as in [20, 22] and related work). Explicitly relating to these results and techniques, and explaining why $\ell_1$-case methods do not directly translate to the trace norm setting, would significantly strengthen the paper. Note that the $\ell_1$-norm case should now follow as a corollary from the trace norm result. This omission may lead some readers to perceive the contribution as less surprising, though I find it highly valuable.  

* Regarding the title, I suggest a minor revision: the primary novelty concerns the loss function (via the linear operator $A$), not the regularizer. The term "structured" in the title appears unnecessary, as the added flexibility stems from the loss side rather than the regularizer.  

* Minor issues:  
  - Notation: The operator $P$ is termed a "sampling" or "mask" operator; "sampling" may imply randomness, but $P$ is fixed.  
  - Line 337: Broken reference.  
  - The definitions of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence are not widely known and should be reiterated for clarity.  
  - The residual $R(X)$ in (6) could be further clarified in relation to the original definition (46) from [20] for better interpretation.  
  - The paper would benefit from more discussion and conclusions to justify the extensive technical details in Lemma 3.2’s proof (Section 3).
