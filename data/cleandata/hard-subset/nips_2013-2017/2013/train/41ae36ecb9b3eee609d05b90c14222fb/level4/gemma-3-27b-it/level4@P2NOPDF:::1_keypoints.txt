- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient (PG) for trace-norm regularized learning where f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex and Lipschitz on compacts.  
- Extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] results (lasso/group lasso) to the trace-norm setting; contribution is non-trivial but mainly technical.  
- Proofs are generally clear but closely resemble Tseng’s arguments; novelty relative to prior work is not sharply highlighted.  
- Main strength: establishes theoretical linear convergence in a new (trace-norm) setting building on existing error-bound techniques.  
- Main weakness: contribution feels incremental/technical rather than a significant conceptual advance.  
- Major weakness: no numerical experiments or semilog plots demonstrating the claimed linear convergence.  
- Recommendation: include a numerical example (semilog plot) and explore cases where h is not strongly convex and where theory may fail.  
- Request clarification of how constants κ1–κ4 depend on parameters α and ̄α for practical applicability.  
- Technical clarification needed: the chain of inequalities before (13) is confusing; the final step relies on trace-norm convexity and Tseng’s inclusion, not the intervening inequality.  
- Overall: logical extension with limited impact until empirical validation is added; suggest also exploring extensions to ADMM as future work.
