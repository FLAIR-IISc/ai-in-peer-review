This paper proves the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems. Notably, it achieves this under assumptions weaker than the strong convexity of the loss function, addressing a crucial problem in many machine learning and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. The analysis closely follows the Lipschitzian error bound technique introduced by Tseng [20], the application of which to the nuclear norm case has remained an open question. To the best of my understanding, the main components of the proof appear correct.

My main suggestion for improvement concerns the connection to the well-studied vector case of l1-norm regularization (e.g., [20, 22] and related work). The paper would be significantly strengthened by a discussion that relates the current results and techniques to those used for the l1-norm. In particular, it would be helpful to explain if or why the techniques from the l1 case do not directly translate to the trace norm setting. Such a discussion would also clarify whether the l1-norm case now follows as a direct corollary of this work. This context is important for assessing the paper's contribution, as some might view the extension from the l1-norm to the trace norm as an expected, if non-trivial, development. Despite this, I consider the result to be a very valuable contribution.

On a separate note, I would suggest a slight change to the paper's title. The main contribution appears to be the added flexibility in the loss function (via the linear operator A), rather than a specific focus on "structured" trace norm regularization. The current title may not fully capture the essence of the paper's novelty.

**Minor Issues**

*   **Notation**: The term "sampling operator" for P could be misleading, as "sampling" often implies randomness, while P is a fixed operator in this work. "Masking operator" might be a more precise alternative.
*   **Line 337**: The reference at this line appears to be broken.
*   **Convergence Definitions**: The concepts of "Q-linear" and "R-linear" convergence may not be universally known. For clarity, it would be helpful to include their definitions.
*   **Residual Definition**: To aid interpretation, the residual R(X) defined in (6) could be further explained by connecting it to the original definition in (46) of [20].

In general, I would recommend adjusting the balance between the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 and higher-level discussion. The paper could be improved by slightly condensing the proof in favor of expanding the discussion or adding a conclusion section to summarize the implications of the work.
