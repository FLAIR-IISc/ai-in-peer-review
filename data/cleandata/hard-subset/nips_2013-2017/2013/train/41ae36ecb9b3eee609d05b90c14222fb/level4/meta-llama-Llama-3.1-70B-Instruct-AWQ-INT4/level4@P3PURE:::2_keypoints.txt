- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity.
- Writing and proof: paper is carefully written and the main proof appears correct, following Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique.
- Novelty/importance: application of Tseng [20] to the nuclear-norm case was an open question and is a valuable contribution.
- Missing comparison: lacks clear comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case (refs [20,22]) and explanation why l1 techniques do not directly translate.
- Corollary: should explicitly state that the l1-norm result follows as a corollary of the trace-norm analysis.
- Title suggestion: the word “structured” may be misleading since the main novelty is in the loss (linear operator A), not the regularizer.
- Notation: call P a “mask operator” rather than “sampling or mask operator,” since it’s fixed and not necessarily random.
- Broken reference: reference [l337] appears to be broken and needs fixing.
- Terminology clarity: definitions/recall of Q- and R-linear convergence would help readers unfamiliar with them.
- Residual explanation: relate the residual R(X) in (6) to Tseng’s original definition (46) for better interpretation.
- Exposition: include more discussion or concluding remarks to support the long technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
- Update: reviewer accepted authors’ responses and leaves the title wording to the authors’ discretion.
