- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)), A linear and h strongly convex on compacts with Lipschitz gradient.
- Extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] results from lasso/group lasso to trace-norm regularization, but contribution appears incremental.
- Analysis is technically sound and leverages the error-bound framework; proofs are generally clear.
- Connection to prior work is insufficiently precise; authors should explicitly state where their results diverge from Tseng [20].
- No numerical experiments are provided to demonstrate linear convergence in practice.
- Missing empirical demonstration for non-strongly convex losses, which is where the contribution would be most relevant.
- Dependence of convergence constants (κ1–κ4) on step sizes (α, ᾱ) is not clarified, limiting practical applicability.
- Authors did not provide requested simulations or discuss conditions under which the theory fails.
- Extension to ADMM analysis was requested but not addressed.
- Minor technical inaccuracy: inequality before (13) should follow from Fermat’s rule/optimality, not the stated convexity argument (see Tseng [20], p.289).
- Paper should discuss limitations and failure cases (e.g., ill-conditioned problems or violated assumptions on h and A).
- Error-bound result may be of independent interest to researchers in composite optimization.
- Recommendation: reject in current form due to incremental novelty and lack of practical validation, with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing the points.
