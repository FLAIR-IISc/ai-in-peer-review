- Summary: Paper proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient methods for trace-norm regularized problems when f = h∘A with h strictly convex and locally strongly convex on compacts, even without global strong convexity.  
- Key technical contribution: A Lipschitzian error bound relating dist(X, X⋆) to the residual ‖Sτ(X − ∇f(X)) − X‖F, enabling application of the Luo–Tseng framework.  
- Spectral insight: Shows optimal X⋆ and −∇f(X⋆) share singular vectors, allowing a spectral perturbation reduction to the vector case and overcoming the trace norm’s non-polyhedrality.  
- Coverage: Results apply to common ML losses (square, logistic) and experiments illustrate the local linear regime.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on standard PGM rates and error-bound frameworks, extending non-polyhedral analysis from ℓ1/group lasso to the trace norm.  
- Methodology: Follows Luo–Tseng error-bound-based convergence template; novelty lies mainly in the spectral observation.  
- Quality: Analysis is technically sound and carefully executed; main lemmas and framework appear correctly applied.  
- Minor gap: Appendix needs a brief derivation/citation (Sra 2012) to remove ambiguity about the κ3 bound relating residual and iterate differences.  
- Experiments: Small-scale but consistent with theory, showing the predicted local linear behavior.  
- Clarity suggestions: State contributions and error bound early, move detailed proofs to appendix, define constants when first used, and specify step-size policies and Lf estimates in experiments.  
- Originality: Incremental overall but with a meaningful, nontrivial spectral argument extending error bounds to the trace-norm case.  
- Significance (theoretical): Establishes a solid asymptotic linear-rate result for trace-norm regularization without strong convexity.  
- Significance (practical): Limited immediate algorithmic impact since results are asymptotic and do not give explicit global conditions or practical constants.  
- Pros: First linear-rate result for trace norm without strong convexity; elegant Lipschitzian error bound; broad applicability; careful analysis.  
- Cons: Rate is asymptotic/local with unspecified constants and radius; limited algorithmic novelty; some presentation and citation clarifications needed.  
- Recommendation: The paper is a theoretically interesting, incremental contribution; accept pending minor clarifications (especially the κ3 inequality and improved exposition).
