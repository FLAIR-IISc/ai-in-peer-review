

The proof for |Rk| ≤ κ |Xk − Xk+1| has been explicitly established in "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting" by Suvrit Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4).  

For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a standard approach. While its sublinear global convergence is well-established, linear convergence is known to hold when the loss is strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption using the framework of Luo and Tseng. Specifically, the authors establish a local error bound to demonstrate asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularization. The proof largely follows conventional techniques for deriving local error bounds, with a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of the relevant matrices.  

**Quality:** The paper appears technically sound. However, Appendix, line 085, requires clarification: why does choosing κ3 (with α < 1) imply the inequality R(X^k) ≤ κ3 \|X^k − X^{k+1}\|_F?  

**Clarity:** The paper is generally readable, though its technical depth suggests benefit from a clearer roadmap. The authors could first state the main claims—particularly the local error bound—and then proceed to the proof. The technical derivation of the local error bound might be deferred to allow immediate focus on the linear convergence result, which follows straightforwardly from the bound and adds little novelty.  

**Originality:** The paper’s originality is moderate. The simultaneous diagonalization technique is novel and interesting, enabling reduction to the vector case. However, the core approach—applying Luo and Tseng’s framework to establish the local error bound—follows existing methodology, and the subsequent convergence analysis is standard.  

**Significance:** The theoretical contribution is modest: extending proximal gradient linear convergence to a relaxed strong convexity setting for trace norm regularization. While valuable for broadening the applicability of the convergence guarantee, the significance is tempered by the asymptotic nature of the result. The matrix diagonalization technique, though not central to the main convergence claim, may hold independent interest. Overall, the work is incremental in theoretical depth but provides a clean extension of known convergence theory for a practically relevant problem. The authors successfully demonstrate that proximal gradient methods achieve linear convergence for trace norm regularized risk minimization under the relaxed condition, largely by adapting the established Luo-Tseng framework with a key diagonalization insight.
