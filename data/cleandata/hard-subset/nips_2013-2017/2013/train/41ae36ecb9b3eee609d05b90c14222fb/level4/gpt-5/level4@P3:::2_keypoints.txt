- Paper proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for F(X)=f(X)+τ‖X‖* with f=h∘A under smooth, strictly convex h and linear A.  
- Key technical contribution: a Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to optima and residual R(X)=Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X.  
- Uses a structural result that any optimum X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors, enabling restricted invertibility via matrix perturbation theory.  
- Leverages Luo–Tseng framework to obtain Q-linear convergence of objective values and R-linear convergence of iterates once error bound holds.  
- Addresses an open question by deriving an error bound for the nonpolyhedral trace-norm regularizer.  
- Builds on prior composite minimization and ℓ1/group-LASSO error-bound literature, avoiding the usual strong-convexity requirement.  
- Technical arguments appear careful and correct; the error-bound proof introduces new ideas beyond the ℓ1 case.  
- Assumptions on h and step sizes are natural and cover common losses (square, logistic); line search for logistic is appropriate.  
- Experiments are small but relevant and confirm asymptotic linear convergence behavior.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized, with a long but structured main proof.  
- Originality: simultaneous SVD observation and trace-norm error bound are novel and insightful.  
- Significance: results are broadly relevant to ML/optimization and apply to matrix completion, multitask learning, logistic models.  
- Strength: resolves an open problem, provides clear assumptions, solid analysis, and empirical validation.  
- Weakness: insufficient explicit comparison to ℓ1/group-LASSO analyses and why those techniques do not transfer.  
- Weakness: should emphasize that the linear rate is local/asymptotic and discuss the size or detection of the neighborhood where it applies.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of constants in the error bound and how adaptive step-size/backtracking affect onset of linear convergence.  
- Experimental suggestions: include runtime or SVD counts and comparisons to accelerated or alternative solvers for practical context.  
- Minor issues: clarify terminology for masking operator P, define Q-/R-linear when first used, relate residual to Luo–Tseng residual, fix broken reference, and improve figure captions.  
- Acceptance pros: novel, technically solid error bound; broad applicability; clear exposition and likely influence on future work.  
- Acceptance cons: limited contrast with ℓ1/group-LASSO literature, asymptotic/local nature of rate needs more practical guidance, and experiments could be more comprehensive.  
- Post-feedback: authors addressed most questions; overall assessment remains positive and contributions stand.
