- Problem/location: Studies proximal gradient for trace-norm-regularized risk minimization, where sublinear global and linear-under-strong-convexity rates are known.  
- Main claim: Proves a local error bound and uses it to show the proximal gradient algorithm converges asymptotically at a linear rate without requiring strong convexity.  
- Technical approach: Builds on Luo and Tseng’s framework and largely follows standard techniques for proving local error bounds.  
- Novel observation: Identifies a new observation that enables simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the matrix problem to the vector case.  
- Quality: Paper appears technically sound overall.  
- Open question: Reviewer asks why a choice of kappa3 (e.g., alpha < 1) yields R(X^k) <= kappa3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F (appendix, line 85).  
- Clarity suggestion: Recommend stating main claims up front and postponing the highly technical proof of the local error bound until after showing the linear convergence result.  
- Originality: Moderate — simultaneous diagonalization is new and interesting, but most of the proof strategy follows existing approaches.  
- Significance: Primarily theoretical with limited direct impact on algorithm design; the asymptotic nature of the result reduces practical impact.  
- Post-rebuttal note: Reviewer cites Sra NIPS'12 (Lemma 4) as explicitly establishing the |R_k| <= kappa ||X_k - X_{k+1}|| result.
