- Proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss.  
- Paper is carefully and clearly written and correctly applies Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique to the nuclear norm case (previously an open question).  
- Needs a clearer comparison and analogy with the vector l1-norm case (e.g., [20,22]), including why l1 techniques don’t directly translate and that the l1 result should follow as a corollary.  
- Suggest reconsidering the title: “structured” is unnecessary/misleading since the main novelty is weakening loss-function assumptions via the linear operator A, not the regularizer.  
- Notation: describe P as a mask/selection operator rather than “sampling” to avoid implying randomness.  
- Broken reference on page 337 needs fixing.  
- Clarify meanings of “Q-linear” and “R-linear” convergence for readers unfamiliar with the terms.  
- Explain the residual R(X) in equation (6) relative to the original definition (46) in [20] to aid interpretation.  
- Add discussion or concluding remarks after the technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 to improve readability.  
- Update: reviewer acknowledges authors addressed most questions, reiterates the title concern but leaves the final choice to the authors.
