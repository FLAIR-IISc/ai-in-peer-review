This paper proves the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems. The result holds under assumptions that are weaker than the strong convexity of the loss function, addressing a crucial open problem in many machine learning and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It builds upon the Lipschitzian error bound technique from Tseng [20], whose application to the nuclear norm case had remained an open question. Based on my reading, the main proof appears to be correct.

My primary suggestions for improvement are as follows:

*   **Comparison with the l1-norm case:** The paper would be significantly strengthened by a clear comparison and analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization (e.g., as in [20, 22] and related works). It would be very helpful if the authors could relate their work to the results and techniques used in the l1-norm setting and explain if, or why, those techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm. It is also worth noting that the l1-norm case should now follow as a corollary of the presented result. This comparison is important for contextualizing the paper's contribution; while some might find the extension from l1 to trace norm unsurprising, I believe it is a very valuable contribution that merits this detailed discussion.

*   **Title Suggestion:** I suggest a slight change to the title. The main contribution seems to stem from the generalization of the loss function (via the linear operator A) rather than making the trace norm regularization itself "structured." Since the new flexibility is on the loss side and not the regularizer, a different title might better reflect the core of the work.

**Minor Issues:**

*   **Notation:** The term "sampling operator" for P could be misleading, as "sampling" often implies randomness, whereas P is fixed in this context. "Mask operator" might be a more precise term.
*   **Reference:** There appears to be a broken reference on line 337.
*   **Clarity on Convergence:** The meanings of Q- and R-linear convergence may not be widely known. For the benefit of a broader audience, it would be helpful to briefly define these terms in the text.
*   **Residual Definition:** To improve interpretability, the definition of the residual R(X) in (6) could be further contextualized by explaining its relationship to the original definition (46) from [20].
*   **Balance of Content:** The paper could be improved by adding more high-level discussion and conclusions, possibly by condensing some of the long technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 (e.g., by moving details to an appendix).

**Update after Author Feedback:**

The authors have addressed most of my questions in their response. Regarding the title, my suggestion was specifically about the word "structured," which I found potentially unnecessary. However, I respect the authors' choice on this matter.
