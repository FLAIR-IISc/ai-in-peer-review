

This paper establishes asymptotic linear convergence for the proximal gradient algorithm in trace norm regularized risk minimization under a relaxed strong convexity condition. The authors leverage the Luo-Tseng framework, proving a local error bound that enables the linear rate result. A key novelty is their observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the matrix problem to a vector case. This observation is technically interesting and central to their proof.

The proof for the error bound $|R_k| \leq \kappa |X_k - X_{k+1}|$ is explicitly established in Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4), resolving the concern raised in the initial review.

**Quality:** The technical core is sound. However, the justification for choosing $\kappa_3$ (e.g., $\alpha < 1$) in Appendix, line 085, to satisfy $R(X^k) \leq \kappa_3 \|X^k - X^{k+1}\|_F$ requires clarification. The connection between the choice of $\alpha$ and the inequality’s validity is not sufficiently explained.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally accessible but could improve by structuring the exposition more effectively. The main contributions—particularly the local error bound and its implication for linear convergence—should be stated upfront before delving into technical details. The proof of the local error bound, while necessary, is highly technical; postponing it after presenting the convergence result would enhance readability, as the convergence proof itself relies on the error bound but does not introduce novel techniques.

**Originality:** The work has moderate originality. The simultaneous diagonalization technique is a novel and valuable insight that streamlines the analysis. However, the overall approach—applying the Luo-Tseng framework to derive a local error bound—follows a well-established pattern. The subsequent steps (e.g., linking the error bound to linear convergence) are standard and not new.

**Significance:** The theoretical significance lies in extending the linear convergence guarantee of proximal gradient methods to a broader class of problems (relaxed strong convexity), which is meaningful for trace norm regularization. However, the asymptotic nature of the convergence result (i.e., linear convergence holds only in the limit) limits practical impact. The diagonalization technique itself may have independent value for related matrix optimization problems. While the work is a theoretically sound incremental advance, its direct implications for algorithm design are limited, and the asymptotic claim slightly diminishes its practical relevance compared to non-asymptotic results. The paper successfully demonstrates that proximal gradient achieves asymptotic linear convergence under the relaxed condition, primarily by adapting the Luo-Tseng framework with a fresh diagonalization perspective. Overall, this is a solid theoretical contribution that advances the understanding of trace norm optimization, though it does not introduce a new algorithm or fundamentally alter the optimization landscape. The incremental nature of the work is clear, but the technical insight on simultaneous diagonalization provides a useful methodological contribution.
