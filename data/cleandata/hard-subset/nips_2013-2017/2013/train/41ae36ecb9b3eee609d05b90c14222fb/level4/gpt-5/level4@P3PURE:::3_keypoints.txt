- Prior work note: The inequality |R_k| ≤ κ |X_k − X_{k+1}| is already established in Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4).
- Problem studied: Trace-norm-regularized risk minimization solved with the proximal gradient algorithm.
- Context: Global sublinear convergence of proximal gradient is known; linear convergence requires strong convexity.
- Main contribution: Relaxes strong convexity via a local error bound (Luo–Tseng framework) to obtain asymptotic linear convergence for trace-norm regularization.
- Technical novelty: Introduces an observation that enables simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, simplifying reduction to the vector case.
- Proof approach: Largely follows standard steps for establishing a local error bound; new diagonalization observation is the main fresh element.
- Quality concern: Paper appears technically sound but has a gap/query in the appendix (line 085) about why the chosen κ3 implies R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F.
- Clarity suggestion: Paper is readable but dense; recommend presenting main claims first and postponing the technical proof, then deriving the linear rate.
- Originality assessment: Moderate—simultaneous diagonalization is interesting and new, but most of the development follows existing methods.
- Significance assessment: Primarily theoretical with limited algorithmic impact; asymptotic nature reduces practical significance, though the diagonalization trick may be independently useful.
- Overall summary: Demonstrates asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization under a relaxed strong-convexity condition, but is incremental and of moderate theoretical interest.
