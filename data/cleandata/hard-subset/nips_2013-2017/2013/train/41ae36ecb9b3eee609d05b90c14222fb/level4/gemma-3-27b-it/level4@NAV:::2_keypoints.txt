- Shows linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity; core proof appears sound and is significant for ML/signal processing.
- Builds on Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique; extending this to the nuclear norm case is a natural and important contribution.
- Lacks explicit comparison to the vector l1-norm case (refs [20,22]); paper should state how/why l1 techniques don’t directly generalize and note that l1 results may follow as a corollary.
- Title should be reconsidered: the main advance is relaxed conditions on the loss (via operator A) rather than a fundamental change to trace-norm regularization.
- Clarify the operator P: specify whether it is a sampling or mask operator, and note that “sampling” could misleadingly imply randomness when P is fixed.
