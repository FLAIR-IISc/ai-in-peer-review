- Main contribution: Technical extension of linear convergence of proximal gradient (PG) to trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) under strong convexity and Lipschitz gradient assumptions on h.  
- Strength: Proofs are logically structured and successfully generalize prior error-bound analyses to the trace-norm setting.  
- Novelty concern: Contribution is primarily technical with limited new conceptual insight beyond Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22].  
- Clarify relation to prior work: Explicitly contrast each proof step with Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], identifying which arguments are new and which mirror prior proofs (esp. Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2).  
- Empirical validation missing: Must include a numerical semi-log plot of function value vs iteration to demonstrate the claimed linear convergence in practice.  
- Non-strongly-convex gap: Add simulations where h is not strongly convex to (a) test whether PG still shows linear convergence and (b) demonstrate specific cases/parameters where the theory fails.  
- Constants dependence unclear: Explain how κ1…κ4 depend on α̲ and ᾱ and how this affects practical convergence rates.  
- Minor technical correction: Fix the misleading inequalities before (13); the final step relies on convexity of the trace norm and the subgradient condition (−Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖*) as in Tseng, not the intermediate inequalities.  
- ADMM discussion needed: Provide a substantive discussion of feasibility and challenges for extending the error-bound analysis to ADMM, including required assumptions for alternating updates.  
- Recommendation: Major revision required—address empirical validation, non-strongly-convex failure analysis, clearer differentiation from prior work, constants dependence, the technical correction, and an ADMM discussion.
