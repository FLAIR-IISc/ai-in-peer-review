

The proof for $|R_k| \leq \kappa |X_k - X_{k+1}|$ has been explicitly established in Lemma 4 of Sra's "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting" (NIPS 2012).  

For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a standard approach. While its sublinear global convergence rate is well understood, linear convergence is known to hold when the loss is strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption by adopting the framework of Luo and Tseng. Specifically, the authors establish a local error bound to demonstrate asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularization. The proof of the local error bound follows the conventional pattern but incorporates a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.  

**Quality:**  
The paper is technically sound. A minor gap remains in Appendix Line 085: please clarify why the choice of $\kappa_3$ (with $\alpha < 1$) implies the claimed inequality $R(X^k) \leq \kappa_3 \|X^k - X^{k+1}\|_F$.  

**Clarity:**  
The paper is generally clear and readable. To enhance accessibility, the authors could state the main contributions upfront before detailing proofs. Specifically, after presenting the local error bound, they might defer the technical proof of the bound and immediately proceed to the linear convergence result—which is straightforward within the established framework.  

**Originality:**  
The originality is moderate. The simultaneous diagonalization observation is novel and valuable, enabling reduction to the vector case. However, the overall approach largely adheres to the Luo-Tseng framework, with subsequent steps being largely derivative.  

**Significance:**  
The theoretical significance is moderate. While extending linear convergence to relaxed strong convexity is meaningful for trace norm regularization, the asymptotic nature of the guarantee limits practical impact. The diagonalization technique itself may hold independent interest. Overall, the work is incremental but provides a clear theoretical extension: the proximal gradient method converges asymptotically linearly for trace norm regularized risk minimization under relaxed strong convexity, via a novel diagonalization argument within the Luo-Tseng framework.
