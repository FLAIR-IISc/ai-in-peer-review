The following is a simulation where the loss function is not strictly convex, but the PG algorithm converges linearly (for various levels of stability), and importantly, discusses when the theory fails. Also discusses how it can be extended for ADMM analysis.

This paper demonstrates the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method in trace-norm regularized learning when the loss function is of the form f (X) = h (A (X)), where A is linear and h has a strictly convex and lipase gradient on any compact set.

This paper is an extension of Seng [20], Seng and ইউন "A gradient method for smooth differentiable minimization" and Zhang et al. [22], which established the same result using the "error-bound condition" for Lasso and group Lasso for trace norms. It is a non-trivial extension but the contribution seems entirely technical.

The presentation of the proof is clear in most cases.

Strengths:
* Seng et al.'s extended proximal gradient algorithm shows linear convergence.

Weaknesses:
* The contribution is entirely technical.
* I would like to see a numerical example showing linear convergence.

Further details:
1. The outline of the proof for theorem 3.1 and lemma 3.2 seems similar to Seng's. The authors should state the main work more clearly to make their contribution clearer.
2. I would like to see a numerical example that actually shows linear convergence (a semi-log plot of function values versus the number of iterations).
3. It is not clear whether the constants \kappa1, ..., \kappa4 depend on the choice of \underbar{\alpha} and \bar{\alpha}.

Small problem:
4. The order of inequalities before the inequality is confusing. The last inequality is due to the convexity of the trace norm and follows that -\bar{G}\in \tau\partial\|X\|_{\ast} and not due to the intermediate inequality (see page 289 of [20]).
