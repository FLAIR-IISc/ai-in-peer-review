- Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems when f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear and h strongly convex on compacts and Lipschitz-gradient.
- Extends prior error-bound based results (Tseng et al., Zhang et al.) from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm.
- Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 4.1 are proved in a detailed, well-structured manner.
- Notable technical ingredients include matrix perturbation theory and decomposition of the matrix Q̄.
- Main strengths: establishes linear convergence for a new class and provides rigorous, detailed proofs.
- Main weakness: contribution is largely technical and the paper underemphasizes its significance and potential impact.
- Paper lacks a numerical example demonstrating the claimed linear convergence.
- Suggestion: add a numerical experiment with a semilog plot of function value vs. iterations to illustrate linear convergence.
- Suggestion: clarify how constants κ1,…,κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ (typo in review: ᾱ repeated).
- Minor issue: clarify the sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) and provide a clearer explanation.
- Originality: incremental extension of existing results; authors should better highlight novel aspects of their approach.
- Overall assessment: well-written and clear but would benefit from added context, examples, and explanations; rating 7/10, accept with minor revisions.
