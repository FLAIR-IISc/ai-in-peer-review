- Studies PGM for minimizing smooth convex loss plus nuclear norm, proving asymptotic linear convergence under f(X)=h(A(X)) with mild curvature on h.
- Proves a local Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to optimal set to the proximal residual; uses Luo–Tseng to get R-linear iterates and Q-linear objectives.
- Main technical novelty: an error bound for trace-norm regularization that avoids strong convexity and exploits that optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors.
- Uses spectral decomposition and a restricted invertibility argument to derive the nuclear-norm-specific error bound.
- Fills a gap relative to prior work by handling the non-polyhedral trace norm where separability-based techniques (ℓ1/group LASSO) fail.
- Clarifies connection to earlier proximal/accelerated methods and explains the observed linear regime in practice.
- Prior gap regarding ‖R(Xk)‖F ≤ κ3‖Xk − Xk+1‖F was addressed in rebuttal but should be included explicitly or cited (Sra NIPS’12, Lemma 4).
- Technically sound contributions: new error bound, spectral structural result, and correct application of Luo–Tseng to obtain linear rates.
- Suggests reordering exposition to present assumptions, main theorems, and sketches first, with full proofs later for readability.
- Requests clearer highlighting of the simultaneous singular-vector proposition and contrasts with ℓ1/group separability.
- Minor presentation fixes: state dependence of constants and neighborhood, clarify stepsize/backtracking requirements, and standardize notation/citations.
- Originality is moderate but meaningful—the core novelty is the nuclear-norm error bound; the rest follows established frameworks.
- Significance is theoretical: explains empirical linear regimes, broadens provable cases, but results are local/asymptotic and do not propose new algorithms.
- Pros for acceptance: first linear convergence result for PGM with nuclear norm sans strong convexity, solid technical insight, likely to be influential.
- Cons for acceptance: asymptotic/local nature with unquantified constants/radii, novelty focused on one lemma, and limited empirical validation.
- Recommendations: add the residual–step inequality proof/citation, emphasize the singular-vector insight, provide guidance on neighborhood/stepsize estimation, and expand discussion on polyhedral vs non-polyhedral regularizers.
