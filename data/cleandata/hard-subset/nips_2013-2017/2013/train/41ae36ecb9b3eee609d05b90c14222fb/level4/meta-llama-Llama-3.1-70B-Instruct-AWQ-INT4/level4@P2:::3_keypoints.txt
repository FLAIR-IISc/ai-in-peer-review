- Studies convergence rate of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems in ML.  
- Proves linear (asymptotic) convergence of PGM without requiring strong convexity of the objective.  
- Introduces convex smooth loss plus trace norm regularization and reviews prior PGM convergence results.  
- Notes standard PGM is sublinear but becomes linear under strong convexity; this work removes that assumption.  
- Main technical contribution: establishing a local (Lipschitzian) error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem.  
- Uses the Luo and Tseng framework to derive the error bound and prove linear convergence.  
- Presents a new, interesting observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.  
- Proofs are technically sound but quite technical and could be reorganized for accessibility (state main claims first).  
- Paper’s originality is moderate—builds on existing frameworks but includes a novel technical observation.  
- Significance is primarily theoretical with limited impact on algorithm design; asymptotic nature reduces practical importance.  
- Quality-wise sound; clarity is good overall but appendix needs clarification on the choice of kappa3.  
- Arguments for acceptance: new convergence result, detailed proof of error bound, technically sound and well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: moderate originality, mainly theoretical significance, and asymptotic claim lowers impact.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions (clarify kappa3 and reorganize for readability).
