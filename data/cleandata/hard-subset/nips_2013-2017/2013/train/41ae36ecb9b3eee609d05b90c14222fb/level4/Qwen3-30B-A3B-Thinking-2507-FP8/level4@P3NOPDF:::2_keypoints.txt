- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker conditions than strong convexity, resolving Tseng’s open question via Lipschitzian error bounds.  
- Technique: builds on Tseng’s error bound approach and adapts it to nuclear norm regularization.  
- Proof quality: core proof is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly presented.  
- Missing comparison: paper lacks a dedicated comparison with the l1-norm (vector) case and relevant works (e.g., [20,22]).  
- Clarify l1 relation: authors should explain why l1 techniques don’t directly extend to trace norm and how the results generalize the l1 theory (e.g., diagonal regularizer special case).  
- Title issue: phrase “structured trace norm regularization” is misleading; title should emphasize weak convexity in the loss (suggested alternative given).  
- Notation: calling P a “sampling operator” is misleading—use “mask” or “projection” since P is fixed.  
- Broken reference: line 337 cites a broken reference that must be fixed.  
- Terminology: briefly reiterate definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence for unfamiliar readers.  
- Residual linkage: explicitly link residual R(X) to Tseng’s residual R_Tseng to clarify its role in the error bound framework.  
- Lemma 3.2 exposition: shorten detailed proof and add a concise summary of its intuition and implications.  
- Practical relevance: add a brief concluding remark on why Assumption 3 (error bound) holds in key applications (e.g., matrix completion, low-rank recovery).  
- Author feedback note: authors clarified “structured” refers to problem structure but phrasing remains confusing and should be revised.  
- Recommendation: technically strong and resolves a literature gap; accept with minor revisions.
