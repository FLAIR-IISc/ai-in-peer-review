This paper proves the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regular problems, under weaker assumptions than the strong convexity of the loss function, which is a significant problem for many ML and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows Seng's [20] Lyapunov error bound technique, the application of which remains an open question in the nuclear norm domain. As far as I understand, the core part of the proof seems correct.

* What I miss in this paper is a clear comparison/similarity with vector fields for l1-norm regularization, such as [20, 22] and related work.

It would be very helpful to relate and explain the results and techniques used in that case, and why l1-case techniques directly translate to trace norms. (Also, the l1-norm case should now be followed as a corollary from the trace norm).

To judge the value of the paper, it should be considered, because some people may not be very surprised that the l1-result also applies to trace norms (I still consider it a very valuable contribution).

Also, I would suggest a slight change in the title of the paper, because the main new contribution is that the loss function is more affected by trace norm regularization than by its formation. (Since the newly acquired flexibility by the linear operator A is in the loss direction, not in the regularization).

* Minor problems
- Symbol: Is P being called a sample or mask operator? (The sample may indicate randomness, but here P is constant)

In general, I would vote for including some more discussion and possibly a conclusion in favor of the long technical part of the proof of Lemma 3.2.
