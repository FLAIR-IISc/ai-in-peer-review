- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity—a significant and valuable contribution.
- Well-written and clearly presented; core proof argument appears sound.
- Extends Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique to the nuclear (trace) norm case, resolving a previously open question.
- Needs stronger comparison and analogy to the l1-norm (vector) case [20,22]; explain why l1 techniques do not directly generalize and note that l1 results should follow as a corollary.
- Clarify novelty by providing context on the expected extension from l1 to trace norm for some readers.
- Title should be revised: “structured” is unnecessary; the main novelty concerns assumptions on the loss (and the linear operator A), not new properties of the trace norm regularizer.
- Notation: clarify whether operator P denotes sampling or masking (current wording suggests a fixed operator, not random sampling).
- Fix broken reference at Line 337.
- Briefly reiterate definitions of “Q-linear” and “R-linear” convergence for reader clarity.
- Clarify residual R(X) in eq. (6) by explicitly relating it to definition (46) in [20].
