- Proves asymptotic linear convergence of PGM for trace-norm regularized problems when f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex.  
- Result applies to practical cases like matrix completion with square loss lacking strong convexity.  
- Main technical contribution is a new Lipschitzian local error bound for the trace-norm problem (Theorem 3.1).  
- Proof leverages a novel observation that the optimal solution and the negative gradient share singular vectors (Proposition 4.2) and matrix perturbation theory.  
- Uses the Luo–Tseng framework to convert the error bound into linear convergence for PGM (Theorem 3.2).  
- Technical arguments appear sound, but the inequality ‖R(Xk)‖F ≤ κ3‖Xk − Xk+1‖F in Theorem 3.2 requires clearer derivation or a direct citation.  
- Paper organization could be improved: present the linear-convergence proof immediately after the error-bound statement and defer the detailed error-bound proof.  
- Originality is moderate: methodology follows known frameworks, but adapting them to the non-polyhedral trace norm is non-trivial and novel.  
- Limitation: the linear rate is local/asymptotic (requires iterates sufficiently close to the optimum), so no change to global worst-case guarantees or the algorithm.  
- Overall a solid, incremental theoretical contribution that resolves an open question and provides techniques of independent interest.
