

The paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function—a significant advancement for machine learning and signal processing applications. The manuscript is well-structured and clearly written, effectively leveraging Tseng's Lipschitzian error bound technique [20], which previously left the nuclear norm case as an open problem. The core proof appears sound and logically developed.  

A key omission is a comparative analysis with the well-studied vector case (l₁-norm regularization), as presented in [20, 22]. The authors should explicitly contrast their approach with these results, clarifying why techniques from the l₁ setting do not directly extend to trace norm regularization. This comparison is crucial: without it, some readers might perceive the trace norm result as merely a straightforward extension of l₁ theory (which, as the reviewer notes, should indeed follow as a corollary). The paper’s value hinges on demonstrating *why* the l₁ framework fails here, making this omission a substantive concern.  

Regarding the title, the term "structured" is misleading. The primary novelty resides in relaxing assumptions on the *loss function* (via the linear operator A), not in the regularizer itself. A revised title such as "Linear Convergence of Proximal Gradient for Trace Norm Regularization under Weaker Loss Assumptions" would better reflect the contribution.  

Minor issues require attention:  
- **Notation**: Avoid labeling the operator *P* as "sampling" or "mask," as this implies randomness (whereas *P* is fixed).  
- **Line 337**: Correct the broken reference.  
- **Convergence terminology**: Define "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence (e.g., "Q-linear convergence implies the error decays exponentially with a fixed rate, while R-linear convergence allows for a variable rate").  
- **Residual interpretation**: Clarify how the residual *R*(X) in (6) relates to Tseng’s definition in [20, Eq. (46)] for better intuition.  
- **Lemma 3.2**: The proof’s technical depth warrants a brief concluding remark in Section 3, emphasizing *why* the error bound holds (e.g., "This bound arises from the equivalence between the trace norm and the l₁ norm of singular values, combined with the loss’s weak strong convexity").  

The paper’s core contribution remains valuable, but the missing l₁ comparison weakens its impact. Addressing this would significantly strengthen the narrative. The authors’ response to the title suggestion (removing "structured") is reasonable, though the revised phrasing remains essential for precision.  

*Recommendation*: Accept with minor revisions. The work is technically sound and addresses an important open problem, but the l₁ comparison gap must be resolved to fully justify its novelty. The minor notational and explanatory points should be addressed to enhance accessibility.
