This paper proves the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method applied to trace-norm regularized learning problems when the loss function has the form of f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is linear and h is strongly convex on any compact set and has Lipschitz gradient. The authors extend the result of Tseng et al. and Zhang et al., which established the same result using the "error-bound condition" for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm.

The presentation of the proofs is mostly clear. The authors provide a detailed and well-structured proof of the main result, Theorem 3.1, and its key component, Lemma 4.1. The use of matrix perturbation theory and the decomposition of the matrix Q̄ are particularly noteworthy.

The paper's strengths include:

* Shows the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm extending the result of Tseng et al.
* Provides a detailed and well-structured proof of the main result.

However, the paper's contribution seems purely technical, and the authors could have done more to highlight its significance and potential impact. Additionally, the paper lacks a numerical example that demonstrates the linear convergence of the algorithm.

To improve the paper, the authors could:

* Provide a numerical example that shows the linear convergence of the algorithm, including a semilog plot of function value vs. the number of iterations.
* Clarify how the constants κ1, ..., κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ and ᾱ.
* Address the minor issue regarding the sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) and provide a clearer explanation.

In terms of originality, the paper's contribution is an extension of existing results, and the authors could have done more to highlight what is new and innovative about their approach.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear and detailed proof of the main result. However, it could benefit from additional context, examples, and explanations to make it more accessible and impactful.

Rating: 7/10
