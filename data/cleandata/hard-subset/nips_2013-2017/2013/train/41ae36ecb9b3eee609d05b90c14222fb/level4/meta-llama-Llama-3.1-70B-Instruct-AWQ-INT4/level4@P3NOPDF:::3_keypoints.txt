- Authors provided a proof for |Rk| <= κ |Xk - X_{k+1}| in the rebuttal, citing Sra (NIPS'12, Lemma 4).
- Paper studies convergence rate of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization.
- Algorithm is known to be globally sublinear and linear under strong convexity.
- Authors relax strong convexity using Luo–Tseng framework and prove a local error bound.
- From the local error bound they show asymptotic linear convergence for the proximal gradient algorithm.
- Paper follows standard techniques but introduces a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.
- Approach is technically sound and the paper is well-organized.
- Gap noted in appendix (line 085): need justification that chosen κ3 (with α < 1) implies R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.
- Clarity is generally good but technical; recommend presenting main claims first and postponing detailed proofs to improve readability.
- Originality is moderate: simultaneous diagonalization is new, but other steps largely follow existing work.
- Significance is mainly theoretical with limited algorithmic impact due to the asymptotic nature of the result.
- Overall scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.
