- Summary: Proves global linear convergence of proximal gradient for nuclear-norm regularization under EB-type conditions weaker than strong convexity, extending Tseng’s framework to the matrix case.  
- Strength: Paper is carefully written, technically solid, and the main proof appears correct.  
- Strength: Establishing linear convergence under EB (not strong convexity) significantly broadens applicability.  
- Strength: Allowing a general linear operator A captures many models (matrix completion, sensing, regression).  
- Strength: Closes a literature gap by adapting EB techniques to trace-norm regularization.  
- Suggestion 1 (l1 comparison): Add explicit comparisons to l1 literature, stating which l1 results are recovered and which parts do not carry over to the matrix case.  
- Suggestion 1 (l1 differences): Explain specific matrix complications (subspace geometry, singular value thresholding, rank stability, subdifferential differences).  
- Suggestion 1 (assumptions): Clarify any extra assumptions needed in the matrix case versus the l1 case.  
- Suggestion 2 (title/structure): Reconsider the word “structured” in the title; emphasize EB-based linear convergence under general linear measurements instead.  
- Suggestion 3 (convergence notions): Restate definitions of Q-linear and R-linear on first use and use them consistently.  
- Suggestion 3 (residual): Explain residual R(X) and relate it explicitly to Tseng’s residual for clarity.  
- Suggestion 4 (proof organization): Add a roadmap and high-level intuition at the start of Section 3 and a brief summary/conclusion of the section’s takeaways and limitations.  
- Suggestion 5 (novelty): Explicitly state why extension from l1 to trace norm is nontrivial and highlight new technical ingredients (low-rank geometry, singular subspace perturbation, active set/rank stability).  
- Minor: Use “mask” instead of “sampling” for a fixed masking operator, fix broken reference at line 337, and ensure all EB assumptions are stated near the main theorem.  
- Minor: Provide simple sufficient conditions on A/loss that guarantee EB (e.g., RSC or quadratic growth) and comment on rank stability under the proximal map.  
- Presentation: Overall clear and well organized; adding the requested comparisons, definitions, and residual discussion will improve accessibility.  
- Post-rebuttal: Reviewer remains concerned about “structured” in the title but defers to authors; overall evaluation stays positive.  
- Overall assessment: Solid theoretical contribution resolving an open question; recommend acceptance after suggested clarifications and minor fixes.
