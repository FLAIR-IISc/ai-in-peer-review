- Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex on compacts and Lipschitz gradient, extending prior results.  
- Extension is non-trivial but primarily technical and incremental, adapting sparse-regularization techniques to the trace norm.  
- Presentation is generally clear but requires more precise referencing and explicit comparison with prior works (Tseng [20], Zhang et al. [22]).  
- Strength: provides theoretical justification for algorithms used in low-rank matrix recovery by extending linear convergence theory to trace-norm regularization.  
- Weakness: lacks a compelling, detailed numerical demonstration to validate the claimed linear convergence.  
- Recommend adding a semilog plot of objective vs iterations, testing different condition numbers and discussing cases where theory fails to predict observed behavior.  
- Weakness: the dependence of convergence constants (κ1–κ4) on step-size parameters ᾱ and α is not clearly explained, limiting practical use.  
- Request clarification of how κ1–κ4 depend on ᾱ and α.  
- Technical correction: the sequence of inequalities before (13) needs clearer justification—the final inequality follows from convexity of the trace norm and -∇f(X*) ∈ τ∂‖X*‖∗ (Tseng [20]).  
- Overall: technically sound but incremental; impact reduced by insufficient numerical validation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on a more comprehensive numerical evaluation and clarification of the constants’ dependence on step sizes.
