- Proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity—important for ML and signal processing.  
- Paper is carefully and clearly written; main proof follows Luo & Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique and appears correct; applying that technique to the nuclear norm was an open question.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case (e.g., [22]); should explain why l1 techniques don’t directly translate and ideally show l1 as a corollary.  
- Suggest revising the title: the main new contribution concerns the loss side (linear operator A) rather than making the trace-norm regularizer itself “structured.”  
- Minor issues: clarify whether “P” is a sampling/mask operator (fixed vs. random); fix broken reference at line 337.  
- Minor clarity improvements: repeat definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence and better explain residual R(X) in (7) relative to [10].  
- Recommend adding more discussion and conclusions to complement the extensive technical proof in Section 4.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant optimization problem, presents a novel extension (linear convergence without strong convexity), proof is sound and clear, and the error bound is valuable.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient connection to l1-norm literature may undercut novelty/surprise; title may misrepresent the core contribution.
