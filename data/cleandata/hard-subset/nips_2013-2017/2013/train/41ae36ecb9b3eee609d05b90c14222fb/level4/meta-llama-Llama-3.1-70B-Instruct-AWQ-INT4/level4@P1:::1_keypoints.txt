- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems when f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear and h strongly convex on compacts and having Lipschitz gradient.  
- Extends prior error-bound results (Tseng; Tseng & Yun; Zhang et al.) to the trace-norm by establishing a new Lipschitzian error bound.  
- Presentation and proofs are mostly clear and the analysis is thorough.  
- Main contribution is technical: a new Lipschitzian error bound used to prove linear convergence.  
- Strength: extends Tseng et al.’s linear-convergence result to trace-norm regularization.  
- Weakness: contribution appears purely technical with limited discussion of practical significance for machine learning.  
- Weakness: no numerical experiments to illustrate practical relevance or show linear convergence when loss is not strongly convex.  
- Suggestion: include simulations (e.g., semilog plots of function value vs iterations) demonstrating linear convergence under various stability levels.  
- Suggestion: discuss possible extensions of the analysis to ADMM to broaden applicability.  
- Minor issue: sequence of inequalities before (13) is confusing; the final inequality follows from trace-norm convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖∗, not the intermediate step.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Additional comments: cite Tseng’s original work more precisely and clarify how constants κ1…κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ.
