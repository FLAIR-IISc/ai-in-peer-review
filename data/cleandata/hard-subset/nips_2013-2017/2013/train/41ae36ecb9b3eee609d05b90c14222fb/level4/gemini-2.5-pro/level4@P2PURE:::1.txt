*Added after authors' feedback*
The authors are requested to include a simulation that demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm for a loss function that is not strongly convex (ideally for varying levels of stability). Crucially, the paper should also include a discussion on the conditions under which the proposed theory fails. Furthermore, a discussion on the potential extension of this analysis to the ADMM algorithm would be a valuable addition.
***

This paper establishes the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized learning problems. The analysis applies to loss functions of the form f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is a linear operator, h has a Lipschitz continuous gradient, and h is strongly convex on any compact set. The work extends previous results by Tseng [20], Tseng and Yun, and Zhang et al. [22], which used the "error-bound condition" to prove linear convergence for lasso and group lasso problems.

While this extension to the trace norm setting is non-trivial, the contribution appears to be primarily theoretical in nature. The presentation of the proofs is mostly clear, but the paper could be improved by addressing the following points.

**Detailed Comments:**

1.  The proof structures for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 appear to be very similar to those in Tseng [20]. To better delineate the novel contributions of this paper, the authors should provide more precise citations and comparisons to this prior work.

2.  The paper would be significantly strengthened by the inclusion of a numerical example that validates the theoretical findings. A semilog plot showing the objective function value versus the number of iterations would be an effective way to demonstrate the claimed linear convergence rate.

3.  The dependency of the constants κ₁, ..., κ₄ on the parameters \underline{α} and ᾱ is not clearly explained.

**Minor Issue:**

4.  The derivation leading to inequality (13) is confusing. Specifically, the final inequality in the sequence seems to follow from the convexity of the trace norm and the subgradient property (-Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖*), rather than from the intermediate inequality presented. This logic appears to be the same as in Tseng [20] (p. 289), and this step should be clarified.
