- Establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems when f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex on compacts and Lipschitz-gradient, even without strong convexity.
- Extends error-bound based convergence analysis (Luo/Tseng line) from polyhedral regularizers to the non-polyhedral trace norm.
- Main technical innovation: derives a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm, using nontrivial matrix perturbation / spectral analysis.
- Strength: Provides a rigorous proof of linear convergence relevant to applications like matrix completion where loss lacks strong convexity.
- Strength: The new error bound is of independent interest and may aid analysis of other algorithms.
- Weakness: Presentation of novelty could be clearer—need an explicit contrast of new techniques versus prior lasso-type analyses and emphasis on the spectral/perturbation tools.
- Weakness: Experiments confirm theory but should probe boundaries (e.g., how convergence rate changes with problem parameters or weakened curvature assumptions on h).
- Weakness: Theorem 3.2 introduces constants κ1, κ2, κ3 without discussing their dependence on step-size bounds and the gradient Lipschitz constant; clarify these dependencies.
- Minor issue: In Theorem 3.1, the step leading to eq. (18) should explicitly state the convexity/subgradient argument (`-Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X̄k‖∗`) to avoid confusion.
