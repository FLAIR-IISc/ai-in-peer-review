- Post-rebuttal note: the bound |Rk| ≤ κ |Xk − Xk+1| was already proven in Suvrit Sra, "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting", NIPS 2012 (Lemma 4).
- Paper studies proximal gradient convergence for trace-norm regularized risk minimization.
- Removes strong convexity assumption and derives local error bounds following Luo & Tseng’s framework.
- Shows proximal gradient converges asymptotically at a linear rate (local/near-optimal).
- Key technical novelty: an observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the problem to the vector case.
- Technical development appears sound.
- Presentation is generally clear but would benefit from reordering: state main claims first, then give detailed derivations.
- Suggestion: prove linear convergence immediately after introducing the local error bound to improve readability.
- Originality is moderate: main novelty is the diagonalization trick; other parts follow established Luo & Tseng methods.
- Significance is primarily theoretical with limited direct impact on algorithm design due to the asymptotic/local nature of the guarantee.
- The diagonalization technique may have independent interest; overall the work is a solid, incremental contribution of moderate community interest.
