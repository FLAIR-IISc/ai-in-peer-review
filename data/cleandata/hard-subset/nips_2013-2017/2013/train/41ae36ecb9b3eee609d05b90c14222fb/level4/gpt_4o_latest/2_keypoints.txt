- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm-regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss.
- Builds on Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound framework [20], extending it to the nuclear norm (previously open); core proof appears correct.
- Missing comparison/analogy to the l1-norm (vector) case [20,22]; needs to explain whether l1 techniques extend and if l1 results are a corollary.
- Clarify novelty: some readers may find extension from l1 to trace norm unsurprising, so the paper should better articulate its distinct contribution.
- Title concern: “structured” may be misleading—the main novelty relates to the loss (and linear operator A) rather than the regularizer, so consider adjusting wording.
- Notation: calling P a “sampling” or “mask” operator is misleading since P is fixed (not random).
- Fix broken reference at Line 337.
- Clarify terminology: briefly restate definitions of Q- and R-linear convergence for readers unfamiliar with them.
- Explain the residual R(X) in (6) and relate it to the original definition (46) in [20] to aid interpretation.
- Manuscript balance: add more discussion and concluding remarks to offset the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
- Post-feedback update: authors addressed most questions; reviewer still questions the use of “structured” in the title but defers to the authors while reaffirming the main result.
