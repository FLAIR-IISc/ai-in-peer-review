This paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized learning when the loss is of the form $f(X) = h(A(X))$, with $A$ linear, $h$ strongly convex on compact sets, and $h$ having a Lipschitz gradient.  
This work extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], which proved similar results for lasso and group lasso using the "error-bound condition," to the trace norm. While the extension is non-trivial, the contribution appears primarily technical.  
The proofs are generally well-presented.  
**Strengths:**  
- Demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending Tseng et al. to the trace norm.  
**Weaknesses:**  
- The contribution is largely technical in nature.  
- A numerical example confirming linear convergence (e.g., a semilog plot of objective value vs. iterations) is required.  
**Detailed comments:**  
1. The proof structures of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirror Tseng's work; the authors should more precisely cite the original to clarify their novel contributions.  
2. A numerical example showing linear convergence (via a semilog plot) is essential.  
3. The dependence of constants $\kappa_1, \dots, \kappa_4$ on $\underline{\alpha}$ and $\overline{\alpha}$ is unclear.  
**Minor issue:**  
4. The sequence of inequalities preceding (13) is confusing; the final inequality relies on trace norm convexity and $-\overline{G} \in \tau \partial \|X\|_{\ast}$ (not the intermediate step), as noted on p. 289 of [20].  
**Summary:** Extension of the "error-bound condition" convergence framework from lasso/group lasso to trace norm.
