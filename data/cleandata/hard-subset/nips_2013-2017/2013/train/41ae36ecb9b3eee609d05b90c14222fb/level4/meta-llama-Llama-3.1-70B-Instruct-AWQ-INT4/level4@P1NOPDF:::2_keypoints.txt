- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss.

- Importance: Addresses a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal-processing applications.

- Presentation and correctness: Paper is carefully written, follows Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique, and the main proof appears correct.

- Missing comparison: Needs a clear comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case (refs [20,22]) and explanation why l1 techniques don’t directly translate.

- Corollary connection: Should explicitly state that the l1-norm result follows as a corollary of the trace-norm case.

- Perceived novelty: Omission of the l1 comparison affects perceived value—some readers may find the extension unsurprising.

- Title suggestion: Recommend reconsidering the word “structured,” since the main novelty concerns the loss/operator A rather than the regularizer.

- Notation issue: Clarify that P is a fixed sampling/mask operator (avoid implying randomness by “sampling”).

- Broken reference: Reference [l337] appears broken and should be fixed.

- Terminology clarity: Define or briefly repeat meanings of “Q-” and “R-” linear convergence for readers unfamiliar with them.

- Residual explanation: Explain the residual R(X) in (6) in relation to Tseng’s original definition (46) for better interpretation.

- Lemma 3.2: Include more discussion/conclusions to support the long technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.

- Update note: Reviewer suggests removing “structured” from the title but ultimately leaves the choice to the authors.
