This paper proves the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method applied to trace-norm regularized learning problems when the loss function has the form of f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is linear and h is strongly convex on any compact set and has Lipschitz gradient.

The paper is an extension of previous works by Tseng, Tseng and Yun, and Zhang et al., which established the same result using the "error-bound condition" for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm. This is a non-trivial extension but the contribution seems purely technical.

The presentation of the proofs is mostly clear. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the proximal gradient method and establish a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem, which is the main contribution of the paper.

Strengths of the paper include:

* The authors show the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending the result of Tseng et al.
* The paper provides a detailed analysis of the proximal gradient method and establishes a new Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm-regularized problem.

Weaknesses of the paper include:

* The contribution is purely technical and does not provide new insights into the problem.
* The paper lacks a numerical example showing the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm.

Minor issues with the paper include:

* The sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing and could be clarified.
* The dependence of the constants κ1, ..., κ4 on the choice of ᾱ and ᾱ is not clear.

To strengthen the paper, the authors could provide a numerical example showing the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm. Additionally, the authors could discuss the implications of their results for the analysis of ADMM and provide more details on the dependence of the constants κ1, ..., κ4 on the choice of ᾱ and ᾱ.

Overall, the paper provides a technical contribution to the field of optimization and machine learning, but could be improved with additional examples and discussion.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides a new result on the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm for trace norm-regularized problems.
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear analysis of the proximal gradient method.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The contribution is purely technical and does not provide new insights into the problem.
* The paper lacks a numerical example showing the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm.
