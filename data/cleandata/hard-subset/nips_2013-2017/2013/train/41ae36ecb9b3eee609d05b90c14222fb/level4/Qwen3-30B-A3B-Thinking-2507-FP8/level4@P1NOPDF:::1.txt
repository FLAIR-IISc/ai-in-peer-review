Please include a simulation demonstrating linear convergence of the PG algorithm when the loss is not strongly convex (for varying stability parameters), and explicitly discuss the regime where the theory fails. Additionally, clarify how this analysis extends to ADMM.  

This paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient (PG) method for trace-norm regularized learning, where the loss takes the form $f(X) = h(A(X))$ with $A$ linear and $h$ strongly convex on compact sets with Lipschitz gradient. The work extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], which proved similar results for Lasso and group Lasso using the "error-bound condition," to the trace-norm setting. While this extension is non-trivial, the contribution is primarily technical.  

**Strengths:**  
- Demonstrates linear convergence of the PG algorithm for trace-norm regularized problems, generalizing prior error-bound-based results.  

**Weaknesses:**  
- The contribution lacks conceptual novelty; it is a technical extension of existing methods.  
- A numerical example showing linear convergence (e.g., a semi-log plot of objective value versus iterations) is missing.  

**Detailed Comments:**  
1. The proof structures for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely parallel those in Tseng [20]. The authors should explicitly clarify their novel technical steps relative to Tseng’s work to better delineate their contribution.  
2. A numerical demonstration of linear convergence is essential. Without it, the theoretical result remains unverified in practice.  
3. It remains unclear how the constants $\kappa_1, \dots, \kappa_4$ depend on the choice of $\underline{\alpha}$ and $\overline{\alpha}$. A sensitivity analysis or explicit dependence would strengthen the result.  
4. The sequence of inequalities preceding (13) is confusing. The final inequality follows from trace norm convexity and $-\bar{G} \in \tau \partial \|X\|_*$ (not the intermediate steps), as noted in Tseng [20, p. 289].  

**Summary:**  
The paper extends the error-bound-based linear convergence framework from Lasso/group Lasso to trace-norm regularization. While the technical extension is valid, the lack of novelty and absence of empirical validation weaken its impact. Addressing the points above—particularly providing a numerical example and clarifying the role of stability parameters—would significantly strengthen the submission.
