- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm-regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity—important for ML and signal processing.
- Paper is carefully and clearly written; proof follows Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique and appears correct; fills an open question for the nuclear norm case.
- Lacks a clear comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case (refs [20,22]); should explain why l1 techniques do not directly translate and note l1 as a corollary.
- Title is misleading: main novelty pertains to the loss (flexibility from linear operator A) rather than making the trace norm a “structured” regularizer—suggest removing “structured.”
- Clarify notation for "P" (called sampling/mask operator but is fixed, not random).
- Fix broken reference [l337].
- Define or briefly repeat meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence for readers unfamiliar with the terms.
- Explain the residual R(X) in (6) in relation to the original definition (46) in Tseng [20] for better interpretation.
- Add more discussion/conclusions to justify and contextualize the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
- Update: Reviewer welcomes authors’ responses and reiterates the title suggestion (remove “structured”), but leaves the final decision to the authors.
