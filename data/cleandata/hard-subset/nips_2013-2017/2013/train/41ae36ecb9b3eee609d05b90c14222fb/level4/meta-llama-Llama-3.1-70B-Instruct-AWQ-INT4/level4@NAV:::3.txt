The proximal gradient algorithm is a widely used method for trace norm regularized risk minimization. While its sublinear global convergence rate is well-established, it is also known that the convergence rate becomes linear when the loss function is strongly convex. This paper extends the existing framework, initially proposed by Luo and Tseng, by relaxing the strong convexity assumption. The authors successfully prove a local error bound, enabling them to demonstrate the asymptotic linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm for trace norm regularization. The paper largely adheres to the conventional structure for proving local error bounds, with a novel observation facilitating the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.
Quality: 
The technical soundness of this paper appears to be satisfactory. However, a clarification is needed regarding the choice of kappa3 (with alpha < 1) in the appendix, line 085. The authors should provide an explanation for how this choice leads to the claimed inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F.
Clarity: 
The paper is generally well-structured and easy to follow. Nevertheless, due to its technical nature, it may be beneficial to present the main claims first, followed by a detailed explanation. Specifically, after stating the local error bound, the authors could consider postponing the technical proof and instead focus on demonstrating the linear convergence rate, which does not introduce significant new concepts.
Originality: 
The originality of this paper is moderate. The novel observation allowing simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is interesting and new; however, the authors largely follow the existing approach to establish the local error bound. The remaining steps do not introduce significant new ideas.
Significance: 
The significance of this paper is primarily theoretical, with limited implications for algorithm design. While extending the linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm to more practical scenarios (i.e., a relaxed form of strong convexity) is a valuable contribution, the asymptotic nature of the proof and claim somewhat reduces its significance. The authors' approach to diagonalizing matrices may be of independent interest. This work demonstrates the asymptotic linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm for trace norm regularized risk minimization under a relaxed form of strong convexity. The authors primarily follow the established framework due to Luo and Tseng, with a new observation enabling the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices. Overall, this work is incremental and moderately interesting from a theoretical perspective.
