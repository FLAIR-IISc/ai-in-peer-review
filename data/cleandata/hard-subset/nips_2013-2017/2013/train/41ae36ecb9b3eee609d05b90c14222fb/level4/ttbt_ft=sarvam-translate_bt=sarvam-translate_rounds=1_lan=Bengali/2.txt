This paper proves the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regular problems, under weaker assumptions than the strong convexities of the loss function, which is a significant problem for many ML and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows Seng's [20] Lyapunov error bound technique, the application of which in the nuclear norm domain remains an open question. As far as I understand, the core of the proof seems correct.

* What I miss in this paper is a clear comparison/similarity with vector fields for l1-norm regularization, such as in [20, 22] and related work.

It would be very helpful to relate the results and techniques used in that case and explain whether/why l1-case techniques directly translate to trace norms. (Also, the l1-norm case should now be followed as a corollary from trace norms).

To judge the value of the paper, it should be considered, because some people may not find it very surprising that the l1-result is also applicable to trace norms (I still consider it a very valuable contribution).

Besides that, I would suggest a slight change in the title of the paper, because the main new contribution is that the loss function is more affected by trace norm regularization than by making it structured. (Since the newly acquired flexibility by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regularization).

* Minor problems
- Symbol: Is P being called a sampling or mask operator? (Sampling may imply randomness, but here P is constant)
-l337: Broken reference
- The meaning of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence is probably not as widely known, and may be repeated for clarity.
- The residual R(X) defined in (6) can be additionally explained with the original definition (46) by [20] for better explanation.

In general, I would vote for including some more discussion and possibly a conclusion in favor of the long technical part of the proof of Lemma 3.2.
