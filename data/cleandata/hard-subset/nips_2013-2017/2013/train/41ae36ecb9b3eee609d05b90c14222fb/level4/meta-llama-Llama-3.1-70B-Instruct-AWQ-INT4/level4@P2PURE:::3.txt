For trace norm regularized risk minimization, a popular method is the proximal gradient algorithm. The sublinear global convergence rate of this algorithm is well-understood, whereas it is also known that the rate is linear if the loss is additionally strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption by following the framework established by Luo and Tseng. In particular, the authors proved a local error bound that allows them to show that the proximal gradient converges asymptotically at a linear rate for trace norm regularization. The paper largely follows the usual pattern to prove the local error bound, with a new observation that enables the simultaneous diagonalization of the matrices.

Quality: 
This paper appears to be technically sound. However, I had a concern regarding the appendix, line 085. The authors should explain why the choice of kappa3 (with alpha < 1) leads to the claimed inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F.

Clarity: 
I was able to follow the paper without much difficulty, although its technical nature might make it challenging for some readers. To improve clarity, it would be beneficial to first outline the main claims and then delve into the details one by one. Specifically, after stating the local error bound, the authors might consider postponing the technical proof of the local error bound and instead immediately prove the linear rate, which does not contain significant new insights.

Originality: 
The originality of this paper is moderate. The observation that allows the authors to simultaneously diagonalize matrices seems to be new and interesting; however, the authors largely follow the existing approach to establish the local error bound, and the remaining steps do not offer significant novelty.

Significance: 
The significance of this paper is mainly theoretical, with limited impact on algorithm design. While it is certainly valuable to extend the linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm to more practical scenarios (i.e., a relaxed form of strong convexity), the significance is somewhat diminished by the asymptotic nature of the proof (and claim). The way the authors handle the diagonalization of matrices might be of some independent interest. This work proves that the proximal gradient algorithm, when applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization, converges asymptotically at a linear rate (under a relaxed form of strong convexity). The authors primarily follow a well-established framework due to Luo and Tseng, with a new observation that allows them to (simultaneously) diagonalize matrices, thus reducing the problem to the vector case. Overall, this work is incremental and moderately interesting from a theoretical perspective.
