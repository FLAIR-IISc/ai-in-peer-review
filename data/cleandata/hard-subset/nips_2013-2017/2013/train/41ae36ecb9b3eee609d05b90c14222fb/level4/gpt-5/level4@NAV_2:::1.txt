This paper proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm–regularized learning when the loss takes the form f(X) = h(A(X)), with A linear and h having a Lipschitz continuous gradient and being strongly convex on any convex compact subset of its domain. 

The work extends the “error-bound condition” framework underlying linear convergence for the LASSO and group LASSO (Tseng; Tseng and Yun; Zhang et al.) to the nuclear norm. While this is a substantial and nontrivial extension, the novelty appears mainly technical.

The exposition of the proofs is largely clear.

Strength:
- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient by extending Tseng-type results to the trace norm setting.

Weakness:
- The contribution seems primarily technical.
- A numerical demonstration of linear convergence would strengthen the paper.

More details:
1. The proof strategies for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirror those in Tseng’s work. Please provide more precise attributions and clearly delineate what is new here versus what follows directly from prior frameworks.
2. Please include an empirical example that clearly illustrates linear convergence (e.g., a semilog plot of objective value versus iterations).
3. It is unclear how the constants κ1, …, κ4 depend on the choices of α̲ and ᾱ. Please clarify these dependencies.

Minor issue:
4. The chain of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is confusing. The final inequality follows from convexity of the trace norm together with −Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖∗, rather than from the intermediate inequality (cf. p. 289 in [20]).

Overall: An extension of prior linear convergence results based on error bounds from LASSO/group LASSO to the trace-norm setting.
