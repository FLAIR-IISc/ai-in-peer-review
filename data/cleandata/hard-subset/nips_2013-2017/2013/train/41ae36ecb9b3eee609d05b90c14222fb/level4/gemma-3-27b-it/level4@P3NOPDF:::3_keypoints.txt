- Main contribution: Proves a local error bound and asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization under a weaker convexity assumption.  
- Key technical novelty: Observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, simplifying analysis by reducing to a vector-valued case.  
- Methodology: Builds on Luo and Tseng’s framework; overall proof strategy largely follows established techniques.  
- Quality: Paper is technically sound and well-motivated.  
- Issue to fix (appendix): Clarify the justification for choosing κ3 (with α<1) and explicitly connect the claim R(Xk) ≤ κ3 ||Xk − Xk+1||F to Sra NIPS’12 Lemma 4.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written, but recommend restructuring—state main results (local error bound and linear rate) up front, then present technical proofs; postpone the standard linear-convergence proof until after the novel local error-bound derivation.  
- Originality: Moderate—simultaneous diagonalization is a valuable novel idea, but the remainder follows known approaches.  
- Significance: Primarily theoretical with limited practical impact due to the asymptotic nature; diagonalization technique may still interest researchers.  
- Overall: Solid, incremental theoretical contribution with moderate interest.
