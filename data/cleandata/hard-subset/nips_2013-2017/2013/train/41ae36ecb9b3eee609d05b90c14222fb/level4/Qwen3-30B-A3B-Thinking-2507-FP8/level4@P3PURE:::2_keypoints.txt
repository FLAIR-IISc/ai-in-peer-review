- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity, relevant to ML and signal processing.  
- Presentation: manuscript is well-written, clearly structured, and follows Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique.  
- Technical validity: core proof arguments appear sound and well-justified.  
- Novelty gap: lacks explicit comparison to the vector ℓ1-norm case (Tseng [20,22]); authors should show how ℓ1 follows as a corollary and why ℓ1 techniques don’t directly transfer.  
- Impact risk: omission of the ℓ1 comparison may make the contribution seem less surprising; adding this context would strengthen impact.  
- Title concern: the term “structured” is misleading; novelty is in the loss-side linear operator A, so title should emphasize loss-side generalization.  
- Notation: clarify that operator P is a fixed mask (not implying randomness by calling it a “mask”).  
- Reference/notation fixes: broken reference at line 337 needs repair.  
- Convergence terminology: briefly define “Q-linear” and “R-linear” convergence for readers unfamiliar with them.  
- Residual linkage: explicitly link residual R(X) in (6) to Tseng’s definition in (46) for clarity.  
- Lemma 3.2: the long proof needs additional discussion or concluding remarks to explain its significance for the overall argument.  
- Overall: paper makes a meaningful contribution; addressing comparative context and minor clarifications would substantially strengthen it.
