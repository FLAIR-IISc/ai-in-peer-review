The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng [20], whose application to the nuclear norm case was still an open question. Upon reviewing the main part of the proof, it appears to be correct as far as I can understand.

However, I feel that the paper would benefit from a clear comparison or analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as seen in [20,22] and related work. It would be helpful to relate the results and techniques used in that case and explain why the l1-case techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm case. Additionally, since the l1-norm case should follow from the trace norm case as a corollary, this connection should be explicitly stated.

When evaluating the value of the paper, this omission should be taken into account, as some readers may find it less surprising that the l1-result also holds for the trace norm (although I still consider it a valuable contribution).

Furthermore, I suggest a slight change to the title of the paper, as the main new contribution affects the loss part more than making the trace norm regularization a "structured" one. The newly gained flexibility introduced by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regularizer.

Minor issues include:

- Notation: The term "P" is referred to as a sampling or mask operator. However, "sampling" might imply randomness, whereas P is fixed.
- The reference [l337] appears to be broken.
- The meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence may not be widely known and could be repeated for clarity.
- The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be additionally explained in relation to the original definition (46) by [20] for better interpretation.

In general, I would recommend including more discussion and conclusions to support the long technical part of the proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
