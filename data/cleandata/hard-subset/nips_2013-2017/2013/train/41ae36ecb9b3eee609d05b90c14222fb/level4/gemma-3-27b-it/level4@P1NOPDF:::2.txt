The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function – a crucial issue for many machine learning and signal processing applications. 

The paper is carefully and clearly written, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng [20]. Applying this technique to the nuclear norm case was previously an open question, and the main part of the proof appears correct to the best of my understanding.

However, the paper would benefit from a clearer comparison or analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as explored in [20, 22] and related work. Relating the results and techniques used in the l1-norm case would be very helpful, and an explanation of why those techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm setting would strengthen the contribution. (Furthermore, the l1-norm case should naturally follow as a corollary of the results presented here.) Considering this point is important when evaluating the paper's value; some readers might find the extension from the l1-norm case to the trace norm case not entirely surprising. Nevertheless, I still consider this a valuable contribution. 

Independently, I suggest the authors consider revising the paper’s title, as the main novelty lies in the relaxation of assumptions on the loss function rather than in characterizing the trace norm regularization as “structured.” (The increased flexibility afforded by the linear operator A is in the loss function, not the regularizer itself.)

*Minor issues:*

- Notation: The operator P is used; should it be described as a sampling or mask operator? While “sampling” might imply randomness, P appears to be a fixed operator in this context.
- Line 337: The reference is broken.
- The notation “Q-” and “R-” linear convergence may not be widely known and could benefit from a brief reiteration for clarity.

- Generally, I would suggest adding more discussion or conclusions to contextualize the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
