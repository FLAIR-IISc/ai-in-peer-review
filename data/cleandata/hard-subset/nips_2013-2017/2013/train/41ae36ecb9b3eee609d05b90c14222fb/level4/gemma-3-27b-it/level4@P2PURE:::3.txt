For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a common choice, with its sublinear global convergence rate and linear rate under strong convexity being well-established. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption, building upon the framework of Luo and Tseng, and proves a local error bound which demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm converges asymptotically at a linear rate for trace norm regularization. The proof of this local error bound largely follows standard techniques, with a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.

Quality:
The paper appears technically sound. I have one clarification request regarding the appendix, line 085. Please explain how a choice of kappa3 (e.g., alpha < 1) leads to the stated inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F.

Clarity:
The paper is generally clear and easy to follow. However, given its technical depth, it would benefit from presenting the main claims upfront, followed by detailed proofs. Specifically, after stating the local error bound, the authors could postpone the complete proof and instead directly demonstrate the linear convergence rate – which, incidentally, contains little novelty.

Originality:
The originality of this work is moderate. The observation allowing simultaneous matrix diagonalization is novel and interesting, but the remainder of the approach to establishing the local error bound largely follows existing methods. The subsequent steps are not new.

Significance:
The significance of this paper lies primarily in its theoretical contribution, with limited direct impact on algorithm design. Extending the linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm to scenarios with a relaxed form of strong convexity is valuable, but the asymptotic nature of the proof and resulting claim somewhat diminishes the significance. The authors’ approach to matrix diagonalization may be of independent interest. In summary, this work demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed form of strong convexity. The authors largely follow the established framework of Luo and Tseng, with a new observation allowing simultaneous matrix diagonalization and reducing the problem, in essence, to the vector case. Overall, this work represents an incremental, moderately interesting theoretical contribution.
