- Paper proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized learning with f(X)=h(A(X)), where h has Lipschitz gradient and is strongly convex on compact sets.
- Contribution extends the "error-bound condition" framework from lasso/group lasso to the trace-norm setting — a non-trivial technical adaptation.
- Proofs are generally well-presented and easy to follow.
- Strength: extends linear convergence analysis of Tseng et al. to proximal gradient for trace-norm problems.
- Weakness: contribution is largely technical with limited broader conceptual novelty.
- Weakness: paper lacks numerical experiments to empirically validate the theoretical linear convergence rate.
- Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 proofs closely follow Tseng’s work and need clearer citations and explanations of the novel adaptations.
- Recommend adding a semilog plot of objective value vs iterations to empirically demonstrate linear convergence.
- The dependency of constants κ1–κ4 on parameters \underbar{\alpha} and \bar{\alpha} is not clearly articulated.
- Minor issue: derivation leading to inequality (13) is confusing; the final step relies on convexity and the subgradient property (-\bar{G} ∈ τ∂||X||*) rather than the stated intermediate inequality.
- Overall: a technical extension of the error-bound framework from lasso/group lasso to the trace-norm setting.
