This paper establishes the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems. This result is achieved under assumptions on the loss function that are weaker than strong convexity, addressing a significant and practical problem in machine learning and signal processing.

The paper is carefully prepared and clearly written. The analysis closely follows the Lipschitzian error bound technique developed by Tseng [20], whose application to the nuclear norm case had remained an open question. Based on my review, the main components of the proof appear to be correct.

While the technical contribution is sound, the paper could be significantly strengthened by providing a more explicit comparison to the analogous vector case of l1-norm regularization, as explored in [20, 22] and related works. A discussion that connects the results and techniques from the l1-norm literature would be highly beneficial. Specifically, explaining if and why the techniques for the l1-norm case do not directly translate to the trace norm setting would better highlight the novelty and challenge of the current work. Although the l1-norm case can now be seen as a corollary of these new results, framing the contribution in this context is important. This is particularly relevant as some readers might perceive the extension from the l1-norm to the trace norm as a natural, if non-trivial, step. A clear explanation of the unique challenges would underscore the value of this contribution.

I would also suggest that the authors reconsider the paper's title. The main contribution appears to be the new flexibility afforded by the linear operator A in the loss function, rather than making the trace norm regularization itself "structured." Therefore, a title that better reflects the contribution to the loss component might be more accurate.

**Minor Suggestions for Improvement:**

*   **Notation:** The term "sampling operator" for P could be potentially misleading, as "sampling" often implies randomness, whereas P is fixed in this context. "Mask operator" might be a clearer alternative.
*   **Definitions:** For the benefit of a broader audience, it would be helpful to briefly restate the definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence within the text.
*   **Clarity on Residual:** The interpretation of the residual R(X) defined in (6) would be enhanced by explicitly connecting its definition to the original formulation in (46) by Tseng [20].
*   **Broken Reference:** The reference on line 337 appears to be broken.
*   **Balance of Content:** The paper dedicates significant space to the technical proof of Lemma 3.2. The authors might consider slightly condensing this section to make room for more high-level discussion, interpretation of the results, and a concluding section, which would improve the paper's overall accessibility and impact.

**Update After Author Feedback:**

I thank the authors for their detailed response, which has answered most of my questions. Regarding the title, my suggestion was aimed at the word "structured," which I found to be a less precise descriptor of the main contribution than an emphasis on the loss function.
