- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace/nuclear-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss.  
- Writing and presentation: paper is careful, clear, and the main proof appears correct.  
- Technique: work closely follows Tseng’s Lipschitzian error-bound approach; applying it to the nuclear norm was previously open.  
- Missing comparison: lacks a clear comparison to the vector l1-norm case (e.g., [20,22]) and related work.  
- l1 corollary: the l1 result should be stated explicitly as a corollary of the trace-norm result and the relationship to l1 techniques should be explained.  
- Novelty note: some readers may find extending l1 results to trace norm unsurprising, though the contribution is still valuable.  
- Title suggestion: “structured” in the title is potentially misleading—flexibility from linear operator A pertains to the loss, not the regularizer.  
- Minor issue — terminology: avoid “sampling” for P (suggest “mask,” “projection,” or “selection”) since P is fixed.  
- Minor issue — reference: broken reference at line 337.  
- Minor issue — notation/explanation: briefly remind readers of Q-linear vs R-linear convergence.  
- Minor issue — residual R(X): further explanation of residual R(X) in (6) and relation to Tseng [20, Eq. (46)] is needed.  
- Presentation suggestion: add more discussion and a short conclusion to balance the technical proof and to clarify implications and limitations of assumptions.  
- Update: after author feedback, most questions were addressed; reviewer reiterates the title concern but affirms the core contribution remains important.
