- Author added reference: inequality |Rk| <= κ |Xk - X_{k+1}| justified by S. Sra, "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting" (NIPS'12, Lemma 4).  
- Context: proximal gradient is standard for trace-norm regularization; known sublinear global rate, linear under strong convexity.  
- Contribution: relaxes strong convexity via a local error bound (Luo & Tseng framework) to show asymptotic linear convergence for proximal gradient on trace-norm problems.  
- Technical novelty: a useful observation that allows simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the problem to a vector case.  
- Proof approach: largely follows conventional local error bound techniques; subsequent steps are standard once reduction is made.  
- Quality: paper appears technically sound overall.  
- Clarification needed: choice of κ3 (e.g., α < 1) in appendix (line 085) and why it yields R(X^k) <= κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F is unclear.  
- Clarity: generally readable; recommend stating main claims up front and postponing technical proof after presenting convergence results.  
- Originality: moderate—matrix diagonalization observation is novel, but most of the local error bound derivation follows existing methods.  
- Significance: mainly theoretical with limited algorithmic impact; asymptotic nature of the result reduces practical significance; overall incremental and moderately interesting.
