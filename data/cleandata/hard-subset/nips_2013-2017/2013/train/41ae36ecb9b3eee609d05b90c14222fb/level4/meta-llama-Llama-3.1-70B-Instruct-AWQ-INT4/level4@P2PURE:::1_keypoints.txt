- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear, h locally strongly convex and Lipschitz smooth.  
- Extends Tseng, Tseng & Yun, and Zhang et al. results from lasso/group lasso to trace norm using an error-bound condition.  
- Extension is non-trivial but appears primarily a technical contribution.  
- Proofs are mostly clearly presented.  
- Strength: demonstrates linear convergence of proximal gradient and builds on prior Tseng et al. work.  
- Weakness: contribution is mainly technical and lacks numerical validation of linear convergence.  
- Recommendation: add a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but proximal gradient still converges linearly, varying stability and discussing failure cases.  
- Recommendation: discuss how the analysis could be extended to ADMM.  
- Comment 1: Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely resemble Tseng’s proofs; provide more precise references to clarify novelty.  
- Comment 2: Include a numerical example (e.g., semilog plot of function value vs iterations) to demonstrate linear convergence.  
- Comment 3: Clarify how constants κ1…κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ and α̿.  
- Minor issue: the inequality sequence before (13) is confusing; the final step follows from convexity of the trace norm and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂‖X‖∗, not the intermediate inequality as in p.289 of [20].
