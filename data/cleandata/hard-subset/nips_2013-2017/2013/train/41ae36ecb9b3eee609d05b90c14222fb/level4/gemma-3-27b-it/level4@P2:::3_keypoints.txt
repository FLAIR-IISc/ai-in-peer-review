- Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm regularized problems under conditions weaker than strong convexity.  
- Main technical contribution: a novel Lipschitzian error bound enabled by a spectral (simultaneous diagonalization) observation about solutions.  
- Technically sound with logical argument progression; main result is a meaningful theoretical extension.  
- Requests clarification in appendix: justify inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F and how choosing kappa3 (with alpha < 1) yields this bound.  
- Writing/structure suggestion: present the main claims (error bound and linear convergence) earlier and move detailed error bound proofs later to improve readability.  
- Originality is moderate: diagonalization insight is notable, but much of the analysis builds on Luo and Tseng’s established framework.  
- Significance is primarily theoretical; relaxation of strong convexity is relevant but the asymptotic nature limits immediate practical impact.  
- Acceptance arguments: rigorous analysis, valuable extension to non-strongly-convex cases, useful diagonalization observation, meaningful extension of existing framework.  
- Rejection arguments: asymptotic result limits practical impact, moderate originality, missing clarification in appendix, limited discussion of algorithmic implications or extensive experiments.
