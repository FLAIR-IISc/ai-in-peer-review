- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)), where h is strongly convex on compacts and has Lipschitz gradient.
- Extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] results from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm; extension is non-trivial but primarily technical.
- Presentation and proofs are mostly clear but contain areas needing clarification and improvement.
- Reviewer recommends adding simulations where the loss is not strongly convex yet proximal gradient converges linearly (for varying stability) and discussing failure cases of the theory.
- Recommend discussing how the convergence analysis could be extended to ADMM.
- Main strength: provides a theoretical linear-convergence result that generalizes prior work.
- Main weaknesses: contribution seems mainly technical and the paper lacks numerical examples (e.g., semilog plots showing linear convergence).
- Proof outlines for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely resemble Tseng’s work; authors should cite and distinguish the original contributions more precisely.
- Unclear how constants κ1–κ4 depend on the choices of ᾱ and α; the paper should clarify these dependencies.
- Minor issue: the inequality chain before (13) is confusing; the final step follows from convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||* (see p.289 in [20]), not the intermediate inequality.
