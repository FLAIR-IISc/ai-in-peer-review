- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm regularized problems without assuming strong convexity.
- Technical novelty: Introduces a Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to the optimum to a residual map, enabling the linear-rate proof.
- Framework extension: Extends Luo–Tseng style error-bound analysis from ℓ1/group lasso to the non-polyhedral trace norm setting.
- Applicability: Results cover important ML problems like matrix completion (square loss) and multi-task learning (logistic loss).
- Strength: Significant theoretical advance clarifying first-order method behavior for trace-norm-regularized problems.
- Weakness: Needs clearer exposition of how the trace-norm proof techniques diverge from prior ℓ1/group-lasso analyses, especially handling coupled singular values.
- Weakness: Experiments show linear convergence but lack depth; should probe dependence of convergence on problem parameters and error-bound constant η.
- Weakness: Limited discussion of practical implications, e.g., guidance on choosing regularization τ or designing operator A for faster convergence.
- Detailed comment: The dependence of constants κ1–κ4 on step-size bounds (α, ᾱ) is not explicitly discussed and should be clarified.
- Detailed comment: Derivation of inequality (18) in Theorem 3.1 needs a clearer explanation that it follows from the subgradient inequality at the optimal point.
- Suggestion: Discuss potential extensions of the error-bound analysis to other algorithms like ADMM.
- Overall: Strong, technically sound theoretical paper that would benefit from clearer positioning against prior work and deeper experimental/practical discussion.
