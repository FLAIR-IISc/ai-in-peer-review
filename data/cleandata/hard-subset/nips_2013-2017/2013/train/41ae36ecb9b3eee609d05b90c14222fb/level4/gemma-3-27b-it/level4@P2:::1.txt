This paper establishes the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for solving trace-norm regularized learning problems, specifically when the loss function takes the form f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is a linear operator and h is strongly convex on any compact set with a Lipschitz gradient. The work extends existing linear convergence results—originally established by Tseng [20] and further developed by Zhang et al. [22] for Lasso and group Lasso—to the trace norm regularization setting. While a non-trivial extension, the core contribution appears to be primarily technical.

The paper’s presentation is generally clear, though some sections could benefit from more precise referencing.

**Strengths:**

*   Demonstrates the linear convergence of the PGM, extending prior results to trace norm regularization. This provides a valuable theoretical underpinning for algorithms commonly used in low-rank matrix recovery and related machine learning tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The primary contribution feels incremental. The work largely adapts existing techniques from sparse regularization to the trace norm, without introducing fundamentally new algorithmic or theoretical ideas.
*   The lack of a compelling numerical demonstration is a significant drawback. While the authors mention numerical experiments in Section 5, these are not presented with sufficient detail to convincingly illustrate the claimed linear convergence behavior.
*   The dependence of constants in the convergence analysis (κ1, κ2, κ3, κ4) on the step size parameters ᾱ and α is not clearly articulated, hindering practical application.

**Detailed Comments:**

1.  The proof outlines for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 4.1 closely mirror those found in Tseng [20] and other related works. The authors should more explicitly acknowledge and contrast their approach with these prior results to clearly delineate their contribution. A more precise citation of relevant sections within these prior works is also recommended.

2.  A key improvement would be to include a detailed numerical example showcasing the linear convergence of the PGM. A semilog plot of the objective function value versus the number of iterations would be particularly informative. This example should explore different levels of stability (e.g., varying the condition number of the problem) and help to validate the theoretical results.  Critically, the authors should discuss scenarios where the theory *fails* to predict observed behavior, providing insights into limitations of the model.

3.  Clarification on how the constants κ1 through κ4 depend on the step size parameters ᾱ and α is needed. This information is crucial for understanding the practical implications of the convergence analysis.

4.  The sequence of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is somewhat unclear. The last inequality is justified by the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that -∇f(X∗) ∈ τ∂‖X∗‖∗ (as indicated in Tseng [20], page 289), rather than directly from the intermediate inequality. This minor correction would improve clarity.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a technically sound result extending existing convergence analyses to a new regularization setting. However, the incremental nature of the contribution, coupled with the lack of a robust numerical validation, weakens its overall impact. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above—particularly providing a detailed numerical evaluation—would significantly strengthen the paper.

**Recommendation:**

Accept with minor revisions. The authors should provide a more comprehensive numerical evaluation and clarify the dependence of the convergence constants on the step size parameters.
