

The paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems under weaker conditions than strong convexity of the loss function—a significant contribution for machine learning and signal processing applications. The work builds on Tseng's Lipschitzian error bound technique [20], resolving the open question of its applicability to nuclear norm regularization. The core proof is technically sound and well-motivated, with clear exposition throughout.

The primary weakness lies in the absence of a comparative discussion with the well-studied l1-norm case (e.g., [20, 22]). A dedicated section contrasting the vector l1 framework with the matrix trace norm setting would greatly enhance the paper's impact. Specifically, the authors should clarify: (1) why techniques from the l1 case (e.g., error bounds for sparse recovery) do not directly extend to trace norm, and (2) how the current results generalize the l1 theory (noting that the l1 case should follow as a special case when the regularizer is diagonal). Without this, readers may undervalue the novelty, as the trace norm extension might seem "expected" to some. This comparison is essential to contextualize the contribution and justify its significance beyond a straightforward generalization.

Regarding the title, the phrase "structured trace norm regularization" is misleading. The key innovation concerns the loss function's conditions (weak convexity), not the regularization structure. The linear operator \(A\) in the loss \(f(Ax)\) introduces flexibility *in the loss*, not in the regularizer. A revised title such as "Linear Convergence of Proximal Gradient for Trace Norm Regularization under Weak Convexity" would better reflect the technical contribution.

Minor issues require attention:  
- **Notation**: The operator \(P\) is termed a "sampling operator," which implies randomness. Since \(P\) is fixed (e.g., a projection mask), "mask" or "projection" is preferable.  
- **Reference**: Line 337 cites a broken reference.  
- **Terminology**: The definitions of "Q-linear" and "R-linear" convergence (Section 2) should be briefly reiterated for readers unfamiliar with these terms.  
- **Residual interpretation**: The residual \(R(X)\) (Equation 6) should be explicitly linked to Tseng's residual \(R_{\text{Tseng}}\) (Equation 46 of [20]) to clarify its role in the error bound framework.  

The paper dedicates substantial space to Lemma 3.2’s proof (Section 3), but a concise summary of its intuition and implications would better serve readers. A brief concluding remark on *why* the error bound condition (Assumption 3) is satisfied in key applications (e.g., matrix completion, low-rank recovery) would strengthen the practical relevance.

**Author Feedback Consideration**: The authors addressed most concerns. Regarding the title, the term "structured" was clarified as referring to the *problem structure* (not the regularizer), but the current phrasing remains confusing. The revised title should unambiguously emphasize the *loss function conditions* as the novel element.

**Overall Assessment**: This is a technically strong paper with a meaningful contribution to non-strongly convex optimization. The core proof is rigorous, and the resolution of Tseng’s open question is valuable. However, the lack of comparison to the l1 case risks diminishing perceived novelty. Addressing this—and refining the title—would elevate the paper’s clarity and impact. The minor issues are easily fixable.  

**Recommendation**: Accept (with minor revisions). The work advances the state-of-the-art for a fundamental class of problems. The proposed revisions are straightforward and would significantly improve accessibility without altering the technical substance. The contribution is not merely "expected" but resolves a specific gap in the literature, making it a solid NIPS submission.
