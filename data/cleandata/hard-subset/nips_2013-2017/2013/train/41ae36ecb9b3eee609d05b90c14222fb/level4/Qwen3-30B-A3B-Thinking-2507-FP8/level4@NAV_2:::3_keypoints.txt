- Scope: Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems without assuming strong convexity.  
- Main result: Proves a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm problems and uses it to obtain asymptotic linear convergence via Luo–Tseng framework.  
- Key technical novelty: New observation on simultaneous diagonalization of certain matrices that permits adapting error-bound techniques to the trace-norm (non-polyhedral) case.  
- Quality assessment: Paper is technically sound overall.  
- Technical concern: Appendix (line 085) — justification for choice of kappa3 (the condition alpha < 1) is insufficient to support R(X^k) ≤ kappa3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written; should state main results up front and postpone the detailed proof of the local error bound to highlight the convergence implications earlier.  
- Originality: Moderate — the diagonalization trick is interesting, but the overall approach follows existing composite optimization methodologies with specific trace-norm adaptations.  
- Significance: Theoretical contribution extending linear convergence guarantees to broader loss functions; asymptotic nature limits immediate practical impact, though the matrix diagonalization idea may be useful in future work.  
- Prior art note: A related inequality |R_k| ≤ κ ||X_k − X_{k+1}|| was previously established in Suvrit Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4).
