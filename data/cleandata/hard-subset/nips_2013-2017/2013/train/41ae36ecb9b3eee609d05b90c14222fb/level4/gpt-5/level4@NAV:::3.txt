For trace-norm–regularized risk minimization, a widely used approach is the proximal gradient method. Its global sublinear rate is well known, and the rate becomes linear when the loss is additionally strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity requirement using the Luo–Tseng framework. Specifically, the authors establish a local error bound, which enables them to show that proximal gradient attains asymptotic linear convergence for trace-norm regularization. The paper largely follows the standard route for proving a local error bound, with a new observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of the matrices.

Quality: 
The paper appears technically sound. The only gap I see is in the appendix, line 085. Please clarify why choosing kappa3 (e.g., with alpha < 1) implies the stated inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F.

Clarity: 
I could follow the paper without major difficulty. Given the technical density, it may help to present the main claims up front and then delve into details. In particular, after stating the local error bound, consider postponing its highly technical proof and proceed directly to the linear-rate result (which, incidentally, contains little that is new).

Originality: 
The paper’s originality is moderate. The observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization appears new and interesting; beyond that, the approach to the local error bound largely mirrors existing methods. The remaining steps are not novel.

Significance: 
The contribution is primarily theoretical, with limited impact on algorithm design. While extending linear convergence of proximal gradient to more practical settings (i.e., a relaxed form of strong convexity) is valuable, the impact is tempered by the asymptotic nature of the result. The matrix diagonalization technique may be of independent interest. This work shows that proximal gradient, applied to trace-norm–regularized risk minimization, converges linearly in the limit under a relaxed strong convexity condition. The authors mostly follow the Luo–Tseng blueprint, using a new simultaneous diagonalization observation to effectively reduce to the vector case. Overall, the contribution is incremental and moderately interesting from a theoretical standpoint.
