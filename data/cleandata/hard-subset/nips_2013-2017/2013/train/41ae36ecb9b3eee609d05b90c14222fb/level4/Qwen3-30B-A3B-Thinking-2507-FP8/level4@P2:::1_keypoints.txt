- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X))+τ‖X‖* without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Main technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization enabling the linear-rate proof.  
- Key insight: optimal solutions and their gradients share singular vectors, exploited via matrix perturbation theory.  
- Extends prior error-bound based linear-convergence results (Tseng et al.) from l1/group-lasso to trace norm, which is non-trivial due to the trace norm’s non-polyhedral epigraph.  
- Theoretical development is technically sound with rigorous proofs that resolve an open question about trace-norm regularization.  
- The new error bound may be of independent interest beyond this convergence analysis.  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion, classification) support the predicted linear convergence in practice.  
- Contribution is primarily technical: an extension of existing error-bound techniques rather than a fundamentally new methodological approach.  
- The paper should better articulate precisely how its approach differs from and builds on Tseng’s framework to clarify novelty.  
- Numerical results lack semilog plots of objective vs iterations; such plots are needed to clearly demonstrate the linear rate.  
- Dependence of constants (κ1–κ4) on step-size parameters (α, ᾱ) is not specified, limiting practical applicability of the bounds.  
- The connection to prior work (e.g., Tseng [20]) needs clearer, more precise citation and explanation of which proof elements are reused.  
- Presentation is generally clear but the sequence of inequalities before (13) is confusing; the final step follows from trace-norm convexity and optimality, not the intermediate inequality.  
- Significance is practical for ML problems with trace-norm regularization but limited to losses of the form h(A(X)); broader impact should be better motivated.  
- The paper should discuss failure modes, e.g., when the linear operator A has a non-trivial nullspace.  
- Suggested revisions: add semilog convergence plots for non-strongly convex losses, detail dependence of constants on problem/step parameters, clarify relation to prior work, and address A’s nullspace case.  
- Extension to ADMM is a promising future direction but not addressed here.
