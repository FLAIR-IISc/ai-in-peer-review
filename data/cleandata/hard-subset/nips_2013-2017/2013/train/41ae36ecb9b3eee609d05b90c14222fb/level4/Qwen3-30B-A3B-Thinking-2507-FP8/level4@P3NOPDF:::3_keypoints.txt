- Paper studies convergence rate of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization, proving asymptotic linear convergence under relaxed strong convexity.  
- Main novelty: a simultaneous diagonalization observation that reduces the matrix problem to a vector case and enables the local error bound proof.  
- Core contribution: proof of a local error bound for trace-norm regularization, yielding asymptotic linear convergence of PG.  
- Technical content is overall sound and builds on Luo–Tseng framework.  
- The local error bound proof relies on Lemma 4 from Sra (NIPS 2012), which addresses a similar relation |R_k| ≤ κ |X_k − X_{k+1}|.  
- A remaining gap: Appendix A (line 085) lacks justification for the choice of κ3 when α < 1; dependence of κ3 on α and other constants should be clarified.  
- Presentation could be improved: the local error bound is stated early but its technical proof is deferred, obscuring the main contribution.  
- The convergence proof in Section 4 is standard within the Luo–Tseng framework and adds little beyond existing analyses.  
- Originality is moderate: the diagonalization trick is novel and useful, but the overall approach extends prior frameworks rather than introducing a fundamentally new method.  
- Significance is modest: theoretical extension to relaxed strong convexity is meaningful, but asymptotic (k → ∞) linear rates limit practical impact.  
- The diagonalization technique may have independent value for other matrix problems, but the paper does not explore broader applications.  
- Recommendation: suitable for publication if the κ3 gap in Appendix A is resolved and the exposition is reorganized to highlight the diagonalization insight earlier.
