- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems without assuming strong convexity.  
- Builds rigorously on the Lipschitzian error bound framework of Luo and Tseng [10]; core argument appears technically sound.  
- Significant novelty: extends Tseng’s technique to the trace norm case, resolving an open question.  
- Broadens applicability of PGM to problems in machine learning and signal processing where strong convexity fails.  
- Should include a clearer comparison to l1-norm results (e.g., [10,22]) and explain why l1 techniques do not directly transfer to trace norm.  
- Recommend showing the l1-norm linear convergence as a direct corollary to highlight novelty and preempt perceptions of trivial extension.  
- Title critique: the term “structured” is unnecessary; a title emphasizing the loss function’s flexibility via operator A would be more accurate.  
- Clarify terminology: distinguish “sampling operator” from “mask operator” since “sampling” may imply randomness while P is fixed.  
- Fix reference [33], which appears broken.  
- Repeat definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence for reader accessibility.  
- Explicitly link the residual R(X) in Eq. (7) to the definition in [10] (Eq. 4.6) to aid interpretation.  
- Paper overly focused on technical proofs (notably Lemma 4.1); add broader discussion of implications and conclusions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical result, technically sound proofs, relevant to applications, and clearly written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited comparative discussion to prior l1 work and an imprecise title.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing the l1 comparison, title refinement, and the listed minor points.
