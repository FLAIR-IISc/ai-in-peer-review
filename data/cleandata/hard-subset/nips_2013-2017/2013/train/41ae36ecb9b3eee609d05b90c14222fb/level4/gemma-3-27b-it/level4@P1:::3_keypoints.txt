Paper studies PGM convergence for trace-norm regularization, aiming to prove linear convergence without strong convexity assumptions.  
Main result: proves a new Lipschitzian error bound and uses it to show linear asymptotic convergence of the PGM.  
Key technical contribution: a Lipschitzian error bound based on a novel observation about the spectral/diagonalization structure of solutions.  
Method: leverages the Luo and Tseng convergence-analysis framework to derive linear asymptotic rates.  
Quality: technically sound and well-structured overall.  
Technical concern: appendix claim |Rk| <= kappa3 |Xk - X_{k+1}|_F (line 85) needs clarification on choosing kappa3 (e.g., why alpha < 1 suffices).  
Related work: should explicitly acknowledge Suvrit Sra (NIPS'12) "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting," which contains a similar lemma (Lemma 4).  
Clarity: paper is dense; recommend a clearer roadmap, present main result first, and postpone detailed error-bound proofs to improve readability.  
Originality: moderate—diagonalization insight is novel, but overall approach follows established techniques.  
Significance: meaningful theoretical advance by relaxing strong convexity for linear convergence, though asymptotic nature limits immediate practical impact.  
Pros for acceptance: establishes linear convergence under weaker assumptions; introduces insightful spectral observation enabling the error bound; advances theoretical understanding of PGM.  
Cons against acceptance: results are asymptotic with limited practical impact; heavy reliance on existing frameworks with limited novelty beyond diagonalization; presentation is dense and could be more accessible.  
Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying the appendix inequality and acknowledging related prior work.
