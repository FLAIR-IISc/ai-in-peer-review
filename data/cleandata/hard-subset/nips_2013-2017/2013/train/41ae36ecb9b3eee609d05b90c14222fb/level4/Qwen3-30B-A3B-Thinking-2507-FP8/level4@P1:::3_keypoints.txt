- Shows proximal gradient method (PGM) attains linear convergence for trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity.  
- Main technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization that enables use of the Luo–Tseng framework.  
- Extends prior strong-convexity results (e.g., Schmidt et al.) to practical settings like matrix completion with non-injective measurement operators.  
- Technically sound and well-executed; proofs are thorough overall.  
- Specific gap: appendix inequality |R(X^k)| ≤ κ₃‖X^k − X^{k+1}‖_F (line 085) needs clearer justification and explanation of choosing κ₃ with α < 1.  
- Numerical experiments (Sections 5.1–5.2) strongly support theoretical claims, showing linear convergence in matrix completion and classification.  
- Well-organized and clearly written, but the proof depth may challenge some readers.  
- Suggestion: present main results and implications earlier and more explicitly connect the error bound to linear convergence for non-experts.  
- Original insight: Proposition 4.2 showing optimal solutions and their gradients share singular vector structures, enabling simultaneous diagonalization.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension of Luo–Tseng-style error-bound analysis to non-polyhedral (trace norm) regularization.  
- Significance: theoretically important extension of linear convergence guarantees to broader loss functions relevant to ML.  
- Limitation: convergence guarantee is asymptotic—only holds when iterates are sufficiently close to the optimum; no guidance on the required proximity or initialization strategies.  
- Practicality: more development needed to connect results to implementation (e.g., estimating thresholds, initialization).  
- Overall assessment: solid, incremental theoretical advance with novel diagonalization insight; recommendation to accept.
