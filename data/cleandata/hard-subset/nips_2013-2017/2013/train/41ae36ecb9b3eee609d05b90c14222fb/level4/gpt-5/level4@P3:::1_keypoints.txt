- Studies composite problem min_X f(X)+τ‖X‖* with f(X)=h(A(X)) under smoothness and localized strong convexity assumptions.  
- Main theoretical contribution: a local Lipschitzian error bound linking distance to the solution set to the residual ‖Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X‖F.  
- Shows proximal gradient method (PGM) has asymptotic linear convergence (R-linear iterates, Q-linear objectives) with standard step-size, without strong convexity of f.  
- Extends error-bound–based linear convergence results from polyhedral regularizers (ℓ1/group LASSO) to the nuclear norm, which is nontrivial due to non-polyhedral epigraph and singular-value coupling.  
- New technical ingredient: spectral alignment at optimality (singular vectors of X̄ and −∇f(X̄) coincide) enabling a restricted invertibility argument via matrix perturbation.  
- Theorems (3.1 error bound, 3.2 PGM convergence) are clearly stated and the proof strategy using Luo–Tseng machinery is sound.  
- Restricted invertibility lemma and spectral decomposition propositions address the nuclear-norm–specific challenges effectively.  
- Assumptions (e.g., on h and A) are reasonable for many ML losses; the analysis is appropriately local/asymptotic.  
- Experiments (matrix completion and classification) show semilog plots consistent with local linear convergence near solutions.  
- Clarity could improve by defining Lf and L consistently and reminding readers early that convergence is asymptotic.  
- Provide explicit expressions or supplement pointers for constants κ1–κ4 to increase transparency and reusability.  
- Some derivation steps (inequality (18) and the chain before (13)) need clearer attribution and a minor correction.  
- Paper is well organized and readable, with mostly consistent notation.  
- Originality: the nuclear-norm extension and spectral alignment/restricted invertibility arguments are novel and nontrivial.  
- Significance: important for low-rank learning; the error bound may enable linear-rate guarantees for other algorithms (coordinate updates, proximal Newton, ADMM).  
- Pros: first error-bound/linear-convergence result for nuclear-norm without f strong convexity; careful analysis; experiments support theory.  
- Cons: contribution is largely technical within an existing framework; dependence of constants on algorithmic parameters is opaque; limited discussion of scope/failure modes.  
- Suggest citing exact Luo–Tseng/ℓ1 prior lemmas/sections to highlight which arguments are adapted and where novelty lies.  
- Recommend additional numerical experiments without strong convexity (e.g., non-injective sampling) and varying conditioning to show rate dependence.  
- Discuss failure modes: when Assumption 2.1(b) fails, sublevel sets unbounded, or restricted invertibility degenerates; clarify effects of non-unique solutions.  
- Provide explicit formulas or bounds for κ1–κ4 (or a concise main-text statement with supplement pointer).  
- Minor correction: clarify the justification for the last inequality before (13), citing convexity of trace norm and subgradient inclusion.  
- Comment on extensions: whether accelerated methods (with restarts) or ADMM would inherit linear rates and what extra assumptions are needed.  
- Fix notation/typos: define residual map R and Sτ at first use, unify L/Lf and step-size symbols, and ensure complete references.  
- Overall assessment: solid, careful theoretical contribution filling a gap in nuclear-norm convergence theory; recommend acceptance after minor revisions.
