The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal processing applications. 
The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Luo and Tseng [10], whose application to the nuclear norm case was an open question. The main part of the proof appears correct, to the best of my understanding. 
A key aspect missing is a more detailed comparison and analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as found in works such as [22] and related literature. It would be beneficial to relate to the results and techniques used in that context and to explain why the techniques for the l1-case do not directly translate to the trace norm case. (Ideally, the l1-norm result should follow as a corollary from the trace norm result.) While I find this a valuable contribution, recognizing this connection will help contextualize its significance, as some researchers might anticipate the extension from l1 to trace norm.
Regarding the value of the paper, this consideration is important. A slight revision of the title is suggested, as the main new contribution primarily affects the loss function component rather than inherently making the trace norm regularization “structured.” (Since the added flexibility arises from the linear operator A on the loss side, not within the regularizer itself.)
Minor issues:
- Notation: Clarify whether “P” represents a sampling or mask operator. While “sampling” implies randomness, “P” is fixed in this context.
- Line 337: A reference is broken.
- The definitions of “Q-linear” and “R-linear” convergence might not be universally known and could benefit from repetition for clarity.
- The residual R(X) defined in (7) could be further explained in relation to the original definition in [10] to facilitate better interpretation.
Generally, I recommend including more discussion and potentially conclusions to complement the extensive technical proof in Section 4.

Arguments for acceptance:
- The paper addresses a significant problem in optimization with direct relevance to machine learning.
- It presents a novel result—linear convergence of PGM for trace norm regularization without strong convexity—extending existing theory.
- The proof appears sound and is presented with clarity.
- The error bound established is a potentially valuable contribution in itself.

Arguments against acceptance:
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion connecting its results to the well-studied l1-norm case, potentially underestimating its novelty or overstating its surprise.
- The title is arguably not fully representative of the core contribution.
