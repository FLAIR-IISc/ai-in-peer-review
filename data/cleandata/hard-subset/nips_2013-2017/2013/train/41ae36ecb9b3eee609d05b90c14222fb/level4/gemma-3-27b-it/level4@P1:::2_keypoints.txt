- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity.  
- Paper is carefully and clearly written and the main proof appears correct.  
- Builds on the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Luo and Tseng, resolving its application to the nuclear-norm case.  
- Lacks direct comparison to the l1-norm (vector) case and related work [22,23]; should explain why l1 techniques don’t directly translate and whether l1 results follow as a corollary.  
- Title may be misleading: the key innovation is the flexibility from the linear operator A in the loss, not the “structured” regularizer.  
- Notation: clarify that P is a fixed sampling/mask operator (avoid implying randomness).  
- Line 337 contains a broken reference.  
- Suggest briefly reminding readers of Q-linear and R-linear convergence definitions.  
- Connect the residual R(X) in Eq. (7) more explicitly to the original definition in [10].  
- Expand discussion and conclusions, especially clarifying technical details of the proof of Lemma 4.1 in Section 4.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, rigorous/structured proof, practical significance, and overall clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited related-work discussion and potentially misleading title; recommend minor revisions.
