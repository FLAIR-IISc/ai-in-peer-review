- Added note: A proof of |R_k| ≤ κ |X_k − X_{k+1}| is given in Suvrit Sra, NIPS 2012 (Lemma 4).  
- Main contribution: Establishes a local error bound for trace-norm–regularized risk minimization, enabling asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient under a relaxed strong-convexity condition.  
- Approach: Follows the Luo–Tseng framework and standard local error bound proofs, with a key new observation allowing simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.  
- Technical soundness: Paper appears correct overall, but there is a gap in the appendix (line 085) — need justification that choosing κ3 (e.g., with α < 1) implies R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Clarity: Generally readable; recommend stating main claims up front and postponing the technical proof of the local error bound to improve flow.  
- Originality: Moderate — simultaneous diagonalization idea is novel and interesting, but most of the local-error-bound argument mirrors existing work.  
- Significance: Primarily theoretical with limited algorithmic impact; asymptotic nature of the linear rate and reliance on existing blueprint make the contribution incremental.
