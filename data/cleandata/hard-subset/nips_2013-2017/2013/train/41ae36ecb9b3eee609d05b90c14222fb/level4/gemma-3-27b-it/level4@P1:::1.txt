This paper proves the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method applied to trace-norm regularized learning problems when the loss function has the form f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is a linear operator and h is strongly convex on any compact set and has a Lipschitz gradient. This work extends previous results by Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], which established similar convergence guarantees for the Lasso and group Lasso problems using an “error-bound condition,” to the trace norm regularization setting. While a non-trivial extension, the primary contribution appears to be technical in nature.

The presentation of the proofs is generally clear and well-structured.

**Strengths:**

*   Establishes the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending existing results to trace norm regularization.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The contribution is primarily technical. The practical impact of this theoretical result is not immediately apparent.
*   The paper lacks a numerical example demonstrating the linear convergence rate in practice. This would significantly strengthen the validation of the theoretical findings.
*   The relationship between the derived constants and the step size parameters ᾱ and α is not sufficiently explained.

**More Detailed Comments:**

1.  The outlines of the proofs of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 4.1 closely resemble those in Tseng [20]. The authors should provide more precise references to the original work and more clearly delineate their specific contributions to better highlight the novelty of their approach.
2.  A numerical example illustrating the linear convergence behavior (e.g., a semi-log plot of the objective function value versus the number of iterations) is strongly recommended. This would provide empirical support for the theoretical results.
3.  The dependence of the constants κ1, κ2, κ3, and κ4 on the step size parameters ᾱ and α should be clarified. A discussion on how the choice of these parameters affects the convergence rate would be valuable.
4.  The sequence of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is somewhat confusing. The final inequality is justified by the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that −∇f(X̄) ∈ τ∂‖X̄‖∗, not by the intermediate inequality as stated (see p. 289 in [20]).

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid theoretical result extending linear convergence guarantees to a specific class of trace-norm regularized problems. However, the contribution is largely technical, and the lack of supporting numerical evidence weakens its impact. While the work is sound and well-written, it would benefit from a more thorough discussion of practical implications and a demonstration of the convergence behavior through numerical experiments.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a relevant problem in optimization and machine learning.
*   The theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-presented.
*   The extension of existing results to trace norm regularization is a valuable contribution, even if primarily technical.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The paper lacks practical validation through numerical experiments.
*   The contribution feels incremental and may not significantly advance the state of the art.
*   The discussion of practical implications and parameter sensitivity is limited.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend a borderline accept/reject decision. The paper is technically sound, but its lack of practical validation and incremental contribution raise concerns. I would encourage the authors to address the concerns regarding numerical experimentation and parameter sensitivity before final acceptance. Specifically, including a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but the PGM algorithm still converges linearly (for different levels of stability) would significantly strengthen the paper. A discussion of the conditions under which the theory may fail would also be beneficial. Finally, exploring the potential extension of this analysis to algorithms like ADMM would broaden the impact of the work.
