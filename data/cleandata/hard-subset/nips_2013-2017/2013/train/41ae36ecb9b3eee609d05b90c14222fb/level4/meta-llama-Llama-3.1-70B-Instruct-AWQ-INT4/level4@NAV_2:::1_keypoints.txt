- Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems when f(X)=h(A(X)), with A linear and h strongly convex on compacts and having Lipschitz gradient.  
- Extends prior error-bound based results (Tseng, Tseng & Yun, Zhang et al.) from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm; non-trivial but mainly a technical extension.  
- Main technical contribution: establishment of a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm-regularized problems.  
- Proofs and analysis are mostly clear and detailed, providing a thorough treatment of the proximal gradient method.  
- Strength: formally shows linear convergence and generalizes Tseng et al.’s results to the trace norm.  
- Weakness: contribution is largely technical and lacks new conceptual insights.  
- Weakness: no numerical example demonstrating the claimed linear convergence.  
- Minor issue: sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing and needs clarification.  
- Minor issue: unclear how constants κ1…κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ.  
- Suggested improvements: add a numerical example of linear convergence, discuss implications for ADMM, and clarify dependence of κ1…κ4 on ᾱ.  
- Arguments for acceptance: new theoretical result and clear, well-written analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: purely technical contribution and missing empirical validation.  
- Overall rating/recommendation: 7/10 — accept with minor revisions (add numerical example and clarify constants).
