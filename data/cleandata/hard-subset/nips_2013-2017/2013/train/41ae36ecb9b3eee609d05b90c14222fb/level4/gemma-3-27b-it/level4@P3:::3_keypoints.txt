- Paper studies proximal gradient method (PGM) convergence for convex smooth loss with trace norm regularization.  
- Shows linear convergence under conditions weaker than strong convexity, improving prior results.  
- Key proof ingredient is a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the trace-norm-regularized problem.  
- The error bound may be of independent interest to the community.  
- Technical development is sound and builds on established convex optimization and proximal algorithm results.  
- Core idea leverages structure of the optimal solution set and is well-motivated and executed.  
- Missing justification in the appendix (line 85) relating the residual norm to the step size must be addressed.  
- Presentation is generally clear but would benefit from reorganizing: state main results (error bound and convergence) up front and defer technical proofs.  
- Originality is moderate: useful observation on simultaneous diagonalization, but approach largely follows Luo and Tseng’s framework.  
- Significance is primarily theoretical—extends linear convergence guarantees to a broader class of losses.  
- Practical impact may be limited due to the asymptotic nature of the convergence result.  
- Author’s link to Sra et al. (NIPS’12) clarification about |Rk| ≤ κ |Xk − Xk+1|_F is helpful.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution with minor weaknesses; fixing the appendix clarification would strengthen the paper.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on authors providing a clear explanation for the appendix inequality (line 85).
