*Added after authors' feedback*
The authors are requested to provide a simulation that demonstrates linear convergence for the PG algorithm with a non-strongly convex loss, exploring various stability levels. Critically, this should be accompanied by a discussion on the conditions under which the proposed theory breaks down. Furthermore, a discussion on the potential for extending this analysis to the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) would be valuable.
***
This work establishes the linear convergence rate for the proximal gradient method in the context of trace-norm regularized learning. The analysis applies to loss functions of the form f(X)=h(A(X)), where A is a linear operator, and h possesses a Lipschitz continuous gradient and is strongly convex over any compact set.

The paper extends prior results from Tseng [20], Tseng and Yun, and Zhang et al. [22], which leveraged the "error-bound condition" to show linear convergence for lasso and group lasso problems. While adapting this framework to the trace norm is a non-trivial undertaking, the contribution appears to be primarily of a technical nature.

The proofs are generally well-presented and easy to follow.

**Strength:**
- The primary strength is extending the linear convergence analysis of Tseng et al. to the proximal gradient algorithm for trace-norm regularized problems.

**Weakness:**
- The contribution, while valid, is largely technical and lacks broader conceptual advancements.
- The paper lacks numerical experiments to empirically validate the theoretical linear convergence rate.

**More details:**
1. The proof structures for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely follow those in Tseng's work. To better delineate their novel contributions, the authors should provide more precise citations and explanations of how their proofs adapt or differ from the original.
2. The inclusion of a numerical experiment would significantly strengthen the paper. Specifically, a semilog plot illustrating the decay of the objective function value against the iteration count is needed to empirically demonstrate the claimed linear convergence.
3. The dependency of the constants κ1 through κ4 on the parameters \underbar{\alpha} and \bar{\alpha} is not clearly articulated.

**Minor issue:**
4. The derivation leading up to inequality (13) is presented in a confusing manner. The final inequality in the sequence is justified by the convexity of the trace norm and the subgradient property that -\bar{G} ∈ τ∂||X||* (as shown in [20], p. 289), rather than by the preceding intermediate inequality as stated.

This paper provides a technical extension of the "error-bound condition" framework for proving linear convergence, applying it from lasso and group lasso settings to the trace norm.
