For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a standard approach. While its sublinear global convergence rate is well-established, linear convergence is known to hold under strong convexity of the loss. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption by adopting the framework of Luo and Tseng, proving a local error bound that implies asymptotic linear convergence for the proximal gradient method in the trace norm setting. The proof of the local error bound largely follows conventional techniques, with a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices. 

Quality: 
The paper is technically sound. A minor gap remains in the appendix (line 085): please clarify why choosing kappa3 (e.g., alpha < 1) yields the inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F. 

Clarity: 
The paper is generally clear and readable. Given its technical depth, it would benefit from stating key claims upfront before detailing proofs. Specifically, after presenting the local error bound, the authors could postpone the technical proof and immediately proceed to the linear convergence result (which, while standard, is a direct consequence). 

Originality: 
The paper exhibits moderate originality. The simultaneous diagonalization observation is novel and valuable; however, the overall approach to establishing the local error bound largely follows established methods, and subsequent steps are not new. 

Significance: 
The theoretical significance is moderate, with limited practical algorithmic impact. Extending linear convergence to relaxed strong convexity is valuable, but the asymptotic nature of the result diminishes its practical weight. The matrix diagonalization technique may hold independent interest. Overall, this work incrementally advances theory by establishing asymptotic linear convergence for the proximal gradient method in trace norm regularization under relaxed strong convexity, primarily by leveraging the Luo-Tseng framework with a novel diagonalization insight.
