- Main claim: Proves asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm for trace-norm regularized risk minimization without requiring strong convexity.  
- Approach: Leverages the Luo–Tseng error bound framework by establishing a local error bound for the trace-norm problem.  
- Core technical novelty: A novel observation enables simultaneous diagonalization of relevant matrices, reducing the analysis to a vector-case problem.  
- Technical soundness: The paper appears technically correct; a reviewer concern about an appendix inequality was resolved by citing Lemma 4 from Sra (NIPS 2012).  
- Clarity: Generally well-written but dense; the reviewer recommends reordering to present the linear convergence implication immediately after the main error bound statement.  
- Originality: Moderate—key matrix diagonalization trick is new and elegant, but the overall error-bound-to-linear-convergence framework is standard.  
- Significance: Primarily a theoretical contribution; establishes an important asymptotic result but may not improve early-stage practical convergence.  
- Broader impact: The simultaneous diagonalization technique could be of independent interest and applicable to other matrix optimization problems.  
- Overall assessment: A solid, incremental theoretical contribution that extends convergence analysis for first-order methods in trace-norm settings.
