This paper addresses the problem of trace norm regularized risk minimization, for which the proximal gradient algorithm is a popular method. The global convergence rate of this algorithm is well understood, with a sublinear rate established in general and a linear rate when the loss function is strongly convex. This work relaxes the strong convexity assumption by following the framework established by Luo and Tseng. The authors prove a local error bound that enables them to show the proximal gradient algorithm converges asymptotically at a linear rate for trace norm regularization. 

The paper follows a familiar pattern in proving the local error bound, with a notable new observation that allows for the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices. 

Quality: 
The paper appears to be technically sound. However, I did identify a gap in the appendix, specifically line 085. The authors should clarify why the chosen value of kappa3 (with alpha < 1) leads to the claimed inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F.

Clarity: 
I was able to follow the paper without significant difficulty, although its technical nature may pose challenges for some readers. To improve clarity, the authors could consider presenting the main claims upfront and then delving into the details. Specifically, after stating the local error bound, they might postpone the technical proof and instead immediately establish the linear convergence rate, which is not particularly novel.

Originality: 
The originality of this paper is moderate. The observation that enables simultaneous diagonalization of matrices appears to be new and interesting. However, the authors largely follow the existing approach to establish the local error bound, and the subsequent steps do not offer significant new insights.

Significance: 
The significance of this paper is primarily theoretical, with limited impact on algorithm design. While extending the linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm to more practical scenarios (i.e., a relaxed form of strong convexity) is certainly valuable, the asymptotic nature of the proof and claim somewhat diminishes its significance. The authors' approach to diagonalizing matrices may be of independent interest. This work demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm, when applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization, converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed form of strong convexity. The authors primarily follow a well-established framework due to Luo and Tseng, with a new observation that allows for the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, effectively reducing the problem to the vector case. Overall, this work is incremental and moderately interesting from a theoretical perspective.
