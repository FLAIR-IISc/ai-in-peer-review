- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)), extending Tseng and Zhang et al.'s results.  
- Contribution is largely a technical extension rather than a new conceptual insight.  
- Proofs are generally clear but need more precise referencing.  
- Must explicitly compare to Tseng [20], clarifying specific adaptations required for the trace norm and differences in proof structure.  
- Lacks experimental validation; a numerical example (e.g., semilog plot of objective vs iterations) is strongly recommended.  
- Recommend testing non-strongly-convex loss scenarios and varying “stability” levels, and discussing cases where theory may fail.  
- The dependence of constants κ1–κ4 on the parameters (ᾱ) is unclear and needs a detailed explanation.  
- The inequality sequence before (13) is confusing; the final step follows from trace-norm convexity and -Ḡ ∈ ∂||X||_* (Tseng p289), not the intermediate inequality.  
- Suggest exploring extension of the analysis to ADMM for trace-norm problems as future work.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution but weakened by lack of experiments and limited discussion of practical implications.
