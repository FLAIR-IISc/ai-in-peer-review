- Proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm–regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity.  
- Manuscript is careful and clearly presented; core proof components appear sound.  
- Builds on the Lipschitzian error‑bound framework of Tseng et al.; extending this to the nuclear norm was an open problem.  
- Lacks an explicit comparison/analogy with the vector ℓ1-regularized case (e.g., [20,22]) and discussion of why ℓ1 arguments do not directly carry over.  
- Should point out that the ℓ1 case likely follows as a corollary of the present analysis.  
- Title suggestion: reconsider or remove the word “structured,” since the new generality arises from the linear operator A rather than extra structure in the regularizer.  
- Notation: call P a “mask” operator rather than “sampling,” because P is fixed (sampling implies randomness).  
- Minor issue: broken reference at line 337.  
- Clarify/restated definitions of Q‑ and R‑linear convergence for readers not familiar with the terms.  
- Connect the residual map R(X) in (6) to the original definition (46) in [20] to aid interpretation.  
- Recommend adding more discussion and takeaways to balance the lengthy technical proof, especially Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.  
- Update: authors addressed most questions; reviewer reiterates the title comment and affirms the result’s relevance to ML and signal processing.
