This paper studies proximal gradient methods for trace-norm–regularized risk minimization. The global sublinear convergence rate of proximal gradient is well known, as is linear convergence under strong convexity of the loss. This work relaxes the strong convexity assumption by following the Luo–Tseng framework. In particular, the authors prove a local error bound that yields asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization. The overall proof strategy follows the standard pattern for establishing local error bounds, with a new observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of the relevant matrices.

Quality:
The paper appears technically sound. My only concern was a gap in the appendix (line 085): why does the chosen κ3 (e.g., with α < 1) imply R(Xk) ≤ κ3 ||Xk − Xk+1||F? After the rebuttal, this point is addressed by Lemma 4 of Sra (NIPS’12), which explicitly establishes an inequality of the required form, i.e., |Rk| ≤ κ |Xk − Xk+1|. With this clarification, I do not see remaining technical gaps.

Clarity:
The paper is generally clear and I could follow the arguments. Given the technical density, it would help to first lay out the main claims and proof roadmap, and only then delve into details. In particular, after stating the local error bound, consider postponing its very technical proof and moving directly to the (largely standard) derivation of the linear rate. This reordering would improve readability without altering the substance.

Originality:
The originality is moderate. The key new ingredient is the observation that enables simultaneous diagonalization of the matrices, which is interesting and seems novel. Beyond this, the authors largely follow existing approaches to establish the local error bound, and the remaining steps are standard within the Luo–Tseng framework.

Significance:
The contribution is primarily theoretical, with limited immediate impact on algorithm design. Extending linear convergence of proximal gradient to settings with relaxed strong convexity is valuable, but the significance is tempered by the asymptotic nature of the result. The diagonalization technique may be of independent interest. Overall, the paper shows that proximal gradient, applied to trace-norm–regularized risk minimization, converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed strong convexity condition. While methodologically incremental, it provides a clean extension of known theory and is moderately interesting from a theoretical perspective.
