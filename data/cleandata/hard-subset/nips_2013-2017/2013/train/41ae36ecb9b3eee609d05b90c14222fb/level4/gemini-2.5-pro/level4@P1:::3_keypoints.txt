- Paper proves PGM achieves asymptotic linear convergence for trace-norm regularized risk minimization without assuming strong convexity for common losses (e.g., square, logistic).
- Analysis follows Luo–Tseng framework and hinges on establishing a local Lipschitzian error bound.
- Main technical contribution: a new local error bound for the trace-norm problem obtained via a novel simultaneous diagonalization/singular-value decomposition observation.
- The diagonalization idea reduces the matrix problem to a tractable spectral/vector-like analysis.
- The core argument (Theorem 3.1) appears technically sound, with the error bound proof as the central piece.
- A key step relates the residual ‖R(X^k)‖_F to iteration progress ‖X^k − X^{k+1}‖_F (inequality 11); a more explicit derivation or citation would strengthen the proof.
- Suggest citing/adapting Lemma 4 of S. Sra (NIPS 2012) which formally establishes a similar residual-to-step relation.
- Clarity could be improved by first showing how the error bound implies linear convergence (Theorem 3.2) before presenting the technical proof of the error bound.
- Originality is moderate: technique for the trace-norm error bound is novel, but overall approach follows established error-bound-based convergence analysis.
- Significance is theoretical: extends linear convergence guarantees to a broader, practically relevant class of problems but only guarantees local/asymptotic linear rate.
- Pros: addresses an important theoretical gap, introduces an elegant diagonalization technique, and applies to tasks like matrix completion and multi-task learning.
- Cons: largely incremental on existing frameworks, result is asymptotic/local so practical pre-asymptotic behavior remains uncharacterized, overall impact is moderate.
- Overall: a solid, incremental theoretical contribution with a neat technical insight, improving understanding of PGM for trace-norm regularization.
