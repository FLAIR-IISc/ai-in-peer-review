- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)), A linear and h strongly convex on compacts with Lipschitz gradient.
- Extends prior error-bound-based results of Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm.
- Proofs are mostly clear but the contribution is primarily technical rather than conceptual.
- Key strength: demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm for the trace-norm case, extending Tseng et al.’s results.
- Major weakness: no numerical example illustrating the claimed linear convergence.
- Recommendation: include a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but proximal gradient still converges linearly, and discuss effects of different stability levels and theory limitations.
- Recommendation: consider extending the analysis to the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM).
- Minor issue: proofs of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely resemble Tseng [20]; provide more precise attribution to original work.
- Minor issue: add a numerical semilog plot (function value vs iterations) to empirically demonstrate linear convergence.
- Minor issue: clarify how constants κ1…κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ (and related parameters).
- Minor issue: the sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing and should be rephrased for clarity.
- Conclusion: technically valuable extension to trace norm, but impact would increase with numerical examples and a deeper discussion of limitations and extensions.
