- Proves asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems min f(X)+τ‖X‖* under weak assumptions on h (Lipschitz gradient on domain, strong convexity on compacts) and arbitrary linear A.  
- Main technical contribution: a novel Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to the optimal set to the residual ‖proxτ(X−∇f(X))−X‖F.  
- Uses spectral analysis showing optimal X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors, enabling a perturbation argument to overcome nonpolyhedrality.  
- Combines the error bound with the Luo–Tseng framework to obtain linear convergence for fixed steps in (0,1/Lf).  
- Covers important ML losses (squared, logistic) and settings where f is not globally strongly convex (A may have nullspace).  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) illustrate empirical linear convergence near optimality.  
- Strength: resolves an open question by extending error-bound techniques beyond polyhedral regularizers to the trace norm.
