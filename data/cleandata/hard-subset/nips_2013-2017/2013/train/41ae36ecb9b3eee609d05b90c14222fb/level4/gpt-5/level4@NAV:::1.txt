Review — Addendum after authors’ response
*
Please add a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex yet the proximal gradient method still displays linear convergence (across varying stability levels), and explicitly delineate the regimes where the theory does not apply. Also discuss how the analysis could be extended to ADMM.
*
This paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm–regularized learning when the loss takes the form f(X) = h(A(X)), with A linear and h strongly convex on any compact set and possessing a Lipschitz-continuous gradient.

The work extends the results of Tseng [20], Tseng and Yun “A coordinate gradient descent method for nonsmooth separable minimization,” and Zhang et al. [22]—which proved analogous statements via the error-bound condition for lasso and group lasso—to the trace-norm setting. While the extension is nontrivial, the contribution appears primarily technical.

The proof presentation is largely clear.

Strength:
- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient, extending the results of Tseng et al.

Weakness:
- The novelty is mostly technical.
- A numerical illustration of linear convergence is missing.

More details:
1. The outlines of the proofs of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely parallel those in Tseng. Please cite the original arguments more precisely to clarify what is new here.
2. Please include a numerical experiment that clearly demonstrates linear convergence (e.g., a semilog plot of the objective value versus iteration count).
3. It is unclear how the constants \kappa1, …, \kappa4 depend on the chosen \underbar{\alpha} and \bar{\alpha}; please clarify this dependence.

Minor issue:
4. The chain of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is confusing. The final inequality follows from the convexity of the trace (nuclear) norm and the inclusion -\bar{G} ∈ τ ∂||X||_* (see p. 289 in [20]), not from the intermediate inequality.

Extension of prior linear-convergence results based on the error-bound condition from lasso and group lasso to the trace norm.
