This paper investigates the convergence rate of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for solving trace norm regularized problems, a common task in machine learning. Motivated by applications where strong convexity assumptions on the loss function are often not met, the authors aim to establish linear convergence without such restrictions. They achieve this by proving a new Lipschitzian error bound for the problem, leveraging a clever observation regarding the spectral structure of solutions. This allows them to apply the convergence analysis framework of Luo and Tseng to demonstrate linear asymptotic convergence of the PGM.

**Quality:** The paper appears technically sound and the arguments are well-structured. The key technical contribution, the Lipschitzian error bound, is crucial for establishing the main result. My only concern relates to the appendix, line 85, where the inequality  |Rk| <= kappa3 |Xk - X_{k+1}|_F is claimed. Clarification on why a specific choice of kappa3 (e.g., alpha < 1) leads to this inequality would strengthen the analysis.  Furthermore, it would be beneficial to explicitly acknowledge the related work "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting" by Suvrit Sra (NIPS'12) where a similar lemma (Lemma 4) is presented. 

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, though the density of technical detail can be challenging. To improve readability, consider restructuring the presentation. A clearer roadmap outlining the main results upfront, followed by a high-level overview of the proof strategy, would be beneficial. The detailed proof of the error bound, while important, could be postponed after presenting the main result of linear convergence, which builds directly upon it and relies on established techniques. This would allow readers to grasp the key contribution more readily. 

**Originality:** The originality of the work is moderate. The insight concerning the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is novel and represents a significant step towards establishing the error bound. However, the overall approach largely follows established methods for proving such bounds. The extension of linear convergence results to a broader class of loss functions is valuable but builds directly upon existing frameworks.

**Significance:** The significance of this paper lies primarily in its theoretical contribution. While the results don’t immediately translate into significant algorithmic improvements, relaxing the strong convexity assumption for linear convergence is a meaningful extension. The diagonalization technique may be of independent interest.  The demonstrated linear asymptotic convergence of the PGM for trace norm regularization under relaxed convexity conditions is a noteworthy addition to the literature. However, the asymptotic nature of the result somewhat limits its practical impact. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Establishes a linear convergence rate for the PGM under weaker assumptions than previously required.
*   Presents a novel and insightful observation regarding the spectral properties of solutions, enabling the proof of the error bound.
*   Contributes to a better understanding of the theoretical properties of proximal gradient methods.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The results are asymptotic, limiting their immediate practical impact.
*   The overall approach builds heavily on existing work, with the novelty primarily residing in the diagonalization technique.
*   The paper is quite dense and could benefit from a more accessible presentation.



**Overall Assessment:** This is a solid theoretical contribution that advances our understanding of the convergence properties of the PGM for trace norm regularization. While the results are incremental and asymptotic, the novel diagonalization technique and the relaxation of the strong convexity assumption are valuable additions to the field. I recommend acceptance, contingent on clarification regarding the claim in the appendix and acknowledgement of related work.
