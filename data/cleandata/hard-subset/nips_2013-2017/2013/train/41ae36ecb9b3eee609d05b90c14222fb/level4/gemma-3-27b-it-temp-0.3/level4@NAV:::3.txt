For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a common choice, with its sublinear global convergence and linear convergence under strong convexity being well-established. This paper investigates the convergence rate when the strong convexity assumption is relaxed, building upon the framework of Luo and Tseng. Specifically, the authors demonstrate a local error bound that implies asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace norm regularization. The proof of this local error bound largely follows standard techniques, with a key new observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.

Quality:
The paper appears technically sound. I have a specific question regarding the appendix, line 085: could the authors elaborate on why the chosen value of kappa3 (specifically, alpha < 1) leads to the stated inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F ? 

Clarity:
The paper is generally well-written and understandable, despite its technical complexity. To improve readability, I suggest presenting the main results upfront before delving into the detailed proofs. Following the statement of the local error bound, the authors might consider immediately proceeding to demonstrate the linear convergence rate – the proof of which largely builds on existing work – before presenting the detailed proof of the local error bound itself.

Originality:
The originality of this work is moderate. The novel observation regarding simultaneous matrix diagonalization is interesting and potentially valuable. However, the remainder of the approach to establishing the local error bound closely follows existing methods, and subsequent steps are not novel.

Significance:
The significance of this paper is primarily theoretical, with limited direct implications for algorithm design. Extending the linear convergence rate to scenarios with relaxed strong convexity is a positive contribution, but the asymptotic nature of the result diminishes its practical impact. The matrix diagonalization technique may be of independent interest. In summary, this work establishes the asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm for trace norm regularized risk minimization under a weaker convexity condition, largely by adapting the Luo and Tseng framework and leveraging a new diagonalization observation to reduce the problem to a vector-valued case. Overall, this is an incremental contribution with moderate theoretical interest.
