- Paper studies proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized minimization and proves linear convergence when f(X)=h(A(X)) is strongly convex on any compact set and has Lipschitz continuous gradient.
- Extends the "error-bound condition" analytical framework (Tseng [20], Zhang et al. [22]) to the trace-norm regularization setting.
- Strength: Non-trivial extension showing linear convergence for trace-norm problems; proofs are generally clear and logically structured.
- Weakness: Contribution is mainly technical and incremental, largely adapting existing analysis without deep new conceptual insights.
- Weakness: Paper lacks empirical validation to support the theoretical linear convergence claims.
- Suggestion: More clearly distinguish novelty from Tseng [20] by detailing unique technical challenges posed by the trace norm and the new steps used to overcome them.
- Suggestion: Include numerical experiments (e.g., semilog objective vs iterations), test cases without global strong convexity, vary problem stability, and discuss scenarios where theory may fail.
- Suggestion: Explicitly clarify how constants κ1…κ4 depend on sublevel set parameters α_underline and α_bar.
- Suggestion: Discuss potential extensions of the analytical approach to other algorithms like ADMM.
- Minor issue: Correct the confusing inequality sequence before (13); the final step follows from trace-norm convexity and the subgradient optimality condition (cf. Tseng [20]).
