- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity, relevant for ML and signal processing.
- Paper is carefully and clearly written and follows Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique; main proof appears correct.
- Missing clear comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case; authors should explain relation and whether l1 techniques translate to trace norm.
- Note that the l1-norm result should follow as a corollary of the trace-norm analysis.
- Contribution may be unsurprising to some but is still a valuable extension of known results.
- Suggest changing the title: “structured” is unnecessary since the main novelty concerns the loss (linear operator A), not the regularizer.
- Minor issues: rename P to avoid “sampling” connotations, fix broken reference l337, clarify Q-/R-linear convergence terminology, and relate residual R(X) in (6) to Tseng’s original definition.
- Recommend adding more discussion and conclusions to justify the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2.
- Reviewer thanks authors for addressing questions and reiterates reservation about the word “structured” in the title.
