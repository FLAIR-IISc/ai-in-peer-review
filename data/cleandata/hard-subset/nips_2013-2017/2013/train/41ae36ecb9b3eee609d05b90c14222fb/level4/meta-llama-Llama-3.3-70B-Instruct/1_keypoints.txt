- Manuscript proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear and h strongly convex on compacts and Lipschitz-gradient.
- Extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] results from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm; extension is non-trivial but mainly technical.
- Proofs are generally clear and well-structured.
- Strength: successfully extends linear convergence results to trace-norm regularization.
- Weakness: contribution does not introduce fundamentally new concepts.
- Weakness: lacks a numerical example illustrating linear convergence (preferably a semilog plot of function value vs iterations).
- More detail: proofs of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 strongly resemble Tseng’s work; authors should give more precise references.
- More detail: dependence of constants κ1,...,κ4 on the choice of α and α is not clearly explained.
- Minor issue: sequence of inequalities before (13) is confusing; the final inequality holds due to trace-norm convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||*, not the intermediate inequality (see p.289 of [20]).
- Overall: a technical extension of error-bound-based linear convergence from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm.
