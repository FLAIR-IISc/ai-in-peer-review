- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity.  
- Analysis applies to losses f(X)=h(A(X)) with h meeting certain smoothness and convexity conditions, covering common ML losses.  
- Follows Luo and Tseng’s framework of establishing linear rates via error bounds.  
- Main technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization.  
- Key novel insight (Proposition 4.2): optimal solutions and their gradients share the same singular vectors, enabling simultaneous diagonalization.  
- Theoretical development appears technically sound but requires clarification on a crucial inequality.  
- Incomplete justification for inequality (11): the derivation of R(Xk) ≤ κ3‖Xk - Xk+1‖F and the choice of κ3 (with α < 1) is not fully explained.  
- Appendix line 085 does not sufficiently verify that the step-size parameters guarantee the κ3 relationship.  
- The connection to Sra’s NIPS 2012 work should be acknowledged in the main text for proper context.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized overall but presents Theorem 3.2 before the key error bound (Theorem 3.1), disrupting narrative flow.  
- Suggest moving dense technical details (error bound proof) to supplementary material for readability.  
- Originality: simultaneous diagonalization insight is novel and is the main technical advance despite using an established framework.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of PGM for trace-norm regularization but convergence is asymptotic, limiting immediate practical impact.  
- Pros: proves linear convergence without strong convexity; introduces novel simultaneous diagonalization idea; valuable theoretical contribution.  
- Cons: convergence is asymptotic; incomplete justification for κ3; limited practical implications; relies on a well-known analytical framework.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — solid theoretical contribution to ML optimization with a novel technical insight.
