- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker conditions than strong convexity, filling an important gap.
- Resolves an open question by proving a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization previously unresolved compared to the ℓ1 case.
- Technically sound, well-structured, and clearly written; main contribution is the new Lipschitzian error bound that enables linear convergence without strong convexity.
- Proof technique builds on and extends the Luo and Tseng framework previously applied to ℓ1 regularization; the technical contribution appears correct and well-motivated.
- Lacks a clear comparison to the vector ℓ1-norm case (e.g., [20,22]); should explain why ℓ1 techniques do not directly transfer to the trace-norm setting.
- Title may be misleading; primary novelty concerns flexibility of the loss via linear operator A rather than making the trace norm regularizer “structured.”
- Minor fixes needed: call P a “mask operator” (Section 5), repair broken reference l337, and briefly restate definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence.
- Improve interpretation by better connecting the residual map R(X) (eq.7) to optimality condition (4) and the definition in [20].
- Add a short overview before Lemma 4.1 to help readers navigate the complex technical proof.
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with practical importance for low-rank problems; accept with minor revisions.
