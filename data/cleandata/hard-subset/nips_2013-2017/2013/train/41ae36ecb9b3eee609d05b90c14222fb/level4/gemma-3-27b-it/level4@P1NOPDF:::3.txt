For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a popular choice. Its sublinear global convergence rate is well-established, and it’s known to achieve a linear rate when the loss function is strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption, building on the framework developed by Luo and Tseng. Specifically, the authors prove a local error bound which they use to demonstrate that the proximal gradient algorithm converges asymptotically at a linear rate for trace norm regularization. The paper largely follows a standard approach for proving such local error bounds, with a key new observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.

Quality:
This paper appears technically sound. I have a single question regarding the appendix, line 85. Could the authors please explain why a choice of kappa3 (e.g., alpha < 1) leads to the claimed inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F?

Clarity:
I was able to follow the paper without significant difficulty. However, given the technical nature of the work, it might be helpful to first clearly state the main claims before delving into the detailed proofs. In particular, after presenting the local error bound, the authors could postpone the highly technical proof of the bound itself and instead immediately proceed to demonstrate the linear convergence rate – which, incidentally, doesn’t introduce substantial novelty.

Originality:
The originality of this paper is moderate. The observation allowing simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is new and interesting. Beyond this, the authors largely follow existing approaches to establish the local error bound, and the remaining steps are not novel. 

Significance:
The significance of this paper is primarily theoretical, with limited direct impact on algorithm design. While extending the linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm to more practical scenarios—specifically, by relaxing the strong convexity assumption—is valuable, the asymptotic nature of the proof (and claim) somewhat diminishes its impact. The authors’ method for handling matrix diagonalization may be of independent interest. In summary, this work demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed form of strong convexity. The authors primarily follow a well-established framework from Luo and Tseng, with a novel observation allowing simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, effectively reducing the problem to the vector case. Overall, this work is incremental and moderately interesting from a theoretical perspective.
