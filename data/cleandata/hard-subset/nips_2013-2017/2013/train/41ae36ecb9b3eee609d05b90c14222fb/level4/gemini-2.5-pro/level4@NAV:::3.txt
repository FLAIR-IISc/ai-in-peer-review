For the problem of trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a widely used method. The algorithm's sublinear global rate of convergence is well-established, as is its linear convergence rate when the loss function is also strongly convex. This paper extends this analysis by relaxing the strong convexity assumption, adopting the framework introduced by Luo and Tseng. Specifically, the authors establish a local error bound, which they then use to prove that the proximal gradient method converges asymptotically at a linear rate for this class of problems. The derivation of the local error bound largely follows a conventional pattern, distinguished by a new insight that enables the simultaneous diagonalization of the involved matrices.

**Quality:**
The paper's technical contributions appear to be sound. My primary question pertained to a detail in the appendix, specifically on line 085. A clarification is requested on why the selected `kappa3` (where `alpha < 1`) ensures that the inequality `R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F` holds.

**Clarity:**
The manuscript was comprehensible without major issues. However, given the technical depth, the paper's structure could be improved by first presenting the main theoretical claims and then proceeding to their detailed proofs. For example, after stating the local error bound, the authors might consider immediately showing how it leads to the linear convergence rate (a fairly standard derivation) and deferring the highly technical proof of the error bound itself.

**Originality:**
The originality of this work is moderate. The observation enabling the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is novel and interesting. Aside from this contribution, the paper's approach to establishing the local error bound and the subsequent steps in the analysis are largely based on existing methods and are not new.

**Significance:**
The significance of this work is primarily theoretical, with minimal direct impact on algorithm design. While proving the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm in more practical scenarios (i.e., under relaxed strong convexity) is a valuable contribution, its importance is tempered by the asymptotic nature of the claim. The matrix diagonalization technique, however, may be of independent interest. In summary, this work demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm, applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization, attains an asymptotic linear convergence rate under a relaxed strong convexity condition. The authors follow a well-known framework from Luo and Tseng, introducing a new observation for simultaneous matrix diagonalization that helps reduce the problem to the vector case. Overall, the work is an incremental and moderately interesting theoretical result.
