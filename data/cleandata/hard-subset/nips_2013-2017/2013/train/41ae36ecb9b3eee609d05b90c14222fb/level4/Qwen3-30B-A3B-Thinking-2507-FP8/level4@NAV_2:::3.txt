

The paper investigates the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized optimization problems. While the sublinear convergence rate of PGM is well-established for such problems, and linear convergence is known under strong convexity assumptions, this work extends the result to a broader class of loss functions without requiring strong convexity. The authors establish a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems, which enables them to prove asymptotic linear convergence using the framework developed by Luo and Tseng. The key technical contribution is a new observation regarding the simultaneous diagonalization of certain matrices, which allows them to adapt existing error bound techniques to the trace norm setting.

Quality: The paper appears technically sound overall. The only concern is in the appendix (line 085), where the choice of kappa3 (specifically, the condition alpha < 1) requires further explanation to justify the claimed inequality R(X^k) ≤ kappa3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.

Clarity: The paper is generally well-written and follows a logical structure. However, the presentation could be improved by stating the main results upfront before delving into technical details. The proof of the local error bound, while necessary, could be appropriately delayed to allow readers to first understand how this bound leads to the linear convergence result, which is the paper's main contribution.

Originality: The originality of this work is moderate. The novel observation enabling the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is interesting and contributes to the paper's main technical insight. However, the overall approach to establishing the error bound largely follows existing methodologies for composite optimization problems, with the trace norm case requiring specific adaptations due to its non-polyhedral epigraph.

Significance: The significance of this paper is primarily theoretical, as it extends the linear convergence guarantee for PGM to a broader class of loss functions commonly used in machine learning applications. While this extension is valuable for understanding the behavior of PGM in practical settings, the asymptotic nature of the convergence result somewhat limits its immediate practical impact. The matrix diagonalization technique developed may be of independent interest for future work on similar optimization problems. Overall, this work makes a modest but meaningful theoretical contribution to the understanding of first-order methods for trace norm regularization. 

Note: The proof for |Rk| ≤ kappa |Xk - X_{k+1}| has been explicitly established in "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting" by Suvrit Sra (NIPS'12, Lemma 4).
