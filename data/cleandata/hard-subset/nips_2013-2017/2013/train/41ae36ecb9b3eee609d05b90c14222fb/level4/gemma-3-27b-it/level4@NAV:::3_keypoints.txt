- Main result: Shows the proximal gradient algorithm converges linearly (asymptotically) for trace-norm regularized risk minimization under a relaxed strong-convexity assumption using the Luo–Tseng framework.  
- Technical approach: Proves a local error bound largely via standard techniques, augmented by a novel observation that allows simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.  
- Novelty: The simultaneous matrix diagonalization trick is a new and interesting contribution; most other steps follow existing methods.  
- Quality/technical soundness: Paper appears technically sound overall, but the reviewer requests clarification on appendix line 085 about the choice of kappa3 (e.g., alpha < 1) yielding R(X^k) <= kappa3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written; suggests presenting main results first and postponing the local error bound proof to improve readability.  
- Significance: The contribution is primarily theoretical and incremental—valuable for extending linear-convergence guarantees but of limited algorithmic impact due to the asymptotic nature.  
- Reduction to vector case: The diagonalization observation effectively reduces the matrix problem to the vector case, enabling known analysis to apply.  
- Rebuttal point: Authors cite Sra NIPS'12 (Lemma 4) proving |R_k| <= kappa ||X_k - X_{k+1}||, which the reviewer notes as relevant.
