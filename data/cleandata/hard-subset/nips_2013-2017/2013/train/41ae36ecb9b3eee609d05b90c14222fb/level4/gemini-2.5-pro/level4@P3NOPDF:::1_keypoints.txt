- Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)), where h is locally strongly convex on compacts and has Lipschitz gradient.
- Central contribution: extends error-bound based linear convergence analysis from vector (lasso) to matrix (trace-norm) regularization.
- Strength: non-trivial theoretical result showing linear rates without requiring global strong convexity; proofs are generally clear and logical.
- Weakness: mainly a technical extension of existing framework—limited apparent novelty unless trace-norm-specific implications are highlighted.
- Weakness: no empirical validation—paper lacks numerical experiments illustrating the proven convergence rates.
- Request to clarify novelty: explicitly compare proofs to Tseng [20] and pinpoint the novel arguments required for the trace norm.
- Request to add a numerical example (e.g., semi-log plot of objective or distance vs iterations) to empirically demonstrate linear convergence.
- Request to clarify dependence of constants κ1…κ4 on the local strong-convexity parameters \underline{α} and \bar{α}.
- Minor correction: the derivation before inequality (13) is confusing—final step should follow from trace-norm convexity and subgradient optimality (revise and reference Tseng [20]).
- Suggestion: include simulations under weaker (non-globally-strongly-convex) conditions to show practical behavior and boundaries of the theory.
- Suggestion: discuss potential extensions of the error-bound analysis to other algorithms (e.g., ADMM) for trace-norm regularized problems.
