- Problem & result: Studies proximal-gradient for trace-norm regularized risk; proves a local error bound leading to asymptotic linear convergence without full strong convexity.  
- Technical novelty: Key observation enables simultaneous diagonalization of certain matrices, reducing parts of the matrix argument to the vector case.  
- Soundness: Analysis appears technically correct and follows the standard blueprint of deriving linear rates from a local error bound plus proximal-gradient properties.  
- Appendix issue: The reviewer’s earlier question about why a specific κ3 implies R(X^k) ≤ κ3||X^k−X^{k+1}||_F is resolved by Sra (NIPS’12); cite that lemma and state its assumptions.  
- Clarity—structure: Recommend stating main claims, assumptions, and constants upfront and deferring long technical proofs to improve flow.  
- Clarity—presentation: After the local error bound, show how it yields linear convergence before presenting the technical proof; briefly summarize the diagonalization fact before details.  
- Notation: Standardize and define the residual R(X^k) at first use and explain its role in the Luo–Tseng framework.  
- Originality: Moderate—simultaneous diagonalization is a neat, likely novel simplification; rest of the approach follows established methods.  
- Significance: Theoretical contribution extending linear rates under relaxed convexity is valuable, but impact is limited by asymptotic nature and lack of new algorithms or acceleration.  
- Suggested fixes: Clearly state neighborhood and dependence of constants for the local error bound; cite and summarize Sra (NIPS’12, Lemma 4); reorganize presentation; consider a small empirical illustration.  
- Overall: A solid incremental theoretical paper—technically sound and of moderate interest to theory audiences, with improvements in exposition and citation suggested.
