## Review of NIPS 2013 Submission: [Paper Title]

This paper presents a significant result: the proof of linear convergence for the standard proximal gradient method applied to trace norm regularized problems, under notably weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function. This is a crucial advancement for applications in machine learning and signal processing where trace norm regularization is frequently employed, particularly in low-rank matrix recovery. The paper is generally well-written and clearly structured, building logically upon the Lipschitzian error bound technique established by Tseng [20]. Based on my understanding, the core of the proof appears sound.

However, the paper would benefit significantly from a more detailed discussion contextualizing these results within the existing literature on l1-norm regularization. While the authors successfully extend the linear convergence analysis to the trace norm setting, a stronger connection to the analogous vector-valued case (as explored in [20, 22] and related works) is currently missing. Specifically, elaborating on *why* techniques readily applicable to l1 regularization do not directly translate to the trace norm case would substantially enhance the paper’s impact and provide valuable insight for researchers. Ideally, the authors could demonstrate how the presented trace norm result generalizes to the l1-norm case as a direct corollary, solidifying the relationship between the two. 

The perceived novelty of the result may be a point of contention for some reviewers. While I believe this is a valuable contribution, the lack of explicit comparison to the l1 case might lead some to view the extension as less surprising. Addressing this through the aforementioned discussion would proactively mitigate this concern and highlight the nuances and challenges inherent in extending the analysis to matrix norms.

Furthermore, I suggest reconsidering the paper’s title. The primary contribution lies in relaxing the assumptions on the *loss function*, rather than in characterizing the trace norm regularization itself as “structured”. The flexibility gained stems from the properties of the linear operator A within the loss function, not inherent properties of the trace norm regularizer. A title reflecting this focus would more accurately represent the paper’s core achievement.

Finally, there are a few minor points that could improve clarity:

* **Notation:** The operator *P* is currently described without a precise characterization. While "sampling operator" might suggest randomness, *P* appears to be a fixed operator. A more neutral term, such as “mask operator” or simply “projection operator,” may be more appropriate.
* **Reference:** Line 337 contains a broken reference that should be corrected.
* **Convergence Rates:** The definitions of “Q-linear convergence” and “R-linear convergence” are not universally known. Briefly restating these definitions would improve accessibility.
* **Residual Term:** The residual *R(X)* defined in equation (6) could benefit from a more explicit connection to the original definition in Tseng [20] (equation (46)). This would aid in interpreting its role within the convergence analysis.
* **Lemma 3.2 Discussion:** The lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 would be strengthened by a more substantial discussion of its implications and key insights, potentially integrated as concluding remarks following the proof. This would provide the reader with a clearer understanding of the lemma’s contribution to the overall result.

**Update after Author Feedback:**

I appreciate the authors' responses to my initial questions. Regarding the title, my comment concerned the use of the term "structured," which I believe is unnecessary in this context. However, I acknowledge that the final decision regarding the title rests with the authors.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite these suggestions, this is a strong paper with a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance, contingent upon addressing the concerns regarding the connection to l1-norm regularization and incorporating the minor improvements outlined above.
