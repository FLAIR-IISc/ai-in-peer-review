- Proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss.  
- Addresses a crucial problem for many ML and signal-processing applications.  
- Paper is carefully and clearly written and follows Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique.  
- Fills an open question by applying Tseng’s technique to the nuclear (trace) norm case; main proof appears correct.  
- Reviewer requests a clear comparison/analogy to the vector l1-norm case and explanation why l1 techniques do not directly translate to trace-norm.  
- Suggests that such a comparison would better highlight the novelty, since extension from l1 to trace norm may seem unsurprising to some readers.  
- Recommends a slight change to the title: the main novelty concerns flexibility in the loss, not making the trace norm “structured.”  
- Minor notation issue: avoid calling P a “sampling” operator if P is fixed; use a more specific term (e.g., mask or selection).  
- Broken reference at line 337 needs fixing.  
- Request to repeat/clarify meanings of “Q-” and “R-” linear convergence for readers unfamiliar with the terms.  
- Ask to explain the residual R(X) in (6) in relation to Tseng’s original definition for better interpretation.  
- Suggests adding more discussion and conclusions to justify the long technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.  
- Notes that authors’ responses addressed most questions; reviewer still finds “structured” in the title unnecessary but leaves final decision to authors.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution that can be strengthened with minor revisions.
