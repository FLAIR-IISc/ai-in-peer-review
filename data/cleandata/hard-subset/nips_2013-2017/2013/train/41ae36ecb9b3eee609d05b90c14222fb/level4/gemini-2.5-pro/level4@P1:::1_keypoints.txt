- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss.  
- Technical novelty: derives a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm objectives, extending error-bound analysis from ℓ1/group lasso to the non-polyhedral, non-separable trace norm.  
- Relevance: result applies to important ML settings (e.g., matrix completion, multi-task learning) where loss functions often lack strong convexity.  
- Presentation: proofs are generally clear and the work is technically sound and complete.  
- Strength: first demonstration that PGM can attain linear rates under trace-norm regularization absent strong convexity.  
- Strength: the new error bound is a significant technical contribution likely of independent interest to optimization researchers.  
- Weakness — positioning/originality: the analysis largely follows the Luo & Tseng template; the paper should more explicitly state where and how it departs from prior work.  
- Weakness — clarity of analysis: in Theorem 3.2 the dependence of constants κ1–κ4 on step-size bounds is not specified and should be clarified in the main text.  
- Weakness — clarity of analysis: the derivation of inequality (18) is terse; a clearer justification (e.g., via the subgradient inequality for the trace norm) would help readability.  
- Weakness — experimental validation: experiments validate theory but should more explicitly tie setups (e.g., operators with nontrivial nullspace) to theoretical conditions.  
- Weakness — experimental validation: discuss practical boundaries of the theory and provide or analyze "failure cases" where the error bound is weak and convergence may be sublinear.  
- Suggestion — future work: be more specific about extensions (e.g., other matrix norms) and discuss applying the error-bound analysis to other algorithms like ADMM.
