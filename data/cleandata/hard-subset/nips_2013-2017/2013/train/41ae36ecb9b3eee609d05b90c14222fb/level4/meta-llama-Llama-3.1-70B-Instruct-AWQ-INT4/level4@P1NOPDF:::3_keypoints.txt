- Post-rebuttal note: the bound |R_k| <= κ |X_k - X_{k+1}| is already established in Suvrit Sra, "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting" (NIPS'12, Lemma 4).
- Problem addressed: trace-norm regularized risk minimization using the proximal gradient algorithm.
- Context: proximal gradient has known sublinear global rates and linear rates under strong convexity; this work relaxes strong convexity.
- Approach: follows Luo and Tseng’s framework to prove a local error bound that yields asymptotic linear convergence.
- Technical novelty: a new observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices to reduce the problem to the vector case.
- Main result: proximal gradient converges asymptotically at a linear rate for trace-norm regularization under the relaxed condition.
- Quality: paper appears technically sound overall but contains a gap in the appendix (line 085) regarding the choice of κ3 and the inequality R(X^k) <= κ3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.
- Clarity: generally readable though technical; reviewer suggests stating main claims up front and postponing technical proofs for clarity.
- Originality: moderate — simultaneous diagonalization is interesting and new, but the rest largely follows established methods.
- Significance: primarily theoretical with limited impact on algorithm design; asymptotic nature of the result reduces practical significance.
- Overall assessment: incremental contribution that is moderately interesting from a theoretical perspective.
