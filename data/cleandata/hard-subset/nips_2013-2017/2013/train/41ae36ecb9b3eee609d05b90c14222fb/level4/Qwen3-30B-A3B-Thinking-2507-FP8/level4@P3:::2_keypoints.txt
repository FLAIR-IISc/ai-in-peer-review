1. Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions, not requiring strong convexity, for losses of the form f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strictly convex and smooth.

2. Resolves an open question about establishing linear convergence for trace-norm regularization without strong convexity, relevant to matrix completion and multi-task learning.

3. Main technical contribution: a novel Lipschitzian error bound tailored to trace-norm regularized problems.

4. Introduces a matrix-perturbation approach leveraging alignment of singular vectors between optimal solutions and gradients to handle the non-polyhedral trace-norm epigraph.

5. Explicitly contrasts with ℓ1-norm error bounds, noting prior vector techniques (variable splitting) do not extend to the trace-norm case due to structural differences.

6. Provides rigorous theoretical analysis: proves the error bound (Theorem 3.1), a key lemma on the optimal solution set (Lemma 4.1), and applies Luo & Tseng’s framework to obtain linear convergence (Theorem 3.2).

7. Empirical results on matrix completion and classification support the theory, showing linear convergence when iterates are near the optimum.

8. Strength: addresses an important open problem with a novel, well-motivated technical tool and clear, rigorous exposition.

9. Strength: numerical experiments effectively complement theoretical claims.

10. Weakness: would benefit from a more detailed, explicit comparison to the vector (ℓ1) case explaining why variable-splitting fails and how the new approach overcomes it.

11. Weakness: title could be refined to emphasize that the novelty hinges on the loss structure f(X)=h(A(X)) rather than on the trace norm alone.

12. Weakness: some technical points need clarification for accessibility (define Q- vs R-linear convergence and more explicitly link the residual map R(X) to optimality conditions).

13. Overall assessment: significant, high-quality contribution with minor addressable weaknesses; recommendation: accept.
