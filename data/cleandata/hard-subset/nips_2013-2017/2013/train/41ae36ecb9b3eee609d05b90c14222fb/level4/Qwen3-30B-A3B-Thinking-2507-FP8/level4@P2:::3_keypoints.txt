- Problem: Analyzes convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm-regularized optimization.  
- Main result: Shows PGM converges linearly for a broad class of losses (e.g., square, logistic) without requiring strong convexity.  
- Technical contribution: Proves a Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization via the observation that optimal solutions and gradients share singular vectors, enabling simultaneous diagonalization.  
- Methodology: Uses standard Luo–Tseng framework to translate the error bound into linear convergence.  
- Novelty: The singular-vector observation and the resulting treatment of the non-polyhedral trace norm are new compared to existing ℓ₁-norm results.  
- Quality: Paper is technically sound and well-structured; proofs are rigorous overall.  
- Proof gap: Appendix requires clarification on the choice of kappa3 in the inequality R(X^k) ≤ kappa3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F (why alpha < 1 suffices).  
- Experiments: Numerical results on matrix completion and classification corroborate the theoretical linear convergence.  
- Clarity: Clearly written with logical flow; recommend stating main contributions (error bound and convergence) earlier for reader guidance.  
- Originality assessment: Moderate—core technical insight is novel, but overall approach follows established frameworks.  
- Significance: Theoretical advance extending linear-convergence guarantees to non-strongly-convex losses; significance is mainly theoretical since the guarantee is asymptotic.  
- Related work note: Should acknowledge connection to Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4) where a similar inequality was established in a more general setting.  
- Overall verdict: Solid, meaningful theoretical contribution to optimization for low-rank matrix recovery, incremental but valuable.
