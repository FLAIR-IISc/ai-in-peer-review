This paper establishes linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, a significant result given the widespread applications of this technique in machine learning and signal processing, and importantly, under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss function. The manuscript is well-written and clear, building upon the Lipschitzian error bound technique introduced by Tseng [20]. Applying this technique to the nuclear norm case represents a natural and important extension, previously left open. The core proof appears sound to the best of my understanding.

However, the paper would benefit from a more explicit discussion of its connection to the well-established vector case of l1-norm regularization, referencing works such as [20,22] and related literature. A clear articulation of how, or why, techniques successful for the l1-case do not directly generalize to the trace norm setting would strengthen the contribution. Furthermore, highlighting that the l1-norm result can be derived as a corollary from the current trace norm results would be valuable.  The perceived novelty of the work, and thus its potential impact, is partially dependent on this comparison, as some readers may anticipate a parallel between the two cases. Despite this, I still consider the contribution a valuable one. 

I also suggest reconsidering the paper’s title, as the primary advancement concerns the flexibility gained in the loss function rather than a fundamental restructuring of the trace norm regularization itself. The benefit of the linear operator A stems from its impact on the loss term, not the regularizer.

Several minor points warrant attention: 
- The operator P is described; clarifying whether it’s a sampling or mask operator would be helpful, noting that “sampling” could imply randomness which may not be accurate given its fixed nature.
- There is a broken reference on line 337.
- The definitions of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence could be briefly reiterated for broader accessibility.
- Expanding on the relationship between the residual R(X) defined in equation (6) and the original definition in [20] (equation 46) would improve interpretability.
- Finally, expanding the discussion and potentially including concluding remarks following the technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 would be beneficial.
