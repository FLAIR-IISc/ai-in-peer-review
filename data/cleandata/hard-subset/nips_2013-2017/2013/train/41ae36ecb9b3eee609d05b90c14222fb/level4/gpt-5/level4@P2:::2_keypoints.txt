- Main contribution: Proves PGM attains linear convergence for trace-norm–regularized problems without assuming strong convexity of the loss.
- Technical novelty: Introduces a new Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to the optimal set to the residual norm ‖Sτ(X − ∇f(X)) − X‖F.
- Model/assumptions: Loss f(X)=h(A(X)) with A linear, h strictly convex with Lipschitz gradient and localized curvature on compact sets; standard step-size conditions used.
- Key proof idea: Uses singular-vector alignment of optimal solutions and matrix-perturbation arguments to control local spectral behavior and derive the error bound.
- Convergence results: R-linear convergence of iterates and Q-linear convergence of objective values via the Luo–Tseng framework.
- Scope: Covers common losses (squared, logistic) and applications like matrix completion, multi-task/multilabel learning, and matrix classification.
- Quality: Main arguments appear sound; localized strong convexity and restricted invertibility are carefully justified.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written; residual map, fixed-point role, and technical proofs (simultaneous SVD) are readable.
- Originality: Novel result—linear rates for nuclear-norm regularization without strong convexity—and a new error bound addressing non-polyhedral geometry.
- Significance: Practically relevant for large-scale matrix problems lacking strong convexity and explains observed fast local PGM convergence.
- Strength: Resolves an open problem by proving a trace-norm Lipschitzian error bound.
- Strength: Provides linear convergence of PGM under broadly applicable, verifiable assumptions with explicit constants.
- Strength: Clear link to Luo–Tseng; empirical results corroborate the theoretical linear rates.
- Weakness: Needs clearer comparison to the ℓ1 literature—explicitly state which ℓ1 arguments fail and how spectral arguments overcome them.
- Weakness/suggestion: Clarify whether the ℓ1 error bound is recoverable as a corollary (e.g., vectors as 1×n matrices).
- Weakness/suggestion: Consider removing or justifying the word “structured” in the title to avoid misleading readers about the regularizer.
- Weakness/suggestion: Add an earlier, brief reminder of Q- vs R-linear convergence and a short discussion summarizing the proof’s key ideas.
- Minor comments: Use “mask” or “projection” instead of “sampling” if P is fixed; fix a broken reference (~line 337); explicitly relate R(X) to Luo–Tseng’s residual and check equation numbering.
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a new technical tool and solid theory backed by experiments.
- Cons for acceptance: Limited discussion of relation to ℓ1 techniques could leave the novelty seeming incremental to some readers.
- Final note: After author feedback, reviewer stands by recommendation—result is convincing and valuable, with only minor title wording left to authors’ discretion.
