- Main claim: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems with f(X)=h(A(X)), where A is linear and h is strongly convex on compacts with Lipschitz gradient.  
- Novelty: extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] “error-bound” framework from Lasso/group Lasso to the trace-norm setting, but is largely incremental.  
- Proof clarity: proofs are reasonably clear but closely mirror Tseng’s arguments.  
- Strength: successfully demonstrates linear convergence and generalizes prior results to trace-norm problems.  
- Weakness: contribution is mainly technical rather than conceptual.  
- Empirical gap: no numerical illustration provided to verify the established linear convergence.  
- Reference/novelty request: authors should explicitly cite Tseng [20] where proofs mirror prior work and better delineate novel aspects.  
- Experiment request: include a semilog plot/numerical experiment showing function value vs iteration, including cases without strong convexity and varying stability.  
- Constants clarification: dependence of constants κ1–κ4 on parameter α is unclear and needs explanation.  
- Minor issue: sequence of inequalities before (13) is convoluted; the final step depends on trace-norm convexity and subgradient inclusion (-G ∈ τ∂||X||_*), as in Tseng [20].  
- Extension request: discuss potential extension of the analysis to ADMM and limitations/conditions under which the theory may fail to predict observed behavior.
