- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for a broad class of trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss.  
- Technical novelty: Leverages an observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices to extend existing analyses.  
- Relation to prior work: Approach builds largely on Luo and Tseng’s framework; innovation is moderate refinement rather than a wholly new technique.  
- Technical quality: Rigorous, thorough analysis with clear, well-presented proofs and attention to edge cases and counterexamples.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a helpful introduction and background.  
- Significance: The result is mainly theoretical, expanding applicability of proximal gradient methods but offering limited immediate impact on algorithm design.  
- Practical impact: Little direct algorithmic innovation or immediate practical consequences reported.  
- Arguments for acceptance: New significant convergence result, rigorous analysis, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Moderate originality due to reliance on prior work and mainly theoretical significance.  
- Recommendation and score: Recommend accept with revisions for clarity/presentation; overall rating 7/10.
