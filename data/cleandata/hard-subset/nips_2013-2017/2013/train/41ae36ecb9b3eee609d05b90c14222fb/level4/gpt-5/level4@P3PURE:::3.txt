Added after authors’ rebuttal:
The inequality |R_k| ≤ κ |X_k − X_{k+1}| is explicitly established in “Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting” (Suvrit Sra, NIPS 2012, Lemma 4).

This paper studies trace-norm-regularized risk minimization via the proximal gradient algorithm. The global sublinear convergence rate of proximal gradient is well known, and linear convergence is known under strong convexity of the loss. Following the framework of Luo and Tseng, the paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption by proving a local error bound, which in turn yields asymptotic linear convergence for trace-norm regularization. The proof largely follows standard steps for establishing a local error bound, with a new observation that enables simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.

Quality:
The paper appears technically sound. The only gap I had in mind is in the appendix, line 085. Please explain why the proposed choice of κ3 (say α < 1) leads to the claimed inequality R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F.

Clarity:
The paper is generally readable, though the technical density suggests reorganizing the presentation. It would help to lay out the main claims first and then develop the details step by step. In particular, after stating the local error bound, consider postponing its highly technical proof and first deriving the linear rate (which, by the way, contains little that is new).

Originality:
The overall originality is moderate. The observation that allows simultaneous diagonalization of matrices appears new and interesting; beyond that, the paper largely follows existing approaches to establish the local error bound. The remaining steps are not new.

Significance:
The contribution is primarily theoretical, with limited implications for algorithm design. While extending linear convergence of proximal gradient to a more practical setting (i.e., a relaxed form of strong convexity) is valuable, the impact is tempered by the asymptotic nature of the result. The diagonalization technique may be of independent interest. In sum, the paper shows that proximal gradient for trace-norm-regularized risk minimization converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed strong-convexity condition, mostly following the Luo–Tseng framework and leveraging a new simultaneous-diagonalization observation to effectively reduce to the vector case. Overall, the contribution is incremental and moderately interesting from a theoretical standpoint.
