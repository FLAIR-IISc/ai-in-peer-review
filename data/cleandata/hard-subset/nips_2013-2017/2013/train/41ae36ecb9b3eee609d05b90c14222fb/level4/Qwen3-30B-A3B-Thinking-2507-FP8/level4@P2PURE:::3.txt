For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a standard approach. While its sublinear global convergence rate is well-established, linear convergence is known to hold when the loss is strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption by adopting the framework of Luo and Tseng. Specifically, the authors establish a local error bound to demonstrate that the proximal gradient method converges asymptotically at a linear rate under this relaxed condition. The paper largely follows conventional methodology for proving the local error bound, with a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices. 

Quality:  
The paper is technically sound. The sole concern pertains to Appendix, Line 085: please clarify why choosing κ₃ (with α < 1) yields the claimed inequality R(X^k) ≤ κ₃ \|X^k - X^{k+1}\|_F.  

Clarity:  
The paper is generally comprehensible, though its technical depth suggests structuring the main contributions upfront before detailing supporting arguments. Specifically, after stating the local error bound, the authors could defer the technical proof of this bound and immediately proceed to establish the linear convergence rate—which, while straightforward, adds little novelty.  

Originality:  
The paper exhibits moderate originality. The simultaneous diagonalization technique is novel and noteworthy; however, the core approach to establishing the local error bound largely follows existing methods. Subsequent steps are not new.  

Significance:  
The theoretical significance is limited, with minimal implications for algorithm design. While extending linear convergence of proximal gradient methods to relaxed strong convexity is valuable, the asymptotic nature of the result diminishes its impact. The matrix diagonalization technique may hold independent interest. Overall, the work confirms asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace norm regularized risk minimization under relaxed strong convexity, largely by adapting the Luo-Tseng framework with a novel diagonalization insight. This constitutes a theoretical increment of moderate interest.
