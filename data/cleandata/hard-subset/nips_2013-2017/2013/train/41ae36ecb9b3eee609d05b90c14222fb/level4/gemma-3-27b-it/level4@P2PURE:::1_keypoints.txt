- Establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex on compact sets and Lipschitz gradient.  
- Extends error-bound based linear convergence results from lasso/group lasso (Tseng [20], Zhang et al. [22]) to the trace-norm setting; contribution is primarily technical.  
- Proofs are presented with reasonable clarity.  
- Strength: Demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending prior work.  
- Weakness: Contribution is largely technical.  
- Weakness: Lacks a numerical example illustrating the linear convergence rate.  
- Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 proofs closely resemble Tseng’s work; authors should provide more precise references to clarify novelty.  
- Suggestion: Include a numerical example (e.g., semilog plot of function value vs iterations) to validate theoretical findings.  
- Unclear dependence of constants κ1…κ4 on parameter choices α and ᾱ; this should be clarified.  
- Minor issue: Inequality sequence before (13) is convoluted; the final step is justified by convexity and subgradient inclusion, not the intermediate inequality (cf. p.289 in [20]).  
- Summary: Paper successfully extends the “error-bound condition” approach to trace-norm problems to obtain linear convergence.  
- Further requests: Add a simulation for a non-strongly-convex loss showing empirical linear convergence under varying stability, discuss when the theory fails, and discuss possible extension to ADMM.
