- Main contribution: Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularization under weaker loss assumptions than strong convexity, advancing ML and signal-processing theory.  
- Soundness: Writing is clear and the core proof appears technically sound, largely following Tseng’s Lipschitz error bound framework and resolving an open question for nuclear norm regularization.  
- Major gap: Lacks explicit comparison to the well-studied ℓ1-norm case; authors should relate techniques to ℓ1 arguments and explain why direct translation fails.  
- Requirement: The ℓ1-norm case should be shown recoverable as a special case of the trace-norm framework rather than treated only as a corollary.  
- Perception risk: Without the ℓ1 connection, the extension may seem unsurprising to some readers despite its technical value.  
- Title issue: “Structured Trace Norm Regularization” is misleading—the novelty is relaxing loss assumptions (via A); title should emphasize weakened loss conditions and linear convergence.  
- Notation fix: Replace “sampling operator” P with “mask operator” since P is fixed (not random).  
- Minor corrections: fix broken reference at line 337; briefly reiterate definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence in Section 2.  
- Clarifications requested: explicitly link residual R(X) (Eq. 6) to Tseng’s original definition (Eq. 46 in [20]) and add a short concluding remark summarizing the significance of the technically dense Lemma 3.2.
