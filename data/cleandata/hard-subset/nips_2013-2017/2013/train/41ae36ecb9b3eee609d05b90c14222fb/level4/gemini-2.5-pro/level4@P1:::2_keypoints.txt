- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity, under f(X)=h(A(X)) with strictly convex h and linear A.
- Technical novelty: derives a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization using matrix perturbation theory, resolving an open question.
- Relevance: result is significant for practical problems (e.g., matrix completion) where loss functions are often not strongly convex.
- Methodological framing: analysis builds on Luo and Tseng’s framework but requires new spectral techniques due to the non-polyhedral trace-norm epigraph.
- Clarity and rigor: paper is well-structured, clearly written, and the main theorems and proofs appear technically sound.
- Comparison suggestion: include an explicit comparison to l1-norm regularization results, explain why separability-based techniques fail and how the spectral approach succeeds; note l1-diagonal case is a corollary.
- Title suggestion: consider retitling to emphasize the structural assumption on the loss (f(X)=h(A(X))) rather than a property of the trace norm alone.
- Minor clarifications: define Q-linear and R-linear convergence on first use; relate residual map R(X) to Luo and Tseng’s residual; rename "sampling operator" P to "masking" or "sub-sampling" operator; fix a broken reference near line 337.
- Content balance: move some technical parts (e.g., intricate steps of Lemma 4.1) to supplementary material to free space for broader discussion and comparisons.
- Overall verdict: technically solid and significant contribution with minor presentation improvements suggested; recommend acceptance.
