

The paper establishes linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, addressing a crucial challenge in machine learning and signal processing. The manuscript is well-written and clearly structured, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound framework of Tseng [20], which previously left the nuclear norm case as an open question. The core proof appears sound based on my assessment.  

* A key omission is a clear comparison to the vector l1-norm regularization case (e.g., as in [20,22]). Explicitly relating to established l1 techniques and explaining why they do not directly extend to trace norm would strengthen the contribution. Notably, the l1 case should logically follow as a corollary from the trace norm result. This context is important for evaluating the paper's novelty, as some readers might view the extension as unsurprising despite its value.  

* The title could be refined to emphasize that the primary novelty lies in relaxing loss function assumptions (via the linear operator A) rather than in structuring the trace norm regularization.  

* Minor issues:  
  - Notation: "P" as a fixed mask operator (not a random sampling operator).  
  - Line 337: Broken reference.  
  - Clarify "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence definitions.  
  - Supplement the interpretation of residual R(X) in (6) relative to [20]'s definition (46).  
  - Enhance discussion/conclusions to better contextualize Lemma 3.2's technical proof.  

* Post-author feedback: The authors addressed most points. Regarding the title, the term "structured" is unnecessary in this context, though the final phrasing remains the authors' decision. The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial problem for many ML and signal processing applications.
