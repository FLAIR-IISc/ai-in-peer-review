The paper proves the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng [20], whose application to the nuclear norm case was left as an open question. Upon reviewing the main part of the proof, it appears to be correct.

However, a clear comparison or analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as seen in [20, 22] and related work, is missing. It would be beneficial to relate the results and techniques used in that case and explain why the l1-case techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm case. Additionally, it should be noted that the l1-norm case should follow as a corollary from the trace norm case. This omission should be considered when evaluating the paper's value, as some readers might find it unsurprising that the l1-result also holds for trace norm (although this reviewer still considers it a valuable contribution).

Furthermore, it is suggested that the title of the paper be revised, as the main new contribution affects the loss part more than making the trace norm regularization a structured one. The flexibility gained by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regularizer.

Minor issues include:
- Notation: The term "P" could be clarified, as it is referred to as a sampling or mask operator, which might imply randomness, but "P" is actually fixed.
- Reference [l337] appears to be broken.
- The meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence might not be widely known and could be repeated for clarity.
- The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be explained in relation to the original definition (46) by [20] for better interpretation.

In general, it would be beneficial to include more discussion and possibly conclusions in favor of the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.

Update after author feedback: The reviewer appreciates the authors' responses to most questions. Regarding the title, the suggestion was to remove the word "structured," which seemed unnecessary in this context. However, this decision is left to the authors.
