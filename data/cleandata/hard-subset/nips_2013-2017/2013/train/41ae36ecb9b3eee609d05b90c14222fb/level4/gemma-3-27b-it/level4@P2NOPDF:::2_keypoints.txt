- Establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker-than-strong-convexity assumptions.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, extending Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique to the nuclear norm and addressing an open question; core proof appears sound.  
- Lacks a stronger connection to l1-norm literature—should compare/contrast to vector-case results (e.g., [20,22]) and explain why l1 techniques may not transfer.  
- Recommend showing the l1-norm result as a corollary of the trace-norm result or explaining its relationship to strengthen novelty claims.  
- Suggest reconsidering the title: main contribution is relaxing loss-function assumptions rather than characterizing the trace norm as “structured.”  
- Notation issue: operator P is ambiguously described—use “mask operator” or “linear operator” instead of “sampling operator.”  
- Fix broken reference at line 337.  
- Clarify definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence for readers unfamiliar with the terms.  
- Explicitly link the residual R(X) in Eq. (6) to its original definition in Tseng [20] (Eq. (46)) for better interpretation.  
- Lemma 3.2’s proof is long; add discussion or concluding remarks after the proof to improve readability and motivation.  
- Update: reviewer appreciates authors’ responses and defers the title decision to the authors.
