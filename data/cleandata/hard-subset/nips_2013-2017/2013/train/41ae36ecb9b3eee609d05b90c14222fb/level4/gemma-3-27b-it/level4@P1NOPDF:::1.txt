This paper proves the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method applied to trace-norm regularized learning problems where the loss function takes the form f(X) = h(A(X)), with A being a linear operator and h being strongly convex on any compact set and having a Lipschitz gradient. 

This work extends the results of Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], which established similar linear convergence guarantees using an “error-bound condition” for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm. While this is a non-trivial extension, the contribution appears largely technical. The presentation of the proofs is generally clear.

**Strengths:**

- Demonstrates the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending existing results by Tseng et al.

**Weaknesses:**

- The contribution is primarily technical in nature.
- A numerical example illustrating the linear convergence behavior would be beneficial. 

**More Details:**

1. The outlines of the proofs for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely resemble those presented by Tseng. To better highlight the novelty of this work, the authors should provide more precise references to the original paper and clearly articulate their specific contributions.

2. Including a numerical example that explicitly demonstrates the linear convergence – for instance, a semilog plot of the function value against the number of iterations – would greatly strengthen the paper. 

3. The dependence of the constants κ<sub>1</sub> through κ<sub>4</sub> on the choices of  <u>α</u> and  ̄α is unclear. Further clarification on this point would be helpful.

**Minor Issue:**

4. The sequence of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is somewhat confusing. The final inequality is justified by the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that -̄G ∈ τ∂||X||<sub>*</sub>, rather than by the intermediate inequality as stated (see p289 in [20]).

**Overall:**

This paper represents an extension of previous linear convergence results, based on the “error-bound condition” originally developed for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm regularization setting. 

**Added after authors' feedback:**

Please include a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but the proximal gradient algorithm still converges linearly (for different levels of stability), and importantly, discuss the conditions under which the theory fails. Additionally, please discuss how this analysis could be extended to the analysis of ADMM.
