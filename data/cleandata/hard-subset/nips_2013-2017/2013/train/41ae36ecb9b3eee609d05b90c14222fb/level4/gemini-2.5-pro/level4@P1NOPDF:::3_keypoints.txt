- Paper analyzes convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization.  
- Relaxes strong convexity and works within Luo and Tseng’s framework.  
- Proves a local error bound and uses it to show asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient method.  
- Introduces a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the problem to the vector case.  
- Analysis largely follows standard templates for error bounds.  
- Technical arguments appear sound; an initial concern about an inequality was resolved by citing Lemma 4 of Sra (NIPS 2012).  
- Writing is generally clear but the presentation could be improved by stating main claims and implications before technical proofs.  
- Originality is moderate—main novelty is the diagonalization trick; other steps are standard.  
- Significance is theoretical: extends linear-rate analysis to less restrictive settings but only gives asymptotic guarantees and proposes no new algorithm.  
- Overall assessment: solid, incremental theoretical contribution of moderate interest to optimization and ML theory communities.
