The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function—a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal processing applications. 

The paper is carefully and clearly written, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng [20]. Applying this technique to the nuclear norm case was previously an open question, and the main portion of the proof appears correct to the best of my understanding. 

However, the paper would benefit from a clearer comparison to, and analogy with, the vector case of l1-norm regularization as explored in [20, 22] and related work. Relating the results and techniques used in the l1-norm case would be highly valuable, specifically explaining why those techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm setting. (Furthermore, as a corollary, the l1-norm result should follow from this trace norm result.) Assessing the paper’s value requires this consideration, as some may find the extension from l1 to trace norm unsurprising. Nevertheless, I still consider this a valuable contribution.

Independently, I suggest reconsidering the paper’s title, as the primary contribution lies in the weakening of assumptions on the loss function rather than characterizing the trace norm regularization as “structured.” (The newly gained flexibility stems from the linear operator A’s impact on the loss, not the regularizer itself.)

Minor issues include:

*   Notation: The operator P may be more accurately described as a mask or selection operator rather than a “sampling” operator, as the latter term might imply randomness, whereas P is fixed.
*   Page 337: A reference is broken.
*   The meanings of “Q-linear” and “R-linear” convergence are not universally known and could be briefly clarified.
*   The residual R(X) defined in equation (6) could be further explained in relation to the original definition (46) from [20] to aid interpretation.
*   In general, adding discussion or conclusions following the technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3 would improve the paper’s readability.
