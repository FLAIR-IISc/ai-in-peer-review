For trace-norm-regularized risk minimization, a common approach is the proximal gradient algorithm. Its sublinear global convergence rate is well understood, and it is also known to achieve a linear rate when the loss is additionally strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption by building on the framework of Luo and Tseng. In particular, the authors prove a local error bound that enables them to show that proximal gradient converges asymptotically at a linear rate for trace-norm regularization. The analysis largely follows the standard approach to establishing a local error bound, with a new observation that permits simultaneous diagonalization of the matrices.

Quality:
The paper appears technically sound. The only gap I see is in the appendix, line 085. Please explain why the specified choice of kappa3 (say alpha < 1) implies the claimed inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F.

Clarity:
I was able to follow the paper without much difficulty. Given the technical nature, it may help to present the main claims first and then develop the details. In particular, after stating the local error bound, the authors might postpone its very technical proof and immediately proceed to establish the linear rate (which, incidentally, contains little that is new).

Originality:
The paper’s originality is moderate. The observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices appears new and interesting; beyond that, the authors largely follow existing approaches to establish the local error bound. The remaining steps are not new.

Significance:
The contribution is mainly theoretical, with limited impact on algorithm design. While it is valuable to extend linear convergence of proximal gradient to more practical settings (i.e., a relaxed form of strong convexity), the significance is tempered by the asymptotic nature of the result and claim. The diagonalization technique may have independent interest. The work shows that proximal gradient, applied to trace-norm-regularized risk minimization, converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed strong-convexity condition. The analysis mostly follows the Luo–Tseng framework, with the new observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization and thereby reducing the problem more or less to the vector case. Overall, the contribution is incremental and of moderate theoretical interest.
