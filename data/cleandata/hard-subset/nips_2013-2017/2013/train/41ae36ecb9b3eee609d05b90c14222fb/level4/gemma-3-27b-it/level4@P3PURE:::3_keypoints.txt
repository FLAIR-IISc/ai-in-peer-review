- Context: Studies proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization, where sublinear global and linear-under-strong-convexity rates are known.  
- Main contribution: Proves a local error bound and uses it to show proximal gradient converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed strong-convexity assumption.  
- Technical approach: Builds on Luo and Tseng’s framework and largely follows standard techniques for establishing local error bounds.  
- Novelty: Introduces a new observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the matrix problem to the vector case.  
- Technical soundness: Paper appears technically correct overall.  
- Open technical question: Reviewer requests clarification in appendix (line 085) on why chosen kappa3 (alpha < 1) implies R(X^k) <= kappa3 ||X^k - X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Clarity suggestion: Recommend stating main claims first and postponing detailed proof of the local error bound, then immediately showing the linear convergence result for readability.  
- Originality assessment: Moderate — the simultaneous-diagonalization observation is new, but most other steps are incremental.  
- Significance assessment: The contribution is primarily theoretical and incremental; asymptotic linearity under relaxed assumptions is useful but has limited practical/algorithmic impact.
