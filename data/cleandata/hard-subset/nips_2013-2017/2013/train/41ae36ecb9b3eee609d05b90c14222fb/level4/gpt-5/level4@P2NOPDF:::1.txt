Summary
The paper proves linear convergence of the proximal-gradient method for trace-norm–regularized learning problems with losses of the form f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is linear and h is strongly convex on any compact set with a Lipschitz-continuous gradient. The work extends results based on the error-bound condition—previously established for the lasso and group lasso by Tseng [20], Tseng and Yun (“A coordinate gradient descent method for nonsmooth separable minimization”), and Zhang et al. [22]—to the trace norm. This is a nontrivial extension, though the novelty appears primarily technical. The proofs are generally clear.

Strengths
- Establishes linear convergence of the proximal-gradient algorithm for trace-norm regularization, extending the error-bound–based results of Tseng et al. and Zhang et al.
- The proof presentation is mostly clear and logically structured.

Weaknesses
- The contribution seems mainly technical, with limited conceptual novelty relative to prior error-bound analyses.
- The paper lacks numerical experiments demonstrating the predicted linear convergence.

Detailed comments
1. Relation to prior work: The outlines of the proofs for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely parallel those in Tseng’s framework. Please provide more precise attributions to the original arguments to clarify which steps are inherited and which are novel in the trace-norm setting. Explicitly distinguishing reused lemmas, modified assumptions, and genuinely new ingredients would make the contribution clearer.

2. Numerical validation: Please add an experiment that empirically demonstrates linear convergence (e.g., a semilog plot of suboptimality versus iterations) for representative trace-norm problems. Such evidence would substantiate the theoretical claims and help illustrate the role of the error-bound constants.

3. Constants and step-size parameters: It is unclear how the constants kappa_1, …, kappa_4 depend on the step-size bounds alpha_lower and alpha_upper. Please make these dependencies explicit, either by closed-form expressions or by clear bounds. This will help practitioners understand robustness to step-size selection and the quantitative rate.

4. Inequality chain before (13): The sequence of inequalities leading up to (13) is confusing. The last inequality follows from the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||_* (see p. 289 in [20]), rather than from the intermediate inequality currently emphasized. Please revise this part to avoid misinterpretation.

Requests for extension and scope
- Non–strongly convex losses with linear convergence: Please include a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex, yet PG exhibits linear convergence, and vary stability conditions to illustrate when linear rates persist. In parallel, clearly state the boundaries of your theory—identify conditions under which the error-bound fails and linear convergence is not guaranteed, and provide counterexamples or negative results if possible.
- Connection to ADMM: Discuss whether the same error-bound framework can yield linear convergence of ADMM for trace-norm–regularized problems. Which aspects (e.g., metric subregularity or calmness of the subdifferential mapping) transfer directly? What additional properties (e.g., strong convexity of a subproblem, exact penalty properties, or over-relaxation bounds) are needed? Even a high-level roadmap would be valuable.

Overall assessment
This paper extends linear-convergence guarantees based on the error-bound condition from lasso/group lasso to trace-norm regularization. The extension is nontrivial but primarily technical. With clearer attribution to prior arguments, explicit dependence of constants on step-size bounds, and empirical validation, the contribution would be significantly strengthened. The additional discussion on failure regimes and potential extensions to ADMM would further enhance the paper’s impact and clarify its scope.
