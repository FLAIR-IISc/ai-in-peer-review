- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems when f(X)=h(A(X)), with A linear and h strongly convex on compacts and Lipschitz gradient.  
- Extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] linear-convergence/error-bound results from lasso/group lasso to trace norm; extension is non-trivial but mainly technical.  
- Presentation and proofs are generally clear; main strength is extending Tseng et al.’s linear convergence result to the trace-norm setting.  
- Main weakness: contribution appears purely technical with no numerical demonstration of linear convergence.  
- Recommend adding a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but proximal gradient still converges linearly (varying stability levels), and discuss failure cases.  
- Suggest discussing how the analysis could be extended to ADMM.  
- Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 proofs resemble Tseng’s—authors should provide more precise references to the original work to clarify novelty.  
- Request a numerical example (e.g., semilog plot of function value vs iterations) to illustrate linear convergence.  
- Ask for clarification on how constants κ1…κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ (and related parameters) and more insight into these dependencies.  
- Minor technical comment: the sequence of inequalities before (13) is confusing—the last inequality holds because of trace-norm convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||*, not due to the intermediate inequality (see p289 in [20]).
