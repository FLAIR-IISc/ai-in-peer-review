1. Proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) with h strongly convex on compacts and Lipschitz gradient.  
2. Extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] error-bound based linear convergence results from lasso/group lasso to the trace-norm setting—nontrivial but mainly technical.  
3. Presentation and proofs are generally clear.  
4. Strength: Demonstrates linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending prior work.  
5. Weakness: Contribution is largely technical in nature.  
6. Weakness: No numerical example illustrating the linear convergence behavior.  
7. Request: Provide more precise references to Tseng where proof outlines resemble his work to better delineate the contribution.  
8. Request: Include a numerical example (e.g., semilog plot of function value vs iterations) to empirically demonstrate linear convergence.  
9. Request: Clarify how constants κ1–κ4 depend on the choices of α and α (dependence not explicitly stated).  
10. Minor issue: Sequence of inequalities before (13) is unclear; final step should be justified by convexity of trace norm and -G ∈ τ∂||X||_* (as noted in Tseng p.289).  
11. Overall: The paper is an extension of previous error-bound linear convergence results to trace-norm regularization.  
12. Revision requests: Add a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but proximal gradient still converges linearly (for varying stability), discuss scenarios where theoretical guarantees fail, and comment on extending the analysis to ADMM.
