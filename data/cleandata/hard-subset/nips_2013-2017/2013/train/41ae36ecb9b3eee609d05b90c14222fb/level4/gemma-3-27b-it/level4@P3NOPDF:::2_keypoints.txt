- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity.  
- Significance: important result for machine learning and signal processing, especially low-rank matrix recovery.  
- Technical basis: proof is generally sound and builds on Tseng’s Lipschitzian error bound technique.  
- Key deficiency: lacks detailed discussion linking results to the l1-norm (vector) literature and why l1 techniques do not directly transfer to the trace-norm case.  
- Suggested enhancement: show how the trace-norm result yields the l1 case as a corollary to clarify novelty and relationship.  
- Novelty concern: without the l1 comparison some reviewers may see the extension as less surprising; addressing this would strengthen impact.  
- Title recommendation: reconsider using “structured” — the main advance relaxes loss-function assumptions (properties of linear operator A), not inherent trace-norm structure.  
- Notation issue: operator P is imprecisely described; use a neutral term like “mask operator” or “projection operator” if P is fixed.  
- Broken reference: fix the broken reference at line 337.  
- Clarity requests: restate definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence for accessibility.  
- Residual term: more explicitly connect R(X) in equation (6) to Tseng [20] (eq. 46) to clarify its role.  
- Lemma 3.2: after the technical proof add discussion of implications and key insights to help readers grasp its contribution.  
- Overall verdict: strong paper, recommend acceptance contingent on addressing the l1-connection and the listed minor improvements.
