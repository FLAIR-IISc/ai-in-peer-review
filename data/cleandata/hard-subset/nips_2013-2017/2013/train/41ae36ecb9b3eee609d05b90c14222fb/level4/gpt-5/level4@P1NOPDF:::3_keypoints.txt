- Paper studies proximal gradient for trace-norm–regularized risk minimization, extending known global sublinear and strong-convexity linear rates.  
- Main contribution: prove a local error bound (via Luo–Tseng framework) that yields asymptotic linear convergence under a relaxed strong-convexity condition.  
- Key technical novelty: an observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of the relevant matrices.  
- Overall proof strategy follows standard patterns for local error bounds; subsequent derivation of the linear rate is largely standard.  
- Technical soundness: proof appears correct after addressing a gap about R(Xk) ≤ κ3 ||Xk−Xk+1||F.  
- Gap resolved by citing Sra (NIPS 2012) Lemma 4, which establishes |Rk| ≤ κ |Xk−Xk+1|.  
- Clarity: generally clear but dense; recommend presenting main claims and roadmap first and postponing the technical local-error-bound proof to improve readability.  
- Originality: moderate — simultaneous diagonalization seems novel, but much of the approach follows existing methods.  
- Significance: primarily theoretical and asymptotic; extends known theory but has limited immediate algorithmic impact.
