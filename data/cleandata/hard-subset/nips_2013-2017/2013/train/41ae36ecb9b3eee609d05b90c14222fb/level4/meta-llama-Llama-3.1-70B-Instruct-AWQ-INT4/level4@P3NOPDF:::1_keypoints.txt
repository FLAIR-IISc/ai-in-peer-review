- Paper proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)=h(A(X)) with h Lipschitz-gradient and strongly convex on compacts.
- Extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22] results from lasso/group lasso to the trace norm; extension is non-trivial but largely technical.
- Presentation and proofs are mostly clear and provide a thorough analysis of the proximal gradient method.
- Main contribution is technical; paper offers limited new insights beyond the extension to trace norm.
- Strength: establishes a valuable linear convergence guarantee for the proximal gradient algorithm in this setting.
- Weakness: lacks numerical experiments illustrating the claimed linear convergence (a semilog plot of objective vs iterations is recommended).
- Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 proofs resemble Tseng [20]; authors should give more precise attribution and clarify novel parts.
- Unclear how constants κ1…κ4 depend on the choices of ᾱ and α; authors should detail derivation and dependence.
- The sequence of inequalities before (13) is confusing; the final step relies on convexity and -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||* rather than the intermediate inequality.
- Suggestion: include simulations where loss is not strongly convex but proximal gradient still shows linear convergence to assess robustness.
- Suggestion: add discussion of failure cases (when the theory does not hold) to clarify limitations.
- Suggestion: consider extending analysis to ADMM to broaden applicability and compare methods.
