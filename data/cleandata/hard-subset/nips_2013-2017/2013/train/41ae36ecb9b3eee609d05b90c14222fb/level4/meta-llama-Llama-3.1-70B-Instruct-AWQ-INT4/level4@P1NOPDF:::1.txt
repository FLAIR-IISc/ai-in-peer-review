This paper proves the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method applied to trace-norm regularized learning problems when the loss function takes the form of f(X) = h(A(X)), where A is linear and h is strongly convex on any compact set and has a Lipschitz gradient.

The paper extends the results of Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], which established linear convergence using the "error-bound condition" for lasso and group lasso, to the trace norm. Although this extension is non-trivial, the contribution appears to be purely technical.

The presentation of the proofs is generally clear. The strengths of the paper include showing the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, thereby extending the results of Tseng et al. However, the weaknesses of the paper are its purely technical contribution and the lack of a numerical example demonstrating linear convergence.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should consider including a simulation where the loss is not strongly convex but the proximal gradient algorithm converges linearly (for different levels of stability), and importantly discuss when the theory fails. Additionally, they should discuss how this analysis can be extended to the study of ADMM.

More specifically, the outlines of the proofs of Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 seem very similar to those of Tseng. To make their contribution clearer, the authors should provide more precise references to the original work.

A numerical example showing linear convergence, such as a semilog plot of function value versus the number of iterations, would also be beneficial. Furthermore, it is unclear how the constants κ1, ..., κ4 depend on the choice of ᾱ and ᾱ. The authors should provide more insight into this relationship.

On a minor note, the sequence of inequalities before inequality (13) is confusing. The last inequality follows due to the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||* and not because of the intermediate inequality (see p289 in [20]).
