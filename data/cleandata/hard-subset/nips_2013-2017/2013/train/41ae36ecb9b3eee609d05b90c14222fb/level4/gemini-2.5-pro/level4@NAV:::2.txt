This paper establishes the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for problems with trace norm regularization, operating under assumptions more relaxed than the strong convexity of the loss function. This is a significant result for numerous applications in machine learning and signal processing. The manuscript is well-written, with a clear and rigorous presentation. The work successfully adapts the Lipschitzian error bound framework from Tseng [20], resolving what had been an open question regarding its application to the nuclear norm. To the best of my assessment, the core components of the proof appear to be sound.

*   The paper would be significantly strengthened by including a direct comparison and analogy to the vector l1-norm regularization case, as explored in [20, 22] and associated literature. A discussion on the relationship between the techniques and results would be beneficial, particularly an explanation of why methods from the l1-norm case are not directly applicable to the trace norm. Furthermore, it should be noted that the l1-norm result can now be seen as a corollary of the authors' findings. In assessing the paper's contribution, this context is important; while some might view the extension from the l1-norm to the trace norm as a logical progression, the result is nonetheless a very valuable contribution to the field.

*   Separately, I recommend reconsidering the paper's title. The primary contribution appears to be the added flexibility in the loss function via the linear operator A, rather than introducing a "structured" trace norm regularization. As such, the current title may not fully reflect the core innovation.

*   **Minor Points for Improvement**
    *   **Notation:** Is "P" best described as a sampling or mask operator? "Sampling" could imply randomness, which does not seem to be the case here.
    *   **l337:** There appears to be a broken reference on this line.
    *   The definitions of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence may not be universally known; defining them explicitly would improve clarity for a broader audience.
    *   For better interpretation, the residual R(X) from equation (6) could be explicitly linked to its original definition (46) in [20].
    *   Overall, I suggest incorporating more high-level discussion and conclusions, perhaps by condensing the extensive technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
