The paper establishes linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace/nuclear-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity of the loss, which is a crucial issue for many ML and signal processing applications.
The manuscript is carefully and clearly written. It closely builds on Tseng’s [20] Lipschitzian error-bound technique, whose application to the nuclear-norm setting had remained open. To the best of my understanding, the main part of the proof is correct.

* What I find missing is a clear comparison/analogy to the vector case with l1-norm regularization, as in [20, 22] and related work.
It would be very helpful to connect to the results and techniques used there, and to explain whether/why the l1-case arguments do not directly transfer to the trace norm. (Also, the l1-norm case should now follow from the trace-norm result as a corollary.)
When assessing the paper’s contribution, this context should be considered, since some readers might not find it extremely surprising that the l1 result extends to the trace norm (I still regard the contribution as valuable).

Independent of that, I recommend a slight modification of the title, since the main new contribution concerns the loss rather than making the trace-norm regularizer “structured.” (The additional flexibility via the linear operator A lies on the loss side, not in the regularizer.)

* Minor issues
- Notation: Should P be called a sampling operator or a mask operator? (“Sampling” may suggest randomness, but here P is fixed.)
- l337: broken reference
- The meanings of “Q-” and “R-” linear convergence may not be widely known and could be briefly recalled for clarity.
- The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be additionally explained in relation to the original definition (46) in [20] for better interpretation.
In general, I would encourage adding a bit more discussion and perhaps conclusions to balance the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
