To further strengthen the paper, consider including a simulation where the loss function is not strongly convex, but the proximal gradient algorithm still exhibits linear convergence, and analyze the impact of varying stability levels. Additionally, discuss the limitations of the theoretical framework and explore potential extensions to the analysis of Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM).

This paper establishes the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized learning problems, where the loss function takes the form f(X) = h(A(X)), with A being linear and h being strongly convex on any compact set and having a Lipschitz gradient.

The work builds upon Tseng [20], Tseng and Yun, and Zhang et al. [22], which demonstrated the same result for lasso and group lasso using the "error-bound condition," and extends it to the trace norm. While this extension is non-trivial, the contribution appears to be primarily technical.

The proof presentations are generally clear.

Strengths:

- The paper demonstrates the linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm, extending the results of Tseng et al.

Weaknesses:

- The contribution is largely technical.
- A numerical example illustrating linear convergence would be beneficial.

Additional comments:

1. The proof outlines for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 bear a strong resemblance to those in Tseng's work. To clarify their contribution, the authors should provide more precise references to the original work.
2. A numerical example showcasing linear convergence (e.g., a semilog plot of function value vs. the number of iterations) would be valuable.
3. The dependence of constants κ1, ..., κ4 on the choice of ᾱ and ᾱ is unclear.
Minor issue:

4. The sequence of inequalities preceding inequality (13) is confusing. The last inequality holds due to the convexity of the trace norm and the fact that -Ḡ ∈ τ∂||X||* , rather than the intermediate inequality (see p289 in [20]).

This work extends the previous linear convergence result based on the "error-bound condition" from lasso and group lasso to the trace norm.
