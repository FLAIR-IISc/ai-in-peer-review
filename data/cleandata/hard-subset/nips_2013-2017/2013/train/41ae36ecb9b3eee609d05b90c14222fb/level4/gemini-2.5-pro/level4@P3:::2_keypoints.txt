- Establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method for trace-norm-regularized problems without assuming strong convexity.  
- Main technical contribution: a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization.  
- Builds on Luo–Tseng composite-convex convergence framework; fills an open gap extending error bounds to the trace norm.  
- Overcomes the challenge of non-separable singular values by using matrix perturbation theory and structural properties of the optimal set.  
- Significance: broadens practical/theoretical applicability of PGM guarantees to important ML problems like matrix completion.  
- Originality/technical quality: first such error bound for the trace norm; proof technique is sophisticated and appears sound.  
- Clarity: paper is well-structured, clearly written, and effectively motivates assumptions and proof structure.  
- Request for clearer comparison to L1-norm results (e.g., Tseng [22]) and explicit explanation why non-separability is a fundamental obstacle.  
- Suggestion to comment whether the L1 case can be recovered as a special case (e.g., diagonal matrices).  
- Suggest expanding discussion/conclusion to cover implications beyond PGM and potential extensions to other matrix norms.  
- Minor fix: include one-sentence definitions of Q-linear and R-linear convergence for broader readership.  
- Minor wording: replace “sampling operator” with “masking operator” or “selection operator” to avoid ambiguity.  
- Minor clarification: relate residual map R(X) in eq. (7) to the general optimality conditions in Tseng [22].  
- Minor editorial: fix the broken reference on line 337.  
- Arguments for acceptance: resolves a non-trivial open problem, provides strong theoretical result, and is technically solid and novel.  
- Argument against acceptance: may be seen by some as an extension of existing Luo–Tseng results to a new regularizer rather than a wholly new framework.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, valuable, non-trivial contribution and a strong candidate for acceptance.
