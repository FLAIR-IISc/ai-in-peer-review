This paper establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient (PG) method for trace-norm regularized learning when the loss has the form $f(X) = h(A(X))$, with $A$ linear and $h$ strongly convex on compact sets with Lipschitz gradient. It extends Tseng [20] and Zhang et al. [22], which proved similar results for lasso and group lasso using the "error-bound condition," to the trace norm. This extension is non-trivial but represents a primarily technical contribution.  

The presentation of proofs is mostly clear.  

**Strengths:**  
- Demonstrates linear convergence of the PG algorithm, generalizing Tseng et al.’s results to the trace norm.  

**Weaknesses:**  
- The contribution is primarily technical, lacking broader conceptual novelty.  
- A numerical example illustrating linear convergence (e.g., a semilogarithmic plot of function value versus iteration count) is missing.  

**Detailed Comments:**  
1. The proof outlines for Theorem 3.1 and Lemma 3.2 closely mirror Tseng [20]. The authors should explicitly reference the original work to clarify their specific technical contributions.  
2. A numerical example showing linear convergence is essential to validate the theoretical claims.  
3. The dependence of constants $\kappa_1, \ldots, \kappa_4$ on $\underline{\alpha}$ and $\overline{\alpha}$ is not specified.  
4. The sequence of inequalities preceding (13) is confusing. The final inequality relies on the convexity of the trace norm and the subgradient condition $-\bar{G} \in \tau \partial \|X\|_{\ast}$ (cf. p. 289 in [20]), not the intermediate steps.  

**Summary:**  
This work extends the error-bound-based linear convergence analysis from lasso/group lasso to trace norm regularization. While technically sound, the contribution is incremental, and the absence of numerical validation weakens its impact. Addressing the requested simulation and theoretical failure cases would substantially strengthen the paper.
