The paper provides a significant contribution by proving the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems. Notably, this result is achieved under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial consideration for many machine learning (ML) and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written, closely following the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng [20]. The authors successfully address the open question of applying this technique to the nuclear norm case. The main part of the proof appears to be correct, based on my understanding.

However, I believe that the paper would benefit from a clear comparison or analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as discussed in [20,22] and related work. Relating the results and techniques used in the l1-case to the trace norm case would be helpful, and explaining why the l1-case techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm case would provide additional insight. Furthermore, since the l1-norm case should follow from the trace norm case as a corollary, highlighting this connection would strengthen the paper.

When evaluating the paper's value, it is essential to consider that some readers may find the extension of the l1-result to the trace norm case not entirely surprising. Nevertheless, I still consider this contribution to be highly valuable.

I suggest a slight modification to the paper's title, as the main new contribution affects the loss part more than making the trace norm regularization a "structured" one. The flexibility gained by the linear operator A is primarily on the loss side, not in the regularizer.

Minor issues that need to be addressed include:

* Notation: The term "sampling or mask operator" could be clarified, as "sampling" might imply randomness, whereas P is fixed.
* Reference [l337] appears to be broken.
* The meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence could be repeated for clarity, as these terms may not be widely known.
* The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be explained in relation to the original definition (46) by [20] for better interpretation.

In general, I recommend including more discussion and conclusions to support the lengthy technical proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.

After receiving the author's feedback, I would like to reiterate my suggestion regarding the title. I found the term "structured" unnecessary in this context, but I leave it to the authors' discretion.
