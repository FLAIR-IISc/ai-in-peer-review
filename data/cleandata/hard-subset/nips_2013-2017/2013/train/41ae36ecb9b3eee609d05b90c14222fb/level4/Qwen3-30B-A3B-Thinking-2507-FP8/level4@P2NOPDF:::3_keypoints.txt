- Establishes asymptotic linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization under a relaxed strong convexity condition.  
- Uses the Luo-Tseng framework and proves a local error bound to enable the linear-rate result.  
- Key novelty: a simultaneous diagonalization observation that reduces the matrix problem to a vector case and is central to the proof.  
- The error-bound step |R_k| ≤ κ |X_k − X_{k+1}| is addressed explicitly via Sra (NIPS 2012, Lemma 4), resolving a prior concern.  
- Technical core is sound, but the choice/justification of κ3 (e.g., the role of α < 1) in the appendix needs clearer explanation.  
- Paper clarity could be improved by stating main contributions (local error bound and its convergence implication) up front before technical proofs.  
- Recommend postponing the highly technical proof of the local error bound until after presenting the convergence result to improve readability.  
- Originality is moderate: simultaneous diagonalization is a valuable new insight, while applying the Luo-Tseng framework follows established patterns.  
- Significance: theoretically extends linear convergence to a broader class (relaxed strong convexity), but the asymptotic nature limits practical impact.  
- The diagonalization technique may have independent methodological value for other matrix optimization problems.  
- Overall: a solid, incremental theoretical contribution that advances understanding of trace-norm optimization but does not introduce a new algorithm or major practical advance.
