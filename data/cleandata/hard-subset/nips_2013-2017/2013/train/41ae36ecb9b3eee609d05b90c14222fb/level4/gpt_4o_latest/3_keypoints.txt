- Paper analyzes proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization, proving sublinear global and asymptotic linear local convergence under a relaxed strong-convexity assumption.  
- Results are obtained by establishing a local error bound within the Luo and Tseng framework.  
- Main novel technical observation: simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, which reduces the matrix problem to the vector case.  
- Overall technically sound.

- Clarity suggestion: state main claims up front and defer the very technical proof of the local error bound, then show the linear-rate derivation.  
- Originality: moderate — diagonalization idea is novel, but most derivations follow established methods.  
- Significance: theoretical interest but limited algorithmic impact; asymptotic nature of linear rate reduces practical significance.
