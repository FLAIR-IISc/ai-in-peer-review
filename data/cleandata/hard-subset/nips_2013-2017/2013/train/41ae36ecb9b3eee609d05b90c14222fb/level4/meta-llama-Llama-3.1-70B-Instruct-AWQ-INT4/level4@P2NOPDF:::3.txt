**Revised Review**

**Added after Authors' Rebuttal**
The proof for |Rk| ≤ κ|Xk - X_{k+1}| has been explicitly established in "Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting" by Suvrit Sra, NIPS'12 (Lemma 4).

For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a popular method. The sublinear global convergence rate of this algorithm is well-understood, and it is also known that the rate is linear if the loss is additionally strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity assumption by following the framework established by Luo and Tseng. Specifically, the authors prove a local error bound that enables them to show that the proximal gradient converges asymptotically at a linear rate for trace norm regularization. The paper largely follows the usual pattern to prove the local error bound, with a new observation that allows for the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.

**Quality**
This paper appears to be technically sound. However, I had one concern regarding the appendix, line 085. The authors should explain why choosing κ3 (with α < 1) leads to the claimed inequality R(X^k) ≤ κ3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F.

**Clarity**
I was able to follow the paper without significant difficulty. Nevertheless, due to its technical nature, it might be beneficial to outline the main claims first and then delve into the details one by one. After stating the local error bound, the authors might consider postponing the technical proof of the local error bound and instead immediately prove the linear rate, which is largely straightforward.

**Originality**
The originality of this paper is moderate. The observation that enables the simultaneous diagonalization of matrices seems to be new and interesting. However, the authors largely follow the existing approach to establish the local error bound, and the remaining steps are not new.

**Significance**
The significance of this paper is primarily theoretical, with limited impact on algorithm design. While it is valuable to extend the linear convergence rate of the proximal gradient algorithm to more practical scenarios (i.e., relaxed form of strong convexity), the significance is somewhat diminished by the asymptotic nature of the proof (and claim). The authors' approach to diagonalizing matrices might be of some independent interest. This work demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm, when applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization, converges asymptotically at a linear rate (under a relaxed form of strong convexity). The authors mostly follow a well-established framework due to Luo and Tseng, with a new observation that allows them to diagonalize matrices and reduce to the vector case. Overall, this work is incremental and moderately interesting in the theoretical aspect.
