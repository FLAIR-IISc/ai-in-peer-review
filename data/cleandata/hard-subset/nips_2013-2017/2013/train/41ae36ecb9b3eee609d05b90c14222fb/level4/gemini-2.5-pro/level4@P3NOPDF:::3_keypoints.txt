- Studies proximal gradient convergence for trace-norm regularized risk minimization, extending analysis beyond known sublinear/global and strongly convex/linear cases.  
- Uses Luo and Tseng’s framework to establish a local error bound and prove asymptotic linear convergence without requiring strong convexity.  
- Core technical novelty: a clever observation permitting simultaneous diagonalization of matrices, reducing the matrix problem to a vector case.  
- Proofs are detailed and mathematically sound; the local error bound is well-supported in the appendix.  
- Presentation is generally clear, but the exposition could be improved by first showing how the error bound implies linear convergence before diving into the technical proof.  
- Originality is moderate—the overall strategy follows existing frameworks, with the diagonalization trick as the main novel element.  
- Significance is primarily theoretical: proving asymptotic linear rate under relaxed conditions is valuable, but practical impact is limited because the result is asymptotic and no new algorithms or experiments are provided.  
- The diagonalization technique may still be of independent interest to researchers working on related matrix problems.  
- Reviewer’s technical concern about an inequality in the appendix was resolved by the authors via a reference to Sra (NIPS 2012) Lemma 4.  
- Overall conclusion: a solid, incremental theoretical contribution that advances understanding of proximal gradient behavior for trace-norm problems.
