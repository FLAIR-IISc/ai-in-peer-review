Summary
This paper proves global linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace/nuclear-norm regularized problems under assumptions strictly weaker than strong convexity of the loss. The analysis follows the Lipschitzian error bound (EB) framework of Tseng [20], resolving an open question about its applicability to the nuclear-norm setting. The problem class is central to machine learning and signal processing, and the results therefore have broad practical relevance.

Strengths
- The paper is carefully written and technically solid. The main proof appears correct to the best of my understanding.
- Establishing linear convergence under EB-type conditions (rather than strong convexity) significantly broadens applicability.
- Allowing a general linear operator A in the loss captures many important models (e.g., matrix completion, sensing, and regression with linear measurements).
- The work closes a gap in the literature by adapting the EB technique to the matrix trace-norm case.

Main suggestions for improvement
1) Positioning relative to the l1 case
- Please add an explicit comparison with the vector l1-regularized literature (e.g., [20, 22] and related work). It would be helpful to:
  - State precisely which l1 results are recovered as special cases or corollaries of your theorems.
  - Identify which parts of the l1 analysis do not carry over verbatim to the trace-norm setting and why (e.g., complications from matrix subspace geometry, singular value thresholding vs. soft-thresholding, rank-stability issues, or properties of the subdifferential).
  - Clarify whether any additional assumptions are needed in the matrix case that are unnecessary in the l1 case, or vice versa.
This will help readers understand the novelty beyond a presumed “vector-to-matrix” analogy and make clear where the technical innovations lie.

2) Clarifying the role of “structure” and the title
- My concern is specifically with the word “structured” in the title. The new generality seems to lie primarily on the loss side (via the linear operator A) rather than in introducing a more structured trace-norm regularizer. Consider a title that emphasizes the EB-based linear convergence under general linear measurements, rather than “structured” trace-norm regularization.

3) Exposition of convergence notions and residuals
- The meanings of Q-linear and R-linear convergence are not universally known. Please restate the definitions on first use and ensure they are used consistently throughout the paper.
- The residual R(X) in (6) would benefit from a short explanatory paragraph connecting it to Tseng’s residual (Definition (46) in [20]). A brief discussion of how your residual specializes to and diverges from Tseng’s definition would greatly improve interpretability.

4) Proof organization and intuition
- Section 3 (in particular Lemma 3.2) is technically dense. A short roadmap at the beginning of the section explaining the logic of the argument and the high-level idea behind the EB verification for the nuclear norm would help readers navigate the details. Concluding the section with a brief summary of key takeaways (and limitations) would also improve readability.

5) Addressing perceived surprise/novelty
- Some readers may expect the extension from l1 to the trace norm to “just work.” It would help to explicitly state why this is not immediate and where the proof requires new ingredients (e.g., handling the geometry of low-rank manifolds, controlling perturbations of singular subspaces, or ensuring stability of active sets/ranks under the proximal operator).

Minor comments
- Notation: If P denotes a fixed masking operator, consider “mask” instead of “sampling,” since “sampling” may suggest randomness.
- Broken reference at line 337: please fix.
- Ensure all assumptions used in the EB condition are clearly stated near the main theorem (e.g., Lipschitz continuity of the gradient, quadratic growth/error bound, properties of A).
- When possible, highlight any simple conditions on A and the loss that guarantee the EB (e.g., restricted strong convexity or quadratic growth), even if only as examples.
- It may help to briefly comment on rank stability across iterations under the proximal map and whether it is required for the EB or rate analysis.

Quality of writing and presentation
- Overall the paper is clear and well organized. Adding the requested comparisons with the l1 literature, restating key convergence definitions, and situating your residual within Tseng’s framework will make the contribution easier to digest for a broad audience.

Post-rebuttal note
- Thank you for the clarifications. My title concern was specifically about the term “structured,” which seems unnecessary here, but I defer to the authors’ judgment. My overall evaluation remains positive: the paper establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under assumptions weaker than strong convexity, addressing an important and timely problem in ML and signal processing.

Overall assessment
This is a solid theoretical contribution that answers a natural and previously unresolved question: does Tseng’s EB-based linear convergence extend to nuclear-norm regularization? The paper presents a careful and convincing analysis. With improved positioning relative to the l1 literature, clearer definitions, and minor editorial fixes, the paper will be strong. I recommend acceptance.
