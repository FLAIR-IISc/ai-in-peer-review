The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal processing applications. 

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Luo and Tseng [10], whose application to the nuclear norm case was previously an open question. The main part of the proof appears correct, to the best of my understanding. 

One aspect missing from this paper is a more direct comparison to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, and related work such as [22, 23]. It would be helpful to relate the results and techniques used in that case, and to explain why techniques from the l1-case do not directly translate to the trace norm setting. (Ideally, the l1-norm result should follow as a corollary from the trace norm result.) While I find this a valuable contribution, acknowledging this connection would help position the work within the broader literature and potentially address concerns that the result might not be entirely surprising.

In light of this, and given the primary contribution lies in relaxing the assumptions on the loss function, I suggest considering a slight change to the paper’s title. The current title emphasizes a “structured” regularization, whereas the key innovation stems from the flexibility gained by the linear operator A in the loss function – not within the regularizer itself.

Minor issues:

*   Notation: The operator P is referred to as both a sampling and a mask operator. While “sampling” might suggest randomness, here P is fixed. Clarifying this would be helpful.
*   Line 337: There appears to be a broken reference.
*   The terms “Q-linear” and “R-linear” convergence might not be widely known, and a brief reminder of their definitions could improve clarity.
*   The residual R(X) defined in equation (7) could be more explicitly connected to the original definition in [10], for better interpretation.
*   In general, I would recommend expanding the discussion and conclusions, particularly in relation to the technical details of the proof of Lemma 4.1 in Section 4.

Arguments for acceptance:

*   **Novelty:** The paper establishes a significant theoretical result – linear convergence of PGM for trace norm regularization – under milder assumptions than previously required.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proof appears rigorous and well-structured, building upon established techniques in convex optimization.
*   **Significance:** The result has practical implications for a wide range of machine learning applications utilizing trace norm regularization, where strong convexity assumptions are often unrealistic.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and relatively easy to follow, given the technical complexity of the subject.

Arguments against acceptance:

*   **Limited Discussion of Related Work:** The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of connections to the l1-norm regularization literature.
*   **Title:** The current title might not fully capture the core contribution of the work.



I would vote to accept the paper, with minor revisions addressing the points outlined above.
