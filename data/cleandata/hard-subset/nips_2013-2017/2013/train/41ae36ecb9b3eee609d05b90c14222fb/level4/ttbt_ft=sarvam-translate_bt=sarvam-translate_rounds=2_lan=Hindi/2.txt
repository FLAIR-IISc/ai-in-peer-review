This paper proves the linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker notions compared to the strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial issue for many ML and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows Tseng's [20] lopsian error bound technique, the application of which in the atomic norm case remains an open question. In my understanding, the main part of the proof seems correct.

* The paper lacks a clear comparison/analog to the vector case of l1-rule regularization, as in [20,22] and related works.

It would be very helpful to explain and relate the results and techniques used in that case as to why l1-case techniques do not directly translate into trace norms. (Also, the l1-rule case should now be followed as a corollary from the trace norm).

To assess the value of the paper, it should be kept in mind, as some people may not find it very surprising that the l1-result is also valid for trace norms (although I still find it a very valuable contribution).

Furthermore, I would suggest a slight change to the title of the paper, as the main new contribution affects the loss part more than structuring the trace norm regularization. (Since the new flexibility obtained by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regulator).

* Minor issues
- Notation: Should P be called the sampling or mask operator? (Sampling may imply randomness, but P is fixed here)
-l337: Broken context
- The meaning of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence is probably not widely known, and may be repeated for clarity.
- The residual R(X) is defined in (6), which can be further explained in relation to the original definition (46) by [20] for better explanation.

In general, I would vote for a little more discussion and perhaps include the conclusions in favor of the long technical part of the proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.
