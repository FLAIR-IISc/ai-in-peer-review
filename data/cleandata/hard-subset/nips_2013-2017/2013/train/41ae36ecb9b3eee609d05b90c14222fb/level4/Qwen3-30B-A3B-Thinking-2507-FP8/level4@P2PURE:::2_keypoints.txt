- Main contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized problems under weaker assumptions than strong convexity, important for ML and signal processing.  
- Technique: follows Lipschitzian error bound approach of Tseng [20]; extends an open question to the nuclear norm case.  
- Correctness: main proof appears sound to the reviewer.  
- Major gap: paper lacks clear comparison to the vector l1-norm case (e.g., [20,22]); explicitly relate results/techniques and explain why l1 methods do not directly carry over.  
- Corollary note: the l1-norm case should follow from the trace-norm result and this connection should be highlighted.  
- Title suggestion: "structured" seems unnecessary; the novelty is in the loss (operator A) rather than the regularizer, so consider revising the title.  
- Notation issue: calling operator P "sampling" may be misleading since P is fixed (not random).  
- Broken reference: line 337 contains a broken citation.  
- Terminology: definitions of Q- and R-linear convergence are not widely known and should be restated for reader clarity.  
- Clarify residual: the residual R(X) in (6) should be better related to definition (46) in [20] for interpretation.  
- Section 3: Lemma 3.2’s long technical proof needs more discussion/conclusions to justify its length and significance.  
- Update: most points were addressed by authors; reviewer reiterates the title wording concern about "structured."
