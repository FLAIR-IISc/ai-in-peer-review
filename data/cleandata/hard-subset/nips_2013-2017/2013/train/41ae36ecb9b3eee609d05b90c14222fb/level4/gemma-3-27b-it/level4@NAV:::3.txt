For trace norm regularized risk minimization, the proximal gradient algorithm is a common choice, with its sublinear global convergence well-established. Linear convergence is known to hold when the loss function is strongly convex. This paper relaxes the strong convexity requirement, building upon the framework of Luo and Tseng, and demonstrates that the proximal gradient algorithm converges linearly, asymptotically, for trace norm regularization. The proof of the local error bound largely follows standard techniques, but incorporates a novel observation enabling simultaneous diagonalization of matrices.

Quality:
The paper appears to be technically sound. I have a specific question regarding the appendix, line 085. Please clarify why a particular choice of kappa3 (e.g., alpha < 1) results in the stated inequality R(X^k) <= kappa3 |X^k - X^{k+1}|_F ? 

Clarity:
The paper is generally well-written and understandable, despite its technical complexity. To improve readability, it might be beneficial to first present the main results and then delve into the detailed proofs. Specifically, after stating the local error bound, the authors could postpone its proof and instead directly proceed to demonstrate the linear convergence rate, which largely follows existing approaches.

Originality:
The originality of this work is moderate. The observation regarding simultaneous matrix diagonalization is novel and potentially interesting. However, the establishment of the local error bound largely follows existing methods. The remaining steps are not particularly new.

Significance: 
The significance of this work lies primarily in its theoretical contribution, with limited implications for algorithm design. Extending the linear convergence guarantee of the proximal gradient algorithm to scenarios with relaxed strong convexity is valuable. However, the asymptotic nature of the results somewhat diminishes the impact. The matrix diagonalization technique could be of independent interest. In summary, this paper demonstrates the asymptotic linear convergence of the proximal gradient algorithm applied to trace norm regularized risk minimization under a relaxed form of strong convexity. The authors leverage the established framework of Luo and Tseng, coupled with a new diagonalization observation to (effectively) reduce the problem to the vector case. Overall, this work represents an incremental but moderately interesting theoretical contribution.

Regarding the authors’ rebuttal: The proof for |Rk| <= kappa |Xk - X_{k+1}| has been explicitly established in “Scalable nonconvex inexact proximal splitting”, Suvrit Sra, NIPS'12 (Lemma 4).
