The paper proves linear convergence of the standard proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems, under weaker assumptions than strong convexity of the loss function, which is a crucial problem for many machine learning and signal processing applications.

The paper is carefully and clearly written. It closely follows the Lipschitzian error bound technique of Tseng, whose application to the nuclear norm case was still left as an open question. The main part of the proof seems correct as far as I can understand.

One aspect that I feel is missing in this paper is a clear comparison or analogy to the vector case of l1-norm regularization, as seen in related work. It would be very helpful to relate to the results and techniques used in that case and to explain if or why the l1-case techniques do not directly translate to the trace norm case. Additionally, it should be noted that the l1-norm case should follow from the trace norm case as a corollary.

When judging the value of the paper, this should be taken into account, as some people might find it not extremely surprising that the l1-result also holds for trace norm (although I still find it a very valuable contribution).

I would suggest a slight change of the title of the paper, as the main new contribution affects the loss part more than making the trace norm regularization a structured one. Since the newly gained flexibility by the linear operator A is on the loss side, not in the regularizer.

Minor issues include:

- Notation: Instead of calling P a sampling or mask operator, consider using a more descriptive term, as "sampling" might hint at randomness, but here P is fixed.
- Reference l337 is broken.
- The meanings of "Q-" and "R-" linear convergence are maybe not that widely known and could be repeated for clarity.
- The residual R(X) defined in (6) could be additionally explained in relation to the original definition by Tseng for better interpretation.

In general, I would suggest including a bit more discussion and maybe conclusions in favor of the long technical part of the proof of Lemma 3.2 in Section 3.

After receiving feedback from the authors, I would like to thank them for answering most of my questions. Regarding the title, I was referring to the word "structured," which I found unnecessary in the context here, but I leave that to the authors' discretion.
