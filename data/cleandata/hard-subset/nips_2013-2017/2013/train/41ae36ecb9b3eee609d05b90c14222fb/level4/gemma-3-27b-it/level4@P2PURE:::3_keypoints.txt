- Main claim: Proximal gradient for trace-norm regularized risk minimization converges asymptotically at a linear rate under a relaxed strong convexity assumption via a proven local error bound.  
- Framework: Builds on Luo & Tseng’s framework; proof of the local error bound largely follows standard techniques.  
- Novel observation: Introduces a new simultaneous matrix diagonalization trick that reduces the problem to the vector case.  
- Technical soundness: Paper appears technically sound overall.  
- Clarification requested: Explain Appendix line 085—how a choice of κ3 (e.g., α < 1) yields R(X^k) ≤ κ3 ||X^k − X^{k+1}||_F.  
- Clarity suggestion: Paper is clear but should present main claims up front and postpone detailed proofs; show linear convergence earlier for readability.  
- Originality: Moderate—diagonalization idea is novel and interesting; remainder of approach is incremental and follows existing methods.  
- Significance: The contribution is mainly theoretical with limited direct impact on algorithm design; asymptotic nature lessens practical significance.
