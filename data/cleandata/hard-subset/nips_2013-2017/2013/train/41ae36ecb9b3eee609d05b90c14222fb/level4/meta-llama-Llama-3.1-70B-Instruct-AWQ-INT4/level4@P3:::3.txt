This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization by establishing the linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for solving a class of trace norm-regularized problems. The authors relax the strong convexity assumption, which is a common requirement for linear convergence, and instead rely on a local error bound that allows them to show asymptotic linear convergence.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the PGM's convergence properties. The key technical tool in the proof is a Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems, which could be of independent interest. The authors also provide numerical experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the PGM in practice.

The paper's clarity is good, and the authors do a good job of explaining the main claims and technical details. However, the paper could benefit from a clearer structure, with the main claims and results presented first, followed by the technical details. This would make it easier for readers to follow the paper and understand the key contributions.

The originality of the paper is moderate, as the authors build on existing work by Luo and Tseng. However, the new observation that allows simultaneous diagonalization of matrices is interesting and novel. The rest of the paper follows established approaches to establish the local error bound, but the authors' contribution is still significant.

The significance of the paper is mainly theoretical, with limited impact on algorithm design. However, the result is still important, as it extends the linear convergence rate of the PGM to more practical scenarios. The authors' work on diagonalizing matrices might also be of independent interest.

Overall, this paper is a solid contribution to the field of optimization, and the authors' results are likely to be of interest to researchers working on related topics. While the paper is not groundbreaking, it is a well-written and technically sound contribution that advances our understanding of the PGM's convergence properties.
