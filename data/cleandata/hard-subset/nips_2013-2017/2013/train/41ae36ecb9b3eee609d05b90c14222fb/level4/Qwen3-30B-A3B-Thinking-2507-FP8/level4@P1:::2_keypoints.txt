- Establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for a broad class of trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss.
- Proves a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization, resolving an open question about such bounds for the trace norm.
- Extends linear convergence guarantees to important ML problems (matrix completion, classification) whose losses (e.g., square, logistic) are not strongly convex but meet the paper’s assumptions.
- Builds on the Luo–Tseng error bound framework while handling the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm.
- Key technical insight: optimal solutions and their gradients share singular vectors, enabling matrix perturbation theory in the proof.
- The main technical contribution (the error bound) appears correct and may have independent applications beyond the convergence analysis.
- Paper is technically sound, rigorous, and well-connected to prior literature.
- Numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification convincingly demonstrate the predicted linear convergence.
- Writing and organization are clear; definitions and notation are consistent.
- Suggests expanding some technical details for readers unfamiliar with matrix perturbation theory.
- Recommends a more explicit comparison to the vector ℓ₁-norm case to clarify why existing techniques do not directly translate.
- Notes the distinction between Q-linear and R-linear convergence could be briefly explained for broader accessibility.
- Points out the title slightly misrepresents the main contribution, which depends more on loss structure (linear operator A) than the trace norm alone.
- The connection showing the trace norm result implies the ℓ₁-norm case as a corollary is not fully developed.
- Overall assessment: significant, novel, practically relevant, and recommended for acceptance; minor issues are easily addressable in the camera-ready version.
