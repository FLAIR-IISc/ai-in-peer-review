- Main contribution: establishes linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for a class of trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity.  
- Theoretical novelty: introduces a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems used to prove linear convergence.  
- Applicability: result applies to a wide range of machine learning problems thanks to the no–strong-convexity requirement.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with a clear and concise introduction, background, and motivation.  
- Presentation: main results and key technical tools are explained in a logical, easy-to-follow manner.  
- Numerical validation: experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification show PGM converges linearly in objective value and solution.  
- Consistency: empirical results align with the theoretical linear convergence guarantees.
