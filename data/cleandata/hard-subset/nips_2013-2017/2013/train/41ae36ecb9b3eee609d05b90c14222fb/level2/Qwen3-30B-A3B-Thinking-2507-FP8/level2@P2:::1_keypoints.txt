- Addresses whether proximal gradient method (PGM) attains linear convergence for trace norm-regularized problems without strong convexity.  
- Establishes a novel Lipschitzian error bound (Theorem 3.1) for trace norm regularization used to prove linear convergence of PGM for losses f(X)=h(A(X)) under mild h conditions.  
- Significant theoretical contribution resolving an open question; error bound appears novel and well-contextualized relative to ℓ1/group LASSO work.  
- Proof techniques are sophisticated, notably using matrix perturbation theory to obtain a restricted invertibility property (Lemma 4.1(c)).  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) empirically demonstrate the predicted linear convergence phase.  
- Paper lacks explicit comparison of the new error bound’s constants and conditions to prior error bounds, limiting assessment of precise improvement.  
- Insufficient discussion of computational implications, e.g., how ∇f’s Lipschitz constant affects practical PGM step-size choices.  
- Experiments are limited in scope (only two problems) and do not compare PGM’s convergence with other methods (e.g., accelerated PGM, ADMM).  
- Backtracking line search overhead is noted but not quantified against fixed step sizes.  
- Major concern: practical conditions for linear convergence are unclear—Theorem 3.1 requires F(X) ≤ ζ and ‖R(X)‖F ≤ ǫ but offers no guidance on estimating these constants.  
- Authors should discuss how to estimate η and ǫ in applications and provide empirical evidence that iterates enter the linear-convergence region.  
- Suggested additions: compare with other first-order methods, clarify step-size/error-bound relationship, and include computational complexity discussion.  
- Overall: technically rigorous, novel, and significant; recommended for acceptance subject to addressing practical-conditions and experimental limitations.
