- Demonstrates linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace norm regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss.  
- Core technical contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound enabling the linear convergence result.  
- Overcomes non-polyhedral epigraph challenges via analysis of singular vectors of optimal solutions and matrix perturbation theory.  
- Connects clearly to prior frameworks (e.g., Luo and Tseng) and positions the contribution within existing theory.  
- Proof strategy is rigorous and sound.  
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) support the theoretical linear convergence, adding credibility.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and addresses a relevant ML problem with broad applicability.  
- Weakness: practical implications of Assumption 2.1 (particularly for h) need more detailed discussion and concrete examples of when it holds or fails.  
- Weakness: related work section could better contrast trace norm vs. ℓ1-norm error bound challenges beyond the non-polyhedral remark.  
- Weakness: numerical experiments are limited and should be expanded to larger datasets, varied regularization parameters, and more complex loss functions.  
- Suggestion: investigate sensitivity of convergence to step size α and compare against alternative algorithms (e.g., accelerated PGM).  
- Minor clarity issue: frequent use of barred notation (e.g., X̄) can be confusing; more descriptive naming recommended.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty (no-strong-convexity linear convergence), soundness of analysis, relevance to ML, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, insufficient discussion of assumptions, and a related work section that could be expanded.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with a significant theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments, clarify assumptions, and enrich related work.
