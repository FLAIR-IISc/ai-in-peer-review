- Studies convergence rate of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems min f(X)+τ||X||*, important for matrix completion, multitask learning, etc.
- Main result: PGM converges linearly even when f is not strongly convex, for losses f(X)=h(A(X)) with strictly convex h and arbitrary linear A.
- Key technical contribution: a novel Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to the optimal set to a residual map measuring fixed-point optimality violation.
- Proves the error bound despite the non-separability of the trace norm by exploiting shared singular-vector structure and using matrix perturbation theory.
- Places the error bound within the Luo–Tseng framework to obtain linear convergence of PGM.
- Significance: resolves a fundamental question and explains fast practical convergence (e.g., matrix completion) without assuming strong convexity.
- Originality: the error bound and proof technique are novel and could be of independent interest for other algorithms.
- Technical quality: arguments are sound and the main proof outline is clearly presented.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-motivated, and precisely states assumptions and main theorems.
- Weakness: convergence result is asymptotic; paper should discuss dependence of constants and threshold K on A, h, and τ.
- Weakness: connection to Restricted Strong Convexity/Restricted Isometry concepts should be discussed to contextualize the "restricted invertibility" condition.
- Weakness: experiments could be stronger (e.g., counterexample when assumptions fail) and fix figure caption/labeling errors.
- Weakness: more intuition in the main text for Proposition 4.3’s decomposition and classification of singular-value cases would help readability.
- Conclusion: substantial, novel, and well-executed work with minor fixable issues; strong recommendation for acceptance.
