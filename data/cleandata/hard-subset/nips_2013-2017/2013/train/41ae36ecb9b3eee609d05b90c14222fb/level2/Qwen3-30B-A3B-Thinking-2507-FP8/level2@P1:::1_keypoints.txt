- Addresses an important theoretical question: proving linear convergence for proximal gradient on trace norm-regularized problems without strong convexity.
- Main contribution: proves a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization that yields linear convergence.
- Applicable to losses of the form f(X)=h(A(X)) where h meets specified smoothness and curvature conditions.
- Key technical insight: optimal solutions and their gradients share singular vectors, enabling matrix perturbation arguments.
- Novelty: first error bound for trace norm regularization that does not require strong convexity, overcoming the non-polyhedral epigraph challenge.
- Contextualizes and improves on prior work that required strong convexity or handled only special cases.
- Theoretical results are rigorous and well-structured (Theorem 3.1 error bound, Theorem 3.2 linear convergence), building on Luo & Tseng’s framework with new technical tools.
- Lemma 4.1 (restricted invertibility property) is elegant and crucial to the analysis.
- Empirical validation: numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification show the predicted linear convergence in practice.
- Writing and organization are clear, with consistent notation and good exposition of challenges and solutions.
- Significance: results impact many ML applications using trace norm regularization and may be of independent interest for related optimization problems.
- Minor limitation: paper does not discuss practical cost of verifying assumptions (e.g., strong convexity of h on compact sets), though examples are provided.
- Recommendation: strong accept for NIPS due to technical novelty, rigorous proofs, empirical support, and practical relevance.
