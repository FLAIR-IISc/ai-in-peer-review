The paper studies proximal gradient methods (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems min f(X)+τ||X||*, with f(X)=h(A(X)) and A linear. The main claims are: (1) PGM enjoys asymptotic linear convergence (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) for a broad class of losses (including square and logistic), without assuming strong convexity of f; (2) this follows from a new local Lipschitzian error bound that relates distance to the optimal set to the proximal residual ||Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X||F. The key proof ingredient is a spectral structure result showing that optimal solutions X* and −∇f(X*) share singular vectors, enabling a tailored perturbation analysis; linear rate then follows via the Luo–Tseng framework.

Relation to prior work: Previous results give sublinear O(1/k)–O(1/k^2) rates for general composite problems and linear rates under strong convexity. Error bounds for composite problems exist for polyhedral nonsmooth parts (ℓ1, group LASSO), but not for the non-polyhedral trace norm. This work fills that gap with a nontrivial simultaneous SVD argument and establishes linear rates for widely used models in matrix completion/classification, aligning with optimization-theoretic work at NIPS on first-order methods.

Quality: The theoretical development is careful and technically sound. Lemma 4.1 and Propositions 4.2–4.3 address the major hurdle (non-polyhedrality) convincingly. The convergence result requires standard step-size conditions. Experiments are illustrative and show linear regimes on synthetic tasks, but they are limited and do not benchmark against alternatives.

Clarity: The paper is well organized, with clear motivation and assumptions. Some crucial inequalities (9–11) and the proof of Proposition 4.3 are deferred to the supplement; providing sketches and explicit constants in the main text would improve readability.

Originality: High. The error bound for trace-norm regularization without strong convexity is, to my knowledge, new and of independent interest.

Significance: The result strengthens our understanding of PGM for low-rank learning and is likely to influence both theory and practice. Practical impact is moderated by the local/asymptotic nature of the guarantee and by the lack of guidance on constants.

Constructive suggestions:
- Quantify the neighborhood/constant in the error bound and discuss how to detect entry into the linear regime.
- Discuss inexact proximal steps (truncated SVD) and whether the error bound is robust.
- Provide comparisons (e.g., APG, ADMM, Frank–Wolfe/SVT) on real datasets with wall-clock time.
- Clarify assumptions on h (e.g., verifying strong convexity on compact sets for logistic) and implications for backtracking.
- Comment on extensions (Schatten norms, KL-based analysis, stochastic PG).

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and nontrivial error bound for trace norm.
- Clear theoretical advance: linear convergence without strong convexity.
- Broad applicability (square/logistic, general linear A).

Cons against acceptance:
- Experimental evaluation is minimal and synthetic only.
- Asymptotic/local result with unspecified constants; limited practical guidance.
- Key proofs partly relegated to supplement.
