- Studies linear convergence of proximal gradient method for trace-norm regularized problems without assuming strong convexity.
- Clearly motivated problem with many practical applications and a gap in existing literature identified.
- Main contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization used to prove linear convergence via Luo & Tseng results.
- Novel idea: leveraging shared singular vectors between the optimum and its negative gradient to obtain the error bound.
- Theoretical proofs are rigorous, well-structured, and assumptions are clearly stated.
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) show empirical linear convergence supporting theory.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized with clear definitions and explanations.
- Proofs are technically dense and may be hard to follow for readers without strong convex optimization/matrix analysis background.
- Experimental scope is limited; more parameter studies and problem sizes would strengthen empirical validation.
- Connection between theoretical assumptions and the experimental setup (e.g., assumptions on h) could be clearer.
- Propositions 4.2 and 4.3 lack intuitive explanation about why their properties hold and how shared singular vectors enable the error bound.
- Discussion of generalization to other matrix norms is brief; expanded treatment of challenges/opportunities would be valuable.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, significant theoretical advance, empirical support, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: technical density, limited experiments, and insufficient intuition for key propositions; overall judged worthy of acceptance with minor revisions (Accept with minor revisions).
