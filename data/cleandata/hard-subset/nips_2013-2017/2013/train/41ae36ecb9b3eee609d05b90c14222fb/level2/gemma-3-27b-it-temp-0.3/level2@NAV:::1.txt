## Review of "On the Linear Convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method for Trace Norm Regularization"

This paper investigates the convergence rate of the Proximal Gradient Method (PGM) for solving trace norm regularized problems, a common setup in various machine learning applications like matrix completion and low-rank approximation. The authors demonstrate linear convergence under relatively mild assumptions – specifically, without requiring strong convexity of the loss function – a notable improvement over existing results. This is achieved through a novel Lipschitzian error bound for the trace norm regularized problem, which itself is a valuable contribution.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the prevalence of trace norm regularization and the limitations of existing sublinear convergence guarantees for PGM when the loss function isn’t strongly convex. The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature, referencing relevant papers on PGM, trace norm minimization, and error bound techniques. The technical presentation is generally strong, with clear definitions and a logical flow. The proof of the error bound, while complex, appears sound and builds upon established techniques, adapting them to the specific challenges posed by the trace norm. The inclusion of numerical experiments demonstrating linear convergence in matrix completion and classification tasks strengthens the empirical validation of the theoretical results.

A strength of this work is the careful handling of the non-polyhedral epigraph of the trace norm, overcoming difficulties present in previous approaches for establishing error bounds. The proposition regarding the spectral properties of optimal solutions (Proposition 4.2) appears crucial and insightful. 

However, the paper could benefit from increased clarity in certain sections. The notation is dense, and while definitions are provided, following the logical steps in the proof of Lemma 4.1 requires significant effort. Expanding on the intuition behind the key steps, particularly in the proof of the error bound, would enhance readability. While the numerical experiments are encouraging, a more detailed discussion of the parameter settings and a comparison to other relevant algorithms would be beneficial. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and significant theoretical result: linear convergence of PGM without strong convexity.
*   Valuable contribution: a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization.
*   Strong technical foundation and rigorous proof.
*   Empirical validation through numerical experiments.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Dense notation and complex proofs may hinder accessibility.
*   Limited discussion of numerical experiment details and comparison to alternatives.

**Overall:** This is a strong paper with a significant theoretical contribution. Despite some challenges in readability, the novelty and importance of the results justify acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand on the numerical experiments.
