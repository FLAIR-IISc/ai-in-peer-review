- Novel contribution: proves linear convergence of proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss.
- Main technical tool: Theorem 3.1 provides a new Lipschitzian error bound critical to the linear-convergence proof.
- Writing and exposition: paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction, background, and motivation.
- Balance of accessibility and rigor: accessible overview for broad audience plus detailed technical proofs for experts.
- Proof quality: thorough, detailed analysis explaining key ingredients and component relationships.
- Numerical validation: well-designed experiments on matrix completion and matrix classification convincingly demonstrate linear convergence.
- Limited scope: results focus on a specific class of loss functions, leaving generalization to other losses unclear.
- Missing comparisons: paper does not compare PGM to other algorithms (e.g., accelerated proximal gradient), which could be relevant.
- Novelty caveat: result builds on previous work and is significant but not entirely unexpected.
- Evaluation scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Acceptance arguments: novel convergence result, clear exposition, rigorous proof, and convincing experiments.
- Rejection arguments: narrow scope, lack of algorithmic comparisons, and limited surprising novelty.
