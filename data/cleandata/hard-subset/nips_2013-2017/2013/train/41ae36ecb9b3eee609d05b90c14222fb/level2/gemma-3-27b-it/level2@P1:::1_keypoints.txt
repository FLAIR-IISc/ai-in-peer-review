- Studies proximal gradient method (PGM) convergence for trace-norm regularized problems f(X)+τ‖X‖∗ and shows linear convergence without requiring strong convexity of f.  
- Main technical contribution is a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularized problems.  
- Result is significant for practical losses (e.g., matrix completion) that are not strongly convex.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and connects to applications like matrix completion and multi-task learning.  
- Technical approach is sound with a clear proof outline and key lemmas/propositions identified.  
- Related work is comprehensive and the paper is well-positioned within existing literature.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate linear convergence in practice and support theoretical claims.  
- Core technical proofs (Lemma 4.1, Propositions 4.2 & 4.3) are primarily in the supplement, making independent assessment harder.  
- Assumption 2.1(b) (strong convexity of h on any convex compact subset) may be strong and deserves discussion on possible relaxation.  
- Theoretical reliance on specific step-size conditions lacks practical guidance on selecting αk in real applications.  
- Numerical evaluation is limited to two problems and the experimental descriptions are somewhat brief.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/significant theory, clear exposition, and supporting numerical evidence.  
- Arguments for rejection: key technical details in supplementary material, potentially restrictive assumptions, and limited experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: move more proof details into the main text, discuss relaxing Assumption 2.1(b), add practical step-size selection guidance, and expand numerical experiments.
