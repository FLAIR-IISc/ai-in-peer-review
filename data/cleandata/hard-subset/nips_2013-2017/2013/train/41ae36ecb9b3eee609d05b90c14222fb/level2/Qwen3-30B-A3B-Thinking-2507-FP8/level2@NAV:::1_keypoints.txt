- Proves linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity.
- Result holds for losses of the form f(X)=h(A(X)) where h meets specified smoothness and convexity conditions.
- Establishes a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization via matrix perturbation analysis and spectral properties.
- Addresses a clear literature gap since prior PGM linear-convergence results required strong convexity.
- The error-bound result could be of independent interest for other optimization problems.
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion, classification) empirically demonstrate the claimed linear convergence.
- Strong field knowledge and appropriate citations; paper is clear and well-organized though some technical proofs could be made more accessible.
- Main limitation: analysis is restricted to the f(X)=h(A(X)) class; extensions to more general losses are not explored.
- Practical implications of the linear rate for specific applications are under-discussed and could be elaborated.
- Overall assessment: original, significant, well-supported contribution — recommend acceptance.
