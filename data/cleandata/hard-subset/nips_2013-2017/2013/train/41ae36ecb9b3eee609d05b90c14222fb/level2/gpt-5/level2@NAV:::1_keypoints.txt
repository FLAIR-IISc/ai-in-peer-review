- Problem: Analysis of proximal gradient methods for trace-norm regularized problems min f(X)+τ||X||*, with f(X)=h(A(X)) and A linear.  
- Main claim 1: PGM enjoys asymptotic linear convergence (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) for a broad class of losses (including square and logistic) without assuming strong convexity.  
- Main claim 2: This follows from a new local Lipschitzian error bound relating distance to the optimum to the proximal residual ||Sτ(X−∇f(X))−X||F.  
- Key proof ingredient: A spectral-structure result showing optimal X* and −∇f(X*) share singular vectors, enabling a tailored perturbation analysis.  
- Convergence derivation: Linear rate follows via the Luo–Tseng framework using the above error bound.  
- Prior work relation: Extends beyond known O(1/k)–O(1/k^2) and strong-convexity linear results by providing an error bound for the non-polyhedral trace norm.  
- Technical quality: Theoretical development is careful and technically sound; Lemma 4.1 and Propositions 4.2–4.3 convincingly address the non-polyhedral hurdle.  
- Algorithmic assumptions: Convergence requires standard step-size conditions.  
- Experiments: Illustrative synthetic experiments show linear regimes but are limited and lack comparisons to alternatives.  
- Clarity: Paper is well organized with clear motivation and assumptions; some crucial inequalities and parts of Proposition 4.3 are deferred to the supplement.  
- Suggested clarity improvement: Provide sketches and explicit constants for deferred inequalities in the main text.  
- Originality: High — the error bound for trace-norm regularization without strong convexity appears new and of independent interest.  
- Significance: Strengthens understanding of PGM for low-rank learning and likely to influence theory and practice, but practical impact is moderated by local/asymptotic nature and unspecified constants.  
- Suggested experiments: Benchmark against APG, ADMM, Frank–Wolfe/SVT on real datasets with wall-clock time.  
- Suggested analysis extensions: Quantify neighborhood/constants in the error bound and discuss detection of entry into the linear regime.  
- Practical robustness suggestion: Discuss inexact proximal steps (truncated SVD) and robustness of the error bound.  
- Assumption clarification: Clarify assumptions on h (e.g., strong convexity on compact sets for logistic) and implications for backtracking.  
- Further extensions: Comment on possible extensions to Schatten norms, KL-based analysis, and stochastic PG.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel error bound for trace norm, clear theoretical advance (linear convergence without strong convexity), broad applicability.  
- Cons against acceptance: Minimal/synthetic experiments, asymptotic/local result with unspecified constants and limited practical guidance, and key proofs relegated to supplement.
