- Main contribution: establishes linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace-norm regularization without assuming strong convexity of the loss.
- Key technical tool: introduces a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm-regularized problems that enables the linear-rate proof.
- Rigorous analysis: provides a thorough, well-written technical analysis of PGM for a class of convex trace-norm problems.
- Clarity and organization: paper is well-organized with a clear introduction and dense but readable technical sections.
- Numerical validation: experiments show linear convergence consistent with theory, but are limited to two specific problems.
- Limitations: analysis targets a specific problem class and assumes a particular loss form, raising questions about generalization to broader settings.
- Discussion of assumptions: authors clearly state assumptions and limitations, helping readers judge applicability.
- Originality: provides a new perspective building on prior work, but scope is somewhat specialized (score 8.5/10).
- Impact: significant potential to influence machine learning and signal processing by improving convergence guarantees (score 9/10).
- Overall quality: high-quality work (score 9/10).
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS 2013; suggest more extensive numerical results and exploration of generalizations to broader problem settings.
