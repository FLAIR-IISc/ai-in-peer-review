

This paper establishes a significant theoretical advance by proving linear convergence of the proximal gradient method (PGM) for trace norm-regularized problems without requiring strong convexity of the loss function. The main claim is that for loss functions of the form f(X) = h(A(X)) where h satisfies certain smoothness and convexity properties, the PGM achieves linear convergence. This is established through a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm regularization, which the authors prove using careful matrix perturbation analysis and spectral properties of the optimal solution set.

The paper's strengths are substantial. The theoretical contribution is original and addresses a clear gap in the literature, as previous linear convergence results for PGM required strong convexity. The error bound result itself could be of independent interest for other optimization problems. The numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification provide empirical support for the theoretical claims, demonstrating linear convergence in practice.

The paper demonstrates strong field knowledge with appropriate citations to relevant work on trace norm regularization and proximal methods. The writing is clear and well-organized, though some technical details in the proof could be made more accessible to a broader audience.

The main limitation is the restricted class of loss functions considered (f(X) = h(A(X))). While this covers many important cases in machine learning, the paper could better discuss whether the techniques could be extended to more general loss functions. Additionally, the practical implications of the linear convergence rate for specific applications could be elaborated.

Overall, this is a high-quality contribution that advances our theoretical understanding of first-order methods for matrix optimization. The work is original, significant, and well-supported. The paper should be accepted as it makes a clear and valuable contribution to the field of optimization for machine learning.
