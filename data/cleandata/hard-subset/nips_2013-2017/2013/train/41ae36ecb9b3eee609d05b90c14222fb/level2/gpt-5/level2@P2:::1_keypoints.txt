- Problem: composite convex optimization F(X)=f(X)+τ||X||* with f=h∘A, h smooth, strictly convex, locally strongly convex on compacts.  
- Main contribution: a local Lipschitzian error bound linking distance to the optimal set to the proximal-gradient residual ||R(X)||F within a level set.  
- Consequence: via Luo–Tseng framework, asymptotic linear convergence of PGM (Q-linear in objective, R-linear in iterates) without assuming strong convexity of f.  
- Key technical tool: Proposition 4.2 — spectral structure showing X̄ and −∇f(X̄) share singular vectors, enabling a matrix-perturbation argument.  
- Technical core: establish a restricted invertibility property from the spectral argument to derive the error bound.  
- Experiments: synthetic matrix completion (squared loss) and matrix classification (logistic loss) show linear behavior near solutions.  
- Novelty: extends error-bound analyses from polyhedral regularizers to the nonpolyhedral nuclear norm; linear PGM rate without f strong convexity appears new.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on Tseng/Luo error-bound analyses and proximal-gradient convergence under such bounds; should be positioned relative to KL-based convergence theory.  
- Technical soundness: assumptions and proofs (simultaneous SVD, perturbation, inequalities for PGM) are plausible and carefully executed.  
- Locality: the error bound and resulting linear rate are local/asymptotic (require ||R(X)||F ≤ ε and bounded level set).  
- Unspecified constants: important constants (η, ε, κi) are not quantified, limiting practical iteration-complexity guidance.  
- Stepsize issue: theory assumes fixed stepsizes bounded away from 0 and <1/Lf, whereas experiments use backtracking—need clarification on ensuring a positive lower bound.  
- Experimental limitations: illustrative, small-scale (n=40, low rank, synthetic), no timing or baseline comparisons.  
- Clarity: well organized with transparent linkage from error bound to convergence; proofs and lemmas are well motivated.  
- Suggested clarifications: state Prop 4.2 with standard nuclear-norm subdifferential, clarify “simultaneous SVD” and block structure, and explain line-search behavior.  
- Minor issues: some references missing years and inconsistent figure numbering.  
- Significance: advances understanding of first-order methods for low-rank regularization; error bound may inform restarts, termination, and analyses of other algorithms.  
- Practical impact: asymptotic nature and lack of explicit constants limit immediate practical utility.  
- Constructive suggestions: quantify dependencies of η and ε on problem data, discuss accelerated PGM with restarts, expand empirical validation (larger n, varying rank, noise, different A), and clarify detection of the linear regime.  
- Pros for acceptance: resolves an open question, technically solid and original spectral argument, clear exposition, applicable to common losses.  
- Cons against acceptance: local/asymptotic result with unspecified constants, minimal small-scale experiments, limited discussion of KL frameworks.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with modest empirical scope; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
