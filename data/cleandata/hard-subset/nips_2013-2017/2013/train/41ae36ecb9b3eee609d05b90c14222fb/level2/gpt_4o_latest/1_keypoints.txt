- Problem addressed: minimizing convex smooth loss with trace norm regularization; authors claim PGM can achieve linear convergence under certain conditions without strong convexity.
- Main contribution: derive a new Lipschitzian error bound for trace norm-regularized problems enabling the linear convergence result.
- Technical novelty: use of matrix perturbation theory to analyze spectral properties and support the error bound.
- Theoretical significance: first linear convergence proof for PGM on trace-norm problems without strong convexity.
- Practical relevance: results applicable to common losses (square loss, logistic loss) and tasks like matrix completion and classification.
- Empirical support: numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification demonstrate observed linear convergence.
- Clarity and positioning: comprehensive related work and well-situated literature review.
- Weakness — experiments: limited empirical validation restricted to two problem settings; broader datasets and scenarios needed.
- Weakness — assumptions: structural/smoothness assumptions on f(X)=h(A(X)) may limit generalizability to other problems.
- Weakness — presentation: dense, complex analysis that may be hard to follow; needs more intuitive explanation of key ideas.
- Weakness — step size: convergence depends on specific step-size conditions; backtracking used in experiments is not theoretically addressed.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify practical implications of step-size constraints.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous theoretical contribution with empirical alignment.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments and potentially inaccessible theoretical exposition.
