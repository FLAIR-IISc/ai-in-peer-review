- Establishes linear convergence for the proximal gradient method (PGM) on trace norm-regularized problems without assuming strong convexity.
- Main contribution is a new Lipschitzian error bound (Theorem 3.1) linking residual norm ‖R(X)‖_F to distance to the optimal solution set.
- Resolves an open question by extending linear convergence results beyond prior strong-convexity settings (e.g., Schmidt et al. 2011).
- Novel use of matrix perturbation theory to handle the spectral structure of trace norm regularization.
- Extends error-bound techniques analogous to ℓ1-norm results to the trace norm, addressing gaps noted by Luo & Tseng and Tseng.
- Technical development is rigorous, well-structured, and carefully proved.
- Lemma 4.1 establishes asymptotic optimality, boundedness of iterates, and restricted invertibility key to the proof.
- Proposition 4.2’s simultaneous SVD of X̄ and -Ḡ gives valuable structural insight into optimal solutions.
- Numerical experiments (matrix completion and classification) empirically demonstrate the claimed linear convergence.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and effectively motivates the problem in the introduction.
- Significance: broadens applicability of PGM to practical problems (e.g., matrix completion) where loss is not strongly convex.
- Theorem 3.1’s error bound may be of independent interest for other matrix optimization problems.
- Minor weakness: could give a more detailed comparison of the error bound to results for other matrix norms/regularizers.
- Minor weakness: numerical experiments lack detailed reporting on parameter selection and computational efficiency.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant; meets NIPS quality criteria and is an important theoretical contribution.
