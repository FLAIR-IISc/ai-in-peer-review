- Studies linear convergence of the Proximal Gradient Method (PGM) for trace-norm regularized problems common in matrix completion and low-rank learning.  
- Main result: proves linear convergence of PGM under mild assumptions without requiring strong convexity of the loss.  
- Key technical contribution: establishes a novel Lipschitzian error bound for trace-norm regularization.  
- Proof technique: adapts the Luo and Tseng framework and uses matrix perturbation theory to handle the non-polyhedral trace-norm epigraph.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and arguments are presented in reasonable detail.  
- Empirical support: brief numerical experiments on matrix completion and classification show linear convergence in practice.  
- Weakness: practical implications and verifiability of assumptions on the linear operator A and smoothness/curvature of h are not fully discussed.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on step-size selection, especially for non-strongly convex cases and in experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant novelty (linear rate without strong convexity), theoretical soundness, and broad potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited discussion of practical applicability of assumptions and lack of experimental step-size details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address practical assumption verification and step-size details.
