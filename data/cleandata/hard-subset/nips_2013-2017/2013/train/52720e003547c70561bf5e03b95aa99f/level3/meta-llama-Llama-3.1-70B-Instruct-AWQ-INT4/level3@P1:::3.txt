The authors propose an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) algorithm for CLIME, which can scale up to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters. The algorithm is designed to take advantage of the column-wise separability of the CLIME estimator, allowing for efficient parallelization.

The paper makes several key contributions. Second, they propose a block-wise solver that can be used in conjunction with the inexact ADMM algorithm, allowing for further parallelization and scalability. Third, they demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on synthetic and real data, showing significant speedups over existing methods.
