- Paper proposes an inexact/linearized ADMM solver for CLIME that reformulates column LPs into column-block problems with closed-form updates.
- Authors prove O(1/T) convergence rates for objective suboptimality and optimality residuals under η ≥ ρ λmax(C)^2.
- They exploit covariance structure (sparse/low-rank C = AA′) to reduce cost of dominant matrix multiplications.
- Implementations include shared-memory and distributed-memory versions using block-cyclic data distribution.
- Experiments compare to CLIME (flare), TIGER, and DC-QUIC, show near-linear scaling to hundreds of cores, and a million-dimensional run in ~11 hours on 400 cores.
- Novelty is practical: a simple column-block ADMM for CLIME combined with HPC-aware data layout and low-rank multiplies, rather than new optimization theory.
- Technical derivation is standard but careful; linearized ADMM yields elementwise soft-thresholding and box projection as expected.
- The O(1/T) rates align with known inexact/linearized ADMM results (e.g., He & Yuan; Wang & Banerjee) under stated assumptions.
- Block-cyclic distribution and low-rank acceleration are appropriate and well-justified for scalability.
- Empirics show compelling scalability but lack sufficient accuracy and fairness details in comparisons.
- For synthetic data, the reviewer requests estimation error metrics (Frobenius, operator, matrix L1) in addition to precision–recall.
- For real data, the reviewer asks for objective values or stability metrics across methods.
- Paper should report parameter selection (λ, ρ, η), stopping criteria, and per-iteration residuals versus wall-clock time.
- Clarify memory footprints and I/O strategy for p = 10^6 (whether Ω̂ is streamed, written in blocks, or compressed).
- Clarify symmetrization/PD post-processing for CLIME and its effect on reported solutions.
- Provide guidance or an autotuning heuristic for choosing column-block size k based on cache, process grid, and sparsity.
- Elaborate convergence theorem assumptions, constants, whether rates are ergodic or non-ergodic, and practical stopping rules.
- Connect the perturbation/thresholding theorem explicitly to Cij thresholding at O(√(log p/n)) and discuss empirical impact.
- Suggestions: report wall-clock vs estimation error and constraint violation, harmonize stopping tolerances across methods, and include QUIC where feasible.
- Implementation suggestions: consider adaptive schemes for ρ and η, provide a rule-of-thumb or autotuning experiment for k, and detail memory usage/output handling at trillion-parameter scale.
- Measure and report breakdowns of time per step (matmul vs elementwise vs communication) and communication volumes to quantify block-cyclic benefits.
- Pros: clear, scalable algorithm using simple primitives; strong scaling on shared/distributed memory; solid convergence analysis.
- Cons: limited accuracy reporting and fairness in experiments; modest novelty in optimization theory; missing practical details on parameter selection, symmetrization, and memory/I/O.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — practically impactful, well-engineered distributed CLIME solver with convincing scalability and theoretical backing, but needs stronger empirical and implementation details.
