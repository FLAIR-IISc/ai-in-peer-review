- Tackles sparse precision matrix estimation at unprecedented scale (millions of variables, trillions of parameters).
- Leverages CLIME’s column-wise separability as the estimation framework.
- Proposes a novel block-wise ADMM solver that exploits sparse and low-rank structures.
- Uses block processing to enable efficient matrix-matrix multiplications and improve performance.
- Employs block cyclic data distribution to optimize memory access and load balancing.
- Introduces an inexact ADMM variant to reduce per-iteration computational cost.
- Provides strong theoretical convergence guarantees (rates for objective and optimality conditions).
- Presents a detailed scalability analysis on shared- and distributed-memory systems with substantial speedups over state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and situates contributions within relevant literature.
- Experimental setup is thorough; analysis of speedup vs. block size and core counts is insightful.
- Considers low-rank structure as an additional practical optimization.
- Weakness: Limited discussion on practical implications and sensitivity of inexact ADMM parameters and their effect on solution accuracy.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation relies heavily on synthetic data; needs broader real-world dataset testing.
- Weakness: Lack of publicly available code limits reproducibility and adoption.
- For acceptance: Addresses a critical scalability need with a novel, theoretically sound, and high-performance approach.
- Against acceptance: Inexactness effects not fully explored, limited real-world validation, and no code release.
- Overall assessment: Strong, significant contribution but reproducibility concerns due to missing code.
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on releasing source code; otherwise reject and encourage resubmission with code.
