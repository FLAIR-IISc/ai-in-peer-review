- Paper: proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence and a distributed framework that scales to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.

Strengths:
- Technical soundness: clear derivations and rigorous convergence guarantees adapting standard techniques to CLIME.
- Scalability: near-linear speedups on distributed-memory architectures for ultra-large problems.
- Parallelization: exploits CLIME structure; heavy work is matrix-matrix multiplication and block-cyclic distribution ensures load balance.
- Originality: novel combination of inexact ADMM with a distributed implementation evaluated on shared and distributed memory.
- Clarity: well-organized, clearly written, with detailed algorithm, implementation, and experimental setup.
- Significance: addresses a key challenge in high-dimensional sparse precision matrix estimation with broad application potential.

Weaknesses:
- Experiment setup: lacks reported accuracy metrics (objective convergence, constraint satisfaction) for each solver.
- Comparative analysis: insufficient discussion of accuracy/statistical trade-offs versus competitors like Tiger and DC-QUIC.
- Limited generalization: framework shown only for CLIME with no concrete examples or experiments on other constrained problems.

Pros:
- Rigorous theory, novel scalable method, clear presentation, and strong empirical runtime/scalability results.

Cons:
- Missing detailed accuracy comparisons, limited applicability beyond CLIME, and partly incremental reliance on standard ADMM techniques.

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions to improve experimental accuracy reporting and comparative analysis.
