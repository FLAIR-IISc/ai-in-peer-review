- Addresses sparse precision matrix estimation in very high dimensions and proposes CLIME-ADMM with a distributed/parallel implementation.  
- Core idea: solve CLIME in column blocks and use block-cyclic data distribution for load-balanced parallel computation.  
- Builds on CLIME and ADMM literature, positioned as a systems-focused scaling of a statistically grounded method.  
- Empirically demonstrates scalability to a million dimensions and compares favorably to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Strength: significant engineering effort yielding impressive scalability and efficient matrix-matrix operations on modern hardware.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation on shared- and distributed-memory architectures using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.  
- Weakness: scattered notation and undefined ADMM parameters (ρ, η); needs a centralized notation table and parameter-setting guidance.  
- Weakness: claim that block-cyclic distribution guarantees load balance/scalability is not theoretically or empirically substantiated.  
- Weakness: column block size k selection appears ad hoc; requires intuition, theoretical guidance, or ablation studies.  
- Weakness: mischaracterized as a “distributed algorithm” without detailing message passing/communication; should clarify as scalable parallel implementation.  
- Minor: contains typos (e.g., "tunning", "summerized") that should be corrected.  
- Overall: valuable contribution advancing large-scale precision matrix estimation, but needs clearer presentation and stronger justification of design choices.
