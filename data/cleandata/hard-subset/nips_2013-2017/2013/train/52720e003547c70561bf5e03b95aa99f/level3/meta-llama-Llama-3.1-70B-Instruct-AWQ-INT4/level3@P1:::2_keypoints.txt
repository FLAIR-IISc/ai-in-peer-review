- Proposal: a large-scale distributed framework using inexact ADMM to solve CLIME by decomposing the problem into matrix multiplications and element-wise operations, with convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions.  
- Implementation: solves CLIME in column-blocks with block-cyclic distribution for load balancing and near-linear scaling with core count.  
- Relation to prior work: extends CLIME and ADMM literature and draws on distributed optimization advances; novelty lies in inexact ADMM + block-cyclic parallelization.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: algorithm and theoretical guarantees are clear and well-presented.  
- Strength — Originality: evaluating ADMM in shared- and distributed-memory settings is novel and yields significant scalability gains.  
- Strength — Importance: addresses an important large-scale sparse precision estimation problem with a simple, robust distributed method.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness — Experimental details: numerical experiments lack sufficient detail (e.g., reported solver accuracies).  
- Weakness — Source of scalability: unclear how much speedup comes from CLIME’s structural simplicity versus the algorithmic innovations.  
- For acceptance: presents a novel, efficient, theoretically justified algorithm with strong empirical scalability.  
- Against acceptance: experimental reporting needs more detail and scalability claims may partly reflect the estimator’s simplicity.  
- Quality: high-quality presentation with solid theoretical and empirical contributions.  
- Clarity: clear exposition and explanations.  
- Originality: interesting and original evaluation of ADMM in distributed settings.  
- Significance: significant contribution for scalable sparse precision matrix estimation; useful for many applications.  
- Overall evaluation: strong contribution that would benefit from more experimental detail but is technically sound and practically valuable.
