- Proposes a scalable framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator.  
- Core contribution: an inexact ADMM algorithm with a proven O(1/T) convergence rate.  
- Develops and evaluates implementations for both shared-memory and distributed-memory systems.  
- Demonstrates scalability up to a million dimensions and near-linear scaling on hundreds of cores.  
- Builds on established CLIME estimator and standard ADMM frameworks; compared against DC-QUIC, Tiger, CLIME-Flare.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly presented.  
- Successfully tackles an important large-scale sparse precision estimation problem.  
- Significant engineering effort yielding practical, scalable implementations.  
- Detailed evaluation of performance trade-offs across architectures is insightful and useful for practitioners.  
- Use of block-cyclic data distribution for load balancing is a valuable, original systems contribution.  
- Final algorithm is simple (element-wise ops and matrix multiplications), making it robust and broadly applicable.  
- Primary weakness: limited algorithmic novelty—derivation is a straightforward application of known optimization techniques.  
- Scalability is largely due to CLIME’s column-wise separability and efficient parallel matrix multiplication, not a fundamental optimization advance.  
- Experimental evaluation overemphasizes runtime and lacks sufficient reporting of solution quality (objective values or errors against ground truth).  
- Additional experimental setup details are needed to improve reproducibility.
