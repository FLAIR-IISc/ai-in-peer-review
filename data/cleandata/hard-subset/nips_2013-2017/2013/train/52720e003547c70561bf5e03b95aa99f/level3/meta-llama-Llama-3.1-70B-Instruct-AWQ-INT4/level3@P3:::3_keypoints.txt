- Tackles large-scale covariance selection via sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator.  
- Proposes a block-wise alternative to column-wise CLIME and a new block-wise solver with an inexact direction method of multipliers.  
- Establishes convergence rates for both objective-value convergence and distance to optimality.  
- Develops scalable parallel implementations for shared- and distributed-memory systems enabling millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real datasets with thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Leverages sparsity and low-rank structure to reduce computation, including proposals for sparse and low-rank multiplications.  
- Scalability analysis shows near-linear speedups with number of cores and across block-size choices.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and connects solidly to related work with accessible notation and exposition.  
- Main concern: source code is not available, limiting reproducibility and impact; reviewer conditions acceptance on release.  
- Potential limitation: focus on CLIME may be narrow compared to other estimators used in practice.  
- Experimental evaluation, though thorough, might not cover all possible scenarios or estimator comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept contingent on releasing source code and encouraged to add further experiments or comparisons with other estimators.
