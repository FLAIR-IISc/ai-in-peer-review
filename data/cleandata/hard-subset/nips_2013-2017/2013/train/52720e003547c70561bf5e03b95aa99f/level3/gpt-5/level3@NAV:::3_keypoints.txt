- Tackles ultra–large-scale sparse precision estimation (millions of variables) by scaling CLIME via block-wise aggregation.  
- Proposes a block-wise inexact ADMM that uses block-cyclic layouts and two matrix multiplications per iteration.  
- Couples elementwise soft/box-thresholding with exploitation of iterate sparsity and low-rank factorization C = AAᵀ to reduce cost from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k).  
- Presents shared- and distributed-memory implementations using OpenMP, MPI, and ScaLAPACK with careful load balancing via block-cyclic distribution.  
- Empirical results show strong runtime and near-linear scaling to 400 cores (p = 10^6, runtime ~11 hours) compared to TIGER, CLIME baselines, and DC-QUIC.  
- Provides theoretical O(1/T) rates for objective suboptimality and optimality residuals under η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)^2, matching inexact ADMM theory.  
- Algorithmic design and convergence analysis are solid, addressing both primal progress and feasibility.  
- Systems engineering (blocking, cache use, load balance) is thoughtful and validated by scalability studies.  
- Experimental evaluation emphasizes runtime over statistical quality; limited metrics beyond a synthetic precision–recall plot.  
- Lacks thorough reporting of estimation error (ℓ1/Frobenius/spectral), held-out likelihood, and post-hoc PD correction for CLIME at scale.  
- Needs clarification on parameter selection (λ, ρ, η), memory footprints, communication cost breakdowns, and symmetrization/PD projection procedures.  
- Paper is well written with clear novelty statements, good links to ADMM/distributed optimization, and understandable pseudocode.  
- Would benefit from a concise complexity/memory table and guidance on choosing block sizes k and block-cyclic tile sizes.  
- Novel combination of block-wise CLIME, inexact ADMM, low-rank and sparsity exploitation, and scalable block-cyclic implementation versus prior solvers.  
- Addressing trillion-parameter regimes is impactful and implementation appears practically valuable.  
- Pros: strong systems contribution, correct convergence guarantees, careful exploitation of structure and parallelism.  
- Cons: limited statistical evaluation and PD handling, reproducibility depends on releasing code, missing ablations on structure exploitation and communication profiling.  
- Recommendation: Accept contingent on releasing source code and scripts, and camera-ready improvements on statistical metrics, PD handling, parameter-tuning guidance, and communication/memory breakdown.
