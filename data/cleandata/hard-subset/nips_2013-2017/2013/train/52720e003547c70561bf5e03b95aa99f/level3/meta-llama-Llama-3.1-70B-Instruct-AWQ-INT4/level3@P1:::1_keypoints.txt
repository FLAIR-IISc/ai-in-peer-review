- Proposes CLIME-ADMM: an inexact ADMM variant for scalable CLIME and a distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Framework applies to both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Provides convergence analysis for objective and optimality residuals.  
- Experimental results compare favorably to existing methods.  
- Demonstrates extreme scalability: estimates a 1M-dimensional precision matrix (1T parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow with clear explanations.  
- Major strength: ability to scale to ultra-high-dimensional problems.  
- Notation is scattered across sections; a notation table is recommended (unclear symbols like ρ and η).  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is proposed but its load-balance/scalability guarantees are not justified or empirically validated.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; lacks intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.  
- Communication/message-passing details are not described, so the method may be better characterized as parallel rather than truly distributed.  
- Novelty: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks instead of column-by-column and empirical analysis using OpenMPI and Scalapak.  
- Quality score: 8/10 — technically sound with thorough analysis and experiments.  
- Clarity score: 8/10 — generally clear but some sections need more explanation and examples.  
- Originality score: 9/10 — presents a novel scalable approach and empirical system contribution.  
- Significance score: 9/10 — strong contribution for large-scale precision matrix estimation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel scalable approach, thorough convergence analysis, strong empirical scalability and system implementation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: notation issues, lack of empirical/theoretical support for block distribution and k selection, and missing communication/ distributed implementation details.
