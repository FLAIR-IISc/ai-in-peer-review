- Paper proposes a column-block inexact/linearized ADMM for CLIME to scale sparse precision estimation to millions of variables.  
- Key innovations: block-wise ADMM enabling BLAS-3 ops, convergence rate guarantees, exploitation of iterate/sample sparsity and low-rank structure (C = A A^T), and shared/distributed implementations with block-cyclic distribution.  
- Empirical results show substantial runtime gains and near-linear scaling to hundreds of cores, including p = 10^6 in ~11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Builds on prior work in sparse inverse covariance estimation (graphical lasso, QUIC), ADMM theory, distributed ML systems, and cache-aware linear algebra.  
- Strength: addresses an important systems bottleneck with convincing engineering and careful parallel/distributed design.  
- Strength: algorithm maps well to modern architectures using BLAS-3/ScaLAPACK and has simple per-iteration steps (soft-threshold, box projection, matmuls).  
- Strength: provides O(1/T) convergence analysis for the inexact ADMM updates under clear conditions.  
- Strength: practical structural insights—iterate sparsity, covariance thresholding justification, and low-rank acceleration when n ≪ p.  
- Strength: extensive, detailed scalability experiments and comparisons to TIGER, CLIME (flare), and DC-QUIC.  
- Weakness: theoretical novelty is limited since O(1/T) rates for linearized/inexact ADMM are standard; need to clarify novel proof elements.  
- Weakness: biggest speedups rely on n ≪ p low-rank trick; unclear competitiveness when n is comparable to p and O(p^2 k) dominates.  
- Weakness/question: how is symmetrization/positive-definiteness handled across column blocks in distributed setting and what is downstream impact?  
- Weakness/question: stopping criteria and tolerances are not fully specified—report exact primal/dual rules and ensure fair matching across baselines.  
- Weakness/question: parameter selection (ρ, η, block size k) is empirical—provide heuristics or auto-tuning guidance considering caches, communication, and sparsity.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 notation (seminorm with I − C^2) is unclear and needs precise restatement and PSD assumption verification.  
- Weakness/question: fairness of baselines—did DC-QUIC and other baselines exploit block structure or optimized solvers beyond flare?  
- Weakness: reproducibility depends on releasing distributed implementation or prototype; recommend code release.  
- Quality: technically sound and well-designed for parallel primitives; tighten some theorem details and reporting.  
- Clarity: generally well-written; suggest clarifying Theorem 2, per-iteration compute/memory/comm costs, symmetrization/PD post-processing, stopping criteria, and k-selection guideline.  
- Originality: main novelty is systems/engineering (column-block ADMM and block-cyclic distribution) rather than new ADMM rates; covariance truncation perturbation is a nice addition.  
- Significance: high practical impact for ultra-large inverse covariance estimation; likely to be adopted by practitioners.  
- Pros for acceptance: solves timely scalability problem, strong empirical scaling, simple primitives, convergence guarantees, and statistical justification for truncation.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, dependence on n ≪ p for gains, missing details on stopping/symmetry/Theorem 2, and reproducibility concerns.  
- Recommendation: weak accept to accept conditioned on releasing implementation or usable prototype.  
- Reviewer confidence: high on systems/scalability assessment; moderate on fine details of convergence proof pending clarification.
