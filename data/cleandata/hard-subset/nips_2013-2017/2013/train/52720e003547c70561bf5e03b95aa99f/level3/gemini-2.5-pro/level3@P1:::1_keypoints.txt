- Problem & contribution: Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable inexact ADMM algorithm and distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation at ultra-large scale (p up to 10^6).

- Key innovation 1: Formulates an inexact ADMM with simple element-wise or matrix-matrix updates and proves O(1/T) convergence for objective and residuals.

- Key innovation 2: Processes precision matrix in column-blocks to leverage high-performance BLAS/ScaLAPACK matrix-matrix routines instead of matrix-vector ops.

- Key innovation 3: Implements and evaluates on shared-memory (OpenMP) and distributed-memory (OpenMPI/ScaLAPACK) systems.

- Strength — significance: Tackles a highly relevant, challenging problem with potential impact in domains like computational biology and climate science.

- Strength — technical quality: Algorithm is technically sound with theoretical convergence guarantees.

- Strength — novelty/practicality: Column-block processing is a simple, effective engineering insight enabling substantial scalability.

- Strength — experiments: Thorough empirical evaluation showing superiority to state-of-the-art solvers, near-linear speedups to 400 cores, and success at p=10^6.

- Weakness — clarity: Presentation issues (e.g., notation such as ρ and η not defined/motivated before use) reduce readability.

- Weakness — distributed claim: Framing as a "distributed framework" overstates novelty since focus is on parallel implementation using existing tools; communication costs/protocols are not detailed.

- Weakness — design justification: No empirical/theoretical support for block-cyclic data distribution choice given evolving sparsity of iterates.

- Weakness — hyperparameter guidance: No guidance or analysis for selecting column-block size k, which strongly affects performance and depends on hardware/problem.

- Minor issues: Several typos (e.g., "tunning" → "tuning") that should be corrected.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a hard problem, provides a practical scalable solver with strong theory and compelling empirical results; column-block idea is impactful.

- Arguments against acceptance: Presentation and justification gaps; contribution leans more toward engineering/implementation than fundamental algorithmic novelty.

- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance, with requested improvements to clarity and more guidance/justification for implementation choices (especially block size k).
