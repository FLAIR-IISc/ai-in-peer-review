- Addresses sparse precision matrix estimation at massive scale (millions of variables, trillions of parameters).
- Focuses on the CLIME estimator and leverages its column-decomposable structure.
- Proposes a novel block-wise inexact ADMM solver, departing from column-by-column methods.
- Exploits matrix-matrix multiplications optimized for sparsity and low-rank structure of the sample covariance.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees (O(1/T) rate).
- Presents a highly scalable parallel implementation for both shared- and distributed-memory systems.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, showing superior performance to state-of-the-art methods.
- Scalability analysis demonstrates near-linear speedups on hundreds of cores.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear exposition and strong background.
- Main weakness: reproducibility and community impact hinge on availability of the complex high-performance implementation.
- Recommendation: strong accept conditional on public release of source code to ensure practical impact and reuse.
