Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes CLIME-ADMM, an inexact ADMM algorithm and parallel/distributed framework for large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation via CLIME. The key ideas are (i) solving CLIME in column blocks rather than column-by-column, enabling matrix–matrix operations and cache-friendly blocking; (ii) exploiting low-rank structure (C = AA′) to reduce multiplication cost; and (iii) deploying a block-cyclic data distribution with OpenMP/OpenMPI/ScaLAPACK. Theoretical results establish O(1/T) convergence of objective and residuals under η ≥ ρλmax(C)2. Experiments compare to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and flare, showing strong scaling to hundreds of cores and a reported 1M-dimensional run in 11 hours. This builds on NIPS work on inverse covariance estimation (e.g., QUIC, DC-QUIC), distributed ADMM (e.g., Parikh & Boyd), and large-scale systems (GraphLab, Hogwild, parameter server).

Quality: The algorithmic derivations are sound and consistent with inexact/linearized ADMM literature. However, practical choices are under-specified: how to set ρ and η (beyond a conservative bound), stopping criteria, and the trade-off between low-rank and sparse multiplies. Claims about load balance from block-cyclic distribution are not supported with communication/load measurements, and the distributed algorithm lacks a communication model or message-passing schedule. Empirical comparisons are promising but would benefit from reporting KKT residuals, communication time breakdowns, and ablations (e.g., effect of k, low-rank vs sparse path).

Clarity: The manuscript would benefit from a notation table early in the paper; several symbols (ρ, η, k) are introduced late or without context. Minor typos: “tunning” → “tuning”, “parameter tunning free” → “parameter tuning free” or simply “tuning-insensitive,” “summerized” → “summarized.”

Originality: Using column-block updates for CLIME to enable high-performance matmuls, and the empirical distributed implementation, are nice practical contributions. The optimization components (inexact ADMM, soft/box operators) are known; novelty is primarily in systems/engineering.

Significance: If reproducible, the scale (p = 10^6) is impressive and relevant to practitioners. Clearer guidance on choosing k and communication-aware design would increase impact.

Pros for acceptance:
- Scalable, practical solver with strong empirical results; uses standard, reliable HPC primitives.
- Clear O(1/T) convergence guarantees for the proposed inexact ADMM variant.
- Useful engineering ideas (column-blocking, low-rank multiplications).

Cons for acceptance:
- Insufficient description/evidence of distributed communication, load balance, and scalability guarantees.
- Ad hoc choice of column-block size k; no theory or practical heuristic.
- Clarity/notation issues; minor typos.

Suggestions:
- Add a notation table; define ρ, η, k at first use; provide tuning guidance.
- Provide a communication model (per-iteration message counts/bytes), overlap strategy, and load-balance metrics (per-process flops/idle time).
- Give intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k (e.g., cache/roofline analysis) and report sensitivity.
- Report KKT residuals and time breakdown (compute vs comm) and release code/scripts for reproducibility.
