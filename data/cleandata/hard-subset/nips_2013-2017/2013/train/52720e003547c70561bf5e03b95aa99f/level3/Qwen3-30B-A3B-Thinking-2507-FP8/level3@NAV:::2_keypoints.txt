- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions.  
- Develops a distributed framework that scales to million-dimensional precision matrices via column-block processing and block-cyclic data distribution.  
- Leverages dense matrix–matrix multiplications that parallelize effectively across architectures.  
- Builds on prior ADMM-based precision-matrix methods [18,23,26] but targets ultra-large-scale settings.  
- Strong theoretical analysis with properly established convergence proofs following standard inexact-ADMM derivations.  
- Novel evaluation across both shared-memory and distributed-memory systems, showing near-linear scalability up to 400 cores.  
- Empirical result: estimates a 1 million-dimensional precision matrix in ~11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Clear presentation and practical implementation details for parallelization.  
- Significant practical contribution to distributed optimization for large-scale statistical problems.  
- Weakness: numerical experiments lack sufficient accuracy metrics for each solver (beyond runtime).  
- Weakness: scalability gains are largely due to CLIME’s structure, limiting claims of algorithmic novelty.  
- Overall: technically sound and valuable for practitioners despite modest core algorithmic innovation.
