Proposes CLIME-ADMM: an inexact ADMM variant and distributed framework for scalable sparse precision matrix estimation.

Establishes convergence rates for both the objective and optimality conditions.

Solves CLIME in column-blocks using a block-cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing.

Scales to millions of dimensions and thousands of cores; experiments show near-linear scaling and outperforming state-of-the-art.

Empirical implementation and analysis use OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.

Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of algorithm and framework.

Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; a notation table (e.g., clarifying ρ and η) is needed.

Block-cyclic distribution claim lacks theoretical explanation and empirical evidence for load balance/scalability.

Choice of column block size k is ad hoc and needs intuition or theoretical guidance.

Algorithm may be better characterized as parallel rather than distributed since message-passing/communication details are not described.

Minor typos and wording errors (e.g., "tunning", "parameter tunning free", "summerized").

Novelty: column-block estimation and large-scale OpenMPI/ScaLAPACK experiments are strengths, but relation to prior work and concrete advancement over existing methods need more discussion.

Overall: technically sound and significant contribution, but clarity and justification gaps remain.

Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.

Arguments for acceptance: scalable CLIME variant, theoretical rates, strong experimental scalability.

Arguments against acceptance: scattered notation, unsupported block-cyclic claims, ad hoc k selection, unclear distributed vs parallel characterization.
