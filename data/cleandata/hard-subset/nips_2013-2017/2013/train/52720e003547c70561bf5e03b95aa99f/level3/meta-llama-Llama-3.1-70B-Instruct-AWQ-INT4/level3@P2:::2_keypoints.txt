- Proposes a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator.  
- Framework uses an inexact ADMM algorithm that decomposes the constrained problem into matrix multiplications and element-wise operations.  
- Authors derive convergence rates for both the objective and optimality conditions.  
- Demonstrates scalability on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Technical derivation is sound and follows standard inexact ADMM techniques.  
- CLIME’s structure enables component-wise subproblem solves; main computation is highly parallelizable matrix-matrix multiplication.  
- Evaluation of ADMM in shared- and distributed-memory environments is interesting and original.  
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and provides thorough background and algorithmic description.  
- Numerical experiments are well-designed and compare against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Experimental setup lacks details such as reported accuracy per solver.  
- Paper should provide more insight into hyperparameter choices and sensitivity analyses.  
- Main contribution is a simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision estimation.  
- Novelty is limited—algorithm builds on existing techniques—though the implementation/evaluation is valuable.  
- Significance is high due to relevance for large-scale machine learning applications.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address experimental detail and hyperparameter concerns.  
- Reviewer score: 8/10.
