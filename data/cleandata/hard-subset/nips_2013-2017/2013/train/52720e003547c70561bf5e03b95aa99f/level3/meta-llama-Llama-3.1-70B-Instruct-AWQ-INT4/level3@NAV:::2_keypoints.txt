- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence.
- Implements the algorithm in both shared-memory and distributed-memory systems.
- Evaluates performance against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Derivation is standard; CLIME’s structure allows componentwise subproblem solves, making ADMM highly parallelizable.
- Main computational cost is matrix-matrix multiplication, which parallelizes effectively.
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and easy to follow.
- Evaluation of ADMM in shared- and distributed-memory environments is interesting and original.
- Tackles an important problem: large-scale sparse precision estimation; a simple, robust distributed algorithm is useful.
- Scalability gains largely arise from CLIME’s simple structure rather than algorithmic novelty.
- Numerical experiment setup lacks sufficient detail (e.g., reported accuracy per solver).
- Strengths: novel inexact ADMM with convergence rate, dual implementations, SOTA comparisons, clear writing.
- Weaknesses: insufficient experimental detail and reliance on CLIME structure for scalability.
- Arguments for acceptance: new algorithmic result with implementations and comparisons that broaden applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: reproducibility concerns and limited applicability to more complex problems.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address missing experimental details.
