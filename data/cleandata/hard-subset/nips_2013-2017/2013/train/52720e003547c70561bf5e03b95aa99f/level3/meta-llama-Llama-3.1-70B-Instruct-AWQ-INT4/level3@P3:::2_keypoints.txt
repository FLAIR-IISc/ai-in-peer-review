- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME to scale sparse precision estimation to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores.  
- Authors prove convergence rates for both objective value and optimality conditions.  
- Algorithm structure enables componentwise subproblem solves, making it highly parallelizable.  
- Main computational cost is matrix-matrix multiplication, which parallelizes well.  
- Derivation is technically sound but largely follows standard inexact ADMM approaches (e.g., [26]).  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on shared- and distributed-memory systems, compared to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Experimental results show substantially better scalability than baselines and near-linear scaling with cores.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with an interesting and original evaluation in distributed environments.  
- Numerical experiment reporting lacks some details (e.g., accuracy achieved by each solver).  
- Scalability gains may primarily reflect CLIME’s simple structure and might not generalize to more complex problems.  
- Originality stems from a novel combination of familiar techniques (inexact ADMM applied to CLIME) with proper related-work placement.  
- Significant practical impact for large-scale sparse precision estimation and related ML/data-analysis applications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve numerical experiment details.
