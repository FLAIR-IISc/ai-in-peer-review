- Proposes CLIME-ADMM: a scalable column-block ADMM variant for sparse precision matrix estimation targeting n << p settings.  
- Implements a distributed/parallel framework with block-cyclic data distribution to scale to very large problems.  
- Demonstrates impressive empirical scalability, including experiments on a 1 million dimension problem using 400 cores.  
- Leverages column-block operations to enable efficient matrix computations and better hardware utilization.  
- Uses OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK in empirical evaluation, showing practical implementation effort.  
- Provides convergence analysis for CLIME-ADMM with O(1/T) theoretical rate.  
- Discusses exploiting sparse and low-rank structure for further performance gains.  
- Notation is unclear and inconsistent (symbols like ρ and η undefined); a notation table is strongly recommended.  
- Choice of column block size k is ad hoc with no theoretical guidance or clear intuition provided.  
- The algorithm is characterized as “distributed” but lacks description of message-passing/communication, appearing closer to a parallel distributed-memory implementation.  
- Claim that block-cyclic distribution guarantees load balance and scalability is insufficiently justified theoretically or empirically.  
- Several minor writing and typo errors (e.g., “tunning”, “summerized”) reduce polish.  
- Overall technically sound and reproducible in parts, but missing details hinder full reproducibility without significant effort.  
- Novel combination of CLIME, ADMM, column-blocks, and distributed implementation is valuable, though comparisons to other parallel sparse estimation methods are limited.  
- Potentially significant for ultra-high-dimensional applications (genomics, finance, sensor networks) if scalability claims and implementation details are clarified.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions to clarify notation, justify design choices (k and load balancing), and accurately describe the distributed framework.
