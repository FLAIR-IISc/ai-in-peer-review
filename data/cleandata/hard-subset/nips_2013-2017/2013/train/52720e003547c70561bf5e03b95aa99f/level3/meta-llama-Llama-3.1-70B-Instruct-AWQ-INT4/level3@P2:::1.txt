This paper proposes a scalable CLIME variant (CLIME-ADMM) and a distributed/parallel framework for sparse precision matrix estimation. The authors establish rates of convergence for both the objective and optimality conditions and demonstrate the scalability of their algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.

The problem of sparse precision matrix estimation is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear overview of the existing literature. The CLIME estimator is a suitable choice for this problem, given its strong statistical guarantees and computational advantages. The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm is an interesting contribution, as it leverages the separable structure of the CLIME estimator to solve the problem in column-blocks.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear description of their algorithm and its implementation. However, there are some minor writing issues, such as the use of "tunning" instead of "tuning" and "parameter tunning free" instead of "tuning". Additionally, the notation is scattered across Sections 1-3, and a notation table would be helpful to clarify the meaning of symbols like ρ and η.

The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the scalability of CLIME-ADMM on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. The comparison with existing algorithms, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare, is also useful. However, the choice of column block size k is ad hoc, and the authors could provide more intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.

One of the key contributions of the paper is the use of block-cyclic data distribution, which is claimed to guarantee load balance and scalability. However, the authors do not provide empirical evidence to support this claim, and it is unclear why this distribution scheme is superior to other approaches.

The algorithm is characterized as a distributed algorithm, but it is unclear what message passing or communication mechanisms are used to facilitate the parallel computation. The authors may want to clarify this point or consider recharacterizing the algorithm as a parallel implementation.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors demonstrate a clear understanding of the problem and the existing literature. The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm is an interesting contribution, and the experimental results are impressive. However, there are some minor issues that need to be addressed to improve the clarity and coherence of the paper.
