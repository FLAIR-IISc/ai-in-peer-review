- Problem and approach: Proposes an inexact ADMM scheme for large-scale sparse precision estimation with O(1/T) convergence and shared/distributed implementations using column-block updates and block-cyclic distribution.  
- Implementation: Reduces ADMM iterations to elementwise operations plus matrix-matrix multiplications (GEMMs), which dominate cost and parallelize/cache well.  
- Positioning: Compared and positioned against CLIME, QUIC/DC-QUIC, and distributed ADMM frameworks, emphasizing CLIME’s separability for parallel column-block solves.  
- Empirical claims: Demonstrates near-linear scaling on hundreds of cores and a 10^6-dimensional run, and compares to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Technical quality: Algorithmic derivation is standard but correct; the inexact ADMM linearization/proximalization and O(1/T) rate match known results.  
- Systems design: Mapping CLIME to highly parallelizable kernels and the shared vs distributed designs are sound and thoughtfully engineered.  
- Experimental weaknesses: Lacks critical details for validation—stopping criteria, target accuracies/KKT residuals per solver, and exact stopping tolerances.  
- Statistical evaluation missing: Does not report statistical accuracy at matched regularization/graph sparsity, precision/recall at matched FPR, or λ-selection protocol.  
- Resource reporting missing: Omits communication volumes, memory footprints, and wall-clock vs accuracy trade-offs.  
- Attribution of gains: Much of scalability benefit stems from CLIME’s structure and use of GEMMs rather than novel algorithmic advances.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized and readable; algorithmic steps and systems choices are clearly explained.  
- Originality: The inexact ADMM itself is not novel, but the end-to-end engineered evaluation across shared and distributed settings is an original, practical contribution.  
- Significance: Solving ultra-large sparse precision problems is important and a robust distributed CLIME solver would be useful to practitioners.  
- Suggestions: Report per-method accuracy (precision/recall, KKT residuals), stopping tolerances, λ-selection, strong/weak scaling, communication and memory usage, and wall-clock vs accuracy plots.  
- Additional suggestions: Clarify post-CLIME handling of symmetry/positive-definiteness and effect of covariance perturbations; compare at matched accuracy to QUIC/graphical lasso baselines.  
- Recommendation summary: Pros—strong systems contribution, reproducible building blocks, practical impact; Cons—limited optimization novelty, insufficient experimental detail, and scalability largely due to implementation choices; overall weak accept.
