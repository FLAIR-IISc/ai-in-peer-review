- Introduces CLIME-ADMM: a scalable algorithm and distributed framework for estimating sparse precision matrices at ultra-large scale.
- Targets problems with millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters by adapting the CLIME estimator.
- Combines an inexact ADMM with a novel column-block decomposition and block-cyclic data distribution for parallel computation.
- Positions the work within prior literature (ADMM, MapReduce) and addresses limitations of methods that cannot load full covariance matrices.
- Column-block processing and block-cyclic distribution are presented as key contributions to handle memory and computation constraints.
- Provides convergence rate analysis for CLIME-ADMM, demonstrating technical soundness.
- Empirical evaluation shows near-linear speedup on shared- and distributed-memory systems, validating scalability claims.
- Compares favorably to existing methods (DC-QUIC, Tiger, flare) and exploits low-rank structure to improve efficiency.
- Notation is scattered across sections, reducing readability; a dedicated notation table would help.
- Insufficient theoretical justification for why the block-cyclic scheme guarantees load balance and scalability.
- Claims about minimizing cache misses and improving memory-hierarchy utilization lack supporting empirical evidence.
- Choice of column block size k appears arbitrary; paper lacks intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.
- Description of the distributed framework underexplains communication mechanisms and message-passing details.
- Implementation reads more like a parallel algorithm in a distributed setting rather than a fully distributed algorithm with complex inter-process communication.
- Minor writing/editing issues (e.g., “tunning” → “tuning”) need correction.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong scalability, technical soundness, novelty in decomposition/distribution, and solid empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical justification for block-cyclic and k selection, limited distributed-detail, and readability problems.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution with strong empirical results; accept with minor revisions to justify block-cyclic design, guide k selection, and clarify distributed implementation.
