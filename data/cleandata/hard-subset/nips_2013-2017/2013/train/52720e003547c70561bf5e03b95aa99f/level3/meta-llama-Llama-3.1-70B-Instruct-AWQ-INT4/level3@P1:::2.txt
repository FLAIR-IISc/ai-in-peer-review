**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME (Constrained L1-minimization for Inverse Matrix Estimation) estimator. The framework is based on an inexact Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) algorithm, which decomposes the constrained optimization problem into elementary matrix multiplications and element-wise operations. The authors establish convergence rates for both the objective and optimality conditions. The proposed framework solves CLIME in column-blocks and uses block cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing. Experimental results show that the algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods and scales almost linearly with the number of cores.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on sparse precision matrix estimation, including the CLIME estimator [3] and the ADMM algorithm [2]. The authors also draw inspiration from recent advances in distributed optimization and machine learning [29, 22, 6, 7, 20, 2, 23, 9]. The proposed framework is distinct from existing methods in its use of inexact ADMM and block cyclic distribution, which enables efficient parallelization and scalability.

**Strengths**

1. **Technical soundness**: The paper is technically sound, with a clear and well-organized presentation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical guarantees.
2. **Originality**: The evaluation of ADMM in shared- and distributed-memory environments is interesting and original, and the scalability gains achieved by the proposed framework are significant.
3. **Importance**: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation is an important problem, and a simple, robust distributed algorithm is useful for many applications.
4. **Clarity**: The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical guarantees.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of detail in numerical experiments**: The paper could benefit from more detail in the numerical experiment setup, such as the reported accuracy achieved by each solver.
2. **Scalability gains**: While the proposed framework achieves significant scalability gains, it is not entirely clear how much of this is due to the simplicity of the CLIME estimator's structure, rather than the specific algorithmic innovations.
