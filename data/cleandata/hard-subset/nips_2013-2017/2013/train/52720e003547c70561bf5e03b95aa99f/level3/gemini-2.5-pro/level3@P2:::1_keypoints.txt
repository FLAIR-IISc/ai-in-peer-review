- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable inexact ADMM for sparse precision matrix estimation targeting ultra-large-scale problems (p up to 10^6).
- Key scalability idea: process the precision matrix in column-blocks to convert repeated matrix-vector ops into efficient matrix-matrix multiplications.
- Implements a parallel/distributed framework (OpenMP, OpenMPI, ScaLAPACK) with block-cyclic data distribution for shared- and distributed-memory architectures.
- Proves an O(1/T) convergence rate for the algorithm.
- Empirically demonstrates near-linear speedups on up to 400 cores and outperforms state-of-the-art solvers (flare/CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC).
- Achieves an impressive empirical result: estimation of a one-trillion-parameter precision matrix.
- Strength: addresses a highly significant, challenging problem and extends feasibility to unprecedented problem sizes.
- Strength: novel and clever use of column-block processing to exploit matrix-matrix multiplication efficiency.
- Strength: solid technical quality with formal convergence analysis and extensive, convincing experiments.
- Strength: effectively leverages problem structure (low-rank C = AAT and sparsity) to accelerate computation.
- Weakness: description of distributed aspects is thin—communication patterns, message passing, and synchronization are not detailed.
- Weakness: no guidance or analysis for choosing the critical column-block size k; discussion is purely empirical.
- Weakness: claim that block-cyclic distribution "largely achieves load balance" lacks clear justification or comparative evidence.
- Weakness: notation and presentation clarity issues (undeclared parameters ρ and η); several minor typos exist.
- Argument for acceptance: dramatically advances scalability for sparse precision estimation with novel algorithmic idea and strong results.
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient detail on distributed algorithm design and practical guidance on key hyperparameters.
- Overall conclusion: strong, technically sound paper with addressable clarity/design-choice weaknesses; recommends acceptance.
