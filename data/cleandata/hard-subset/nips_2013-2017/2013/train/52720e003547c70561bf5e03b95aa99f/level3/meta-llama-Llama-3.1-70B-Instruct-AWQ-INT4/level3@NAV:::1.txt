This paper proposes a scalable variant of the CLIME estimator, CLIME-ADMM, and a distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation. The authors establish rates of convergence for both the objective and optimality conditions and demonstrate the scalability of their algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear overview of the problem, the proposed solution, and the experimental results. The authors have done a good job in positioning their work within the context of previous research in the field, including the CLIME estimator and other distributed optimization methods.

The proposed algorithm, CLIME-ADMM, is an inexact alternating direction method of multiplier (ADMM) algorithm that solves the CLIME estimator in column-blocks instead of column-by-column. The authors show that this approach can significantly reduce the computational complexity of the algorithm and make it more scalable.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in terms of scalability and accuracy. The authors compare their algorithm with other state-of-the-art methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare, and show that CLIME-ADMM is more scalable and achieves better performance on large-scale problems.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved:

* The notation is scattered across Sections 1-3, and a notation table would be helpful to clarify the meaning of symbols like ρ and η.
* The block-cyclic data distribution is an interesting approach, but it is unclear why it guarantees load balance or scalability. More empirical evidence or theoretical analysis would be helpful to support this claim.
* The column block size k is chosen ad hoc, and more intuition or theoretical guidance would be helpful to select k.
* The algorithm may be better characterized as a parallel implementation rather than a distributed algorithm, since message passing/communication mechanisms are not described.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of sparse precision matrix estimation. With some minor revisions to address the above points, it has the potential to be a high-quality publication.
