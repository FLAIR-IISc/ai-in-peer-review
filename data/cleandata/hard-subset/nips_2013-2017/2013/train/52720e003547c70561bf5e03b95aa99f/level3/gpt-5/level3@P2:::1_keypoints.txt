- Paper proposes an inexact/linearized ADMM for CLIME with simple per-iteration closed-form proximal steps and BLAS-3 matrix multiplications.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence for objective gap and KKT residuals under standard η assumptions.  
- Systems contributions: column-block processing, exploiting low-rank sample covariance C = A A′ to reduce matmul cost, and block-cyclic data distribution in a distributed-memory framework.  
- Implementation built on commodity libraries (OpenMP/MPI/ScaLAPACK) and shows near-linear scaling, including p = 10^6 in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Builds on prior work: CLIME theory, ADMM/inexact ADMM literature, cache-aware blocking and distributed ADMM frameworks.  
- Strength: clear, implementable algorithm with closed-form steps and efficient BLAS-3 matmuls.  
- Strength: formal convergence guarantees for both objective and optimality residuals.  
- Strength: sensible exploitation of problem structure (column-blocking, low-rank C, potential sparsity).  
- Strength: practical distributed framework with block-cyclic layout and parallel I/O.  
- Strength: strong empirical scaling and comparisons to relevant baselines (TIGER, flare ADMM, DC-QUIC).  
- Weakness: notation and clarity issues—symbols introduced late/ambiguously and missing definitions; algorithm needs explicit dimensions.  
- Weakness: distributed algorithm section is under-specified; missing per-iteration communication counts, synchronization points, and overlap details.  
- Weakness: load-balance claims are anecdotal; lacks experiments comparing block-cyclic vs large-block layouts and reporting imbalance metrics.  
- Weakness: column-block size k selection is ad hoc; needs heuristics or auto-tuning guidance related to grids, block sizes, caches, and sparsity.  
- Weakness: evaluation emphasizes runtime but omits accuracy-vs-time metrics like held-out NLL or estimation error traces across methods.  
- Weakness: perturbation/sparsification analysis (Theorem 3) is non-constructive; needs concrete truncation thresholds and empirical validation on graph recovery/error.  
- Weakness: algorithmic novelty is limited—main novelty is systems engineering; paper should explicitly position this relative to prior ADMM solvers.  
- Technical soundness: inexact ADMM updates and convergence claims are reasonable, proofs OK for supplement, but distribution/load claims need quantitative support.  
- Empirics: scaling is compelling but should include compute/communication breakdowns and systematic studies of k and block-size effects.  
- Clarity: generally readable but needs notation consolidation, variable definitions on first use, typo fixes, and clearer Algorithm 1.  
- Originality: moderate—linearized ADMM is known; contributions lie in column-block matmul use, distributed framework, and low-rank exploitation.  
- Significance: high practical impact for enabling CLIME at million-scale; methodological novelty is incremental but systems contribution is valuable.  
- Key author questions: how are ρ and η set in practice; per-iteration communication cost for W=A′X and U=AW; handling of tail blocks when k not multiple of kb; symmetry exploitation for Ω; report weak scaling and compute/communication breakdown.  
- Pros for acceptance: important scalability problem, solid guarantees, sensible low-rank exploitation, and strong empirical scale.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited optimization novelty, under-specified distributed analysis, and evaluation focused more on runtime than statistical accuracy.  
- Overall recommendation: 7 (accept) with minor revisions to bolster distributed analysis, clarity, and accuracy-vs-time evaluation.
