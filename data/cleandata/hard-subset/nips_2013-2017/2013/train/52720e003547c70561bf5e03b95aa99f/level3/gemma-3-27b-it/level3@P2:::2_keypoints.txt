- Presents an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME with a scalable distributed framework that solves column-blocks and uses optimized matrix-matrix multiplications for cache efficiency.
- Demonstrates ability to handle problems with millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters, scaling to hundreds of cores.
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis with rate O(1/T).
- Builds on recent statistical and optimization literature and situates the contribution well among relevant prior work.
- Technical soundness: ADMM derivation and convergence analysis are well-supported by existing literature.
- Scalability: primary strength is demonstrated large-scale performance (e.g., million-dimension problems on ~400 cores).
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with consistent notation and accessible explanations.
- Originality: novel combination of inexact ADMM, column-block formulation, and block-cyclic distributed computation optimized for caches.
- Significance: addresses an important problem with practical impact across domains (bioinformatics, finance, signal processing).
- Weakness — experimental detail: lacks accuracy metrics (e.g., precision/recall or estimation error) and full parameter settings for reproducibility.
- Weakness — incremental gain: unclear how much improvement arises from algorithmic innovations versus inherent CLIME structure; ablation studies suggested.
- Weakness — limited comparison depth: comparisons focus on runtime and miss deeper trade-off analyses (e.g., Tiger’s higher recall vs other costs).
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses critical scalability need, is technically sound, demonstrates impressive performance, and is clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental evaluation incomplete, specific innovation benefits not fully quantified, and comparisons need more depth.
- Overall assessment: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to include accuracy metrics and more thorough analysis of algorithmic contributions.
