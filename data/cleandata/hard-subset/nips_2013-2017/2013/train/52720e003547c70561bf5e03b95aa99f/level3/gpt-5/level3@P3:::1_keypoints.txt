- Paper proposes a scalable inexact ADMM for CLIME (CLIME-ADMM) with column-block updates, linearized X-update, and exploitation of C = A A′ structure for p ≫ n.  
- The method solves k columns at a time to leverage BLAS-3 matrix–matrix operations instead of column-by-column updates.  
- The ADMM X-update is linearized to yield a simple soft-thresholding proximal step.  
- When p ≫ n the authors compute CX via A(A′X) to reduce FLOPs and memory.  
- They prove standard O(1/T) rates for objective suboptimality and residuals under η ≥ ρ λmax(C)^2.  
- Implementations include shared-memory (OpenMP) and distributed-memory (OpenMPI + ScaLAPACK) versions with block-cyclic distribution.  
- Experiments compare against DC-QUIC, TIGER, and a CLIME variant on synthetic and real data, showing scaling to p = 10^6 on 400 cores.  
- Novelty is primarily in systems/algorithm-engineering (column-block CLIME mapping to GEMM) rather than new theory.  
- Strength: practical scalability design trading column-wise separability for GEMM-friendly blocks and leveraging low-rank structure.  
- Strength: clean per-iteration structure (elementwise proximal steps + two matrix multiplications) amenable to HPC libraries.  
- Strength: empirical head-to-head comparisons with precision–recall and wall-clock analyses; useful speedup metrics.  
- Strength: realistic implementation choices (OpenMPI, ScaLAPACK, parallel I/O, block-cyclic distribution).  
- Strength: key practical insight reducing FLOPs from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k) when p ≫ n.  
- Weakness: notation and definitions missing or scattered (ρ, η, scaled dual Y); recommend a consolidated notation table and consistent factors.  
- Weakness: column block size k chosen ad hoc in experiments without heuristic or guidance; request rule-of-thumb tied to cache, ScaLAPACK nb, process grid, sparsity, and bandwidth.  
- Weakness: block-cyclic distribution claims lack empirical evidence—need per-process utilization, runtime variance, communication volume, and comparisons to other distributions.  
- Weakness: communication pattern for distributed claims is unspecified; ask for explicit message-passing description, cost model (latency/bandwidth), and scaling with r×c grids and k.  
- Weakness: parameter selection and reproducibility unclear—how ρ and η are chosen, how λmax(C) is estimated at scale, and how λ is tuned to match sparsity.  
- Weakness: statistical handling under low-rank/approximate C is underexplored—address symmetrization, PD handling, and effects on statistical accuracy.  
- Weakness: empirical validation for “near-linear” scaling incomplete—add strong-scaling efficiency, compute vs communication vs I/O breakdowns, and per-node memory footprints.  
- Weakness: writing and typos; suggest reorganizing notation and fixing minor errors.  
- Quality: technically sound; derivations and rates are standard; main gaps are clarity on parameters, communication analysis, and load-balance validation.  
- Clarity: generally readable but notation scattered and Algorithm 1 underspecified; systems section needs concrete communication cost modeling.  
- Originality: moderate—practical column-block CLIME mapping to BLAS-3 and large-scale implementation are useful but theoretical contributions are not novel.  
- Significance: high on systems/practice for ultra-high-dimensional precision estimation; approach enables previously infeasible scales.  
- Key questions for authors: provide principled k selection heuristic, explicit communication patterns and costs for GEMMs, choices and estimation of ρ and η (and λmax(C)), memory/I/O footprint for 1M run, and details on symmetrization/PD handling.  
- Pros for acceptance: compelling large-scale demonstrations, clear engineering mapping to HPC kernels, and relevance to scalable ML community.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient communication/load-balance evidence, ad hoc parameter/k choices limiting reproducibility, and notation/clarity issues.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on stronger systems analysis (communication/load balance), clearer parameter choices and notation, and additional empirical evidence for block-cyclic benefits.
