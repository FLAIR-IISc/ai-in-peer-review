- Proposes a scalable CLIME variant using linearized (inexact) ADMM with a column-block formulation and distributed block-cyclic implementation.  
- Reformulates CLIME with a split variable, uses soft-thresholding and box projections, and replaces the heavy X-update by a proximal-linearized step needing two matrix multiplications per iteration.  
- Provides O(1/T) rates for objective suboptimality and KKT residuals under η ≥ ρ·λmax(C)^2 and shows iterate sparsity and low-sample low-rank structure reduce cost.  
- Distributed framework (OpenMP/OpenMPI + ScaLAPACK) solves multiple columns jointly (block size k), claims better cache/memory use and employs block-cyclic data distribution.  
- Experiments show near-linear speedups and a 1M-dimensional run on 400 cores in ~11 hours.  
- Situates work relative to QUIC/DC-QUIC (graphical lasso) and prior CLIME solvers; linearized ADMM analysis follows known results but is specialized here.  
- Block-cyclic distribution leverages classical dense linear algebra ideas and is connected to parameter-server/GraphLab/GPBS and distributed ADMM discussions.  
- Theory: non-ergodic O(1/T) rates and residual bounds are reasonable with explicit assumptions; perturbation result is interesting but not applied in experiments.  
- Theory critique: more practical guidance on constants (e.g., η, ρ) and integration of the perturbation result into practice are needed.  
- Algorithmic soundness: column-block formulation is faithful and enables BLAS-3, but practical details are underspecified (ρ/η selection, stopping criteria, symmetrization).  
- Empirics: strong scaling and large-scale runs are convincing, but accuracy reporting is limited (only precision–recall on synthetics; no residuals or convergence traces).  
- Empirics critique: lack of convergence/optimality traces, constraint residuals, estimation errors, and ablations for block-cyclic vs alternative layouts.  
- Clarity: presentation needs a consolidated notation table; symbols appear before definition and some operators are redundantly defined.  
- Distributed description reads high-level; message-passing schedules, collective algorithms used, and communication/computation breakdowns are missing.  
- Minor typos and wording errors (e.g., "tunning", "summerized", "achetectures", "reguarlized") need correction.  
- Originality: method is incremental (linearized ADMM + block-columnization) but originality lies in the large-scale block-cyclic implementation and avoiding explicit C via low-rank exploitation.  
- Significance: demonstrates CLIME at massive scale with near-linear speedups and reusable design patterns (elementwise ops + GEMM), but needs real case studies and statistical accuracy evidence.  
- Specific request: provide k-selection guidelines (cache/GEMM considerations) and sensitivity of speedups to k and sparsity patterns.  
- Specific request: quantify communication cost/latency per iteration, overlap with computation, and compare block-cyclic to other distributions.  
- Specific request: explain practical choice/adaptation of ρ and η, stopping thresholds, and typical iteration counts.  
- Specific request: report KKT residuals vs time, constraint violation ‖CX − Z‖∞, estimation errors on synthetics, and symmetrization/post-processing details.  
- Specific request: clarify symmetrization/PD projection steps used for CLIME outputs when solving in blocks.  
- Suggestion: release code and scripts to improve reproducibility.  
- Pros: impressive scalability to p=10^6, simple elementwise+GEMM algorithm, low-rank exploitation, and tailored convergence guarantees.  
- Cons: insufficient detail on distributed communication and load balancing, lack of guidance/auto-tuning for k and ADMM parameters, under-reported accuracy metrics, and readability/notation issues.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying distributed design, adding k/parameter guidance and sensitivity analyses, and expanding accuracy/convergence reporting.
