- Tackles sparse precision matrix estimation at massive scale (millions of variables, trillions of parameters).  
- Focuses on the CLIME estimator and proposes a block-wise alternative to improve computational efficiency.  
- Main algorithmic contribution: a novel block-wise solver paired with an inexact ADMM.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis establishing convergence rates.  
- Demonstrates a scalable parallel implementation for both shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and real data validate strong performance and near-linear speedup with increasing cores.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear statement of novelty and balanced technical/theoretical exposition.  
- Properly situates the work within prior sparse precision and scalable ML literature and distinguishes its approach.  
- Strengths: important problem, well-motivated and technically sound solution, solid theoretical guarantees, and comprehensive scalability/experimental analysis.  
- Primary weakness: absence of publicly available code, limiting reproducibility and practical impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant scalability, strong theoretical foundation, clarity, novel block-wise ADMM, and problem importance.  
- Argument against acceptance: lack of code release.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution; recommend acceptance contingent on authors releasing source code.
