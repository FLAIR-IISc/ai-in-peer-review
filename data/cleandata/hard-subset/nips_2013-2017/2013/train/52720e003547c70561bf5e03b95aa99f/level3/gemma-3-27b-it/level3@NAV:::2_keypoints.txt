- Addresses scalable estimation of sparse precision matrices (CLIME) for millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.
- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME combined with a distributed, column-block computation framework.
- Leverages CLIME’s decomposability into independent linear programs to enable efficient parallelization.
- Emphasizes matrix-matrix multiplications and parallel architectures for computational efficiency.
- Builds on prior work in sparse precision estimation and distributed optimization, and cites relevant inexact-ADMM literature.
- Convergence analysis is sound and clearly presented, though standard.
- Explores both shared-memory and distributed-memory implementations, demonstrating algorithmic versatility.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and tackles a highly relevant problem for practitioners.
- Numerical experiments claim substantial scalability but lack reporting of solver solution accuracy alongside runtimes.
- Discussion of exploiting sparse and low-rank structure is promising but feels disjoint from practical implementation and measured gains.
- Impact of block size 'k' on performance is explored but not fully elucidated or recommended for different scenarios.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, addresses a challenging high-dimensional problem, original shared/distributed ADMM evaluation, and practical utility.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental detail on solution accuracy, weak integration of sparse/low-rank discussion with implementation, and limited analysis of optimal block size.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution leaning toward acceptance pending improved experimental detail on accuracy and clearer treatment of implementation choices.
