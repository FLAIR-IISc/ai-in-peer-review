- Problem & scope: scales sparse precision matrix estimation to millions of variables and trillions of parameters, focusing on CLIME with a block-wise approach.  
- Core algorithmic contribution: novel inexact ADMM solving column-block subproblems via element-wise updates and matrix multiplications amenable to BLAS3.  
- Theoretical guarantees: establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for objective value and distance to optimality.  
- Structural exploitation: leverages low-rank (n << p) and sparsity in the sample covariance to drastically reduce matrix-multiplication costs.  
- Parallel implementation: scalable shared- and distributed-memory implementations using block-cyclic data distribution for load balancing.  
- Empirical performance: extensive experiments on synthetic and real data show large improvements over baselines and near-linear speedups with cores.  
- Strength — significance: tackles a highly important, ambitious scaling challenge (e.g., 10^12 parameters solved in hours).  
- Strength — originality: the block-wise CLIME formulation with a tailored inexact ADMM is a novel and effective design.  
- Strength — technical quality: solid algorithm derivation, convergence theory, and substantial engineering effort.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and balanced across theory, algorithm, and experiments.  
- Main concern: reproducibility — authors do not state whether the high-performance implementation/source code will be released.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance but urge making acceptance contingent on publicly releasing the source code.
