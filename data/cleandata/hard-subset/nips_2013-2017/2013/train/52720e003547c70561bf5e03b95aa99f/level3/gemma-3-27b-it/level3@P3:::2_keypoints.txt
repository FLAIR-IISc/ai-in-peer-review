- Paper tackles large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation using a tailored inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME with a distributed framework for millions of dimensions.  
- Leverages CLIME’s componentwise subproblems and parallel matrix-matrix multiplies to achieve strong scalability.  
- Builds on relevant prior work in sparse precision estimation and distributed optimization, situating itself within Big Data scaling trends.  
- Uses inexact ADMM appropriately, with Theorems 1 and 2 providing convergence guarantees.  
- Core innovation: column-block ADMM combined with a block-cyclic data distribution and optimized parallel matrix multiplication.  
- Demonstrated scalability: claims estimating a 1M-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Technical soundness: derivation and theoretical grounding appear correct and well-justified.  
- Originality: novel combination of inexact ADMM, column-block solves, and distributed data layout; evaluation in shared and distributed memory is novel.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and consistent in notation.  
- Effectively exploits CLIME structure to align algorithm design with problem structure for parallelization.  
- Weakness: experiments lack sufficient detail on solution accuracy (e.g., recovered sparsity, precision/recall) and tuning of baselines.  
- Weakness: comparisons focus mainly on runtime; broader quality comparisons across methods and parameters are needed.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3 on covariance perturbation lacks practical guidance on accuracy impact and perturbation selection.  
- Weakness: discussion of block size selection (pb x nb x kb) is insufficiently detailed for practical use.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, novel and sound scalable approach, strong empirical scalability, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental reporting on solution accuracy and limited comparison scope.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with significant scalability and soundness; acceptance recommended pending more comprehensive accuracy experiments.
