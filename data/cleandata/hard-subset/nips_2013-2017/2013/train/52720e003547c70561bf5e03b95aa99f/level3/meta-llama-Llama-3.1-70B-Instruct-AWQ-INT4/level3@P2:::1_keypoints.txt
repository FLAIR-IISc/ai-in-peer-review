- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable CLIME variant and distributed/parallel framework for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Establishes convergence rates for the objective and optimality conditions.  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Problem is well-motivated with a clear overview of relevant literature.  
- CLIME is an appropriate estimator choice due to strong statistical guarantees and computational benefits.  
- CLIME-ADMM leverages CLIME’s separable structure to solve the problem in column-blocks.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear algorithm description and implementation details.  
- Minor writing issues (e.g., "tunning" vs. "tuning") and scattered notation across Sections 1–3; a notation table is recommended.  
- Experimental comparisons with DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare are useful and results are impressive.  
- Choice of column block size k is ad hoc; more intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k is needed.  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is proposed to guarantee load balance and scalability but lacks empirical evidence and justification versus alternatives.  
- Characterization as a distributed algorithm is unclear because message passing/communication mechanisms are not described; consider clarifying or calling it a parallel implementation.  
- Overall technically sound, original contribution with potential significant impact in ML.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm leveraging CLIME structure, strong scalability results, and relevance to an important ML problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scattered notation, ad hoc block-size choice, and unclear communication/parallelization details.
