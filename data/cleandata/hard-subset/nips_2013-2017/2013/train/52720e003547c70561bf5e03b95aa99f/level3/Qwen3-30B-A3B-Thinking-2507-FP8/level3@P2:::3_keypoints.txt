- Tackles scaling sparse precision estimation to millions of variables and trillions of parameters.  
- Focuses on the CLIME estimator and proposes a block-wise inexact ADMM that uses matrix-matrix operations for efficiency.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for both objective value and optimality conditions.  
- Introduces a scalable distributed framework with block cyclic distribution for load balancing and cache optimization.  
- Shows how to exploit sparsity and low-rank structure to further improve computation.  
- Strong technical quality with rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Thorough experimental validation against state-of-the-art on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Scalability studies cover shared- and distributed-memory architectures, varying block sizes and core counts.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and support theoretical claims with empirical evidence.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, well-structured pseudocode, and lucid explanation of block cyclic distribution.  
- Figures effectively illustrate scalability and performance results.  
- Novelty: block-wise processing of CLIME and block cyclic distribution are significant advances over column-wise solvers.  
- Novel theoretical analysis of convergence for the block-wise approach.  
- High significance: demonstrates estimating a 1M-dimensional precision matrix (1T parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Demonstrates near-linear scalability with increasing core counts.  
- Key strengths: combination of theoretical rigor, implementation/memory-hierarchy optimizations, and comprehensive experiments.  
- Main weakness: no concrete plan or timeline to release source code for reproducibility.  
- Additional weakness: theoretical analysis could more explicitly relate block size to convergence rate.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an important problem, novel method with strong guarantees, comprehensive experiments, clear presentation, and demonstrated scalability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of code release commitment and some missing theoretical detail.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with expectation authors will release code; meets major conference criteria.
