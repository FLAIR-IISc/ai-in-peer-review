- Proposal: CLIME-ADMM — an inexact ADMM algorithm and parallel/distributed framework for large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation via CLIME.  
- Key algorithmic ideas: solve CLIME in column blocks (matrix–matrix ops, cache-friendly blocking).  
- Key algorithmic ideas: exploit low-rank structure C = AA′ to reduce multiplication cost.  
- Systems/design: block-cyclic data distribution implemented with OpenMP/OpenMPI/ScaLAPACK.  
- Theory: O(1/T) convergence of objective and residuals under η ≥ ρ·λmax(C)^2.  
- Experiments: strong scaling to hundreds of cores; reported 1M-dimensional run in ~11 hours; comparisons to DC-QUIC, TIGER, flare.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on CLIME/QUIC family, distributed ADMM literature, and large-scale systems work.  
- Quality: derivations are sound and consistent with inexact/linearized ADMM literature.  
- Quality concern: practical choices (ρ, η), stopping criteria, and low-rank vs sparse multiply trade-offs are under-specified.  
- Quality concern: load-balance and communication claims lack supporting measurements or a communication/message-passing model.  
- Empirical gaps: missing KKT residuals, communication time breakdowns, and ablation studies (e.g., effect of block size k, low-rank vs sparse path).  
- Clarity: needs a notation table and earlier definition of symbols (ρ, η, k); contains minor typos.  
- Originality: main novelty is systems/engineering (column-block updates and distributed implementation); optimization pieces are standard.  
- Significance: if reproducible, reported scale (p ≈ 10^6) is impressive and practically relevant; clearer guidance on k and communication-aware design would boost impact.  
- Pros: scalable practical solver using standard HPC primitives; provable O(1/T) convergence; useful engineering optimizations.  
- Cons: insufficient evidence/description of distributed communication and load balance; ad hoc choice of block size k; clarity/notation and minor typos.  
- Suggestions: add a notation table and define ρ, η, k at first use; provide tuning guidance.  
- Suggestions: provide a per-iteration communication model (message counts/bytes), overlap strategy, and load-balance metrics (per-process flops/idle time).  
- Suggestions: give intuition/theory for selecting k (e.g., cache/roofline analysis) and sensitivity studies.  
- Suggestions: report KKT residuals, compute vs communication time breakdowns, and release code/scripts for reproducibility.
