- Addresses sparse precision matrix estimation at massive scale (millions of variables, trillions of parameters), a significant gap in ultra-high-dimensional statistics.
- Proposes a novel block-wise CLIME-ADMM algorithm that solves column-block-wise rather than column-by-column, exploiting sparsity and low-rank structure.
- Algorithmic innovation enables efficient matrix-matrix multiplications that outperform repeated matrix-vector operations.
- Theoretical results establish O(1/T) convergence rates for objective value and optimality residuals.
- Implementation includes careful load balancing (block cyclic distribution) and is designed for both shared- and distributed-memory architectures.
- Experiments demonstrate remarkable scalability: estimating a 1M-dimensional precision matrix (1T parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores with near-linear speedup.
- Presentation is clear and well-structured, with strong literature connections distinguishing it from prior distributed optimization work.
- Main concern: lack of public source code, which hinders reproducibility and community adoption.
- Meets NIPS criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance); recommended for acceptance contingent on authors releasing the implementation.
