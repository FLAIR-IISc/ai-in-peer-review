- Proposes a scalable distributed framework for solving CLIME via an inexact ADMM operating on column-blocks of the precision matrix.
- Reformulates computation so the main bottleneck becomes matrix–matrix multiplications, exploiting their parallel efficiency.
- Provides convergence analysis for the inexact ADMM variant, establishing an O(1/T) rate.
- Implements both shared-memory and distributed-memory versions, using block-cyclic data distribution for load balancing.
- Empirical results show impressive scalability: solving a 1M-dimensional, 1-trillion-parameter problem in hours on 400 cores.
- Reports near-linear speedup in distributed experiments, supported by the presented benchmarks.
- Paper is technically sound and well-written; Algorithm 1 and the parallel framework are clearly explained.
- Primary novelty is engineering and system design rather than a new optimization algorithm.
- Significant practical contribution: pushes computational feasibility for ultra-large sparse precision matrix estimation.
- Scalability largely enabled by the decomposable CLIME structure and efficient parallel matrix multiplication.
- Experimental comparisons with other solvers lack crucial solution-quality metrics (final objective or constraint violation).
- Hyperparameter selection details (e.g., ADMM ρ and η) and tuning procedures are not reported, harming reproducibility and fairness.
- Recommendation: accept for strong systems/empirical contribution, but revise to include accuracy comparisons and hyperparameter details.
