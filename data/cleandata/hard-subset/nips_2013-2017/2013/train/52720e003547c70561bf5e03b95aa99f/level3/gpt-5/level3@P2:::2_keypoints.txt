- Main contribution: Inexact/linearized ADMM tailored to CLIME with closed-form soft-threshold X-update, simple box-projection Z-update, and O(1/T) convergence guarantees.  
- Implementation: Scalable shared-memory and distributed implementations using column-block processing, BLAS3-style operations, cache-friendly blocking, and block-cyclic process-grid distribution.  
- Computational bottleneck: Reduces to parallelizable matrix-matrix multiplies (CX and CY); authors exploit low-rank C = AA′ when n ≪ p.  
- Scalability: Experiments show near-linear strong scaling and a p = 10^6 problem solved in ~11 hours on 400 cores; larger column blocks improve speed.  
- Baselines: Compared against CLIME (flare), TIGER, and DC-QUIC and reports strong scalability advantages.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on CLIME, QUIC/DC-QUIC, and ADMM literature; systems design relates to block-splitting and ScaLAPACK traditions.  
- Theoretical notes: O(1/T) rates seem correctly adapted but need clarity on ergodic vs non-ergodic rates, constants, and explicit norm/positivity conditions (‖·‖^2_{η/ρ I−C^2}).  
- Algorithmic soundness: Column-block formulation and updates are implementable, but the paper omits discussion on symmetrization/positive-definiteness of the final precision estimate.  
- Systems critique: Design choices (shared vs distributed, block-cyclic) are appropriate but communication patterns and data-movement costs (broadcasts/reductions for A′X and AY) are not quantitatively characterized.  
- Experimental gaps — stopping/accuracy: Missing per-dataset stopping criteria, primal/dual tolerances, achieved KKT residuals, and objective gap reporting.  
- Experimental gaps — estimation quality: Real-data evaluation lacks out-of-sample likelihood, stability selection, or surrogate risk; synthetic experiments need estimation error vs iteration/time.  
- Covariance perturbation: Theorem is interesting but lacks empirical validation and practical guidance on threshold choice and runtime/accuracy trade-offs.  
- Clarity issues: Inconsistencies in Algorithm 1 (box() signature), unclear definitions for Vt/V̂t and their updates, and missing statement on final symmetrization and column recombination across blocks.  
- Presentation suggestion: Provide a concise per-iteration complexity and communication table (compute, memory, messages) and fix minor typos/grammar.  
- Originality: Algorithmic ingredients are largely known; novelty lies in integrating column-block ADMM with a scalable implementation and tailored convergence guarantees.  
- Significance: Practical systems contribution that enables million-scale sparse precision estimation; theoretical advance is incremental but useful in applied contexts.  
- Pros: Simple closed-form updates, O(1/T) guarantees, thoughtful systems design, strong scalability, and exploitation of low-rank structure.  
- Cons: Limited statistical accuracy evaluation, modest algorithmic novelty, missing PD/symmetrization discussion, and unquantified communication/memory footprint.  
- Questions for authors: Report stopping criteria and achieved residuals; describe λ and η/ρ selection and sensitivity; explain symmetrization/PD enforcement at scale; give communication volume per iteration; provide experiments for covariance perturbation.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — technically sound and valuable systems contribution but needs stronger accuracy reporting and empirical validation.  
- Reviewer confidence: 3/5.
