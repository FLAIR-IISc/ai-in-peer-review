- Proposes a scalable CLIME-ADMM variant that processes precision matrix columns in column-blocks and a distributed framework scaling to 1M dimensions (1T parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores.

- Introduces an inexact ADMM with O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and optimality residuals (Theorems 1 and 2).

- Uses column-block processing to leverage matrix-matrix operations for improved computational efficiency.

- Implements block-cyclic data distribution for load balancing in shared- and distributed-memory (OpenMP, OpenMPI, ScaLAPACK) settings and demonstrates broad empirical scalability.

- Strong empirical evaluation comparing to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare across architectures.

- Notation is scattered and incomplete (symbols like ρ and η undefined); a dedicated notation table is needed for clarity.

- Claims that block-cyclic distribution “guarantees load balance and scalability” without theoretical or empirical justification.

- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc (k=100) with no guidance, theory, or sensitivity analysis on how to select k.

- The paper lacks detailed discussion of message passing/communication; distinction between “distributed” and “parallel” is unclear and may be misleading.

- Contains minor writing/typo errors (e.g., “tunning,” “summerized”) that should be corrected.

- Technically sound with solid theoretical and empirical results, but missing theoretical justification for key design choices.

- Clarity would improve with a notation table and clearer explanation/justification of block-cyclic distribution and block-size selection.

- The column-block approach is original and a meaningful extension of CLIME solvers.

- The work is highly significant for enabling ultra-large-scale precision matrix estimation.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address notation, block-cyclic justification, block-size selection, and clarity on distributed vs. parallel implementation.
