- Proposes a novel generative image encoding model combining non-linear feature combinations with explicit position encoding.  
- Model explicitly targets occlusion handling and translation invariances.  
- Effectiveness demonstrated via numerical experiments on artificial and natural image datasets.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with a thorough introduction and clear technical exposition.  
- Model learns component masks and features qualitatively different from linear models.  
- Uses truncated posterior approximation and directional annealing for efficient learning and to avoid local optima.  
- Presents a simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision estimation and evaluates it across architectures.  
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard and not novel.  
- Numerical experiment setup lacks specificity (e.g., per-solver accuracy) and needs clarification.  
- Some results may be sensitive to hyperparameter choices.  
- Technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; authors provide balanced evaluation.  
- Originality lies in the novel combination of non-linear feature combinations and explicit position encoding.  
- High significance: advances state of the art and likely to be adopted or built upon by others.  
- Recommendation: Accept; rating 8/10; reviewer confidence 0.8.
