- Presents CLIME-ADMM, a scalable inexact ADMM algorithm and distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation at massive scale.  
- Tackles applying the CLIME estimator to problems with millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters via parallelization across hundreds of cores.  
- Novel use of column-block ADMM with block-cyclic data distribution to exploit matrix-multiply efficiency and balance load.  
- Empirical results show strong scalability, e.g., estimating a one‑million‑dimension precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Compares favorably to existing methods (CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC) and analyzes impact of column block size (k) in different memory environments.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for the ADMM algorithm, strengthening the contribution.  
- Notation is inconsistent and lacks a consolidated table, making the presentation hard to follow.  
- Symbols (e.g., ρ, η) are introduced without clear definitions, forcing readers to search the text.  
- Justification for block-cyclic data distribution is hand-wavy and lacks detailed explanation or supporting empirical evidence on communication overhead.  
- Selection of column block size (k) is ad hoc with no systematic guidance or theoretical justification provided.  
- Characterizing the framework as a "distributed algorithm" is debatable; communication/message-passing details are insufficient.  
- Minor writing errors (e.g., "tunning" vs. "tuning") detract from polish.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, novel approach, strong empirical scalability, and theoretical guarantees.  
- Arguments for rejection: unclear notation/explanations, weak justification for k and block-cyclic scheme, and potentially misleading distributed claim.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add notation table, elaborate/block-cyclic rationale and communication costs, provide guidance for k selection, clarify distributed vs. parallel claims, and fix writing errors.
