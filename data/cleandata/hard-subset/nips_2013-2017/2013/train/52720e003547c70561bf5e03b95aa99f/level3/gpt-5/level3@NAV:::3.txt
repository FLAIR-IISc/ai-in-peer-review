This paper tackles ultra–large-scale sparse precision estimation via CLIME [3], targeting settings with millions of variables. Building on CLIME’s column-separable LP, the authors propose a block-wise inexact ADMM variant that aggregates columns into blocks to exploit high-performance matrix–matrix kernels and block-cyclic data layouts. The algorithm couples elementwise soft/box-thresholding with two matrix multiplications per iteration, and leverages both iterate sparsity and the low-rank factorization C = AAᵀ (when n ≪ p) to reduce cost from O(p2k) to O(npk). On the systems side, they present shared- and distributed-memory implementations using OpenMP, MPI, and ScaLAPACK with careful load balancing via block-cyclic distribution. Empirically, they compare to TIGER [19], CLIME baselines, and DC-QUIC [13], showing strong runtime and near-linear scaling up to 400 cores (p = 10^6, ~11 hours). Theoretically, they establish O(1/T) rates for objective suboptimality and optimality residuals under η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)^2, in line with inexact ADMM theory [12, 26].

Quality: The algorithmic design is sound, and the convergence analysis addresses both primal progress and feasibility. Systems considerations (blocking, cache, load balance) are thoughtfully engineered and validated with scalability studies. However, the experimental section emphasizes runtime over statistical quality: beyond a synthetic precision–recall plot, metrics such as estimation error (ℓ1/Frobenius/spectral), held-out likelihood, and post-hoc PD correction for CLIME are limited. Clarification is also needed on parameter selection (λ, ρ, η), memory footprints, communication cost breakdowns, and symmetrization/PD projection at scale.

Clarity: The paper is well written, with clear statements of novelty, solid connections to ADMM/distributed optimization [2, 23], and understandable pseudocode. A concise complexity/memory table and guidance on choosing block sizes k and block-cyclic tile sizes would help practitioners.

Originality: Moving from column-wise to block-wise CLIME with an inexact ADMM update, combined with systematic exploitation of low-rank and sparsity and a scalable block-cyclic implementation, is a novel and useful combination relative to prior CLIME solvers, QUIC/DC-QUIC [13], and GPBS [23].

Significance: Addressing trillion-parameter regimes is impactful; the implementation appears practically valuable to the community.

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong systems contribution with convincing scalability and engineering detail.
- Clear, correct convergence guarantees for the proposed variant.
- Careful exploitation of structure and parallelism; broad applicability.

Cons against acceptance:
- Limited statistical evaluation and discussion of PD correction and accuracy on real data.
- Reproducibility hinges on code; absence of released implementation would blunt impact.
- Missing ablations on structure exploitation (sparsity vs low-rank) and communication profiling.

Recommendation: Accept contingent on releasing the source code and scripts. Strengthen the camera-ready with statistical metrics, PD handling details, parameter-tuning guidance, and a brief communication/memory breakdown.
