- Addresses large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation for CLIME, targeting million-dimension problems.
- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm that processes column-blocks instead of single columns for better parallelism.
- Introduces block cyclic distribution to balance load and reduce cache misses, enabling extreme scaling.
- Leverages CLIME's separable column structure and block matrix multiplications for efficient parallel implementation.
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions (standard for inexact ADMM).
- Implementation covers both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures with careful load-balancing design.
- Experimental results show nearly linear scalability with cores and major speedups over DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Demonstrates practical scalability (e.g., 1M dimensions solved in ~11 hours on 400 cores).
- Technical derivation and convergence analysis are rigorous and well-referenced.
- Paper is very clear and well-organized, with useful pseudocode and discussion of sparse/low-rank exploitation.
- Significant practical impact for high-dimensional statistics and applications (genomics, finance, networks).
- Original contribution: novel use of block cyclic distribution and column-block ADMM for CLIME.
- Main weakness: experiments lack comprehensive statistical accuracy metrics (e.g., Frobenius error, PR curves).
- Additional weakness: some implementation details (optimal block size selection) and distinction from simple column-parallelization need clearer explanation.
- Overall judgment: strong, well-motivated technical contribution with practical scalability — good candidate for acceptance.
