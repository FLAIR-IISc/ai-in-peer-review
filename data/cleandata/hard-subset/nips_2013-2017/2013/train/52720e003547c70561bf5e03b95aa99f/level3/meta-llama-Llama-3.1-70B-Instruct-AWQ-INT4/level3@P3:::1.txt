This paper proposes a scalable variant of the CLIME estimator, CLIME-ADMM, and a distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation. The authors establish rates of convergence for both the objective and optimality conditions and demonstrate the scalability of their algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a large-scale distributed framework for precision matrix estimation using CLIME. The authors propose an inexact ADMM algorithm that can scale up to millions of dimensions and use up to thousands of cores. The framework solves CLIME in column-blocks and uses block cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing. Experimental results show that the algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods and scales almost linearly with the number of cores.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their algorithm and framework. However, there are a few areas that could be improved. The notation is scattered across Sections 1-3, and it would be helpful to have a notation table to clarify the meaning of symbols like ρ and η. Additionally, the block-cyclic data distribution is an interesting approach, but it is unclear why it guarantees load balance or scalability, and no empirical evidence is provided to support this claim.

The choice of column block size k is also ad hoc, and it would be helpful to have more intuition or theoretical guidance on how to select k. Furthermore, the algorithm may be better characterized as a parallel implementation rather than a distributed algorithm, since message passing/communication mechanisms are not described.

There are also some minor writing/typo issues, such as "tunning" instead of "tuning", "parameter tunning free" instead of "tuning", and "summerized" instead of "summarized".

In terms of novelty, the paper highlights the estimation of the precision matrix by column blocks (instead of column-by-column) and the empirical analysis using OpenMPI and Scalapak. However, it would be helpful to have more discussion on how this approach differs from previous contributions and how it advances the state of the art in precision matrix estimation.

Overall, the paper is a good contribution to the field of precision matrix estimation, and the authors have made a significant effort to develop a scalable algorithm and framework. However, there are a few areas that could be improved to make the paper more clear and convincing.
