- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant of the CLIME estimator and a distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Provides convergence rates for the objective and optimality conditions.  
- Demonstrates scalability on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Well-written with clear problem statement, solution, experiments, and good literature positioning.  
- CLIME-ADMM is an inexact ADMM that solves CLIME in column-blocks rather than column-by-column.  
- Column-block approach significantly reduces computational complexity and improves scalability.  
- Experimental results show CLIME-ADMM outperforms DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare in scalability and large-scale performance.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; a notation table is recommended (e.g., for ρ and η).  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is interesting but lacks empirical/theoretical evidence for load balance and scalability claims.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; more intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k is needed.  
- Algorithm may be better characterized as a parallel implementation rather than a distributed algorithm because message passing/communication mechanisms are not described.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor revisions needed; Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
