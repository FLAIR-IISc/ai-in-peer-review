- Introduces CLIME-ADMM, a scalable CLIME variant and distributed framework claimed to handle millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.  
- Empirical evaluation uses OpenMPI and Scalapak to demonstrate large-scale parallel capabilities.  
- Estimates precision matrix by column blocks, enabling efficient parallelization and distribution of computations.  
- Uses block-cyclic data distribution and matrix multiplication to achieve load balance and scalability.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees, including rates for the objective and optimality conditions.  
- Notation is poorly organized (e.g., ρ and η undefined); a notation table would improve readability.  
- Choice of column block size appears ad hoc with no theoretical guidance or intuition on its impact.  
- Claim of being a truly distributed algorithm is questionable due to lack of explicit message-passing/communication details.  
- Minor typographical errors (e.g., "tuning", "summarized") need correction.  
- Addresses an important, hard problem in precision matrix estimation with a solution suited for ultra-large-scale datasets.  
- Empirical results show near-linear scaling with number of cores, supporting practical scalability.  
- Overall contribution is significant but acceptance should require clearer notation, justification of block-size choice, and clarification of distribution/communication mechanisms.
