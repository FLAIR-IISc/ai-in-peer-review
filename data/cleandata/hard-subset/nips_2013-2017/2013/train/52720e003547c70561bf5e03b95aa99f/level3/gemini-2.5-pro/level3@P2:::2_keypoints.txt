- Tackles sparse precision matrix estimation in high dimensions using the CLIME estimator.  
- Main contribution: a large-scale distributed framework with an inexact ADMM operating on blocks of columns.  
- Theoretical guarantee: establishes an O(1/T) convergence rate for the proposed algorithm.  
- Implementation and evaluation on shared- and distributed-memory systems, scaling to ~1M dimensions (1 trillion parameters) on hundreds of cores.  
- Technical approach is sound; inexact ADMM and handling of the L1 norm are well-justified.  
- Experimental methodology is generally good, using synthetic and real data and relevant baselines.  
- Experimental comparison is incomplete: runtimes reported but final objective values and constraint satisfaction (||CΩ̂ − I||∞) are not provided.  
- Paper is very clear, well-organized, and likely reproducible, with useful diagrams and Algorithm 1.  
- Originality lies in the systems/engineering and large-scale demonstration rather than a fundamentally new optimization method.  
- Key insight: CLIME’s column-wise separability makes it highly amenable to parallelization and matrix-matrix multiply bottleneck exploitation.  
- Significance is high: enables estimation at scales far beyond prior work, with practical applicability in many fields.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, demonstrates state-of-the-art scalability, is technically sound, and is well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: moderate algorithmic novelty and experimental comparisons need stronger accuracy reporting.  
- Overall conclusion: strong practical and empirical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
