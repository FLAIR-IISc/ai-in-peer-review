- Proposes a distributed column-block variant of CLIME-ADMM to scale sparse precision matrix estimation to ultra-high dimensions (e.g., 1M dims, 1T parameters).
- Leverages column-block processing to enable efficient matrix-matrix operations and better cache performance versus column-by-column methods.
- Incorporates exploitation of sparse and low-rank structure and a block cyclic distribution strategy for load balancing in distributed settings.
- Builds on prior CLIME and ADMM work but introduces a novel block-based approach that yields substantial performance gains.
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis (O(1/T) for objective and residuals) grounded in prior optimization literature.
- Strong experimental validation showing scalability on shared-memory (20 cores) and distributed-memory (400 cores), including an 11-hour run for a 1M-dimensional problem.
- Demonstrates significant practical impact and potential adaptability to other large-scale constrained optimization problems.
- Lacks theoretical analysis of how column block size affects convergence rates and optimal block size selection remains empirical.
- Experimental comparisons are limited—insufficient memory-usage and communication-overhead metrics and incomplete benchmarking against other recent distributed methods (e.g., Tiger, [23,6,9]).
- Unclear whether the distributed block-processing approach preserves CLIME’s statistical guarantees; the connection to Theorem 3 needs clarification.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant contribution with minor concerns about block-size theory and more comprehensive experimental comparisons.
