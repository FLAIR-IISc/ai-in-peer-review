- Proposes a distributed CLIME-ADMM that solves sparse precision estimation in column-blocks with block-cyclic distribution for memory efficiency.
- Claims O(1/T) convergence (inexact ADMM) and demonstrates scalability to p = 1,000,000 on 400 cores (≈11 hours).
- Theoretical guarantees are presented (Theorems 1–2) but proofs are deferred to the supplement and theoretical novelty over inexact ADMM is not clearly established.
- Experimental evaluation compares to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC on synthetic and real data but omits recent distributed/state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., Wang et al. [23], methods in [13]).
- The claimed “linear speedup” is questionable: figures show diminishing returns at high core counts.
- Lacks ablation studies on block-size selection and sensitivity to parameters.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; Algorithm 1 and figures are helpful.
- Section 3 (sparse/low-rank structure) is vague and needs concrete examples; scalability discussion needs deeper analysis of block-size effects.
- The use of ADMM for distributed precision estimation is not novel; the column-block idea is somewhat incremental and similar block approaches exist in distributed linear algebra.
- Fails to clearly differentiate the block-cyclic distribution from existing schemes like GPBS [23].
- Scaling to 1M dimensions is an impressive engineering achievement addressing an important problem.
- Practical significance is underdeveloped: paper doesn’t justify the necessity of million-dimensional precision matrices or show downstream application benefits.
- Strengths: impressive scalability, theoretical convergence rates, clear presentation, and broad experimental work.
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to current distributed methods, overstated speedup claims, insufficient distinction from prior distributed ADMM work, limited theoretical novelty, and weak discussion of practical impact/limitations.
- Recommendation: reject—work is technically competent but lacks sufficient novelty; suggest submission to a specialized venue if revised.
- Revision suggestions: add comprehensive comparisons with recent distributed methods, clearly articulate theoretical/methodological novelty, and better justify the need and practical impact of ultra-high-dimensional precision estimation.
