- Summary: Proposes a distributed column-block ADMM variant of CLIME to scale sparse precision estimation to p=10^6, using inexact ADMM, column-block processing, and block-cyclic data distribution; demonstrates 1M-dimension estimation in 11 hours on 400 cores.

- Strength: Significant practical contribution—scales precision matrix estimation to 1M dimensions (1 trillion parameters), far beyond prior p > 10^4 limits.

- Strength: Technical soundness—provides O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and constraint violations with justified inexact ADMM design.

- Strength: Practical implementation—column-block processing leverages efficient matrix-matrix multiplication and cache hierarchies for good scalability.

- Strength: Rigorous experiments—comprehensive comparisons to Tiger, DC-QUIC, and CLIME across synthetic and real data in shared- and distributed-memory settings.

- Weakness: Lacks statistical accuracy comparison—no Frobenius/spectral norm errors or graph recovery metrics to show whether speedups sacrifice estimation quality.

- Weakness: Unclear how to choose column block size k—paper notes dependencies (grid, cache, sparsity) but gives no practical guidance.

- Weakness: Limited discussion of trade-offs—doesn't analyze potential loss of statistical optimality due to the distributed/approximate approach.

- Weakness: Inadequate differentiation from related work—insufficiently distinguishes the block-cyclic approach from prior distributed optimization (e.g., GPBS).

- Quality: Technically solid with convincing scalability results, but needs thorough evaluation of statistical properties alongside runtime.

- Clarity: Well-organized with clear pseudocode and figures, though connection between O(1/T) theory and practical iteration counts is under-discussed.

- Originality: Novel and meaningful—column-block ADMM and block-cyclic distribution are new and justified by efficiency gains.

- Significance: High—addresses an important modern problem (e.g., genomics, networks) and demonstrates notable applicability to other large constrained optimization tasks.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on adding statistical accuracy evaluations and practical guidance.

- Revision 1: Add a table comparing runtime and statistical accuracy (Frobenius/spectral norm, precision/recall) across methods on synthetic datasets.

- Revision 2: Provide practical guidelines for selecting column block size k based on problem size, memory, and hardware.

- Revision 3: Expand discussion of computational vs. statistical trade-offs to clarify any costs of the speedup.

- Revision 4: Strengthen related work to clearly differentiate the column-block/block-cyclic approach from existing distributed frameworks.

- Revision 5: Include specific speedup numbers (e.g., x-fold speedup on given problem sizes) to contextualize performance claims.
