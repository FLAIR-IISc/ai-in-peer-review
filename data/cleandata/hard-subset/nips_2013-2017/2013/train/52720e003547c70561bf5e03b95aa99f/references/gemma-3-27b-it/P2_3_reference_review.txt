## Review of "Large Scale Distributed Sparse Precision Estimation"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a distributed algorithm, CLIME-ADMM, for estimating sparse precision matrices in ultra-high dimensional settings (p >> n). It builds upon the CLIME statistical estimator and the ADMM optimization technique, addressing the limitations of existing methods that struggle with memory constraints and scalability. The core innovations include an inexact ADMM formulation tailored for CLIME, solving the problem in column blocks to leverage matrix multiplication efficiencies, and a block cyclic data distribution scheme for optimal parallelization. The authors demonstrate strong theoretical convergence guarantees and empirical scalability up to a million dimensions on 400 cores.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a critical problem:** Estimating precision matrices in high-dimensional, low-sample settings is a fundamental challenge with numerous applications.  Scaling existing methods to truly massive datasets is a major bottleneck.
* **Solid theoretical foundation:** The paper provides convergence guarantees for the proposed inexact ADMM algorithm, building on existing literature and extending it to the specific context of CLIME. Theorems 1 & 2 offer valuable insights into the algorithm’s behavior.
* **Novel algorithmic contributions:**  Solving the problem in column blocks and exploiting matrix multiplication efficiencies is a clever optimization. The block cyclic data distribution for parallelization appears well-motivated and addresses load balancing concerns.
* **Strong empirical results:** The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the scalability of CLIME-ADMM, achieving impressive performance on a dataset with one million dimensions.  The comparison with existing methods highlights the advantages of the proposed approach.
* **Clear writing & organization:** The paper is generally well-written and structured. The explanations of the algorithmic innovations are clear, and the notation is consistent.
* **Comprehensive related work:** The authors provide a thorough review of existing approaches to precision matrix estimation and distributed optimization, positioning their work effectively within the broader landscape.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited comparison to strong baselines:** While comparisons are made to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC, the experimental setup lacks a more comprehensive evaluation against state-of-the-art distributed estimation algorithms (if they exist) beyond scaling up existing single-machine methods. The choice of evaluation metrics is standard, but deeper analysis on the quality of the estimated precision matrix (e.g., in terms of graph recovery) beyond precision/recall would be beneficial.
* **Parameter sensitivity:** The choice of parameters (λ, ρ, η, block size k) can significantly impact performance. While the paper provides guidance, a more systematic analysis of parameter sensitivity and robustness would strengthen the results. It would be beneficial to include a discussion of how these parameters might be automatically tuned.
* **Low-rank assumption utilization:** While the authors mention utilizing the low-rank structure of the sample covariance matrix, the explanation remains relatively high-level. A more detailed discussion of *how* this assumption is exploited in the implementation and its impact on performance would be valuable. The impact of sparsity in the covariance matrix itself on runtime isn’t clearly quantified.
* **Implementation details:** The paper refers to using ScaLAPACK for distributed matrix multiplication, but provides limited details about the specific implementation choices (e.g., block sizes, communication strategies).  More implementation details would improve reproducibility.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of sparse precision matrix estimation. The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm addresses a critical scalability bottleneck and offers a practical solution for tackling ultra-high dimensional problems. The theoretical analysis is sound, and the empirical results are compelling. While some areas could be strengthened with more detailed analysis and experimentation, the overall quality of the work is high.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a very important and challenging problem.
* Provides a novel and effective solution with strong theoretical guarantees.
* Demonstrates impressive scalability through empirical results.
* Well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Comparison to the most state-of-the-art distributed methods could be more comprehensive.
* Parameter sensitivity analysis could be more detailed.
* Further explanation of low-rank assumption exploitation would be beneficial.

**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (9/10):** Technically sound, with strong theoretical justification and compelling experimental results. A complete piece of work that makes a significant contribution.
* **Clarity (8/10):** Generally well-written and organized, though some implementation details could be more fleshed out.
* **Originality (8/10):** The combination of CLIME, ADMM, column-block solving, and the block cyclic distribution scheme is novel and effective.  The authors clearly differentiate their work from previous contributions.
* **Significance (9/10):** The results are important and have the potential to impact a wide range of applications. The algorithm advances the state-of-the-art in scalable precision matrix estimation.




**Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept**.  I believe this paper is a valuable contribution to NIPS and warrants acceptance. The strengths significantly outweigh the weaknesses, and the work addresses a critical problem in a novel and effective manner.  I recommend that the authors address the minor weaknesses outlined in this review during the rebuttal phase to further strengthen the paper.
