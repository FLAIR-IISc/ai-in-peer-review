Presents a scalable algorithm-and-systems framework for CLIME up to p=1e6 using column-separable LPs and an inexact linearized ADMM.

Key practical ideas: column-blocking to convert repeated mat–vec into mat–mat, exploit low-rank C = A A^T to reduce complexity to O(n p k), and a block-cyclic distributed layout.

Algorithm details: X-update is proximal soft-thresholding after linearization, Z-update is an ∞-norm box projection, and the dual update is standard ADMM.

Theoretical result: O(1/T) ergodic bounds for objective suboptimality and residuals under η ≥ ρ λ_max^2(C).

Secondary theorem: covariance sparsification/perturbation that can preserve the sup-norm deviation bound required for CLIME.

Empirical results: strong shared- and distributed-memory scaling; 1e6-dimensional run on 400 cores in ~11 hours.

Strength — importance: addresses a practical bottleneck where p is in the millions and p^2 cannot be materialized.

Strength — algorithm choice: linearized ADMM yields elementwise proximal updates and shifts heavy work to parallelizable mat–mat multiplies.

Strength — systems design: column-blocking, low-rank multiplies, and block-cyclic distribution align with HPC best practices.

Strength — convergence: provided bounds are consistent with inexact ADMM literature.

Strength — empirical demonstration: convincing scaling results and large-scale demonstration.

Strength — perturbation insight: useful explanation of when covariance sparsification is statistically safe.

Weakness — novelty: components (inexact ADMM, block-cyclic layouts, mat–mat optimization) are known; novelty is incremental integration and column-blocking.

Weakness — limited statistical evaluation: emphasis on runtime and matched-sparsity precision/recall without error-vs-time curves or analysis of optimization vs statistical error.

Weakness — baselines: compares to methods solving different estimators (graphical lasso/TIGER) and misses other large-scale CLIME/ADMM implementations, risking unfair comparisons.

Weakness — missing implementation details: symmetrization of Ω̂, estimation of λ_max(C) at scale, stopping criteria linking optimization to statistical error, and communication patterns are under-specified.

Weakness — theoretical breadth: some claims (e.g., Theorem 3) lack precise assumptions (independence, tail parameters, constants) and empirical validation.

Quality: optimization and systems engineering are technically sound; empirical scaling is convincing; perturbation argument needs tighter conditions.

Clarity: generally clear and well organized, but important implementation details and some notational ambiguities should be clarified.

Originality: incremental but useful — integration at this scale is novel though constituent ideas are standard.

Significance: high practical impact for large-scale precision estimation with potential generalization to other separable constrained estimators.

Suggestions: detail symmetrization and its cost, provide stopping rules tying optimization to statistical error, clarify η/ρ and λ_max estimation, add a communication-cost model for A^T X and A W, empirically evaluate covariance sparsification, and expand baselines/report accuracy–time Pareto curves.

Recommendation: Weak accept — strong systems contribution with solid optimization underpinnings, moderate novelty, and some methodological/statistical details to tighten.

Reviewer confidence: 4/5.
