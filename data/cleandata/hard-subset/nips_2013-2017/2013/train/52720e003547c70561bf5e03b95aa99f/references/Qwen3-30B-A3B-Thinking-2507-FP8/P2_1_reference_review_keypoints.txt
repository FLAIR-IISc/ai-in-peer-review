- Proposes a scalable distributed framework for sparse precision estimation in ultra-high dimensions using an inexact ADMM variant of CLIME that operates on column-blocks.
- Introduces a block cyclic distribution strategy for load balancing and leverages sparse + low-rank structure for computational efficiency.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and constraint residuals (Theorems 1 and 2).
- Algorithmic description (Algorithm 1) is clear, detailed, and reproducible with consistent notation.
- Comprehensive experiments compare to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC on synthetic and real data, demonstrating clear scalability advantages.
- Demonstrates estimating a 1M-dimension precision matrix (1 trillion parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores, showing near-linear scalability with cores.
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with effective figures and logical progression from CLIME to column-block ADMM.
- Novel contributions: column-block processing vs. column-by-column, block cyclic distribution for load balancing, and dual use of sparse/low-rank structure.
- Builds on prior ADMM work but makes a meaningful, problem-specific advance with thorough citation and differentiation.
- Significant practical impact for genomics, climate science, and other fields needing million-dimensional precision estimation.
- Strengths: strong theory, novel algorithm, unprecedented scalability, comprehensive validation, and clear implementation details.
- Weaknesses: limited guidance on selecting column block size k, incomplete exploration of tuning parameter λ's impact on scalability.
- Weaknesses (cont.): comparisons could include more recent methods ([20,21]) and real-world evaluations are limited to two datasets.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, clearly written, and highly significant; recommended for acceptance.
