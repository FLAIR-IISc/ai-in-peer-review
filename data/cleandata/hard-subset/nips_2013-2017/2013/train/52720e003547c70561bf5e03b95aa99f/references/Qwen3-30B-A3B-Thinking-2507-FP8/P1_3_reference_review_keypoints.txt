- Summary: Proposes a distributed inexact-ADMM column-block algorithm for CLIME to estimate sparse precision matrices at ultra-high dimensions (p≥1e6), with O(1/T) convergence and near-linear scaling to 400 cores (11 hrs).

- Strength — Technical Quality: Rigorous theoretical claims (Theorems 1–2) and comprehensive experiments comparing to CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC, demonstrating scalability and sound implementation.

- Strength — Clarity: Well-organized, clearly written presentation with precise algorithm (Alg.1) and effective figures illustrating the framework and scalability.

- Strength — Originality: Novel column-block ADMM and block-cyclic data distribution that processes multiple columns simultaneously to overcome previous scalability bottlenecks.

- Strength — Significance: Solves a practically important problem for high-dimensional applications (e.g., genomics), enabling million-dimensional precision estimation previously infeasible.

- Weakness — Block Size Selection: Lacks principled guidance for choosing block size k based on hardware (cache, process grid) and sparsity patterns.

- Weakness — Theoretical Depth: Convergence proofs in Appendix A are brief; needs clearer discussion of how the inexact ADMM variant differs from standard analyses.

- Weakness — Experimental Scope: Comparisons focus on runtime with limited evaluation of statistical performance and trade-offs on real-world data.

- Recommendation 1: Add a subsection with practical guidance for selecting block size k tied to hardware and sparsity considerations.

- Recommendation 2: Expand theoretical discussion to delineate differences between their inexact ADMM and standard convergence results.

- Recommendation 3: Include analysis of statistical accuracy trade-offs from the sparse perturbation techniques in Section 3.1.

- Overall Assessment: Strong, significant contribution with high technical quality and empirical validation; weaknesses are minor and addressable.

- Recommendation: Accept — merits publication at NIPS.

- Pros: Scales to 1M+ dimensions, novel column-block ADMM with convergence guarantees, nearly linear multi-core scalability, clear writing and thorough experiments.

- Cons: Insufficient guidance on optimal block size, some theoretical detail missing, limited exploration of statistical accuracy trade-offs.
