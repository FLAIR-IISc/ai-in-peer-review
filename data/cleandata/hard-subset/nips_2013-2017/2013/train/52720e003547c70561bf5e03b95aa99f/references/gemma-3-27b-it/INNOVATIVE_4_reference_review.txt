This paper tackles the challenging problem of scaling sparse precision matrix estimation to ultra-high dimensions – a setting where p is in the millions. The authors focus on the CLIME estimator and present a distributed ADMM variant, CLIME-ADMM, designed for parallel computation on shared and distributed memory architectures. The core innovations include an inexact ADMM formulation tailored to CLIME, solving the problem in column blocks to leverage matrix multiplication efficiencies, and a block cyclic data distribution scheme for scalability.

The paper’s strength lies in its ambition and the potential impact of the proposed solution. Estimating precision matrices with trillions of parameters is a truly daunting task, and successfully scaling CLIME to this regime represents a significant step forward. The combination of theoretical convergence analysis (O(1/T) rates) with a practical, scalable implementation is commendable. The insights regarding block-wise computation and the careful consideration of data distribution strategies (block cyclic vs. consecutive) demonstrate a deep understanding of the computational bottlenecks. The experimental results, showcasing the algorithm’s performance on a million-dimensional problem with 400 cores, are impressive. The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of scaling machine learning algorithms.

However, the paper is not without its weaknesses. While the theoretical guarantees are stated, the practical implications of the convergence rate (O(1/T)) aren’t thoroughly discussed. How does this rate translate into wall-clock time for realistically sized problems, and how does it compare to other scalable methods in practice? The experimental section, while demonstrating scalability, could benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with alternative scalable approaches specifically designed for large-scale sparse estimation (beyond the listed methods, which don't all directly address this scale). The impact of the “perturbed covariance” approach (Theorem 3) isn’t fully explored experimentally. How much perturbation is needed to achieve the sparsity benefits, and what is the trade-off between accuracy and computational efficiency? The discussion on low-rank structure is somewhat superficial; a more detailed exploration of how the low-rank approximation is implemented and its impact on performance would be valuable. There's a lot of reference dropping, and the discussion feels somewhat dense at times.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

* **Novelty:** The approach of scaling CLIME to this dimensionality is genuinely novel. The combination of inexact ADMM, block-wise computation, and optimized data distribution is a significant contribution.
* **Significance:** Successfully estimating precision matrices at this scale has the potential to unlock new applications in areas like genomics, finance, and sensor networks.
* **Technical soundness:** The theoretical analysis provides a reasonable level of confidence in the algorithm's convergence. The experimental results, while needing expansion, are promising.
* **Boldness:** The paper tackles a problem of immense practical difficulty and proposes a solution that appears to be effective.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* **Incomplete validation:** While scalability is demonstrated, a more thorough comparison with existing scalable methods is needed. The impact of the theoretical results needs further context.
* **Clarity:** The paper could benefit from a more streamlined presentation and a deeper discussion of the practical implications of the theoretical results.
* **Superficial exploration of extensions:** The discussion of low-rank structure and covariance perturbation could be more detailed and supported by more comprehensive experiments.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite some weaknesses, I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field. The authors tackle a very difficult problem with a novel and promising approach. While further validation and clarification are desirable, the potential impact of this work justifies acceptance. I lean towards accepting the paper, prioritizing the novelty and boldness of the approach over methodological perfection. I recommend the authors carefully address the clarity issues and expand the experimental section to include a more thorough comparison with relevant alternatives. The discussion of the theoretical guarantees needs to be grounded in practical considerations.



**Recommendation:** Accept.
