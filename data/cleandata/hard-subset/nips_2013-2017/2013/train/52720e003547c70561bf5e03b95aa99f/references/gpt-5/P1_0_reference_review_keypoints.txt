- Problem & approach: Scales CLIME (sparse precision estimation) to very large p using a column-block linearized (inexact) ADMM with closed-form soft-threshold and box-projection updates.  
- Systems design: Processes blocks of k columns (matrix-matrix GEMM) with block-cyclic distribution, low-rank trick C = AA^T to reduce compute, and both shared-memory (OpenMP) and distributed (MPI + ScaLAPACK) implementations.  
- Theoretical claim: Provides O(1/T) convergence rates for objective suboptimality and KKT residuals under η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)^2.  
- Notational/theoretical gap: Theorem 2’s weighted norm needs C^2 explicitly defined (C^T C vs C^2) and PSD assumptions made explicit.  
- Feasibility statement: The feasibility gap bound is on averaged iterates X̄T; primal feasibility should be stated clearly (e.g., ‖CX̄T − E‖∞ ≤ λ + o(1)).  
- Theorem 3 critique: The covariance perturbation/truncation result is too general/misleading—random perturbation framing is unnecessary and sup-norm of Δ must be O(√(log p/n)) to preserve deviation bounds.  
- Suggested fix for Theorem 3: Replace with deterministic truncation lemma or state precise magnitude/independence conditions for perturbations; cite covariance thresholding literature.  
- Implementation praise: Parallelization choices and block-GEMM approach are sound and consistent with HPC practice.  
- Missing systems analysis: Communication patterns and per-iteration synchronization/communication costs are not quantified.  
- Empirical scaling: Shows favorable scaling and near-linear speedups; headline 1M-dim run (~11 hours on 400 cores) is impressive.  
- Experimental fairness concerns: Methods are tuned to match sparsity, which can bias comparisons; parameter-free runs or solution-path timings would improve fairness.  
- Accuracy evaluation gap: Accuracy (precision/recall) only evaluated on synthetic data; largest-scale estimation error and sparsity of final estimator are not assessed.  
- Missing implementation details: Stopping criteria, choices/sensitivity for λ, ρ, η, and CLIME symmetrization/PD post-processing are not specified.  
- Memory/output question: The 1M-dim experiment needs clarification on memory footprint, storage/writing of the trillion-parameter estimator, and whether the final solution is sparse enough to fit across nodes.  
- Clarity suggestions: Precisely define C^2 and weighted norm, provide default/heuristic choices and sensitivity for parameters, and separate deterministic truncation from random perturbation in presentation.  
- Originality: Novel and useful combination of linearized ADMM, column-block processing, low-rank multiplies, and scalable HPC implementation for CLIME.  
- Significance: Scaling to p ~ 10^6 is practically compelling, but real-world impact depends on realistic n, storage, and end-to-end use cases.  
- Pros: Clear engineering, simple closed-form updates with O(1/T) guarantees, strong systems design, and meaningful large-scale empirical demonstration.  
- Cons: Weak justification for Theorem 3, incomplete discussion of parameter selection/stopping/symmetrization/communication, fairness and large-scale accuracy concerns, and notational clarity issues.  
- Suggested improvements: Replace Theorem 3 with deterministic truncation + citations; add stopping criteria, parameter defaults and sensitivity analysis; specify symmetrization and PD handling; include per-iteration communication/computation breakdown and profiling; report memory footprint and final estimator sparsity; add comparisons to parameter-free TIGER and graphical lasso baselines.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept (leaning accept) pending fixes to theory clarity, implementation details, and experimental completeness.
