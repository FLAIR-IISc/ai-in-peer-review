- Proposal: Introduces CLIME-ADMM, a distributed algorithm for sparse precision matrix estimation in ultra-high dimensions (p >> n).
- Method: Combines inexact ADMM with a novel column-block decomposition and block cyclic data distribution for efficient parallelization.
- Theory: Provides convergence guarantees for the ADMM algorithm.
- Empirics: Demonstrates strong scalability and speedups, including estimating a 1M-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on CLIME and ADMM-based/distributed optimization literature and connects to parallel matrix multiplication and cache optimization research.
- Strength — problem significance: Addresses a critical computational bottleneck in high-dimensional precision estimation.
- Strength — technical novelty: Clever integration of inexact ADMM, column-block decomposition, block cyclic distribution, and low-rank exploitation.
- Strength — experimental validation: Compelling results on shared and distributed memory architectures with detailed implementation insights.
- Strength — presentation: Well-written, organized paper with clear algorithmic explanations.
- Weakness — baselines: Limited comparison to more recent or highly optimized methods (e.g., sketching-based approaches).
- Weakness — parameter sensitivity: Insufficient exploration of the impact of parameters (ρ, η) and lack of automatic tuning strategies.
- Weakness — low-rank assumption: Does not fully address how to determine whether sample covariance has sufficient low-rank structure.
- Weakness — deployment complexity: Framework may be hard to implement/deploy for users unfamiliar with MPI/ScaLAPACK; needs more practical guidance.
- Weakness — polish: Minor typographical errors and inconsistent notation in equations.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
- Revision requests: Expand baseline comparisons, analyze parameter sensitivity and tuning, discuss assessing low-rank suitability, improve deployment guidance, and proofread for typos.
