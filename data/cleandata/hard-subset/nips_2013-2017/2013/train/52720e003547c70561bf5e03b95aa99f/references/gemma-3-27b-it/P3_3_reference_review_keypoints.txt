- Addresses scalable estimation of sparse precision matrices in ultra-high dimensions (n < p) via a distributed approach.
- Builds on the CLIME estimator and proposes a distributed ADMM variant (CLIME-ADMM) with a scalable framework.
- Positions work within distributed ML literature, noting limitations of MapReduce and gradient-descent approaches for constrained problems.
- Demonstrates strong scalability: estimates a 1,000,000-dimensional precision matrix on 400 cores in ~11 hours.
- Provides theoretical analysis showing O(1/T) convergence for objective and residual errors of the inexact ADMM algorithm.
- Introduces practical optimizations: inexact ADMM, column-block updates, and leveraging low-rank structure in matrix multiplication.
- Detailed implementation and comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets with comparisons to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC.
- Practical system-level considerations addressed, including block cyclic data distribution for load balancing.
- Paper is dense with heavy notation; readability and intuitive explanations (e.g., column-block motivation) need improvement.
- Insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (ρ, η, k) and their impact on performance.
- Limited comparison to other distributed optimization frameworks beyond ADMM; deeper evaluation recommended.
- Real-data experiments focus on runtime; offer little interpretation of estimated precision matrices or domain insights.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, technically sound, scalable, and well-evaluated.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, missing hyperparameter analysis, and limited comparative scope.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on improving clarity, hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and broader comparisons.
