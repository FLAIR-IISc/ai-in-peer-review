- Problem: Estimating sparse precision matrices at ultra-high dimensions (p = 10^6) via a distributed ADMM-based approach for CLIME.  
- Key contributions: (1) an inexact ADMM algorithm with O(1/T) convergence for CLIME; (2) a column-block processing strategy enabling efficient matrix-matrix operations; (3) a scalable block-cyclic distributed framework for load balancing.  
- Theoretical strength: Rigorous convergence guarantees (Theorems 1–2) for the proposed inexact ADMM.  
- Computational design: Leverages both sparse and low-rank structures to optimize computation and adapt to data characteristics.  
- Experimental evaluation: Comprehensive comparisons with CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Scalability results: Demonstrates linear scalability across cores and claims estimating a 1M-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Originality: Novel column-block processing for CLIME and practical block-cyclic distribution strategy.  
- Relation to prior work: Clearly differentiates from prior ADMM/GPBS and parameter-server approaches, explaining their unsuitability for this constrained problem.  
- Significance: Opens up applications in genomics, climate science, etc., by making million-dimensional precision estimation feasible.  
- Clarity weakness: Needs clearer connection between O(1/T) convergence rates and real-world performance; more explicit explanation of “inexact” ADMM trade-offs and block-size (k) selection guidance.  
- Theoretical weakness: Lacks detailed discussion on how inexact ADMM affects statistical properties of the estimator beyond convergence rates.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with meaningful theoretical and practical contributions, well-suited for NIPS.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Pro arguments: Addresses a significant problem, provides rigorous guarantees, shows remarkable scalability, differentiates from related work, and supplies practical open-source code.  
- Con arguments: Requires stronger theoretical-statistical connections, better guidance on block size selection, and more justification of the statistical impact of inexact ADMM.
