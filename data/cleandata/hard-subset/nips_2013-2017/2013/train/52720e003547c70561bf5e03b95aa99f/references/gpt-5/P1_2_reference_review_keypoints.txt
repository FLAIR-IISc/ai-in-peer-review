- Paper targets large-scale CLIME precision estimation via a block-wise linearized (inexact) ADMM that updates k columns at a time with closed-form elementwise X (soft-threshold) and Z (box projection) updates and two matrix multiplications per iteration.  
- Authors prove O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and residuals under η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)^2.  
- Computational accelerations: use BLAS-3 matrix–matrix multiplies for blocks, exploit low-rank C = A A' as A(A'X) when n ≪ p, and leverage sparsity in iterates/C.  
- Systems implementation: shared- and distributed-memory scalable design with block-cyclic distribution and ScaLAPACK, showing near-linear scaling to hundreds of cores.  
- Empirical claim: estimated a p = 10^6 precision matrix in ≈11 hours on 400 cores, with comparisons to flare-CLIME, TIGER, and DC-QUIC and scaling studies.  
- Strength: addresses a significant practical bottleneck by scaling precision estimation to millions of variables.  
- Strength: optimization is well-matched to modern architectures and aligns with first-order O(1/T) theory.  
- Strength: careful systems engineering, including block sizing and using A(A'X) to avoid materializing C.  
- Strength: thorough empirical scaling and baseline comparisons on synthetic and real data.  
- Weakness: statistical correctness of “sparsifying C” is misstated—Theorem 3 assumes independent perturbations but zeroing entries induces deterministic, dependent errors.  
- Fix suggestion: restate sparsification result deterministically (|Δ|∞ ≤ τ with τ = O(√(log p/n))) rather than assuming independence.  
- Weakness: CLIME does not enforce symmetry/PD; paper must state the symmetrization (and any PD projection) used in experiments.  
- Weakness: practical parameter selection unclear—η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)^2 requires estimating λ_max(C)=σ_max(A)^2 without forming C; authors should state how σ_max(A) is estimated and show sensitivity to (ρ,η).  
- Weakness: experiments lack stopping criteria, primal/dual residuals, and solution-accuracy metrics (feasibility/suboptimality), especially for the 10^6 run.  
- Weakness: baselines could be broadened and fairness clarified—need more CLIME-specific column-wise ADMM baselines and consistency on block-diagonal thresholding.  
- Weakness: communication complexity is not analyzed per iteration; practitioners need message counts, bandwidth costs for A'X and AY, and synchronization points to choose k and blocks.  
- Weakness: presentation issues—Algorithm 1 indexing/extrapolation in V-update is confusing, some references incomplete, and some figures/tables need to be self-contained.  
- Quality assessment: algorithmic core is sound and well-integrated, computational tricks are standard but effectively combined; sparsification theorem needs a cleaner statement; experimental methodology is solid but missing accuracy details.  
- Clarity assessment: overall clear but needs tightened pseudocode, parameter details, stopping criteria, and post-processing description.  
- Originality: components are known (linearized ADMM, BLAS-3, low-rank multiplies); novelty is in their combination for block-based, low-rank-aware distributed CLIME—incremental but useful.  
- Significance: high practical relevance for large-scale graphical modeling; the systems contribution and scale achieved will interest practitioners and researchers.  
- Key questions for authors to answer: how are ρ and η chosen without forming C and how sensitive is convergence; what symmetrization/PD post-processing was used; what stopping criteria and feasibility/suboptimality were achieved in large runs; for the 10^6 experiment, what were n, per-node memory footprint, and per-iteration communication volume; can you provide an ablation of block size k on compute and communication and heuristics to pick k; please restate the C-sparsification result deterministically.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an important scalability problem with strong systems engineering, demonstrated large-scale performance, and provides convergence guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty, incorrect/misleading statistical claim about sparsifying C, missing reproducibility details on accuracy/stopping/symmetrization, and incomplete baseline fairness.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept—useful, well-engineered contribution that needs corrections on sparsification, more algorithmic details, and accuracy reporting.
