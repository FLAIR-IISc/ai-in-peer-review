This paper presents a distributed algorithm, CLIME-ADMM, for large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation, building upon the CLIME statistical estimator. The core contribution lies in efficiently scaling the computation to ultra-high dimensions (millions of variables) by leveraging inexact ADMM, column-block solving, and optimized matrix multiplications. The authors demonstrate scalability through experiments on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, achieving impressive results, including estimating a million-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours using 400 cores.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a critical bottleneck in precision matrix estimation: computational scalability. The problem statement is clear and concise, and the connection to prior work in statistical estimation and optimization is well-established. The authors effectively position their work within the broader context of scaling machine learning algorithms. The proposed method logically extends existing approaches, such as column-wise CLIME solvers, by introducing block-wise processing and carefully considering matrix multiplication optimization. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Scalability:** The primary strength is the demonstrated scalability to very high dimensions. Achieving a million dimensions with a trillion parameters is a significant accomplishment.
*   **Sound Methodology:** The combination of inexact ADMM, column-block updates, and optimized matrix multiplication is well-justified and appears to be a thoughtful approach.
*   **Theoretical Analysis:**  The inclusion of convergence rate theorems (Theorems 1 & 2) provides a solid theoretical foundation. While the proof technique relies on existing work (cited), the application to CLIME-ADMM is valuable.
*   **Comprehensive Experiments:** The experimental evaluation is thorough, encompassing both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, synthetic and real datasets, and comparisons with relevant existing methods.
*   **Practical Considerations:** The discussion of block cyclic data distribution and the consideration of cache misses demonstrates a practical awareness of performance optimization.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Individual Components:** While the *combination* of techniques is novel, individual components (ADMM, block-wise solving, optimized matrix multiplication) are not entirely new.  The paper would benefit from more clearly articulating the unique synergy achieved by their combination.
*   **Parameter Tuning:**  The paper mentions the need to set parameters like λ and ρ, but doesn't discuss a systematic way to do so. While this is common in these types of algorithms, a brief discussion of practical parameter selection guidelines would be helpful.
*   **Complexity Analysis:** A more detailed complexity analysis comparing CLIME-ADMM to other methods, specifically in terms of computational complexity (e.g., Big O notation) as a function of *p* and *n*, would strengthen the claims of scalability.
*   **Discussion of Low-Rank Structure:** While section 3.2 briefly mentions leveraging the low-rank structure of the covariance matrix, the actual implementation details and performance improvements gained are not clearly quantified in the experiments. It feels somewhat disconnected from the rest of the paper.
*  **Clarity of Algorithm 1:** The description within Algorithm 1 is dense, and the notation (especially with the tilde notation for data distribution) could be clarified.  Adding a small example illustrating the block cyclic distribution would be highly beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a significant challenge in sparse precision matrix estimation – scalability – and offers a practical and effective solution.
*   The experimental results demonstrate compelling performance gains, particularly for large-scale problems.
*   The theoretical analysis provides some guarantees on convergence.
*   The work is well-written and clearly presented, although some minor improvements in clarity could be made.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The individual components of the algorithm are not entirely novel, raising questions about the overall contribution.
*   The lack of a detailed complexity analysis and parameter tuning discussion limits the paper’s practical impact.
*   The connection between the low-rank structure discussion and the experimental results is weak.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of sparse precision matrix estimation. While the individual components may not be groundbreaking, the intelligent combination and successful implementation resulting in significant scalability gains are noteworthy. The thorough experimental evaluation and theoretical grounding further strengthen the paper.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  **Clarify the novelty:** Explicitly discuss the synergy achieved by combining the chosen techniques and what makes their approach uniquely effective.
2.  **Provide parameter tuning guidance:** Include a brief discussion of practical strategies for setting parameters such as λ and ρ.
3.  **Enhance complexity analysis:** Provide a more detailed computational complexity analysis comparing CLIME-ADMM to existing methods.
4.  **Elaborate on low-rank structure:**  Either fully integrate the low-rank optimization into the experiments and quantify the gains, or remove the discussion if it's not a core component of the implementation.
5. **Improve clarity of Algorithm 1**: add a small example for block cyclic distribution.



These revisions would elevate the paper and further solidify its contribution to the NIPS community.
