- Significant contribution: proposes distributed CLIME-ADMM to scale sparse precision estimation to millions of dimensions/trillions of parameters; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Addresses high-dimensional precision matrix estimation with limited samples, building on CLIME and distributed ADMM and exploiting separable structure for parallelism.
- Novel technical innovations: inexact ADMM, column-block processing, and careful data distribution strategies tuned to hardware.
- Demonstrated scalability: solved a 1 million-dimensional problem in ~11 hours on 400 cores.
- Strong theoretical analysis: convergence rate results (Theorems 1 and 2) and discussion of leveraging sparse/low-rank structure.
- Practical system optimizations: block-cyclic distribution, cache-aware implementation, and column-block processing for efficiency.
- Comprehensive experiments: comparisons to state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real data and analysis of speedups in shared/distributed memory.
- Clear writing and organization despite paper length; some details are placed in the supplement.
- Paper is dense and can be hard to follow; many details relegated to the supplement.
- Lacks practical guidance on parameter tuning (ρ, η, block size) for different problem instances.
- Limited comparisons to specialized methods tailored to particular sparse matrix structures.
- Real-data evaluation focuses on runtime; lacks deeper analysis of recovered precision matrices or network structure.
- Suggested revisions: simplify presentation, add parameter-tuning guidelines, expand real-data analysis (visualizations/ground-truth comparisons), and clarify data-distribution assumptions (e.g., sub-Gaussianity).
