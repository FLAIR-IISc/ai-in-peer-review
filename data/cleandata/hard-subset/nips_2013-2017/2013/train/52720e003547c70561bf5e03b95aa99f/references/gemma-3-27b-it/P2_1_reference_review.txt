## Review of "Large Scale Distributed Sparse Precision Estimation"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the challenging problem of estimating sparse precision matrices in ultra-high dimensional settings (p >> n) by presenting a distributed and scalable algorithm, CLIME-ADMM. The authors build upon the CLIME estimator and the ADMM optimization framework, introducing several key innovations: an inexact ADMM variant tailored to CLIME, column-block updates for improved matrix multiplication efficiency, and a block cyclic data distribution strategy for scalable parallel computation. The paper demonstrates strong theoretical guarantees (convergence rates) and empirical performance, showcasing the ability to estimate a precision matrix with one million dimensions and one trillion parameters in a reasonable timeframe (11 hours on 400 cores).

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper positions itself within the growing body of work on scalable machine learning algorithms. It correctly identifies the limitations of existing methods for precision matrix estimation when dealing with truly massive datasets.  The work builds on prior ADMM-based approaches (e.g., [23, 9]) but distinguishes itself through its focus on column-block updates and a more general block cyclic distribution scheme for optimal cache utilization.  It also connects to recent advances in sparse and low-rank matrix computations [15, 5, 11] and acknowledges the broader trend of scaling up machine learning algorithms via parallelization [29, 22, 6, 7, 20, 2, 23, 9].  The authors correctly reference relevant literature on CLIME [3] and related sparse estimation techniques.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Estimating precision matrices in ultra-high dimensions is crucial for many applications, and existing methods struggle to scale.
* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm appears well-motivated and theoretically justified. The convergence analysis (Theorems 1 & 2) provides confidence in the algorithm’s behavior.
* **Scalability:** The demonstrated ability to handle a 1 million-dimensional problem on 400 cores is impressive and highlights the algorithm's scalability. The block-cyclic distribution and column-block updates are key contributions.
* **Novelty:** The combination of inexact ADMM, column-block updates, and a thoughtful data distribution strategy represents a novel approach to this problem.  The perturbation technique (Theorem 3) is a clever way to leverage sparsity.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results provide compelling evidence for the algorithm’s performance and scalability, comparing favorably to existing methods.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the key concepts and algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Detail in Experiments:** While the experiments demonstrate scalability, they lack detail regarding parameter tuning (beyond mentioning setting sparsity to 0.05).  A more thorough sensitivity analysis would strengthen the claims.  What are the trade-offs involved in choosing different block sizes (k)?  How does performance vary with different levels of sparsity?
* **Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** The comparison to existing algorithms is somewhat limited.  While comparisons to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC are presented, a more comprehensive benchmark against the latest scalable methods would be valuable.  The authors acknowledge Tiger’s superior recall but don’t fully address why CLIME-ADMM might be preferred in other scenarios.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** Implementing the described framework, especially the distributed memory version with block cyclic distribution, seems complex and requires significant engineering effort. This isn't necessarily a weakness of the *research*, but it's a practical consideration for potential users.
* **Low-Rank Assumption:** The paper mentions leveraging the low-rank structure of the sample covariance matrix, but the discussion is somewhat superficial. More details about *how* this is exploited in practice and the conditions under which it's effective would be beneficial.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* This paper presents a significant advance in the field of scalable precision matrix estimation.
* The technical contributions are substantial and well-supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results.
* The algorithm addresses a real and important problem in machine learning.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental section could be more thorough and provide more detailed analysis of the algorithm's behavior.
* The comparison to state-of-the-art methods could be more comprehensive.
* The practical complexity of implementing the framework is a potential barrier to adoption.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept** for NIPS. The technical contributions are significant, the problem is important, and the results are compelling. While the experimental section could be strengthened and a more comprehensive comparison to existing methods would be beneficial, the overall quality of the work is very high.  The authors successfully address a challenging problem with a novel and scalable solution.



**Quality:** Excellent
**Clarity:** Very Good
**Originality:** Excellent
**Significance:** Excellent
