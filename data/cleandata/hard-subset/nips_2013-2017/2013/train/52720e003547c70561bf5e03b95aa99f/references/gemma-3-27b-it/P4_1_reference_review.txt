This paper addresses the problem of large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation, specifically focusing on scaling the CLIME estimator to dimensions in the millions. The authors propose a distributed CLIME-ADMM algorithm leveraging column-block updates and efficient matrix multiplication techniques. The key innovations lie in an inexact ADMM formulation, solving the problem in column blocks to facilitate matrix-matrix operations, and a block cyclic data distribution scheme for efficient parallel computation.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the challenges of existing methods in handling ultra-high dimensional data. It correctly positions the work within the broader context of scaling machine learning algorithms and highlights the limitations of approaches like parameter servers and graph projection block splitting. The theoretical analysis provides convergence rate guarantees for the proposed algorithm, and the experimental results demonstrate significant speedups compared to existing methods, achieving the estimation of a million-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours using 400 cores. The organization of the paper is logical, with a clear presentation of the methodology, theoretical results, and empirical validation. The notation is generally clear, although some symbols are not explicitly defined upon first use (e.g., `η`).

**Strengths:**

*   **Scalability:** The primary strength of this work is its demonstrated scalability to truly large-scale problems (millions of dimensions). This is a significant contribution, as existing methods struggle with such dimensions.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical analysis provides convergence guarantees, supporting the validity of the proposed algorithm. The use of inexact ADMM and column-block updates appears well-justified.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the algorithmic details and the rationale behind the design choices. The discussion of the block cyclic distribution scheme is particularly helpful.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results provide strong evidence of the algorithm's effectiveness and scalability. Comparing against existing methods solidifies the improvements.
*   **Novelty:** The combination of inexact ADMM with column-block updates and a carefully designed data distribution scheme represents a novel approach to tackling this challenging problem.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Comparison Depth:** While comparisons are made to existing methods, the scope is somewhat limited. A more thorough comparison with highly optimized libraries or specialized solvers might be beneficial.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity Discussion:** The impact of key parameters (e.g., `λ`, `ρ`, block size `k`) on performance is not thoroughly explored. A sensitivity analysis would enhance the understanding of the algorithm's behavior.
*   **Implementation Details:** The paper briefly mentions OpenMP and ScaLAPACK, but lacks detailed implementation considerations. Describing the specific optimization techniques used for matrix multiplication and data distribution could be valuable.
*   **Theoretical Proofs:** The reference to "Appendix A in the supplement" is a standard practice, but a brief summary of the key ideas behind the proof in the main paper would make it more accessible.
*   **Real-World Data:** Although real data is used, the claims of performance improvements on these datasets are not compelling. The comparison to other algorithms is not as extensive as the synthetic data.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Significant scalability to a challenging problem with broad relevance.
*   Solid theoretical foundation and convergence guarantees.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.
*   Strong empirical validation demonstrating substantial speedups.
*   Novel combination of techniques.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Limited depth of comparison with existing methods.
*   Lack of a detailed parameter sensitivity analysis.
*   Insufficient details regarding implementation considerations.
*   Somewhat limited exploration of the algorithm's performance on real-world data.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation. The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm addresses a critical bottleneck in many applications, and the demonstrated scalability is truly impressive. While there are some minor weaknesses related to comparison depth and implementation details, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns. This work is of high quality and is worthy of acceptance to NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above. Specifically, I suggest the authors expand the comparisons to include more competitive baselines, provide a more thorough parameter sensitivity analysis, and elaborate on the implementation details of the matrix multiplication and data distribution schemes.
