- Summary: Proposes a distributed column-block inexact ADMM variant of CLIME that uses block cyclic distribution and sparse/low-rank acceleration to estimate a 1M-dimensional precision matrix (1T parameters) in ~11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Quality: Technically sound with rigorous convergence (Theorems 1–2) and comprehensive experiments showing near-linear speedup and clear advantage over CLIME/Tiger/DC-QUIC.  
- Clarity: Well-organized and readable; Algorithm 1 is implementable and figures are effective; Section 4 needs slightly more detail on block cyclic implementation.  
- Originality: Novel combination of column-block processing, inexact ADMM, and block cyclic distribution with new convergence analysis for the column-block approach.  
- Significance: Substantially advances scalability of precision estimation to p=10^6, enabling applications in genomics, climate, etc., and generalizable to other large constrained optimization tasks.  
- Strengths: Solves an unsolved practical problem, provides O(1/T) guarantees, strong empirical results (including 1M-dim demo), and an effective parallelization strategy.  
- Weaknesses: Lacks evaluation for non-sparse precision matrices, more detailed distributed complexity analysis, broader comparison to the newest large-scale methods, and discussion of λ’s effect on scalability.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept (final score 8); meets NIPS criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and impact.
