- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a distributed algorithm for sparse precision matrix estimation in ultra-high dimensional settings (p >> n).  
- Introduces an inexact ADMM tailored to CLIME, solving in column blocks to exploit matrix multiplication efficiencies.  
- Uses a block cyclic data distribution scheme to achieve balanced parallelization.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees (Theorems 1 & 2) for the inexact ADMM formulation.  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability up to one million dimensions on 400 cores with strong performance vs. some existing methods.  
- Well-written, clearly organized, and situates the work with a comprehensive related-work review.  
- Limited experimental comparison to the strongest state-of-the-art distributed baselines.  
- Lacks a systematic parameter sensitivity and robustness analysis (λ, ρ, η, block size k) and auto-tuning discussion.  
- Explanation of how the low-rank structure of the sample covariance is exploited is too high-level; impact on runtime not fully quantified.  
- Insufficient implementation details about distributed linear algebra choices (ScaLAPACK block sizes, communication strategies), hurting reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: significant, technically sound contribution with high impact potential.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, with suggested revisions to address comparative experiments, parameter sensitivity, low-rank exploitation, and implementation detail gaps.  
- Qualitative scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
