- Summary: Scales the CLIME sparse precision estimator to ultra-large dimensions via an inexact ADMM, column-block processing, and a distributed computation framework, achieving a million-dimensional estimate in 11 hours on 400 cores.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on statistical and computational sparse precision literature and distributed ML systems, differentiating via a general blocking scheme and better handling of global constraints.

- Strength — Practical relevance: Addresses a crucial scalability bottleneck for high-dimensional precision estimation in real applications.

- Strength — Sound methodology: Uses a well-motivated inexact ADMM with convergence analysis (standard O(1/T) rate).

- Strength — Scalability: Demonstrates convincing shared- and distributed-memory speedups, including a million-dimensional run.

- Strength — Implementation detail: Provides thoughtful data distribution and cache-optimization implementation details.

- Strength — Clear presentation: Paper is well-written and organized, making technical content accessible.

- Weakness — Convergence rate: O(1/T) is standard for ADMM and not a theoretical advance; practical implications need discussion.

- Weakness — Parameter tuning: Sensitivity of performance to the tuning parameter λ is not thoroughly evaluated.

- Weakness — Limited baselines: Experimental comparisons omit some recent, highly optimized sparse precision libraries and broader scalability ranges.

- Weakness — Low-rank exploitation: Low-rank and sparse-structure optimizations are discussed but not integrated into experiments; benefits remain unrealized.

- Weakness — Reproducibility: Code is not provided, limiting reproducibility and impact.

- Arguments for acceptance: Tackles critical scalability problem, presents technically sound and practical methods with strong empirical results and implementation guidance.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited theoretical novelty, incomplete experimental comparisons and sensitivity analyses, and unexploited low-rank opportunities.

- Overall assessment: Strong, practically significant paper that successfully scales CLIME; weaknesses are minor and addressable.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Suggested revisions: discuss practical implications of O(1/T); perform λ sensitivity analysis; compare to additional state-of-the-art libraries; provide preliminary low-rank optimization results if possible; release code to improve reproducibility.
