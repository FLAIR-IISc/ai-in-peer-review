- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a distributed algorithm using inexact ADMM, column-block processing, and block-cyclic data distribution for scalable sparse precision estimation.
- Demonstrates strong theoretical convergence guarantees for objective and constraint residuals.
- Empirically achieves substantial speedups on shared- and distributed-memory systems, estimating a 1M-dimension precision matrix in ~11 hours on 400 cores.
- Builds on CLIME and prior ADMM-based estimators, situating the work within large-scale ML and distributed optimization literature.
- Novel integration of techniques (inexact ADMM + column blocks + block-cyclic distribution) for parallelization and memory management.
- Justifies column-block processing via optimized matrix multiplication despite CLIME’s column-wise separability.
- Practical discussion on exploiting sparse and low-rank structure to further improve performance.
- Well-written and organized presentation with a concrete algorithm description (Algorithm 1).
- Limited baseline comparisons; more recent scalable methods should be included or justified.
- Lacks thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (λ, ρ, η, block size) and its impact on accuracy/runtime.
- Experimental reliance on assumed low-rank structure may be optimistic and could misrepresent real-world performance.
- Minor clarity issues in notation and explanation, notably the block-cyclic distribution description.
- Technically sound and complete, with supported claims from theory and experiments.
- Original contribution with potential high impact across domains like genomics, finance, and imaging.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand comparisons and include parameter sensitivity analysis.
