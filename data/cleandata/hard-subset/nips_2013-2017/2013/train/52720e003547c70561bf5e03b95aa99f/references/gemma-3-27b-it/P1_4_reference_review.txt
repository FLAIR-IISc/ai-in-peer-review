## Peer Review for "Large Scale Distributed Sparse Precision Estimation"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a distributed algorithm, CLIME-ADMM, for estimating sparse precision matrices in ultra-high dimensional settings (p >> n). The authors address a critical bottleneck in precision matrix estimation – the computational cost of solving the CLIME optimization problem for massive datasets. Their approach builds on the CLIME estimator and leverages an inexact ADMM formulation, combined with a novel column-block decomposition strategy and a block cyclic data distribution scheme for efficient parallelization. The paper demonstrates both theoretical convergence guarantees and empirical scalability, showing significant speedups on shared and distributed memory architectures, even estimating a 1 million-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon a substantial body of work in sparse precision matrix estimation (e.g., [3, 19, 25]). It acknowledges and improves upon existing distributed optimization frameworks like parameter servers [6] and ADMM-based approaches [23, 9], addressing their limitations for constrained optimization problems and focusing on efficient data distribution for matrix operations.  The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of scaling machine learning algorithms [29, 22, 6, 7, 20, 2, 23, 9].  The connections to existing work on parallel matrix multiplication [15, 5, 11] and cache optimization [16, 5, 15] are well-articulated.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a critical problem:** Estimating precision matrices in ultra-high dimensions is a significant challenge, and this paper offers a practical solution.
* **Novel combination of techniques:** The inexact ADMM, column-block decomposition, and block cyclic distribution represent a clever combination of existing ideas tailored to this specific problem. The recognition and exploitation of low-rank structure is also a key strength.
* **Strong theoretical grounding:** The paper provides convergence guarantees for the ADMM algorithm, lending credibility to the approach.
* **Excellent empirical validation:** The experimental results demonstrate significant scalability and speedups on both shared and distributed memory architectures. The demonstration of estimating a 1 million-dimensional matrix is particularly impressive.
* **Clear writing and organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized, with clear explanations of the algorithm and its components.
* **Detailed implementation considerations:** The discussion of block cyclic distribution and its advantages over other schemes, and the attention to cache optimization, are valuable insights for practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited comparison to truly competitive baselines:** While comparisons are made to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC, the evaluation doesn’t fully explore more recent or highly optimized implementations for large-scale precision matrix estimation.  It would be valuable to compare against methods that explicitly exploit sparsity and low-rank structure, such as those incorporating sketching techniques.
* **Parameter sensitivity:** While the paper touches on parameter tuning, more in-depth exploration of the impact of parameters like ρ and η on performance and convergence would be beneficial. A discussion of strategies for automatic parameter selection would be helpful.
* **Assumptions about low-rank structure:** The exploitation of low-rank structure is a significant optimization, but the paper doesn’t fully address how to determine if the sample covariance matrix exhibits sufficient low-rankness to benefit from this approach.
* **Complexity of the framework:**  Implementing and deploying the distributed framework may be complex for users unfamiliar with MPI and ScaLAPACK. A more detailed discussion of practical deployment considerations would be valuable.
* **Minor typographical errors and inconsistencies:** A few minor errors were observed in the notation and equations, particularly related to matrix dimensions.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a very important and challenging problem with a novel and well-motivated approach.
* The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the proposed algorithm.
* The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate significant scalability.
* The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the technical details.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The comparison to existing baselines could be more comprehensive.
* The parameter sensitivity and practical deployment considerations could be addressed more thoroughly.
* Minor typographical errors detract from the overall polish of the paper.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistical estimation and distributed optimization.  The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm offers a practical and scalable solution for a challenging problem.  The strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses, and I **recommend acceptance** with minor revisions.

**Recommendations for Revision:**

* **Expand the baseline comparisons:**  Include comparisons to more state-of-the-art methods for large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation, particularly those leveraging sketching or other dimensionality reduction techniques.
* **Discuss parameter sensitivity:**  Provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of key parameters (ρ, η) on performance, and explore strategies for automatic parameter selection.
* **Address low-rank assumption:**  Discuss methods for assessing whether the sample covariance matrix exhibits sufficient low-rankness to justify the use of the low-rank approximation.
* **Improve deployment guidance:** Add a section discussing practical considerations for deploying the distributed framework, including software dependencies and configuration requirements.
* **Proofread carefully:**  Correct any typographical errors and inconsistencies in the notation and equations.
