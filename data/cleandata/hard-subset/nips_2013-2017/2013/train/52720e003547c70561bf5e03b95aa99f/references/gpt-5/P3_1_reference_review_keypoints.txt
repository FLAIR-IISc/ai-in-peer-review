- Paper targets ultra–high-dimensional CLIME precision estimation with a distributed block-parallel ADMM that scales to p ≈ 10^6.  
- Core idea: reformulate CLIME with a splitting variable and use linearized/inexact ADMM where the X-step is a closed-form soft-threshold.  
- Use column-block updates (k columns) to enable BLAS-3 matrix–matrix products and improve cache locality.  
- Exploit low-rank C = AA′ (p ≫ n) to compute CX as A(A′X), reducing complexity from O(p^2k) to O(npk).  
- Employ block-cyclic data distribution for load balance across nodes.  
- Theoretical claim: O(1/T) convergence rates for objective gap and residuals under η ≥ ρ λ_max^2(C).  
- Experiments show strong speedups on shared and distributed memory and a 1e6-dimensional run in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Comparisons to TIGER, CLIME (flare), and DC-QUIC suggest better scalability.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on CLIME consistency literature and existing scalable solvers/ADMM, novelty lies in distributed block-parallel integration.  
- Strength: Addresses an important practical scaling challenge and is compatible with shared and distributed memory.  
- Strength: Low-rank exploitation is natural and materially reduces computational cost for p≫n.  
- Strength: Clear separation of dense matrix multiplies from elementwise updates enables efficient blocking and cache use.  
- Strength: Provides convergence analysis for linearized ADMM with a simple sufficient condition on η.  
- Strength: Comprehensive systems evaluation including scaling studies and a large-scale run.  
- Weakness: Inconsistent ADMM/augmented Lagrangian notation and derivation mismatch with the implemented (scaled) form—needs fixing.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1’s statement is unusual; should explicitly state ergodic optimality gap and feasibility violation.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 mixes norm definitions and needs a precise weighted-norm definition and PSD-condition explanation.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3 (perturbed covariance) is under-specified/incorrect as stated; requires precise sub-exponential parameters or replacement by a deterministic triangle-inequality argument.  
- Weakness: No discussion of CLIME post-processing (symmetrization and optional PD projection) and its computational cost.  
- Clarity issues: notational inconsistencies, typos (e.g., box(·) with E, process grid notation), and ambiguous norm/eigenvalue definitions.  
- Reproducibility gap: parameter selection and stopping criteria (ρ, η, λ, k, tolerances) are under-specified; practical guidance is needed.  
- Experimental fairness: comparisons mix single-core and multi-core runs; baselines should be parallelized and reported per hardware resource.  
- Originality: method is an effective integration of known optimization and HPC techniques rather than fundamentally new theory.  
- Significance: Strong systems contribution enabling CLIME at extreme scales, valuable if code is released.  
- Statistical gap: lacks analysis of how approximate optimization and covariance perturbations affect estimation error; experiments prioritize speed over estimation quality.  
- Suggestions: fix ADMM derivation/notation and add proof sketches for Theorems 1–2 clarifying η’s role.  
- Suggestions: replace or rigorously restate Theorem 3, or use deterministic bounds and cite standard thresholding results.  
- Suggestions: clarify/implement symmetrization and PD projection, and report their costs.  
- Suggestions: provide ablations and guidance on choosing k, ρ, η, and stopping tolerances and show sensitivity.  
- Suggestions: report memory per node, communication costs, time breakdown (multiplies vs elementwise), and low-rank vs dense/sparse multiply ablation.  
- Suggestions: ensure fair baselines (parallel TIGER, block-diagonal exploitation) and consistent wall-clock vs core-count reporting.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical scalability, solid empirical scaling, and broadly adoptable algorithmic components.  
- Cons for acceptance: theoretical inconsistencies (especially Theorem 3), limited statistical-accuracy analysis, and writing/notation issues.  
- Overall: borderline accept pending fixes to theoretical claims, clarity, and fairer comparisons.
