- Paper proposes a distributed column-block ADMM algorithm for CLIME to estimate sparse precision matrices at ultra-high dimensions (up to 1M, 1T parameters).  
- Key innovation: solve in column blocks with block-cyclic distribution to exploit sparse and low-rank structure for efficient parallelization.  
- Achieves practical scalability: 1M dimensions in ~11 hours on 400 cores, outperforming CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC.  
- Provides O(1/T) convergence guarantees for objective and constraint residuals, with rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive across synthetic and real datasets (Leukemia, Climate) and both shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Positions work within distributed optimization literature and clearly contrasts with previous parallelization methods.  
- Major strength: addresses a challenging, high-impact problem with substantial practical implications for genomics, climate science, etc.  
- Major strength: strong theoretical foundation with provable convergence rates tailored to the column-block ADMM setting.  
- Major strength: novel algorithmic approach combining column-block processing and block-cyclic distribution for parallel efficiency.  
- Major strength: rigorous, well-designed experiments demonstrating scalability and comparison to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Major strength: clear presentation and effective use of figures to explain the distributed framework.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on whether the distributed implementation preserves statistical guarantees of original CLIME.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation guidance on selecting block size k and its relation to dimensions and hardware.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to some recent distributed precision-estimation methods (e.g., newer distributed graphical lasso approaches).  
- Weakness: scalability analysis stops at 400 cores and does not fully explore diminishing returns or parallel-efficiency limits at larger scales.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and significant contribution with minor concerns; recommended for acceptance.
