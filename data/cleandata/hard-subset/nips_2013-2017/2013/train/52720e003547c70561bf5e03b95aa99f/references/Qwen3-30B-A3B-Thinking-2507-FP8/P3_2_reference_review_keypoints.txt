- Addresses ultra-high-dimensional precision matrix estimation (up to p=10^6) via a distributed Column Block ADMM variant for CLIME.  
- Exploits column-block decomposition and sparse/low-rank structure to improve scalability over column-wise methods.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and constraint residuals (Theorems 1–2).  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability on shared- and distributed-memory systems, estimating a 1M-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Shows clear empirical advantages over existing methods (CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC) for very large problems.  
- The statistical effects of using a perturbed covariance matrix (Section 3.1) are only partially addressed; implications for final estimator need more discussion.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes clear pseudocode and consistent notation.  
- Figures effectively illustrate scalability, but the theoretical link between block size and convergence rate is not explored.  
- The column-block approach and block-cyclic distribution for load balancing are novel and practically valuable.  
- The work meaningfully extends prior CLIME/ADMM/distributed optimization ideas to an unprecedented scale.  
- Significant practical impact for fields requiring massive precision-matrix estimation (e.g., genomics, climate science).  
- Empirical scaling is near-linear with cores, a major practical contribution.  
- Strength: tackles a critical scalability challenge in precision matrix estimation.  
- Strength: supplies rigorous convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: delivers impressive empirical results and an implemented distributed framework.  
- Strength: implementation and practical details are provided.  
- Weakness: effect of block size on solution quality and convergence is under-explored.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to the very latest distributed precision-matrix methods (e.g., [22,23]).  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis doesn’t explain why larger blocks yield better speedup.  
- Weakness: communication overhead in distributed settings is not analytically quantified (only via speedups).  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of statistical implications from covariance perturbation.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution despite areas needing deeper analysis.  
- Specific suggestion: expand discussion on block size vs. convergence and solution quality.  
- Specific suggestion: compare with more recent distributed methods.  
- Specific suggestion: include analysis of communication overhead in distributed settings.  
- Specific suggestion: clarify statistical consequences of using a perturbed covariance matrix.
