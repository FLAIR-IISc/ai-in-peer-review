## Peer Review: Large Scale Distributed Sparse Precision Estimation

**Summary:**

This paper presents a distributed algorithm, CLIME-ADMM, for estimating sparse precision matrices in ultra-high-dimensional settings (p >> n). Building upon the CLIME estimator, the authors leverage inexact ADMM and a column-block decomposition strategy to achieve scalability to millions of dimensions and thousands of cores. Key innovations include exploiting low-rank structure in the sample covariance matrix and employing block cyclic data distribution for efficient parallel computation. The authors provide theoretical convergence guarantees and demonstrate significant speedups compared to existing methods through experiments on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, showcasing the ability to estimate a million-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours using 400 cores.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The work builds upon a strong foundation of research in sparse precision matrix estimation (Cai et al., Banerjee et al., Yuan et al.) and distributed optimization (Boyd et al., Dean et al., Niu et al.).  It addresses a critical gap in scaling existing methods (CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC) to truly massive datasets. The paper acknowledges the limitations of MapReduce and parameter server approaches for constrained optimization and leverages advancements in parallel matrix multiplication (Choi et al., Goto et al.) to accelerate computation. It appropriately positions itself within the broader trend of scaling machine learning algorithms for "Big Data" (Low et al., Raina et al.).

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Estimating high-dimensional precision matrices is a fundamental task with applications in various fields, and scaling existing methods to truly massive datasets is a significant challenge.
* **Novel combination of techniques:** The combination of inexact ADMM, column-block decomposition, leveraging low-rank structure, and block cyclic data distribution represents a novel approach to distributed precision matrix estimation.
* **Strong theoretical foundation:**  The paper provides theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm, supporting its effectiveness.
* **Compelling experimental results:**  The experimental results demonstrate significant speedups and scalability compared to existing methods, validating the practical benefits of the proposed approach.  The ability to estimate a million-dimensional precision matrix in a reasonable timeframe is particularly impressive.
* **Clear and well-structured presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a clear presentation of the algorithm, theoretical analysis, and experimental setup.
* **Practical considerations:** The discussion of block size optimization and memory hierarchy considerations demonstrates a practical awareness of the challenges in implementing scalable algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited comparison to state-of-the-art:** While comparisons are made to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC, the evaluation primarily focuses on runtime. A more thorough comparison of solution accuracy (e.g., precision, recall of estimated graph structure) across different methods would be valuable. The justification for choosing the compared methods feels slightly ad-hoc.
* **Low-rank assumption:** The performance gains from exploiting low-rank structure are significant, but the paper doesn't fully discuss the conditions under which this assumption is valid or how to assess it in practice.  More exploration of the sensitivity of the algorithm to the rank of the sample covariance matrix would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter sensitivity:** The performance of ADMM algorithms is often sensitive to the choice of parameters (rho, eta). The paper mentions the setting of η ≥ ρλ2max(C) but a more detailed discussion of parameter tuning strategies and their impact on performance would be useful.
* **Details of distributed implementation:** While the framework is described, more detail on the specific distributed computing environment (e.g., network bandwidth, communication overhead) would provide better context for understanding the scalability results.
* **Repetitive language:** Some sections contain redundant phrasing, and the paper could benefit from some editing to improve conciseness.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper tackles a crucial problem in high-dimensional statistics with a novel and well-motivated approach.
* The theoretical guarantees and experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness and scalability of the proposed algorithm.
* The work represents a significant contribution to the field of distributed optimization and sparse precision matrix estimation.
* The authors address practical considerations related to implementation and performance optimization.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The comparison to other methods is somewhat limited, particularly in terms of solution accuracy.
* The reliance on the low-rank assumption and the lack of detailed parameter tuning analysis could be seen as limitations.
* The distributed implementation details could be more comprehensive.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**. The authors present a significant contribution to the field of scalable precision matrix estimation. The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm is a practical and efficient solution for ultra-high-dimensional problems, and the theoretical and experimental results are compelling. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. The paper addresses an important problem, proposes a novel approach, and provides strong evidence of its effectiveness.  Addressing the weaknesses identified above in a revision would further strengthen the paper.

**Confidence:** 8/10
