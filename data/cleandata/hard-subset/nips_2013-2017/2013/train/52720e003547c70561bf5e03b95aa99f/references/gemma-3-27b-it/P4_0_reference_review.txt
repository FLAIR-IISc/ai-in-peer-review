This paper addresses the problem of large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation, a crucial task in various fields. The authors focus on scaling the CLIME estimator, known for its statistical guarantees and computational advantages, to problems with millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters. They present a distributed CLIME-ADMM algorithm incorporating several key innovations: an inexact ADMM variant, column-block solving, and leveraging sparse and low-rank structure.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the challenges of scaling existing methods to ultra-high dimensions. The authors correctly position their work within the broader context of scaling machine learning algorithms and appropriately cite relevant literature, including recent NIPS publications. The proposed approach builds upon existing ADMM-based solvers for CLIME but significantly extends their scalability through algorithmic innovations and a thoughtful parallelization framework. The theoretical analysis demonstrating convergence rates is a strong point, and the empirical validation on both synthetic and real datasets convincingly demonstrates the algorithm’s performance and scalability, achieving impressive results – estimating a million-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.

**Strengths:**

* **Scalability:** The primary strength is the demonstrated scalability to problems with a massive number of variables and parameters, overcoming limitations of existing methods.
* **Theoretical Analysis:** Providing convergence rate analysis for the proposed inexact ADMM variant strengthens the technical soundness of the work.
* **Algorithmic Innovations:** The column-block solving strategy and the exploitation of sparse and low-rank structure are valuable contributions for enhancing efficiency.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** Experiments on both synthetic and real datasets, coupled with comparisons to existing algorithms, provide strong evidence of the method's effectiveness.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, clearly explaining the key ideas and technical details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the scalability is impressive, the implementation details, particularly regarding the block cyclic distribution and optimal block size selection, appear complex. The paper would benefit from more explicit guidance on practical implementation considerations.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The performance appears sensitive to the block size (k). While explored in the experiments, a more systematic analysis of the impact of this parameter, and perhaps a heuristic for selecting it automatically, would be beneficial.
* **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art:** While comparisons are made, the number of competing methods considered is relatively limited. Including a comparison to more recent and highly optimized solvers for sparse inverse covariance estimation would strengthen the results.
* **Assumptions about Low Rank:** The discussion of leveraging low-rank structure mentions using it as an optimization. More details about how this is actually implemented and the impact on performance and statistical guarantees would be helpful.
* **Discussion of Memory Requirements:** While mentioned, a more detailed analysis of the memory requirements for different configurations (number of cores, block size) would be useful for practitioners.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant challenge in sparse precision matrix estimation—scalability to ultra-high dimensions—with a novel and effective approach.
* The theoretical analysis and empirical validation provide strong evidence of the algorithm’s correctness and performance.
* The innovations introduced (inexact ADMM, column-block solving, leveraging structure) represent valuable contributions to the field.
* The work is relevant to a broad audience interested in large-scale machine learning and statistical inference.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The implementation complexity and parameter sensitivity might limit the practical adoption of the method.
* The limited comparison to state-of-the-art algorithms leaves some questions about the method's relative performance.
* The discussion on leveraging low-rank structure could be more detailed and concrete.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of sparse precision matrix estimation. The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm effectively addresses the challenge of scalability, and the theoretical and empirical results are convincing. While some aspects of the implementation and parameter tuning could be further clarified, the overall quality of the work is high. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the weaknesses identified above, primarily focusing on providing more practical guidance for implementation and a more comprehensive comparison to alternative methods.
