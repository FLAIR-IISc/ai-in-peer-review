- Summary: Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a distributed inexact-ADMM algorithm solving CLIME in column blocks with efficient matrix multiplication, scaling to a 1M-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on CLIME and distributed ADMM literature, distinguishing itself by exploiting block structure and fast matrix multiplication for massive-scale problems.  
- Strength: Tackles an important scalability problem with a technically sound algorithm and convergence analysis.  
- Strength: Demonstrates convincing scalability on shared- and distributed-memory systems with impressive large-scale experiments.  
- Strength: Includes practical optimizations (sparse/low-rank exploitation, block cyclic distribution) and clear presentation.  
- Weakness: Empirical comparisons are limited, focusing more on runtime than estimation quality (e.g., false positives/negatives).  
- Weakness: Algorithm likely sensitive to hyperparameters (λ, ρ, block size k) with insufficient tuning guidance.  
- Weakness: Reliance on low-rank sample covariance acceleration lacks strong justification for practical settings.  
- Weakness: Some theoretical and implementation details are under-specified (e.g., perturbation Δ in Theorem 3, cache optimizations); assumptions on η in Theorems 1–2 need clearer practical implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant problem, scalable and sound method, theoretical guarantees, and compelling experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited comparisons, unresolved parameter sensitivity, questionable low-rank assumption, and missing details.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable contributions; minor weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: broaden empirical comparisons and accuracy metrics; provide parameter-tuning guidance; justify low-rank assumptions; clarify theoretical assumptions and perturbation details; expand cache/implementation details.
