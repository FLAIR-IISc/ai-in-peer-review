- Significant practical contribution: scales sparse precision estimation to ~1M dimensions (1 trillion parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores, addressing real-world needs.
- Well-motivated technical approach: column-block processing and block-cyclic distribution exploit matrix-matrix operations and load balancing for efficiency.
- Strong experimental validation: comprehensive comparisons (synthetic and real), near-linear speedups on shared and distributed architectures, and demonstration that ADMM scales to massive problems.
- Practical insights: analysis of iterate sparsity and a perturbation approach to sparsify the sample covariance while preserving required deviation bounds.
- Limited theoretical novelty: convergence results (O(1/T)) follow standard inexact ADMM analyses and no new guarantees specific to the column-block approach are provided.
- Block size impact unclear: the effect of block size k on solution accuracy is not thoroughly investigated.
- Low-rank assumption ambiguity: experiments do not specify n/p ratios, so claimed low-rank advantages are not quantified.
- Comparison with recent work lacking: should include and distinguish from more recent distributed/ADMM-based methods (e.g., [23], [9]).
- Overall assessment: strong practical paper with thorough experiments and valuable insights, but modest theoretical novelty.
- Recommendation: Accept.
- Suggestions: analyze block-size trade-offs for efficiency and accuracy; clarify n vs p in experiments; discuss perturbation vs statistical guarantees; add comparisons with recent distributed methods.
