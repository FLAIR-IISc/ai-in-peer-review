Summary and relation to prior work
The paper addresses scalable estimation of sparse precision matrices in the high-dimensional, low-sample regime via the CLIME estimator. The core contribution is an inexact ADMM variant tailored to CLIME that (a) operates on column blocks rather than one column at a time, (b) exploits matrix–matrix multiplications for higher arithmetic intensity and cache locality, (c) leverages the low-rank structure of the sample covariance C = AAᵀ when n ≪ p to reduce cost from O(p²k) to O(npk), and (d) is embedded in a shared-memory and distributed-memory framework using block-cyclic data distribution (OpenMP/MPI + ScaLAPACK). The paper establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for objective suboptimality and constraint residuals under a condition η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)², and discusses using sparsity of iterates and a truncation/perturbation of C to enable sparse multiplications while preserving CLIME’s deviation-based statistical guarantees. Experiments compare against CLIME (LP), TIGER, and DC-QUIC, show speedups from column-blocking and parallelization, and demonstrate a run with p = 10⁶ in ~11 hours on 400 cores.

This work builds on: CLIME [Cai et al.], TIGER [Liu & Wang], QUIC/DC-QUIC [Hsieh et al.], distributed and linearized ADMM [Boyd et al., He & Yuan, Yang & Zhang], block-cyclic distribution and high-performance BLAS/ScaLAPACK, and covariance thresholding ideas (e.g., Mazumder & Hastie). The emphasis here is on algorithm–systems co-design for ultra-large p.

Strengths
- Practical scalability: Clear attention to computational bottlenecks; block-column updates, matrix–matrix kernels, and block-cyclic distribution are sensible and effective. The million-dimensional demo is impressive.
- Simple per-iteration steps: Elementwise soft-thresholding/box-projection and matrix multiplications map well to modern architectures.
- Convergence guarantees: O(1/T) rates for objective and residuals for the proposed linearized ADMM variant under standard Lipschitz-like conditions.
- Low-rank exploitation: Avoiding explicit formation of C and using A(AᵀX) is a strong, pragmatic choice in the n ≪ p regime.
- Distributed design details: Process grid, block sizes, and parallel I/O are discussed; empirical scaling plots in both shared and distributed settings are informative.

Weaknesses
Quality/technical soundness
- Augmented Lagrangian/dual scaling: There is an inconsistency between stating Y is a scaled dual variable and writing Lρ with a linear dual term ρ⟨Y, CX − Z⟩. In scaled ADMM the linear term is absent; in unscaled ADMM the Y-update differs. The derivations and Algorithm 1 are closer to scaled ADMM; please reconcile the notation and correct the Lagrangian to avoid confusion.
- Convergence statements: Theorems give O(1/T) bounds but do not explicitly state convergence to a primal–dual optimal solution or ergodic rates for feasibility and optimality gaps in standard form. The norm in Theorem 2, ‖XT − XT−1‖²_{(η/ρ)I − C²}, needs clearer definition and positivity conditions; the requirement η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)² is introduced but its practical selection is not discussed.
- Symmetrization: CLIME solutions require a symmetrization step (e.g., taking the elementwise minimum of absolute values in Ω̂ and Ω̂ᵀ). The paper does not describe how symmetry is enforced across column blocks, which matters for correctness and storage.
- Perturbation/sparsification of C: Theorem 3 is plausible (triangle inequality plus concentration) but underspecified (constants, dependence on sub-exponential parameters, independence assumptions). The suggestion to truncate entries below c√(log p/n) connects to covariance thresholding literature; a more rigorous statement and empirical ablation would help.
- Statistical evaluation: Experiments emphasize runtime and PR curves, but do not report estimation errors (e.g., spectral/Frobenius/M_∞ norms vs Ω₀) or constraint residuals, nor the effect of the proposed C-perturbation on statistical accuracy.

Clarity
- Notation is sometimes inconsistent (elementwise vs operator norms, scaled vs unscaled dual). Algorithm 1 mixes mathematical details with code-like comments; consider separating pseudocode from math and defining all operators precisely.
- Important practical choices (ρ, η, k, block sizes) lack guidance; how to estimate λ_max(C) efficiently (e.g., a few power iterations on A) should be stated.
- The million-dimensional experiment lacks key parameters (n, λ), memory footprint per node, and communication/computation breakdowns.

Originality
- The optimization building blocks (linearized ADMM with O(1/T), matrix–matrix acceleration, block-cyclic distribution) are known; the novelty lies in combining them for CLIME at extreme scales and discussing sparse/low-rank structure exploitation. The perturbation argument for C is incremental relative to known covariance thresholding.

Significance
- If reproducible, the demonstrated scale and near-linear speedups are impactful for large-scale graphical model estimation and constrained optimization more broadly. However, the absence of detailed accuracy measurements and some methodological clarifications weakens the overall impact.

Pros for acceptance
- Addresses an important scalability bottleneck in precision estimation with a practical, well-engineered solution.
- Solid performance results, including p = 10⁶ case; clear system-level insights (column-block size, shared vs distributed).
- The approach is generalizable to other constrained problems with similar structure.

Cons for acceptance
- Limited methodological novelty beyond careful engineering of known components.
- Inconsistencies in derivations and missing details (dual scaling, symmetrization, parameter selection).
- Insufficient statistical evaluation and ablations (accuracy vs speed, effect of C-thresholding).

Suggestions for improvement
- Fix the ADMM derivation and clearly state scaled/unscaled variables and the exact augmented Lagrangian; provide a theorem guaranteeing convergence to optimality (ergodic/non-ergodic) with precise norms.
- Describe and implement the symmetrization step across blocks; report its impact on accuracy and cost.
- Provide guidance on setting ρ, η, and k; include a brief procedure to estimate λ_max(C).
- Add statistical error metrics on synthetic data (operator/L1/Frobenius errors) and convergence plots of residuals; include an ablation on low-rank vs forming C, and on C-thresholding.
- For the large-scale run, report n, λ, memory per node, communication/computation breakdown, and I/O time; consider releasing code or detailed configs.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The paper demonstrates impressive scaling and a useful system, but the methodological novelty is limited and several technical and clarity issues need to be addressed. With the above revisions and stronger statistical evaluation, this work would be competitive. Confidence: medium.
