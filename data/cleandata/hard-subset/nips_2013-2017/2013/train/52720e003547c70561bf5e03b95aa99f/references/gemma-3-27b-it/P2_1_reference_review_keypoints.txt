- Problem: Presents CLIME-ADMM to estimate sparse precision matrices in ultra-high-dimensional settings (p >> n).
- Methodological innovations: Introduces an inexact ADMM variant, column-block updates, and a block-cyclic data distribution for scalability.
- Theoretical guarantees: Provides convergence rates (Theorems 1 & 2) and a perturbation sparsity result (Theorem 3).
- Scalability claim: Demonstrates estimation for a 1M-dimensional precision matrix (1 trillion parameters) in ~11 hours on 400 cores.
- Empirical validation: Experiments show strong performance and scalability, comparing favorably to some existing methods.
- Novelty: Unique combination of inexact ADMM, column-block updates, and data distribution strategy; leverages sparsity/low-rank ideas.
- Writing and presentation: Paper is generally clear, well-written, and well-organized.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on ADMM and CLIME literature and situates itself among scalable sparse/low-rank matrix computation efforts.
- Experimental detail lacking: Insufficient information on parameter tuning and sensitivity analyses (e.g., block size k, varying sparsity levels).
- Limited baselines: Comparisons omit some recent scalable methods and do not fully address why CLIME-ADMM is preferred when competitors (e.g., Tiger) have better recall.
- Implementation complexity: Distributed memory and block-cyclic implementation is complex and may require significant engineering to reproduce.
- Low-rank usage unclear: Discussion of exploiting low-rank structure of the sample covariance is superficial and needs clarification.
- Arguments for acceptance: Significant technical advance, strong theory, important problem, and convincing empirical scalability.
- Arguments against acceptance: Weaknesses in experimental thoroughness, baseline comparisons, and practical adoption complexity.
- Overall verdict: Reviewer recommends a strong accept; rates quality, originality, and significance as excellent and clarity as very good.
