- Proposes a distributed inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME using an equality-constrained reformulation with a box constraint and a linearized X-update admitting soft-thresholding.  
- Solves CLIME in column blocks (k columns) to leverage BLAS-3 matrix–matrix multiplies and better cache/parallel efficiency.  
- Exploits low sample size (C = A A^T low-rank) to compute CX as A(A^T X) reducing matmul cost to O(n p k), and optionally leverages sparsity.  
- Implements a scalable block-cyclic data distribution for shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Provides O(1/T) ergodic convergence rates for objective suboptimality and primal residuals under a stepsize condition η ≥ ρ λ_max^2(C).  
- Empirical results show substantial speedups versus column-wise CLIME solvers, TIGER, and DC-QUIC, including a p=10^6 run on 400 cores (11 hours).  
- Strength: well-motivated shift to column-block updates and reliance on BLAS-3 and block-cyclic distribution for practical speedups.  
- Strength: reasonable linearized/inexact ADMM design with closed-form X and Z updates, reducing core ops to elementwise transforms plus two matmuls per iteration.  
- Strength: convergence analysis is consistent with linearized/inexact ADMM literature.  
- Strength: strong systems perspective covering process grid, load balance, cache efficiency, and parallel I/O.  
- Strength: credible empirical scalability and near-linear distributed speedups, including a very large-scale demonstration.  
- Weakness: paper omits how the final precision estimate is symmetrized and does not discuss positive-definiteness or downstream implications.  
- Weakness: theoretical statements are modest/narrow (unusual bounds in Theorem 1, no explicit ε-iteration complexity, no practical stopping criteria).  
- Weakness: stepsize condition η ≥ ρ λ_max^2(C) is potentially hard to set at scale; practical selection strategies are not described.  
- Weakness: perturbation/thresholding of C offers limited novelty and lacks empirical evaluation of runtime vs. accuracy trade-offs.  
- Weakness: baseline comparisons are not fully fair (DC-QUIC solves GLasso, TIGER differs from CLIME); missing comparisons to other large-scale GLasso/CLIME solvers and multi-core TIGER.  
- Weakness: memory and I/O concerns for ultra-large runs (storing/writing X, Z, Y, V, V̂) are not addressed; sparsity/storage format and pruning mechanisms unclear.  
- Weakness: clarity/organization issues in Algorithm 1 indexing and some theorem notation; some references are incomplete.  
- Questions for authors: how is symmetry enforced in practice; how are ρ and η set without λ_max(C); per-iteration costs, iterations to convergence, and communication breakdowns; empirical results for thresholded C; sparsity and storage strategy for the p=10^6 run.  
- Suggestions: add symmetrization/post-processing details, stopping criteria, and parameter selection guidance.  
- Suggestions: strengthen experiments with accuracy metrics for large p, ablations on k and communication, perturbation/truncation experiments, and stronger baselines.  
- Suggestions: discuss memory footprint and I/O strategies for very large outputs and consider on-the-fly pruning.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important scalability bottleneck with principled, implementable approach and solid HPC systems design.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited statistical analysis, incomplete symmetrization treatment, unfair/insufficient baselines, and standard (O(1/T)) theoretical contributions.  
- Overall: practically motivated and well-engineered with credible systems contributions; borderline accept for systems-oriented work but needs clearer estimator details and stronger large-scale accuracy evaluation.
