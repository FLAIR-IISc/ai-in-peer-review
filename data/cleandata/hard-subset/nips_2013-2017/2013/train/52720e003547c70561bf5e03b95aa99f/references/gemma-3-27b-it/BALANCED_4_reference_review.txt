This paper tackles the challenging problem of large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation, specifically focusing on scaling the CLIME estimator to millions of dimensions using a distributed framework. The authors present CLIME-ADMM, an efficient variant of the ADMM algorithm, and detail a scalable distributed computation strategy leveraging column-block updates and block-cyclic data distribution. The core idea of employing column-block updates coupled with careful consideration of matrix multiplication efficiency is a strong point. The paper provides theoretical convergence analysis and demonstrates substantial performance gains through experiments on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, even achieving estimation of a one-million-dimensional precision matrix in a reasonable timeframe.

**Strengths:**

* **Important Problem:** Estimating high-dimensional precision matrices is crucial in various fields, and scaling existing methods to truly massive datasets is a significant challenge.
* **Novel Approach:** The combination of inexact ADMM with column-block updates, coupled with the block-cyclic distribution strategy, is a novel and well-motivated approach to address scalability.
* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides convergence rate analysis for the proposed algorithm, bolstering confidence in its reliability.
* **Compelling Experimental Results:**  The experimental validation demonstrates substantial speedups compared to existing methods, particularly on large-scale problems.  The ability to estimate a 1 million-dimensional matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores is impressive.
* **Detailed Implementation Considerations:** The paper delves into practical implementation details, such as block size selection and data distribution strategies, which is valuable for reproducibility and adoption.
* **Thorough Related Work:** The authors provide a comprehensive overview of related work in both precision matrix estimation and large-scale machine learning.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Parameter Sensitivity:** While the paper mentions tuning parameter λ, it lacks a thorough discussion of how to select optimal values for ρ and η in the ADMM algorithm. The claim that η ≥ ρλ2max(C) seems conservative, and exploring more adaptive strategies for these parameters could improve performance.
* **Limited Comparison to Strong Baselines:** While comparisons are made to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC, the experimental setup feels slightly unbalanced. Tiger, known for its tuning-free nature and optimal rates, is highlighted as performing well in recall, but the comparison doesn't fully explore parameter tuning of CLIME-ADMM to achieve comparable recall. A more comprehensive parameter sweep for CLIME-ADMM would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** The framework appears complex to implement, requiring expertise in distributed computing and careful consideration of data distribution and synchronization. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the engineering challenges involved and potential avenues for simplification.
* **Assumptions about Low-Rank Structure:** While leveraging the low-rank structure is a valuable optimization, the paper doesn't thoroughly investigate the prevalence of this structure in real-world datasets and the sensitivity of performance to deviations from this assumption. A more rigorous analysis of the conditions under which the low-rank approximation is effective would be beneficial.
* **Lack of Hyperparameter Tuning Details:** The choice of block sizes, especially for the matrix multiplication, is crucial for performance. The paper describes the rationale but lacks a systematic exploration of optimal block sizes for various datasets and hardware configurations.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant and challenging problem in a novel and theoretically sound manner.
* The experimental results demonstrate substantial performance gains, showcasing the practical value of the proposed approach.
* The detailed implementation considerations and thorough related work contribute to the paper’s overall quality and impact.
* The convergence analysis adds to the rigor of the work.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* The lack of detailed parameter tuning guidance and a more comprehensive comparison to strong baselines weakens the claims of superior performance.
* The complexity of the implementation and the sensitivity to assumptions about data structure could limit the practical adoption of the framework.
* The parameter selection and the impact of deviations from the low-rank assumption warrant further investigation.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of large-scale precision matrix estimation. While there are some weaknesses, the novelty, theoretical grounding, and compelling experimental results outweigh these concerns.  I believe the paper is suitable for acceptance, *contingent upon the authors addressing the weaknesses related to parameter tuning and baseline comparisons*. Specifically, the authors should provide guidance on parameter selection, perform a more thorough parameter sweep for CLIME-ADMM in the experiments, and discuss the limitations of the approach when the low-rank assumption doesn't hold.  Providing more detailed implementation advice would also be beneficial.

**Rating:** 7/10 (Accept with minor revisions)
