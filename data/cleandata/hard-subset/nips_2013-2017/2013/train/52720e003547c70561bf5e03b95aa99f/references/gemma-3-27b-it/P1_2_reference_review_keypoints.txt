- Presents CLIME-ADMM, a distributed algorithm for sparse precision matrix estimation scalable to ultra-high dimensions (p >> n).  
- Uses inexact ADMM with column-block decomposition and block-cyclic data distribution for parallelism.  
- Exploits low-rank structure in the sample covariance to accelerate computation.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed algorithm.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical scalability and speedups, including estimating a million-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Positions work relative to CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC and distributed optimization literature, addressing scaling gaps.  
- Novel combination of techniques (inexact ADMM + decomposition + low-rank + block-cyclic) for distributed precision estimation.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with practical implementation considerations (block size, memory hierarchy).  
- Limited empirical comparison to state-of-the-art on solution accuracy (e.g., precision/recall of graph recovery).  
- Insufficient discussion on when the low-rank assumption holds and sensitivity to covariance rank.  
- Lacks detailed analysis and guidance on ADMM hyperparameter tuning (rho, eta) and its impact.  
- Additional details needed on distributed environment specifics (network bandwidth, communication overhead).  
- Some repetitive language; paper could be more concise with editing.  
- Overall: strong accept—significant contribution with solid theory and experiments, minor weaknesses that can be addressed.
