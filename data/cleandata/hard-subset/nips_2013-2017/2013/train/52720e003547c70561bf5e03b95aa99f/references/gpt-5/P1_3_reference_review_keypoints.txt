- Proposes a scalable distributed solver for CLIME using an inexact/linearized ADMM with column-block updates to exploit BLAS-3 and cache locality.  
- Key algorithmic tricks: linearize quadratic term for closed-form soft-thresholding, use splitting variable to turn ∞-norm into box projection, and process k columns at a time.  
- Theoretical result: O(1/T) convergence for objective suboptimality and constraint residuals under the spectral condition η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)^2.  
- Exploits structure: iterate sparsity, safe covariance perturbation/thresholding (Theorem 3), and low-rank C = A A^T to reduce complexity to O(n p k).  
- Provides concrete engineering: shared-memory (OpenMP) and distributed-memory (MPI/ScaLAPACK) implementations with block-cyclic data distribution and demonstrated linear scaling.  
- Empirical claim: scales to p = 10^6 using 400 cores (≈11 hours) and compares against CLIME (flare), TIGER, and DC-QUIC.  
- Builds on prior CLIME, ADMM, and distributed optimization literature; novelty is in the tailored inexact/linearized ADMM with block updates and the practical distributed implementation.  
- Strength: well-motivated column-block design likely yields substantial speedups on distributed systems.  
- Strength: provides clear O(1/T) theoretical guarantees and an explicit spectral condition for convergence.  
- Strength: sensible structural exploitation (low-rank, sparsity, covariance truncation) and a useful perturbation theorem.  
- Strength: solid engineering and load-balance rationale, with a convincing large-scale demonstration.  
- Major weakness: possible scaling/typo in X-update (uses ρ η instead of expected ρ/η), which affects stability, reproducibility, and the step-size condition.  
- Notational inconsistencies: dimensions of A and norm definitions are unclear and used inconsistently.  
- Experimental gaps: missing stopping criteria, parameter values (λ, ρ, η), tolerances, and detailed wall-clock vs accuracy trade-offs.  
- Storage/I/O concern: unclear how output is stored for p=10^6 (dense precision is infeasible); sparsity levels and memory footprint not reported.  
- Theorem 3 caveat: perturbation by sub-exponential noise may not be practically realizable via A A^T and could break PSD; hard-thresholding should be more directly recommended with constants/citations.  
- Missing comparisons/references: more baselines for distributed precision estimation and clearer relation to other ADMM-based CLIME solvers and DC-QUIC block decompositions are needed.  
- Overall quality: algorithmically sound in spirit and standard ADMM rates useful here, but the scaling/tuning discrepancy must be fixed.  
- Clarity: generally organized but hampered by scaling/notation typos; a concise correct pseudo-code and cost table would help.  
- Originality: moderate—main novelty is engineering a scalable CLIME solver; theoretical contributions are incremental.  
- Significance: high practical impact for very large-scale precision estimation and reusable BLAS-3/block-cyclic techniques.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong scalability, hardware-aware algorithmic design, theoretical guarantees, and important practical problem focus.  
- Cons for acceptance: crucial X-update scaling typo, clarity/experimental detail gaps, engineering-centric novelty, and limited accuracy evaluation on real data.  
- Suggested fixes: correct and derive the ADMM update scaling, unify notation, report stopping criteria/parameters/memory/sparsity/I/O, ablate block size and parameter sensitivity, and add accuracy vs runtime metrics.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept (leaning accept) contingent on fixing the ADMM scaling issue and improving experimental reproducibility/details.
