- Paper proposes CLIME-ADMM: a distributed column-block inexact/linearized ADMM for ultra–high-dimensional CLIME using A(A′Ω) to avoid forming C and exploit low-rank structure.
- Key algorithmic ideas: block column updates (BLAS-3 mat-mat), block-cyclic distribution for load balance, and inexact ADMM with O(1/T) convergence claims.
- Empirical claims: strong scalability up to p=10^6 on 400 cores with runtime/report of an 11-hour run, compared to CLIME (flare), TIGER, and DC-QUIC.
- Novelty is mainly systems/engineering: tailoring linearized ADMM to CLIME at column-block granularity and an engineered distributed implementation.
- Strength: Addresses an important, challenging large-scale setting with pragmatic algorithmic/system design and notable speedups.
- Strength: Avoiding explicit C by using A-based multiplications is an elegant, crucial practical insight.
- Strength: Column-block approach and block-cyclic distribution are well-motivated for BLAS-3 efficiency and load balancing.
- Strength: Provides O(1/T) convergence rates under a plausible majorization condition and connects to linearized ADMM literature.
- Strength: Good scalability experiments in shared and distributed memory with informative speedup plots.
- Concern: Theorem statements are unclear—distinction between ergodic (averaged) vs last-iterate guarantees and exact residual/objective gap definitions need clarification.
- Concern: Derivation of the linearized/extrapolated V-update is terse; must precisely state variant and justify majorization condition η ≥ ρ‖C‖^2.
- Concern: Symmetry/symmetrization of CLIME solutions is not described for distributed/blockwise computation; PD discussion absent.
- Concern: Theorem 3’s probabilistic perturbation assumption is inappropriate for deterministic hard-thresholding; needs deterministic re-statement and conditions on Σ0.
- Concern: Several dimensional inconsistencies and typos (e.g., A/U dimensions, process grid q^2×2) and mismatch between text and Algorithm 1 hurt reproducibility.
- Clarity ask: Provide clean, dimension-consistent pseudocode specifying shapes, linearization, over-relaxation, and stopping criteria.
- Scalability ask: Include a communication-complexity analysis per iteration for A′X and AY under block-cyclic distribution (SUMMA-like).
- Originality caveat: Must better position relative to prior distributed/large-scale precision estimation work (QUIC/DC-QUIC, BigQUIC) and clarify differences (CLIME vs log-det).
- Empirical fairness: Runtime comparisons mix objectives; need a strong CLIME baseline (e.g., flare ADMM) on multi-core and per-iteration cost/convergence criteria.
- Empirical metrics: Report matrix-norm errors and residual/training convergence traces in addition to precision/recall; show parameter sensitivity and selection rules.
- Large-scale run detail request: For p=10^6, add iteration counts, residual curves, memory per node, and ablations showing benefits of column-blocking and low-rank multiplications.
- Pros summary: Tackles important problem, solid systems design, avoids forming C, and supplies some convergence guarantees.
- Cons summary: Theory underspecified/possibly incorrect in places, clarity/dimension errors, limited/partially unfair empirical comparisons, missing symmetrization and communication discussion.
- Questions for authors: clarify linearized ADMM variant and extrapolation role; state whether Theorem 2 is last-iterate or ergodic.
- Questions for authors: explain how CLIME solution is symmetrized in distributed setting.
- Questions for authors: specify stopping criteria/tolerances and iterations for the 1M-dim run.
- Questions for authors: provide deterministic/data-dependent thresholding guarantees or conditions on Σ0 for covariance coarsening.
- Recommendation: Borderline—promising and scalable but requires theoretical clarifications, clarity fixes, and stronger empirical evaluation to be a strong contribution.
