- Paper proposes scalable inexact ADMM and distributed framework to solve ultra–high-dimensional CLIME sparse precision estimation.
- Reformulates CLIME with splitting variable Z so X-update is soft-thresholding and Z-update is a box projection, requiring only two matrix multiplies per iteration.
- Column-block strategy (k columns at a time) enables BLAS-3 efficient matrix–matrix multiplies and parallelization via block-cyclic distribution.
- Leverages low-rank structure C = A A' (n << p) to reduce multiplies from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k).
- Exploits sparsity in iterates and proposes optional sparsification of C with a theorem claiming sup-norm deviation preservation under sub-exponential perturbations.
- Provides O(1/T) convergence rate for objective suboptimality and primal residuals under η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)^2.
- Implements shared- and distributed-memory frameworks (OpenMP/OpenMPI/ScaLAPACK) and demonstrates near-linear speedups, including p=10^6 in ~11 hours on 400 cores.
- Builds on CLIME and prior ADMM/graphical lasso work, adding an inexact ADMM tailored to CLIME and an HPC-oriented block-column distributed implementation.
- Theoretical convergence rates align with standard (linearized) ADMM results; covariance perturbation connects to screening/thresholding literature.
- Strength: maps CLIME to BLAS-3 and distributed operations with a practically effective column-block approach.
- Strength: solid convergence analysis with explicit conditions on η and ρ.
- Strength: careful systems engineering (block-cyclic layout, process grids, parallel I/O, cache-aware block sizes).
- Strength: strong empirical scalability in both shared and distributed experiments.
- Strength: pragmatic use of low-rank structure avoids storing C explicitly.
- Weakness: limited statistical accuracy evaluation—real-data results report runtime only and synthetic metrics are incomplete.
- Weakness: baseline fairness concerns (DC-QUIC and TIGER comparisons may be inappropriate; missing CLIME baseline using same low-rank multiplies or strong graphical lasso).
- Weakness: Theorem 3 on covariance perturbation lacks explicit constants and guidance on adjusting λ for added noise.
- Weakness: truncation/sparsification strategy relies on unknown deviation constant c, leaving threshold selection unclear.
- Weakness: unclear whether perturbation/sparsification preserves PSD, which may matter for reuse.
- Weakness: symmetrization of CLIME solutions is not described nor costed in the distributed setting.
- Weakness: missing experimental details on hyperparameters (ρ, η), λ_max(C) estimation, stopping criteria, and iteration counts.
- Weakness: sensitivity to n not evaluated; performance unclear when n is not much smaller than p.
- Weakness: presentation issues—incomplete references, notation mistakes, and typos impede verification.
- Quality: algorithm and convergence analysis are plausible; perturbation theorem is plausible but underspecified.
- Empirical evaluation: excellent for scalability, weaker for statistical accuracy and fair comparisons.
- Clarity: high-level ideas are understandable but manuscript needs clearer notation, complete references, and explicit practical details.
- Originality: novel combination of CLIME with linearized ADMM, block-column updates, and distributed HPC implementation; theoretical additions are modest.
- Significance: practically significant ability to scale CLIME to millions of variables with near-linear speedups; useful engineering template.
- Suggestions: report accuracy-runtime tradeoffs (Frobenius/spectral, AUC/PR), detail symmetrization and its cost, provide hyperparameter/stopping rules, tighten Theorem 3 (constants, λ adjustments, thresholding rule), and evaluate larger n and communication/memory costs.
- Pros for acceptance: tackles important scalability problem with principled method and strong systems results.
- Cons against acceptance: limited accuracy evaluation, underspecified theoretical claims, and missing reproducibility details.
- Overall: compelling systems/methods paper that leans toward acceptance pending stronger accuracy analyses and clarified theoretical/practical details.
