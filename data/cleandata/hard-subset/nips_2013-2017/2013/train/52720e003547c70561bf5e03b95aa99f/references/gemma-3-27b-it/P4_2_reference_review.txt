This paper introduces a distributed algorithm, CLIME-ADMM, for estimating sparse precision matrices in high-dimensional settings where the sample size is smaller than the dimension. The core idea is to leverage the column-wise separability of the CLIME estimator and parallelize its computation using an inexact ADMM variant coupled with block matrix operations for efficiency. The authors address the challenges of scaling to ultra-large problems, where the entire sample covariance matrix cannot fit into memory, by distributing computations across multiple cores or machines.

The paper is well-motivated, building upon existing work in precision matrix estimation and distributed optimization. The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing methods when dealing with extremely high-dimensional data. The proposed CLIME-ADMM variant builds on established techniques (ADMM, block matrix multiplication) and combines them effectively to address the scalability issues. The theoretical analysis provides convergence guarantees for the algorithm, and the experimental results demonstrate significant performance improvements over existing methods, particularly for large-scale problems. The connection to recent advancements in scaling machine learning algorithms is also appropriately highlighted.

**Strengths:**

*   **Scalability:** The proposed algorithm demonstrates strong scalability on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, achieving linear speedup with an increasing number of cores. The experimental results, particularly the estimation of a 1 million-dimensional precision matrix in 11 hours using 400 cores, are impressive.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The paper provides convergence analysis for the CLIME-ADMM algorithm, establishing rates of convergence for both the objective function and constraint violations.
*   **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly defines the problem of large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation and justifies the need for a distributed solution.
*   **Well-Structured Presentation:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The technical details are explained thoroughly, and the notation is consistent.
*   **Practical Innovations:** The column-block ADMM approach and block cyclic data distribution appear to be valuable practical innovations that significantly improve performance.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Implementation Details:** While the paper describes the overall framework, certain implementation details, like the choice of block size (pb x nb x kb) and data distribution scheme, seem somewhat ad-hoc. A more systematic exploration of the impact of these parameters on performance would be beneficial.
*   **Comparison Limited:** While results demonstrate CLIME-ADMM’s superiority, the comparison is somewhat limited. More comparisons with a wider range of state-of-the-art distributed optimization algorithms would strengthen the findings.
*   **Low-Rank Structure Leverage:** The discussion of leveraging low-rank structure feels somewhat brief. A more detailed explanation and experimental results demonstrating the benefits of this optimization could be included.
*   **Hyperparameter Tuning:** The influence of the tuning parameter λ and the ADMM penalty parameter ρ on the algorithm’s performance is not thoroughly discussed.
*   **Reproducibility:** While the paper provides references to external software packages, a complete and readily reproducible implementation would significantly enhance the value of the work.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important and challenging problem in high-dimensional statistics.
*   The proposed algorithm demonstrates significant scalability and performance improvements over existing methods.
*   The theoretical analysis provides strong guarantees for the algorithm’s convergence.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Some implementation details could be more thoroughly explored.
*   The comparison to other distributed optimization algorithms is somewhat limited.
*   The discussion on leveraging low-rank structure could be more detailed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation. The proposed CLIME-ADMM algorithm is well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically validated. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend acceptance to NIPS.
