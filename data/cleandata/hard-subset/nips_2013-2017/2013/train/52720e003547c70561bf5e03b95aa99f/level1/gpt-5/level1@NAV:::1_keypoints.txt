- Proposes an inexact ADMM solver for CLIME that operates on column blocks and maps naturally to shared- and distributed-memory systems with block-cyclic data distribution.  
- Linearizes the CX coupling to get a closed-form soft-thresholding X-update and uses a box projection for Z.  
- Proves O(1/T) rates for objective suboptimality and feasibility residuals under η ≥ ρλmax(C)^2, aligning with non-ergodic ADMM theory while tailoring proofs to CLIME.  
- Exploits low-rank factorization C = AAT to reduce matrix multiplies to O(n p k), leverages iterate sparsity, and uses block-cyclic distribution to balance load and cache use.  
- Empirical strong-scaling results are convincing, including p = 10^6 (~10^12 parameters) on 400 cores in ~11 hours.  
- Comparisons against CLIME (flare), TIGER, and DC-QUIC show markedly better scaling; statistical accuracy comparisons are limited.  
- The algorithmic derivations are sound and consistent with inexact ADMM literature; block updates and complexity accounting are clear.  
- Theorem 3 on covariance perturbations is interesting but not validated experimentally and raises PSD and factorization-maintenance questions.  
- Clarity is generally good but the paper contains typos/incomplete references and missing implementation details (stopping criteria, parameter selection ρ/η/λ).  
- Communication costs and memory footprint at extreme scale are not analyzed or reported.  
- Role and practical implications of the covariance perturbation are underexplained.  
- Originality: integration of CLIME, ADMM, and block-cyclic distribution into a column-block ADMM with closed-form inexact updates is a useful nontrivial systems contribution.  
- Significance: high practical impact for large-scale Gaussian graphical model estimation and potential broader applicability to constrained convex programs.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong systems contribution with impressive scaling, solid tailored convergence analysis, and careful engineering (column blocks, low-rank multiplies, block-cyclic layout).  
- Cons for acceptance: limited large-scale accuracy/estimation-quality evaluation (TIGER may be better statistically), perturbation theorem unvalidated and PSD/factorization issues unaddressed, and incomplete references/implementation details.  
- Suggested additions: add communication/runtime breakdowns and memory-use reports.  
- Suggested experiments: provide ablations for block size k and sensitivity to η/ρ, and evaluate estimation error on larger real and synthetic datasets.  
- Suggested clarifications: explain applicability/limitations of covariance perturbation, address PSD and factorization maintenance, and fix/complete references and stopping-criteria/parameter-selection details.
