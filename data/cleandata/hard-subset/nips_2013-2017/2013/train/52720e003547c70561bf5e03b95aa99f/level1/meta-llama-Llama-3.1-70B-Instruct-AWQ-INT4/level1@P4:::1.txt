**Summary**

The paper proposes a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator. The authors present an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME, which scales to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores. The framework solves CLIME in column-blocks, leveraging the separable structure of the problem, and uses block cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing. Experimental results show that the algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods and scales almost linearly with the number of cores.

**Strengths**

1. **Scalability**: The proposed framework is designed to handle ultra-large-scale problems, making it a significant contribution to the field of precision matrix estimation.
2. **Efficient algorithm**: The inexact ADMM algorithm is shown to have a O(1/T) convergence rate for both the objective function and the residuals of optimality conditions, making it a efficient solution for large-scale problems.
3. **Load balancing**: The use of block cyclic distribution achieves load balancing, which is essential for large-scale parallel computations.
4. **Experimental evaluation**: The authors provide a comprehensive experimental evaluation of the algorithm, including comparisons with existing methods and scalability studies.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited theoretical analysis**: While the authors provide some theoretical analysis, including convergence rates, they do not provide a comprehensive theoretical analysis of the algorithm's behavior.
2. **Assumptions on data**: The authors assume that the data is sub-Gaussian, which may not always be the case in practice.
3. **Sensitivity to hyperparameters**: The algorithm has several hyperparameters, including the tuning parameter λ, the penalty parameter ρ, and the block size k, which may require careful tuning in practice.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel framework for large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of inexact ADMM and block cyclic distribution are new ideas that have not been previously explored in this context.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for large-scale precision matrix estimation, which is a crucial problem in many fields, including genomics, finance, and social network analysis. The proposed framework has the potential to enable the estimation of large-scale precision matrices, which is currently a challenging task.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed framework, including the algorithm and the theoretical analysis. The experimental evaluation is also clearly presented, making it easy to follow.
