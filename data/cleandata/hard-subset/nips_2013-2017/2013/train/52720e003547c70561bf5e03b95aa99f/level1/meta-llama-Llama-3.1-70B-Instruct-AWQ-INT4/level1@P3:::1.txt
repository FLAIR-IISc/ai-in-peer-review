This paper proposes a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator. The authors present an inexact alternating direction method of multiplier (ADMM) algorithm for CLIME and establish rates of convergence for both the objective and optimality conditions. The proposed framework solves CLIME in column-blocks and uses block cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, making it easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation and the CLIME estimator. They also provide a thorough review of related work, including existing methods for solving CLIME and other approaches to sparse precision matrix estimation.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant. The authors propose a novel inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME, which they show has a O(1/T) convergence rate for both the objective function and the residuals of optimality conditions. They also develop a large-scale distributed framework for the computations, which scales to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores.

The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating the scalability and efficiency of the proposed algorithm. The authors show that their algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods and scales almost linearly with the number of cores.

The paper has several strengths:

* The proposed algorithm is novel and has a strong theoretical foundation.
* The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the scalability and efficiency of the algorithm.
* The paper is well-written and well-organized, making it easy to follow.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a high level of familiarity with the CLIME estimator and sparse precision matrix estimation. Readers without a strong background in these areas may find the paper difficult to follow.
