- Addresses large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME with an inexact ADMM algorithm and a distributed framework.  
- Solves CLIME in column blocks using elementwise operations and parallel matrix multiplications to handle millions of dimensions.  
- Provides convergence rate analysis for the proposed inexact ADMM variant.  
- Empirically validates the method on shared-memory and distributed-memory systems, showing near-linear scalability with cores.  
- Well-motivated choice of CLIME due to theoretical properties and computational advantages.  
- Column-block approach and inexact ADMM are reasonable and effective strategies for scaling computation.  
- Detailed discussion of load balancing and a block cyclic data distribution in the distributed framework.  
- Originality stems from combining inexact ADMM tailored to CLIME with distributed column-block solving and careful data distribution choices.  
- Significant impact potential for genomics, finance, image processing, and other high-dimensional applications.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees combined with comprehensive scalability experiments.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of practical parameter tuning (η, λ, ρ) and sensitivity.  
- Weakness: limited and incomplete comparison to existing methods across varying conditions (e.g., DC-QUIC and sparsity effects).  
- Weakness: experimental figures are sometimes hard to interpret—labeling/scaling could be improved.  
- Weakness: claimed exploitation of sample covariance sparsity (Theorem 3) is not fully integrated into experiments to show practical gains.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses and suitable for acceptance with suggested improvements.
