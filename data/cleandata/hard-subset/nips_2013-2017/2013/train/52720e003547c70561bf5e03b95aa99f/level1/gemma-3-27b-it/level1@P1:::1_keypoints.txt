Paper addresses large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation (CLIME) with an inexact ADMM and distributed framework.  
Key innovation: solve CLIME in column-blocks and leverage parallel matrix multiplications with block-cyclic distribution.  
Demonstrated ability to handle millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores.  
Empirical results show near-linear scalability with increasing core count on shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
Provides theoretical convergence rates for objective function and optimality conditions.  
Novelty lies in block-wise ADMM, load-balancing, and cache-aware matrix multiplication optimizations.  
Builds on prior CLIME and ADMM literature and situates work within large-scale ML/distributed optimization trends.  
Extensive experimentation strengthens the credibility of claimed performance gains.  
Comparisons to baseline methods are limited; missing evaluation against newer distributed frameworks and specialized sparse libraries.  
Implementation appears complex and the engineering effort required for deployment is not fully discussed.  
Sensitivity to tuning parameters (λ, ρ, η) is insufficiently explored.  
Justification and analysis of benefits from low-rank approximations is underdeveloped.  
Guidance on selecting block size k is vague; lacks concrete heuristics.  
Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
Requested revisions: expand comparative baselines, provide parameter and block-size guidance, and discuss practical implementation considerations.
