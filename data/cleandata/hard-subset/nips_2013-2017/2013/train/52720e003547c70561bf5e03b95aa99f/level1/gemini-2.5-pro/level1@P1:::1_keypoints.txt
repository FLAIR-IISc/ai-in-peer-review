- Proposes a highly scalable, distributed algorithm for CLIME to estimate sparse precision matrices in p >> n settings, demonstrated up to a million dimensions.
- Introduces an inexact ADMM algorithm that processes blocks of columns simultaneously (column-block strategy) to convert computations to efficient matrix-matrix multiplications.
- Provides convergence analysis for the inexact ADMM method, establishing an O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Implements the algorithm for shared- and distributed-memory systems, leveraging low-rank sample covariance structure (C = AA^T) to reduce complexity from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k) per block.
- Uses block cyclic data distribution in the distributed implementation for load balancing and efficient memory usage.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, showing strong scalability and outperforming state-of-the-art methods (e.g., DC-QUIC, flare), including a landmark million-dimensional run.
- Theoretical contributions include Theorem 3, which justifies sparsifying the sample covariance matrix, though it was not used in main experiments.
- Paper is well-written, with clear motivation for design choices and helpful diagrams and result presentations.
- Originality lies in the synthesis: applying column-block ADMM and a tailored distributed framework for CLIME is novel and effective.
- Significance is high: enables estimation of graphical models at previously infeasible scales, opening applications in genomics, neuroscience, and social networks.
- Limitation: theoretical convergence leverages existing inexact ADMM techniques rather than providing a fundamental new optimization theory.
- Practical limitation: lacks discussion and practical guidance on tuning ADMM hyperparameters (ρ, η), and computing λ_max(C) is infeasible at extreme scales.
- Experimental gap: sparsity-exploiting techniques discussed were not validated empirically; a demonstration would strengthen the connection between theory and practice.
- Baseline comparison note: Tiger and other column-decomposable methods could be parallelized; a direct parallelized comparison is missing.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — the strengths and impact substantially outweigh the drawbacks.
