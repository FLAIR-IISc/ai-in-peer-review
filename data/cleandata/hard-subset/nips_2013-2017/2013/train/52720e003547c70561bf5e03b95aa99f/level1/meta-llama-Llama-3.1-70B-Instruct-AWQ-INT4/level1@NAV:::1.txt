**Summary of the Paper**

The paper presents a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator. The authors propose an inexact alternating direction method of multiplier (ADMM) algorithm for CLIME and establish rates of convergence for both the objective and optimality conditions. They also develop a scalable distributed framework for the computations, which can scale to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores. The proposed framework solves CLIME in column-blocks and uses block cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing.

**Strengths**

1. **Scalability**: The proposed framework can scale to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores, making it suitable for large-scale applications.
2. **Efficiency**: The authors demonstrate that their algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods and scales almost linearly with the number of cores.
3. **Theoretical guarantees**: The authors establish rates of convergence for both the objective and optimality conditions, providing a solid theoretical foundation for their algorithm.
4. **Flexibility**: The proposed framework can be applied to various large-scale constrained optimization problems, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The proposed algorithm and framework may be complex to implement, especially for those without experience in distributed computing and ADMM algorithms.
2. **Assumptions**: The authors assume that the true precision matrix is sparse, which may not always be the case in practice.
3. **Comparison to other methods**: While the authors compare their algorithm to existing methods, the comparison is limited to a few methods, and a more comprehensive comparison would be beneficial.

**Originality**

1. **Novel algorithm**: The authors propose a novel inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Scalable framework**: The proposed distributed framework is a new contribution, and the authors demonstrate its scalability and efficiency.

**Significance**
