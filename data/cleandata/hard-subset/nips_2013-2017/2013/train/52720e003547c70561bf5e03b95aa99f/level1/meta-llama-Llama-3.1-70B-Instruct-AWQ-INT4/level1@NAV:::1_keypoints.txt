- Summary: Proposes a distributed, block-wise CLIME solver using an inexact ADMM with convergence rates, designed to scale to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters via block cyclic distribution and hundreds of cores.

- Strength — Scalability: Framework scales to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores.

- Strength — Efficiency: Algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art baselines and scales almost linearly with cores.

- Strength — Theoretical guarantees: Provides convergence rates for the objective and optimality conditions.

- Strength — Flexibility: Framework can be applied to other large-scale constrained optimization problems.

- Weakness — Complexity: Implementation may be complex for users without distributed computing or ADMM experience.

- Weakness — Assumptions: Relies on sparsity of the true precision matrix, which may not hold in all applications.

- Weakness — Limited comparisons: Empirical comparisons are restricted to a few methods; broader benchmarking would help.

- Originality — Novel algorithm: Introduces a new inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME.

- Originality — Scalable framework: Presents a new distributed, block-wise framework demonstrating large-scale scalability.

- Significance — Impact: Potentially significant for large-scale machine learning and optimization.

- Significance — Applications: Demonstrates practical uses in precision matrix estimation and graphical model inference.

- Quality — Clarity: Paper is well-written and clear for the target audience.

- Quality — Organization: Logical structure and good flow of ideas.

- Quality — Technical soundness: Solid theoretical foundation and well-explained technical details.

- Recommendation: Recommend accept, with request to address implementation complexity, assumption limitations, and expand empirical comparisons.
