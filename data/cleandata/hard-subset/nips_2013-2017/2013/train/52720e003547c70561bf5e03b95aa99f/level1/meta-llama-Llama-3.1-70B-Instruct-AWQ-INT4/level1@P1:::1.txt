This paper presents a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator. The authors propose an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) algorithm for CLIME and establish rates of convergence for both the objective and optimality conditions. The proposed framework solves CLIME in column-blocks and uses block cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing. Experimental results show that the algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods and scales almost linearly with the number of cores.
