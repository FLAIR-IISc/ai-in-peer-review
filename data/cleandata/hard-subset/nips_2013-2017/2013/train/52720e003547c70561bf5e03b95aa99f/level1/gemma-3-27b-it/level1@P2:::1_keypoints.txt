- Addresses large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME with an inexact ADMM and distributed framework for datasets with millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.  
- Core idea: solve in column blocks and exploit matrix multiplications for scalable computation.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents problem, method, and experiments.  
- Related work is cited and connected to precision matrix estimation, distributed optimization, and sparse computations.  
- Notation is mostly clear but could better define matrix dimensions when first introduced.  
- Strength: demonstrated scalability, e.g., estimating a 1M-dimension precision matrix on 400 cores in 11 hours.  
- Strength: novel combination of inexact ADMM, column-block strategy, and block-cyclic distributed design.  
- Strength: theoretical convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions are provided.  
- Strength: practical considerations discussed, including leveraging sparse/low-rank structure and justification for block-cyclic distribution.  
- Weakness: limited experimental comparison scope, relying mainly on synthetic data and a few real datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning and fairness of method comparisons.  
- Weakness: block size (k) selection analysis is ad-hoc; lacks systematic guidance tied to problem characteristics.  
- Weakness: complexity and engineering effort for implementing the distributed framework are not fully addressed.  
- Recommendation: overall positive — novel, scalable, and technically sound work recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and detail parameter/block-size guidance.
