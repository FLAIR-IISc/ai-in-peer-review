- Proposes a large-scale distributed framework using inexact ADMM for CLIME to estimate sparse precision matrices at millions of dimensions.
- Solves CLIME in column-blocks with block cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing across hundreds of cores.
- Demonstrates near-linear scalability and substantially better performance than state-of-the-art methods in experiments.
- Algorithm has O(1/T) convergence rate for objective and optimality residuals, indicating efficiency for large-scale problems.
- Block cyclic distribution effectively balances load for large-scale parallel computation.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation with comparisons and scalability studies supports empirical claims.
- Theoretical analysis is limited and does not fully characterize the algorithm's behavior.
- Assumes sub-Gaussian data, which may not hold in many practical settings.
- Method is sensitive to multiple hyperparameters (λ, ρ, block size k) that may require careful tuning.
- Presents a novel combination of inexact ADMM and block cyclic distribution for large-scale precision matrix estimation.
- Significant potential impact for applications in genomics, finance, and social network analysis by enabling much larger estimations.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the framework, algorithm, theory, and experiments.
- Pro-accept: novel, scalable, efficient method with strong empirical validation.
- Con-accept: limited theory, restrictive data assumptions, and hyperparameter tuning challenges.
