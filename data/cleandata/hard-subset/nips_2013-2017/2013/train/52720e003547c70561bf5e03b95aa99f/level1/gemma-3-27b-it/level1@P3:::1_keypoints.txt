- Tackles large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME with an inexact ADMM and a distributed framework for millions of dimensions.  
- Core idea: solve in column blocks and leverage matrix-matrix multiplications to scale to ultra-high dimensionality.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents problem, method, and experiments.  
- Adequate connection to related work, citing key papers in sparse precision estimation and distributed optimization.  
- Provides theoretical analysis with convergence rates for the ADMM algorithm.  
- Experimental validation on shared- and distributed-memory systems convincingly demonstrates scalability.  
- Thorough empirical comparison to CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC, showing advantages on very large instances.  
- Discussion of exploiting sparse and low-rank structure points to useful future improvements but is not fully implemented.  
- Theoretical analysis feels cursory; convergence condition (η ≥ ρλ2max(C)) lacks detailed justification or practicality discussion.  
- Insufficient analysis of tuning parameters (λ, ρ, η) and practical guidance for their selection.  
- Claim that low-rank covariance accelerates computation needs concrete empirical quantification and comparisons.  
- Block size choice (k) is empirical; lacks systematic study linking k to problem size, memory, and cores.  
- Code availability is not mentioned; releasing code is important for reproducibility given framework complexity.  
- Paper doesn’t explore hybrid approaches combining ADMM with other methods for small subproblems.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant scalability (million-dimensional problem solved), novel column-block + inexact ADMM approach, theoretical grounding, and strong practical relevance.  
- Rejection/concern points: limited theoretical depth, inadequate empirical validation of enhancements (low-rank, block size), and lack of publicly available code.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen theoretical analysis, empirically validate low-rank/block-size choices, and provide code.
