- Presents a large-scale distributed framework for estimating sparse precision matrices via the CLIME estimator.
- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm that scales to millions of dimensions and hundreds of cores.
- Solves CLIME in column-blocks with block-cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing.
- Establishes convergence rates for both the objective and optimality conditions.
- Positions the work relative to prior sparse precision estimation methods and Big Data scaling efforts.
- Strength: scalable, efficient algorithm capable of large-scale problems.
- Strength: provides theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.
- Weakness: assumes precision matrix sparsity, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: block-cyclic distribution may not be optimal across all problem sizes and architectures.
- Weakness: lacks comparisons with state-of-the-art alternatives (e.g., graphical lasso).
- Argument for acceptance: significant, well-motivated, theoretically grounded contribution with demonstrated scalability.
- Argument against acceptance: practical assumptions and limited empirical comparisons leave relative performance unclear.
- Overall: strong, impactful paper but needs further evaluation and comparison to fully assess effectiveness.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
