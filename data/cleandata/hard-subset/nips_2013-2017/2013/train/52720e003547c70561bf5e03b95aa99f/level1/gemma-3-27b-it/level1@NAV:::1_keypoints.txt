- Tackles scalable estimation of sparse precision matrices for datasets with millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.  
- Proposes a distributed implementation of CLIME using an inexact ADMM algorithm.  
- Introduces practical optimizations: column-block processing and block-cyclic data distribution for shared and distributed-memory systems.  
- Builds on prior work in sparse precision estimation and distributed optimization, with CLIME chosen for its parallel-friendly structure.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical scalability, showing near-linear speedup and performance on million-dimensional data.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for the proposed ADMM algorithm.  
- Implementation and algorithmic details are clearly and effectively presented.  
- Experimental validation is compelling and supports the method’s effectiveness at large scale.  
- Comparisons to existing methods are limited and could be made more comprehensive.  
- Lacks thorough sensitivity analysis for key parameters (block size, regularization, etc.).  
- Notation section is overly detailed and could be streamlined.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, scales to previously infeasible sizes, backed by theory and experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete method comparisons and insufficient parameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand comparisons and add parameter sensitivity analysis.
