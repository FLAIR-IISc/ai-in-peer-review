- Presents a distributed framework for CLIME-based sparse precision matrix estimation targeting ultra-high dimensions (millions).
- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm that solves CLIME in column-blocks instead of column-by-column.
- Employs a block-cyclic data distribution for load balancing and exploits sparse/low-rank structure for efficiency.
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for both objective value and optimality conditions.
- Shows near-linear scalability on shared- and distributed-memory systems; solves a 1M-dimension problem in 11 hours on 400 cores.
- Builds on prior distributed/ADMM work (e.g., Hsieh et al., Boyd et al.) but advances scalability substantially.
- Column-block strategy is a clear innovation over previous per-column CLIME solvers.
- Strengths: strong theoretical guarantees, excellent empirical scalability versus Tiger and DC-QUIC, and a well-structured algorithm/presentation.
- Weaknesses: limited comparison to very recent distributed methods (e.g., Hogwild! variants) and insufficient contextualization of the 11-hour/400-core result’s practical significance.
- Theoretical analysis is sound but somewhat brief, with detailed proofs relegated to the appendix.
- Cons: limited discussion of real-world deployment challenges and theoretical novelty viewed as incremental rather than transformative.
- Overall: significant, well-presented contribution to scalable precision matrix estimation; recommended for acceptance.
