- Proposes a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator.  
- Introduces an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME with proven convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions.  
- Develops a scalable distributed implementation that handles millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters across hundreds of cores.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains the approach clearly and concisely.  
- ADMM is an appropriate choice, enabling decomposition into elementary matrix multiplications and elementwise operations.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis of convergence rates, which is an important contribution.  
- Empirical results show substantially better scalability than state-of-the-art methods and near-linear scaling with cores.  
- Strength: ability to scale to extremely large problem sizes.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough empirical comparison and detailed analysis versus existing methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of practical applications and real-world use cases.  
- Weakness: limited novelty — not a significant new theoretical insight beyond applying ADMM to CLIME.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, citing significant scalability and solid analysis despite some limitations.
