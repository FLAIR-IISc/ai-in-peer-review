1. Paper proposes a distributed inexact ADMM algorithm for sparse precision estimation using CLIME, solving column-blocks instead of columns.  
2. Introduces a block cyclic distribution strategy to balance load across parallel architectures and improve cache/memory efficiency.  
3. Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions with proofs in the supplement.  
4. Demonstrates scalability to 1 million dimensions (1 trillion parameters) in ~11 hours on 400 cores, outperforming CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC.  
5. Shows near-linear speedup with increasing cores and efficiency gains from block matrix operations over repeated matrix-vector products.  
6. Builds on prior CLIME, ADMM, and distributed optimization literature, extending methods to ultra-high dimensions.  
7. Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis (Theorems 1–2) aligned with existing ADMM results.  
8. Strength: compelling experimental validation showing substantial practical scalability.  
9. Strength: practical, implementable framework addressing load balancing and memory hierarchy concerns.  
10. Strength: clear exposition, good notation, algorithms, and well-presented experiments.  
11. Weakness: column block size k is treated empirically without principled selection guidance for varied problems/hardware.  
12. Weakness: convergence analysis assumes η ≥ ρλ_max(C), requiring knowledge of λ_max(C) which may be hard to obtain at scale.  
13. Weakness: comparisons to Tiger are limited/unequal due to different methodological assumptions (tuning-free vs. λ-tuned CLIME).  
14. Weakness: insufficient exploration of performance across datasets with varying sparsity patterns or noise levels.  
15. Pro: tackles a major practical challenge, offers substantial scalability improvements, and provides rigorous guarantees.  
16. Con: novelty is partly incremental (scaling/extending ADMM), and some parameter/theoretical details need deeper treatment.  
17. Overall: strong, well-positioned contribution with excellent technical and empirical results despite minor practical/theoretical gaps.  
18. Meets review criteria: high quality, clear, reasonably original (column-block distributed CLIME), and highly significant for large-scale computation.
