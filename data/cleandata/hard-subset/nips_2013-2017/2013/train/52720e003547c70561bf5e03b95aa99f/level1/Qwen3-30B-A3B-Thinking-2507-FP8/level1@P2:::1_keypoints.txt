- Introduces a distributed CLIME-ADMM framework that scales sparse precision matrix estimation to millions of dimensions.  
- Builds on CLIME estimator to handle ultra-high dimensional settings (p up to 10^6, p^2 parameters).  
- Novel column-block approach enabling matrix-matrix multiplications instead of repeated matrix-vector operations.  
- Uses block cyclic distribution for load balancing on distributed memory systems.  
- Leverages sparse and low-rank structures to further accelerate computations.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions (Theorems 1-2).  
- Demonstrates empirical scalability to p=10^6 (10^12 parameters) on 400 cores, comparing against CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with clear pseudocode (Algorithm 1) and illustrative figures.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing regarding scalability and performance.  
- Lacks detail on practical implementation choices (e.g., how block sizes are chosen).  
- Theoretical proofs (e.g., Theorem 2) are relegated to the appendix and would benefit from more accessible presentation and intuition.  
- Sparse-structure perturbation results are theoretical but lack empirical validation showing practical speedups.  
- Does not sufficiently contrast with prior distributed ADMM work; related-work discussion could be more comprehensive.  
- Could strengthen impact by citing concrete real-world applications/datasets that require p > 10^5 dimensions.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel, and practically significant contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor improvements.
