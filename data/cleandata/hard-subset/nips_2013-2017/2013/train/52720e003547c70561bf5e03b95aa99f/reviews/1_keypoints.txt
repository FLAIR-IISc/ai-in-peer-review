- Paper proposes a scalable CLIME variant (CLIME-ADMM) and a distributed/parallel framework for sparse precision matrix estimation, with experiments vs DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; recommend a notation table because symbols like ρ and η are undefined.  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is interesting but unclear why it guarantees load balance or scalability; no empirical evidence provided.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; need intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.  
- Algorithm may be better characterized as a parallel implementation rather than a distributed algorithm, since message passing/communication mechanisms are not described.  
- Minor writing/typo issues: "tunning" → "tuning", "parameter tunning free" → "tuning", "summerized" → "summarized".  
- Novelty highlighted: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks (instead of column-by-column) and empirical analysis using OpenMPI and Scalapak.
