- Problem: tackles covariance selection at massive scale (millions of variables, trillions of parameters).  
- Estimator: focuses on CLIME, which is naturally column-wise solvable; authors propose a block-wise alternative.  
- Algorithmic contribution: new block-wise solver coupled with an inexact direction method of multipliers.  
- Theoretical results: convergence rates established for objective value and distance to optimality.  
- Structure exploitation: leverages sparsity and low-rank structure to reduce computation.  
- Implementation: scalable parallel implementation for both shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Experiments: runtime comparisons on synthetic and real data demonstrate very strong performance.  
- Scalability analysis: detailed study of speedups across block sizes and number of cores.  
- Presentation: well written, clear statements of novelty, solid literature connections and balanced technical/theoretical exposition.  
- Main concern/recommendation: paper’s value depends on releasing source code; publication should be contingent on making the tool available to the community.
