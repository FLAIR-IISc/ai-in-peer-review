- Proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence and implementations for shared-memory and distributed-memory systems, compared against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Derivation of inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard (reference provided).
- CLIME’s structure allows componentwise subproblem solves; the main cost is matrix-matrix multiplication, making ADMM highly parallelizable.
- Matrix-matrix multiplications are well-known to parallelize effectively.
- Numerical experiment setup needs more detail, e.g., reported accuracy achieved by each solver.
- Paper is well-organized and clear.
- Evaluation of ADMM in shared- and distributed-memory environments is interesting and original.
- Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation is important; a simple, robust distributed algorithm is useful, but the scalability gains largely stem from CLIME’s simple structure.
