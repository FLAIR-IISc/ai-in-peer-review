- Paper proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence and implementations for shared- and distributed-memory.
- Numerical comparisons include DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Technically sound.
- Derivation of inexact ADMM follows standard procedures and offers no new methodological insights.
- CLIME’s structure permits component-wise subproblem computation; matrix-matrix multiplication is the main cost but is efficiently parallelizable.
- Recommend reporting the solution accuracy achieved by each solver to improve numerical comparisons.
- Well-organized and easy to follow.
- Evaluating ADMM across shared- and distributed-memory architectures is an interesting and useful contribution.
- A simple, robust distributed algorithm is significant for large-scale sparse precision estimation, but scalability benefits largely stem from CLIME’s simple structure rather than novel algorithmic advances.
