This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program in the CLIME estimator, establishing a convergence rate of O(1/T). The authors implement the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, providing numerical comparisons with other methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

Quality: The paper is technically sound.

Minor comments:
1. The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard and can be found in existing literature, such as http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm.
2. The CLIME estimator's structure allows for component-wise computation of each subproblem, with matrix-matrix multiplication being the most expensive operation. This property enables ideal parallelization of ADMM for CLIME, as matrix-matrix multiplications are known to be well-suited for parallelization.
3. To further improve the setup of numerical experiments, it would be beneficial to specify the accuracy achieved by each solver.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and easy to follow.

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is an interesting contribution.

Significance: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems is a challenging task, and a simple yet robust distributed algorithm can be very helpful. The evaluation of ADMM on different architectures is a valuable contribution, although the derivation of inexact ADMM is standard, and the good scalability is largely due to the simple structure of CLIME.
