This paper discusses the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation using the Constrained L1-minimization for Inverse Matrix Estimation (CLIME) algorithm and presents a scalable variant, CLIME-ADMM, along with a distributed framework for computations. The authors also provide empirical results comparing the CLIME-ADMM algorithm to state-of-the-art techniques such as DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.

One major suggestion for improvement is to consolidate the notation, which is currently scattered throughout Sections 1, 2, and 3. A table summarizing all the notations would greatly enhance readability. For instance, the parameters ρ and η are not explicitly defined in the text. Clarifying these notations would make the paper more accessible to readers.

The block cyclic data distribution scheme proposed by the authors appears to be an interesting approach. However, it is not immediately clear why this scheme would achieve load balance and scalability. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation or theoretical justification for this choice. Furthermore, empirical results demonstrating the effectiveness of this scheme are lacking and would be beneficial in supporting the authors' claims.

Another concern is the choice of column block size, k, which seems to be set on an ad-hoc basis. The authors should provide some intuition or theoretical guidance on how to select this parameter. A more thorough discussion of the trade-offs involved in choosing k would strengthen the paper.

A more general concern is that the algorithm presented may not be truly "distributed" in the classical sense, as it lacks an explicit mechanism for message passing and communication. Instead, it may be more accurate to consider this a parallel implementation. The authors should clarify this distinction and its implications for the algorithm's scalability and performance.

Minor comments include:

* Section 1: "where λ is a tuning parameter" should be replaced with "tuning" (correct spelling).
* Section 5.1: "As Tiger is parameter tuning free" should be replaced with "tuning" (correct spelling).
* Section 2: "CLIME is summerized in" should be replaced with "CLIME is summarized in" (correct spelling).

In summary, the authors present a large-scale parallel algorithm for estimating sparse precision matrices using CLIME. The novelty of the approach lies in estimating the precision matrix by column blocks, rather than column-by-column. Empirical analysis using OpenMPI and Scalapak is also presented. However, addressing the concerns mentioned above would significantly improve the clarity and impact of the paper.
