This paper presents an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) designed to address the linear program inherent in the CLIME estimator. The authors demonstrate a convergence rate of O(1/T) and implement the algorithm utilizing both shared-memory and distributed-memory parallel architectures. The performance of the proposed method is evaluated through numerical comparisons against existing techniques, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

**Quality:** The work is technically rigorous and sound.

**Minor Comments:**

1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM applied to CLIME follows established procedures, as evidenced in related work (e.g., http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm).
2) Leveraging the inherent structure of CLIME, the subproblems within the ADMM framework can be solved component-wise, with matrix-matrix multiplication representing the computational bottleneck. This characteristic lends itself to efficient parallelization, benefiting from the well-established parallelizability of matrix-matrix operations.
3) Further detail regarding the accuracy achieved by each solver within the numerical experiments would be beneficial.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and clearly presented.

**Originality:** The investigation of ADMM’s performance across both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures represents a noteworthy contribution.

**Significance:** Addressing large-scale sparse precision estimation remains a significant challenge. This work offers a potentially valuable, simple, and robust distributed algorithm for tackling this problem. While the underlying inexact ADMM derivation is standard, the observed scalability is a direct result of CLIME’s inherent structural properties and the effective parallel implementation.
