- Paper introduces CLIME-ADMM and a distributed/parallel framework for sparse precision matrix estimation, with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; provide a consolidated symbol table to improve readability.  
- Parameters ρ and η are not defined in the text and must be explicitly specified.  
- The block-cyclic data distribution is proposed but lacks justification or analysis for its load balancing and scalability claims.  
- No empirical evidence is provided to support the claimed load balancing and scalability of the distribution scheme.  
- Choice of column block size k appears ad hoc; include theoretical or empirical guidance for selecting k.  
- The algorithm omits explicit communication/message-passing protocols, so it should be described as a parallel implementation rather than a truly distributed algorithm.  
- Minor typos to fix: "tunning" → "tuning" (Section 1), "parameter tunning free" → "tuning free" (Section 5.1), "summerized" → "summarized" (Section 2).  
- Novel contribution: column-block CLIME (vs. column-by-column) estimation is a notable methodological innovation.  
- Empirical validation uses OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK, demonstrating large-scale parallel implementation.
