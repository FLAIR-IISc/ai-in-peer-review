- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a large-scale parallel algorithm estimating sparse precision matrices by processing column blocks instead of column-by-column.  
- Includes empirical comparisons with DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare, implemented with OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; consolidate into a single table for clarity and define parameters like ρ and η formally.  
- The block cyclic data distribution is interesting but the paper doesn't explain why it yields better load balancing/scalability nor provide empirical support.  
- Choice of column block size k seems ad-hoc; authors should give intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.  
- Terminology is misleading: the paper calls the method “distributed” but lacks discussion of message passing/communication—frame as a “parallel implementation” or add communication details.  
- Minor language/typo fixes needed: “tunning” → “tuning”, “summerized” → “summarized”, and other similar corrections.
