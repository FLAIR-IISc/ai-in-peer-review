- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable CLIME variant and parallel/distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation (uses ADMM, OpenMPI, ScaLAPACK) and compares to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and Flare.  
- Main novelty: estimate the precision matrix by column blocks instead of column-by-column.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; include a consolidated symbol table; ρ and η are not defined.  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is interesting but the paper does not explain why it guarantees load balance and scalability.  
- The paper should provide theoretical justification or empirical evidence that block-cyclic partitioning produces balanced workloads and scalable performance.  
- Column-block size k is chosen ad hoc; need intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k and analysis of how k affects performance and accuracy.  
- The method may not be truly “distributed”: there is no description of explicit communication/message-passing patterns or analysis of communication costs.  
- As presented, the approach might be more accurately described as a parallel implementation rather than a distributed algorithm.  
- Empirical results lack analysis of how data partitioning and communication affect runtime and accuracy.  
- Minor typos to fix: “tunning” → “tuning” (Sections 1 and 5.1), “summerized” → “summarized” (Section 2).
