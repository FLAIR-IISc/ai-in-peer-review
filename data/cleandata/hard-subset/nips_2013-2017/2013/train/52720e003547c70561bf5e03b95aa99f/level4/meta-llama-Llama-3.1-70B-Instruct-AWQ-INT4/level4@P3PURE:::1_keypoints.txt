- Paper proposes CLIME-ADMM: a scalable variant of CLIME for sparse precision matrix estimation and a distributed computation framework.  
- Empirical comparisons presented against state-of-the-art methods (DC-QUIC, Tiger, Flare).  
- Notation is inconsistent across Sections 1–3; recommend a notation table since parameters (e.g., ρ and η) are undefined.  
- Block cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea but the paper does not justify why it ensures load balance or scalability.  
- No empirical results are provided to support claims that the block cyclic scheme achieves load balance/scalability.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; authors should provide intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.  
- The method may not be truly “distributed” because the paper lacks explicit discussion of message passing or communication mechanisms; it may be better described as a parallel implementation.  
- Novelty: estimating precision matrix by column blocks rather than column-by-column.  
- Empirical analysis uses OpenMPI and Scalapak to demonstrate large-scale parallel performance.  
- Minor language/typo fixes needed: “tunning”→“tuning”, “tunning free”→“parameter tuning free”, “summerized”→“summarized”.
