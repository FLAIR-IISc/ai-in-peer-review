## Review - Summary of the Paper

This work addresses covariance selection in high-dimensional settings, scaling to datasets with millions of features and trillions of parameters. The authors focus on the CLIME estimator, recognized for its ability to estimate the precision matrix of a Gaussian vector via a column-wise solution process, facilitating distributed computation and offering potentially simpler theoretical analysis compared to Graphical Lasso. They present a novel algorithm for fitting CLIME, employing a block-wise solution approach coupled with an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers. The paper includes theoretical results establishing the convergence rate of this algorithm, quantified in terms of both objective function value and distance to optimality. The authors specifically leverage sparse and low-rank matrix structures to minimize computational cost.  A scalable parallel implementation, compatible with both shared and distributed memory architectures, is also presented.  The algorithm’s performance is evaluated through numerical studies, comparing runtime against competing methods on both synthetic and real datasets. A comprehensive analysis investigates the algorithm’s speedup with varying block sizes and core counts across shared and distributed memory systems.

## Comments

This is a well-written and exceptionally clear paper. The novelty and contributions of the proposed method are clearly articulated in the introduction, and its relationship to existing work is well-established with thorough referencing. The algorithm is presented effectively, striking a good balance between technical details of numerical analysis/algebra and the theoretical convergence guarantees. The numerical analysis is appropriate, and the implementation demonstrates remarkably strong performance. 

My primary concern relates to the accessibility of this method.  The impact of this work will be significantly limited if the source code – built largely upon open-source libraries – is not made available to the research community. 

Overall, this is a strong algorithmic contribution addressing the critical practical problem of covariance selection in very high dimensions. Publication is strongly recommended, contingent upon public release of the code.
