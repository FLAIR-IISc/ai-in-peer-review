This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program inherent in the CLIME estimator. The authors establish a convergence rate of O(1/T) and implement the algorithm using both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons are provided with other methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound.

**Minor comments:**

1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME follows a standard approach. A relevant resource is available at: http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm.
2) Leveraging the structure of CLIME, each subproblem can be solved componentwise, with matrix-matrix multiplication being the most computationally expensive operation. Consequently, ADMM is ideally suited for parallelization in the context of CLIME, as matrix-matrix multiplications are known to parallelize effectively.
3) The setup of the numerical experiments could benefit from further specification. For example, reporting the accuracy achieved by each solver would be valuable.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized.

**Originality:** The evaluation of ADMM on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is an interesting contribution.

**Significance:** Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems remains a significant challenge. A simple yet robust distributed algorithm is therefore highly desirable. The evaluation of ADMM across different architectures is interesting, and while the derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard, the observed good scalability is attributable to the inherent simplicity and structure of CLIME.
