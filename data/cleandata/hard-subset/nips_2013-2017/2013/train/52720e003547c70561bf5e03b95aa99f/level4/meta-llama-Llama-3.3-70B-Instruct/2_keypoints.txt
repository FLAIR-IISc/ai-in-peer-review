- Presents an inexact ADMM for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence, implemented on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures and compared to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Technical aspects are sound.
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM follows established procedures seen in prior work (not novel).
- CLIME subproblems decompose component-wise; matrix-matrix multiplication is the main computational bottleneck.
- Matrix-matrix multiplication is highly parallelizable, making ADMM well-suited for CLIME parallelization.
- Numerical experiments lack some setup details—e.g., solver accuracies should be reported.
- Paper is clearly and effectively organized.
- Evaluation of ADMM performance across shared and distributed architectures provides interesting and useful insights.
- Significance: offers a simple, robust, distributed approach for large-scale sparse precision estimation, though good scalability largely stems from CLIME's simple structure.
