- Problem & approach: Scalable, distributed solver for CLIME using an inexact ADMM that processes column-blocks (enabling matrix–matrix multiplications) with O(1/T) convergence.  
- Main contribution: Large-scale computational framework (block-cyclic distribution) implemented on shared and distributed memory to scale CLIME.  
- Empirical result: Demonstrates estimating a 1M-dimensional precision matrix (~1T parameters) in ~11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Technical quality: Derivation and convergence analysis are correct; implementation and experiments are thorough and technically sound.  
- Experimental concern: Comparisons lack stopping criteria and final solution-quality metrics (objective/constraint violation), making speedups potentially unfair.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, with clear diagrams, algorithm summary, and consistent notation.  
- Originality: Novelty lies in systems engineering and empirical scaling rather than new optimization ideas; inexact ADMM and linearization are standard.  
- Reason for scalability: CLIME’s column-wise separability and reliance on optimized parallel matrix routines largely explain the strong scaling.  
- Significance: Enables ultra-large-scale statistical estimation and provides a practical blueprint for scaling l1-regularized problems in real applications.  
- For acceptance: Important problem, complete contribution (algorithm + theory + implementation), impressive scalable results, and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: Limited algorithmic novelty, scalability partly expected from problem structure and existing libraries, and unclear experimental fairness.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revision — require reporting convergence criteria and solution quality for all compared solvers.
