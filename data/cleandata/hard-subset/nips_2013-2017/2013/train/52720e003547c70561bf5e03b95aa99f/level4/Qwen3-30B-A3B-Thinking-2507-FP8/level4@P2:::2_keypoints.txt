- Addresses large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation by proposing a distributed, scalable solver based on the CLIME estimator.  
- Develops an inexact ADMM variant for CLIME that scales to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.  
- Key innovation: solve CLIME in column-blocks (not column-by-column) to leverage cache-efficient, parallel matrix-matrix multiplications.  
- Uses block cyclic data distribution for load balancing across shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for both objective value and optimality residuals.  
- Implementation details account for memory hierarchies and practical parallelization, with thorough shared- and distributed-memory descriptions.  
- Empirical results show substantial scalability and linear speedup, outperforming DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare on synthetic and real data.  
- Demonstrates a one-million-dimension precision matrix estimate (one trillion parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Builds on prior distributed ADMM and CLIME literature; columnwise separability of CLIME is appropriately exploited.  
- Novelty is mainly practical/engineering (distributed column-block CLIME and block cyclic distribution) rather than theoretical ADMM advances.  
- Derivation of inexact ADMM follows standard methodology and needs better contextualization with specific prior work on inexact ADMM.  
- Experiments lack final accuracy metrics (e.g., Frobenius norm error) and more details on parameter choices across methods.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; algorithm description and motivation for column-block processing are convincing.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add accuracy reporting and stronger literature contextualization.
