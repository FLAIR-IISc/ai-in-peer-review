- Addresses covariance selection at extreme scale (millions of features, trillions of parameters).
- Uses CLIME estimator which decomposes into column-wise subproblems and has simpler theory than Graphical Lasso.
- Proposes a new block-wise algorithm for fitting CLIME coupled with an inexact direction method of multipliers.
- States convergence-rate guarantees for objective suboptimality and distance to optimality.
- Exploits sparsity and low-rank structure during computation to reduce cost.
- Implements a scalable parallel framework for both shared-memory and distributed-memory systems.
- Empirical evaluation compares runtime against direct competitors on synthetic and real datasets and shows impressive performance.
- Provides detailed analysis of speedup as a function of block size and number of cores for both architectures.
- Paper is well written, with clear presentation of novelty, solid theoretical/algorithmic development, and good bibliography.
- Main concern: lack of publicly released source code would limit the work’s impact.
- Overall: solid algorithmic contribution that should be published if the implementation is made available to the community.
