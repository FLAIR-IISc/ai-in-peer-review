This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program problem in the CLIME estimator. The authors establish a convergence rate of O(1/T) and implement the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons with other methods, such as DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare, are provided.

Quality: The paper is technically sound.

Minor comments:

1. The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard and can be found in various sources, including http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm. While the authors' presentation is clear, it would be helpful to acknowledge the existing literature on this topic.

2. The structure of CLIME allows each subproblem to be solved componentwise, making the most expensive operation matrix-matrix multiplication. As matrix-matrix multiplications can be parallelized efficiently, the ADMM algorithm can be ideally parallelized for CLIME. This point could be emphasized to highlight the advantages of the proposed method.

3. The setup of the numerical experiments could be further specified. For instance, it would be useful to report the accuracy achieved by each solver, which would provide a more comprehensive comparison of the different methods.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and methodology.

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is an interesting contribution of this paper.

Significance: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems is a challenging task, and a simple yet robust distributed algorithm can be very helpful. The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is a valuable addition to the existing literature. However, it is worth noting that the derivation of inexact ADMM is standard, and the good scalability of the algorithm is largely due to the simple structure of CLIME.
