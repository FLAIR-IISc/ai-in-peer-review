- Contribution: Introduces CLIME-ADMM, a scalable adaptation of CLIME, and a distributed computational framework with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation issues: Sections 1–3 present fragmented notation; a consolidated notation table is needed and symbols like ρ and η are not formally defined.  
- Data distribution concern: Block cyclic data distribution is interesting but the rationale for expected load balance and scalability is not clearly articulated.  
- Empirical validation missing: Claims about load balance and scalability lack supporting experiments.  
- Parameter choice: Column block size (k) is set heuristically; the paper should provide theoretical or intuitive guidance for choosing k.  
- “Distributed” vs “parallel”: The paper lacks details on message passing/communication, so the method may be more accurately characterized as a parallel implementation rather than distributed.  
- Minor corrections: Fix typos—Section 1 “tunning”→“tuning”; Section 5.1 “parameter tunning free”→“parameter tuning free”; Section 2 “summerized”→“summarized”.  
- Overall summary: Presents a large-scale parallel algorithm for sparse precision matrix estimation using column-block estimation (with OpenMPI and Scalapak experiments) as the key innovation.
