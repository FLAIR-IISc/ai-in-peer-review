- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME sparse precision estimation with O(1/T) convergence and implementations for shared- and distributed-memory.
- Technically sound algorithm that correctly identifies and exploits matrix-matrix multiplication as the computational bottleneck for parallelization.
- Convergence analysis appears reasonable.
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard and not algorithmically novel (e.g., He & Yuan 2013).
- Main strength is scalable implementation and empirical evaluation rather than ADMM formulation novelty.
- Paper would benefit from a brief discussion of CLIME’s theoretical/statistical guarantees motivating its use.
- Well-organized, clearly written, and consistent notation.
- Experimental setup lacks detail (accuracy metrics, parameter tuning for competitors), hurting reproducibility.
- Thorough evaluation across shared- and distributed-memory architectures is a valuable contribution.
- Block-cyclic data distribution for distributed-memory appears particularly promising.
- Needs more detailed comparison to existing distributed sparse precision estimation methods, if any.
- Demonstrates near-linear scalability with number of cores; implementation likely useful in practice.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, provides a practical scalable algorithm, shows excellent scalability, and is clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: ADMM formulation not novel, experimental details insufficient, and comparisons to distributed methods incomplete.
- Overall: solid paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing experimental detail and related-work comparisons.
