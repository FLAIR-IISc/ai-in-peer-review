- Paper proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant of CLIME, and a distributed computing framework with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and Flare.
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; recommend a consolidated notation table since symbols like ρ and η appear undefined.
- Block-cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea, but the paper does not explain why it guarantees load balance and scalability nor provide experiments to support the claim.
- The choice of column-block size k appears ad hoc; the paper should provide intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.
- The approach may not be truly “distributed” because no explicit message-passing or communication mechanisms are described; it may be more accurate to call it a parallel implementation.
- Minor typos to fix: “tunning” → “tuning” (Section 1 and 5.1) and “summerized” → “summarized” (Section 2).
- Main novelty is estimating the precision matrix by column blocks instead of column-by-column; empirical results using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK are reported.
