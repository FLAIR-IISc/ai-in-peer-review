- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME linear program, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements it on shared- and distributed-memory systems with comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Derivation of inexact ADMM follows a standard approach (relevant resource: http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm).
- CLIME subproblems decompose componentwise; matrix-matrix multiplication is the main cost, making ADMM well-suited to parallelization.
- Numerical experiments lack some details—should report solver accuracy and other setup specifics.
- Paper is well-organized and clear.
- Originality lies in evaluating ADMM on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.
- Significance: provides a simple, robust, and scalable distributed algorithm addressing large-scale sparse precision estimation, with good empirical scalability due to CLIME’s structure.
