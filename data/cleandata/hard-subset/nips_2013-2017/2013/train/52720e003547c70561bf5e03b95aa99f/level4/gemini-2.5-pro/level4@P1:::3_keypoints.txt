- Problem: sparse precision matrix estimation at ultra-large scale, targeting the CLIME estimator.  
- Method: novel inexact ADMM that processes column-blocks (not column-by-column) for efficient parallelization and matrix-matrix operations.  
- Theory: proves O(1/T) convergence rate for objective value and optimality conditions.  
- Structure exploitation: discusses using underlying sparse and low-rank data structure to speed computations.  
- Implementation: distributed framework for shared- and distributed-memory with block-cyclic data distribution and use of OpenMP/OpenMPI/ScaLAPACK.  
- Empirical performance: nearly linear scaling with cores; demonstrated on problems up to 10^6 dimensions and 10^12 parameters on 400 cores.  
- Significance: tackles covariance selection at scales beyond most existing methods.  
- Originality and quality: clear presentation, balanced technical details, and substantive theoretical and algorithmic novelty.  
- Practical engineering: careful attention to load balancing and optimized matrix operations in the parallel implementation.  
- Weakness: implementation not publicly available, limiting reproducibility and community impact.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with a strong suggestion to release the source code.
