- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving the CLIME linear program with proven O(1/T) convergence.
- Implements the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.
- Provides numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Derivation of inexact ADMM is standard; should acknowledge existing literature.
- CLIME’s structure makes subproblems componentwise and matrix-matrix multiplication the main cost, enabling efficient parallelization—this advantage should be emphasized.
- Numerical experiment setup needs more detail; report solver accuracies for comprehensive comparison.
- Paper is well-organized and clear.
- Evaluation of ADMM in shared- and distributed-memory settings is an interesting/original contribution.
- Scalability/value is significant for large-scale sparse precision estimation, though scalability largely stems from CLIME’s simple structure and the standard nature of the derivation.
