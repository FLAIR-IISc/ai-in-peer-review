- Addresses large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation using a novel, scalable CLIME-ADMM algorithm and distributed computing framework.  
- Adapts ADMM for CLIME and parallelizes by solving column blocks of the precision matrix simultaneously.  
- Introduces a block cyclic data distribution strategy to improve load balance and scalability.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for the objective and optimality conditions.  
- Demonstrates near-linear scalability on shared- and distributed-memory systems, e.g., estimating a 1M-dimension precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic and real datasets with comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Leverages block-wise computation to exploit matrix multiplication optimizations.  
- Clearly defines the problem and motivates the need for extreme-scale precision matrix estimation.  
- Notation is scattered; parameters like ρ and η are introduced without initial definition—needs a consolidated notation table.  
- Rationale for why block cyclic distribution yields load balance and scalability is under-explained and needs more justification or evidence.  
- Selection of column block size k appears ad hoc; lacks guidelines or theoretical trade-off analysis (e.g., communication vs. computation).  
- Use of the term “distributed” may be misleading—implementation is more parallel than a fully distributed message-passing algorithm.  
- Limited discussion of memory hierarchy and how block sizes/data distribution optimize cache utilization.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and impactful contribution with strengths in scalability and novelty that outweigh the minor weaknesses.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, demonstrates impressive scalability, includes theoretical analysis, and provides comprehensive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification for block cyclic distribution and block-size choice, potentially misleading terminology, and minor clarity/notation issues.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the noted clarifications and minor weaknesses.
