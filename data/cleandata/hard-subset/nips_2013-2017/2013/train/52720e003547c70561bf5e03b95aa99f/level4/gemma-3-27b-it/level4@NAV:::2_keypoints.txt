- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving the CLIME linear program with proven O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Implements the algorithm for both shared-memory and distributed-memory parallel architectures.
- Benchmarks performance against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Technical approach is sound, but the ADMM derivation follows established methods.
- Notes that CLIME’s structure permits efficient componentwise subproblem solutions; matrix–matrix multiplication is the dominant cost.
- Dominant computational cost (matrix–matrix multiply) is naturally amenable to parallelization, explaining observed scalability.
- Suggests adding more detail on solver accuracy in the numerical experiments to strengthen the presentation.
- Paper is clearly organized and makes a significant contribution by demonstrating a scalable approach for large-scale sparse precision estimation.
