- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving the linear program underlying the CLIME estimator, with proven O(1/T) convergence.
- Demonstrates implementations on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.
- Provides numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard and builds on well-established techniques (reference noted).
- Given CLIME’s structure, subproblems decompose componentwise and the main bottleneck is matrix-matrix multiplication.
- ADMM is therefore well-suited to parallelization due to the parallelizable nature of matrix-matrix multiplications.
- Numerical experiments need clearer setup; specifically, report achieved accuracy for each solver.
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow.
- Original contribution lies in empirically evaluating ADMM performance across shared- and distributed-memory systems.
- Significance: a simple, robust, distributed solver for large-scale sparse precision estimation is valuable and relevant to practitioners.
