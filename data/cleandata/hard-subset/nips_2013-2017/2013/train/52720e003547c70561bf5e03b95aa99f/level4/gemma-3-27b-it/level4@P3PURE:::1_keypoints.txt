- Proposes CLIME-ADMM and a distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation, with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is inconsistent across Sections 1–3; a dedicated notation table is recommended and parameters ρ and η are undefined.  
- Proposes a block-cyclic data distribution but the rationale for load balancing and scalability is unclear.  
- Lacks empirical evidence demonstrating the claimed load balance and scalability of the distribution scheme.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; the paper should provide intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.  
- The characterization as “distributed” is questionable because no message passing or communication mechanism is described; it may be more accurate to call it parallel.  
- Empirical implementation uses OpenMPI and Scalapak.  
- Primary novelty is estimating the precision matrix by column blocks instead of column-by-column.  
- Minor wording/typo fixes needed: replace “tunning” with “tuning” and “summerized” with “summarized”; adjust related phrases in Sections 1, 2, and 5.
