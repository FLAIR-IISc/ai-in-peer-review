- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME linear program, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements it on shared- and distributed-memory systems with comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

- Paper is technically sound and well-organized.

- Tackles large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation; empirical evaluation on parallel architectures is interesting and useful.

- A simple, robust distributed algorithm for this problem would be a valuable contribution.

- Novelty and overall significance are limited; the inexact ADMM derivation is standard.

- Impressive scalability is largely an expected consequence of CLIME’s structure (component-wise subproblems and parallelizable matrix-matrix multiplies) rather than a novel algorithmic effect; authors should clarify this.

- Minor: similar approaches exist in the literature (reference/link provided).

- Minor: numerical experiment setup needs more detail—report stopping criteria and final accuracy for each solver to ensure fair comparisons.
