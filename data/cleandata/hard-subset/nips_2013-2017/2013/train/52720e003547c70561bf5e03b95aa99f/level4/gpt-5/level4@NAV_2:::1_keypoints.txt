- Presents a scalable CLIME-ADMM variant and distributed computation framework for sparse precision matrix estimation, with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and flare.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; provide a consolidated notation table and define symbols (e.g., ρ and η) at first use.  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is interesting but the paper does not sufficiently justify why it ensures load balancing and scalability; include empirical evidence isolating this effect.  
- Column block size k selection appears ad hoc; offer theoretical analysis or empirical heuristics for choosing k.  
- The manuscript reads more like a parallel implementation than a distributed algorithm because explicit communication/message-passing mechanisms are not described.  
- Minor typos to fix: “tunning” → “tuning” (Section 1, 5.1) and “summerized” → “summarized” (Section 2).  
- Overall contribution: a large-scale parallel approach to CLIME with the key novelty of solving in column blocks (rather than per-column), demonstrated using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.
