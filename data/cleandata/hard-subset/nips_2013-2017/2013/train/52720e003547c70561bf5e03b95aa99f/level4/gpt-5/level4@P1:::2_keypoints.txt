- Proposes an inexact ADMM solver for CLIME that updates column blocks with soft-thresholding and box projection, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements shared- and distributed-memory versions exploiting dynamic sparsity and low-rank C = A A^T.  
- Builds on CLIME, ADMM/inexact-ADMM literature, and parallel/block-cyclic matrix multiplication; contribution is mainly engineering/design rather than a new optimization method.  
- Technical derivation and convergence analysis are sound and align with known non-ergodic O(1/T) rates under standard parameter conditions.  
- Systems design choices (column-block updates, block-cyclic distribution, low-rank exploitation) are sensible and well-motivated.  
- Empirically shows near-linear speedups and handles up to p ≈ 10^6 (~10^12 parameters) in ~11 hours on 400 cores, scaling better than DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME (flare) at high dimensionalities.  
- Experimental methodology lacks clarity on stopping criteria and tolerances; needs reporting of primal/dual residuals, KKT violations, or objective gaps for fair runtime comparisons.  
- Needs clear description of how λ and other tuning parameters are selected per method, and reporting of solution accuracy (constraint violation, objective value, estimation error), especially on real data.  
- For DC-QUIC, clarify the thresholding used to induce block structure and its impact on solution accuracy.  
- Provide a runtime breakdown (matrix multiplications vs elementwise ops) and report communication costs to identify bottlenecks and portability issues.  
- Improve clarity by explicitly stating stopping conditions and how accuracy is matched across compared methods.  
- Give more details on data distribution and block-size choices (pb × nb × kb) and their practical selection.  
- Fix minor typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., “Comparision,” “acheived,” E vs I in box projection).  
- Originality is limited at the algorithmic level (standard inexact/linearized ADMM), with main novelty in making the approach scalable via implementation choices.  
- Significance is high due to enabling ultra-high-dimensional sparse precision estimation with good scalability; releasing code would increase impact.  
- Pros: addresses a timely large-scale problem, provides standard convergence guarantees, demonstrates thoughtful systems design, and conducts large-scale experiments.  
- Cons: limited algorithmic novelty, insufficient reporting of accuracy/optimality metrics, limited analysis of communication and memory costs, and some presentation issues.  
- Minor suggested additions: add precise citations for inexact/linearized ADMM (e.g., He and Yuan), emphasize parallelizable matrix-matrix cost structure, and report achieved solver accuracies.  
- Overall: technically sound with moderate methodological novelty and strong systems/scalability contributions; would be a solid paper after improving experimental accuracy reporting and comparison protocol.
