This paper addresses the challenging problem of sparse precision matrix estimation in high dimensions, specifically focusing on scalability to extremely large datasets with millions of variables and trillions of parameters. The authors leverage the CLIME estimator, known for its theoretical properties and suitability for column-wise computation, and propose an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) algorithm. A key innovation is solving the problem in column-blocks rather than column-by-column, coupled with a scalable distributed framework. Convergence rates for the algorithm are established, and the authors exploit sparse and low-rank matrix structures to further improve efficiency. Extensive experiments on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures demonstrate significant performance gains over state-of-the-art methods.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear, presenting a complex topic in an accessible manner. The novelty and contributions are clearly articulated in the introduction, and the work is appropriately contextualized with thorough references to related research. The algorithm is presented with a good balance of technical detail and theoretical justification, including convergence rate analysis. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, showcasing the algorithm's impressive performance and scalability. 

A potential concern is the availability of the implementation. The impact of this work would be significantly enhanced if the source code, largely built upon open-source libraries, were made accessible to the research community. 

Overall, this is a strong paper addressing an important practical problem – covariance selection in very high-dimensional settings. The proposed method is a valuable contribution, and publication would be warranted, particularly if accompanied by public code release.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with clear theoretical justifications and strong experimental validation. The analysis of convergence rates and the consideration of sparse and low-rank structures demonstrate careful attention to detail. It represents a complete piece of work, not merely a position paper, and the authors address both strengths and weaknesses of their approach.
**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The explanations are clear and concise, and the notation is consistent. The algorithm and its implementation details are thoroughly described.
**Originality:** The combination of a block-wise ADMM approach for CLIME with a scalable distributed framework is novel and addresses a significant challenge in the field. The exploitation of matrix structure further enhances the originality. Related work is appropriately cited.
**Significance:** The results are important, as they provide a practical solution for estimating precision matrices in extremely high-dimensional settings where existing methods struggle. The scalability of the algorithm and its demonstrated performance suggest that it could be widely adopted by researchers and practitioners. It advances the state of the art in sparse precision matrix estimation and offers a unique approach to tackling this challenging problem.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
* Addresses a highly relevant and challenging problem.
* Presents a novel and well-justified algorithm.
* Demonstrates strong theoretical results and empirical performance.
* Provides a scalable and efficient implementation.
* Well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments against acceptance:**
* Limited impact without public code release.
