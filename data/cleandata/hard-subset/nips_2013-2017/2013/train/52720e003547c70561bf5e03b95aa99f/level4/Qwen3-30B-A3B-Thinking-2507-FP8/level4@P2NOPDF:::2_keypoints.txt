- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving the CLIME linear program and proves an O(1/T) convergence rate.  
- Implements the method for both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Compares performance empirically against DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Technical derivation is sound but follows standard inexact ADMM procedures (e.g., He and Ma, 2013).  
- Key insight: CLIME’s structure allows element-wise subproblem solutions and makes matrix-matrix multiplication the dominant, parallelizable cost.  
- Scalability is primarily due to CLIME’s simplicity, not novelty in the ADMM framework.  
- Experimental reporting is incomplete—critical details like achieved accuracy (e.g., relative duality gap) per solver are missing.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Originality is moderate: distributed/shared-memory evaluation for CLIME is novel, but methodological contributions are conventional.  
- Significance is practical: provides a simple, robust, and scalable tool for large-scale sparse precision estimation, though theoretical novelty is limited.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to improve experimental rigor and clarify contributions relative to existing ADMM work.
