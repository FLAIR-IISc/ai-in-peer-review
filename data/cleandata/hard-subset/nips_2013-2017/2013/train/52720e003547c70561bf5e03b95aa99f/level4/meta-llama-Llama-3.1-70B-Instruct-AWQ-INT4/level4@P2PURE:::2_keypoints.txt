- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME linear program, proving an O(1/T) convergence rate.  
- Implements the algorithm on shared-memory and distributed-memory systems and compares numerically to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Paper is technically sound.  
- Derivation of inexact ADMM is standard and appears in existing literature.  
- CLIME’s structure permits component-wise solution of subproblems; matrix-matrix multiplication is the dominant cost.  
- That structure makes ADMM highly parallelizable, as matrix-matrix multiplications parallelize well.  
- Minor suggestion: report the accuracy achieved by each solver in experiments.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow.  
- Evaluating ADMM across architectures is an interesting/original contribution.  
- Significance: a simple, robust distributed method for large-scale sparse precision estimation; strong scalability largely stems from CLIME’s simple structure.
