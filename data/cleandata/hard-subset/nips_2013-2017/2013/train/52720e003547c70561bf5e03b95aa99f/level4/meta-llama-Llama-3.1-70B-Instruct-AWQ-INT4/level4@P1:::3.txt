Summary of the paper

This paper addresses the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation in high dimensions using the CLIME estimator. The authors propose an inexact alternating direction method of multiplier (ADMM) algorithm for CLIME, which can scale to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores. The algorithm solves CLIME in column-blocks, leveraging elementwise operations and parallel matrix multiplications. The authors also develop a large-scale distributed framework for the computations, which can be implemented on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods and scales almost linearly with the number of cores.
