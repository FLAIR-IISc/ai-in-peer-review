This paper addresses the challenging problem of sparse precision matrix estimation in high-dimensional settings, scaling to millions of variables and trillions of parameters. The authors focus on the CLIME estimator, leveraging its desirable theoretical properties and inherent computational advantages. They present an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) algorithm for CLIME, accompanied by a scalable distributed framework for computation. Theoretical convergence rates are established, and the algorithm’s performance is evaluated on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, demonstrating near-linear scalability with the number of cores.

This is a well-written and clear paper. The authors effectively establish the context of the problem, clearly articulate the novelty of their contributions, and provide sound bibliographic references connecting their work to the existing literature. The algorithmic approach is well-presented, balancing technical details with theoretical guarantees regarding convergence rates. The numerical evaluation is appropriate, and the reported performance gains are impressive, particularly the ability to estimate a precision matrix with one million dimensions and one trillion parameters in a reasonable timeframe. The consideration of sparse and low-rank structure to further optimize computations is a valuable addition. 

The paper builds upon existing work in sparse precision matrix estimation [28, 3, 19, 4] and distributed optimization [29, 22, 6, 7, 20, 2, 23, 9]. The choice of CLIME is well-justified, given its theoretical properties and potential for parallelization. The use of an inexact ADMM variant, combined with block-wise computation, appears to be a key innovation enabling scalability. The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing methods when applied to ultra-large-scale problems. The discussion of block cyclic data distribution and its benefits in terms of load balancing and cache efficiency is insightful. The experimental validation on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures strengthens the claims of scalability.

**Strengths:**

*   **Scalability:** The algorithm demonstrably scales to very large problem sizes, a significant achievement in this field.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The convergence rate analysis provides confidence in the algorithm's behavior.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and easy to understand, even for readers not intimately familiar with the specific techniques used.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The problem addressed is important, and the proposed solution has the potential to impact a variety of applications.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The experiments are thorough and provide compelling evidence of the algorithm's effectiveness.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Code Availability:** The most significant concern is the lack of mention regarding code availability. Releasing the implementation would substantially increase the impact of this work and facilitate further research in this area. Without code availability, reproducing the results and building upon this work is considerably more difficult.
*   **Comparison Depth:** While comparisons are made with existing algorithms, a more detailed analysis of the trade-offs between CLIME-ADMM and alternatives (e.g., in terms of memory usage, parameter tuning sensitivity) could be beneficial.
*   **Assumptions:** While the authors mention leveraging low-rank structure, the specific assumptions made about the covariance matrix (e.g., degree of sparsity, rank) and their impact on performance could be more explicitly discussed.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a significant advance in the scalability of sparse precision matrix estimation.
*   The theoretical analysis provides a solid foundation for the algorithm.
*   The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed approach.
*   The work addresses an important and challenging problem with potential for broad impact.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The lack of code availability limits the reproducibility and potential impact of the work.
*   The comparisons with existing methods could be more in-depth.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of high-dimensional statistics and machine learning. The demonstrated scalability of CLIME-ADMM is particularly impressive. However, the lack of code availability is a significant concern. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend acceptance contingent upon the authors making their code publicly available. If code availability is not feasible, I would lower my recommendation to borderline reject, as the practical impact of the work is significantly diminished without it.
