- Paper tackles sparse precision matrix estimation at ultra-high dimensions using CLIME with an inexact ADMM algorithm processing column-blocks for distributed computation.  
- Proposes exploiting sparse and low-rank structure to enable column-block solving and scalable parallel implementations for shared and distributed memory.  
- Provides theoretical O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions.  
- Implementation demonstrates substantial speedups and near-linear scaling with cores on very large synthetic and real datasets.  
- Builds on prior CLIME and distributed optimization work and clearly positions itself relative to related literature.  
- Quality: technically sound, well-motivated algorithm design, and strong theoretical guarantees.  
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written, organized, and presented with effective visualizations.  
- Originality: novel column-block adaptation of ADMM that leverages problem structure for massive-scale CLIME.  
- Significance: enables precision matrix estimation at scales (e.g., 1M dimensions) previously infeasible, with wide application potential.  
- Main weakness: no clear statement committing to release the implementation/source code, limiting practical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on authors committing to make their implementation available upon publication.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel scalable algorithm, comprehensive experiments, clear exposition, high practical relevance.  
- Argument against acceptance: lack of clear code availability commitment (easily addressable).
