- Presents an inexact ADMM algorithm for the CLIME estimator with O(1/T) convergence and scalable shared- and distributed-memory implementations.
- Tackles an important problem: scalable sparse precision matrix estimation for high-dimensional settings (n < p).
- Contribution is significant in enabling CLIME to handle millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters via distributed computing.
- Convergence analysis is provided and algorithmic steps are clearly described, indicating technical soundness.
- Demonstrates near-linear scaling with number of cores and strong empirical performance on synthetic and real datasets.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly organized, aiding readability.
- Empirical evaluation compares to established methods (DC-QUIC, Tiger, CLIME-Flare), supporting claimed performance gains.
- Originality is incremental: ADMM, block-wise optimization, and distributed computation are not novel individually; novelty is mainly in applying and implementing them for CLIME.
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard and similar to existing resources.
- Experimental setup lacks detail on solution quality metrics (e.g., relative error, false positive/negative rates) beyond runtime.
- Discussion of alternative distributed optimization strategies and trade-offs is limited.
- Theoretical contributions are standard for ADMM; deeper analysis of inexactness impact or CLIME-specific guarantees is missing.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical relevance, excellent scalability, thorough empirical support, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty of core ideas, incremental theoretical contribution, and incomplete evaluation of solution quality.
- Overall assessment: a solid, practically valuable contribution with strong empirical results; recommended for acceptance.
