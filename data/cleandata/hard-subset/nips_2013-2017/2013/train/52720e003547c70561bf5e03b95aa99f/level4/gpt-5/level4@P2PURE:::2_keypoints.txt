- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME linear program, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements it on shared- and distributed-memory architectures with comparisons to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard (similar work exists).
- CLIME subproblems decompose componentwise, so the dominant cost is matrix–matrix multiplication.
- Matrix–matrix multiplications parallelize effectively, explaining ADMM’s good parallel performance.
- Numerical experiment setup lacks detail (e.g., requested solver accuracies).
- Paper is well organized and clear.
- Evaluation of ADMM on shared- and distributed-memory architectures is an interesting/original contribution.
- Significance: a simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision estimation is valuable, though observed scalability is largely due to CLIME’s simple structure and standard derivation.
