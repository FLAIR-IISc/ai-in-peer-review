- Tackles sparse precision matrix estimation in ultra-high-dimensional settings (millions of variables, trillions of parameters).
- Centers on the CLIME estimator, leveraging its theoretical properties and suitability for distributed computation.
- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME with established convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions.
- Develops a scalable distributed framework using column-block operations and optimized matrix multiplications.
- Implements and evaluates performance on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, showing strong scalability.
- Paper is well-written, with clear novelty, thorough connections to related work, and balanced technical/theoretical presentation.
- Convergence analysis and theoretical justification are solid and well-presented.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and demonstrates substantial performance gains.
- Authors note potential further gains by exploiting sparse and low-rank structure.
- Minor concern: source code is not publicly available, limiting reproducibility and impact.
- Overall assessment: strong algorithmic contribution addressing an important practical problem; publication recommended if software is released.
