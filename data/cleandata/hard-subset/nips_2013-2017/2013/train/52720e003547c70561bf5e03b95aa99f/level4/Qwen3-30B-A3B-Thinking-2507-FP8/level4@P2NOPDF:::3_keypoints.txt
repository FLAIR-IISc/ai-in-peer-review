- Addresses covariance selection in extremely high-dimensional settings (millions of features, trillions of parameters).
- Focuses on the CLIME estimator for Gaussian graphical models, enabling column-wise precision estimation and distributed computation.
- Proposes a novel block-wise algorithm for CLIME combined with an inexact ADMM solver.
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for the objective and solution distance to optimality.
- Improves computational efficiency via specialized handling of sparse and low-rank matrix structures.
- Implements the method in a scalable parallel framework supporting shared and distributed memory.
- Demonstrates superior runtime performance against competitors on synthetic and real datasets.
- Includes a detailed scalability analysis studying speedup versus block size and core count across systems.
- Paper is very well written with strong contextualization and comprehensive references.
- Algorithmic development balances numerical/linear-algebraic depth with rigorous convergence guarantees.
- Major concern: lack of publicly available source code undermines reproducibility and practical impact.
- Recommendation: strong contribution deserving publication contingent on releasing the implementation.
