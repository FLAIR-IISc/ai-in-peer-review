- Proposes a scalable distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME, targeting ultra-high-dimensional problems (p up to 10^6).  
- Introduces an inexact ADMM algorithm that operates on column blocks instead of individual columns to enable efficient parallelization.  
- Implements block cyclic data distribution for distributed-memory and shared-memory architectures to improve load balancing and cache efficiency.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for both the objective function and optimality conditions.  
- Empirically demonstrates substantial scalability and near-linear speedup, handling problems with up to p^2 = 10^12 parameters.  
- Builds on prior CLIME and ADMM work; core ADMM derivation is largely standard and cites relevant literature.  
- Main innovations are the column-block approach and distributed framework rather than novel ADMM theory.  
- Experiments convincingly show scalability improvements over existing methods (e.g., Tiger, CLIME-Flare, DC-QUIC).  
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous convergence analysis and thorough experimental validation.  
- Would benefit from a detailed computational-complexity comparison between column-block and column-by-column approaches, especially regarding cache efficiency.  
- Clarity is good: well-organized, precise algorithm descriptions, and adequate experimental setup.  
- Lacks specific details on numerical tolerances, ADMM stopping criteria, and exact accuracy metrics reported for each method.  
- Comparison should clarify why parameter-free Tiger (with minimax rates) is not preferred for large-scale problems despite its theoretical strengths.  
- Originality: significant practical advance in scaling CLIME to million-dimensional problems via implementation and distribution insights.  
- Significance: enables precision matrix estimation previously infeasible for applications in genomics, finance, climate science; demonstrated 11-hour runtime for p=10^6 on 400 cores.  
- Weaknesses: limited novelty in ADMM core, insufficient numerical/accuracy details, weaker discussion of broader applicability, and need for clearer comparative justification versus alternatives.  
- Recommendation: add discussion on adapting the framework to other large-scale constrained optimization problems to broaden impact.
