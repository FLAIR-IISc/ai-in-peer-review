This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program in the CLIME estimator. The authors establish a convergence rate of O(1/T) and implement the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons with other methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare, are provided.

Quality: The paper is technically sound.

Minor comments:

1. The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME follows a standard approach, similar to that presented in http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm.

2. The structure of CLIME allows the solution of each subproblem to be computed componentwise, with the most expensive operation being matrix-matrix multiplication. As matrix-matrix multiplications can be parallelized efficiently, ADMM is well-suited for parallelization in the context of CLIME.

3. The setup of the numerical experiments could be further specified. For instance, it would be helpful to report the accuracy achieved by each solver.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized, making it easy to follow.

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is an interesting contribution.

Significance: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems is a challenging task, and a simple yet robust distributed algorithm can be highly beneficial. The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is a valuable addition to the field. However, it is worth noting that the derivation of inexact ADMM is a standard technique, and the good scalability of the algorithm is largely due to the simple structure of CLIME.
