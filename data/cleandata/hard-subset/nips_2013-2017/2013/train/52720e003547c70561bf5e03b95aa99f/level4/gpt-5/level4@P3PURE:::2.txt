The authors propose an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear programming problem arising in the CLIME estimator. They establish a convergence rate of O(1/T) and implement the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons with other methods, including DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare, are provided.

Quality: The paper is technically sound.

Minor comments:
1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard; see, for example: http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm
2) Owing to the structure of CLIME, each subproblem can be solved componentwise, and the most expensive operation is matrix–matrix multiplication. Consequently, ADMM is ideally parallelizable for CLIME, and matrix–matrix multiplications are well known to parallelize efficiently.
3) The setup of the numerical experiments could be specified in greater detail. For example, what accuracy is achieved by each solver?

Clarity: The paper is well organized.

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.

Significance: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems is challenging, and a simple yet robust distributed algorithm is valuable. The evaluation of ADMM on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting. The derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard, and the good scalability observed is largely due to the simple structure of CLIME.
