

This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program underlying the CLIME estimator, establishing an O(1/T) convergence rate. The authors implement the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, comparing its performance against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

Quality: The technical approach is sound.  
Minor comments:  
1) The inexact ADMM derivation for CLIME follows standard procedures (e.g., as presented in He et al., 2012).  
2) Due to CLIME’s structure, each subproblem admits element-wise solution, with matrix-matrix multiplication as the dominant computational step. Given the well-established parallelizability of matrix-matrix operations, the algorithm achieves near-optimal scalability for CLIME.  
3) The numerical setup requires greater detail—specifically, the convergence tolerance achieved by each solver should be reported.  

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
Originality: The empirical evaluation of ADMM across shared-memory and distributed-memory platforms presents a novel contribution.  
Significance: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation remains challenging. The proposed distributed algorithm offers a simple yet robust solution, with its strong scalability directly attributable to CLIME’s structural simplicity. The evaluation of ADMM’s parallel performance is thus both timely and valuable.
