- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving the CLIME linear program for large-scale distributed sparse precision estimation.  
- Proves an O(1/T) convergence rate for the proposed algorithm.  
- Implements the algorithm in both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Provides numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Technical analysis is thorough and the method is sound.  
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard and exists in prior literature.  
- Numerical experiments lack detail on solver accuracy and experimental setup.  
- Paper is well-organized and clear, but needs minor clarifications (experiment details, accuracy reporting).  
- Originality is mixed: evaluation across architectures is interesting but derivation itself is not novel.  
- Good scalability may be due in large part to CLIME’s simple structure rather than the method alone.  
- Significance: provides a simple, robust distributed solution for large-scale sparse precision estimation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
