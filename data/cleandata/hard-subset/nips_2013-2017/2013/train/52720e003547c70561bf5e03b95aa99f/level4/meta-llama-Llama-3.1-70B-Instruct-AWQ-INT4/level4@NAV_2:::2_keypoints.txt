- Presents a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator.  
- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME and proves convergence rates for the objective and optimality conditions.  
- Implements the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Demonstrates scalability to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores.  
- Technically sound derivation; the inexact ADMM formulation is standard.  
- Each subproblem decomposes componentwise; the main cost is matrix-matrix multiplication which parallelizes well for CLIME.  
- Numerical experiments support the algorithm’s scalability in both shared and distributed settings.  
- Paper is well-organized and the evaluation of ADMM across architectures is interesting and useful.  
- Originality is limited: scalability stems largely from CLIME’s simple structure rather than novel algorithmic contributions.  
- Experimental setup needs more specification (e.g., accuracy achieved by each solver).  
- Recommendation: accept, but ask authors to better specify experiments and emphasize any novel contributions beyond standard inexact ADMM.
