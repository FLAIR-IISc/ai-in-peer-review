- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME linear program, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements/tests it on shared- and distributed-memory systems against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

- Technical contributions are solid.

- The inexact ADMM derivation follows well-established procedures in prior literature.

- ADMM fits CLIME well because subproblems decompose componentwise; the main cost is matrix-matrix multiplication, which is highly parallelizable.

- Numerical experiments lack detail—need clarification of stopping criteria and accuracy for each solver.

- Manuscript is clearly written and well-structured.

- Main novelty is the empirical evaluation across both shared- and distributed-memory architectures.

- Significance: addresses large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation; a simple, robust distributed algorithm and its cross-architecture evaluation are valuable.

- Limitation: derivation is standard and the scalability gains are largely expected given CLIME’s parallelizable structure.
