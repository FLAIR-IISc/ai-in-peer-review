The authors discuss the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME and present a scalable variant CLIME-ADMM along with a distributed framework for computations. Empirical results comparing the CLIME-ADMM algorithm to state-of-the-art techniques such as DC-QUIC, Tiger and Flare are presented.

The main idea of the paper is to scale up the CLIME estimator to high-dimensional problems by proposing an inexact alternating direction method of multiplier (ADMM) algorithm for CLIME, and establishing rates of convergence for both the objective and optimality conditions. The proposed framework solves CLIME in column-blocks and uses block cyclic distribution to achieve load balancing. Experimental results show that the algorithm is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods and scales almost linearly with the number of cores.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and their proposed solution. The technical content is sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithm. The experimental results are also well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of a clear explanation of the block cyclic data distribution scheme. While the authors mention that this scheme achieves load balance and scalability, it is not entirely clear why this is the case. Some additional explanation or empirical results to support this claim would be helpful.

Another potential concern is the choice of the column block size, which appears to be set on an ad hoc basis. Some intuition or theory to guide the choice of this parameter would be helpful.

The paper is well-organized, and the notation is generally clear. However, some notation is scattered throughout the text, and a table summarizing the notation would be helpful.

Minor comments include a typo in Section 1 ("tunning" instead of "tuning"), a typo in Section 5.1 ("parameter tunning free" instead of "parameter tuning free"), and a typo in Section 2 ("summerized" instead of "summarized").

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel solution to the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME.
* The proposed algorithm is scalable and achieves good performance on high-dimensional problems.
* The experimental results are well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The block cyclic data distribution scheme is not entirely clear, and some additional explanation or empirical results would be helpful.
* The choice of the column block size appears to be set on an ad hoc basis, and some intuition or theory to guide this choice would be helpful.
* The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of the notation and a table summarizing the notation.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a novel solution to the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME. While there are some minor concerns and areas for improvement, the paper is generally strong and warrants acceptance.
