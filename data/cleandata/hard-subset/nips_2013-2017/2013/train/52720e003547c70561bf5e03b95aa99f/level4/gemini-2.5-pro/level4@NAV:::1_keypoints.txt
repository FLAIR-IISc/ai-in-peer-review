- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a large-scale parallel algorithm estimating sparse precision matrices by column blocks rather than column-by-column.  
- Empirical implementation uses OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK and compares CLIME-ADMM to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is introduced inconsistently across Sections 1–3; a consolidated notation table would improve readability.  
- Parameters ρ and η are used without explicit definitions and should be defined.  
- The block cyclic data distribution idea is interesting but its load-balancing and scalability advantages are not clearly justified.  
- Claims about the block scheme need empirical validation demonstrating load balance and scalability benefits.  
- Choice of column block size k appears ad-hoc; provide theoretical intuition or practical guidelines for selecting k.  
- Calling the work “distributed” is misleading without discussion of message passing and communication overhead; it may be more accurately described as a parallel implementation.  
- Minor corrections: fix typos — "tunning" → "tuning" (Section 1), "summerized" → "summarized" (Section 2), and "parameter tunning free" → "parameter tuning free" (Section 5.1).
