- Introduces CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant of CLIME, and a distributed/parallel computational framework for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Empirical comparisons are presented against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is inconsistent and scattered across Sections 1–3; a consolidated notation table is recommended.  
- Parameters ρ and η are used without prior definition in the main text.  
- Block cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea but lacks justification and empirical evidence for load balance and scalability.  
- Choice of column block size k appears ad hoc; authors should provide theoretical or empirical guidance on k’s impact.  
- Claiming the method is “distributed” is questionable without explicit message-passing/communication details; it may be more accurate to call it parallel.  
- Clarify any communication mechanisms between computational nodes to distinguish distributed vs. parallel implementation.  
- Minor wording/typo fixes needed: “tunning” → “tuning”, “parameter tunning free” → “tuning-free”, “summerized” → “summarized”.  
- Core novelty: estimating the precision matrix by operating on column blocks instead of individual columns.  
- Empirical implementation uses OpenMPI and Scalapak; addressing notation, block-size justification, and distribution details would improve impact.
