- Formulates CLIME as LPs and proposes an inexact/linearized ADMM with column-block decomposition exploiting C = AA′ to reduce matmul cost from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k) and proves O(1/T) convergence for objective and residuals.  
- Develops a distributed block-cyclic data distribution for shared- and distributed-memory systems emphasizing load balance and cache efficiency.  
- Empirically demonstrates near-linear speedups and the ability to handle p ≈ 10^6 (≈10^12 parameters) on hundreds of cores, comparing to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Builds on existing sparse precision estimation and ADMM literature; the inexact ADMM updates and O(1/T) rate align with known results.  
- Technical soundness: algorithm and convergence guarantees are correct and consistent with established theory.  
- Empirics: implementation shows strong scalability and uses representative baseline comparisons.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized and clearly describes algorithm steps and the distributed framework.  
- Originality: core optimization derivation is standard; main novelty is in scalable system design (column-block ADMM, low-rank matmul, block-cyclic distribution).  
- Significance: scaling sparse precision estimation to millions of dimensions is impactful and useful for practitioners.  
- Strength: clearly identifies matmul as the computational bottleneck and effectively exploits low-rank structure C = AA′.  
- Strength: practical distributed design with attention to block-cyclic layout, cache behavior, and load balance.  
- Strength: strong empirical scalability including a challenging p = 10^6 experiment.  
- Strength: provides theoretical guarantees (non-ergodic O(1/T) rates) for the proposed inexact ADMM variant.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—linearized/inexact ADMM derivation is standard.  
- Weakness: experimental setup lacks key specifics (stopping criteria, primal/dual tolerances, enforcement of infinity-norm feasibility at termination).  
- Weakness: missing accuracy metrics across solvers (constraint violation, objective value, statistical error) to complement runtime.  
- Weakness: unclear parameter selection for λ across methods and the impact of tuning differences (TIGER vs CLIME).  
- Weakness: post-processing (symmetrization/positive-definiteness enforcement) is not described nor its computational cost reported.  
- Weakness: no sensitivity analysis on column-block size k, sparsity patterns, or sample size n; lacks guidance for choosing k.  
- Weakness: memory footprint per node (A, intermediates, column-block variables) and maximal problem sizes per resource are not quantified.  
- Weakness: comparisons should consider graphical lasso/QUIC without block-diagonal pre-thresholding to assess performance when C is not block-diagonal.  
- Pro-acceptance: addresses an important scalability gap with a clean, well-engineered distributed solution and convincing empirical results plus convergence analysis.  
- Con-acceptance: limited methodological novelty and gaps in empirical evaluation (accuracy, stopping conditions) and practical documentation (PD enforcement, parameter selection, sensitivity).  
- Minor comments: better position rate claims with references (e.g., He & Yuan 2012; Boyd et al.), emphasize matmul as the dominant parallel kernel, and report per-solver residuals and final objectives (not just runtime).
