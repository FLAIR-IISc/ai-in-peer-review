This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multiplier (ADMM) for solving the linear program problem in the CLIME estimator, which is used for large scale distributed sparse precision estimation. A convergence rate of O(1/T) is established. The algorithm is implemented using both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons with other methods, such as DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare, are provided.

Quality: The paper is technically sound. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rate of their proposed algorithm and demonstrate its scalability on large datasets. However, there are a few minor comments that can be made. Firstly, the derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard and can be found in other sources (e.g., http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm). Secondly, due to the structures of CLIME, the solution of each subproblem can be computed componentwise, and the most expensive operation is matrix-matrix multiplication. Hence, ADMM can be parallelized ideally for CLIME, and it is well known that matrix-matrix multiplications can be parallelized well. Lastly, the set up of the numerical experiments can be further specified. For example, what is the accuracy achieved by each solver?

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation and the CLIME estimator. The proposed algorithm and its implementation are also clearly explained.

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting and novel. While ADMM has been widely used in various applications, its performance in different architectures is not well studied. The authors' work provides valuable insights into the scalability of ADMM in different settings.

Significance: Solving large scale sparse precision estimation problems is challenging, and a simple yet robust distributed algorithm is helpful. The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting and can be useful for other applications. However, it is worth noting that the derivation of inexact ADMM is standard, and the good scalability is due to the simple structure of CLIME. Nevertheless, the authors' work provides a useful contribution to the field of large scale machine learning.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel algorithm for large scale distributed sparse precision estimation.
* The algorithm is implemented using both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, and its scalability is demonstrated on large datasets.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the convergence rate of the proposed algorithm.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The derivation of inexact ADMM is standard and can be found in other sources.
* The good scalability of the algorithm is due to the simple structure of CLIME, which may not be generalizable to other problems.
* The set up of the numerical experiments can be further specified, and the accuracy achieved by each solver is not reported.
