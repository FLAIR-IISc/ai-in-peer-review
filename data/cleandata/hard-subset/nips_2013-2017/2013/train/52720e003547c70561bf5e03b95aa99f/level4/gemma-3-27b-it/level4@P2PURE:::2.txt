This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) to solve the linear program arising in the CLIME estimator, and establishes a convergence rate of O(1/T). The algorithm is implemented for both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, and its performance is compared to existing methods including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

Quality: The paper is technically sound. 

Minor comments:
1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM applied to CLIME follows a standard approach, as seen, for example, in http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm.
2) Given the structure of CLIME, each subproblem can be solved componentwise, with matrix-matrix multiplication dominating the computational cost. This lends itself to ideal parallelization within the ADMM framework, leveraging the well-known parallelizability of matrix-matrix multiplications.
3) The numerical experiments could benefit from further specification, particularly regarding the accuracy achieved by each solver.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized. 

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM across shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is of interest. 

Significance: Efficiently solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems remains a challenge. A simple and robust distributed algorithm is valuable, and the evaluation of ADMM on different architectures is a positive contribution. While the derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard, the observed good scalability is attributable to the inherent structure of the CLIME problem.
