- Paper proposes CLIME-ADMM and a framework for distributed/parallel sparse precision matrix estimation using column-block computations.
- Empirical comparisons are provided against DC-QUIC, TIGER, and Flare, with experiments using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.
- Main novelty is computing the precision matrix in column blocks rather than strictly column-by-column.
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; a consolidated notation table is needed and symbols like ρ and η are undefined.
- The block-cyclic data distribution is interesting but it is unclear why it guarantees load balance and scalability; no theoretical justification is given.
- No experiments are provided to substantiate the load-balance and scalability claims.
- Column-block size k appears chosen ad hoc; the paper should provide intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.
- The approach may not be truly “distributed” since message-passing/communication mechanisms are not described; “parallel implementation” may be a more accurate label.
- Minor typos to fix: “tunning” → “tuning” (Section 1 and 5.1) and “summerized” → “summarized” (Section 2).
