- Problem: Scales CLIME sparse precision estimation to extreme dimensions via a column-block inexact ADMM algorithm.  
- Method: Column-block ADMM reduces per-iteration work to elementwise ops and matrix-matrix products, exploiting C = AA′ low-rank structure (n ≪ p).  
- Theory: Proves O(1/T) convergence for objective suboptimality and constraint residuals under condition η ≥ ρ λ_max^2(C); provides a perturbation result for sparsifying C.  
- Systems: Implements shared-memory and distributed-memory versions (OpenMP, OpenMPI, ScaLAPACK) with block-cyclic data distribution.  
- Empirics: Shows near-linear speedups, competitive to superior scaling versus flare ADMM and DC-QUIC, and solves p = 10^6 (~10^12 parameters) in ~11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on CLIME’s separability and ADMM literature; emphasizes CLIME-specific decomposition and communication-friendly matrix multiplications compared to DC-QUIC/glasso.  
- Strength — scalability design: Column-block updates map to efficient matrix-matrix products and cache-friendly/block-cyclic distribution.  
- Strength — theory: Provides convergence rates and justification for truncation-based sparsification without losing deviation guarantees.  
- Strength — systems implementation: Demonstrates scalable implementations and a large-scale real-world run.  
- Strength — empirical analysis: Detailed scaling studies (cores, block size k) and discussion of shared vs distributed tradeoffs.  
- Weakness — clarity/notation: Notation scattered and some symbols (ρ, η) used before definition; recommend a consolidated notation table and explicit PSD condition for weighted norm in Theorem 2.  
- Weakness — distributed details: Insufficient description of per-iteration communication patterns, collectives, and consistency/aggregation of variables Y, Z across processes.  
- Weakness — block-cyclic claim: No ablation isolating benefits of block-cyclic vs cyclic vs contiguous partitioning; missing load-balance and cache-miss measurements.  
- Weakness — block size k: Choice appears ad hoc; needs heuristics or a roofline-style model relating k to arithmetic intensity, bandwidth, cache, and communication.  
- Weakness — evaluation fairness: Report objective values, residuals, solution quality metrics, and detailed breakdown (A′X, AX, soft, box, communication) and memory per node, especially for the trillion-parameter run.  
- Weakness — ablation with covariance blocking: Suggest comparing CLIME-ADMM using the same block-thresholding/DC-QUIC blocking to quantify potential speedups.  
- Weakness — Theorem 3 guidance: Needs practical guidance on truncation thresholds and how to adjust λ when using perturbed C + Δ to retain guarantees.  
- Assessment — quality: Algorithmic design and rates are sound but need clarifications on assumptions and practical tuning implications.  
- Assessment — clarity: Readable but needs reorganization, notation table, and clearer distributed operation descriptions.  
- Assessment — originality: Novel integration of known components (column-block CLIME, linearized ADMM, low-rank multiplies, block-cyclic distribution) for ultra-high-dimensional CLIME.  
- Assessment — significance: High practical significance for large-scale graphical model estimation; demonstrated scale is noteworthy.  
- Pros for acceptance: Addresses scalability with practical engineering, provides convergence guarantees, and shows impressive cluster-scale results.  
- Cons for acceptance: Lacks distributed communication detail and ablation of block-cyclic benefits; limited guidance on k/block sizing; clarity and minor theoretical ambiguities.  
- Minor comments: Typos and wording fixes ("tunning"→"tuning", "parameter tunning free"→"parameter tuning free", "summerized"→"summarized", "Scalapak"→"ScaLAPACK"); define ρ and η at first use.
