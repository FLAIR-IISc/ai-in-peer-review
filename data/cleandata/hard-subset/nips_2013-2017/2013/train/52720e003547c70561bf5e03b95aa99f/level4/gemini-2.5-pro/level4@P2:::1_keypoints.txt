- Problem addressed: scaling sparse precision matrix estimation (CLIME) to ultra-large dimensions (p up to 10^6).
- Primary contribution: CLIME-ADMM, an inexact ADMM algorithm designed for block-wise column processing.
- Column-block processing: solve blocks of columns simultaneously to convert repeated matrix-vector ops into efficient matrix-matrix multiplications.
- Inexact ADMM formulation: element-wise updates plus matrix multiplications, provable O(1/T) convergence for objective and constraint violations.
- Parallelization-friendly operations: updates are suitable for parallel execution on modern hardware.
- Scalable distributed framework: implementations for shared-memory (OpenMP) and distributed-memory (OpenMPI + ScaLAPACK).
- Data distribution: use of block cyclic distribution to balance load and exploit memory hierarchies across processes.
- Empirical results: demonstrates scalability to trillion-parameter problems and near-linear speedups with hundreds of cores.
- Performance vs prior work: substantial speedups over state-of-the-art methods (flare, DC-QUIC) on synthetic and real datasets.
- Novelty: novel combination of column-block strategy with inexact ADMM and focus on optimized matrix kernels for CLIME.
- Theoretical grounding: convergence proofs lend statistical and algorithmic credibility.
- Weakness — hyperparameter guidance: insufficient guidance on choosing column block size k and its dependence on problem/hardware.
- Weakness — evidence for block cyclic choice: lacks ablation comparing block cyclic distribution to simpler schemes to quantify its benefit.
- Weakness — presentation clarity: notation scattered across sections and ADMM parameters (ρ, η) defined after Algorithm 1; suggest consolidating notation.
- Weakness — communication discussion: limited treatment of communication costs and synchronization patterns despite distributed framing.
- Minor editorial issues: several typos (e.g., "tunning" -> "tuning", "summerized" -> "summarized").
