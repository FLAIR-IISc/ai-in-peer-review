- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving CLIME’s LP, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements it in shared- and distributed-memory with comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Quality: technically sound.  
- Derivation largely follows standard inexact ADMM procedures (e.g., He et al., 2013).  
- CLIME subproblems admit componentwise solutions; dominant cost is matrix-matrix multiplication, making ADMM highly parallelizable.  
- Efficient parallel matrix-matrix multiplication is well-established, supporting the implementation’s scalability.  
- Experiments need more specification, notably the achieved accuracy for each solver.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized.  
- Originality: novel and valuable evaluation of ADMM in shared- and distributed-memory settings.  
- Significance: provides a simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision estimation; scalability stems from CLIME’s structural simplicity.
