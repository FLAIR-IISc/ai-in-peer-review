The authors propose an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for the linear programs arising in the CLIME estimator and establish an O(1/T) convergence rate. They provide implementations on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures and compare performance against DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.

Quality: The paper appears technically sound.

Clarity: The manuscript is clearly written and well organized.

Originality: While the derivation of inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard, the evaluation on both shared- and distributed-memory platforms is a useful and interesting contribution.

Significance: Large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation is challenging, and a simple, robust, and distributed algorithm is valuable. The good scalability observed here seems largely attributable to the simple structure of CLIME.

Minor comments:
- The inexact ADMM derivation for CLIME follows standard approaches; see, for example: http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm
- Owing to CLIME’s structure, each subproblem can be solved componentwise, and the dominant cost is matrix–matrix multiplication. Since such multiplications parallelize well, ADMM is naturally well suited for parallelization in this setting.
- The setup of the numerical experiments could be specified in greater detail. For example, what accuracy does each solver achieve?
