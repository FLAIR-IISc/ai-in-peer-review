

This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program in the CLIME estimator, establishing an O(1/T) convergence rate. The algorithm is implemented across shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, with numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
Quality: The paper is technically sound.  
Minor comments:  
1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME follows standard approaches; see, for example: http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm  
2) Due to CLIME’s structure, each subproblem’s solution is computed componentwise, with matrix-matrix multiplication as the dominant operation. Consequently, ADMM achieves ideal parallelization for CLIME, and the well-known parallelizability of matrix-matrix multiplication supports this.  
3) The numerical experiment setup requires further specification, such as the accuracy achieved by each solver.  
Clarity: The paper is well-organized.  
Originality: The evaluation of ADMM in both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is noteworthy.  
Significance: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation is challenging; a simple yet robust distributed algorithm is valuable. While the inexact ADMM derivation is standard, the good scalability stems from CLIME’s simple structure.
