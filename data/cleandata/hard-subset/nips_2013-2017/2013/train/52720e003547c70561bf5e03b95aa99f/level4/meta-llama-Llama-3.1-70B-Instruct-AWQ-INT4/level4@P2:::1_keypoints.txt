- Paper tackles scalable sparse precision matrix estimation via a CLIME-ADMM algorithm and a distributed computation framework.  
- Presents empirical comparisons of CLIME-ADMM against DC-QUIC, TIGER, and Flare showing scalability.  
- Builds on prior work on CLIME and sparse precision estimation, addressing lack of scalability in existing CLIME solvers.  
- Algorithm and distributed framework are clearly explained and the paper is well-written.  
- Empirical results are well-presented and demonstrate the proposed method's scalability.  
- Notation is scattered and could be improved; a notation table is suggested (e.g., ρ and η are undefined).  
- Block cyclic data distribution scheme is under-explained; unclear why it achieves load balance and no empirical validation is provided.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad-hoc with no theoretical guidance or intuition, which may limit practicality.  
- The method may be more of a parallel implementation than a classical distributed algorithm because messaging/communication details are not discussed.  
- Minor typos: replace "tunning" with "tuning" (Section 1, Section 5.1) and "summerized" with "summarized" (Section 2).  
- Pro: Novel, scalable algorithm for CLIME with strong empirical performance against state-of-the-art.  
- Pro: Clear exposition of algorithm and distributed framework.  
- Con: Notation and presentation need improvement for readability and completeness.  
- Con: Insufficient justification and empirical support for the block cyclic distribution choice.  
- Con: Lack of theory or guidance for selecting column block size k.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to address notation, distribution scheme explanation/validation, and justification for block sizing.
