

The authors address sparse precision matrix estimation via CLIME, introducing a scalable variant (CLIME-ADMM) and a distributed computational framework. Empirical comparisons with state-of-the-art methods (DC-QUIC, Tiger, Flare) are provided.  

Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3, hindering readability. A centralized notation table would improve clarity; for instance, $\rho$ and $\eta$ are not defined in the text.  

The block cyclic data distribution scheme is intriguing but lacks justification for achieving load balance and scalability. No empirical results are provided to substantiate this claim.  

The algorithm sets the column block size ad hoc. Is there theoretical guidance or intuition for choosing $k$?  

A fundamental concern is that the work does not constitute a truly distributed algorithm, as no explicit communication protocol or message-passing mechanism is discussed. The approach appears better characterized as a parallel implementation rather than a distributed one.  

Minor corrections:  
- Section 1: "tunning" → "tuning"  
- Section 5.1: "parameter tunning free" → "tuning free"  
- Section 2: "summerized" → "summarized"  

The core novelty lies in estimating the precision matrix by column blocks (rather than column-by-column), supported by large-scale parallel experiments using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.
