- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME linear program, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements it on shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Paper is technically sound.  
- Applying inexact ADMM to CLIME is a standard derivation (prior work reference noted).  
- CLIME’s separable structure means ADMM subproblems can be solved component-wise; matrix-matrix multiplication dominates cost and is highly parallelizable.  
- Experimental setup lacks detail (e.g., convergence tolerances and final accuracies for each solver).  
- Paper is well-organized and clear.  
- Empirical evaluation of ADMM on shared- and distributed-memory architectures is an interesting contribution.  
- Main significance is a simple, robust, scalable distributed algorithm for large sparse precision matrix estimation, though strong scalability is expected given CLIME’s decomposability.
