- Proposes a scalable inexact ADMM algorithm (CLIME-ADMM) for sparse precision matrix estimation using a novel column-block strategy.  
- Column-block approach converts core work to optimized matrix-matrix multiplications, enabling heavy optimization and parallelization.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates (O(1/T) for objective and optimality residuals).  
- Presents parallel framework for shared- and distributed-memory systems using block cyclic data distribution for load balancing.  
- Empirically demonstrates major speedups over flare, Tiger, and DC-QUIC and near-linear scaling to 400 cores; solves a 1M-dimension (1T-parameter) problem in ≈11 hours.  
- Significant and impactful: extends precision-matrix estimation to previously intractable scales relevant to real-world big-data domains.  
- Algorithmic/engineering novelty: effective combination of inexact ADMM with column-block processing to leverage BLAS-3/ScaLAPACK.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation with comprehensive baselines and impressive scalability results.  
- Theoretical soundness: convergence guarantees (Theorems 1 and 2) support the optimization method.  
- Presentation clarity needs improvement: notation scattered and key parameters (ρ, η) not centrally defined.  
- Algorithm description could use more concrete examples/diagrams, especially for block cyclic distribution and its effects on W = A'X and U = AW.  
- Lacks practical guidance for selecting column block size k; performance sensitive to k and hardware specifics.  
- Design choice (block cyclic vs. naive distribution) is not empirically justified; an ablation study is missing.  
- Terminology: should emphasize massively parallel nature rather than framing primarily as “distributed,” since classic distributed concerns (asynchronicity, faults) are not addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant problem, offers a novel and effective method, strong empirical results, and solid theoretical analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues and insufficient practical guidance on key hyperparameter k.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strengths substantially outweigh fixable presentation weaknesses.
