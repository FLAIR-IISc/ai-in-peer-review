- Paper proposes a scalable CLIME estimator implementation using a specialized inexact ADMM tailored for large-scale distributed environments.
- Key design choices: process the precision matrix in column-blocks, use block-cyclic data distribution, and leverage matrix-matrix multiplications for efficiency.
- Theoretical guarantee: the algorithm achieves an O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Strength — Significance & scalability: demonstrates estimation at ~1M dimensions / 1T parameters with near-linear speedup on hundreds of cores.
- Strength — Algorithm design: column-block strategy recasts computation to exploit highly optimized matrix-matrix operations (e.g., ScaLAPACK).
- Strength — Technical soundness: formal convergence analysis (Theorems 1 and 2) supports the inexact ADMM variant.
- Strength — Empirical evaluation: comprehensive comparisons on synthetic and real datasets across shared- and distributed-memory architectures.
- Weakness — Parameter guidance: no practical heuristics or theory for selecting critical block size k, despite its large performance impact.
- Weakness — Distribution justification: block-cyclic distribution is motivated but not empirically compared to simpler alternatives (no ablation study).
- Weakness — Communication costs: lacks discussion/analysis of communication patterns and overhead in the distributed setting.
- Weakness — Presentation clarity: notation is scattered (e.g., ρ and η defined inline); recommend consolidating notation for readability.
- For acceptance: scales sparse precision estimation to unprecedented size and uses a novel, effective column-block approach with theoretical and empirical support.
- Against acceptance: limited practical guidance on k, unvalidated claims about distribution benefits, and missing communication discussion reduce practical utility.
- Minor comments: typos — "tunning" → "tuning" and "summerized" → "summarized."
