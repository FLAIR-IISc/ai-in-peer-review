- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving CLIME’s linear programming subproblem and proves an O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Implements the algorithm on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures and compares performance to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Addresses a relevant challenge in large-scale sparse precision estimation where distributed computation is essential.
- Technical content is sound, with a clear inexact ADMM framework and rigorous, well-motivated convergence analysis.
- Experimental design is generally appropriate but lacks some important methodological details.
- Paper is well-structured and readable, with clear articulation of key contributions.
- Novelty primarily in the empirical evaluation and architecture-specific implementation/ scalability assessment; the ADMM derivation follows standard techniques.
- Significance lies in providing a simple, robust, and scalable distributed solution with strong parallel performance across architectures.
- Minor comment 1: The inexact ADMM derivation is standard; authors should contrast their CLIME-specific adaptations and any problem-specific optimizations.
- Minor comment 2: Clarify that ideal parallelism arises from CLIME’s component-wise separability, not merely from parallelizable matrix-matrix multiplication.
- Minor comment 3: Experimental setup needs termination criteria and achieved accuracy metrics (e.g., relative duality gap, residual norms) for each solver to ensure fair comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: Solid, practical contribution with minor addressable issues — recommend acceptance.
