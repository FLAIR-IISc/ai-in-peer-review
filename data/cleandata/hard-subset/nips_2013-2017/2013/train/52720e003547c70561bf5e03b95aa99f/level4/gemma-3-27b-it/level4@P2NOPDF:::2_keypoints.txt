- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving the CLIME linear program, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements it for shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Empirically compares the proposed method against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Technically sound; derivation of inexact ADMM follows standard approaches and existing literature.  
- Correctly emphasizes strong parallelization potential due to componentwise subproblems and dominant matrix–matrix multiplications.  
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow.  
- Original contribution lies in evaluating ADMM’s performance in both shared- and distributed-memory environments for CLIME.  
- Significant for large-scale sparse precision estimation: offers a simple, scalable distributed algorithm leveraging CLIME structure.  
- Minor suggestion: report achieved accuracies (e.g., relative error/tolerance) for each solver in experiments to better compare trade-offs.
