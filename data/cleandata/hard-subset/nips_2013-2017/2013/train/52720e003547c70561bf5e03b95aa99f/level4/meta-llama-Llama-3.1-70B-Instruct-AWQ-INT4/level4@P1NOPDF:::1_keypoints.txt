- Proposes a scalable CLIME variant (CLIME-ADMM) and a distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation, with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Recommend consolidating notation into a single table; variables ρ and η are not defined in the text.  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is introduced but it’s unclear why it yields load balance and scalability and no empirical evidence is provided.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; authors should provide intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.  
- Concern that the method may not be truly distributed because message passing/communication mechanisms are not discussed; may be better described as a parallel implementation.  
- Minor typos to fix: "tunning" → "tuning" (Sections 1 and 5.1) and "summerized" → "summarized" (Section 2).  
- Novel contribution: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks rather than column-by-column.  
- Empirical analysis implemented using OpenMPI and Scalapak.
