- Presents CLIME-ADMM, a scalable CLIME variant and a distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation, with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; consolidate into a notation table and explicitly define parameters (e.g., ρ and η).
- The proposed block-cyclic data distribution is interesting but lacks theoretical justification for load balance and scalability.
- No empirical evidence specifically demonstrates the effectiveness of the block-cyclic distribution; add experiments to support claims.
- Choice of column block size k appears ad-hoc; provide intuition, theoretical guidance, and a discussion of trade-offs for selecting k.
- The method may be a parallel implementation rather than a truly distributed algorithm—clarify whether/how message passing and communication are handled and discuss scalability implications.
- Empirical analysis uses OpenMPI and Scalapak, but additional experiments addressing block scheme and k selection are needed.
- Main novelty claimed: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks rather than column-by-column.
- Minor corrections: fix typos/wording in Sections 1, 2, and 5.1 (e.g., "tuning", "summarized").
- Addressing these concerns would substantially improve the paper’s clarity and impact.
