- Proposes a scalable CLIME variant (CLIME-ADMM) and a distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation; empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3, reducing readability; a consolidated notation table is recommended.  
- Symbols ρ and η appear without prior definition.  
- Block cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea, but the rationale for load balance and scalability is unclear.  
- No empirical results are provided to demonstrate load balance or scalability of the block cyclic distribution.  
- Column block size k is chosen in an ad hoc manner with no theoretical justification or guiding intuition.  
- Questionable whether the method is truly “distributed” since message passing/communication mechanisms are not described; might be better characterized as a parallel implementation.  
- Empirical analysis uses OpenMPI and Scalapak; novelty highlighted as estimating by column blocks instead of column-by-column.  
- Minor language/typo corrections needed: “tunning” → “tuning”, “summerized” → “summarized”, and phrasing about “parameter tuning free.”
