The authors address the challenge of sparse precision matrix estimation, building upon the CLIME method and introducing a scalable variant, CLIME-ADMM, alongside a distributed computational framework. The paper includes empirical comparisons of CLIME-ADMM against established techniques like DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.

However, the notation used throughout Sections 1, 2, and 3 is somewhat dispersed. Consolidating these notations into a dedicated table would significantly improve readability. Specifically, the parameters ρ and η are not explicitly defined within the text. 

The proposed block cyclic data distribution is an interesting approach, but the rationale behind its expected load balance and scalability benefits is not immediately clear. Supporting empirical evidence demonstrating these advantages would strengthen the work. 

Furthermore, the current method for setting the column block size (k) appears to be determined heuristically. Providing theoretical justification or intuition for this choice would be valuable.

A broader point to consider is whether the presented approach qualifies as a truly ‘distributed’ algorithm, as the paper lacks discussion of explicit message passing or communication mechanisms. Characterizing it as a parallel implementation might be more accurate.
