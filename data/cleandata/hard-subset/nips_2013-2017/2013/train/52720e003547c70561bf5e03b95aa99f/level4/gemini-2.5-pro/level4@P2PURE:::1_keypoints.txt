- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant of CLIME that estimates sparse precision matrices in column blocks (novel vs. column-by-column methods).  
- Implements a parallel/distributed framework (OpenMPI + ScaLAPACK) and compares empirically to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is scattered and unclear across Sections 1–3; a consolidated symbol table is recommended; parameters ρ and η lack formal definition.  
- The block-cyclic data distribution is introduced without theoretical or empirical justification for its load balancing and scalability benefits.  
- Column block size k appears chosen ad hoc; the paper needs intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.  
- Characterizing the algorithm as "distributed" is questionable since inter-node message passing/communication details are not discussed—"parallel" may be more accurate.  
- Minor copyediting issues: "tunning" → "tuning", "summerized" → "summarized", and other similar typos in Sections 1, 2, and 5.1.
