- Introduces CLIME-ADMM and a distributed computational framework for sparse precision matrix estimation; provides empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; add a centralized notation table—parameters ρ and η are not defined.  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is proposed but not justified; no empirical evidence shown for load balance or scalability.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; paper lacks theoretical guidance or intuition for selecting k.  
- Method appears to be a parallel implementation rather than a truly distributed algorithm—no explicit communication protocol or message-passing mechanism is described.  
- Minor typos to correct: "tunning" → "tuning" (Sec.1), "parameter tunning free" → "tuning free" (Sec.5.1), "summerized" → "summarized" (Sec.2).  
- Core novelty: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks (instead of column-by-column), demonstrated via large-scale parallel experiments using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.
