- Proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME to solve distributed sparse precision estimation with a proven O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Implements the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures and demonstrates scalability on large datasets.
- Provides a thorough and technically sound convergence analysis.
- Presents numerical comparisons with DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Notes that the derivation of inexact ADMM is standard and available in prior work.
- Observes that CLIME’s structure allows componentwise subproblem solutions, making matrix-matrix multiplication the dominant cost.
- Points out that ADMM is naturally parallelizable for CLIME since matrix-matrix multiplications parallelize well.
- Requests more detailed numerical experiment setup, specifically the accuracy achieved by each solver.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains the problem, estimator, algorithm, and implementations.
- Evaluating ADMM across different architectures is an interesting and novel contribution.
- Scalability may largely stem from CLIME’s simple structure, limiting generalizability to other problems.
- Overall a useful contribution to large-scale machine learning, though some novelty and experimental clarity concerns remain.
