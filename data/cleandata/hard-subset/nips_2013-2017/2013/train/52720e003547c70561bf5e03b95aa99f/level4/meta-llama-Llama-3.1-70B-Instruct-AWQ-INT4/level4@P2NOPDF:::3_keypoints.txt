- Paper addresses covariance selection at extreme scale (millions of features, trillions of parameters).  
- Focuses on the CLIME estimator because it is column-wise solvable and amenable to distributed computation.  
- Claims CLIME has simpler theoretical analysis than Graphical Lasso.  
- Proposes a novel block-wise algorithm for CLIME coupled with an inexact ADMM-style method.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for the algorithm (objective value and distance to optimality).  
- Exploits matrix structure (sparsity and low-rank) to reduce computational burden.  
- Implements a scalable parallel framework for both shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Numerical experiments compare runtime to competitors and study speedup vs. block size and core count.  
- Paper is well-written, balances numerical, algebraic, and theoretical presentation, with sound references.  
- Implementation demonstrates impressive performance in experiments.  
- Main concern: lack of publicly available source code would limit the paper’s practical impact; publication should require accessible implementation.
