- Addresses very large-scale covariance selection (millions of features, trillions of parameters) using the CLIME estimator.  
- Observes CLIME's column-wise nature makes it amenable to distributed computation.  
- Proposes a new block-wise algorithm for CLIME coupled with an inexact direction method of multipliers.  
- Provides theoretical convergence-rate results for objective value and distance to optimality.  
- Exploits structural properties (sparsity and low rank) to reduce computational burden.  
- Implements a scalable parallel framework for both shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Empirical evaluation compares runtime against competitors on synthetic and real data and studies speedup vs. block size and core count.  
- Paper is well written, with clear novelty, solid connections to prior work, and balanced numerical/ theoretical treatment.  
- Main concern: method/source code availability to the community; recommends publication conditional on releasing code.
