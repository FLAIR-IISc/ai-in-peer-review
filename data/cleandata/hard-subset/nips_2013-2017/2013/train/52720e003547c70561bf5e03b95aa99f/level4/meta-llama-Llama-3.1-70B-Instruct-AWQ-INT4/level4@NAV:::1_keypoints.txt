- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant of CLIME, with a distributed computational framework for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Empirical results compare CLIME-ADMM against state-of-the-art methods (DC-QUIC, Tiger, Flare).  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; recommend consolidating into a single table for readability.  
- Key symbols (e.g., ρ and η) are not explicitly defined in the text.  
- Block cyclic data distribution is interesting but the rationale for load balance and scalability is unclear and unsupported by experiments.  
- Choice of column block size k appears ad hoc; theoretical justification or guiding intuition is needed.  
- The method lacks discussion of message passing/communication, so it may be a parallel implementation rather than truly distributed.  
- Minor typos to fix: “tunning” → “tuning”, “parameter tunning free” → “parameter tuning free”, “summerized” → “summarized”.  
- Novelty highlighted: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks instead of column-by-column.  
- Empirical implementation details include use of OpenMPI and Scalapak.
