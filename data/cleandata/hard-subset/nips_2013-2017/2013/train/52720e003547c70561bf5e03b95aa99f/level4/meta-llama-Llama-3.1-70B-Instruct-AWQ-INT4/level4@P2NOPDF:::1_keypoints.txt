- Presents CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant of CLIME, with a distributed/parallel framework and empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.
- Recommendation to consolidate notation into a single table (rho and eta are not defined and notation is scattered across Sections 1–3).
- Block-cyclic data distribution is interesting but its load balance and scalability claims are unclear and lack empirical validation.
- Need stronger explanation and experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the block-cyclic distribution scheme.
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; provide intuition or theoretical guidance on selecting k since it significantly affects performance.
- Major concern: the method may be a parallel implementation rather than a truly distributed algorithm (no explicit message-passing/communication mechanisms described).
- Minor typos to fix: replace "tunning" with "tuning" in Section 1 and 5.1, and "summerized" with "summarized" in Section 2.
- Positive note: novelty in estimating the precision matrix by column blocks instead of column-by-column, and valuable empirical analysis using OpenMPI and Scalapak.
