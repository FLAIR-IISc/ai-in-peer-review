## Review - Summary of the paper

This work addresses covariance selection in high-dimensional settings, scaling to datasets with millions of features and trillions of parameters. The authors focus on the CLIME estimator, known for its column-wise solution approach that facilitates distributed computation and offers potentially simpler theoretical analysis compared to Graphical Lasso. They present a novel algorithm for fitting CLIME by employing a block-wise solution strategy coupled with an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers. The paper includes theoretical results establishing the convergence rate of this algorithm, quantified in terms of both objective function value and distance to optimality. The authors leverage special matrix structures – sparsity and low rank – to minimize computational cost.  A scalable parallel implementation, compatible with both shared and distributed memory systems, is presented.  The algorithm's performance is evaluated through extensive numerical studies comparing runtime against competing methods on both synthetic and real datasets. A detailed performance analysis assesses the impact of block size and core number on speedup in shared and distributed memory environments.

## Comments

This is a well-written and exceptionally clear manuscript. The novelty and contributions of the proposed method are clearly articulated in the introduction, and the connection to existing literature is thoroughly established with strong bibliographic support. The algorithm is presented effectively, striking a good balance between technical details of numerical analysis/algebra and rigorous theoretical convergence guarantees. The numerical evaluation is appropriate, and the reported implementation performance is remarkably strong. 

My primary reservation concerns the accessibility of this method.  Without public release of the source code – largely based on open-source libraries – the practical impact of this work is limited. 

Overall, this is a strong algorithmic paper tackling the significant practical challenge of covariance selection in very high dimensions.  Publication is strongly recommended, contingent upon making the code available to the broader research community.
