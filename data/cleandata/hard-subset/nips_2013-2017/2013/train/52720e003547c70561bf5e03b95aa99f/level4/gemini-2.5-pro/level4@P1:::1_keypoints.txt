- Summary: Proposes CLIME-ADMM, an inexact ADMM that processes column-blocks to turn CLIME into matrix-matrix multiplications, proves O(1/T) convergence, uses block-cyclic distribution for shared/distributed architectures, and demonstrates scalability up to a 1M-dimensional precision matrix on 400 cores with comparisons to baselines.

- Strength: Significant and scalable — enables sparse precision estimation at million-scale with near-linear speedup on hundreds of cores.

- Strength: Algorithmic novelty — column-block reformulation leverages optimized parallel matrix multiplication for high computational efficiency.

- Strength: Technical quality — formal convergence guarantees and thorough experimental comparisons across baselines, architectures, and datasets.

- Weakness: Insufficient justification for block-cyclic distribution — lacks intuition or experiments comparing it to simpler distributions for load balancing.

- Weakness: Hyperparameter guidance missing — no theoretical or practical heuristics for choosing column-block size k, which strongly affects performance.

- Weakness: Ambiguous "distributed" claim — needs clearer description of communication/synchronization overhead and whether the approach is truly distributed vs. massively parallel.

- Weakness: Presentation issues — notation scattered across sections and some algorithm parameters (ρ, η) not defined in the main text.

- Minor: Typos — "tunning" → "tuning", "summerized" → "summarized", "tunning" → "tuning".
