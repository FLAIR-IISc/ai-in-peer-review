This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program underlying the CLIME estimator. The authors establish a convergence rate of O(1/T) and demonstrate implementation on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons are provided with other methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound.

**Minor comments:**

1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME is standard and builds upon well-established techniques; see, for example, http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm.
2) Given the structure of CLIME, each subproblem can be solved componentwise, with matrix-matrix multiplication representing the computational bottleneck. Consequently, ADMM is ideally suited for parallelization in this context, leveraging the well-known parallelizability of matrix-matrix multiplications.
3) The setup of the numerical experiments could benefit from further specification. In particular, reporting the achieved accuracy of each solver would be valuable.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and easy to follow.

**Originality:** The evaluation of ADMM’s performance across shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is a noteworthy contribution.

**Significance:** Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems remains a challenging task. A simple, robust, and distributed algorithm is therefore highly desirable. While the derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard, the achieved scalability is a direct result of CLIME’s inherent structure, making this work relevant and potentially useful to practitioners.
