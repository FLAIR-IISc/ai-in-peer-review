- Presents CLIME-ADMM, an inexact ADMM algorithm for sparse precision estimation and a distributed framework scaling to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.  
- Establishes convergence rates for the objective and optimality conditions of the proposed algorithm.  
- Builds on prior CLIME and ADMM work, adding a scalable variant and exploiting sparse/low-rank structure.  
- Empirical results compare CLIME-ADMM to state-of-the-art methods and demonstrate large-scale performance.  
- Strength: develops a scalable algorithm addressing high-dimensional sparse precision estimation.  
- Strength: provides thorough theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Weakness: notation is scattered and hinders readability; a unified notation table is suggested.  
- Weakness: block cyclic data distribution scheme is under-explained and lacks empirical support for load balance claims.  
- Weakness: column block size is chosen ad hoc with no guiding intuition or theoretical justification.  
- Weakness: unclear whether the approach is truly “distributed” since message-passing/communication mechanisms are not discussed; may be more a parallel implementation.  
- Minor issues: typos (e.g., “tunning” → “tuning”, “parameter tunning free” → “parameter tuning free”).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid convergence analysis, empirical validation, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: scattered notation, unclear data distribution and block-size choices, limited discussion of distributed communication.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
