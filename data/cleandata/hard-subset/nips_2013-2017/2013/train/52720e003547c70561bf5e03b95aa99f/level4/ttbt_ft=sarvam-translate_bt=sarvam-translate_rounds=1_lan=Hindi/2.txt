The author proposed an inaccurate alternating direction method (ADMM) for solving linear programming problems in CLIME estimator. A convergence rate of O(1/T) is established. The algorithm is implemented using both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons are provided with other methods such as DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound.

**Minor Comments:**

1) The derivation of the inaccurate ADMM for CLIME is standard. See for example:
http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm
2) Due to the structures of CLIME, the solution of each subproblem can be computed component-wise, and the most expensive operation is matrix-matrix multiplication. Therefore, ADMM can be ideally parallelized for CLIME. It is well known that matrix-matrix multiplication can be well-parallelized.
3) The establishment of numerical experiments can be further specified. For example, what is the accuracy achieved by each solver?

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized.

**Originality:** The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.

**Importance:** Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems is challenging. A simple but robust distributed algorithm is helpful. The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting. The derivation of the inaccurate ADMM is standard, and good scalability is due to the simple structure of CLIME.
