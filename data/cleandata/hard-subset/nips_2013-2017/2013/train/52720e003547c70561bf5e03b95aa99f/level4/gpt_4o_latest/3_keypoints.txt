- Tackles covariance selection at extreme scale (millions of features, trillions of parameters).  
- Focuses on the CLIME estimator for its column-wise solvability and simpler theoretical analysis vs Graphical Lasso.  
- Proposes a novel block-wise algorithm combined with an inexact direction method of multipliers.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both objective value and distance to optimality.  
- Exploits matrix structure (sparsity, low-rank) to reduce computational complexity.  
- Implements the method in a scalable parallel framework supporting shared and distributed memory.  
- Presents thorough numerical experiments comparing runtime with competitors on synthetic and real data.  
- Analyzes speedup effects of varying block sizes and core counts in both memory environments.  
- Writing and exposition are clear, with strong connections to prior work and good references.  
- Main concern: practical impact depends on publicly releasing the source code (largely based on open-source libraries).  
- Overall: robust, publishable algorithmic contribution if the implementation is made accessible to the community.
