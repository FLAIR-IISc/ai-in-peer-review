- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving the CLIME linear program and proves an O(1/T) convergence rate.  
- Implements the algorithm for both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Empirically compares performance with DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-organized.  
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM follows a standard approach (not novel).  
- CLIME subproblems decompose componentwise; matrix-matrix multiplication dominates cost.  
- Structure makes the method naturally amenable to parallelization within ADMM.  
- Numerical experiments need more detail on solver accuracy.  
- Evaluation of ADMM across architectures is a useful contribution.  
- A simple, robust distributed algorithm that scales well is significant for large-scale sparse precision estimation.
