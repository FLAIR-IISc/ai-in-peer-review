- Presents a distributed framework that scales CLIME precision-matrix estimation to million-dimensional problems.
- Proposes an inexact ADMM variant that solves CLIME in column-blocks with a block-cyclic distribution for parallelism.
- Implements both shared-memory and distributed-memory versions with cache-efficient, load-balanced block-cyclic packing.
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for the objective and optimality conditions.
- Demonstrates nearly linear scalability with number of cores in experiments.
- Builds on CLIME (statistical guarantees) and ADMM (optimization framework); positions vs prior work like DC-QUIC and Tiger that lack ultra-high scalability.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing to state-of-the-art methods on synthetic and real data.
- Empirical highlight: estimates a 1M-dimensional precision matrix (~1 trillion parameters) in ~11 hours on 400 cores.
- Technical development and convergence analysis are rigorous and correct.
- The inexact ADMM derivation follows standard procedures; theoretical contributions are modest extensions of known results.
- Main originality is practical: the column-block + block-cyclic distribution enabling massive-scale efficiency.
- Clarity and organization are strong; notation and reproducibility details are good.
- Experimental setup needs clearer reporting of final solver accuracy and convergence criteria for large-scale runs.
- Overall recommendation: strong practical contribution and valuable to practitioners; recommend acceptance.
