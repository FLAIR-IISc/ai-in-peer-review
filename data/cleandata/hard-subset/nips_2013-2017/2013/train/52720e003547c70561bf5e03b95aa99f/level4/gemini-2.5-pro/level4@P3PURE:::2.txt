This paper proposes an inexact Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear programming problem associated with the CLIME estimator. The authors establish a convergence rate of O(1/T) and implement the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons with other methods, such as DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare, are also provided.

The paper is technically sound and well-organized. It addresses the significant challenge of large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation, and the empirical evaluation of ADMM on different parallel architectures is interesting and potentially useful. A simple and robust distributed algorithm for this problem would be a valuable contribution to the field.

However, the novelty and overall significance of the work are somewhat limited. The core technical contribution, the derivation of an inexact ADMM for the CLIME problem, is standard. Furthermore, the impressive scalability demonstrated is largely an expected consequence of the problem's structure. Due to the formulation of CLIME, the ADMM subproblems can be computed component-wise, making matrix-matrix multiplication the most expensive operation. It is well-established that matrix-matrix multiplication is highly amenable to parallelization. The authors should clarify that the excellent scalability is primarily a feature of the problem structure rather than a novel property of their specific algorithmic application.

Minor Comments:
1.  The derivation of the inexact ADMM for this class of problems is standard. Similar approaches can be found in existing literature (e.g., http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm).
2.  The setup for the numerical experiments should be specified in more detail. For instance, to allow for a fair comparison, the paper must report the stopping criteria or the final accuracy level achieved by each solver.
