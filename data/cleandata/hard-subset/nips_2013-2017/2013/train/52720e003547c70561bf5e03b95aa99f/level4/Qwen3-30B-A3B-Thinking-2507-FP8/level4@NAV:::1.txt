

The authors address sparse precision matrix estimation via CLIME, introducing a scalable variant CLIME-ADMM alongside a distributed computational framework. Empirical comparisons between CLIME-ADMM and state-of-the-art methods (DC-QUIC, Tiger, Flare) are provided.  
Notation is dispersed throughout Sections 1, 2, and 3; consolidating all symbols in a dedicated table would significantly enhance readability. For instance, parameters \(\rho\) and \(\eta\) remain undefined in the text.  
The block cyclic data distribution scheme is intriguing but lacks justification for its claimed load balancing and scalability. No empirical evidence supports this assertion.  
The column block size \(k\) appears to be selected ad hoc; a theoretical or empirical basis for its choice should be provided.  
A fundamental concern is that the algorithm is not truly "distributed," as it omits explicit communication protocols and message-passing mechanisms. This should be reclassified as a parallel implementation.  
Minor corrections:  
- Section 1: "tunning" → "tuning"  
- Section 5.1: "parameter tunning free" → "tuning free"  
- Section 2: "summerized" → "summarized"  
The work presents a large-scale parallel algorithm for sparse precision matrix estimation using column-block CLIME, with novelty in column-block (vs. column-by-column) estimation. Empirical validation via OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK is included.
