- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the linear program from the CLIME estimator, proves O(1/T) convergence, and implements it on shared- and distributed-memory architectures with comparisons to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.

- Paper is technically sound.

- Derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard (reference provided).

- Due to CLIME’s structure, subproblems decompose componentwise and the dominant cost is matrix–matrix multiplication, making ADMM well-suited to parallelization.

- Matrix–matrix multiplications are well known to parallelize efficiently, explaining observed scalability.

- Numerical experiment setup needs more detail (e.g., solver accuracies achieved).

- Paper is well organized and clear.

- Evaluation on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is an interesting contribution.

- Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation is important; the simple, robust distributed algorithm is valuable, but the strong scalability is largely attributable to CLIME’s simple structure rather than a novel ADMM derivation.
