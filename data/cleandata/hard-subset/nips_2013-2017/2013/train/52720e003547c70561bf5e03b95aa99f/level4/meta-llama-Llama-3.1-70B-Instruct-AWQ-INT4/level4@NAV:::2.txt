This paper presents an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program problem in the CLIME estimator, with a demonstrated convergence rate of O(1/T). The authors implement the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures and provide numerical comparisons with existing methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

Quality: The technical soundness of the paper is evident.

Minor comments:
1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME follows established procedures, as seen in prior work (e.g., http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm).
2) The inherent structure of CLIME allows for component-wise computation of subproblems, with matrix-matrix multiplication being the most computationally intensive operation. This, in turn, enables ideal parallelization of ADMM for CLIME, leveraging the well-known parallelization efficiency of matrix-matrix multiplications.
3) Further details on the numerical experiment setup would be beneficial, such as specifying the accuracy achieved by each solver.

Clarity: The paper's organization is clear and logical.

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is a notable contribution.

Significance: Addressing large-scale sparse precision estimation problems is a challenging task, and a simple yet robust distributed algorithm can be highly valuable. The assessment of ADMM on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting, although the derivation of inexact ADMM is a standard procedure, and the good scalability is largely due to the straightforward structure of CLIME.
