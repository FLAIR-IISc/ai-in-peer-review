- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME linear program with an O(1/T) convergence rate and implementations on shared- and distributed-memory systems.  
- Provides numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Quality: technically sound.  
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM follows standard procedures (e.g., He et al., 2010).  
- CLIME’s structure makes each subproblem solvable componentwise, with matrix–matrix multiplication as the dominant, highly parallelizable operation.  
- Experimental setup needs clearer specification, especially the convergence accuracy achieved by each solver.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized.  
- Originality: novel evaluation of ADMM across shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Significance: while the algorithmic derivation is standard, the demonstrated scalability for large-scale sparse precision estimation is a meaningful practical contribution.
