- Summary: Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a column-block inexact ADMM for sparse precision estimation that uses elementwise ops and matrix multiplies, proves O(1/T) rates, leverages low-rank/sparsity, and demonstrates large-scale shared/distributed scaling (including a million-dim run on 400 cores).

- Strength: Tackles an important scaling problem for sparse inverse covariance estimation with both theory and systems work.

- Strength: Clear computational design—column-block ADMM enables batching, efficient matrix–matrix multiplies, and better use of memory hierarchies.

- Strength: Theoretical guarantees—O(1/T) convergence for objective and feasibility residuals under stated conditions.

- Strength: Practical contributions—distributed implementation (OpenMP/OpenMPI/ScaLAPACK), low-rank multiplications for p ≫ n, and empirically strong shared/distributed results.

- Strength: Empirical comparisons show strong scalability relative to state-of-the-art (DC-QUIC, TIGER, flare).

- Weakness: Notation and symbols are scattered and under-defined (e.g., rho, eta); recommend a consolidated notation table.

- Weakness: Block-cyclic distribution rationale is plausible but under-supported; needs clearer explanation and empirical ablation against contiguous layouts with load/cache/communication metrics.

- Weakness: Choice of column-block size k appears ad hoc; request rules-of-thumb, sensitivity analysis, and guidance tied to process grid, cache, p/n, and sparsity.

- Weakness: Distributed algorithm details are under-specified—must state data/variable partitioning, message-passing primitives, per-iteration communication volume, sync points, and overlap strategies.

- Weakness: Missing an iteration-level complexity model including flops and bandwidth/latency scaling with p, n, k, and process grid.

- Weakness: Evaluation omissions—report stopping criteria, choices of rho and eta and their effects, per-node memory footprints, and quantified speedups from low-rank multiplications.

- Quality: Algorithmic development and convergence analysis are sound and align with inexact ADMM literature; empirical scaling is strong but reproducibility/details need bolstering.

- Clarity: Generally well written, but organization of notation and implementation/communication details should be improved.

- Originality: Novel combination of column-block inexact ADMM, block-cyclic distributed design, and low-rank multiplications relative to prior CLIME/ADMM solvers.

- Significance: High—enables near-linear scaling to million-dimensional precision estimation, valuable for massive graphical-model problems.

- Pros for acceptance: Addresses a hard large-scale problem with theory and practical system; shows impressive scalability and a flexible framework.

- Cons for acceptance: Insufficient ablation/justification for block-cyclic layout, limited guidance on k/rho/eta, and under-specified distributed communication costs.

- Minor comments (typos): “tunning”→“tuning”; “parameter tunning free”→“parameter tuning free”; “summerized”→“summarized”; “Comparision”→“Comparison”; “achitectures”→“architectures”.

- Overall assessment: Strong systems-oriented contribution; would be significantly strengthened by clearer notation, explicit communication/parameter details, and targeted experiments justifying block-cyclic layout and block-size selection.
