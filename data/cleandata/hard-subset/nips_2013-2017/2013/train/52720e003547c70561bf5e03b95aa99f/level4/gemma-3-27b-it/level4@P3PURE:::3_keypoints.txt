- Tackles covariance selection at extreme scale (millions of features, trillions of parameters) using the CLIME estimator.  
- Emphasizes CLIME’s column-wise structure as computationally advantageous relative to Graphical Lasso for distributed settings.  
- Proposes a novel block-wise algorithm for CLIME solved via an inexact ADMM approach.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for the algorithm in terms of objective value and distance to optimality.  
- Exploits sparsity and low-rank matrix structures to reduce computational cost.  
- Implements the method in a scalable parallel framework supporting shared and distributed memory.  
- Presents numerical studies comparing runtime to competitors on synthetic and real data, plus analysis of block size and core-count speedups.  
- Paper is well-written, contributions are clearly articulated, and related work is thoroughly cited.  
- Primary concern: lack of public source code limits reproducibility and impact.  
- Overall assessment: strong algorithmic contribution meriting publication conditional on releasing the implementation.
