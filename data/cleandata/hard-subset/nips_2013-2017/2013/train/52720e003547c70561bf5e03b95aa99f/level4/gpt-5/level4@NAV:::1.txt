The paper addresses sparse precision matrix estimation via CLIME and introduces a scalable variant, CLIME-ADMM, together with a distributed computing framework. Empirical comparisons of the CLIME-ADMM algorithm against state-of-the-art methods, including DC-QUIC, TIGER, and Flare, are provided.

Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3. A consolidated notation table would improve readability; for example, ρ and η appear to be undefined.

The block-cyclic data distribution is interesting, but it is not clear why this scheme guarantees load balance and scalability, and there are no experiments supporting this claim.

The column-block size appears to be chosen ad hoc—please provide intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k.

A broader concern is that the work may not be truly “distributed,” as no explicit message-passing or communication mechanisms are described. It may be more accurate to characterize this as a parallel implementation.

The paper presents a large-scale parallel algorithm for estimating a sparse precision matrix using CLIME. The main novelty is estimating the precision matrix by column blocks rather than column-by-column. Empirical results using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK are reported.
