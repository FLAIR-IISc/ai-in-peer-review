- Proposes CLIME-ADMM: a scalable, distributed variant of CLIME for sparse precision matrix estimation.
- Main technical contribution: an inexact ADMM algorithm solving CLIME in column-blocks with proven convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions.
- Distributed framework uses block cyclic data distribution to achieve load balancing across compute nodes.
- Empirical comparison to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare shows CLIME-ADMM is substantially more scalable and scales nearly linearly with cores.
- Paper is well-written, with clear introduction, sound technical analysis, and thorough convergence proofs.
- Experiments are well-presented and demonstrate effectiveness of the proposed algorithm on high-dimensional problems.
- Weakness: block cyclic data distribution scheme is not clearly explained; needs more justification or supporting experiments.
- Weakness: column block size appears chosen ad hoc; intuition or theoretical guidance for this parameter is missing.
- Presentation issue: notation is scattered—adding a notation summary/table would improve clarity.
- Minor typos: "tunning" (Section 1), "parameter tunning free" (Section 5.1), and "summerized" (Section 2).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel scalable CLIME solution with strong empirical and theoretical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient clarity on block distribution, ad hoc block-size choice, and notation presentation.
