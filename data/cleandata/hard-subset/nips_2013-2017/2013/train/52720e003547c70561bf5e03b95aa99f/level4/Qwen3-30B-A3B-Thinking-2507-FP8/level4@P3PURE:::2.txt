

This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program in the CLIME estimator, establishing an O(1/T) convergence rate. The algorithm is implemented across both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, with numerical comparisons provided against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound.  
*Minor comments:*  
1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME follows standard procedures (e.g., as presented in He et al., *Journal of Optimization Theory and Applications*, 2010).  
2) Due to CLIME’s structure, each subproblem’s solution is computable componentwise, with the dominant operation being matrix-matrix multiplication. Since matrix-matrix multiplications parallelize efficiently, ADMM achieves ideal parallelization for CLIME.  
3) The experimental setup requires further specification, particularly the convergence accuracy achieved by each solver.  

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized.  
**Originality:** The evaluation of ADMM across shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures presents a novel contribution.  
**Significance:** Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation remains challenging, and a simple, robust distributed algorithm is valuable. While the derivation of inexact ADMM is standard, the demonstrated scalability—stemming from CLIME’s inherent structure—is a meaningful practical contribution.
