- Proposes a scalable CLIME-based sparse precision estimation framework using an inexact ADMM that processes column-blocks and proves an O(1/T) convergence rate.  
- Develops large-scale shared- and distributed-memory implementations that scale to problems with 10^6 dimensions and 10^12 parameters.  
- Empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and flare show superior scalability of the proposed approach.  
- Paper is technically sound: algorithm clearly described and supported by theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Numerical results are impressive and convincingly demonstrate massive-scale capability.  
- The inexact ADMM derivation is a relatively standard application and should acknowledge and cite foundational inexact/linearized ADMM work.  
- Excellent scalability is largely due to CLIME’s columnwise decomposability and the inherently parallelizable nature of matrix-matrix multiplications; the paper should discuss this and frame its contribution more as engineering/implementation.  
- Experimental setup lacks crucial details (e.g., stopping criteria, solution accuracy for each solver), making runtime comparisons potentially unfair.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a logical progression from algorithm to distributed framework to experiments.  
- Originality lies mainly in careful engineering and large-scale implementation rather than a novel optimization algorithm; the column-block evaluation on large systems is a valuable practical contribution.  
- Significance: provides a practical, highly scalable solution for ultra-high-dimensional sparse precision estimation that will benefit practitioners handling massive datasets.  
- Significance is somewhat tempered because the scalability is an expected consequence of applying a standard parallelizable algorithm to a decomposable problem, but demonstrating a robust system at this scale remains valuable.  
- Strengths: addresses an important problem, delivers an impressive distributed implementation with near-linear speedup, reaches scales beyond existing public methods, and offers extensive empirical evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: core algorithmic novelty is limited (standard ADMM), needs better contextualization of scalability sources, and omits key experimental comparison details.
