- Proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME that operates on column blocks with closed-form, parallel updates using only elementwise ops and matrix multiplies.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for both objective value and optimality residuals.  
- Leverages sparsity and low-rank structure (C = A A′ with n ≪ p) to reduce cost from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k).  
- Implements a scalable parallel framework for shared- and distributed-memory with block-cyclic data distribution for load balance and cache efficiency.  
- Empirical results show strong speedups and near-linear scaling across hundreds of cores, including solving p = 10^6 in ~11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Well positioned relative to prior work (graphical lasso, QUIC, CLIME, TIGER) and distributed ADMM/parameter-server approaches; CLIME chosen to avoid PD constraints.  
- Algorithmic development is sound: linearized inexact ADMM yields simple, fully parallelizable updates with clear complexity analysis.  
- Convergence guarantees are provided with appropriate parameter conditions for objective and feasibility residuals.  
- Implementation choices (column-blocking, low-rank multiplies, block-cyclic distribution) are practical and well motivated.  
- Empirical evaluation is appropriate, convincing, and includes relevant baselines and scalability studies.  
- Paper is clear, well organized, with crisp notation, pseudo-code, and accessible discussion of distributed implementation.  
- Original contribution: novel combination of inexact ADMM tailored to column-block CLIME, structure-exploiting matrix multiplies, and engineered distributed framework scaling to million-variable regimes.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem and demonstrates scalable, practical applicability for large scientific/industrial datasets.  
- Strengths: clear formulation, provable O(1/T) convergence, exploitation of sparsity/low-rankness, strong engineering, extensive experiments.  
- Main weakness/concern: impact is contingent on releasing source code—lack of code would limit reproducibility and adoption.  
- Recommendation: strong accept if authors make the implementation available to the community.
