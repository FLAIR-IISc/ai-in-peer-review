- Tackles covariance selection at ultra-high dimensions (millions of features, trillions of precision entries) using the CLIME estimator.  
- Highlights CLIME’s column-wise solvability, enabling distributed computation and simpler theory versus Graphical Lasso.  
- Proposes a novel block-wise algorithm combining inexact ADMM with specialized handling of sparse and low-rank structure to speed computation.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for both the objective value and the optimality gap.  
- Implements a scalable parallel framework supporting both shared- and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Numerical experiments show runtime advantages over competitors on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Includes detailed analysis of speedup versus block size and core count across architectures.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear articulation of novelty and strong literature contextualization.  
- Algorithmic exposition balances numerical/linear-algebra detail with rigorous convergence guarantees.  
- Major concern: lack of publicly available source code undermines reproducibility and limits the paper’s practical impact; open-source release is requested.
