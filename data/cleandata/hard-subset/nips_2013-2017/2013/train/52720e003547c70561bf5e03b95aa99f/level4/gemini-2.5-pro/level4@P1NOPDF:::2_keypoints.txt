- Proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence and implementations on shared- and distributed-memory architectures, compared to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Derivation of inexact ADMM for CLIME follows standard, known approaches (prior references exist).
- Strong parallel performance is largely expected because CLIME subproblems decompose component-wise and matrix-matrix multiplies (highly parallelizable) dominate cost.
- Numerical experiment details are insufficient—must report convergence tolerances/final accuracies for each solver for fair comparison.
- Paper is well-organized and clear.
- Originality is limited: main novelty is thorough empirical evaluation on parallel architectures rather than new theory or algorithmic ideas.
- Significance is practical: provides a simple, robust distributed solution and useful empirical guidance for large-scale sparse precision estimation, though theoretical novelty and surprising scalability are limited.
