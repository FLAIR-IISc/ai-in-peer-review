Summary of the paper
This paper addresses covariance selection at very large scale (up to millions of features and trillions of parameters). The estimator considered is CLIME, a well-known approach for estimating the precision matrix of a Gaussian vector that can be solved column-wise, making it more amenable to distributed computation. Its theoretical analysis is also claimed to be easier than that of the Graphical Lasso. The authors propose a new algorithm for fitting CLIME by solving the problem block-wise rather than column-wise, coupled with an inexact direction method of multipliers. They provide theoretical convergence-rate guarantees in terms of both objective value and distance to optimality. The method exploits structural properties (sparsity and low rank) during computation to reduce the computational burden. The algorithm is implemented within a scalable parallel computing framework for both shared- and distributed-memory systems. In the numerical studies, comparisons are made in terms of runtime against direct competitors on both synthetic and real datasets. A detailed study also evaluates speedup as a function of block size and number of cores on both shared-memory and distributed-memory systems.

Comments
The paper is well written and very clear. The novelty and contributions are clearly stated in the introduction, and the connections to existing work are well established with sound bibliographic references. The algorithm is presented with a good balance between numerical analysis/algebraic details and theoretical convergence guarantees. The empirical evaluation is appropriate, and the implementation demonstrates very impressive performance.

My only concern is availability: if the source code (largely based on open-source libraries) is not released to the community, there is no point in such a work.

Overall, this is a sound algorithmic paper that addresses the important practical problem of covariance selection at very large scale (millions of variables). Such a powerful tool should be published, provided it is made available to the community.
