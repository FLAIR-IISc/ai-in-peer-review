The authors address the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME and introduce a scalable variant, CLIME-ADMM, alongside a distributed computational framework. They present empirical results comparing CLIME-ADMM to state-of-the-art methods including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.

The notation is somewhat scattered throughout Sections 1, 2, and 3, and a table of notations would significantly improve readability. Specifically, symbols such as ρ and η are not explicitly defined within the text. 

The proposed block cyclic data distribution is an interesting approach; however, the rationale behind its ability to achieve load balance and scalability is not immediately clear, and this claim is not supported by empirical evidence. 

Furthermore, the choice of the column block size, *k*, appears to be made on an ad hoc basis. Providing the intuition or theoretical basis for selecting *k* would strengthen the work. 

A broader concern is whether this qualifies as a truly ‘distributed’ algorithm, as the paper does not detail any explicit message passing or communication mechanisms. Characterizing this as a parallel implementation may be more accurate.

This work presents a large-scale parallel algorithm for estimating sparse precision matrices using CLIME. The novelty lies in estimating the precision matrix by column blocks, rather than column-by-column, with empirical analysis conducted using OpenMPI and Scalapak.
