Review- The authors propose an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) to solve the linear program arising in the CLIME estimator. They establish an O(1/T) convergence rate. The algorithm is implemented on both shared-memory and distributed-memory platforms. Numerical comparisons with other methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare, are presented.

Quality: The paper is technically sound.

minor comments:
1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME is fairly standard; see, for example:
http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm
2) Owing to CLIME’s structure, each subproblem decouples componentwise, and the dominant cost is matrix–matrix multiplication. Consequently, ADMM is particularly amenable to parallelization for CLIME, and matrix–matrix multiplications are well known to parallelize efficiently.
3) The setup of the numerical experiments could be specified in more detail. For instance, what accuracy is achieved by each solver?

Clarity: The paper is well organized.

Originality: The assessment of ADMM on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.

Significance: Addressing large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation is challenging. A simple yet robust distributed algorithm is valuable. The evaluation of ADMM on shared-memory and distributed-memory systems is interesting. The derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard, and the strong scalability largely follows from CLIME’s simple structure.
