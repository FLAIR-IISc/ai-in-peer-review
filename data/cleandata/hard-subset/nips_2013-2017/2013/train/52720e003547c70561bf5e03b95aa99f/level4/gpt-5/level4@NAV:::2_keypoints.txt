- Proposes an inexact ADMM to solve the CLIME linear program and proves an O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Implements the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory platforms.
- Presents numerical comparisons with DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM is fairly standard (prior work exists).
- CLIME subproblems decouple componentwise; dominant cost is matrix–matrix multiplication.
- ADMM is particularly amenable to parallelization for CLIME due to this decoupling and efficient parallel matrix–matrix multiplication.
- Numerical-experiment setup needs more detail (e.g., reported solver accuracies).
- Paper is well organized and clear.
- Originality: evaluating ADMM on both shared- and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.
- Significance: offers a simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation.
- Caveat: the derivation is standard and the observed strong scalability largely follows from CLIME’s simple structure.
