- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm to solve the CLIME linear program for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Establishes an O(1/T) convergence rate for the proposed method.  
- Emphasizes implementation and evaluation on shared-memory and distributed-memory parallel architectures.  
- Empirically compares performance against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare on synthetic and real datasets.  
- Technically sound and addresses large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation, offering a simple, robust, distributable solver.  
- Main novelty lies in implementation and large-scale evaluation rather than core algorithmic development.  
- The inexact ADMM formulation follows standard optimization techniques and established derivation patterns.  
- Reported excellent scalability likely stems from problem structure—component-wise decomposition and matrix-matrix multiplication amenable to parallelization.  
- Authors should clarify whether any non-obvious implementation challenges or algorithmic insights were needed to achieve the reported scalability.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Original contribution is the thorough implementation and empirical analysis on modern parallel systems.  
- Suggestion: specify convergence criteria/accuracy tolerances used in numerical experiments for fair, reproducible runtime comparisons.
