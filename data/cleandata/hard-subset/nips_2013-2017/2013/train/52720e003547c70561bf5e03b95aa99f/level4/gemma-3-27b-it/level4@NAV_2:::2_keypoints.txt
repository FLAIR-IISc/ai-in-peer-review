- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving the CLIME linear program with proven O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Implements the algorithm for both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.
- Empirically compares performance against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM follows standard ADMM theory (e.g., He & Yuan 2010), so theory is incremental.
- CLIME subproblems are componentwise and dominated by matrix-matrix multiplication, making ADMM well-suited for parallelization.
- Scalability gains largely stem from CLIME’s inherent structure and efficient parallel matrix multiplication, not novel algorithmic ideas.
- Numerical experimental setup needs clearer reporting of achieved accuracy for each solver, beyond runtimes.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and presents a well-motivated method.
- Evaluating ADMM on both shared- and distributed-memory systems is a valuable contribution.
- Developing a simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision estimation is a positive outcome.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, presents a sound ADMM approach, shows good scalability, and includes empirical comparisons.
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical novelty is limited, scalability owes to problem structure, and experiments lack detailed accuracy metrics.
