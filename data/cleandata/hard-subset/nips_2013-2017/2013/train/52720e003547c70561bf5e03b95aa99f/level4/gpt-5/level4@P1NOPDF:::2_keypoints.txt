- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME LP with an O(1/T) convergence rate and implementations on shared- and distributed-memory, compared to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-FLARE.
- Paper is technically sound.
- The inexact ADMM derivation is standard (see provided reference).
- CLIME subproblems decompose component-wise; dominant cost is matrix-matrix multiplication, making ADMM a natural fit for parallelization.
- Matrix-matrix multiplication parallelizes efficiently, which explains much of the observed scalability.
- Numerical experiment setup needs more detail (e.g., what accuracy each solver achieves).
- Paper is well organized and clear.
- Evaluation of ADMM on both shared- and distributed-memory architectures is interesting/original.
- Significance: provides a simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision estimation, but novelty is limited since the derivation is standard and scalability largely follows from CLIME's structure.
