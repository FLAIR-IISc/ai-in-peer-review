- Addresses covariance selection at massive scale (millions of features, trillions of parameters) using the CLIME estimator.  
- Highlights CLIME’s column-wise solvability, suitability for distributed computation, and theoretical advantages over Graphical Lasso.  
- Proposes a novel block-wise algorithm combined with an inexact direction method of multipliers to fit CLIME.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rates for both objective value and distance to optimality.  
- Optimizes the algorithm for special matrix structures (sparse and low-rank) to reduce computation.  
- Implements the method in a scalable parallel framework supporting shared and distributed memory systems.  
- Presents numerical studies comparing runtime to competitors on synthetic and real data.  
- Analyzes speedup effects with varying block sizes and numbers of cores across memory systems.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, situates contributions in prior work, and balances technical and theoretical aspects.  
- Numerical results are thorough and demonstrate impressive performance.  
- Major concern: source code is not released, limiting community impact.  
- Recommendation: paper merits publication provided the implementation is made accessible to the community.
