- Proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME, proves O(1/T) convergence, implements on shared and distributed memory, and compares to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.
- Technically sound derivation, correctly specialized to CLIME; empirical study and system implementation are competent.
- Strength: clear problem focus with algorithm matched to CLIME’s separable structure.
- Strength: provides a theoretical O(1/T) convergence guarantee.
- Strength: practical emphasis on parallel and distributed implementations addressing scalability.
- Strength: broad empirical comparisons with established baselines.
- Weakness: algorithmic novelty is limited—the inexact ADMM derivation is standard.
- Weakness: missing experimental details (stopping criteria, accuracy targets) reduce reproducibility and interpretability.
- Weakness: unclear whether the O(1/T) rate is ergodic or non-ergodic and which residuals/metrics it refers to.
- Weakness: parallelization novelty is not well articulated beyond leveraging BLAS-friendly kernels and data partitioning.
- Clarity: paper is well organized and generally easy to follow, but several experimental details should be specified.
- Originality: contribution is primarily empirical and systems-oriented; theoretical derivation follows prior inexact ADMM literature.
- Significance: important application area; a simple, robust, and scalable method is valuable and practitioners benefit from demonstrated scalability.
- Suggestion: explicitly state whether the O(1/T) rate is ergodic or non-ergodic and the exact optimality metrics used.
- Suggestion: detail how inexactness is controlled and whether penalty/relaxation parameters are adapted.
- Suggestion: report stopping tolerances and accuracy criteria for all solvers and ensure they are matched across methods.
- Suggestion: provide accuracy vs. time and accuracy vs. iterations to separate algorithmic from implementation speed.
- Suggestion: fully describe data generation, problem dimensions, sparsity, condition numbers, regularization parameters, and preprocessing.
- Suggestion: specify hardware/software details (threads, BLAS/LAPACK/MKL versions, compiler flags, network topology/bandwidth) and competitor configurations.
- Suggestion: include strong/weak scaling plots, breakdown computation vs. communication, and report memory usage and communication volume.
- Suggestion: describe exploitation of columnwise separability, block partitioning, load balancing, expensive kernels, reuse/precomputation, numerical stability, and penalty sensitivity.
- Suggestion: ensure fairness of baselines by reporting hyperparameter choices, warm starts, screening rules, and enabling multi-core/node use where applicable.
- Minor: cite prior inexact ADMM references, emphasize matrix–matrix multiplication cost, and specify achieved accuracy and stopping conditions.
- Overall: solid, well-executed systems paper with credible guarantees and useful evaluation; primary novelty is implementation/evaluation—leaning toward acceptance pending clarifications.
