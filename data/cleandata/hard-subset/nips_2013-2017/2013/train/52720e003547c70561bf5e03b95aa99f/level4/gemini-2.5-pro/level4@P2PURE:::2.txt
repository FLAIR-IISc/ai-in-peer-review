This paper proposes an inexact Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear programming problem arising from the CLIME estimator. The authors establish a convergence rate of O(1/T) and implement the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. The proposed method is numerically compared with other solvers, including DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-FLARE.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound.

**Minor Comments:**
1.  The application of inexact ADMM to the CLIME formulation is a standard derivation. See, for example:
    http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm
2.  Due to the structure of the CLIME problem, the ADMM subproblems can be solved component-wise, making matrix-matrix multiplication the most computationally expensive operation. It is well-established that matrix-matrix multiplication is highly parallelizable, which explains why ADMM is ideally suited for this application.
3.  The experimental setup should be specified in more detail. For example, what was the convergence tolerance or final accuracy achieved by each solver?

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized.

**Originality:** The empirical evaluation of ADMM on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.

**Significance:** Solving large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation problems is challenging, and a simple yet robust distributed algorithm is valuable. The paper's primary contribution is the evaluation of the ADMM algorithm on modern parallel architectures. However, it is important to note that the derivation of the inexact ADMM for this problem is standard, and the strong scalability observed is an expected consequence of the decomposable structure of the CLIME formulation.
