This paper presents an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) tailored for solving the linear program inherent in the CLIME estimator, accompanied by a convergence rate analysis demonstrating O(1/T) convergence. The proposed algorithm is implemented utilizing both shared-memory and distributed-memory parallel computing architectures, and its performance is benchmarked against existing methods including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.

The work is technically solid. A few minor points could be addressed: the derivation of the inexact ADMM approach for CLIME follows established methodologies (e.g., as outlined in http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm), and the inherent structure of the CLIME problem allows for efficient componentwise solution of subproblems with matrix-matrix multiplication representing the dominant computational cost – a cost readily amenable to parallelization. Further detail regarding the accuracy achieved by each solver in the numerical experiments would strengthen the presentation.

The paper is clearly organized and presents an interesting evaluation of ADMM across different memory architectures.  The application of ADMM to large-scale sparse precision estimation, a known challenging problem, and the resulting scalable algorithm are significant contributions. While the ADMM derivation itself is standard, the observed scalability is effectively attributed to the underlying structure of the CLIME problem.
