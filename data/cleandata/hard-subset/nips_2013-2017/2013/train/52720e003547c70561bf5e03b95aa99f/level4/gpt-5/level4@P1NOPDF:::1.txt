The authors address sparse precision matrix estimation via CLIME and propose CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant, together with a parallel/distributed framework. The main novelty is estimating the precision matrix by column blocks rather than column-by-column. Empirical results using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK compare CLIME-ADMM to state-of-the-art methods, including DC-QUIC, TIGER, and Flare.

However, the notation is scattered across Sections 1–3. A consolidated table of symbols would improve readability. In particular, ρ and η do not appear to be defined.

The proposed block-cyclic data distribution is interesting, but it is not clear why this scheme guarantees load balance and scalability. The paper would benefit from either a theoretical justification or empirical evidence demonstrating that block-cyclic partitioning leads to balanced workloads and scalable performance in this setting.

Relatedly, the column-block size k appears to be chosen ad hoc. Please provide intuition or theoretical guidance for selecting k, and discuss how performance and accuracy depend on this choice.

A broader concern is that the method does not seem truly “distributed.” The paper does not describe explicit communication or message-passing patterns, nor does it analyze communication costs. As presented, it may be more accurate to characterize the work as a parallel implementation rather than a distributed algorithm.
