- Paper proposes a scalable ADMM-based CLIME variant (CLIME-ADMM) with a column-block estimation strategy and an OpenMPI/ScaLAPACK implementation, compared to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and Flare.
- Strength: Presents a scalable CLIME formulation using ADMM and a column-block approach that may exploit parallel resources better than column-wise updates.
- Strength: Provides an HPC-oriented implementation and comparisons to strong baselines.
- Notation unclear and dispersed; consolidate symbols in a table and define ρ and η at first use with their algorithmic roles.
- Block-cyclic data layout is interesting but lacks justification; provide analysis of expected load balance for varying sparsity and block sizes.
- Empirically validate block-cyclic placement with strong/weak scaling, per-processor utilization, and communication/computation breakdowns versus alternatives.
- Choice of column block size k appears ad hoc; give intuition/theory for scaling with p, sparsity, processors, cache, and network characteristics.
- Provide a sensitivity analysis of performance and accuracy as k varies and recommend a robust default.
- Clarify “distributed” vs “parallel”: specify system model (multi-node MPI, distributed memory), per-iteration communication patterns (collectives vs point-to-point), synchronization, and communication complexity.
- Describe any overlap of computation and communication and how fault tolerance/stragglers are handled, or reframe as primarily a parallel/HPC implementation if appropriate.
- Strengthen empirical section with scaling plots (cores/nodes vs runtime), efficiency metrics, throughput for varying sizes, and memory/runtime impact of block-cyclic layout and k.
- Report accuracy metrics (support recovery, estimation error) alongside runtime to ensure no loss in estimation quality.
- Minor typos and wording fixes: “tunning”→“tuning,” “summerized”→“summarized,” standardize capitalization for TIGER and ScaLAPACK, and define all symbols upon first use.
- Overall: promising large-scale parallel CLIME approach whose clarity and distributed-claims would be strengthened by consolidated notation, block-cyclic justification, k-selection guidance, and detailed communication/empirical validation.
