- Proposes CLIME-ADMM and a distributed framework for scalable sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Empirically compares CLIME-ADMM to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Recommend consolidating notation into a single table; parameters ρ and η are not defined.  
- Block cyclic data distribution is interesting but unclear why it yields load balance and scalability, and lacks empirical support.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; needs intuition or theoretical guidance for selection.  
- Concerns that the method may not be truly “distributed” due to no discussion of message passing/communication—might be a parallel implementation.  
- Minor typos to fix: “tunning” → “tuning”; “parameter tunning free” → “parameter tuning free”; “summerized” → “summarized”.  
- Novelty lies in estimating the precision matrix by column blocks rather than column-by-column.  
- Empirical implementation uses OpenMPI and Scalapak.  
- Overall: a large-scale parallel algorithm for CLIME-based sparse precision matrix estimation.
