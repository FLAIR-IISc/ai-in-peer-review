- Proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence and implements it on shared- and distributed-memory, comparing to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Technical soundness is evident.  
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM largely follows established procedures (prior work cited).  
- CLIME’s structure permits component-wise subproblem solves, with matrix-matrix multiplication as the dominant cost.  
- The problem’s structure enables near-ideal parallelization of ADMM using efficient parallel matrix-matrix multiplication.  
- Numerical experiment details are insufficient; e.g., report the accuracy achieved by each solver.  
- Paper is clearly and logically organized.  
- Evaluation of ADMM on shared- and distributed-memory architectures is a meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: the distributed algorithm is valuable for large-scale sparse precision estimation, though the derivation is standard and scalability largely arises from CLIME’s simple structure.
