The authors propose an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear programming problem in the CLIME estimator and establish an O(1/T) convergence rate. The algorithm is implemented on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, with numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.

Quality: The paper is technically sound.

Minor comments:
1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard; see, for example:
http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm
2) Owing to the structure of CLIME, each subproblem can be solved componentwise, and the most expensive operation is matrix-matrix multiplication. Consequently, ADMM parallelizes very well for CLIME. It is well known that matrix-matrix multiplications parallelize effectively.
3) The setup of the numerical experiments could be specified in greater detail. For example, what accuracy does each solver achieve?

Clarity: The paper is well organized.

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.

Significance: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems is challenging, and a simple yet robust distributed algorithm is valuable. The shared- and distributed-memory evaluation of ADMM is of interest. The derivation of the inexact ADMM is standard, and the good scalability observed is largely due to the simple structure of CLIME.
