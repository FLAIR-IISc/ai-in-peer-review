- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for solving the CLIME linear program for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Establishes a convergence rate of O(1/T) for the proposed method.  
- Implements the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
- Provides numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Technically sound and builds on well-established ADMM and CLIME techniques.  
- The inexact ADMM derivation follows standard approaches (e.g., He and Yuan 2013) and should be acknowledged.  
- Identifies matrix–matrix multiplication as the computational bottleneck, motivating parallelization.  
- Empirical validation shows strong runtime performance but lacks detailed discussion of solver accuracy and robustness.  
- Well-organized and generally clear writing; notation could be slightly expanded for readability.  
- Main novelty is efficient implementation and scaling (column-block updates, block-cyclic distribution) for CLIME.  
- Parallelizing ADMM for matrix computations is not novel; novelty lies in exploiting CLIME structure for efficiency.  
- Demonstrates near-linear scalability with cores, making CLIME more practical for large-scale problems.  
- Would be strengthened by discussing specific applications that benefit from the scalability.  
- Limitations: no major theoretical breakthrough, reliance on standard convergence results, and empirical evaluation could be more comprehensive.
