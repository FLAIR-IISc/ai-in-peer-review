- Tackles scaling sparse precision matrix estimation (CLIME) to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.
- Proposes a novel inexact ADMM algorithm (CLIME-ADMM) and a distributed framework for extreme-scale problems.
- Core idea: solve CLIME in column blocks, exploit matrix multiplication, and use block cyclic data distribution for performance.
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis and rates for the CLIME-ADMM algorithm.
- Empirical evaluation shows strong speedups on shared-memory and distributed-memory systems versus DC-QUIC, Tiger, Flare.
- Well-situated within related work and justified choice of CLIME for theoretical and computational benefits.
- Paper is generally well-written and explains concepts and technical details clearly.
- Notation is scattered early on; key parameters (e.g., ρ, η) are introduced without definition — a consolidated notation table is needed.
- Justification for block cyclic data distribution is shallow; needs deeper explanation and comparison to alternative schemes.
- No clear methodology or theoretical guidance for choosing column block size k; current approach seems ad-hoc and requires trade-off discussion (cache, communication).
- Characterizing the implementation as fully "distributed" is debatable; inter-process communication details and communication cost scaling are insufficient.
- Limited analysis of how different sparsity patterns affect performance; more exploration is needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, offers a novel and effective scalable solution, has theoretical backing and extensive experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak justification of distribution scheme, lack of guidance on k, potential mischaracterization of distributedness, and minor presentation issues.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that merits acceptance, contingent on addressing notation, block distribution justification, k-selection discussion, and clarifying "distributed" vs. "parallel."
