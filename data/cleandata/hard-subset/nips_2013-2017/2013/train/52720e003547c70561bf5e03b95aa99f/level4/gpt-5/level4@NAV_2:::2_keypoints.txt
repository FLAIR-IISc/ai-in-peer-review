- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME LP with O(1/T) convergence for objective value and optimality-violation, plus shared- and distributed-memory implementations and empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.
- Quality: technically sound.
- Minor comment 1: The inexact ADMM derivation follows standard lines (cf. cited reference).
- Minor comment 2: CLIME’s componentwise separability makes matrix–matrix multiplication the dominant cost, so ADMM is a natural fit and parallel matrix–matrix multiplication is well established.
- Minor comment 3: Experimental setup needs more precise specification (e.g., solver accuracy/tolerance achieved).
- Clarity: clearly written and well organized.
- Originality: evaluation of ADMM on both shared- and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.
- Significance: valuable simple and robust distributed approach for large-scale sparse precision estimation; cross-architecture evaluation is noteworthy, though the derivation is standard and strong scalability mainly leverages CLIME’s structural simplicity.
