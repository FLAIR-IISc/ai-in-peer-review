- Proposes an inexact ADMM for the CLIME estimator for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Establishes a convergence rate of O(1/T) for the algorithm.  
- Implements the method on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures to demonstrate scalability.  
- Provides thorough empirical evaluation including comparisons with DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Main contribution: a distributed framework for CLIME that scales to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.  
- Solves CLIME in column-blocks and uses a block-cyclic data/parameter distribution for load balance and memory-hierarchy efficiency.  
- Demonstrates substantially better scalability than state-of-the-art methods and near-linear scaling with number of cores.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly explains the algorithm and implementation.  
- Minor critique: the derivation of inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard and not novel.  
- Suggested improvement: provide more details on numerical experiment setup and the accuracy achieved by each solver.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss implications for other large-scale constrained optimization problems and potential applications.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution showing ADMM’s potential for large-scale distributed optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel scalable framework, proven convergence, extensive evaluation, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: standard derivation, missing experimental detail and broader discussion.
