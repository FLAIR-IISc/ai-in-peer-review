- Proposes a scalable distributed inexact ADMM that processes CLIME precision matrix in column blocks (not column-by-column) for ultra-high-dimensional problems.

- Presents a block-cyclic data distribution and process grid to balance load and reduce cache misses in large-scale settings.

- Establishes convergence rates (O(1/T)) for both objective and constraint violations, aligning with recent inexact ADMM theory.

- Demonstrates substantial scalability and speedups over DC-QUIC, Tiger, and flare on synthetic and real datasets up to million-dimensional problems.

- Implementation uses OpenMP/OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK and shows near-linear scaling on shared- and distributed-memory architectures.

- Fills an important gap by enabling precision matrix estimation at scales (≈1M dims, 1T parameters) that prior methods cannot handle due to memory limits.

- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3, making the paper harder to read; a single notation table is recommended.

- Block-cyclic distribution is described but lacks empirical or theoretical evidence that it minimizes cache misses or is superior to alternatives.

- Choice of column block size k (e.g., k=100) appears ad hoc; no clear theoretical or practical guidance is provided for selecting k.

- Claims of being "distributed" are ambiguous because the paper omits details on communication patterns, message passing, and synchronization, making it read more like a parallel implementation.

- Limited discussion on how column-block processing affects statistical properties compared to column-by-column estimation.

- Technical quality and experiments are strong and thorough, supporting the algorithm's scalability and correctness.

- High significance for fields with high-dimensional data (genomics, climate science) and clear positioning relative to related distributed ML work.

- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify distributed communication, justify/block-select k, and add a notation table to improve readability.
