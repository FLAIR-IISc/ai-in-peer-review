The authors propose a scalable variant of CLIME, denoted as CLIME-ADMM, for sparse precision matrix estimation, accompanied by a distributed computational framework. The empirical results provided compare the performance of the CLIME-ADMM algorithm against state-of-the-art methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.

To enhance readability, it would be beneficial to consolidate the notation into a single table, as it is currently dispersed throughout Sections 1, 2, and 3. For instance, the definitions of \rho and \eta are not explicitly stated in the text.

The block cyclic data distribution scheme appears to be an interesting approach; however, it is unclear why this method would achieve load balance and scalability. Furthermore, there is a lack of empirical evidence to support this claim.

The algorithm's current method of setting the column block size seems to be based on an ad hoc approach, and it would be helpful to provide some theoretical justification or intuition guiding the choice of k.

A more significant concern is that the algorithm may not be truly 'distributed' in nature, as it does not explicitly discuss mechanisms for message passing and communication. It might be more accurate to consider this a parallel implementation.
