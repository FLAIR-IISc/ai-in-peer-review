- Paper proposes CLIME-ADMM and a distributed computational framework for sparse precision matrix estimation, with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; include a table of notations to improve readability.
- Key symbols (e.g., ρ and η) are not explicitly defined in the text.
- Block cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea but the rationale for load balance and scalability is unclear.
- Claim of load balance and scalability for block cyclic distribution is not supported by empirical evidence.
- Choice of column block size k appears ad hoc; provide intuition or theoretical basis for selecting k.
- Unclear whether the method is truly “distributed” — no explicit message-passing or communication mechanisms are described.
- The implementation may be more accurately characterized as parallel rather than distributed.
- Novelty: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks (rather than column-by-column).
- Empirical analysis is conducted using OpenMPI and Scalapak.
- Minor language edits: “tunning parameter” → “tuning parameter” (Sec. 1); “parameter tunning free” → “tuning free” (Sec. 5.1); “CLIME is summerized in” → “CLIME is summarized in” (Sec. 2).
