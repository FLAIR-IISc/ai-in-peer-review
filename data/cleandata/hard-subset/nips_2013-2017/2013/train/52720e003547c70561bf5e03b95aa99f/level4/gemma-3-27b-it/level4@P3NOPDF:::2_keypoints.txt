- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for solving the linear program in the CLIME estimator with an O(1/T) convergence rate.  
- Implements the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures and reports scalability.  
- Presents numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Technically sound application of ADMM to CLIME, but the ADMM derivation itself has limited novelty.  
- Main contribution is practical implementation and evaluation, especially parallelization and scalability analysis.  
- Paper is well-organized and the ADMM formulation is clearly presented.  
- Evaluation across both memory architectures and resulting scalability insights are valuable and differentiate the work.  
- Large-scale sparse precision estimation is important; a simple, robust distributed algorithm is a useful contribution.  
- Experiments lack sufficient detail on solver accuracy (primal/dual feasibilities or optimality gaps) and stopping criteria versus time limits.  
- Needs more detail on how subproblem parallelization is realized in practice (specific libraries and parallelization strategies like BLAS/LAPACK/MPI).  
- Should discuss practical implications of the O(1/T) rate and how inexactness affects real-world performance.  
- Would benefit from a brief comparison of strengths and weaknesses of DC-QUIC, Tiger, CLIME-Flare versus the proposed ADMM and scenario-based method recommendations.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, incremental contribution with good scalability results; accept with minor revisions addressing the above concerns.
