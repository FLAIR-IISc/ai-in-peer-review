The paper addresses sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME, proposes a scalable variant (CLIME-ADMM), and introduces a framework intended for distributed computation. It also provides empirical comparisons against state-of-the-art methods, including DC-QUIC, TIGER, and Flare. The main novelty is computing the precision matrix in column blocks rather than strictly column-by-column. Empirical results using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK are presented.

However, the notation is scattered across Sections 1–3. A consolidated notation table would improve readability; for instance, ρ and η do not appear to be defined.

The proposed block-cyclic data distribution is interesting, but it is not clear why this strategy guarantees load balance and scalability, and no experiments are provided to substantiate these claims.

The column-block size k seems to be chosen ad hoc. Please provide intuition or theoretical guidance on how to select k.

A broader concern is that the work may not be truly “distributed,” since there is no explicit description of the message-passing or communication mechanisms. It may be more accurate to describe the approach as a parallel implementation.
