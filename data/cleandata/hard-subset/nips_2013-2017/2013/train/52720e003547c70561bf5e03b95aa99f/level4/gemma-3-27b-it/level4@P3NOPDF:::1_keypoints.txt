- Paper proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable/parallel variant of CLIME with a distributed computing framework and empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Motivation is clear: scaling CLIME for large datasets is important and practical.  
- Estimating precision matrix by column blocks (vs column-by-column) is a reasonable, parallelizable efficiency improvement.  
- Empirical evaluation provides baseline comparisons and uses both OpenMPI and Scalapak for distributed environments.  
- Notation is inconsistent and key parameters (e.g., ρ, η) are undefined, hurting readability.  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is presented without sufficient rationale or empirical evidence for load balancing and scalability.  
- Choice of column block size k appears ad hoc; no discussion of trade-offs or sensitivity analysis.  
- The claim of a “distributed” algorithm is unclear—no description of message passing or inter-node communication; may be merely parallel.  
- Paper lacks empirical scaling results with varying data sizes, processor counts, and network conditions.  
- Minor typos and wording errors need correction (several examples cited in Sections 1, 2, 5.1).  
- Suggest adding a comprehensive notation table to improve clarity.  
- Suggest performing scalability experiments (strong/weak scaling) and studying network impact.  
- Suggest analyzing block size selection and sensitivity, with guidelines for choosing k.  
- Suggest clarifying/ documenting distributed implementation details or reframing as parallel, and adding an ablation study of CLIME-ADMM components.  
- Overall: promising and practical approach but insufficient clarity and scalability evidence; recommend rejection in current form but possible reconsideration after major revisions.
