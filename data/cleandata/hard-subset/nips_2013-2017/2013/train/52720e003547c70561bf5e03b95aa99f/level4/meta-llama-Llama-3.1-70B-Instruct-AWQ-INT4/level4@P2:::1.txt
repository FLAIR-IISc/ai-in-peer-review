The authors discuss the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME and present a scalable variant CLIME-ADMM along with a distributed framework for computations. Empirical results comparing the CLIME-ADMM algorithm to state-of-the-art techniques such as DC-QUIC, Tiger and Flare are presented.

The paper relates to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere on sparse precision matrix estimation, which has been an active area of research in recent years. The CLIME estimator, which is the focus of this paper, has been shown to have strong statistical guarantees and is a popular choice for sparse precision matrix estimation. However, existing methods for solving CLIME are not scalable to high-dimensional problems, which is the primary contribution of this paper.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the CLIME-ADMM algorithm and its distributed framework. The empirical results are also well-presented and demonstrate the scalability of the algorithm.

One minor comment is that the notation could be improved. The notation is scattered throughout the paper, and it may be helpful to have a table with all the notations for ease of readability. For example, \rho and \eta are not defined in the text.

Another minor comment is that the block cyclic data distribution scheme is not well-explained. It is not obvious why this scheme would achieve load balance and scalability, and there are no empirical results to prove this point.

A more significant concern is that the algorithm currently sets the column block size on an ad-hoc basis. There does not appear to be any intuition or theory to guide the choice of k, which could be a limitation of the algorithm.

Finally, it is worth noting that this is perhaps not a 'distributed' algorithm in the classical sense, as no explicit mechanism of message passing and communication are discussed. Perhaps it would make sense to consider this a parallel implementation.

Minor comments:

* Section 1: 'where \lambda is a tunning parameter' should be replaced with 'tuning'
* Section 5.1: 'As Tiger is parameter tunning free' should be replaced with 'tuning'
* Section 2: 'CLIME is summerized in' should be replaced with 'CLIME is summarized in'

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and scalable algorithm for sparse precision matrix estimation using CLIME.
* The empirical results demonstrate the scalability of the algorithm and its performance compared to state-of-the-art techniques.
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the algorithm and its distributed framework.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The notation could be improved, and a table with all the notations would be helpful.
* The block cyclic data distribution scheme is not well-explained, and there are no empirical results to prove its effectiveness.
* The algorithm currently sets the column block size on an ad-hoc basis, and there is no intuition or theory to guide the choice of k.
* The paper may not be suitable for NIPS, as it is more focused on parallel implementation rather than distributed algorithms.

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper, but with some revisions to address the minor comments and concerns mentioned above.
