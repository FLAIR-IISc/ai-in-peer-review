- Introduces CLIME-ADMM: a scalable, block-wise CLIME variant for sparse precision matrix estimation with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is scattered and confusing; add a notation table and define symbols like ρ and η.  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is proposed but not justified; unclear why it ensures load balance or scalability and no supporting experiments.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; provide theoretical guidance or intuition for selecting k since it critically affects performance.  
- The framework lacks explicit communication/ message-passing details, so it reads as a parallel implementation rather than a truly distributed method.  
- Minor typos to fix: "tunning" → "tuning" (Sec.1), "parameter tunning free" → "parameter tuning free" (Sec.5.1), "summerized" → "summarized" (Sec.2).  
- Core contribution is meaningful: block-wise column estimation vs column-by-column; empirical validation uses OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK, but scalability and distributed-framework technicals need stronger evidence.
