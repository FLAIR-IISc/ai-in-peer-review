This author proposes an Inexact Alternating Direction of Multiply (ADMM) for solving linear programming problems in the CLIME paradigm. A convergence rate of O(1/T) has been established. The algorithm is implemented using both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures. Numerical comparisons with other methods such as DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare are provided.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound.

**Minor Comments:**

1) The emergence of Inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard. See for example:
http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm
2) Due to the structure of CLIME, each sub-problem solution can be computed component-wise, and the most expensive operation is matrix-matrix multiplication. Therefore, ADMM for CLIME can ideally be made parallel. It is well known that matrix-matrix multiplication is well-parallelizable.
3) The numerical test setup can be further specified. For example, what is the accuracy achieved by each solver?

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized.

**Originality:** The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.

**Importance:** Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation problems is difficult. A simple but powerful distributed algorithm is helpful. The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting. The emergence of Inexact ADMM is due to the simple structure of CLIME and the standard and good scalability.
