- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm to solve the CLIME linear program with proven O(1/T) convergence.
- Implements the algorithm on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.
- Provides numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Derivation of inexact ADMM follows a standard approach similar to existing references.
- CLIME’s structure permits componentwise subproblem solutions; the main cost is matrix-matrix multiplication.
- Matrix-matrix multiplications are highly parallelizable, making ADMM well-suited for parallelization in CLIME.
- Numerical experiment setup needs more detail, particularly reporting solver accuracy.
- Paper is well-organized and easy to follow.
- Evaluating ADMM on shared- and distributed-memory systems is an interesting and original contribution.
- A simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision estimation is significant and useful.
- Caveat: the novelty is limited since the inexact ADMM derivation is standard and scalability largely stems from CLIME’s simple structure.
