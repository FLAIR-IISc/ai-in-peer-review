- Tackles covariance selection at ultra-large scales (millions of variables, trillions of parameters).
- Focuses on the CLIME estimator, leveraging its columnwise decomposition for distributed computation and simpler analysis vs. Graphical Lasso.
- Proposes a new solver that optimizes CLIME in column blocks rather than single columns.
- Combines block optimization with an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM).
- Provides convergence-rate guarantees for both objective suboptimality and optimality residuals.
- Explicitly exploits sparsity and low-rank structure to reduce computation.
- Implements a scalable parallel framework for both shared-memory and distributed-memory systems.
- Empirically compares runtime against strong baselines on synthetic and real datasets.
- Presents an in-depth scaling study showing speedups as a function of block size and number of cores in both environments.
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and connects appropriately to prior work with suitable citations.
- Concern: lack of released source code would substantially diminish practical impact; publication recommended if code is made accessible.
