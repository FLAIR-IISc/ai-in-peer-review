This paper addresses the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME algorithm and introduces a scalable variant, CLIME-ADMM, along with a distributed computational framework. The authors present empirical comparisons of CLIME-ADMM against established techniques like DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.

The paper’s presentation could be improved through greater consistency in notation. While the mathematical notation is generally standard, its introduction feels scattered across Sections 1, 2, and 3, hindering readability. A consolidated table of notations would be highly beneficial to the reader. Specifically, the parameters ρ and η are used without prior definition within the main text.

The proposed block cyclic data distribution strategy is an interesting approach. However, the justification for its effectiveness in achieving load balance and scalability is not immediately clear. Supporting this claim with empirical evidence demonstrating performance characteristics under varying conditions would significantly strengthen the paper. 

The selection of the column block size, *k*, currently appears to be determined *ad hoc*. The authors should discuss the rationale behind this choice, providing either theoretical guidance or empirical insights into how *k* impacts performance. Justifying this parameter setting is crucial for reproducibility and practical application of the algorithm.

A broader concern relates to the characterization of the algorithm as “distributed.” The paper lacks a detailed description of explicit message passing or communication mechanisms between computational nodes. Considering the implementation as a parallel algorithm might be a more accurate description, given the current level of detail provided. Elucidating the communication aspects, if any, would clarify the distinction.

Several minor points require attention:

*   Section 1: Replace “tunning parameter” with “tuning parameter.”
*   Section 5.1: Replace “parameter tunning free” with “tuning-free.”
*   Section 2: Replace “CLIME is summerized in” with “CLIME is summarized in.”

Overall, the paper presents a potentially valuable contribution – a large-scale parallel algorithm for estimating sparse precision matrices using CLIME. The core novelty lies in estimating the precision matrix by operating on column blocks instead of individual columns. Empirical analysis employing OpenMPI and Scalapak is included. Addressing the concerns regarding notation, justification of the block size, and clarification of the “distributed” nature of the algorithm would significantly enhance the paper’s impact and clarity.
