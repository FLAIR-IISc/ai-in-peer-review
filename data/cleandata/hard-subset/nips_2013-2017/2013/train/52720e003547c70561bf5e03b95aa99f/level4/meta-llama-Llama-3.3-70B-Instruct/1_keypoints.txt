- Paper proposes CLIME-ADMM: a scalable variant of CLIME and a distributed computational framework for sparse precision matrix estimation, compared empirically to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3 and should be consolidated (e.g., definitions of ρ and η are missing).
- Block cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea but the paper does not justify why it ensures load balance or scalability.
- There is no empirical evidence presented to support claims of load balancing and scalability for the block cyclic scheme.
- Choice of column block size k appears ad hoc; the paper needs theoretical grounding or intuitive guidance for selecting k.
- The implementation may be parallel rather than truly distributed: message passing and communication mechanisms are not described.
- Minor language/typo fixes needed: "tunning" → "tuning" (Section 1), "parameter tunning free" → "tuning" (Section 5.1), "CLIME is summerized in" → "CLIME is summarized in" (Section 2).
- Novelty highlighted: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks (instead of column-by-column).
- Empirical analysis uses OpenMPI and Scalapak, but further experimental validation of distributed claims is required.
