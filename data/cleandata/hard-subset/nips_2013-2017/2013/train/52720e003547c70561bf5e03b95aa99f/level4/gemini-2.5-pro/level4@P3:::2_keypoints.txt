- Addresses sparse precision matrix estimation in ultra-high dimensions and demonstrates scaling to p = 10^6.  
- Empirical results show impressive scalability with near-linear speedups on distributed memory and a trillion-parameter problem solved.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation comparing against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and flare, with separate analyses of parallelization (S_core) and block processing (S_col).  
- Paper is clear and well-written, with lucid algorithm presentation and helpful diagrams.  

- Algorithmic originality is limited: the inexact ADMM with linearization and proximal soft-thresholding is a standard technique.  
- Much of the scalability stems from CLIME’s column-wise decomposability and existing highly optimized parallel matrix-matrix libraries; the novelty is mainly engineering/system integration.  
- Experimental setup lacks rigorous reporting of solution quality (final objective values or constraint violations), making runtime comparisons potentially unfair.  

- Overall: a well-engineered, highly scalable distributed framework with strong practical impact but limited fundamental algorithmic novelty.  
- Recommendation: valuable contribution likely acceptable for the community due to scale and practicality despite limited originality.  

- For acceptance: addresses a significant problem, demonstrates unprecedented scale (10^12 parameters), presents a well-designed framework, and provides extensive convincing experiments.  
- For rejection: core algorithm is standard, scalability largely derives from problem structure and existing libraries rather than new parallel techniques, and experimental comparisons lack convergence/accuracy metrics.
