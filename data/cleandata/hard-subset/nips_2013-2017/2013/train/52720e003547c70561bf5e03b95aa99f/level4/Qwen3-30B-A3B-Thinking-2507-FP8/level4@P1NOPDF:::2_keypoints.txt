- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving CLIME’s linear program and proves an O(1/T) convergence rate.  
- Implements the algorithm on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures and compares against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Technical approach is sound.  
- The inexact ADMM derivation largely follows standard procedures (e.g., He et al., 2012).  
- CLIME’s structure yields element-wise subproblem solutions; matrix-matrix multiplication is the dominant computation.  
- Matrix-matrix operations’ parallelizability leads to near-optimal scalability for the algorithm.  
- Numerical experimental setup lacks detail—report convergence tolerances achieved by each solver.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Empirical evaluation of ADMM across shared- and distributed-memory platforms is a novel contribution.  
- The distributed algorithm is a simple, robust solution with strong scalability due to CLIME’s structural simplicity.  
- Evaluating ADMM’s parallel performance for large-scale sparse precision estimation is timely and valuable.
