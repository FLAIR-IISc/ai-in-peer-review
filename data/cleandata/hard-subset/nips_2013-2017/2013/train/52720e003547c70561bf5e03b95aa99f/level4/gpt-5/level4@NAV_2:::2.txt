The authors present an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for the linear program at the core of the CLIME estimator. They establish an O(1/T) convergence rate for both the objective value and violations of the optimality conditions. Implementations are provided for both shared-memory and distributed-memory systems, and empirical comparisons are reported against DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.

Quality: The paper is technically sound.

minor comments:
1) The inexact ADMM derivation for CLIME follows standard lines; see, e.g.,
http://math.nju.edu.cn/~hebma/English%20Version.htm
2) Owing to CLIME’s structure, each subproblem is separable componentwise, making matrix–matrix multiplication the dominant cost. Consequently, ADMM is a particularly natural fit for parallelization in this setting, and efficient parallelization of matrix–matrix multiplications is well established.
3) The experimental setup could be specified more precisely. For instance, what accuracy/tolerance does each solver achieve?

Clarity: The manuscript is clearly written and well organized.

Originality: The evaluation of ADMM on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is interesting.

Significance: Tackling large-scale sparse precision estimation is challenging, and a simple yet robust distributed approach is valuable. The cross-architecture evaluation of ADMM is noteworthy, though the inexact ADMM derivation is standard and the strong scalability largely leverages CLIME’s structural simplicity.
