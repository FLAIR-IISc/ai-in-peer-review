- Addresses large-scale covariance selection (millions of variables) using CLIME and proposes a block-wise solver with inexact ADMM and convergence guarantees.
- Clear, well-written presentation with good literature positioning.
- Moving from column-wise to block-wise updates is well-motivated for parallel and distributed architectures.
- Provides solid theoretical analysis with explicit guarantees on objective value and distance to optimality.
- Engineering exploits sparsity and low-rank structure to reduce computation and memory.
- Parallel implementation supports shared- and distributed-memory and shows impressive scalability.
- Experiments comprehensively evaluate runtime and scalability, including ablations over block size and core count.
- Reproducibility concern: source code, build scripts, and experiment configurations should be publicly released.
- Evaluation metrics: must report statistical performance (Frobenius/operator norm error, support recovery precision/recall/F1, downstream performance) in addition to runtime.
- Baselines: need clearer, stronger comparisons to prominent methods (e.g., QUIC, nodewise regression) using optimized implementations.
- Regularization/hyperparameters: clarify how tuning is done at scale (cross-validation, theoretical choices, warm-starts, screening) and its computational cost.
- Symmetry and positive-definiteness: specify how symmetry is enforced in block-wise/distributed settings and whether final estimates are PD or post-processed.
- Memory and communication: provide detailed scaling of memory with problem size/sparsity/block size and quantify communication overhead and computation vs communication time.
- Block-size choice: add guidance or an adaptive rule for selecting block sizes based on sparsity, conditioning, or hardware.
- Stopping criteria: detail primal/dual tolerances, stopping rules, relation to theory, and empirical effects on runtime and accuracy.
- Structural exploitation: clarify how low-rank structure is detected or imposed, overhead of structural updates, and effect of approximate projections on accuracy.
- Robustness and data model: discuss robustness to non-Gaussian/heavy-tailed data and whether preprocessing (rank-based correlations, truncation) can be integrated without breaking guarantees.
- Minor fixes: align terminology (inexact ADMM), correct phrasing and wording issues.
- Provide hardware/software details (CPU, memory, interconnect, BLAS/LAPACK/MPI, compiler flags) and release reproducibility scripts and minimal examples.
- Overall assessment: strong methodological and systems contribution with solid theory and engineering; recommend publication conditional on code release and addressing accuracy, baselines, symmetry/PD handling, and practical guidance on block size and stopping.
