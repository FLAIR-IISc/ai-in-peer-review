- Proposes CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant of CLIME with a distributed/parallel computational framework and empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.  
- Notation is dispersed across Sections 1–3; consolidate into a single table (symbols like ρ and η are undefined).  
- Block-cyclic data distribution is an intriguing idea but its load-balancing and scalability properties are unclear and unvalidated by experiments.  
- Column block size k appears chosen ad hoc; the paper lacks theoretical justification or intuition for this choice.  
- The claim of being “distributed” is questionable because no message-passing or inter-process communication mechanisms are described—may be more accurately described as a parallel implementation.  
- Minor typographical corrections suggested: “tunning” → “tuning” (Section 1), “tunning” → “tuning” (Section 5.1), “summerized” → “summarized” (Section 2).  
- Key algorithmic innovation: estimate the precision matrix by column blocks rather than column-by-column.  
- Empirical implementation leverages OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK to demonstrate large-scale parallel performance.
