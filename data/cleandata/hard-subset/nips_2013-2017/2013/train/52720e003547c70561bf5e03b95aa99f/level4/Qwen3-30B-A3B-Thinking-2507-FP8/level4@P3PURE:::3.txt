

Summary of the paper  
This paper addresses covariance selection in extremely large-scale settings, handling up to millions of features and trillions of parameters. The authors focus on the CLIME estimator for the precision matrix of a Gaussian vector, which enables column-wise solution and thus facilitates distributed computation. Theoretical analysis is claimed to be more straightforward than for the Graphical-Lasso. To fit CLIME, the authors develop a novel block-wise algorithm—coupled with an inexact direction method of multipliers—replacing the standard column-wise approach. Theoretical convergence rates are established for both the objective function and the distance to optimality. The method further leverages special matrix structures (sparsity and low rank) during computation to reduce cost. Implemented within a scalable parallel framework supporting both shared and distributed memory systems, the algorithm demonstrates exceptional runtime performance in numerical studies. Comparisons with direct competitors are provided on synthetic and real data, alongside a detailed analysis of speedup dependencies on block size and core count across shared and distributed memory architectures.  

Comments  
This paper is exceptionally well-written and clear. The novelty and contributions are explicitly stated in the introduction, with well-established connections to existing work and sound bibliographical references. The algorithm is introduced with an excellent balance between technical numerical analysis/algebra and theoretical convergence guarantees. The numerical analysis is appropriate, and the implementation achieves impressive performance.  

My sole concern pertains to reproducibility: if the source code (primarily built upon open-source libraries) is not made publicly available, the work's impact is severely limited. A sound algorithmic contribution tackling the critical practical problem of high-dimensional covariance selection—where dimensions reach millions of variables—deserves publication only if accessible to the broader community. This powerful tool should be shared to maximize its utility.
