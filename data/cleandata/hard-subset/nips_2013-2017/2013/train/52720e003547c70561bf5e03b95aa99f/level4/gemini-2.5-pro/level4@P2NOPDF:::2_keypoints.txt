- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving CLIME with a proven O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Main contribution is implementation and empirical evaluation on shared- and distributed-memory parallel architectures.
- Thorough empirical evaluation across architectures is a major strength and useful for practitioners.
- Theoretical novelty is limited: applying inexact ADMM to CLIME follows a relatively standard approach.
- Scalability results are largely expected given CLIME’s structure (component-wise decomposition and matrix-matrix multiplies that are highly parallelizable).
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, easy to follow.
- Suggestion: better contextualize the algorithmic contribution by citing foundational inexact ADMM work and framing novelty as large-scale implementation/empirics.
- Suggestion: explicitly attribute and discuss why the algorithm scales well, highlighting problem structure as a primary factor.
- Suggestion: strengthen experiments by reporting termination criteria, convergence thresholds, and final accuracy/objective or constraint violation for each solver.
