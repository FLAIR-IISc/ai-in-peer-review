Summary of the paper
The paper addresses covariance selection at extremely large scales (up to millions of features and trillions of parameters). It focuses on the CLIME estimator, which yields a precision-matrix estimate for a Gaussian vector and can be solved column by column, making it amenable to distributed computation. Theoretical analysis is also claimed to be easier than for the Graphical Lasso. The authors propose a new algorithm for fitting CLIME that solves the problem block-wise rather than column-wise, coupled with an inexact direction method of multipliers. They provide theoretical convergence-rate results, both in terms of the objective value and distance to optimality. The algorithm explicitly exploits matrix structure (sparsity and low rank) to reduce computational cost. It is implemented in a scalable parallel framework for both shared- and distributed-memory systems. The numerical studies compare runtime against direct competitors on synthetic and real data and include a detailed speedup analysis as a function of block size and number of cores in both shared- and distributed-memory environments.

Comments
- The paper is well written and very clear. The novelty and contributions are clearly stated in the introduction, and the connections to existing work are well established with sound bibliographical references.
- The algorithm is presented with a good balance between numerical analysis/algebraic details and theoretical convergence guarantees.
- The numerical evaluation is appropriate, and the implementation demonstrates very impressive performance.

Concern
My only concern is the availability of the method: if the source code (mostly based on open-source libraries) is not made available to the community, there is no point in such a work.

Overall assessment
This is a sound algorithmic paper addressing the important practical problem of covariance selection at very large scale (millions of variables). Such a powerful tool should be rewarded by publication, provided it is made available to the community.
