1. Proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME with proven O(1/T) convergence.  
2. Evaluates performance on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.  
3. Provides numerical comparisons with DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
4. Method is technically sound and well-motivated for large-scale sparse precision estimation.  
5. Derivation of inexact ADMM is standard; prior related work should be acknowledged.  
6. ADMM benefits from parallelization due to component-wise subproblems and parallelizable matrix-matrix multiplications.  
7. Numerical experiments should report achieved accuracy for each solver.  
8. Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
9. Originality mainly in the architecture-specific evaluation rather than the ADMM derivation.  
10. Observed scalability is largely due to CLIME's simple problem structure, not major algorithmic novelty.  
11. Significance: offers a simple, robust distributed algorithm useful for large-scale problems.  
12. Overall assessment: solid contribution that needs minor revisions to increase impact.
