- Proposes an inexact ADMM for CLIME with an established O(1/T) convergence rate.
- Provides implementations on both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures.
- Empirically compares performance against DC-QUIC, TIGER, and CLIME-Flare.
- Paper is technically sound.
- Manuscript is clearly written and well organized.
- Derivation of inexact ADMM for CLIME is standard, so originality is limited.
- Evaluation on both shared- and distributed-memory platforms is a useful, interesting contribution.
- Method is significant for large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation; a simple, robust, distributed algorithm is valuable.
- Observed scalability is largely due to CLIME’s simple structure.
- Subproblems are solvable componentwise; dominant cost is matrix–matrix multiplication which parallelizes well.
- Reviewer suggests citing prior standard approaches (link provided).
- Numerical experiment setup needs more detail, e.g., what accuracy each solver achieves.
