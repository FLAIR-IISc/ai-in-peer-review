- Addresses sparse precision matrix estimation at extreme scale (millions of variables, trillions of parameters).
- Builds on the CLIME estimator and proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm tailored for scalability.
- Key innovation: solves in column-blocks instead of column-by-column to improve efficiency.
- Implements a scalable distributed framework to handle very large datasets.
- Establishes convergence rates for the proposed algorithm with theoretical justification.
- Exploits sparse and low-rank matrix structures to further reduce computation and memory costs.
- Extensive experiments on shared- and distributed-memory systems demonstrate large performance gains over state-of-the-art methods.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and well-organized with consistent notation.
- Technical soundness is strong, with careful analysis and complete experimental validation.
- The block-wise ADMM + distributed implementation is a novel and significant contribution.
- Results are important for practical covariance selection in very high-dimensional settings and likely to be widely adopted.
- Main concern: impact limited unless the implementation (largely based on open-source libraries) is publicly released.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of publication, ideally accompanied by public code release.
