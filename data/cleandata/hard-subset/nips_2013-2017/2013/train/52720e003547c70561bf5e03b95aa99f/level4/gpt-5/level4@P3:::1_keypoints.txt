- Contribution: Proposes an inexact (linearized) ADMM for CLIME with O(1/T) convergence for objective and feasibility under η ≥ ρλmax(C)^2, using column-block updates for better matrix–matrix kernels and parallelism.  
- Engineering: Exploits iterate- and covariance-sparsity and low-rank factorization C = A A^T to reduce per-iteration cost from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k).  
- Systems: Presents a block-cyclic distributed framework (OpenMP, OpenMPI + ScaLAPACK) enabling near-linear speedups and a 1M-dimensional run (~11 hours on 400 cores).  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on CLIME’s column-wise decomposability and ADMM solvers, but emphasizes block updates and HPC-style blocking/distribution.  
- Novelty: The aggregate of inexact ADMM specialization, column-block matrix–matrix emphasis, and scalable block-cyclic implementation is a useful and relatively novel systems contribution.  
- Theoretical quality: Linearized ADMM updates and O(1/T) rates are standard and plausible, but proofs are relegated to supplements and a main-text sketch is missing.  
- Parameter assumptions: Requirement η ≥ ρλmax(C)^2 is reasonable but not operationalized; guidance on choosing (ρ, η) is absent.  
- Statistical aspects: Theorem 3 motivates thresholding C for sparsity, but practical threshold selection and its impact on estimation error are unclear and not empirically validated.  
- Implementation detail gap: Communication patterns, collectives, overlap of comm/comp per ADMM step, and per-iteration communication costs are not detailed.  
- Experimental strengths: Demonstrates strong scalability and competitive runtime against CLIME (flare), TIGER, and DC-QUIC across regimes.  
- Experimental weaknesses: Accuracy evaluation focuses on precision/recall; missing metrics like spectral/Frobenius error, held-out likelihood, and controlled comparisons matching λ or error.  
- Reproducibility issues: Stopping criteria, tolerances, q, k, block sizes, and some figure/table details are not reported.  
- Block-size guidance: No theory- or model-based guidance or auto-tuning for column block size k; dependence on n, p, q, and cache/network characteristics is unexplained.  
- Distributed specifics requested: Need description of primitives (e.g., SUMMA/PDGEMM), synchronization points per iteration, and compute vs. communication time breakdown.  
- Block-cyclic claim needs ablation: No comparison to contiguous layouts or load-balance metrics to substantiate block-cyclic superiority.  
- Positive-definiteness: No description of post-processing (symmetrization or PD projection) for CLIME solutions and its impact on timing/accuracy.  
- Hyperparameter reporting: λ, ρ, η selection procedures and sensitivity plots are missing.  
- Stopping and tolerances: Stopping criteria and iteration counts are not reported.  
- Practical sparsification: Details on how often C is sparsified/thresholded and empirical effect on runtime/accuracy are missing.  
- Presentation issues: Notation and definitions scattered; a consolidated notation table and clearer definition of operators (soft, box) are needed.  
- Minor editorial issues: Multiple typos, incomplete/inconsistent references, duplicate citations, and unclear algorithm notation (E vs I, dimensions in updates).  
- Memory reporting: Per-process memory usage and whether C is explicitly formed are not reported.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important scaling bottleneck with strong engineering, scalability, and applicability to global-constraint estimators.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Lacks detailed communication/memory characterization, hyperparameter guidance, ablation studies, and stronger statistical evaluation.
