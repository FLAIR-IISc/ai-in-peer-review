This paper addresses the problem of sparse precision matrix estimation by proposing CLIME-ADMM, a scalable variant of the CLIME estimator. The authors introduce a distributed framework where the precision matrix is estimated in column blocks, a key novelty over column-by-column methods. Empirical results are presented comparing CLIME-ADMM to state-of-the-art techniques, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare, using an OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK implementation.

While the approach is promising, the paper could be improved in several areas:

*   **Clarity of Notation:** The notation is scattered throughout Sections 1, 2, and 3, which makes the paper difficult to follow. A consolidated table defining all symbols would greatly improve readability. For instance, the parameters ρ and η appear to be used without a formal definition.

*   **Justification for Data Distribution:** The proposed block cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea; however, it is not obvious why this particular scheme achieves effective load balancing and scalability. The paper would be stronger if it included theoretical justification or empirical results to support this design choice.

*   **Guidance on Hyperparameter Selection:** The column block size, `k`, appears to be set on an ad-hoc basis. The authors should provide some intuition or theoretical guidance for choosing this parameter.

*   **"Distributed" vs. "Parallel" Implementation:** A more general concern is the characterization of the algorithm as 'distributed.' Since the paper does not discuss explicit mechanisms for message passing or inter-node communication, it might be more accurately described as a 'parallel' implementation.

**Minor Comments:**

*   Section 1: "tunning parameter" should be "tuning parameter".
*   Section 2: "CLIME is summerized in" should be "CLIME is summarized in".
*   Section 5.1: "As Tiger is parameter tunning free" should be "As Tiger is parameter tuning free".
