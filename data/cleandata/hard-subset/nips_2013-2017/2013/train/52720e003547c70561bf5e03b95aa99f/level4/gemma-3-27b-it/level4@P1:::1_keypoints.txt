- Paper proposes CLIME-ADMM and a distributed/parallel framework for sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Empirical comparisons are provided against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and flare.  
- Notation is scattered across Sections 1–3; key symbols (e.g., ρ and η) are undefined — add a notation table.  
- Block cyclic data distribution is proposed but the rationale for load balancing and scalability is unclear and lacks empirical validation.  
- Column block size k selection is not explained; no intuition or theoretical guidance is given.  
- The term “distributed” may be misleading because inter-process communication and message-passing details are not discussed; it might be better described as parallel.  
- Demonstrated scalability: solved a 1M-dimension problem in 11 hours on 400 cores, showing capability on very large problems.  
- Theoretical contributions: convergence rates are established for the objective and optimality conditions of CLIME-ADMM.  
- Practical implementation uses standard libraries (OpenMP, OpenMPI, ScaLAPACK), making the approach accessible.  
- Main methodological novelty: estimating the precision matrix by column blocks instead of column-by-column.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation and empirical evidence for effectiveness of block cyclic distribution.  
- Weakness: ad hoc/undefined procedure for choosing column block size k limits practical applicability.  
- Weakness: imprecise use of “distributed” vs. “parallel” should be clarified.  
- Weakness: scattered/undefined notation reduces readability and clarity.  
- Minor language/typo fixes needed: replace “tunning” with “tuning” and “summerized” with “summarized,” etc.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a relevant high-dimensional problem, shows strong scalability, provides theoretical guarantees, and offers a practical implementation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate justification of block distribution, lack of block-size guidance, imprecise terminology, and notation/clarity issues.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify block distribution, block-size choice, communication model, and notation.
