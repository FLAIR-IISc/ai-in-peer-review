This work proposes a large-scale parallel algorithm, CLIME-ADMM, for sparse precision matrix estimation based on the CLIME method. The core novelty is an approach that estimates the precision matrix by column blocks, as opposed to a column-by-column basis. The paper presents an empirical analysis, implemented using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK, comparing the proposed algorithm with state-of-the-art methods, including DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare.

The notation is introduced disparately across Sections 1, 2, and 3, which hinders readability. The paper would benefit from a consolidated table of notations to improve clarity for the reader. For example, the parameters \rho and \eta are used without being explicitly defined.

While the proposed block cyclic data distribution is an interesting concept, its advantages are not immediately apparent. The manuscript should provide a clearer justification for how this scheme achieves load balancing and enhances scalability. These claims would be further strengthened by the inclusion of empirical results to validate them.

The methodology for selecting the column block size, k, appears to be determined on an ad-hoc basis. It would be beneficial to include some theoretical intuition or practical guidelines to inform the choice of this parameter.

A more general point of concern is the description of the algorithm as 'distributed'. Without a discussion of explicit message passing mechanisms and communication overhead, it seems more appropriate to classify this contribution as a parallel implementation.
