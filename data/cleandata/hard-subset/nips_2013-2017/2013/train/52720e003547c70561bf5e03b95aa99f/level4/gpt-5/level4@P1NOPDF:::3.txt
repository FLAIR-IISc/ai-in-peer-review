Summary of the paper
This paper addresses large-scale covariance selection problems (up to millions of features and trillions of parameters). The estimator considered is CLIME, which estimates the precision matrix of a Gaussian vector and can be solved column by column, making it naturally amenable to distributed computation. Its theoretical analysis is also claimed to be simpler than that of the graphical lasso. The authors develop a new algorithm to fit CLIME by solving the problem block-wise rather than column-wise, coupled with an inexact direction method of multipliers. They provide theoretical convergence-rate results, in terms of both the objective value and distance to optimality. They also exploit structural properties (sparsity and low rank) during computation to reduce the computational burden. The algorithm is implemented within a scalable parallel framework for both shared- and distributed-memory systems. In the numerical studies, they compare runtime with direct competitors on both synthetic and real data. They also provide a detailed study of speedup as a function of block size and number of cores on both shared-memory and distributed-memory systems.

Comments
This paper is well written and very clear. The novelty and contributions are clearly stated in the introduction, and the connections to existing work are well established, with sound bibliographical references. The algorithm is nicely introduced, with a good balance between numerical linear-algebra considerations and theoretical convergence guarantees. The empirical evaluation is appropriate, and the implementation exhibits very impressive performance.

My only concern is the availability of the method: if the source code (largely based on open-source libraries) is not made available to the community, there is no point in such a work.

Overall, this is a sound algorithmic paper that tackles the important practical problem of covariance selection at very large scales (millions of variables). Such a powerful tool should be rewarded with publication if it is made available to the community.
