- Proposes a scalable CLIME variant (CLIME-ADMM) and a distributed/parallel framework with empirical comparisons to DC-QUIC, TIGER, and FLARE.  
- Notation is dispersed across Sections 1–3; a centralized notation table is recommended (ρ and η are undefined).  
- The block-cyclic data distribution scheme is interesting but lacks justification and empirical evidence for its claimed load balancing and scalability.  
- Column block size k is chosen ad hoc; the paper needs theoretical guidance or intuition for selecting k.  
- The method may not be truly “distributed” since explicit message passing/communication mechanisms are absent—better described as a parallel implementation.  
- Minor corrections: "tunning" → "tuning" (Sec. 1), "parameter tunning free" → "tuning free" (Sec. 5.1), "summerized" → "summarized" (Sec. 2).  
- Key contribution: large-scale parallel algorithm estimating the precision matrix by column blocks rather than column-by-column, with empirical analysis using OpenMPI and Scalapak.
