- Proposes an inexact ADMM for solving CLIME with an established O(1/T) convergence rate.  
- Implements the algorithm on shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures and compares it to DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  
- Paper is technically sound.  
- Derivation of the inexact ADMM follows standard approaches (reference provided).  
- CLIME’s structure yields componentwise subproblem solutions; matrix-matrix multiplication is the dominant operation enabling ideal parallelization.  
- Numerical experiment setup needs more detail, e.g., the accuracy achieved by each solver.  
- Paper is well-organized and clear.  
- Evaluating ADMM in both shared- and distributed-memory settings is a noteworthy contribution.  
- Significance: provides a simple, robust distributed algorithm for large-scale sparse precision estimation, with scalability driven by CLIME’s simple structure.
