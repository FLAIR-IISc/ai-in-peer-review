This paper presents a large-scale parallel algorithm, CLIME-ADMM, for sparse precision matrix estimation based on the CLIME method. The primary novelty lies in a distributed framework that estimates the precision matrix in column blocks rather than column-by-column. The paper includes an empirical comparison of CLIME-ADMM against state-of-the-art methods such as DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare, with an implementation using OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.

While the approach is promising, the paper could be strengthened in several areas:

*   The notation is scattered across Sections 1, 2, and 3, which hinders readability. The authors should consider consolidating all notation into a single table for easy reference. For instance, the parameters `\rho` and `\eta` appear to be used without a formal definition.

*   The proposed block cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea; however, the paper does not make it clear why this particular scheme achieves better load balancing and scalability. Furthermore, this claim is not substantiated with empirical results.

*   The choice of the column block size, `k`, appears to be ad-hoc. The authors should provide some intuition or theoretical guidance on how to select this parameter optimally.

*   A more general concern relates to the terminology used. The authors describe the algorithm as "distributed," yet the paper lacks a discussion of the explicit message passing and communication mechanisms that are fundamental to distributed systems. It might be more accurate to frame the contribution as a "parallel implementation" rather than a distributed algorithm.
