

Review  
Summary of the paper  
This paper addresses covariance selection in very large-scale settings (up to millions of features and trillions of parameters). The authors focus on the CLIME estimator, which provides a precision matrix estimator for Gaussian vectors solvable column-wise, enabling more straightforward distributed computation and offering theoretically simpler analysis than Graphical-Lasso. They develop a novel algorithm for CLIME that solves the problem block-wise rather than column-wise, coupled with an inexact direction method of multipliers. Theoretical convergence rates for the algorithm are established, in terms of both the objective function and distance to optimality. Special attention is given to leveraging sparse and low-rank matrix structures during computation to reduce complexity. The algorithm is implemented within a scalable parallel framework supporting both shared and distributed memory systems. Numerical studies compare runtime against direct competitors on synthetic and real data, and include a detailed analysis of speedup as a function of block size and core count across shared and distributed memory architectures.  

Comments  
The paper is well-written and clear. The novelty and contributions of the method are explicitly stated in the introduction, with clear connections to existing work and sound bibliographical references. The algorithm is introduced with an appropriate balance between technical numerical analysis/algebraic details and theoretical convergence guarantees. The numerical analysis is thorough, and the implementation demonstrates highly impressive performance.  

My sole concern pertains to reproducibility: if the source code (primarily built on open-source libraries) is not made publicly available, the work lacks practical impact. This is a sound algorithmic contribution addressing the critical practical challenge of covariance selection at massive scale (millions of variables). Such a powerful tool deserves publication if made available to the research community.
