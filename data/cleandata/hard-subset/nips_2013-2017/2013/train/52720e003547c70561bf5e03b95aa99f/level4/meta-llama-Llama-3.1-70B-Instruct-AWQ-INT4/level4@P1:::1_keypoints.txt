- Presents a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator.
- Proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME and proves convergence rates for objective and optimality conditions.
- Develops a distributed computation framework that scales to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters on hundreds of cores.
- Novel contribution: focuses on efficient distributed algorithm design for ultra-large-scale precision matrix estimation.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with detailed algorithmic description and useful notation.
- Experimental results are well-presented, compare to state-of-the-art, and demonstrate scalability.
- Notation could be improved (missing notation table; ρ and η not defined).
- Block-cyclic data distribution scheme needs clearer justification or empirical evidence for load balance and scalability.
- Column block size k is chosen ad-hoc; requires intuition or theory to guide selection.
- Communication and synchronization mechanisms (data distribution and parallelization) need more detailed explanation despite use of OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.
- Strengths: novelty, detailed algorithm/framework, demonstrated scalability.
- Weaknesses: unclear notation, insufficient support for block distribution claims, ad-hoc block sizing, and limited communication/synchronization detail.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the above concerns.
