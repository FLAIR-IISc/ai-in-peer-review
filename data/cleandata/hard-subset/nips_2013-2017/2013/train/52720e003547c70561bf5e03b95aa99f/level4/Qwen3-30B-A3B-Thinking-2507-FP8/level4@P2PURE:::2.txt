

This paper proposes an inexact alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) for solving the linear program in the CLIME estimator, establishing a convergence rate of $O(1/T)$. The algorithm is implemented using both shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures, with numerical comparisons against DC-QUIC, Tiger, and CLIME-Flare.  

Quality: The paper is technically sound.  
Minor comments:  
1) The derivation of the inexact ADMM for CLIME follows standard procedures (e.g., as detailed in He et al., "Inexact Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers," 2013).  
2) Due to the structure of CLIME, each subproblem admits a componentwise solution, with the dominant computational cost being matrix-matrix multiplication. Consequently, ADMM can be ideally parallelized for CLIME, and efficient parallelization of matrix-matrix multiplication is well-established.  
3) The experimental setup requires further specification, including the achieved accuracy for each solver.  

Clarity: The paper is well-organized.  
Originality: The evaluation of ADMM in shared-memory and distributed-memory architectures is novel and valuable.  
Significance: Solving large-scale sparse precision estimation remains challenging, and a simple, robust distributed algorithm is beneficial. While the inexact ADMM derivation is standard, the demonstrated scalability stems directly from CLIME’s structural simplicity.
