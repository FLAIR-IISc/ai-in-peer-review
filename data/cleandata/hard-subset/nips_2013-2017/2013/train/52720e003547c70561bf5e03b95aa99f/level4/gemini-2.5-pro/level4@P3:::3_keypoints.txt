- Problem: Estimating sparse precision matrices at ultra-large scale (up to millions of variables / ~10^12 parameters).
- Method: Novel inexact ADMM algorithm operating on blocks of columns for the CLIME estimator.
- Computational advantage: Block-wise approach turns the bottleneck into optimized matrix–matrix multiplications.
- Theory: Proven O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and residuals of the inexact algorithm.
- Implementation: Distributed framework for shared- and distributed-memory systems using block-cyclic data distribution.
- Structure exploitation: Method can leverage low-rank sample covariances and potential sparsity in data/intermediate iterates.
- Significance: Tackles a frontier, high-impact problem and achieves orders-of-magnitude scalability (10^6 dims in hours).
- Originality/quality: Novel algorithmic design and solid theoretical foundations for the inexact block ADMM approach.
- Systems contribution: Non-trivial, carefully engineered distributed implementation critical to performance.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly links design choices to performance benefits.
- Experiments: Comprehensive empirical evaluation vs. state-of-the-art (flare, DC-QUIC) showing superior scalability and near-linear speedup.
- Weakness — code availability: Recommend publicly releasing source code to maximize impact and reproducibility.
- Weakness — sparsity exploitation: Unclear whether sparsity-based speedups were implemented and experimentally validated.
- Weakness — model comparison: Tiger achieves better recall; authors should clarify the paper’s computational focus and not overclaim statistical superiority.
- Overall assessment: Excellent, impactful, and well-supported work with minor issues; clear candidate for acceptance.
