This paper presents a large-scale parallel algorithm for sparse precision matrix estimation based on the CLIME method. The authors propose a scalable variant, CLIME-ADMM, and a framework for its computation. The primary novelty lies in estimating the precision matrix in column blocks rather than column-by-column. The paper includes an empirical analysis comparing CLIME-ADMM to state-of-the-art methods like DC-QUIC, Tiger, and Flare, using an implementation based on OpenMPI and ScaLAPACK.

While the proposed method is interesting, the paper could be improved in several areas:

1.  **Clarity of Notation:** The notation is scattered across Sections 1, 2, and 3, which hinders readability. The authors should consider consolidating all notation into a single table for clarity. For instance, the parameters \rho and \eta appear to be used without a formal definition.

2.  **Justification for Block Cyclic Distribution:** The proposed block cyclic data distribution is an interesting idea. However, the paper does not sufficiently explain why this scheme should achieve better load balancing and scalability. This claim is not substantiated by empirical results, which would be necessary to validate its effectiveness.

3.  **Guidance on Parameter Selection:** The selection of the column block size, `k`, appears to be ad hoc. Is there any theoretical guidance or practical intuition for choosing an optimal value for this parameter? A discussion on the sensitivity of the algorithm's performance to this parameter would strengthen the paper.

4.  **"Distributed" vs. "Parallel" Implementation:** A more general concern is the classification of the proposed method as a "distributed" algorithm. Since the paper does not explicitly discuss mechanisms for message passing or inter-node communication, it might be more accurately described as a "parallel" implementation. Clarifying this distinction would be beneficial.

**Minor Corrections:**

*   Section 1: "...where \lambda is a tunning parameter" -> "...where \lambda is a **tuning** parameter"
*   Section 2: "CLIME is summerized in" -> "CLIME is **summarized** in"
*   Section 5.1: "As Tiger is parameter tunning free" -> "As Tiger is parameter **tuning** free"
