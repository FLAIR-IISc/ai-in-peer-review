- Strength: Addresses a timely problem — scalable high-dimensional precision matrix estimation.  
- Strength: Methodological contribution — reformulates CLIME with ADMM and processes column blocks to enable parallelism.  
- Strength: Practical emphasis — implementation leverages common HPC tools (MPI, ScaLAPACK).  
- Strength: Comparative evaluation includes appropriate strong baselines (DC-QUIC, TIGER, Flare).  

- Major concern 1 (Clarity & notation): Notation is scattered and some symbols (e.g., rho, eta) are undefined; provide a consolidated notation table, define all hyperparameters on first use, and state the per-block ADMM subproblems and optimization constraints compactly.  
- Major concern 2 (Block-cyclic distribution & scalability): The choice of block-cyclic distribution is not justified; provide analytical/empirical justification plus strong/weak scaling experiments and load-balance metrics (per-process workloads/Gantt charts), and discuss when block-cyclic outperforms block/cyclic given typical sparsity.  
- Major concern 3 (Choice of column block size k): k appears ad hoc; supply theoretical/heuristic tradeoffs (parallelism, locality, communication, convergence), sensitivity analysis of runtime/accuracy vs k, and a practical rule-of-thumb.  
- Major concern 4 (“Distributed” vs “parallel”): Communication patterns and synchronization are unspecified; describe message-passing scheme (collectives vs point-to-point), quantify per-iteration communication (messages/bytes) and bottlenecks, and clarify fault-tolerance assumptions or explicitly call it a parallel implementation.  
- Major concern 5 (Experimental details & fairness): Ensure baselines are fairly tuned/stopped with consistent criteria, report wall-clock times, iteration counts, and solution quality (sparsity, estimation error), and include ablations isolating ADMM penalty, over-relaxation, and k.  
- Major concern 6 (Exposition & reproducibility): Provide pseudocode with inputs/outputs and parameters, summarize computational complexity and per-process memory as functions of p, k, nnz, and release code/scripts if possible.  

- Minor comments/typos: Fix typos (“tunning”→“tuning”, “summerized”→“summarized”), use consistent capitalization (TIGER, ScaLAPACK), and ensure all symbols are defined at first mention with a caption or appendix table of notation.  

- Overall assessment: Promising and practical contribution, but needs clearer optimization exposition, principled treatment of k, and detailed communication/scaling experiments plus fairness/ablation studies to meet systems-and-algorithms standards.
