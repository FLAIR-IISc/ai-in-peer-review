- Proposes a scalable distributed algorithm for CLIME using an inexact ADMM variant and column-block decomposition.
- Leverages matrix-matrix multiplications and block-cyclic data distribution to improve efficiency and scalability.
- Targets the critical challenge of applying CLIME to ultra-large problems with millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters.
- Well-motivated identification of quadratic parameter scaling in precision matrix estimation as a major bottleneck.
- Sensible strategy to enhance scalability by solving CLIME in column blocks.
- Theoretical analysis provides convergence rates for the ADMM algorithm, adding rigor.
- Strong experimental evaluation on shared- and distributed-memory systems showing significant speedups.
- Demonstrates impressive scalability, including estimating a one-million-dimension precision matrix in 11 hours on 400 cores.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and uses consistent notation.
- Related work coverage is comprehensive and situates the contribution well.
- Comparisons with existing methods (CLIME, Tiger, DC-QUIC) are present but could be deeper and more explanatory.
- Lacks comprehensive guidance on selecting the column block size (k) in practice and its relation to sparsity and memory.
- Discussion of exploiting low-rank structure is brief and lacks implementation details and practical evaluation.
- Limited analysis of communication costs and patterns in the distributed-memory setting; more detail is needed on how overhead is minimized.
- Theoretical results could be strengthened by exploring tighter convergence bounds or conditions for faster convergence.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, presents a novel scalable method, has theoretical guarantees, strong experiments, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: comparisons, block-size guidance, low-rank exploitation, and communication analysis are insufficiently developed.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with practical impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing block-size selection and distributed communication analysis.
