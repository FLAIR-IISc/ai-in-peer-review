- Presents a distributed algorithm for CLIME using an inexact ADMM variant, novel column-block solving, and block-cyclic data distribution for ultra-high-dimensional precision estimation.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly motivated, and addresses memory and computational bottlenecks in scaling CLIME.
- Authors relate work to recent distributed optimization advances and provide clear notation and Algorithm 1; discussion of leveraging sparsity/low-rank structure is useful.
- Strength — Scalability: demonstrates near-linear scaling to ~1M dimensions and ~1T parameters on hundreds of cores.
- Strength — Novel column-block approach and matrix multiplication optimizations improve performance.
- Strength — Theoretical convergence rate analysis for the proposed ADMM variant is provided.
- Strength — Practical system-level considerations (load balancing, block-cyclic distribution, memory hierarchy) are addressed.
- Strength — Experimental comparison against CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC is reasonably comprehensive.
- Weakness — Limited novelty in the ADMM component; the inexact ADMM appears standard and lacks deep justification.
- Weakness — Experimental setup is overly complex (varying block sizes, shared vs distributed memory), making it hard to isolate key innovations.
- Weakness — Insufficient detail on parameter tuning for CLIME-ADMM; no sensitivity analysis for ρ and λ is reported.
- Weakness — Limited discussion of how the distributed/inexact implementation affects statistical guarantees and numerical stability.
- Weakness — Missing deeper comparison to related work on data distribution (e.g., GPBS [23]) and trade-offs of schemes.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a relevant problem, provides a practical scalable solution that outperforms baselines, includes convergence theory, and offers parallelization insights.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental algorithmic novelty, complex experiments, and incomplete treatment of parameter sensitivity and statistical implications.
- Overall: Solid, practically valuable paper recommended for acceptance contingent on addressing inexactness justification, simplifying experimental analysis, and discussing statistical guarantees.
