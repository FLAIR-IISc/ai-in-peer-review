- Presents a large-scale distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator.  
- Main claim: an inexact ADMM algorithm enables scaling to millions of dimensions and hundreds of machines.  
- Theoretical support: establishes convergence rates for objective and optimality residuals of the inexact ADMM.  
- Experimental support: evaluations on shared- and distributed-memory systems show substantially better scalability than baselines.  
- Empirical scaling is nearly linear with number of cores in presented experiments.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and consistent notation.  
- Thorough related-work review and correct, sound mathematical derivations.  
- Practical usefulness: addresses critical scaling challenges and is applicable to other large-scale constrained optimization problems.  
- Novelty: new approach combining inexact ADMM with a block cyclic distribution scheme for CLIME scaling.  
- Completeness and reproducibility: sufficient algorithmic and experimental details provided.  
- Limitations acknowledged: challenges for very large datasets and need for further tuning of block size and parameters.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, convincing experiments, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: could expand discussion of broader applications and provide deeper insight into computational complexity and scalability–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address limitations and complexity discussion.
