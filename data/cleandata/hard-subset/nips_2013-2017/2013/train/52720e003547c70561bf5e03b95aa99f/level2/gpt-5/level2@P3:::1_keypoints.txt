- Proposes a CLIME-specific linearized (inexact) ADMM that updates column blocks with closed forms, enabling BLAS-3 matrix-matrix kernels and avoiding explicit formation of the p×p covariance.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for objective suboptimality and feasibility residuals under the condition η ≥ ρ λmax(C)^2.  
- Exploits low-rank structure C = AA′ when n ≪ p to reduce multiplication cost from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k).  
- Leverages sparsity in iterates and a truncated covariance to further reduce computation.  
- Implements a distributed block-cyclic data/parameter layout (ScaLAPACK-style) achieving near-linear scaling to hundreds of cores.  
- Demonstrates solving p = 10^6 (≈10^12 parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Empirically compares to CLIME (flare), TIGER, and DC-QUIC for scaling and accuracy.  
- Main novelty is the combination: CLIME-tailored linearized ADMM, column-block updates, explicit O(1/T) rates, and a high-performance distributed implementation.  
- Connects a “safe” covariance truncation idea to prior thresholding work but frames it for CLIME’s deviation guarantees.  
- Strength: avoids O(p^2) storage by never forming C and uses low-rank multiplications.  
- Strength: clear algorithmic decomposition (elementwise ops + parallel matrix multiplies) and cache/BLAS-3 motivated column-blocking.  
- Strength: explicit convergence analysis clarifying when linearization is contractive.  
- Strength: thoughtful systems design with guidance on block sizes and core placement.  
- Strength: impressive extreme-scale experiments and near-linear speedups.  
- Weakness: limited statistical evaluation—insufficient estimation-error metrics and stability analysis, especially on real data.  
- Weakness: Theorem on safe truncation lacks constants, actionable guidance for choosing Δ, and empirical validation.  
- Weakness: notational inconsistencies and minor errors (augmented Lagrangian scaling, box(·) definition, scaled vs. unscaled multipliers) hurt reproducibility.  
- Weakness: comparisons focus on single-node baselines; no distributed column-wise ADMM baseline to isolate column-blocking benefits.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis of communication/memory tradeoffs and per-iteration communication volume.  
- Essential fix: correct notation/algorithm inconsistencies and provide precise pseudocode with stopping criteria and defaults.  
- Essential fix: add per-iteration communication/memory analysis (messages/words moved for A′X and AY).  
- Essential fix: expand statistical evaluation (spectral/Frobenius/L1 errors, support recovery/stability, subsampling).  
- Essential fix: provide practical guidance for choosing ρ, η, k, and block sizes.  
- Optional: empirically study truncation runtime vs. accuracy trade-offs and compare to a distributed column-wise ADMM baseline; release code for reproducibility.  
- Verdict: technically sound and high practical significance; with the suggested revisions (statistical evaluation, truncation validation, notation fixes, communication analysis) would be a strong submission.
