- Proposes a scalable, distributed inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME that solves optimization in column-blocks to exploit matrix-matrix multiplications.  
- Establishes convergence analysis with O(1/T) rates for the proposed algorithm.  
- Presents a large-scale computational framework for shared- and distributed-memory architectures using block-cyclic data distribution.  
- Empirically demonstrates near-linear speedup up to 400 cores and solves a p = 10^6 problem (trillion-parameter scale).  
- Experiments leverage low-rank structure; the connection to the paper’s sparsity-inducing perturbation (Theorem 3) is unclear.  
- Use of column-blocks and block-cyclic distribution is a technically effective design for load balance and memory-hierarchy efficiency.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates contributions in both statistical estimation and systems/HPC literature.  
- Work is complete, combining a novel algorithmic variant, theoretical analysis, distributed implementation, and thorough experiments.  
- Paper provides little concrete guidance or heuristics for choosing the critical column-block size k.  
- Claim that block-diagonal covariance structure can be easily incorporated to match DC-QUIC gains is not substantiated with experiments or detailed explanation.  
- Overall assessment: significant, technically solid, and influential advance with strong empirical evidence; strong recommendation for acceptance.
