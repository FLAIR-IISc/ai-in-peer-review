- Tackles extreme-scale sparse precision estimation by adapting CLIME into a column-block linearized/inexact ADMM with distributed block-cyclic implementation.  
- Leverages sparse iterates and low-rank sample covariance (C = AA^T, n ≪ p) to reduce computation and memory.  
- Updates reduce to elementwise operations or matrix-matrix multiplies, enabling efficient distributed execution.  
- Establishes O(1/T) convergence rates for objective and optimality residuals under η ≥ ρ‖C‖_2^2.  
- Proposes small-entry thresholding of C and argues it preserves CLIME’s statistical deviation bounds.  
- Experiments compare to CLIME (flare), TIGER, and DC-QUIC on synthetic and real data, showing near-linear core scaling.  
- Demonstrates solving p ≈ 10^6 (≈10^12 parameters) in ≈11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Positions work relative to CLIME theory, scalable precision estimation (QUIC/DC-QUIC), ADMM literature, and parallel systems.  
- Core novelty: CLIME-specific linearized ADMM with column-blocking and a systems-aware distributed implementation.  
- Theorem 1: proves an averaged-iterate bound but does not explicitly connect to original box-constrained feasible set or optimality gap — needs clarification on feasibility.  
- Theorem 2: gives a clear residual bound using a KKT point and condition (η/ρ)I − C^2 ≽ 0; practical stopping criteria discussion is lacking.  
- Statistical truncation argument is reasonable; deterministic thresholding bound |∆_ij| ≤ c√(log p/n) should be emphasized as primary justification.  
- Empirical system-level scalability is compelling and detailed (block sizes, process grids).  
- Comparisons tuned for matching sparsity are fair for runtime but insufficient for statistical accuracy; TIGER shows better recall in synthetic tests.  
- Recommend reporting edge F1/AUC or ‖Ω̂−Ω_0‖ errors and held-out likelihoods for real data to assess accuracy alongside runtime.  
- Missing reporting of communication volume per iteration/block and a complexity model to clarify scaling limits.  
- Clarify practical choices: stopping conditions, selection/estimation of ρ and η, and baseline implementation details (column-by-column, warm starts).  
- Discuss memory footprint, I/O time, and fault tolerance/checkpointing for extreme-scale (p = 10^6) runs.  
- Suggest comparison against parallel column-wise CLIME to isolate benefits of column-blocking vs simple parallelism.  
- Overall assessment: strong systems-oriented contribution with principled algorithm and impressive scalability; clarify Theorem 1 and enrich statistical/communication evaluations before publication.
