- Proposes a scalable distributed framework for sparse precision matrix estimation using the CLIME estimator for problems with millions of dimensions.  
- Introduces an inexact ADMM algorithm tailored for CLIME.  
- Proposes a column-block ADMM approach that leverages matrix-matrix operations for improved cache efficiency.  
- Develops a distributed framework using block-cyclic data distribution for better load balancing.  
- Significantly improves scalability, addressing the quadratic parameter growth with dimensionality.  
- Theoretical analysis (Theorems 1–2) provides convergence rates.  
- Experimental results demonstrate estimating a 1M-dimensional precision matrix (1 trillion parameters) in 11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Empirical performance substantially outperforms state-of-the-art methods on large-scale problems.  
- Block-cyclic distribution is well-justified and yields better load balancing than alternatives.  
- Theoretical contribution is somewhat incremental, with convergence rates similar to prior work ([26], [12]).  
- Experimental comparison with DC-QUIC lacks quantitative metrics to support claims about its slowdown with increasing sparsity.  
- Low-rank acceleration (Section 3.2) is mentioned but its empirical impact is not demonstrated.  
- The claim of "almost linear scaling" is overstated; speedups show diminishing returns at high core counts, especially in shared-memory settings.  
- The paper should more precisely quantify scaling behavior and relate theoretical rates to practical parameter choices (e.g., η).  
- Well-organized and clearly written, with consistent notation, pseudocode, complexity analysis, and effective figures.  
- Column-block ADMM idea is not entirely novel, but its application to CLIME and the distributed analysis is a significant and useful contribution.  
- Ability to handle unprecedented problem scale (1M dimensions) is a meaningful advance and the framework could generalize to other large constrained optimization problems.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, technically sound and practically significant; minor issues are fixable and I recommend acceptance.
