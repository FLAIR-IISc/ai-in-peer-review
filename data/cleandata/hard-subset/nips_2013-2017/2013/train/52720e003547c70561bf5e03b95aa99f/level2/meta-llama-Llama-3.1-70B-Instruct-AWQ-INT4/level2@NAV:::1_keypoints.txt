- Summary: Proposes a scalable distributed inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME sparse precision estimation with convergence guarantees and a framework that scales to millions of dimensions.  
- Claim 1: CLIME-ADMM is scalable and efficient for large-scale sparse precision matrix estimation.  
- Claim 2: The algorithm attains O(1/T) convergence for the objective and optimality residuals.  
- Claim 3: The distributed framework can scale to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores.  
- Support: Theoretical convergence analysis and experimental results on synthetic and real datasets back the claims.  
- Usefulness: Practically valuable for large-scale precision estimation in domains like finance, biology, and networks.  
- Field knowledge: Builds on established ML/optimization techniques (ADMM, block cyclic distribution) with appropriate citations.  
- Novelty: Novel combination of inexact ADMM and block-cyclic distribution for scalable precision estimation.  
- Completeness: Provides sufficient algorithmic and framework details for reproducibility.  
- Limitations: Requires many cores for scalability and experiments are limited to a specific set of datasets.  
- Constructive criticism: Broaden experimental evaluation to more diverse datasets to better demonstrate generality.  
- Overall assessment: Significant contribution with solid theory and experiments; clear and well-written.  
- Pro arguments: Novel, effective approach with clear exposition and demonstrated scalability.  
- Con arguments: Limited experimental diversity and dependence on large compute resources.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to expand experimental validation.
