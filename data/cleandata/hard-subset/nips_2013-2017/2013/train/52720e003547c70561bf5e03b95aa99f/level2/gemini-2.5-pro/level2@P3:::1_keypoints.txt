- Tackles sparse precision matrix estimation at extreme scale, enabling estimation with millions of dimensions and ~10^12 parameters.
- Proposes a highly scalable, distributed inexact ADMM algorithm (CLIME-ADMM) for the CLIME estimator formulated as a linear program.
- Introduces column-block optimization replacing column-wise solves, turning core work into efficient matrix-matrix multiplications that exploit low-rank sample covariance (n << p).
- Develops a distributed computation framework for shared- and distributed-memory systems using block-cyclic data distribution for load balance and memory-efficiency.
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis showing an O(1/T) convergence rate for the algorithm.
- Demonstrates extensive empirical scalability and efficiency, outperforming existing methods and showing near-linear speedup with added cores.
- Builds on prior work (graphical lasso, CLIME solvers, ADMM) but is original in synthesizing a system tailored to CLIME at unprecedented scale.
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and supported by helpful diagrams and interpretable experimental results.
- Significant practical and theoretical impact by making ultra-high-dimensional sparse precision estimation feasible.
- Technically sound algorithmic design and strong implementation engineering leveraging parallel linear algebra techniques.
- Disconnect between theory and experiments: unclear whether the sparsity-inducing perturbation (Theorem 3) was used in experiments or is only theoretical.
- Lacks discussion and guidance on selection and sensitivity of key hyperparameters (ρ, η, block size k), hurting reproducibility.
- Baseline comparisons are strong for moderate sizes but do not fully address the paper’s claimed distributed-scale regime; more discussion on why general distributed frameworks fail would help.
- Suggest framing the perturbation result as future work if not used empirically, and add guidance on hyperparameter trade-offs (e.g., block size k) for different architectures.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a major challenge, novel and effective algorithm-system combination, impressive large-scale empirical results, solid theory and implementation.
- Arguments against acceptance: minor clarity gap on practical use of theoretical sparsity results and limited detail on hyperparameter tuning.
