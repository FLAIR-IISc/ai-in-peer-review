- Proposes a distributed column-block ADMM algorithm for CLIME enabling sparse precision estimation at ultra-high dimensions (e.g., 1M), with O(1/T) convergence.  
- Uses block-cyclic data distribution and decomposition into independent column-blocks for scalable distributed implementation.  
- Exploits low-rank structure via C = A A^T to reduce matrix multiplication complexity from O(p²k) to O(n p k).  
- Achieves near-linear scalability on shared and distributed memory systems, demonstrated on 400 cores (11 hours for 1M dims).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, providing convergence rates for objective and feasibility residuals under inexact ADMM.  
- Framework addresses prior scalability/memory bottlenecks and balances workload while minimizing cache misses.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: synthetic and real datasets show superior scalability vs. CLIME, Tiger, and DC-QUIC.  
- Situates work well in literature and avoids overstating novelty.  
- Theorem 3’s justification for covariance perturbation ∆ preserving statistical deviation bounds is insufficiently detailed.  
- Real-data comparisons omit some post-2013 methods (e.g., recent QUIC variants), limiting benchmarking significance.  
- No theoretical guidance for selecting column-block size k; only empirical tuning is provided.  
- Discussion of cache effects is descriptive and lacks a principled selection criterion or formula.  
- Recommended revisions: clarify Theorem 3 proof to bound ∆_ij tails, provide guidance/analysis for choosing k relative to hardware, and add comparison to at least one recent method.  
- Overall: strong, well-supported contribution that bridges theory and practice; minor theoretical and benchmarking gaps do not undermine acceptance.
