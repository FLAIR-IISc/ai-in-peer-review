- Proposes a scalable distributed framework for CLIME using an inexact ADMM that processes column blocks for efficient parallelization and load balancing.  
- Evaluated on shared- and distributed-memory systems, showing substantial scalability and near-linear speedup with cores.  
- Establishes theoretical convergence rates for the proposed algorithm (objective and optimality conditions).  
- Leverages sparsity and low-rank structure in the sample covariance to reduce computational complexity.  
- Demonstrates scalability to millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters, outperforming existing methods empirically.  
- Algorithmic descriptions and proofs are dense and could use clearer exposition or illustrative diagrams.  
- Paper insufficiently discusses limitations such as dependence on precision-matrix sparsity and sensitivity to hyperparameters (block size, λ).  
- Comparison with related methods (e.g., Tiger, DC-QUIC) is limited, lacking detailed theoretical and practical trade-off analysis.  
- Reproducibility is weak due to missing implementation details (parameter settings, hardware configurations).  
- Addresses a critical challenge in high-dimensional statistics and offers a significant contribution to scalable precision matrix estimation.  
- Method is both theoretically grounded and empirically validated with strong scalability and efficiency results.  
- Work is highly relevant to the community interested in large-scale optimization and distributed computing.  
- Could be improved for accessibility for readers unfamiliar with ADMM or distributed optimization.  
- Lack of detailed limitations and reproducibility details may hinder broader adoption despite strong results.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested improvements to clarity and more detailed discussion of limitations and implementation for maximal impact.
