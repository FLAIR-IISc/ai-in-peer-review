- Paper proposes an inexact ADMM algorithm for CLIME and establishes convergence rates for the objective and optimality conditions.  
- Authors develop a scalable distributed framework that handles millions of dimensions and trillions of parameters using hundreds of cores.  
- Method scales almost linearly with number of cores and is substantially more scalable than state-of-the-art methods.  
- Experimental results demonstrate scalability, efficiency, and strong empirical performance on large-scale datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough introduction and explanation of methods.  
- Technical contributions are sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Major strength: ability to scale to ultra-high-dimensional problems not handled by existing methods.  
- Originality is moderate: distributed/parallel approach is not entirely new, but implementation and results are novel.  
- Significance is high: method is practical for large-scale machine learning applications.  
- Weakness: no comparison with other distributed optimization methods that could be applied to the problem.  
- Weakness: assumption that the sample covariance matrix is low-rank, which may not hold in practice.  
- Suggestion: explore alternative approaches or regularizations for handling high-rank sample covariance matrices.  
- Recommendation: with additional comparisons and exploration of high-rank cases, paper could be a top-tier publication.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
