- Paper targets ultra-high-dimensional sparse precision estimation (CLIME) with a scalable inexact ADMM solving column-blocks.  
- Derives closed-form elementwise updates: soft-thresholding for X and box projection for Z, reducing bottleneck to matrix multiplications.  
- Proves O(1/T) convergence rates for objective suboptimality and optimality residuals under η ≥ ρ λ_max(C)^2.  
- Exploits low-rank sample covariance C = AA^T to reduce matmul cost from O(p^2 k) to O(n p k) when p >> n.  
- Implements block-cyclic data/parameter distribution and parallel/distributed solutions for load balance and cache efficiency.  
- Discusses leveraging sparsity in iterates and a perturbed sample covariance to further accelerate computation.  
- Empirical results show strong/near-linear speedups on shared and distributed memory, including p = 10^6 solved in ~11 hours on 400 cores.  
- Compares scalability favorably to TIGER, CLIME (flare), and DC-QUIC, though objectives differ across methods.  
- Algorithmic derivation is standard but sound (linearized ADMM with proximal term yields closed-form updates).  
- Low-rank multiplication and block-cyclic distribution are technically appropriate and well-justified.  
- Convergence theory matches known non-ergodic linearized ADMM rates; step-size condition is explicit.  
- Theory gap: limited practical guidance on selecting ρ and η and no experimental sensitivity analysis or stopping-criterion guidance.  
- Theory gap: symmetrization of column-wise CLIME solutions and handling of PD/symmetry in block setting not specified.  
- Theory gap: Theorem 3 on covariance perturbation is high-level and does not quantify end-to-end impact on CLIME estimation error or PSD/symmetry preservation.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough on scalability but limited on statistical accuracy—PR curves only on synthetic data and few estimation error/objective comparisons.  
- Missing experimental details: memory usage, I/O overhead, and communication costs for largest runs are not reported.  
- Clarity issues: need explicit description of symmetrization/PD postprocessing, scaled vs unscaled ADMM notation inconsistencies, and practical parameter choices.  
- Originality: components are known, but their integrated column-block ADMM + low-rank + HPC systems design for distributed CLIME at this scale is a meaningful contribution.  
- Significance: enables precision estimation at previously unreachable scales; primarily a computational/systems contribution with statistical guarantees inherited from CLIME.  
- Pros: addresses important scalability bottleneck, closed-form updates, solid HPC implementation, impressive large-scale experiments, and reusable framework.  
- Cons: insufficient guidance on parameters/stopping, unspecified symmetrization/PD handling, perturbation theory not fully developed or empirically validated, and mixed-objective method comparisons.  
- Suggested improvements: add symmetrization/PD postprocessing section and its runtime/accuracy impact.  
- Suggested improvements: provide practical defaults and sensitivity studies for ρ, η, k, stopping tolerances, and residual-vs-iteration plots.  
- Suggested improvements: include estimation error metrics (Frobenius/spectral) and objective comparisons on synthetic data.  
- Suggested improvements: expand perturbation theory or provide empirical validation showing negligible statistical degradation for truncation/sparsification.  
- Suggested improvements: report memory, I/O, communication breakdowns and parallel I/O/data format for reproducibility; clean up ADMM notation.  
- Overall: strong systems-oriented contribution with sound algorithms and convincing scalability; some theoretical and empirical details need strengthening.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept to accept.
