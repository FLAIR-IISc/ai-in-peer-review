- Introduces a novel method for inferring dynamics of large neural populations by stitching sequential, overlapping subnetworks via a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Addresses a major neuroscience limitation: inability to simultaneously record thousands of neurons.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on simulated data and real mouse somatosensory calcium imaging, predicting noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.  
- Situates work within prior literature and distinguishes itself by targeting non-simultaneous recordings.  
- Strength: Conceptually elegant framing of stitching as a missing-data latent dynamical system.  
- Strength: Potentially high impact by enabling analysis of much larger circuits than current imaging allows.  
- Strength: Empirical validation on both simulated and real datasets strengthens claims.  
- Strength: Clear motivation linking the method to experimental constraints.  
- Weakness: Relies on linearity and Gaussianity assumptions without detailed robustness analysis or failure-mode characterization.  
- Weakness: Unclear theoretical explanation of what additional information stitching provides beyond collecting more data from a larger single recording.  
- Weakness: Requires substantial overlap between recordings (≈60% in experiments) without sufficient explanation of why overlap is necessary.  
- Weakness: Predicted noise correlations may reflect shared latent inputs rather than direct couplings; ambiguity in interpreting inferred couplings.  
- Weakness: Insufficient mathematical/technical detail (particularly the EM M-step), hindering reproducibility and assessment.  
- Weakness: Imbalanced presentation—extensive introduction/discussion but sparse technical derivations and implementation detail.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel solution to a significant problem, supported by simulations and real data, and well-motivated framing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks robustness analysis, unclear incremental benefit and overlap dependence, and insufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Overall: Promising and potentially impactful but requires revisions on robustness, theory, overlap justification, latent-input confounds, and fuller mathematical exposition.
