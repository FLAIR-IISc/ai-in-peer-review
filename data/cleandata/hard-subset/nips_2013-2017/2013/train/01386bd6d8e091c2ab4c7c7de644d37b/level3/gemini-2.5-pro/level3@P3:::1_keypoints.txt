- Paper presents a statistical "stitching" method that models sequential partial recordings as missing observations in a linear Gaussian dynamical system and uses EM to infer full-circuit dynamics.  
- Validated on simulated data and real mouse calcium imaging; key claim is recovery of pairwise noise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously.  
- Significance/originality: tackles a major experimental limitation and offers a principled, scalable approach for integrating non-simultaneous recordings.  
- Technical quality: appropriate LDS formulation, sensible EM estimation, and a smart "fictional imaging scenario" with upper- and lower-bound baselines for evaluation.  
- Strong core result: stitching recovers cross-session pairwise noise correlations nearly as well as a fully observed upper-bound model and much better than a naive baseline.  
- Weakness: large unexplained drop in coupling matrix recovery from simulation (r=0.73) to real data (r=0.38), undermining claims about recovering underlying functional couplings.  
- Weakness: method required ~20% neuron overlap in real-data experiments despite simulated success without overlap; this practical requirement is insufficiently emphasized.  
- Weakness: figures and legends are sparse and could be more self-contained and better annotated to improve clarity (e.g., explicitly label simultaneous vs non-simultaneous quadrants).  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should discuss causes of simulation vs real-data discrepancies (e.g., model mismatch, non-stationarity).  
- Recommendation: explicitly state and analyze the impact of neuron overlap on performance, ideally showing degradation as overlap decreases.  
- Recommendation: revise figures and legends to be more descriptive and immediately interpretable.
