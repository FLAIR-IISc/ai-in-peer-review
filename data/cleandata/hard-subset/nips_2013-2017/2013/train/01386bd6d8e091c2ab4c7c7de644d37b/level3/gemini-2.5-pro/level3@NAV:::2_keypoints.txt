- Introduces a novel method to "stitch" sequential, partial neural recordings by fitting a latent linear dynamical system.  
- Enables estimation of noise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously, addressing a critical experimental limitation.  
- Conceptually original and significant: expands effective field of view and is broadly applicable (e.g., to point-process data).  
- Validated on simulated and real calcium-imaging data with clear benchmarking against "fully observed" and "naive" baselines.  
- Methods section lacks technical depth and detailed derivations, notably for the EM algorithm M-step.  
- Insufficient methodological detail undermines reproducibility and obscures the precise technical novelty.  
- Paper does not analyze robustness to violations of the linear-Gaussian assumptions or provide intuition about failure modes under nonlinear dynamics.  
- Conceptual ambiguity about the incremental benefit of adding more subsets versus more data from a single subset.  
- Empirical need for overlapping populations is mentioned but not explained — why overlap is critical is unclear.  
- Learned couplings may reflect common latent inputs rather than direct pairwise interactions; Figure 3b shows a relatively poor match that needs deeper interpretation.  
- Overall: promising and important idea, but needs a thorough revision with detailed derivations and a more critical discussion of assumptions, limitations, and interpretability.
