- Proposes a statistical “stitching” method modeling sequential recordings as a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Uses a linear Gaussian dynamical system and EM for tractable parameter estimation.  
- Validated on simulated and real calcium imaging data; can predict noise correlations between neurons never simultaneously recorded.  
- Novel focus on non-simultaneous recordings, distinguishing it from prior connectivity inference methods.  
- Simulated data shows strong recovery of couplings and high correlation in predicted noise correlations (corr ≈ 0.73).  
- Real data shows much lower performance in predicted correlations (corr ≈ 0.38), a concerning and unexplained discrepancy.  
- Paper should more explicitly relate its implementation to existing missing-data/dynamical-systems theory (citations [21–23]).  
- Performance depends on substantial overlap between imaging sessions; the trade-offs between overlap, coverage, and accuracy are not sufficiently explored.  
- Clarity issues in figure legends and descriptions (e.g., Figure 3a caption and the “fictional imaging scenario” in Sec 2.3) hinder accessibility.  
- Strengths: novel solution to a fundamental problem, solid statistical framework, and potential to scale circuit-level analyses.  
- Weaknesses: unexplained sim-vs-real discrepancy, overlap requirement limiting applicability, and insufficient explanatory detail.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the discrepancy, analyzing overlap effects, and improving figure/experimental clarity.
