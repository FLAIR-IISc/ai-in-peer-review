- Presents a novel statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neurons by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Proposes a simple linear Gaussian dynamical system with four parameters estimated via an EM variant.  
- Validated on simulated and experimental calcium imaging data from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Demonstrates ability to infer noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs and outperform a naive baseline.  
- Addresses a major limitation of current imaging by enabling study of larger neural population dynamics.  
- Strength: original contribution tackling inability to simultaneously record large neuron populations.  
- Strength: novel capability to infer functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Strength: thorough validation on both simulated and real datasets showing robustness and practical utility.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing that makes methods and results accessible.  
- Weakness: method depends on population overlap; trade-offs between overlap and total coverage are not fully explored.  
- Weakness: figures lack sufficient descriptions/legends and are not adequately discussed in the text.  
- Weakness: linear Gaussian model is a simplification that does not capture nonlinearities or spiking dynamics.  
- Arg for acceptance: high novelty, significant potential impact, rigorous development, and practical adoption potential.  
- Arg against acceptance: scalability concerns due to overlap dependence, model simplicity limiting applicability, and presentation clarity issues.  
- Overall: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to improve figure clarity and discuss overlap and model limitations.
