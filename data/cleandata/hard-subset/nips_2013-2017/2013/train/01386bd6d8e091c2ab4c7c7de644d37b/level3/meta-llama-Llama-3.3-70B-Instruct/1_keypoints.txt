- Introduces DisDCA, a distributed stochastic dual coordinate ascent for regularized loss minimization solving the dual problem.  
- Provides strong convergence guarantees for both smooth and non-smooth loss functions.  
- Well-written with a clear problem statement and algorithm description.  
- Thorough analysis of the computation–communication tradeoff in distributed optimization.  
- Offers a practical variant of DisDCA that improves performance over the basic version.  
- Experimental results show competitiveness with other distributed methods (e.g., SGD, ADMM).  
- Novel contribution by bridging stochastic dual coordinate ascent and distributed optimization.  
- Significant potential impact for distributed regularized loss minimization applications.  
- Lacks sufficient implementation details for DisDCA and its variants.  
- Experiments are limited—need broader comparisons to other algorithms and more datasets.  
- Assumes a star network topology, which may limit applicability to other distributed setups.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing experiments and implementation details.
