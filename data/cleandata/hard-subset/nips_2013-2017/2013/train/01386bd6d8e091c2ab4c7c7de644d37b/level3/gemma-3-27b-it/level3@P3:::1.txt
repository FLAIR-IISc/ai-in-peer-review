This paper presents a novel statistical method for combining sequentially imaged neuronal populations to expand analyzable population sizes and characterize circuit dynamics beyond the limits of simultaneous imaging. The approach frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a linear Gaussian model, estimating parameters via a variant of the EM algorithm. This builds upon prior work in inferring neural connectivity from calcium imaging [6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11] but uniquely addresses the challenge of non-simultaneous recordings. It also differentiates itself from methods dealing with subsampled observations [12, 13, 14, 15] by specifically tackling the scenario where imaging sessions are long relative to neural dynamics.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation and methodology. The mathematical formulation is concise, and the application to both simulated and real data provides a compelling demonstration of the method's potential. The core contribution – effectively recovering pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously imaged neurons – is significant, as it offers a promising path towards overcoming fundamental limitations of current imaging techniques and scaling up circuit-level analyses. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The approach of "stitching" together sequential recordings to infer functional connections is original and fills a gap in existing methods.
* **Soundness:** The use of a latent dynamical system and EM algorithm provides a statistically principled framework.
* **Demonstrated Performance:** The method works well on simulated data and shows encouraging results on real calcium imaging data.
* **Significance:** The potential to expand the scale of analyzable neural circuits is highly valuable to the field.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Discrepancy between Simulation and Real Data:** The significantly better correlation recovery in simulated data (Fig. 2c) compared to real data (Fig. 3b) is a major concern. The authors do not adequately explain this discrepancy, which casts doubt on the generalizability of the method.  The authors note overlap is critical for real data performance, but the magnitude of the difference remains unexplained.
* **Insufficient Figure Detail:**  Figure legends and the surrounding text lack sufficient description and elaboration. For example, the criteria for selecting the 50 neurons displayed in Figure 3a are not clearly stated, hindering full understanding of the results. Details about stimulus presentation and animal behavior are similarly lacking context.
* **Limited Exploration of Model Assumptions:** The paper relies on a linear Gaussian model, which is a simplification of the complex dynamics of neural circuits. While acknowledged, the potential impact of these assumptions on the accuracy and robustness of the method is not thoroughly explored.
* **Dependence on Overlap:** The requirement for substantial overlap between imaging sessions limits the applicability of the method.  The authors mention 60% overlap was used, but a more detailed analysis of performance across varying overlap levels would be valuable.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant challenge in neuroscience: scaling up circuit analysis beyond the limitations of simultaneous imaging.
* The proposed method is novel, statistically sound, and demonstrably effective on simulated data.
* The preliminary results on real data, while imperfect, are promising and suggest the potential for broader applicability.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presents the methodology and findings.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The unexplained discrepancy between simulation and real data raises concerns about the robustness and generalizability of the method.
* The lack of detailed figure descriptions hinders understanding and reproducibility.
* The reliance on substantial overlap between imaging sessions limits the practical applicability of the method.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper with a potentially significant contribution to the field. However, the unexplained discrepancy between simulation and real data is a serious weakness that needs to be addressed. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on the authors providing a more thorough explanation for this discrepancy and improving the clarity and detail of the figure descriptions. A discussion of the implications of the linear Gaussian assumption and a more detailed analysis of the influence of overlap on performance would further strengthen the paper.  

**Quality:** 7/10 - Technically sound, but the unexplained discrepancy lowers the score.
**Clarity:** 8/10 - Generally well-written, but figure descriptions need improvement.
**Originality:** 9/10 - Novel approach to a significant problem.
**Significance:** 8/10 - High potential impact on the field.
