- Proposes a novel statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets into one joint model, expanding analyzable population size beyond simultaneous imaging.  
- Formulates the problem as a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a linear Gaussian model.  
- Estimates model parameters (coupling, receptive fields, noise covariances) via an EM variant.  
- Validated on simulated and experimental calcium imaging data, showing recovery of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Effectively recovers pairwise correlations between neuron pairs not measured simultaneously.  
- Real-data performance requires substantial overlap between imaged populations for reasonable results.  
- Demonstrates potential to overcome limits of simultaneous imaging and scale up circuit-level analyses.  
- Unexplained discrepancy: large difference in correlation results between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b).  
- Paper could benefit from more detailed figure legends and figure-related text to improve interpretability.  
- Reliance on a linear Gaussian model may limit applicability to more complex neural dynamics.
