- Problem: Current 2-photon calcium imaging cannot record thousands of neurons simultaneously; paper proposes "stitching" sequential recordings to infer full population dynamics.  
- Approach: Frame stitching as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations to recover correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Novelty: Novel contribution in combining non-simultaneous recordings without the strong assumptions or simultaneous-measurement requirements of prior methods.  
- Strength: Simulations convincingly recover coupling matrices and noise correlations (0.92 correlation between predicted and true correlations).  
- Strength: Real data validation on mouse somatosensory cortex shows stitched model correlations align with fully observed model (0.84 correlation) versus poor naive performance (0.15).  
- Strength: Solid theoretical grounding in latent dynamical systems and appropriate EM-based inference.  
- Weakness: Large unexplained discrepancy between simulated (0.92) and real data (0.38) correlation results is not analyzed.  
- Weakness: Requirement for substantial overlap in real data (c=60%) is not adequately justified, especially given simulations worked with non-overlapping sets.  
- Weakness: Figure legends and text lack sufficient detail to understand experimental setup and the fictional imaging scenario (Section 3.2).  
- Quality: Methodologically sound but the unexplained sim-vs-real performance gap is a major quality concern.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written and organized, but key experimental details and figures need clearer explanations.  
- Originality: Distinct from prior sub-sampling work and addresses a practical experimental limitation in a novel way.  
- Significance: High potential impact by enabling circuit-level analyses of much larger neural populations than current simultaneous imaging allows.  
- Argument for acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded method with demonstrated success on simulated and real data and clear practical relevance.  
- Argument against acceptance: Serious unresolved discrepancy between simulation and real-data performance, unjustified overlap requirement, and insufficient figure/detail clarity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions to explain the sim-vs-real discrepancy, justify overlap requirements, and improve figure legends and related text.
