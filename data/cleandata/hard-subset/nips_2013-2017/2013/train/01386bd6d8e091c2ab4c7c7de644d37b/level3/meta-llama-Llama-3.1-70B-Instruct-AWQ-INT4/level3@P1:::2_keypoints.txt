- Proposes a statistical "stitching" method to infer dynamics of large neural populations from sequentially recorded subnetworks.  
- Method combines multiple partial observations to estimate a joint model capturing correlation structure and stimulus selectivity.  
- Differs from prior functional-connectivity work by focusing on non-simultaneously observed neurons.  
- Validated on simulated data and mouse somatosensory cortex recordings.  
- Demonstrates ability to predict correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs, even under weak stimulus drive.  
- Main contribution: a procedure for stitching partial recordings to recover joint population activity models.  
- Strength: novel approach with potential broad applicability and extension to point-process data.  
- Strength: accurate cross-subset correlation prediction shown in experiments.  
- Limitation: robustness and failure modes when assumptions are violated are unclear.  
- Limitation: unclear incremental benefit of incorporating multiple subsets.  
- Limitation: method requires overlapping populations, but the necessity and requirements are not fully explained.  
- Limitation: real-data results are ambiguous and could reflect common inputs/latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings.  
- Quality: technically sound but needs more detailed derivations and clearer estimation procedure exposition.  
- Presentation: imbalanced, with excessive introductory/discussion material and insufficient technical detail.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions — provide clearer derivations, explain benefits of multiple subsets and overlap requirements, and clarify interpretation of real-data results.
