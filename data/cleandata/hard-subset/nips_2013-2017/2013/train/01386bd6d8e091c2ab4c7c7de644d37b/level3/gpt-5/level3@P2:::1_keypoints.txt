- Proposes stitching sequentially imaged, partially overlapping neural populations by treating unobserved neurons as missing entries in a single linear Gaussian state-space model (LDS) learned via EM and inferred via Kalman smoothing.  
- Builds on prior latent dynamical/state-space and spike/calcium modeling literature but addresses novel cross-session alignment and noise-correlation prediction for non-simultaneously observed pairs.  
- Synthetic experiments show strong recovery of cross-session couplings and noise correlations (r ≈ 0.92); real data outperforms a naïve baseline and approaches a fully observed upper bound (e.g., r ≈ 0.84 vs. 0.15).  
- Assumes linear Gaussian continuous dynamics, diagonal Q, stationary dynamics, and known neuron identity across sessions — pragmatic but restrictive for real, nonstationary awake data.  
- Real-data performance required substantial session overlap, unlike simulations; this discrepancy is noted but not deeply analyzed.  
- EM methodology is appropriate but initialization, regularization (e.g., A stability, sparsity), convergence diagnostics, and computational scaling (N, K, T) are underreported.  
- Manuscript is generally clear, but figure legends and methods need more detail on preprocessing, train/test splits, overlap effects, error bars, and online EM hyperparameters.  
- Originality: novel and useful framing of stitching via missing-entry LDS and alignment via overlap; distinct from existing subsampling/connectivity work.  
- Significance: addresses an important experimental bottleneck and is likely extensible, but impact is tempered by overlap dependence and simulation–real performance gap.  
- Strength: clear formulation, strong synthetic validation, real-data gains, and thorough benchmarking against upper/lower bounds.  
- Weakness: lack of systematic analysis of overlap fraction, session length, SNR, stimulus drive, and model mismatch (nonlinearity, nonstationarity, correlated noise).  
- Suggestion: analyze performance vs. overlap/SNR/stimulus, test low-rank/shared-noise Q, and compare to multi-view latent and subspace-identification baselines.  
- Suggestion: report regularization/identifiability choices, parameter uncertainty (e.g., bootstrap CIs), and effect of sparsity/stability constraints on A.  
- Suggestion: justify using fluorescence vs. deconvolved spikes and report whether deconvolution affects stitching accuracy.  
- Suggestion: provide computational run-time/memory scaling and practicality for thousands of neurons.  
- Questions: what minimum overlap is needed for reliable predictions under realistic noise, and how does this scale with SNR and stimulus richness?  
- Questions: was A constrained to be stable in EM, how was this enforced, and how sensitive are results to initialization/local optima?  
- Questions: can the method handle low-rank Q or non-diagonal R, and how robust is neuron identity matching across sessions?  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, practical solution with strong synthetic results and meaningful real-data gains; framework likely to be adopted.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient analysis of overlap requirement and simulation–real gap, missing baselines/ablations, and incomplete methodological/computational details.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — original and impactful but requires additional analyses, baselines, and methodological clarification.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
