- Proposes a novel "stitching" method that fits a latent dynamical system with missing observations to combine sequentially imaged neurons.  
- Validated on simulated data and real calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Main contribution: ability to predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.  
- Method is broadly applicable and can be used with different data types, including point-process data.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, with a detailed, easy-to-follow methods section.  
- Results are well-structured with clear figures and tables that communicate findings effectively.  
- Concern about robustness: method may be limited to near-linear Gaussian data and lacks intuition on failure modes when assumptions are violated.  
- Paper lacks a clear explanation of the overlap requirement needed for application to real datasets.  
- Unclear incremental benefit: unclear how much additional coupling information multiple subsets provide versus more data from a single subset.  
- Builds on prior work (Pillow & Latham) but the approach is distinct; originality is not complete novel.  
- Overall assessment: significant and useful contribution but with important limitations and open questions to address.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, powerful idea; broad applicability; clear presentation; effective empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted assumptions, unclear overlap requirement, unclear practical gains from multiple subsets, and partial novelty.
