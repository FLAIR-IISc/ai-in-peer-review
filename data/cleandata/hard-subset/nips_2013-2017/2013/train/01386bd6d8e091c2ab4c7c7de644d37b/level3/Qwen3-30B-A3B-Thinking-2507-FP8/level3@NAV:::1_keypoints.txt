- Proposes a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets into a unified model, addressing limits of simultaneous imaging.  
- Frames problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a linear Gaussian model; validated on simulated and experimental calcium imaging.  
- Key contribution: recovers correlations across non-simultaneously imaged neurons, a capability absent in prior work.  
- Technical approach: well-structured variant of EM for inference, showing strong methodological quality.  
- Simulated results: effectively recovers pairwise correlations (0.92 correlation between predicted and true noise correlations).  
- Significance: could substantially expand population sizes for circuit-level neural analyses.  
- Major weakness: unexplained performance gap between simulated and real experimental data (much better on simulations).  
- Practical limitation: model requires substantial overlap in real data for reasonable results, not well-justified.  
- Presentation issue: insufficient detail in figure legends and accompanying text, hindering assessment of experimental validation.  
- Originality: distinguishes itself from related sub-sampling work and addresses a novel challenge.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution but needs major revisions to explain real-data discrepancy and improve presentation.
