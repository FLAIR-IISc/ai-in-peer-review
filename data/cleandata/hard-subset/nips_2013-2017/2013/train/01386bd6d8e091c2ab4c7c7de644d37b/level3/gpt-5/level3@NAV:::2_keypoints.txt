- Proposes “stitching” sequential partial recordings into a single population model by fitting a latent LDS with missing observations and known selection matrices using EM (including online EM).

- Strength: Original, broadly useful framing for inferring large-network properties from sequentially recorded subnetworks, practically relevant for calcium/ephys data.

- Strength: Clear empirical objective—predicting noise correlations for neuron pairs never observed simultaneously—with sensible upper/lower-bound baselines.

- Strength: Simple, scalable LDS instantiation that is accessible and extendable to other observation models.

- Weakness: Method appears limited to near linear-Gaussian regimes; failure modes under nonlinear dynamics or non-Gaussian observations are not analyzed.

- Weakness: Real-data gains may reflect low-rank latent common inputs rather than true direct couplings; stitched couplings correlate poorly with fully observed couplings (Fig. 3b).

- Weakness: Paper does not quantify whether multiple subsets add information beyond a single long recording of one subset—identifiability up to latent transforms is not addressed.

- Weakness: Robustness to degree of overlap, observation noise, and mis-specified noise/covariance (R/Q) is unclear.

- Clarity issue: Important estimation details (M-step updates, regularization/prior choices, stability constraints, initialization, convergence criteria) are missing, hurting reproducibility and novelty assessment.

- Originality: Stitching via overlap is novel, but the core LDS/EM methodology is standard and related to prior partially observed population models.

- Significance: Potentially high impact if robustness and identifiability are clarified; current evidence is strongest for correlation prediction and weaker for interpretable coupling recovery.

- Suggestion: Theorize observability/identifiability conditions and empirically report performance vs overlap (including 0%) and varying session counts.

- Suggestion: Stress-test on nonlinear and point-process simulations and compare to PLDS/latent-GLM and subspace-ID baselines.

- Suggestion: Disentangle sparse direct couplings vs low-rank common input (e.g., sparse+low-rank models) and test whether added sessions improve beyond longer single-session data.

- Suggestion: Provide full derivations, hyperparameter settings, and code to enable reproducibility.

- Argument for acceptance: Novel and practical idea with convincing correlation-prediction results and broad applicability.

- Argument against acceptance: Insufficient technical detail, unclear robustness and identifiability, ambiguous interpretation of learned couplings, and unexplained reliance on overlap.
