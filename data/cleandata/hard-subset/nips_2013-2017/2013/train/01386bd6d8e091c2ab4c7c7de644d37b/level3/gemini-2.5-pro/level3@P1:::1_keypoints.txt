- Proposes a statistical "stitching" method that frames sequential, partial neural recordings as parameter estimation in a latent linear-Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations, fitted by EM.  
- Claims the method can predict noise correlations between neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously, validated on simulations and two-photon calcium imaging.  
- Technically sound: logical use of latent state-space models and a well-designed validation using fictional imaging scenarios to define upper/lower bounds.  
- Strength: recovers pairwise correlations for non-simultaneously recorded neurons on real data, with performance approaching the full-observation upper bound.  
- Major weakness 1: real data required overlapping neuron subsets (≈20% overlap) for good performance, unlike simulations; the paper does not investigate why overlap is necessary.  
- Major weakness 2: substantial drop in coupling-parameter recovery from simulation (r=0.73) to real data (r=0.38), raising concerns about parameter identifiability and interpretability.  
- Clarity: generally clear, but figures and text should more explicitly connect recovered coupling structure to the resulting correlation recovery.  
- Originality: novel application of LDS/EM to infer interactions between non-simultaneously observed neurons, distinct from prior approaches.  
- Significance: potentially high impact—could let experimentalists build larger circuit models from sequential recordings if the method is robust and generalizable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important experimental bottleneck, method is novel and theoretically grounded, and key claims are convincingly demonstrated on real data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unexplained need for overlap, unexplained parameter recovery degradation on real data, and presentation gaps linking metrics and figures.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance, but authors should explicitly investigate and discuss discrepancies between simulated and real-data performance.
