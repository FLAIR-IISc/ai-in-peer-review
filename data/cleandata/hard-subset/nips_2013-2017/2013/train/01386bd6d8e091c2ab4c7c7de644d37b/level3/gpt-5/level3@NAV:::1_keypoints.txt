- Proposes a principled "stitching" framework that combines partially overlapping calcium-imaging sessions into a single latent linear dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Model components: coupling matrix A, stimulus drive B, noise covariances Q (neural) and R (measurement), estimated via mini-batch EM with session-specific selection matrices Ck.  
- Goal: recover correlations (and by proxy functional couplings) between neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously.  
- Situates itself relative to latent GLMs/LDS and subspace identification for partially observed populations; novelty is targeting cross-session correlations without assuming monotonic stimulus/noise correlation links.  
- Strength: sound formulation, standard appropriate inference, and convincing synthetic results recovering off-diagonal couplings and noise correlations for never-co-recorded pairs.  
- Real-data result: substantially outperforms a naive baseline and approaches a fully observed upper bound for correlations, but gains are more modest than in simulation.  
- Unexplained gap between high simulated coupling recovery and modest real-data recovery.  
- Model assumptions (linear Gaussian dynamics, diagonal Q, simple observation model ignoring calcium-to-spike dynamics and heteroscedastic noise) likely drive sensitivity to session overlap; this sensitivity is not analyzed.  
- Baselines are limited; comparisons to PLDS/GPFA, subspace-ID, or deconvolution+GLM are missing and would strengthen claims.  
- Evaluation metrics limited: no held-out likelihood or predictive metrics beyond correlations; stability/identifiability conditions for A are not discussed.  
- Clarity issues: figure legends and text are terse; panels, axes, evaluation metrics need clearer descriptions and CIs/error bars; report dataset-specific preprocessing.  
- Originality: high — novel and timely contribution for inferring cross-session correlations without simultaneity.  
- Significance: high potential if robustness to overlap, noise, and model mismatch is clarified.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear principled formulation, scalable EM implementation, strong synthetic recovery and meaningful real-data gains, addresses an important experimental bottleneck.  
- Cons for acceptance: dependence on substantial overlap without systematic analysis, unexplained sim-vs-real discrepancy, limited baselines and simplified observation/noise model.  
- Suggestions: quantify trade-offs vs. overlap fraction, stimulus strength, and noise; add stronger baselines and ablations; report predictive likelihood and activity cross-prediction; examine stability/identifiability and sparsity priors on A; incorporate or discuss calcium dynamics/deconvolution in the observation model.
