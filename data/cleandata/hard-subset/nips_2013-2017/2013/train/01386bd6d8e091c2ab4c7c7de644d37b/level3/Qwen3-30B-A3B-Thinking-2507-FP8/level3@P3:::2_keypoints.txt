- Introduces a novel "stitching" method that fits a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system (LDS) to sequentially recorded neural subpopulations to infer joint dynamics and noise correlations.  
- Leverages overlapping neuron subsets between sessions to recover functional couplings and noise correlations for non-simultaneously observed neuron pairs.  
- Demonstrated on simulated data and 2-photon calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Extends prior partially observed data work by addressing general noise correlations without strong monotonicity assumptions.  
- Strength: conceptually novel and significant for scaling population analyses beyond simultaneous recording limits.  
- Strength: shows practical utility in predicting noise correlations and has a compelling real-data application.  
- Strength: LDS framework is sensible under its assumptions and recovers couplings well on simulated data relative to a naive baseline.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on linear-Gaussian assumptions with poor justification and no analysis of failure modes under non-Gaussian, nonlinear, or non-stationary dynamics.  
- Weakness: real data shows poor coupling recovery (Fig. 3b), suggesting the model may conflate common input with direct coupling.  
- Weakness: lacks robustness tests on nonlinear synthetic data (e.g., spiking, calcium kinetics), undermining generalizability claims.  
- Weakness: key estimation details (e.g., M-step updates for R and d) are oversimplified or omitted, reducing technical clarity.  
- Weakness: the overlap requirement is asserted but not theoretically explained; unclear why overlap constrains models better than non-overlapping subsets.  
- Weakness: inconsistent claims in real-data results—low coupling correlation yet claimed explanation of noise correlations—hinting at misinterpretation of common input.  
- Presentation issue: disproportionate space on motivation/results but insufficient derivations, identifiability discussion, and technical depth.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept with major revisions required to add robustness analyses, clarify overlap/identifiability, reconcile real-data ambiguity, expand technical derivations, and quantify the incremental benefit of overlap.
