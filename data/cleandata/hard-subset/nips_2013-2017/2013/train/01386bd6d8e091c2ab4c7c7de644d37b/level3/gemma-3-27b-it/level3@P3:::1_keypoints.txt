- Introduces a novel statistical method to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuronal populations to analyze larger circuits beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations and estimates parameters via an EM variant.  
- Builds on prior calcium-imaging connectivity work but uniquely addresses non-simultaneous recordings and long imaging-session dynamics.  
- Paper is well-written with concise mathematical formulation and demonstrations on simulated and real data.  
- Core contribution: effectively recovering pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Strength: method is novel, statistically principled, and fills an important methodological gap.  
- Strength: demonstrated strong performance on simulated data and promising results on real calcium imaging.  
- Strength: potential to substantially expand analyzable neural-circuit scale.  
- Weakness: large discrepancy between simulation and real-data correlation recovery is unexplained and raises generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: figure legends and text lack sufficient detail (e.g., selection criteria for neurons, stimulus/behavioral context).  
- Weakness: reliance on a linear Gaussian model is a strong simplification; impacts on accuracy and robustness are not deeply explored.  
- Weakness: dependence on substantial overlap between imaging sessions limits applicability; analysis across varying overlap levels is insufficient.  
- For acceptance: addresses a significant neuroscience challenge with a novel, sound method and promising empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: unexplained sim-vs-real discrepancy, insufficient figure detail, and heavy overlap requirements undermine robustness and practicality.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to explain the discrepancy, improve figure clarity, discuss modeling assumptions, and analyze overlap effects.  
- Summary scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
