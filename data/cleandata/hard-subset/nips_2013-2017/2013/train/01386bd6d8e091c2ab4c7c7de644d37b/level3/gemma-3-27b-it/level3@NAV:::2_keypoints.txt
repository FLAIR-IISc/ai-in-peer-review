- Proposes a novel “stitching” method framing sequentially imaged neuron sets as a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Leverages overlapping recordings to infer dynamics across a larger network, addressing limits of simultaneous recording.  
- Distinct from existing functional-connectivity methods; most related to Pillow & Latham and extends to point-process data.  
- Strength: innovative approach with demonstrations on simulated and real (mouse somatosensory) data.  
- Strength: successful prediction of noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Clearly articulates the problem and experimental motivation.  
- Weakness: method appears limited to near linear-Gaussian data; robustness and failure modes not analyzed.  
- Weakness: unclear incremental benefit of multiple subsets if a single subset can recover the model up to a latent transform.  
- Weakness: reliance on overlapping populations in real data is insufficiently justified or explained.  
- Weakness: recovered noise correlations may be confounded by common input or latent variables; predicted vs true couplings show discrepancies.  
- Major omission: lacks detailed derivations and estimation details (especially the M-step), hindering reproducibility and novelty assessment.  
- Presentation imbalance: extensive introduction/discussion but inadequate technical exposition.  
- Recommendation: reject as-is but encourage revision addressing robustness, design justification, technical detail, and presentation.
