- Presents a method to stitch sequential, partially overlapping neural recordings by modeling the full population as a latent linear dynamical system and estimating parameters with EM.  
- Demonstrates on simulated and two-photon calcium imaging data that the approach can predict noise correlations between never-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Formulation as an LDS with missing data is elegant and technically sound; EM fitting and benchmarking (fully observed upper bound and naive lower bound) are appropriate.  
- Strong simulated results: accurately recovers coupling matrix and noise correlations (e.g., correlation 0.92 for noise correlations).  
- Major weakness: method fails on real calcium imaging data without substantial overlap; authors needed ~60% overlap to get good performance.  
- The dependence on overlap is a critical limitation that is insufficiently explored or discussed.  
- Large unexplained performance gap between simulated (coupling recovery corr 0.73) and real data (corr 0.38) undermines confidence in practical applicability.  
- Potential causes for the gap (not analyzed in the paper) include model mismatch (linear Gaussian assumptions), non-stationarity, and lower SNR in real recordings.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, with a clear introduction and concise methods.  
- Presentation could be improved: sparse figure legends, under-detailed description of the "fictional imaging scenario," and unclear axes labels in Figures 3d and 4.  
- Originality is high: first explicit use of latent dynamical models to combine non-simultaneous recordings and infer unobserved pairwise statistics.  
- Potential significance is high: could enable estimation of correlation structure for large circuits and influence experimental design toward overlapping sequential recordings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a major experimental limitation, proposes a novel and elegant framework, provides strong proof-of-concept on simulations, and shows promising real-data utility over naive baselines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: crucial limitation about required overlap is under-reported, the performance gap between simulated and real data is unexplained, and minor clarity/reproducibility issues remain.  
- Recommendation: revise to analyze and discuss causes of real-data performance drop and the overlap requirement to strengthen the manuscript.
