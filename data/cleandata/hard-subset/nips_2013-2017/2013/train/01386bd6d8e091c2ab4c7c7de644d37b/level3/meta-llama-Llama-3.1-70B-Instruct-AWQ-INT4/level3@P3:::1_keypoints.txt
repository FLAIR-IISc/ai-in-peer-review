- Proposes a novel statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets into a single model, expanding population-size for dynamics beyond simultaneous imaging.  
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Demonstrates method on both simulated data and experimental calcium imaging in mice.  
- Recovers the true coupling matrix, including off-diagonal blocks for neuron pairs not imaged simultaneously.  
- Accurately predicts noise correlations for non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs where naive approaches fail.  
- Provides a thorough comparison against a naive approach and a fully observed model, highlighting pros and cons.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and detailed methods, though some method details/examples could be expanded.  
- Major strength: effective reconstruction of pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neurons.  
- Weakness: figure legends and figure-related text lack sufficient description, hindering figure interpretation.  
- Weakness: authors do not explain the large difference between simulated (fig.2c) and real-data (fig.3b) correlation results.  
- Paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations and potential applications.  
- Originality rated high; method clearly distinguished from prior contributions.  
- Significance rated high; has potential to overcome limits of simultaneous imaging and scale circuit-level analyses.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, effective method validated on real and simulated data with comprehensive comparisons and strong potential impact.  
- Rejection arguments: insufficient figure descriptions, unexplained simulation vs real-data discrepancy, and limited discussion of limitations/applications.
