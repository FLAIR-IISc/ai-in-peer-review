Addresses inability to record very large neural populations by proposing a method to “stitch” sequentially imaged neurons via a latent linear dynamical system with missing observations.

Novel focus on inferring correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons, tackling a largely unaddressed, challenging problem.

Original and potentially significant idea that could enable large-scale circuit-level analyses currently out of reach.

Sound model formulation and EM-based inference that convincingly recovers pairwise correlations in simulated, idealized data.

Major unexplained discrepancy: method performs poorly on real calcium imaging data and requires substantial neuron overlap between sessions.

Paper fails to investigate or hypothesize reasons for the large simulation-versus-real-data performance gap, weakening confidence in applicability.

Figure legends and descriptions lack sufficient detail for full interpretation and reproducibility.

Promising contribution overall, but practical utility is limited until the simulation–experiment gap and overlap constraint are thoroughly addressed.
