- Introduces a method to infer dynamics of large neural populations by “stitching” sequentially recorded, overlapping subnetworks via a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Explicitly addresses combining non-simultaneously observed neurons, distinguishing from prior connectivity inference work.  
- Problem is important and well-motivated; proposed solution is elegant and potentially impactful.  
- Demonstrations on simulated and real mouse somatosensory data are encouraging, showing prediction of noise correlations for never-simultaneously-recorded neurons.  
- Simulated results show recovery of ground-truth coupling matrices and correlations under linear-Gaussian conditions.  
- Main limitation: robustness unclear — limited exploration of performance degradation under nonlinearity or non-Gaussian neural activity.  
- Incremental benefit of adding more subnetworks is not established; a single large subset might suffice, questioning added value of sequential recordings.  
- Requirement for overlapping populations is asserted but not well-explained; lacks theoretical or empirical analysis of why overlap constrains the model better than non-overlapping sampling.  
- Interpretation of inferred correlations is ambiguous — they may reflect common input or latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings.  
- Mismatch between inferred and true couplings (e.g., Fig. 3b) underscores ambiguity and need for further disentangling.  
- Technical exposition is lacking in key areas (notably the EM M-step), hindering understanding, reproducibility, and assessment of novelty.  
- Presentation is imbalanced, with too much space on introduction/discussion and insufficient technical detail.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful approach; strong empirical results; clear connection to related work; addresses a practical experimental challenge.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited robustness analysis, unclear overlap justification, ambiguous correlation interpretation, insufficient technical detail, and unclear benefit of sequential recordings.  
- Overall recommendation: promising paper but current weaknesses warrant rejection; encourage revision with deeper robustness analysis, overlap justification, clearer interpretation, and fuller technical details.
