- Introduces a method to "stitch" sequential, partial neural recordings by treating unobserved neurons as latent variables in a latent dynamical system.  
- Implements the idea with a linear-Gaussian LDS fit by EM and validates on simulated and 2-photon calcium imaging data.  
- Main empirical claim: the method can accurately predict joint population statistics (e.g., noise correlations) for neuron pairs never simultaneously recorded.  
- Strength: highly original and significant—addresses the practical limitation of limited field-of-view and enables modeling larger circuits.  
- Strength: elegant/statistically principled framing as an LDS with missing data, extensible to more complex models.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation—recovers coupling and noise correlations on simulations; stitched model approaches fully-observed performance on real-data benchmarks.  
- Weakness: demonstrated only with linear-Gaussian assumptions—unclear robustness to nonlinear dynamics or non-Gaussian spiking and absent discussion of failure modes.  
- Weakness: insufficient technical detail—M-step updates lack derivations, hindering reproducibility and expert assessment.  
- Weakness: ambiguity in interpreting learned A matrix—moderate correlation with fully-observed couplings suggests latent dynamics may capture shared variability rather than direct pairwise couplings.  
- Weakness: unclear how much new information multiple subset recordings provide versus simply more data constraining the same latent dynamics.  
- Weakness: authors claim overlap between subsets is critical but provide no explanation; need explicit discussion of overlap’s role in aligning latent representations.  
- Recommendation: accept due to novelty and impact, but revise to add derivations, discuss limitations/failure modes, and clarify interpretation of parameters and role of overlap.
