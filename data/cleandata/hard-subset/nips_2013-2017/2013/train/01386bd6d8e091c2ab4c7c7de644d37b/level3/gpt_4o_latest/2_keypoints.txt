- Proposes stitching sequential subnetwork recordings into a unified latent linear dynamical system (LDS) to infer network properties from non-simultaneous neural data.

- Novel method enabling inference of interactions between non-simultaneously observed neural populations.

- Significant potential to analyze larger neural circuits under experimental recording constraints.

- Empirical validation on simulated and real calcium imaging data shows stitching outperforms naive baselines and approaches fully observed models.

- Theoretical grounding by adapting dynamical systems results to a practical application.

- Relies on linear-Gaussian assumptions, limiting applicability to nonlinear or non-Gaussian neural dynamics.

- Requires overlapping neuron subsets; method fails without overlap and the overlap vs. coverage trade-off is not systematically explored.

- Paper lacks detailed explanations (notably the EM M-step) and clearer organization, hindering reproducibility.

- Predicts noise correlations well but struggles to recover true coupling correlations, possibly due to unmodeled common inputs, with insufficient analysis.

- Recommend discussing applicability to non-linear/non-Gaussian data and potential extensions.

- Recommend providing detailed derivations/clarification of the estimation process, especially the M-step.

- Recommend systematically exploring overlap vs. coverage trade-offs in experimental design.

- Recommend analyzing limitations in capturing true coupling correlations and proposing remedies.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel and impactful, strong empirical results, and potential to advance neural circuit analysis.

- Arguments against acceptance: limited generalizability due to linear-Gaussian assumption, dependence on overlap, and insufficient methodological clarity.

- Overall conclusion: valuable contribution and strong candidate for acceptance, contingent on revisions to address clarity and practical limitations.
