- Proposes a statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged neuron sets into one model to study population dynamics beyond simultaneous recordings.  
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Validated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging in mice; demonstrates recovery of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Novel contribution: infers functional connections between neurons not imaged simultaneously, distinguishing from prior work.  
- Effectively recovers pairwise correlations for neuron pairs not measured simultaneously.  
- Demonstrates robustness by applying the method to multiple populations in both anesthetized and awake mice.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear explanations of model formulation, parameters, and inference procedures.  
- Figure legends and figure-related text need more detailed descriptions and clearer explanations (e.g., coupling matrices, noise correlations).  
- Addresses a major limitation of current imaging techniques and offers a promising approach to scale up circuit-level analyses.  
- Unresolved issue: large discrepancy between correlation results in simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b) is not explained.  
- Unclear why substantial overlap between imaged populations is required for reasonable results; authors do not explain this dependence.  
- Overall evaluation: high scores (Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10) and overall rating 8/10.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, validated on both simulated and real data, robust across conditions, addresses key imaging limitation.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient figure detail, unexplained simulation vs. real-data discrepancy, unexplained need for substantial overlap.
