- Proposes a novel "stitching" method that combines sequentially imaged neuron subsets into a single latent dynamical system to predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.  
- Frames the problem as latent dynamical system inference with missing observations, building on theoretical reconstruction results [21–23].  
- Addresses a literature gap: prior work focused on simultaneous recordings and did not combine non-simultaneous data.  
- Uses a linear Gaussian dynamical system and an EM-like algorithm with clear mathematical formulation and parameter updates.  
- Validated on simulated data and real calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Substantially outperforms a naive baseline that averages session parameters, especially for non-simultaneous pair correlations.  
- Method is technically sound, well-motivated, and compared carefully to appropriate baselines (fully observed and naive models).  
- Requires substantial session overlap (~60% in real data), which may limit applicability.  
- Linear Gaussian model may be too simplistic for complex neural dynamics.  
- Significant unexplained performance gap between simulated results (0.73 correlation) and real data (0.38 correlation), weakening generalizability claims.  
- Figure legends and related text lack detail needed to fully interpret key results, notably Figure 3b.  
- Original contribution in neuroscience: novel use of latent dynamical systems to stitch non-simultaneous recordings and clear differentiation from prior methods.  
- Potentially significant: could expand scale of neural circuit analysis if robust across diverse circuits.  
- Strengths summarized: novelty, strong validation, ability to predict non-simultaneous correlations, justified baselines, and high potential impact.  
- Weaknesses summarized: unexplained sim-vs-real gap, high overlap requirement, linear-model limitations, and insufficient figure detail.  
- Pro argument: novel, well-founded, practically validated approach with significant potential impact.  
- Con argument: generalizability concerns due to performance gap and overlap requirement, and model simplicity.  
- Recommendation: Accept, but revise to address the simulation–real data discrepancy and provide clearer explanations of real-world results.
