- Proposes DisDCA, a distributed stochastic dual coordinate ascent algorithm for regularized loss minimization on multiple machines/cores.  
- Provides a practical variant that yields substantial improvements over the basic DisDCA.  
- Analyzes the computation–communication tradeoff in detail.  
- Presents thorough convergence rate analysis.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via experiments on real-world datasets.  
- Bridges stochastic dual coordinate ascent and distributed optimization, with clear motivation and applicability.  
- Well-written and clearly organized, making the method and analysis easy to follow.  
- Technically sound with concise presentation of algorithm and theoretical results.  
- High originality by introducing a new approach to distributed optimization.  
- High significance for large-scale machine learning applications.  
- Limitation: assumes convex loss and strongly convex regularizer, which may not hold in practice.  
- Limitation: lacks detailed comparison with other distributed methods (e.g., distributed SGD, ADMM).  
- Limitation: could benefit from additional experiments and further empirical analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
