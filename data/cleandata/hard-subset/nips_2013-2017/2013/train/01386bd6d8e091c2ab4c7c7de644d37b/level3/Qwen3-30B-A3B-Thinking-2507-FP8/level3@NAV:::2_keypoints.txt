- Proposes stitching sequentially recorded neural subnetworks using a latent dynamical system to infer large-scale population dynamics.  
- Builds on Pillow & Latham’s partially observed spiking population idea and extends it to calcium imaging with overlapping subnetworks.  
- Demonstrates promising performance on simulated data and real mouse somatosensory cortex recordings.  
- Method can predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs nearly as well as a fully observed model.  
- Conceptual novelty is strong and the application addresses an important experimental bottleneck in neuroscience.  
- Robustness claims are unsupported; method appears limited to near-linear-Gaussian regimes.  
- Lacks analysis of failure modes when assumptions (non-Gaussian dynamics, strong nonlinearities) are violated.  
- Incremental benefit is unclear because a full model can be recovered from one subset up to latent transform, questioning extra subsets’ added value.  
- The requirement for overlapping populations is critical for real data but not adequately explained or justified.  
- Real-data interpretation of inferred noise correlations as direct pairwise couplings is questionable; Figure 3b shows poor match to true couplings.  
- Technical presentation is deficient: M-step updates and derivations are glossed over, hindering assessment of novelty and soundness.  
- Paper is imbalanced with extensive introduction/discussion but insufficient technical exposition and detail.  
- Overall: promising idea with high significance, but substantial revisions needed on robustness, technical detail, and interpretation; reviewer recommends rejection.
