- Problem: inferring dynamics of large neural populations beyond simultaneous recording limitations.  
- Proposal: statistically "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron subsets by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a linear Gaussian model.  
- Demonstration: evaluated on simulated data and mouse somatosensory cortex recordings, claiming prediction of noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Literature context: extends prior work by explicitly addressing non-simultaneous recordings and scaling to larger populations.  
- Strength: well-motivated biologically with a clear, tractable mathematical formulation.  
- Strength: strong simulation results and use of fully observed and naive baselines for benchmarking.  
- Strength: validated on both anesthetized and awake experimental datasets.  
- Weakness: experimental results are less robust and require substantial overlap between imaged subsets.  
- Weakness: large unexplained performance gap between simulation (corr ~0.73) and experimental (corr ~0.38) results.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of factors determining required overlap and transferability to real data.  
- Weakness: figure legends and result descriptions are sparse; some experimental scenarios (e.g., "fictional imaging") need clearer explanation.  
- Originality: novel application of latent dynamical systems/EM to infer connections from non-simultaneous recordings.  
- Significance: potentially high impact for scaling circuit inference and predicting non-simultaneous correlations if validated.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—accept with revisions to improve clarity and address simulation-versus-experiment discrepancies and overlap requirements.
