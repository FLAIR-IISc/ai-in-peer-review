- Introduces a novel statistical method to "stitch" multiple partial recordings and infer joint neural population dynamics.  
- Distinct from prior work by inferring functional connections between neurons not observed simultaneously.  
- Method uses a latent dynamical system fitted via an expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Validated on simulated data and mouse somatosensory cortex recordings.  
- Demonstrates recovery of the true coupling matrix, including off-diagonal blocks for non-simultaneous neuron pairs.  
- Shows ability to predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Main strength: novel, broadly applicable idea of stitching sub-models to infer large-network properties.  
- Potential extension to point-process data highlighted.  
- Limitation: method may be restricted to near linear-Gaussian regimes.  
- Paper lacks intuition and analysis of failure modes when model assumptions are violated.  
- Unclear explanation for why overlap between recorded populations is necessary.  
- Presentation imbalance: too much emphasis on introduction/discussion and insufficient technical exposition.  
- Estimation and derivation details are under-explained, undermining clarity and perceived novelty.  
- Overall technically sound with supported claims, but would benefit from more detailed derivations and clearer estimation procedures.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to expand derivations, analyze limitations/failure modes, and improve technical presentation balance.
