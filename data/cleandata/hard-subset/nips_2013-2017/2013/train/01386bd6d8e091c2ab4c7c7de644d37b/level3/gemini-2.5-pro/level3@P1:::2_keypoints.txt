- Introduces a statistical "stitching" method to infer dynamics of a large neural population from multiple sequential, partial recordings using a latent linear-Gaussian dynamical system (LDS).

- Demonstrated on simulations and two-photon calcium imaging; can predict noise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously.

- Novel and significant idea with broad potential to expand effective recording scale and extendable to other model classes.

- Stitch model outperforms a naive baseline and approaches performance of a fully observed model on shown datasets.

- Primary technical concern: reliance on linear-Gaussian LDS; unclear robustness to strong nonlinearities or non-Gaussian noise in real neural data.

- Low correlation (0.38) between inferred couplings and fully observed couplings indicates learned parameters may be ambiguous.

- Parameters may capture shared variability or effective connections mediated by unobserved inputs rather than true direct couplings.

- Authors found overlapping neurons were empirically necessary for good performance, contradicting the claimed identifiability from non-overlapping sets and not explained.

- Methods lack key technical detail (e.g., explicit EM M-step updates) given the novel missing-data structure, hurting reproducibility.

- Unclear precisely what additional recordings contribute: resolving linear transform ambiguity versus simply adding more data to constrain parameters.

- Presentation imbalance: heavy on intro/discussion but too brief on technical derivations and fitting procedure.

- Arguments for acceptance: high novelty, strong empirical results, and significant potential impact on neuroscience experiments.

- Arguments for rejection: insufficient methodological depth, missing theoretical justification for key claims, and limited exploration of model assumption violations.

- Overall: promising proof-of-concept with high impact potential, but needs clearer technical exposition and analysis of limitations before full endorsement.
