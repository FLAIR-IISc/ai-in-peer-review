- Proposes a latent dynamical system with EM to stitch sequentially imaged neuron sets and infer correlations beyond simultaneous recordings.  
- Builds on prior connectivity and missing-observation methods but uniquely targets non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Validated on simulated and mouse somatosensory calcium imaging data.  
- Simulation results show effective recovery of pairwise correlations for non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Major weakness: substantial, unexplained discrepancy between simulation and real-data performance.  
- Method requires large overlap in real data to work well, limiting practical applicability.  
- Clarity issues: figure legends and related text lack detail; data processing and parameter choices (e.g., 60% overlap) are insufficiently justified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and significant problem, strong simulation results, potential to scale circuit analyses.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unexplained simulation–real gap, presentation clarity problems, reliance on overlap.  
- Recommendation: reject with request for revision—address the discrepancy, improve clarity, and discuss/mitigate overlap limitations.
