- Proposes a novel statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets into a single model to expand analyzable population sizes.  
- Validated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging in mice, demonstrating recovery of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Technically sound with a clear, well-organized presentation and thorough explanation of model formulation, parameter estimation, and inference.  
- Makes a significant contribution by addressing the fundamental limit of simultaneous imaging and enabling larger-scale population dynamics analysis.  
- Demonstrates strong ability to recover pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs.  
- Method requires substantial overlap between imaged populations for reasonable results, which may limit applicability.  
- Authors do not explain the large discrepancy between correlation results on simulated versus real data, indicating potential need for further refinement.  
- Original and distinct approach: latent dynamical system model with missing observations and novel combination of techniques.  
- Overall well-written with sufficient detail and potential for wide applicability.
