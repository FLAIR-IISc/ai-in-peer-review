- Problem: stitch multiple partial, non-simultaneous neural recordings into a unified latent dynamical system to infer dynamics and correlations across unobserved neuron pairs.
- Method: formulate as a latent dynamical system with missing observations, use a linear Gaussian model and EM-based inference for computational tractability.
- Novelty: extends LDS methods to handle missing observations across sequential imaging sessions, addressing stitching non-simultaneous populations (distinct from prior sub-sampling work).
- Main strength (simulations): accurately recovers coupling matrices (corr 0.73 for non-simultaneous pairs) and noise correlations (corr 0.92 with ground truth), outperforming a naive zero-correlation baseline.
- Main strength (real data): produces coupling matrices resembling a fully observed model (corr 0.38 for non-simultaneous pairs) and predicts pairwise correlations (corr 0.84 vs 0.15 naive).
- Technical quality: clear problem formulation, well-explained EM procedure, careful experimental design, and appropriate benchmarks (naive and theoretical upper bound).
- Limitation: method requires substantial session overlap (~60%) on real data, but the paper lacks theoretical justification or guidance for this requirement.
- Unexplained discrepancy: significant gap between simulated and real-data coupling recovery (0.73 vs 0.38) is not analyzed thoroughly; possible causes like model mismatch or real neural complexity are not discussed.
- Clarity issues: several figure legends lack detail (e.g., selection criteria for "50 most correlated neurons" and interpretation of different coupling matrices) and figures are not fully connected to claims.
- Originality: application of LDS to stitch non-simultaneous recordings is an original and distinct contribution despite using established modeling tools.
- Significance: method could substantially expand analyzable population size beyond current imaging limits, with clear practical utility for neuroscience.
- Suggested extension 1: discuss applicability to more than two imaging sessions and the effect of session order.
- Suggested extension 2: consider replacing or augmenting the linear Gaussian model with more biologically realistic components.
- Suggested extension 3: more thoroughly discuss experimental-design implications of required session overlap.
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution with practical impact; recommend acceptance.
