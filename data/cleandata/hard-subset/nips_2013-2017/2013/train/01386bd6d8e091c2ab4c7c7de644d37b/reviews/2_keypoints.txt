- Novel, powerful idea: infer large-network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and stitching sub-models; extends to point-process data and is broadly applicable.  
- Few similar prior works; closest is Pillow & Latham on partially observed spiking populations.  
- Unclear robustness: method may be limited to near linear-Gaussian data and the paper lacks intuition on failure modes when assumptions are violated.  
- Unclear incremental benefit: if full model can be recovered from one subset (up to latent transform), it’s unclear how much extra coupling information other subsets provide versus simply more data from one subset.  
- Overlap requirement: real-data model only works with overlapping populations, and the paper doesn’t explain why overlap better constrains the model.  
- Real-data ambiguity: explained noise correlations might reflect common input/latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings, especially since learned couplings poorly match true couplings in Fig. 3b.  
- Estimation detail deficiency: paper is light on estimation/derivation details (notably the M-step), undermining clarity and perceived novelty.  
- Presentation imbalance: lots of intro/discussion but insufficient technical exposition; the idea is promising but needs clearer explanation and derivations.
