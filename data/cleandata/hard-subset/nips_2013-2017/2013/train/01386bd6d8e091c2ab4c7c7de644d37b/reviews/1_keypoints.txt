- Contribution: Proposes a statistical method to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuron sets to expand analyzable population sizes and characterize circuit dynamics beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Applications: Method validated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging in mice, claiming recovery of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Model formulation: Frames problem as a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a linear Gaussian model.  
- Parameters and inference: Defines coupling matrix, stimulus receptive-field matrix, neural and measurement noise covariances, estimated via a variant of EM.  
- Strength: Effectively recovers pairwise correlations between neuron pairs not measured simultaneously.  
- Simulated vs real data issue: Model works well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real data requires substantial overlap for reasonable results.  
- Unexplained discrepancy: Authors do not explain the large difference in correlation results between simulated (fig. 2c) and real data (fig. 3b).  
- Clarity shortcomings: Paper is well written overall but figure legends and figure-related text lack sufficient description and elaboration.  
- Originality: Novel in inferring functional connections for non-simultaneously imaged neurons compared to prior work.  
- Significance: Offers a promising approach to overcome fundamental limits of simultaneous imaging and scale up circuit-level analyses.
