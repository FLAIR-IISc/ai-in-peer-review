- Tackles stitching non-simultaneous neural population recordings by fitting an LDS with known per-session selection matrices via EM/online-EM.
- Builds on prior system-identification and latent dynamical modeling work; novelty is empirical framing and demonstration rather than a new estimator.
- Strength: clear problem motivation and direct relevance to current imaging limits.
- Strength: method is simple, scalable, reproducible (Kalman smoothing + EM with selection matrices).
- Strength: experimental design includes simulation ground truth, fully-observed upper bound, naive lower bound, and relevant metrics (correlation, prediction).
- Strength: encouraging results—accurate noise correlations in simulation and improved prediction/paired correlation estimates on split real data.
- Strength: paper is clearly written and acknowledges limitations.
- Major concern: real-data validation uses a “fictional split” of simultaneous recordings, avoiding practical session-to-session issues (drift, nonstationarity, cell registration).
- Major concern: method critically depends on neuron overlap; failure without overlap is reported but not systematically analyzed.
- Major concern: strong modeling assumptions (linear-Gaussian dynamics, diagonal innovation noise, Gaussian measurement noise) may misattribute shared variability and bias coupling estimates.
- Major concern: identifiability and observability claims are heuristic; no clear theoretical conditions on Ck patterns, stimulus richness, overlap, or data length.
- Major concern: parameter recovery for couplings is modest and degrades with population size; regularization or latent inputs are mentioned but not evaluated.
- Minor issues: notation/typo in measurement equation, missing details on preprocessing, sampling, hyperparameters, convergence, runtime, and scaling.
- Quality assessment: technically sound within LDS framework and experiments are competently executed, but broader stitching claims are not demonstrated.
- Clarity request: correct measurement equation, state preprocessing/sampling/stationarity assumptions, and report computational details.
- Originality: incremental—standard LDS+EM applied in a new empirical context focused on noise correlations.
- Significance: potentially valuable if validated on real multi-session recordings; currently moderate due to overlap and modeling limitations.
- Suggestions: test truly separate recordings (different FOVs/days), systematically study performance vs overlap/data length/stimulus richness, and probe zero-overlap conditions in realistic sims.
- Suggestions: incorporate realistic observation models or correlated latent inputs and add regularization (sparse or low-rank + sparse) to stabilize estimates.
- Suggestions: provide identifiability/observability analysis tailored to selection-matrix setting, report runtime/memory, and release code for adoption.
- Overall: clear and promising empirical contribution within LDS limits, but incremental novelty and insufficient real-session validation and theoretical analysis limit practical readiness.
