This paper addresses the problem of inferring the dynamics of a neural population when only partial, non-simultaneous recordings are available. The core idea is to formulate the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system, leveraging the assumption that nearby neurons are strongly coupled. The authors demonstrate, both on simulated and real calcium imaging data, that this approach allows for accurate prediction of correlations, even between neurons never recorded simultaneously.

The paper is well-motivated, clearly outlining the experimental limitations that motivate the work and relating it to prior efforts in neural connectivity inference. The authors appropriately position their work relative to existing literature, acknowledging related methods and highlighting the novelty of addressing correlations between non-simultaneously observed neurons. The method itself – employing a latent dynamical system and EM algorithm – is a reasonable and well-established approach.  The results on simulated data are compelling, showing good recovery of both coupling parameters and noise correlations.  Application to real calcium imaging data demonstrates the method's potential for analyzing complex neural recordings. The inclusion of both anesthetized and awake data strengthens the findings. The comparison to both an upper (fully observed) and lower (naive) bound provides a useful benchmark for evaluating performance.

Strengths:

* **Novelty:** The core contribution – inferring correlations between *non-simultaneously* recorded neurons – is a novel and valuable extension to existing methods.
* **Technical Soundness:** The use of a latent dynamical system and EM algorithm is a theoretically sound approach. The mathematical formulation is clear.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the problem, the proposed method, and the results.
* **Empirical Validation:**  Demonstration on both simulated and real data strengthens the claims.
* **Comparison to Bounds:** Providing upper and lower bounds on performance (fully observed vs. naive) gives a good sense of how well the method is working.
* **Relevant Problem:** The tackled problem is highly relevant to current neuroscience research, given limitations in recording technology.

Weaknesses:

* **Model Simplicity:** The use of a linear Gaussian dynamical system is a simplifying assumption. While justified for tractability, it might limit the method’s ability to capture more complex, nonlinear dynamics present in real neural circuits. The authors acknowledge this but a discussion on how the approach might be extended to more complex models would be beneficial.
* **Parameter Tuning:** The paper lacks detail on how parameters were tuned (e.g., regularization strength, initial conditions for EM). This information is crucial for reproducibility.
* **Overlap Requirement:** The finding that some overlap between recording sessions is *critical* for performance is noted, but the sensitivity to the degree of overlap isn’t fully explored.  A more systematic investigation of how performance degrades with decreasing overlap would be valuable.
* **Computational Cost:** While not explicitly stated, the EM algorithm for latent dynamical systems can be computationally expensive, especially for large populations.  A discussion of the computational limitations and potential scalability issues would be helpful.
* **Limited Biological Interpretation:** While the method predicts correlations, deeper interpretation in terms of underlying biological mechanisms is missing. The authors demonstrate they can infer *that* correlations exist, but not necessarily *why*.



Arguments for Acceptance:

* The paper addresses a significant and practically relevant problem in neuroscience.
* The proposed method is technically sound and demonstrates promising results on both simulated and real data.
* The novelty of inferring correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons is a valuable contribution.
* The comparison to upper and lower bounds provides a clear assessment of performance.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The simplifying assumption of a linear Gaussian dynamical system may limit the applicability of the method to more complex neural systems.
* Lack of detail on parameter tuning and potential computational limitations.



Overall, this is a solid and well-executed paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of neural data analysis. The strengths outweigh the weaknesses, and I believe it is worthy of acceptance to NeurIPS, contingent on addressing the minor concerns regarding parameter tuning, overlap sensitivity, and computational cost in a revised version.
