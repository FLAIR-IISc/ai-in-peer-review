- Presents a method to stitch partial recordings of the same neural circuit using a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system to infer joint dynamics and correlations.
- Demonstrates accurate prediction of noise correlations for non-simultaneously recorded neurons on simulated and real calcium imaging data.
- Fills a gap in existing methods by inferring connections between neurons observed in separate recording sessions, distinguishing from stimulus-correlation and non-scalable prior work.
- Addresses an important neuroscience bottleneck by enabling inference of larger population dynamics than simultaneously observable.
- Uses a sound theoretical foundation (LDS + EM) with principled parameter estimation.
- Provides strong empirical validation and clear benchmarks, including comparison to a fully observed model and naive approaches.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and sufficiently detailed to support reproducibility.
- Model simplicity: linear Gaussian LDS may not capture nonlinear or non-Gaussian neural dynamics, limiting applicability.
- Requires substantial overlap between recording sessions (60% used), posing a practical constraint; performance at lower overlap needs more study.
- Insufficient discussion of parameter tuning and sensitivity (e.g., Q covariance, EM settings).
- Computational cost and scalability are not thoroughly analyzed despite mention of online-EM.
- Limited exploration of stimulus dependence; analysis focuses mainly on noise correlations.
- Overall assessment: strong and promising work with solid validation but some methodological and practical limitations.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant problem with a principled, validated approach and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on a simplified model, overlap requirement, and lack of detailed tuning/scalability analysis.
- Rating: Strong Accept (8/10).
- Recommendations: discuss extensions to nonlinear/non-Gaussian models, analyze impact of overlap, detail computational cost/scalability, and comment on applicability to other modalities (e.g., electrophysiology).
