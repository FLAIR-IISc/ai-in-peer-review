- Paper addresses inferring joint population dynamics across non-simultaneously recorded neurons via a “stitching” LDS/VAR(1) with session-specific selection matrices.  
- Parameters are learned by EM with Kalman smoothing under missing observations; Q and R diagonal, A unconstrained, global stationary prior.  
- Method predicts pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons much better than a naive baseline and near a fictional “fully observed” upper bound.  
- Evaluation includes simulations and two 2p calcium datasets (anesthetized and awake mouse S1) and cross-prediction of unobserved activity.  
- Core methodology is classical (Kalman/EM, LDS); novelty is pragmatic: multi-session stitching using selection matrices and overlap.  
- Related to subspace/system identification, multi-view alignment, multi-session latent-state models, and matrix completion of covariance.  
- Strength: tackles an important experimental constraint with a simple, tractable solution and clear exposition.  
- Strength: EM with session-specific observation matrices is appropriate and computationally feasible.  
- Strength: Predictive tasks (correlations, cross-prediction) align with scientific use-cases and are informative.  
- Weakness: No formal identifiability/observability conditions (coverage, overlap size, excitation, stability) are provided or analyzed.  
- Weakness: Sharing A, B, Q, R, d across sessions likely underfits session-specific effects like gains, baselines, photobleaching, or motion.  
- Weakness: Diagonal Q and R forces all correlations into A, misattributing instantaneous shared inputs or measurement correlations to dynamics.  
- Weakness: No stability or regularization constraints on A; risk of unstable/overfit O(N^2) dynamics for N≈100–200.  
- Weakness: Evaluation often compares to a model-based “fully observed” LDS upper bound rather than empirical correlations/predictive likelihood on held-out simultaneous data.  
- Weakness: Baselines are weak; stronger comparisons (low-rank LDS/PLDS, sparse VAR, subspace ID with missing data) are missing.  
- Weakness: Modeling fluorescence directly with VAR(1) conflates neuronal coupling and slow calcium/measurement dynamics; explicit calcium dynamics or deconvolution needed.  
- Clarity issues: notation/typos in equations, inconsistent indices, and insufficient detail on the fictional split procedure.  
- Originality: contribution is mainly pragmatic/application-focused rather than theoretical; acceptable if empirical evidence is strong.  
- Significance: approach could guide experimental design (overlap vs coverage) but impact limited by lack of theory, robustness analysis, and strong baselines.  
- Suggestions: add per-session gains/offsets and Rk, test robustness to session drift, and report sensitivity.  
- Suggestions: incorporate stability and sparsity regularization on A and provide ablation studies.  
- Suggestions: include low-rank latent inputs (PLDS) to capture common input instead of diagonal Q only.  
- Suggestions: evaluate against empirical noise correlations and predictive log-likelihood on held-out simultaneous data, and add stronger baselines.  
- Suggestions: study overlap size, scaling with N and T, computation time, and consider modeling calcium dynamics or using deconvolved spikes.  
- Pros for acceptance: timely problem, simple useful method, empirical gains on real datasets, potential experimental impact.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, missing theory/identifiability, weak baselines, and strong modeling assumptions that may bias conclusions.
