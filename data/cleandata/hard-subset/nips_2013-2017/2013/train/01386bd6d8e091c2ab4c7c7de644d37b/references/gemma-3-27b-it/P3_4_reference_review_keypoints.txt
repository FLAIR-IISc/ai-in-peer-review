- Presents a method to stitch multiple partial neural recordings via a latent dynamical system to infer larger population dynamics.  
- Well-motivated by experimental limits (e.g., 2-photon calcium imaging) and positioned within related work on partial observations and connectivity inference.  
- Uses an LDS framework with EM (including online-EM) for parameter estimation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical validation on simulated data and real mouse somatosensory calcium recordings.  
- Shows accurate recovery of coupling parameters and noise correlations in simulations, and plausible performance on experimental data.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with effective equations and figures.  
- Significant potential impact by enabling integration of multiple recordings to study large-scale neural circuits.  
- Major limitation: reliance on a linear Gaussian model that may not capture neural nonlinearity or calcium non-Gaussianity.  
- Computational scalability concerns remain despite online-EM; more discussion of large-population solutions (e.g., sparsity, dimensionality reduction) is needed.  
- Requires some overlap between sessions for good performance; optimal overlap level is not fully explored.  
- Requests more detail on preprocessing of calcium imaging data (denoising, baseline correction).  
- Questions sensitivity to the choice of the 50 most correlated neurons used in experiments.  
- Suggests showing correlation matrices alongside coupling matrices for intuitive interpretation.  
- Asks for elaboration on incorporating priors on network topology and their potential benefits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, technically sound, well-validated, clearly written, and impactful.  
- Arguments against acceptance: simplifying linear-Gaussian assumption, scalability concerns, and overlap requirement.  
- Final recommendation: strong accept — significant contribution despite acknowledged limitations.
