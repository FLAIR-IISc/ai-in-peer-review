- Tackles inferring dynamics of large neural populations from multiple partial, non-simultaneous recordings using a stitching approach.  
- Proposes a latent dynamical system (linear Gaussian LDS) to integrate partial observations and predict unobserved neurons.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on simulated and real calcium imaging data, outperforming a naive per-session approach.  
- Positions work within prior literature on connectivity inference and subsampled observations, highlighting novelty in inferring between non-simultaneously observed neurons.  
- Cites Takens’ embedding theorem as theoretical justification for the approach.  
- Strength: Addresses a practically important limitation of current imaging technologies.  
- Strength: Provides a principled statistical framework with sound theoretical grounding.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation with convincing results (high correlation values reported).  
- Strength: Clear, well-organized presentation and reproducible methods.  
- Weakness: Model simplicity—reliance on linear Gaussian LDS may not capture neural non-linearities and non-Gaussian activity.  
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of parameter sensitivity and tuning (regularization, initialization).  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns for EM-based estimation on very large populations are not thoroughly addressed.  
- Weakness: Dependence on session overlap is noted but minimum overlap and degradation with less overlap are not explored.  
- Weakness: Limited biological insight into mechanisms generating predicted correlations.  
- Qualitative: Technically sound, rigorous experimental design, and clear mathematical exposition.  
- Qualitative: Novel application of latent dynamical systems to stitching partial recordings; potentially significant impact.  
- Pro accept arguments: Practical importance, sound method, strong validation, clear writing.  
- Con accept arguments: Simple model, limited parameter/scalability discussion, constrained biological interpretation.  
- Overall assessment: Solid contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss extensions to non-linear dynamics, add parameter tuning/sensitivity discussion, explore/acknowledge overlap requirements, and discuss scalability for large populations.
