- Problem: Inferring joint population dynamics and noise correlations from multiple partially overlapping neural recordings where full simultaneous imaging is infeasible.

- Method: Proposes a stitching framework using a linear Gaussian state-space model (LDS) with known session selection matrices C_k and shared latent dynamics.

- Learning: Parameters estimated with EM (online mini-batch then batch EM) and Kalman smoothing.

- Key claim: Jointly fitting sessions enables reconstruction of cross-couplings and prediction of noise correlations for neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously.

- Simulations: N=60, structured A, K=10 trials show strong recovery (noise-correlation r≈0.92; off-diagonals r≈0.73).

- Real data: Two 2-photon calcium imaging datasets (mouse S1, anesthetized & awake) with synthetic splits; predicted noise correlations correlate well with fully observed LDS (anesthetized r≈0.84).

- Baselines: Compared to upper bound (fully observed LDS) and lower bound (independent LDS per session), showing improvement over naive baseline.

- Overlap dependence: Performance degrades without overlap; overlap is critical for successful stitching.

- Scalability: Conceptually simple and computationally tractable; demonstrated up to ~100–200 neurons.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on latent dynamical models, LDS with missing data, system ID, and multi-session alignment; novelty is joint LDS stitching for never-co-recorded noise correlations.

- Strength: Timely, impactful problem with clear experimental relevance and solid empirical validation.

- Strength: Transparent about assumptions, limitations, and possible extensions (low-rank noise, sparsity priors).

- Weakness: Observation model mismatch — LDS ignores nonlinear spiking→calcium dynamics; needs experiments showing robustness to realistic nonlinearities or use of PLDS/deconvolved spikes.

- Weakness: Lack of formal identifiability conditions for LDS with missing observations and limited overlap.

- Weakness: Real-data evaluation compares to a fully observed LDS model rather than empirical residual correlations from held-out simultaneous data.

- Weakness: Insufficient quantification of performance versus overlap fraction, session length T, and number of sessions — needs ablations to guide experimental design.

- Weakness: Regularization and stability of A (spectral constraints, priors) and runtime/complexity details are sparse.

- Weakness: Noise model limited to diagonal Q and R; low-rank plus diagonal Q or factor-analytic observation noise not evaluated.

- Weakness: Assumes perfect cell identity matching across sessions; no robustness analysis to mis-registration.

- Clarity: Generally clear but notation dense; suggest pseudocode, initialization details, and clearer description of noise-correlation computation.

- Originality: Natural combination of known techniques but a useful and novel empirical application to stitching multi-session calcium imaging.

- Significance: High practical significance for large-scale imaging and experimental design; likely extensible to more complex latent models.

- Questions for authors: Request performance vs overlap/T/sessions, empirical-heldout correlation comparisons, stability/regularization details, runtime/scaling limits, and robustness to mis-registered cells.

- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on addressing empirical held-out correlation evaluation, overlap/length ablations, and stability/regularization clarifications.
