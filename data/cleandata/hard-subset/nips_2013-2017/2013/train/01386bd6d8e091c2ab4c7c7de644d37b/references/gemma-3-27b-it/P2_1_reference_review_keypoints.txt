- Paper proposes an EM-based method using a linear-Gaussian latent dynamical system to “stitch” partial neural recordings and infer larger population dynamics.
- Demonstrates efficacy on simulated and real calcium imaging data, recovering coupling matrices and predicting noise correlations between never-simultaneously-recorded neurons.
- Positions itself relative to prior work, distinguishing the problem of inferring connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons and building on latent dynamical system literature.
- Novel and significant: addresses a major limitation in neuroscience by enabling reconstruction of larger circuits from partial recordings; noise-correlation prediction is valuable.
- Strong empirical validation: comparisons to an ideal fully observed model and a naive baseline, with encouraging real-data results.
- Clear, reproducible methodology and reasonable choice of linear-Gaussian model as a tractable starting point.
- Solid theoretical foundation linking to system identification theory.
- Well-written and organized with informative figures.
- Major simplification: linear-Gaussian model may not capture nonlinear, non-Gaussian neural activity and robustness to violations is unclear.
- Lacks thorough parameter sensitivity analysis, making EM’s susceptibility to local optima a concern.
- Requires substantial overlap between imaging sessions (≈60%), which limits applicability and should be mitigated or further explored.
- Computational cost and scalability to thousands of neurons are not explicitly addressed despite using mini-batch EM.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, statistically sound approach, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: simplifying assumptions, parameter sensitivity/local optima risk, high overlap requirement, and potential scalability issues.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper with non-fatal weaknesses that could be addressed in future work.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions focused on model limitations, parameter sensitivity analysis, overlap-performance relationship, and computational complexity discussion.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
