- Novel statistical method for "stitching" partial neural recordings to infer joint dynamics, including unobserved neurons.
- Models the population as a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system with missing observations and fits via EM with Kalman smoothing.
- Leverages strong coupling between nearby neurons to infer correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Validated on simulated data with ground truth and on real calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex (anesthetized and awake).
- Accurately predicts noise correlations and joint statistics, approaching a fully observed model and outperforming naive session-averaging.
- Distinguishes itself from prior work by specifically addressing stitching non-simultaneous recordings at scale.
- Technical formulation is sound, with clear mathematical detail and a proper EM/Kalman fitting procedure.
- Strong validation strategy using both ground-truth simulations and a fictional imaging benchmark comparing upper and lower bounds.
- Authors acknowledge key limitations, especially the simplifying linear Gaussian assumption and potential extensions to more realistic models.
- Paper is well-written and organized; figures (notably Figs. 2–4) effectively communicate results.
- Would benefit from a clearer definition of "noise correlations" and more detail on how the fictional two-session data were created.
- Original contribution: novel application of established LDS tools to the stitching problem, addressing a previously unsolved experimental challenge.
- Significant practical impact: enables better use of existing imaging tech and could help map dynamics of much larger neural circuits.
- Strengths summarized: novel problem formulation, strong validation, solid theoretical grounding, practical relevance, and clear presentation.
- Weaknesses summarized: simplified model, artificial benchmarking, unclear scalability to thousands of neurons, unspecified minimum session overlap, limited comparison to other methods.
- Recommendation: accept—the paper is a meaningful, well-validated contribution despite noted limitations.
- Final assessment scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 8.5/10, Significance 9/10; overall "strong accept."
