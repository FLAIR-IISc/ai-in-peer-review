- Proposes a novel method to "stitch" multiple partial neural recordings via a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system (LDS) and validates on simulated and real calcium imaging data.  
- Clear problem statement and strong motivation: addresses inability to record all neurons simultaneously and practical importance for neuroscience.  
- Rigorous experimental validation: recovers coupling/noise correlations in simulations, establishes upper/lower performance bounds, and tests on two real datasets (anesthetized and awake).  
- Quantitative results: presents clear metrics showing strong performance (e.g., 0.84 correlation with the upper bound for noise correlations).  
- Practical utility: method can extend the value of existing datasets without new hardware.  
- Theoretical foundation is weak: lacks rigorous analysis, error bounds, and clear conditions for successful stitching.  
- Model limitations: LDS is a strong simplification—uncertain performance with nonlinear dynamics, without incorporating calcium dynamics, and potential impacts on real data.  
- Scalability concerns: demonstrated on 50–80 neurons but no discussion of computational complexity or effectiveness for populations of hundreds to thousands.  
- Overlap requirements not fully explored: only a few overlap levels tested (60% worked, 50% failed) without systematic analysis of the trade-off.  
- Comparison to related work could be clearer: needs to better distinguish from closely related methods (e.g., [21]) and clarify unique formulation.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and practically valuable with compelling empirical results, but would benefit from stronger theory and scalability analysis.  
- Recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to address theoretical grounding and scalability concerns.
