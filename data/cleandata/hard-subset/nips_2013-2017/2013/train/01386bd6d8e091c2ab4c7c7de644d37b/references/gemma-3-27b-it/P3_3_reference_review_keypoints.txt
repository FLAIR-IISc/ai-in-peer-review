- Overall: Presents a valuable, technically sound method for stitching partial neural recordings via a latent dynamical system; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.

- Problem summary: Combines multiple non-simultaneous, overlapping recordings to infer full neural population dynamics using an LDS and EM.

- Positioning: Well-situated among neural connectivity and sub-sampled observation literature, and clearly differentiated by focusing on correlations between non-simultaneously observed neurons.

- Motivation clarity: Problem formulation is clear and well-motivated (effective whisker barrel example).

- Methodology: Uses a linear Gaussian LDS with EM—appropriate, tractable, and computationally simple for an initial investigation.

- Empirical validation: Convincing results on simulated and real data, notably accurate recovery of noise correlations.

- Benchmarking: Rigorous comparison to upper (fully observed) and lower (naive) bounds highlights method benefits.

- Writing and reproducibility: Paper is well-written, organized, and methods are described in sufficient detail for replication.

- Neuroscience relevance: Method can significantly improve study of large neural circuits given current experimental limits.

- Linearity/Gaussianity limitation: The linear Gaussian model may not capture inherent nonlinear, non-Gaussian neural dynamics.

- Scalability concerns: EM (even with mini-batching) may be computationally prohibitive for very large populations (thousands of neurons).

- Overlap dependence: Performance relies on sufficient overlap between recordings; impact of minimal overlap is not fully explored.

- Initialization sensitivity: EM performance can be sensitive to initialization and may converge to local optima without robust strategies.

- Biological plausibility: Limited analysis of whether inferred connections/dynamics match known anatomy or biology.

- Pro acceptance points: Addresses a major practical problem, is technically sound, demonstrates strong empirical performance, and has potential high impact.

- Con acceptance points: Simplifying model assumptions, potential scalability issues, and reliance on recording overlap.

- Key questions for authors: How performance scales with overlap; alternatives for scalable inference (variational methods/SGD); initialization strategies and stability; alignment with known anatomical connections; extension to more than two recordings.

- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions after addressing the outlined weaknesses and questions.
