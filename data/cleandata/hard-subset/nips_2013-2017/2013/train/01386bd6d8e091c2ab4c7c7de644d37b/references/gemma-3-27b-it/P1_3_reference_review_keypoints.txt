- Proposes stitching non-simultaneous neural recordings by modeling latent linear Gaussian dynamics and using EM to infer correlations, including between never-simultaneously-recorded neurons.  
- Demonstrates method on simulations and real calcium imaging, outperforming a naive per-session approach and approaching a fully observed benchmark.  
- Situates work within latent dynamical systems and incomplete-data literature, and distinguishes itself from prior methods that assume specific stimulus-noise relationships.  
- Novel and practically important problem; clever reuse of statistical tools for a new data-acquisition scenario.  
- Strong theoretical foundation via a linear Gaussian dynamical system model.  
- Empirical validation is compelling: simulated and real-data results, and sensible baselines.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized.  
- High practical relevance for large-scale recordings where full simultaneous observation is infeasible.  
- Authors are thorough in acknowledging limitations such as model simplicity and required session overlap.  
- Main weakness: linear Gaussian assumption simplifies real neural dynamics; impact of this simplification needs more discussion.  
- Main weakness: method requires overlap between sessions, but the sensitivity to amount of overlap and sufficient overlap thresholds are not studied.  
- Main weakness: scalability concerns for EM on very large populations; mini-batch EM is mentioned but more analysis and alternatives (e.g., SGD) are needed.  
- Main weakness: limited analysis of sensitivity to parameter choices (e.g., diagonal noise covariance Q).  
- Main weakness: limited biological interpretation of inferred correlations and links to known circuit motifs.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, provides a novel statistically sound solution, and shows strong empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: simplifying assumptions, overlap and scalability limitations, and insufficient parameter sensitivity and biological interpretation.  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper whose strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss model limitations (linearity/Gaussianity), analyze sensitivity to session overlap, provide more detail on computational cost/scalability, and justify/comment on parameter choices like diagonal Q.
