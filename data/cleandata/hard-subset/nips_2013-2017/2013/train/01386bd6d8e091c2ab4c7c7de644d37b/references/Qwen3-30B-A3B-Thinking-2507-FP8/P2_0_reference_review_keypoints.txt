- Introduces a method to stitch multiple partial recordings to infer full neural population dynamics and correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Models activity as a linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations and fits parameters via EM with Kalman smoothing.
- Validated on simulated and real calcium imaging data, showing clear improvement over a naive averaging baseline.
- Quantitative performance: recovers 73% of true off-diagonal couplings in simulation and achieves 0.84 vs 0.15 correlation to fully observed model on real data.
- Uses appropriate baselines (fully observed upper bound and naive lower bound) to establish method value.
- Requires some overlap between recorded subsets for identifiability; overlap is critical for stitching to work.
- Acknowledges the linear model is a simplification and that more complex models might improve real-data performance.
- Limited exploration of scalability to very large populations (e.g., >1000 neurons) and sensitivity to choice of latent dimensionality.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and provides sufficient methodological detail and clear visualizations.
- Novel contribution distinct from prior work by addressing stitching of temporally separated, non-simultaneous recordings.
- High potential impact for neuroscience by enabling inference of correlations using existing imaging technologies across sessions.
- Strengths: technically sound, well-validated, clear writing, meaningful baselines, and practical relevance.
- Weaknesses: linearity assumption, overlap requirement, limited scalability analysis, and incomplete sensitivity analysis of model parameters.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong, novel, and practically relevant contribution suitable for NIPS.
