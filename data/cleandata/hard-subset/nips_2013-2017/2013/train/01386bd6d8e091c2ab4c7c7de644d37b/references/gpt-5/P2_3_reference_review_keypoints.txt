- Tackles stitching non-simultaneous neural recordings by modeling fluorescence with an LDS and session-specific observation matrices learned via EM.  
- Claims recovery of cross-couplings and noise correlations for non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs from overlapping partial recordings.  
- Conceptually related to classic system identification, LDS with missing data, and latent GLM/LDS neural analyses; novelty is framing and multi-session stitching.  
- Modeling/algorithm (LDS + EM, Kalman smoothing, online-EM variant) is standard and appropriate for the task.  
- Paper lacks formal conditions (observability/controllability, required overlap, input excitation) for identifiability and alignment success.  
- Strong simplifying assumptions: linear-Gaussian dynamics, stationary noise, diagonal Q and R, and simplified calcium dynamics.  
- Synthetic experiments convincingly recover couplings and noise correlations (high r values) with known ground truth.  
- Real-data validation uses a fictional split of simultaneous recordings rather than truly non-simultaneous sessions, limiting ecological validity.  
- Baselines are limited to naive and fully observed LDS; missing comparisons to shared-latent FA/GPFA, Poisson/LDS, and multi-view CCA.  
- Some reported metrics are relative to a fully observed model rather than empirical held-out correlations; more direct metrics suggested.  
- Regularization, overfitting risk (A has O(N^2) parameters), and scalability to large populations are not thoroughly analyzed or demonstrated.  
- Reproducibility issues: notation typos, limited details on initialization, convergence criteria, and computational complexity.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, but contains notation errors that should be fixed.  
- Originality is moderate: mainly an application of standard LDS/EM tools to a useful neuroscience problem.  
- Significance is practical for neuroscience experimental design, but of moderate interest to NeurIPS due to limited theoretical advances.  
- Pros: addresses an important problem, principled/simple model, convincing synthetic validation, real-data gains over naive baseline, practical experimental guidance.  
- Cons: limited methodological novelty, fictional real-data split, missing theoretical identifiability analysis, sparse baseline comparisons, and unclear scalability/regularization.  
- Suggested improvements: give theoretical/empirical phase diagrams for overlap requirements, test on genuine non-simultaneous sessions, compare to multi-view latent methods, report empirical held-out correlations, explore sparsity/structure in A, and provide timing/complexity and initialization details.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept (6/10).  
- Reviewer confidence: 3/5.
