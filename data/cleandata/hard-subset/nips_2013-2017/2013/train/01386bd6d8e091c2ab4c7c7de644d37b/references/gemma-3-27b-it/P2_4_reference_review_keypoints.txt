- Problem: inferring full neural population dynamics from multiple partial, spatially-separated recordings.  
- Method: use a latent dynamical system (LDS) with EM to "stitch" sessions by leveraging strong local coupling.  
- Main result: simulations and mouse calcium imaging show accurate prediction of correlations, including noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Novelty: leveraging spatial proximity and local coupling to infer unobserved population activity is a new, practical contribution.  
- Relation to prior work: clearly positioned relative to prior connectivity and subsampling methods; avoids strong stimulus–noise correlation assumptions made in some prior work (e.g., [12]).  
- Soundness: clear mathematical formulation; linear Gaussian LDS provides tractable analysis and recovers ground-truth in simulations.  
- Empirical validation: applied to real calcium data and outperforms fully observed and naive baselines; awake dataset results are particularly compelling.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, with helpful figures.  
- Weakness — linearity: reliance on linear Gaussian dynamics and Gaussian assumptions for calcium data may limit applicability to real, nonlinear neural circuits.  
- Weakness — computational cost: use of online-EM/minibatches noted, but scalability to thousands of neurons and computational bottlenecks are not fully addressed.  
- Weakness — overlap requirement: stitching requires some session overlap; minimum required overlap and degradation with less overlap are not analyzed.  
- Weakness — parameter sensitivity: sensitivity to parameter choices (e.g., diagonal noise covariance Q) is not investigated.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated paper with convincing results, but with acknowledged limitations that warrant further analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important bottleneck, novel and theoretically sound method, compelling sim+real results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about linearity assumption, scalability, and lack of parameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Rating: Strong Accept (7/10).
