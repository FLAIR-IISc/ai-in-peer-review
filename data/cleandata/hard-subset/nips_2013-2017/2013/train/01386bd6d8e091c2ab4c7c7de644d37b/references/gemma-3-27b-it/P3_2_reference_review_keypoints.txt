- Overall assessment: Strong, theoretically sound method for stitching partial neural recordings; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Main idea: Infer full-population latent dynamics by combining multiple spatially offset partial recordings using a latent dynamical system.  
- Novelty: Original application of established state-space methods to stitching multiple partial recordings and inferring noise correlations.  
- Theoretical foundation: Principled formulation using latent linear dynamical systems with Kalman smoothing and EM for parameter estimation.  
- Robust performance: Convincing results on simulated data and real somatosensory cortex data, approaching fully observed upper bounds.  
- Biological relevance: Directly addresses experimental limits of 2-photon imaging and is well-motivated for neuroscience applications.  
- Presentation: Clear writing, organized exposition, informative figures.  
- Evaluation: Thorough comparison to ideal and naive baselines, demonstrating clear benefits.  
- Key limitation — linear Gaussian assumption: Simplification may miss nonlinear, non-Gaussian neural dynamics and calcium signal properties.  
- Key limitation — overlap requirement: Method requires session overlap; sensitivity to amount of overlap is not fully characterized.  
- Key limitation — scalability: EM complexity may become prohibitive for much larger populations; scalability discussion and optimizations needed.  
- Key limitation — parameter identification: Potential near-degeneracies for large systems; identifiability and regularization need more exploration.  
- Significance: High impact potential by enabling inference of dynamics and connectivity from partial recordings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theory, robust empirical results, clear writing, and important practical utility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Simplifying assumptions (linear Gaussian), overlap dependency, and scalability concerns.  
- Questions for authors: How performance varies with overlap levels; what is EM computational complexity and practical population size limits.  
- Questions for authors (cont.): Have nonlinear or non-Gaussian dynamical models been explored; how to incorporate prior connectivity/dynamics knowledge.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing linear Gaussian assumption, overlap sensitivity, and scalability.
