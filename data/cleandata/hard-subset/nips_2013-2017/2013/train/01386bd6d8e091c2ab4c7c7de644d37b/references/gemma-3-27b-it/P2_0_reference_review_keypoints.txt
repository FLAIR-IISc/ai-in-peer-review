- Introduces a method to “stitch” multiple non-simultaneous neural recordings by modeling population activity with a latent dynamical system to infer whole-circuit dynamics.  
- Positions the work within prior functional connectivity and sub-sampled observation literature and distinguishes it from related methods (notably [12]).  
- Novelty: tackles inferring correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously, a practically important and previously under-addressed problem.  
- Soundness: uses a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system and EM, with model choices and assumptions clearly justified.  
- Empirical validation: strong simulation results and real calcium imaging tests, compared to “fully observed” and “naive” baselines.  
- Clarity: well-written, methods are detailed for expert reproduction, and figures are informative.  
- Significance: enables leveraging partial recordings to study large-scale neural circuit dynamics and infer cross-session correlations.  
- Weakness: reliance on linear Gaussian dynamics may limit realism and the impact of this simplification isn’t fully explored.  
- Weakness: method performance requires session overlap; sensitivity to overlap and guidance for experiment design are insufficient.  
- Weakness: scalability to very large populations (thousands of neurons) and computational cost/optimizations are not addressed.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed reporting on parameter tuning (e.g., regularization) used in EM, reducing reproducibility.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, presents a novel and theoretically sound approach, and provides convincing empirical evidence.  
- Against acceptance: simplifying assumptions, incomplete analysis of overlap effects, and unanswered scalability concerns.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with acknowledged limitations; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
