- Problem: Inferring joint population dynamics and noise correlations from multiple partial, non-simultaneous recordings via a stitched LDS with missing observations.  
- Method: Multi-session linear-Gaussian dynamical system (A,B,Q,R,d) learned by EM with Kalman smoothing; session-specific selection matrices Ck define observed neurons.  
- Main claims: Cross-couplings between non-simultaneous neurons can be inferred, enabling prediction of correlations and activity of unobserved neurons.  
- Validation: Synthetic (no-overlap) and two real 2p GCaMP6s datasets (partial overlap ~60%) with comparisons to fully-observed LDS (upper bound) and per-session fit (naive lower bound).  
- Result: Stitched LDS recovers noise correlations for non-simultaneous pairs and improves held-out neuron activity prediction over naive baseline.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on classic system ID/subspace methods and latent-variable neural models (GPFA, PLDS, latent GLMs); novelty is multi-session framing with known cell IDs.  
- Strength: Addresses an important experimental bottleneck for combining fields-of-view and sessions into full-circuit models.  
- Strength: Simple, scalable approach (LDS + Kalman + EM) with clear mapping from experiments to selection matrices.  
- Strength: Convincing synthetic and real-data empirical demonstrations, including upper/lower bound comparisons.  
- Strength: Sensible discussion of extensions (sparsity, latent inputs) and practical considerations (need for overlap).  
- Weakness: No theoretical identifiability analysis; conditions (observability/controllability, overlap size, input richness) are not precisely stated.  
- Weakness: Real-data evaluation compares to a model-based “fully observed” LDS rather than empirical test-set correlations, creating model-to-model circularity.  
- Weakness: Parameterization uses full N×N A with diagonal Q,R, risking overfitting and instability as N grows; lacks explicit regularization or stability constraints.  
- Weakness: Linear-Gaussian model on dF/F ignores calcium/spiking nonlinearities; interpreting A as functional coupling is problematic given slow sampling.  
- Weakness: Baselines are limited; comparisons to shared-factor models (FA/GPFA/PLDS) or low-rank+neuron-specific observation models are missing.  
- Weakness: Some methodological clarity and notation issues (measurement equation, R indices, undefined hk1:T, stimulus regressors, A stability during EM).  
- Weakness: No real-data demonstration of no-overlap success; sensitivity to overlap fraction and number of sessions is unexplored.  
- Significance: Highly relevant to 2p imaging; empirical finding that stitching can recover non-simultaneous noise correlations is useful though algorithmic novelty is modest.  
- Quality: Generally sound and well executed but needs stronger empirical ground truth validation, clearer assumptions, and more competitive baselines.  
- Pros for acceptance: Practical impact, solid real-data gains over naive baselines, and an easily adoptable method for labs.  
- Cons against acceptance: Missing identifiability theory, reliance on model-based ground truth, limited baselines, and potential over-parameterization without regularization.  
- Suggestions: Directly compare predicted pairwise correlations to empirical test-set correlations from simultaneous data.  
- Suggestions: Empirically map performance vs overlap fraction, session count, and stimulus richness; clarify identifiability conditions.  
- Suggestions: Add regularization/stability constraints (sparsity, spectral radius), and compare to low-rank latent models adapted for missing data.  
- Suggestions: Fix notation/clarity issues, specify stimulus usage, and provide runtime/scaling details.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on addressing evaluation, identifiability, baseline, and clarity concerns.
