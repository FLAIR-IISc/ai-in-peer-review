- Proposal: Joint linear Gaussian dynamical system (LDS) with session-specific selection matrices to "stitch" partially observed recordings and infer cross-session couplings/noise correlations.  
- Inference: Parameters learned by EM with Kalman smoothing across sessions; missing neurons treated as unobserved entries.  
- Evaluation setup: Tested on simulations and calcium imaging using fictional splits of the same recording; compared to a fully observed upper bound and a naive per-subset baseline.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on PLDS/GPFA/system-identification literature and prior calcium-imaging connectivity inference, but explicitly addresses multi-session partial observations.  
- Novelty: Simple but apparently novel demonstration that joint dynamics and noise correlations can be inferred across non-simultaneous recordings.  
- Modeling concern: LDS on fluorescence is computationally tractable but ignores spiking, calcium nonlinearities, neuropil contamination, and assumes diagonal Q/R.  
- Inference concern: EM+Kalman smoothing is appropriate, but no explicit discussion of stability constraints on A or regularization beyond brief notes.  
- Simulation results: Recovers cross-block couplings (r≈0.73) and noise correlations (r≈0.92); strong activity prediction for unobserved neurons.  
- Real-data results: On fictional splits, stitched models predict cross-session correlations well (r≈0.84 vs 0.15 for naive) and approach the fully observed upper bound in awake data.  
- Key limitation: Real-data tests use splits of the same recording, so method is not validated on true multi-session nonstationarities or cell-tracking errors.  
- Identifiability: Paper discusses identifiability up to invertible transforms and how overlap resolves ambiguities, but inconsistency between simulations (no-overlap success) and real data (failure without overlap) needs analysis.  
- Computational scaling: No discussion of runtime or scaling with N, T, and session count; A is dense so smoothing may be cubic in N unless structure exploited.  
- Baselines: Naive baseline is weak; missing comparisons to multi-session factor models, subspace ID, or PLDS/GPFA variants with shared dynamics.  
- Regularization/prioring: Lack of explicit priors/regularization and hyperparameter selection; potential instability of learned A not addressed.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and organized, but has notational typos, and lacks details on initialization, convergence criteria, and complexity for reproducibility.  
- Suggested experiments: Test on truly separate sessions with overlap, add per-session offsets/gains/drift, and measure sensitivity to nonstationarity.  
- Suggested analyses: Characterize sample complexity and performance vs overlap fraction, T per session, SNR, and number of sessions; formalize conditions for no-overlap identifiability.  
- Suggested model extensions: Allow per-session affine calibration, non-diagonal R or low-dimensional observation noise factors, and compare to spike/LDS or PLDS alternatives.  
- Suggested checks: Report whether A is constrained to be stable and how often instability occurs.  
- Reproducibility request: Release code and provide implementation details (initialization strategy, stopping criteria, runtime).  
- Strengths: Clear multi-session LDS formulation, conceptual identifiability insight, solid simulations, controlled real-data evaluation with upper/lower bounds, and useful quantitative metrics.  
- Weaknesses: Missing real multi-session validation, simplistic observation model for calcium imaging, inconsistent overlap findings, limited baselines, and omitted computational/regularization details.  
- Significance: If robust, method could influence experimental design (overlap vs coverage) and analysis pipelines for imaging experiments.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — promising idea and results but needs true multi-session validation, stronger baselines, and robustness/identifiability analysis to be a stronger NIPS contribution.
