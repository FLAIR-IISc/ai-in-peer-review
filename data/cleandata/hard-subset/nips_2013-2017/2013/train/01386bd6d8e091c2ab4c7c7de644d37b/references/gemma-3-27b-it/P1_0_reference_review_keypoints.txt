- Proposes an EM-based method using a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system to stitch multiple partial recordings and infer joint neural population dynamics and correlations.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on simulated data and real mouse calcium imaging, accurately predicting noise correlations including for neurons never simultaneously recorded.
- Positions work relative to prior connectivity/dynamics methods and information-theoretic approaches, highlighting novelty in inferring correlations between non-simultaneously observed neurons.
- Provides theoretical grounding via connection to Takens’ embedding theorem.
- Addresses an important neuroscience limitation: inability to record large populations simultaneously.
- Uses a principled statistical framework (latent dynamical system) that allows tractable inference and clear concept demonstration.
- Offers strong empirical validation with comparisons to fully observed and naive baselines.
- Presents methods and results clearly with informative figures and sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Relies on a simplifying linear Gaussian model, limiting applicability to inherently nonlinear and non-Gaussian neural dynamics.
- Performance is sensitive to the amount of overlap between recording sessions; fewer overlapping neurons degrade stitching.
- Scalability concerns due to computational cost of EM for very large populations.
- Limited exploration of more complex latent models (e.g., nonlinear or time-varying dynamics).
- Lacks extensive discussion of parameter tuning and robustness to model parameter variations.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled approach with strong empirical results, clarity, reproducibility, and high potential impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive linear Gaussian assumption and practical limitations from overlap sensitivity and scalability.
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses; notably impressive noise-correlation prediction.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: discuss implications of linear Gaussian assumption and possible nonlinear extensions; analyze effects of varying overlap; address scalability and computational-efficiency strategies; include discussion of parameter tuning and robustness.
