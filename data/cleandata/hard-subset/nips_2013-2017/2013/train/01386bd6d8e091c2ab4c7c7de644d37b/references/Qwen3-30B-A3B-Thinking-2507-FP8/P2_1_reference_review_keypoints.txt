- Proposes a "stitching" statistical method to combine multiple partial, non-overlapping neural recordings into a coherent latent linear dynamical system (LDS) using EM with Kalman smoothing.  
- Addresses the novel problem of inferring joint dynamics and correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons, which prior methods do not solve.  
- Mathematical formulation is clear and correctly handles missing observations; implementation details are reproducible for experts.  
- Validated on simulations: recovers coupling matrices with correlation 0.73 for non-simultaneous pairs (0.95 for simultaneous) and noise correlations with 0.92 correlation to ground truth.  
- Validated on real 2-photon calcium imaging: predicted correlations for non-simultaneous pairs correlate 0.84 with the fully observed upper bound, far exceeding the naive baseline (0.15).  
- Uses appropriate baselines (fully observed upper bound and naive lower bound) and presents thoughtful discussion of limitations.  
- Writing and figures are excellent; visualizations effectively convey coupling matrices and correlation metrics.  
- Novel contribution: first to adapt latent dynamical systems to stitch non-simultaneous partial recordings at scale.  
- High practical significance: enables inference of noise correlations and joint dynamics from existing partial-recording datasets, guiding experiment design.  
- Strengths: well-motivated problem, sound statistical framework, strong empirical validation, clear presentation, and practical utility.  
- Weaknesses: linear Gaussian LDS may be too simplistic for complex neural dynamics; performance degrades with larger populations.  
- Method requires some overlap between sessions, which may limit applicability; paper does not fully explore how varying overlap affects performance.  
- Open problems noted: scalability to very large circuits and systematic study of overlap-degree effects.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept; technically sound, original, significant, and well-presented — recommend acceptance with confidence.
