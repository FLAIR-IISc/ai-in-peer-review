- Tackles reconstructing large neural population dynamics from spatially-separated, partial recordings by "stitching" sessions.  
- Proposes a latent dynamical system (linear Gaussian LDS) and EM-based estimation to leverage spatial correlations across sessions.  
- Demonstrates in simulations and real calcium-imaging data that the method predicts correlations (especially noise correlations) between non-simultaneously recorded neurons better than a naive baseline.  
- Methodologically well-motivated and flexible; linear Gaussian choice favors tractability and clear exposition.  
- Strong empirical validation: realistic simulations, fictional imaging scenario, and real-data results bolster claims.  
- Clear writing, coherent organization, and informative figures with transparent mathematical notation.  
- Thorough literature review situating the contribution relative to functional connectivity and latent-variable modeling.  
- Major simplification: linear Gaussian assumption may not capture neural nonlinearities and could bias correlation estimates.  
- Model identifiability concerns, especially with limited session overlap, are not fully addressed and may explain performance plateauing with population size.  
- Performance is sensitive to the degree of overlap between recording sessions; behavior with minimal or no overlap needs more analysis.  
- Computational demands are unclear; mini-batch EM is used but resource/time scaling for large populations is not quantified.  
- Stimulus dependence is underexplored: model includes stimulus terms but analysis focuses mainly on noise correlations.  
- Overall: strong, principled, and well-validated contribution with practical impact on studying neural circuits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key practical limitation, solid methodology, strong empirical support, clear presentation, and advances over prior approaches.  
- Arguments against acceptance: relies on simplifying linear Gaussian assumptions, potential identifiability issues, and sensitivity to session overlap.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: expand discussion of linear Gaussian limitations and their impact; address model identifiability in more depth (e.g., Hessian or constraints); analyze overlap percentage vs. prediction accuracy more thoroughly; quantify computational cost and scaling; assess effect on stimulus-dependent response estimation.
