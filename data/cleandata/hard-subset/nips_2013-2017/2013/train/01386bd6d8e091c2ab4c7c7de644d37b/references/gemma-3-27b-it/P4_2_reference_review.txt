This paper addresses the problem of inferring neural population dynamics from multiple partial recordings of the same neural circuit. The core idea is to “stitch” together information from non-simultaneous recordings by formulating the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system. The authors demonstrate, both on simulated and experimental data (mouse somatosensory cortex calcium imaging), that this approach can accurately predict correlations between neurons that were not simultaneously recorded.

The paper is well-motivated, building upon the limitations of current imaging technology and the strong connectivity within neural circuits. The problem is clearly stated, and the proposed method is conceptually sound, drawing from established techniques like latent dynamical systems and the EM algorithm. The experimental validation on real calcium imaging data adds significant value, showcasing the applicability of the method to a biologically relevant problem. The comparison to both a fully observed model (upper bound) and a naive approach (lower bound) provides a clear benchmark for assessing performance. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The approach of combining partial recordings to infer joint dynamics is a valuable contribution, particularly given the limitations of current experimental techniques. While the individual components (latent dynamical systems, EM) are not new, their application to this specific problem of "stitching" together data is novel and impactful.
* **Technical Soundness:** The mathematical formulation of the model is clear and well-defined. The use of a linear Gaussian dynamical system allows for tractable inference, and the authors acknowledge the potential for more complex models in future work. The EM algorithm is appropriately applied and justified.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The methods section provides sufficient detail for reproduction, and the results are presented clearly with informative figures. The explanation of the "fictional imaging scenario" is helpful for understanding the evaluation metrics.
* **Significance:** The ability to infer correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons has significant implications for understanding neural circuit function. The method could facilitate the analysis of larger circuits than currently possible and enable the testing of theories of neural computation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Model Simplifications:** The use of a linear Gaussian dynamical system is a simplification of the underlying biological reality. While justified for tractability, it may limit the accuracy of the model in capturing complex neural dynamics. The authors acknowledge this but a more detailed discussion of the potential impact of these simplifications would be beneficial.
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The paper does not thoroughly address the sensitivity of the results to parameter choices (e.g., the choice of initial conditions for the EM algorithm, the regularization parameters). A sensitivity analysis would increase confidence in the robustness of the method.
* **Computational Cost:** The authors mention using mini-batch EM for computational efficiency, but a more detailed discussion of the computational demands of the method, especially for large-scale datasets, would be valuable.
* **Overlap Requirement:** The finding that some overlap between recording sessions is critical for success is significant but requires more detailed exploration. How does the amount of overlap influence performance? Are there optimal strategies for designing recording sessions to maximize overlap?

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses an important and challenging problem in neuroscience.
* The proposed method is novel, technically sound, and well-validated.
* The experimental results demonstrate the applicability of the method to real-world data.
* The paper is clearly written and organized, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The reliance on a simplified linear Gaussian dynamical system may limit the generalizability of the results.
* A more thorough sensitivity analysis and discussion of computational cost are needed. 



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed method is novel and has the potential to significantly advance our understanding of neural circuit function. While the simplifications made in the model and the lack of a detailed sensitivity analysis are weaknesses, they are outweighed by the strengths of the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above. Specifically, a discussion on the limitations imposed by the linear Gaussian assumption and a brief sensitivity analysis would strengthen the paper considerably.
