- Proposes stitching non-simultaneous neural recordings by fitting a linear Gaussian LDS with session-specific selection matrices via EM/online EM.  
- Demonstrates on simulations that cross-couplings and noise correlations between non-overlapping subsets can be recovered.  
- Uses fictional multi-session splits of 2-photon calcium data to show stitched model predicts pairwise correlations for non-simultaneous pairs better than a naive baseline and near a “fully observed” upper bound.  
- Builds on prior latent state-space and partially observed population models, framing a simple, scalable instantiation for calcium imaging.  
- Strength: tackles a high-impact experimental constraint (limited field-of-view / multi-session aggregation).  
- Strength: conceptually clear formulation (LDS + known selection matrices) with a replicable EM procedure.  
- Strength: empirical support from simulations and real-data splits indicating improved prediction of non-simultaneous correlations.  
- Strength: provides practical guidance that overlap across sessions is important and discusses potential extensions (correlated inputs, sparsity).  
- Weakness: evaluation on real data lacks direct comparison to empirical ground-truth correlations and absolute error metrics (e.g., RMSE); should report correlation-with-empirical and uncertainty.  
- Weakness: baseline comparisons are too weak; needs stronger baselines (e.g., FA/GPFA alignment, shared-latent LDS, shared stimulus + low-rank noise, regularized MAR with missing data).  
- Weakness: identifiability claims are informal; requests formal conditions for identifiability and sensitivity analysis varying overlap fraction/selection.  
- Weakness: model misspecification risk—linear-Gaussian LDS with diagonal Q/R ignores calcium dynamics and nonlinearities, conflating measurement dynamics with network couplings.  
- Weakness: regularization and stability details are missing—report spectral/ridge/sparsity constraints, initialization, eigenvalue spectra, and training diagnostics.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—EM/Kalman smoothing scales poorly with latent dimensionality; request runtime/memory scaling, wall-clock times, and strategies for large N (block-diagonal, low-rank+ sparse).  
- Weakness: validation of “noise correlations” depends on model; recommend computing empirical residual correlations after removing stimulus-locked responses where possible.  
- Weakness: real multi-session stitching faces cell-registration errors and drift; a demonstration on real separate sessions or robustness to mis-assignments is needed.  
- Quality: core method is technically sound and simulations are reasonable, but real-data evaluation needs stronger baselines and ground-truth comparisons.  
- Clarity: generally clear but has notation typos and missing details (Eq.2, definitions, whether R is diagonal, initialization, stopping criteria, hyperparameters).  
- Originality: straightforward synthesis rather than novel methodology; novelty mainly in application/demonstration to calcium imaging.  
- Significance: high potential impact if validated more rigorously; promising but requires stronger empirical rigor for broad adoption.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on stronger empirical evaluation (direct empirical comparisons, at least one stronger baseline), and clearer treatment of identifiability, regularization, and scaling.
