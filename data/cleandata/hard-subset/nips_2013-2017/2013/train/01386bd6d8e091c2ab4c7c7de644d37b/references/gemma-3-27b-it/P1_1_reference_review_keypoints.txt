- The paper proposes a latent linear-Gaussian dynamical system to “stitch” partial neural recordings and infer joint population dynamics across sessions.  
- It positions itself against prior connectivity and subsampling methods, highlighting its novel focus on non-simultaneously observed neurons and citing Takens’ embedding as theoretical grounding.  
- Addresses an important experimental limitation (limited field-of-view) with a principled statistical framework.  
- Methodology is sound and well-detailed, including an EM implementation and computational considerations.  
- Empirical validation is strong: simulated and real calcium data show recovery of noise correlations and near “fully observed” performance.  
- Writing and organization are clear, with a well-motivated introduction and structured results.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge model limitations (linearity, Gaussianity) and scope for more complex models.  
- Reliance on a linear Gaussian model may fail to capture realistic nonlinear neural dynamics, limiting applicability.  
- Parameter identifiability on real data is uncertain because benchmarking uses a fictional imaging scenario without ground truth.  
- Method performance appears to require substantial overlap between sessions (e.g., ~60%), reducing applicability when overlap is low.  
- Scalability claims lack detailed computational benchmarks for very large populations (thousands of neurons); EM cost could be a bottleneck.  
- For acceptance: tackles a practically important problem with a novel, theoretically-grounded method and strong empirical results.  
- For acceptance: clear writing, organization, and potential to expand studies of large neural circuits.  
- Against acceptance: strong linear/Gaussian assumptions and fictional benchmarking raise concerns about real-data robustness.  
- Against acceptance: overlap requirement and unclear scalability may limit practical use in many experimental settings.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with rigorous implementation and convincing validation, but constrained by model assumptions and practical requirements.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: discuss impacts of linear-Gaussian assumptions and possible nonlinear extensions, provide more detailed computational scaling/benchmarks, and investigate or quantify minimum overlap needed for reliable inference.
