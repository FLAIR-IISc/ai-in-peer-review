- Overall: Recommends acceptance — method is compelling, technically sound, and well-demonstrated on simulations and real data.  
- Problem: Tackles inferring full-population dynamics from multiple partial recordings of the same neural circuit.  
- Core idea: Uses a latent dynamical system to stitch sequential recordings by leveraging local coupling between nearby neurons.  
- Related work: Positions method relative to functional connectivity and subsampled-data literature, highlighting gaps in inferring connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons.  
- Novelty/significance: Inferring correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously is a major advance for reconstructing larger circuits.  
- Technical soundness: Methodology (latent dynamics + EM) is appropriate, clearly formulated, and assumptions (e.g., Gaussian noise) are justified for tractability.  
- Empirical validation: Strong results on simulated and real calcium imaging data, with useful comparisons to fully observed and naive baselines.  
- Clarity/organization: Paper is very well written, methods are accessible, and figures are informative.  
- Relevance to venue: Combines machine learning techniques with a biologically relevant problem, making it suitable for NIPS.  
- Limitation — linear Gaussian: The linear Gaussian model is a simplifying assumption that may not capture real neural nonlinearity.  
- Limitation — overlap requirement: Method performance depends on overlap between recorded populations; minimal overlap is a practical constraint.  
- Limitation — computational cost: Scaling to thousands of neurons could be challenging despite online-EM efficiency.  
- Limitation — parameter sensitivity: Results may depend on choices like EM initialization and noise covariance structure and need discussion.  
- Suggestion — Q matrix: Briefly discuss how assuming diagonal Q limits modeling of correlated noise sources.  
- Suggestion — coupling truncation: Clarify rationale for truncating coupling weights in simulations.  
- Suggestion — accessibility: Make the explanation in Section 3.1 more accessible to readers unfamiliar with latent-variable identification.  
- Suggestion — figures: Adjust Figure 3 color scales for better visual clarity of coupling matrices.  
- Suggestion — broader applications: Mention potential applications beyond correlation inference, such as decoding or improved circuit modeling.  
- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a novel, well-validated, and clearly presented method.  
- Against acceptance: Concerns include the linear-Gaussian simplification, overlap requirement, and potential scalability issues.
