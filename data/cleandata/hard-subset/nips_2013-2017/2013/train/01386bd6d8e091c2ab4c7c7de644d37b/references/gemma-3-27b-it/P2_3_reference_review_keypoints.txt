- Proposes a statistical method to "stitch" partial recordings into a joint latent dynamical model to infer cross-couplings and noise correlations across non-simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Situates the work well within prior literature and highlights novelty relative to latent GLMs and anatomical reconstruction approaches.
- Addresses a practically important neuroscience bottleneck by leveraging existing partial-imaging data.
- Uses a well-motivated latent dynamical system framework and clearly explains an adapted EM algorithm.
- Provides strong empirical validation on simulated and real calcium imaging data, outperforming naive baselines and approaching fully observed model performance.
- Presents results and figures clearly; paper is well-written and organized.
- Novel application of established techniques (LDS, EM) to stitching partial recordings and inferring across-session noise correlations.
- Relies on a linear Gaussian dynamical system assumption; limited exploration of extensions to nonlinear or non-Gaussian dynamics.
- Lacks thorough investigation of sensitivity to parameter choices (EM initialization, regularization) and robustness analysis.
- Requires some overlap between sessions for successful stitching; the dependence on overlap amount and minimum requirements is underexplored.
- Limited discussion and analysis of computational complexity and scalability to populations larger than ~60–200 neurons.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, technically sound, strongly validated, clearly presented, and novel.
- Arguments against acceptance: simplifying assumptions may limit applicability, limited parameter sensitivity and scalability analysis, and insufficient study of overlap requirements.
- Overall assessment: a strong, original, and significant contribution with acknowledged but addressable limitations.
- Recommendation: Accept.
