- Proposes a novel statistical method to "stitch" multiple partial recordings to infer joint neural population dynamics and correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Models the problem as a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system fit with EM and Kalman smoothing.  
- Technically sound with clear theoretical foundation demonstrating recovery of parameters for non-simultaneous neuron pairs.  
- Thorough experimental validation on synthetic data (with ground truth) and real calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Strong baselines: compares to a naive averaging baseline and a fully observed upper-bound model.  
- Simulation results: stitching recovers noise correlations with 0.92 correlation to ground truth vs. 0.15 for naive.  
- Real-data results: stitching predicts correlations with 0.84 correlation to fully observed model vs. 0.15 for naive; predicts unobserved activity with 0.70 vs. 0.23 for naive.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: linear Gaussian assumption, need for overlap between recordings, and potential parameter degeneracy in large systems.  
- Well-written and organized; methods are detailed enough for reproducibility and figures effectively illustrate results.  
- Minor clarity improvement: motivate why linear Gaussian assumption is reasonable despite neural non-linearities.  
- Original contribution: enables inference of correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons without restrictive assumptions used by prior work.  
- Significant practical impact: allows better use of existing imaging data to study larger circuits despite experimental recording limits.  
- Weaknesses: simplifying linear Gaussian model, limited exploration of scaling beyond two sessions, unclear sensitivity to overlap amount, and no treatment of non-Gaussian noise or nonlinear dynamics.  
- Overall recommendation: technically strong, novel, and impactful—recommended for acceptance (Accept).
