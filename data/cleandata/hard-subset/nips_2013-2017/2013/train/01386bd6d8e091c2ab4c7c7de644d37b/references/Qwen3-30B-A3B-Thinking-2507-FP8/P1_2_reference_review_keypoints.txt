- Presents a method to "stitch" partial, non-simultaneous neural recordings to infer joint population dynamics.  
- Models neural activity with a linear Gaussian dynamical system and fits parameters via EM with Kalman smoothing.  
- Validated on synthetic and real calcium imaging data; claims to predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  

Strengths:
- Clear problem statement and strong motivation using a concrete neuroscience example.  
- Solid theoretical grounding drawing on prior results about reconstructing dynamics from partial observations.  
- Thorough validation with comparisons to upper- and lower-bound baselines.  
- Appropriate, tractable technical approach (LDS + EM) with reproducible implementation details.  
- Results are clearly presented with effective visualizations.  

Weaknesses:
- Linear Gaussian model is overly simplistic and limits applicability to nonlinear, non-Gaussian neural dynamics.  
- Insufficient systematic analysis of sensitivity to key parameters, especially required overlap between sessions.  
- Abstract and discussion overstate the method's significance given current limitations.  
- Lacks practical guidance for practitioners (number of sessions, optimal overlap, computational costs).  
- Limited comparison to alternative methods (e.g., nonlinear models or other dynamical formulations).  

Qualitative evaluation:
- Quality: technically sound and well-supported, but reliance on linear model is a major caveat.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized, though some EM/Kalman implementation details need clarification.  
- Originality: novel application of stitching non-simultaneous recordings using established tools.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem but significance is tempered by model and overlap constraints.  

Recommendation:
- Recommend accept with minor revisions.  

Requested revisions:
- More thorough discussion of limitations of the linear Gaussian model and failure modes.  
- Systematic analysis of performance vs. degree of session overlap.  
- Moderate claims about the method's broad significance.  
- Add practical implementation guidance for neuroscience users.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and well-motivated approach to an important neuroscience problem.  
- Technically sound method with comprehensive validation and clear baselines.  
- Fills a gap in the literature on stitching non-simultaneous recordings.  

Cons against acceptance:
- Simplistic model may limit real-world applicability.  
- Some claims are overstated and key parameter sensitivity is underexplored.  
- Practical implementation and comparisons to other approaches are insufficient.
