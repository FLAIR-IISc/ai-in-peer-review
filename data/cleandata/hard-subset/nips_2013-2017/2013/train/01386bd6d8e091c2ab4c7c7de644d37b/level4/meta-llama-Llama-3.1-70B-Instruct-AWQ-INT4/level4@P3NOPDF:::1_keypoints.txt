- Contribution: Introduces a novel statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets, expanding analyzable population sizes for dynamics analysis.
- Applications: Demonstrated on simulated data and mouse calcium imaging, estimating correlations across populations not imaged simultaneously.
- Model: Frames the problem as a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations, parameterized by functional coupling, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.
- Estimation: Parameters are learned from data using a variant of the EM algorithm.
- Performance discrepancy: Works well on simulated data (even with non-overlapping populations) but real data requires population overlap; authors don’t explain this discrepancy or the differing correlation results between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b).
- Strength: Able to recover pairwise correlations for non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs, a notable capability.
- Writing/figures: Paper is well-written overall but figure legends and in-text figure descriptions need more detail and clearer takeaways.
- Originality: Builds on prior connectivity inference work but is novel in inferring functional connections among non-simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Significance: Offers a promising approach to overcome limits of simultaneous imaging, enabling larger-scale circuit-level neural computation studies.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper using a relatively simple linear dynamics model to recover measures that are otherwise difficult to obtain.
