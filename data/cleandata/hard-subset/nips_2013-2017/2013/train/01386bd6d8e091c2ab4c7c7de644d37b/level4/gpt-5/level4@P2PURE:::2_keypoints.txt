- Strong, powerful idea: sequentially recording subnetworks and stitching submodels can infer properties of a large network; extension to point-process data could broaden applicability.
- Reviewer unaware of closely related work; cites Pillow & Latham as the closest prior.
- Unclear how method depends on linear-Gaussian assumptions; paper lacks intuition on how fitting fails when assumptions are violated.
- In the extreme case where a single subset can estimate the full model up to a linear transform, it’s unclear what additional subsets add versus collecting more data from one subset.
- Real-data results only work when populations overlap, suggesting simulations don’t capture full complexity; need intuition for how overlap constrains the model.
- For real neural data, explained noise correlations may reflect common input (latent variables) rather than direct pairwise couplings; couplings’ poor match to ground truth in Fig. 3b raises this concern.
- Paper is light on estimation details: the M-step equation is presented as a main contribution but is neither derived nor explained.
- If the M-step is trivial to LDS experts, that would reduce the paper’s novelty—clearer exposition is needed to convey significance.
- Overall: neat idea and promising real-data tests, but several critical details and explanations remain unclear.
