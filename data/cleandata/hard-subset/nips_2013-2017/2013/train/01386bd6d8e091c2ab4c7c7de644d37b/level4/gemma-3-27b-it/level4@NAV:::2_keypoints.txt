- Novel, potentially powerful approach to inferring large-network properties by sequentially modeling subnetworks and integrating sub-models.  
- Extension to point-process data is promising and applicable when channel availability changes frequently.  
- Appears largely original, distinct from but related to Pillow & Latham (2008).  
- Unclear how the method performs when assumptions deviate from linear-Gaussian; no analysis of performance degradation with increasing departures.  
- Unclear incremental benefit of adding additional subsets versus simply increasing data from a single subset when the full model is estimable from one subset.  
- Requirement for population overlap in real-data analysis needs further investigation; mechanism by which overlap constrains the model is unexplained.  
- Real-data noise correlations may be driven by common input; fitted pairwise couplings show limited correspondence to true couplings (Fig. 3b).  
- A latent-variable model for noise correlations might be more appropriate than pairwise couplings given likely violations of linearity.  
- Manuscript spends substantial space on intro/discussion but lacks sufficient detail and derivation of the estimation process (notably the M-step), weakening perceived novelty.  
- Overall promising idea validated on real data but requires clearer explanations and more thorough methodological details.
