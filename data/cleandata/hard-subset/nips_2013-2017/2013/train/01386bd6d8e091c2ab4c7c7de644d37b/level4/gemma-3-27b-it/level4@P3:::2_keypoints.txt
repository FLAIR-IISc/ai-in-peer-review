- Proposes a novel statistical method to “stitch” sequential, partially observed neural recordings by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Addresses a practical limitation of neural recording technologies by enabling dynamics inference for larger populations and predicting noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Builds on latent dynamical systems; claimed to be first to infer functional connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons in this manner.  
- Related prior work does not directly address non-simultaneous recordings or suffers from differing assumptions/scalability issues.  

Strengths:
- Novelty: tackles a crucial neuroscience problem with a new approach.  
- Theoretical foundation: grounded in established latent dynamical systems framework.  
- Empirical validation: demonstrated on simulations and real calcium imaging, with accurate noise-correlation predictions.  
- Potential impact: could substantially expand the size of populations for which dynamics are characterized.  

Weaknesses:
- Linear-Gaussian assumption limits applicability; paper lacks sufficient analysis of robustness or mitigation strategies.  
- Unclear what additional information non-overlapping subsets provide, especially when a single subset could identify the model up to linear transform.  
- Requirement for overlap between recorded populations in real data is surprising and unexplained.  
- Noise-correlation interpretation is ambiguous—could reflect common input rather than direct couplings; model extensions to address this are not discussed.  
- Estimation details (notably the M-step) are terse and need clearer, more accessible explanation.  

Overall assessment:
- Strong, theoretically sound, and empirically promising work, but weakened by linear-Gaussian limitations, unexplained overlap requirement, and sparse estimation details.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Solves a significant practical problem, proposes a theoretically grounded solution, and shows promising simulated and real-data results with high potential impact.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Applicability limited by linear-Gaussian assumption, unexplained overlap requirement, and insufficient methodological detail for full evaluation.  

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions: expand discussion on linear-Gaussian limitations and mitigation, clarify information gain from non-overlapping subsets, investigate and explain overlap requirement, and provide more detailed estimation (M-step) exposition.
