- Proposes stitching sequential partial recordings into a latent linear dynamical system (LDS) with missing observations.  
- Validated on simulated data and two-photon calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Key result: successfully predicts noise correlations between neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously.  
- Method is technically sound; LDS is a reasonable, tractable first choice.  
- Strong validation design using clear upper (fully observed) and lower (naive) bounds.  
- Central strength: effectively recovers population statistics (noise correlations) on real cortical data.  
- Simulated data allowed recovery without overlap, but real-data experiments required partial neuron overlap; method failed with no overlap.  
- Needs explanation why overlap is necessary in real data (likely due to simulation simplifications: linearity, stationarity, high SNR).  
- Recovery of coupling matrix A drops substantially on real data (0.38) versus simulated (0.73), suggesting model mismatch or identifiability issues.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, but figure legends are too minimal and should be expanded.  
- Framing is novel and distinct from prior work that requires strong assumptions or lacks scalability.  
- High significance: could enable construction of functional models of full microcircuits from partial recordings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, principled and novel method, strong empirical demonstrations, high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unexplained simulation vs real-data discrepancies and clarity issues (figure legends).  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the noted discrepancies and clarity improvements.
