- Novel idea: sequentially record from subnetworks and combine sub-models to infer large-network properties; extension to point-process data is useful and appears novel (closest work: Pillow & Latham).
- Method seems powerful but limitations under deviations from the linear-Gaussian assumption are not clearly addressed.
- Paper lacks analysis or intuition on how performance degrades when data departs from the linear-Gaussian assumption.
- Unclear how much extra information other subsets provide when a full model can be estimated from a single subset up to linear transformations.
- Requirement of overlap between populations for real-data application is concerning and suggests simulations do not capture full problem complexity.
- No intuition provided on how overlap constrains model identifiability or estimation.
- In real-data experiments, it is unclear whether explained noise correlations reflect direct pairwise couplings or common inputs/latent variables.
- Noise correlations might be better modeled with latent variables rather than pairwise couplings, casting doubt on coupling estimates.
- Paper spends much space on introduction/discussion but provides limited details on the estimation procedure.
- The M-step equation presented as a main contribution is not thoroughly explained or derived, reducing perceived novelty.
- Overall: promising, novel idea tested on real data but insufficient explanation and missing analyses reduce the paper’s impact.
