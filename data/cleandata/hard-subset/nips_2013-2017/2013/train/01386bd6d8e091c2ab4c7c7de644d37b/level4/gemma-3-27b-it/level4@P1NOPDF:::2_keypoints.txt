- Demonstrates stitching subnetwork recordings to infer large network properties, extendable to point-process data and potentially widely applicable.  
- Core idea appears powerful and not closely matched by prior work (closest: Pillow & Latham).  
- Unclear how well the method generalizes beyond the linear-Gaussian assumption; paper lacks intuition on failure modes.  
- If the full model is identifiable from one subset up to a linear transform, it’s unclear what additional subsets add versus more data from the same subset.  
- The necessity of overlap between recorded populations is not explained; need intuition for how overlap better constrains the model.  
- In real (nonlinear) data, explained noise correlations may reflect common input or latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings; estimated couplings weakly correlate with true couplings (Fig. 3b).  
- Estimation details are sparse: the M-step equation is presented without sufficient explanation or derivation, undermining perceived novelty.  
- Overall: promising and neat idea tested on real data, but several methodological details and explanations are missing.
