- Proposes stitching sequentially recorded subnetworks (extended to point-process data) to infer network properties — novel and compelling contribution.  
- Unclear how robust the method is to deviations from linear-Gaussian assumptions; paper lacks intuition on failure modes.  
- Unresolved whether adding more subsets yields more coupling information versus simply collecting more data from a single subset.  
- Real-data model appears to require overlap between populations; simulations do not capture this and the paper doesn’t explain how overlap constrains the method.  
- In real data, noise correlations are well fit but inferred couplings poorly match ground truth — may reflect common input/latent structure rather than true pairwise couplings.  
- Major methodological details (notably the M-step equation) are presented without derivation or explanation, weakening the perceived novelty.  
- Paper spends much on intro/discussion but provides minimal detail on estimation and algorithmic implementation.  
- Overall: promising idea with real-data validation, but key methodological explanations and analyses of limitations are inadequately explained.
