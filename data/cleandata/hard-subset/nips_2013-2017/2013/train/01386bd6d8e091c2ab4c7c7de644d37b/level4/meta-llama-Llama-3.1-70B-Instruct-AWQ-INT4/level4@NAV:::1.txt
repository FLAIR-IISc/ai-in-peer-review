The paper presents a novel statistical approach to integrate sequentially imaged neuronal populations, thereby enhancing the characterization of population dynamics beyond the limitations imposed by simultaneous imaging. This method offers a more comprehensive understanding of neural circuits that cannot be imaged in their entirety due to experimental and technical constraints. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their approach using both simulated and experimental calcium imaging data from mice, showcasing its ability to estimate correlations in variability across non-simultaneously imaged populations.

The authors formulate the problem as a latent dynamical system with missing observations, employing a simple linear Gaussian system. The model incorporates four key parameters: a functional coupling matrix, a stimuli receptive fields' matrix to capture stimulus-dependent population activity, and two covariance matrices for neural and measurement noise. Parameter estimation from experimental data is achieved using a variant of the standard EM algorithm.

Quality:
The model performs well on simulated data with non-overlapping neuronal populations but requires a certain level of overlap between populations to yield reasonable results when applied to real data. This raises the question of why overlap was not necessary in the simulated data example. Furthermore, the authors do not address the significant difference in correlation estimates between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b). The model's strength lies in its ability to recover pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs.

Clarity:
The paper is well-written, although figure legends could be more descriptive, and the text accompanying figures in the main body could provide more elaboration.

Originality:
While the authors acknowledge previous works on inferring functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging data and electrophysiological measurements, their approach uniquely addresses the inference of functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.

Significance:
The method proposed in this paper offers a potential solution to the fundamental limitations of simultaneous neuronal imaging, which restricts our understanding of neural computation at the circuit level. By expanding these capabilities, this paper contributes significantly to the field.

Overall, this is a well-crafted paper that leverages a relatively simple linear dynamics model to capture elusive measures, making it a valuable contribution to the field.
