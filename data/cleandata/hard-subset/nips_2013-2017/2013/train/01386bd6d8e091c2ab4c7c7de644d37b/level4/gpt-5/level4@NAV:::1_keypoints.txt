- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuronal populations to characterize population dynamics beyond simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Applies the method to simulated data and mouse calcium imaging, showing estimation of shared variability across non-simultaneously recorded populations.
- Formulates the problem as a latent dynamical system with missing observations instantiated as a linear Gaussian model.
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive field matrix, neural (process) noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.
- Parameters are learned using a variant of the EM algorithm.
- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real data requires some population overlap for reasonable estimates.
- Unclear why the simulated example required no overlap and why there is a substantial discrepancy between simulated and real-data correlation estimates.
- Strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neuron pairs never measured simultaneously.
- Manuscript is generally well written, but figure legends lack detail and the main text under-explains figures.
- Originality: builds on prior connectivity inference work but uniquely infers functional relations among neurons not imaged concurrently.
- Significance: addresses fundamental limits of simultaneous imaging and extends the ability to probe circuit-level computations.
