- Introduces a statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged neuronal populations, enabling characterization of larger circuits and estimating correlations between neurons not imaged simultaneously; validated on simulated and mouse calcium data.  
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations in a linear-Gaussian model, with four parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Parameters are estimated from data using a modified EM algorithm.  
- Model performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real-data results require a minimum level of population overlap; the discrepancy between simulated and real correlation estimates is not explained.  
- Main strength is recovering pairwise correlations between neurons imaged at different times.  
- Paper is well written, but figure legends lack detail and the text insufficiently explains figures' key insights.  
- Original and significant: addresses a gap in prior work by inferring interactions between neurons never imaged simultaneously, expanding circuit-level analysis beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Overall: a strong, practical advance using a simple linear dynamical model to recover otherwise inaccessible measures for circuit neuroscience.
