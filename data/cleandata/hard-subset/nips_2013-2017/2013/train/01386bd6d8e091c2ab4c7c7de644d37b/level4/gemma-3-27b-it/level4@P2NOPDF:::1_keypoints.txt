- Presents a statistical method to integrate sequentially imaged neuronal populations, expanding effective population size for circuit analysis.  
- Frames the problem as parameter estimation of a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system (coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural and measurement noise covariances).  
- Parameters are estimated using a modified Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Validated on both simulated and experimental calcium imaging data, recovering pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping populations, but real-data results require substantial overlap between imaged populations for reliability.  
- The paper does not explain why simulations succeeded without overlap while real data needs overlap—this discrepancy needs investigation.  
- Authors fail to address the notable difference in correlation magnitudes between simulated and experimental results; potential causes (noise levels, real activity complexity) are not discussed.  
- Generally well-written and methodologically clear, but figure legends and figure-related text lack sufficient detail and explanation.  
- Original contribution: specifically tackles inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged populations, unlike prior work focused on simultaneous recordings.  
- Significant because it offers a practical way to mitigate the experimental limitation of not being able to simultaneously record large neural populations.  
- Overall assessment: strong methodological paper that extracts valuable pairwise correlations from limited data; addressing overlap and correlation-magnitude concerns would strengthen it—recommend accept.
