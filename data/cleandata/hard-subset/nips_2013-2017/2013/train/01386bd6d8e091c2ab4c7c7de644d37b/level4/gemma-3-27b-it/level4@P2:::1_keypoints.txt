- Introduces a method to infer neural population dynamics by "stitching" sequentially recorded subsets into a larger latent dynamical system.  
- Frames the problem as fitting a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system with missing observations, estimated via EM.  
- Demonstrates recovery of functional coupling, stimulus receptive fields, and noise covariances on simulated and experimental mouse somatosensory data.  
- Core result: predicts noise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously.  
- Strong performance on simulated data, accurately recovering coupling matrices and correlations.  
- Experimental performance depends heavily on substantial overlap between sequential recordings.  
- There is a notable discrepancy in correlation recovery between simulations (strong) and experiments (weaker) that needs explanation.  
- Method is technically sound but would benefit from deeper discussion of limitations and error sources.  
- Paper is generally well-written and logically structured.  
- Figure legends and in-text figure explanations are insufficiently detailed and should be expanded.  
- Builds on existing latent dynamical system literature but is novel in addressing stitching of non-simultaneous recordings.  
- Significant potential impact for systems neuroscience by enabling study of larger circuits and predicting noise correlations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel problem focus, demonstrated on simulated and real data, predictive power for non-simultaneous correlations, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: sensitivity to session overlap, unexplained simulation-vs-experiment discrepancy, and unclear figure descriptions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address performance discrepancy and improve figure clarity.
