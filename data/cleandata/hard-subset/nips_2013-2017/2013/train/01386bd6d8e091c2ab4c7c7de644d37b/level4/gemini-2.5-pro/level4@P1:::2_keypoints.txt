- Proposes stitching multiple sequential, overlapping recordings into a single latent linear dynamical system (LDS) to infer full-population properties.  
- Demonstrates method on simulated and two real 2-photon calcium imaging datasets, including anesthetized and awake animals.  
- Successfully recovers noise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously, approaching performance of a fully observed model.  
- Novel application of latent-variable stitching distinct from prior partially observed population work.  
- Strength: addresses an important experimental limitation and could apply broadly (e.g., multi-day recordings).  
- Strength: validation strategy includes upper/lower performance bounds and realistic real-data tests.  
- Weakness: methods section lacks detail—M-step updates are presented without derivation or clear explanation.  
- Weakness: reliance on linear-Gaussian LDS raises concerns about sensitivity to nonlinearity and non-Gaussian noise in real neural data.  
- Weakness: overlapping neurons appear necessary on real data but not simulations; the paper lacks analysis explaining this discrepancy.  
- Weakness: inferred coupling parameters correlate weakly with fully observed-model couplings, suggesting noise correlations may reflect common input or low-dimensional latent structure rather than direct pairwise couplings.  
- Recommendation: accept only if authors substantially clarify the estimation derivations and provide a deeper discussion/analysis of model assumptions and the role of overlap.
