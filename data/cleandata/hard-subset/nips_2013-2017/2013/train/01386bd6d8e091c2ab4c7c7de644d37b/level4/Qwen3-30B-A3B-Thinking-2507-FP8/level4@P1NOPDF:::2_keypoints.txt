- Proposes sequentially sampling subnetworks, estimating local models, and stitching them to infer global network properties; potentially valuable for sparsely recorded point-process data.
- Core idea is promising and largely novel; closest prior is Pillow and Latham (2008).
- Unclear how method generalizes beyond linear-Gaussian assumptions and how performance degrades with deviations.
- Unanswered whether additional subsets provide information beyond simply collecting more data from a single subset when the full model is recoverable up to linear transforms.
- Real-data success requires overlap between recorded populations, but the paper gives no intuition for why overlap is necessary or what structural property it provides.
- Linear-Gaussian simulations appear not to capture real-world complexities, given the overlap dependence in real data.
- Noise correlations fit well, but pairwise couplings correlate poorly with ground truth, raising concern that fitted correlations may reflect common input rather than direct interactions.
- Suggests latent-variable models might better explain noise correlations than pairwise couplings in non-linear/real settings.
- Paper emphasizes introduction and discussion but provides minimal technical detail on the estimation procedure and M-step; lacks derivation or intuition.
- Omission of derivation undermines novelty claims, as the key M-step may be trivial for LDS experts.
- Recommends deeper analysis of non-Gaussian scenarios, clearer justification of overlap requirements, and a rigorous derivation of the core algorithm to validate real-world utility.
