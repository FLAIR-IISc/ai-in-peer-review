- Presents a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuronal populations to characterize population dynamics beyond simultaneous imaging limits.
- Validated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging from mice, showing ability to estimate correlations between neurons not recorded concurrently.
- Formulates the problem as inference in a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system with missing observations.
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, receptive field matrix (stimulus dependence), neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance; estimated via an EM variant.
- Performs well on simulated datasets with completely non-overlapping populations.
- Requires some overlap between recorded populations to achieve reasonable results on experimental data.
- Paper should clarify why overlap was unnecessary in simulations and why inferred correlation magnitudes differ markedly between simulation and experiment.
- Key strength: elegant recovery of pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neurons.
- Manuscript is well-written, but figure legends and main-text figure descriptions need to be more descriptive and detailed.
- Novelty: specifically tackles inferring functional connections between neurons not recorded concurrently, a gap in prior methods.
- Significance: offers a valuable approach to overcome technical limits on simultaneous imaging and access larger-scale circuit properties.
- Overall: strong contribution using a relatively simple linear dynamical system that captures important circuit properties.
