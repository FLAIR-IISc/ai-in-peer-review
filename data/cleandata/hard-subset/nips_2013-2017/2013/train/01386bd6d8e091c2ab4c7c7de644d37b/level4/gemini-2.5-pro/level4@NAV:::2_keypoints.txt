- Proposes a novel method to infer large-network properties by sequentially observing and integrating subnetworks; extension to point-process data would increase applicability; closest prior work is Pillow & Latham.
- Core concept is compelling and potentially useful for experiments with shifting recording channels.
- Primary concern: sensitivity to violations of the linear-Gaussian assumption; manuscript lacks intuition about failure modes when this assumption is broken.
- Unclear marginal benefit of incorporating additional subsets versus collecting more data from an initial subset when a single sub-model already identifies the system up to a linear transform.
- Real-data results raise why overlap between observed populations is necessary for success, suggesting simulations may not capture real-world complexities.
- In real experiments, model reproduces noise correlations but estimated couplings poorly match ground truth, implying correlations may stem from unobserved common inputs rather than direct pairwise couplings.
- A latent-variable model might more parsimoniously explain observed noise correlations; this alternative is not explored.
- Manuscript is sparse on technical details of model estimation; the M-step update is presented without derivation or clear explanation.
- If the M-step/result is standard, novelty is reduced; if not, lacking exposition undermines its significance.
- Overall: promising idea with real-data potential, but impact is limited by methodological and conceptual clarity gaps.
