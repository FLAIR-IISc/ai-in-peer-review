- Novel and potentially powerful idea: infer large-network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and stitching sub-models; novel relative to prior work (e.g., Pillow & Latham).
- Approach could extend to point-process data and datasets with few channels observed daily.
- Unclear applicability beyond linear-Gaussian assumptions; no intuition or analysis on how deviations affect performance.
- Unclear how much additional coupling information is gained by stitching multiple subsets versus using more data from a single subset.
- Real-data results require overlap between populations, suggesting simulations don’t capture real-world constraints; paper lacks explanation for why overlap is necessary.
- In real-data experiments, fitted noise correlations may not reflect true pairwise couplings; poor correlation with ground truth and possible confounding by common latent input.
- Authors emphasize introduction and discussion but provide unusually light detail on the estimation procedure and derivations.
- The M-step equation is presented as a main contribution but lacks derivation or explanation, undermining claimed novelty.
- Overall promising and tested on real data, but key methodological details and exposition need substantial improvement to assess significance.
