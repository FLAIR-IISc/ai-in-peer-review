- Proposes stitching models from sequential recordings to infer large-network properties; extension to point-process data would increase real-world applicability.
- Method appears novel with little prior work closely related (closest: Pillow & Latham).
- Unclear how strongly performance depends on the linear-Gaussian assumption and how performance degrades when violated.
- No intuition or analysis for how much additional information multiple subsets add versus collecting more data from a single subset.
- Real-data results require overlapping recorded populations; paper does not explain why overlap is necessary or how it constrains the model.
- Linear-Gaussian simulations may not capture complexities of real data, given the overlap requirement.
- In real-data experiments, well-fit noise correlations might arise from common input/latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings.
- Inferred couplings show little correlation with true couplings (Fig. 3b), supporting the latent-common-input concern.
- Paper lacks detail on the estimation procedure (e.g., unexplained M-step equation) making novelty and correctness hard to assess for non-experts.
- Overall promising idea but needs clearer methodology, derivations, and thorough exploration of model limitations.
