- Introduces a statistical method to combine sequential partial recordings to infer dynamics of the full neural population via a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Frames the stitching problem with a linear dynamical system (LDS) and estimates parameters using an expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Validated on simulated data and two-photon calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex, successfully predicting noise correlations between neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously.  
- Fills a novel niche by specifically addressing non-simultaneous observations, which most prior methods do not tackle.  
- Strength: addresses a significant practical bottleneck in systems neuroscience, potentially enabling modeling of larger circuits beyond recording limits.  
- Strength: original and elegant application of standard tools (LDS, EM) to the stitching problem, with clear differentiation from related work.  
- Strength: thorough evaluation using synthetic and real data, including well-defined upper and lower benchmark bounds.  
- Weakness: real-data performance required session overlap despite simulations showing non-overlap success; needs deeper discussion of causes (model mismatch, noise, stationarity).  
- Weakness: notable gap in coupling-parameter recovery accuracy between simulated (~0.73) and real (~0.38) data that warrants explanation.  
- Weakness/clarity: figure legends and textual explanations are terse and should be expanded to better guide the reader.  
- Overall: technically sound and valuable contribution likely to be adopted and extended, with minor improvements needed on limitations discussion and presentation.
