- Proposes stitching submodels from sequential, overlapping recordings to infer network properties of large neural populations (linear-Gaussian framework; conceptually extended to point processes).  
- Timely and potentially impactful for multi-day/limited-coverage experimental settings; novel relative to prior work like Pillow & Latham.  
- Strength: addresses a practical problem of reconstructing network structure from partial, sequential data.  
- Strength: conceptually elegant use of overlap to align and merge submodels.  
- Strength: demonstrated on simulated data and applied to real neural recordings.  
- Strength: potentially widely applicable if scalable and robust.  
- Major concern: sensitivity to linear-Gaussian assumptions is unclear; need characterization of failure modes under nonlinear dynamics, non-Gaussian/temporally correlated noise, and nonstationarities.  
- Major concern: unclear information gain from recording additional subsets versus just longer recordings of a single subset; needs Fisher-information/asymptotic analysis or controlled simulations.  
- Major concern: role and required amount of overlap is not theoretically clarified; need intuition/theory on how overlap constrains alignment and resolves latent ambiguities.  
- Major concern: on real data inferred couplings correlate poorly with ground truth, raising the possibility that common input (latent factors) rather than direct couplings drive fit; need comparisons to latent-variable baselines.  
- Major concern: estimation details and novelty are under-explained—M-step is presented without derivation, algorithmic steps, complexity, convergence, or practical guidance.  
- Suggested experiment: robustness tests under controlled deviations from linear-Gaussian assumptions (nonlinear dynamics, Poisson/overdispersion, correlated noise, nonstationarity).  
- Suggested experiment: overlap ablation varying fraction/identity of overlapping neurons, including zero-overlap and chain overlap cases.  
- Suggested experiment: compare adding new subsets versus extending recording duration under equal data budgets to quantify information trade-offs.  
- Suggested experiment: fit low-rank latent models (PLDS/GPFA) and report predictive performance, noise-correlation fits, and coupling recovery to control for common-input explanations.  
- Suggested experiment: use metrics beyond correlations (held-out predictive log-likelihood, multi-step forecasts, directed-effect recovery like Granger/impulse responses).  
- Practical suggestions: test robustness to session drift, spike-sorting variability, and alignment errors; provide derivations, pseudocode, and code for reproducibility.  
- Clarity request: expand methods with explicit notation for stitched parameters, alignment transforms, identifiability constraints, and derivation of the M-step and expectation computations.  
- Overall: promising idea with practical relevance but needs stronger methodological detail, theoretical identifiability analysis (especially overlap), and empirical probes of when it succeeds or fails.
