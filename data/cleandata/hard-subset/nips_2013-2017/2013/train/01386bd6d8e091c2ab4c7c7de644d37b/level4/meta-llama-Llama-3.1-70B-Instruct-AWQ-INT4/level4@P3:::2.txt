This paper proposes a novel approach to inferring neural population dynamics from multiple partial recordings of the same neural circuit. The authors present a statistical method for "stitching" together sequentially imaged sets of neurons into one model by phrasing the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations. This method allows for the substantial expansion of population sizes for which population dynamics can be characterized, beyond the number of simultaneously imaged neurons.

The paper is well-motivated, and the idea of stitching together multiple recordings is a powerful one. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on both simulated and real data, including calcium imaging measurements from the mouse somatosensory cortex. The results show that the method can accurately predict correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs, even in cases where the linearity assumption may break.

However, there are some limitations and open questions that need to be addressed. Firstly, it is unclear how much the method is limited to data that is close to linear-Gaussian, and how the fitting might break down from various deviations to this assumption. Additionally, the paper does not provide a clear intuition on how the overlap between the two populations affects the model, and why the method only works if there is some overlap.

Furthermore, the paper is light on the details of the estimation, and the equation for the M-step is presented without derivation or explanation. This may be a trivial result to LDS experts, but it would be beneficial to include more details to properly convey the significance of the result.

In terms of the criteria for evaluation, the paper scores well on originality and significance. The idea of stitching together multiple recordings is novel and has the potential to be widely applicable. However, the paper could be improved in terms of clarity and technical soundness. The authors should provide more details on the estimation procedure and address the limitations and open questions mentioned above.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and powerful approach to inferring neural population dynamics.
* The method is demonstrated to be effective on both simulated and real data.
* The paper has the potential to be widely applicable and to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper is light on the details of the estimation procedure.
* The limitations and open questions mentioned above need to be addressed.
* The paper could be improved in terms of clarity and technical soundness.

Recommendation:

* The paper should be accepted, but the authors should be asked to address the limitations and open questions mentioned above.
* The authors should provide more details on the estimation procedure and include derivations and explanations where necessary.
* The paper should be revised to improve clarity and technical soundness.
