- Introduces a statistical method to integrate sequentially imaged neuronal populations, expanding effective population size beyond simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Validated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging from mice, showing ability to estimate correlations between populations not imaged simultaneously.  
- Models the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a linear Gaussian framework.  
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields matrix, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Parameters estimated from data using a variant of the EM algorithm.  
- Performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping neuron populations.  
- Real-data application requires a minimum level of population overlap for reasonable results, which limits applicability.  
- Unexplained inconsistency: simulations suggest no overlap requirement (Fig. 2c), but real data show reliance on overlap and different correlation estimates (Fig. 3b).  
- Key strength: recovers pairwise correlations between neurons never imaged simultaneously.  
- Writing is generally clear, but figure legends lack detail and the text underexplains figures' key insights.  
- Novel contribution relative to prior work: infers functional connections between neurons not imaged simultaneously.  
- Significant impact: addresses experimental constraints on simultaneous imaging and expands the scope of accessible neural dynamics.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, and technically sound methodological contribution using a simple model to recover otherwise inaccessible measures.
