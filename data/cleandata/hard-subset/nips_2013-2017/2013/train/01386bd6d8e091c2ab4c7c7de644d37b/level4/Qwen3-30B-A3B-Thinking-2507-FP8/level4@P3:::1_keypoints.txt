- Addresses inability to image thousands of neurons simultaneously by proposing a statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged subpopulations.
- Uses a latent linear dynamical system (LDS) with missing observations to estimate parameters (coupling A, stimulus fields B, noise covariances Q, R).
- Adapts EM with Kalman smoothing to perform parameter estimation under missing data.
- Demonstrates accurate prediction of noise correlations for non-simultaneous pairs in simulation (corr 0.92 vs. ground truth).
- Performs well on in vivo calcium imaging (anesthetized and awake), approaching fully observed model performance (corr 0.84).
- Shows robustness across population sizes and experimental conditions.
- Simulation recovers non-simultaneous couplings with corr 0.73, but real data recovery is lower (corr 0.38) — discrepancy unexplained.
- Real-data performance requires ≥60% overlap between subpopulations, while simulation succeeded at 50% overlap; reasons for this gap are not analyzed.
- Linear LDS assumption is acknowledged but may limit modeling of nonlinear dynamics and complex stimulus responses.
- Figure legends and links between text and figures need more descriptive detail for clarity.
- Scalability to more than two subpopulations is mentioned but computational complexity and practical scalability are not explored.
- Method is original relative to prior work on connectivity inference and subsampled connectivity, addressing partial temporal observations rather than spatial subsampling.
- Significant contribution: enables characterization of dynamics for much larger neural populations, facilitating new experimental studies.
- Overall recommendation: acceptance — technically sound, well-validated, with minor clarifications needed on discrepancies and overlap dependency.
