- Novel, potentially powerful method for inferring network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and integrating sub-models, especially relevant for point-process data.
- Related work is sparse; Pillow & Latham (2013) is noted as a partial analogue but not directly comparable.
- Major concern about sensitivity to violations of the linear-Gaussian assumption and lack of intuition on when the method fails.
- Unclear what additional benefit multiple subsets provide beyond increasing dataset size from a single subset; need clearer justification for the sequential subnetwork approach.
- Requirement for overlap between populations in real data suggests simulations don’t capture real complexities; authors should explain why overlap is necessary (nonlinearities, shared input, etc.).
- Fit to explained noise correlations may reflect common input rather than direct pairwise couplings; Figure 3b shows weak correspondence between estimated and true couplings.
- Suggestion that latent-variable models to capture shared driving forces might better represent underlying dynamics.
- Key estimation equation (bottom of page 3) is under-explained/under-derived, reducing accessibility and perceived novelty; more technical detail is needed.
- Paper allocates disproportionate space to introduction/discussion and too little to algorithmic/estimation details.
- Recommendation: reject in current form; major revisions required to address limitations, intuition, and technical exposition before reconsideration.
