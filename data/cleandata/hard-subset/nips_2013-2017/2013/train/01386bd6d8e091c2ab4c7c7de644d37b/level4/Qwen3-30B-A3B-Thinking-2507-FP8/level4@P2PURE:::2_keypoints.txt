- Novel method: sequentially record from subnetworks and stitch sub-models to infer global network properties; extends to point-process data and appears powerful and novel (closest is Pillow & Latham, 2008).  
- Unclear robustness: unclear how much the method relies on linear-Gaussian assumptions and how deviations from those assumptions cause fitting to fail.  
- Identification question: if the full model can be estimated from a single subset (up to a linear transform), it’s unclear what additional information other subsets provide versus simply more data from one subset.  
- Overlap requirement: real data experiments require population overlap between subsets, suggesting simulations don’t capture real-world constraints and lacking intuition on why overlap improves identification.  
- Noise vs coupling: in real data, explained noise correlations may reflect common input rather than direct pairwise couplings; inferred couplings poorly match true couplings—latent-variable modeling of noise correlations might be preferable.  
- Insufficient technical detail: paper overemphasizes introduction/discussion and is brief on estimation details; the presented M-step equation is given without derivation/explanation, weakening perceived novelty.  
- Overall: promising approach tested on real data, but critical methodological and explanatory details are missing so the significance isn’t convincingly demonstrated.
