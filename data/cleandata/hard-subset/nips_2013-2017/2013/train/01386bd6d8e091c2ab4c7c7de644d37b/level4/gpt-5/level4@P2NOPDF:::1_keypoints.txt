- Introduces a statistical framework to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuronal populations, enabling characterization of population dynamics beyond simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Formulates the problem as inference in a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system with missing observations, using four components (state transition, stimulus receptive fields, latent noise covariance, measurement noise covariance) and EM for parameter learning.  
- Evaluated on simulated data and mouse calcium imaging; claims to estimate cross-population variability/correlations between groups never imaged together.  
- Simulations with non-overlapping populations perform well, but real data requires some overlap for reliable estimates.  
- Needs clarification of simulation assumptions (model correctness, SNR, identifiability, shared stimulus) that made overlap unnecessary in simulations.  
- Lacks analysis of the pronounced discrepancy between correlation estimates in simulations (Fig.2c) versus real data (Fig.3b); possible causes include model mismatch, noise, biological variability, or preprocessing.  
- Major strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neurons that were never recorded concurrently.  
- Writing is generally clear but figure legends are too terse and figures are not well integrated into the narrative; more descriptive captions and guided interpretation are needed.  
- Originality: fills a gap by linking neurons not imaged simultaneously, unlike prior connectivity inference work.  
- Significance: offers a practical method to extend inference beyond experimental imaging limits, enabling larger-scale analyses of population dynamics.  
- Overall assessment: strong, simple modeling contribution but would benefit from explanations of overlap necessity, analysis of simulation-vs-real discrepancies, and improved figure captions/discussion.
