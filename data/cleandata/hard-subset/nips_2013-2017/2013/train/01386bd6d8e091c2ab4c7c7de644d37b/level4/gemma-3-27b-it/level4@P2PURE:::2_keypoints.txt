- Presents a promising method to infer large-system network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and integrating sub-models, with potential for point-process data; novel relative to prior work (closest: Pillow & Latham 2013).  
- Unclear how strongly performance depends on the linear-Gaussian assumption and how deviations from it affect fitting.  
- If a full model is recoverable from a single subset up to linear transforms, it’s unclear what extra information additional subsets provide versus more data from the initial subset.  
- The requirement for overlap between populations is concerning and suggests linear-Gaussian simulations may not capture real dynamics; more insight into overlap’s role in constraining the model is needed.  
- In real-data tests, explained noise correlations may reflect common input rather than direct pairwise couplings, and inferred couplings correlate poorly with true couplings (Fig. 3b).  
- Noise correlations might be better modeled with latent variables than with pairwise couplings.  
- The paper provides insufficient detail and derivation for the estimation process (notably the M-step), hurting clarity and the ability to assess novelty.  
- Overall: neat idea validated on real data but requires clearer explanation and more analysis of assumptions and limitations.
