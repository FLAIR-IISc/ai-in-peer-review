- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets, enabling analysis of larger populations than can be imaged simultaneously.  
- Applies the method to simulated data and experimental mouse calcium imaging, showing estimation of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged populations.  
- Models the problem as a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system with missing observations and four parameter types (coupling, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, measurement noise covariance).  
- Parameters are estimated using a variant of the expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping populations, recovering intended statistics.  
- Requires substantial overlap between populations to get reasonable results on real data; reasons for this discrepancy with simulations are not explained.  
- Does not address the significant difference in correlation results between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b).  
- Strength is recovery of pairwise correlations between neurons not imaged simultaneously.  
- Paper is generally well-written but figure legends and in-text figure explanations need more detail and discussion.  
- Novel contribution relative to prior work because it infers functional relationships between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Significant and valuable given experimental limits on simultaneous imaging; offers a promising approach to expand accessible circuit-level analyses.  
- Overall assessment: a good paper using a simple linear dynamics model to capture otherwise inaccessible measures, with promising utility despite some empirical uncertainties.
