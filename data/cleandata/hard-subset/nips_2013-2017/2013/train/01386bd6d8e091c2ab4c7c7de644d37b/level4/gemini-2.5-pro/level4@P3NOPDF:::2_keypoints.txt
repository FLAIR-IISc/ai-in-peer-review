- Novel and original idea: fit sub-models from overlapping recordings and "stitch" them to reconstruct a full network, with practical importance for neurophysiology.  
- Closest related work is Pillow & Latham; the method’s originality is plausible but should be contextualized against this precedent.  
- Paper emphasizes intuition and discussion but lacks technical detail and derivation—especially the M-step update presented as central.  
- If the M-step is a straightforward LDS extension, the methodological novelty may be limited; a clear derivation (e.g., appendix) is needed.  
- Unclear sensitivity to the linear-Gaussian assumption; the paper does not analyze failure modes under nonlinearity or non-Gaussian noise.  
- Need clarity on the concrete benefit of multiple overlapping subsets versus simply collecting more data from one subnetwork.  
- Empirical finding that neuronal overlap between sub-networks is required on real data suggests simulations do not capture real-data complexity and requires explanation.  
- The paper should explain what constraints overlap provides that non-overlapping recordings lack, and what this implies about real neural data structure.  
- On real data, model captures noise correlations but estimated pairwise couplings poorly match ground truth, raising concern that couplings may be capturing shared unobserved inputs.  
- Consideration of models with explicit latent variables is needed to assess whether learned couplings truly reflect network interactions.  
- Overall: promising contribution but obscured by missing derivations, limited analysis of assumptions and failure modes, and incomplete interpretation of real-data results—addressing these would strengthen the paper.
