- Paper presents a compelling idea: infer large-network properties by sequentially recording small subnetworks and stitching submodels together.  
- Extension to point-process data could apply to datasets where channels change daily.  
- Reviewer is unaware of close prior work; Pillow & Latham (partially observed spiking populations) is the closest.  
- Unclear how method generalizes beyond approximately linear-Gaussian data; failure modes when assumptions are violated are not discussed.  
- In the limiting case where one subset recovers the full model up to a linear transform, it’s unclear how much extra coupling information multiple subsets add versus more data from one subset.  
- Real-data results only work when populations overlap, suggesting linear-Gaussian simulations miss important factors; need intuition for why overlap helps.  
- For real data, explained noise correlations may reflect common input/latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings.  
- Figure 3b: couplings show little correlation with true couplings despite well-fit noise correlations — raises concerns about interpretability of fitted couplings.  
- Manuscript emphasizes intro/discussion but is light on estimation details; key M-step equation is presented without explanation or derivation.  
- If the M-step is standard to LDS experts, that reduces novelty; clearer exposition and derivations are needed to convey significance.  
- Overall: neat and promising idea tested on real data, but several methodological and explanatory details remain unclear.
