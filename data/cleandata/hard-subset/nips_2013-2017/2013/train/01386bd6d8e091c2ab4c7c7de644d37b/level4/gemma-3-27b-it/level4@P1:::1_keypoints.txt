- Contribution: Introduces a statistical method to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuron sets, expanding analyzable population sizes beyond simultaneous imaging limits.
- Demonstration: Validated on simulated data and mouse calcium imaging, showing estimation of correlations between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Model: Frames problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations and four parameter types estimated via a variant of EM.
- Parameterization: Includes a functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive field matrix, and covariance matrices for neural and measurement noise.
- Simulation performance: Recovers dynamics well for two non-overlapping simulated populations.
- Real-data limitation: Requires significant overlap between populations for reasonable results on experimental data; why differs from simulations is unexplained.
- Correlation discrepancy: Substantial difference in correlation strengths between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b) is not addressed.
- Recovery claim: Method can recover pairwise correlations for non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs, but practical discrepancies need more discussion.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written overall but figure legends and in-text figure descriptions are insufficiently detailed for full understanding or reproduction.
- Originality: Novelly addresses inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons, a gap in existing literature.
- Significance: Offers a valuable approach to overcome limits of simultaneous imaging, enabling characterization of larger neural circuits.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper using a simple linear dynamics model to extract otherwise inaccessible circuit information.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach to an important problem; predicts correlations between non-simultaneous neurons; clear presentation; successful on simulated and experimental data.
- Arguments against acceptance: Unexplained need for overlap in real data vs. simulations; unexplained correlation-strength discrepancies; figure legends and figure-text connections need improvement.
