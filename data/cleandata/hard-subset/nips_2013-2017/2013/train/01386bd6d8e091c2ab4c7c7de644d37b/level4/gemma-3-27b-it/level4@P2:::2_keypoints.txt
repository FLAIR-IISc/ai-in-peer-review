- Introduces a method for inferring large-population dynamics by “stitching” sequentially recorded subnetworks via a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Potentially high impact for datasets where simultaneous whole-population recording is impractical (electrophysiology, calcium imaging).  
- Core idea of combining sequential recordings to infer larger-scale network properties is novel and promising.  
- Method relies on linear-Gaussian assumptions, raising concerns about robustness to neural data non-linearity and non-Gaussianity.  
- Paper lacks analysis of how performance degrades with increasing non-linearity or non-Gaussian noise.  
- Unclear what additional information is gained by combining subsets when a complete model can be estimated from one subset up to a linear transform.  
- Requirement for overlap between recorded populations in experiments is puzzling and suggests simulations don’t fully capture real data complexity.  
- More explanation is needed on how overlap constrains the model and why it’s required in practice.  
- In real-data analysis, reconstructed noise correlations may conflate direct pairwise couplings and shared inputs.  
- A latent-variable model explicitly accounting for common input might be more appropriate for interpreting correlations.  
- Methodological implementation details are insufficient, especially the M-step equation lacking context or derivation.  
- Providing more accessible derivations and clearer methodological exposition would improve clarity and perceived novelty.  
- Demonstrated successful application to real calcium imaging data supports practical utility.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, real-data validation, potential to address limitations in studying large neural populations.  
- Rejection concerns: sensitivity to linear-Gaussian violations, unclear benefit over larger single-subset data, unexplained role of overlap, and limited methodological detail.  
- Overall: promising and validated by experiments but requires major revision to clarify limitations, justifications, and implementation details.
