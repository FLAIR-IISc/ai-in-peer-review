- Proposes stitching sequentially recorded subnetworks to infer large-system network properties; potentially powerful and applicable to point-process datasets (few channels moved daily); limited related work cited (Pillow & Latham).

- Unclear how strongly the method relies on linear-Gaussian assumptions and lacks intuition for how estimation fails when assumptions are violated.

- In the extreme case where a single subset identifies the full model up to a linear transform, it’s unclear how additional subsets improve estimates versus simply collecting more data from that subset.

- Real-data results show the method only works with overlap between populations, suggesting simulations (linear-Gaussian) miss relevant structure; needs intuition for how overlap constrains the model.

- In real data, noise correlations are fit well but inferred couplings correlate poorly with true couplings (Fig. 3b); unclear how much explained covariance comes from common input/latent variables versus direct pairwise couplings.

- Exposition is unbalanced: detailed intro/discussion but insufficient estimation details; the M-step equation (claimed main contribution) is neither derived nor explained.

- If the M-step is trivial for LDS experts the novelty is limited; if not, the paper must clearly justify and detail it, and more information is needed on limitations and significance.

- Overall: interesting, tested on real data, but several important methodological and explanatory details remain unclear.
