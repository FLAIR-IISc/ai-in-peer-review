- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron populations to characterize dynamics beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Models the problem as a latent linear-Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations to estimate functional relationships between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Validated on simulated and experimental calcium imaging data, showing recovery of cross-population correlation structures otherwise inaccessible.  
- Uses four key parameters (coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, measurement noise covariance) estimated via a tailored EM algorithm.  
- Core innovation: inferring pairwise correlations between neurons imaged in separate sessions, enabling scalable circuit-level analyses.  
- Performs well on simulations, recovering correlations even with non-overlapping populations.  
- Real-data performance depends heavily on population overlap; correlations are substantially lower than in simulations, raising concerns about practical reliability.  
- Paper fails to explain the simulation vs. real-data discrepancy (why overlap unnecessary in simulations but required in practice).  
- Quality: method is technically sound and compelling, but the unresolved simulation-real data gap weakens confidence in real-world applicability.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but figure legends and text lack necessary detail and explicit explanation of results.  
- Originality: novel emphasis on non-simultaneous neuron pair inference, distinct from prior simultaneous-recording methods.  
- Significance: addresses a key bottleneck in neural circuit analysis by enabling stitching of sequential recordings to expand population-level studies.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution, recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to address the simulation/real-data discrepancy and improve figure interpretation.
