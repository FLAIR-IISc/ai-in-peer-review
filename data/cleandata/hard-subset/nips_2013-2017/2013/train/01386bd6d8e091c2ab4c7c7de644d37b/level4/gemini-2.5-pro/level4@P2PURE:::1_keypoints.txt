- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets, enabling larger-scale population dynamics analysis beyond simultaneous imaging.
- Validated on simulated and experimental calcium imaging data; demonstrates estimation of correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron populations.
- Frames problem as a latent dynamical system with a linear Gaussian state-space model.
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, receptive field (stimulus) matrix, latent dynamics covariance, and measurement noise covariance; estimated via an EM variant.
- Performs well on simulated data with non-overlapping populations, but real data requires some overlap between populations for reasonable performance.
- Paper does not explain why overlap was unnecessary for the simulated data.
- Authors fail to address the substantial discrepancy in correlation magnitudes between the simulated (Fig. 2c) and experimental (Fig. 3b) results.
- Key strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neurons that were never recorded simultaneously.
- Clarity is generally good, but figure legends and main-text descriptions lack sufficient detail to fully interpret figures.
- Originality: builds on prior connectivity inference work but uniquely addresses inference between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Significance: addresses technical limits on simultaneous recordings and aids circuit-level analyses beyond a few hundred cells.
- Overall assessment: a strong paper applying a simple linear dynamical model to extract otherwise inaccessible metrics like cross-population correlations.
