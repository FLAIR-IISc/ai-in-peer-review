The paper presents a novel statistical method to integrate sequentially imaged sets of neurons, effectively expanding the population sizes that can be characterized for population dynamics. This approach overcomes experimental and technical limitations that prevent simultaneous imaging of entire circuits, providing a more comprehensive understanding of the underlying circuitry.

The authors model the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations, utilizing a simple linear Gaussian system. The model is defined by four parameters: a functional coupling matrix, a stimuli receptive fields' matrix that captures stimulus-dependent population activity, and two covariance matrices for neural and measurement noise. These parameters are estimated from experimental data using a variant of the standard EM algorithm.

The proposed method is applied to both simulated data and experimental calcium imaging data in mice. The results demonstrate the ability to estimate correlations in variability across two populations that were not imaged simultaneously. This capability is particularly significant, as it enables the recovery of pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs.

However, the model's performance on real data is contingent upon a certain level of overlap between populations, whereas this requirement is not evident in the simulated data example. It is unclear why overlap is not necessary in the simulated case, and this discrepancy warrants further investigation. Furthermore, the authors do not address the substantial difference in correlation observed when applying the model to simulated data (Figure 2c) and real data (Figure 3b).

The paper is well-written, but the figure legends could be more descriptive, and the text accompanying the figures in the main body could provide more elaboration. This would enhance the clarity of the presentation.

The originality of the work lies in its ability to infer functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons, a capability not demonstrated in previous studies that have addressed functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging data or electrophysiological measurements.

The significance of this paper stems from the fundamental limits imposed by the number of neurons that can be simultaneously imaged, which restricts our understanding of neural computation at the circuit level. The proposed method offers a viable solution to expand these limited capabilities.

Overall, this is a solid paper that effectively employs a relatively simple linear dynamics model to capture measures that are otherwise challenging to retrieve.
