- Demonstrates feasibility of inferring large-network properties by sequentially sampling subnetworks and integrating sub-models.  
- Extension to point-process data is notable and broadly applicable to datasets with daily-modified channel subsets.  
- Concept appears novel; closest related work is by J. W. Pillow and P. Latham.  
- Limitations are not clearly established, particularly for data that deviate from linear–Gaussian assumptions.  
- Practical implications are uncertain when the full model can sometimes be estimated from a single subset—unclear how much additional information other subsets provide.  
- Requirement for overlap between populations in real-data experiments raises questions about model constraints and overlap’s role in performance.  
- Real-data experiments likely violate linearity; unclear separation between common input and direct pairwise couplings in explaining noise correlations.  
- Latent-variable models might better explain noise correlations than pairwise couplings, but this is not addressed.  
- Paper lacks detailed explanations of the estimation process; key steps are under-explained.  
- The presented M-step equation is not adequately derived or explained, weakening accessibility and claimed novelty for non-experts.  
- Overall promising idea but needs clearer explanations and clarification of limitations to fully appreciate its significance.
