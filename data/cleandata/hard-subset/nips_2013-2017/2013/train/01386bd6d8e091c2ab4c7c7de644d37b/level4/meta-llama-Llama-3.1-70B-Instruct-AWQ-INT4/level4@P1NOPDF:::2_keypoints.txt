- Proposes an innovative method to infer large-network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and combining sub-models, extendable to point-process data and novel relative to prior work.

- Promising idea but its limitations and range of applications are not fully explored.

- Unclear how much the method depends on the linear-Gaussian assumption and how fitting breaks down when that assumption is violated.

- When the full model is recoverable from a single subset (up to a linear transform), it’s unclear what additional information other subsets add versus more data from the same subset.

- The method appears to require overlap between recorded populations on real data, suggesting simulations don’t capture key complexities; need intuition on how overlap constrains identifiability.

- In real data, good fit to noise correlations does not imply accurate recovery of couplings (Fig. 3b); correlations may arise from common input or latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings.

- Manuscript is light on estimation details: the presented M-step equation is not thoroughly derived or explained, weakening the claimed contribution.

- Overall: neat, experimentally-tested idea but requires clearer exposition and additional analysis to address the above concerns.
