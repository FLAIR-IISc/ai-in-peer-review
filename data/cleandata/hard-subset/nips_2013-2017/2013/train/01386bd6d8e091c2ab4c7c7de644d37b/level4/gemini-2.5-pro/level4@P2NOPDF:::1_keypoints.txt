- Presents a statistical "stitching" method to infer large-population neural dynamics from sequential, partially overlapping recordings.  
- Models activity as a linear Gaussian state-space model with parameters: coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Estimates parameters via a variant of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Validated on simulated data and mouse visual cortex calcium imaging, recovering correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously.  
- Key strength: can recover pairwise correlations across non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Method performs well on simulations even with no overlap, but real data requires substantial overlap for reasonable results.  
- Paper lacks discussion explaining why overlap is unnecessary in simulations but crucial for experimental data.  
- Observed discrepancy in correlation magnitudes between simulated and real data is not addressed; potential causes (model mismatch, biological differences) are unexplored.  
- Clarity is generally good, but figures and legends are too brief and need more detailed explanations.  
- Novelty: extends prior connectivity inference work to the non-simultaneous recording case, addressing an important practical problem.  
- Significance: offers a principled way to integrate multiple sessions to study larger effective populations beyond recording limits.  
- Overall impression: solid, elegant, and useful contribution with minor evaluation and presentation weaknesses.
