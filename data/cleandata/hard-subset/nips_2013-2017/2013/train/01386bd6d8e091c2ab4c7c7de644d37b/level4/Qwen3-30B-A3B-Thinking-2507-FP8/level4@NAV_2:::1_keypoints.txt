- Presents a statistical "stitching" method to infer joint dynamics from sequential, non-simultaneous neural recordings.  
- Models the problem as a latent linear Gaussian state-space with missing observations to estimate functional couplings and noise correlations.  
- Validated on simulated data and two mouse somatosensory cortex datasets (anesthetized and awake).  
- Accurately predicts inter-population correlations and activity (cross-population coupling corr 0.73 vs 0.95 for simultaneous pairs in simulations).  
- Recovers noise correlations with high fidelity (0.92 correlation with ground truth) and outperforms a naive baseline by >2× on real data (Fig. 3d).  
- Uses EM-based parameter estimation suitably adapted for missing data.  
- Raises an important unresolved issue: population overlap appears necessary in real data but not in the simulation, requiring clarification.  
- Linear Gaussian dynamics are simple but justified by predictive success; extensions to nonlinear dynamics could strengthen the approach.  
- Paper is well-organized and reproducible, but figure legends lack descriptive detail and explicit statistical significance references (e.g., Fig. 2c, Fig. 2b).  
- Contribution is novel relative to prior NIPS work by addressing non-simultaneous observation stitching rather than simultaneous-recording methods.  
- Significantly advances circuit-scale neuroscience by enabling dynamics inference beyond simultaneous imaging limits and predicting noise correlations.  
- Pros: novel, impactful problem, strong real-data validation, technically rigorous.  
- Cons: needs deeper explanation of overlap requirement, linear model may limit applicability, and simulation–real data performance gap should be clarified.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — minor limitations do not outweigh novelty and impact.
