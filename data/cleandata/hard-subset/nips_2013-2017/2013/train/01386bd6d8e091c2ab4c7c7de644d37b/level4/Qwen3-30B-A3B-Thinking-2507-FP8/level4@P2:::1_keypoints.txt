- Proposes a "stitching" method that combines sequential partial recordings into a unified latent dynamical system to predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Models the problem as a latent LDS with missing observations using a linear Gaussian framework to infer functional couplings from non-overlapping sessions.  
- Validated on synthetic data (accurate recovery of coupling matrices and noise correlations) and real calcium imaging data (awake/anesthetized), outperforming naive baselines.  
- Simulation results show strong recovery for non-simultaneous pairs (cross-coupling r=0.73; noise correlations r=0.92).  
- Real-data stitching requires substantial session overlap (≈60%) for reliability, but the simulation used artificial overlap without clear justification.  
- Notable performance gap between simulation (r=0.73) and real data (r=0.38) for non-simultaneous pairs; causes (biological noise, calcium nonlinearity, model simplification) need analysis.  
- EM-based parameter estimation is well-implemented and the "fully observed" and "naive" baselines form rigorous benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, but figure legends lack detail and the text underexplains why stitching succeeds (particularly the role of overlap).  
- Original contribution: uniquely handles non-simultaneous neuron inference, filling a gap left by prior simultaneous-recording and sub-sampling approaches.  
- Significant impact: enables study of large-scale circuit dynamics beyond current imaging limits, with practical relevance to neural coding and systems neuroscience.  
- Pros: addresses a concrete high-impact problem, elegant theoretical framing, strong empirical validation, and direct experimental relevance.  
- Cons: lack of theoretical justification for overlap requirement, unexplained sim-vs-real performance gap, and possible oversimplification from the linear Gaussian model.  
- Overall conclusion: high-quality, impactful work meriting acceptance, contingent on clarifications about overlap requirements and real-data performance differences.
