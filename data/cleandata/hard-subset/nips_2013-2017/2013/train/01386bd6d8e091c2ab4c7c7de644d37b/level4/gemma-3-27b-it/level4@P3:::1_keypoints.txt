- Proposes a novel method to infer large-scale neural population dynamics by “stitching” sequentially imaged subsets into a latent dynamical system.  
- Motivated by experimental limits on simultaneous recording and the need to analyze collective circuit activity.  
- Uses a linear Gaussian dynamical system with EM for parameter estimation (coupling matrix, receptive fields, noise covariance).  
- Validated on simulations and real mouse somatosensory calcium imaging, and can predict noise correlations across sessions.  
- Method is technically sound with a clear mathematical formulation and convincing simulation results.  
- Reliance on a linear Gaussian model is a simplifying assumption; extensions to nonlinear dynamics are needed.  
- Real-data performance depends on session overlap, unlike simulations, and this discrepancy needs deeper investigation.  
- Disparity in correlation magnitudes between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b) requires further clarification.  
- Figure legends and figure-related text are insufficiently descriptive and should be expanded.  
- Novelty lies in explicitly addressing non-simultaneous recordings and inferring connections between neurons recorded in different sessions.  
- Application to biologically relevant data (mouse somatosensory cortex) strengthens the contribution.  
- Potentially high impact by enabling analysis of larger neural populations where simultaneous recording is infeasible.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, presents a novel sound method, validated on data, predicts biologically relevant metrics, and is well written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: linear model limitation, unexplained real-vs-sim performance differences, and weak figure explanations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, pending minor revisions to address the noted concerns.
