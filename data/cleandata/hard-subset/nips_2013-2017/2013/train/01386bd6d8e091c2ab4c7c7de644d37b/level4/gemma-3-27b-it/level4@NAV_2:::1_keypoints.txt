- Presents a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neurons to expand population sizes for characterizing dynamics.  
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations to estimate correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Demonstrates the approach on simulated and experimental calcium imaging data from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Models dynamics as a linear Gaussian dynamical system and estimates coupling, receptive fields, and noise covariances via EM.  
- Core strength: can predict noise correlations between neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously.  
- Strong performance on simulated data, showing accurate recovery of correlations.  
- Experimental results require substantial overlap between imaging sessions, raising practical applicability concerns.  
- Unclear why overlap was necessary in real data but not in simulations; discrepancy between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real (Fig. 3b) performance needs investigation.  
- Linear Gaussian assumption simplifies analysis and offers a useful, tractable baseline.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized but figure legends and figure-text integration need improvement.  
- More context on parameter choices and model assumptions would strengthen clarity.  
- Original and novel: addresses inferring connections and dynamics across non-simultaneous recordings, unlike prior work.  
- Significant: provides a promising route to overcome limitations of 2-photon imaging and characterize larger neural circuits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important limitation, presents a novel method, and shows promising results on real and simulated data.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overlap requirement in real data, performance discrepancies between sim and real data, and clarity issues in figures and explanations.
