- Proposes a statistical method to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuron sets, expanding the effective population size beyond simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Demonstrates ability to estimate correlations in variability across neurons that were not imaged concurrently.  
- Validated on both simulated and experimental mouse calcium imaging data.  
- Models the problem as a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Parameters are estimated via a variant of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Performs well on simulated data (including two non-overlapping populations), though overlap was surprisingly unnecessary in simulations.  
- Requires a certain degree of overlap for reasonable results on real data; authors do not explain the large discrepancy in estimated correlation strength between simulated and real data.  
- Key strength: recovery of pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs.  
- Paper is well-written but figure legends and accompanying text lack sufficient detail.  
- Builds on prior connectivity inference work but uniquely infers functional connections between neurons not imaged simultaneously.  
- Significant contribution: offers a practical route to overcome limits on simultaneously imageable neurons and improve circuit-level characterization.  
- Overall: strong methodological paper using a simple linear dynamics model to capture difficult-to-obtain information.
