- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron populations by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations (linear Gaussian model).  
- Key model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, and neural/measurement noise covariances, estimated via a modified EM algorithm.  
- Validated on simulated data and experimental mouse calcium imaging; recovers pairwise correlations between neurons not imaged simultaneously.  
- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real-data performance requires substantial population overlap for reliable stitching.  
- Authors do not explain why overlap was unnecessary in the simulation (Fig. 2c) or the large difference between simulated and real correlation estimates (Figs. 2c vs. 3b).  
- Figure legends and text lack sufficient methodological detail (e.g., overlap percentage thresholds) and clearer links between figures and claims.  
- Novel contribution: addresses the specific problem of inferring connections between neurons imaged in separate sessions, filling a gap in prior functional connectivity methods.  
- Significant practical impact: enables scaling circuit analyses beyond simultaneous imaging limits, important for studying larger, more natural neural circuits.  
- Overall assessment: strong, elegant technical contribution with experimental utility; recommended for acceptance despite the need to clarify overlap requirements and simulation-versus-real discrepancies.
