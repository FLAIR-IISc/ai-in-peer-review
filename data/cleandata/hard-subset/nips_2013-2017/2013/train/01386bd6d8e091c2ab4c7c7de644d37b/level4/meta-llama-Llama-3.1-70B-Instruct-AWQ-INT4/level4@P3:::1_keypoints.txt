- Introduces a novel method for "stitching" sequentially imaged neuron sets to infer population dynamics beyond simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a simple linear-Gaussian model.  
- Estimates parameters (functional coupling, stimulus receptive fields, neural/measurement noise covariances) via a variant of the EM algorithm.  
- Demonstrates promising performance on both simulated and experimental data.  
- Requires overlap between populations in the real data example but not in simulations, raising questions about model assumptions and limitations.  
- Fails to address the substantial discrepancy in correlation results between simulated and real data (Figures 2c vs. 3b).  
- Successfully recovers pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs — a notable achievement.  
- Paper is generally well written, but some figure legends and corresponding text need more descriptive detail.  
- Method is distinct from prior work by enabling inference of functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Addresses a significant experimental limitation in imaging and could substantially advance understanding of circuit function.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel valuable method, promising results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overlap requirement concern, unexplained correlation differences, and insufficiently detailed figures/text.  
- Overall: strong paper with potential high impact contingent on revisions to address the highlighted concerns.
