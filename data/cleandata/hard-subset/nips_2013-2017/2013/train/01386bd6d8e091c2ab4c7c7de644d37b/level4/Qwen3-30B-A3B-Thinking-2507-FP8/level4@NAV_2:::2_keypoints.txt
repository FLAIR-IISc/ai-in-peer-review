- Introduces a method to stitch sequential partial neural recordings into a unified dynamical model to infer correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Extends latent dynamical systems (LDS) approaches to combine non-overlapping imaging sessions.  
- Demonstrates that the LDS framework predicts noise correlations in mouse somatosensory cortex, effectively expanding population size beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Validated on synthetic and real data (anesthetized and awake mice) with clear upper/lower baselines; consistently outperforms a naive baseline.  
- Novelty: stitching approach is original and directly addresses a key experimental limitation in cortical circuit studies.  
- Significance: enables characterization of population dynamics for much larger circuits where simultaneous imaging is infeasible.  
- Quality: experiments are well-structured and results are moderate-to-strong, though some aspects are only moderate in rigor.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on linear-Gaussian assumptions without sufficient justification or robustness analysis to model deviations.  
- Weakness: technical derivations (e.g., M-step) lack presentation/intuition, reducing clarity on the fitting procedure.  
- Weakness: mechanism and necessity of population overlap for identifiability are not explained, only empirically shown.  
- Weakness: interpretation of noise correlations as direct pairwise couplings is questionable; alternative latent common-input explanations are not explored.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance pending major revisions to clarify assumptions, provide technical details, and address interpretational concerns.
