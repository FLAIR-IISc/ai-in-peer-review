- Proposes a novel, promising method to infer large-network properties by stitching sub-models from overlapping subnetworks; related to Pillow & Latham and potentially impactful for neurophysiology/point-process data.

- Major concern about strong reliance on the linear-Gaussian assumption and little intuition on how performance degrades when data deviate from it.

- Unclear what additional coupling information is gained by incorporating subsequent subsets when the full model is identifiable from a single subset (up to linear latent transform), versus simply using more data from the initial subnetwork.

- Paper lacks a thorough analysis of robustness and limitations, which is critical for assessing practical applicability.

- Real-data results suggest the method requires overlap between sub-populations, indicating simulations may not capture real-world complexities and necessitating an intuitive explanation of how overlap constrains the model.

- In real data the model captures noise correlations but inferred couplings poorly match true couplings (Fig. 3b), suggesting model misspecification—common input or latent-variable explanations may be more appropriate.

- Manuscript is light on technical estimation details: key contributions like the M-step update are presented without derivation or explanation, making it hard to judge technical novelty (may be standard LDS result).

- Overall: interesting and potentially impactful idea, but clarity, technical detail, discussion of limitations, and interpretation of empirical findings need significant improvement.
