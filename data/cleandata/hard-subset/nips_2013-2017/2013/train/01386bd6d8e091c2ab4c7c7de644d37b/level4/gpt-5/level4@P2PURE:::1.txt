This paper introduces a statistical method to “stitch” together sequentially imaged neuronal populations. The approach increases the effective population size for which population dynamics can be characterized beyond the number of neurons imaged simultaneously, and provides a more complete view of circuits that cannot be imaged in their entirety due to experimental and technical constraints. The method is evaluated on simulated data and applied to experimental calcium imaging data in mice. The authors demonstrate that they can estimate correlations in variability across two populations that were not imaged simultaneously.

The authors formulate the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations, specifically a simple linear-Gaussian system. The model includes four parameter sets: a functional coupling matrix; a stimulus receptive-field matrix capturing stimulus dependence of population activity; and two covariance matrices for neural and measurement noise. Parameters are estimated from data using a variant of the standard EM algorithm.

Quality
The model performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping neuronal populations, but on real data the populations must have some degree of overlap to obtain reasonable results. While it is intuitive that greater overlap improves performance, why was overlap not required in the simulated example? In addition, the authors do not address the source of the large discrepancy in correlations between the simulated results (Fig. 2c) and the real data (Fig. 3b). A key strength of the model is its ability to recover pairwise correlations between neuron pairs that were not measured simultaneously.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written, but the figure legends are not sufficiently descriptive, and the corresponding text in the main body does not provide enough explanation of the figures.

Originality
The authors cite several prior works on inferring functional connectivity from two-photon imaging or electrophysiology, but those methods do not infer functional connections between neurons that were not imaged simultaneously.

Significance
There are fundamental limits on how many neurons can be imaged simultaneously, which constrains our ability to characterize neural computation at the circuit level (beyond a few hundred cells). This paper proposes a method that helps extend these capabilities.

Overall, this is a solid modeling paper that uses a relatively simple linear dynamical model and recovers quantities that are otherwise difficult to obtain.
