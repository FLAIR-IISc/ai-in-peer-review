This paper presents a novel approach to inferring neural population dynamics from multiple partial recordings of the same neural circuit. By "stitching" together sequentially imaged sets of neurons into one model, the authors demonstrate that it is possible to substantially expand the population sizes for which population dynamics can be characterized.

The paper relates to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere, particularly in the area of inferring functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging data or electrophysiological measurements. However, the authors' approach is distinct in that it attempts to infer functional connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons.

The strengths of the paper include its innovative approach, clear presentation, and successful application to real calcium imaging measurements from the mouse somatosensory cortex. The authors' use of a simple linear gaussian dynamical system model is a key contribution, as it provides a statistical description of the observed data.

However, there are also some weaknesses and areas for improvement. For example, the authors' assumption of linearity and gaussianity may not hold in all cases, and it is unclear how the fitting might break down from various deviations to these assumptions. Additionally, the authors' reliance on overlap between the two populations to constrain the model is not fully explained, and it would be helpful to have more intuition about what the overlap does to better constrain the model.

In terms of the criteria for evaluation, the paper scores well on originality, as the authors' approach is novel and distinct from previous work. The paper also scores well on significance, as the results have the potential to be highly impactful in the field of neuroscience. However, the paper could be improved in terms of clarity, as some of the details of the estimation are not fully explained. Additionally, the paper could be improved in terms of technical quality, as some of the equations and derivations are not fully presented.
