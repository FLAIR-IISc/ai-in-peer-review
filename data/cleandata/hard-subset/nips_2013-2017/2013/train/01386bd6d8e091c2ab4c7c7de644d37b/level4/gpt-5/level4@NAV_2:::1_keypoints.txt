- Proposes a statistical “stitching” method to combine sequentially imaged neuronal subsets into a single population model, increasing effective population size beyond simultaneous observation.
- Evaluated on simulations and 2-photon calcium imaging in mice; claims recovery of variability correlations between neuron pairs never recorded concurrently.
- Models data as a latent linear Gaussian state-space with four parameter sets (coupling matrix, stimulus→neuron receptive fields, process noise covariance, measurement noise covariance) learned via an EM variant.
- Simulation results: performs well even with non-overlapping neuron groups; real data requires overlap between subsets for robust inference.
- Unclear why overlap is unnecessary in simulations and why there is a large discrepancy in parameter correlations between simulation (Fig.2c) and real data (Fig.3b).
- Manuscript is generally clear and organized, but figure captions are terse and textual descriptions do not sufficiently unpack figures.
- Originality: extends prior connectivity inference work by enabling estimation of functional relationships among neurons never recorded simultaneously.
- Significance: offers a principled way to overcome simultaneous-imaging limits and probe circuit-level computations with larger effective populations.
- Overall assessment: solid modeling paper that leverages a simple linear dynamical framework to recover otherwise hard-to-obtain quantities.
