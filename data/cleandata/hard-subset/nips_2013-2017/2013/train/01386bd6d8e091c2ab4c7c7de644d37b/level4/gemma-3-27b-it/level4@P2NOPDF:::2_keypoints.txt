- Novel and promising method: sequentially record from subnetworks and stitch sub-models to infer large-network properties, useful for partially observed, dynamic datasets.
- Lack of analysis of practical limitations, especially sensitivity to deviations from linear-Gaussian assumptions.
- Unclear incremental benefit of adding more subnetworks versus collecting more data from a single representative subset.
- Need clearer explanation of why and when additional subnetworks improve recovery of the coupling matrix.
- Role of overlap between recorded populations is underexplored; authors should specify which network structures or dynamics overlap helps constrain.
- Fitted noise correlations may reflect common input/latent drive rather than direct pairwise couplings; interpretation on real (nonlinear) data is concerning.
- Estimation details are insufficiently explained—key contributions like the M-step equation lack derivation and clarity.
- Paper devotes too much space to intro/discussion and would benefit from a more detailed exposition and rigorous justification of the core algorithmic contribution.
