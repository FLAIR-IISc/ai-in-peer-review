- Presents a method to infer large-network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and integrating sub-models.  
- Extension to point-process data could apply to many datasets with partially observed channels.  
- Reviewer is unaware of closely related work; Pillow & Latham (2012) is the most relevant prior.  
- Promising idea overall.  
- Unclear robustness to deviations from the linear-Gaussian assumption; paper lacks intuition on failure modes.  
- Not clear what additional information multiple overlapping subsets provide when a full model can be recovered from a single subset (up to linear transform).  
- Requirement for overlap in real-data analysis raises questions and suggests simulations don’t capture data complexity.  
- Needs clearer explanation of how overlap constrains the model.  
- In real data, linearity likely fails and fitted noise correlations may reflect common input or latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings.  
- Estimated couplings do not strongly correlate with true couplings (Fig. 3b), questioning interpretability.  
- Estimation procedure details are sparse; the M-step equation is presented without sufficient explanation or derivation.  
- Paper devotes much space to intro/discussion but lacks methodological detail necessary to convey novelty.
