- Proposes a statistical method to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuron sets, expanding population sizes for population-dynamics analysis.
- Demonstrated on simulated and experimental calcium imaging data, estimating correlations across populations not imaged concurrently.
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with four parameters: functional coupling, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.
- Parameters are estimated using a variant of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.
- Performs well on simulated data with non-overlapping populations, but real-data results require significant overlap between imaged populations.
- Paper does not explain why overlap was unnecessary in simulations or why correlation magnitudes differ greatly between simulated and real data.
- Main strength is recovering pairwise correlations between neurons never measured simultaneously.
- Clarity issues: figure legends and figure-referencing text lack sufficient detail and discussion.
- Originality: builds on prior connectivity inference work but uniquely infers connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Significance: provides a useful method to overcome limits of simultaneous imaging and analyze larger neural circuits.
- Overall: a strong methodological paper using a simple linear dynamics model to extract otherwise inaccessible information.
