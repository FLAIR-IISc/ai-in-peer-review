- Contribution: Proposes a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets, enabling analysis of larger neural populations than can be imaged simultaneously.  
- Application: Method validated on simulated data and on experimental mouse calcium imaging, showing estimation of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Model: Frames problem as a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations, estimated via a variant of the EM algorithm.  
- Parameters: Model includes four parameter sets—functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Strength: Recovers pairwise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously, potentially expanding circuit-level analyses beyond imaging limits.  
- Quality on simulations: Method performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping populations.  
- Quality on real data: Requires substantial overlap between populations to produce reasonable results on experimental data.  
- Unresolved discrepancy: Authors do not explain why overlap was unnecessary in simulations but required in real data, nor the large correlation differences between fig.2c (simulations) and fig.3b (real data).  
- Clarity: Manuscript is generally well written but figure legends lack detail and the main text does not sufficiently discuss figures.  
- Originality: Builds on prior connectivity-inference work but is novel in inferring functional connections across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Significance: Addresses a major experimental limitation by offering a way to expand effective population sizes for neural-circuit analyses.  
- Minor concerns / reasons against acceptance: Insufficient details about the simulated data, unexplained result differences between simulated and real data, and weak figure legends/figure discussions.
