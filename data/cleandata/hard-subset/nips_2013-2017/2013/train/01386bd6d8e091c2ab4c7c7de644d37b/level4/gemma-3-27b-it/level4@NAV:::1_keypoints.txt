- Introduces a statistical method to integrate sequentially acquired neuronal activity, increasing effective population size beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Demonstrated on simulated and mouse calcium imaging data, successfully estimating correlations between neurons not recorded concurrently.  
- Frames the task as parameter estimation for a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with four parameters: coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Parameters are estimated using a modified Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Performs well on simulated data even with non-overlapping neuronal populations.  
- Real experimental data requires substantial overlap between imaged populations for reliable results.  
- Paper fails to explain why overlap wasn’t necessary in simulations and does not address the large discrepancy in correlation magnitudes between simulated and experimental results.  
- Strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neurons not measured simultaneously.  
- Writing is generally clear, but figure legends and accompanying text lack sufficient detail.  
- Original contribution: distinguishes itself from prior functional connectivity work by targeting neurons imaged at different times.  
- Significant impact: offers a promising approach to overcome simultaneous-recording limits, potentially enabling circuit-level studies involving many more cells.  
- Overall: a solid methodological paper leveraging a simple linear dynamics model to extract otherwise inaccessible information.
