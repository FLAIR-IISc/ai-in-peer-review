- Proposes stitching neuronal populations across sessions by fitting a latent linear-Gaussian dynamical system (LDS) with missing observations to recover cross-session correlations.
- Model includes functional coupling, stimulus receptive fields, neural and measurement noise; parameters learned via an EM variant.
- Demonstrated on simulations and mouse calcium imaging, reporting recovery of cross-session variability correlations.

- Strength: Clear, principled framing of stitching as LDS inference with missing data.
- Strength: Can recover pairwise correlations between neurons never recorded together, addressing experimental limits.
- Strength: Simple, interpretable model components and standard EM inference.
- Strength: Potentially broad utility for session-by-session mosaicked neuroscience datasets.

- Concern: Method succeeds on simulations without overlap but real data requires overlap—need explanation of this discrepancy.
- Concern: Simulations may assume idealized conditions (linear-Gaussian dynamics, identical stimulus drive, stationarity, no neuropil/nonlinearities, long recordings) that don’t hold in real data.
- Concern: Real-data factors (baseline/gain drift, calcium nonlinearities, deconvolution errors, behavior/state changes, motion/registration) may break identifiability without overlap.
- Concern: Magnitude of recovered correlations differs markedly between simulations and real data; requires analysis of sources (SNR, common inputs, nonstationarity, stimulus mis-specification) and inclusion of negative controls (e.g., shuffled sessions).

- Concern: Identifiability is weak for linear-Gaussian LDS with unknown observation masks—need theoretical/empirical conditions under which cross-session correlations or coupling are identifiable (e.g., stimulus diversity, overlap anchors, priors, binning/drift control).
- Concern: Insufficient quantitative validation—request predictive performance (cross-validated log-likelihood), recovery error vs SNR/session length/overlap, EM sensitivity and convergence diagnostics, and uncertainty/confidence intervals for stitched correlations.
- Concern: Model mismatch with calcium imaging (nonlinearities, temporal filtering); assess sensitivity and consider explicit calcium/spike-latent variants.
- Concern: Stimulus term unclear—specify dimensionality, parameterization, alignment across sessions, and handling of slow drift or time-warping.

- Concern: Need comparisons to baselines and ablations (naïve overlap extrapolation, CCA/GPFA/LDS without coupling, session concatenation via overlap, removing stimulus or coupling terms).
- Request: Provide practical guidance—performance vs percent overlap, SNR, recording length, and computational complexity/run-time for typical datasets.

- Clarity request: Expand figure captions to define variables/colors/axes and state dataset details; add schematic of stitching setup; show robustness curves with uncertainty bands; include negative controls.

- Originality: Novel framing—stitching as LDS inference with missing observations fills a gap relative to prior connectivity inference work.

- Significance: If identifiability, validations, and practical guidelines are clarified, the method could substantially extend circuit-level analyses beyond single-session limits.

- Overall: Promising and well-motivated method, but main concerns are role of overlap in real vs simulated data, discrepancy in correlation magnitudes, and limited quantitative validation and uncertainty analysis; addressing these would strengthen impact.
