- Proposes a novel sequential subnetwork-sampling and stitching method (notably extended to point-process data), which is a significant and practically relevant departure from prior work.  
- Paper relies on linear-Gaussian assumptions but fails to contextualize how deviations (nonlinearities, heavy-tailed or non-Gaussian noise) would affect performance.  
- Unclear what additional subsets provide beyond more data from a single subset; the marginal gain of multiple subsets is not quantified.  
- Real-data success requires overlap between sampled subnetworks, contradicting linear-Gaussian simulations and suggesting unmodeled factors (e.g., latent alignment) drive practical behavior.  
- Model explains noise correlations well but its inferred couplings correlate poorly with ground truth, implying possible confounding of common input effects with direct pairwise couplings.  
- Authors do not discuss whether latent-variable models would better account for shared inputs or whether their approach inherently confounds these sources.  
- Key technical pieces (notably the M-step) lack derivation, motivation, and comparison to existing LDS estimation updates, while background discussion is disproportionately long.  
- Overall: conceptually appealing with promising real-data validation, but robustness, necessity of the sequential approach, and distinctiveness are insufficiently demonstrated; clarify assumptions, overlap role, and provide rigorous M-step derivation.
