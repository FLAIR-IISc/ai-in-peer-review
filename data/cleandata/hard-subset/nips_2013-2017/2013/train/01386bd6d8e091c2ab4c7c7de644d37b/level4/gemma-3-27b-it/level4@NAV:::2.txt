This paper demonstrates a novel approach to inferring the properties of large networks by sequentially modeling subnetworks and integrating the resulting sub-models. The extension to point-process data is particularly promising, offering potential applicability to numerous datasets where channel availability changes frequently. This work appears to be largely original; while Pillow & Latham (2008) addresses related concepts in partially observed spiking neuron populations, this method presents a distinct and potentially powerful strategy.

However, the method’s limitations regarding deviations from linear-Gaussian assumptions remain unclear. The paper doesn’t provide sufficient intuition regarding how performance degrades with increasing departures from these assumptions. Furthermore, when the full model can be estimated from a single subset of data, the incremental benefit of incorporating additional subsets – versus simply increasing the data volume from the initial subset – is not well-defined. 

The requirement for population overlap in the real-data analysis also warrants further investigation. While indicating that the linear-Gaussian simulations do not fully capture the underlying dynamics, the mechanism by which overlap constrains the model remains unexplained. 

Concerning the real-data experiments, and acknowledging the likely violation of linearity assumptions, it is crucial to consider the potential contribution of common input to the well-fit noise correlations. Given the limited correspondence between fitted couplings and true couplings (Fig. 3b), a latent variable approach to modeling noise correlations may be more appropriate than pairwise couplings.

The manuscript dedicates substantial space to introduction and discussion, yet lacks detail in its description of the estimation process. The presented M-step equation (bottom of page 3), positioned as a key contribution, receives insufficient explanation or derivation. While potentially straightforward for those experienced in latent dynamics modeling, this omission weakens the perceived novelty of the work. Overall, this is a promising idea, but its significance would be better conveyed with a more thorough and accessible explanation. 

A compelling idea validated with real data, but further clarification of key details is needed.
