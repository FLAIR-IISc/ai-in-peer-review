- Presents a novel method to infer large-network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and combining sub-models, extendable to point-process data.
- Work appears distinct from prior work (closest: Pillow & Latham), with noteworthy originality and potential impact.
- Unclear how method performs when the linear-Gaussian assumption is violated in practice.
- Paper lacks intuition about failure modes and how the fitting process breaks down under different scenarios.
- Uncertain how much extra information about the coupling matrix is gained by combining subsets versus using more data from a single subset, especially when a single subset can estimate the full model up to a linear transform.
- Requirement for overlap between populations is concerning and suggests simulated linear-Gaussian data may not capture real dynamics.
- Needs intuition and analysis on how the amount of overlap affects model performance.
- On real data, unclear whether explained noise correlations stem from direct pairwise couplings or common input; latent variables might model noise correlations better.
- Excessive space devoted to introduction and discussion while providing limited estimation details.
- The M-step equation presented as the main contribution is not clearly derived or explained, reducing perceived novelty for LDS experts.
- Overall promising and innovative idea tested on real data, but key methodological details and explanations require clarification.
