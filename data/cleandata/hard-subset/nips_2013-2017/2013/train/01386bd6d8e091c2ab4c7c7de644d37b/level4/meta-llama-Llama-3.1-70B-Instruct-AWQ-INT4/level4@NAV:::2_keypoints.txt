- Proposes a novel method to infer network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and combining sub-models; extension to point-process data is promising.  
- Claim of originality is strong; only closely related prior work cited is Pillow & Latham.  
- Practical limitations under deviations from linear-Gaussian assumptions are unclear, with little intuition on how fitting might fail.  
- Unclear how much additional information other subsets add when a full model can already be estimated from a single subset versus collecting more data from that subset.  
- The requirement of overlap between populations for real data is problematic and poorly explained, especially since simulated linear-Gaussian data did not need overlap.  
- In real-data experiments, it’s unclear whether explained noise correlations arise from common input or direct pairwise couplings; latent-variable models may be more appropriate.  
- Estimation details are lacking; the presented M-step equation is not adequately explained or derived.  
- The M-step may be trivial to LDS experts, which reduces the perceived novelty of the contribution.  
- Overall the idea is clever and tested on real data but requires clearer methodological explanations and result clarifications.
