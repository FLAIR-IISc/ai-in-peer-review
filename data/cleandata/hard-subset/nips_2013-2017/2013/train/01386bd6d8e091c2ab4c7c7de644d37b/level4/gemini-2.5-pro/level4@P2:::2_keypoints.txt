- Introduces a stitching method that frames multiple sequential, partially overlapping recordings as parameter estimation in a latent linear-Gaussian dynamical system with missing data.  
- Demonstrates the method can predict noise correlations between neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously.  
- Validated on both synthetic data (with ground truth) and two real 2-photon calcium imaging datasets.  
- Offers a potentially powerful tool to scale circuit characterization beyond simultaneous recording limits.  
- Novel application of stitching separate sessions to infer cross-session functional connectivity, building on related partially observed population work.  
- Empirical validation includes a “fully observed” upper-bound comparison showing stitching approaches this bound.  
- Paper lacks clear derivation and context for the M-step/update equations, leaving technical novelty ambiguous.  
- Concern about robustness: linear-Gaussian assumptions may be fragile, since overlapping populations were required for real data but not simulations.  
- Ambiguity in interpretation: good noise-correlation fits but poor recovery of individual coupling parameters (correlation ~0.38), raising the possibility of effective couplings masking unobserved common inputs.  
- Suggests considering explicit low-dimensional latent-variable models as a more parsimonious alternative to many weak pairwise couplings.  
- Missing analysis of practical trade-offs for experimental design (e.g., value of recording a second population vs. more data from the first).  
- Overall recommendation: accept, but revise to add technical detail and deeper discussion of model limitations and experimental guidance.
