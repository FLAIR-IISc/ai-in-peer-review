- Introduces a statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged neuronal populations to characterize dynamics beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Validated on simulated and experimental mouse calcium imaging data, showing estimation of correlations between non-simultaneously imaged populations.  
- Models the problem as a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations and fits parameters using a variant of the EM algorithm.  
- Specifies four key parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real-data performance requires a minimum level of population overlap.  
- Fails to explain why overlap was unnecessary in simulations and does not address the large discrepancy between simulated and real correlation estimates.  
- Main strength is recovering pairwise correlations between neuron pairs that were not recorded simultaneously.  
- Paper is well-written overall, but figure legends lack sufficient detail and the text underexplains figure insights.  
- Originality: extends prior connectivity inference work by addressing connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Significance: offers a promising approach to study larger-scale neural dynamics beyond current simultaneous imaging constraints.  
- Overall: a well-executed, relatively simple linear-dynamics model that retrieves otherwise difficult-to-obtain measures.
