- Proposes a novel method to infer large-network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and combining sub-models, extendable to point-process data and distinct from Pillow & Latham’s work.  
- Powerful idea but practical limitations are unclear, especially when data deviate from the linear-Gaussian assumption.  
- Insufficient intuition or analysis on how the fitting procedure fails under various deviations from linear-Gaussian assumptions.  
- Unclear how much additional information is gained by combining multiple subsets versus collecting more data from a single subset when a full model can be estimated from one subset.  
- Real-data experiments require overlap between populations, suggesting the linear-Gaussian simulations do not capture key aspects; the role of overlap needs clearer explanation.  
- In real (likely nonlinear) data, explained noise correlations could stem from common input/latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings—latent-variable models should be considered.  
- Paper overemphasizes introduction/discussion and underexplains the estimation process; the M-step equation is presented without sufficient derivation or intuition, weakening the contribution.  
- Overall, an innovative idea with real-data tests but requires more clarification, analysis, and detailed explanation to establish significance.
