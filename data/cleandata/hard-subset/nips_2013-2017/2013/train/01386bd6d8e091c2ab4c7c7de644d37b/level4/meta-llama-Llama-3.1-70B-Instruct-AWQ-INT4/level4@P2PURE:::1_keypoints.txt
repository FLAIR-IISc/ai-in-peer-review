- Presents a statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged neuron sets, enabling analysis of larger neural populations than simultaneous imaging allows.
- Applies a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations, parameterized by coupling, stimulus receptive fields, and neural/measurement noise covariances, fitted via an EM variant.
- Demonstrates success on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging from mice, showing ability to estimate correlations across non-simultaneously imaged populations.
- Model performs well on simulations even with non-overlapping populations, but real data require substantial population overlap for reasonable results.
- Paper does not explain why overlap is unnecessary in simulations or the discrepancy between simulated correlations (Fig. 2c) and real-data correlations (Fig. 3b).
- Strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neuron pairs that were never measured simultaneously.
- Clarity: overall well-written but figure legends and main-text figure descriptions need more detail and elaboration.
- Originality: novel in inferring functional relationships between non-simultaneously imaged neurons compared to prior work.
- Significance: offers a practical way to overcome simultaneous-imaging limits and study circuit-level neural computation at larger scales.
- Overall assessment: a solid paper using a simple linear dynamics model to obtain otherwise hard-to-measure statistics.
