- Proposes a "stitching" method that infers dynamics of a large neural population by combining sequential, partially overlapping recordings using a linear-Gaussian latent dynamical system with missing data.  
- Demonstrated on simulated data (recovers connectivity, predicts noise correlations between non-simultaneously observed neurons).  
- Applied to real 2-photon calcium imaging from mouse S1 and predicts correlations across separately recorded neuron pairs.  
- Novel and practically important approach for combining non-simultaneous recordings, distinct from prior single-recording or structured-missing-data methods.  
- Strength — Significance & originality: addresses a key experimental limitation and could apply broadly (e.g., multi-day electrophysiology).  
- Strength — Quality: validated on synthetic and in-vivo data with meaningful baselines (fully observed and naive models).  
- Strength — Clarity: core concept and key figures (Figs. 2–4) are well explained and persuasive.  
- Weakness — Method details: M-step update equations are presented without derivation or citation, leaving unclear whether they are standard or novel.  
- Weakness — Robustness/model assumptions: reliance on linear-Gaussian dynamics is a strong simplification; paper needs discussion of effects when assumptions are violated and why overlap was required on real data.  
- Weakness — Interpretation of inferred structure: good prediction of noise correlations but weaker recovery of coupling parameters, raising the possibility of unobserved common inputs or low-dimensional latent explanations.  
- Weakness — Justification for stitching: needs clearer intuition for how additional recording sessions resolve latent-space ambiguities versus simply collecting more data from the first subset.
