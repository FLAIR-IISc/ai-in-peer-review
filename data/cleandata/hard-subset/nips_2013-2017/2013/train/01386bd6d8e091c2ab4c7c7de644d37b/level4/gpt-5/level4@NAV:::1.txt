Review- The paper introduces a statistical approach to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuronal populations. This enables characterization of population dynamics beyond the number of neurons recorded at the same time and offers a more complete picture of circuits that cannot be fully imaged due to experimental and technical constraints. The method is applied to both simulated data and mouse calcium imaging data. The authors demonstrate that they can estimate shared variability correlations across two populations that were not recorded simultaneously.
The authors formulate the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations, instantiated as a linear Gaussian model. The model includes four parameter sets: a functional coupling matrix; a stimulus receptive field matrix capturing stimulus-driven activity; and two covariance matrices representing neural (process) noise and measurement noise. Parameters are learned from data using a variant of the standard EM algorithm.

Quality
The model performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping neuron populations, but in real data, a certain degree of population overlap is required to obtain reasonable estimates. While it is intuitive that greater overlap improves performance, it remains unclear why no overlap was needed in the simulated example.
Additionally, the authors do not discuss the source of the substantial discrepancy in correlation estimates between the simulated results (Fig. 2c) and the real data (Fig. 3b).
A key strength of the method is its ability to recover pairwise correlations between neuron pairs that were not measured simultaneously.

Clarity
The manuscript is well written overall, but the figure legends lack sufficient detail, and the main text does not elaborate enough on the figures.

Originality
The authors cite several studies on inferring functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging or electrophysiology. However, those methods do not infer functional connections among neurons that were not imaged concurrently.

Significance
There are fundamental limits on the number of neurons that can be imaged simultaneously, which constrains our ability to probe circuit-level computation beyond a few hundred cells. This paper offers a method that helps extend these capabilities.

Overall, this is a solid modeling paper that employs a relatively simple linear dynamical framework and recovers quantities that are otherwise difficult to access.
