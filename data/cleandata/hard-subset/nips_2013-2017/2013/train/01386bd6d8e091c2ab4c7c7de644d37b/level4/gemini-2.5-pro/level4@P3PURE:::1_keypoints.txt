- Presents a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially recorded neural populations to study population dynamics larger than simultaneous recordings.
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian state-space model with missing observations.
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive field matrix, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.
- Parameters estimated via a variant of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.
- Validated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging from mice, showing ability to estimate across-population variability correlations.
- Performs well on simulations even with non-overlapping populations, but experimental data require some overlap for reasonable results.
- Paper lacks explanation for why overlap is unnecessary in simulations but required in experiments.
- Does not address the large difference in correlation magnitudes between simulated (Figure 2c) and experimental data (Figure 3b).
- Major strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously.
- Generally well-written but needs more descriptive figure legends and more detailed figure explanations in the main text.
- Novelty: focuses on inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously recorded neurons, not addressed by prior work.
- Significance: offers a valuable approach to overcome limits on simultaneous neuron recordings, expanding scale of neural circuit analysis.
- Overall: strong paper that captures complex statistical relationships using a relatively simple linear dynamical model.
