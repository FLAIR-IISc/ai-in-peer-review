- Introduces a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system to "stitch" sequentially recorded neuron sets into a single population model using EM.
- Treats full population activity as latent state and each session as partial, noisy observation; estimates A, B, Q, R from partial recordings.
- Validated on simulated data and two-photon calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.
- Key empirical success: predicts noise correlations between neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously.
- Strength: elegant, technically sound framework well-suited to integrate partial observations and recover pairwise correlations.
- Concern: simulations used non-overlapping populations while real data required overlap—authors should explain this discrepancy (e.g., SNR, dynamics complexity, model mismatch).
- Concern: coupling matrix recovery drops from corr=0.73 (sim) to 0.38 (real); authors should discuss sources (nonlinear dynamics, non-Gaussian noise, experimental factors).
- Limitation: reliance on a linear Gaussian model may fail to capture nonlinear or non-Gaussian aspects of real neural data.
- Presentation issue: figure legends are too brief and need to be more self-contained; in-text figure discussion should better guide readers through panels.
- Originality: novel and practical method for inferring functional connections among non-simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Significance: high — offers a principled way to extend the effective scale of neural recordings and build larger circuit-level models.
- Overall: strong, impactful contribution with clear potential, but needs clearer explanations of real-vs-sim performance gaps and improved figure clarity.
