This paper presents an innovative approach to inferring network properties of a large network by sequentially recording from subnetworks and then combining the resulting sub-models. When extended to point-process data, this method could be applied to a wide range of datasets where only a subset of channels is recorded at a time. Notably, this work appears to be unique, with the closest related study being "Neural characterization in partially observed populations of spiking neurons" by J. W. Pillow and P. Latham. The idea seems powerful, but its limitations and potential applications require further exploration.

In practice, it is unclear how much this method relies on data that is close to linear-Gaussian, and the paper does not provide sufficient intuition on how the fitting might break down when deviating from this assumption. In the extreme case, where the full model can be estimated from one subset (up to a linear transformation of the latent dimensions, as the authors state), it is unclear how much additional information about the coupling matrix is gained from adding other subsets, as opposed to simply using more data from one subset.

Another point that raises questions is why the model only works on real data when there is some overlap between the two populations. This suggests that the linear-Gaussian simulated data does not capture the full complexity of the problem, and it would be beneficial to have at least some intuition about how the overlap constrains the model. In the experiments on real data, where the linearity assumption likely breaks, one must also consider how much of the explained noise correlations (which are fit well, but the couplings themselves hardly correlate to the true couplings at all in Figure 3b) are due to common input and not direct, pair-wise couplings. It is possible that the noise correlations could be better modeled with latent variables than pairwise couplings.

The authors devote a significant amount of space to the introduction and discussion but are remarkably light on the details of the estimation process. The equation for the M-step on the bottom of page 3 is presented as the main contribution, but it is not thoroughly explained or derived. While this result may be trivial for experts in Linear Dynamical Systems (LDS), the lack of explanation erodes the novelty of the paper. It seems that the authors had a valuable idea, but it needs to be explained more clearly to convey the significance of the result.

Overall, the paper presents a neat idea tested against real data, but some details are unclear and require further clarification.
