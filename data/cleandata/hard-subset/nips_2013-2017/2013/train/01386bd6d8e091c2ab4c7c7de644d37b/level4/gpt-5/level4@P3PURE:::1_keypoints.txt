- Proposes a statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged neuronal populations to characterize dynamics beyond simultaneously recorded neurons; demonstrated on simulations and mouse calcium imaging and can estimate correlations across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.

- Formulates the problem as a linear Gaussian state-space (latent dynamical) model with four parameter components—functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive field matrix, process noise covariance, and observation noise covariance—fitted via an EM variant.

- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real-data results appear to require population overlap; the reason for this discrepancy is not explained.

- Reports a large, unexplained difference between correlation estimates in simulation (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b); clarification is needed.

- Key strength: recovers pairwise correlations between neuron pairs that were never measured simultaneously.

- Clarity issue: figure legends are insufficiently descriptive and the text does not guide figure interpretation enough.

- Originality: novel in inferring functional connections among neurons not imaged simultaneously, extending beyond prior methods for 2-photon imaging and electrophysiology.

- Significance: addresses practical limits on simultaneous imaging and enables expanded circuit-level analyses by stitching sequential recordings.

- Overall: solid modeling paper leveraging a simple linear dynamical system; resolving why overlap is needed on real data and explaining the sim-vs-real correlation discrepancy would strengthen the work.
