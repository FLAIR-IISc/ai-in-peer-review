- Proposes a novel statistical method to integrate sequentially imaged neuronal populations, extending analysis beyond simultaneous-imaging limits.  
- Validates the approach on simulated and experimental calcium imaging data, showing it can estimate correlations across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Frames the task as a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive-field matrix, neural-noise covariance, and measurement-noise covariance.  
- Uses a variant of the EM algorithm for parameter estimation on experimental data.  
- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real-data results require some population overlap—this discrepancy is not explained.  
- The authors do not address the large difference in correlation estimates between simulated and real data.  
- Main strength is recovering pairwise correlations between neurons that were not measured simultaneously.  
- Paper is generally well-written, but figure legends and accompanying figure text need more descriptive detail.  
- Method is original in inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons compared to prior work.  
- Significant contribution: offers a potential solution to limitations of simultaneous neuronal imaging, enabling richer circuit-level analysis.  
- Overall assessment: a well-crafted, valuable contribution that uses a simple linear dynamics model to capture elusive measures.
