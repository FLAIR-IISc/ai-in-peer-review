- Promising idea: sequentially record subnetworks and stitch submodels to infer properties of a large network; extension to point-process data is useful for neural datasets.
- Related work: closest prior is Pillow & Latham (“Neural characterization in partially observed populations of spiking neurons”).
- Unclear dependence on linear–Gaussian assumptions; no intuition or diagnostics on how performance degrades with nonlinearity or non-Gaussian noise.
- Identifiability concern: when a single subset already identifies the model up to linear latent transforms, it’s unclear what extra information additional subsets provide versus more data from one subset.
- Real-data requirement for overlap: method appears to work only with overlap between recorded populations, suggesting simulations don’t capture crucial aspects; need explanation of how overlap constrains the model and which parameters benefit.
- Ambiguity between shared input and direct couplings: on real data the model fits noise correlations but inferred couplings correlate poorly with ground truth, so separation of common input vs pairwise coupling is unclear.
- Estimation details lacking: the M-step (p.3) is presented as a main contribution but is neither derived nor explained—if it follows standard LDS results this should be stated, otherwise provide derivation/rationale.
- Presentation imbalance: too much space on intro/discussion and insufficient detail on methodological assumptions, identifiability, and estimation procedures.
