- Introduces a novel statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially recorded neuron subsets to infer large-scale population dynamics.
- Models the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations to predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.
- Enables characterization of population dynamics for sizes beyond the number of simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Builds on prior partially observed population work (e.g., Pillow & Latham 2008) but advances by combining multiple partial observations to infer cross-population dynamics.
- Properly contrasts with other sub-sampling methods (Wohrer et al., Pakman et al.) by focusing on sequential recordings of the same circuit.
- Strong technical quality with a clear theoretical foundation in latent dynamical systems.
- Validated on simulated data and real calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex, substantially outperforming a naive session-averaging baseline.
- On real data, the stitched model predicted noise correlations with 0.84 correlation to the fully observed model versus 0.15 for the naive approach.
- Demonstrates substantial practical significance by extending effective population sizes beyond current imaging limits; validated in both anesthetized and awake mice.
- Methodological clarity is lacking: M-step updates are presented without derivation or intuitive explanation, obscuring novelty versus standard LDS implementations.
- Insufficient analysis of the linear–Gaussian assumption’s limitations and robustness to violations (e.g., nonlinear neural dynamics).
- Unclear why recording-session overlap is necessary: experiments require ~60% overlap despite theoretical claims suggesting less should suffice.
- Inadequate examination of whether predicted noise correlations reflect direct pairwise couplings or common input, especially since coupling parameters poorly correlate with the fully observed model.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on clearer derivations of the estimation procedure, explanation of overlap requirements, and deeper discussion of model assumptions and limitations.
