- Introduces an EM-based method to stitch sequential, partial neural recordings into a unified latent linear-Gaussian dynamical system (LDS) to infer full-population dynamics.  
- Treats unobserved neurons in each session as missing data and estimates global parameters to predict cross-session noise correlations.  
- Validated on simulated and real 2-photon calcium imaging data, accurately predicting noise correlations between never-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Clever validation using a fully observed dataset split into partially overlapping sessions, compared to fully observed and naive baselines.  
- Technically sound with convincing quantitative evaluation showing strong empirical performance.  
- Presentation is generally clear, but methodological details (M-step updates for d and R) lack derivation, context, and citations.  
- The specific application of stitching multiple non-simultaneous sessions into one model appears novel and broadly applicable.  
- Highly significant for overcoming experimental field-of-view limits and enabling larger-scale circuit modeling.  
- Needs clearer discussion of the role of overlapping ("anchor") neurons, since real data required overlap while simulations did not.  
- Should discuss robustness to model mismatch (limits of linear-Gaussian assumptions and effects of nonlinearity or non-Gaussian spiking).  
- Must clarify interpretation: model recovers effective noise correlations well but may not identify true monosynaptic couplings.  
- Could benefit from explicit modeling of common inputs or low-dimensional latents to separate effective interactions from true connectivity.  
- Lacks analysis of information gain and experimental design trade-offs (recording new neurons vs. longer recordings of the same neurons).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, significant, strong validation, and broad applicability across recording modalities.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient methodological clarity on EM updates and incomplete exploration of limitations (overlap requirement, linearity).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance contingent on improved methodological exposition and deeper discussion of assumptions and overlap requirements.
