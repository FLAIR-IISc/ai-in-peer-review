- Proposes stitching subnetworks recorded sequentially to infer properties of a large network; potentially powerful and novel for neurophysiological point-process data.  
- Little prior work in this exact area; closest is Pillow & Latham; core idea appears promising.  
- Major concern: heavy reliance on linear-Gaussian assumption with no analysis of how performance degrades when data deviate from it.  
- Unclear benefit of additional subsets versus collecting more data from a single subset in the extreme case where full model is estimable up to a linear transform.  
- Real-data experiments require overlap between observed populations, suggesting simulations do not capture real-data complexities and this requirement is unexplained.  
- Need intuition/analysis for why overlap constrains the model and how much overlap is necessary.  
- In real data, well-fit noise correlations may arise from unobserved common input or latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings.  
- Inferred couplings show little correlation with ground truth (Fig. 3b), casting doubt on interpretability of coupling estimates.  
- Presentation weaknesses: sparse methodological details, especially lacking derivation/explanation of the M-step update presented as a key contribution.  
- Paper’s significance is obscured by unclear assumptions, missing derivations, and insufficient discussion of limitations and applicability.
