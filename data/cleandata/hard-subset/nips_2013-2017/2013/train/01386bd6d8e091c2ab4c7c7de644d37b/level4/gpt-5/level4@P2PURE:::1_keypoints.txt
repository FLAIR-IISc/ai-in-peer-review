- Introduces a statistical method to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuronal populations, increasing effective population size for characterizing dynamics.  
- Evaluated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging in mice, demonstrating estimation of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged populations.  
- Formulates the problem as fitting a latent linear-Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive-field matrix, and neural and measurement noise covariance matrices.  
- Parameters are estimated using a variant of the EM algorithm.  
- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real data requires some overlap between populations for reasonable results.  
- Reviewer questions why overlap wasn’t required in simulations and notes an unexplained discrepancy between simulated (Fig.2c) and real-data correlations (Fig.3b).  
- Key strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neuron pairs that were not measured simultaneously.  
- Writing is generally clear, but figure legends and main-text explanations of figures are insufficiently descriptive.  
- Originality: extends prior connectivity-inference work by inferring functional connections across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Significance: helps overcome limits on simultaneous imaging and extends ability to characterize circuit-level computation.  
- Overall: a solid modeling paper using a simple linear dynamical model to recover quantities otherwise difficult to obtain.
