- Paper presents a novel, compelling idea: infer global network properties by sequentially recording smaller subnetworks and combining sub-models; potentially broadly applicable to point-process data.
- No directly comparable prior work identified, making the contribution innovative.
- Major concern about reliance on the linear-Gaussian assumption and lack of analysis/intution for how deviations affect fitting or cause failure.
- Unclear how much additional information other subsets provide when a single subset can recover the full model up to linear latent transforms.
- Need clarification on why real-data models require overlap between populations, suggesting simulations don’t capture real-data complexities.
- In real data, explained noise correlations may be due to shared inputs or latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings; couplings don’t match true couplings in Fig. 3b.
- Paper focuses heavily on introduction/discussion but provides limited detail on the estimation process and key equations.
- The presented M-step equation is highlighted as a contribution but is neither adequately explained nor derived, weakening the exposition.
- Overall: intriguing and promising idea tested on real data, but methodological details and explanations are insufficient.
