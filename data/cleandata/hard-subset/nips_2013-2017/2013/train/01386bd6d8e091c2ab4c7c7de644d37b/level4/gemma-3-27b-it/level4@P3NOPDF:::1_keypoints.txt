- Introduces a novel statistical method to integrate sequentially imaged neural populations, expanding observable network size beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Formulates the problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance, estimated via EM.  
- Demonstrates utility on simulated and experimental calcium imaging data, recovering pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neurons.  
- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real data requires substantial overlap for reasonable results—why simulation succeeded without overlap is unexplained.  
- Observed discrepancy in correlation magnitudes between simulated and experimental data is not addressed, leaving uncertainty about model limits versus biological differences.  
- Strength: ability to recover correlations that are otherwise unobservable, potentially enabling study of larger circuits.  
- Clarity issues: figure legends and in-text figure discussion lack detail; more context on simulation choices (e.g., Fig. 2) is needed.  
- Originality: novel focus on stitching sequential recordings to infer functional connections not handled by prior methods.  
- Significance: method could overcome a fundamental experimental limitation in systems neuroscience and impact circuit-level analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper recommended for acceptance pending clarifications about overlap necessity and correlation magnitude discrepancies.
