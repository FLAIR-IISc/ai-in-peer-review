- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially recorded neural populations to characterize larger-scale population dynamics.  
- Validated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging from mice; claims ability to estimate correlations between non-simultaneously recorded populations.  
- Frames problem as a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a simple linear Gaussian model.  
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance; estimated via an EM variant.  
- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations, but real data requires overlap between recordings—authors should clarify this discrepancy.  
- Paper does not address the large difference in estimated correlation values between simulated and real data.  
- Key strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neurons that were never recorded simultaneously.  
- Generally well-written, but figure legends and main-text explanations lack sufficient detail for some figures.  
- Originality: extends prior work by inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Significance: helps overcome limits of simultaneous imaging, enabling broader circuit-level analyses.  
- Overall: strong paper demonstrating that a simple linear dynamical model can recover important circuit-level metrics otherwise hard to obtain.
