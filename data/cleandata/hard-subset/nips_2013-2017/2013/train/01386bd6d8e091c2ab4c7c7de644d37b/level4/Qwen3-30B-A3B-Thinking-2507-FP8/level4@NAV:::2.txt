

This paper demonstrates that network properties of large systems can be inferred by sequentially recording from subnetworks and subsequently stitching the resulting sub-models. Extended to point-process data, this approach could be broadly applicable to datasets where only a few channels are recorded daily. To my knowledge, no prior work closely parallels this concept (J. W. Pillow and P. Latham, "Neural characterization in partially observed populations of spiking neurons" comes closest), making it a compelling contribution.  

However, it remains unclear how severely the method is constrained by deviations from linear-Gaussian assumptions, and the paper offers insufficient intuition regarding failure modes under such deviations. Specifically, when the full model can be estimated from a single subset (up to a linear transformation of latent dimensions), it is unclear how much additional information about the coupling matrix is gained by incorporating further subsets versus simply increasing data from one subset.  

Another unresolved issue concerns the necessity of overlap between populations for the real-data model to function. This implies the linear-Gaussian simulations do not fully capture real-world behavior, yet the paper lacks explanation of how overlap constrains the model.  

In real-data experiments—where linearity likely fails—the observed fit to noise correlations (as shown in 3b) appears strong, but the couplings themselves show poor correlation with ground-truth couplings. It is unclear whether the explained noise correlations primarily arise from common input rather than direct pairwise couplings, suggesting latent variables might better model noise correlations than pairwise couplings.  

The authors dedicate significant space to the introduction and discussion but provide minimal detail on estimation. The M-step equation (bottom of page 3) is presented as the core contribution yet lacks derivation or explanation. While this may be trivial for LDS experts, its absence undermines the perceived novelty of the approach. The core idea is promising, but the paper requires clearer exposition to convey its significance.  

A promising concept with real-data validation, but key methodological details remain inadequately explained.
