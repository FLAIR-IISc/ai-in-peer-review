- Contribution: Proposes a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets to characterize population dynamics beyond simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Model: Frames the problem as a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations, parameterized by coupling, stimulus receptive fields, and neural/measurement noise covariances, fit via an EM variant.  
- Validation: Applied to simulated data and mouse calcium imaging, demonstrating estimation of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged populations.  
- Performance on simulations: Model performs well on simulated data even with non-overlapping populations.  
- Performance on real data: Requires a certain level of population overlap to obtain reasonable results.  
- Unaddressed issue: Unclear why overlap wasn't needed in simulations and why correlation estimates differ substantially between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b).  
- Strength: Able to recover pairwise correlations between neuron pairs never measured simultaneously.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written overall, but figure legends and in-text figure explanations need more detail.  
- Originality: Novel relative to prior work because it infers functional relationships between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Significance: Offers a way to overcome limits of simultaneous imaging, expanding the scale at which circuit dynamics can be studied.  
- Overall: Strong paper leveraging a simple linear dynamics model to extract otherwise inaccessible measures, valuable for the neuroscience community.
