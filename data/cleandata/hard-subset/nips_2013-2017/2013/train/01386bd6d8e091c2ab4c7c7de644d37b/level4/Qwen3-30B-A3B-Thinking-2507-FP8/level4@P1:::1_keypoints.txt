- Novel method: latent dynamical system (LDS) approach to "stitch" sequentially recorded neural population data and infer joint dynamics with missing observations.  
- Problem addressed: overcomes 2-photon imaging limits by predicting noise correlations between neurons never recorded together.  
- Validation: tested on simulated populations and in vivo mouse somatosensory calcium imaging, recovering cross-population correlations (simulation: high correlations; real data: strong but lower correlations).  
- Technical strength: well-motivated LDS model, rigorous execution, and clear theoretical identifiability when sufficient session overlap exists.  
- Performance metrics: simulation noise-correlation recovery r≈0.92 (and cross-couplings r≈0.73); real-data noise-correlation recovery r≈0.84 (cross-couplings r≈0.38).  
- Key discrepancy: unexplained gap between simulation and real-data correlation recovery and differing overlap requirements (simulation works without overlap; real data requires overlap).  
- Modeling limitation: reliance on linear Gaussian assumptions; authors note more complex models could improve accuracy.  
- Clarity: paper is well-organized and precise, but some figure legends lack detail and key results should be highlighted in the text.  
- Originality: novel treatment of non-simultaneous recordings, distinct from prior connectivity and sub-sampling methods that impose restrictive assumptions or lack scalability.  
- Significance: addresses a fundamental neuroscience bottleneck, enabling circuit-scale analyses beyond current simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Applicability limit: requirement for sufficient overlap between sessions may restrict use in sparse recording scenarios.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, impactful work with Accept recommendation.  
- Pros: novel approach, strong empirical validation, solid theory, high practical significance.  
- Cons: unexplained sim-vs-real discrepancy, potential oversimplification from linear model, overlap requirement.
