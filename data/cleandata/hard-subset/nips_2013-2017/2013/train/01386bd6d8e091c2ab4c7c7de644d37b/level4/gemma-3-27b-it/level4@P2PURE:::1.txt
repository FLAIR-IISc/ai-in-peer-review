This paper presents a statistical method for integrating activity data from sequentially imaged sets of neurons, effectively increasing the population size available for studying neural dynamics. This addresses a critical limitation in current experimental techniques, where complete circuit imaging is often impossible. The method is demonstrated using both simulated and experimental calcium imaging data from mice, successfully estimating correlations in variability between populations that were not simultaneously observed.

The authors formulate the problem as estimating the parameters of a latent dynamical system with missing observations, specifically employing a linear Gaussian system. The model incorporates four key parameters: a functional coupling matrix, a stimulus receptive fields matrix describing stimulus-dependent activity, and covariance matrices for neural and measurement noise. These parameters are estimated from experimental data using a modified Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.

Regarding quality, the model performs well on simulated data with non-overlapping neuronal populations. However, application to real data requires a substantial degree of overlap between imaged populations to yield reasonable results. While the benefit of increased overlap is intuitive, the paper does not explain why overlap was not a requirement for successful reconstruction in the simulation example. Furthermore, the authors do not address the significant discrepancy in correlation magnitude observed between the simulated data (Fig. 2c) and the real data (Fig. 3b). Despite these points, the model’s ability to recover pairwise correlations between neurons measured at different times remains a notable strength.

In terms of clarity, the paper is generally well-written, but the figure legends lack sufficient detail and the text provides insufficient elaboration on the figures themselves. 

The work exhibits originality because, while other studies have focused on inferring functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging and electrophysiological data, none have explicitly addressed the inference of functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.

The significance of this work lies in its potential to overcome a fundamental limitation of current neuroimaging techniques: the restricted number of simultaneously recordable neurons. This limitation hinders our ability to understand neural computation at the circuit level. This paper offers a promising method for extending these capabilities. 

Overall, this is a solid modeling paper that leverages a relatively simple linear dynamics model to extract information – particularly pairwise correlations – that would otherwise be difficult to obtain.
