- Proposes a novel statistical method to “stitch” sequential partial recordings into a single latent dynamical system model.  
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations to infer neural population dynamics.  
- Enables characterization of much larger population dynamics than the number of simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on simulated data and real calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Method accurately predicts correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs, even when linearity assumptions are challenged.  
- Unclear how sensitive the method is to deviations from the linear-Gaussian assumptions and where fitting breaks down.  
- Lacks intuition and analysis on how the amount of overlap between recordings affects performance and why overlap is necessary.  
- Paper is light on estimation details; key equations (e.g., M-step) are presented without derivation or explanation.  
- Strong on originality and potential significance; stitching idea is powerful and broadly applicable.  
- Needs improved clarity and technical soundness, with more detailed estimation procedures and derivations.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on revisions to address limitations, add derivations/explanations, and improve clarity.  
- Overall rating: 7/10.
