- Introduces a novel statistical method to integrate sequentially imaged neuron sets, increasing effective population size for studying population dynamics beyond simultaneous imaging limits.
- Demonstrates ability to estimate correlations across non-simultaneously imaged populations using simulated and experimental mouse calcium imaging data.
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations via a linear Gaussian model with four parameters: coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.
- Parameters are estimated from data using a variant of the EM algorithm.
- Performs well on simulated non-overlapping populations but requires population overlap to produce reasonable results on real data.
- Authors do not explain why overlap is unnecessary in simulations but required in real data.
- Significant discrepancy in correlation magnitudes between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b) is not addressed.
- Key strength is recovering pairwise correlations between neurons that were not measured simultaneously.
- Paper is well written overall, but figure legends lack descriptive detail and the text could better elaborate on figures.
- Originality: extends prior connectivity inference work by targeting functional relationships between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Significance: offers a promising way to overcome limits on simultaneous imaging and to probe circuit-level neural computation using a relatively simple linear dynamics model.
