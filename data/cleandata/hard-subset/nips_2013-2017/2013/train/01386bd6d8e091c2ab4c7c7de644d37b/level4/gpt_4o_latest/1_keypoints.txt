- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuronal populations to characterize larger population dynamics than can be simultaneously imaged.
- Demonstrated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging from mice, showing estimation of correlations between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.
- Model includes four key parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive field matrix, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance, estimated via a variant of EM.
- Performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping populations.
- Real-data performance requires some overlap between populations; reason for lack of need for overlap in simulations is unclear.
- Authors do not explain the large discrepancy in correlation values between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b).
- Strength: can recover pairwise correlations between neurons not measured simultaneously.
- Paper is generally well-written but figure legends and main-text figure descriptions lack sufficient detail.
- Originality: novel in inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons compared to prior 2-photon/electrophysiology work.
- Significance: addresses experimental limits on simultaneous imaging to enable study of larger neuronal circuits.
- Overall assessment: strong methodological contribution using a relatively simple linear dynamics model to extract otherwise hard-to-obtain measures.
