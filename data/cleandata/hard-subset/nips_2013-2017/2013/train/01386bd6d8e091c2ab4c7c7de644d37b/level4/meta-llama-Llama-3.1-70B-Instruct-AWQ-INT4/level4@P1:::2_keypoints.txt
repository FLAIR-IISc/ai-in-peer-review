- Novel approach: sequentially record subnetworks and "stitch" sub-models to infer large-network properties, tested on simulations and 2-photon mouse data.  
- Method: fits a latent dynamical system with missing observations to reconstruct dynamics and predict correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.  
- Clear, concise introduction and literature review; results are well-presented.  
- Assumes linear-Gaussian dynamics, which may not hold for many neural populations.  
- Key fitting step (M-step) is not well explained; main contribution lacks detailed derivation.  
- Requires overlap between populations in real-data experiments; unclear how overlap constrains the model or performance without overlap.  
- Unclear whether predicted noise correlations reflect common input (latent factors) or direct pairwise couplings.  
- Strength: novel, promising approach with broad applicability to partially recorded datasets.  
- Strength: effective demonstration on both simulated and real data.  
- Strength: clear problem framing and literature review.  
- Weakness: reliance on linear-Gaussian assumption limits generality.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation and derivation of the M-step/fitting procedure.  
- Weakness: overlap requirement not justified or systematically explored.  
- Weakness: does not disentangle common input versus pairwise coupling in noise correlations.  
- Accept arguments: novelty, potential applicability, and clear presentation.  
- Reject arguments: questionable assumptions, unclear methodological novelty, unexplored overlap issue, and unresolved noise-correlation sources.  
- Recommendation: provide detailed derivation and explanation of the M-step.  
- Recommendation: evaluate robustness to deviations from linear-Gaussian dynamics.  
- Recommendation: analyze role and necessity of overlap between recorded populations.  
- Recommendation: explore modeling noise correlations with latent variables instead of pairwise couplings.
