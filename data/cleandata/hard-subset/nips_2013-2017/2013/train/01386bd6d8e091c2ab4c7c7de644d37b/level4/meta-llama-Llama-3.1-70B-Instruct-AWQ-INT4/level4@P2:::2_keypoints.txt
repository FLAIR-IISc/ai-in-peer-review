- Proposes a novel "stitching" method to infer network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and combining sub-models.
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations to estimate joint activity from partial recordings.
- Validated on simulated data and real mouse somatosensory calcium imaging, demonstrating practical applicability.
- Main contribution: a statistical tool for combining multiple partial observations of the same neural circuit into a joint model.
- Method can accurately predict correlated dynamics of large populations, including non-simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Real-data performance approaches the upper bound set by a fully observed model trained on all neurons.
- Strength: clear, concise writing that aids understanding of the method and reasoning.
- Strength: simulated experiments effectively illustrate method properties and intuition.
- Strength: well-presented, convincing experimental results on real data.
- Limitation: relies on linearity and Gaussianity assumptions that may not hold in practice; lack of intuition on failure modes.
- Limitation: unclear explanation of how overlap between population recordings affects performance.
- Limitation: estimation procedure lacks detailed derivation or explanation, notably the M-step equation.
- Limitation: no clear comparison to alternative approaches (e.g., more data from subsets, latent-variable common input models).
- Recommendation: provide intuition on how deviations from linear/Gaussian assumptions impact fitting.
- Recommendation: clarify and quantify the role of overlap between recorded populations.
- Recommendation: include detailed derivations and explanations of the estimation procedure (M-step).
- Recommendation: compare the method empirically or theoretically to other relevant approaches.
