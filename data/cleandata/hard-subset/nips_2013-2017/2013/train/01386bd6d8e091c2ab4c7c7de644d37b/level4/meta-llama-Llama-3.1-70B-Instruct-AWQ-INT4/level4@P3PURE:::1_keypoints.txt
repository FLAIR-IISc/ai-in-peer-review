- Paper proposes a statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged neuron sets to expand measurable population dynamics beyond simultaneous imaging limits.
- Method framed as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive-field matrix, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.
- Parameters estimated from data using a variant of the EM algorithm.
- Demonstrated on simulated data and experimental mouse calcium imaging, showing ability to estimate correlations between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping populations; real-data performance requires a certain level of population overlap, with better overlap yielding better results.
- Unclear why overlap was unnecessary in the simulated example and why correlations differ markedly between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b).
- Strength of the approach is recovering pairwise correlations for neuron pairs not imaged simultaneously.
- Paper is well written but figure legends and in-text figure descriptions need more detail and elaboration.
- Novel contribution relative to prior work because it infers functional relationships across non-simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Significance: addresses fundamental limits of simultaneous imaging by enabling larger-scale circuit analysis.
- Overall assessment: a good model paper using a simple linear dynamics framework to recover otherwise inaccessible measures.
