- Proposes a novel method to infer large-network properties by sequentially recording subnetworks and stitching sub-models, extendable to point-process data and useful when few channels move daily.  
- Idea is powerful and promising, with closest related work being Pillow & Latham’s study of partially observed spiking populations.  
- Unclear how dependent the method is on the linear-Gaussian assumption and how fitting fails under deviations from that assumption.  
- Uncertainty whether additional subsets add coupling information beyond what a single subset (up to linear latent transform) provides versus simply collecting more data from one subset.  
- Requirement for overlap between populations to work on real data suggests simulations don’t capture full complexity; need intuition on how overlap constrains the model.  
- In real-data experiments, explained noise correlations may reflect common input or latent variables rather than true pairwise couplings, questioning interpretation of learned couplings.  
- Paper is light on estimation details: the presented M-step equation is not thoroughly derived or explained, which weakens the claimed contribution.  
- Overall: neat, promising idea tested on real data, but needs clearer explanation, more derivations, and better discussion of limitations to convey its significance.
