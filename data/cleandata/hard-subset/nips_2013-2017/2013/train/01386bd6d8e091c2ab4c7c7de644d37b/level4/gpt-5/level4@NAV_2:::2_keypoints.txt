- Proposes stitching sequentially recorded subnetwork models to recover properties of a large neural circuit; potentially powerful and extendable to spiking (point-process) datasets.  
- Reviewer is unaware of closely related prior work (closest is Pillow & Latham), suggesting novelty.  
- Unclear how restricted the approach is to linear-Gaussian data; paper lacks intuition about failure modes under departures from these assumptions.  
- In the case where a single subset identifies the full model up to a linear transform, it’s unclear how much extra information additional subsets provide versus more data from the same subset.  
- On real data the method only works with overlap between populations, implying simulations don’t capture key complexities; need explanation for how overlap constrains the model and why performance drops without it.  
- For real (nonlinear) data, recovered noise correlations may reflect common input or low-dimensional latent variables rather than direct pairwise couplings; couplings show poor correspondence to reference.  
- Paper emphasizes introduction and discussion but is brief on estimation details; the presented M-step equation is neither explained nor derived, reducing perceived novelty.  
- Overall judgment: compelling idea with real-data tests, but several methodological details and exposition need strengthening.
