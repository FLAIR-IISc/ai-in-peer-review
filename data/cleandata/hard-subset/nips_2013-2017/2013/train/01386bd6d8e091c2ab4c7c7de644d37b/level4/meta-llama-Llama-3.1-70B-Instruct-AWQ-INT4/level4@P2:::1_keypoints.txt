- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neuron sets, enabling characterization of population dynamics beyond simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Applies the method to simulated and experimental calcium imaging data, showing estimation of correlations across non-simultaneously imaged populations.  
- Models the problem as a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations and fits parameters via a variant of EM.  
- Defines four model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Demonstrates model performance on both simulated and real data, recovering pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs.  
- Requires a certain level of overlap between populations to yield reasonable results on real data, unlike in simulations.  
- Fails to explain the substantial difference in correlation results between simulated data (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b).  
- Paper is well written and situates the work clearly within existing literature, highlighting novelty in inferring functional connections from sequential imaging.  
- Some figure legends and the main-text discussion of figures need more descriptive detail and elaboration.  
- Significance: offers a practical approach to overcome experimental limits on simultaneous imaging and expand circuit-level neural computation analysis.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel method, validated on simulations and real data, and potentially significant impact on neuroscience experiments.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited applicability due to overlap requirement, unexplained simulation vs. real-data discrepancy, and clarity issues in figures/text.
