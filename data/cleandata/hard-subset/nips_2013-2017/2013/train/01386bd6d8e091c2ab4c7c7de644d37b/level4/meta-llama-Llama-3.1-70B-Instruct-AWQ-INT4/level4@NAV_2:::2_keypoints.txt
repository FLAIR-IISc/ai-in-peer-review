- Novel method for inferring neural population dynamics by "stitching" sequential partial recordings into one model.  
- Distinct contribution: infers functional connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons.  
- Uses a simple linear Gaussian dynamical system as the core statistical model.  
- Clear presentation and successful application to real calcium imaging data from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Approach enables substantially larger effective population sizes for characterizing dynamics.  
- Key weakness: linearity and Gaussian assumptions may not hold and robustness to violations is unclear.  
- Reliance on overlap between recorded populations to constrain the model is not fully explained; needs more intuition.  
- Some estimation details, equations, and derivations are insufficiently presented, reducing clarity.  
- Scores highly on originality and potential significance for neuroscience.  
- Technical quality could be improved by providing fuller derivations and clearer estimation procedures.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, simple effective model, and successful real-data demonstration.  
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable modeling assumptions and gaps in clarity/technical detail.
