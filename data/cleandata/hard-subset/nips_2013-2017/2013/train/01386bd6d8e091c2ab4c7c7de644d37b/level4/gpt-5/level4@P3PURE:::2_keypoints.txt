- Proposes stitching submodels from sequentially recorded subnetworks to infer large-system network properties.
- Potentially applicable to point-process data and datasets with daily channel subsets; related work includes Pillow & Latham.
- Overall described as a powerful and promising idea.
- Unclear reliance on linear-Gaussian assumptions and how performance degrades when assumptions are violated.
- Unclear how much extra information multi-subset recordings provide versus more data from a single subset.
- Real data appears to require overlap between recorded populations; need intuition for why overlap constrains the model.
- Real-data fits noise correlations well but inferred couplings poorly match true couplings; need to separate effects of common input from direct pairwise couplings.
- Estimation procedure is under-specified; the M-step is presented without derivation or explanation, leaving novelty ambiguous.
- Recommended improvements: analyze robustness to nonlinearity/non-Gaussianity, explain the role of overlap, disentangle common input vs direct couplings, and provide detailed derivations of estimation steps.
