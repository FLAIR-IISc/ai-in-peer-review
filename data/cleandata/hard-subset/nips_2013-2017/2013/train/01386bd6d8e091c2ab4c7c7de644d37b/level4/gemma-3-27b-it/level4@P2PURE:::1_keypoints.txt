- Proposes a statistical method to integrate sequentially imaged neuronal activity, increasing effective population size for studying neural dynamics.
- Targets the experimental limitation that full-circuit simultaneous imaging is often impossible.
- Validated on simulated and experimental calcium imaging from mice, estimating correlations between non-simultaneously observed populations.
- Casts the problem as estimating parameters of a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance estimated via a modified EM algorithm.
- Performs well on simulated data with non-overlapping neuronal populations.
- Real-data results require substantial overlap between imaged populations; the paper does not explain why simulations succeeded without overlap.
- Does not address the large discrepancy in correlation magnitudes between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real (Fig. 3b) data.
- Key strength is recovering pairwise correlations between neurons recorded at different times.
- Generally well-written, but figure legends and in-text figure explanations lack sufficient detail.
- Original contribution: explicitly inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons.
- Significant potential to overcome limits of simultaneous recording and extend circuit-level neuroimaging analysis.
- Overall assessment: a solid modeling paper using a simple linear dynamics model to extract otherwise inaccessible pairwise correlations.
