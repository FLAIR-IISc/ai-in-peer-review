- Presents a statistical method to “stitch” sequentially imaged neuronal populations to recover population dynamics beyond simultaneously imaged cells.  
- Validated on simulated data and mouse calcium imaging, claiming estimation of shared variability between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Models the problem as a linear Gaussian state-space (latent dynamical) model with coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, process noise covariance, measurement noise covariance, learned via EM.  
- On simulated data, the model performs well even with non-overlapping populations, but real data requires population overlap for reasonable performance.  
- Reviewer requests explanation of why overlap was unnecessary in simulations (possible factors: SNR, correct model assumptions, trial structure).  
- Notes a large, unexplained discrepancy between inferred correlations in simulation and real data; asks discussion of causes (model mismatch, calcium noise, nonstationarities, stimulus/behavioral differences).  
- Highlights a key strength: recovery of pairwise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously.  
- Critiques figure legends and figure integration as insufficiently descriptive; asks for expanded captions and clearer text guidance.  
- Notes novelty: stitching sequential recordings to infer relationships across non-simultaneous neurons is a novel and practical contribution.  
- Emphasizes significance: method could overcome experimental limits on simultaneously imaged neuron counts and aid experimental design.  
- Overall assessment: solid methodological paper using a simple linear dynamical model and EM, but must address overlap role and sim-vs-real correlation discrepancies to clarify robustness and requirements.
