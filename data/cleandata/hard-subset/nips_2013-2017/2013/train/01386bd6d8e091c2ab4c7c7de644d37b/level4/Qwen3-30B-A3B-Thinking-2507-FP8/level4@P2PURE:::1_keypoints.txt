- Introduces a statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged neuronal populations to expand analyzable population size.
- Validated on simulated data and experimental calcium imaging from mice, showing ability to estimate variability correlations across non-simultaneously imaged populations.
- Formulates problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations using a linear Gaussian framework.
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive-field matrix, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.
- Parameters are estimated from data using a variant of the EM algorithm.
- Performs well on simulated data with two non-overlapping populations.
- Requires a minimum level of overlap between populations for reasonable results on real data.
- Unclear why overlap was unnecessary in the simulated example and why simulated vs. real correlation estimates differ substantially.
- Main strength is recovering pairwise correlations between neurons not imaged simultaneously.
- Paper is well-written but figure legends lack detail and the main text underexplains the figures.
- Novelty: extends prior connectivity inference work by addressing neurons imaged non-simultaneously.
- Significance: offers a way to overcome experimental imaging limits and characterize larger circuits.
- Overall: strong model paper using a simple linear dynamics framework to recover otherwise difficult-to-measure statistics.
