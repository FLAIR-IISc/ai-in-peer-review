- Presents a novel statistical method to integrate sequentially imaged neuron sets, expanding measurable population sizes for dynamics.  
- Models the problem as fitting a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Model parameters: functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive-field matrix, neural noise covariance, and measurement noise covariance.  
- Parameters are estimated from data using a variant of the Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Applied to both simulated data and experimental calcium imaging in mice.  
- Demonstrates ability to recover correlations and pairwise relationships between neurons that were not imaged simultaneously.  
- Real-data performance depends on overlap between populations, whereas simulated data did not require overlap — this discrepancy is unexplained.  
- Authors do not address the substantial difference in correlation results between simulated (Fig. 2c) and real data (Fig. 3b).  
- Paper is well written, but figure legends and the main-text figure descriptions need more detail and elaboration.  
- Original contribution: inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons, not shown in prior 2-photon or electrophysiology studies.  
- Significance: provides a practical approach to overcome limits of simultaneous imaging and better characterize circuit-level neural computation.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper that uses a relatively simple linear dynamics model to estimate otherwise hard-to-retrieve measures.
