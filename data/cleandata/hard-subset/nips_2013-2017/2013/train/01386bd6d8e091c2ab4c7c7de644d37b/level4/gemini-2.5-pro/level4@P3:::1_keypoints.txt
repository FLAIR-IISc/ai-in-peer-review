- Proposes a statistical method (LDS + EM) to infer joint dynamics from multiple sequential, partially overlapping recordings.
- Tackles the important "stitching" problem caused by limited simultaneous recordings in current imaging techniques.
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations and learns parameters via EM.
- Validated on simulated data and two real mouse calcium-imaging datasets.
- Demonstrates the ability to predict noise correlations between neurons that were never recorded simultaneously.
- Novel contribution: principled integration of non-simultaneous recordings into a single circuit-dynamics model.
- Practical significance: could influence experimental design and enable larger-scale circuit analyses with current technologies.
- Strength: principled, statistically sound latent-variable formulation (linear-Gaussian LDS) that is understandable and extensible.
- Strength: strong empirical results that approach an upper bound and outperform a naive baseline.
- Clear motivation and accessible introduction linking experimental limitations to the modeling goal.
- Key weakness: unexplained performance gap between simulations and real data.
- Key weakness: overlap between sessions appears necessary in real data but not in simulations; requires deeper analysis (noise, recording length, model mismatch).
- Key weakness: degraded parameter recovery on real data (coupling matrix correlation 0.38 vs 0.73 in simulation), likely reflecting model mismatch and identifiability issues that need discussion.
- Presentation could be improved by making figures and legends more self-contained and descriptive.
- Overall assessment: original and significant work; acceptance recommended if limitations and practical requirements (e.g., overlap necessity, model assumptions) are discussed more thoroughly.
