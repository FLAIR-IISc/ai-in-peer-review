The paper "Inferring neural population dynamics from multiple partial recordings of the same neural circuit" presents a novel statistical method for "stitching" together sequentially imaged sets of neurons, enabling the characterization of population dynamics beyond the number of simultaneously imaged neurons. This approach addresses the experimental and technical limitations of imaging entire neural circuits, providing a more comprehensive understanding of circuit function.

The authors model the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations, using a simple linear Gaussian system. The model parameters, including the functional coupling matrix, stimulus receptive fields, and covariance matrices for neural and measurement noise, are estimated from experimental data using a variant of the standard EM algorithm.

Quality: The model demonstrates promising results on both simulated and experimental data. However, the requirement for overlap between populations in the real data example, but not in the simulated data, raises questions about the underlying assumptions and limitations of the model. Additionally, the authors do not address the significant difference in correlation results between the simulated and real data examples (Figures 2c and 3b). The recovery of pairwise correlations between non-simultaneously measured neuron pairs is a notable achievement of the model.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, but some figure legends and corresponding text in the body could be more descriptive and elaborate. This would facilitate a deeper understanding of the results and methods.

Originality: The authors acknowledge previous work on inferring functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging data and electrophysiological measurements. However, their method is distinct in its ability to infer functional connections between non-simultaneously imaged neurons, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

Significance: The paper addresses a significant limitation in current imaging capabilities, which restricts our understanding of neural computation at the circuit level. By providing a method to expand these capabilities, the authors offer a promising solution for advancing our knowledge of neural circuits and their function.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and valuable method for characterizing population dynamics beyond the limitations of current imaging techniques.
* The model demonstrates promising results on both simulated and experimental data.
* The authors provide a clear and well-written presentation of their work.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The requirement for overlap between populations in the real data example, but not in the simulated data, raises questions about the underlying assumptions and limitations of the model.
* The authors do not address the significant difference in correlation results between the simulated and real data examples.
* Some figure legends and corresponding text could be more descriptive and elaborate.

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field. With some revisions to address the mentioned concerns, it has the potential to make a significant impact on our understanding of neural circuits and their function.
