- Summary: Proposes stitching sequential partial neural recordings into a full-population latent linear dynamical system (LDS) using EM with Kalman smoothing; validated on simulated and split real calcium-imaging data, accurately predicting pairwise correlations and recovering connectivity, outperforming session-wise models and approaching fully observed upper bound.

- High significance/impact: Addresses a key experimental bottleneck by enabling larger-scale population analyses from feasible partial recordings.

- Novelty/originality: Novel application of LDS with missing data to stitch non-simultaneous recordings and predict unobserved cross-correlations.

- Technical soundness and evaluation: Method is well-explained and rigorously validated with ground-truth simulations, a clever “fictional imaging” real-data test, and benchmarks against upper/lower bounds across brain states.

- Clarity and organization: Paper is well-written with informative figures that support the claims.

- Limitation — linear-Gaussian model: LDS is a simplifying assumption that may miss nonlinear dynamics and spiking statistics; should discuss limits and possible extensions (e.g., switching or nonlinear models).

- Limitation — reliance on overlap: Method fails without overlapping neurons; requires analysis of performance degradation as overlap percentage decreases to guide experiments.

- Limitation — scalability and identifiability: Experiments up to ~100 neurons show parameter quality drops with size; algorithm scales cubically in N, raising concerns for thousands-neuron circuits and potential parameter degeneracies.

- Limitation — stationarity assumption: Assumes stable circuit parameters across sessions, which may be violated by plasticity, state changes, or experimental drift and should be explicitly noted.

- Overall assessment: Excellent, timely, and well-executed contribution with high impact for ML and neuroscience; strongly recommended for acceptance.
