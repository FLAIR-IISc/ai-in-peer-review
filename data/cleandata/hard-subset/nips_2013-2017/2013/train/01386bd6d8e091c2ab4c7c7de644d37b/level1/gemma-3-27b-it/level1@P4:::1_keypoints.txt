- Problem & contribution: Proposes fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations to “stitch” sequential neural recordings and predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Novelty: Framing sequential-recording integration as a latent dynamical system is a clever, potentially powerful approach addressing limitations of current imaging.  
- Technical soundness: Uses established techniques (EM, Kalman smoothing) within a clear linear Gaussian LDS framework; simulations and experiments are reproducible.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear motivation, detailed methods, and informative figures and equations.  
- Significance: Method can expand effective population sizes studied and has practical relevance shown on real somatosensory cortex data.  
- Model simplification concern: Reliance on a linear Gaussian dynamical system may not capture complex nonlinear neural dynamics and robustness to violations is unclear.  
- Computational scalability concern: Method reportedly does not scale well beyond tested population sizes, limiting applicability to very large circuits.  
- Overlap requirement: Needs substantial session overlap (≈60%), which may constrain experimental design and flexibility.  
- Limited biological interpretation: Paper focuses on statistics and correlation prediction with insufficient discussion of biological implications of inferred parameters.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem, is novel and technically sound, demonstrates key result of predicting cross-session correlations, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Strong simplifying assumptions, scalability issues, and large overlap requirements may limit real-data applicability.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong, valuable contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to discuss linear-Gaussian limitations and scalability improvements.
