- Introduces a statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neurons by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on simulated data and real calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Enables prediction of noise correlations between neuron pairs not recorded simultaneously.  
- Builds on prior connectivity-inference and subsampling work but is novel in inferring relations between non-simultaneously observed neurons.  
- Notes that recent subsampling/statistical methods are not directly applicable to this stitching problem.  
- Technically sound with clear organization; claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Authors are careful and provide discussion of strengths, weaknesses, and possible extensions.  
- Clearly written and accessible to non-experts; figures and details appear sufficient for reproduction.  
- Novel combination of known techniques; inferring functional links across non-simultaneous recordings is a new contribution.  
- Significant potential impact by extending characterization of population dynamics beyond simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Pros: novel, well-presented, theoretically and empirically supported, and potentially impactful.  
- Cons: method limited to linear Gaussian dynamical systems, uncertain generalization to other models and data types, and perceived insufficiency in discussing some limitations.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8.5/10, Significance 9/10; overall recommendation: accept.
