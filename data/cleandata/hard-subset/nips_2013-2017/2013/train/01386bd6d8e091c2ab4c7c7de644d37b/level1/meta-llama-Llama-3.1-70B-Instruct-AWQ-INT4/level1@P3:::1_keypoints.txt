- Introduces a statistical "stitching" method using a latent dynamical system with missing observations to combine sequentially imaged neurons into one model, validated on mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Demonstrates the method can predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.  
- Extends prior work on inferring connectivity from imaging/electrophysiology by targeting functional links between non-simultaneously observed neurons.  
- Notes related subsampling approaches but highlights they do not address this specific stitching problem.  
- Method is technically sound with clear presentation and thorough evaluation on simulated and experimental data.  
- Claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; choice of a linear Gaussian dynamical model is justified and limitations acknowledged.  
- Evaluation includes useful upper and lower performance bounds, strengthening the empirical assessment.  
- Authors transparently discuss weaknesses, including the need for overlap between populations and potential overfitting for large populations.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses figures/tables effectively; discussion outlines implications and future directions.  
- The approach is novel and creative, using latent dynamics with missing data to infer non-simultaneous interactions.  
- The unique evaluation framework clarifies the method's strengths and limitations.  
- For acceptance: novelty, rigorous evaluation, and significant implications for mapping population dynamics; Against: reliance on a simple linear Gaussian model, overlap requirement, and overfitting risks.
