This paper proposes a statistical method for "stitching" together sequentially imaged sets of neurons into one model, allowing for the characterization of population dynamics beyond the number of simultaneously imaged neurons. The authors model the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations, using a linear Gaussian dynamical system (LDS) to capture the correlation structure and stimulus selectivity of the population from partial observations of the population activity.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on both simulated and real calcium imaging data from the mouse somatosensory cortex. They show that their method can accurately predict correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs, even when the stimulus drive is weak. They also demonstrate that their method can predict the activity of neurons in the unobserved subset, given the activity of the observed subset.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the methods and results. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the limitations of their method and potential avenues for future work. The use of simulated and real data to demonstrate the effectiveness of the method is also a strength of the paper.

One potential weakness of the paper is that the authors do not provide a detailed comparison of their method to other existing methods for inferring neural population dynamics. While they mention several related studies, they do not provide a direct comparison of their method to these studies. This would be useful for understanding the strengths and weaknesses of the method in relation to other approaches.

Another potential weakness is that the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the robustness of their method to different types of noise or errors in the data. While they mention that their method is robust to some types of noise, they do not provide a systematic analysis of the effects of different types of noise on the performance of the method.

Overall, the paper is well-written and makes a significant contribution to the field of neural population dynamics. The method proposed by the authors has the potential to be widely applicable and could be an important tool for understanding the neural basis of behavior.
