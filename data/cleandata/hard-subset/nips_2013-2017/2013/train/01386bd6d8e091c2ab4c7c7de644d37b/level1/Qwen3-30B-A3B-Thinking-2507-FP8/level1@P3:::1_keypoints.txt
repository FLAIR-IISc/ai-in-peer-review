- Core contribution: Statistically grounded method to "stitch" partial neural recordings into a unified latent dynamical model to predict noise correlations between neurons never imaged simultaneously.

- Novelty/context: Extends prior work on connectivity inference and latent dynamical systems by specifically addressing stitching non-simultaneous recordings to predict cross-session noise correlations.

- Strength — theoretical foundation: Frames the problem as a latent dynamical system with missing observations, providing solid statistical grounding and near–upper-bound recovery in simulations.

- Strength — validation: Thorough validation on synthetic and real (anesthetized and awake mouse) datasets, with comparisons to naive baselines and fully observed upper bounds.

- Strength — experimental demonstration: Empirical results show strong prediction of non-simultaneous noise correlations (e.g., 0.84 vs 0.15 for naive).

- Strength — practical significance: Addresses a key experimental limitation in imaging by enabling analysis of larger circuits than can be imaged simultaneously.

- Weakness — model simplification: Use of linear Gaussian dynamical systems oversimplifies inherently nonlinear, discrete neural and calcium dynamics and lacks analysis of failure modes.

- Weakness — overlap requirement: Paper notes overlap is critical but fails to quantify minimum overlap or performance scaling with overlap percentage.

- Weakness — stationarity assumption: Assumes dynamics are stationary across sessions without discussing tests for or consequences of stationarity violations, especially in awake recordings.

- Weakness — scalability concerns: Does not analyze computational complexity or feasibility of EM+Kalman smoothing for very large populations (thousands of neurons).

- Recommendation: High-quality, impactful paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address scalability and overlap analysis.

- Pro-acceptance arguments: Novel solution to a known experimental limitation; rigorous synthetic and real-data validation; sound theoretical framing; directly relevant to experimental neuroscience.

- Con-acceptance arguments: Simplistic linear-Gaussian model may miss complex dynamics; stationarity assumption may limit applicability; limited discussion of computational scalability.
