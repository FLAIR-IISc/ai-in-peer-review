- Problem: Addresses stitching multiple partial neural recordings to infer full population dynamics, motivated by limits of 2-photon calcium imaging.  
- Method: Frames problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations, using a linear Gaussian dynamical system for tractability.  
- Evaluation: Validated on simulated data and real mouse somatosensory calcium recordings.  
- Performance: Accurately predicts noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs and substantially outperforms naive baselines.  
- Theoretical grounding: Builds on relevant prior work reconstructing dynamical systems from partial observations.  
- Validation quality: Thorough comparisons to meaningful baselines (including fully observed) provide strong empirical evidence.  
- Practical utility: Demonstrated applicability to awake and anesthetized mouse data, expanding feasible population sizes for analysis.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Clearly written with sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Strengths: Novel application of latent dynamical systems, strong empirical validation, and clear experimental relevance.  
- Limitations: Limited exploration of theoretical limits (e.g., minimal overlap required) and insufficient analysis of when the linear approximation fails for highly nonlinear dynamics.  
- Scalability concern: Paper does not fully address scaling the method to many (>2) recording sessions.  
- Overall judgment: Technically sound, original, significant contribution; strong recommendation for acceptance.
