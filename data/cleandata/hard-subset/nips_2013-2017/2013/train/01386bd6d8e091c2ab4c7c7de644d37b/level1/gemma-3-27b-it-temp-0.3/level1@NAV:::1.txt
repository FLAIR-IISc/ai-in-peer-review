## Review of "Inferring neural population dynamics from multiple partial recordings of the same neural circuit"

This paper addresses the important problem of extending the scope of neural population dynamics characterization beyond the limits of simultaneous recording capabilities. The authors propose a statistical method – framing the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations – to “stitch” together sequentially imaged sets of neurons, effectively increasing the population size that can be analyzed. This is particularly relevant for cortical circuits where the complete population size can be in the thousands, exceeding current imaging technology. The approach is grounded in established statistical methods (linear Gaussian dynamical systems and EM algorithm) and motivated by the strong coupling expected between nearby neurons.

The paper clearly explains the motivation and approach. The authors effectively relate their work to existing literature, acknowledging prior efforts in functional connectivity inference, handling subsampled observations, and latent variable modeling. They appropriately highlight how their method differs by specifically addressing the inference of connections *between* non-simultaneously recorded neurons, a gap in the existing literature. The method is well-described, and the inclusion of details regarding simulated and experimental data, as well as the quantification of performance, strengthens the paper. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The approach of stitching together sequential recordings to infer dynamics beyond simultaneous observation is a valuable contribution.
* **Sound Methodology:** Utilizing a latent dynamical system framework and the EM algorithm provides a statistically principled approach.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically organized, making the method accessible.
* **Validation:** Demonstrating the method’s effectiveness on both simulated and real (mouse somatosensory cortex) data is compelling. The comparison to upper and lower bound performance metrics is particularly insightful.
* **Significance:** The ability to infer correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons has the potential to significantly advance our understanding of neural circuit function.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Linearity Assumption:** The reliance on a linear Gaussian dynamical system is a limitation. While acknowledged, the impact of this simplification on the accuracy of inferred dynamics in more complex, non-linear systems is not fully explored.
* **Scalability:** While the authors mention scalability, a more detailed discussion of computational complexity and potential bottlenecks for very large populations would be beneficial.
* **Overlap Requirement:** The finding that some overlap between recording sessions is critical for performance raises questions about optimal experimental design. Further investigation into the relationship between overlap and accuracy would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant limitation in current neural recording techniques.
* Presents a novel and statistically sound method.
* Demonstrates promising results on both simulated and real data.
* Clearly written and well-motivated.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The linearity assumption may limit the applicability of the method to certain neural systems.
* Further investigation into scalability and optimal experimental design is needed.



**Overall:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field. While the linearity assumption is a limitation, the method is well-motivated, clearly presented, and demonstrates promising results. The potential impact of this work on our ability to study large-scale neural circuits is significant. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised regarding scalability and the impact of the linearity assumption.
