- Proposes stitching partial recordings by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations, assuming strong local coupling.
- Demonstrates method on simulations and mouse somatosensory cortex, predicting noise correlations for non-simultaneously recorded pairs.
- Addresses an important, high-impact problem given limits of simultaneous 2-photon imaging and large-scale circuit computations.
- Framing as a latent dynamical system is natural, well-motivated, and mathematically sound.
- Results are convincing; predicting noise correlations in real data is a significant achievement.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and positions itself well relative to related work.
- Inclusion of both simulated and real data increases confidence in generalizability.
- Authors thoughtfully acknowledge limitations and outline future directions.
- Use of a linear Gaussian dynamical system is a simplifying assumption; effects of nonlinearity and non-Gaussianity need discussion.
- Simulation parameters (e.g., coupling, stimulus drive) are somewhat arbitrary; lacks systematic parameter sweep/sensitivity analysis.
- Experimental stimulus protocol is insufficiently described and needs more detail.
- The fictional imaging scenario benchmark is clever but somewhat artificial; robustness to deviations (e.g., overlap degree) is unclear.
- Comparison only to a basic "naive" baseline is weak; more sophisticated baselines are needed.
- Mention of sparsifying penalties is brief; include comparisons with and without such penalties.
- Code is not available, which limits reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions (discuss nonlinearities, expand stimulus details, and ideally provide code).
