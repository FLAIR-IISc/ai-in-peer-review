- Presents a novel statistical method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neural populations by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations to infer joint population dynamics.
- Validated on simulated and real mouse somatosensory calcium imaging data, showing accurate reconstruction of joint dynamics and prediction of noise correlations.
- Scientific contribution: addresses the experimental limitation of non-simultaneous imaging and enables inference of large-scale circuit dynamics.
- Technical rigor: builds on linear dynamical systems and EM estimation, with theoretical intuition and empirical validation.
- Experimental validation: outperforms a naive baseline and is benchmarked against fully observed and naive models for contextualized performance.
- Potential impact: could broaden population-level analyses to circuits spanning thousands of neurons.
- Weakness—model assumptions: reliance on a linear Gaussian LDS may limit applicability to nonlinear or non-Gaussian neural dynamics.
- Weakness—dependence on overlap: method requires some overlap between sequential recordings; effects of varying overlap are not thoroughly explored.
- Weakness—scalability: EM computational complexity and scalability to thousands of neurons are not fully evaluated.
- Weakness—comparison to related work: discussion and comparison to alternative approaches (e.g., latent GLMs, sub-sampling methods) could be more detailed.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant problem, provides a novel and computationally efficient solution, and is robustly validated on real and synthetic data.
- Arguments against acceptance: linear-Gaussian assumption, untested scalability to very large populations, and limited treatment of overlap and related methods.
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address model generalization, scalability, and comparative discussion.
