- Presents a novel method to combine multiple partial neural recordings into a single coherent model.  
- Models population dynamics as a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Uses a linear Gaussian dynamical system (LDS) fit with an expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments show the method is technically sound.  
- Simulations recover the true coupling matrix, including off-diagonal blocks for non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.  
- Accurately predicts noise correlations between neurons not recorded simultaneously.  
- Applied to two real calcium-imaging datasets from mouse somatosensory cortex and performs well across sessions.  
- Outperforms a naive baseline that fits separate session-specific dynamical models.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains methods and results.  
- Addresses an important and broadly applicable neuroscience problem with potential for new insights.  
- Main limitation: reliance on a linear Gaussian dynamical system, which may not capture complex neural dynamics.  
- Lacks a thorough discussion of applicability limits across different neural data types.  
- Needs more detailed comparison to other existing methods for partial-recording inference.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address limitations and expand method comparisons.
