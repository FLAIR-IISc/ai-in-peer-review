- Addresses inability to record all neurons simultaneously by stitching partial recordings via a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Key insight: overlapping subsets across sessions allow inference of full-population dynamics and noise correlations for neurons never recorded together.  
- Demonstrated on simulated and real calcium imaging data, substantially outperforming naive approaches and approaching a fully observed model.  
- Distinct from prior subsampling methods by leveraging temporal dynamics rather than assuming monotonic stimulus–noise relationships.  
- Technically sound with clear mathematical formulation, replicable implementation, and comprehensive empirical validation.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations including the need for substantial session overlap (~60%) and potential parameter degeneracy in larger systems.  
- Linear Gaussian model provides useful statistical description but may be too simplistic for complex neural dynamics.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and includes clear, informative figures and experimental details.  
- Novel contribution: applying latent dynamical systems to combine non-simultaneous recordings of the same circuit.  
- Significant practical impact: enables study of much larger neural populations and prediction of noise correlations between unobserved neuron pairs.  
- Strengths: well-motivated problem, theoretical and empirical rigor, clear improvements over naive baselines, and actionable guidance for experiments.  
- Weaknesses: model simplicity, overlap requirement may limit applicability, unclear scalability to thousands of neurons, limited discussion of alternative missing-data methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept — meets criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
