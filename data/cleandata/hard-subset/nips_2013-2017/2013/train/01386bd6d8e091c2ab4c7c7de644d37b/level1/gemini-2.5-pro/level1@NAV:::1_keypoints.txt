- Presents a novel statistical method to combine non-simultaneous neuronal recordings into a single latent dynamical system by treating missing neurons as unobserved latents.  
- Builds on dynamical systems theory (e.g., Takens) but differs from prior work by not requiring simultaneous recordings or strong correlation assumptions.  
- Demonstrates the "stitching" method on simulated data with ground truth and on 2-photon calcium imaging, showing recovery of couplings and correlations.  
- Significance & originality: addresses a fundamental experimental limitation and enables modeling of larger neural circuits than current recordings allow.  
- Technical quality: sound methodology with rigorous evaluation, including ground-truth simulations and a realistic "fictional imaging" benchmark against upper and lower bounds.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivates and explains the approach and results.  
- Weakness: relies on a linear Gaussian model; needs discussion of potential failure modes under strong nonlinear neural dynamics.  
- Weakness: method requires some overlap between recorded neuronal subsets; a systematic analysis of performance as overlap decreases would be valuable.  
- Recommendation: strong, elegant, and well-validated contribution with minor weaknesses that are avenues for future work, supporting acceptance.
