- Problem & approach: Fit a single linear-Gaussian dynamical system across multiple partially overlapping sessions using known selection matrices and EM to “stitch” recordings and infer cross-session couplings and noise correlations.

- Synthetic & real experiments: Synthetic data shows good recovery of couplings; fictional splits of real 2-photon data show stitched model predicts cross-session correlations close to a fully observed upper bound, with overlap important.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on connectivity inference, latent-variable GLMs, and subsampled connectivity work; novelty is joint LDS fitting to stitch non-simultaneous recordings rather than per-session models or stimulus-based inference.

- Technical soundness: Modeling and EM/Kalman smoothing inference are appropriate; stationarity via Lyapunov is reasonable but stability constraints on A and diagonal Q/R assumptions are not fully addressed.

- Empirical support: Controlled synthetic and real-data (fictional-split) experiments support claims, though real non-simultaneous-session validation is missing.

- Limitations: No evaluation on truly separate sessions (drifts/registration errors), diagonal Q/R and simple observation model may be restrictive, and baseline comparisons are limited.

- Clarity: Clear mathematical description and experimental setup; minor typos and unspecified details (noise term, initialization, exact overlap effects).

- Originality: Simple but novel application of classical system-identification ideas to stitch multiple imaging sessions and infer cross-session couplings.

- Significance: Potentially expands inference from imaging by estimating correlations between neurons never recorded together, but practical impact is tempered by missing real-session validation and stronger baselines.

- Strengths: Clear formulation, correct and efficient inference, strong synthetic validation, promising controlled real-data results, and extensibility to richer priors/models.

- Weaknesses: Validation restricted to fictional splits, restrictive noise/observation assumptions, weak baseline comparisons, limited analysis of overlap/session length/scaling, and degraded coupling estimates with larger populations.

- Suggestions: Test on real separate sessions with cell registration, add low-rank/shared-latent and correlated-innovation baselines, systematically study overlap/session duration/regularization and stability constraints, incorporate realistic calcium/spike observation models, and report computational costs.

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a principled, simple method and convincing controlled results; broadly applicable and extensible.

- Arguments against acceptance: Lack of validation on truly non-simultaneous data, restrictive modeling assumptions, and insufficient analysis of overlap/identifiability and stronger baselines limit confidence in real-world impact.
