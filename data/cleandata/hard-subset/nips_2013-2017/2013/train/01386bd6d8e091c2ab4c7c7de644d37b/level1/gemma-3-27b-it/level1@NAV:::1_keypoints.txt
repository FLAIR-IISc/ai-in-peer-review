- Problem: inferring dynamics of large neural circuits from multiple partial sequential recordings.  
- Method: proposes a statistical “stitching” approach by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Benefit: enables estimating correlations between neurons never simultaneously recorded, effectively expanding observable population size.  
- Validation: demonstrated on simulated data and real mouse somatosensory cortex calcium imaging, showing promising noise-correlation predictions.  
- Novelty: extends prior work by handling neurons that are never simultaneously observed—a scenario not addressed before.  
- Soundness: grounded in latent dynamical systems and rigorously tested with simulations.  
- Significance: can improve understanding of neural circuit function and test computational theories by scaling inferred populations.  
- Clarity: well-written with thorough descriptions of simulations and experimental data.  
- Weakness — model simplicity: relies on a linear Gaussian dynamical system; potential effects of nonlinearity/non-Gaussianity are not fully explored.  
- Weakness — overlap requirement: method requires substantial overlap between recorded subsets, limiting applicability in some experimental designs.  
- Weakness — parameter identifiability: concerns remain about identifiability and local optima in complex real-world scenarios.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem with a novel, theoretically sound method and convincing validations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong modeling assumptions and overlap requirement may limit practical utility.  
- Overall recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance, noting acknowledged limitations do not outweigh the contribution.
