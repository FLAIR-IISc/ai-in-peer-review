- Contribution: Introduces a novel statistical "stitching" method to combine sequentially imaged neuron sets into a single model to recover joint population dynamics beyond simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Model: Proposes a latent linear Gaussian dynamical system with an EM algorithm to estimate joint dynamics from partial observations.
- Evaluation: Demonstrates effectiveness on simulated data and real mouse somatosensory cortex calcium imaging, accurately predicting correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.
- Writing and organization: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough introduction and related-work context.
- Strength — novelty: Method addresses a significant limitation of current imaging techniques by enabling larger-scale population analyses.
- Strength — thoroughness: Comprehensive evaluation on both simulated and real data strengthens the empirical claims.
- Strength — accessibility: Clear and concise presentation makes the work accessible to a broad audience.
- Strength — impact: Potential to enable characterization of dynamics in larger neural circuits and new insights into neural computation.
- Weakness — model assumption: Relies on a linear Gaussian dynamical system, which may not capture complex nonlinear neural dynamics.
- Weakness — experimental requirement: Requires some overlap between sequential populations, which may be infeasible in some experimental designs.
- Weakness — overfitting risk: Potential for over-fitting, especially with very large neuron populations.
- Recommendation: Reviewer favors acceptance, citing strengths outweigh weaknesses, and suggests minor revisions to address model assumptions and limitations.
