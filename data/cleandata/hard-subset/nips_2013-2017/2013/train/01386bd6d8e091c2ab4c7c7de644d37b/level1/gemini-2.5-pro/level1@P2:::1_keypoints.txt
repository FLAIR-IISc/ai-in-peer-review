- Problem & approach: Stitch multiple partial, overlapping neural recordings by treating unobserved neurons as missing data and fitting a single latent dynamical system.  
- Model: Uses a linear Gaussian dynamical system (A, B, Q, R) fitted with EM; Kalman smoothing handles missing observations.  
- Key contribution: Recovers the full coupling matrix A, including interactions between neurons never recorded simultaneously, enabling prediction of noise correlations.  
- Theoretical basis: Builds on system-identification results and explicitly handles non-simultaneous recordings unlike prior connectivity methods.  
- Scalability: Presented as more scalable for large populations than latent GLM approaches.  
- Simulated validation: Recovers ground-truth dynamics and off-diagonal blocks far better than naive methods.  
- Real-data validation: Uses a “fictional imaging” scenario (split fully-observed data) to benchmark against clear upper/lower bounds, showing strong performance.  
- Strength — validation strategy: Combined simulated and clever real-data benchmarks provide convincing support for claims.  
- Weakness — model simplicity: Linear-Gaussian assumptions ignore spike non-linearities and non-Gaussian calcium observations; potential failure modes need more discussion.  
- Weakness — overlap dependence: States overlap is critical but lacks systematic analysis of performance vs. session overlap.  
- Clarity: Exceptionally clear writing, concise methods, and informative figures.  
- Originality: Method is an existing algorithmic tool applied in a novel, impactful way to a new neuroscience problem.  
- Significance: High potential impact for building large-scale functional circuit models and enabling new experiments/analyses.  
- Recommendation: Strengths outweigh weaknesses; strongly recommended for acceptance.
