- Proposes a statistical "stitching" method using a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system (LDS) with missing observations to combine sequentially imaged neurons into one population model.  
- LDS models capture correlation structure and stimulus selectivity from partial observations.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness on simulated data and real mouse somatosensory calcium imaging.  
- Accurately predicts correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs, even with weak stimulus drive.  
- Can predict activity of unobserved neurons from observed subset activity.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Authors provide a thorough discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Strength: validation on both simulated and real data supports method utility.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed, direct comparisons to existing methods for inferring neural population dynamics.  
- Weakness: limited systematic analysis of robustness to different types of noise or data errors.  
- Overall評価: technically sound, novel extension of prior latent-dynamics approaches, significant and widely applicable (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include more detailed method comparisons and systematic noise-robustness analyses.
