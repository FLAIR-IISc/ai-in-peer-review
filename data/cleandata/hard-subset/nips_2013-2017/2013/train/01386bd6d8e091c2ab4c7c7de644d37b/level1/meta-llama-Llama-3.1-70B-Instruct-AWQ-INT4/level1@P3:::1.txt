**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper introduces a statistical method for "stitching" together sequentially imaged sets of neurons into one model, allowing for the characterization of population dynamics beyond the number of simultaneously imaged neurons. The method is based on a latent dynamical system with missing observations and is demonstrated using recordings in mouse somatosensory cortex. The authors show that their method can predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs, which is a crucial aspect of understanding neural computation.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous research in inferring functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging data and electrophysiological measurements. However, unlike previous studies, this work focuses on inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons. The authors also mention related work on dealing with sub-sampled observations of neural activity or connectivity, but highlight the limitations of these approaches in addressing their specific problem.
