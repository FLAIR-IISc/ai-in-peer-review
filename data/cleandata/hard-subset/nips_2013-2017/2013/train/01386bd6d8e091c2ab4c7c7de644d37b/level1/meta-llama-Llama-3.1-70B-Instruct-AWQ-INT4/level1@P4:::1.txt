**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper introduces a statistical method for "stitching" together sequentially imaged sets of neurons into one model, allowing for the characterization of population dynamics beyond the number of simultaneously imaged neurons. The authors model the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations and demonstrate its effectiveness on both simulated and real calcium imaging data from the mouse somatosensory cortex. The method enables the prediction of noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs, which is not possible with conventional approaches.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The authors build upon previous work on inferring functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging data and electrophysiological measurements. However, their approach differs from existing methods in that it attempts to infer functional connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons. The authors also reference recent studies on statistical methods for dealing with sub-sampled observations of neural activity or connectivity, but note that these approaches are not applicable to their problem.
