**Summary and Relation to Previous Work**

The paper "Inferring neural population dynamics from multiple partial recordings of the same neural circuit" presents a novel statistical method for combining multiple partial recordings of neural activity into a single model, allowing for the inference of population dynamics and interactions between neurons. This work builds upon previous studies that have addressed the question of inferring functional connectivity from 2-photon imaging data or electrophysiological measurements. However, unlike previous approaches, this method focuses on inferring functional connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-supported theoretical framework for their method. The use of a linear Gaussian dynamical system model is well-justified, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in predicting correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons. The experimental results, both on simulated and real data, are convincing and demonstrate the potential of the method.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the problem and the proposed solution, and the methods section is detailed and well-organized. The use of figures and tables is effective in illustrating the results and supporting the text.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel approach to inferring neural population dynamics from multiple partial recordings. While previous studies have addressed similar questions, the focus on non-simultaneously observed neurons is a significant contribution. The method is also original in its use of a linear Gaussian dynamical system model to combine multiple recordings.

**Significance**

The results presented in the paper have significant implications for the field of neuroscience, particularly in the context of understanding neural population dynamics and interactions. The ability to infer functional connections between non-simultaneously observed neurons opens up new possibilities for analyzing and understanding neural circuits. The method has the potential to be widely applicable and could lead to new insights into the neural basis of behavior and cognition.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

**Pro:**

* The paper presents a novel and original approach to inferring neural population dynamics from multiple partial recordings.
* The method is technically sound and well-supported by theoretical and experimental results.
* The paper has significant implications for the field of neuroscience and could lead to new insights into the neural basis of behavior and cognition.

**Con:**

* The method relies on a relatively simple linear Gaussian dynamical system model, which may not capture the full complexity of neural population dynamics.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential biases of the method.
* The experimental results, while convincing, are based on a limited number of datasets and may not be representative of all possible applications.

**Recommendation**

I recommend accepting the paper for publication, subject to minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above. The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of neuroscience and has the potential to be widely applicable.
