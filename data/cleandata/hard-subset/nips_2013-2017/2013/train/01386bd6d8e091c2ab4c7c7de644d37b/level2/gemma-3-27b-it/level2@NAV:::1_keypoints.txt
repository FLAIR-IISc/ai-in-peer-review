- Tackles the important problem of stitching sequential neural recordings by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Addresses a key bottleneck in population neuroscience: extending analysis beyond simultaneous-recording limits.  
- Proposes a statistically principled method using a linear Gaussian dynamical system to combine partial recordings.  
- Method is tractable and well-motivated, with a clear problem statement and motivation.  
- Simulations show the method can recover underlying coupling matrices and noise correlations for non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Real-data application (mouse somatosensory cortex) predicts correlations close to a fully observed benchmark and outperforms naive approaches.  
- Major limitation: reliance on the linear Gaussian model reduces applicability to systems with strong nonlinear dynamics.  
- Insufficient exploration of sensitivity to the amount of overlap between imaging sessions.  
- Comparison to existing methods ([12–15]) is cursory and needs a more detailed explanation of their shortcomings here.  
- Reproducibility concern: code is not publicly available.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant challenge, offers a principled approach, and shows strong simulated and real-data performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: simplifying model assumptions, limited analysis of those assumptions, lack of code, and shallow comparison to prior work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add deeper discussion of model assumptions and improve reproducibility by releasing code.
