- Addresses inferring neural population dynamics from partial sequential recordings by framing it as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Well-motivated: overcomes 2-photon imaging limits to study larger circuits.  
- Proposes a statistical "stitching" method that predicts noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.  
- Builds on existing latent dynamical system work and is computationally tractable.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on both simulated data (controlled evaluation) and real mouse somatosensory cortex data (biological relevance).  
- Clear explanation of method and useful comparisons to a naive approach and a fully observed upper bound.  
- Code is available, supporting reproducibility.  
- Related work discussion is superficial; should compare to state-space methods (e.g., Kalman/particle filtering) and recent scalable latent GLMs.  
- Use of a linear Gaussian dynamical system is a simplifying assumption; authors should discuss its impact and sensitivity and consider more complex models.  
- Experimental data and pre-processing lack sufficient detail (imaging parameters, ROI definition, filtering rationale—e.g., 1s high-pass filter).  
- Heavy reliance on correlation coefficients; should include additional metrics like explained variance or prediction error.  
- Reported near-degeneracy of parameters for larger populations is abrupt; needs expansion on practical implications and mitigation strategies.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with compelling results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the above points.
