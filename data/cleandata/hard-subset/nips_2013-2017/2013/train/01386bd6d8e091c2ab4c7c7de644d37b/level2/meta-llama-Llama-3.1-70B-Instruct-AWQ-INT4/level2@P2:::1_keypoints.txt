- Proposes a statistical latent dynamical system method to "stitch" multiple partial sequential recordings into a single model.  
- Demonstrates method on simulated data and mouse somatosensory cortex experiments, showing effectiveness.  
- Paper is technically sound with solid theoretical analysis and supporting experimental results.  
- Provides clear mathematical formulation and an expectation-maximization algorithm, with well-presented figures and tables.  
- Does not analyze limitations in detail (effects of population size, number of sessions, or noise levels).  
- Comparison is limited—only a “naive” per-session baseline is thoroughly addressed, not broader existing methods.  
- Well-written and easy to follow, but assumes substantial neuroscience and machine-learning background.  
- Lacks detailed introductory explanations of latent dynamical systems and the EM algorithm for non-expert readers.  
- Method is novel and distinct from approaches requiring simultaneous full-population recordings.  
- Includes a useful comparison to a naive approach that fits sessions separately then combines parameters.  
- Significant potential impact for neuroscience and analysis of large-scale imaging data (e.g., 2-photon calcium imaging).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, stitching capability, demonstrated advantages over naive baseline, and scientific significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing limitation analysis, high assumed background knowledge, and insufficient comparisons to other methods.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze limitations (population size, sessions, noise), provide clearer model/EM explanations, and compare with other existing inference methods.
