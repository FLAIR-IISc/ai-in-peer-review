- Novel statistical method for “stitching” non-simultaneous neural recordings to recover joint dynamics and noise correlations.  
- Frames problem as latent dynamical system with missing observations and fits parameters via EM.  
- Demonstrated strong empirical performance on synthetic and real mouse somatosensory calcium imaging data.  
- Outperforms naive approaches and approaches fully observed-model performance.  
- Significant contribution enabling analysis of larger neural populations despite imaging limits.  
- Method is technically sound, grounded in linear dynamical systems with rigorous validation.  
- Real-world applicability shown by predicting noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear synthetic and real-data analyses.  
- Limitation: linear Gaussian model may oversimplify inherently nonlinear neural dynamics.  
- Limitation: method depends on overlap between recorded populations, which may be infeasible experimentally.  
- Limitation: scalability to thousands of neurons is untested despite claims of analyzing large circuits.  
- Limitation: comparisons are limited to a naive baseline; lacks evaluation against other advanced missing-data methods.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for more discussion of scalability and experimental-design trade-offs.
