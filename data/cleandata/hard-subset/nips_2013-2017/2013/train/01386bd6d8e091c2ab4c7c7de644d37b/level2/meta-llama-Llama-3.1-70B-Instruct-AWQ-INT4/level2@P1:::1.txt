This paper presents a statistical method for "stitching" together sequentially imaged sets of neurons into one model by phrasing the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations. The method allows for the expansion of population-sizes for which population dynamics can be characterized beyond the number of simultaneously imaged neurons. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method using recordings in mouse somatosensory cortex, showing that it is possible to predict noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.
