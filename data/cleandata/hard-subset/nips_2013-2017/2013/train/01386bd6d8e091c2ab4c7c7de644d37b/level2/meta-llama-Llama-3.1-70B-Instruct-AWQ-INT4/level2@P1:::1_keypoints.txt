- Proposes a statistical "stitching" method framing sequential imaging as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Enables characterization of population dynamics beyond the number of simultaneously imaged neurons.  
- Demonstrated on mouse somatosensory cortex data, successfully predicting noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with clear explanations of methods and results.  
- Provides thorough background, motivation, and a detailed, well-explained methods section.  
- Evaluation includes both simulated and real data, showing convincing empirical performance.  
- Strengths: novel method, potential high impact, and thorough empirical demonstration.  
- Results are impressive in accurately predicting correlations across non-simultaneous recordings.  
- Weakness: uses a simple linear Gaussian dynamical system that may not capture complex neural dynamics.  
- Authors acknowledge model simplicity and argue it is a useful starting point.  
- Weakness: limited evaluation, lacking direct comparisons to other methods (including the "naive" approach).  
- Would benefit from comparisons to alternative approaches and a deeper discussion of limitations.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful method with thorough evaluation and strong results.  
- Arguments for rejection: model simplicity, limited evaluation/direct comparisons, and need for more discussion of weaknesses.
