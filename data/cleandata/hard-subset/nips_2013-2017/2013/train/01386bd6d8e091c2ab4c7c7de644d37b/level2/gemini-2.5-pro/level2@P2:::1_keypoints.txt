- Novel method frames stitching non-simultaneous neural recordings as inference in a latent linear dynamical system (LDS) fit with EM to combine partial noisy observations.  
- Addresses an important experimental bottleneck (limited field-of-view) and is the first practical solution to combine non-simultaneous recordings into one dynamical model.  
- Thorough and convincing validation on simulated and real data, including a clever "fictional imaging" split to create a ground-truth upper bound.  
- Strong empirical results: accurately predicts noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons, approaching the performance of a model with access to all data.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and explains the method and motivation effectively.  
- Limitation: LDS (linear-Gaussian) is a simplification of true nonlinear, non-Gaussian neural dynamics; applicability to high-temporal-resolution spiking data is unclear.  
- Crucial dependence on overlapping neurons: method fails without shared "anchor" neurons; needs systematic analysis of performance vs. overlap fraction for experimental guidance.  
- Scalability concern: Kalman smoother in E-step is O(N^3), so practical limits for thousands of neurons are unclear and should be discussed.  
- Parameter recovery vs. correlation recovery divergence: model recovers pairwise correlations well but estimates of coupling matrix A degrade with size, suggesting near-degeneracies worth discussing.  
- Overall assessment: novel, well-validated, practically useful method with clear avenues for future extensions and clarifications; strong recommendation for acceptance.
