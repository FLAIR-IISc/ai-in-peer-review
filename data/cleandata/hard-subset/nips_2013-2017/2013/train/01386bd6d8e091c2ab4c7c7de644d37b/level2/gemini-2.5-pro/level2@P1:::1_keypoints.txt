- Paper proposes an LDS-based "stitching" method to infer joint dynamics and functional connectivity from sequential partial neural recordings using EM.
- Significance/Novelty: Addresses a practical bottleneck in neural recording by computationally expanding effective population size; novel application of LDS to non-simultaneous recordings.
- Methodological Soundness: Uses well-founded LDS and EM techniques, appropriately adapted for structured missing data across sessions.
- Evaluation: Thorough validation on simulated data (recovers coupling and noise correlations) and a clever real-data "fictional imaging" test comparing to upper/lower bounds.
- Clarity: Well-written, organized, and provides helpful intuition for identifiability across sessions.
- Dependence on Overlap: Performance relied on neuron overlap between sessions in experiments; needs analysis of whether overlap is fundamentally required or an optimization artifact.
- Linear-Gaussian Limitations: LDS simplification may fail for strongly nonlinear or bursting neural dynamics; discuss potential failure modes.
- Identifiability Issues: Coupling parameter estimates degrade with population size, suggesting near-degeneracy where different connectivity matrices yield similar correlations; formal identifiability conditions are needed.
- Scalability: Lacks discussion of computational scaling with neuron count, session number, and session duration; practical runtime limits should be addressed.
- Overall: High-quality, principled, and well-validated work of broad interest and a strong candidate for acceptance, with suggested clarifications to strengthen practical utility.
