- Problem: inferring neural population dynamics from multiple sequentially recorded subsets due to limited simultaneous recordings.  
- Method: fit a latent dynamical system with missing observations to “stitch” partial recordings and predict correlations between neurons never observed together.  
- Motivation and writing: compelling motivation and clearly written presentation.  
- Model choice: uses a linear Gaussian dynamical system for tractability, acknowledged as a simplification.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on state-space modeling and distinguishes itself by predicting correlations for non-simultaneously observed neurons.  
- Experiments: simulated and real calcium imaging experiments are well-designed and convincingly show gains over a naive approach; comparison to a fully observed model is a useful benchmark.  
- Strength: experimental validation on mouse somatosensory calcium imaging is a significant asset.  
- Limitation: need for discussion on extending to nonlinear models and justification for why the linear model performs well despite its simplicity.  
- Reproducibility: experimental details (imaging setup like laser power/objective, preprocessing such as denoising and drift correction) are insufficiently specified.  
- Fictional scenario clarity: unclear construction and justification of the “fictional imaging scenario” and the choice of 60% overlap.  
- Naive baseline claim: the claim that the naive approach overestimates correlations due to averaging couplings requires deeper explanation.  
- Robustness/failure modes: lacks analysis of how performance degrades with stronger temporal correlations or under model misspecification.  
- Acceptance arguments: important problem, novel and practical method, convincing real-data results, and clear exposition.  
- Rejection arguments: heavy reliance on linear Gaussian model, incomplete experimental details, and insufficient exploration of limitations and failure modes.  
- Overall: strong, impactful contribution that warrants acceptance if the minor concerns (model extensions, experimental detail, robustness analysis) are addressed.
