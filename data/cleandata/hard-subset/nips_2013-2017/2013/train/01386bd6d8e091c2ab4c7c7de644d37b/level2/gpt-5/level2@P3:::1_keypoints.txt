- Paper proposes a “stitching” framework that models multiple partially overlapping recordings as a single latent linear dynamical system (LDS) with missing observations.  
- Parameters are learned with EM (including an online variant) using known cell correspondences across sessions.  
- On simulations and two 2-photon datasets, the method predicts noise correlations for non-simultaneous neuron pairs far better than per-session LDS baselines and near a “fully observed” upper bound.  
- Builds on prior latent-variable and system-identification work; novelty lies in integrating partially overlapping sessions to recover cross-session correlations.  
- LDS-with-missing-observations formulation is principled and tractable; M-step and identifiability intuition are reasonable though heuristic.  
- Empirical results show recovery of cross-couplings in simulation and reliable cross-session correlation prediction on real data.  
- Validation limitation: real-data comparisons use a “fully observed” model as an upper bound rather than empirical correlations from held-out simultaneous test data.  
- Model, assumptions, and fitting procedures are clearly described; notation and observation mask are helpful.  
- Minor clarity issues: typographical ambiguity in observation equation and later statement of diagonal Q and R assumptions.  
- Stitching formulation and empirical demonstration are novel and practically important for 2-photon workflows.  
- Method can expand effective population size beyond simultaneously imaged neurons and is scalable to O(10^2) in practice.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical problem in calcium imaging with a clear LDS+EM formulation and efficient implementation.  
- Strength: Strong simulation and real-data performance using meaningful metrics (correlations, cross-prediction).  
- Essential suggestion: Directly compare predicted pairwise correlations to empirical correlations from a held-out simultaneous test segment.  
- Essential suggestion: Systematically analyze performance versus overlap fraction and session count, and state identifiability conditions or cite relevant results.  
- Essential suggestion: Add stronger baselines (e.g., GPFA/FA-LDS with alignment, subspace-ID methods for missing outputs).  
- Optional suggestion: Explore regularization (sparse or structured priors) for A and report effects on recovery.  
- Optional suggestion: Investigate correlated measurement noise or calcium dynamics/deconvolution sensitivity to diagonal Q/R assumptions.  
- Optional suggestion: Assess robustness to cell mis-registration, ROI selection, and neuropil contamination.  
- Optional suggestion: Report runtime/memory scaling and test larger N and >2 sessions to support scalability claims.  
- Reproducibility suggestion: Clarify observation equation and publish code.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical, convincing simulations and real-data demos addressing a pressing neuroscience need.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of direct empirical ground-truth validation, limited baselines, and incomplete characterization of identifiability and scalability.
