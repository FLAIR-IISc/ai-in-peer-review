- Novel statistical method for "stitching" multiple partial neural recordings into a single coherent model.
- Frames the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations and predicts noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Leverages strong local coupling to combine data across sessions and infer dynamics beyond the field of view.
- Well-motivated by limits of 2-photon imaging versus larger cortical circuit sizes.
- Clearly positioned relative to prior work; uniquely addresses stitching non-simultaneous recordings.
- Effective baseline comparisons to naive parameter averaging and a fully observed upper-bound.
- Uses a linear Gaussian dynamical system with EM and Kalman smoothing for computational tractability.
- Comprehensive evaluation on simulated data and real anesthetized and awake mouse data using multiple metrics.
- Strengths: clear problem articulation, rigorous evaluation, demonstrated real-data applicability, discussion of limitations, and solid related-work integration.
- Weakness: reliance on linear Gaussian assumptions without detailed pathways to extend to nonlinear or spike-based models.
- Weakness: insufficient practical guidance on how much overlap between sessions is required for successful stitching.
- Weakness: limited analysis of robustness when core model assumptions (linear dynamics, Gaussian noise) are violated.
- Overall significance: substantial methodological contribution likely to expand population-size studies and understanding of noise correlations; recommendation for acceptance.
