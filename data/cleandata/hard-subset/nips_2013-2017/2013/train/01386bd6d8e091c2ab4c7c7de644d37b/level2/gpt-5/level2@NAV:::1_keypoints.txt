- Tackles stitching partially overlapping two-photon recordings to infer joint population dynamics.  
- Models the problem as a linear Gaussian state-space model with missing observations, trained by EM with Kalman smoothing.  
- Claims: accurate recovery of functional couplings, robust prediction of noise correlations between never-simultaneously-observed neuron pairs, and cross-prediction of unobserved activity.  
- Simulations support identifiability up to expected linear transforms.  
- Real-data tests (mouse S1, artificial splits) achieve performance near a fully observed upper bound and well above a naive baseline.  
- Novelty: explicit cross-session stitching using shared cells to resolve latent indeterminacies relative to prior PLDS/GPFA/GLM work.  
- Quality: model and estimation are technically sound and evaluations include appropriate upper/lower reference baselines.  
- Major limitation: validation uses artificial session splits, so robustness to real cross-session nonstationarities (drift, photobleaching, behavior) is untested.  
- Limitation: conditions for successful stitching (overlap size, observability/stability) are discussed qualitatively but not quantified.  
- Clarity: generally clear and reproducible at the LDS level, but initialization, regularization, and model selection details need expansion.  
- Originality: LDS with missing data is standard, but application to stitching non-simultaneous recordings and predicting cross-session noise correlations is timely and useful.  
- Significance: high practical value for imaging labs and scaling data-driven circuit models.  
- Pros: addresses a real limitation with a simple, scalable tool; shows strong empirical gains; connects to prior theory and literature.  
- Cons: lacks evaluation on truly sequential recordings; sensitivity to nonstationarity and registration errors untested; dependence on overlap not systematically characterized; limited baseline comparisons and scalability profiling.  
- Suggestions: validate on sequential/interleaved datasets and quantify robustness to drift/misalignment; systematically vary/report performance vs. overlap size and spectral radius; compare to PLDS/GPFA and spike-based observation models; report runtime/memory and release code.  
- Overall: promising contribution, reviewer leans toward acceptance contingent on strengthened real-data validation and baseline comparisons.
