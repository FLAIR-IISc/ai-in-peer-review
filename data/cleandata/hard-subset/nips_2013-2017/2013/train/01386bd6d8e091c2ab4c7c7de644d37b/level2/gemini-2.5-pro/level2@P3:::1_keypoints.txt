- Problem addressed: infer dynamics of a large neural population from multiple sequential, partially overlapping recordings to overcome inability to record all neurons simultaneously.  
- Method: model full population as latent state of a linear-Gaussian dynamical system and estimate parameters (including connectivity) via Expectation-Maximization, treating each session as a partial observation.  
- Main contribution: shows “stitching” multiple partial recordings can predict noise correlations between neurons never recorded simultaneously.  
- Validation: tested on simulated data with ground truth and on real 2-photon calcium imaging using a fictional imaging scenario for benchmarking.  
- Quality strength: technically sound, appropriate as a proof-of-concept, rigorous evaluation using ground truth and well-chosen baselines.  
- Clarity strength: clearly written, well-organized, with effective figures and sufficient methodological detail for experts.  
- Originality strength: novel application of LDS+EM to combine non-simultaneous recordings, filling a distinct gap in prior work.  
- Significance strength: high potential impact for experimental neuroscience—could enable circuit-level analyses and inform experimental design.  
- Suggestion — model assumptions: discuss limitations of linear-Gaussian assumptions and robustness to neural nonlinearity and non-Gaussian spiking.  
- Suggestion — scalability: evaluate or discuss performance when stitching many sessions (beyond two), including chained connections (A–B–C).  
- Suggestion — overlap importance: quantify how stitching performance depends on the percentage of overlapping neurons to guide experimental design.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important practical problem with a sound, novel method and convincing validation; likely high impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on a simplified linear-Gaussian model and demonstration limited to two sessions leave open questions about robustness and scalability.  
- Overall judgment: strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses; paper is novel, significant, and well-executed, with suggested analyses to further strengthen it.
