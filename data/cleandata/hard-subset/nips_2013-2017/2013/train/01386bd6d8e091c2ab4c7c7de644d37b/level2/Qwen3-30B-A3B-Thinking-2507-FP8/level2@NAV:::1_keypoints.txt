- Proposes a statistical framework to "stitch" sequential, non-overlapping neural recordings into a unified dynamical model to infer noise correlations across non-simultaneously observed neuron pairs.  
- Core claim: a latent linear dynamical system (LDS) with missing observations can reconstruct population dynamics beyond simultaneous imaging limits.  
- Demonstrated on simulated data and mouse somatosensory cortex recordings.  
- Benchmarked against a "fully observed" upper bound and a naive baseline that cannot predict non-simultaneous correlations.  
- Achieves high correlation with the upper bound: 0.92 in simulations and 0.84 in real data, versus 0.15 for the naive baseline.  
- Addresses an important neuroscience bottleneck—scaling population analysis beyond imaging limits—and builds on relevant prior literature.  
- Novelty: avoids restrictive assumptions of prior methods and scales to large populations (100+ neurons).  
- LDS choice is well-motivated for computational tractability and validated on two distinct datasets (anesthetized and awake).  
- Strengths: clear problem formulation, thorough quantitative evaluation, and thoughtful discussion of limitations.  
- Main limitation: reliance on a linear Gaussian model is a simplification; more biologically realistic models (e.g., calcium dynamics) should be tested.  
- Suggests quantifying how performance degrades with increasing population size.  
- The "fictional imaging" test is clever but needs more discussion about mapping to real experimental constraints (e.g., session-to-session variability).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, impactful, reproducible, well-written — Recommendation: Accept.
