- Tackles an important problem: inferring dynamics of larger neural populations from sequential, partial recordings.  
- Proposes a principled "stitching" approach by fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Uses a linear Gaussian latent dynamical system for tractable inference and clear conceptual demonstration.  
- Clear and well-written presentation with well-defined equations and an easy-to-follow workflow.  
- Convincing results on simulations and real mouse somatosensory calcium imaging, including prediction of noise correlations.  
- Provides useful benchmarks by comparing to naive and fully observed models.  
- Thorough ablation study that defines performance bounds and shows the method performs well within them.  
- Strong simplification: the linear Gaussian assumption may not capture realistic neural nonlinearities and its impact needs more discussion.  
- Limited exploration of alternative model complexities (e.g., nonlinear state-space models or GLMs) to test robustness of the stitching idea.  
- Lack of analysis on sensitivity to parameter choices and regularization settings.  
- Important dependence on session overlap is noted but the optimal degree of overlap is not thoroughly explored.  
- Missing citation/discussion of recent state-space methods for calcium imaging (e.g., CaImAn) for contextualization.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, and impactful work despite some modelling simplifications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a hard problem, principled solution, strong empirical demonstrations, and clear analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: applicability limited by linear Gaussian assumption and insufficient sensitivity/overlap analyses.  
- Recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to address the noted limitations.
