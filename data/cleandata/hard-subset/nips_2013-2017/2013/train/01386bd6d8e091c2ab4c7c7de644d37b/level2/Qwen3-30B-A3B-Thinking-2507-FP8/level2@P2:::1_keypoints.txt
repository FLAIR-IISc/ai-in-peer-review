- Addresses practical neuroscience limitation: stitches multiple partial neural recordings into a single dynamical model to handle non-simultaneous recordings.  
- Models problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations to predict correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons.  
- Strong empirical demonstration on simulated and real mouse somatosensory cortex data.  
- Shows stitching can recover coupling matrices and predict noise correlations nearly as well as a fully observed model.  
- Substantially outperforms a naive parameter-averaging across sessions.  
- Sound theoretical foundation: linear Gaussian dynamical system (LDS) with expectation maximization and clear model/fitting exposition.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge the simplicity of the LDS and room for more complex models.  
- Major concern: insufficient justification that a linear model is adequate given inherent neural and calcium-imaging nonlinearities.  
- Major concern: ambiguity in claiming to predict “noise correlations”—method predicts latent-system correlation structure, which may not equal biologically meaningful noise correlations.  
- Minor experimental concern: choice of 60% overlap split in the simulated “fictional imaging scenario” is unexplained and may affect results.  
- Paper could better articulate how stitching would change experimental design and trade-offs (imaging time, coverage, overlap).  
- Recommendation: temper claims about predicting noise correlations and include more analysis of model limitations and relation between latent and true noise correlations.
