- Proposes a novel statistical method that "stitches" sequential partial recordings into a single latent dynamical system to infer population dynamics.  
- Main claim: the method can accurately predict correlations between neuron pairs that were never recorded simultaneously.  
- Validation provided via simulations and experimental data from mouse somatosensory cortex.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and offers a thorough review of related work.  
- Method is technically sound with a detailed model description and justified use of EM for parameter estimation.  
- Results are strong, outperforming a naive approach and comparable to a fully observed model in key metrics.  
- Significance: enables analysis of population dynamics beyond the limits of simultaneous imaging and could broaden insights into neural systems.  
- Overall evaluation: excellent quality and a significant contribution to the field.  
- Limitation: relies on a linear Gaussian dynamical system, which may not capture complex neural dynamics.  
- Limitation: may require substantial modifications to apply to different types of neural systems or data.  
- Improvement suggestion: include a more detailed discussion of method limitations and directions for future work.
