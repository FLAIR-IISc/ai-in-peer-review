- Introduces a method to "stitch" sequentially imaged neurons into a single model by framing the problem as fitting a latent dynamical system with missing observations.  
- Core contribution: a statistical framework that predicts noise correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs by combining partial observations.  
- Models the problem as a linear Gaussian dynamical system (LDS) fit with EM, using Kalman smoothing in the E-step.  
- Enables effective expansion of analyzable population size beyond what simultaneous imaging allows.  
- Validated on simulated data (ground truth) and real mouse somatosensory cortex recordings.  
- Benchmarked against a naive average-parameters baseline and an upper-bound fully observed model.  
- Performance: stitching achieved correlation 0.92 with true noise correlations in simulation.  
- Real-data performance: stitching produced correlation 0.84 with fully observed model vs 0.15 for the naive method.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Strengths: clear problem articulation, rigorous evaluation, appropriate baselines, and strong practical significance.  
- Weaknesses: limited exploration of performance versus different overlap levels between sessions and insufficient discussion of computational scaling as population size grows.  
- Main limitation: reliance on a relatively simple LDS model; authors acknowledge this and suggest more complex models as future work.  
- Overall assessment: meaningful, novel contribution with high significance and immediate applicability to neuroscience; suitable for a strong conference submission.
