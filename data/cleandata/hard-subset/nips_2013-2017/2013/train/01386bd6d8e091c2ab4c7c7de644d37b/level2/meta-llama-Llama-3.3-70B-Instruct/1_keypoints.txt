- Proposes a statistical method to stitch sequentially imaged neuron sets into a single model to study population dynamics beyond simultaneously recorded neurons.
- Introduces a latent dynamical system that predicts correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs, demonstrated on simulated and mouse calcium imaging data.
- Main claim: the method accurately predicts correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neurons and outperforms a naive per-session fitting approach.
- Strength: novel stitching method for sequential imaging.
- Strength: validated on both simulated and real datasets.
- Strength: compared against naive and fully observed baseline models.
- Strength: authors discuss potential applications and limitations.
- Weakness: relies on a linear Gaussian dynamical system that may not capture real neural complexity.
- Weakness: requires significant overlap between populations across sessions, which may not always be feasible.
- Weakness: lacks clear discussion of computational complexity and scalability to larger datasets.
- Overall: significant and promising contribution to neuroscience, but further work needed to address limitations and demonstrate scalability.
- Pro acceptance: novel, empirically supported method with impressive results and potential field impact.
- Con acceptance: restrictive modeling assumptions, overlap requirements, and unclear scalability may limit real-world applicability.
