- Introduces a novel statistical method to infer joint dynamics of a large neural population from multiple non-simultaneous subset recordings.  
- Frames the "stitching" problem within a latent dynamical system: full population activity as latent state, each session as partial observation.  
- Uses a linear dynamical system (LDS) to estimate functional connectivity and predict noise correlations for neuron pairs never recorded simultaneously.  
- Leverages system identification and latent-variable modeling to address limited field-of-view in modern neural imaging.  
- Validation is strong: evaluates on simulated data with ground truth and a “fictional imaging” scenario on real 2-photon data.  
- Compares against fully observed (upper bound) and naive (lower bound) baselines, showing substantial improvement.  
- Paper is well-written and concepts are communicated with excellent clarity.  
- Main weakness: reliance on a linear Gaussian model may miss nonlinear/non-Gaussian neural dynamics; limitations not fully explored.  
- Practical constraint: some overlap between recorded subsets is reported as "critical," but no systematic analysis of performance degradation with decreasing overlap.  
- Overall judgment: novel, significant, rigorously evaluated work with clear experimental relevance; strong recommendation for acceptance.
