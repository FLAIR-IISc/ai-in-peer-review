- Presents a novel "stitching" statistical method to infer neural population dynamics from multiple partial, non-simultaneous recordings.
- Method fits a latent dynamical system with missing observations to combine sequentially imaged neuron sets.
- Demonstrated on simulated data and real calcium imaging from mouse somatosensory cortex.
- Technically sound with clear, well-organized presentation and reproducible methodological details.
- Claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results, with multiple evaluation metrics.
- Clearly written; figures and explanations effectively illustrate the method.
- Original application of latent dynamical systems to stitching non-simultaneous neuron recordings.
- Significant because it can accurately predict correlations between non-simultaneously recorded neuron pairs.
- Discussion includes limitations and potential extensions, useful for future work.
