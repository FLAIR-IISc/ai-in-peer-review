## Review of NIPS 2013 Submission: [Paper Title – Replace with Actual Title]

This paper presents theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm utilizing regularization via the latent Schatten norm. A key contribution is the improvement over existing bounds derived using the overlapped Schatten norm for regularization.

**Quality:** The paper delivers solid theoretical results regarding tensor decomposition with latent Schatten norm regularization. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a particularly interesting and valuable finding. However, the experimental evaluation feels somewhat cursory. While results are presented, they lack sufficient detail for a complete assessment. Specifically, the method of selecting Tucker ranks (r<sub>1</sub>, …, r<sub>k</sub>) for the test examples is not explained, hindering reproducibility and a full understanding of the results. Furthermore, the paper doesn’t address how group regularization techniques, such as l<sub>1</sub> or l<sub>∞</sub> norms, function when the tensor exhibits low rank across all modes simultaneously. A crucial omission is a comparative performance analysis against non-convex tensor decomposition methods, which would provide valuable context and demonstrate the practical benefits – or limitations – of the proposed convex approach. 

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and generally easy to follow. The mathematical notation is clear and the derivations are presented in a logical manner.

**Originality:** The primary original contribution of this work lies in the established duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This duality result appears to be of independent theoretical interest, extending beyond the specific application to tensor decomposition.

**Significance:** Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with wide-ranging applications in areas such as machine learning, signal processing, and data analysis. By improving upon existing theoretical guarantees for tensor decomposition, this paper represents a significant contribution. The theoretical results are interesting and the presentation is well-crafted. However, the brevity of the experimental section limits a full evaluation of the practical impact of the proposed method. 

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable theoretical advancement in the field of convex tensor decomposition. The duality result regarding Schatten norms is a noteworthy contribution. However, the lack of thorough experimentation – particularly the absence of comparisons to non-convex methods and detailed explanations of experimental setup – weakens the overall impact. 

**Recommendations:**

* **Expand the experimental section:** Include a more comprehensive performance comparison against state-of-the-art non-convex tensor decomposition algorithms.
* **Detail experimental setup:** Provide a clear explanation of how Tucker ranks were chosen for the examples and justify these choices. 
* **Address group regularization:** Discuss the behavior of group regularization strategies (e.g., l<sub>1</sub>, l<sub>∞</sub>) when applied to tensors with simultaneous low rank in all modes.
* **Consider limitations:** A brief discussion of the practical limitations of the proposed approach, particularly regarding scalability or sensitivity to parameter settings, would be beneficial.



I believe that addressing these points would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its potential impact on the field.
