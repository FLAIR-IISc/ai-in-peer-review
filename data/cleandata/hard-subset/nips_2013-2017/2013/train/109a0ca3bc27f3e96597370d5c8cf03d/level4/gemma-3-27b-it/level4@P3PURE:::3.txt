This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon existing results achieved with the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality:** The paper presents valuable theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly interesting. However, the experimental evaluation is somewhat brief and lacks detail. For instance, the method of selecting the Tucker ranks (r<sub>1</sub>,…r<sub>k</sub>) for the examples is not explained. Furthermore, the behavior of group regularization techniques, such as l<sub>1</sub>/l<sub>∞</sub> norms, when the tensor exhibits simultaneous low-rank structure across all modes, is not discussed. A comparison to non-convex approaches would also strengthen the evaluation.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, facilitating understanding of the presented material.

**Originality:** The main original contribution of this work is the demonstrated duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, a result that appears to be of independent interest. 

**Significance:** Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with applications across numerous fields. This paper’s improvement upon existing results in this area constitutes significant work. The theoretical findings are interesting, and the paper is well-written overall. Nonetheless, the experiments are treated rather briefly towards the end of the manuscript.
