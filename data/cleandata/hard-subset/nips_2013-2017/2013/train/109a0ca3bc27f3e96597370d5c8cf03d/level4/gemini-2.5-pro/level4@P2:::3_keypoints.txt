- Introduces a general framework for structured Schatten norms and proves a novel duality between overlapped and latent formulations.  
- Provides first rigorous denoising analysis for the latent approach, showing MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank.  
- Theoretical result explains why latent can outperform overlapped when tensor is low-rank in only one unknown mode.  
- Numerical experiments confirm the predicted scaling behavior of the latent method.  
- The duality result (Lemma 1) is elegant and connects these regularizers to structured sparsity literature.  
- Addresses an important, widely applicable problem (noisy tensor decomposition) with valuable theoretical insight.  
- Offers a clear, rigorous explanation for a previously observed empirical phenomenon favoring the latent approach.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow from definitions to performance bounds.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and feels preliminary, lacking broader method comparisons.  
- Omits comparisons to classical non-convex baselines (e.g., ALS, HOSVD), hindering assessment of practical performance.  
- Analysis and experiments focus almost exclusively on the S1/1 case, leaving other structured Schatten norms underexplored.  
- Experimental methodology (generation of random low-rank tensors and choice of multilinear ranks) needs more detail.  
- A discussion of when other norms (e.g., overlapped S1/∞) would be advantageous is missing but suggested.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant contribution with recommendation for acceptance despite empirical gaps.
