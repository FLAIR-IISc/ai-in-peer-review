This paper explores convex norm-regularized optimization approaches for factorizing approximately low-rank tensors, with a focus on the recently proposed "latent" norm approach and its comparison to the previously used "overlap" norm approach. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the "latent" norm's mean-squared-error behavior under various noise assumptions, which favorably compare to the existing analysis on the overlap norm. This supports the latent norm's better empirically observed performance in previous studies. The theory is also validated through numerical simulations. Furthermore, the paper generalizes both norms and proves a duality theorem for the generalized norm.

The paper is generally well-structured and easy to follow, appearing to be technically sound, although I did not thoroughly examine the proofs in the supplementary material. The results may be useful for researchers focusing on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, I have some concerns regarding the paper's relevance and impact.

Firstly, the results are narrow and of a technical nature, with only peripheral connections to machine learning. While the authors make a justified case that tensor factorizations can arise in machine learning, the paper does not attempt to examine the proposed approaches in the context of machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally. This limited scope may reduce the paper's potential impact at NIPS.

Secondly, the results seem somewhat incremental, and I did not find them particularly surprising. They provide additional theoretical backing to a specific recently proposed approach, which was already argued to work well empirically. The techniques used also do not appear to be novel.

A minor defect is that the upper bounds are not accompanied by lower bounds, which is potentially problematic since the upper bounds are used to compare different algorithms and argue for the superiority of one over the other.

Additional Comments:

- The abstract begins with "We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...". However, I think this is slightly misleading. The paper primarily focuses on analyzing two existing approaches (overlapped and latent), and the generalization is only briefly introduced in section 2.1 and proved in Lemma 1. No particular algorithms are proposed based on this generalized Schatten norm, nor is their behavior analyzed or tested experimentally.

- Figure 1 is presented in the introduction but is not interpretable without the background information provided in section 2 onwards.
