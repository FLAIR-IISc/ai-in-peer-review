This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon the result known for regularization using the overlapped Schatten norm.

Quality: The paper obtains theoretical results for tensor decomposition using latent Schatten norm, including the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norm, which is particularly interesting. However, the experiments are somewhat briefly considered and lack elaboration. For instance, the selection process for the Tucker ranks (r1,...rk) of the examples is not clearly explained. Additionally, the paper does not discuss how group regularization, such as l_1,\infty, performs when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes. A comparison with non-convex approaches would also be beneficial. Nevertheless, the theoretical results are a significant contribution to the field.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clear to understand, making it accessible to readers familiar with the topic.

Originality: The main original result in the paper is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which seems to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is an important problem encountered in many applications, and this paper improves upon existing results for this problem. Therefore, I believe it is significant work. The theoretical results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. While the experiments could be more detailed, the paper's contributions make it a valuable addition to the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper provides novel theoretical results for tensor decomposition using latent Schatten norm.
* The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norm is a significant contribution.
* The paper is well-organized and clear to understand.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The experiments are somewhat brief and lack elaboration.
* The paper does not discuss the selection process for the Tucker ranks (r1,...rk) of the examples.
* A comparison with non-convex approaches would be beneficial.

Overall, I believe the paper's contributions outweigh its limitations, and I recommend acceptance.
