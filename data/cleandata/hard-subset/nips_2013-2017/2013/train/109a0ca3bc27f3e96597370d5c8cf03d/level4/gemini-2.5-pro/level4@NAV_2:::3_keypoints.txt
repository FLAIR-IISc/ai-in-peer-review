- Paper provides a theoretical analysis of the latent Schatten-norm convex tensor decomposition, explaining empirical benefits observed in prior work.  
- Main contribution: rigorously shows latent approach MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, while overlapped approach depends on the average mode-k ranks.  
- Presents a novel duality result linking latent and overlapped Schatten norms, connecting to structured sparsity literature.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong, technically sound, and may be of independent interest (Lemma 1 especially).  
- Analysis explains why latent method outperforms overlapped when the tensor is low-rank in one unknown mode.  
- Empirical validation is present but brief and could be expanded to be more convincing.  
- Experimental details are sparse; data generation and rank-selection methodology need clearer description.  
- Comparisons are limited to two convex approaches and should include non-convex baselines (e.g., alternating least squares).  
- Suggests exploring other structured regularizers (e.g., overlapped S1/∞-norm) and scenarios where all modes are low-rank.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and effectively motivates and explains the results.  
- Originality: first paper to provide performance guarantees for the latent Schatten-norm approach; duality characterization is novel.  
- Significance: provides practical theoretical guidance showing latent approach adapts to unknown low-rank structure and can match an oracle that knows the lowest-rank mode.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first rigorous justification of latent approach, elegant duality result, significant theoretical bounds, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: empirical evaluation is limited and should be strengthened with more detailed experiments and broader comparisons.
