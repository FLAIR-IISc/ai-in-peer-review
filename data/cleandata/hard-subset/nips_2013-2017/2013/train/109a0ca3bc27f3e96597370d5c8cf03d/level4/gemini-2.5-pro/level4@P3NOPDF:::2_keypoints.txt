- Paper proposes a convex, overlapped Schatten-norm approach for Tucker decomposition with improved reconstruction guarantees, especially for asymmetric mode ranks.  
- Manuscript lacks clear ML motivation; recommend framing via a latent-variable model where observed moment tensors have Tucker decompositions linked to latent dimensions.  
- Emphasize advantage: method handles cases where only some latent variables are low-dimensional while others are not.  
- Theorem 2 is imprecise: component recovery is only up to permutation and scaling and this caveat should be stated.  
- The derivation from equation (12) to (13) is asserted to be immediate but needs a clear, justified explanation.  
- Proof of Lemma 5 (crucial to Theorem 2) is missing and must be included.  
- Proof of Lemma 4 should be provided explicitly in the paper rather than only citing another source to keep the work self-contained.  
- Related work/terminology: explicitly note that “mode-rank” = “multilinear rank” and acknowledge other tensor rank notions.  
- Expand related work to include HOSVD and its approximation bounds (e.g., Kolda 2009).  
- Clarify or remove the comparison of the paper’s incoherence assumption to matrix sparse+low-rank incoherence, since the notions and noise models differ.  
- Add a brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed optimization algorithm for practical relevance.  
- Notation issues: change confusing superscript \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)} for estimated components and standardize k-mode rank notation (e.g., mult_rank(T)).  
- Typos: fix “result for structures Schatten” → “structured Schatten” in abstract and add missing parenthesis on page 12 of the supplement.
