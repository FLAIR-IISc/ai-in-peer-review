This paper establishes theoretical performance guarantees for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm employing latent Schatten norm regularization, achieving an improvement over existing bounds derived using the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality:** The work presents valuable theoretical findings concerning tensor decomposition with latent Schatten norm regularization. The dual characterization of both the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a particularly noteworthy contribution. However, the experimental evaluation feels somewhat cursory and lacks detail. Specifically, the selection criteria for the Tucker ranks (r<sub>1</sub>,…r<sub>k</sub>) in the examples used would benefit from clarification. Further discussion is needed regarding the behavior of group regularization techniques (e.g., l<sub>1</sub>, l<sub>∞</sub>) when the tensor exhibits simultaneous low-rank structure across all modes. Finally, a comparative analysis against non-convex methods would strengthen the empirical validation.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and readily comprehensible.

**Originality:** The primary original contribution lies in the demonstrated duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, a result that appears to hold independent interest.

**Significance:** Given the broad applicability of tensor decomposition, and the improvement this paper offers over current state-of-the-art results for this problem, I consider this to be a significant contribution. The theoretical results are compelling and the paper is well-written; however, the experimental section could be expanded to provide a more thorough evaluation.
