- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the "latent" norm versus the "overlap" norm.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models and shows they compare favorably to overlap-norm analysis.  
- Confirms theoretical results with numerical simulations.  
- Introduces a generalization of the norms and proves a duality theorem for that generalization.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly presented, and appears technically sound (supplementary proofs not checked in detail).  
- Relevance and impact concerns: results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning.  
- No theoretical or experimental evaluation of the proposed approaches on concrete machine-learning problems, limiting potential NIPS impact.  
- Results feel incremental and not particularly surprising, mainly providing added theoretical backing for an empirically known approach.  
- Techniques do not appear especially novel.  
- Upper bounds are provided without matching lower bounds, which undermines algorithmic comparisons.  
- Minor presentation issues: misleading abstract wording about proposing a new class of norms, Figure 1 placed before necessary background, and several small typos/grammar errors.  
- Arguments for acceptance: thorough analysis of latent norm, favorable theoretical comparisons to overlap norm, and supporting simulations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow ML relevance, lack of ML-focused experiments/theory, incremental contributions, limited novelty, and missing lower bounds.
