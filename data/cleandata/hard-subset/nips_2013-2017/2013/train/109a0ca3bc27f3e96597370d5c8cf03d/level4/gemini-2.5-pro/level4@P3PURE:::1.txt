This paper analyzes convex norm-regularized optimization methods for the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on a comparison between the recently proposed "latent" norm [25] and the more established "overlap" norm. The primary contribution is the derivation of theoretical bounds on the mean-squared error for the latent norm under several noise assumptions. These bounds compare favorably to existing results for the overlap norm, providing a theoretical justification for the superior empirical performance of the latent norm observed in prior work. The theoretical findings are supported by numerical simulations. Additionally, the authors introduce a generalization of both norms and prove a corresponding duality theorem.

The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The technical arguments appear sound, although I have not verified the proofs in the supplementary material in detail. The results may be of interest to researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, I have several concerns:

1.  **Narrow Scope and Limited Impact:** The paper's scope is narrow and highly technical. While the authors motivate the work with applications in machine learning, the paper itself is only peripherally related to the field and may have limited impact on the broader NIPS audience. The analysis, both theoretical and experimental, is not situated within the context of a specific machine learning problem.

2.  **Incremental Contribution:** The contributions appear to be somewhat incremental. The main result provides theoretical backing for a recently proposed method [25] that was already shown to perform well empirically, making the findings not particularly surprising. Furthermore, the analytical techniques employed seem to be standard for this line of research.

3.  **Lack of Lower Bounds:** A more minor, yet important, technical weakness is the absence of lower bounds to complement the derived upper bounds. This omission is significant because the authors use these upper bounds to argue for the superiority of the latent norm over the overlap norm. Without knowing how tight these bounds are, such a comparison may be inconclusive.

**Additional Comments:**

*   The abstract claims to "propose a new class of structured Schatten norms," which is slightly misleading. The paper's focus is on analyzing two existing norms (latent and overlap). The proposed generalization is only briefly introduced to prove a duality theorem (Lemma 1); no algorithms based on this general class are proposed, analyzed, or experimentally validated.
*   Figure 1 is presented in the introduction but is difficult to interpret without the context provided in Section 2 and beyond. It would be more effective if moved or introduced later.
*   Line 38: "Tensor is" should be "Tensor modeling is" or similar.
*   Line 40: A comma is missing after "Conventionally".
*   Line 395: "also in such situations" would be better phrased as "in such situations as well".

In summary, the paper's main contribution is a set of theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition method. While the paper seems technically sound, the results are somewhat incremental and may have limited impact at NIPS.
