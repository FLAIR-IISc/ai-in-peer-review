This paper presents theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm incorporating latent Schatten norm regularization, offering an improvement over existing results that utilize overlapped Schatten norm regularization.

Quality: The paper's theoretical contributions to tensor decomposition via latent Schatten norm are notable, with the dual characterization of overlapped and latent Schatten norms being a highlight. However, the experimental section is somewhat concise and lacks elaboration. Key aspects, such as the selection process for Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) in the examples and the efficacy of group regularization methods like l_1,\infty when the tensor exhibits low rank in all modes, warrant further discussion. Additionally, a comparison with non-convex approaches would be beneficial.

Clarity: The paper's organization and clarity make it easily understandable.

Originality: The primary original contribution of this paper lies in the duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, a result that holds independent interest.

Significance: Given the importance of tensor decomposition in various applications, this paper's improvement upon existing results renders it significant. The theoretical results are engaging, and the paper is well-written. Although the experimental section is brief, the paper's overall value and contributions to the field of tensor decomposition are substantial.
