- Compares convex optimization methods for low-rank tensor decomposition using overlapped vs latent structured Schatten norms.  
- Main contribution: derives MSE bounds for the latent approach showing error scales with the minimum mode-k rank.  
- Introduces a generalized class of structured Schatten norms that encompasses both approaches.  
- Proves a duality result connecting overlapped and latent norms (Lemma 1).  
- Provides numerical simulations that confirm the predicted error-scaling behavior.  
- Strength: Offers rigorous theoretical justification for latent’s empirical superiority, especially when only one mode is low-rank.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents the theory logically.  
- Strength: The generalization and duality link tensor methods to structured sparsity, an elegant and useful connection.  
- Weakness: Contributions feel incremental—primarily analysis of an existing method using established techniques.  
- Weakness: Narrow scope focused on a synthetic denoising problem, with no connection to real-world ML applications.  
- Weakness: Lacks lower bounds, so the tightness and fundamental optimality of the derived upper bounds are uncertain.  
- Suggestion: Rephrase the abstract to reflect analysis of existing norms rather than proposing a new class.  
- Suggestion: Move or improve Figure 1’s caption/placement to aid interpretation before formal definitions.  
- Suggestion: Add semi-synthetic or real-data experiments to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Summary: For acceptance—technically sound, clear, and offers an elegant duality; Against acceptance—incremental and narrowly focused for the NIPS audience.
