- The paper provides novel theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm using latent Schatten norm regularization.  
- It improves existing bounds compared to methods regularized with the overlapped Schatten norm.  
- The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a novel and independently interesting contribution.  
- Theoretical contributions are significant given the wide applicability of tensor decomposition.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak and treated too briefly, undermining empirical support for the claims.  
- Key experimental details are missing (e.g., criteria for selecting Tucker ranks in synthetic experiments).  
- The paper lacks important empirical comparisons, such as against group regularization (l1,∞) when tensors are low-rank in all modes.  
- It also omits comparisons to leading non-convex tensor decomposition methods.  
- The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the theoretical arguments easy to follow.  
- Overall, the theoretical work is strong but the paper would be much improved by a more thorough empirical evaluation.
