This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon the existing results for regularization using the overlapped Schatten norm.

Quality: The paper's main contribution lies in its theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. Notably, the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is an interesting aspect of the work. However, the experimental section is somewhat brief and lacks elaboration. For instance, it would be helpful to know how the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) of the examples were chosen. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion on how group regularization, such as l_1,\infty, performs when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes. Furthermore, a comparison of the proposed method with non-convex approaches would provide valuable insights into its relative performance.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clear to understand, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and results.

Originality: The main original result in the paper is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This result appears to be of independent interest and contributes to the broader understanding of tensor decomposition.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem in many applications, and this paper's improvement upon existing results makes it a significant contribution to the field. The theoretical results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. However, the brevity of the experimental section is a drawback. Overall, the paper's strengths lie in its theoretical contributions, and with more comprehensive experiments, it could be even more impactful.
