- Presents theoretical analysis of convex tensor decomposition regularized by the latent Schatten norm, showing latent > overlapped especially when tensor is low-rank in a single unknown mode.
- Models W as sum of K component tensors with mode-specific rank penalties, contrasting with overlapped approach that penalizes all unfoldings.
- Proves a novel duality between overlapped Sp/q-norms and latent Sp*/q*-norms, linking to structured sparsity literature.
- Derives performance bounds showing latent MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, while overlapped scales with the average of sqrt mode-k ranks.
- Significance/originality: addresses known limitation of overlapped regularization and explains latent’s adaptive advantage without prior mode knowledge.
- Technical quality: rigorous analysis with an elegant duality lemma (Lemma 1) and clear main theorems (Theorems 2 and 3).
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and effectively connects to structured sparsity.
- Experimental scope is limited and brief; more detail on synthetic rank selection (r1,r2,r3) is needed.
- Empirical comparisons are restricted to two convex methods; should include non-convex baselines (e.g., HOSVD, ALS).
- Lacks explicit experiments for tensors low-rank in all modes, where overlapped is theoretically favored.
- Analysis focuses on S1/1; should discuss potential uses and benefits of other Sp/q norms (e.g., S1/∞).
- Arguments for acceptance: strong novel theory, impactful min-rank error bound, and an elegant duality result of independent interest.
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow experimental evaluation lacking broader baselines and regime exploration.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution that merits acceptance, but experiments should be expanded for fuller empirical validation.
