This paper presents a convex optimization-based approach for Tucker tensor decomposition using a structured (overlapped) Schatten norm. The authors provide theoretical guarantees demonstrating that this method leads to improved reconstruction results. The paper is well-written, and its technical contributions are significant. However, addressing several issues related to motivation, technical clarity, and notation will substantially improve the work.

**Major Comments:**

1.  **Motivation for Machine Learning:** To improve the paper's accessibility and impact for a broader machine learning audience, the motivation for this problem should be significantly strengthened. A concrete way to achieve this would be to introduce a latent variable model where this form of tensor decomposition naturally arises for parameter learning. For example, consider a model where a latent variable h1 connects to an observed variable x1, h2 to x2, and so on, with linear mappings from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the h_i's. The resulting observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition where the multilinear ranks correspond to the dimensions of the latent variables (dim(h_1), dim(h_2), ...). Including this discussion, ideally with a graphical model diagram, would provide valuable context. Furthermore, this framing would allow the authors to explicitly highlight a key advantage of their results: the ability to handle cases where only a subset of the latent variables are low-dimensional.

2.  **Clarifications Regarding Theorem 2 and Its Proof:** Several technical clarifications are needed for the main theoretical results:
    *   The statement of Theorem 2 is technically imprecise, as the components are recovered only up to permutation. This should be explicitly mentioned.
    *   The claim in the proof that (12) immediately leads to (13) requires justification.
    *   The proof for Lemma 5, which is a key component in the proof of Theorem 2, is missing and must be included.
    *   For the sake of completeness and readability, the proof of Lemma 4 should be provided explicitly in the paper rather than just citing another source, even if it is straightforward.

**Minor Comments:**

*   **Related Work:** The discussion of related work could be expanded to include the Higher-Order SVD (HOSVD) and its variants, along with their associated approximation bounds for tensor decomposition (e.g., as reviewed in Kolda's survey on tensor decompositions, Section 4).

*   **Terminology:** In the introduction, it would be helpful to mention that the "mode-rank" used in the paper is also commonly referred to as "multilinear rank" and to briefly note the existence of other notions of tensor rank.

*   **Computational Complexity:** A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods should be added.

*   **Incoherence Condition:** The discussion of the incoherence condition following equation (10) needs clarification. The authors claim it is the same as the conditions in [1,3,10], but this comparison seems inappropriate. The conditions in [1,3,10] are for sparse + low-rank recovery and concern the incoherence of singular vectors with standard basis vectors. Here, the noise is not sparse, and the condition appears to relate to the "energy" being spread across components and modes. It is recommended to remove this comparison to avoid confusion.

**Notational Issues and Typos:**

*   The superscript notation for components, `\hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}`, is potentially confusing and could be replaced with a different symbol.
*   The notation for k-mode rank is inconsistent (`\bar{r}k` and `\underline{r}k` are both used). Adopting a single, clear notation, such as `mult_rank(T)`, would be preferable.
*   A closing parenthesis appears to be missing in the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material.
*   In the abstract, "result for structures Schatten" should be corrected to "result for structured Schatten".

**Summary:**
The paper makes a novel contribution by proposing the use of an overlapped Schatten norm for Tucker tensor decomposition and provides corresponding recovery guarantees. The work is technically strong, but its impact would be significantly enhanced by addressing the points above, especially regarding motivation and the clarity of the theoretical results.
