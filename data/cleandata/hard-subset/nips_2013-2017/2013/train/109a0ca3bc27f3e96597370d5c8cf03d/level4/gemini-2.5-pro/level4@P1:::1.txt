This paper analyzes convex, norm-regularized optimization for the decomposition of approximately low-rank tensors. The primary focus is on a recently proposed "latent" approach [25], which is compared to the previously studied "overlapped" approach. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared error for the latent method under various noise assumptions. These bounds compare favorably with existing analyses of the overlapped method and provide a theoretical explanation for the superior empirical performance of the latent approach reported in prior work. The theoretical claims are supported by numerical simulations. As a secondary contribution, the paper introduces a generalized class of structured Schatten norms and proves a new duality theorem.

The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and appears to be technically sound, though I have not verified the proofs in the supplementary material in detail. The theoretical analysis and duality results will likely be useful for researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decomposition. However, I have several concerns regarding the paper's contribution.

**Weaknesses**

*   **Limited Scope and Impact:** The contributions are primarily theoretical and somewhat narrow. While the paper motivates the work with potential applications in machine learning, it does not evaluate the methods on any real-world machine learning tasks. This lack of demonstrated practical application makes the connection to the broader NIPS community tenuous and may limit the paper's overall impact.

*   **Incremental Contribution:** The results, while sound, feel incremental. The work provides theoretical justification for a specific, recently proposed method [25] that had already been shown to perform well empirically. The findings are not particularly surprising, and the analytical techniques employed appear to be standard for this line of research.

*   **Lack of Lower Bounds:** The analysis provides only upper bounds on the estimation error. The absence of matching lower bounds is a notable weakness, as the paper's central argument for the superiority of the latent approach rests entirely on a comparison of these upper bounds.

**Additional Comments**

*   The claim in the abstract to "propose a new class of structured Schatten norms" is slightly misleading. The paper's focus is on analyzing two existing norms. The generalized class is only introduced briefly in the context of proving a duality result (Lemma 1) and is not explored further; no algorithms based on this generalization are proposed, analyzed, or experimentally tested.

*   For improved clarity, consider moving the discussion of Figure 1 until after the necessary background has been introduced in Section 2. As it stands, the figure is difficult to interpret in the introduction.

*   Minor corrections:
    *   Line 38: "Tensor is" should probably be "Tensor modeling is".
    *   Line 40: Add a comma after "Conventionally".

In summary, while the paper is technically competent and provides a useful theoretical analysis for a niche topic, its contributions feel incremental and its relevance to the broader machine learning community is not clearly demonstrated.
