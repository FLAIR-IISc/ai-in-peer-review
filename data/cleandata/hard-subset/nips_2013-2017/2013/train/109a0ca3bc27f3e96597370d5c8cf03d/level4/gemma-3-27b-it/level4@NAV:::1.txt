This paper investigates convex norm-regularized optimization techniques for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, with a central focus on a recent “latent” norm approach [25] and a comparison to the established “overlap” norm method. The authors provide theoretical bounds on the mean-squared-error performance of the latent norm under various noise conditions. These bounds demonstrate advantages over existing analyses of the overlap norm, aligning with previously reported empirical results. The theoretical findings are supported by numerical simulations, and the work concludes with a generalization of both norms alongside a corresponding duality theorem.

The paper is generally well-written and appears technically correct, though a detailed verification of the proofs in the supplementary material was not undertaken. The results likely hold value for researchers in optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, my primary concerns relate to the scope and impact of the work:

(i) The results are highly technical and narrowly focused, with only a tangential connection to machine learning. While the authors acknowledge the relevance of tensor factorizations to machine learning, the paper lacks any investigation of the proposed approaches within the context of actual machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally, which limits its potential appeal at NIPS.

(ii) The contributions feel somewhat incremental and not particularly surprising. The work primarily provides additional theoretical justification for a recently proposed and already empirically validated approach [25], utilizing established techniques. 

A further point is the absence of corresponding lower bounds to complement the presented upper bounds, which poses a risk when using these bounds for comparative algorithmic analysis.

Additional Comments:
-----------------
- The abstract’s claim of “proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms” is potentially misleading. The paper predominantly *analyzes* two existing approaches (overlap and latent). The generalization appears later, primarily serving as a foundation for proving a duality theorem (Lemma 1) rather than driving the development of new algorithms or analysis.
- Figure 1, presented early in the introduction, requires background knowledge from Section 2 to be meaningfully interpreted.
- Line 38: Consider revising “Tensor is” to “Tensor modelling is”.
- Line 40: Add a comma after “Conventionally”.
- Line 395: Replace “also in such situations” with “in such situations as well”. 

In summary, the paper’s main contribution lies in establishing theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor decomposition method. While technically sound, the results are incremental and may have limited impact within the NIPS community.
