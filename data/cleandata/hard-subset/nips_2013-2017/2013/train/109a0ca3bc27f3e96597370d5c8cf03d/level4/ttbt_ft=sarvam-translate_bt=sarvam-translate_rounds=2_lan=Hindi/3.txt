This paper provides theoretical limits on the performance of convex tensor decomposition algorithms regulated by the hidden shadow standard. It improves the known result for regularization using the overlap shadow standard.

Quality: This paper achieves theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the hidden shadow standard. In particular, the dual characterization of overlap and hidden shadow is interesting. However, experiments are briefly considered and not discussed in detail. For example, how were the Tucker ranks (r1,...rk) of the examples chosen? How does group regularization like l_1,\infty work when the tensor is low rank across all modes simultaneously? How does this method perform compared to non-convex approaches?

Clarity: This paper is well-organized and clear to understand.

Originality: The main original result in the paper is the duality between the overlap and hidden shadow standards. This result appears to be independently interesting.

Importance: Tensor decomposition is a significant problem in many applications. Since this paper improves existing results for this problem, I believe it is important work.
