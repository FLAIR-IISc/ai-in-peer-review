- Introduces a general framework for structured Schatten norms for tensors and applies it to convex tensor decomposition.  
- Distinguishes two approaches: "overlapped" (penalize all mode unfoldings) and "latent" (sum of mode-specific low-rank components).  
- Main theoretical contribution: latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, explaining its empirical advantage when few modes are low-rank.  
- Develops a novel duality result between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms (Lemma 1), linking to structured sparsity literature.  
- Validates theoretical findings with numerical experiments showing predicted MSE scaling for both approaches.  
- Strength: first theoretical performance guarantees for the latent convex tensor decomposition method.  
- Strength: provides significant insight that the latent method adapts to the lowest-rank mode without prior knowledge.  
- Strength: technically deep framework and duality result that unifies perspectives and may inspire future work.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and empirical results support theory.  
- Weakness: motivation from a machine-learning perspective is weak; recommending adding latent-variable examples and graphical models to illustrate when few modes are low-rank.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 needs clearer statement about non-unique decomposition (permutation ambiguity) and a more detailed derivation from (12) to (13).  
- Weakness: supporting lemmas incomplete—proof for Lemma 5 missing and Lemma 4 only cited; supply full proofs for completeness.  
- Weakness: comparison of incoherence assumption to prior work is misleading; clarify differences from standard incoherence conditions.  
- Weakness: broader context lacking—should discuss alternative tensor methods (e.g., HOSVD), clarify "mode-k rank" vs. CP rank.  
- Weakness: add a short discussion of computational complexity for solving latent and overlapped optimization problems.  
- Minor issues: notation confusion between latent components Ŵ^(k) and mode-k unfoldings W(k); recommend distinct symbols.  
- Minor issues: ensure consistent use of mode-k rank notation throughout.  
- Minor issues: typographical errors—"structures Schatten" → "structured Schatten" and a missing parenthesis in supplementary material.  
- Conclusion: strong, novel, and technically impressive paper; addressing motivation, proof details, related work, and minor fixes will further strengthen it; recommend acceptance.
