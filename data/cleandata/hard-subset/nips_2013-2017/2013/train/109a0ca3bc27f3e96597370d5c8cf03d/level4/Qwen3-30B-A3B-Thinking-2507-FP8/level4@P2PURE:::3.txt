

Quality: The paper establishes theoretical bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon prior results for overlapped Schatten norm regularization. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a notable contribution. However, the experimental section is brief and lacks sufficient detail. Key questions remain unanswered: how were the Tucker ranks (r₁, ..., rₖ) selected in the examples? How does group regularization (e.g., ℓ₁,∞) function when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes? How does the method compare to non-convex approaches?  
Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
Originality: The central original contribution is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which appears to be of independent interest.  
Significance: Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with broad applications. This work advances the state of the art by improving theoretical guarantees for the problem, rendering it significant. While the theoretical results are interesting and the paper is well-written, the experimental evaluation is insufficiently detailed.
