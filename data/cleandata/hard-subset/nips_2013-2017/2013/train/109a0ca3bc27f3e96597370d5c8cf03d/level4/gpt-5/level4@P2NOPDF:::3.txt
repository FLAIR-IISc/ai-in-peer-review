This paper derives theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon prior results for regularization with the overlapped Schatten norm.

Quality: The paper presents solid theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization connecting the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly interesting. However, the experimental section is brief and lacks sufficient detail. For example:
- How were the Tucker ranks (r1, …, rk) selected in the experiments?
- How does group regularization, such as the l1,∞ norm, behave when the tensor is simultaneously low rank across all modes?
- How does the proposed method compare empirically with non-convex approaches?

Clarity: The paper is well organized and easy to follow.

Originality: The primary original contribution is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which appears to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with many applications, and the paper advances the state of the art by strengthening existing theoretical guarantees. The results are interesting and the manuscript is well written. That said, the experimental evaluation is too brief and would benefit from more comprehensive analysis.
