

Additional comments after the rebuttal:  
The authors must substantially improve the problem motivation for machine learning to broaden accessibility. They should introduce a latent variable model (with graphical representation) demonstrating how tensor decomposition learns parameters. Specifically, consider a model where latent variables \(h_1, h_2, \ldots\) connect to observed variables \(x_1, x_2, \ldots\), with linear mappings from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the \(h_i\). In this setting, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank equals \((\dim(h_1), \dim(h_2), \ldots)\). The authors must add this discussion and include a diagram of the model. They should also clarify that only some hidden variables may have small dimensions (a key advantage of their result), not all.  

The paper presents a convex-optimization approach to tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, employing the overlapped Schatten norm to achieve stronger reconstruction guarantees. It is well-written and makes technically significant contributions. Addressing minor technical and notational issues will enhance readability.  

*Clarifications on Theorem 2 and proof:*  
- Theorem 2’s statement is technically inaccurate: components are recovered only up to permutation. This must be explicitly noted.  
- The claim that (12) immediately implies (13) requires justification.  
- The proof of Lemma 5 (a key component of Theorem 2) is omitted and must be provided.  
- The proof of Lemma 4 should be clarified explicitly instead of referencing another paper, despite its straightforward nature.  

*Minor issues:*  
- Expand related work on HOSVD and tensor decomposition methods. Specifically, cite how higher-order SVD and variants yield approximation bounds (e.g., Kolda’s review, Section 4).  
- Clarify the rank terminology: the "mode-rank" used is synonymous with "multilinear rank." The introduction should acknowledge differing tensor rank definitions.  
- Briefly discuss the computational complexity of the proposed methods.  
- Revise the incoherence discussion after (10). The authors equate their condition (spreading "energy" across mixture components/modes) to incoherence in [1,3,10], but this is incorrect. The cited works use incoherence for *sparse+low-rank* recovery, whereas here the noise is non-sparse. The conditions differ fundamentally (e.g., [1,3,10] requires singular vectors to be incoherent with basis vectors, unrelated to the authors’ condition). Remove this comparison.  

*Typos/notational issues:*  
- The superscript notation \(\hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}\) for components is confusing; adopt a distinct symbol.  
- Replace conflicting notations \(\bar{r}_k\) and \(\underline{r}_k\) for \(k\)-mode rank with a unified convention (e.g., \(\text{mult\_rank}(\mathcal{T})\)).  
- Correct missing parentheses in the last line of page 12 (supplementary material).  
- Abstract: "result for structures Schatten" → "result for structured Schatten norms."  

The paper is well-written and offers a novel contribution in leveraging overlapped Schatten norms for Tucker decomposition with recovery guarantees. However, the requested technical clarifications must be addressed to ensure correctness and completeness.
