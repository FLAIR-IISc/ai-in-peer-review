- Studies structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, comparing "latent" vs "overlapped" approaches.  
- Proves a novel duality: the dual of the overlapped Schatten norm is the latent Schatten norm (Lemma 1).  
- Shows rigorous error bounds (Theorems 2 and 3) where latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, not the average rank.  
- Explains the empirical superiority of the latent approach in settings where the tensor is low-rank in only one mode.  
- Numerical experiments confirm theoretical predictions, showing MSE scales with the predicted LR complexity.  
- Builds on prior convex tensor decomposition work and connects to structured sparsity literature, increasing relevance.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound with well-constructed proofs and clear connections to prior work.  
- Presentation is exceptionally clear: precise definitions, consistent notation, and logical organization.  
- Supplementary material provides necessary technical details without overloading the main text.  
- Originality is strong: the duality result and the min-rank dependence are novel contributions.  
- Significance is substantial due to broad tensor decomposition applications (neuroimaging, collaborative filtering, etc.).  
- Main experimental weakness: insufficient detail on how Tucker ranks (r₁,...,rₖ) were chosen for synthetic data.  
- Lacks comparison with non-convex tensor decomposition methods (e.g., HOSVD, ALS), which dominate practice.  
- Limited practical guidance on selecting the regularization constant despite noting its dimensional scaling.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong, well-written, meaningful theoretical advance; reviewer recommends acceptance.
