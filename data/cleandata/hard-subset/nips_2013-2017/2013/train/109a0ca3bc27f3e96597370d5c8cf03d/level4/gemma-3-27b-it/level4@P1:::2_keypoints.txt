- Paper analyzes convex tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, focusing on the latent vs. overlapped norms.  
- Main theoretical result: latent approach error scales with the minimum mode-k rank, explaining its empirical advantage when tensor is low-rank in few modes.  
- Presents a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms relevant to structured sparsity.  
- Empirical simulations validate the theoretical mean-squared-error scaling predictions.  
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and accessible to the community.  
- Strength: solid theoretical rigor and clear explanation of why latent outperforms overlapped in certain settings.  
- Strength: novelty and potential broader impact of the duality result.  
- Strength: empirical validation supports the theory.  
- Weakness: needs stronger machine-learning motivation, e.g., a latent-variable model showing when Tucker/multilinear ranks arise.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 should explicitly state component recovery is up to permutation.  
- Weakness: Transition from inequality (12) to (13) in Theorem 2 needs clearer justification (how minimal mode-k rank is obtained).  
- Weakness: Proof of Lemma 5 is missing and is crucial for Theorem 2.  
- Weakness: Lemma 4’s proof should be clarified in the paper rather than only citing another work.  
- Weakness: Related work should be expanded to include HOSVD and standard tensor decomposition reviews (e.g., Kolda).  
- Weakness: Authors should state explicitly which notion of tensor rank they use (e.g., multilinear rank).  
- Weakness: Add a brief discussion of computational complexity of the proposed methods.  
- Weakness: The connection to prior “incoherence” conditions is questionable and should be clarified to avoid misleading comparisons.  
- Minor issue: confusing superscript notation for components (e.g., \hat{W}^{(k)}); suggest alternative notation.  
- Minor issue: inconsistent use of \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k for k-mode rank; recommend clear notation like mult_rank(tensor).  
- Minor issue: typographical errors (missing parenthesis in supplement, “structures Schatten” → “structured Schatten” in abstract).  
- Recommendation: overall a strong paper; accept contingent on addressing the technical clarifications, notation fixes, related-work expansion, and motivation improvements.
