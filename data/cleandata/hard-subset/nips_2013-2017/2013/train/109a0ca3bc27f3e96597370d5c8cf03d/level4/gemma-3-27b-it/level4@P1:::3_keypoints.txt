- Provides theoretical performance bounds for convex tensor decomposition regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving on overlapped Schatten results.
- Dual characterization of overlapped and latent Schatten norms is an interesting and novel contribution.
- Theoretical proofs appear sound and carefully constructed (verification limited to main text).
- Experimental evaluation is brief and lacks detailed discussion of choices and results.
- Unclear how tensor ranks (r1,...,rK) in experiments were chosen.
- Missing evaluation of group regularization (e.g., l1,∞) when tensor is low-rank in all modes.
- Lacks comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods.
- Paper is well organized, clearly written, and uses consistent notation with sufficient background.
- Originality: latent Schatten analysis and the duality result are novel and connect to structured sparsity.
- Significance: improves theoretical understanding of an important problem and demonstrates better performance than overlapped approach.
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution; limited experiments are a minor weakness but not critical.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound analysis; interesting duality; clear writing; important problem; supportive empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental evaluation not comprehensive; connections to group regularization (l1,∞) underexplored.
