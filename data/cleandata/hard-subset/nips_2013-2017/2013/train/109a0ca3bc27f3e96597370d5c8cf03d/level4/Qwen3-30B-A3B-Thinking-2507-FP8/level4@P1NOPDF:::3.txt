

This paper establishes theoretical bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, building upon prior work on overlapped Schatten norm regularization.  

**Quality:** The theoretical results are sound, with the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms representing a particularly valuable contribution. However, the experimental evaluation is superficial and lacks sufficient detail. For instance, the selection process for Tucker ranks (r₁, ..., rₖ) in the examples is not explained. Additionally, the implementation and effectiveness of group regularization (e.g., the l_{1,∞} norm) when the tensor exhibits low rank across all modes remain unclear. Crucially, the paper omits a comparison against non-convex tensor decomposition methods, which are commonly used in practice and often achieve superior empirical performance.  

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the technical contributions accessible.  

**Originality:** The core novelty lies in the duality relationship between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This theoretical insight stands as a significant contribution in its own right, independent of the tensor decomposition application.  

**Significance:** Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with broad applications, and this work advances the state-of-the-art in convex regularization for this task. The improved theoretical guarantees represent a meaningful contribution to the field. While the paper is well-written and the theoretical results are interesting, the underdeveloped experimental section limits its overall impact. The lack of detailed empirical analysis—particularly regarding rank selection, group regularization mechanics, and comparisons to non-convex alternatives—prevents a comprehensive assessment of the method’s practical utility.
