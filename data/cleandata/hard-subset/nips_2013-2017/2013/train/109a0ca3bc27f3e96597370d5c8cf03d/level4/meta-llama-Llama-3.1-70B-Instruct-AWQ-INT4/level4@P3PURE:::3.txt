This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon existing results that utilize the overlapped Schatten norm for regularization.

Quality: The paper makes a valuable contribution by deriving theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly noteworthy. However, the experimental section is somewhat brief and lacks elaboration. For instance, the authors do not explain how the Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) of the examples were chosen. Furthermore, it would be beneficial to discuss how group regularization, such as l_1,\infty, performs when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes. Additionally, a comparison with non-convex approaches would be helpful in assessing the method's performance.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clear to understand, making it accessible to readers familiar with the subject matter.

Originality: The main original contribution of the paper is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which appears to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem in many applications, and this paper's improvement upon existing results makes it a significant work. The theoretical results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. However, the brief treatment of experiments towards the end of the paper is a notable limitation.
