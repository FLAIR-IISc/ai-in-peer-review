This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by a latent shadow norm. It improves upon known results for regularization using overlapped shadow norms.

Quality: This paper achieves theoretical results for tensor decomposition using latent shadow norms. In particular, the duality between overlapped and latent shadow norms is interesting. However, the experiments are briefly considered and not discussed in detail. For example, how were the rank of the examples (r1,...rk) chosen? How does group regularization like l_1,\infty work when the tensor is low-ranked in all modes simultaneously? How does this method perform compared to non-convex methods?

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and easy to understand.

Originality: The main original result of the paper is the duality between overlapped and latent shadow norms. This result seems to be of independent interest.

Importance: Tensor decomposition is an important problem encountered in many applications. Since this paper improves upon existing results in this problem, I believe it is an important work.
