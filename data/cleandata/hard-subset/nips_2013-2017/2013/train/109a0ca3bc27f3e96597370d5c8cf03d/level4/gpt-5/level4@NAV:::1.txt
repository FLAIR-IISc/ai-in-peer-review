This paper studies convex, norm-regularized optimization methods for decomposing approximately low-rank tensors. It centers on the recently introduced “latent” norm approach ([25]) and its comparison with the previously used “overlap” norm. In particular, the authors derive mean-squared error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, which compare favorably with existing analyses of the overlap norm, aligning with earlier empirical findings of superior performance. The theoretical claims are supported with numerical simulations. The paper also presents a generalization that encompasses both norms and proves a corresponding duality result.

The paper is generally clear and appears technically sound (though I did not scrutinize the supplementary proofs in detail). The results may be of interest to researchers working on optimization for tensor decompositions. My main concerns are that
(i) The contributions are fairly narrow and technical, only tangentially connected to machine learning, and likely of limited impact for NIPS. While the authors make a reasonable case that tensor factorizations arise in ML, the work does not explore the proposed approaches within machine learning tasks, either theoretically or empirically.
(ii) The advances feel somewhat incremental and not particularly surprising (recognizing this is subjective). The work primarily adds theoretical support for a specific recent approach [25] that had already been shown to perform well empirically, and the techniques employed do not appear especially novel.

A smaller issue is the absence of lower bounds to accompany the upper bounds. This can be problematic when using the upper bounds to compare algorithms and argue for the superiority of one over another.

Additional Comments

- The abstract opens with “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...,” which seems slightly misleading. The paper’s main focus is analyzing two existing approaches (overlapped and latent). Unless I missed something, the generalization appears only in Section 2.1 along with a dual norm characterization (Lemma 1). No specific algorithms are proposed based on these generalized Schatten norms, their behavior is not analyzed, and they are not evaluated empirically.
