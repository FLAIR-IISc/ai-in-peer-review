- Proposes a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors and applies them to convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition.  
- Analyzes the latent approach and shows it can outperform the overlapped approach in certain settings.  
- Paper is well-written, technically strong, and contains thorough, well-organized proofs.  
- Provides a clear overview of related work and positions the contribution within tensor decomposition and structured sparsity literature.  
- Introduces recovery guarantees using the new norms and supports theoretical results with numerical experiments.  
- Paper organization and writing balance technical detail and high-level explanation, making it accessible.  

Strengths:
- Significant technical contributions to tensor decomposition and structured sparsity.  
- Clear and thorough presentation of technical results.  
- Numerical experiments strongly support theoretical claims.  
- Good balance between theory and exposition.

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed approach.  
- Some notation and terminology are unclear or confusing.  
- Incoherence condition in the proof of Theorem 2 needs more context and explanation.

Minor issues:
- Add more related work on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods.  
- Clarify the notion of rank and note it is also called multilinear rank.  
- Briefly discuss computational complexity of employed methods.  
- Clarify incoherence discussion after equation (10).  
- Change confusing superscript notation for components \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}.  
- Replace \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k with mult_rank(tensor) to denote multilinear rank.  
- Fix missing parentheses in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material.

Overall:
- With minor revisions addressing complexity, notation, and incoherence, the paper has strong potential to make a significant impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial technical contribution, clear presentation, and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need for clearer complexity analysis, notation fixes, and better explanation of incoherence condition.
