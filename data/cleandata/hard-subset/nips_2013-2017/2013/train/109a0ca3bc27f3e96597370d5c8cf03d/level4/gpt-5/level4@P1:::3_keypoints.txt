- Unifies overlapped and latent convex tensor decomposition via a class of structured Schatten norms and proves a duality between them.
- Provides analysis of the latent approach showing MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average (sqrt) of ranks.
- Theoretical contributions include consistency, deterministic error bounds under incoherence, and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds.
- Clarifies choice of regularization and the role of the dual norm; makes the role of spectral-norm constraint (α) and regularization level (λ) explicit.
- Connects tensor norms to structured sparsity (overlapped vs latent group lasso) and extends duality results to structured Schatten norms.
- Relates to prior work: builds on overlapped Schatten-1 analyses [26], formalizes latent approach theory (previously proposed in [25]), and matches lower-bound dependence on min mode rank [19].
- Dual characterization of overlapped vs latent Schatten norms is elegant and of independent interest.
- Bounds for the latent approach are sharper when the true tensor is low-rank primarily in one mode; analyses carefully track dimensions, ranks, and noise.
- Empirical simulations support the theoretical scaling but the experimental section is brief and under-detailed.
- Missing methodological details limit reproducibility—e.g., how Tucker ranks (r1,…,rK) were chosen in synthetic experiments.
- Behavior of group regularization (e.g., ℓ1,∞) when tensors are simultaneously low-rank in all modes is not explored.
- No empirical comparisons to standard non-convex baselines (ALS, HOOI/Tucker-HOOI, CP-ALS) in accuracy or runtime.
- Sensitivity of performance to λ and how to choose λ in practice without known noise variance is not addressed.
- Computational cost and scalability of the ADMM solver relative to overlapped formulations and non-convex methods are not reported.
- Paper is well organized and clearly written; definitions and assumptions are stated clearly.
- Main novelty is the duality between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms and the resulting performance guarantees.
- Contribution is significant for tensor decomposition theory and may influence practice, but stronger empirical validation would increase impact.
- Pros: general duality result, sharper denoising guarantees for latent approach, consistency and high-probability bounds, and conceptual unification with structured sparsity.
- Cons: brief experimental evaluation, missing non-convex baselines, limited exploration of simultaneous low-rank settings and alternative penalties, and limited practical guidance on λ and α.
- Suggested experiments: specify Tucker-rank selection, evaluate wider rank configurations including simultaneously low-rank tensors, and test ℓ1,∞ and other group penalties.
- Suggested baselines: include ALS/HOOI/CP-ALS with model selection and report both accuracy and runtime.
- Suggested methodology: provide a principled λ-tuning procedure (noise estimation or cross-validation) and sensitivity analysis.
- Suggested reporting: give computational complexity, ADMM convergence criteria, and wall-clock timings.
- Overall assessment: theoretically strong and well-written paper with valuable duality and error bounds; expanding the empirical section as suggested would make the contribution more compelling.
