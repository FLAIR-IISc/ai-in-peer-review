This paper establishes theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition method regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving over prior results based on the overlapped Schatten norm.

Quality: The paper delivers theoretical guarantees for tensor decomposition under the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization relating the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly noteworthy. However, the experimental evaluation is rather brief and lacks detail. For example, how were the Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) chosen in the examples? How does group regularization such as l_1,∞ behave when the tensor is low rank in all modes simultaneously? How does the method compare against non-convex approaches?

Clarity: The paper is well structured and easy to follow.

Originality: The primary novel contribution is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which appears to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is an important problem with many applications. Since this work strengthens existing results for this problem, I view it as a significant contribution. It improves upon established results for tensor decomposition. The theoretical findings are interesting and the paper is well written. Nevertheless, the experiments are treated rather briefly toward the end.
