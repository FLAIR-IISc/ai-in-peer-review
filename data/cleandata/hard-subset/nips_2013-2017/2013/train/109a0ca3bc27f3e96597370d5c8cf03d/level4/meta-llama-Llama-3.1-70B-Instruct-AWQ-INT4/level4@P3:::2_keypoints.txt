- Introduces a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, encompassing two recent norms.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the "latent" approach can outperform the "overlapped" approach, especially when the true tensor is low-rank in an unknown mode.  
- Presents a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms relevant to structured sparsity.  
- Paper is well-written and makes technically important contributions.  
- Theorem 2 needs clarification: component recovery is only up to permutation and the statement/proof should reflect that.  
- Proof of Lemma 5 is missing; provide the missing proof and justify how equation (12) leads to (13).  
- Add more related work on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods.  
- Clarify the notion of rank used (multilinear rank) and consider introducing notation like mult_rank(tensor).  
- Discuss the computational complexity of the proposed methods.  
- Clarify the discussion of incoherence following equation (10).  
- Fix minor notational issues (superscript notation for components; inconsistent use of \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k).  
- Better motivate the problem for machine learning audiences and provide a latent variable model showing how the decomposition recovers parameters.  
- Point out that only some hidden variables may be low-dimensional in practical models.  
- Overall: provides good Tucker decomposition results using overlapped Schatten norms with recovery guarantees; minor clarifications would increase impact.  
- Strengths summarized: novel norms, theoretical analysis of latent approach, technically strong writing.  
- Weaknesses summarized: technical/notation issues, limited ML motivation, missing related work on HOSVD.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel contributions, solid theory, clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: outstanding technical/notational issues and insufficient ML contextualization.  
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
