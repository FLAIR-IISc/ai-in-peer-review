This paper presents theoretical error bounds for a convex tensor decomposition method regularized by a latent Schatten norm, and demonstrates improvements over prior guarantees established for the overlapped Schatten norm.

Quality: The work delivers solid theoretical analysis for tensor decomposition under the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization linking overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly noteworthy. The experimental section, however, is quite brief and lacks detail. For example: How were the Tucker ranks (r1, …, rk) selected in the synthetic setups? How does a group-style regularizer such as ℓ1,∞ behave when the tensor is simultaneously low rank across all modes? How does the proposed approach compare empirically with standard non-convex baselines?

Clarity: The manuscript is clearly written and well structured, making the technical contributions easy to follow.

Originality: The primary novel contribution is the duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which appears to be of independent interest beyond the immediate application.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a central problem with broad applications. By strengthening existing results for this setting, the paper represents a meaningful contribution. The theory is compelling and the exposition is strong, though the experimental evaluation is relatively brief and could be expanded.
