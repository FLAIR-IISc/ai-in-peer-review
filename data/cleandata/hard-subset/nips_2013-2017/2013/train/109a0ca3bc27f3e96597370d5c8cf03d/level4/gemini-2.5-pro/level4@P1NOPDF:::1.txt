This paper considers convex norm-regularized optimization approaches to the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on a recently proposed "latent" norm approach [25] and its comparison to the more established "overlap" norm. The paper's central contribution is the derivation of theoretical bounds on the mean-squared error for the latent norm under several noise assumptions. These new bounds are more favorable than existing ones for the overlap norm, providing theoretical support for the superior empirical performance of the latent norm reported in prior work. The theory is also validated through numerical simulations. Finally, the paper introduces a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for this generalized class.

The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and appears to be technically sound, although I have not carefully verified the proofs in the supplementary material. The results may be useful for researchers specializing in optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, I have several concerns regarding the paper's contribution and its suitability for NIPS.

My main concerns are as follows:

1.  **Limited Scope and Relevance to Machine Learning:** The results are narrow and highly technical, with only a peripheral connection to the broader machine learning community. While the authors correctly argue that tensor factorizations arise in machine learning applications, the paper makes no attempt to examine these norms in the context of a specific machine learning problem, either theoretically or experimentally. Consequently, the potential impact of this work at NIPS may be limited.

2.  **Incremental Contribution:** The results seem somewhat incremental. The work primarily provides additional theoretical justification for a recently proposed method [25] that was already shown to perform well empirically. The theoretical techniques employed do not appear to be particularly novel.

A more minor weakness is the lack of corresponding lower bounds for the derived upper bounds. This is a potential issue, as the paper relies on these upper bounds to compare the two norms and argue for the superiority of the latent norm. Without lower bounds, it is difficult to assess the tightness of the analysis or how much of the performance gap is captured by the theory.

**Additional Comments**

*   The abstract opens with, "We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...", which I found slightly misleading. The main focus of the paper is the analysis of two existing norms (the overlap and latent norms). The generalized class is only briefly introduced in Section 2.1 to prove a duality result (Lemma 1). No algorithms based on this generalization are proposed, analyzed, or tested.
*   Figure 1 is presented in the introduction, but it is not interpretable without the background material provided in Section 2. It might be better placed later in the paper.
*   Line 38: "Tensor is" might be improved to "Tensor modeling is".
*   Line 40: A comma is missing after "Conventionally".
*   Line 395: "also in such situations" could be rephrased as "in such situations as well" for better flow.

**Conclusion**

The paper's main contribution is a set of theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed approach to low-rank tensor decomposition. While the work appears technically sound, the results are arguably incremental and may have limited impact on the NIPS audience due to their narrow theoretical focus.
