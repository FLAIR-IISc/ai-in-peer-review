Additional comments after the rebuttal:
The paper studies convex-optimization–based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, with a focus on the overlapped Schatten norm, and provides improved reconstruction guarantees. The paper is clearly written and technically solid. Addressing the issues below—especially motivation, modeling, and several technical clarifications—will make it accessible to a broader machine learning audience and strengthen its impact.

Motivation and modeling for the ML audience:
- Please better motivate the problem within standard latent-variable modeling. Provide an explicit latent variable model and a graphical representation in which the proposed tensor decomposition learns model parameters. For example: let hidden variables h1, h2, … each connect linearly to corresponding observed variables x1, x2, …, and allow arbitrary dependence among the hi’s. In this setting, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition whose multilinear ranks are dim(h1), dim(h2), … . Add a discussion of this construction and, ideally, a figure to illustrate the model.
- Emphasize that some hidden variables may have small dimension while others do not; this heterogeneity is a key advantage highlighted by your results.

Clarifications on Theorem 2 and proofs:
- Theorem 2: recovery is only identifiable up to a permutation of components. Please state this explicitly.
- The step from equation (12) to (13) is asserted to be immediate; provide a brief justification or reference for this implication.
- The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, yet it is central to Theorem 2. Please include the proof (or a complete sketch with precise references).
- For completeness, it would be better to outline the proof of Lemma 4 rather than only referencing prior work, even if the argument is straightforward.

Related work, terminology, and complexity:
- Expand the related work on HOSVD and its variants, including known approximation guarantees. Kolda and Bader’s survey (see Section 4) is a natural reference point.
- Clarify terminology: what you call “mode-rank” is commonly referred to as “multilinear rank.” Note in the introduction that there are multiple notions of tensor rank (e.g., CP rank vs. multilinear rank) and specify which you target.
- Briefly discuss computational complexity for the proposed optimization problems and algorithms (per-iteration cost, dependence on tensor dimensions and ranks, and any acceleration or proximal strategies used).

Incoherence discussion:
- After equation (10), you describe an “incoherence” condition meaning that energy is spread across components and modes (no component or mode has excessively large singular values). You equate this to the incoherence assumptions in [1,3,10]. This comparison is misleading. In [1,3,10], incoherence typically concerns alignment of singular vectors with the standard basis and is used in sparse+low-rank recovery settings, whereas your noise is not sparse and your condition differs in nature. Please either remove this comparison or clearly articulate the distinctions and the precise relationship, if any.

Typos and notational issues:
- The superscripted component notation \hat{W}^{(k)} is confusing. Consider using a different symbol or index scheme (e.g., \hat{W}_k or \hat{W}[k]) to avoid conflating superscripts with exponents.
- Notation for k-mode rank is inconsistent: both \bar{r}_k and \underline{r}_k appear. Use a single convention, or introduce a clear function such as mult_rank(T) or r_k^{(ml)} for the k-th multilinear rank.
- Supplementary material, last line of page 12: a parenthesis is missing.
- Abstract: change “result for structures Schatten” to “results for structured Schatten.”

Summary:
The paper provides a compelling convex approach to Tucker-style tensor decomposition via the overlapped Schatten norm, along with nontrivial recovery guarantees. With stronger motivation through a standard latent-variable model, clearer connections to established tensor methods (HOSVD and variants), precise statements and complete proofs (especially for Theorem 2 and Lemma 5), a brief complexity analysis, and corrected terminology/notation, the work will be significantly clearer and more broadly accessible.
