- Paper studies convex, norm-regularized methods for factoring approximately low-rank tensors, comparing latent Schatten norm to overlapped norm and deriving MSE bounds under several noise models.  
- Shows theoretical advantages for the latent-norm estimator over existing analyses for the overlapped norm, consistent with prior empirical results, and supports findings with simulations.  
- Introduces a generalized family of structured Schatten norms (including overlapped and latent) and proves a duality result for this class.  
- Paper is generally clear and appears technically sound, though some supplementary proofs were not fully checked.  
- Main concern: narrow, technical scope with only peripheral connection to mainstream ML and no evaluation on ML tasks or benchmarks.  
- Main concern: incremental contribution—primarily strengthens theory for an existing method [25]; techniques and results not seen as especially novel or surprising.  
- Smaller issue: provides only upper bounds without lower bounds, making comparisons and claims of superiority potentially unreliable.  
- Additional: abstract wording is misleading about novelty; generalized norms are introduced but not used algorithmically or empirically.  
- Additional: Figure 1 appears too early and is hard to interpret before background; several minor wording/typo corrections suggested.  
- Overall: technically sound theoretical error bounds for the latent-norm method, but contributions feel incremental and may have limited impact for NIPS.
