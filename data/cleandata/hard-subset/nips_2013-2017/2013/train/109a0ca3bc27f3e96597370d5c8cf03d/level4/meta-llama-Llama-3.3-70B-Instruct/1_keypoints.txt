- Applies convex norm-regularized optimization to approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the "latent" norm vs the "overlap" norm.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, favorable compared to overlap norm analysis.  
- Validates theoretical findings with numerical simulations.  
- Introduces a generalized form of both norms and proves a duality theorem for this generalization.  
- Manuscript is well-structured and appears technically sound, though supplementary proofs were not examined in detail.  
- Results will interest researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions.  
- Scope is narrow and mainly technical, with only peripheral connections to machine learning, limiting potential NIPS impact.  
- Authors do not explore or test the approaches within concrete machine learning problems, despite claiming relevance.  
- Contributions are somewhat incremental and techniques are not especially novel; primarily provide additional theory for an existing approach [25].  
- Important omission of lower bounds to complement the provided upper bounds, weakening comparative claims.  
- Abstract may misleadingly imply a new class of structured Schatten norms, whereas the generalization is brief and unused for algorithms or experiments.  
- Figure 1 is placed early and lacks necessary context before section 2.  
- Minor editorial issues and phrasing errors are noted (specific line corrections provided).  
- Primary contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor decomposition, but impact at NIPS may be limited.
