This paper presents a theoretical analysis of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm. The main contribution is the derivation of performance bounds that improve upon existing results for methods using the overlapped Schatten norm. A key theoretical tool developed to achieve this is a novel dual characterization of the latent and overlapped Schatten norms.

**Quality and Significance**

The paper addresses the important problem of tensor decomposition, which has numerous applications in machine learning and data analysis. The theoretical contributions are sound and represent a clear advance in the understanding of convex relaxations for this problem. The improvement over existing bounds for the overlapped Schatten norm is a significant result. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly elegant and is likely a result of independent interest to the community working on structured sparsity and tensor methods.

The primary weakness of the paper is its limited experimental validation. The experiments are presented briefly and lack sufficient detail for a thorough evaluation of the method's practical performance. To strengthen the paper, the authors should consider the following points:

1.  **Experimental Details:** The setup for the experiments is not fully described. For instance, how were the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) for the synthetic examples chosen and what impact does this choice have on performance? Providing more details would improve the reproducibility of the results.

2.  **Comparison with Baselines:** The evaluation is missing crucial comparisons that would help contextualize the proposed method's performance. A comparison against established non-convex tensor decomposition methods is essential to demonstrate the practical advantages or trade-offs (e.g., in terms of recovery accuracy versus computational cost) of this convex approach.

3.  **Comparison with other Regularizers:** It would be valuable to compare the latent Schatten norm regularization with other convex approaches that promote low-rank structure. For example, how does it compare to group regularization using norms like the l_1,∞ norm, particularly in settings where the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes?

**Clarity**

The paper is well-organized, and the technical arguments are presented clearly. The progression from the problem setup to the theoretical results is logical and easy to follow.

**Originality**

The main original contribution is the theoretical analysis, particularly the duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. While convex relaxations for tensor decomposition have been studied before, the specific bounds and the theoretical machinery used to derive them are novel.

**Summary**

This is a strong theoretical paper that makes a valuable contribution to the literature on convex tensor decomposition. The improved performance bounds and the novel duality characterization are significant and well-presented. However, the paper would be substantially strengthened by a more comprehensive and detailed experimental section that includes comparisons to relevant non-convex and alternative convex baselines. While the theoretical results are compelling, the lack of thorough empirical validation currently limits the paper's overall impact.
