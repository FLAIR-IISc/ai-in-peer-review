This paper investigates convex norm-regularized optimization methods for the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors. The work centers on a comparison between the recently introduced "latent" norm from [25] and the previously established "overlap" norm. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared-error for the latent norm under various noise models. These bounds show an advantage over existing analyses for the overlap norm, providing a theoretical justification for the superior empirical performance of the latent norm approach reported in prior work. These theoretical findings are further corroborated by numerical simulations. Finally, the paper introduces a generalization of these two norms and proves a corresponding duality theorem.

The manuscript is well-written and the technical arguments appear sound, though the supplementary proofs were not verified in detail. The findings could be valuable for researchers working on optimization techniques for tensor decomposition. My primary concerns are as follows:

(i) The contributions are highly specialized and technical, making them somewhat tangential to the core interests of the machine learning community. This may limit the paper's overall impact on the NIPS audience. Although the authors correctly note the relevance of tensor factorization to machine learning, the paper makes no effort to connect the analyzed methods to specific machine learning applications, either through theoretical analysis or empirical evaluation.

(ii) The work appears to be somewhat incremental. Its main contribution is to provide theoretical support for the method in [25], whose strong empirical performance was already established. From this perspective, the results are not especially surprising. Furthermore, the analytical techniques employed do not seem to represent a significant departure from existing methods.

A more minor limitation is the absence of corresponding lower bounds for the derived upper bounds. This weakens the argument for the superiority of one norm over the other, as the comparison relies solely on upper bounds which may not be tight.

Additional Comments
-----------------
- The claim in the abstract, "We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...", could be seen as an overstatement. The paper's analysis is almost entirely dedicated to two existing norms (overlapped and latent). The generalization is only briefly introduced in Section 2.1 with a proof of its dual norm (Lemma 1), but no algorithms based on it are proposed, analyzed, or experimentally tested.
- Figure 1 is placed in the introduction but cannot be interpreted by the reader until the background material from Section 2 is presented. It would be more effective if moved to a later section.
- Line 38: "Tensor is" could be rephrased as "Tensor modelling is".
- Line 40: A comma is missing after "Conventionally".
- Line 395: Suggest changing "also in such situations" to "in such situations as well".

In summary, this paper provides a technically sound analysis with theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor decomposition method. However, the contributions are arguably incremental and their specialized nature may limit the paper's appeal to the broad NIPS readership.
