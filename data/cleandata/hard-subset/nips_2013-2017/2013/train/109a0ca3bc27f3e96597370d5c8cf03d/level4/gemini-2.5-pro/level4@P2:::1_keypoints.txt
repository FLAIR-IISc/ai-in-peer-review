- Paper analyzes convex low-rank tensor decomposition, focusing on the "latent" structured Schatten-norm approach and comparing it to the "overlapped" method.
- Main theoretical result: mean-squared error for the latent approach scales with the minimum mode-k rank, explaining its empirical advantage when only one mode is low-rank.
- Introduces a generalized class of structured Schatten norms that unifies the overlapped and latent norms and proves a novel duality (Lemma 1).
- Numerical simulations validate the predicted MSE scaling and support the theoretical analysis.
- Strength: technically sound, clearly written, and presents an elegant, solid proof of the norm duality.
- Strength: rigorously explains why the latent approach outperforms the overlapped method in certain rank-structure regimes.
- Strength: experiments are reproducible and well-designed to confirm theoretical claims.
- Weakness: narrow, highly technical focus with limited connection to broader machine learning applications, reducing NIPS appeal.
- Weakness: contribution is incremental—a theoretical follow-up to prior empirical work—and relies on adapted existing proof techniques.
- Weakness: no lower bounds are provided, so optimality of the derived rates and the fundamental gap between methods remain unconfirmed.
- Suggestion: abstract overstates novelty—paper analyzes existing norms within a unified framework rather than proposing new algorithms.
- Suggestion: Figure 1 is hard to interpret in the introduction and should be moved or given a more detailed caption.
- Minor writing edits suggested for clarity and grammar (three line-specific fixes noted).
