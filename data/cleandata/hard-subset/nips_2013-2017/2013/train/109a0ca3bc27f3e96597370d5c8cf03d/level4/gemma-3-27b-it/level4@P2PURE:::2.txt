Additional comments after the rebuttal: 
To broaden the paper’s accessibility to the machine learning community, the authors should more clearly motivate the problem. Specifically, they should present the latent variable model from which this tensor decomposition arises. For example, consider a model where latent variables *h<sub>1</sub>* connect to observed variables *x<sub>1</sub>*, *h<sub>2</sub>* to *x<sub>2</sub>*, and so on, with a linear relationship from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the *h<sub>i</sub>*'s. In this framework, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, where its multilinear rank corresponds to dim(*h<sub>1</sub>*), dim(*h<sub>2</sub>*), etc. Including this discussion, ideally with a corresponding graphical model illustration, would be beneficial. The authors should also emphasize that the paper’s key advantage lies in the possibility that only some latent variables have small dimensions, while others do not.

The paper presents a convex-optimization approach to tensor decomposition, utilizing structured Schatten norms. The authors demonstrate that employing the overlapped Schatten norm yields improved reconstruction guarantees. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and represents a significant contribution. Addressing a few technical and notational points will further enhance its readability.

*Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:* 
The statement of Theorem 2 is technically imprecise; the recovered components are only determined up to a permutation, which the authors should acknowledge. 
The derivation from equation (12) to (13) in Theorem 2 requires further justification. 
The proof of Lemma 5 is currently missing, yet it is crucial for the proof of Theorem 2. 
While straightforward, explicitly clarifying the proof of Lemma 4, rather than referring to another paper, would improve the paper’s self-containedness. 

*Minor issues:* 
The authors should expand the related work section to include discussion of HOSVD and other established tensor decomposition methods. Specifically, mentioning how higher-order SVD and its variants provide approximation bounds for tensor decomposition (as reviewed by Kolda, section 4) would be valuable.
The authors’ use of “mode-rank” for the tensor rank should be clarified by noting its equivalence to “multilinear rank” and acknowledging the existence of different tensor rank definitions in the introduction.
A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods would be informative.
The discussion of incoherence following equation (10) requires clarification. The authors interpret incoherence as requiring energy to be spread across mixture components and modes – that is, no component or mode should have excessively large singular values. The claim that this is equivalent to the incoherence conditions used in [1,3,10] is questionable. The cited works utilize incoherence for sparse-plus-low-rank recovery, whereas the noise here is not sparse. Moreover, the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10] relate to the incoherence between singular vectors and basis vectors, differing from the condition presented in this paper. Removing this comparison is recommended.

*Typos/notational issues:* 
The superscript notation for components,  \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}, is potentially confusing; an alternative symbol is suggested. 
The use of both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k to denote the k-mode rank is inconsistent and should be rectified. The authors could introduce a notation like `mult_rank(tensor)` to clearly denote the multilinear rank.
A parenthesis is missing in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material.
Abstract: "result for structures Schatten" should be changed to "structured."

Overall, the paper is well written and offers a novel contribution by finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm, accompanied by recovery guarantees. However, some technical clarifications are needed, and addressing these points should strengthen the paper.
