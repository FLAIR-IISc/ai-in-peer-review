- Paper compares convex tensor decomposition methods using structured Schatten norms, focusing on the "latent" vs "overlapped" approaches.  
- Introduces a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  
- Shows latent approach error bounds scale with the minimal mode-k rank of the true tensor, unlike overlapped which depends on average rank.  
- Resolves an empirical gap from Tomioka et al. (2014) explaining why latent outperforms overlapped when only one mode is low-rank.  
- Provides tight denoising guarantees under Gaussian noise.  
- Empirical experiments validate theoretical predictions, showing MSE scaling with latent rank complexity (Figure 2).  
- Strength: Theoretical contributions are robust and well-motivated, with Lemma 1 generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Strength: Theorems 2–3 give sharp, practically insightful error bounds.  
- Strength: Empirical validation precisely confirms predicted scaling behavior.  
- Originality: Establishes a novel connection between tensor decomposition and structured sparsity and gives the first theoretical explanation for latent’s empirical advantage.  
- Significance: Results directly inform practitioners (e.g., neuroimaging, collaborative filtering) by favoring latent approach in noisy settings due to minimal-rank dependence.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized, technically precise, with clear definitions and reproducible supplementary material.  
- Weakness: Lacks concrete ML motivation and would benefit from a graphical model example linking multilinear rank to latent variable dimensions.  
- Weakness: Theorem 2 needs clarification that recovered components are determined only up to permutation.  
- Weakness: The derivation step from (12) to (13) requires explicit justification (singleton decomposition argument).  
- Weakness: Lemma 5 is referenced without proof and should be included to bolster rigor.  
- Weakness: Incoherence condition (10) is compared misleadingly to Agarwal et al. (2012) and needs clarification about its purpose.  
- Weakness: Should contrast structured norms with standard tensor methods (e.g., HOSVD) and use the standard term "multilinear rank" instead of "mode-rank."  
- Pro acceptance: Resolves a long-standing empirical gap with tight, interpretable theory and a significant minimal-rank error bound advance.  
- Con acceptance: Missing ML context and minor technical gaps need fixes but do not undermine core contributions.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested minor revisions to address clarity and technical details.  
- Revision 1: Add a graphical-model example paragraph to motivate tensor decomposition in ML and explain multilinear rank relevance.  
- Revision 2: Clarify Theorem 2 to state component recovery is up to permutation and that bounds hold for the optimal permutation.  
- Revision 3: Justify the (12)→(13) step by explaining the singleton decomposition and replacement by min_k r_k.  
- Revision 4: Remove or correct the incoherence comparison with Agarwal et al., and explain condition (10) as bounding singular values to prevent component interference.  
- Revision 5: Define "multilinear rank" early and cite Kolda & Bader (2009) for HOSVD context.  
- Overall: Technically excellent paper that will be impactful once accessibility and minor technical clarifications are addressed.
