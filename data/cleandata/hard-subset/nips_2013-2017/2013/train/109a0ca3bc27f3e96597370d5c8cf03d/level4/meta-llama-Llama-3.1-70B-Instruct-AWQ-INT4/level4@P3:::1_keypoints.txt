- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the "latent" norm to the previously used "overlap" norm.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models and shows they compare favorably to overlap-norm analysis.  
- Confirms theoretical predictions with numerical simulations.  
- Introduces a generalization of the norms and proves a duality theorem for that generalization.  
- Paper is easy to follow and appears technically sound.  
- Results are mainly of technical interest and likely useful to researchers on tensor optimization methods.  
- Contributions are narrow and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting potential impact at NIPS.  
- Does not evaluate or analyze the approaches in the context of concrete machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally.  
- Results appear incremental and the analytical techniques are not particularly novel.  
- Upper bounds are provided without matching lower bounds, making algorithmic comparisons potentially fragile.  
- Abstract is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms; generalization is only briefly defined and not developed into algorithms or experiments.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 appears too early and is uninterpretable without later background; several minor typos/phrasing errors noted.  
- Strengths summarized: thorough latent-norm analysis, useful theoretical bounds, and confirming simulations.  
- Weaknesses summarized: narrow ML relevance, lack of ML experiments, incremental novelty, and missing lower bounds.  
- Acceptance arguments: provides rigorous analysis and useful bounds for a recently proposed approach.  
- Rejection arguments: limited ML relevance/impact, incremental work, and absence of lower bounds for comparisons.
