This paper considers convex norm-regularized optimization approaches for factorization of approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on a recently proposed "latent" norm approach [25], and its comparison to a previously used "overlap" norm approach. Specifically, the paper derives theoretical bounds on the "latent" norm mean-squared-error behavior under several noise assumptions, which compare favorably to the existing analysis on the overlap norm, supporting its better empirically-observed performance in a previous paper. The theory is also confirmed via some numerical simulations. Finally, the paper considers a generalization of both these norms, and proves a duality theorem for it. 

The paper is generally easy to follow, and appears to be technically sound (although I haven't checked the proofs in the supplementary material carefully). The results may be useful for researchers focusing on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. My main concerns are that 
(i) The results are narrow and of a technical nature, only peripherally related to machine learning, and likely to have limited impact at NIPS. While the authors justify that tensor factorizations can arise in machine learning, the paper does not attempt to examine the proposed approaches in the context of machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally. 
(ii) The results seem somewhat incremental, and I did not find them to be particularly surprising (admittedly this is a subjective notion). They provide additional theoretical backing to a specific recently proposed approach [25], which was already argued to perform well empirically. The techniques used also do not seem particularly novel. 
A minor weakness is the lack of corresponding lower bounds. This is potentially problematic, as the upper bounds are used to compare different algorithms and argue for the superiority of one over the other. 

Additional Comments:
----------------- 
- The abstract begins with "We study a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...". However, this is slightly misleading. The paper primarily analyzes two existing approaches (overlapped and latent). The generalization is only introduced in Section 2.1, along with the proof of its duality (Lemma 1). No particular algorithms are proposed based on this generalized Schatten norm, their behavior is not analyzed, nor are they tested experimentally. 
- Figure 1 is presented in the introduction, but is not fully interpretable without the background provided in Section 2.

- Line 40: Missing comma after "Conventionally" 
- Line 395: "also in such situations" -> "in such situations as well"

The paper’s main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition approach. The paper appears technically sound, but the results are somewhat incremental and may have limited impact at NIPS.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of a recently proposed tensor decomposition technique (the latent approach) that has shown promising empirical results.
*   The duality result for structured Schatten norms is a novel theoretical contribution.
*   The numerical simulations confirm the theoretical predictions.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly explains the technical details.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The results are narrow in scope and may not be of broad interest to the NIPS community.
*   The results are somewhat incremental and do not represent a major breakthrough in the field.
*   The paper lacks a connection to real-world machine learning applications.
*   The absence of lower bounds weakens the comparison between different approaches.
