This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, including the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, and their application to convex tensor decomposition. The authors rigorously study the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition, establishing its consistency and analyzing its denoising performance. The paper also presents a novel duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which is of independent interest.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow for readers familiar with the topic. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the background and motivation for the work, and the technical sections are well-structured and easy to understand.

The main contribution of the paper is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which is a significant result that improves upon existing work in the field. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition, including its consistency and denoising performance. The experimental results presented in the paper are interesting and provide evidence for the effectiveness of the latent approach.

However, the experimental section could be improved by providing more details on the setup and methodology used. For example, it would be helpful to know how the Tucker ranks of the examples were chosen and how the group regularization was implemented. Additionally, it would be interesting to see a comparison of the proposed method with non-convex approaches.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides significant contributions to the field of tensor decomposition. The theoretical results are interesting, and the experimental results provide evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed method.
