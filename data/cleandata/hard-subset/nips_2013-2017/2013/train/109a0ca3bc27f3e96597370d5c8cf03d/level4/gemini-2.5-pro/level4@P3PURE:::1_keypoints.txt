- Paper derives theoretical mean-squared error bounds for the latent norm in approximate low-rank tensor factorization and compares them favorably to overlap-norm results.  
- Numerical simulations support the theoretical findings and help explain prior empirical superiority of the latent norm.  
- Authors introduce a generalization of both norms and prove a duality theorem (Lemma 1).  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound, though proofs in the supplement were not fully checked by the reviewer.  
- Scope is narrow and highly technical, with limited direct relevance or impact for the broader NIPS/machine-learning audience.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental: theoretical justification for a method ([25]) already known to work well empirically.  
- Analytical techniques appear standard for the area rather than novel.  
- Missing lower bounds: absence of complementary lower bounds weakens claims about the latent norm’s superiority.  
- Claim of proposing a “new class of structured Schatten norms” is misleading since the generalization is only briefly used for a duality result without algorithms or experiments.  
- Figure 1 is poorly placed/unclear in the introduction and would be more effective later with context.  
- Minor writing/typo issues: suggested rewordings at lines 38, 40, and 395.
