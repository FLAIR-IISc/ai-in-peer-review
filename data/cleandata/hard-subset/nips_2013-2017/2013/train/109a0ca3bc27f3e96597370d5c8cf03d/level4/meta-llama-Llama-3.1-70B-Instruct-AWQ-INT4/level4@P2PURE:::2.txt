Additional comments after the rebuttal:

To increase the paper's accessibility to a broader audience, the authors should provide a more thorough motivation for the problem in the context of machine learning. Furthermore, they should explicitly define the latent variable model, ideally accompanied by a graphical representation, that utilizes tensor decomposition to learn parameters. For instance, consider a model where latent variable h1 is connected to observed variable x1, h2 to x2, and so on, assuming a linear relationship between hidden and observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the hi's. In this scenario, the observed moment tensor exhibits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank is equivalent to the dimensions of h1, h2, etc. The authors should incorporate this discussion and preferably include an illustration of the model. Additionally, they should highlight the possibility that only some hidden variables may have low dimensions, not all, which was a primary advantage of the new result presented in the paper.

The paper explores convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors employ the overlapped Schatten norm and demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving better reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well-written and makes significant technical contributions. Addressing a few technical and notational issues will enhance the paper's readability.

*Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:

The statement of Theorem 2 is technically inaccurate, as the components are only recovered up to a permutation. Although a minor issue, the authors should acknowledge this.

In Theorem 2, the transition from (12) to (13) requires justification.

The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, which is a crucial component of Theorem 2.

It would be beneficial to explicitly clarify the proof of Lemma 4, rather than referring to another paper, despite its straightforward nature.

*Minor issues:

The authors should provide more related work on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods, highlighting how higher-order SVD and its variants yield approximation bounds for tensor decomposition (see Kolda's review on tensor decompositions, section 4).

The authors should note that the notion of rank they use, referred to as mode-rank, is also known as multilinear rank. They can mention this and acknowledge the existence of different notions of tensor rank in the introduction.

A brief discussion on the computational complexity of the employed methods would be beneficial.

The authors should clarify their discussion on incoherence after (10). They use the term to imply that the "energy" should be spread across different mixture components and modes, ensuring no component or mode has an excessively high singular value. However, this is not equivalent to the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], which are used for sparse+low rank recovery. The conditions in [1,3,10] involve singular vectors being incoherent with basis vectors, differing from the condition presented in this paper. It is suggested to remove this comparison.

*Typos/notational issues:

The superscript notation for components \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)} is confusing. Consider using an alternative symbol for components.

The notation \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k, both used for k-mode rank, should be changed. Introducing mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank could be a solution.

In the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material, some parentheses are missing.

The abstract should be corrected to read "result for structured Schatten" instead of "result for structures Schatten."

The paper is well-written and makes novel contributions in terms of finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm, providing guarantees for recovery. However, some technical clarifications are needed, which will hopefully be addressed by the authors.
