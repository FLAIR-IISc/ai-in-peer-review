- Main contribution: derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm in convex norm-regularized tensor factorization and compares them favorably to overlap-norm bounds.  
- Supplemental experiments: numerical simulations confirm the theoretical findings.  
- Additional theoretical result: proposes a generalized class of structured Schatten norms and proves a duality lemma.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and arguments are generally easy to follow.  
- Strength: technical results appear sound and useful for researchers on tensor optimization.  
- Weakness: narrow scope and limited direct relevance or demonstrated implications for broader machine learning applications.  
- Weakness: contribution feels incremental, mainly providing theoretical backing for an empirically known method using standard techniques.  
- Weakness: lacks matching lower bounds, making comparisons of upper bounds between norms potentially inconclusive.  
- Clarity issue: abstract is misleading by implying a new norm class is a main contribution when the paper mainly analyzes existing norms.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 is placed too early and is hard to interpret before background is introduced.  
- Minor corrections: suggested wording fixes at Lines 38, 40, and 395.  
- Conclusion: technically sound but incremental and narrowly focused, potentially limiting impact at NIPS due to lack of lower bounds and application relevance.
