- Paper studies convex tensor decomposition using overlapped vs latent structured Schatten norms and establishes a duality between them.
- Provides statistical guarantees for the latent approach: consistency, deterministic error bounds under an incoherence-like condition, and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds.
- Key theoretical insight: latent regularization error scales with the minimum mode rank, while overlapped regularization scales with an average of ranks.
- Empirical results validate theoretical scaling and show latent often outperforms overlapped, even for jointly low-rank tensors.
- Connects convex tensor methods to structured sparsity (overlapped vs latent group lasso) and complements prior overlapped-norm and HOSVD/Tucker results.
- Strength: clear, unified framework and useful duality linking overlapped and latent formulations.
- Strength: interpretable theoretical guarantees showing latent adapts to the smallest mode rank without prior knowledge.
- Strength: strong empirical validation aligning with theoretical predictions.
- Strength: insightful connection to structured sparsity literature, broadening impact.
- Request: better motivate the ML setting via a latent-variable generative model and include a graphical depiction.
- Clarification request: state component identifiability explicitly (recoverable up to permutation).
- Clarification request: justify the step from equation (12) to (13), e.g., by using a singleton decomposition aligned with the smallest mode rank.
- Proof fixes: include the missing proof of Lemma 5 (central to Theorem 2) and add a brief/self-contained proof sketch for Lemma 4 rather than only citing prior work.
- Related work: expand discussion of HOSVD/Tucker methods and position guarantees relative to non-convex approaches (cite Kolda & Bader).
- Notation: clarify rank terminology early (call mode-k rank multilinear rank and note other rank notions like CP rank).
- Computational: discuss optimization complexity and scalability (e.g., ADMM per-iteration cost dominated by SVDs on mode-k unfoldings and how K/dimensions affect runtime/memory).
- Incoherence: refine comparison to prior incoherence notions, explain the distinct role of the paper’s spectral-norm bounds on cross-mode unfoldings, and avoid claiming equivalence to standard incoherence.
- Minor fixes: disambiguate superscript/subscript notation for components, unify r̄_k/r̲_k notation for mode-k ranks, fix missing parenthesis in supplement, and correct “structures Schatten” → “structured Schatten” in abstract.
- Pros for acceptance: novel duality, sharp interpretable guarantees, validated empirical benefits, and bridging of convex tensor and structured sparsity frameworks.
- Cons/concerns: insufficient ML motivation and figure, ambiguities/gaps in Theorem 2 and supporting lemmas, limited discussion of computational scalability, and overstated incoherence comparisons.
- Overall: technically solid and well-written paper that, with stronger motivation, clarified proofs, refined incoherence discussion, and improved notation, would be ready for publication.
