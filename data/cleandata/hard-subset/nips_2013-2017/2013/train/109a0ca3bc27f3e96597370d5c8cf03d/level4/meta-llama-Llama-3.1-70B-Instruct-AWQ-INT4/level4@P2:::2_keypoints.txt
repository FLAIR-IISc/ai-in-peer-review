- Proposes a convex tensor decomposition method using structured Schatten norms and analyzes the latent approach.  
- Shows the latent approach performs as well as knowing the mode with smallest rank and provides a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms.  
- Positions work relative to prior tensor decomposition and Schatten-norm regularization literature, and links to structured sparsity; builds on analysis of the overlapped approach ([26]).  
- Strength: novel and significant contribution to tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical analysis of the latent approach, including consistency and a deterministic error bound.  
- Strength: numerical simulation corroborates the theoretical scaling behavior.  
- Weakness: motivation in a machine-learning context is weak; needs a latent-variable model and graphical illustration of applications.  
- Weakness: notation and terminology are inconsistent/confusing (e.g., superscripted components, mode-rank vs. multilinear rank).  
- Weakness: missing proof of Lemma 5 and Lemma 4’s proof needs clarification.  
- Pro-accept: novelty, solid analysis, and supporting simulations justify acceptance.  
- Con-accept: paper needs clearer motivation, improved notation, and complete proofs before publication.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 7/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address motivation, notation, and missing proofs.
