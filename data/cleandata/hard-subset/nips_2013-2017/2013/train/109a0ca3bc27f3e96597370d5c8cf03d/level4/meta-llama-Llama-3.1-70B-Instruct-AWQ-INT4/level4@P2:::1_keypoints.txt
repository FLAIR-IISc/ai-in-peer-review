- Paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the "latent" norm versus the "overlap" norm.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable comparison to overlap norm.  
- Numerical simulations support and confirm the theoretical results.  
- Paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound.  
- Results are likely useful for researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions.  
- Contribution to the broader machine learning community is limited; approaches are not examined in ML problem contexts.  
- Results appear incremental and the techniques do not seem particularly novel.  
- Upper bounds are provided without accompanying lower bounds, which weakens comparative claims.  
- Abstract is misleading by claiming a new class of structured Schatten norms while mainly analyzing two existing approaches.  
- The generalized Schatten norms are only defined and their dual shown; no algorithms or analyses are proposed or tested for them.  
- Several minor grammatical errors are noted (e.g., "Tensor is" → "Tensor modelling is", missing comma after "Conventionally", awkward phrasing on line 395).  
- Main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor approach, but impact may be limited for NIPS acceptance.
