This paper presents theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm employing latent Schatten norm regularization, achieving an improvement over existing results utilizing the overlapped Schatten norm. 

**Quality:** The paper’s primary contribution lies in establishing theoretical guarantees for tensor decomposition under latent Schatten norm regularization. The dual characterization of both the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a particularly noteworthy and interesting result. However, the experimental evaluation feels somewhat cursory. Further detail regarding experimental setup would strengthen the findings; specifically, the method used to select Tucker ranks (r<sub>1</sub>,…r<sub>k</sub>) for the test examples warrants explanation. Moreover, the paper would benefit from a discussion of how group regularization techniques, such as l<sub>1</sub> or l<sub>∞</sub> norms, behave when a tensor exhibits low-rank structure simultaneously across all modes. Finally, a comparison to the performance of non-convex tensor decomposition methods would provide valuable context for assessing the proposed approach.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and readily understandable. The authors present their arguments in a clear and logical manner.

**Originality:** The central novel contribution of this work is the established duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This result appears to be of independent theoretical interest, extending beyond the specific application to tensor decomposition. 

**Significance:** Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with broad applicability across numerous fields. This paper represents a significant contribution by improving upon existing theoretical results for this problem. The theoretical findings are compelling, and the paper is generally well-written. While the experimental section is somewhat limited in scope, the overall theoretical advancements presented justify considering this a valuable piece of work. The improved bounds offered by this approach have the potential to inform and advance the development of practical tensor decomposition algorithms.
