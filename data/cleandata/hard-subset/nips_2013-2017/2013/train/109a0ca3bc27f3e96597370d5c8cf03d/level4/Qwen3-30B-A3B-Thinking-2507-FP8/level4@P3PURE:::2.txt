

Additional comments after the rebuttal:  
The authors need to significantly strengthen the problem motivation for machine learning applications to broaden the paper's accessibility. Specifically, they must introduce a latent variable model (with a graphical representation) demonstrating how the proposed tensor decomposition learns parameters. Consider a model where latent variables \(h_1, h_2, \dots\) connect to observed variables \(x_1, x_2, \dots\) via linear mappings, with arbitrary dependencies among the \(h_i\). In this setting, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank equals \(\dim(h_1), \dim(h_2), \dots\). The authors should add this discussion, preferably with a diagram illustrating the model, and explicitly note that only *some* hidden variables may have small dimensions—a key advantage enabling their new result.  

The paper presents a technically significant contribution via convex optimization for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, with the overlapped Schatten norm yielding improved reconstruction guarantees. The writing is clear, but minor technical and notational refinements are needed for greater readability.  

*Clarifications on Theorem 2 and proofs:*  
- Theorem 2’s statement is technically incorrect: components are recovered only up to permutation. This must be explicitly stated.  
- The claim that (12) implies (13) requires justification.  
- Lemma 5 (critical to Theorem 2) lacks a proof and must be provided.  
- Lemma 4’s proof should be clarified explicitly (rather than deferring to another paper), even if straightforward.  

*Minor technical and presentation improvements:*  
- Expand related work to include HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods, citing Kolda’s review (Section 4) for approximation bounds.  
- Clarify terminology: the "mode-rank" used is standardly called "multilinear rank." The introduction should note the existence of multiple tensor rank definitions.  
- Briefly address computational complexity of the proposed methods.  
- Revise the incoherence discussion after (10). The authors claim their condition matches [1,3,10], but this is incorrect: those works use incoherence for *sparse+low-rank recovery* (involving singular vector coherence with basis vectors), whereas here the noise is not sparse. The conditions are fundamentally different—this comparison should be removed.  

*Notational and typographical corrections:*  
- Replace confusing superscript notation \(\hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}\) for components with a distinct symbol.  
- Standardize rank notation: replace inconsistent \(\bar{r}_k\) and \(\underline{r}_k\) (for \(k\)-mode rank) with a unified \(\text{mult\_rank}(\mathcal{T})\).  
- Correct "result for structures Schatten" to "structured Schatten" in the abstract.  
- Fix missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 (supplementary material).  

The paper makes a novel contribution by leveraging an overlapped Schatten norm for Tucker decomposition with recovery guarantees. Addressing the above points will substantially improve its technical rigor and clarity.
