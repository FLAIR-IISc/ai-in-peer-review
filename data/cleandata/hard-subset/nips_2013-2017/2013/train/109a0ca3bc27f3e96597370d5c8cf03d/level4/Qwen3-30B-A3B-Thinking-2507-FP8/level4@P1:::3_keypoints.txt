- Compares convex tensor decomposition using overlapped vs latent Schatten norms.  
- Primary contributions: (1) novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms; (2) theory showing latent MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank.  
- Duality result generalizes structured sparsity literature and is an elegant theoretical contribution.  
- Analysis explains empirical superiority of latent approach when the tensor is nearly full-rank in all but one mode.  
- Builds on prior convex tensor decomposition work ([25,26]) and provides the first theoretical justification for latent’s empirical performance.  
- Extends prior noiseless tensor completion results by addressing the practical noisy setting.  
- Theoretical quality is strong: Lemma 1 is significant and Theorems 1–3 give clear, structure-aware performance bounds.  
- Experiments validate theoretical predictions, showing MSE scales with the predicted complexity measures.  
- Experimental section is brief and lacks detailed implementation and parameter-selection descriptions.  
- Does not compare the latent convex approach to non-convex tensor methods (e.g., HOOI) on real-world datasets.  
- Writing and organization are excellent; notation and exposition are precise and accessible.  
- Originality: the duality insight is novel and deepens understanding of overlapped vs latent approaches.  
- Significance: results have practical implications across applications and show latent behaves as if it knew the smallest-rank mode.  
- Minor improvements suggested: more practical guidance on parameter selection and broader empirical comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — a strong theoretical contribution with compelling implications.
