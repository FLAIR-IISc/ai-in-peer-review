- Presents a theoretical analysis of convex tensor decomposition regularized by the latent Schatten norm.  
- Main contribution: derives improved performance bounds versus methods using the overlapped Schatten norm.  
- Develops a novel dual characterization of the latent and overlapped Schatten norms as a key theoretical tool.  
- Theoretical results are sound and represent a clear advance for convex relaxations in tensor problems.  
- Improvement over existing overlapped Schatten norm bounds is a significant result.  
- Primary weakness: experimental validation is limited and under-detailed.  
- Experimental details lacking: unclear how Tucker ranks were chosen and how that choice affects performance/reproducibility.  
- Missing comparisons to established non-convex tensor decomposition methods to assess practical trade-offs (accuracy vs. cost).  
- Missing comparisons to other convex regularizers (e.g., group norms like l_1,∞) for simultaneously low-rank tensors.  
- Paper is well-organized and technically clear, with a logical progression of results.  
- Overall: strong and novel theoretical contribution (notably the duality result) but weakened by insufficient empirical validation.
