- Provides theoretical performance bounds for convex tensor decomposition regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving on overlapped-norm results and proving a novel duality between the two norms.
- Frames tensor decomposition as important for applications (neuroimaging, collaborative filtering) and contrasts non-convex methods with convex relaxations.
- Highlights limitations of the overlapped Schatten norm when tensors are low-rank in only a subset of modes.
- Focuses on the latent approach and shows it achieves scaling comparable to knowing the mode with the smallest rank.
- Establishes a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms with implications for structured sparsity.
- Supports theoretical findings with numerical simulations that confirm the predicted scaling behavior.
- Technical quality is strong: rigorous analysis and thorough proofs (provided in supplementary material).
- Derivation of the duality is a particularly interesting and potentially impactful result.
- Experimental section is limited: lacks detail on Tucker rank selection for generated tensors.
- Experiments offer limited comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods.
- Missing discussion on how standard group regularization (e.g., l1/∞) behaves when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank in all modes.
- Clarity is good: well-organized, clear writing, consistent notation, and logical argument flow.
- Originality: primary contribution is the duality result; latent-approach analysis is a meaningful advance.
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding and practical performance bounds with broad relevance to high-dimensional data analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions; experimental expansion would strengthen it.
- Recommendation: Accept.
