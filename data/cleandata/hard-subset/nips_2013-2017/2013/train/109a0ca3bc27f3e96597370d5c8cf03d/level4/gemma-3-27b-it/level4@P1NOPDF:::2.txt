Additional comments after the rebuttal:

The authors would significantly improve the paper's accessibility to a broader machine learning audience by more clearly motivating the problem. Specifically, they should provide a latent variable model—illustrated with a graphical model—that demonstrates how this tensor decomposition can learn model parameters. For example, consider a model where a latent variable *h₁* connects to observed variable *x₁*, *h₂* to *x₂*, and so on, with a linear relationship from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependence among the *hᵢ’s*. In this case, the observed moment tensor has a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank is dim(*h₁*), dim(*h₂*), etc. Adding this discussion, and ideally a corresponding figure, would be highly beneficial. The authors should also emphasize that the key advantage of their result is the possibility that only *some* hidden variables have small dimensions, while others do not.

The paper considers convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, employing the overlapped Schatten norm to achieve improved reconstruction guarantees. It is well-written and makes a technically important contribution. Addressing a few technical and notational points will further enhance its readability.

*Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:*

The statement of Theorem 2 is technically incomplete: the recovered components are only identifiable up to a permutation, and the authors should acknowledge this. 

Furthermore, the derivation from equation (12) to (13) in Theorem 2 requires justification.

The proof of Lemma 5 is currently missing, yet it is a crucial component of the proof for Theorem 2.

While straightforward, it would be clearer to explicitly present the proof of Lemma 4 rather than referring to another paper.

*Minor issues:*

The paper would benefit from a more comprehensive overview of related work on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods. The authors could mention how higher-order SVD and its variants yield approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, referencing Kolda’s review (section 4) as a starting point.

The authors’ use of “mode-rank” for the tensor rank is also commonly referred to as “multilinear rank.” They should mention this equivalence and acknowledge the existence of different notions of tensor rank in the introduction.

A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the employed methods would be valuable.

The discussion of incoherence following equation (10) requires clarification. The authors interpret incoherence as requiring the “energy” to be spread across different mixture components and modes, ensuring no component or mode has a dominant singular value. While they claim this is analogous to incoherence conditions in [1, 3, 10], this comparison appears inaccurate. The incoherence concepts in those references are typically used for sparse plus low-rank recovery scenarios, whereas the noise model here is not sparse. Those conditions involve incoherence between singular vectors and basis vectors, which differs from the condition presented in this paper. I recommend removing this comparison.

*Typos/notational issues:*

The superscript notation for components,  \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}, is confusing. A different symbol for components would be preferable.

The use of both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k to denote the k-mode rank is inconsistent and should be revised.  Consider introducing `mult_rank(tensor)` to explicitly denote the multilinear rank.

In the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material, a parenthesis is missing.

Abstract: "result for structures Schatten" should be changed to "structured Schatten."

In summary, the paper is well-written and presents a novel contribution by finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm and providing recovery guarantees. However, some technical clarifications are needed, and I hope the authors will address these points.
