This paper proposes a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors and studies their application to convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition. The authors analyze the performance of the latent approach for tensor decomposition and show that it outperforms the overlapped approach in certain settings. The paper is well-written and makes significant technical contributions.

The paper is closely related to previous work on tensor decomposition and structured sparsity. The authors provide a clear overview of the existing literature and explain how their work builds upon and extends previous results. The technical presentation is sound, and the proofs are thorough and well-organized.

One of the main contributions of the paper is the introduction of a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors show that these norms can be used to regularize tensor decomposition problems and provide guarantees for recovery. The theoretical results are supported by numerical experiments, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.

The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise. The authors provide a good balance between technical details and high-level explanations, making the paper accessible to a broad audience.

Strengths:

* The paper makes significant technical contributions to the field of tensor decomposition and structured sparsity.
* The authors provide a clear and thorough presentation of the technical results.
* The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.
* The numerical experiments provide strong support for the theoretical results.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed approach.
* Some of the notation and terminology could be clarified or improved.
* The authors could provide more context and explanation for the incoherence condition used in the proof of Theorem 2.

Minor issues:

* The authors should add more related work on HOSVD and other methods for tensor decomposition.
* The notion of rank used in the paper should be clarified, and the authors should mention that it is also referred to as multilinear rank.
* The authors should briefly discuss the computational complexity of the methods employed.
* The authors should clarify their discussion about incoherence after equation (10).
* The superscript notation for components \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)} is confusing and should be changed.
* The notation \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k should be changed to mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank.
* The last line of page 12 in the supplementary material is missing some parentheses.
