- Compares convex norm-regularized low-rank tensor factorization approaches, focusing on latent vs. overlapped norms.  
- Derives theoretical error bounds for the latent norm showing MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank rather than the average rank.  
- Provides a theoretical explanation for the empirical superiority of the latent approach and confirms it with numerical simulations.  
- Establishes a duality relationship between the two structured Schatten norms, linking to structured sparsity literature.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and technically sound.  
- Numerical experiments support and validate the theoretical predictions.  
- Shows the latent approach performs as well as if one knew the mode with the smallest rank.  
- Major concern: no engagement with machine learning problems or real datasets, limiting practical ML impact for a NIPS submission.  
- Theoretical bounds are not particularly novel and mainly formalize previously observed empirical results.  
- Techniques used (duality, error bounds) are standard; contribution appears incremental.  
- Lacks lower bounds for error rates, leaving uncertainty about tightness of the results.  
- Presentation issues: abstract overclaims a “new class” of norms though the generalization is minor.  
- Figure 1 is introduced too early and is uninterpretable without Section 2 background.  
- Paper does not clearly distinguish between the “latent” approach and the defined “latent norm,” which may confuse readers.  
- Overall: technically solid but narrowly focused with limited relevance to broader machine learning—marginal fit for NIPS.
