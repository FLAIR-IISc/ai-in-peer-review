- Paper derives theoretical MSE bounds for the "latent" norm tensor factorization and compares them favorably to the "overlap" norm.  
- Numerical simulations confirm the theoretical predictions for the latent norm.  
- Proves a duality theorem for a generalized class of structured Schatten norms.  
- Paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound.  
- Results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting potential impact at NIPS.  
- Paper lacks theoretical or experimental evaluation of the approaches in real ML problems.  
- Results feel incremental and not particularly surprising; techniques are not especially novel.  
- Absence of corresponding lower bounds weakens comparative claims between algorithms.  
- Abstract wording is misleading about introducing a new class of norms; generalization is minor and not experimentally explored.  
- Figure 1 appears prematurely in the introduction and is not interpretable without Section 2 context.  
- Several minor typos/phrasing issues (suggested fixes at Lines 38, 40, 395).  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous analysis of a recently proposed method, novel duality result, simulations match theory, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow scope, incremental contribution, weak ML relevance, and missing lower bounds.
