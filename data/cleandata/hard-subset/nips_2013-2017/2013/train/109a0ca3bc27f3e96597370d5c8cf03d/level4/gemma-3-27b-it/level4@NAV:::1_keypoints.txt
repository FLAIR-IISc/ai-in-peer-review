- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximate low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the latent norm and comparing it to the overlap norm.
- Provides theoretical mean-squared-error upper bounds for the latent norm under various noise conditions.
- Shows these bounds demonstrate advantages over existing analyses of the overlap norm, consistent with prior empirical results.
- Supports theoretical findings with numerical simulations.
- Presents a generalization of both norms and proves a corresponding duality theorem (Lemma 1).
- Paper is generally well-written and appears technically correct, though proofs in the supplement were not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Main concern: results are highly technical and narrowly focused with only a tangential connection to machine learning, lacking ML-theory or ML-experiment evaluations.
- Contributions feel incremental, mainly providing additional theoretical justification for an already empirically validated approach [25] using established techniques.
- Missing lower bounds to complement the presented upper bounds, limiting comparative algorithmic analysis.
- Abstract’s claim of “proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms” is potentially misleading since the paper primarily analyzes existing approaches.
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 needs background from Section 2 to be interpretable; several minor wording and punctuation edits suggested.
