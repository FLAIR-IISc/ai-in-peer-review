This paper explores the application of convex norm-regularized optimization techniques to the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors. The focus is on a recently introduced "latent" norm approach ([25]) and its comparison to the previously utilized "overlap" norm approach. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared-error behavior of the "latent" norm under various noise assumptions, which exhibit favorable performance compared to existing overlap norm analyses. These findings support the superior empirically observed performance of the "latent" norm approach in a prior study. Numerical simulations further validate the theoretical results. Additionally, the paper presents a generalized version of these norms and establishes a duality theorem for it.

The paper is generally well-organized and appears to be technically sound, although a thorough examination of the proofs in the supplementary material is warranted. The results may be of interest to researchers focused on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, several concerns are noted. Firstly, the results are narrow and technical in nature, with only peripheral relevance to machine learning, which may limit their potential impact at NIPS. Although the authors argue that tensor factorizations can arise in machine learning contexts, they do not investigate the proposed approaches in this context, either theoretically or experimentally.

Secondly, the results seem somewhat incremental, and their significance may not be immediately apparent. They appear to provide additional theoretical justification for a recently proposed approach ([25]), which was already demonstrated to be effective empirically. Furthermore, the techniques employed do not appear to be particularly novel. A minor limitation is the absence of lower bounds to accompany the upper bounds, which could be problematic when comparing different algorithms and arguing for the superiority of one over another.

Additional Comments
-----------------

- The abstract's claim of proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors may be misleading. The paper's primary focus is on analyzing two existing approaches (overlap and latent), with the generalization only briefly introduced in section 2.1 and proved in Lemma 1. No specific algorithms are proposed or analyzed based on this generalized Schatten norm, nor are they experimentally tested.

- Figure 1 is presented in the introduction but lacks context without the background information provided in section 2 onwards.

- Minor corrections are suggested: "Tensor is" -> "Tensor modelling is" (line 38), and a comma is needed after "Conventionally" (line 40). Additionally, "also in such situations" -> "in such situations as well" (line 395).

The paper's primary contribution is the derivation of theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition approach. While the paper appears to be technically sound, the results are somewhat incremental and may have limited impact at NIPS.
