- Studies convex, norm-regularized low-rank tensor decomposition focusing on overlapped and latent structured Schatten-norms.  
- Proves a duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, extending structured-sparsity dualities.  
- Provides risk bounds for the latent approach: consistency, deterministic bound under incoherence, and high-probability Gaussian-noise rates.  
- Main message: latent approach error scales with the minimum mode rank, unlike overlapped methods which depend on average mode ranks.  
- Experiments on synthetic data validate predicted scaling and show latent can outperform overlapped even for simultaneously low-rank tensors.  
- Builds on prior convex tensor estimation and structured-sparsity literature, adapting decomposable-regularizer techniques.  
- Strength: clear theoretical contribution with a useful duality result and technically sound analysis.  
- Strength: improved error rates that explain empirical advantages of the latent approach.  
- Strength: practical guidance via regularization choice independent of unknown ranks and interpretable Gaussian-noise rates.  
- Strength: empirical corroboration through simulations matching theory.  
- Weakness: narrow scope—focuses on denoising and synthetic data with limited connection to downstream ML tasks or real benchmarks.  
- Weakness: incremental novelty, mainly adapting established techniques to the latent tensor setting.  
- Weakness: lacks lower bounds or tightness guarantees to complement the upper bounds.  
- Weakness: deterministic bound relies on incoherence/spectral constraint α that is hard to verify or tune empirically.  
- Quality: technically sound proofs (in supplement); would benefit from brief lemma sketches in the main text.  
- Clarity: generally readable but issues include premature Figure 1, overstated “new class” phrasing in abstract, minor writing errors, and need intuition for Lemma 1.  
- Originality: duality is a natural extension—useful but not surprising; performance analysis clarifies when latent is preferable.  
- Significance: valuable for convex tensor estimation researchers but limited broader ML impact without real-data applications.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear theory explaining empirical phenomena, general duality result, and aligned empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited ML relevance, incremental contribution, no lower bounds, and unverified assumptions on α.  
- Suggestions: add real-data or completion experiments, study/justify α empirically, include proof sketches, discuss tightness or relate more to lower bounds, and clarify/extend Sp/q examples.  
- Overall: technically solid and informative for tensor estimation; leans toward rejection for NIPS due to limited ML relevance but could be strong with minor empirical and exposition additions.
