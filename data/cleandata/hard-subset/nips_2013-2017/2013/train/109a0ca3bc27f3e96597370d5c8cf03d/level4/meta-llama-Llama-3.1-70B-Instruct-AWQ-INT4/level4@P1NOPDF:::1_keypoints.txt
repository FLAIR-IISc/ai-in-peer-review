- Paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the "latent" norm to the "overlap" norm and deriving MSE bounds under various noise models.  
- Theoretical results are supported by numerical simulations and a duality theorem for a generalized norm is proved.  
- Paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound (proofs in supplement not checked in detail).  
- Results may be useful to researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions.  
- Main concern (i): Results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to ML, limiting potential impact at NIPS; no ML-theoretic or experimental evaluation of the approaches.  
- Main concern (ii): Results seem incremental and unsurprising, offering additional theoretical backing for an existing approach [25] using techniques that are not particularly novel.  
- Minor defect: Upper bounds are provided without any lower bounds, which undermines algorithm comparisons and claims of superiority.  
- Additional: Abstract is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms—generalization is limited to definition and a dual norm lemma with no algorithms or empirical/theoretical analysis.  
- Additional: Figure 1 is presented too early (in introduction) and is not interpretable without later background.  
- Additional: Several typos/phrasing issues noted (Line 38, Line 40, Line 395).  
- Overall: Main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor decomposition approach; technically sound but incremental and of potentially limited NIPS impact.
