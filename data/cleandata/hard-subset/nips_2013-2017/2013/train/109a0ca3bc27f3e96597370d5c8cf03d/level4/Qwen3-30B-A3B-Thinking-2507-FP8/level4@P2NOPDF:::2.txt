

The paper presents a convex-optimization approach for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, with a focus on the overlapped Schatten norm for improved reconstruction guarantees. While the technical contributions are significant and the writing is generally clear, several revisions are required to enhance accessibility, correctness, and precision. Below are key suggestions:

**Major Revisions Required**  
The paper lacks sufficient motivation for machine learning audiences. The authors must introduce a latent variable model to contextualize the tensor decomposition problem. Specifically, they should present a graphical model where latent variables \(h_1, h_2, \dots\) connect to observed variables \(x_1, x_2, \dots\) via a linear model (e.g., \(x_k = A_k h_k + \epsilon_k\)), with arbitrary dependencies among \(h_i\). In this setting, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank equals \(\dim(h_1), \dim(h_2), \dots\). Crucially, the authors must clarify that their method’s advantage arises when *only some* hidden dimensions are small (not all), which is central to their novel result. A diagram illustrating this model would greatly improve accessibility.  

**Theoretical Clarifications**  
- **Theorem 2**: The statement is incorrect as written; components are recovered *up to permutation*. This must be explicitly stated. Additionally, the transition from (12) to (13) requires justification.  
- **Lemma 5**: The proof is missing but is essential for Theorem 2’s validity. Its inclusion is mandatory.  
- **Lemma 4**: The proof should be self-contained (not deferred to another paper), even if straightforward.  

**Notational and Conceptual Issues**  
- **Incoherence**: The authors compare their condition to [1,3,10], claiming equivalence. This is misleading. Those works address *sparse+low-rank recovery*, where incoherence involves singular vectors being incoherent with basis vectors. Here, noise is non-sparse, and the condition concerns energy spreading across components/modes (not vector incoherence). The comparison should be removed.  
- **Rank Terminology**: The "mode-rank" used is standardly called *multilinear rank*. The introduction should note this and acknowledge alternative tensor rank definitions (e.g., CANDECOMP/PARAFAC rank).  
- **Notation**:  
  - Replace confusing superscript \(\hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}\) with a clearer symbol (e.g., \(\hat{W}_k\)).  
  - Standardize \(r_k\) notation: Replace conflicting \(\bar{r}_k\) and \(\underline{r}_k\) with a unified convention (e.g., \(\text{mult\_rank}(\mathcal{T})\)).  
  - Fix the missing parenthesis in the supplementary material (p. 12, last line).  
- **Abstract**: Correct "result for structures Schatten" → "result for structured Schatten".  

**Minor Improvements**  
- **Related Work**: Expand discussion of HOSVD and tensor decomposition methods. Cite Kolda’s review (Section 4) to contextualize approximation bounds from higher-order SVD.  
- **Computational Complexity**: Briefly address the complexity of the proposed method relative to existing approaches.  
- **Clarity**: In the incoherence discussion (after (10)), explicitly define the intended meaning (energy spreading) to avoid ambiguity.  

**Summary**  
The paper makes a valuable contribution to tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms, with strong theoretical guarantees. However, the current draft suffers from insufficient motivation, a critical gap in the latent variable model, and several technical inaccuracies. Addressing the above points—particularly the latent variable model, Theorem 2’s permutation ambiguity, and the incoherence miscomparison—will significantly strengthen the paper’s impact and clarity. The authors’ rebuttal should explicitly address these issues. With these revisions, the paper will be suitable for publication.
