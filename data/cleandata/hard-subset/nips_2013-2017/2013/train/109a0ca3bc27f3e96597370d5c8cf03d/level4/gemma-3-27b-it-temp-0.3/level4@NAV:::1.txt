This paper investigates convex norm-regularization techniques for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, specifically comparing a recent “latent” norm approach [25] with a previously established “overlap” norm. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared-error performance of the latent norm under various noise conditions, demonstrating improvements over existing analyses of the overlap norm and aligning with previously reported empirical advantages. These theoretical findings are supported by numerical simulations. Furthermore, the paper generalizes both norms and establishes a duality theorem for this generalized form.

The paper is generally well-written and appears technically correct, though a detailed verification of the supplementary material’s proofs was not possible. The results likely hold value for researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, my primary concerns are:

(i) The work presents highly specialized, technical results with a peripheral connection to machine learning, potentially limiting its impact at NIPS. While the authors acknowledge the relevance of tensor factorizations to machine learning, the paper lacks exploration of the proposed methods within the context of actual machine learning problems, both theoretically and empirically.

(ii) The contributions feel somewhat incremental and not particularly surprising. The paper largely provides theoretical justification for a recently introduced approach [25] that was already shown to perform well empirically. The techniques employed do not appear to be novel. 

A further point is the absence of corresponding lower bounds to complement the presented upper bounds. This is a potential concern, as the upper bounds are used for comparative algorithm analysis and to support claims of superiority.

Additional Comments:

- The abstract’s claim of proposing “a new class of structured Schatten norms” is potentially misleading. The paper primarily *analyzes* existing approaches (overlap and latent). The generalization appears later, defined in Section 2.1, with Lemma 1 establishing the dual norm. No new algorithms based on this generalized norm are proposed, analyzed, or experimentally evaluated.
- Figure 1, presented in the introduction, requires background information from Section 2 to be properly understood.
- Line 38: Consider changing “Tensor is” to “Tensor modelling is”.
- Line 40: Add a comma after “Conventionally”.
- Line 395: Replace “also in such situations” with “in such situations as well”. 

The paper’s core contribution lies in its theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor decomposition method. While technically sound, the results are somewhat incremental and may have limited impact within the NIPS community.
