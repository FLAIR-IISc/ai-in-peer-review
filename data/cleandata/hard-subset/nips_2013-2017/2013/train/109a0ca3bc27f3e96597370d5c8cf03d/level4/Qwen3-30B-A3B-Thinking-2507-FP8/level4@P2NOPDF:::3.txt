

This paper establishes theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, offering an improvement over existing results using overlapped Schatten norm regularization. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms represents the paper's most significant theoretical contribution, which is of independent interest and strengthens the paper's core novelty.  

The theoretical analysis is sound and well-executed, with clear derivations supporting the main bounds. However, the experimental section lacks sufficient detail to fully assess the method's practical utility. Specifically:  
- The selection criteria for Tucker ranks (r₁, ..., rₖ) in the examples are not justified.  
- The behavior of group regularization (e.g., ℓ₁,∞) when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes requires clarification.  
- A comparative evaluation against non-convex tensor decomposition approaches is missing, which would contextualize the method's empirical advantages.  

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with logical flow from problem formulation to theoretical results. The significance of the work is strong: tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem in machine learning and signal processing, and the improved theoretical guarantees advance the state of the art. While the theoretical contribution is compelling, the experimental shortcomings prevent a fully confident assessment of the method's real-world impact.  

The work is technically solid and addresses an important problem, but the current experimental evaluation does not adequately support the claimed improvements. To strengthen the paper, the authors should: (1) elaborate on Tucker rank selection, (2) provide a thorough comparison with non-convex baselines, and (3) clarify the interplay between group regularization and multi-mode low-rank structure. Without these additions, the empirical validation remains insufficiently detailed to fully endorse the theoretical claims.  

In summary, this is a high-quality theoretical contribution with clear novelty in the duality result. The paper’s significance is enhanced by its improvement over prior work, but the experimental section requires substantial expansion to match the rigor of the theoretical analysis. The authors have made a valuable step toward bridging convex tensor decomposition theory and practice, but the current empirical evidence does not yet fully substantiate the method's practical superiority.
