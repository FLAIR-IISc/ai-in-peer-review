This paper studies convex, norm-regularized optimization methods for factoring approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on the recently proposed latent Schatten norm [25] and compares it to the earlier overlapped norm. The authors derive mean-squared error bounds for the latent-norm estimator under several noise models, showing advantages over existing analyses for the overlapped norm, consistent with previously reported empirical results. These findings are supported by simulations. The paper also introduces a generalized family of structured Schatten norms that includes both approaches and proves a duality result for this class.

The paper is generally clear and appears technically sound (though I did not check all supplementary proofs in detail). The results are potentially useful for researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions.

Main concerns:
(i) Scope and relevance: The contributions are quite narrow and technical, with only peripheral connection to mainstream machine learning. While the motivation that tensor factorizations arise in ML is reasonable, the paper does not examine these approaches on machine learning tasks—neither through task-specific theory nor through empirical evaluation on ML benchmarks.
(ii) Incrementality and novelty: The work primarily strengthens theoretical support for a specific recent method [25] that already has empirical backing. The techniques do not appear especially novel, and the results are not particularly surprising (acknowledging the subjectivity of this assessment).

A smaller issue is that the paper provides only upper bounds without corresponding lower bounds. This can be problematic when using the upper bounds to compare algorithms and argue for superiority, since it is unclear whether the bounds are tight or reflect artifacts of the analysis.

Additional comments:
- The abstract begins with “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...,” which is somewhat misleading. Most of the paper analyzes two existing norms (overlapped and latent). The generalization is introduced in Section 2.1 and its dual norm identified (Lemma 1), but no algorithms based on the generalized norms are proposed, analyzed, or tested experimentally.
- Figure 1 appears in the introduction but is difficult to interpret before the background in Section 2 is presented.
- Line 38: “Tensor is” → “Tensor modeling is.”
- Line 40: add a comma after “Conventionally.”
- Line 395: “also in such situations” → “in such situations as well.”

Overall, the main contribution is a set of theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition method based on the latent norm. The paper appears technically sound, but the contributions feel incremental and may have limited impact for NIPS.
