### Review

**Additional comments after the rebuttal:**

To make the paper more accessible to a wider machine learning audience, the authors should provide stronger motivation. For instance, they could introduce a latent variable model for which this tensor decomposition method is suited to learn the parameters. For example, consider a generative model where a latent variable h1 influences an observed variable x1, h2 influences x2, and so on, with a linear mapping from latent to observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the latent variables (h_i). In such a model, the moment tensor of the observed variables admits a Tucker decomposition where the multilinear rank is determined by the dimensions of the latent variables (dim(h1), dim(h2), ...). The authors should add this discussion, perhaps with a graphical model to illustrate it. This would effectively highlight a key advantage of the proposed method: its ability to handle cases where only a subset of the latent variables are low-dimensional.

---

This paper introduces a convex optimization framework for tensor decomposition based on a new class of structured Schatten norms. Specifically, it analyzes the "latent" Schatten norm, contrasting it with the previously proposed "overlapped" Schatten norm. The authors provide theoretical guarantees demonstrating the superior performance of the latent approach, particularly when the underlying tensor is low-rank in only a single, unknown mode.

The paper is well-written and presents significant technical contributions. However, addressing the following technical and notational points would further improve its readability and impact.

**Clarifications regarding Theorem 2 and its proof:**

*   The statement of Theorem 2 should be clarified: the components Ŵ(k) are only recovered up to a permutation of the true components W*(k). While this is a minor point, it should be explicitly mentioned.
*   The paper claims in Theorem 2 that inequality (13) follows from (12). This step requires a more detailed justification.
*   The proof of Lemma 5, which is a key component in the proof of Theorem 2, appears to be missing from the supplementary material.
*   For completeness, it would be preferable to include the proof of Lemma 4 explicitly in the supplementary material rather than citing another paper, even if the proof is straightforward.

**Minor issues:**

*   **Related Work:** The discussion of related work could be expanded. For instance, the authors should discuss how Higher-Order SVD (HOSVD) and its variants provide approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, citing relevant surveys such as Kolda and Bader's review (Section 4).
*   **Terminology:** The paper uses the term "mode-k rank." It would be helpful to mention in the introduction that this is also known as "multilinear rank" and that other notions of tensor rank exist.
*   **Computational Complexity:** A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed optimization algorithms would be a valuable addition.
*   **Incoherence Assumption:** The discussion of the incoherence assumption following equation (10) requires clarification. The authors state that this assumption is similar to the incoherence conditions used in [1, 3, 11] for sparse + low-rank matrix recovery. However, this comparison seems misplaced. The conditions in [1, 3, 11] relate the singular vectors to the standard basis vectors to handle sparse noise, whereas the condition here appears to ensure that the "energy" of the signal is spread across different mixture components and modes. Given that the noise model in this paper is not sparse, the connection is unclear. The authors should either clarify this analogy or remove the comparison.

**Typos and notational issues:**

*   The superscript notation Ŵ^(k) for the components is potentially confusing, as superscripts are also used for unfoldings (e.g., W(k)). Using a different symbol for the components would improve clarity.
*   The notation for ranks (e.g., r_k and r̄_k) could be made clearer. Please ensure the definitions are distinct and the notation is used consistently throughout the paper.
*   A parenthesis appears to be missing in the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material.
*   In the abstract, "result for structures Schatten" should be corrected to "structured Schatten."

**Summary:**

Overall, this is a well-written paper that makes a novel and important contribution to convex tensor decomposition. By analyzing the "latent" Schatten norm and providing strong theoretical guarantees for its performance, the authors make a compelling case for its superiority over the "overlapped" approach in certain regimes. However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by addressing the technical and presentational issues outlined above. I am optimistic that the authors can address these points in a revision.
