- Paper analyzes convex tensor decomposition with a latent Schatten-norm regularizer (tensor as sum of components low-rank in specific modes) versus the overlapped Schatten-norm approach.
- Main theoretical contributions: a novel duality between overlapped and latent structured Schatten norms.
- Derived denoising performance bound showing MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the true tensor.
- Explains theoretically why the latent approach can outperform overlapped when tensor ranks are highly anisotropic (bound depends on minimum, not average, rank).
- Theoretical originality and depth: the duality result is clean and of independent interest.
- Clarity of insight: provides a compelling theoretical justification for empirically observed benefits of the latent approach.
- Significance: advances understanding of convex relaxations for tensor decomposition; paper is well-written.
- Limited experimental scope: experiments mainly confirm scaling laws and do not fully demonstrate practical relevance.
- Missing baseline comparisons: no comparison to standard non-convex methods (e.g., alternating least squares) to assess accuracy and computational trade-offs.
- Lack of experimental detail (e.g., how multilinear ranks r1,r2,r3 were chosen) reduces reproducibility.
- Limited exploration of other Sp/q norms (analysis focuses on S1/1); testing norms like S1/∞ could strengthen the work.
