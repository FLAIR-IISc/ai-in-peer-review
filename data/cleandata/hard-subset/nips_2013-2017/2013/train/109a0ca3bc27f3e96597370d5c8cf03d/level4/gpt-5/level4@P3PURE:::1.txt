This paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization for factorizing approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on the recently proposed “latent” norm approach [25] and compares it to the previously used “overlap” norm. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared error of the latent norm estimator under several noise models; these bounds compare favorably to existing analyses of the overlap norm and help explain the previously observed empirical advantages reported in [25]. The theory is supported by numerical simulations. The paper also proposes a generalization encompassing both norms and proves a duality result for this generalized class.

The paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound (though I have not carefully checked the proofs in the supplementary material). The results may be useful to researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions.

Main concerns:
(i) The contributions are narrow and primarily technical, with only peripheral connection to core machine learning questions, which may limit impact at NIPS. While the authors make a reasonable case that tensor factorizations arise in ML, the paper does not investigate the proposed approaches in concrete machine learning settings, either theoretically (e.g., learning-theoretic implications) or experimentally (e.g., performance on ML tasks).
(ii) The results feel somewhat incremental and unsurprising. They mainly provide additional theoretical support for a specific, recently proposed method [25] that was already shown empirically to perform well. The techniques also do not appear particularly novel.

A further concern is that the paper presents only upper bounds, with no corresponding lower bounds. Using only upper bounds to compare algorithms can be problematic, as it may overstate superiority without matching lower limits.

Additional comments:
- The abstract begins with “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...,” which is somewhat misleading. The bulk of the paper analyzes two existing approaches (overlapped and latent). The generalization is introduced in Section 2.1, and the main result for it is the characterization of its dual norm (Lemma 1). No specific algorithms are proposed based on these generalized norms, their behavior is not analyzed, and they are not evaluated experimentally.
- Figure 1 appears in the introduction but is difficult to interpret without the background developed in Section 2 onward. Consider moving it or adding explanatory context earlier.
- Line 38: “Tensor is” → “Tensor modeling is” (or “Tensor modelling is”).
- Line 40: Add a comma after “Conventionally,”.
- Line 395: “also in such situations” → “in such situations as well”.

Overall assessment: The main contribution is a set of theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition approach. The paper appears technically sound, but the contribution seems incremental and its likely impact at NIPS may be limited.
