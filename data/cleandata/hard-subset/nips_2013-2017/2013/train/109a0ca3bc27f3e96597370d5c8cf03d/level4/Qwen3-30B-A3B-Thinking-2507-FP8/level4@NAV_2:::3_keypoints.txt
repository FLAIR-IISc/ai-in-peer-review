- Studies structured Schatten norms for tensors, focusing on the "latent" convex tensor decomposition approach versus the "overlapped" approach.  
- Establishes a novel duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing structured-sparsity results.  
- Provides theoretical denoising guarantees showing MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank (not the average rank).  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous, well-motivated, and accompanied by proofs.  
- Experimental section is brief and lacks detail on setup and choice of Tucker ranks (r1,...,rK).  
- No comparison to non-convex tensor decomposition methods is provided.  
- Needs more analysis of group regularization (e.g., l1,∞-norm) when the tensor is low-rank in all modes.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with technical material and proofs in supplements.  
- Original contribution is the duality result, which is of independent interest beyond tensor decomposition.  
- Significance: provides theoretical justification for latent approach outperforming overlapped, and shows latent can match knowing the smallest-rank mode.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but experiments should be expanded for a stronger submission.
