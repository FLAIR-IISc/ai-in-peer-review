- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing a recent “latent” norm to the previously used “overlap” norm.
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, showing improvements over existing overlap-norm analyses.
- Numerical simulations validate the theoretical findings.
- Introduces a generalization of both norms and proves a corresponding duality lemma.
- Paper is generally well-written and appears technically sound (supplementary proofs not fully verified by reviewer).
- Contributions are specialized and largely theoretical with a weak connection to practical machine learning, limiting likely impact at NIPS.
- Results feel incremental and unsurprising, largely providing theoretical justification for an already empirically promising method; techniques are not novel.
- Missing lower bounds to complement the presented upper bounds, which undermines algorithm-comparison claims.
- Abstract’s claim of proposing a “new class” of structured Schatten norms is misleading since the generalization is brief and not developed into algorithms or experiments.
- Figure 1 is hard to interpret without the background from later sections.
- Minor editorial/typo issues (suggested fixes at lines 38, 40, and 395).
