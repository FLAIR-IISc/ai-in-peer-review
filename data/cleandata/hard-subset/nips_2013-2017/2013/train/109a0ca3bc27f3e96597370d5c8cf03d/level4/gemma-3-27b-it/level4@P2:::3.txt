This paper investigates the theoretical performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm utilizing the latent Schatten norm as a regularizer, demonstrating improvements over existing results obtained with the overlapped Schatten norm. A key contribution is the establishment of a duality between these two norms, which is of independent interest.

**Quality:** The paper presents sound theoretical results concerning tensor decomposition with the latent Schatten norm. The derived dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly noteworthy. However, the experimental evaluation feels somewhat limited. Details regarding the selection of Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) for the examples would strengthen the analysis. Additionally, a discussion of how group regularization methods (e.g., l1,∞ norms) perform when the tensor exhibits simultaneous low-rank structure across all modes would be valuable. Finally, a comparison to non-convex decomposition approaches would provide further context for the results.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the concepts accessible to the reader. The notation is consistent, and the mathematical arguments are presented in a logical fashion.

**Originality:** The primary original contribution lies in the demonstrated duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This result appears to be a valuable addition to the field, potentially impacting related areas beyond tensor decomposition. While convex tensor decomposition has been explored previously (e.g., [9, 15, 23, 25, 26] cited in the paper), the theoretical analysis provided here advances the understanding of the latent approach.

**Significance:** Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with applications in numerous fields. This paper's improvement upon existing results for this problem suggests significant potential impact. The theoretical findings are compelling, and the paper is well-written. Expanding the experimental section would further enhance the paper’s significance.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper provides a novel theoretical analysis of the latent Schatten norm for tensor decomposition.
*   The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a valuable contribution in its own right and generalizes previous work on structured sparsity [2, 13, 17, 21].
*   The theoretical results are clearly stated and appear to be rigorously proven.
*   The paper addresses an important problem with broad applicability.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The experimental evaluation is somewhat brief and lacks detailed discussion of parameter selection and comparison to alternative methods (especially non-convex ones).
*   While the theoretical results are strong, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the practical implications of the findings.
*   The paper does not explore connections to related areas such as compressed sensing or matrix completion in detail, which could strengthen the broader impact.
