The paper considers convex-optimization based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors employ the latent Schatten norm and demonstrate, both theoretically and empirically, that it leads to better reconstruction guarantees compared to the previously proposed "overlapped" approach, particularly when the underlying tensor is low-rank in only a few modes. The paper is well written and makes technically important contributions. Addressing a few technical and notational issues will enhance the paper’s readability.

**Summary:**

The authors study a class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, including those recently proposed for convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition (“overlapped” and “latent” norms). They theoretically analyze the “latent” approach, which has been empirically shown to outperform the “overlapped” approach in certain settings. They demonstrate that this is indeed the case, showing that the latent approach performs as well as knowing the mode with the smallest rank when the true tensor is low-rank in a specific, unknown mode. A novel duality result for structured Schatten norms is also presented, which is of broader interest in the context of structured sparsity. The theory is validated through numerical simulations, demonstrating accurate prediction of the mean squared error scaling.

**Detailed Review:**

This paper addresses the important problem of tensor decomposition with a focus on convex relaxation techniques. The motivation for using convex optimization over non-convex methods is clearly stated, and the challenges of model selection (specifying ranks for each mode in the Tucker decomposition) are well highlighted. The introduction provides good context and relates the work to existing research in areas like neuroimaging and collaborative filtering.

The paper’s main contribution lies in the theoretical analysis of the “latent” approach to tensor decomposition. The key result – demonstrating that the latent approach’s error scales with the minimum mode-k rank – provides a compelling explanation for its empirical success. The duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is also a valuable contribution, connecting this work to the broader field of structured sparsity.

**Strengths:**

*   **Theoretical Rigor:** The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for the latent approach, offering guarantees on its performance.
*   **Clear Explanation:** The core idea behind the latent approach and its advantage over the overlapped approach are clearly explained and supported by theoretical analysis.
*   **Novelty:** The duality result for structured Schatten norms is a novel contribution with potential for broader impact.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The numerical results confirm the theoretical predictions and demonstrate the effectiveness of the latent approach.
*   **Well-Written:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written, making it accessible to researchers familiar with tensor decomposition and convex optimization.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Motivation for Machine Learning:** The paper would benefit from a stronger motivation of the problem within a machine learning context. The authors should consider including a discussion of a latent variable model (potentially with a graphical model representation) where this tensor decomposition could be used to learn parameters. For example, a model where latent variables connect to observed variables linearly, and dependencies among latent variables exist. In such a scenario, the observed moment tensor has a Tucker decomposition, and the multilinear rank relates to the dimensions of the latent variables. It’s important to highlight that only *some* latent variables might be low-dimensional, providing a strong justification for the latent approach.
*   **Clarification of Theorem 2:** The statement of Theorem 2 requires clarification. The authors should explicitly state that component recovery is only guaranteed up to a permutation.
*   **Justification for Equation Linking (12) and (13):** The transition from inequality (12) to (13) in Theorem 2 needs further justification. A brief explanation of how the minimal mode-k rank is obtained would be helpful.
*   **Missing Proof:** The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, and this lemma is a crucial part of Theorem 2.
*   **Proof Clarity:** While straightforward, clarifying the proof of Lemma 4 explicitly, rather than simply referring to another paper, would improve readability.
*   **Related Work:** Expanding the related work section to include more discussion on Higher-Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and other established methods for tensor decomposition (e.g., referencing Kolda’s review on tensor decompositions) would strengthen the paper.
*   **Notion of Rank:** The authors should acknowledge the different notions of tensor rank (e.g., multilinear rank) and explicitly state which one they are using, particularly in the introduction.
*   **Computational Complexity:** A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods would be valuable.
*   **Incoherence Discussion:** The connection drawn between the incoherence condition used in this paper and that in [1, 3, 10] is questionable. The authors should reconsider this comparison, as the incoherence conditions in those papers typically apply to sparse+low-rank recovery scenarios with non-sparse noise, whereas the current setting differs. The term "incoherence" should be clarified to mean spreading energy across mixture components and modes, avoiding the implication of a specific incoherence condition related to singular vectors.

**Minor Issues:**

*   The superscript notation for components (e.g.,  \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}) is potentially confusing. Consider using an alternative notation.
*   The use of both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k for the k-mode rank is inconsistent. Introducing a clear notation like `mult_rank(tensor)` could improve clarity.
*   There appears to be a missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material.
*   The abstract contains the phrase "result for structures Schatten"; this should be "structured Schatten".

**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   Strong theoretical foundation for the latent approach.
*   Novel duality result with potential for broader impact.
*   Empirical validation of the theoretical results.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   Lack of a strong machine learning motivation.
*   Minor technical clarifications needed in Theorem 2 and associated proofs.
*   Some inconsistencies in notation and terminology.
*   Limited discussion of related work.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The theoretical analysis and empirical validation are convincing, and the paper is generally well-written. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above would significantly improve the paper and make it even more impactful. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing the identified issues.
