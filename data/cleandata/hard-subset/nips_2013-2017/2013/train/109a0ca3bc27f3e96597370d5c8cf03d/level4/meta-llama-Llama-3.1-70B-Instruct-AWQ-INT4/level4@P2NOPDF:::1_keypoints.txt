- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximate low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the "latent" and "overlap" norm approaches.
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise assumptions.
- Theoretical bounds favorably compare to existing analysis of the overlap norm.
- Numerical simulations support the theoretical findings and help explain the empirical superiority of the latent norm.
- Presents a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for the generalized class.
- Paper is well-organized, easy to follow, and technically sound.
- Relevance to machine learning is limited and the paper only peripherally connects tensor factorizations to ML applications.
- Contributions appear incremental, building on a recently proposed approach with limited novelty in techniques.
- Absence of lower bounds weakens the comparative claims based solely on upper bounds.
- Presentation issues: abstract may be misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms.
- Other presentation problems: Figure 1 is introduced prematurely and there are minor typos and punctuation errors.
