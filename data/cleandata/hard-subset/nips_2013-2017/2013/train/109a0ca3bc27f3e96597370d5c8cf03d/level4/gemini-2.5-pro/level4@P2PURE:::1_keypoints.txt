- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the latent and overlap norms.
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error upper bounds for the latent norm under various noise assumptions.
- Shows latent-norm bounds are more favorable than existing overlap-norm bounds, providing theory for prior empirical advantages.
- Provides numerical simulations that corroborate the theoretical findings.
- Introduces a generalization of both norms and proves a corresponding duality theorem.
- Paper is well-written and appears technically sound, pending verification of supplementary proofs.
- Scope is highly technical and narrow, with no evaluation within concrete machine-learning problems, limiting broader NIPS impact.
- Results seem incremental and not particularly surprising; analytical techniques are not a major departure from prior work.
- Lacks corresponding lower bounds, making it difficult to assess the tightness of the upper bounds and the claimed superiority.
- Abstract’s opening sentence is misleading about proposing a new class; the generalization is only briefly introduced (definition + one lemma).
- Figure 1 is hard to interpret in the introduction because required background appears later.
- Minor editorial issues: suggested wording changes and a missing comma at specific line references.
