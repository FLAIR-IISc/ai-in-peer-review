- Paper compares convex tensor decomposition approaches, focusing on the latent norm vs. the established overlapped norm.  
- Theoretical result: latent approach MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, unlike overlapped which depends on average rank.  
- Establishes a duality relationship between the two structured Schatten norms and connects to structured sparsity literature.  
- Empirical simulations validate the theoretical scaling behavior of the MSE.  
- Generalizes the norms and proves a novel duality result (Lemma 1).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and mathematically consistent.  
- Theoretical contributions are technically sound and adapt existing analysis effectively.  
- Numerical experiments (Section 4) effectively confirm theoretical predictions.  
- Strength: provides rigorous theoretical explanation for the latent approach’s superior empirical performance.  
- Limitation: claimed "new class of structured Schatten norms" is overstated—paper mainly analyzes two existing approaches without new algorithms.  
- Limitation: generalized norms lack empirical validation and no new methods are proposed.  
- Limitation: weak connection to practical machine learning applications or datasets, limiting NIPS relevance.  
- Limitation: theoretical analysis lacks lower bounds, relying only on upper bounds for comparisons.  
- Limitation: contributions are somewhat incremental; techniques largely adapt prior work and novelty is limited.  
- Clarity issue: Figure 1 is presented without sufficient context, making it hard to interpret early on.  
- Overall judgment: solid theoretical and empirical work but narrow scope and limited ML relevance make it a marginal acceptance candidate.
