- Paper analyzes convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing "latent" and "overlap" norms.  
- Authors derive theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models.  
- Theoretical results favorably compare to existing analysis of the overlap norm and support prior empirical findings.  
- Numerical simulations validate the theoretical findings.  
- Paper introduces a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for this generalized norm.  
- Paper is well-organized and appears technically sound (proofs in supplement not fully checked).  
- Main contribution is narrowly technical and may have limited impact for a ML-focused venue like NIPS.  
- Lack of exploration or experiments linking the approaches to concrete machine learning problems.  
- Results seem incremental and not particularly surprising, mainly justifying a previously proposed approach [25].  
- Techniques used do not appear especially novel.  
- Upper bounds are provided but no lower bounds, weakening comparative claims of superiority.  
- Abstract wording is slightly misleading about proposing a new class of norms; generalization is introduced later but not used algorithmically.  
- Figure 1 is unclear when presented in the introduction without later background.  
- Minor editorial issues/typos noted (specific line edits suggested).
