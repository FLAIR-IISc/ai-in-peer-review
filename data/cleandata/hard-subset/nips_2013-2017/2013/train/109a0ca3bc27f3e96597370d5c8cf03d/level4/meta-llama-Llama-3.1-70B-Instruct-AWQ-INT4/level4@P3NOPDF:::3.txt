This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon existing results that utilize the overlapped Schatten norm for regularization.

Quality: The paper's primary contribution lies in its theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. Notably, the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is an interesting aspect of the work. However, the experimental section is somewhat brief and lacks elaboration on several key points. For instance, the authors do not provide details on how the Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) of the examples were chosen. Additionally, it would be beneficial to discuss how group regularization, such as l_1,\infty, performs when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes. A comparison of the proposed method with non-convex approaches would also be a valuable addition. Addressing these points would strengthen the paper's overall quality.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clear to understand, making it accessible to readers familiar with the subject matter.

Originality: The main original result in the paper is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. This finding appears to be of independent interest and contributes to the paper's novelty.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with numerous applications, and this paper's improvement upon existing results makes it a significant work. The theoretical results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. However, the brevity of the experimental section detracts from the overall impact of the paper. Nevertheless, the authors' contributions to the field of tensor decomposition are noteworthy, and the paper is a valuable addition to the existing literature.
