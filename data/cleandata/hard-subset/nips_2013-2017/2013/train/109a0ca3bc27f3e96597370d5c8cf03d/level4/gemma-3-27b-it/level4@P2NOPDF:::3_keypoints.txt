- Main contribution: provides theoretical performance bounds for convex tensor decomposition using latent Schatten norm regularization, improving over overlapped Schatten norm results.  
- Notable theoretical result: establishes the dual characterization between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, of independent interest.  
- Experimental evaluation: feels cursory and limited in scope, weakening empirical support for the claims.  
- Experimental detail needed: clarify how Tucker ranks (r1,…,rk) were selected for the test examples.  
- Missing comparison: should compare against non-convex tensor decomposition methods to contextualize performance.  
- Additional analysis requested: discuss behavior of group regularizers (e.g., l1, l∞) when a tensor is low-rank across all modes.  
- Clarity: paper is well-structured and arguments are presented clearly and logically.  
- Originality: the duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a novel and meaningful theoretical contribution.  
- Significance: advances theoretical understanding of tensor decomposition with improved bounds that could inform practical algorithm development despite limited experiments.
