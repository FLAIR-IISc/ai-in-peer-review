- Studies convex norm-regularized methods for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the latent norm to the overlapped norm and deriving theoretical MSE bounds under several noise models.  
- Numerical simulations corroborate the theoretical findings showing favorable performance of the latent norm.  
- Introduces a generalization that encompasses both norms and proves a duality result for the generalized Schatten norms.  
- Paper is generally clear and appears technically sound (supplementary proofs not fully verified by reviewer).  
- Main concern: contributions are narrow, largely technical, only tangentially connected to machine learning, so likely limited impact at NIPS without application to concrete ML settings.  
- Secondary concern: results feel incremental and not particularly surprising; techniques do not seem especially novel.  
- Missing lower bounds to complement the provided upper bounds, which weakens algorithm-comparison claims.  
- Abstract wording is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms; the paper mainly analyzes existing approaches and does not develop algorithms or empirical evaluation for the generalization.  
- Figure 1 appears too early and is not interpretable without Section 2 background.  
- Minor typos/wording issues noted (Line 38, Line 40, Line 395).  
- Overall: main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recent method; technically sound but incremental with limited anticipated impact.
