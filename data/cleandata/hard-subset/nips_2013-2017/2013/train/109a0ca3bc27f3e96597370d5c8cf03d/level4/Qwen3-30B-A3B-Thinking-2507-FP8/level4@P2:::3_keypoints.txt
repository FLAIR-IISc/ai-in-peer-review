- Paper studies structured Schatten norms for convex tensor decomposition and compares "overlapped" vs "latent" approaches.  
- Establishes a duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Shows latent approach MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, while overlapped scales with the average mode-k rank.  
- Theoretical results are rigorously proven (Lemma 1, Theorems 1–3) and explain prior empirical observations.  
- Numerical experiments confirm the predicted scaling behavior.  
- Significant technical contribution: elegant duality result extending structured sparsity to tensors.  
- Addresses a gap in literature by providing theoretical justification for latent approach's superiority.  
- Experimental section is underdeveloped: no details on how Tucker ranks (r1,…,rK) were chosen for synthetic data.  
- Experiments lack comparisons to common non-convex methods (e.g., ALS, HOOI), reducing practical relevance.  
- The optimal regularization λ is shown independent of unknown ranks, but practical λ-selection guidance is insufficient.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and helpful figures.  
- Originality: duality result is novel and has independent interest beyond tensor decomposition.  
- Significance: results have immediate practical implications, recommending latent approach for noisy tensor decomposition.  
- Main weaknesses: incomplete experimental comparisons and insufficient exploration of cases favorable to the overlapped approach.  
- Overall recommendation: strong theoretical contribution with recommendation for acceptance, contingent on improving experimental rigor.
