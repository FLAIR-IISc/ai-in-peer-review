Summary
This paper studies convex, norm-regularized approaches to low-rank tensor decomposition, focusing on two structured Schatten-norm formulations: the overlapped norm (penalizing all mode-unfoldings jointly) and the latent norm (decomposing the tensor into a sum of mode-specific low-rank components). The authors present a general framework of structured Schatten norms and prove a duality between overlapped Sp/q and latent Sp*/q* norms, extending analogous dualities known for structured sparsity (e.g., group lasso with overlaps). The main technical contribution is a set of risk bounds for the latent approach in a tensor denoising setting: a general consistency result, a deterministic bound under an incoherence-type condition, and high-probability rates for Gaussian noise. The key message is that the latent approach achieves error scaling with the minimum mode rank of the ground-truth tensor, in contrast to prior results for the overlapped approach whose complexity depends on an average of mode ranks. Experiments on synthetic data support the predicted scaling and show cases where the latent approach outperforms the overlapped counterpart, even when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank.

Relation to prior work: The overlapped formulation and its analysis have appeared in earlier work on convex tensor completion and estimation (e.g., Gandy et al., Liu et al., Signoretto et al., Tomioka et al.). The present analysis for the latent formulation builds on techniques from high-dimensional M-estimation with decomposable regularizers (Agarwal et al., Negahban et al.) and connects to structured sparsity literature (Jenatton et al., Obozinski et al.). The comparison to the lower bounds in Mu et al. is relevant and helps situate the results.

Strengths
- Clear theoretical contribution: The duality between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is clean and useful, and the resulting analysis for the latent approach is technically sound and well-motivated.
- Improved error rates: The bounds that scale with the minimal mode rank provide a compelling explanation for empirical observations that the latent approach can outperform the overlapped approach, especially when the tensor is low-rank in a single mode.
- Practical guidance: The choice of regularization independent of unknown ranks is appealing. The Gaussian-noise rates and consistency results are easy to interpret.
- Empirical corroboration: Simulations validate the predicted scaling with both problem size and latent rank.

Weaknesses
- Scope and significance for NIPS: The paper is largely technical and focused on a denoising setup with synthetic data. The connection to downstream machine learning tasks (e.g., supervised learning with tensor features, real-world completion/recommendation benchmarks) is not explored, which may limit impact for the NIPS audience.
- Incremental novelty: The techniques (duality, decomposable regularizers, spectral norm control) closely follow established methods. The main novelty is in adapting this machinery to the latent tensor setting and showing favorable scaling.
- No lower bounds: The paper uses upper bounds to compare latent and overlapped methods but does not provide matching lower bounds or tightness guarantees in the denoising setting.
- Assumptions and constraints: The deterministic bound relies on additional spectral norm constraints (incoherence via α) that may be hard to verify or tune in practice; guidance on α and its empirical effect is limited.

Quality
- Technical soundness appears solid; the bounds are derived with appropriate dual norm control and decomposability arguments. The comparison to the overlapped approach is fair and references prior analyses and lower bounds. Proofs are in the supplement; a brief sketch of key lemmas (beyond statements) in the main text would aid readability.

Clarity
- The paper is generally well-organized and readable. However:
  - Figure 1 is introduced in the introduction before the reader has the necessary background; consider moving it to Section 2 or adding more explanation early.
  - The abstract emphasizes a “new class” of structured Schatten norms; while accurate, the paper primarily analyzes two existing norms and establishes their duality. Consider clarifying that the main contributions are duality and performance analysis rather than proposing new algorithms.
  - Minor writing issues:
    - “Tensor is” → “Tensor modelling is”
    - Add a comma after “Conventionally,”
    - “also in such situations” → “in such situations as well”
  - Provide intuition for Lemma 1 (duality) in the main text.

Originality
- The duality result is a natural extension of known structured-sparsity dualities. The adaptation to tensors and connection between overlapped/latent Schatten norms are useful but not surprising. The performance analysis clarifies when the latent approach is preferable, which is meaningful for this niche.

Significance
- For researchers working on convex methods for tensor estimation, the results are valuable and likely to be cited. However, the lack of applications to realistic ML tasks and datasets may reduce broader impact at NIPS.

Pros and cons for acceptance
Pros:
- Clear, correct theory explaining an empirical phenomenon (latent vs. overlapped).
- General duality result likely useful beyond this specific application.
- Empirical results align with theory.

Cons:
- Limited connection to ML applications and real data.
- Incremental relative to existing convex tensor literature.
- No lower bounds or tightness results in the presented setting.
- Additional assumptions (incoherence via α) not fully justified empirically.

Suggestions for improvement
- Provide empirical studies on real datasets or on tensor completion with missing entries to demonstrate relevance to ML problems.
- Discuss the practical selection and impact of α in the constrained formulation; consider experiments varying α.
- Include a brief proof sketch for core lemmas to improve accessibility.
- Add discussion on tightness: relate to known lower bounds more extensively or provide problem instances showing near-optimality.
- Give concrete examples or algorithms leveraging the generalized Sp/q norms beyond the 1/1 case, or clarify why such extensions are primarily conceptual here.
- Reorganize Figure 1 or add explanatory captions to make it self-contained in the introduction.

Overall assessment
A well-executed, technically sound paper that advances understanding of convex tensor decomposition by clarifying when latent Schatten-norm regularization is advantageous. Its primary impact is theoretical and methodological within tensor estimation. Given the limited connection to ML tasks and incremental nature, I lean toward rejection for NIPS, but with relatively minor additions (stronger empirical validation, clearer positioning, and discussion of tightness), this work would be strong for a venue focused on optimization or signal processing.
