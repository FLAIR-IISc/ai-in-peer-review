

This paper investigates structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, specifically comparing the overlapped and latent approaches. The authors provide theoretical analysis showing that the latent approach performs better when the underlying tensor is low-rank in a specific unknown mode, with performance scaling according to the minimal mode-k rank rather than the average rank as with the overlapped approach. The paper demonstrates a novel duality between these structured norms, which connects to broader structured sparsity literature. The authors confirm their theoretical predictions through numerical experiments showing precise scaling behavior of mean squared error.

The paper makes a technically important contribution by rigorously analyzing the latent approach for tensor decomposition, providing performance guarantees that explain its empirical superiority over the overlapped approach. The theoretical results are sound and well-motivated, with clear connections to existing work in structured sparsity. The paper is well-written and makes a novel contribution to the field of tensor decomposition.

*Technical clarifications needed:*
The statement of Theorem 2 is technically incorrect as the components are only recovered up to permutation, which should be explicitly mentioned. The derivation from inequality (12) to (13) requires justification. The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, and this lemma is crucial for Theorem 2. The proof of Lemma 4 should be clarified explicitly rather than relying on reference to another paper.

*Minor issues:*
The authors should add a more detailed discussion of the latent variable model where tensor decomposition applies, including a graphical model representation showing how hidden variables connect to observed variables with linear relationships. The paper should explicitly address cases where only some hidden variables have small dimensions, which is a key advantage of the latent approach. The authors should expand on related work concerning HOSVD and tensor decomposition methods, particularly referencing Kolda's review of tensor decompositions (Section 4). The paper should clarify the tensor rank terminology, noting that "mode-rank" is also referred to as "multilinear rank" and mention the existence of different tensor rank definitions. A brief discussion of computational complexity would be valuable. The incoherence condition after equation (10) should be clarified to explain that it requires energy to be spread across mixture components and modes, rather than comparing to sparse+low-rank recovery conditions in [1,3,10] which are not directly relevant.

*Notational issues:*
The superscript notation for components (e.g., \hat{W}^{(k)}) is confusing and should be changed. The use of both \bar{r}_k and \underline{r}_k for mode-k rank should be standardized, with the recommendation to introduce a notation like mult_rank(tensor) for multilinear rank. A missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material needs correction. The abstract contains a typo: "result for structures Schatten" should be "structured Schatten."

The paper makes a strong contribution to tensor decomposition with valuable theoretical guarantees, but requires addressing these technical and notational clarifications before publication. The work is significant as it provides a comprehensive theoretical explanation for the empirical success of the latent approach, which has practical implications for tensor-based machine learning applications.
