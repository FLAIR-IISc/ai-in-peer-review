- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing "latent" and "overlap" norms.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable comparison to overlap.  
- Empirically validates theoretical claims with numerical simulations.  
- Introduces a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for the generalized structured Schatten norm.  
- Paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound (supplementary proofs not fully checked by reviewer).  
- Results may be useful to researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions.  
- Main concern: work is narrow, highly technical, only peripherally tied to machine learning, and may have limited impact at NIPS.  
- Paper does not study the proposed approaches in practical machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally.  
- Results seem incremental and not particularly surprising; techniques lack apparent novelty.  
- Minor defect: only upper bounds are provided—no lower bounds—weakening algorithmic comparisons.  
- Abstract wording is slightly misleading about novelty; generalization is introduced but no algorithms or experiments based on it are provided.  
- Figure 1 appears prematurely in the introduction and is not interpretable without section 2 background.  
- Minor editorial/typo suggestions: "Tensor is" → "Tensor modelling is"; add comma after "Conventionally"; rephrase "also in such situations" → "in such situations as well".  
- Main contribution: theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor decomposition approach; technically sound but incremental with limited NIPS relevance.  
- Acceptance arguments: rigorous theoretical analysis, novel duality result, simulations support theory, addresses a challenging tensor problem.  
- Rejection arguments: narrow/theoretical scope, incremental contribution, missing lower bounds, limited broader ML impact.
