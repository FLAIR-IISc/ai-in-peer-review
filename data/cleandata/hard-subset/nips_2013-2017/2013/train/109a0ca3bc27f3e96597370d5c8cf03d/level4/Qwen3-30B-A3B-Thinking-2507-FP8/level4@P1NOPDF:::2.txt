

Additional comments after rebuttal:  
The authors must significantly strengthen the problem motivation to make the paper accessible to a broader machine learning audience. Specifically, they should introduce a latent variable model (with graphical representation) demonstrating how the proposed tensor decomposition learns parameters. Consider a model where each latent variable \(h_i\) linearly influences an observed variable \(x_i\), with arbitrary dependencies among the \(h_i\)'s. In this setting, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank equals \(\text{dim}(h_1), \text{dim}(h_2), \ldots\). The authors should include this discussion and a schematic diagram of the model. Crucially, they must clarify that only *some* hidden variables may have small dimensions (not all), which is the key advantage enabling their novel theoretical results.  

The paper presents a technically valuable contribution: convex optimization for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms, specifically the overlapped Schatten norm, with improved reconstruction guarantees. The writing is generally clear, but minor technical and notational refinements would enhance readability.  

**Technical clarifications required:**  
*   **Theorem 2:** The statement requires clarification. Components are recovered only *up to permutation*; this must be explicitly noted. The claim that (12) "immediately leads to" (13) lacks justification.  
*   **Lemma 5:** The proof is missing. As Lemma 5 is central to Theorem 2, its inclusion is essential.  
*   **Lemma 4:** While straightforward, the proof should be explicitly detailed rather than deferred to another paper.  

**Minor issues for improvement:**  
*   **Related Work:** Expand discussion of HOSVD and tensor decomposition methods. Cite Kolda’s review (Section 4) to contextualize how higher-order SVD and variants provide approximation bounds.  
*   **Tensor Rank Terminology:** The term "mode-rank" (used by authors) is standardly called "multilinear rank." Clarify this in the introduction and note the existence of alternative tensor rank definitions.  
*   **Computational Complexity:** Briefly address the computational cost of the proposed method.  
*   **Incoherence Conditions:** The authors claim their "incoherence" condition matches those in [1,3,10]. This is incorrect. Those works use incoherence for *sparse+low-rank* recovery (where singular vectors must be incoherent with basis vectors), but the noise here is *not sparse*. The authors' condition—requiring energy spread across mixture components/modes (i.e., no single component/mode dominates via large singular values)—is unrelated. Remove the comparison to [1,3,10].  
*   **Notation:**  
    -   The superscript notation \(\hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}\) for components is confusing. Suggest an alternative symbol (e.g., \(\hat{W}_k\)).  
    -   Avoid conflicting notations \(\bar{r}_k\) and \(\underline{r}_k\) for \(k\)-mode rank. Introduce a consistent notation like \(\text{mult\_rank}(\mathcal{T})\) for multilinear rank.  
    -   Correct the missing closing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 (supplementary material).  
*   **Abstract:** "result for structures Schatten" → "result for structured Schatten."  

The paper makes a strong technical contribution by developing a novel Tucker decomposition method using an overlapped Schatten norm with provable recovery guarantees. Addressing the above points—particularly the latent variable motivation, Theorem 2 clarification, and incoherence mischaracterization—will substantially improve the paper’s rigor and accessibility. The authors’ rebuttal addressed some concerns, but the core motivation gap and technical clarifications remain critical for acceptance.
