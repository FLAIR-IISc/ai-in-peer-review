This paper explores convex norm-regularized optimization approaches for factorizing approximately low-rank tensors, focusing on a recently proposed "latent" norm approach ([25]) and its comparison to a previously used "overlap" norm approach. Specifically, the paper derives theoretical bounds on the "latent" norm's mean-squared-error behavior under several noise assumptions, which compare favorably to the existing analysis on the overlap norm, supporting its better empirically-observed performance in a previous paper. These theoretical findings are also validated through numerical simulations. Additionally, the paper introduces a generalization of both these norms and proves a duality theorem for it.

The paper is generally well-organized and easy to follow, and its technical soundness appears to be intact (although I have not thoroughly checked the proofs in the supplementary material). The results may be useful for researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions.

However, I have two main concerns. Firstly, the results are narrow and of a technical nature, with only peripheral connections to machine learning. While the authors make a justified case that tensor factorizations can arise in machine learning, the paper does not attempt to examine the proposed approaches in the context of machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally. This limited scope may restrict the paper's potential impact at NIPS.

Secondly, the results seem somewhat incremental, and I did not find them particularly surprising (admittedly, this is a subjective notion). They appear to provide additional theoretical backing to a specific recently proposed approach [25], which was already argued to work well empirically. The techniques employed also do not seem particularly novel.

A minor defect is that the upper bounds are not accompanied by lower bounds. This omission is potentially problematic, as the upper bounds are used to compare different algorithms and argue for the superiority of one over the other.

Additional Comments
-----------------

* The abstract begins with "We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...". However, I think this is slightly misleading. The paper's focus is devoted to analyzing two existing approaches (overlapped and latent). Unless I missed something, the generalization only appears in defining it in section 2.1 and proving the dual norm (Lemma 1). In particular, no particular algorithms are proposed based on this generalized Schatten norm, the behavior of such algorithms is not analyzed, nor are they tested experimentally.
* Figure 1 is presented in the introduction, but at this stage, it is not interpretable without the background information appearing in section 2 onwards.
* Line 38: "Tensor is" should be "Tensor modelling is".
* Line 40: A comma is missing after "Conventionally".
* Line 395: "also in such situations" should be "in such situations as well".

The paper's primary contribution is the theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition approach. While the paper appears to be technically sound, the results are somewhat incremental and may suffer from limited impact at NIPS.
