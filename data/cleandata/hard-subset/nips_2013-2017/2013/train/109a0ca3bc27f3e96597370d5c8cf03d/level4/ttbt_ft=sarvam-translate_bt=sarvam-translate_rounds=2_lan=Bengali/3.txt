This paper provides a theoretical limit on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm using a latent shadow norm. It improves upon results known for regularization using overlapped shadow norms.

Quality: This paper achieves theoretical results for tensor decomposition using latent shadow norms. In particular, the duality between overlapped and latent shadow norms is interesting. However, the tests are briefly considered and not discussed in detail. For example, how were the ranks (r1,...rk) of the examples chosen? How does group regularization like l_1,\infty work if the tensor is low-ranked in all modes? How does this method perform compared to non-convex methods?

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and easy to understand.

Fundamental: The main fundamental result of the paper is the duality between overlapped and latent shadow norms. This result seems to be of independent interest.

Importance: Tensor decomposition is an important problem encountered in many applications. Since this paper improves upon existing results in this problem, I believe it is an important work.
