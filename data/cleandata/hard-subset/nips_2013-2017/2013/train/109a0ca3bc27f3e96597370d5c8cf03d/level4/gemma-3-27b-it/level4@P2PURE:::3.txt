This paper presents theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, achieving an improvement over existing results utilizing the overlapped Schatten norm. 

Quality: The paper delivers theoretical results for tensor decomposition with latent Schatten norm regularization. The dual characterization of both the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly noteworthy. However, the experimental evaluation is somewhat brief and lacks detail. For example, the criteria for selecting the Tucker ranks (r<sub>1</sub>,…r<sub>k</sub>) in the examples are not explained. Furthermore, the behavior of group regularization techniques, such as l<sub>1</sub>/l<sub>∞</sub> norms, when the tensor exhibits simultaneous low-rank structure across all modes is unclear. A comparison of the method’s performance against non-convex approaches would also be valuable.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and readily understandable.

Originality: The key original contribution of this work is the duality result between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which appears to be of independent interest. 

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with applications across numerous fields. Given that this paper advances the state-of-the-art for this problem, I consider it to be a significant contribution. The theoretical findings are interesting and the paper is well-written; however, the experiments receive relatively limited attention at the end of the manuscript.
