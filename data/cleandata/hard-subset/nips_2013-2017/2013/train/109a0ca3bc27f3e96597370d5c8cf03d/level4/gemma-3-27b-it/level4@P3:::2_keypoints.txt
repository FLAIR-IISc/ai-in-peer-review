- The paper analyzes structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, focusing on overlapped vs. latent regularization.  
- The authors theoretically justify the latent approach’s superior performance when the true tensor is low-rank in an unknown specific mode.  
- They establish MSE bounds and show scaling behavior based on the minimal mode-k rank.  
- A novel duality result between overlapped and latent Schatten norms is presented, with broader implications for structured sparsity.  
- Numerical experiments validate the theoretical predictions and the latent approach’s superior scaling.  
- The paper is generally well-written, logically organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- The introduction lacks concrete, ML-friendly motivation and would benefit from a latent-variable model example and graphical representation.  
- Theorem 2 needs clarification: recovered components are guaranteed only up to permutation and derivation steps (eq.13 from eq.12) require more detail.  
- The proof of Lemma 5 is missing and must be included, as it is crucial for Theorem 2.  
- Lemma 4’s proof is omitted; a concise in-paper explanation would improve readability despite the external reference.  
- The related work section should better cover HOSVD variants, approximation bounds, and different notions of tensor rank (e.g., multilinear rank).  
- The comparison to incoherence conditions in prior sparse+low-rank work is misleading and should be removed or revised.  
- Notational issues: confusing superscript component notation (e.g., \hat{W}^{(k)}), inconsistent rank symbols (\bar{r}_k vs. \underline{r}_k), and a missing parenthesis in the supplement need correction.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical analysis resolving an empirical puzzle, valuable duality result, strong empirical support, and overall solid writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak ML motivation, missing technical clarifications, potentially misleading incoherence discussion, and notational problems.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on motivation, technical detail clarifications, related-work expansion, and notational fixes.
