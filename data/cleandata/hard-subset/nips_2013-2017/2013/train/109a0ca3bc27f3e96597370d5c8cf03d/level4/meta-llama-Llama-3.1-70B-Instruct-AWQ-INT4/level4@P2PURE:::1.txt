This paper explores convex norm-regularized optimization approaches for the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors, focusing on a recently proposed "latent" norm approach ([25]) and its comparison to the previously used "overlap" norm approach. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared-error behavior of the "latent" norm under several noise assumptions, which compare favorably to existing analysis on the overlap norm, supporting its better empirically-observed performance in a previous paper. These theoretical findings are also validated through numerical simulations. Furthermore, the paper introduces a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for this generalized norm.

The paper is generally well-organized and appears to be technically sound, although I did not thoroughly examine the proofs in the supplementary material. The results may be useful for researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, I have two main concerns. Firstly, the results are narrow and primarily of a technical nature, with only peripheral connections to machine learning, which may limit their potential impact at NIPS. While the authors justify the relevance of tensor factorizations in machine learning, they do not explore the proposed approaches in the context of machine learning problems, either theoretically or experimentally.

Secondly, the results seem somewhat incremental, and I did not find them particularly surprising. They appear to provide additional theoretical justification for a specific recently proposed approach [25], which was already shown to work well empirically. The techniques employed do not seem especially novel. A more minor issue is that the upper bounds are not accompanied by lower bounds, which could be problematic when comparing different algorithms and arguing for the superiority of one over the other.

Additional Comments
-----------------

- The abstract begins with "We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...". However, I believe this statement is slightly misleading. The paper primarily focuses on analyzing two existing approaches (overlap and latent), and the generalization is only introduced in section 2.1 with the proof of the dual norm (Lemma 1). Unless I missed something, no specific algorithms are proposed based on this generalized Schatten norm, nor is the behavior of such algorithms analyzed or experimentally tested.

- Figure 1 is presented in the introduction but is not interpretable without the background information provided in section 2 onwards.

- Line 38: "Tensor is" should be corrected to "Tensor modelling is".

- Line 40: A comma is missing after "Conventionally".

- Line 395: "also in such situations" should be rephrased to "in such situations as well".
