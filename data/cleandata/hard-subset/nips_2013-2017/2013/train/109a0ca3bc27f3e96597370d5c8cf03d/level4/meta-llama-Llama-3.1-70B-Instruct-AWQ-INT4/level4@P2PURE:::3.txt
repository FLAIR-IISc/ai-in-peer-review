This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon existing results that utilize the overlapped Schatten norm for regularization.

Quality: The paper makes a notable contribution by deriving theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm, with the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms being a particularly interesting aspect. However, the experimental section is somewhat brief and lacks elaboration. Specifically, it would be beneficial to know how the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) were chosen for the examples presented. Additionally, the paper could be strengthened by exploring how group regularization, such as l_1,\infty, performs when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes, as well as comparing the proposed method to non-convex approaches.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clear in its presentation.

Originality: The primary original contribution of the paper lies in establishing the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, a result that appears to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with numerous applications, and this paper's improvement upon existing results makes it a significant work. The theoretical results are interesting, and the paper is well-written. Nevertheless, the brief treatment of experiments in the paper is a notable limitation.
