- Establishes theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm.  
- Offers improvement over existing results using overlapped Schatten norm regularization.  
- Dual characterization of overlapped and latent Schatten norms is the paper’s most significant and independently interesting theoretical contribution.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound, well-executed, and clearly derived.  
- Experimental section lacks sufficient detail to assess practical utility.  
- Selection criteria for Tucker ranks (r₁,…,rₖ) in experiments are not justified.  
- Behavior of group regularization (e.g., ℓ₁,∞) when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes is unclear.  
- Missing comparative evaluation against non-convex tensor decomposition approaches.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with logical flow from formulation to theory.  
- Significance is strong given tensor decomposition’s importance and the improved theoretical guarantees.  
- Experimental shortcomings prevent a confident assessment of real-world impact.  
- Recommended fixes: elaborate on Tucker rank selection, include thorough non-convex baseline comparisons, and clarify interplay between group regularization and multi-mode low-rank structure.
