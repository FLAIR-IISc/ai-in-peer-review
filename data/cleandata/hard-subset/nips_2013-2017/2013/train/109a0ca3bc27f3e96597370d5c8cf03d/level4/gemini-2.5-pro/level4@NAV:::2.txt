Additional comments following the rebuttal:

To improve the paper's accessibility for a broader machine learning audience, the motivation for the problem needs to be significantly strengthened. The authors should introduce a latent variable model, ideally accompanied by a graphical representation, where this tensor decomposition framework can be applied to learn the model parameters. For instance, consider a model where each latent variable `h_i` influences a corresponding observed variable `x_i` through a linear relationship, while allowing for arbitrary dependencies among the `h_i`. In such a model, the observed moment tensor naturally admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank corresponds directly to the dimensions of the latent variables (dim(h1), dim(h2),...). This discussion should also highlight a key advantage of the proposed result: its effectiveness even when only a subset of the latent variables is low-dimensional.

The paper introduces a convex optimization-based approach for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms. Specifically, the authors utilize the overlapped Schatten norm to achieve improved reconstruction guarantees. The work is well-articulated and provides technically significant contributions. Addressing a few technical and notational points would further enhance the paper's readability.

*   **Clarifications regarding Theorem 2 and its proof:**
    *   The statement of Theorem 2 is technically imprecise; it should be explicitly mentioned that the components are recovered only up to a permutation.
    *   The assertion that equation (13) follows immediately from (12) requires a more thorough justification.
    *   The proof for Lemma 5, a crucial component for establishing Theorem 2, is missing and should be included.
    *   For the paper to be self-contained, the proof of Lemma 4 should be explicitly provided rather than merely citing another work, even if the proof is straightforward.

*   **Minor issues:**
    *   The related work section should be expanded to include HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods. The authors could discuss how HOSVD and its variants provide approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, referencing seminal works such as Kolda's survey (Section 4).
    *   The authors should clarify that the "mode-rank" they use is also widely known as "multilinear rank." It would be beneficial to mention in the introduction that various definitions of tensor rank exist.
    *   A brief discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed methods should be included.
    *   The discussion on incoherence after equation (10) requires revision. The authors use the term to imply that energy is distributed across different components and modes. This deviates from the standard incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], which apply to sparse+low-rank recovery and concern the relationship between singular vectors and basis vectors. Since the noise model here is not sparse, this comparison is misleading and should be removed.

*   **Typos and notational issues:**
    *   The superscript notation for components, `\hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}`, is confusing and could be replaced with a more distinct symbol.
    *   The notation for k-mode rank is inconsistent, with both `\bar{r}k` and `\underline{r}k` being used. A consistent notation, such as `mult_rank(T)`, should be adopted.
    *   A parenthesis appears to be missing in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material.
    *   In the abstract, "result for structures Schatten" should be corrected to "result for structured Schatten."

In conclusion, this is a well-written paper presenting a novel and valuable contribution: a Tucker tensor decomposition method based on an overlapped Schatten norm with theoretical recovery guarantees. However, a number of technical clarifications are necessary, which I trust the authors can address effectively.
