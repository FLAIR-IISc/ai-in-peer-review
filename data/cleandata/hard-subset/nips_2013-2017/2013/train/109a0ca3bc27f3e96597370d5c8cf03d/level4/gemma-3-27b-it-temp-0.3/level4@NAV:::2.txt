The paper presents a convex-optimization approach to tensor decomposition, leveraging structured Schatten norms – specifically, an overlapped Schatten norm – to achieve improved reconstruction guarantees. The work is well-written and represents a technically significant contribution to the field. However, several points require attention to enhance clarity and completeness.

First, the authors should strengthen the motivation for framing this problem within a machine learning context to broaden the paper’s accessibility. Crucially, the paper would benefit from a discussion of the underlying latent variable model that this tensor decomposition aims to learn. A graphical model representation illustrating this connection would be particularly helpful. For example, consider a model where latent variables *h<sub>i</sub>* connect to observed variables *x<sub>i</sub>*, with a linear relationship from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the *h<sub>i</sub>*. In this framework, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, where the multilinear rank corresponds to the dimensions of the latent spaces (dim(*h<sub>1</sub>*), dim(*h<sub>2</sub>*), etc.). Highlighting that only *some* latent variables may be of low dimension – a key advantage of the presented result – would further strengthen this connection.

Regarding specific technical aspects, Theorem 2’s statement is technically incomplete; the recovered components are only identifiable up to a permutation, a nuance the authors should acknowledge. The derivation from equation (12) to (13) in Theorem 2 also requires further justification. Furthermore, the proof of Lemma 5 is currently missing, as it is a critical component supporting Theorem 2. While Lemma 4’s proof is straightforward, explicitly presenting it rather than referencing another paper would improve readability.

Several minor issues also warrant attention. The introduction should include a more comprehensive overview of related work, specifically mentioning HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods, and referencing approximation bounds achievable with higher-order SVD variants (e.g., Kolda’s review). The authors should also clarify that the rank they employ – termed “mode-rank” – is commonly known as “multilinear rank” and acknowledge the existence of different tensor rank definitions. A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods would be valuable.

The discussion of incoherence following equation (10) requires clarification. The authors’ use of “incoherence” to describe energy distribution across mixture components and modes differs from the standard incoherence conditions used in sparse-plus-low-rank recovery (as seen in [1,3,10]). The latter relies on incoherence between singular vectors and basis vectors, which is not the same condition present here. Removing this comparison would be prudent.

Finally, several notational inconsistencies should be addressed. The superscript notation for components (e.g.,  \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}) is potentially confusing and should be revised. The use of both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k to denote k-mode rank is problematic; introducing a notation like `mult_rank(tensor)` would be clearer. A missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material should also be corrected. The abstract should be revised to read "result for *structured* Schatten" instead of "structures Schatten."

Overall, this is a strong paper with a novel contribution in achieving effective Tucker tensor decomposition via an overlapped Schatten norm and providing corresponding recovery guarantees. Addressing these technical clarifications will significantly enhance the paper’s impact and readability.
