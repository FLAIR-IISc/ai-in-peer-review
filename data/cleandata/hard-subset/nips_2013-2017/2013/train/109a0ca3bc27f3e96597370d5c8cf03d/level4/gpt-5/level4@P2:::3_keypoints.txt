- Proposes structured Schatten norms unifying overlapped and latent tensor formulations and proves their duality (overlapped Sp/q dual = latent Sp*/q*).  
- Analyzes latent S1/1 norm for noisy tensor denoising and provides finite-sample error bounds.  
- Key takeaway: latent approach attains error rates scaling with the minimal mode rank when the tensor is low-rank mainly in one unknown mode.  
- Provides consistency bounds, deterministic bounds under incoherence-type constraints, and high-probability Gaussian-noise bounds.  
- Simulations validate predicted scaling and often show latent outperforming overlapped, even for tensors low-rank across modes.  
- Technical development is solid, leveraging decomposable regularization and random matrix tools; the duality is of independent interest.  
- Error bounds are interpretable and favor the latent approach via dependence on minimum mode rank.  
- Some assumptions (e.g., spectral-norm incoherence) are reasonable but need discussion regarding verifiability and practical relevance.  
- Scope limited to denoising with full observations; completion is mentioned only as future work.  
- Regularization parameter conditions are clear; notable that optimal λ does not depend on unknown ranks.  
- Authors should discuss tightness of constants and whether bounds are minimax or close in representative regimes.  
- Experimental section is concise but lacks reproducibility details (e.g., how Tucker ranks and latent ranks were chosen).  
- Need experiments assessing sensitivity to incoherence α and to violations like correlated components across modes.  
- Missing comparisons to standard nonconvex baselines (HOOI/Tucker truncation, HOSVD truncation, CP-ALS) in accuracy and runtime.  
- Request for a practical, data-driven rule for setting λ (and guidance on choosing α) and sensitivity analyses.  
- Suggest adding intuition for latent decomposition (e.g., illustrative figure) and a brief ADMM solver description with computational cost summary.  
- Recommend summarizing assumptions (including α constraints) in one place and discussing how to check/approximate them in practice.  
- Originality: duality generalizes group-sparsity results to tensor norms; statistical refinement to minimal-rank dependence is nontrivial.  
- Significance: results guide when to prefer latent vs overlapped formulations and can influence algorithm selection in noisy multiway data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear duality result, stronger latent statistical guarantees, well-written presentation, and supportive empirical evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental evaluation (synthetic only), sparse baseline comparisons, and brief practical guidance on λ/α and assumption verification.  
- Overall assessment: well-executed theoretical contribution with clear message; strengthening empirical validation and practical guidelines would increase impact.
