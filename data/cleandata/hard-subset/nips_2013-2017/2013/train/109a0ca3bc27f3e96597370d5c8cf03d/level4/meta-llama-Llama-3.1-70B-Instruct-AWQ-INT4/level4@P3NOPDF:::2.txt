This paper presents a convex-optimization based approach to tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors introduce the overlapped Schatten norm and demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving better reconstruction guarantees. Overall, the paper is well-written and makes significant technical contributions to the field.

However, to make the paper more accessible to a broader audience, the authors need to provide a clearer motivation for the problem in the context of machine learning. Specifically, they should introduce a latent variable model with a graphical representation to illustrate how the tensor decomposition can learn the model parameters. Consider a model where latent variables $h_1$, $h_2$, and so on, connect to observed variables $x_1$, $x_2$, and so on, respectively, assuming a linear relationship between the hidden and observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among the latent variables. In this case, the observed moment tensor can be represented using a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank is determined by the dimensions of the latent variables. The authors should include a discussion of this model, preferably accompanied by a diagram, to facilitate understanding.

Moreover, the authors should highlight the possibility that only some hidden variables may have small dimensions, which is a key advantage of their new result. This clarification will help readers appreciate the significance of the proposed approach.

Regarding the technical aspects, the authors need to address a few issues. The statement of Theorem 2 is not entirely accurate, as the components are only recovered up to a permutation. Although this is a minor issue, it is essential to acknowledge this limitation. Additionally, the authors should provide a justification for the claim that equation (12) immediately leads to equation (13) in the proof of Theorem 2.

The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, which is a crucial component of Theorem 2. The authors should provide a complete proof of this lemma to ensure the validity of their results. Furthermore, it would be helpful to clarify the proof of Lemma 4 explicitly, rather than referring to another paper, to maintain the paper's self-contained nature.

In terms of minor issues, the authors can enhance the paper by adding more related work on Higher-Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and other methods for tensor decomposition. Specifically, they can mention how HOSVD and its variants yield approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, as discussed in Kolda's review on tensor decompositions (section 4).

The authors should also note that the notion of rank they employ, referred to as mode-rank, is also known as multilinear rank. They can mention this in the introduction and acknowledge the existence of different notions of tensor rank.

A brief discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed methods would be beneficial in providing a comprehensive understanding of the approach.

The authors' discussion on incoherence after equation (10) requires clarification. They use the term to imply that the "energy" should be spread across different mixture components and modes, meaning that no component or mode can have too high a singular value. However, this is not equivalent to the incoherence conditions used in [1,3,10], which involve sparse and low-rank recovery. The condition in [1,3,10] requires the singular vectors to be incoherent with the basis vectors, which is distinct from the condition used in this paper. It is recommended that the authors remove this comparison to avoid confusion.

Finally, there are a few notational issues that need to be addressed. The superscript notation for components $\hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}$ is confusing and can be replaced with a more distinct symbol. Additionally, the notation for $k$-mode rank is inconsistent, with both $\bar{r}_k$ and $\underline{r}_k$ being used. It is suggested that the authors introduce a more consistent notation, such as $\text{mult\_rank}(\text{tensor})$, to denote the multilinear rank.

There are also a few typos and minor errors, including a missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material and a typo in the abstract ("result for structures Schatten" should be "result for structured Schatten").

In conclusion, the paper makes novel contributions to the field of tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm and provides guarantees for recovery. However, addressing the technical clarifications and minor issues mentioned above will enhance the paper's clarity and overall quality.
