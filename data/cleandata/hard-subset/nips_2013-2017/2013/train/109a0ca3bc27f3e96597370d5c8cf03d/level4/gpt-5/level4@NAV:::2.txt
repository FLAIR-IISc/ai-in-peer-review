Review — Additional remarks following the rebuttal:
The paper’s motivation from a machine learning perspective should be substantially strengthened to make it accessible to a broader readership. Please include a latent-variable model (with a graphical model) in which the proposed tensor decomposition recovers parameters: for example, let latent variable h1 connect to observed x1, h2 to x2, and so on. Assume a linear mapping from hidden to observed variables, with arbitrary dependencies among the hi. Under this setup, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition whose multilinear ranks are dim(h1), dim(h2), …. This discussion should be added, ideally with a figure illustrating the model. Please also emphasize that only some hidden variables may be low-dimensional while others are not, which is a key advantage highlighted by your new result.

The paper studies convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured tensor Schatten norms. By employing the overlapped Schatten norm, the authors derive stronger reconstruction guarantees. The manuscript is clearly written and makes technically meaningful contributions. Addressing a few technical and notational points will further improve readability.

*Clarifications regarding Theorem 2 and its proof:
The statement of Theorem 2 is not strictly correct: the components are identifiable only up to permutation. While minor, this should be stated.
In Theorem 2, the claim that (12) immediately implies (13) requires justification.
The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, even though Lemma 5 is central to Theorem 2.
It would be better to spell out the proof of Lemma 4 explicitly rather than only citing another paper, even if the argument is straightforward.

*Minor issues:
Please expand the related work on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods:
You could note how higher-order SVD and its variants provide approximation guarantees for tensor decompositions; see Kolda’s review (Section 4).
The “mode-rank” used here is also known as multilinear rank; please state this and clarify in the introduction that different tensor rank notions exist.
Briefly discuss the computational complexity of the proposed methods.
Please clarify the discussion of incoherence after (10). You use “incoherence” to mean that energy is spread across components and modes (i.e., no component or mode has an excessively large singular value). You then assert this matches the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10]. I do not see the equivalence. In [1,3,10], incoherence is used for sparse+low-rank recovery with sparse noise, and requires singular vectors to be incoherent with the canonical basis—this differs from your condition. I recommend removing this comparison.

*Typos/notational issues:
The superscript notation for components \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)} is confusing; consider using a different symbol for components.
Problematic notation: both \bar{r}_k and \underline{r}_k are used for the k-mode rank. Please unify. You could introduce mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank.
On the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material, a parenthesis is missing.
Abstract: “result for structures Schatten” should be “structured.”

Overall, the paper is well written and offers a novel contribution by obtaining a robust Tucker tensor decomposition via an overlapped Schatten norm with recovery guarantees. Some technical clarifications remain, which I hope the authors can address.
