This paper presents a convex optimization-based approach for tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms. By employing the overlapped Schatten norm, the authors achieve improved reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well-written and offers technically significant contributions. Addressing the following points would further enhance the clarity and impact of the work.

**Additional comments after the rebuttal:**

To make the paper more accessible to a broad machine learning audience, the authors should significantly improve the motivation for the problem. A key addition would be to present a latent variable model for which this tensor decomposition learns the parameters. For instance, consider a model where latent variables h_i connect to observed variables x_i via a linear transformation, and the h_i variables have arbitrary dependencies among themselves. The resulting observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition where the multilinear rank corresponds to the dimensions of the latent variables (dim(h1), dim(h2), ...). This discussion, ideally accompanied by a graphical model diagram, would ground the work in a familiar machine learning context.

Crucially, this framing would also allow the authors to highlight a key advantage of their proposed method: its ability to handle cases where only a subset of the latent variables are low-dimensional.

**Major Technical Clarifications:**

The following points regarding Theorem 2 and its proof need to be addressed:

*   The statement of Theorem 2 is imprecise: the components are recovered up to permutation, a detail that should be explicitly mentioned.
*   The claim in the proof that equation (13) follows immediately from (12) lacks justification and requires further explanation.
*   The proof for Lemma 5, which is a key component of the proof for Theorem 2, is missing.
*   For completeness, the proof of Lemma 4 should be presented explicitly within the paper rather than citing another source, even if the proof is straightforward.

**Minor Issues and Suggestions:**

*   **Related Work:** The discussion of related work could be strengthened by including other tensor decomposition methods, such as the Higher-Order SVD (HOSVD). The authors should discuss how HOSVD and its variants provide approximation bounds, referencing established surveys like Kolda and Bader's review (e.g., Section 4).
*   **Tensor Rank Notation:** In the introduction, the authors should clarify that the 'mode-rank' they use is also known as 'multilinear rank.' It would also be helpful to briefly mention that other distinct notions of tensor rank exist.
*   **Computational Complexity:** A brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods should be included.
*   **Incoherence Condition:** The discussion of 'incoherence' following equation (10) requires revision. The authors claim their condition—requiring the 'energy' to be spread across components and modes—is analogous to the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10] for sparse+low-rank recovery. This comparison seems misplaced, as the conditions in [1,3,10] concern the incoherence of singular vectors with standard basis vectors in a sparse noise setting, which is different from the scenario here. It is recommended that this comparison be removed.

**Typos and Notational Issues:**

*   The superscript notation for components, \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}, is potentially confusing and could be replaced with a different symbol.
*   The notation for k-mode rank is inconsistent, using both \bar{r}_k and \underline{r}_k. This should be standardized. Using a dedicated operator, such as mult_rank(T), could improve clarity.
*   A parenthesis is missing in the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material.
*   In the abstract, "result for structures Schatten" should be corrected to "result for structured Schatten".

**Summary:**

In summary, this paper makes a novel and valuable contribution by proposing a Tucker decomposition method based on the overlapped Schatten norm and providing corresponding recovery guarantees. The work is well-written, but its quality would be significantly enhanced by addressing the technical and presentational issues outlined above.
