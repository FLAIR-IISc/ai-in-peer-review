This paper provides theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm. It improves upon existing results known for regularization using the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality:** The paper obtains theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm, and the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly interesting. The experimental evaluation, however, is somewhat brief and lacks detailed discussion. For instance, how were the ranks (r1, ..., rK) of the examples chosen? How does group regularization, such as the l1,∞ norm, perform when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank in all modes? A comparison with non-convex approaches would also be valuable. The theoretical contributions appear sound, and the proofs seem carefully constructed (though detailed examination is limited to the main text).

**Clarity:** The paper is well organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the main ideas and contributions. The notation is consistent, and the authors provide sufficient background information.

**Originality:** The primary original result is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which appears to be of independent interest and connects to related work on structured sparsity. The analysis of the latent Schatten norm in the context of tensor decomposition also represents a novel contribution.

**Significance:** Tensor decomposition is an important problem with applications in many fields. This paper improves upon existing results for this problem, and the theoretical results are interesting and well-motivated. The demonstration of improved performance compared to the overlapped approach is significant. While the experimental results are limited, they support the theoretical findings.

**Overall:** This is a solid theoretical contribution to the field of tensor decomposition. The paper is well-written and clearly presents a novel and significant result. The brevity of the experimental section is a minor weakness, but does not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound analysis of the latent Schatten norm.
*   Interesting duality result with connections to structured sparsity.
*   Clear writing and organization.
*   Addresses an important problem with potential applications in various domains.
*   Empirical results, though limited, support the theoretical claims.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive, including comparisons to non-convex methods and a more detailed analysis of the parameter choices.
*   The connection to existing group regularization methods (l1,∞) could be more thoroughly explored.
