This paper studies convex norm-regularized optimization methods for factorizing approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on a recently proposed “latent” norm approach ([25]) and compares it to the previously used “overlapped” norm. The authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared error of the latent norm under several noise models, showing favorable comparisons to existing analyses of the overlapped norm and thereby supporting the previously observed empirical advantages. These theoretical findings are corroborated by numerical simulations. The paper also introduces a generalization encompassing both norms and proves a corresponding duality result.

The paper is generally clear and appears technically sound (though I did not carefully verify the proofs in the supplementary material). The results should be of interest to researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. My main concerns are:

(i) The contributions are narrow and largely technical, only tangentially connected to machine learning, and therefore likely to have limited impact at NIPS. Although the authors rightly note that tensor factorizations arise in machine learning, the paper does not investigate the proposed approaches within concrete machine learning settings, either theoretically or empirically.

(ii) The results feel incremental and not particularly surprising (admittedly a subjective assessment). They provide additional theoretical support for a specific recent approach [25] that had already shown strong empirical performance, and the techniques employed do not seem especially novel.

A more minor issue is the absence of lower bounds to complement the upper bounds. This is potentially problematic, since the upper bounds are used to compare algorithms and argue for the superiority of one approach over another.

Additional comments:
- The abstract opens with “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...,” which seems slightly misleading. The paper primarily analyzes two existing approaches (overlapped and latent). The generalization appears mainly in Section 2.1, where the dual norm is characterized (Lemma 1). No specific algorithms based on these generalized Schatten norms are proposed, their behavior is not analyzed, and they are not evaluated experimentally.
- Figure 1 appears in the introduction but is not interpretable at that point without the background provided from Section 2 onward.
- Line 38: “Tensor is” -> “Tensor modelling is” (?)
- Line 40: Missing comma after “Conventionally”
- Line 395: “also in such situations” -> “in such situations as well”

In summary, the paper’s main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition method. While the work appears technically sound, it seems somewhat incremental and may have limited impact at NIPS.
