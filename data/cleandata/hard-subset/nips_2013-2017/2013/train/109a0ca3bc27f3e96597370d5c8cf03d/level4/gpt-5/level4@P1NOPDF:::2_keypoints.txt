- Paper makes a technically important contribution: convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured (overlapped) Schatten norms with improved reconstruction guarantees.  
- Theorem 2 must explicitly state component recovery is only up to permutation.  
- Provide a clear justification for the implication from equation (12) to (13) (it is not immediate as written).

- Expand related work discussion to include HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods and note existing approximation bounds (e.g., Kolda’s review, Section 4).  
- Clarify terminology: replace or define “mode-rank” as the commonly used “multilinear rank” and note multiple, non-equivalent tensor rank notions.  
- Briefly discuss computational complexity: per-iteration cost, dependence on tensor dimensions and ranks, and practical bottlenecks.  
- Reconsider the incoherence comparison: the paper’s incoherence notion differs from the sparse+low-rank literature [1,3,10], so remove or revise that comparison.  
- Change confusing superscript notation for components (\hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}) to a clearer indexing scheme.  
- Make k-mode rank notation consistent (choose between \bar{r}_k and \underline{r}_k or introduce mult_rank(tensor)).  
- Fix minor typos: missing parenthesis in supplementary material (last line of page 12) and abstract typo “result for structures Schatten” → “result for structured Schatten.”  
- Overall: the approach is novel and valuable—overlapped Schatten norm can recover Tucker-type low-multilinear-rank tensors with provable guarantees—and the suggested clarifications and fixes will substantially strengthen the paper.
