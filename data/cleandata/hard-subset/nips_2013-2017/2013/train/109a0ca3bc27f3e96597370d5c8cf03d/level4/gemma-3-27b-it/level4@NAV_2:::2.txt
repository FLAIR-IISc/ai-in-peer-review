The paper considers convex-optimization based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors employ the overlapped Schatten norm and show how it leads to better reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well written and makes technically important contributions. Fixing a few technical and notational issues will make the paper even more readable.

*Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:* 
The statement of Theorem 2 is technically not entirely correct: the components are only recovered up to a permutation. Although a minor issue, the authors should acknowledge this. 
In Theorem 2, it is stated that (12) immediately leads to (13). This requires justification. 
The proof of Lemma 5 is missing. Lemma 5 forms a key part of Theorem 2. 
It is better to clarify the proof of Lemma 4 explicitly rather than referring to another paper, although it is straightforward. 

*Minor issues:* 
More related work on HOSVD and other methods for tensor decomposition should be added. 
The authors could mention how the higher order SVD and its variants yield approximation bounds for tensor decomposition. See Kolda's review on tensor decompositions (section 4). 
The notion of rank that the authors use, which they refer to as mode-rank, is also referred to as multilinear rank. The authors can mention this and note there are different notions of tensor rank in the introduction. 
The authors could briefly discuss the computational complexity of the methods employed. 
The authors need to clarify their discussion about incoherence after (10). The authors use the term to mean that they need the "energy" to be spread across different mixture components and modes, i.e., no component or mode can have too high a singular value. The authors mention that this is the same as the incoherence conditions of [1,3,11]. I do not see this. In [1,3,11], incoherence notions are used for sparse+low rank recovery. However, here, the noise is not sparse. The condition of [1,3,11] involves the singular vectors being incoherent with the basis vectors. This is different from the condition here. Suggest removing this comparison. 

*Typos/notational issues:* 
Superscript notation for components \( \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)} \) is confusing. Suggest using some other symbol for components. 
Bad notation: \( \bar{r}_k \) and \( \underline{r}_k \) are both used for k-mode rank. This should be changed. Could introduce `mult_rank(tensor)` instead to denote the multilinear rank. 
In the last line of page 12 in the supplementary material, some parenthesis is missing. 

Abstract: "result for structures Schatten" should change to structured.

The paper is well written and makes a novel contribution in terms of finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using a latent Schatten norm and provides guarantees for recovery. There are, however, some technical clarifications needed which will hopefully be addressed by the authors.
