The paper considers convex-optimization based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms for tensors. The authors employ the overlapped Schatten norm and show how it leads to better reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well written and makes technically important contributions. Addressing a few technical and notational issues will enhance the paper's readability.

**Additional comments after the rebuttal:**

To broaden the paper’s accessibility to the machine learning community, the authors should more clearly motivate the problem. Specifically, they should provide a latent variable model – including a graphical model representation – illustrating how this tensor decomposition can learn model parameters. For example, consider a model where latent variables *h<sub>1</sub>* connect to observed variables *x<sub>1</sub>*, *h<sub>2</sub>* to *x<sub>2</sub>*, and so on, with a linear model from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependence among the *h<sub>i</sub>*’s. In this case, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, and its multilinear rank is dim(*h<sub>1</sub>*), dim(*h<sub>2</sub>*), and so forth. Including this discussion, preferably with a corresponding diagram, would be beneficial. The authors should also explicitly point out that only some hidden variables may be of small dimension, which is a key advantage of the presented result.

*Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:*

The statement of Theorem 2 is technically incomplete; the components are recovered only up to a permutation, and this should be acknowledged. Additionally, the derivation from equation (12) to equation (13) in Theorem 2 requires justification. The proof of Lemma 5 is currently missing, which is problematic as it is a crucial component of the proof of Theorem 2. While the proof of Lemma 4 is straightforward, explicitly stating it rather than referencing another paper would improve clarity.

*Minor issues:*

The related work section should be expanded to include more discussion of HOSVD and other existing methods for tensor decomposition. The authors could mention how higher-order SVD and its variants yield approximation bounds for tensor decomposition, referencing, for example, Kolda’s review on tensor decompositions (section 4). The authors should also acknowledge that the rank they utilize, referred to as mode-rank, is also commonly known as multilinear rank, and briefly mention the existence of different notions of tensor rank in the introduction. A discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods would also be valuable.

The discussion of incoherence following equation (10) requires clarification. The authors equate their “energy spread” condition – where no component or mode has an overly dominant singular value – with the incoherence conditions of [1, 3, 10]. However, this comparison appears inaccurate. References [1, 3, 10] typically employ incoherence notions in the context of sparse plus low-rank recovery, whereas the noise model here is not sparse. The conditions in [1, 3, 10] involve the incoherence between singular vectors and basis vectors, a different requirement than the condition presented here. Removing this comparison would be advisable.

*Typos/notational issues:*

The superscript notation for components,  \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}, is confusing. An alternative symbol is suggested. The use of both \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k to represent k-mode rank should be avoided. Introducing a notation like `mult_rank(tensor)` to denote the multilinear rank would enhance clarity. In the supplementary material, on page 12, the last line of text is missing a parenthesis. 

Abstract: “result for structures Schatten” should be corrected to “structured Schatten”.

Overall, the paper presents a well-written and novel contribution in finding a good Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm and provides recovery guarantees. However, addressing the technical clarifications outlined above will significantly strengthen the paper.
