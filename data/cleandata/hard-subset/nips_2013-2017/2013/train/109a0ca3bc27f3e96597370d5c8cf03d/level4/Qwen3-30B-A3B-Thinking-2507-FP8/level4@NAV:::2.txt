

Additional comments after the rebuttal:  
The authors must substantially improve the problem motivation for machine learning applications to enhance accessibility. Specifically, they should introduce a latent variable model (with graphical representation) demonstrating how tensor decomposition learns parameters. Consider a model where latent variable $h_1$ connects to observed $x_1$, $h_2$ to $x_2$, etc., with linear mappings from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependencies among $\{h_i\}$. In this setting, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition, with multilinear rank equal to $\dim(h_1), \dim(h_2), \ldots$. The authors must add this discussion, preferably with a diagram, and clarify that only some hidden variables may have small dimensions—a key advantage of their result.  

The paper proposes convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms (specifically the overlapped Schatten norm) for improved reconstruction guarantees. It is well-written and makes technically significant contributions. Addressing minor technical and notational issues will further enhance readability.  

*Clarification on Theorem 2 and its proof:*  
The statement of Theorem 2 is technically inaccurate: components are recovered only up to permutation. The authors must correct this. Additionally, the claim that (12) immediately implies (13) requires justification. The proof of Lemma 5 (a critical component of Theorem 2) is missing. The proof of Lemma 4 should be clarified explicitly instead of referencing another paper, even if straightforward.  

*Minor issues:*  
- Expand related work to include HOSVD and tensor decomposition methods, citing Kolda’s review (Section 4) for approximation bounds.  
- Clarify that the "mode-rank" used is equivalent to "multilinear rank" and note the existence of alternative tensor rank definitions in the introduction.  
- Briefly discuss computational complexity of the proposed methods.  
- Revise the incoherence discussion after (10): The authors’ condition (energy spread across mixture components/modes) differs from incoherence in [1,3,10] (which applies to sparse+low-rank recovery with singular vector incoherence). Remove this misleading comparison.  

*Typos/notational issues:*  
- Replace confusing superscript notation $\hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}$ with an alternative symbol for components.  
- Standardize $k$-mode rank notation: replace inconsistent $\bar{r}_k$ and $\underline{r}_k$ with $\text{mult\_rank}(\mathbf{T})$.  
- Correct the missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 (supplementary material).  
- Fix "result for structures Schatten" to "structured Schatten" in the abstract.  

The paper makes a novel contribution by leveraging an overlapped Schatten norm for Tucker decomposition with recovery guarantees. Addressing the specified technical clarifications will significantly strengthen the work.
