- Paper analyzes convex norm-regularized tensor factorization, comparing a recent “latent” norm to the previously used “overlap” norm.  
- Authors derive theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, showing improved performance over the overlap norm.  
- Theoretical results corroborate prior empirical observations and are validated by numerical simulations.  
- Paper generalizes both norms and proves a duality theorem for the generalized formulation.  
- Manuscript is well-written and technically sound, though reviewer could not fully verify supplementary proofs.  
- Results are mainly of interest to researchers in optimization and tensor decompositions rather than the broader ML community.  
- Paper’s scope is limited and lacks theoretical or empirical connections to broader machine learning applications.  
- Contribution is incremental: provides theory for an approach already known to work empirically, with limited technical novelty.  
- Techniques, while rigorous, do not appear substantially novel.  
- Absence of complementary lower bounds undermines assessment of tightness and optimality of the presented upper bounds.  
- Framing in the abstract is misleading about introducing a “new class” of structured Schatten norms; the paper mainly analyzes existing approaches.  
- No new algorithms based on the generalized norm are proposed, analyzed, or tested.  
- Figure 1 is poorly placed in the introduction and requires background from Section 2 to interpret.  
- Minor editorial suggestions: change “Tensor is” to “Tensor modelling is” (Line 38); add comma after “Conventionally” (Line 40); reword Line 395 to “in such situations as well.”  
- Arguments for acceptance: rigorous theoretical analysis, informative error bounds, novel duality result, and supportive simulations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental advance, weak ML relevance, missing lower bounds, and narrow scope; overall recommendation: weak accept, better suited to a specialized venue.
