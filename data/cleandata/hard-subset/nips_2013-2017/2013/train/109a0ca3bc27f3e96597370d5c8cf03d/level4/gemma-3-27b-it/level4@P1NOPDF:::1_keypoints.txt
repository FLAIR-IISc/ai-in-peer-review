- Paper analyzes convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the latent norm to the overlap norm and deriving MSE bounds under noise.  
- Theoretical bounds favor the latent norm and are validated by numerical simulations; the paper also presents a generalized norm and proves its duality theorem.  
- Paper is generally well-written and appears technically sound, though proofs in the supplement were not fully checked.  
- Results may be valuable to researchers on tensor optimization methods but have a peripheral connection to machine learning and limited demonstrated ML impact.  
- Main concern: scope is narrow and technical, lacking theoretical or empirical exploration on actual machine learning problems.  
- Results feel incremental and unsurprising, primarily offering additional justification for an existing approach [25]; techniques are not especially novel.  
- Important omission: no lower bounds are provided to accompany the upper bounds, weakening algorithmic comparisons and claims of superiority.  
- Abstract wording is misleading about “proposing” a new class of structured Schatten norms—the paper mainly analyzes existing approaches and only defines the generalization and its dual.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 1 appears in the introduction but requires background from section 2 to be interpretable.  
- Minor editorial fixes: Line 38 “Tensor is” → “Tensor modeling is”; Line 40 add comma after “Conventionally”; Line 395 “also in such situations” → “in such situations as well.”  
- Overall: primary contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor decomposition approach; technically sound but incremental with limited NIPS impact.
