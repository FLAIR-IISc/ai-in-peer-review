This paper derives theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon known results for regularization using the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality and Originality:**
The paper's main theoretical contributions are strong. It provides new performance guarantees for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm, and the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a novel and interesting result of independent interest.

The primary weakness lies in the experimental evaluation, which is presented too briefly and lacks sufficient detail. To strengthen the paper, the authors should elaborate on the following points:
*   How were the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) selected for the experiments?
*   How does group regularization, such as l_1,∞, perform in the setting where the tensor is simultaneously low-rank in all modes?
*   How does the proposed method's performance compare against established non-convex approaches?

**Clarity:**
The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the theoretical arguments easy to follow.

**Significance:**
Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with wide-ranging applications. By improving upon existing theoretical results for this problem, this paper represents a significant contribution. The theoretical results are interesting and the paper is well-written. However, its overall impact is currently limited by the brief experimental section.
