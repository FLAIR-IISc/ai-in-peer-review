This paper presents theoretical bounds on the performance of a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving upon prior results for regularization with the overlapped Schatten norm.

Quality: The paper delivers solid theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly interesting and appears broadly useful. However, the experimental section is too brief and lacks sufficient detail. For example:
- How were the Tucker ranks (r1, …, rk) selected for the examples?
- How does group regularization such as the l1,∞ norm behave when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes?
- How does the proposed method compare with non-convex approaches in practice?

Clarity: The paper is well organized and generally easy to follow.

Originality: The central original contribution is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which seems to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is an important problem with many applications, and this work advances the state of the art by improving existing theoretical results for the tensor decomposition problem. The theoretical contributions are compelling and the paper is well written, but the empirical evaluation is treated too briefly at the end and would benefit from greater depth and comparative analysis.
