Additional comments after the rebuttal:
- The paper needs a stronger motivation of the problem from a machine learning perspective to be accessible to a broader audience. Please introduce the latent-variable setting in which the proposed tensor decomposition estimates model parameters, ideally with a graphical model. For example, consider latent variables h1, h2, …, each connected to observed variables x1, x2, …, respectively, with a linear mapping from hidden to observed variables and arbitrary dependence among the hi’s. In this setup, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition whose multilinear ranks are dim(h1), dim(h2), …. Please add this discussion and, if possible, include a figure illustrating the model. Also highlight that only some hidden variables may have small dimensions while others may not—a key advantage enabled by the new results in the paper.

Summary and overall assessment:
- The paper studies convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition via structured Schatten norms for tensors. Using the overlapped Schatten norm, the authors derive improved reconstruction guarantees. The paper is well written and makes technically important contributions. Addressing the technical and notational issues below will further improve clarity and readability.

Clarifications regarding Theorem 2 and proofs:
- Theorem 2 as stated is not technically correct: the components are identifiable only up to a permutation. This should be explicitly noted.
- The claim that (12) “immediately” implies (13) requires a justification.
- The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, even though it is central to Theorem 2; please include it.
- It would be better to provide an explicit proof of Lemma 4 rather than only referring to another paper, even if the argument is straightforward.

Minor issues and related work:
- Please add more related work on HOSVD and related tensor decomposition methods, including approximation guarantees. See Kolda and Bader’s review (Section 4).
- The “mode-rank” terminology you use corresponds to the multilinear rank. Please note this and mention in the introduction that multiple, non-equivalent notions of tensor rank exist.
- Briefly discuss the computational complexity of the proposed methods.

Incoherence discussion after (10):
- Your use of “incoherence” appears to mean that energy is distributed across mixture components and modes, i.e., no component or mode has an excessively large singular value. You state this is the same as the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], but I do not see the equivalence. In [1,3,10], incoherence is used in sparse+low-rank recovery with sparse noise and typically constrains singular vectors to be incoherent with the standard basis; here, the noise is not sparse, and the condition differs. I recommend removing or revising this comparison.

Typos and notational issues:
- The superscript notation for components, e.g., \hat{\cal{W}}^{(k)}, is confusing; consider a different symbol or indexing convention.
- The notation \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k are both used for the k-mode rank; please unify. You could introduce a function such as mult_rank(tensor) to denote the multilinear rank.
- A parenthesis is missing on the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material.
- Abstract: “result for structures Schatten” should be “result for structured Schatten.”

Conclusion:
- The paper presents a novel and useful approach to Tucker tensor decomposition via an overlapped Schatten norm with recovery guarantees. With the added motivation, clarifications, and fixes above, it will be significantly stronger and more broadly accessible.
