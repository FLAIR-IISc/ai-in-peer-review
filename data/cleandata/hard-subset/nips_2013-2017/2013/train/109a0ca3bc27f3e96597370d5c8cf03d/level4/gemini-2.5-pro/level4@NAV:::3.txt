This paper establishes theoretical performance guarantees for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm that utilizes latent Schatten norm regularization. These guarantees represent an improvement over existing bounds for methods based on the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality**: The paper's theoretical contributions are solid, with the characterization of the dual relationship between the latent and overlapped Schatten norms being a particularly noteworthy finding. The experimental validation, however, is underdeveloped and lacks sufficient detail. For example, the authors do not explain the selection criteria for the Tucker ranks (r1,...rk) in the examples, nor do they clarify how group regularization like l_1,∞ functions when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank in all modes. A key omission is a performance benchmark against prominent non-convex methods.

**Clarity**: The manuscript is well-structured and lucidly written, making the arguments easy to follow.

**Originality**: The primary novel contribution is the establishment of the duality between the latent and overlapped Schatten norms. This result is of theoretical value and likely holds independent interest for the research community.

**Significance**: Tensor decomposition is a fundamental problem with widespread applications. By improving upon existing theoretical results, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field. While the theoretical findings are compelling and the paper is well-presented, its impact is somewhat diminished by an experimental section that is too brief to be conclusive.
