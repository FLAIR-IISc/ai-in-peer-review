- Introduces a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, encompassing two recent convex norms for tensor decomposition.  
- Analyzes the latent approach and shows it performs as well as if the mode with smallest rank were known.  
- Proves a novel duality result for structured Schatten norms, relevant to structured sparsity.  
- Numerical simulations confirm theoretical predictions of mean squared error scaling.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically solid.  
- Provides a concise introduction and thorough review of prior tensor decomposition work.  
- Main contribution: detailed theoretical analysis of the latent approach, including consistency and a deterministic performance bound.  
- Demonstrates latent approach can outperform the overlapped approach in certain settings.  
- Clear and concise proofs; well-designed and clearly presented experiments.  
- Results are original and potentially significant for improving tensor decomposition methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel contribution, strong analysis, clear writing, rigorous proofs, supportive simulations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: may be too technical for some readers; needs more discussion of implications.  
- Suggestion for improvement: include more related work on HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
