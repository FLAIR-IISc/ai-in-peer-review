- Studies convex, norm-regularized tensor decomposition, introducing a generalized class of structured Schatten Sp/q norms and proving a duality between overlapped and latent versions.  
- Main theoretical contributions: consistency bound for latent Schatten-1 under a spectral-norm noise condition.  
- Main theoretical contributions: deterministic error bounds under an incoherence assumption that decompose error across latent components.  
- Main theoretical contributions: Gaussian-noise rates showing MSE scales with the minimum mode rank (or sum of latent ranks), contrasting with overlapped analyses that depend on average mode ranks.  
- Empirical simulations corroborate predicted scaling and show latent approach can outperform overlapped, especially when the tensor is low-rank primarily in a single mode.  
- Observes that the theoretically optimal regularization parameter λ can be chosen independently of the true ranks.  
- Builds on prior convex tensor decomposition/completion work and extends duality ideas from structured sparsity (overlapped vs latent group lasso).  
- Clear problem formulation and careful connection to structured sparsity.  
- Provides a useful, general duality theorem for structured Schatten norms.  
- Error bounds align with empirical behavior and clarify when latent is advantageous.  
- Simulations validate scaling predictions and give practical guidance on regularization.  
- Scope is narrow and largely technical, with weak ties to core ML applications and only synthetic experiments.
