This paper provides a significant contribution to the field of convex tensor decomposition by establishing theoretical bounds on the performance of an algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm. Notably, it improves upon existing results that utilize the overlapped Schatten norm for regularization.

Quality: The paper's primary strength lies in its theoretical results for tensor decomposition using the latent Schatten norm. A particularly interesting aspect is the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms. However, the experimental section feels somewhat brief and lacks elaboration. Several questions remain unanswered, such as the criteria used to select the Tucker ranks (r1,...rk) for the examples, the efficacy of group regularization methods like l_1,\infty when the tensor is simultaneously low rank in all modes, and a comparison of the proposed method's performance to non-convex approaches.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized and clear in its presentation, making it easy to follow.

Originality: The main original contribution of the paper is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which appears to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is a crucial problem with numerous applications, and this paper's improvement upon existing results makes it a significant work. The theoretical results are intriguing, and the paper is well-written. However, the brief treatment of experiments towards the end of the paper is a notable limitation.
