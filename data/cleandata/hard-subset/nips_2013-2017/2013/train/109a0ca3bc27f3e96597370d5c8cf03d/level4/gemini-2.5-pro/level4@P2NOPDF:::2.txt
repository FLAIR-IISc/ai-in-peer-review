This paper presents a convex optimization framework for Tucker tensor decomposition, leveraging a novel overlapped structured Schatten norm. The authors provide theoretical guarantees that demonstrate superior reconstruction performance compared to existing methods. The work is well-written and offers a significant technical contribution to the field. However, addressing the following points regarding motivation, theoretical clarity, and presentation would substantially strengthen the paper.

### Major Revisions Recommended

1.  **Motivation and Connection to Machine Learning:**
    To enhance the paper's accessibility and impact for a broader machine learning audience, the motivation for the problem needs to be strengthened. A clear connection to latent variable models would be highly beneficial. For instance, the authors could introduce a graphical model where latent variables ($h_i$) connect to observed variables ($x_i$) via a linear transformation, with arbitrary dependencies among the $h_i$. In such a model, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition where the multilinear ranks correspond to the dimensions of the latent variables ($\text{dim}(h_i)$). Including this discussion, perhaps with a diagram, would ground the theoretical contributions in a familiar machine learning context. Furthermore, this framing would allow the authors to better highlight a key advantage of their method: its ability to handle cases where only a subset of the latent variables have low dimensionality.

2.  **Clarifications Regarding Theorem 2 and Its Proof:**
    Several key technical and theoretical clarifications are necessary, particularly concerning Theorem 2 and its supporting lemmas:
    *   **Theorem 2 Statement:** The claim should be corrected to state that the tensor components are recovered up to a permutation, which is a standard but important detail in decomposition problems.
    *   **Justification for (13):** The paper asserts that equation (13) follows immediately from (12). This step requires explicit justification, as the connection is not obvious.
    *   **Missing Proof of Lemma 5:** The proof for Lemma 5, which is crucial for the main result in Theorem 2, is currently missing and must be included.
    *   **Proof of Lemma 4:** While the authors refer to another paper for the proof of Lemma 4, it would significantly improve the self-containedness and readability of this work to provide an explicit, albeit brief, proof or proof sketch.

### Minor Suggestions for Improvement

*   **Related Work:** The discussion of related work could be expanded to include methods like the Higher-Order Singular Value Decomposition (HOSVD) and its variants. Citing seminal reviews, such as Kolda and Bader's work on tensor decompositions (e.g., Section 4 of their 2009 SIAM Review article), and discussing how these methods provide approximation bounds would provide valuable context for the paper's contributions.

*   **Terminology for Tensor Rank:** The introduction should clarify the different notions of tensor rank. The authors use the term "mode-rank," which is also commonly known as "multilinear rank." Explicitly stating this equivalence and briefly mentioning other rank definitions (e.g., CP rank) would prevent potential confusion for readers.

*   **Computational Complexity:** A brief discussion on the computational complexity of the proposed optimization algorithm would be a valuable addition for practitioners interested in applying this method.

*   **Incoherence Condition Discussion:** The comparison of the paper's "incoherence" condition (following equation (10)) to the conditions used in sparse + low-rank recovery literature [1, 3, 10] appears to be inaccurate. The conditions in [1, 3, 10] typically relate the singular vectors to standard basis vectors in the context of sparse noise, which is different from the condition here, where the "energy" is spread across components and modes. It is recommended to revise or remove this comparison to avoid misleading readers.

### Typos and Notational Issues

*   **Notation for Components:** The superscript notation for components, $\hat{\mathcal{W}}^{(k)}$, can be confusing. Consider using a different symbol (e.g., a subscript) to improve clarity.
*   **Inconsistent Rank Notation:** The symbols $\bar{r}_k$ and $\underline{r}_k$ are both used to denote the k-mode rank. This should be made consistent. Using a dedicated operator like `mult_rank(·)` could be a clear alternative.
*   **Supplementary Material:** A closing parenthesis appears to be missing in the last line of page 12.
*   **Abstract:** In the abstract, the phrase "result for structures Schatten" should be corrected to "result for structured Schatten."

### Summary

The paper makes a novel and valuable contribution by proposing an overlapped Schatten norm for Tucker decomposition with strong recovery guarantees. The core ideas are sound and technically important. By addressing the required clarifications in the theoretical section and improving the motivation and contextualization, the paper can be made even stronger and more accessible.
