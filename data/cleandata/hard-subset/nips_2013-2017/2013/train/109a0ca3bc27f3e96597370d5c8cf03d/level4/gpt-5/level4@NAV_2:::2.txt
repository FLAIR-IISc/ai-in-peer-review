Additional comments after the rebuttal:
To make the paper accessible to a broader machine learning audience, the motivation needs to be strengthened. Please present an explicit latent-variable model (ideally with a graphical model figure) for which the proposed tensor decomposition recovers the model parameters. For example, consider latent variables h1, h2, … with hi connected linearly to observed variables x1, x2, … respectively, and allow arbitrary dependence among the hi’s. In this setup, the observed moment tensor admits a Tucker decomposition whose multilinear rank is dim(h1), dim(h2), …. This discussion—and a corresponding illustration—would greatly aid readers. It would also be helpful to emphasize that some hidden variables may be low-dimensional while others are not, which is precisely the scenario in which your new results offer an advantage.

The paper studies convex-optimization-based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms. The authors use the overlapped Schatten norm and derive improved reconstruction guarantees. The manuscript is clearly written and the technical contributions are significant. Addressing a few technical and notational issues would further improve readability.

Clarification about Theorem 2 and its proof:
- The statement of Theorem 2 is not strictly accurate: components are recoverable only up to permutation. This is minor, but should be noted.
- The claim that (12) “immediately” implies (13) requires a brief justification.
- The proof of Lemma 5 is missing, even though it is central to Theorem 2.
- It would be preferable to spell out the proof of Lemma 4 rather than only citing prior work, even if the argument is straightforward.

Minor issues:
- Please expand the related work on HOSVD and other tensor decompositions. In particular, discuss how higher-order SVD and its variants provide approximation guarantees; see Kolda and Bader’s review (Section 4).
- The rank notion used (called mode-rank in the paper) is commonly referred to as multilinear rank. Please mention this and clarify in the introduction that multiple tensor-rank notions exist.
- Include a brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods.
- The “incoherence” discussion after (10) should be clarified. As written, incoherence is interpreted as the “energy” being distributed across mixture components and modes (i.e., no component/mode having excessively large singular values). This is not the same as the incoherence conditions in [1,3,10], which concern sparse + low-rank recovery with sparse noise and impose incoherence of singular vectors with canonical bases. Since your setting differs, I recommend removing that comparison.

Typos/notational issues:
- The superscript notation for components, such as hat{W}^{(k)}, is confusing; consider an alternative symbolization for components.
- Both \bar{r}_k and \underline{r}_k are used for the k-mode rank; this should be unified. You might introduce a function like mult_rank(tensor) to denote multilinear rank.
- A closing parenthesis appears to be missing on the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material.
- Abstract: change “result for structures Schatten” to “result for structured Schatten.”

Overall, the paper is well written and presents a novel contribution toward obtaining accurate Tucker tensor decompositions using an overlapped Schatten norm, along with recovery guarantees. A few technical clarifications as noted above should resolve the remaining concerns.
