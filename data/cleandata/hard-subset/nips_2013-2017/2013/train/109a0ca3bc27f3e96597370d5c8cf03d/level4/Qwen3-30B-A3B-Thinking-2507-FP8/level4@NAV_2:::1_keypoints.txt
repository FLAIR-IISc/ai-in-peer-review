- Compares structured Schatten norms for tensor decomposition, focusing on the "latent" vs "overlapped" approaches.  
- Theoretical result: latent approach MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank of the tensor.  
- Overlapped approach MSE depends on the average (square root) of the mode-k ranks.  
- The theory explains previously observed empirical superiority of the latent approach in some settings.  
- Proves a novel duality between the two structured Schatten norms, linking tensor decomposition to structured sparsity.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and technically sound.  
- Numerical experiments confirm the predicted MSE scaling and validate theory.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to readers familiar with tensor decomposition and convex optimization.  
- Authors situate the work effectively within prior structured sparsity and tensor decomposition literature.  
- Main strength: rigorous theoretical analysis demonstrating latent approach advantages.  
- Main strength: novel duality result extending structured sparsity understanding.  
- Main strength: comprehensive numerical validation and clear exposition.  
- Primary weakness: scope limited to theory with no demonstrated machine learning applications.  
- Primary weakness: results may be seen as incremental—providing theory for an empirical advantage rather than a new method.  
- Primary weakness: lack of lower bounds on error rates limits conclusiveness of comparisons.  
- Overall contribution is primarily theoretical and important technically but would benefit from explicit ML applications to strengthen relevance for NIPS.
