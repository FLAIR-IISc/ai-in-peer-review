This paper investigates convex norm-regularized optimization methods for the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on a recent “latent” norm approach [25] and compares it to a previously used “overlap” norm approach. Specifically, the authors derive theoretical bounds on the mean-squared error performance of the latent norm under various noise assumptions. These bounds are favorable compared to existing analyses of the overlap norm, providing theoretical support for the latent norm’s better empirically-observed performance in a prior work. The theory is validated through numerical simulations, and the paper concludes with a generalization of both norms and a proof of its duality theorem.

The paper is generally well-written and appears technically sound, though I haven't thoroughly checked the proofs in the supplementary material. The results may be valuable to researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, my main concerns are:

(i) The results are quite narrow in scope and technical in nature, with only a peripheral connection to machine learning, suggesting potentially limited impact at NIPS. While the authors rightly point out that tensor factorizations appear in machine learning, the paper doesn’t explore the proposed approaches in the context of actual machine learning problems, either theoretically or empirically.

(ii) The results feel somewhat incremental and not particularly surprising (though this is admittedly subjective). They largely provide additional theoretical justification for a specific, recently proposed approach [25] that was already shown to perform well empirically. The techniques employed also don’t appear especially novel.

A further point is the absence of lower bounds to accompany the upper bounds. This is potentially problematic, as the upper bounds are used to compare different algorithms and claim the superiority of one over another.

Additional Comments:

- The abstract states, “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors…” This feels slightly misleading. The paper primarily *analyzes* two existing approaches (overlap and latent). The generalization appears only with its definition in section 2.1 and the subsequent proof of its dual norm (Lemma 1). No algorithms are proposed based on this generalized Schatten norm, nor is their behavior analyzed or tested experimentally.

- Figure 1 is presented in the introduction, but requires the background information from section 2 onwards to be fully interpretable.
