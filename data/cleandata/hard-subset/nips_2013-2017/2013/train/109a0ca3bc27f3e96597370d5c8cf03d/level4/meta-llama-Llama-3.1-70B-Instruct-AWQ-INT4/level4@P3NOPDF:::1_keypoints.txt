- Paper analyzes convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on latent vs. overlap norms.  
- Derives theoretical MSE bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, favorably comparing to overlap norm.  
- Theoretical results help explain previously observed empirical superiority of the latent norm.  
- Numerical simulations validate the theoretical predictions.  
- Generalizes both norms and proves a duality theorem for the generalized norm.  
- Paper is well-structured and appears technically sound (supplementary proofs not fully checked by reviewer).  
- Concern: Results are narrow, highly technical, and only peripherally connected to machine learning applications.  
- Concern: No theoretical or experimental evaluation in concrete machine-learning problem contexts, limiting impact.  
- Concern: Results seem incremental and unsurprising, with techniques that are not novel.  
- Concern: Upper bounds lack accompanying lower bounds, weakening algorithmic comparisons.  
- Minor issues: Abstract wording misleads about novelty of proposed norms; Figure 1 is uninterpretable without later sections; a few small phrasing and punctuation errors.
