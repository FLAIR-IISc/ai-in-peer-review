

This paper investigates convex norm-regularized optimization methods for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, with a focus on a recently introduced "latent" norm approach ([25]) and its comparison to the established "overlap" norm. It derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, demonstrating favorable comparisons to existing overlap norm analysis and supporting the empirically observed superior performance of the latent norm reported in prior work. These theoretical findings are corroborated through numerical experiments. Additionally, the paper generalizes both norms and establishes a duality theorem for the extended framework.  

The paper is well-structured and technically sound (though I have not verified the supplementary proofs in detail). The results hold potential utility for researchers developing optimization methods for tensor decompositions. My primary concerns are:  
(i) The work is narrowly technical and only tangentially relevant to machine learning, likely limiting its impact at NIPS. While the authors justify tensor factorizations' relevance to ML, they fail to integrate their methods into ML problem contexts (theoretically or experimentally).  
(ii) The contributions appear incremental; the results provide additional theoretical support for a recently proposed method [25] already empirically validated, without novel techniques or surprising insights.  

A minor issue is the absence of lower bounds for the derived upper bounds, which is problematic when using these bounds to claim algorithmic superiority.
