The paper presents a convex-optimization approach to tensor decomposition, leveraging structured Schatten norms – specifically, an overlapped Schatten norm – to achieve improved reconstruction guarantees. The work is well-written and represents a technically significant contribution to the field. Addressing several points will further enhance the clarity and impact of the paper.

A key area for improvement lies in motivating the problem within a machine learning context and broadening accessibility. The authors should explicitly present the underlying latent variable model from which their tensor decomposition arises. A graphical model illustrating this connection – for example, latent variables *h<sub>i</sub>* connecting to observed variables *x<sub>i</sub>* via a linear model with arbitrary dependencies among the *h<sub>i</sub>*s – would be highly beneficial.  Connecting the observed moment tensor to a Tucker decomposition with a multilinear rank determined by the dimensions of these latent variables (dim(*h<sub>1</sub>*), dim(*h<sub>2</sub>*), etc.) is crucial. This discussion should also highlight the advantage of the presented work: that only *some* of these latent variables may require low dimensionality.

Regarding technical details, Theorem 2’s statement requires refinement to acknowledge that component recovery is only guaranteed up to permutation. Furthermore, the derivation progressing from equation (12) to (13) in Theorem 2 requires additional justification. The proof of Lemma 5 is currently missing, which is a critical omission given its central role in the proof of Theorem 2. While Lemma 4’s proof is straightforward, explicitly detailing it rather than referencing another paper would improve readability.

Several minor enhancements would also be valuable. The introduction should include a more comprehensive review of related work, specifically mentioning HOSVD and other tensor decomposition methods, and referencing approximation bounds achieved through higher-order SVD and its variants (e.g., Kolda’s review).  The authors should also acknowledge that the tensor rank they utilize, termed “mode-rank,” is also commonly known as “multilinear rank,” and briefly discuss different notions of tensor rank. A discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods would also be helpful.

Finally, the explanation of incoherence following equation (10) needs clarification. The current framing, linking it to incoherence conditions used in sparse+low rank recovery ([1,3,10]), appears inaccurate given the non-sparse nature of the noise in this work. Removing this comparison is recommended.

Several notational issues should be addressed. The superscript notation for components,  *Ŵ<sup>(k)</sup>*, is potentially confusing and an alternative symbol should be considered.  The use of both  *r̄<sub>k</sub>* and *r̂<sub>k</sub>* to denote the k-mode rank is inconsistent; introducing a notation like `mult_rank(tensor)` could improve clarity.  A missing parenthesis in the last line of page 12 of the supplementary material should be corrected. The abstract should also be revised to read "result for *structured* Schatten" rather than "structures Schatten."



In summary, this is a strong paper with a novel contribution regarding Tucker tensor decomposition using an overlapped Schatten norm, accompanied by recovery guarantees. Addressing the outlined technical clarifications and minor issues will significantly strengthen the manuscript.
