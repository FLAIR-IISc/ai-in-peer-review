This paper provides theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm using latent Schatten norm regularization. The analysis improves upon existing results for methods based on the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality**: The paper's strengths lie in its theoretical results for tensor decomposition, with the dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms being particularly interesting. However, the experimental evaluation is brief and lacks sufficient detail. For instance, the paper does not specify how the Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) were chosen for the experiments. It is also unclear how a group regularization approach like l_1,∞ would perform when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank across all modes. Furthermore, the authors do not include a performance comparison against non-convex methods.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-organized and clearly written.

**Originality**: The main original contribution is the duality established between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, a result that appears to be of independent interest.

**Significance**: Tensor decomposition is an important problem with numerous applications. This paper advances the field by improving upon existing theoretical guarantees for convex decomposition methods, making it a significant contribution. While the theoretical results are compelling and the writing is clear, the work is constrained by a brief experimental section that lacks sufficient detail and comparative analysis.
