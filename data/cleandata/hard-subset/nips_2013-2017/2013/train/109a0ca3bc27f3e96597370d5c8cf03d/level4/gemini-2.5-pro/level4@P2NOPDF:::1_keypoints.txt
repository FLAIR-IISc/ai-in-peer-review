- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, focusing on the latent norm versus the overlap norm.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error (MSE) upper bounds for the latent norm under various noise models.  
- Shows these new bounds compare favorably to existing overlap-norm results, providing theoretical support for previously reported empirical superiority of the latent norm.  
- Supports theoretical findings with numerical simulations.  
- Introduces a generalized class of norms that includes both latent and overlap norms and proves a duality theorem.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound, though supplementary proofs were not checked in detail.  
- Scope is narrow and highly technical with no connection to specific machine-learning tasks or real-world experiments, limiting broader impact.  
- Contribution is incremental: provides theoretical backing for an empirically known method and uses standard techniques that lack clear novelty.  
- Lacks corresponding lower bounds for the error guarantees, so superiority claims based solely on upper bounds are potentially inconclusive.  
- The abstract overstates the novelty of the proposed "new class" of structured Schatten norms; the generalization is brief and not developed into algorithms or empirical tests.  
- Figure 1 is placed in the introduction but is hard to interpret without later background; it should be moved or better explained.  
- Minor grammatical and stylistic edits suggested (three specific line revisions).
