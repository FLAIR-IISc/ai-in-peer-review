- Paper studies convex-optimization–based tensor decomposition using structured Schatten norms and analyzes recovery properties of the overlapped Schatten norm with improved reconstruction guarantees.  
- Overall assessment: technically important and well written, but needs fixes to technical, notational, and exposition issues.  
- Theorem 2: explicitly state that components are identifiable only up to a permutation.  
- Theorem 2: justify the step that equation (12) implies equation (13) with a clear argument.  
- Provide the missing proof of Lemma 5, since it is key to Theorem 2.  
- Present an explicit proof of Lemma 4 in the paper rather than only referring to another work.

- Include a brief discussion of the computational complexity of the proposed methods.  
- Incoherence discussion: current usage is unclear and differs from incoherence in sparse+low-rank literature—either remove the comparison or clearly distinguish the conditions.  
- Notation: superscript component notation (e.g., \hat{𝒲}^{(k)}) is confusing; use subscripts or alternative symbols for components.  
- Notation: unify \bar{r}k and \underline{r}k for k-mode rank; consider a function like mult_rank(tensor) to denote multilinear rank.  
- Fix minor typos: missing parenthesis in supplementary material (last line of page 12).  
- Fix abstract typo: change “result for structures Schatten” to “result for structured Schatten.”  
- Conclusion: the paper makes a novel, valuable contribution; with stronger motivation, requested clarifications (especially Theorem 2 and lemmas), and notational/related-work refinements, it will be significantly strengthened.
