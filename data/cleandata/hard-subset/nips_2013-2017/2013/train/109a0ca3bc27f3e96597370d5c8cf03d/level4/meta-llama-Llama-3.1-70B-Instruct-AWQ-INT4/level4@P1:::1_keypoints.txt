- Paper studies convex norm-regularized factorization for approximately low-rank tensors, comparing the "latent" norm to the "overlap" norm.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models.  
- Theoretical results favorably compare to existing overlap-norm analysis and align with prior empirical findings.  
- Numerical simulations corroborate the theoretical claims.  
- Introduces a generalization of both norms and proves a duality theorem for that generalization.  
- Paper is generally easy to follow and appears technically sound (full proof checks in supplement needed).  
- Results are narrow, technical, and only peripherally related to machine learning, limiting potential impact at NIPS.  
- Paper lacks application-focused theoretical or experimental evaluation on real ML problems.  
- Contributions appear incremental and the techniques not especially novel; novelty and surprise value are unclear.  
- Upper bounds are provided without matching lower bounds, weakening comparative claims about algorithm superiority.  
- Abstract wording is misleading about proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms—generalization is limited and not empirically or algorithmically explored.  
- Figure 1 appears too early in the paper and is not interpretable without later background.  
- Several minor editorial issues/typos noted (suggested wording fixes at lines 38, 40, 395).
