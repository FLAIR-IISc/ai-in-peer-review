- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the "latent" norm to the "overlap" norm.  
- Derives theoretical mean-squared-error (MSE) bounds for the latent norm under various noise assumptions, favorably comparing to overlap norm.  
- Numerical simulations corroborate the theoretical findings.  
- Generalizes both norms and establishes a duality theorem for the generalization.  
- Paper is generally well-structured and appears technically sound (proofs not fully verified by reviewer).  
- Main concern: results are narrowly technical and only tangentially related to machine learning, with no evaluation in ML contexts.  
- Contribution appears incremental, providing theoretical justification for an already empirically validated method with limited novelty in techniques.  
- Missing lower bounds for the derived upper bounds, undermining claims of algorithmic superiority.  
- Abstract’s claim of proposing a new class of structured Schatten norms is misleading given limited novelty and lack of algorithms/experiments for the generalization.  
- Figure 1 is unintelligible in the introduction without background from Section 2.  
- Minor editorial issues: suggested wording changes at lines 38, 40, and 395.  
- Overall: main contribution is theoretical error bounds for a recent low-rank tensor method; technically sound but likely limited impact for NIPS.
