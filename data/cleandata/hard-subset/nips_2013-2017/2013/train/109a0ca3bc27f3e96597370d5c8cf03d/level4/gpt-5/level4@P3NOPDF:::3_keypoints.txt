- Studies convex tensor decomposition with latent Schatten-norm regularization, deriving improved performance bounds and a dual characterization relating overlapped and latent Schatten norms.  
- Theoretical results are solid and improve on prior bounds for the overlapped Schatten norm.  
- The dual characterization is elegant and likely of independent interest beyond the specific estimation problem.  
- Paper exposition is clear and well organized.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and lacks important detail.  
- Unclear how Tucker ranks (r1,…,rk) were chosen—known to the algorithm, estimated, or cross-validated—and sensitivity to mis-specification is not addressed.  
- Unclear how group regularization (ℓ1,∞) behaves when the tensor is simultaneously low-rank in all modes and whether it biases toward incompatible sparsity patterns.  
- Missing empirical comparison to common non-convex methods (Tucker/CP alternating minimization, projected gradient, factorization methods) for runtime and estimation accuracy.  
- Limited practical guidance on setting regularization parameter(s) in practice and whether there is theory-backed scaling with noise, tensor size, or ranks.  
- Required noise models and structural assumptions (e.g., incoherence, mode-wise conditioning) for the bounds are not clearly stated or discussed for plausibility.  
- Unclear whether the dual characterization yields computational advantages (proximal operators, efficient projections, or tighter certificates) in practice.  
- Need explicit statement of regimes where the latent norm outperforms the overlapped norm (e.g., partial-mode low-rankness or unbalanced ranks) with intuitive explanation.  
- Should highlight how the improved bounds scale with dimensions and ranks compared to prior overlapped-norm results (side-by-side expressions suggested).  
- Missing discussion of computational cost, memory requirements, and scalability to higher-order or larger-dimensional tensors and optimization bottlenecks.  
- Suggest expanding experiments to systematically vary Tucker ranks across modes, noise levels, and rank imbalance, including cases where only a subset of modes is low-rank.  
- Suggest direct comparisons among latent Schatten, overlapped Schatten, and group regularizers (ℓ1,∞), including sensitivity to hyperparameters and mis-specified ranks.  
- Suggest including non-convex factorization baselines with metrics such as estimation error, test reconstruction error, and runtime.  
- Recommend providing practical guidance for regularization selection and discussing robustness to deviations from assumptions (e.g., incoherence violations).  
- Recommend adding a concise intuition section explaining why the latent norm and its dual relationship yield better behavior in certain structures.  
- Recommend sketching how the dual view informs proofs or yields algorithmic benefits (e.g., dual certificates or proximal computations).  
- Request reproducibility details: dataset descriptions, optimization parameters, stopping criteria, and code availability.  
- Overall: a strong theoretical paper with clear writing and potentially impactful results, but acceptance contingent on substantially strengthening experiments and clarifying practical regimes, parameter selection, and assumptions.
