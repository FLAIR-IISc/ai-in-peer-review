- Paper analyzes convex norm-regularized optimization for approximate low-rank tensor factorization, comparing the "latent" and "overlap" norms.  
- Authors derive theoretical mean-squared-error upper bounds for the latent norm under various noise models.  
- Numerical simulations corroborate the theoretical findings and prior empirical superiority of the latent norm.  
- The paper generalizes both norms and proves a duality theorem for the extended framework.  
- Paper is well-structured and technically sound, though supplementary proofs were not fully verified by the reviewer.  
- Major concern: contributions are narrowly technical and only tangentially related to machine learning, limiting NeurIPS relevance.  
- The work is incremental: mainly provides additional theoretical justification for an already empirically validated approach, with no notable methodological novelty.  
- Lack of lower bounds weakens claims of latent norm superiority, since only upper bounds are provided.  
- The claim of proposing a "new class of structured Schatten norms" is misleading; the generalization is minor and lacks new algorithms or empirical ML validation.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 appears too early in the introduction and requires later background to interpret.  
- Minor editorial corrections suggested for Lines 38, 40, and 395.  
- Overall: technically competent and of interest to optimization specialists, but limited impact for the broader ML community.
