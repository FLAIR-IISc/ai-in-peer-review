This paper derives theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm that utilizes latent Schatten norm regularization. The results improve upon existing bounds for methods regularized with the overlapped Schatten norm.

**Quality & Significance**
The paper's primary strength lies in its theoretical contributions to the important problem of tensor decomposition. The dual characterization of the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is a particularly novel and interesting result that may be of independent interest. Given the prevalence of tensor decomposition in many applications, this work, which improves upon existing results, is a significant contribution.

The main weakness of the paper is its experimental evaluation, which is treated too briefly and lacks sufficient detail. To strengthen their claims, the authors should elaborate significantly on their experimental setup. For example, the criteria for choosing the Tucker ranks (r1,...,rk) for the synthetic examples are not described. The paper would also benefit from a more comprehensive set of comparisons. Several key questions remain unanswered:

*   How does the proposed method compare against group regularization approaches, such as l_1,∞, in scenarios where the tensor is simultaneously low-rank in all modes?
*   How does the performance of this convex approach compare to that of leading non-convex methods?

**Clarity & Originality**
The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the theoretical arguments easy to follow. The central original contribution is the establishment of the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms.

**Summary**
In summary, this paper presents valuable theoretical results for the tensor decomposition problem. The analysis is novel, and the writing is clear. However, the work is currently undermined by a superficial experimental section. While the theoretical contributions are significant, the paper would be much stronger if it included a more thorough empirical evaluation that addresses the questions raised above.
