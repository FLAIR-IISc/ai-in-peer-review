This paper investigates convex norm-regularized optimization for the factorization of approximately low-rank tensors. The analysis centers on a recently proposed "latent" norm [25], comparing it to the more established "overlap" norm. The authors derive theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise assumptions. These bounds are shown to be more favorable than existing bounds for the overlap norm, thereby providing a theoretical justification for the superior empirical performance of the latent norm observed in prior work. Numerical simulations are provided to corroborate the theoretical findings. The paper also introduces a generalization of both norms and proves a corresponding duality theorem.

Overall, the paper is well-written and appears technically sound, pending a detailed verification of the proofs in the supplementary material. Its contributions could be valuable for researchers working on optimization methods for tensor decompositions. However, I have several concerns:

(i) The contributions are highly technical and narrow in scope. While the authors correctly note that tensor factorizations arise in machine learning, the paper itself does not explore this connection. The proposed approaches are not evaluated, either theoretically or empirically, within the context of specific machine learning problems. Consequently, the paper may have limited impact on the broader NIPS audience.

(ii) The results appear somewhat incremental and not particularly surprising. The main contribution is to provide theoretical support for a recent method from [25] that was already shown to perform well empirically. Furthermore, the analytical techniques employed do not seem to represent a significant departure from existing methods.

(iii) A notable limitation is the absence of corresponding lower bounds for the derived upper bounds. This makes it difficult to assess the tightness of the analysis and weakens the claims about the superiority of one norm over the other, as these comparisons are based solely on the upper bounds.

**Additional Comments**
-----------------
- The abstract's opening sentence, "We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...", is slightly misleading. The paper's primary focus is on analyzing two existing norms (latent and overlap). The proposed generalization is only briefly introduced with a definition and a single duality result (Lemma 1); no algorithms, further analysis, or experiments are presented for this general class.

- Figure 1 is difficult to interpret in the introduction, as it requires background information that is not presented until Section 2.

- Line 38: "Tensor is" should perhaps be "Tensor modeling is".

- Line 40: A comma is missing after "Conventionally".

- Line 395: "also in such situations" could be rephrased as "in such situations as well".

The paper's main contributions are theoretical error bounds for a recently proposed low-rank tensor decomposition approach. The paper seems technically sound, but the results are somewhat incremental and may have limited impact at NIPS.
