- Studies convex norm-regularized optimization for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, comparing latent and overlap norms.  
- Main contribution: derives theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under several noise models.  
- Shows these new bounds are more favorable than existing bounds for the overlap norm and supports prior empirical superiority of the latent norm.  
- Validates theoretical results with numerical simulations.  
- Introduces a generalized class of structured Schatten norms and proves a duality theorem for that class.  
- Paper is well-written and appears technically sound (proofs not fully checked by reviewer).  
- Major concern: narrow, highly technical scope with only peripheral connection to broader ML applications and no experiments/theory on a specific ML problem.  
- Major concern: contribution seems incremental—mainly theoretical justification for an already empirically successful method, using non-novel techniques.  
- Minor weakness: lacks corresponding lower bounds, making it hard to judge tightness of the upper-bound comparisons.  
- Additional critique: abstract phrasing misleading about proposing a new class; generalization is only briefly introduced and not used algorithmically.  
- Presentation issues: Figure 1 is prematurely placed in the introduction and is not interpretable without Section 2; several minor wording/grammar edits suggested.  
- Conclusion: technically sound results but arguably incremental and of limited impact for the NIPS audience.
