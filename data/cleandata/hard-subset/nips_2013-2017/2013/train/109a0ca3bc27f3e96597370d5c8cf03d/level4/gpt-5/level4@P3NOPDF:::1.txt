Summary
This paper studies convex norm-regularized methods for factorizing approximately low-rank tensors. It focuses on the recently proposed latent Schatten norm [25] and compares it to the previously used overlapped norm. The authors derive mean-squared-error bounds for estimators using the latent norm under several noise models, showing rates that compare favorably to existing analyses for the overlapped norm and aligning with prior empirical findings. Numerical simulations support the theory. The paper also introduces a generalized class of structured tensor Schatten norms and proves a duality result (Lemma 1).

Overall, the paper is clearly written and appears technically sound, though I did not verify all proofs in the supplement in detail. The results should interest researchers working on convex optimization and tensor decomposition.

Strengths
- Clear problem formulation and careful theoretical development.
- Provides theoretical support for the empirically observed advantages of the latent norm over the overlapped norm.
- Simulation results are consistent with the theory.
- The duality result for the generalized class of norms is tidy and may be useful to others.

Weaknesses and main concerns
- Limited machine learning impact: The work is narrow and primarily technical, with only peripheral connection to ML applications. While tensor factorization is relevant to ML, the paper does not analyze or demonstrate the methods in concrete ML tasks (e.g., tensor completion for recommendation, spatiotemporal prediction, multi-relational learning), either theoretically or empirically.
- Incremental contribution: The results, while solid, feel like a modest extension that strengthens the case for a specific recent approach [25]. The findings are not particularly surprising, and the techniques appear standard for this line of analysis.
- Lack of lower bounds: Only upper bounds are provided. Since the bounds are used to argue superiority between approaches, the absence of lower bounds (or tightness guarantees) weakens the comparative claims.
- Scope of the “new class of norms” is overstated: The abstract claims a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors, but the paper primarily defines this generalization and proves its dual norm (Lemma 1). There are no algorithms, analyses, or experiments based on the generalized norms beyond what already exists for the latent/overlapped cases.

Suggestions for improvement
- Strengthen ML relevance: Include experiments on established ML benchmarks (e.g., tensor completion, video inpainting, knowledge graph completion), comparing latent vs. overlapped norms and other baselines, with metrics of practical interest. Discuss how assumptions (e.g., mode-wise low-rank structure) map to real data.
- Tightness and lower bounds: Provide lower bounds where possible, or at least discuss tightness (e.g., matching up to constants/log factors) and identify regimes where the bounds are known to be unimprovable. If full lower bounds are infeasible, consider instance-dependent lower bounds or minimax rates under your noise models.
- Clarify novelty: More explicitly delineate what is new relative to [25] and prior analyses of the overlapped norm. Highlight any new technical ingredients (e.g., sharper concentration, chaining arguments, decomposition lemmas) and where they are essential.
- Substantiate the “new class of norms”: Either temper the claim in the abstract or expand the treatment—provide illustrative examples, basic properties (e.g., decomposability, subdifferentials), proximal operators, and a brief discussion of algorithmic implications and potential benefits over latent/overlapped norms.
- Presentation: Move or better motivate Figure 1 so it is interpretable at first appearance, or defer it until after the necessary background. Expand the figure caption to explain axes, settings, and takeaways.

Minor comments and editorial notes
- Abstract: “We propose a new class of structured Schatten norms for tensors...” is somewhat misleading given the limited role of the generalized norms in the paper’s development and experiments. Consider softening this claim or broadening the treatment as suggested above.
- Figure 1: As presented in the introduction, it is difficult to interpret without the background from Section 2 onward. Consider moving it or adding an explanatory caption.
- Line 38: “Tensor is” → “Tensor modeling is” (or “Tensor modelling is,” but be consistent with your spelling convention).
- Line 40: Add a comma after “Conventionally,”.
- Line 395: “also in such situations” → “in such situations as well”.

Overall assessment
The paper’s main contribution is a set of upper bounds for estimators based on the latent norm, supporting prior empirical observations that it can outperform the overlapped norm. The work is technically careful and likely correct. However, the contribution feels incremental, and the lack of ML-oriented experiments or applications, as well as the absence of lower bounds, limits its potential impact at NIPS. Strengthening the connection to ML tasks, clarifying novelty, and addressing tightness would substantially improve the paper.
