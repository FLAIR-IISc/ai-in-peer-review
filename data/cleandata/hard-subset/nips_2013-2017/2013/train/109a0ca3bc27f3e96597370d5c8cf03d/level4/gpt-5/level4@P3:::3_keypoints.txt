- Formalizes overlapped Sp/q-norm and latent Sp*/q*-norm for tensors and proves duality between them, generalizing structured sparsity results.  
- Shows latent approach denoising MSE scales with the minimum mode-k rank, contrasting overlapped bounds that scale with average sqrt ranks.  
- Proves a norm inequality bounding the latent Schatten-1 norm by sqrt(min_k r_k) times the Frobenius norm.  
- Provides consistency and deterministic error bounds with explicit rank dependence.  
- Derives Gaussian noise bounds with a practical λ depending only on dimensions and noise level, not on unknown ranks.  
- Empirical synthetic simulations confirm predicted latent-rank scaling and show latent method often outperforms overlapped.  
- Solver code is available, aiding reproducibility.  
- Theoretical development is technically sound and connects to high-dimensional M-estimator and structured sparsity frameworks.  
- Constants in bounds are not fully explicit (universal c, factor F), though dependencies are clear.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to synthetic denoising; no real datasets (e.g., video, hyperspectral, neuroimaging) are tested.  
- No comparisons to standard non-convex baselines (ALS/HOOI/Tucker/CP) for MSE and runtime, reducing practical context.  
- Limited empirical exploration of λ selection; sensitivity analysis or cross-validation guidance is missing.  
- Deterministic analysis relies on an incoherence-type spectral bound with parameter α and spectral constraints; experimental enforcement and choice of α are unclear.  
- Lacks discussion on when α is small in practice or how to estimate it, affecting assumption transparency.  
- Rank notation is potentially confusing (r_k vs r̄_k) and could be streamlined earlier.  
- Paper should include a short algorithmic summary (beyond ADMM reference) to improve reproducibility without code.  
- Minor editorial issues and typos (e.g., “betters”) should be corrected.  
- Novelty: duality and min-rank statistical analysis are original and meaningfully extend prior work to tensors.  
- Significance: results could influence theory and practice by showing latent convex methods can exploit the smallest mode rank.  
- Questions for authors: how were synthetic Tucker ranks chosen; were spectral constraints with α enforced and how chosen; sensitivity to α; comparisons to non-convex baselines; extensions to tensor completion; guidance on choosing overlapped S1/1 vs alternatives.  
- Acceptance pros: strong novel theory, clear conceptual insight, broad relevance and potential impact.  
- Acceptance cons: narrow empirical validation, missing non-convex baselines, and unexamined dependence on α in practice.
