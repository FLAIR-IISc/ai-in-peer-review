This paper derives theoretical performance bounds for a convex tensor decomposition algorithm regularized by the latent Schatten norm, improving on prior results for overlapped Schatten-norm regularization.

Quality: The paper presents solid theoretical results for tensor decomposition with the latent Schatten norm. The dual characterization connecting the overlapped and latent Schatten norms is particularly interesting. However, the experimental evaluation is brief and not well elaborated. For example: How were the Tucker ranks (r1, ..., rk) chosen in the experiments? How does group regularization via the l1,∞ norm behave when the tensor is simultaneously low rank across all modes? How does the proposed method perform compared to non-convex approaches?

Clarity: The paper is well organized and easy to follow.

Originality: The main novel contribution is the duality between the overlapped and latent Schatten norms, which seems to be of independent interest.

Significance: Tensor decomposition is an important problem in many applications. By improving upon existing results for this setting, the paper is significant. The theoretical results are compelling and the writing is strong. However, the experiments are treated rather briefly toward the end of the paper.
