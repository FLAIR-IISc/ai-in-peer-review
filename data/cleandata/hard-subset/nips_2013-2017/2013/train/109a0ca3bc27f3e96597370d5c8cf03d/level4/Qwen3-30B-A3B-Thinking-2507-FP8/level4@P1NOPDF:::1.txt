

This paper investigates convex norm-regularized optimization methods for approximately low-rank tensor factorization, with a focus on comparing two approaches: the recently proposed "latent" norm (from [25]) and the previously used "overlap" norm. The authors derive theoretical mean-squared-error bounds for the latent norm under various noise models, demonstrating favorable comparisons to existing overlap norm analysis and supporting the empirically observed superior performance reported in prior work. These theoretical findings are corroborated by numerical simulations. The paper further generalizes both norms and establishes a duality theorem for the extended framework.  

The paper is well-structured and technically sound (though the supplementary proofs were not fully verified). The theoretical results may interest researchers in tensor decomposition optimization. However, I have significant concerns regarding impact and novelty.  

First, the contributions are narrowly technical and tangentially related to machine learning. While the authors reasonably note that tensor factorizations arise in ML contexts, the paper makes no attempt to evaluate the proposed methods within actual ML problems—neither theoretically nor empirically. This limits the relevance to NIPS, which prioritizes broader ML impact.  

Second, the results appear incremental. The work primarily provides additional theoretical justification for the latent norm approach ([25]), which was already empirically validated. The techniques employed (e.g., norm analysis, error bounds) are not notably novel, and the findings do not represent a substantial conceptual advance.  

Third, the absence of lower bounds for the derived upper bounds is problematic. Since the paper uses these upper bounds to claim superiority of the latent norm over the overlap norm, the lack of matching lower bounds weakens the comparative analysis.  

**Additional Comments:**  
- The abstract claims to "propose a new class of structured Schatten norms," but this is misleading. The paper primarily analyzes two existing norms (overlap and latent). The generalization in Section 2.1 (and Lemma 1 on duality) is a minor extension with no novel algorithms, algorithmic analysis, or experimental validation.  
- Figure 1 appears prematurely in the introduction; its interpretation requires background from Section 2 onward.  
- Line 38: "Tensor is" → "Tensor modeling is."  
- Line 40: Add comma after "Conventionally."  
- Line 395: "also in such situations" → "in such situations as well."  

In summary, while the paper presents technically competent theoretical work on tensor norm regularization, its narrow scope, incremental nature, and lack of ML context significantly limit its potential impact for NIPS. The results are of interest to a specialized optimization audience but do not advance the broader ML community.
